

Technical Quality: I rate this paper a 3 (Accept) after considering the rebuttal. The model definition is clear, and the algorithm extends a well-established framework [14] in a technically sound manner. However, the initial submission did not sufficiently demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior. Specifically, Figure 2 fails to quantify how SSMF improves upon the baselines—e.g., whether spatial boundaries are smoother (and by how much), or if SSMF identifies neuroanatomically connected regions absent in the alternatives. The claim that SSMF results are "more structured" lacks quantitative justification based on neuroscience criteria. *Added after rebuttal:* The new results in the rebuttal are informative, leading me to upgrade the technical quality rating from 2 to 3. I strongly recommend the authors include these results in the revision and explicitly quantify the structural improvement (e.g., via smoothness metrics or region connectivity analysis) and provide neuroscience-grounded interpretations of the dictionary atoms.  

Novelty/Originality: The paper makes a notable contribution by extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to enforce spatial contiguity in dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data.  

Potential Impact: Dictionary learning is valuable for explaining group neuroimaging data, and this efficient online algorithm with spatial priors offers a useful tool for neuroscience and medical imaging. The method could further benefit applications like image segmentation or regional sensing.  

Presentation: The paper’s organization is acceptable but requires substantial editing. Key issues include:  
- Line 67–68: Transpose symbol on $V^j$ ($j=1,\dots,r$) should apply to each $V^j$, not the group.  
- Line 73: Missing reference to the HCP dataset.  
- Line 83: "can imposes" → "can impose".  
- Line 146: Bolded text appears to be an unresolved co-author comment.  
- Equations (6) and (8): Sign discrepancy in the last line ($V^j + A_t[j,j]^{-1}(V A_t^j - B_t^j)$ vs. $A_t[j,j] V^j - (V A_t^j - B_t^j)$). In Equation (6), $V$ likely denotes the dictionary from the last iteration (not the $j$-th column variable).  
- Line 157: "a single, a single" → "a single".  
- Line 169: "We did an extensive study of th how" → "We did an extensive study of the how".  
- Section 3.3 (near Line 147): The solution to Equation (6) should be explicitly stated (e.g., in the appendix).  
- Figure 2: Results for different methods are shown at inconsistent slices (e.g., horizontal vs. sagittal), hindering direct comparison. The claim that SSMF results are "more structured" (Line 222) is unsupported by the visual evidence.  
- Figure 3: The prediction task (e.g., regressing brain data on behavioral metrics) is unclear. It is unknown whether predictions use $U_i$ or if the PCA baseline is shown. The caption’s reference to "R² > 0.1" (Lines 224–227) lacks context on which curves to examine.
