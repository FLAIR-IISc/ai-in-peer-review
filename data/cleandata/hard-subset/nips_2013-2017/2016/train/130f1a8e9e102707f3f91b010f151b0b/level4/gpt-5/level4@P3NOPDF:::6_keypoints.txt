- Introduces a novel regularizer that jointly enforces sparsity and smoothness for structured matrix factorization.
- Proposes an optimization algorithm to minimize the resulting objective.
- Empirical results show promising improvements over competitive baselines.
- Algorithm is described clearly at a high level and experiments support practical utility.
- Optimization problem is non-convex and the paper does not prove existence of a minimizer (no coercivity/compactness arguments).
- No formal convergence guarantees are provided for the proposed algorithm (e.g., convergence to a stationary point).
- Writing has clarity and typographical issues that require polishing (unclear sentences, undefined symbols).
- Recommend providing theoretical conditions guaranteeing a minimizer (or state assumptions under which existence holds).
- Suggest including convergence analysis or at least discussion of known results and monotonic decrease of the objective.
- Advise clarifying the intuition for how the regularizer balances sparsity vs. smoothness and adding an ablation study isolating each component’s effect.
- Request reporting sensitivity to hyperparameters and initialization strategies (multiple restarts, warm starts).
- Ask to improve presentation: tighten notation, fix typos, define symbols on first use, and revise unclear sentences.
- Recommend including per-iteration computational complexity and practical runtime comparisons to baselines.
- Overall: novel and practically useful regularizer with strong empirical results, but weakened by lack of theoretical guarantees and presentation issues.
- After rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied with authors’ convergence clarifications and raises the Clarity and Presentation rating from 2 to 3.
