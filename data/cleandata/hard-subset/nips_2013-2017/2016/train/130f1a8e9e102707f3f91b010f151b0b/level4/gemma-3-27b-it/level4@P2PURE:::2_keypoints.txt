- Presents an online structured-dictionary learning algorithm extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms.
- Model definition is clear and algorithm builds on a solid, established framework.
- Technical contribution initially judged marginally below a 3, later raised from 2 to 3 after informative rebuttal results; authors should incorporate those results into the revision.
- Empirical benefits of the Sobolev prior are not convincingly demonstrated in the manuscript.
- Figure 2 lacks detail and comparability, so it does not clearly explain why SSMF outperforms alternatives or whether spatial boundaries are quantitatively smoother.
- Reviewer requests explicit, neuroscience-grounded criteria and quantitative measurements to demonstrate improved structure and interpretability of learned atoms.
- Novelty is notable: applying Sobolev priors to dictionary learning is a meaningful extension with potentially significant impact for neuroimaging analysis.
- Potential future applications suggested include image segmentation and regional sensing.
- Manuscript needs careful copyediting; multiple confusing descriptions, typos, and leftover comments hinder understanding.
- Specific notation/formatting issues: misplaced transpose in matrix decomposition (lines 67–68) and missing HCP dataset reference (line 73).
- Grammar and leftover-edit errors: “The Graph-Net penalty can imposes…” (line 83), bolded remnant sentence (line 146), repeated “a single, a single” (line 157), and “We did an extensive study of th how” (line 169).
- Mathematical inconsistencies: last lines of Equations (6) and (8) are inconsistent and unclear about whether V refers to the previous iteration’s dictionary.
- Request to provide an explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6) (e.g., in the appendix).
- Figure 2 compares different methods on different slices, making direct visual comparison difficult and weakening claims of greater “structure.”
- Figure 3 is unclear about what is being predicted, lacks explanation of legends and the PCA baseline, and the reported R^2>0.1 claims are ambiguous about which curves are referenced.
