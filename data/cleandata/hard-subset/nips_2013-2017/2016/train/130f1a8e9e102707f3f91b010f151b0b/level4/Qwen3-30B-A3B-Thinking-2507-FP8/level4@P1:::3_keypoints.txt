- Proposes Smooth-SODL: SODL extended with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce spatial smoothness in dictionary atoms for neuroimaging.  
- Clear technical contribution that meaningfully extends online dictionary learning with structured spatial regularization.  
- Well-motivated problem statement arguing limitations of standard ROI approaches in neuroimaging.  
- Demonstrates scalability: Smooth-SODL runs ~3x slower than SODL (340s vs. 110s), presented as a reasonable trade-off.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation across metrics (explained variance, stability, prediction) with detailed hyperparameter and convergence analysis.  
- Main weakness: primary claim (better capture of inter-subject variability) is supported mostly by subjective visual inspection of brain maps.  
- Quantitative metrics (explained variance, prediction) do not convincingly establish the advantage in capturing inter-subject variability.  
- Learning-curve result shows a 14-fold gain at very small sample sizes (17 subjects), but this is not representative of typical large datasets like HCP.  
- Performance gap between Smooth-SODL and SODL narrows on the full HCP dataset, undermining the claim of benefit across small, medium, and large regimes.  
- Insufficient comparison with prior structured dictionary learning work for neuroimaging (e.g., Varoquaux et al. 2013, Abraham et al. 2014).  
- Comparison with CanICA is superficial and not fully appropriate given methodological differences (ICA vs. sparse dictionary learning).  
- Unclear that improved visual/spatial map quality translates to better scientific insights or more accurate interpretations of brain function.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound and scalable but evaluation methodology is weak and evidence for claimed benefits is unconvincing; recommendation to reject.
