The paper studies under-complete dictionary learning for fMRI and introduces a combined Sobolev/L1 regularization to promote smoothness and sparsity in the learned components. The idea is interesting, and the use of a joint Sobolev/L1 penalty is a reasonably original contribution in this context, aiming to capture spatial smoothness while maintaining sparse representations.

However, the empirical benefits over simpler baselines appear modest or are not clearly demonstrated. The manuscript does not convincingly establish when the proposed method outperforms standard approaches or why the combined regularizer leads to superior functional components. The improvement, if present, is insufficiently explained.

The presentation also feels rushed. There are still draft comments (e.g., at line 146), and the exposition is at times tedious and confusing, which makes it difficult to assess the contribution.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Clearly quantify the gains relative to simpler baselines and method variants (e.g., without Sobolev, without L1), with both quantitative metrics and qualitative visualizations.
- Provide a stronger rationale for the Sobolev/L1 combination: explain how each term influences the learned components, the trade-offs introduced, and sensitivity to hyperparameters.
- Improve clarity and organization: remove draft comments, tighten the narrative, define notation and assumptions early, and ensure figures/tables and captions are self-contained.
- Include essential implementation and optimization details (objective, constraints, solver choices, initialization, and convergence criteria) to enable reproducibility.

Overall, the approach is promising, but the empirical evidence and exposition need to be strengthened. A clearer demonstration of benefits over simpler methods, along with a more polished presentation, would significantly improve the paper.
