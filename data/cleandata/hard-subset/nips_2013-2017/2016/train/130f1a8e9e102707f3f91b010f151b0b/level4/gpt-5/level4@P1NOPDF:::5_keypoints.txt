1) Novelty concern: Method seems like a straightforward extension of Mairal et al. (2010) with an added Sobolev term and appears incrementally novel for NIPS standards.  
2) Need for clearer contribution: Authors must clarify what fundamentally new theoretical or empirical capabilities the Sobolev term provides beyond prior work.  
3) Ambiguous notion of “structure”: The paper does not define “structure”; the choice of Sobolev regularization and why it’s preferable to other priors (or how it relates to sparsity) is not justified.  
4) Missing alignment with brain organization: No clear explanation of what spatial smoothness/continuity the Sobolev penalty enforces and why that matches expected brain region organization.  
5) Possible typo in Eq. (1): Equation likely has a formatting/transpose error (e.g., should read [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T).  
6) Justification for ℓ2 on codes (Eq. (2)): Switching from the common ℓ1 sparse codes to ℓ2 requires rationale and analysis of its effects on representation quality and interpretability.  
7) Unclear statement at Line 146: The line is ambiguous and needs clarification or more detail if it denotes a definition/algorithmic step.  
8) Writing quality issues: Numerous typos and awkward phrasing; needs proofreading and editing by a fluent English speaker.  
9) Overall assessment: Interesting application and scalability, but lacks compelling justification for the Sobolev regularizer, limited novelty, and needs clearer definitions, empirical validation, and technical clarity.
