- Paper proposes an online dictionary learning algorithm for neuroimaging that extends Mairal et al. (2010) by adding a Sobolev prior for spatial contiguity, applied to fMRI and compared to two alternatives.
- Model and algorithm are clearly defined and technically sound, following a well-established framework.
- Empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior is not clearly demonstrated; need quantitative metrics (e.g., boundary smoothness, detection of known connected regions) and neuroscientific justification rather than vague claims of being “more structured.”
- Rebuttal provided informative additional results; reviewer raised their score and requests these results be included in the revision and further quantitative demonstration of structure and neuroscientific meaning.
- Originality: main contribution is the extension of online dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior, which is a notable and original idea.
- Significance: method could be a valuable, efficient tool for large-cohort neuroimaging and may improve interpretability; authors could explore other applications like image segmentation.
- Manuscript has multiple clarity and presentation issues, appears hastily prepared, and requires careful proofreading.
- Equation (1) dictionary definition has a potential transpose/notation ambiguity that should be clarified.
- Ensure the HCP dataset reference is present and correct.
- Fix typographical errors (examples: “The Graph-Net penalty can imposes...”, “a single, a single”, “of th how”).
- Remove or address an unaddressed co-author comment left in bold text.
- Clarify inconsistency in the dictionary update rule and the correct form of the argmin in Equations (7) and (8).
- In Equation (7), explicitly state that VA_jt uses the previous-iteration dictionary with the j-th column not being optimized.
- For reproducibility, provide the explicit solver (e.g., FISTA) or algorithmic details for the subproblem in Equation (7), possibly in an appendix.
- Figure 2a comparisons are misleading because methods are shown on different anatomical slices; present all methods on the same slices for fair visual comparison.
- Figure 2c is unclear: specify which behavioral variables are predicted, what features are used (e.g., codes U_i), explain the legend, include the PCA baseline, and make the R^2 claims verifiable.
