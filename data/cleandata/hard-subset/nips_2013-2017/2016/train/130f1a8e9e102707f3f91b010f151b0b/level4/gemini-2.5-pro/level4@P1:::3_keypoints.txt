- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online sparse dictionary learning extension adding a Sobolev (Laplacian) spatial smoothness prior for fMRI atoms.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) to produce spatially structured, smooth, compact brain maps.  
- Demonstrates method on large-scale HCP task fMRI and compares to standard sparse dictionary learning and ICA.  
- Contribution: scalable integration of spatial smoothness into an efficient online framework with modest computational overhead.  
- Main weakness: evaluation relies primarily on subjective visual comparisons (Figure 2) that are subtle and not convincing to non-experts.  
- Obviousness concern: smoother components are expected given the penalty—unclear if they reflect true neurobiology or merely regularization artifacts.  
- Lacks objective evidence that smoother atoms are more meaningful or faithful representations of underlying brain function.  
- Dataset choice questioned: HCP is large, so spatial priors may be less necessary; method might be more appropriate for low-data or noisy settings.  
- Results (Table 1) indicate the spatial regularization benefits mainly in low-data regimes, undermining the HCP application motivation.  
- Validation is insufficient: needs quantitative assessments like inter-subject consistency, reproducibility metrics, or correspondence with established brain atlases.  
- Overall impact unclear due to subjective validation and insufficient demonstration that the method yields biologically meaningful improvements.
