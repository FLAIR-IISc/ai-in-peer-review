**Summary of the Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

This paper proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for obtaining decompositions of brain images with structured and sparse components. The approach extends the online dictionary-learning framework of Mairal et al. (2010) by incorporating a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to impose spatial structure on the dictionary atoms. The method is applied to brain imaging data, where it is used to extract structured and denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.

The proposed approach is related to previous work on online dictionary learning, such as Mairal et al. (2010), but introduces a new spatial regularization term to impose structure on the dictionary atoms. This is in contrast to previous methods, such as Canonical ICA (CanICA), which do not model spatial variability across subjects.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

Strengths:

* The proposed method extends the online dictionary-learning framework to incorporate spatial structure, which is important in brain imaging applications.
* The approach is scalable and can handle large datasets.
* The experimental results show that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of extracting structured and denoised dictionaries.

Weaknesses:

* The novelty of the paper is relatively weak, as it builds upon existing work on online dictionary learning.
* The definition of "structure" in the context of the Sobolev regularization term is not clearly explained.
* The motivation for using Sobolev regularization is not well justified.
* There are several typos and grammatical errors in the paper.
