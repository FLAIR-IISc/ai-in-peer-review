- Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning by adding a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty on atoms to encourage spatial smoothness and compactness, combined with an l1 sparsity constraint.
- Claim: Maintains scalability of Mairal et al.'s online algorithm with only modest additional computational cost.
- Goal: Produces blob-like, more interpretable dictionary atoms that better reflect functional brain regions, especially with limited data.
- Evaluation: Tested on large HCP fMRI data and compared against standard SODL and CanICA (PCA/ICA-based) baselines.
- Strength — Significance: Addresses an important problem of extracting structured, interpretable components from massive neuroimaging datasets.
- Strength — Originality/Clarity: Clear, well-motivated extension using a Sobolev prior with a sound technical adaptation of block-coordinate descent.
- Strength — Scalability: Empirical runtime comparisons show the Laplacian term incurs only a modest extra cost, preserving online applicability.
- Weakness — Qualitative evaluation: Main visual comparisons (e.g., Fig. 2a) are subjective and not clearly convincing to non-experts; both methods recover similar large-scale networks.
- Weakness — Inter-subject variability: Paper motivates capturing variability but does not directly quantify improved stability or consistency across subject subsamples.
- Weakness — High-data regimes: Benefit of the smoothness prior is clear in low-data settings but is less convincing on large HCP datasets where standard SODL may already learn coherent components.
- Weakness — Need for objective metrics: Overreliance on interpretability; lacks stronger quantitative validations (e.g., stability analyses, downstream task improvements) and Smooth-SODL does not consistently outperform all baselines on prediction tasks.
