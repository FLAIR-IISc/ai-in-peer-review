- Proposal: Smooth-SODL augments online dictionary learning with a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to produce spatially smooth, compact “blob-like” dictionary atoms.
- Implementation: codes obtained by ridge regression; atoms updated by block-coordinate descent with an inner proximal solver (e.g., FISTA) under l1/simplex constraints.
- Scalability: mini-batch training with modest overhead (≈2–3× SODL) and reasonable wall times on a single CPU.
- Experimental scope: large HCP task fMRI dataset; comparisons to vanilla SODL and CanICA; reported explained variance, qualitative structure, stability, and behavioral prediction.
- Relation to prior work: efficiently integrates spatial structure into online dictionary learning, bridging unstructured SODL and slower batch structured penalties (TV-l1/GraphNet).
- Strength — modeling: clear and well-motivated idea to combine sparsity and a Laplacian penalty for spatial coherence and compactness.
- Strength — optimization: sound block-coordinate descent scheme with a proximal inner solver; convergence follows SODL framework.
- Strength — practicality: scalable algorithmic choices and empirical demonstration on a large dataset.
- Strength — empirical analyses: considered hyperparameter effects, preliminary stability analysis, rebuttal added learning curves.
- Strength — impact: method could produce reproducible, interpretable ROIs useful for atlas building and downstream prediction.
- Weakness — quantitative structure: lacks rigorous quantitative metrics proving atoms are more structured (beyond visual comparison).
- Suggested metrics: report Laplacian energy, total variation, connected components, compactness, Moran’s I, blob size distribution, and energy fraction in top component.
- Weakness — reproducibility details: need precise definition of split-half matching/matching algorithm, stability metric, and confidence intervals across splits.
- Weakness — behavioral prediction clarity: unclear feature pipeline, regressors, hyperparameter tuning, confound control, and need for PCA/RAW baselines and variability reporting.
- Weakness — baselines: missing structured baselines (TV-l1/GraphNet) to isolate value of Laplacian prior or justify omission.
- Weakness — hyperparameters: report chosen γ and τ ranges, selection protocol, and sensitivity/learning-curve plots in main text.
- Clarity — figures: align anatomical slices and thresholds across methods and show unthresholded vs thresholded maps consistently.
- Clarity — algorithmic details: provide explicit proximal update (closed form or FISTA steps) and exact projection onto constraint set used.
- Clarity — notation: ensure consistency between code penalty (ridge vs l1), constraint set (simplex Sp(τ)), and algorithm description.
- Originality: incremental but meaningful contribution—integrating Laplacian penalty into efficient online updates is a useful advance.
- Significance: potentially impactful for population neuroimaging if atoms are shown to be more reproducible and compact at modest cost.
- Specific technical issues: fix transpose placements, sign inconsistencies between Eq. (6) and (8), and clarify that V refers to previous-iteration dictionary during updates.
- Notation/definitions needed: define ∆, ∇, Lipschitz constants, and role of At[j,j]/t in γ scaling; reconcile claim of “no step size to tune.”
- Manuscript fixes: add HCP citation (Van Essen et al., 2013), correct typos/wording, and remove internal comments.
- Algorithms/figures details requested: state stopping criteria, number of inner iterations, typical γ values, mini-batch size, runtime scaling with p/k/batch, and clarify Fig.2 captions and Fig.2c prediction details.
- Pros summary: simple, well-motivated, scalable method with promising qualitative improvements and useful hyperparameter discussion.
- Cons summary: insufficient quantitative evidence of improved structure, clarity/notation/figure issues, and incomplete baseline comparisons.
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditioned on added quantitative analyses, clearer figures/notation, broader baselines, and preferably code release (e.g., Nilearn) to improve reproducibility.
