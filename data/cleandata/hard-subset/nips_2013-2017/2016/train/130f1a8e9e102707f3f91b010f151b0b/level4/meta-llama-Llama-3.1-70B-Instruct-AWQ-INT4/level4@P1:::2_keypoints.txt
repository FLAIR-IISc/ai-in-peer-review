- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary-learning algorithm with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous dictionary atoms and evaluates it on neuroimaging data against two alternatives.

- Model definition and algorithms are clear and built on a solid, well-established framework.

- Empirical benefit of the Sobolev prior is not clearly demonstrated in the results; comparative advantages (e.g., smoother boundaries, recovery of known connected regions) lack quantitative metrics.

- Reviewer requests neuroscientifically justified criteria and quantitative measurements to show atoms are more structured and meaningful.

- After the rebuttal, the reviewer found the new results informative and raised the technical-quality rating from 2 to 3, asking that those results be included in the revision.

- Novelty: notable original contribution in extending the framework to apply Sobolev priors for spatially structured atoms.

- Potential impact: method could be a useful tool for group neuroimaging analysis and other applications (image segmentation, regional sensing), in principle improving results via spatial priors.

- Presentation is acceptable overall but the manuscript is poorly edited, with confusing descriptions and multiple typos that hinder readability.

- Specific math/notation issues: ambiguous transpose placement in the matrix decomposition; inconsistency/sign mismatch between Equations (6) and (8); unclear whether V in Eq.(6) refers to previous dictionary iterate.

- Missing/incorrect references and wording: missing HCP dataset reference; several grammar/typo errors noted (e.g., "can imposes", duplicated words, stray bold sentence).

- Algorithmic clarity: requester asks for an explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6), at least in the appendix.

- Figure issues: Figure 2 shows different methods at different slices making comparisons hard and does not convincingly show Smooth-SODL is more "structured."

- Figure 3 is unclear about the prediction target, the role of U_i, missing explanation of legends and PCA baseline, and ambiguous presentation of R^2 results.
