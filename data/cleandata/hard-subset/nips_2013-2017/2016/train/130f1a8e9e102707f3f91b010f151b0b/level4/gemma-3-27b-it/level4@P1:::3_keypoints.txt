- Extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior to combine sparsity and smoothness for better brain region delineation — relevant and sensible.
- Primary results are visual and thus hard to interpret for readers unfamiliar with neuroimaging, making impact assessment challenging.
- Method scales well, building on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning.
- Authors argue spatial regularization may be less useful with very large datasets but show an initial explained-variance advantage in low-data regimes.
- Strong connection to prior work and clear positioning relative to ICA/PCA and other baselines.
- Hyperparameter tuning is acknowledged and practical guidelines are provided, but more detail is needed.
- Validation feels subjective; current metrics (explained variance, behavioral prediction) are insufficiently rigorous.
- Recommend adding quantitative validation such as inter-subject consistency metrics or comparisons to established brain atlases.
- Cross-validation procedure (folds, metrics) is under-specified and should be detailed.
- Sensitivity analysis for the Laplacian penalty parameter is missing and should be explored.
- Computational-cost discussion is vague (“slightly less than 3 times slower”); report wall-clock times across dataset sizes and dictionary complexities.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled spatial extension, scalability, improved low-data performance, and well-written presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: subjective validation, unclear benefits for very large datasets like HCP, and imprecise computational-cost reporting.
- Overall recommendation: technically sound and potentially useful for low-subject settings; lean toward acceptance conditional on minor revisions addressing validation and cost details.
