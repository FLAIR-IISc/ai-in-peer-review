- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary-learning algorithm with a Sobolev prior to enforce spatial contiguity, applied to group neuroimaging data with parameter sensitivity analysis and benchmarks.  
- Technical quality: rated 3 (Accept); model and algorithm are well defined and build on [14].  
- Main empirical concern: original manuscript did not convincingly quantify the Sobolev prior’s advantage—“more structured” claims lacked visual or quantitative evidence.  
- Required quantitative metrics: recommend neuroscience-justified measures (e.g., boundary smoothness, regional connectivity coherence) to show improvement.  
- After rebuttal the added results were informative and justified the technical contribution; the authors must include these results in the final paper.  
- Novelty: core contribution is extending [14] with Sobolev priors to impose spatial structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Potential impact: useful for group-level neuroimaging and medical imaging; spatial priors should improve interpretability and the online algorithm is computationally attractive.  
- Suggested broader impact: explore applications in image segmentation or regional sensing to increase relevance.  
- Eq. (1) notation: transpose on V^j likely applies to each V^j rather than the group—clarify.  
- Missing citation: Line 73 should reference the HCP dataset.  
- Typos/edits: Line 83 (“can imposes”), Line 157 (“a single, a single”), Line 169 (“We did an extensive study of th how”) need correction.  
- Line 146: remove what appears to be an unremoved co-author comment (bolded sentence).  
- Equations (6) & (8): sign inconsistency and unclear use of V (final dictionary vs. j-th column); explicitly provide the solution to Eq. (6), at least in the appendix.  
- Figure 2: methods are shown at inconsistent slices (horizontal/sagittal), preventing direct comparison; visual claim of “more structured” is unsupported.  
- Figure 3: ambiguous prediction target and unexplained legends; PCA baseline mentioned in caption is missing and R^2>0.1 curve assignments are unclear.  
- Overall recommendation: integrate rebuttal experiments, fix presentation/notation/typos, and provide quantitative neuroscientific evidence of increased structural coherence to strengthen the paper.
