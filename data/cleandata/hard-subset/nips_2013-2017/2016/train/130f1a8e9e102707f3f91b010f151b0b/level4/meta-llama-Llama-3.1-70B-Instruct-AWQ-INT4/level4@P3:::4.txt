The paper proposes an under-complete dictionary learning method with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization to identify functional components in fMRI data. This approach appears to be an interesting extension of existing dictionary learning techniques. The unique contribution of the paper lies in the use of dictionary learning with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization, which encourages both smoothness and sparsity of the components.

The authors provide a thorough overview of the proposed method, including the formulation of the problem, the optimization algorithm, and the experimental setup. The paper is well-structured, and the authors have made a clear effort to explain the technical details of the method.

The experiments are conducted on a large public dataset, and the results are compared to state-of-the-art methods. The authors demonstrate that their proposed method, Smooth-SODL, extracts structured and denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability in small, medium, and large-scale regimes.

However, the manuscript could benefit from a more polished presentation. There are some minor errors and typos throughout the text, and some of the sentences are wordy and unclear. Additionally, the authors could provide more context and background information on the problem they are trying to solve, as well as the relevance of their method to the broader field of neuroimaging.

One of the main concerns is that the results of the new approach do not seem to be significantly improved compared to simpler methods. While the authors demonstrate that their method performs better in some aspects, such as capturing inter-subject variability, the improvement is not substantial enough to justify the added complexity of the method. The authors could provide more insight into why their method is necessary and how it addresses the limitations of existing approaches.

Furthermore, the manuscript could benefit from a clearer discussion of the limitations of the method and potential avenues for future research. The authors mention some of the challenges associated with dictionary learning, such as the choice of hyperparameters, but they do not provide a detailed analysis of the limitations of their specific method.

Overall, the paper proposes an interesting method for dictionary learning with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization. However, the manuscript could benefit from a more polished presentation, a clearer discussion of the results, and a more detailed analysis of the limitations of the method.
