

This paper addresses dictionary learning for brain region modeling, proposing a large-scale online dictionary learning framework augmented with Sobolev regularization to enforce structural properties. My major comments are as follows. 1) The work appears to merely extend online dictionary learning (J. Mairal, JMLR 2010) by adding a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, the novelty is limited and does not meet NIPS standards. 2) The paper employs Sobolev regularization to impose structure constraints on the learned dictionary, but the precise definition of "structure" remains unclear. If "structure" refers to sparsity, the justification for how sparsity in the dictionary itself yields significant improvements in the problem context is not adequately explained. The motivation for using Sobolev regularization is therefore insufficiently justified. 3) Equation (1) likely contains an error; it should read $[V^1, V^2, \ldots, V^r]^T$. 4) The construction of Equation (2) requires further elaboration and analysis. Specifically, the rationale for using the $\ell_2$ norm on the codes (as opposed to the $\ell_1$ norm used in J. Mairal, JMLR 2010) needs clarification. 5) The reference to line 146 is unclear. 6) The manuscript contains numerous typographical errors and requires substantial language refinement.
