- Proposes an online structured dictionary learning algorithm for neuroimaging that extends [14] by adding a Sobolev prior to enforce spatial contiguity of dictionary atoms.  
- Technical approach and algorithm are clear and grounded in prior work, but initial empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior was not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Rebuttal supplied quantitative smoothness metrics and anatomical validation; these results must be integrated into the final paper.  
- Authors should explicitly quantify structural improvements (e.g., boundary smoothness, region connectivity scores) and connect them to neuroscience literature.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension applying Sobolev priors to structured dictionary learning for spatially correlated neuroimaging data.  
- Potential impact: useful tool for efficient, spatially constrained dictionary learning across subjects with applications in neuroscience and medical imaging.  
- Recommend exploring additional applications (image segmentation, regional sensing) to strengthen impact.  
- Presentation is weak: paper has confusing explanations, typos, and unedited co-author text that impede understanding.  
- Equation discrepancy: Eq. (6) vs. (8) show inconsistent signs—clarify the correct form and confirm notation for V as final dictionary.  
- Matrix/transposition ambiguity: clarify whether transpose applies to each V^j or to the group.  
- Missing references and typos: HCP dataset ref missing (Line 73), "can imposes" (Line 83), repeated phrase (Line 157), and other errors.  
- Figure 2 issues: inconsistent slice positions across methods prevent direct visual comparison; claim of "more structured" lacks objective justification.  
- Figure 3 issues: unclear prediction target, unexplained legends, missing PCA baseline, and ambiguous "R² > 0.1" claim.  
- Algorithm 2 needs explicit derivation/solution of Eq. (6) (appendix acceptable).  
- Final recommendation: integrate rebuttal quantitative results, fix notation/typos/figures, and add neuroscience-grounded metrics to make the contribution solid.
