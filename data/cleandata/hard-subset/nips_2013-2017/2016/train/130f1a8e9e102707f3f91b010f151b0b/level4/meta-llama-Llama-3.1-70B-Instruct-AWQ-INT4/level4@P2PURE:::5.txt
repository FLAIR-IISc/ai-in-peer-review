This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for brain region learning, incorporating an additional Sobolev regularization term for structural constraints. My major comments are as follows.

1. Upon reviewing the paper, it appears that the authors have primarily extended the online dictionary learning framework (J Mairal, JMLR 2010) by adding a Sobolev regularization term. In my opinion, this extension does not provide sufficient novelty to meet the NIPS standard.

2. The authors employ Sobolev regularization to impose structural constraints on the learned dictionary. However, the definition of "structure" in this context is unclear. If the structure refers to sparsity, it is not evident why the sparsity of the dictionary itself would lead to significant improvements in the problem at hand. The motivation behind using Sobolev regularization is not well justified.

3. I suspect a potential error in Eq. (1), where it should be [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T instead.

4. Further explanation and analysis of the construction of Eq. (2) are necessary. For instance, it is unclear why the l2 norm is imposed on the codes instead of the l1 norm used in (J Mairal, JMLR 2010).

5. The meaning of line 146 is not clear and requires clarification.

6. The language and writing style of the paper need refinement. Several typos were found throughout the manuscript, which detract from its overall quality.
