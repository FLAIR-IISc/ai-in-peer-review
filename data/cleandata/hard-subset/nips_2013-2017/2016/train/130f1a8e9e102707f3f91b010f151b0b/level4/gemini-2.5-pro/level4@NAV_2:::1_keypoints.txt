- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning algorithm adding a Laplacian spatial-smoothness penalty to encourage compact, blob-like sparse brain components.  
- Aims to scale to massive neuroimaging datasets (e.g., HCP) by using an online framework.  
- Empirically compared to SODL and CanICA on task-fMRI, showing improvements in interpretability, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Significance: addresses an important, scalable, data-driven approach for defining meaningful brain ROIs.  
- Originality: non-trivial integration of spatial smoothness into a scalable online dictionary learning scheme.  
- Quality: solid empirical evaluation on a large dataset, with useful analysis of trade-offs and learning curves.  
- Clarity/presentation: manuscript needs substantial proofreading and polishing; typos and awkward phrasing detract from quality.  
- Theoretical contribution: lacks deeper, self-contained analysis of algorithmic properties and how the Laplacian affects optimization or convergence.  
- Novelty: incremental extension of established methods—combines known priors (sparsity and smoothness) within an online optimizer rather than introducing fundamentally new concepts.  
- Overall: well-motivated and empirically useful method, but presentation and theoretical depth need improvement before top-tier publication.
