- Introduces Smooth-SODL, extending online dictionary learning with Laplacian regularization to enforce spatial smoothness on dictionary atoms for brain imaging.
- Simultaneously enforces sparsity (ℓ1 constraints) and spatial smoothness to better delineate brain structures from functional images.
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning framework and maintains scalability to large datasets.
- Empirical results on HCP task fMRI show improved interpretability and better capture of inter-subject variability versus SODL and CanICA.
- Computationally efficient, reported to be only 2–3× slower than standard SODL while scaling to modern neuroimaging datasets.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation including visual assessments, explained variance, stability metrics, and behavioral prediction tasks.
- Main shortcoming: no theoretical guarantees provided (no convergence analysis, error bounds, or optimality conditions).
- Lack of theoretical foundation makes it hard to assess fundamental properties and impacts of regularization parameters.
- Manuscript has numerous writing errors and unprofessional comments left in the final version, undermining credibility.
- Recommendation: empirical promise is strong but acceptance requires thorough theoretical justification and better proofreading.
