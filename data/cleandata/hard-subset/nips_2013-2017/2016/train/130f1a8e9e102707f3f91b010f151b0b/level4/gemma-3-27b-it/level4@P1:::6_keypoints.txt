- Introduces a novel regularizer combining l1 sparsity and a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to enforce sparse, piecewise-smooth dictionary atoms for neuroimaging.
- Extends online dictionary learning (Mairal et al., 2010) to multivariate brain images, preserving scalability for large datasets.
- Empirically outperforms ICA and standard online dictionary learning on HCP data in interpretability, variance explained, and predictive power.
- Demonstrates practical guidance on hyperparameter tuning and initialization for successful application.
- Shows modest runtime increase (≈3x) versus baseline, indicating a computational overhead despite scalability.
- Lacks theoretical guarantees: problem is non-convex and no proof of solution existence or convergence provided (validated empirically).
- Writing clarity needs improvement; some phrasing is awkward and could be more concise (example: line 146).
- Novelty is convincing: the specific combination of l1 and Laplacian regularization for dictionary learning in neuroimaging appears original.
- Significance is high: method provides more interpretable, structured ROI definitions and captures inter-subject variability with predictive utility.
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated, novel approach with strong empirical results and practical relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theoretical guarantees, clarity issues, and increased computational cost could limit adoption.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worth accepting based on empirical strength and practical impact despite theoretical and clarity limitations.
