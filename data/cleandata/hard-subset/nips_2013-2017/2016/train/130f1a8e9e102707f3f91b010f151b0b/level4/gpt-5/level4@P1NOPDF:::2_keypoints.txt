- Proposes an online algorithm for structured dictionary learning on neuroimaging, extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms.  
- Technical quality: model and algorithms are well defined and based on a solid framework, but empirical evidence for the Sobolev prior’s added value is weak.  
- Reviewer initially rated technical quality below 3, raised to 3 after rebuttal; asks authors to include the new rebuttal results in the revision.  
- Requests explicit, literature-grounded quantitative criteria (e.g., spatial contiguity, atlas alignment, cluster compactness) to demonstrate improvements from the Sobolev prior.  
- Novelty: substantive contribution by incorporating a Sobolev prior into [14], relevant for group-level neuroimaging analysis.  
- Potential impact: efficient online algorithm with usefulness for neuroscience/medical imaging and possible applications in image segmentation and regional sensing.  
- Presentation issues: manuscript needs careful editing—confusing descriptions, typographical and grammatical errors, and at least one unresolved co-author comment.  
- Equation/matrix concerns: clarify transpose application to V^j, inconsistent signs/scaling between Eq. (6) and (8), clarify notation for V in Eq. (6), and provide the explicit solution to Eq. (6) (e.g., in an appendix).  
- Missing reference: HCP dataset citation is absent (line 73).  
- Figures problematic: Figure 2 shows different slices across methods preventing direct visual comparison; Figure 3 is unclear about prediction target, legends, inclusion of PCA baseline, and which curves justify R^2 claims.  
- Empirical validation: encourage quantitative evidence that SSMF learns more structured and neuroscientifically meaningful atoms, with appropriate references.  
- Overall: promising and technically sound extension, but needs stronger quantitative validation of the Sobolev prior’s benefits and clearer, corrected presentation.
