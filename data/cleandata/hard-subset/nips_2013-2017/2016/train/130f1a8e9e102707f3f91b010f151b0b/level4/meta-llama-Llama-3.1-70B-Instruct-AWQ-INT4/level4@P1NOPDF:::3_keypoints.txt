- Extension of dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior is interesting, but the impact and improvement are hard to assess.
- Main results depend on visual comparison of blob clarity/consistency on brain images, which may be unclear to untrained viewers.
- Data quality can be as important as the algorithm; smoothness/spatial penalties unsurprisingly produce smoother blobs.
- The approach may help with limited-data settings, but is less compelling given large datasets like the Human Connectome Project.
- The paper fails to consolidate inter-subject brain-region differences or use cross-subject consistency as validation.
- Validation appears subjective and the benefits of the proposed method are not clearly demonstrated.
