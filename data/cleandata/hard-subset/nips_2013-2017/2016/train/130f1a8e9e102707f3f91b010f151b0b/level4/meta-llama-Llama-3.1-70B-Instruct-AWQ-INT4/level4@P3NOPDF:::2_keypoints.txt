- Proposes an online structured dictionary learning method for neuroimaging that incorporates a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms.
- Builds on a well-established framework [14]; model definition and algorithms appear sound but presentation undermines confidence.
- Reviewer initially rated technical quality below 3 due to presentation and evidence issues, later raised to 3 after rebuttal.
- Empirical benefit of the Sobolev prior is not clearly demonstrated—Figure 2’s claims of being “more structured” are vague.
- Authors should provide quantitative, neuroscience-justified metrics (e.g., boundary smoothness, recovery of known connected regions) to validate improvements.
- Novelty: meaningful extension of [14] by introducing Sobolev priors for spatial structure.
- Potential impact: useful tool for group neuroimaging analysis and possibly for image segmentation or regional sensing.
- Manuscript is poorly edited with confusing descriptions, typos, and an apparent leftover co-author comment.
- Specific errors to correct: transpose placement in matrix decomposition, missing HCP dataset reference, multiple grammatical mistakes.
- Equation problems: sign mismatch between Eq (6) and Eq (8), unclear notation in Eq (6); request explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Eq (6) (e.g., in appendix).
- Figure problems: Figure 2 uses different slices across methods hindering direct comparison; Figure 3 unclear about prediction target, legends, and missing PCA baseline.
- Overall: paper is promising but requires substantial revisions to improve empirical validation, clarity, and presentation.
