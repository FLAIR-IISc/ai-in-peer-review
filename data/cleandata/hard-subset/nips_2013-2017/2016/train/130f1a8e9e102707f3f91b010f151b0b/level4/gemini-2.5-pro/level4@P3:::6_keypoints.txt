- Introduces Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to produce sparse, spatially smooth component maps for neuroimaging.  
- Integrates spatial smoothness into the Mairal et al. (2010) online DL framework, promoting compact, blob-like, interpretable components.  
- Proposes a Block-Coordinate Descent (BCD) algorithm that preserves online scalability for very large datasets.  
- Validated on the Human Connectome Project (HCP) dataset and compared against SODL and CanICA baselines.  
- Novel and significant combination of online learning, L1, and Laplacian penalties tailored to spatial data.  
- Technically sound with clear derivation and convergence discussion, incurring a modest 2–3x runtime increase over SODL.  
- Comprehensive experiments using qualitative visuals and quantitative metrics (explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction), showing gains especially in low/medium-data regimes.  
- Weakness: non-convex optimization leads to potential sensitivity to initialization; more quantitative stability analysis needed.  
- Weakness: requires tuning multiple hyperparameters (α, τ, γ), making practical adoption computationally burdensome without heuristics or adaptive schemes.  
- Weakness: some technical sections are dense (e.g., dictionary update derivation) and would benefit from expanded intuition and clearer exposition.  
- Overall summary: valuable, scalable, and well-validated contribution that improves interpretability; reviewer recommends acceptance.
