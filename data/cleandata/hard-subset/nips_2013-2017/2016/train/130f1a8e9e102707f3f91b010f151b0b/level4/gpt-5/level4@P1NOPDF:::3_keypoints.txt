- Extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior — interesting direction but impact is hard to assess.  
- Main results are based on visual comparisons of brain “blobs,” which are not straightforward for non-expert interpretation.  
- Explicit smoothness/spatial-consistency penalties naturally yield smoother blobs, so smoother outputs are expected rather than necessarily novel.  
- With large datasets like the Human Connectome Project, data scarcity (where such priors help most) is not the primary limitation.  
- Paper lacks consolidation across subjects and quantitative use of inter-subject consistency/differences; validation feels subjective and overall benefit remains unclear.
