- Paper proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method enforcing atom sparsity and spatial smoothness via ℓ1/simplex constraints and a Laplacian penalty, with FISTA-based proximal updates.
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online sparse dictionary learning by adding a smoothness prior and claims modest (2–3×) computational overhead.
- Related to online matrix factorization, structured sparsity/spatial regularization (TV-ℓ1, GraphNet), and multi-subject decompositions (CanICA, MELODIC).
- Strength: Addresses a relevant problem—scalable, reproducible spatial components for large neuroimaging cohorts.
- Strength: Clear, practical algorithmic integration of Laplacian penalty into online BCD with per-atom proximal subproblems and FISTA use.
- Strength: Retains scalability and memory footprint of prior online methods; mini-batches and closed-form ridge codes suit large n, p.
- Strength: Large-scale empirical study on HCP (n=500) with comparisons to SODL and CanICA and useful learning-curve analyses.
- Strength: Qualitative improvements—cleaner, blob-like atoms—and improved stability across data splits versus SODL.
- Weakness: Quantitative gains are mixed—CanICA often has higher explained variance and better behavioral prediction despite improved interpretability.
- Weakness: Lack of strong quantitative spatial metrics (compactness, smoothness, reproducibility at matched support) to back spatial-quality claims.
- Weakness: Implausible claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e−6 accuracy; tolerance effects and proximal-solver impact on convergence are not studied.
- Weakness: Limited ablations—no isolated tests for Laplacian-only, sparsity-only, ℓ1 vs ridge codes, or simplex vs ℓ1-ball choices.
- Weakness: Manuscript clarity and presentation issues—typos, formatting, inconsistent notation, and hard-to-follow algorithm boxes.
- Weakness: Inconsistent use and justification of constraint set (ℓ1 ball vs simplex) and unclear role of non-negativity.
- Weakness: Insufficient justification for Laplacian vs TV (trade-offs like edge preservation vs smoothness not discussed beyond speed).
- Originality: Methodological novelty is incremental—natural combination of existing ingredients with efficient online integration.
- Significance: Practical message is valuable but limited by weaker quantitative evidence and CanICA's competitive performance.
- Suggestions: Add quantitative spatial metrics (dispersion, TV, Laplacian energy, Dice/ICC), statistical tests across splits.
- Suggestions: Run ablations for Laplacian-only, sparse-only, ℓ1 vs ridge codes, simplex vs ℓ1 ball, and compare to TV-ℓ1 dictionary learning.
- Suggestions: Clarify hyperparameter strategies, preprocessing, normalization, stopping criteria, and FISTA tolerances; improve writing and fix editorial issues.
- Acceptance pro: Practical, scalable approach that improves interpretability and stability with modest overhead, validated on large public data and accompanied by planned code release.
- Acceptance con: Incremental novelty, limited quantitative superiority on core metrics, and substantial polishing and stronger experiments required.
- Recommendation: Weak reject; promising idea but needs stronger empirical evidence, clearer ablations/justifications, and significant editorial improvements.
