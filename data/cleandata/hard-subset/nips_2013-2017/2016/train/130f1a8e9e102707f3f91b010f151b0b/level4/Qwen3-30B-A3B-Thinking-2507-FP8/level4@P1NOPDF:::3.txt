

The authors extend dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior, addressing an interesting problem. However, it is difficult to assess the algorithm's impact or tangible improvements over existing methods. The primary result relies on visual comparison of blob clarity and consistency in brain images, which is not readily apparent to an untrained observer. Moreover, in this domain, data quality is at least as critical as the algorithm itself. It is unsurprising that imposing smoothness and spatial consistency penalties yields smoother blobs; this is a direct consequence of the regularization. While this approach may be particularly valuable for isolating regions with sparse data, the abundance of high-quality data available through the Human Connectome Project suggests this is not the primary challenge. The paper would have been significantly strengthened by addressing inter-subject variability—consolidating differences across brain regions and using inter-subject consistency as a validation metric. Instead, the validation presented appears subjective, and it remains unclear what practical benefit the proposed method offers over simpler alternatives. The lack of rigorous validation against established benchmarks or biological ground truth further diminishes the claimed contribution.
