- Introduces a novel regularizer combining L1 sparsity with a Laplacian penalty to encourage spatially contiguous, blob-like dictionary atoms.  
- Presents an algorithm for online dictionary learning incorporating this regularizer and shows empirical improvements.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance versus CanICA and standard online dictionary learning (SODL), including behavioral prediction and inter-subject variability capture.  
- Validated on a large-scale HCP neuroimaging dataset, showcasing applicability to real-world, large datasets.  
- Regularizer improves interpretability and denoising of learned components for neuroimaging applications.  
- Builds on a scalable online DL framework, enabling application to large datasets.  
- Optimization is non-convex and the paper lacks theoretical guarantees or a proof of solution existence.  
- Heavy reliance on empirical validation without sufficient theoretical underpinning.  
- Writing could be clearer and more concise; specific phrasing (e.g., line 146) needs reworking.  
- Hyperparameter selection description is present but insufficiently detailed.  
- Computational cost is higher (2–3x slowdown vs SODL); scalability analysis with increasing dataset size is lacking.  
- Overall assessment: promising and impactful empirical work deserving acceptance despite theoretical and clarity shortcomings; authors responsive to feedback.
