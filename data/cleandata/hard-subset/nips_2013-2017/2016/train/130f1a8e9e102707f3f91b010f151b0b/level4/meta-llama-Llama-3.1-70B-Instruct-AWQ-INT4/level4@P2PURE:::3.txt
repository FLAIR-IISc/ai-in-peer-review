The authors propose an extension of dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior, which is an interesting problem. However, it is challenging to assess the impact or improvement of this newly proposed algorithm. The main results figure relies heavily on visual comparisons of the clarity and consistency of identified brain blobs, but these differences may not be apparent to an untrained observer. Furthermore, in my experience with similar decomposition techniques, the quality of the data is equally, if not more, important than the algorithm itself. By introducing penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, it is not entirely surprising that the resulting blobs appear smoother. While this approach may be beneficial for isolating regions with limited data, it seems less relevant in the context of the Human Connectome Project, where vast amounts of data are available. I had hoped that this paper would provide a more comprehensive analysis of the differences between brain regions across subjects, potentially utilizing inter-subject differences and consistency as a means of validating the algorithm's performance. Unfortunately, the validation presented in this work appears subjective, and the benefits of the proposed approach are not clearly demonstrated.
