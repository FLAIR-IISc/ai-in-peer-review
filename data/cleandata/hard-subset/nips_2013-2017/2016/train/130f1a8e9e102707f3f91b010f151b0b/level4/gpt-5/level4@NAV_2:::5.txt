This paper studies dictionary learning for identifying brain regions. Specifically, the authors introduce a scalable online dictionary-learning framework augmented with a Sobolev (Laplacian) regularizer to encode structure. The work targets dictionary learning in the context of brain-region discovery, concretely extending online dictionary learning with an added Sobolev term to promote structured components.

My major comments are as follows.
1) In my understanding, this work primarily augments the online dictionary learning approach of Mairal et al. (JMLR 2010) with a Sobolev regularization term. As a result, the degree of novelty appears limited and may fall short of the NIPS standard.
2) The paper uses Sobolev regularization to impose a structural constraint on the learned dictionary. However, the definition of “structure” is not sufficiently precise. If “structure” is intended to mean sparsity, please clarify; if not, please clearly differentiate smoothness/compactness from sparsity. Moreover, why would sparsity of the atoms itself yield substantial gains for your tasks? Overall, the motivation for adopting the Sobolev prior needs to be better articulated.
3) I suspect a mistake in Eq. (1); it may need to be written as [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T.
4) Additional explanation and analysis for the formulation in Eq. (2) would be helpful. For example, why is an ℓ2 penalty used on the codes rather than the ℓ1 norm as in Mairal (JMLR 2010)?
5) What is the meaning of line 146?
6) The manuscript would benefit from language polishing; there are numerous typographical errors.
