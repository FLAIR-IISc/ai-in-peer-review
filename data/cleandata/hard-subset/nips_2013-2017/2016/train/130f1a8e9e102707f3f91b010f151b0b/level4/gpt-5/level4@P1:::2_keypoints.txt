- Proposes an online multivariate dictionary-learning method with a Sobolev/Laplacian prior to produce spatially smooth, compact atoms.  
- Extends Mairal et al.'s online dictionary-learning by adding Laplacian regularization, ridge-coded updates, and block-coordinate descent with FISTA.  
- Evaluated on large-scale HCP task fMRI, with comparisons to Sparse Online Dictionary Learning (SODL) and CanICA.  
- Relates to structured sparse neuroimaging methods (e.g., TV-ℓ1) but emphasizes scalability via cheaper Laplacian regularization.  
- Model and algorithms are well defined and based on a solid existing framework.  
- Reviewer initially rated technical quality below 3 due to insufficient empirical evidence for the Sobolev prior’s advantage.  
- Figure 2 does not clearly demonstrate smoother or more compact spatial boundaries or uniquely recovered contiguous regions.  
- Reviewer requests quantitative spatial-structure metrics (connectedness, compactness, Laplacian energy, component sizes, sparsity) with neuroscience references.  
- Rebuttal results improved the reviewer’s technical rating from 2 to 3; inclusion of those results in the final paper is encouraged.  
- Main novelty: integrating a Sobolev/Laplacian prior into an online dictionary-learning framework while preserving scalability.  
- Potential impact: useful scalable tool for modeling inter-subject variability and applicable to tasks like segmentation or region sensing.  
- Manuscript contains multiple clarity and presentation issues (typos, confusing passages, equation sign inconsistencies, missing references).  
- Specific corrections requested for equations, algorithm details, SVD-based solution and complexity for Eq. (6), and several line-specific typos.  
- Figures need consistent slices/thresholding across methods and clearer captions/legends (especially Figure 2 and Figure 3).  
- Behavioral prediction setup is unclear—clarify targets, features (X vs Ui), protocol, and ensure reported baselines (e.g., PCA) appear.  
- Strengths: clear formulation, sensible algorithmic choices, scalable implementation, and large-scale empirical evaluation.  
- Weaknesses: unclear empirical benefit of Laplacian prior, presentation flaws hindering reproducibility, and unsupported “more structured” claims.  
- Suggested improvements: include rebuttal analyses, add quantitative structure metrics, standardize visual comparisons, clarify prediction experiments, and fix all noted errors.  
- Overall: promising, scalable contribution that needs clearer empirical validation of the spatial prior and improved presentation to be a valuable neuroimaging tool.
