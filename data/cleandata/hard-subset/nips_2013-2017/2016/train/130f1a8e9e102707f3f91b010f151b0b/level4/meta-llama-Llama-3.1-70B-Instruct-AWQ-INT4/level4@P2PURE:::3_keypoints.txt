- Proposes extending dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior—interesting idea but impact is hard to assess.
- Main results rely heavily on visual comparisons of blob clarity/consistency, which may not be evident to untrained observers.
- Lack of quantitative evaluation or objective metrics makes it difficult to judge improvement over baselines.
- Data quality likely drives decomposition results as much or more than the algorithm itself.
- Enforcing smoothness/spatial consistency naturally produces smoother blobs, so the outcome is unsurprising.
- Method may help in low-data settings but is less compelling for large datasets like the Human Connectome Project.
- Paper misses a comprehensive analysis of inter-subject differences and cross-subject consistency as validation.
- Overall validation is subjective and the benefits of the proposed approach are not clearly demonstrated.
