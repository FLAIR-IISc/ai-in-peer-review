

The authors extend dictionary learning models by integrating a Sobolev prior, addressing an interesting problem. However, the practical significance or measurable improvement of the proposed algorithm remains difficult to assess. The primary results rely on subjective visual comparisons of blob clarity and consistency in brain imagery, which are not readily interpretable by non-specialists. Furthermore, in this domain, data quality and quantity are often as critical as the algorithm itself. Given that spatial smoothness and consistency penalties inherently yield smoother reconstructions, the observed results are unsurprising. While this approach might aid in isolating regions with sparse data, its relevance is questionable given the extensive datasets now available through the Human Connectome Project. I had anticipated the paper would more effectively leverage inter-subject variability to differentiate brain regions and use consistency across subjects as a validation metric. The current validation, however, lacks objective criteria and fails to demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
