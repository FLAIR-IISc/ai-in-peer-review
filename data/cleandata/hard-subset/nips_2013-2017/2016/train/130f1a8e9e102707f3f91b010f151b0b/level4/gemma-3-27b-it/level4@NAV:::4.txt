This paper introduces an under-complete dictionary learning method incorporating both Sobolev and L1 regularization, applied to the decomposition of functional MRI data. The core idea – utilizing combined Sobolev/L1 regularization within a dictionary learning framework to promote both smoothness and sparsity in the identified components – presents a potentially valuable direction. However, the demonstrated performance gains over more established methods are modest and not convincingly justified by the presented results. Furthermore, the manuscript would benefit from significant polishing; the writing is currently dense and occasionally unclear, and contains remnants of the drafting process (e.g., line 146). Overall, while the approach holds promise, the presentation and strength of empirical validation require substantial improvement.
