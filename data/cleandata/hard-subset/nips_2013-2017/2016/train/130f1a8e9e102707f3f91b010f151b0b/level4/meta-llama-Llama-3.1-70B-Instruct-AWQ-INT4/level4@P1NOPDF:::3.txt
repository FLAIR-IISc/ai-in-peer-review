The authors extend dictionary learning models to incorporate a Sobolev prior, which is an interesting problem. However, it is challenging to assess the impact or improvement of this newly proposed algorithm. The main results figure relies heavily on visually comparing the clarity and consistency of identified blobs on the brain, but this may not be immediately clear to an untrained eye. In my experience with this type of decomposition, the quality of the data is equally, if not more, important than the algorithm itself. By incorporating penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, it is not entirely surprising that the resulting blobs are smoother. This approach may be more useful when dealing with limited data, but given the vast amount of data available through the Human Connectome Project, this does not appear to be a significant issue. I had hoped that this paper would do more to consolidate the differences between brain regions across subjects and utilize inter-subject differences and consistency as a means of validating performance. Unfortunately, the validation presented here seems subjective, and the benefits of the proposed approach are not clearly demonstrated.
