

Technical Quality: I would rate this paper as 2.5 (marginally below 3 on the 1–5 scale). The model definition is clear, and the algorithms follow a well-established framework [14], appearing technically sound. However, the presentation somewhat undermines the contribution, as the paper does not clearly demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior in the results. Specifically, Figure 2 fails to specify *how* SSMF outperforms the two alternative methods. Were spatial boundaries quantitatively smoother? Did SSMF identify known connected brain regions that other methods missed? Beyond vaguely stating SSMF results were "more structured," the authors should explicitly describe neuroscience-justified criteria and provide quantitative metrics for improvement. Added after rebuttal: The rebuttal results are highly informative. I have revised my technical quality rating from 2 to 3. I strongly recommend the authors incorporate these results in the revision, along with quantitative evidence demonstrating that SSMF atoms are both more structured (via specified metrics) and more meaningful (with references to neuroscience literature).  

Novelty/Originality: The paper makes a notable contribution through its novel extension of [14] by incorporating Sobolev priors to enforce spatial structure in dictionary atoms.  

Potential Impact/Usefulness: Dictionary learning is valuable for explaining group neuroimaging data, and spatial priors like the Sobolev prior should theoretically improve results. This efficient online algorithm provides a useful tool for neuroscience and medical imaging. The authors could further explore applications in image segmentation and regional sensing.  

Presentation: The paper’s organization is acceptable, but it requires significant editing. Confusing descriptions and typos hinder comprehension. Specific issues include:  
- Line 67–68: Matrix decomposition equation—should the transpose symbol apply to each $V^j$ (j = 1,..., r), not the group?  
- Line 73: Missing reference to HCP dataset.

- Line 146: Bolded text appears to be a co-author’s unedited comment.  
- Equations (6) and (8): Discrepancy in sign (e.g., $V^j + At[j,j]^{-1}(VAt^j - Bt^j)$ vs. $At[j,j] V^j - (VAt^j - Bt^j)$). In Equation (6), does $V$ denote the dictionary from the last iteration (so $V^j$ is not the variable)?

- Section 3.3 (near Line 147): The mathematical solution to Equation (6) should be explicitly provided (e.g., in the appendix).  
- Figure 2: Results for different methods are shown at varying slices (horizontal/sagittal), preventing direct comparison. The claim in Line 222 that SSMF results are "more structured" lacks supporting evidence from the figure.  
- Figure 3: Unclear what is being predicted (e.g., regression of brain data on behavioral metrics? Based on $U_i$?). Legends are unexplained in the main text or figure caption. The figure does not display the PCA baseline mentioned in the caption, and the reference to "R² > 0.1" (Lines 224–227) is uninterpretable without specifying which curves.
