- Proposal: extend dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior to encourage smoothness and spatial consistency in learned components.
- Insufficient empirical evidence: the paper does not convincingly demonstrate impact or performance improvement of the proposed algorithm.
- Validation methods: primary validation is visual comparison of component clarity/consistency, which is subjective and unclear to non-experts.
- Expected outcome: smoother components are a direct consequence of the imposed penalties, so smoothness alone is not a strong efficacy claim.
- Applicability concern: the prior may help in data-sparse settings but is less clearly relevant for data-rich applications (e.g., Human Connectome Project).
- Missing stronger metrics: consolidation across subjects and inter-subject consistency would provide a more objective and convincing evaluation.
- Practical benefit unclear: overall validation is subjective and does not establish clear practical advantages of the method.
