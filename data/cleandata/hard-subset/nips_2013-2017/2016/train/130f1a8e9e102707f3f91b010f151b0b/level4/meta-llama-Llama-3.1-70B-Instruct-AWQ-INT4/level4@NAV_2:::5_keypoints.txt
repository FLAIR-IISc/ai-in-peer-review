- Proposes Smooth-SODL: a multivariate online dictionary-learning method adding a Sobolev penalty for piecewise-smooth, compact dictionary atoms.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning framework and extends it with smoothness regularization.  
- Evaluated on a large public brain-imaging dataset and compared to state-of-the-art models.  
- Claims improved extraction of structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.  
- Reports superior performance over competitors in explained variance, stability, and reproducibility.  
- Presentation is clear and the experimental evaluation is thorough.  
- Includes a concise study of hyperparameter impacts and describes the optimality regime.  
- Novelty is potentially limited since the work mainly extends existing online dictionary-learning approaches.  
- The notion of “structure” under Sobolev regularization is not clearly defined and its motivation could be stronger.  
- The model construction needs more detailed analysis, particularly the choice of l2 norm for codes instead of l1.  
- Minor issues: possible mistake in Eq.(1), unclear statement on line 146, and language/typos that need polishing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension with Sobolev penalty, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unclear definition/motivation of structure, and insufficient explanation of modeling choices.  
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with strong evaluation; strengths outweigh weaknesses and it is a good candidate for acceptance.
