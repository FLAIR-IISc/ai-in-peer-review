Summary
The paper addresses dictionary learning for the analysis of brain regions. It proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework augmented with a Sobolev regularization term intended to enforce structure on the learned dictionary.

Major comments
1) Novelty: The contribution appears to be a straightforward extension of online dictionary learning (Mairal, JMLR 2010) with an additional Sobolev regularization term. As presented, the incremental novelty seems limited and does not meet the NIPS standard.

2) Definition and motivation of “structure”: The paper uses Sobolev regularization to impose structure on the learned dictionary, but the notion of “structure” is insufficiently defined. If “structure” is intended to mean sparsity, please clarify why promoting sparsity in the dictionary itself (rather than the codes) is expected to yield significant improvements for the targeted brain-region problems. Overall, the motivation for using a Sobolev penalty in this context is not clearly articulated and needs stronger justification.

3) Possible typo in Eq. (1): There may be a mistake in Eq. (1); it likely should be [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T.

4) Construction of Eq. (2): More explanation and analysis are needed for Eq. (2). In particular, why is an ℓ2 norm imposed on the codes, instead of the ℓ1 norm used in Mairal (JMLR 2010)? Please discuss the trade-offs, theoretical rationale, and empirical consequences of this choice.

5) Line 146: The content at line 146 is unclear. Please clarify what is meant there.

6) Writing quality: The manuscript would benefit from substantial language editing. There are many typos and phrasing issues that impede readability.

Overall assessment
While the application area is important and large-scale methods are valuable, the paper’s novelty relative to prior online dictionary learning work is limited, and key modeling choices (especially the role and benefit of Sobolev regularization and the use of ℓ2 on codes) are insufficiently motivated and explained. Combined with clarity and presentation issues, the submission in its current form falls short of the NIPS bar.
