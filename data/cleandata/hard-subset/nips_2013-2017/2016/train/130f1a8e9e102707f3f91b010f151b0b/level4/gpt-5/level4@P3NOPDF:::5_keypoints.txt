- Summary: Proposes large-scale online dictionary learning with a Sobolev regularizer to encourage spatially structured (smooth) atoms for neuroimaging, aiming for interpretable, stable, scalable bases.

- Strength: Tackles timely problem of large-scale neuroimaging with online learning.

- Strength: Incorporating spatial structure via Sobolev regularization is a reasonable inductive bias for brain data.

- Novelty/positioning concern: Method appears a straightforward extension of Mairal et al. (2010) with limited conceptual/algorithmic novelty; needs clearer new contributions and comparisons to related structured/conv. DL.

- Definition of “structure” concern: “Structure” is underdefined—specify domain, Sobolev norm order, anisotropy, boundary conditions, and discretization (finite differences vs graph Laplacian).

- Motivation concern: Must justify penalizing derivatives vs alternatives (TV, Laplacian/graph, convolutional models) and link to neurobiological priors with references.

- Objective/equations concern: Notational errors (e.g., stacking), use of ℓ2 on codes instead of ℓ1 needs justification or ablations; all symbols, normalization, and regularization weights must be defined.

- Optimization/theory concern: Provide derivation of dictionary update with Sobolev term, practical proximal computation on voxel grids, boundary handling, and whether online convergence results still hold; report per-iteration complexity and empirical scaling.

- Experiments concern: Include strong structured and non-DL baselines (graph/Laplacian, TV, convolutional DL, ICA, NMF), perform ablations (Sobolev order/strength, ℓ1 vs ℓ2), and use multiple datasets with preprocessing details.

- Evaluation metrics concern: Go beyond reconstruction error to measure interpretability/stability (smoothness, atlas overlap, test–retest, cross-subject reproducibility, downstream task performance) with statistical tests.

- Clarity/presentation concern: Paper has unclear passages, typos, inconsistent equation numbering and notation; needs careful editing.

- Minor requests: Provide pseudocode for online updates, discuss initialization and hyperparameter sensitivity, relate Sobolev penalty to Laplacian/quadratic form, cite more spatial DL/neuroimaging work, and release code for reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Idea is plausible but incremental; lacks sufficient motivation, theoretical grounding, and rigorous empirical comparisons—currently not recommended for acceptance.
