This paper introduces a novel regularizer for matrix factorization aimed at enforcing both sparsity and smoothness. While an algorithmic procedure to find a solution is provided, the paper lacks a theoretical proof for the existence of such a solution. The empirical results demonstrate superior performance against a state-of-the-art method.

The primary contribution and a key strength of this work is the formulation of this new regularizer. Furthermore, the experimental evaluation is compelling and highlights the promise of the proposed technique.

However, there are notable weaknesses. As the authors correctly state, the optimization problem is non-convex, and a significant theoretical gap is the lack of proof for the solution's existence. Consequently, the proposed algorithm is justified only by its empirical performance rather than by theoretical guarantees. Additionally, the manuscript requires revisions for clarity and language, with line 146 serving as one example.
