- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness while preserving sparsity.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) and structured dictionary learning literature to target interpretable spatial representations in neuroimaging.  
- The Laplacian-regularized approach yields more interpretable brain region decompositions that capture inter-subject variability.  
- Computationally efficient: only about 2–3× slower than standard SODL.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on HCP comparing against SODL and CanICA across explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Demonstrates clear empirical advantages over state-of-the-art methods and scalability to large datasets.  
- Provides practical insights into hyperparameter selection and trade-offs between sparsity, structure, and explanatory power.  
- Addresses an important neuroimaging problem without relying on anatomical priors or cross-modality correspondences.  
- Novelty: meaningful advancement by combining online dictionary learning with Laplacian spatial regularization.  
- Weakness: lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of solution existence and convergence beyond local optimality for the non-convex problem.  
- Writing had minor clarity issues (e.g., line 146), though the rebuttal addressed these and improved clarity.  
- Overall: technically sound, practically significant contribution with strong empirical support despite limited theoretical guarantees.
