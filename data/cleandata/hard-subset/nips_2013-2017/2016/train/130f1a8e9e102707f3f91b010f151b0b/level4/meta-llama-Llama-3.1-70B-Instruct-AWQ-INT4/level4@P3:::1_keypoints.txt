- Addresses a crucial neuro-imaging challenge by proposing a large-scale online dictionary-learning framework to find interesting brain regions.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning by incorporating a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce structured, sparse components.  
- Identifies limitations of prior methods (CanICA, SODL) that ignore spatial variability or produce dense/noisy atoms.  
- Main contribution: Smooth-SODL algorithm combining sparsity and spatial smoothness for structured dictionary atoms.  
- Provides algorithmic details including the optimization formulation, Block-Coordinate Descent scheme, and dictionary update routine.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on a large public HCP dataset with comparisons to CanICA and SODL across multiple scales.  
- Empirical results show Smooth-SODL extracts more structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of algorithm effectiveness and convergence guarantees.  
- Writing quality needs improvement—contains typos and informal comments that should be removed.  
- Paper would benefit from a deeper discussion of result significance and the broader impact on neuro-imaging applications.  
- Reviewer score 7/10 with recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add theory, fix writing, and expand discussion.
