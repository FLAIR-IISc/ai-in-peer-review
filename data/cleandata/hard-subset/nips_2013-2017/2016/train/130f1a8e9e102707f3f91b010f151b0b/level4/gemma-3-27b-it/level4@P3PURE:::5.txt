This paper addresses dictionary learning in the context of brain region analysis. Specifically, the authors propose a large-scale online dictionary learning framework incorporating a Sobolev regularization term to enforce structure on the learned dictionary. My major concerns are as follows:

1) This work appears to primarily extend existing online dictionary learning methods (e.g., Mairal, JMLR 2010) by adding a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, I find the novelty to be limited and question whether it meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS.

2) While the paper utilizes Sobolev regularization to impose structural constraints on the learned dictionary, the precise definition of this “structure” remains unclear. If the intended structure is sparsity, the justification for why dictionary sparsity itself yields significant improvements in this application is not compelling. Overall, the motivation for employing Sobolev regularization lacks sufficient clarity.

3) I believe there may be an error in Eq.(1); it should likely be transposed to [V<sup>1</sup>,V<sup>2</sup>,…,V<sup>r</sup>]<sup>T</sup>.

4) Further explanation and analysis regarding the formulation of Eq.(2) are needed. Specifically, the rationale for using an l2 norm on the codes, rather than the l1 norm employed in Mairal (JMLR 2010), should be explicitly stated and justified. 

5) Line 146 is unclear and requires attention.

6) The manuscript requires substantial refinement of its language; I identified numerous typos and grammatical errors throughout the text.
