- Proposes integrating a Sobolev prior into dictionary learning for brain imaging — interesting extension.
- Significance and improvement over existing methods are difficult to assess.
- Main results rely on visual comparisons of blob clarity/consistency, which are hard for non-experts to evaluate.
- Data quality may be as important as the algorithm; smoothing/spatial penalties naturally produce smoother blobs.
- Smoothness penalties may help with limited data but seem less relevant given the large Human Connectome Project dataset used.
- Should evaluate consolidation of differences across brain regions and use inter-subject variability as a validation mechanism.
- Current validation is subjective and does not clearly demonstrate the method's advantages.
