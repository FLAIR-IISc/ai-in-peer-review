This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for identifying brain regions, using a Sobolev regularization term to enforce structure in the learned dictionary atoms. While the application is interesting, I have several major concerns regarding the work in its current form.

1.  **Limited Novelty:** The core technical contribution appears to be the addition of a Sobolev regularization term to the online dictionary learning framework of Mairal et al. (JMLR 2010). This seems to be an incremental extension of an existing, well-established method. As such, I am concerned that the novelty of the proposed framework is insufficient for publication at NIPS.

2.  **Unclear Motivation for Sobolev Regularization:** The paper states that Sobolev regularization is used to impose a "structure" constraint on the learned dictionary, but this concept of structure is not precisely defined. The motivation for this specific choice of regularizer is not clearly articulated. For instance, if the intended structure is sparsity, the authors should explain why sparsity in the dictionary atoms themselves (as opposed to the sparse codes) is expected to yield significant improvements for this particular problem. The lack of a clear justification makes the contribution feel ad-hoc.

3.  **Potential Typo in Equation (1):** I believe there may be a mistake in Equation (1). Should the term be `[V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T`? The authors should double-check this formulation.

4.  **Lack of Justification for the Objective Function:** The construction of the objective function in Equation (2) needs more analysis and justification. Specifically, the authors impose an L2 norm on the codes. This is a notable deviation from the L1 norm typically used in sparse coding frameworks like Mairal et al. (2010) to encourage sparsity. The rationale for this design choice is not explained, which is a critical omission.

5.  **Clarity:** The statement on line 146 is unclear. Please revise this for clarity.

6.  **Writing and Presentation:** The manuscript requires significant revision to improve its readability. I found numerous typographical and grammatical errors throughout the text, which should be corrected by a thorough proofreading.
