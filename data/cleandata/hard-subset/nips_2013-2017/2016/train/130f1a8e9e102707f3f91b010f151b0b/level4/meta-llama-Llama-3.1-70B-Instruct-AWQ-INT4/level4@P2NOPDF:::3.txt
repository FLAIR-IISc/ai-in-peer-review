The authors propose an extension of dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior, which addresses an interesting problem. However, it is challenging to assess the impact or improvement of this new algorithm. The primary results figure relies on visual comparisons of the clarity and consistency of identified brain blobs, but this may not be apparent to non-experts. In my experience with similar decompositions, the quality of the data is often equally or even more crucial than the algorithm itself. By introducing penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, it is not surprising that the resulting blobs appear smoother. This approach might be more beneficial when dealing with limited data, but given the vast amount of data available through the Human Connectome Project, this does not seem to be a significant concern. I had expected this paper to further explore the differences between brain regions across subjects and potentially utilize inter-subject differences and consistency as a validation metric for the algorithm's performance. The validation presented in the paper appears subjective, and the benefits of this approach are not clearly demonstrated.
