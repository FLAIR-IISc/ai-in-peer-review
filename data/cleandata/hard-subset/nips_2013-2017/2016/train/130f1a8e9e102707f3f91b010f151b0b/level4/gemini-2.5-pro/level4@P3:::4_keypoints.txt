- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian (Sobolev-type) smoothness penalty plus l1 sparsity to produce blob-like, interpretable brain components.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning framework to include spatial smoothness, solved with a Block Coordinate Descent (BCD) algorithm.  
- Dictionary update reduces to a GraphNet-like denoising problem; added smoothness introduces only minor computational overhead versus unstructured SODL.  
- Evaluated on large-scale fMRI data from the Human Connectome Project.  
- Highly relevant and significant for scalable, data-driven ROI identification in massive neuroimaging datasets.  
- Novel integration of a spatial smoothness prior into a fast online learning framework bridging unstructured SODL and slower structured batch methods.  
- Algorithm derivation and convergence arguments are technically sound and consistent with the original BCD framework.  
- Strong empirical advantage in low-data regimes: up to 14× improvement in explained variance over SODL with few subjects.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor: rushed writing, dense exposition, incomplete equation (placeholder in Eq. 7), and improperly formatted references.  
- Experimental claims of superiority are not consistently supported across metrics and need tempering.  
- Predictive performance is weaker than a simple CanICA baseline on behavioral prediction (Figure 2c), which is not adequately discussed.  
- Explained variance, while improved over SODL, remains substantially lower than CanICA (Figure 2b); interpretability gains should be framed as a trade-off.  
- Qualitative improvement over SODL is clear, but gains versus thresholded CanICA (tCanICA) are less convincing.  
- Recommended major revisions: improve clarity, complete derivations/references, and soften claims of general superiority.  
- Strengthen experimental analysis: highlight learning-curve results, analyze why smoothing may hurt prediction (e.g., removal of predictive fine-grained features), and justify hyperparameter choices.  
- Overall verdict: interesting, technically sound contribution with clear utility in data-limited settings, but requires significant rewriting and a more balanced experimental discussion before acceptance.
