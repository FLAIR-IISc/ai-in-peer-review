- Paper introduces an online algorithm extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data.  
- Model and algorithms are technically sound and build on established framework, though initial technical rating was below standard (raised from 2 to 3 after rebuttal).  
- Empirical benefit of the Sobolev prior is not clearly demonstrated; figures and text fail to quantify how SSMF outperforms alternatives.  
- Authors should provide explicit, neuroscience-grounded criteria and quantitative metrics (e.g., boundary smoothness, connected region detection) to demonstrate improved structure and meaning of atoms.  
- Encouraged to include the informative additional results from the rebuttal in the revision.  
- Novelty: notable original contribution by adding Sobolev priors to [14] to encourage spatial structure.  
- Potential impact: useful online dictionary-learning tool for neuroimaging; applicability to image segmentation and regional sensing noted.  
- Manuscript suffers from presentation and editing problems (confusing descriptions, typographical and grammatical errors).  
- Missing or unclear details in equations: transpose in matrix decomposition, sign disagreement between Equations (6) and (8), unclear variable references in Equation (6), and request to include mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6) in appendix.  
- Missing reference for the HCP dataset (Line 73).
