- Paper proposes an online Structured Dictionary Learning method (Smooth-SODL) with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data.
- Technical approach builds on Mairal et al. (2010) and is clearly defined, but initially lacked strong empirical evidence for the Sobolev prior’s advantage.
- Reviewer requests quantitative measures of spatial smoothness and identification of known brain regions to substantiate “improved structure.”
- Rebuttal provided informative results that significantly bolster technical claims; reviewer raised rating to 3 and asks authors to integrate these results into the manuscript.
- Novelty: significant and valuable extension of prior work by incorporating Sobolev priors for spatially structured atoms.
- Potential impact: useful, scalable algorithm for neuroimaging and medical imaging communities, with possible extensions to segmentation and regional sensing.
- Presentation requires major editing for clarity, consistency, and grammar before publication.
- Specific presentation issues: misplaced transpose in matrix equation, missing HCP reference, grammatical errors, and a stray co-author comment that should be removed.
- Specific technical clarifications needed: sign discrepancy between Equations (6) and (8) and clarification of the meaning of V in Equation (6).
- Algorithmic clarity lacking: Section 3.3 and appendix do not clearly present the mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6).
- Figure problems: Figure 2 does not clearly show improved structure; slice selection hampers comparison; claim of “more structured” is not evident.
- Figure 3 issues: unclear legends, unclear target of prediction, absent PCA baseline, and confusing description of R^2 results.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound extension; efficient and scalable algorithm; rebuttal demonstrates empirical gains; positive potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: initial lack of quantitative demonstration of benefits and numerous presentation issues that need correction.
- Overall assessment: promising paper that, after revisions to incorporate rebuttal results and fix presentation/clarity issues, merits acceptance with minor revisions.
