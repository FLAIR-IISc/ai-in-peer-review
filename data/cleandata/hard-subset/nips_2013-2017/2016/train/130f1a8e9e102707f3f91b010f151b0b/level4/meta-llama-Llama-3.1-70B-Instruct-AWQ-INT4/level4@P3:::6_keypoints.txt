- Proposes Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary-learning method for extracting structured, sparse brain-image components.  
- Incorporates a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Demonstrates improved dictionary interpretability and better capture of inter-subject variability on a large public dataset.  
- Well-motivated introduction and clear problem framing.  
- Thorough related-work review highlighting limitations of existing methods.  
- Technical presentation is generally clear but some sections are dense and need more explanation or visual aids.  
- Strong algorithmic development with comprehensive description of the optimization, BCD algorithm, and dictionary updates.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough with comparisons to competing methods across settings.  
- Lacks formal proof of solution existence and convergence; algorithmic claims are supported only empirically.  
- Recommends a more rigorous convergence analysis to address theoretical limitations.  
- Writing needs polishing in places (e.g., dense text around line 146).  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 4, Clarity 3, Originality 5, Significance 4; paper has potential but requires revisions.
