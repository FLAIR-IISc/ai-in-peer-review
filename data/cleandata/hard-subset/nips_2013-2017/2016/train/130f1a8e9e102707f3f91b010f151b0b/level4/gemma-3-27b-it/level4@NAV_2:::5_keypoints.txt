- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning for brain images combining L1 sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce smooth, compact dictionary atoms.
- Extends online dictionary learning (Mairal et al., 2010) and is demonstrated on HCP data, showing improved interpretability and capture of inter-subject variability.
- Key motivation: learn ROIs directly from data with spatial priors, addressing limits of segmentation and cross-modality correspondence.
- Strength — Scalability: online framework handles large neuroimaging datasets better than batch methods.
- Strength — Integration of prior knowledge: Laplacian penalty plus L1 provides a principled way to enforce smooth, localized brain regions.
- Strength — Empirical validation: comparisons with ICA and standard online dictionary learning show improved interpretability on HCP.
- Strength — Theoretical grounding: connections to Block-Coordinate Descent and GraphNet strengthen the methodological basis.
- Strength — Practical guidance: paper discusses hyperparameter tuning and initialization for real-world use.
- Weakness — Incremental novelty: combination of L1 and Sobolev regularization feels like a modest extension rather than a major advance.
- Weakness — Ambiguous definition of “structure”: needs more precise/formal characterization and links to neuroimaging literature on anatomy or connectivity.
- Weakness — Writing issues: multiple typographical errors and awkward phrasing reduce clarity; requires careful proofreading.
- Weakness — Equation (1) contains a transpose-related typo that must be corrected.
- Weakness — Choice of L2 norm on codes (Ui) instead of L1 (as in Mairal et al.) is insufficiently justified.
- Weakness — Reference to “line 146” is ambiguous and needs clarification.
- Argument for acceptance: tackles a relevant ROI-definition problem, is scalable, empirically effective, and theoretically/practically well-motivated.
- Argument for rejection: limited novelty, weak motivation for the specific regularization combination, and subpar writing quality.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed extension with promising results but a weak accept recommendation contingent on stronger motivation, justification of design choices, and thorough proofreading.
