1. Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning with a Laplacian spatial penalty to learn structured, sparse dictionary atoms for brain imaging.  
2. Motivation: A principled method to define brain regions using inter-subject variability, bridging clustering ideas and linear mixture modeling.  
3. Novelty: Integration of spatial (Laplacian) regularization into online dictionary learning is a modest but meaningful contribution.  
4. Significance: Addresses an important neuroimaging problem and shows potential to improve interpretability of brain regions.  
5. Experiments: Evaluated on large HCP dataset; shows more interpretable maps and competitive performance versus SODL and CanICA.  
6. Clarity (positive): Algorithm description and pseudocode are generally clear; experimental setup is well-documented.  
7. Scalability: Online formulation is computationally efficient and demonstrated on 500-subject data.  
8. Technical gap: No theoretical guarantees (convergence or quality) provided for the Laplacian-augmented algorithm.  
9. Presentation issues: Manuscript contains writing errors and leftover editorial comments, indicating poor proofreading.  
10. Related work: Insufficient differentiation and comparison with other structured dictionary learning approaches (e.g., Baldassarre, Abraham).  
11. Hyperparameters: Lacks systematic guidance and sensitivity analysis for choosing regularization parameters (γ, τ).  
12. Overall quality: Technically sound and experimentally validated but undermined by missing theory and presentation problems.  
13. Recommendation summary: Reviewer recommends rejection pending theoretical analysis, careful proofreading, and stronger comparisons.
