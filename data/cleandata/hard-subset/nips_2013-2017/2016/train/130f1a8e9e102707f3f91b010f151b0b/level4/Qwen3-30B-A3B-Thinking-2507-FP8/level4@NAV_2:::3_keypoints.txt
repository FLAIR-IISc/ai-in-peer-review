- Extends online dictionary learning (Mairal et al. 2010) with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce spatial smoothness in brain image decompositions.  
- Maintains online scalability of SODL with only a modest runtime increase (~2–3× slower).  
- Demonstrated on HCP data, showing structured dictionaries that appear spatially coherent and capture inter-subject variability.  
- Thorough experimental validation across multiple metrics: visual atom inspection, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Technical approach is sound with appropriate theoretical grounding and convergence guarantees.  
- Scalability is well-motivated and empirically demonstrated for large datasets.  
- Comparisons against relevant baselines (SODL, CanICA) are comprehensive.  
- Hyper-parameter analysis is useful and implementation in Nilearn enhances reproducibility.  
- Primary weakness: interpretability claims rely on visual inspection and lack objective validation that patterns are biologically meaningful.  
- Validation metrics used do not convincingly show that structured patterns map to known functional brain organization.  
- Paper should more explicitly relate to prior structured dictionary learning work (e.g., Varoquaux et al., 2012).  
- Significance is limited by absence of concrete evidence that smoother components yield new neuroscientific insights beyond visual coherence and modest predictive gains.  
- Key suggested improvements: add objective validation (compare to neuroanatomical atlases, align components with cognitive processes, statistically validate inter-subject variability).  
- Overall: technically solid and novel integration of Sobolev regularization, but needs stronger biological validation to boost scientific impact.
