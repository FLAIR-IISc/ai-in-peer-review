- Proposal extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior but the algorithmic impact and tangible improvements over existing methods are unclear.  
- Main results rely on visual comparisons of blob clarity and consistency, which are not convincing to untrained observers.  
- Data quality in this domain is as important as the algorithm, complicating claims of algorithmic advantage.  
- Imposing smoothness and spatial-consistency penalties predictably yields smoother blobs; this outcome is unsurprising and may be trivial.  
- The method could help isolate regions with sparse data, but abundant high-quality datasets (e.g., Human Connectome Project) make this less compelling.  
- The paper fails to address inter-subject variability; consolidating differences across subjects and using inter-subject consistency as validation would strengthen the work.  
- Validation appears subjective and does not demonstrate clear practical benefits over simpler alternatives.  
- There is a lack of rigorous validation against established benchmarks or biological ground truth, which undermines the claimed contribution.
