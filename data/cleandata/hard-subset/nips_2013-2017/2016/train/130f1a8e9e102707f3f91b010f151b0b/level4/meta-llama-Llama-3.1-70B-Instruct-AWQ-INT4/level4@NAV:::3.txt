The authors' extension of dictionary learning models to include a Sobolev prior is an intriguing concept. However, assessing the significance and improvement of this novel algorithm is challenging. The primary results figure, which relies on visual comparisons of the clarity and consistency of identified brain blobs, may not be readily interpretable to non-experts. In my experience with similar decompositions, data quality often plays a crucial role, potentially overshadowing the algorithm's impact. The incorporation of smoothness and spatial consistency penalties is expected to yield smoother blobs, which may be beneficial for isolating regions with limited data. Nevertheless, given the vast data availability through the Human Connectome Project, this advantage seems less significant. I had anticipated that this paper would further investigate the consolidation of brain region differences across subjects, utilizing inter-subject differences and consistency as a validation metric. Unfortunately, the presented validation appears subjective, and the benefits of this approach remain unclear.
