This paper proposes a new regularization term for matrix factorization that promotes both sparsity and smoothness. The authors present an algorithm to obtain a solution, but do not establish the existence of a solution or provide theoretical guarantees. Empirically, the method outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on the reported benchmarks, indicating strong practical potential.

Strengths:
- Introduction of a novel regularizer that jointly encourages sparsity and smoothness is interesting and relevant.
- The experimental results are promising and suggest the approach can outperform existing methods.

Weaknesses:
- The optimization problem is non-convex, yet there is no proof of existence of a minimizer or analysis of stationarity and convergence properties.
- The algorithm is supported primarily by empirical evidence, with limited theoretical justification.
- The writing requires improvement for clarity and precision (e.g., issues around line 146). More careful editing would enhance readability and understanding of the technical contributions.

Suggestions:
- Provide theoretical results on existence (e.g., conditions ensuring a minimizer exists) and clarify what the algorithm is guaranteed to find (e.g., a stationary point).
- Add a convergence analysis or at least boundedness/monotonicity guarantees under reasonable assumptions.
- Improve the exposition, especially in the problem setup and algorithmic description, and fix local writing issues.

After rebuttal:
I am satisfied with the authors’ clarification regarding the algorithm’s convergence. I am willing to revise my Clarity and Presentation rating from 2 to 3.
