- Introduces Smooth-SODL: adds a Laplacian spatial regularizer to online dictionary learning to produce sparse, spatially contiguous brain maps; scalable and shown on HCP to outperform SODL and CanICA in interpretability and stability.

- Addresses an important, well-motivated problem of finding meaningful population-level functional regions with a scalable, structured decomposition.

- Novel technical contribution: integrates Laplacian spatial smoothness into Mairal et al.’s online dictionary learning with sound BCD update derivation.

- Strong experimental validation on a large modern dataset with relevant baselines, multiple quantitative and qualitative metrics, and evidence of improved performance in low-data regimes.

- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with effective motivation, model presentation, and systematic results.

- Concern about theoretical clarity: need to more explicitly state convergence properties for the non-convex joint learning problem (convergence to a stationary point).

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: model performance depends on α, γ, τ; more concrete guidance or robust parameter-selection strategies would increase practical utility.

- Minor presentation issues: requires final proofreading to fix typos and inconsistent capitalization.

- Post-rebuttal: authors clarified convergence guarantees (inheritance from Mairal et al.), resolving the main theoretical ambiguity and increasing reviewer confidence.
