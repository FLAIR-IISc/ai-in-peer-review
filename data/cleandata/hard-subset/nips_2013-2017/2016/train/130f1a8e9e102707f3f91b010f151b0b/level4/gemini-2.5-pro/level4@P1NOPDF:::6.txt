This paper introduces a new regularizer for matrix factorization designed to impose both sparsity and smoothness on the solution. The main contribution is the formulation of this regularizer within the optimization problem. While the authors acknowledge that the resulting problem is non-convex and do not provide a proof for the existence of a solution, they present an algorithm to find one. The experimental results are promising, demonstrating that the proposed method can be superior to state-of-the-art approaches.

The key strength of the paper is the introduction of this novel regularizer. The empirical validation shows the potential of this direction.

However, the work has several weaknesses. The primary concern is the lack of theoretical grounding. As the authors state, the problem is non-convex, and the existence of a solution is not formally established. Consequently, the proposed algorithm is supported solely by its empirical performance, without theoretical guarantees. Furthermore, the manuscript itself would benefit from significant revision to improve its clarity and writing quality (for instance, line 146).
