- Introduces large-scale online dictionary learning combining L1 sparsity and a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially smooth, compact dictionary atoms (“blobs”) for brain images.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) with modest extra computational cost and demonstrates the method on a large HCP fMRI dataset.  
- Addresses the important problem of data-driven ROI definition by leveraging population variability to improve statistical power.  
- Motivation for structured sparsity is sound because brain regions are expected to be spatially localized.  
- Novel contribution: combining online dictionary learning with an explicit Laplacian spatial regularizer.  
- Scalability: online learning framework allows application to large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Empirical results: improved interpretability and better capture of inter-subject variability compared to some alternatives.  
- Practical considerations: paper includes hyperparameter tuning and implementation details.  
- Lacks a thorough theoretical analysis, particularly on the impact and justification of the Laplacian penalty.  
- Clarity and presentation problems (internal comments left, dense notation) indicate the manuscript needs careful editing.  
- Comparison to related work is limited; evaluation should include more structured-sparsity baselines in neuroimaging.  
- Some empirical claims (e.g., “14-fold gain” in explained variance) appear overstated and need more justification and context.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, scalable approach with promising empirical performance on a public large dataset.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak theoretical grounding, presentation issues, and incomplete comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but requires substantial revisions; reviewer recommends weak reject pending fixes.  
- Recommended revisions: add rigorous theoretical analysis of the Laplacian term; clean up presentation and notation; expand related-work comparisons; temper and better justify empirical claims.
