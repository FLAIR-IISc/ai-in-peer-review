This paper proposes an under-complete dictionary learning framework with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization to extract functional components from fMRI data. The approach is interesting, and the main contribution—using a dictionary learning formulation with a Sobolev/L1 penalty to jointly promote smoothness and sparsity—is at least somewhat original in this context.

However, the empirical gains over simpler alternatives appear limited, or at least are not convincingly demonstrated. The manuscript does not clearly explain where and why the proposed method improves upon baseline methods, nor does it adequately justify the added complexity relative to the observed benefits. A clearer empirical narrative is needed to quantify improvements, highlight when they matter, and make the case that the combined regularization is essential.

The paper also appears rushed in its current form. There are still draft comments present (e.g., line 146), and the writing is often tedious and at times confusing, which impedes comprehension of both the method and results.

Overall, the idea has merit, but the paper needs:
- Clearer and more compelling evidence of improvement over simpler methods, with explicit comparisons and explanations.
- Substantially improved exposition to make the method and results easier to follow.
- Thorough proofreading to remove draft artifacts and polish the presentation.
