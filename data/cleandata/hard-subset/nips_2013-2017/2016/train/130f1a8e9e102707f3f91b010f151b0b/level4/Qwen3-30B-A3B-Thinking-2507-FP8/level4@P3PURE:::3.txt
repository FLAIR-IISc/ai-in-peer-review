

The authors extend dictionary learning models to incorporate a Sobolev prior, presenting an interesting problem. However, it is difficult to assess the algorithm's impact or relative improvement due to the reliance on a single visual comparison of brain blob clarity and consistency in the main results figure. This comparison is subjective and difficult to assess objectively without domain-specific expertise. Crucially, for this type of decomposition, data quality and quantity are equally critical as the algorithm itself. The observed smoothness in the results—achieved through penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency—is unsurprising and does not constitute a significant advancement. While such an approach might be beneficial for isolating regions with sparse data, the availability of large-scale datasets from initiatives like the Human Connectome Project renders this a less pressing concern. The paper would have been strengthened by addressing inter-subject variability, such as consolidating brain region differences across subjects or leveraging inter-subject consistency as a validation metric. The current validation approach remains subjective, and the specific benefits of the proposed method over existing approaches are not clearly demonstrated.
