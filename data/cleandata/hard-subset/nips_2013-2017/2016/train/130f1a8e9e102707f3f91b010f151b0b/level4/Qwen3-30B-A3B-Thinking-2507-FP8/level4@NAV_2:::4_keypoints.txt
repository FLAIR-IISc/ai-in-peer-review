- Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness for extracting interpretable fMRI brain regions.
- Strength: Incorporates spatial structure into online dictionary learning with modest computational overhead (~3× slower than SODL).
- Strength: Method is well-motivated for brain imaging, where spatial smoothness of functional regions matters.
- Strength: Experiments suggest the method yields structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability across scales.
- Weakness: Improvement over simpler baselines (SODL, CanICA) is not convincingly demonstrated; provided evidence (e.g., Fig. 2) is unclear.
- Weakness: Manuscript has clarity issues and leftover draft comments, making the algorithm and results hard to follow.
- Weakness: The connection and differences relative to prior structured dictionary learning (e.g., Varoquaux et al., 2014) are not clearly stated.
- Weakness: Significance is not fully justified; no strong evidence that captured variability improves downstream tasks.
- Quality: Technical approach appears sound, but experimental validation and comparisons are insufficient to support claims.
- Clarity: Paper is insufficiently polished and contains unclear explanations in several places.
- Originality: Combining online dictionary learning with spatial regularization is somewhat novel but the novelty vs. prior work is unclear.
- Significance: The problem is important, but the paper fails to convincingly show substantial advantages over existing approaches.
- Pro/Con summary: Pro — adds spatial structure to online dictionary learning with low overhead; Con — claims not well supported, clarity and significance concerns.
