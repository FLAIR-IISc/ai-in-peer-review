

The paper proposes under-complete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev and L1 regularization to identify functional components in fMRI data. The approach presents an interesting methodological twist by leveraging Sobolev and L1 regularization to simultaneously promote spatial smoothness and sparsity in the extracted components. However, the empirical results fail to convincingly demonstrate that this novel framework outperforms simpler baselines. The claimed improvements over existing methods appear marginal, and the paper does not adequately justify why the added complexity of the combined regularization yields meaningful gains. For instance, quantitative comparisons with standard sparse dictionary learning or PCA are either absent or superficial, leaving the practical advantage of the proposed technique unclear.  

Additionally, the manuscript exhibits signs of being rushed and lacks polish. Specific issues include draft comments still present in the text (e.g., Line 146), inconsistent notation, and sections that are difficult to follow due to unclear exposition. The presentation of the theoretical motivation for the regularization terms is particularly opaque, making it challenging to assess the validity of the core assumptions. While the technical idea itself is plausible, the current write-up undermines the contribution by failing to clearly articulate *why* the proposed method is necessary or superior. The authors should strengthen the evaluation by providing more rigorous comparisons, explicit ablation studies, and a clearer explanation of the added value of the Sobolev constraint beyond standard L1 regularization. Without these revisions, the paper’s impact remains limited, and its novelty is difficult to justify relative to existing work in the field.
