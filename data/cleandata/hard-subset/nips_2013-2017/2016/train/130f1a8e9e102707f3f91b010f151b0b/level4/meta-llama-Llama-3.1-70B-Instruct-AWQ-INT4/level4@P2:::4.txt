The paper proposes an under-complete dictionary learning method with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization to extract functional components from fMRI data. The approach appears interesting, as it leverages the strengths of both regularization techniques to encourage smoothness and sparsity in the components. However, upon closer examination, the results of the new approach do not seem to offer significant improvements over simpler methods. Furthermore, the manuscript could benefit from additional explanations to clarify the extent of these improvements.

The authors' decision to use dictionary learning with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization is somewhat original and warrants further discussion. The Sobolev regularization, in particular, is an interesting choice, as it promotes piece-wise smoothness in the components. However, the manuscript could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the motivations behind this choice and how it compares to other regularization techniques.

The experimental results presented in the paper are extensive, but the comparisons to other methods are not entirely convincing. The authors claim that their approach outperforms other methods in certain aspects, but the differences in performance are not always clear. For instance, the explained variance plots in Figure 2(b) show that the proposed method performs similarly to other methods on the test set. Additionally, the prediction results in Figure 2(c) indicate that the proposed method is outperformed by other methods in predicting certain behavioral variables.

The manuscript could also benefit from a more polished presentation. There are still draft comments and incomplete sentences, which detract from the overall readability of the paper. Furthermore, some sections are quite tedious and confusing to read, which may be due to the rushed nature of the manuscript.

In conclusion, while the proposed approach is interesting, the manuscript requires additional work to convince the reader of its merits. The authors should provide more detailed explanations of their approach, clarify the improvements over simpler methods, and polish the presentation to make it more readable.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The proposed approach is interesting and warrants further exploration.
* The use of combined Sobolev/L1 regularization is somewhat original and may offer advantages over other regularization techniques.
* The experimental results are extensive and provide a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed method.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The manuscript requires significant polishing to improve readability.
* The results of the proposed approach do not seem to offer significant improvements over simpler methods.
* The comparisons to other methods are not entirely convincing, and the authors should provide more detailed explanations of their approach.
