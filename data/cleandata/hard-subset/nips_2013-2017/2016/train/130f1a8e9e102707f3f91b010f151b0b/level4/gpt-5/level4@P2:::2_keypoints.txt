- Proposes an online multivariate dictionary-learning method (Smooth-SODL) combining ℓ1 sparsity and a Sobolev/Laplacian prior to produce piecewise-smooth, compact atoms for neuroimaging data.  
- Builds on Mairal et al.'s online sparse coding (SODL) and adds spatial smoothness to encourage blob-like components that are more neurobiologically plausible than dense PCA/ICA maps.  
- Algorithmic contribution: retains online scalability with block-coordinate updates and efficient proximal steps for Laplacian-regularized atom updates.  
- Experiments: compares Smooth-SODL to SODL and CanICA on HCP task fMRI maps and studies hyperparameter effects.  
- Strength: modeling framework and optimization scheme are clearly defined and grounded in established methods (block-coordinate, ridge coding, Laplacian updates).  
- Main empirical weakness: the paper initially lacked quantitative evidence that the Laplacian prior improves spatial structure versus SODL/CanICA.  
- Reviewer requests inclusion of quantitative spatial-structure metrics (e.g., Laplacian energy, connected components, boundary smoothness/compactness, Dice with atlases, reproducibility across splits).  
- Rebuttal: additional results partially addressed the gap; reviewer asks these be integrated into the revision with further quantitative measures and citations.  
- Originality: notable methodological advance in integrating a Sobolev/Laplacian prior into online dictionary learning while preserving scalability.  
- Significance: scalable approach could produce more interpretable, reproducible, and predictive components useful for atlas construction, dimensionality reduction, and downstream tasks.  
- Clarity/presentation issues: typos, unclear statements, and notational glitches that impede understanding and reproducibility.  
- Specific corrections requested: transpose placement in matrix decomposition (Lines 67–68), add missing HCP reference (Line 73), fix grammar (Line 83), remove internal comment (Line 146), fix duplicate phrase (Line 157) and typo (Line 169).  
- Mathematical consistency: Equations (6) and (8) have mismatched signs/factors; clarify whether V in updates is the previous iterate and ensure consistent Bt and Aj notation.  
- Algorithmic detail request: provide the explicit atom-update algorithm (e.g., FISTA steps), Lipschitz constant, projection onto C, and stopping criteria in an appendix.  
- Figure/experiment improvements: Figure 2 should use identical slices across methods and include quantitative structure metrics; Figure 3 must clarify predicted targets, features (U_i vs raw maps), training/evaluation protocol, legend definitions, and include PCA baseline.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled, scalable extension with potential for more interpretable/reproducible components and relevant applications in population neuroimaging.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient quantitative evidence for Laplacian benefit in main paper, notational/editorial issues, and non-standardized baseline comparisons/figures.  
- Overall recommendation: promising and timely contribution contingent on integrating rebuttal results, adding quantitative spatial-structure/reproducibility evaluations, and tightening presentation and figures.
