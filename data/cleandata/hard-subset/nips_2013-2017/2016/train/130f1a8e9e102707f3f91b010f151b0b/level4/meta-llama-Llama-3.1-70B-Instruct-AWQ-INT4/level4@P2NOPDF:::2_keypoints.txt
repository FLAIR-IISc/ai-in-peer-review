- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary-learning algorithm for neuroimaging, extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms.  
- Model definition and algorithmic framework are clear and based on a well-established method.  
- Presentation issues undermine perceived technical quality despite a solid algorithmic foundation.  
- Empirical results do not clearly demonstrate the advantage of the Sobolev prior; Figure 2 fails to show in what aspect SSMF outperforms alternatives.  
- Authors should provide neuroscience-justified quantitative criteria (e.g., boundary smoothness, recovery of known connected regions) and metrics to demonstrate improvements.  
- Rebuttal included additional informative results that improved the reviewer’s technical-quality rating; these should be incorporated into the revision.  
- Novelty: meaningful original contribution by incorporating Sobolev priors into the framework of [14].  
- Potential impact: an efficient online algorithm for spatially-regularized dictionary learning could be valuable for neuroscience, medical imaging, and related tasks (e.g., segmentation, regional sensing).  
- Figure 2 comparisons are hard because methods are shown on different slices; direct, controlled comparisons are needed.  
- Figure 3 is unclear: prediction target, legends, and PCA baseline are not explained; the claim that R^2 > 0.1 is not substantiated in the text or caption.
