- Addresses an important neuroimaging problem by combining sparsity and spatial smoothness to discover interpretable brain ROIs from population data.  
- Proposes a scalable online algorithm (Smooth-SODL) that extends Mairal et al. (2010) with a Laplacian penalty, claimed to incur only a modest (2–3×) extra cost.  
- Empirically validated on large HCP data against SODL and CanICA using qualitative and quantitative metrics (visuals, explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction), showing improved interpretability and performance, especially with limited data.  
- Lacks formal theoretical analysis: no discussion of effects of the Laplacian penalty on convergence, sample complexity, or dictionary recovery guarantees.  
- Poor presentation and proofreading (numerous grammatical issues, incomplete/inconsistent bibliography) and contains an obvious leftover internal comment, undermining confidence in the submission.  
- Overall: promising, well-motivated and relevant work but not ready for publication in current form; needs careful revision and ideally added theoretical justification.
