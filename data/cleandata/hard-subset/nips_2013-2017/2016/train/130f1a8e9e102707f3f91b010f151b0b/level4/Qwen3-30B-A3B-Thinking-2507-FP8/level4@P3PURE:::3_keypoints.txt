- Paper extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior — an interesting extension.  
- Impact and relative improvement are hard to assess because results rely on a single visual comparison of brain blob clarity.  
- The visual comparison is subjective and difficult to evaluate without domain-specific expertise.  
- Data quality and quantity are as important as the algorithm; observed smoothness arises from imposed smoothness/spatial-consistency penalties.  
- Achieved smoothness is unsurprising and does not represent a major methodological advance.  
- Method could help where data is sparse, but large datasets (e.g., Human Connectome Project) reduce the need for this approach.  
- The paper fails to address inter-subject variability or consolidate brain-region differences across subjects.  
- Validation is subjective and does not clearly demonstrate specific benefits over existing approaches.
