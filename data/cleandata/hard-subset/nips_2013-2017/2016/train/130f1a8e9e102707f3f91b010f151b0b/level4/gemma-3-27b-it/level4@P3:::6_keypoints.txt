- Introduces Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary learning method for decomposing brain images into sparse and spatially structured components.
- Core idea: combine L1 sparsity penalty with a Laplacian smoothness penalty to produce localized, smooth dictionary atoms.
- Builds on existing online dictionary learning (Mairal et al., 2010) to handle large neuroimaging datasets and inter-subject variability.
- Positions the method as addressing a gap in prior work that often ignores explicit spatial smoothness in learned components.
- Claimed computational advantage as a more efficient alternative to slow alternating-optimization approaches for large-scale data.
- Strength: principled incorporation of spatial regularization (Laplacian) into sparse dictionary learning.
- Strength: experimental results on the HCP dataset show more interpretable and robust representations than sparse DL and ICA baselines.
- Strength: online framework scales well to large datasets, demonstrated with runtime analyses.
- Strength: extensive experimental validation, including hyperparameter sensitivity analyses.
- Strength: prediction experiments linking learned representations to behavioral variables show practical utility.
- Weakness: no theoretical guarantees or analysis of convergence to global optima for the non-convex objective.
- Weakness: reliance primarily on empirical validation; theoretical insights would strengthen the contribution.
- Weakness: explanation of algorithmic convergence (Section 3.3) is dense and over-reliant on citations; needs a clearer, intuitive exposition.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison of memory usage relative to other methods despite focus on scalability.
- Weakness: minor grammatical issues and stylistic inconsistencies requiring copyediting.
- Weakness: related-work discussion is limited; should better cover existing spatial-regularization methods in dictionary learning.
- Recommendation: overall positive—novel, practically significant, empirically strong; reviewer recommends acceptance.
- Confidence: reviewer rated confidence 8/10 and after rebuttal increased clarity score from 3 to 4.
