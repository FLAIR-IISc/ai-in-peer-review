This paper introduces a method for identifying functional components in fMRI data using under-complete dictionary learning. The primary contribution is the application of a combined Sobolev and L1 regularization scheme to the dictionary atoms, which is designed to promote both spatial smoothness and sparsity in the learned components.

The approach is well-motivated, as enforcing spatial coherence (smoothness) and sparsity are desirable properties when interpreting brain networks. The integration of a Sobolev norm into the dictionary learning framework to explicitly model spatial structure is a novel and interesting idea.

However, the paper has two significant weaknesses in its current form.

First, the empirical evaluation does not convincingly demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method. The performance gains over simpler baselines appear to be marginal. The paper lacks a detailed analysis or discussion to justify the added complexity of the combined regularization, as the reported improvements are not substantial. To strengthen the claims, the authors should provide a more thorough analysis explaining the conditions under which their method offers a clear advantage.

Second, the manuscript's presentation requires substantial revision. The paper appears to have been prepared in haste, as evidenced by the presence of draft comments in the text (e.g., line 146). More critically, the writing is often difficult to follow, making the paper a tedious and at times confusing read. The exposition of the model, the algorithm, and the experimental setup needs to be significantly clarified to improve readability and allow the reader to fully grasp the technical contributions of the work.

In summary, while the core idea is promising, the weak empirical justification and poor presentation quality detract significantly from the paper's impact. I would encourage the authors to refine their experimental analysis and thoroughly revise the manuscript for clarity and professionalism.
