- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to yield spatially smooth, contiguous dictionary atoms.  
- Demonstrated on HCP fMRI and compared to SODL and CanICA on explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  

Strengths:
- Novel and significant: integrates spatial smoothness into a scalable online dictionary-learning framework.  
- Scalable: preserves online efficiency with only modest runtime overhead.  
- Potential impact: useful for ROI definition, dimensionality reduction, biomarker discovery; planned Nilearn release aids adoption.  

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient empirical justification: visual examples are inconclusive; lacks quantitative metrics for smoothness, compactness, or contiguity.  
- Presentation and clarity are poor: numerous typos, inconsistencies, and unclear figures impede understanding.  

Detailed issues to address:
- Add quantitative measures of atom structure (smoothness/compactness/contiguity) and neuroanatomical correspondence.  
- Fix Figure 2a: display corresponding atoms on the same canonical slices for direct comparison.  
- Fix Figure 2c: add legend, clarify PCA baseline, label curves, and state which features (e.g., subject codes Ui) were used for prediction.  
- Clarify notation and matrix dimensions (confirm X≈VU vs common UV^T convention and state sizes).  
- Resolve sign/inconsistency in Equations (7) and (8); state that V in updates is the previous dictionary.  
- Provide explicit FISTA gradient and algorithmic steps for reproducibility.  
- Thorough proofreading: correct typos, inconsistent factors (e.g., 1/2 in ridge penalty), and reference issues.  

Post-rebuttal note:
- Rebuttal added learning curves showing spatial regularization benefits in low-data regimes (Table 1); include these results in the revised manuscript.  

Final assessment:
- Promising and well-motivated method but requires stronger quantitative validation and careful editorial revision; acceptance contingent on addressing these points.
