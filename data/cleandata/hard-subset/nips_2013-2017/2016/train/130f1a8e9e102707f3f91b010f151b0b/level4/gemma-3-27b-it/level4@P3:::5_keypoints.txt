- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning combining L1 sparsity and a Sobolev structural penalty to identify spatially localized brain regions.  
- Emphasizes scalability via an online framework suitable for large datasets like the HCP.  
- Claims the L1+Sobolev combination yields more spatially coherent, biologically plausible “blob”-like atoms.  
- Empirical validation shows improved explained variance and behavioral prediction versus SODL [16] and CanICA [22] on HCP.  
- Provides a clear algorithmic presentation with BCD optimization and discussion of Laplacian penalty computational issues.  
- Includes a helpful ablation study over regularization parameters.  
- Novelty is limited; the extension (L1 + Sobolev) is incremental and not strongly justified as a non-trivial advance.  
- Motivation for the Sobolev penalty and the definition of “structure” are weak—needs concrete explanation and prior evidence linking smoothness to brain regions.  
- Choice of L2 norm on codes instead of L1 requires further justification.  
- Several technical/clarity issues: equation (1) typo, unclear line 146, and numerous minor typos/grammar problems harming readability.  
- Related work section is incomplete; lacks deeper comparison to graph-based methods, other structured sparsity techniques, and recent spatial-prior papers.  
- Empirical gains are promising but insufficient to offset limited novelty and weak motivation.  
- Overall assessment: reasonable extension but needs stronger motivation, technical clarification, and polish.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; revise motivation for Sobolev prior, fix technical issues, and improve writing for reconsideration.
