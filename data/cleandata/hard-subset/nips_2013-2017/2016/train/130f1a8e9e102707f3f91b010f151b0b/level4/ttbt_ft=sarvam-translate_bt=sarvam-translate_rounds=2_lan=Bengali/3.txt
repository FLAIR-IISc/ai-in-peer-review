The authors have expanded the dictionary learning models to include a Sobolev prior. This is an interesting problem, but it is difficult to judge the impact or improvement of this newly proposed algorithm. The main result relies on visually comparing the clarity/consistency of the identified brain blobs, but this is not obvious to an untrained eye. Also, in my experience with this type of analysis, the algorithm (if not more than that) is as important as the data itself. By including penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, it is not surprising that you will get a smooth blob. Perhaps it is more useful for distinguishing an area with little data, but it does not seem to be a problem because a large amount of data is accessible with the Human Connectome project. I hoped that this paper would do more to further consolidate the differences in brain regions of different people and perhaps use inter-subject differences/consistencies as a valid measure of performance. The validity presented here seems very subjective and its advantage is not clear.
