- Proposal: under-complete online dictionary learning with combined Sobolev (Laplacian) and L1 regularization to identify functional components in fMRI data.  
- Novelty: key contribution is the combined Sobolev/L1 regularization encouraging both smoothness and sparsity.  
- Scalability: online algorithm enables handling large-scale neuroimaging datasets.  
- Modeling: sparsity enforced via L1 norm; spatial coherence enforced via Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce piecewise-smooth “blobs.”  
- Extension: method builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning with only a modest runtime increase (2–3×).  
- Empirical claim: preliminary experiments show more interpretable and structured dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.  
- Comparisons: authors compare against relevant baselines (CanICA, standard SODL) and position work relative to ICA/PCA and TV-L1 approaches.  
- Strength — Combined regularization: sensible integration of sparsity and smoothness for fMRI components.  
- Strength — Clear motivation: paper articulates the need to address inter-subject variability rather than treating it as residual noise.  
- Weakness — Modest improvements: reported performance gains over simpler methods (e.g., SODL) are small or marginal in some metrics.  
- Weakness — Clarity and polish: manuscript is dense, contains draft comments, and can be tedious or confusing to read.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: parameter tuning discussion is incomplete and recommended settings appear ad hoc.  
- Weakness — Laplacian justification: connection between Laplacian penalty and desired blob-like structure lacks intuitive/visual explanation.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel technique addressing a relevant neuroimaging problem with scalability and comparative evaluation.  
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient empirical gains, need for substantial manuscript revision, and inadequate hyperparameter analysis.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and promising work but requires substantial revision; reviewer recommends rejection in current form with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
