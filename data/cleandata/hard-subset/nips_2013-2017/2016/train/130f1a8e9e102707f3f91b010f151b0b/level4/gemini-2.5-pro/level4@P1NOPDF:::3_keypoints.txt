- Adds a Sobolev prior to dictionary learning; approach is interesting but its practical impact is unclear.
- Primary empirical validation is based on subjective visual comparisons of spatial brain maps.
- Claimed improvements are not readily apparent to non-expert readers.
- Data quality often matters more than algorithmic details for this decomposition.
- Smoothness of components is an expected consequence of the chosen prior, not necessarily a novel benefit.
- Motivation to isolate regions with sparse data is weak given use of the large Human Connectome Project dataset.
- Paper would be stronger if applied to a more challenging task, e.g., identifying consistent brain regions across subjects.
- Recommends using inter-subject consistency as an objective, quantitative validation metric.
- Overall validation is largely subjective, making it difficult to assess practical benefits.
