- Proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for brain images with structured and sparse components.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by incorporating a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to impose spatial structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a large-scale brain-imaging dataset, extracting structured, denoised dictionaries that capture inter-subject variability.  
- Positions work within prior literature on dictionary learning, sparse coding, and brain imaging, citing relevant related work.  
- Identifies limitations of PCA/ICA and standard online dictionary learning (need for pre-smoothing, lack of spatial structure).  
- Strength: clear presentation and detailed algorithmic description, including optimization procedure and hyperparameter choices.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation with comparisons to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Experimental results show improved extraction of structured, denoised components and better modeling of inter-subject variability.  
- Weakness: novelty is questionable—adding Sobolev regularization may be a relatively minor contribution for NIPS standards.  
- Weakness: the notion of "structure" is not clearly defined, and the necessity/effectiveness of the Sobolev term is not convincingly justified.  
- Weakness: minor errors and typos (incorrect equation (1), unclear line 146) and overall language/readability issues.  
- Suggestion: authors should better motivate and clarify their contribution, define "structure," justify the regularizer, and fix presentation errors.
