This paper presents an online algorithm for learning a structured dictionary to characterize neuroimaging data across subjects, extending the established framework in [14] by adding a Sobolev prior to promote spatial contiguity of dictionary atoms. The authors apply the method to empirical datasets, examine the influence of tuning parameters, and compare performance with two alternative approaches.

Technical quality: I would rate marginally below 3 if that option were available. The model specification is clear. The algorithms follow a well-established framework [14] and appear solid. However, perhaps due to presentation, the results do not clearly demonstrate the empirical benefit of introducing the Sobolev prior. In particular, in Figure 2 it is unclear how SSMF improves over the two baselines. Are the spatial boundaries quantitatively smoother? Are there known connected regions recovered only by SSMF? Beyond the vague claim that SSMF results are “more structured,” the authors should define criteria grounded in the neuroscience literature and report quantitative improvements according to those criteria. Added after reading the rebuttal: The additional results provided in the rebuttal are very informative. I changed my technical quality rating from 2 to 3. I encourage the authors to include these new results in their revision and, in addition, to demonstrate quantitatively that SSMF atoms are more structured and more meaningful, with references to the neuroscience literature.

Novelty/originality: The paper’s notable original contribution is the extension of [14] with Sobolev priors to promote spatially structured dictionary atoms.

Potential impact or usefulness: Dictionary learning is a useful approach for modeling neuroimaging data across large cohorts, and incorporating Sobolev or similar spatial priors should theoretically improve results. This paper offers an efficient online algorithm to that end and could provide a valuable tool for the neuroscience/medical imaging community. The authors might also consider additional applications such as image segmentation and regional sensing.
