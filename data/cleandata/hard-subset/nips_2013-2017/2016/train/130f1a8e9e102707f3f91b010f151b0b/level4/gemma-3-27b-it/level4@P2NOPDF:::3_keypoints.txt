- Proposes extending dictionary learning by incorporating a Sobolev prior for spatial smoothness.
- Significance and magnitude of the algorithmic improvement are unclear from the presented results.
- Primary evaluation is visual comparison of brain-region clarity, lacking objective, quantitative measures.
- Results may be hard to interpret for reviewers without neuroimaging expertise due to subjective visuals.
- Data quality and preprocessing often matter as much or more than the algorithm in sparse decomposition tasks.
- Smoother results are expected given penalties enforcing smoothness and spatial consistency.
- Method’s utility appears limited to sparse-data scenarios, raising concerns about applicability with large datasets (e.g., Human Connectome Project).
- Paper should address inter-subject variability and consolidation of region differences across subjects.
- Current validation is largely subjective; a rigorous, quantitative evaluation is needed to demonstrate practical benefits over existing techniques.
