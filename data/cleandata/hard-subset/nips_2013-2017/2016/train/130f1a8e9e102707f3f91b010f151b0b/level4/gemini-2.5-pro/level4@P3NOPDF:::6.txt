This paper proposes a novel regularization technique for matrix factorization, designed to simultaneously enforce both sparsity and smoothness on the resulting factors. The authors present an algorithm to solve the corresponding optimization problem and provide experimental evidence suggesting that their method is superior to existing state-of-the-art approaches.

**Strengths:**

The primary strength of this work is the introduction of a new, well-motivated regularizer. The goal of combining sparsity and smoothness is compelling and has clear applications. The paper's contribution here is significant.

Furthermore, the empirical results are another key strength. The experiments are designed to convincingly demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior performance compared to established techniques. This highlights the practical potential and immediate value of the approach.

**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement:**

My primary concern lies with the theoretical underpinnings of the proposed method. As the authors correctly acknowledge, the resulting optimization problem is non-convex. A significant theoretical gap is the lack of a proof for the existence of a solution to this problem. Consequently, the proposed algorithm's justification rests solely on its empirical performance, without formal guarantees. While the experimental results are promising, the absence of this theoretical analysis weakens the paper's overall contribution from a foundational perspective.

Additionally, the clarity of the presentation could be improved. There are several instances of awkward phrasing and grammatical errors throughout the manuscript (e.g., around line 146), which occasionally hinder the reader's understanding and detract from the quality of the paper.

**Post-Rebuttal Assessment:**

I thank the authors for their detailed rebuttal. They have satisfactorily addressed my concerns regarding the convergence of the proposed algorithm. This clarification has alleviated one of my main concerns about the algorithm's behavior. In light of this, I have raised my score for Clarity and Presentation from 2 to 3. While the fundamental issues related to the non-convex nature of the problem and the lack of a proof for the existence of a solution remain, the clarification on the algorithm is a welcome improvement.
