- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce sparse, spatially smooth brain-region atoms.
- Scalable: builds on online framework to handle very large neuroimaging datasets (e.g., HCP).
- Addresses a significant problem: learning interpretable ROIs to improve downstream neuroimaging analyses.
- Empirical gains: produces more structured, reproducible components and improved performance in low-data regimes versus sparser ODL and ICA.
- Computationally practical: adds only a modest (2–3×) overhead relative to the optimized baseline.
- Limited technical novelty: main contribution is adding a Laplacian penalty to an existing online dictionary-learning framework.
- Regularizer choice unclear: needs stronger justification for using the Sobolev/Laplacian penalty versus alternatives like Total Variation.
- Model formulation needs justification: L2 penalty on codes departs from standard L1 sparse coding and requires empirical/theoretical motivation.
- Presentation issues: notation inconsistencies (e.g., vector/matrix dimensions), typos, and grammatical errors reduce clarity.
- Overall: practical and well-engineered for neuroimaging, but weakened by limited novelty and insufficient justification of modeling choices.
