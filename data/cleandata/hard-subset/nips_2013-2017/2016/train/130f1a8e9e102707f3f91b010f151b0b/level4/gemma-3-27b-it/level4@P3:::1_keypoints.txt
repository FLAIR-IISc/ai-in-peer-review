- Proposes a multivariate online dictionary learning method combining L1 sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to learn smooth, compact “blob” atoms for brain images.  
- Builds on online dictionary learning (Mairal et al., 2010) to handle inter-subject variability in neuroimaging.  
- Demonstrates the method on large-scale fMRI data from the Human Connectome Project.  
- Motivation: offers a data-driven alternative to manual ROI definition and leverages inter-subject variability often discarded by univariate models.  
- Positions the approach relative to clustering, PCA, ICA, and structured sparse decomposition with clear connections to prior work.  
- Strength — Novel combination: Laplacian penalty with online dictionary learning enforces structural priors missing in existing methods.  
- Strength — Scalability: online framework enables application to large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Strength — Promising empirical results: interpretable dictionaries, improved explained variance and prediction versus standard SODL.  
- Strength — Clear motivation and reasonably thorough experimental evaluation against CanICA and SODL, including runtime and qualitative assessments.  
- Weakness — Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis, particularly regarding the Laplacian penalty’s effect on convergence and dictionary quality.  
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity (α, γ, τ) with only limited guidance; needs more systematic parameter analysis or principled selection methods.  
- Weakness — Clarity and polish issues: occasional awkward phrasing, inconsistent notation, and leftover draft comments indicate insufficient revision.  
- Weakness — Limited comparisons: missing benchmarks against other structured-sparse/TV-regularized methods to strengthen novelty claims.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, scalable method addressing an important neuroimaging problem with encouraging preliminary results.  
- Argument for rejection: insufficient theoretical rigor, clarity/polish problems, and incomplete comparisons weaken the contribution.  
- Overall assessment: promising contribution but not ready for acceptance without theoretical analysis, clearer presentation, and broader comparisons.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject with encouragement to add theory, systematic hyperparameter study, more comparisons, and careful revision for resubmission.
