- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension with a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty and l1 sparsity to extract spatially smooth, compact brain-region atoms from large-scale fMRI.  
- Online algorithm scales to massive neuroimaging datasets (e.g., HCP), addressing memory/computation limits of batch methods.  
- Using a Laplacian penalty is a principled way to encourage spatial smoothness and compact “blob”-like atoms.  
- Authors derive block-coordinate updates and analyze complexity/convergence, showing the structural prior adds little computational overhead.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on HCP fMRI, comparing to SODL and CanICA with qualitative visuals, explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction, and learning curves.  
- Spatial regularizer shows clear benefits, especially in low-data regimes (learning curve results).  
- Main novelty is limited: core contribution is adding a Laplacian penalty to an existing online DL framework.  
- Paper should better contextualize choice of Laplacian vs other spatial priors (e.g., Total Variation) and discuss trade-offs like edge preservation.  
- Need clearer motivation separating roles of l1 atom sparsity (zeroed voxels) and Laplacian smoothness (compact nonzeros) to avoid framing “structure” as synonymous with sparsity.  
- Justify use of l2 (Ridge) penalty on codes U instead of l1 sparse coding, and explain why dense/stable codes are preferable in this neuroimaging setting.  
- Minor technical issue: dimensional inconsistency in Eq. (1) between Xi (row vector) and VUi (column vector) needs correction.  
- Manuscript requires careful proofreading to fix typos and grammatical errors.  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, scalable, and practically significant with strong empirical support — recommend acceptance if clarity and modeling-justification issues are addressed.
