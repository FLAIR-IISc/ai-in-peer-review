- Proposes an online structured dictionary learning algorithm for neuroimaging that adds a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms.  
- Model definition and algorithmic framework are clear and build on a well-established method [14].  
- Empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate the advantage of the Sobolev prior; qualitative claims of “more structured” atoms lack quantitative support.  
- Paper should define neuroscientifically grounded criteria and report quantitative metrics showing smoother boundaries or recovery of known connected regions.  
- Rebuttal added informative results that improved the reviewer’s technical rating from 2 to 3; those results should be included in the revision.  
- The extension using Sobolev priors is novel and original.  
- The approach could be impactful and useful for neuroimaging and other applications (e.g., image segmentation, regional sensing).  
- Manuscript needs better editing—there are confusing descriptions, typos, and unresolved author comments throughout.  
- Specific notation/matrix-transpose ambiguity between Lines 67–68 needs clarification.  
- Reference for the HCP dataset is missing (Line 73).  
- Grammar errors and duplicated phrases occur (e.g., Line 83, Line 157, Line 169).  
- A bolded unresolved co-author comment appears in the text (Line 146).  
- Equations (6) and (8) contain sign inconsistencies and unclear variable notation; provide explicit derivation/solution (at least in an appendix).  
- Section 3.3 should include the explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6).  
- Figure 2 presents different methods on different slices, preventing direct visual comparison and weakening the claim of more structured results.  
- Figure 3 is unclear about what is being predicted, how predictions are derived (U_i’s?), missing PCA baseline, and ambiguous R^2 reporting; caption and legend need clarification.  
- Overall: promising and technically sound method, but empirical evidence for the Sobolev prior’s added value is currently unconvincing and presentation issues must be addressed.
