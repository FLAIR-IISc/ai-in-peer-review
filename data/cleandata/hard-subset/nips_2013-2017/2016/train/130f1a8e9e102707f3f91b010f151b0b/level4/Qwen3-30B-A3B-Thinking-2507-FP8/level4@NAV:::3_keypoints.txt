- Extends dictionary learning models by integrating a Sobolev prior (interesting methodological extension).
- Practical significance and measurable improvement are unclear and hard to assess.
- Primary results rely on subjective visual comparisons of blob clarity/consistency in brain images, not interpretable to non-specialists.
- Data quality and quantity in this domain are often as critical as the algorithm, potentially confounding claims.
- Spatial smoothness/consistency penalties inherently produce smoother reconstructions, so the results are unsurprising.
- Approach might help isolate regions with sparse data, but relevance is questionable given large datasets like the Human Connectome Project.
- Expected better use of inter-subject variability and cross-subject consistency as validation, which is lacking.
- Current validation lacks objective criteria and fails to demonstrate a clear advantage over existing methods.
