The authors extend dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior. This is an interesting direction, but it is difficult to assess the impact or improvement of the proposed algorithm. The main results rely on visual comparisons of the clarity and consistency of identified brain blobs, which are not straightforward to interpret for a non-expert reader.

In my experience with this type of decomposition, the data are at least as important as the algorithm. Given the explicit penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, obtaining smoother blobs is expected. This may be more useful for isolating regions when data are scarce, but with the large datasets available from the Human Connectome Project, data limitations do not seem to be the primary issue.

I had hoped the paper would do more to consolidate brain-region delineations across subjects and perhaps use inter-subject differences and consistency to validate performance. As presented, the validation feels largely subjective, and the overall benefit of the method remains unclear.
