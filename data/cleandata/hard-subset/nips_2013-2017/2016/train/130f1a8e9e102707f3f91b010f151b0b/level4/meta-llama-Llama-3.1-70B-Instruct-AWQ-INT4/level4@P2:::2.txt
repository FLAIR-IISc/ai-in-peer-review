This paper proposes an online algorithm for learning structured dictionaries to characterize neuroimaging data across individuals, extending the framework in [16] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of dictionary atoms. The authors apply their method to empirical data, report the effect of varying tuning parameters, and compare the performance with two alternative methods in the field.

The technical quality of the paper is solid, with a clear model definition and algorithms that follow a well-established framework. However, the presentation could be improved. The paper would benefit from a clearer demonstration of the empirical advantage of introducing the Sobolev prior in the results section. For example, the authors could explicitly describe criteria justified by neuroscience literature and provide quantitative measurements of the improvement according to these criteria.

The paper makes a notable original contribution by extending [16] with Sobolev priors to encourage spatially structured dictionary atoms. The proposed method has the potential to improve the results of dictionary learning in neuroimaging data analysis. The authors provide an efficient online algorithm, which could be a useful tool in the neuroscience/medical imaging community.

The presentation of the paper is generally okay, but there are some confusing descriptions and typos that make it harder to understand the contents. For example, the matrix decomposition equation between Line 67 and 68 is unclear, and the reference to the HCP dataset is missing in Line 73. Additionally, there are some typos, such as "The Graph-Net penalty can imposes ..." in Line 83.

To improve the paper, the authors should address the following issues:

* Clarify the matrix decomposition equation between Line 67 and 68.
* Add the missing reference to the HCP dataset in Line 73.
* Fix the typo in Line 83.
* Provide a clearer demonstration of the empirical advantage of introducing the Sobolev prior in the results section.
* Explicitly describe criteria justified by neuroscience literature and provide quantitative measurements of the improvement according to these criteria.
* Improve the presentation of Figure 2 by presenting the results by different methods at the same horizontal and sagittal slices.
* Clarify the prediction task in Figure 3 and explain the legends in the main text or figure caption.
* Add the PCA baseline mentioned in the caption of Figure 3.

Overall, the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field of neuroimaging data analysis, but it requires some revisions to improve the presentation and clarify the results.
