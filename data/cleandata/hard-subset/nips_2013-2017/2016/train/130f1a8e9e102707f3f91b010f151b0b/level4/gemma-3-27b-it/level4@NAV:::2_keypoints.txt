- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary-learning algorithm using a Sobolev prior to promote spatially contiguous atoms and evaluates it empirically against two methods.  
- Reviewer raised initial score below 3 but revised to a 3 after reading the rebuttal.  
- Model formulation is clear and the algorithm is sound, closely following the framework of [14].  
- Empirical benefits of the Sobolev prior are not clearly demonstrated in the manuscript.  
- Figure 2 does not make it clear how SSMF differs from comparison methods; need quantitative smoothness measures and neuroscience-informed criteria.  
- Authors should quantify spatial boundary smoothness and identify regions uniquely delineated by SSMF, using neuroscientific validation.  
- Rebuttal added informative results; those should be incorporated along with more quantitative analysis of atom structure and neurobiological relevance.  
- Novelty is the extension of [14] with Sobolev priors to encourage spatial structure—this is a valuable contribution.  
- Potential impact is significant for neuroscience/medical imaging; extending to tasks like image segmentation or regional sensing could broaden relevance.
