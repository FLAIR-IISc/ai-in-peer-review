This paper focuses on dictionary learning in the context of brain region learning. The authors propose a large-scale online dictionary learning framework with an additional Sobolev regularization term to incorporate structural information. My major comments are as follows.

1. The paper appears to extend the online dictionary learning framework of Mairal (JMLR 2010) by incorporating an additional Sobolev regularization term. In my opinion, the novelty of this contribution is fairly weak and may not meet the NIPS standard.

2. The authors utilize Sobolev regularization to impose a structure constraint on the learned dictionary. However, the definition of "structure" is not clearly defined. If the structure refers to sparsity, it is unclear why the sparsity of the dictionary itself would bring significant improvement in the problem at hand. The motivation behind the use of Sobolev regularization is not well-explained.

3. I suspect a mistake in Eq. (1); it should be [V^1,V^2,…,V^r]^T.

4. More explanation and analysis of the construction of Eq. (2) are needed. For instance, why is the l2 norm imposed on the codes instead of the l1 norm used in Mairal (JMLR 2010)?

5. The meaning of line 146 is unclear.

6. The language of the paper requires refinement, as I have found numerous typos throughout the text.

Overall, while the authors present an interesting application of dictionary learning to brain region learning, the paper requires significant revisions to address the above concerns and improve its clarity and technical soundness.
