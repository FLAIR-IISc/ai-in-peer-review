1. Proposes adding a Sobolev prior to dictionary learning to enforce spatial smoothness in learned atoms.  
2. Manuscript currently makes it hard to assess the real impact and benefits of the method.  
3. Primary weakness: evaluation is qualitative and relies on subjective visual comparisons.  
4. No quantitative metrics are provided, so improvement over baselines is unclear.  
5. Producing smoother blobs is an expected outcome of the penalties, not necessarily an insightful result.  
6. The paper must show concrete advantages of the enforced smoothness (e.g., correspondence to known functional areas or improved predictive power).  
7. Motivation for this specific prior is unclear, especially given high-quality datasets like the Human Connectome Project.  
8. The method’s utility should be tied to a specific limitation of existing techniques, particularly for large datasets.  
9. Stronger validation suggested: inter-subject analysis or inter-subject consistency as objective performance metrics.  
10. Overall: validation is too subjective; include objective, quantitative evaluation to demonstrate scientific/practical benefits.
