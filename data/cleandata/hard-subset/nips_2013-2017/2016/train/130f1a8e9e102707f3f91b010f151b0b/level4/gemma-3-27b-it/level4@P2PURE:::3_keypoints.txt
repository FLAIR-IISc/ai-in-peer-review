- Proposes extending dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior to enforce smoothness and spatial consistency.
- Hard to evaluate the algorithm’s impact; improvements are difficult to quantify.
- Main results are visual comparisons of brain-region clarity and consistency, which may be hard to interpret without domain expertise.
- Data quality often drives decomposition results, potentially more than the algorithm itself.
- Smooth, consistent regions are expected given the imposed smoothness and spatial penalties.
- Benefit for small or noisy datasets is plausible, but less relevant for large, high-quality datasets like the Human Connectome Project.
- Expected focus on consolidating differences across subjects (using inter-subject variation as validation) is missing.
- Validation is subjective and the practical advantage of the proposed approach remains unclear.
