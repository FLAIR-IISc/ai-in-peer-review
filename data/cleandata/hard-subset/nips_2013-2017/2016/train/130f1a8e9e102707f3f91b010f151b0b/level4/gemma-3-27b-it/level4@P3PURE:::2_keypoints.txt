- Paper proposes an online dictionary-learning algorithm with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms for neuroimaging data.  
- Technical assessment initially marginally below 3, revised to 3 after rebuttal.  
- Model definition and algorithm are clear and generally follow a sound, established framework ([14]).  
- Empirical advantages of the Sobolev prior are not clearly demonstrated in the current presentation.  
- Figure 2 does not quantitatively show improved spatial boundary smoothness or clearer anatomical connectivity for SSMF versus baselines.  
- Authors should define neuroscience-justified criteria and provide quantitative measures showing SSMF’s improvement (e.g., boundary smoothness, known region connectivity).  
- Rebuttal results addressed concerns and should be incorporated into the revision.  
- Novelty: meaningful extension of [14] by applying Sobolev priors to encourage spatial structure in dictionary atoms.  
- Potential impact: useful tool for population neuroimaging and medical imaging; spatial priors have clear theoretical benefits.  
- Suggest exploring broader applications (e.g., image segmentation, regional sensing) to increase impact.  
- Manuscript requires careful copyediting—numerous confusing descriptions and typos hinder understanding.  
- Eq. decomposition (lines 67–68): transpose placement is unclear—should it apply to each V^j?  
- Line 73: missing reference for the Human Connectome Project (HCP) dataset.  
- Line 83: grammatical error (“can imposes” → “can impose”).  
- Line 146: bolded sentence appears to be an internal co-author comment and should be removed or integrated.  
- Equations (6) and (8) show inconsistent final lines and unclear variable usage (V vs. previous-iteration dictionary).  
- Line 157: duplicated phrase (“a single, a single”).  
- Line 169: typo (“th how” → “how”).  
- Section 3.3 / Algorithm 2: provide explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6), perhaps in an appendix.  
- Figure 2: showing different slices across methods prevents direct visual comparison and weakens the claim that SSMF is more “structured.”  
- Figure 3: unclear what is being predicted, whether regression uses U_i, unexplained legends, missing PCA baseline referenced in caption, and ambiguous R^2 references/curves.
