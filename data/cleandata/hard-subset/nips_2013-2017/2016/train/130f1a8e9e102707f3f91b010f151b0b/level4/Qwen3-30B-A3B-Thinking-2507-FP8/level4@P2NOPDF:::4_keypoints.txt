- Introduces undercomplete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization for fMRI component extraction.  
- Novelty is limited—method primarily extends existing sparse representation and smoothness constraints without substantial theoretical or methodological innovation.  
- Core contribution (Sobolev + L1) described but lacks clear explanation of how the two penalties interact in the optimization.  
- Empirical gains are marginal and fail to demonstrate compelling advantages over established baselines.  
- Quantitative metrics (reconstruction error, component interpretability) show only minor differences compared to standard L1-based dictionary learning or spatial smoothing.  
- Authors do not justify why the small improvements matter for fMRI analysis or practical use.  
- Missing ablation studies to isolate the contribution of each regularization term.  
- Manuscript has presentation problems (residual draft comments, convoluted methodology, vague explanations) undermining credibility.  
- Experimental setup lacks reproducibility details (hyperparameters, preprocessing), and figures are poorly labeled.  
- Results discussion sometimes conflates statistical significance with neuroscientific or clinical relevance without justification.  
- Recommendation: either strengthen empirical evidence and reproducibility (including ablations) or reframe the contribution to clarify theoretical/practical novelty; substantially revise writing and presentation.
