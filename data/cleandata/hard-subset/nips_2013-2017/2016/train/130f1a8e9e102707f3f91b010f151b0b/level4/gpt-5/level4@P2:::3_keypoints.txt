- Proposal: extends online dictionary learning with a Sobolev/Laplacian penalty for spatially smooth, compact atoms and ridge/ℓ1 codes; Smooth-SODL uses block-coordinate descent with SVD coding and FISTA atom updates.

- Empirical claim: on HCP task fMRI Smooth-SODL yields more visually interpretable “blob-like” components, competitive explained variance, higher stability, and modest runtime overhead (~2–3× SODL).

- Strength: addresses a relevant neuroimaging need for spatially coherent, sparse components that can reflect functional regions.

- Strength: scalable online algorithm with modest computational overhead, suitable for large n and p.

- Strength: clear relation to prior work (SODL, GraphNet/TV), convergence arguments, and implementable updates.

- Strength: experiments use a large public dataset and report some quantitative metrics (EV, stability, downstream prediction).

- Strength: provides practical hyperparameter/init guidance and plans to open-source code.

- Weakness: heavy reliance on qualitative visual inspection; interpretability claims are subjective and hard to evaluate for non-experts.

- Weakness: spatial-regularization benefit is unsurprising; unclear if it produces objectively better or more reproducible brain regions.

- Weakness: with abundant HCP data, necessity and benefit of strong spatial regularization are questionable; reported learning-curve evidence is limited and lacks confidence intervals.

- Weakness: incomplete comparisons—missing direct benchmarking against structured baselines (TV-ℓ1/GraphNet, related TV-constrained DL).

- Weakness: potential unfairness in comparisons because methods use inconsistent smoothing/thresholding; need harmonized preprocessing and reporting.

- Weakness: several methodological details are unclear (FISTA stopping/tolerances, γ tuning/schedule, sensitivity to τ and constraints, Laplacian discretization/boundary handling, ridge vs ℓ1 effects).

- Quality: algorithmic extension is technically sound and consistent with prior theory, but empirical claims (e.g., 1–2 FISTA iterations suffice) need stronger support and runtime/tolerance reporting.

- Clarity: paper is generally readable but contains typos/formatting issues; figures and method descriptions should be more self-contained and precise.

- Originality: moderate—spatial smoothness plus sparsity is established; novelty mainly in adapting these ideas to online DL and demonstrating scalability.

- Significance: potentially useful in low/medium-data regimes, but current evidence is not decisive and benefits decrease with large sample sizes.

- Suggested fixes: add objective spatial metrics (TV, compactness, connected components, Dice), split-half/test-retest reproducibility with CIs, ablations for γ/τ, and runtime/memory profiles across k,p,batch.

- Suggested fixes: benchmark against strong structured baselines with comparable runtimes; harmonize preprocessing/thresholding across methods; release reproducible code and scripts.

- For acceptance: scalable, simple extension with convergence guarantees and some empirical gains in structure and stability.

- Against acceptance: predominantly qualitative validation, missing key structured comparisons, and insufficient experimental/methodological documentation—lean toward rejection unless addressed.
