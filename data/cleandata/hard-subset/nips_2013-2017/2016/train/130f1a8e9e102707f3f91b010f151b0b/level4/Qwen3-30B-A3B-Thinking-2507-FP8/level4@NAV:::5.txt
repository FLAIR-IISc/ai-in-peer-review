

This paper addresses dictionary learning for brain region modeling. Specifically, the authors introduce a large-scale online dictionary learning framework incorporating Sobolev regularization to enforce structural properties. My primary concerns are as follows.  
1) The work primarily extends online dictionary learning (Mairal et al., JMLR 2010) by adding Sobolev regularization, resulting in insufficient novelty to meet NeurIPS standards.  
2) The paper employs Sobolev regularization to impose structure on the learned dictionary, yet the precise definition of "structure" remains ambiguous. If "structure" refers to sparsity, the justification for how dictionary sparsity significantly improves performance in this context is unclear. Consequently, the motivation for using Sobolev regularization is not adequately justified.  
3) Equation (1) appears to contain a notational inconsistency; it should read $[V^1, V^2, \ldots, V^r]^T$.  
4) Greater elaboration is required regarding the formulation of Equation (2), particularly why an $\ell_2$ norm is applied to the codes (as opposed to $\ell_1$ in Mairal et al., JMLR 2010).  
5) The reference to "line 146" is unclear and requires specification.  
6) The manuscript contains numerous typographical errors and requires significant language refinement.
