This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for the application of learning brain regions. The primary technical contribution is the addition of a Sobolev regularization term to the objective function to enforce structure on the learned dictionary atoms. While the application is interesting, I have several major concerns regarding the methodology and its presentation.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Limited Novelty:** The core contribution of this paper is the extension of the online dictionary learning framework of Mairal et al. (JMLR 2010) with a Sobolev regularization term. In my assessment, this represents an incremental contribution, and the technical novelty may not meet the standards for a NIPS publication.

2.  **Unclear Motivation and Formulation:**
    *   The paper introduces Sobolev regularization to impose a "structure" constraint on the learned dictionary. However, the term "structure" is not clearly defined in this context. The authors should provide a more precise definition and a stronger justification for why Sobolev regularization is the appropriate tool for this task. For instance, if "structure" refers to sparsity within the dictionary atoms themselves, it is crucial to explain why this property is beneficial for the specific application of learning brain regions.
    *   The formulation of the objective function in Equation (2) requires further justification. Specifically, the authors should explain the choice of an l2-norm penalty on the codes, which deviates from the l1-norm sparsity-inducing penalty commonly used in dictionary learning, including the foundational work of Mairal et al. An analysis of the implications of this choice is needed.

**Minor Comments and Questions:**

*   There appears to be a potential typo in Equation (1). Should the term be `[V^1, V^2, ..., V^r]^T`?
*   Clarity is needed regarding the statement or reference on line 146.
*   The manuscript would benefit from careful proofreading to correct numerous grammatical errors and typos, which currently impede readability.
