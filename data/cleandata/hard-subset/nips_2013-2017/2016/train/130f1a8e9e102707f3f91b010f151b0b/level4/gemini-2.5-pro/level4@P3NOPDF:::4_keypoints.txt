- Proposes under-complete dictionary learning for fMRI with a novel Sobolev (H1) + L1 regularization to enforce spatial smoothness and sparsity.  
- Motivated idea: enforcing neuroscientifically relevant structural priors (smoothness + sparsity) is well justified.  
- Combining Sobolev and L1 penalties in the dictionary learning objective is an interesting and novel formulation.  
- Empirical results are unconvincing: reported performance gains are marginal and not statistically validated.  
- Paper fails to demonstrate clear tangible benefits of the Sobolev term over simpler baselines like L1-only.  
- Lacks sufficient qualitative analysis (e.g., visual side-by-side component comparisons) to show improved interpretability or reduced noise.  
- Exposition is dense and confusing, with insufficient intuition and unclear mathematical motivation.  
- Manuscript appears unfinished and unprofessional, containing draft comments and proofreading errors.  
- Suggested improvements: perform statistical significance testing and ablation studies (L1-only, Sobolev-only, combined).  
- Suggested improvements: add strong qualitative comparisons of learned components and restructure methods for clearer high-level intuition.  
- Suggested improvements: thoroughly proofread to remove typos, editorial comments, and improve overall readability.  
- Overall judgment: interesting concept but poor empirical validation and presentation; not ready for acceptance in current form.
