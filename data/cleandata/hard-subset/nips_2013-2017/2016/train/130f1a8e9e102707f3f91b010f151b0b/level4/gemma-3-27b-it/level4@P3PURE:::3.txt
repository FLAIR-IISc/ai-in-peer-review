The authors extend dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior. This is an interesting problem, but the impact and improvement offered by the proposed algorithm are difficult to assess. The primary results are presented through visual comparison of the clarity and consistency of identified brain regions, which may not be readily apparent to readers unfamiliar with the data or task. Furthermore, in my experience with similar decomposition techniques, data quality and pre-processing are often as, if not more, important than the algorithm itself. Given the imposed penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, the resulting smoother regions are not particularly surprising. This approach might prove more valuable when dealing with sparse data, but this seems less relevant given the substantial datasets available through initiatives like the Human Connectome Project. I had hoped to see this work leverage inter-subject differences and consistency as a means of validating performance and consolidating representations of brain regions across subjects. The validation presented is largely subjective, and the practical benefit of the proposed method remains unclear.
