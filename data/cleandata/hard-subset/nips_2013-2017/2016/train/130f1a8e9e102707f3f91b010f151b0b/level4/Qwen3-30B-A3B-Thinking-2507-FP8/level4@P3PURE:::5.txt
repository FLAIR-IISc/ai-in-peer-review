

This paper addresses dictionary learning for brain region learning applications, proposing a large-scale online dictionary learning framework augmented with Sobolev regularization to enforce structural constraints. My major comments follow:  

1. **Novelty**: The work extends online dictionary learning (Mairal et al., JMLR 2010) by adding Sobolev regularization. However, this incremental modification lacks sufficient novelty to meet NIPS standards.  

2. **Motivation for Sobolev Regularization**: The paper employs Sobolev regularization to impose "structure" on the dictionary, but the definition of "structure" remains unclear. If "structure" refers to sparsity, the rationale for how sparsity in the dictionary itself significantly improves performance is not justified. The motivation for using Sobolev regularization requires clearer explanation.  

3. **Equation (1)**: Equation (1) appears incorrect; the expression should be $[V^1, V^2, \dots, V^r]^T$.  

4. **Equation (2) Justification**: The construction of Equation (2) needs more detailed justification. Specifically, why is the $\ell_2$ norm imposed on the codes (as in Equation (2)) rather than the $\ell_1$ norm, as used in prior work (Mairal et al., JMLR 2010)?  

5. **Line 146**: The content of line 146 is unclear and requires clarification.  

6. **Language and Typos**: The manuscript contains numerous typographical errors and requires substantial language refinement for clarity and professionalism.
