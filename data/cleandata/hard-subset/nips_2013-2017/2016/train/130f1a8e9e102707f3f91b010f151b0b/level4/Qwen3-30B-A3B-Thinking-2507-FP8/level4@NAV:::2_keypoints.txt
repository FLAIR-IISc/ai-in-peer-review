- Paper introduces an online structured dictionary learning algorithm for group neuroimaging, extending [14] with a Sobolev prior to promote spatially contiguous atoms.  
- Authors applied the method to empirical data, studied tuning-parameter effects, and benchmarked against two alternative approaches.  
- Technical quality initially scored below 3 but was revised to 3 after a rebuttal; model definition and algorithm follow a solid established framework.  
- Initial submission failed to clearly demonstrate empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior; results did not quantify how SSMF outperformed alternatives.  
- Figure 2 lacked quantitative metrics showing smoother spatial boundaries or unique identification of known connected brain regions.  
- Claim that SSMF results were “more structured” lacked neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative evaluation.  
- Rebuttal supplied informative results that justified the score upgrade; these results should be included in the revision.  
- Authors must explicitly quantify structural improvement of SSMF atoms using neuroscience-relevant metrics and show neuroscientific meaningfulness.  
- Novelty: notable originality in incorporating Sobolev priors into [14] for spatially structured dictionary atoms.  
- Potential impact: useful tool for group-level neuroimaging, medical imaging, and other spatial tasks like image segmentation or regional sensing.
