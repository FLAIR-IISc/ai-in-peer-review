- Authors add a Sobolev prior to dictionary learning for spatial smoothness, an interesting idea but its impact is unclear.
- Main results rely on visual comparison of blob clarity/consistency, which may not be convincing to untrained observers.
- Data quality can dominate decomposition outcomes, so algorithmic gains may be limited by input quality.
- Penalizing smoothness and spatial consistency predictably yields smoother blobs, so improvements are not surprising.
- Method seems most useful for limited-data scenarios, but abundant HCP data reduces this necessity.
- Paper fails to exploit inter-subject differences/consistency for stronger validation.
- Validation is subjective and does not clearly demonstrate the proposed method’s benefits.
