Paper extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior — an interesting direction.  
Impact and improvement of the proposed algorithm are difficult to evaluate.  
Main results rely on subjective visual comparisons of the clarity/consistency of detected brain “blobs,” which is unconvincing for non-experts.  
Data quality/quantity likely as important as algorithm; with abundant HCP data, data scarcity doesn’t seem limiting.  
Added smoothness and spatial-consistency penalties predictably yield smoother blobs, so observed gains may be unsurprising.  
The paper fails to consolidate regions across subjects or use inter-subject differences/consistency as objective validation.  
Overall validation is highly subjective and the method’s benefits are not clearly demonstrated.
