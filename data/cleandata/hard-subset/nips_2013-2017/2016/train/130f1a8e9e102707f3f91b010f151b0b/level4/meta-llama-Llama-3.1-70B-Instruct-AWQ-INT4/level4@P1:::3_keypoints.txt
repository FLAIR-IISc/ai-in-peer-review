- Novelty: Incorporates a Sobolev prior into dictionary learning, which is an interesting and original idea.  
- Technical soundness: The algorithm is technically sound and clearly explained.  
- Main evaluation weakness: Impact and improvement of the method are hard to judge from the presented results.  
- Visual results: Main figure relies on visual comparison of brain blobs that are unclear to untrained readers.  
- Need for explanation: Authors should more clearly explain visual differences between methods and their practical significance.  
- Effect of prior vs. data: Smoother, more consistent blobs may simply reflect the imposed penalties rather than true improvement, especially given large HCP datasets.  
- Data dependence: With abundant Human Connectome Project data, spatial regularization may be less necessary or beneficial.  
- Quantitative metrics: Some metrics (explained variance, atom stability) are reported but not thoroughly discussed.  
- Validation concerns: Current validation is subjective; objective, quantitative comparisons with existing approaches are needed.  
- Inter-subject analysis: The paper misses opportunities to use inter-subject differences/consistency as stronger validation.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written but needs clearer discussion of visual results and metric interpretations.  
- Significance uncertain: The practical significance and broader impact of the method are not convincingly demonstrated.
