- Proposes an online, under-complete dictionary learning method combining L1 atom sparsity with a Sobolev/Laplacian penalty to produce smooth, blob-like neuroimaging components.  
- Aims to scale to large fMRI datasets and yield interpretable functional components capturing inter-subject variability.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning with a reported 2–3× runtime overhead.  
- Addresses an important problem: discovering localized, interpretable functional components in large neuroimaging cohorts.  
- The combination of L1 sparsity and Laplacian smoothness is reasonable and likely to produce neuroanatomically aligned, blob-like atoms.  
- Using an online framework is practical for scalability and population studies.  
- Empirical improvements over simpler methods (e.g., sparse dictionary learning, PCA/ICA) are unclear or modest.  
- Manuscript presentation is rushed, contains draft comments/artifacts, and is sometimes confusing to follow.  
- Experimental section fails to convincingly link the proposed regularization to observed outcomes.  
- Empirical evidence is insufficiently strong to fully support the main claims.  
- Paper needs clearer, quantitative comparisons to standard baselines with consistent preprocessing and metrics.  
- Should report effect sizes, confidence intervals, statistical tests, and interpretability/stability metrics.  
- Recommend ablation studies varying the Laplacian weight and sparsity to show mechanism of improvement.  
- Exposition must be tightened: remove artifacts, clarify motivation for the Laplacian penalty, and explain algorithmic details and hyperparameter choices.  
- Reproducibility needs improving via summarized implementation details and fair baseline comparisons.  
- Potentially useful and incremental contribution, but significance is limited by current empirical support.
