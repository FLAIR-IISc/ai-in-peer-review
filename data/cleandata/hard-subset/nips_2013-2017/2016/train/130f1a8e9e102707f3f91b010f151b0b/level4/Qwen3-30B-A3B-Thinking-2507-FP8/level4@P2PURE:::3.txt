

The authors extend dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior, addressing an interesting problem. However, the impact and improvement of the proposed algorithm remain difficult to assess. The primary results rely on visually comparing the clarity and consistency of identified brain blobs, which is not objectively assessable by an untrained eye. Furthermore, in my experience with such decompositions, data quality is at least as important as the algorithm itself (if not more). Introducing penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency unsurprisingly yields smoother blobs; this approach may be beneficial for isolating regions with sparse data, but the Human Connectome Project provides vast publicly available data, making sparse data an unlikely primary concern. I had anticipated the paper would better characterize differences across brain regions among subjects and leverage inter-subject consistency/differences as a validation metric. The validation presented, however, is highly subjective, and the specific benefit of the proposed method remains unclear.
