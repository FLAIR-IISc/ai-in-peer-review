- Proposes multivariate online dictionary-learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) spatial regularization to yield structured, sparse, and spatially coherent brain decompositions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear theoretical and algorithmic exposition.  
- Extensive evaluation on a large-scale brain imaging dataset and comparisons to state-of-the-art methods are provided.  
- Unclear whether spatial coherence gains come from the Sobolev prior or from hyperparameter choices and dataset specifics.  
- Evaluation relies heavily on visual comparisons, which may not suffice to prove superiority.  
- Lacks quantitative metrics assessing spatial coherence, interpretability, and inter-subject consistency.  
- Needs analysis of robustness across different datasets and hyperparameter settings.  
- Suggests adding quantitative evaluations and more detailed cross-dataset/hyperparameter analyses.  
- Strength: novel incorporation of spatial regularization into online dictionary learning; method is well-motivated and theoretically grounded.  
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative evaluation and overreliance on visual results.  
- Acceptance requires additional quantitative demonstrations of superiority, broader dataset/hyperparameter analyses, and addressing concerns about the algorithm’s true impact.  
- Recommendation: Major revisions required; rating 6/10.
