- Proposes an online structured dictionary-learning method augmenting [14] with a Sobolev/Laplacian prior to produce spatially contiguous, piecewise-smooth atoms.  
- Empirical evaluation fails to convincingly and quantitatively demonstrate the benefits of the Sobolev prior.  
- Figure 2 does not clearly show in what measurable ways SSMF improves over baselines; claims of “more structured” need quantifiable neuroscience-grounded metrics (e.g., spatial compactness, boundary smoothness, network contiguity).  
- Technical development and algorithm are sound and rooted in established methods, but the empirical case for the prior is undercut by presentation and measurement gaps.  
- Reviewer raised technical-quality score from 2 to 3 after seeing rebuttal results and urges the authors to integrate those results into the revision.  
- Authors should demonstrate with quantitative metrics and appropriate citations that SSMF learns more neurobiologically meaningful, structured atoms.  
- Originality: the extension of [14] with a Sobolev prior is nontrivial and original.  
- Significance: the online algorithm and spatial prior could be a useful tool for neuroscience/medical imaging and extendable to tasks like segmentation and regional sensing.
