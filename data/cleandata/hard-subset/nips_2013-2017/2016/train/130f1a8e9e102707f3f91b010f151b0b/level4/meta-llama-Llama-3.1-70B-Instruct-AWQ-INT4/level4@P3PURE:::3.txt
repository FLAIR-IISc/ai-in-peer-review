The authors extend dictionary learning models to incorporate a Sobolev prior, an interesting problem that warrants exploration. However, it is challenging to assess the impact or improvement of this newly proposed algorithm. The main results figure relies heavily on visually comparing the clarity and consistency of identified blobs on the brain, but this comparison may not be clear to an untrained eye.

In my experience with this type of decomposition, the quality of the data is equally, if not more, important than the algorithm itself. By incorporating penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, it is not entirely surprising that the results yield smoother blobs. While this approach may be beneficial in isolating regions with limited data, the vast amount of data available through the Human Connectome Project diminishes the significance of this issue.

I had expected this paper to further consolidate the differences between brain regions across subjects and potentially utilize inter-subject differences and consistency as a means of validating the performance. Unfortunately, the validation presented in the paper appears subjective, and the benefits of the proposed approach are unclear.
