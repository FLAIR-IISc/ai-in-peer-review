- Proposes online dictionary learning (Smooth-SODL) with L1 sparsity and Laplacian spatial prior, using block coordinate descent and proximal updates (FISTA); ~2–3x runtime overhead, scalable to large n and p.
- Experiments on HCP fMRI compare Smooth-SODL to unstructured SODL and PCA/ICA (CanICA) on interpretability, explained variance, reproducibility, and behavioral prediction.
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) and structured sparsity literature (TV-L1, GraphNet); novelty is a scalable online integration of a Laplacian prior.
- Strength: addresses a practical need for scalable, spatially localized, interpretable components in neuroimaging.
- Strength: conceptually simple extension with few optimization tuning knobs and planned code release.
- Strength: large-scale experiments show clear qualitative improvements in spatial coherence and denoising of atoms versus unstructured SODL.
- Strength: reasonable computational cost and standard convergence properties for the optimization scheme.
- Weakness: quantitative improvements are modest and inconsistent; CanICA attains higher explained variance and gains lack statistical assessment.
- Weakness: limited baseline comparisons—missing strong structured methods like TV-L1 or GraphNet-based dictionary learning.
- Weakness: hyperparameter selection (γ, τ, α) seems delicate and lacks systematic sensitivity analysis or practical guidance.
- Weakness: manuscript clarity issues—typos, leftover draft comments, dense notation, and scattered/underspecified algorithmic details (stopping criteria, projections, schedules).
- Quality: technically sound optimization approach, but would benefit from ablations isolating Laplacian vs L1 effects and clearer complexity analysis.
- Clarity: presentation needs polishing; figures, captions, and notation should be clearer and self-contained.
- Originality: moderate—useful practical integration but constituent methods are well known; main novelty is scalable combination.
- Significance: potentially useful for practitioners, but limited empirical evidence weakens broader impact.
- Pros for acceptance: practical scalable method, qualitative spatial improvements, solid optimization foundation, extensive real-data experiments.
- Cons for acceptance: incremental novelty, missing structured baselines, clarity/organization problems, and uncharacterized hyperparameter sensitivity and statistics.
- Suggestions: add ablations (SODL vs Smooth-SODL), vary γ/τ, report standardized spatial metrics; include structured baselines; provide statistical tests/confidence intervals; clarify algorithmic details and tuning; improve writing and figures.
- Overall assessment: promising, practically relevant extension but borderline due to modest demonstrated gains and clarity issues; needs stronger empirical validation and clearer presentation.
