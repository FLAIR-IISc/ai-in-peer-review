- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method with an added Laplacian penalty for spatial smoothness and L1 for sparsity.  
- Main novelty is making spatially structured decomposition scalable via an online algorithm.  
- Produces visually cleaner, more spatially coherent dictionary atoms than unstructured SODL (improved interpretability).  
- Shows substantially higher explained variance than SODL in low-data regimes, demonstrating the value of the spatial prior.  
- Computational overhead is modest: about 2–3× slower than optimized vanilla SODL.  
- On full (large) datasets, Smooth-SODL does not consistently outperform baselines; CanICA often has higher explained variance.  
- Behavioral prediction results show CanICA outperforming Smooth-SODL on the test set; Smooth-SODL’s gains over SODL are modest.  
- Empirical trade-offs between interpretability and raw predictive power are not sufficiently discussed.  
- The Laplacian and L1 penalties are well-known; novelty is primarily in engineering/algorithmic integration rather than new modeling concepts.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor: dense writing, draft-stage comments, and non-self-contained derivations (e.g., equation (7) references other work).  
- Paper needs careful proofreading, clearer derivations, and a more balanced discussion of results and trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: lean towards acceptance conditional on thorough revision to improve presentation and contextualize empirical findings.
