- Paper extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior to enforce smoothness/spatial consistency.
- Overall impact and measurable improvement of the proposed algorithm are difficult to assess.
- Main results rely on visual comparisons of “brain blobs,” which are not objectively assessable by untrained viewers.
- Data quality likely influences decomposition results as much as the algorithm; smoothness penalties unsurprisingly produce smoother components.
- Method may help isolate regions with sparse data, but the Human Connectome Project’s large dataset makes sparsity less relevant.
- The paper fails to quantitatively characterize differences across brain regions or across subjects.
- Validation is highly subjective and does not clearly demonstrate the specific benefits of the proposed approach.
