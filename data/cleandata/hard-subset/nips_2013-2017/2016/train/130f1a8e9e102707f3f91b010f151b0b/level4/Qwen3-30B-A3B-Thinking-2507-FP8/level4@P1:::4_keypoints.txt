- Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning with Sobolev (Laplacian) and L1 penalties to enforce spatial smoothness and sparsity for fMRI atoms.  
- Claim: Better captures inter-subject variability than SODL and CanICA with a 2–3x runtime overhead.  
- Experiments: HCP task-fMRI (500 subjects, 260k voxels) show improved visual interpretability, stability (mean correlation 0.4 vs. CanICA 0.1), and behavioral prediction, but lower explained variance (55% vs. CanICA 60%).  

Strengths  
- Technical soundness: Laplacian penalty and BCD algorithm are well-formulated with convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical analysis: Runtime comparison and ablation study clarify hyperparameter effects (e.g., γ balances sparsity/data fidelity).  
- Originality: Novel integration of Sobolev regularization into scalable online dictionary learning for neuroimaging.  
- Practical value: Helps define interpretable ROIs for small-to-medium datasets; open-source implementation (Nilearn) aids reproducibility.  

Weaknesses  
- Clarity problems: Inconsistent terminology, unclear Laplacian derivation (∇x,∇y,∇z as p×p matrices), leftover draft comments, and repetitive phrasing.  
- Overstated significance: Improvements appear confined to small-data regimes; SODL closes the gap with more data and explained variance is lower.  
- Evaluation issues: Unfair baseline comparison—CanICA was run on pre-smoothed data while Smooth-SODL used raw data.  
- Scalability concern: 3x runtime penalty is nontrivial and lacks evaluation on larger datasets (e.g., 1,000+ subjects).  

Recommendations  
- Clarify technical definitions and Laplacian implementation; clean manuscript of drafts and repetition.  
- Reframe claims to emphasize small-data advantages and avoid overstating universal benefits.  
- Standardize preprocessing across baselines and report scalability on larger cohorts.  
- Quantify hyperparameter sensitivity (provide numeric safe ranges for γ and τ).  

Overall assessment  
- Technically solid and novel with clear neuroimaging relevance, but significance is modest and overstated; suitable as a Weak Accept pending revisions to clarity, fair comparisons, and scalability evidence.
