- Paper proposes an online dictionary learning algorithm for multi-subject neuroimaging with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms.
- Technical quality improved to 3 after rebuttal; model well-defined and grounded in prior work [14], but empirical support for the Sobolev prior was initially weak.
- Need quantitative evaluations to support claims of more meaningful and spatially coherent atoms (e.g., measures of spatial structure and neuroscientific plausibility).
- Main contribution—integrating a Sobolev prior into online dictionary learning—is original and well-motivated despite building on existing algorithmic frameworks.
- Potential impact is positive: method could improve interpretability in large neuroimaging cohorts and be useful for segmentation and regional sensing.
- Empirical evaluation currently inconclusive; Figure 2 does not clearly show SSMF outperforming baselines.
- Recommend reporting quantitative spatial metrics (total variation, boundary smoothness, connected components, surface-based smoothness, cluster contiguity) with statistical tests.
- Recommend linking identified patterns to known neuroanatomical/functional networks with citations to support neuroscientific validity.
- Integrate rebuttal results into the paper and add requested quantitative demonstrations of structure and validity.
- Presentation has confusing descriptions, typos, and notational inconsistencies; needs careful editing.
- Clarify matrix transpose notation around Lines 67–68 and reconcile Equations (6) and (8) regarding signs, scaling, and role of V.
- Add missing HCP dataset citation and fix multiple typos/accidental co-author comment (Lines 73, 146, 157, 169).
- Provide explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6) (e.g., proximal/coordinate update with Sobolev prior), at least in an appendix.
- Revise Figure 2 to align slices across methods/subjects, annotate ROIs, and substantiate claims of more structured atoms.
- Clarify Figure 3: specify predictors, targets, cross-validation, evaluation metric, legend/curves, include PCA baseline if referenced, and state which conditions achieve R^2 > 0.1.
- Show sensitivity analyses for tuning parameters, especially the Sobolev prior weight relative to sparsity and other regularizers, with plots and metrics.
- Provide computational performance analysis (time/memory complexity, wall-clock comparisons to baselines) to support practicality of the online approach.
- Improve reproducibility by providing code or detailed pseudocode with default hyperparameters and preprocessing steps.
- Overall assessment: strong motivation and principled prior but empirical benefits not yet convincingly quantified; borderline positive recommendation contingent on revisions.
