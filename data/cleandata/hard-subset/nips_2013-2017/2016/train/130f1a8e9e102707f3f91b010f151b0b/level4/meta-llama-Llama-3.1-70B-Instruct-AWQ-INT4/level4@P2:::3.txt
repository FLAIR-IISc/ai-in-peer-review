The authors propose a multivariate online dictionary-learning method that incorporates a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to impose structured and sparse components on brain image decompositions. This approach extends the standard online dictionary-learning framework with a spatial regularization term, allowing for the extraction of more interpretable and spatially coherent brain regions.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear overview of the proposed method, including its theoretical underpinnings and algorithmic details. The authors also provide an extensive evaluation of their method on a large-scale brain imaging dataset, comparing its performance to state-of-the-art methods.

However, I have some concerns regarding the impact and improvement of the proposed algorithm. While the method appears to produce more spatially coherent brain regions, it is unclear whether this improvement is solely due to the incorporation of the Sobolev prior or if it is also influenced by other factors, such as the choice of hyperparameters or the specific dataset used.

Furthermore, I was expecting a more comprehensive evaluation of the method's performance, including a more detailed analysis of its ability to capture inter-subject differences and consistency. The current evaluation relies heavily on visual comparisons of the identified brain regions, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the method's superiority.

To strengthen the paper, I suggest that the authors provide additional quantitative evaluations, such as metrics that assess the spatial coherence and interpretability of the extracted brain regions. Additionally, a more detailed analysis of the method's performance across different datasets and hyperparameter settings would be beneficial in demonstrating its robustness and generalizability.

Overall, while the proposed method appears to be a promising approach for brain image decomposition, I believe that a more comprehensive evaluation is necessary to fully demonstrate its impact and improvement over existing methods.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach for incorporating spatial regularization into online dictionary learning.
* The method is well-motivated and theoretically grounded.
* The evaluation is extensive and includes a comparison to state-of-the-art methods.

Weaknesses:

* The evaluation relies heavily on visual comparisons, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the method's superiority.
* The paper could benefit from additional quantitative evaluations and a more detailed analysis of the method's performance across different datasets and hyperparameter settings.

Acceptance criteria:

* The authors provide additional quantitative evaluations that demonstrate the method's superiority over existing methods.
* The authors provide a more detailed analysis of the method's performance across different datasets and hyperparameter settings.
* The authors address the concerns regarding the impact and improvement of the proposed algorithm.
