The authors extend dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior. This is an interesting problem, but it is difficult to evaluate the impact or improvement of the proposed algorithm. The main results figure relies on visual assessment of the clarity/consistency of identified blobs in the brain, which is not obvious to a non-expert. Also, in my experience with this class of decompositions, the data is at least as important as the algorithm. When adding penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, it is not surprising to observe smoother blobs. This could be helpful for isolating regions when data are limited, but given the large amount of data available from the Human Connectome Project, that issue does not seem pressing. I had hoped the paper would go further in consolidating differences in brain regions across subjects and perhaps use inter-subject differences/consistency as a validation of performance. The validation presented here feels quite subjective, and the benefit is not clearly established.
