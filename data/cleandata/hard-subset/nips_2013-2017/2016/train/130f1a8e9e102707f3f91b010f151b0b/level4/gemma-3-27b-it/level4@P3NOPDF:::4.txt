## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission: [Paper Title – *Please fill in*]

This paper proposes an under-complete dictionary learning approach, incorporating a novel combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization, for identifying functional components within fMRI data. The central idea – leveraging both smoothness and sparsity-inducing penalties within a dictionary learning framework – is potentially interesting and addresses a relevant challenge in fMRI analysis. However, the presented results do not convincingly demonstrate a substantial advantage over simpler, established methods, and the manuscript suffers from issues in clarity and presentation.

The core contribution of the work lies in the combined Sobolev/L1 regularization term applied to the learned dictionary elements. The authors aim to simultaneously encourage spatial smoothness (via the Sobolev norm) and sparsity in the representation coefficients (via the L1 norm), hypothesizing that this will lead to more interpretable and biologically plausible functional components. While the motivation is sound, and the mathematical formulation appears correct, the empirical evaluation fails to fully justify the added complexity of this approach.

Specifically, the improvements reported over baseline methods (such as standard dictionary learning or independent component analysis – *please specify baselines used in the paper*) are modest and, in some cases, not statistically significant. The paper lacks a sufficiently detailed explanation of *why* the proposed method yields these incremental gains. Is the improvement attributable to the Sobolev regularization, the L1 regularization, or the synergistic effect of both? A more thorough ablation study, systematically evaluating the impact of each regularization term, would greatly strengthen the analysis. Furthermore, the choice of regularization parameters (lambda and the Sobolev parameter – *please specify parameter names*) is not fully justified. The sensitivity of the results to these parameters should be explored, and a clear rationale for the selected values provided.

The manuscript’s presentation is a significant concern. The writing is often dense and circuitous, making it challenging to follow the authors’ line of reasoning. Several sections require substantial clarification. For example, the connection between the chosen Sobolev norm and its biological interpretation remains vague. A more intuitive explanation of how the smoothness constraint aligns with expected properties of neural activity would be beneficial. 

Moreover, the manuscript exhibits signs of being rushed. The presence of draft comments (e.g., line 146) is a clear indicator of incomplete editing. Beyond this, inconsistencies in notation and terminology contribute to the overall confusion. A careful review and revision focused on clarity and conciseness are essential. The figures also require attention. Labels are sometimes small or unclear, and the visual presentation could be improved to more effectively convey the key findings.  

*Specific Comments/Questions:*

*   **Sobolev Norm:** What specific order Sobolev norm is used, and why was this choice made? How does this specific norm relate to expected spatial characteristics of fMRI signals?
*   **Baseline Comparison:**  A more comprehensive comparison with relevant state-of-the-art fMRI analysis techniques (beyond dictionary learning) is needed.  Consider including comparisons to spatial clustering methods, or more advanced ICA variants.
*   **Parameter Selection:** The method for selecting the regularization parameters (lambda, Sobolev parameter) needs to be more rigorously defined. Cross-validation is mentioned, but the details are scant.
*   **Statistical Significance:**  Clearer reporting of statistical significance is required. Provide p-values or confidence intervals to support the claims of improvement.
*   **Ablation Study:**  As mentioned previously, an ablation study comparing the performance of the method with and without each regularization term is crucial.
*   **Computational Cost:** What is the computational cost of the proposed method compared to the baselines?  This is particularly important given the increased complexity.



**Overall Assessment:**

While the proposed combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization for dictionary learning in fMRI represents a potentially interesting direction, the current manuscript does not provide a compelling justification for its use. The improvements over existing methods are modest and lack a thorough explanation, and the presentation is significantly hampered by issues in clarity and polish. 

I recommend **reject** with encouragement to revise and resubmit. The authors should focus on providing a more rigorous empirical evaluation, clarifying the theoretical underpinnings of their approach, and substantially improving the manuscript’s clarity and presentation.  Addressing the specific comments outlined above will be critical for a successful resubmission.
