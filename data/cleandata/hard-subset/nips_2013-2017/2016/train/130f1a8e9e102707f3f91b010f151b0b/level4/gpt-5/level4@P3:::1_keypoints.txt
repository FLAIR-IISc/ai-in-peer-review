- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method enforcing sparsity (l1 ball/simplex) and spatial smoothness via a Laplacian penalty for brain image decomposition.  
- Extends Mairal et al.’s online dictionary learning with structured atom updates solved by a few FISTA iterations within block-coordinate descent; claims only 2–3× runtime overhead.  
- Positions work relative to TV–l1/GraphNet, structured sparse PCA, and multi-subject ICA (CanICA), arguing prior structured methods were offline and did not scale.  
- Experiments on HCP (n=500, k=40) compare Smooth-SODL to SODL and CanICA on interpretability, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Empirical findings: Smooth-SODL produces sparser, smoother, more localized components, improves stability over SODL/thresholded ICA, has moderate explained variance (lower than CanICA), and competitive behavioral prediction.  
- Strength: tackles a relevant large-scale neuroimaging problem combining matrix factorization, online optimization, and structured sparsity.  
- Strength: methodological contribution of integrating a Laplacian prior into online dictionary learning with efficient FISTA-based updates.  
- Strength: demonstrates scalability with mini-batching and sufficient statistics, keeping overhead modest.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation uses a large public dataset, appropriate baselines, multiple assessment metrics, and learning-curve analysis for low-data regimes.  
- Strength: provides practical hyperparameter guidance (e.g., αt ∝ t−1/2) and recommended safe ranges for (γ, τ).  
- Weakness: paper suffers from typos, duplicated sentences, unresolved editor notes, and inconsistent notation, harming readability.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis specific to the Laplacian penalty and lack of principled justification for the "1–2 FISTA iterations suffice" claim.  
- Weakness: comparisons may be unfair/inconsistent (e.g., CanICA pre-smoothed but SODL methods not); missing additional structured baselines.  
- Weakness: evaluation details are insufficiently specified (stability matching protocol, confidence intervals, behavioral prediction pipeline).  
- Weakness: hyperparameter selection methodology (γ, τ, batch size, k) is not fully specified and lacks ablation/sensitivity analyses.  
- Quality: algorithmically sound as an extension of online BCD with proximal updates, but theoretical support is limited and empirical validation needs more rigor.  
- Clarity: high-level ideas are clear but notation inconsistencies and missing algorithmic details (stopping criteria, projection sets) reduce clarity.  
- Originality: novel integration of Laplacian prior into scalable online dictionary learning; FFT-based variant and practical tips strengthen contribution.  
- Significance: potentially useful for scalable, interpretable decompositions in neuroimaging and imaging more broadly, contingent on stronger validation and reproducibility.  
- Specific fix: remove editorial artifacts, fix notation (V/v), and correct typos.  
- Specific fix: clearly define constraint set C (l1 ball vs simplex) and discuss non-negativity implications.  
- Specific fix: provide explicit FISTA stopping criteria, tolerances, and sensitivity analysis on inner iterations vs runtime/accuracy.  
- Specific fix: report memory usage, per-iteration complexity, and wall-clock timings across k and batch sizes.  
- Specific fix: ensure fair preprocessing across baselines and add structured baselines (e.g., TV–l1) on smaller subsets if needed.  
- Specific fix: add confidence intervals/variability bands for metrics and detail atom-matching strategy (e.g., Hungarian algorithm).  
- Specific fix: release code and scripts (anonymized if necessary) to ensure reproducibility.  
- Accept arguments: addresses an important problem with a scalable method, improves interpretability/stability, and shows solid empirical results on a large dataset.  
- Reject arguments: presentation and reproducibility issues, limited theoretical novelty, and incomplete/balanced comparisons reduce confidence.  
- Overall: promising and timely contribution but requires substantial polishing, more rigorous and balanced evaluation, and clearer reporting; reviewer leans weak accept if revisions address these gaps.
