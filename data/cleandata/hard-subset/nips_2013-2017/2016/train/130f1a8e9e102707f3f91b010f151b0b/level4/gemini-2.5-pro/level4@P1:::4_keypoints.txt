- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method for fMRI that adds a Sobolev (Laplacian) spatial-smoothness penalty plus L1 sparsity to produce spatially coherent components and scale to large datasets.  
- Strength: Methodologically novel—integrates spatial smoothness into a scalable online dictionary-learning framework.  
- Strength: Addresses an important need for discovering structured functional patterns in large neuroimaging datasets and builds on a well-established algorithm (Mairal et al.).  
- Weakness: Poor manuscript clarity and presentation—dense, sometimes confusing writing with leftover draft comments, suggesting the paper was rushed.  
- Weakness: Empirical results are unconvincing—qualitative smoothness improvements are shown but quantitative gains are limited.  
- Weakness: Outperformed by standard CanICA on the behavioral prediction task, undermining practical advantage.  
- Weakness: Main advantage appears only in small-to-medium data regimes; in large-scale settings (the motivation for online methods) performance gains shrink.  
- Overall verdict: Interesting idea but not ready for publication due to presentation issues and insufficient empirical justification.  
- Argument for acceptance: Tackles a relevant, scalable problem and presents a technically sound, non-trivial extension of online learning for structured neuroimaging.  
- Argument for rejection: Rushed writing and lack of strong experimental superiority—especially at scale—do not justify the added model complexity.
