This paper proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for decomposing brain images into structured and sparse components. The authors aim to delineate brain structures from functional images by combining an `1-norm constraint for sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce smoothness and compactness in the dictionary atoms – effectively creating “blobs.” This work builds upon the online dictionary-learning approach of Mairal et al. (2010) and demonstrates scalability to large datasets, with a reported runtime increase of only a factor of 2 or 3. Preliminary experiments on brain data suggest the method extracts more interpretable dictionaries and better captures inter-subject variability compared to state-of-the-art models.

The paper addresses an important problem in neuroimaging: handling inter-subject variability and defining regions of interest (ROIs) without relying on potentially biased segmentation or cross-modality correspondence. The proposed approach, leveraging the variability across subjects to define these regions, is conceptually sound and related to existing clustering techniques [21, 14] but offers a more model-based signal decomposition akin to PCA or ICA [5].  The combination of sparsity and smoothness constraints is a reasonable strategy to extract meaningful and localized brain structures.

**Strengths:**

*   **Scalability:** The online learning framework allows the method to handle large-scale datasets, a crucial advantage for neuroimaging applications.
*   **Principled Approach:**  The use of sparsity and smoothness penalties provides a mathematically grounded approach to ROI definition, avoiding purely data-driven or heuristic methods.
*   **Promising Results:** The experiments demonstrate improvements in dictionary interpretability and the capture of inter-subject variability compared to standard dictionary learning.
*   **Clear Connection to Existing Work:** The paper explicitly builds upon the work of Mairal et al. (2010) and positions itself within the broader context of dimensionality reduction and ROI definition in neuroimaging.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Incremental Novelty:** While the addition of the Sobolev penalty is a contribution, the core idea of extending existing online dictionary learning with a regularization term might be considered incremental, and the novelty may not fully meet NIPS standards.
*   **Lack of Clarity on “Structure”:** The definition of “structure” could be more precise.  The motivation for using the Sobolev penalty beyond simply inducing smoothness and compactness isn’t fully explained.  Why does this specific type of structure lead to improved performance in this application?
*   **Technical Details and Presentation:** There are several areas where the technical explanation could be improved, and a few minor errors exist. (See specific comments below).
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The importance of careful hyperparameter tuning is acknowledged, but a more in-depth analysis of the sensitivity to these parameters and a clear guidance on their selection would be beneficial.

**Specific Comments:**

*   Equation (1) appears to have a transposition error; it should likely be `Xi ≈ VUi` or `X = VU`.
*   The motivation for using an `l2` norm on the codes `Ui` in Eq. (2) instead of the `l1` norm used in Mairal et al. (2010) is not adequately explained.
*   Line 146 is incomplete and unclear.
*   The manuscript contains several minor typos and requires careful proofreading for clarity and fluency.
*   While the comparison to CanICA is valuable, a more detailed discussion of the computational cost of CanICA and its limitations in handling large datasets would strengthen the argument for the proposed online approach.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The method offers a scalable and principled approach to a challenging problem in neuroimaging.
*   The experimental results demonstrate tangible improvements in dictionary interpretability and performance.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly connected to relevant existing work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the approach is incremental.
*   The definition of “structure” and the motivation for using the Sobolev penalty are not fully convincing.
*   The technical presentation could be clearer and more polished.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a technically sound and potentially valuable contribution to the field of neuroimaging. However, the incremental novelty and areas for improvement in clarity and presentation raise concerns. I would recommend **rejection with encouragement to revise and resubmit**. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly clarifying the motivation for the Sobolev penalty, improving the technical explanations, and thoroughly proofreading the manuscript, could significantly strengthen the paper and increase its potential for acceptance in a future submission.
