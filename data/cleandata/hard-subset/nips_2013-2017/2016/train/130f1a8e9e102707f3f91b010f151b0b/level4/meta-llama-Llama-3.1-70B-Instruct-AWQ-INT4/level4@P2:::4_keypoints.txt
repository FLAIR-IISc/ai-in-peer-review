- Proposes under-complete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization to extract fMRI functional components.  
- Approach leverages Sobolev for smoothness and L1 for sparsity, an interesting and somewhat original combination.  
- Motivation for choosing Sobolev regularization and comparison to other regularizers is insufficiently explained.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive but does not convincingly show substantial improvement over simpler methods.  
- Explained variance plots (Fig. 2b) show comparable test performance to other methods.  
- Prediction results (Fig. 2c) indicate the method is outperformed by others on some behavioral variables.  
- Comparisons to baseline methods are not always clear or compelling; performance differences are ambiguous.  
- Manuscript presentation is rough, with draft comments, incomplete sentences, and confusing/tedious sections.  
- Needs clearer exposition to clarify the practical gains and methodological choices.  
- For acceptance: method is interesting, original regularization idea, and thoroughly evaluated.  
- Against acceptance: limited empirical gains, unconvincing comparisons, and significant polishing required.
