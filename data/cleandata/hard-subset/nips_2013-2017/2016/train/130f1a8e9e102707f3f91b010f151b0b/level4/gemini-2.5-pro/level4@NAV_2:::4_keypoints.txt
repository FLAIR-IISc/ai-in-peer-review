- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method adding a Laplacian penalty for spatial smoothness and an l1 constraint for sparsity to extract compact, blob-like components.  
- Targets scalable, data-driven ROI definition for large neuroimaging datasets, capturing inter-subject variability.  
- Evaluated on Human Connectome Project fMRI data against SODL and CanICA using visual quality, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Strength — Significance & scalability: online algorithm handles large-scale datasets better than batch structured decomposition methods.  
- Strength — Novelty: efficient integration of a spatial smoothness prior into online dictionary learning with modest computational overhead (2–3x runtime).  
- Strength — Thorough evaluation: comprehensive benchmarks and learning-curve analysis showing benefits in low-to-medium data regimes.  
- Weakness — Clarity & presentation: dense writing, organization issues, and leftover editorial artifacts make technical details hard to follow.  
- Weakness — Magnitude of improvement: visual interpretability gains are clear but quantitative improvements (explained variance, behavioral prediction) are modest or absent, warranting deeper trade-off discussion.
