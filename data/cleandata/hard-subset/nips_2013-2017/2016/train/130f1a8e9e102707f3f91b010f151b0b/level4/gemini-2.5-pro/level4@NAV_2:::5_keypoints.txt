- Proposes Smooth-SODL: extends online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to learn piece-wise smooth, compact dictionary atoms (ROIs).
- Algorithm is online and scales to massive neuroimaging datasets (tested on HCP).
- Empirical results: yields more interpretable, spatially structured, and stable components than SODL and CanICA, especially in low-data regimes.
- Modest computational overhead compared to standard SODL.
- Significance: addresses an important, practical problem of data-driven ROI discovery in large-scale neuroimaging.
- Originality concern: main novelty is adding a Laplacian penalty to an existing framework; conceptual advance over prior structured sparse coding is limited and should be better positioned.
- Needs comparison/positioning with other structured penalties (e.g., Total Variation) used in neuroimaging.
- Modeling choices unclear: the preference for Laplacian regularization and for L2 (ridge) on codes instead of L1 need stronger neuro-anatomical and methodological justification.
- Presentation issues: manuscript requires proofreading and clearer exposition (e.g., BCD update, Eq. (8), Algorithm 2 are dense/awkward).
- Overall: solid, scalable empirical work with practical benefits, but would be strengthened by clearer motivation, positioning, and improved writing.
