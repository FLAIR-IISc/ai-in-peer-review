- Proposes an online multivariate dictionary-learning method with an L1 + Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to enforce sparsity and spatial smoothness of brain image atoms.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning framework with a novel Sobolev prior for spatial contiguity.  
- Claims scalability to arbitrarily large neuroimaging datasets via the online algorithm.  
- Applies the method to empirical neuroimaging data and compares performance with two alternative methods.  
- Reports effects of varying tuning parameters on results.  
- Authors assert the method yields more interpretable, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.  
- Technical quality is good, with a detailed optimization procedure, but some algorithmic details could be clearer.  
- Presentation suffers from typos and confusing descriptions that hinder readability.  
- Lacks sufficient quantitative measurements demonstrating improvement over baselines.  
- Missing explicit evaluation criteria justified by neuroscience literature to support interpretability claims.  
- Novelty is notable due to the Sobolev prior extension.  
- Potential impact/usefulness is high for group-level neuroimaging analysis.  
- Suggestions: add quantitative metrics, justify criteria with neuroscience references, clarify optimization details, explore other applications (e.g., segmentation), and fix presentation issues.  
- Reviewer scores: technical quality 3, novelty 4, impact 4, presentation 3.  
- Acceptance arguments: original extension with Sobolev prior, promising improvements, applied to real data with detailed optimization.  
- Rejection arguments: weak presentation, insufficient neuroscience-justified criteria, and limited quantitative evidence of improvement.
