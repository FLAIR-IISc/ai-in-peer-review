- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary-learning algorithm for neuroimaging that extends [14] by adding a Sobolev prior to promote spatially contiguous atoms.  
- Technical soundness: model and algorithm follow a solid, well-established framework but presentation weakens perceived technical quality.  
- Main empirical critique: authors fail to clearly demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior—Figure 2 does not quantify how SSMF is superior or in what specific aspects.  
- Request for quantitative evidence: reviewer asks for explicit, neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative metrics showing atoms are more structured and meaningful.  
- Rebuttal effect: reviewer raised technical score from 2 to 3 after seeing rebuttal results and requests those results be included in the revision.  
- Novelty: notable and original extension of [14] by incorporating Sobolev priors to encourage spatial structure.  
- Potential impact: method could be a valuable, efficient tool for neuroscience/medical imaging and could be applied to tasks like segmentation or regional sensing.  
- Presentation quality: manuscript reads unedited with confusing descriptions and multiple typos that impede understanding.  
- Specific technical/notation issues: unclear transpose placement in matrix decomposition, inconsistent signs in Equations (6) and (8), and ambiguous use of V in Eq. (6).  
- Missing or unclear references/details: HCP dataset reference missing; Section 3.3 needs explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Eq. (6) (can be in appendix).  
- Figures unclear: Figure 2 uses different slices making comparisons hard and does not substantiate "more structured" claim; Figure 3’s prediction target, legends, PCA baseline, and R^2 claims are not explained.  
- Minor typos/phrasing problems: multiple grammatical errors and stray co-author comment (lines 83, 146, 157, 169) that should be fixed.
