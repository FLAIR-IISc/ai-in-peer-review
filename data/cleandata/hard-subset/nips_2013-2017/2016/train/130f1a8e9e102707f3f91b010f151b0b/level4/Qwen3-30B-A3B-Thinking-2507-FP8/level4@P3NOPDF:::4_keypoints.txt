- Introduces under-complete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev and L1 regularization to enforce spatial smoothness and sparsity in fMRI components.  
- Conceptually interesting and relevant to neuroimaging, but the main contribution is undermined by weak empirical support.  
- Reported performance gains (1–3% reconstruction accuracy) are modest, not statistically significant, and not rigorously analyzed.  
- No ablation studies to isolate the effect of Sobolev regularization versus L1, leaving the novel contribution unclear.  
- Computational cost and scalability are not discussed despite the Sobolev term likely adding significant overhead.  
- Manuscript contains draft comments and inconsistent notation (e.g., conflicting use of "S"), indicating poor polish.  
- Methodology section is dense, poorly structured, and hard to follow; key derivations and motivations are under-explained.  
- Justification for the combined regularization (Equation 5) and the link to Sobolev spaces is superficial and unclear for non-experts.  
- Claims about component "smoothness" lack quantitative validation such as gradient-magnitude or spatial-coherence metrics.  
- Recommended revisions: add ablation studies (Sobolev-only, L1-only, combined), perform statistical significance testing, and include runtime/memory benchmarks.  
- Further recommended revisions: clarify mathematical formulations, remove draft artifacts, and improve exposition to make technical contributions accessible.  
- Overall verdict: plausible extension of existing techniques but currently incremental, under-validated, and poorly presented—insufficient for acceptance without substantial revision.
