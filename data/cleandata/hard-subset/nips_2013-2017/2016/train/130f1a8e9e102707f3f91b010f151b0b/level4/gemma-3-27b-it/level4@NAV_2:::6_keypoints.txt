- Introduces a novel regularizer for matrix factorization that enforces sparsity and spatial smoothness simultaneously.  
- Provides a practical algorithm to obtain solutions but does not prove solution existence.  
- Experimental results show performance superior to state-of-the-art methods.  
- New regularizer and empirical results are the paper's main strengths.  
- Optimization problem is non-convex, and algorithm validity is primarily supported by experiments.  
- Reviewer initially requested more convergence evidence but was satisfied after rebuttal and raised Clarity score from 2 to 3.  
- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning with L1 (sparsity) and Sobolev/Laplacian (smoothness) penalties for brain images.  
- Learned dictionary atoms are spatially localized “blobs,” improving interpretability.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning with modest runtime increase.  
- Demonstrates better capture of inter-subject variability and improved behavioral prediction.  
- Technically sound and logically structured, with clear theoretical motivation for combining penalties.  
- Lacks theoretical convergence analysis for the non-convex optimization, weakening theoretical guarantees.  
- Comparison to CanICA is included and considered a valuable baseline.  
- Writing is generally organized but could be more concise; some sections are dense and require clarification (e.g., line 146).  
- Reproducibility may require more implementation details.  
- Originality: simultaneous L1 and Laplacian in an online dictionary framework is novel relative to prior work.  
- Significance: addresses relevant neuroimaging challenges, offers scalability for large datasets, and yields interpretable components.  
- Concerns: missing convergence theory, clarity/implementation detail gaps, and insufficient rigorous study of hyperparameter impacts.
