- Paper introduces a Sobolev prior in dictionary learning to encourage smoothness and spatial coherence in components.  
- Direction is interesting but the impact and improvement over existing methods are unclear from the presentation.  
- Main results rely on visual comparisons of brain “blobs,” which are subjective and not clearly distinguishable to untrained readers.  
- Because the method penalizes roughness, smoother components are expected and may not indicate a substantive algorithmic advance.  
- The approach may help in low-data regimes, but given abundant Human Connectome Project data, data scarcity is likely not the bottleneck here.  
- The paper does not sufficiently analyze or consolidate inter-subject differences, which would be a natural validation target.  
- Validation feels largely subjective and the practical benefits of the method remain unclear.  
- Quantitative, objective measures—especially assessments of inter-subject consistency—are needed to strengthen the evaluation.
