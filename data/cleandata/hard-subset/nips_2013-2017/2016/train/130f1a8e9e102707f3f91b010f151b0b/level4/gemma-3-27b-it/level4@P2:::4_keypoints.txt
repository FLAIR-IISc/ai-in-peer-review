- Introduces a multivariate online dictionary learning method combining L1 and Sobolev (Laplacian) penalties to learn sparse, smooth brain components.  
- Motivates method by limitations of univariate/ROI analyses and the value of inter-subject variability; scalable via online framework and evaluated on HCP data vs CanICA and SODL.  
- Strength: Novel combination of L1 and Sobolev penalties to promote sparsity and spatial coherence, yielding more interpretable components.  
- Strength: Scalable approach leveraging online learning, suitable for large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation on a large public HCP dataset with comparisons to ICA and standard sparse online dictionary learning.  
- Strength: Clear motivation and intention to release implementation in Nilearn, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Improvements over baselines are incremental and sometimes small (e.g., limited gain in behavioral prediction vs CanICA).  
- Weakness: Manuscript clarity and organization are poor, with dense/confusing writing and leftover draft comments.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity is insufficiently resolved; optimal parameter regimes remain unclear.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical novelty—builds on existing frameworks without stronger new analysis.  
- Weakness: Related-works section lacks a clear discussion of key differences from prior methods.  
- Argument for acceptance: Addresses a relevant neuroimaging problem with a scalable, plausible method and empirical results; code release increases impact.  
- Argument for rejection: Incremental gains, presentation flaws, and limited theoretical contribution may not justify acceptance at a top venue.  
- Overall assessment: Reasonable contribution but incremental and marred by clarity issues—recommend weak accept with major revisions.  
- Revision request 1: Rewrite for clarity and organization; remove all draft comments.  
- Revision request 2: Strengthen quantitative analysis with rigorous statistical comparisons to baselines.  
- Revision request 3: Deepen discussion and analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and its effects.  
- Revision request 4: Expand related-work discussion to clearly differentiate this method from prior approaches.
