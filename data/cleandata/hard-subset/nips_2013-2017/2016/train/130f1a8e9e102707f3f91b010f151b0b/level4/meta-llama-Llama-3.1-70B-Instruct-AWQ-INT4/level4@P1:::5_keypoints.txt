- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning framework with Sobolev regularization for structured, sparse brain-image decompositions that scales to large datasets.
- Extension to Mairal et al. (2010) is non-trivial and motivated by need for interpretable, spatially structured brain regions.
- Sobolev regularization is introduced to impose spatial smoothness on dictionary atoms, improving structure and denoising.
- Technical contributions and algorithmic description (optimization and objective construction) are sound and clearly presented.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing to state-of-the-art and demonstrating better capture of inter-subject variability across dataset sizes.
- Novelty could be emphasized more by clearly highlighting differences from existing online dictionary-learning methods.
- The meaning of "structure" under the Sobolev regularizer needs clarification and more intuition for its benefits.
- Equation numbering is inconsistent and some equations lack numbers; consistent renumbering is needed.
- Language and grammar contain several typos and minor errors that should be corrected for clarity.
- More details requested on objective construction, particularly rationale for using an L2 norm for codes instead of L1 (as in Mairal et al.).
- Strengths: novel Sobolev-regularized extension, clear motivation, thorough algorithmic description, and strong experiments.
- Weaknesses: minor formatting and language issues, insufficient emphasis on novelty, and limited intuition about Sobolev benefits.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the points above.
