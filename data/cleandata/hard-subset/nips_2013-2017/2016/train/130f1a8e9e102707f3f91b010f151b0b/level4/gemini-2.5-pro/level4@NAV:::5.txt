This manuscript introduces a framework for learning brain regions via dictionary learning. Specifically, the authors present a large-scale online dictionary learning algorithm augmented with Sobolev regularization to model structural information. While the application is interesting, I have several major concerns:

1.  The primary contribution appears to be the addition of a Sobolev regularization term to the well-established online dictionary learning framework (Mairal et al., JMLR 2010). This raises concerns about the paper's novelty, as it seems to be an incremental extension. The contribution, as presented, may not meet the originality threshold for this conference.

2.  The motivation for incorporating Sobolev regularization is not sufficiently explained. The paper uses this term to impose a "structure" constraint, yet it does not provide a precise definition of the "structure" it aims to impose. If this "structure" pertains to sparsity, a clearer justification is needed for why sparsity in the dictionary atoms themselves would yield significant improvements for this particular application. The rationale behind this specific choice of regularizer needs to be strengthened.

3.  Regarding Equation (1), could the authors confirm if there is a typographical error? It appears it should be presented as [V^1, V^2, ..., V^r]^T.

4.  Further discussion and analysis regarding the construction of Equation (2) are necessary. For instance, what is the reasoning behind imposing an l2-norm on the codes, a notable deviation from the l1-norm used in the cited work by Mairal et al. (2010)? An analysis of this design choice is needed.

5.  The statement on line 146 is unclear and requires clarification.

6.  Finally, the manuscript would benefit significantly from thorough proofreading. I identified multiple typographical and grammatical errors, which detract from the paper's overall readability and clarity.
