The authors extend the models for learning dictionaries to include Sobolev's theory. This is an interesting problem, but it is difficult to assess the impact or improvement of this newly proposed algorithm. The main results depend on comparing the clarity/consistency of the spots identified on the brain, but this is not obvious to an untrained eye. Also, in my experience with this type of decomposition, the algorithm is as important (if not more) as the data. By including penalties for smoothness and spatial stability, it is not surprising that you get smooth spots. Perhaps this is more useful for isolating areas with less data, but this does not seem to be an issue with the vast amount of accessible data with the human connectome project. I expected this paper to do more to consolidate the differences in brain regions across subjects and perhaps use inter-subject differences/consistency as a verification of performance. The verification presented here seems very subjective and it is not clear what its benefit is.
