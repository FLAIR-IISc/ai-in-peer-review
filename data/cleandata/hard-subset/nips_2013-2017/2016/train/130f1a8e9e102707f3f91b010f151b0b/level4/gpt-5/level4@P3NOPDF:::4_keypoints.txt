- Proposed method: under-complete dictionary learning for fMRI combining a Sobolev smoothness regularizer with L1 sparsity.
- Motivation: leverages known fMRI properties (spatial/temporal smoothness and parsimony) and could be broadly applicable.
- Strength: integrating smoothness into optimization rather than pre-smoothing is a natural and potentially useful design.
- Strength: under-complete setting is reasonable for finding compact functional modes and may reduce overfitting.
- Weakness: novelty seems incremental; similar smoothness+sparsity combos exist (TV/L1, Laplacian, fused lasso).
- Weakness: empirical improvements over baselines are modest and not convincingly analyzed.
- Weakness: objective and optimization details are under-specified (which Sobolev norm, discretization, where smoothness acts).
- Weakness: convergence properties, step-size choices, and stopping criteria are not described.
- Weakness: no justification for under-complete dictionary size or analysis of performance vs. number of components.
- Weakness: baselines are limited; missing comparisons to ICA, sparse PCA/NMF, L1-only, and other smoothness-regularized methods.
- Weakness: reproducibility unclear—code, parameters, initialization, splits, and preprocessing not documented.
- Weakness: writing is rushed with drafting artifacts, inconsistent notation, and unclear figures.
- Clarification needed 1: precisely define the optimization problem, Sobolev seminorm, discretization, and which variables are regularized.
- Clarification needed 2: detail the optimization algorithm, proximal operators used, and any convergence guarantees.
- Clarification needed 3: explain how regularization parameters were chosen and provide sensitivity analyses.
- Clarification needed 4: fully detail data preprocessing and rule out double-smoothing effects.
- Clarification needed 5: justify under-complete regime and show results across dictionary sizes (e.g., 20–200 components).
- Clarification needed 6: provide stronger baselines and ablations (L1-only, Sobolev-only, pre-smoothed+L1, TV/Laplacian variants).
- Clarification needed 7: quantify improvements with multiple metrics (reconstruction error, sparsity/smoothness, stability, atlas alignment, downstream performance).
- Clarification needed 8: report statistical tests, confidence intervals, and subject/session consistency.
- Clarification needed 9: discuss computational complexity, runtime, memory footprint, and practical tricks (warm starts, screening).
- Clarification needed 10: clarify whether smoothness is spatial, temporal, or both and handling of TR/filtering and brain boundaries.
- Suggestion: reorganize exposition—clear problem statement, motivation, exact objective, algorithm pseudocode, then ablations/comparisons.
- Suggestion: add a schematic of the model and alternating optimization flow.
- Suggestion: improve figures (readable fonts, consistent color scales, side-by-side baseline comparisons).
- Suggestion: include robustness analyses (initialization sensitivity, iterations, random seeds).
- Suggestion: tighten writing, remove drafting artifacts, fix notation, and streamline presentation.
- Minor: define all symbols at first use and keep notation consistent.
- Minor: clarify whether “components” mean spatial maps, temporal courses, or both; use precise terms (atoms vs. loadings).
- Minor: state dataset details (subjects, runs, task/rest, voxel resolution) and train/test splits.
- Overall assessment: sensible idea with potential but limited novelty and insufficient methodological/experimental detail; not recommended without substantial revisions.
