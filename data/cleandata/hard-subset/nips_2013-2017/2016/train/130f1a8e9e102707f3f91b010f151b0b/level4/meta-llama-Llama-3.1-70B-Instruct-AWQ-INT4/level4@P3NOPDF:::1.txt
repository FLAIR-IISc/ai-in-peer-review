This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework aimed at identifying interesting brain regions, a topic that is inherently captivating. However, the authors fail to provide a theoretical analysis that demonstrates the effectiveness of their proposed algorithm, a significant omission that detracts from the overall quality of the submission.

Furthermore, the writing quality and attention to detail in the paper are lacking. It appears that the authors did not thoroughly review the draft before submission, as evidenced by the presence of internal comments and queries, such as "146 XXX: I don't understand the switch between V and v." This oversight suggests a lack of diligence in the preparation of the manuscript.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should provide a rigorous theoretical analysis that validates the proposed algorithm's performance and demonstrates its advantages over existing methods. Additionally, a thorough editing process is necessary to eliminate errors, clarify ambiguous points, and ensure that the writing is polished and free of internal comments. Until these issues are addressed, it is challenging to fully evaluate the merits of the proposed framework.
