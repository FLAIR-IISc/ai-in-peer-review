

This paper introduces a novel regularization technique for matrix factorization that enforces both sparsity and smoothness. The primary strength lies in the innovative design of the regularizer, which effectively integrates these two properties into the optimization framework. Experimental results demonstrate clear superiority over state-of-the-art methods on benchmark datasets, suggesting promising practical utility.

However, the paper contains two significant shortcomings that require attention. First, while the authors present an algorithm to compute solutions, they do not establish the existence of an optimal solution for the non-convex problem they formulate. The non-convex nature of the objective is acknowledged in the paper, but the absence of theoretical guarantees regarding solution existence weakens the foundation of the proposed approach. Second, the algorithm's efficacy is supported solely by experimental evidence, with no additional theoretical analysis or convergence guarantees provided. This reliance on empirical results alone is a notable limitation for a theoretical contribution.

Additionally, the writing requires substantial improvement. For instance, line 146 contains unclear phrasing that impedes comprehension. The manuscript would benefit from a more precise and polished exposition throughout to enhance readability.

After the rebuttal, I am satisfied with the authors' clarification regarding the algorithm's convergence behavior. Their response adequately addressed my initial concerns about the practical implementation of the method. Consequently, I am willing to upgrade my rating for Clarity and Presentation from 2 to 3. This adjustment reflects the improved understanding gained from the authors' rebuttal, though the core theoretical limitations (existence of solution, non-convexity) remain unaddressed. The paper's technical contribution is promising, but stronger theoretical grounding would significantly strengthen its impact.
