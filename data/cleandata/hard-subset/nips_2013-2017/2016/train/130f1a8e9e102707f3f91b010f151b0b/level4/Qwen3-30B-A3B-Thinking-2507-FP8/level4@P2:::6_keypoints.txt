- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online multivariate dictionary-learning method combining sparsity and a Laplacian spatial-smoothness penalty to extract brain regions.  
- Extends Mairal et al.'s (2010) online dictionary learning by adding a Laplacian penalty on atoms to enforce spatial structure.  
- Well-motivated by neuroimaging needs: avoids anatomical priors and addresses noise/limitations of standard ROI approaches.  
- Tackles an important challenge: defining spatial regions that reflect functional organization and inter-subject variability.  
- Strong technical contribution with clear algorithmic development and scalability preserved from the original online framework.  
- Provides theoretical foundations, convergence discussion, and computational complexity analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: compares to CanICA and SODL on HCP data using visual interpretability, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Empirical results show more interpretable, spatially-structured dictionaries while maintaining or improving predictive performance.  
- Novelty: applies Laplacian regularization within an online dictionary-learning context, retaining computational advantages.  
- Significant for neuroimaging: scalable to large datasets (e.g., 500 subjects) and especially useful in small-to-medium data regimes.  
- Main weakness: lacks formal theoretical guarantees for the non-convex optimization problem (no proof of solution existence).  
- Presentation issues: explanation of Laplacian penalty implementation and time-varying α rationale need clarity.  
- Some sections (e.g., Section 3.1) are overly technical and could be streamlined for accessibility.  
- Limited discussion of method limitations across different data regimes and of the penalty’s relation to specific brain anatomy.  
- Writing generally good; practical implementation provided in Nilearn; overall recommendation: accept.
