- Proposal: Introduces Smooth-SODL, a multivariate online dictionary-learning method that adds a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to impose spatial structure on dictionary atoms.  
- Contribution: Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning framework with a Sobolev prior for spatial smoothness.  
- Dataset: Evaluated on a large public Human Connectome Project dataset.  
- Writing/clarity: Paper is well-written and explains the method and relation to prior work clearly.  
- Novelty: The use of a Sobolev prior to enforce spatial structure is an interesting and original idea.  
- Empirical findings: Smooth-SODL extracts more structured, denoised, and interpretable dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.  
- Baselines: Thorough comparison against SODL and CanICA across small, medium, and large-scale regimes, showing improved interpretability.  
- Validation concerns: Performance validation is somewhat subjective and relies heavily on visual comparisons of brain blobs.  
- Quantitative metrics: Some metrics (explained variance, atom stability) are provided but are insufficiently comprehensive.  
- Need for objective evaluation: More objective evaluation criteria would strengthen the claims.  
- Impact of prior: The paper lacks detailed analysis of how the Sobolev prior (e.g., regularization parameter γ) affects results and potential drawbacks.  
- Limited-data claim: Authors claim utility for limited data but provide no experiments to support this.  
- Related work: Provides a comprehensive overview but could better clarify differences and improvements over existing approaches.  
- Overall assessment: Interesting and technically sound contribution, but weakened by subjective validation and limited analysis of the Sobolev prior.  
- For acceptance: Novel contribution, effective empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: Subjective validation, need for more objective metrics, insufficient analysis of the prior’s impact, and missing experiments for scarce-data scenarios.
