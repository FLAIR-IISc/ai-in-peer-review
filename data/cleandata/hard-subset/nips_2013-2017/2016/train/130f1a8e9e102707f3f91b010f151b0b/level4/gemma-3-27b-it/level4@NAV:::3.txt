The authors present an extension to dictionary learning using a Sobolev prior, addressing a potentially valuable problem. However, quantifying the practical impact and demonstrable improvement offered by this new algorithm proves challenging based on the presented evidence. The primary results are illustrated through visual comparison of identified brain regions, assessing clarity and consistency; however, this assessment relies heavily on expert interpretation and may not be readily interpretable by those unfamiliar with the specific neuroimaging data. Furthermore, given the established importance of data quality in decomposition methods, the observed smoother results, achieved through smoothness and spatial consistency penalties, are somewhat expected. While this approach may prove beneficial in scenarios with limited data, its utility appears less significant given the extensive datasets available through initiatives like the Human Connectome Project. The paper would have benefited from a greater focus on leveraging inter-subject variability – both differences and consistencies – to rigorously validate the algorithm’s performance and better demonstrate its ability to consolidate brain region distinctions across individuals. The current validation strategy is largely subjective, and the practical benefits of the proposed method remain unclear.
