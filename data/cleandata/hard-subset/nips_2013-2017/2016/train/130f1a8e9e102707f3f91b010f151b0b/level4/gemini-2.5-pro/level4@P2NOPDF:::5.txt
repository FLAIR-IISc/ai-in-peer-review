This paper presents a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for the application of identifying brain regions. The primary technical contribution is the addition of a Sobolev regularization term to encourage structure in the learned dictionary atoms. While the application is interesting, the paper has several weaknesses in its current form.

**Major Comments**

1.  **Novelty and Contribution:** The proposed method appears to be a direct extension of the online dictionary learning framework of Mairal et al. (JMLR 2010), with the main difference being the inclusion of a Sobolev regularization term. The incremental nature of this contribution raises questions about its significance for a top-tier conference like NIPS. The authors should better highlight the conceptual and technical challenges addressed by this extension to more clearly establish the paper's novelty.

2.  **Motivation and Clarity of Sobolev Regularization:** The manuscript's central claim is that Sobolev regularization imposes a "structure constraint" on the dictionary. However, the term "structure" is used ambiguously and is not clearly defined. The authors should explicitly state what structural properties (e.g., spatial smoothness, contiguity) they aim to enforce. Furthermore, the motivation for choosing Sobolev regularization over other possible structure-imposing priors is not well-justified. Why is this specific regularizer uniquely suited for the problem of learning brain regions, and what are its advantages over simpler alternatives? A more compelling motivation is necessary.

3.  **Justification for Model Formulation (Eq. 2):** The objective function in Equation (2) uses an l2-norm penalty on the codes. This is a significant departure from the l1-norm typically used in sparse dictionary learning (including the work by Mairal et al.), which is known for promoting sparse representations. The authors should provide a clear rationale for this choice. Why are dense, small-magnitude codes (encouraged by the l2-norm) preferable to sparse codes for this application? A discussion of the implications of this modeling decision is needed to justify the formulation.

**Minor Comments and Questions for Clarification**

1.  **Equation (1):** Please verify Equation (1). There appears to be a potential typographical error, and the term should perhaps be `[V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T`.

2.  **Unclear Statement:** The sentence on line 146 is unclear. Please revise this line for better readability.

3.  **Writing and Presentation:** The manuscript requires thorough proofreading to correct numerous grammatical errors and typos. Improving the overall clarity of the writing would strengthen the paper and make its contributions easier for the reader to understand.
