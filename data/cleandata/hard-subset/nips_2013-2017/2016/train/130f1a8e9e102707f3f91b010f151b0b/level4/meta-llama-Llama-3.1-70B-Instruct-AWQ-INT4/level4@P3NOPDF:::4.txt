The paper proposes under-complete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization to identify functional components in fMRI data. This approach appears to be a novel application of dictionary learning techniques to neuroimaging data. The authors' main contribution lies in the use of dictionary learning with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization, which aims to promote both smoothness and sparsity in the extracted components.

While the proposed approach is intriguing, the results presented in the paper do not convincingly demonstrate a significant improvement over simpler methods. The authors should provide a more thorough comparison with existing techniques and clearly explain the benefits of their approach. As it stands, the advantages of the proposed method are not adequately justified.

Furthermore, the manuscript requires revisions to improve its overall clarity and readability. The presence of draft comments, such as the one on line 146, suggests that the paper was submitted in a rush. The writing is often tedious and confusing, making it challenging for readers to follow the authors' arguments and understand the methodology. The authors should thoroughly revise the manuscript to address these issues and provide a clear, concise presentation of their work.

In its current form, the paper falls short of expectations. To strengthen the manuscript, the authors should focus on providing more robust results, a clearer explanation of their approach, and a well-organized, polished presentation of their work.
