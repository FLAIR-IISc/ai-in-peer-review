This paper focuses on dictionary learning for brain region identification. The authors propose a large-scale online dictionary learning framework that incorporates Sobolev regularization to impose structural constraints. My major comments are as follows:

1. In my opinion, this paper primarily extends the online dictionary learning framework (Mairal, JMLR 2010) by adding a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, I believe the novelty of this work is relatively weak and may not meet the NIPS standard.

2. To impose structural constraints on the learned dictionary, the authors employ Sobolev regularization. However, the definition of "structure" in this context is unclear. If the structure refers to sparsity, it is unclear why the sparsity of the dictionary itself would lead to significant improvements in this problem. Overall, the motivation behind using Sobolev regularization is not well justified.

3. I suspect there may be an error in Eq. (1), which should likely read [V^1,V^2,…,V^r]^T.

4. Further explanation and analysis of the construction of Eq. (2) are necessary. For instance, why is the l2 norm imposed on the codes instead of the l1 norm, as in Mairal (JMLR 2010)?

5. The text does not provide sufficient context for line 146; additional information would be helpful.

6. The language throughout the paper requires refinement. I have identified multiple typos that need correction.
