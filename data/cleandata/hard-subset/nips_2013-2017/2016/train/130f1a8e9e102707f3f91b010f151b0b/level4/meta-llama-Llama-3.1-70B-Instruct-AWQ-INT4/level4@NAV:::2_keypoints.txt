- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary learning method with a Sobolev prior for neuroimaging and compares it to two alternatives.  
- Technical quality: model and algorithms are clear and build on [14], but empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Figure 2 lacks quantitative evidence showing SSMF yields smoother boundaries or identifies meaningful connected regions.  
- Reviewer requests explicit, neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative metrics to demonstrate that SSMF atoms are more structured and meaningful.  
- After rebuttal the reviewer raised technical rating from 2 to 3 and asks authors to include rebuttal results in the revision.  
- Novelty: notable contribution by extending [14] to incorporate Sobolev priors that encourage spatial structure.  
- Potential impact: provides an efficient online algorithm useful for group neuroimaging analysis and potentially for image segmentation/regional sensing.  
- Presentation: manuscript is poorly edited with confusing descriptions and typos that hinder understanding.  
- Clarify matrix decomposition: should the transpose apply to each individual V^j in Lines 67–68?  
- Missing reference: add HCP dataset citation (Line 73).  
- Grammar/typos to fix: Line 83 ("can imposes"), Line 157 ("a single, a single"), Line 169 ("We did an extensive study of th how").  
- Remove unresolved co-author comment in bold at Line 146.  
- Equations (6) and (8) show inconsistent signs and unclear notation—clarify algebra and whether V refers to previous dictionary and how the jth column is treated.  
- Provide the mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6) (e.g., in the appendix).  
- Figure 2 presentation uses different slices across methods, preventing direct comparison; the claim that SSMF is more “structured” is unsupported.  
- Figure 3 is unclear about the prediction task, legends are unexplained, the PCA baseline mentioned is not shown, and the R^2 > 0.1 claim is ambiguous about which curves to inspect.
