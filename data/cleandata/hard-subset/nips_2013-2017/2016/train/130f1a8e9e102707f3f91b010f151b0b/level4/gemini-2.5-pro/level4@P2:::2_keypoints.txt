- Paper proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce spatially smooth, contiguous atoms for fMRI.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated extension addressing scattered, less interpretable components in neuroimaging.
- Strength: clear algorithmic formulation built on Mairal et al. (2010) with only a modest (2–3×) computational overhead, preserving scalability.
- Weakness: empirical evidence for improved spatial structure is insufficient; qualitative comparisons (e.g., Fig. 2a) are subtle and not convincing.
- Suggestion: include quantitative metrics (spatial compactness, contiguity, smoothness) and relate components to known neuroscientific networks to demonstrate meaningfulness.
- Clarity issue: inconsistency between Equation (7) and Algorithm 2 (line 4); require clarification of the correct dictionary update.
- Algorithmic detail missing: explicit solver or more detailed description for the subproblem (Eq. 8), e.g., FISTA specifics or appendix pseudocode.
- Figure/presentation problems: different anatomical slices used across methods, unclear captions/legends (Fig. 2c), and PCA baseline referenced but not shown.
- Typos and proofreading: multiple grammatical errors and a missing HCP dataset reference reduce readability and reproducibility.
- Rebuttal update: authors added Table 1 showing strong benefits of spatial regularization in low-data regimes, e.g., a 14× gain in explained variance for Smooth-SODL vs SODL.
- Recommendation: incorporate the rebuttal results into the main paper and fix presentation, equations, algorithms, figures, and typos to make the contribution solid.
