- Paper proposes an online structured dictionary learning method for neuroimaging that integrates a Sobolev prior to enforce spatial contiguity of atoms.  
- Core contribution is novel and addresses a meaningful gap by reducing fragmented atom representations.  
- Technical quality initially weak (2→3 after rebuttal) because empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior was not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Rebuttal provided quantitative spatial smoothness metrics that strengthened the empirical case; these must be integrated into the main text.  
- Reviewer requests explicit linking of atom structure to neuroscience criteria (e.g., cortical folding, anatomically connected regions).  
- Novelty/originality rated 4/5: principled extension of prior work [14] with Sobolev priors is notable.  
- Potential impact rated 4/5: scalable online algorithm useful for group-level, longitudinal neuroimaging and related tasks (segmentation, regional sensing).  
- Presentation problems are substantial (rating 2/5) and hinder comprehension; major editing required.  
- Mathematical inconsistencies need correction (e.g., Equation (6) sign mismatch, ambiguous V in Equation (8), transpose/notation issues).  
- Figures are flawed: Figure 2 shows inconsistent slices and lacks quantitative support; Figure 3 has unclear prediction target and missing PCA baseline and legends.  
- Multiple minor errors and typos must be fixed (missing HCP citation, repeated words, stray co-author comments, grammar issues).  
- Clarity gaps: require explicit solution details for Eq. (6) (appendix), correct matrix decomposition notation, and clearer description of prediction task in Figure 3.  
- Recommendation: Accept with major revision contingent on quantitative validation, figure revision, mathematical and grammatical corrections, and clearer neuroscientific justification of spatial structure.
