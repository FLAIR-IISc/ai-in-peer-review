The authors propose an extension to dictionary learning models by incorporating a Sobolev prior. While the problem is well-motivated, the paper does not sufficiently demonstrate the impact or performance improvement of the proposed algorithm. The primary validation relies on visually comparing the clarity and consistency of identified spatial components in a figure, but the claimed improvements are not obvious to a non-expert reader.

Furthermore, since the model explicitly incorporates penalties for smoothness and spatial consistency, the fact that it produces smoother components is an expected outcome of the formulation rather than a compelling demonstration of its efficacy. Such a prior may be most useful for isolating regions in data-sparse scenarios, but its relevance is less apparent for applications involving data-rich resources like the Human Connectome Project, where data scarcity is not the primary challenge. A more convincing demonstration of the method's utility could have involved consolidating findings across subjects and using inter-subject consistency as a more objective performance metric. As presented, the validation is highly subjective, and the practical benefits of the proposed method are not clearly established.
