- Paper proposes an online multivariate dictionary-learning method for brain images, adding a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to encourage spatial contiguity of atoms.  
- Sparsity is imposed via an L1-norm constraint; structure via the Sobolev penalty.  
- Method is an extension of Mairal et al. (2010) and is designed to scale to arbitrarily large datasets (online algorithm).  
- Problem motivation (defining ROIs in neuroimaging) is clear and well-explained.  
- Related work is thoroughly reviewed and limitations of existing methods are clearly stated.  
- Optimization problem and algorithmic details are clearly formulated.  
- Experiments on a large public dataset are promising and show more interpretable, denoised dictionaries.  
- Results indicate better capture of inter-subject variability across small, medium, and large spatial scales.  
- Presentation suffers from confusing descriptions, multiple typos, and grammatical errors that hinder clarity.  
- Figures and result presentation need improvement for clearer comparisons.  
- Paper lacks sufficient quantitative measurements comparing improvements over existing methods.  
- Technical quality rated 3 (model clear, algorithms solid but presentation weak).  
- Novelty/originality rated 4 (notable contribution: Sobolev prior in online dictionary learning).  
- Potential impact/usefulness rated 4 (useful tool for neuroscience/medical imaging community).
