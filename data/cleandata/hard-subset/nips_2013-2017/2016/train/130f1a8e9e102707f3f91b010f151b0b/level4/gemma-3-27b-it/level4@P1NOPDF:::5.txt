This paper addresses dictionary learning in the context of brain region analysis. Specifically, the authors propose a large-scale online dictionary learning framework incorporating Sobolev regularization to enforce structure. My major concerns are as follows:

1) As far as I can tell, this paper primarily extends existing online dictionary learning methods (e.g., Mairal, JMLR 2010) by adding a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, I find the novelty to be relatively weak and question whether it meets the standards for NIPS publication.

2) The paper utilizes Sobolev regularization to impose structure on the learned dictionary, but the precise definition of this “structure” remains unclear. If the intended structure is sparsity, it’s not immediately obvious why enforcing sparsity *on the dictionary itself* would lead to significant improvements in this application. Overall, the motivation for employing Sobolev regularization isn't sufficiently clear.

3) I believe there is a typographical error in Eq.(1); it should be represented as [V<sup>1</sup>, V<sup>2</sup>,…, V<sup>r</sup>]<sup>T</sup>.

4) Further explanation and analysis regarding the formulation of Eq.(2) are necessary. Specifically, the rationale for using an l2 norm on the codes, rather than the l1 norm employed in Mairal (JMLR 2010), should be clarified.

5) Could the authors please clarify line 146? The current text is unclear.

6) The language throughout the paper requires refinement. I noted numerous typos and instances where phrasing could be improved for clarity and fluency.
