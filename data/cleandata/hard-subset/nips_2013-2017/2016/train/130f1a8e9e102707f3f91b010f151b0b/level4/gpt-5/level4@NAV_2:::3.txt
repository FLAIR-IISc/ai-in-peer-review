The paper augments dictionary-learning with a Sobolev/Laplacian prior on the atoms. While the problem is interesting, the magnitude of the advance and its practical improvement are hard to assess. The main results figure relies on visual comparisons of the clarity/consistency of identified brain blobs, which is difficult to interpret without more objective criteria. In my experience with this class of decompositions, the data can matter at least as much as the method; once explicit smoothness and spatial coherence penalties are added, obtaining smoother, more compact maps is expected. This might be most useful in low-data settings, but with the extensive Human Connectome Project data, scarcity does not seem to be the limiting factor. I was hoping the paper would more directly consolidate cross-subject regional differences and use inter-subject variability/consistency as validation of performance. As it stands, the validation appears largely subjective, and the tangible benefit is not clear.
