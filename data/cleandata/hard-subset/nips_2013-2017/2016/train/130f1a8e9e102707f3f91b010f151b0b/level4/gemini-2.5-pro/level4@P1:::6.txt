This paper introduces a new regularization method for matrix factorization designed to impose both sparsity and spatial smoothness on the learned components. An online algorithm for solving the resulting optimization problem is presented. The method is evaluated experimentally on fMRI data, where it is shown to produce results superior to state-of-the-art approaches.

**Strengths:**
*   The introduction of a novel regularizer that combines penalties for sparsity and smoothness is the paper's main strength. This is a well-motivated and valuable contribution, particularly for applications like neuroimaging where extracted components should be both localized and spatially coherent.
*   The experimental results are promising and effectively demonstrate the potential of the proposed method. The comparisons clearly show the advantages of the approach over existing techniques.

**Weaknesses:**
*   The primary weakness is the lack of theoretical analysis. As the authors state, the optimization problem is non-convex. However, the paper does not provide a formal proof for the existence of a solution. Consequently, the algorithm's validity rests solely on its strong empirical performance rather than on theoretical guarantees.
*   The quality of the writing could be improved. The manuscript would benefit from a thorough proofread by a native English speaker to enhance clarity and fluency.
