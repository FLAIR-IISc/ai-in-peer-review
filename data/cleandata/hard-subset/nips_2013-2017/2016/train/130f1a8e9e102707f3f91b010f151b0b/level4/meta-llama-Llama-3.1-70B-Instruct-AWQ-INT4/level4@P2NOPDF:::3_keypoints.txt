- Proposal: Extend dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior to enforce smoothness and spatial consistency in identified brain components.  
- Impact unclear: Hard to assess algorithmic improvement or practical benefit from the presented results.  
- Evaluation method: Main results rely on visual comparisons of blob clarity/consistency, which may be opaque to non-experts.  
- Data vs. method: Data quality often drives decomposition results, so smoother blobs may reflect data characteristics as much as the algorithm.  
- Expected effect: Smoothness/spatial penalties naturally produce smoother components, so the outcome is not surprising.  
- Limited-data niche: Approach could help when data are scarce, but large datasets like the Human Connectome Project reduce this need.  
- Validation gap: Paper lacks quantitative/inter-subject validation and instead presents subjective assessments; benefits are not clearly demonstrated.
