- Paper proposes large-scale online dictionary learning for identifying brain regions, with main contribution being a Sobolev regularization term to impose structure on dictionary atoms.

1. The method is primarily an extension of Mairal et al. (2010) with a Sobolev term, which may be an incremental novelty insufficient for NIPS.

2. Motivation for Sobolev regularization is unclear; "structure" is not defined and the authors must justify why atom sparsity (if intended) benefits this application.

3. Possible typo in Equation (1); it likely should read [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T.

4. The objective in Equation (2) needs justification: why use an l2 penalty on codes rather than the l1 norm commonly used to enforce sparsity?

5. The text around line 146 is unclear or contains a typo and needs correction.

6. The manuscript requires careful proofreading to fix numerous typos and grammatical errors.
