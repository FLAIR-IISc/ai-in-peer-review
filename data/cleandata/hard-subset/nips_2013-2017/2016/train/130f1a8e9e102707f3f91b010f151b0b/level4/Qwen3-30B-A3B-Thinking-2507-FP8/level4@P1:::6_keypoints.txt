- Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning by adding a Laplacian spatial-smoothness penalty tailored for brain imaging.  
- Novelty: Introduces Laplacian-based regularization that enforces spatial smoothness without large computational overhead.  
- Methodological foundation: Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning and aligns dictionary atoms with known brain functional organization.  
- Empirical gains: Outperforms SODL and CanICA on visual interpretability, explained variance, and behavioral prediction.  
- Experimental evaluation: Thorough tests on large-scale HCP fMRI data across small/medium/large regimes show more structured, interpretable maps.  
- Computational cost: Reported runtime is 3x slower than SODL but 1.6x faster than CanICA; timing results support the claim.  
- Hyperparameter analysis: Careful study of hyperparameter selection provided and practically useful.  
- Reproducibility and presentation: Algorithmic description is detailed and reproducible, though some mathematical notation could be clearer.  
- Theoretical concern: No proof of existence of an optimal solution for the non-convex formulation and limited justification that the algorithm converges to meaningful solutions.  
- Clarity issues: Explanation of the Laplacian penalty and several technical terms need clearer exposition for non-experts.  
- Limited limitations discussion: Paper provides little discussion of method limitations.  
- Significance: Substantial impact for neuroimaging and potential applicability to other spatially structured data.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS; clarity rating improved after rebuttal.
