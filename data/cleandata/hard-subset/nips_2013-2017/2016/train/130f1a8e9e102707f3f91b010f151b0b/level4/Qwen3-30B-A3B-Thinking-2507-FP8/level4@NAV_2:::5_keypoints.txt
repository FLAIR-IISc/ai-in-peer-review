- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning method with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce spatial structure on brain imaging dictionary atoms.  
- Shows more interpretable, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability versus SODL and CanICA across small, medium, and large datasets.  
- Retains scalability of Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning, suitable for large datasets like HCP.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: multiple baselines, multiple metrics (explained variance, stability, reproducibility, predictive performance).  
- Provides practical insights on hyperparameter selection and demonstrates robustness across data regimes.  
- Novelty is limited—mainly an extension adding spatial regularization; needs clearer distinction from prior spatially-structured dictionary learning work and discussion of theoretical/computational differences.  
- The paper’s definition of “structure” (piece-wise smooth and compact) is vague and should be connected to known brain organization and alternative regularizations.  
- Notational inconsistency in Equation (1) (matrix V stacking) that requires clarification.  
- Use of a ridge (‖Ui‖₂²) penalty on codes instead of L1 sparsity (as in Mairal et al.) is unjustified and has significant modeling implications.  
- Line 146 references an unclear section/equation, causing reader confusion.  
- Multiple grammatical errors and typos undermine clarity.  
- Overall: solid practical contribution with strong validation and NIPS-level technical quality/significance, but novelty and some modeling choices need clearer articulation and justification.
