This paper studies dictionary learning in the context of brain region learning. Concretely, the authors introduce a large-scale online dictionary learning framework with an added Sobolev regularization to impose structure. This work centers on dictionary learning for brain region learning. Specifically, the authors present a scalable online dictionary learning approach augmented with a Sobolev regularizer to encode structure.

My major comments are as follows.
1) In my view, this work essentially extends online dictionary learning (J. Mairal, JMLR 2010) by appending a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, the contribution appears only marginally novel and does not meet the NIPS standard.
2) To enforce structural constraints on the learned dictionary, the paper employs Sobolev regularization. However, the precise meaning of “structure” is not clearly defined. If by structure you mean sparsity, why would sparsity of the dictionary itself markedly improve performance for your tasks? Overall, the motivation for using Sobolev regularization is not well justified.
3) I suspect a mistake in Eq.(1); it likely should read [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T.
4) Additional clarification and analysis of the construction of Eq.(2) are needed. For example, why is an l2 norm used on the codes instead of the l1 norm as in (J. Mairal, JMLR 2010)?
5) What is line 146?
6) The writing requires polishing; I found many typos.
