- Proposes extending dictionary learning models by adding a Sobolev prior.  
- Impact and improvement of the proposed algorithm are difficult to assess.  
- Primary results rely on visual comparisons of blob clarity/consistency, which are hard to interpret for untrained observers.  
- Data quality may be as important as the algorithm for decomposition performance.  
- Smoothness and spatial-consistency penalties naturally produce smoother blobs, so smoother results are unsurprising.  
- Approach could help isolate regions with limited data, but large datasets like the Human Connectome Project reduce this need.  
- Expected focus on consolidating inter-subject differences and using inter-subject variability for validation is missing.  
- Validation is highly subjective and practical benefits of the method remain unclear.
