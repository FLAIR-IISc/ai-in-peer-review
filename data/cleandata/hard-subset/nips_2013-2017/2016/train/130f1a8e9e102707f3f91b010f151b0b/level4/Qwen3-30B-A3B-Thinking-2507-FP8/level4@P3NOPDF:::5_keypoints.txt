- Limited novelty: the method is a minor extension of Mairal et al. (2010) by adding Sobolev regularization and lacks a substantial theoretical or methodological advance.  
- Unclear definition of "structure": the paper never precisely defines what "structure" means (spatial, temporal, etc.) or why Sobolev regularization enforces the relevant structure for brain regions.  
- Weak motivation for Sobolev regularization: authors fail to justify why Sobolev smoothing (vs. other penalties) is particularly appropriate or beneficial for the brain-region learning task.  
- Technical errors/ambiguities: equation (1) appears to have a typo, equation (2) lacks justification for using an ℓ2 norm over ℓ1, and line 146 is unclear.  
- Lack of comparative analysis: no ablation studies isolate the effect of the Sobolev term, and empirical claims about its benefit are unsupported.  
- Missing theoretical justification: the paper does not explain theoretically why Sobolev regularization should produce meaningful structure in brain data rather than generic smoothness.  
- Presentation and language issues: numerous grammatical errors, typos, and inconsistent notation reduce readability and professionalism.  
- Recommendation and required fixes: reject in current form unless authors precisely define "structure," justify Sobolev use, provide ablations/theory, correct technical errors, and thoroughly revise the writing.
