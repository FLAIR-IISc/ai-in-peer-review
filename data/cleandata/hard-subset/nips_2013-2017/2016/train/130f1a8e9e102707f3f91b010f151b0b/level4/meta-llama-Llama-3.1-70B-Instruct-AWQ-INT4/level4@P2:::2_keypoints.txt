Proposes an online algorithm extending [16] with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data.

Technical quality is solid with a clear model and algorithms built on an established framework.

Empirical results need clearer demonstration that the Sobolev prior gives practical advantages.

Recommend defining neuroscience-justified evaluation criteria and reporting quantitative improvements according to those criteria.

Presentation has confusing descriptions and typos that hinder understanding (e.g., unclear matrix decomposition between Lines 67–68, missing HCP dataset reference at Line 73, typo in Line 83).

Improve Figure 2 by aligning results from different methods at the same horizontal and sagittal slices.

Clarify the prediction task and legends in Figure 3 (explain in main text or caption) and include the PCA baseline mentioned in the caption.

Paper makes an original contribution and provides an efficient online algorithm useful to the neuroscience/medical imaging community.

Potential to improve dictionary learning results in neuroimaging, but empirical evidence of benefit is currently insufficiently clear.

Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid technical grounding, and practical algorithmic contribution.

Arguments for rejection: presentation flaws and insufficiently demonstrated empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior.

Overall recommendation: revise to fix presentation errors and strengthen empirical validation before acceptance.
