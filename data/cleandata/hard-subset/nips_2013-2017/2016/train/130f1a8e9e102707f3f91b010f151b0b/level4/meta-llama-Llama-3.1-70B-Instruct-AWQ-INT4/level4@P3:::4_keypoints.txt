- Proposes an under-complete dictionary learning method (Smooth-SODL) with combined Sobolev/L1 regularization to enforce smoothness and sparsity.  
- Novelty: interesting extension of dictionary learning by integrating Sobolev regularization with L1.  
- Method presentation: thorough overview of formulation, optimization algorithm, and experimental setup.  
- Experiments: evaluated on a large public fMRI dataset and compared to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Results: Smooth-SODL extracts structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability across scales.  
- Improvement magnitude: performance gains over simpler methods are modest and not clearly substantial.  
- Justification: authors need to better explain why the added complexity is necessary and how it overcomes existing limitations.  
- Limitations: manuscript lacks detailed analysis of method-specific limitations and hyperparameter selection challenges.  
- Clarity/presentation: well-structured but contains wordy/unclear sentences, minor errors, and needs polishing.  
- Background/context: more context on the neuroimaging problem and broader relevance would strengthen the paper.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound and original (Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10).  
- Acceptance/rejection balance: arguments for acceptance include novelty, thorough method description, and large-scale experiments; arguments for rejection focus on weak improvements, unclear necessity of complexity, and presentation issues.
