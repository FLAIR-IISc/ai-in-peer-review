- Proposal: multivariate online dictionary-learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce spatial structure and sparsity for brain-image decompositions, extending Mairal et al. (2010) to capture inter-subject variability.  
- Relation to prior work: extends online dictionary learning by adding spatial regularization, unlike methods such as CanICA that do not model spatial variability.  
- Strength: integrates spatial structure (via Sobolev penalty) relevant for brain imaging.  
- Strength: scalable to large datasets.  
- Strength: experimental results show improved extraction of structured and denoised dictionaries versus state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness: novelty is limited—largely builds on existing online dictionary-learning frameworks.  
- Weakness: the notion of "structure" under the Sobolev regularizer is not clearly defined.  
- Weakness: motivation and justification for using Sobolev regularization are weak.  
- Weakness: paper contains several typos and grammatical errors.  
- Argument for acceptance: useful, practical extension with promising empirical performance and brain-imaging applications.  
- Argument against acceptance: insufficient novelty and needs clearer explanation of the Sobolev term and improved writing.  
- Quality: 7/10 — well-organized but hindered by typos/grammar.  
- Clarity: 8/10 — generally clear but some sections need clarification.  
- Originality: 6/10 — incremental contribution; Sobolev term is a useful addition.  
- Significance: 8/10 — promising impact for brain imaging, though significance could be more clearly articulated.
