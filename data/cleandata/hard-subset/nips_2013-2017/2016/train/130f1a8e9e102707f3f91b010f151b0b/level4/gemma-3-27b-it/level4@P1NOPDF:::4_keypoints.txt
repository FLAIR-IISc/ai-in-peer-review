- Proposes under-complete dictionary learning with combined Sobolev + L1 regularization to encourage smoothness and sparsity in fMRI components.  
- Primary contribution is the combined regularization strategy, which is conceptually interesting and well-motivated.  
- Experimental gains are modest and not convincingly better than simpler methods.  
- Synthetic improvements may be due to parameter tuning; no thorough sensitivity analysis for regularization parameters (lambda, Sobolev) provided.  
- Parameter selection procedure is unclear and robustness to different choices is not demonstrated.  
- Real fMRI results show minimal improvement over PCA and standard sparse coding.  
- Claims of improved functional connectivity are weakly supported; visual comparisons in Figures 3–4 are insufficient.  
- Lacks quantitative comparisons of functional connectivity (e.g., correlations, graph metrics) to substantiate claims.  
- Manuscript writing is poor: awkward phrasing, confusing flow, inconsistent notation, and unclear definition/implementation of the Sobolev norm.  
- Algorithm description is dense and would benefit from step-by-step explanation and pseudocode.  
- Insufficient discussion of connections to existing dictionary learning and regularization literature.  
- Contains draft comments in the text (e.g., line 146), indicating lack of careful proofreading.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but open to reconsideration after substantial revision addressing clarity, experiments, and robustness.
