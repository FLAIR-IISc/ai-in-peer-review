This manuscript proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework for identifying functionally relevant brain regions from neuroimaging data. The application of scalable machine learning techniques to this domain is a timely and interesting research direction with the potential for significant impact.

However, the paper has two major weaknesses that, in its current state, prevent a recommendation for acceptance.

First, the manuscript lacks any theoretical analysis to support the proposed algorithm. While the authors present an online learning framework, they do not provide convergence guarantees, an analysis of the convergence rate, or a formal justification for the method's effectiveness in this problem space. The absence of this theoretical grounding makes it difficult to assess the algorithm's robustness, reliability, and generalizability. The paper would be substantially strengthened by a formal analysis demonstrating the conditions under which the proposed method is expected to perform well.

Second, the quality of the manuscript's presentation is significantly below the standard expected for a NIPS publication. The paper appears to have been submitted without careful proofreading, as it contains numerous grammatical errors and inconsistencies that hinder readability. Most notably, the draft includes an unresolved, internal comment left by the authors (e.g., "146 XXX: I don't understand the switch between V and v."). The presence of such an artifact strongly suggests that the manuscript was not reviewed thoroughly before submission. This lack of diligence in preparation undermines the credibility of the research and makes it difficult for a reviewer to fairly evaluate the technical contributions.

In conclusion, while the research topic is promising, the paper is critically flawed by the complete omission of theoretical analysis and a concerning lack of careful preparation. Due to these significant issues, I cannot recommend acceptance of the manuscript in its present form.
