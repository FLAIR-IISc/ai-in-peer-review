- Proposes an undercomplete dictionary learning method for fMRI with combined Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization; idea is interesting and somewhat original.  
- Empirical benefits over standard or simpler methods are not convincingly demonstrated; reported improvements are marginal or unclear.  
- Unclear which component (Sobolev term, L1 term, undercomplete constraint) drives any gains or whether gains come from interactions.  
- Manuscript presentation feels rushed and contains draft comments, making it tedious and confusing to read.  
- Needs a clearer problem statement and a precise definition of the optimization objective, with all terms and hyperparameters explicitly introduced.  
- Algorithm description lacks transparency about implementation details, convergence, and stopping criteria.  
- Should include more thorough empirical comparisons against simple baselines (standard dictionary learning, ICA, sparsity-only/smoothness-only variants).  
- Recommend ablation studies to isolate the effects of the Sobolev term, the L1 term, and the undercomplete dictionary.  
- Clarify the rationale for using an undercomplete setting in fMRI and explain how dimensionality was selected.  
- Report sensitivity analyses for key hyperparameters (weights of Sobolev and L1 penalties) and provide practical guidance for selection.  
- Improve figure/table captions and visualizations to better show qualitative advantages of learned components.  
- Remove draft comments and perform careful editing to improve clarity and flow.  
- Overall: method is promising but the paper does not yet establish a clear advantage over simpler alternatives and requires substantial polishing.
