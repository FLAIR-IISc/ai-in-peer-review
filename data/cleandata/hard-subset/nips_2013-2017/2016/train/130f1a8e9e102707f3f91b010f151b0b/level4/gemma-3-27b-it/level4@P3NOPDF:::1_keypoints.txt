- Proposes large-scale online dictionary learning for identifying brain regions from neuroimaging; promising topic but flawed execution.  
- Major weakness: no theoretical analysis or guarantees explaining why the algorithm should work or when it generalizes.  
- Presentation is poorly written and unpolished, including leftover internal comments in the manuscript.  
- Methodology lacks clarity—update rules, scalability strategies, and computational costs are not sufficiently detailed.  
- Empirical evaluation omits comparisons to established feature-selection or dimensionality-reduction baselines.  
- Evaluation metrics are unjustified; alternatives and metric limitations are not discussed.  
- Biological interpretability is limited; identified regions aren’t convincingly linked to known brain function.  
- No analysis of parameter sensitivity or robustness of results to hyperparameter choices.  
- Recommendation: Reject; needs substantial revisions (theory, writing, and stronger empirical validation) for reconsideration.
