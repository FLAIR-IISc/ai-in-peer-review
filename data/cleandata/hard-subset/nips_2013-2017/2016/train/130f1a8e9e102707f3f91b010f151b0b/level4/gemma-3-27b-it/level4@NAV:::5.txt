This paper investigates dictionary learning applied to brain region analysis, specifically presenting a large-scale online dictionary learning framework incorporating Sobolev regularization. 

My primary concerns are outlined below:

1) The contribution appears largely as an extension of existing online dictionary learning methods (e.g., Mairal, JMLR 2010) through the addition of a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, the novelty seems limited and may not fully meet the standards for acceptance at NeurIPS.

2) While Sobolev regularization is employed to enforce structural constraints on the learned dictionary, the precise definition of this “structure” remains unclear. If the intended structure relates to sparsity, the rationale for why dictionary sparsity specifically leads to improvements in this application is not adequately explained. The motivation behind utilizing Sobolev regularization requires further clarification.

3) There appears to be a potential typographical error in Eq.(1); it should likely be represented as [V<sup>1</sup>,V<sup>2</sup>,…,V<sup>r</sup>]<sup>T</sup>.

4) The derivation and justification for Eq.(2) would benefit from more detailed explanation. Specifically, the choice of an l2 norm on the codes, as opposed to the l1 norm used in Mairal (JMLR 2010), warrants further discussion and analysis.

5) Line 146 is unclear and requires attention.

6) The manuscript would significantly benefit from careful proofreading and refinement of the language. Numerous typographical errors were noted throughout the text.
