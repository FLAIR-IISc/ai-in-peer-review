- Proposes a Sobolev-type prior for spatially regularized dictionary learning in brain imaging; motivation and visual results are appealing.  
- Main evaluation is qualitative; smoother maps are expected from the prior and may not indicate true neurobiological validity.  
- Needs quantitative metrics (held-out reconstruction/explained variance) to show regularization does not overly degrade fit.  
- Should report test–retest or split-half reliability to assess within-subject component stability.  
- Require cross-subject consistency measures (e.g., Dice, spatial correlation, clustering stability) after alignment.  
- Assess robustness/stability under resampling, bootstrap, input perturbations, and initialization seeds.  
- Demonstrate downstream utility (predicting behavioral/clinical variables) beyond visualization.  
- Clarify novelty relative to existing spatial regularizers (Laplacian/Tikhonov/TV/graph methods) and state theoretical/empirical advantages of the Sobolev term.  
- Include baselines: unregularized dictionary learning, Laplacian/TV/graph penalties, and non-dictionary methods (ICA, sparse NMF).  
- Provide ablations varying Sobolev order and weight to characterize the smoothness–accuracy trade-off.  
- Show performance across data regimes (low vs high sample sizes) and verify smoothing does not blur distinct parcels via atlas comparisons and boundary fidelity metrics.  
- Add practical details: hyperparameter selection procedure, computational complexity/runtime, scalability to high-resolution data, and robustness to preprocessing/registration choices and surface vs volume analyses.  
- Improve presentation with clearer figures, quantitative summaries and significance testing, and an explicit discussion of limitations (oversmoothing, alignment dependence).  
- Overall assessment: method is promising but currently insufficiently validated; recommendation borderline to weak reject pending stronger quantitative validation and comparisons.
