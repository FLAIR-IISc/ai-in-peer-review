This paper explores dictionary learning in the context of brain region analysis, presenting a large-scale online dictionary learning framework that incorporates Sobolev regularization for structural purposes. The authors' proposal revolves around this framework, which is also the primary focus of the paper. My key comments are as follows:

1. Upon examination, it appears that this paper builds upon existing online dictionary learning methods (J Mairal, JMLR 2010) with the addition of a Sobolev regularization term. Consequently, I believe the novelty of this work is somewhat limited and does not fully align with the standards set by NIPS.

2. The authors employ Sobolev regularization to impose structural constraints on the learned dictionary. However, the definition of "structure" in this context is not clearly articulated. If the intended structure refers to sparsity, it is unclear why the sparsity of the dictionary itself would yield significant improvements in the addressed problem. Overall, the motivation behind the use of Sobolev regularization is not adequately explained.

3. I suspect there may be an error in Eq. (1), as it should likely be represented as [V^1,V^2,…,V^r]^T.

4. Further explanation and analysis of the construction of Eq. (2) are necessary. For instance, it is unclear why the l2 norm is imposed on the codes instead of the l1 norm, as seen in J Mairal's work (JMLR 2010).

5. The content of line 146 is not specified.

6. The language and overall writing quality of the paper require refinement. Multiple typos were found throughout the manuscript.
