- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online structured sparse dictionary learning method with a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to enforce spatially smooth, compact brain regions from fMRI data.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by incorporating spatial structure while preserving online computational efficiency.  
- Addresses a key limitation of prior sparse dictionary methods that produce scattered, non-biological activation patterns.  
- Motivated by the need to define brain regions without anatomical segmentation or cross-modality correspondence.  
- Provides a clear theoretical foundation showing how the Laplacian penalty enforces spatial structure in the online learning framework.  
- Algorithmic details are well-justified, using appropriate optimization tools (e.g., FISTA) for structured subproblems.  
- Experiments on the Human Connectome Project dataset compare against relevant baselines (SODL, CanICA, PCA) across multiple metrics.  
- Results show structured dictionaries better capture known brain networks and maintain good behavioral prediction performance.  
- Spatial regularization yields substantial benefits in small-data regimes (e.g., 31% vs. 2% explained variance with 17 subjects).  
- The novelty claim is reasonable: adding a Laplacian penalty in this online context is a non-trivial, impactful extension.  
- Responses to draft-review concerns are persuasive: “structure” means spatial smoothness, X_i ≈ V U_i notation is standard, and l2 codes are justified for stability.  
- Paper is technically sound, significant for neuroimaging, and has potential broader applications in medical imaging.  
- Minor issues: some grammatical improvements could help, but the claim of many typos is overstated.  
- Suggested improvements: relate the Laplacian penalty more explicitly to neuroanatomy, compare computational complexity with other spatial methods, and clarify differences from [23].  
- Overall recommendation: meets NIPS criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance — recommend acceptance.
