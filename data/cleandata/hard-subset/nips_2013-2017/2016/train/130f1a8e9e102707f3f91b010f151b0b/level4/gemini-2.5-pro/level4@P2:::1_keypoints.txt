- Summary: Proposes Smooth-SODL, adding a Laplacian spatial-smoothness penalty to online sparse dictionary learning to produce blob-like, interpretable brain atoms at scale; evaluated on HCP against SODL and CanICA.

- Strength — Significance: Addresses an important, scalable need for data-driven, interpretable brain ROIs from large neuroimaging cohorts.

- Strength — Method: Principled combination of L1 sparsity and Laplacian smoothness integrated into an online framework with only modest extra cost (≈2–3× vs SODL).

- Strength — Empirics: Strong empirical evaluation on a large public dataset with multiple metrics (qualitative maps, explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction) and convincing learning-curve benefits of smoothing.

- Weakness — Theory: Limited theoretical novelty or analysis; no new convergence or properties analysis for the combined L1+Laplacian objective.

- Weakness — Presentation: Manuscript has proofreading issues and leftover drafting artifacts that undermine clarity and professionalism.

- Weakness — Originality: The work may be seen as incremental since it combines established techniques rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.

- Comment — Proofreading: Authors should thoroughly fix typos, grammar, and remove draft comments to improve readability.

- Comment — Algorithm details: Provide a more self-contained derivation/explanation of the BCD dictionary update (Eq. 7) for readers unfamiliar with Mairal et al. (2010).

- Comment — Theoretical discussion: Add high-level discussion on how the Laplacian penalty affects conditioning, stationary points, or optimization landscape.

- Comment — Hyperparameter selection: Clarify how baseline hyperparameters (number of components, smoothing for CanICA, sparsity for SODL) were chosen and whether they were cross-validated.

- Comment — TV vs Laplacian: Justify choice of Laplacian (L2 gradient) over Total Variation (L1 gradient) and discuss trade-offs (e.g., smooth blobs vs piecewise-constant regions with sharp edges).
