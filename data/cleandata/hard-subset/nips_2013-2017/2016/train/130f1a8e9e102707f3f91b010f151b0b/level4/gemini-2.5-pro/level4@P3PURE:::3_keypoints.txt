- Introduces a Sobolev prior for dictionary learning, but the overall impact and improvement are unclear.
- Main results rely on visual comparisons of brain-region clarity, which aren’t convincing to non-experts.
- The primary benefit—smoother, more spatially consistent components—is expected given the model’s direct smoothness penalty.
- The approach may help in data-sparse settings, but its necessity is questionable for large datasets like the Human Connectome Project.
- Evaluation would be more compelling if it measured inter-subject consistency or consolidation of regions across subjects.
- Validation is subjective and the practical advantages of the proposed method are not clearly demonstrated.
