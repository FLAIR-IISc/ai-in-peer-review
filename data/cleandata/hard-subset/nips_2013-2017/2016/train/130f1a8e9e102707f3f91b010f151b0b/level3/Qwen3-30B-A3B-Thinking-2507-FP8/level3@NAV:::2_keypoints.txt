- Proposes an online structured dictionary learning algorithm (Smooth-SODL) that adds a Sobolev/Laplacian prior to enforce spatially contiguous dictionary atoms for neuroimaging.
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning framework by incorporating a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure.
- Motivation: produce more interpretable, spatially contiguous brain-region components from fMRI than existing methods (SODL, CanICA).
- Technical foundation is sound, building on established online dictionary learning with a well-defined Laplacian regularizer and convergence guarantees.
- Algorithmic development and practical implementation details appear solid.
- Empirical evaluation is weak: visual claim that Sobolev regularization yields more structured atoms (Fig. 2) is unsupported and lacks quantitative metrics.
- Comparisons are flawed because different brain slices are shown for different methods, preventing direct visual assessment.
- Figure 3 is unclear, missing key baselines (e.g., PCA) and contains ambiguous R² claims.
- Rebuttal provided additional results that strengthen technical quality but these need to be incorporated into the paper.
- Presentation issues: inconsistent notation (notably Eqs. 6 and 8), missing HCP dataset reference, multiple typos.
- Important mathematical detail (derivation of dictionary update, Eq. 6) should be explicitly presented in the appendix.
- Novelty is modest (applying Sobolev priors to online dictionary learning), potential impact for neuroimaging is promising but not convincingly demonstrated for downstream analysis.
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions to fix empirical validation and presentation shortcomings.
