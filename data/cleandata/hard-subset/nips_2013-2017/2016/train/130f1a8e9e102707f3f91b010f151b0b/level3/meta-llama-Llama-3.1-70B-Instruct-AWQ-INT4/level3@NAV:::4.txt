This paper proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method, dubbed Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL), to extract functional components from fMRI data. The approach combines Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization within dictionary learning, which is an interesting and novel contribution. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on a large-scale fMRI dataset, showing that it extracts structured and denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method, its advantages, and its experimental results. The authors provide a thorough discussion of related work and demonstrate the superiority of their approach over state-of-the-art methods.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The experimental improvements over simpler methods are minor or unclear, and the authors do not provide sufficient explanation or analysis for the observed (lack of) performance gains. Additionally, the manuscript seems rushed and contains draft comments, which makes it sometimes tedious and confusing to read.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

Regarding clarity, the paper is well-organized, but the writing could be improved to make it more accessible to non-experts. The authors provide enough information for an expert reader to reproduce their results, but some sections could be rewritten for better readability.

The originality of the paper lies in the combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization within dictionary learning, which is a novel approach. The authors clearly explain how their work differs from previous contributions and provide adequate references to related work.

The significance of the paper is high, as it addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research. The proposed method has the potential to advance the state of the art in fMRI data analysis and provide unique insights into brain function.

**Arguments pro acceptance:**

1. Novel combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization within dictionary learning.
2. Effective extraction of structured and denoised dictionaries from fMRI data.
3. Superior performance over state-of-the-art methods.
4. Potential to advance the state of the art in fMRI data analysis.

**Arguments con acceptance:**

1. Minor or unclear experimental improvements over simpler methods.
2. Insufficient explanation or analysis for the observed (lack of) performance gains.
3. Rushed manuscript with draft comments.
4. Limited discussion of potential limitations and future work.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend accepting this paper, but with minor revisions to address the limitations mentioned above. The authors should provide more analysis and explanation for the observed performance gains and revise the manuscript to make it more readable and polished.
