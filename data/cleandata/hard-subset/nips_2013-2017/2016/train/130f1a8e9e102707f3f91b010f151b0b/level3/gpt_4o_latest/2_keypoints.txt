- Introduces a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to enforce spatial smoothness in Sparse Online Dictionary Learning, a principled and original algorithmic contribution for neuroimaging.  
- Method retains SODL’s computational efficiency and scales to large neuroimaging datasets, addressing a key limitation of existing spatially regularized methods.  
- Broad potential impact beyond neuroimaging (e.g., image segmentation, regional sensing), making the approach widely useful.  
- Authors responded to rebuttal feedback and provided additional results that clarified technical and empirical aspects.  
- Empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior over SODL and CanICA is not consistently demonstrated; visual claims of “more structured” results are unconvincing (Figure 2 issues).  
- Manuscript suffers from numerous typos, unclear matrix notation, inconsistent equations, and problematic figures (missing legends, poor explanations).  
- Evaluation relies heavily on qualitative assessment and a limited set of quantitative metrics (explained variance, stability, R²) without statistical significance testing.  
- Performance appears highly sensitive to hyperparameters (γ, τ), and hyperparameter tuning/documentation is insufficient, harming reproducibility.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, efficient approach to structured dictionary learning with strong applicability and interpretability benefits.  
- Argument against acceptance: empirical results do not robustly support superiority, presentation quality is poor, and evaluation lacks depth.  
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on substantial improvements to presentation and stronger, more comprehensive empirical validation.
