- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning for fMRI combining L1 sparsity and Laplacian spatial smoothness to extract structured brain regions.  
- Builds on Mairal et al.'s SODL, adding a Laplacian penalty while preserving computational efficiency.  
- Demonstrated on HCP fMRI data, extracting functional maps that capture inter-subject variability.  
- Empirical results are thorough and compelling, showing advantages over SODL and CanICA on explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Validated across small, medium, and large-scale regimes, showing practical utility for neuroimaging.  
- Novel contribution by combining spatial structure with online dictionary learning, extending prior structured dictionary work.  
- Major theoretical weakness: non-convex formulation lacks proofs of solution existence or uniqueness.  
- No theoretical convergence guarantees for the block coordinate descent–based algorithm; reliance is primarily empirical.  
- Manuscript clarity issue was addressed in rebuttal (line 146), but theoretical gaps remain.  
- Overall significant and practically valuable for neuroimaging; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address theoretical limitations.
