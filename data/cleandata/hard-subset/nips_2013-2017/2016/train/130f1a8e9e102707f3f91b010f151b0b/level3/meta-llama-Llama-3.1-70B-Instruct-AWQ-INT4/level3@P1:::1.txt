The paper "Learning brain regions via large-scale online structured sparse dictionary learning" proposes a novel approach for identifying interesting brain regions using a large-scale online dictionary learning framework. The authors extend the standard online dictionary-learning framework to incorporate spatial structure, allowing for the extraction of structured and sparse components from brain images.

The main idea of the paper is to use a multivariate online dictionary-learning method to obtain decompositions with structured and sparse components, which are more interpretable and better capture inter-subject variability in small, medium, and large-scale regimes. The authors propose a penalized dictionary-learning model that combines an `1 penalty for sparsity with a Laplacian penalty for smoothness, resulting in a more robust and efficient algorithm.

The paper is well-motivated, and the topic is interesting and potentially valuable. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem and the proposed approach, and the manuscript is well-organized. The experimental results are promising, showing that the proposed method outperforms state-of-the-art methods in terms of explained variance and stability.

However, the manuscript lacks theoretical analysis to demonstrate the proposed algorithm's effectiveness. While the authors provide some intuition and empirical results, a more rigorous theoretical analysis would strengthen the paper. Additionally, the manuscript quality is poor, with careless writing and leftover comments indicating that the authors did not proofread the manuscript thoroughly.

Overall, the paper has potential, but it requires significant revisions to address the theoretical analysis and manuscript quality issues.
