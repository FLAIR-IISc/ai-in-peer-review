- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online structured dictionary learning method using a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms for neuroimaging.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning and aims for scalable extraction of interpretable spatial features.  
- Mathematical formulation is largely sound but contains inconsistencies, ambiguous notation, and unclear algorithmic details.  
- Convergence claim (citing Bertsekas 1999) is plausible but needs clearer assumptions and limitations given the Laplacian penalty.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak and fails to convincingly show advantages of the proposed method.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor: confusing descriptions, many typos, inconsistent notation, and hard-to-follow Algorithm 2.  
- Originality is moderate: novelty in combining online learning with a Sobolev prior for neuroimaging, but not fully positioned against prior spatial regularization work.  
- Significance is promising: scalable, potentially useful for brain region delineation, functional networks, and large datasets (e.g., HCP).  
- Strength: scalability afforded by the online learning framework.  
- Strength: incorporation of a spatial prior promoting interpretable, contiguous atoms.  
- Strength: logical extension of established online dictionary learning methods with potential broad applicability.  
- Weakness: Figures (especially Figure 2) do not allow fair visual comparison and do not substantiate claims of greater structure.  
- Weakness: No quantitative metrics provided to evaluate “structuredness” or spatial coherence of atoms.  
- Weakness: Algorithmic ambiguity—Equation (6) lacks explicit solution or derivation.  
- Weakness: Mathematical errors/inconsistencies in Equations (6) and (8) and update steps.  
- Weakness: Missing reference to the Human Connectome Project dataset and numerous typographical errors.  
- Requested fixes: provide derivation/algorithm for Equation (6), include quantitative smoothness/structure metrics, and standardize figure slices for fair comparison.  
- Requested fixes: clarify Figure 3 (predicted variable, features used, baselines such as PCA), correct mathematical notation, add missing HCP citation, and integrate rebuttal results.  
- Recommendation: reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit after strengthening empirical validation, fixing clarity/technical issues, and incorporating rebuttal results.
