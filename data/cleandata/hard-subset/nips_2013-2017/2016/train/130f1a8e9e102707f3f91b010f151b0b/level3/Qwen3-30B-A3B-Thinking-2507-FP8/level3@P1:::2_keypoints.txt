- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning extension with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce spatially contiguous dictionary atoms.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) SODL framework, retaining online scalability.  
- Aims to address neuroimaging challenges like inter-subject variability and ROI definition by producing blob-like atoms.  
- Main contribution is a technically sound spatial regularization added to dictionary learning.  
- Algorithm maintains scalability (tested up to 500 subjects, 260k voxels) with ~2–3× runtime increase.  
- Theoretical foundation and convergence guarantees follow established frameworks and are solid.  
- Application to HCP fMRI data is relevant and timely for neuroimaging research.  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, well-motivated extension, practical implementation details, and interpretable atoms that appear to better capture inter-subject variability than SODL and CanICA.  
- Computational efficiency is a notable practical strength for large-scale neuroimaging.  
- Major empirical weakness: Figure 2’s visual comparisons are misleading (different slices shown), preventing direct comparison and not quantitatively proving the Sobolev prior’s benefit.  
- Lacks quantitative metrics to support “structured” atoms (e.g., spatial coherence, blob compactness, anatomical correspondence).  
- Mathematical presentation issues: inconsistent notation and unclear transpose placements in Equations (6) and (8); ridge regression solution ambiguity.  
- Recommendation to include a derivation of the ridge solution in the appendix to clarify equations.  
- Missing a citation/reference to the Human Connectome Project (HCP) dataset in the main text.  
- Presentation problems: multiple typos, inconsistent phrasing (e.g., “Graph-Net penalty can imposes”), and editing errors reducing readability.  
- Figure 3 caption is insufficient: baselines (PCA) not explained and the meaning of R^2 > 0.1 claims is unclear.  
- Paper fails to clearly explain the prediction task in Figure 3 and how it connects to the dictionary learning framework.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental given prior spatially-structured dictionary work, but the online/efficient extension is valuable.  
- Significance: potential to improve ROI extraction in neuroimaging and applicability to other imaging domains like segmentation.  
- Technical quality is good but would be strengthened by addressing empirical evaluation gaps and fixing mathematical/presentation issues.  
- Authors provided additional rebuttal results that should be integrated into the final manuscript.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions to empirical evaluation, quantitative metrics, mathematical clarity, and presentation.
