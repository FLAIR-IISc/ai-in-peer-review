This paper proposes a large-scale online dictionary learning framework to identify interesting brain regions from functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data. The framework, called Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL), combines sparsity and spatial regularization to extract structured and interpretable dictionaries that capture inter-subject variability.

The topic of the paper is interesting and potentially valuable, as it addresses the challenge of analyzing large-scale brain imaging data. The proposed framework is an extension of the online dictionary learning work of Mairal et al. (2010), and it incorporates a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on the dictionary atoms. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on a large public dataset, showing improvements over traditional `1-regularized dictionary learning.

However, the paper lacks theoretical analysis to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm. While the authors provide some insights into the optimization problem and the convergence of the algorithm, they do not provide a rigorous analysis of the algorithm's performance. This is a significant limitation, as it makes it difficult to understand the strengths and weaknesses of the approach.

Furthermore, the manuscript quality is poor, with careless writing and leftover comments (e.g., "146 XXX: I don't understand the switch between V and v.") that indicate the authors did not proofread the manuscript carefully. This lack of attention to detail is disappointing, as it detracts from the overall quality of the paper.
