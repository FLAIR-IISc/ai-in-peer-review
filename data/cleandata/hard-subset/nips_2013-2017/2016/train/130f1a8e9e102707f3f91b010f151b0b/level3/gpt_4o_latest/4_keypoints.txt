- Introduces Smooth-SODL: combines Sobolev (Laplacian) and L1 regularization to extract sparse, spatially smooth fMRI components.
- Extends Mairal et al.'s SODL framework to incorporate spatial regularization at minor additional computational cost.
- Evaluated on Human Connectome Project task fMRI, showing improved interpretability, stability, and capture of inter-subject variability versus CanICA and unstructured SODL.
- Originality: novel integration of Sobolev regularization into online dictionary learning, addressing a gap in neuroimaging methods.
- Scalability: computationally efficient and suitable for large neuroimaging datasets.
- Interpretability: produces more interpretable and denoised dictionary atoms qualitatively and quantitatively.
- Comprehensive evaluation: assessed across explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction metrics.
- Incremental contribution: gains over simpler methods (e.g., SODL) are modest and not clearly transformative.
- Clarity and presentation issues: manuscript is poorly organized, contains draft comments, and key choices (e.g., hyperparameters) are underexplained.
- Insufficient explanation of improvements: lacks in-depth discussion of mechanisms behind stability and behavioral prediction gains.
- Limited significance: practical impact on the field is unclear given incremental improvements versus added complexity.
- Required revisions for acceptance: improve organization, remove draft comments, provide deeper analysis of improvements, and clarify practical significance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel spatial+sparsity constraint combination, scalability, and improved interpretability.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental advance, poor clarity/presentation, and insufficient discussion of broader significance.
- Overall: technically sound with potential but requires substantial revisions to meet conference standards.
