- Proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method (Smooth-SODL) combining a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty and L1 sparsity to extract structured, sparse brain-image components.  
- Main originality is combining Sobolev and L1 penalties within an online dictionary-learning framework with only a minor computational overhead.  
- Provides clear background and motivation, highlighting limitations of existing ROI-definition approaches in neuroimaging.  
- Presents a detailed description of the optimization problem and algorithm, extending Mairal et al. (2010).  
- Experiments run on a large public HCP dataset, comparing Smooth-SODL to SODL and CanICA.  
- Evaluation is thorough, using qualitative visuals and quantitative metrics (explained variance, atom stability, behavioral prediction).  
- Results indicate smoother, denoised, more interpretable dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability across scales.  
- Experimental improvements over simpler methods are minor or unclear, with insufficient analysis of why gains are limited.  
- Manuscript appears rushed and contains draft comments, harming readability and clarity.  
- More details requested on the optimization algorithm and implementation specifics.  
- Writing should include clearer explanations and examples for non-experts.  
- More discussion needed on how the method differs from prior work and its practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve explanations, provide additional analyses, and clean up the manuscript.
