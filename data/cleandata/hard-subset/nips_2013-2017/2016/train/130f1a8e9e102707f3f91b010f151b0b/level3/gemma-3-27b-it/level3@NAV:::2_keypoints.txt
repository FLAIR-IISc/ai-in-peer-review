- Proposes Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary learning algorithm with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms for neuroimaging.
- Motivation is well-grounded: leverages inter-subject variability to define principled ROIs, addressing neuroimaging needs.
- Builds on established methods (ICA, PCA) aiming for improved interpretability via sparsity and spatial structure.
- Technical foundation uses block coordinate descent and existing optimization techniques, appearing solid overall.
- Initial technical assessment was marginally below average, though the rebuttal improved the technical rating.
- Presentation suffers from inconsistencies and typos (matrix transpose placement, issues in Equations (6) and (8)) that hinder clarity.
- Missing citation to the Human Connectome Project (HCP) dataset is a notable omission.
- Rebuttal added results that improved evaluation; those results should be fully integrated into the revised manuscript.
- Primary empirical weakness: lack of compelling quantitative evidence that the Sobolev prior yields clear advantages.
- Figure 2 fails to provide quantitative metrics or direct visual comparison (different slices shown), making the “more structured” claim subjective.
- Baseline explanation is incomplete (PCA omitted); prediction target and the practical significance of R^2 > 0.1 are unclear.
- Reviewer requests explicit, neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative measures of “structuredness” (e.g., spatial autocorrelation, compactness).
- Need to include the algorithmic solution to Equation (6) and correct mathematical inconsistencies.
- Arguments for acceptance: potentially valuable neuroimaging tool with scalable online learning and applications to segmentation/regional sensing.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical support for the Sobolev prior, unclear presentation, and remaining mathematical/typographical issues.
- Overall verdict: interesting and promising approach but requires substantial revision to empirical validation, clarity, and technical correctness before acceptance.
