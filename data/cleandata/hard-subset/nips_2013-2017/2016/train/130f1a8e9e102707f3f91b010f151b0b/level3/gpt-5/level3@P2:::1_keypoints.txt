- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with sparse, Laplacian-smoothed atoms for large-scale brain imaging.
- Novelty: integrates Laplacian prior into online dictionary learning with per-atom proximal updates, targeting scalable cohort-level mapping.
- Strength: tackles a timely problem of interpretable, spatially localized components for population neuroimaging.
- Strength: sensible algorithmic design using convex per-atom subproblems and standard proximal solvers with modest extra complexity over SODL.
- Strength: empirical results on HCP (n=500, p≈260k, k=40) show improved interpretability, stability, and learning-curve insights favoring spatial regularization in low-data regimes.
- Strength: practical hyperparameter guidance, implementation details, and planned open-source integration increase potential impact.
- Weakness: lacks tailored theoretical analysis for the online algorithm with Laplacian constraints and time-varying γt; no convergence or regret guarantees.
- Weakness: implausible claims about FISTA convergence speed and heuristic γt schedule without supporting proofs or justification.
- Weakness: limited and potentially unfair evaluation — single HCP task, missing key baselines (TV-L1, GraphNet, structured SPCA/NMF, recent online structured methods).
- Weakness: preprocessing/method mismatch (CanICA smoothed but SODL variants not), complicating comparisons; behavior prediction favors CanICA on test data.
- Weakness: manuscript contains many typos, leftover editorial comments, inconsistent notation, and unclear figure/table references that undermine confidence.
- Weakness: insufficient documentation of implementation specifics (Laplacian boundary conditions, FFT acceleration applicability, constraints per experiment, code availability).
- Weakness: methodological choices (simplex vs L1-ball, ridge vs L1 codes) are not consistently justified and lack ablation studies.
- Quality: technically plausible steps but partial soundness — online-theory gaps and anecdotal runtime claims weaken the technical contribution.
- Clarity: below conference standards; needs thorough proofreading, clearer algorithm presentation, and expanded experimental details.
- Originality: incremental but useful — combining Laplacian smoothing with online dictionary learning is underexplored though related to existing spatial penalties.
- Significance: potentially impactful for large-scale neuroimaging and ROI discovery if code is released and evaluations broadened.
- Suggestions: provide convergence analysis for the online setting, clarify γt/αt roles, and justify optimization claims.
- Suggestions: expand baselines (TV-L1, GraphNet, structured SPCA/NMF), align preprocessing, and test additional datasets (other HCP tasks, resting-state).
- Suggestions: report reproducibility metrics (spatial overlap), ablations (ridge vs L1, simplex vs L1-ball), hyperparameter sensitivity, and matching details for stability evaluation.
- Suggestions: clarify Laplacian construction, FFT use, warm-starts, stopping criteria, and actual FISTA iterations; release code.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important scalable problem, improves interpretability/stability, and has community utility if polished.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient theory, limited/unfair empirical comparison, and poor presentation quality.
- Recommendation: weak reject — promising idea but needs stronger theory, broader/fairer evaluation, and a polished manuscript.
- Reviewer confidence: medium.
