- Proposal: Smooth-SODL — online structured sparse dictionary learning with ℓ1/simplex atom constraints and a Sobolev/Laplacian penalty producing piecewise‑smooth, compact “blob-like” atoms via alternating ridge-coded updates and block-coordinate atom updates using FISTA.

- Scalability: Designed for large n and p with reported ~2–3× runtime overhead vs unstructured online DL; evaluated on HCP task fMRI against online DL and CanICA with maps, EV, split‑half stability, runtime, and behavioral prediction.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) and large-scale matrix factorization; contrasts to TV‑ℓ1/GraphNet and ICA/PCA methods; using a quadratic smoothness term to enable fast first‑order inner loops is sensible and practically novel in online DL.

- Modeling/optimization quality: Formulation and optimization (online BCD, ridge codes, proximal‑FISTA for Laplacian quadratic) are standard, appropriate, and convergence claims are plausible given separate convexity and compact constraints.

- Empirical insufficiency: Evidence that the Laplacian prior improves scientific utility is underquantified — “more structured and interpretable” is largely asserted, not robustly measured.

- Notation/clarity issues: Several notational inconsistencies and unclear variable references (notably around Eqs. (6) and (8)) hinder verification and reproducibility.

- Manuscript clarity: Readable in parts but hampered by typos, inconsistent notation, ambiguous algorithmic definitions, and poorly standardized figure design choices that complicate comparisons.

- Rebuttal: Authors addressed some issues and provided additional results; these should be integrated into the revised manuscript.

- Originality: Embedding a spatial Sobolev/Laplacian prior in an online DL framework to enable efficient updates is a nontrivial, novel, and practically relevant extension.

- Significance: If convincingly demonstrated, the method could be a useful tool for data‑driven ROI discovery and dimensionality reduction across imaging modalities, but current evidence stops short of proving clear advantages.

- Strength: Solid, scalable formulation with clear computational benefits and modest overhead relative to unstructured online DL.

- Strength: Sensible Laplacian penalty that promotes spatially compact atoms without costly TV optimization.

- Strength: Experiments on large public data with split‑half stability, EV, prediction metrics, and runtime comparisons; rebuttal improved technical completeness.

- Weakness: Lacks quantitative metrics for spatial structure/interpretability (e.g., atlas overlap, compactness metrics); visuals are not standardized.

- Weakness: Algorithmic/notation errors (Eqs. (6)/(8), variable naming) reduce clarity and hinder reproducibility.

- Weakness: Behavioral prediction experiment lacks methodological clarity (features, baselines, CV protocol) and omits key baselines like PCA.

- Weakness: Typos, editing problems, and some missing or unclear citations throughout the text.

- Revision request — spatial quantification: Add metrics like number of connected components, average cluster size, compactness (perimeter/area), Laplacian energy v^TΔv, TV norm, Moran’s I, and atlas Dice/correlation across thresholds.

- Revision request — figures: Standardize slices, color scales, thresholds (and show unthresholded maps), consistently match atoms across methods, and report per‑atom stability/overlap.

- Revision request — behavioral prediction: Clarify whether regressors are U_i or raw maps, standardize k across methods, include PCA baseline, use nested CV, and report test R^2 with confidence intervals and target definitions.

- Revision request — algorithmic details: Provide derivation of Eq. (6), per‑minibatch complexity, precise definitions for Eq. (8) quantities (A_t[j,j], A_jt, B_jt, V_j), fix transpose/sign typos, give exact FISTA update, step size (L = 1+γ̃λ_max(Δ)), stopping criteria, and projection operator details for C.

- Revision request — hyperparameter/schedule justification: Justify α_t ~ t^-1/2 and γ_t constraints; provide sensitivity analyses and recommended parameter ranges.

- Revision request — presentation and references: Cite HCP in‑text at first mention, include GraphNet/structured PCA refs appropriately, and fix typos/grammar throughout.

- Acceptance arguments: Scalable, principled extension of online DL with practical utility; conceptually clear and technically mature; rebuttal shows responsiveness.

- Rejection arguments: Current empirical evidence does not convincingly demonstrate neuroscientific advantages; clarity/notation issues impede reproducibility; figures and metrics do not support interpretability claims.

- Recommendation: Weak reject (borderline); with requested quantitative analyses and clarity fixes the paper could become a strong contribution — reviewer confidence: medium.
