- Proposes an online structured sparse dictionary learning method for fMRI by adding a Sobolev/Laplacian penalty to obtain sparse, spatially structured atoms.
- Integrates Laplacian smoothness into the SODL (Mairal et al.) framework with near-constant computational overhead.
- Optimization uses online block-coordinate updates with sufficient statistics (A, B); atom updates solved as a strongly convex denoising subproblem via FISTA inside BCD; codes via ridge regression.
- Method scales to large n and p and reports runtime overhead ≈2–3× relative to SODL.
- Experiments evaluate runtime, explained variance, stability/reproducibility, and behavioral prediction on HCP task fMRI, comparing to SODL and CanICA.
- Strength: fills a practical gap by providing a scalable, principled structured decomposition approach for neuroimaging.
- Strength: solid optimization framing with careful discussion of Lipschitz constants and regularization scaling.
- Strength: approach may generalize to other tasks (segmentation, ROI discovery) and code release could aid adoption.
- Weakness: claims of “more structured, meaningful” atoms rely on visual inspection with inconsistent slices and thresholding—no convincing quantitative evidence.
- Weakness: neuroscientific relevance of “structure” is not operationalized; lacks metrics like contiguity, compactness, atlas overlap, connected components.
- Weakness: confusing/inconsistent mathematical notation and numerous typos (notably in Eqs. (6) and (8) and variable names) impede clarity.
- Weakness: baselines and figures are inconsistently presented (PCA missing in prediction plots; differing smoothing choices and slices across methods).
- Technical quality: optimization formulation is sound, but paper should state convergence results for time-varying αt, γt and minibatches, and clarify whether γ is fixed or adaptive.
- Technical quality: must report FISTA stopping criteria, tolerances, and iteration counts to substantiate “1–2 iterations suffice.”
- Technical quality: explicitly derive the ridge coding step (Eq. 6), show SVD-based efficiency and computational cost, and discuss numerical stability.
- Clarity: fix typos/notation, define all symbols at first use, and recheck signs/indices/matrix dimensions in key equations.
- Figures: show identical anatomical slices, color scales, and consistent/no thresholding across methods; include PCA and clear legends.
- Requested quantitative evaluations: connected components per atom, compactness (radius of gyration), Moran’s I/spatial autocorrelation, Laplacian energy, TV, and proportion of mass in largest component.
- Requested reproducibility analyses: split-half stability with optimal matching (e.g., Hungarian), report mean/median correlations with CIs and permutation tests.
- Requested neuroscientific validation: Dice/IoU overlap with standard atlases (AAL, Yeo, Glasser MMP) and task ROIs with multiple-comparisons correction.
- Requested experimental controls: ensure consistent preprocessing (smoothed vs unsmoothed) across methods and match component dimensionality and regularization for prediction comparisons.
- References: properly cite the HCP dataset in-text and complete missing/partial bibliographic entries.
- Recommendation: Borderline — leaning weak reject unless authors add requested quantitative spatial evaluations, clarify algorithmic details (Eqs. 6/8, stopping criteria, αt/γ), ensure fair baselines/visualizations, and fix presentation issues.
