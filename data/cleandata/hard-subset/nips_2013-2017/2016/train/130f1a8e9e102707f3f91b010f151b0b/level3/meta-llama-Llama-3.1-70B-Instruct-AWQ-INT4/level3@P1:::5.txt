The paper "Learning brain regions via large-scale online structured sparse dictionary learning" proposes a novel approach to extract brain regions from functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data using online dictionary learning with Sobolev regularization. The authors extend the online dictionary learning framework of Mairal et al. (2010) by incorporating a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on the dictionary atoms. This approach is designed to capture the variability of brain activity across subjects and to provide a more interpretable representation of brain function.

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of the Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL) algorithm, which combines the benefits of online dictionary learning with the spatial regularization provided by the Laplacian penalty. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on a large public dataset, showing that their method extracts structured and denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability in small, medium, and large-scale regimes.

However, there are several concerns regarding the paper's novelty, motivation, and presentation. Firstly, the novelty of the approach is weak, as it builds upon existing work on online dictionary learning with minimal modifications. The addition of Sobolev regularization may not be sufficient to meet the NIPS standards for novelty and impact.

Furthermore, the motivation for using Sobolev regularization is unclear, and the paper does not provide a clear definition of "structure" or justify why dictionary sparsity improves the problem. The authors should provide a more detailed explanation of the benefits of Sobolev regularization and how it addresses the limitations of existing approaches.
