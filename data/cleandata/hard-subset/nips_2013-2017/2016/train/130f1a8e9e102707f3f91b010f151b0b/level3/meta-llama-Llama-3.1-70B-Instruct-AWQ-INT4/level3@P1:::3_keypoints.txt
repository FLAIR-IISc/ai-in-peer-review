- Proposes Smooth-SODL: a multivariate online dictionary-learning method with a Sobolev prior to enforce spatially structured, sparse dictionary atoms.
- Method extends standard online dictionary-learning by incorporating spatial smoothness via a Sobolev prior.
- Demonstrated on a large public dataset, showing more structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability.
- Paper is well-written and explanations of method and principles are clear.
- Use of a Sobolev prior is interesting and yields more interpretable brain regions.
- Impact and improvement over existing methods are unclear and hard to quantify.
- Main results rely on visual comparisons of brain “blobs,” which may be subjective and unclear to non-experts.
- Data quality and dataset choice strongly affect results; smoothing predictably produces smoother blobs.
- With very large datasets (e.g., HCP), smoothing may not address any real data scarcity issue.
- Validation is subjective; lacks rigorous quantitative metrics such as consolidated brain-region differences or inter-subject consistency measures.
- Originality: novel extension to online dictionary learning, but Sobolev prior usage is not entirely new and needs more contextual discussion.
- Significance: addresses an important neuroimaging problem and has potential broader applications (e.g., computer vision).
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.
- Acceptance arguments: novel framework extension, potential scientific impact, clear presentation.
- Rejection arguments: unclear impact, subjective/insufficient validation, and lack of rigorous cross-subject consolidation and consistency analysis.
