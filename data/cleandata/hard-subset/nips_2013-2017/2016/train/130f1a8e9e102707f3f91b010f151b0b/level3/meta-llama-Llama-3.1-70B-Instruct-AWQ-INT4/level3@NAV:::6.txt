This paper proposes a new regularizer for matrix factorization that enforces sparsity and smoothness, called Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL). The method is designed for large-scale online structured sparse dictionary learning and is applied to brain imaging data. The authors provide an algorithm for obtaining a solution, but the existence and uniqueness of a solution are not proven. The problem is non-convex, and the algorithm lacks theoretical guarantees.

The experimental results demonstrate the superiority of Smooth-SODL over state-of-the-art methods, such as Canonical ICA (CanICA) and Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (SODL). The proposed method extracts structured and denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability in small, medium, and large-scale regimes. The evaluation relies primarily on empirical evidence rather than theoretical analysis.

Strengths of the paper:

* The proposed method is novel and extends the online dictionary-learning framework to learn structured regions representative of brain organization.
* The experimental results show significant improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
* The method is scalable and can handle large-scale data.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The problem is non-convex, and the algorithm lacks theoretical guarantees.
* The existence and uniqueness of a solution are not proven.
* The evaluation relies primarily on empirical evidence rather than theoretical analysis.
* The manuscript clarity needs improvement, particularly at line 146.
