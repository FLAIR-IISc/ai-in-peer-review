- Introduces Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) spatial-smoothing penalty and l1-ball atom sparsity for brain imaging ROI discovery.  
- Aim: provide data-driven ROIs that capture inter-subject variability in large-scale fMRI cohorts.  
- Methodologically extends Mairal et al. (2010) by adding a Laplacian penalty to encourage spatially smooth, contiguous "blob" atoms.  
- Enforces sparsity via an l1-ball on atoms and uses an l2 (ridge) penalty on subject codes; online algorithm ensures scalability.  
- Evaluated on HCP data against SODL and CanICA, showing more interpretable, stable, and predictive components, especially in low-data regimes.  
- Strength: tackles a significant, well-motivated neuroimaging problem with a population-level approach to ROI learning.  
- Strength: scalable to very large datasets with only a modest (2–3×) computational overhead from the spatial regularizer.  
- Strength: thorough empirical validation (visuals, explained variance, stability, downstream prediction) and a learning-curve showing a 14× small-sample explained-variance gain.  
- Weakness: limited methodological novelty—main contribution is combining existing online dictionary learning with a standard Laplacian regularizer.  
- Weakness: weak justification for the Laplacian penalty over alternatives (e.g., TV) and insufficient discussion of why codes use l2 rather than l1 sparsity.  
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies and ambiguous variable definitions (e.g., Xi described as a row vector but used as a column).  
- Weakness: poor writing quality with numerous typos and grammatical issues that hinder readability.  
- Detailed requests: clarify Xi dimensions in Eq.(1), provide a more self-contained/intuitive derivation of the dictionary update, and offer practical heuristics for selecting γ and τ beyond generic cross-validation.  
- Overall: practically useful and experimentally convincing but needs stronger novelty, clearer modeling justification, and substantial writing revisions; more appropriate for an applied venue than a top ML conference.
