- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian spatial regularizer to learn compact, piecewise-smooth brain regions.  
- Key innovation: spatial regularization yields more interpretable, less scattered dictionary atoms than standard SODL.  
- Empirical demonstration on HCP fMRI shows improved interpretability and capture of inter-subject variability versus SODL and CanICA.  
- Claims computational efficiency: only 2–3× slower than standard SODL, suitable for large-scale neuroimaging.  
- Method reportedly works across small, medium, and large datasets.  
- Manuscript contains numerous proofreading errors and unclear writing (e.g., inconsistent notation like switching between "V" and "v").  
- Lacks theoretical analysis: no convergence guarantees, no discussion of optimization landscape, and no formal treatment of the Laplacian penalty’s effect.  
- No justification for algorithmic choices (e.g., “1 or 2 iterations of FISTA are sufficient” is unsupported).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough but lacks theoretical grounding and broader parameter exploration.  
- Results are reported on a single dataset (HCP) only, limiting generality.  
- Comparison with CanICA is problematic because CanICA uses smoothing preprocessing while Smooth-SODL does not.  
- No statistical significance testing reported for the observed improvements.  
- Relationship between the Laplacian penalty and spatial structure is not clearly explained; supplementary materials are inadequately integrated.  
- Originality limited: approach is a straightforward extension of prior online dictionary learning and resembles existing spatial regularization techniques.  
- Significance limited: qualitative interpretability gains are not rigorously validated or linked to known neuroanatomy.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound algorithm but undermined by poor writing and missing theoretical/rigorous evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Reject; revise with theoretical guarantees or substantially improved clarity and more rigorous experimental validation before resubmission.
