- Introduces Smooth-SODL: an online structured sparse dictionary learning algorithm applying a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to encourage spatially contiguous, smooth atoms for neuroimaging.
- Novelty is a practical, relevant combination of known online dictionary learning with a spatial regularizer for neuroscience applications.
- Conceptually sound and potentially impactful; could provide a scalable tool to extract structured components from large neuroimaging datasets.
- Technical approach follows established optimization frameworks and rebuttal results clarified some methodological concerns.
- Empirical validation is weak: insufficient quantitative evidence that the Sobolev prior yields meaningful improvements.
- Lack of neuroscience-justified metrics for assessing atom "structure" or interpretability.
- Figure 2 is misleading: different slices used across methods prevent direct visual comparison and weaken the visual evidence.
- Manuscript presentation is poor: numerous typos, confusing descriptions, and formatting errors that impede understanding.
- Mathematical inconsistencies and unclear notation, including possible sign/term errors in Equations (6) and (8) and unclear variable references.
- Reproducibility issues: need a detailed derivation for the solution to Equation (6).
- Missing reference for the HCP dataset.
- Figure 3 is unclear about the prediction task, baselines used, and which results support the paper’s claims.
- Recommendation: major revision required to strengthen empirical evidence, clarify figures/claims, fix notation and presentation, and integrate rebuttal results.
