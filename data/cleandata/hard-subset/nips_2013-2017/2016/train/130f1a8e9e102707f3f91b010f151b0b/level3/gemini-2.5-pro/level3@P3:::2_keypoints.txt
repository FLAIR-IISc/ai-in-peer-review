- Addresses an important neuroimaging problem; scalable, interpretable component extraction could impact functional atlas creation and other domains.  
- Algorithmic scalability: online framework handles massive datasets with only ~2–3x extra compute.  
- Novelty: combines Sobolev (Laplacian) prior with online dictionary learning to enforce spatial smoothness.  

- Empirical support is weak; central claim that the Sobolev prior yields more meaningful atoms is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Figure 2 is unconvincing because different axial slices are shown across methods and no quantitative metrics (compactness, contiguity, smoothness) are provided.  
- Figure 3 (prediction task) is poorly explained: unclear input features, incomplete legend (PCA missing), and ambiguous reporting of R^2 results.  

- Notational and mathematical clarity issues: inconsistent vector/matrix dimensions (Xi, Xt, Ut), Eq.(6) appears incorrect, and Eq.(8)/VAj are ambiguous.  
- Key algorithmic details missing: no formal citation for HCP, and no explicit solution or algorithmic steps for solving Eq.(7).  
- Presentation is marred by numerous typos and proofreading errors that impede readability.  

- Rebuttal added useful quantitative results and visuals, improving the technical rating from 2 to 3; these should be incorporated into the revised paper.  

- Overall: promising and sound core idea but currently hampered by weak experiments, technical ambiguities, and poor presentation; substantial revision needed to be a solid contribution.
