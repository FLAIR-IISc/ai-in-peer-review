- Proposes an online structured dictionary learning algorithm using a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms for neuroimaging data.
- Main contribution is incorporating Sobolev priors to extend prior structured dictionary learning work.
- Potentially useful for neuroscience/medical imaging and other tasks like image segmentation and regional sensing.
- Method is technically sound and follows a well-established modeling and algorithmic framework.
- Initial technical rating was marginally below average.
- Paper needs neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative metrics to show atoms are more structured and meaningful.
- Empirical support is weak and does not clearly demonstrate the advantage of the Sobolev prior.
- Figure 2 fails to quantify or clearly show how the proposed method outperforms alternatives.
- Rebuttal provided additional results that improved the technical quality rating.
- Presentation quality is poor: confusing descriptions, typos, and editing issues impede understanding.
- Mathematical inconsistencies and possible typos in matrix transpose placement, signs/terms in Equations (6) and (8), and unclear variable references in updates.
- Missing references, notably the HCP dataset citation.
- Algorithmic detail is lacking; the explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6) is requested.
- Figure 2 and Figure 3 have issues: different slices used across methods hinder direct comparison, and legends/captions are unclear.
- Minor typos and stray comments throughout the manuscript need fixing.
- With revisions addressing these issues, the paper has potential to become a strong contribution.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, potential utility in neuroimaging, and comprehensive related-work review.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical evidence, unclear empirical advantage of the prior, poor presentation, and missing references/inconsistencies.
- Recommendation: revise to add neuroscience-justified metrics, stronger experiments/comparisons, fix presentation and typos, add missing refs, provide explicit solution to Eq (6), and improve figures for direct comparison.
