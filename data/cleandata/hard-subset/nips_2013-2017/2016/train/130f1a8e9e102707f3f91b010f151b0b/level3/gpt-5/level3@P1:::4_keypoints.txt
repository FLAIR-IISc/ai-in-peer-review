- Proposal: online under-complete dictionary learning with atom-level L1 sparsity and Laplacian/Sobolev smoothness to produce compact, blob-like ROIs.  
- Algorithm: ridge regression codes, online sufficient statistics, per-atom BCD updates solved with a few FISTA iterations; extends Mairal et al. (2010) by adding a Laplacian penalty.  
- Experiments: tested on HCP task fMRI (n=500, p≈2.6e5, k=40) against SODL and CanICA; reports modest interpretability/stability gains and 2–3x runtime overhead.  
- Relation to prior work: incremental relative to existing GraphNet/TV-ℓ1 spatial regularizers and online DL; novelty is mainly the Laplacian inside an online DL loop and scalability.  
- Strength: clear modeling goal—sparsity + smoothness to yield interpretable ROIs.  
- Strength: sensible algorithmic design (ridge codes for stability, standard online updates, proximal per-atom FISTA).  
- Strength: demonstrated scalability to full-brain data with practical runtimes on a single CPU.  
- Strength: empirical evaluation covers stability, explained variance, qualitative maps, behavioral prediction, and learning curves.  
- Weakness: empirical gains are modest/unclear—test explained variance similar to SODL and dominated by CanICA; reported stability improvements lack matching-protocol details and CIs.  
- Weakness: tCanICA baseline under-specified (threshold selection and reproducibility not described), risking biased comparisons.  
- Weakness: claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e-6 accuracy is not credible without reporting inner-loop tolerances, iteration counts, and time distribution.  
- Weakness: time-varying αt∼t−1/2 and γt scaling change the objective over time, undermining convergence claims that assume a fixed objective and accurate inner solves.  
- Weakness: ambiguous statement that Alg. 2 “converges to a global optimum” without clarifying this applies only to convex per-atom subproblems.  
- Clarity issues: manuscript contains typos, formatting glitches (equations, min/max mistakes), informal language, and reads rushed.  
- Missing implementation details: Laplacian boundary conditions, how ∆ is applied without forming p×p, handling of brain masks, constraint set choice (simplex vs ℓ1-ball) and sign effects, stopping criteria for BCD/FISTA, and batch size sensitivity.  
- Presentation issues: figures/tables need clear metric definitions, confidence intervals, axis labels, and legibility; referenced materials sometimes absent.  
- Originality concern: useful engineering contribution but conceptually incremental; need clearer positioning vs TV/GraphNet and explanation of how Laplacian vs TV changes maps.  
- Significance concern: current results insufficiently convincing—suggest cross-dataset generalization, quantitative spatial-compactness metrics, robustness across k/hyperparameters with CIs, and fuller behavioral benchmarks.  
- Specific requests: clarify convergence assumptions with time-varying hyperparameters, report inner-loop accuracy/timings, provide ablations (fixed vs adaptive α, γ effects, simplex vs ℓ1), justify Laplacian vs TV (e.g., edge preservation), and document tCanICA thresholding sensitivity.  
- Pros for acceptance: tackles an important scalable ROI learning use case, simple well-motivated extension of online DL, and some evidence of improved stability/interpretability at modest cost.  
- Cons for acceptance: incremental novelty, insufficiently justified convergence under changing hyperparameters and approximate updates, and clarity/reproducibility issues that hinder assessment.
