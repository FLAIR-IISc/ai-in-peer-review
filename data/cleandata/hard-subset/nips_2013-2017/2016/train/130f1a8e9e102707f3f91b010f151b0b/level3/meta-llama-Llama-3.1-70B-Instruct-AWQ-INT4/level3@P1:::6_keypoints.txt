- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for structured, sparse brain image decomposition.  
- Combines L1-norm sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce smoothness on dictionary atoms.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning to incorporate spatial structure and scale to large datasets.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over traditional L1-regularized dictionary learning on a large public dataset.  
- Provides a concise study of hyperparameter effects and describes the optimality regime.  
- Paper is generally well-structured with clear explanations of method and principles.  
- Experimental results are thorough, well-presented, and comprehensive.  
- Novel contribution: integrating spatial smoothness via Sobolev penalty into online dictionary learning.  
- Significant potential for neuroimaging applications (dimensionality reduction, ROI extraction, predictive modeling).  
- Major weakness: non-convex problem and algorithm lacks rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Evaluation relies primarily on empirical evidence, limiting theoretical generalizability.  
- Manuscript needs improved clarity and presentation, especially algorithmic/implementation details and hyperparameter choices.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method with strong empirical performance and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical analysis, presentation issues, and reliance on empirical validation.  
- Rating: 8/10; Recommendation: accept with minor revisions focused on clarity and presentation.
