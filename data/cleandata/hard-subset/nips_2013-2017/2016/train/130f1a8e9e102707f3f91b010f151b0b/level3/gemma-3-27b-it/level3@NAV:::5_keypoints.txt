- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to encourage spatially smooth dictionary atoms for brain images.  
- Motivated by automating ROI definition from inter-subject variability in fMRI and scaling to large datasets (e.g., HCP).  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) by adding spatial regularization to online dictionary learning.  
- Experiments on HCP data show improvements in dictionary interpretability, explained variance, and behavioral score prediction versus CanICA and SODL.  
- Authors analyze hyperparameter effects and claim only a modest (2–3×) runtime increase from the Laplacian penalty.  
- Novelty is questionable — extension feels incremental and may not meet NIPS standards.  
- Justification for using Sobolev regularization is unclear; “structure” is not well defined nor is the benefit of smooth, blob-like atoms convincingly argued.  
- Potential technical issues: possible error in Eq.(1) and no justification for using l2 norm in Eq.(2) instead of the l1 in Mairal et al.  
- Discussion of computational cost and optimization details (impact of Laplacian) is superficial and underexplored.  
- Paper presentation suffers from poor writing, numerous typos, awkward phrasing, and distracting minor errors (e.g., reference to “line 146”).  
- While the idea has potential and scalability advantages, technical errors, weak motivation, and presentation problems undermine the contribution.  
- Overall recommendation: rejection; substantial revisions required before reconsideration.
