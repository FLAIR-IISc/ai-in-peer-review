- Proposes Smooth-SODL: a multivariate online dictionary-learning method applying a Sobolev prior for spatially smooth, sparse brain image components.  
- Extends standard online dictionary learning by imposing spatial smoothness on atoms via a Sobolev prior.  
- Evaluated on a large public dataset, showing improved explained variance, stability, and interpretability of dictionaries.  
- Technically sound with clear, well-organized presentation and supported theoretical analysis.  
- Experimental claims are supported, but overall impact and gains over existing methods are unclear.  
- Reproducible for expert readers; notation and derivations are clear.  
- Paper should provide a more detailed explanation of the Sobolev prior and its effects on learning.  
- Novel combination of familiar techniques; addresses a specific brain-imaging problem.  
- Related work section is incomplete and should discuss other structured dictionary-learning approaches.  
- Results are promising for large-scale brain imaging, but validation is somewhat subjective and not fully convincing.  
- Pros: novel effective method, well-motivated, and demonstrated experimentally.  
- Cons: unclear impact, subjective validation, and need for expanded related work and Sobolev prior exposition.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the concerns above.
