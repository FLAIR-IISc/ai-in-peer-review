- Novel contribution: integrates Laplacian spatial smoothness into online dictionary learning to produce blob‑like, interpretable brain regions.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms SODL and CanICA on visual interpretability, explained variance, stability, reproducibility, and behavioral prediction; especially strong in small-data regimes.  
- Computational efficiency: maintains online scalability with only a 2–3× runtime increase over SODL.  
- Practical significance: addresses defining functionally meaningful brain regions without relying on predefined anatomical ROIs.  
- Weakness — theoretical guarantees: no proofs of existence/uniqueness or convergence for the non‑convex problem.  
- Weakness — empirical focus: primary support is experimental rather than theoretical, weakening scientific contribution for a theory conference.  
- Weakness — clarity: some notation and explanation issues around the Laplacian penalty, though rebuttal improved clarity.  
- Pro-accept: method solves a meaningful neuroimaging problem, is novel, well‑motivated, computationally practical, and thoroughly validated.  
- Con-accept: missing theoretical guarantees, limited guidance on hyperparameter selection, and incomplete comparison to other structured dictionary learning methods.  
- Overall assessment: recommend acceptance with minor revisions to acknowledge/mitigate theoretical limitations.
