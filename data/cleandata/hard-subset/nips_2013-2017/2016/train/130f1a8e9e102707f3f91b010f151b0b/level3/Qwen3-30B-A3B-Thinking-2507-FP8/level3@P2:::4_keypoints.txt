1. Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning with L1 sparsity plus Sobolev (Laplacian) smoothness to extract structured brain regions from fMRI.

2. Motivation: Aims to enforce spatial structure while preserving scalability versus previous slower alternating-minimization methods.

3. Relation to prior work: Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) and relates to GraphNet and other structured sparse learning approaches.

4. Strength — technical soundness: Regularization terms and algorithmic extension are well justified.

5. Strength — complexity: Computational overhead is modest (reported ~2–3× increase over SODL).

6. Strength — experiments: Evaluation on the Human Connectome Project and detailed hyperparameter analysis are relevant and helpful.

7. Weakness — marginal gains: Empirical improvements over SODL/CanICA are small (e.g., 55% vs 50% explained variance) and not shown to be statistically significant.

8. Weakness — insufficient analysis: Paper fails to explain why gains occur and shows performance gap shrinking with more data.

9. Weakness — clarity problems: Manuscript contains draft comments, confusing sections, and poorly explained figures (notably Fig.2(c)) and Laplacian implementation.

10. Weakness — originality concerns: Paper does not clearly distinguish its contribution from prior structured dictionary-learning work in neuroimaging.

11. Quality assessment: Technically sound but experimental validation and comparisons are inadequate to support the claims.

12. Clarity assessment: Requires substantial polishing and clearer presentation of methods and results.

13. Originality assessment: Combination of Sobolev + L1 is novel, but differentiation from prior art is insufficiently argued.

14. Significance assessment: Problem is important but the reported marginal improvements do not convincingly advance the state of the art.

15. Overall recommendation: Reject — needs stronger experiments, rigorous statistical analysis, clearer comparisons to baselines, and improved manuscript quality.
