- Proposal: Smooth-SODL — a large-scale online dictionary learning framework to identify spatially structured, sparse brain regions.  
- Core idea: Add a Laplacian penalty to Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning to enforce spatial smoothness of dictionary atoms.  
- Aim: Provide a computationally efficient alternative to slower structured decomposition methods for massive datasets (e.g., HCP).  
- Strength (Significance & Originality): Tackles a relevant neuroimaging problem with a novel, pragmatic integration of spatial smoothing into a scalable online DL framework.  
- Strength (Empirical Quality): Strong experiments against SODL and CanICA on a large public dataset showing more coherent, interpretable maps with modest runtime overhead.  
- Strength (Low-data performance): Demonstrates superior performance in low-data regimes.  
- Weakness (Theoretical): No formal theoretical analysis or convergence guarantees about the impact of the Laplacian penalty on convergence rates or solution properties.  
- Weakness (Clarity/Presentation): Manuscript suffers from careless writing and leftover internal comments, impairing readability and credibility.  
- Overall: Promising empirical contribution hindered by poor presentation and lack of theoretical depth; needs thorough revision and proofreading.
