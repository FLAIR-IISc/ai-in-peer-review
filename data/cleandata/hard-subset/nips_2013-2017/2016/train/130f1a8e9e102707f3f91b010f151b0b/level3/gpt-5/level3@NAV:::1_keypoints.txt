- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with Laplacian spatial priors and sparsity to yield interpretable, blob-like brain regions.  
- Optimization via block-coordinate descent with ridge-coded updates and per-atom FISTA denoising; claims 2–3x runtime over vanilla SODL and preserved scalability.  
- Empirical results on HCP task-fMRI show improved interpretability and stability vs SODL and PCA/ICA baselines, with modest loss in explained variance.  
- Relates to prior work on structured sparsity, GraphNet, TV-ℓ1 dictionary learning, structured sparse PCA, and recent large-scale dictionary learning.  
- Strength: addresses a timely, valuable neuroimaging need for scalable, structured ROI discovery.  
- Strength: methodologically natural combination of Laplacian smoothness with online DL likely improves interpretability.  
- Strength: empirical trends sensible, with small-data advantages and potential practical impact via Nilearn integration and public data use.  
- Weakness: theoretical support is thin—no convergence rates, regret/sample-complexity, or guarantees on Laplacian effects (bias–variance or recovery).  
- Weakness: key algorithmic claims (e.g., 1–2 FISTA iterations to 1e-6, γ schedule) lack proofs and sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to a single task and omit stronger structured baselines (TV-ℓ1 DL, GraphNet penalties, elastic-net atoms, pre-smoothing + SODL) and full ablations.  
- Weakness: manuscript clarity is poor—numerous typos, inconsistent notation, leftover comments, incomplete refs, unclear figure/table pointers, and no released code yet.  
- Originality: moderate—practical integration of known techniques with an incremental advance via online Laplacian-structured atoms.  
- Potential significance: good if theory, baselines/ablations, and presentation are substantially improved.  
- Pros for acceptance: timely, scalable, practical method with improved interpretability/stability and use of large public data.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient theory, limited validation/baselines, manuscript quality and reproducibility concerns; current recommendation leans to rejection pending major revisions.
