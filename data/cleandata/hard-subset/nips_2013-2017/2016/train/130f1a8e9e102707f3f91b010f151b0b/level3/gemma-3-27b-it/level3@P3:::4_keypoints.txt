- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning for fMRI combining Sobolev (Laplacian) smoothness and L1 sparsity to extract spatially localized components.  
- Novelty: sensible extension of online dictionary learning (Mairal et al. 2010) by adding Laplacian regularization.  
- Positions method relative to TV-L1, CanICA, ICA, PCA as a potentially more computationally efficient, structured decomposition.  
- Aims to capture inter-subject variability and improve interpretability of learned brain components.  
- Scalability advantage due to the online learning framework for large datasets.  
- Empirical gains over standard SODL and CanICA are minor or inconsistent and thus unconvincing.  
- Claimed advantage in capturing inter-subject variability weakens as dataset size grows; initial explained-variance gap closes with more data.  
- Behavioral prediction better than SODL but comparable to CanICA, questioning the added value of spatial regularization.  
- No statistical significance testing reported, so performance differences may be due to chance.  
- Hyperparameter selection (α, γ, τ) lacks justification and sensitivity analysis; impact on results unclear.  
- Theoretical discussion on convergence and the role/limits of the Laplacian parameter γ is incomplete.  
- Trade-offs between sparsity and smoothness and effects on interpretability are not explored.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor: rushed writing, draft comments, undefined abbreviations, and convoluted organization.  
- Figures (notably Fig. 2) are cluttered and hard to interpret; presentation hampers grasping main findings.  
- Recommendation: reject with encouragement to revise—needs stronger empirical validation, deeper theoretical analysis, and major clarity/editing improvements.
