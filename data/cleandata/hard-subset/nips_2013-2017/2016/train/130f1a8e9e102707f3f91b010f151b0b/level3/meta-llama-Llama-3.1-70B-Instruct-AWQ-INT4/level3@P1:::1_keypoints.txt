- Proposes a novel large-scale online dictionary-learning method that incorporates spatial structure to identify brain regions.
- Extends standard online dictionary learning with an L1 sparsity penalty and a Laplacian smoothness penalty for structured, sparse components.
- Produces more interpretable decompositions that capture inter-subject variability across small, medium, and large scales.
- Experimental results are promising, showing improved explained variance and stability over state-of-the-art methods.
- Paper is well-motivated and provides a thorough, well-organized introduction to the problem and approach.
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis to demonstrate the proposed algorithm's effectiveness.
- Manuscript quality is poor, with careless writing and leftover comments indicating inadequate proofreading.
- Requires significant revisions to address theoretical gaps and improve manuscript quality.
- Potentially significant contribution to neuroimaging, brain mapping, and cognitive neuroscience applications.
