## Review of "Learning brain regions via large-scale online structured sparse dictionary learning"

This paper proposes a novel regularizer for online dictionary learning, specifically tailored for decomposing brain imaging data. The authors aim to learn spatially localized and sparse representations of brain activity, combining an L1 penalty for sparsity with a Laplacian penalty to enforce smoothness – effectively encouraging “blob-like” structures corresponding to brain regions. This builds directly upon the online dictionary learning work of Mairal et al. (2010), addressing the need for scalable methods capable of handling large neuroimaging datasets and capturing inter-subject variability. 

The core contribution lies in the integration of the Laplacian penalty within an online learning framework, allowing for efficient processing of large-scale data. While the algorithm is presented with clear algorithmic steps (Algorithms 1 & 2), a significant weakness is the lack of theoretical guarantees regarding the existence and uniqueness of solutions, given the non-convex nature of the problem. The authors acknowledge this, but it limits the theoretical impact of the work. The empirical evaluation, conducted on HCP data, demonstrates promising results, showing superior performance compared to ICA-based methods (CanICA) and the original SODL in terms of dictionary interpretability, explained variance, and prediction of behavioral scores. 

However, the paper suffers from clarity issues, as noted by the authors themselves (line 146). While the rebuttal addressed concerns about the algorithm’s practical implementation, the overall presentation could be improved for better readability. The discussion of hyperparameter tuning, while detailed, feels somewhat disconnected from the main flow. The comparison to existing methods is strong, but a more thorough discussion of related work in spatially regularized dictionary learning for neuroimaging (e.g., Abraham, 2016; Baldassarre et al., 2013) would strengthen the paper.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel combination of L1 and Laplacian regularization within an online dictionary learning framework.
*   Strong empirical results on a large, publicly available dataset (HCP).
*   Potential for improved interpretability and understanding of brain organization.
*   Scalability due to the online learning approach.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of theoretical guarantees regarding solution existence and uniqueness.
*   Clarity of presentation needs improvement.
*   Limited discussion of related work in spatially regularized dictionary learning.



**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a promising approach to learning brain regions from large-scale data. While the lack of theoretical analysis and clarity issues are concerns, the strong empirical results and potential impact on neuroimaging analysis warrant consideration. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand the related work discussion.
