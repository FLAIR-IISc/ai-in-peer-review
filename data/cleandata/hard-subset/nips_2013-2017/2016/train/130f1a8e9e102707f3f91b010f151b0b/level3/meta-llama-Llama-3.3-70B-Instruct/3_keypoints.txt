- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a multivariate online dictionary-learning method that adds a Sobolev (spatial smoothness) prior to standard sparse dictionary learning.
- Novelty: combines sparsity and spatial smoothness penalties within a dictionary learning framework for neuroimaging.
- Demonstrates improved performance over baselines (Canonical ICA, sparse online DL) in explained variance, stability, and interpretability of learned dictionaries.
- Methodology is thoroughly explained, with clear theoretical support and algorithmic details.
- Experiments are well-designed and results are presented clearly with visual comparisons and quantitative metrics.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a concise introduction and related-work discussion.
- Validation relies heavily on subjective visual assessment of blob clarity and consistency, raising robustness concerns.
- Smoothness penalties may trivially produce smoother blobs, potentially reducing the novelty or impact of the findings.
- Quantitative metrics (explained variance, stability) are provided but may not fully address the subjective validation issue.
- Lacks deeper analysis of inter-subject variability and consolidation of regional differences across subjects; more exploration would strengthen claims.
- Technically sound and theoretically supported; claims are generally backed by experiments.
- Significant potential impact for neuroimaging and related fields (neuroscience, psychology, medicine).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid methodology, clear results, and relevance to a key neuroimaging problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: subjective validation, possible triviality of smoothness-induced results, and insufficient inter-subject analysis.
