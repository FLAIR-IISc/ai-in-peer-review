- Proposes Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary-learning method for neuroimaging with structured and sparse components.
- Extends prior framework by incorporating a Sobolev prior to encourage spatial contiguity of dictionary atoms.
- Model definition and optimization framework are clear, but the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Technical quality rated marginally below average.
- Original contribution with potential impact for neuroscience and medical imaging.
- Presentation is poorly edited, with confusing descriptions, typos, and inconsistencies in equations and figures.
- Results section lacks clear evaluation criteria and quantitative measurements to substantiate improvements.
- Compared to state-of-the-art (Canonical ICA, sparse online dictionary-learning), shows promising structured and denoised dictionaries capturing inter-subject variability.
- Potential applicability beyond neuroimaging (e.g., image segmentation, regional sensing) but needs further exploration.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, potential impact, and clear modeling/optimization.
- Arguments against acceptance: technical shortcomings, presentation issues, and insufficient quantitative validation.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve technical clarity, presentation, and results reporting.
