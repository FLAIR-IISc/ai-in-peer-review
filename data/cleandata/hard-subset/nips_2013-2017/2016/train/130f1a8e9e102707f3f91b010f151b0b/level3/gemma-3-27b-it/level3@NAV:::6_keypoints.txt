- Proposes a novel regularizer for online dictionary learning tailored to brain imaging: L1 sparsity plus a Laplacian penalty to produce smooth, compact “blob” atoms.  
- Introduces Smooth-SODL, building on Mairal et al. (2010) online framework for a computationally efficient, scalable algorithm.  
- Motivation: incorporate spatial structure for interpretability and to address inter-subject variability beyond ICA/PCA and ROI analyses.  
- Empirical strength: outperforms CanICA and standard SODL on the HCP dataset in explained variance and behavioral prediction.  
- Provides an ablation study and hyperparameter discussion that give practical insights into method behavior.  
- Main theoretical weakness: lacks rigorous analysis—no proofs of solution existence or uniqueness for the proposed formulation.  
- Convergence guarantees are limited; non-convexity is acknowledged but its implications are not fully explored or extended from prior work.  
- Presentation issues: update-rule exposition (Algorithm 2) was dense and needed clarification, though partially improved after rebuttal.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important neuroimaging problem with a practical, scalable method and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theoretical guarantees and manuscript clarity still needs improvement.
