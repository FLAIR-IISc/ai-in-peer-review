- Paper proves population likelihood for Gaussian mixtures with M ≥ 3 has bad local maxima even for equally-weighted, well-separated spherical Gaussians.  
- Shows EM and its first-order variant with random initialization converge to bad critical points with high probability.  
- Well-structured and clearly written with a thorough introduction and clearly defined problem setup.  
- Main results are presented logically and coherently, with each section building on the previous.  
- Detailed proofs are provided, lending credibility to the claims.  
- Raises important questions about EM behavior and the need for careful initialization.  
- Results are limited to the population setting, so generalization to finite samples is unclear.  
- Assumes equally-weighted, spherical mixture components, limiting practical applicability.  
- No numerical experiments are provided to demonstrate practical implications.  
- Analysis and EM-related results are novel and extend understanding, while building on prior work.  
- Significant implication: even benign instances may require careful initialization strategies for EM.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Pro points: thorough analysis, novel/significant results, raises important practical questions.  
- Con points: population-only results, restrictive assumptions (weights/shape), lack of empirical validation.
