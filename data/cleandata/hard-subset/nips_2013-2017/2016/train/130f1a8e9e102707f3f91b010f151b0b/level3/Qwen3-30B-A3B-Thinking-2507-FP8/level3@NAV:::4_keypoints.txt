- Proposes SODL extension with Sobolev (Laplacian) regularization plus L1 to enforce spatial smoothness for fMRI dictionary learning.  
- Technically novel: combines smoothness and sparsity to extract structured, interpretable brain regions missing from prior methods.  
- Modest computational overhead reported (approximately 2–3× compared to baseline SODL).  
- Algorithmic adaptation is sound: uses FISTA for Laplacian regularization within block-coordinate descent.  
- Practical implementation integrated into Nilearn and validated empirically on HCP data.  
- Experimental gains are marginal (e.g., ~5% higher explained variance) and diminish as dataset size grows.  
- Paper fails to explain why spatial regularization helps in small-data regimes and lacks rigorous analysis for this claim.  
- Visual improvements in dictionaries are subjective and not quantitatively compared to baselines.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor (draft comments, typos, inconsistent terminology, ambiguous claims).  
- Significance overstated: incremental gains do not justify added complexity for most neuroimaging applications.  
- Verdict: technically sound and novel but weak experimental justification and presentation; recommends rejection pending major revision.
