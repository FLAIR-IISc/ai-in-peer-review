- Proposes large-scale online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to obtain spatially smooth, compact dictionary atoms for fMRI-based brain region identification.  
- Well-motivated: leveraging inter-subject variability to define principled ROIs is relevant and clearly articulated.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by adding a structural penalty; promising direction beyond purely data-driven or anatomically-defined ROIs.  
- Correctly situates work relative to ICA/PCA, clustering, and prior spatial-penalty approaches.  
- Main strength: scalability—online learning handles high dimensionality and large fMRI datasets better than batch methods.  
- Experiments on the HCP dataset show feasibility, improved interpretability, and ability to capture inter-subject variability; comparisons with CanICA, SODL, and raw data prediction are favorable.  
- Major weakness: no rigorous theoretical analysis of the Laplacian penalty—unclear effects on recovery and conditions for simultaneous sparsity and smoothness.  
- Regularization parameter γ is only explored empirically; theoretical guidance for its optimal setting is missing.  
- Claimed runtime overhead (“factor of 2 or 3” vs Mairal et al.) seems understated and lacks detailed justification.  
- Manuscript quality is poor, with careless writing errors and leftover editorial comments that undermine the presentation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important neuroimaging problem, offers a scalable online approach, and presents promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical grounding, poor manuscript editing, and weak justification of computational costs.  
- Overall assessment: weak reject—interesting idea with potential but requires theoretical development and substantial writing improvements.
