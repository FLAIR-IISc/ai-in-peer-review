- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online sparse dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to encourage smooth, compact spatial atoms.  
- Retains scalability of Mairal et al.'s online SODL with only a modest (2–3x) runtime increase.  
- Applied to large HCP task-fMRI dataset and compared against SODL and CanICA.  
- Clear, technically sound derivation and well-explained update steps.  
- Smoothness prior helps in low-data regimes, improving performance when subjects are scarce (Table 1).  
- Main validation is qualitative visual comparison showing smoother, blob-like components.  
- Visual improvements are unsurprising and do not prove the components are more neurobiologically accurate (circular validation).  
- Quantitative results are ambiguous: Smooth-SODL beats SODL but is worse than CanICA on explained variance and behavioral prediction.  
- Authors argue CanICA components are dense/uninterpretable, but this does not substitute for stronger quantitative validation of Smooth-SODL.  
- Inter-subject consistency is an appropriate but underused validation; reported stability (0.4 vs 0.1) is promising but briefly treated.  
- Unclear necessity of the prior on very large, high-quality datasets like HCP since gaps narrow with more data.  
- Writing and organization are good, but experimental section needs clearer metric justification and more critical interpretation.  
- Combination of Laplacian/Sobolev prior with online dictionary learning is novel and relevant to neuroimaging.  
- Significance is potentially high but currently limited by weak experimental validation; stronger objective tests (cross-subject generalization, neuroscientific downstream tasks) are needed.
