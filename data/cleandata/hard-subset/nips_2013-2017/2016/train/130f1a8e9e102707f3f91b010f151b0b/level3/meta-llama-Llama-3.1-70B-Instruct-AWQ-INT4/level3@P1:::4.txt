This paper proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method for extracting functional components from fMRI data, with a novel combination of Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization. The approach is interesting and has the potential to improve the interpretability of the extracted components. However, the experimental improvements over simpler methods are minor or unclear, and the manuscript could benefit from more detailed explanations and analyses of the observed performance gains.

The paper's main contribution is the introduction of a new regularization term, which combines the benefits of both smoothness and sparsity in dictionary learning. This is a significant contribution, as it addresses the limitations of existing methods that rely solely on L1 regularization. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a large-scale fMRI dataset, showing that it can extract more interpretable and robust components compared to state-of-the-art methods.

However, the experimental results are not entirely convincing, and the manuscript could benefit from more detailed analyses of the performance gains. For example, the authors report that their method outperforms simpler methods in terms of explained variance, but the differences are relatively small. Additionally, the manuscript could benefit from more detailed explanations of the observed performance gains, including discussions of the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future improvement.

Furthermore, the manuscript seems rushed and contains draft comments, which can make reading tedious and sometimes confusing. The authors should revise the manuscript to address these issues and provide a more polished and clear presentation of their work.

In terms of the review criteria, the paper scores well on originality, as the combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization is a novel contribution. However, the paper could benefit from more detailed explanations and analyses of the observed performance gains, which would improve its clarity and significance.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization in dictionary learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a large-scale fMRI dataset, showing that it can extract more interpretable and robust components compared to state-of-the-art methods.
* The paper has the potential to improve the interpretability of the extracted components, which is an important aspect of brain imaging analysis.

* The paper could benefit from more detailed explanations of the observed performance gains, including discussions of the limitations of the proposed method and potential avenues for future improvement.
