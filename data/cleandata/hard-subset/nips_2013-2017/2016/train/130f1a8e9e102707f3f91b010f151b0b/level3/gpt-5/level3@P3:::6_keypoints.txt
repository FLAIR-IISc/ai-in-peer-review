- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with per-atom ℓ1 (simplex/ℓ1-ball) constraint and Laplacian (Sobolev) smoothness, solved with FISTA inside a BCD loop (2–3× overhead).
- Situates method relative to GraphNet, structured sparsity/SPCA, and TV atlas learning, arguing online solver enables scalability missing in prior work.
- Experiments on HCP (n=500, p≈260k, k=40) vs SODL and CanICA show more spatially coherent atoms, improved stability and early-sample generalization, and competitive explained variance / behavioral prediction.
- Practical and scalable: minimal modification to a known online DL framework that runs at HCP scale on a single CPU with clear runtime comparisons.
- Well-motivated regularizer: sparsity + Laplacian yields blob-like, anatomically plausible, contiguous regions versus ℓ1-only or dense PCA/ICA.
- Empirical breadth: qualitative maps, explained variance, split-half stability, learning curves, and downstream behavioral prediction; smoothness helps in low-data regimes.
- High relevance and impact: addresses neuroimaging need for data-driven ROIs/atlases and ties into common tools (Nilearn).
- Limited theory: no existence/uniqueness analysis or global convergence guarantees for the full nonconvex joint problem beyond inherited results.
- Notation and clarity issues: atom-update around Eq. (7–8) is ambiguous (Fγ(At[j,j]/t)−1), key steps under-explained and should clarify γ scaling and residual shift.
- Evaluation is mostly empirical: lacks sample-complexity, identifiability, or consistency analyses; γ safe-range presented heuristically.
- Baseline/preprocessing concerns: CanICA pre-smoothed vs SODL/Smooth-SODL not, and tCanICA hard-thresholding complicate fair comparisons; sensitivity analyses needed.
- Technical quality: per-atom updates are strongly convex and FISTA is appropriate, but overall nonconvex scheme lacks convergence rates and stability guarantees for time-varying γ,α; code not yet released.
- Manuscript clarity: generally understandable but contains notational/editorial issues, typos, and needs tighter presentation and proofing.
- Originality: incremental but meaningful—combining ℓ1 constraint with Laplacian in an online, scalable DL framework is a practical novelty.
- Significance: likely useful for practitioners—improves interpretability and stability with modest overhead, attractive for cohort analyses and atlas learning.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, scalable, practical value with strong empirical evidence and insightful learning-curve analysis.
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical development, presentation/notation issues, potential preprocessing bias in baselines, and code not yet available.
- Suggestions: clarify γ schedule and dependence on At[j,j]/t; specify Laplacian discretization and boundary conditions; report results with/without pre-smoothing for all methods; add existence argument for a minimizer or state non-uniqueness.
- Recommendation: borderline accept (weak accept) initially; after rebuttal and clarifications the reviewer remained satisfied and kept weak-accept.
- Proposed scores: Quality 6; Clarity 3 (after rebuttal); Originality 6; Significance 7; Overall 6 (weak accept).
