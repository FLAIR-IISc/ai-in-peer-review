- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to produce sparse, spatially smooth brain image components (data-driven ROIs).
- Enforces sparsity via an l1-norm constraint on atoms and encourages contiguous “blob”-like atoms with a graph Laplacian regularizer.
- Extends Mairal et al.’s online dictionary learning with a BCD algorithm that preserves scalability and adds modest computational overhead.
- Empirical evaluation on large HCP fMRI data compares against SODL and CanICA using qualitative visuals, explained variance, stability, and downstream prediction.
- Results show clear advantages over baselines, with particularly strong gains in low- to medium-data regimes and good solution stability across runs.
- Practicality: method is only ~2–3× slower than optimized SODL, making it feasible for large-scale use given improved interpretability.
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis—nonconvex problem and BCD only guarantees local optima; more discussion on solution properties would help.
- Weakness: presentation density—key algorithmic derivations (eq.7, Alg.2) and the GraphNet connection are condensed and not fully self-contained.
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity—performance depends on sparsity τ and Laplacian weight γ, and tuning via cross-validation can be costly.
- Overall assessment: novel, significant, and well-validated contribution to neuroimaging parcellation; empirical strengths outweigh the theoretical and presentation shortcomings.
