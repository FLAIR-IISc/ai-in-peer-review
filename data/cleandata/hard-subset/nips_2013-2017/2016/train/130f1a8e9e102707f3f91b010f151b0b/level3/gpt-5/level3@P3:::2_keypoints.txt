- Proposes an online dictionary learning method for neuroimaging with L1 sparsity and a Sobolev/Laplacian prior to enforce spatial smoothness/contiguity, extending Mairal et al.'s framework with proximal-gradient atom updates.

- Evaluates Smooth-SODL vs SODL, CanICA, and PCA on HCP task-fMRI Z-maps for explained variance, visual quality, stability, and behavioral prediction.

- Strength: addresses the need for scalable, structured decompositions for large neuroimaging datasets.

- Strength: methodologically grounded—combines established online dictionary learning with a classical Laplacian prior and solvable block-coordinate updates.

- Strength: produces compact, blob-like atoms that could improve interpretability, ROI definition, and downstream prediction; implementation intent in Nilearn increases impact.

- Strength: rebuttal provided additional useful results that improved the reviewer’s assessment.

- Weakness: empirical evidence that the Sobolev prior improves spatial “structure” is weak and not quantitatively demonstrated.

- Weakness: presentation and notation problems (typos, inconsistent factors/transposes) impede understanding and reproducibility.

- Weakness: some algorithmic details—especially principled derivations/solutions for the Laplacian-regularized atom update—are not fully spelled out.

- Weakness: figures hinder direct comparison due to different slices, inconsistent color scales/thresholds, and unclear legends/baselines.

- Weakness: missing/incomplete references and multiple typos throughout the manuscript.

- Quality: technical framework is solid but contains inconsistencies and likely typographical errors in equations and lacks explicit derivations for key steps.

- Empirical evaluation: supports scalability but does not convincingly isolate gains from the Sobolev prior with neuroscience-justified metrics.

- Clarity: currently below NeurIPS standards; manuscript needs substantial editing to be self-contained and reproducible.

- Originality: incremental novelty—incorporates Sobolev/Laplacian prior into online dictionary learning, relating to GraphNet/TV ideas in a scalable setting.

- Significance: potentially practical and impactful if clarified and supported by quantitative evidence demonstrating advantages over strong baselines.

- Revision request 1: add quantitative structure/neuroscientific plausibility metrics (Laplacian energy, TV, connected components, spatial autocorrelation, compactness, overlap with atlases) and stability measures with confidence intervals.

- Revision request 2: make figures comparable (same anatomical slices, consistent color scales/thresholds), clarify prediction setup (targets, regressors, model, CV, leakage prevention), and include PCA as a baseline everywhere.

- Revision request 3: provide detailed algorithmic derivations—correct ridge/code update objective, explicit proximal-gradient derivation for atom update, step size/Lipschitz bounds, and FFT conditions/complexity if used.

- Revision request 4: fix notation and sign conventions for the Laplacian penalty, consistently distinguish vectors/rows, and correct transpose placements in decomposition and updates.

- Revision request 5: complete and correct references (e.g., HCP Van Essen 2013), cite related TV+L1 work, and define thresholding procedures for baselines like tCanICA.

- Revision request 6: thorough copyediting to fix typos/grammar, provide an algorithm box or appendix with hyperparameters, initialization, stopping criteria, and make code/method reproducible.

- Pros: practical, scalable extension with sound optimization framework and useful potential for neuroimaging practitioners; code availability is a plus.

- Cons: insufficient quantitative evidence for the Sobolev prior’s added value, clarity/notation issues, and figures/predictive analyses are underspecified.

- Overall recommendation: weak reject (encourage resubmission after revision); borderline-positive pending stronger quantitative evidence, clearer derivations, corrected notation, and improved presentation.
