- Proposal: extends dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior / Laplacian penalty to obtain structured, sparse decompositions of brain images using a multivariate online dictionary-learning method.
- Writing and organization: paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with a strong introduction and thorough related work.
- Technical soundness: formulation and algorithm are well-explained and justified; Laplacian penalty for spatial structure is a reasonable choice.
- Experiments: uses a large public dataset with clear description of dataset, setup, and evaluation metrics.
- Main impact concern: unclear improvement over existing methods and ambiguous overall impact.
- Result presentation issue: primary results are visual (“brain blobs”) that are hard to interpret for untrained readers and may simply reflect expected smoothing.
- Data-choice concern: with large datasets (e.g., HCP), adding spatial smoothness may not address real data-scarcity problems and may not yield meaningful gains.
- Validation weakness: lacks consolidation across subjects and objective inter-subject consistency metrics; validation is largely subjective.
- Comparison shortfall: limited comparisons to methods like SODL and CanICA; unclear performance across scenarios.
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques but unclear differentiation from prior work and lacking unique theoretical/practical contribution.
- Significance: results are unsurprising and the contribution appears mainly empirical with limited demonstrated impact.
- Suggestions: add more objective quantitative evaluations (inter-subject consistency), broader/more detailed comparisons, clarify novelty, and include more experiments or datasets to strengthen claims.
- For acceptance: interesting idea (Laplacian spatial penalty), technically sound, and experiments are clearly presented.
- Against acceptance: unclear impact, subjective/visual validation, limited comparisons, and data-choice concerns.
