This paper proposes a multivariate online dictionary-learning method, dubbed Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL), for extracting structured and sparse components from brain images. The method extends the standard online dictionary-learning framework by incorporating a Sobolev prior, which imposes spatial smoothness on the dictionary atoms. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a large public dataset, showing improvements in terms of explained variance, stability, and interpretability of the dictionaries obtained.

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the impact of the proposed algorithm is unclear, and it is difficult to judge the improvement over existing methods solely based on the paper.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-written, and the authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results. The notation is clear, and the mathematical derivations are well-presented. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the Sobolev prior and its implications on the dictionary-learning process.

**Originality**: The paper proposes a novel combination of familiar techniques, incorporating a Sobolev prior into the online dictionary-learning framework. The approach is new and addresses a specific problem in brain imaging analysis. However, the related work section could be more comprehensive, discussing other approaches that have been proposed for structured dictionary learning.

**Significance**: The results presented in the paper are important, as they demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in extracting structured and sparse components from brain images. The method has the potential to advance the state of the art in brain imaging analysis, particularly in the context of large-scale datasets. However, the validation is subjective and does not clearly demonstrate the benefit of the proposed method.

**Arguments pro and con acceptance**:

Pro:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective method for structured dictionary learning in brain imaging analysis.
* The method is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the underlying principles.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

Con:

* The impact of the proposed algorithm is unclear, and it is difficult to judge the improvement over existing methods solely based on the paper.
* The validation is subjective and does not clearly demonstrate the benefit of the proposed method.
* The paper could benefit from a more comprehensive related work section and a more detailed explanation of the Sobolev prior.

Overall, I would recommend acceptance of the paper, but with some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.
