- Proposes Smooth-SODL: a dictionary-learning method for fMRI combining Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization.  
- Well-motivated with clear explanation of shortcomings in existing methods.  
- Extension of Mairal et al.'s online dictionary-learning framework.  
- Claims to extract structured, denoised dictionaries capturing inter-subject variability at multiple scales.  
- Technically sound with detailed optimization algorithm and hyper-parameter choices.  
- Experimental comparisons include CanICA and SODL and are well-presented.  
- Reported experimental improvements over simpler methods are minor or unclear.  
- Lacks sufficient explanation or analysis for observed (lack of) performance gains.  
- Manuscript appears rushed and contains draft comments, harming readability.  
- Originality: novel, non-trivial combination of Sobolev and L1 regularization, but unclear why it improves performance.  
- Significance: addresses an important neuroimaging problem but does not convincingly demonstrate an advance or field impact.  
- Overall score: 6/10.  
- Pro-acceptance points: novel method, potential wide applications, technically thorough.  
- Con-acceptance points: weak empirical gains, poor manuscript polish, unclear demonstrable advancement.  
- Recommended improvements: deeper experimental analysis, clearer explanation of benefits and limitations, and thorough editing to remove draft content.
