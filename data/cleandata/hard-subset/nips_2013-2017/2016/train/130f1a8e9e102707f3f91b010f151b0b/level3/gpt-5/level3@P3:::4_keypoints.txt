- Proposal: Smooth-SODL — an online under-complete dictionary-learning method combining L1-type sparsity with a Sobolev/Laplacian smoothness prior; codes via ridge regression and atoms updated with proximal/FISTA steps.
- Strength: Conceptual novelty — clean hybrid of sparsity and Sobolev smoothness in an online DL setting.
- Strength: Scalability/practicality — implementation is only 2–3× slower than SODL, uses simple updates and is computationally feasible for large p and n.
- Strength: Interpretability — qualitative maps are smoother, more compact, and show improved atom reproducibility across splits.
- Strength: Relevance — addresses timely problem of learning structured population-level functional patterns in neuroimaging.
- Weakness: Empirical gains are modest or inconsistent — explained variance and behavioral-prediction improvements are small and CanICA often outperforms.
- Weakness: Lack of analysis explaining when/how smoothness helps — limited exploration of γ and τ trade-offs and learning-curve behavior.
- Weakness: Missing ablations and stronger baselines — no systematic comparisons (Laplacian vs TV, Laplacian-only vs L1-only, simplex vs L1-ball, ridge vs lasso, k sensitivity, TV–L1 DL, GraphNet, spatial SPCA).
- Weakness: Algorithmic clarity/validation issues — claims about FISTA iterations and convergence with time-varying γt and inexact inner solves are not substantiated.
- Weakness: Presentation quality — rushed manuscript with typos, draft comments, and unclear notation that hinder reproducibility and readability.
- Quality assessment: Formulation and optimization are sensible and efficient, but empirical support and analysis are insufficient.
- Clarity assessment: Core idea is understandable but requires major editing, clearer finite-sample objective, and precise stopping criteria.
- Originality assessment: Novel combination of Sobolev smoothness and L1 in online DL for fMRI; incrementally meaningful relative to prior work.
- Significance assessment: Potentially useful for neuroimaging practice, but current evidence does not show clear superiority on EV or prediction.
- Suggested experiments: Provide targeted ablations (γ=0 vs >0, Laplacian vs TV, constraint set choices, ridge vs lasso, k and minibatch sensitivity).
- Suggested analysis: Show when smoothness helps (learning curves, SNR stratification), and reconcile improved stability with weaker prediction.
- Suggested validation: Report inner-iteration counts, runtime breakdown, effect of γ on Lipschitz constants/stepsizes, and impact of inexact solves.
- Suggested baselines: Include TV–L1 DL, GraphNet-type structured SPCA/ICA, and other spatially-regularized sparse PCA/ICA methods.
- Reproducibility: Release code, list hyperparameters/initialization/preprocessing, define stability metric and report distributions not just means.
- Writing fixes: Remove draft notes, fix typos, move derivations to supplement, and streamline Sections 2–3.
- Pros for acceptance: interesting/practical formulation, scalable implementation, improved qualitative interpretability and split-half stability.
- Cons for acceptance: modest quantitative gains, missing ablations/analysis, and presentation/algorithmic claims insufficiently substantiated.
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — promising idea but needs cleaner writing, thorough ablations, stronger baselines, and deeper empirical analysis.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
