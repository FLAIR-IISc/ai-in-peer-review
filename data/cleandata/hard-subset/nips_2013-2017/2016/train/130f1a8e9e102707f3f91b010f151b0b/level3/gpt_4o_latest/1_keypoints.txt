- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a multivariate online dictionary-learning method with a Laplacian penalty to identify structured, sparse ROIs in brain imaging.  
- Extends Mairal et al.’s SODL by adding spatial smoothness to produce interpretable, denoised dictionary atoms.  
- Demonstrates scalability and competitive runtime suitable for large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Evaluated on HCP fMRI data with comparisons to CanICA and SODL across explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Claims improved interpretability and stability while maintaining predictive power.  
- Novel contribution addressing sparsity and spatial regularization in an online framework with practical impact for atlas creation and dimensionality reduction.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence and optimality for the proposed algorithm.  
- Manuscript organization and explanations are poor, with unclear discussion of hyperparameters and the Laplacian penalty.  
- Contains unaddressed reviewer comments and proofreading issues, indicating insufficient attention to detail.  
- Limited discussion and comparison with related methods (e.g., clustering-based ROI methods and other structured decompositions).  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after addressing theoretical gaps, clarity, and reviewer feedback.
