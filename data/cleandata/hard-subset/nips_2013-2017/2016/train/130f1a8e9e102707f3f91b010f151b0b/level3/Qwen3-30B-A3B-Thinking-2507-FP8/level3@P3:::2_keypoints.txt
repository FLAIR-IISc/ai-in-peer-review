- Proposal: online structured dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms for neuroimaging, extending Mairal et al. (2010).  
- Claim: method yields more interpretable, spatially structured atoms and better captures inter-subject variability on the HCP dataset.  
- Technical quality: framework conceptually sound and algorithmic details mostly clear, with convergence references.  
- Empirical weakness: no quantitative metrics validating that atoms are “more structured”; visual comparisons (Figure 2) are not directly comparable.  
- Rebuttal improved technical rating but new results need integration into the revised manuscript.  
- Runtime: ~3× slower than SODL; overhead reasonable but benefits are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Presentation issues: inconsistent notation, errors in Equations 4, 6, and 8, and multiple typos/formatting problems.  
- Missing critical information: HCP dataset reference omitted.  
- Figures: Figure 2 shows different slices across methods preventing direct comparison; Figure 3 caption/legend unclear and missing PCA baseline.  
- Originality: novel application of a Sobolev prior to online dictionary learning for neuroimaging, but needs clearer differentiation from prior structured dictionary work.  
- Significance: potential for neuroscience and large-scale imaging, but impact limited by weak empirical evidence for the prior’s advantage.  
- Strengths: well-motivated problem, appropriate methodological extension, reasonable computational efficiency, experiments on a large dataset.  
- Weaknesses: weak empirical support for the Sobolev prior, figure and presentation errors, missing dataset citation, insufficient justification for spatial regularization.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—add quantitative, neuroscience-justified metrics, fix presentation/errors, include algorithmic details (e.g., Eq. 6) in an appendix, and revise figures to permit direct comparisons.
