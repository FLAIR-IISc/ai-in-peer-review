- Paper proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning framework adding Sobolev (Laplacian) regularization to enforce spatially smooth, structured sparse atoms for neuroimaging.  
- Evaluated on HCP task fMRI and compared against SODL and CanICA.  
- Strength — Scalability: online algorithm handles very large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Strength — Improved interpretability: Laplacian penalty yields more spatially structured and interpretable dictionary atoms than SODL/CanICA.  
- Strength — Comprehensive evaluation: multiple metrics reported (explained variance, stability, behavioral prediction).  
- Strength — Practical utility: works across small, medium, and large-scale data regimes.  
- Weakness — Limited novelty: primarily an extension of Mairal et al. (2010) with added Laplacian term, incremental contribution.  
- Weakness — Unclear motivation for Sobolev regularization and vague definition/implications of “structured.”  
- Weakness — Mathematical error: Eq. (1) likely incorrect and needs correction ([V^1, V^2, ..., V^r]^T).  
- Weakness — Regularization choice unclear: l2 norm on codes in Eq. (2) is not well-justified vs. l1 for sparsity.  
- Weakness — Clarity and presentation issues: typos, unclear references, and language reduce readability.  
- Pro: Scalable, practical method with improved interpretability and thorough experiments addressing a relevant neuroimaging problem.  
- Con: Incremental contribution, weak motivation/rationale for design choices, mathematical and clarity issues undermine quality.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on stronger justification of design choices, correction of errors, and manuscript refinement.
