- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning to decompose brain images into structured (smooth) and sparse components for data-driven region identification without predefined ROIs.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) by combining L1 sparsity with a Laplacian penalty to encourage spatially smooth, compact dictionary atoms.  
- Uses an online framework that scales to arbitrarily large/high-dimensional neuroimaging datasets.  
- Demonstrated on Human Connectome Project fMRI data, reportedly improving interpretability and capturing inter-subject variability versus ICA and standard SODL.  
- Related methods (clustering, ICA) are discussed and an ablation on the Laplacian penalty is provided.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis specific to the combined L1+Laplacian regularization and its recovery/convergence guarantees.  
- No discussion of how regularization parameter choices affect theoretical guarantees or recovery of meaningful structures.  
- Experimental evaluation is somewhat superficial: limited metrics (explained variance, behavioral prediction) and missing analyses (functional connectivity, anatomical alignment).  
- Runtime overhead (reported 2–3× slower than SODL) is nontrivial and lacks a detailed computational cost breakdown.  
- Stability claim about random initialization is asserted but not backed by formal sensitivity analysis.  
- Manuscript quality is poor with unclear writing, awkward phrasing, and visible editing remnants, indicating inadequate proofreading.  
- Improvement over existing methods is not overwhelmingly significant given increased computational cost.  
- Overall recommendation: weak reject pending stronger theoretical justification, broader experiments, and substantial manuscript revision.
