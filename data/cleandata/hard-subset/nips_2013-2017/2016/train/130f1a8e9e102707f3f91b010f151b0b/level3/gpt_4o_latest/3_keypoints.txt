- Proposal: Introduces Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary learning extension with a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to enforce spatial smoothness and compactness in dictionary atoms for brain imaging.

- Claimed benefit: Aims to improve interpretability and capture inter-subject variability better than existing methods, especially on small- and medium-scale datasets.

- Algorithmic properties: Retains SODL’s computational efficiency, scales to large datasets, and comes with convergence guarantees.

- Empirical benchmarking: Evaluated against CanICA and vanilla SODL, showing advantages in interpretability, stability, and predictive performance.

- Novelty/Relevance (strength): The Sobolev prior integration is a meaningful contribution for spatially coherent neuroimaging decompositions.

- Scalability (strength): Method remains computationally efficient and applicable to large datasets like HCP.

- Empirical validation (strength): Thorough comparisons across metrics (explained variance, stability, predictive performance), with benefits highlighted in small-data regimes.

- Implementation (strength): Planned integration into Nilearn increases reproducibility and accessibility.

- Impact assessment (weakness): Improvements are not consistently quantified; visual comparisons of blobs are subjective and hard to assess objectively.

- Validation limitations (weakness): Validation strategy lacks robust statistical measures and emphasis on inter-subject consistency.

- Data-quality dependency (weakness): Paper does not sufficiently address how data quality affects performance and may be less relevant for large datasets where regularization is less critical.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity (weakness): Performance depends heavily on hyperparameter tuning (e.g., Laplacian γ) with limited practical guidance.

- Overall recommendation: Technically sound and innovative but needs stronger quantitative validation, clearer hyperparameter guidance, and better evidence of inter-subject consistency for broader adoption.

- Arguments for acceptance: Novel methodological contribution, computational efficiency/scalability, and promising results in small- to medium-scale data.

- Arguments against acceptance: Reliance on subjective evaluation, insufficient inter-subject consistency analysis, and reduced relevance for large-scale datasets.
