- Introduces a Laplacian (Sobolev) smoothness prior into online dictionary learning; non-trivial derivation and complexity analysis.  
- Algorithm scales to large n and p with modest computational overhead compared to SODL; empirical timing supports scalability.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates itself relative to prior work.  
- Significance is unclear: unsurprising that a smoothness prior yields smoother atoms; unclear whether this gives meaningful scientific or practical gains.  
- Evaluation relies heavily on subjective visual inspection; needs objective/quantitative measures of interpretability or atlas alignment.  
- Quantitative results are inconclusive: CanICA often outperforms, Smooth-SODL gives ~5% EV gain over SODL and small behavioral-prediction improvements, with strongest gains only in low-data regimes.  
- Main experimental focus on HCP (large dataset) contrasts with the method’s strongest advantage being in small-data settings.  
- Missed opportunity: insufficient analysis of inter-subject consistency/reproducibility beyond a brief stability score; needs deeper analysis and visualization.  
- Request for authors to provide quantitative interpretability assessments (e.g., overlap with canonical networks/atlases).  
- Request for details and visualization of how the reported stability score (0.4 vs 0.1) was computed.  
- Ask whether the method is primarily intended for small-scale studies given the low-data gains, or if it offers substantive advantages for large-scale datasets beyond smoother maps.  
- Overall: technically sound and scalable, but the contribution’s practical significance is not convincingly demonstrated without more objective validation of the learned components.
