- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with l1 sparsity and Laplacian spatial smoothness to learn compact brain-region atoms.  
- Novel and significant: integrates spatial smoothness into a scalable online framework, bridging slow structured methods and fast unstructured ones.  
- Technical soundness: non-trivial extension of Mairal et al., uses block-coordinate descent and addresses computational issues from the Laplacian term.  
- Strong empirical evidence: qualitative maps are cleaner and more coherent than SODL and CanICA, with convincing learning-curve improvements.  
- Notable result: up to 14-fold improvement in explained variance over SODL with few subjects.  
- Manuscript quality is poor: many grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and leftover author comments impede comprehension.  
- Limited theoretical depth: minimal analysis of how the Laplacian affects optimization and convergence; relies on prior results without new theoretical insight.  
- Hyperparameter selection is empirical only (cross-validation) with no theoretical guidance on α, γ, τ interplay.  
- Ambiguous predictive performance: behavioral prediction results are mixed and CanICA outperforms Smooth-SODL, contradicting some claims.  
- Possible trade-off: spatial smoothness may improve interpretability/stability but discard predictive fine-grained information — needs exploration.  
- Suggested fixes: thorough proofreading, remove internal comments, and expand discussion of convergence guarantees for the modified objective.  
- Suggested analysis: provide nuanced discussion of behavioral prediction results and why CanICA may perform better.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, offers a novel scalable method, and shows strong empirical utility for interpretable brain maps.  
- Arguments for rejection: poor manuscript quality, limited new theoretical contribution, and partially undermined experimental claims on inter-subject variability.  
- Conclusion: valuable and original work with strong potential, but requires major revision on clarity and deeper analysis before acceptance.
