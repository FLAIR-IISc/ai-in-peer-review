- Proposal: Introduces Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL) that adds a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to online dictionary learning for brain imaging.
- Goal: Enforce spatial smoothness so learned components form interpretable, region-like "blobs."
- Scalability: Online algorithm claimed to scale to massive neuroimaging datasets.
- Experiments: Applied to fMRI and compared qualitatively against standard sparse online dictionary learning and ICA methods.
- Strength: Tackles an important problem—data-driven, interpretable brain atlases—from large cohorts.
- Strength: Online formulation is appropriate for large-scale data and produces visually more structured components.
- Originality concern: Novelty is limited—mainly applying a standard Sobolev regularizer to an existing framework.
- Motivation issue: Insufficient justification for choosing Sobolev regularization and no formal definition of the targeted "structure."
- Technical clarity problems: Possible notational error in Eq.(1) and unclear/possibly erroneous technical details.
- Major methodological concern: Use of an l2 norm on codes (Eq.(2)) departs from Mairal et al. (2010)’s l1 sparse coding without adequate explanation or analysis.
- Presentation weaknesses: Manuscript has many grammatical errors, typos, and unclear references (e.g., "line 146").
- Recommendation: Not ready for acceptance; requires major revision to justify modeling choices and improve clarity.
