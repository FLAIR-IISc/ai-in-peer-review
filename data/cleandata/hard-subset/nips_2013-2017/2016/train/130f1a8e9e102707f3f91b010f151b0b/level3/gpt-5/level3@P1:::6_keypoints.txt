- Proposal: Smooth-SODL — online dictionary learning for brain images combining an ℓ1-type compact constraint on atoms with a Laplacian (Sobolev) spatial prior; codes by ridge regression, atoms by block-coordinate descent with per-atom proximal (FISTA) denoising.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online sparse coding by embedding a spatial regularizer; conceptually related to GraphNet/TV-ℓ1 and structured-sparsity PCA but novel in scalable online integration.  
- Scalability/runtime: Retains scalability to large n and p with a reported 2–3× runtime overhead versus SODL.  
- Empirical evaluation: Tested on HCP task fMRI (n≈500, p≈260k, k=40); yields more spatially compact/interpretable atoms and improved stability/reproducibility across splits.  
- Predictive performance: Competitive on explained variance and behavioral prediction — underperforms PCA/ICA on EV but outperforms SODL and thresholded ICA on several behavioral targets.  
- Data-regime insight: Spatial regularization particularly beneficial in small-to-medium data regimes (learning curves support this).  
- Optimization soundness: Online updates + sufficient-statistics and per-atom proximal updates are reasonable; FISTA usage and Lipschitz-constant treatment are appropriate.  
- Existence/uniqueness gap: Paper lacks proofs of existence/uniqueness for Laplacian-regularized atom subproblems and for the overall nonconvex problem; local convergence not formalized for added Laplacian/time-varying regularization.  
- Theoretical guarantees: No global optimality or consistency results; discussion of γ scaling is helpful but needs formal statements under explicit assumptions (bounded data, coercivity, etc.).  
- Comparative baselines missing: No direct empirical comparison to alternative structured penalties (TV-ℓ1, ElasticNet-constrained atoms); citing related work is good but insufficient to calibrate Laplacian gains.  
- Clarity/organization: Overall clear organization with balanced model/algorithm/experiments and useful hyperparameter guidance.  
- Notation issue: Ambiguous presentation around line 146 — objective dependence on t/γ unclear; request to define all symbols at first use.  
- Experimental clarity: Specify the constraint set used in experiments (simplex vs ℓ1 ball) up front and provide compact pseudo-code for per-atom proximal steps including projection onto C.  
- Ablations requested: Add small ablation on ridge vs ℓ1 codes and sensitivity analyses for k and γ/τ.  
- Rebuttal outcome: Author clarifications improved presentation; reviewer raised clarity score from 2 to 3.  
- Originality assessment: Meaningful, nontrivial extension of online dictionary learning by integrating Laplacian prior and practical γ scheduling — novel and practically valuable.  
- Significance assessment: Addresses an important neuroimaging problem with likely practitioner uptake (Nilearn implementation); empirical gains in interpretability/stability suggest real impact, though broader evaluation/theory would increase significance.  
- Pros for acceptance: Important problem, scalable effective algorithm, novel integration of spatial prior, clear empirical gains, practical guidance, open-source intent.  
- Cons against acceptance: No formal theoretical guarantees, single-dataset evaluation, missing direct TV-ℓ1/structured-comparison, some notation/presentation issues and limited ablations.  
- Requested changes: Fix ambiguous notation (line 146), define symbols locally, discuss existence/uniqueness for atom subproblem, include/describe TV-ℓ1 comparison if possible, and report sensitivity to k and ridge vs ℓ1 codes.  
- Preliminary scores: Quality 6/10; Clarity 3/5; Originality 7/10; Significance 7/10; Overall recommendation: Weak accept.
