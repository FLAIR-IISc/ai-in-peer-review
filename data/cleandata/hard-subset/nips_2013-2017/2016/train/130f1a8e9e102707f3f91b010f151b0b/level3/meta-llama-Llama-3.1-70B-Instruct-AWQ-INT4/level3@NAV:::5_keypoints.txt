- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online structured sparse dictionary-learning method with Sobolev regularization for learning brain regions.
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning by incorporating a Sobolev term to impose spatial structure on atoms.
- Applied to Human Connectome Project task fMRI and compared against CanICA and SODL.
- Claims improved performance over competing methods on explained variance, stability, and interpretability.
- Main contribution is the Sobolev regularization enabling structured, sparse atoms that better capture inter-subject variability.
- Novelty is limited because method builds directly on Mairal et al. (2010) with a relatively simple additional regularizer.
- Motivation for choosing Sobolev regularization is under-justified and needs stronger explanation.
- Notational error noted in Eq.(1) (likely should be [V^1, V^2, …, V^r]^T).
- Choice of l2 norm in Eq.(2) is not sufficiently analyzed or justified versus the l1 norm used in prior work.
- Unclear reference to "line 146" that requires clarification.
- Paper language, presentation, and numerous typos detract from quality and readability.
- Meets conference criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance, but overall novelty and justification are limited.
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel application, real-world dataset evaluation, and demonstrated empirical improvements.
- Con-acceptance arguments: limited novelty, weak methodological motivation, technical errors, insufficient analysis, and poor presentation.
