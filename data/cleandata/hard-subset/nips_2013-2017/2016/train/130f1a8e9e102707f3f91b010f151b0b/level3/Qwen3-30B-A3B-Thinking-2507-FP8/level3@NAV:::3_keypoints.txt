- Proposes Smooth-SODL: adds a Sobolev (Laplacian) spatial smoothness penalty to online dictionary learning to extract structured, sparse brain regions from fMRI.

- Extends Mairal et al.'s (2010) online dictionary learning with spatial regularization, claiming improved capture of inter-subject variability while retaining scalability.

- Evaluated on HCP data using mostly qualitative visual comparisons of "blobs" and limited quantitative metrics.

- Technical approach is well-motivated with clear algorithmic details and convergence guarantees.

- Laplacian penalty produces smoother spatial patterns as expected.

- Major claims of significance are hard to substantiate because results rely heavily on visualizations that lack clear interpretation for non-experts.

- Fails to demonstrate correspondence between identified blobs and known neuroanatomical structures.

- Authors acknowledge ample data in HCP but do not justify why spatial regularization is necessary across small, medium, and large-scale regimes.

- Validation is problematic: comparisons to CanICA and SODL neglect proper treatment of inter-subject consistency as a metric.

- Quantitative results (explained variance, behavioral prediction) do not clearly show superiority; CanICA is comparable or better in some cases.

- Paper does not address whether smoother blobs reflect meaningful improvements or are merely consequences of the smoothing penalty.

- Overall, technically sound but does not convincingly demonstrate scientific contribution or practical utility; needs more rigorous validation against neuroanatomy and clearer cases where spatial regularization provides tangible benefits.
