- Proposes a novel regularizer combining L1 sparsity and Laplacian smoothness for matrix factorization tailored to brain imaging.
- Extends online dictionary learning to include a Laplacian penalty, encouraging spatially coherent (blob-like) dictionary atoms.
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art methods (CanICA, SODL) on brain data in interpretability, explained variance, and behavioral prediction.
- Scales to large datasets via online learning; runtime increase (2–3× over SODL) is acceptable given structural gains.
- Well-motivated problem: learning ROIs from population variability addresses limitations of univariate models and segmentation-based ROI definition.
- Builds appropriately on prior work (Mairal et al. 2010) and cites relevant structured-sparsity and neuroimaging literature.
- Novelty: applying a Laplacian penalty within online dictionary learning for spatial structure is an original contribution.
- Practical relevance: could improve ROI definition in neuroimaging and the authors plan to release code in Nilearn.
- Major weakness: non-convex optimization lacks theoretical guarantees on existence, uniqueness, and convergence beyond base method inheritances.
- Manuscript clarity issues noted (specific problem at line 146) and overall readability needs polishing.
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (α, γ, τ); more systematic analysis or automated tuning is needed.
- Comparisons should include more spatial-regularization alternatives (e.g., total variation) for completeness.
- Quality: technically sound implementation and experiments, but weakened by missing theoretical analysis.
- Significance: results are meaningful for ROI extraction and behavioral prediction, with potential adoption by neuroimaging researchers.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—address clarity issues (line 146), discuss limitations and alternatives to the Laplacian penalty, and acknowledge theoretical gaps.
