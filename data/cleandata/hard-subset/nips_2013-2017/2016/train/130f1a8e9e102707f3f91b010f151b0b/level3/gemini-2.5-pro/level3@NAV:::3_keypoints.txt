- Proposes Smooth-SODL: adds a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to online sparse dictionary learning for neuroimaging to encourage spatially smooth, contiguous atoms.
- Claims smoother, blob-like components are more neuroscientifically interpretable than scattered patterns from standard sparse models.
- Introduces a scalable, computationally efficient online algorithm that retains comparable convergence guarantees.
- Strength: technically sound extension with clear motivation and non-trivial incorporation of spatial priors into a scalable framework.
- Strength: Addresses an important problem—learning data-driven, interpretable brain regions—from a principled modeling perspective.
- Weakness: Experimental validation is weak and relies mainly on visual comparisons that are not convincing to untrained observers.
- Weakness: The outcome that a smoothness penalty yields smoother results is unsurprising and not by itself sufficient justification.
- Weakness: Uses the large-scale HCP dataset, which may not benefit from spatial smoothing, reducing the practical relevance of the prior.
- Weakness: Prediction experiments show competitiveness but not clear superiority over baselines, so added complexity is not clearly justified.
- Recommendation: Needs stronger quantitative evaluation (e.g., inter-subject consistency, downstream predictive gains) beyond subjective visual assessments to demonstrate scientific significance.
