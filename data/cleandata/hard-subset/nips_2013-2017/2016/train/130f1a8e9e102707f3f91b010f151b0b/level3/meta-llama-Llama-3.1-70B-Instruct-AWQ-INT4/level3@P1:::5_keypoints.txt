- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning algorithm with Sobolev/Laplacian spatial regularization to extract brain regions from fMRI and capture inter-subject variability.  
- Empirical results on a large public dataset show the method produces structured, denoised dictionaries across small, medium, and large-scale regimes.  
- Main novelty is limited: method builds on Mairal et al. (2010) with the relatively minor addition of Sobolev regularization.  
- Motivation is unclear: the paper does not define "structure" clearly or justify why Sobolev regularization and dictionary sparsity are the right choices.  
- Technical issues: Equation (1) has a notation error; Equation (2) lacks justification, particularly for using an l2 norm on codes instead of the l1 in prior work.  
- Presentation problems: unclear references (e.g., "line 146"), many typos, and generally poor writing that hinders comprehension.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important neuroimaging problem, proposes a plausible approach, and supports claims with large-scale experiments.  
- Arguments for rejection: weak novelty, insufficient motivation/definitions, technical/notation errors, and poor presentation.  
- Recommendation: revise to strengthen novelty/motivation, provide technical justifications and clarifications, and thoroughly proofread before resubmission.
