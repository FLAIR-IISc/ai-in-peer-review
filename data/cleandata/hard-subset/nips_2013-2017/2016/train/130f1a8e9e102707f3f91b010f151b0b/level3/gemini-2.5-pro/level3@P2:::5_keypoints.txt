- Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning with a Sobolev/Laplacian penalty to learn spatially structured, blob-like components from large-scale brain imaging.  
- Aim: Extract meaningful, reproducible brain regions that capture inter-subject variability in a data-driven way.  
- Scalability: Online framework scales to massive datasets (e.g., HCP); spatial regularization adds only modest computational cost (~3x).  
- Empirical validation: Comprehensive experiments on HCP including qualitative dictionaries, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.  
- Improved interpretability: Laplacian penalty produces more structured, compact, and less noisy atoms than SODL and more interpretable maps than CanICA.  
- Low-data benefits: Spatial regularization yields large gains in small-to-medium data regimes (reported ~14x improvement in explained variance with few subjects).  
- Limited originality: Method is largely an incremental combination of Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning and a standard Sobolev penalty.  
- Regularization choice unclear: No sufficient justification for using Sobolev/Laplacian over alternatives like Total Variation.  
- Code penalty choice unclear: Using L2 (Ridge) on codes instead of L1 sparsity is asserted to give “stable codes” but lacks explanation of the trade-offs.  
- Derivation gaps: Key derivations (e.g., dictionary update with Laplacian, Eq.7) are deferred and should be made more self-contained.  
- Presentation issues: Numerous typos, grammatical errors, and awkward phrasing; needs thorough proofreading and copy-editing.  
- Notation ambiguities: Dimensions in Eq.(1) (Xi, V, Ui) are inconsistent and should be clarified (e.g., X ≈ UV^T).  
- Algorithm details missing: Algorithm 2’s stopping criterion and exact iteration/convergence procedure are unspecified.  
- Hyperparameter schedule ad-hoc: Time-varying alpha schedule (Eq.10) appears heuristic and lacks theoretical or stronger empirical justification.  
- Overall: Practically significant and effective for neuroimaging with strong empirical results, but limited ML novelty and needs clearer justification, derivations, and presentation improvements.
