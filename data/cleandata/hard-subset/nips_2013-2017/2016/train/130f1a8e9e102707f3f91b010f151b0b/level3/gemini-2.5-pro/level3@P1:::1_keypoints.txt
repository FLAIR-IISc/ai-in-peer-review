- Proposes Smooth-SODL: adds a Laplacian spatial-smoothness penalty to online sparse dictionary learning for neuroimaging to produce blob-like, interpretable components.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning and adapts a Block-Coordinate Descent scheme to include L1 sparsity and Laplacian regularization.  
- Targets large-scale fMRI data decomposition to identify meaningful brain regions in a scalable, data-driven way.  
- Strong empirical evaluation on the HCP dataset with relevant baselines (SODL, CanICA) and multiple metrics (visuals, explained variance, stability, behavior prediction).  
- Results show smoother, more coherent maps than SODL and better trade-offs between fidelity and stability; large gains in low-data regimes.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis: no formal study of how the Laplacian affects dictionary properties, convergence rates, or sample complexity.  
- Reliance on existing convergence claims (Mairal et al.) is insufficient; interplay of sparsity (τ) and smoothness (γ) is not analyzed.  
- Manuscript quality is poor: many typos, grammatical errors, and leftover internal comments that hinder readability and credibility.  
- Key derivations (e.g., equation (7)) are too condensed and assume heavy familiarity with cited work; need clearer, more self-contained explanation.  
- Hyper-parameter discussion is practical but should be formally linked to optimization issues (e.g., conditioning when γ is large).  
- Missing comparisons with other structured-sparsity approaches (e.g., Total Variation) to contextualize the choice of Laplacian regularizer.  
- Overall: promising and practically useful approach but requires major revision for clarity and stronger theoretical/contextual grounding; not ready for acceptance as submitted.
