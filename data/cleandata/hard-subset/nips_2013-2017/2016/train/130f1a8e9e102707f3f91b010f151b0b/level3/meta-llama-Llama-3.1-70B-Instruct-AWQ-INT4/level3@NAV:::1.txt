The paper "Learning brain regions via large-scale online structured sparse dictionary learning" proposes a novel framework for identifying interesting brain regions using a multivariate online dictionary-learning method. The method, dubbed Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL), combines the strengths of sparse and structured dictionary learning to extract meaningful brain regions from large-scale brain imaging data.

The paper is well-motivated, and the topic is indeed interesting and potentially valuable. The authors provide a clear and concise overview of the background and related work, highlighting the limitations of existing methods and the need for a more effective approach.

The proposed method is technically sound, and the authors provide a detailed description of the algorithm and its components. The use of a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial structure on the dictionary atoms is a novel and effective approach. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the convergence properties of the algorithm and its computational complexity.

However, the manuscript quality is poor, with careless writing and leftover comments throughout the text. For example, the comment "146 XXX: I don't understand the switch between V and v" suggests that the authors did not thoroughly proofread the manuscript. Additionally, the writing style is often unclear and convoluted, making it difficult to follow the authors' arguments.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel combination of sparse and structured dictionary learning, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of the method using a large-scale brain imaging dataset, which demonstrates the effectiveness of the approach.

However, the paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed algorithm's effectiveness. While the authors provide some intuition and empirical evidence for the method's performance, a more rigorous theoretical analysis would strengthen the paper's contributions.

Overall, I would rate the paper as follows:

* Quality: 6/10 (due to the poor manuscript quality and lack of theoretical analysis)
* Clarity: 7/10 (due to the unclear writing style and leftover comments)
* Originality: 8/10 (due to the novel combination of sparse and structured dictionary learning)
