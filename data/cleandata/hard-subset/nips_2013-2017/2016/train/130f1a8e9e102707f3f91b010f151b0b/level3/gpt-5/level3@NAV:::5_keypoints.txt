- Paper summary: Proposes Smooth-SODL—an online dictionary-learning method with l1-constrained atoms, a Sobolev/Laplacian spatial penalty, ridge-coded coefficients, and FISTA-based atom updates; evaluated on HCP task-fMRI vs SODL and CanICA.

- Strength: Addresses a practical, large-scale neuroimaging problem with a scalable, online approach.

- Strength: Integrates spatial structure into ODL and reports empirical runtimes and an ablation (γ=0).

- Strength: Preliminary results show visually more interpretable, smoother atoms and competitive explained variance.

- Weakness (novelty): Method is largely ODL with a Laplacian penalty—modest theoretical/algorithmic contribution over prior work (Mairal 2010).

- Weakness (motivation): Sobolev/Laplacian regularization motivation is underdeveloped; “structure” is not defined or quantified.

- Weakness (comparisons): Lacks quantitative measures (smoothness/compactness) and direct comparisons to TV-l1/GraphNet-style methods.

- Weakness (codes): Eq. (2) uses l2 ridge coding without justification; no analysis of identifiability, sparsity, interpretability, nor an l1-coding ablation.

- Weakness (notation/technical): Inconsistent notation and stacking conventions, ambiguous references, and unqualified claims about “global optimum” for subproblems.

- Clarity: Paper requires substantial language and presentation polishing—many typos and undefined terms reduce readability.

- Originality: Prior TV-l1 DL, GraphNet, and multi-subject DL work cover similar ingredients; clearer differentiation needed.

- Significance: Potentially useful but limited by weak novelty and incomplete justification; needs stronger baselines, quantitative structure metrics, and theory on convergence/scaling with γ.

- Pros for acceptance: Scalable implementation, relevant application domain, and promising interpretability.

- Cons for acceptance: Limited novelty, unclear Sobolev motivation, missing analyses, and presentation issues.

- Recommendation: Reject as-is, but the work is potentially promising after substantial revision.
