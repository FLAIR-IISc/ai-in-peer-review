- Proposal: Introduces Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary-learning method with a Laplacian penalty to learn sparse, spatially smooth brain components.  
- Algorithmic approach: Extends Mairal et al.'s online framework and uses a Block-Coordinate Descent (BCD) solver for a non-convex objective.  
- Motivation: Laplacian penalty yields compact, interpretable “blob”-like components versus scattered patterns from sparsity-only methods.  
- Evaluation data: Tested on large-scale HCP fMRI data and compared to SODL and CanICA (group-ICA).  
- Originality/significance: Novel and well-motivated integration of spatial smoothness into a scalable online learning framework with clear neuroimaging relevance.  
- Experimental quality: Strong empirical validation with qualitative component maps and quantitative trade-offs between explained variance and stability/interpretability.  
- Learning-curve insight: Spatial regularization gives substantial benefits in low-to-medium data regimes common in many neuroimaging studies.  
- Predictive validation: Learned components improve behavioral prediction, indicating capture of meaningful inter-subject variability.  
- Scalability: Maintains online scalability with only modest (~3x) computational overhead over unstructured online methods.  
- Theoretical weakness: Lacks deeper analysis of the non-convex optimization landscape, solution uniqueness, and sensitivity to initialization.  
- Presentation clarity: Algorithm description is sometimes terse (e.g., unexplained Lipschitz constant of 4D for the discrete Laplacian), hurting reproducibility.  
- Hyperparameter concerns: Multiple critical hyperparameters (α, γ, τ) require careful tuning; more guidance or heuristics would be helpful.  
- Acceptance pros: Strong novel contribution with practical impact and convincing empirical results.  
- Acceptance cons: Weak theoretical grounding and some clarity issues; needs improved exposition for reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance, contingent on clearer algorithmic presentation in the final version.
