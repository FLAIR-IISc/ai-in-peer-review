This paper proposes a novel multivariate online dictionary-learning method, dubbed Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL), for obtaining decompositions of brain images with structured and sparse components. The method combines an `1-norm constraint for sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to impose smoothness on the dictionary atoms. This approach extends the standard online dictionary-learning framework of Mairal et al. (2010) to incorporate spatial structure, resulting in a method that can scale to arbitrarily sized datasets.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on a large public dataset, showing improvements over traditional `1-regularized dictionary learning. They also provide a concise study of the impact of hyper-parameter selection on the problem and describe the optimality regime based on relevant criteria.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its underlying principles. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, providing a comprehensive evaluation of the approach.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective method for incorporating spatial structure into online dictionary learning.
* The approach is well-motivated and clearly explained, with a thorough discussion of the underlying principles and assumptions.
* The experimental results are comprehensive and well-presented, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed method over state-of-the-art approaches.

Weaknesses:

* The problem is non-convex, and the algorithm lacks theoretical guarantees. While the authors provide some discussion of the convergence properties of the algorithm, more rigorous analysis would be beneficial.
* The manuscript could benefit from improved clarity and presentation, particularly in the discussion of the algorithm and its implementation.
* The evaluation relies primarily on empirical evidence rather than theoretical analysis, which may limit the generalizability of the results.

Originality:

* The paper proposes a novel method for incorporating spatial structure into online dictionary learning, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The approach combines an `1-norm constraint for sparsity with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to impose smoothness on the dictionary atoms, which is a new and effective way to incorporate spatial structure into the method.

Significance:

* The paper demonstrates the potential of the proposed method for extracting structured and denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability in brain imaging data.
* The approach has implications for a range of applications in neuroimaging and beyond, including dimensionality reduction, ROI extraction, and predictive modeling.
