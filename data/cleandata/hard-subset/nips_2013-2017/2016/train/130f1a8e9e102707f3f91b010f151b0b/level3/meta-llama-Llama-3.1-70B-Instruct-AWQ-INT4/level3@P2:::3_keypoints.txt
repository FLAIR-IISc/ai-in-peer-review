- Proposes Smooth Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (Smooth-SODL) to decompose brain images into structured, sparse components.  
- Extends online dictionary-learning with a Laplacian penalty to impose spatial smoothness on dictionary atoms.  
- Enforces sparsity via bounded "1-like" norms on atoms and smoothness via Laplacian regularization.  
- Optimization solved with a Block-Coordinate Descent (BCD) scheme adapted from Mairal et al. (2010).  
- Demonstrated on the large Human Connectome Project dataset.  
- Reports that Smooth-SODL yields more interpretable, denoised, and structured dictionaries than SODL and CanICA.  
- Claims better capture of inter-subject variability across small, medium, and large scales.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and situates the method relative to prior work.  
- Main impact is unclear: improvements are largely shown via visual comparisons of brain “blobs,” which may be subjective and hard to judge.  
- Spatial smoothing unsurprisingly produces smoother components and may not address true data scarcity in large datasets.  
- Lacks consolidation of brain-region differences across subjects and use of inter-subject consistency in validation.  
- Validation is subjective; paper needs more quantitative performance measures.  
- Missing implementation details: hyperparameter choices and computational complexity are not fully described.  
- Review recommendation: accept with minor revisions, asking for more implementation detail, discussion of limitations/future work, and stronger quantitative validation.
