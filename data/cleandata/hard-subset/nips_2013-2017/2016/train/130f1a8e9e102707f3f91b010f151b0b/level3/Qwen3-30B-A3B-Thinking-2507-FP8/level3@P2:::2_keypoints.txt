- Proposal: Extend online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to learn spatially structured, sparse brain-region atoms from large neuroimaging datasets.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on structured sparse dictionary learning (Varoquaux, Jenatton) and online DL (Mairal), addressing computational bottlenecks for large-scale neuroimaging.

- Technical contribution: Well-motivated algorithmic derivation with convergence guarantees for the proposed online structured DL.

- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments on a large HCP dataset comparing against CanICA and SODL across visual quality, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction.

- Implementation and reproducibility: Implementation to be released via Nilearn, aiding reproducibility.

- Computational efficiency: Only ~2–3x slower than standard SODL, making it practical for large datasets.

- Main empirical weakness: Insufficient quantitative evidence that the Sobolev prior yields meaningfully more structured atoms.

- Problematic visualization: Figure 2(a) shows different slices for methods, preventing direct visual comparison.

- Need for metrics: Authors should report quantitative spatial-structure metrics (e.g., blob compactness, smoothness) to support claims.

- Presentation issues: Multiple editing problems, inconsistent mathematical notation, typos, and confusing equation formatting impede understanding.

- Algorithm clarity: Section 3.1 and the dictionary update procedure are difficult to follow due to inconsistent variable references.

- Missing derivations: A complete derivation/appendix for the solution to Equation (6) is required.

- Missing references/context: HCP dataset citation (Van Essen et al., 2013) is absent and neuroscientific rationale for structured vs sparse atoms is underexplained.

- Figure 2(c) ambiguity: Baselines and the "R^2 > 0.1" claim are unclear; PCA is mentioned but not shown.

- Technical soundness overall: Method is technically sound, but empirical validation is insufficient to justify interpretability/performance claims.

- Originality/significance: Novelty is the online adaptation of Laplacian-regularized DL; significance is moderate but not conclusively superior to prior methods.

- Arguments for acceptance: Computationally efficient, tackles an important neuroimaging problem, public implementation, clear computational advantage over prior structured approaches.

- Arguments against acceptance: Lacks quantitative proof of benefits, has presentation flaws, empirical analysis is inadequate, missing references and unclear descriptions.

- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on strengthening empirical support and fixing presentation issues.

- Required revisions: Add quantitative spatial-structure metrics; correct notation/typos; provide Equation (6) derivation; use consistent slices in Figure 2; add HCP reference and clarify behavioral prediction methodology.
