- Proposes an online structured sparse dictionary learning extension with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness for brain imaging atoms.  
- Evaluated on HCP task fMRI with comparisons to SODL and CanICA across multiple metrics (visual quality, explained variance, stability, prediction).  
- Demonstrates clearer spatial structure and interpretability than SODL, and better performance in small-to-medium data regimes.  
- Computationally efficient claim supported: method incurs ~3x slowdown relative to SODL but remains practical.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis: no convergence, optimality, or parameter-effect guarantees to support the approach.  
- The claim of non-trivial extension over Mairal et al. (SODL) is unsubstantiated without theoretical justification.  
- Manuscript quality is poor with grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, and leftover reviewer comments, undermining credibility.  
- Insufficient discussion of why the Laplacian penalty is appropriate for brain anatomy and no empirical comparison to alternative spatial regularizers (e.g., total variation).  
- Algorithm is well-described and empirically effective, but technical contribution appears primarily empirical rather than fundamentally novel.  
- Novelty is limited: Laplacian spatial smoothing is standard and extending it to online learning is straightforward.  
- The problem addressed is significant for neuroimaging and improvements in spatially structured region identification are valuable.  
- Overall assessment: promising empirical results but weakened by lack of theory and poor manuscript presentation.  
- Recommendation: reject; authors should add theoretical justification and substantially revise the manuscript before resubmission.
