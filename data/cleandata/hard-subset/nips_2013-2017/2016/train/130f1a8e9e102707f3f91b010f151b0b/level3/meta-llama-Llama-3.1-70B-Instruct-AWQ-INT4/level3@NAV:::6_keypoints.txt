- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a novel regularizer for matrix factorization enforcing sparsity and smoothness, aimed at large-scale online structured sparse dictionary learning for brain imaging.
- Provides an algorithm to obtain solutions but does not prove existence or uniqueness of solutions.
- Problem formulation is non-convex and the algorithm lacks theoretical convergence or optimality guarantees.
- Experimental results show Smooth-SODL outperforms CanICA and SODL empirically.
- Method extracts structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability at small, medium, and large scales.
- Evaluation is driven primarily by empirical evidence rather than theoretical analysis.
- Strength: novel extension of online dictionary-learning to learn structured brain regions.
- Strength: significant empirical improvements over state-of-the-art methods.
- Strength: scalable and applicable to large-scale datasets.
- Weakness: no theoretical guarantees for the non-convex algorithm.
- Weakness: no proofs of solution existence or uniqueness.
- Weakness: manuscript clarity needs improvement (noted specifically at line 146).
- Pro-acceptance: novelty, strong empirical performance, and scalability.
- Con-acceptance: theoretical shortcomings and reliance on empirical validation.
- Overall: paper has potential but needs clearer exposition and more theoretical analysis; reviewer might raise score if addressed.
