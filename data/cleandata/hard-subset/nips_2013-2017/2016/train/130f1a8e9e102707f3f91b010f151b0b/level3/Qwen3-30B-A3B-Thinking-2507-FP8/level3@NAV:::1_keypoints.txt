- Proposal: Smooth-SODL augments SODL with a Laplacian regularizer to enforce spatial smoothness in fMRI dictionary learning.  
- Core idea fits neuroimaging trends (using inter-subject variability for interpretable regions) but is mainly an algorithmic adaptation, not a conceptual advance.  
- Strength: Addresses a significant need for principled ROI definition beyond heuristic segmentation.  
- Strength: Scalable implementation (claims handling massive datasets; ~3× slower than SODL) is compelling.  
- Strength: Experiments on HCP demonstrate improved interpretability and stability vs SODL/CanICA across dataset sizes.  
- Strength: Practical contribution includes a Nilearn implementation and clear ablation studies (e.g., 14× gain on sparse data).  
- Weakness: Lacks any theoretical analysis—no convergence guarantees, error bounds, or formal justification for the Laplacian penalty.  
- Weakness: Manuscript quality is poor (careless writing, unresolved comments), harming clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Originality is marginal—a straightforward penalty addition without novel theoretical insights.  
- Verdict: Promising empirics but fails as a rigorous scientific contribution and does not meet NIPS technical-soundness standards.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject; requires substantial theoretical development and thorough revision before publication.
