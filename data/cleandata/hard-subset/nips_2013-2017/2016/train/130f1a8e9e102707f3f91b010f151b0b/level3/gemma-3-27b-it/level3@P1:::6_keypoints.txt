- Proposed Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with L1 and Laplacian penalties to yield sparse, smooth spatial “blobs” for brain images.  
- Motivation: exploit inter-subject variability to automatically delineate brain structures without manually defined ROIs.  
- Methodological position: builds on Mairal et al. (2010), sits between clustering and PCA/ICA, and emphasizes computational efficiency over some structured-sparsity alternatives.  

Strengths:
- Novelty: first to combine L1 + Laplacian within an online DL framework for neuroimaging interpretability.  
- Scalability: online approach handles high voxel counts and large subject cohorts efficiently.  
- Empirical evidence: HCP experiments show more interpretable dictionaries and better behavioral prediction; learning curves highlight benefits, especially with limited data.  
- Practical relevance: automates region delineation, reducing reliance on manual ROIs.  
- Clear problem statement and motivation.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical analysis: no convergence proof or theoretical justification for hyperparameters (α, γ, τ) in this non-convex setting.  
- Non-convexity: potential for local optima; stability shown but solution landscape not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Clarity/presentation: some dense sections and weak equation–algorithm connections (partially addressed after rebuttal).  
- Comparisons: lacks evaluation against other structured-sparse methods (e.g., total variation or graph-based regularization).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, scalable, practically relevant method with compelling empirical results and clear motivation.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Missing theoretical guarantees, concerns about non-convex optimization robustness, and initial presentation issues.

Overall assessment:
- Promising and worthwhile contribution recommended for acceptance, with future work needed on theoretical guarantees and exploration of the optimization landscape.
