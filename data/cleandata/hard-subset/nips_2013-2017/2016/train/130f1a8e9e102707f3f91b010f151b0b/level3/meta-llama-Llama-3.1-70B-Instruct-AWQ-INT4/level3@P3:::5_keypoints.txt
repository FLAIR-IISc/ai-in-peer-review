- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning method adding a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial structure in brain-image atoms.  
- Clearly motivated by need to handle large-scale brain imaging and extract structured, sparse components.  
- Method formulation, algorithm, and convergence guarantees are thoroughly described.  
- Paper is well-organized and explains technical details clearly.  
- Novelty is unclear since the approach builds on existing online dictionary learning; the Laplacian addition’s impact is not well delineated.  
- Motivation for using a Laplacian penalty is insufficiently explained—what “structure” means and why it’s necessary is unclear.  
- Contains concrete errors/unclear references (e.g., Equation (1) notation, ambiguous “line 146” reference).  
- Analysis of the optimization problem is incomplete, particularly rationale for using l2 norm on codes instead of l1 as in prior work.  
- Empirical evaluation is strong: demonstrates effectiveness on a large-scale dataset with comparative analysis.  
- Recommendations: clarify novelty and contributions, better justify Laplacian penalty, fix errors, and deepen optimization/l2 vs l1 analysis.  
- Strengths summarized: solid contribution to brain imaging, effective empirical results, clear organization.  
- Weaknesses summarized: unclear novelty, weak motivation for Laplacian, some errors, and insufficient optimization analysis; overall reviewer rating ~6/10.
