- Novel method: combines L1 sparsity and Laplacian smoothness within an online dictionary-learning framework to produce spatially coherent atoms.  
- Scalability: preserves the online nature of Mairal et al. (2010), making it suitable for large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Strong low-data performance: shows a large (≈14×) explained-variance improvement over unstructured SODL with only 17 subjects.  
- Presentation issues: manuscript is rushed, contains placeholders, and needs major editing for clarity and credibility.  
- Weak large-data results: visual improvements are modest and quantitative metrics (explained variance, behavioral prediction) are worse than CanICA and only slightly better than SODL.  
- Insufficient analysis: authors do not explain why the benefit of the smoothness prior diminishes with more data or properly foreground the low-data finding.  
- Argument for acceptance: well-motivated, scalable extension with highly relevant low-sample benefits for clinical studies.  
- Argument against acceptance: poor writing, underperformance against standard ICA in large-sample tests, and lack of thorough discussion.  
- Recommendation: interesting and potentially valuable method but requires major revisions—improve writing, deepen analysis, and reframe experiments to emphasize low-data advantages.
