- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty for structured, sparse brain-image decompositions.  
- Claims it extracts structured, denoised dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability at multiple scales.  
- Well-motivated by the need for principled ROI definition in neuroimaging and clearly frames the challenge.  
- Novelty is limited — essentially Mairal et al. (2010) extended with Sobolev regularization.  
- Motivation for Sobolev regularization is unclear; the paper doesn’t clearly define “structure” or justify why sparsity helps.  
- The assertion that the Sobolev penalty produces “blobs” on atoms is vague and not concretely explained.  
- Method presentation is clear and organized, including optimization, algorithm, and convergence guarantees.  
- Paper contains minor errors/typos (e.g., Eq.(1) notation, unclear reference to “line 146”).  
- Analysis is limited: choice of l2 codes vs. l1 needs stronger justification and deeper study of Sobolev effects.  
- Experiments are well-presented and compare to state-of-the-art but are limited to a single dataset.  
- Overall: promising and potentially useful for neuroimaging but needs stronger novelty and more thorough analysis.  
- Review scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear presentation, potential contribution, solid experimental comparisons.  
- Cons for rejection: weak novelty, limited analysis, typographical issues, and limited dataset evaluation.
