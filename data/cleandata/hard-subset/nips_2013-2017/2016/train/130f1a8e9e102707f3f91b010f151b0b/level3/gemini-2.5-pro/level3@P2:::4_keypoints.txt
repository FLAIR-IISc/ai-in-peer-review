- Proposes online dictionary learning for fMRI with l1 sparsity plus Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce spatially smooth, blob-like atoms.  
- Technical approach is sound; block-coordinate descent updates appear correctly derived.  
- Main novelty is integrating a Laplacian smoothness prior into a scalable online dictionary learning framework (incremental over prior batch methods).  
- Shows clear and substantial performance improvement in low-data regimes.  
- In large-data settings, gains over unstructured SODL are modest and the dense CanICA model outperforms on key quantitative metrics.  
- Experimental support is mixed and lacks a deep analysis explaining why structured priors do not yield better predictive performance at scale.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor: typos, awkward phrasing, and excessive reliance on external references reduce self-containment and reproducibility.  
- No runtime or scalability benchmarks against older non-online structured methods to concretely demonstrate computational advantage.  
- Claims of superiority in “large-scale regimes” are not fully supported; learning curves show convergence with the baseline.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem and is potentially valuable for small/clinical datasets, but impact is limited by current results and analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant problem, provides a scalable engineered solution, and excels in low-data scenarios.  
- Arguments against acceptance: rushed manuscript, inconsistent experimental strength, outperformed by standard ICA, and insufficient discussion of trade-offs.  
- Recommendation: reject now but encourage revision—improve writing, expand experimental analysis, and clarify conditions where the method is most beneficial.
