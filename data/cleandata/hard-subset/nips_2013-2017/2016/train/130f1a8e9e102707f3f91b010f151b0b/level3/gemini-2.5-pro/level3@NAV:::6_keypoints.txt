- Introduces Smooth-SODL, an online dictionary learning method for large-scale neuroimaging.  
- Key contribution: a new regularizer combining l1 sparsity with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness in dictionary atoms.  
- Aims to extract more interpretable, structured brain regions (coherent smooth atoms) from functional images.  
- Strong empirical validation on large-scale fMRI data.  
- Outperforms standard sparse online dictionary learning (SODL) and Canonical ICA.  
- Learned atoms are qualitatively more interpretable (smooth blobs) and show better reproducibility.  
- Spatial regularization is especially beneficial in low-data regimes.  
- Online algorithm is scalable and practical compared to slower batch-structured decomposition methods.  
- Main weakness: relies on empirical evidence with limited theoretical analysis for its non-convex objective.  
- Lacks formal proofs for existence/uniqueness and only provides local-minimum convergence guarantees via block-coordinate descent.  
- Initial clarity issues around the dictionary update were raised but satisfactorily clarified in rebuttal; reviewer raised clarity score.  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution balancing interpretability and scalability; recommend acceptance.
