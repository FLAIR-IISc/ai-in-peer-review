- Proposes extending online dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior to enforce sparsity and smoothness for data-driven brain region (ROI) identification from fMRI.  
- Builds on prior dictionary-learning and spatial-regularization work; idea is intuitively appealing.  
- Technically sound mathematical integration of the Sobolev prior within an online framework.  
- Includes practical implementation details such as hyperparameter tuning and initialization.  
- Primary results rely on subjective visual comparisons of “blobs,” making assessment difficult without neuroimaging expertise.  
- Quantitative metrics (explained variance, behavioral prediction) do not convincingly show substantial improvement over methods like CanICA.  
- Validation is weak: no strong evidence that smoothing addresses data scarcity or that regions are consistent across subjects.  
- Comparison to hard-thresholded CanICA may be unfair due to different modeling assumptions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solid technical contribution, relevant problem, practical details.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited evidence of benefit, subjective evaluation, insufficient validation, unclear significance given large dataset.  
- Overall recommendation: paper feels incomplete; authors should add more quantitative, reproducible validation (e.g., inter-subject consistency) to strengthen claims.
