- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online sparse dictionary-learning extension with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to learn spatially smooth, blob-like fMRI atoms.
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online framework; encourages contiguous, interpretable ROIs and scales to large datasets like HCP.
- Evaluated against standard SODL and CanICA on a large fMRI dataset.
- Technical approach is principled and grounded; Laplacian regularization and block-coordinate descent are sensible.
- Initial empirical support was weak: visual evidence unconvincing and prediction gains modest.
- Rebuttal added results showing clearer benefits, especially in low-data regimes — these should be integrated into the main paper.
- Notational/derivation issues need correction (Eq.1 dimension inconsistency, Eq.8 likely missing transpose on U, make Eq.6 derivation explicit).
- Manuscript has major clarity problems: many typos, grammatical errors, and awkward phrasing requiring thorough proofreading.
- Figures are poorly designed for comparison (Figure 2a uses different slices across methods; Figure 2c caption/legend confusing and missing PCA).
- Missing citation for the Human Connectome Project (HCP) dataset.
- Originality: novel combination of Sobolev prior with scalable online dictionary learning for neuroimaging, situating clearly relative to prior work.
- Significance: potentially valuable tool for data-driven ROI definition and dimensionality reduction; method could generalize to other spatial domains.
- Strengths: well-motivated idea, scalability, relevance to neuroimaging community.
- Weaknesses: poor writing, insufficient quantitative evidence of structural advantage, unclear figures, and mathematical/notation errors.
- Recommendation: revise before publication by integrating rebuttal results, adding a quantitative neuroscience-justified metric for atom structure, proofreading, fixing math/notation, and remaking figures for fair comparisons.
