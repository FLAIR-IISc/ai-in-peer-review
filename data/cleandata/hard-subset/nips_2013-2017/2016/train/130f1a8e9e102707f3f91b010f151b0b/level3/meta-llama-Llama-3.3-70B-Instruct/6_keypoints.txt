- Paper proves two main results: existence of bad local maxima in the population likelihood of GMMs and high-probability convergence of EM with random initialization to bad critical points.  
- The bad-local-maxima result holds even for equally-weighted, well-separated, spherical Gaussians, demonstrated via a simple three-component example.  
- EM with random initialization is likely to converge to suboptimal solutions, highlighting sensitivity to initialization.  
- First-order EM (gradient-ascent variant) also converges to bad critical points with high probability.  
- Evidence suggests convergence failures are due to bad local maxima rather than saddle points.  
- Authors provide rigorous proofs, clear technical exposition, and well-explained arguments.  
- Experiments support the theoretical claims.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and situates itself thoroughly within related work.  
- Originality and significance are high, with important practical implications for using local-search algorithms on GMMs.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept; no cons listed.
