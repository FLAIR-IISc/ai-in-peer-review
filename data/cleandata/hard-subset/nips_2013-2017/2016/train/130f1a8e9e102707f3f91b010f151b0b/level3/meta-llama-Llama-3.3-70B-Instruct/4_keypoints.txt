- Proposes Smooth-SODL: a novel dictionary-learning method for fMRI combining Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization.  
- Extends Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary-learning framework to capture structured, denoised components and inter-subject variability.  
- Methodology is original and technically sound, with clear descriptions of optimization and hyperparameter choices.  
- Experiments on the Human Connectome Project dataset show the method extracts structured dictionaries and compares favorably to Canonical ICA and sparse online dictionary-learning.  
- Results show only modest improvement over simpler methods, and the source of improvement is not adequately explained.  
- Manuscript appears rushed, contains draft comments, and is sometimes tedious or confusing to read.  
- Writing and presentation need improvement to be more accessible to a broader audience.  
- Paper meets conference criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and claims are supported by experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid methodology, and demonstrated effectiveness vs. state-of-the-art.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited empirical gains, unclear explanations of improvements, and poor manuscript polish.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—clarify results, improve writing, and consider additional experiments.
