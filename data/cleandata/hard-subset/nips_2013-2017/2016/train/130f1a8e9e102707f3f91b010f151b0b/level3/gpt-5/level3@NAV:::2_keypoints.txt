- Proposes online structured dictionary learning with a Sobolev/Laplacian prior to produce contiguous, piecewise-smooth “blob-like” atoms for neuroimaging, building on Mairal et al. and related work.  
- Combines ℓ1 sparsity with Laplacian regularization and uses block-coordinate descent with fast proximal updates for large-scale fMRI/PET.  
- Method and convergence arguments are sound and align with SODL assumptions.  
- Empirical evidence for the Sobolev prior’s advantage is weak in the current draft.  
- Figure 2 lacks quantitative metrics and fair visual comparisons (different slices across methods).  
- Figure 3’s predictive setup is underspecified (unclear prediction target, role of Ui, missing PCA baseline, ambiguous R^2 > 0.1 support).  
- Rebuttal added analyses that improved the technical assessment; these should be integrated into the revision.  
- Manuscript has confusing descriptions, inconsistent notation, and typos that hinder reproducibility (issues in Eqs. (6)/(8), transpose placement, variable references).  
- Request to include explicit derivation/algorithmic solution for the atom update (Eq. (6)/(7)/(8)) in the appendix and fix HCP dataset citation.  
- Novelty is moderate: a natural but meaningful integration of a Sobolev prior into online dictionary learning.  
- Potential significance: could become a useful tool for data-driven ROI discovery and extend to segmentation and regional sensing if validated.  
- Stronger quantitative validation requested: Laplacian energy, compactness/contiguity indices, spatial autocorrelation, overlap with anatomical ROIs, split-half reproducibility, and stability across runs, with matched slices/thresholds.  
- Pros: scalable, principled extension; potential impact for ROI discovery and downstream prediction; rebuttal added informative results.  
- Cons: insufficient empirical validation, clarity/notation/typo problems, Figures 2–3 do not substantiate claims and baselines are incomplete.
