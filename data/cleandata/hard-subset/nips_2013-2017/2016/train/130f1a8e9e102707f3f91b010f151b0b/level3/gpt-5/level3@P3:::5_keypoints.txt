- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev/Laplacian penalty for spatial structure, ℓ1-type atom constraints, ridge (ℓ2) coding, and block-coordinate updates solved by projected FISTA.  
- Positioned as a scalable extension of Mairal et al. (2010) to incorporate spatial smoothness; evaluated on HCP fMRI Z-maps versus SODL and CanICA with improved interpretability/stability and moderate runtime overhead.  
- Technical approach closely follows prior online DL work; spatial regularization relates to GraphNet/TV-ℓ1 dictionary learning in neuroimaging.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical need for large-scale, interpretable, localized brain decompositions.  
- Strength: Scalable implementation reuses efficient online machinery; Laplacian updates add modest cost.  
- Strength: Empirical gains in visual coherence and stability versus SODL; competitive prediction performance; code planned for release.  
- Weakness: Limited novelty—essentially Mairal (2010) plus a Laplacian penalty, a known form of structured sparsity.  
- Weakness: Poorly motivated choice of Sobolev/Laplacian regularizer; no formal definition of “structure” or principled comparison to TV-ℓ1, GraphNet, or learned graph priors.  
- Missing empirical ablations comparing Laplacian vs TV-ℓ1/GraphNet baselines to attribute gains to the Sobolev prior.  
- Formulation/notation errors: dimensional inconsistencies in Eq.(1), unclear atom-stacking notation, and min/max typos.  
- Needs justification for using ℓ2 (ridge) coding instead of ℓ1: model rationale, empirical comparison to ℓ1-coded SODL, and discussion of identifiability/bias with simplex/ℓ1 atom constraints.  
- Algorithmic detail concerns: ad-hoc γ schedule in Eq.(9) lacks convergence/stability analysis; claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e-6 is implausible without reported stopping criteria and optimality gaps.  
- Modeling issue: simplex constraint enforces nonnegativity of atoms despite many maps having positive and negative effects—requires justification and discussion of bias.  
- Experimental fairness issues: key structured baselines (TV-ℓ1/GraphNet) are omitted; CanICA preprocessing differs (smoothed) from SODL/Smooth-SODL, creating asymmetry.  
- Experimental clarity lacking: unclear data split protocol, ambiguous atom matching/stability metric details, and no statistical significance reporting for key metrics.  
- Missing implementation details: boundary conditions and discretization for the Laplacian ∆ and whether FFT-based updates were used.  
- Quality concerns: plausible algorithm but notation errors, ambiguous claims, and potential equation mistakes reduce confidence.  
- Clarity: manuscript needs substantial language editing and consistent notation.  
- Originality: incremental contribution rather than a major methodological advance.  
- Significance: useful for neuroimaging practitioners, but insufficient theoretical/methodological novelty for NIPS without deeper analysis.  
- Pros: practical need addressed, fast implementation, planned code release, improved interpretability/stability.  
- Cons: limited novelty, insufficient modeling justification for Sobolev and ℓ2 choices, notational errors, missing baselines and fairness problems in experiments.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject; could be suitable for an application venue after clearer motivation/definitions of “structure,” corrected notation, stronger baselines (TV-ℓ1/GraphNet), principled ℓ2-vs-ℓ1 analysis, and improved writing.
