- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning for brain images enforcing atom sparsity (constraint) and spatial smoothness via Laplacian penalty; codes via ridge regression and per-atom updates solved with FISTA (2–3x runtime vs SODL).
- Method is technically similar to GraphNet/TV approaches but novelty is integrating a Laplacian prior into a scalable online BCD scheme with a practical γ schedule.
- Per-atom subproblem is strongly convex and FISTA is appropriate, but the overall nonconvex procedure lacks a formal convergence guarantee; conditions/citations should be stated.
- No global existence/uniqueness proof for the joint problem; Lipschitz constant and γ schedule currently heuristic.
- Empirical evaluation is strong and well-designed on HCP (n=500, p≈260k): compares interpretability, EV, stability, runtime, and downstream prediction.
- Hyperparameter discussion (τ, γ, αt ~ t^−1/2) is useful but needs more actionable defaults and sensitivity analyses (γ/τ sweeps, robustness to k).
- Clarity issues: incorrect “maxUi” vs “minUi”, ambiguous minibatch indexing (η), and other notation problems need fixing.
- Specify boundary conditions and grid spacing for the Laplacian; derive or cite the constant “1+4Dγ̃” which depends on discretization.
- Be consistent about sparsity being enforced via a constraint versus an l1 penalty; current wording is misleading.
- Clarify the claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations yield 1e−6 accuracy—likely refers to practical downstream sufficiency, not absolute optimality.
- After rebuttal the authors resolved the main dictionary-update concern and improved clarity (reviewer raised clarity score from 2 to 3).
- Originality rated moderate: combines known structured penalties with online DL, but appears novel at scale with practical runtime trade-offs.
- Significance high for neuroimaging and scalable structured matrix factorization; stability and interpretability gains are practically valuable.
- Suggested theory addition: state convergence-to-stationary-point under standard assumptions and cite relevant block-coordinate results.
- Suggested derivations/experiments: provide Lipschitz constant derivation/boundary conditions; add synthetic blob recovery study vs γ/τ and a TV baseline comparison on a subset.
- Practical suggestions: report memory usage and wall-clock breakdown (coding vs dictionary update) to guide practitioners.
- Clarify and justify whether constraint set is simplex or l1 ball in experiments and discuss nonnegativity/interpretability implications.
- Pros: simple scalable integration of spatial structure, strong empirical results, modest runtime overhead, relevant to ROI/population neuroimaging with planned open-source release.
- Cons: lacking formal convergence guarantees, evaluation focused on one dataset with limited structured baselines, heuristic hyperparameter selection, and minor clarity/typo issues.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — practically useful and empirically convincing, but would benefit from clearer theory, additional comparisons, and minor clarifications.
