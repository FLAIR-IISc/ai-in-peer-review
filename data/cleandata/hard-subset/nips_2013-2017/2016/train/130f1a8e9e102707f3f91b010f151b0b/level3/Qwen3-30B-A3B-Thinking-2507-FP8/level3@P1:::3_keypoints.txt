- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian (Sobolev) penalty to enforce spatial smoothness while keeping L1 sparsity for brain imaging.  
- Claim: spatial penalty yields more interpretable, structured brain regions than SODL or ICA (CanICA).  
- Builds on prior structured dictionary learning; extends online framework with only 2–3x computational cost over SODL.  
- Strong implementation (Nilearn) and evaluation context (HCP dataset).  
- Main claims of significant improvement are not convincingly supported by presented evidence.  
- Validation relies primarily on visual "blob" comparisons, lacking objective interpretability metrics.  
- Authors acknowledge spatial regularization may be unnecessary with large datasets, but do not specify when it is actually needed.  
- Learning-curve result: Smooth-SODL outperforms SODL on very small sample (17 subjects), but gap narrows on full HCP (500 subjects).  
- Inter-subject consistency is inadequately validated: reported stability (0.4 vs 0.1) is not fully connected to core claims or compared to proper baselines.  
- Behavioral prediction: Smooth-SODL beats SODL but does not clearly outperform CanICA, undermining main claims.  
- Originality is limited: Laplacian/structured penalties are known in the literature; contribution is largely application-specific.  
- Clarity and reproducibility are good: clear math, algorithm description, and contextualization.  
- Overall: technically sound and scalable but incremental; lacks rigorous quantitative validation to justify claimed significance.
