- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary learning framework with a Laplacian penalty to encourage spatially smooth, compact atoms for brain image decomposition.  
- Motivated by automating brain-region definition from inter-subject fMRI variability to avoid manual ROIs.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning and adds spatial structure to sparsity-driven methods.  
- Relevant and potentially valuable for neuroimaging; aligns with known functional brain organization.  
- Offers a scalable alternative to computationally expensive structured decomposition methods.  
- Empirical results on a large HCP dataset are promising but lack rigorous statistical validation.  
- Paper lacks theoretical analysis: no justification for the Laplacian penalty or analysis of the regularization parameter γ.  
- Unclear under what conditions Smooth-SODL outperforms standard methods; trade-offs between sparsity, smoothness, and error are unexamined.  
- Manuscript quality is poor: careless writing, grammatical errors, and leftover comments reduce credibility.  
- Notation, abbreviations, and organization are often unclear or inconsistent, hindering readability.  
- Insufficient discussion of sensitivity to hyperparameters beyond a single figure.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, novel method combination, promising large-scale results, and good scalability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theory, unprofessional manuscript presentation, weak statistical evaluation, and limited hyperparameter analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage resubmission after thorough proofreading, added theoretical analysis, stronger statistical evaluation, and clearer writing.
