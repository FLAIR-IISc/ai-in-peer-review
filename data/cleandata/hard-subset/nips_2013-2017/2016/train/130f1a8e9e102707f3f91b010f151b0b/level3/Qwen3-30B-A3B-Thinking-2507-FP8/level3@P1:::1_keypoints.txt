- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial smoothness for interpretable brain-region atoms.  
- Claims scalability is retained with only a modest 2–3× increase in computational cost.  
- Empirical evaluation on HCP fMRI shows more spatially coherent dictionaries that better capture inter-subject variability than SODL and CanICA.  
- Method is particularly useful in small-to-medium data regimes where vanilla dictionary learning struggles.  
- Contribution is relevant to the ML–neuroimaging intersection and promising for large-scale datasets.  
- Major weakness: no theoretical analysis of how Laplacian regularization affects optimization, convergence, or generalization.  
- Empirical results lack statistical significance tests, so reported improvements may not be meaningful.  
- Manuscript quality is poor, with leftover comments, inconsistent notation (e.g., V vs v), and unclear writing.  
- Experimental details and hyperparameter selection are insufficiently specified for reproducibility.  
- Originality is limited: approach is a straightforward extension of existing work and not well differentiated from related methods (e.g., Varoquaux et al. 2013).  
- Significance is narrow and claims of broad applicability are unsupported by evidence beyond neuroimaging experiments.  
- Overall: promising idea but undermined by lack of theory, poor presentation, and weak empirical validation; needs substantial revision to meet conference standards.
