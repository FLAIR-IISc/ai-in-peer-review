- Introduces Smooth-SODL: a novel regularization for matrix factorization that enforces sparsity and spatial smoothness via a Laplacian penalty.  
- Extends Sparse Online Dictionary-Learning (SODL) specifically for neuroimaging to yield spatially structured, interpretable components.  
- No formal proof of existence for the proposed optimization solution; provides a block-coordinate descent (BCD) algorithm that converges to a local optimum.  
- Empirical results on HCP task fMRI show Smooth-SODL outperforms SODL and CanICA in interpretability, stability, and predictive utility.  
- Strength: original contribution by adding Laplacian regularizer to bridge sparse and structured decomposition methods.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation demonstrating explained variance and behavioral prediction gains and capture of inter-subject variability.  
- Strength: retains SODL scalability, suitable for large-scale neuroimaging datasets.  
- Strength: implementation to be released in Nilearn, boosting reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical guarantees for existence of a solution, weakening methodological rigor.  
- Weakness: non-convex problem only guarantees convergence to local optima; no detailed analysis of initialization sensitivity.  
- Weakness: manuscript clarity hampered by dense technical writing and occasional grammatical errors.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter selection is heuristic and may be challenging for practitioners.  
- For acceptance: addresses a critical neuroimaging challenge, shows strong empirical advantages, and promises scalable open-source implementation.  
- Against acceptance: missing theoretical guarantees and suboptimal writing/clarity.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and add discussion on theoretical limitations.
