This paper proposes an online structured dictionary learning algorithm with a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data. The main contribution is extending prior work by incorporating Sobolev priors for spatial structure. The method has the potential to be a useful tool for neuroscience/medical imaging and might be applied to other tasks like image segmentation and regional sensing.

The paper is technically sound, and the model definition and algorithms follow a well-established framework. However, the initial technical rating was marginally below average. The paper could benefit from more explicit, neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative measurements showing that the proposed method's atoms are more structured and meaningful.

The empirical support for the method is weak, and the paper does not clearly demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior. Figure 2 does not quantify or clearly show in what way the proposed method outperforms alternatives. The additional results provided in the rebuttal were informative and raised the technical quality rating.

The presentation quality of the manuscript is poor, with confusing descriptions, typos, and editing issues that impede understanding. There are also inconsistencies and possible typos in matrix decomposition transpose placement, signs and terms in Equations (6) and (8), and unclear variable references in updates.

The paper is missing references, including the HCP dataset reference. The algorithmic detail is also requested, including an explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6). Figure 2 and Figure 3 have issues, including different slices shown for different methods hindering direct comparison, and unclear legends and captions.

The minor typos and stray comments need fixing. Despite these issues, the paper has potential, and with revisions, it could be a strong contribution to the field.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper proposes a novel method for online structured dictionary learning with a Sobolev prior.
* The method has the potential to be a useful tool for neuroscience/medical imaging.
* The paper provides a comprehensive review of related work.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical support for the method is weak.
* The paper does not clearly demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior.
* The presentation quality of the manuscript is poor.
* The paper is missing references and has inconsistencies and possible typos in equations.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend that the authors revise the paper to address the issues mentioned above. Specifically, they should:

* Provide more explicit, neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative measurements showing that the proposed method's atoms are more structured and meaningful.
* Improve the empirical support for the method by providing more comprehensive experiments and comparisons with alternative methods.
* Revise the manuscript to improve presentation quality, including fixing typos, inconsistencies, and unclear variable references.
* Add missing references, including the HCP dataset reference.
* Provide an explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution to Equation (6).
* Revise Figure 2 and Figure 3 to improve clarity and direct comparison between methods.
