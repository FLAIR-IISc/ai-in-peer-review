- Proposes a novel multivariate online dictionary-learning method combining Sobolev (smoothness) and L1 (sparsity) regularization.  
- Main contribution: new regularization term that integrates smoothness and sparsity for dictionary learning.  
- Demonstrated on a large-scale fMRI dataset, claiming more interpretable and robust components than state-of-the-art.  
- Potential to improve interpretability of extracted brain-imaging components.  
- Experimental improvements over simpler methods are minor or unclear (small explained-variance gains).  
- Manuscript lacks detailed analyses explaining why and when the method outperforms baselines.  
- Paper appears rushed and contains draft comments, hurting readability and polish.  
- Originality is strong due to the novel Sobolev + L1 combination.  
- Acceptance pros: novelty, applicability to large-scale fMRI, improved interpretability.  
- Acceptance cons: unconvincing experimental gains, unclear limitations, and presentation issues.  
- Recommended fixes: add deeper analyses, clarify limitations and failure modes, and include more experiments.  
- Recommended fixes (presentation): thoroughly revise manuscript, remove draft comments, and improve clarity.
