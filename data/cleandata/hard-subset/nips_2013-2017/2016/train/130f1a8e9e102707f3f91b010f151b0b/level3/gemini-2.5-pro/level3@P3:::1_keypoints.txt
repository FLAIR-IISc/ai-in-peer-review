- Proposes Smooth-SODL: an online dictionary-learning extension with a Laplacian penalty to produce spatially smooth, blob-like brain components.
- Integrates l1 sparsity with a spatial prior in a scalable online framework bridging fast unstructured methods and slower structured decompositions.
- Demonstrated on the Human Connectome Project dataset with comparisons to SODL and CanICA.
- Empirical evaluation uses visual quality, explained variance, stability, and behavioral prediction, showing advantages for Smooth-SODL.
- Learning-curve results (Table 1) indicate the spatial regularizer helps especially in small-to-medium data regimes.
- Method is scalable and interpretable, with potential high impact and likely adoption (e.g., Nilearn inclusion).
- Main weakness: lacks theoretical analysis of the extension, especially effects of the Laplacian penalty on convergence and convergence rate.
- Unclear formal characterization of the trade-off between regularization strength (γ) and update speed; only empirical remarks provided.
- Manuscript quality is poor: numerous typos, awkward phrasing, and a leftover author comment reduce clarity and confidence.
- Presentation of the algorithm and derivation (e.g., BCD dictionary update Eq. (7)) is dense and relies heavily on prior work for understanding.
- Actionable request: remove the leftover comment on line 146 and thoroughly proofread the manuscript.
- Request for quantitative justification that 1–2 FISTA iterations suffice for the BCD subproblem (e.g., relative improvement metrics).
- Request for clearer guidance on setting γ: clarify whether the adaptive choice obviates tuning or only provides an upper bound.
- Suggest providing more concrete or automated hyperparameter-tuning procedures for practitioners.
- Note that the claimed non-trivial extension of Mairal et al. is accurate but would be stronger with accompanying theoretical contributions.
