- Proposes multivariate online dictionary learning with combined L1 and Sobolev (Laplacian) regularization to extract spatially localized brain components.  
- Motivation: provide principled ROI definition leveraging inter-subject variability often discarded in neuroimaging.  
- Core technical contribution: non-trivial extension of Mairal et al. (2010) by adding a Sobolev penalty to encourage smoothness.  
- Claims the combination of sparsity and smoothness improves interpretability and captures meaningful brain structures.  
- Online learning aspect addresses scalability for large neuroimaging datasets.  
- Experimental evidence is weak: reported gains (early explained variance) are modest and not clearly sustained on test data compared to methods like CanICA.  
- Qualitative visual improvements in smoothness are suggested but lack rigorous quantitative validation.  
- Experiments on HCP are reasonable but analysis is incomplete (e.g., overfitting of RAW models not fully explored, unclear data amounts for reported gains).  
- Hyperparameter selection discussion is convoluted and offers no clear practical guidance.  
- Presentation and writing are poor: dense, repetitive, contains draft remnants, and equations lack sufficient explanation or biological interpretation.  
- Convergence discussion is brief and depends on prior work without independent verification details.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel sparsity+smoothness idea, scalability via online learning, and provided implementation in Nilearn.  
- Cons for acceptance: unconvincing experimental improvements, unclear/poor writing, superficial analysis, and unclear hyperparameter tuning.  
- Overall recommendation: weak reject pending significant revisions—more thorough experiments, clearer exposition, and careful editing required.
