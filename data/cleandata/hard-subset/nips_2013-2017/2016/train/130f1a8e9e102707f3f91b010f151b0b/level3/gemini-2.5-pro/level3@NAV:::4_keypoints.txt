- Proposal: Smooth-SODL augments online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to produce sparse, spatially structured fMRI components.  
- Novelty: The integration of a Sobolev penalty into a scalable online dictionary-learning framework is novel and well-motivated.  
- Potential impact: A fast method producing interpretable, blob-like brain maps would be valuable for neuroimaging; qualitative examples look promising.  
- Quantitative weakness: Gains over standard SODL are marginal on the full dataset (explained variance and predictive power).  
- Limited scope of benefit: The main quantitative advantage appears only in the low-data regime, without explanation for the lack of improvements with more data.  
- Clarity and presentation: Manuscript contains typos, formatting errors, leftover editorial comments, and hand-waved derivations (e.g., Eq. 7), undermining credibility.  
- Overall: Core idea is sound but requires stronger quantitative justification and a thorough, professional revision before acceptance.
