Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes an online structured dictionary learning method that augments the Mairal et al. (2010) framework with a Sobolev/Laplacian prior on dictionary atoms to encourage spatially contiguous, piecewise-smooth “blob-like” components in neuroimaging data. The approach combines an ℓ1-type constraint for sparsity with Laplacian regularization, optimized via block-coordinate descent with fast proximal updates, and targets large-scale fMRI/PET settings. This builds on prior work in online sparse coding (Mairal et al.; Mensch et al.), spatially structured penalties (GraphNet; TV-ℓ1), and multi-subject brain decomposition (CanICA), aiming to retain scalability while imposing anatomical plausibility.

Quality: The model and algorithms are largely sound and consistent with established frameworks; convergence arguments mirror SODL assumptions. However, empirical evidence for the Sobolev prior’s advantage is weak in the current draft. Figure 2 does not quantify “more structured” atoms nor enables fair visual comparison (different slices across methods). Figure 3’s predictive setup is insufficiently specified (what is predicted, whether regression uses Ui, missing PCA baseline, unclear which curves support R^2 > 0.1). In the rebuttal, additional analyses improved the technical assessment (raising from 2 to 3); these results should be integrated into the revision.

Clarity: The manuscript suffers from confusing descriptions, inconsistent notation, and typos that impede reproducibility. Specific issues include transpose placement and signs/terms in Eqs. (6) and (8), unclear variable references in the update rules, and minor typos (“Graph-Net penalty can imposes,” duplicated words, stray bold sentence). Please include an explicit derivation/algorithmic solution for the atom update (Eq. (6)/(7)/(8)) in the appendix. The HCP dataset needs a complete and consistent citation.

Originality: Novelty is moderate: the main contribution is to integrate a Sobolev prior for spatial structure into an online dictionary learning framework. This is a natural but meaningful extension with practical value.

Significance: If convincingly validated, the method could become a useful tool for data-driven ROI discovery in neuroscience/medical imaging and may extend to image segmentation and regional sensing.

Requests for stronger evidence: Provide neuroscience-justified, quantitative criteria (e.g., Laplacian energy, compactness/contiguity indices, spatial autocorrelation, overlap with anatomical ROIs, split-half reproducibility, stability across runs) demonstrating that the Sobolev prior yields more structured and meaningful atoms; ensure matched slices/thresholds across methods.

Pros:
- Scalable, principled extension of online dictionary learning with spatial structure.
- Potential impact for ROI discovery and downstream prediction.
- Rebuttal adds informative results.

Cons:
- Empirical support and quantitative validation currently insufficient.
- Clarity/notation issues and typos hinder evaluation.
- Figures 2–3 do not substantiate claims; baselines incomplete.
