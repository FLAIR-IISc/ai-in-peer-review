- Proposal: extends online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to enforce spatial smoothness for extracting brain regions from fMRI, claiming scalability.
- Technical quality: straightforward extension with sound implementation and convergence guarantees but weak theoretical justification for the Laplacian regularization.
- Modeling choice issue: using an l2 penalty on codes instead of l1 is not justified and appears arbitrary.
- Empirical results: only marginal improvements in interpretability and explained variance/predictive performance; runtime increases by ~3x versus SODL.
- Clarity problems: mathematical notation errors, typos, confusing algorithm description, and vague explanation for why the Laplacian penalty is appropriate.
- Originality: limited novelty — a simple modification of prior online dictionary learning without new theoretical or algorithmic insights, and insufficient distinction from existing structured methods.
- Significance: limited — qualitative interpretability gains and small quantitative improvements do not justify increased computational cost or claimed benefits for inter-subject variability.
- Acceptance arguments: well-motivated application, scalable and technically sound implementation, some interpretability gains, and planned code release.
- Rejection arguments: weak novelty, inadequate theoretical support for regularization, presentation issues, marginal performance gains, and substantial runtime penalty.
- Recommendation: reject for NIPS; requires stronger theoretical justification, clearer presentation, and more compelling empirical evidence to be competitive.
