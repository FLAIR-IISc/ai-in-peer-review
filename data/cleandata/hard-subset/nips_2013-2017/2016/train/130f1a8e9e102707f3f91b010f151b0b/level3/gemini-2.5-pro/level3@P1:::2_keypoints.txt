- Proposal: Smooth-SODL extends online dictionary learning by adding a Laplacian (Sobolev) smoothness prior to produce spatially contiguous, blob-like atoms.  
- Motivation: Designed to yield more neuroscientifically interpretable fMRI components and scale to massive datasets via an online algorithm.  
- Novelty/Significance: Novel integration of spatial smoothness into a scalable online DL framework with potential applications beyond neuroimaging.  
- Technical approach: Uses block-coordinate descent with first-order solvers (e.g., FISTA) for dictionary updates and leverages convergence guarantees from prior online DL work.  
- Empirical weakness: Lacks convincing quantitative evidence that the Sobolev prior yields more structured or interpretable atoms; needs explicit neuroscience-justified smoothness/contiguity metrics.  
- Visual comparisons insufficient: Figure 2(a) shows different anatomical slices and only subtle differences, preventing a direct and compelling visual comparison.  
- Confusing prediction experiment: Figure 2(c) is unclear on which features are used, the meaning of "RAW" baseline, missing PCA legend, and ambiguous R^2 claims.  
- Notational issues: Inconsistent/ambiguous matrix notation (e.g., Xi ≈ VUi vs. transposes and dimensions) requires clarification throughout.  
- Derivation clarity: Key derivations around Equations (6)–(8) and terms like At[j,j], Bjt, VAj are insufficiently shown and need a detailed appendix.  
- Presentation problems: Numerous typos, grammatical errors, awkward phrasing, and stray formatting reduce readability and should be corrected.  
- Missing citation: Human Connectome Project (HCP) reference [20] is absent from the bibliography.  
- Rebuttal impact: Additional quantitative results supplied in rebuttal improved the reviewer’s technical assessment and should be incorporated into the manuscript.  
- Recommendation: Promising and leans toward acceptance conditional on a major revision to fix clarity/notation/typos and to include strong quantitative validation of the spatial regularization.
