- Proposes Smooth-SODL, a novel multivariate online dictionary-learning method combining sparsity and Laplacian smoothness for structured, interpretable brain imaging dictionaries.
- Extends standard online dictionary-learning by incorporating a Laplacian penalty to enforce spatial structure.
- Clear introduction highlighting limitations of existing methods and the need for principled ROI definition in brain imaging.
- Thorough related-work review covering online sparse dictionary-learning and spatially-structured decompositions.
- Well-motivated method with detailed derivation and a Block-Coordinate Descent (BCD) optimization scheme.
- Evaluated on a large public dataset with results demonstrating superiority over state-of-the-art methods.
- Strengths: originality, clarity, thoroughness, and well-presented experimental results.
- Main weakness: non-convex problem formulation and lack of theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Manuscript needs improved clarity and presentation, especially in the introduction and conclusion.
- Evaluation relies primarily on empirical evidence rather than theoretical analysis, limiting generalizability.
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality but could improve significance and broader impact.
- Recommendation: accept subject to minor revisions addressing theoretical analysis and presentation issues.
