- Proposes an online matrix factorization/dictionary-learning framework combining L1 sparsity with a Sobolev/Laplacian penalty to produce smooth, compact “blob-like” atoms.  
- Algorithm alternates ridge-based code updates and FISTA-based atom updates within a block-coordinate descent loop, scaling to large n and p.  
- Empirical evaluation on HCP task fMRI vs SODL and CanICA shows strong performance and improved interpretability, with stability/EV/prediction analyses.  
- Scalability achieved with modest overhead (~2–3× SODL) and practical hyperparameter guidance; code promised in Nilearn.  
- Smoothness+sparsity regularization is well-motivated for ROI discovery and likely to be adopted by practitioners.  
- Core theoretical weaknesses: non-convex optimization problem with no proofs of existence/uniqueness, no convergence-rate guarantees, and no global critical-point convergence guarantee.  
- Heuristic schedule for γ (to control Lipschitz constant) is reasonable but lacks formal analysis.  
- Theoretical framing is limited relative to the breadth of empirical experiments; evaluation is primarily empirical.  
- Incremental novelty: L1 + Laplacian regularization is known; main contribution is making it online and efficient.  
- Clarity issues: heavy notation, sketched derivations, several typos, and specific ambiguity around the definition/use of Fγ and A_t[j,j] (line 146).  
- Suggest proving existence of minimizers, clarifying (non-)uniqueness, and establishing convergence to stationary points (e.g., under KL conditions for BCD with proximal steps).  
- Suggest adding a synthetic recovery study and comparisons with a TV-regularized baseline.  
- Suggest improving exposition of Algorithm 2, Lipschitz constants, step sizes, and fixing typographical errors.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear practical impact in neuroimaging, strong empirical superiority, interpretability, and a scalable algorithmic gap filler.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical guarantees and incremental methodological novelty.  
- After rebuttal: authors satisfactorily addressed algorithmic questions and clarified implementation; reviewer raised clarity score from 2 to 3 and remains mildly positive.
