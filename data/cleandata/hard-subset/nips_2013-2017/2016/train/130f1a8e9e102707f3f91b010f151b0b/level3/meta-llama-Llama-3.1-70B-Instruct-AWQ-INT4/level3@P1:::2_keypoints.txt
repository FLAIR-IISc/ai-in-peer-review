- Proposal: Introduces Smooth-SODL, an online structured dictionary-learning algorithm using a Sobolev (Laplacian) prior to encourage spatially contiguous dictionary atoms for neuroimaging data.

- Technical soundness: Model and algorithms follow a well-established framework and are technically solid overall.

- Notation issues: Initial submission contained inconsistencies, possible typos, and confusing mathematical notation that lowered the initial technical rating.

- Novelty: The use of a Sobolev prior to impose spatial structure is a novel extension of prior work.

- Clarity of explanation: The model and algorithm are generally explained clearly despite presentation problems.

- Empirical support weak: Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate the empirical advantage of the Sobolev prior.

- Visualization shortcomings: Figure 2 fails to provide a clear visual comparison between methods.

- Lack of quantitative evidence: Claims that Smooth-SODL produces more “structured” atoms are not backed by quantitative, neuroscience-justified metrics.

- Rebuttal improvements: Additional results in the rebuttal improved the reviewer’s view and technical rating; these should be included in the revision.

- Presentation quality poor: Manuscript suffers from confusing descriptions, typos, and editing issues that hinder understanding.

- Requests for clarity: Reviewer asks for explicit mathematical/algorithmic solutions, clearer notation, and resolved variable references.

- Missing references: Some relevant references are missing and should be added.

- Potential impact: Method could significantly benefit neuroscience and medical imaging tasks (e.g., segmentation, regional sensing).

- Needed comparisons: Authors should provide explicit comparisons to other methods and stronger quantitative evaluations.

- Strengths summary: Novel Sobolev prior extension, potential impact on neuroscience/medical imaging, and generally clear algorithmic explanation.

- Weaknesses summary: Weak empirical support, poor presentation, notation inconsistencies/typos, and missing references.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions addressing empirical evidence, presentation, notation, and references.

- Ratings: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
