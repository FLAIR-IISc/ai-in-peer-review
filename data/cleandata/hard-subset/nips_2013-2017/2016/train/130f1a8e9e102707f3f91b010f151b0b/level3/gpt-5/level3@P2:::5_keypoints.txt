- Paper proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Laplacian (Sobolev) smoothness prior on atoms, L1/simplex atom constraints, and ridge-coded updates solved with inner FISTA steps.

- Main contribution: integrating a Laplacian penalty into the online SODL framework with modest runtime overhead and improved stability/interpretability.

- Technical clarity: Eq.(2)’s use of lim_{n→∞} is confusing — rewrite as empirical online risk and relate to population expectation.

- Code choice: defaulting to ridge (L2) codes needs justification and systematic comparison to L1-coded SODL due to different sparsity/identifiability properties.

- Laplacian prior: a Laplacian promotes smoothness but not compact, blob-like support; interaction with L1/simplex and non-negativity must be analyzed and justified.

- Comparison to TV: TV-based penalties more directly encourage spatial compactness — include comparison or discussion with TV/GraphNet approaches.

- Convergence/inner solves: claim that 1–2 FISTA iterations reach 1e−6 is not credible; report stopping criteria, actual tolerances, and justify time-varying γ_t heuristic.

- Notation/derivations: check constants in ΩLap, clarify boundary conditions for Laplacian, and discuss convergence under inexact block-coordinate solves.

- Empirical ablations missing: no isolation of Laplacian vs sparsity vs non-negativity effects.

- Baselines lacking: need comparisons to TV-L1/GraphNet dictionary-learning or structured methods, even on smaller subsets.

- Behavioral prediction evaluation: missing details on train/test splits, confidence intervals, thresholding choices for tCanICA, and statistical rigor.

- Stability metrics: report atom stability with error bars and explicit matching methodology (e.g., Hungarian matching).

- Interpretability claims: quantify overlap with known atlases or provide sparsity/smoothness metrics to support visual claims.

- Clarity and presentation: paper contains typos, organizational issues, inconsistent notation, and unclear cross-references (e.g., “line 146”).

- Originality: idea is incremental — spatial smoothness priors are known; novelty is primarily integrating Laplacian into online SODL and demonstrating scalability.

- Significance: method is potentially useful if robust/reproducible, but current empirical evidence and novelty are insufficient for top-tier impact.

- Pros: addresses scalable structured decompositions, reasonable runtime overhead, large-scale HCP evaluation, and planned open-source implementation.

- Cons: limited novelty, weak motivation/theory for design choices, clarity problems, and insufficient empirical comparisons/ablations.

- Suggested experiments: compare L1 vs L2 codes, vary γ and τ, test with/without non-negativity, include TV-L1/GraphNet baselines, and report sensitivity/runtime impacts.

- Suggested fixes: clarify Eq.(2), define ΩLap and boundary conditions, state voxel grid/mask details, fix typos, and improve organization.

- Recommendation: borderline reject — promising scale and practicality but needs stronger theory, clearer exposition, and more rigorous empirical comparisons.
