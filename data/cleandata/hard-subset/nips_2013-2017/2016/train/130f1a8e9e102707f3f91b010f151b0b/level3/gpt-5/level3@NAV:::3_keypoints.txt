- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev/Laplacian prior on atoms to produce smooth, spatially compact blobs for fMRI decomposition.
- Connects to existing structured sparsity and spatial regularization methods (GraphNet/Tikhonov, TV-ℓ1, structured sparse PCA) and group ICA; main novelty is applying a Laplacian penalty on atoms in an online framework.
- Optimization approach (block coordinate descent, ridge coding, FISTA for Laplacian denoising) is plausible and computational overhead appears modest.
- Empirical evidence is mostly visual and subjective; objective assessment of “better blobs” is lacking.
- Quantitative results (explained variance, stability, prediction) are small, sensitive to hyperparameters, and sometimes favor ICA.
- Validation does not isolate the effect of the Sobolev prior versus simple alternatives (input pre-smoothing, TV penalties, Laplacian on codes).
- Important implementation choices (preprocessing, Laplacian construction, boundary conditions, hyperparameter selection) are under-specified.
- Figures rely on visual inspection; an untrained reader cannot objectively judge improvements.
- Originality is incremental: spatial regularization of latent maps is well-known; contribution is mainly the scalable online implementation and HCP-scale empirical study.
- Significance is unclear: with large datasets like HCP smoothing may be unnecessary, and the paper does not demonstrate clear benefits in genuinely low-n or practical problematic regimes.
- Missing expected neuroimaging validations: split-half reproducibility, inter-subject consistency, and atlas correspondence are absent or only qualitative.
- Pros: simple, scalable extension of a widely used online DL framework; practical interest for neuroimaging; modest computational overhead and clear algorithmic recipe.
- Cons: unclear impact, subjective validation, limited comparisons to established structured penalties and pre-smoothing, and unresolved hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Suggested experiments/analyses: quantify split-half/stability (Dice/MI/correlation), inter-subject consistency and atlas correspondence, compare to TV and pre-smoothing, demonstrate benefits in low/medium-n, report statistical significance for prediction, and run thorough ablations (γ, τ, Laplacian vs none).
