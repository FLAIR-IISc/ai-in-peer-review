- Proposes an online dictionary learning method combining L1 sparsity and Sobolev smoothness for fMRI to extract meaningful, spatially coherent brain regions.  
- Builds on Mairal et al. (2010) online dictionary learning and addresses inter-subject variability in neuroimaging.  
- Novelty lies in integrating Sobolev and L1 regularization within a scalable online framework.  
- Aligns with population-level ROI methods and contrasts with ICA/PCA and TV-L1 approaches.  
- Experimental gains over standard online dictionary learning (SODL) are minor and not clearly convincing.  
- Method is roughly three times slower than SODL, raising computational cost concerns.  
- Paper lacks analysis explaining why Sobolev regularization yields limited benefits, especially regarding hyperparameter interactions (α, γ, τ).  
- Manuscript quality is poor: draft comments remain, writing is dense, and mathematical derivations lack intuition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty of regularization combination, potential interpretability, and scalability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: marginal empirical improvement, insufficient analysis of limitations, and weak presentation.  
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage revision with deeper analysis, more experiments, and substantial rewriting for clarity.
