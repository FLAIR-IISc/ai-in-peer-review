- Addresses finding meaningful brain regions without predefined ROIs by extending online dictionary learning with spatial structure.  
- Introduces a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty to encourage piecewise-smooth, compact ("blob") atoms.  
- Maintains online scalability, suitable for large datasets (e.g., HCP ~500 subjects).  
- Authors plan to release implementation as part of the Nilearn library.  
- Core novelty is weak: essentially Mairal et al. (2010) plus Sobolev regularization, presented as incremental.  
- Fails to adequately position or compare with prior structured dictionary learning work (e.g., TV-ℓ1 methods).  
- Use of ℓ2 (ridge) on codes is insufficiently justified; no theoretical or empirical support given.  
- Contains presentation issues: likely notation error in Eq.(1), unclear reference to "line 146", and many typos.  
- Explanation and motivation for Sobolev regularization and the meaning of "structure" are unclear.  
- Experimental support is mainly qualitative; quantitative advantages over existing methods are not convincingly shown.  
- Computational cost increases by a factor of ~2–3, and this overhead is not justified given marginal gains.  
- Authors acknowledge spatial regularization may be unnecessary with large data, weakening the claimed benefit.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound but incremental, poorly motivated and presented; does not meet NIPS novelty standards.  
- Recommendation: Reject.
