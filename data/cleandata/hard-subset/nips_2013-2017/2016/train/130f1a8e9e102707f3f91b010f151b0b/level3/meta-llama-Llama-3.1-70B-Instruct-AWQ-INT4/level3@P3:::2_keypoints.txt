- Main contribution: novel extension of online structured dictionary learning by incorporating a Sobolev prior to encourage spatially contiguous atoms.
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear problem setup and solid algorithmic framework built on prior work.
- Uses combined ℓ1 and Laplacian penalties to impose sparsity and spatial smoothness; Laplacian prior is central to encouraging spatial structure.
- Initial technical rating was marginally below average due to presentation and notation issues.
- Empirical results on a large public dataset suggest more interpretable, denoised dictionaries and better inter-subject variability capture, but evidence is weak.
- Figure 2 does not quantify advantages or clearly demonstrate the Sobolev prior’s empirical benefit; visual comparisons are inconclusive.
- Reviewer asks for neuroscience-justified criteria and quantitative measures showing atoms are more structured and meaningful.
- Paper needs more detailed analysis and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods to establish empirical advantage.
- Rebuttal added informative results that improved technical assessment; the reviewer recommends including these results in the revision.
- Presentation quality suffers from confusing descriptions, typos, and editing issues that impede understanding.
- Specific mathematical/notation issues: inconsistencies and possible typos in matrix transpose placement, signs/terms in Equations (6) and (8), and unclear variable references in update rules.
- Missing citation/reference to the HCP dataset used in experiments.
- Reviewer requests an explicit mathematical/algorithmic solution and derivation for Equation (6) to be included in the appendix.
- Figure 2 problems: different slices shown across methods hinder direct comparison and the “more structured” claim is unsupported visually or quantitatively.
- Figure 3 problems: unclear prediction target, ambiguity whether regression uses U_i, missing baselines (PCA), unclear legends/captions, and ambiguous R^2 > 0.1 claims.
- Contains minor typos and stray comments that need fixing.
- Overall assessment: promising and original idea but requires significant revisions to improve clarity, fix technical/notation errors, strengthen empirical validation, and include missing references.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel Sobolev prior contribution and some supportive empirical results on a large dataset.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak empirical support, presentation and notation flaws, missing HCP reference, and insufficient comparative evaluation.
