- Proposes Smooth-SODL: online dictionary learning with a Sobolev (Laplacian) penalty for spatial structure in brain images.
- Application to neuroimaging is interesting but technical contribution is limited.
- Novelty is weak — method is a straightforward addition of Laplacian regularization to Mairal et al. (2010).
- Motivation for Sobolev regularization is unclear and "structure" is not precisely defined.
- No justification for why Laplacian regularization outperforms standard sparsity constraints.
- Claims of a "non-trivial" extension of Mairal et al. appear unsupported by theoretical or algorithmic innovation.
- Technical issues: likely notational error in Eq. (1) for dictionary representation.
- Unjustified use of l2 norm for codes in Eq. (2) instead of l1 given the sparsity focus.
- Unclear reference to "line 146" and multiple typos/poor presentation reduce clarity.
- Experiments show more spatially structured dictionaries but the practical significance is limited.
- Computational overhead (2–3x slower) is marginal and does not justify the limited theoretical contribution.
- Fails to demonstrate advancement over existing structured dictionary learning; recommends rejection.
