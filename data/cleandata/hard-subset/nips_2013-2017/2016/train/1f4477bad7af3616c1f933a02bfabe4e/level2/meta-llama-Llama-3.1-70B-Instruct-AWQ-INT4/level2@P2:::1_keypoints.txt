- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training generalized polynomial neural networks (GPNs) with a guarantee of global optimality.  
- Method achieves a linear convergence rate, making it practically feasible.  
- Applies to a specific class of feedforward networks and requires nonnegativity of weights.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical analysis and convergence proof under certain conditions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of method and theoretical foundations.  
- Innovative use of nonlinear spectral methods and demonstrated effectiveness on UCI datasets.  
- Strength: rigorous analysis and proof of convergence to global optimum.  
- Strength: model class offers greater interpretability and easier analysis.  
- Weakness: nonnegativity constraint on weights may limit applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks clear guidance for choosing model hyperparameters (hidden units, activations).  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional datasets; scalability to high-dimensional data is unclear.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but authors should address noted weaknesses.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10, confidence: 8/10.
