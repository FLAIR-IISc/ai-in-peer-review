- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for training generalized polynomial neural networks with one or two hidden layers.
- Method achieves global optimality under certain architecture and data conditions.
- Provides a theoretical framework guaranteeing convergence to the global optimum with a linear convergence rate.
- Main contribution is a globally optimal training algorithm that differs from traditional SGD.
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise presentation and good introductory context.
- Thorough explanation of the method and underlying theory with consistent notation.
- Experimental results on several UCI datasets show competitive performance versus kernel SVM and ReLU networks.
- Method is not fully optimized and has room for empirical improvement.
- Major limitation: assumes data are nonnegative, which may be unrealistic for many applications.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a few UCI datasets and should include more challenging datasets (e.g., ImageNet, CIFAR-10).
- Originality is strong but the nonlinear spectral idea is not entirely new; relationship to related methods should be discussed.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and significant contribution with high scores (Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, potential practical utility, strong theoretical guarantees, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive nonnegativity assumption, limited experiments, insufficient discussion of related work.
