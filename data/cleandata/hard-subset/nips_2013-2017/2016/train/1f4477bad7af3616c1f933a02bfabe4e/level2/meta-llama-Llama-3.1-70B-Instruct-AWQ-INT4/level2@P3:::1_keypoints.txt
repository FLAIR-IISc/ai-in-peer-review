- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training generalized polynomial neural networks (1–2 hidden layers) that requires nonnegative weights and optimizes a modified objective.  
- Proves global optimality and linear convergence under certain conditions and reports competitive results on several UCI datasets.

- Strength: Provides a global optimality guarantee for the training method.  
- Strength: Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral approach that could extend to other networks.  
- Strength: Supplies thorough theoretical analysis, including convergence proofs.  
- Strength: Includes experimental validation showing competitive performance.

- Weakness: Experiments are limited to a few small UCI datasets and lack larger-scale evaluation.  
- Weakness: No detailed analysis of the computational complexity and practical runtime.  
- Weakness: Relies on assumptions about data and architecture (e.g., nonnegativity) that may not hold broadly.

- Originality/Significance: Method is original and significant for offering global optimality in neural network training.  
- Clarity/Organization: Paper is well organized and clearly presents the method and theory.  
- Writing Quality: Writing is clear, concise, and technically correct.

- For acceptance: Novel method with theoretical global-optimality guarantees and solid analysis.  
- Against acceptance: Limited experimental breadth and missing complexity analysis.

- Recommendation: Accept, with a request to include additional experiments on more and larger datasets.
