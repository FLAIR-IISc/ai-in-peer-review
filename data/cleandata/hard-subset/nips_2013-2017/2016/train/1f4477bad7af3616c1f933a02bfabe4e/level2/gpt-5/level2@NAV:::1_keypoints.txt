- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training feedforward nets with polynomial activations, nonnegative inputs, and nonnegative weights, claiming global optimality and linear convergence if ρ(A)<1.
- Frames approach using nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory and contraction in the Thompson metric, yielding a unique global optimum and extendable to two hidden layers.
- Empirical results show fast convergence and competitive accuracy on small UCI datasets but are limited in scale.
- Theoretical development is careful and largely self-contained with solid fixed-point mapping and contraction proofs; pre-run check via spectral radius is practical.
- Guarantees rely on strong modeling constraints (nonnegativity of data/weights, output-sum and ε terms, distinct exponents).
- Derived bounds appear conservative and may require large p values, potentially affecting model behavior.
- Paper is mathematically dense and generally well organized, but notation is heavy and practical guidance for choosing α, ρ, and p is limited.
- Originality is high: novel application of nonlinear spectral and multi-homogeneous Perron–Frobenius theory to global NN training.
- Significance is conceptual, showing a route to practical global optimality beyond shallow cases, but practical impact is limited by assumptions and sparse empirical validation.
- Pros: novel fixed-point training with verifiable linear-convergence condition, parameter-free iteration, and extension to two layers.
- Cons: strong restrictions (nonnegativity, modified objective, distinct exponents), conservative conditions, unclear trade-offs from large p and normalization, and small-scale empirical evaluation.
- Suggestion: provide ablations on output-sum and ε, analyze sensitivity to α, p, ρ and tightness of A-bounds.
- Suggestion: explore reparameterizations to handle signed data and relax nonnegativity (e.g., shifts, monotone decompositions).
- Suggestion: evaluate on larger nonnegative datasets (MNIST, bag-of-words), compare to NMF/monotone networks and constrained-positive ReLU baselines, automate parameter selection, and release code.
