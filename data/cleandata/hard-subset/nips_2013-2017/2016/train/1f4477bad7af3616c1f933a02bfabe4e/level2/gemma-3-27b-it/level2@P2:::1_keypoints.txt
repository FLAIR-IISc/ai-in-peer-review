- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method claiming linear convergence to a global optimum for generalized polynomial neural networks under nonnegativity assumptions.  
- Significant theoretical contribution because global optimization of neural nets is hard.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and places the work in context of prior optimization literature.  
- Mathematical development is rigorous with detailed proof strategy, though some parts are deferred to supplementary material.  
- Experiments show feasibility and competitive performance on low-dimensional UCI datasets, compared to SVM and ReLU networks.  
- Strong nonnegativity constraint on weights is a major limitation that reduces network expressiveness and practical applicability.  
- Need for comparison against unconstrained networks and exploration of regularization/stability alternatives is not addressed.  
- Derived parameter bounds (pu, pw, etc.) appear conservative; tighter analysis is needed.  
- Scalability concerns: spectral radius computation and overall complexity for larger networks/datasets are not analyzed; experiments limited by matrix size.  
- Justification for the modified objective (sum of outputs) is insufficient beyond a Perron–Frobenius connection; more intuition required.  
- Relationship between choice of α (polynomial powers) and model expressiveness is hand-wavy; guidance for selecting α is lacking.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: small dataset range, small networks, and lacks statistical significance testing (e.g., p-values).  
- Strengths summarized: novel globally optimal approach, rigorous proofs, clear presentation, demonstrated feasibility.  
- Weaknesses summarized: strong nonnegativity constraint, conservative bounds, scalability issues, weak objective justification, limited experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with linear convergence and initial empirical feasibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability due to constraints, unclear scalability, and conservative parameter bounds.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, conditional on addressing scalability, parameter bounds, nonnegativity impact, and expanding experimental validation.
