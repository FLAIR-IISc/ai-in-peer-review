- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training neural networks that guarantees global optimality under certain conditions.  
- Claims to be the first practically feasible algorithm achieving global optimality for a non-trivial neural network model.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, detailed explanations, mathematical derivations, and algorithmic implementation.  
- Main contribution: method for networks with one or two hidden layers that converges to the global optimum at a linear rate.  
- Provides analysis of conditions required for the convergence guarantees.  
- Experimental results on several UCI datasets demonstrate the method's effectiveness.  
- Strength: clear exposition and solid theoretical backing for the proposed approach.  
- Strength: empirical validation supporting practical utility on small benchmark datasets.  
- Weakness: method is limited to networks with one or two hidden layers; extension to deep networks is unclear.  
- Weakness: requires careful hyperparameter tuning (hidden units, activations, regularization).  
- Weakness: not directly comparable to common optimizers like SGD; scalability to larger datasets is unclear.  
- Recommendation: accept, but require authors to address limitations and provide more experiments on larger datasets.  
- Suggested revisions: compare to other optimization methods (e.g., SGD) and provide detailed computational-complexity analysis.  
- Encourage authors to work on extending the approach to deeper networks and exploring broader applications.
