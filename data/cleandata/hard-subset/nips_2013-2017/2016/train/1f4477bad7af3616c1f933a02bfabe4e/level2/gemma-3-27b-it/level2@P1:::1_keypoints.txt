- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method to train generalized polynomial neural networks with provable global optimality and linear convergence.
- Enforces non-negativity on weights and modifies the objective (adds sum of outputs) to enable theoretical guarantees.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presented, with complex theory broken into manageable sections.
- Strong theoretical contribution: global optimality guarantee and linear convergence distinguish it from SGD-based methods.
- Novel perspective by applying nonlinear spectral methods to neural network training.
- Experimental validation on UCI datasets shows feasibility and competitive performance, supporting the theory.
- Detailed analysis of algorithm dependence on model parameters and derived convergence conditions.
- Extension to two hidden layers increases practical relevance of the approach.
- Major limitation: non-negativity constraint restricts representational power and may hurt performance where negative weights help.
- Unusual modification of the objective (adding outputs) could alter learning dynamics; impact on generalization needs deeper analysis.
- Scalability concerns: computational complexity and handling of high-dimensional feature spaces are not clearly addressed.
- Derived bounds (e.g., spectral radius) are conservative, limiting efficiency and applicability to complex architectures.
- Comparisons to baselines are limited; more thorough evaluation against modern optimizers (Adam, RMSprop) is needed.
- Proofs are dense and notation-heavy, relying on supplementary material; would benefit from more intuitive explanations and intermediate steps.
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound, novel method with demonstrated feasibility and extension to deeper networks.
- Arguments against acceptance: practical applicability limited by non-negativity, scalability concerns, and incomplete baseline comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical paper worthy of acceptance pending revisions to address scalability, impact of non-negativity, and broader empirical comparisons.
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions).
