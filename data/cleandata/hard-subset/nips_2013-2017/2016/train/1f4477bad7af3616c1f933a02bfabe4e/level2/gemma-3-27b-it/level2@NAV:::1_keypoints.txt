- Introduces a novel method for training Generalized Polynomial Neural Networks (GPNNs) with guarantees of global optimality and linear convergence.  
- Achieves tractability by enforcing non-negative weights and reformulating the objective for a “nonlinear spectral method.”  
- Builds on prior globally optimal training work ([8, 11]) while aiming for more practical applicability.  
- Theoretical guarantees are clear and tied to the spectral radius of a small matrix, providing rigorous conditions for global optima.  
- Experimental validation on UCI datasets shows competitive performance and supports the theoretical claims.  
- Comparison to SGD with varying learning rates (Figure 2) is informative, demonstrating empirical advantages.  
- The non-negativity constraint is a strong limitation that may reduce network expressiveness and applicability.  
- Proofs are dense, heavily notated, and hard to follow without advanced spectral theory and optimization background.  
- Practical aspects of parameter choices (e.g., α, p, q) and their impact on convergence are not sufficiently explored.  
- Experimental scope is limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets and lacks comparisons with more state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions due to novelty, theoretical rigor, and initial experiments.  
- Suggested revisions: add intuitive explanations of key theory, discuss practical implications/mitigations for non-negativity, and expand experiments and comparisons.
