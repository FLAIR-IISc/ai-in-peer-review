- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks.  
- Claims linear convergence to a global optimum under relatively weak assumptions.  
- Method is restricted to one- and two-hidden-layer networks.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on low-dimensional UCI datasets.  
- Avoids practical difficulties of prior global methods (no density estimation or complex non-degeneracy checks).  
- Builds on recent globally optimal training work and positions itself well in related literature.  
- Mathematical development is rigorous with a reasonable proof sketch and deferred detailed proofs.  
- Theoretical guarantee of global optimality and linear convergence is a major strength.  
- Provides a practical procedure to verify assumptions before running the algorithm.  
- Leverages Perron–Frobenius theory as the theoretical foundation.  
- Experiments are limited in scope (small datasets) and not comprehensive.  
- Non-negativity constraint on weights is a significant and insufficiently justified limitation.  
- Use of generalized polynomial activations feels restrictive and its impact on performance is underexplored.  
- The bounds on pu and pw and related parts of Theorems 1 and 5 are complex and could be simplified.  
- Claims of “no tuning required” are misleading because spectral-method parameters still need cross-validated selection.  
- Comparison to standard optimizers (e.g., Adam, RMSprop) and larger datasets is missing.  
- Notation is dense and heavy; simplifying it would improve readability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, with specific requests to discuss non-negativity impact, broaden experiments, simplify notation, and clarify hyperparameter claims.
