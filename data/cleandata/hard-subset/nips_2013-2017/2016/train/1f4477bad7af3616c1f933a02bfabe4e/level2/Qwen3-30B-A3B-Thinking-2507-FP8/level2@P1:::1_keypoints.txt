- Proposes a globally optimal training method for a class of feedforward nets with generalized polynomial activations and nonnegative weights using nonlinear spectral methods.  
- Reformulates training as a fixed-point problem in the positive orthant and leverages nonlinear Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees (global optimality and linear convergence) for one- and two-hidden-layer networks under specified conditions.  
- Convergence condition is checkable a priori without running the algorithm.  
- Strength: rigorous, novel theoretical contribution with clear, correct proofs uncommon in neural network training literature.  
- Strength: more practically feasible than prior global methods (e.g., avoids estimating data-generating measures).  
- Strength: explicit conditions and implementation guidelines; algorithmic description is clear.  
- Experiments on UCI datasets support theoretical claims and show competitive performance with ReLU nets and SVMs.  
- Weakness: nonnegative weights constraint severely limits applicability and expressive power in real-world problems.  
- Weakness: authors do not sufficiently justify why nonnegative constraint won't harm expressiveness.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional datasets and do not demonstrate scalability to high-dimensional, modern tasks.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of practical implications of parameter choices and the use of generalized polynomial activations versus standard activations.  
- Overall assessment: technically strong and novel theoretical advance, but practical impact is limited by restrictive assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better address limitations and practical implications; future work should relax nonnegativity, extend to deeper nets, and test on larger datasets.
