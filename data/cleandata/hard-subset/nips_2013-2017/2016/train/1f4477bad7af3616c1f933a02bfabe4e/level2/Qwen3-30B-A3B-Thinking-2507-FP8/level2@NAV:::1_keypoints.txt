- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method (NLSM) with theoretical guarantees for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks with nonnegative weights.
- Builds on Perron-Frobenius theory for nonlinear eigenproblems; proofs (Theorems 1 and 5) are rigorous and provide clear convergence conditions.
- Shows convergence to a unique global optimum for one- and two-hidden-layer networks under specific data and weight constraints (nonnegative data/weights).
- Gives a checkable convergence condition: spectral radius of a small matrix < 1, verifiable before training.
- Claims linear convergence rates and no hyperparameter tuning required.
- Empirically competitive on low-dimensional UCI datasets (e.g., Cancer, Banknote), with NLSM1/NLSM2 often matching or beating ReLU networks and SVMs.
- Demonstrates compelling linear convergence vs. SGD variants (Figure 2).
- Major limitation: nonnegative weight requirement severely restricts applicability to real-world networks that need negative weights.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to low-dimensional UCI tasks; scalability is questionable since theoretical bounds grow with hidden units.
- Lacks thorough discussion on selecting optimal parameters for practical applications.
- Recommendation: the paper would be stronger by relaxing the nonnegativity constraint or demonstrating performance on higher-dimensional datasets.
