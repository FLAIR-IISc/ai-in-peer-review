- Studies a special class of neural networks and proves the model has a single stationary point.  
- Introduces a fast algorithm that converges to the unique fixed point which is the global minimum of the cost.  
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose fixed point equals the unique minimizer.  
- Highlights an interesting result that complex models with carefully chosen constraints can admit unique stationary points.  
- Convergence proof depends on bounding the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, limiting scalability to larger hidden layers.  
- Method requires many hyperparameters to be tuned, making the procedure unintuitive and cumbersome.  
- The spectral-condition must be verified for every parameter choice, adding extra verification overhead.  
- Experiments involved ~150,000 parameter combinations selected by cross-validation.  
- Empirically, the method outperformed linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, calling the practical utility of the investigated network into question.
