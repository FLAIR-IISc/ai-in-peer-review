- Paper defines a class of feedforward neural networks with a provable unique global optimizer.  
- Presents an algorithm that finds this global optimizer with linear convergence.  
- Mathematical derivations are instructive but some parts are unclear.  
- Question: Theorem 3 uses first-order derivative inequalities while Theorem 4 involves second derivatives — why the shift?  
- Question: The last layer is treated separately; is the gradient w.r.t. u just the gradient of vectorized u, and is this consistent across the paper?  
- Concern: Several constructions (e.g., bounding u in a ball) and treatments of u are not clearly justified.  
- Question/concern: Why does the model maximize the 1-norm of connection parameters in (2) when typical NNs use weight decay to reduce norms?  
- Observation: The small epsilon in (2) appears to be only to force strictly positive gradients (V_++), which seems like a proof artifact lacking practical justification.  
- Concern: Requiring non-negative data and strictly positive weights (no zeros) may make the model impractical and prone to unbounded growth.  
- Experiments: Comparison is made to SVM due to its global optimizer; question whether the proposed NN offers practical advantages over common NNs (e.g., with ReLU).
