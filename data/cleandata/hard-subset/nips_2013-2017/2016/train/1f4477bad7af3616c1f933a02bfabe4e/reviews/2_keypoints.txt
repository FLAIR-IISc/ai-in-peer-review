- Paper studies feedforward nets trainable to global optimum with linear convergence via a nonlinear spectral method for 1- and 2-hidden-layer networks, with experiments on real datasets.  
- Method imposes restrictive/counterintuitive assumptions (non-negative weights, heavy regularization) and requires an uncommon generalized polynomial activation, which is not realistic for many applications.  
- Authors' claim that the targeted class retains sufficient expressive power is not convincingly supported by the experiments.  
- Empirical comparisons are unfair: linear SVM baseline appears untuned, and no nonlinear SVM baselines (e.g., RBF or polynomial kernels) are used.  
- Datasets (UCI) are too small in sample size and dimensionality to validate scalability or real-world applicability.  
- Bounds on the spectral radius of matrix A grow with hidden-unit count, raising scalability issues for high-dimensional data.  
- The optimality condition may not be satisfied for practical parameter settings; checking is possible but infeasible without priors or a large parameter search—need faster ways to identify valid candidates.  
- It is unclear if global optimality is due to the special objective; recommend testing whether SGD can also empirically reach global optima on the datasets.
