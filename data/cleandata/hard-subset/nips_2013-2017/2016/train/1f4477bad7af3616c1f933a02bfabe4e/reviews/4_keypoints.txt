- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a particular class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence rate.  
- Global optimality condition depends on network architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The approach is new and interesting conceptually.  
- Major limitation: requires non-negativity constraints on network weights.  
- Major limitation: uses non-standard, very specific activation functions.  
- These strong restrictions may hinder practical performance of the neural networks.  
- Empirical results show the method performs worse than SVM in the reported experiments.  
- Minor comment: claim that linear convergence "typically" implies convergence in fewer than 10 iterations is exaggerated.
