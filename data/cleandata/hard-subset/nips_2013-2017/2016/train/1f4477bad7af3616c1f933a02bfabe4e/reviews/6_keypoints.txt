- Proposes a method to train a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks, proves optimal convergence, and demonstrates applicability on some real-world problems.  
- The problem is interesting and the theoretical advance is appreciated.  
- Major concern: paper does not convincingly show that the restricted network class is useful in practice; expressiveness needs explicit discussion (what is possible vs out of reach).  
- Concurrent/related approaches are not sufficiently discussed or contrasted.  
- Experiments are too limited: comparisons only against some SVMs and omit other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms.  
- Experimental results do not clarify the model’s applicability limits; missing information on computational resources for the three algorithms.  
- Presentation needs polishing: introduction is good but later sections (especially Section 2) lack fluency.  
- Several typos and notation issues that should be fixed (e.g., “polyomial” p60; “one CAN model” p67; p1 and p2 not introduced; R_{++} undefined).  
- Technical clarifications required: motivate the function Φ and the ε term in the paper (not just rebuttal); give an informal explanation before stating Theorem 1; clarify that the “converges quickly” claim is based on empirical observation.
