- Proposes a method to train generalized polynomial neural networks with provable global optimality and linear convergence.  
- Addresses non-convexity by imposing constraints that make the optimization tractable and globally solvable.  
- Builds on prior work [8,11] while avoiding their practical limitations (measure estimation, limited applicability).  
- Theoretical results are rigorous with a clear proof roadmap and a logical extension to two hidden layers.  
- Experimental results are limited to small datasets but act as a proof of concept; algorithm included for reproducibility.  
- Convergence bounds (Equations 4 and 9) are conservative and the authors explicitly acknowledge this.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with well-defined notation and helpful lemmas/theorems guiding the theory.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: provide more intuition before presenting equations in Section 4 (motivation for Ψαp,q).  
- Originality stems from the specific network class (positive inputs, linear weights, generalized polynomial activations) and the nonlinear spectral method.  
- Novel technique: constraint satisfaction check via the spectral radius of a small matrix.  
- Significant potential impact: globally optimal training for a restricted class could reduce hyperparameter tuning and improve reliability.  
- Major limitation: non-negativity constraint on inputs/weights restricts model expressiveness.  
- Practicality concern: using different activation functions per layer is unusual and may limit applicability.  
- Risk of collapsing networks: spectral radius constraint could force reduced linear weights and near-linear behavior.  
- Scalability concerns: constraint may be harder to satisfy as input/parameter dimensionality increases.  
- Limited empirical validation: need experiments on larger, more realistic datasets.  
- For acceptance: addresses a fundamental optimization challenge with compelling provable results.  
- Against acceptance: applicability is severely limited by non-negativity and scalability issues.  
- Suggested experiments: empirically plot how p1,p2,pw,pu, input dimension, and network size affect spectral radius ρ(A).  
- Suggested research directions: explore relaxation strategies for the non-negativity constraint and evaluate on larger datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: reviewer finds the work highly encouraging and recommends acceptance despite limitations.
