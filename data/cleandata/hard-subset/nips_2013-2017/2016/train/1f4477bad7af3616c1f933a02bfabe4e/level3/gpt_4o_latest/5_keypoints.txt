- Theoretical contribution: Proves existence of a unique global optimizer for the proposed architecture and derives convergence conditions for a nonlinear spectral method.  
- Linear convergence: Algorithm achieves a linear convergence rate under specified conditions, outperforming typical SGD rates.  
- Mathematical rigor: Detailed derivations using fixed-point theory and Banach fixed-point theorem provide a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Practical feasibility: Method is computationally feasible and avoids complex operations like tensor decomposition.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on low-dimensional UCI datasets show competitive results versus ReLU networks and kernel SVMs on some tasks.  

- Clarity of derivations: Transition from first-order to second-order derivative arguments (Theorems 3→4) is unclear and needs more explanation or examples.  
- Network design: Treating the last layer differently raises interpretability concerns, especially about gradients for parameters like u.  
- Practical constraints: Requirement for non-negative data and strictly positive weights limits applicability and generalizability.  
- 1-norm maximization: Use of 1-norm maximization for connection parameters contradicts standard weight decay and lacks practical justification.  
- Experimental scope: Limited to low-dimensional datasets; scalability and performance on high-dimensional or complex datasets are untested and ReLU comparisons are constrained by architectural limits.  

- For acceptance: Addresses a hard problem with a novel, theoretically grounded method offering global optimality and linear convergence.  
- For acceptance: Provides a computationally feasible algorithm that offers new perspectives on provable NN optimization.  
- Against acceptance: Practical limitations (non-negativity, weight constraints) restrict real-world applicability.  
- Against acceptance: Unclear mathematical transitions and limited experiments weaken impact.  
- Against acceptance: Comparisons with standard neural networks are not comprehensive, leaving practical benefits uncertain.  

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify derivations, justify design choices, and expand experimental evaluation to higher-dimensional datasets and stronger baselines.
