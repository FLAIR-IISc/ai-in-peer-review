- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks that attains global optimality.
- Method requires nonnegativity of network weights and optimizes a modified objective (loss plus negative total output sum).
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with a main theorem guaranteeing convergence to the global optimum.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on several UCI datasets.
- Originality lies in a novel combination of familiar techniques and a clear distinction from prior work.
- Significance: advances state of the art by addressing a difficult problem with a unique theoretical/practical approach.
- Practicality concern: the restricted network class’s usefulness in real applications is not convincingly shown.
- Expressiveness of the model is not discussed or justified.
- Lacks thorough experimental comparisons with other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithms.
- Experimental results do not clarify the model’s applicability limits and omit computational resource details for the algorithms.
- Presentation issues: Section 2 lacks fluency; several typos and notation problems need fixing.
- Technical clarifications requested: motivate the function Φ and the ε term; provide an informal explanation before Theorem 1.
- Reproducibility: enough for expert readers but some sections require more detail.
- Recommendation: revise to include broader comparisons, clarify applicability/compute costs, discuss expressiveness, fix notation/typos, and improve presentation.
