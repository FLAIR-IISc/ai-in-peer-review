- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method claiming global optimality with linear convergence for 1–2 hidden layer networks.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous fixed-point/Banach analysis and comprehensive proofs.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear progression and supplementary material for intermediate results.  
- Experiments on UCI datasets show competitive performance versus ReLU networks and linear SVM.  
- Method requires restrictive assumptions (non-negative weights, specific generalized polynomial activations) limiting applicability.  
- Authors’ claim that constraints still yield complex decision boundaries is not convincingly supported by experiments.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: uses linear SVM baseline instead of nonlinear kernels and relies on small, low-dimensional datasets.  
- Scalability concerns: spectral-radius bounds grow with hidden units, questioning applicability to larger/high-dimensional datasets.  
- No practical guidance for setting hyperparameters (n1, α, ρw, ρu); authors resort to costly random search over 100 combos.  
- The spectral-radius condition ρ(A) < 1 must be checked per configuration with no efficient strategy provided.  
- Pro: first theoretically rigorous globally optimal training method with linear convergence and sound math.  
- Con: restrictive assumptions, insufficient empirical rigor, heavy parameter search, and unclear practical advantage over standard training.  
- Overall: interesting and solid theory but limited practical significance and questionable scalability.  
- Recommendation: Weak Reject.
