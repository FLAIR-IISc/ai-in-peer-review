- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training "generalized polynomial neural networks" with provable global optimality and linear convergence.
- Global-optimality conditions are simple and depend only on the spectral radius of a nonnegative matrix derived from network parameters.
- Paper is well-written and clear, with rigorous theoretical analysis supporting the claims.
- Experimental validation on a 2D synthetic dataset and small UCI datasets demonstrates the method’s effectiveness.
- Main novelty: a potentially practical network training algorithm with explicit optimality and convergence guarantees under checkable preconditions.
- Major limitation: requires nonnegative inputs and nonnegative linear-layer weights, which may reduce model expressiveness.
- Architectural limitation: each hidden layer uses a different activation function, which is unnatural and restricts applicability.
- Practical concern: the spectral-radius < 1 constraint forces adjusting p-values and pw/pu that effectively shrink linear weights and can collapse deep networks toward near-linear behavior.
- Scalability concern: the above constraint raises doubts about extending the method to deep architectures.
- Suggestion: empirically investigate and plot how p1, p2, pw, pu, input dimension, and network size affect the spectral radius ρ(A).
- Claims are technically sound and the paper provides enough detail for expert reproduction.
- Overall assessment and recommendation: high originality/significance, highly relevant to NIPS, reviewer recommends acceptance.
