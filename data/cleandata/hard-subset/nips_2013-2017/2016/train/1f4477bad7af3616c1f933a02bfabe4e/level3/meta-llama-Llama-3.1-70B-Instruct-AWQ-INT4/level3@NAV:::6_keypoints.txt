- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method to train generalized polynomial neural networks with proofs of global optimality and convergence.
- Tackles the fundamental non-convexity problem in neural network optimization.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including convergence rate characterization.
- Novel combination of nonlinear eigenvalue theory and fixed point theory applied to neural network training.
- Practical usefulness of the restricted network class is unclear; more discussion on expressiveness and limitations required.
- Lacks thorough comparison with concurrent/related optimization methods and state-of-the-art approaches.
- Experiments are limited; needs more datasets and empirical comparisons to other training methods.
- Presentation issues—Section 2 lacks fluency—and several typos/notation problems to fix.
- Paper is generally well-organized and reproducible for experts but needs more intuitive explanations.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on addressing expressiveness discussion and expanding experiments.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical approach and significant contribution towards solving non-convexity.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient empirical evaluation, weak comparisons, and presentation deficiencies.
