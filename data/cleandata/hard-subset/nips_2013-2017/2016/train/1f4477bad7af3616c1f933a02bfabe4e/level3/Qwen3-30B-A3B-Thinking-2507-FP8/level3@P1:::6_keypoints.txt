- Proposes a globally optimal training method for a restricted class of feedforward nets using nonlinear spectral methods with convergence guarantees.  
- Proves global optimality and linear convergence for generalized polynomial networks with nonnegative weights and real-power activations.  
- Key innovation: modified objective Φ enabling application of Perron–Frobenius theory to neural network training.  
- Theoretical strength: rigorous proofs for one- and two-hidden-layer networks, advancing understanding of globally optimal training.  
- Empirical claim: method is practically feasible and competitive on UCI datasets vs SVMs and ReLU networks.  
- Major limitation: restrictive assumptions (nonnegative weights, power activations) substantially limit expressiveness compared to standard networks.  
- Missing analysis of expressiveness: no characterization of possible/impossible decision boundaries (e.g., XOR-like patterns).  
- Related work coverage is weak: insufficient contrast with Haeffele & Vidal, Janzamin et al., and omission of SOS/convex-relaxation global-training methods.  
- Experimental baselines are limited: comparisons exclude standard optimizers (SGD, Adam, Adagrad) and other relevant methods.  
- Lacks computational-cost analysis: no wall-clock time, memory usage, or scalability results (behavior with dimensionality/dataset size and parameters like ρ(A) < 1).  
- Presentation problems: Section 2 is technically dense without intuitive explanation; motivation for Φ and ε is unclear before Theorem 1.  
- Several typos and inconsistent notation (e.g., “polyomial”, undefined R_{++}, inconsistent p1/p2) need correction.  
- Some claims are overstated or unsupported (e.g., “converges quickly”, “first practically feasible” require stronger justification).  
- Recommended fixes: analyze expressiveness limits, add stronger baselines and computational profiling, clarify Φ/ε motivation, and transparently discuss limitations.  
- Overall verdict: notable theoretical contribution but practical relevance is questionable; substantial revision required before publication.
