This paper presents a novel approach to training feedforward neural networks with a provable unique global optimizer. The authors propose a class of neural networks that can be trained globally optimal with a linear convergence rate using a nonlinear spectral method. The main contribution of the paper is the development of a framework that allows for the global optimization of a specific class of neural networks, which is a significant departure from traditional stochastic gradient descent methods.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear presentation of the main ideas and results. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and the limitations of existing methods, which helps to motivate the need for a new approach. The mathematical derivations are instructive and provide a good understanding of the underlying principles.

However, there are some parts of the paper that are unclear or require further justification. Furthermore, some constructions, such as bounding u in a ball, are not clearly justified. The authors should provide more explanation for these choices and how they affect the overall results. Additionally, the model's maximization of the 1-norm of connection parameters in (2) is not well-motivated, as typical neural networks use weight decay to reduce norms.

The small epsilon in (2) appears to be only a proof artifact lacking practical justification. The authors should provide more explanation for the necessity of this term and how it affects the overall results.

Moreover, requiring non-negative data and strictly positive weights (no zeros) may make the model impractical and prone to unbounded growth. The authors should discuss the limitations of their approach and how it can be applied to real-world problems.
