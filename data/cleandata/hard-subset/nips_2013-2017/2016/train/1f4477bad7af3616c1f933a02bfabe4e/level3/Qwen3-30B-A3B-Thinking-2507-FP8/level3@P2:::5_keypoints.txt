- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for training feedforward nets with nonnegative weights/data and polynomial (α ≥ 1) activations, claiming global optimality and linear convergence.
- Practical method with convergence rate guarantee, building on Janzamin et al. (2015) and Haeffele & Vidal (2015) but offering greater implementability.
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous but contains gaps, notably an unexplained shift from first-order to second-order analysis between Theorems 3 and 4.
- Inconsistent treatment of the gradient with respect to the weight matrix u (sometimes vectorized, sometimes matrix) requires clarification.
- Nonnegativity constraints on weights and data are highly restrictive and limit practical applicability to standard networks.
- Maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters and inclusion of a small ε term appear to be proof artifacts and lack clear practical justification.
- Mathematical development is generally clear and well-structured; use of the Thompson metric is insightful.
- Technical details are unclear in places—construction of matrix A in Theorem 1 and rationale for certain constraints need more explanation and intuition.
- Originality: first practically feasible method with provable global optimization for this class, linking neural network training to nonlinear Perron-Frobenius theory.
- Significance: important theoretical advance; experiments on UCI datasets show competitive performance but comparisons are limited.
- Convergence condition ρ(A) < 1 is conservative and may not hold for larger or more complex networks.
- Method does not generalize to standard architectures (e.g., ReLU) and comparisons to standard neural-network training procedures are limited.
- Recommendation: accept as an important theoretical step, provided authors fix proof gaps, better justify modeling choices, and broaden empirical comparisons.
