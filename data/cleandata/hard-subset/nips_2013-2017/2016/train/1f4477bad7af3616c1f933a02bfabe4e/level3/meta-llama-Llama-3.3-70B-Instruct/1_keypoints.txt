- Presents a comprehensive, global analysis of the EM algorithm for Gaussian mixture models, characterizing stationary points and dynamics in the large-sample (population) limit.  
- Uses the Population EM framework to obtain global characterizations not achievable by traditional local-convergence analyses.  
- Shows statistical consistency: EM can compute consistent parameter estimates without special initialization under the model assumptions.  
- Analysis and proofs are rigorous and thorough, with theoretical results supported by experiments.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with consistent notation and helpful figures/tables.  
- Provides a comprehensive review of related work and situates its contributions clearly.  
- Novel contribution: applying Population EM to give new insights into EM’s global behavior.  
- Potentially significant impact on statistical modeling and EM-based parameter estimation in machine learning.  
- Main limitation: analysis is restricted to Gaussian mixture models, so generalization to other latent-variable models is unclear.  
- Assumes large sample sizes (population limit), which may limit practical applicability in small-sample settings.  
- Could benefit from additional experimental results to better illustrate practical implications.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, original, and significant, despite the noted limitations.
