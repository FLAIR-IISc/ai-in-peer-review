- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral algorithm that yields a provable unique global optimizer for a class of feedforward neural networks.  
- Claims linear convergence rate and global optimality, a substantial improvement over SGD.  
- Applies to one- and two-hidden-layer networks with experiments on UCI datasets showing competitive performance vs SVM and ReLU networks.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains mathematical derivations and theorems.  
- Main contribution is a globally optimal training algorithm for a specific network class.  
- Strength: clarity, structure, and concise summary of main results.  
- Major concern: requires non-negative data and strictly positive weights, which may be impractical and risk unbounded growth; needs justification.  
- Concern: objective function modification that maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters is unclear and its relation to weight decay must be explained.  
- Concern: role and practical significance of the small epsilon in the objective are unclear and appear to be a proof artifact.  
- Needs discussion of how results generalize to broader neural network architectures and the limitations of the approach.  
- Originality: novel and innovative use of nonlinear spectral methods in neural network training.  
- Significance: potentially important implications for optimization in neural networks, but limited by restrictive assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address assumptions, objective choice, epsilon, and generalizability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical guarantees, clarity, and impact on optimization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: impractical assumptions (non-negativity/positive weights), unclear objective choice, and unexplained epsilon.
