- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method guaranteeing global optimality and linear convergence for generalized polynomial neural nets with one or two hidden layers.  
- Core idea: enforce non-negative weights, heavy regularization, and polynomial activations to convert the non-convex problem into a globally solvable one.  
- Theoretical framework is rigorous, leveraging nonlinear spectral theory and Perron–Frobenius, and avoids tensor-decomposition challenges.  
- Extension to two hidden layers is presented, suggesting limited scalability of the approach.  
- Major limitation: restrictive assumptions (non-negative weights and polynomial activations) severely limit real-world applicability.  
- Claim of sufficient expressive power for the targeted class is not convincingly supported by experiments.  
- Empirical validation is weak: toy example and small UCI datasets are insufficient to demonstrate learning complex decision boundaries or scalability.  
- Baseline comparisons are unfair/incomplete: linear SVM appears untuned and nonlinear SVMs (RBF/polynomial) are missing.  
- Practical scalability concern: spectral radius of matrix A grows with hidden units, potentially preventing application to larger networks/datasets.  
- Verifying theoretical conditions in practice may be hard and bounds could be conservative, implying nontrivial tuning/search is needed.  
- Open question whether global optimality is due to the modified objective (regularization) rather than inherent problem structure; need to test standard SGD on same tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel global-optimality guarantee, rigorous math, and first practically feasible globally optimal algorithm for neural nets under constraints.  
- Arguments against acceptance: highly restrictive assumptions, unconvincing experiments, scalability issues, and questionable relevance to standard NN training.  
- Overall recommendation: reject, with encouragement to relax assumptions, strengthen empirical evaluation (stronger baselines, larger datasets), and examine whether SGD can reach similar optima.
