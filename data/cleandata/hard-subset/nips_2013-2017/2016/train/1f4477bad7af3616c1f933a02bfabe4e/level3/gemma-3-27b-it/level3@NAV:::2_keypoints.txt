- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimizing one- and two-hidden-layer feedforward networks under non-negativity and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves conditions for global optimality and linear convergence.  
- Provides a practical check for convergence conditions using the spectral radius of a small matrix.  
- Extends the approach to two hidden layers, indicating potential generalization.  
- Imposed constraints (non-negative weights, specialized activation) are highly restrictive and deviate from standard practice.  
- Expressive power claims are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Experimental validation is weak: small UCI datasets and potentially unfair baselines.  
- Missing crucial nonlinear SVM baselines (RBF, polynomial) and tuning concerns for the linear SVM baseline.  
- Conservative spectral radius bounds suggest scalability issues as hidden unit counts increase.  
- Checking/identifying valid parameters to meet optimality conditions is impractical in most settings.  
- Unclear whether observed global optimality arises from the modified objective or could be achieved by standard methods like SGD; necessary control experiments are missing.  
- Recommendation: reject; requires stronger experiments, scalability demonstration, and analysis comparing to standard optimization methods for reconsideration.
