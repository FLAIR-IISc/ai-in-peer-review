- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for a restricted class of feed-forward nets with generalized polynomial activations and non-negative weights.  
- Main contribution: proof that under checkable conditions the method converges linearly to the unique global optimum.  
- Builds on prior work aiming to overcome non-convexity, offering a potentially more practical alternative to tensor-based/theoretical methods.  
- Strength: strong theoretical contribution tackling global optimality in non-convex neural network training.  
- Strength: novel use of Perron-Frobenius theory and an elegant, a priori spectral-radius check for convergence.  
- Major weakness: restrictive model assumptions (non-negative data/weights, polynomial activations) raise questions about practical utility.  
- Lacks analysis of model expressiveness—unclear which functions the network can or cannot approximate.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited, comparing only to SVMs and simple ReLU nets and omitting other relevant training methods.  
- Experiments do not demonstrate performance on more complex tasks or clarify model limitations.  
- Missing crucial experimental details such as computational resource requirements, hindering assessment of practical efficiency.  
- Related work discussion is brief and fails to adequately contrast with other provable network-training approaches.  
- Presentation issues: later theoretical sections lack fluency and intuitive explanations.  
- Needs informal motivation before Theorem 1 and better integration of the objective Φ and technical ε term.  
- Numerous typos and notational inconsistencies (e.g., “polyomial,” undefined symbols) reduce readability.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem and provides a novel, theoretically sound method with linear convergence.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient demonstration of practical utility/expressiveness, weak experimental validation, sparse related work discussion, and significant clarity problems.
