- Introduces a method to train a class of feedforward nets to a guaranteed global optimum with linear convergence, requiring nonnegative inputs/weights and per-unit polynomial activations (x^alpha with distinct alphas).
- Formulates training as constrained maximization on the positive orthant and develops a nonlinear spectral method using Perron–Frobenius theory; Theorem 1: if rho(A)<1 then a unique global maximizer exists and the algorithm converges linearly.
- Offers a practical algorithm whose preconditions are simple and checkable a priori, unlike prior tensor-based/global results that depend on data distributions or complex conditions.
- Theoretical development is rigorous and clear, leveraging fixed-point theory on convex cones and the Thompson metric.
- The optimality condition rho(A)<1 is simple to verify: A is a small (K+1)x(K+1) matrix computable from model hyperparameters.
- Proof-of-concept experiments on a synthetic 2D dataset and small UCI datasets show competitive performance versus ReLU nets and SVMs.
- Main weakness: restrictive nonnegativity constraints on inputs and especially weights limit expressiveness by disallowing inhibitory connections.
- Architectural constraint requiring distinct activation powers per hidden unit (alpha_i != alpha_j) is unnatural and complicates network design.
- Scalability concern: entries of A grow with number of hidden units, input norm, and weight norms, making rho(A)<1 harder to satisfy for larger/deeper networks.
- Meeting rho(A)<1 may force increasing p-values or shrinking weight norms, which risks collapsing the network toward near-linear behavior and causing vanishing signals in deep models.
- Reviewer recommends empirically studying how rho(A) varies with n1, input dimensionality, and hyperparameters (pw, pu, rho_w, rho_u) to map the method’s practical feasibility region.
- Reviewer asks for a deeper discussion of trade-offs in satisfying rho(A)<1, particularly the impact of shrinking weight norms on model expressiveness.
- Overall verdict: Accept — significant, novel, and rigorous contribution that opens a promising research direction despite expressiveness and scalability limitations.
