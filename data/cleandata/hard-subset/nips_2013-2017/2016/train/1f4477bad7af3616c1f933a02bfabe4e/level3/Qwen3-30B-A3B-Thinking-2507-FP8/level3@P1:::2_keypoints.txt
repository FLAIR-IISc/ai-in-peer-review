- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of one- and two-hidden-layer feedforward networks with linear convergence.  
- Claims practical global optimality under constraints: non-negative weights, generalized polynomial activations with distinct exponents, and a modified objective.  
- Convergence guarantee hinges on spectral radius of a small matrix being <1, which is checkable a priori.  
- Builds on prior global optimization work but offers a practical algorithm that avoids density estimation (unlike tensor methods).  
- Demonstrated on several UCI datasets, reporting competitive performance versus ReLU networks and SVMs.  
- Theoretical strengths: technically sound fixed-point theory, elegant mathematical presentation, and clear linear convergence proof.  
- Major limitation: restrictive assumptions (non-negative weights, specific activations) severely limit real-world applicability.  
- Empirical support for "models are rich enough" is weak; results are only marginally competitive and do not prove constraints don't harm representation learning.  
- Experimental comparisons are unfair—SVM baseline appears linear; standard nonlinear SVMs (RBF/polynomial) were not used.  
- UCI datasets are too small and low-dimensional to demonstrate scalability or practical relevance.  
- Scalability concern: spectral radius grows with hidden unit count, with no proposed remedies or empirical scalability analysis.  
- Practical implementation issue: checking spectral radius reportedly requires ~100 random parameter trials, which is impractical without parameter-selection guidance.  
- Unclear whether global optimality arises from the algorithm itself or from the specially modified objective; need SGD comparisons to test reachability of the same optima.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and clarity contributions and originality, but practical limitations, unfair experiments, and lack of scalability guidance substantially reduce impact.  
- Suggested improvements: include nonlinear SVM baselines, larger/high-dimensional datasets, clearer parameter-selection guidance, and tests comparing to tuned SGD.
