- Proposes Nonlinear Spectral Method (NLSM) for training "Generalized Polynomial Neural Networks" with global optimality under certain data assumptions.  
- Demonstrates applicability on several real-world datasets with competitive performance versus some baselines.  
- Major theoretical advance: provides a provably globally convergent training method for a restricted neural network class.  
- Applicability strength: method shown to work on real data, suggesting practical relevance.  
- Weakness — limited expressiveness: paper does not convincingly show the restricted network class is broadly useful in practice.  
- Weakness — limited comparisons: experiments compare mainly to some SVMs and omit other neural-network methods and modern baselines.  
- Weakness — missing computational cost details: no information on computational resources or runtime for the compared algorithms.  
- Weakness — presentation issues: later sections (especially Section 2) lack fluency and need polishing for readability.  
- Weakness — typos and notation problems: several errors (e.g., "polyomial"), undefined or unexplained notation (e.g., R_{++}, p1, p2).  
- Originality: approach is novel and distinct from existing training methods.  
- Significance: significant contribution as a method providing global training guarantees for a neural network class.  
- Clarity: overall well-structured and explained, but later sections require improvement.  
- Quality: high-quality theoretical contribution, but empirical and presentation issues need addressing.  
- Recommendation: conditional accept, requiring discussion of expressiveness, broader comparisons, computational resource reporting, presentation polishing, and fixing typos/notation.
