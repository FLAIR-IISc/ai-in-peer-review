- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method to train generalized polynomial neural networks (GPNs) with a provable global optimum and linear convergence under conditions.  
- Main contributions: defines a class of feedforward networks with a unique global optimizer and provides an algorithm that converges linearly to it.  
- Paper is well-written and mathematical derivations are instructive, though some derivation steps are unclear and require careful reading.  
- Main theorem and its implications are clearly explained and accessible to non-experts.  
- Strength: gives a provable global optimum for a class of networks, addressing non-convex optimization issues in deep learning.  
- Unclear justification for shifting from first-order derivative inequalities in Theorem 3 to second derivatives in Theorem 4.  
- Inconsistent treatment of the last layer; gradient w.r.t. u is not clearly defined (vectorized or not).  
- Some constructions (e.g., bounding u in a ball) are not clearly justified.  
- Objective in (2) maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters, which is atypical compared to usual weight decay—needs explanation.  
- The small epsilon in (2) appears to be a proof artifact to force strictly positive gradients (V_++), lacking practical justification.  
- Requirement of non-negative data and strictly positive (nonzero) weights may be impractical and could lead to unbounded growth; implications and mitigations should be discussed.  
- Experimental comparison to SVM is not entirely fair; should compare to common neural networks (e.g., ReLU) to demonstrate practical advantages.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted technical and experimental concerns.
