- Presents a comprehensive, global analysis of the EM algorithm for Gaussian mixture model parameter estimation.  
- Characterizes stationary points and dynamics of EM in the large-sample (population) limit.  
- Establishes statistical consistency of EM for specific models, notably mixtures of two Gaussians.  
- Analysis is rigorous and uses a variety of mathematical tools (linear algebra, probability).  
- Provides a significant improvement over prior work focused on local convergence.  
- Shows EM can yield statistically consistent estimates without specialized initialization.  
- Analysis is restricted to Gaussian mixture models, limiting generality to other latent-variable models.  
- Results are asymptotic, leaving finite-sample performance and practical robustness unclear.  
- Paper could use more discussion of practical implications and guidance for practitioners.  
- Well-written and easy to follow, though some notation/terminology may be unfamiliar to non-experts.  
- Original contribution: novel global perspective on EM and notable theoretical advances.  
- Potentially significant impact on ML and statistics (clustering, density estimation, regression).  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
