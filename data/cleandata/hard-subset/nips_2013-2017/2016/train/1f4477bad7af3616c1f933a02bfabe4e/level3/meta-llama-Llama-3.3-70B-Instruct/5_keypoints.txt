- Presents a comprehensive global analysis of EM for Gaussian mixture models, characterizing stationary points and algorithm dynamics in the large-sample limit.  
- Establishes statistical consistency of EM, showing it can compute consistent parameter estimates without special initialization.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, making arguments and results easy to follow.  
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous and instructive overall.  
- Some derivation steps need clarification, notably the transition from first to second derivatives in Theorems 3 and 4.  
- Contributions are significant, advancing theoretical understanding of EM behavior for GMMs.  
- Results have important practical implications, suggesting EM can be reliable and efficient for parameter estimation.  
- Raises interesting, open questions about extending results to settings where dimensionality grows with sample size; authors conjecture results may still hold.  
- Scores well on conference criteria: quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Main limitations: assumes data are generated from a Gaussian mixture model and may not generalize to more complex or high-dimensional settings.  
- Recommendation: accept, as the paper provides novel, rigorous, and practically relevant contributions despite minor issues.
