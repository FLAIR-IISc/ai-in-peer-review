- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training a specific class of feedforward neural networks.  
- Method is conceptually new and provides a thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Main theoretical strength: globally optimal training for the considered network class.  
- Achieves linear convergence rate, claimed improvement over SGD.  
- Analysis includes dependence of the spectral radius on model parameters.  
- Method requires non-negativity constraints on network weights, limiting practicality.  
- Uses non-standard, very specific activation functions, reducing general applicability.  
- Empirical results reported are worse than SVM on the tested tasks, raising practical-performance concerns.  
- Paper is well-written and well-organized but assumes high mathematical sophistication.  
- Originality: significant new training method but limited to a narrow class of networks.  
- Significance: important theoretical result, but practical impact remains unclear without more experiments.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, linear convergence, and thorough spectral analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive weight constraints, peculiar activations, and weak empirical results.  
- Recommendation: accept but request authors address limitations, relax constraints/explore activations, add experiments, and detail computational complexity.
