- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method with a provable convergence guarantee for globally optimizing a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks.  
- Addresses an important and challenging problem: achieving global optimality in neural network training.  
- The considered network class is highly restrictive; paper lacks characterization of representational capacity and examples of functions inside/outside this class.  
- Claim that models are “rich enough” for real-world tasks is unsupported by theoretical or empirical justification.  
- Related work discussion is insufficient; fails to contrast with recent non-convex optimization approaches and alternatives like SDP or ADMM-based methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is narrow: comparisons only to SVMs and no baselines such as standard gradient-based training or alternative optimizers.  
- Datasets are limited (mainly low-dimensional UCI), reducing generalizability of results.  
- Missing computational/resource analysis: no runtime, memory usage, or scaling behavior with network size and data dimensionality.  
- Presentation issues: Section 2 lacks fluency; contains typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., “polyomial”, “one CAN model”, undefined p1, p2, R_{++}).  
- Technical points need clarification: motivation for Φ and ε, justify/qualify the “quick convergence” claim as empirical, and provide an informal explanation before Theorem 1.  
- Strengths summarized: strong theoretical foundation, novelty of approach, and tackling a significant problem.  
- Main weaknesses summarized: limited expressiveness, inadequate related-work comparison, narrow experiments, and presentation/clarity problems—leading to recommendation for rejection unless addressed.
