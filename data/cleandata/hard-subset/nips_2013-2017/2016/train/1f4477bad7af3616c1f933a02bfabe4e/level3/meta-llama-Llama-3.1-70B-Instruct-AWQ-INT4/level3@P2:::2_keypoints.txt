- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training feedforward neural networks with a global optimality guarantee and linear convergence rate.  
- Method is restricted to one- and two-hidden-layer networks and requires non-negative weights plus a generalized polynomial activation.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: Lipschitz constant characterization, uniqueness of fixed point, and dependence of spectral radius on model parameters.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on several UCI datasets.  
- Restrictive assumptions (non-negative weights and heavy regularization) may be unrealistic for many applications.  
- Generalized polynomial activation may not be broadly suitable and expressive-power claims are not convincingly supported by experiments.  
- Empirical comparisons are unfair: linear SVM baseline appears untuned and no nonlinear SVM baselines (RBF/polynomial) were used.  
- Datasets used are small and low-dimensional, so results do not validate scalability or real-world applicability.  
- Bounds on the spectral radius of matrix A grow with hidden-unit count, raising scalability concerns for high-dimensional data.  
- The optimality condition may not be satisfied in practical parameter settings and verifying it is infeasible without strong priors or large parameter searches.  
- Unclear whether global optimality is intrinsic to the method or an artifact of the special objective; recommend testing if SGD can reach global optima on the same datasets.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-written with clear exposition.  
- Originality is notable but incremental: nonlinear spectral methods are not entirely new and more comparison/context is needed.  
- Significance is promising but limitations must be addressed before broad adoption.
