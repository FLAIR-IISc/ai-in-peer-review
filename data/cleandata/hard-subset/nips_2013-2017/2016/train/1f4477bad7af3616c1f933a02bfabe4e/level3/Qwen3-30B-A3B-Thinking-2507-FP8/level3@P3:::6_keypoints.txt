- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training a restricted class of feed-forward networks with provable global optimality under nonnegative data/weights and a modified objective.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong: rigorous proofs, linear convergence rate, and no hyperparameter tuning required.  
- Builds on nonlinear Perron-Frobenius and structured matrix factorization work (e.g., Haeffele & Vidal, Janzamin et al.) and claims practical feasibility.  
- Convergence guarantees require specific conditions (nonnegative data/weights, ε-term in objective) that limit generality.  
- Major concern: restricted network class (nonnegative weights) raises doubts about practical expressiveness and utility.  
- Experiments are limited to UCI datasets and do not compare against standard NN training methods (e.g., SGD with tuning).  
- Paper omits discussion of computational resources and runtime for the compared algorithms.  
- Related work section is inadequate; needs explicit contrasts and stronger justification for claiming novelty/practical feasibility over prior methods.  
- Presentation problems: Section 2 unclear, multiple typos and notation issues (e.g., "polyomial", "one CAN model", undefined R_{++}).  
- Motivation for the Φ function and the ε term is underexplained beyond mathematical derivation.  
- Empirical claim of "converges quickly" lacks theoretical justification.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound but practical relevance is questionable due to limited experiments and missing expressiveness analysis.  
- Originality: meaningful theoretical advance using nonlinear spectral methods, but impact limited by restrictive assumptions.  
- Recommendation: reject unless authors expand discussion of model expressiveness, add comprehensive experiments including standard baselines, better compare to related work, and improve presentation.
