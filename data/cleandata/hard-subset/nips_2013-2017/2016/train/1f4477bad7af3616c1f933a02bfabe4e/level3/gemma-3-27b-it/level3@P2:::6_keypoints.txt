- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks with proven linear convergence to a global optimum.  
- Theoretical guarantees are significant and address a central challenge in non-convex neural network optimization.  
- Method is demonstrated for one- and two-hidden-layer networks; authors claim generalizability but provide limited evidence.  
- Restriction to non-negative weights and polynomial activations substantially limits network expressiveness.  
- Paper lacks a clear analysis of which functions the network can or cannot represent and the trade-off between expressiveness and global optimality.  
- Experimental evaluation on UCI datasets shows feasibility and competitive performance but is limited in scope and dataset difficulty.  
- Comparisons are mainly against SVMs and ReLU networks; missing stronger baselines and other modern training/optimization methods.  
- No details provided on computational resources, training time, or memory usage to justify practical feasibility.  
- Related work discussion is insufficient; needs broader contrast with recent/global-optimality and landscape-analysis methods.  
- Motivation and intuition for the objective Φ and the added ε term are under-explained before presenting theoretical results.  
- Claim of “converges quickly” is empirically asserted but lacks deeper empirical or theoretical substantiation.  
- Presentation issues: later sections are less fluent, with typos and notation inconsistencies that should be fixed.  
- Strengths summarized: strong theoretical guarantee, novel approach, and a well-motivated introduction.  
- Recommendation: interesting theoretical contribution but practical relevance questionable; reviewer recommends rejection pending improvements in expressiveness analysis, experimental breadth, comparisons, and clarity.
