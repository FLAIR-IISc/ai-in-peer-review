- Paper proposes a theoretically interesting method for globally optimal training of feedforward networks with nonnegative weights and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method that provably converges linearly to the unique global minimum using fixed-point and Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Claims broader practicality than prior work by only requiring nonnegative data (common in images, word counts).  

Strengths:
- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous global optimality proof for a specific network class and novel application of fixed-point/Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Convergence guarantee: linear convergence to the global optimum without learning-rate tuning.  
- Clear exposition: well-organized paper with accessible theory and complete proofs in the supplement.  

Weaknesses:
- Strong constraints: requires nonnegative weights and α_i ≥ 1, significantly limiting model expressiveness versus standard networks.  
- Scalability concerns: spectral-condition involves a matrix whose size grows with hidden units and must be verified per parameter choice, limiting to low-dimensional experiments.  
- Hyperparameter burden: many hyperparameters (n1, α, pw, pu, ρw, ρu) needing extensive tuning (~150k combinations) with no clear selection guidance.  
- Limited empirical gains: outperforms linear SVM on only 2/7 datasets and is worse than unconstrained ReLU nets on some tasks.  

Evaluation:
- Quality: technically sound and well-proven but restricted to a narrow network class.  
- Clarity: clearly written and reproducible.  
- Originality: novel theoretical approach.  
- Significance: high theoretical value but questionable practical impact.  

Recommendation:
- Worthy theoretical contribution but practical utility is limited; paper needs more discussion or extensions toward general architectures to be compelling for acceptance.
