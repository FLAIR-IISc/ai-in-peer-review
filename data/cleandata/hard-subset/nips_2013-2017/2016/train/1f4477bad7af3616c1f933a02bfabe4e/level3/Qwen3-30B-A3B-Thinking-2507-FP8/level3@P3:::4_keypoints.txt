- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training a class of feedforward nets with global optimality guarantees and linear convergence.
- Targets networks with nonnegative weights and generalized polynomial activations (real powers α_i ≥ 1).
- Key theoretical insight: global optimality reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix, checkable before running the algorithm.
- Method is inspired by Perron-Frobenius theory and multihomogeneous fixed-point analysis.
- Theorem 1 gives clear conditions for global optimality and linear convergence, with rigorous proofs using nonlinear Perron-Frobenius tools.
- Demonstrated on one- and two-hidden-layer networks and extendable to deeper architectures in principle.
- Practical applicability is severely limited by the nonnegativity constraint on weights and non-standard activation functions.
- Empirical results (UCI datasets) generally underperform compared to SVM and do not convincingly show practical advantage.
- Claimed "linear convergence" is overstated because the rate depends on the spectral radius ρ(A), which may be close to 1 in practice.
- The assertion that convergence typically occurs in fewer than 10 iterations is unsubstantiated and should be removed.
- Paper is well-written and organized; proofs and supplementary material are clear and thorough.
- Conceptually original by connecting neural network training to nonlinear spectral/Perron-Frobenius theory, distinct from tensor or matrix-factorization approaches.
- Significance is limited: interesting theoretical advance but modest practical impact given strong model constraints and poor empirical performance.
- Strengths: first practically feasible globally optimal algorithm for this model class, no hyperparameter tuning, clear theory, real-data demonstrations, novel theoretical connection.
- Weaknesses: strong nonnegativity requirement, specialized activations, worse empirical performance, exaggerated convergence claims, conservative spectral-radius bounds limit dimensionality, and lack of parameter-selection guidance.
- Recommendation: reject due to limited practical applicability and insufficient empirical advantages despite solid theoretical contributions.
