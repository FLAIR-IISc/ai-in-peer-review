- Novel theoretical contribution: introduces a nonlinear spectral method with guaranteed convergence to the global optimum at a linear rate for 1–2 hidden layer feedforward nets.  
- The approach is inspired by Perron–Frobenius theory for non-negative matrices and differs from tensor-based prior work.  
- Practical advantage: the algorithm is parameter-free (no learning-rate tuning) compared to SGD.  
- Empirical applicability: authors provide experiments on real-world datasets, going beyond purely theoretical claims.  
- Restrictive assumptions: requires non-negative weights, a non-standard generalized polynomial activation, and a heavily modified objective, limiting practical relevance.  
- Unconvincing empirical evaluation: experiments use small UCI datasets, offer inconsistent performance vs. baselines, and lack sufficient baseline-tuning details.  
- Scalability concerns: spectral-radius bounds grow with hidden units and no efficient procedure is provided to find parameters satisfying the key condition ρ(A) < 1.  
- Suggested improvements: evaluate whether standard SGD can reach the global optimum on the constrained objective and run stronger comparisons with rigorously tuned baselines on larger, higher-dimensional datasets.
