- Proposes a method for global analysis of EM for parameter estimation in mixtures of two Gaussians, focusing on Population and Sample-based EM dynamics.  
- Provides characterization of stationary points and convergence behavior of Population EM iterates for two proposed models (Model 1 and Model 2).  
- Offers consistency results for Sample-based EM iterates in the large-sample limit.  
- Analyzes relationship between Population EM dynamics and the expected log-likelihood, characterizing its stationary points.  
- Paper is well-structured and explanations of models and algorithms are generally clear.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis including fixed-point characterization and conditions determining which fixed point is reached.  
- Lacks convincing discussion of practical usefulness and real-world applicability of the restricted models.  
- Needs clearer discussion of model expressiveness, limitations, and comparison with concurrent approaches.  
- Experimental section is unclear about model applicability limits, baseline comparisons, and computational resource demands.  
- Presentation issues: typos, unclear definitions/notation (notably Section 2 and the loss function), and some theorems (e.g., Theorem 1) need informal explanation.  
- Rebuttal clarified some points but those clarifications should be incorporated into the paper.  
- Overall strong theoretical contribution but requires more discussion of practical implications, improved presentation, and stronger experimental validation.
