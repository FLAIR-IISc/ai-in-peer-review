- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method to train 1–2 hidden layer feedforward nets with provable global optimality and linear convergence.
- Requires restrictive assumptions: non-negative weights, heavy regularization, and a generalized polynomial activation that is uncommon in practice.
- Claim that the targeted network class retains sufficient expressive power is not convincingly supported by the experiments.
- Experimental results on UCI datasets are promising but insufficient to demonstrate scalability or real-world applicability.
- Empirical comparisons are unfair: linear SVM baseline appears untuned and no nonlinear SVM baselines (RBF/polynomial) are included.
- Datasets used are too small and low-dimensional to validate scalability or applicability to large problems.
- Bounds on the spectral radius of matrix A grow with the number of hidden units, raising scalability concerns for high-dimensional settings.
- It is unclear whether observed global optimality stems from the method or the special objective; authors should test SGD’s empirical behavior on these tasks.
- Technically sound and well-organized presentation, but limited assumptions and experimental setup weaken the paper’s overall quality.
- Well-written with clear explanations, though some technical details and notation may be challenging for non-experts.
- Novel contribution in training neural networks, but restrictive assumptions reduce originality and impact.
- Some significance due to the new approach and promising results, yet limitations curb its overall impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clear presentation, and promising experimental outcomes.
- Arguments for rejection: restrictive/counterintuitive assumptions, unfair/limited experiments, unproven scalability, and spectral-radius scalability issues.
