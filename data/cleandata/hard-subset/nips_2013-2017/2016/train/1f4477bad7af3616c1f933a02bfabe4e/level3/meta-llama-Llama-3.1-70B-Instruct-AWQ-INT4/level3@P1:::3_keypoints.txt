- Proposes a novel class of neural networks (positive inputs, nonnegative linear weights, generalized polynomial activations) trained with a global optimality guarantee.  
- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method yielding linear convergence to global optimum.  
- Method is grounded in (sub)-homogeneous nonlinear eigenproblems on convex cones and inspired by Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Provides explicit, checkable preconditions for global optimality based on the spectral radius of a constructed nonnegative matrix.  
- Extends the framework to deep networks and characterizes the Lipschitz constant of the mapping GΦ to derive contraction conditions.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on a 2D synthetic dataset and small UCI datasets.  
- Claims this is the first potentially practical neural network with explicit optimality and convergence guarantees under simple preconditions.  
- Main limitation: requires nonnegative inputs and nonnegative linear-layer weights, which may reduce model expressiveness (though input shifting is possible).  
- Unnatural design choice: different activation function per hidden layer, limiting applicability.  
- Spectral-radius < 1 constraint forces adjusting p-values and pw/pu, shrinking linear weights and risking near-linear behavior in deep networks.  
- Scalability concern: satisfying the spectral-radius constraint may require further weight reduction as dimensionality/depth grows, possibly collapsing deep models.  
- Paper is well-written, offers thorough theoretical analysis, and is recommended for acceptance despite concerns.
