- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method to train a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks, claiming global optimality and linear convergence.  
- Provides a key theorem and detailed analysis guaranteeing convergence, with applications to one- and two-hidden-layer networks.  
- Strength: technically rigorous and novel method with theoretical convergence rate analysis.  
- Shows applicability with experiments on real-world problems but experimental scope is limited.  
- Major weakness: does not convincingly demonstrate the practical usefulness or expressiveness of the restricted network class.  
- Insufficient discussion and contrast with concurrent or related approaches and literature.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited (only some SVMs), omit other neural-network training methods and resource/compute details.  
- Presentation issues: later sections (notably Section 2) lack fluency and there are typos/notation problems.  
- Recommendation: conditional accept pending revisions addressing expressiveness, related work, experiments, and presentation.  
- Review score: 6/10, reviewer confidence 8/10.
