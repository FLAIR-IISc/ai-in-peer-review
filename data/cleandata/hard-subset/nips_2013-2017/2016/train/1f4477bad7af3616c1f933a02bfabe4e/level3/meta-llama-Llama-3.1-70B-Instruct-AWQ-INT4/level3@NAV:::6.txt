**Review of "Globally Optimal Training of Generalized Polynomial Neural Networks with Nonlinear Spectral Methods"**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a method to train a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks, namely generalized polynomial neural networks, using a nonlinear spectral method. The authors prove that this method achieves global optimality under certain conditions and demonstrate its applicability on some real-world problems. The paper presents a novel combination of techniques from nonlinear eigenvalue theory and fixed point theory to guarantee the convergence of the algorithm to the global optimum.

**Strengths**

* The paper addresses a fundamental problem in deep learning, namely the non-convexity of the optimization problem behind neural networks.
* The authors provide a rigorous theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including a proof of global optimality and a characterization of the convergence rate.
* The paper presents a novel application of nonlinear spectral methods to neural network training, which is a significant contribution to the field.

**Weaknesses**

* The paper does not convincingly show that the restricted network class is useful in practice. The authors should provide more discussion on the expressiveness of the proposed network class and its limitations.
* The paper lacks a thorough comparison with concurrent or related approaches. The authors should discuss how their method differs from other optimization methods for neural networks and provide a more comprehensive analysis of the state-of-the-art.
* The experiments are limited, and the authors should consider including more datasets and comparing their method with other neural network training methods.
* The presentation of the paper could be improved, particularly in Section 2, which lacks fluency.
* There are several typos and notation issues that should be fixed.

**Originality**

* The paper presents a novel combination of techniques from nonlinear eigenvalue theory and fixed point theory to guarantee the convergence of the algorithm to the global optimum.
* The application of nonlinear spectral methods to neural network training is a significant contribution to the field.

**Significance**

* The paper addresses a fundamental problem in deep learning, namely the non-convexity of the optimization problem behind neural networks.
* The proposed method has the potential to improve the training of neural networks, particularly in cases where the optimization problem is highly non-convex.

**Quality**

* The paper provides a rigorous theoretical analysis of the proposed method, including a proof of global optimality and a characterization of the convergence rate.
* The authors are careful to evaluate both the strengths and weaknesses of their method.

**Clarity**

* The paper is well-organized, but the presentation could be improved, particularly in Section 2, which lacks fluency.
* The authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce the results, but the paper could benefit from more intuitive explanations of the proposed method.

**Recommendation**

* The paper is a significant contribution to the field of deep learning, and I recommend accepting it for publication.
* However, I suggest that the authors address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly the lack of discussion on the expressiveness of the proposed network class and its limitations, and the limited experiments.

**Arguments for acceptance**

* The paper presents a novel combination of techniques from nonlinear eigenvalue theory and fixed point theory to guarantee the convergence of the algorithm to the global optimum.
* The application of nonlinear spectral methods to neural network training is a significant contribution to the field.
* The paper addresses a fundamental problem in deep learning, namely the non-convexity of the optimization problem behind neural networks.

**Arguments against acceptance**
