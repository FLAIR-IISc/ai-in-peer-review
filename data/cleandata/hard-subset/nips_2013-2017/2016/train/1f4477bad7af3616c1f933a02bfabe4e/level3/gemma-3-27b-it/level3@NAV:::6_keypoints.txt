- Proposes a novel training method for generalized polynomial NNs with non-negative weights and proves global convergence with linear rate via a nonlinear spectral method.  
- Theoretical contribution is valuable for tackling non-convexity in neural network training.  
- Practical relevance is unconvincing because the restricted network class raises expressiveness concerns.  
- Paper needs a clear discussion of which functions can/cannot be modeled and the trade-offs from non-negativity and polynomial activations.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: comparisons only to SVMs and ReLU networks, missing other globally optimal or efficiently trainable NN methods and alternative optimizers.  
- Computational resource and benchmark details are omitted, preventing fair comparison.  
- Results do not clarify the method’s applicability limits or the dataset conditions where it succeeds or fails.  
- Presentation problems: Section 2 lacks fluency and there are numerous typos and notation errors that hurt readability.  
- Technical clarifications required for the motivation of Φ and the ε term.  
- Claim of "quick convergence" should be tied to empirical evidence, not just theory.  
- An informal explanation before Theorem 1 would improve accessibility.  
- For acceptance: novel theoretical result, addresses an important problem, and offers initial empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: limited expressiveness, insufficient experiments, presentation/notation issues, and inadequate contextualization of related work.  
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision focused on expressiveness discussion, expanded experiments, and careful proofreading/clarification.
