- Presents a novel training method for generalized polynomial neural networks with non-negative weights that guarantees global optimality and linear convergence.
- Leverages nonlinear spectral methods and builds on structured matrix factorization and tensor decomposition literature.
- Claims practical feasibility compared to prior globally optimal approaches that are computationally heavy or require estimating data-generating measures.
- Strong theoretical results with clear convergence guarantees under relatively weak assumptions (non-negativity of data).
- Linear convergence rate is a notable theoretical advantage.
- Includes experimental results on small UCI datasets and comparisons to SVM and ReLU networks.
- Mathematical derivations are dense and occasionally unclear, hindering readability.
- Transition from first-order derivative inequalities (Theorem 3) to second derivatives (Theorem 4) lacks clear motivation and explanation.
- Treatment of the input-layer weight vector u is opaque; gradient definition and consistency are unclear.
- Several constructions (e.g., bounding u within a ball) lack sufficient justification, affecting reproducibility.
- The small epsilon term in the objective appears to be a technical artifact without practical motivation.
- Requiring non-negative data and strictly positive weights is a significant practical limitation, reducing applicability.
- Non-negativity constraint may cause instability or unbounded growth in network outputs.
- Maximizing the L1 norm of connection parameters is unusual versus common L2 regularization; rationale is insufficiently explained.
- Experimental validation is limited in scope and does not convincingly demonstrate practical advantage over standard architectures and SGD.
- Performance on UCI datasets is competitive but not clearly superior to existing methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty in addressing global optimization, mathematical rigor, potential impactful applications, and detailed mathematical framework.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practicality due to constraints, insufficient experimental validation, clarity issues in derivations, and questionable objective formulation.
- Overall recommendation: interesting and theoretically sound but recommend rejection pending stronger practical justification, clearer exposition, and more comprehensive experiments.
