- Summary: Recasts training of nonnegative-weight, generalized-polynomial-activation feedforward nets as a nonlinear Perron–Frobenius fixed-point problem with a checkable spectral-radius condition guaranteeing a unique global optimizer and linear convergence.

- Relation to prior work: Connects tensor/structured-factorization global-optimum NN training (Janzamin et al., Haeffele & Vidal) with nonlinear spectral methods and cone contractions.

- Theoretical contribution: Provides a computable condition ρ(A)<1 for existence/uniqueness of the global optimum and an iterative map with linear convergence.

- Extension: Framework and proofs extended to networks with two hidden layers, suggesting possible further generalization.

- Strength: Clear theoretical target and constructive algorithm that does not require learning-rate tuning.

- Strength: Elegant use of multi-homogeneous Perron–Frobenius theory and Thompson metric to obtain contraction-based results.

- Empirics: Initial experiments produce nontrivial classifiers and competitive accuracy on several UCI datasets with fair baselines including SVM and ReLU.

- Derivation gap: Theorem 3 uses first-order contraction bounds (Lemma 4) while Theorem 4 seems to rely on second-order control of GΦ—need clarification on where second derivatives are used and why first-order is insufficient.

- Notation/consistency issue: Clarify treatment of the last layer and precisely define ∇uΦ (is it gradient w.r.t. vectorized u with ψ applied elementwise?) to ensure consistency across equations and lemmas.

- Objective-design concern: Maximizing the 1-norm of parameters scaled by ε encourages large weights (opposite of weight decay); need statistical motivation or bounds without this term and an ablation with ε=0 or negative penalty.

- Role of ε: Question whether ε is merely a proof artifact to ensure strict positivity of gradients (V++), and whether interior initialization or barrier terms could avoid biasing the solution.

- Practicality/assumptions: Nonnegative data and strictly positive, nonzero weights are restrictive; justify Lp-sphere constraints versus standard regularization and address preprocessing for signed data, sparsity, and stability.

- Experimental limitations: Small-scale experiments and occasional underperformance versus SVM; need larger-scale comparisons, parameter-matched baselines, runtime-to-accuracy, sensitivity analyses for α, p, and ρ(A), and ablations of the sum-of-outputs term.

- Originality/significance: Conceptually novel synthesis of nonlinear spectral methods with NN training, but significance limited by restrictive modeling assumptions and modest empirical gains.

- Pros for acceptance: Theoretically clean global optimality with linear convergence, a checkable condition, and bridging PF theory with NN optimization.

- Cons for acceptance: Restrictive assumptions (nonnegativity, strict positivity, ε term), objective conflicts with standard regularization, unclear derivations/notation, and limited empirical validation.

- Overall recommendation: Promising theory that requires clarifications in derivations/notation and stronger empirical validation to establish practical relevance.
