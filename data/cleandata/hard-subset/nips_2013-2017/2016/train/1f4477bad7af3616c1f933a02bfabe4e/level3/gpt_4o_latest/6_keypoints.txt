- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training certain feedforward networks with global optimality under specific assumptions.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees, including linear convergence rates and a fixed-point theory framework.  
- Demonstrates applicability to one- and two-hidden-layer networks with experimental proof-of-concept on UCI datasets.  
- Introduction is clear and well-motivated, situating the work in the optimization literature.  
- Method is novel and claimed to be the first practically feasible algorithm achieving global optimality for non-trivial neural networks.  
- Practical utility is limited by strong constraints (nonnegative weights, specific activations) that reduce expressiveness.  
- Inadequate comparison to modern optimization baselines (e.g., Adam, second-order methods) beyond SGD and SVMs.  
- Experiments are limited to small, low-dimensional datasets and do not demonstrate scalability to complex, high-dimensional problems.  
- Comparisons to SVMs and ReLU networks are insufficiently detailed.  
- Computational resource requirements and runtime/efficiency metrics are not reported.  
- Section 2 and Theorem 1 lack clarity and informal explanation; loss function, Phi function, and epsilon term are poorly motivated.  
- Typos and unclear notation (e.g., "polyomial," "R_{++}") detract from readability.  
- Experiments do not probe model limitations, such as behavior when nonnegativity assumptions are violated.  
- Recommended improvements: discuss expressiveness/limitations, broaden baseline comparisons, run larger-scale experiments, report resource usage, and improve exposition of key theorems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical rigor, novelty, and first global optimality result for a non-trivial neural network model.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited practical utility, scalability concerns, insufficient experimental validation, and clarity/presentation issues.  
- Overall: promising theoretical contribution that requires substantial improvements in practical validation and clarity for top-tier acceptance.
