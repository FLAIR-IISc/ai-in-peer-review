- Presents a novel nonlinear spectral method guaranteeing global optimality with linear convergence for a class of feedforward networks.  
- Strong theoretical contribution using Perron-Frobenius theory and rigorous analysis culminating in Theorem 1.  
- Clearly defines the network class and conditions for achieving global optimality.  
- Provides initial experimental validation on UCI datasets showing feasibility, though limited.  

- Some mathematical derivations lack clarity, especially the jump from Theorem 3 (first-order) to Theorem 4 (second-order).  
- Treatment of the output-layer gradient w.r.t. u is insufficiently justified and may oversimplify complexities.  
- Several constructions (e.g., bounding u in a ball) appear ad-hoc and lack motivation.  
- Objective maximizes the 1-norm of weights (Eq. 2), an unusual choice compared to L2 regularization, with unclear effects on generalization.  
- The epsilon term seems like a proof artifact to ensure positive gradients; its practical role and sensitivity are unclear.  
- Non-negativity requirements for data and strictly positive weights severely limit applicability and raise stability/unboundedness concerns.  
- Paper does not convincingly show practical advantages over standard NNs (e.g., ReLU) despite theoretical guarantees.  

- Request clarification on why the derivative order shifts between Theorems 3 and 4.  
- Ask for clarification of the gradient treatment for the final layer variable u.  
- Request motivation and implications for maximizing the 1-norm of weights.  
- Ask how sensitive the method is to the choice of epsilon in Eq. (2).  
- Ask for strategies to mitigate limitations from non-negativity constraints.  

- Overall: theoretically interesting and sound, but practical limitations and clarity issues undermine impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, rigorous mathematics, and initial feasibility evidence.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive non-negativity constraints, weak experimental support for practical benefit, and unclear derivations.  

- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision addressing 1-norm rationale, non-negativity implications, clearer derivations, and stronger experiments.
