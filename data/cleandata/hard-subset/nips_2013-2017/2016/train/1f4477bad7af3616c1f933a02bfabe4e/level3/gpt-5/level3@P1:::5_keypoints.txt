- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method to train generalized polynomial NNs with nonnegative inputs/weights and activations (uᵗx)^α, yielding global optimality with linear convergence.  
- Uses a modified cross-entropy objective (adds sum over outputs and small positive sum of parameters) and constrains layers to products of p-norm spheres.  
- Main technical contribution: recasts training as finding a fixed point of a multi-block, cone-preserving, multi-homogeneous map GΦ and proves it is a contraction in the Thompson metric under an explicit checkable condition ρ(A) < 1.  
- Shows the map converges linearly to a unique fixed point which is the unique global optimizer; provides extension from one to two hidden layers with explicit A matrices.  
- Connects to prior work on tensor-based shallow-net guarantees, factorization-regularizer global optimality, and Perron–Frobenius theory for multi-homogeneous maps.  
- Provides proof-of-concept experiments on several UCI datasets, comparing to RBF-SVM and ReLU networks trained by SGD.  
- Theory: fixed-point and contraction argument is clean and (to reviewer) novel in NN training; ρ(A)<1 is explicit and characterizes architecture/norm dependence.  
- Results are strong: linear convergence and uniqueness of maximizer are significant contributions.  
- Major assumptions limit applicability: require nonnegative inputs, strictly positive weights, norm constraints, distinct α_l, and the modified objective—need discussion of necessity vs proof convenience.  
- Request clarification: Theorem 3 uses first-order Jacobian bounds while Theorem 4 appears to use second-derivative information—clarify where the Hessian enters and required smoothness (C1 vs C2).  
- Request clarification: last layer is treated as a matrix block—make explicit that gradients w.r.t. u are vectorized then reshaped, and ensure consistency of Thompson metric block weights γ across Lemmas/Theorems.  
- Request clarification: proofs/algorithm alternate between ball B++ and sphere S++; include a lemma summarizing inclusions and why contraction on B++ implies convergence to maximizer on S++.  
- Question the added objective terms (sum of outputs and parameter sum): justify their practical effect, show ablations removing each term, and discuss bias toward dense positive weights.  
- Ask about ε>0: it is introduced to force entrywise-positive gradients—state if it is needed numerically, the ε value used, and behavior when ε = 0.  
- Concern about strict positivity: handling zeros in x or weights may be numerically fragile—describe implementation handling (flooring) and whether signed features can be accommodated (e.g., split into pos/neg) without breaking guarantees.  
- Presentation improvements suggested: add a high-level derivation sketch of A for one-layer case, unify p and p′ notation and norms, and include algorithm boxes with explicit vectorization conventions.  
- Originality: the use of nonlinear Perron–Frobenius/multi-homogeneous contraction for global optimality in NN training appears novel and offers a practically checkable condition.  
- Practical significance limited by modeling constraints and small empirical scope; recommend evaluating at least one larger nonnegative dataset (e.g., MNIST) and reporting ρ(A) and observed convergence factors.  
- Ask for wall-clock comparisons to reach a given training loss vs tuned SGD/Adam and sensitivity analyses to architecture/hyperparameters and α (including repeated α leading to trivial solutions).  
- Pros for acceptance: rigorous global optimality with linear convergence, checkable precondition, novel theoretical approach, and promising initial empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: strong, possibly proof-driven modeling constraints; limited empirical validation and mixed accuracy; some technical/notation clarifications needed; unclear practical applicability to standard NN workloads.  
- Specific questions for authors: formalize role of second derivatives in Theorem 4; prove gradient w.r.t. u is consistently defined via vectorization; report ε used and effect of ε=0; report measured ρ(A), chosen γ, and observed contraction rates across datasets; explain preprocessing for signed data and whether guarantees survive transforms.  
- Recommendation: leaning marginally positive, contingent on clarifying derivative-order and block-gradient treatment, providing ablations for added objective terms, and adding at least one larger empirical demonstration.
