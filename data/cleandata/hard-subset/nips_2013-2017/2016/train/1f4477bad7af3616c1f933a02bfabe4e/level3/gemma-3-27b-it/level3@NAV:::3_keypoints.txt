- Presents a method for globally optimal training of feedforward networks with non-negative inputs, non-negative weights, and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method that achieves linear convergence to a global optimum under specified conditions.  
- Novelty: combines a restricted architecture with a spectral method to give explicit, checkable optimality guarantees (spectral radius condition).  
- Practical advantage over prior global methods by avoiding complex tensor decompositions and being easier to apply.  
- Extends results from one to two hidden layers, increasing applicability within the constrained setting.  
- Empirical results on small datasets show competitive performance and validate the approach.  
- Main limitations: strong architectural constraints (non-negativity of inputs/weights and layer-specific activations) reduce expressiveness and generality.  
- Scalability concern: the spectral radius constraint may force weight shrinkage as dimensionality grows, risking near-linear behavior and limiting scalability.  
- Reviewer requests empirical analysis of ρ(A) versus p1, p2, pw, pu, input dimension, and network size to assess scalability.  
- Suggests examining how activation choices affect convergence rate and potential need for tuning.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, practical, theoretically grounded, and advances provable optimal training methods.  
- Rejection concerns: restrictive architecture, potential scalability issues, and experiments limited to small datasets; recommends addressing scalability and constraints in the camera-ready.
