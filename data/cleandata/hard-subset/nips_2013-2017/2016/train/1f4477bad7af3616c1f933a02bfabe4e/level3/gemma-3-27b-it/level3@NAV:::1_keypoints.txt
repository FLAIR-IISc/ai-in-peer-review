- Presents a method to train generalized polynomial neural networks with non-negative weights to global optimality.
- Introduces a “nonlinear spectral method” that builds a contractive map whose fixed point is the unique minimizer.
- Proves linear convergence under certain conditions and connects the approach to Perron–Frobenius theory.
- Claims advantage over prior tensor-based methods by avoiding estimation of the data-generating measure, relying instead on a spectral radius calculation.
- Paper is well-structured with a clear theoretical framework, algorithm, and experimental setup.
- Major scalability concern: convergence proof depends on bounding the spectral radius of a matrix that grows with hidden units.
- High user burden due to a large number of hyperparameters that must be tuned.
- Spectral condition must be verified for every parameter choice, adding significant overhead.
- Empirical results are weak: after ~150,000 hyperparameter trials, the method outperforms a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets.
- Practical relevance is questionable given limited empirical gains despite guaranteed global optimality.
- Reviewer recommends rejection in current form, citing theoretical interest but insufficient practical utility.
- Suggests addressing scalability and demonstrating stronger empirical performance, e.g., by relaxing constraints or approximating the spectral radius.
