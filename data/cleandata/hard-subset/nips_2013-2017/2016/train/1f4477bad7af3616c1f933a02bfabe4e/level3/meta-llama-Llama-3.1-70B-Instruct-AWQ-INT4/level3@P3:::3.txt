This paper introduces a novel method for training generalized polynomial neural networks with a global optimality guarantee. The authors propose a nonlinear spectral method that achieves a linear convergence rate under certain conditions on the network parameters. The main contribution of this work is the first practically feasible algorithm that guarantees global optimality for a non-trivial neural network model.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the main ideas and a thorough discussion of the related work. The authors provide a detailed proof of their main result, which is a significant contribution to the field of neural network optimization.

The main strengths of this paper are its originality, technical soundness, and significance. The authors tackle a challenging problem in the field of neural network optimization and provide a novel solution that is backed by a rigorous proof. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

One of the main weaknesses of this paper is its limited applicability. The authors require nonnegative inputs and nonnegative linear-layer weights, which may limit the expressiveness of the model. Additionally, each hidden layer uses a different activation function, which may restrict the applicability of the method. However, the authors acknowledge these limitations and provide a clear discussion of the trade-offs involved.

The experimental results presented in the paper are promising, but limited to low-dimensional datasets. The authors compare their method to stochastic gradient descent and other baseline methods, demonstrating the superiority of their approach. However, the results are not comprehensive, and further experimentation is needed to fully understand the strengths and weaknesses of the method.

Overall, I believe that this paper is a significant contribution to the field of neural network optimization and deserves to be accepted. The authors have made a substantial effort to provide a rigorous proof of their main result, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow. While the method has some limitations, the authors provide a clear discussion of the trade-offs involved, and the experimental results are promising.
