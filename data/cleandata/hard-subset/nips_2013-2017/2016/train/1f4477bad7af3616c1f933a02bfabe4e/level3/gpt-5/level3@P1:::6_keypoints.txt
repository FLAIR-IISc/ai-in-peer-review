- Contribution: Introduces NLSM that trains a restricted class of feedforward networks to global optimality with linear convergence.  
- Model assumptions: Nonnegative inputs/weights, generalized polynomial activations with unit-specific αl ≥ 1, per-layer norm constraints, and a modified objective (negative cross-entropy + sum of outputs + small ε).  
- Main tool: Casts training as a fixed-point iteration GΦ and uses multihomogeneous nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory.  
- Key condition: Contraction and uniqueness follow under a computable spectral condition ρ(A) < 1 for a small (K+1)-by-(K+1) nonnegative matrix A.  
- Convergence guarantee: Uniqueness of global maximizer on S++ and linear convergence in the Thompson metric.  
- Technical scope: One-hidden-layer case presented in detail; two-hidden-layer extension sketched with proofs partly deferred to supplement.  
- Experiments: Small UCI datasets show competitive accuracy with ReLU and RBF-SVM on some tasks and much faster training than SGD for the proposed objective.  
- Relation to prior work: Differs from tensor-based and matrix-factorization global-training approaches and connects to polynomial networks and multihomogeneous PF theory.  
- Claim assessment: Plausible claim of being the first "practically feasible" globally optimal algorithm for a nontrivial NN class, but needs more systematic contrast to related constrained/monotone methods.  
- Theoretical quality: Rigorous, clear fixed-point characterization, contraction analysis, and spectral-radius condition; nontrivial two-layer extension.  
- Practical relevance concern: Expressiveness of constrained architecture and conservativeness of ρ(A) < 1 are unclear for realistic settings.  
- Modified objective issue: Need clarification on how the output-sum and ε terms affect classifier decisions, calibration, and whether decision boundaries change if removed.  
- Clarity problems: Later sections grow technical quickly; suggest an informal roadmap before Theorem 1 and earlier, example-driven explanation of Φ and ε.  
- Presentation issues: Several typos and inconsistent notation (e.g., “polyomial”, undefined R++, S++, inconsistent p/pw/pu and ρ notations).  
- Originality: Novel application of nonlinear spectral methods to derive globally convergent training for generalized polynomial networks.  
- Significance: Theoretically significant for global optimization under structure but empirical evidence too narrow to establish practical impact.  
- Strengths (summary): Rigorous global optimality and linear convergence guarantees, elegant PF connection, explicit A matrix/condition, parameter-free iteration once architecture fixed.  
- Weaknesses (summary): Restricted model class, limited experiments and missing baselines, no detailed runtime comparisons, incomplete discussion of related methods, presentation/notation issues.  
- Requested clarifications: Motivate Φ and ε with examples, give informal overview of GΦ/A before Theorem 1, characterize expressiveness and monotonicity, and provide practical guidance on preprocessing and hyperparameters.  
- Experimental suggestions: Add polynomial-kernel SVM, constrained-NN baselines, runtime/iteration metrics, plot objective gap vs iterations versus predicted rate from ρ(A), test at least one moderate-scale nonnegative dataset, and include synthetic comparisons to [11].  
- Editorial fixes: Correct typos, define R++/S++ at first use, standardize p/ρ notations, and ensure consistent vector/matrix norm notation.  
- Acceptance arguments: Strong, novel theoretical contribution with verifiable convergence condition and potential to inspire further work.  
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient empirical validation, unclear practical impact due to constrained architecture, missing baselines and runtime analysis, and presentation needs polishing.
