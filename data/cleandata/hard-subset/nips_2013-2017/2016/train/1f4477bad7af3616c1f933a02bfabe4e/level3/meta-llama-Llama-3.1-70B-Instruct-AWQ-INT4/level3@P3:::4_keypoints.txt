- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training a class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence rate.  
- Reformulates neural network training as a nonlinear spectral problem on a convex cone, enabling new analysis.  
- Leverages nonlinear Perron-Frobenius theory to establish global optimality under weak data assumptions.  
- Provides a proof of convergence to the global optimum and characterizes the Lipschitz constant of the mapping.  
- Highlights the importance of nonnegativity constraints on weights and the activation function for global optimality.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and uses consistent notation with useful lemmas and theorems.  
- Originality is high: method is distinct from existing neural network training approaches.  
- Main limitation: requirement of nonnegativity constraints on weights may reduce practical applicability.  
- Secondary limitation: reliance on non-standard activation functions limits applicability to some network types.  
- Empirical results are limited: tested on UCI datasets only, lacking comparisons to state-of-the-art and detailed dataset analysis.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, thorough theoretical analysis, and insightful contributions on network properties.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weight nonnegativity and activation assumptions plus limited empirical evaluation.
