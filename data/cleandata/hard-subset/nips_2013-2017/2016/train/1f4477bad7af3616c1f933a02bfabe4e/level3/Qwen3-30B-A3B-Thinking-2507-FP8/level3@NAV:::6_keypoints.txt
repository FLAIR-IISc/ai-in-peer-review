- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training a restricted class of 1–2 hidden-layer feed-forward networks with global optimality and linear convergence under certain conditions.
- Requires nonnegative weights and a modified objective (with Φ and ε) to guarantee convergence; global convergence verified by checking the spectral radius of a small matrix.
- Claims practical feasibility and is positioned as the first globally optimal training method with convergence guarantees for such networks, avoiding learning-rate tuning.
- Theoretical contribution is strong: rigorous proofs, easy-to-verify conditions, and linear convergence rates.
- Introduction and motivation are well-written and the technical content is rigorous.
- Main weakness: no convincing evidence that the restricted network class is practically useful or expressive enough for real problems.
- Paper fails to discuss expressiveness limits or clearly compare the restricted class to standard neural network architectures.
- Experiments are severely limited: only compared to some SVMs and omit standard neural network baselines (e.g., SGD-trained ReLU networks).
- Experimental section lacks computational resource and runtime details, preventing assessment of efficiency.
- Presentation needs polishing: Section 2 lacks fluency, there are typos (e.g., "polyomial") and undefined notation (e.g., R_{++}).
- Should clarify why the modified objective (Φ and ε) is necessary and provide an informal explanation before Theorem 1; explicitly state empirical basis for "converges quickly."
- Related work section is insufficiently contrasted with concurrent approaches; authors should expand comparisons and address expressiveness and experimental shortcomings before recommending acceptance.
