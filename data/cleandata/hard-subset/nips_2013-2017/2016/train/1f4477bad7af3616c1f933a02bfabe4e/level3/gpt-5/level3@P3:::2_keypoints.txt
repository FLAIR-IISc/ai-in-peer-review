- Proposal: Introduces a nonlinear spectral method (NLSM) training 1–2 hidden-layer NNs to a unique global optimum with linear convergence using polynomial activations, nonnegativity and norm constraints, and a modified objective; global optimality iff ρ(A)<1.

- Key theoretical ingredients: unit-specific generalized polynomial activations, nonnegative weights/inputs, norm constraints plus output-sum/linear terms, and a contraction proof via multi-homogeneous Perron–Frobenius and Thompson metric.

- Practical check: Global-optimality reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small (K+1)×(K+1) nonnegative matrix A.

- Novelty: Fresh application of nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory to neural network training with an explicit fixed-point algorithm and nonasymptotic linear convergence guarantee.

- Theoretical care: Rigorous derivation from constrained objective to contraction mapping with explicit dependence of ρ(A) on architecture and norms.

- Clarity of core method: Mapping GΦ is clearly specified and the need for distinct αl is explained to avoid permutation invariance.

- Restrictive assumptions: Nonnegative weights/inputs and monotone polynomial activations limit expressivity and are counterintuitive for many real tasks.

- Objective mismatch: The modified objective (loss − outputs + small linear term) departs from standard losses; unclear whether global optimality stems from the objective rather than architecture/algorithm.

- Validity-set concerns: ρ(A)<1 may not hold for practical parameter ranges; paper relies on conservative bounds and search over hyperparameters to find feasible models.

- Suggestions for validity: Derive tighter bounds for ρ(A), give heuristics to set pu,pw,ρu,ρw from data, use Gershgorin/monotone rescaling, and continuation on norms to find feasible regimes.

- Input preprocessing suggestion: Improve expressivity/feasibility by splitting features into positive/negative channels or recentering inputs.

- Scalability concerns: Entries of A and ρ(A) grow with hidden units and input norms, threatening applicability to high-dimensional data.

- Experimental limitations: Experiments use small UCI datasets and do not probe scalability or modern workloads; expressivity claims are insufficiently supported.

- Required experiments/baselines: Test larger datasets (e.g., MNIST variants), include well-tuned nonlinear baselines (RBF/polynomial SVM, GBDT, kNN), report tuning grids, training time, and sample complexity.

- Ablation studies requested: Compare SGD on the modified objective, effect of removing sum-of-outputs term, varying α/norms/constraints, and input sign-splitting.

- Clarity improvements: Add intuition for why sum-of-outputs and positivity yield contraction, practical guidance/flowchart for choosing pu,pw,ρu,ρw, and simplify/move technical definitions to the appendix.

- Originality: Approach is original within NN optimization by combining multi-homogeneous PF theory with tailored architecture/objective for global optimality.

- Significance: Strong theoretical significance but limited practical impact so far due to restrictive assumptions, feasibility/scalability issues, and narrow empirical validation.

- Questions for authors: characterize expressive power under nonnegativity; empirical curves of ρ(A) vs. architecture/data scaling; whether SGD on modified objective finds same optimum; can nonnegativity be reparameterized (e.g., squared weights) without harming convergence.

- Pros: Clear, novel theory with a computable sufficient condition and linear convergence; implementable method; no learning-rate tuning.

- Cons: Restrictive modeling assumptions, scalability/feasibility concerns from growth of ρ(A), and narrow/incomplete empirical validation.

- Overall recommendation: Marginal reject (leaning reject) — elegant theory but insufficient empirical support and practical guidance for acceptance.
