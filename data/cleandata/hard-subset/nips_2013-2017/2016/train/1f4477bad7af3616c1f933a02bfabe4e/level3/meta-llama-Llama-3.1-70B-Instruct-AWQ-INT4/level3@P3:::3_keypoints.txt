- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for training generalized polynomial neural networks with a global optimality guarantee.
- Method achieves linear convergence rate under certain conditions on network parameters.
- Claims to be the first practically feasible algorithm guaranteeing global optimality for a non-trivial neural network model.
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a clear explanation of ideas and related work.
- Provides a detailed, rigorous proof of the main result, enhancing technical soundness.
- Main strengths: originality, technical rigor, and significance to neural network optimization.
- Main weaknesses: limited applicability due to required nonnegative inputs and nonnegative linear-layer weights.
- Additional limitation: each hidden layer uses a different activation, which may restrict broader applicability.
- Experimental results are promising but limited to low-dimensional datasets and not comprehensive.
- Recommendation: accept—paper is a significant contribution despite limitations.
- Suggestions: expand experiments to higher-dimensional datasets and more baselines, discuss applications/limitations in more detail, and consider relaxing parameter assumptions.
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 9/10.
