- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method to train generalized polynomial neural networks achieving global optimality.
- Proves global optimality and linear convergence rate under certain data and model assumptions.
- Algorithm constructs a contractive fixed-point map whose unique fixed point equals the global minimizer.
- Requires nonnegativity constraints on weights and minimizes a modified objective (loss minus total output sum).
- Introduces a special class of networks that admit unique stationary points and are trainable to global optimum.
- Convergence proof hinges on bounding the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.
- Spectral-radius-based bound limits scalability to larger hidden layers and may be loose.
- Experimental results on low-dimensional UCI datasets show competitive performance vs. linear SVM and ReLU nets.
- Method requires tuning many hyperparameters, making it unintuitive and cumbersome in practice.
- Spectral condition must be checked for every parameter choice, adding extra verification overhead.
- Paper is well-written, clearly notated, and provides thorough analysis and detailed derivations.
- The approach is original and potentially significant for broader applications in vision and NLP.
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication; work is a valuable contribution despite limitations.
