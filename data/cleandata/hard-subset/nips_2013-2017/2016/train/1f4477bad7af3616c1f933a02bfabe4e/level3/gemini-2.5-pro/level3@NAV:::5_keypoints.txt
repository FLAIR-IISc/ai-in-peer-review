- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training "generalized polynomial neural networks" with proof of linear convergence to a unique global optimizer under certain conditions.
- Major strength: significant theoretical contribution guaranteeing a unique global optimum and linear convergence for a non-trivial network class.
- Elegant mathematical development using nonlinear Perron-Frobenius theory and the Banach fixed-point theorem.
- Algorithm is parameter-free, avoiding learning-rate tuning.
- Major weakness: restrictive assumptions requiring non-negative data and strictly positive weights limit applicability.
- Objective in (2) is counter-intuitive because it maximizes a term related to the 1-norm of weights, contradicting typical weight decay regularization.
- The small epsilon term appears to be a proof artifact to enforce strictly positive gradients and lacks practical justification.
- Several constructions (e.g., bounding u in a ball) are insufficiently justified.
- Clarification requested: why does Theorem 3 require the objective to be C2 (twice differentiable) when Lemma 4 uses first-order inequalities?
- Clarification requested: whether the gradient with respect to u is consistently treated as the gradient of its vectorized form.
- Experimental results do not show a clear practical advantage over standard ReLU networks despite a logical comparison to SVM.
- Overall assessment: theoretically elegant but currently a specialized theoretical result rather than a broadly applicable practical method; addressing concerns would strengthen the paper.
