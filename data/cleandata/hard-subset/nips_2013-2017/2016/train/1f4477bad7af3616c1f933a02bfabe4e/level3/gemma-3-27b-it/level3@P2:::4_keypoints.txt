- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks with linear convergence under weak data assumptions.
- Imposes non-negativity constraints on weights and modifies the objective to obtain a tractable optimization problem with guaranteed global optimum.
- Contrasts with typical non-convex training (e.g., SGD) by providing convergence guarantees and avoiding local minima.
- Builds on recent globally optimal training work but avoids estimating data-generating measures and complex tensor decompositions.
- Global optimality condition is verifiable via the spectral radius of a small matrix, making it computationally efficient.
- Theoretical contributions (Theorem 1 and Theorem 5) are rigorous and clearly presented, leveraging Perron–Frobenius theory.
- Extends the method to two hidden layers, showing potential for generalization beyond single-layer networks.
- Major limitation: non-negativity constraints and restriction to polynomial activations reduce practical applicability and expressiveness.
- Empirical results are limited and unconvincing: method underperforms SVM and is only competitive with ReLU on low-dimensional UCI datasets.
- Scalability is unclear due to limited experimental evaluation on small datasets.
- Claim of typically converging in fewer than 10 iterations is overstated because the convergence constant may be large.
- Method is sensitive to hyperparameters (e.g., pu and pw) and the provided bounds may be conservative, making tuning difficult.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, mathematically rigorous approach with significant theoretical contributions and potential to stimulate further research.
- Arguments against acceptance: strong constraints limit practicality, weak empirical evidence, and potential inability to reach state-of-the-art on complex data.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and interesting but practical limitations and limited empirical validation lead to a weak accept recommendation; major revisions and stronger experiments are suggested.
