- Proposes training Generalized Polynomial Neural Networks (GPNNs) with nonlinear spectral methods, guaranteeing global optimality and linear convergence.  
- Method requires non-negative data and weights and modifies the objective (maximizing 1-norm with a small epsilon).  
- Theoretical development is rigorous, using fixed-point theory with dense but instructive derivations.  
- Main contribution: provable global optimality and linear convergence in a non-convex training problem.  
- Empirical validation is limited to UCI datasets and shows competitive results under the imposed constraints.  
- Justification for bounding hidden-layer activations 'u' in a ball is unclear.  
- The shift from first-order derivative conditions in Theorem 3 to second-derivative use in Theorem 4 is unexplained and arbitrary-feeling.  
- Treatment of the last layer gradient (vectorized 'u') needs clearer, consistent exposition.  
- Practical constraints (non-negative data/weights and 1-norm maximization) are restrictive and raise applicability concerns.  
- The small epsilon in the objective appears to be a technical artifact to enforce positive gradients without intuitive justification.  
- Potential for unbounded weight growth under strictly positive weights is a real concern.  
- Comparison baseline is limited (SVM only); lacks comparisons to standard neural architectures like ReLU networks.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify constructions, discuss practical limitations, and expand experimental evaluation.
