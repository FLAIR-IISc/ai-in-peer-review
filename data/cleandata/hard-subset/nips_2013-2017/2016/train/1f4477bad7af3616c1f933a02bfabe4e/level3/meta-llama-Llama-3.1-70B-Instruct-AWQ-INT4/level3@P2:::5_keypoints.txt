- Presents a novel class of feedforward neural networks trainable to a provable unique global optimizer using a nonlinear spectral method with linear convergence.
- Main contribution: framework enabling global optimization of a specific NN class, diverging from SGD-based approaches.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides instructive mathematical derivations and thorough related-work discussion.
- Experiments are well-designed and include a sensible comparison to SVM as a global-optimizer baseline.
- Some arguments and constructions lack clear justification (e.g., bounding u in a ball and the small epsilon in (2)).
- The shift from first-order derivative inequalities (Theorem 3) to second-derivative arguments (Theorem 4) is not well-explained.
- Treatment of the last layer is inconsistent and unclear—uncertainty why ∇_u equals the gradient of vectorized u.
- Maximization of the 1-norm of connection parameters in (2) is not well-motivated versus typical weight-decay norms used in practice.
- The small epsilon term in (2) appears to be a proof artifact without practical justification or discussion of its effect.
- Requiring non-negative input data and strictly positive (nonzero) weights may be impractical and can encourage unbounded growth; limitations need discussion.
- Authors should provide more detailed explanations of key derivations and modeling choices to improve clarity.
- Paper should include comparisons to common neural networks (e.g., ReLU-based models) to demonstrate practical advantages.
- Technically sound with supported claims, but acceptability hinges on revisions addressing the above theoretical and practical concerns.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions.
