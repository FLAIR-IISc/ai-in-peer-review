**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes a novel method for training a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks, which the authors term "Generalized Polynomial Neural Networks." The method, called Nonlinear Spectral Method (NLSM), is proven to achieve globally optimal convergence under certain weak assumptions on the data. The authors demonstrate the applicability of their method on several real-world datasets and compare its performance with other machine learning algorithms.

**Strengths**

1. **Theoretical advance**: The paper presents a significant theoretical contribution by providing a method for globally optimal training of a class of neural networks. This is a notable achievement, as the optimization problem behind neural networks is highly non-convex, and most existing methods rely on stochastic gradient descent and its variants, which provide no guarantee of achieving the global optimum.
2. **Applicability**: The authors demonstrate the applicability of their method on several real-world datasets, achieving competitive performance with other machine learning algorithms.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited expressiveness**: The paper does not convincingly show that the restricted network class is useful in practice. The authors should provide a more explicit discussion on the expressiveness of their model, including what is possible and what is out of reach.
2. **Limited comparisons**: The experiments are limited to comparisons with some SVMs and omit other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms. The authors should provide a more comprehensive comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.
3. **Lack of computational resources**: The experimental results do not provide information on the computational resources required for the three algorithms. This information is essential for understanding the practicality of the proposed method.
4. **Presentation**: The introduction is well-written, but later sections, especially Section 2, lack fluency. The authors should polish the presentation to improve readability.
5. **Typos and notation issues**: There are several typos and notation issues that should be fixed, such as "polyomial" instead of "polynomial," undefined notation like R_{++}, and unexplained notation like p1 and p2.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel method for training a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning. The authors' approach is original and distinct from other existing methods.

**Significance**

The paper's contribution is significant, as it provides a method for globally optimal training of a class of neural networks. This can have a substantial impact on the field of machine learning, as most existing methods rely on stochastic gradient descent and its variants, which provide no guarantee of achieving the global optimum.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its theoretical foundations. However, the presentation can be improved, especially in later sections.

**Quality**

The paper is of high quality, with a significant theoretical contribution and a clear explanation of the method and its applications. However, the authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above to improve the paper's overall quality.

**Recommendation**

Based on the review, I recommend accepting the paper for publication, but with the condition that the authors address the weaknesses mentioned above. Specifically, the authors should:

1. Provide a more explicit discussion on the expressiveness of their model.
2. Conduct more comprehensive comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
3. Provide information on the computational resources required for the three algorithms.
4. Polish the presentation to improve readability.
5. Fix typos and notation issues.

By addressing these weaknesses, the authors can improve the paper's overall quality and make it more suitable for publication.
