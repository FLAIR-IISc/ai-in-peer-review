- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, claiming global optimality with linear convergence.
- Reformulates network training as a nonlinear eigenproblem solvable by a spectral method, offering guarantees unlike SGD.
- Method is applicable to one- and two-hidden-layer networks under certain weak data assumptions and claimed competitive performance on real datasets.
- Requires non-negativity constraints on network weights, which may hinder practical performance.
- Uses non-standard, very specific activation functions, limiting broad applicability.
- Empirical results indicate the method performs worse than SVM in reported experiments, questioning its competitiveness.
- The claim that linear convergence “typically” means convergence in fewer than 10 iterations is exaggerated and problem-dependent.
- Conceptually interesting and provides a new perspective on neural network training.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clear, though some technical details may be hard for non-experts.
- Reviewer scores: Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10, Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Overall 8/10.
