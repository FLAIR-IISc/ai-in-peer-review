- Proposes a novel training approach for feedforward neural networks with a provable unique global optimizer.  
- Introduces a class of generalized polynomial neural networks and a nonlinear spectral method to achieve global optimality.  
- Method uses a specially designed objective and a mapping that converges linearly to the global optimum.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear high-level explanations.  
- Mathematical derivations are instructive but some parts are unclear and require careful reading.  
- Paper is technically sound with a thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Experimental results on several UCI datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Concern: Theorem 3 uses first-order derivative inequalities while Theorem 4 uses second derivatives; the reason for this shift is unclear.  
- Concern: Last layer is treated separately; unclear whether the gradient w.r.t. u is simply the gradient of vectorized u and whether this is consistent.  
- Concern: Constructions and treatments of the parameter u are not clearly justified and need more explanation.  
- Concern: The objective maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters (eq. (2)) rather than using standard weight decay; rationale and practical impact are unclear.  
- Concern: The small epsilon in eq. (2) appears to be a proof artifact; its practical significance is unclear.  
- Overall originality and potential significance are strong; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Recommendation caveat: Authors should address the clarity and methodological concerns raised before final publication.
