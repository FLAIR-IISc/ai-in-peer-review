- Proposes feedforward NNs with nonnegative weights and generalized polynomial activations where training has a unique global optimizer via a fixed-point formulation.  
- Recasts training as finding a fixed point of a multi-homogeneous, order-preserving map GΦ and proves contraction in the Thompson metric when a small nonnegative matrix A has spectral radius < 1.  
- Shows fixed-point iteration converges linearly to the unique global optimum under the spectral-radius condition.  
- Provides results for one and two hidden layers and proof-of-concept experiments on UCI datasets.  
- Builds on nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory, contraction mappings in cones, and relates to tensor-method and structured factorization global optimality results.  
- Method is practically implementable, requires only nonnegativity and a checkable spectral-radius condition, and yields linear convergence under mild architecture constraints.  
- Theoretical development is careful, coherent, and technically strong with a well-motivated proof strategy (interior attainment → fixed point → contraction → linear convergence).  
- Bounds used to construct the matrix A are conservative and the assumptions (nonnegative data, strictly positive weights, distinct α, fixed ℓp norms) are strong and limit applicability.  
- Role and justification of modeling choices (sum-of-outputs term, ε-weight sum, norm constraints) are only partially explained and need more intuition.  
- Experiments are small-scale and show competitiveness but not superiority to strong baselines like RBF-SVM and ReLU nets.  
- Clarity issues: need simple examples illustrating ε and gradient positivity at the boundary (Lemma 5).  
- Clarity issues: necessity of fixed ℓp norms and their relation to scale invariance of multi-homogeneous networks should be explained more prominently.  
- Clarity issues: the switch from first- to second-order information when establishing bounds is not explicitly highlighted.  
- Notation: gradients w.r.t. matrix parameters should explicitly state vectorization when applying ψp and norms.  
- Originality: combining multi-homogeneous Perron–Frobenius theory with NN training to obtain a checkable global optimality condition and linearly convergent algorithm is novel.  
- Extension to two hidden layers is meaningful but makes conditions more restrictive.  
- Significance: advances theory of globally optimal NN training with a practically implementable algorithm for a restricted class, but practical impact is limited by modeling constraints and modified objective.  
- Technical clarification: Theorem 3 assumes first-order bounds; Theorem 4 uses second derivatives to verify those bounds by controlling the Hessian of Φ.  
- Technical clarification: last layer treated as K separate blocks and hidden-layer weights as a single block; gradients for u are implicitly vectorized and consistent.  
- Purpose of ℓp-sphere constraints: remove scale indeterminacy, yield compact feasible set, and keep contraction constants finite—mathematically necessary for uniqueness and convergence.  
- ε-weight sum is a technical device to ensure strict gradient positivity (interior attainment); its practical impact should be shown insensitive or replaced by cleaner positivity mechanisms.  
- Recommendation: borderline—strong and novel theory but constrained practicality, under-explained modeling choices, and limited empirical validation; improve clarity and experiments to strengthen acceptance.
