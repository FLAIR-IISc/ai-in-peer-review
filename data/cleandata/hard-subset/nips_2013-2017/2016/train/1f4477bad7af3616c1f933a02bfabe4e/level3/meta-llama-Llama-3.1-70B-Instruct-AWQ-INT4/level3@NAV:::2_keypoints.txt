- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method to train one- and two-hidden-layer generalized polynomial neural networks (GPNs) with a modified objective and regularization.
- Provides a rigorous global optimality proof for the proposed training method.
- Shows linear convergence rate to the global optimum.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on several UCI datasets.
- Imposes restrictive assumptions (non-negative weights, specific activation form) limiting applicability.
- Uses an uncommon generalized polynomial activation that may not generalize to typical practice.
- Scalability concerns: spectral radius bounds grow with hidden units, hurting high-dimensional scaling.
- Optimality condition may fail in practical settings, requiring extensive parameter search to find valid configurations.
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparison to nonlinear SVM baselines.
- Novel combination of nonlinear spectral methods and GPNs, plus a specific objective design, is original.
- Significant contribution by offering global optimality with linear convergence for neural network training.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains methods and theory.
- Recommend more experiments on larger datasets and architectures.
- Recommend adding nonlinear SVM comparisons and discussing scalability solutions.
- Recommend deeper discussion and practical guidance on the optimality condition.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance for NIPS despite noted limitations.
