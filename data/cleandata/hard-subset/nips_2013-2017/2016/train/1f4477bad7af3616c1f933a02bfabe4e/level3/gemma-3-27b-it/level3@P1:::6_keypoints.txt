- Novel method: introduces nonlinear spectral methods for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: proves linear convergence to a global optimum under relatively weak assumptions (Theorem 1).  
- Extension: method extended to two hidden layers (Theorem 5) with potential to generalize to deeper networks.  
- Well-motivated: situates work within difficulties of SGD and literature on provable training.  
- Dense but careful math: thorough mathematical framework, though presentation is dense.  
- Limited expressiveness analysis: insufficient discussion of what functions the constrained network class can/cannot approximate.  
- Related work gaps: should more explicitly contrast with tensor methods and structured matrix factorization approaches.  
- Weak experimental validation: experiments limited, mainly compared to SVMs and ReLU networks, neglecting other baselines.  
- Missing resource metrics: no training time or memory usage reported, making practical viability unclear.  
- Anecdotal convergence claims: "converges quickly" lacks rigorous substantiation.  
- Clarity problems: Section 2 lacks fluency, notation is dense, and intuitive explanations/intermediate steps are sparse.  
- Typos and undefined notation: several typos and undefined symbols (e.g., “polyomial”, “one CAN model”, p1/p2, R_{++}).  
- Unclear objective motivation: form of objective Φ and small ε term need clearer justification.  
- Recommendation: reject but encourage revision to strengthen experiments and expressiveness analysis.
