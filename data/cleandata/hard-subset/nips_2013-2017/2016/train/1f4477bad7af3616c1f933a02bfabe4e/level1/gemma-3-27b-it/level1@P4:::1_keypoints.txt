- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks with non-negativity constraints and a modified objective.
- Claims global optimality with linear convergence rate under certain spectral-radius conditions.
- Builds on prior work (Haeffele & Vidal 2015; Janzamin et al. 2015) and leverages Perron-Frobenius and nonlinear spectral theory.
- Novel combination of non-negativity constraints and spectral methods appears original.
- Substantial theoretical analysis with lemmas, theorems, and extension to two hidden layers.
- Proof strategy and mathematical derivations are clearly outlined overall.
- Notation and derivations are dense, with several logical jumps that reduce readability.
- Spectral-radius and parameter bounds (pu, pw) appear overly conservative and may limit practical applicability.
- Rationale for adding the sum-of-outputs term to the objective feels ad-hoc and insufficiently justified.
- The distinctness constraint on αi is strong and potentially restrictive, with no clear remedy provided.
- Experiments are limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets and show no clear advantage over SVM; parameter tuning is minimal.
- Missing contextual references (e.g., Thompson metric) and some literature coverage gaps.
- No discussion of computational complexity or scalability to higher-dimensional data and larger networks.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded method with rigorous analysis and preliminary empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues, conservative conditions, limited/weak experiments, and practical scalability concerns.
- Overall recommendation: reject, with encouragement to improve clarity, expand experiments, justify objective modifications, discuss scalability, and relax α constraints before resubmission.
