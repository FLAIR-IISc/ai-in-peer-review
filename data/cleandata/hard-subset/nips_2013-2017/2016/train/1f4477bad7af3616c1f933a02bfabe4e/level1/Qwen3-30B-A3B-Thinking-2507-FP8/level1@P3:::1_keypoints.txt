- Paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method that achieves global convergence for a class of neural networks with nonnegative weights and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Main theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 gives conditions for global optimality with linear convergence using Perron-Frobenius theory and fixed-point/Thompson metric arguments.  
- Approach differs from prior work by not requiring knowledge of the data-generating distribution and only assuming nonnegativity of data.  
- Strength: Significant theoretical advance—first practically feasible globally optimal training algorithm for this network class.  
- Strength: Proof strategy is sound and well-executed, linking multihomogeneous Perron-Frobenius theory to neural network training.  
- Strength: Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with technical proofs delegated to supplementary material.  
- Strength: Practical advantages include no hyperparameters and a checkable convergence condition (ρ(A) < 1), with experiments showing rapid linear convergence versus SGD.  
- Weakness: Nonnegativity constraint on weights greatly limits model expressiveness relative to standard neural networks.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets and do not demonstrate scalability to modern deep learning tasks (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet).  
- Weakness: Theoretical bounds for matrix A in Theorem 1 are conservative and lack a systematic way to assess tightness.  
- Weakness: No principled method for selecting model parameters (n1, α, ρw, ρu); authors rely on limited random search.  
- Significance: Original and valuable theoretical contribution, but practical impact is unclear due to model constraints and limited experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—strengthen discussion of limitations and provide more evidence of practical significance and trade-offs.
