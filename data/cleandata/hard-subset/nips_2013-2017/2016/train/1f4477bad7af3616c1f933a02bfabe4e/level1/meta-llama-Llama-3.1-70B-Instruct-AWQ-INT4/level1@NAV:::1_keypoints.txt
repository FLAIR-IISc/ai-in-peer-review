- Summary: Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks using fixed‑point iteration, with convergence proof and UCI experiments.

- Strength 1 (Theoretical guarantees): Provides rigorous analysis and a proof of convergence to the global optimum under certain conditions.

- Strength 2 (Novel approach): Introduces a new nonlinear spectral method that solves a non‑convex optimization via fixed‑point iteration.

- Strength 3 (Experimental results): Shows effectiveness on several UCI datasets.

- Weakness 1 (Limited applicability): Method is restricted to generalized polynomial feedforward networks; extension to general architectures is unclear.

- Weakness 2 (Computational complexity): No detailed complexity or scalability analysis for large datasets or complex models.

- Weakness 3 (Hyperparameter tuning): Lacks guidance or analysis on selecting and tuning hyperparameters.

- Originality: Novel contribution offering global optimality guarantees in neural network training.

- Significance: Potentially important due to rare global guarantees, but broader impact depends on extending applicability.

- Quality: Well‑written with clear presentation of method, theory, and experiments.

- Clarity: Paper is well‑organized and easy to follow.

- Argument for acceptance 1: Novel method with global optimality promise.

- Argument for acceptance 2: Strong theoretical guarantees and convergence proof.

- Argument for acceptance 3: Empirical validation demonstrating effectiveness.

- Argument for rejection 1: Applicability limited to a specific class of networks.

- Argument for rejection 2: Missing analysis of computational cost and scalability.

- Argument for rejection 3: Insufficient discussion on hyperparameter selection.
