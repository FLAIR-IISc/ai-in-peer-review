- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training generalized polynomial neural networks using Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Imposes non-negativity constraints on weights and modifies the objective to enable global optimality analysis.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees, including global optimality under specific conditions and a linear convergence rate.  
- Requires only data non-negativity, a weaker and more practical assumption than prior work that needs data-generating measure estimates.  
- Avoids computationally expensive steps like tensor decomposition used by some prior globally optimal methods.  
- Extends the theory to two hidden layers, broadening applicability beyond single-layer results.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains motivation and theoretical foundation despite dense math.  
- Experimental results on UCI datasets demonstrate feasibility and competitive performance versus ReLU networks and SVMs.  
- Novelty is a major strength — applying Perron-Frobenius in this context is original and significant.  
- Theoretical guarantees and linear convergence are strong and compelling contributions.  
- Practical feasibility is improved by weaker assumptions and simpler computations than some alternatives.  
- Non-negativity constraints on weights are restrictive and may limit network expressiveness in practice.  
- Scalability concerns: matrix A grows with hidden units and spectral radius computation may become a bottleneck.  
- Parameter sensitivity is an issue; guidance for selecting hyperparameters (α, ρw, ρu) is insufficient.  
- Limited experimental comparison and small-scale datasets; need broader evaluation on larger, complex benchmarks.  
- Mathematical density may hamper accessibility for readers not specialized in spectral/matrix theory.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance (Strong Accept) but requests revisions addressing scalability, parameter selection guidance, and expanded experiments.
