- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks with nonnegative weights and data and linear convergence guarantees.
- Advances prior work [8,11] by providing the first practically feasible algorithm that guarantees global optimality under the weak nonnegativity condition.
- Key insight: maps neural network training to Perron-Frobenius theory for multihomogeneous problems, enabling convergence analysis via spectral properties of a small matrix.
- Strong theoretical quality with rigorous proofs and a verifiable global convergence condition (spectral radius < 1).
- Method has no free hyperparameters (unlike SGD), simplifying optimization.
- Excellent clarity and reproducibility, with well-structured exposition and implementation detail.
- High originality as the first practical globally optimal training method for neural nets.
- Significance is substantial for theoretical ML, though practical impact is limited by nonnegativity restrictions.
- Empirical validation on UCI datasets is competitive (slightly worse than SVMs, better than some ReLU networks) and notable given prior lack of experiments.
- Main weaknesses: restrictive nonnegativity assumptions, limited to one/two hidden layers, and unclear impact of nonnegative constraints on model expressiveness.
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS based on rigorous theoretical contribution, with practical limitations to be acknowledged in the final version.
