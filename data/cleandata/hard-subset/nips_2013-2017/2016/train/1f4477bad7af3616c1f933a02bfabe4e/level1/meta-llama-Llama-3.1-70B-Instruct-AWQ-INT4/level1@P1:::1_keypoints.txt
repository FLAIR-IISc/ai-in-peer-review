- Main idea: Proposes a nonlinear spectral method to train generalized polynomial neural networks, proving global optimality with linear convergence for 1–2 hidden layers under weak assumptions.
- New objective: Introduces an objective combining loss and regularization that can be optimized via a nonlinear eigenvalue approach on convex cones.
- Strength — Novelty: Presents a novel alternative to SGD for potentially globally optimal training.
- Strength — Theory: Provides detailed convergence proofs and theoretical guarantees.
- Strength — Empirics: Shows competitive performance on several UCI datasets.
- Weakness — Data assumptions: Requires nonnegative data, which may be unrealistic for many tasks.
- Weakness — Experiments: Empirical evaluation is limited to a few UCI datasets and may not generalize.
- Weakness — Complexity: Method may be computationally expensive for large-scale data.
- Originality: Distinct contribution in the area of global optimality for neural networks compared to prior work.
- Significance: Potentially impactful approach with applications in CV and NLP if limitations are addressed.
- Clarity: Well-written overall but technically dense and hard for non-experts.
- Pro acceptance: Novel global training method, rigorous proofs, and competitive small-scale results.
- Con acceptance: Restrictive nonnegativity assumption, possible high computational cost, and limited empirical validation.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions — expand experimental scope and provide clearer analysis of computational complexity.
