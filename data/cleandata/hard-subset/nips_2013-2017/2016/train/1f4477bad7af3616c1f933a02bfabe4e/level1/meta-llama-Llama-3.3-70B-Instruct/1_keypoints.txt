- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for training neural networks that guarantees global optimality under certain conditions.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including convergence properties and mathematical proofs.  
- Experimental results on several UCI datasets show the method outperforms SGD and is competitive with kernel SVM and ReLU networks.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear, concise explanations and derivations.  
- Experiments are well-presented with clear comparisons and discussion of limitations and improvements.  
- Main weakness: method requires non-negative weights and a specific activation function, raising questions about practical applicability.  
- Secondary weakness: bounds on the spectral radius of matrix A are likely not tight, potentially limiting scalability to larger networks.  
- Review scores the paper highly on quality, clarity, originality, and significance.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
