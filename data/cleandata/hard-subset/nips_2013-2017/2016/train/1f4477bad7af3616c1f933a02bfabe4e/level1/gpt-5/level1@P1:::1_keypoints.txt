- Proposal: NLSM trains feedforward NNs with nonnegative inputs/weights and generalized polynomial activations to global optimality with linear convergence under verifiable conditions.
- Objective modification: training loss alters cross-entropy by adding the sum-of-outputs term and small positive linear weight terms, with Lp-norm constraints and maximization.
- Technical core: stationarity is recast as a fixed-point GΦ(w,u)=(w,u) and shown to be a contraction under the Thompson metric.
- Contraction control: a nonnegative matrix A (dependent on architecture, α, p-norms, radii, and input bounds) controls the Lipschitz constant; ρ(A)<1 yields guarantees.
- Guarantees: if ρ(A)<1 then a unique positive critical point exists, is the global maximizer, and the fixed-point iteration converges linearly from any positive start.
- Extensibility: construction extends to two hidden layers with an analogous matrix A and is in principle extendable to deeper nets.
- Checkability: the ρ(A)<1 condition can be checked a priori and enforced conservatively by choosing large p’s and appropriate radii.
- Theoretical novelty: first practical global-optimality method for a nontrivial NN class with explicit, checkable sufficient conditions and linear-rate guarantees.
- Mathematical rigor: careful use of Thompson metric, Banach fixed-point theorem, and multi-homogeneous Perron–Frobenius theory; explicit convergence rate ρ(A).
- Practical strengths: no step-size tuning, simple fixed-point iteration, and fast convergence in reported experiments.
- Modeling limitations: requires nonnegative inputs/weights, distinct α≥1 power activations, norm constraints, and a modified loss whose statistical meaning is underexplored.
- Conservativeness: bounds used to form A are loose, likely forcing large p and small radii that may shrink hypothesis capacity.
- Missing diagnostics: no empirical reporting of actual ρ(A) values versus theoretical bounds or how they scale with architecture.
- Limited experiments: evaluations only on small UCI datasets, often below kernel SVM, and no medium-scale scalability tests.
- Missing baselines: lacks comparisons to polynomial kernel SVM, explicit polynomial-feature models, and NMF-like or multiplicative-update baselines.
- Clarity issues: some equations are missing/poorly rendered, and the role/magnitude of ε and its interaction with normalization and calibration need clearer discussion.
- Complexity gap: per-iteration complexity and wall-clock/time comparisons (e.g., vs SGD) are not provided.
- Data practicality: handling signed features (feature splitting/offsets) and problems with negative inputs for non-integer α are not addressed.
- Suggested experiments: report actual and tighter ρ(A), ablate p-norm and radii choices, remove/add output-sum term to test necessity, and compare runtimes to SGD.
- Suggested comparisons: include polynomial kernel SVM, explicit polynomial features, and NMF-like baselines; add decision-boundary visualizations beyond toy cases.
- Acceptance arguments: elegant, novel theoretical framework with practical algorithmic simplicity and provable global convergence may stimulate follow-up work.
- Rejection arguments: restrictive architecture/loss, conservative conditions potentially limiting capacity, and modest empirical validation lacking key baselines.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept for theoretical novelty and potential impact, conditional on improved clarity and stronger empirical validation.
