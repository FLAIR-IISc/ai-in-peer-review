- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method casting training as a fixed-point problem on a positive cone using multi-homogeneous Perron–Frobenius theory and the Thompson metric.  
- Proves global optimality and linear convergence under a checkable spectral-radius condition ρ(A) < 1 for a small nonnegative matrix A (dim K+1 or K+2).  
- Treats one- and two-hidden-layer networks with generalized polynomial activations and requires distinct exponents per hidden unit.  
- Assumes nonnegative inputs and weights, normalization constraints, and optimizes a modified objective (cross-entropy + sum-of-outputs + small positive regularizer).  
- Provides explicit Lipschitz bounds tied to ρ(A) and a clear proof roadmap; theoretical development is careful and technically solid.  
- Conditions and bounds appear conservative, limiting applicability to small models; experiments confirm scalability limits.  
- Empirically competitive on small UCI datasets and converges in few iterations.  
- Novel use of nonlinear spectral/Perron–Frobenius tools for practically checkable global optimality and linear rates, distinct from tensor and matrix-factorization methods.  
- Practical impact limited by strong structural constraints (nonnegativity, distinct exponents) and the modified objective whose statistical implications are not fully analyzed.  
- Manuscript is well written for a mathematical audience, but some methodological choices need clearer motivation and guidance.  
- Lacks guidance on choosing α, the role/limit of ε→0, and practical per-iteration computational cost.  
- Needs tighter, data-dependent bounds on A and discussion of statistical/generalization effects of the modified objective.  
- Missing comparisons to related monotone/nonnegative models and NMF-based classifiers.  
- Pros: rigorous verifiable theory, novel methodology, extension to two layers, and algorithm is parameter-free once the model is fixed.  
- Cons: strong assumptions, conservative bounds limiting model size, limited empirical evaluation, and limited hyperparameter/expressivity guidance.
