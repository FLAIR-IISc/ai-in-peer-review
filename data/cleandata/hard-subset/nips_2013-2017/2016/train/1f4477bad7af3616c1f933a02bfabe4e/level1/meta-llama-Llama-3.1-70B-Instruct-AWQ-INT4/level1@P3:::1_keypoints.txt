- Novel approach: nonlinear spectral method for training generalized polynomial neural networks (GPNs) that guarantees global optimality under weak data assumptions.  
- Theoretical guarantees: proof of convergence to the global optimum and characterization of the Lipschitz constant of the mapping.  
- Efficiency: method is more efficient than SGD and achieves linear convergence rate.  
- Clarity and rigor: paper is well-organized with clear notation and rigorous mathematical derivations.  
- Experimental validation: demonstrated effectiveness on several UCI datasets.  
- Originality: approach is novel and distinct from standard SGD and existing tensor methods.  
- Potential impact: method could potentially be applied to other types of neural networks.  
- Limitation — data assumption: requires nonnegative data, which may not hold in many practical settings.  
- Limitation — computation: requires computing the spectral radius of a nonnegative matrix, which may be costly for large datasets.  
- Related work: thorough discussion and comparison to prior methods (e.g., Janzamin et al.).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; authors should address limitations and add experiments on larger datasets.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
