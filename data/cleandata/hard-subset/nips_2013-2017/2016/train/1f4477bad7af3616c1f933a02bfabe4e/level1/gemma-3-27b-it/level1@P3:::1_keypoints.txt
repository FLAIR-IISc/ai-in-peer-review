- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training generalized polynomial neural networks with global optimality guarantees under mild assumptions.  
- Shows global optimality for a class of networks assuming non-negative weights and inputs.  
- Establishes linear convergence rate for the proposed training method, contrasting typical non-convex behavior.  
- Builds on prior global-optimality work (Haeffele & Vidal, Janzamin et al.) and argues improved practicality.  
- Mathematical development is rigorous, well-structured, and clearly presents conditions for global optimality.  
- Provides a detailed proof strategy and extends the method to networks with two hidden layers.  
- Experimental results on small UCI datasets give initial support and show competitiveness with standard methods.  
- Claims practical feasibility relative to prior methods, though experiments are limited.  
- Major limitation: requirement of non-negative weights restricts expressiveness and practical applicability.  
- The added term (sum of outputs) to the loss feels ad-hoc and its practical impact on learned representations is unclear.  
- Experiments are limited in scope and do not demonstrate scalability to larger real-world tasks (e.g., image or NLP benchmarks).  
- Derived bounds (ensuring ρ(A) < 1) are likely conservative, possibly underestimating the true guarantee region.  
- Paper lacks explicit discussion of computational cost, especially computing spectral radius and per-iteration overhead for large networks.  
- Requirement of distinct α values per hidden unit is theoretically justified but may be restrictive in practice.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theory, linear convergence, rigorous presentation, and extension beyond one hidden layer.  
- Arguments against acceptance: non-negativity constraint, limited experimental validation, unclear computational costs, and restrictive activation/parameter choices.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with practical limitations; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: expand experiments to larger datasets, analyze impact of non-negativity constraint, detail computational complexity, seek tighter bounds on ρ(A), and clarify role/effects of the added loss term.
