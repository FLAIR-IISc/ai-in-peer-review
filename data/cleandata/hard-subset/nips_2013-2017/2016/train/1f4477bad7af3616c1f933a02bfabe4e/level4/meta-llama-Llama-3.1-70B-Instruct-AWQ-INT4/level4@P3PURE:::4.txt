The paper presents a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a particular class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The condition guaranteeing global optimality depends on the parameters of the network architecture and boils down to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. The proposal of a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a particular class of feedforward neural networks is new and interesting. However, I have concerns about the limitations of this approach, primarily the imposition of non-negativity on the network weights. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and very specific. This limitation appears to be quite strong, potentially hindering the performance of the neural networks. Indeed, numerical experiments demonstrate performance that is not significantly better than SVM, which may indicate the limitations of the approach. 

Minor comment: On lines 90-91, the statement "Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to..." seems exaggerated, as a linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply convergence in a few iterations.
