- Introduces a nonlinear spectral training method for a class of feedforward networks with a theoretical guarantee of global optimum convergence at a linear rate under a spectral-radius condition.  
- Main strength: notable theoretical contribution linking global optimality to the spectral radius of a small non-negative matrix derived from the network architecture.  
- Major weakness: requires all network weights to be non-negative, severely limiting expressiveness and preventing modeling of inhibitory/negative relationships.  
- Major weakness: depends on specialized, non-standard activation functions, making applicability to common activations (ReLU, sigmoid, tanh) unclear.  
- Major weakness: empirical results are weak—the proposed model does not outperform a standard SVM—calling practical value of the guarantees into question.  
- Minor comment: claim that linear convergence implies rapid convergence in "less than 10 iterations" is misleading without the contraction constant; authors should rephrase or provide empirical support.
