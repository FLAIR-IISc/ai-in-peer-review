- Proves a specific class of neural networks has a single stationary point (unique global minimizer).  
- Introduces a fast algorithm that converges to this unique fixed-point/global minimum.  
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map to establish uniqueness beyond convexity.  
- Convergence proof depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.  
- Spectral-radius requirement limits scalability (prevented exploration of larger hidden-layer networks).  
- Method involves many parameters that require careful tuning and is unintuitive to use; spectral condition must be checked per setting.  
- Experiments evaluated ~150,000 parameter combinations with cross-validation-based selection.  
- Method outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, raising doubts about practical advantage.
