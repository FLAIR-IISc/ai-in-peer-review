This paper introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific type of feedforward neural network, achieving a linear convergence rate. The method’s guarantee of global optimality is predicated on architectural parameters and requires calculating the spectral radius of a relatively small, nonnegative matrix. The proposal of a nonlinear spectral approach to optimal neural network training is a promising and interesting contribution. However, I have concerns regarding the method’s limitations. Specifically, the requirement of nonnegative weights represents a potentially significant constraint. Furthermore, the paper relies on a rather restrictive and unconventional selection of activation functions. These limitations may negatively impact the practical performance of networks trained with this method, as evidenced by the numerical results which demonstrate performance inferior to that of Support Vector Machines. A minor point: the claim in lines 90-91 that the method “converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations” due to its linear convergence rate appears overstated, as linear convergence does not inherently guarantee rapid convergence in terms of iteration count.
