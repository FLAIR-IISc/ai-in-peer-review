The paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The condition that guarantees global optimality depends on the network architecture parameters and is determined by computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This approach appears novel and interesting, as it provides an optimal training method for a particular class of feedforward neural networks.

However, I have concerns about the limitations of this approach. The requirement that the network weights be nonnegative may be overly restrictive. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and very specific. These limitations may significantly impact the performance of the neural networks. Indeed, the numerical experiments show mediocre performance compared to SVM.

Minor comment: L.90-91: The statement "Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to..." seems exaggerated. A linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply convergence in a few iterations.
