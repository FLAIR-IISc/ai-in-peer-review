- Novel nonlinear spectral method that attains global optimality under a spectral-radius condition, with linear convergence guarantees and computationally tractable low-dimensional spectral analysis; technically sound.  
- Requires non-negativity on all network weights, a highly restrictive constraint likely to harm representational capacity and unexamined for generalization effects.  
- Relies on highly specialized activation functions (e.g., tailored piecewise-linear) that deviate from standard activations, raising transferability concerns and no exploration of alternative activations or relaxations.  
- Empirical results are weak (1.5–3% lower accuracy on CIFAR-10 vs. SVM RBF), use small networks, and omit comparisons to modern deep baselines (e.g., ResNet-18), undermining practical relevance.  
- The statement that the method "converges quickly in a few (<10) iterations" is misleading: linear convergence rate depends on the spectral radius and initial error and may require many more iterations.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject—interesting theoretical contribution but insufficient practical impact without relaxing constraints or providing stronger empirical validation on challenging benchmarks.
