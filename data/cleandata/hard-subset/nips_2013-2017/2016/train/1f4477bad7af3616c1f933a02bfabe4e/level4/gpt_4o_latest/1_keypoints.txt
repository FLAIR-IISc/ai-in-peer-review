- Paper proves a specific class of neural networks has a single stationary point (unique minimizer).
- Author proposes a fast algorithm that converges to this unique fixed point, which equals the global minimum.
- Methodology uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive mapping whose fixed point is the minimizer.
- Shows that a relatively complex model can exhibit uniqueness under carefully chosen constraints.
- Convergence proof depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.
- The spectral-radius requirement prevents scaling the approach to networks with many hidden units.
- Method requires tuning a large number of hyperparameters, reducing intuitiveness and practicality.
- For every parameter setting the spectral condition must be checked, adding computational overhead.
- Experiments involved ~150,000 parameter combinations selected via cross-validation.
- Empirical results were weak: the method beat linear SVMs on only 2 out of 7 datasets, casting doubt on effectiveness.
