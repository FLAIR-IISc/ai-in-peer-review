- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a claimed linear convergence rate.
- Global optimality guarantee depends on network architecture and requires computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.
- Introduction of a nonlinear spectral training method for this network class is novel and intriguing.
- Major limitation: requires non-negativity constraints on network weights.
- Major limitation: uses non-standard, highly specific activation functions.
- These constraints may significantly limit the practical performance of the neural networks.
- Numerical experiments show the method performs worse than SVM in the reported tests.
- Minor comment: Claim that linear convergence implies convergence in “a few (less than 10) iterations” may be overstated.
