The paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The condition ensuring global optimality depends on the network’s architectural parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This is a novel and interesting direction.

However, I am concerned about the method’s limitations. It imposes nonnegativity constraints on all network weights, and the activation functions employed are nonstandard and highly specialized. These restrictions appear quite strong and may hinder neural network performance. Indeed, the numerical experiments show performance that is not competitive with SVMs.

Minor comment (L.90–91): The statement “Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly, typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to …” seems overstated, since a linear convergence rate does not, by itself, imply convergence in only a few iterations.
