- Summary: Proposes a nonlinear spectral method (NLSM) with provable global optimality and linear convergence for restricted feedforward nets (NLSM1/2) and gives checkable optimality conditions plus empirical tests.

- Strength: Targets escaping local minima by providing provable global optimality and linear convergence under explicit conditions.

- Strength: Optimality conditions are checkable a priori, offering practical verification in principle.

- Strength: Extending the analysis from one to two hidden layers is nontrivial and theoretically interesting.

- Weakness: Modeling assumptions are highly restrictive and counterintuitive (nonnegative weights, generalized polynomial activations, strong regularization) with little motivation for practical relevance.

- Weakness: Claimed expressive power is unconvincing; linear SVM often outperforms NLSM, and baselines are inadequately chosen/tuned.

- Weakness: Empirical evaluation uses small UCI datasets, which do not stress-test expressivity, robustness, or scalability.

- Weakness: Spectral radius bound becomes harder to satisfy as hidden units increase, raising scaling concerns with no mitigation strategies.

- Weakness: No practical method is provided to select architectures or regularization likely to satisfy the optimality condition without intractable search.

- Weakness: Unclear whether global optimality is due to the objective design or the spectral method; lacks comparisons to SGD and activation ablations.

- Request: Justify key assumptions—when nonnegative weights and polynomial activations are appropriate and beneficial in applications.

- Request: Clarify why the method appears to “maximize” regularization and how regularization affects the spectral conditions, bias/variance, and generalization.

- Request: Elaborate expressive power—what function classes are representable, provide formal approximation results or richer visualizations beyond toy cases.

- Request: Detail spectral radius bound implications—scaling with width/dimension/data normalization, design rules to manage it, and principled procedures to choose architectures/regularization.

- Request: Strengthen experimental protocol—include nonlinear SVMs, well-tuned MLPs (SGD/Adam), tree ensembles; match hyperparameter tuning budgets across methods.

- Request: Test scalability—evaluate on larger, higher-dimensional datasets; report accuracy, training time, and fraction of configurations satisfying the optimality condition.

- Request: Compare NLSM to SGD on the same objective/architecture and provide sensitivity analyses for activations and nonnegativity/regularization strengths.

- Minor: Clarify notation and definition of the matrix whose spectral radius is constrained and relate its entries to architecture and data statistics.

- Minor: Specify dataset details, preprocessing, and exact hyperparameter search spaces for all baselines for reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Technically interesting but empirical evidence, practical guidance, and applicability are insufficient; restrictive assumptions and limited experiments warrant substantial revision before acceptance.
