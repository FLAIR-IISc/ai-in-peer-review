- Paper proves existence of a global optimum with linear convergence for a class of neural nets and proposes a nonlinear spectral-method optimizer.  
- Theory requires all input data and linear-layer weights to be positive and activation functions to be generalized polynomials.  
- Proven for single-hidden-layer networks and extended to multi-layer architectures.  
- Preconditions reduce to a spectral-radius bound on a nonnegative matrix built from network parameters.  
- Claims applicability to arbitrary depth and shows reasonable results on small UCI datasets.  
- Main limitation: positivity constraint on network parameters may reduce model expressiveness.  
- Unrealistic requirement that all hidden-layer activation functions be different, limiting practical applicability.  
- Spectral-radius constraint (rho(A) < 1) enforced by increasing p1,p2 and decreasing pw,pu may force linear-unit weights toward zero.  
- As input/network dimension grows, shrinking linear weights may make activations nearly linear, undermining depth-based expressiveness and generalization.  
- Despite limitations, paper is an encouraging, valuable initial attempt and merits acceptance.  
- Minor: unclear quantitative relationship between p1,p2,pw,pu, input/network dimensions, and rho(A); empirical plots or analysis recommended.  
- Minor: authors should discuss implications for network design (activation choice, architecture) and evaluate on larger datasets.
