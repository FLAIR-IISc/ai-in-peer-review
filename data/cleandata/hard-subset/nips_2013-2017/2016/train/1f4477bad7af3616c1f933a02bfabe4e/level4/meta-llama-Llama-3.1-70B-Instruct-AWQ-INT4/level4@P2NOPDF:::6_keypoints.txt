- Proposes a method for training a specific feed-forward neural network class, proves optimal convergence, and shows applicability on some real-world tasks.  
- The problem is interesting and the theoretical contribution is appreciated.  
- Fails to convincingly demonstrate the practical usefulness of the restricted model.  
- Insufficient discussion of the model’s expressiveness limitations and lack of proposed workarounds; unclear what is achievable vs. out-of-scope.  
- No comparison with concurrent approaches; experimental baselines are weak.  
- Experiments only compare to SVM and ignore other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms.  
- Missing details about computational resources used by the three algorithms.  
- Introduction is strong, but overall presentation needs improvement; Section 2 lacks fluency.  
- Several typos and undefined notation (e.g., “polyomial”, “one CAN model”, undefined p1/p2, and R_{++} not introduced).  
- Loss function is straightforward but the function Φ lacks motivation; the role of the epsilon term should be included in the paper.  
- Theorem 1 would benefit from an informal explanation preceding the formal statement; the theorem statement is hard to parse.  
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” should be clarified as an empirical observation.
