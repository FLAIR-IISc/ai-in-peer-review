This paper presents a non-linear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The condition that ensures global optimization depends on the network's architecture parameters and is limited to the computation of the spectral radius of a small, non-negative matrix. The proposed non-linear spectral method appears new and interesting, providing optimal training for a specific class of feedforward neural networks. At the same time, I am concerned about the method's limitations; it imposes a non-negativity condition on the network's weights. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and very specific. The limitation seems very strong, which could hinder the good performance of neural networks. In fact, numerical tests do not show much better performance than SVMs. Small comment: L.90-91: "Note that the non-linear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and therefore converges quickly, usually in a few (less than 10) iterations..." This sentence seems exaggerated because a linear convergence rate is not necessary for a few iterations.
