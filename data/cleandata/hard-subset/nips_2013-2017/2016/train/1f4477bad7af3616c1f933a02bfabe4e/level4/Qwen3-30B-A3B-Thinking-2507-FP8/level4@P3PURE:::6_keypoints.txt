- Strong theoretical contribution: method for training a restricted class of feed-forward nets with optimal convergence guarantees and some real-world demos; problem well-motivated.  
- Practical relevance unclear: authors fail to convince that the restricted model class is useful in practice.  
- Expressiveness discussion incomplete: although addressed twice, the paper does not discuss feasible extensions or inherent limitations of the approach.  
- Lack of contextualization: concurrent methods are not explicitly contrasted with the proposed approach.  
- Weak experimental evaluation: comparisons are only against SVMs, omitting other neural-network training methods and algorithmic paradigms.  
- Missing computational-cost reporting: resource requirements for all compared algorithms are unreported.  
- Writing issues: introduction strong but Section 2 lacks fluency and contains minor problems.  
- Typos and notation gaps: "polyomial" (line 60), "one CAN model" (line 67), undefined p1, p2 (line 67), and undefined ℝ_{++} (line 67) which affects later variables.  
- Incomplete motivation: loss defined clearly, but Φ lacks motivation; the rationale for ε (explained in rebuttal) should be moved into the main text.  
- Theorem 1 presentation: needs an informal explanation before the formal statement; current phrasing is hard to parse.  
- Empirical claim needs qualification: statement that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” is based on experiments and must be explicitly presented as observational.
