1. Paper proposes a feedforward NN with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm that converges linearly, but several derivations are unclear.  
2. The jump from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4 lacks explicit justification or motivation.  
3. The architecture treats the last layer differently from hidden layers, creating potential inconsistencies in analysis and interpretation.  
4. The parameter matrix u is treated as a vectorized gradient in G^Φ (eq. 3), raising questions about consistency and the validity of bounding u in a ball.  
5. Equation (2) requires maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters, which conflicts with standard weight-decay practices that reduce the 1-norm.  
6. Introducing an arbitrarily small ε to ensure a strictly positive gradient appears to be a theoretical device rather than a practical design choice.  
7. The model’s lack of free parameters and constraints (non-negative data, exclusively positive weights, cumulative additions) raise practical applicability concerns.  
8. Experimental comparison to SVMs is limited and prompts whether this NN design offers practical benefits over standard architectures (e.g., ReLU networks).  
9. Overall practicality and real-world advantages of the proposed design need further empirical exploration and clearer theoretical exposition.
