- Paper studies feedforward nets trainable to global optimality with a nonlinear spectral method for 1–2 hidden layers, but core contributions are questioned.  
- Model imposes restrictive, counterintuitive assumptions (non-negative weights and maximization of weight regularization) with little practical justification.  
- Requires an uncommon activation (generalized polynomial) for optimality, which is not well-motivated for real data.  
- Authors fail to explain how their assumptions relate to real-world datasets or practical scenarios.  
- Claims of sufficient expressive power are not convincingly supported by the empirical results.  
- Baseline comparisons are flawed: using a linear SVM against a nonlinear model is inappropriate; nonlinear baselines (e.g., kernel SVMs) are needed.  
- Experimental datasets (UCI) are too small in sample size and dimensionality to validate claims.  
- Scalability is problematic: bounds on the spectral radius of A grow with the number of hidden units, hindering applicability to high-dimensional data.  
- The paper does not propose mitigation strategies for the scalability limitations, making the method impractical for large-scale problems.  
- The optimality condition appears highly sensitive and may not hold under typical parameter settings.  
- No practical procedure is provided to efficiently identify valid parameter candidates without extensive search.  
- It is unclear whether global optimality is intrinsic to the objective or the method; the authors should test whether SGD can reach the same global optima on the datasets.  
- Due to these gaps, the paper’s contributions are limited in scope and real-world applicability.
