- Paper proves existence of a single stationary point for a specific constrained class of neural networks and shows it is the global minimum.
- Authors propose a fast-convergence method to this unique fixed point by constructing a contractive map.
- The approach leverages fixed-point theory to establish uniqueness beyond convexity/geodesic convexity.
- Convergence proof crucially depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.
- The spectral-radius condition restricts applicability to networks with a small number of hidden units.
- Method requires many hyperparameters that are unintuitive and need careful tuning.
- For every parameter setting, the required spectral condition must be separately verified.
- Experimental evaluation involved ~150,000 parameter combinations selected by cross-validation.
- Empirical results were weak: method outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, casting doubt on practical benefits.
