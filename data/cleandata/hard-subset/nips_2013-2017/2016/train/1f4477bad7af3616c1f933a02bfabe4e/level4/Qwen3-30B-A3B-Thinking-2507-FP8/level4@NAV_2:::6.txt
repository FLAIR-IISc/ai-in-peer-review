

The paper introduces a novel method for training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with guaranteed global optimality under certain assumptions. The authors establish theoretical convergence guarantees for their nonlinear spectral method, demonstrating linear convergence rates for networks with one or two hidden layers. While the theoretical contribution is significant, the paper does not sufficiently establish the practical utility of the proposed model. The authors acknowledge the expressiveness limitations of their restricted architecture twice in the paper, but fail to provide meaningful discussion on how these limitations affect real-world applications or what tasks might be intractable for their approach.

The experimental section would benefit from a more comprehensive comparison with concurrent methods. The results only compare against SVM and ReLU-based networks, omitting other relevant training methods for neural networks and algorithmic paradigms. Additionally, the computational resources required by each method are not reported, making it difficult to assess practical trade-offs.

The introduction is well-structured, but Section 2 lacks fluency. Several typos were identified: "polyomial" on line 60, "CAN model" on line 67 (should be "can model"), missing definitions for p1 and p2, and undefined notation R_{++} (which was clarified in the rebuttal but should be in the main text).

The loss function is clearly defined, but the motivation for the objective function Φ is insufficient. The role of the small epsilon term, explained in the rebuttal, should be incorporated into the main text. Theorem 1 would be more accessible with an informal explanation before its formal statement. The claim about "very fast convergence typically in fewer than 10 iterations" should be clarified as being based on experimental observations rather than theoretical guarantees.

The paper makes an interesting theoretical contribution to neural network optimization, but the practical relevance of the proposed method requires further justification. The experimental results are limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets, which does not fully demonstrate the approach's applicability to more complex real-world problems.
