- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for training a class of feedforward networks with global optimality guarantees.  
- Achieves linear convergence to the global optimum under weak nonnegativity assumptions on data.  
- Builds on prior global optimization work [8,11] while addressing their practical limitations (no hyperparameter tuning required).  
- Trains generalized polynomial neural networks (real-valued activation exponents, nonnegative weights) via a fixed-point iteration.  
- Proves uniqueness of the global maximum and linear convergence when the spectral radius of a derived matrix is < 1.  
- Provides rigorous, well-structured proofs using a fixed-point framework motivated by Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Presentation is generally clear; supplementary material appropriately contains intermediate results.  
- Demonstrates originality by using a non-standard architecture and modified objective to enable global optimality.  
- Adequately situates work within related literature, including recent NIPS contributions.  
- Offers a theoretically grounded alternative to SGD for specific networks, potentially removing hyperparameter tuning.  
- Experiments on UCI datasets show competitive performance with ReLU networks and SVMs and verify spectral radius conditions.  
- Nonnegativity constraint on weights is restrictive and may limit model expressiveness.  
- Experimental gains are modest and limited (outperforming linear SVMs on 2/7 datasets); experimental scope is narrow.  
- Parameter tuning (100+ random combinations per dataset) appears cumbersome in practice.  
- Spectral radius condition limits scalability to deeper networks or larger hidden layers and needs clearer practical interpretation.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with excellent technical quality, but practical applicability is constrained by architectural and experimental limitations.
