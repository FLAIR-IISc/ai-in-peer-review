- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a stated linear convergence rate.
- Global optimality condition depends on network architectural parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.
- The approach is novel and an interesting research direction.
- Method requires nonnegativity constraints on all network weights, which is a significant limitation.
- Uses nonstandard, highly specialized activation functions, further restricting applicability.
- These restrictions may hinder practical neural network performance; experiments show results not competitive with SVMs.
- Minor critique: claiming that a linear convergence rate implies convergence in only a few (less than 10) iterations is overstated.
