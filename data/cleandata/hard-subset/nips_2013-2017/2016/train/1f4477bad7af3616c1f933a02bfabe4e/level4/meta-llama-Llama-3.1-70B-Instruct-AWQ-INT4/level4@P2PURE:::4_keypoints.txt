- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, with a proven linear convergence rate.  
- Global optimality condition depends on network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The approach is novel and intriguing as a nonlinear spectral training method for this network class.  
- Method requires non-negativity constraints on network weights, potentially limiting applicability.  
- Uses non-standard, highly specific activation functions, further restricting generality.  
- These limitations may hurt practical performance; experiments show the method underperforms relative to SVM.  
- Minor critique: claiming convergence in "less than 10 iterations" is exaggerated—linear convergence does not guarantee such rapid convergence.
