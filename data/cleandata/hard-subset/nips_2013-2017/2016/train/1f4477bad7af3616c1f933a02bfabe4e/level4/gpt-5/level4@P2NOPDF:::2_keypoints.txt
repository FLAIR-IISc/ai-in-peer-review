- The paper presents an interesting theoretical result (global optimality with linear convergence) but studies a highly restrictive class of networks.  
- Modeling constraints (non-negativity, very strong regularization, and an uncommon generalized polynomial activation) appear counterintuitive and need application-driven justification.  
- The paper lacks evidence that these constraints are realistic or advantageous in practice rather than imposed solely for theoretical guarantees.  
- Empirical results do not convincingly show sufficient expressive power; in some cases a linear SVM outperforms NLSM variants.  
- Baselines are insufficiently competitive—only a linear SVM is used; nonlinear baselines (e.g., polynomial/RBF SVMs) are needed for fair comparison.  
- Experiments rely mainly on small UCI datasets, limiting assessment of scalability and practical relevance.  
- Theoretical bounds (spectral radius of A growing with hidden units) suggest scalability problems; discussion of mitigation strategies (architectural constraints, normalization, preprocessing) is missing.  
- It is unclear how often the optimality conditions hold in realistic hyperparameter ranges; guidance, bounds, or heuristics to narrow the search space are needed.  
- The paper should clarify whether observed global optimality stems from the objective/constraints or the optimization method—compare to standard training (e.g., SGD) under the same constraints.  
- Overall, interesting theory but weakened impact due to strong assumptions, limited/unfair empirical evaluation, and unclear scalability; stronger validation and practical guidance are required.
