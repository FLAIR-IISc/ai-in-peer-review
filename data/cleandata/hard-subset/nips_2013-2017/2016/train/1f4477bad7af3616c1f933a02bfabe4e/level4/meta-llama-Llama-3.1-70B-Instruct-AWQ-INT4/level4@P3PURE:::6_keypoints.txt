- Proposes a method to train a restricted feed-forward neural network with theoretical optimal convergence and demonstrates applicability to some real-world problems.  
- Paper addresses an interesting problem and contributes a theoretical advance but weakly demonstrates practical usefulness of the proposed network class.  
- Authors repeatedly raise concerns about the expressiveness of their restricted model; the paper should include discussion of the model's capabilities and limitations.  
- A comparison with concurrent and alternative approaches is missing and would strengthen the paper.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient to clarify the model’s applicability limits.  
- Experiments compare only to SVMs and omit other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms.  
- The manuscript lacks information about computational resources used by the three algorithms.  
- Introduction is well-written, but the presentation after that (notably Section 2) lacks fluency and needs improvement.  
- Several typos and minor language issues need correction (e.g., "polyomial" → "polynomial", rephrase "one CAN model").  
- The loss function is clear but the function Φ lacks motivation; the role of the ε term should be added to the paper.  
- Theorem 1 should be prefaced by an informal explanation to aid readability; the convergence speed claim should be stated as an experimental observation.  
- Variables p1 and p2 should be introduced before use and the notation R_{++} must be defined.
