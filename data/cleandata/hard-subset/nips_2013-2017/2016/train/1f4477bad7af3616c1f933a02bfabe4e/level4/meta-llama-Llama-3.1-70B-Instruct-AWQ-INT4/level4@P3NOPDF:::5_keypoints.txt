- Proposes a feedforward network with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm that converges at a linear rate.
- Mathematical derivation is instructive but several steps are unclear.
- Unclear motivation for shifting from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4.
- Notation/interpretation ambiguity: last layer appears treated separately and gradients w.r.t. parameter matrix u are handled as vectorized gradients.
- Similar vectorization/notation issues recur when bounding u within a ball.
- The requirement to maximize the 1-norm of connection parameters (eq. 2) is unusual and not well-justified.
- Introducing an arbitrarily small ε to ensure a strictly positive gradient appears to be a mathematical convenience rather than a practical necessity.
- Lack of explanation for the absence of free parameters in the model.
- Practical limitations: requires non-negative data and uses only positive weights, which may cause weight accumulation and limit applicability.
- Experimental comparison to SVMs is interesting but insufficient; no comparisons to common architectures (e.g., ReLU networks).
- Overall: interesting theoretical result but practical implications, justification of assumptions, and broader empirical validation are underexplored.
