- Proposes a training method for a restricted class of feed-forward networks and proves optimal convergence under certain assumptions.
- Theoretical contribution is valuable for understanding neural network training dynamics.
- Introduction is well-written and clearly motivates the problem and approach.
- Core theoretical result (optimal convergence for the restricted architecture) is a significant step forward.
- Practical utility and scope of the restricted network class are insufficiently characterized; need analysis of what functions the model can/cannot represent.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and does not convincingly demonstrate advantages or limitations of the method.
- Baselines are limited (only SVM); must compare to standard gradient-based training (SGD/Adam) and other competitive algorithms.
- No information provided on computational cost (training time, memory, wall-clock convergence) to assess practical viability.
- Experiments are cherry-picked to show success and do not probe the method's failure modes or expressiveness limits.
- Presentation quality drops after the introduction; Section 2 is hard to follow and Theorem 1 is dense and needs an informal explanation beforehand.
- The loss function (Φ) and the role of the epsilon term lack motivation and should be explained in the main text.
- The convergence claim about the nonlinear spectral method should be presented as an empirical observation rather than a general property.
- Minor typos/notation issues: "polyomial" → "polynomial"; awkward phrasing "one CAN model"; p1 and p2 introduced without definition; R_{++} notation should be defined.
