The paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The condition ensuring global optimality relies on the network’s architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small, nonnegative matrix. Proposing a nonlinear spectral method for optimal neural network training is a novel and interesting contribution. 

However, I have concerns regarding the limitations of this approach. First, it imposes a non-negativity constraint on the network’s weights. Second, the activation functions used within the neural network are non-standard and highly specific. These constraints seem quite strong and could potentially limit the performance of the resulting neural networks. Indeed, the numerical experiments presented do not demonstrate competitive performance compared to Support Vector Machines (SVMs). 

A minor comment regarding line 90-91: The statement “Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to…” appears overstated, as a linear convergence rate does not guarantee fast convergence in terms of iteration count.
