- Proves a special class of neural networks has a single stationary point.
- Proposes a fast method that converges to the unique fixed point, which is the global minimum.
- Novel contribution: shows a relatively complex, constrained model can still have only one stationary point.
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose fixed point equals the minimizer.
- Convergence proof relies on bounded spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, restricting scalability to larger hidden layers.
- Method involves many tunable parameters and requires checking the spectral condition for each parameter choice, making it unintuitive.
- Empirically tested ~150,000 parameter combinations via cross-validation but outperformed linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, raising doubts about practical effectiveness.
