- Proposes a training method for a specific class of feed-forward neural networks with a proof of optimal convergence.  
- Demonstrates applicability on several real-world datasets, but practical utility of the restricted network class is not convincingly established.  
- Theoretical contributions are valuable and the research problem is compelling.  
- The paper lacks a thorough discussion of the model's expressiveness: which functions it can or cannot represent.  
- Needs comparison and discussion of concurrent methods in the literature.  
- Experimental evaluation fails to reveal practical boundaries and limitations of the proposed model.  
- Baseline comparisons are too narrow (only SVMs); missing alternative neural-network training methods and other algorithmic families.  
- Experiments omit crucial computational-cost information (training time, memory usage).  
- Presentation quality drops after the introduction; Section 2 is unclear and hard to follow.  
- The motivation for the function Φ and the role of the epsilon term should be explained in the main text.  
- Theorem 1 is hard to parse and should be preceded by an informal, high-level explanation of its implications.  
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method "converges quickly" is empirical and should be stated as an experimental observation, not a general theoretical guarantee.  
- Several typos and notation issues: "polyomial" → "polynomial", "one CAN model" unclear, p1 and p2 used without definition, and R_{++} not defined.
