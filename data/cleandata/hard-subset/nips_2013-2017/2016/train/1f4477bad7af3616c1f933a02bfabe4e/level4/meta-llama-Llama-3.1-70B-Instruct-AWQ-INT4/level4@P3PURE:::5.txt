This paper presents a specific type of feedforward neural network for which a unique global optimizer can be obtained. As a result, the authors define an algorithm that finds this global optimizer, which exhibits a linear convergence rate. The mathematical derivation is instructive, although some parts are unclear to me.

Firstly, I find the transition from Theorem 3 to Theorem 4 to be somewhat abrupt, as the former involves the computation of first-order derivatives, while the latter involves second-order derivatives. What motivates this shift in the level of derivatives?

Secondly, the design of this network architecture appears to treat the last layer separately from the hidden layers. Specifically, since u is a matrix representing the parameters connecting to the hidden layer, the gradient with respect to u in G^\Phi (3) seems to be computed in terms of the vectorization of u. Similar questions arise in other parts of the paper, such as when bounding u to be within a ball.

I also have concerns regarding the maximization of the 1-norm of the connection parameters in equation (2). In most neural network models, weight decay is used to reduce the 1-norm, which seems to be at odds with the approach taken here. It appears that the introduction of an arbitrarily small epsilon in equation (2) is necessary to ensure that the gradient is strictly positive, thus belonging to V_++. However, this justification seems to be primarily driven by the need to facilitate the proof, rather than being grounded in practical neural network design. Furthermore, this approach does not seem to introduce any free parameters, which could be seen as a limitation.

The requirement that all data be non-negative and the use of all positive weights (without even allowing zero weights) may also limit the practicality of this design. The fact that the model keeps adding up weights without any form of regularization or decay may not be desirable in practice.

In the experimental section, the proposed neural network is compared to SVM, which also has a global optimizer. However, it is unclear whether this type of neural network would offer any advantages over more commonly used neural network architectures, such as those with ReLU activations.
