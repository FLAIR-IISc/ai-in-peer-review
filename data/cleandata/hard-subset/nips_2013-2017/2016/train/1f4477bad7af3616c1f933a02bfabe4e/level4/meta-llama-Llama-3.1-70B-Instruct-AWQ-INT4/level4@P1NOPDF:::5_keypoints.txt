- Paper claims a specific feedforward network has a unique global optimizer and gives an algorithm with linear convergence, but some mathematical steps are unclear.
- Unclear rationale for the shift from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4.
- Architecture treats the last layer separately from hidden layers; unclear how gradients w.r.t. matrix parameter u are handled (vectorization?) and how constraints like bounding u in a ball are applied.
- Concern about maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters in eq. (2) — it conflicts with common weight-decay practices and the epsilon added to ensure strictly positive gradients seems ad hoc.
- Question whether the epsilon trick guarantees absence of free parameters and whether that is justified for practical networks.
- Practicality concerns: model requires non-negative data and strictly positive weights (no zeros), which may be unrealistic and limit usefulness.
- Experimental comparison to SVM is reasonable, but it’s unclear whether this network offers advantages over standard architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) in practice.
