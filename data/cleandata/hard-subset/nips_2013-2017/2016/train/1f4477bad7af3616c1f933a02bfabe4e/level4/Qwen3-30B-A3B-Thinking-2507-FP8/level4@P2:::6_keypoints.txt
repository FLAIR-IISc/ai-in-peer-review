- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for a class of feedforward neural networks with provable global optimality under mild nonnegativity assumptions.  
- Proves global convergence with linear rate for generalized polynomial networks (Theorem 1).  
- Theoretical advance over standard SGD methods which lack global guarantees.  
- Builds on prior global optimality work but avoids data density estimation and practical limitations of earlier approaches.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous, verifiable conditions and detailed proofs.  
- Novel approach inspired by Perron-Frobenius theory and practically implementable algorithm.  
- Potentially significant step toward theoretically grounded, reliable neural network training.  
- Paper is generally well-organized with clear definitions and useful supplementary material.  
- Practical relevance limited by nonnegativity constraints and specific activation functions; unclear feasible applications.  
- Experimental evaluation is sparse (few UCI datasets) and lacks comparisons to relevant baselines and computational resource metrics.  
- Presentation issues: typos, inconsistent notation, and a hard-to-parse Theorem 1 without informal explanation.  
- Insufficient comparison with concurrent theoretically grounded methods and unclear practical improvements.  
- Pros: first practically feasible globally optimal training method, rigorous guarantees, competitive benchmark performance, minimal hyperparameter tuning, solid theory.  
- Cons: limited practical applicability discussion, weak experimental comparisons, incomplete expressiveness analysis, presentation problems, no computational cost analysis.  
- Recommendation: reject—strong theory but inadequate demonstration of practical significance; needs much stronger experiments, comparisons, and discussion of trade-offs.
