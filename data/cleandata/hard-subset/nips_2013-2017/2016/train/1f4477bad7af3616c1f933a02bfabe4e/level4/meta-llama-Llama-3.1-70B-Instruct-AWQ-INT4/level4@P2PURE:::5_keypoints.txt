- Paper proposes a feedforward network with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm with linear convergence, but several clarifications are needed.  
- The jump from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4 is not adequately explained.  
- The network treats the last layer separately from hidden layers, raising questions about how gradients w.r.t. the matrix u are computed.  
- It is unclear whether the gradient w.r.t. u is the gradient of the vectorized u and how this is handled elsewhere.  
- Bounding u within a ball is presented without sufficient explanation of implications or computation.  
- The objective’s maximization of the 1-norm of connection parameters (eq. 2) is not well-justified compared to common weight-decay practices.  
- Introducing an arbitrarily small ε to force strictly positive gradients appears to be a technical proof device rather than a practical design rationale.  
- The absence of free parameters in the model is concerning and not well motivated.  
- Requiring non-negative data and all positive weights limits practical applicability and generality.  
- Experimental comparison to SVM is unconvincing because SVM is not the typical baseline for the evaluated tasks.  
- The paper should compare against standard neural architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) to demonstrate practical benefits.
