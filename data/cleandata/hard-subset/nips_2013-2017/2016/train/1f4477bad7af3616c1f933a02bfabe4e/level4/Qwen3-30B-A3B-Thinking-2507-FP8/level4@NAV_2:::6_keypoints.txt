- Introduces a novel training method for a restricted class of feedforward networks with guaranteed global optimality under certain assumptions.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees (linear rates) for the nonlinear spectral method on networks with one or two hidden layers.
- Theoretical contribution is significant, but practical utility of the proposed model is not sufficiently established.
- Authors acknowledge expressiveness limitations of their restricted architecture twice but fail to discuss impact on real-world applications or which tasks may be intractable.
- Experimental comparisons are limited to SVM and ReLU networks, omitting other relevant neural-network training methods and algorithmic paradigms.
- Computational resources and runtime details for each method are not reported, hindering assessment of practical trade-offs.
- Introduction is well-structured, but Section 2 lacks fluency and needs rewriting for clarity.
- Several typos and notation issues: "polyomial", "CAN model" (should be "can model"), missing definitions for p1 and p2, and undefined R_{++} (clarified only in rebuttal).
- Loss function is defined, but the motivation for the objective Φ is insufficiently explained in the main text.
- The small epsilon term (explained in rebuttal) should be incorporated into the main text with rationale.
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement to aid accessibility.
- The claim of "very fast convergence typically in fewer than 10 iterations" should be labeled as an empirical observation, not a theoretical guarantee.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets and does not demonstrate applicability to more complex, real-world problems.
