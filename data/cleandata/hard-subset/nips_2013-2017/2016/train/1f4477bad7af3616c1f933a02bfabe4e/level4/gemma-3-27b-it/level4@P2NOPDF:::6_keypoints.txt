- Introduces a novel training method for a specific feed-forward network class and provides a valuable convergence guarantee.
- Practical utility is not fully established; unclear how useful the approach is in real tasks.
- Model expressiveness is limited and discussed as restrictive; paper should clarify what problems it can and cannot solve.
- Needs broader comparison with related work and alternative training paradigms to contextualize contributions.
- Experimental evaluation is weak—comparisons are limited (only SVMs) and omit relevant neural-network baselines.
- Missing computational-resource details (runtime, memory) for reproducibility and practical assessment.
- Introduction is strong, but later presentation (especially Section 2) lacks clarity and logical flow.
- The loss function’s Phi (and the epsilon term) lacks sufficient motivation; rebuttal explanation should be incorporated into the paper.
- Theorem 1 is dense; provide an informal explanation of its implications before the formal statement.
- The claim about rapid convergence of the nonlinear spectral method appears empirical and should be explicitly stated as such.
- Several minor typographical and clarity issues: “polyomial” → “polynomial”, “one CAN model” → “one can model”.
- Notation problems: p1 and p2 are used without introduction, and R_{++} is not defined despite frequent use.
