- Introduces a class of feedforward networks provably trainable to a global optimum with linear convergence via a nonlinear spectral method.  
- Demonstrates the method on networks with one and two hidden layers across several real-world datasets.  
- Network class is highly restrictive: enforces non-negativity of weights and maximal regularization without clear justification.  
- Requires an uncommon activation (generalized polynomial), limiting practical applicability.  
- Authors should justify constrained assumptions with application-specific benefits or real-data insights rather than prioritizing global optimality.  
- Claims of sufficient expressive power and strong performance are not convincingly supported by the empirical results.  
- Comparison to a linear SVM is inappropriate and likely unfair due to apparent under-tuning of the SVM baseline.  
- A fairer benchmark would include nonlinear SVMs (e.g., polynomial or Gaussian kernels).  
- Experiments use small UCI datasets, limiting the generalizability of the findings.  
- Spectral radius bounds grow with the number of hidden units, raising scalability concerns for high-dimensional data.  
- Verifying the global optimality condition is intractable in large parameter spaces; methods to identify smaller candidate sets are needed.  
- It’s unclear whether optimality arises from the specialized objective; testing whether SGD can empirically reach global optima on these datasets is a valuable direction.
