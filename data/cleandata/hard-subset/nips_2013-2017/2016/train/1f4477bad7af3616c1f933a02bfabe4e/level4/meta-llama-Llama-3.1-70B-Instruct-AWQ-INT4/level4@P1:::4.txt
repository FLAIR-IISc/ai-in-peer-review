The paper presents a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a particular class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. This is a new and interesting approach, as it provides a guarantee of global optimality under certain weak assumptions on the data. The condition that guarantees global optimality depends on the parameters of the architecture of the network and can be checked by computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This is a significant contribution, as it addresses the issue of non-convex optimization in neural networks.

However, I have some concerns about the limitations of the approach. The method imposes non-negativity constraints on the weights of the network, which may hinder the performance of the neural network. Additionally, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and very specific. These limitations may restrict the applicability of the method to certain types of problems.

The experimental results show that the method achieves competitive performance on some UCI datasets, but not-so-good performance compared to SVM. This raises questions about the effectiveness of the method in practice. Furthermore, the statement in lines 90-91 that the nonlinear spectral method converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations seems exaggerated, as the linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply a few iterations.
