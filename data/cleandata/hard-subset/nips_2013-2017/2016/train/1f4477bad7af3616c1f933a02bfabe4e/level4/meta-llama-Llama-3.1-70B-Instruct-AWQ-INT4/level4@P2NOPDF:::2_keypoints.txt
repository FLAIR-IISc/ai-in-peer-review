- Studies a restrictive class of networks enforcing non-negativity and heavy weight regularization.  
- Requires generalized polynomial activation functions, which are uncommon and need justification for practical use.  
- Claim of sufficient expressive power is not convincingly supported by the experiments.  
- Linear SVM often outperforms NLSM1/NLSM2; using a linear SVM baseline is surprising and nonlinear SVM baselines (polynomial/Gaussian) are missing.  
- Experiments use small UCI datasets with limited sample sizes and feature dimensionality, limiting generalizability.  
- Bounds on the spectral radius grow with the number of hidden units, raising scalability concerns for high-dimensional data.  
- The optimality condition may not hold for all practical parameter settings and is difficult to ensure without priors.  
- No fast method is provided to obtain a small set of valid parameter candidates for which the optimality condition holds.  
- The global-optimal property seems tied to a special objective; it’s unclear whether standard optimizers like SGD can empirically reach the global optimum.
