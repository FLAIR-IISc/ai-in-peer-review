This paper proposes a specific type of feedforward neural network for which a unique global optimizer can be obtained, along with an algorithm to find this optimizer that converges at a linear rate. The mathematical derivation is instructive, but some parts are unclear.

One concern is the transition from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4. It is not immediately apparent what motivates this shift. Additionally, the design of the network appears to treat the last layer separately from the hidden layers. Specifically, the gradient with respect to the parameter matrix u in equation (3) seems to be a gradient in terms of the vectorization of u. This is also true for other parts of the paper, such as when bounding u within a ball.

A further concern is the requirement to maximize the 1-norm of the connection parameters in equation (2). In most neural network models, weight decay is used to reduce the 1-norm. The introduction of an arbitrarily small epsilon in equation (2) to ensure a strictly positive gradient seems to be a mathematical convenience rather than a practical consideration. It is not clear why this is necessary or what justifies the absence of free parameters in this model.

The proposed network has some limitations in terms of practicality. It requires all data to be non-negative and uses only positive weights, which can lead to an accumulation of weights. This design may not be suitable for all types of data.

The experimental comparison with Support Vector Machines (SVMs) is interesting, as SVMs also have a global optimizer. However, it is unclear whether this type of neural network offers any advantages over more commonly used neural networks, such as those with ReLU activations. Further experiments comparing the proposed network to these more typical architectures would be beneficial in demonstrating its value.

Overall, while the paper presents an interesting theoretical result, its practical implications and limitations need to be more thoroughly explored.
