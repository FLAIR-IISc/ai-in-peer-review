The paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The condition guaranteeing global optimality relies on the network's architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This approach appears novel and intriguing, as it offers a nonlinear spectral method for training a particular class of feedforward neural networks. However, I have concerns regarding the limitations of this approach. Specifically, it imposes a non-negativity constraint on the network weights, which may restrict its applicability. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and highly specific. These limitations seem significant and may hinder the performance of the neural networks. Indeed, the numerical experiments demonstrate less-than-impressive performance compared to SVM. Minor comment: L.90-91: The statement claiming that the nonlinear spectral method converges quickly, typically in a few (less than 10) iterations, due to its linear convergence rate, seems exaggerated, as linear convergence does not necessarily imply rapid convergence within a few iterations.
