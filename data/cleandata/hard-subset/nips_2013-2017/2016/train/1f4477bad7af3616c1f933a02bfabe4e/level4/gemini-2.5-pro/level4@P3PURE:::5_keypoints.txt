- Contribution: Proposes a class of feedforward networks with a guaranteed unique global optimum and an algorithm that finds it with linear convergence, supported by mathematical derivations.
- Theorem gap: The transition from Theorem 3 (first-order conditions) to Theorem 4 (second-order conditions) is unclear and needs motivation for increasing derivative order.
- Parameter ambiguity: The treatment of the parameter matrix u is ambiguous (e.g., whether gradients are taken w.r.t. vectorized u) and operations/constraints on u should be clearly defined.
- Theory-driven design: The model appears tailored to satisfy proof conditions (e.g., maximizing L1-norm and adding a small ε to force positive gradients) rather than justified by ML modeling principles.
- Practicality concerns: Requiring non-negative inputs and strictly positive weights restricts the network to additive positive aggregations, limiting general-purpose applicability.
- Limited experiments: Empirical evaluation is weak; comparisons should include standard non-convex neural networks (e.g., ReLU models) to assess whether global-optimum guarantees yield practical benefits.
