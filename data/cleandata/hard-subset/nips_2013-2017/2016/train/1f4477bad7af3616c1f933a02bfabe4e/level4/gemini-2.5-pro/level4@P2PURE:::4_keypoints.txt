- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with provable linear convergence and a global-optimality condition testable via the spectral radius of a small non-negative matrix.

- Major practical limitation: requires non-negativity of all weights and uses non-standard, highly specific activation functions, imposing strong constraints that likely reduce expressiveness.

- Empirical results appear to confirm the practical concern: the method performs worse than an SVM in the reported numerical experiments.

- Minor critique: the claim that the method "converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations" is overstated—linear convergence does not guarantee convergence in so few iterations.
