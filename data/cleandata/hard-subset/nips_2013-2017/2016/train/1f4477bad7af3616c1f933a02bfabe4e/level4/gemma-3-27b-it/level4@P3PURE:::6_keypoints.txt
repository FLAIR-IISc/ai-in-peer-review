- Paper presents a theoretically interesting training method for a restricted feed-forward network but falls short on demonstrating practical utility.
- Authors acknowledge limited expressiveness of the model; add discussion of workarounds and inherent limitations.
- Concurrent related work is not discussed or contrasted with the proposed method.
- Experimental results do not clarify the model’s applicability limits.
- Comparisons are incomplete — only SVMs are used; missing other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithmic baselines.
- Report computational resources and runtime for each compared algorithm.
- Section 2 lacks fluency and overall presentation could be improved.
- The function Φ is introduced without clear motivation.
- The rationale for the epsilon term should be added to the paper (it was only explained in the rebuttal).
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement to aid readability.
- The claim about rapid convergence (“typically in a few (less than 10) iterations”) is based on experiments and should be explicitly stated as an empirical observation.
- Typos/minor issues to fix: “polyomial” → “polynomial”; “one CAN model” → “one can model”; p1 and p2 are used before introduction; R_{++} is not defined.
