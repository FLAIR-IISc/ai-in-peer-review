- Paper shows a specific neural network has a single stationary point and provides an algorithm that converges to that unique fixed-point (global minimum).
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map to prove uniqueness of the minimizer, rather than convexity-based arguments.
- Demonstrates an intriguing result that a complex model with carefully chosen constraints can exhibit only one stationary point.
- Convergence proof depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, which is a significant limitation.
- Spectral-radius requirement restricts experiments to networks with a small number of hidden units.
- Method requires tuning many parameters, and the spectral condition must be checked for each parameter choice, making application cumbersome.
- Experimental protocol swept ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected models via cross-validation.
- Empirically, the proposed approach beat linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, calling into question the practical effectiveness of the network.
