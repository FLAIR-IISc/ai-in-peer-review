The paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The condition guaranteeing global optimality is dependent on the network architecture parameters and involves computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. The introduction of a nonlinear spectral method for training a particular class of feedforward neural networks is novel and intriguing. However, concerns arise regarding the limitations of this approach, primarily the requirement of non-negativity on the network weights. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and highly specific. These limitations may significantly hinder the performance of the neural networks. Indeed, the numerical experiments demonstrate subpar performance compared to SVM.

Minor comment: L.90-91: The statement "Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to..." may be overstated, as a linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply convergence in a few iterations.
