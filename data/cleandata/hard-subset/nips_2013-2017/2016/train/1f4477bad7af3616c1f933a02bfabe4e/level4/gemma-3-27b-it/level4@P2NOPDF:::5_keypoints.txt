- Introduces a feedforward network class with a provable globally optimal solution and an algorithm that converges linearly.  
- Mathematical exposition is largely strong and insightful but some derivations need clarification.  
- The move from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4 is unclear and needs justification.  
- The final layer is treated differently from hidden layers; gradient treatment for parameter vector u (and its vectorization) requires clearer explanation.  
- Bounding u within a ball constraint and related arguments appear inconsistent with the gradient/vectorization treatment.  
- Maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters (Eq. 2) is unconventional and lacks compelling practical motivation versus standard weight decay.  
- The maximization justification (to ensure positive gradients and membership in V_{++}) feels like theoretical convenience rather than a practical necessity.  
- It’s unclear whether the 1-norm maximization introduces any effectively free or tunable parameters.  
- Requiring non-negative data and strictly positive weights (no zeros) raises concerns about activation accumulation and restrictive behavior.  
- Practical applicability is questionable due to architectural constraints despite strong theoretical guarantees.  
- Experimental comparison to SVMs does not demonstrate advantages over common architectures (e.g., ReLU networks); clearer empirical benefits are needed.  
- Overall, an interesting theoretical contribution that falls short on demonstrating compelling practical relevance.
