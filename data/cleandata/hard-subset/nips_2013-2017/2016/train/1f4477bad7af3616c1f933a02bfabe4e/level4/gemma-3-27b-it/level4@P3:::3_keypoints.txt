- Presents a theoretical framework proving global optimality and linear convergence for training feedforward networks with non-negative inputs and weights using generalized polynomial activations.
- Theory developed for single-hidden-layer networks and extended to architectures with two hidden layers.
- Proposes an optimization algorithm based on a nonlinear spectral method.
- Empirical results on synthetic and UCI datasets show competitive performance on small-scale tasks.

Strengths:
- Provides a rare theoretical guarantee of global optimality for neural network training.
- Proves linear convergence rate, improving over SGD-like methods without such guarantees.
- Preconditions (non-negativity and a spectral radius check) are relatively simple to verify in practice.
- Extends theoretical results beyond one hidden layer, indicating broader applicability.
- Empirical validation offers initial proof-of-concept support.
- Novelly combines spectral methods with neural network optimization.

Weaknesses:
- Strong restrictions (non-negative weights and polynomial activations, distinct α per hidden unit) limit expressiveness and applicability.
- Scalability to high-dimensional data and deeper networks is questionable; required weight reductions may harm expressiveness.
- Experiments are limited to small datasets and do not demonstrate real-world scalability.
- Spectral radius bounds may be overly conservative, reducing practical usefulness.
- No ablation study to isolate effects of constraints like non-negativity.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Genuinely novel approach with solid theoretical foundations.
- Significant theoretical contribution via global optimality and linear convergence guarantees.
- Opens new directions for research into globally optimal training methods.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains concepts.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Practical applicability is limited by restrictive architectural and data constraints.
- Scalability concerns reduce potential long-term impact.

Overall assessment:
- Valuable and encouraging contribution despite limitations; theoretical guarantees and proof-of-concept results justify acceptance.

Minor comments:
- Request more analysis/plots quantifying how required weight reductions scale with input dimension and network size.
- Suggest discussion of how non-negativity constraints limit the class of learnable functions.

Recommendation:
- Accept.
