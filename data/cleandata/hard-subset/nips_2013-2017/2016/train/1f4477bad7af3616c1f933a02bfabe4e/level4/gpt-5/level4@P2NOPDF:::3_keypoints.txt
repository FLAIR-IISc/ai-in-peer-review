- Proposes a novel neural-network class with provable global optimum and linear convergence, unlike prior works with impractical conditions.  
- Conditions are relatively simple and verifiable, relying mainly on the spectral radius of a nonnegative parameter-derived matrix.  
- Theory is extended to arbitrary depth and supported by modest empirical results on small synthetic and UCI datasets.  
- Applicability is limited by requiring nonnegative inputs and weights, generalized polynomial activations, and a spectral-radius < 1.  
- Forcing nonnegative weights may substantially reduce model expressiveness.  
- Requiring distinct activation functions across hidden layers is unnatural and may hinder practical adoption.  
- Satisfying the spectral-radius constraint may force exponent adjustments and shrink linear weights, making activations nearly linear and limiting depth scalability.  
- Request clarification on how p1, p2, pw, pu, input dimensionality, and network size jointly affect the spectral radius ρ(A).  
- Ask how ρ(A) typically scales with input dimension and whether pw/pu must be reduced linearly, polynomially, or exponentially.  
- Recommend an empirical study plotting ρ(A) versus dimensionality and parameters to build intuition and practical guidance.  
- Overall: meaningful and novel contribution with verifiable guarantees; recommend acceptance despite restrictive assumptions.
