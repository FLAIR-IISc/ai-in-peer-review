- Paper introduces a feedforward NN class with provable unique global optimum and an algorithm with linear convergence, but several technical aspects are unclear.

- Why does Theorem 3 rely on first-order derivative inequalities while Theorem 4 appears to require second-order considerations—what necessitates moving to second derivatives?

- Is ∇_u Φ in GΦ (eq. (3)) taken with respect to a vectorized form of the hidden-layer matrix u, and are other gradients/constraints likewise applied to vectorized parameters?

- The architecture treats the output layer differently from hidden layers; clarity is needed on how parameters for different layers are represented and constrained.

- The objective includes a term that effectively maximizes the 1-norm of connection weights (via an arbitrarily small ε), which runs counter to usual regularization that shrinks norms.

- The small ε appears to be a technical device to force strictly positive gradients (in V++); its practical justification and impact on training are unclear.

- The claim of “no free parameters” is questionable given required data/model constraints (nonnegative inputs and strictly positive weights with no zeros).

- Requiring strictly positive weights implies activations continually accumulate—this modeling choice may be impractical; more discussion on feasibility is needed.

- Experiments compare only to SVM; authors should clarify practical benefits of this network family over common architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) and when one would prefer it.
