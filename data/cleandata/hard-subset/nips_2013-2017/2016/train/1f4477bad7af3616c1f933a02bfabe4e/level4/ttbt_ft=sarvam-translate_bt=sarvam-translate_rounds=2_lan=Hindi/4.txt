This research paper presents a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific type of feedforward neural network with a linear convergence rate. The condition guaranteeing global optimality depends on the parameters of the network's architecture and the computation of the spectral radius of a small non-negative matrix. Proposing a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific type of feedforward neural network seems novel and interesting. However, I am concerned about the limitations of this approach; it imposes a non-negativity condition on the network's weights. Furthermore, the selection of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and very specific. The limit seems too strong, which could hinder the neural network's good performance. In fact, numerical experiments do not show such good performance on SVM. Small note: L.90-91: "Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus generally converges quickly in a few (less than 10) iterations..." The sentence saying this seems exaggerated because a linear convergence rate does not necessarily mean a few iterations.
