- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of a specific class of feedforward networks with linear convergence.
- Global optimality condition depends on network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.
- Novel contribution: applying a nonlinear spectral approach to optimal network training.
- Major limitation: method requires all network weights to be nonnegative.
- Uses highly specific, non-standard activation functions, which may limit practical applicability.
- Numerical experiments reflect these restrictions, showing worse performance than SVM.
- Minor critique: stating that linear convergence implies convergence in under 10 iterations is overstated, since linear rates don't guarantee rapid convergence.
