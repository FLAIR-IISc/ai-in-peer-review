- Introduces a novel globally optimal training algorithm for a class of generalized polynomial neural networks using a nonlinear spectral method.  
- Claims linear convergence rate to the global optimum for the proposed network class.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical derivation and detailed convergence analysis.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains ideas and results.  
- Strength: offers a unique global optimizer for neural networks, addressing a long-standing problem.  
- Practicality concern: method requires non-negative input data and strictly positive weights, limiting applicability.  
- Empirical/architectural comparison is limited; more thorough comparisons with common architectures (e.g., ReLU) are needed.  
- Authors include some comparisons with SVM and ReLU networks, but these are not comprehensive.  
- Overall assessment: significant, original contribution with strong clarity and rigor.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, mathematical rigor, clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restricted practicality due to data/weight constraints and insufficient comparative evaluation.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.
