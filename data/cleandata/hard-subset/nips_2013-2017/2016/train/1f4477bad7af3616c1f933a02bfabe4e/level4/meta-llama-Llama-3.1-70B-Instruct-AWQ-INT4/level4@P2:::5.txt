The paper "Globally Optimal Training of Generalized Polynomial Neural Networks with Nonlinear Spectral Methods" presents a novel approach to training neural networks with a unique global optimizer. The authors propose a specific class of feedforward neural networks that can be trained globally optimal with a linear convergence rate using their nonlinear spectral method.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a globally optimal algorithm for training neural networks, which is a significant departure from traditional stochastic gradient descent methods. The authors provide a rigorous mathematical derivation of their approach, including a detailed analysis of the optimization problem and the convergence properties of their algorithm.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the main ideas and results. The authors also provide a thorough discussion of related work and the limitations of their approach.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unique global optimizer for neural networks, which is a long-standing problem in the field. The authors' approach is based on a nonlinear spectral method, which is a novel application of this technique to neural networks.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One concern is the practicality of the proposed neural network design, which requires all data to be non-negative and uses only positive weights. This may limit the applicability of the approach to certain types of problems.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other neural network architectures, such as those using ReLU activation functions. While the authors do provide some comparisons with SVM and ReLU networks, it would be helpful to see a more comprehensive analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of their approach.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of neural networks and provides a novel approach to training neural networks with a unique global optimizer. While there are some limitations to the approach, the paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the main ideas and results.
