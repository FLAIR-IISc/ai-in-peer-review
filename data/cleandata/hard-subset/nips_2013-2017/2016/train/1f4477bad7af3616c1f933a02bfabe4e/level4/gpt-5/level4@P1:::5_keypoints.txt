- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method (NLSM) that globally optimizes certain feedforward nets with generalized polynomial activations, nonnegative inputs, and strictly positive weights.  
- Recasts training as constrained maximization of a modified objective (loss + sum of outputs + small linear parameter term) and applies fixed-point theory on cones using the Thompson metric and Perron–Frobenius–type arguments.  
- Provides a simple, checkable spectral condition (ρ(A) < 1) guaranteeing linear convergence to a unique global maximizer.  
- Theorems are given for one- and two-hidden-layer networks and the approach could in principle extend to deeper nets.  
- Experiments on several UCI datasets show competitive accuracy vs ReLU networks but generally below RBF-SVM; training converges rapidly with no learning-rate tuning.  
- Connects to prior global-guarantee work (tensor methods, structured factorization) but avoids tensor score functions and uses nonnegative matrix / nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory.  
- Strength: clear theoretical contribution—fixed-point map GΦ with linear convergence and an interpretable, easily verifiable spectral condition.  
- Strength: analysis is technically interesting, bridging nonlinear eigenproblems and neural training.  
- Strength: empirical results show nontrivial decision boundaries and fast convergence without step-size tuning.  
- Weakness: unclear derivative-order exposition—Theorem 3 uses first-order bounds while Theorem 4 seems to require second derivatives; explanation of Hessian role is missing.  
- Weakness: unclear treatment/notation for u (vectorization and norms); need consistent statement whether gradients/norms use vec(u) and induced p-norms.  
- Weakness: modified objective adds sum of outputs and positive linear parameter term (with small ε) to ensure ∇Φ ∈ V++; potential bias toward large weights and unclear sensitivity to ε; alternatives (barrier/entropy) not discussed.  
- Weakness: claim of “no free parameter” is misleading—many design choices remain (n1,n2, α, β, ρw,ρu,ρv, pw,pu,pv) and pw/pu/pv need conservative lower bounds; guidance on selection is missing.  
- Weakness: nonnegativity and strictly positive weights restrict applicability vs modern signed-weight architectures; need discussion of domains where this inductive bias is natural.  
- Weakness: requirement αi ≠ αj for uniqueness introduces architectural asymmetry that may trade off expressivity/invariance.  
- Weakness: limited empirical scope—small UCI datasets, performance often below SVM, unclear scalability as hidden units increase (ρ(A) worsens).  
- Weakness: comparison protocol needs more detail on hyperparameter search budgets and computational cost (e.g., 100 random NLSM configs vs grid searches for baselines).  
- Quality: theoretical development is careful and appears sound but needs clearer exposition on derivatives and vectorization conventions.  
- Clarity: paper is dense but organized; requests to explicitly state vec(u)/p-norm choices, justify second-derivative needs, and specify practical magnitude/role of ε.  
- Originality: novel combination of nonlinear spectral methods with constrained polynomial networks for global optimality and linear convergence.  
- Significance: theoretically meaningful but practical impact depends on applicability under nonnegativity and modified-objective constraints; more domain-specific demonstrations needed.  
- Pro for acceptance: strong, novel theoretical guarantees with a simple verifiable condition and linear convergence; bridges important mathematical tools to NN training.  
- Con for acceptance: restrictive modeling assumptions, open theoretical/notation questions, and limited empirical validation where performance often lags SVM.  
- Key author questions requested: clarify second-derivative necessity (Theorem 4), confirm gradient/vectorization conventions for u, provide principled guidance for setting α/ρ/ p-values and ε sensitivity, better motivate practical benefit of added objective terms, and identify real-world settings where this architecture outperforms ReLU/SVM.
