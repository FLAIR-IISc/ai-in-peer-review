- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for training feed-forward networks with generalized polynomial activations and non-negative weights.
- Main theoretical contribution: provable linear convergence of an iterative method to the unique global optimum under checkable architectural conditions.
- Connects nonlinear eigenproblem and Perron–Frobenius theory to neural network training and offers a practical alternative to prior global-optimality methods that require distributional knowledge or tensor decompositions.
- Demonstrated for one- and two-hidden-layer networks and evaluated on several UCI datasets.
- Strength: novel and significant as a practical algorithm with global optimality guarantees for a non-trivial network class.
- Strength: solid theoretical grounding with a convergence proof and linear convergence rate.
- Strength: practical guarantees — conditions can be verified a priori and method avoids SGD learning-rate tuning.
- Weakness: limited practical utility due to restrictive model class (non-negativity constraints and specific polynomial activations) with unclear expressiveness.
- Weakness: no clear analysis of what the constrained model can or cannot represent compared to standard networks.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is preliminary — limited baselines (only RBF SVM and ReLU), unclear applicability boundaries, and insufficient task diversity.
- Weakness: missing discussion of computational costs (training time, memory) despite practical-feasibility claims.
- Weakness: presentation clarity issues from Section 2 onward; key constructs and motivations are underexplained.
- Weakness: objective Φ (Equation 2) and its ad-hoc additions (sum of outputs, ε term) are not sufficiently motivated.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 lacks preceding intuition or informal explanation, making it hard to parse.
- Weakness: claim of "converges very fast in only a few iterations" needs clarification on whether it’s theoretical or empirical.
- Suggestion: restructure Section 2 with a high-level overview and motivation before formalism and provide an informal explanation of Theorem 1.
- Suggestion: add a dedicated section analyzing the representation power and limitations imposed by non-negativity constraints.
- Suggestion: broaden experiments to include more baselines, scalability tests, computational-efficiency analysis, and sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., exponents α_l).
- Minor fixes: change "Up to our knowledge" → "To our knowledge", correct "polyomial" → "polynomial", and make the definition/use of ℝ_{++} more prominent.
