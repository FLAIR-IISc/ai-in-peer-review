- Paper studies a restricted class of feedforward nets (NLSM1/NLSM2) trained to global optimality via a nonlinear spectral method and evaluated on real datasets.

- Model class is highly constrained (nonnegative weights, maximized regularization, and nonstandard generalized-polynomial activations), which is hard to justify for typical practical applications.

- Experiments do not convincingly support expressive-power claims: a (possibly untuned) linear SVM sometimes outperforms NLSM, and using only a linear baseline is inadequate for a nonlinear method.

- Missing fair nonlinear baselines: should include well-tuned nonlinear SVMs (polynomial/RBF) and comparable hyperparameter tuning across methods.

- Empirical evaluation is limited to small UCI datasets, leaving scalability and performance on high-dimensional, large-scale tasks untested.

- Spectral-radius bounds grow with hidden-unit count, suggesting potential scalability issues for larger networks; need mitigation strategies (alternative parameterizations, tighter bounds, architectural constraints).

- The optimality condition is not guaranteed across parameter space and is impractical to check a priori in large models; request for fast screening or initialization strategies to find feasible candidates.

- Need clarification whether global optimality stems from the special objective, the architectural constraints, or both, and experiments to see if standard optimizers (e.g., SGD) can reach the same solutions.

- Recommendations: provide application-driven motivation for constraints, add fair nonlinear baselines, evaluate larger/higher-dimensional datasets, address spectral-radius and feasibility-check issues, and compare to standard training methods.
