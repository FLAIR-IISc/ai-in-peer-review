- Presents theory showing a class of neural networks has a global optimum reachable with linear convergence under positivity and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Theory proven for single-hidden-layer networks and extended to deeper networks; authors propose a nonlinear spectral method optimizer.  
- Experiments show strong performance on a 2D synthetic dataset and reasonable results on small UCI datasets.  
- Major pro: first potentially practical neural network with both optimality and convergence guarantees.  
- Pro: Preconditions are simple and depend only on the spectral radius of a nonnegative parameter matrix, easier to check than prior data-dependent conditions.  
- Major con: Requires inputs and linear-layer weights to be nonnegative and activations to be generalized polynomials, limiting applicability.  
- Con: Positivity constraint on weights may reduce model expressiveness; requiring all hidden-layer activations to be different is unnatural.  
- Con: Satisfying the spectral-radius constraint forces adjusting p1,p2,pw,pu which effectively decreases linear weights and can make activations nearly linear.  
- Concern: When linear units become very small, added layers are effectively linear compositions, so method may not extend well to deep architectures.  
- Minor comment: Recommend empirical study of how p1,p2,pw,pu, input dimension, and network size affect rho(A) (e.g., linear vs. exponential scaling).  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, mathematically consistent, with clear experiments and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Overall verdict: Technically sound, original, and potentially significant; reviewer supports acceptance.
