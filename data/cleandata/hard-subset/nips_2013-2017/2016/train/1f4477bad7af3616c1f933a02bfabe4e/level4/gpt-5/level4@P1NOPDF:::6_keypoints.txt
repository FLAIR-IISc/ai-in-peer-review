- The paper proposes a method with solid theoretical convergence guarantees for a restricted class of feedforward networks and shows some real-world experiments.  
- The practical usefulness of the restricted model is unclear; the expressiveness of the model needs explicit discussion.  
- Clearly delineate what the model can and cannot represent and which task families are suitable versus out of reach.  
- Discuss and contrast related and concurrent approaches, including alternative NN training methods and non-NN algorithmic paradigms.  
- Experiments are weak: comparisons are limited to a few SVM baselines and omit other neural-network training methods and strong non-NN baselines.  
- Provide computational-resource comparisons (wall-clock time, iteration counts, memory) for the evaluated algorithms to assess real-world utility.  
- Exposition needs improvement—Section 2 is difficult to follow and should be clarified.  
- Better motivate the function Φ and explicitly include the role and motivation of the ε term (as explained in the rebuttal).  
- Precede Theorem 1 with an informal explanation and intuition to make the statement easier to parse.  
- Distinguish empirical observations from formal guarantees: the “converges quickly (typically <10 iterations)” claim should be presented as experimental, not theoretical, evidence.  
- Minor typos and notation issues: fix “polyomial”→“polynomial” and “one CAN model”→“one can model.”  
- Define p1 and p2 at first use and explicitly define R_{++} since other variables depend on it.
