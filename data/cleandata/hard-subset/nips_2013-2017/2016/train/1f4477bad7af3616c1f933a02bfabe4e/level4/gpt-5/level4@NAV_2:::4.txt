This paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method that yields globally optimal training for a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The global optimality condition depends solely on architectural parameters and reduces to checking the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. The idea of using a nonlinear spectral approach to obtain globally optimal training for such networks appears novel and interesting.

However, I am concerned about the scope of the method. It enforces nonnegativity on the network weights, and the activation functions are unconventional and highly specialized. These constraints seem quite strong and may limit the practical effectiveness of the networks. In line with this, the empirical results generally do not surpass SVM baselines.

Minor comment: L.90–91: The statement that a linear convergence rate typically yields convergence in a few (fewer than 10) iterations appears overstated; linear convergence does not, by itself, guarantee convergence in so few iterations.
