- Paper proposes a theory guaranteeing existence of a global optimum and linear convergence for a class of neural networks under specific verifiable conditions (nonnegative inputs/weights, generalized polynomial activations, spectral-radius < 1) and extends results from single to multiple hidden layers with a nonlinear spectral optimization algorithm.

- Strength: First practically implementable NN framework (to reviewer’s knowledge) offering both global optimality and linear convergence guarantees under checkable conditions.

- Strength: Main precondition reduces to a spectral-radius check of a nonnegative matrix, which is simpler and more practical than many prior data-distribution assumptions.

- Strength: Clear theoretical development—single-layer analysis followed by extension to arbitrary depth and an associated optimization method.

- Strength: Empirical results on synthetic and small UCI datasets support practical viability within the method’s intended scope.

- Limitation: Applicability is restrictive due to requirements: nonnegative inputs, nonnegative linear weights, generalized polynomial activations, and the spectral-radius constraint.

- Limitation: Nonnegativity of weights may reduce expressiveness compared to unconstrained networks.

- Limitation: Requirement that every hidden layer use a different activation (if necessary) is unnatural and may further restrict applicability.

- Limitation: Enforcing ρ(A) < 1 may force very small linear weights as dimension grows, making deep networks effectively linear and limiting scalability.

- Limitation: Experimental scope is modest; larger or higher-dimensional experiments are needed to clarify practical limits imposed by constraints.

- Clarification requested: Is using different generalized polynomial activations across hidden layers strictly required by the proofs, or can identical activations be used?

- Clarification requested: Provide more intuition on how the spectral-radius matrix A is constructed and how its entries relate to hyperparameters (p1,p2,pw,pu), input dimensionality, and network width.

- Empirical request: Characterize how ρ(A) scales with input dimension and network width (linear/polynomial/exponential) and how pw/pu should be adjusted to maintain ρ(A) < 1.

- Clarification requested: Can positivity constraints be relaxed (e.g., factorizing weights as differences of nonnegative matrices) while retaining some guarantees?

- Clarification requested: How sensitive is the method to choices of generalized polynomial degrees and coefficients, and are there practical guidelines for selecting p1,p2,pw,pu?

- Suggestions: Expand empirical analysis with ablations on p1,p2,pw,pu, depth-scaling experiments, and comparisons to strong baselines under similar constraints.

- Suggestions: Discuss practical procedures for verifying/enforcing ρ(A) < 1 during training (projections, adaptive rescaling) and their computational cost.

- Suggestions: Consider alternative generalized-polynomial activations that might improve expressiveness under the same constraints.

- Overall assessment: Rigorous and thoughtful work that advances theoretical understanding and offers promising initial practical evidence despite expressiveness and depth-scalability limitations; recommend acceptance with requested clarifications and empirical characterizations.
