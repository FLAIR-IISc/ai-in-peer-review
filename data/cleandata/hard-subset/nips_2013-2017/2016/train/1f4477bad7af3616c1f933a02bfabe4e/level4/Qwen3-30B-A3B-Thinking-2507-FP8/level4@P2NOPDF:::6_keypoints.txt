- Introduces a theoretically grounded method for training a restricted class of feed-forward networks, proving optimal convergence rates and showing some real-world applicability.  
- Strong theoretical contribution, but practical utility is not convincingly established.  
- Authors note expressiveness limitations twice but do not offer workable solutions or clearly delineate inherent boundaries, weakening adoption versus more expressive models.  
- Experimental comparisons are inadequate—only SVMs are used; missing baselines like SGD/backprop and other neural-network training methods.  
- Experiments omit computational cost analysis (runtime, memory, hardware), leaving practical feasibility unclear.  
- Section 2 has poor flow, technical inconsistencies, undefined notation (e.g., R_{++}, p1, p2), and typos/grammatical errors.  
- Loss function is defined but the role/motivation of Φ is not explained; ε’s role must be incorporated into the main text.  
- Theorem 1 is hard to parse and needs an informal explanation preceding the formal statement/proof.  
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly” is empirical and should be explicitly qualified as such.  
- Recommendations: address expressiveness limits with practical workarounds, expand experimental baselines and report resource usage, define all notation and clarify ε, and improve Section 2’s clarity and correctness.  
- Overall: promising theoretical insight but insufficient justification of practical relevance or distinction from existing approaches; presently of mainly theoretical interest.
