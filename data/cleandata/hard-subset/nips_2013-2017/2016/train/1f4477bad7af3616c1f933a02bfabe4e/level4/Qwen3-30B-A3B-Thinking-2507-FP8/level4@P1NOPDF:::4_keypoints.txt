- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for a specific class of feedforward networks with provable global convergence and linear rate.  
- Theoretical reduction: global optimality depends only on architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- Technically interesting and a meaningful theoretical advance for the restricted network class.  
- Major limitation: requires strict non-negativity on all weights, likely severely restricting applicability and expressive power.  
- Major limitation: uses highly specialized, non-standard activation functions, limiting practical utility beyond the narrow analysis scope.  
- Empirically modest: experiments show underperformance relative to SVM baselines, suggesting constraints harm competitiveness.  
- Minor issue: claim that linear convergence implies convergence “typically in a few (<10) iterations” is overstated and should be tempered.  
- Overall: sound theory for a restricted setting, but stringent architectural constraints and weak empirical results hinder broader adoption; discussion on relaxing these limitations is needed.
