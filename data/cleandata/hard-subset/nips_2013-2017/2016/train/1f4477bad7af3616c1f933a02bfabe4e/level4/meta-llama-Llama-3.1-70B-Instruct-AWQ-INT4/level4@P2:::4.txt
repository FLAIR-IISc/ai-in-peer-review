This paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of a particular class of feedforward neural networks. The method's guarantee of global optimality relies on a condition that depends on the network architecture and involves computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This approach appears to be novel and intriguing.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method through experiments on several UCI datasets, comparing its performance to that of nonlinear RBF-kernel SVM and Rectified-Linear Unit networks. While the results show that the nonlinear spectral method is competitive with ReLU networks, it performs slightly worse than kernel SVM.

The paper's main contribution is the development of a globally optimal training method for a specific class of neural networks. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the method's properties, including its convergence rate and the conditions under which it guarantees global optimality.

However, I have some concerns regarding the limitations of the approach. The method imposes a non-negativity condition on the weights of the network, which may restrict its applicability. Additionally, the choice of activation functions is non-standard and very specific, which may limit the method's performance.

The experimental results, while encouraging, also raise some concerns. The performance of the nonlinear spectral method is not significantly better than that of ReLU networks, and it is outperformed by kernel SVM. This may indicate that the method's limitations, such as the non-negativity condition on the weights, may hinder its ability to achieve good performance.

Minor comment: The statement on lines 90-91, "Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to...", seems exaggerated. A linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply a small number of iterations.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper introduces a novel and intriguing method for globally optimal training of a particular class of feedforward neural networks.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the method's properties, including its convergence rate and the conditions under which it guarantees global optimality.
* The experimental results show that the nonlinear spectral method is competitive with ReLU networks.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The method imposes a non-negativity condition on the weights of the network, which may restrict its applicability.
* The choice of activation functions is non-standard and very specific, which may limit the method's performance.
* The experimental results show that the nonlinear spectral method performs slightly worse than kernel SVM and is not significantly better than ReLU networks.

Overall, I think the paper has some interesting ideas and contributions, but it also has some limitations that need to be addressed. With some revisions to address these concerns, the paper could be more compelling.
