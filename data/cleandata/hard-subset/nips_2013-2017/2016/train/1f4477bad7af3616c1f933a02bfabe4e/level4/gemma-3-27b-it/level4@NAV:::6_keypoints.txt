- Introduces a training method for a restricted class of feed-forward networks with theoretical guarantees of optimal convergence.
- The problem area and theoretical contribution are compelling and valuable.
- Practical utility of the restricted neural network class is not fully established.
- Authors acknowledge expressiveness limitations; discussion should be expanded to include workarounds and inherent limits.
- Paper needs a more thorough comparison with concurrent and alternative methods to contextualize contributions.
- Experimental evaluation does not clearly delineate the model’s applicability boundaries.
- Comparisons focus mainly on SVMs and omit other neural training techniques and alternate algorithms.
- Report computational resource requirements for each algorithm to strengthen experimental analysis.
- Overall presentation needs refinement; Introduction is good but Section 2 lacks clarity and flow.
- Several typographical errors and undefined/unclearly introduced variables (e.g., p1, p2, R_{++}) need correction or definition.
- Motivation behind the function Φ is unclear and should be clarified in the text.
- The rebuttal’s explanation of the epsilon term should be incorporated into the paper.
- Theorem 1 should be preceded by an informal explanation for accessibility.
- The claim about rapid convergence to the global maximum is based on experiments and should be explicitly labeled as empirical.
