- Paper proposes a class of feedforward NNs with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm that attains it with linear convergence; mathematical development is informative but parts are unclear.

- Why does Theorem 4 require second-order derivatives when Theorem 3 uses only first-order conditions — what necessitates the jump to second-order analysis?

- The output-layer parameters appear treated differently from hidden-layer parameters: is the gradient w.r.t. u in G^Φ(3) meant as the gradient w.r.t. vec(u)? How are parameterizations and constraints (e.g., u in a ball) represented precisely?

- Why does formulation (2) maximize the 1-norm of connection parameters (versus the usual penalization/weight decay)? The small ε forcing strictly positive gradients looks like a proof device — is there any free parameter and is this design practical?

- The model requires non-negative inputs and enforces strictly positive (nonzero) weights so contributions only add — how realistic and useful is this constraint for practical networks?

- Experiments compare to SVM; in what practical respects does this NN offer advantages over standard architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) that practitioners use?
