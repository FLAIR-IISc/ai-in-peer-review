This paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method that achieves globally optimal training for a specific subclass of feedforward neural networks, with a proven linear rate of convergence. The condition for guaranteeing global optimality is tied to the network's architectural parameters and reduces to the calculation of the spectral radius of a small, non-negative matrix. The application of a nonlinear spectral method to optimally train this class of feedforward networks is a novel and interesting contribution.

Despite its novelty, I have significant reservations about the approach's practical limitations. Specifically, the method imposes a non-negativity constraint on all network weights and relies on a highly specific and non-standard set of activation functions. These are severe constraints that can be expected to significantly hinder the model's practical performance. This concern appears to be validated by the numerical experiments, which report inferior performance compared to a standard SVM baseline.

Minor comment: The claim on L.90-91 that "the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations" seems overstated. A linear convergence rate does not inherently imply rapid convergence in just a few steps, as the actual speed is also determined by the convergence factor.
