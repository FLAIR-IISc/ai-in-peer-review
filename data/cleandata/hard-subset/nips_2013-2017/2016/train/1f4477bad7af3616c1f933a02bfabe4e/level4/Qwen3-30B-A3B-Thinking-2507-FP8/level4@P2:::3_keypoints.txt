- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral framework that yields globally optimal training for certain feedforward networks under mild assumptions.
- Proves global optimality and linear convergence when the spectral radius of a derived matrix A is < 1, with the condition computable before running the algorithm.
- Requires nonnegative data and weights and generalized polynomial activations, with distinct activation exponents across hidden units.
- Builds on tensor and structured matrix factorization work but replaces data-generating assumptions with a practical spectral radius check.
- Main contribution: a theoretically sound, implementable algorithm transforming training into a nonlinear eigenvalue problem on convex cones using Perron–Frobenius theory.
- Strength (Quality): Rigorous mathematical derivations and comprehensive proofs of global convergence and optimality.
- Strength (Originality): First practically feasible method claiming provable global optimality for neural network training without data-measure assumptions.
- Strength (Significance): Addresses lack of global guarantees in deep learning, offering linear convergence and reduced hyperparameter tuning compared to SGD.
- Strength (Clarity): Well-organized presentation, clear notation, and thorough supplementary technical details.
- Weakness (Practicality): Nonnegativity constraints on data and weights and distinct activation exponents restrict model expressiveness and applicability.
- Weakness (Generality): Limitation to generalized polynomial activations reduces relevance to standard neural networks.
- Weakness (Empirical impact): Competitive but not superior empirical performance on UCI datasets; theoretical gains not clearly reflected in practice.
- Weakness (Scalability): Spectral radius constraint may become more restrictive for deeper networks, limiting scalability to complex tasks.
- Weakness (Exposition): Insufficient practical discussion on how spectral radius depends on parameters (p_w, p_u, ρ_w, ρ_u) and how to tune them.
- Pros for acceptance: First practical globally optimal training method, rigorous theory, simple verifiable spectral condition, applicability to multi-layer networks, and supportive experiments.
- Cons for rejection: Strong nonnegativity and activation restrictions, unnatural requirement for distinct exponents, limited empirical advantage, and potential depth-related scalability issues.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong theoretical contribution with rigorous proofs and reasonable empirical validation, despite practical constraints that motivate future work.
