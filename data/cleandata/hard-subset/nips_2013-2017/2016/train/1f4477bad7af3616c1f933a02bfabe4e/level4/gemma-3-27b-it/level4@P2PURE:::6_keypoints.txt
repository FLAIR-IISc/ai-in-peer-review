- Introduces a training method for a restricted class of feed-forward networks with a proof of optimal convergence and demonstrations on real-world tasks.  
- The problem is interesting and the theoretical contribution is valuable.  
- Practical utility of the proposed architecture is not fully established.  
- Authors repeatedly acknowledge limited expressiveness of the restricted model; include discussion of workarounds and inherent constraints.  
- Need a more thorough comparison with concurrent work and existing alternatives.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient to address the model’s applicability limits.  
- Comparisons focus mainly on SVMs and omit other relevant methods, alternative neural-network training techniques, and different algorithmic paradigms.  
- Report computational resource requirements (runtime, hardware) for each algorithm.  
- Introduction is well-written, but Section 2 lacks clarity and fluency.  
- Several typos and wording errors need correction.  
- The loss function is clear, but the function Φ is not sufficiently motivated; incorporate the rebuttal’s explanation of the ε term.  
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement to improve readability.  
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “typically converges quickly” is based on experiments and should be explicitly stated as empirical observation.  
- Specific errors: “polyomial”→“polynomial”; “one CAN model”→“one can model”; p1 and p2 are undefined; R_{++} is not defined and should be clarified.
