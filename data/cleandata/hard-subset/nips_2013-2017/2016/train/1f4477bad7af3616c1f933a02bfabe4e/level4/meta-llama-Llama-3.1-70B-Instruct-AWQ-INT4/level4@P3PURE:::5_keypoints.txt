- Proposes a feedforward neural network with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm that finds it with linear convergence; math is instructive but some parts are unclear.
- Abrupt transition from Theorem 3 (first-order derivatives) to Theorem 4 (second-order derivatives) with unclear motivation for the shift in derivative level.
- Network design treats the last layer separately from hidden layers; gradient w.r.t. u uses vectorization and bounding u in a ball raises questions about consistency and interpretation.
- Objective maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters and introduces a tiny epsilon to force positive gradients—seems driven by proof needs and at odds with common weight-decay practices.
- Requires non-negative data and strictly positive weights (no zeros), which limits practical applicability and model expressiveness.
- No regularization or decay and apparently no free hyperparameters may be a practical limitation.
- Experimental comparison only against SVM leaves unclear whether the approach offers advantages over standard neural architectures (e.g., ReLU networks).
