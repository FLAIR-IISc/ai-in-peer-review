The paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The condition that guarantees global optimality is dependent on the network's architecture parameters and can be determined by computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This approach is novel and intriguing, as it offers a new perspective on training neural networks.

However, I have concerns regarding the limitations of this method. The requirement of non-negativity on the network's weights may be too restrictive, potentially hindering the performance of the neural network. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions is non-standard and very specific, which may limit the applicability of this approach. These limitations seem significant, and the numerical experiments support this concern, showing that the method's performance is not as good as that of Support Vector Machines (SVM).

In terms of presentation, I would like to point out that the statement in lines 90-91, "Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to...", may be overstated. A linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply convergence in a few iterations. This claim should be tempered to more accurately reflect the method's performance.

Overall, while the proposed nonlinear spectral method is an interesting contribution, its limitations and restrictive requirements may impact its practicality and effectiveness. Further investigation is needed to fully understand the potential of this approach and its applicability to real-world problems.
