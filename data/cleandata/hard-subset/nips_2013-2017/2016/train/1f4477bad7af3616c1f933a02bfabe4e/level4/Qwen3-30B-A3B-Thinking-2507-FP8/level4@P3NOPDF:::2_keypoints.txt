1. Proposes NLSM for a constrained class of feedforward nets with global optimality and linear convergence, but practical relevance is doubtful.  
2. Restrictive assumptions: non-negative weights and specialized generalized polynomial activations seem counterintuitive and application-agnostic.  
3. Paper fails to justify where these constraints arise in real-world systems or provide concrete application examples.  
4. Claim that the constrained class retains expressive power is unsupported by empirical evidence.  
5. Experimental baseline choice is flawed: compares NLSM (nonlinear) only to linear SVM instead of appropriate nonlinear baselines (e.g., RBF/polynomial SVM).  
6. Reported results (e.g., Wine dataset) show linear SVM outperforming NLSM, undermining expressive-power claims.  
7. Datasets used are too small and low-dimensional to assess scalability or the impact of spectral radius growth with hidden units.  
8. No discussion or experiments addressing feasibility on high-dimensional data (images, text) or mitigation strategies (regularization/architectural constraints).  
9. Parameter search is problematic: spectral radius constraint must be verified before training, with no guidance on efficient feasible-region identification.  
10. Unclear whether global optimality arises from objective structure or imposed constraints; SGD behavior on this model is not investigated.  
11. Authors overstate applicability; work is mainly theoretical without evidence of practical advantage over standard nonlinear models.  
12. Recommended revisions: justify constraints with real applications, use nonlinear baselines, test larger datasets, provide efficient parameter-validation methods, and study SGD on the model.
