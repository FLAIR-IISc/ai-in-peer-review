- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training a class of feedforward neural networks that achieves global optimality with a linear convergence rate.  
- Contribution is novel, interesting, and practically feasible under weak data assumptions.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations and good relation to prior work.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of the optimization problem and properties of the nonlinear spectral method.  
- Strengths: novelty, clarity, global optimality, and linear convergence.  
- Main limitation: requires non-negativity constraints on network weights, which may hinder performance.  
- Limitation: uses non-standard and very specific activation functions, limiting applicability.  
- Numerical experiments show competitive performance with some methods but are worse than SVM, raising practical effectiveness concerns.  
- Claim that the method typically converges in a few (<10) iterations is likely exaggerated given linear convergence alone.  
- Rates well on quality, clarity, and originality under NIPS guidelines; overall significance limited by strong architectural and activation assumptions.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should acknowledge limitations and potential performance impact.
