- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for optimal training of a specific class of feedforward neural networks with provable linear convergence.  
- Global optimality condition is characterized by the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix determined by the network architecture.  
- Potentially significant contribution by applying nonlinear spectral techniques to neural network training.  
- Major limitation: requires nonnegative weights, which restricts model expressiveness and applicability.  
- Further limitation: relies on highly specialized, non-standard activation functions, narrowing practical use.  
- Empirical results show inferior performance to Support Vector Machines, raising practical concerns.  
- Minor critique: claim of convergence “in a few (less than 10) iterations” is overstated—linear convergence does not guarantee rapid iteration count.
