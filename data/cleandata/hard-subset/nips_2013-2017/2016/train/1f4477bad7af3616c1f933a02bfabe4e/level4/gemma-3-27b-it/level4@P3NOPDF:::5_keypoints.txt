- Introduces a novel feedforward neural network class with a provable unique global optimizer and an algorithm that achieves linear convergence — strong theoretical contribution.  
- Concern that the restrictive constraints required for the guarantees may limit practical benefits beyond the theoretical proof.  
- Unclear justification for the shift from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4; needs explanation.  
- Ambiguity in layer treatment: final layer appears handled differently from hidden layers, and the gradient w.r.t. 'u' seems to vectorize parameters, suggesting a mismatch with standard NN parameterization.  
- The objective maximizes the 1-norm of connection weights (forcing strictly positive weights) rather than the usual 1-norm minimization, which appears proof-driven and may introduce bias and prohibit sparsity.  
- Requiring strictly positive weights and non-negative inputs is a strong constraint that reduces applicability to real datasets and may cause activation accumulation, instability, or saturation.  
- Experimental comparison to SVMs is insufficient; missing comparisons to standard neural architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) on accuracy, efficiency, robustness, and generalization.  
- Need to address sensitivity to “free” parameters: demonstrate the solution’s stability under small data or hyperparameter perturbations.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting but strong assumptions hinder practicality and generalizability; more justification and broader empirical evaluation are required.
