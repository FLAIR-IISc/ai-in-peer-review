The authors introduce a nonlinear spectral method that achieves optimal training for a specific class of feedforward neural networks, characterized by a linear convergence rate. The condition ensuring global optimality is contingent upon the network architecture parameters and can be determined by computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. The proposal of a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a particular class of feedforward neural networks appears to be a novel and intriguing contribution. However, concerns arise regarding the limitations of this approach, primarily the requirement of non-negativity for the network weights. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is unconventional and highly specific. This limitation may significantly impact the performance of the neural networks, as evidenced by the subpar performance compared to SVM in numerical experiments. A minor note: the statement on L.90-91, suggesting that the nonlinear spectral method converges rapidly within a few iterations (less than 10) due to its linear convergence rate, may be overstated, as linear convergence does not necessarily imply rapid convergence within a fixed number of iterations.
