This paper proposes a method for training a specific class of feed-forward neural networks under certain assumptions. The authors provide a theoretical analysis with proofs of optimal convergence and demonstrate the method's applicability on several real-world problems.

The problem addressed is significant, and theoretical advances in this area are welcome. However, the paper does not sufficiently convince the reader of the practical utility of the proposed restricted class of neural networks. Specifically, the authors mention the expressiveness of their model but do not provide a thorough discussion of its capabilities and limitations. A more detailed analysis of what functions this architecture can and cannot represent would significantly strengthen the paper. Furthermore, the work would benefit from a discussion and comparison with concurrent approaches.

The experimental evaluation is another area that needs improvement. The results do not sufficiently explore the boundaries of the model's applicability. The comparison is limited to SVMs, omitting important baselines such as other neural network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms. Additionally, the paper lacks crucial information about the computational costs (e.g., training time, memory usage) of the proposed method compared to the baselines.

Regarding the presentation, the introduction is well-written, but the clarity declines in subsequent sections. Section 2, in particular, could be revised for better readability. I have several specific suggestions for improving clarity:
*   While the loss function is straightforward, the motivation for the function $\Phi$ is unclear. The role of the epsilon term, which was clarified in the author rebuttal, should be explicitly explained in the main text.
*   Theorem 1 is difficult to parse. It would be highly beneficial to provide an informal, high-level explanation of the theorem's statement and implications before presenting the formal version.
*   The claim following Theorem 1, stating that the nonlinear spectral method "converges quickly... in a few... iterations," is presented as a general fact. As clarified in the rebuttal, this is an empirical observation. The text should be updated to reflect that this is based on the authors' experiments rather than a theoretical guarantee.

Finally, there are several minor typos and notational issues:
*   Line 60: "polyomial" should be "polynomial".
*   Line 67: The phrasing "one CAN model" is awkward.
*   Line 67: Variables p1 and p2 are used without being introduced.
*   Line 67: The notation R_{++} is not defined, which causes confusion as other definitions rely on it. This should be defined for clarity.
