- Proposes a training method with theoretical convergence guarantees for a restricted class of feed-forward networks but practical utility is unclear.
- Major concern about the expressiveness of the proposed model; authors should better state strengths, weaknesses, and problem classes outside scope.
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion of related and concurrent work and clear contrasts with alternative algorithmic paradigms.
- Experimental comparison is too narrow (only against SVMs); should include other neural-network training methods and related algorithms.
- Missing evaluation of computational resources (training time, memory), which is necessary to judge practicality.
- Writing quality is uneven: strong introduction but Section 2 needs restructuring and overall presentation requires improvement.
- Multiple typos and notational inconsistencies undermine clarity and should be corrected.
- Motivation for the central function Φ is insufficient; the rebuttal’s epsilon-term explanation should be incorporated into the paper.
- Theorem 1 needs a preceding informal explanation to convey its core idea before the dense formal statement.
- The claim about quick convergence of the nonlinear spectral method appears empirical; it should be explicitly stated as such and supported by citations or analysis.
- Notational issues to fix: “polyomial” → “polynomial,” “one CAN model” → “one can model,” introduce p1/p2 before use, and define R++ used throughout.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on substantial revisions addressing expressiveness, comparisons, experiments, computational costs, and clarity.
