The paper proposes a method to train a specific type of feed-forward neural networks under certain assumptions, proving optimal convergence and demonstrating its applicability to a selection of "real-world" problems. The problem under investigation is interesting, and any theoretical advance in this direction is appreciated.

However, the paper falls short in convincing the reader that the proposed class of neural networks is useful in practice. The authors raise the issue of the expressiveness of their restricted model, but they do not provide sufficient thoughts on what can be done and what is out of reach for their approach. A discussion on concurrent approaches and their comparison with the proposed method would be beneficial. The experimental results do not clarify the limitations of applicability of their model either.

When reading the experimental results, it is surprising that the authors only compare their approach to some SVM and ignore other methods, both other training methods for some neural networks and other algorithmic paradigms. Information about the computational resources demanded by the three algorithms would also be helpful.

The introduction is well-written, but the presentation has some room for improvement. Section 2 does not read fluently, and there are some typos. Some specific remarks include:

* The definition of the loss function is straightforward, but the function Φ lacks motivation. The role of the epsilon term was explained in the rebuttal, and it would be beneficial to add this explanation to the paper.
* Theorem 1 should be explained informally before being stated. The statement itself is hard to parse.
* The statement that "the nonlinear spectral method [...] converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to the global maximum" should be clarified. As explained in the rebuttal, this statement stems from experimental observations.

Minor errors include:

* 60: "polyomial" should be "polynomial"
* 67: "one CAN model" is unclear
* 67: p1 and p2 are not introduced
* 67: R_{++} is not introduced, which is unfortunate since there are several other variables whose definition points to that one.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel method for training a specific type of feed-forward neural networks.
* The authors provide a theoretical analysis of their method, proving optimal convergence.
* The experimental results demonstrate the applicability of their method to real-world problems.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper does not convincingly demonstrate the usefulness of the proposed class of neural networks in practice.
* The experimental results are limited and do not provide a comprehensive comparison with other methods.
* The presentation has some room for improvement, including minor errors and unclear explanations.

Overall, while the paper proposes an interesting method and provides a theoretical analysis, it falls short in demonstrating the practical applicability of their approach. With some revisions to address these concerns, the paper could be more convincing and effective in presenting its contributions.
