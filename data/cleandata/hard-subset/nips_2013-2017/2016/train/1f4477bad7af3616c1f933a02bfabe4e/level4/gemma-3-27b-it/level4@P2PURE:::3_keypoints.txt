- Main contribution: provable global optimality and linear convergence for a class of neural networks under positivity constraints and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Theory established for single-hidden-layer networks and extended to arbitrarily deep networks.  
- Algorithmic contribution: a novel optimization method based on a nonlinear spectral method.  
- Experiments: effective results on a small synthetic 2D dataset and reasonable performance on several small UCI datasets.  
- Strength: appears to be the first practical neural network architecture with provable optimality and convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: theory uses simple, checkable preconditions based on the spectral radius of a nonnegative parameter matrix, unlike prior intractable precondition checks.  
- Strength: demonstrates applicability to networks of arbitrary depth and validates on small real-world datasets.  
- Weakness: limited applicability due to requirements of positive input data, positive network parameters, and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Weakness: needing a different activation function per hidden layer is unnatural and may limit practicality.  
- Weakness: the spectral radius <1 constraint forces tuning of p1,p2,pw,pu that effectively reduces linear-unit weights (pw,pu), potentially harming expressiveness.  
- Weakness: scaling concern — as input dimensionality or network size grows, pw and pu must shrink proportionally, making activations near-linear and reducing benefit from added layers.  
- Minor request: clarify how rho(A) scales with input dimension and model size (linearly or exponentially) and whether pw/pu must decrease linearly or exponentially to compensate.  
- Suggestion: include an empirical plot on synthetic data illustrating the relationship between p1,p2,pw,pu, input dimension, model size, and rho(A).  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
