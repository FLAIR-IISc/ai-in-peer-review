1. Paper proposes a class of feedforward nets with a unique global optimizer and a linearly convergent algorithm; math is instructive but needs clarifications.  
2. Unclear rationale for moving from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4.  
3. Final layer is treated differently from hidden layers; gradient w.r.t. matrix u seems defined via vectorization and constraints/bounding of u (e.g., in Theorem 4 proof) are ambiguous.  
4. Objective maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters, which contradicts standard weight-decay practices and appears done solely to ensure strictly positive gradients via an arbitrary ε.  
5. Design choices imply impractical requirements—non-negative inputs, strictly positive (no-zero) weights, and potential unbounded weight accumulation—and the lack of free parameters is unexplained.  
6. Experimental comparison to SVMs is superficial and does not demonstrate practical advantages over standard neural network architectures (e.g., ReLU networks).
