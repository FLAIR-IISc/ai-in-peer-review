- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of a specific class of feedforward networks with linear convergence and verifiable global optimality under mild (non-negative data) conditions.  
- Claims practical feasibility as the first method of its kind, checking guarantees via the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- Builds on prior global optimization work but is more implementable than [8] and avoids the tensor computation required by [11].  
- Strength: provides rigorous global optimality guarantees.  
- Strength: linear convergence rate enables theoretically efficient training.  
- Strength: optimality conditions are easily verifiable through spectral radius computation.  
- Strength: shows reasonable empirical performance on several UCI datasets.  
- Weakness: requires non-negative weights, greatly limiting applicability to most real-world problems.  
- Weakness: uses non-standard generalized polynomial activations with specific exponents, reducing practical suitability.  
- Weakness: experimental performance is slightly worse than kernel SVM on most datasets, raising practical-utility concerns.  
- Weakness: statement that the method "converges quickly in a few (<10) iterations" is misleading because linear convergence does not guarantee few iterations.  
- Recommendation: accept for strong theoretical contribution, but emphasize limited practical utility and contexts where non-negativity holds.
