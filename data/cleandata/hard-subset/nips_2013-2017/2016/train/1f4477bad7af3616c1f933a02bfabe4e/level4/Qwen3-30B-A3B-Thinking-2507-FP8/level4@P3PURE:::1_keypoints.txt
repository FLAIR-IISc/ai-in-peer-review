- Paper proves existence of a single stationary point for a specialized, constrained neural network model.  
- Authors construct a contractive map whose unique fixed point equals the global minimizer of the cost.  
- They introduce a fast algorithm that provably converges to that unique fixed point (global minimum).  
- Theoretical approach leverages fixed-point theory as an alternative to convexity/geodesic-convex methods.  
- Key limitation: convergence proof depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.  
- Spectral-radius constraint prevents scaling to networks with larger numbers of hidden units.  
- Method requires tuning many parameters and is non-intuitive; the spectral condition must be checked for each parameter setting.  
- Experiments involved ~150,000 parameter combinations selected by cross-validation.  
- Empirical results are weak: method outperforms linear SVMs on only 2 of 7 datasets, questioning practical utility.
