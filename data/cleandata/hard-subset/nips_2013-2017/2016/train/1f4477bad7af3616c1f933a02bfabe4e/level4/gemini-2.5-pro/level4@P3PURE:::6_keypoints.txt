- Proposes a method for training a specific class of feed-forward neural networks with a proof of optimal convergence and demonstrations on real-world problems.
- The problem is interesting and the theoretical advance is a welcome contribution.
- Main weakness: paper does not convincingly show the proposed network class is useful in practice.
- Limited discussion on model expressiveness; authors mention it twice but do not elaborate on capabilities and limitations.
- Paper needs a clear discussion of which functions are representable and which are out of reach for the restricted architecture.
- Lacks thorough discussion and comparison with concurrent approaches and alternative training methods.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and does not clarify the model’s applicability boundaries.
- Comparisons are restricted to SVMs; other relevant methods and algorithmic paradigms are ignored.
- Experiments should report computational resources required by compared algorithms for practical assessment.
- Presentation quality drops after the introduction; Section 2 reads poorly.
- The loss function’s Phi is not motivated; incorporate the rebuttal’s epsilon-term explanation into the paper.
- Theorem 1 is hard to parse and needs a preceding informal explanation to build intuition.
- The claim about rapid convergence of the nonlinear spectral method should be explicitly attributed to the authors’ experimental observations.
- Typographical/notation issues: “polyomial”→“polynomial”; phrasing “one CAN model.”; p1 and p2 are used without introduction; R_{++} is undefined.
