- Novel contribution: proposes a nonlinear spectral method that provably trains a class of feedforward networks with global optimality guarantees.  
- The global-optimality condition depends on the architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- Claims a linear convergence rate for the method.  
- Major scope concern: requires all network weights to be nonnegative and uses very specific, nonstandard activation functions, likely limiting representational power and practical applicability.  
- Empirical results are not competitive with SVMs, calling into question practical performance.  
- Minor critique: the claim that linear convergence implies convergence in "a few (less than 10) iterations" is overstated—iteration count depends on contraction factors, conditioning, and initialization.
