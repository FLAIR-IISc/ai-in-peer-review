- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method that achieves globally optimal training with a proven linear convergence rate for a specific subclass of feedforward networks.  
- Global optimality condition depends on network architectural parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small non-negative matrix.  
- Application of a nonlinear spectral method to train this class of feedforward networks is a novel and interesting contribution.  
- Major practical limitation: requires non-negativity of all weights.  
- Major practical limitation: relies on a highly specific, non-standard set of activation functions.  
- These severe constraints are likely to hinder real-world performance.  
- Empirical results show worse performance than a standard SVM baseline, supporting practical concerns.  
- Minor critique: claiming linear convergence implies convergence in fewer than ~10 iterations is overstated, since actual speed depends on the convergence factor.
