- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method to train a restrictive class of feedforward networks to global optimality with linear convergence; applied to networks with one and two hidden layers and tested on real datasets.

- The studied network class is restrictive and counterintuitive, enforcing non-negativity and maximal regularization of weights.

- Global optimality requires the uncommon generalized polynomial activation, reducing real-world applicability.

- Assumptions are arguably unreasonable for many applications; the paper lacks application- or dataset-oriented justification for them.

- Authors claim sufficient expressive power, but this is not convincingly supported by experiments.

- Experimental comparisons are weak/unfair: linear SVM often outperforms NLSM variants, and nonlinear SVM baselines (e.g., polynomial or RBF kernels) are missing.

- Datasets are small (UCI), limiting evidence for scalability and expressive capacity on larger or higher-dimensional problems.

- Theoretical bounds on the spectral radius grow with hidden units, which may impede applicability to high-dimensional data and are not addressed.

- The optimality condition may not be satisfiable by any practical parameter settings, creating concern about real-use applicability.

- Checking optimality without priors or with very large parameter spaces is problematic; paper lacks fast methods to find valid candidate parameters.

- It is unclear whether the model’s global optimality arises from the special objective function rather than broader model properties.

- The paper should empirically test whether stochastic gradient descent can also reach global optima on the datasets used.

- Overall: interesting approach but needs stronger justification, fairer baselines, and discussion/solutions for high-dimensional and optimality-condition limitations.
