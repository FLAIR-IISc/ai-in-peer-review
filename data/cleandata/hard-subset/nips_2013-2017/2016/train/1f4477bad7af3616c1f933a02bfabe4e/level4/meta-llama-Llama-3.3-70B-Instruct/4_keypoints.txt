- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method guaranteeing optimal training for a specific class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence.  
- Global optimality guarantee depends on network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The method appears novel and intriguing in concept.  
- Major limitation: requires non-negativity constraints on network weights.  
- Activation functions used are unconventional and highly specific, limiting generality.  
- These constraints are overly restrictive and may harm practical neural network performance.  
- Numerical experiments show the method performs worse than SVM in the reported tests.  
- Minor critique: claiming convergence "in a few (<10) iterations" may be overstated since linear convergence does not ensure very rapid convergence.
