- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method that provably trains a restricted class of feed-forward (generalized polynomial) neural networks under certain assumptions.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical guarantee of global optimality for the proposed model class.  
- Shows linear convergence rate for the method, which is practically advantageous.  
- Novel use of nonlinear spectral methods for neural network training.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on several real-world (UCI) datasets.  
- Limited applicability: method currently only for networks with one or two hidden layers.  
- Model expressiveness concern: restriction to generalized polynomial networks with non-negative weights may limit practical usefulness.  
- Authors repeatedly raise expressiveness concerns; paper should discuss what is achievable vs. out of reach.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited (only compared to SVMs); missing comparisons to other NN training methods and architectures.  
- No discussion of computational cost or required computational resources for compared algorithms.  
- Presentation issues: Section 2 and overall exposition need clearer writing and flow.  
- Specific missing motivations/explanations: function Φ lacks motivation and the role of epsilon should be added to the paper.  
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement; the statement is hard to parse.  
- Empirical claim about quick convergence (“typically in fewer than 10 iterations”) should be clarified as experimental observation.  
- Parameter sensitivity unclear: impact of hyperparameters (epsilon, network size) on performance and convergence not investigated.  
- Concurrent related work is insufficiently discussed and contrasted.  
- Typos and notation problems: "polyomial" → "polynomial"; "one CAN model" → "one can model"; p1/p2 and R++ not introduced.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions if weaknesses (expressiveness discussion, broader experiments, computational cost, clarity) are addressed.
