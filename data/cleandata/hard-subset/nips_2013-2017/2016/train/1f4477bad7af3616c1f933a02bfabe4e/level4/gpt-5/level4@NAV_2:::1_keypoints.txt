- Proves a class of neural networks has a single stationary point and constructs a contractive mapping whose fixed point is the unique minimizer.  
- Presents a fast algorithm that provably converges to that unique fixed point/global minimizer.  
- Uses fixed-point theory as the main tool to establish uniqueness beyond convexity/geodesic convexity.  
- Convergence guarantee depends on bounding the spectral radius of a nontrivial matrix, a restrictive technical requirement.  
- The spectral condition prevents experiments with larger numbers of hidden units, limiting scalability.  
- The method requires many hyperparameters and checking the spectral condition for each parameter setting, making it unintuitive in practice.  
- Experiments involved ~150,000 parameter combinations selected by cross-validation, yet the model beat a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets.  
- The limited empirical improvements raise questions about the practical strength and utility of the proposed architecture.
