This paper introduces a method for training a specific class of feed-forward neural networks under a defined set of assumptions. The authors establish theoretical guarantees of optimal convergence and demonstrate the method’s applicability to a set of practical problems. The investigated problem is interesting, and theoretical contributions in this area are valuable. However, the paper doesn’t fully establish the practical utility of the proposed neural network class. The authors acknowledge limitations in the expressiveness of their restricted model, and I recommend expanding the discussion to address potential workarounds and inherent limitations of this approach. A more thorough comparison with concurrent work in the field is also needed, contrasting the strengths and weaknesses of different methods.

The experimental evaluation does not adequately illuminate the practical limitations of the model. The comparison is limited to SVMs, omitting other relevant neural network training techniques and alternative algorithmic approaches. Furthermore, reporting computational resource requirements for each algorithm would provide valuable context. 

While the introduction is well-written, the overall presentation could be improved. Specifically, Section 2 lacks clarity and flow. Several typos are present (detailed below). Regarding specific points: the motivation behind the function $\Phi$ is unclear; the explanation provided in the rebuttal regarding the role of the epsilon term should be incorporated into the paper. Theorem 1 would benefit from an informal explanation prior to its formal statement, as the current statement is difficult to follow. The claim immediately following Theorem 1 – regarding rapid convergence to the global maximum – appears to be based on experimental observation and should be explicitly stated as such.

Specific remarks:

*   Page 60: “polyomial” should be “polynomial”.
*   Page 67: “one CAN model” should be “one can model”.
*   Page 67: Variables *p1* and *p2* are not introduced before use.
*   Page 67: *R<sub>++</sub>* is not defined, which is problematic given its use in referencing other defined variables. Clarification, as provided in the rebuttal, would be helpful.
