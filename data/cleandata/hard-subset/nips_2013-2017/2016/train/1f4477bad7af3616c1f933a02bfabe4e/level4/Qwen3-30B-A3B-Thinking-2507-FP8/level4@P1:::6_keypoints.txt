- Proposes a method for globally optimal training of a class of feedforward nets via nonlinear spectral methods with linear convergence guarantees and a practical algorithm.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: rigorous proofs, clear convergence guarantees, and a novel link between network training and nonlinear eigenvalue problems.  
- Experiments show competitive performance on benchmarks, supporting practical implementability.  
- Model expressiveness is limited (acknowledged twice) but the paper fails to analyze practical implications or delineate which problem classes are tractable.  
- Experimental evaluation is too narrow—only compares to SVMs and ReLU nets and omits other global/alternative methods (e.g., tensor-based/Janzamin et al.).  
- No discussion of computational resources (runtime/memory) relative to other approaches despite claims of fast convergence.  
- Presentation issues: objective Φ lacks motivation and omission of ε explanation from the main text; Theorem 1 is dense and needs an informal explanation; empirical convergence claims should be tied to experiments.  
- Specific errors to fix: correct "polyomial"→"polynomial", introduce p1 and p2, and define R_{++} given reliance on positive orthant constraints.  
- Verdict: significant theoretical value but practical analysis, experiments, and clarity need major revision—recommend rejection with invitation to resubmit after addressing these concerns.
