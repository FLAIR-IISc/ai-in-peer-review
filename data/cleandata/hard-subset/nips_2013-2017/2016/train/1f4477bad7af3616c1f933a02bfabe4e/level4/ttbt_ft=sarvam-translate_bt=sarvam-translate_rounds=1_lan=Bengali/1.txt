This paper discusses a specific class of neural networks where the author has successfully proven that it has only one fixed point. The author presents a fast method that converges to the unique fixed point, which is the global minimum of the cost function. This paper addresses an interesting problem of how to show a relatively complex model with cleverly chosen constraints converges to only one fixed point. Besides convexity (or its generalization, geodesic convexity), a common method for showing the uniqueness of a fixed point is to use fixed-point theory; this method is used in this paper. The author creates a contractile map, whose fixed point is the unique minimizer of the cost function. A drawback of the proposed method is the dependence of the convergence proof on the restriction of the spectral radius of some non-trivial matrices. The authors note that this is why they did not attempt to use networks with a larger number of hidden units. Another problem with the method is the many parameters that need to be tuned, which makes the use of the method quite intuitive. Furthermore, the spectral condition should be verified for each choice of parameters. In the experimental results, the authors tried approximately 150,000 different combinations of possible parameter values and chose the best among them based on cross-validation accuracy. However, their method can only outperform linear-SVM for 2 out of 7 datasets. This result raises questions about the true capabilities of the neural network they are studying.
