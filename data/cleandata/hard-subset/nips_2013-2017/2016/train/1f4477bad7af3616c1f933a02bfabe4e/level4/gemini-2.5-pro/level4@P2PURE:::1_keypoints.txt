- Proves that for a specific constrained class of neural networks the cost has a single stationary point.  
- Introduces a fast algorithm that provably converges to that unique global minimizer.  
- Theoretical novelty: leverages fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose fixed point equals the minimizer (rather than using convexity).  
- Convergence proof hinges on a spectral-radius bound for a non-trivial matrix, a condition hard to satisfy in practice.  
- This spectral-radius requirement prevents scaling experiments to larger numbers of hidden units.  
- Method involves many hyperparameters and requires checking the spectral-radius condition for every parameter combo, making it unintuitive and cumbersome.  
- Authors performed ~150,000 parameter-combination cross-validation runs, highlighting heavy tuning cost.  
- Empirical results are weak: after extensive tuning the model outperforms a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, questioning practical utility and representational power.
