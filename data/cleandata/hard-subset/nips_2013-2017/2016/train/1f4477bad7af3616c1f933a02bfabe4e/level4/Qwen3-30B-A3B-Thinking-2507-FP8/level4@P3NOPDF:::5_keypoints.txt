- Theoretical claim of a unique global optimizer hinges on strong structural constraints; the practical relevance of those constraints is unclear.  
- The transition from Theorem 3 (first-order) to Theorem 4 (second-order) is unexplained—need justification why second derivatives are required for uniqueness.  
- Notation and parameterization of the final-layer parameter u are ambiguous (matrix vs. vectorization) and the ball/constraint on u lacks clarification (e.g., Frobenius vs. vector norm).  
- Equation (2) maximizes the 1-norm of weights, which contradicts standard weight-decay/regularization practice; the small ε explanation is technical but provides no practical motivation for maximizing weights.  
- Requiring all inputs ≥0 and all weights >0 prevents weight cancellation and severely limits applicability; authors give no path to relax these unrealistic constraints.  
- Experimental comparison to SVMs is inappropriate and uninformative; no comparisons to standard neural-network baselines (e.g., ReLU nets) are provided.  
- Mathematical exposition needs clearer derivations and consistent handling of parameters to ensure the proofs are coherent and reproducible.  
- The linear convergence result is technically interesting but insufficient without stronger motivation, relaxed constraints, or empirical evidence demonstrating advantage over conventional NNs.
