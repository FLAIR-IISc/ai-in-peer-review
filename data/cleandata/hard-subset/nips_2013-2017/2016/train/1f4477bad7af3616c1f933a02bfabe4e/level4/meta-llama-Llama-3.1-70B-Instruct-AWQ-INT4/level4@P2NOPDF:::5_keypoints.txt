- Proposes a feedforward neural network with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm that finds it with linear convergence.  
- Mathematical derivations are instructive but several parts lack clarity.  
- Unclear why Theorem 3 uses first-order derivatives while Theorem 4 shifts to second-order derivatives — the reason for the change is not explained.  
- Network treats the last layer separately from hidden layers, raising questions about gradient computation for the parameter matrix u (is the gradient a vectorization of u?) and the rationale for bounding u within a ball.  
- The objective in Eq.(2) requires maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters, which contradicts standard weight-decay practices and seems unjustified.  
- Introducing an arbitrarily small epsilon in Eq.(2) to force strictly positive gradients appears ad hoc and lacks practical motivation.  
- The claim of no free parameters in the model is stated but not explained.  
- Practical limitations: requires non-negative data and strictly positive weights, which can cause weight accumulation and reduce applicability to real-world problems.  
- Experimental comparison with SVMs is unconvincing; SVMs are not the most relevant baseline for neural networks.  
- Missing empirical comparison to standard neural architectures (e.g., ReLU networks), so practical advantages over common models are unclear.
