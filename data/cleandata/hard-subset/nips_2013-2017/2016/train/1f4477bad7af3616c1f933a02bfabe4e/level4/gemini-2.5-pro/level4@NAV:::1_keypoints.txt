- Demonstrates existence of a single stationary point for a specialized class of neural networks.
- Proposes an efficient algorithm that provably converges to this unique fixed-point, which is the global minimizer.
- Addresses the theoretical question of guaranteeing uniqueness without convexity by using fixed-point theory.
- Constructs a contractive map whose fixed-point equals the unique minimizer of the cost function.
- Convergence proof depends on bounded spectral radius of a complex matrix, limiting scalability (prevents experiments with larger hidden layers).
- Method requires many unintuitive hyperparameters and the spectral condition must be rechecked for each configuration.
- Experiments involved ~150,000 hyperparameter combinations selected by cross-validation.
- Practical performance is weak: model outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, questioning practical effectiveness.
