- Proposes a novel training approach for feedforward networks with a global optimality guarantee via nonlinear spectral methods.  
- Targets a restrictive class: networks with nonnegative weights and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Applies method to networks with one and two hidden layers and runs experiments on real-world datasets.  
- Main technical contribution: thorough optimization analysis and a nonlinear spectral solver.  
- Method has a proven linear convergence rate with detailed convergence proofs.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents theory and methods.  
- Experimental results are presented and compared to other methods (SGD, SVM).  
- Experimental evidence is weak — performance not significantly better than alternatives.  
- Assumptions (nonnegativity, polynomial activations) limit model expressiveness and practical relevance.  
- Comparison baseline is unfair (linear SVM used instead of a nonlinear SVM).  
- Authors don’t clearly justify advantages over SGD, which can reach good optima empirically.  
- Scalability concerns: spectral radius bounds grow with hidden units, problematic for high-dimensional data.  
- Paper is original and potentially significant but needs more empirical support and discussion of limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing limitations, stronger experiments, fairer baselines, and clearer applicability discussion.
