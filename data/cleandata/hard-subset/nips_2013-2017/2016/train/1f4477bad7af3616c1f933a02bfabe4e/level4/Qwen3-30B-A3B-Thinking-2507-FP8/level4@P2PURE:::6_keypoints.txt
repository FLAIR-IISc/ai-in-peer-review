- Paper proposes training method for a restricted class of feedforward nets, proves optimal convergence rates, and shows applicability to some real-world problems (positive contribution).  
- Fails to convincingly establish practical utility of the restricted network class; expressiveness limitations are raised but not thoroughly discussed.  
- Authors should add a clear discussion of which tasks are achievable and the inherent limitations of their approach.  
- Concurrent and alternative methods are not compared or contrasted; include comparisons to other neural-network training methods and algorithmic paradigms.  
- Experiments compare only against SVMs and omit other baselines; this does not clarify the model’s applicability boundaries.  
- Report computational resource requirements (runtime, hardware) for the three algorithms evaluated.  
- Introduction is well written, but overall presentation needs improvement; Section 2 lacks fluency.  
- Multiple typos and notation issues: "polyomial" → "polynomial"; ambiguous "one CAN model"; p₁ and p₂ undefined; R_{++} used without definition.  
- Loss function definition is straightforward, but the motivation for function Φ is lacking and should be explained.  
- The role of ε (currently explained in rebuttal) must be incorporated into the paper text.  
- Theorem 1 needs a preceding informal explanation; the formal statement is difficult to parse.  
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” is based on experiments and should be explicitly presented as an empirical observation.
