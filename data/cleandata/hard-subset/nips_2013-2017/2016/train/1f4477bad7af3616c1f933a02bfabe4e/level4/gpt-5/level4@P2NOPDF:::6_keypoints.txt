- Paper introduces a method with optimal convergence guarantees for a specific class of feed-forward networks and shows real-world applicability.
- Theoretical contribution is solid and nontrivial, but practical utility of the restricted network class is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Main concern: expressiveness — authors must clarify what functions/tasks the model can or cannot represent.
- Experiments do not clarify method scope; comparisons are limited (only a few SVM baselines) and omit standard neural-network training baselines and alternative algorithms.
- Report computational resources and efficiency metrics (wall-clock time, iteration counts, hardware, memory) for all methods since training-time guarantees are central.
- Section 2 and some notation are hard to follow; definitions and notational choices are insufficiently motivated or introduced.
- The role of Phi and the epsilon term is under-motivated; include the rebuttal explanation to make the objective and constraints self-contained.
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation and intuition before the formal statement, with clarified assumptions and practical meaning of “optimal convergence.”
- The empirical convergence claim about the nonlinear spectral method should be explicitly labeled empirical and qualified with references to experiments/conditions.
- Experiments should include broader baselines, analyze failure modes, and discuss where the proposed model performs well or poorly.
- Fix minor issues and typos (e.g., “polyomial” → “polynomial”, “one CAN model” → “one can model”) and define symbols like p1, p2, and R_{++} on first use.
- Ensure all notation and sets are defined at first appearance and used consistently throughout the paper.
- Recommendation: (1) articulate model expressive capacity and limits, (2) broaden/strengthen experiments with resource reporting, (3) improve exposition of definitions and theorems to increase impact and clarity.
