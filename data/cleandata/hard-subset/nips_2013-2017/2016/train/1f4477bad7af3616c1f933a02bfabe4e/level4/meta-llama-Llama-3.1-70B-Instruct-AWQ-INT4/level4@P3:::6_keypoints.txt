- Proposes a novel method to train a specific class of feed-forward neural networks with proven optimal convergence under certain assumptions.  
- Demonstrates applicability of the method on a selection of "real-world" problems.  
- Investigates an interesting problem and provides a valuable theoretical advance.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making the argument easy to follow.  
- Introduction gives thorough background and effectively situates the work relative to prior NIPS and other literature.  
- Technical contributions are sound, with complete theoretical analysis and supporting experimental results.  
- Authors are careful and balanced in evaluating strengths and weaknesses.  
- Paper shows originality by combining familiar techniques in a unique training approach.  
- Fails to convincingly show the practical usefulness or expressiveness of the restricted network class.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of model limitations and potential real-world applications.  
- Concurrent and alternative approaches are not thoroughly discussed or contrasted.  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete and compares mainly to some SVMs while ignoring other training methods and paradigms.  
- Does not report computational resource requirements or runtime comparisons for the algorithms.  
- Needs clearer explanation and motivation for the function Φ.  
- Role of the epsilon term in the loss function is not well-explained.  
- Theorem 1 is hard to parse and should be preceded by an informal explanation.  
- The claim about nonlinear spectral method convergence should be clarified as coming from experimental observation.  
- Contains minor errors and typos (e.g., "polyomial", "one CAN model") and missing introductions/notation (p1, p2, R_{++}).  
- Conclusion: valuable contribution but would benefit from broader discussion of limitations, applications, and more thorough comparisons.  
- Reviewer recommendation: mixed — arguments for acceptance (novelty, theory, clarity) and against acceptance (practical usefulness, experiments, explanations); overall rating 7/10 with confidence 8/10.
