- Paper proposes a class of feedforward nets with a provable unique global optimum and an algorithm that converges linearly to it.  
- Mathematical derivations are generally instructive but several parts need clearer exposition.  
- The transition from first-order conditions in Theorem 3 to second-order in Theorem 4 is unexplained and needs motivation.  
- Ambiguity in how hidden-layer weight matrix u is handled (e.g., is gradient in G^Φ computed w.r.t. vec(u)?), including when u is constrained to a ball.  
- The objective maximizes the L1-norm of connection parameters, which contradicts standard weight-decay practice and lacks practical/theoretical justification beyond proof convenience.  
- The use of a small epsilon to ensure strictly positive gradients appears to be a mathematical convenience rather than a justified modeling choice.  
- Model constraints (non-negative inputs, strictly positive weights) are highly restrictive and imply monotonically increasing outputs, raising practical applicability concerns.  
- Experiments compare only to SVMs; the paper should compare to standard neural nets (e.g., ReLU networks) to assess practical advantages of a global-optimum guarantee.
