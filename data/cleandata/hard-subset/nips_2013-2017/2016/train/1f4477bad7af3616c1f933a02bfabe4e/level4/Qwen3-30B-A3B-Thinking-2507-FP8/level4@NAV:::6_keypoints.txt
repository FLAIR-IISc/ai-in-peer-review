- Paper proposes a method with optimal convergence guarantees for a class of feed-forward networks and makes commendable theoretical progress.  
- The practical utility of the proposed network class is not sufficiently established given noted expressiveness limitations.  
- Authors should elaborate on achievable outcomes and inherent constraints of their approach given expressiveness issues.  
- Concurrent methods are not explicitly compared or contrasted and should be discussed.  
- Experiments are inadequate: comparisons are limited to SVMs and omit other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms.  
- Report computational resource requirements (runtime, hardware) for all three algorithms in experiments.  
- Presentation needs refinement: Introduction is good but Section 2 lacks fluency.  
- Several typos and undefined notation must be fixed (e.g., "polyomial", "one CAN model", undefined p₁, p₂, and ℝ₊₊).  
- Loss function is clear but Φ lacks motivation; the epsilon term’s purpose (explained in rebuttal) should be included in the main text.  
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement to aid readability.  
- The claim of "quick convergence typically in fewer than 10 iterations" should be explicitly qualified as an empirical observation.
