- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence.
- Global optimality is guaranteed under a condition computed via the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix dependent on network architecture.
- The approach is interesting and appears to provide an optimal training method for that particular network class.
- Major limitation: requires network weights to be nonnegative, which may be overly restrictive.
- Major limitation: relies on very specific, non-standard activation functions.
- These restrictions may significantly hurt practical performance; numerical experiments show mediocre results compared to SVM.
- Minor comment: the claim that linear convergence implies convergence in a few (<10) iterations is exaggerated.
