- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method to optimally train a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate.  
- Guarantee for global optimality depends on architectural parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small non-negative matrix.  
- Practical applicability is severely limited by the assumption that all network weights must be non-negative.  
- Method is restricted to non-standard, highly specific activation functions, likely reducing expressivity and performance.  
- Numerical experiments indicate the proposed model underperforms a standard SVM.  
- Minor: Claim that the method “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” is overstated—linear convergence rate alone does not ensure few iterations since the rate constant can be close to one.
