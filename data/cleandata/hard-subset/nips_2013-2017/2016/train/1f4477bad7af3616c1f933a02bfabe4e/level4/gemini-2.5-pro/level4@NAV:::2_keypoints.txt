- Paper studies a class of feedforward nets solvable to global optimum with a nonlinear spectral method, applied to 1- and 2-hidden-layer networks and tested on real datasets.
- The network class imposes strong, non-standard constraints (non-negative weights, strong regularization) and uses an uncommon generalized polynomial activation, with little practical justification provided.
- Authors claim sufficient expressive power, but empirical evidence is unconvincing.
- Comparisons are unfair: linear SVM often outperforms NLSM models, yet nonlinear baselines (e.g., polynomial/Gaussian SVMs) are not used.
- Experiments use small UCI datasets, raising concerns about scalability and real-world applicability.
- Theoretical bounds (spectral radius of A) grow with hidden units, suggesting potential problems in high dimensions and no strategy is given to address this.
- The optimality guarantee depends on specific parameter settings that are not guaranteed or easy to find in large search spaces; no fast method is proposed to identify valid parameters.
- It is unclear whether the global-optimal property stems from the special objective; the reviewer suggests testing if standard optimizers (e.g., SGD) can reach the same global optimum.
