- Novel theoretical result: provides a practical algorithm with formal guarantees of global optimality and linear convergence for a non-trivial feedforward network class.  
- A priori convergence check: the convergence condition (spectral radius ρ(A) < 1) can be computed and verified before training.  
- No learning-rate tuning: deterministic iterative updates require no optimization hyperparameters like learning rates.  
- Restrictive model assumptions: requires non-negative data and weights, specific generalized polynomial activations, and a modified objective, limiting practical applicability.  
- Modified objective concerns: inclusion of the negative total sum of outputs is mathematically motivated but its practical impact and justification are unclear.  
- Unconvincing empirical results: NLSM1/NLSM2 are often outperformed by RBF-SVM and sometimes by standard ReLU networks on tested datasets.  
- Limited evidence of expressiveness: constrained architecture struggles on simple datasets where high accuracy is achievable, weakening claims of creating complex decision boundaries.  
- Scalability issues: bounds on ρ(A) grow with hidden units, making the convergence condition harder to satisfy for larger, higher-dimensional networks.  
- Difficulty in finding valid architectures: paper offers limited guidance for efficiently selecting hyperparameters (units, exponents, norms) that both satisfy ρ(A)<1 and yield strong performance.  
- Suggestion to clarify objective role: need more intuition on how maximizing sum(f_r(x_i)) affects learned decision boundaries beyond proof requirements.  
- Suggestion for empirical comparison: compare the spectral method to Batch-SGD on the exact same objective to test whether the specialized method is necessary.  
- Suggestion to relax bounds: explore ways to tighten or relax bounds on matrix A to improve scalability and allow more hidden units.
