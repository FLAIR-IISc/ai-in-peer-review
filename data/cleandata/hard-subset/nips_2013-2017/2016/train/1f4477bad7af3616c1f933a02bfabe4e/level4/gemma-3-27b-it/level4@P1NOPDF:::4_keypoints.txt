- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method to optimally train a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a proven linear convergence rate.  
- Global optimality condition depends on network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- Proposing a nonlinear spectral method for optimal neural network training is a novel and interesting contribution.  
- Method requires non-negativity constraints on network weights, which is a significant limitation.  
- Uses non-standard, highly specific activation functions, further restricting applicability.  
- These constraints may limit practical performance; experiments do not show competitiveness with SVMs.  
- Minor critique: claiming linear convergence implies very fast convergence (e.g., <10 iterations) is overstated, since linear rate alone does not guarantee few iterations.
