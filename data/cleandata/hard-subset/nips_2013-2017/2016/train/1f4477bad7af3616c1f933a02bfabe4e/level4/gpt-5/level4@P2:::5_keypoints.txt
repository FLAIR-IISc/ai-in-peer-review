- Paper studies generalized polynomial networks with nonnegative inputs/weights and gives a nonlinear spectral method with a verifiable spectral condition ρ(A) < 1 guaranteeing a unique global optimum and linear convergence.
- Method requires no learning-rate tuning, uses nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory, and is extended from one to two hidden layers.
- Objective departs from standard practice by maximizing cross-entropy plus the sum of outputs and adding a small positive linear weight term to ensure strict positivity of gradients.
- Experiments on several UCI datasets show competitive accuracy with ReLU nets but slightly worse than RBF-SVM on average.
- Positions itself against prior global-optimality results as more practically checkable (avoids tensor score estimation and density estimation).
- Strength: clear theoretical contribution—a globally convergent algorithm with linear rate and a computable spectral condition.
- Strength: elegant use of nonlinear Perron–Frobenius and fixed-point contractions on positive cones.
- Strength: no learning-rate hyperparameter and convergence rate tied to computable ρ(A).
- Strength: analogous guarantees for two hidden layers and empirical demonstration of nontrivial decision boundaries.
- Weakness: unclear smoothness assumptions—Theorem 3 uses first-order Lipschitz bounds but Theorem 4 appears to require second derivatives; need clarification on C2 requirements and where ∇²Φ is used.
- Weakness: ambiguity in treating matrix variable u—need explicit vectorization/shape conventions for ψ, normalization, gradients, and ℓp norms over u.
- Weakness: role of ε term unclear—report which ε used, sensitivity of performance to ε, whether it discourages sparsity, and if alternative interior-point mechanisms could ensure positivity without modifying the objective.
- Weakness: “no free parameter” claim is misleading—many architectural and norm hyperparameters (n1, α, ρw, ρu, pw, pu, ε) remain and ρ(A)<1 often forces large pw, pu; practical guidance for choosing these is needed.
- Weakness: nonnegativity and strictly positive weights enforce monotonicity and forbid sign cancellations—implications for expressivity and calibration, and recommended preprocessing (shifting/scaling) for x ≥ 0, are not addressed.
- Weakness: selection/tuning of per-unit exponents α is underexplored—need heuristics, regularization, or ablations showing impact on permutation invariance and ρ(A).
- Weakness: conservativeness of bounds defining A—authors should report empirical ρ(A) values, iteration counts, and potential tightened bounds to reduce dependence on hidden-unit count.
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to low-dimensional datasets—questions remain on scalability to higher dimensions, wall-clock time/iteration comparisons, and tighter baselines.
- Weakness: more baselines requested, including monotone networks, nonnegative factorization classifiers, and unconstrained ReLU with nonnegativity preprocessing to isolate positivity constraints’ effect.
- Presentation: exposition is dense—add a proof map, state vectorization conventions, summarize each Φ term’s role, provide pseudocode with shapes/norms, and report ε and ρ(A) used in experiments.
- Pros for acceptance: novel rigorous global optimality with a practical check, a clean algorithm without learning-rate tuning, and first demonstration of globally optimal NN training with empirical performance.
- Cons for acceptance: practical limitations from positivity constraints, objective modification with unclear empirical impact, conservative bounds, and limited experiments where performance trails RBF-SVM.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but needs clarifications on derivatives, matrix-variable handling, ε/positivity effects, and broader/stronger empirical validation to strengthen practical relevance.
