- Proves existence of a single stationary point for a specific class of neural networks.  
- Presents a rapid convergence method to the unique fixed-point, which equals the global minimum.  
- Uses fixed-point theory and constructs a contractive mapping to establish uniqueness beyond convexity notions.  
- Convergence proof depends on bounded spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.  
- Spectral-radius requirement limits applicability to networks with few hidden units.  
- Method involves many tunable parameters, making it unintuitive to apply.  
- Verifying the spectral condition for each parameter configuration is complex and costly.  
- Experiments used an extensive (~150,000) parameter search with cross-validation.  
- Empirically outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, showing limited practical gains.  
- High computational tuning cost and modest performance improvements weaken practical impact.  
- Recommends future work to relax the spectral constraint or automate parameter tuning.
