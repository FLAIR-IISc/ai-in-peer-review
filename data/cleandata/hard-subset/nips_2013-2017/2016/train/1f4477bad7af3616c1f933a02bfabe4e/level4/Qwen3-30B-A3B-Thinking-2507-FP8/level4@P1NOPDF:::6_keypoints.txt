- Proposes a method for training a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks with optimal convergence guarantees; theory is a welcome contribution.  
- Paper fails to convince that the restricted model class has practical utility; authors should elaborate on scope and inherent expressiveness limitations.  
- Lacks contrast with concurrent approaches, leaving unclear how this work advances beyond existing methods.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: comparisons only against SVMs, omitting relevant baselines such as standard neural network optimizers (SGD, Adam) and other algorithmic paradigms.  
- No reporting of computational resources or runtimes for the proposed method and baselines, preventing assessment of efficiency trade-offs.  
- Experimental results therefore do not clarify practical applicability or relative strengths of the method.  
- Introduction is clear, but later sections (especially Section 2) lack fluency and need substantial refinement.  
- The ε term in the loss is not motivated in the main text; justification provided in rebuttal should be integrated into the paper.  
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation of intuition and significance before the formal statement to improve readability.  
- Claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” is based on experiments and should be explicitly stated as empirical observation, not a general theorem.  
- Several typos and notation issues must be fixed: “polyomial” → “polynomial”; “one CAN model” likely → “one CNN model”; p₁ and p₂ undefined; ℝ₊₊ used without definition.  
- Overall recommendation: address expressiveness limitations, include relevant comparisons and computational cost reporting, and clarify explanations and notation to strengthen the paper’s practical impact.
