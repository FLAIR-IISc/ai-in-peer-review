- Paper studies a constrained neural network class and shows it has a single stationary point.  
- Presents an efficient algorithm that converges to that unique fixed-point, which equals the global minimum.  
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map to prove uniqueness of the minimizer.  
- The convergence proof depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.  
- This spectral-radius requirement likely prevents scaling the method to larger numbers of hidden units.  
- The method uses many tunable parameters and requires checking the spectral condition for each parameter choice.  
- Experiments searched ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected the best by cross-validation.  
- The proposed method beat linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, casting doubt on its practical potential.
