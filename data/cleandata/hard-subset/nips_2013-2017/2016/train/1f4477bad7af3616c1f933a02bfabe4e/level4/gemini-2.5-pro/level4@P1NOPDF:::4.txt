This paper presents a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, demonstrating a linear convergence rate. A key theoretical contribution is the condition for guaranteeing global optimality, which depends on the network's architectural parameters and can be verified by computing the spectral radius of a small, non-negative matrix. The proposal of a spectral method that achieves provably optimal training for this class of neural networks is both novel and interesting.

However, I have significant concerns regarding the practical limitations of the proposed approach. A major limitation is the constraint that all network weights must be non-negative. Furthermore, the method relies on a set of non-standard and highly specific activation functions. Together, these constraints are highly restrictive and are likely to compromise the expressive power and overall performance of the resulting neural networks. This concern appears to be validated by the numerical experiments, which show that the proposed method underperforms a standard SVM baseline. This suggests that the theoretical guarantees may come at a high cost in terms of predictive accuracy.

Minor comment:
On lines 90-91, the claim that the method "converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations" due to its linear convergence rate seems exaggerated. A linear convergence rate does not, by itself, guarantee convergence in such a small number of iterations, as the actual speed also depends on the constant factor.
