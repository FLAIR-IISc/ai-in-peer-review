- Theoretical result of global optimality with linear convergence is appealing but its practical impact is limited by restrictive assumptions.  
- Core limitation: non-negativity constraints and maximized regularization on weights are unjustified and overly constrain the model class.  
- Requirement of generalized polynomial activations is uncommon and further reduces applicability.  
- Empirical evidence does not convincingly show the constrained class retains sufficient expressive power; needs application- or data-driven justification.  
- Experimental comparisons are inappropriate: NLSM (nonlinear) should be compared to nonlinear SVMs (polynomial/Gaussian), not just linear SVMs.  
- Small UCI datasets (limited samples and feature dimensionality) weaken empirical claims about performance and expressivity.  
- Scalability concern: spectral-radius bounds grow with hidden units, likely preventing application to high-dimensional problems.  
- Paper lacks methods to control or approximate the spectral radius to address scalability.  
- Verification of the optimality condition is not practically discussed; checking becomes intractable for large/unstructured parameter spaces.  
- Need for efficient procedures to identify promising parameter configurations and to clarify whether global optimality stems from the objective formulation.  
- Suggest empirically studying SGD on this model class to see if it also reaches global optima and to compare practical advantages.  
- Overall: theoretical contribution is noteworthy but restrictive assumptions, flawed experimental comparisons, and scalability/verification issues require substantial revision.
