This paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a particular class of feedforward neural networks with a provable linear convergence rate. The condition guaranteeing global optimality depends on network-architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. The idea of using a nonlinear spectral method to obtain global optimality for this class of networks appears new and interesting.

That said, I have serious concerns about the scope and practicality of the approach. The method requires non-negativity constraints on the network weights, which is a strong restriction. In addition, the activation functions used are non-standard and highly specialized. Together, these constraints appear quite limiting and may hinder the practical performance and applicability of the resulting networks. Indeed, the numerical experiments show performance that is not competitive with SVMs.

Minor comment:
- Lines 90–91: The statement “the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations” seems overstated. A linear convergence rate does not, by itself, guarantee convergence in only a few iterations.
