1. Contribution: Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for a constrained class of feedforward networks, claiming guaranteed global optimality and linear convergence for one- and two-hidden-layer models, evaluated on real datasets.

2. Major weakness — restrictive assumptions: The theoretical guarantees rely on strong, practically unjustified constraints limiting real-world applicability.

3. Non-negative weights: Forbids negative weights, severely restricting representational capacity compared to standard networks.

4. Maximizing L2 norm: The objective maximizes weight norms, contradicting common regularization practices that penalize large weights.

5. Uncommon activation: Requires a "generalized polynomial" activation, further distancing the model from mainstream architectures.

6. Need for motivation: Authors must justify why this constrained class is useful in practice beyond enabling theory.

7. Major weakness — experimental issues: Empirical evaluation is unconvincing and uses unfair baselines.

8. Inappropriate baselines: NLSM models (nonlinear) are mainly compared to a linear SVM instead of strong nonlinear baselines like kernel SVMs or MLPs trained with SGD.

9. Weak empirical performance: Linear SVM often outperforms NLSM models, suggesting limited expressive power or utility despite tuned NLSM structures.

10. Small-scale datasets: Experiments use small UCI datasets, inadequate to validate claims about modeling complex decision boundaries.

11. Major weakness — scalability/practicality: The method’s scalability and practical deployment are questionable.

12. Spectral radius growth: Bounds on the spectral radius of matrix A increase with hidden units, implying poor scaling to high-dimensional or wide networks.

13. Finding valid parameters: It’s unclear how to efficiently find parameter sets that satisfy the optimality condition if the feasible space is small or complex.

14. Questions for authors: Request efficient methods to identify candidate parameters, clarification whether global optimality stems from the objective or the algorithm (and if SGD reaches it), and strategies for high-dimensional/large networks.

15. Conclusion: The paper is an interesting theoretical exercise for a niche network class but lacks practical relevance due to restrictive assumptions, weak/unfair experiments, and unresolved scalability issues; claims of sufficient expressive power are unsupported.
