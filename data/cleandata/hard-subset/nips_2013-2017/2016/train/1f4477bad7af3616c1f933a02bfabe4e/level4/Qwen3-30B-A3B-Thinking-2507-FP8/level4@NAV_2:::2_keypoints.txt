- Novel theoretical contribution: presents a nonlinear spectral method guaranteeing global optimality with linear convergence for a specific class of feedforward networks.
- Method evaluated on one- and two-hidden-layer networks and shown empirically on several UCI datasets.
- Restrictive and unconventional modeling assumptions: requires non-negative weights and generalized polynomial activations with real powers.
- Assumptions are not well justified for practical applications and may limit applicability.
- Authors claim capability to model complex decision boundaries, but empirical evidence is weak.
- Baseline choice is problematic: comparing only to linear SVM is inappropriate for a nonlinear model; nonlinear SVMs (polynomial/Gaussian) would be better baselines.
- Linear SVM often matches or outperforms NLSM1/NLSM2 across datasets even without tuning, questioning practical advantage.
- Datasets used are small in sample size and dimensionality, limiting generalizability of results.
- Scalability concern: the spectral radius condition for global optimality becomes harder to satisfy as hidden units increase.
- Unclear how practitioners would efficiently find valid parameter configurations in a huge parameter space despite the condition being checkable.
- Open question whether global optimality depends on the special objective function used or could extend to standard loss functions.
- Suggestion to empirically compare against properly tuned standard SGD methods to contextualize the proposed method's advantages.
