- Proposes a novel method to train a specific class of feed-forward neural networks and proves optimal convergence.  
- The problem is interesting and the theoretical advance is appreciated.  
- Paper fails to convincingly show that the restricted network class is useful in practice.  
- Insufficient discussion on the expressiveness limits of the proposed model and what tasks are out of reach.  
- Lacks comparison to concurrent approaches and other neural-network training methods or algorithmic paradigms.  
- Experimental results are limited and do not clarify applicability limitations of the model.  
- Comparisons are restricted to some SVMs—other baselines are missing.  
- No information provided about computational resources required by the algorithms.  
- Presentation needs improvement: Section 2 is not fluent and there are typos.  
- The function Φ lacks motivation; the epsilon term explanation from the rebuttal should be added to the paper.  
- Theorem 1 should be given an informal explanation before its formal statement; the statement is hard to parse.  
- The claim that the method “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” should be clarified as an empirical observation.  
- Minor errors to fix: "polyomial"→"polynomial"; unclear phrase "one CAN model"; p1 and p2 not introduced; R_{++} not defined.  
- Overall: promising theory and some experiments, but needs stronger empirical validation, comparisons, and clearer presentation to be convincing.
