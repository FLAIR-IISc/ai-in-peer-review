- Shows a specific class of neural networks has a single stationary point and provides a method converging rapidly to that unique fixed-point/global minimum.  
- Tackles an interesting theoretical problem: a relatively complex model can have only one stationary point under carefully chosen constraints.  
- Uses fixed-point theory and constructs a contractive mapping whose fixed-point equals the unique minimizer (beyond convexity approaches).  
- Convergence proof depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, limiting applicability.  
- The spectral-radius constraint prevented exploration of networks with larger numbers of hidden units.  
- The method requires tuning many parameters, is unintuitive, and demands checking the spectral condition for every parameter configuration.  
- Experiments involved ~150,000 parameter combinations via cross-validation to select the best set.  
- Empirically outperformed linear-SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, raising doubts about practical efficacy of the architecture.
