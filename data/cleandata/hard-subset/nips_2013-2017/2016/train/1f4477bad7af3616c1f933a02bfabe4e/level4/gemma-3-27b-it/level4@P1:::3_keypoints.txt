- Main contribution: Proposes a theoretical framework showing a class of feedforward NNs with non-negative inputs/weights and generalized polynomial activations can be trained to global optimality with linear convergence.
- Scope of theory: Proven for single hidden-layer networks and extended to multi-layer networks.
- Algorithm: Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for optimization and evaluates it on synthetic and UCI datasets.
- Key novelty: First potentially practical NN model with guaranteed global optimality and convergence under simple verifiable conditions.
- Simpler preconditions: Guarantees rely on the spectral radius of a constructed non-negative matrix rather than hard-to-verify data-generating assumptions.
- Empirical result: Demonstrates reasonable (but not state-of-the-art) performance on small real-world datasets.
- Major constraint: Requires non-negative input data and non-negative network parameters, limiting expressiveness.
- Activation restriction: Uses generalized polynomial activations and demands distinct activation functions per hidden layer, which is somewhat unnatural.
- Spectral radius condition: Requires ρ(A)<1, which may force parameter adjustments that reduce linear unit weights and harm expressiveness.
- Scalability concern: Potential diminishing weights and expressiveness as input dimensionality and depth grow, raising doubts about deep-network scalability.
- Minor query: Requests analysis or empirical plotting of how ρ(A) scales with parameters (p1,p2,pw,pu), input dimensionality, and network size.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach, clear theoretical guarantees, demonstrated practical feasibility on small datasets, tackles a difficult non-convex problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited applicability due to constraints, scalability concerns for deep architectures, and only modest empirical gains.
- Recommendation: An encouraging initial attempt with acceptability endorsement despite noted limitations.
