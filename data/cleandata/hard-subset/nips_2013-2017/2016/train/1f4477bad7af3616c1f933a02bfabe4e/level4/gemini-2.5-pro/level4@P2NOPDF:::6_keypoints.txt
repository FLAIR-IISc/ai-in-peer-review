- Paper proposes a training method for a restricted class of feed-forward networks, proves optimal convergence under assumptions, and shows applications on real problems.
- Main weakness: unclear practical utility and limited scope of the proposed network class; needs substantive discussion of expressiveness and limitations.
- Expand discussion on what functions or data structures the model can/cannot represent and trade-offs versus standard architectures.
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient to demonstrate advantages or limitations of the method.
- Limited baselines: comparison only to an SVM; should include standard neural nets of comparable size (SGD-trained) and other relevant algorithms.
- Missing comparison to concurrent theoretical or restricted-architecture training methods to properly situate the contribution.
- No computational analysis: training time, memory, and resource requirements versus baselines are not reported.
- Improve clarity and flow in Section 2 to make the exposition more readable.
- Provide explicit motivation for the loss component Φ and explain the role of the epsilon term (incorporate rebuttal explanation into the paper).
- Before the formal statement, give a high-level, informal explanation of Theorem 1 and its implications.
- Clarify the convergence claim: if “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” is empirical, state it as such rather than a general fact.
