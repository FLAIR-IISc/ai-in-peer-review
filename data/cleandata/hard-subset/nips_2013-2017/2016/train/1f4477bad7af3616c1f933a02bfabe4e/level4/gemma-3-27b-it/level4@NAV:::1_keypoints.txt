- Paper proves existence of a single stationary point for a specific neural network architecture.
- Authors propose an efficient algorithm that converges to this unique fixed point, corresponding to the global minimum.
- Method relies on careful constraint design and a fixed-point/contractive-map construction to establish uniqueness beyond convexity.
- Convergence proof requires a bounded spectral-radius condition on a key matrix, limiting scalability to larger hidden layers.
- The approach introduces a large number of hyperparameters, hindering practical use and intuitive understanding.
- The spectral-radius condition must be checked for every parameter configuration.
- Empirical evaluation involved an extensive hyperparameter search (~150,000 combinations) with cross-validation.
- The method outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, casting doubt on its practical advantage and the network’s benefits.
