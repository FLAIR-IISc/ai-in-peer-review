

The paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The global optimality condition depends on network architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. While the proposal of a nonlinear spectral method for optimal training is novel and interesting, the approach imposes a strong limitation: it requires all network weights to be nonnegative. Additionally, the activation functions employed are highly specific and non-standard, which may limit the method's practical applicability. This constraint appears restrictive and is reflected in the numerical experiments, which demonstrate subpar performance relative to SVM. Minor comment: Lines 90–91 state that "the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations." This overstates the implication of linear convergence, as a linear rate does not necessarily guarantee convergence within a small number of iterations.
