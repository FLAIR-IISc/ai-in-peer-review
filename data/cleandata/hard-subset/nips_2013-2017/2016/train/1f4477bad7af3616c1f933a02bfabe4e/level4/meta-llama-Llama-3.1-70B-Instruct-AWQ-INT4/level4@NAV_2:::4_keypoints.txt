- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method that trains a class of feedforward networks with a guaranteed global optimum and linear convergence.
- Claims a practically feasible alternative to stochastic gradient descent with provable optimality.
- Paper is well-structured and provides a clear mathematical framework and convergence proof.
- Uses generalized polynomial neural networks as the function class, an interesting and distinctive choice.
- Experiments on several UCI datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness but not superiority to SVM.
- Provides a unique solution to the optimization problem, a significant advantage over existing methods.
- Shows competitiveness with methods like kernel SVM and ReLU networks in some scenarios.
- Major limitation: non-negativity constraint on network weights may be too restrictive in practice.
- Major limitation: activation functions are non-standard and specific, potentially hindering general performance.
- Numerical experiments indicate the approach does not outperform SVM on the tested datasets.
- Technical quality is high; theoretical claims and experimental results are well-supported.
- Originality is strong—method is a substantive departure from existing training approaches.
- Significance stems from guaranteed global optimality and linear convergence, relevant for various applications.
- Clarity is good: writing, notation, figures, and tables are consistent and understandable.
- Minor comment: the claim of convergence in fewer than 10 iterations is likely exaggerated given linear convergence.
- Minor comment: the paper should provide more details on activation choice and the non-negativity weight constraint.
- Overall recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the noted comments.
