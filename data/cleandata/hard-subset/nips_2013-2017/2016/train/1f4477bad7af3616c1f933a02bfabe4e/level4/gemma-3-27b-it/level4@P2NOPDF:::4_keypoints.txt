- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for training a class of feedforward networks with provable linear convergence.
- Theoretical guarantee hinges on computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix tied to the network architecture.
- Method guarantees global optimality under the specified conditions.
- Requires network weights to be nonnegative, a strong constraint likely reducing representational capacity.
- Relies on highly specific, non-standard activation functions, further limiting applicability.
- Combined weight and activation restrictions may prevent use on standard architectures and complex datasets.
- Empirical results are underwhelming: performance is not competitive with SVMs on tested datasets.
- Claim of very fast convergence (typically <10 iterations) is overly optimistic because the convergence constant matters.
- Overall: interesting theoretical contribution but practical utility and broader impact are doubtful without relaxing constraints or further empirical validation.
