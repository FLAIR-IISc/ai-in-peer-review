- Paper proposes a provably convergent training method for a class of feed-forward networks and applies it to several real-world problems; the problem and theoretical advance are interesting.
- The paper does not convincingly demonstrate the practical utility of the proposed network class and admits limited expressiveness.
- Authors should discuss what the model class can and cannot represent, including practical and theoretical limitations.
- Missing discussion and comparison with concurrent approaches in the literature.
- Experiments are narrow: comparisons only against an SVM, omitting other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithms.
- Lack of information about computational resources (time, memory) required by the compared algorithms.
- Presentation quality drops after the introduction; Section 2 is hard to follow and lacks fluency.
- Motivation for the function Φ is unclear; the role of the epsilon term should be explicitly explained in the paper.
- Theorem 1 is difficult to parse and needs a preceding informal explanation or intuition.
- The claim that the method “converges quickly in a few (less than 10) iterations” is empirical and should be stated as such rather than as a general theoretical claim.
- Typo: Line 60 "polyomial" → "polynomial".
- Typo/phrasing: Line 67 "...one model..." → "...one can model...".
- Notation issues: p1 and p2 are not introduced; R_{++} is not defined though used in variable definitions.
