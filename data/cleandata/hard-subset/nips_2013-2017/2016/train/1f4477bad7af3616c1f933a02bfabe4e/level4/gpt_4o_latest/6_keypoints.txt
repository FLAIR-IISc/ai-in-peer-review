- Paper proposes a training method for a restricted class of feed-forward networks and proves optimal convergence under certain assumptions.  
- Theoretical progress is valuable, but the practical utility and expressiveness of the restricted model are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Authors should add a discussion of the capabilities and limitations (expressiveness) of their model—this is mentioned by them twice but not analyzed.  
- Missing comparison with concurrent methods; a thorough empirical and theoretical comparison should be included.  
- Experiments do not clarify the boundaries of model applicability; more diverse benchmarks and ablations are needed.  
- Experimental baselines are weak — only SVM is used; comparisons to other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms are required.  
- Report computational resources and runtimes for all three algorithms to assess practical feasibility.  
- Presentation issues: Section 2 lacks fluency and better organization; the paper’s exposition needs polishing.  
- Important notation and definitions are unclear or missing (e.g., R_{++}, p1/p2 variables); clarify in the text.  
- The loss definition is fine, but the function Φ is not well-motivated and needs justification.  
- Include the rebuttal explanation of the epsilon term in the paper.  
- Theorem 1 needs an informal intuition before the formal statement to improve accessibility.  
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly (typically <10 iterations)” is empirical and should be explicitly labeled as observation from experiments.  
- Multiple typographical errors to fix (e.g., “polyomial” → “polynomial”; clarify “one CAN model”).
