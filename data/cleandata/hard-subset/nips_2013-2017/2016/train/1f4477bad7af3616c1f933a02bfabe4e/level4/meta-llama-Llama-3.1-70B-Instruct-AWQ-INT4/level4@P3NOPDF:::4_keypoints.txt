- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate.
- Global optimality condition depends on network architecture and is determined by the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.
- The approach is novel and offers a new perspective on neural network training.
- Requirement that network weights be non-negative is potentially too restrictive and may hinder performance.
- Use of non-standard, very specific activation functions likely limits the method’s applicability.
- Numerical experiments indicate the method performs worse than SVM, supporting concerns about effectiveness.
- Claim that linear convergence implies convergence in a few (<10) iterations is overstated and should be tempered.
- Overall, an interesting contribution but restrictive assumptions and limitations may reduce practicality; further investigation is needed.
