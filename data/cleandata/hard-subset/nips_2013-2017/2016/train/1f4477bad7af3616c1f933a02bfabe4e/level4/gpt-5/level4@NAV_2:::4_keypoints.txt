- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method yielding globally optimal training for a class of feedforward networks with linear convergence.
- Global optimality depends only on architectural parameters and reduces to checking the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.
- The use of a nonlinear spectral approach for global training appears novel and interesting.
- Method enforces nonnegativity of weights and uses unconventional, highly specialized activation functions, imposing strong constraints.
- These constraints likely limit practical effectiveness; empirical results generally do not surpass SVM baselines.
- Minor: Claim that linear convergence typically yields convergence in fewer than 10 iterations (L.90–91) is overstated.
