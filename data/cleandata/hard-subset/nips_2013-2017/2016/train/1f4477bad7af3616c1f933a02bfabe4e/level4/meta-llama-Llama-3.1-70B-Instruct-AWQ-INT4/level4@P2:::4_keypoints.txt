- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of a specific class of feedforward neural networks.
- Global optimality guarantee relies on a condition computed via the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix tied to the network architecture.
- The approach is novel and intriguing.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including convergence rate and conditions for global optimality.
- Reviewer questions the claim that linear convergence implies very few (<10) iterations.
- Empirical evaluation on several UCI datasets compares the method to RBF-kernel SVM and ReLU networks.
- Results: competitive with ReLU networks but slightly worse than kernel SVM.
- Key limitation: imposes a non-negativity constraint on weights, which may restrict applicability.
- Additional limitation: uses non-standard, very specific activation functions that may harm performance.
- Experimental results not significantly better than ReLU and inferior to SVM, suggesting practical limitations.
- Overall: interesting contributions but notable limitations; revisions required to strengthen the paper.
