- Proves a special constrained neural network has a single stationary point and constructs a contractive map whose fixed-point is the unique global minimizer.  
- Introduces a fast method that converges to that unique fixed-point (global minimum).  
- Novelty: demonstrates that a relatively complex model with carefully chosen constraints can admit uniqueness via fixed-point theory.  
- Convergence proof depends on bounding the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, limiting scalability (e.g., number of hidden units).  
- Method has many hyperparameters that must be tuned, making it unintuitive and cumbersome to use.  
- The spectral condition must be checked for every parameter choice, adding overhead and fragility.  
- Experimental exploration involved ~150,000 parameter combinations selected by cross-validation, but the model outperformed linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets.  
- Empirical results raise questions about the practical expressive power and real-world advantage of the proposed network.
