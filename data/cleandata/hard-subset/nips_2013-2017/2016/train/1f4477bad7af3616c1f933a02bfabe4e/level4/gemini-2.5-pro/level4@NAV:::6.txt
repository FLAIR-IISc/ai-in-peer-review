This paper introduces a training method for a particular class of feed-forward neural networks, subject to specific assumptions. The authors provide a proof of optimal convergence and demonstrate the method's applicability to several real-world datasets. The research problem is compelling, and theoretical contributions in this area are highly valuable. However, the paper does not sufficiently establish the practical utility of the proposed neural network class.

Specifically, since the authors repeatedly bring up the expressiveness of their restricted model, a more thorough discussion of its capabilities and limitations is warranted. An analysis of what functions are representable and which are beyond the model's scope would strengthen the paper. Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a discussion and comparison with concurrent methods in the literature.

The experimental evaluation also fails to illuminate the practical boundaries and limitations of the proposed model. The choice of baselines for comparison is surprisingly narrow; the evaluation is limited to a comparison against SVMs, neglecting other relevant methods, including alternative training techniques for neural networks and different algorithmic families. Additionally, the experimental section lacks crucial information regarding the computational costs (e.g., training time, memory usage) of the evaluated algorithms.

While the introduction is well-crafted, the quality of the presentation deteriorates in the subsequent sections. Section 2, in particular, lacks clarity and is difficult to follow. Several typographical errors were noted and are listed below.

Specific Remarks:
*   While the loss function's definition is clear, the motivation for the function $\Phi$ is not. The authors' rebuttal clarified the purpose of the epsilon term; this explanation should be incorporated into the main text.
*   Theorem 1 is difficult to parse in its current form. An informal, high-level explanation of the theorem's implications should precede the formal statement to improve readability.
*   Following Theorem 1, the claim that the nonlinear spectral method "converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations" is presented as a general property. The rebuttal indicated this is an empirical finding. The paper should explicitly state that this is an experimental observation rather than a theoretical guarantee.

Typos and Notations:
*   60: "polyomial" should be "polynomial".
*   67: "one CAN model".
*   67: The variables p1 and p2 are used without prior definition.
*   67: The notation R_{++} is not defined, which causes confusion as other definitions rely on it. While this was clarified in the rebuttal, this non-standard notation must be explicitly defined in the paper.
