- Tackles global optimization of neural network training for generalized polynomial feedforward networks with non-negative weights.  
- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method that yields global optimality with linear convergence under certain conditions.  
- Uses fixed-point theory to build a contractive map whose unique fixed point is the global minimum.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous convergence proofs and connection to Perron–Frobenius theory.  
- Claimed to be more practically feasible than prior global-optimality methods (e.g., Haeffele & Vidal) and avoids data-measure estimation required by other work.  
- Clearly presented key ideas and methodology.  
- Provides experimental validation on UCI datasets, but experiments are limited in scope.  
- Restrictive assumptions: non-negative weights and specific architecture limit generalizability.  
- Method is sensitive to parameter choices (architecture, α, ρw, ρu) and requires checking a spectral radius condition.  
- Authors performed an extensive parameter search (~150,000 combos), highlighting tuning complexity and limited intuitiveness.  
- Scalability concerns: spectral radius condition becomes harder to satisfy as hidden units increase.  
- Modest empirical gains: outperforms SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, questioning practical advantage.  
- Modified objective (adding sum of outputs) is non-standard and needs stronger justification beyond enabling theory.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, guarantees global optimality, and opens new research directions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, parameter sensitivity, scalability doubts, weak experimental superiority, and ad-hoc objective change.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution but practical limitations and limited empirical impact temper immediate significance.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better discuss limitations, justify objective modification, explore extensions, and clarify the parameter search.
