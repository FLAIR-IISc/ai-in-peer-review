The paper presents a nonlinear spectral method that provably trains a particular class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The condition ensuring global optimality depends on the network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This appears to be a novel and interesting contribution: a nonlinear spectral algorithm with global optimality guarantees for a nontrivial network class.

That said, I am concerned about the method’s scope. It requires all network weights to be nonnegative, and the admissible activation functions are nonstandard and very specific. These constraints seem quite strong and may substantially limit representational power and practical applicability. In line with this concern, the numerical results show performance that is not competitive with SVMs.

Minor comment (L.90–91): The statement “the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly, typically in a few (less than 10) iterations” is overstated. A linear convergence rate by itself does not imply convergence in a small number of iterations; the actual number depends on constants such as the contraction factor and problem conditioning, as well as initialization.
