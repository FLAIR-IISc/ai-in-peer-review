- The studied feedforward networks are highly restrictive and counterintuitive, enforcing non-negativity of weights and strong regularization.  
- The approach requires an uncommon generalized polynomial activation, further limiting practical applicability.  
- Authors claim sufficient expressive power, but this claim is not convincingly supported by the empirical results.  
- Empirical comparisons are questionable: linear SVM often outperforms NLSM when not optimally tuned, while NLSM benefits from architecture tuning.  
- Using only linear SVM as a baseline is unfair; nonlinear SVMs (polynomial/Gaussian kernels) should be included.  
- Experiments use small UCI datasets with low sample size and dimensionality, limiting conclusions about real-world performance.  
- Spectral radius bounds grow with hidden units, posing scalability issues for high-dimensional data.  
- The optimality condition is not guaranteed across all parameter settings and is expensive to verify without prior knowledge.  
- Need for methods to quickly identify a smaller set of valid candidate parameters; current approach is impractical for large parameter spaces.  
- Unclear whether global optimality is due to the specific objective; question if SGD could empirically reach the global optimum on tested datasets.
