- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method that provably and optimally trains a certain class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate.  
- Global optimality condition is architecture-dependent and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The methodological contribution is novel and interesting.  
- Major limitation: requires non-negativity constraints on network weights.  
- Major limitation: uses non-standard, highly specific activation functions, reducing generality.  
- These constraints likely limit practical performance; experiments show results not competitive with SVMs.  
- Minor comment: the claim that linear convergence implies rapid convergence (e.g., <10 iterations) is overstated.
