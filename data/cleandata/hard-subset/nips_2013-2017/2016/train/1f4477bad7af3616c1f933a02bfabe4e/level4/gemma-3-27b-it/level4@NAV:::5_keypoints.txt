- Introduces a novel feedforward network with a uniquely identifiable global optimizer and an algorithm with provable linear convergence.  
- Mathematical treatment is generally clear and illuminating but needs further clarification in places.  
- The shift from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4 is unexplained and unclear.  
- The architecture appears to treat the final layer differently from hidden layers, needing justification.  
- Gradient calculation in G^Φ(3) seems to operate on the vectorization of parameter vector u, which is not clearly explained.  
- Similar ambiguity arises for bounds on u (e.g., the ball constraint) and how they’re applied.  
- Maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters in (2) contradicts typical weight decay; the motivation and practical implications are unclear.  
- The use of a small epsilon to maintain strictly positive gradients appears to be a proof artifact; it’s unclear if it’s necessary in practice or introduces hidden degrees of freedom.  
- Restriction to non-negative data and exclusively positive weights raises concerns about practical applicability and potential unbounded accumulation.  
- Experimental comparison to SVMs is understandable but it’s unclear whether the proposed network outperforms or offers advantages over standard neural architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) in practice.
