This paper introduces a training method for a specific class of feed-forward neural networks, operating under a defined set of assumptions. The authors establish theoretical guarantees of optimal convergence and validate their approach on a set of practical problems. I find the investigated problem area to be compelling, and theoretical contributions in this direction are valuable. However, the paper doesn’t fully establish the practical utility of the proposed neural network class. The authors acknowledge limitations in the expressiveness of their restricted model, and I recommend expanding the discussion to include potential workarounds and inherent limitations of this approach. A more thorough comparison with concurrent work in the field is also needed to contextualize the contributions.

The experimental evaluation does not sufficiently illuminate the boundaries of applicability for the model. The comparison primarily focuses on Support Vector Machines, omitting relevant alternatives, including other neural network training techniques and alternative algorithmic approaches. Furthermore, reporting computational resource requirements for each algorithm would strengthen the analysis. 

While the introduction is well-crafted, the overall presentation could benefit from refinement. Specifically, Section 2 lacks clarity and flow. Several typographical errors are present (detailed below). Regarding specific points: the motivation behind the function $\Phi$ is unclear; the explanation provided in the rebuttal regarding the epsilon term should be incorporated into the paper. Theorem 1 would be more accessible with an informal explanation preceding its formal statement, as the current statement is difficult to interpret. The claim immediately following Theorem 1, concerning rapid convergence to the global maximum, is based on experimental observation and should be explicitly stated as such.
