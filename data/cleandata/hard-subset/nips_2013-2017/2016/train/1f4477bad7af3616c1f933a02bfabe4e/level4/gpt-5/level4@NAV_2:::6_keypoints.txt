- Paper proposes a method that trains a specific class of feed-forward nets to global optimality with convergence guarantees and real-world demonstrations; problem and theory are valuable.
- Reviewer doubts practical usefulness of the proposed network class and requests a clear discussion of model expressiveness (what it can and cannot capture).
- Competing approaches are not discussed or contrasted; the paper should compare to alternative methods.
- Empirical results fail to delineate the boundaries of the model’s applicability.
- Experiments compare only to a single SVM baseline; missing comparisons to other neural training schemes and algorithmic paradigms.
- Report computational resources used for all three methods in experiments.
- Presentation issues: introduction good but Section 2 reads poorly and several typographical/notation problems exist.
- Objective Φ lacks motivation; include the rebuttal’s explanation of the epsilon term in the paper.
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation preceding the formal statement because the theorem is hard to parse.
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” is empirical and should be explicitly stated as such.
- Specific typos/notation errors: “polyomial” (Line 60), “one CAN model.” (Line 67), p1 and p2 not introduced (Line 67), R_{++} not introduced (used elsewhere).
