- Summary: Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training a restricted feed-forward NN class with optimal convergence guarantees and empirical demos, but practical relevance/scope are not convincingly shown.

- Strength: Clear motivation for provably convergent NN training methods.

- Strength: Nontrivial theoretical contribution with optimal convergence guarantees.

- Strength: Empirical demonstrations on real datasets.

- Strength: Well-written introduction framing problem and contributions.

- Weakness — expressiveness: Paper fails to articulate representational limitations/capabilities; needs discussion, examples/theory, and ablation/synthetic tests probing expressiveness.

- Weakness — positioning: Related work and experiments focus on SVMs; must compare to SGD/Adam for comparable architectures, other spectral/tensor/convex methods, logistic regression, kernel methods beyond RBF, and tree ensembles.

- Weakness — experiments: Applicability limits unclear; request reporting hardware, wall-clock time, iterations, memory, scaling behavior, hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., epsilon), and dataset selection rationale.

- Theorem 1: Statement is hard to parse; add an informal explanation, simplify notation, or break into assumptions/conclusion/remarks.

- Convergence claim: “Typically <10 iterations” is empirical; explicitly state this, and support with iteration-count plots/tables and discussion of when fast convergence holds or fails.

- Motivation for Φ and epsilon: Insufficiently motivated; explain why epsilon is needed (e.g., numerical stability/positivity), how it’s chosen, and its effect on performance/convergence.

- Clarity/presentation: Reorder Section 2 (model/assumptions → loss/Φ → algorithm → theorem → proofs), add a “design choices” subsection, and provide intuition alongside formal results.

- Minor comments/typos: Fix “polyomial”→“polynomial”, “one CAN model”→“one can model”, introduce p1/p2 and define R_{++} at first use.

- Suggestions to strengthen: Provide a task taxonomy where assumptions hold/fail, add a baseline NN trained with standard gradients, include synthetic benchmarks highlighting strengths/limitations, and report runtime/memory and per-iteration/epoch complexity.

- Overall assessment: Promising work with solid theory and empirical signals that requires clearer scope/explainability, stronger baselines, and more informative experiments to be compelling.
