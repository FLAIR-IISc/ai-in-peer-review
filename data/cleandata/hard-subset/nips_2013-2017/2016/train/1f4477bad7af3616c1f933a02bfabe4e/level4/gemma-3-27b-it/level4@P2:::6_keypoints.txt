- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for globally optimizing a specific class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence under assumptions.  
- Addresses the important problem of achieving global optimality in neural network training, offering a valuable theoretical advance.  
- Practical utility is unclear due to restrictions on the model class and limited expressiveness; the paper needs a clearer discussion of capabilities and limits.  
- Builds on recent related work but lacks a comprehensive comparison with concurrent approaches in the literature.  
- Experimental evaluation shows feasibility on UCI datasets but is narrow — only compared to SVMs and missing broader neural-network and algorithmic baselines.  
- Computational cost (training time, memory) is not reported, leaving questions about scalability and resource requirements.  
- Introduction is well-written and motivating, but Section 2 suffers from clarity, minor typos, and inconsistent notation.  
- Theorem 1 is central but stated complexly; an informal explanation before the formal statement would aid understanding.  
- The claim of rapid convergence should be qualified as an empirical observation rather than a theoretical consequence.  
- The added ε term in the objective is not well-motivated in the paper itself and is only explained in the rebuttal.  
- Strengths: strong theoretical contribution (global optimality), novel methodological direction, clear motivation, and demonstrated feasibility.  
- Weaknesses: limited model expressiveness, insufficient experimental comparisons, presentation and notation issues, missing ε motivation, and no computational-cost data.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with a significant global-optimality guarantee and feasible experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical impact limited by expressiveness and narrow evaluation; presentation needs improvement.  
- Specific typos/clarity issues: “polyomial” → “polynomial”; “one CAN model” → “one can model”; p1/p2 and R_{++} undefined; ensure consistent notation.  
- Overall assessment: interesting theoretical work but practical impact unclear; reviewer leans weak reject unless experiments and discussion of limitations are substantially expanded.
