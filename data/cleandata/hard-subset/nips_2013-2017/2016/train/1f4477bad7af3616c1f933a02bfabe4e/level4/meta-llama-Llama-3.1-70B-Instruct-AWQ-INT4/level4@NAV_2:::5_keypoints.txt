- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method to train generalized polynomial neural networks with global optimality under certain conditions.  
- Main contribution: a globally optimal training method for a specific class of feedforward networks via nonnegativity constraints and a modified objective.  
- Introduces algorithm GΦ, shown to converge to the global optimum with a linear rate, offering an alternative to SGD.  
- Provides a comprehensive analytical framework and thorough mathematical derivations using nonlinear spectral methods.  
- Proof clarity issue: the transition from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4 needs clearer exposition.  
- Network design concern: treats the last layer separately from hidden layers, which may limit generality.  
- Practicality concern: requires non-negative data and all-positive weights, potentially restricting real-world applicability.  
- Epsilon term concern: adding an arbitrarily small epsilon to enforce strictly positive gradients may be unjustified in practice.  
- Experimental results are promising and competitive on several UCI datasets.  
- Baseline comparison issue: comparing to SVM is not ideal; comparisons to other neural network architectures (e.g., ReLU models) would be more relevant.  
- Evaluation: high scores for originality and technical quality, but significance could be improved with clearer practical implications and applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: innovative approach, solid math, and promising empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive assumptions on data/weights, questionable epsilon modification, and insufficiently relevant baselines.
