This paper introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for the optimal training of a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The proposed method hinges on a global optimality condition determined by the network’s architecture—specifically, the spectral radius of a small, nonnegative matrix. This approach of utilizing a nonlinear spectral method for optimal neural network training is a potentially significant contribution. However, I have concerns regarding the method’s limitations. The requirement of nonnegative weights is a notable restriction, and the use of highly specialized, non-standard activation functions further narrows the applicability of the technique. This strong limitation may negatively impact the practical performance of networks trained with this method, as evidenced by the numerical results which demonstrate performance inferior to that of Support Vector Machines. A minor point for consideration: the claim in lines 90-91 regarding rapid convergence (“typically in a few (less than 10) iterations”) seems overstated, as a linear convergence rate does not inherently guarantee fast convergence in terms of iteration count.
