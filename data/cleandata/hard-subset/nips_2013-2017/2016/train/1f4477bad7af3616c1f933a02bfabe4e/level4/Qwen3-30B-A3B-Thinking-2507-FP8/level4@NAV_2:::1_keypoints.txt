- Presents a novel globally optimal training approach for a class of feedforward nets using a nonlinear spectral method under nonnegative weights/data and specific activations.  
- Key innovation: reduces global optimality to checking the spectral radius of a small matrix, verifiable without running the algorithm.  
- Achieves theoretical linear convergence rates and requires no learning-rate tuning, offering an alternative to SGD.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior NIPS work by being more practically feasible than methods that required impractical assumptions or were hard to implement.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: well-constructed fixed-point/Thompson metric proof framework with Perron-Frobenius connections.  
- Provides explicit convergence bounds and a detailed theoretical analysis of the spectral-radius condition.  
- Experimental results show competitive performance on UCI datasets and highlight the practical advantage of having no hyperparameters.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation and logical progression from theory to experiments.  
- Major weakness: strict nonnegativity constraints on weights severely limit applicability to general networks.  
- Empirical gains are modest—outperforming linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets—raising questions about practical impact.  
- Dependence on the spectral-radius condition restricts scalability to larger networks and high-dimensional data.  
- Parameter tuning is complex and costly, requiring verification of the spectral condition across many combinations (~150,000 tested).  
- Experiments lack thorough parameter-sensitivity analysis or ablation studies to explain dataset-dependent performance.  
- Pro-accept: first practically feasible globally optimal training algorithm for a neural-net class with guarantees and linear convergence.  
- Con-accept: practical constraints, marginal empirical improvements, tuning complexity, and limitation to low-dimensional datasets reduce impact.  
- Overall: technically sound and original in theory, but constrained applicability and limited empirical evidence weaken its significance for a top-tier venue.  
- Clarity is high but technical depth may limit accessibility; originality is strong theoretically but less so as a general neural-network solution.
