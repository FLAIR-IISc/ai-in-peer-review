- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence.
- Provides a guarantee of global optimality under certain weak data assumptions.
- Global-optimality condition depends on network architecture and is verifiable via the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.
- Addresses non-convex optimization in neural networks, which is a significant contribution.
- Imposes non-negativity constraints on network weights, which may limit performance.
- Uses non-standard, very specific activation functions, restricting applicability.
- Experimental results are competitive on some UCI datasets but underperform compared to SVM.
- The claim that the method typically converges in fewer than 10 iterations is exaggerated given only a linear convergence rate.
- Quality rated 7/10: well-written but practical limitations raise concerns.
- Clarity rated 8/10: organized and easy to follow, though some technical details may challenge non-experts.
- Originality rated 9/10: the approach is novel and interesting.
- Significance rated 7/10: important theoretical guarantee but practical applicability may be limited.
