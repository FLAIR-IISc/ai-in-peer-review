- Proves a special class of neural networks has a unique stationary point.
- Proposes a fast algorithm that converges to that unique fixed-point (the global minimum).
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map to guarantee uniqueness beyond convexity.
- Convergence proof requires a spectral-radius bound on a non-trivial matrix.
- The spectral-radius constraint prevents easy scaling to networks with more hidden units.
- The method has many tunable parameters and requires checking the spectral-radius condition for each parameter choice, making it unintuitive in practice.
- Experiments searched ≈150,000 parameter combinations with cross-validation to pick the best.
- Empirically outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, questioning practical utility.
