- Paper presents a theoretical framework guaranteeing a global optimum with linear convergence for networks with non-negative inputs/weights and generalized polynomial activations, plus a nonlinear spectral optimization algorithm and experiments on small datasets.
- Strength: First potentially practical method with provable global optimality and linear convergence.
- Strength: Conditions are simple and checkable pre-training (depend only on spectral radius of a constructed non-negative matrix).
- Strength: Theory extends to arbitrary depth and yields competitive results on small real-world datasets.
- Weakness: Strong constraints—non-negative inputs/weights, generalized polynomial activations, and requirement ρ(A) < 1.
- Weakness: Non-negativity constraint on weights may greatly limit model expressive power.
- Weakness: Requiring distinct activation exponent α for each hidden unit is unconventional and may reduce practicality.
- Weakness: Scalability concern—satisfying ρ(A) < 1 may force weight norms to shrink as dimension/width increases, weakening nonlinear effects and potentially preventing scaling to deep/high-dimensional models.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance despite the concerns.
- Minor comment: Request empirical/analytical study of how ρ(A) scales with input dimension and network size and whether weight norms must decrease polynomially or exponentially to maintain ρ(A) < 1.
