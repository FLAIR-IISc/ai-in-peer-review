- Proposes a special neural-network class with a single stationary point that is the global minimum, plus a fast iterative method guaranteed to converge to it.
- Uses fixed-point theory and constructs a contractive map whose unique fixed point equals the global minimizer.
- Convergence guarantee depends on a spectral-radius boundedness condition that limits network size, expressiveness, and scalability.
- Method requires many hyperparameters and expensive tuning; the spectral condition must be checked for each hyperparameter set.
- Authors performed an extensive grid search (~150,000 configurations), highlighting practical tuning burden.
- Empirical performance is weak: the method outperformed a linear SVM baseline on only 2 of 7 datasets.
- Overall, theoretical novelty is outweighed by restrictive conditions, cumbersome tuning, and limited real-world effectiveness.
