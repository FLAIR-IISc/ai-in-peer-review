- Introduces a novel class of feedforward NNs with a proven unique global optimizer and an algorithm that finds it with linear convergence.
- Strong theoretical contribution: existence and uniqueness of a global optimum for a non-trivial neural architecture.
- Major concern: maximizing the L1-norm of hidden weights u is counterintuitive to standard regularization and seems a mathematical convenience rather than practical motivation.
- Major concern: requiring non-negative inputs and positive weights severely restricts applicability and may lose important information under transformations.
- Major concern: experiments compare only to SVM; lacks comparison to standard neural networks (e.g., ReLU) to demonstrate practical advantages or trade-offs.
- Clarification requested: explain the transition from first-order conditions in Theorem 3 to the second-order analysis in Theorem 4.
- Clarification requested: specify whether gradients w.r.t. u are computed on a vectorized u and clarify operations like bounding u within a ball.
- Clarification requested: justify the architectural choice to treat final-layer parameters separately from hidden-layer weights u.
- Summary: theoretically elegant but practical relevance is questionable; needs stronger practical justification and broader experimental validation.
