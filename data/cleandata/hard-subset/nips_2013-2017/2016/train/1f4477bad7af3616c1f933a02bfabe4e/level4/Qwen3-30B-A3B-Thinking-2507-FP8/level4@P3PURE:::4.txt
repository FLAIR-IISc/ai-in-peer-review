

The paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The global optimality condition depends solely on the network architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. This approach is novel and interesting in its formulation of a nonlinear spectral method for optimal training. However, I am concerned about significant limitations: the method requires all weights to be nonnegative, a strong constraint that may severely limit practical applicability. Furthermore, the activation functions employed are non-standard and highly specific, which likely restricts the method's generality and performance. The numerical experiments demonstrate suboptimal performance compared to SVM, supporting the concern that these limitations hinder the network's effectiveness. Minor point: Lines 90-91 state that the "nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations." This is misleading; a linear convergence rate does not guarantee convergence within a small number of iterations, as the constant factor in the convergence bound can be large.
