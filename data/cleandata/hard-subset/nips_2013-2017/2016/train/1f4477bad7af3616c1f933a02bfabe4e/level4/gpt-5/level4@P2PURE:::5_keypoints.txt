- Contribution summary: Proposes a class of feedforward NNs with a unique global optimum and an algorithm that finds it with linear convergence; math is instructive but several issues are unclear.

- Theorem jump: Why does Theorem 3 use first-order derivatives while Theorem 4 requires second-order derivatives — what necessitates this escalation?

- Layer/parameter treatment: The final layer appears treated differently from hidden layers; unclear if gradients like ∇_u G^Φ are with respect to vec(u) and how constraints (e.g., u in a ball) are applied.

- Objective (2) and ε: Why maximize the ℓ1-norm of connection parameters (counter to typical weight decay)? The small ε seems only to force strictly positive gradients for proofs and lacks practical motivation.

- Positivity and free parameters: The model forces non-negative data and strictly positive weights (no zeros) — what guarantees no free parameters and how practical is this restrictive design?

- Experiments and practical value: Comparison to SVM is odd; unclear what practical advantages this NN class provides over common architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) in real-world settings.
