- Proposes a training method for a restricted feed-forward neural network class with a proof of optimal convergence and real-world demonstrations.
- Problem is intriguing and theoretical advances are welcome, but practical utility of the proposed network class is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Authors raise expressiveness concerns about their restricted model but do not sufficiently discuss its capabilities and limitations.
- Paper lacks comparison with concurrent approaches and alternative training methods for neural networks.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only compared to SVM, omits other baselines and algorithmic paradigms.
- Experiments do not clarify the limitations or applicability range of the proposed model.
- No information provided about computational resources or runtime for the three algorithms.
- Introduction is well-written, but the rest of the presentation (notably Section 2) lacks fluency.
- Definition of loss is straightforward but the function Φ lacks motivation and explanation.
- The epsilon term’s role was only explained in rebuttal and should be included in the paper.
- Theorem 1 is difficult to parse and should be preceded by an informal explanation.
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly (typically <10 iterations)” is based on experiments and should be clearly stated as such.
- Typos and notation errors: “polyomial” (line 60), “one CAN model” (line 67), “p1 and p2” are not introduced (line 67).
- Notation issue: R_{++} is used without introduction or definition.
