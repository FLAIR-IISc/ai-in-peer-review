- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method that yields a global optimizer and linear convergence for generalized polynomial neural networks.  
- Provides clear mathematical derivations and theoretical foundations for the proposed method.  
- Main contribution: a globally optimal training algorithm for a specific class of feedforward networks under certain conditions.  
- Experimental results on several UCI datasets show promising performance and include a comparison with SVM.  
- Transition from Theorem 3 to Theorem 4 is unclear, appearing to jump from first- to second-order derivatives without sufficient explanation.  
- Network design treats the last layer separately from hidden layers, raising practicality concerns.  
- Objective includes maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters, which contradicts common weight-decay practice and lacks sufficient justification.  
- The epsilon used to make gradients strictly positive is not a convincing practical justification for the 1-norm choice.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited; the paper should compare against other neural network models (e.g., ReLU-based) beyond SVM.  
- Overall well-written and structured, with instructive derivations but some clarity gaps remaining.  
- Original and significant contribution to neural network training theory, offering potential practical implications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing Theorem transition, 1-norm justification, and broader experimental comparisons.
