- Paper proposes a feedforward neural network with a provable unique global optimizer and an algorithm achieving linear convergence; derivations are informative but need clearer exposition.  
- Unclear motivation for switching from first-order derivatives in Theorem 3 to second-order derivatives in Theorem 4.  
- Network treats the last layer differently from hidden layers; role and computation of the gradient of u in G^Φ (3) (vectorized u representing hidden-layer connections) need clarification.  
- Questions about bounding u within a ball and how that interacts with the network parameterization.  
- Maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters in (2) contradicts common weight-decay practice; rationale for this objective is unclear.  
- The small epsilon added in (2) seems a mathematical fix to ensure strictly positive gradients, but its practical implications are unclear.  
- The model claims no free parameters; this assertion requires further justification.  
- Requiring non-negative data and strictly positive weights may limit real-world applicability.  
- Experimental comparison to SVM is interesting but it’s unclear if the proposed network offers advantages over standard architectures (e.g., ReLU networks).
