- Proposes a specialized class of feedforward networks with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm that converges linearly.  
- Mathematical derivations are generally instructive but several technical clarifications are needed.  
- Theorem 3 uses first-order derivative inequalities while Theorem 4 relies on second-order derivatives; the justification for changing derivative order is unclear.  
- Ambiguity about notation and computation: is ∇_u G^Φ in Eq. (3) taken after vectorizing matrix u, and how are constraints like bounding u in a ball applied?  
- Why does Eq. (2) maximize the 1-norm of connection parameters when standard practice (weight decay) minimizes this norm?  
- The introduction of an arbitrarily small ε in Eq. (2) appears to be a proof device to ensure strictly positive gradients (placing them in V_{++}) and lacks practical justification.  
- The model’s requirement of non-negative data and strictly positive (non-zero) weights risks unbounded accumulation and raises practicality concerns.  
- Need clarification why the positivity constraints are necessary and how they operate without free parameters or practical motivation.  
- Experimental evaluation compares to SVM (also globally optimal) but does not demonstrate clear practical advantages over conventional architectures (e.g., ReLU networks) on real-world tasks.
