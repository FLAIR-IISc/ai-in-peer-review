- Proposes a training method for a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks with a proof of optimal convergence.
- Theoretical contribution is interesting, but practical utility of the restricted model is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Authors should discuss expressiveness and limitations of their restricted model.
- Paper needs comparisons with concurrent approaches and alternative neural-network training methods.
- Experiments only compare to SVM; other baselines and algorithmic paradigms are missing.
- Report computational resources and runtimes for the three algorithms used in experiments.
- Introduction is well-written, but overall presentation (especially Section 2) lacks fluency.
- The function Φ is introduced without sufficient motivation; explain its role.
- The role of the epsilon term (clarified in rebuttal) should be added to the paper.
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement to aid comprehension.
- The claim about the nonlinear spectral method’s quick convergence should be stated as an empirical observation.
- Several minor corrections: fix typos and wording (e.g., "polyomial" → "polynomial"; rephrase "one CAN model").
- Introduce variables p1 and p2 before use and define the notation R_{++} where referenced.
