- Proposes a training method for a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks with proven optimal convergence and some real-world demonstrations.
- Theoretical advance is valuable, but practical usefulness of the restricted model is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Authors repeatedly raise expressiveness concerns; paper should discuss what the model can and cannot represent.
- Concurrent and related approaches are not discussed or contrasted sufficiently.
- Experimental evaluation is limited—comparisons are only to SVMs and omit other neural-network training methods and paradigms.
- Experiments do not report computational resources or runtime costs for the compared algorithms.
- Presentation needs improvement (Section 2 lacks fluency) and the paper contains several typos.
- The function Φ and the role of the epsilon term lack motivation in the manuscript and the epsilon explanation from the rebuttal should be included.
- Theorem 1 is hard to parse and should be preceded by an informal explanation.
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method converges in fewer than 10 iterations is empirical and should be clarified as such.
- Strength: provides a theoretical guarantee of global optimality under relatively weak data assumptions.
- Strength: demonstrates a linear convergence rate, suggesting efficient training.
- Strength: achieves competitive performance on UCI datasets despite limited experiments.
- Weakness: restricted model class (generalized polynomial networks with non-negative weights) may limit practical applicability.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experimental validation.
