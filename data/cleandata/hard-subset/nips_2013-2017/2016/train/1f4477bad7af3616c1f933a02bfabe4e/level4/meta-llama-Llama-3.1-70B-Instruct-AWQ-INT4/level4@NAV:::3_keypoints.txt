- Paper proves existence of a global optimum with linear convergence for a class of neural nets under certain conditions.
- Main restrictions: input data and linear-layer weights must be positive, and activations must be generalized polynomials.
- Theory developed for single-hidden-layer nets and extended to arbitrary-depth networks.
- Proposes an optimization algorithm based on nonlinear spectral methods.
- Experiments: strong performance on a 2D synthetic dataset and reasonable results on small UCI datasets.
- Novelty/strength: provides practical network with optimality and convergence guarantees with simple preconditions based on the spectral radius of a nonnegative parameter matrix.
- Applicability shown for arbitrary depth and promising empirical results on small real-world datasets.
- Limitation: positivity constraint on weights may restrict model expressiveness despite input shifting being possible.
- Limitation: requirement that all hidden-layer activations be different may reduce applicability.
- Limitation: necessity that spectral radius (Theorem 1) < 1 may force parameter adjustments (increase p1,p2; decrease pw,pu) that effectively linearize units and hurt expressiveness, especially in deep networks.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance as an encouraging initial attempt.
- Minor suggestion: empirical analysis of how p1,p2,pw,pu, input dimension, and network size affect the spectral radius rho(A), since formal proof may be difficult.
