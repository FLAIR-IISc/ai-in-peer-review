The paper "Globally Optimal Training of Generalized Polynomial Neural Networks with Nonlinear Spectral Methods" presents a novel approach to training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a guaranteed global optimum and a linear convergence rate. The authors' contribution to the field of neural networks is noteworthy, as they provide a practically feasible method for achieving global optimality, which is a significant improvement over existing stochastic gradient descent methods.

The paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their nonlinear spectral method, including the mathematical framework and the proof of convergence. The use of generalized polynomial neural networks as the function class is an interesting choice, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on several UCI datasets.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unique solution to the optimization problem, which is a significant advantage over existing methods. The authors also demonstrate the competitiveness of their approach with respect to other methods, such as kernel SVM and ReLU networks.

However, there are some limitations to the approach. The condition of non-negativity on the weights of the network may be too restrictive, and the choice of activation functions is non-standard and specific. These limitations may hinder the good performance of neural networks, as evidenced by the numerical experiments, which show that the approach does not outperform SVM.
