- Contributions: defines a specific feedforward architecture, provides an algorithm guaranteed to find the unique global optimum, and proves linear convergence with solid mathematical derivations.  
- Concern — L1-norm maximization: maximizing the L1 norm of weights is counter-intuitive versus typical regularization, appears to be a technical device using epsilon to force positivity, and lacks a strong ML-motivated justification.  
- Concern — model constraints practicality: requiring non-negative inputs and strictly positive weights severely limits applicability and forces the network to learn only monotonic functions, reducing expressive power.  
- Concern — experimental comparisons: experiments only compare to an SVM; need benchmarks against standard non-convex neural networks (e.g., MLPs/ReLU) to demonstrate practical benefits of the guaranteed optimum.  
- Question — Theorems 3→4 transition: clarify why the analysis moves from first-order derivative conditions in Theorem 3 to second-order conditions in Theorem 4 and what necessitates that shift.  
- Question — treatment of weight matrices: clarify whether gradients for the final-layer matrix u are treated as vectorized, and specify the exact vector/matrix norms and constraint operations used throughout.  
- Recommendation: discuss implications of the positivity/non-negativity assumptions or identify real-world domains where they naturally hold.  
- Summary: theoretically interesting but practical relevance is questionable; expand experimental evaluation and strengthen the justification for the objective and constraints.
