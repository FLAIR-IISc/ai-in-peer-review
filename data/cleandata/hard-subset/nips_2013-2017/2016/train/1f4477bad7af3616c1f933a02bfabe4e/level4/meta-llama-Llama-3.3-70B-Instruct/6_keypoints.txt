- Presents a method for training a restricted class of feed-forward networks with a proof of optimal convergence.
- Demonstrates applicability to several real-world problems, addressing an interesting and worthwhile topic.
- Falls short in convincingly demonstrating the practical utility of the restricted neural network class.
- Authors themselves raise concerns about model expressiveness twice; the paper needs a fuller discussion of capabilities and limitations.
- Worse-than-needed experimental comparisons: only SVM is used, omitting other neural-network training methods and algorithmic baselines.
- Experimental results lack clarity about the model’s limitations, applicability, and regimes where it fails or succeeds.
- Missing information on computational resources and runtime requirements for the proposed algorithms.
- Introduction is well-written, but the rest of the presentation needs improvement—Section 2 is not fluent.
- Multiple typos and notation issues (e.g., “polyomial”, unexplained R{++}, and variables p1, p_2).
- Theorem 1 should be prefaced by an informal explanation to aid readability.
- The motivation for the function Φ and the role of the epsilon term must be clarified in the paper (not only in the rebuttal).
- The claim about nonlinear spectral method convergence after Theorem 1 should be explicitly phrased as an experimental observation if that is the case.
