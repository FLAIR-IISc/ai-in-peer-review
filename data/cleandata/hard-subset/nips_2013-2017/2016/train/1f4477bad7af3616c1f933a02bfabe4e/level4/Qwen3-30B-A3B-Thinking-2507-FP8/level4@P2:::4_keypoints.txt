- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for training feedforward NNs with global optimality under nonnegative weights and generalized polynomial activations; linear convergence and optimality depend on the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.

- Method avoids needing the data-generating measure (unlike prior tensor/structured-matrix approaches) and is presented as practically feasible with linear convergence.

- Theoretical strength: rigorous proofs of global optimality and linear convergence, leveraging Perron–Frobenius and fixed-point theory; supplementary proofs enhance credibility.

- Originality: first practical application of nonlinear spectral methods to obtain global guarantees for non-trivial NN models under specified constraints.

- Significance: provides important theoretical insight into NN optimization and a simple pre-training check (spectral radius); requires no hyperparameter tuning.

- Practical limitation: nonnegativity constraint on weights is restrictive, reducing expressivity and lacking strong justification; empirical performance is generally worse than SVM/ReLU.

- Experimental weakness: evaluation limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets with insufficient analysis of parameter selection (n1, α, ρw, ρu).

- Convergence claim overstated: while rate is linear, actual speed depends on spectral radius (which grows with hidden units) and no concrete dataset-specific rates are shown.

- Additional cons: limited to shallow architectures, uses constrained/non-standard activations, and practical impact is questionable given performance trade-offs.

- Recommendation: weak accept — significant and novel theoretical contribution but substantial practical drawbacks; paper should discuss trade-offs, parameter selection, and potential relaxation of nonnegativity in future work.
