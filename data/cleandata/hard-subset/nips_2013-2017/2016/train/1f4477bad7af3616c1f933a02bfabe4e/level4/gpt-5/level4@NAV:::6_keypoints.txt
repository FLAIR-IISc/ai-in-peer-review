- Presents a training method (under assumptions) for a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks and proves optimal convergence.  
- Claims applicability to “real-world” problems but practical utility of the restricted model is unconvincing.  
- Authors repeatedly raise concerns about the expressiveness of their model; paper should clearly state what it can and cannot achieve.  
- Lacks discussion and contrast with competing approaches and alternative training methods for neural networks.  
- Experiments are insufficient to clarify the model’s applicability limits and comparisons are limited to SVM baselines.  
- Omits comparison to other algorithmic paradigms and alternative NN training methods.  
- Missing information about computational resources required by the three algorithms.  
- Introduction is strong, but later presentation (notably Section 2) is unclear and could be improved.  
- Several typos and notation issues that need fixing.  
- The function Φ lacks motivation; the role of the ε-term (explained in rebuttal) should be added to the paper.  
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement to aid readability.  
- The claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly… in a few (less than 10) iterations” is based on experiments and should be clarified as such.  
- Specific issues: “polyomial” (p.60); phrasing “one CAN model” (p.67); p1 and p2 not introduced (p.67); R_{++} not defined.
