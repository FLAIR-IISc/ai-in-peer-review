- Summary: Proposes a method to train a restricted class of feed-forward networks with proven optimal convergence and demonstrations on real problems; interesting theoretical contribution.

- Practical relevance and expressiveness: Paper does not convincingly show the proposed model is useful in practice; needs a clear discussion of what the approach can and cannot represent given its restrictions.

- Relation to concurrent work: Concurrent approaches (other NN training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms) are not discussed or contrasted and should be.

- Empirical evaluation: Experiments fail to clarify limits of applicability, compare only to some SVMs (omitting other NN methods and paradigms), and omit details on computational resources used.

- Presentation flow: Introduction is strong but later sections (notably Section 2) are not fluent and need improved presentation.

- Loss function and Φ: The loss is straightforward but the function Φ lacks motivation; the role of the ε term (explained in the rebuttal) should be included in the paper.

- Theorem 1 exposition: Theorem 1 should be preceded by an informal explanation; the current formal statement is hard to parse.

- Convergence claim: The statement that the nonlinear spectral method typically converges in few (≤10) iterations is based on empirical observation and should be explicitly presented as such.

- Typo (Line 60): "polyomial" should be "polynomial".

- Ambiguous phrase (Line 67): "one CAN model" is unclear and should be corrected or clarified.

- Missing definitions (Line 67): p1 and p2 are not introduced.

- Missing notation (Line 67): R_{++} is not defined and the notation is unfamiliar; introduce it.
