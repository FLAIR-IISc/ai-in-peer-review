- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence.
- Global optimality condition reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix dependent on network architecture.
- The nonlinear spectral approach is new and interesting for this class of networks.
- Major limitation: requires non-negativity constraints on network weights.
- Major limitation: uses very specific, non-standard activation functions.
- These restrictions may strongly hinder the network’s practical performance.
- Empirical results show performance not significantly better than SVM, suggesting practical limitations.
- Minor concern: claiming “linear convergence rate… typically in a few (less than 10) iterations” is exaggerated, since linear convergence does not guarantee very few iterations.
