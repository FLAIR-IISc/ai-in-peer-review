- Paper proposes a training method with provable convergence for a restricted feed-forward network class and shows some real-world applications.  
- Theoretical advance is interesting, but the practical usefulness of the restricted model is not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Authors raise expressiveness concerns but fail to analyze what their model can or cannot represent.  
- Lacks discussion of concurrent/related approaches and contrasts with the proposed method.  
- Experimental results do not clarify applicability limits of the model.  
- Comparisons are insufficient: only SVM baselines used; other neural-network training methods and algorithmic paradigms are ignored.  
- No information on computational resources or runtime requirements for the algorithms.  
- Presentation issues: Section 2 reads awkwardly and the paper contains typos.  
- The function Φ is introduced without sufficient motivation.  
- The role of the epsilon term was explained in rebuttal and should be added to the paper.  
- Theorem 1 should be preceded by an informal explanation because its statement is hard to parse.  
- The claim about rapid convergence (typically <10 iterations) is based on experiments and should be explicitly stated as such.  
- Minor errors to fix: "polyomial" → "polynomial"; rephrase "one CAN model"; introduce p1 and p2; define R_{++}.  
- Overall: interesting ideas but needs clearer presentation, more thorough experiments, and additional comparisons to increase impact.
