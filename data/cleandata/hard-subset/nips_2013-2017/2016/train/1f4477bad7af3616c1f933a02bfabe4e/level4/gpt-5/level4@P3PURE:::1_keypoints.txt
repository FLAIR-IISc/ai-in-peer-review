- Studies a restricted class of neural networks and proves the cost function has a single stationary point under the proposed constraints.  
- Proposes a fast algorithm that provably converges to the unique fixed point, which is the global minimum.  
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose fixed point is the unique minimizer.  
- Convergence proof relies on bounding the spectral radius of a nontrivial matrix.  
- Spectral-radius requirement prevents experiments with networks having more hidden units.  
- Method requires tuning a large number of parameters, is unintuitive, and the spectral condition must be re-verified for each setting.  
- Empirically evaluated ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected models via cross-validation.  
- Method outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, casting doubt on its practical effectiveness.
