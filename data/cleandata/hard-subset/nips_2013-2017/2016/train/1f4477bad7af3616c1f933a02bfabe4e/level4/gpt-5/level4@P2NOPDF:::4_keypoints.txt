- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method that provably trains a specific class of feedforward networks with linear convergence.  
- Novel reduction: global optimality condition checks the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- Conceptually interesting and elegant: spectral methods give explicit, verifiable global-optimality guarantees.  
- Condition’s clarity and potential practicality within its intended scope is appealing.  
- Applicability is limited by required nonnegativity of weights and highly specific, nonstandard activation functions.  
- These restrictions likely reduce expressive power compared to common architectures and may hurt real-world performance.  
- Empirical results show the method underperforms standard SVM baselines, reinforcing practical concerns.  
- Recommend the paper discuss the trade-off between strong theory and restrictive modeling, and clarify suitable application domains.  
- Minor critique: claiming linear convergence implies very few iterations is overstated; actual iteration counts depend on contraction factors—provide tempered claims or empirical iteration data.  
- Overall: original and theoretically solid but practically constrained; needs clearer scope, tempered claims, and stronger empirical support.
