- Paper proves a specific neural network class has a unique stationary point and presents a fast method that converges to this fixed point (the global minimum).
- Demonstrates that a relatively complex model, with careful constraints, can admit only one stationary point.
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose fixed point is the unique minimizer.
- Convergence proof depends on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, limiting applicability (e.g., prevents networks with more hidden units).
- Method involves many tunable parameters and requires verifying the spectral condition for each parameter choice, making it unintuitive.
- Experiments searched ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected the best via cross-validation.
- Empirical performance is weak: method outperformed linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, casting doubt on the model’s practical capabilities.
