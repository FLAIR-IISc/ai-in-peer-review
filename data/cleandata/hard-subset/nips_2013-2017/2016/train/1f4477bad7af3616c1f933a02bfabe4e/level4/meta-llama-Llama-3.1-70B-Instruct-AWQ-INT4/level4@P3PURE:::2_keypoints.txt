- The paper studies a restrictive class of feedforward networks with non-negativity and heavy weight regularization plus generalized polynomial activations, which is counterintuitive and needs real-world justification.

- The claimed expressive power is not supported by experiments; linear SVM sometimes outperforms NLSM1/NLSM2, undermining the authors' claims.

- Baseline selection is weak/unfair — using only linear SVM rather than nonlinear SVMs (e.g., polynomial or RBF kernels) makes comparisons unconvincing.

- UCI experiments are limited (small sample sizes and low dimensionality), raising questions about generalization to larger, high-dimensional datasets.

- Bounds on the spectral radius grow with the number of hidden units, creating potential scalability issues that require discussion and possible remedies.

- The optimality condition may not be achievable for practical parameter settings; although checkable, finding valid priors or candidate parameters in large spaces is problematic and needs fast approaches.

- It should be examined whether global optimality arises from the special objective function, and whether standard methods like SGD can empirically reach global optima on tested datasets.
