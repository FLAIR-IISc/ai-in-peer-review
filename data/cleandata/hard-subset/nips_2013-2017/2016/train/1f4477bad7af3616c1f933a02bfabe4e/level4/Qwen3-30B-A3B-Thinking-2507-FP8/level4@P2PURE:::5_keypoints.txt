- Paper proposes a class of feedforward nets with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm that computes it with linear convergence.  
- Mathematical derivations are generally instructive but require clarifications.  
- Why does Theorem 3 analyze first-order derivatives while Theorem 4 shifts to second-order — what motivates that progression?  
- Final layer is treated differently from hidden layers; gradients w.r.t. matrix u seem derived from a vectorized form, creating ambiguity.  
- Additional ambiguities include constraints like bounding u within a ball and how those are applied.  
- Major concern: Equation (2) maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters, contrary to typical weight-decay minimization—this choice is unexplained.  
- The tiny ε in Equation (2) appears to be a proof device to force strictly positive gradients, not a principled design choice, and the model has no free parameter justification.  
- The requirement for non-negative data and strictly positive (nonzero) weights forces perpetual accumulation of contributions — practicality of this constraint is questionable.  
- Experiments compare to SVM but do not show clear advantages over standard architectures (e.g., ReLU networks); theoretical guarantees aren’t shown to yield empirical gains.
