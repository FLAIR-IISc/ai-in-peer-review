- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a provable linear convergence rate.
- Global optimality condition depends on network-architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.
- Using a nonlinear spectral method for global optimality in this network class is novel and interesting.
- Method requires non-negativity constraints on network weights, which is a strong and restrictive assumption.
- Activation functions are non-standard and highly specialized, further limiting generality.
- Combined weight and activation constraints likely hinder practical performance and applicability of the networks.
- Numerical experiments show performance that is not competitive with SVMs.
- Minor critique: the claim that linear convergence implies convergence in a few (<10) iterations is overstated; linear rate alone does not guarantee that.
