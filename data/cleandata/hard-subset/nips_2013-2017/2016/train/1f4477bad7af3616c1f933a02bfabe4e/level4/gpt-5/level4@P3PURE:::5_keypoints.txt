- Paper defines a class of feedforward networks with a unique global optimizer and proposes an algorithm that provably finds it with linear convergence.

- Strength: clear, ambitious goal to characterize a network class with a unique global optimum.

- Strength: linear convergence guarantee is appealing and derivations are generally insightful.

- Clarification needed: relationship between Theorem 3 (first-order inequalities) and Theorem 4 (second-order reasoning); why second-order arguments are necessary and whether Theorem 3’s inequalities are insufficient.

- Clarification needed: precise treatment of last-layer parameter u — is the gradient taken w.r.t. vectorized u, and are constraints/norms on u using Frobenius (vectorized) or another norm; consistently state shapes and norms.

- Modeling concern: objective (2) maximizes the L1 norm of parameters, which contradicts common practice (weight decay); the small ε appears proof-motivated to enforce positive gradients — justify empirically or provide an alternative that preserves theory.

- Modeling concern: claim of “no free parameter” is misleading because ε and imposed norm/positivity constraints effectively act as hyperparameters and should be clarified.

- Modeling concern: positivity constraints and requirement of strictly positive weights and nonnegative data limit practicality — concerns about handling signed inputs, inhibitory interactions, sparsity, and representation power.

- Experimental concern: comparison to SVM is reasonable for global optimality, but the paper should compare to standard neural nets (e.g., ReLU MLPs) to demonstrate practical advantages (accuracy, robustness, calibration, sample efficiency, stability).

- Suggestion: clarify necessity of second-order arguments in proofs, and explicitly connect first- and second-order conditions.

- Suggestion: make parameter/vectorization/norm conventions explicit throughout the paper.

- Suggestion: explain role of ε and V_{++}, justify L1 maximization beyond proof convenience, and discuss when the all-positive design is advantageous.

- Suggestion: expand experiments to include standard ReLU networks of similar capacity to evaluate whether theoretical guarantees yield practical benefits.
