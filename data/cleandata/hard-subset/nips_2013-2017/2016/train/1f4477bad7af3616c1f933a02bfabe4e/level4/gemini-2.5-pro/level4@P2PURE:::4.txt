This paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a provable linear convergence rate. The condition for guaranteeing global optimality depends on the network's architectural parameters and can be evaluated by computing the spectral radius of a small, non-negative matrix.

While the proposed method is novel and interesting, I have significant concerns about its practical limitations. The approach imposes a non-negativity constraint on all network weights and relies on non-standard, highly specific activation functions. These are strong restrictions that are likely to hinder the expressive power and performance of the resulting neural network. This concern appears to be validated by the numerical experiments, where the proposed method's performance is inferior to that of an SVM.

Minor comment: The claim on L.90-91 that the method "converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations" due to its linear convergence rate seems exaggerated. A linear convergence rate does not necessarily guarantee convergence in such a small number of iterations.
