- Presents a novel training approach for feedforward nets with guaranteed global optimality for networks with nonnegative weights and real-power (generalized polynomial) activations.  
- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method (NLSM) that achieves global convergence with a linear rate under a checkable spectral radius condition.  
- Builds on prior global optimization work but claims greater practical feasibility than tensor-based or other complex methods.  
- Requires only nonnegative data (common in images, word frequencies), avoiding the need for the unknown data-generating measure.  
- Develops a theoretical framework linking the optimization to fixed-point theory on convex cones using Perron-Frobenius for multihomogeneous problems.  
- Key technical idea: impose nonnegativity and add a sum-of-outputs term to create a contractive mapping whose unique fixed point is the global maximizer.  
- Method avoids hyperparameter tuning typical of SGD because convergence can be guaranteed a priori via spectral radius computation.  
- Main theoretical strength: rigorous proof of global optimality and linear convergence for a nontrivial class of neural networks.  
- Practical limitation: nonnegativity constraint on weights is restrictive and may reduce representational capacity.  
- Requires careful selection of several parameters (hidden units, activation powers, normalization constants) to satisfy ρ(A) < 1.  
- Parameter tuning can be extensive in practice (authors tested ~150,000 parameter combinations).  
- Empirical results are modest: competitive on UCI datasets but outperform kernel SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets and generally slightly worse than SVM.  
- Performance is competitive with ReLU networks but constrained compared to unconstrained models.  
- Scalability concern: spectral radius condition may prevent extension to deeper networks.  
- Strengths summarized: technically sound proofs, clear presentation, and first practically feasible method with provable global optimality yielding nontrivial classification results.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions, urging better discussion of when nonnegativity is reasonable and whether the spectral condition can be relaxed.
