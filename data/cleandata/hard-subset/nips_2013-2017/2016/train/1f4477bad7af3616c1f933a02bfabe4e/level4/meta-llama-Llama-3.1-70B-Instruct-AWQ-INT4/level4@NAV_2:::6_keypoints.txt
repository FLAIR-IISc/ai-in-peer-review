- Proposes a new method to train a specific class of feed-forward neural networks with proven optimal convergence under certain assumptions.  
- Theoretical advance is appreciated and seen as significant for neural network training.  
- Demonstrates applicability on a selection of "real world" problems but practical relevance is questioned.  
- Clear, well-written introduction that effectively conveys the problem and significance.  
- Later presentation has issues: Section 2 reads poorly and there are typos throughout the paper.  
- Originality is strong, but the significance is uncertain due to limited experimental evaluation.  
- Experimental comparison to other methods is not comprehensive, limiting claims about practicality.  
- Practical applicability of the proposed class of networks is unclear; experiments do not clarify limitations.  
- Loss definition is straightforward but the function Φ lacks motivation; the role of the epsilon term should be added to the paper.  
- Theorem 1 needs an informal explanation before the formal statement, as the current statement is hard to parse.  
- The empirical claim about quick convergence of the nonlinear spectral method should be clearly labeled as experimental observation.  
- Notation issues: R_{++} is not introduced, and other definitions rely on it, causing confusion.  
- Recommend adding discussion of what the approach can/cannot do, contrasting with concurrent methods, and detailing computational resource requirements.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, clear summary relating to prior work, and strong introductory exposition; arguments against: limited experiments, insufficient computational details, and typos/notation problems.
