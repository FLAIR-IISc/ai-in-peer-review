- Paper defines a feedforward network class with a unique global optimizer and gives an algorithm with linear convergence; math is instructive but needs clarification.
- Why does Theorem 3 rely on first-order derivatives while Theorem 4 moves to second-order derivatives — the motivation for this shift is unclear.
- The final layer appears treated differently from hidden layers; clarify whether the gradient w.r.t. u is computed over vectorized u and how u is bounded (e.g., within a ball).
- Maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters in (2) contradicts common weight-decay practice and seems driven by proof requirements rather than practical justification.
- Using an arbitrarily small epsilon to enforce strictly positive gradients and ensure parameters lie in V_{++} feels ad hoc and may hide a degree of freedom without practical rationale.
- Requiring non-negative data and strictly positive (nonzero) weights leads to continuous accumulation; practicality of these constraints is questionable.
- Experimental comparison to SVMs is odd; it’s unclear whether the proposed network offers real advantages over common neural nets (e.g., ReLU-based) in practice.
