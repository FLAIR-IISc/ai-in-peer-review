- Paper proves a specific class of neural networks has a single stationary point and provides a fast convergence method to that unique fixed-point (the global minimum).
- Interesting theoretical contribution: shows a relatively complex model can exhibit a unique stationary point under carefully chosen constraints, using fixed-point theory beyond convexity.
- Method constructs a contractive map whose fixed-point matches the unique minimizer of the cost function.
- Key limitation: convergence proof relies on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, restricting applicability to networks with few hidden units.
- Practical drawback: the method requires tuning many parameters and checking the spectral condition for each parameter configuration, making it unintuitive to apply.
- Experimental setup explored ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected the best via cross-validation.
- Empirical performance is weak: the method beat a linear SVM on only 2 out of 7 datasets, questioning the practical benefit of the architecture.
