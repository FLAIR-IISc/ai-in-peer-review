- Proves existence of a single stationary point (unique fixed-point) for a constrained class of neural networks.  
- Presents a fast convergence method to that unique fixed-point, which is the global minimizer of the cost.  
- Novel application of fixed-point theory and construction of a contractive map to guarantee global optimality.  
- Theoretical construction and presentation are clear.  
- Convergence proof relies on a restrictive bounded spectral radius condition for a non-trivial matrix.  
- Spectral radius constraint limits scalability to networks with few hidden units.  
- Method requires many tunable parameters, reducing practical usability and intuitive appeal.  
- Spectral radius condition must be verified for each parameter setting, adding computational overhead.  
- Extensive experimental tuning (~150,000 parameter combinations) produced limited empirical gains.  
- Method outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 out of 7 datasets, questioning practical advantage.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution but diminished impact due to scalability and weak empirical results; recommend acceptance with major revisions or rejection otherwise.  
- Questions for authors: can the spectral radius constraint be relaxed, can tuning be automated, what explains success on the two datasets, and can spectral verification be made more efficient?
