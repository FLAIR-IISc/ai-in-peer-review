- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a class of feedforward neural networks with a provable linear convergence rate.  
- Global optimality condition depends only on network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The method is novel and promising for this specific training task.  
- Requires non-negative weights, which is a restrictive assumption.  
- Uses highly specific, non-standard activation functions, further limiting generality.  
- These restrictions likely curtail practical applicability and real-world use.  
- Numerical results show worse performance than SVM on tested problems.  
- Minor critique: claiming convergence "in a few (<10) iterations" is overstated—linear convergence does not guarantee such fast iteration counts.
