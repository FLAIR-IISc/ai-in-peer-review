- Introduces a specific class of neural networks and proves a single stationary point that is the global minimum of the cost.
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose unique fixed point is the global minimizer.
- Proposes an efficient iterative algorithm with guaranteed convergence to that fixed point.
- Main contribution is theoretical: establishing a unique global minimum for a non-convex model.
- Convergence proof requires a strict spectral radius bound on a particular matrix, limiting applicability.
- The spectral radius constraint prevents exploring larger hidden-layer sizes, restricting model capacity.
- Method involves many hyperparameters that need careful tuning, making it unintuitive to apply.
- The spectral radius must be checked for every hyperparameter combination, complicating model selection.
- Empirically, after ~150,000 hyperparameter trials the model outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets.
- Conclusion: theoretical result is sound but practical utility and representational power remain limited.
