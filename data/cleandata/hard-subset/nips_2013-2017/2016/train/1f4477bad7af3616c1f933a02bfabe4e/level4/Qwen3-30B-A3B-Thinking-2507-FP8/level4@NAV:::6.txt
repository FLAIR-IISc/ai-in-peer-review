

The paper introduces a method for training a specific class of feed-forward neural networks under defined assumptions, establishing optimal convergence guarantees and demonstrating applicability to selected "real-world" problems. The investigated problem is of interest, and theoretical progress in this area is commendable. However, the paper does not sufficiently establish the practical utility of the proposed neural network class. Specifically, given the authors' repeated discussion of the model's expressiveness limitations, I recommend elaborating on achievable outcomes and inherent constraints of their approach. Additionally, concurrent methods should be explicitly compared and contrasted. The experimental section fails to clarify the model's practical boundaries; notably, the authors limit comparisons to SVMs while omitting other neural network training methods and alternative algorithmic paradigms. Computational resource requirements for all three algorithms should also be reported. The introduction is well-crafted, though the presentation requires refinement, particularly in Section 2 which lacks fluency. Minor issues include typos and undefined notation:  
- "polyomial" (line 60)  
- "one CAN model" (line 67)  
- Undefined variables p₁, p₂ (line 67)  
- Undefined set ℝ₊₊ (line 67), referenced multiple times  
The loss function definition is clear, but Φ lacks motivation; the epsilon term's role (clarified in the rebuttal) should be included in the main text. Theorem 1 requires an informal explanation prior to its formal statement, as the current phrasing is difficult to parse. The subsequent claim about "quick convergence typically in fewer than 10 iterations" (immediately after Theorem 1) relies on experimental observations and should be explicitly qualified as such.
