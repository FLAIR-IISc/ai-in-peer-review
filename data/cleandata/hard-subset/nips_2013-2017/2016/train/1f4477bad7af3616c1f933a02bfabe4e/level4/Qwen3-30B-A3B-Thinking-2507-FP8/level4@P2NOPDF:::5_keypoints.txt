- Introduces a class of feedforward networks with a unique global minimum and an algorithm with linear convergence; math is largely sound but needs clarifications.  
- The jump from first-order analysis (Theorem 3) to second-order analysis (Theorem 4) is unjustified and requires explicit motivation for higher-order terms.  
- Ambiguity in parameter handling: unclear if the final-layer parameter u is treated as a matrix or vectorized in gradient computations and constraints; needs formal matrix/vector calculus.  
- Maximizing the 1-norm of weights (Equation 2) contradicts standard regularization, appears to be a technical artifact (via ε) and risks unbounded weights and poor generalization.  
- The non-negative data and strictly positive-weight assumptions are highly restrictive and not addressed for relaxation or necessity in practice.  
- Lack of empirical validation on datasets that violate the model’s assumptions undermines practical relevance.  
- Experimental comparison to SVM is inappropriate and insufficient; fails to compare against standard neural architectures (e.g., ReLU networks).  
- Paper does not show clear advantages over existing methods or how to handle practical issues, making it appear more theoretical than useful.  
- Recommend sharper exposition, rigorous justification of assumptions, or empirical demonstrations on non-trivial datasets to establish real-world utility.
