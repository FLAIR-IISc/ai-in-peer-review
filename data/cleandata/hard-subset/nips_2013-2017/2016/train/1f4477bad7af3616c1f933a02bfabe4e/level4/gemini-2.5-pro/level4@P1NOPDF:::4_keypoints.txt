- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a proven linear convergence rate.
- Provides a theoretical global-optimality condition verifiable by computing the spectral radius of a small non-negative matrix.
- The spectral-method approach achieving provable optimal training for this network class is novel and interesting.
- Major practical limitation: requires all network weights to be non-negative.
- Major practical limitation: depends on non-standard, highly specific activation functions.
- These constraints together are highly restrictive, likely reducing expressive power and predictive performance; experiments show the method underperforms a standard SVM.
- Minor critique: the claim of convergence “in a few (less than 10) iterations” is exaggerated, since linear convergence alone doesn’t guarantee such a small iteration count without favorable constants.
