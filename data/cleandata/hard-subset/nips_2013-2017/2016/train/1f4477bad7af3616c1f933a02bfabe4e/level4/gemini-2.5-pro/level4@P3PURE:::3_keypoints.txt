- Presents theory showing a class of neural nets has a global optimum reachable with linear convergence under positivity and generalized-polynomial activations.  
- Theory proven for single-hidden-layer and extended to multi-layer networks.  
- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method optimization algorithm.  
- Experiments show strong performance on a 2D synthetic dataset and reasonable results on small UCI datasets.  
- Contribution: one of the first potentially practical NN frameworks with both optimality and convergence guarantees.  
- Preconditions are relatively simple and depend only on the spectral radius of a non-negative parameter matrix.  
- Limitation: requires all inputs and linear-layer weights to be positive, and activations to be generalized polynomials.  
- Unconventional requirement that each hidden layer use a different activation may limit applicability.  
- Concern: meeting the spectral-radius condition may force smaller linear weights and larger exponents, leading activations to behave nearly linearly and reducing expressiveness in deep/wide networks.  
- Minor suggestion: empirically study how spectral radius ρ(A) scales with hyperparameters (p1,p2,pw,pu), input dimension, and layer sizes (e.g., whether ρ grows linearly or exponentially and how weights must be scaled).
