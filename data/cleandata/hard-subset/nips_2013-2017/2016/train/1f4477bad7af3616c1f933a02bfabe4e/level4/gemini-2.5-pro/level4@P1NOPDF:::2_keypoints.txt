1. Paper proposes a nonlinear spectral method (NLSM) for certain feedforward networks with provable global optimality and linear convergence; evaluated on 1–2 hidden-layer networks and real datasets.

2. Theoretical guarantees rely on strong, counterintuitive assumptions: non-negative weights and maximizing the regularization term.

3. Global optimality requires a non-standard generalized polynomial activation, making the assumptions restrictive and of unclear practical justification.

4. Authors claim sufficient expressive power, but empirical support for this claim is unconvincing.

5. Baseline comparisons are inadequate: models are only compared to a linear SVM instead of appropriate nonlinear baselines (e.g., RBF or polynomial SVM).

6. Baseline tuning is unclear and potentially unfair: NLSM architectures were tuned while it’s unclear if the SVM was properly tuned.

7. Experiments use small UCI datasets, limiting evidence for real-world applicability and scalability.

8. Scalability concern: theoretical bounds on the spectral radius grow with the number of hidden units, suggesting issues for high-dimensional or large models.

9. The optimality condition is not guaranteed for arbitrary networks and may be hard to satisfy or search for a priori across large parameter spaces.

10. Paper lacks discussion of mitigation strategies for scalability and efficient ways to identify valid parameter candidates.

11. Open question whether global optimality stems from the unusual objective; recommend ablation (e.g., test if SGD also reaches global optima) to clarify the source of benefits.

12. Overall, paper would be strengthened by clearer real-world motivation for restrictive assumptions and stronger, fairer empirical validation.
