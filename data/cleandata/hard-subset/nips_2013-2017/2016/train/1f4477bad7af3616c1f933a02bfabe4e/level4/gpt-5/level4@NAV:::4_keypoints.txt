- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a certain class of feedforward neural networks with linear convergence.  
- Global optimality condition depends on network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The method's novelty and theoretical interest are clear.  
- Method requires nonnegativity of network weights, a strong and potentially limiting constraint.  
- Uses nonstandard, highly specialized activation functions, further restricting applicability.  
- Practical performance appears limited: numerical experiments show worse results than SVMs.  
- Minor issue: claim that linear convergence implies convergence in "a few (less than 10) iterations" is overstated and unjustified.
