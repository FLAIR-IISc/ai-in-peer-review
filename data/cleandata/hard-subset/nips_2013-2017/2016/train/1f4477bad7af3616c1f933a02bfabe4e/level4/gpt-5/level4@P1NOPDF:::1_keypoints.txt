- Proves that a special class of neural networks has a single stationary point, which is the global minimum.
- Introduces a fast algorithm that converges to that unique fixed point by constructing a contractive map.
- Highlights that fixed-point theory can establish uniqueness for constrained nonconvex models beyond convexity/geodesic convexity.
- Convergence proof depends on bounding the spectral radius of a nontrivial matrix, limiting scalability to larger networks.
- Method requires tuning many hyperparameters and verifying a spectral condition for each setting, making it unintuitive.
- Experiments searched ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected the best via cross-validation.
- Empirically, the method beat a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, raising doubts about practical effectiveness.
