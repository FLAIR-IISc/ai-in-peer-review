The paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method that optimally trains a certain class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The condition ensuring global optimality depends on the network architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. Proposing a nonlinear spectral method to optimally train this specific class of feedforward networks appears new and interesting. At the same time, I am concerned about the method’s limitations: it enforces nonnegativity on the network weights, and the activation functions used are nonstandard and very specialized. These constraints seem quite strong and may hinder the practical performance of neural networks. Indeed, the numerical experiments show performance that is not as good as SVMs. Minor comment: L.90–91: The sentence stating “Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to...” seems overstated, since a linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply convergence in a few iterations.
