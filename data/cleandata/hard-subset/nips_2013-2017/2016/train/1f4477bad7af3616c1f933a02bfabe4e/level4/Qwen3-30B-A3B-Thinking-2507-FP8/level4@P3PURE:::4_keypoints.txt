- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for optimal training of a specific class of feedforward networks, claiming linear convergence.  
- Global optimality condition depends only on network architecture and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The approach is novel and interesting in its formulation of a nonlinear spectral method for optimal training.  
- Major limitation: method requires all weights to be nonnegative, which may severely limit practical applicability.  
- Uses non-standard, highly specific activation functions, likely restricting generality and performance.  
- Numerical experiments show suboptimal performance compared to SVM, supporting concerns about the method’s practical effectiveness.  
- Minor critique: stating linear convergence implies convergence in <10 iterations is misleading, since linear rate alone doesn’t guarantee a small iteration count due to constant factors.
