- Paper proposes a specialized feedforward network with a unique global optimizer and an algorithm claiming linear convergence.  
- Theorem 3 uses first-order derivative inequalities but Theorem 4 jumps to second-order derivatives without justification—this transition needs explicit rationale.  
- Ambiguity in parameterization: the final layer is treated differently, and ∇_u G^Φ in Eq.(3) should be the vectorized form of u; constraints bounding u (e.g., in Theorem 2) are unclear.  
- Objective in Eq.(2) maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters, contrary to standard weight decay practices; this choice appears driven only by the need for arbitrarily small ε to keep gradients strictly positive.  
- ε seems like a technical artifact for proofs rather than a practically justified design choice; the paper does not explain why maximizing norms is beneficial in real applications.  
- The model requires non-negative data and strictly positive (nonzero) weights, causing continuous accumulation of positive contributions and severely limiting applicability to real-world, mixed-sign datasets.  
- Experimental evaluation is inadequate: comparing only to SVM (a convex solver) is misleading—meaningful baselines would be standard neural networks (e.g., ReLU architectures).  
- Overall, missing justifications and restrictive assumptions undermine practical relevance; authors must clarify derivative-order reasoning, resolve u-gradient/vectorization ambiguity, justify 1-norm maximization and ε, relax or justify non-negativity constraints, and demonstrate concrete advantages against standard NN baselines.
