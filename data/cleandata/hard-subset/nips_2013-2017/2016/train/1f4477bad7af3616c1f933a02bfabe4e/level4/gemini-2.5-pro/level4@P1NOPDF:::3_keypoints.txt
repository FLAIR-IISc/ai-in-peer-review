- Paper proposes theory guaranteeing a global optimum and linear convergence for neural nets with positive inputs/weights and generalized polynomial activations, plus a nonlinear spectral optimization method and experiments on synthetic and UCI data.  
- Novelty: one of the first potentially practical neural network frameworks with both optimality and convergence guarantees.  
- Preconditions are simpler than prior work, depending only on the spectral radius of a non-negative matrix from network parameters.  
- Theory extends from single-hidden-layer to arbitrary-depth networks and shows promising small-scale empirical results.  
- Main limitation: strong assumptions—positive inputs and weights, generalized polynomial activations, and spectral radius ρ(A) < 1—restrict applicability.  
- Positive-input constraint is manageable, but forcing all weights to be positive may greatly reduce model expressivity.  
- Requiring each hidden layer to use a different activation function is unconventional and may hinder practical use.  
- Satisfying the spectral-radius constraint (by increasing p1,p2 and decreasing pw,pu) forces small weights, making activations near-linear and limiting expressivity especially as depth increases.  
- Concern that the approach may not scale effectively to deep architectures because small weights make layer composition effectively linear.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance despite concerns.  
- Minor request: empirical or analytical study of how p1,p2,pw,pu, input dimensionality, and parameter count affect the spectral radius ρ(A) and how pw/pu must scale as dimension grows.
