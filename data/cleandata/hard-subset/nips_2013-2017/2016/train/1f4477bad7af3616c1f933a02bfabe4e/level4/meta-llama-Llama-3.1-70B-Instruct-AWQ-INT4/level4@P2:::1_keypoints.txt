- Paper proves a special class of neural networks has a single stationary point and introduces a fast method converging to that unique global minimizer.
- Approach builds a contractive map whose fixed point equals the unique minimizer, using fixed-point theory rather than convexity arguments.
- Method guarantees global optimality and exhibits a fast convergence rate.
- Authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis and situate the method relative to existing neural network work.
- Main limitation: relies on boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix, restricting applicability to larger networks.
- Practical drawback: many tunable parameters make the procedure unintuitive and harder to use.
- For every parameter choice the spectral condition must be checked, adding computational/verification overhead.
- Experiments used ~150,000 parameter combinations selected by cross-validation.
- Empirical performance is limited: method outperforms linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, questioning practical advantage.
- Overall contribution is valuable and novel, but applicability and empirical power need further improvement.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestions to address scalability and reduce parameter-tuning burden.
