- Method imposes restrictive, counterintuitive constraints (non-negativity of weights, maximal weight regularization) and requires generalized polynomial activations, with weak justification.
- Authors should provide application-oriented explanations rather than further restricting the model to obtain global optimality.
- Claims of sufficient expressive power and strong performance are not convincingly supported by the experiments.
- Experimental comparisons are unfair: using linear SVM as a baseline for a nonlinear model is inappropriate; nonlinear SVMs (polynomial/Gaussian) with proper tuning should be included.
- UCI datasets are too small in sample size and feature dimensionality to demonstrate real-world applicability.
- Spectral radius bounds for matrix A grow with the number of hidden units, creating scalability issues for high-dimensional data that must be mitigated.
- The optimality condition may not hold under practical parameter settings; its checkability without training does not resolve validity when the parameter space is large.
- Authors should propose efficient methods to identify a small set of valid parameter candidates that satisfy the optimality condition.
- It is crucial to determine whether global optimality stems from the specific objective; empirically test whether SGD converges to the global optimum on the tested datasets.
