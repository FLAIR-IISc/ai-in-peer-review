

The paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The global optimality condition depends solely on network architecture parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix. The proposal of a nonlinear spectral method for this training task is novel and promising. However, the approach imposes a restrictive requirement of non-negative weights, and employs highly specific, non-standard activation functions. These limitations appear significant and may limit the practical applicability of the method. Indeed, the numerical results demonstrate inferior performance compared to SVM. Minor comment: L.90-91: The claim that the nonlinear spectral method "converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations" due to its linear convergence rate is overstated; linear convergence does not inherently guarantee convergence within a small fixed number of iterations.
