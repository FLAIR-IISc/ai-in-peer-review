- Studies a specific class of neural networks and proves it has only one stationary point.
- Proposes a fast algorithm that converges to this unique fixed-point, which is the global minimum of the cost.
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map to establish uniqueness.
- Tackles an interesting problem: a relatively complex model with constraints that yield a single stationary point.
- Convergence proof depends on the boundedness of the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.
- The spectral-radius constraint prevents scaling experiments to networks with more hidden units.
- The method involves many tuning parameters, making the procedure unintuitive.
- The spectral condition must be verified for every parameter configuration.
- Experiments evaluated ~150,000 parameter combinations and used cross-validation to pick the best.
- Empirical performance is weak: the method outperformed linear SVM on only 2 out of 7 datasets.
- Theoretical contribution is valuable, but practical implications are limited by spectral requirements and extensive tuning.
