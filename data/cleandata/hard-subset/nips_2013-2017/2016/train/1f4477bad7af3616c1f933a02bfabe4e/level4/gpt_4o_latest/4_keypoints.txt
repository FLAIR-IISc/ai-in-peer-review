- Introduces a nonlinear spectral method to optimally train a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a claimed linear convergence rate.  
- Global optimality condition depends on network architectural parameters and the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The approach is novel and intriguing for this class of networks.  
- Major limitation: method requires all network weights to be nonnegative.  
- Activation functions used are non-standard and highly specific, limiting generality.  
- These constraints likely restrict network expressivity and practical performance.  
- Numerical experiments show worse performance than SVM, supporting concerns about effectiveness.  
- Minor comment: claiming linear convergence implies convergence in "less than 10 iterations" is overstated.
