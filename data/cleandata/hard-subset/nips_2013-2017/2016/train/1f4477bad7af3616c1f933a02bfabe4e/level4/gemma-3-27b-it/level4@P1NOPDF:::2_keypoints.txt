- Method studies a restrictive class of feedforward networks with non-negativity and maximal weight regularization plus uncommon generalized polynomial activations, limiting practical appeal.  
- Theoretical guarantees hinge on these counterintuitive assumptions, so more application-driven justification or real-data analysis is needed.  
- Authors claim retained expressive power, but empirical results do not convincingly support this.  
- Linear SVM baseline is inappropriate for a nonlinear model; comparisons should include nonlinear SVMs (e.g., polynomial/Gaussian kernels).  
- In experiments, linear SVMs often outperform NLSM models, undermining the claimed advantages.  
- Used UCI datasets are small and low-dimensional, so scalability and real-world relevance are unclear.  
- Spectral radius bounds scale with number of hidden units, posing a challenge for high-dimensional problems; mitigation strategies are needed.  
- The checkable theoretical optimality condition is difficult to satisfy/practically verify when prior knowledge is lacking or parameter space is large.  
- Need faster methods to identify valid parameter candidates and to determine if global optimality arises from the specific objective function.  
- Suggest investigating whether stochastic gradient descent can empirically reach the global optimum on the tested datasets.
