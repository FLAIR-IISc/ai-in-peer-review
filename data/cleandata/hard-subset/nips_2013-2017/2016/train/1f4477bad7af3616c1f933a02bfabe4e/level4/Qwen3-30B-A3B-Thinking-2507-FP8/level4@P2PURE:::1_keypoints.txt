- Paper proves a special class of neural networks has a single stationary point and presents a fast method converging to that unique fixed point (global minimum).
- Uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose fixed point equals the unique minimizer.
- Addresses an interesting question: complex models with carefully chosen constraints can yield unique stationary points beyond convexity.
- Convergence proof depends on a bounded spectral radius condition for a non-trivial matrix, limiting scalability to larger hidden-layer sizes.
- Method requires verifying the spectral condition for each parameter configuration, adding overhead and fragility.
- Procedure involves a large number of tunable parameters, making it unintuitive and hard to apply.
- Experiments searched ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected the best by cross-validation.
- Empirical performance was weak: the method beat linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, questioning practical utility.
