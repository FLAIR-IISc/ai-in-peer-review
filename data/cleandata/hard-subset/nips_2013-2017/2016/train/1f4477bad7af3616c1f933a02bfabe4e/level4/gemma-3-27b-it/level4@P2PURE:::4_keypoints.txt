- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks.  
- Method achieves a linear convergence rate.  
- Global optimality guarantee depends on network architecture parameters and is decidable by computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- The approach is limited to networks with nonnegative weights.  
- It requires non-standard, highly specific activation functions.  
- These constraints appear restrictive and may limit the practical performance of the trained networks.  
- Numerical experiments show the method performs worse than SVMs.  
- Minor critique: claiming it “converges quickly (less than 10 iterations)” is overstated since linear convergence does not guarantee few iterations.
