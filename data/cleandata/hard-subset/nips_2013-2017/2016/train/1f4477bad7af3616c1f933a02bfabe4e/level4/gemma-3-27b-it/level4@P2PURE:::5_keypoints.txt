- Proposes a class of feedforward networks with a guaranteed global optimizer and an algorithm with linear convergence; mathematical derivations are generally insightful but some steps lack clarity.  
- Theorem 3 relies on first-order derivative inequalities while Theorem 4 uses second-order derivatives; the reason for this methodological shift is unclear.  
- The network architecture treats the final layer differently from hidden layers; the gradient w.r.t. u in G^Φ (3) seems to involve vectorization of u, which is not clearly explained.  
- Similar ambiguity arises when bounding u within a ball—derivation and interpretation are unclear.  
- The objective in (2) maximizes the 1-norm of connection parameters, contrary to common weight-decay practice; its motivation appears to be to force strictly positive gradients via a small ε and enable inclusion in V_++, suggesting a proof-driven choice.  
- It is unclear whether this 1-norm maximization introduces unconstrained or free parameters.  
- Requiring non-negative data and strictly positive weights (no zeros) raises practical concerns, including potential for unbounded accumulation.  
- Experimental comparison to SVM is provided, but the paper does not demonstrate practical advantages of this architecture over conventional networks (e.g., ReLU-based models).
