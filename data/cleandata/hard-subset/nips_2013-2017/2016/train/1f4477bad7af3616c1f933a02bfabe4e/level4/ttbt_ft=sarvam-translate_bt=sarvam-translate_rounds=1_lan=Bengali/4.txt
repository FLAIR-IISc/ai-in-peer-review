This paper presents a non-linear spectral method that optimally trains a certain class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The condition that guarantees global optimization depends on the network's architecture parameters and is limited to the computation of the spectral radius of a small non-negative matrix. The proposed non-linear spectral method seems new and interesting, optimally training a certain class of feedforward neural networks. At the same time, I am concerned about the method's limitations; it imposes a non-negativity condition on the network's weights. Furthermore, the choice of activation functions in the neural network is non-standard and very specific. The limitation seems very strong, which could hinder the good performance of neural networks. In fact, numerical tests do not show much better performance than SVMs. Small comment: L.90-91: "Note that the non-linear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and therefore usually converges quickly in a few (less than 10) iterations..." This sentence seems exaggerated because a linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply a few iterations.
