- Novel nonlinear spectral method for a specific class of feedforward networks, claiming linear convergence and a computable spectral-radius condition guaranteeing global optimality.
- Theoretical contribution: provides a computable global-optimality condition in a non-convex setting, which is significant despite applying to a restricted model class.
- Major limitation: enforces non-negativity of all network weights, likely reducing expressive power and deviating from standard neural network design.
- Major limitation: depends on non-standard, highly specific activation functions; unclear if guarantees extend to common activations (ReLU, sigmoid, tanh), limiting generalizability.
- Empirical concern: experiments show the method is outperformed by a standard SVM, questioning practical utility despite theoretical optimality.
- Minor comment: claiming convergence “in a few (less than 10) iterations” is overstated—linear convergence does not guarantee rapid convergence if the rate is near one.
