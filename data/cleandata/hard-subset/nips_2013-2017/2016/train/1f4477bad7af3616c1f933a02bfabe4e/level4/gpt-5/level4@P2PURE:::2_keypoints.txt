- The studied network class is restrictive and counterintuitive (non-negative weights, maximal regularization, and an uncommon generalized-polynomial activation) and needs stronger application-driven justification.

- Empirical results are unconvincing: linear SVM sometimes outperforms NLSM, baseline selection is unfair (no nonlinear SVMs), and tuning procedures between methods are inconsistent, so expressive power claims are not established.

- Experiments use small UCI datasets with low dimensionality, while theoretical bounds (spectral radius of A) worsen with more hidden units, implying scalability problems for high-dimensional, real-world tasks.

- The optimality condition is not guaranteed for reasonable parameter choices; although checkable, searching a huge parameter space is impractical—there’s no fast method proposed to find likely candidate parameters.

- It’s unclear whether the global optimality arises from the particular objective; the paper should test if standard optimizers like SGD can empirically reach a global optimum on the datasets.
