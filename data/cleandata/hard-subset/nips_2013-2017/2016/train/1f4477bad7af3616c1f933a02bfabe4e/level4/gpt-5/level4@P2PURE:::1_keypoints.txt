- Paper proves a special class of neural networks has a single stationary point (unique minimizer).
- Authors introduce a fast method that converges to that unique fixed point/global minimum.
- Approach employs fixed-point theory by constructing a contraction mapping whose fixed point is the minimizer.
- Paper addresses how constrained complex models can be guaranteed a unique stationary point beyond convexity.
- Convergence proof depends on bounding the spectral radius of a nontrivial matrix.
- Spectral-radius requirement prevents extending the analysis to networks with more hidden units.
- The procedure requires tuning many parameters and checking the spectral condition for each setting, making it unintuitive.
- Experiments evaluated ~150,000 parameter combinations and selected the best via cross-validation.
- Method outperformed a linear SVM on only 2 of 7 datasets, raising doubts about practical effectiveness.
