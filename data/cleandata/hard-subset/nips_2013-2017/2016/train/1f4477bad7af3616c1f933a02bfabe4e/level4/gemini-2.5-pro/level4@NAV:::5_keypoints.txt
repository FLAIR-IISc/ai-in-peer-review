- Paper proposes a feedforward NN class with a guaranteed unique global optimum and a linearly convergent optimizer; proofs are insightful but need clarification.
- Why does Theorem 3 use first-order derivatives while Theorem 4 switches to second-order derivatives — what justifies the change in derivative order?
- Clarify whether the gradient w.r.t. the final-layer parameter matrix u is computed in vectorized form and how the ball constraint on u is applied.
- The objective maximizes the L1-norm of connection parameters, which contradicts standard regularization (weight decay) and seems like a theoretical device to ensure strictly positive gradients.
- Practicality concerns: model requires non-negative inputs and strictly positive weights, has no free parameters, and yields a monotonic additive model — applicability is questionable.
- Experiments compare to SVMs; a more informative baseline would be conventional neural networks (e.g., ReLU) to demonstrate tangible advantages in practice.
