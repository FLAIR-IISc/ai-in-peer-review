- Paper proposes NLSM that achieves global optimal training with linear convergence for certain feedforward networks (one- and two-hidden layers) and presents experiments on real datasets.
- Studied model class imposes restrictive, counterintuitive constraints: non-negativity on all weights and maximization of weight regularization, with no clear justification.
- Requirement of a nonstandard generalized polynomial activation further limits the method’s applicability.
- Claim that the constrained class retains sufficient expressive power is unconvincing given the restrictive constraints.
- Empirical results are weak: linear SVM often outperforms NLSM1/NLSM2 despite SVM being suboptimally tuned while NLSM structures are tuned.
- Using linear SVM as the baseline for a nonlinear model is inappropriate; comparisons to nonlinear SVMs (polynomial, Gaussian kernels) are needed.
- Datasets used (UCI) are small in sample size and feature dimensionality, limiting the evidence for real-world applicability.
- Key theoretical concern: spectral radius bounds for matrix A scale with hidden units, creating critical scalability issues for high-dimensional data that are not addressed.
- The optimality condition lacks guidance for selecting parameter settings and, although checkable, is impractical to validate across the vast parameter space without a fast candidate-selection method.
- It is unclear whether global optimality arises from the specific objective choice and whether standard methods like SGD could empirically reach global convergence on the tested problems.
