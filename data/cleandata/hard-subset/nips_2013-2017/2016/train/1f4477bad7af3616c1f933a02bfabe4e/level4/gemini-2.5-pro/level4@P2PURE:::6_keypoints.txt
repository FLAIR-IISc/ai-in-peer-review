- Paper proposes a method with theoretical convergence for training a restricted class of feed-forward networks and shows real-world applicability.
- Problem is significant and the theoretical contribution is welcome.
- Practical utility of the restricted network class is not convincingly demonstrated.
- Authors claim expressiveness but do not analyze what functions the architecture can or cannot represent.
- The paper lacks discussion and comparison with concurrent/alternative approaches.
- Experimental evaluation is weak and does not probe the model’s applicability boundaries.
- Baselines are limited to SVMs; important baselines like other neural-network training methods are omitted.
- Computational cost details (training time, memory usage) versus baselines are missing.
- Introduction is clear, but clarity deteriorates in later sections, especially Section 2.
- Motivation for the function Φ and the role of the epsilon term should be explicitly explained in the main text.
- Theorem 1 is hard to parse and needs an informal, high-level explanation before the formal statement.
- The claim about the nonlinear spectral method “converging quickly” should be presented as an empirical observation, not a general theoretical fact.
- Several typos and notational issues: "polyomial" → "polynomial", awkward phrase "one CAN model", p1 and p2 used without introduction, and R_{++} not defined.
