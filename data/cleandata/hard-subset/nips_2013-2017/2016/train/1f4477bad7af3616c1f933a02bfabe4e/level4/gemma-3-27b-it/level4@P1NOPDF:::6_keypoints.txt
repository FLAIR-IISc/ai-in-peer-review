- Paper proposes a method to train a restricted class of feed-forward neural networks, proving optimal convergence and demonstrating some real-world applicability.  
- The problem is interesting and the theoretical advance is valuable.  
- The restricted model has limited expressiveness; the paper should discuss workarounds and inherent limitations.  
- Concurrent related work is missing and should be discussed and contrasted.  
- Experiments do not clarify applicability limits and only compare to SVMs, omitting other neural-network training methods and alternative algorithms.  
- Include a discussion of computational resources required by each compared algorithm.  
- Presentation needs improvement (Section 2 is not fluent) and the manuscript contains several typos.  
- The function Φ lacks sufficient motivation; the paper should incorporate the rebuttal’s explanation of the epsilon term.  
- Theorem 1 should be preceded by an informal explanation; the claim that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly” is empirical and should be stated as such.  
- Specific typos/clarifications: “polyomial”→“polynomial”; “one CAN model”→“one can model”; p1 and p2 are not introduced; R_{++} is not defined.
