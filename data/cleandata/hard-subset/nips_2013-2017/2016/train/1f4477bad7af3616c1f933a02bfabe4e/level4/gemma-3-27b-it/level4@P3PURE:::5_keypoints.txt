- Paper defines a feedforward network class with a unique global optimizer and gives an algorithm with linear convergence.  
- Mathematical derivations are generally instructive but need clearer explanations in places.  
- Theorem 3 uses first-order derivatives while Theorem 4 invokes second-order derivatives; the reason for this transition is not explained.  
- The final layer is treated differently from hidden layers; gradients w.r.t. u appear to involve vectorization and need clarification.  
- Bounding u within a ball raises similar questions about gradient/vectorization handling.  
- Maximizing the 1-norm of connection parameters in (2) is counterintuitive (contrasts with common weight decay) and seems motivated by proof needs to ensure strictly positive gradients via an epsilon, not practical design.  
- It is unclear whether the 1-norm maximization or positivity constraints introduce unconstrained or problematic parameters.  
- Requiring non-negative data and strictly positive (nonzero) weights causes cumulative summation behavior, raising concerns about practical applicability.  
- Experiments compare the model to SVM, but it’s unclear if the proposed network provides tangible advantages over standard architectures (e.g., ReLU-based networks) in real applications.
