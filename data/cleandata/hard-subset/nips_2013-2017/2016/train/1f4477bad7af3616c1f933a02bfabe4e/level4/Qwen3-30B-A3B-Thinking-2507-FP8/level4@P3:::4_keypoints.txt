- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral method that achieves globally optimal training for a class of feedforward networks under mild (nonnegative data) assumptions.  
- Shows global optimality depends only on the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix, which can be checked before running the algorithm.  
- Proves complete theoretical guarantees including linear convergence rate for one- and two-hidden-layer networks.  
- Builds on prior work (Janzamin et al., Haeffele & Vidal) but avoids their practical limitations by using nonnegative weights and generalized polynomial activations.  
- Leverages nonlinear Perron-Frobenius theory to obtain the main results.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong, technically complete, and computationally verifiable; no hyperparameters required.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written, with precise notation and a detailed proof roadmap.  
- Experimental results on UCI datasets demonstrate practical feasibility and competitive performance with SVM and ReLU networks.  
- Main limitation: restrictive nonnegativity constraint on weights may reduce expressive power and practical applicability.  
- Use of generalized polynomial (real-power) activations is non-standard and needs stronger motivation versus ReLU/sigmoid.  
- Applicability may be confined to domains with inherently nonnegative data (e.g., counts, image pixels).  
- Experiments are competitive but do not show superiority over existing methods, limiting immediate practical impact.  
- Paper should better discuss trade-offs between theoretical guarantees and expressiveness, and justify the necessity of the nonnegativity constraint.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen discussion of limitations, potential applications, and the nonnegativity justification.
