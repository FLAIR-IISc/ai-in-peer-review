- Novel contribution: introduces a nonlinear spectral method that attains optimal training with linear convergence for a specific class of feedforward networks.  
- Global optimality condition: depends on architecture and is checkable by computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix.  
- Major limitation: requires non-negativity of network weights, restricting applicability.  
- Activation choice concern: uses unconventional, highly specific activation functions that further limit generality.  
- Empirical issue: these limitations likely cause poorer performance versus SVM in experiments.  
- Overclaim: stating convergence in “less than 10 iterations” is overstated—linear convergence does not guarantee rapid convergence within a fixed small number of iterations.
