- Paper studies a special class of neural networks that, under specific constraints, has a unique stationary point.
- Authors propose a fast iterative method that converges to the unique fixed point, which is the global minimum of the cost.
- Theoretical approach uses fixed-point theory by constructing a contractive map whose fixed point is the minimizer.
- Convergence proof relies on bounding the spectral radius of a non-trivial matrix.
- The spectral-radius constraint prevented experiments with networks having more hidden units.
- The method requires a large number of hyperparameters, making it unintuitive and hard to use.
- The spectral condition must be checked for every new hyperparameter choice, adding overhead.
- Experiments involved searching ~150,000 hyperparameter combinations and selecting the best by cross-validation.
- Empirically the method outperformed a linear SVM baseline on only 2 out of 7 datasets.
- The limited empirical gains call into question the practical utility and representational power of the investigated network class.
