- Proposal: A nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of certain feedforward nets with theoretical guarantees for one- and two-hidden-layer networks under nonnegative data/weights.

- Novelty: Builds on prior global optimization work (Janzamin et al., Haeffele & Vidal) but avoids density estimation and non-degeneracy conditions.

- Strength: First practically feasible globally optimal training method with a simple check for global optimality (spectral radius of a small matrix).

- Strength: Clear, technically sound proof strategy using Banach fixed-point theorem and Thompson metric.

- Strength: Experiments show competitive accuracy on UCI datasets and much faster convergence than SGD.

- Weakness: Limited justification for practicality of the restricted model (nonnegative weights and modified objective); expressiveness limitations inadequately discussed.

- Weakness: Experimental comparisons are narrow (only SVM and ReLU); missing comparisons to standard optimizers (Adam, Adagrad, tuned SGD) and other training paradigms.

- Weakness: No reporting or discussion of computational resources (time, memory) required by the method versus alternatives.

- Weakness: Theorem 1 presentation is hard to parse; lacks an intuitive explanation and the role of the ε term is unclear.

- Weakness: Practical feasibility claim is undermined by extensive parameter tuning (n1, α, ρw, ρu) and no guidance or cost analysis for checking spectral radius across configurations.

- Argument for acceptance: Significant theoretical advance in global optimality for neural network training.

- Argument for acceptance: Provides a concrete alternative to SGD with provable convergence and favorable empirical speed.

- Argument for acceptance: Solid technical development and mathematical justification.

- Argument against acceptance: Practical relevance of the constrained model is not convincingly demonstrated.

- Argument against acceptance: Experimental evaluation and comparisons are insufficient to support practical feasibility.

- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions—need detailed discussion of model limitations, expanded experiments including standard optimizers and resource reporting, clearer intuitive presentation of Theorem 1 and ε, and guidance on parameter selection.
