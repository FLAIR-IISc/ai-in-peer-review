This paper introduces a nonlinear spectral method for optimally training a specific class of feedforward neural networks, achieving a linear convergence rate. The guarantee of global optimality relies on network architecture parameters and can be determined by computing the spectral radius of a small, nonnegative matrix. Proposing a nonlinear spectral method for optimal neural network training is a novel and interesting contribution. However, I have concerns regarding the method’s limitations. Specifically, it requires network weights to be nonnegative, and the activation functions used are non-standard and highly specific. These constraints appear restrictive and could potentially limit the performance of the trained networks. The numerical experiments presented support this concern, demonstrating performance inferior to that of SVMs. A minor point: lines 90-91 state that the nonlinear spectral method “converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations…” due to its linear convergence rate. This claim seems overstated, as a linear convergence rate does not inherently imply fast convergence in terms of iteration count.
