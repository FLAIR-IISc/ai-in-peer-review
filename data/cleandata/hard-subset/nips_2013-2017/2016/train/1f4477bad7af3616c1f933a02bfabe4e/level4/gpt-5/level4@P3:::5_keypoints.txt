- Summary: Analyzes training of nonnegative generalized polynomial feedforward networks and proves linear convergence to a unique global optimum under a computable spectral condition ρ(A)<1 via a fixed-point/Perron–Frobenius approach.  
- Relation to prior work: Trades generality for practicality compared to tensor-factorization methods by imposing nonnegativity and specific activations, yielding an algorithm without step-size tuning.

Strengths:
- Provides a novel, rigorous global optimality guarantee with explicit linear convergence rate and a priori verifiable condition ρ(A)<1.  
- Elegant formulation mapping training to a nonlinear eigenproblem on a positive cone, using the Thompson metric and tailored Lipschitz bounds.  
- Extends the proof strategy from one to two hidden layers with analogous conditions.  
- Gives explicit (though conservative) parameter bounds showing how hyperparameters affect ρ(A) and how large norms can ensure ρ(A)<1.  
- Algorithm is simple, requires no step-size tuning, and has an explicit convergence rate.

Weaknesses / Questions — Clarity & notation:
- Theorem 3 vs 4: Unclear whether C2 (second-order differentiability) is required and where second derivatives are actually used versus first-order inequalities.  
- Treatment of u: Ambiguity about vectorization—need explicit statement that gradients, ψ, and Lp norms on u are applied entrywise/vectorized (e.g., Frobenius as Lp over entries).  
- ε and modified objective: ε is not explicit in Eq.(2); unclear which added linear/output-sum terms are scaled by ε and whether guarantees hold as ε→0+.  
- Nonnegativity and expressiveness: Restrictive monotone model; need broader discussion of expressive power, approximation guarantees, and practical preprocessing for x≥0 (or extensions to signed weights via input splitting).  
- “No free parameter” claim is misleading given many hyperparameters (n1,n2,α,β,ρ*,p*); need practical guidance beyond ensuring ρ(A)<1 and sensitivity analysis for α.

Weaknesses / Questions — Experiments:
- Scope and baselines: Experiments limited to small, low-dimensional UCI sets; should include domains naturally nonnegative (text/counts/histograms) and stronger baselines like nonnegative logistic regression, NMF+classifier, polynomial/monotone networks.  
- Fairness and metrics: Must report both the training objective and standard classification metrics, wall-clock time, and iterations-to-ε compared to tuned SGD to support efficiency claims.  
- Tightness of ρ(A) bounds: Bounds appear conservative — paper should report actual spectral radii, corresponding γ eigenvectors, and empirical contraction rates vs theory.

Quality / Clarity:
- Theoretical development seems sound but several definitions/proofs need clearer notation (matrix norms, ε, differentiability), explicit cross-entropy definition, and justification for “distinct αl” requirement.

Originality:
- Original application of nonlinear Perron–Frobenius and contraction mapping to yield checkable spectral conditions and linear convergence for multilayer networks.

Significance:
- Significant under stated modeling constraints (nonnegativity and modified objective) by providing a practical, verifiable global-optimality condition; empirical scope is limited.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, rigorous theoretical contribution with a practical verifiable condition and linear convergence; extends beyond single hidden layer and connects fixed-point/eigenvalue theory with training.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Restrictive modeling assumptions and modified objective limit practical impact; clarity issues (ε, norms, derivative orders), overstatements about hyperparameters, and limited experiments.

Requested clarifications / revisions:
- Resolve whether Theorem 3/4 require C2 and make ε explicit throughout (including Lemma 5).  
- Explicitly state vectorization/norm conventions for u and inner-product usage in Lemma 4.  
- Provide ablation/sensitivity analysis for ε and added terms, guidance for selecting α and ρ hyperparameters, and report empirical ρ(A) and contraction rates.  
- Discuss expressiveness, preprocessing strategies for nonnegativity, and possible extensions to signed weights while preserving contraction.
