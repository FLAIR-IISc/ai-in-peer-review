- Introduces a novel nonlinear spectral training method for a specific class of feedforward networks with provable global convergence at a linear rate.  
- Global optimality reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small nonnegative matrix that depends only on network architecture.  
- Offers theoretical clarity and an explicit convergence guarantee as a significant contribution.  
- Requires all weights to be nonnegative, a strong and impractical constraint that limits applicability and representational capacity.  
- Relies on highly specific, non-standard activation functions tailored to theory, further reducing generality.  
- Experimental results are marginal or inferior to SVMs; the weight and activation constraints likely contribute to poor empirical performance.  
- The claim of "converges in <10 iterations" is misleading—linear convergence does not guarantee a small fixed number of iterations and should be qualified.  
- Recommendation: relax or justify the constraints (e.g., explore sign-agnostic variants) or risk the method remaining a narrow theoretical result.
