The paper presents a nonlinear spectral method that provably and optimally trains a specific class of feedforward neural networks with a linear convergence rate. The condition guaranteeing global optimality depends on the network’s architectural parameters and reduces to computing the spectral radius of a small, nonnegative matrix. Proposing a nonlinear spectral method for optimal training of feedforward neural networks is a novel and interesting contribution. However, I have concerns regarding the limitations of the approach. Specifically, it imposes a non-negativity constraint on the network weights, and the choice of activation functions is both non-standard and highly specific. These constraints appear substantial and may limit the potential performance of networks trained using this method. Indeed, the numerical experiments demonstrate performance that is not competitive with that of SVMs. A minor comment: Lines 90-91 state that "Note that the nonlinear spectral method has a linear convergence rate and thus converges quickly typically in a few (less than 10) iterations to…". This claim seems overstated, as a linear convergence rate does not necessarily imply rapid convergence in a small number of iterations.
