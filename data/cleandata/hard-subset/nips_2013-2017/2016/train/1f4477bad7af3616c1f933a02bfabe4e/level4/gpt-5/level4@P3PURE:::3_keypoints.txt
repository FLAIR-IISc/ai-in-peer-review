- Develops a theoretical framework showing a class of neural networks admits a global optimum reachable at linear convergence under positive inputs/weights and generalized polynomial activations; extends from one to many hidden layers and proposes a nonlinear spectral optimization method, with experiments on small datasets.

- Novelty/pro: First potentially practical neural network with both optimality and convergence guarantees.

- Novelty/pro: Preconditions are simpler than prior work, depending only on the spectral radius of a nonnegative matrix constructed from network parameters rather than the data-generating measure.

- Novelty/pro: Theory applies to arbitrary depth and yields reasonable empirical performance on small real datasets.

- Con: Limited applicability due to requirements of positive inputs and parameters, generalized polynomial activations, and spectral radius strictly less than 1.

- Con: Enforcing positive network parameters may reduce model expressiveness despite input shifting being possible.

- Con: Requiring all hidden layers to use different activation functions is unnatural and may further limit applicability.

- Con: Ensuring the spectral radius constraint as input dimensionality and network size grow may force very small linear weights, making deep networks effectively linear and undermining expressiveness.

- Minor comment: Reviewer asks how p1, p2, pw, pu, input dimension, and parameter sizes affect the spectral radius ρ(A) and suggests empirical plots to study whether ρ(A) scales linearly or exponentially and how to adjust pw/pu.

- Recommendation: Encouraging initial attempt; reviewer recommends acceptance despite the noted concerns.
