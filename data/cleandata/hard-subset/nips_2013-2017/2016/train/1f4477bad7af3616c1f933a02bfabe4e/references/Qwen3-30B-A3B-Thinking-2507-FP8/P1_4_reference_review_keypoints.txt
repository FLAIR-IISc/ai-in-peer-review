- Summary: Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks with nonnegative weights and a modified objective, with provable linear convergence under checkable spectral-radius conditions.

- Strength 1 (Theoretical): First practically implementable globally optimal training algorithm for a class of neural networks using fixed-point theory and Banach's theorem.

- Strength 2 (Convergence Guarantees): Provides clear, pre-checkable global optimality condition (ρ(A) < 1) without needing to run the algorithm.

- Strength 3 (Rate): Demonstrates linear convergence to the global optimum (‖·‖_∞ ≤ Rρ(A)^k), faster than typical SGD rates.

- Strength 4 (Experiments): Empirical results on UCI datasets show competitive performance versus ReLU networks and SVMs and exhibit the claimed linear convergence.

- Strength 5 (Presentation): Paper is well-structured and clearly presents problem, theory, and experiments.

- Weakness 1 (Nonnegativity): Requiring nonnegative weights is a major practical limitation that reduces expressiveness compared to standard networks.

- Weakness 2 (Modified Objective): The addition of a negative total-sum-of-outputs term lacks sufficient intuition or justification regarding its effect on performance.

- Weakness 3 (Experimental Scope): Experiments are limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets and do not demonstrate scalability to high-dimensional, large networks.

- Weakness 4 (Comparisons): Incomplete comparison to more recent global-optimization/convex-relaxation work (e.g., Zhang et al. 2016).

- Weakness 5 (Hyperparameters): Insufficient practical guidance on choosing hyperparameters (n1, α, ρw, ρu); claim of no tuning is undermined by random search/CV in experiments.

- Quality Assessment: Technically rigorous with solid proofs and supporting experiments, but empirical scope and practical implications are limited.

- Clarity Assessment: Well-written math and organization, though dense nonlinear spectral theory may challenge non-experts; supplementary proofs appropriate.

- Originality Assessment: Novel combination of nonlinear spectral theory, nonnegative weights, polynomial activations, and a modified objective; builds on but extends prior global-opt work.

- Significance Assessment: Theoretical advance is notable, but practical impact is limited by the nonnegative-weight constraint and scalability concerns.

- Recommendation: Accept with reservations — strong technical contribution but practical limitations need addressing.

- Requested Revision 1: Discuss implications of the nonnegative weight constraint for model expressiveness more thoroughly.

- Requested Revision 2: Include a more detailed comparison with recent global optimization and convex-relaxation approaches.

- Requested Revision 3: Discuss possible extensions to handle negative weights, even if as future work.

- Requested Revision 4: Provide clearer guidance on hyperparameter selection beyond random search and cross-validation.

- Pro (summary): First implementable globally optimal algorithm for a neural-network class, rigorous guarantees, linear convergence, competitive small-scale results, clear presentation.

- Con (summary): Nonnegative-weight constraint, limited experiments, weak justification for objective modification, limited hyperparameter guidance, not addressing common network architectures.
