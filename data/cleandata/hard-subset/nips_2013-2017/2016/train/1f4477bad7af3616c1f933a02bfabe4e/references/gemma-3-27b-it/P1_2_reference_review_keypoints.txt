- Proposes a novel nonlinear spectral algorithm (GΦ) to globally optimally train generalized polynomial neural networks with non-negative weights.  
- Formulates training as a maximization problem using Perron-Frobenius theory and provides a globally convergent iterative method.  
- Theoretical guarantee: convergence to a global optimum if the spectral radius ρ(A) < 1.  
- Achieves linear convergence rate, improving efficiency over many nonconvex methods.  
- Avoids complex or intractable computations like score-function tensors and heavy matrix factorizations.  
- Builds on and differentiates from prior provable-training work (Haeffele & Vidal; Janzamin et al.) by using verifiable, architecture-related conditions.  
- Experimental validation on UCI datasets shows competitive performance, demonstrating practical feasibility.  
- Major limitation: requirement of non-negative weights significantly restricts model expressiveness and applicability.  
- Convergence condition depends heavily on architecture and hyperparameters (hidden units, activation params, ρw, ρu), with conservative bounds that may be hard to satisfy.  
- Scalability concerns: matrix A and spectral-radius computation may become bottlenecks for large/high-dimensional problems.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small datasets and lacks validation on larger, more complex benchmarks.  
- The added summation term in the objective feels ad-hoc; its role needs clearer, more intuitive justification.  
- Some derived bounds (Section 4) are complex and may be difficult for practitioners to apply directly.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical novelty, global optimality guarantee, clear math, and promising practical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive non-negativity constraint, unclear scalability, and limited experiments.  
- Overall: strong, well-written paper with a significant theoretical contribution; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions addressing scalability and architecture-selection guidance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.
