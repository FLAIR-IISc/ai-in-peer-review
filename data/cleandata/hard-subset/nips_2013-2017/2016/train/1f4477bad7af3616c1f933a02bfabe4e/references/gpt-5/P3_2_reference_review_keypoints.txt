- Proposed method: nonlinear spectral training for feedforward nets with nonnegative weights/inputs and generalized polynomial activations, using a modified cross-entropy plus an output-sum and small weight regularizer, with parameters constrained on product ℓp spheres.  
- Algorithmic idea: map parameters to normalized positive powers of gradients (Perron–Frobenius–style fixed-point); contraction condition ρ(A)<1 in Thompson metric yields unique fixed point and linear convergence.  
- Extension: method generalized to two hidden layers with an analogous (K+2)×(K+2) matrix A.  
- Experiments: proof-of-concept on small UCI datasets showing competitive accuracy with small ReLU nets and RBF-SVM and faster linear convergence than batch SGD on the modified objective.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on nonlinear Perron–Frobenius theory and relates to global optimality results via structured factorizations and tensor methods; reminiscent of NMF multiplicative updates and geometric programming.  
- Strength — novelty: clean fixed-point/contraction formulation turning nonconvex training into a spectral-radius check for global convergence.  
- Strength — theoretical completeness: clear pathway from optimization to fixed-point theory, existence/uniqueness of interior maximizer, and explicit linear rates.  
- Strength — algorithmic simplicity: parameter-free iteration once hyperparameters/norms chosen, with explicit rates and stopping criterion via ρ(A).  
- Strength — practical checkability: global-optimality regime determined by a small matrix depending only on model hyperparameters and an input norm bound.  
- Strength — empirical contribution: first real-data experiments for this class of global methods, showing the constrained model can learn nontrivial boundaries.  
- Weakness — restricted model/expressivity: no biases, nonnegative weights/inputs, monotone power activations, and output-sum term restrict hypothesis class and preclude many practical non-monotone decision boundaries.  
- Weakness — modified objective: the added sum-of-outputs term (coefficient unclear) substantially alters cross-entropy behavior and may encourage larger logits; ε on weights is ambiguous in Eq. (2).  
- Weakness — distinct αl requirement: forcing all αl different to avoid symmetry is unusual, may trade away symmetry/identifiability, and lacks empirical guidance on choosing α’s or effects of coincidences.  
- Weakness — practicality of guarantee regime: making pw, pu large can force ρ(A)<1 but may hurt geometry/expressivity; paper lacks quantification of conservativeness and sensitivity to p.  
- Weakness — clarity/missing details: loss L(y,f) not explicitly defined, ε ambiguous, bias inclusion and data preprocessing for nonnegativity not discussed, and some constants (e.g., Ψ) lack intuition.  
- Weakness — limited experiments: small-scale datasets, SVM often stronger, insufficient tuning/regularization for baselines, no variance/robustness reporting, no wall-clock runtime, and no ablations (α choices, p values, output-sum term).  
- Questions for authors: can biases be included while preserving analysis; clarify exact loss and scaling of additional terms; how to choose αl and effect of coincidences; report measured ρ(A) vs theoretical bounds; can nonnegativity be relaxed via feature transforms?  
- Minor requests: precisely define L(y,f), clarify ε’s role, explain data preprocessing and input normalization, and fix typos/incomplete citations.  
- Overall evaluation: elegant and novel theoretical contribution but limited practical impact due to modeling constraints and sparse empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject — strengthen experiments, clarify objective/details, and address expressivity (e.g., biases) to improve significance.
