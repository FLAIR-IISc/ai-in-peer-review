- Presents an algorithm for globally optimal training of single-hidden-layer networks with generalized polynomial activations using nonlinear spectral (Perron-Frobenius–inspired) methods.  
- Provides convergence guarantees based on the spectral radius of a small matrix and achieves linear convergence.  
- Avoids estimating the data-generating distribution and reports competitive performance on UCI datasets.  

- Correctly situates the work relative to Haeffele & Vidal and Janzamin et al., and connects to Perron-Frobenius and spectral-method literature.  
- Acknowledges broader non-convex optimization and theoretical deep learning literature.  

- Core theoretical guarantee of global optimality under mild conditions (non-negativity and spectral check).  
- Linear convergence rate indicating efficient training compared to standard SGD.  
- Does not require data distribution estimation, improving practicality over some prior methods.  
- Clear, rigorous mathematical formulation and useful explicit matrix A.  
- Helpful outline of the proof strategy clarifying technical contributions.  
- Shows potential extension to two hidden layers, indicating broader applicability.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on benchmark UCI datasets.  

- Strong non-negativity constraint on weights limits expressiveness and real-world applicability.  
- Modified loss (adding total output sum) appears ad-hoc and may harm generalization without stronger justification.  
- Derived bounds on pu and pw are likely conservative, potentially requiring heavy tuning.  
- Experiments are limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets, leaving scalability and large-scale performance untested.  
- Method is sensitive to choice of α (polynomial powers) with insufficient practical guidance.  
- Lacks comparison to well-tuned regularized SGD baselines (dropout/L1/L2).  
- Theorem 4 and the Ψαp,q formulation are overly complex and obscure the main idea.  

- Novel, theoretically sound approach to global neural network optimization merits interest.  
- Linear convergence and no data-distribution estimation are significant methodological advantages.  
- Strong mathematical rigor and proof details strengthen the contribution.  
- Extension to deeper architectures (two hidden layers) is promising.  

- Practical applicability limited by strict non-negativity requirement.  
- Conservative bounds and limited experiments cast doubt on scalability.  
- Modified objective lacks sufficient justification, weakening empirical claims.  

- Overall: technically strong and novel with impressive theoretical results but practical usefulness is constrained by non-negativity and limited empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on addressing key weaknesses.  

- Requested revisions: explore relaxing or mitigating the non-negativity constraint (or data pre-processing strategies).  
- Expand experiments to larger, more complex datasets to assess scalability and real-world performance.  
- Derive tighter, less conservative bounds for pu and pw or justify current choices more thoroughly.  
- Provide stronger justification for the modified objective (adding total output sum).  
- Include comparisons to well-tuned regularized SGD baselines.
