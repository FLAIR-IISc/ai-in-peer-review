- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method to train constrained feedforward networks with nonnegative weights and per-unit polynomial activations, using an iterative fixed-point map GΦ.  
- Main theorem: if the spectral radius ρ(A) of a constructed nonnegative matrix is < 1, GΦ is a contraction in the Thompson metric and iterates converge linearly to the unique global maximizer.  
- The ρ(A)<1 condition is checkable a priori and depends on architecture and data norms; can be enforced by choosing large p-norm exponents.  
- Framework is extended from one to two hidden layers, with analogous (slightly larger) A matrices and guarantees.  
- Theoretical contribution is novel: combines multi-homogeneous Perron–Frobenius theory, Thompson metric, and fixed-point contraction to yield global optimality.  
- Algorithm is parameter-free (no step-size) and shows fast empirical convergence on the modified objective.  
- Proofs appear technically sound and careful, using appropriate tools (Collatz–Wielandt, metric weights, etc.).  
- Strong modeling constraints: nonnegative data/weights, fixed p-norm constraints, and specialized polynomial activations limit representational power and domains.  
- Objective modification (adding sum of outputs and small linear term) is nonstandard and may bias classification or calibration; its statistical effect is not analyzed.  
- The A-matrix bounds are conservative; guarantees often require very large p, raising questions about practical usefulness.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small low-dimensional UCI datasets and lacks scalability tests on higher-dimensional, naturally nonnegative domains.  
- Comparisons miss more relevant baselines (nonnegative networks, polynomial networks, NMF classifiers); comparing to standard ReLU/SGD is potentially unfair due to objective differences.  
- Limited ablation studies: no systematic exploration of removing the ∑r fr term, varying p/ρ, or sensitivity to α choices.  
- Missing details and clarity gaps: exact loss definition and interaction with +∑r fr, some incomplete references, and only a sketch for why distinct αl avoid triviality.  
- No empirical study of how the added output-sum term affects generalization, calibration, or robustness to preprocessing (e.g., centered features).  
- Practical guidance is missing: per-iteration computational complexity, wall-clock comparisons to SGD, and parameter-selection advice.  
- Originality: the combination of nonlinear spectral methods and a checkable spectral-radius condition for global training of a NN class is original and distinct from tensor-based/global-optimality prior work.  
- Significance: conceptually important for understanding global solvability of NN training, but practical impact is limited by constraints and conservative conditions.  
- Suggestions: precisely specify loss, justify/absorb the ∑r fr term statistically, document preprocessing for nonnegativity, run ablations (remove term, vary p/ρ), report costs and sensitivities, and test higher-dimensional nonnegative datasets.  
- Pros: rigorous global convergence with linear rate, parameter-free fixed-point algorithm, extension to two hidden layers.  
- Cons: strong modeling constraints and nonstandard objective, conservative sufficient conditions, and limited small-scale experiments.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject; theory is solid but empirical scope and clarity need substantial strengthening to merit acceptance.
