- Addresses global optimality in neural network training by proposing a "nonlinear spectral method" for networks with nonnegative weights and nonnegative data.  
- Builds on prior global-optimality work but avoids impractical tensor computations by using nonlinear Perron-Frobenius theory and a fixed-point formulation.  
- Theoretical novelty: rigorous connection to nonlinear spectral methods with proofs using the Banach fixed point theorem, Thompson metric, and Collatz-Wielandt formula.  
- Provides linear convergence guarantees to the global optimum and an elegant, well-explained proof strategy.  
- Practical feasibility: no learning rate or hyperparameter tuning required and a simple pre-checkable condition (spectral radius < 1); empirical convergence often in 10–20 iterations.  
- Experimental validation: first demonstration of global optimality for a non-trivial NN on real (UCI) datasets, with competitive results and two-hidden-layer variant outperforming baselines on some datasets.  
- Clear organization and exposition with helpful algorithm description and clear theorems/proofs.  
- Major limitation: requirement of nonnegative weights and data severely restricts applicability to most real-world datasets that include negative features.  
- Limited experimental scope: evaluations only on low-dimensional UCI datasets, with no evidence of scalability to high-dimensional problems where NN’s are typically used.  
- Lack of principled parameter selection: key parameters (α, n1, etc.) chosen by limited random search with no theoretical guidance, impractical for large-scale use.  
- Incomplete comparative analysis: comparisons to ReLU networks are problematic because ReLU allows negative weights, so model-capacity differences are not adequately discussed.  
- Conservative theoretical bounds: spectral radius bounds appear loose and the paper lacks empirical evidence that actual spectral radii are much smaller in practice.  
- Recommended improvements: discuss extensions for negative data, provide principled parameter-selection guidance, test on higher-dimensional datasets, and clarify trade-offs of the nonnegative constraint.  
- Overall assessment: strong and novel theoretical contribution with practical feasibility for a narrow class of problems, but restricted applicability and limited experiments reduce impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
