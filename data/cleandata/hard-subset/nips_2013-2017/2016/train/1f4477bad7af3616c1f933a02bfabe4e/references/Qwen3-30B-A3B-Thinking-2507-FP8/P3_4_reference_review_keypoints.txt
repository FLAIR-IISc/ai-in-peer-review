- Proposal: Introduces a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial neural networks with nonnegativity constraints and verifiable spectral-radius conditions.  
- Theoretical contribution: Theorem 1 gives rigorous conditions and linear convergence to the global optimum using nonlinear spectral theory and Banach fixed-point.  
- Perron-Frobenius link: Well-motivated connection to Perron-Frobenius theory for multihomogeneous problems.  
- Restrictive constraints: Nonnegativity of weights is extremely restrictive and limits expressiveness (e.g., fails to match ReLU performance on Iris).  
- α-parameter issue: Requirement of distinct α values per hidden unit is inadequately justified beyond avoiding permutation symmetry.  
- Conservative bounds: Spectral-radius bounds in Theorem 1 are highly conservative, likely making practical application more restrictive than theory suggests.  
- Limited experiments: Empirical validation is limited to small UCI datasets, lacks scalability analysis and broader benchmarks.  
- Performance gap: Method is outperformed by SVMs on most UCI datasets, questioning practical competitiveness.  
- Parameter selection unclear: Choice of n1, α, ρ_w, ρ_u is described as random without principled guidance.  
- Objective modification unclear: Motivation for adding the ∑_r f_r(w,u)(x_i) term to the objective is not sufficiently explained.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and mathematically precise for a technical audience.  
- Originality: Novel theoretical connection between neural network training and nonlinear spectral methods, but limited by specific constraints.  
- Significance limited: Valuable theoretical insight but practical significance is reduced by severe architectural restrictions and limited experiments.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on addressing nonnegativity limitations, improving experimental validation and scalability, tightening theoretical bounds, and clarifying parameter selection.
