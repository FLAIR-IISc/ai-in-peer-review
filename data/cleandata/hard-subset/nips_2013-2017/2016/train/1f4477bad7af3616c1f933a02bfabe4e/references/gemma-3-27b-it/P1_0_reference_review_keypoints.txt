- Presents a novel algorithm that trains generalized polynomial neural networks with non-negative weights to a global optimum using nonlinear spectral theory and Perron–Frobenius concepts.  
- Guarantees global optimality and linear convergence under a spectral-radius condition on a constructed matrix.  
- Avoids estimating data-generating measures and complex non-degeneracy checks required by some prior global-optimum methods.  
- Demonstrates competitive initial empirical results on UCI datasets.  
- Correctly situates the work relative to Haeffele & Vidal (2016) and Janzamin et al. (2016), highlighting practical advantages.  
- Provides a well-explained theoretical foundation connecting to Perron–Frobenius and nonlinear spectral theory.  
- Strength: theoretical global optimality guarantee under relatively mild, checkable conditions.  
- Strength: practicality—checking the spectral radius of a small matrix is computationally tractable.  
- Strength: linear convergence rate offers a potential speed advantage over iterative methods like SGD.  
- Strength: clear, step-by-step theoretical presentation and rigorous metric-space treatment (Thompson metric, Banach fixed-point).  
- Strength: novel application of nonlinear spectral methods to neural network training.  
- Weakness: strict non-negativity constraint on weights limits model expressiveness and practical applicability.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited in scope; needs larger, more complex datasets and stronger baselines.  
- Weakness: derived bounds for ensuring ρ(A) < 1 are likely conservative and may restrict applicability.  
- Weakness: requirement of distinct α values per hidden unit raises practical concerns and potential overfitting.  
- Weakness: modification of the objective (adding sum of outputs) appears ad-hoc and lacks thorough justification or ablation.  
- Recommendation for acceptance: novel, practical, competitive, and well-written with significant theoretical contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: non-negativity constraint, limited empirical validation, and conservative convergence bounds.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper worthy of acceptance pending minor revisions to expand experiments and discuss/relax the non-negativity constraint.
