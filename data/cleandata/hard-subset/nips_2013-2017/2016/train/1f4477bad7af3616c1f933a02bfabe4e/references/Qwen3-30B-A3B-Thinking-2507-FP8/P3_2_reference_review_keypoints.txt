- Proposes a globally optimal training algorithm for generalized polynomial neural nets with nonnegative weights/inputs using a nonlinear spectral method.  
- Theoretical framework is technically sound; Theorem 1 gives conditions for global optimality with linear convergence.  
- Authors acknowledge nonnegativity is a strong limitation required for their theory.  
- Modified objective (adding sum of outputs) is necessary for guarantees but lacks practical justification and impact analysis on generalization.  
- Nonnegativity constraint severely limits model expressiveness and is not thoroughly analyzed regarding learnable function classes.  
- Experimental section is underdeveloped: UCI results are competitive but lack parameter selection and implementation details.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear connection to Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Needs a more intuitive explanation of why nonnegativity is required beyond mathematical convenience.  
- Requires a clearer, practical comparison to prior global-optimality work (Haeffele & Vidal, Janzamin et al.).  
- Experimental methodology lacks reproducibility details, particularly parameter tuning on UCI datasets.  
- Using nonlinear spectral/Perron-Frobenius ideas is novel and interesting for global optimization.  
- Originality is limited by the restrictive nonnegative model and insufficient argument that this is more practically feasible than prior approaches.  
- Practical significance is limited: results are a proof of concept but likely not applicable where negative weights are needed; scalability is unproven.  
- Overall: technically valid contribution but practically constrained; paper needs more honest discussion of limitations and justification for the objective modification.  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on (1) deeper discussion of nonnegativity limitations, (2) justification for the modified objective, (3) expanded experimental details on parameter selection/implementation, and (4) clearer comparison to prior work.
