- Summary: Proposes Nonlinear Spectral Method (NLSM) with Perron-Frobenius–based guarantees for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial networks with non-negative weights; shows spectral-radius check and linear convergence with UCI experiments.

- Strength: Global optimality guarantee rare and valuable for neural network training.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation using Perron-Frobenius theory and spectral analysis.

- Strength: Demonstrated linear convergence rate indicating fast training under conditions.

- Strength: Algorithm is explicitly defined and implementable.

- Strength: Experimental validation on UCI datasets shows competitive performance.

- Strength: Novel approach that offers a new perspective on network training.

- Weakness: Non-negative weight constraint is a major limitation that reduces expressiveness and may hurt performance.

- Weakness: Scalability concerns—spectral-radius dependence on hidden units and conservative bounds limit high-dimensional applicability.

- Weakness: Experiments limited to small UCI datasets and lack comparisons to stronger deep-learning baselines.

- Weakness: Modified objective (loss plus negative total output) and epsilon term are non-standard and their effect on generalization is unclear.

- Weakness: Paper is dense and mathematically heavy; lacks intuitive explanations (e.g., motivation for Thompson metric).

- Weakness: Bounds are overly conservative; effective spectral radius is typically smaller, indicating room for improvement.

- Quality: Rated 8/10 — technically sound and rigorous but weakened by limited experiments and strong constraints.

- Clarity: Rated 6/10 — precise but challenging to read; needs more intuition.

- Originality: Rated 9/10 — highly original connection between spectral methods and global training.

- Significance: Rated 7/10 — strong theoretical result with limited practical impact due to constraints and scalability.

- Pro (accept): Provides globally optimal algorithm, rigorous analysis, linear convergence, and novelty.

- Con (against): Non-negativity constraint, limited scalability and experiments, and dense presentation.

- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Required revisions: expand experiments to larger/stronger baselines; discuss non-negativity impact; improve intuitive explanations/clarity; investigate tightening conservative bounds.
