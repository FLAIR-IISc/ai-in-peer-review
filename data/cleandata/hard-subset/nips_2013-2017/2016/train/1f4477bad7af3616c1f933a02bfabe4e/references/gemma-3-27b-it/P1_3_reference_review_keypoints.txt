- Presents a novel method for globally optimal training of one- and two-hidden-layer feedforward networks using nonlinear spectral methods and Perron-Frobenius theory.  
- Reformulates training as a maximization problem with provable global optimality when the spectral radius condition ρ(A) < 1 holds, and shows linear convergence.  
- Avoids estimating the data-generating measure by relying on a pre-checkable spectral radius of a relatively small matrix.  
- Enforces non-negativity constraints on weights and adds the sum of outputs to the loss to ensure a strictly positive gradient.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear proofs and connections to matrix analysis and prior work on polynomial activations.  
- Novel connection between nonlinear spectral methods and neural network optimization, justifying generalized polynomial activations.  
- Experimental validation on UCI datasets demonstrates competitive performance and fast convergence, albeit limited in scope.  
- Key strength: guaranteed global optimality with a condition that can be checked before training.  
- Strength: more practical than prior global methods by avoiding tensor/density estimation and complex computations.  
- Strength: empirically observed linear convergence rate supporting fast training.  
- Major weakness: strict non-negativity constraint significantly limits expressivity and real-world applicability.  
- Weakness: inclusion of the sum-of-outputs term is non-standard and its impact on learned representations is unclear.  
- Weakness: convergence bounds are conservative, so theoretical guarantees may be unnecessarily restrictive.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to low-dimensional UCI datasets and lack high-dimensional or image classification benchmarks.  
- Weakness: complex sensitivity of spectral radius to model hyperparameters (n1, α, ρw, ρu, p, q) with little practical guidance.  
- Weakness: constructing and evaluating matrix A may still be costly for large networks.  
- For acceptance: offers a genuinely novel, rigorous, and practically advantageous approach to a fundamental deep learning problem.  
- Against acceptance: applicability is limited by non-negativity, limited empirical validation, and conservative theoretical bounds.  
- Overall: strong contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions despite notable limitations.  
- Suggested revisions: expand discussion on non-negativity effects, justify the modified objective, seek tighter bounds, perform larger-scale experiments, and provide practical parameter-selection guidance.
