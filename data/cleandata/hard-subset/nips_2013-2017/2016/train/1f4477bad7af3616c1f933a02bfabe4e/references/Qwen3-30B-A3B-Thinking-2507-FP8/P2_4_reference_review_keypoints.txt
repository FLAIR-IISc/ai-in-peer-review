- Proposes a nonlinear spectral method for globally optimal training of generalized polynomial networks with nonnegative weights/inputs; linear convergence and verifiable conditions via a small spectral-radius check.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: rigorous proofs using Perron–Frobenius theory, Thompson metric, and Banach fixed-point theorem.  
- Novelty: first to combine nonlinear spectral methods with neural network training while avoiding reliance on the data-generating measure.  
- Verifiable global optimality conditions (Theorem 1) are practical to check.  
- Experimental results are a proof-of-concept on UCI datasets; NLSM2 performs competitively and shows linear convergence vs SGD.  
- Major practical limitation: nonnegative weight/input constraint is severe and limits applicability to real-world tasks that need negative weights.  
- Experimental scope limited to low-dimensional UCI data; no experiments on high-dimensional settings where neural nets typically excel.  
- Theoretical assumptions (nonnegative inputs/weights) are restrictive and not explored for relaxation while keeping guarantees.  
- Comparisons with ReLU baselines are somewhat unfair given ReLU allows negative weights; paper doesn’t justify negligible performance loss from the constraint.  
- Overall assessment: significant and novel theoretical advance but limited practical impact; recommended weak acceptance with caveats.  
- Recommendation caveats: clearly acknowledge practical limits, discuss handling negative inputs (e.g., data transforms), and add at least one small high-dimensional experiment.
