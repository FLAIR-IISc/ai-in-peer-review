This paper presents a globally optimal training algorithm for generalized polynomial neural networks, leveraging nonlinear spectral methods. The core idea – formulating training as an eigenvalue problem and exploiting the Perron-Frobenius theorem – is genuinely novel and a bold departure from the typical gradient-based approaches dominating deep learning. The authors correctly identify the challenges with existing globally optimal training methods (density estimation in [11], impracticality in [8]) and offer a compelling alternative with a surprisingly simple convergence check (spectral radius of a small matrix). The extension to two hidden layers, while brief, demonstrates a path toward increased complexity.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach is remarkably original. Globally optimal training is a highly desirable goal, and framing it as a spectral problem is a clever and potentially powerful insight.
*   **Theoretical Guarantees:** The authors provide a convergence guarantee to a global optimum, a significant achievement, even with the constraints of non-negativity. The linear convergence rate is also a strong positive.
*   **Practicality (Potential):** Unlike prior work, the proposed method appears computationally tractable. The convergence check, based on the spectral radius, is simple and efficient.
*   **Clear Motivation:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of existing methods and positions the new approach as a solution to these limitations.
*   **Well-structured Presentation:** The paper is logically organized, with a clear progression from problem statement to theoretical results to experimental validation.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Strong Non-Negativity Constraint:** The requirement of non-negative weights is a significant limitation. While the authors attempt to mitigate this by showing expressiveness, it undoubtedly restricts the applicability of the method. This needs further investigation.
*   **Limited Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments are somewhat limited, focusing on relatively small UCI datasets. While competitive performance is reported, a more thorough evaluation on larger, more challenging datasets is needed. The comparison with ReLU networks is not comprehensive.
*   **Conservative Bounds:** The authors acknowledge the bounds derived for the spectral radius are conservative. This implies the method might be applicable in scenarios where the theoretical analysis suggests it shouldn’t be. Investigating tighter bounds would be valuable.
*   **Objective Function Modification:** The added term to the objective function (sum of outputs) feels somewhat ad-hoc. A more intuitive explanation or justification would strengthen the paper.
*   **Parameter Sensitivity:** The dependence of the convergence guarantee on parameters like `p`, `ρu`, and `ρw` is not fully explored in the experimental section. The practical sensitivity of the results to these parameters is unclear.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The core idea is exceptionally novel and potentially transformative.
*   The theoretical guarantees are solid and provide a strong foundation for the method.
*   The method *does* achieve global optimality under its constraints, which is a significant accomplishment.
*   The authors acknowledge limitations and outline future research directions.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The strong non-negativity constraint significantly limits practical applicability.
*   The empirical evaluation is not extensive enough to fully demonstrate the method's potential.
*   The objective function modification feels somewhat artificial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a highly promising paper with a genuinely novel approach to a fundamental problem in deep learning. While the non-negativity constraint is a serious limitation, the theoretical guarantees and potential for practical application are compelling. The experimental evaluation, while limited, is sufficient to demonstrate the method's viability. The paper prioritizes novelty and a bold approach over exhaustive validation, which aligns with my reviewing preferences. I believe this work has the potential to open up a new direction in neural network training, even if it isn’t immediately applicable to all scenarios. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept** – I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should consider: (1) exploring ways to relax the non-negativity constraint; (2) conducting more extensive experiments on larger datasets; (3) providing a more intuitive justification for the modification to the objective function; and (4) investigating tighter bounds for the spectral radius. The supplementary material is also quite extensive and could be streamlined for better readability.
