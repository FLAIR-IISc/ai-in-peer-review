- Overall: Fascinating, ambitious approach that achieves global optimality for a restricted class of networks; reviewer leans weak accept pending fixes.
- Core idea: Proposes a nonlinear spectral method using Perron-Frobenius theory to train generalized polynomial networks with non-negative weights.
- Guarantee: Algorithm provably attains global optimum for single-hidden-layer networks (extensions up to two layers) with a practical spectral-radius check.
- Practical tweak: Method requires modifying the objective with a sum-of-outputs regularizer to ensure non-zero outputs.
- Strength — theory: Provides strong, rigorous theoretical guarantees and a linear convergence proof.
- Strength — novelty: Applies nonlinear spectral methods to neural-network training, opening new optimization directions.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written and explains the mathematics accessibly.
- Weakness — non-negativity: The non-negative weight constraint is a major expressiveness limitation (e.g., excludes ReLU networks).
- Weakness — experiments: Empirical evaluation is limited (mainly UCI datasets) and not clearly superior to existing methods.
- Weakness — conservative bounds: Conditions ensuring ρ(A)<1 may be overly conservative, possibly hiding broader applicability.
- Weakness — objective term: The added sum-of-outputs term feels ad-hoc and needs deeper analysis of its effect on representations and performance.
- Weakness — scalability: Scalability to large/deeper networks is unclear; memory and matrix operations may become bottlenecks.
- Request — non-negativity mitigation: Ask authors to discuss strategies (shifted activations, reparameterizations) to relax or mitigate the non-negativity constraint.
- Request — experiments: Recommend testing on higher-dimensional and more complex datasets and stronger baselines/optimizers.
- Request — sensitivity & cost: Ask for parameter-sensitivity analysis (n1, α, ρw, ρu) and a detailed computational-cost scaling study.
- Request — deeper networks: Seek a fuller discussion of challenges and limitations for extending the method beyond two hidden layers.
- For acceptance: Strong theoretical novelty and guarantees, clear writing, and relevance to a central problem.
- Against acceptance: Restrictive assumptions, limited empirical validation, and unresolved scalability concerns.
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing experimental breadth, practical limitations, and scalability concerns.
