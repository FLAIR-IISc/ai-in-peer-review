- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-free and gradient-based) for non-convex Supervised PageRank learning.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rate and complexity guarantees for both methods.  
- Gradient-free method: random gradient-free optimization with inexact oracle; gradient-based: adaptive projected gradient with inexact first-order oracle.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear problem statement and theoretical analysis.  
- Experiments show the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based method on the reported loss.  
- Strength: novel methods with theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: empirical improvement over existing method.  
- Weakness: assumes loss is convex in a small vicinity of the optimum, which may not hold.  
- Weakness: no comparisons to other optimizers (e.g., SGD, quasi-Newton).  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation limited to a single dataset, limiting generalizability.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; address additional comparisons, dataset limitations, implementation and computational details.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10, Overall 8.2/10.
