- Proposes two two-level methods for learning Supervised PageRank using gradient-based and gradient-free optimization.
- Develops a gradient-free method for convex problems with an inexact zero-order oracle.
- Develops an adaptive gradient method for possibly non-convex composite problems with an inexact first-order oracle.
- Provides theoretical convergence rate analysis and complexity bounds for both methods.
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art on all test sets.
- Authors analyze computational complexity and demonstrate scalability of the methods.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly motivated, and relates work to prior literature.
- Notation and technical explanations are consistent and well-written.
- Novel use of inexact oracle information and adaptive gradient strategies distinguishes the work.
- Contributions have potential impact on information retrieval, web search, and other ranking problems.
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with optimization and Supervised PageRank, making technical details challenging for some.
- Weakness: lacks a deeper analysis of limitations and directions for future work.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, strong empirical and theoretical results, recommended for acceptance (score 9/10).
