- Summary: Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-free and gradient-based) for Supervised PageRank using node/edge features; both tackle non-convex loss minimization with theoretical convergence guarantees and empirical comparison to a state-of-the-art method.
- Relation to prior work: Extends Supervised PageRank optimization by addressing limitations of prior methods that required convexity or exact oracle information, supporting non-convex problems and inexact oracles.
- Strength: Thorough theoretical analysis with convergence rate guarantees and complexity bounds.
- Strength: Empirical results show effectiveness on a real-world ranking task.
- Strength: Advances efficient optimization methods for large-scale problems.
- Weakness: Assumes the loss is convex in a small neighborhood of the optimum, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed comparisons to other optimization algorithms (e.g., SGD, quasi-Newton).
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to a single dataset, leaving generalization unclear.
- Quality: Technically sound and well-organized analysis, but would benefit from broader empirical comparisons.
- Clarity: Well-written with consistent notation and readable figures, though some technical details may be hard for non-experts.
- Originality: Introduces novel optimization methods and new convergence/complexity analysis for Supervised PageRank.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for large-scale ranking, recommendation, and network analysis applications.
- Pro acceptance arguments: novel methods, strong theoretical analysis, and promising experimental results.
- Con acceptance arguments: restrictive convexity assumption, insufficient comparator algorithms, and limited experimental scope.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to address comparison and evaluation limitations.
