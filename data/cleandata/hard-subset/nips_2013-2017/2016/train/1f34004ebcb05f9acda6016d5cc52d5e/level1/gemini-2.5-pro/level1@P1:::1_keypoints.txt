- Tackles learning parameters for Supervised PageRank, where loss and gradients depend on an inexact stationary distribution oracle.
- Frames the problem as optimization with an inexact oracle and analyzes upper-level methods accounting for lower-level oracle cost.
- Proposes a gradient-free method (GFN): adapts Nesterov’s random gradient-free framework to constrained problems with deterministic function-value error and gives convergence rates under local convexity.
- Proposes a gradient-based method (GBN): an adaptive projected gradient method for non-convex constrained problems using an inexact first-order oracle without needing the Lipschitz constant.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and complexity bounds for learning Supervised PageRank, a first for this problem.
- Derives trade-offs between lower-level oracle accuracy and overall optimization complexity.
- Empirical results on a large-scale proprietary web-ranking task show both methods outperform the prior state-of-the-art (GBP).
- Finds the gradient-free method often performs best in practice, avoiding costly derivative computations.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presented, with logical flow from model to oracles to algorithms and experiments.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor, bridging abstract inexact-oracle theory with a practical ranking application.
- Strength: novel methodological extensions applicable beyond this specific problem (inexact zero/first-order oracles, adaptive projected method).
- Strength: demonstrable empirical improvement in final loss over previous work on a real-world dataset.
- Weakness: experiments use a proprietary dataset, hindering reproducibility and broader comparison.
- Weakness: GFN’s convergence proof requires convexity on the domain (local convexity), a restrictive assumption for a globally non-convex problem.
- Weakness: missing practical experiment details, particularly wall-clock timing, weakening claims about real-world speed improvements.
- Pro: fills an important theoretical gap with first convergence guarantees and general-purpose inexact-oracle methods.
- Con: lack of reproducibility, reliance on local convexity for GFN, and absence of timing comparisons are significant practical concerns.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss the convexity assumption and, if possible, add timing/practical performance details.
