

# Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

## Summary
This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, which extend traditional PageRank to incorporate node and edge features through linear combinations of features with model parameters. The authors propose two optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for solving the non-convex loss-minimization problem involved in learning these models. Unlike previous work [21], which lacked convergence guarantees, the authors provide theoretically sound convergence rate guarantees for both methods by using the concept of an inexact oracle. The paper builds upon existing work on inexact oracle methods [7, 16, 25] and extends them to the specific context of Supervised PageRank learning. The authors demonstrate that their methods outperform the state-of-the-art on a real-world web ranking task.

## Strengths

**Technical Soundness and Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with rigorous theoretical analysis. The authors provide clear proofs of convergence rates for both optimization methods, which is a significant contribution over previous work. The complexity bounds for the algorithms are well-justified and provide practical insights into the trade-offs between accuracy and computational cost. The paper carefully handles the challenges of inexact oracle information and provides precise bounds on the required accuracy of the lower-level approximations. The experimental results on a real-world web ranking task provide strong empirical validation of the theoretical claims, with statistically significant improvements over the state-of-the-art method.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a logical progression from problem formulation to theoretical analysis and experimental validation. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined throughout. The connection between the theoretical framework and the practical application is clearly articulated, making the paper accessible to researchers in optimization and machine learning. The supplementary materials provide sufficient detail for reproducibility.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution to the literature on Supervised PageRank and optimization with inexact oracles. While previous work [21] established the Supervised PageRank framework, it lacked convergence guarantees. This paper provides the first theoretically guaranteed optimization methods for this problem. The authors' adaptation of random gradient-free methods [16] to constrained optimization with inexact oracles is novel in this specific context, as they explicitly address the constraints inherent in the parameter space. The paper properly situates its work within the existing literature and clearly differentiates its contributions from previous approaches.

**Significance:** The problem of learning Supervised PageRank is important for web ranking applications, and the proposed methods provide both theoretical guarantees and practical improvements. The paper demonstrates that their algorithms outperform the state-of-the-art in terms of ranking quality, which has direct implications for search engine performance. The theoretical framework developed for optimization with inexact oracles could potentially be applied to other large-scale optimization problems in machine learning.

## Weaknesses

The paper is largely strong, but has a few minor weaknesses:

1. The paper assumes local convexity in the neighborhood of the optimum for the gradient-based method, which might limit the applicability in some cases. While this is a common assumption in non-convex optimization, a brief discussion of how this might affect practical applications would strengthen the paper.

2. The experimental section, while showing clear improvements, could be enhanced with a more detailed analysis of the trade-offs between computational cost and accuracy across different graph sizes. The paper mentions studying dependency on graph sizes but doesn't provide sufficient detail in the main text.

3. The paper references NIPS 2016, but doesn't explicitly compare with other NIPS papers on similar topics (e.g., optimization methods for graph-based learning). However, the related work section appears comprehensive.

## Recommendation

The paper makes a significant contribution to the field by providing theoretically guaranteed optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, the experimental validation is compelling, and the paper is well-written. The authors successfully address a gap in the literature by providing convergence rate guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods, which previous work lacked.

Pro: Theoretical contribution with rigorous convergence rate guarantees for both optimization methods; clear experimental validation showing improvements over state-of-the-art; well-organized and clearly written.

Con: Minor limitation in the assumption of local convexity for the gradient-based method; experimental section could provide more detailed analysis of computational trade-offs.

The paper meets all NIPS criteria: it is technically sound with well-supported claims, clearly written, substantially novel compared to previous work (particularly NIPS 2016 proceedings), and addresses a significant problem with practical implications for web ranking applications. The authors have made a valuable contribution to the field of optimization for graph-based learning.

I recommend acceptance.
