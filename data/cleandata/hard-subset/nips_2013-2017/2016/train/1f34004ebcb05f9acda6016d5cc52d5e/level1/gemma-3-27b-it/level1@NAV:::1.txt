## Review of “Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods”

This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, a ranking technique extending traditional PageRank by incorporating node and edge features. The core contribution lies in proposing both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods for this non-convex problem, accompanied by theoretical convergence guarantees – a notable improvement over existing approaches. The authors leverage the concept of an inexact oracle to achieve these guarantees and demonstrate empirically that their methods outperform the state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.

The work builds upon the Supervised PageRank framework [21], which suffers from a lack of convergence analysis for its gradient-based optimization. The authors address this by carefully constructing algorithms utilizing an inexact oracle, proving convergence rates for both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches. This is a significant step forward, as many existing methods for similar problems rely on strong convexity assumptions or lack theoretical backing.  The comparison to related optimization techniques like those in [16, 7] clearly delineates the challenges posed by the constrained non-convex loss function and the need for an inexact oracle approach. 

The strengths of the paper are substantial. The theoretical convergence guarantees are a key contribution, differentiating this work from purely empirical studies. The thorough exploration of trade-offs between accuracy and computational complexity is also valuable.  The experimental validation on a real-world web ranking dataset demonstrates practical effectiveness.  The detailed complexity analysis provided in Theorems 2 and 4 is particularly commendable. The authors address the limitations of existing methods and provide a clear justification for their approach.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. While the experimental results show improvements, the magnitude of those improvements isn’t dramatically highlighted. Further analysis of the ranking quality, beyond just the loss function value, would be beneficial (e.g., NDCG, MAP).  The paper is quite dense and could benefit from more intuitive explanations of the mathematical derivations.  The connection to recent advances in optimization, particularly in non-convex settings (e.g., variance reduction techniques), could be more explicitly discussed. The choice of hyperparameters and justification for specific values (like L = 10^-4) is not fully elaborated.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel theoretical convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods.
*   Addresses a clear gap in the literature regarding convergence analysis of Supervised PageRank optimization.
*   Empirically demonstrates improved performance on a real-world dataset.
*   Thorough analysis of algorithmic complexity.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Experimental improvements, while positive, are not overwhelmingly large.
*   The paper is mathematically dense and could benefit from increased clarity.
*   Limited discussion of related recent advances in optimization.



**Overall:** This is a solid and theoretically grounded paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of ranking and optimization. Despite some minor weaknesses in presentation and experimental analysis, the strong theoretical results and empirical validation warrant acceptance. I recommend accepting this paper.
