- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by incorporating node and edge features into PageRank models.  
- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods with theoretical convergence guarantees: a gradient-based and a gradient-free method.  
- Develops a general framework for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles, extending prior gradient and gradient-free work.  
- Proves convergence rates and provides complexity bounds that account for the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computation (Theorems 1–4).  
- Explicitly distinguishes improvements over prior work (e.g., [21]) by supplying theoretical guarantees missing in earlier methods.  
- Empirical results on Yandex web ranking show GFN significantly outperforms GBP and GBN outperforms GBP on the largest test set.  
- Improvements are statistically significant (paired t-test p < 0.005).  
- Parameter sensitivity analysis indicates the proposed methods are robust to reasonable hyperparameter choices.  
- Gradient-free method avoids computing large-matrix derivatives (practical advantage) but needs more iterations than the gradient-based method.  
- Theoretical claims are technically sound and supported by proofs in the main paper and supplementary materials.  
- Paper is generally clear with consistent notation and logical organization, though some supplementary proofs may be hard to follow.  
- Originality: applying the inexact-oracle optimization framework to Supervised PageRank is a clear advance over prior PageRank optimization work.  
- Significance: fills a theoretical gap by providing convergence guarantees and demonstrates practical advantages for ranking applications.  
- Strengths: first convergence-rate results for Supervised PageRank, novel two-level framework, real-world performance gains, and careful complexity analysis.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on supplementary materials for full proofs may reduce accessibility; experiments lack more comprehensive runtime and parameter analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with acceptance recommended, pending more complete practical efficiency evaluation.
