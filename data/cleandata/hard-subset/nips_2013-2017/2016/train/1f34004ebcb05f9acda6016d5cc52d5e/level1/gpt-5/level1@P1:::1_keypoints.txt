- Paper tackles learning Supervised PageRank parameters with restart/transition probabilities linear in features and a nonconvex pairwise squared hinge loss over query graphs.
- Proposes two two-level algorithms: a gradient-free method on the sphere and an adaptive projected gradient method, both using inexact oracles.
- Uses the Nesterov–Nemirovski linearly convergent iteration (and its derivative) for inner-level stationary distribution and derivative approximation instead of the power method.
- Provides convergence guarantees and complexity bounds, analyzing the trade-off between inner accuracy and outer convergence.
- Theory: precise lemmas quantify inner iteration counts to approximate f(ϕ) and ∇f(ϕ) with explicit dependence on problem parameters, though some constants (e.g., β1) are opaque and proofs are deferred to the supplement.
- Theory: gradient-free analysis yields expected suboptimality bounds for a biased two-point oracle on the unit sphere; adaptive gradient method guarantees convergence to a stationary point with inexact oracle and backtracking on unknown L.
- Experiments: evaluation on Yandex browsing graphs (600 queries) showing lower training loss and faster lower-level convergence than a power-method baseline.
- Experiments: lacks standard ranking metrics (NDCG@k, MAP) on held-out data, no wall-clock time or memory comparisons, limited hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability analysis.
- Clarity: generally readable but marred by typos, notation inconsistencies (ℓ1 vs ℓ2), and reliance on supplementary material for key definitions and proofs.
- Originality: meaningful extension of zeroth-order and inexact-oracle methods to constrained/noisy settings and novel application of Nesterov–Nemirovski to Supervised PageRank learning.
- Significance: relevant to optimization researchers working with inexact oracles and Markov-chain models; limited practical ranking impact due to narrow baselines and evaluation.
- Pros: provides convergence-rate guarantees for both methods, explicit inner-outer error coupling, practical lower-level solver improvement, and empirical gains vs prior Supervised PageRank method.
- Cons: empirical evaluation is limited (no standard IR metrics, runtime/memory, or broader baselines), some theoretical assumptions are restrictive or under-emphasized, and presentation needs polishing.
- Practicality concern: gradient-free method’s cost per iteration and dependence on feature dimension m are not experimentally quantified.
- Suggestions: report NDCG/MAP on held-out queries, provide wall-clock/memory and inner-vs-outer cost breakdowns, compare to mainstream learning-to-rank baselines, and analyze hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability.
- Suggestions: clarify and bound constants like β1 in the main text, unify norm usage, justify α/R choices and positivity constraints, and cite noisy derivative-free methods (e.g., SPSA) to position contributions.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept — solid and technically interesting optimization contributions but needs stronger, standard empirical validation and presentation improvements.
