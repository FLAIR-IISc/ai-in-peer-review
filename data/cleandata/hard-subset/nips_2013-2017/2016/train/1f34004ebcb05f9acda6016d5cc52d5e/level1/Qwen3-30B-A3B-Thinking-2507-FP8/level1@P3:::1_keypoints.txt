- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by proposing two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with convergence guarantees, extending Nesterov's inexact-oracle framework to constrained problems.  
- Strong technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis and detailed proofs; complexity bounds justify trade-offs between approximation accuracy and cost.  
- Empirical results show clear superiority over prior method [21] on a real-world web ranking task, but experiments lack dataset details and comparisons with other optimizers (e.g., SGD variants).  
- Well-structured and clearly written overall, but very dense technically; would benefit from more intuitive explanations of key theorems.  
- Original contributions: first convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank, novel extension of gradient-free methods to constrained inexact-oracle settings, and a general framework for inexact first-order oracles in non-convex problems.  
- Theoretically and practically significant: advances constrained non-convex optimization theory and improves real-world ranking performance; practical computational benefits need further discussion.  
- Strengths: first convergence rates for the problem, novel method extensions, comprehensive complexity analysis, empirical superiority, and rigorous proofs.  
- Weaknesses: limited experimental baselines and dataset description, insufficient intuitive presentation of theoretical results, and unclear practical computational advantages beyond ranking quality.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution with practical impact, though empirical evaluation could be strengthened.
