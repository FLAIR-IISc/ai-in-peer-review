**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes two optimization methods, gradient-based and gradient-free, to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem of learning Supervised PageRank models. The authors introduce the concept of an inexact oracle to provide theoretically guaranteed convergence rates for both methods. The paper also presents experimental results comparing the performance of the proposed methods with the state-of-the-art gradient-based method on a web page ranking problem.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on Supervised PageRank [21] and extends the framework of random gradient-free methods [1, 16, 7] to handle constrained optimization problems with inexact oracle. The authors also draw inspiration from the work on gradient methods with inexact oracle [7] and adapt it to the case of non-convex optimization problems.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of the proposed optimization methods, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in solving the web page ranking problem, outperforming the state-of-the-art gradient-based method.
3. The paper introduces a novel concept of an inexact oracle, which is a useful tool for handling optimization problems with uncertain or noisy data.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper assumes that the objective function is convex in a small vicinity of the optimal solution, which may not always be the case in practice.
2. The computational complexity of the proposed methods is high, which may limit their applicability to large-scale problems.
3. The paper does not provide a comprehensive comparison of the proposed methods with other optimization algorithms, which would be useful for understanding their relative strengths and weaknesses.

1. The development of a gradient-free optimization method for general convex problems with inexact zero-order oracle.
2. The adaptation of the gradient method with inexact oracle to the case of non-convex optimization problems.
