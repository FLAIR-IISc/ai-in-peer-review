- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods for Supervised PageRank with provable convergence rates.  
- Uses an inexact oracle and a linearly convergent lower-level approximation to enable theoretical guarantees.  
- Provides detailed trade-off analysis between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Experimental results on a real-world ranking task show improved ranking quality over the state-of-the-art [21].  
- Presents both gradient-based and gradient-free algorithms, widening applicability.  
- Offers a comprehensive related-work survey situating the contribution within PageRank and optimization literature.  

- Paper is dense and difficult to follow; many lemmas/theorems and frequent supplementary references disrupt flow.  
- Analysis depends on assumptions (e.g., convexity of f(ϕ) on Φ) that may not hold in practice.  
- Performance may be sensitive to hyperparameters (L, ε, R); paper lacks practical tuning guidance.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset/task, reducing generalizability.  
- Contribution is largely incremental rather than transformative.  

- For acceptance: resolves a major limitation by providing convergence guarantees and shows empirical improvement over prior work.  
- Against acceptance: complexity limits accessibility, assumptions and narrow experiments weaken generalizability, and novelty is modest.  

- Overall: solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, add hyperparameter guidance, and expand experiments.
