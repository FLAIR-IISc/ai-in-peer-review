- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank with proven convergence-rate guarantees.
- Core contribution is providing convergence rate guarantees missing in prior work (notably [21]).
- Uses inexact oracle information to handle computational challenges of exact gradient/value computation.
- Experimental results claim superiority over the state-of-the-art on a real-world dataset.
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear connection to limitations of existing PageRank algorithms.
- Problem setting is standard, well-defined, and the link to prior work is explicit.
- Extensive theoretical analysis with clearly stated assumptions and supporting lemmas.
- Theoretical proofs appear rigorous, addressing both methods’ convergence properties.
- Mathematical derivations are lengthy, dense, and hard to follow, reducing accessibility.
- Heavy and complex notation increases reader effort and decreases clarity.
- Experimental evaluation is reasonable but somewhat limited in scope and depth.
- Reported empirical improvements are not always large; magnitude of gains is sometimes modest.
- Ablation studies (e.g., on accuracy parameter N) are insufficient and need expansion.
- Convergence guarantees focus on local/stationary behavior; sensitivity to initialization and escaping local optima is not addressed.
- Lacks explicit discussion linking theoretical convergence rates to observed empirical convergence speeds.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and significant; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand experiments.
