- Paper proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods with inexact-oracle analysis to learn Supervised PageRank models and provides convergence rate guarantees.  
- Technical strength: rigorous theoretical analysis with clear convergence proofs and well-justified complexity bounds.  
- Experimental strength: empirical validation on real-world web ranking showing statistically significant improvements over state-of-the-art.  
- Clarity: well-organized, consistently notated, with clear connection between theory and practice and reproducible supplementary material.  
- Originality: first to provide theoretically guaranteed optimization methods for Supervised PageRank and novel adaptation of random gradient-free methods to constrained inexact-oracle settings.  
- Significance: important for web ranking applications and offers a theoretical framework potentially applicable to other large-scale ML optimizations.  
- Weakness: gradient-based method assumes local convexity near the optimum, which may limit applicability and needs brief practical discussion.  
- Weakness: experimental section lacks detailed analysis of computational cost vs. accuracy trade-offs across different graph sizes.  
- Weakness: references NIPS 2016 but does not explicitly compare with other NIPS works on related optimization-for-graphs topics.  
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance due to rigorous theory, strong experiments, clear writing, and significant practical implications.  
- Pro: substantial theoretical contribution with convergence guarantees and strong empirical improvements.  
- Con: minor limitation from local convexity assumption and insufficient experimental trade-off analysis.
