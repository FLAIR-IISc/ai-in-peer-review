- Tackles learning Supervised PageRank with restart/transition probabilities linear in features and a pairwise squared-hinge loss over query-dependent graphs.  
- Proposes two two-level algorithms: (i) gradient-based method for constrained nonconvex problems with an inexact first-order oracle, and (ii) random gradient-free method for constrained convex problems with an inexact zero-order oracle.  
- Uses Nesterov–Nemirovski (NN) for approximate stationary distributions and generalizes it to approximate derivatives, yielding tunable accuracy and explicit arithmetic counts.  
- Integrates lower-level approximation accuracy into provable upper-level convergence, providing total complexity bounds that trade off inner/outer work.  
- Builds on prior inexact-oracle and zero/first-order optimization literature and prior supervised PageRank/graph ranking work; novelty is in constrained inexact-oracle handling and coupling inner/outer complexities.  
- Theoretical contributions are technically sound: clear oracle-error models, convergence guarantees for both methods, and expected bias/variance trade-offs in parameters.  
- Gradient-based method guarantees stationarity under an inexact oracle for nonconvex problems; gradient-free method relies on spherical smoothing with bounded additive oracle error.  
- Assumptions: gradient-free requires convexity of feasible set and interior minimizer, an unverified local convexity assumption in an otherwise nonconvex problem.  
- Empirics show improved training loss over prior Supervised PageRank and faster convergence of NN vs. power iteration, but are limited in scope.  
- Experimental limitations: small dataset (600 queries, ~1.7k pairs), reporting only training objective (no NDCG/MAP), missing runtime/iteration breakdowns, and few baselines beyond [21].  
- Clarity is mostly acceptable but dense; notation, constants, and some derivations are opaque without the supplement, with typos and inconsistent references.  
- Originality is incremental methodologically but stronger in integrating NN-based inner approximations with end-to-end complexity analysis for ranking tasks.  
- Theoretical significance is moderate and potentially transferable; practical significance is limited without more comprehensive experiments and standard LTR baselines.  
- Strengths: fills a real gap (convergence with inexact inner solves), solid oracle-error modeling and convergence analyses, NN-based speedups, and derivative approximation with tunable accuracy.  
- Weaknesses: gradient-free theoretical reliance on convexity, limited empirical validation, key proofs/constants relegated to supplement, and incomplete/poorly formatted references.  
- Suggested improvements: add NDCG/MAP tests, stronger LTR baselines (e.g., LambdaMART), wall-clock and iteration breakdowns, ablations on δ/τ/N, and clearer derivative-algorithm description plus complexity table.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled algorithms addressing a real methodological gap with provable guarantees and promising empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: narrow empirical validation, incremental novelty over existing inexact-oracle frameworks, and unverified convexity assumption for the gradient-free method.  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject; solid theory but insufficient empirical evidence and clarity for acceptance.
