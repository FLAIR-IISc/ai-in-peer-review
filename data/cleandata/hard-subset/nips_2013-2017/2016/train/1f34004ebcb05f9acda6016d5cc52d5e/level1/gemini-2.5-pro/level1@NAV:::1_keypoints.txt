- Addresses non-convex parameter learning for Supervised PageRank with node and edge features.  
- Identifies limitation in Zhukovskii et al. (2015): use of an inexact gradient (power method) lacks theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization algorithms: a gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method.  
- Provides formal convergence rate guarantees by framing the problem with an inexact oracle.  
- Adapts general optimization techniques (Nesterov, Ghadimi, Lan) to the constrained, non-convex setting.  
- Theoretical contributions summarized in Theorems 1–4 establishing convergence results.  
- Empirical results show the proposed methods outperform prior state-of-the-art on a real-world web ranking task.  
- Strength — significance: first convergence proofs for learning Supervised PageRank, filling a notable theoretical gap.  
- Strength — originality: novel and non-trivial application/adaptation of advanced optimization theory to this problem.  
- Strength — clarity: well-structured exposition and clear distinction between lower-level oracle and upper-level updates.  
- Weakness — clarity/density: presentation is dense and requires familiarity with cited optimization literature.  
- Weakness — assumption: gradient-free analysis assumes local convexity near a local minimum, a strong simplification for non-convex problems.  
- Weakness — novelty: main contribution is application/adaptation of known methods rather than inventing new algorithms.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves an open theoretical problem, shows empirical superiority, and applies rigorous optimization theory to a practical ML problem.  
- Argument against acceptance: limited methodological novelty beyond adapting existing techniques.  
- Overall judgment: strengths and theoretical plus empirical advances outweigh weaknesses; paper is a solid, important advance for graph-based ranking.
