- Summary: Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods for non-convex Supervised PageRank, introduces an inexact oracle, gives convergence guarantees, and presents experiments versus a state-of-the-art method.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Supervised PageRank and random gradient-free methods, extending inexact-oracle gradient methods to constrained, non-convex settings.  
- Strength: Provides thorough convergence analysis for both proposed optimization methods.  
- Strength: Experimental results show improved web-ranking performance over the state-of-the-art gradient-based method.  
- Strength: Introduces the novel concept of an inexact oracle for handling noisy or uncertain optimization.  
- Weakness: Assumes local convexity near the optimum, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Proposed methods have high computational complexity, limiting scalability.  
- Weakness: Lacks comprehensive comparisons with other optimization algorithms.  
- Originality: Develops a gradient-free method for convex problems with an inexact zero-order oracle.  
- Originality: Adapts gradient methods with inexact oracle to non-convex optimization.  
- Originality: Applies the proposed methods to the web page ranking problem.  
- Significance: Offers meaningful contributions to optimization and ML with potential impact on search and recommendation systems.  
- Quality: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and supported by experiments.  
- Clarity: Explanations, notation, and mathematical derivations are clear and consistent.  
- For acceptance: Original, significant contributions with solid theoretical analysis and empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: Reliance on local convexity assumption, high computational cost, and incomplete baseline comparisons.
