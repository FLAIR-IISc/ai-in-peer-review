- Summary: Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with inexact-oracle convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank.  
- Quality: Strong technical rigor with formal problem setup and provable convergence and complexity results (Theorems 1–4).  
- Experiments: Empirical validation on real-world web ranking shows consistent improvement over prior state-of-the-art [21].  
- Clarity: Well-organized and clearly written overall, but some technical sections (e.g., 4.1, 5.1) need more intuitive explanation.  
- Originality: First work to provide convergence rate guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning and novel adaptation of inexact-oracle methods.  
- Significance: Relevant and practically important for search and recommendation systems, offering both theoretical and empirical advances.  
- Strength: Provides the first theoretical convergence guarantees for this problem setting.  
- Strength: Thorough complexity analysis with practical implementation guidance.  
- Strength: Clear experimental validation demonstrating empirical superiority over [21].  
- Strength: Proper treatment of the inexact oracle setting critical for PageRank computations.  
- Strength: Well-structured, technically sound presentation suitable for NIPS audience.  
- Weakness: Some technical sections are hard to follow for readers without deep optimization background.  
- Weakness: Choice of hyperparameter R = 0.99 is insufficiently justified.  
- Weakness: Lacks more ablation studies to isolate parameter impacts.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to other general-purpose optimization methods beyond [21] are limited.  
- Pro: Substantial theoretical contribution addressing an important literature gap.  
- Pro: Strong experimental evidence of practical superiority.  
- Pro: Advances optimization literature by applying inexact-oracle methods to this domain.  
- Pro: Complexity analysis gives valuable practical guidance.  
- Pro: Clear practical significance for web search and ranking applications.  
- Con: Some sections are difficult without deep optimization knowledge.  
- Con: Certain hyperparameter choices lack justification.  
- Con: Limited exploration of convergence speed vs. accuracy trade-offs.  
- Con: Limited baseline comparisons beyond the primary prior work.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — significant theoretical and empirical contribution with clear practical impact.
