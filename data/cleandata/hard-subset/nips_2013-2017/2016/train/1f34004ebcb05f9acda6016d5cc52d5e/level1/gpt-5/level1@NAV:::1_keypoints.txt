- Problem/formulation: Learns Supervised PageRank by casting parameter estimation as a constrained, generally nonconvex loss minimization where node/edge features affect restart and transition probabilities.  
- Two-level algorithmic scheme: Lower level approximates query-dependent stationary distributions and derivatives via Nesterov–Nemirovski averaging; upper level uses either a random gradient-free inexact zero-order oracle or an adaptive projected gradient with an inexact first-order oracle and unknown Lipschitz.  
- Theoretical contributions: Derives convergence-rate guarantees and complexity bounds in terms of inner accuracy and prescribes inner/outer accuracy balancing to meet a target tolerance.  
- Empirical findings: On a Yandex browsing-graph dataset both methods reduce loss compared to the prior supervised PageRank baseline, and the lower-level routine is faster than the power method.  
- Literature connections: Builds on and extends work on PageRank/HITS, supervised random walks, inexact-oracle optimization, random gradient-free methods, and nonconvex first-order methods by handling constraints and inexact oracles in this application.  
- Novelty/quality: The constrained inexact-oracle formulations and their application here are careful and, to the reviewer’s knowledge, new.  
- Key theoretical concern: The gradient-free guarantee requires convexity of Φ while the problem is nonconvex; the paper asserts local convexity without empirical or theoretical verification.  
- Experimental limitations: Evaluation is narrow—missing standard IR metrics (NDCG/MAP), lacks comparisons to strong LTR baselines (e.g., RankSVM/LambdaMART) and other graph-ranking learners.  
- Potential leakage: Stopping criteria and tuning appear to reference test sets; validation/data-split protocol is unclear.  
- Clarity and presentation: Paper is dense with important constants and proofs in the supplement, making tightness and implementation details hard to assess; stopping criteria and data splits need clearer exposition.  
- Reproducibility issues: Uses proprietary data, unclear code availability, and only brief sensitivity analyses (N1/N2, α, graph size).  
- Practical guidance lacking: Limited discussion/experiments to justify practical parameter choices (L, ε, α) and to probe the local convexity assumption.  
- Pros summary: New convergence guarantees for inexact-oracle methods in constrained settings, useful inner/outer trade-off analysis, improved lower-level complexity, and real-data loss improvements.  
- Cons summary: Narrow empirical scope, possible evaluation leakage, unverified convexity assumption for gradient-free method, limited practical parameter guidance, and reproducibility concerns.  
- Suggested fixes: Add proper validation split (no test-driven stopping), report NDCG/MAP, compare to standard LTR and graph baselines, include public datasets, probe or justify local convexity, and clarify constants and implementation details.
