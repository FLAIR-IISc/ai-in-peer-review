- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by incorporating node/edge features into PageRank-based ranking.
- Proposes both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods tailored to the non-convex problem.
- Key theoretical contribution: convergence guarantees for both methods using an inexact oracle framework.
- Leverages inexact oracle concept to overcome lack of strong convexity and provide provable rates.
- Provides detailed complexity analysis, with notable results in Theorems 2 and 4.
- Empirical evaluation on a real-world web ranking dataset shows improvements over prior methods.
- Explores trade-offs between accuracy and computational complexity in the proposed algorithms.
- Builds on prior Supervised PageRank work and fills a gap in convergence analysis for gradient-based approaches.
- Experimental gains are positive but not dramatically large, limiting practical impact clarity.
- Evaluation focuses on loss reduction; lacks broader ranking quality metrics such as NDCG or MAP.
- Paper is mathematically dense and would benefit from more intuitive explanations of derivations.
- Insufficient discussion of recent non-convex optimization advances (e.g., variance reduction techniques).
- Some hyperparameter choices (e.g., L = 10^-4) are not fully justified or explained.
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical and empirical contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
