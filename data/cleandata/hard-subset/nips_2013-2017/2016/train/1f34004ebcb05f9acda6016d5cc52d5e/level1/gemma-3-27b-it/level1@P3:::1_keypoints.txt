- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free two-level algorithms to learn Supervised PageRank parameters, with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Tackles non-convex optimization by estimating stationary distributions (and derivatives) at the lower level and using inexact oracle information at the upper level.  
- Provides convergence rate guarantees for both methods, filling a gap left by prior work (e.g., [21]).  
- Explicitly models node and edge features to improve PageRank beyond standard formulations.  
- Thorough theoretical treatment of the inexact oracle from the lower-level estimation.  
- Builds a comprehensive optimization framework by adapting existing techniques to the constrained non-convex, inexact setting.  
- Experimental validation on a real-world web ranking dataset shows improvement over the state-of-the-art [21] and untuned Supervised PageRank.  
- Detailed analysis includes computational complexity bounds, aiding assessment of scalability.  
- Clear and well-organized presentation of methods and theoretical results.  
- Dataset used (≈11.7K vertices, ≈7.5K edges per query) is relatively small, raising scalability and generalizability concerns.  
- Gradient-based method sensitive to hyperparameters (notably Lipschitz constant L); choice of L=10^-4 appears arbitrary and needs more analysis.  
- Comparisons are limited largely to [21]; missing benchmarks against broader Learning-to-Rank methods (e.g., RankBoost, LambdaMART).  
- Relies on a local convexity assumption whose validity and impact require further justification.  
- Lacks a detailed ablation study isolating contributions of lower-level method and update rules.  
- Many proofs and detailed explanations are in the supplementary materials; key results should be integrated into the main text.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address hyperparameter sensitivity, broader comparisons, justification of local convexity, and integration of essential supplementary content.
