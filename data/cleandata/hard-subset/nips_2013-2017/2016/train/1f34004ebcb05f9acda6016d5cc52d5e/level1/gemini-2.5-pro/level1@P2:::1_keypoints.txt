- Problem: learning parameters of Supervised PageRank framed as a non-convex loss minimization over node/edge-feature-dependent transition and restart probabilities.  
- Identified weakness in prior work [21]: using the power method at the lower level yields imprecise stationary distributions and derivatives, preventing convergence guarantees.  
- Main contribution: two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with formal convergence rate guarantees.  
- Technical approach: replace the power method with a linearly convergent solver [17] to create an inexact oracle that approximates loss and gradient to arbitrary accuracy.  
- Gradient-free method: extends the random gradient-free approach of [16] to handle constrained problems.  
- Gradient-based method: generalizes the adaptive-step framework of [7] to constrained non-convex problems.  
- Theoretical results: full convergence and complexity analyses provided, with detailed proofs in the supplement.  
- Empirical results: large-scale web ranking experiments show the proposed methods—especially the gradient-free one—outperform the previous state of the art.  
- Quality: technically solid and rigorous; problem formulation and analyses are well-supported.  
- Claims: central claims (first convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank training; empirical superiority) are substantiated.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized, notation consistent; material is dense but accessible to experts.  
- Minor wording suggestion: soften the absolute claim that [16] and [7] “cannot” solve the problem—better to say they cannot be directly applied without the paper’s extensions.  
- Originality: novel synthesis and extension of existing optimization techniques applied to Supervised PageRank, yielding provable guarantees.  
- Significance (practical): makes Supervised PageRank more practical and robust for real-world ranking applications.  
- Significance (theoretical): provides general frameworks for optimization with inexact oracles, of independent interest for large-scale ML problems.  
- Advancement: advances the state of the art by combining theoretical guarantees with empirical improvements.  
- Pros summary: first provable convergence for the problem, elegant application of optimization theory, empirical gains, and clear exposition.  
- Con (1): gradient-free method’s convergence analysis relies on a local convexity assumption despite the global non-convexity of the problem—needs discussion of practical implications.  
- Con (2): complexity bounds in Theorems 2 and 4 are intricate; the paper should briefly discuss practical meaning and scaling with feature dimension m.  
- Recommendation: strengths far outweigh minor weaknesses; strongly recommended for acceptance.
