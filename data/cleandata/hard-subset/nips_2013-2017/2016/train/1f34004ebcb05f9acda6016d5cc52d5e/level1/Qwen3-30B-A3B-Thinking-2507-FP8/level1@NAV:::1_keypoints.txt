- Problem: Learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node and edge features.
- Methods: Two-level optimization with a gradient-based approach and a gradient-free method.
- Theory: Provides convergence rate guarantees and complexity bounds missing in prior work [21].
- Framework: Extends the inexact oracle framework to constrained non-convex parameter learning for PageRank.
- Key innovation: Handling inexact stationary distribution computation while proving convergence rates.
- Quality: Rigorous theoretical analysis with full convergence proofs.
- Experiments: Real ranking task shows statistically significant improvements over the state-of-the-art.
- Writing: Well-structured and clear, though mathematical notation may be dense for non-specialists.
- Originality: First work to provide convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank with these optimization methods.
- Significance: Moderate—demonstrated on a specific ranking task but potentially generalizable to other graph problems.
- Strengths: Strong theoretical guarantees, clear empirical validation, and well-motivated problem formulation.
- Weaknesses: Limited discussion of broader applicability and practical implementation challenges; dense notation may hinder accessibility.
- Recommendation summary: Strong theoretical and empirical contribution, but could better argue broader significance and practical issues.
