## Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of learning parameters in a Supervised PageRank model, a generalization of PageRank that incorporates node and edge features. The authors identify limitations of existing optimization methods for this problem, specifically regarding convergence guarantees and handling constraints. They propose two two-level optimization methods: one gradient-based and one gradient-free, both leveraging a more accurate estimation of the stationary distribution via a linearly convergent method.  They provide convergence analysis for both methods and experimentally demonstrate improved ranking quality compared to a state-of-the-art baseline on a web page ranking task.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:** A key strength is the provision of convergence guarantees for both proposed methods. This is a significant improvement over the existing state-of-the-art, which lacks such guarantees. The convergence rate analysis, though complex, is a valuable contribution.
* **Addressing a Challenging Problem:** The paper addresses a non-convex optimization problem with constraints and inexact oracles, which are common challenges in practical machine learning scenarios.
* **Novel Combination of Techniques:** The paper combines existing optimization techniques (gradient descent, gradient-free optimization, power methods) in a novel way to address the specific challenges of Supervised PageRank learning.  The use of a more accurate stationary distribution estimation is also a good contribution.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed methods outperform the baseline in ranking quality, supporting the theoretical claims.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The paper includes a thorough analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms and explores the trade-offs between accuracy and complexity.
* **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly defines the Supervised PageRank model and the optimization problem, making it accessible to readers.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity and Readability:** The paper is very dense and mathematically involved. The numerous lemmas and theorems, while contributing to rigor, make it challenging to follow the core ideas without significant effort.  More intuitive explanations alongside the mathematical derivations would be beneficial.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments demonstrate improvement, they are limited to a single dataset (Yandex web browsing data).  Testing on additional datasets with different characteristics (e.g., different graph structures, feature distributions) would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Practical Considerations:** The paper focuses heavily on theoretical guarantees. A discussion of practical considerations, such as parameter tuning and scalability to very large graphs, would be valuable.  How sensitive are the results to the choice of hyperparameters?
* **Incremental Improvement:** While the improvements over the baseline are statistically significant, the magnitude of the improvement (as indicated by the experimental results) might not be substantial enough to warrant a strong acceptance.  It feels like an incremental step rather than a breakthrough.
* **Dependence on Prior Work:** A lot of the theoretical foundation relies heavily on prior work (e.g., Nesterov's methods). The paper doesn’t always clearly articulate *how* it builds upon these foundations or what new insights are provided.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and the claims are generally well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.  However, the complexity of the analysis makes it difficult to fully assess.
* **Clarity:** The paper suffers from a lack of clarity due to its density and mathematical notation.  The organization could be improved, and more intuitive explanations would be helpful.
* **Originality:** The combination of techniques and the provision of convergence guarantees are original contributions. However, the underlying techniques are not entirely novel.
* **Significance:** The results are important in the context of Supervised PageRank learning, but the practical impact might be limited. The paper does advance the state-of-the-art, but not dramatically.



**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Provides theoretical guarantees for a challenging optimization problem.
* Demonstrates empirical improvement over the state-of-the-art.
* Offers a novel combination of optimization techniques.
* Thorough analysis of complexity and trade-offs.

**Against:**

* High complexity and low readability.
* Limited scope of experiments.
* Incremental improvement over baseline.
* Heavy reliance on existing work without sufficient clarification of contributions.



**Overall Assessment:**

I rate this paper as a **weak accept**.  The theoretical contributions and convergence guarantees are valuable, and the experimental results are promising. However, the paper's complexity, limited scope of experiments, and incremental nature of the improvement raise concerns.  If the authors could address the clarity issues and expand the experimental validation, the paper would be a stronger contender for acceptance. I suggest the authors focus on simplifying the presentation and highlighting the key novel aspects of their work.
