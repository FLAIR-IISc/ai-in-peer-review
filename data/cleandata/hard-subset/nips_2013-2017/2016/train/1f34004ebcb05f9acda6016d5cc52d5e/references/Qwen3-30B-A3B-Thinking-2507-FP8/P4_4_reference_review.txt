

# Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

This paper proposes two two-level optimization methods for learning the parameters of Supervised PageRank, addressing a significant gap in the existing literature where the state-of-the-art method [21] lacks convergence guarantees. The authors develop a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle and a gradient-free method adapted for constrained optimization with inexact oracle. The work builds on previous Supervised PageRank formulations [21] and extends theoretical optimization frameworks [16, 7] to handle the specific challenges of the PageRank learning problem.

The paper makes several technically sound contributions. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the numerical calculation of the loss function and its gradient using a linearly convergent method [17], which allows them to establish precise complexity bounds. They then develop two novel two-level algorithms with theoretical convergence guarantees, which they prove for both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches. The experimental results on a real-world web page ranking task demonstrate clear improvements over the state-of-the-art method [21] in terms of ranking quality while providing theoretical guarantees that were previously missing.

Regarding quality, the paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis supported by detailed proofs in the supplementary materials. The authors carefully address the challenges of inexact oracle information in the context of constrained optimization problems. The experimental section provides sufficient evidence of the method's effectiveness, with statistical significance testing for the performance improvements. The paper acknowledges limitations, particularly regarding the computational overhead of the two-level approach, and demonstrates how the accuracy of the lower-level algorithm affects the overall complexity.

The clarity of the paper is generally good, with clear problem formulation, well-structured mathematical exposition, and logical flow. The paper effectively uses lemmas and theorems to build its theoretical contributions. However, some sections could benefit from more detailed explanation of the experimental setup, particularly regarding hyperparameter selection and the rationale for specific parameter values used in the experiments.

The originality of the work is strong. The paper addresses a clear gap in the literature (lack of convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank optimization) and proposes two novel methods that combine existing optimization frameworks with the specific requirements of the PageRank learning problem. The adaptation of gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracle is particularly novel. While the paper cites related work on PageRank and optimization methods appropriately, it could more clearly distinguish its contributions from similar recent work on optimization with inexact oracles [25, 26].

The significance of this work is substantial. Web page ranking is a fundamental problem with broad practical applications, and the authors demonstrate that their methods can improve ranking quality while providing theoretical guarantees. The paper's claim that their methods can "increase the reliability and speed of a search engine" is well-supported by the experimental results. The theoretical contributions to optimization with inexact oracles also have broader applicability beyond the specific PageRank problem.

Strengths:
- Provides the first theoretical convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning
- Novel adaptation of gradient-free and gradient-based optimization to constrained problems with inexact oracle
- Comprehensive theoretical analysis with complexity bounds
- Clear experimental validation on a real-world ranking task
- Careful analysis of the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity

Weaknesses:
- The experimental comparison with other optimization methods could be more comprehensive
- The paper could more thoroughly discuss the practical computational overhead of the two-level approach
- Some details about hyperparameter selection in experiments are missing
- The claim about being "the first time" combining these ideas with inexact oracle may be overstated (see [25, 26])

Pro-acceptance arguments:
1. The paper addresses a significant gap in the literature by providing convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank optimization
2. The theoretical contributions are sound and well-justified
3. The experimental results clearly demonstrate superior performance over the state-of-the-art
4. The work has clear practical significance for web ranking applications
5. The paper is well-organized and clearly written

Con-acceptance arguments:
1. Some details about experimental setup and hyperparameter selection are insufficient
2. The paper could more thoroughly discuss the computational trade-offs of the two-level approach
3. The claim about novelty could be more carefully qualified

Overall, this paper makes a solid contribution to both the optimization and information retrieval communities. The theoretical guarantees fill an important gap in the Supervised PageRank literature, and the experimental results validate the practical utility of the proposed methods. The paper meets all the criteria for a high-quality NIPS submission with significant technical contribution, clear presentation, and meaningful results. The work advances the state of the art in both the specific problem of learning Supervised PageRank and the broader area of optimization with inexact oracles.
