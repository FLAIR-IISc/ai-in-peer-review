- Addresses learning parameters in Supervised PageRank, tackling non-convexity, constraints, and inexact loss/gradient computation.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization methods: a gradient-based and a gradient-free approach using iterative stationary distribution approximations.  
- Main contribution: provides convergence guarantees for both methods, filling a gap left by prior work ([21]).  
- Analyzes trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity with detailed complexity analysis.  
- Empirically validates methods on a real-world web page ranking task (Yandex dataset), outperforming prior state-of-the-art [21].  
- Situates work within prior literature (builds on [21], uses power method and draws from [16] and [6]) and compares to standard ranking methods for context.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical guarantees addressing the inexact oracle nature of the problem.  
- Strength: tackles a challenging, large-graph learning problem with a clear two-level methodological presentation.  
- Strength: comprehensive complexity analysis useful for practical deployment.  
- Strength: experimental results show significant improvement over baseline.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning, notably the Lipschitz constant in the gradient-free method.  
- Weakness: missing implementation specifics (e.g., projection operator ΠX, random vector generation) limiting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations in non-convex landscapes (risk of local optima).  
- Weakness: supplementary proofs are lengthy and sometimes repetitive, hurting readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: proves convergence for previously unproven algorithm, addresses a relevant problem, shows empirical gains, and offers complexity insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental validation, lack of implementation details, and insufficient discussion of limitations.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand experiments to more datasets, detail hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity, add implementation details, and discuss limitations in non-convex settings.
