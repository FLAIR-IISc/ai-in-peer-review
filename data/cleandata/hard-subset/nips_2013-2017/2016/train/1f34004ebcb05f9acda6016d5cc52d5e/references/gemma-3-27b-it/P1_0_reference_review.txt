## NIPS 2024 Review: Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of learning parameters in a Supervised PageRank model, where node and edge features influence the random walk dynamics. The core issue is the non-convexity and constrained nature of the optimization problem, coupled with the difficulty of accurately evaluating the loss function and its gradient. The authors propose two two-level optimization methods: one gradient-based and one gradient-free, both utilizing a linearly convergent method for approximating the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk (Method 3.1). They provide convergence guarantees for both methods and analyze the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity.  Experimental results on a web page ranking task demonstrate that their methods outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based approach from prior work.

**Related Work & Context:**

The paper builds upon the established foundations of PageRank and its supervised extensions (Supervised PageRank). It correctly situates itself within the existing literature on random walk ranking models, acknowledging the limitations of purely structural approaches and the need to incorporate node/edge features. The connections to optimization techniques for non-convex problems, particularly gradient-based and gradient-free methods, are clearly stated.  The reference to [6] and subsequent work on inexact gradient estimation is relevant.  The authors clearly articulate the challenges in applying existing optimization algorithms directly to Supervised PageRank due to constraints and oracle inaccuracies.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:** A significant strength is the provision of convergence rate analysis for both the gradient-based and gradient-free methods.  This is a critical contribution, as previous work on Supervised PageRank lacked such guarantees.  The analysis regarding the accuracy of the lower-level approximation (Method 3.1) and its impact on overall convergence is well-developed.
* **Addressing a Challenging Problem:** The paper tackles a genuinely difficult optimization problem inherent in Supervised PageRank, overcoming the limitations of existing methods.
* **Comprehensive Analysis:** The authors carefully analyze the trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy, computational complexity, and convergence rate.  This nuanced understanding is valuable.
* **Practical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed methods, outperforming the state-of-the-art on a real-world web page ranking task. The comparisons on different sized query sets provide a useful perspective.
* **Clear Presentation (Generally):** The paper is largely well-structured and logically organized. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the algorithms are presented clearly.
* **Novelty:**  The adaptation of random gradient-free methods and the development of a convergence framework for inexact oracles in this context appears novel.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Density of Technical Detail:** The paper is *very* dense with mathematical notation and technical details.  While rigorousness is appreciated, this makes the paper challenging to follow for readers not already deeply familiar with the underlying optimization concepts.  More intuitive explanations of the key ideas would be beneficial.
* **Limited Explanation of Algorithm Choices:**  While the authors state advantages of each method (gradient vs. gradient-free), more detailed justification for choosing specific algorithms (e.g., Algorithm 1 and Algorithm 2) within the respective frameworks would be helpful. Why these particular choices and not alternatives?
* **Parameter Sensitivity:** The experiments section briefly mentions sensitivity to parameters (e.g., L in the gradient-free method), but more thorough analysis of parameter tuning and robustness would strengthen the results.  Is the performance highly sensitive to these parameters, or are the methods relatively stable?
* **Experimental Scope:** While the experimental results are positive, the comparison is limited to a single dataset and task.  Exploring the performance of the methods on other graph ranking problems or different types of data would increase confidence in their generalizability.
* **Overly Optimistic Claims:** Phrases like "significantly outperforms" without specific quantitative comparisons and statistical significance testing (beyond mentioning p-values) feel overstated.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound and provides rigorous analysis. The convergence guarantees and the detailed complexity analysis are particularly strong. The proofs (referenced in the supplementary materials) are crucial and contribute to the overall quality.
* **Clarity (6/10):** The paper suffers from a lack of clarity due to its density and the rapid pace at which technical concepts are introduced. More explanation and intuition would significantly improve readability.
* **Originality (8/10):** The combination of random gradient-free methods, inexact oracle optimization, and application to Supervised PageRank is a novel contribution.
* **Significance (7/10):** The results are important for advancing the field of Supervised PageRank and potentially applicable to other graph ranking problems. The theoretical guarantees and improved performance are valuable contributions.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundations and convergence guarantees.
* Novel application of optimization techniques to a challenging problem.
* Demonstrated improvement over the state-of-the-art.
* Thorough analysis of computational complexity and trade-offs.

**Against Acceptance:**

* High level of technical detail may hinder accessibility for a broad audience.
* Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
* Some potential for improved clarity and explanation.

**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper, contingent on minor revisions to improve clarity and address the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  **Expand explanations:** Provide more intuitive explanations of the key concepts and algorithm choices.
2.  **Parameter Analysis:** Conduct a more thorough analysis of parameter sensitivity.
3. **Clarify Significance:** Quantify performance improvements and provide statistical significance testing.
4. **Consider Additional Experiments:**  Exploring performance on additional datasets or tasks would be valuable.

Despite its density, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field with its strong theoretical foundation, novel approach, and promising experimental results. The potential impact of this work warrants acceptance, provided the authors address the suggested revisions.
