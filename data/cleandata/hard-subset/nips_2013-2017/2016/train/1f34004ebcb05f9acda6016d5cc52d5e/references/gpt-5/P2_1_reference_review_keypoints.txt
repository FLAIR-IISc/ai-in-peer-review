- Paper addresses learning Supervised PageRank with transition/restart probabilities linear in features, where loss and gradient must be approximated via iterative PageRank.
- Proposes two two-level methods: a gradient-based method using Nesterov–Nemirovski for stationary distribution and derivative approximations, and a gradient-free method adapting random zeroth-order optimization for bounded-error oracles.
- Provides error bounds for lower-level approximations of f and ∇f with explicit iteration counts and end-to-end arithmetic complexity estimates.
- Extends inexact first-order oracle theory to constrained nonconvex composite problems with backtracking and analyzes gradient-free smoothing for constrained domains with inexact oracles.
- Technical concern: loss uses squared hinge (‖(Aπ)+‖^2) which is nondifferentiable; paper assumes smoothness and needs clarification on subgradients or smoothing.
- Technical concern: gradient-free convergence guarantee assumes local convexity, limiting generality since the problem is generally nonconvex.
- Technical concern: zeroth-order oracle queries x and x+τξ may exit the feasible ball; need to explain how feasibility is enforced (resampling or clipping τ).
- Technical concern: some constants (e.g., β1) and derivations are deferred to supplement; main paper lacks enough detail to assess magnitudes and practical choices.
- Paper is well-structured but marred by typos, inconsistent notation, incomplete references, and many proofs/constants only in supplement.
- Originality: combining known PageRank iteration and inexact-oracle/zeroth-order techniques is incremental but the integration with end-to-end guarantees is a useful contribution.
- Practical significance: problem is relevant and methods are principled, but empirical evidence is limited and not fully convincing.
- Experimental limitations: small dataset (600 queries, ~1.7K pairs), results reported only on training objective, and no standard IR metrics (NDCG/MAP) on held-out data.
- Experimental limitations: comparisons limited to prior Supervised PageRank method; missing comparisons to standard learning-to-rank baselines (e.g., LambdaMART) or simpler feature-based rankers.
- Experimental limitations: lack of wall-clock runtimes and detailed lower-level iteration counts; sensitivity to α and practical parameter effects not reported.
- Pros: addresses an important problem, gives explicit convergence/complexity bounds, extends PageRank derivative computation with accuracy control, and shows improved objective and faster lower-level convergence vs. power method.
- Cons: reliance on local convexity for gradient-free, unresolved nondifferentiability vs. smoothness assumptions, underspecified algorithmic details (boundary handling, choice of τ, δ, N), and weak experimental evaluation.
- Questions for authors: How is nondifferentiability of (·)+ handled in theory/practice (smoothing vs. subgradients)? 
- Questions for authors: How do you ensure x+τξ ∈ Φ near boundaries (resample or shrink τ)? 
- Questions for authors: Can you provide wall-clock runtimes, lower-level iteration counts, and sensitivity to α? 
- Questions for authors: How large is β1 in typical graphs and how does it affect N1/N2 in practice?
- Suggestions: report NDCG/MAP on held-out labels, compare to standard LTR baselines, add runtime plots and ablations (τ, δ, Nesterov–Nemirovski vs. power), and clarify assumptions with brief proof sketches and key constants in the main text.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — technically solid and well-motivated with end-to-end guarantees and promising results, but needs stronger empirical validation and clarification of theoretical assumptions.
