- Tackles learning parameters in Supervised PageRank using two-level gradient-based and gradient-free methods that handle inexact stationary distribution oracles.  
- Provides convergence guarantees and complexity analysis for both methods, including accuracy vs. computational cost trade-offs.  
- Builds on prior Supervised PageRank work and inexact optimization/stationary-distribution approximation literature, improving theory and empirical performance.  
- Strength: Rigorous analysis of optimization with inexact oracle information and convergence rate estimations.  
- Strength: Detailed complexity analysis relevant to scalability.  
- Strength: Empirical results show superiority over prior state-of-the-art on a web ranking task.  
- Strength: Clear problem formulation and logical extension of inexact optimization frameworks.  
- Weakness: Dense, mathematically involved presentation that may be hard to follow; needs more intuitive explanations.  
- Weakness: Insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., α, R, τ, step sizes, Lipschitz constants).  
- Weakness: Experiments limited to a single dataset/task; broader evaluation on other graphs is needed.  
- Weakness: Minor inconsistent notation issues that increase cognitive load.  
- Weakness: The claim of convexity on Φ is weak and requires stronger justification or discussion of non-convexity impacts.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with solid theory, convergence guarantees, and effective empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Paper’s density, limited experimental scope, and unresolved convexity assumption concerns.  
- Overall: Strong contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and justify convexity.  
- Recommendation: Accept (with minor revisions to simplify presentation and expand convexity discussion).
