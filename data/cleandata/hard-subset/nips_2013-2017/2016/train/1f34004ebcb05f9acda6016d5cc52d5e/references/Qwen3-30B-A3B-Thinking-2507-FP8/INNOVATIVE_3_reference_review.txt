

# Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

This paper addresses an important gap in the Supervised PageRank literature by proposing two novel two-level optimization methods with convergence guarantees. The authors recognize that the existing method [21] for learning Supervised PageRank parameters lacks theoretical convergence guarantees due to the inexact nature of the stationary distribution calculation. They propose a gradient-based method using a linearly convergent lower-level approximation of the stationary distribution and a gradient-free method adapted from [16] for constrained optimization with inexact oracle.

The key contributions include: (1) theoretical convergence rates for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods with inexact oracle, (2) analysis of the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and computational complexity, (3) complexity bounds for the two-level algorithms, and (4) experimental validation showing superior performance over the state-of-the-art [21] on a web ranking task.

**Strengths:**
- **Originality:** The paper identifies a significant gap in the Supervised PageRank literature (lack of convergence guarantees) and provides novel solutions to address it. The two-level approach with inexact oracles is a fresh perspective on optimization for graph-based ranking problems.
- **Theoretical Contributions:** The authors provide rigorous convergence analysis for both methods, including complexity bounds. The adaptation of gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracle is particularly novel.
- **Significance:** The problem addressed has clear practical implications for search engines and web ranking. The experimental results demonstrate consistent improvement over the state-of-the-art on real-world data.
- **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized with clear mathematical formulations. The problem setup, methods, and analysis are presented logically.

**Weaknesses:**
- The paper focuses on a specific application (Supervised PageRank) without sufficient discussion of how the proposed optimization framework might generalize to other graph-based learning problems.
- The experimental section, while adequate for the specific task, lacks thorough analysis of the algorithm's behavior on different graph sizes and structures beyond what's presented in Table 1.
- The paper doesn't sufficiently discuss the practical limitations of the proposed methods (e.g., how the lower-level iteration count scales with graph size).
- The theoretical complexity analysis, while present, doesn't provide clear intuition about when the proposed methods would be preferable to existing approaches.

**Comparison to Related Work:**
The paper correctly identifies the gap in [21] and positions its work relative to recent advances in optimization with inexact oracles ([16], [7], [25]). The authors effectively distinguish their work from these prior contributions by addressing the specific challenges of Supervised PageRank (non-convexity, inexact oracle, constrained optimization).

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper makes a strong contribution to the field by providing the first convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank parameters. The two-level optimization framework with inexact oracle is novel and well-motivated. While the paper is technically sound and provides valuable theoretical insights, its broader impact could be strengthened by more comprehensive experiments and discussion of generalizability.

The paper excels in addressing a real-world problem with a theoretically rigorous approach, which aligns well with NIPS' emphasis on both theoretical and practical contributions. The authors have successfully bridged the gap between optimization theory and a specific application domain.

**Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- First paper to provide convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning
- Novel two-level optimization framework with inexact oracle
- Strong experimental validation showing consistent improvement over state-of-the-art
- Clear theoretical analysis with complexity bounds

*Con:*
- Limited discussion of generalizability beyond Supervised PageRank
- Insufficient exploration of practical limitations (e.g., scalability with graph size)
- Experimental section lacks depth in analyzing algorithm behavior across different graph properties

**Recommendation:**

I recommend accepting this paper. While not without minor limitations, the paper's novelty, theoretical rigor, and practical significance outweigh its weaknesses. The authors have identified an important gap in the literature and provided a novel solution with rigorous theoretical guarantees. The paper represents a significant advance in the optimization of graph-based ranking algorithms and contributes valuable insights to the broader field of optimization with inexact oracles. The work is well-suited for NIPS as it combines theoretical innovation with practical impact.
