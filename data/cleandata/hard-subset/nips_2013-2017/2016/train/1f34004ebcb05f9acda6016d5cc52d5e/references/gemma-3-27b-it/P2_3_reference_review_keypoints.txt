- Proposes two-level gradient-based and gradient-free methods with convergence guarantees for learning parameters in Supervised PageRank.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods, improving on prior work.  
- Adapts gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracles and develops a gradient method with similar properties.  
- Analyzes accuracy vs. computational complexity and supplies detailed lemmas and complexity bounds.  
- Empirical evaluation on a real-world web ranking task shows improvements over the state-of-the-art.  
- Connects well to existing optimization literature and explicitly addresses prior limitations.  
- Presentation is dense and heavily mathematical, making it hard for non-experts to follow.  
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset and task, reducing generalizability.  
- Paper lacks adequate analysis of algorithm sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.  
- Improvements over prior methods are useful but incremental rather than transformative.  
- Quality: 8/10 — technically sound and rigorous, but experimental breadth could improve.  
- Clarity: 6/10 — precise but notation-heavy and not easily accessible.  
- Originality: 7/10 — novel application/combination though based on established techniques.  
- Significance: 7/10 — meaningful advance for supervised PageRank, but not a major breakthrough.  
- Arguments for acceptance: delivers theoretical guarantees, novel practical methods, empirical improvements, and strong motivation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dense presentation, narrow experiments, and only incremental performance gains.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, expand experiments, and discuss hyperparameter sensitivity.
