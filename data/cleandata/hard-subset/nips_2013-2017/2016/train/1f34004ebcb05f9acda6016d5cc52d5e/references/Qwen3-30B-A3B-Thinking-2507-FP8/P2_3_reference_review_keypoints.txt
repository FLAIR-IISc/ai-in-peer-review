- Proposes two-level learning for Supervised PageRank with novel gradient-based and gradient-free methods that handle inexact stationary-distribution oracles.  
- Provides rigorous convergence proofs and complexity bounds for both methods on the non-convex loss-minimization problem.  
- Correctly formulates the main challenge: non-convex optimization with inexact oracle due to approximate stationary distributions.  
- Lower-level/upper-level framework is well-motivated: control lower-level approximation accuracy to enable reliable upper-level optimization.  
- Experimental results on the Yandex dataset show both methods outperform prior state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality.  
- Authors analyze trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity, important for practical use.  
- Clear, well-structured writing and consistent mathematical notation; algorithms and proofs are well-documented (supplementary materials included).  
- Uses lemmas to rigorously justify lower-level approximation accuracy critical for upper-level steps.  
- Novel adaptation of random gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracles.  
- Provides the first complexity analysis specific to learning Supervised PageRank, advancing prior work that lacked guarantees.  
- Significance is high: practical improvements in web ranking and broader applicability to graph-based problems (recommendation, social networks).  
- Gradient-free method advantage: avoids derivative calculations on large matrices, useful for scalability.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to a single dataset and task; need broader empirical validation.  
- Weakness: limited direct comparison with other non-convex/inexact-oracle optimization approaches in practice.  
- Weakness: does not explore generalization to other ranking loss functions (pairwise/listwise).  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and empirical contribution; recommended for acceptance.
