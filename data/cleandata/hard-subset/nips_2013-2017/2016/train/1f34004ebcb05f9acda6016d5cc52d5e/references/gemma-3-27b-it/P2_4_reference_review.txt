## Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of learning parameters in a Supervised PageRank model, addressing limitations of existing methods related to convergence guarantees and computational complexity. The authors propose two two-level optimization methods: one gradient-based and one gradient-free. They provide convergence rate analysis for both methods, investigate the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall complexity, and demonstrate improvements over the state-of-the-art (SOTA) method [21] on a web page ranking task.  A significant contribution is the theoretical analysis supporting convergence, which was previously lacking in the SOTA.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:** A major strength is the provision of convergence guarantees for both proposed algorithms. The SOTA method lacked such guarantees, making this work a significant step forward. The authors rigorously analyze the convergence rates and complexity bounds.
* **Comprehensive Analysis:** The paper thoroughly investigates the interplay between lower-level accuracy (in approximating the stationary distribution) and the overall computational cost.  This trade-off analysis is valuable for practical implementation.
* **Two Approaches:** Providing both gradient-based and gradient-free methods broadens the applicability of the work. The gradient-free approach avoids derivative calculations, potentially useful in scenarios where gradients are difficult to compute.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on a real-world web ranking task demonstrate performance improvements over the SOTA method, supporting the theoretical findings.  The ablation studies regarding parameter choices (e.g., for the gradient-free method) are also commendable.
* **Clear Presentation of Math:** The mathematical formulations are generally well-presented and easy to follow, aiding in understanding the proposed methods and analyses.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Presentation:** While mathematically sound, the paper is dense and can be challenging to read. The constant jumping between theoretical results, lemmas, and algorithms hinders readability.  A more streamlined presentation of the core ideas could improve accessibility.
* **Incremental Improvement:** While demonstrating improvements over the SOTA, the magnitude of the performance gains isn't dramatically substantial. The paper would be strengthened by demonstrating applicability to a wider range of graph types or highlighting scenarios where the proposed methods are *significantly* better.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:**  The experiments are focused on a single dataset. Expanding the experimental evaluation to other ranking tasks or graph datasets would bolster the robustness of the findings.
* **Dependence on Existing Work:** The work heavily relies on previous publications by the same authors (e.g., [21]). While building upon prior work is normal, the paper sometimes feels like a series of incremental improvements to an existing framework rather than a radically new approach.
* **Notation Overload:** The paper uses a lot of notation, making it hard to keep track of the variables.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, and the experimental results support the claims. The thoroughness of the analysis is a strength. However, the incremental nature of the improvements raises concerns about the overall impact.
* **Clarity:**  The paper suffers from a lack of clarity. The dense mathematical formulations and frequent references to lemmas make it challenging to follow the main ideas.  The organization could be improved by separating the theoretical contributions more distinctly.
* **Originality:** The originality lies in providing convergence guarantees and a detailed complexity analysis for Supervised PageRank learning. However, the core ideas build heavily on prior work, particularly [21]. It's a solid contribution but not a groundbreaking leap.
* **Significance:** The work addresses an important problem in graph ranking. The theoretical guarantees and improvements in ranking quality are valuable contributions. However, the limited scope of experiments and incremental nature of the improvements temper the significance.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Provides much-needed theoretical guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning.
* Demonstrates improved performance over the SOTA method.
* Thoroughly analyzes the trade-off between accuracy and complexity.
* Offers both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches, increasing applicability.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper is dense and difficult to read.
* The performance improvements, while present, are not substantial.
* The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset.
* The work relies heavily on previous publications by the same authors.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **weak accept**. While it addresses an important problem and provides valuable theoretical contributions, the lack of clarity, incremental improvements, and limited experimental scope prevent it from being a strong acceptance. The theoretical guarantees are a significant strength, and the empirical results provide some support for the claims. However, the paper would benefit greatly from improved clarity, a broader experimental evaluation, and a more compelling demonstration of its significance.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions focusing on clarity and expanding the experimental validation.
