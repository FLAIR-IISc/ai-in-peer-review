### Summary and Relation to Prior Work

This paper addresses the problem of learning parameters for Supervised PageRank, a model introduced by Zhukovskii et al. [21] that enriches the standard PageRank algorithm by incorporating node and edge features. The learning task is formulated as a non-convex loss-minimization problem. The authors identify a critical gap in the existing state-of-the-art optimization method [21]: it relies on the power method to approximate the stationary distribution and its derivatives, which constitutes an inexact first-order oracle, but it lacks any theoretical guarantee of convergence to a stationary point.

The main contribution of this paper is to propose two new two-level optimization algorithms for this problem, both of which come with formal convergence guarantees.
1.  **A Gradient-Based Method (GBN):** This method adapts and generalizes the framework for optimization with inexact oracles, particularly related to the work of Devolder et al. [25] and Ghadimi & Lan [7]. The authors extend this framework to handle constrained, non-convex problems and incorporate an adaptive line-search procedure to estimate the local smoothness constant.
2.  **A Gradient-Free Method (GFN):** This method builds upon the random gradient-free optimization literature, particularly Nesterov [16]. The authors extend this approach to handle constrained optimization problems and, crucially, to work with an inexact zero-order oracle where the function value can only be computed with a known, bounded error.

For both proposed methods, the authors provide a rigorous analysis of the computational complexity. They establish a clear trade-off between the accuracy of the lower-level oracle (approximating the PageRank vector and its derivatives using the method from [17]) and the number of iterations required by the upper-level optimization algorithm to reach a desired solution accuracy. The paper concludes with experiments on a real-world web page ranking task, demonstrating that the proposed methods outperform the previous state-of-the-art method [21].

### Strengths and Weaknesses

#### Quality

**Strengths:**
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper is technically very strong. The core theoretical contributions—providing convergence proofs for gradient-based and gradient-free methods under a constrained setting with inexact oracles—are rigorous and well-founded. The analysis leading to Theorems 1-4 appears correct and addresses a significant limitation in the prior work.
*   **Completeness:** The work is a complete research contribution. It starts from a well-defined practical problem, identifies a theoretical gap in existing solutions, develops novel theoretical tools to close this gap, instantiates these tools into concrete algorithms, and validates them with empirical results.
*   **Honest Evaluation:** The authors are careful to state their assumptions, such as the local convexity required for the gradient-free method's convergence analysis (Theorem 2). They also clearly delineate the contributions of the lower-level oracle (Lemmas 1 and 2) from the upper-level optimization algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**
*   **Experimental Depth:** While the experiments show that the proposed methods achieve a lower loss value than the baseline, the comparison could be more comprehensive. The paper does not report wall-clock time or total floating-point operations, which would directly connect the empirical results to the theoretical complexity analysis. The baseline method (GBP) is stopped based on a progress criterion, which makes it difficult to compare the number of iterations directly against the proposed methods, which are run until a pre-defined accuracy ε is met.

#### Clarity

**Strengths:**
*   **Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized. The separation of the general optimization frameworks (Sections 4.1 and 5.1) from their specific application to the Supervised PageRank problem (Sections 4.2 and 5.2) makes the paper's logic easy to follow. This structure effectively showcases both the general-purpose nature of the optimization methods and their successful application.

**Weaknesses:**
*   **Density of Writing:** The paper is quite dense and heavy on mathematical notation. While precise, this can make it challenging for readers not already deeply versed in optimization theory. For instance, the interplay between different accuracy parameters (δ, δ1, δ2, ε) and iteration counts (N, N1, N2, M) is complex. A small table summarizing the key notation might improve readability.
*   The proofs are relegated to supplementary materials, which is standard practice but prevents a full, self-contained verification of the theoretical claims from the main paper alone.

#### Originality

**Strengths:**
*   **Novel Combination of Ideas:** The primary originality lies in the rigorous synthesis of two distinct fields: graph-based ranking algorithms and modern optimization theory for inexact oracles. While the Supervised PageRank model and the core optimization concepts existed previously, this paper is the first to formally connect them, providing a principled way to handle the inherent inexactness of computing PageRank within an optimization loop.
*   **Novel Extensions to Existing Methods:** The contributions are not merely applications of existing work. The extension of random gradient-free methods to the constrained setting with a known error bound is a novel and useful contribution. Similarly, the adaptive gradient method for non-convex constrained problems with an inexact first-order oracle is a valuable addition to the optimization toolkit.

#### Significance

**Strengths:**
*   **Practical Importance:** The paper addresses a problem of significant practical relevance in web search, social network analysis, and recommendation systems. By providing optimization algorithms with convergence guarantees, this work makes the Supervised PageRank model more reliable and robust, which is crucial for real-world production systems.
*   **Broader Impact on Machine Learning:** The presented optimization frameworks have significance beyond the PageRank problem. Many machine learning models involve a two-level structure where a sub-problem must be solved iteratively (e.g., MCMC-based estimation, solving fixed-point equations, variational inference). This paper provides a clear and rigorous blueprint for how to analyze the convergence and complexity of such systems under the "inexact oracle" paradigm.
*   **Advancing the State of the Art:** The paper clearly advances the state of the art for learning Supervised PageRank. It replaces an ad-hoc optimization procedure with principled algorithms that are not only theoretically guaranteed to converge but also empirically superior in terms of solution quality.

### Conclusion and Recommendation

This is a high-quality paper that makes significant contributions to both optimization theory and its application in machine learning. The theoretical work is rigorous, addressing a key open question about the convergence of learning algorithms for Supervised PageRank. The proposed gradient-based and gradient-free methods, complete with convergence guarantees and complexity analyses, are important contributions in their own right and are of broad interest. The experimental results, while they could be more detailed, successfully demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed methods. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, albeit dense.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
1.  Provides the first (to my knowledge) convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank.
2.  Develops novel and broadly applicable extensions to optimization methods for constrained problems with inexact oracles.
3.  The work is a complete package of theory, algorithms, and experiments, and is technically very sound.
4.  Addresses a problem of high practical and academic significance.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
1.  The experimental evaluation could be strengthened with wall-clock time comparisons and analysis against a wider range of modern optimizers.

The strengths of this paper substantially outweigh its minor weaknesses. It represents a solid and important contribution to the NIPS community. I recommend acceptance.
