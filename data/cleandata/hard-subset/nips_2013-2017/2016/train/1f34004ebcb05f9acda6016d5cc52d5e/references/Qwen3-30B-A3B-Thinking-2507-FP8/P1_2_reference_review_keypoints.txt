- Addresses gap in Supervised PageRank by proposing two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with controllable lower-level approximations and convergence guarantees.  
- Provides a gradient-based method with convergence guarantees for non-convex problems and a gradient-free method adapted to constrained optimization with inexact oracle.  
- Delivers thorough complexity analysis and theoretical convergence rates with detailed proofs (Lemmas 1-2, Theorems 1-4).  
- Empirically validates methods on a real-world web ranking task, showing better ranking quality than prior state-of-the-art [21].  
- Clearly explains advantages of each method and offers practical guidance for parameter selection and implementation.  
- Significant theoretical novelty by proving convergence guarantees previously missing in Supervised PageRank literature.  
- Noteworthy adaptation of gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracle.  
- Experiments are limited to a single, relatively small dataset (Yandex, ≈11.7K vertices), raising scalability questions.  
- Lacks a concrete computational-complexity comparison between gradient-based and gradient-free implementations.  
- Assumes local convexity near the optimum without sufficient discussion of how often this holds in practice.  
- Comparison is limited to one prior method [21]; no evaluation against standard optimizers like SGD or L-BFGS.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with solid theoretical and empirical support.  
- Recommendation: accept (NIPS) given the paper's significance despite limited experimental scope.  
- Suggestions: compare with standard optimizers (SGD, L-BFGS), detail practical trade-offs and complexity for large-scale use, analyze scalability to very large graphs, and clarify the local convexity assumption.
