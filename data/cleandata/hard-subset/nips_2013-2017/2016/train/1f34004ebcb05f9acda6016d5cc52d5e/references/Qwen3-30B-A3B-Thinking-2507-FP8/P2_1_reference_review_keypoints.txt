- Proposes two-level optimization (linearly convergent lower-level stationary distribution approximation + gradient-based or gradient-free upper-level) for learning Supervised PageRank.  
- Addresses lack of convergence guarantees in prior method [21] by providing theoretical convergence proofs.  
- Develops complexity bounds (Theorems 2 and 4) and analyzes trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and overall computation.  
- Empirical results show modest improvement over state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: only compared to one baseline (GBP [21]) and lacks broader baseline comparisons.  
- Missing sensitivity analysis for hyperparameters (L, ε, lower-level iterations) limits understanding of practical performance.  
- Supplementary materials referenced for proofs are not included, hindering verification of theoretical claims.  
- Presentation and notation are generally clear and well-organized, making methods understandable to experts.  
- Some exposition gaps: unclear construction of seed set U, data preprocessing details, and rationale for choice Φ = {ϕ : ‖ϕ - e_m‖_2 ≤ 0.99}.  
- Originality: novel combination of inexact-oracle methods with gradient-based and random gradient-free optimization for this problem, but relation to similar methods in other domains could be emphasized more.  
- Significance: theoretical contribution important for optimization theory, but practical impact and real-world search relevance are not convincingly demonstrated.  
- Recommendation: technically sound but limited empirical impact and missing details lead to a weak rejection; authors should expand experiments, include supplementary proofs, and clarify practical implications.  
- Strengths: provides convergence guarantees, thorough theoretical analysis, and clear problem formulation.  
- Weaknesses: limited experiments, overstated practical significance, missing supplementary materials, and insufficient experimental/hyperparameter detail.
