- Studies learning parameters of Supervised PageRank where restart and transition probabilities are linear in node/edge features, optimizing a pairwise squared-hinge loss over stationary distributions.  
- Key challenge: stationary distributions and their derivatives cannot be computed exactly at scale.  
- Proposes two two-level approaches: a gradient-free (one-point randomized zeroth-order) method with constrained inexact oracle and a gradient-based adaptive projected method using an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Uses a linearly convergent Nesterov–Nemirovski inner solver for PageRank values and a new iterative scheme for derivatives.  
- Provides lemmas controlling oracle accuracy and theorems giving overall complexity bounds for both methods.  
- Experiments on a Yandex browsing-graph (600 queries) show improved training loss versus prior Supervised PageRank [21] and sometimes fewer upper-level iterations.  
- Positions work relative to prior supervised random-walk/PageRank, zeroth-order bandit methods, inexact-oracle first-order methods, and Markov chain stationary computation literature.  
- Theory: upper-level analyses align with known techniques and give appropriate bias/variance and gradient-mapping bounds for nonconvex composite problems.  
- Concern: gradient-free guarantees require f to be convex on a local ball Φ, an unverified and strong assumption for the learning problem.  
- Concern: derivative-approximation details (Equations 3.2–3.3) and constant β1 are deferred to the supplement, hindering assessment and implementation.  
- Practical guidance is limited: global complexity bounds have large constants/logs and little advice for choosing τ, δ.  
- Experiments are limited: only training loss reported, missing standard ranking metrics (NDCG/MAP), few baselines, unclear baseline configurations, and no wall-clock/runtime comparisons.  
- Iteration counts reported do not reflect inner-loop costs since N,N1,N2 are tuned for oracle accuracy.  
- Paper clarity suffers from typos, grammatical issues, incomplete references, and postponed key algorithmic details; reproducibility would benefit from pseudo-code and succinct derivations.  
- Originality: the integrated combination of linearly convergent PageRank inner solver, inexact-oracle zeroth- and first-order methods, and end-to-end complexity analysis is novel in aggregate though components are close to known techniques.  
- Significance: moderate theoretical contribution; practical impact limited until empirical validation is strengthened.  
- Pros: clear inexact-oracle framework, end-to-end complexity bounds, potential practical value where exact gradients are costly, and use of a linearly convergent inner method.  
- Cons: reliance on unverified local convexity for gradient-free guarantees, weak experimental evaluation, deferred technical details, editorial issues, and ad hoc feasible-set choice.  
- Suggestions: report NDCG/MAP and compare to more baselines (supervised random walks, learning-to-rank), provide wall-clock times and inner-iteration counts, ablations for accuracy–cost trade-offs, clarify β1 and derivative pseudo-code, and justify or relax local convexity assumption.  
- Overall recommendation: Borderline reject due to weak empirical validation and clarity issues despite solid theoretical development.
