- Tackles learning Supervised PageRank parameters with feature-based transition/restart probabilities and a pairwise squared hinge loss, requiring approximate stationary distributions and derivatives.
- Proposes two two-level schemes: a gradient-based method using Nesterov–Nemirovski for stationary approximation plus an adaptive projected gradient with inexact oracle, and a gradient-free method using uniform sphere sampling for inexact zero-order oracles.
- Provides theoretical guarantees and complexity bounds for both methods (Theorems 1–4) and lower-level accuracy results for stationary distributions and derivatives (Lemmas 1–2).
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level oracle accuracy (number of iterations) and total optimization complexity.
- Empirical evaluation on a proprietary Yandex dataset (~600 queries, ~1.7K labeled pairs) shows improved loss and convergence speed vs a prior power-method baseline.
- Strength: sound problem formulation with careful treatment of inexact oracles and an appropriate two-level perspective.
- Strength: solid theoretical contributions adapting constrained inexact zero-/first-order methods to the PageRank learning problem.
- Strength: practical improvement by replacing the power method with a linearly convergent scheme and quantifying accuracy vs. work.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited—only training/test objective reported; standard ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP) are missing.
- Weakness: figures/tables are not visible and statistical testing is sparse and limited to loss p-values.
- Weakness: comparisons are restricted to a single baseline; missing comparisons to other supervised random-walk and strong learning-to-rank methods.
- Weakness: no wall-clock time or memory usage reported to validate theoretical complexity claims empirically.
- Weakness: gradient-free guarantees rely on convexity of f on Φ, but the problem is generally nonconvex and convexity is not empirically verified.
- Weakness: feasible set Φ restricted to a small ball around the all-ones vector may unduly limit parameter search and is not justified or sensitivity-tested.
- Weakness: clarity and presentation issues—typos, deferred notation/constants to supplement, unclear baseline descriptions and stopping criteria.
- Weakness: key implementation details missing (whether α is learned, derivative computation details, exploitation of sparsity).
- Weakness: reproducibility is limited due to a proprietary dataset and no provided code or public/synthetic benchmarks.
- Quality: theoretical development appears technically sound but practical tuning constants and intuition are largely deferred to supplementary material.
- Clarity: overall structure is clear but language issues and missing details reduce readability; request adding pseudocode and main-text definitions of constants.
- Originality: incremental but meaningful—combines and extends known inexact-oracle and smoothing ideas to a constrained setting with new complexity results.
- Significance: techniques are broadly useful beyond PageRank, but empirical evidence is insufficient to claim state-of-the-art ranking performance.
- Suggestions: report NDCG/MAP with tests, add runtime/memory and ablations for N,N1,N2 and oracle accuracy, clarify α and Φ choices, improve baseline descriptions, move key constants to main text, and release code plus public/synthetic experiments.
- Pros: principled handling of inexact oracles; convergence and complexity guarantees for both methods; empirical improvements over a prior method on a realistic dataset.
- Cons: limited and unclear experiments, gradient-free guarantees hinge on local convexity, and presentation/reproducibility issues.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (6/10); strengthen experiments and presentation for a stronger contribution.
