- Proposes two-level gradient-based and gradient-free algorithms to learn Supervised PageRank parameters with provable convergence guarantees.  
- Uses a more efficient method to approximate the stationary distribution and analyzes accuracy vs. computational complexity.  
- Empirically outperforms the prior Supervised PageRank approach on a real-world web ranking task.  
- Builds on prior Supervised PageRank and inexact optimization literature, comparing to power-method approaches and related optimization techniques.  
- Primary strength: provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both proposed methods, addressing a key gap.  
- Strength: efficiency improvements via a better stationary-distribution approximation and detailed complexity analysis.  
- Strength: offers both gradient-based and gradient-free options, increasing applicability when derivatives are expensive.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and rigorous lemmas/theorems supporting the methods.  
- Weakness: dense, highly mathematical presentation that may be hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Weakness: experimental hyperparameter sensitivity is underexplored, notably the effect of 'L' in the gradient-free method.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons beyond [21]; lacks benchmarks against mainstream ranking methods like LambdaMART or RankBoost.  
- Weakness: experiments are on a single web ranking dataset, so generalizability to other graph tasks is unclear.  
- Weakness: relies on the strong convexity assumption of f(ϕ), which may not hold in practice and whose impact under non-convexity is not fully discussed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: fills an important theoretical gap with well-motivated, empirically validated methods and rigorous complexity analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation complexity and limited experimental breadth reduce accessibility and raise generalization concerns.  
- Overall: strong theoretical and practical contribution; recommend accept with suggestions to simplify exposition and broaden experimental evaluation.
