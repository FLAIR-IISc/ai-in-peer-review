- Tackles learning parameters in Supervised PageRank, extending PageRank with node and edge features.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization approaches (gradient-based and gradient-free) using a linearly convergent method for stationary distribution approximation.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both algorithms, explicitly handling inexact oracle information.  
- Empirically outperforms the existing state-of-the-art on a real-world web page ranking task.  
- Builds on prior Supervised PageRank and optimization literature, including inexact-oracle and random gradient methods.  
- Novelty: combining linear stationary-distribution approximation with both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization techniques.  
- Detailed complexity analysis with insights into trade-offs between accuracy and computational cost.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and clearly written.  
- Weakness: heavily tailored to Supervised PageRank, so generality to other domains is unclear.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons—primarily compared only to baseline [21].  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity not thoroughly explored for the proposed methods.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical scalability and implementation strategies for extremely large graphs.  
- Weakness: convexity assumption within the feasible region for the gradient-based method may be strong and needs more justification.  
- For acceptance: solid theoretical foundation, novel algorithms with guarantees, empirical improvements, and treatment of inexact oracle issues.  
- Against acceptance: narrow scope, limited evaluation, and unclear practical scalability.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and rigorous contribution that merits acceptance despite a somewhat narrow focus.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Confidence: 8/10.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 7/10.
