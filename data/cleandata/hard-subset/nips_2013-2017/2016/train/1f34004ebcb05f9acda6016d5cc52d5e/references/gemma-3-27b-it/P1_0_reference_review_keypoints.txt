- Tackles learning parameters in Supervised PageRank with non-convex, constrained optimization and inaccurate loss/gradient evaluation.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) leveraging a linearly convergent method for approximating the Markov chain stationary distribution (Method 3.1).  
- Provides convergence guarantees and complexity analysis for both methods, accounting for lower-level approximation accuracy.  
- Analyzes trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy, computational complexity, and overall convergence.  
- Experimental results on a web page ranking task show the proposed methods outperform a prior state-of-the-art gradient-based approach.  
- Situates work well within PageRank and optimization literature, citing relevant inexact oracle research.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: convergence rate analysis absent in prior Supervised PageRank work.  
- Novelty in adapting random gradient-free methods and building an inexact-oracle convergence framework for this problem.  
- Paper is generally well-structured with consistent mathematical notation and clear algorithm presentation.  
- Paper is technically dense and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with advanced optimization concepts.  
- Lacks detailed justification for choosing specific algorithms (e.g., Algorithms 1 and 2) over alternatives.  
- Insufficient analysis of parameter sensitivity and robustness (e.g., choice of L in gradient-free method).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset/task, reducing confidence in generalizability.  
- Some performance claims feel overstated without detailed quantitative comparisons and thorough statistical testing.  
- Qualitative scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novel methods, empirical improvement, detailed complexity/trade-off analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility issues due to density, limited experiments, and clarity concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with Minor Revisions.  
- Requested revisions: add intuitive explanations, more thorough parameter sensitivity analysis, quantify significance with statistical tests, and consider additional experiments/datasets.
