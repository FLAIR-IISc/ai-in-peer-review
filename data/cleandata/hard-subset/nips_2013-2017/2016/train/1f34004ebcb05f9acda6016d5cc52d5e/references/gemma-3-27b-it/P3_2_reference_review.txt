## NIPS 2016 Review: Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods

**Summary:**

This paper addresses the problem of learning parameters in a Supervised PageRank model, a generalization of the classic PageRank algorithm that incorporates node and edge features. The authors identify the challenges of optimizing the loss function in this context – non-convexity, constraints, and the difficulty of exact loss/gradient calculation. They propose two two-level optimization methods: one gradient-based and one gradient-free, both employing an iterative approach to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk on the lower level, and using this approximation to tune parameters on the upper level.  A key contribution is providing convergence guarantees for these methods, a missing element in prior work (specifically [21]).  The paper also investigates the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity and validates the approach with experiments on a web page ranking task.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds directly on the Supervised PageRank framework introduced in [21]. It correctly identifies the limitations of existing optimization techniques (mentioned as [16] and [7]) and the lack of convergence proofs for the state-of-the-art method in [21].  The use of the power method for stationary distribution estimation is standard (references [18], [2], [21]). The gradient-free optimization method draws inspiration from [16], while the gradient-based approach is positioned as an extension of the work in [6].  The paper demonstrates awareness of relevant optimization techniques and appropriately situates its contributions within the broader literature. The comparison to standard PageRank and other ranking methods (HITS, etc.) in the introduction is useful for context.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:**  The most significant strength of this paper is the provision of convergence guarantees for both the gradient-based and gradient-free methods. This is a critical advancement over the prior work [21], where convergence was not proven.  The analysis appears rigorous and addresses the inexact oracle nature of the problem effectively.
* **Addressing a Challenging Problem:** Learning Supervised PageRank is inherently complex due to the non-convexity of the loss function and the computational challenges of dealing with large graphs.  The authors tackle these challenges head-on with well-defined methods.
* **Detailed Complexity Analysis:**  The paper includes a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed algorithms, considering the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall runtime. This is valuable for practical application.
* **Comprehensive Experimental Validation:** The experiments on a real-world web page ranking dataset demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, outperforming the state-of-the-art approach [21].
* **Clear Presentation:** Despite the technical complexity, the paper is reasonably well-written and organized. The two-level structure of the methods is clearly explained.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Dataset Specificity:** The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset from Yandex. While it demonstrates improvement over the baseline, it would be beneficial to see results on additional datasets or with different graph structures to assess the generalizability of the methods.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions tuning the Lipschitz constant (L) in the gradient-free method, but the explanation of how this was done and the impact of different values is limited. More discussion about hyperparameter sensitivity would be valuable.
* **Implementation Details:**  The paper lacks specific details regarding the implementation of the methods. Details like the choice of projection operator (ΠX) in the gradient-based method or the specific implementation of the random vector generation in the gradient-free method would be helpful for reproducibility.
* **Limited Discussion of Limitations:** While the authors acknowledge the non-convexity of the problem, a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the methods in such a landscape (e.g., potential for getting stuck in local optima) would strengthen the paper.
* **Repetitive Lemmas/Theorems:** The detailed proofs in the supplementary materials feel quite lengthy and, at times, repetitive. A more concise presentation might improve readability.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Provides theoretical guarantees for a previously unproven algorithm.
* Addresses a relevant and challenging problem in graph ranking.
* Demonstrates significant performance improvement over the state-of-the-art.
* Offers a comprehensive complexity analysis.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited experimental validation on a single dataset.
* Lack of detailed implementation specifics.
* Could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of limitations.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Supervised PageRank and graph ranking in general. The provision of convergence guarantees for the proposed optimization methods is a major accomplishment. While the experimental evaluation could be more extensive and the paper would benefit from more implementation details, the theoretical and practical contributions are substantial. 

**Recommendation:**  **Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Consider testing the methods on additional datasets or different graph structures.
2.  **Provide more details on hyperparameter tuning:** Explain the process used to choose the Lipschitz constant (L) and assess the sensitivity of the results to different values.
3.  **Include more implementation details:** Provide information about the projection operator and random vector generation.
4. **Discuss Limitations:** Add a section on the limitation of the methods in the case of non-convex landscapes.
