- Overall: Proposes two two-level optimization schemes (gradient-based and gradient-free) for Supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence analysis and experimental validation; recommends acceptance.  
- Problem: Addresses learning parameters in Supervised PageRank where loss is non-convex and exact gradients are unavailable.  
- Novelty: Introduces a novel combination of two-level framework, refined lower-level stationary distribution approximation, and gradient-free optimization adapted to this problem.  
- Methods: Presents two algorithms (gradient-based and gradient-free) and leverages an improved lower-level approximation method to boost efficiency.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides rigorous convergence rate estimates and complexity analysis for both methods under inexact-oracle conditions.  
- Inexact oracle: Explicitly handles inexact gradient/value information, extending applicability to large-scale problems.  
- Practical validation: Empirical results on real web page ranking tasks show improvement over prior state-of-the-art [21].  
- Significance: Results are important for ranking algorithms and likely useful to researchers and practitioners.  
- Strength: Strong mathematical rigor and clear impact of lower-level approximation on overall complexity.  
- Strength: Novel adaptation and combination of existing techniques to fill a literature gap on convergence guarantees.  
- Weakness: Exposition is dense with heavy notation and lacks intuitive explanations, reducing accessibility.  
- Weakness: Some assumptions (e.g., local convexity) are insufficiently justified and sensitivity to assumptions is not explored.  
- Weakness: Experimental setup lacks detail on hyperparameters and baseline configurations, and comparisons to other ranking methods are limited.  
- Recommendation & fixes: Accept with minor revisions — simplify notation, add intuitive explanations, justify assumptions, and provide more experimental details and broader comparisons.
