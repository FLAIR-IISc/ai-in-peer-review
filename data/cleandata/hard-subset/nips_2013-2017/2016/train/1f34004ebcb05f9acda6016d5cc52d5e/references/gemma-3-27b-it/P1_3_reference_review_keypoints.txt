- Tackles learning parameters in Supervised PageRank, addressing non-convexity and difficulty computing stationary distributions.  
- Proposes two two-level methods: a gradient-based and a gradient-free approach using a linearly convergent lower-level solver ([17]).  
- Provides convergence guarantees and complexity analysis for both methods, relating lower-level accuracy to overall cost.  
- Empirically outperforms prior state-of-the-art [21] on a real-world web ranking task (Yandex logs).  
- Builds on and improves the Supervised PageRank framework from [21], adding theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Positions work within random-walk ranking literature and connects to optimization methods (SGD, gradient-free, inexact oracle).  
- Key strength: delivers rigorous theoretical convergence rates for both optimization approaches.  
- Key strength: novel combination of lower-level approximation ([17]) with upper-level optimization techniques.  
- Key strength: detailed trade-off and complexity analysis useful for practical implementation and tuning.  
- Key strength: empirical validation demonstrating practical improvements over prior work.  
- Key strength: generally clear presentation and organized exposition of model, computation, and optimization.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is dense and may be hard to follow without strong optimization background.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (τ, step size, L) not sufficiently explored; unclear how L was chosen.  
- Weakness: experiments limited to a single dataset (Yandex); broader datasets would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: limited baseline comparisons—should include standard ranking methods (e.g., PageRank, BM25).  
- Weakness: many important proofs and experiments are in supplementary material, reducing immediate accessibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical advancement, practical performance gains, and useful complexity insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dense/less accessible theory, limited experimental scope, and over-reliance on supplementary material.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with notable contributions; weaknesses are addressable.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add intuitive explanations, parameter sensitivity analysis, more datasets/baselines, and summarize key supplementary results in main text.
