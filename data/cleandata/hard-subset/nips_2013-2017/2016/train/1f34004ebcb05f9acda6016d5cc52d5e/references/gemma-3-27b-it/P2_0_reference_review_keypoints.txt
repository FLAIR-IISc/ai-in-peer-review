- Paper proposes two two-level methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank parameters using improved stationary distribution estimation.
- Provides convergence guarantees and rate analysis for both proposed methods, addressing limitations of prior work.
- Tackles a non-convex, constrained optimization problem with inexact oracles, common in practical ML.
- Novelly combines gradient descent, gradient-free optimization, and power-method-style stationary distribution estimation.
- Empirical results show improved ranking quality over a state-of-the-art baseline on a web page ranking task.
- Includes detailed computational complexity analysis and explores accuracy–complexity trade-offs.
- Clearly defines the Supervised PageRank model and the associated optimization problem.
- Theoretical guarantees are a significant contribution compared to prior methods lacking them.
- Paper is dense and mathematically heavy, making core ideas hard to follow without more intuition.
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset (Yandex), reducing confidence in generality across graph types and features.
- Lacks practical discussion on parameter tuning, scalability to very large graphs, and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Improvements over baseline are statistically significant but may be modest in magnitude, suggesting incremental advance.
- Heavy reliance on prior optimization work (e.g., Nesterov) with insufficient clarity on novel insights beyond adaptation.
- Clarity and organization need improvement; more intuitive explanations alongside formal derivations are recommended.
- Overall technically sound and original in its combination of techniques, but practical impact is uncertain.
- Reviewer recommendation: weak accept contingent on improved clarity and expanded experimental validation.
