- Tackles learning parameters for Supervised PageRank and proposes two novel two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with convergence analyses.  
- Identifies lack of convergence guarantees in prior gradient-based methods and analyzes the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall complexity.  
- Empirically shows the proposed methods outperform state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.  

- Addresses an important, practical problem by improving optimization for feature-rich ranking models.  
- Provides novel theoretical convergence guarantees and a rigorous analysis of accuracy vs. complexity trade-offs.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on a real-world dataset, comparing favorably to prior work.  
- Presents a clear two-level optimization framework motivating specialized methods for each level.  
- Explores both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches and supplies supplementary material.  

- Paper is very dense with heavy notation and challenging flow, especially Section 3; needs more intuitive explanations and decomposition of complex lemmas.  
- Core optimization techniques are not novel; originality is primarily in applying these methods to Supervised PageRank and tailoring the analysis.  
- Experimental hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., choice of L) is noted but not thoroughly explored or analyzed for robustness.  
- Evaluation is limited to a single dataset, restricting evidence of generality across tasks and graph types.  
- Excessive number of lemmas/theorems makes it hard to see the main theoretical insights; needs higher-level summaries.  

- Technically sound with rigorous math and solid experiments, though full verification would be effortful.  
- Clarity is a significant weakness due to density and heavy notation.  
- Originality is moderate: methodological application and analysis are valuable but techniques themselves are standard.  
- Significance is high for researchers/practitioners in ranking and graph ML because of improved guarantees and empirical performance.  

- Offers valuable theoretical guarantees for optimizing Supervised PageRank.  
- Shows superior empirical performance versus the state-of-the-art.  
- Provides a comprehensive study of both gradient-based and gradient-free strategies.  
- Fills a literature gap on convergence analysis for these algorithms.  

- Paper is dense and difficult to read.  
- Originality is incremental rather than groundbreaking.  
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset.  

- Overall assessment: above average and recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and additional experimental/hyperparameter analysis.  
- Reviewer confidence: 7/10, somewhat lowered by the paper’s density and the effort needed to fully verify technical details.
