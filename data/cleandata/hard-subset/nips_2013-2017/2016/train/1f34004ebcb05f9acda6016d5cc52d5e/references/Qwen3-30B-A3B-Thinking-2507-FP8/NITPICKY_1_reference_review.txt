

# Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

This paper proposes two-level optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank, with theoretical convergence guarantees. The work is relevant to NIPS as it combines optimization theory with practical ranking applications. However, the paper contains numerous minor technical and formatting issues that undermine its technical quality and clarity.

## Technical Issues

The paper contains several critical notational inconsistencies. In Equation (2.1), the summation "∑ l∈U 〈ϕ1,Vl〉" should use proper LaTeX formatting as "∑_{l∈U} 〈ϕ₁,Vₗ〉". Similarly, Equation (2.2) has "∑ l:i→l〈ϕ2,Eil〉" which should be "∑_{l:i→l} 〈ϕ₂,Eᵢₗ〉". These formatting errors persist throughout the paper, including in Lemma 1 and Lemma 2 where "⌈ 1 α ln 8r δ1 ⌉ − 1" should be "⌈ (1/α) ln(8r/δ₁) ⌉ - 1".

The paper claims to provide theoretical guarantees but contains logical inconsistencies. In Section 5.2, it states "the main advantage of the first gradient-based algorithm: the guarantees of a convergence do not require the convexity" yet the convergence analysis in Section 5.1 is for convex problems. This contradiction needs to be resolved.

The paper mentions "a.o." throughout (e.g., "3mps+ 3psN + 6r a.o.") without defining this notation. This is not standard in academic writing and should be replaced with "arithmetic operations" or similar.

In Algorithm 1, the definition "gτ (x, δ) = mτ (f̃(x+ τξ, δ)− f̃(x, δ))ξ" uses "m" without definition. It should be explicitly stated that "m" is the dimension of the parameter space.

Theorem 1 contains a critical notational error: "EΞM−1f(yM ) − f∗" should be "E[f(y_M) - f*]" with proper spacing and notation. The expectation operator should be clearly defined as being over the random choices ξ₀,..., ξ_{M-1}.

The paper states "we obtain the p-values of the paired t-tests for all the above differences on the test sets of queries, all these values are less than 0.005" but fails to provide these p-values in the main text, which is essential for reproducibility and scientific rigor.

## Formatting and Presentation Issues

The paper contains numerous typos: "concider" (should be "consider"), "probablities" (should be "probabilities"), "surfer" (should be "surfer"), and inconsistent use of "ϕ" versus "φ".

The references are poorly formatted with incomplete entries (e.g., "O, A. Agarwal, Dekel and L. Xiao, Optimal algorithms for online convex optimization with multi-point bandit feedback, ,"). The paper also claims "the first time when the ideas of random gradient-free and gradient optimization methods are combined" without adequately citing related work that might have done this.

The paper mentions "the set Φ = {ϕ ∈ Rm : ‖ϕ− em‖2 ≤ 0.99}" but fails to define "em" (the vector of all ones) earlier, which is needed for proper understanding.

In Section 6.2, the paper claims "GFN significantly outperforms the state-of-the-art algorithms on all test sets" but provides no statistical evidence beyond the vague statement about p-values. The paper should include actual p-values or statistical test results.

## Strengths

The paper addresses an important problem in web ranking with a theoretically grounded approach. The two-level optimization framework is novel in combining gradient-based and gradient-free methods with the inexact oracle concept. The experimental results do show improvements over the state-of-the-art method.

## Weaknesses

The paper's technical quality is undermined by the numerous notational inconsistencies, logical contradictions, and formatting errors. The theoretical guarantees are not clearly stated or justified. The experimental results lack sufficient statistical evidence to support the claims of "significant" improvements.

The paper's claims about convergence guarantees are inconsistent with the actual analysis provided. The gradient-based method is claimed to work for non-convex problems, but the convergence analysis in Section 5.1 is for convex problems, creating a contradiction that needs to be resolved.

The supplementary materials are referenced frequently but not provided, making it impossible to verify the proofs of Lemmas 1 and 2 or the detailed experimental results.

## Conclusion

While the paper addresses an important problem with potentially useful contributions to optimization theory and web ranking, the numerous technical and formatting issues significantly undermine its quality. The contradictions in the theoretical claims, the inconsistent notation, and the lack of statistical evidence for the experimental results make it difficult to assess the validity of the claims.

I recommend rejection due to the substantial number of minor flaws that collectively undermine the paper's technical quality and scientific rigor. The paper would benefit from a thorough revision to fix these issues before consideration for publication.
