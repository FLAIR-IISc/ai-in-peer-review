- Paper proposes two two-level Supervised PageRank learning methods: gradient-based and gradient-free, with convergence analyses.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and complexity bounds missing from prior SOTA.
- Analyzes trade-off between lower-level stationary distribution accuracy and overall computational cost.
- Gradient-free method useful when gradients are hard to compute; two approaches increase applicability.
- Empirical results show improvements over the SOTA [21] on a web ranking task.
- Ablation studies for parameter choices (especially for gradient-free method) are provided.
- Mathematical formulations are generally clear and well-presented.
- Paper is dense and hard to read due to frequent switching between theory, lemmas, and algorithms.
- Performance gains over SOTA are present but not dramatically large.
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset, reducing empirical robustness.
- Work heavily builds on the authors' prior publications, feeling incremental rather than radically new.
- Heavy notation makes the paper harder to follow.
- Technically sound and rigorous, but incremental impact tempers overall significance.
- Clarity and organization need improvement; theoretical contributions could be separated more clearly.
- Originality lies mainly in adding convergence guarantees and complexity analysis to existing frameworks.
- Significance is moderate: addresses an important problem but limited by scope and incremental gains.
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretical guarantees, empirical improvement, trade-off analysis, two method options.
- Arguments against acceptance: dense readability, modest performance gains, single-dataset evaluation, strong reliance on prior work.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept, subject to minor revisions for clarity and broader experimental validation.
