

# Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

## Summary
This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank by proposing two two-level optimization methods: a gradient-based approach and a gradient-free approach. The authors recognize that the state-of-the-art method [21] lacks convergence guarantees due to the use of inexact oracle information (approximations of stationary distributions and their derivatives). They fill this gap by developing a framework that combines linearly convergent methods for approximating the stationary distribution with constrained optimization algorithms that provide theoretical convergence guarantees. The paper makes contributions to both optimization theory (for constrained non-convex problems with inexact oracle) and the application of Supervised PageRank for web ranking.

## Strengths

**Quality:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for both proposed algorithms (Theorems 1-4), which is a significant contribution. The authors carefully analyze the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity. The experimental results demonstrate clear improvements over the state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality (Table 1), with statistical significance (p < 0.005). The theoretical analysis builds appropriately on prior work in optimization (e.g., [7, 16, 25]) while addressing the specific challenges of the Supervised PageRank problem.

**Originality:** The two-level framework for learning Supervised PageRank with convergence guarantees is novel. While the use of inexact oracle for PageRank has been explored before ([3, 4, 6, 10, 12, 20, 21]), this paper provides the first convergence guarantees for such methods. The adaptation of gradient-free methods ([16]) to constrained optimization with inexact oracle is particularly noteworthy, as is the development of a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle.

**Significance:** The problem addressed (learning Supervised PageRank) has clear practical importance for web search and ranking applications. The paper demonstrates that the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art both theoretically (with convergence guarantees) and empirically (in ranking quality). The results suggest potential for improving search engine reliability and speed, which is a significant practical contribution.

## Weaknesses

**Clarity:** The paper is technically dense and could benefit from more intuitive explanations of the core ideas. While the mathematical presentation is rigorous, some key concepts (e.g., the two-level optimization framework) could be better motivated with a high-level overview before diving into the technical details. The connection between the theoretical results and practical implementation could also be strengthened.

**Experimental Scope:** The experiments are limited to a single dataset from Yandex, which raises questions about generalizability. The paper doesn't compare with other optimization approaches for similar problems (e.g., stochastic gradient descent with different step sizes, or other inexact oracle methods). It would be valuable to see how the proposed methods scale to larger graphs (e.g., the entire web graph) and to include more diverse datasets to demonstrate broader applicability.

**Theoretical Context:** While the paper correctly identifies the gap in the literature (lack of convergence guarantees), it doesn't clearly distinguish how their approach differs from other optimization methods for inexact oracle problems. A more thorough comparison with related work in optimization (e.g., [25, 26]) would strengthen the contribution statement.

**Practical Impact:** The paper claims that the proposed algorithms "can increase the reliability and speed of a search engine," but doesn't provide concrete evidence of the speed improvement. The complexity bounds (Theorems 2 and 4) are theoretically interesting, but it's unclear how much faster the proposed methods are compared to the state-of-the-art [21] in practice.

## Recommendations for Improvement

1. **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Include results on additional datasets (e.g., from other search engines or standard benchmark datasets) to demonstrate broader applicability. Compare with other optimization methods (e.g., stochastic gradient descent with different step sizes) to better contextualize the performance gains.

2. **Clarify the practical impact:** Provide more concrete evidence of speed improvements (beyond just the number of iterations), such as actual runtime comparisons. Discuss the trade-offs between the proposed methods and the state-of-the-art in terms of computational resources.

3. **Strengthen the theoretical context:** Explicitly compare the proposed inexact oracle framework with related work in optimization (e.g., [25, 26]) to better highlight the novelty. Consider adding a brief discussion of how the proposed methods might generalize to other graph-based learning problems.

4. **Improve the exposition:** Add a high-level overview of the two-level framework before presenting the technical details. Consider including a simple example illustrating the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.

## Overall Assessment

This paper makes a solid contribution to the literature by providing the first convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank. The theoretical results are rigorous, and the empirical results demonstrate clear improvements over the state-of-the-art. However, the paper would be significantly stronger with a more comprehensive experimental evaluation, clearer articulation of the practical impact, and better contextualization within the broader optimization literature.

The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it addresses a significant problem in machine learning with both theoretical and practical contributions. The technical quality is high, and the results are convincing. I recommend acceptance with the understanding that addressing the suggested improvements would further strengthen the paper.
