- Problem & contribution: Addresses learning Supervised PageRank, identifies missing convergence guarantees in prior work, and proposes two two-level methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with theoretical guarantees and improved empirical ranking.

- Theoretical quality: Provides rigorous lemmas and theorems (Lemmas 1–2, Theorems 1–4) establishing approximation accuracy, convergence rates, and complexity bounds; proofs in supplementary materials.

- Experiments: Evaluation on a real-world Yandex dataset shows consistent ranking improvements over prior state-of-the-art [21] and a more efficient lower-level approximation than the power method.

- Clarity: Well-organized and precise problem formulation with detailed algorithms, though technical sections may be hard for non-experts and the introduction could be more concise.

- Originality: Novel application of inexact-oracle two-level optimization to Supervised PageRank, controlling lower-level accuracy and analyzing the accuracy–complexity trade-off.

- Significance: Results are important for search/recommendation systems, combining practical performance gains with theoretical guarantees and potential applicability to other graph-based problems.

- Strengths: Strong theoretical contributions, principled two-level framework, clear empirical improvements, useful complexity analysis, and successful theory–application bridge.

- Weaknesses: Focused on a single application domain; lacks comparison to newer methods (e.g., Fresh BrowseRank [22]); no concrete runtime comparisons for the gradient-free method; parameter sensitivity analysis is buried in supplementary material.

- Overall assessment: Strong technical paper with convincing experiments and meaningful contributions, limited mainly by narrow application focus but with broader methodological relevance.

- Recommendation: Accept.
