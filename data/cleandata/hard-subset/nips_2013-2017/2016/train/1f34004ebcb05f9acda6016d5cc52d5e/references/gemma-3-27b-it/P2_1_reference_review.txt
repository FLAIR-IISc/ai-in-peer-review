## Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

**Summary:**

This paper tackles the problem of learning parameters in a Supervised PageRank model, a generalization of PageRank that incorporates node and edge features. The authors identify the challenges in optimizing this model – non-convexity, constraints, and the difficulty of exact loss function calculation – and propose two two-level optimization methods: one gradient-based and one gradient-free. They provide convergence guarantees for both methods and analyze the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity. Empirical validation on a web page ranking task demonstrates improved performance compared to a state-of-the-art method.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:**  A significant strength is the provision of convergence rate analysis for *both* proposed methods. This is crucial, as the original Supervised PageRank method lacked such guarantees.  The authors address a key limitation of the earlier work.
* **Comprehensive Approach:** The paper tackles a non-trivial optimization problem with constraints and inexact oracles, making it relevant to real-world applications.  They explore both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches, providing flexibility.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The authors perform a detailed complexity analysis and investigate the trade-offs between accuracy and computational cost, offering practical insights.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The experimental results demonstrate improvements over the existing state-of-the-art method on a relevant ranking task.
* **Well-defined Problem & Context:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of Supervised PageRank learning and its limitations, situating the work within the broader context of ranking algorithms.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Presentation:** The paper is *extremely* dense and mathematically involved. While thoroughness is appreciated, the presentation could be significantly improved for clarity. Many lemmas and theorems are stated without sufficient motivation or explanation of their practical relevance.  Supplementary materials are heavily relied upon.
* **Limited Novelty of Core Ideas:** While the combination of techniques and the rigorous analysis are valuable, the core ideas (using power methods for stationary distribution estimation, adapting existing optimization algorithms) aren't radically novel. The originality lies more in the careful application and analysis within the specific Supervised PageRank context.
* **Experimental Setup Could Be More Rigorous:**  The experimental section, while showing improvements, lacks detailed comparisons.  More detailed ablation studies or comparisons with other ranking algorithms beyond the single baseline would be beneficial.  The selection of hyperparameters and justification for their choices are not thoroughly explained.
* **Dependency on Supplementary Material:**  A large portion of the proof and explanation is relegated to the supplementary materials, making it hard to quickly grasp the essential arguments.  Critical information regarding complexity analysis is also there.
* **Limited Discussion of Scalability:** While complexity analysis is provided, the practical scalability of these methods to truly massive graphs isn't thoroughly discussed.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with rigorous mathematical analysis backing up the claims.  The completeness is good, but hampered by the density of presentation. The authors are relatively careful about acknowledging limitations and tradeoffs.
* **Clarity:**  This is the biggest weakness. The paper is very difficult to follow, even for someone familiar with optimization and graph algorithms.  Significant rewriting is needed to improve clarity and organization.
* **Originality:** The originality is moderate.  The core ideas are built upon existing techniques, but the specific application to Supervised PageRank, combined with the rigorous analysis, adds value.
* **Significance:** The results are potentially significant, particularly for applications where accurate ranking is crucial.  The theoretical guarantees and empirical improvements suggest that this work could be useful to other researchers and practitioners.



**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Provides theoretical guarantees for a previously unanalyzed algorithm.
* Offers a comprehensive approach to a challenging optimization problem.
* Demonstrates improved performance on a real-world dataset.
* Contributes to a better understanding of the trade-offs involved in Supervised PageRank learning.

**Against Acceptance:**

* The paper is excessively dense and difficult to follow.
* The novelty of the core ideas is limited.
* The experimental validation could be more thorough and rigorous.
* Heavy reliance on supplementary material hinders quick assessment.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is **borderline accept**.  The technical contributions are valuable, and the theoretical guarantees are a significant step forward. However, the lack of clarity and the density of presentation substantially detract from the paper’s impact.  

**Recommendation:**

I recommend accepting this paper with **major revisions**.  The authors should prioritize improving the clarity and organization of the paper. Specifically:

1. **Rewriting for Clarity:** Simplify the presentation, provide more motivation for the mathematical derivations, and explain the practical implications of the results.
2. **Reducing Reliance on Supplementary Materials:**  Move essential proofs and explanations into the main text.
3. **Strengthening Experimental Evaluation:** Add more thorough comparisons with other ranking algorithms and perform ablation studies.
4. **Addressing Scalability Concerns:** Discuss the limitations of the methods on very large graphs and potential avenues for improvement.



If these revisions are made, the paper has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field.  As is, the lack of accessibility limits its impact.
