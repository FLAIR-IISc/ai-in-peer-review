- Paper addresses learning Supervised PageRank parameters with restart/transition probabilities linear in features, where πq(ϕ) and its gradients are inexact/unavailable.
- Proposes a two-level approach: lower-level uses a truncated/renormalized Neumann series (Nesterov–Nemirovski) to approximate π and its derivatives with linear convergence guarantees.
- Upper-level methods: (i) random gradient-free two-point spherical smoothing adapted to constrained optimization with bounded oracle error; (ii) adaptive projected gradient for nonconvex composite problems with an inexact first-order oracle.
- Provides complexity bounds that trade off lower-level inner iteration accuracy and outer-loop convergence.
- Empirical evaluation on a Yandex query-dependent browsing graph compares gradient-based (GBN), gradient-free (GFN), and prior power-iteration-based (GBP) methods, reporting improved training objective values.
- Theoretical extensions adapt existing inexact-oracle and zero-order frameworks to constrained settings and deterministic bounded oracle error, but key constants/proofs are deferred to the supplement.
- Lower-level truncation and differentiation of the Neumann series are plausible with explicit δ-dependent iteration counts, but tightness is hard to verify without full proofs.
- Critical correctness issue: the gradient-free analysis assumes local convexity of f on Φ, which is generally unverified and undermines Theorem 2’s practical relevance.
- The gradient-based method is more appropriate since it does not require the convexity assumption.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: primary metric is training objective; no standard IR metrics (NDCG/MAP) or held-out test evaluations are reported despite ranking claims.
- Baselines are weak/limited: only prior supervised PageRank compared; no comparisons to strong LTR methods (e.g., LambdaMART, RankSVM) or supervised random walks.
- Risk of data leakage: stopping criteria based on test-set objective for GBP is concerning.
- Runtime, scalability, and wall-clock comparisons—especially claimed lower-level speedups over power iterations—are not empirically demonstrated.
- Clarity issues: numerous typos, incomplete references, essential proofs/algorithms (Algorithms 2 and 4) in supplement, and scattered important assumptions.
- Originality is incremental: differentiating the Neumann series is standard, but integrating it with upper-level inexact-oracle methods and explicit complexity trade-offs is novel within this niche.
- Significance limited by weak empirical validation; theoretical contributions are useful but not sufficient without stronger experiments.
- Pros: addresses inexact stationary distribution learning, offers a practical lower-level derivative scheme, provides an adaptive inexact-oracle projected gradient, and explicit inner/outer complexity analysis.
- Cons: unsupported convexity assumption for GFN, insufficient experiments and baselines, potential test leakage, missing runtime/scale analysis, and lack of ablation/sensitivity studies.
- Suggested fixes: report NDCG/MAP on held-out sets versus strong baselines, use proper validation for hyperparameters, provide wall-clock/memory/runtime scaling and comparisons, verify/justify convexity assumption, include sensitivity analyses and explicit constants, and clean up references/typos.
- Recommendation: Reject in current form due to experimental weaknesses and clarity issues; could be competitive after stronger empirical validation and clearer exposition.
