- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank parameters.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and complexity analysis for both methods.
- Addresses a key limitation of prior work [21], which lacked convergence guarantees due to imprecise stationary distribution and derivative estimates.
- Empirical results on a real-world Yandex dataset show both methods outperform the state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality.
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous proofs (Lemmas 1-2, Theorems 1-4) and thorough complexity analysis.
- Writing is clear, well-organized, mathematically precise, and the experimental setup is reproducible.
- Minor clarity issue: needs a more detailed explanation of why [21] lacks convergence guarantees beyond stating imprecision.
- Original contribution: first theoretical convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank.
- Novel use of gradient-free optimization in an inexact oracle setting for PageRank.
- Insightful two-level analysis of the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity.
- Significance: bridges optimization theory and graph-based ranking with practical applications in search and recommendation.
- Practical advantage: gradient-free method avoids computing large-matrix derivatives, useful for real-world deployment.
- Strengths: theoretical novelty, comprehensive complexity analysis, practical methods with different trade-offs, strong real-world results, clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: limited justification for the local convexity assumption, unclear scalability to extremely large graphs, insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, limited comparison to other graph-based ranking methods.
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS — contributions are significant, empirically validated, and well-situated in the literature.
