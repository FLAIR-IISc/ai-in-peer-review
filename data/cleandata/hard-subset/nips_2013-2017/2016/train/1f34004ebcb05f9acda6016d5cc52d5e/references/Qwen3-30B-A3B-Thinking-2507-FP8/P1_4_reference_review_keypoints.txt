- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank parameters, combining a linearly convergent lower-level stationary distribution approximation with upper-level optimization.

- Identifies limitation in prior work [21]: lack of convergence guarantees due to inexact stationary distribution computation.

- Provides the first convergence guarantees and complexity bounds for learning Supervised PageRank parameters in the inexact-oracle setting (Theorems 1–4).

- Theoretical analysis carefully quantifies trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity, building on established optimization frameworks.

- Empirical results on real-world Yandex web browsing data show consistent improvements over the state-of-the-art [21] across multiple query sets.

- Experiments include investigation of hyperparameter effects (ε, L) and comparison with an untuned Supervised PageRank baseline.

- Strength: Novel application of inexact-oracle optimization to Supervised PageRank with technically sound proofs and meaningful practical relevance.

- Weakness: Experimental comparisons omit other relevant optimization methods (e.g., stochastic gradient methods), limiting contextualization of advantages.

- Weakness: Assumption of local convexity of f(ϕ) is restrictive for a non-convex problem and its practical impact is not explored.

- Weakness: Complexity analysis lacks concrete wall-clock timing comparisons, so iteration reductions (GBN vs GBP) may not reflect real speedups.

- Quality: Technically rigorous with well-structured proofs; some complex derivations could use more intuition.

- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; supplementary materials are comprehensive though some technical details might belong in the main paper.

- Originality: Combination of inexact oracles and optimization methods for this specific problem is novel and well-cited against related work.

- Significance: Advances learning of graph-based ranking algorithms with practical relevance to search and recommendation systems.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to include broader experimental comparisons and concrete computational time measurements.
