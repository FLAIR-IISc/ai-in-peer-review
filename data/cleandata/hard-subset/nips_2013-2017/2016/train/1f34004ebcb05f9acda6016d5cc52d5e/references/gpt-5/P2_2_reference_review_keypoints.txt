- Paper studies learning parameters of Supervised PageRank where restart/transition probabilities are linear in features, improving on prior two-level nonconvex optimization work [21].  
- Proposes two two-level schemes with theoretical guarantees: a constrained gradient-free method for inexact zero-order oracles and an adaptive gradient-based method for inexact first-order oracles.  
- Gradient-free method extends spherical randomization to constrained, inexact-oracle settings and provides expected error bounds depending on dimension, smoothing radius, diameter, and oracle accuracy.  
- Gradient-based method provides an adaptive projected gradient framework for nonconvex composite problems with unknown Lipschitz constant and returns points with small prox-gradient norm.  
- Replaces power iteration with a linearly convergent PageRank estimator and generalizes it to approximate derivatives, quantifying accuracy/complexity trade-offs (Lemmas 1–2).  
- Upper- and lower-level accuracies are tightly coupled and propagated to overall complexity bounds (Theorems 2 and 4).  
- Experiments on a Yandex query-dependent browsing graph show improved loss and better ranking vs prior method [21].  
- Theoretical contributions are strong: careful development of inexact-oracle analyses for both zero- and first-order settings (Theorems 1 and 3).  
- Lower-level accuracy results give explicit iteration counts (N, N1, N2) to guarantee prescribed f and ∇f accuracies.  
- Gradient-free guarantees assume local convexity (f convex on feasible ball and x* in interior), a strong unverified assumption limiting general applicability.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: focuses on training loss, omits standard ranking metrics like NDCG@k or MAP in the main text.  
- Baselines are narrow; missing comparisons to strong learning-to-rank methods (e.g., LambdaMART) and other supervised random walk variants.  
- Dataset scale is small (~600 queries, ~11.7K vertices), so claims about “huge-scale” applicability are unsupported; wall-clock time and memory are not reported.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (α, L, ε) and robustness are only lightly explored.  
- Reproducibility concerns: some constants deferred to supplement, and code/implementation details are not provided.  
- Clarity issues: numerous typos, formatting problems, incomplete references, and some confusing notation switches.  
- Originality: novel combination of constrained inexact zero-order, adaptive inexact first-order, and linearly convergent PageRank estimator with derivative generalization.  
- Theoretical significance is solid and broadly useful beyond PageRank; practical significance is promising but under-demonstrated.  
- Pros: provable two-level architecture, efficient lower-level estimator with derivative control, and extensions of inexact-oracle theory.  
- Cons: gradient-free method rests on strong local convexity assumption, limited experiments (scale, baselines, metrics, runtimes), clarity and reproducibility gaps.  
- Suggested improvements: empirically verify convexity regions, report standard ranking metrics and runtimes, compare to stronger baselines, perform sensitivity analyses, and clarify key constants and lemma steps.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but borderline acceptability due to insufficient empirical validation and reliance on a strong assumption for the gradient-free method.
