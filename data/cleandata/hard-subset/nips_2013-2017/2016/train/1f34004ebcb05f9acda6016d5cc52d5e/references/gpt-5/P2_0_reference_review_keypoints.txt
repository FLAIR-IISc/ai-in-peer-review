- Paper addresses learning Supervised PageRank parameters where restart/transition probs are linear in features; objective is nonconvex and requires approximate stationary distributions and derivatives.  
- Proposes two two-level methods: (i) inexact first-order (adaptive projected gradient) and (ii) random gradient-free with inexact zero-order oracle.  
- Lower level replaces power method with Nesterov–Nemirovski linearly convergent estimator and extends it to derivative estimation with accuracy-complexity trade-offs.  
- Provides convergence analyses for constrained optimization with inexact oracles and ties oracle accuracy to lower-level iteration counts.  
- Builds on supervised random walks and inexact-oracle optimization literature; novelty in adapting linear stationary distribution methods to derivative estimation and constrained inexact-zero-order optimization.  
- Theorems explicitly relate accuracy, dimension, and Markov chain parameters (α) to complexity.  
- Introduces constrained, inexact-oracle variants of gradient-free and adaptive gradient methods that have broader applicability.  
- Lower-level contribution: provable linear convergence and complexity bounds for stationary distribution and derivative estimation on large graphs.  
- Empirically outperforms prior [21] on proprietary Yandex dataset in terms of surrogate pairwise squared loss and lower-level convergence speed.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to surrogate loss; lacks standard ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP) and wall-clock/runtime comparisons.  
- Baselines are narrow: no comparisons to mainstream learning-to-rank methods (e.g., LambdaMART, Backstrom & Leskovec) or ablation studies isolating components.  
- Gradient-free method assumes local convexity of f on Φ, a strong unverified assumption in experiments.  
- Clarity and reproducibility issues: many constants deferred to supplement, incomplete references, typos, and no code or public data.  
- Derivative estimation recurrence (Πk) needs clearer derivation and implementation guidance for large-scale use.  
- Fairness concerns: baseline lower-level power iterations (fixed at 100) may not be tuned comparably for accuracy/time.  
- Theoretical developments are mostly sound and careful about error propagation, but rely on supplemental constants to validate key oracle condition (5.1).  
- Empirical claims are suggestive but incomplete; practical efficiency requires time/memory metrics and standard ranking measures.  
- Presentation is dense with heavy notation; would benefit from pseudocode for derivative estimator and a parameter/complexity summary table.  
- Originality is moderate-to-good: nontrivial adaptations to constrained inexact-oracle settings and PageRank derivative estimation.  
- Significance is potentially high for principled learning of random-walk rankers, but immediate impact limited by evaluation and reproducibility gaps.  
- Key questions requested: report NDCG/MAP@k and runtimes on public data, sensitivity to α/R/feature scaling, ablations for N/N1/N2 and tolerances, explicit constants (β1 etc.), empirical convexity diagnostics for gradient-free method, and efficient large-scale derivative implementation details.  
- Pros: sound inexact-oracle theory, provable lower-level PageRank/derivative estimation, empirical improvement over prior work on a real dataset.  
- Cons: limited/unfair empirical evaluation, strong local convexity assumption, reproducibility and clarity issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (6/10), contingent on stronger experiments and clarification of constants/assumptions.  
- Reviewer confidence: 3/5.
