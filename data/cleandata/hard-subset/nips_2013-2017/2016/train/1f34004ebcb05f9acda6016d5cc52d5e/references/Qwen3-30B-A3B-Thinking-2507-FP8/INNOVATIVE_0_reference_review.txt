

# Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

This paper introduces two novel two-level optimization methods for learning the parameters of Supervised PageRank, a graph-based ranking algorithm that incorporates node and edge features. The authors address a significant gap in the existing literature: the state-of-the-art method [21] lacks theoretical convergence guarantees due to the inexact nature of the oracle information (stationary distribution and its derivatives are approximated via iterative methods). The paper's core contribution is the development of two algorithms that provide theoretical convergence rates for this non-convex optimization problem with inexact oracle, while also demonstrating practical improvements over existing methods.

The paper's main novelty lies in the two-level framework where the lower level provides approximate stationary distributions (via a linearly convergent method [17]) and the upper level uses these approximations for optimization. The gradient-based method adapts the framework from [7] to constrained non-convex problems with inexact first-order oracle, while the gradient-free method extends [16] to handle constrained optimization with inexact zero-order oracle. The authors prove convergence rates and complexity bounds for both methods, which is a significant theoretical contribution. The empirical results on the Yandex search engine dataset demonstrate clear improvements over the state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality, with the gradient-free method showing particular advantages in avoiding derivative calculations for large matrices.

The paper excels in originality: it's the first to provide convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning, and it successfully bridges the gap between optimization theory and graph-based ranking algorithms. The two-level framework with inexact oracle is a bold and novel approach that could influence optimization research beyond graph-based ranking. The paper clearly differentiates itself from prior work (e.g., [21], [16], [7]) by addressing the convergence issue that has been overlooked in previous approaches.

The quality of the paper is high, with rigorous theoretical analysis (though some proofs are in supplementary materials). The experimental setup is realistic and well-motivated, using a large-scale commercial search engine dataset. The authors provide detailed complexity analysis that shows how the accuracy of the lower-level approximation affects the overall computational complexity.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with a logical flow from problem statement to theoretical development to empirical validation. The mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined throughout.

Strengths:
- Significant theoretical novelty with convergence guarantees for a previously unsolved problem
- Clear practical improvements demonstrated on a real-world ranking task
- Well-justified complexity analysis showing the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall complexity
- Novel adaptation of gradient-free optimization to constrained problems with inexact oracle
- Strong empirical validation with realistic dataset from a major search engine

Weaknesses:
- Some proofs are relegated to supplementary materials, making the main paper less self-contained
- The comparison to other optimization methods is somewhat limited (only compared to [21])
- The paper doesn't thoroughly discuss the limitations of the approach (e.g., sensitivity to hyperparameters)

This paper represents a significant step forward in the field of graph-based ranking algorithms. The authors have identified a critical gap in the theoretical understanding of Supervised PageRank learning and provided a novel solution that offers both theoretical guarantees and practical improvements. The two-level framework with inexact oracle is a bold and innovative approach that could have broader implications for optimization in large-scale machine learning problems.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold approaches, I find this paper highly compelling. The theoretical contributions are substantial, and the practical improvements are clear. While the paper has minor weaknesses in completeness (supplementary proofs), these are outweighed by the paper's significant novelty and technical contributions. The work advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way, addressing a difficult problem with a novel solution.

The significance of this work is evident: it provides a reliable method for learning Supervised PageRank, which has direct applications in search engine ranking. The theoretical guarantees make the approach more trustworthy for practitioners, and the practical improvements could lead to better search results for millions of users.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel two-level optimization framework for Supervised PageRank with convergence guarantees
- Theoretical contributions extend existing optimization theory to inexact oracle settings
- Clear empirical improvements over state-of-the-art on real-world dataset
- Addresses a previously unsolved theoretical problem in the field

Arguments against acceptance:
- Some proofs in supplementary materials
- Limited comparison to other optimization methods

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The novelty and significance of the work outweigh the minor weaknesses. The paper makes a clear contribution to both optimization theory and graph-based ranking algorithms, with potential to influence both theoretical research and practical applications in search engines. The authors have successfully bridged a gap between theoretical optimization and practical ranking problems, which is exactly the kind of bold, novel work that NIPS values.
