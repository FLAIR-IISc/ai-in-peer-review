- Revisits Supervised PageRank with restart/transition probabilities linear in features and learning via pairwise hinge-squared loss requiring stationary distribution π(ϕ).
- Central difficulty: π(ϕ) and ∇π(ϕ) require solving/differentiating a Markov-chain fixed point per query, which must be approximated.
- Proposes a lower-level Neumann-series-like estimator for π with geometric weights (linear convergence rate 1−α) and tighter complexity than plain power iteration.
- Extends the lower-level approach to approximate the Jacobian dπ/dϕ via a natural derivative recursion derived from differentiating the fixed point.
- Introduces two outer optimization schemes: a gradient-free projected method with random directions and bounded additive inexact oracle, and an adaptive projected gradient method for constrained nonconvex composite objectives with an inexact first-order oracle.
- Provides theoretical guarantees: expected suboptimality for the zeroth-order method (Thm.1), complexity with inner approximations (Thm.2), stationarity guarantee for the first-order method (Thm.3), and total complexity when linked to inner solvers (Thm.4).
- Novelty: integrates controlled-accuracy inner computations, constrained gradient-free optimization with additive noise, and adaptive inexact first-order methods to give end-to-end complexity bounds for SPR.
- Strength: principled lower-level estimator with explicit error control and linear convergence, improving over standard power methods.
- Strength: derivative approximation via truncated series/recursion is a natural and practical extension for computing ∇π.
- Strength: inexact-oracle optimization analysis is cleanly adapted to constrained domains and bounded additive error settings.
- Strength: end-to-end complexity accounting ties inner iteration counts (N, N1, N2) to outer tolerances, filling a gap in prior SPR work.
- Empirical results on Yandex browsing graphs show improved training loss and some statistically significant gains over prior SPR training.
- Weakness: gradient-free method’s theoretical guarantee requires local convexity of f on Φ and interior x*, an unverified and unlikely assumption for the generally nonconvex problem.
- Weakness: first-order analysis assumes L-smoothness of f, but smoothness/Lipschitz constants (e.g., L, β1) are not explicitly bounded in terms of α, feature norms, or graph degrees.
- Weakness: justification for the derivative recursion and conditions for interchanging differentiation and series truncation are deferred to the supplement and need concise derivation.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation focuses on training loss; standard IR metrics on held-out data (NDCG, MAP, ERR) are missing to validate ranking performance.
- Weakness: baselines are limited—missing comparisons to other supervised random-walk methods and standard learning-to-rank baselines using the same features.
- Weakness: fairness/tuning concerns—GBP baseline uses a fixed 100 iterations; residual-based stopping, wall-clock times, and per-iteration costs should be reported.
- Weakness: scale limitations—dataset (~600 queries, ~11.7K vertices) is small for web-scale claims and complexities depend on potentially large pair counts r and |Q|.
- Clarity/presentation issues: typographical errors, inconsistent notation, and key constants/algorithm details are relegated to the supplement, reducing readability.
- Suggestions: provide explicit bounds for L and β1 in terms of α, feature norms, and degrees; evaluate sensitivity to Φ (e.g., simplex projection); report test NDCG@k/MAP and wall-clock times; consider stochastic pair sampling for scalability.
- Overall assessment: technically solid with meaningful theoretical contributions and end-to-end analysis, but requires stronger experimental validation, clearer specification of smoothness constants/assumptions, and improved presentation; recommendation: weak accept (6/10) conditional on revisions.
