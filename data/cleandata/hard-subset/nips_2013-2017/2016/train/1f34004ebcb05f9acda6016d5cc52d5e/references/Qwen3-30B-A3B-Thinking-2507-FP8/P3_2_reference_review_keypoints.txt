- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods (inexact first-order and zero-order oracles) to learn Supervised PageRank and shows empirical improvements over prior work.
- Strength: Provides novel theoretical convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank, addressing a gap in prior work.
- Strength: Addresses a relevant, practical problem (web page ranking) and reports empirical gains over a baseline.
- Strength: Two-level framework and use of Nesterov’s method for lower-level approximation are well-motivated and methodologically sound.
- Strength: Convergence analyses for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods are rigorous with supporting theorems and proofs.
- Weakness: Local convexity claim (“convex on Φ” near ϕ*) is insufficiently justified and needs more explanation of its validity and practical impact.
- Weakness: Complexity bounds include terms like ln(1/ε) that may harm scalability; experiments lack detailed runtime and memory comparisons.
- Weakness: Claim that the gradient-free method avoids large-matrix derivatives is misleading since complexity still scales with m per Theorem 2.
- Weakness: Insufficient comparison to recent related methods (e.g., Fresh BrowseRank [19]) weakens the state-of-the-art claim.
- Weakness: Experimental section lacks hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., choice of L) and computational-efficiency comparisons with baselines.
- Critical: Theoretical contributions are strong, but practical implications and computational advantages are not convincingly demonstrated.
- Critical: Paper should clarify the computational trade-offs and provide concrete evidence for any claimed efficiency gains of the gradient-free method.
- Critical: Recommend adding a clearer justification for the local convexity assumption and its effect on convergence guarantees.
- Significance: Novel combination of inexact-oracle methods with Supervised PageRank is original and potentially broadly applicable.
- Limitation: Practical adoption is unclear due to lack of demonstrated computational benefits over existing methods.
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, empirically convincing, suitable for NIPS pending minor revisions addressing practicality and convexity concerns.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: clarify gradient-free computational advantages, discuss theory vs. practicality trade-offs, strengthen comparisons with recent work, and justify local convexity assumption.
