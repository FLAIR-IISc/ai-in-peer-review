- Main contribution: First theoretical convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank using two-level gradient-based and gradient-free methods with inexact oracles.  
- Builds on prior Supervised PageRank work and adapts linearly convergent stationary-distribution solvers to obtain tighter complexity bounds.  

Strengths:
- Strong theoretical analysis establishing convergence rates and complexity bounds for the inexact-oracle setting.  
- Clear and consistent problem formulation, loss definition, and notation.  
- Empirical validation showing statistically significant ranking improvements over prior state-of-the-art on real Yandex data.  
- Practical relevance to web search ranking with demonstrations on real-world datasets.  
- Clear comparison and positioning relative to prior work, especially [21].  

Weaknesses:
- Many key proofs and detailed complexity analyses are relegated to the supplement, reducing accessibility.  
- Experimental comparisons are mainly against [21], lacking broader baselines (e.g., other non-convex stochastic methods).  
- Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (L, ε, R) in the main paper.  
- Insufficient discussion of limitations, such as scalability to much larger graphs or behavior on different graph structures.  

Specific recommendations:
- Move critical proofs from the supplement into the main paper for better accessibility.  
- Add a concise sensitivity discussion for key hyperparameters in the main text.  
- Include comparisons with additional optimization methods for non-convex problems.  
- Expand discussion of method limitations and scaling considerations for practitioners.  

Final assessment:
- Quality 9/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
