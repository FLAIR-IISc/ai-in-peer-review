- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods for Supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence guarantees missing in prior work.
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.
- Demonstrates empirical performance gains on a real-world web page ranking task, outperforming state-of-the-art.
- Paper is well-structured, tackles a relevant problem, and clearly positions itself relative to prior work.
- Related work is appropriately discussed, including recent optimization techniques.
- Main strength: provides convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods.
- Uses an inexact oracle framework to handle practical inexactness in computations.
- Complexity analysis offers useful insights into accuracy vs. computation trade-offs.
- Solid empirical validation on a real dataset supports practical effectiveness.
- Presentation is clear, with well-explained two-level algorithm structure and notation.
- Experimental setup lacks sufficient detail and would benefit from a more thorough ablation study.
- Yandex dataset description is limited—needs more specifics on size, characteristics, and labels for reproducibility.
- Practical guidance from the complexity analysis is limited; recommendations for parameter selection are lacking.
- Limited discussion on the choice of Euclidean norm and potential impacts of other norms (e.g., L1).
- The convexity assumption on Φ should be further justified or its limitations discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with strong empirical results on a relevant problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail and limited practical applicability of complexity analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution and recommended for acceptance; weaknesses are minor and addressable.
