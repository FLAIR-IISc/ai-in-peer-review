- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-free and gradient-based) for non-convex learning of Supervised PageRank.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees on convergence rates and complexity bounds for both methods, improving over prior gradient-based work.  
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based method on the loss.  
- Introduces a novel combination of random gradient-free and gradient-based optimization using an inexact oracle, with potential wider applicability.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Assumes familiarity with optimization and linear algebra, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.  
- Focus is limited to Supervised PageRank, potentially restricting broader applicability.  
- Does not provide detailed sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters (e.g., Lipschitz constant, accuracy) that affect performance.  
- Recommend adding more intuitive explanations and examples to aid non-expert readers.  
- Recommend a more comprehensive hyperparameter sensitivity analysis and exploration of applicability to other problems.  
- Recommend additional comparisons with other state-of-the-art optimization algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: significant and potentially impactful contribution if weaknesses are addressed.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical guarantees, and improved experimental performance.  
- Con-acceptance arguments: specialist assumptions, limited scope, and insufficient hyperparameter analysis.
