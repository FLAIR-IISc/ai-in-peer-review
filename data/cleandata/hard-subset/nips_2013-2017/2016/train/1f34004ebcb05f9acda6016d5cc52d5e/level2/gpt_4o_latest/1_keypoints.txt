- Paper introduces two novel optimization methods (gradient-based and random gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank using an inexact oracle.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and detailed complexity analysis for both algorithms.
- Adapts gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracles (novel theoretical contribution).
- Experimental validation on a commercial search engine dataset shows improved ranking quality over a state-of-the-art gradient-based approach.
- Gradient-based method requires fewer input parameters; gradient-free method avoids derivative calculations.
- Thorough analysis of trade-offs between accuracy and computational cost useful for practitioners.
- Experiments are well-designed, compare to baseline and state-of-the-art, and report statistical significance.
- Strong mathematical formulation and rigorous derivations of algorithms and convergence properties.
- Presentation is dense and may be difficult for readers unfamiliar with optimization theory.
- Paper lacks explicit discussion of limitations (e.g., scalability to larger datasets, sensitivity to hyperparameters like Lipschitz constant).
- Experimental comparisons are limited to a specific state-of-the-art method and omit broader optimization techniques (e.g., advanced stochastic gradient methods).
- Insufficient discussion of practical implementation challenges such as parameter tuning and resource requirements.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical and practical contributions, rigorous analysis, and demonstrated advantages on a relevant problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns due to dense presentation and a less comprehensive experimental evaluation.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and expand discussion of limitations and comparisons.
