- Tackles non-convex learning of Supervised PageRank with restart and transition probabilities parameterized by node/edge features.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization algorithms: an inexact-oracle gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method.  
- Provides full theoretical analysis with convergence rate guarantees, unlike prior work [21].  
- Extends optimization theory: adapts random gradient-free for constrained problems with bounded error.  
- Generalizes adaptive gradient methods to constrained non-convex problems with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Includes complexity analysis trading off lower-level oracle accuracy and overall computational cost.  
- Empirical evaluation on a large real-world web ranking dataset shows the proposed methods outperform the baseline [21], with GFN notably better on final loss.  
- Strength: rigorous and novel theoretical contributions that address a key limitation of prior work.  
- Strength: meaningful extensions to existing optimization frameworks with non-trivial technical advances.  
- Strength: practical relevance and strong empirical results on a commercial-scale dataset.  
- Strength: clear exposition, well-structured presentation, and precise algorithm/theory statements.  
- Weakness: gradient-free convergence analysis assumes local convexity of f(ϕ), a strong assumption that contradicts the non-convex problem statement and needs justification or non-convex analysis.  
- Weakness: experimental scope limited—should report standard IR metrics (e.g., NDCG/MAP) and compare to additional baselines.  
- Weakness: limited clarity on hyperparameter choices (e.g., L = 10^-4); a brief sensitivity analysis would help practitioners.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper with minor issues; reviewer recommends acceptance.
