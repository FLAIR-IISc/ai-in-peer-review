- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for Supervised PageRank that incorporate node and edge features.
- Main contribution: provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both methods, filling a gap missing in prior work [21].
- Proves convergence rates for the gradient-free method (Theorem 1) and the gradient-based method (Theorem 3).
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art [21] on a large real-world web ranking task using a Yandex browsing graph.
- Strong theoretical foundation with rigorous convergence proofs and clear handling of the inexact oracle problem.
- Experimental setup is sound, uses large-scale data, proper baselines, and reports statistical significance (p-values < 0.005).
- Paper situates itself well in the literature, citing relevant PageRank and optimization work [18, 11, 21, 16, 7].
- Minor weakness: insufficient discussion of sensitivity to hyperparameters (ε, L, R) and lack of guidance for choosing them.
- Minor weakness: does not clearly explain why the gradient-free method avoids derivative calculations for large matrices.
- Well-written, logically structured, and practically relevant to improving search engine ranking reliability and speed.
