- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank models by incorporating node/edge features and proposes two optimization methods: a gradient-based approach (with non-convex convergence guarantees) and a gradient-free method for constrained optimization.  
- Main contribution: provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both methods, which prior work [21] lacks.  
- Develops an inexact-oracle framework with a two-level approach: lower-level controlled approximation of the stationary distribution and upper-level optimization using these approximations.  
- Theoretical strengths: solid convergence proofs for Lemmas 1–2 and Theorems 1–4, with detailed supplementary material.  
- Experimental strengths: real-world web ranking experiments show both methods outperform [21], with the gradient-free method particularly promising for large-scale problems.  
- Practical clarity: clearly explains trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and computational complexity for practitioners.  
- Suggested improvement: include more intuitive motivation for the inexact-oracle framework and explicitly compare why standard optimization methods (e.g., [16], [7]) cannot be applied directly.  
- Suggested improvement: expand experimental analysis of algorithm scalability with graph size beyond the limited scope of Table 1 and Figure 1.  
- Minor concern: the gradient-based method (GBN) needs careful tuning of the Lipschitz constant L; more sensitivity analysis would help despite reported robustness.  
- Overall judgment: well-written, technically sound, significant theoretical and empirical contribution to optimization for large-scale ranking; recommend acceptance.
