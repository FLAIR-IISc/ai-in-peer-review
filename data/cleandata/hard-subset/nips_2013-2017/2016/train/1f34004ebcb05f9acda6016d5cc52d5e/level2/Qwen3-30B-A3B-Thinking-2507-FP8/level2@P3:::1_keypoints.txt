- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by incorporating node and edge features, extending traditional PageRank.  
- Prior two-level gradient method [21] lacked convergence guarantees; this paper fills that gap.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization methods: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN), both with proven convergence rates.  
- Provides a general framework for gradient methods with inexact first-order oracles for non-convex optimization, extending prior work.  
- Adapts random gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact zero-order oracles, delivering new theoretical guarantees.  
- Derives complexity bounds and analyzes the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Uses a linearly convergent lower-level solver [17] and carefully quantifies its interplay with upper-level complexity.  
- Empirical evaluation on Yandex web-ranking data shows GFN outperforms state-of-the-art GBP on all test sets and GBN outperforms GBP on subsets.  
- Experimental setup is detailed (600 queries, ~11.7K vertices, ~7.5K edges; 26 node and 52 edge features).  
- Ablation studies on hyperparameters (L, ε, step sizes) provide insights into algorithm stability and sensitivity.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and includes sufficient mathematical detail for reproduction.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and grounded in optimization literature [16, 25, 7].  
- Minor weakness: choice of feasible set Φ = {ϕ : ‖ϕ−em‖2 ≤ 0.99} seems arbitrary and needs justification for convergence claims.  
- Minor weakness: limited comparison to ranking models beyond [21], weakening broader significance arguments.  
- Minor weakness: lacks explicit practical comparison of computational complexity against GBP despite theoretical bounds.  
- Minor weakness: the claimed practical computational advantages of the gradient-free method are not fully explained or quantified.  
- Meets NIPS criteria: technically sound (quality), clear and reproducible (clarity), contains novel convergence guarantees (originality), and advances learning for graph-based ranking (significance).
