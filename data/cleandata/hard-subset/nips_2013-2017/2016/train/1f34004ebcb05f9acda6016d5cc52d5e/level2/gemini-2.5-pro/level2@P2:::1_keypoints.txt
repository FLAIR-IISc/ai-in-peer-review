- Tackles learning parameters for Supervised PageRank, formulating it as a non-convex loss minimization problem.
- Identifies weakness in prior state-of-the-art [21]: inexact power-iteration estimates of stationary distribution/derivatives lack convergence guarantees.
- Proposes two two-level optimization algorithms (gradient-based and random gradient-free) with formal convergence rate guarantees.
- Frames the problem using an inexact oracle perspective and uses a more efficient lower-level solver from [17] instead of power iteration.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis, including complexity bounds that trade off lower-level oracle accuracy and overall performance.
- Empirical results on a real-world web ranking task show the proposed methods achieve lower final loss than the baseline.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical contribution—first convergence guarantees for this learning problem, strong non-convex analysis for the gradient method.
- Strength: Original application of random gradient-free and adaptive gradient methods with inexact oracles to Supervised PageRank.
- Strength: Significant practical impact for IR and graph-based learning; methods are adaptable to other iterative-approximation problems.
- Strength: Paper is well-structured and clearly written; general frameworks are neatly specialized to the problem.
- Weakness: Gradient-free method’s convergence proof assumes local convexity near a minimum, limiting applicability in fully non-convex settings.
- Weakness: Experiments are limited—evaluation uses only loss value rather than ranking metrics like NDCG or MAP.
- Weakness: Lacks empirical wall-clock time comparisons; adaptive methods may be more costly per iteration.
- Weakness: Uses a proprietary dataset; results would be more reproducible and comparable on public benchmarks (e.g., LETOR).
- Weakness: Baseline stopping criterion appears heuristic; need fairer/time-equivalent or standardized convergence comparisons.
- Weakness: Some theoretical constants (e.g., β1) and dependencies are relegated to the supplement; brief intuition in main text would help.
