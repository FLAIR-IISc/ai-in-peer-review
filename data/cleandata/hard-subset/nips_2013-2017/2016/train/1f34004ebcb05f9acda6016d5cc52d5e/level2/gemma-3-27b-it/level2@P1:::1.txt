This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, proposing both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods. The core contribution lies in providing theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods, a feature lacking in previous work [21]. The authors also investigate the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity, and demonstrate empirical performance gains on a web page ranking task.

The paper is well-structured and tackles a relevant problem in ranking and graph analysis. Supervised PageRank is a valuable extension of the classic PageRank algorithm, and the difficulty in obtaining convergence guarantees for existing methods is a significant issue. The authors clearly articulate this problem and position their work as a direct response.  Related work is discussed, citing key papers in PageRank, supervised ranking, and optimization methods. The inclusion of references to more recent optimization techniques like those in [16, 7] demonstrates an awareness of the broader optimization landscape.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:** The primary strength of the paper is the provision of convergence rate guarantees for both the gradient-based and gradient-free methods. This is a significant advancement over the existing state-of-the-art [21].
* **Inexact Oracle Approach:**  The use of an inexact oracle framework is a sensible approach, acknowledging the practical difficulties of obtaining exact solutions in this context.
* **Complexity Analysis:** Investigating the trade-off between accuracy and computational complexity is valuable and provides practical insights for users of the proposed algorithms.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The experimental results on a real-world web page ranking task demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed methods, outperforming the state-of-the-art.
* **Clear Presentation:**  The paper is generally well-written and the mathematical notation is clear. The two-level structure of the algorithms is explained well.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Experimental Detail:** While the paper demonstrates improved performance, the experimental setup could benefit from more detail.  For example, the specific parameter settings used for each algorithm (beyond the mentioned L and ε) are not fully elaborated. A more thorough ablation study exploring the impact of different parameter settings would strengthen the empirical claims.
* **Dataset Description:** The description of the Yandex dataset is somewhat limited. Providing more specifics about its size, characteristics, and the nature of the relevance labels would enhance reproducibility.
* **Practical Implications of Complexity Analysis:** The complexity analysis, while theoretically sound, might be difficult to translate directly into practical guidance.  It would be helpful to discuss how the identified trade-offs manifest in real-world scenarios and offer recommendations for choosing appropriate parameter settings based on dataset size and computational resources.
* **Norm Choice:** The paper motivates the choice of the Euclidean norm for the optimization process, but a brief discussion on the potential impact of using other norms (e.g., L1) could be beneficial.
* **Assumptions:** The assumption of convexity on Φ, while stated, could be explored further.  Is this assumption reasonable for a wide range of datasets, or are there scenarios where it might not hold?


**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to learning Supervised PageRank models.
* The convergence guarantees are a significant contribution to the field.
* The empirical results demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed methods.
* The paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem in ranking and graph analysis.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The experimental detail is somewhat limited, and a more thorough ablation study would be beneficial.
* The complexity analysis, while theoretically sound, may be difficult to apply directly in practice.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a strong contribution to the field and is **accept** worthy. The theoretical guarantees, coupled with the empirical results, make it a valuable addition to the NIPS program.  The weaknesses identified are relatively minor and could be addressed through more detailed experimental analysis and a more in-depth discussion of the practical implications of the complexity analysis.  I encourage the authors to consider these suggestions for future work.
