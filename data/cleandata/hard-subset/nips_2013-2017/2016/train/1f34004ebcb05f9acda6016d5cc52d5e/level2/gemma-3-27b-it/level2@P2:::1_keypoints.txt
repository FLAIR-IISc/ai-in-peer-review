- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods for Supervised PageRank with provable convergence rate guarantees, improving over prior work.  
- Uses inexact oracle information to handle non-convexity, parameter constraints, and inability to compute exact loss.  
- Empirically outperforms the state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly presents problem, methods, and results.  
- Motivation to address convergence issues of existing methods is strong and well-articulated.  
- Main contribution: theoretical convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods.  
- Provides valuable analysis of trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Experimental validation on a real-world dataset strengthens the paper’s claims.  
- Limited connection to broader optimization literature; discussion of inexact oracle vs. noisy-gradient results (e.g., Nesterov & Polyak 2006, Ghadimi & Lan 2012) is insufficient.  
- Overabundance of lemmas and theorems makes the presentation dense and can hurt readability.  
- Notation and assumptions are dense; constraints on parameter vector Φ and choice of prox-function need clearer explanation.  
- Experimental setup lacks detail on hyperparameter tuning; choice of L=1e-4 appears arbitrary and unjustified.  
- Final selected step size in GBP is not clearly stated despite mentioning step-size effects.  
- Evaluation focuses on loss reduction; ranking-quality improvements are not quantified with standard metrics (e.g., NDCG, MAP).  
- Missing discussion of limitations: risk of local optima in non-convex problem, scalability to larger graphs, and the positive feature-vector assumption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical guarantees, empirical improvement, relevance to graph-based learning, and detailed accuracy vs. cost analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak connection to broader optimization work, overly dense theory, insufficient experimental detail, missing limitations discussion, and occasional lack of clarity.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution warranting acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity, related-work linkage, experimental details, and discussion of limitations.
