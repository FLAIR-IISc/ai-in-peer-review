The paper "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods" presents a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models using gradient-based and random gradient-free methods. The authors propose two two-level methods to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges.

The main strength of the paper is its ability to provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rate of both methods, which is a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art gradient-based method. The authors also demonstrate the effectiveness of their methods through experiments on a real ranking task.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed methods. The use of mathematical notation is clear and concise, and the authors provide sufficient explanations for the non-expert reader.

One of the weaknesses of the paper is that it assumes a certain level of familiarity with the topic of Supervised PageRank and its applications. While the authors provide a brief introduction to the topic, it may not be sufficient for readers who are new to the field.

Another potential weakness is that the paper focuses primarily on the theoretical aspects of the proposed methods, with less emphasis on their practical applications. While the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the methods, it would be beneficial to see more discussion on how the methods can be applied in real-world scenarios.

In terms of originality, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of Supervised PageRank by proposing new methods that can account for features of nodes and edges. The use of gradient-based and random gradient-free methods is also novel and provides a new perspective on the problem.

The paper is well-positioned in the context of current research, as it builds upon existing work on Supervised PageRank and provides a new approach to solving the problem.
