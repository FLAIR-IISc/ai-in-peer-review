- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank with convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods.
- Builds on prior work [21] and fills the gap by providing rigorous convergence proofs missing in earlier methods.
- Core contribution is theoretical analysis with convergence rate guarantees and detailed complexity bounds for both proposed methods.
- Proposes combining inexact oracle techniques with convergence analysis for Supervised PageRank—a novel approach.
- Algorithms are described in detail and the paper carefully analyzes trade-offs between accuracy and computational complexity.
- Connects the number of lower-level iterations to overall performance and articulates practical implementation considerations.
- Explores both gradient and gradient-free approaches, enabling applicability under different computational constraints.
- Experimental results on a web page ranking task show performance improvements over the state-of-the-art method.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and mathematically precise with a concise problem formulation.
- Experimental reporting lacks comprehensiveness: actual p-values, hyperparameter tuning details, and sensitivity analyses are missing.
- The choice of the L2 norm and the rationale for the specific parameter set Φ are only briefly explained and need expansion.
- Related-work discussion should be broadened to include more recent optimization advances for non-convex problems.
- Notation is occasionally dense and some symbols (e.g., ‘a.o.’) and variable meanings need clearer explanations.
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and practical contributions with minor weaknesses to address; recommended for acceptance.
