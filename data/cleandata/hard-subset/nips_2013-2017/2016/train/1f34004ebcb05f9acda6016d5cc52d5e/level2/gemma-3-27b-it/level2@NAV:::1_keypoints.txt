- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank with node/edge features using gradient-based and gradient-free optimization.
- Main contribution: theoretical convergence guarantees for both proposed algorithms in a non-convex setting.
- Key technical idea: use of an inexact oracle to estimate the stationary distribution enabling convergence analysis.
- Builds on and clearly situates work within PageRank and supervised PageRank literature ([18,11,3,4,6,21]).
- Proposed algorithms are presented in sufficient detail and the convergence analysis appears rigorous.
- Experimental evaluation shows improved performance on a real-world web ranking dataset compared to the state-of-the-art [21].
- Includes a useful discussion on the trade-off between accuracy and computational complexity.
- Strength: significant theoretical advancement by providing convergence proofs where prior work lacked them.
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to other ranking algorithms beyond Supervised PageRank.
- Weakness: experimental section lacks detailed statistical significance analysis and comprehensive ablation studies.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen experiments and broader comparisons.
