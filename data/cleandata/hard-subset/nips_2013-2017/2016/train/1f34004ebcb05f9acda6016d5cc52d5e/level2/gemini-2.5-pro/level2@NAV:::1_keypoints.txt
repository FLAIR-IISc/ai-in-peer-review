- Addresses non-convex parameter learning for Supervised PageRank incorporating node and edge features.
- Points out prior method [21] lacks theoretical convergence due to inexact power method estimations.
- Introduces two novel algorithms: a gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method.
- Frames both methods using an inexact oracle perspective.
- Provides full theoretical analysis with provable convergence rate guarantees.
- Novelty in adapting general inexact-oracle optimization frameworks [7,16] to this constrained non-convex problem.
- Rigorous quality: includes complexity bounds for both proposed algorithms.
- Strong experiments on a large-scale real-world web browsing dataset from a commercial search engine.
- Empirical results show the proposed methods, especially the gradient-free one, outperform baseline [21] on final loss.
- Clarity could be improved: make the link between general frameworks (Sections 4.1, 5.1) and the specific application more explicit.
- Needs practical justification for the local convexity assumption and guidance on selecting the region Φ.
- Experimental comparison is narrow (only compares to [21]); broader baselines would give better context.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution with minor issues; recommendation to accept.
