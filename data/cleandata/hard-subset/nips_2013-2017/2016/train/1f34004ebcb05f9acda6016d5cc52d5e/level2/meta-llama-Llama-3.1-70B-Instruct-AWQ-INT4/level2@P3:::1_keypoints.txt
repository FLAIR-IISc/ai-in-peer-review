- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-free and adaptive gradient with inexact oracle) for learning Supervised PageRank with node/edge features and provides convergence rate analysis.  
- Strength: Introduces two novel optimization methods that incorporate node and edge features.  
- Strength: Provides convergence rate proofs for both proposed methods.  
- Strength: Applies methods to a real-world web-page ranking task and reports better loss than the state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: Well-written with clear explanations of methods and analyses.  
- Weakness: Assumes linear parameterization of node/edge feature vectors, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed methods.  
- Weakness: Experiments are limited to a single dataset, leaving generalization to other datasets unclear.  
- Weakness: Does not compare against other optimization baselines like SGD or quasi-Newton methods.  
- Originality/Significance: Methods are original and provide a meaningful contribution to optimization for Supervised PageRank.  
- Quality of Writing: Clear, well-presented, and easy to follow with good experimental discussion.  
- Clarity/Organization: Paper is well-organized with a clear introduction, method description, and results.  
- Relevance to NIPS: Relevant due to novel optimization methods and theoretical analysis.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance for publication.
