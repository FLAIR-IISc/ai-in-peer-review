- Paper studies convergence for a non-convex graph-based ranking model where node/edge weights are defined by feature-driven random walks.  
- Existing optimization methods are inapplicable because they require exact objective values, motivating a two-level algorithm.  
- First level: a linearly convergent method estimates the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk.  
- Authors show the loss value can be approximated to any prescribed precision using that estimator.  
- Second level: a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle is developed and proven to converge to a stationary point.  
- Main technical contribution: adapting inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to known precision.  
- Paper is well written and proofs appear correct, though not all were checked in detail.  
- Concern about novelty: key optimization ideas resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and the supervised PageRank algorithm has prior work.  
- Minor issues: missing conclusion section and recommendation to present the optimization problem in a more general context to clarify contributions relative to state-of-the-art.
