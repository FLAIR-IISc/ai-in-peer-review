- Motivates supervised PageRank with a feature-based node/edge weight model and notes prior learning algorithm lacked performance guarantees.  
- Proposes combining stationary-distribution approximation for Markov walks with a gradient-free optimization method for noisy loss estimates.  
- Reproduces an existing framework under a different name and notation from [21], with a possibly incorrect citation (calls it Supervised PageRank while [21] has semi-supervised PageRank).  
- States several technical assumptions and a loss function, and argues the learning problem is hard.  
- Presents lemmas bounding the approximate gradient and describes a general biased gradient-free oracle with a performance theorem.  
- Introduces additional assumptions and constructs a tailored learning algorithm, deriving further theoretical bounds.  
- Experimental section outlines tests on a web graph used in related work but defers detailed results and analysis to the appendix.  
- Key experimental parameters are not reported, and the extent or significance of the claimed improvement over baselines is not discussed.  
- Unclear whether experiments covered a diverse set of graphs or multiple datasets.  
- Main technical content (proofs and empirical details) is relegated to the appendix; no proof sketches or full experimental results appear in the main text.  
- Important choices (e.g., how phi0 and radius R were selected) are not explained in the empirical section.  
- Writing quality is poor with grammatical errors and sloppy citations.  
- Some technical assumptions may be unrealistic on real data, undermining practical applicability.  
- The constraint of searching phi within an R-ball around phi0 could severely limit efficacy and is not discussed.  
- Overall, strong theoretical bounds but paper fails to convincingly demonstrate practical impact or empirical validation.
