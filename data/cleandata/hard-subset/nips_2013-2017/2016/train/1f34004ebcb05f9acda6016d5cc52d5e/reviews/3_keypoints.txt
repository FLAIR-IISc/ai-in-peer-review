- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- GBN is a gradient-based method with theoretical convergence rate guarantees and can reach a given accuracy.  
- GFN is a gradient-free method with a guaranteed decrease in the loss function.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective function values and provide convergence rate estimates.  
- Methods avoid computing large matrices while still achieving good results.  
- Empirical performance reportedly outperforms state-of-the-art in ranking quality.  
- Hyper-parameters for both methods are provided.  
- Datasets are not public but are described clearly enough to likely enable reproduction.  
- The two-level optimization builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref. 17); the lower and upper level follow that framework.  
- Detailed method descriptions are in Sections 2–5 and all proofs are in the supplementary material.  
- Reviewer requests a symbol table because many symbols make the paper hard to follow (e.g., N used before defined).  
- Experiments compare with GBP only on loss, not ranking quality; reviewer asks for direct comparison with ref. 17 since the work generalizes it.
