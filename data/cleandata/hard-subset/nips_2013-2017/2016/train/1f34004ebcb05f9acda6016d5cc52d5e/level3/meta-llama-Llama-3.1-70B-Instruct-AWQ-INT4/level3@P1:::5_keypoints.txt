- Proposes two optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models: a gradient-based and a gradient-free approach.  
- Gradient-based method uses a linearly convergent algorithm to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution.  
- Gradient-free method uses a biased gradient-free oracle to estimate the loss gradient.  
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for both methods.  
- Empirically outperforms a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline on a real-world ranking task.  
- Paper is well-organized, clear, and the theoretical analysis is thorough.  
- Experimental setup lacks details on key hyperparameters and the choice/selection of the radius R.  
- Does not analyze statistical or practical significance of improvements over baselines.  
- Fails to discuss practical limitations, notably the constraint of searching within an R-ball around a fixed phi0.  
- Writing quality needs improvement due to grammatical errors and sloppy citations.  
- Original and potentially significant for applications like web search, recommender systems, and social networks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel methods, solid theoretical guarantees, convincing experimental performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient experimental detail and analysis, missing discussion of practical constraints, and writing issues.
