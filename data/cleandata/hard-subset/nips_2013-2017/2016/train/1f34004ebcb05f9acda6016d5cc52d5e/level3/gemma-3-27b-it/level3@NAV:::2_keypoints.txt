- Paper extends PageRank to Supervised PageRank using node/edge features and formulates learning as a constrained non-convex optimization problem.
- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods that use inexact oracles to handle computational challenges.
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for both methods, addressing a gap in prior work.
- Use of inexact oracles and analysis of their impact on convergence is a sensible and practical approach for large graphs.
- Major flaw: all experimental results are placed in the supplementary material, preventing assessment of practical performance.
- Detailed Comment 1: experimental section must be moved into the main text with summaries and illustrative figures.
- Detailed Comment 2: Theorem 1 assumes convexity, which conflicts with the explicitly non-convex loss in Eq. (2.4); authors must clarify how Φ ensures convexity or revise the theorem.
- Detailed Comment 3: Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - xK+1) but it’s unclear if this suffices to guarantee convergence to a stationary point; connection must be clarified.
- Detailed Comment 4: Supplementary Figure 1 suggests slow convergence of GFNM; authors should report runtime/iteration efficiency versus GBP [21] and analyze accuracy vs. cost trade-offs.
- Arguments for acceptance: solid theoretical contributions, relevant problem, and practical consideration of inexact oracles.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of main-paper results, unclear convexity assumptions, and insufficient explanation of convergence criteria.
- Overall recommendation: reject pending major revisions—include experiments in the main text and address the theoretical issues before resubmission.
