- Provides first convergence rate guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank with gradient-based optimization.  
- Introduces novel gradient-free methods tailored for constrained optimization with inexact zero-order oracles.  
- Establishes complexity bounds for both proposed algorithms.  
- Reports superior performance on a real-world web ranking task (results placed in supplement).  
- Main experiments are relegated to the supplement, making the main paper incomplete and hard to evaluate.  
- Theorem 1 assumes local convexity of f(ϕ) in a neighborhood but gives no justification or method to choose such a neighborhood.  
- Theorem 3 bounds ‖MK(xK - xK+1)‖2 but does not show convergence to a stationary point (gradient norm → 0) for the non-convex problem.  
- Gradient-free method appears to converge very slowly compared to the gradient-based method per Supplementary Figure 1.  
- Lacks reported computational-efficiency comparisons (runtime, iterations to reach accuracy) between methods.  
- Notation is inconsistent (e.g., f̃(ϕ, δ) vs f̃(ϕ, δ1)), reducing clarity.  
- Explanation of how the inexact oracle is used in practice and how oracle accuracy affects overall convergence is vague.  
- Technically interesting and theoretically strong contribution, but practical significance is limited by experimental placement, unsupported convexity assumption, and unclear convergence interpretation.  
- Recommended revisions: move key experiments to main text, justify/clarify the convexity assumption in Theorem 1, and provide convergence-to-stationary-point interpretation and computational-efficiency comparisons.
