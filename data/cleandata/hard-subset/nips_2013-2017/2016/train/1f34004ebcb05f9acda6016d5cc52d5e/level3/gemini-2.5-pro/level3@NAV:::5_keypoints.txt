- Addresses learning parameters for a feature-based Supervised PageRank model and notes prior algorithm [21] lacks theoretical guarantees.  
- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) using an inexact oracle framework.  
- Core idea: combine approximate stationary-distribution computation with optimization for noisy loss estimates.  
- Main strength: provides novel theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for this Supervised PageRank optimization.  
- Rigorous analysis with lemmas bounding approximate gradients and a theorem for biased gradient-free oracle.  
- Limited originality: model reproduces Zhukovskii et al. [21] (misnamed by authors), so novelty is mainly in optimization, not the model.  
- Not self-contained: essential technical content and experimental details are mostly in the appendix; main text lacks proof sketches and sufficient exposition.  
- Poor writing and inconsistent citations hinder readability.  
- Empirical validation is weak: missing reproducibility details (initial ϕ0, radius R), no discussion of effect sizes or statistical significance, and experiments on a single dataset.  
- Practical concerns: some technical assumptions may be unrealistic, and restricting parameter search to a small ball around ϕ0 could severely limit real-world performance.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but insufficient experimental evidence and clarity to demonstrate practical impact.
