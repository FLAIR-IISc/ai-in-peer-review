- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (GBN and GFN) for supervised PageRank to solve a non-convex loss-minimization problem.  
- Builds on Nesterov & Nemirovski’s framework (ref. 17) with gradient-based and gradient-free approaches using inexact oracle information.  
- Methods avoid exact objective evaluations and expensive matrix computations, making them suitable for large-scale ranking.  
- GBN comes with theoretical convergence rate guarantees for non-convex problems.  
- GFN guarantees a monotone decrease in the loss function.  
- Empirically achieves superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strong theoretical contributions: rigorous convergence and complexity analysis, extending inexact-oracle optimization to supervised PageRank.  
- Clear method descriptions in Sections 2–5 and careful discussion of lower-level accuracy vs. overall complexity.  
- Comprehensive empirical validation on real-world ranking tasks, but datasets are not publicly available.  
- Clarity issues: dense notation (e.g., N used before definition) and lack of a symbol table hinder readability.  
- Experimental shortcomings: comparison with GBP only on loss values (not ranking quality) and no direct comparison with ref. 17 as requested.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, significant, and original work; accept with minor revisions to address clarity and complete experimental comparisons.
