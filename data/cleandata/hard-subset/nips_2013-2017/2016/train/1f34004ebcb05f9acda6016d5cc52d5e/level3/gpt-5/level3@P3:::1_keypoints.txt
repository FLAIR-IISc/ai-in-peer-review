- Problem: Learns feature-driven Supervised PageRank where objective requires stationary distributions of parameter-dependent Markov chains, making exact function/gradient evaluation unavailable.  
- Main idea: Two-level approach — lower-level linearly convergent Markov-chain estimators for stationary distributions and derivatives; upper-level inexact-oracle optimization methods.  
- Lower-level contribution: Adaptation of Nesterov–Nemirovski power-method with derivative estimation and explicit accuracy–complexity bounds proving arbitrary-precision loss approximation.  
- Upper-level contributions: (i) constrained gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle extending Nesterov–Spokoiny-style smoothing; (ii) adaptive projected gradient for constrained non-convex problems with inexact first-order oracle.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Convergence to stationary points and end-to-end complexity bounds trading inner (Markov-chain) and outer accuracies.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Supervised PageRank/supervised random walks, power methods for PageRank, inexact-oracle literature, composite gradient and random gradient-free methods.  
- Strength: Addresses practical setting with inexact function/gradient evaluations and provides well-motivated two-level design.  
- Strength: Solid lower-level analysis with explicit estimator construction, derivative estimation, and provable precision control.  
- Strength: Upper-level methods have technically solid convergence proofs and handle constrained settings with inexact oracles.  
- Strength: Useful trade-off analysis and explicit dependence on graph/query parameters in complexity bounds.  
- Empirical result: Both proposed methods outperform a prior gradient-based baseline on a real ranking task and use fewer outer iterations.  
- Weakness: Optimization ideas are incremental and closely related to known frameworks; main novelty is adaptation to constrained/inexact setting.  
- Weakness: Gradient-free convergence assumes local convexity on Φ, limiting applicability to the non-convex problems emphasized.  
- Weakness: Relation to prior supervised PageRank work and improvements in features/loss/training protocol are unclear.  
- Weakness: Experiments focus on pairwise-square loss; missing standard ranking metrics (NDCG/ERR), runtime breakdowns, and ablations (sensitivity to inner accuracies).  
- Weakness: Several constants and assumptions are deferred to the supplement, making some complexity expressions opaque and hurting reproducibility.  
- Quality: Technically careful and convincing reductions from inexact approximations to inexact-oracle framework; adaptive line-search scheme is well designed.  
- Clarity: Well written but heavy notation; would benefit from upfront general problem statement, intuitive schematics, and clearer presentation of key constants/assumptions.  
- Originality: Moderate — main novelty is integrating inexact-oracle adaptations with Markov-chain estimators rather than fundamentally new optimization techniques.  
- Significance: Reasonable practical/methodological relevance for graph learning with inexactness, but incremental theoretical contributions temper impact.  
- Key questions: Can gradient-free be extended to provable stationary-point convergence under non-convexity; sensitivity to α and Φ radius; stability of derivative approximations in ill-conditioned chains; report NDCG/ERR and wall-clock split?  
- Suggestions: Present general optimization framework first; explicitly compare to related works; expose constants/assumptions in main text; add standard metrics, ablations, and runtime breakdowns; clarify conclusion and guidance on method choice.  
- Overall: Solid, careful paper with moderate novelty and practical relevance; borderline accept pending clarification of novelty, stronger experiments, and presentation sharpening.
