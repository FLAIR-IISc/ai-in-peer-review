- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-based and random gradient-free) for non-convex supervised PageRank learning.  
- Gradient-based method uses an inexact oracle to approximate the loss gradient.  
- Random gradient-free method uses randomization to approximate the gradient.  
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for both methods.  
- Shows better convergence rates than the state-of-the-art gradient-based method.  
- Experimental results: gradient-free method outperforms the state-of-the-art algorithm on all test sets.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Strength: thorough convergence-rate analysis, a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance of the proposed methods, especially the gradient-free one.  
- Strength: clear presentation and organization.  
- Weakness: no detailed comparison of computational runtime versus the state-of-the-art.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of potential applications in other fields (e.g., computer vision, NLP).  
- Weakness: experimental setup details (e.g., hyperparameters) are insufficient.  
- Recommendations: add computational time comparisons, discuss cross-domain applications, and provide more experimental setup details.  
- Overall: rated 8/10, recommended accept with minor revisions.
