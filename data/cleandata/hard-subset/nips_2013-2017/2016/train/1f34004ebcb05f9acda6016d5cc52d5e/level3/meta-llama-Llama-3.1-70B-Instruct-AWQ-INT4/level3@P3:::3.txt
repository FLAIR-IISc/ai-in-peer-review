This paper proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank, namely a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN). The authors provide theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both methods and demonstrate their empirical performance on a real-world ranking task.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, making it easy to follow. The authors provide a clear description of the problem, the proposed methods, and the experimental setup. The use of mathematical notation is consistent throughout the paper, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of the algorithms and their components.

The proposed methods are based on the concept of an inexact oracle, which allows for approximations of the objective function and its derivatives. The authors show that these approximations can be controlled with high accuracy, leading to convergence guarantees for both methods.

The experimental results demonstrate that both GBN and GFN outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based method (GBP) in terms of ranking quality. The authors also provide a comparison of the computational complexity of the methods, showing that GBN and GFN require fewer iterations and less computational time than GBP.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed methods, including convergence rate guarantees and computational complexity estimates. Secondly, the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in a real-world setting. Finally, the paper is well-written and easy to follow, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.

However, there are a few weaknesses in the paper. Firstly, the authors do not provide a direct comparison with the method proposed by Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref. 17), which is the basis for the two-level optimization framework used in the paper. Secondly, the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the sensitivity of the methods to hyperparameters, which could be an important consideration in practice.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel combination of gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods for supervised PageRank. The use of an inexact oracle and the two-level optimization framework are also novel contributions.

The significance of the paper lies in its potential to improve the ranking quality of web pages. The proposed methods could be used in a variety of applications, including web search, recommendation systems, and social network analysis.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper for publication. The authors have made a significant contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.
