- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by incorporating node and edge features into PageRank models.
- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods: a gradient-based and a gradient-free approach for non-convex loss minimization.
- Introduces an inexact oracle framework that approximates the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees and rates for both methods, filling a gap left by prior work [21].
- Develops a general framework for gradient methods with inexact oracles for non-convex problems.
- Extends random gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with an inexact zero-order oracle and proves convergence.
- Empirical results on real Yandex data show both methods outperform state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality.
- Thorough analysis of the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy (power method iterations) and overall computational complexity.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation and solid theoretical presentation.
- Major weakness: omits computational runtime (seconds) for the proposed methods, limiting practical assessment.
- Weak comparison to recent NIPS literature on inexact oracles; needs better contextualization with Nesterov [25] and Nesterov & Spokoiny [16].
- Lacks clear guidance on when to prefer the gradient-free method over the gradient-based one.
- Significance: high—both theoretical guarantees and practical improvements applicable to search engines and ML optimization.
- Originality: novel application of inexact-oracle convergence analysis to Supervised PageRank, despite prior inexact-oracle literature.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding computational runtime measurements in the final version.
