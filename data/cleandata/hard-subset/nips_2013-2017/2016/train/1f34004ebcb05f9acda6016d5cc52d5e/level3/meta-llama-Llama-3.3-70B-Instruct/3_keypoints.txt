- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG).  
- q-KG is derived from a decision-theoretical perspective.  
- Demonstrates q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art benchmarks on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning.  
- Shows particularly strong performance in noisy evaluation settings, with larger advantages over benchmarks when noise is present.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review and clearly explains motivation and contributions.  
- Technical content is sound with a detailed description of the algorithm and its implementation.  
- Lacks a more detailed empirical comparison with other batch Bayesian optimization methods.  
- Does not provide sufficient analysis of computational complexity or scalability to high-dimensional problems.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, and originality.
