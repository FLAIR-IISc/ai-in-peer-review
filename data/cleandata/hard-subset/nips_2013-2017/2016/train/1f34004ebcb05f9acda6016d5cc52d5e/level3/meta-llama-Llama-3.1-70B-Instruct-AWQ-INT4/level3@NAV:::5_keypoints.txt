- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for non-convex loss minimization in Supervised PageRank.  
- Central idea: use an inexact oracle to approximate the loss value and its gradient.  
- Gradient-based method approximates gradients via the inexact oracle; gradient-free method uses randomized gradient approximation.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both proposed methods.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on a web page ranking task with empirical results.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations of problem, methods, and theory.  
- Technical soundness is good, and the inexact-oracle approach is novel for this setting.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset, reducing evidence of generality.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity for the proposed algorithms.  
- Writing quality needs polishing; contains grammatical errors and could be clearer.  
- Recommends acceptance conditional on revisions: add complexity analysis, test on more datasets, and improve writing.
