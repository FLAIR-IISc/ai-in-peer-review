- Addresses learning parameters for a Supervised PageRank model where loss/gradient depend on an inexact stationary distribution of a large Markov process.
- Frames the problem as optimization with an inexact oracle due to inability to compute stationary distribution exactly.
- Proposes two two-level optimization algorithms: a random gradient-free method for noisy function evaluations and a gradient-based method for inexact first-order oracles.
- Provides full theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both algorithms, unlike prior work [21].
- Analyzes overall complexity, linking upper-level accuracy requirements to lower-level PageRank approximation iterations.
- Demonstrates empirical improvement in final ranking quality on a large-scale commercial web ranking dataset, with the gradient-free method (GFN) performing particularly well.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor—first convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank and useful complexity bounds (Theorems 2 and 4).
- Strength: novel and non-trivial adaptation of existing inexact-oracle optimization frameworks to the constrained, non-convex Supervised PageRank setting.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation separating general frameworks from specific application details.
- Strength: experiments use a large real-world dataset, showing practical relevance and better final loss than the baseline (GBP).
- Weakness: lacks empirical runtime (wall-clock) comparisons, so practical efficiency versus baseline is unclear despite theoretical bounds.
- Weakness: gradient-free method’s convergence analysis assumes local convexity in Φ, a strong unverified assumption given the non-convex problem.
- Weakness: baseline (GBP) experimental stopping criterion is unclear/unusual and may not be a fair or standard comparison.
- Overall: solid, theoretically significant work with promising experiments; recommend acceptance with a request to add runtime discussion and clarify baseline parameters.
