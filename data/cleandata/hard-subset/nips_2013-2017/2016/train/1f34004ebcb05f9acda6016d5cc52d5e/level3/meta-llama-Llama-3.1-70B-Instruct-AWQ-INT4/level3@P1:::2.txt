This paper proposes two novel optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges in a graph. The authors develop a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle. The paper provides convergence rate guarantees for both methods and demonstrates their effectiveness in a web page ranking task.

The main ideas of the paper are well-motivated and clearly explained. The authors provide a thorough review of existing methods for learning PageRank models and highlight the limitations of these approaches. The proposed methods are based on the concept of an inexact oracle, which allows for efficient computation of the loss function and its derivatives.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed methods and their theoretical guarantees.
* The paper includes a thorough experimental evaluation of the proposed methods, which demonstrates their effectiveness in a real-world task.
* The authors provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed methods, which is essential for large-scale optimization problems.

However, there are some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The experimental results are placed in the supplement, which makes the main paper incomplete. It would be better to include the experimental results in the main text to make the paper self-contained.
* Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the objective function (2.4) is non-convex. It is unclear how to choose Φ to guarantee objective convexity.
* Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - x{K+1}) but it is unclear whether this ensures convergence of x_k to a stationary point.
* Supplementary Figure 1 shows that GFNM converges slowly, and the authors should report and compare the algorithmic efficiency of the proposed methods.

In terms of related work, the paper provides a thorough review of existing methods for learning PageRank models. However, it would be beneficial to discuss the relationship between the proposed methods and other optimization techniques, such as stochastic gradient descent or quasi-Newton methods.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning. The proposed methods have the potential to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of web page ranking tasks. However, the authors should address the weaknesses and areas for improvement mentioned above to strengthen the paper.
