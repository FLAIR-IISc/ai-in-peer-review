- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for learning Supervised PageRank models.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods.  
- Builds on prior random-walk/PageRank frameworks and Nesterov & Nemirovski’s iterative Markov chain methods.  
- Methods are designed for non-convex loss-minimization and include convergence rate estimates.  
- Clear and detailed description of both lower and upper levels of the two-level optimization framework.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis of convergence rates, a significant technical contribution.  
- Empirical results reportedly outperform state-of-the-art in ranking quality.  
- Datasets and experimental setup are clearly described, aiding reproducibility in principle.  
- Paper uses many symbols and dense notation; a symbol table would improve clarity.  
- Experiments compare loss to the state-of-the-art gradient-based method but lack direct comparison on ranking quality and to Nesterov & Nemirovski’s method.  
- More insight is needed on hyperparameter choices and their effect on performance.  
- Datasets used are not publicly available, which may limit reproducibility.  
- Overall technically sound and well-organized; recommendation to accept with revisions addressing notation, comparisons, hyperparameters, and dataset availability.
