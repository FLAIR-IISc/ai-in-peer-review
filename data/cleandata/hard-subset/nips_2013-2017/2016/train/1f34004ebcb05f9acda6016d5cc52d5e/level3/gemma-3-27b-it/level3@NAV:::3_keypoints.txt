- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank and proposes two two-level optimization methods: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- Core contribution: theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods, missing in prior work like [21].
- Leverages an inexact oracle to avoid costly exact objective and derivative computations.
- Estimates convergence rates and avoids computing large matrices, improving scalability.
- Builds on Nesterov & Nemirovski’s two-level optimization framework [17]; method details in Sections 2–5 with proofs in the supplement.
- Experimental results show GBN and GFN outperform state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality.
- Provides hyperparameter settings and dataset descriptions to aid reproducibility (datasets not public).
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and practical ranking improvements.
- Weakness: readability hampered by extensive symbol usage and no symbol table; many symbols introduced before definition.
- Experimental comparison is incomplete: only loss compared to [17], lacking direct ranking-performance comparison.
- Analysis of experimental results is limited; needs more nuanced discussion and fuller statistical testing beyond reported p-values.
- Related-work discussion should better cover large-scale ranking and optimization beyond PageRank variants.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity and more comprehensive experiments.
