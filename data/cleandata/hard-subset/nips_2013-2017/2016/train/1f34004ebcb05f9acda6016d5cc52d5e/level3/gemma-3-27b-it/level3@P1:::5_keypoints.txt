- Addresses learning parameters for Supervised PageRank by proposing gradient-based and gradient-free methods with provable convergence, but mislabels [21] as “Supervised PageRank.”
- Core idea: approximate the stationary distribution via an iterative method and embed it in a two-level optimization to handle non-convexity and parameter constraints.
- Builds on PageRank variants and feature-based transition methods, identifying prior lack of convergence proof due to inexact power method.
- Contribution: theoretical convergence rates for both gradient-based and gradient-free algorithms and analysis of accuracy vs. computational complexity trade-offs.
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor using inexact-oracle analysis and Lipschitz continuity to derive convergence guarantees.
- Strength: explicitly addresses a known gap in prior work by attempting to provide convergence proofs.
- Strength: explores both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches, widening potential applicability.
- Strength: provides complexity analysis offering insights into scalability.
- Weakness: poor writing, grammatical errors, dense notation, and unclear exposition hinder readability.
- Weakness: experimental section is thin, lacks statistical analysis, hyperparameter details, and ablation/full baseline comparisons.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset (Yandex) with insufficient description, limiting claims of generalizability.
- Weakness: restrictive assumption of searching parameters within a ball of radius R around φ̂ is inadequately justified and may be unrealistic.
- Weakness: key proofs and technical details are deferred to supplementary material, reducing accessibility in the main text.
- Weakness: citation/mischaracterization error regarding [21] undermines credibility.
- For acceptance: theoretical contributions advance rigorous understanding of learning in these models.
- For acceptance: comparison of two optimization paradigms is useful and broadens applicability.
- Against acceptance: insufficient empirical validation makes practical significance unclear.
- Against acceptance: poor presentation and writing quality detract from the paper’s impact.
- Against acceptance: unrealistic assumptions and narrow experiments raise generalizability concerns.
- Against acceptance: essential methodological details are missing from the main paper, forcing reliance on supplementary files.
- Overall recommendation: interesting and theoretically ambitious but lacks empirical support and clarity; recommend rejection with advice to strengthen experiments and presentation before resubmission.
