- Problem addressed: learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node/edge features via linear parameterization for ranking.  
- Methods proposed: two-level optimization framework with gradient-based and gradient-free methods for the non-convex loss.  
- Lower-level solver: uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov stationary distribution.  
- Upper-level solver: applies constrained non-convex gradient methods with inexact oracle information.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides convergence rates and complexity bounds for both optimization approaches, filling a gap in prior work.  
- Empirical contribution: experiments on a real-world web ranking task show improved ranking quality over state-of-the-art methods with statistical validation.  
- Quality: technically sound, rigorous proofs and complete empirical evaluation, though not every proof was verified in detail.  
- Clarity: well-written and organized with consistent notation and detailed experimental setup, but lacks a conclusion and deeper comparison to Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
- Originality: applies inexact-oracle methods to constrained non-convex Supervised PageRank—novel in application but methods resemble prior work.  
- Significance: important for search/recommendation systems; offers practical and theoretical advances (especially the gradient-free option).  
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical guarantees, well-motivated two-level framework, convincing empirical improvements, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty of core optimization techniques, insufficient differentiation from prior Supervised PageRank work, missing conclusion section.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen novelty claims, clarify relation to prior NIPS work, and add a conclusion.
