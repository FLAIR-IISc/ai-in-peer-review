- Tackles non-convex loss-minimization for learning Supervised PageRank parameters using two-level optimization.  
- Proposes two algorithms: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a random gradient-free method (GFN).  
- Designs both methods to operate with an "inexact oracle" that approximates objective and gradient to desired accuracy.  
- Replaces the power method for stationary distribution estimation with a faster linearly convergent method by Nesterov & Nemirovski.  
- Develops an adaptive gradient method for constrained non-convex problems with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Extends random gradient-free optimization to constrained problems.  
- Provides the first theoretical convergence rate guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank.  
- Carefully analyzes trade-offs between lower-level oracle accuracy and upper-level complexity; proofs provided (supplement).  
- Empirical evaluation on a large commercial web-ranking dataset shows lower final loss than the GBP baseline, with GFN showing substantial improvement.  
- Main experimental weakness: evaluation reports only loss values and omits standard ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG, MAP).  
- Readability issues: paper is mathematically dense and lacks convenient notation reference (e.g., missing table of symbols).  
- Insufficient experimental ablation: no comparison where baseline uses the improved lower-level oracle to isolate upper-level gains.  
- Pros: strong novel theoretical contribution, generalizable optimization frameworks, and promising empirical losses.  
- Cons: incomplete practical validation (missing ranking metrics) and clarity problems; recommendation is acceptance with request to add ranking metrics and ablation comparing oracle choices.
