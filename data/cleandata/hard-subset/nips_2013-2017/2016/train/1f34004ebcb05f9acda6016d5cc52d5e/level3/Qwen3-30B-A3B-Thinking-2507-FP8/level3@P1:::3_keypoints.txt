- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (GBN and GFN) for learning Supervised PageRank that incorporate node and edge features.  
- Uses Nesterov and Nemirovski’s method to approximate stationary distributions as the lower-level component of the framework.  
- Upper-level optimization uses gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN) methods with inexact oracles.  
- Primary contribution: provides theoretical convergence rate analysis for both methods, unlike prior work [21].  
- GBN: gradient-based method with convergence guarantees to stationary points without requiring convexity.  
- GFN: gradient-free method that avoids computing derivatives for large matrices, enabling large-scale applicability.  
- Detailed complexity analysis shows how lower-level accuracy trades off with overall computational cost.  
- Theoretical claims are well-supported by lemmas, theorems, and complete proofs in the supplement.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art gradient-based method [21] on a real-world web page ranking task.  
- Statistically significant improvements reported (p<0.005) for GBN across most datasets.  
- Provides clear description of the Yandex dataset (600 queries, 1.7K labeled pairs) aiding reproducibility despite non-public data.  
- Hyperparameter choices are well-justified and consistent across experiments.  
- Paper is well-written and logically structured but would benefit from a symbol table to handle dense notation.  
- Appropriately situates work relative to prior research and correctly generalizes and extends foundational results [17, 23, 24].  
- Acknowledges limitations: problem non-convexity and need for small vicinity of local minimum for GBN applicability.  
- Strengths: theoretical guarantees, complexity analysis, superior empirical performance, strong theoretical foundations, clear dataset/experimental setup.  
- Weaknesses: missing symbol table, experiments report loss values but not ranking metrics, main text lacks explicit explanation of how methods generalize [17], dataset not public.  
- Overall recommendation: technically strong, original, significant for practical deployment of supervised PageRank models; recommend acceptance.
