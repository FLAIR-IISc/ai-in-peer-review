- Proposes two optimization methods for Supervised PageRank: a gradient-free method adapted for constrained problems and a gradient-based method using an inexact oracle for non-convex optimization.  
- Gradient-free approach adapts random gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with calculable precision.  
- Gradient-based approach develops an inexact oracle and proves convergence to stationary points for non-convex problems.  
- Main contribution is adapting existing methods to constrained settings with provable precision, improving over previous work.  
- Provides thorough convergence-rate and complexity analyses for both methods.  
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based method on web page ranking benchmarks.  
- The gradient-free method yields significant improvements across all test sets.  
- Concern: work may be viewed mainly as adaptation of prior methods, limiting perceived originality.  
- Suggestion to present the optimization problem in a more general context to better position the contribution within the state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-written and well-analyzed but should add a conclusion summarizing contributions and implications.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address originality concerns and broaden contextual presentation.
