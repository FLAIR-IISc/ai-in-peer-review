- Problem: Learns Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization, proposing gradient-free and gradient-based methods with inexact oracles and improved stationary distribution computation.  
- Relation: Builds on Zhukovskii et al. (2016) and fills a gap by providing convergence guarantees previously missing.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical contribution: Provides convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods in the inexact-oracle constrained non-convex setting.  
- Technical contribution: Develops efficient approximations for the stationary distribution and its derivatives with controlled error bounds (Lemmas 1 and 2).  
- Empirical claim: Experiments (in supplement) show both methods outperform prior state-of-the-art, with gradient-free performing especially well.  

Weaknesses:
- Experimental placement: All experiments are in the supplement, making the main paper non-self-contained and hindering reproducibility.  
- Theorem 1 issue: Assumes convexity for convergence despite the problem being stated as non-convex; no guidance on choosing feasible set Φ to ensure convexity.  
- Theorem 3 issue: Provides a bound on MK(xK - x{K+1}) but does not clarify whether this implies convergence to a stationary point in the non-convex case.  
- Algorithmic efficiency: Lacks main-text comparison of runtime/iterations vs. prior work; supplement shows the gradient-free method converging slowly (Supplementary Fig. 1).  

Evaluation:
- Quality: Technically sound but weakened by unclear convexity assumptions, incomplete convergence arguments, and missing main-text experimental evidence.  
- Clarity: Well-organized theoretically but clarity suffers from omitted experiments and lack of Φ-selection details.  
- Originality: Novel theoretical framework for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles; application to Supervised PageRank is appropriate though not entirely new.  
- Significance: Potentially significant for large-scale ranking, but impact is diminished by missing comparisons and theoretical gaps.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel theoretical contributions and convergence guarantees for both methods.  
- Practical demonstration of outperforming prior methods (albeit in supplement).  

Cons for acceptance:
- Violates self-contained requirement by placing all experiments in supplement.  
- Contradictory convexity assumption in Theorem 1 vs. non-convex problem statement.  
- Unclear interpretation of Theorem 3 regarding convergence to stationary points.  
- No direct algorithmic efficiency comparisons in the main text.  

Recommendation:
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; request revisions to move experiments to the main paper, clarify how to choose Φ for convexity, interpret Theorem 3 in terms of stationary-point convergence, and include algorithmic efficiency comparisons.
