- Studies learning feature-based node/edge weights for PageRank-style random walks under a nonconvex loss and claims prior methods lack guarantees.
- Approximates the stationary distribution via an iterative scheme based on Nesterov–Nemirovski.
- Couples the approximation with two upper-level optimizers: an adaptive inexact first-order oracle and a biased random gradient-free method tolerating bounded function noise.
- Derives lemmas bounding approximate-gradient error and proves convergence rates and complexity bounds for both oracles.
- Experimental evaluation performed on a query-dependent browsing graph but with limited reporting.
- Theoretical development and complexity bounds are a clear strength.
- Core proofs and most empirical details are relegated to the appendix.
- Several technical assumptions (local convexity in an R-ball around ϕ0, positivity, bounded errors) are strong and not validated on real data.
- Key experimental parameters (ϕ0, R, α, τ, N/N1/N2, step rules), dataset scope, and runtime/variance metrics are missing.
- Writing quality is below conference standards: grammatical issues, inconsistent notation, sloppy citations, and potential mis-citation of prior “Supervised PageRank.”
- Modeling and loss largely reproduce prior supervised/semi-supervised PageRank frameworks; novelty mainly in adapting inexact-oracle analyses to this setting.
- Practical impact is unconvincing due to the R-ball constraint and insufficient empirical demonstration.
- Pros: solid inexact-oracle analysis, clear biased gradient-free oracle exposition, addresses important graph ranking problems.
- Cons: insufficient empirical evaluation, questionable/unvalidated assumptions, poor writing/citation, main technical content pushed to appendix.
- Recommendation: Reject but encourage resubmission after clarifying novelty vs. prior work, integrating proof sketches and thorough experiments (multiple datasets, metrics, ablations, sensitivity to R and ϕ0, runtime), and fixing writing/citation issues.
