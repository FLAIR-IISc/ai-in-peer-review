- Addresses learning parameters of a Supervised PageRank model where loss depends on an inexactly computed stationary distribution of a large Markov chain.  
- Notes prior state-of-the-art [21] lacks theoretical performance guarantees for this problem.  
- Main contribution: two optimization algorithms (gradient-based and random gradient-free) designed for an inexact-oracle setting.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis for both methods, including convergence rates and complexity bounds.  
- Introduces general frameworks for constrained optimization with inexact oracles, potentially applicable beyond this model.  
- Tackles a challenging combination of non-convexity, parameter constraints, and inexact computations.  
- Strength: first known convergence guarantees for learning feature-based PageRank under inexact gradient/value computations.  
- Strength: novel analyses (biased gradient-free for constrained problems; adaptive gradient for non-convex inexact first-order oracles) of independent interest.  
- Weakness: poor clarity and organization—key technical proofs and empirical details are relegated to the supplement and writing quality is weak.  
- Weakness: empirical validation is insufficient and unconvincing.  
- Weakness (reproducibility): key hyperparameters (initial ϕ̂, radius R) and choices are not justified, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness (scope): experiments run on a single commercial dataset, limiting assessment of generalizability.  
- Weakness (analysis): claims of outperforming baselines lack substantive evaluation (no ranking metrics like NDCG or qualitative analysis).  
- Weakness (practical assumptions): constrained search within a Euclidean ball around ϕ̂ is restrictive and sensitivity to ϕ̂ and R is not discussed.  
- Weakness (theoretical assumption): gradient-free analysis assumes local convexity of the non-convex objective within the search ball, a strong and unjustified condition.  
- Weakness (originality): the model is taken from prior work [21]; the paper should better frame itself as a new optimization/theoretical contribution rather than reintroducing the model.  
- Overall: strong theoretical advance but undermined by organization, limited and irreproducible experiments, and strong unexamined assumptions, leaving practical impact unclear.
