- Studies supervised PageRank with node/edge features parametrizing restart and transition probabilities; objective is constrained and non-convex.  
- Proposes two two-level algorithms: (i) gradient-free method with random gradient estimator and inexact zero-order oracle, and (ii) gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle for constrained nonconvex problems.  
- Uses Nesterov–Nemirovski linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution and its derivatives instead of the standard power method.  
- Provides finite-time convergence guarantees for both upper-level optimizers under oracle inexactness and analyzes the accuracy vs. overall complexity trade-off.  
- Empirical results on a Yandex browsing graph show improved surrogate loss compared to a prior gradient method [21].  
- Extends prior models (Supervised PageRank, BrowseRank) and leverages literature on random gradient-free and inexact-oracle optimization.  
- Contribution: adapts zeroth- and first-order methods to constrained deterministic inexactness from Markov-chain computations, explicitly coupling oracle accuracy with lower-level iteration counts.  
- Theoretical outputs include error/complexity lemmas and theorems (Lemma 1–2, Theorems 1–4) characterizing function/gradient approximation errors and optimizer complexity.  
- Strength: clear motivation and practical relevance; model supports rich node/edge features for ranking tasks.  
- Strength: methodological novelty with provable convergence rates and explicit control of oracle accuracy via N, N1, N2.  
- Strength: lower-level choice (Nesterov–Nemirovski) yields linear convergence and enables tight error control that accounts for sparsity and graph degree.  
- Strength: theoretical complexity bounds and adaptation of existing inexact-oracle frameworks to this setting.  
- Weakness: main experimental results are largely in the supplement; the main text lacks primary comparisons (loss curves, final loss, IR metrics, runtimes, iteration counts).  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 assumes local convexity in a neighborhood around a local minimum without justification; needs sufficient conditions or adoption of nonconvex zeroth-order guarantees.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3’s stationarity bound is given as a step-size-weighted iterate difference and lacks an explicit relation to projected-gradient mapping or a first-order optimality residual.  
- Weakness: Algorithmic efficiency concerns—supplementary figures suggest slow convergence of the gradient-free method; need wall-clock times, adaptive lower-level iteration counts, and sensitivity analyses for L and ε.  
- Weakness: Evaluation focuses on surrogate loss; should report standard IR metrics (NDCG@k, MAP) on held-out data to assess practical ranking quality.  
- Weakness: Claim of “lowest proved complexity” for the lower-level method relative to [5] is strong and needs precise comparison including assumptions, sparsity role, and constants/log-factors.  
- Weakness: Key constants and proofs are in the supplement; main text should include definitions, proof sketches, and notation streamlining; fix minor typos/grammar.  
- Quality: Technical development is generally sound but key assumptions (local convexity, stationarity interpretation) require clarification to fully support claims.  
- Clarity: Paper is dense but mostly well-organized; moving essential experimental figures/tables into the main paper would improve readability.  
- Originality: Meaningful and nontrivial adaptation of gradient-free and adaptive gradient methods to constrained inexact-oracle Markov-chain settings.  
- Significance: Learning feature-augmented PageRank with guarantees is valuable; impact would increase with stronger empirical evidence and clarified assumptions.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid theoretical advances, generalizable algorithmic framework, and empirical indications of improved performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient justification of key assumptions, primary empirical evidence relegated to supplement, limited evaluation metrics, and ambiguity in parameter selection and comparative fairness.  
- Suggestions: move core experiments into main text, justify or relax local convexity, provide projected-gradient-mapping/subgradient residual bounds for stationarity, and include ablations on δ1, δ2, N, N1, N2 with practitioner guidance.
