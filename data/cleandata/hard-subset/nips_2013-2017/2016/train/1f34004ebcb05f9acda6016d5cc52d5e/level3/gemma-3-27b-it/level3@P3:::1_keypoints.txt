- Paper studies learning Supervised PageRank via non-convex optimization with feature-driven random walks, proposing gradient-based and gradient-free methods using an inexact oracle.
- Main contribution: adapting inexact-oracle gradient methods to constrained optimization with quantifiable precision in function-value computation.
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and includes lemmas with deferred proofs for readability.
- Theoretical guarantees: provides convergence rate bounds for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods in a non-convex setting.
- Inexact oracle handling: effectively addresses practical difficulties of evaluating loss and gradients for random walks.
- Comprehensive approach: offers both gradient-based and gradient-free algorithms to suit different computational settings.
- Experimental validation: methods outperform the state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.
- Detailed complexity analysis: includes runtime and complexity bounds for both algorithms.
- Limited novelty: core optimization ideas resemble prior work (e.g., Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and supervised PageRank literature.
- Contribution mainly incremental: novelty is primarily in the specific combination and theoretical analysis rather than fundamentally new techniques.
- Problem context is insufficiently framed: needs broader positioning within constrained non-convex optimization literature.
- Unclear uniqueness: the paper should clarify why the inexact oracle setting here is more challenging or different from other constrained problems.
- Missing conclusion section: omission of a concluding summary of contributions, limitations, and future work.
- Limited empirical comparison: should compare against a wider range of optimization baselines beyond the specific GBP method.
- Overall quality: technically sound, claims supported by theory and experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Recommended revisions: add a conclusion, expand discussion of novelty within broader literature, and clarify unique challenges of the feature-driven random walk setting.
