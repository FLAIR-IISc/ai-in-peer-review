- Problem: Learns Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node and edge features to extend traditional PageRank.  
- Methods: Proposes two two-level optimization methods (GBN and GFN) using a lower-level linear-convergence solver and upper-level gradient-based or gradient-free optimization with inexact oracles.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides the first convergence rate guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank, addressing gaps in prior work.  
- Technical quality: Strong, well-supported theoretical analysis with proofs in supplementary material.  
- Experiments: Real-world web-ranking evaluation shows consistent, statistically significant improvements in loss values (p<0.005).  
- Practical concern: Improvements are reported on loss values but not directly on ranking metrics like NDCG to show practical impact.  
- Non-convexity issue: Paper notes non-convexity but lacks discussion of practical implications and guarantees regarding local minima.  
- Clarity problems: Excessive symbol usage, missing symbol table, and awkward notation (e.g., N used before definition) hinder readability.  
- Originality: Novel adaptations—first convergence guarantees, gradient-free methods for constrained inexact oracles, and a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on [17] but does not clearly distinguish the two-level framework from [17] in the main text.  
- Scalability advantage: Gradient-free method avoids derivative computation on large matrices, beneficial for large-scale web ranking.  
- Pro-accept arguments: Theoretical guarantees, novel methods, empirical improvements, practical importance, and meaningful extension of prior work.  
- Con-accept arguments: Readability issues, limited demonstration of ranking-quality gains, unclear distinction from [17], and insufficient treatment of non-convexity implications.  
- Overall verdict: Strong paper, recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and demonstrate practical ranking metrics.
