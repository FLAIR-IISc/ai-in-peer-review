This paper presents a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges in a graph. The authors propose two optimization methods, a gradient-based and a gradient-free method, to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem of learning these models. The paper makes several key contributions, including the development of a linearly convergent method for estimating the stationary distribution of a Markov random walk, and the adaptation of inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to known precision.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear to be correct, although not all were checked in detail. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the optimization problem and the proposed methods, including a detailed discussion of the computational complexity and convergence rates.

One potential concern is the novelty of the optimization ideas, which resemble those presented in Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015). Additionally, the supervised PageRank algorithm has prior work, and it is not entirely clear what new insights or contributions this paper brings to the field.

Despite these concerns, the paper presents a thorough and well-researched approach to learning Supervised PageRank models, and the proposed optimization methods appear to be effective in practice. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed methods outperform existing state-of-the-art algorithms, and the paper provides a useful contribution to the field of graph-based ranking models.

In terms of the quality of the paper, it is well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the optimization problem and the proposed methods. The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the computational complexity and convergence rates of the proposed methods.

Overall, I would recommend accepting this paper, but with some revisions to address the concerns about novelty and to provide a clearer explanation of the contributions relative to state-of-the-art.
