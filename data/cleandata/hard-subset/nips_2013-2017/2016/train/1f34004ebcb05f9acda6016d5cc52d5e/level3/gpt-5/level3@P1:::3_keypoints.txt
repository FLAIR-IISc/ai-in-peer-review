- Tackles learning supervised PageRank with query-dependent node/edge features via constrained, nonconvex loss minimization over feature weights ϕ.
- Main contributions: two two-level algorithms using inexact oracles — GBN (gradient-based, adaptive projected gradient for nonconvex composite objectives) and GFN (gradient-free, random-direction finite differences on the sphere with bounded adversarial zero-order noise).
- Inner level uses the Nesterov–Nemirovski Markov chain solver [17] and extends it to compute stationary distributions and Jacobians with provable accuracy-cost trade-offs.
- Outer level leverages inner-accuracy guarantees to prove convergence-rate bounds: GBN converges to stationary points; GFN achieves ε-optimality under local convexity.
- Provides explicit complexity bounds and analyzes the interplay between inner solver accuracy and overall runtime, improving on prior supervised PageRank work [21] that lacked inexact-gradient guarantees.
- Theoretical development aligns with the Devolder–Glineur–Nesterov inexact oracle framework and properly adapts it to nonconvex constrained settings (Theorem 3).
- Gradient-free analysis (Theorem 1) uses sphere randomization and gives explicit bias-variance terms in smoothing parameter τ and noise δ.
- Lemmas 1–2 link inner iteration counts (N, N1, N2) to outer-level oracle tolerances and derive arithmetic operation counts.
- Assumptions are transparent: GBN requires only nonconvex stationarity targets, while GFN needs local convexity in the chosen ball Φ.
- Empirically, methods reduce the surrogate loss vs. GBP and untuned baseline, with sensitivity analyses for L and ε reported.
- Empirical shortcomings: evaluation uses only surrogate loss — lacks standard IR metrics (NDCG@k, MAP, pairwise accuracy), runtime, memory, and detailed inner-solver comparisons.
- Clarity issues: paper is dense and symbol-heavy, some notation appears before definition, norm choices switch, and key constants are introduced late or in supplement.
- Presentation would benefit from a consolidated symbol table, earlier algorithmic overview figure, and moving key constants into the main text.
- Originality: novel extension of gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with bounded adversarial zero-order noise and adaptive inexact-first-order methods for nonconvex constrained problems.
- Technical novelty: extending [17] to compute derivatives with accuracy guarantees and systematically integrating it into outer convergence proofs.
- Significance: methods are applicable beyond supervised PageRank to any setting with objectives approximable via Markov chain solvers or simulators, and avoid full Jacobian computations via GFN.
- Specific suggestions: report NDCG/MAP or pairwise accuracy, wall-clock time, inner iteration counts, memory, sensitivity to α and radius R, and clarify GBP baseline tuning and fairness.
- Recommend adding direct empirical comparison of inner solvers ([17] vs. power method) in the main text and releasing code or public/synthetic benchmarks for reproducibility.
- Pros: solid theoretical framework for inexact oracles, two practical algorithms exploiting accuracy-cost trade-offs, clear advancement over [21].
- Cons: empirical evaluation is incomplete for practical ranking assessment, limited baselines and fairness concerns, heavy notation and deferred details, and GFN’s guarantees rely on local convexity.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — strong theoretical contributions but empirical validation needs strengthening to be more compelling.
