- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank with gradient-based and gradient-free optimization for non-convex loss in ranking problems.  
- Proposes both gradient-based and gradient-free methods and provides convergence rate guarantees for each.  
- Key technical contribution: use of an inexact oracle to handle costly stationary distribution and derivative computations.  
- Builds on PageRank/supervised extensions and incorporates node/edge features beyond graph structure.  
- Provides detailed analysis of the trade-off between lower-level algorithm accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Experimental results on a real-world web ranking task show superior performance over prior work [21], with reported p-values.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly presented, and organized for following technical details.  
- Missing actual runtime/computational time measurements for the proposed methods in experiments.  
- Limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (e.g., tuning of L0) and insufficient exploration of its impact.  
- Experimental scope is narrow—needs more datasets and graph structures to validate robustness/generalizability.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough but dense and may be hard to digest for readers unfamiliar with optimization theory.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel methods with strong theoretical guarantees, empirical improvements, detailed complexity analysis, and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of runtime reporting, limited hyperparameter and experimental scope, and potentially inaccessible theoretical exposition.  
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to include runtime measurements and expanded hyperparameter/experimental analysis.
