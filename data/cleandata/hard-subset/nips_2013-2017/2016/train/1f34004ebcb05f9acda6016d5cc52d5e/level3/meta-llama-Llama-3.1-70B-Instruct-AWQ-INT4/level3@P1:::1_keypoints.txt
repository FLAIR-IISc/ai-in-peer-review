- Proposes a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node and edge features.  
- Introduces two optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for the non-convex loss-minimization problem.  
- Develops a linearly convergent method for estimating the stationary distribution of a Markov random walk.  
- Adapts inexact-oracle gradient techniques to constrained problems where function values are known up to precision.  
- Paper is well-written with seemingly correct proofs and thorough analysis of optimization, computational complexity, and convergence rates.  
- Concern that the optimization ideas closely resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), raising novelty questions.  
- Concern that supervised PageRank has prior work, making the paper’s new insights or contributions unclear.  
- Experimental results indicate the proposed methods outperform existing state-of-the-art algorithms.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but requests revisions to address novelty concerns and clarify contributions.  
- Suggested revisions: explicitly clarify contributions relative to prior work, present the optimization problem in a more general context, and add a concluding summary.
