- Studies learning Supervised PageRank where transition/restart probabilities are linear in features and loss is pairwise squared hinge over query-dependent browsing graphs.
- Key technical challenge: stationary distribution and its parameter derivatives are available only approximately, giving inexact loss and gradient oracles.
- Proposes two two-level algorithms: (i) gradient-based with inexact first-order oracle, and (ii) random gradient-free with inexact zero-order oracle.
- Uses a linearly-convergent Nesterov–Nemirovski-type averaging scheme to estimate stationary distributions instead of the classical power method.
- Provides convergence to a stationary point for the inexact gradient method in constrained nonconvex settings.
- Provides ε-accurate convergence for the gradient-free method in a constrained convex neighborhood with inexact zero-order oracle.
- Derives theoretical complexity bounds quantifying the trade-off between lower-level accuracy (Markov chain iterations) and overall optimization complexity.
- Empirically outperforms a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline on a Yandex browsing-graph dataset (600 queries, ~1.7K labeled pairs).
- Builds directly on prior supervised PageRank/supervised random-walk frameworks and on inexact-oracle and gradient-free smoothing literature.
- Extends existing inexact-oracle analyses to constrained settings with explicitly controlled oracle inaccuracies derived from PageRank computations.
- Lower-level estimator argued to have superior complexity to power iterations and admits target-accuracy control.
- Strength: addresses a practical setting with provable guarantees under inexact function/gradient information.
- Strength: explicit convergence rates and careful accounting of lower-level accuracy effects on upper-level convergence.
- Strength: adapts gradient-free smoothing to constrained domains and develops an adaptive projected gradient scheme for nonconvex composite problems.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—many core ideas closely follow prior theory; contribution is mainly in the constrained adaptation and explicit lower-level control.
- Weakness: gradient-free method assumes local convexity of f(·) in Φ; paper must clarify when this holds and consequences if violated.
- Weakness: complexity bounds have large constants/log-factors; need empirical guidance on choosing ε, δ, τ and impact on runtime (choice of L seems ad hoc).
- Weakness: experiments compare mainly to a single baseline and training loss; should report standard ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG@k), more baselines, wall-clock time, and memory.
- Weakness: gradient-based method still requires derivative propagation through PT(ϕ); computational cost and memory at web scale need clearer discussion.
- Presentation suggestion: introduce the optimization problem in a general inexact-oracle constrained framework before specializing to Supervised PageRank.
- Quality: analysis is technically careful with appropriate lower-level accuracy-to-complexity mapping, though proofs in the supplement were not fully checked.
- Clarity: generally well written; needs clearer upfront statements on convexity/Lipschitz assumptions and practical parameter guidance.
- Originality: useful synthesis tying inexact-oracle methods to feature-augmented PageRank with controllable lower-level errors; constrained inexact-oracle extensions and two-level complexity analysis are the most original parts.
- Significance: important problem for graph-based ranking; provable convergence under realistic inexactness and empirical gains suggest practical impact.
- Suggested improvements: clarify local convexity assumption and diagnostics, report NDCG/Precision@k and runtimes, expand sensitivity analysis for L, ε, τ and α, and summarize key constants/complexities with practical parameter recipes.
- Acceptance arguments: solid theoretical framework, clear convergence guarantees, and empirical improvements on real data.
- Rejection arguments: incremental novelty, limited experimental breadth, and under-discussed local convexity and parameter choices.
