- Proposes a novel formulation for learning Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization using node and edge features.  
- Introduces two solution methods: a gradient-free approach with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence rate guarantees for both methods.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly formulated, and includes a thorough related-work review.  
- Methods are well-explained and the theoretical contributions are a significant strength.  
- All experimental results are relegated to the supplement, leaving the main paper incomplete and less self-contained.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the objective is non-convex and the paper gives no practical guidance on choosing Φ to ensure convexity.  
- Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK − xK+1), but it is unclear whether this implies convergence to a stationary point; further discussion is needed.  
- Supplementary Figure 1 indicates the gradient-free method (GFNM) converges slowly; algorithmic efficiency and runtime comparisons are not reported.  
- Recommend including key experimental results and efficiency comparisons in the main text and clarifying theoretical assumptions and convergence implications.
