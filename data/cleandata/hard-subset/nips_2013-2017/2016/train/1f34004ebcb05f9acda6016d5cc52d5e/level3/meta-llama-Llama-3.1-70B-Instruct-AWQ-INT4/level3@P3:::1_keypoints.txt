- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node and edge features.  
- Tackles a non-convex loss-minimization problem with function values computable up to known precision (inexact-oracle setting).  
- Main technical contribution: adapts inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained optimization problems.  
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for the proposed methods, improving over prior gradient-based work.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on a real-world ranking task.  
- Paper is well-written and technically sound; proofs appear correct though not all were checked in detail.  
- Clarity and organization are good; enough information for expert reproduction, but some proofs are placed in supplementary material, which may hinder non-experts.  
- Novelty concerns: key optimization ideas resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
- Prior work on supervised PageRank exists, and differences from existing approaches are not fully clarified.  
- Originality is somewhat diminished overall, though the constrained inexact-oracle adaptation is a significant novel element.  
- Significance: offers more efficient/effective methods for learning Supervised PageRank with implications for ranking tasks and related optimization problems.  
- Minor issue: lacks a conclusion section summarizing contributions and implications.
