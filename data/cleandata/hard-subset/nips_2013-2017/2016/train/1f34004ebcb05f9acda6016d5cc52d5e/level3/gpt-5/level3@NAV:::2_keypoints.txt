- Paper formulates supervised PageRank learning as a constrained, feature-aware optimization and proposes two inexact-oracle solvers (biased zero-order and adaptive inexact first-order).  
- Both methods use a fast lower-level routine to approximate stationary distributions and sensitivities, with complexity bounds and convergence-rate guarantees.  
- Manuscript connects to supervised random walks, PageRank acceleration, zero-order convex optimization, and inexact-oracle nonconvex methods, positioning itself as a principled constrained/inexact extension.  
- Theoretical development is thoughtful and potentially useful, but several assumptions and conclusions are unclear or inconsistent.  
- Theorem 1 is stated for convex f whereas the learning objective (2.4) is nonconvex, creating a mismatch.  
- Section 4.2 assumes local convexity of Φ but gives no criteria or procedure to select Φ to ensure convexity.  
- Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK − xK+1) but does not show this implies convergence to a stationary point under the inexactness and adaptive Lk.  
- Empirical results are only in the supplement, leaving the main paper incomplete and lacking in-text evidence.  
- Supplementary Figure 1 suggests the gradient-free method converges slowly, but no wall-clock, runtime, or oracle-call comparisons are reported.  
- Clarity could be improved by explicitly stating where convexity vs. smoothness is required throughout the paper.  
- The paper should tie the MK(xK − xK+1) criterion to standard stationarity measures (e.g., gradient mapping or projected gradient norm).  
- Provide a concrete recipe for selecting Φ that guarantees well-defined probabilities and, when needed, convexity.  
- Originality: novel and nontrivial extension of inexact-oracle zero- and first-order methods to constrained supervised PageRank.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for large-scale ranking but weakened by unclear stationarity claims and limited in-text experiments.  
- Major revision requested: move key experiments into the main text with datasets, metrics, ablations, time-to-accuracy, oracle calls, and power-iteration counts.  
- Compare GFNM/GBNM to state-of-the-art baselines with matched computational budgets.  
- Clarify whether a nonconvex zeroth-order analysis is possible if convexity of Φ cannot be guaranteed.  
- For Theorem 3, relate MK(xK − xK+1) to gradient-mapping norm and state whether limit points are stationary under the given oracle errors and adaptivity.  
- Discuss and quantify the observed slow convergence of the gradient-free method and report efficiency comparisons.  
- Pros: solid theoretical framework for constrained inexact-oracle optimization and clear integration with a practical, feature-based ranking model.  
- Cons: incomplete main paper experiments, unclear convexity and stationarity guarantees, and limited empirical efficiency analysis; reviewer leans toward rejection unless addressed.
