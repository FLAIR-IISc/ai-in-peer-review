- Proposes two optimization algorithms for learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node/edge features via feature-driven random walks.  
- Addresses a constrained, non-convex loss minimization where exact loss values are unavailable, making standard methods inapplicable.  
- Develops a two-level framework: a lower-level linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov stationary distribution to arbitrary accuracy.  
- Upper-level applies gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Extends prior Supervised PageRank work by providing the first convergence rate guarantees for this model.  
- Generalizes Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) random gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracles.  
- Provides rigorous convergence rate analysis and clear complexity bounds, analyzing trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and cost.  
- Empirical results on a real-world web ranking dataset show significant improvement over state-of-the-art [21] (p < 0.005).  
- Gradient-free method is practically valuable for large-scale problems because it avoids computing large-matrix derivatives.  
- Main novelty is limited: core ideas closely resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and differentiation from that work is not emphasized enough.  
- Insufficient discussion and comparison with other inexact-oracle approaches (e.g., [7, 25]).  
- Presentation issues: missing conclusion and could frame the optimization problem in a broader context.  
- Scope could be expanded: better articulation of applicability beyond web ranking to other graph-based learning tasks.  
- Overall: technically solid, well-written, strong empirical validation, but novelty claim weakened by limited differentiation from prior inexact-oracle literature.  
- Recommendation: accept — advances optimization theory and practical graph-based learning with solid theoretical and empirical support.
