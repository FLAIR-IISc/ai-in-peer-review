- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-based and random gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank models.  
- Formulates learning as a non-convex loss-minimization problem that accounts for node and edge features.  
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for both proposed methods.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on a real-world ranking task.  
- Builds on prior PageRank and Supervised PageRank work, noting gaps in non-convex convergence guarantees.  
- Argues standard global optimization methods are inapplicable due to constraints and inability to compute exact loss.  
- Strength: introduces novel methods that incorporate node/edge features.  
- Strength: supplies theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Strength: shows practical gains in experiments.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational-complexity analysis of the methods.  
- Weakness: does not discuss hyperparameter choices or their impact.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons with other state-of-the-art Supervised PageRank methods and no reported runtimes.  
- Quality/Clarity: technically sound and well-written with clear method descriptions.  
- Originality/Significance: original contribution with potential impact on information retrieval.  
- Recommendation: add complexity analysis, hyperparameter study, comparisons to baselines, and runtime reporting.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
