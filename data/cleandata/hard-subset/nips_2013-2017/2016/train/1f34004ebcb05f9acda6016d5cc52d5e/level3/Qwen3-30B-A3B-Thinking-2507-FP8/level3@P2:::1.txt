

# Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, which extend traditional PageRank by incorporating node and edge features through linear combinations with model parameters. The authors propose two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for solving the non-convex loss-minimization problem inherent in this learning framework. The lower level employs a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, while the upper level uses gradient methods for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle information.

The work builds upon the Supervised PageRank framework introduced in Zhukovskii et al. (2015) and extends the theoretical foundation of optimization with inexact oracles, drawing from Nesterov's work on gradient methods. The authors provide theoretical convergence guarantees for both optimization approaches, which is a significant contribution over the state-of-the-art method that lacks such guarantees. The paper also includes experimental results on a real-world web ranking task demonstrating superior performance compared to existing methods.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis of both optimization methods. The authors establish convergence rates and complexity bounds for their algorithms, addressing a key limitation of previous work. The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against the state-of-the-art on a real-world ranking task with appropriate statistical validation. The proofs appear correct, though not all were verified in detail. The paper provides a complete piece of work rather than a position paper, with clear theoretical guarantees supported by empirical results.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-written and well-organized. The two-level optimization framework is clearly explained, and the mathematical notation is consistent throughout. The experimental setup is described with sufficient detail to understand the methodology and results. However, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of how the proposed optimization methods specifically differ from the general approaches in Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and other related work. The conclusion section is missing, which would have helped summarize the main contributions and their significance.

**Originality:** The core technical contribution lies in adapting inexact-oracle gradient methods to the specific constrained optimization problem of learning Supervised PageRank. While the optimization techniques themselves are inspired by Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), the paper makes a novel contribution by applying these techniques to a constrained non-convex problem where function values can only be computed with known accuracy. However, the novelty is somewhat limited, as the Supervised PageRank algorithm itself has prior work (Zhukovskii et al., 2015), and the optimization framework bears strong resemblance to existing approaches. The paper could better articulate how their specific application differs from previous work.

**Significance:** The problem of learning ranking algorithms is highly significant for search engines and recommendation systems. The proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art in both convergence guarantees and ranking quality, with the gradient-free method offering a practical advantage by avoiding derivative calculations for large matrices. The paper makes a clear contribution to the field by providing theoretically guaranteed methods for a practical ranking problem, which has direct applications in industry. The work advances the state of the art by providing convergence guarantees where none existed previously.

**Strengths:** The paper provides rigorous theoretical guarantees for both optimization methods, which is a significant contribution. The two-level framework is well-motivated by the specific problem constraints. The experimental results are convincing and demonstrate clear improvements over the state-of-the-art. The paper is well-organized and clearly written.

**Weaknesses:** The novelty of the core optimization methods is limited, as they closely resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015). The paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contribution from prior work on Supervised PageRank. The connection to NIPS work could be more explicitly established. The missing conclusion section reduces the paper's impact.

**Arguments for acceptance:** The paper makes a clear theoretical contribution with convergence guarantees for a practical ranking problem. The methods are theoretically sound, well-validated experimentally, and offer a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art in terms of both theoretical guarantees and empirical performance. The problem is important for industry applications.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The core optimization techniques are not sufficiently novel, as they closely follow existing work. The paper does not adequately distinguish its contribution from prior work on Supervised PageRank. The connection to NIPS work could be strengthened.

**Overall assessment:** This paper presents a theoretically sound approach to a practical ranking problem with clear empirical advantages over the state-of-the-art. While the novelty of the core optimization methods is somewhat limited, the specific application to Supervised PageRank with convergence guarantees represents a meaningful contribution to the field. The paper would be stronger with a more detailed discussion of related work and a conclusion section. Given the significance of the problem and the quality of the theoretical and empirical results, I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen the novelty statement and add a conclusion section.
