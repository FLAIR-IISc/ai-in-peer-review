- Proposes gradient-based and random gradient-free methods to solve non-convex loss minimization for supervised PageRank that incorporate node/edge features.  
- Provides a thorough, well-organized introduction explaining limitations of existing methods and motivation for new approaches.  
- Methods are based on an inexact oracle framework and the paper supplies convergence rate guarantees for both algorithms.  
- Technical details are well-explained and the analysis of convergence rates is thorough.  
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline in ranking quality.  
- Strength: clear and concise problem/method exposition.  
- Strength: convincing experimental comparison and detailed empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed discussion of the limitations of the proposed methods.  
- Weakness: does not report runtime or computational cost of the optimization methods.  
- Weakness: some references are improperly cited and literature review could be more complete.  
- Quality: technically sound but would benefit from expanded discussion of limitations and applications.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, but some notation and terminology need clearer explanations.  
- Originality: presents new methods for a relevant non-convex problem, but novelty vs. existing work needs clearer articulation.  
- Significance: addresses an important information-retrieval problem with potential practical impact, though practical applications and impact should be discussed more.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address limitations, runtime reporting, citation issues, and clearer novelty/impact discussion.
