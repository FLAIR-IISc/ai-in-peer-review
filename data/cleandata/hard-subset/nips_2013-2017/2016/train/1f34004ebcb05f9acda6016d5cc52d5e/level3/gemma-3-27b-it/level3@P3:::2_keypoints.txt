- Proposes Supervised PageRank formulated as a constrained non-convex optimization with gradient-based and gradient-free methods using node/edge features.  
- Introduces inexact zero-order and inexact first-order oracles to handle large-scale graph computation issues.  
- Provides theoretical convergence and complexity guarantees for both proposed algorithms.  
- Builds on prior PageRank and supervised ranking work, extending gradient-free and gradient methods to constrained/inexact settings.  
- Novel formulation that integrates node/edge features into a principled constrained non-convex framework.  
- Significant contribution in supplying convergence rate guarantees absent in prior supervised PageRank work.  
- Pragmatic use of inexact oracles addressing real-world difficulty of exact gradients/function values.  
- Comprehensive theoretical analysis with proved theorems on convergence and complexity.  
- Potentially significant practical impact for ranking tasks in web search and social networks.  
- Main paper omits experiments (placed in supplement), making practical evaluation incomplete and unclear.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity while the loss is stated non-convex (Equation 2.4); needs clarification or limitation acknowledgement.  
- Theorem 3’s bound on MK(xK - xK+1) is unclear as to whether it guarantees convergence to a stationary point or local minimum.  
- Gradient-free method appears to converge slowly per supplementary figure; runtime/iteration comparisons for GFNM, GBN, GBP are missing.  
- Paper lacks thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., L) and guidance for choosing values.  
- Overall: technically strong and promising but practically under-evaluated due to missing main-text experiments and unresolved theoretical clarifications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach; rigorous theoretical analysis; potential for improved real-world ranking.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental results not in main text; ambiguous convexity and convergence claims; insufficient algorithmic efficiency discussion.  
- Recommendation: Weak reject until experiments are summarized in main paper, convexity/convergence issues clarified, and algorithmic efficiency addressed.
