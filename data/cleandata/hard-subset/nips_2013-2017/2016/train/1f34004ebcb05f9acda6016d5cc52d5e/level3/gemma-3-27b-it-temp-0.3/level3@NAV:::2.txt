## Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

This paper tackles the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, extending the standard PageRank approach to incorporate node and edge features. The authors formulate this as a constrained non-convex optimization problem and propose two solution approaches: a gradient-free method leveraging an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method utilizing an inexact first-order oracle. The core contribution lies in providing convergence rate guarantees for both methods, a feature lacking in previous work on this topic, particularly the state-of-the-art gradient-based approach [21]. 

The work builds upon the established foundation of PageRank and its variants [18, 11], addressing their limitations by incorporating feature information [3, 4, 6]. The proposed methods aim to improve upon existing optimization techniques [6, 21] which struggle with the constraints and inability to calculate the exact loss function. The theoretical analysis, including Theorems 1 and 3, attempts to establish convergence properties, and the experimental setup focuses on a web page ranking task using a real-world dataset.

However, a significant weakness of this paper is the placement of *all* experimental results in the supplementary material. This makes the main paper incomplete and hinders a proper evaluation of the proposed methods. Without the results readily available, it’s difficult to assess the practical impact and effectiveness of the theoretical contributions. 

Specifically, Detailed Comment 1 is crucial: the paper *must* include key experimental findings in the main text to be self-contained. Furthermore, Theorem 1’s assumption of convexity is questionable given the non-convexity of (2.4), as highlighted in Detailed Comment 2. The authors need to clarify how the choice of Φ guarantees objective convexity or acknowledge the limitations of the theorem. Detailed Comment 3 raises concerns about the convergence guarantee provided by Theorem 3; it’s unclear if the bound on MK(xK - x{K+1}) is sufficient to ensure convergence to a stationary point. Finally, the slow convergence of the GFNM observed in Supplementary Figure 1 (Detailed Comment 4) warrants a more thorough discussion and comparison of algorithmic efficiency with other methods.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel formulation of Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem.
*   Theoretical convergence guarantees for both proposed optimization methods, a significant improvement over existing work.
*   Potential for practical application in web ranking and other graph-based learning tasks.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   **Major flaw:** Lack of experimental results in the main paper renders it incomplete.
*   Questionable convexity assumption in Theorem 1.
*   Unclear convergence guarantee in Theorem 3.
*   Slow convergence of GFNM requires further investigation and comparison.



**Overall Assessment:**

While the theoretical contributions are promising, the current state of the paper is insufficient for acceptance due to the critical issue of missing experimental results. Addressing the detailed comments and, most importantly, including key experimental findings in the main text are essential for a proper evaluation of this work. I recommend **rejection** with the strong encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing these concerns.
