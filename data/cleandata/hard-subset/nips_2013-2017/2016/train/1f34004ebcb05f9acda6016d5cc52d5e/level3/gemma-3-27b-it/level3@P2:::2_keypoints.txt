- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank with node/edge features via non-convex loss minimization.  
- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods built on an inexact oracle.  
- Provides convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods.  
- Connects methods to existing optimization literature and clearly formulates the problem and assumptions.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees for non-convex optimization are a notable contribution.  
- Strength: using an inexact oracle is a practical solution to expensive loss/gradient evaluations.  
- Strength: offering both gradient-based and gradient-free options highlights trade-offs and flexibility.  
- Major weakness: all experimental results are only in the supplement, leaving the main paper empirically incomplete.  
- Major weakness: Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite the problem being non-convex; justification or clarification of feasible set Φ is missing.  
- Major weakness: Theorem 3’s bound (MK(xK - x{K+1})) lacks a clear link to convergence to a stationary point.  
- Major weakness: algorithmic efficiency concerns—gradient-free method appears slow and runtime/cost comparisons are not provided.  
- Overall: theoretically solid but empirical placement, convexity assumption, and efficiency analysis prevent strong acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Reject, with advice to include main-paper experimental summaries, clarify convexity assumptions, and add detailed runtime/efficiency analysis for resubmission.
