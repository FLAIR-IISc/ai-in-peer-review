- Addresses learning parameters of a Supervised PageRank model formulated as a non-convex loss minimization where objective depends on an inexact stationary distribution of a large Markov chain.  
- Key challenge: objective and its gradients cannot be computed exactly, preventing direct use of standard optimization algorithms.  
- Proposes a two-level optimization framework: a lower-level iterative method to approximate the stationary distribution and its derivatives, and an upper-level optimizer that uses the resulting inexact oracle.  
- Lower-level: employs a linearly convergent iterative method (from [17]) that controls approximation error and provides a known-error inexact oracle for function and gradient evaluations.  
- Upper-level: develops two algorithms — a random gradient-free method for convex settings and an adaptive gradient-based method for general non-convex settings.  
- Main theoretical contribution: full convergence analysis and computational complexity bounds for the proposed methods, filling a gap left by prior work [21].  
- Empirical validation: experiments on a web page ranking task show the methods outperform the existing baseline.  
- Strength: strong technical quality and significance — first convergence guarantees for this practical, non-convex graph-based ranking problem.  
- Strength: clear and well-organized presentation that separates general optimization frameworks from the application.  
- Strength: completeness — problem formulation, algorithms, proofs, and experiments are all provided.  
- Weakness: originality is limited because core optimization ideas build on existing methods (Nesterov & Spokoiny, framework in [7], and Supervised PageRank model from [21]).  
- Weakness: contribution positioning could be sharper — should present the optimization advances in a more general context before focusing on the application.  
- Suggestion: explicitly compare and contrast the constrained inexact-oracle analysis with prior inexact-oracle work (e.g., Devolder et al. [25]) to clarify novelty.  
- Suggestion: expand the conclusion to better summarize findings, emphasize the importance of theoretical guarantees, and propose future extensions to other graph-based models.
