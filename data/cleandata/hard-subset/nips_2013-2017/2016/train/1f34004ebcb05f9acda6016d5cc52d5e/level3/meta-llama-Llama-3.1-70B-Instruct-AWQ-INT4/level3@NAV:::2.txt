**Paper Review**

**Paper Title:** Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods

**Summary:**
The paper proposes two optimization methods, gradient-based and gradient-free, to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem of learning Supervised PageRank models. The authors provide convergence rate guarantees for both methods and demonstrate their effectiveness on a web page ranking problem.

**Strengths:**

1. The paper proposes two novel optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of both methods, which is essential for understanding their behavior.
3. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods on a real-world problem, which is a significant advantage.

**Weaknesses:**

1. The main paper is incomplete, as all experimental results are placed in the supplement. This makes it difficult to evaluate the paper without referring to the supplement.
2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the problem (2.4) is non-convex. It is unclear how to choose Φ to guarantee objective convexity.
3. Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - x{K+1}) but it is unclear whether this ensures convergence of x_k to a stationary point.
4. Supplementary Figure 1 shows that GFNM converges slowly. The authors should report and compare algorithmic efficiency.

**Quality:** 7/10
The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of both methods. However, the main paper is incomplete, and some of the theorems have unclear assumptions.

**Clarity:** 8/10
The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed methods and their analysis. However, the supplement is not well-organized, and some of the figures are not clearly labeled.

**Originality:** 9/10
The paper proposes two novel optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of both methods.

**Significance:** 8/10
The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods on a real-world problem, which is a significant advantage. However, the impact of the paper is limited to the specific problem of learning Supervised PageRank models.

**Recommendation:**
I recommend accepting the paper, but the authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above, particularly the incompleteness of the main paper and the unclear assumptions in some of the theorems. Additionally, the authors should improve the organization and clarity of the supplement.
