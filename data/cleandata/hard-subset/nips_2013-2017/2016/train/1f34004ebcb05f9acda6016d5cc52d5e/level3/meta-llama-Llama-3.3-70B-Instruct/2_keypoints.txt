- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, q-KG, derived from a decision-theoretical perspective.  
- q-KG optimizes the expected incremental value of measurements in a parallel (batch) evaluation setting.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently maximize the q-KG acquisition function and compute its gradient.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic functions and practical ML problems, working in both noise-free and noisy settings.  
- Experimental results show q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art benchmark algorithms.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a strong, motivating introduction.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and highlights differences from existing batch BO methods.  
- Technical contributions are significant: a new batch-aware acquisition function and an efficient gradient computation method.  
- Assumes feasible domain A is finite or discretizable; lacks discussion on handling infinite/continuous domains.  
- Computational complexity of q-KG and the IPA approach is only briefly discussed and needs deeper analysis.  
- Paper could better discuss practical significance, impact, and application contexts for q-KG.  
- More empirical comparison with other methods is needed, particularly in the noisy setting.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound with convincing experiments; minor revisions would strengthen impact and completeness.
