- Paper studies supervised, feature-parameterized PageRank via two-level optimization: inner approximate stationary distributions and derivatives, outer parameter updates with inexact oracles.
- Uses Nesterov–Nemirovski linearly convergent inner method and extends it to compute sensitivities w.r.t. node/edge feature weights.
- Develops (i) a biased, inexact zero-order (random gradient-free) algorithm for constrained problems, and (ii) an adaptive projected gradient method for composite nonconvex objectives with inexact first-order oracles.
- Provides convergence-rate bounds and end-to-end complexity estimates linking inner accuracy to outer progress.
- Empirical evaluation on a Yandex browsing-graph dataset comparing to a prior gradient-based baseline using power iterations.
- Relation to prior work: builds on supervised PageRank/supervised random walks literature and adapts inexact-oracle optimization techniques to this setting.
- Strength: clearly identifies the convergence-gap in prior supervised PageRank training and provides end-to-end guarantees.
- Strength: technical contributions include inexact biased zero-order scheme, adaptive projected gradient with inexact oracle, derivative-approximation recursions with error control, and inner–outer trade-off analysis.
- Strength: sensible inner solver choice (Nesterov–Nemirovski averaging) with linear convergence for moderate α.
- Strength: includes empirical comparison on a real industrial dataset and some attention to graph-size effects.
- Weakness: presentation issues—grammar, citation problems, heavy reliance on supplement for proofs/constants/experimental details.
- Weakness: confusion and insufficient reconciliation with closely related methods (naming/citation ambiguity around [21] and variants).
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions limit applicability—gradient-free analysis assumes local convexity; inexact-oracle assumptions (bounded δ, Lipschitz) may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: constraining parameters to a Euclidean ball (R=0.99 around 1) may limit expressiveness; no sensitivity analysis provided.
- Weakness: experiments lack crucial details—ranking metrics (NDCG/MAP), absolute improvements, variance, runtimes, inner iteration counts, ablations, and robustness checks; possible typo in GBP stopping rule.
- Quality: theory appears solid but main text needs proof sketches, explicit constants, and clearer assumptions; empirical methodology needs more rigor.
- Originality: incremental but nontrivial—adapts known inexact-oracle and gradient-free frameworks to constrained, biased-oracle PageRank setting with a derivative scheme.
- Significance: could be impactful if empirically substantiated, but current experiments undermine practical significance.
- Pros for acceptance: end-to-end convergence guarantees, useful inexact-oracle extensions, and a sensible inner solver with benefits over power iteration.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient empirical validation, unclear relation to prior baselines, strong unverified assumptions, and poor presentation.
- Suggestions: clarify relation to prior work and terminology, add main-text proof sketches and constants, report standard ranking metrics and runtimes with confidence intervals, provide ablations and sensitivity analyses (N, N1, N2, R, ϕ̂), fix/clarify GBP stopping rule, and consider less restrictive positivity parameterizations.
- Recommendation: Weak reject due to inadequate empirical evidence and clarity despite sound theoretical contributions.
