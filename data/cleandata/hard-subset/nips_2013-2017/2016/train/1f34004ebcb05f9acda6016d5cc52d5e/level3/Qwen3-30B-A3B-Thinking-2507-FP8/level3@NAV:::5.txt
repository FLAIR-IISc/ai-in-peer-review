

This paper proposes gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models with theoretical convergence guarantees. The authors build upon the framework introduced in [21] (which they incorrectly label as "Supervised PageRank" instead of "semi-supervised PageRank"), extending it with theoretical analysis of convergence rates for both optimization approaches. They develop a framework for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles, leveraging stationary distribution approximations for Markov walks.

Strengths include strong theoretical contributions: the paper provides rigorous proofs of convergence rates for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods, addressing a gap in prior work. The technical assumptions and loss function are well-defined, and the authors establish meaningful theoretical bounds for their algorithms.

However, the paper suffers from significant weaknesses. The core theoretical framework essentially reproduces existing work under different notation, undermining originality claims. The experimental section is severely lacking: key parameters are unreported, results are deferred to the appendix, and the claimed improvements over baselines are not properly justified or contextualized. The paper fails to demonstrate whether the algorithms work well on diverse datasets or whether the constrained parameter search within an R-ball around phi0 (which lacks justification) limits practical utility. The writing contains grammatical errors and sloppy citations, and the main text omits crucial details that would enable reproducibility.

While the theoretical bounds are strong, the paper fails to convincingly demonstrate practical impact. The experimental validation is insufficient to support the claim that the proposed methods outperform existing approaches in real-world scenarios. The constraint of searching within an R-ball around phi0 appears unnecessarily restrictive without justification, potentially undermining real-world applicability.

In summary, this paper makes a minor theoretical contribution to an existing framework but fails to deliver convincing empirical validation or practical insights. The theoretical analysis is sound but does not sufficiently advance the field given the lack of demonstrated practical impact. I recommend rejection due to insufficient empirical validation and overstated novelty claims.
