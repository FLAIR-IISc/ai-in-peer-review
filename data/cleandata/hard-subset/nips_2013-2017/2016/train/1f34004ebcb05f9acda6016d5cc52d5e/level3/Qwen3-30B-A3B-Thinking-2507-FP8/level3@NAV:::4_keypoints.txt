- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank via non-convex loss minimization, proposing gradient-based and random gradient-free methods.  
- Builds on Supervised PageRank framework to incorporate node and edge features.  
- Key theoretical contribution: provides convergence rate guarantees for both methods, missing in prior work.  
- Uses the inexact oracle framework and analyzes trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall complexity.  
- Strong theoretical rigor: rigorous proofs for convergence in constrained non-convex optimization settings.  
- Empirical results: methods outperform existing approaches on a real-world web ranking task in ranking quality.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and connects appropriately to prior PageRank and optimization literature.  
- Major limitation: fails to report computational runtime of the proposed methods.  
- Omission of runtime data prevents assessment of practical efficiency and trade-offs, undermining claims of practical utility.  
- Recommendation: authors should include runtime measurements in a revision to validate practical applicability.
