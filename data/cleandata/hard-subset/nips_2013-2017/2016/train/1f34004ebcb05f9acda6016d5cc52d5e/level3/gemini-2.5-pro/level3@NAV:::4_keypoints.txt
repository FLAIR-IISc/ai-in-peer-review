- Problem: learning parameters for Supervised PageRank via non-convex loss-minimization.  
- Prior limitation: previous gradient-based method [21] lacks theoretical convergence guarantees because it uses an inexact oracle (power method).  
- Main contribution: two new optimization algorithms (gradient-based and random gradient-free) tailored to work with an inexact oracle and provide formal convergence-rate guarantees.  
- Methodological advance: extends existing optimization frameworks [7,16] to constrained problems and formally analyzes the trade-off between lower-level oracle accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Theoretical strength: provides rigorous complexity bounds and the first convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank models.  
- Originality: novel adaptation of inexact-oracle optimization techniques to a practical information-retrieval problem.  
- Empirical result: experiments on real-world browsing data show the proposed methods—particularly the gradient-free (GFN)—outperform the state-of-the-art baseline in final loss.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation omits wall-clock runtime comparisons; iteration counts and operation counts may not reflect practical efficiency.  
- Recommendation: report actual runtimes (time to convergence) for GFN, GBN, and baseline GBP to clarify practical trade-offs and efficiency.
