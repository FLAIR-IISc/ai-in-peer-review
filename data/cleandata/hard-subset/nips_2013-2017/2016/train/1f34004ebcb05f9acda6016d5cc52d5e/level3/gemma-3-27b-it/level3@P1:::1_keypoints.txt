- Problem: Extends PageRank to Supervised PageRank with node/edge features, leading to a non-convex loss where exact values/derivatives are unavailable.  
- Main contribution: Proposes two two-level methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) using an inexact-oracle framework.  
- Lower-level approach: Approximates the stationary distribution via a linearly convergent method.  
- Upper-level approach: Tunes model parameters using either gradient descent or a gradient-free optimizer on the inexact oracle.  
- Theoretical result: Proves convergence rates for both methods and analyzes the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and total complexity.  
- Empirical result: Methods outperform the prior state-of-the-art [21] on a real web page ranking task.  
- Technical quality: Analysis appears rigorous with clear convergence and complexity discussions.  
- Writing and organization: Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Novelty concern: Limited novelty — mainly applies established inexact-oracle and gradient-free ideas to supervised PageRank.  
- Related work gap: Needs stronger discussion of Nesterov & Spokoiny [26] and other inexact-oracle literature.  
- Theoretical impact: Solid but incremental advancement rather than a groundbreaking theoretical contribution.  
- Presentation weakness: Lacks a concise conclusion and broader framing relating the problem to general constrained non-convex optimization.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add a concluding section and better contextualize related work.  
- Pros summary: Strong technical analysis, empirical improvement, practical relevance, clear presentation.  
- Cons summary: Limited novelty, missing conclusion, and insufficient broader contextualization.
