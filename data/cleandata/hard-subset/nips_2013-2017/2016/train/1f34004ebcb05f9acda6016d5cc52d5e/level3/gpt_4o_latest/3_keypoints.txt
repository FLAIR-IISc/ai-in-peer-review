- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for supervised PageRank that handle non-convex loss using inexact oracle information.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees: gradient-based method converges without convexity assumptions; gradient-free method ensures loss decrease and both have rate estimates.  
- Avoids expensive large-matrix operations and analyzes the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and computational complexity.  
- Demonstrates empirical superiority over state-of-the-art on a real-world dataset (improved ranking quality).  
- Paper is technically rigorous with detailed proofs and analyses supporting claims.  
- Novel combination of random gradient-free and gradient-based methods with inexact oracle concepts for constrained non-convex optimization.  
- Presentation clarity is weak due to excessive symbols and delayed definitions (e.g., N); a symbol table is recommended.  
- Experimental evaluation focuses more on loss than on direct ranking-quality metrics, which limits the practical assessment.  
- Dataset used is not publicly available, reducing reproducibility and benchmarking ability.  
- Lacks a direct comparison with an important reference work (ref: 17), weakening relative evaluation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a challenging problem, introduces novel methods with strong theory, and shows empirical improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: suboptimal clarity, insufficient experimental robustness and comparisons, and reproducibility concerns.  
- Recommendations: add a symbol table and earlier definitions, expand experiments to include ranking metrics and ref:17 comparison, and release synthetic or partial data.  
- Conclusion: Significant contribution; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing clarity and experimental/reproducibility issues.
