- Problem: learning Supervised PageRank models with non-convex optimization where node/edge weights are defined by feature-driven random walks.  
- Method: a two-level optimization framework — level 1 estimates the Markov stationary distribution with a linearly convergent method; level 2 uses a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle.  
- Technical contribution: adapts inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to known precision.  
- Theory: provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods; proofs appear correct (not all verified in detail).  
- Experiments: web-ranking experiments show significant improvements over the state-of-the-art [21], with the gradient-based method especially effective.  
- Writing and clarity: clear, well-organized, and makes technical contributions accessible.  
- Novelty concern: novelty is limited—core ideas resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and supervised PageRank has prior work; literature contextualization is weak.  
- Missing content: paper lacks a conclusion section and should present the optimization problem in a more general context to highlight differences from prior work.  
- Significance: moderate — theoretically grounded and relevant to ranking, but incremental optimization advances may limit broader impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address novelty concerns and add a conclusion.
