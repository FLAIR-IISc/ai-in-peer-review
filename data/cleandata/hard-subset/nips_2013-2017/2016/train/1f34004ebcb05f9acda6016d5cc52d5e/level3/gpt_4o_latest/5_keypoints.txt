- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by proposing a gradient-based and a gradient-free optimization method to improve over prior work [21].
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods, filling a gap in existing algorithms.
- Adapts gradient-free methods to constrained optimization with inexact oracles, an innovative methodological contribution.
- Incorporates node and edge features into the PageRank framework and analyzes accuracy vs. computational complexity trade-offs.
- Demonstrates method performance on a real-world web ranking task, claiming better ranking quality than the state-of-the-art.
- Experimental details are sparse in the main text, with critical results and setup moved to the appendix, harming reproducibility.
- Paper writing is poor with grammatical errors, unclear explanations, and sloppy citations, reducing accessibility.
- Originality is questioned because the framework appears to reproduce [21] with different notation and naming.
- Generalizability is unclear since experiments use only a single web graph dataset.
- Practical significance and some technical assumptions are questionable and may not hold in real-world settings.
- The restricted parameter search space (ϕ) limits applicability and is not adequately discussed.
- Recommendation: Reject, but encourage resubmission after addressing clarity, originality, and empirical validation issues.
- Suggested fixes: improve clarity and organization, expand experimental discussion and details, explicitly differentiate from [21], and test on diverse datasets.
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretical convergence guarantees and the novel gradient-free approach could inspire follow-up work.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor presentation, limited empirical validation, questionable novelty, and unclear practical impact.
