- Problem: Learns Supervised PageRank (SPR) models by incorporating node/edge features into PageRank for ranking tasks.  
- Methods: Proposes two novel optimization methods — a gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method for non-convex SPR learning.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides the first convergence guarantees for SPR optimization under constrained non-convex objectives with inexact oracle.  
- Technical results: Develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle and proves convergence to stationary points.  
- Technical results: Adapts Nesterov & Spokoiny’s random gradient-free method to constrained problems with inexact oracle and proves convergence.  
- Complexity analysis: Thorough trade-off analysis between lower-level approximation accuracy (power method iterations) and overall computational complexity with explicit bounds.  
- Empirical results: Experiments on Yandex web-ranking data show both methods outperform prior state-of-the-art, with the gradient-based method especially strong.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear with precise problem formulation and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- Motivation gap: Some parameter choices (e.g., R = 0.99) lack sufficient justification and discussion of their effect.  
- Originality: Significant — first to provide convergence guarantees for SPR and to extend gradient-free methods to constrained inexact-oracle settings.  
- Significance: High theoretical and practical impact; advances optimization theory with applicability beyond SPR and improves ranking quality in practice.  
- Major weakness: Does not report actual computational runtimes, limiting assessment of practical utility despite theoretical complexity bounds.  
- Minor weakness: Limited discussion and empirical evidence on scalability to extremely large graphs.  
- Overall assessment: Strong theoretical and empirical contribution that merits acceptance, though strengthened by runtime and larger-scale experiments.
