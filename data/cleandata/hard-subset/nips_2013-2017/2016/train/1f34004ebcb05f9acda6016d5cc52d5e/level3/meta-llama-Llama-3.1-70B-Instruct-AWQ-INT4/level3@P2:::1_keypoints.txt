- Proposes two optimization methods for Supervised PageRank combining a linearly convergent stationary-distribution estimator with a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle.  
- Clear and concise introduction that explains limitations of existing methods and motivation for the new approach.  
- Provides a detailed algorithmic description including the linear-convergence estimator and the constrained inexact-gradient method.  
- Significant technical contribution: adapts inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable to known precision.  
- Convergence proofs appear correct (not all checked in detail).  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation and presentation.  
- Experimental results are convincing, showing the methods outperform state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.  
- Concerns about novelty: key optimization ideas resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and supervised PageRank has prior work.  
- Suggestion to present a more general formulation of the optimization problem to better clarify contributions relative to prior art.  
- Recommend acceptance, but ask authors to address novelty concerns, provide more experimental setup and parameter-tuning details, and explore broader applications.
