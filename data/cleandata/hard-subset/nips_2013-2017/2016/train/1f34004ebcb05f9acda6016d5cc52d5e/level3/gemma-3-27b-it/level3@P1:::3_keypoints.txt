- Proposes two two-level methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for Supervised PageRank that incorporate node and edge features.  
- Handles non-convex optimization via an inexact oracle to efficiently approximate loss and derivatives for large graphs.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both methods, improving over prior work (ref. 21).  
- Builds on iterative methods for stationary distribution estimation (ref. 17) and extends them to constrained optimization with inexact information.  
- Well-motivated problem with practical relevance for network analysis and ranking.  
- Clearly positions work within existing literature (refs. 18, 11, 21, 16, 7).  
- Theoretical analysis is detailed (in supplement) and strengthens confidence in convergence properties.  
- Authors articulate trade-offs between accuracy and computational complexity and analyze computational cost.  
- Experimental results show improvement over state-of-the-art on a real-world web ranking task.  
- Experimental setup is described in detail (dataset and hyperparameters), aiding reproducibility despite dataset not being public.  
- Presentation of mathematical derivations is dense and can be hard to follow.  
- Missing a symbol table; many symbols are introduced without clear explanation.  
- Experimental evaluation focuses on loss values and lacks direct comparisons on ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG, MAP) versus ref. 21.  
- Lacks a direct experimental comparison to ref. 17 to demonstrably show claimed generalization.  
- Limited discussion of method limitations, sensitivity to hyperparameters, and scalability to extremely large graphs.  
- Unclear what assumptions on graph structure or feature distributions are needed for convergence guarantees.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve notation clarity, add ranking metric evaluations, and compare to ref. 17.
