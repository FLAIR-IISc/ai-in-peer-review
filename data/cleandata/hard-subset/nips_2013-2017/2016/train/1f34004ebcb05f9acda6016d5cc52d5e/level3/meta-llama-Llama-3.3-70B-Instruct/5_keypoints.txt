- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm (q-KG) for parallel evaluation of multiple points.
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretical perspective and shows Bayes-optimality when only one decision remains.
- Paper is well-structured with clear background on Gaussian processes and related work.
- Presents a computationally efficient method to maximize the q-KG acquisition function.
- Experimental results show q-KG is competitive with or outperforms state-of-the-art on synthetic functions and some ML problems in both noisy and noise-free settings.
- Strength: addresses the common practical scenario of parallel evaluations in Bayesian optimization.
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation via decision-theoretic derivation.
- Strength: provides a practical, scalable approach for acquisition optimization.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple settings.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of the q-KG algorithm’s computational complexity in parallel settings.
- Weakness: experiments are largely on synthetic functions and a limited set of practical problems; more real-world/large-scale tests needed.
- Weakness: insufficient insight into hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance.
- Weakness: assumes expensive function evaluations; performance under cheap-evaluation scenarios is not explored.
- Meets conference criteria for technical quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
- Pro-accept: novel algorithm, strong theoretical basis, and effective empirical performance.
- Con-accept: needs more complexity analysis, broader real-world experiments, and clearer hyperparameter tuning details; minor revisions recommended.
