This paper proposes two optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, which account for features of nodes and edges in a graph. The main idea is to develop a two-level algorithm that combines a linearly convergent method for estimating the stationary distribution of a Markov random walk with a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, explaining the limitations of existing optimization methods and the need for a new approach. They also provide a detailed description of the proposed algorithm, including the linearly convergent method for estimating the stationary distribution and the gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization.

The technical contributions of the paper are significant, as the authors adapt inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to known precision. The proofs of the convergence theorems appear correct, although not all were checked in detail.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas. The experimental results are also convincing, showing that the proposed methods outperform existing state-of-the-art algorithms on a web page ranking problem.

However, there are some concerns about the novelty of the paper. The key optimization ideas resemble those of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), and the supervised PageRank algorithm has prior work. Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more general presentation of the optimization problem to clarify the contributions relative to the state-of-the-art.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is also well-organized, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their ideas.
