Paper addresses parameter learning in Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem.  
Proposes two methods: a random gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle, and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.  
Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both methods, claiming to fill a prior gap.  
Strength: strong theoretical rigor and novel use of the inexact oracle framework for Supervised PageRank.  
Major clarity issue: all experiments are placed in the supplementary material, leaving the main paper empirically incomplete.  
Technical concern: Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite the loss being non-convex, with no practical guidance on ensuring convexity of Φ.  
Unclear theoretical claim: Theorem 3 bounds ||MK(xK - xK+1)|| but does not explain why this implies convergence of iterates to a stationary point.  
Practical significance concern: gradient-free method appears to converge very slowly (Supp. Fig. 1) and lacks comparisons in wall-clock time or oracle calls.  
Overall: interesting theoretical advance but insufficient empirical validation and problematic assumptions—paper not yet ready for publication.
