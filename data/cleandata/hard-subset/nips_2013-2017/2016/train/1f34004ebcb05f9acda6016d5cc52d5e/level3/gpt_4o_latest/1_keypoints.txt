- Problem: Convergence for non-convex loss-minimization in Supervised PageRank (graph-based ranking) with constrained parameters.  
- Method: Novel two-level optimization framework to avoid needing exact objective values for constrained problems.  
- Lower level: Approximates Markov stationary distribution via a linearly convergent method.  
- Upper level: Uses gradient-based or gradient-free optimization with inexact-oracle guarantees.  
- Theory: Provides convergence proofs and complexity bounds for both algorithms within the two-level framework.  
- Experiments: Validated on a real-world ranking task and shown to outperform state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: Rigorous proofs and well-motivated use of inexact oracles addressing a gap in constrained non-convex optimization.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, organized, and systematic presentation of methods and results.  
- Strength — Significance: Advances learning of graph-based ranking models with theoretical guarantees and practical gains.  
- Strength — Experimental validation: Thorough comparisons on real data demonstrating advantages.
