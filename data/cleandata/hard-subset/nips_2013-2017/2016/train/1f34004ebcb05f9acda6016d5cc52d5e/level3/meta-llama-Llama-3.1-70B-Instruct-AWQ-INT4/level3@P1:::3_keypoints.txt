- Proposes two novel optimization methods (GBN — gradient-based, GFN — gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Methods solve a non-convex loss-minimization problem and come with analyzed convergence rates.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Provides detailed descriptions of both methods, aiding reproducibility and understanding.
- Theoretical analysis of convergence rates is a significant contribution.
- Experiments on a real-world ranking task demonstrate the methods’ effectiveness.
- Strength: novelty of GBN and GFN along with theoretical guarantees.
- Strength: thorough, clear presentation and organization.
- Weakness: no symbol table despite heavy use of symbols, making it hard to track notation.
- Weakness: experiments compare to GBP only by loss, not by ranking quality metrics.
- Weakness: should include a direct experimental comparison with Nesterov & Nemirovski (ref. 17), which the work generalizes.
- Quality assessment: technically sound with thorough analysis.
- Clarity assessment: clear introduction and detailed method exposition.
- Originality assessment: presents novel methods with theoretical guarantees.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — add a symbol table and include comparison to ref. 17.
- Rating: 8/10.
