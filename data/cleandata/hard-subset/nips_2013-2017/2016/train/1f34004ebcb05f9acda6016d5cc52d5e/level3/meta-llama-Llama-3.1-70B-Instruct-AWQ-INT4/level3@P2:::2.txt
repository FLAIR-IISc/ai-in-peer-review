This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models by formulating the problem as a constrained non-convex optimization that incorporates node and edge features. The authors introduce two solution methods: a gradient-free approach with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle. Both methods are theoretically grounded, and the authors provide convergence rate guarantees.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem formulation is clear. The authors provide a thorough review of related work, including existing methods for learning PageRank models and optimization techniques. The proposed methods are well-explained, and the theoretical analysis is rigorous.

However, there are some concerns regarding the experimental results. All the experimental results are placed in the supplement, making the main paper incomplete. It would be beneficial to include at least some of the experimental results in the main text to make the paper self-contained. This would allow readers to better understand the performance of the proposed methods and their advantages over existing approaches.

Another concern is the assumption of convexity in Theorem 1. The objective function (2.4) is non-convex, and it is unclear how to choose Φ to guarantee objective convexity. The authors should provide more guidance on how to select Φ in practice.

Additionally, Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - x{K+1}), but it is unclear whether this ensures convergence of x_k to a stationary point. The authors should provide more discussion on the implications of this result and how it relates to the convergence of the algorithm.

Finally, Supplementary Figure 1 shows that GFNM converges slowly, and the authors should report and compare the algorithmic efficiency of the proposed methods. This would provide a more complete picture of the performance of the methods and help readers understand their practical implications.

* The proposed methods are well-explained, and the convergence rate guarantees are a significant contribution.

Weaknesses:

* The experimental results are not included in the main text, making the paper incomplete.
