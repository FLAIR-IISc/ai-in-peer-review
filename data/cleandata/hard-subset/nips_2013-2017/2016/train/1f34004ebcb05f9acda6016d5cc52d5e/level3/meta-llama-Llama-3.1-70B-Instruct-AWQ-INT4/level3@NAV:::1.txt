This paper presents a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges in a graph. The authors propose two two-level methods to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem of learning these models. The first level of both methods uses a linearly convergent method to estimate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, while the second level uses a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear to be correct, although not all were checked in detail. The main technical contribution of the paper is adapting inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to known precision.

The paper has several strengths:

1. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their methods, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. The proposed methods are shown to outperform existing state-of-the-art algorithms in experiments on a real-world ranking task.
3. The paper presents a novel combination of techniques from optimization and machine learning, which could lead to new insights and applications in both fields.

However, there are also some weaknesses and concerns:

1. The novelty of the key optimization ideas is not entirely clear, as they resemble previous work by Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015).
2. The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods, including a discussion of the advantages and disadvantages of each approach.
3. The experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset, and it would be beneficial to see results on additional datasets to confirm the generality of the findings.
4. The paper lacks a conclusion section, which would help to summarize the main contributions and implications of the work.
5. The optimization problem is not presented in a more general context, which would help to clarify the contributions relative to the state-of-the-art.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning, and the authors have made a good effort to present their work in a clear and concise manner. However, addressing the concerns mentioned above would strengthen the paper and make it more suitable for publication in a top-tier conference.
