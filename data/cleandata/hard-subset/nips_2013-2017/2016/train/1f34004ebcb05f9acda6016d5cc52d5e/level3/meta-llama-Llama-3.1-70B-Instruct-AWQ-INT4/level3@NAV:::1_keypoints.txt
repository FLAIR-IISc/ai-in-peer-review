- Presents a novel approach for learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node and edge features.  
- Proposes two two-level methods: level one estimates the stationary distribution with a linearly convergent method, level two uses a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle.  
- Main technical contribution: adapts inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to known precision.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct.  
- Thorough analysis of convergence rates is a significant contribution.  
- Methods outperform existing state-of-the-art in experiments on a real-world ranking task.  
- Novel combination of optimization and machine-learning techniques with potential broader impact.  
- Novelty of key optimization ideas is unclear due to resemblance to Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015).  
- Needs more detailed comparison with existing methods, discussing pros and cons.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a single dataset; additional datasets are needed to confirm generality.  
- Paper lacks a conclusion section to summarize contributions and implications.  
- Optimization problem is not presented in a broader/general context to clarify relative contributions.  
- Recommendations: add a conclusion, provide more detailed comparisons, run experiments on additional datasets, and present the optimization problem in a more general context.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution but requires minor revisions; Rating 8/10, Accept with minor revisions, Confidence 8/10.
