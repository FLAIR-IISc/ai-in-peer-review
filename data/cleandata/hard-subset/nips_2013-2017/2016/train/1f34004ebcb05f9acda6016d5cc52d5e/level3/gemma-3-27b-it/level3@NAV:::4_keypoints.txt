- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by proposing both gradient-based and random gradient-free methods for non-convex loss minimization.
- Provides novel convergence-rate guarantees for both methods, improving over prior work (e.g., [21]) that lacked such guarantees.
- Extends established PageRank/random-walk models to incorporate node and edge features, situating the work well within existing literature.
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level algorithm accuracy and overall computational complexity carefully.
- Empirical results show the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art in ranking quality.
- Paper is generally well-written and logically organized, though mathematically dense in parts.
- Major omission: no reported runtimes or empirical scalability analysis; computational time comparisons with baseline [21] are missing.
- Limited discussion of method limitations, especially regarding convexity assumptions.
- Insufficient detail on sensitivity to hyperparameters beyond exploration of L.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on adding runtime measurements and expanded discussion of convexity and hyperparameter sensitivity.
