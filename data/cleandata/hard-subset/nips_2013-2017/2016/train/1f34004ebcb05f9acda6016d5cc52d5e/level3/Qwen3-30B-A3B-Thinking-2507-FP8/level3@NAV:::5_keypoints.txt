- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods for Supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Builds on prior framework ([21]) but mislabels it as "Supervised PageRank" instead of "semi-supervised PageRank."  
- Develops constrained non-convex optimization framework using inexact oracles and stationary distribution approximations for Markov walks.  
- Strong theoretical contribution: provides rigorous convergence proofs and meaningful bounds for both methods.  
- Technical assumptions and loss function are well-defined.  
- Core framework largely reproduces existing work under different notation, weakening originality.  
- Experimental section is inadequate: key parameters unreported, results relegated to appendix, and claimed improvements not properly justified.  
- Limited empirical validation: unclear performance across diverse datasets and insufficient demonstration of real-world impact.  
- The R-ball constraint around phi0 lacks justification and may limit practical applicability.  
- Writing quality issues and sloppy citations; main text omits crucial reproducibility details.  
- Overall: sound theoretical analysis but insufficient empirical evidence and overstated novelty—recommend rejection.
