This paper proposes two novel optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges. The authors develop a gradient-based and a gradient-free optimization method to solve a non-convex loss-minimization problem. The main technical contribution of the paper is adapting inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to a known precision.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear to be correct, although not all of them were checked in detail. The authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed methods, and the theoretical analysis of their convergence rates.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rates of the proposed methods, which is a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art gradient-based method. The authors also demonstrate the effectiveness of their methods through experiments on a real-world ranking task.

However, there are some concerns about the novelty of the key optimization ideas, which resemble the work of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015). Additionally, the supervised PageRank algorithm has prior work, and it is not entirely clear how the proposed methods differ from existing approaches.

A minor issue with the paper is the lack of a conclusion section, which would provide a summary of the main contributions and implications of the work. Furthermore, it would be helpful to present the optimization problem in a more general context to clarify the contributions relative to the state-of-the-art.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

The clarity of the paper is good, and the organization is logical. The authors provide enough information for an expert reader to reproduce their results. However, some sections, such as the proof of Theorem 1, are relegated to the supplementary materials, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow the paper.

The originality of the paper is somewhat diminished by the similarities with existing work. However, the authors' contribution to adapting inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems is significant and novel.

The significance of the paper lies in its ability to provide a more efficient and effective method for learning Supervised PageRank models. The results have important implications for ranking tasks, and the methods proposed in the paper can be applied to other optimization problems with similar characteristics.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper provides a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges.
* The authors provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rates of the proposed methods, which is a significant improvement over the state-of-the-art gradient-based method.
* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed methods through experiments on a real-world ranking task.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The key optimization ideas resemble the work of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), which may raise concerns about novelty.
* The supervised PageRank algorithm has prior work, and it is not entirely clear how the proposed methods differ from existing approaches.
* The paper lacks a conclusion section, which would provide a summary of the main contributions and implications of the work.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a significant contribution to the field of optimization. While there are some concerns about novelty, the authors' ability to provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rates of the proposed methods and demonstrate their effectiveness through experiments makes the paper a valuable contribution to the field.
