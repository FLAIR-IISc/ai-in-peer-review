- Tackles learning feature-based Supervised PageRank with goal of providing convergence guarantees missing in prior work.  
- Proposes combining stationary-distribution approximation with both gradient-free and gradient-based optimization and offers theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong: lemmas bounding approximate gradients, a biased gradient-free oracle, and a performance theorem with tailored algorithms.  
- Uses a linearly convergent method [17] for stationary distribution approximation, addressing a limitation of prior approaches.  
- Motivation is sound: existing PageRank methods often ignore node/edge features and prior supervised methods lacked guarantees.  
- Significant clarity problems: poor writing, grammatical errors, and sloppy citations hinder understanding.  
- Citation error: incorrectly refers to Zhukovskii et al. [21] as "Supervised PageRank" when it is "Semi-supervised PageRank."  
- Some technical assumptions may be unrealistic, notably constraining parameter search to an R-ball around a specific ϕ0 without justification.  
- The choice and justification for R and ϕ0 are absent in experiments, raising robustness and sensitivity concerns.  
- Experimental section is weak: key parameters (α, R, features) are not reported and detailed results are pushed to the appendix.  
- Reported empirical improvements (GFN vs GBP) lack quantitative details, statistical significance, and discussion of effect size.  
- Unclear whether experiments used diverse graphs or multiple datasets, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Overreliance on appendix for proofs, experimental setup, and results makes assessment difficult.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical rigor and addressing prior lack of convergence guarantees.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor writing, insufficient empirical validation, and questionable practical applicability of assumptions.  
- Overall: theoretically promising but fails to demonstrate practical impact; requires major revision with improved writing and thorough experiments.
