The paper "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods" presents a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges. The authors propose two optimization methods, a gradient-free method and a gradient-based method, to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem. Both methods are based on the concept of an inexact oracle and provide theoretical guarantees on convergence rates.

The paper is well-motivated, and the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models is well-defined. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem and the existing methods, highlighting the limitations of the current approaches. The proposed methods are well-explained, and the mathematical derivations are sound.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors propose two novel optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges.
* The methods are based on the concept of an inexact oracle, which allows for efficient computation of the loss function and its derivatives.
* The authors provide theoretical guarantees on convergence rates for both methods.
* The experimental results show that the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based method.

However, there are some weaknesses and concerns:

* All experimental results are placed in the supplement, making the main paper incomplete. It would be better to include the experimental results in the main paper to make it self-contained.
* Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the loss function (2.4) is non-convex. It is unclear how to choose Φ to guarantee objective convexity.
* Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - x{K+1}) but it is unclear whether this ensures convergence of x_k to a stationary point.
* Supplementary Figure 1 shows that GFNM converges slowly, and the authors should report and compare algorithmic efficiency.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the completeness of the paper is affected by the placement of experimental results in the supplement.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, and the mathematical derivations are clear. However, some notations and definitions could be improved for better readability.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes novel optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, which is a significant contribution to the field.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the field of web-page ranking, and the proposed methods have the potential to improve the reliability and speed of search engines.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper, but with some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above.
