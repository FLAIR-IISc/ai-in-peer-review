- Paper studies supervised PageRank with linear feature-based restart/transition probabilities and proposes two two-level algorithms: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- GBN: projected adaptive gradient with inexact first-order oracle using Nesterov–Nemirovski solver for stationary distribution and derivative approximations; proves convergence to stationary points and gives complexity bounds.
- GFN: random gradient-free projected method using a biased finite-difference estimator and an inexact zero-order oracle; extends prior zeroth-order results to constrained, bounded-noise settings with complexity bounds.
- Main novelty is integrating a linearly convergent stationary-distribution solver into upper-level optimization under an inexact-oracle framework, yielding end-to-end rates and explicit accuracy/compute trade-offs.
- Theoretical lemmas for approximating f and ∇f with controllable accuracy are plausible and fill gaps from prior work relying on power methods.
- Inexact-oracle convergence results for projected gradient and constrained gradient-free methods appear technically sound; proofs are provided in the supplement.
- A strong caveat: GFN’s analysis assumes local convexity of f on Φ, which is a significant limitation given the overall nonconvexity and should be clearly stated.
- GBN’s guarantee is on prox-gradient norm (stationarity), not on objective suboptimality; this distinction should be explicitly stated when claiming convergence rates.
- Experiments use a real Yandex dataset and report loss improvements over a GBP baseline, but ranking metrics (NDCG/MAP) are not reported despite the ranking task.
- Empirical claims of improved ranking quality are unsupported without standard retrieval metrics and statistical tests; wall-clock times and lower-level iteration counts (N, N1, N2) are missing.
- Baseline (GBP) tuning details are limited and the stopping rule “difference < −1e−5” appears incorrect/unclear and needs clarification.
- Clarity issues: heavy notation, symbols (e.g., N, N1, N2) used before definition and overloaded, and a symbol table would help readability.
- Several references are incomplete or inconsistently formatted; algorithm numbering and supplement references could be streamlined; minor typos present.
- Originality: novel adaptation of random gradient-free methods to constrained bounded-noise settings and of projected adaptive gradient schemes with inexact oracles in nonconvex constrained supervised PageRank context.
- Significance: practical relevance for graph-based ranking with provable convergence; theoretical framework potentially reusable in other inexact-oracle graph learning problems.
- Pros: clear problem formulation, integration of lower/upper-level methods, convergence guarantees for both approaches, complexity bounds, and use of a real dataset with reported hyperparameters.
- Cons: empirical focus on loss rather than ranking metrics, limited baseline tuning and no runtime comparisons, reliance on unstated local convexity for GFN, and clarity/notation problems.
- Suggested experiments: report NDCG/MAP with p-values, wall-clock times, number of lower-level iterations, and compare to power-method lower level and other upper-level optimizers (e.g., Ghadimi–Lan).
- Suggested clarifications: detail GBP setup (step sizes, stopping rule, power iterations), explain how L, β1, δ are estimated and provide sensitivity analysis, and discuss practical impact of local convexity assumption.
- Presentation fixes: add a symbol table, unify notation for N/N1/N2, complete all references, fix typos/grammar, and consider releasing code and a public or synthetic dataset for reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: promising theoretical contributions but empirical evaluation and clarity need strengthening; reviewer leans weak accept if experiments and assumption clarifications are improved, otherwise borderline.
