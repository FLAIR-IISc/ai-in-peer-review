- Proposes two novel optimization methods for Supervised PageRank: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Provides convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods and demonstrates effectiveness on a web page ranking task.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and contains a thorough review of existing PageRank learning methods.  
- Strength: Clear explanations and theoretical guarantees for the methods.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation (though placed in the supplement) showing real-world effectiveness.  
- Strength: Detailed analysis of computational complexity important for large-scale problems.  
- Weakness: Experimental results are relegated to the supplement, making the main paper feel incomplete.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 assumes convexity while the objective (Eq. 2.4) is non-convex; unclear how to choose Φ to ensure convexity.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - x{K+1}) but it is unclear whether this implies convergence to a stationary point.  
- Weakness: Supplementary Figure 1 indicates GFNM converges slowly; algorithmic efficiency comparisons are missing.  
- Related work: Thorough review provided, but lacks discussion relating the proposed methods to SGD, quasi-Newton, and other optimization techniques.  
- Some mathematical derivations and proofs could be clarified for better understanding.  
- Overall assessment: Significant and novel contribution with potential practical impact, but needs to address theoretical and experimental presentation issues.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel methods with theoretical guarantees and potential to improve web ranking efficiency; well-written presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: Incomplete main paper due to supplemental experiments, questionable convexity assumption, and lack of clear algorithmic efficiency comparison.
