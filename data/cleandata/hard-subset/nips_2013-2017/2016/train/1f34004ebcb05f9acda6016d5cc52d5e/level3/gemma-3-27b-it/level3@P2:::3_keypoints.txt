- Tackles learning Supervised PageRank with two optimization methods: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).  
- Main contribution: theoretical convergence guarantees for both algorithms, addressing gaps in prior work [21].  
- Develops analysis and control of accuracy trade-offs under inexact oracle computations.  
- Uses a two-level optimization framework building on prior supervised PageRank approaches [6, 21].  
- Justifies use of method [17] for approximating stationary distributions due to favorable complexity.  
- Provides convergence rate proofs for both GBN and GFN in the non-convex setting.  
- Empirical results on a web ranking task show improvements over state-of-the-art [21].  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods.  
- Strength: explicit treatment of inexact oracle challenges relevant to large-scale graphs.  
- Strength: novel combination of two-level optimization and inexact oracle analysis.  
- Strength: clear problem definition and connection to existing literature.  
- Strength: experimental validation demonstrates practical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: dense notation and symbol overload; a symbol table is needed.  
- Weakness: experiments focus on loss only; missing direct ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP) and comparison to [21] and [17].  
- Weakness: dataset is not publicly available, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning process and sensitivity analysis are under-detailed.  
- Weakness: convexity assumption for f(ϕ) on Φ requires clarification and discussion of practical implications.  
- For acceptance: significant theoretical contributions, promising empirical improvements, and strong technical grounding.  
- Against acceptance: limited experimental scope (no ranking metrics), readability issues, and non-public dataset.  
- Overall: solid contribution with notable theoretical results but clarity and experimental limitations reduce impact.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, including a symbol table, ranking metric comparisons (NDCG/MAP) vs [21] and [17], hyperparameter details/sensitivity, and clarification of the convexity assumption.
