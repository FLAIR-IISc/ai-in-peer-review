- Proposes learning Supervised PageRank with feature-based weights using stationary-distribution approximation plus gradient-based and gradient-free optimization, offering theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Main theoretical contribution: rigorous lemmas bounding approximate gradients and a biased gradient-free oracle with performance theorems.
- Valuable framing around inexact oracles connecting Markov walk approximation to optimization theory.
- Incorrectly refers to semi-supervised PageRank as "Supervised PageRank" and includes an inaccurate citation, indicating weak familiarity with relevant prior work.
- Questionable originality in parts of the approach due to insufficient differentiation from prior art.
- Experimental evaluation is severely underdeveloped: results pushed to the appendix with minimal main-text discussion.
- Key experimental parameters are unreported, hindering reproducibility.
- Experiments are limited to a single web graph, raising concerns about generalizability.
- Poor writing quality, grammatical errors, and sloppy citations reduce clarity and readability.
- Some technical assumptions appear potentially unrealistic, affecting practical applicability.
- Insufficient discussion of the R-ball radius choice and its impact on method efficacy.
- Lack of proof sketches and comprehensive experimental details in the main text impedes understanding and assessment.
- Overall: strong theoretical work but weak presentation and empirical validation; recommends rejection and major revision before reconsideration.
