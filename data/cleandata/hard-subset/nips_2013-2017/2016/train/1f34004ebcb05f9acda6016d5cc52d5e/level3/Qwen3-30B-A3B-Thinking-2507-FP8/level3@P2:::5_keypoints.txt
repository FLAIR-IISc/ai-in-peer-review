- Paper proposes two-level optimization methods for Supervised PageRank, providing convergence guarantees for non-convex loss minimization.
- Main theoretical contribution: rigorous convergence rate guarantees for gradient-based and gradient-free methods under inexact oracle conditions.
- Complexity analysis for the two-level algorithms is thorough and provides useful bounds.
- Correctly identifies and addresses the lack of convergence guarantees in prior work ([21]).
- Writing quality is poor: grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, and sometimes sloppy mathematical presentation.
- Experimental validation is severely lacking and key results are relegated to the appendix rather than the main text.
- Important experimental details are missing: selection of phi0 and R, values of L for methods, and other critical hyperparameters.
- Reported improvements over prior work are not convincingly quantified or contextualized in the main text.
- Dataset description is insufficient (no details on query selection or graph diversity).
- The constraint of searching phi within an R-ball (R=0.99) is unjustified and may limit model expressiveness.
- Significance of practical impact is not demonstrated; statistical significance claims are not properly presented in the main text.
- Originality is limited: adapting inexact-oracle methods to Supervised PageRank appears straightforward and largely reproduces [21]’s framework.
- Quality assessment: strong theoretical content but insufficient empirical validation to support practical value.
- Recommendation: weak rejection due to inadequate experiments, missing experimental details, poor presentation, and insufficient demonstration of practical significance.
- Suggested improvements: include full experimental results in the main text, report and justify all hyperparameters, clarify relation to [21], contextualize practical significance, and improve writing and mathematical clarity.
