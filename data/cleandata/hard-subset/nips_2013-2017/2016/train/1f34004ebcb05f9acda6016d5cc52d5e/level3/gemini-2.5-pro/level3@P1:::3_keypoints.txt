- Addresses non-convex parameter learning for Supervised PageRank with node/edge features and proposes two two-level methods: gradient-based (GBN) and random gradient-free (GFN).
- Both methods are designed to work with an inexact oracle, avoiding exact loss/gradient computations.
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees and complexity analyses, unlike prior state-of-the-art [21].
- Lower-level oracle approximating stationary PageRank and derivatives is based on Nesterov & Nemirovski [17].
- Upper level uses an adaptive gradient method (GBN) or a random gradient-free method (GFN).
- Experimental results on real-world web ranking show lower final loss than the baseline GBP from [21].
- Strength: rigorous theoretical contribution, including generalizing random gradient-free methods to constrained problems and adaptive gradient methods with inexact oracles.
- Strength: practical relevance for large-scale ranking problems where exact stationary distributions are costly.
- Strength: complete work with problem formulation, algorithms, proofs, complexity analysis, and experiments.
- Weakness: paper is mathematically dense and hard to follow; many symbols introduced without central reference or notation table.
- Weakness: experiments only report final loss, not standard ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG, MAP), so impact on actual ranking quality is unclear.
- Weakness: baseline comparison may be unfair — gains could come from the superior lower-level oracle; lacked a GBP baseline using the Nesterov-Nemirovski oracle.
- Weakness: GFN’s convergence analysis assumes convexity and interior optimum, which contradicts the general non-convex setting and needs discussion on practicality.
- Suggestions: add a table of notation, include IR metrics (NDCG/MAP) on test data, compare GBP with the improved oracle, and report wall-clock times to convergence.
