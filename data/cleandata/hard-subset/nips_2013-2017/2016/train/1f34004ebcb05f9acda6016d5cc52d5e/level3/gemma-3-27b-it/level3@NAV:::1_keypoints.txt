- Problem addressed: learning Supervised PageRank with node/edge features via two-level gradient-based and gradient-free methods using inexact oracle estimates of the stationary distribution.
- Main contribution: convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free algorithms in the inexact-oracle, constrained non-convex setting.
- Theoretical strength: rigorous analysis showing how to approximate the loss to desired precision and provide convergence proofs.
- Technical novelty: notable adaptation of inexact-oracle gradient methods to constrained non-convex problems, though building on prior work.
- Experimental validation: real-world web ranking experiments demonstrate improved performance over state-of-the-art.
- Novelty concern: approach draws heavily on existing techniques (e.g., Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and supervised PageRank is not entirely new.
- Presentation issues: paper lacks a concluding section and could place the optimization problem in a broader, more general context.
- Suggested improvements: better position within supervised ranking literature and more clearly highlight specific advantages in convergence and practical performance.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add a conclusion and clarify broader contributions.
