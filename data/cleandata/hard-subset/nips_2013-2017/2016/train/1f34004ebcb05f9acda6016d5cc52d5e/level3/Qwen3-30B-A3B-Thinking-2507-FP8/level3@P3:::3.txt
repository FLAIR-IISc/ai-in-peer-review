

# Review: Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods

This paper proposes two novel two-level optimization methods (GBN and GFN) for learning Supervised PageRank models, addressing a non-convex loss-minimization problem. The authors build upon Nesterov and Nemirovski's framework (ref. 17) to develop both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches that avoid exact computation of large matrices while providing theoretical convergence guarantees. Unlike previous work (e.g., [21]), which lacked convergence proofs due to inexact oracle information, the proposed methods establish rigorous convergence rates for both the loss function and the parameters.

The key contributions include: (1) a gradient-based method (GBN) with theoretical convergence rate guarantees for non-convex problems; (2) a gradient-free method (GFN) with guaranteed decrease in the loss function; (3) a detailed analysis of the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and computational complexity; and (4) empirical validation on a real-world web ranking task showing superior performance over state-of-the-art methods.

**Quality:** The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with well-supported theoretical claims. The authors provide complete proofs for all convergence theorems in the supplementary material, establishing meaningful guarantees for both optimization methods. The complexity analysis (Theorems 2 and 4) provides concrete bounds on computational requirements, which is particularly valuable for large-scale applications. The experimental design is sound, with careful hyperparameter selection and comparison against the state-of-the-art (GBP) on a relevant ranking task. However, the empirical evaluation would be strengthened by direct comparison with the reference [17] (Nesterov-Nemirovski method), which the authors generalize, as requested by the reviewer.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-structured and follows a logical flow from problem formulation to theoretical development to empirical validation. The mathematical exposition is precise, though the paper would benefit significantly from a symbol table as requested by the reviewer (e.g., N is used before definition in Section 3). The methods are clearly described in Sections 2-5, and the supplementary materials contain all necessary proofs. The experimental section (Section 6) provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, though the datasets are not publicly available.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution to the field by extending Nesterov and Nemirovski's framework to constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles. The two-level optimization approach for Supervised PageRank is novel, particularly in providing convergence guarantees where previous work lacked them. The paper appropriately situates its work in relation to prior research, citing relevant literature including [5, 6, 17, 21]. The combination of gradient-free and gradient-based methods for this specific application represents a meaningful contribution.

**Significance:** The proposed methods address a practical problem in web ranking with significant real-world applications. The empirical results demonstrate clear improvements in ranking quality over state-of-the-art methods, which could directly benefit search engine performance. The theoretical guarantees provide researchers and practitioners with confidence in the convergence behavior, which is particularly valuable for large-scale applications where exact optimization is intractable. The avoidance of large matrix computations while maintaining high accuracy represents a substantial practical advantage.

**Strengths:**
- Strong theoretical contributions with complete convergence rate guarantees
- Practical implementation avoiding exact computation of large matrices
- Superior empirical performance on real-world ranking task
- Detailed complexity analysis for both methods
- Clear connection to Nesterov and Nemirovski's framework

**Weaknesses:**
- Missing symbol table makes the paper harder to follow
- Experiments compare with GBP only on loss function, not ranking quality
- Lacks direct comparison with the reference [17] (Nesterov-Nemirovski method) that the work generalizes
- Datasets not publicly available (though well-described)

**Recommendation:** I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a significant contribution to both theoretical optimization and practical ranking applications. The theoretical guarantees fill an important gap in the literature, and the empirical results demonstrate clear practical advantages. The weaknesses noted are relatively minor and can be addressed in the camera-ready version (e.g., adding a symbol table, providing the requested comparison with ref. 17, and clarifying the dataset availability).

The paper meets all four criteria for quality at NIPS: it is technically sound with well-supported claims, clearly written (with minor improvements possible), original in its approach to the optimization problem, and significant in both theoretical contribution and practical impact. The work represents a meaningful advancement in the field of optimization for graph-based ranking problems.
