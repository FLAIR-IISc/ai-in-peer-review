- Proposes two-level optimization methods (GBN gradient-based, GFN gradient-free) for learning Supervised PageRank on a non-convex loss with inexact oracles.  
- Extends Nesterov & Nemirovski’s framework to avoid exact large-matrix computations while providing theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Main contributions: GBN with convergence-rate guarantees for non-convex problems; GFN with guaranteed loss decrease; analysis of lower-level accuracy vs. complexity; empirical validation showing superior performance.  
- Strong technical quality with complete proofs in the supplement and concrete complexity bounds (Theorems 2 and 4).  
- Experimental design is careful (hyperparameter tuning, comparison to GBP) but lacks direct comparison to the original ref. [17] and ranking-quality comparisons vs. GBP.  
- Well-structured and mathematically precise, but clarity would improve with a symbol table and fixing symbols used before definition (e.g., N in Sec. 3).  
- Original contribution: novel extension to constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles and first convergence-guaranteed two-level approach for Supervised PageRank.  
- Significant practical impact: improves web-ranking quality, avoids large matrix computations, and provides guarantees valuable for large-scale applications.  
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical guarantees, practical algorithmic implementation, superior empirical results, detailed complexity analysis, clear link to prior framework.  
- Weaknesses: missing symbol table, experiments only compare GBP on loss (not ranking quality), no direct comparison to ref. [17], and datasets are not publicly available.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical and practical contribution; noted weaknesses are minor and fixable in the camera-ready.  
- Meets NIPS criteria: technically sound, clear with minor fixes, original, and significant.
