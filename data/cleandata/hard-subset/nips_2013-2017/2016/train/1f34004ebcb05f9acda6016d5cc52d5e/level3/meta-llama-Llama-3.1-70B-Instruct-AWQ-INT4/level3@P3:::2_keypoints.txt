- Presents a novel approach for learning Supervised PageRank that incorporates node and edge features.  
- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-free and gradient-based) based on an inexact oracle.  
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for both methods.  
- Well-motivated problem, clear introduction, and sound mathematical derivations.  
- Experimental results (in supplement) show the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based method.  
- Main paper is incomplete because all experimental results are placed in the supplement; should include key experiments in the main text.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite the loss (2.4) being non-convex; unclear how to choose Φ to guarantee convexity.  
- Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - x{K+1}) but it is unclear whether this implies convergence of x_k to a stationary point.  
- Supplementary Figure 1 indicates GFNM converges slowly; the paper should report and compare algorithmic efficiency.  
- Technically sound: claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Generally clear and well-written, but some notations and definitions need improvement for readability.  
- Original and significant contribution to learning Supervised PageRank with potential impact on web-page ranking.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted theoretical and experimental concerns.
