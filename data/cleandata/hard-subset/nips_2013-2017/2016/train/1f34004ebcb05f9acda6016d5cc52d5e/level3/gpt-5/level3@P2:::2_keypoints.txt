- Problem: Learns Supervised PageRank with linear feature-parameterized node/edge weights; objective is pairwise squared-hinge loss requiring stationary distributions and derivative approximations.
- Contribution (lower-level): Uses Nesterov–Nemirovski method to approximate stationary distributions and extends it to derivatives with error bounds and arithmetic complexity (claimed better than power method).
- Contribution (upper-level): Proposes two inexact-oracle algorithms — a random gradient-free method for constrained convex problems and an adaptive projected gradient method for possibly nonconvex composite problems — with convergence analyses.
- Complexity results: Provides end-to-end complexity bounds tying lower-level accuracy to upper-level progress and including graph parameters (|Q|, p, s, α).
- Empirics: On a Yandex browsing-graph dataset (~600 queries) both methods outperform a prior SPR learner using power iteration; gradient-based method uses fewer upper-level iterations.
- Strength: Principled two-level treatment of inexact oracles for a practical learning-to-rank-on-graphs problem.
- Strength: Provides convergence-rate guarantees and a controllable derivative approximation scheme that prior SPR training lacked.
- Strength: Complexity accounting explicitly links lower-level work to upper-level convergence and includes relevant graph parameters.
- Weakness: Theorem 1 assumes convexity though the objective is nonconvex; no verifiable conditions given to choose the convex ball Φ, limiting applicability of the guarantee.
- Weakness: Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK − xK+1) but does not explicitly relate this to a standard stationarity measure; needs explicit connection to projected gradient mapping and KKT/stationarity.
- Weakness: Gradient-free method requires known Lipschitz constant L, which is not bounded in the paper and is set heuristically in experiments; needs a bound or an adaptive/backtracking scheme.
- Weakness: Claim of “lowest proved complexity bound” versus prior work is imprecise and should specify method class, complexity measure, and aligned assumptions.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is thin in the main text with many details deferred to the supplement; key tables/plots, statistical tests, and wall-clock costs are missing.
- Clarity issue: Notation is dense and several constants (e.g., β1) are only defined in the supplement; provide high-level bounds or interpretations in the main text.
- Clarity issue: Description of constraint set Φ and positivity constraints is confusing; suggest using a simplex constraint or clearly state conditions that guarantee positivity of feature inner products.
- Citation/formatting issue: References are inconsistently formatted and some key related works are missing or incomplete.
- Originality: Meaningful synthesis of inexact-oracle optimization with PageRank lower-level approximations; novel extension of zeroth-order methods to bounded-error oracles and derivative estimation.
- Significance: Theoretical framework is relevant for learning with Markov processes, but practical impact requires stronger experiments (add NDCG@k/MAP, wall-clock, iteration counts, more baselines).
- Pro accept: Addresses an important problem, provides new convergence analyses and complexity accounting, and shows empirical gains over a relevant baseline.
- Con accept: Reliance on unverified convexity, unclear stationarity guarantees, thin main-paper experiments and runtime analysis, and clarity/reference issues weaken acceptance.
- Suggestion: Move core experimental tables/figures and runtime/IR metrics into the main paper and add ablations on τ, δ, and solver trade-offs (Nesterov–Nemirovski vs power method).
- Suggestion: Either give verifiable sufficient conditions ensuring local convexity on Φ or revise the gradient-free analysis to target nonconvex stationarity (e.g., Ghadimi & Lan).
- Suggestion: Explicitly state that MK(xK − xK+1) is the gradient mapping and provide the inequality relating it to dist(0, ∂ψ(x) + N_X(x)) or ∥∇f(ϕ)∥.
- Suggestion: Clarify and justify Φ and positivity maintenance (consider simplex due to scale invariance) and improve reference formatting and main-text definitions of constants.
- Overall: Promising theoretical contributions with practical relevance, but needs stronger experimental evidence and clearer, verifiable assumptions/guarantees.
