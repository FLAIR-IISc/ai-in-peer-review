- Summary: Learns parameters for Supervised PageRank via constrained non-convex optimization and proposes two inexact-oracle algorithms (random gradient-free and inexact gradient-based) with theoretical convergence guarantees.

- Strength 1: Rigorous theoretical framework — formalizes optimization with inexact oracles and provides convergence theorems addressing prior lack of proofs.

- Strength 2: Novel application of optimization techniques — adapts modern inexact-oracle methods to develop a constrained gradient-free method and an adaptive gradient method for this problem.

- Strength 3: Problem significance — learning to rank on graphs is important and the theoretically grounded methods could be impactful for practice.

- Weakness 1: Incomplete main paper — experimental section, tables, and figures are only in the supplement, preventing verification of empirical claims.

- Weakness 2: Unjustified convexity assumption for Theorem 1 — assumes local convexity of the loss in Φ without justification or guidance on how to choose Φ.

- Weakness 3: Ambiguous convergence criterion in Theorem 3 — the provided bound does not clearly imply convergence to a stationary/KKT point or projected-gradient vanishing.

- Weakness 4: Lack of efficiency analysis — experiments report final loss but omit wall-clock time, oracle-call counts, or operation counts; gradient-free method appears slow.

- Question 1: Under what graph/feature conditions does the local convexity assumption for Theorem 1 hold?

- Question 2: How does the stopping criterion in Theorem 3 relate to convergence to a KKT point or standard stationarity measures?

- Question 3: Why were all experimental results placed in the supplement rather than included in the main text?
