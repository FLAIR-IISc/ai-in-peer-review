- Paper tackles learning Supervised PageRank with node/edge features, framing it as a non-convex loss-minimization and proposing gradient-based and gradient-free methods.  
- Main contribution: provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods, addressing inexact oracle issues missing in prior work [21].  
- Extends Nesterov’s inexact-oracle framework to constrained non-convex optimization and adapts a random gradient-free method for constrained settings.  
- Proves convergence rates and analyzes the trade-off between lower-level Markov chain accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Experiments on a web ranking task show both methods outperform prior state-of-the-art [21], with gradient-free method avoiding costly derivative computations.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical guarantees and novel adaptation of methods to the inexact constrained non-convex setting.  
- Strength: practical relevance—empirical improvements in ranking quality and comprehensive complexity-accuracy trade-off analysis.  
- Weakness: no runtime (wall-clock) measurements provided; only arithmetic operation counts reported.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited—single dataset and few baselines.  
- Weakness: implementation details for lower-level Markov chain computations are insufficiently described.  
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous proofs; experiments support theory but lack of runtime weakens practical assessment.  
- Clarity: well-organized and clearly written, though supplementary materials need more detailed convergence proof steps.  
- Originality: novel combination of gradient-based and gradient-free approaches with inexact-oracle concepts applied to Supervised PageRank.  
- Significance: meaningful implications for web ranking systems and potential applicability to other graph-based ranking problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: first provable convergence guarantees for this problem, empirical gains, and a general theoretical framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing runtime evaluation, limited experiments, and need for stronger links to recent related NIPS work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include runtime measurements to strengthen practical evaluation.
