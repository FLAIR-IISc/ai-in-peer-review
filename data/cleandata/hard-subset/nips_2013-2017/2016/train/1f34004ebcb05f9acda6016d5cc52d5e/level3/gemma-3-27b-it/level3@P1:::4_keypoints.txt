- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank by incorporating node and edge features and proposes gradient-based and gradient-free optimization methods for the non-convex loss.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both proposed methods, filling a gap in prior work.  
- Adapts existing optimization techniques to a constrained, inexact-oracle Supervised PageRank setting, offering novelty.  
- Analyzes trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Empirically shows improved web page ranking performance over the state-of-the-art baseline [21].  
- Clearly defines the problem, model, and optimization challenges with generally well-handled notation.  
- Includes rigorous lemmas, theorems, and explicit algorithmic complexity analysis.  
- Paper is dense and hard to follow in Sections 3–5; needs more intuitive explanations of algorithms and theory.  
- Lacks direct runtime (computational time) comparisons with the state-of-the-art, limiting practical impact.  
- Insufficient discussion of parameter sensitivity (e.g., tuning of L in GFN) and justification for chosen values.  
- Experiments are limited to a single dataset, reducing confidence in generalizability across graph types.  
- Limited connection to recent advances in distributed optimization and adaptive learning rates.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, strong theoretical guarantees, and promising empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: readability issues, missing runtime comparison, and narrow experimental scope.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—improve clarity, add runtime comparisons, discuss parameter sensitivity, and broaden experiments.
