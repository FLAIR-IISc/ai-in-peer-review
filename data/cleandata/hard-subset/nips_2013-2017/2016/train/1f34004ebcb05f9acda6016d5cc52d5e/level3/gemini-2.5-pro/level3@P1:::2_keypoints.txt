- Problem: Learning parameters for Supervised PageRank where loss and gradients depend on an intractable stationary distribution of a Markov chain.

- Main contribution: Proposes two inexact-oracle optimization algorithms — a random gradient-free method (for convex problems) and an adaptive gradient-based method (for non-convex problems).

- Theoretical guarantees: Provides convergence rate bounds for both methods and improves on prior work [21] that lacked such guarantees.

- Analysis technique: Bounds the inexact-oracle error by controlling iterations of a power-method-like algorithm used to approximate the stationary distribution.

- Experiments: Empirical comparison against prior gradient-based approach on a web page ranking task.

- Strength — significance/originality: Tackles a practical problem and delivers the first theoretically convergent algorithms for Supervised PageRank.

- Strength — theoretical quality: Strong linkage between low-level stationary distribution estimator accuracy and high-level optimization convergence, with complete complexity bounds.

- Strength — clarity: Paper is generally well-written; problem setup, inexact oracle concept, and algorithms are clearly explained.

- Weakness — experiments/self-containment: Main paper lacks detailed experimental results (e.g., convergence plots, wall-clock time); key figures are in the supplement, hindering assessment of practical trade-offs.

- Weakness — gradient-free theory vs practice: Theorem 1 assumes convexity in a local vicinity, but the actual loss is generally non-convex and the paper gives no guidance on finding such a convex region.

- Weakness — gradient-based convergence criterion: Theorem 3’s criterion ||M_K(x_K - x_{K+1})||^2 being small is not explicitly connected to standard first-order stationarity notions for constrained non-convex optimization.

- Detailed recommendation: Move key supplementary results (loss vs iterations/time, efficiency comparisons) into the main text for self-containment.

- Detailed recommendation: Address or relax the convexity assumption for the gradient-free method, provide evidence of local convexity, or reformulate guarantees for the non-convex case.

- Detailed recommendation: Clarify how the convergence measure ||M_K(x_K - x_{K+1})||^2 relates to ε-approximate stationary points to improve interpretability of the guarantees.

- Overall summary: Strong theoretical contribution with provable methods for Supervised PageRank, but weakened by a practical-theoretical gap for the gradient-free method and an incomplete experimental evaluation.
