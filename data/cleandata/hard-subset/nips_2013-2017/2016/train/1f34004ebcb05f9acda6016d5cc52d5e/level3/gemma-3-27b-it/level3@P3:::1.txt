This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, a non-convex optimization task where node and edge weights are determined by feature-driven random walks. The authors propose both gradient-based and gradient-free methods leveraging an inexact oracle approach due to the difficulty of obtaining exact objective and gradient values. Their main contribution lies in adapting inexact-oracle gradient methods to constrained optimization problems with quantifiable precision in function value computation.

The paper is well-written and clearly presents the problem, proposed solutions, and theoretical analysis. The proofs appear correct, although a complete verification wasn’t possible within the review timeframe. The organization is logical, building from model description to optimization methods and experimental validation. The inclusion of lemmas and deferred proofs enhances readability.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Guarantees:** A significant strength is the provision of convergence rate guarantees for both the gradient-based and gradient-free methods, which is often lacking in similar approaches, particularly in the non-convex setting.
* **Inexact Oracle Handling:** The paper effectively addresses the practical challenge of evaluating the loss function and its gradient in the context of random walks, successfully adapting inexact oracle techniques.
* **Comprehensive Approach:**  The authors offer both gradient-based and gradient-free options, providing flexibility depending on problem characteristics and computational resources.
* **Experimental Validation:** The experiments on a web page ranking task demonstrate the practical effectiveness of the proposed methods, outperforming the state-of-the-art.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The paper includes a detailed complexity analysis for both algorithms, offering insight into computational requirements.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty:** The core optimization ideas bear resemblance to existing work. The adaptation of inexact oracle methods feels akin to Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), and the supervised PageRank framework itself builds upon prior work. The novelty appears to be in the specific *combination* and theoretical analysis within this constrained, feature-driven random walk context, but this isn’t strongly emphasized.
* **Problem Context:** The presentation of the optimization problem feels somewhat isolated. Framing it more broadly within the landscape of constrained non-convex optimization, and explicitly contrasting it with existing approaches *beyond* the immediate supervised PageRank literature, would strengthen the perceived contribution. Is this problem particularly difficult? What makes the inexact oracle setting unique here compared to other constrained problems?
* **Missing Conclusion:** The absence of a concluding section is a minor, but noticeable omission. A conclusion should summarize the key contributions, limitations, and potential future directions.
* **Limited Comparison:** While the paper outperforms the state-of-the-art, a more extensive comparison against alternative optimization techniques *beyond* the specific GBP method would be beneficial.



**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. Claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. It feels like a complete piece of work, though further contextualization would strengthen it. The authors appear careful and honest in evaluating strengths and weaknesses.

**Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The notation is consistent, and the explanations are concise but thorough. 

**Originality:** While the components are not entirely novel, the specific combination and adaptation to this problem domain are a worthwhile contribution.  However, the paper could benefit from a more explicit discussion of how it advances the state-of-the-art beyond incremental improvements.

**Significance:** The results are potentially important. Improving ranking algorithms has practical implications, and the theoretical advancements in inexact oracle optimization could be applicable to other areas.  The paper addresses a challenging problem in a potentially impactful way.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for algorithms in a challenging non-convex setting.
* Offers practical improvements over the state-of-the-art in a real-world application.
* Presents a well-written and technically sound contribution.
* Addresses a common practical problem – dealing with inexact function evaluations.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The core optimization ideas are not entirely novel.
* The paper lacks a broader contextualization of the problem and its contributions within the broader optimization literature.
* Absence of a concluding section.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a solid contribution to the field. While the novelty is incremental, the rigorous theoretical analysis, practical improvements, and clear presentation justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  **Add a conclusion section** summarizing key contributions, limitations, and future work.
2.  **Expand the discussion of novelty** by explicitly positioning the work within the broader literature on constrained non-convex optimization.
3. **Clarify the unique challenges** posed by the feature-driven random walk setting and why existing methods struggle.
