- Proposes learning Supervised PageRank via a constrained non-convex optimization formulation using node/edge features.
- Develops two solvers: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.
- Key theoretical claim: provides convergence rate guarantees for both methods, filling a gap in prior work that lacked such theory.
- Presents a general inexact-oracle optimization framework and applies it to the Supervised PageRank problem.
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and computational complexity, relevant for large-scale settings.
- Extends recent inexact-oracle methods to constrained non-convex problems.
- Major flaw: all experimental results are only in the supplement, leaving the main paper non-self-contained and unverifiable.
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite Eq. (2.4) defining a non-convex problem, creating a theoretical inconsistency without guidance on ensuring Φ is convex.
- Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - x{K+1}) but does not show this implies convergence to a stationary point, weakening optimization guarantees.
- Supplementary Figure 1 indicates slow convergence of the gradient-free method, yet the main text lacks comparative efficiency analysis or discussion.
- The absence of main-text experiments and the theoretical gaps undermine claims of empirical superiority and practical applicability.
- Overall: strong theoretical contributions and motivation, but significant completeness and correctness issues justify rejection for the conference.
