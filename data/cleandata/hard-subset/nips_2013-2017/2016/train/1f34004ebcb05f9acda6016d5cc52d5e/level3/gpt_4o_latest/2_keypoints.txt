- Proposes two methods (gradient-free GFN and gradient-based GBN) for constrained non-convex Supervised PageRank learning, handling inexact oracles.  
- GFN notable for avoiding derivative computations, helpful for large-scale problems.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods, improving over prior work lacking such analysis.  
- Demonstrates practical utility on a real-world web ranking task (Yandex), with both methods outperforming baselines.  
- Explores computational-accuracy trade-offs and uses Nesterov–Nemirovski for approximating stationary distributions.  
- Key experimental results are relegated to the supplementary material, leaving the main text incomplete.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite the objective in Eq.(2.4) being non-convex; the convexity of set Φ is not adequately justified.  
- Theorem 3’s bound on MK(xK - x{K+1}) is unclear about guaranteeing convergence to a stationary point, weakening theoretical claims.  
- Empirical results (supplement) show GFN converges slowly; algorithmic efficiency is not reported clearly or compared to state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is dense and technical with limited clarity; algorithms and theory need more intuitive explanation for broader accessibility.  
- Recommendation for acceptance: significant theoretical and practical contributions relevant to optimization and graph learning.  
- Concerns against acceptance: incomplete main-text experiments, unclear convexity assumptions, and slow GFN convergence.  
- Final decision: recommend acceptance with minor revisions — move key experiments to main text, clarify convexity in Theorem 1, and provide a detailed discussion of algorithm efficiency.
