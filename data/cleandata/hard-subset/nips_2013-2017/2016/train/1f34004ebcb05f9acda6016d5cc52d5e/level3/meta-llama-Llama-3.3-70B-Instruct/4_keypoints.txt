- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm (q-KG) derived from a decision-theoretical perspective.  
- Designed for parallel optimization of expensive-to-evaluate functions.  
- Algorithm can handle noisy function evaluations and learns the noise level from data.  
- Demonstrated to outperform or be competitive with state-of-the-art benchmarks on synthetic tests and practical ML hyperparameter tuning.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Mathematical formulation and computational implementation of q-KG are clearly explained.  
- Experimental results show effectiveness in both noise-free and noisy settings.  
- Lack of discussion and reported measurements on computational cost/runtime of q-KG.  
- Missing runtime data makes it harder to evaluate practical applicability in real-world scenarios.  
- Authors claim originality and significance; q-KG is a notable contribution with potential for broad adoption.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
