- Paper studies learning a feature-parameterized random-walk ranking model (Supervised PageRank) where restart/transition probs are linear in node/edge features and parameters constrained to a Euclidean ball.
- Objective is a pairwise square-loss over query-specific graphs requiring stationary distributions and derivatives that can only be approximated.
- Proposes two two-level algorithms: a biased inexact gradient-free projected method and an adaptive inexact first-order projected method for constrained nonconvex problems.
- Lower-level uses Nesterov–Nemirovski iterative scheme to approximate stationary distributions and their parameter derivatives with linear convergence.
- Derives error bounds for approximate function values and gradients and convergence-rate guarantees for the upper-level methods under oracle inexactness.
- Experiments on a Yandex browsing-graph dataset show reported improvements in ranking loss versus a prior gradient baseline.
- Work is related to Supervised/Personalized PageRank, BrowseRank, supervised random walks, and known inexact-oracle/zero-order optimization literature.
- Novelty is moderate: adapts known optimization tools to constrained biased inexact oracles and quantifies lower-level approximation impact end-to-end.
- Strength: tackles a practical problem with explicit bounds linking lower-level iterations to target accuracy.
- Strength: extends random gradient-free methods to constrained biased inexact cases and gives an adaptive projected gradient method for nonconvex inexact oracles.
- Strength: provides end-to-end complexity bounds and uses a lower-level method with favorable linear convergence.
- Weakness: clarity and presentation suffer from grammatical issues, inconsistent citations, and overreliance on the supplement.
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions (convexity, oracle form) may be strong or unverified for the nonconvex loss.
- Weakness: parameter constraint as a small Euclidean ball around a center may severely limit model expressivity without ablation or alternatives.
- Weakness: experimental reporting is incomplete—missing key parameters, wall-clock times, resource usage, and broader datasets/baselines.
- Weakness: methodological choices (initialization, R selection, stopping criteria, step-size) and statistical significance claims are insufficiently justified.
- Quality: technical approach is sound in spirit but many details, constants, and oracle conditions are deferred or unclearly derived.
- Clarity: manuscript needs substantial editing, better notation hygiene, and relocation of key proofs/intuition into the main text.
- Originality: mainly a combination of known methods; constrained biased inexact-oracle treatment and full complexity accounting are incremental contributions.
- Significance: guarantees could be valuable for large-scale ranking but limited empirical breadth reduces immediate impact.
- Key questions: sensitivity to R and center ϕ̂; convexity/local convexity justification; detailed runtime/arithmetic cost vs baselines.
- Key questions: choice and sensitivity of τ, δ, N, N1, N2; and comparison to additional baselines and standard ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP).
- Pros for acceptance: solid theoretical development, clear upper–lower decomposition, and promising preliminary empirical results.
- Cons for acceptance: poor writing, incomplete citations, strong/unjustified assumptions, and insufficient experimental detail.
- Suggestions: substantially revise writing and references, add ablations and runtime results, broaden empirical evaluation, and justify assumptions or alternative positivity enforcement.
- Recommendation: reject currently; paper has potential but requires clearer exposition and stronger empirical validation.
