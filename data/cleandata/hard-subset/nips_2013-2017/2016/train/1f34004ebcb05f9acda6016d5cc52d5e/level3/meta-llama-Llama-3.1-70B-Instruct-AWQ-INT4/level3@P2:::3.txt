This paper proposes two two-level optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN). The authors provide theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both methods and demonstrate their effectiveness in a real-world ranking task.

The main ideas of the paper are related to previous work on random walk models, such as PageRank and its variants. The authors build upon the framework of Nesterov and Nemirovski's work on iterative methods for Markov chains. The proposed methods are designed to handle non-convex loss-minimization problems and provide convergence rate estimates.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a clear and detailed description of the proposed methods, including the lower and upper levels of the two-level optimization framework.
* The paper includes a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of the proposed methods, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The empirical performance of the proposed methods is reportedly better than the state-of-the-art in ranking quality.
* The authors provide a clear description of the datasets used in the experiments, which should enable reproduction of the results.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The paper uses many symbols, which can make it difficult to follow. A symbol table would be helpful to clarify the notation.
* The experiments only compare the proposed methods with the state-of-the-art gradient-based method (GBP) in terms of loss, but not in terms of ranking quality. A direct comparison with Nesterov and Nemirovski's work, which the paper generalizes, would be useful.
* The authors could provide more insight into the choice of hyperparameters for the proposed methods and how they affect the performance.
* The datasets used in the experiments are not publicly available, which may limit the reproducibility of the results.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear.
