- Proposes gradient-based and random gradient-free methods for non-convex loss-minimization in Supervised PageRank, with convergence rates and complexity bounds.
- Formulates the Supervised PageRank loss-minimization problem and notes prior methods lack theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Gradient-based method approximates the stationary distribution with a linearly convergent algorithm.
- Random gradient-free method estimates gradients via randomization on a Euclidean sphere.
- Both methods are proven to have theoretical convergence and complexity guarantees.
- Experiments on a web-ranking task show the proposed methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline on the loss.
