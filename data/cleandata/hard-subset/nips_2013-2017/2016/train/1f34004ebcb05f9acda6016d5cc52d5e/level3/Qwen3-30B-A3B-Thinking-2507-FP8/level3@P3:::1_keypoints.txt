- Problem: Learns Supervised PageRank with feature-driven random walks using two-level optimization where stationary distributions must be approximated.  
- Contribution 1: Develops a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution to any prescribed precision.  
- Contribution 2: Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods for constrained non-convex problems with inexact oracles and proves convergence to stationary points.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Provides the first theoretical convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank with clear complexity bounds and rigorous proofs.  
- Experiments: Both algorithms outperform prior state-of-the-art [21] on a real ranking task; gradient-based has fewer hyperparameters and gradient-free avoids large-matrix derivatives.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound work with correct analysis, rigorous proofs, and experiments supporting theoretical claims.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized with precise mathematics and a clear two-level framework, but needs a more detailed conclusion and broader problem context.  
- Originality: Application to Supervised PageRank is novel, but core optimization techniques closely resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and novelty is limited.  
- Relation to prior work: Does not sufficiently distinguish its contributions from earlier supervised PageRank methods [21,22].  
- Significance: Addresses an important practical problem and fills a gap by adding guarantees, though impact is tempered by limited methodological novelty.  
- Strengths: First convergence guarantees for this problem, solid theoretical analysis, empirical superiority, and good theory–experiment balance.  
- Weaknesses: Limited novelty of optimization methods, insufficient distinction from prior supervised PageRank, missing conclusion, and weak contextualization relative to state-of-the-art.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with suggestions to add a detailed conclusion, better contextualize the optimization problem, and clearly distinguish contributions from prior work.  
- Venue fit: Well-suited for NIPS due to its motivated application and combination of theoretical and empirical contributions.
