- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods to learn Supervised PageRank models with node and edge features.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both methods, addressing a gap in prior work.  
- Positions contribution as improving over the state-of-the-art [21], which lacked convergence proofs due to lower-level approximation issues.  
- Presents a comprehensive approach offering both optimization paradigms to trade off their advantages.  
- Empirically validates methods on a web ranking task and shows improved performance over baseline [21].  
- Supplies detailed analysis with lemmas and proofs for approximation accuracy and complexity bounds.  
- Complexity is analyzed in arithmetic operations, but lacks direct runtime (seconds/minutes) comparisons to baseline.  
- Parameter sensitivity (e.g., choice of Lipschitz constant L) is only briefly discussed and needs more guidance and plots in the main text.  
- Notation and dense equations can impede readability; clearer and more consistent notation is recommended.  
- Experimental comparisons are limited primarily to [21]; broader evaluation against other ranking algorithms is missing.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound contribution with convergence guarantees and strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing runtime comparisons, insufficient parameter sensitivity analysis, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with rigorous theory and practical validation; weaknesses are relatively minor.  
- Recommendation: accept (strongly consider), contingent on adding runtime comparisons and expanded parameter sensitivity discussion.
