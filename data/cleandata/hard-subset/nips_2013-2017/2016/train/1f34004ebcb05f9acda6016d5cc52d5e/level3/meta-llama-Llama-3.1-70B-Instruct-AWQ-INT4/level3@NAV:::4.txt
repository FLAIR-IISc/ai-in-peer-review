The paper "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods" proposes two novel optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models. The authors introduce a non-convex loss-minimization problem and develop a gradient-based and a random gradient-free method to solve it. They provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rate of both methods and demonstrate their effectiveness through experiments on a real-world ranking task.

**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges in a graph. The authors propose two optimization methods: a gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method. Both methods are designed to solve a non-convex loss-minimization problem, and they provide theoretical guarantees on their convergence rates.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on PageRank and Supervised PageRank models. The authors mention that existing optimization methods, such as the state-of-the-art gradient-based method, do not provide convergence guarantees for non-convex problems. They also note that standard global optimization methods cannot be applied due to the presence of constraints and the impossibility of calculating the exact value of the loss function.
