

This paper addresses the challenging problem of learning Supervised PageRank models with non-convex optimization, where node/edge weights are defined by feature-driven random walks. The authors propose a two-level optimization framework to overcome the inapplicability of existing methods, which require exact objective values. The first level employs a linearly convergent method to estimate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, while the second level develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle. The main technical contribution lies in adapting inexact-oracle gradient approaches to constrained problems where function values are computable up to known precision.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality with correct proofs (though not all verified in detail) and provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods. The experimental results on a web ranking task show significant improvements over the state-of-the-art method from [21], with the gradient-based method particularly effective. The writing is clear and well-organized, making the technical contributions accessible.

However, the novelty claim is somewhat weak. The core optimization ideas closely resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and the supervised PageRank framework has prior work. The authors would strengthen their contribution by better contextualizing their work within the existing literature on inexact oracle methods for constrained optimization. Additionally, the paper lacks a conclusion section, and would benefit from presenting the optimization problem in a more general context to clarify how it differs from state-of-the-art.

The significance of this work is moderate, as it provides theoretically grounded methods for an important ranking problem, but the incremental nature of the optimization contributions may limit its broader impact. The paper satisfies quality standards with sound theoretical analysis and empirical validation, and demonstrates good clarity in presenting the technical content.

Overall, this is a technically sound paper with clear contributions to the specific problem of learning Supervised PageRank models. While the novelty is somewhat limited by resemblance to prior work on inexact oracles, the theoretical guarantees and experimental improvements over existing methods provide value to the community. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the novelty concerns and add a conclusion section.
