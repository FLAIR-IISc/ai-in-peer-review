- Paper tackles supervised PageRank learning with parametric node/edge features and minimizes a non-convex pairwise square-hinge loss under an ℓ2-ball constraint.
- Main methodological contribution: two-level approach using Nesterov–Nemirovski (NN) for lower-level PageRank and derivative approximation, replacing the power method.
- Upper-level optimizers: adaptive projected gradient method for non-convex composite objectives with an inexact first-order oracle, and a randomized gradient-free method for constrained problems with biased/inexact evaluations.
- Provides convergence rate guarantees and complexity bounds for both gradient-based and gradient-free variants via an inexact oracle framework.
- Empirical results on a Yandex browsing graph (600 queries) show lower training loss than previous power-method-based two-level gradient approach.
- Relates to supervised random walks/PageRank training, PageRank solvers (power vs NN), zeroth-order and non-convex optimization, and inexact-oracle convex optimization literature.
- Novel extension: gradient-free optimization analysis for constrained problems with biased, inexact function values.
- The adaptive projected gradient method generalizes composite gradient methods to non-convex objectives with unknown Lipschitz constants and provides practical backtracking and stationarity guarantees.
- Zeroth-order analysis controls bias from inexact function values and gives finite-time bounds under convexity on Φ.
- Gradient-free method’s guarantees assume convexity of f on Φ, tempering its non-convex claim.
- Both methods rely on accurate control of lower-level NN approximation errors (parameters N, N1, N2), which is well motivated.
- Experiments focus on training objective; missing standard learning-to-rank metrics (e.g., NDCG/MAP/ERR@k) on held-out data to validate ranking quality.
- Empirical comparison lacks wall-clock runtimes, iteration counts at both levels, and memory usage despite theoretical complexity emphasis.
- Reproducibility: many details in supplement but key constants (e.g., β1) and full algorithms are deferred; a main-text pseudocode and symbol table would help.
- Clarity issues: dense presentation, notational overload, minor typos, and reliance on supplement impede readability.
- Suggested clarity improvements: include main-text pseudocode/flow diagram, intuition for NN vs power method, and clarify dependence on α, sparsity s, and feature dimensionality.
- Pros: clean two-level design with provable rates, both gradient and gradient-free options, solid complexity accounting, and empirical improvements over prior baseline.
- Cons: gradient-free theory requires convexity, missing runtime/hardware/per-iteration cost reporting, evaluation limited to loss and lacks broader baselines, and heavy supplement dependence for key details.
- Requests for authors: report runtimes, iteration counts, average NN iterations per step, hardware/implementation, loss vs time and NDCG vs time plots.
- Requests: provide NDCG@k/MAP on test sets with significance, sensitivity analyses for ε/L0/α/R, justify GBP configuration and step sizes, and clarify computation/magnitude of β1 and memory cost of derivative computations.
- Recommendation: strong paper with meaningful theoretical and practical contributions; lean toward acceptance conditional on adding comprehensive runtime comparisons and standard ranking evaluations.
