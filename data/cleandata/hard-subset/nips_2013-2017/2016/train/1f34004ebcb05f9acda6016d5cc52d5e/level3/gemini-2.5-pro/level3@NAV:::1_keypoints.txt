- Problem: learning parameters for a non-convex, feature-driven Supervised PageRank model where the objective depends on an intractable stationary distribution.  
- Core challenge: the stationary distribution (and thus the objective) cannot be computed exactly, making standard optimization inapplicable.  
- Method: two-level algorithm — lower level estimates the stationary distribution with a linearly convergent method.  
- Oracle: lower-level estimator yields an "inexact oracle" allowing function values to be approximated to any prescribed precision.  
- Upper level: gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization using the inexact oracle.  
- Theoretical contribution: rigorous convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank, absent in prior work.  
- Technical quality: clear writing and sound proofs that adapt inexact-oracle methods to constrained problems.  
- Relation to prior work: closely related to existing inexact-oracle frameworks (e.g., Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and prior Supervised PageRank formulations.  
- Originality concern: novelty mainly in handling constraints; fundamental optimization ideas are similar to existing frameworks.  
- Suggested improvement: better contextualize the constrained adaptation within the broader inexact-optimization literature to emphasize novelty.  
- Minor issues: missing a dedicated conclusion section and recommendation to present the optimization problem in a general context before the PageRank specialization.
