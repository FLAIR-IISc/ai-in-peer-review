This paper proposes gradient-based and random gradient-free methods to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem of learning Supervised PageRank models. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed methods, including their convergence rates and complexity bounds.

The paper starts by introducing the concept of Supervised PageRank, which is a general ranking framework that allows for the incorporation of node and edge features into the ranking process. The authors then formulate the loss-minimization problem, which involves finding the optimal parameters for the ranking model. They note that the existing optimization method for this problem has no theoretical guarantees on convergence rate.

The authors propose two new methods to solve the loss-minimization problem: a gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method. The gradient-based method uses a linearly convergent algorithm to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, while the gradient-free method uses a randomization on a Euclidean sphere to estimate the gradient of the loss function. Both methods are shown to have theoretical guarantees on convergence rate and complexity bounds.

The paper also presents experimental results on a web page ranking problem, where the proposed methods are compared to the state-of-the-art gradient-based method. The results show that the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art method in terms of the loss function.
