- Problem: Learning parameters for a non-convex, feature-driven Supervised PageRank where the loss depends on an intractable stationary distribution.
- Method: Two-level approach—iterative linearly-convergent lower-level method to approximate the stationary distribution and an upper-level gradient-based algorithm using an inexact oracle.
- Theoretical result: Proof that the upper-level algorithm converges to a stationary point with controllable oracle precision.
- Strength (technical): Strong, rigorous technical analysis addressing a practical, computationally intractable objective.
- Strength (significance): Relevant to ranking and optimization communities; framework applicable to other problems with estimable objectives.
- Strength (clarity): Well-written and organized presentation of the two-level structure and theoretical results.
- Weakness (originality): Core optimization ideas resemble prior work (e.g., Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and Supervised PageRank is not new; contribution is mainly an adaptation to constrained settings.
- Weakness (framing): Paper overly focused on Supervised PageRank; should present the general constrained inexact-oracle problem first to better highlight broader contributions.
- Weakness (minor): Missing a conclusion section summarizing contributions and future directions.
