## Review of "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods"

This paper tackles the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, extending the standard PageRank approach to incorporate node and edge features. The authors formulate this as a constrained non-convex optimization problem and propose both gradient-based and gradient-free methods for solving it, leveraging the concept of inexact oracles to address the computational challenges. The theoretical contributions include convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods, a notable advancement given the lack of such guarantees in previous work [21]. The core idea of using inexact oracles and analyzing their impact on convergence is a sensible approach for this type of problem.

The paper builds upon the foundational work of PageRank [18] and subsequent extensions [8, 9, 19] by incorporating feature information, as seen in related approaches [3, 4, 6, 10, 12, 20, 21]. The contributions appear novel in the specific combination of inexact oracle optimization techniques applied to Supervised PageRank, and the theoretical analysis provided is a significant step forward. However, a major weakness severely hinders evaluation: **all experimental results are relegated to the supplementary material.** This is unacceptable for a NIPS submission, as it prevents a clear assessment of the practical performance and effectiveness of the proposed methods.

Specifically,

* **Detailed Comment 1:** The experimental section *must* be included in the main text. The paper currently feels incomplete without a thorough presentation and discussion of the results. A summary of key findings, along with illustrative figures, is crucial for demonstrating the impact of this work.
* **Detailed Comment 2:** The assumption of convexity in Theorem 1 seems at odds with Equation (2.4), which explicitly defines a non-convex loss function. The authors need to clearly explain how the choice of Φ guarantees the required convexity for the theorem to hold, or revise the theorem accordingly.
* **Detailed Comment 3:** Theorem 3 provides a bound on MK(xK - xK+1), but it's unclear whether this is sufficient to guarantee convergence of xK to a stationary point. A clearer connection between this bound and convergence criteria is necessary.
* **Detailed Comment 4:** The supplementary figure 1 suggests that the GFNM method converges slowly. The authors should discuss this in the main text, report algorithmic efficiency (e.g., runtime, number of iterations) in comparison to GBP [21], and analyze the trade-offs between accuracy and computational cost.

**Arguments for acceptance:** The theoretical contributions are solid, and the problem addressed is relevant. The use of inexact oracles is a practical consideration for large-scale graph problems.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The lack of results in the main paper is a critical flaw. The unclear assumptions regarding convexity and the insufficient explanation of convergence criteria also detract from the paper’s quality.

**Overall:** While the paper presents a potentially valuable contribution to the field, the current state of the manuscript is insufficient for acceptance. The authors *must* incorporate the experimental results into the main text and address the theoretical concerns raised above. I recommend **rejection** with the strong encouragement to resubmit after these revisions.
