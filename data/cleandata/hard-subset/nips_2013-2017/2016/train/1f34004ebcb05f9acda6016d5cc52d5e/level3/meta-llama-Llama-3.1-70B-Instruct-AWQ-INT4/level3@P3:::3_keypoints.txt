- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN), with theoretical convergence rate guarantees.
- Methods rely on an inexact oracle to approximate the objective and derivatives, with controllable approximation accuracy.
- Empirical results on a real-world ranking task show GBN and GFN outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based method (GBP) in ranking quality.
- Experimental comparison also indicates GBN and GFN require fewer iterations and less computational time than GBP.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation, making the methods and experiments easy to follow.
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis, including convergence guarantees and computational complexity estimates.
- Strength: combination of theoretical guarantees and strong empirical performance in a real-world setting.
- Weakness: lacks a direct comparison with the Nesterov and Nemirovski method (ref. 17), which underpins the two-level framework.
- Weakness: does not analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters, which may affect practical applicability.
- Originality: novel integration of gradient-based and gradient-free optimization for supervised PageRank and novel use of an inexact oracle in this context.
- Significance: potential to improve web search, recommendation systems, and social network analysis via better ranking quality.
- Recommendation: accept; reviewer scores — Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
