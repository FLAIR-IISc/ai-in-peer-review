- Addresses learning parameters for feature-based Supervised PageRank and critiques prior method [21] for lacking convergence guarantees.
- Main contribution: two new optimization algorithms (gradient-based and gradient-free) with formal convergence rate guarantees.
- Methods use a two-level scheme: efficient iterative approximation of the stationary distribution (inexact oracle) and adaptive projected or gradient-free updates.
- Strong theoretical rigor with explicit convergence rates and complexity bounds (Theorems 1–4).
- Sound formulation connecting inexact-oracle analysis to modern optimization literature.
- Incomplete presentation: proofs and detailed experiments are relegated to the supplement, making the paper feel not self-contained.
- Weak empirical validation: experiments are cursory, lack hyperparameter justification, and give limited details for reproducibility.
- Critical modeling choice (constrained search space Φ as a ball around ϕ̂) is unexplained and potentially restrictive.
- Unrealistic assumption: gradient-free analysis assumes local convexity, which is strong for an inherently non-convex problem.
- Clarity issues: frequent grammatical errors, sloppy citations (e.g., misnaming [21]), and poor organization relying heavily on appendices.
- Limited originality: problem setup follows [21] and algorithms adapt existing techniques, though the convergence analysis is novel.
- Practical significance uncertain: evaluation on a single commercial dataset, reporting only loss without standard ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP).
- Arguments for acceptance: first theoretical convergence guarantees for this model and solid analysis of constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak, non-reproducible experiments; poor writing and presentation; and unjustified modeling choices undermining practical applicability.
