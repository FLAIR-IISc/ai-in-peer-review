- Paper tackles learning Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem with node and edge features.
- Proposes two two-level methods: a gradient-free approach with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based approach with an inexact first-order oracle.
- Provides theoretical convergence-rate guarantees and complexity bounds for both methods, extending Nesterov & Spokoiny to constrained settings.
- Builds on prior supervised ranking and Markov chain work but improves on state-of-the-art by addressing convergence limitations.
- Experimental results (in supplement) claim both methods outperform prior work [21] in ranking quality.
- Major weakness: all experimental results are relegated to the supplement, undermining empirical validation in the main paper.
- Critical inconsistency: Theorem 1 assumes convexity while the objective (2.4) is explicitly non-convex; convexity on feasible set Φ needs justification or theorem revision.
- Ambiguity: Theorem 3 bounds ||MK(xK - xK+1)||², but it’s unclear whether this implies convergence to a stationary point for the non-convex problem.
- Empirical efficiency concern: supplementary Figure 1 shows the gradient-free method (GFN) converges slower than the gradient-based method (GBN); authors should discuss this trade-off.
- Missing comparison: need computational complexity comparisons between GFN, GBN, and prior method [21] to guide practitioners.
- Writing and structure are strong, with rigorous theoretical analysis and useful complexity bounds.
- Originality is solid: combining gradient-free/gradient methods with inexact oracles for Supervised PageRank is novel.
- Significance is promising for web ranking systems but reduced by lack of visible experimental validation.
- Recommendation: reject as submitted; authors should include key experimental results in the main text, resolve the convexity assumption, clarify the stationarity implication of Theorem 3, and discuss efficiency/computational trade-offs.
