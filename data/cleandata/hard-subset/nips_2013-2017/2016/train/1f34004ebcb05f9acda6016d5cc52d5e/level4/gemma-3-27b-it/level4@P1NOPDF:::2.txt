This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained, non-convex optimization problem, leveraging node and edge properties. The authors propose two approaches to solve this problem: a gradient-free method utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle, and a gradient-based method employing an inexact first-order oracle. My primary concern is the omission of experimental results from the main text, rendering the paper incomplete. Detailed comments follow.

1. The inclusion of experimental results solely in the supplementary material significantly detracts from the completeness of the main text.

2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the function in (2.4) is demonstrably non-convex. How can the set $\Phi$ be chosen to ensure the convexity of the objective function? Clarification on this point is crucial.

3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. Does this bound guarantee the convergence of the sequence $x_k$ to a stationary point? A discussion of convergence guarantees would strengthen the theoretical contribution.

4. As shown in Figure 1 of the supplementary material, GFNM exhibits slow convergence. The authors should also report the computational efficiency of both algorithms, perhaps with runtime comparisons, to provide a more comprehensive evaluation.
