

The paper proposes two optimization methods—gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN)—for solving the supervised PageRank problem. Unlike existing approaches, the GBN method provides a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate for achieving a specified accuracy, while the GFN method ensures a guaranteed decrease in the loss function. Both methods avoid requiring exact evaluations of the objective function and provide convergence rate estimates, demonstrating superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art baselines. Hyperparameters are explicitly provided, and while the datasets are not publicly available, the experimental description is sufficiently clear to enable reproducibility. The two-level optimization framework builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref:17), with the lower and upper levels designed to avoid explicit large-matrix computations while maintaining strong performance. The technical details, including all proofs, are comprehensively covered in Sections 2–5 and the supplementary material.  

However, several improvements would strengthen the paper. First, a symbol table would significantly enhance readability, as the current notation becomes difficult to track (e.g., the symbol $N$ in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 denotes the number of steps but is only clarified in Line 193). Second, the experimental comparison focuses solely on loss function values against GBP, but ranking quality—a primary metric for supervised PageRank—should be the basis for comparison. Finally, since the work generalizes ref:17, direct performance comparisons with this baseline would clarify the relative gains of the proposed methods. Addressing these points would substantially improve the paper’s clarity and impact.
