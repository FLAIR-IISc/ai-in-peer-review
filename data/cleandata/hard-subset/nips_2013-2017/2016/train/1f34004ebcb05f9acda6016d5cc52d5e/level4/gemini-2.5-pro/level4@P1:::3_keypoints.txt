- Proposes two-level optimization (GBN and GFN) for non-convex Supervised PageRank that operate with an inexact oracle, enabling scalability to large graphs.
- Gives rigorous convergence-rate guarantees for both algorithms, addressing a gap left by prior work [21].
- Uses the Nesterov–Nemirovski method for the lower-level oracle to approximate PageRank stationary distribution and derivatives efficiently.
- Empirically outperforms the state-of-the-art baseline in final loss on a real-world web page ranking task, with GFN performing especially well.
- Experimental setup and hyperparameter choices are clearly described, aiding reproducibility despite a proprietary dataset.
- Paper is mathematically dense with heavy notation, which reduces readability.
- Strongly recommend adding a centralized table of notation defining major symbols (e.g., N, N1, N2, δ, ε, M, K).
- Recommend defining symbols at their first appearance (e.g., N is used in Eq. 3.1 before being explained).
- Experiments focus on loss minimization but should also report standard IR metrics (NDCG, MAP) to validate actual ranking quality improvements.
- Recommend isolating the impact of the lower-level oracle by comparing GBN using Nesterov–Nemirovski versus a GBN variant using the power method.
