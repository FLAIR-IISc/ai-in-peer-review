- Mislabels the task as "Supervised PageRank" and inconsistently cites prior work ([21]), effectively reusing [21]’s concepts under new notation without justification.

- Adapts gradient-free optimization to a biased oracle and proves theoretical bounds, but bases analysis on the strong assumption that φ remains in an R-ball around a fixed φ₀.

- Fails to justify or empirically validate the R-ball φ₀ assumption (how φ₀ is chosen, whether R is realistic), putting the applicability of the theoretical guarantees in doubt.

- Relies on the appendix for all substantive experimental results; the main text contains no tables, figures, or concrete metrics.

- Claims of outperforming baselines are unquantified and lack context (no performance margins, metrics, or graph types reported).

- Critical experimental details (φ₀ and R values, number of graphs, dataset diversity) are missing, making replication impossible.

- Evaluation appears to use only a single web graph, which is insufficient to support generalization or scalability claims.

- Manuscript has numerous grammatical errors, inconsistent terminology (e.g., "constriction"), and poorly executed citations that undermine credibility.

- Theoretical section lacks clarity and rigor: proof sketches are omitted and links between assumptions and empirical feasibility are underdeveloped.

- Does not explain why a gradient-free method is necessary versus standard optimization methods for this problem.

- Problem has moderate interest, but practical significance is unproven due to lack of empirical evidence and restrictive assumptions.

- Recommendation: move critical empirical results and parameter choices to the main text or substantially revise the problem framing to justify assumptions; as written, the paper should be rejected.
