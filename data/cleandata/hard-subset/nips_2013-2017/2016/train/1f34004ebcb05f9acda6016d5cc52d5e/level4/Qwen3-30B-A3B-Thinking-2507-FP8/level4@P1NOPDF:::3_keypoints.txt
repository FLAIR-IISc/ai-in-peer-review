- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).  
- GBN provides a theoretical convergence rate guarantee for achieving a specified accuracy.  
- GFN ensures a monotonic decrease in the loss function.  
- Both methods avoid exact objective evaluations, using estimates to enable efficient computation without large-matrix operations.  
- Empirical results claim superior ranking quality over state-of-the-art, with hyperparameters clearly specified.  
- Datasets are not public, but experimental descriptions are detailed enough to facilitate replication.  
- Theoretical development builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski (Ref. 17), structuring lower- and upper-level optimizations accordingly.  
- Convergence-rate analysis includes precise bounds and comprehensive proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Presentation issues: heavy symbol usage and undefined symbols (e.g., N) create confusion; a symbol table is recommended.  
- Experimental weakness: comparisons use only loss values against GBP and omit direct evaluation of ranking quality—the primary metric.  
- Missing direct comparison with Ref. 17 leaves unclear whether the methods improve on the foundational approach.  
- Overall: solid technical contributions and promising results, but presentation clarity and evaluation (ranking metrics and baseline comparison) need strengthening.
