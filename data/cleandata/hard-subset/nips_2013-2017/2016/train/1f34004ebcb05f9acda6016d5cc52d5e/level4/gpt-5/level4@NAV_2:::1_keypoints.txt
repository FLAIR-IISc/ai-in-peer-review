- Studies convergence for nonconvex loss-minimization to learn parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by a feature-dependent random walk.  
- Exact objective evaluations are inapplicable, so the authors propose a two-level algorithmic approach.  
- Lower level: use a linearly convergent procedure to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution and show the loss can be approximated to arbitrary accuracy.  
- Upper level: design a gradient method for constrained, nonconvex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adapt and prove convergence of this inexact-oracle framework for constrained settings where function values are known up to a tolerance.  
- Paper is clearly written and proofs appear sound (not all details verified).  
- Main reservation: core optimization components largely adapt prior work (e.g., Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and the supervised PageRank framework is not novel.  
- Minor suggestions: add a dedicated conclusion section and present the optimization task in a broader, problem-agnostic formulation to clarify contributions.
