- Addresses learning parameters for Supervised PageRank by incorporating node/edge features into PageRank via linear parameterization.
- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with theoretical convergence guarantees absent in prior work [21].
- Gradient-based method: convergence guarantees for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle.
- Gradient-free method: adaptation to constrained optimization with function values computed to known accuracy.
- Provides theoretical analysis and complexity bounds for both methods, including trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall complexity.
- Empirical evaluation shows improvements over the state-of-the-art on a real web ranking task.
- Quality: technically sound methods and rigorous theoretical development, but key experimental details are relegated to the appendix.
- Clarity: writing suffers from grammatical errors, inconsistent notation, and dense, disorganized presentation of mathematical framework and algorithms.
- Originality: extends gradient-free methods to constrained inexact-oracle settings and applies two-level optimization to Supervised PageRank with novel convergence analysis.
- Significance: relevant for web ranking applications, but practical implications and real-world impact are not convincingly demonstrated or quantified.
- Strengths: theoretical convergence guarantees, careful complexity analysis, empirical improvements, and attention to lower-level loss/gradient computation challenges.
- Weaknesses: insufficient main-paper detail, ambiguous presentation of key parameters (e.g., Φ choice), unclear practical implications, grammatical/notation issues, and unclear effect of technical assumptions on applicability.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant theoretical contributions and empirical gains on a real dataset strengthen the optimization and Supervised PageRank literature.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor presentation forcing reliance on appendix, weak articulation of practical significance, and overall poor writing quality.
- Recommendation: reject pending substantial revision to improve clarity, move critical experimental details into the main text, and better justify practical relevance.
