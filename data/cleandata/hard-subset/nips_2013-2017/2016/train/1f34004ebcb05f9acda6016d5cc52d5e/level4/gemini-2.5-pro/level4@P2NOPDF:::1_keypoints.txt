- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem in a graph-based ranking model defined by a feature-parameterized random walk.  
- Key challenge: objective depends on the random walk's stationary distribution, which cannot be computed exactly, ruling out standard exact-value optimization methods.  
- Proposes a two-level approach: first, a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution and estimate the loss to arbitrary precision.  
- Second level: a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization that works with an inexact oracle.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proof of convergence to a stationary point when the objective is evaluated with known, controllable precision.  
- Applies the proposed method to optimize the ranking model's parameters.  
- Writing and proofs are clear and appear sound (not exhaustively verified).  
- Primary concern: novelty—techniques resemble adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and the supervised PageRank model is based on prior work.  
- Suggestion 1: Generalize the problem formulation to frame the ranking algorithm as an instance of a broader class and clarify relations to inexact-oracle literature.  
- Suggestion 2: Add a conclusion section to summarize results, highlight contributions, and propose future work.
