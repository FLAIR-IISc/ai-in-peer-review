- Studies convergence for learning parameters of a non-convex graph-based ranking model defined by a random walk whose transition weights depend on node/edge features.  
- Model’s transition weights are themselves determined by random walks over node and edge features.  
- Optimization cannot use existing methods that require exact objective evaluations.  
- Proposes a two-stage approach: first, approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk using a linearly convergent method.  
- Shows the stationary-distribution approximation yields loss values accurate to any prescribed precision.  
- Develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution is adapting convergence analysis to the constrained setting where function values are computed only within known precision.  
- Paper is well written and proofs appear correct (not fully checked in detail).  
- Concern: core optimization components are adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and supervised PageRank has prior work.  
- Minor issue: missing conclusion section.  
- Suggestion: present the optimization problem in a more general framework and position the proposed algorithm as a special case to clarify novelty.
