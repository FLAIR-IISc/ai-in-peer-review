- Addresses supervised PageRank parameter learning as a non-convex loss minimization problem with practical importance.  
- Proposes two optimization approaches: a gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method.  
- Gradient-free method presented as a useful alternative when full gradient computation is expensive.  
- Dual-method proposal offers flexibility for different computational constraints and problem structures.  
- Paper is generally well-written with a clear problem formulation and motivations.  
- Major weakness: experimental evaluation omits computational cost and runtime analysis.  
- Lack of wall-clock or time-per-iteration comparisons makes practical viability unclear, especially for large-scale use.  
- The key claim that the gradient-free method helps when gradients are costly is not empirically substantiated.  
- Suggested improvements: report training time to converge, compare time per iteration between methods, and include runtime comparisons against relevant baselines.  
- A comprehensive evaluation should demonstrate both accuracy and computational efficiency to clarify trade-offs.
