- Proposes two optimization methods for Supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method with convergence-rate guarantees and prescribed-accuracy attainment, and a gradient-free method that certifies loss decrease.  
- Both methods work in an inexact-oracle setting (no exact objective values), provide convergence-rate estimates, and empirically outperform prior state of the art on ranking quality.  
- Hyperparameters for both methods are specified; datasets are not public but sufficiently described to feasibly reproduce results.  
- Methods are two-level procedures building on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref. 17), avoiding expensive large-matrix computations while maintaining strong performance.  
- Sections 2–5 clearly present technical details with proofs in the supplement, but a notation table is recommended due to many symbols (e.g., N is introduced in Eq. 3.1 without immediate clarification).  
- Experiments emphasize loss comparisons rather than ranking metrics, and the paper lacks a direct empirical comparison against ref. 17, which it generalizes.
