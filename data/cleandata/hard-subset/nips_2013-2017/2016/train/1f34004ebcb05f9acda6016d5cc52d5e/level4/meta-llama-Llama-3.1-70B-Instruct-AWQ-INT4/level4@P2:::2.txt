This paper addresses the Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem: a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.

The paper relates to previous work on PageRank, HITS, and their variants, as well as supervised ranking frameworks. The authors also mention the limitations of existing methods, such as the inability to account for features of nodes and edges. The proposed methods aim to address these limitations by incorporating these features into the optimization problem.

The strengths of the paper include the development of two novel optimization methods for the Supervised PageRank problem. The gradient-free method is based on a general framework for random gradient-free methods with inexact oracle, while the gradient-based method is based on a general framework for gradient methods with inexact first-order oracle. The authors provide convergence rate analysis for both methods and demonstrate their effectiveness on a real-world ranking task.

However, there are some weaknesses and concerns. Firstly, the experiment results are not included in the main text, which makes the paper incomplete. The authors should consider moving the experiment results to the main text to make the paper more self-contained.

Secondly, the convexity assumption in Theorem 1 is not justified for the non-convex function in (2.4). The authors should provide more discussion on how to choose the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function.

Thirdly, Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$, but it is not clear if this bound ensures that the sequence $xk$ converges to a stationary point. The authors should provide more discussion on the convergence of the algorithm.

Lastly, the efficiency of the algorithms is not reported. By Figure 1 in the supplement, GFNM converges slowly. The authors may want to report the efficiency of the algorithms in terms of the number of iterations and computational time.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes two novel optimization methods for the Supervised PageRank problem.
* The authors provide convergence rate analysis for both methods.
* The experiment results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods on a real-world ranking task.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The experiment results are not included in the main text.
* The convexity assumption in Theorem 1 is not justified for the non-convex function in (2.4).
* The convergence of the algorithm is not clear.
* The efficiency of the algorithms is not reported.

Overall, the paper has some strengths, but also some weaknesses and concerns that need to be addressed. With some revisions to address these concerns, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
