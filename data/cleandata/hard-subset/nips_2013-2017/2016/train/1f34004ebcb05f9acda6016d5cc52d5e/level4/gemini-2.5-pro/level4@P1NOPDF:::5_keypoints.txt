- Introduces a supervised PageRank framework with feature-parameterized node/edge weights and a new algorithm combining stationary-distribution approximation with gradient-free optimization, building on prior work.  
- Provides theoretical contributions: lemmas bounding approximate gradients and a theorem on a biased gradient-free oracle, yielding performance guarantees under technical assumptions.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and strong bounds for the proposed gradient-free optimization method.  
- Major issue: main paper is not self-contained—essential proofs and full empirical results are relegated to the appendix with no proof sketches or experimental summaries in the body.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: claims of outperforming baselines lack quantitative metrics, practical interpretation, and key hyperparameter reporting (e.g., phi_0, R).  
- Experiments appear limited to a single graph, raising concerns about generalizability across diverse graph types.  
- Practicality concern: technical assumptions (notably the R-ball constraint around a pre-selected phi_0) may be unrealistic and could severely restrict applicability; their impact isn’t discussed.  
- Clarity and scholarship issues: numerous grammatical errors and possible miscitation (claiming supervised PageRank while cited work addresses semi-supervised variants).  
- Impact is not well motivated; paper fails to justify the practical importance or applications of this supervised PageRank formulation.  
- Requests for authors: include main experimental table/figure in the main paper and discuss magnitude/practical significance of improvements.  
- Requests for authors: report how phi_0 and R were chosen and analyze sensitivity to these hyperparameters.  
- Requests for authors: clarify whether multiple graphs were evaluated and comment on expected generalizability.  
- Requests for authors: provide intuition/justification for technical assumptions and discuss limitations, especially the R-ball constraint.  
- Conclusion: promising theoretical work but not convincing due to hidden core evidence, vague experiments, and questionable assumptions; reviewer cannot recommend acceptance in current form.
