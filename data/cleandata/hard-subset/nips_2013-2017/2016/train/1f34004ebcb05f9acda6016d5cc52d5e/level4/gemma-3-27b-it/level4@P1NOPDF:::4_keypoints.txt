- Missing runtime/performance reporting for the proposed optimization methods.  
- Clear motivation and well-defined problem of learning supervised PageRank models.  
- Lack of detailed experimental comparison to existing state-of-the-art embedding methods.  
- Main contribution: gradient-based and gradient-free optimization algorithms for a non-convex loss.  
- Theoretical analysis of gradient-based method is useful but required assumptions (smoothness, step size) need practical discussion.  
- Unclear if convergence assumptions hold for real-world network datasets.  
- Gradient-free method interesting but lacks justification for the chosen random perturbation strategy.  
- No comparison of the gradient-free approach to other random search or evolutionary algorithms.  
- Experiments are limited: synthetic data may not reflect real-world complexity and Cora comparison uses few baselines.  
- Parameter tuning details and reproducibility information are insufficient.  
- Should compare against established embedding baselines (node2vec, DeepWalk, LINE).  
- Reported AUC improvements are hard to assess without broader benchmarking.  
- Writing quality and notation are inconsistent and need clarity; Section 3 and loss discussion are awkward.  
- Minor issues: weak figure captions, missing discussion of computational complexity, and unclear term “random-gradient-free.”  
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage revision with more experiments, clearer assumptions, algorithmic details, and comparisons.
