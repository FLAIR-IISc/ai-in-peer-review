- Formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained, non-convex optimization that incorporates node and edge features.  
- Proposes two solvers: a gradient-free method using an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method using an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Major weakness: all experimental results are relegated to the supplement, leaving the main paper without empirical validation.  
- Reviewer requests a concise experimental section in the main text: datasets, features, baselines, metrics, key quantitative results, plots, and computational costs.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the objective (Eq. 2.4) is non-convex—need clarification on when/if the objective becomes convex.  
- Must specify how the parameter set Φ should be chosen or restricted to satisfy convexity, or explicitly separate results for convex vs non-convex regimes.  
- Theorem 3 bounds M_K(x^K − x^{K+1}); reviewer asks whether this implies convergence of {x^k} or convergence to a stationary point, and in what sense.  
- If convergence to stationarity is claimed, additional assumptions (e.g., Lipschitz continuity, Kurdyka–Łojasiewicz property, bounded oracle variance) should be stated or proven.  
- Practical performance concerns: GFNM appears to converge slowly; report wall-clock time, iteration counts, oracle calls, hyperparameter sensitivity, and performance-vs-time trade-offs.  
- Compare proposed methods to standard learning-to-rank baselines on graphs and present those comparisons in the main text.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, principled integration of node/edge features, treatment of inexact oracles, and formal iteration analysis.  
- Weaknesses: lack of in-text empirical validation, unclear convexity assumptions vs non-convex objective, limited discussion/proofs of stationarity in non-convex case, and insufficient efficiency reporting.  
- Recommended fixes: move key experiments into the main paper, clarify the role of Φ for Theorem 1, strengthen or circumscribe non-convex convergence claims, and provide comprehensive efficiency metrics and explanations for GFNM’s slowness.
