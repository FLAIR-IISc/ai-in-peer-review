- Paper tackles learning parameters for Supervised PageRank under an inexact oracle where loss and gradients are approximate.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization algorithms: a gradient-based method and a random gradient-free method.  
- Provides the first theoretical convergence rate guarantees for Supervised PageRank and complexity bounds linking upper-level convergence with lower-level approximation accuracy.  
- Experiments on a large-scale web ranking dataset show the proposed methods (especially gradient-free) achieve lower final loss than the prior baseline.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical contribution and novel adaptation of inexact-oracle optimization to this application.  
- Strength: practical significance — improves ranking quality on real-world data and demonstrates applicability.  
- Strength: clear organization and presentation of model, problem, and methods.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks wall-clock runtime comparisons, limiting practical efficiency assessment.  
- Weakness: theoretical analysis of the gradient-free method assumes local convexity despite the overall problem being non-convex, creating a gap between theory and practice.  
- Suggestion: report wall-clock timings to compare practical runtime trade-offs of GFN, GBN, and baseline.  
- Suggestion: discuss sensitivity and selection of the Lipschitz constant L for the gradient-free method or include an ablation study.  
- Suggestion: add a notation/table summarizing key parameters (ε, δ, N, N1, N2, M, L) for clarity and fix minor typos (e.g., "concider" → "consider").
