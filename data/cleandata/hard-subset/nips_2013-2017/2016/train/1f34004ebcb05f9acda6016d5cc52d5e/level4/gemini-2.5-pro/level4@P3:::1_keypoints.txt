- Paper tackles non-convex parameter learning for Supervised PageRank where the loss depends on an inexact stationary distribution oracle.  
- Proposes a two-level framework: iterative lower-level solver with accuracy bounds and two upper-level optimization methods (gradient-free and gradient-based).  
- Provides the first theoretical convergence-rate guarantees and full complexity analysis for learning Supervised PageRank.  
- Empirical results on a web-ranking dataset show both proposed methods outperform the previous state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: closes a significant theoretical gap by formalizing the inexact-oracle setting and proving convergence.  
- Strength: methodology is sound—lower-level algorithm gives controllable error and enables upper-level analysis.  
- Strength: paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.  
- Strength: experiments validate theoretical claims and show practical improvement over prior work.  
- Weakness: optimization methods are mainly adaptations of existing frameworks, so novelty is incremental.  
- Weakness: gradient-free method’s convergence relies on a local convexity assumption that may be unrealistic for the non-convex problem.  
- Weakness: missing a dedicated conclusion section to summarize findings and future work.  
- Pro-accept: significant theoretical advance and a rigorous, potentially broadly applicable framework.  
- Pro-accept: theoretical results are supported by empirical improvements over prior approaches.  
- Con-accept: limited originality in optimization techniques (extensions rather than new principles).  
- Con-accept: restrictive convexity assumption for the gradient-free convergence proof.  
- Suggestion: present the optimization framework more generally before applying to Supervised PageRank to highlight broader impact.  
- Suggestion: add a dedicated conclusion section summarizing key takeaways and future directions.  
- Suggestion: discuss the local convexity assumption for the gradient-free method and its practical implications or sensitivity.
