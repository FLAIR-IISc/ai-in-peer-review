- Problem: Learning Supervised PageRank models via non-convex loss minimization with node/edge weights as linear feature combinations.  
- Methods: Proposes two two-level optimization methods — a gradient-based approach and a gradient-free method — using an inexact oracle framework.  
- Novelty/innovation: Adapts Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) gradient-free framework to constrained optimization with known approximation accuracy and develops a constrained non-convex gradient method with inexact oracle guarantees.  
- Theoretical contributions: Provides convergence guarantees and complexity bounds (Theorems 1–4) for both gradient-based and gradient-free settings.  
- Practical analysis: Thorough trade-off analysis between lower-level accuracy and overall computational cost.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on Yandex web browsing graphs show consistent, statistically significant improvements over prior methods.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with precise algorithm definitions and adequate supplementary material.  
- Strength: Rigorous proofs and actionable complexity results that address limitations of prior work [21].  
- Strength: Problem is well-motivated; incorporating features improves ranking quality versus structure-only PageRank.  
- Weakness: Originality framing is weak — insufficiently contrasts with related NIPS optimization work (e.g., Ghadimi & Lan 2013; Gasnikov et al. 2014).  
- Weakness: Needs stronger emphasis that applying convergence-guaranteed optimization to Supervised PageRank is a novel and meaningful contribution.  
- Minor issue: Conclusion is underdeveloped and lacks a concise summary of contributions and implications.  
- Minor issue: Phrasing that methods are mere "adaptations" of Nesterov & Spokoiny is misleading given the constrained-optimization and complexity-analysis extensions.  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, significant contribution to theory and practice of graph-based ranking with solid empirical support.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested revisions to explicitly contrast prior inexact-oracle work and to strengthen the conclusion.
