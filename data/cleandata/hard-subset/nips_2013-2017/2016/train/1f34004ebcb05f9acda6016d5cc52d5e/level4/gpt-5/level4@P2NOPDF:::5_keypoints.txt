- Paper proposes a feature-weighted PageRank framework and combines a Markov-chain stationary distribution approximation with a gradient-free optimization adapted for noisy loss estimates.
- Framework largely reproduces ideas from prior work [21] under different terminology and notation, raising novelty concerns.
- Authors introduce technical assumptions and a loss function, then state lemmas bounding an approximate gradient.
- Paper adapts a biased gradient-oracle-driven gradient-free scheme and provides a general performance theorem.
- With extra assumptions the authors instantiate an algorithm for their learning problem and derive further bounds.
- Experiments are briefly described on a single web graph, with most details deferred to the appendix.
- Crucial experimental parameters are not reported, and claimed performance improvements over baselines are not quantified or assessed for significance.
- Unclear whether the method was tested on multiple or diverse graphs, limiting claims of robustness.
- Writing contains many grammatical errors and inconsistent citations; the “Supervised PageRank” label conflicts with [21] which appears to address semi-supervised PageRank.
- Theoretical bounds are strong but key assumptions (e.g., restricting parameter search to an R-ball around phi0) may not hold in practice and could limit applicability.
- Major concern: substantive content (proofs and empirical results) is relegated to the appendix, leaving the main text without proof sketches or concrete experimental results.
- Major concern: empirical setup omits how phi0 and R were chosen, whether hyperparameters were tuned on validation data, and whether multiple datasets were evaluated.
- Major concern: practical impact is not demonstrated—unclear what improvements matter for real ranking tasks, computational costs versus baselines, and robustness across metrics/datasets.
- Requested additions: clearly differentiate contribution from [21], include proof outlines, report full experimental details and metrics, quantify improvements, add more graphs, and provide sensitivity analyses for phi0 and R.
- Overall assessment: theoretical development appears solid but presentation, empirical validation, and positioning relative to prior work are insufficient, leaving practical relevance unconvincing.
