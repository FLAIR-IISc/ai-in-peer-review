- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) with provable convergence/accuracy and a gradient-free method (GFN) guaranteeing loss decrease per iteration.
- Both methods avoid computing the exact objective value, provide estimated convergence rates, and reduce expensive large-matrix computations.
- Methods build upon Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17) for lower- and upper-level optimization.
- Experiments show improved ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- Hyperparameters for both methods are clearly stated.
- Datasets are not publicly available, but dataset descriptions are sufficient for reproduction.
- Sections 2–5 give detailed method explanations and proofs are provided in the supplementary material.
- Review recommends adding a symbol table because many symbols (e.g., N) are introduced without clear early definitions.
- Experimental comparisons focus on loss vs. GBP; reviewer suggests comparing ranking quality as more informative.
- Reviewer requests a direct comparison to ref:17 (the baseline generalized by this work) to clarify performance gains.
