This paper proposes two optimization methods to solve the supervised PageRank problem, which distinguishes itself from existing methods by providing a gradient-based approach with a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate that can achieve a given accuracy. Additionally, the proposed gradient-free method ensures a decrease in the loss function value. Notably, both methods do not require the exact value of the objective function and offer an estimate of the convergence rate, outperforming state-of-the-art methods in terms of ranking quality. The hyper-parameters used in both methods are provided, and although the datasets are not publicly available, their clear description facilitates potential reproduction of the results.

The authors introduce two two-level optimization methods, namely gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN), to solve the supervised PageRank problem, building upon the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref: 17). The proposed methods operate at two levels, leveraging the lower-level and upper-level optimization techniques from the aforementioned reference. A key advantage of the proposed approach is that it does not require the exact value of the objective function and provides a proven estimate of the convergence rate given a specified accuracy. Consequently, this method can avoid computing large matrices while still achieving good results.

The authors provide a detailed explanation of their methods from Section 2 to Section 5, including proofs in the supplementary material. However, the inclusion of a symbol table would greatly enhance readability, as the numerous symbols in the equations can be overwhelming. For instance, the variable $N$ is introduced in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 without explicit explanation until Line 193. Furthermore, the experimental section only compares the proposed work with GBP in terms of the loss function, whereas a comparison based on ranking quality would be more comprehensive. Moreover, given that this work generalizes the approach in ref: 17, a direct comparison with the reference method would be beneficial, allowing readers to assess whether the proposed method offers improved performance.
