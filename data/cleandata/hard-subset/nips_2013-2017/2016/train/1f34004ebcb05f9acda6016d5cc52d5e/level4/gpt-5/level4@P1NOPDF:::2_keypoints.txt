- Paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained nonconvex optimization with node/edge features and proposes two solvers: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.
- Main concern: experimental results are absent from the main text, making the paper feel incomplete and limiting assessment of practical relevance.
- All experiments are relegated to the supplement; the main paper should include a concise empirical evaluation to substantiate claims, show typical performance, and justify design choices.
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the objective in (2.4) is nonconvex; the authors must explain how the feasible set Φ can be chosen to ensure convexity or explicitly state applicable special cases/conditions.
- Theorem 3 gives a bound for MK(xK − xK+1) but it is unclear whether this implies convergence to a stationary point; the paper should state additional conditions required (e.g., error summability, bounded MK, Lipschitz continuity) and whether convergence is for the whole sequence or a subsequence and which stationarity notion is used.
- Supplementary Figure 1 suggests the gradient-free method (GFNM) converges slowly; the authors should report efficiency metrics (wall-clock time, oracle calls, iterations, stopping criteria, sensitivity to oracle errors) and evaluate scalability on PageRank-scale graphs.
- Overall: the problem and methods are relevant for handling inexact oracles, but the paper needs core empirical evidence in the main text and clearer theoretical assumptions/convergence statements to be convincing.
