- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank with non-convex loss using gradient-based and gradient-free methods with convergence guarantees.
- Builds on Supervised PageRank [21] by incorporating node/edge features but lacks clear distinction from prior work.
- Main theoretical contributions: gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle and convergence to stationary points.
- Adapts random gradient-free methods to constrained settings with known-accuracy function evaluations.
- Provides detailed complexity bounds and trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall complexity.
- Proves key lemmas bounding approximation error for the loss and gradient, enabling theoretical guarantees.
- Experiments use Yandex web-ranking data, but empirical details are inadequately reported in the main text.
- Lacks main-text quantitative results, tables, p-values/statistical tests, and reporting of initial parameter φ₀ and radius R.
- The R-ball constraint (‖φ-φ₀‖₂ ≤ R) may be overly restrictive; paper fails to analyze its practical impact or robustness.
- Writing and citation issues: grammatical errors, inconsistent citations (e.g., [21] mislabeled), and unclear contribution delineation.
- Strengths: strong theoretical analysis, inexact-oracle framework, clear complexity treatment, and gradient-free method avoids costly derivatives.
- Recommendations: move key experiments to main text, report concrete performance metrics and statistical tests, address R-ball limitation, clarify relation to [21], and fix writing.
