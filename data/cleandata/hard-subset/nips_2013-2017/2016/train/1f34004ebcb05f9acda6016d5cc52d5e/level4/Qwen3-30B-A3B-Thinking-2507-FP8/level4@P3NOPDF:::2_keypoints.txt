- Paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem and proposes two solvers: an inexact zeroth-order (gradient-free) method and an inexact first-order (gradient-based) method.  
- Major flaw: core experimental results are missing from the main text and relegated to the supplement, violating NIPS standards for substantive contributions.  
- Because results are omitted from the main body, there are no quantitative comparisons, convergence plots, or benchmark performance metrics to assess practical efficacy.  
- Authors should move key experimental results (accuracy, convergence rates, scalability comparisons) into the main paper; leave only auxiliary details in the appendix.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the objective in Eq.(2.4) is non-convex; the authors must clarify how the parameter set Φ is chosen to ensure convexity or revise the theorem.  
- Theorem 3 only bounds ||K(x_K − x_{K+1})|| and does not guarantee convergence to a stationary point; the paper should state whether lim_{k→∞} ||∇f(x_k)|| = 0 or clarify the theorem’s actual scope.  
- Supplement Figure 1 suggests the gradient-free method (GFNM) converges slowly versus baselines; report efficiency metrics (iterations to tolerance, wall-clock time) to contextualize practical utility.  
- Terminology: use the standard phrase “zeroth-order oracle” rather than “zero-order oracle.”  
- Missing comparison to prior work on constrained non-convex PageRank (e.g., ICML 2015); this weakens claims of novelty.  
- Constraints in the optimization formulation are not justified—explain why they are necessary for Supervised PageRank and how they were selected.  
- Writing issues and ambiguity (e.g., grammar, vague phrases like “incorporating properties”); specify which properties (e.g., node labels, edge weights).  
- Theorem 2’s proof sketch is insufficient; include a concise argument demonstrating the bound’s tightness.  
- Overall: without relocating key results to the main text, resolving the convexity/nonconvexity contradiction, clarifying convergence guarantees, and reporting efficiency metrics, the paper’s claims remain unverified and it does not meet NIPS standards.
