

Review:  
This paper addresses the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for graph-based ranking models, where node and edge weights are defined via random walks parameterized by node and edge features. The authors identify that standard optimization methods fail here due to the inability to compute exact objective values. To resolve this, they propose a two-level approach. At Level 1, they employ a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the underlying Markov random walk, rigorously establishing that the loss function can be approximated to any desired precision. At Level 2, they develop a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle—where function values are accessible only within a known error bound—and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The key contribution lies in adapting this inexact-oracle framework to constrained settings, which the authors successfully integrate into their two-level algorithm.  

The paper is well-structured, with proofs appearing sound (though I have not verified all technical details). However, two concerns require clarification. First, the core optimization techniques closely mirror those in Nesterov and Spokoiny (Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015), particularly their random gradient-free minimization framework. Second, the supervised PageRank algorithm used as a case study has precedents in prior work (e.g., [cite specific prior paper if known, otherwise generalize]). While the adaptation to constrained optimization with inexact oracles is valid, the novelty relative to existing literature warrants stronger articulation.  

The authors should explicitly position their work within the broader landscape of constrained non-convex optimization under inexact information. Framing the graph-ranking problem as a specific instance of a general framework—rather than presenting it as a standalone case—would clarify how their method advances the state of the art. For instance, highlighting how their inexact-oracle convergence guarantees extend beyond the graph-ranking context would strengthen the contribution.  

Additionally, the paper lacks a conclusion section despite ample space, which is expected in NIPS submissions. A concise summary of key insights, limitations, and future directions would significantly improve the paper’s completeness.  

In summary, the technical approach is solid and well-executed, with clear convergence guarantees. The primary weaknesses are insufficient differentiation from established methods (Nesterov & Spokoiny, prior PageRank variants) and an underdeveloped contextualization of the contribution. Addressing these would elevate the paper’s impact. I recommend acceptance pending revisions to: (1) clarify novelty via a general framework, (2) cite relevant prior work on constrained optimization with inexact oracles, and (3) add a conclusion section.
