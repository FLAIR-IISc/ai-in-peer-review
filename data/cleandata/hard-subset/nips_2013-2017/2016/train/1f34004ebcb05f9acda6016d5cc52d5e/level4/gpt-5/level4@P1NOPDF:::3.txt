This paper proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) with a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee that can achieve a prescribed accuracy, and a gradient-free method (GFN) that guarantees a decrease in the loss. Both methods operate without requiring exact evaluations of the objective function and come with provable convergence-rate estimates. The approach avoids explicit computation of large matrices while still producing strong results. The authors specify the hyperparameters used. Although the datasets are not publicly available, the descriptions are clear enough that reproducing the experiments should be feasible. The paper reports performance that is better than the state of the art in terms of ranking quality.

Both the lower-level and upper-level optimizations are based on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref: 17). Sections 2 through 5 provide methodological details, and the proofs are included in the supplementary material.

That said, the presentation would benefit from improved notation management. A symbol table would help significantly, as the number of symbols makes it easy to lose track. For example, the symbol N appears in line 139 and in Equation 3.1 but is not defined (as the number of steps) until line 193.

The experimental evaluation raises a few concerns. The paper compares primarily against GBP in terms of loss, not in terms of ranking quality. Given that the claimed advantage is better ranking quality, a direct comparison using standard ranking metrics would make the empirical case much stronger. Moreover, because this work is a generalization of ref: 17, a direct empirical comparison to ref: 17 would be valuable to demonstrate the practical gains over that baseline.
