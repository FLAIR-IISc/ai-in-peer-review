- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem for a graph-based ranking model defined by random walks with node/edge weights derived from feature-based random walks.
- The optimization problem is not solvable by methods requiring exact objective values, so a two-level approach is proposed.
- Level 1: uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution, enabling the loss to be approximated to arbitrary precision.
- Level 2: develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point; main novelty is adapting this to constrained settings with known precision.
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not fully verified by reviewer).
- Main concern: optimization contributions are largely adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and supervised PageRank has prior work, raising novelty questions.
- Minor issues/suggestions: paper lacks a conclusion and should present the optimization problem in a more general context to better position its contributions.
