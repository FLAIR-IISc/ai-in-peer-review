- Paper proposes two bilevel optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN) that operate with inexact objective information.
- Both methods provide convergence-rate guarantees under an inexact/oracle framework (building on Nesterov & Nemirovski), and GFN guarantees monotonic loss decrease.
- The algorithms avoid explicit construction/inversion of large matrices and use iterative primitives to improve scalability.
- The manuscript provides hyperparameter settings, implementation details, and full proofs in the supplement to support reproducibility.
- Experiments show competitive or improved performance, but the empirical evaluation has notable limitations.
- Strength: Clear methodological contribution framing supervised PageRank as a bilevel problem with provable algorithms under inexact oracles.
- Strength: Practical design that sidesteps heavy linear algebra, improving potential scalability to PageRank-scale problems.
- Strength: Theoretical guarantees (convergence rates and descent for GFN) strengthen the contribution.
- Strength: Algorithmic setup, hyperparameters, and proofs are described to aid reproducibility despite non-public data.
- Weakness: Notational overload and missing early definitions (e.g., N introduced late) make the paper hard to follow.
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation focuses on loss comparisons and lacks comprehensive standard ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP) to support ranking-quality claims.
- Weakness: Missing direct empirical comparison to the key baseline (ref. 17) and other learning-to-rank baselines to contextualize benefits.
- Weakness: Reproducibility concerns due to non-public datasets and lack of released code, pseudocode, and parameter search ranges.
- Weakness: Empirical efficiency claims need concrete runtime, memory, per-iteration complexity, and convergence-profile results.
- Questions for authors: Clarify the oracle/inexactness model (type of noise/estimates) and how it affects convergence guarantees.
- Questions for authors: Explain how target accuracy is chosen in practice and its trade-offs with runtime and ranking quality.
- Questions for authors: Detail variance/sample complexity and function-evaluation cost of the gradient-free estimator.
- Questions for authors: Specify the inner iterative solver for PageRank and how its tolerance couples to outer-loop accuracy.
- Questions for authors: Provide hyperparameter sensitivity/ablation results for step sizes, smoothing, and regularization.
- Suggestions: Add a notation table, define key symbols at first use, expand experiments with standard metrics and stronger baselines, report runtime/memory and scalability plots, provide pseudocode, and release code or synthetic benchmarks if data cannot be shared.
- Overall assessment: Technically solid and well-motivated with strong theory; acceptability depends on improving notation clarity and substantially strengthening the empirical evaluation.
