This paper formulates the Supervised PageRank model as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

My major concern with the paper is that it lacks experiment results in the main text, rendering it incomplete. This omission detracts from the overall impact of the paper, as it forces readers to refer to the supplement to fully understand the proposed methods' performance.

Several specific issues require attention:

1. The decision to relegate experiment results to the supplement is problematic, as it disrupts the flow of the main text and makes it difficult for readers to assess the proposed methods' efficacy. It would be beneficial to integrate the most critical experiment results into the main text to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the approaches.

2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the function in (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should clarify how to choose the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function. This detail is crucial in ensuring the optimization problem's solvability and the proposed methods' applicability.

3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. However, it is unclear whether this bound is sufficient to ensure that the sequence $xk$ converges to a stationary point. The authors should provide additional analysis or discussion to address this concern and establish the convergence properties of the proposed methods.

4. Figure 1 in the supplement suggests that the Gradient-Free Newton Method (GFNM) converges slowly. The authors should consider reporting the efficiency of the algorithms, including GFNM, to provide a more complete picture of their performance. This information would be invaluable in helping readers understand the practical implications of the proposed approaches.

By addressing these concerns and incorporating experiment results into the main text, the authors can significantly enhance the paper's clarity, completeness, and overall impact.
