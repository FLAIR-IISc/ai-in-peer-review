- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank, offering a different approach from existing methods.  
- GBN has a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate allowing achievement of a specified accuracy.  
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function value without requiring gradient information.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective-function values and provide convergence-rate estimates.  
- Methods avoid computing large matrices while still achieving satisfactory results.  
- Claims performance surpasses state-of-the-art in ranking quality.  
- Two-level optimization framework builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref. 17) using lower- and upper-level optimization.  
- Paper includes detailed methods and proofs, but readability suffers from many symbols and missing symbol table (e.g., unclear use of N).  
- Experiments compare loss only to GBP; ranking-quality comparisons are missing and should be added.  
- Direct comparison to ref. 17 is lacking despite generalizing that approach.  
- Datasets are not public but are clearly described; hyper-parameters are provided, aiding reproducibility.
