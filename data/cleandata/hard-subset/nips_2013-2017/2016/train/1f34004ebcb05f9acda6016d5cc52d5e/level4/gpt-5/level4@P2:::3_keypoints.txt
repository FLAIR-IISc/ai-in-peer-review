- Tackles learning Supervised PageRank with node/edge features by minimizing a nonconvex loss over query-specific graphs using two-level optimization.  
- Lower level: uses Nesterov–Nemirovski method to approximate stationary distributions and derivatives instead of the power method.  
- Upper level: proposes two optimizers — a constrained gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle (GFN) and an adaptive projected gradient method with inexact first-order oracle (GBN).  
- Provides convergence-rate guarantees for both upper-level methods, accounting for inexact oracle information.  
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Empirical evaluation on a Yandex browsing-graph dataset shows better loss values and faster convergence than prior GBP baseline.  
- Confirms Nesterov–Nemirovski converges faster than the power method for stationary distribution computation.  
- Clear problem formulation with constraints on parameters and feature-based restart/transition modeling.  
- Novel extension of random gradient-free optimization to constrained settings with inexact function values.  
- New adaptive projected gradient method for nonconvex composite problems with inexact first-order oracles.  
- Theoretical complexity bounds explicitly include oracle accuracy and inexactness.  
- Careful analysis linking lower-level solver accuracy to upper-level performance and cost.  
- Experiments use a real-world, query-dependent browsing graph, demonstrating practical improvements.  
- Paper is notation-heavy; a symbol table and earlier symbol definitions would improve readability.  
- Experimental metrics focus on surrogate loss; missing standard ranking measures (NDCG@k, MAP, Precision@k) and statistical significance analysis.  
- Narrow baselines: comparison mostly to GBP; should include ablation of lower-level solvers and broader learning-to-rank or supervised random-walk baselines.  
- Reproducibility concerns: dataset not public — recommend releasing code and synthetic/anonymized data, plus wall-clock time, memory, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Relation to [17] insufficiently explored end-to-end; need explicit quantification of replacing power method with [17] on objective and runtime.  
- Quality: theoretical development appears sound with careful inexact-oracle frameworks and supporting empirical claims, but runtime and ranking metric analysis are limited.  
- Clarity: organized with detailed algorithms and lemmas, but heavy notation and delayed definitions hinder accessibility.  
- Originality: meaningful extensions for inexact zero- and first-order optimization applied to Supervised PageRank, though lower-level builds on [17].  
- Significance: potentially impactful for large-scale PageRank-like models due to inexact computation guarantees and derivative-free options, pending broader empirical validation.  
- Pros: solid theoretical contributions, integrated two-level learning framework, empirical improvements on real data, and useful accuracy–cost complexity analysis.  
- Cons: limited reporting on ranking metrics and runtime, high notational load, narrow baseline coverage, and dataset non-public limiting reproducibility.  
- Overall: technically strong with meaningful contributions; leans toward acceptance conditional on improved clarity and a more comprehensive empirical evaluation.
