- Proposes two optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- GBN comes with a provable convergence rate to reach a specified accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function.
- Both methods avoid requiring exact objective evaluations.
- Both provide convergence rate estimates.
- Empirically achieve better ranking quality than state-of-the-art methods.
- Hyper-parameters for both algorithms are clearly specified.
- Datasets are not public, but the methodological description is detailed enough for reproducibility.
- Two-level optimization framework is rigorously developed (Sections 2–5) with proofs in the supplement.
- Readability suffers from high symbol density; a symbol table is recommended (e.g., N is undefined until later).
- Experimental comparison with GBP only evaluates loss, not ranking quality.
- A direct comparison with ref:17 (the foundational work generalized here) is needed to contextualize improvements.
