This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem, leveraging properties of both nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve this problem: a gradient-free method utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle, and a gradient-based method employing an inexact first-order oracle. My primary concern is the omission of experimental results from the main text, rendering the paper incomplete. Detailed comments follow.

1. The placement of experimental results solely within the supplementary material significantly detracts from the completeness of the main text.
2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the function in (2.4) is demonstrably non-convex. The paper should address how the set $\Phi$ is chosen to ensure the convexity of the objective function, or clarify the conditions under which the theorem holds.
3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. It is unclear whether this bound is sufficient to guarantee convergence of the sequence $x_k$ to a stationary point.
4. As shown in Figure 1 of the supplement, the Gradient-Free Newton Method (GFNM) exhibits slow convergence. The authors should also report on the computational efficiency of both algorithms.
