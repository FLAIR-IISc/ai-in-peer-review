- Introduces two novel two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based (GBN) and a gradient-free (GFN) approach.  
- Methods draw on Nesterov and Nemirovski [17] and do not require exact objective values, avoiding costly large-matrix operations.  
- Strong theoretical guarantees: GBN has provable convergence to specified accuracy; GFN guarantees a decrease in loss per iteration.  
- Empirical results show the proposed methods outperform state-of-the-art baselines in ranking quality.  
- Authors provide hyperparameters and clear dataset descriptions, though datasets are not publicly available.  
- Technical contributions are well-supported by proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Main presentation weakness: dense mathematical notation that reduces readability and harms accessibility.  
- Recommends adding a symbol table to clarify notation (e.g., delayed definition of N).  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete: comparison with GBP reports only loss values, lacking direct ranking metrics like NDCG or MAP.  
- Lacks a direct empirical comparison to the baseline method from Nesterov and Nemirovski [17], which would clarify practical benefits.  
- Overall: theoretically sound and promising empirically, but impact would improve with clearer notation and stronger experimental comparisons.
