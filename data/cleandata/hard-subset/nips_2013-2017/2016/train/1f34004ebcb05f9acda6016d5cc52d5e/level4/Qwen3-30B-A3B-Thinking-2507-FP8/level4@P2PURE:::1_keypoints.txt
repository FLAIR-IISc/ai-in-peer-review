- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem in a graph-based ranking model.  
- Model: a random walk whose node and edge weights are defined by random walks over node/edge features.  
- Existing methods fail because they require exact objective function values.  
- Proposed two-level approach: level 1 uses a linearly convergent method to estimate the Markov chain’s stationary distribution, allowing loss approximation to any precision.  
- Develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adapts techniques to constrained optimization when function values can be computed with known precision and applies this in level 2.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not exhaustively verified).  
- Major concern: core optimization techniques are adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and the supervised PageRank idea is not novel.  
- Minor issue: conclusion is missing despite available space.  
- Suggestion: frame the optimization problem more generally so the proposed algorithm reads as a specific instance and clarify novelty relative to prior work.
