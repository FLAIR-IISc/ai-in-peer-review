- Proposes two bilevel optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- GBN has a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee to any prescribed accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss at each iteration.
- Both methods work without exact objective evaluations yet still provide convergence-rate estimates.
- Methods avoid forming large matrices and demonstrate strong empirical performance.
- Hyperparameters are specified; datasets are not public but descriptions make reproduction plausible.
- Sections 2–5 clearly explain the algorithms; proofs are provided in the supplementary material.
- The lower- and upper-level procedures build on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref:17).
- Clarity issue: many symbols and potential ambiguity (e.g., N used inconsistently); a symbol table is recommended.
- Experimental concern: comparisons focus on loss (vs. GBP) rather than ranking-quality metrics.
- Suggestion: include direct empirical comparison to ref:17 baseline and report standard ranking metrics if claiming improved ranking quality.
