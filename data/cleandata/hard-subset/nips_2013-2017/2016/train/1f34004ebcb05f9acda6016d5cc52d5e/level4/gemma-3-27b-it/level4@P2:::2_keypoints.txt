- Tackles learning Supervised PageRank with node/edge features as a constrained non-convex optimization problem.  
- Proposes two solution approaches: a gradient-free method (inexact zero-order oracle) and a gradient-based method (inexact first-order oracle).  
- Key contribution: provides convergence rate analyses for both proposed methods.  
- Major concern: experimental results are omitted from the main text, hindering empirical evaluation.  
- Paper situates itself well in related work, citing key PageRank and supervised ranking literature.  
- Motivation for incorporating node and edge features is clearly articulated.  
- Problem formulation (Section 2) is well-defined and builds on prior Supervised PageRank work.  
- Methods are described with reasonable detail and theoretical contributions are noteworthy.  
- Strength: theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods on a challenging non-convex problem.  
- Strength: novel adaptation of existing optimization techniques to constrained settings with inexact oracles.  
- Strength: analysis of accuracy vs. computational complexity trade-offs.  
- Weakness: experimental results only in supplementary material—key results should appear in the main text.  
- Weakness: apparent inconsistency between claimed non-convexity and the convexity assumption in Theorem 1—needs clarification on parameter space Φ.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3 bounds distance between iterates but doesn’t clearly show convergence to a stationary point.  
- Weakness: gradient-free method shows slow convergence (supplement), requiring further efficiency and cost comparison.  
- Acceptance arguments: strong theoretical guarantees, novel methodological adaptation, and complexity-accuracy analysis.  
- Rejection arguments: missing main-text experiments, convexity/assumption inconsistencies, unclear convergence link, and slow gradient-free performance.  
- Overall assessment: theoretically solid but incomplete without main-text experiments and clarifications; current recommendation is reject but open to reconsideration after revisions.
