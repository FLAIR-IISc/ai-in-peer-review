- Paper proposes two supervised PageRank optimization methods: a gradient-based (GBN) method with a convergence-rate guarantee to reach prescribed accuracy and a gradient-free (GFN) method with guaranteed loss decrease.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective evaluations and come with convergence-rate estimates.  
- Authors report superior ranking quality compared to the state of the art and provide hyper-parameter settings.  
- Methods are bilevel optimizations building on Nesterov & Nemirovski (ref. 17) and avoid constructing the large matrix explicitly.  
- Technical details are in Sections 2–5 and full proofs are provided in the supplementary material.  
- Datasets are not publicly available, but the dataset description appears sufficient for reproducing results.  
- Notation is dense and confusing; a symbol table is recommended (e.g., N appears in line 139/Eq.3.1 but is only defined later).  
- Experiments compare with GBP only in terms of loss rather than ranking quality—ranking-metric comparisons are needed.  
- Since the work generalizes ref. 17, a direct empirical comparison against ref. 17 is necessary to demonstrate improved performance.
