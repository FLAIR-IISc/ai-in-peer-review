This paper formulates the Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem. The first one is a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle. The second one is a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed methods. They also provide theoretical guarantees for the convergence rates of the proposed methods. However, there are some issues with the presentation of the results.

Firstly, the experiment results are not presented in the main text, which makes the paper incomplete. The authors should consider including the experiment results in the main text to make it more comprehensive.

Secondly, the convexity assumption in Theorem 1 is not justified. The function in (2.4) is non-convex, and it is not clear how to choose the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function. The authors should provide more discussion on this point.

Thirdly, Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$, but it is not clear if this bound ensures that the sequence $xk$ converges to a stationary point. The authors should provide more discussion on this point.

Finally, the authors should consider including more details about the efficiency of the algorithms. By Figure 1 in the supplement, GFNM converges slowly, and the authors may also want to report the efficiency of the algorithms.

Strengths:

* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed methods.
* The authors provide theoretical guarantees for the convergence rates of the proposed methods.

Weaknesses:

* The experiment results are not presented in the main text.
* The convexity assumption in Theorem 1 is not justified.
* The bound in Theorem 3 does not ensure convergence to a stationary point.
* The efficiency of the algorithms is not reported.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Include the experiment results in the main text.
* Provide more discussion on the convexity assumption in Theorem 1.
* Provide more discussion on the bound in Theorem 3.
* Report the efficiency of the algorithms.
