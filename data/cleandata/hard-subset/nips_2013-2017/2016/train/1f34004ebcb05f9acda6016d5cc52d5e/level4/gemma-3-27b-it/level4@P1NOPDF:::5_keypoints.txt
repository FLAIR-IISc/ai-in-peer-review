- Paper revisits PageRank and introduces a feature-based node/edge weighted framework for learning parameters.  
- Proposes combining stationary-distribution approximation for Markov walks with an adaptation of a gradient-free optimizer for estimated losses.  
- Framework largely reproduces prior work ([21]) under different notation.  
- Paper states many technical assumptions and defines a challenging loss/optimization problem.  
- Several lemmas bound the approximate gradient and a theorem analyzes a biased gradient-free oracle.  
- Authors adapt the gradient-free methods to their specific learning problem and provide additional bounds.  
- Experimental evaluation uses a web graph from related work but detailed results and analysis are mostly in the appendix.  
- Key experimental parameters are not reported, and it’s unclear how φ₀ and R were chosen.  
- Claim of outperforming existing approaches lacks substantive discussion of extent, implications, and practical consequences.  
- Unclear whether evaluation covered diverse graph structures or multiple datasets.  
- Core contributions (detailed proofs and experimental results) are primarily relegated to the appendix rather than the main text.  
- Several grammatical errors and inconsistent citation styles; mislabels [21] as addressing “Supervised PageRank” though it is semi-supervised.  
- Reviewer doubts practical impact and validity of restrictive technical assumptions (e.g., search space limited to an R-ball around φ₀).
