This paper proposes gradient-based and random-gradient-free methods to solve the non-convex loss minimization problem of learning supervised PageRank models. The authors' approach to tackling this complex problem is well-motivated and well-executed.

One potential improvement to the paper would be to include a detailed analysis of the computational time required for the proposed optimization methods. Reporting the time complexity or providing experimental results that demonstrate the efficiency of these methods would strengthen the paper and provide valuable insights for practitioners.

The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear. The authors do a good job of explaining the background and motivation for their work, and the technical details are well-presented. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are well-analyzed.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. First, the authors could provide more context about the supervised PageRank model and its applications. While the paper does a good job of explaining the technical details of the model, it would be helpful to have a clearer understanding of why this model is important and how it is used in practice.

Second, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing methods. While the authors do compare their approach to a few baseline methods, it would be helpful to have a more comprehensive evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses of different approaches to learning supervised PageRank models.

Overall, this is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field. With a few revisions to address the areas mentioned above, it has the potential to be even stronger.
