- Proposes two novel two-level supervised PageRank learning methods: a gradient-based algorithm (GBN) and a gradient-free algorithm (GFN).  
- Extends inexact-oracle framework (building on Nesterov & Nemirovski [17]) to provide convergence guarantees for both methods.  
- Addresses lack of convergence proofs in prior gradient-based approach [21] by establishing rates to stationary points despite inexact lower-level computations.  
- Theoretical contributions include bounds on inexact-oracle approximation errors (Lemma 1, Lemma 2) and computational complexity guarantees (Theorems 2 and 4).  
- Demonstrates superior empirical ranking performance on real-world web ranking tasks, with GFN showing particularly strong results.  
- Carefully analyzes the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy (N) and overall computational complexity.  
- Paper quality is high with rigorous analysis and clear experimental setup using real data.  
- Clarity suffers from excessive mathematical symbols and lack of a comprehensive symbol table; some parameters (e.g., N) are defined late and may confuse readers.  
- Originality is strong: first application of inexact-oracle methods to supervised PageRank and extension to a broader class of constrained non-convex problems.  
- Significance is substantial: practical, theoretically sound methods that avoid large matrix computations and improve ranking quality.  
- Strengths summarized: convergence guarantees, rigorous complexity analysis, superior empirical performance, careful trade-off treatment, and clear real-world experiments.  
- Weaknesses summarized: missing symbol table hampers readability, experiments emphasize loss values over direct ranking metrics, and insufficient direct comparison with foundational work [17].  
- Recommendation: improve symbol clarity (add a symbol table) and provide more direct comparisons to [17] to better demonstrate improvements.
