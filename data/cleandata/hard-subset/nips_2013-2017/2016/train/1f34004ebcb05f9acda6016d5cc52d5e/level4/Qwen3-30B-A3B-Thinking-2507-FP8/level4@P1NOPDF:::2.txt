

This paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by leveraging node and edge properties, proposing two solution approaches: a gradient-free method using an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method using an inexact first-order oracle. While the technical contributions are interesting, the most significant issue is the absence of experimental results in the main text, which substantially weakens the paper's impact and verifiability.  

1.  **Missing Experimental Results**: The authors relegate all experimental results to the supplement, leaving the main text devoid of empirical validation. This violates standard NIPS practice and prevents readers from assessing the core claims without navigating supplementary material. The authors should present key results (e.g., accuracy, convergence rates) in the main paper.  

2.  **Convexity Contradiction in Theorem 1**: Theorem 1 assumes convexity of the objective function, yet Equation (2.4) explicitly describes a non-convex problem. The paper does not clarify how the set $\Phi$ is chosen to ensure convexity. Without this justification, the theorem’s applicability to the stated problem remains unclear. The authors must resolve this inconsistency.  

3.  **Convergence Guarantee in Theorem 3**: Theorem 3 provides a bound for $M \|x_K - x_{K+1}\|$, but this does not necessarily imply convergence of $\{x_k\}$ to a stationary point. The authors should explicitly state whether this bound suffices for convergence or clarify the relationship between the bound and stationarity.  

4.  **Algorithm Efficiency**: Figure 1 (supplement) shows the gradient-free method (GFNM) converging slowly compared to other baselines. The authors should report computational efficiency metrics (e.g., runtime per iteration, total wall-clock time) to contextualize this observation. Without such data, the practical utility of GFNM remains questionable.  

Overall, the theoretical framework is promising but undermined by the lack of core experimental validation in the main text. The convexity contradiction in Theorem 1 requires urgent clarification, and the convergence analysis in Theorem 3 needs strengthening. Addressing these issues would significantly improve the paper’s rigor and clarity. The supplementary results alone are insufficient for publication at NIPS.
