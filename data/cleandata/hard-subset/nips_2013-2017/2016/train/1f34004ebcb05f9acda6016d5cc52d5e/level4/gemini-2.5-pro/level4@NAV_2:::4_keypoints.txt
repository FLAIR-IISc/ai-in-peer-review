- Addresses learning parameters for Supervised PageRank by incorporating node/edge features into a non-convex, constrained loss-minimization problem.  
- Key technical challenge: loss and gradient cannot be computed exactly, requiring methods that work with an inexact oracle.  
- Main contribution: two two-level optimization algorithms (gradient-based and random gradient-free) tailored to this problem.  
- Provides the first convergence rate guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank, unlike prior work [21].  
- Theoretical framework adapts and extends inexact-oracle and random gradient-free optimization results to the constrained, non-convex setting.  
- Full complexity analyses are given (Theorems 2 and 4) linking low-level oracle accuracy to high-level convergence.  
- Empirical validation on a real-world web ranking task shows the proposed methods outperform the existing baseline, especially the gradient-free algorithm (GFN).  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized, separating general optimization frameworks from their application to Supervised PageRank.  
- Weakness: experiments omit wall-clock time or computational cost comparisons, leaving practical efficiency unclear.  
- Weakness: convergence analysis for the gradient-free method assumes local convexity in the domain Φ; more discussion on sensitivity when this fails is needed.  
- Minor clarifications requested: rationale and practical setting of hyperparameters (e.g., L = 10^-4).  
- Minor clarifications requested: more detailed description and tuning procedure for the baseline GBP implementation.
