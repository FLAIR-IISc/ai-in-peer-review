- Proposes a supervised PageRank framework that accounts for node and edge features.  
- Introduces two new optimization methods for the non-convex loss: a gradient-based and a gradient-free approach.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both optimization methods.  
- Demonstrates method effectiveness on a real-world ranking task via experiments.  
- Situates work relative to prior PageRank variants and non-convex optimization literature.  
- Technical content is sound and methods/results are clearly explained.  
- Strength: novel optimization methods with theoretical guarantees.  
- Strength: empirical validation on a practical ranking problem.  
- Weakness: main paper lacks detailed results and analysis, much is deferred to the appendix.  
- Weakness: writing has grammatical errors and citations are sloppy.  
- Weakness: limiting assumptions (e.g., restricting phi to an R-ball around phi0) may reduce generality and need discussion.  
- Originality/significance: moderate—new methods are a meaningful contribution but practical impact and detailed analysis are underdeveloped.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address presentation, results reporting, and assumption discussion.
