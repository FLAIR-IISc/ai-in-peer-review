- Proposes two novel optimization methods for Supervised PageRank: a gradient-based (GBN) and a gradient-free (GFN) method within a two-level framework using Nesterov's inexact oracle.  
- Provides convergence rate guarantees for both methods, addressing a major gap in prior work.  
- Extends the theoretical foundations of optimization for Markov chain–based ranking, building on Supervised PageRank [21].  
- Proves bounded computational complexity to achieve specified accuracy under appropriate assumptions.  
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity, important for large-scale problems.  
- Experimental results claim improved ranking quality over state-of-the-art [21] on a real web browsing dataset.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous and supported by detailed proofs (in supplementary material).  
- Correctly connects to Nesterov & Nemirovski [17] and identifies why standard optimization methods are limited here.  
- Writing is generally clear but suffers from ambiguous notation and would benefit from a symbol table.  
- The parameter N (lower-level iterations) is not clearly defined early, causing confusion around Equation 3.1.  
- The relationship between error bounds (δ1, δ2) and overall algorithm parameters should be made more explicit.  
- Experimental comparisons lack standard ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG, MAP) to substantiate "better ranking quality" claims.  
- Insufficient empirical comparison with the baseline Nesterov method [17]; need to show advantages of the two-level framework.  
- Significance: meaningful advance for ranking community by offering convergence guarantees without exact loss computation.  
- Overall: strong technical contribution suitable for NIPS pending minor revisions to notation clarity and stronger experimental comparisons.
