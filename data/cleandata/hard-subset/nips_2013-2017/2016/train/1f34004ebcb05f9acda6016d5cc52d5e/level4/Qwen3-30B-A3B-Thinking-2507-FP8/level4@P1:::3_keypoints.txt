- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (GBN — gradient-based, GFN — gradient-free) for Supervised PageRank with node/edge features.
- Introduces an inexact oracle framework to address lack of theoretical convergence guarantees in prior work [21].
- Provides rigorous theoretical convergence rates and complexity bounds for the non-convex loss-minimization problem.
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level stationary-distribution approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.
- Empirical results show both methods outperform the state-of-the-art GBP in ranking quality on a real-world web ranking task.
- GBN offers convergence guarantees without convexity assumptions and has fewer hyperparameters.
- GFN avoids computing derivatives of large matrices, giving a significant computational advantage.
- Paper is technically sound, well-structured, and supplies rigorous proofs (in supplementary material).
- Weakness: excessive use of symbols with delayed definitions (e.g., N undefined early); a symbol table is recommended.
- Weakness: limited evaluation using standard ranking metrics — should report NDCG, MAP, etc., to assess practical impact.
- Weakness: claims to generalize [17] but lacks direct experimental comparison to that base method.
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution with minor presentation/evaluation fixes; recommend acceptance to NIPS.
