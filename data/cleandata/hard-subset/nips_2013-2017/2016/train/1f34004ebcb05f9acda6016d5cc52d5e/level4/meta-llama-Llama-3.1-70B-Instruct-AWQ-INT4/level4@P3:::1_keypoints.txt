- Proposes a solution for non-convex loss-minimization to learn parameters of Supervised PageRank.  
- Develops two two-level optimization methods: one gradient-based and one gradient-free.  
- Model: random walk where node/edge weights are linear combinations of features.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct.  
- Provides a thorough convergence analysis for the proposed methods.  
- Experiments on a real-world ranking task show the methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient method.  
- Main technical contribution: adapts existing optimization methods to constrained problems with inexact oracles.  
- Presents a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle, proving convergence to stationary points.  
- Presents a gradient-free method for convex problems with inexact zero-order oracles.  
- Methods are adaptations of prior work (notably Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015).  
- Supervised PageRank algorithm itself was previously proposed (not novel).  
- Suggests presenting the optimization problem in a more general context to clarify broader implications.  
- Overall positive assessment but requests clearer positioning of contributions relative to prior work.
