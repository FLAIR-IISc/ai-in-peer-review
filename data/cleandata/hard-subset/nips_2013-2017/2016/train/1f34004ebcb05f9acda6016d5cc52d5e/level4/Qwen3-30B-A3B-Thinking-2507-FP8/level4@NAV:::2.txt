

This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by leveraging node and edge properties. The authors introduce two solution approaches: a gradient-free method utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle, and a gradient-based method employing an inexact first-order oracle. My primary concern is that all experimental results are relegated to the supplement, rendering the main text incomplete. Detailed comments:  
1. The absence of experiment results in the main text compromises the paper's completeness.  
2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the objective function in (2.4) is non-convex. Clarification is needed on how to select the set $\Phi$ to ensure convexity.  
3. Theorem 3 establishes a bound for $MK(x_K - x_{K+1})$. Does this bound suffice to guarantee convergence of the sequence $x_k$ to a stationary point?  
4. Figure 1 in the supplement indicates slow convergence of GFNM; the authors should also report algorithmic efficiency.
