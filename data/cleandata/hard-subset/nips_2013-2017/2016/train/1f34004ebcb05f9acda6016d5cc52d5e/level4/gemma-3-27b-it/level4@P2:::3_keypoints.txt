- Proposes gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN) methods for Supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence guarantees for both.  
- Methods operate with inexact function evaluations and provide estimated convergence rates.  
- Extends the two-level optimization framework from [21] using Nesterov & Nemirovski [17] to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution.  
- Avoids computing exact derivatives and large matrices, improving potential scalability.  
- Provides detailed theoretical analysis and complexity estimates, with proofs in the supplement.  
- Empirical results on a Yandex web-ranking dataset show improved ranking quality over state-of-the-art [21].  
- Strength: convergence guarantees and practicality of inexact oracle usage.  
- Strength: improved empirical performance and thorough theoretical treatment.  
- Weakness: dense notation and poor clarity; lacks a symbol table and some symbols (e.g., N) are unclear.  
- Weakness: experiments focus on loss rather than standard ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP).  
- Weakness: no direct comparison to the original method in [17] as a baseline.  
- Weakness: dataset is not publicly available, limiting reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: overall strong and recommend acceptance if clarity is improved and evaluation extended (add symbol table and standard ranking metrics).
