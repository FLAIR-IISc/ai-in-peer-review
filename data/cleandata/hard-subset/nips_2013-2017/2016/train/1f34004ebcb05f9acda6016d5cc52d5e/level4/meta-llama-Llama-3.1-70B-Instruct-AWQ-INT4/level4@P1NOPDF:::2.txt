This paper formulates the Supervised PageRank model as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle. However, my primary concern is that the main text lacks experimental results, rendering the paper incomplete.

Detailed comments are as follows:

1. The authors have relegated experimental results to the supplement, which detracts from the completeness of the main text. It would be beneficial to include at least a summary of the key findings in the main text to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed methods.

2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, whereas the function in equation (2.4) is non-convex. It is unclear how to choose the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function. The authors should provide more insight into this aspect or relax the convexity assumption in Theorem 1.

3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. However, it is uncertain whether this bound ensures that the sequence $xk$ converges to a stationary point. Further clarification on the implications of this bound for convergence is necessary.

4. Figure 1 in the supplement indicates that the Gradient-Free Newton Method (GFNM) converges slowly. In addition to reporting the accuracy of the algorithms, the authors should also discuss their computational efficiency to provide a more thorough evaluation of the proposed methods.
