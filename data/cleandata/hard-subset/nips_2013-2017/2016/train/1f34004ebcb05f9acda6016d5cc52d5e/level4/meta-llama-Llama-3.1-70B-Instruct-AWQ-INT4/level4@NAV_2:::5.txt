The authors propose a framework for learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges. They introduce two new optimization methods, a gradient-based and a gradient-free approach, to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem. The authors provide theoretical guarantees on the convergence rate of both methods and demonstrate their effectiveness in experiments on a real-world ranking task.

The paper relates to previous work on PageRank and its variants, as well as optimization methods for non-convex problems. The authors acknowledge the limitations of existing methods and propose new approaches to address these challenges. The technical content is sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their methods and results.

The strengths of the paper include the development of new optimization methods with theoretical guarantees, the application of these methods to a real-world problem, and the demonstration of their effectiveness in experiments. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach and results.

The weaknesses of the paper include the lack of detailed reporting of results and analysis in the main body of the paper, with most of the content relegated to the appendix. The writing is also littered with grammatical errors, and citations could be improved. Additionally, some assumptions made by the authors, such as the restriction of the search space of phi to an R-ball around some chosen phi0, may be limiting and should be discussed.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes new optimization methods and applies them to a real-world problem, which is a significant contribution. However, the authors could have done more to discuss the practical consequences of their improvements and provide more detailed results and analysis in the main body of the paper.

The significance of the paper is moderate, as it addresses a specific problem in the field of PageRank and provides new optimization methods with theoretical guarantees. However, the impact of the paper could be greater if the authors had provided more detailed results and analysis and discussed the practical consequences of their improvements.
