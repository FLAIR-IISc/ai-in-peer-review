- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- GBN offers a gradient-based approach with a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and controllable accuracy.  
- GFN is gradient-free and guarantees a decrease in the loss function value.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective function values and provide convergence rate estimates.  
- Methods avoid computing large matrices while maintaining good results, building on Nesterov & Nemirovski (ref:17).  
- Experimental results claim better ranking quality than state-of-the-art methods.  
- Hyperparameters for both methods are provided.  
- Datasets are not public but are clearly described, making reproduction feasible.  
- Paper includes detailed method descriptions (Sections 2–5) and proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Readability issue: many symbols are used without a symbol table; e.g., N is undefined until later (Line 139 vs. Line 193).  
- Experimental limitation: comparison is only to GBP using loss; should include ranking-quality comparisons.  
- Evaluation gap: should directly compare with ref:17 to quantify generalization/performance improvement.
