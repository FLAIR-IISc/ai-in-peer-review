- Studies gradient-based and random gradient-free optimization for learning supervised PageRank models in a non-convex setting.  
- Motivation is clear: standard PageRank uses only topology, authors propose learning to incorporate node features.  
- Loss formulation is reasonable, balancing graph consistency with feature-based similarity.  
- Optimization methods are standard; novelty lies in applying them to supervised PageRank rather than algorithmic innovation.  
- Paper lacks practical implementation details (step size choice, random-search parameters) and hyperparameter tuning/sensitivity analysis.  
- Runtime performance (wall-clock training/inference times and hardware) is not reported, hindering practicality assessment.  
- No runtime comparison to established embedding methods (e.g., DeepWalk, node2vec).  
- Experiments on benchmark datasets show competitive performance and often outperform baselines.  
- Baseline comparisons are not exhaustive or fully up-to-date; more recent node embedding methods should be included.  
- Statistical significance of results is inconsistently reported; statistical tests and p-values are needed.  
- Writing is generally good but clarity could be improved; a glossary of symbols would help readers.  
- Reproducibility is weak: connection between theory and implementation should be clearer and detailed pseudocode provided.  
- Strengths: addresses a relevant problem, provides solid theoretical framework, demonstrates optimization applicability, and achieves competitive empirical results.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient hyperparameter/practical detail, missing runtime analysis, incomplete baselines, inconsistent statistical reporting, and limited clarity/reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: promising work but recommended as Weak Reject pending revisions addressing runtime, experimental rigor, and implementation clarity.
