- Paper proposes weighted PageRank learning by combining stationary distribution approximation with a gradient-free optimization for estimable loss.
- Authors reframe an existing framework under different name/notation (similar to [21]).
- Several technical assumptions and a loss function are presented, with discussion of problem difficulty.
- Lemmas are provided to bound the approximate gradient.
- A general gradient-free method using a biased gradient-free oracle is described and a performance theorem is stated.
- Additional assumptions lead to a specialized algorithm adapting the gradient-free method to the learning problem with further bounds.
- Experimental section is brief, deferring detailed reporting and analysis to the appendix.
- Key experimental parameters are omitted and selection of φ₀ and R is not explained.
- Claims that the new algorithm outperforms others lack discussion of magnitude or statistical significance.
- It is unclear whether experiments were run on multiple or diverse graphs.
- Main text lacks tables, specific results, and comprehensive empirical evidence.
- Many grammatical errors and inconsistent citations (e.g., mislabeling [21] as Supervised PageRank).
- Concern that several technical assumptions may be unrealistic or too restrictive in practice (e.g., restricting φ to an R-ball around φ₀).
- Major substantive content (proofs and detailed experiments) is relegated to the appendix rather than the main paper.
- Overall: theoretical bounds and methods are promising but the paper fails to provide sufficient empirical evidence or clarity to be convincing.
