- Paper analyzes convergence for learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by a feature-dependent random walk where node/edge weights determine the stationary distribution.

- Main challenge: the objective cannot be computed exactly, preventing use of optimization methods that require precise function values.

- Proposed two-level approach: (1) use a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution, enabling loss approximation to arbitrary precision.

- (2) Develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.

- Primary technical contribution: adapting the inexact-oracle optimization framework to constrained problems where function values are available only up to known accuracy.

- Theoretical analysis is well-written and appears sound, though not every proof was verified in detail by the reviewer.

- Main concern: novelty — core optimization techniques seem to be adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) methods, and supervised PageRank has prior work.

- Recommendation: more clearly delineate the paper’s novel contributions relative to existing foundations.

- Minor suggestions: add a conclusion section and present the optimization problem in a broader context, framing the studied algorithm as an instance of a wider class of problems.
