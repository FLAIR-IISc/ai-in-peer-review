- Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods for non-convex loss minimization in Supervised PageRank using inexact oracle information.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both methods, improving over prior gradient-based approach [21].  
- Builds on PageRank/HITS and supervised ranking literature, formulating a two-level optimization problem.  
- Novel application of inexact oracle concepts to handle non-convex optimization challenges.  
- Analyzes trade-off between accuracy and computational complexity.  
- Detailed complexity analysis with proofs and supplementary material.  
- Empirical results show improvement over state-of-the-art [21] on a real-world ranking task.  
- Practical implications of the theoretical convergence rates are not fully explored.  
- Experiments could be more comprehensive, notably lacking runtime performance comparisons.  
- Dense notation and heavy reliance on supplementary materials hinder readability.  
- Parameter selection discussion (e.g., choice of L) is insufficient in the main text; sensitivity analysis relegated to supplement.  
- Recommendation: accept, with revisions to address runtime evaluation, notation clarity, and parameter-selection discussion.
