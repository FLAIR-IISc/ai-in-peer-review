- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node and edge features to overcome structure-only PageRank limitations.
- Proposes a two-level optimization: lower level approximates stationary distribution and derivatives; upper level applies gradient-based or gradient-free learning.
- Combines a Markov chain stationary approximation method [17] with adaptations of gradient and gradient-free optimization techniques.
- Provides convergence rate guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods, filling a gap in prior work.
- Utilizes an inexact oracle framework suitable for large-scale, intractable exact computations.
- Analyzes computational complexity and the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall runtime.
- Empirical results on a web page ranking task show improvements over a state-of-the-art method [21].
- Clearly defines the Supervised PageRank learning problem and its challenges.
- Writing suffers from numerous grammatical errors and awkward phrasing, reducing readability.
- Experimental section lacks sufficient detail, including missing tables/figures and unclear reporting of improvement magnitude.
- Key experimental parameters (e.g., ϕ₀ and R) and parameter selection are not fully explained.
- Experiments use a single dataset, limiting evidence of generalizability across diverse graphs.
- Practical significance and real-world impact of improvements are not convincingly articulated.
- There appears to be a discrepancy in the citation of prior work [21] related to Supervised PageRank.
- The constraint of parameter space Φ to an R-ball around ϕ₀ may be restrictive and requires further justification.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel theoretical contributions, useful convergence guarantees, and promising experimental performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: poor writing quality, insufficient empirical detail, limited dataset diversity, and unclear practical impact.
- Overall assessment: valuable and theoretically rigorous contribution but requires significant polishing and expanded experiments.
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance with major revisions — improve clarity, add detailed experimental results (tables/figures), justify assumptions, and include parameter sensitivity analyses.
