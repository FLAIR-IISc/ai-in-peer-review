- Paper tackles convergence for non-convex loss minimization in graph-based ranking where node/edge weights come from feature-parameterized random walks and exact objective values are unavailable.  
- Authors propose a two-level algorithm: Level 1 approximates the Markov stationary distribution with linear convergence, enabling arbitrary-precision loss approximation.  
- Level 2 presents a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle (known error bound on function values) and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution is extending the inexact-oracle framework to constrained settings and integrating it into the two-level algorithm for graph ranking.  
- Paper is well-structured and the proofs appear sound (not fully verified by reviewer).  
- Concern: core optimization techniques closely resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) random gradient-free minimization, raising novelty questions.  
- Concern: supervised PageRank case study has prior precedents, so the empirical/algorithmic novelty is unclear.  
- Recommendation: explicitly position the work within broader constrained non-convex optimization under inexact information and frame graph-ranking as a specific instance of a general framework.  
- Suggestion: emphasize how the inexact-oracle convergence guarantees extend beyond the graph-ranking context.  
- Missing element: paper lacks a conclusion section summarizing insights, limitations, and future directions.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid with clear convergence guarantees but needs stronger differentiation from existing methods; recommend acceptance pending revisions.  
- Requested revisions: (1) clarify novelty via a general framework, (2) cite relevant prior work on constrained optimization with inexact oracles, (3) add a conclusion section.
