This paper addresses the formulation of Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem. The first one is a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle, and the second one is a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.

The paper provides a thorough introduction to the problem, discussing the limitations of existing methods and the advantages of incorporating features of nodes and edges. The authors also present a clear and concise description of their proposed methods, including the gradient-free and gradient-based optimization algorithms.

The theoretical analysis of the algorithms is rigorous, and the authors provide proofs for the convergence rates and complexity bounds. The experimental results, although relegated to the supplement, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in outperforming the state-of-the-art gradient-based method.

However, there are a few concerns that need to be addressed. Firstly, the assumption of convexity in Theorem 1 is not justified, as the function in (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should provide a clear explanation of how to choose the set Φ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function.

Secondly, Theorem 3 provides a bound for MK(xK - x{K+1}), but it is unclear whether this bound ensures that the sequence xk converges to a stationary point. The authors should provide additional analysis or discussion to clarify this point.

Lastly, the experimental results show that GFNM converges slowly, and the authors may want to report the efficiency of the algorithms in terms of computational time or number of iterations.

In terms of quality, the paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant effort to provide a clear and concise presentation of their work. However, the concerns mentioned above need to be addressed to strengthen the paper.
