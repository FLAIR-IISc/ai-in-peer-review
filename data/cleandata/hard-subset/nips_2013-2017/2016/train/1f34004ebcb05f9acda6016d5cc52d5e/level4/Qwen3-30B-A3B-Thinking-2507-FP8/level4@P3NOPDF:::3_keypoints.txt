- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Core contributions: GBN with provable convergence rate; GFN guaranteeing loss decrease; both avoid exact objective evaluations and provide convergence-rate estimates.
- Empirical claims: superior ranking quality (e.g., NDCG@k) over baselines like GBP; hyperparameters and experimental setup are thoroughly reported enabling reproducibility despite unavailable public datasets.
- Theoretical foundation builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski (Ref:17); all proofs are in the supplementary material.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis filling a gap where prior supervised PageRank methods lack formal rate bounds.
- Strength: scalability via avoidance of explicit large-matrix computations (iterative approximations) with supporting theory.
- Weakness: notation is unclear—many symbols introduced without a symbol table, forcing readers to backtrack and impairing readability.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation omits direct comparison of ranking quality with the foundational Ref:17, weakening the novelty claim.
- Suggestion: include a symbol table in the main text to improve accessibility.
- Suggestion: compare ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG) directly against Ref:17, not just GBP, and clarify if loss improvements translate to ranking gains.
- Suggestion: emphasize empirical computational efficiency (e.g., runtime comparisons) alongside ranking quality.
- Overall verdict: valuable contribution with meaningful convergence guarantees and empirical gains; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
