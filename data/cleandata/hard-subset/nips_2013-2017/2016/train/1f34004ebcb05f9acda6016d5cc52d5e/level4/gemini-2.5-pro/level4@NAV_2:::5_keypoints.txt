- Problem: Learning parameters of a Supervised PageRank model where node/edge weights are feature-dependent.  
- Gap in prior work: Existing gradient-based method [21] lacks theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Main contribution: Two two-level optimization algorithms (gradient-based and random gradient-free) with formal convergence rates and complexity bounds.  
- Core idea: Model PageRank stationary distribution and its derivatives as an inexact oracle with controllable accuracy.  
- Methodology: Adapt general frameworks for optimization with inexact first-order and zeroth-order oracles to the constrained, non-convex learning problem.  
- Theoretical novelty: Provides rigorous convergence guarantees and complexity analysis for constrained non-convex and convex zeroth-order settings.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on a commercial web page ranking dataset; gradient-free method achieves lower loss than the baseline.  
- Strength — theoretical soundness: Rigorous proofs address a significant gap and may interest the optimization community.  
- Strength — originality: Novel combination of inexact-oracle optimization theory with graph ranking learning.  
- Strength — practical relevance: Real-world dataset evaluation demonstrates potential applicability.  
- Weakness — clarity: Paper has numerous grammatical and stylistic errors and needs proofreading.  
- Weakness — presentation of proofs: All proofs in supplementary material; main text lacks proof sketches or high-level intuitions for key theorems.  
- Weakness — experimental detail: Hyperparameter choices (e.g., center em, radius R=0.99, initial Lipschitz L0) are unexplained, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness — theoretical assumptions: Gradient-free analysis assumes convexity on feasible set Φ despite the loss being introduced as non-convex; implications and practicality of this assumption are not discussed.  
- Weakness — practical impact: Lower loss is shown, but standard IR metrics (e.g., NDCG) are not reported to link loss improvement to ranking quality.  
- Weakness — evaluation scope: Experiments are limited to user browsing graphs; broader graph types would strengthen generality.
