- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- GBN offers a gradient-based method with a theoretical convergence rate and the ability to reach a specified accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function without requiring exact objective values.
- Both methods do not require exact objective evaluations and provide estimates of convergence rates, avoiding large matrix computations.
- Experimental results claim superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- Hyper-parameters for both methods are provided; datasets are not public but are clearly described for reproducibility.
- Sections 2–5 and supplementary material include detailed method explanations and full proofs.
- Reviewer suggests adding a symbol table because many symbols are introduced and can be hard to track.
- Noted confusion: symbol N is used early (Line 139, eq. 3.1) but only explained later (Line 193).
- Experiments compare methods to GBP only on the loss function, not on ranking quality.
- Since the work generalizes ref. 17, the reviewer recommends a direct comparison with ref. 17 to demonstrate improved performance.
