- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.
- Both methods have theoretical convergence guarantees and do not require exact objective function values.
- Methods include estimated convergence rates given an accuracy.
- Lower- and upper-level optimization build on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17).
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform state-of-the-art in ranking quality.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes all proofs in the supplementary material.
- Methods are empirically effective and theoretically well-supported.
- Paper is technically sound and claims are well-supported by analysis and experiments.
- Original contribution: two novel optimization approaches for supervised PageRank.
- Significance: potential to improve reliability and speed of search engines.
- Weakness: numerous symbols—paper would benefit from a symbol table for readability.
- Weakness: comparisons with GBP are limited to loss function, not ranking quality.
- Weakness: lacks a direct performance comparison with ref:17.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
