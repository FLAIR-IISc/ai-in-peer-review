

The paper proposes two optimization methods for the supervised PageRank problem: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN). Unlike existing approaches, GBN provides a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate for achieving a specified accuracy, while GFN ensures a guaranteed decrease in the loss function. Both methods avoid requiring exact evaluations of the objective function and provide convergence rate estimates, demonstrating superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods. Hyperparameters for both approaches are provided. Although the datasets are not publicly available, the clear description facilitates reproducibility. The two-level optimization framework (GBN and GFN) builds upon Nesterov and Nemirovski's work (ref:17), with lower-level and upper-level optimization leveraging their techniques. The methods avoid explicit computation of large matrices while maintaining strong performance. The paper details the methods in Sections 2–5, with all proofs included in the supplementary material. However, a symbol table would significantly improve readability due to the high density of symbols in equations (e.g., $N$ is used in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 without definition until Line 193, where it is identified as the number of steps). Additionally, the experimental comparison solely evaluates loss function performance against GBP (likely a typo for GBN), neglecting direct comparison of ranking quality. Given the work's generalization of ref:17, a direct performance comparison with this baseline would strengthen the claims.
