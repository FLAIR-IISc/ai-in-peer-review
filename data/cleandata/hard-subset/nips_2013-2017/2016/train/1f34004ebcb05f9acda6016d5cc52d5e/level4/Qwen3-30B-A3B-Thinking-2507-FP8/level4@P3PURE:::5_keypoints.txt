- Introduces a framework for learning node and edge weights in a feature-based PageRank model, motivated by prior work.
- Proposes combining a stationary-distribution approximation with a gradient-free optimization method for loss functions available only via estimation.
- Framework largely reproduces [21] under new terminology and notation, offering little apparent novelty.
- Establishes technical assumptions, defines a loss function, and demonstrates the problem's hardness.
- Provides key lemmas bounding the approximate gradient and analyzes a general gradient-free method using a biased oracle, with a performance theorem.
- Adapts the algorithm for the learning problem under further assumptions and supplies additional error bounds.
- Experimental results are briefly sketched in the main text, with detailed analysis relegated to the appendix.
- Main paper omits critical experimental details (specific results, parameter choices such as φ₀ and R) and lacks validation across diverse graphs.
- The claim of "outperforming" competitors is unsupported by discussion of effect size or practical significance.
- Citation mistake: "Supervised PageRank" reference is inconsistent with [21], which describes semi-supervised PageRank.
- Key technical assumption—restricting φ to an R-ball around a fixed φ₀—is overly restrictive and unjustified for practical applicability.
- Core contributions (proofs, detailed results, experimental analysis) being confined to the appendix undermines the paper’s persuasiveness despite technical rigor.
