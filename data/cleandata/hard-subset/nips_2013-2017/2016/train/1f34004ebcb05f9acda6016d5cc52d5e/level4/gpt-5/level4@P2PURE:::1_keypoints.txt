- Problem: convergence analysis for non-convex loss minimization to learn parameters of a graph-based ranking model (Markov random walk with node/edge weights from feature-based random walks).  
- Challenge: objective cannot be exactly evaluated, so existing methods requiring exact evaluations are inapplicable.  
- Approach — Level 1: use a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain’s stationary distribution and show the loss can be approximated to any prescribed accuracy.  
- Approach — Level 2: develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.  
- Contribution: adaptation of inexact-oracle techniques to constrained problems where the objective is computable only to known precision, and integration into a two-level algorithm.  
- Empirical/technical quality: paper well written and proofs appear correct (not all details verified).  
- Main concern: core optimization techniques largely adapt prior work (Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and supervised PageRank has been proposed before.  
- Minor suggestions: add a conclusion section and present the optimization problem in a more general framework to better position the algorithm relative to prior art.
