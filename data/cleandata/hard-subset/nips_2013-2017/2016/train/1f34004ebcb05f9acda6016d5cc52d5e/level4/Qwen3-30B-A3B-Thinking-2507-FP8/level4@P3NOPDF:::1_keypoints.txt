- Addresses convergence analysis for learning parameters in a graph-based ranking model where transition weights depend on node and edge features.  
- Key challenge: objective cannot be evaluated exactly, so standard optimization methods are inapplicable.  
- Proposes a two-level algorithm: (1) linearly convergent estimator of the Markov chain stationary distribution to approximate the loss; (2) novel gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both algorithmic levels and validates on the ranking model.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and core proofs appear sound.  
- Technical contribution: adapting inexact oracle techniques to constrained non-convex optimization is a useful extension.  
- Novelty concerns: core optimization components are closely aligned with Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015) and need clearer differentiation from that prior convex work.  
- Case study concern: the supervised PageRank model has prior art (e.g., Zhou et al., 2007); authors should clarify whether the model is novel or only the optimization analysis is new.  
- Missing a dedicated conclusion summarizing insights, limitations, and future directions.  
- Recommend reframing the work as a specific instance of constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle to highlight broader relevance.  
- Suggest explicit comparison with related work beyond Nesterov and Spokoiny and clearer positioning of technical advances.  
- Minor grammatical and stylistic edits are needed (several examples provided).  
- Actionable fixes recommended: contrast with prior convex framework, present a general problem formulation situating graph-ranking as an application, and add a concise conclusion.  
- Overall: promising rigorous analysis for a practical problem but risks being seen as an application of existing techniques without stronger contextualization and novelty claims.
