1. Studies convergence of optimization algorithms for supervised PageRank where node/edge weights are feature-driven via a random walk.  
2. Addresses core challenge of non-convex loss and inexact solutions by proposing a two-level optimization scheme.  
3. Lower-level: uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution to a user-specified precision.  
4. Upper-level: develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
5. Adapts the approach to constrained problems where function values are computed with known accuracy and demonstrates convergence in that setting.  
6. Paper is clearly written and proofs appear sound (though not fully verified by reviewer).  
7. Major concern: much of the optimization methodology heavily builds on existing work, notably Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
8. Concern: supervised PageRank framework itself is not novel and is already established.  
9. Novelty of proposed methods relative to existing literature is not clearly articulated.  
10. Experimental results show improvements over state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.  
11. Paper lacks a concluding section summarizing key findings and outlining future directions.  
12. Suggestion: frame the optimization problem in a broader context to highlight how the algorithm fits within a wider class of techniques.  
13. Arguments for acceptance: tackles a challenging problem, provides theoretical convergence guarantees, shows empirical improvements, and is well written.  
14. Arguments against acceptance: core techniques are largely adaptations, the framework lacks novelty, the conclusion is missing, and novelty vs Nesterov & Spokoiny is unclear.
