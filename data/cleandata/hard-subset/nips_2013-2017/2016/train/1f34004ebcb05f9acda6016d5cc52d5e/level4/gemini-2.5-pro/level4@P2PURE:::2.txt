This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that incorporates node and edge properties. To solve this problem, the authors propose two approaches: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

My primary concern is that all experimental results have been relegated to the supplementary material. This makes the main paper incomplete and difficult to evaluate as a self-contained contribution.

Detailed Comments:
1.  There appears to be a contradiction regarding the convexity assumption. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, while the objective function in (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should clarify how the set $\Phi$ can be chosen to guarantee that the objective function satisfies this assumption.
2.  Theorem 3 provides a bound for $M_K(x_K - x_{K+1})$. It is not clear whether this bound is sufficient to ensure that the sequence $\{x_k\}$ converges to a stationary point. This point requires further clarification.
3.  Based on Figure 1 in the supplement, the GFNM algorithm appears to converge slowly. The authors should report on the computational efficiency of their proposed algorithms, including metrics like runtime or the number of iterations required for convergence.
