- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss from learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined via random-walk node/edge weights.  
- Proposes a two-level algorithm: inner level approximates the Markov chain stationary distribution with a linearly convergent method.  
- Shows the inner method can approximate the loss to arbitrary precision despite intractability of exact objective evaluation.  
- Develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Extends the inexact-oracle framework to constrained settings where function values are only known up to a specified precision and applies it in the algorithm.  
- Paper is clearly written and proofs appear sound (full verification not performed).  
- Main concern: core optimization techniques are closely related to Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
- Prior work already introduced supervised PageRank, reducing novelty of the application.  
- Paper lacks a concluding section summarizing results and implications.  
- Would benefit from broader contextual framing showing the proposed algorithm as a specific instance within existing literature to better highlight novelty.
