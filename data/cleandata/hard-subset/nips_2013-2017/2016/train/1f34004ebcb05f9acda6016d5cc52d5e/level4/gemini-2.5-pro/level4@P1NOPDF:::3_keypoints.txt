- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods (GBN — gradient-based, GFN — gradient-free) for supervised PageRank, building on Nesterov & Nemirovski [17].  
- Main contribution: pair of algorithms with strong theoretical properties and convergence guarantees.  
- GBN has a theoretical guarantee on convergence rate to a specified accuracy.  
- GFN is guaranteed to decrease the loss function (gradient-free alternative).  
- Practical advantage: neither method requires exact objective values, avoiding expensive dense-matrix computations.  
- Provides convergence rate estimates and theoretical proofs (in supplementary material).  
- Experiments claim the methods outperform state-of-the-art in ranking quality.  
- Presentation weakness: dense notation and many symbols introduced out of order (e.g., N defined late); a symbol table is recommended.  
- Experimental weakness: comparison to GBP uses loss only, not final ranking quality, which is more meaningful.  
- Experimental weakness: lacks direct empirical comparison with the foundational method [17], despite framing as its generalization.  
- Reproducibility: hyper-parameters are clearly listed; datasets are not public but are well-described to aid reproduction.
