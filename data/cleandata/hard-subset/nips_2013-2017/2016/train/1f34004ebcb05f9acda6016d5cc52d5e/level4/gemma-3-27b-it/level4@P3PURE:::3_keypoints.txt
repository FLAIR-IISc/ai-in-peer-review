- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN).
- GBN offers a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and can reach a specified accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function value.
- Both methods avoid needing exact objective function values and provide estimated convergence rates.
- Methods avoid costly large-matrix computations and build on Nesterov & Nemirovski’s two-level optimization framework.
- Empirically show improved ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- Hyperparameters are clearly reported; dataset descriptions are detailed enough for reproduction despite not being public.
- Paper includes thorough methodological detail and all proofs in the supplementary material.
- Paper is difficult to follow due to numerous symbols; a symbol table is recommended.
- Some symbols (e.g., N) are used before being defined, causing clarity issues.
- Experiments compare to GBP only by loss values and omit ranking-quality comparisons; a direct comparison to ref:17 (the generalized baseline) is missing.
