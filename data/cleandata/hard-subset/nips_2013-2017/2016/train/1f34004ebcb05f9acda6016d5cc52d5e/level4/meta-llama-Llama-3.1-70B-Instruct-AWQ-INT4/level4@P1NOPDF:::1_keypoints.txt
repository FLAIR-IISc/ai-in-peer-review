- Analyzes convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem in a graph-based ranking model defined by feature-driven random walks.  
- The optimization cannot be handled by existing methods that require exact objective values.  
- Proposes a two-level approach: (1) linearly convergent method to approximate the random-walk stationary distribution, (2) gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle.  
- First-level method is experimentally validated and shown to approximate the loss to arbitrary precision.  
- Develops and proves convergence of a gradient method to stationary points for constrained problems when function values are known up to a given precision.  
- Key contribution: adapting inexact-oracle gradient techniques to constrained optimization and combining both levels into a practical algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not exhaustively checked).  
- Major concern: core optimization techniques are adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
- Additional concern: supervised PageRank has been proposed previously.  
- Minor issue: paper lacks a conclusion despite available space.  
- Suggestion: present the optimization problem in a more general context and position the proposed algorithm as a specific case to better highlight novelty.
