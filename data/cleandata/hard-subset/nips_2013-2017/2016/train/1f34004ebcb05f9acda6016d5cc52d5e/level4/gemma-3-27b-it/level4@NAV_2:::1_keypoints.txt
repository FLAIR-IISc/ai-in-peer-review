- Paper studies convergence for non-convex loss minimization to learn parameters of a graph-based ranking model using a random-walk weighted by node/edge features.  
- Key technical challenge: existing methods requiring exact objective values cannot be directly applied.  
- Proposed two-level approach: a linearly convergent routine approximates the Markov chain stationary distribution to arbitrary precision.  
- Second component: a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle, proven to converge to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adapting convergence analysis to constrained problems where function values are computed with known precision, with corresponding theorems.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear sound (full verification not performed).  
- Major concern: core optimization techniques largely extend prior work (notably Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015).  
- Additional concern: supervised PageRank itself was introduced earlier and is not novel here.  
- Missing a concluding section summarizing contributions and future work.  
- Suggestion: reframe the problem in a broader optimization context to better highlight generalizability and position relative to state-of-the-art.  
- Arguments for acceptance: provides theoretical convergence guarantees, addresses inexact oracle in non-convex setting, shows empirical improvements, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments against acceptance: techniques are mostly adaptations, the underlying model is not new, lacks a conclusion, and needs stronger framing of generalizability.
