- Proposes PageRank framework with node/edge weights from a feature model and learning parameters.
- Identifies lack of performance guarantees in prior work and proposes combining stationary-distribution approximation with gradient-free optimization for noisy losses.
- Framework largely reproduces prior work [21] under different nomenclature and notation.
- Paper states many technical assumptions, defines a loss function, and describes core optimization challenges.
- Presents lemmas bounding the approximate gradient and a general gradient-free method using a biased oracle.
- Provides a performance theorem for the gradient-free approach under stated assumptions.
- Introduces further assumptions to derive a practical learning algorithm adapted from the gradient-free method, with additional bounds.
- Reports experiments on a web graph used in related work, but main results and analysis are relegated to the appendix.
- Several experimental parameters are not reported, making the claimed superiority of the novel algorithm unclear.
- Unclear whether evaluation covered diverse graph structures or only a single graph.
- Writing has numerous grammatical errors and imprecise citations (e.g., mislabels semi-supervised PageRank as “Supervised PageRank”).
- Doubts about the significance of the problem advancement and whether technical assumptions hold empirically.
- Some assumptions appear particularly restrictive (e.g., constraining φ search to an R-ball around φ0), potentially limiting applicability.
- Major content is deferred to the appendix; the main text lacks proof outlines and complete empirical results.
- Key experimental details (choice of φ0 and R) and tables/specific results are missing, hindering evaluation.
- Practical impact and consequences of the reported improvements are not clearly demonstrated.
