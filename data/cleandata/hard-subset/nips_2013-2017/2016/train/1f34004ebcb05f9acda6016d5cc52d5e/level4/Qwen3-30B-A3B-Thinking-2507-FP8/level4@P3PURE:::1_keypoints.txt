- Paper studies convergence for non-convex parameter learning in a graph-based ranking model where node/edge weights come from random walks over features.  
- The objective function cannot be evaluated exactly, preventing use of standard optimization methods.  
- Authors propose a two-level algorithm: first level approximates the Markov chain’s stationary distribution with a linearly convergent method.  
- They show the loss can be approximated to arbitrary precision via the stationary-distribution approximation.  
- Second level develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Primary contribution is adapting the inexact-oracle framework to constrained problems with function evaluations available to known precision.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not all details were verified).  
- Major concern: core optimization techniques closely adapt methods from Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
- Additional concern: supervised PageRank algorithm used has been proposed previously.  
- Manuscript lacks a conclusion section despite available space.  
- Recommendation: authors should position their algorithm as an instance of a broader theoretical framework to clarify novelty relative to prior work.
