- Formulates Supervised PageRank with node/edge features as a constrained non-convex optimization problem.
- Proposes two two-level solution approaches: a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.
- Uses a linearly convergent lower-level method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution, claiming the lowest proved complexity bound in the literature.
- Develops a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle and estimates its convergence rate to a stationary point.
- Proposes a gradient-free method for convex problems with inexact zero-order oracle and an adaptive gradient method for possibly nonconvex composite problems, both with convergence analyses.
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity for both proposed algorithms.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides thorough theoretical analysis.
- Experimental results are placed in the supplement; the reviewer requests these be included in the main text.
- Unclear how to choose the set Φ to guarantee convexity used in Theorem 1, since the objective in (2.4) is nonconvex.
- Theorem 3 gives a bound for MK(xK − xK+1) but it is unclear whether this ensures convergence of the sequence xk to a stationary point.
- Supplement Figure 1 suggests GFNM converges slowly; the reviewer requests reporting of algorithmic efficiency.
- Lack of main-text experiments and efficiency metrics weakens empirical validation.
- Originality: the two inexact-oracle methods are novel contributions.
- Significance: rigorous analysis and clear exposition support importance, but empirical and theoretical gaps limit impact.
- Pros for acceptance: new methods, thorough analysis, clear presentation.
- Cons for acceptance: missing main-text experiments, unclear Φ selection for convexity, unresolved xk convergence guarantee, and unreported efficiency.
