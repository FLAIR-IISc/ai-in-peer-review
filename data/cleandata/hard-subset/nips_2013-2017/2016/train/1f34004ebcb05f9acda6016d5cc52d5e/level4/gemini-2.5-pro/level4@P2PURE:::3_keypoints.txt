- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- Main contribution: both methods come with theoretical guarantees uncommon in prior work.
- GBN has a provable convergence rate to a specified accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function value.
- Both methods achieve state-of-the-art ranking quality in experiments.
- Methods do not require exact objective values, avoiding computation of a large matrix while still providing estimated convergence rates.
- Optimization framework builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski [17].
- Authors provide hyper-parameters and clear dataset descriptions (datasets not public), aiding reproducibility.
- Technical exposition is thorough (Sections 2–5) with proofs in the supplementary material.
- Presentation weaknesses: heavy use of symbols makes equations hard to follow (e.g., symbol N defined late); a symbol table is recommended.
- Experimental weaknesses: comparisons are limited to GBP on loss; need ranking-quality comparisons and direct empirical comparison to [17], since the work generalizes that method.
