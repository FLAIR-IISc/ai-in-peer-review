

This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models by formulating it as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that incorporates node and edge features. The authors propose two two-level optimization approaches: a gradient-free method using an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method using an inexact first-order oracle. This work extends the Supervised PageRank framework introduced in [21], which generalizes traditional PageRank by incorporating node and edge features through linear combinations with model parameters. Previous work on learning PageRank models [12, 20, 21] has typically focused on model formulation without providing strong theoretical guarantees for the optimization process. The authors' key contribution is establishing convergence rate guarantees for both optimization methods, which are absent in the state-of-the-art approach [21].

The paper has several strengths. The theoretical contributions are well-motivated, with thorough convergence rate analysis for both methods under the inexact oracle setting, which is crucial for practical implementation given the computational challenges of estimating stationary distributions in large-scale graphs. The numerical experiments demonstrate that both proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality, supporting the authors' claims about their practical advantages.

However, I have several concerns that need addressing:

1. The main text lacks experimental results, with all performance metrics relegated to the supplement. This significantly weakens the paper, as empirical validation is essential for a paper in this domain. The authors should include key results in the main text to support their claims about the superiority of their methods.

2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity of the objective function, but the loss function in Equation (2.4) is explicitly non-convex. The paper does not adequately explain how the authors select the feasible set Φ to guarantee convexity in the region of interest. This is a critical gap since the authors claim their methods work for non-convex problems.

3. Theorem 3 provides a bound on ‖MK(xK - xK+1)‖2, but it's unclear whether this bound ensures convergence to a stationary point. The authors should clarify the relationship between this bound and the convergence properties of the sequence {xk}.

4. The gradient-free method (GFNM) appears to converge slowly based on Figure 1 in the supplement. The authors should provide a more detailed analysis of computational efficiency, including comparisons of iteration counts and time complexity per iteration between the methods.

5. The paper claims both methods "have theoretical guarantees on convergence rate," but fails to specify the exact convergence rates or conditions under which these guarantees hold. This is a key contribution that should be explicitly stated.

Overall, the paper presents a theoretically sound approach to learning Supervised PageRank models with important convergence guarantees. The main weakness is the lack of sufficient empirical results in the main text and the need for clarification regarding the convexity assumption in Theorem 1. With these issues addressed, this paper could make a valuable contribution to the field.

Pro:
- Theoretical convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods
- Clear formulation of the problem as a constrained non-convex optimization problem
- Thorough analysis of the inexact oracle setting

Con:
- Lack of key experimental results in main text
- Unclear how convexity assumption in Theorem 1 reconciles with non-convex loss function
- Insufficient discussion of convergence properties from Theorem 3
- Limited analysis of computational efficiency

I recommend acceptance with major revisions to address the concerns raised above. The paper has strong theoretical foundations, but the empirical validation needs to be properly presented in the main text to support the claims of superior performance.
