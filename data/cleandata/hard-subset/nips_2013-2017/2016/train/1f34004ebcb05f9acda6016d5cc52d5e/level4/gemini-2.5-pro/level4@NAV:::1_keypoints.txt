- Presents convergence analysis for non-convex loss minimization to learn parameters of a complex graph-based ranking model using random-walk-defined node/edge weights.
- Optimization is intractable for methods needing exact objective evaluations, motivating a two-level algorithmic approach.
- Level 1: a linearly convergent solver to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution; validated and able to estimate the loss to arbitrary precision.
- Level 2: a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle; proven to converge to a stationary point.
- Main technical contribution: adapting the inexact-oracle framework to constrained settings where the objective is only approximable with known precision, applied to parameter learning.
- Manuscript is well written and proofs appear sound (not exhaustively checked).
- Primary concern: limited novelty — core optimization techniques resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and the supervised PageRank model is preexisting.
- Minor suggestion: add a conclusion section.
- Minor suggestion: frame the optimization problem in a broader context first, then present the graph-ranking algorithm as a special case to better situate contributions.
