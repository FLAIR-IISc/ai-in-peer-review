- Studies convergence of a non-convex loss minimization for a graph-based ranking model driven by random-walk node/edge weights.  
- Proposes a two-tier approach because existing optimizers requiring exact objective values are inapplicable.  
- Tier 1: uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution and shows loss can be approximated to arbitrary precision.  
- Tier 2: develops a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adapts the inexact-oracle convergence approach to constrained problems where function values have known precision, and applies it in the second tier.  
- Paper is well-structured and proofs appear correct (not exhaustively checked).  
- Concern: key optimization ideas build on prior work (notably Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and supervised PageRank has been proposed before.  
- Minor suggestions: add a conclusion and better contextualize the problem within a broader framework, framing the algorithm as a specific instance relative to state-of-the-art.
