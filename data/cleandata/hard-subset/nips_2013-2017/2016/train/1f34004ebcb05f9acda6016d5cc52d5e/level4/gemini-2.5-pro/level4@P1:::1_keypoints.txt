- Tackles non-convex loss minimization for Supervised PageRank where objective and gradient are inexact.
- Proposes a two-level inexact-oracle approach: lower-level linearly convergent iterative method to compute approximations.
- Upper-level introduces two optimization algorithms: a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex problems and a constraint-adapted random gradient-free method.
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both proposed optimization methods.
- Empirical results on web page ranking show the algorithms outperform the existing state-of-the-art.
- Strength: provides formal convergence guarantees, improving over prior methods that lacked theoretical foundations.
- Strength: paper is well-written and the theoretical analysis/proofs appear correct.
- Strength: adaptation of optimization frameworks for inexact oracles under constraints is a generally useful contribution.
- Weakness: limited originality—core techniques are adaptations of existing methods (e.g., Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and Supervised PageRank itself is not new.
- Suggestion: present the optimization framework in a general context first, then treat Supervised PageRank as an application to clarify novelty.
- Suggestion: add a conclusion section summarizing key results and future directions.
