In this manuscript, the authors introduce two novel optimization techniques to tackle the supervised PageRank problem. Notably, these methods diverge from existing approaches by incorporating a gradient-based technique that theoretically guarantees convergence rate and achieves a specified accuracy. Additionally, the proposed gradient-free method ensures a decrease in the loss function value. A key advantage of these methods is that they do not necessitate the exact objective function value and provide an estimate of the convergence rate, ultimately yielding superior performance compared to state-of-the-art methods in terms of ranking quality. The hyperparameters employed in both methods are explicitly stated. Although the datasets used are not publicly accessible, the descriptions provided are clear, making it feasible to reproduce the results. The authors propose two two-level optimization methods, namely gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN), to solve the supervised PageRank problem, drawing on the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17) for the lower and upper level optimization. A significant advantage of the proposed methods is that they do not require the exact objective function value and provide a proven estimate of the convergence rate for a given accuracy, thereby avoiding the need to compute large matrices while maintaining a good outcome. The authors provide a detailed explanation of their methods in Sections 2-5, including proofs in the supplementary material. However, a symbol table would greatly enhance the readability of the manuscript, as the numerous symbols in the equations can be easily misconstrued. For instance, the symbol $N$ is introduced in Line 139 and equation 3.1 without explanation until Line 193. Furthermore, in the experimental section, the authors only compare their work to GBP in terms of the loss function, whereas a comparison based on ranking quality would be more informative. Given that this work generalizes ref:17, a direct comparison with this reference would be valuable in assessing the performance improvement.
