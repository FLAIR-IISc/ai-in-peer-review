- Paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization using node/edge features.  
- Proposes two solution approaches: a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.  
- Provides thorough analysis and theoretical convergence-rate guarantees for the proposed methods.  
- Experimental results are missing from the main text, making the presentation incomplete.  
- The convexity assumption in Theorem 1 is unjustified because the objective in (2.4) is non-convex and the choice of set Φ to ensure convexity is unclear.  
- Theorem 3 gives a bound for MK(xK − xK+1) but it is unclear whether this implies convergence of xk to a stationary point.  
- Algorithm efficiency is not reported; supplemental Figure 1 indicates GFNM converges slowly.  
- Suggestion: include experimental results in the main text.  
- Suggestion: provide discussion/justification for the convexity assumption in Theorem 1 and how to choose Φ.  
- Suggestion: clarify whether the bound in Theorem 3 implies convergence to a stationary point.  
- Suggestion: report and discuss algorithmic efficiency and runtime comparisons.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form but reconsider after substantial revisions addressing the above issues.
