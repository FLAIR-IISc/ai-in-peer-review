1) Paper addresses learning Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem incorporating node and edge features.

2) Proposes two two-level optimization methods: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

3) Main theoretical contribution: establishes convergence rate guarantees for both methods, filling a gap in prior work [21].

4) Strength: thorough convergence rate analysis under the inexact oracle setting, important for large-scale graph implementations.

5) Strength: numerical experiments (in supplement) show both methods outperform state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality.

6) Major concern: key experimental results are omitted from the main text and placed in the supplement, weakening empirical validation.

7) Major concern: Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the loss is explicitly non-convex; the feasible set Φ and how convexity is guaranteed are not explained.

8) Major concern: Theorem 3 bounds ‖MK(xK - xK+1)‖2, but it's unclear whether this implies convergence to a stationary point or the behavior of {xk}.

9) Major concern: Gradient-free method (GFNM) appears to converge slowly (per supplemental Figure 1); computational efficiency and per-iteration complexity comparisons are missing.

10) Major concern: paper claims convergence rate guarantees but fails to explicitly state the exact convergence rates and the conditions under which they hold.

11) Overall: theoretically sound and promising, but requires major revisions to present main-text empirical results and clarify theoretical assumptions and convergence interpretations.

12) Reviewer recommendation: accept with major revisions.
