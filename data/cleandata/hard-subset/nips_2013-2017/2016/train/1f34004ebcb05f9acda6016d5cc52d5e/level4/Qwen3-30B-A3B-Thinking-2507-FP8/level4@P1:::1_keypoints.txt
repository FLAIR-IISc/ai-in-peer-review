- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank models by incorporating node/edge features and casting parameter learning as a non-convex loss-minimization problem.  
- Identifies a gap in Zhukovskii et al. [21]: lack of convergence guarantees due to approximate stationary distribution and derivative estimates.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) that provide theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Technical contribution 1: a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution to any desired accuracy with the lowest known complexity bound.  
- Technical contribution 2: a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and convergence-rate analysis to stationary points.  
- Technical contribution 3: a gradient-free method adapted to constrained problems with function values computed to known accuracy.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis and complexity bounds, including trade-offs between lower-level accuracy and overall computational cost.  
- Empirical results show both proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art [21] on a real-world web page ranking task.  
- Paper is well-structured and the mathematical arguments appear sound.  
- Effectively connects to related work and extends Nesterov & Spokoiny [25] to constrained settings, though the connection is somewhat implicit.  
- Strength: first theoretical convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning, combining theory and practical performance.  
- Strength: elegant two-level framework separating stationary-distribution approximation from upper-level optimization.  
- Weakness: need to more explicitly highlight how the constrained framework extends the unconstrained setting of [25].  
- Weakness: conclusion is brief and should better synthesize broader implications for large-scale graph learning.  
- Weakness: experiments show numerical superiority but lack deeper explanation of why the methods outperform [21].  
- Overall concern: insufficient positioning of the contribution within broader optimization literature; a more detailed comparison would strengthen the paper.
