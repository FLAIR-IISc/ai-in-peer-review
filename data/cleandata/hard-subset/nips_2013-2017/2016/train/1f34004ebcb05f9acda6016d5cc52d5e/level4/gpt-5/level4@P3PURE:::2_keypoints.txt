1. Formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained, non-convex optimization using node/edge features and proposes two solvers: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

2. Major concern: No experimental results appear in the main paper; all empirical evaluation is relegated to the supplement, making the submission feel incomplete and hard to assess practically.

3. Empirical results should be moved into the main text since relying on the supplement prevents proper evaluation of effectiveness and practicality.

4. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the objective is non-convex—clarify how the feasible set Φ (and any other components) is chosen so the theorem’s convexity assumption holds.

5. Theorem 3 bounds M_K(x^K − x^{K+1}), but it’s unclear whether this guarantees convergence to a stationary point—specify the connection or state additional assumptions required.

6. Supplementary Figure 1 suggests GFNM converges slowly; report algorithmic efficiency metrics (iteration counts, oracle calls, wall-clock time) for both methods to enable fair practical comparison.

7. Overall: The formulation and inexact-oracle approach are interesting, but missing main-text experiments and unresolved theoretical clarifications limit clarity and evaluability; addressing these would substantially strengthen the paper.
