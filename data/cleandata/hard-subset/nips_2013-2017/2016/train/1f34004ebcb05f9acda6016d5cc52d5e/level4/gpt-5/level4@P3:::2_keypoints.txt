- Studies learning Supervised PageRank by optimizing restart and transition parameters via a constrained, generally nonconvex formulation with inexact oracles.  
- Proposes a two-level framework: a zero-order (random gradient-free) upper method and an adaptive first-order projected gradient upper method, both using inexact oracles.  
- Uses a linearly convergent Nesterov–Nemirovski iteration to approximate query-dependent stationary distributions and extends it to compute derivatives.  
- Adapts Nesterov–Spokoiny random gradient-free optimization to constrained problems with inexact function values.  
- Develops an inexact-oracle projected gradient scheme with backtracking for nonconvex composite objectives and unknown Lipschitz constant.  
- Provides convergence rate bounds and complexity estimates that trade off lower-level accuracy and upper-level progress.  
- Empirical evaluation on Yandex browsing graphs shows improved ranking loss over prior power-iteration-based Supervised PageRank training.  
- Clear optimization-centric framing and explicit inexact-oracle modeling are strengths.  
- Two complementary upper-level methods (zero-order and adaptive first-order) are a notable contribution.  
- Complexity results explicitly expose the accuracy–cost trade-off and are informative.  
- Lower-level use of a linearly convergent iteration and derivative extension improves efficiency over power iterations.  
- Experimental results are mostly in the supplement; main text lacks key tables/figures, wall-clock times, and robustness metrics.  
- The zero-order method (Theorem 1) assumes convexity on a neighborhood Φ, but the training loss is generally nonconvex and nonsmooth; Φ selection and verification are not addressed.  
- Theorem 3’s bound on MK(xK − xK+1) should be connected explicitly to projected gradient mapping and first-order stationarity.  
- Gradient derivation appears to differentiate through the positive-part operator; the paper should clarify subgradient use or smoothing for nondifferentiable points.  
- Efficiency reporting is incomplete: missing wall-clock times, lower-level iteration counts (N, N1, N2), and total oracle calls; gradient-free method seems slow in iterations.  
- Several constants and assumptions (e.g., β1) are deferred to the supplement without intuition; notation and typos hurt readability.  
- The theoretical development is largely sound, but key assumptions (local convexity, nonsmoothness handling, stationarity measure) need explicit statement and justification.  
- Recommend moving primary experimental results into main text and reporting wall-clock times, oracle calls, and lower-level iteration counts.  
- Suggest explicitly defining the stationarity measure, clarifying nonsmoothness handling, and making local convexity assumptions and Φ-selection guidance explicit.  
- Ask authors to provide empirical checks for local convexity (e.g., Hessian PSD), clarify subgradient vs smoothing choices, and report sensitivity to α, R, and L/L0.  
- Request comparisons to other supervised random walk and learning-to-rank baselines, plus broader robustness across query subsets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, fills a theoretical gap in Supervised PageRank training, and presents thoughtful inexact-oracle analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficiently justified assumptions (zero-order convexity, nonsmoothness), underreported experiments especially runtime, and slow practical convergence of gradient-free method.
