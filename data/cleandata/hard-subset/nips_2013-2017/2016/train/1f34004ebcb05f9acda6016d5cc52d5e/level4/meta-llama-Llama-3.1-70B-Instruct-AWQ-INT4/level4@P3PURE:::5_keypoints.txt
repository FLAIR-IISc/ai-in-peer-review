- Paper extends PageRank to include node/edge weights from a feature model and criticizes prior learning algorithm for lacking guarantees.  
- Proposes a novel method combining stationary-distribution approximation for Markov walks with a gradient-free optimizer for noisy loss estimates.  
- Reproduces a framework from [21] under different notation and states technical assumptions and a loss function, highlighting solution challenges.  
- Presents lemmas that bound the approximate gradient and a general gradient-free method using a biased oracle, with a performance theorem.  
- Adapts the gradient-free method to the specific learning problem under additional assumptions and provides further bounds.  
- Experimental results are briefly summarized in the paper but detailed results and analysis are deferred to the appendix.  
- Important experimental parameters are missing, and the paper lacks discussion on the magnitude and implications of the reported improvements.  
- Unclear whether experiments used diverse graphs or more than the one web graph from related work.  
- Writing suffers from grammatical errors and sloppy citations (e.g., mislabeling [21] as Supervised PageRank when it discusses semi-supervised PageRank).  
- Doubts about the practical relevance due to potentially unrealistic technical assumptions (e.g., restricting φ to an R-ball around φ0) that may limit applicability.  
- Major concern: essential details, proofs, and empirical evidence are relegated to the appendix, leaving the main text unconvincing about the work's significance.
