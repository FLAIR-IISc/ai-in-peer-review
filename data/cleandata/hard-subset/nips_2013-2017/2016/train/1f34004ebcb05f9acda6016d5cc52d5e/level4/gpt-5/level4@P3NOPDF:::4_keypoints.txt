- Studies supervised PageRank with a non-convex loss and proposes a gradient-based and a randomized gradient-free optimizer for when gradients are hard to compute or unavailable.
- Topic is timely and relevant to large-scale link analysis, ranking, and graph mining.

- Strength: tackles a practical problem of optimizing supervised PageRank under non-convexity.
- Strength: offers complementary algorithms for regimes with reliable gradients and for zeroth-order settings, increasing applicability.
- Strength: randomized gradient-free method is useful when gradients are noisy/costly and can leverage parallel function evaluations.
- Strength: positioned at the intersection of large-scale optimization and ranking, with potential IR impact.

- Weakness: empirical evaluation omits clear computational-efficiency reporting (wall-clock time, per-iteration time, convergence speed).
- Weakness: unclear comparison to strong baselines (SGD/Adam/Adagrad, stochastic variants, existing supervised PageRank or LTR methods) in both performance and efficiency.
- Weakness: objective, assumptions, and differentiability details are imprecise (how gradients through PageRank are computed, damping/restarts, feature parametrization).
- Weakness: convergence behavior and stopping criteria for the non-convex problem are insufficiently discussed (stationarity measures, tolerances, iteration counts).
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (step sizes, smoothing for zeroth-order, batch sizes) appears underexplored.
- Weakness: reproducibility details are limited or missing (datasets, preprocessing, exact model specs, random seeds, hardware).

- Request: report comprehensive runtime metrics (wall-clock to target, time/iteration, iterations to stop) and hardware/implementation details.
- Request: compare both methods against strong gradient-based and supervised PageRank baselines, reporting runtime and final performance.
- Request: include ablations on initialization, gradient accuracy, sampling dimension, smoothing/noise, and number of function evaluations.
- Request: clarify optimization details (step-size strategy, momentum, stopping criteria, how gradients are computed/approximated, constraint/regularization handling).
- Request: expand experiments with standard benchmarks, ranking metrics (NDCG/MAP), statistical testing, scalability plots, and memory-usage reporting.
- Request: improve presentation by precisely defining the model/feature map/loss, summarizing theoretical properties and assumptions, streamlining notation, and adding short algorithm sketches.

- Overall: promising contribution (especially the gradient-free approach) but currently lacking sufficient empirical evidence of computational efficiency and comparative advantage for large-scale, real-world deployment.
