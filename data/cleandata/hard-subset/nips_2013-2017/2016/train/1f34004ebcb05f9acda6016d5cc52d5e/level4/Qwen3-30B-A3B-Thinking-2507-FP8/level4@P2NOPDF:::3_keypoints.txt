- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- GBN has a proven convergence rate achieving specified accuracy; GFN guarantees loss decrease without exact objective evaluations.
- Both methods avoid explicit computation of large matrices while providing convergence rate estimates.
- Theoretical foundations build on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s framework for lower-level optimization; upper-level solved by the proposed algorithms.
- Convergence guarantees for both methods are rigorously established; detailed proofs provided in the supplement.
- Empirically outperform state-of-the-art baselines in ranking quality; hyperparameters are clearly specified.
- Datasets are not public but experimental descriptions are detailed enough for reproducibility.
- Manuscript lacks a symbol table and some symbols (e.g., N) are defined later, hindering readability.
- Experimental comparisons focus on loss vs GBP; need explicit ranking-metric comparisons (e.g., NDCG, MAP) against GBP and ref:17.
- Should include runtime and scalability measurements to substantiate claims about avoiding large matrix computations.
- Overall assessment: novel, technically solid, relevant to the community; recommend acceptance pending the above clarifications.
