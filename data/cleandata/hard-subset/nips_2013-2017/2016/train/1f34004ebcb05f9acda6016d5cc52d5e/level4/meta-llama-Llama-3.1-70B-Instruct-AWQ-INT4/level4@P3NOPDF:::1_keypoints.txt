- Paper studies convergence for non-convex loss minimization in a graph-based ranking model defined by random walks with feature-dependent node/edge weights.  
- Existing optimization methods are inapplicable because they require exact objective values.  
- Authors propose a two-level algorithm: inner level linearly converges to the Markov chain stationary distribution; outer level performs constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle.  
- They show the loss can be approximated to arbitrary precision by their stationary-distribution estimator.  
- They develop and prove convergence of a gradient method for constrained non-convex problems using an inexact oracle, converging to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution is adapting and proving this two-level approach for constrained problems where function values are computed with known precision.  
- Paper is well written and proofs appear correct (not exhaustively checked).  
- Primary novelty concern: key optimization ideas resemble Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) adaptations.  
- Prior work concern: supervised PageRank has been proposed before, reducing novelty of the application.  
- Suggested improvements: add a conclusion section and present the optimization problem in a more general context to better situate the contribution within state of the art.
