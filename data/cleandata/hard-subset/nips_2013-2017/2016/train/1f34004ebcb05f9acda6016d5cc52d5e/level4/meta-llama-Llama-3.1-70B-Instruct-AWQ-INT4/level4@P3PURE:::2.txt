This paper formulates the Supervised PageRank model as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

My major complaint is that the paper lacks experiment results in the main text, rendering it incomplete. Below, I provide some detailed comments.

1. The decision to relegate experiment results to the supplement makes the main text incomplete. It would be beneficial to include at least a summary of key findings in the main text to provide a comprehensive understanding of the proposed methods.

2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, whereas the function in (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should clarify how to choose the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function.

3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. However, it is unclear whether this bound ensures that the sequence $xk$ converges to a stationary point. Further discussion or analysis on this aspect would strengthen the theoretical contribution of the paper.

4. Figure 1 in the supplement suggests that the Gradient-Free Newton Method (GFNM) converges slowly. To provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms, the authors may also want to report their efficiency, in addition to their convergence properties.
