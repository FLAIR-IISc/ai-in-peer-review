- Introduces a supervised PageRank framework combining node and edge features via linear parameterized feature vectors.  
- Identifies that prior work [21] lacks theoretical convergence guarantees for parameter learning.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization methods using an inexact-oracle framework to fill that gap.  
- Combines a linearly convergent lower-level Markov stationary distribution solver with gradient-free and gradient-based upper-level methods.  
- Provides convergence rate guarantees and detailed complexity bounds for both algorithms, unlike prior work.  
- Analyzes trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements on a web-ranking task compared to [21].  
- Strength: Strong theoretical contribution with rigorous convergence analyses for both methods.  
- Strength: Careful complexity bounds and analysis of accuracy–cost trade-offs.  
- Strength: Experimental results show consistent improvement over the state of the art.  
- Weakness: Main paper omits key empirical details, pushing critical experimental parameters (e.g., ϕ₀ and R) to the appendix.  
- Weakness: Mislabels the problem as “Supervised PageRank” while [21] describes “semi-supervised,” causing citation/context confusion.  
- Weakness: Restricting parameters to a Euclidean ball around a chosen ϕ₀ may limit practicality and is not well justified.  
- Weakness: Multiple grammatical errors hurt readability and presentation quality.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of real-world search engine impact and lack of details on testing diverse graph structures.  
- For acceptance: Convergence guarantees address a clear prior limitation and theoretical contributions are significant, supported by empirical gains.  
- Against acceptance: Poor presentation, missing main-paper empirical evidence, inconsistent labeling/citations, and potentially limiting assumptions weaken the paper’s impact.
