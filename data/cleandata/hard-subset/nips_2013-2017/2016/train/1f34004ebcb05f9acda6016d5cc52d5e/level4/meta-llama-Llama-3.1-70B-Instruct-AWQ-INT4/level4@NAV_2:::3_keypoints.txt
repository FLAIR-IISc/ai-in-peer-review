- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- GBN is a gradient-based method with a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee that can achieve a given accuracy.  
- GFN is a gradient-free method with a guaranteed decrease in the loss function.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective values and provide estimates of convergence rates.  
- Methods avoid computing the large matrix while maintaining good results.  
- Reported performance is better than state-of-the-art in ranking quality.  
- Hyper-parameters for both methods are provided.  
- Data sets are not publicly available, but descriptions are clear and likely reproducible.  
- The two-level optimization builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref. 17).  
- Sections 2–5 explain methods in detail and all proofs are in the supplementary material.  
- Reviewer requests a symbol table because many symbols are used and some (e.g., N) are not defined until later.  
- Experiments compare to GBP only on loss, not ranking quality; reviewer suggests also comparing directly to ref. 17.
