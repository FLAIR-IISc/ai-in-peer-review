This paper proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN). Unlike prior approaches, the gradient-based method comes with a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee and can reach a prescribed accuracy. The gradient-free method guarantees a decrease in the loss function. Both methods avoid requiring exact evaluations of the objective function and include estimates of their convergence rates. The reported ranking quality outperforms the state of the art. The authors provide the hyperparameters used in both methods. Although the datasets are not publicly available, the descriptions are sufficiently detailed that reproducing the results should be feasible.

The lower- and upper-level procedures build on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref: 17). By not relying on exact objective values, the approach avoids forming large matrices while still achieving strong results. Sections 2 through 5 present the methods in detail, with all proofs deferred to the supplementary material.

A few clarity issues remain. A symbol table would help, as the notation is dense and easy to lose track of. For instance, N appears in Equation (3.1) and around Line 139, but its meaning (number of steps) is not clarified until Line 193. In the experiments, the paper compares against GBP primarily on the loss function rather than on ranking quality. Moreover, since this work generalizes ref: 17, a direct empirical comparison to ref: 17 would be valuable to assess whether the performance is indeed improved.
