This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that incorporates node and edge properties. To solve this problem, the authors propose two algorithms: a gradient-free method utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

While the proposed formulation and algorithms are interesting, the paper suffers from a major structural flaw: the main text is not self-contained. All experimental results have been placed in the supplementary material. For a conference paper, it is essential that the main body contains sufficient empirical evidence to support its claims. This omission makes it very difficult to assess the practical performance and significance of the proposed methods, rendering the paper incomplete in its current form.

Detailed Comments:

1.  **Absence of Experiments in the Main Text:** As noted above, the primary weakness is that the experimental evaluation is entirely absent from the main paper. The main text should include at least a summary of the key results, datasets, and comparisons to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approaches. The current structure forces the reader to switch between documents to understand the full contribution, which is not appropriate for a conference submission.

2.  **Clarity on Convexity Assumption:** There appears to be a contradiction in the theoretical analysis. Theorem 1 relies on a convexity assumption, but the objective function defined in Equation (2.4) is generally non-convex. The authors should clarify the conditions under which the objective function becomes convex. For instance, is there a specific way to choose the set $\Phi$ that guarantees this property? Without such clarification, the applicability of Theorem 1 to the problem at hand is questionable.

3.  **Convergence Guarantees:** The convergence analysis needs to be more conclusive. Theorem 3 provides a bound on the difference between successive iterates, $\|x_k - x_{k+1}\|$. However, it is not clear whether this bound is sufficient to guarantee that the sequence $\{x_k\}$ converges to a stationary point. The authors should provide a formal proof of convergence to a stationary point, which is a standard and critical result for algorithms designed for non-convex optimization.

4.  **Algorithm Efficiency:** Based on Figure 1 in the supplementary material, the Gradient-Free Nesterov's Method (GFNM) appears to converge slowly. To provide a comprehensive evaluation, the authors should report on the computational efficiency of their proposed algorithms. This could include metrics like wall-clock time or the number of oracle calls required to reach a solution, compared against relevant baselines. This would give readers a clearer understanding of the practical trade-offs of the methods.
