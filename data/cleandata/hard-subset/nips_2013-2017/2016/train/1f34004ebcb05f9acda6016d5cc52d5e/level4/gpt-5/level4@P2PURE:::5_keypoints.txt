- Repackages the framework of [21] under different name/notation rather than offering a clearly novel formulation.
- Proposes combining a stationary-distribution approximation with a gradient-free optimization method for parameter learning.
- Presents technical assumptions, defines a challenging loss, and states lemmas bounding the approximate gradient.
- Gives a general biased gradient-free oracle method and a performance theorem, then adapts it to the learning problem with additional assumptions and bounds.
- Experimental section is terse, uses a single web graph from prior work, with full results and parameter settings deferred to the appendix.
- Missing reported parameters and no discussion of the magnitude or implications of claimed performance improvements.
- Unclear whether evaluations cover diverse graphs or multiple datasets.
- Writing has numerous grammatical errors and sloppy citations (labels problem “Supervised PageRank” though [21] addresses semi-supervised PageRank).
- Doubts about the practical impact and whether the technical assumptions hold on real data.
- Some assumptions are restrictive (e.g., constraining phi to an R-ball around phi_0) and may limit applicability; selection of phi_0 and R is unexplained.
- Major concern: substantive proofs and empirical evidence are relegated to the appendix, leaving the main paper unpersuasive.
