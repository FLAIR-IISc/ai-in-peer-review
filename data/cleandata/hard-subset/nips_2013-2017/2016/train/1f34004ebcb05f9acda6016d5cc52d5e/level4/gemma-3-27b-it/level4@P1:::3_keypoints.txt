- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.
- Both methods come with theoretical convergence guarantees and can reach a specified accuracy.
- Methods operate with an inexact oracle (do not require exact objective values) and provide convergence rate estimates.
- Potentially reduce computational burden by avoiding large matrix operations while maintaining good results.
- Experimental results claim improved ranking quality over state-of-the-art methods.
- Hyperparameters are clearly reported; datasets are not public but sufficiently described for reproduction.
- Paper builds on and generalizes Nesterov & Nemirovski [17], so a direct comparison to that baseline is important but missing.
- Presentation suffers from symbol overload and inconsistent notation (e.g., ‘N’ used before definition); a symbol table is needed.
- Experiments focus mainly on loss reduction rather than direct ranking-quality metrics.
- Proofs and detailed method explanations are provided in the supplementary material.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on addressing clarity (symbol table) and adding a direct comparison to [17].
