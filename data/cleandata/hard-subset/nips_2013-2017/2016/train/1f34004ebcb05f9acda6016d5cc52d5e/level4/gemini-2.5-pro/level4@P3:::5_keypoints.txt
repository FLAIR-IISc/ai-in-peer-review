- Paper tackles learning parameters for Supervised PageRank and identifies lack of theoretical convergence guarantees in prior work.
- Introduces two novel optimization methods: an adaptive gradient-based algorithm and a random gradient-free algorithm for the non-convex loss.
- Develops an inexact-oracle optimization framework acknowledging approximated loss and gradient computations.
- Combines an efficient iterative scheme for approximating the Markov chain stationary distribution with adapted general-purpose optimization techniques.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis for both methods, including convergence rates and complexity bounds.
- Empirical comparison against the baseline [21] on a web page ranking task shows proposed methods (GFN, GBN) outperform GBP in reported metrics.
- Major strength: first theoretical convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank, advancing theoretical understanding.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated algorithms—gradient-free useful when gradients are expensive; adaptive gradient-based handles inexact first-order oracle without knowing Lipschitz constant.
- Strength: addresses an important practical problem (feature-based graph ranking) with clear applications.
- Major weakness: paper is not self-contained and relies heavily on supplementary material; key algorithms and proofs missing from main text.
- Experimental weaknesses: insufficient detail (e.g., choice of initial ϕ̂ and radius R), limited dataset diversity, and lack of discussion connecting loss improvements to ranking metrics like NDCG.
- Assumptions (local convexity, parameter space constrained to a ball) are not well-motivated or analyzed for practical impact and sensitivity.
- Presentation issues: numerous grammatical errors and inconsistent terminology (e.g., citation naming), reducing readability.
- Recommendation: revise to include proof sketches, detailed algorithm descriptions, expanded experimental results and discussion, clarification of experimental setup and assumptions; reviewer would reconsider after such revisions.
