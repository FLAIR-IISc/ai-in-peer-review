- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Gradient-based method offers provable convergence rate to a specified accuracy; gradient-free method guarantees loss decrease.
- Both methods operate without requiring exact objective function values and estimate their convergence rates.
- Methods avoid computing large matrices, maintaining strong performance and efficiency.
- Experimental results show improved ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- Hyperparameter settings are clearly reported for reproducibility.
- Datasets are not publicly available, but dataset descriptions are detailed enough to facilitate reproduction.
- Methods build on and generalize Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref:17); direct comparison to that baseline is missing.
- Paper includes detailed method descriptions and proofs in the supplementary material.
- Presentation is hard to follow due to numerous symbols; a symbol table is recommended.
- Some symbols (e.g., ‘N’ for number of steps) are used before being defined, hurting clarity.
- Experiments primarily compare loss values to GBP rather than directly evaluating ranking quality, limiting empirical assessment.
