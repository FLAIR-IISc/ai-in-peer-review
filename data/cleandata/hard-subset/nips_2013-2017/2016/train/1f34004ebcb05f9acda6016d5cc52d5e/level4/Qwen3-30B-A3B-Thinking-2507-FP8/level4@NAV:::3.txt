

This paper introduces two optimization approaches—gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN)—to solve the supervised PageRank problem. Unlike prior methods, GBN provides a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate to achieve a specified accuracy, while GFN ensures a guaranteed decrease in the loss function. Both methods avoid requiring exact objective function evaluations, offer convergence rate estimates, and demonstrate superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art techniques. Hyper-parameters for both methods are clearly provided. Although the datasets are not publicly available, the methodological description is sufficiently detailed for reproducibility. The two-level optimization framework (lower-level Nesterov/Nemirovski-based, upper-level) is rigorously developed in Sections 2–5, with all proofs in the supplementary material. However, a symbol table would significantly enhance readability due to the high density of symbols (e.g., $N$ in Line 139/Equation 3.1 is undefined until Line 193). Additionally, the experimental comparison with GBP solely focuses on loss function performance rather than ranking quality, and a direct comparison with ref:17 (the foundational work generalized here) would better contextualize the performance gains.
