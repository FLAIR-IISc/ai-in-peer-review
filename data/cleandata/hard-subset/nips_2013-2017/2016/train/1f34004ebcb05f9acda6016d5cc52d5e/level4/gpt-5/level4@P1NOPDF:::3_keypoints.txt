- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- GBN has a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee and can achieve a prescribed accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss.
- Both methods operate without requiring exact objective evaluations and include provable convergence-rate estimates.
- The approach avoids explicit computation of large matrices while still yielding strong results.
- Hyperparameters used in experiments are specified.
- Datasets are not publicly available but are described well enough to allow reproduction.
- Reports empirical performance better than state of the art in ranking quality.
- Methodology builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref. 17); detailed methods in Sections 2–5 and proofs in the supplement.
- Presentation suffers from notation management issues; a symbol table is recommended.
- Experimental evaluation is weak in comparisons: mainly compares to GBP on loss rather than ranking metrics and lacks a direct comparison to ref. 17.
