- Introduces two novel two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- GBN guarantees a theoretical convergence rate and achieves a specified accuracy.  
- GFN ensures a decrease in the loss function value.  
- Both methods do not require the exact objective value and provide estimated convergence rates, avoiding large matrix computations.  
- Reported to outperform state-of-the-art methods in ranking quality.  
- Hyperparameters for both methods are thoroughly documented.  
- Datasets used are not publicly available but are described in enough detail to permit replication.  
- Methods are grounded in Nesterov and Nemirovski (reference 17) and build on their two-level optimization framework.  
- Methodology is thoroughly explained (Sections 2–5) with proofs provided in the supplementary material.  
- Review recommends adding a symbol table due to many confusing symbols (e.g., N introduced without immediate clarification).  
- Experiments mainly compare to GBP on loss rather than ranking quality; a direct comparison to reference 17 is suggested to confirm performance gains.
