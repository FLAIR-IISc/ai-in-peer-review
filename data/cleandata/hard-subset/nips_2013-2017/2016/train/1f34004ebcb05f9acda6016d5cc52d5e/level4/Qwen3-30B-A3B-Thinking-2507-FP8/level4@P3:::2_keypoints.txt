- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem incorporating node and edge features.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization methods: a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.  
- Provides convergence rate guarantees for both methods, improving over prior work [21] which lacks such theoretical guarantees.  
- Develops a framework for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles, extending ideas from [16] and [7].  
- Establishes clear complexity bounds useful for practical implementation on large-scale graphs.  
- Empirical results show both methods outperform state-of-the-art [21], with the gradient-based method significantly better on 50% of test queries (p < 0.005).  
- Technically sound analysis that carefully studies trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Problem formulation and experimental setup are well-motivated by real-world web search ranking applications.  
- Major weakness: key experimental results are in the supplement, making the main text incomplete; key performance comparisons and convergence plots should be moved to the main text.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite the stated non-convex loss; insufficient justification is given for choosing Φ to guarantee local convexity near a minimum.  
- Theorem 3 bounds ‖MK(xK - xK+1)‖2 rather than directly showing convergence to a stationary point; the connection to stationary convergence needs clarification.  
- Empirical discrepancy: gradient-free method converges more slowly than gradient-based in Figure 1, contradicting the claimed advantage of avoiding derivatives; needs a balanced assessment of efficiency.  
- Well-written, well-organized, and properly situated within related graph-based ranking literature.  
- Recommendation: valuable contribution but requires major revision to include key experiments in the main text, justify convexity assumptions, and clarify convergence properties.
