In this paper, the authors propose two optimization methods to solve the supervised PageRank problem. Unlike other existing methods, this paper theoretically proposes a gradient-based method with guaranteed convergence rate and can achieve a certain accuracy. The proposed gradient-free method has a guaranteed loss function reduction value. Both of these methods do not require an exact value of the objective function and estimate the convergence rate, while the performance in terms of ranking quality is better than state-of-the-art. Hyper-parameters used in both methods are provided. The data sets are not publicly available, but the details are clear, so it may not be difficult to reproduce their results. The authors proposed two two-level optimization methods to solve the supervised PageRank problem, namely gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN). The lower-level and upper-level optimization are based on the work of Nesterov and Nemirovsky (reference:17). The proposed method does not require an exact value of the objective function and has a proven estimate of the convergence rate given the accuracy. Therefore, the proposed method can avoid computing large matrices, yet the result will be good. From section 2 to section 5, the authors explained the details of their methods and included all the evidence in the supplement. However, it would be very helpful if the authors could provide a symbol table because there are many symbols in their equations and it is really easy to keep track of them. For example, in line 139 and equation 3.1, the authors used $N$ and they did not mention its meaning (number of steps) until line 193. Furthermore, in the experiment section, the authors compared their work with GBP only in relation to the loss function rather than ranking quality. Also, since this work is a generalization of reference:17, I think it would be good if the authors could directly compare it so we can see if the performance is better or not.
