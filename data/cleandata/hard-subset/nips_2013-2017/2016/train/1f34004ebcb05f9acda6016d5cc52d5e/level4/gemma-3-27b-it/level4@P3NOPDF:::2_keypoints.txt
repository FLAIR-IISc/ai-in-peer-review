- Proposed formulation: Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization using node and edge properties, with two solvers (gradient-free zero-order and gradient-based first-order inexact oracles).  
- Critical weakness: All experimental results are relegated to the supplementary material, leaving the main text without empirical evaluation.  
- Main-text requirement: The paper should include a concise, comprehensive summary of experimental findings, baseline comparisons, and trend discussion.  
- Convexity concern: Theorem 1 assumes convexity but equation (2.4) appears non-convex; clarify how the choice of Φ affects convexity and any trade-off with feature expressiveness.  
- Convergence concern: Theorem 3’s bound (MK(xK − x_{K+1})) is not clearly shown to imply convergence to a stationary point; explicitly connect the bound to convergence guarantees.  
- Performance/efficiency concern: Supplement shows slow convergence for the gradient-free method; main text must discuss this and provide quantitative efficiency metrics (runtime, memory, scalability, oracle calls) and compare both methods’ costs.  
- Notation clarification: Explicitly define symbols like M and K in Theorem 3 and related statements.  
- Strength: Novel and potentially useful formulation integrating node/edge properties into Supervised PageRank.  
- Strength: Algorithmic contribution offering both gradient-free and gradient-based options for different settings.  
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis including convergence bounds.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; require main-text experiments, convexity/convergence clarifications, and efficiency/scalability discussion for reconsideration.
