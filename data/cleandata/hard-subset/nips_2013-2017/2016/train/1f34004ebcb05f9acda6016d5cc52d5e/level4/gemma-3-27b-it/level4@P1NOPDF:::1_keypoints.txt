- Paper studies convergence for learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by feature-driven random walks, a supervised PageRank-style setup.
- Optimization is challenging because exact objective evaluations are unavailable; authors use a two-level approach to address this.
- Level 1: propose a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk and show the loss can be approximated arbitrarily well.
- Level 2: develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.
- Key contribution: adapting inexact-oracle optimization techniques to constrained problems where function values are known only up to a given precision, and applying this to the ranking model.
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not all details verified).
- Main concern: core optimization techniques are adaptations of existing methods from Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015).
- Additional concern: supervised PageRank concept has prior work in the literature, raising novelty questions.
- Minor suggestions: include a concluding section summarizing results and future work.
- Minor suggestion: present the optimization problem in a broader context, framing the algorithm as a special case to better position novelty.
