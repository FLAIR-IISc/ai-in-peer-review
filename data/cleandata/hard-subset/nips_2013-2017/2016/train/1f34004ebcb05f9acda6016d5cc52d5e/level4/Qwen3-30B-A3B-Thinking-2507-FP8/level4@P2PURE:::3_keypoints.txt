- Proposes two optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN).
- GBN offers a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate to achieve a specified accuracy.
- GFN ensures a guaranteed decrease in the loss function.
- Both methods avoid requiring exact objective evaluations and provide convergence rate estimates.
- Empirically demonstrates superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods.
- Hyperparameters for both GBN and GFN are provided.
- Datasets are not publicly available, but the paper’s clear descriptions facilitate reproducibility.
- Two-level optimization framework builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17), using their lower- and upper-level techniques.
- Methods avoid explicit computation of large matrices while maintaining strong performance.
- Methods and derivations are detailed in Sections 2–5, with all proofs in the supplementary material.
- Readability suffers from high symbol density; a symbol table is recommended (e.g., N used before being defined).
- Experiments compare loss only (against “GBP,” likely a typo for GBN) and neglect direct ranking-quality metrics.
- Lacks direct performance comparison with the ref:17 baseline, which would strengthen the paper’s claims.
