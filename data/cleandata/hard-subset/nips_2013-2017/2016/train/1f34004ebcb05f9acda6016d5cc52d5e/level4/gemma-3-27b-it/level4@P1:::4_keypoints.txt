- Proposes two two-level methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for non-convex loss minimization in Supervised PageRank.  
- Targets a gap where existing methods lack convergence guarantees.  
- Builds on the inexact oracle concept to handle intractable exact computations.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both proposed algorithms.  
- Includes analysis of the accuracy required from the lower-level algorithm for overall convergence.  
- Presents trade-off analysis between lower-level accuracy, computational complexity, and two-level performance.  
- Empirically validates methods on a web page ranking task and reports improved ranking quality over the state-of-the-art.  
- Reiterates the two-level structure shared with prior work [21] (lower-level stationary estimate, upper-level parameter updates).  
- Notes that prior gradient-based method [21] lacks convergence proofs due to power-method estimation errors.  
- Strength: theoretical guarantees address a key limitation of prior work.  
- Strength: practical use of inexact oracle and analysis of needed accuracy.  
- Strength: comprehensive theoretical/technical contributions for inexact-oracle and constrained optimization settings.  
- Strength: experimental results support theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: paper is dense and mathematically heavy, reducing accessibility.  
- Weakness: unclear sensitivity to hyperparameters and limited guidance on parameter tuning/robustness.  
- Weakness: limited experimental comparisons beyond [21]; broader baseline inclusion recommended.  
- Weakness: experiments are on a single Yandex dataset, limiting evidence of generalizability.  
- Weakness: some redundancy and repetitive exposition in sections (e.g., model introduction).  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important gap, strong theory, and empirical improvement.  
- Arguments against acceptance: readability challenges and insufficiently comprehensive experiments.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable theoretical and practical contributions; accept with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: streamline presentation, add parameter-tuning guidance, test on additional datasets, and report computational time.
