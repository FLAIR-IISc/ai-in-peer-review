This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem arising in learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model. The model is defined by a random walk with weights assigned to nodes and edges, which are themselves determined by random walks on the nodes' and edges' features. Due to the intractability of directly optimizing this loss function with existing methods requiring exact objective function values, the authors propose a two-level approach. First, a linearly convergent method estimates an approximation to the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk. The authors validate this approach and demonstrate that the loss function can be approximated to any desired precision. Second, they develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems employing an inexact oracle, and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The core contribution lies in adapting this approach to constrained optimization when the function value is known only with a specified precision, proving the method’s convergence and utilizing it in the second level of their algorithm. The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear sound, though a complete verification was not possible within the scope of this review. 

My primary concern is that the key optimization techniques presented build heavily upon prior work, notably “Yurii Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny, Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions, Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015, pp. 1–40”, and the concept of a supervised PageRank algorithm has been explored previously. On a minor point, the paper would benefit from a concluding section summarizing the findings and outlining potential future directions. Furthermore, presenting the optimization problem in a more general context, positioning the studied algorithm as a specific instance within that framework, would better illuminate the contribution relative to the existing state-of-the-art.
