- Introduces two novel optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).  
- GBN has a provable convergence rate to a specified accuracy; GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss at each iteration.  
- Both methods operate without exact objective values and provide estimated convergence rates for efficient computation.  
- Empirical results claim superior ranking quality versus state-of-the-art and include clear hyper-parameter descriptions.  
- Datasets are not publicly available but are described sufficiently to potentially reproduce results.  
- Two-level optimization framework builds on Nesterov & Nemirovski and avoids computing large matrices, improving efficiency.  
- Methodological details and proofs are comprehensively explained across Sections 2–5 and the supplementary material.  
- Paper suffers from dense notation and needs a symbol table (e.g., 'N' used before being defined), harming readability.  
- Experimental evaluation emphasizes loss comparisons (with GBP) but lacks direct ranking-quality comparisons and a direct comparison to ref:17.
