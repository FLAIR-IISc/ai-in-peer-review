

This paper investigates the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning parameters in a general graph-based ranking model, defined through a random walk where node and edge weights are determined by feature-driven random walks on the graph. The optimization challenge cannot be addressed by conventional methods, which require exact objective function evaluations. The proposed two-level approach first employs a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, validated through comparative analysis to achieve loss function approximation with arbitrary precision. The authors further develop a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle, establishing its convergence to a stationary point. The key contribution lies in adapting this framework to constrained settings where objective function evaluations are available with known precision, with convergence rigorously proven and leveraged in the second algorithmic level. The paper is well-structured, and the proofs appear sound, though a full verification was not performed. The primary concern is that the core optimization techniques are adaptations of those in Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015), and the supervised PageRank algorithm has been previously introduced. A minor issue is the absence of a conclusion section despite ample space. To better contextualize the contribution, the optimization problem should be framed within a broader theoretical context, explicitly positioning the proposed method as a specific instance of a more general class of algorithms.
