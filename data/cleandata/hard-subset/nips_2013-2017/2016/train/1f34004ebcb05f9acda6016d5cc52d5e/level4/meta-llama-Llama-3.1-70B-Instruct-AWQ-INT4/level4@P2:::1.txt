This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model, defined by a random walk conducted by weights of nodes and edges, which are in turn defined by nodes' and edge's features. The optimization problem cannot be solved by existing optimization methods, which require exact values of the objective function. The proposed approach operates in two levels. At the first level, a linearly convergent method is used to estimate an approximation to the stationary distribution of Markov random walk. This approach is validated among others, and the authors show that the value of the loss function can be approximated with any given precision.

The authors develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems using an inexact oracle and prove its convergence to the stationary point of the problem. The contribution is in the adaptation of the approach to the case of constrained optimization problems when the value of the function can be calculated with some known precision. They prove the convergence of this method and exploit it on the second level of the proposed algorithm.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs seem to be correct, although I did not go through all of them. My main concern is that the optimization methods proposed here are adaptations of those proposed in "Yurii. Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny, Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions, Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015, pp. 1–40". Additionally, the supervised PageRank algorithm has been proposed previously.

To improve the paper, I would suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general context, showing the studied algorithm as a specific case. This would allow better placement of the contribution with respect to the state-of-the-art. Furthermore, a conclusion section would be beneficial in summarizing the key findings and implications of the research.
