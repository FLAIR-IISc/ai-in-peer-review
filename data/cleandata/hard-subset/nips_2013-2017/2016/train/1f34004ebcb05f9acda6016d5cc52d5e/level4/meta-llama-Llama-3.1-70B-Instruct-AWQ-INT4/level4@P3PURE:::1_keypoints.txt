- Analyzes convergence for a non-convex graph-based ranking model defined by random walks whose weights depend on node/edge features.  
- Proposes a two-level approach: first, a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution and hence the loss to arbitrary precision.  
- Second level: develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adaptation of inexact-oracle optimization techniques to constrained problems where function values are only approximately computable.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not checked in full detail).  
- Major concern: key optimization insights are adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) random gradient-free minimization.  
- Additional concern: supervised PageRank has been proposed previously, raising prior-art issues.  
- Minor note: paper lacks a conclusion despite available space.  
- Suggestion: present the optimization problem in a broader context and position the studied algorithm as a specific instance to better situate the contribution.
