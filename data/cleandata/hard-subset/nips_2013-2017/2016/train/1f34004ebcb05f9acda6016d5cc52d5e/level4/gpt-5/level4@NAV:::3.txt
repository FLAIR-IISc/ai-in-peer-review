In this paper, the authors present two optimization approaches for the supervised PageRank problem. Unlike prior work, they propose a gradient-based method with a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee that can attain a prescribed accuracy, and a gradient-free method with a guaranteed decrease in the loss. Both methods operate without requiring exact evaluations of the objective function and come with convergence-rate estimates; they also report superior ranking quality compared with the state of the art. The hyper-parameters used in both methods are specified. Although the datasets are not publicly available, the description is clear enough that reproducing the results should be feasible.

The authors introduce two bilevel optimization methods—gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN)—to solve supervised PageRank. The lower- and upper-level procedures build on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref: 17). The proposed framework does not require exact objective values and provides proven convergence-rate estimates for a target accuracy. Consequently, it avoids constructing the large matrix while still delivering strong results. Sections 2 through 5 detail the methods, with full proofs provided in the supplementary material.

However, a symbol table would be very helpful, as the notation is dense and easy to lose track of. For instance, in line 139 and Eq. 3.1 the symbol N appears, but its meaning (number of steps) is not clarified until line 193. In the experiments, the authors compare only with GBP in terms of the loss, rather than ranking quality. Moreover, since this work generalizes ref: 17, a direct comparison against it would be valuable to determine whether the performance is indeed better.
