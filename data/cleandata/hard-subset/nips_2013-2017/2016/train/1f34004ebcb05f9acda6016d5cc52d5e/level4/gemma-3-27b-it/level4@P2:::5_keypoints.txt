- Problem: Learning Supervised PageRank models with node/edge features and addressing lack of convergence guarantees for prior gradient-based methods.
- Approach: Two-level optimization (lower: approximate stationary distribution; upper: tune parameters) with gradient-based and gradient-free methods using inexact oracle estimates.
- Core contribution: Theoretical convergence rate guarantees and complexity bounds for both proposed algorithms.
- Methodology: Builds on [21]’s two-level framework and uses approximation method from [17] plus an adaptation of gradient-free technique from [16].
- Strength: Strong theoretical rigor, providing convergence proofs missing from prior work.
- Strength: Novel combination of inexact oracle estimations with tailored gradient-based and gradient-free optimization for this problem.
- Strength: Potential practical impact for search/ranking applications if improvements generalize.
- Strength: Directly addresses a known limitation (lack of convergence proofs) in the literature.
- Weakness: Writing and presentation suffer from grammatical errors and unclear mathematical exposition.
- Weakness: Experimental section lacks detail (key parameters like ϕ₀ and R not explained) and relegates important results to the appendix.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited to a single dataset (Yandex browsing graph), so generalizability is unclear.
- Weakness: Restrictive assumptions (e.g., constraining ϕ to an R-ball) may limit practical applicability and are not fully discussed.
- Weakness: Citation inaccuracy—[21] is cited as Supervised PageRank though it primarily addresses semi-supervised ranking.
- Weakness: Paper does not convincingly contextualize real-world impact or the significance of performance improvements.
- For acceptance: Strong theory, novelty, empirical improvement over prior state-of-the-art, and addresses prior method’s limitation.
- Against acceptance: Poor clarity, insufficient experimental detail and scope, restrictive assumptions, inaccurate citation, and unclear broader impact.
- Overall assessment: Theoretically sound and potentially valuable, but clarity and empirical validation are insufficient to demonstrate practical significance.
- Recommendation: Reject but encourage revision addressing writing quality, expanded and better-documented experiments, discussion of assumptions, corrected citations, and moving key results into the main text.
