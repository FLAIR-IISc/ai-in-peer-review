- Studies convergence for non-convex loss minimization in a graph-based ranking model defined via random walks with feature-based node/edge weights.  
- Notes existing methods are intractable because they require exact objective values.  
- Proposes a two-level approach: first estimate the Markov chain stationary distribution using a linearly convergent method.  
- Shows the stationary distribution estimator allows approximating the loss to arbitrary precision.  
- Develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution is adapting this inexact-oracle approach to constrained problems where function values have known precision and using it in the two-level algorithm.  
- Paper is well written and proofs appear correct, though not all were fully checked.  
- Optimization contributions mainly adapt ideas from Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) on random gradient-free minimization.  
- The supervised PageRank algorithm used has been previously proposed.  
- Paper lacks a concluding section and should include one.  
- Recommends presenting the optimization problem in a broader context, framing the studied algorithm as a specific case to clarify its position in the state of the art.
