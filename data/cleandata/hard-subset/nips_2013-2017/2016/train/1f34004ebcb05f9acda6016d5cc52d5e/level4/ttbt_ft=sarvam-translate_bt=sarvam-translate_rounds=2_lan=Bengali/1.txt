This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for parameter learning in a general graph-based ranking model. This model is defined by a random walk driven by the weights of nodes and edges, which is defined by a random walk driven by the characteristics of nodes and edges. This optimization problem cannot be solved by existing optimization methods, which require exact values of the objective function. Therefore, the proposed method works in two stages. In the first stage, a linearly convergent method is used to estimate an approximate value of the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk. This method has gained validity among other methods, and the authors have shown that the value of the loss function can be approximated with any given accuracy. They developed a gradient method for general bounded non-convex optimization problems using an implicit oracle and proved its convergence towards the stable point of the problem. This contribution lies in the adaptation of the method to bounded optimization problems, where the function value can be computed with some known accuracy. They have proven the convergence of this method and used it in the second stage of the proposed algorithm. The paper seems well-written, and the proofs are correct, although I have not seen all of them. My concern is that the main optimization points made here have been suggested in "Yuri. Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny, Random gradient-free minimization of convex functions, Foundations of computational mathematics, 2015, pp. 1-40" and the page rank algorithm has also been suggested in the past. A small amount of conclusion is expected, especially where there is something to write about. I would suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general context, where the algorithm studied is shown as a specific example. This will help to better place the contribution in relation to the state of the art.
