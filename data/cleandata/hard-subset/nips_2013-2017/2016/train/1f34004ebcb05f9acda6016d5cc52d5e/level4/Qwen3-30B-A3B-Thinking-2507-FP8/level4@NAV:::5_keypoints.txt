- Proposes combining PageRank with node/edge weights from a feature model and solving via stationary-distribution approximation plus gradient-free optimization for sampled loss.
- Framework largely reproduces prior work [21] under different terminology and notation.
- Paper states technical assumptions, defines a loss, and provides lemmas bounding the approximate gradient.
- Presents a general gradient-free optimization method using a biased oracle with a performance theorem.
- Adds further assumptions to derive an adapted algorithm for the learning problem and proves additional bounds.
- Experimental section is brief and only reports experiments on a single web graph from prior work; full results are deferred to the appendix.
- Key experimental details are missing: parameter values, selection rationale for φ0 and R, and how parameters were chosen.
- The claimed superior performance lacks discussion of improvement magnitude or practical significance.
- Unclear whether the method was tested on diverse graphs or multiple datasets.
- Writing issues: grammatical errors and inconsistent/misleading citations (e.g., "Supervised PageRank" citation error).
- Technical assumptions appear restrictive and possibly unjustified empirically (e.g., constraining search space to an R-ball around φ0).
- Major substantive content (proof outlines, full empirical results, parameter choices, multi-graph testing) is relegated to the appendix rather than the main text.
- Main text lacks tables, concrete results, and discussion of practical implications, weakening the presented contributions.
