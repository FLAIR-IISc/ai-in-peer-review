This paper addresses the formulation of Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem. The first one is a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle. The second one is a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.

The main idea of the paper is to learn Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges. The authors propose two two-level methods to solve the problem. On the lower level of these methods, they use the linearly convergent method to calculate an approximation to the stationary distribution of Markov random walk. They show that this method allows to approximate the value of the loss function at any given accuracy and has the lowest proved complexity bound among methods proposed in the literature.

The authors develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems with inexact oracle and estimate its convergence rate to the stationary point of the problem. They exploit this gradient method on the upper level of the two-level algorithm for learning Supervised PageRank. Another contribution consists in investigating both for the gradient and gradient-free methods the trade-off between the accuracy of the lower-level algorithm and the computational complexity of the two-level algorithm as a whole.

The authors also propose a gradient-free optimization method for general convex problems with inexact zero-order oracle and an adaptive gradient method for possibly nonconvex general composite optimization problems with inexact first-order oracle. For both methods, they provide convergence rate analysis.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear description of the problem and the proposed methods. However, I have some concerns regarding the experiment results, which are not included in the main text. I would like to see the experiment results in the main text to better understand the performance of the proposed methods.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed methods. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear description of the problem and the proposed methods. However, I would like to see more discussion on the choice of the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function.

Regarding the clarity of the paper, the authors provide a clear description of the problem and the proposed methods. However, I would like to see more discussion on the convergence of the sequence $xk$ to a stationary point.

In terms of the originality of the paper, the authors propose two new methods to solve the optimization problem. The gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle and the gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle are new contributions to the field.

In terms of the significance of the paper, the authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed methods. The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear description of the problem and the proposed methods.

Here are some specific comments on the paper:

1. The authors put experiment results in the supplement, which makes the main text incomplete. I would like to see the experiment results in the main text to better understand the performance of the proposed methods.

2. Convexity is assumed in Theorem 1, while the function in (2.4) is non-convex. How to choose the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function?

3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. Could this bound ensure that the sequence $xk$ converges to a stationary point?

4. By Figure 1 in the supplement, GFNM converges slowly. The authors may also want to report the efficiency of the algorithms.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes two new methods to solve the optimization problem.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed methods.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear description of the problem and the proposed methods.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The experiment results are not included in the main text.
* The choice of the set $\Phi$ to guarantee the convexity of the objective function is not clear.
* The convergence of the sequence $xk$ to a stationary point is not guaranteed.
* The efficiency of the algorithms is not reported.
