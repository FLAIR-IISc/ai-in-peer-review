

This paper investigates the convergence properties of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning parameters in a general graph-based ranking model. The model defines node and edge weights via random walks over node and edge features, resulting in an optimization problem where the objective function cannot be evaluated exactly—precluding the use of standard optimization methods. The authors propose a two-level approach: at the first level, they employ a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, demonstrating that the loss function value can be approximated to any desired precision. At the second level, they develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The primary contribution lies in adapting this inexact oracle framework to constrained optimization problems where function evaluations are available with known precision, and leveraging this adaptation within the proposed algorithm. The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear correct, though the reviewer did not verify all details. A significant concern is that the core optimization techniques closely adapt methods from Nesterov and Spokoiny (Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015), and the supervised PageRank algorithm addressed in the paper has been previously proposed. Additionally, the paper lacks a conclusion section despite having space for one. To better contextualize the contribution, the authors should frame the optimization problem within a broader theoretical framework, explicitly positioning their algorithm as a specific instance of a more general class of methods. This would clarify the novelty relative to the state-of-the-art.
