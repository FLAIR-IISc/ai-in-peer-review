- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (GBN gradient-based, GFN gradient-free) for supervised PageRank with convergence guarantees and target accuracy without requiring exact objective values.  
- Avoids costly inversion of a large matrix, improving scalability for large-scale ranking problems.  
- Reports empirical improvements in ranking quality over state-of-the-art methods.  
- Presents a framework that generalizes Nesterov and Nemirovski [17].  
- Theoretical strength: provides convergence rate guarantees and practical accuracy attainment without exact objective evaluation.  
- Computational strength: methods are scalable and computationally efficient by circumventing matrix inversion.  
- Performance strength: experimental results indicate superior ranking quality.  
- Reproducibility: authors supply hyperparameters and clear dataset descriptions, though datasets are not public.  
- Clarity weakness: paper is dense and notation-heavy; some symbols (e.g., N) are not defined at first use.  
- Suggestion: include a symbol table or dedicated notation section and define symbols upon first appearance.  
- Experimental weakness: comparison with GBP is limited to loss values rather than ranking-quality metrics.  
- Suggestion: extend comparisons to primary ranking metrics and include a direct empirical comparison with Nesterov and Nemirovski [17].
