- Paper formulates SPR learning as a two-level problem and treats lower-level approximations as an inexact oracle with convergence-rate guarantees for gradient-based and gradient-free upper-level methods.  
- Uses Nesterov–Nemirovski scheme to approximate stationary distributions and their derivatives, arguing favorable complexity vs. power-method approaches.  
- Proposes a constrained gradient-free algorithm that handles uniformly bounded zero-order oracle errors.  
- Develops an adaptive projected gradient method for constrained nonconvex composite problems with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Provides analysis of the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall complexity, with complexity bounds for both methods.  
- Empirical evaluation on Yandex web ranking shows improved loss and claimed ranking quality over prior gradient-based SPR.  
- Theoretical framing and inexact-oracle analysis are carefully formulated with clear assumptions and convergence guarantees.  
- Extensions (constrained zero-order with oracle error, adaptive line-search with inexact first-order oracle) are technically solid and broadly useful.  
- Combining inexact-oracle perspective with SPR learning and providing convergence rates is novel in this context.  
- Learning PageRank-like models remains significant; provable convergence under realistic inexactness is valuable for large-scale deployment.  
- Gradient-free variant is appealing when derivative computation is prohibitive.  
- High-level structure and motivation are clear; hyperparameters and implementation choices are stated.  
- Manuscript is notation-heavy; needs a symbol table and earlier/standardized definitions (e.g., N, norms, query-specific vs global quantities).  
- Experiments mainly report training loss and iterations; must include standard IR metrics (NDCG@k, MAP, ERR) on held-out data, statistical significance, runtime/wall-clock comparisons, and stronger LTR baselines.  
- Gradient-free analysis assumes local convexity of f on Φ, a strong assumption for nonconvex SPR that should be made explicit and justified; clarify how Φ is chosen.  
- Relate stationarity measure used for gradient-based method to standard measures (e.g., projected gradient norm) for interpretability.  
- Reproducibility limited by proprietary Yandex data; authors should release code and synthetic/anonymized data and detail feature construction and splits.  
- Presentation issues: typos, inconsistent references, undefined constants (e.g., β1), and inconsistent algorithm naming (GBN, GFN, GBP) need fixing.  
- Overall evaluation: strong theoretical development but empirical validation needs richer metrics, baselines, and runtime analysis; clarity can be improved.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid theoretical contributions, relevant SPR application, and careful accuracy–complexity analysis.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited experimental evaluation, strong unverified assumptions, and heavy notation/minor editorial problems.  
- Requested author clarifications: report NDCG@k/MAP/ERR with wall-clock times and matrix–vector counts; sensitivity to Φ and empirical convexity; compare lower-level NM scheme to power method ([17]) on accuracy vs iterations and speed; specify practical stopping criterion in standard stationarity terms.
