- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- Lower- and upper-level optimization builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s framework.  
- Both methods have theoretical convergence guarantees and proven convergence-rate estimates given accuracy.  
- Methods do not require exact objective-function values, avoiding computation of large matrices.  
- Detailed method descriptions in Sections 2–5 with full proofs in supplementary materials.  
- Paper would benefit from a symbol table due to numerous symbols in equations.  
- Experiments show the proposed methods outperform state-of-the-art GBP on the loss function.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks comparison of ranking quality (primary objective) against GBP.  
- Missing direct comparison with the original Nesterov and Nemirovski method despite being a generalization.  
- Strength: novel optimization methods with theoretical guarantees that avoid exact objective evaluation.  
- Strength: empirical results indicate effectiveness (loss improvement).  
- Weakness: hard-to-follow notation—no symbol table provided.  
- Weakness: limited experimental comparison focused only on loss, not ranking quality.  
- Weakness: no direct empirical comparison to Nesterov and Nemirovski’s method.  
- Recommendation arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical guarantees, and supportive experimental results.  
- Recommendation arguments against acceptance: notation clarity and incomplete experimental comparisons.  
- Quality score: 8/10.  
- Clarity score: 7/10.  
- Originality score: 9/10.  
- Significance score: 8/10.
