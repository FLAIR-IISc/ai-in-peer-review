This paper formulates the learning of Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that incorporates node and edge features, and proposes two solution strategies: (i) a randomized gradient-free method based on an inexact zero-order oracle, and (ii) a gradient-based method using an inexact first-order oracle. While the theoretical framework provides convergence-rate guarantees for both approaches, my main concern is that essentially none of the experimental results appear in the main text, which makes the submission feel incomplete.

Detailed comments.
1. The key empirical results are placed in the supplementary material, leaving the main text incomplete and making it difficult to assess the practical impact of the methods without constantly consulting the appendix.
2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, whereas the objective in (2.4) is non-convex. How is the feasible set Phi chosen to ensure that f becomes convex over Phi in practice?
3. Theorem 3 bounds MK(x_K − x_{K+1}). Does this guarantee that the sequence {x_k} converges to a stationary point (e.g., in terms of gradient mapping or projected gradient norm), or is the bound only a stopping criterion surrogate?
4. According to Figure 1 in the supplement, the gradient-free method (GFN) appears to converge slowly. It would be helpful to report and compare computational efficiency (e.g., wall-clock time, iteration counts, and lower-level oracle costs) across methods.
