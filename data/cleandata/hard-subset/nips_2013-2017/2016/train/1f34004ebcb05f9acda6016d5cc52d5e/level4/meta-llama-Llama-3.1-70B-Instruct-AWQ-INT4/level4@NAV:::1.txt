This paper examines the convergence of a non-convex loss minimization problem for learning parameters in a general graph-based ranking model. This model is defined by a random walk, where node and edge weights are determined by random walks based on node and edge features. The optimization problem is intractable using existing methods, which require exact objective function values. To address this, the authors propose a two-level approach. Firstly, they utilize a linearly convergent method to estimate the stationary distribution of a Markov random walk, validating this approach and demonstrating that the loss function value can be approximated to any desired precision. Additionally, they develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization problems using an inexact oracle and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The key contribution lies in adapting this approach to constrained optimization problems where function values can be calculated with known precision. The authors prove the convergence of this method and leverage it in the second level of their proposed algorithm. The paper is well-written, with seemingly correct proofs, although not all were thoroughly examined. However, the primary optimization contributions appear to be adaptations of those proposed by Yurii Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny in their 2015 work on random gradient-free minimization of convex functions. Furthermore, the supervised PageRank algorithm has been previously proposed. A notable omission is a concluding section, which would be beneficial given the available space. It is suggested that the optimization problem be presented in a broader context, positioning the studied algorithm as a specific case. This would facilitate a clearer understanding of the contribution's position within the state-of-the-art.
