This paper introduces two novel optimization approaches—a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN)—designed to address the supervised PageRank problem. A key strength of this work lies in the theoretical guarantees provided; the gradient-based method offers a provable convergence rate to a specified accuracy, while the gradient-free method guarantees a decrease in the loss function with each iteration. Importantly, both methods operate without requiring exact objective function values and offer estimated convergence rates, enabling efficient computation while maintaining strong performance. Empirical results demonstrate superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art techniques, and the authors provide a clear description of the employed hyper-parameters. Although the datasets are not publicly available, the provided descriptions appear sufficient for result reproduction.

The proposed two-level optimization framework, GBN and GFN, builds upon the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17) for both the lower and upper level optimizations. By circumventing the need to compute large matrices, the methods achieve competitive results with improved efficiency. The methodological details, from Section 2 to Section 5, are comprehensively explained, with proofs included in the supplementary material. 

However, the manuscript would benefit significantly from the inclusion of a symbol table. The density of notation in the equations makes it challenging to follow the derivations; for instance, the meaning of 'N' (representing the number of steps) is not explicitly defined until Line 193, despite its initial use in Line 139 and Equation 3.1. Furthermore, the experimental evaluation focuses primarily on loss function comparison with GBP, and lacks a direct comparison of ranking quality. Given that this work generalizes ref:17, a direct performance comparison with this prior work would provide valuable context and insight into the advancements achieved.
