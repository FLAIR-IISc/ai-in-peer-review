- Paper tackles Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem incorporating node and edge features.  
- Proposes two solution approaches: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Situates work relative to PageRank, HITS, and supervised ranking frameworks and claims to address their inability to use node/edge features.  
- Strength: introduces two novel optimization methods tailored for the Supervised PageRank problem.  
- Strength: both methods are built on general frameworks (random gradient-free and inexact first-order gradient methods).  
- Strength: provides convergence rate analysis for both proposed methods.  
- Strength: demonstrates effectiveness on a real-world ranking task (experiments in supplement).  
- Weakness: experimental results are omitted from the main text, making the paper feel incomplete and less self-contained.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but this is not justified given the non-convex objective in (2.4); need discussion on selecting Φ to guarantee convexity.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK - xK+1) but does not clearly show convergence to a stationary point; convergence properties need clarification.  
- Weakness: algorithmic efficiency (iterations and runtime) is not reported; supplement suggests GFNM converges slowly.  
- Overall: promising contributions with solid analysis, but key theoretical justifications, experimental placement, and efficiency reporting must be addressed before acceptance.
