- Proposes gradient-based and random gradient-free methods for non-convex loss-minimization in supervised PageRank learning.  
- Employs the inexact oracle framework and provides theoretical convergence rates for both methods.  
- Presents a detailed time-complexity analysis of the proposed algorithms.  
- Empirical results show the proposed methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline on the loss metric.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains problem, methods, and experiments clearly.  
- Strength: introduces two new methods specifically for supervised PageRank non-convex optimization.  
- Strength: rigorous and detailed time complexity analysis.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental comparison with a strong baseline.  
- Weakness: does not include a detailed analysis of space complexity.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion of potential applications beyond the presented setting.  
- Originality: offers novel algorithmic approaches with theoretical guarantees.  
- Significance: relevant contribution to machine learning and optimization, with potential use in information retrieval and data mining.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add space-complexity analysis and discuss broader applications.
