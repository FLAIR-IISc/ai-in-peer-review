- Paper addresses learning Supervised PageRank models by incorporating node and edge features and proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods using inexact oracles.
- Builds on prior work for approximating Markov chain stationary distributions and adapts random gradient-free optimization to constrained settings.
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods, improving over a previous approach [21] that lacked convergence guarantees.
- Analyzes the trade-off between oracle accuracy and computational complexity within an inexact oracle framework.
- Experimental results claim improved ranking quality on a real-world dataset.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and formal convergence guarantees for both methods.
- Strength: tackles a well-motivated ranking problem and adapts optimization techniques to a non-convex constrained setting.
- Strength: empirical validation demonstrates performance gains (though details are limited).
- Weakness: numerous grammatical errors and imprecise citations reduce clarity and credibility.
- Weakness: important assumptions (e.g., restricting parameter space to an R-ball) may limit applicability and need more discussion.
- Weakness: substantial technical details, proofs, and experimental results are relegated to the supplementary material.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset, raising concerns about generalizability.
- Weakness: implementation choices (e.g., selection of ϕ̂ and R) and quantitative/statistical analysis of improvements are not clearly reported.
- Overall recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after addressing clarity, expanded experiments, and inclusion of key details in the main text.
