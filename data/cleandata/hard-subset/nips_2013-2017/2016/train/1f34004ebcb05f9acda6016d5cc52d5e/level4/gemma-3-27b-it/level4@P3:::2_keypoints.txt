- Frames Supervised PageRank learning as a constrained non-convex optimization problem incorporating node and edge features.  
- Proposes both gradient-free and gradient-based algorithms tailored to this problem.  
- Leverages inexact oracles to handle expensive loss and derivative evaluations.  
- Provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods.  
- Core contribution: delivers convergence guarantees where prior methods lacked them.  
- Empirical results are relegated to the supplementary material, severely limiting practical assessment.  
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite the stated non-convexity of the loss; the choice of set Φ and local convexity need clarification.  
- Theorem 3 bounds distance between iterates but does not explicitly guarantee convergence to a stationary point.  
- Observed slow convergence of the gradient-free method (Figure 1 in supplement) raises practical efficiency concerns.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis addressing a key gap in prior work.  
- Strength: novel adaptation of gradient-free and gradient-based methods to constrained non-convex Supervised PageRank.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and sensible use of inexact oracles.  
- Weakness: omission of main-text experimental results undermines impact and evaluation.  
- Acceptance argument: important problem with sound theoretical contributions and novelty.  
- Rejection argument: missing experimental evidence, questionable convexity assumptions, and slow empirical convergence justify weak reject pending revisions.
