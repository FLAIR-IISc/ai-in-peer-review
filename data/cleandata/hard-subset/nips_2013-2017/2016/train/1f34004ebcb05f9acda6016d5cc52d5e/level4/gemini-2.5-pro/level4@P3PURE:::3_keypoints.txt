- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based (GBN) and a gradient-free (GFN) approach that do not require exact objective values.  
- GBN comes with a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee to a specified accuracy; GFN ensures a guaranteed decrease in the loss.  
- Main technical contribution: adapts Nesterov & Nemirovski-style two-level optimization to provably converge without computing the exact objective, avoiding expensive operations like large matrix inversions.  
- Theoretical development is detailed in Sections 2–5 with proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Empirically claims state-of-the-art ranking quality; hyperparameters are provided and datasets are clearly described though not public.  
- Clarity/notation issue: many symbols are introduced without an upfront symbol table (e.g., N is used before being defined), harming readability.  
- Experimental comparison is incomplete: evaluation compares to GBP only on loss, not on the key metric of ranking quality.  
- Missing direct empirical comparison to the baseline method in [17], despite presenting the work as a generalization of that method.
