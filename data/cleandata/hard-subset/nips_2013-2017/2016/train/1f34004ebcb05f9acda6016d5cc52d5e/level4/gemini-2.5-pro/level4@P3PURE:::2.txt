This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that incorporates node and edge properties. To solve this problem, the authors propose two methods: a gradient-free approach using an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based approach using an inexact first-order oracle.

A major weakness of this paper is the complete absence of experimental results in the main text. Relegating all empirical validation to the supplementary material makes the main paper incomplete and difficult to evaluate on its own merits.

Detailed comments:

1.  There appears to be a discrepancy in the theoretical analysis. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, whereas the objective function defined in (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should clarify how the set Φ can be chosen to ensure the convexity required by the theorem.

2.  The convergence guarantees require further justification. Theorem 3 provides a bound for the term $M_K(x_K - x_{K+1})$, but it is not immediately clear whether this bound is sufficient to ensure that the sequence $\{x_k\}$ converges to a stationary point.

3.  The practical performance of the proposed methods should be more thoroughly discussed. According to Figure 1 in the supplement, the GFNM algorithm appears to converge slowly. To provide a comprehensive evaluation, the authors should also report on the computational efficiency of their algorithms, for instance by including runtimes or a complexity analysis.
