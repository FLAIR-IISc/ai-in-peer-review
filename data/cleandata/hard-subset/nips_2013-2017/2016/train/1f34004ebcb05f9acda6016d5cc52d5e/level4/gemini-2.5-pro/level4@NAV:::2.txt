This work formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that integrates node and edge features. To solve this problem, the paper introduces two distinct algorithms: a gradient-free method using an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method utilizing an inexact first-order oracle.

My primary concern is that the main manuscript lacks any experimental results. By relegating all empirical evaluation to the supplementary material, the paper stands as an incomplete work, making it difficult for a reader to assess the practical performance of the proposed methods.

Detailed Comments:

1.  The placement of all experimental validation in the supplement fundamentally compromises the completeness of the main paper.
2.  There is an apparent discrepancy concerning the convexity assumption. While Theorem 1 assumes convexity, the objective function in (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should explain how the set Φ can be chosen to satisfy the conditions required by the theorem.
3.  Theorem 3 establishes a bound for the term $M_K(x_K - x_{K+1})$. It is not clear if this bound is sufficient to guarantee the convergence of the sequence $\{x_k\}$ to a stationary point. This claim requires further theoretical justification.
4.  Based on Figure 1 in the supplement, the convergence of the GFNM method appears to be slow. It would strengthen the paper to include a discussion on the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithms, potentially by reporting execution times or other relevant performance metrics.
