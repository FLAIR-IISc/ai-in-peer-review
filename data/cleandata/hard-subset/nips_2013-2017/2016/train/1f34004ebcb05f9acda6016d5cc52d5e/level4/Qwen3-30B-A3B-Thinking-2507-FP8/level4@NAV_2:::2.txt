

This paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that incorporates node and edge features through linear combinations of their features. The authors propose two two-level optimization methods: a gradient-free approach utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method employing an inexact first-order oracle. This work extends the Supervised PageRank framework introduced in [21] by providing theoretical convergence guarantees that were missing in the state-of-the-art gradient-based method [21]. The paper makes a significant contribution to the understanding of optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models by establishing convergence rates and complexity bounds for both approaches.

Strengths:
1. The paper provides theoretically rigorous convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods, which is a substantial advancement over the state-of-the-art [21] that lacks such theoretical foundations.
2. The authors develop a comprehensive framework for handling inexact oracle information in both gradient-free and gradient-based optimization contexts, with appropriate complexity analysis.
3. The experimental results demonstrate that both proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art approach in terms of ranking quality on a real-world web page ranking task.
4. The work clearly articulates the trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall algorithmic complexity, which is valuable for practical implementation.

Weaknesses:
1. The main text lacks all experimental results, which are exclusively presented in the supplementary materials. This omission significantly hinders the evaluation of the paper's contributions, as the key evidence supporting the claimed performance improvements is not accessible in the main body.
2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity of the objective function, while the problem formulation in (2.4) is explicitly non-convex. The paper does not adequately explain how the set Φ can be chosen to guarantee convexity in the neighborhood of the optimal solution.
3. Theorem 3 provides a bound on ‖MK(xK - xK+1)‖2, but it is unclear whether this bound ensures convergence to a stationary point for the non-convex problem, which is a critical concern for the paper's main contribution.
4. Figure 1 in the supplement shows relatively slow convergence of the gradient-free method (GFN), which raises questions about the practical efficiency of the proposed methods compared to existing approaches.

The paper makes a theoretically strong contribution to the optimization of Supervised PageRank models, but the absence of experimental results in the main text substantially weakens its impact. The authors should address these issues before the paper can be considered for acceptance, as the experimental evidence is crucial for validating the practical significance of the theoretical advances.
