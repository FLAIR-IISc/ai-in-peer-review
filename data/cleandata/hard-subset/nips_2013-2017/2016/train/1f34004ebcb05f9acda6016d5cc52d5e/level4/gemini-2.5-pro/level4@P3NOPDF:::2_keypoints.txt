- Paper formulates Supervised PageRank with node/edge features and proposes two solvers: a gradient-free method (inexact zero-order oracle) and a gradient-based method (inexact first-order oracle).  
- Work is well-motivated and the formulation is interesting, but presentation and clarity of theoretical claims are problematic.  
- Major concern: all experimental results are relegated to the supplementary material; key empirical validation must be included in the main paper.  
- Clarify convexity assumption: Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but Equation (2.4) is non-convex—need conditions on Φ (or explanation) under which the theorem applies.  
- Convergence guarantees: Theorem 3 bounds ||x_k - x_{k+1}|| but does not clearly imply convergence to a stationary point for the non-convex problem; a formal connection/proof is required.  
- Algorithm efficiency: gradient-free method appears slow (per supplementary Figure 1); authors should report computational complexity, empirical runtimes, and comparative efficiency vs. accuracy.  
- Overall: interesting contribution but significant presentational issues and unresolved theoretical/empirical questions prevent a positive recommendation.
