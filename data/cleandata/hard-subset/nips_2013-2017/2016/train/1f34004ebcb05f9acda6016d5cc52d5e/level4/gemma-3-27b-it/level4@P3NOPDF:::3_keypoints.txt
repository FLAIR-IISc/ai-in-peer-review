- Proposes two novel methods (GBN — gradient-based, GFN — gradient-free) for supervised PageRank optimization.  
- GBN has provable convergence rate to a specified accuracy; GFN guarantees monotonic loss decrease per iteration.  
- Both methods avoid needing exact objective values and provide estimates of convergence behavior.  
- Experiments claim superior ranking quality versus existing state-of-the-art.  
- Builds on Nesterov & Nemirovski (ref:17) using a two-level optimization that circumvents large-matrix computations.  
- Methods are thoroughly described with detailed proofs in the supplement and hyperparameter settings provided.  
- Datasets are not public but are described clearly enough to suggest feasible replication.  
- Major notational issues: many symbols are unclear or introduced late (e.g., N), so a symbol table is strongly recommended.  
- Experimental evaluation is incomplete: comparisons are mainly on loss, lacking direct ranking-quality metrics.  
- Missing baseline: no direct empirical comparison to the original method generalized (ref:17).  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept with major revisions — require a symbol table and expanded experiments including ranking metrics and comparison to ref:17.
