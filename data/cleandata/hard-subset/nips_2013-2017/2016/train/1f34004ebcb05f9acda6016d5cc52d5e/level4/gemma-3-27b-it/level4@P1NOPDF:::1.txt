This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model. The model is defined by a random walk with weights assigned to nodes and edges, which are themselves determined by random walks based on node and edge features. This optimization problem is challenging because existing methods require exact evaluations of the objective function, which are unavailable here. The proposed approach therefore operates in two levels. At the first level, a linearly convergent method is used to estimate an approximation of the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk. The authors validate this approach and demonstrate that the loss function can be approximated with arbitrary precision. They also develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems using an inexact oracle, and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The key contribution lies in adapting this approach to constrained optimization problems where the function value can be calculated only to a known precision. They prove the convergence of this method and then apply it in the second level of their algorithm. 

The paper is well-written and the proofs appear to be correct, although I did not verify all details. My primary concern is that the main optimization techniques presented are adaptations of those proposed in Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015) – specifically, their work on random gradient-free minimization of convex functions. Furthermore, the concept of a supervised PageRank algorithm has been previously introduced in the literature. 

On a minor point, a conclusion summarizing the results and potential future work would be beneficial, as space allows. I would also suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general context, framing the studied algorithm as a specific case within that broader framework. This would better position the contribution relative to the state-of-the-art and highlight its novelty.
