- Paper analyzes convergence for learning parameters of a general graph-based ranking model defined by nested random walks (node/edge features → weights → random walk).
- Optimization problem is non-convex and cannot be solved by existing methods that require exact objective values.
- Proposed two-level approach: (1) linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov walk; (2) gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle.
- Authors prove convergence of the combined two-level algorithm and apply it in experiments.
- Work builds on prior methods (PageRank/HITS, power method, Nesterov) but adapts them to constrained problems with inexact function evaluations.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and proofs appear correct.
- Strengths: clear problem presentation, detailed convergence analysis, and empirical demonstration of effectiveness.
- Weaknesses: main optimization ideas are adaptations of prior work and novelty is not strongly emphasized.
- Could improve by presenting a more general optimization perspective and positioning the algorithm as a special case.
- Paper lacks a detailed discussion of method limitations and future research directions.
- Significance: potential to improve efficiency/effectiveness of ranking algorithms in web search and related applications.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to better highlight novelty, generalize presentation, and discuss limitations.
