- Analyzes convergence for learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by feature-driven random walks (node/edge weights).

- Existing optimization methods fail because they require exact objective values; authors propose a two-level approach.

- First level: linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk and approximate the loss to arbitrary precision.

- Second level: develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.

- Main contribution: adaptation of the inexact-oracle approach to constrained problems where function values are known up to a given precision, applied in the two-level algorithm.

- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (reviewer did not check all details).

- Main concern: proposed optimization methods are adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) rather than wholly novel techniques.

- Novelty question: supervised PageRank has been proposed previously, reducing perceived originality.

- Suggestion: present the optimization problem in a more general context and frame the proposed algorithm as a specific instance to better position relative to state-of-the-art.

- Weakness: paper lacks a concluding section summarizing main contributions and implications; authors should add one.

- Acceptance arguments: novel two-level approach, inexact-oracle gradient method with convergence proof, clear writing.

- Rejection arguments: methods largely adaptations of prior work, prior existence of supervised PageRank, and missing conclusion.
