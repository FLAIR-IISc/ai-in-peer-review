- Introduces two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- Gradient-based method has theoretical convergence guarantees and can achieve a specified accuracy.  
- Gradient-free method guarantees a decrease in the loss function.  
- Both methods operate without requiring the exact objective value and provide convergence-rate estimates.  
- Methods avoid large matrix computations while delivering strong results.  
- Empirical results reportedly outperform state-of-the-art techniques in ranking quality.  
- Hyper-parameters are explicitly provided; dataset descriptions are detailed enough to enable reproduction despite datasets not being public.  
- Methods build on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s two-level optimization framework (ref:17).  
- Paper includes thorough explanations and all proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Reviewer requests a symbol table because many symbols (e.g., N) are unclear until later in the text.  
- Experimental concern: comparison with GBP is reported only on loss, not on ranking quality.  
- Suggestion: include a direct comparison with ref:17 to assess whether the proposed methods improve over that baseline.
