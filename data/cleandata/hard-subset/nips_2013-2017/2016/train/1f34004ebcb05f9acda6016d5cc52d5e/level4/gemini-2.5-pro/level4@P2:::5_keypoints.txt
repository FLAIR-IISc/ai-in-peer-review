- Paper addresses learning parameters for Supervised PageRank, identifying prior gradient-based methods lack convergence guarantees.  
- Main contribution: two new optimization methods (gradient-based and random gradient-free) with formal convergence rate guarantees.  
- Technical approach: cast optimization in an inexact oracle framework to handle approximate stationary distributions and derivatives.  
- Empirical validation: methods shown to outperform prior state-of-the-art on a web page ranking task.  
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: first convergence rate guarantees for this problem with comprehensive inexact-oracle analysis and two-level complexity bounds.  
- Strength — Novel combination: integrates an efficient stationary-distribution solver with upper-level optimization frameworks to handle inexact function/gradient evaluations.  
- Strength — Potential impact: methods could improve reliability and performance of graph-based learning-to-rank systems if results generalize.  
- Weakness — Clarity/organization: heavy reliance on supplementary material; main text lacks proof sketches, detailed algorithms, and key empirical figures/tables.  
- Weakness — Experimental shortcomings: unclear hyperparameter selection (center ϕ̂ and radius R), single-dataset evaluation, and no assessment of practical ranking metrics (e.g., NDCG/MAP).  
- Weakness — Practicality of assumptions: gradient-free analysis assumes local convexity within Φ despite overall non-convexity; constraint to a Euclidean ball may be restrictive or limit expressiveness.  
- Minor issues: writing/grammar need polishing and inconsistent terminology between "Supervised PageRank" and "Supervised Nested PageRank."  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution that would be much stronger with clearer presentation and a more comprehensive, practically-focused experimental evaluation.
