This paper studies convergence for a nonconvex loss-minimization problem aimed at learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model. The ranking is defined via a random walk whose node and edge weights depend on feature vectors of nodes and edges. Existing optimization techniques that rely on exact objective evaluations are not applicable here. The authors therefore propose a two-level approach. At the lower level, they use a linearly convergent procedure to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, and demonstrate that the loss can be approximated to arbitrary accuracy. They further design a gradient method for constrained, nonconvex optimization with an inexact oracle and establish convergence to a stationary point. The key contribution is adapting this framework to constrained settings where function values are available up to a known tolerance, proving convergence, and using it at the upper level of their algorithm. The paper is clearly written, and the proofs appear sound, although I did not verify all details.

My main reservation is that the core optimization components largely adapt prior work (notably: Yurii Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny, Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions, Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015), and the supervised PageRank framework itself has been introduced previously. On the minor side, a dedicated conclusion section would be appropriate given the available space. I also recommend formulating the optimization task in a broader, problem-agnostic setting and then positioning the studied algorithm as a specific instance; this would clarify the contribution relative to the existing literature.
