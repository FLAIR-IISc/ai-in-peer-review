1. Paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization integrating node and edge features and proposes two solvers: a gradient-free method (inexact zero-order oracle) and a gradient-based method (inexact first-order oracle).

2. Primary concern: all experimental results are relegated to the supplement, making the main manuscript incomplete and preventing assessment of practical performance.

3. Placing all experimental validation in the supplement fundamentally compromises the completeness and readability of the main paper.

4. Apparent discrepancy in convexity: Theorem 1 assumes convexity but objective (2.4) is non-convex—authors must explain how the feasible set Φ can be chosen to satisfy theorem conditions.

5. Theorem 3’s bound on M_K(x_K − x_{K+1}) is unclear as to whether it guarantees convergence of {x_k} to a stationary point and requires further theoretical justification.

6. Empirical concern: Figure 1 (supplement) suggests slow convergence of the GFNM method; the paper should discuss computational efficiency and report execution times or other performance metrics.
