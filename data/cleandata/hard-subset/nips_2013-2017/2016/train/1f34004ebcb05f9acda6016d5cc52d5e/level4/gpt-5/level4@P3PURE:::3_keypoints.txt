- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).  
- GBN has a provable convergence-rate guarantee and can achieve a prescribed accuracy.  
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss; both methods provide convergence-rate estimates.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective values and avoid expensive large-matrix computations.  
- Practical details and hyper-parameter settings are provided; all proofs are in the supplementary material.  
- Datasets are not public but are described sufficiently to allow reproducibility.  
- Reported empirical performance appears to improve ranking quality over the state of the art.  
- Notation is cluttered and confusing (many symbols undefined or introduced late); a symbol table is recommended.  
- Experimental comparisons focus on loss versus GBP rather than on ranking metrics; direct ranking comparisons are needed.  
- Should include a direct empirical comparison to ref:17 (the theoretical baseline) to quantify gains.
