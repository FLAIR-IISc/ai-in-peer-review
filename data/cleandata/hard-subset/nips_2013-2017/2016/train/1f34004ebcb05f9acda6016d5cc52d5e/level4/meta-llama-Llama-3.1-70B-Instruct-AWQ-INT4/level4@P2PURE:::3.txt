The authors of this paper propose two optimization methods to solve the supervised PageRank problem, which distinguish themselves from existing methods by providing a gradient-based approach with a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and the ability to achieve a specified accuracy. Additionally, the proposed gradient-free method ensures a decrease in the loss function value. Notably, both methods do not require the exact value of the objective function and provide an estimate of the convergence rate, outperforming state-of-the-art methods in terms of ranking quality. The hyperparameters used in both methods are provided, and although the datasets are not publicly available, their descriptions are clear, making it feasible to reproduce the results.

The authors introduce two two-level optimization methods, namely gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN), to solve the supervised PageRank problem, building upon the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17). The proposed methods do not require the exact value of the objective function and offer a proven estimate of the convergence rate for a given accuracy, thereby avoiding the need to compute large matrices while maintaining good results. Sections 2-5 provide a detailed explanation of the methods, including proofs in the supplementary material. However, the inclusion of a symbol table would greatly enhance readability, as the numerous symbols in the equations can be easily lost track of. For instance, the variable $N$ is used in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 without explanation until Line 193.

Furthermore, the experimental section only compares the proposed methods with GBP in terms of the loss function, whereas a comparison in terms of ranking quality would be more informative. Additionally, since this work generalizes ref:17, a direct comparison with this reference would be beneficial in assessing the performance improvement.
