- Paper studies convergence for learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by a Markov random walk whose transitions depend on feature-based node and edge weights.  
- Existing optimization methods requiring exact objective evaluations do not apply because objective/gradients can only be approximated.  
- Proposed two-level approach: inner procedure linearly converges to an approximate stationary distribution and can approximate the loss to any prescribed accuracy.  
- Outer level: a gradient method for constrained nonconvex optimization with an inexact oracle, with proved convergence to a stationary point when function values are available only up to known precision.  
- Contribution: adaptation of the inexact-oracle framework to constrained problems, establishment of convergence guarantees, and integration into the two-level algorithm.  
- Writing and proofs are clear and appear correct (not exhaustively checked).  
- Main concern: limited novelty — central optimization techniques are adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
- Application originality is limited because supervised PageRank–style algorithms have been previously introduced.  
- Minor suggestions: add a dedicated conclusion section and present the optimization problem in a more general framework, showing the proposed algorithm as a specific instance.
