This paper addresses the problem of learning parameters for a feature-based PageRank model, where node and edge weights are determined by a feature model. The authors correctly note that a prior algorithm for this task lacks performance guarantees. To overcome this, they propose a new method that combines an approach for approximating the stationary distribution of a Markov random walk with a gradient-free optimization technique, for which they provide theoretical performance bounds.

**Strengths:**

The main strength of this work is its theoretical contribution. The authors provide a rigorous analysis, including performance guarantees for their proposed gradient-free optimization method and several technical lemmas with strong bounds. The proposed algorithm appears to be novel and potentially powerful for this class of problems.

**Weaknesses and Major Concerns:**

1.  **Critical Content Relegated to the Appendix:** The paper's most significant weakness is its over-reliance on the appendix. Key components required for a thorough evaluation, including detailed experimental results, analysis, and proof outlines, are entirely relegated to the supplementary material. A conference paper should be largely self-contained, and the current manuscript fails to provide sufficient evidence in the main body to support its claims.

2.  **Insufficient Empirical Evaluation:** The empirical evaluation presented in the main paper is inadequate. While the authors claim their algorithm outperforms others on a web graph from related work, the main text lacks any concrete results, tables, or figures. The extent and practical implications of the reported improvements are not discussed. Furthermore, critical details about the experimental setup are missing, such as how key hyperparameters (e.g., `phi_0` and `R`) were chosen. It is also unclear if the evaluation was conducted on more than one graph, which is essential for assessing the generalizability of the proposed method.

3.  **Practicability of Theoretical Assumptions:** While the theoretical analysis is a strength, there are concerns about the practical applicability of the underlying assumptions. It is unclear whether the technical assumptions required for the bounds will hold for real-world empirical data. For instance, the constraint that the parameter vector `phi` must lie within an R-ball of a chosen `phi_0` seems particularly limiting. The paper does not discuss the potential impact of this constraint on the algorithm's efficacy or how to select `phi_0` and `R` in practice.

4.  **Presentation and Scholarship:** The paper suffers from several presentation issues. The writing contains numerous grammatical errors that hinder readability. The scholarship also appears to be lacking in rigor; for example, the framework seems to be a restatement of the one in [21] under different notation. The problem is referred to as "Supervised PageRank" with a citation to [21], which only appears to discuss a semi-supervised variant. This raises questions about the novelty and positioning of the work. Finally, the motivation for the problem itself could be strengthened to better convey its impact.

In summary, while the theoretical analysis and the proposed algorithm may be powerful, the paper in its current form is not convincing due to the insufficient empirical evidence in the main body, the questionable practical assumptions, and the issues with presentation and scholarship.
