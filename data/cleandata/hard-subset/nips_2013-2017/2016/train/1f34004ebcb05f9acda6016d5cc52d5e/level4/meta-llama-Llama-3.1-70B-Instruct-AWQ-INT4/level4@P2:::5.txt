This paper proposes a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models, which can account for features of nodes and edges in a graph. The authors introduce a non-convex loss-minimization problem and develop two optimization methods, a gradient-based and a gradient-free approach, to solve this problem. The proposed algorithms are based on the concept of an inexact oracle and provide theoretical guarantees on convergence rates.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors clearly explain the limitations of existing methods for learning Supervised PageRank models. They also provide a thorough analysis of the problem and the proposed solutions. The technical assumptions and the loss function are well-defined, and the lemmas bounding the approximate gradient are useful.

The general gradient-free method is well-explained, and the theorem on its performance is a valuable contribution. The adaptation of this method to the learning problem is also well-done, and the bounds on the complexity of the algorithm are useful.

The experimental results are promising, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a web graph. However, the reporting of the results is limited, and the analysis is relegated to the appendix. The authors should consider including more details on the experimental setup, such as the choice of parameters, the size of the graph, and the number of iterations.

The writing is clear, but there are some grammatical errors that need to be corrected. The citations are mostly accurate, but there are some minor issues, such as the citation to [21] for Supervised PageRank, which only appears to be semi-supervised page rank.

One of the main concerns is that the problem solved may not be impactful enough. While the authors provide strong bounds and analysis, it is unclear whether the technical assumptions will hold in empirical data. Some assumptions, such as the restriction of the search space of phi to an R-ball around some chosen phi0, may be limiting and should be discussed.

Another concern is that the paper relies heavily on the appendix, which may not be easily accessible to all readers. The authors should consider including proof outlines and full empirical results in the main body of the paper.

In terms of the practical consequences of the improvements, the authors should provide more discussion on the implications of their results. What are the benefits of using their approach compared to existing methods? How can their algorithm be applied in practice?

To address these concerns, the authors could consider the following:

* Provide more details on the experimental setup and results in the main body of the paper.
* Discuss the implications of their technical assumptions and how they may affect the applicability of their algorithm.
* Include proof outlines and full empirical results in the main body of the paper.
* Provide more discussion on the practical consequences of their improvements and how their algorithm can be applied in practice.

Overall, the paper has some valuable contributions, but it needs to be revised to address the concerns mentioned above.
