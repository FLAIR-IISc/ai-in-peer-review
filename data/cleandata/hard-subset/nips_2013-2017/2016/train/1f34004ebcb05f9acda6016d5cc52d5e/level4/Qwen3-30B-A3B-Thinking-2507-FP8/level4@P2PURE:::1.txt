

This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning parameters in a general graph-based ranking model. The model is defined by a random walk whose node and edge weights are themselves determined by random walks over node and edge features. The optimization problem cannot be solved by existing methods requiring exact objective function values. The proposed approach operates in two levels: at the first level, a linearly convergent method estimates the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, enabling approximation of the loss function value to any desired precision. The authors also develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle, proving its convergence to a stationary point. The primary contribution lies in adapting this approach to constrained optimization when the function value can be computed with known precision, which is then exploited in the second level of the algorithm. The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear correct though I did not verify all of them. My main concern is that the core optimization techniques are adaptations of those in Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015), and the supervised PageRank algorithm has been previously proposed. On a minor note, a conclusion is missing despite space being available. I suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general context to position the algorithm as a specific case, thereby better clarifying the contribution relative to the state-of-the-art.
