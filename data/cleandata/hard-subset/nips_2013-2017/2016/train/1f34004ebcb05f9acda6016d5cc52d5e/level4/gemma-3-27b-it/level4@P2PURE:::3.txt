This paper presents two optimization methods for solving the supervised PageRank problem. In contrast to existing approaches, the authors propose a gradient-based method with a theoretical convergence rate guarantee, capable of achieving a specified accuracy, and a gradient-free method with a guaranteed loss function decrease. Both methods operate without requiring exact objective function values and provide an estimate of their convergence rate, demonstrating improved ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods. The authors clearly state the hyperparameter settings used in their experiments. While the datasets are not publicly available, the descriptions are sufficiently detailed to facilitate reproduction of the results. Specifically, the authors introduce two-level optimization techniques, gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN), built upon the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17). By avoiding the need to compute large matrices, the proposed methods maintain strong performance while providing a proven estimate of the convergence rate for a given accuracy.

The paper thoroughly details the methods from Section 2 to Section 5, with proofs included in the supplementary material. However, the numerous symbols used throughout the equations make the paper difficult to follow, and a symbol table would be highly beneficial. For example, the meaning of ‘N’ (representing the number of steps) is not explicitly stated until Line 193, despite its use earlier in Line 139 and Equation 3.1. Furthermore, the experimental evaluation primarily compares the proposed methods to GBP in terms of loss function values, rather than directly evaluating ranking quality. Given that this work generalizes ref:17, a direct comparison to that baseline would be valuable to assess the performance improvements achieved by the authors.
