This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss minimization problem arising in the learning of parameters for a general graph-based ranking model. This model is defined by a random walk weighted by node and edge attributes, which are themselves determined by random walks over node and edge features. Due to the intractability of directly optimizing this loss function with existing methods that require exact objective function values, the authors propose a two-level approach. First, they employ a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk. They validate this approximation, demonstrating that the loss function can be approximated to any desired precision. Second, they develop a gradient method for constrained, non-convex optimization problems using an inexact oracle, and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The core contribution lies in adapting this approach to constrained optimization scenarios where the function value is available only with a known precision, proving the method’s convergence, and applying it within the second level of their algorithm. The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear correct, though a complete verification was not performed. However, the primary optimization techniques presented appear to be adaptations of those found in Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), and the concept of supervised PageRank has been previously introduced. A concluding section summarizing the work and future directions would be beneficial. Furthermore, framing the optimization problem in a more general context, and demonstrating how the proposed algorithm represents a specific instance of a broader class of methods, would help to better position the contribution relative to the existing state-of-the-art.
