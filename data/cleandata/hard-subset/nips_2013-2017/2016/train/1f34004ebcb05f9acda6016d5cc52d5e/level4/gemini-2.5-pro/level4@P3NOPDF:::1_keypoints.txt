- Paper studies convergence for non-convex loss minimization in a graph-based ranking model where random-walk transition probabilities are feature-parameterized.  
- The objective depends on the stationary distribution of the random walk, which cannot be computed exactly, preventing standard optimization.  
- Authors propose a two-level optimization: an inner linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution and an outer gradient-based method.  
- Inner-level result: the stationary distribution (and thus the loss) can be approximated to any given precision.  
- Outer-level result: a gradient-based algorithm for constrained non-convex optimization that operates with an inexact oracle (approximate objective and gradients).  
- Main theoretical contribution: a convergence proof to a stationary point under inexact objective/gradient evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written, addresses a significant problem, and the theoretical analysis and proofs appear sound.  
- Contribution is valuable for problems where exact function evaluations are computationally infeasible.  
- Concern about novelty: optimization techniques seem like a direct adaptation of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), and supervised PageRank has prior work.  
- Recommendation to better articulate unique contributions and detail the theoretical hurdles overcome in extending prior frameworks to constrained, non-convex settings with inexact oracles.  
- Suggestion to present the optimization problem in a more general context and show the graph-ranking algorithm as an instance to clarify generality.  
- Minor issue: the paper lacks a conclusion—add a summary, limitations, and future research directions.
