- Addresses non-convex loss minimization for Supervised PageRank parameters using two-level optimization with inexact oracle and provides convergence guarantees lacking in prior work [21].
- Adapts Nesterov & Spokoiny’s gradient-free method [16] and Nesterov’s gradient methods [7] to constrained non-convex problems with inexact oracle information.
- Main contribution: tailoring existing optimization frameworks to Supervised PageRank where loss and derivatives are approximated with known accuracy.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence rate guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods.
- Experimental results show both proposed algorithms outperform state-of-the-art [21], with the gradient-based method achieving better convergence.
- Well-structured paper with clear mathematical formulations and seemingly sound proofs.
- Carefully analyzes the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity, aiding practical implementation.
- Novelty is limited because core optimization techniques are adaptations rather than fundamentally new methods.
- Does not sufficiently emphasize the connection to Nesterov & Spokoiny [16] or clearly differentiate from prior Supervised PageRank works [21, 22].
- Conclusion is brief and could better synthesize main contributions and their significance.
- Lacks a broader discussion of how the proposed framework fits within the wider optimization landscape and its practical implications.
- Technically sound with well-supported theoretical claims.
- Clear writing and consistent notation; experiments are clear but practical implications of theory need more discussion.
- Original application to Supervised PageRank with inexact oracle is meaningful but should more explicitly establish novelty.
- Significant practical relevance: improved performance on a real ranking task and new convergence guarantees for this problem.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to better articulate novelty and contextualize the work within related literature.
