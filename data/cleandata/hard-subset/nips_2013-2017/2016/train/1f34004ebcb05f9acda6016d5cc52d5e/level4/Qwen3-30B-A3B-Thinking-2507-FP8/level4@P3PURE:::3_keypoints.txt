- Proposes two methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- GBN offers a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate to a given accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function.
- Both methods avoid exact objective evaluations and provide convergence rate estimates.
- Experiments show superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art baselines.
- Hyperparameters are explicitly provided; datasets aren’t public but experiments are described clearly enough for reproducibility.
- Two-level optimization framework builds on Nesterov & Nemirovski (ref:17) and avoids explicit large-matrix computations while retaining strong performance.
- Technical details and all proofs are comprehensively covered in Sections 2–5 and the supplement.
- Suggest adding a symbol table to improve readability (notation is hard to track; e.g., N is ambiguous).
- Recommend comparing ranking quality (the primary metric) rather than only loss values against GBP.
- Recommend direct performance comparisons with ref:17 to clarify the gains from the generalization.
