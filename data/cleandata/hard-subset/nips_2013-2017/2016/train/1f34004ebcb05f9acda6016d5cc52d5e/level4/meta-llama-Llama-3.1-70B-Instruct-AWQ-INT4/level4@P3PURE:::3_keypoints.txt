- Proposes two optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence guarantees without needing exact objective values.
- Gradient-free method guarantees decrease in loss; both methods estimate convergence rates and avoid computing large matrices.
- Claims to outperform state-of-the-art methods in ranking quality.
- Hyper-parameters are provided; datasets are not public but are clearly described to facilitate reproduction.
- Methods are two-level optimizations building on Nesterov & Nemirovski (ref. 17), leveraging their lower- and upper-level techniques.
- Paper includes detailed method descriptions and proofs in supplements, but readability suffers from many symbols and missing early definitions.
- Recommends adding a symbol table and explicitly defining variables (e.g., N) when first introduced.
- Experiments compare only to GBP on loss; should include ranking-quality comparisons and a direct comparison to ref. 17 to assess improvement.
