- Problem: Learning parameters for Supervised PageRank yields a non-convex loss where objective and gradient cannot be computed exactly.
- Approach: Two-level optimization scheme combining a lower-level inexact PageRank oracle with an upper-level parameter optimizer.
- Lower-level: Use a linearly convergent method to approximate the PageRank stationary distribution and its derivatives, providing a controllable inexact oracle.
- Upper-level methods: A random gradient-free method (adapted from Nesterov & Spokoiny) for constrained problems with noisy function values.
- Upper-level methods: A gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization operating with an inexact first-order oracle.
- Theory: Formal convergence analyses and computational complexity bounds linking lower-level precision to upper-level iterations.
- Empirics: Experiments on web page ranking show the proposed methods outperform the existing gradient-based Supervised PageRank baseline.
- Strength — Rigor: First formal convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank, addressing a prior theoretical gap.
- Strength — Methodology: Clear adaptation and analysis of gradient-free and gradient-based frameworks for constrained inexact-oracle settings.
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, well-structured paper separating general frameworks from the specific application.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Practical effectiveness demonstrated alongside theoretical results.
- Weakness — Originality: Core algorithms are adaptations of known methods, so novelty mainly lies in application and analysis rather than new optimization principles.
- Weakness — Framing: Paper focuses on Supervised PageRank despite frameworks being more generally applicable; broader positioning would increase impact.
- Suggestions: Add a conclusion section summarizing findings and future work.
- Suggestions: Reframe introduction/methods to present the optimization frameworks generally and Supervised PageRank as a motivating application.
- Overall verdict: Solid, technically sound contribution with valuable theory and experiments; leans toward acceptance despite limited fundamental novelty.
