- Paper extends PageRank to include node/edge weights from a feature model and combines stationary-distribution approximation with gradient-free optimization for noisy loss estimates.
- Authors reproduce a framework from prior work [21] under different name/notation and discuss challenging technical assumptions and loss structure.
- Provide lemmas bounding the approximate gradient and outline a general biased gradient-free oracle method.
- Construct a specific algorithm for the learning problem by adapting the gradient-free method and state performance bounds.
- Experimental results are brief and deferred to the appendix, with main-body lacking detailed results, tables, or analysis.
- Some experimental parameters are not reported (e.g., choice of phi0 and R), and it’s unclear if multiple or diverse graphs were tested.
- Claims that the novel algorithm outperforms others are not substantiated with clear extent or practical implications.
- Concern that technical assumptions (e.g., restricting phi search to an R-ball around phi0) may not hold in real data and could limit applicability.
- Majority of theoretical and empirical content is relegated to the appendix, with no proof sketches or full results in the main text.
- Writing quality and grammar are poor, and citations are sloppy (misnaming supervised vs semi-supervised PageRank).
- Reviewer questions the practical impact and significance of the work despite strong theoretical bounds.
