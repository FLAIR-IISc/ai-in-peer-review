

This paper proposes two novel two-level optimization methods for learning Supervised PageRank models, addressing a non-convex loss-minimization problem that accounts for features of nodes and edges in graphs. The authors introduce both gradient-based and gradient-free approaches that provide theoretical convergence guarantees, which is a significant advancement over the state-of-the-art method by Zhukovskii et al. (NIPS 2015, "Supervised Nested PageRank") that lacked such guarantees. The work builds upon and extends the framework of inexact oracles, drawing connections to recent NIPS contributions such as Nesterov's work on inexact oracle methods (NIPS 2015, "First-order methods of smooth convex optimization with inexact oracle") and the gradient-free optimization methods of Nesterov and Spokoiny (NIPS 2011, "Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions").

The paper makes several significant contributions: (1) it provides the first convergence rate guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank models using gradient-based optimization; (2) it develops a gradient-free method for constrained optimization problems with inexact oracle; (3) it analyzes the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity; and (4) it demonstrates empirical superiority over the state-of-the-art method on a real-world ranking task.

The strengths of this paper are substantial. The theoretical contributions are strong and well-motivated, with careful analysis of the convergence rates for both proposed methods. The paper provides precise complexity bounds for the algorithms, which is a crucial aspect of optimization work. The experimental section is well-designed, with appropriate comparison to the state-of-the-art method on a meaningful web ranking task using real-world data from Yandex. The results consistently show that both proposed algorithms outperform the existing method in terms of loss function values, with statistically significant improvements (p < 0.005 in all cases). The paper also demonstrates the practical relevance of the work by showing that the algorithms can increase the reliability and speed of search engines.

However, there are several weaknesses that need to be addressed. The paper lacks a detailed discussion of the computational time complexity of the proposed methods compared to the state-of-the-art. While the paper provides arithmetic operation counts, it does not convert these into actual running times or provide a time complexity analysis that would help practitioners understand the practical efficiency of the algorithms. The experimental section should include timing information for the different methods to allow readers to assess the practical trade-offs between accuracy and speed.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the assumptions made in the theoretical analysis. The authors assume that f(ϕ) is convex on the feasible set Φ, but the paper does not provide evidence or discussion about how restrictive this assumption is in practice, especially for the specific ranking task used in the experiments. The paper also lacks a comparison with other optimization methods that might be applicable to this problem, such as stochastic gradient methods.

Regarding the four key criteria:
- Quality: The theoretical analysis is sound and well-executed, with careful attention to convergence rates and complexity bounds. The experimental validation is comprehensive and well-designed, though the lack of timing information weakens the practical assessment.
- Clarity: The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear mathematical formulations. However, some technical details in the proofs (particularly in the supplementary materials) could be made more accessible to a broader audience of NIPS readers.
- Originality: The paper makes a clear contribution to the literature by providing the first convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank models, extending recent work on inexact oracles to a new application domain. The combination of gradient-free and gradient-based methods with inexact oracle concepts represents a novel approach.
- Significance: The results are significant for both theoretical optimization and practical applications in web ranking. The demonstrated improvements in ranking quality with theoretical guarantees have clear potential for real-world impact in search engine technology.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Provides the first convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank models
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and well-executed
- Empirical results demonstrate consistent improvements over the state-of-the-art
- Addresses a practically important problem in web ranking
- Makes a clear contribution to optimization theory through the inexact oracle framework

Arguments against acceptance:
- Lacks detailed computational time analysis, which is crucial for practical adoption
- The assumption of convexity on the feasible set may be restrictive in practice
- Does not compare with other potential optimization methods that might be applicable to this problem
- Some technical details in the proofs could be made more accessible

Overall, this paper makes a strong theoretical contribution to optimization with clear practical implications for web ranking applications. The theoretical guarantees provided are particularly valuable as they address a critical limitation of previous work. The main weakness is the lack of computational time analysis, which should be addressed to fully demonstrate the practical viability of the proposed methods. Given the significance of the problem and the strength of the theoretical contributions, I recommend acceptance with the understanding that the authors should provide additional timing information in the camera-ready version.

The paper is well-suited for NIPS as it addresses an important problem in machine learning with both theoretical depth and practical relevance, aligning well with the conference's focus on high-quality scientific contributions to the field.
