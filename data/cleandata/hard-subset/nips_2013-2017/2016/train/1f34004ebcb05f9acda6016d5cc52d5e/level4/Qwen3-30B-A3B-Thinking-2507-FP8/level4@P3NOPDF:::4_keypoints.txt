- Proposes gradient-based and random-gradient-free optimization methods for training supervised PageRank under a non-convex loss framework.  
- Core contribution: adapts optimization techniques to a learning problem where convex methods don't apply.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for the gradient-based method.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements over baselines on benchmark datasets.  
- Technical approach is sound with clear exposition of optimization challenges for non-convex PageRank.  
- Random-gradient-free methods (e.g., CMA-ES/Bayesian optimization variants) are relevant when gradients are expensive or noisy.  
- Empirical evaluation compares against relevant baselines and shows competitive accuracy and robustness.  
- Major omission: no wall-clock training times reported, preventing assessment of computational efficiency and scalability.  
- Recommends adding a table of total training times across datasets/hardware to clarify trade-offs between methods.  
- Suggests sharpening the problem statement by briefly explaining the source of non-convexity (e.g., composition of PageRank operator with the loss).  
- Minor issues: occasional grammar errors (e.g., subject-verb agreement) and need to better distinguish novelty from prior non-convex graph-optimization work.  
- Overall judgement: promising and technically plausible but borderline for NIPS until runtime/efficiency data and minor clarifications are added.
