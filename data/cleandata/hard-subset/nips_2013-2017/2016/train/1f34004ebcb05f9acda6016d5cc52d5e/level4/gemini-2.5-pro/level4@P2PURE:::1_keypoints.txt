- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem in a graph-based ranking model.  
- Model: random walk with node/edge weights determined by a secondary random walk over node/edge features.  
- Key challenge: objective function cannot be computed exactly, so standard optimization methods that need exact values are inapplicable.  
- Proposed two-level approach: first level uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution, enabling arbitrarily precise loss approximation.  
- Second level: develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adapts inexact-oracle optimization to constrained problems where function values are computable only up to a known precision.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not verified in detail).  
- Major concern: core optimization techniques seem to be adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and supervised PageRank has prior work.  
- Minor issues: paper lacks a conclusion.  
- Suggestion: present the optimization problem in a more general context and then show the studied algorithm as a specific instance to better position contributions.
