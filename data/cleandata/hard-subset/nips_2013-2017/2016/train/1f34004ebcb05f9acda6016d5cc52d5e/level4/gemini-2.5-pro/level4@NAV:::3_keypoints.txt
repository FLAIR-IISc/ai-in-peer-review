- Proposes two novel optimization algorithms for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based (GBN) and a gradient-free (GFN) method.
- GBN has a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee and can reach a pre-specified accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a quantifiable decrease in the loss function.
- Both methods operate without needing the exact objective value and include proven convergence-rate estimates.
- Empirical results show superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art techniques.
- Necessary hyperparameters are provided for both methods.
- Datasets are not public but are described in enough detail to likely allow reproduction.
- Core contribution: two-level optimization framework built on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref. 17) that avoids forming/manipulating large matrices.
- Theoretical and methodological details are presented in Sections 2–5, with full proofs in the supplementary material.
- Paper clarity suffers from dense notation; a symbol table is recommended.
- Example of clarity issue: the symbol N is used early (Line 139, Eq. 3.1) but only defined later (Line 193).
- Experimental comparison against the GBP baseline is limited to loss; final ranking quality should also be compared.
- A direct empirical comparison with the key baseline (ref. 17) is missing and would strengthen claims of generalization and improvement.
