- Addresses learning parameters for Supervised PageRank where loss/gradient depend on an intractable stationary distribution of a large Markov chain.  
- Proposes two two-level optimization algorithms (gradient-based GBN and random gradient-free GFN) that use inexact numerical oracles for loss and gradient.  
- Main contribution: first theoretical convergence rate guarantees and complexity bounds for these methods applied to Supervised PageRank.  
- Adapts general optimization frameworks to a constrained, non-convex setting and analyzes the trade-off between oracle accuracy and overall complexity.  
- Empirical validation on a large real-world web-ranking dataset shows GBN and GFN outperform prior baseline (GBP) in final test loss.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis addressing inexact oracles with explicit theorems (Theorems 1–4).  
- Strength: practical significance demonstrated by improved empirical performance on a commercial-scale dataset.  
- Strength: paper is generally clear, well-structured, and explains the upper/lower-level distinction effectively.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks practical computational-cost comparisons (e.g., wall-clock time or operation counts).  
- Weakness: unconventional plotting of test loss per iteration risks leakage/model-selection concerns; training loss curves should be shown instead.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (Lipschitz constant L and accuracy ε); more analysis should be moved into the main paper for reproducibility.
