- Analyzes convergence for learning parameters of a general graph-based ranking model defined by a feature-driven random walk.  
- Existing optimization methods are inapplicable because they require exact objective values.  
- Proposes a two-level algorithm: first level uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution.  
- Shows the loss function value can be approximated to any prescribed precision via the first-level estimator.  
- Develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Adapts known techniques specifically to the constrained case where function values are computed with bounded error.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (not exhaustively checked).  
- Main concern: proposed optimization methods are adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), reducing novelty.  
- Additional concern: supervised PageRank has prior work and is not entirely new.  
- Suggestion: present the optimization problem in a more general context and position the algorithm as a specific instance.  
- Suggestion: add a conclusion section summarizing key findings and implications.  
- Strengths: novel approach to a non-convex graph-ranking learning problem, inexact-oracle gradient method with convergence proof, clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: limited originality due to adaptations of existing methods, prior supervised PageRank work, missing conclusion section.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.
