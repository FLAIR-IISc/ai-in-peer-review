This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem, leveraging node and edge properties. The authors propose two solution approaches: a gradient-free method utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle, and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle. My primary concern is the omission of experimental results from the main text, rendering the paper incomplete. Detailed comments follow: 1. The placement of experimental results solely in the supplementary material detracts from the completeness of the main text. 2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the function in (2.4) is demonstrably non-convex. The paper lacks a discussion on how to select the set $\Phi$ to ensure convexity of the objective function. 3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. It is unclear whether this bound guarantees convergence of the sequence $x_k$ to a stationary point. 4. As shown in Figure 1 of the supplementary material, GFNM exhibits slow convergence. Reporting on the computational efficiency of both algorithms would be beneficial.
