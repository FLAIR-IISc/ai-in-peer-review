- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).  
- GBN offers a gradient-based approach with a provable convergence rate and ability to reach a specified accuracy.  
- GFN is gradient-free and guarantees a decrease in the loss function value.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective values and provide convergence rate estimates.  
- Methods avoid computing large matrices while still achieving strong results.  
- Claims to outperform state-of-the-art methods in ranking quality.  
- Hyper-parameters are provided; experimental description is clear enough for reproduction despite datasets not being public.  
- Methods build on Nesterov & Nemirovski’s two-level optimization framework (ref:17); should compare directly to that reference.  
- Paper gives detailed method exposition with full proofs in supplementary material.  
- Clarity issues: many symbols are introduced without a symbol table and some (e.g., N) are used before explanation.  
- Experimental limitation: comparison is only to GBP on loss function, lacking direct ranking-quality comparison to ref:17.  
- Overall assessment: promising results but needs revisions (symbol clarity and broader experimental comparisons) to strengthen contribution.
