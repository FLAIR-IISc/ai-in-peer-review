In this paper, the authors propose two optimization methods to solve the supervised PageRank problem. Unlike other existing methods, this paper presents a gradient-based method with a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and the ability to achieve a given accuracy. The proposed gradient-free method has a guaranteed decrease in the loss function value. Notably, these two methods do not require the exact value of the objective function and provide an estimate of the convergence rate. Moreover, their performance surpasses state-of-the-art methods in terms of ranking quality. The hyper-parameters used in both methods are provided, and although the datasets are not publicly available, their descriptions are clear, making it feasible to reproduce the results.

The authors propose two two-level optimization methods, namely gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN), to solve the supervised PageRank problem. The lower-level and upper-level optimization is based on Nesterov and Nemirovski's work (ref: 17). A key advantage of the proposed method is that it does not require the exact value of the objective function and has a proven estimate of the convergence rate, given a specified accuracy. Consequently, the proposed method can avoid computing large matrices while still achieving good results.

From Section 2 to Section 5, the authors provide a detailed explanation of their methods and include all the proofs in the supplementary material. However, a symbol table would be a valuable addition, as the numerous symbols in the equations can be overwhelming, making it easy to lose track of them. For instance, the authors use the symbol $N$ in Line 139 and equation 3.1 without explaining its meaning (number of steps) until Line 193. Furthermore, in the experiment section, the authors only compare their work with GBP in terms of the loss function, rather than the ranking quality. Additionally, since this work is a generalization of ref: 17, a direct comparison with it would be beneficial, allowing readers to assess whether the performance is indeed improved.
