This paper studies the convergence of a non-convex loss minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model, specified by a random walk whose transition weights depend on nodes and edges, which themselves are determined by random walks over node and edge features. The optimization task cannot be handled by existing methods that require exact evaluations of the objective function. The proposed approach therefore operates in two stages. At the first stage, a linearly convergent method is employed to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk. This step is validated, and the authors show that the loss value can be approximated to any prescribed accuracy. They also develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle, and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The contribution lies in adapting the approach to the constrained setting where the function value can be computed only within a known precision. They prove convergence of this method and use it at the second stage of the overall algorithm. The paper is well written, and the proofs appear correct, although I did not check all of them in detail. My concern is that the main optimization components are adaptations of those in “Yurii Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny, Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions, Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015, pp. 1–40,” and the supervised PageRank algorithm has also been proposed previously. On a minor note, a conclusion section is expected, especially since there seems to be room for one. I suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general framework, positioning the studied algorithm as a special case. This would better situate the contribution with respect to the state of the art.
