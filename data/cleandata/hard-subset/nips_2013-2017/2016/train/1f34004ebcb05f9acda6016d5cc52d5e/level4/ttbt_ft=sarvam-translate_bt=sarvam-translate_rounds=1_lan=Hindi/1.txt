This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem to learn the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model, defined by a random walk driven by the weights of nodes and edges, which in turn are defined by random walks driven by the features of the nodes and edges. The optimization problem cannot be solved by existing optimization methods, which require exact values of the objective function. Therefore, the proposed approach works in two stages. In the first stage, a linearly convergent method is used to estimate the constant distribution of the Markov random walk. This approach has been validated by others, and the authors have shown that the value of the loss function can be estimated with any given precision. They also develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems using an exact oracle, and prove its convergence at the stable point of the problem. The contribution of the approach to optimization in the case of constrained optimization problems lies when the value of the function can be computed with some known precision. They prove the convergence of this method and use it in the second stage of the proposed algorithm. The paper is well-written, and the evidence seems correct, although I have not seen all of it. My concern is that the main optimization points created here are adaptations of those proposed in "Yuri. Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny, Random gradient-free minimization of convex functions, Foundations of computational mathematics, 2015, pp. 1-40," and the supervised page rank algorithm has also been proposed before. At a trivial level, a conclusion is expected, especially that there is a place to write one. I would suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general context, showing the algorithm studied as a specific case. This would allow for better placement of contributions with respect to the state of the art.
