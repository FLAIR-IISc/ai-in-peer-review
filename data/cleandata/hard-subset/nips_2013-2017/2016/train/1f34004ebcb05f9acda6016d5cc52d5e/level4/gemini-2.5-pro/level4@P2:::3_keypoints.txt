- Proposes two two-level optimization algorithms for Supervised PageRank: gradient-based (GBN) and random gradient-free (GFN), using an inexact oracle framework.  
- Uses Nesterov & Nemirovski’s method to efficiently approximate PageRank stationary distribution on the lower level.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees: GBN converges to stationary points for non-convex problems; GFN has provable rates for convex problems.  
- Empirically outperforms prior state-of-the-art (GBP) on a large proprietary web browsing dataset by achieving lower loss values.  
- Strength: first work to give formal convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank, moving beyond heuristics.  
- Strength: practically relevant for large-scale graphs by avoiding exact stationary/gradient computation; gradient-free method reduces gradient-matrix cost.  
- Strength: experimental validation on a large real-world dataset supports empirical claims.  
- Weakness: paper is technically dense with confusing notation and many symbols introduced without immediate definition.  
- Suggestion: include a symbol table and define key parameters (e.g., N) at first appearance to improve readability.  
- Weakness: evaluation reports only loss values, not standard ranking metrics, so claims of improved ranking quality are not fully substantiated.  
- Suggestion: report ranking metrics (nDCG, MAP, Kendall’s Tau) on held-out test data to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Weakness: choice of lower-level method vs. power method is justified only in supplementary material; include that comparison in the main paper.  
- Weakness: dataset is proprietary, limiting reproducibility despite clear dataset construction description.
