- Addresses learning parameters for Supervised PageRank and identifies lack of convergence guarantees in prior work [21].
- Main contribution: two new optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with formal performance guarantees treating loss/gradient as an inexact oracle.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis, including convergence rates and complexity bounds for both algorithms.
- Strength: places Supervised PageRank training on firmer theoretical foundations with provable convergence and analysis of oracle accuracy vs. computational cost.
- Weakness: essential proofs and detailed experimental results are relegated to the supplementary material, leaving the main text non–self-contained.
- Experimental section is too brief and lacks crucial hyperparameter details (e.g., choice of center ϕ̂ and radius R for parameter ball).
- Evaluation scope is limited to a single user browsing graph from one data source, reducing evidence of robustness and generalizability.
- Insufficient reporting: main text lacks quantitative results/tables and discussion linking loss reduction to practical search ranking improvements.
- Theoretical assumptions are not justified for real-world use: local convexity for gradient-free method is questionable in practice.
- Practical limitation: constraining parameters to a predefined Euclidean ball may be overly restrictive and hinder finding true optima.
- Originality/citations concern: formulation appears to closely reimplement Supervised Nested PageRank [21], and the paper inconsistently names/précises prior work.
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contributions but requires substantial revision to include key experimental results, hyperparameter details, and discussion of practical assumptions to be publishable.
