- Proposes gradient-based and random-gradient-free methods to solve a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning supervised PageRank models.
- Addresses a relevant and challenging learning-to-rank problem on graph-structured data.
- Offering both first-order and zeroth-order solvers broadens applicability to settings where gradients are expensive or unavailable.
- Main weakness: empirical evaluation omits analysis of computational efficiency.
- Recommend reporting wall-clock training time to convergence and/or time per iteration for each method.
- Suggest providing convergence plots (loss vs. time or iterations) to illustrate practical behavior.
- Advise direct runtime comparisons between gradient-based, random-gradient-free methods, and relevant baselines.
- Optional but recommended: analyze scalability of training time with graph size and training set size.
- Conclude that including a thorough computational-efficiency study is essential to fully assess practical trade-offs.
