- Proposes learning node and edge weights in a PageRank-like model by combining stationary-distribution approximation with a gradient-free optimizer for estimated losses.  
- Strong theoretical development: lemmas bounding approximate gradients and a general theorem on the gradient-free method’s performance.  
- Major presentation issues: substantial core material is relegated to the appendix and exposition lacks proof sketches.  
- Incorrect attribution: claims to reproduce “Supervised PageRank” while [21] actually describes semi-supervised PageRank, suggesting poor literature familiarity.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient and mostly in the appendix, using only a single web graph from prior work.  
- Key experimental details (e.g., parameter settings, choices of φ₀ and R) are omitted or unexplained.  
- Unclear whether experiments were run on more than one graph, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Claims of outperforming existing methods lack discussion of effect size or statistical significance.  
- Absence of result tables and concrete metrics in the main text impedes assessment of practical impact.  
- The restriction of the search space to an R-ball around φ₀ may unduly limit efficacy and is not analyzed.  
- Concerns about whether the theoretical assumptions hold on real-world data.  
- Overall: promising theory and methods but failing to convincingly demonstrate practical benefits or generalizability.
