

This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models, which extend traditional PageRank by incorporating node and edge features through linear combinations with model parameters. The authors propose two novel two-level optimization methods: a gradient-based approach and a gradient-free method, both designed to solve the non-convex loss-minimization problem introduced in [21]. Their work builds upon the foundational Supervised PageRank framework presented at NIPS 2015 [21] and extends the theoretical understanding of optimization for this problem. Unlike previous work that used gradient-based methods without convergence guarantees [21], the authors provide rigorous convergence rate analysis for both their methods, leveraging the concept of an inexact oracle. The paper also investigates the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity, which is a significant theoretical contribution.

The paper demonstrates strong theoretical quality, providing detailed convergence rate guarantees for both optimization methods. The authors thoroughly analyze the complexity bounds for their algorithms, showing how the accuracy of the lower-level approximations affects the overall computational requirements. The experimental evaluation on a web page ranking task demonstrates that both proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art [21] in terms of ranking quality, with the gradient-based method showing particular promise in reducing the number of iterations required for convergence.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, with precise mathematical formulations that would allow expert readers to reproduce the results. The connection to previous NIPS work on PageRank and supervised ranking [12, 21] is well-established, and the authors appropriately cite relevant literature.

The main strengths of this paper include:
1. Theoretical novelty in providing convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank optimization
2. Rigorous analysis of computational complexity and trade-offs
3. Demonstrated empirical superiority over existing methods
4. Clear presentation of the mathematical framework

The main weaknesses include:
1. Limited discussion of computational time in the experiments, which is crucial for practical applications
2. The gradient-free method's implementation details could be more thoroughly explained
3. The paper would benefit from a more detailed comparison of the two proposed methods in terms of computational efficiency

The significance of this work lies in its application to web ranking problems, where the authors demonstrate that their methods can improve ranking quality while providing theoretical guarantees. This has direct implications for search engine optimization and could lead to more reliable ranking systems. The paper advances the state of the art by being the first to provide convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning using both gradient-based and gradient-free methods.

Overall, this is a strong contribution to the field with clear theoretical advances and practical applications. The work meets all the criteria for a high-quality NIPS paper, particularly in terms of quality and significance. The main suggestion for improvement is to include more detailed computational time analysis in the experimental section to fully demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed methods.
