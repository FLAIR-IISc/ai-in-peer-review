- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- GBN offers a gradient-based approach with theoretical convergence rate guarantees and target accuracy.  
- GFN ensures a monotonic decrease in the loss function without needing gradients.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective function values and avoid computing large matrices while providing convergence-rate estimates.  
- Reported results claim superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods.  
- Hyperparameters for both methods are explicitly specified.  
- Datasets are not public, but dataset descriptions are clear enough to enable reproduction.  
- Methods build on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17) for lower- and upper-level optimization.  
- Manuscript contains detailed method descriptions (Sections 2–5) and proofs in the supplementary material.  
- Readability issues: many symbols; recommend a symbol table (e.g., N introduced in Line 139/equation 3.1 but not explained until Line 193).  
- Experimental weaknesses: comparisons are limited to GBP on loss—should include ranking-quality comparisons and a direct comparison to ref:17.
