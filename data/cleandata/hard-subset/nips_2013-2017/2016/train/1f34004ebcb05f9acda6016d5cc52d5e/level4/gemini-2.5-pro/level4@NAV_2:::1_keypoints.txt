Addresses non-convex loss-minimization for learning Supervised PageRank parameters and identifies lack of formal convergence guarantees in prior power-method-based training.  
Proposes a two-level optimization framework based on an inexact oracle with full convergence analysis.  
Lower level: uses a linearly convergent solver from [17] to approximate the stationary distribution and control approximation error for function and gradient.  
Upper level: develops a novel gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization with convergence guarantees.  
Upper level: adapts Nesterov & Spokoiny’s random gradient-free method to constrained settings with known, bounded function evaluation error.  
Provides theoretical convergence rates and computational-complexity analysis that balance lower-level precision and overall performance.  
Empirical results on a real-world web ranking task show improvements over the prior state-of-the-art.  
Strength: fills an important theoretical gap by giving the first formal convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank training.  
Strength: technically sound derivations and careful complexity bounds that account for the two-level structure.  
Strength: well-written, clearly structured presentation that makes the approach understandable to experts.  
Strength: experimental validation supports theoretical claims and shows better final loss than baseline.  
Weakness: core algorithms are adaptations of existing frameworks rather than fundamentally new methods.  
Weakness: contribution could be positioned more broadly by presenting the general optimization framework first to emphasize applicability beyond PageRank.  
Minor suggestion: add a dedicated conclusion section to summarize contributions and suggest future work.
