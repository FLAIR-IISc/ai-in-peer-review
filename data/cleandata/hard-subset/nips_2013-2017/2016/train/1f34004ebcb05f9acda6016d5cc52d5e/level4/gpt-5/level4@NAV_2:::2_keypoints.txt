- Paper frames supervised PageRank learning as a constrained non-convex problem with node/edge features and proposes two solvers (randomized gradient-free with inexact zero-order oracle; gradient-based with inexact first-order oracle) with convergence-rate guarantees, but experiments are mostly in the supplement, making the submission feel incomplete.

- Key empirical results are relegated to the supplementary material, leaving the main text incomplete and requiring constant reference to the appendix.

- Theorem 1 assumes convexity while the objective is non-convex; unclear how the feasible set Phi is chosen so f becomes convex over Phi in practice.

- Theorem 3 provides a bound on MK(x_K − x_{K+1}); unclear if this guarantees convergence to a stationary point (e.g., via gradient mapping or projected gradient norm) or is only a stopping-criterion surrogate.

- Figure 1 (supplement) suggests the gradient-free method converges slowly; the paper should report and compare computational efficiency metrics (wall-clock time, iteration counts, lower-level oracle costs) across methods.
