- Summary: Proposes a two-level framework for learning feature-aware Supervised PageRank with provable convergence in an inexact-oracle setting; lower level approximates stationary distributions and derivatives with a linearly convergent method, upper level uses a constrained random gradient-free method and an adaptive inexact first-order method, with complexity guarantees and empirical loss improvements.

- Relation to previous work: Builds on Supervised PageRank and inexact/zeroth-/first-order optimization literature, replacing power iteration with a Nesterov–Nemirovski linearly convergent solver and adapting known optimization methods to constrained, noisy oracles.

- Strengths: Rigorous two-level design controlling oracle errors, convergence-rate guarantees for both gradients-free and gradient-based variants, adaptive step-size without known Lipschitz, extended derivative estimation, and end-to-end complexity bounds validated empirically.

- Weaknesses and questions: Incremental originality (adaptations of known methods); experiments focus on training loss not standard IR metrics; limited dataset scale/diversity and missing runtime comparisons; unclear assumptions and hyperparameter sensitivity; reproducibility details lacking.

- Quality: Theoretical development is well-structured with sound lemmas/theorems linking lower-level accuracy to upper-level convergence; end-to-end complexity accounting is valuable though constants/assumptions need clarification.

- Clarity: Generally well written but has grammatical issues, notation overload, and could benefit from reorganizing to present general frameworks first and a concise conclusion; references need cleanup.

- Originality: Moderate — extensions and end-to-end guarantees are useful, but core methods largely adapt established techniques.

- Significance: Potentially useful for practical training of feature-aware random-walk ranking models with inexact oracles, but limited empirical scope weakens real-world impact claims.

- Pros for acceptance: Strong theoretical guarantees, practical two-level scheme with experimental improvement over baseline, careful handling of inexact oracles with broadly applicable extensions.

- Cons for acceptance: Incremental novelty, limited experimental metrics/baselines and dataset scale, presentation and assumption-clarity issues.

- Suggestions for improvement: Add NDCG/MAP on held-out queries, runtime and variance across seeds, ablations for N/N1/N2 and hyperparameters, comparisons to power iteration, clearer assumptions and hyperparameter guidance, reorganize presentation, fix grammar and references.

- Overall: Well-executed application of inexact-oracle optimization to Supervised PageRank with sound analysis and promising results, but moderate novelty and a need for stronger, broader empirical evaluation.
