- Paper studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem for a graph-based ranking model where node/edge weights are given by random walks on features.  
- The optimization problem cannot be solved by existing methods that require exact objective values.  
- Authors propose a two-level approach: level one estimates the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk using a linearly convergent method, validated experimentally.  
- They show the loss function value can be approximated to arbitrary precision.  
- They develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adapting and proving convergence of this inexact-oracle approach for constrained problems where function values are computable with known precision, and applying it at the second level.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear correct (though not exhaustively checked).  
- Concerns about novelty: methods resemble adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) gradient-free techniques.  
- Supervised PageRank has been proposed previously, raising questions about originality of the application.  
- Minor issue: missing conclusion section despite available space.  
- Suggestion: present the optimization problem in a more general context and position the studied algorithm as a specific case to clarify contribution and relation to the state-of-the-art.
