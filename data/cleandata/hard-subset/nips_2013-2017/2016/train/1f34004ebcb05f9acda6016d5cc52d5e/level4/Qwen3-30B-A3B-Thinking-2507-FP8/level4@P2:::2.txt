

This paper addresses the problem of learning Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem that incorporates node and edge features. The authors propose two novel two-level optimization methods: a gradient-free approach using an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based approach using an inexact first-order oracle. The theoretical contributions are significant as the authors provide convergence rate guarantees for both methods, which was previously missing in the state-of-the-art approach [21]. The paper builds upon prior work in supervised ranking [12, 21] and Markov chain methods [18, 23], but makes a clear contribution by addressing the convergence limitations of existing approaches.

The paper's strengths are substantial. The theoretical analysis is comprehensive, with rigorous convergence proofs for both optimization methods (Theorems 1, 3, and 4), and the authors provide complexity bounds that are valuable for practical implementation. The paper also makes a meaningful contribution to the literature by establishing a framework for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles, which extends the work of Nesterov and Spokoiny [16] to constrained settings. The experimental results, while relegated to the supplement, demonstrate that both proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality.

However, the paper has several significant weaknesses that require attention. First, the experimental results are entirely relegated to the supplement, making the main text incomplete and diminishing the paper's impact. For a conference like NIPS, where empirical validation is crucial, this is a serious omission. The authors should include key results in the main paper, such as the loss function values from Table 1 and the convergence behavior shown in Figure 1.

Second, there is a critical inconsistency regarding convexity. Theorem 1 assumes convexity for the gradient-free method, but the objective function (2.4) is explicitly non-convex, as acknowledged in the paper. The authors need to clarify how they ensure the objective is convex on the feasible set Φ. Without this justification, the theoretical guarantees for Theorem 1 are questionable. The authors should either provide conditions for local convexity around the optimal solution or revise the theorem to apply to non-convex settings.

Third, Theorem 3 provides a bound for ||MK(xK - xK+1)||², but it's unclear whether this bound guarantees convergence to a stationary point for the non-convex problem. The authors should explicitly address this connection, as it's essential for understanding the practical implications of their theoretical results.

Fourth, the supplementary Figure 1 shows that the gradient-free method (GFN) converges relatively slowly compared to the gradient-based method (GBN). The authors should discuss this efficiency trade-off in the main text, including computational complexity comparisons between their methods and the state-of-the-art [21]. This information would significantly enhance the paper's significance by helping practitioners choose between methods based on their specific computational constraints.

The paper is well-written and clearly structured, with strong theoretical foundations. The authors have made a meaningful contribution to the field by providing convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning that were previously unavailable. However, the omission of key experimental results from the main text substantially weakens the paper's impact and credibility. For a NIPS submission, empirical validation is as important as theoretical contributions, and the lack of visible results in the main body is a significant drawback.

Regarding the four evaluation criteria:
- Quality: Strong theoretical contributions, but weakened by the missing experimental results and convexity inconsistency
- Clarity: Excellent structure and writing, but the experimental results' absence creates confusion
- Originality: The combination of gradient-free/gradient methods with inexact oracles for Supervised PageRank is novel
- Significance: The practical implications for web ranking systems are promising, but the significance is diminished by the lack of visible experimental validation

In summary, the paper presents valuable theoretical advances that could improve web ranking systems. However, the reviewers recommend rejecting the paper as submitted due to the critical omission of key experimental results from the main text, the unresolved convexity assumption in Theorem 1, and the unclear connection between the convergence bound in Theorem 3 and the identification of stationary points. The authors should address these issues, particularly by including essential experimental results in the main text, before the paper could be considered for acceptance.
