- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning parameters of a graph-based random-walk ranking model.
- Model: random walk transition weights depend on node/edge features via parameterized random-walks (supervised PageRank style).
- Problem cannot use methods that require exact objective evaluations.
- Proposes a two-level algorithm: outer optimization with inexact oracle, inner solver to approximate Markov chain stationary distribution.
- Inner method: linearly convergent algorithm to approximate stationary distribution and thereby approximate the loss to arbitrary precision.
- Outer method: gradient-based constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle, with proof of convergence to a stationary point.
- Main contribution: adapting optimization to constrained problems where function values are only available up to known precision and integrating this into the ranking-learning task.
- Paper is well-written with apparently correct proofs (not all verified in detail).
- Concern: optimization techniques are largely adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and supervised PageRank was previously proposed.
- Minor suggestions: add a conclusion and present the optimization problem in a more general context, framing the algorithm as a specific instance.
