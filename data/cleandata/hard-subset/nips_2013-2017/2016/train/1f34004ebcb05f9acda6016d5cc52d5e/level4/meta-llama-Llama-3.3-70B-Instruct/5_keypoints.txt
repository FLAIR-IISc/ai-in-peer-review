- Proposes combining PageRank with node/edge feature weights and points out lack of guarantees in prior parameter learning.
- Introduces a method that approximates the stationary distribution of a Markov walk plus a modified gradient-free optimizer for noisy loss estimates.
- Framework is essentially reintroduced under a new name/notation, similar to prior work [21].
- Paper states many technical assumptions, defines a loss, and provides lemmas bounding the approximate gradient.
- Outlines a general gradient-free method using a biased gradient-free oracle and states a performance theorem.
- Adds further assumptions to derive a concrete learning algorithm and gives additional theoretical bounds.
- Experiments are brief, use a single web graph from related work, and detailed results are deferred to the appendix.
- Key experimental parameters are not reported, and claimed performance gains are not quantified or discussed.
- Testing appears limited to one graph; unclear whether results generalize or multiple datasets were used.
- Main text lacks proof outlines and full empirical results; most content is relegated to the appendix.
- Choice of phi0 and radius R in experiments is unexplained, raising reproducibility concerns.
- Writing quality has grammatical errors and citations are sloppy (e.g., misnaming the referenced problem).
- Some technical assumptions may not hold empirically, calling into question practical impact.
- Restricting the search space to an R-ball around phi0 could severely limit algorithm efficacy and applicability.
- Absence of tables or specific results in the main body makes it hard to assess practical significance.
- Although theoretical bounds and the algorithm may be strong, the presentation and lack of comprehensive results fail to convince.
