- Paper proposes incorporating node/edge weights into PageRank via a feature-based model and combines stationary-distribution approximation with a modified gradient-free optimizer.
- Authors recreate a previously presented framework ([21]) under new name/notation rather than presenting a clearly novel formulation.
- Technical development includes assumptions, a loss function, lemmas bounding the approximate gradient, and a general gradient-free method using a biased oracle plus a performance theorem.
- Additional assumptions are introduced to build a concrete algorithm for the learning problem and derive further bounds.
- Experimental evaluation is terse: results on a web graph are briefly mentioned and detailed analysis is deferred to the appendix.
- Key experimental parameters (e.g., phi0 and R) and whether multiple graphs were tested are not reported or explained.
- Claim that the new algorithm outperforms others lacks quantitative detail and discussion of practical significance in the main text.
- Major parts of the proofs and empirical results are relegated to the appendix, with no proof outlines or comprehensive results in the main body.
- Writing quality suffers from grammatical errors and inconsistent citation/naming (e.g., calling the problem Supervised PageRank despite [21] referring to semi-supervised).
- Several technical assumptions may not hold in real data, undermining practical applicability.
- Restricting the search space to an R-ball around phi0 is questionable and may limit the algorithm’s effectiveness.
- Absence of tables and specific main-body results leaves the practical impact of the theoretical improvements unclear.
- Overall, while the theoretical bounds and methods may be strong, the presentation and empirical validation fail to convincingly demonstrate value.
