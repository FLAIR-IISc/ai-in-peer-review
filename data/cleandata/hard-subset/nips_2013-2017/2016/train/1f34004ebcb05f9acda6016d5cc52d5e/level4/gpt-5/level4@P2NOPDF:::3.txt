This paper proposes two bilevel optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based approach (GBN) and a gradient-free approach (GFN). The gradient-based method comes with a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee to any prescribed accuracy. The gradient-free method guarantees a decrease in the loss at each iteration. Notably, both methods operate without exact evaluations of the objective function yet still provide convergence-rate estimates. They also avoid forming large matrices, while delivering strong empirical performance.

The authors specify the hyperparameters used in both methods. Although the datasets are not publicly available, the descriptions are sufficiently detailed to make reproduction plausible. Sections 2–5 clearly explain the algorithms, and the proofs are provided in the supplementary material. The lower- and upper-level procedures build on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s work (ref:17).

Clarity could be improved with a symbol table. The manuscript uses many symbols, and it is easy to lose track of definitions. For example, N appears in Line 139 and in Equation 3.1, but its meaning (number of steps) is not clarified until Line 193.

Regarding experiments, the paper primarily compares to GBP on the loss function rather than on ranking-quality metrics. Since this work generalizes ref:17, a direct empirical comparison to that baseline would strengthen the evaluation. If the claim is improved ranking quality over the state of the art, please include standard ranking metrics and report results accordingly.
