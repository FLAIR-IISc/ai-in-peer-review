This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve the optimization problem: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle. However, my major criticism is that the paper lacks experiment results in the main text, rendering it incomplete.

In the detailed comments below, I elaborate on this and other concerns. 

1. The decision to relegate experiment results to the supplement is problematic, as it makes the main text incomplete. Including key results in the main text would significantly enhance the paper's clarity and impact.

2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but the function in (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should provide guidance on choosing the set $\Phi$ to ensure the convexity of the objective function.

3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. However, it is unclear whether this bound guarantees the convergence of the sequence $xk$ to a stationary point.

4. Figure 1 in the supplement suggests that GFNM converges slowly. To strengthen the paper, the authors should also report the efficiency of the algorithms, providing a more comprehensive evaluation of their methods.
