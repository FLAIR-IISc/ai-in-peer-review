- Problem & approach: Learns Supervised PageRank via non-convex two-level optimization, proposing gradient-based and gradient-free methods with convergence guarantees.  
- Key technical fix: Addresses inexact oracle issues of prior work [21] by providing precise error bounds.  
- Main theoretical contributions: First convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization in this setting.  
- Method development: Designs a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracle and adapts random gradient-free methods to constrained problems with inexact evaluations.  
- Complexity analysis: Analyzes trade-offs between lower-level Markov chain approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Empirical results: Experiments show both methods outperform state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality across datasets.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with rigorous proofs of convergence rates and complexity bounds; experiments validate theoretical claims.  
- Missing analyses: Lacks detailed computational complexity breakdown and runtime comparisons.  
- Clarity: Well-structured, consistent notation, and thorough experimental setup; some terms (e.g., “inexact oracle”) need brief intuitive explanation.  
- Originality: Novel by providing convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank and combining inexact-oracle techniques with gradient and gradient-free methods.  
- Significance: Practical implications for improving web ranking and potential applicability to other graph-based learning problems; needs more on integration into real systems and UX impact.  
- Strengths summary: Theoretical novelty, trade-off analysis, superior empirical performance, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses summary: Limited runtime/complexity analysis, sparse discussion of implementation challenges, needs better contextualization within broader optimization literature, and unclear parameter selection details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: First convergence guarantees, demonstrated practical improvements, significant contribution at intersection of graph learning and optimization, well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Missing runtime comparisons, limited practical implementation discussion, some theoretical contextualization lacking.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add detailed computational complexity analysis and runtime comparisons.
