This paper proposes two two-level optimization methods, one gradient-based (GBN) and one gradient-free (GFN), for solving the supervised PageRank problem. A key contribution is that, unlike many existing methods, the proposed approaches come with theoretical guarantees. Specifically, the GBN method has a guaranteed convergence rate to a specified accuracy, while the GFN method ensures a decrease in the loss function value. Both methods achieve state-of-the-art ranking quality.

A significant strength of this work is that the methods do not require the exact value of the objective function, which allows them to avoid computing a large matrix while still providing an estimated convergence rate. The optimization framework is based on the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski [17]. The authors provide the hyper-parameters used, and while the datasets are not publicly available, their descriptions are clear, which should aid in reproducing the results. The technical details of the methods are thoroughly explained from Section 2 to Section 5, with proofs included in the supplementary material.

Despite these strengths, the paper has weaknesses in its presentation and experimental evaluation. The exposition suffers from a large number of symbols, making the equations difficult to follow. For example, the symbol $N$ is introduced in Line 139 and Equation 3.1, but its definition (number of steps) is not provided until Line 193. The inclusion of a symbol table would significantly improve readability.

Furthermore, the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive. The authors only compare their methods against GBP with respect to the loss function, but a comparison based on ranking quality is also necessary for a complete assessment. Moreover, since this work is a generalization of the method in reference [17], a direct empirical comparison against [17] would be valuable to demonstrate the practical advantages of the proposed generalization.
