This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model. The model is defined by a random walk with node and edge weights that are, in turn, determined by random walks on node and edge features.

The resulting optimization problem cannot be solved by existing methods that require exact values of the objective function. The proposed approach therefore operates on two levels. At the first level, a linearly convergent method is used to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk. The authors show that this allows the loss function to be approximated to any given precision. At the second level, they develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems using an inexact oracle and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The main contribution here is the adaptation of this optimization approach to the constrained case where the function can only be calculated with a known precision. The authors prove the convergence of this method and apply it at the second level of their proposed algorithm.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear to be correct, although I have not verified them in full detail. My primary concern is that the main optimization contributions are adaptations of those proposed in "Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions" by Nesterov and Spokoiny (Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015), and the supervised PageRank algorithm has also been proposed previously.

On a minor note, the paper lacks a conclusion, which is expected and for which there appears to be sufficient space. I would also suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general context, showing the studied algorithm as a specific instance. This would help to better position the paper's contribution with respect to the state-of-the-art.
