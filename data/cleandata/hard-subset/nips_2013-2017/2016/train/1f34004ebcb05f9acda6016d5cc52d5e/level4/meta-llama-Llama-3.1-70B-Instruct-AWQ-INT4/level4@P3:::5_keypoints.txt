- Proposes a novel approach to learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporates node and edge features.
- Introduces two optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) for the non-convex loss-minimization problem.
- Novel use of a biased gradient-free oracle with a proven performance theorem.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for both optimization methods.
- Thorough analysis of computational complexity, technical assumptions, and loss formulation.
- Experiments on a real-world ranking task show the proposed methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline.
- Experimental reporting is limited — lacks tables, specific values, and detailed analysis of improvements.
- No explanation of how key parameters (phi0 and R) were chosen or whether they were tuned.
- Missing discussion of practical consequences or real-world impact of the ranking improvements (e.g., engagement or revenue).
- Assumptions—particularly restricting phi to an R-ball around phi0—may limit efficacy and applicability in practice.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivates limitations of existing methods.
- Recommendations: add parameter-selection details, report detailed numerical results, discuss practical implications, and clarify limitations of assumptions.
