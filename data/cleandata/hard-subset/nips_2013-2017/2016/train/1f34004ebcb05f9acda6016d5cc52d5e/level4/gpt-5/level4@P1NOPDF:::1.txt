This paper analyzes the convergence of a nonconvex loss minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model. The model defines a Markov random walk whose transition probabilities depend on node and edge weights, which are themselves functions of node and edge features. Because existing optimization methods typically require exact evaluations of the objective, they are not directly applicable here.

The proposed approach operates on two levels. At the first level, the authors use a linearly convergent procedure to approximate the stationary distribution of the underlying Markov chain. Among other results, they show that the loss value can be approximated to any prescribed accuracy. At the second level, they develop a gradient method for general constrained nonconvex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove its convergence to a stationary point when function values are available only up to a known precision. The contribution lies in adapting this inexact-oracle framework to constrained problems, establishing convergence guarantees, and employing it within the outer level of their algorithm.

The paper is clearly written, and the proofs appear correct, although I did not check all details.

My main concern is the degree of novelty. The central optimization techniques seem to be adaptations of methods proposed by Nesterov and Spokoiny (Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions, Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015). Moreover, supervised PageRank-style algorithms have been introduced previously, which limits the originality of the application component.

Minor comments:
- A dedicated conclusion section would be valuable; there seems to be space to include one.
- I suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general framework and then showing the studied algorithm as a specific instance. This would more clearly position the contribution relative to the state of the art.
