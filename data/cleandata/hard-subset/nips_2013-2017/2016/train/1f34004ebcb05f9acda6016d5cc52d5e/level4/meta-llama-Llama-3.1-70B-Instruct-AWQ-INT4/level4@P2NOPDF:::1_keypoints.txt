- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem to learn parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by Markov random walks on node/edge features.  
- Problem cannot be handled by existing optimizers that require exact objective values.  
- Proposes a two-level approach: Level 1 uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov walk and shows the loss can be approximated to any precision.  
- Level 2 develops a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main contribution: adapting the inexact-oracle approach to constrained problems where function values are known up to given precision and proving convergence.  
- Paper is well-written and the proofs appear correct (not exhaustively checked).  
- Originality concern: key optimization methods seem to be adaptations of Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015).  
- Methodological overlap: supervised PageRank has been proposed previously.  
- Minor issues: missing conclusion section and would benefit from presenting the optimization problem in a broader context to better position the contribution.
