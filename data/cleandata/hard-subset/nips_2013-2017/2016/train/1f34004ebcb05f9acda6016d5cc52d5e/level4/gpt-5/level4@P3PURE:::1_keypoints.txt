- Studies convergence for non-convex loss minimization in a graph-based ranking model where transition weights depend on node/edge features via a random walk.
- Notes the objective cannot be evaluated exactly, making standard optimization methods that need exact function values inapplicable.
- Proposes a two-level approach: an inner linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain’s stationary distribution.
- Shows the stationary-distribution approximation allows computing the loss to any prescribed accuracy.
- Develops an outer gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.
- Main technical contribution: adapting the inexact-oracle approach to constrained problems with provable convergence when function values are available only up to known precision.
- Paper is clearly written and proofs appear correct (not fully checked in detail).
- Main concern: optimization techniques seem to largely adapt Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) and supervised PageRank is previously introduced—novelty relative to prior work is unclear.
- Minor suggestions: add a concluding section and present the optimization problem in a more general framework, positioning the studied algorithm as a specific instance.
