This paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained, non-convex optimization problem that incorporates properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve this problem: (i) a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle, and (ii) a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

Major concern: The main paper contains no experimental results; all empirical evaluation is deferred to the supplement. This makes the submission feel incomplete and prevents a fair assessment of the practical impact of the proposed methods.

Detailed comments:
1) Empirical results should be presented in the main text. Relying solely on the supplement leaves the main paper incomplete and makes it difficult to evaluate effectiveness and practicality.
2) Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the objective in equation (2.4) is non-convex. How is the set Φ chosen to guarantee convexity of the objective? Please clarify the conditions on Φ (and any other components) under which the theorem applies.
3) Theorem 3 provides a bound for M_K(x^K − x^{K+1}). Does this bound ensure that the sequence {x^k} converges to a stationary point? If so, please make the connection precise; if not, clarify what additional assumptions or arguments are needed.
4) According to Figure 1 in the supplement, GFNM converges slowly. Please report the efficiency of both algorithms, including measures such as iteration counts, oracle calls, and/or wall-clock time, to enable a clear comparison of their practical performance.

Overall assessment: The problem formulation and the use of inexact oracles are interesting, but the absence of results in the main text and the unresolved theoretical points (convexity assumptions for Theorem 1 and stationarity implications of Theorem 3) limit the clarity and evaluability of the submission. Including key experimental findings and addressing the theoretical questions above would substantially strengthen the paper.
