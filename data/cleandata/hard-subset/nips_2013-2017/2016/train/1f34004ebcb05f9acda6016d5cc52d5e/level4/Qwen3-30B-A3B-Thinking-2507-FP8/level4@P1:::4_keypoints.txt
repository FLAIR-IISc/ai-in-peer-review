- Problem: Learns Supervised PageRank parameters via non-convex loss minimization, filling a gap by providing convergence guarantees missing in prior work.  
- Methods: Proposes two-level optimization—gradient-based and random gradient-free—using an inexact oracle framework and a linearly convergent lower-level method from [17].  
- Theory: Proves convergence rates for both methods and provides complexity bounds linking lower-level approximation accuracy to overall computational cost (Theorems 2 and 4).  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates superior performance on real-world Yandex ranking tasks, outperforming [21] in loss and ranking quality (GFN especially strong, p < 0.005).  
- Strength (theory): Rigorous theoretical contribution resolving the convergence gap in previous Supervised PageRank learning methods.  
- Strength (practical): Complexity analysis offers actionable guidance; experimental design is robust and relevant to real applications.  
- Weakness: Lacks explicit runtime comparisons against [21] and other baselines—readers need concrete wall-clock timings to assess scalability.  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion of how the feasible-set parameter R affects practical convergence (only briefly in supplement).  
- Originality/significance: Novelly integrates random gradient-free optimization with inexact oracle techniques for constrained non-convex problems, advancing state of the art.  
- Clarity/quality: Well-structured, precise, and technically sound with thorough supplementary proofs; only minor phrasing issues.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with a suggestion to add detailed runtime analysis in the final version.
