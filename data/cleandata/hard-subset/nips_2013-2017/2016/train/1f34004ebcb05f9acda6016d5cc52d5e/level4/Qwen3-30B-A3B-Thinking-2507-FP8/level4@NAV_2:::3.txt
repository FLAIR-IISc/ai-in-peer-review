

This paper addresses the supervised PageRank learning problem by proposing two novel two-level optimization methods: a gradient-based algorithm (GBN) and a gradient-free algorithm (GFN). The authors build upon the framework of inexact oracles, extending Nesterov and Nemirovski's work [17] to provide theoretically grounded convergence guarantees for both methods. Unlike the state-of-the-art gradient-based approach [21], which lacks convergence proofs due to imprecise lower-level computations, the proposed methods establish convergence rates to stationary points while maintaining practical efficiency. The work demonstrates significant improvements in ranking quality over existing methods on real-world web ranking tasks.

The paper excels in quality, providing rigorous theoretical analysis with convergence rate guarantees for both optimization methods. The authors carefully establish the theoretical foundations, including Lemma 1 and Lemma 2 that bound the approximation errors in the inexact oracle calculations, and Theorems 2 and 4 that quantify the computational complexity. The experimental results clearly demonstrate that both proposed algorithms outperform the state-of-the-art [21] in ranking quality, with GFN achieving particularly strong results. The paper also provides a thorough investigation of the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity.

The clarity of the paper is generally good, though the abundance of mathematical symbols without a comprehensive symbol table creates some difficulty in following the technical details. For instance, the parameter N (number of iterations in the lower-level method) is introduced in equation 3.1 without definition until later in the text (Line 193), which could confuse readers. A dedicated symbol table would significantly improve readability.

The originality of the work is strong, as it represents the first application of inexact oracle methods to supervised PageRank learning with convergence guarantees. The authors properly contextualize their work against previous contributions, including [21], [5], and [17], and clearly articulate how their approach differs from prior work. The problem formulation and theoretical framework extend beyond the specific supervised PageRank application to a general class of constrained non-convex optimization problems with inexact oracles.

The significance of this work is substantial, as it provides a theoretically sound and practically effective solution to an important problem in web ranking. The algorithms' ability to avoid computing large matrices while maintaining high performance could have significant implications for search engine applications. The results demonstrate clear improvements in ranking quality, and the theoretical guarantees make the approach more reliable for practical deployment.

Strengths:
- Theoretical convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods
- Rigorous complexity analysis
- Superior empirical performance over state-of-the-art
- Careful treatment of the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall complexity
- Clear experimental setup with real-world data

Weaknesses:
- Lack of comprehensive symbol table makes following the mathematical derivations challenging
- Experimental evaluation focuses primarily on loss function values rather than direct ranking quality metrics
- Insufficient comparison with the foundational work [17] to demonstrate how the proposed methods improve upon it

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field, particularly for applications requiring reliable optimization of non-convex ranking models. The authors should address the symbol clarity issue and provide more direct comparisons with the foundational work [17] to better demonstrate the improvements achieved.
