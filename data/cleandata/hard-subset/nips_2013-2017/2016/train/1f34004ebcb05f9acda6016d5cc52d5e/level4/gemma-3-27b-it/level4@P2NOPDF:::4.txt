This paper introduces gradient-based and gradient-free (random) methods for minimizing the loss function in supervised PageRank model learning. The work addresses a non-convex optimization problem central to this learning task. 

The primary strength of this submission lies in its exploration of both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization techniques within the specific context of supervised PageRank learning. This is a valuable contribution, as gradient-free methods can be particularly useful when dealing with non-convex landscapes or when gradient computation is expensive or unavailable. The theoretical analysis appears sound, and the proposed algorithms offer potential benefits for practical implementations.

However, a significant omission is a detailed reporting of the computational time required by the proposed optimization methods. While the paper demonstrates convergence and potentially improved solutions, understanding the practical efficiency of these methods – specifically *how long* they take to converge – is crucial for assessing their real-world applicability. Comparing the runtime performance of the gradient-based and gradient-free approaches would further strengthen the analysis. It is difficult to gauge the usefulness of the proposed methods without this information. Were experiments conducted with different dataset sizes? How did runtime scale with problem complexity?

Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison to existing state-of-the-art methods for supervised PageRank learning. While related work is mentioned, a more direct empirical comparison—showing the proposed methods’ performance (in terms of both solution quality *and* runtime) against established algorithms—would significantly enhance the paper’s impact. Specifically, it would be helpful to see results comparing against methods explicitly designed for non-convex PageRank optimization.

The presentation is generally clear, but some sections could be more concise. For example, the motivation behind the choice of certain parameters could be elaborated upon more clearly. Providing more intuition regarding the algorithm's behavior and the impact of different parameter settings would be beneficial. 

**Specific suggestions:**

*   **Runtime Analysis:** Add a section, or incorporate into the existing experimental results, a detailed analysis of the runtime performance of both proposed methods, ideally including scaling with problem size.
*   **Benchmarking:** Include a comprehensive comparison to existing supervised PageRank algorithms, evaluating both solution quality (e.g., loss values) and computational time.
*   **Parameter Discussion:** Expand the discussion of parameter selection and its impact on the algorithm’s performance.
*   **Clarity:** Review the paper for opportunities to improve conciseness and clarity, particularly in sections detailing the mathematical derivations.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of supervised PageRank learning by exploring effective optimization strategies. However, the lack of runtime analysis and a thorough comparative evaluation limit its immediate impact. Addressing these issues would significantly strengthen the paper and increase its value to the NIPS community. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors addressing the aforementioned concerns, particularly the inclusion of runtime results and a more comprehensive comparison to existing methods.
