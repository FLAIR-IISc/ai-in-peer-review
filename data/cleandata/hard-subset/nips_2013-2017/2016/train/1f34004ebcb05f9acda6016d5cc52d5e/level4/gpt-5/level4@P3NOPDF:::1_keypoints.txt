- Paper proposes a two-level approach: inner linear-convergent solver approximates Markov-chain stationary distribution; outer inexact-oracle gradient method optimizes nonconvex constrained loss to a stationary point.
- Strength: clear separation between inner stochastic approximation and outer optimization layers.
- Strength: convergence analysis for constrained nonconvex optimization with an inexact oracle, giving guarantees to stationary points.
- Strength: theoretically sound presentation and generally clear, well-organized writing.
- Strength: inner linearly convergent solver allows arbitrarily precise objective approximation, important when exact evaluations are infeasible.
- Weakness: core optimization techniques appear to adapt known inexact/zero-order methods (e.g., Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015); novelty is not crisply delineated.
- Weakness: supervised PageRank has prior art; unclear what conceptual or algorithmic advances beyond existing formulations are offered.
- Request: precisely state smoothness/regularity assumptions (Lipschitz gradients, bounded domain, projection properties) and the inexact-oracle noise model (bias/variance, dependence on inner tolerance).
- Request: make explicit dependence of outer convergence rate and total complexity on target accuracy epsilon, spectral properties (spectral gap), and problem size; give inner/outer accuracy trade-off schedule.
- Request: quantify how inner-level approximation errors propagate to gradient and function-value errors in the outer level, and specify tolerances needed for descent and convergence.
- Request: clarify constraint treatment (projected gradients, proximal, barrier/penalty) and how the inexact oracle affects feasibility and constraint guarantees.
- Request: state precise conditions for linear convergence of the inner Markov solver (ergodicity, spectral gap) and how these relate to feature parametrization and graph structure; mention preconditioning/restart if needed.
- Positioning: compare framework and proofs to Nesterov & Spokoiny and related inexact-gradient methods, highlighting concrete novelties (constraint handling, oracle model, tighter bounds, error coupling).
- Positioning: explicitly contrast this supervised PageRank instantiation with prior supervised PageRank algorithms (model choices, optimization, theoretical guarantees), ideally via side-by-side comparison.
- Presentation suggestion: present the optimization problem and oracle model in a general, self-contained form first, then specialize to the graph-ranking case.
- Presentation suggestion: summarize main theorems with assumptions and implications up front, move technical lemmas to the appendix, and provide proof sketches that emphasize new ideas.
- Presentation suggestion: add a concise conclusion stating main theoretical contributions, limitations (e.g., spectral-gap dependence, nonconvexity), and future directions (variance reduction, adaptive schedules, stochastic extensions).
- Overall assessment: well written and practically relevant, but novelty of optimization contributions and supervised PageRank application is unclear; strengthen positioning and explicitly state what is genuinely novel.
