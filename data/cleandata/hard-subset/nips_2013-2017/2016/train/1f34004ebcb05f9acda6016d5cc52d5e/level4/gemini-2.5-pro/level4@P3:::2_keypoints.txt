- Tackles learning parameters for Supervised PageRank where objective and gradient are inexact due to dependence on a large Markov chain stationary distribution.  
- Identifies prior state-of-the-art lacks theoretical convergence guarantees because of inexact gradient use.  
- Main contribution: two two-level algorithms with formal convergence-rate guarantees — a random gradient-free method (inexact zero-order oracle) and an adaptive gradient-based method (inexact first-order oracle).  
- Both methods use an efficient iterative lower-level scheme to compute stationary distributions and derivatives with controllable accuracy.  
- Empirical results on a web-ranking task show the proposed methods achieve lower loss than the baseline.  
- Theoretical significance: first formal convergence guarantees for Supervised PageRank learning and general frameworks for constrained problems with inexact oracles.  
- Originality/technical quality: novel combination of inexact oracles, gradient-free and adaptive gradient methods with sound complexity analysis.  
- Clarity: well-structured exposition of problem, challenges, and two-level solution motivation.  
- Empirical validation is limited but indicates improved ranking quality over the baseline.  
- Weakness: gradient-free method’s convergence proof assumes local convexity, but the loss is generally non-convex and identifying such convex neighborhoods is unclear in practice.  
- Weakness: convergence criterion for the gradient-based method is given in terms of ‖M_K(x_K − x_{K+1})‖^2, with no explicit relation to standard stationarity measures.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation omits wall-clock time or total computation, leaving practical efficiency comparisons incomplete.  
- Suggested improvement: discuss practical implications of the convexity assumption and sensitivity to domain Φ or provide empirical evidence of robustness in non-convex settings.  
- Suggested improvement: clarify how the stated convergence criterion relates to approximate stationary points.  
- Suggested improvement: report runtimes or computational-cost metrics and move key convergence plots (Figure 1) into the main paper for readability.  
- Overall judgment: strong technical contribution with important theoretical novelty; merits acceptance subject to clarifying practical assumptions and improving efficiency evaluation.
