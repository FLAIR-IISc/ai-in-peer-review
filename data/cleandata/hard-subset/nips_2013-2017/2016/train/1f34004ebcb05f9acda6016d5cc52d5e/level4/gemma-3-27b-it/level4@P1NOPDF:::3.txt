In this paper, the authors propose two optimization methods for solving the supervised PageRank problem. Unlike existing approaches, the proposed gradient-based method offers a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and can achieve a specified accuracy. Furthermore, the gradient-free method provides a guaranteed decrease in the loss function with each iteration. Both methods avoid the need to compute the exact value of the objective function and provide an estimated convergence rate, while also demonstrating improved ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods. The authors clearly state the hyperparameters used in both methods. While the datasets are not publicly available, the descriptions are sufficient to facilitate reproduction of the results. 

The authors present two two-level optimization methods – gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN) – to address the supervised PageRank problem. The lower and upper level optimization procedures build upon the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17). By avoiding the need for exact objective function values and offering a provable convergence rate estimate, the proposed method avoids computationally expensive large matrix calculations while still achieving strong performance. 

Sections 2 through 5 provide a detailed explanation of the methods, with proofs included in the supplementary material. However, a symbol table would be a valuable addition, as the equations contain numerous symbols that can be difficult to track. For example, the variable *N* appears in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 without a clear definition (representing the number of steps) until Line 193. 

In the experimental section, the authors primarily compare their method to GBP in terms of loss function value, but a comparison of ranking quality would be more informative. Given that this work generalizes ref:17, a direct comparison to that baseline would also be beneficial, allowing for a clear assessment of the performance improvements.
