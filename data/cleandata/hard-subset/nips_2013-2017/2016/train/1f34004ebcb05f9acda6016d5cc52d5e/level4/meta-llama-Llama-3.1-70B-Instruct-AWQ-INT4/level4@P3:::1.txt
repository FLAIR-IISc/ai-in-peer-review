This paper proposes a solution to a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model, specifically the Supervised PageRank model. The model is defined by a random walk conducted by weights of nodes and edges, which are in turn defined by linear combinations of nodes' and edges' features. The authors develop two two-level methods to solve this problem, one based on gradient-based optimization and the other on gradient-free optimization.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear to be correct. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence of their methods and demonstrate their effectiveness through experiments on a real-world ranking task.

The main contribution of the paper is the adaptation of existing optimization methods to the case of constrained optimization problems with inexact oracles. Specifically, the authors develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems using an inexact oracle and prove its convergence to the stationary point of the problem. They also propose a gradient-free optimization method for general convex problems with inexact zero-order oracles.

The authors' approach is validated through experiments, which show that their methods outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based method on a real-world ranking task. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in solving the non-convex loss-minimization problem.

However, it is worth noting that the optimization methods proposed in the paper are adaptations of existing methods, specifically those proposed by Yurii Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny in their 2015 paper "Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions". Additionally, the Supervised PageRank algorithm has been previously proposed.

To better place the contribution of the paper with respect to the state-of-the-art, it would be helpful to present the optimization problem in a more general context, showing the studied algorithm as a specific case. This would allow the reader to understand the broader implications of the authors' work.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a thorough analysis of the convergence of the proposed methods. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the methods in solving the non-convex loss-minimization problem. However, the paper could benefit from a more general presentation of the optimization problem and a clearer discussion of the contributions with respect to the state-of-the-art.
