- Paper develops two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based and gradient-free) with theoretical convergence guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank parameters.  
- Addresses a non-convex constrained loss-minimization problem with inexact oracle information specific to PageRank.  
- Adapts a linearly convergent method to approximate PageRank stationary distributions to arbitrary accuracy, enabling inexact oracle construction.  
- Presents a gradient-based algorithm extending prior constrained non-convex frameworks and a gradient-free algorithm adapted to constrained settings with function-value approximations.  
- Provides rigorous analysis of the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity, with theoretical complexity bounds.  
- Experimental validation shows both algorithms outperform prior state-of-the-art [21] on a real-world web ranking task with statistically significant results (p < 0.005).  
- Strength: Clear theoretical contributions with rigorous convergence analysis for both methods.  
- Strength: Well-structured two-level optimization framework and consistent, clear presentation.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experiments and thorough analysis of parameter effects and trade-offs.  
- Weakness: Core optimization techniques are adaptations of existing methods (Nesterov [16], [7]) rather than wholly novel methodologies.  
- Weakness: Paper could better articulate how theoretical guarantees translate to broader practical advantages beyond presented experiments.  
- Weakness: Discussion of broader impact beyond web ranking and positioning relative to NIPS interests is limited.  
- Writing and proofs are technically sound, clear, and well-presented, though full verification of proofs was not performed by the reviewer.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — meaningful contribution to Supervised PageRank by providing first theoretical convergence guarantees and practical algorithms that outperform prior work.
