- Problem: learning parameters of Supervised PageRank formulated as constrained, non-convex loss-minimization.  
- Contribution: two optimization algorithms — a random gradient-free method (inexact zero-order oracle) and an adaptive gradient-based method (inexact first-order oracle).  
- Theoretical novelty: first convergence rate guarantees for learning Supervised PageRank models; rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Generality: develops general frameworks for constrained optimization with inexact oracles, potentially useful beyond PageRank.  
- Empirical claim: authors report that the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art baseline on a web page ranking task.  
- Weakness — presentation: key experimental results are omitted from the main text and placed in the supplement, making the paper not self-contained.  
- Weakness — theoretical assumptions: Theorem 1 assumes convexity while the loss is non-convex; unclear how to identify the proposed locally convex set Φ in practice.  
- Weakness — clarity of guarantees: Theorem 3 bounds the gradient-mapping norm but lacks an explicit explanation of how this implies convergence to a stationary point for the non-convex problem.  
- Weakness — practical efficiency: gradient-free method appears to converge slowly (supplement Fig.1); authors should report wall-clock time or operation counts to reach given accuracy.  
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution, but needs in-text experimental evidence and stronger justification/clarification of key theoretical assumptions and practical implications.
