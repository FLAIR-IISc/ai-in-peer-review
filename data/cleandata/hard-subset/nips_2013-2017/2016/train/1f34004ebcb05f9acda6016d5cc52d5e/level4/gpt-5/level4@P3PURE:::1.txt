This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model. The model is defined by a random walk on a graph whose transition weights depend on node and edge weights, which in turn are determined by features of nodes and edges (via random-walk-based constructions). Because the objective cannot be evaluated exactly, existing optimization methods that require exact function values are not directly applicable.

The authors propose a two-level approach. At the first level, they introduce a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk. They show that, using this approximation, the value of the loss function can be computed to any prescribed accuracy. At the second level, they develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove its convergence to a stationary point. The contribution lies in adapting this inexact-oracle approach to constrained problems when the function value is available only up to a known precision, proving convergence of the method, and employing it as the outer loop of the algorithm.

The paper is clearly written, and the proofs appear correct, although I did not verify every detail.

My main concern is that the optimization components seem largely to adapt techniques already proposed in Nesterov and Spokoiny (Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions, Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015), and the supervised PageRank algorithm has also been introduced previously. Clarifying the novelty relative to these works would strengthen the contribution.

Minor comments:
- A concluding section would be helpful; there is room to include one.
- Presenting the optimization problem in a more general framework and then positioning the studied algorithm as a specific instance would better situate the contribution with respect to the state of the art.
