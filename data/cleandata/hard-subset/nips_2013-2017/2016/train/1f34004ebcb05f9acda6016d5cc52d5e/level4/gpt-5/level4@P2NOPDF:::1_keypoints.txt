- Studies convergence for a non-convex graph-based ranking loss where ranking is a Markov random walk with transition weights from feature-dependent node/edge weights and the exact objective is unavailable.  
- Proposes a two-level approach: an inner linearly convergent algorithm to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution, yielding loss approximations to any prescribed accuracy.  
- Develops an outer gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.  
- Main technical contribution: adaptation of inexact-oracle techniques to constrained problems where function values are computable only up to known precision.  
- Paper is clearly written and proofs appear sound (not all details verified).  
- Major concern about novelty: many components seem adapted from known techniques (e.g., inexact/zeroth-order methods like Nesterov & Spokoiny) and prior supervised PageRank variants exist.  
- Paper should more explicitly position itself relative to prior work and clearly state what is genuinely new (e.g., specific constrained convergence rates, error-control mechanisms, problem-specific insights).  
- Suggest adding a brief concluding section summarizing theoretical/practical takeaways, limitations, and future directions.  
- Recommend presenting the optimization framework in a general form first, then the studied algorithm as a concrete instance to better situate contributions.  
- Recommend providing direct comparisons (assumptions, oracle models, rates, constraints) with closely related methods to highlight distinct advantages.
