- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank models that incorporate node and edge features via linear parameterizations.  
- Proposes two novel two-level optimization methods: a gradient-based approach and a gradient-free method for the non-convex loss.  
- Provides rigorous convergence guarantees for both methods, unlike prior work that lacked such proofs.  
- Uses an inexact-oracle framework to analyze and bound convergence rates.  
- Thoroughly analyzes computational complexity and the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall runtime.  
- Empirical evaluation on a web ranking task shows both methods outperform the prior state-of-the-art [21].  
- Gradient-based method notably reduces the number of iterations required for convergence.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and mathematically precise, enabling reproducibility.  
- Strong connection to and extension of prior NIPS work on Supervised PageRank and supervised ranking.  
- Main strengths: theoretical novelty, rigorous complexity analysis, empirical superiority, and clear presentation.  
- Main weaknesses: limited reporting of computational time in experiments.  
- Additional weaknesses: insufficient implementation details for the gradient-free method and limited comparative analysis of computational efficiency between the two proposed methods.
