This paper proposes two optimization methods to solve the supervised PageRank problem, differing from existing approaches by introducing a gradient-based method with a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and the ability to achieve a given accuracy. Additionally, a gradient-free method is proposed, which guarantees a decrease in the loss function value. Notably, both methods do not require the exact value of the objective function and provide an estimate of the convergence rate, outperforming state-of-the-art methods in terms of ranking quality. The hyper-parameters used in both methods are provided, and although the datasets are not publicly available, the description is clear, making it feasible to reproduce the results.

The authors propose two two-level optimization methods, namely gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN), to solve the supervised PageRank problem. These methods are based on the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17), utilizing lower and upper level optimization. A key advantage of the proposed methods is that they do not require the exact value of the objective function and provide a proven estimate of the convergence rate given a specified accuracy. This allows the methods to avoid computing large matrices while still achieving good results.

The authors provide a detailed explanation of their methods in Sections 2 to 5, including all proofs in the supplementary material. However, to improve clarity, it would be beneficial to include a symbol table, as the numerous symbols in the equations can be easily lost track of. For instance, the symbol $N$ is used in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 without explanation until Line 193. Furthermore, in the experiment section, the authors only compare their work with GBP in terms of the loss function, rather than ranking quality. It would be valuable to include a comparison with the method in ref:17, as this work is a generalization of it, to assess whether the performance is improved.

Overall, the proposed methods demonstrate promising results, and with some revisions to address the mentioned concerns, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
