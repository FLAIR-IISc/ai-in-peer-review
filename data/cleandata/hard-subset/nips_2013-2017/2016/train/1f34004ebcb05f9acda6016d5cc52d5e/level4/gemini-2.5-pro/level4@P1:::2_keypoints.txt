- Problem & contribution: Formulates Supervised PageRank parameter learning as a constrained non-convex optimization and proposes two algorithms (random gradient-free with inexact zero-order oracle; adaptive gradient-based with inexact first-order oracle).  
- Theoretical guarantee: Provides rigorous convergence rate guarantees for both algorithms, improving over prior work that lacked such analysis.  
- Methodological novelty: Successfully adapts recent optimization techniques to the graph-ranking setting and handles inexact-oracle issues.  
- Completeness: Paper presents problem setup, two algorithms, theoretical analysis, and experimental comparison against a baseline.  
- Empirical evaluation: Methods are tested on a web page ranking task, but key empirical results are placed in the supplement.  
- Clarity/self-containment issue: Important figures (e.g., convergence plots) are relegated to the supplement, reducing the paper’s self-containment.  
- Convexity assumption concern: Theorem 1 assumes convexity locally around a minimum despite the loss being non-convex; the “vicinity Φ” assumption is strong and needs justification or guidance on how to ensure it.  
- Lack of empirical evidence for local convexity: Authors should provide practical guidance or empirical evidence that the loss landscape is locally convex near minima.  
- Convergence interpretation gap: Theorem 3 bounds ‖MK(xK−xK+1)‖^2 as a stopping criterion, but the link to convergence to a stationary point (gradient norm) should be made explicit.  
- Efficiency evaluation missing: Experiments focus on final loss; need comparisons of practical efficiency (wall-clock time or arithmetic ops) since the gradient-free method appears slower in iterations.
