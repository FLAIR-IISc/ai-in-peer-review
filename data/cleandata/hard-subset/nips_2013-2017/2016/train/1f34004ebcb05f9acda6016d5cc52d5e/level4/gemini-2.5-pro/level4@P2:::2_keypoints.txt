- Paper studies learning parameters for Supervised PageRank by formulating a constrained non-convex loss-minimization problem.  
- Main contribution: two two-level optimization algorithms — a random gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and an adaptive gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Theoretical advance: both algorithms come with formal convergence rate guarantees and complexity analyses, unlike prior work [21].  
- Methods extend existing optimization frameworks to handle constrained problems with inexact oracle information, addressing a practical large-scale need.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical treatment and novel development of gradient methods for constrained non-convex problems with inexact oracles.  
- Strength: well-motivated, significant practical problem; methods outperform prior state-of-the-art in experiments (in supplement).  
- Major weakness: experimental results are omitted from the main paper and relegated to the supplement, leaving the main text incomplete.  
- Request: include a summary of experimental setup, key results (e.g., Table 1, Figure 1), and discussion in the main paper.  
- Theoretical concern: Theorem 1 assumes convexity of f while the loss is non-convex; relying on local convexity of a chosen feasible set Φ is a strong, potentially unjustified assumption.  
- Ask authors to justify how Φ is selected, how likely local convexity is in practice, and behavior if the convexity assumption fails.  
- Theorem 3 bounds ‖MK(xK − xK+1)‖^2, but the relation to convergence to a stationary point (e.g., ‖∇f(x)‖ → 0) is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Efficiency concern: gradient-free method appears to converge more slowly in iterations; practical efficiency (wall-clock time or operation counts) is not reported or discussed.  
- Suggestion: provide runtime comparisons or operation counts to guide practitioners in method selection.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contributions and potential impact, but presentation is hampered by missing main-text experiments and unresolved theoretical/efficiency questions; inclusion of experiments and clarifications would strengthen the paper.
