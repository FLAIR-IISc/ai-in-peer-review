- Proposes two two-level optimization methods for supervised PageRank: a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN).
- GBN comes with a theoretical convergence-rate guarantee and can reach a prescribed accuracy.
- GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function.
- Both methods avoid requiring exact objective evaluations and provide estimates of their convergence rates.
- Methodology builds on Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref. 17) and avoids forming large matrices while retaining strong performance.
- Reported ranking quality outperforms the state of the art.
- The authors provide the hyperparameters used for both methods.
- Datasets are not publicly available, but descriptions are detailed enough to make reproduction feasible.
- Sections 2–5 present methods in detail, with all proofs deferred to the supplementary material.
- Clarity issues: notation is dense and a symbol table would help; the meaning of N is unclear until later in the paper.
- Experimental concerns: comparisons to GBP focus primarily on loss rather than ranking quality, and a direct empirical comparison to ref. 17 is missing but desirable.
