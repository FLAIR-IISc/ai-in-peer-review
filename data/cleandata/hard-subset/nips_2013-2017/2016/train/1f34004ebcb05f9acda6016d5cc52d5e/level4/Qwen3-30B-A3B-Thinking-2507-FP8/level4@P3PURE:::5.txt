

The authors introduce a framework for learning node and edge weights within a feature-based PageRank model, motivated by prior work. They note that existing parameter-learning algorithms lack performance guarantees and propose a solution combining a Markov random walk stationary distribution approximation with an adaptation of a gradient-free optimization method suitable for loss functions accessible only via estimation. However, the framework essentially reproduces [21]'s approach under new terminology and notation, without significant novelty. The paper establishes technical assumptions, defines a loss function, and demonstrates the problem's hardness. Key lemmas bound the approximate gradient, and a general gradient-free method using a biased oracle is analyzed, with a performance theorem provided. Subsequent assumptions lead to an adapted algorithm for the learning problem, accompanied by additional bounds. Experimental results on a web graph from prior work are briefly outlined in the main text, with detailed analysis relegated to the appendix. Crucially, the main paper omits critical details: specific results, parameter choices (e.g., how φ₀ and R were selected in experiments), and empirical validation across diverse graphs. The claim that the novel algorithm "outperforms" competitors lacks discussion of the magnitude or practical significance of improvements. The citation for "Supervised PageRank" is inconsistent with [21], which describes semi-supervised PageRank. While the theoretical bounds are strong, the technical assumptions—particularly constraining φ to an R-ball around a fixed φ₀—appear overly restrictive and are not adequately justified for empirical applicability. The paper’s core contributions (proofs, detailed results, and experimental analysis) are confined to the appendix, leaving the main body without sufficient evidence to support the claimed impact. The lack of main-body empirical results, unaddressed assumptions, and unclear practical implications undermine the paper’s persuasiveness despite its technical rigor.
