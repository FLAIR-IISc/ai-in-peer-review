- Studies convergence for a non-convex loss-minimization problem in a feature-driven graph-based ranking model defined by random walks (node/edge weights from features).
- Conventional optimization methods fail here because they require exact objective evaluations, which are unavailable.
- Proposes a two-level approach: level one uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain stationary distribution.
- Shows that the stationary-distribution approximation yields arbitrary-precision approximation of the loss function via comparative analysis.
- Develops a gradient-based method for constrained non-convex optimization using an inexact oracle and proves convergence to a stationary point.
- Main contribution: adapts this framework to constrained settings with known-precision objective evaluations and provides rigorous convergence proofs used in the second level.
- Paper is well-structured and proofs appear sound, though the reviewer did not fully verify all proofs.
- Primary concern: core optimization techniques largely adapt ideas from Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015), and supervised PageRank was introduced previously.
- Minor issue: missing conclusion section despite available space.
- Recommendation: better frame the optimization problem in a broader theoretical context and explicitly position the method as an instance of a more general algorithm class.
