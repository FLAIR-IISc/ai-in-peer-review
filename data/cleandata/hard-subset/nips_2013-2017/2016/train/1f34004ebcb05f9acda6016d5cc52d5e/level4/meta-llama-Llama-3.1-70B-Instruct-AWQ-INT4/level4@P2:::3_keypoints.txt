- Proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank, grounded in Nesterov and Nemirovski's work.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees: GBN has a provable convergence rate; GFN guarantees a decrease in the loss function.  
- Both methods do not require exact objective function values and include estimated convergence rates.  
- Experimental results claim improved ranking quality over state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear method descriptions and proofs provided in the supplementary materials.  
- Main contribution: novel optimization methods for supervised PageRank with theoretical guarantees and practical advantages.  
- Strong applicability to domains like web search and recommendation systems.  
- Technical soundness affirmed; proofs and methodology are adequately supported.  
- Clarity is generally good but the paper uses many symbols, making it easy to lose track.  
- Suggestion: include a symbol table (e.g., clarify usage of "N" which is defined late).  
- Experimental comparison is incomplete: loss is compared to GBP but ranking quality is not compared directly with GBP.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison with ref:17 despite being a generalization of that work.  
- Meets evaluation criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance overall.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel methods, theoretical guarantees, practical benefits, and broad significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: heavy symbolic notation without a table, incomplete experimental comparisons (ranking quality vs GBP and against ref:17).
