- Introduces a PageRank-based framework that incorporates node and edge weights from a feature model.  
- Addresses limitations of a prior parameter-learning algorithm by combining stationary-distribution approximation with a gradient-free optimizer for estimated losses.  
- Framework is largely similar to [21] (different notation), raising novelty/clarity questions.  
- Presents technical assumptions, defines the loss, and explains the problem's challenges.  
- Provides lemmas bounding the approximate gradient and a general gradient-free method using a biased gradient-free oracle.  
- States a theorem demonstrating the performance of the gradient-free method.  
- Adapts the gradient-free method into a concrete learning algorithm and derives bounds for it.  
- Reports experiments on a web graph used in prior work, but detailed results and analyses are deferred to the appendix.  
- Fails to report some experimental parameters and does not clarify the magnitude or implications of claimed improvements.  
- Unclear whether experiments considered diverse graphs or multiple datasets.  
- Writing quality and citations are poor (grammatical errors; mis-citation of “Supervised PageRank” vs. “semi-supervised”).  
- Questions the practical impact despite strong theoretical bounds and doubts the empirical validity of technical assumptions.  
- Specific concern that restricting phi to an R-ball around phi0 may severely limit applicability and is insufficiently discussed.  
- Major criticism: heavy reliance on the appendix—proofs, full empirical results, tables, and key experimental choices (phi0, R) are missing from the main text.
