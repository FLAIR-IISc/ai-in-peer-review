- Paper studies convergence for a non-convex loss in learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by random walks with feature-based node and edge weights.
- Direct optimization is intractable, so the authors propose a two-level algorithmic approach.
- First level: estimate an approximation to the Markov chain's stationary distribution using a linearly convergent method and show arbitrary precision can be achieved.
- They develop a gradient method for constrained, non-convex optimization that works with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.
- Core contribution: extending convergence analysis to constrained optimization when function values are only known to limited precision, and applying this within the algorithm's second level.
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear sound, though full verification was not possible in the review.
- Major concern: optimization techniques largely build on prior work (notably Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015) and related supervised PageRank literature.
- Suggested minor improvements: include a concluding section summarizing findings and future directions.
- Suggested framing improvement: position the algorithm as an instance of a more general framework to clarify novelty and relation to state-of-the-art.
