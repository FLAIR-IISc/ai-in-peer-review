This paper investigates the convergence properties of a non-convex loss minimization problem arising in the context of learning parameters for a general graph-based ranking model. The model is defined via a random walk process governed by node and edge weights, themselves determined by random walks based on node and edge features. Recognizing the intractability of directly solving this optimization problem with standard methods requiring exact objective function values, the authors propose a two-level approach. The first level employs a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, demonstrating the ability to achieve any desired level of precision in approximating the loss function.  Furthermore, they introduce a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization utilizing an inexact oracle, and establish its convergence to a stationary point. The key contribution lies in adapting this methodology to constrained optimization scenarios where function values are known only to a certain precision, proving convergence and applying it within the second level of their algorithm. The paper is clearly written and the proofs appear sound, although a complete verification was not undertaken.  However, a significant portion of the optimization techniques presented build upon prior work, notably “Yurii. Nesterov and Vladimir Spokoiny, Random Gradient-Free Minimization of Convex Functions, Foundations of Computational Mathematics, 2015, pp. 1–40”, and the concept of supervised PageRank has also been previously explored.  A concluding section summarizing the findings would be beneficial. Finally, framing the optimization problem within a broader, more general context – positioning the proposed algorithm as a specific instance of a more general framework – would help to more clearly delineate its contribution relative to existing state-of-the-art methods.
