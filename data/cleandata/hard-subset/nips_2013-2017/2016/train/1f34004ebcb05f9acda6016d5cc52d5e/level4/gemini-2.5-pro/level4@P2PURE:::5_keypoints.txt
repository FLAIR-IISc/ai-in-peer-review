- Proposes a feature-based PageRank framework learning both node and edge weights and notes prior learning algorithm lacked performance guarantees.  
- Introduces a new method combining stationary-distribution approximation for Markov walks with an adapted gradient-free optimization method.  
- Provides theoretical results: lemmas bounding the approximate gradient, a general biased-oracle gradient-free optimization theorem, and performance bounds for a specialized learning algorithm.  
- Main strength is rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Manuscript clarity is poor with numerous grammatical errors.  
- Core contributions (proof outlines and detailed empirical results) are placed in the appendix, leaving the main text non–self-contained.  
- Related-work discussion is imprecise (e.g., mislabeling prior work as “Supervised PageRank” when it seems semi-supervised).  
- Key technical assumptions (notably constraining φ to an R-ball around φ₀) are strong and their empirical validity and impact are not justified.  
- Practical relevance of the theoretical bounds is unclear due to the unaddressed strength of assumptions.  
- Experimental reporting is inadequate: no tables or specific quantitative results in the main paper.  
- Claims of outperforming baselines lack discussion of magnitude or practical significance.  
- Experimental setup is underspecified (e.g., selection of φ₀ and R not reported) and evaluation is limited to a single graph, raising generalizability concerns.  
- Overall, the paper fails to convincingly demonstrate its empirical contributions and impact despite a potentially strong theoretical contribution.
