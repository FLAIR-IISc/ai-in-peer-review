- Analyzes convergence for a non-convex loss in learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model.
- Model: random walk weighted by node/edge attributes, which themselves come from random walks over node/edge features.
- Direct optimization is intractable, so authors propose a two-level algorithmic approach.
- Level 1: use a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov chain’s stationary distribution.
- Show the stationary distribution approximation yields loss approximations to arbitrary precision.
- Level 2: develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.
- Main contribution: adapting convergence analysis to constrained problems where function values are only known up to a specified precision.
- Paper is well-written and proofs seem correct, though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Techniques largely adapt methods from Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), and supervised PageRank is not novel.
- Recommendation: add a concluding section summarizing results and future directions.
- Suggest framing the optimization problem more generally and showing the algorithm as an instance of a broader class to clarify novelty relative to prior work.
