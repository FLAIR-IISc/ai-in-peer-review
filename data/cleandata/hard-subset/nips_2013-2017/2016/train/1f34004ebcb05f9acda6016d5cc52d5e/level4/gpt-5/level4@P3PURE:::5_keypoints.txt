- Paper proposes a feature-based learning framework for PageRank-like models and combines stationary-distribution approximation with a gradient-free optimization adapted to estimated losses.
- The framework largely reproduces prior work ([21]) under new name/notation, raising concerns about novelty and citation accuracy.
- Theoretical contribution: nontrivial bounds for a biased, gradient-free procedure applied to an estimated loss are presented.
- Motivation is sensible for settings where the loss is only indirectly observable.
- Major concern: novelty and positioning are unclear because [21] appears to address a closely related problem (semi-supervised PageRank).
- Major concern: key technical assumptions (notably restricting parameter search to an R-ball around phi0) may be unrealistic and could limit practical applicability.
- Major concern: empirical evidence is insufficient—unclear how many or what types of graphs were tested, key parameters and quantitative results are missing from the main text.
- Major concern: substantive content (proof outlines and experimental details) is deferred to the appendix, hindering assessment of contributions.
- Major concern: writing contains grammatical errors and sloppy citations; mismatch in terminology (“Supervised” vs “semi-supervised” PageRank) is confusing.
- Request for clarification: how were phi0 and R chosen, and what sensitivity analysis supports those choices?
- Request for clarification: were experiments run on more than one graph, and what was the diversity of graph types and sizes?
- Request for clarification: what are the concrete, quantitative practical consequences of the theoretical improvements for real-world use?
- Overall assessment: ideas and bounds could be powerful, but unclear novelty, restrictive assumptions, weak presentation, and insufficient empirical support prevent a convincing case for impact.
