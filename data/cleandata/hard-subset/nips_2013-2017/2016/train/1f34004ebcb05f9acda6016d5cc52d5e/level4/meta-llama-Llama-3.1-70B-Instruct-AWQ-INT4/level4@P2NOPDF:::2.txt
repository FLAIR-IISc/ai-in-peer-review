This paper formulates the Supervised PageRank model as a constrained non-convex optimization problem by incorporating properties of nodes and edges. The authors propose two approaches to solve this optimization problem: a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

My primary concern with the paper is that the main text lacks experimental results, making it feel incomplete. Below, I provide detailed comments on the paper.

1. The decision to relegate experimental results to the supplement is problematic, as it renders the main text incomplete. It would be beneficial to include at least a summary of the key findings in the main text to provide context and support the proposed methods.

2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the function in equation (2.4) is non-convex. The authors should clarify how to select the set $\Phi$ to ensure the convexity of the objective function. This is crucial for the theoretical guarantees provided in the paper.

3. Theorem 3 establishes a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. However, it is unclear whether this bound is sufficient to guarantee the convergence of the sequence $xk$ to a stationary point. The authors should provide additional insights or analysis to address this concern.

4. Figure 1 in the supplement suggests that the Gradient-Free Newton Method (GFNM) converges slowly. To provide a more comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithms, the authors should also report their efficiency, including computational time and resources required. This would facilitate a more informed comparison with existing methods.

Overall, while the paper proposes an interesting approach to Supervised PageRank, it would benefit from a more comprehensive presentation of experimental results and a clearer discussion of the theoretical guarantees and algorithmic efficiency.
