In this paper, the authors propose two optimization methods for solving the supervised PageRank problem. Unlike existing approaches, the proposed gradient-based method offers a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and can achieve a specified accuracy. Furthermore, the gradient-free method provides a guaranteed decrease in the loss function value. Both methods avoid the need for exact objective function values and offer an estimated convergence rate, while also demonstrating improved ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art methods. The authors clearly report the hyperparameters used in both methods, and while the datasets are not publicly available, their descriptions are sufficiently detailed to facilitate reproduction of the results. 

The authors present two two-level optimization methods – gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN) – to address the supervised PageRank problem. The lower- and upper-level optimization procedures are based on the work of Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17). By not requiring exact objective function values, the proposed methods avoid computationally expensive large matrix calculations while still achieving strong performance. The paper thoroughly details the methods from Section 2 to Section 5, including all proofs in the supplementary material. 

However, the numerous symbols used throughout the equations make the paper difficult to follow; a symbol table would be extremely beneficial. For example, the variable *N* is used in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 without its meaning (number of steps) being defined until Line 193. Additionally, in the experimental section, the authors only compare their methods to GBP in terms of loss function values, neglecting a comparison of ranking quality. Finally, given that this work generalizes ref:17, a direct comparison to that baseline would be valuable to assess the performance improvement achieved by the proposed methods.
