- Paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization and proposes two two-level methods (gradient-free with inexact zero-order oracle and gradient-based with inexact first-order oracle), extending [21] with theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: Provides rigorous convergence rate guarantees for both proposed methods, improving over prior work [21].  
- Strength: Develops a comprehensive framework for handling inexact oracle information with appropriate complexity analysis.  
- Strength: Experiments (in supplement) show both methods outperform the state-of-the-art in ranking quality on a real-world web page task.  
- Strength: Clearly explains trade-offs between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall algorithmic complexity for practical implementation.  
- Weakness: All experimental results are omitted from the main text and only appear in the supplement, hindering evaluation of claimed performance improvements.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 assumes convexity while the problem is explicitly non-convex; the paper fails to explain how Φ can be chosen to guarantee local convexity.  
- Weakness: Theorem 3 bounds ‖MK(xK - xK+1)‖2 but does not clarify whether this implies convergence to a stationary point for the non-convex problem.  
- Weakness: Supplementary Figure 1 shows relatively slow convergence of the gradient-free method (GFN), raising practical efficiency concerns.  
- Overall: The paper is a strong theoretical contribution but needs main-text experimental evidence and clearer treatment of convexity/convergence issues before acceptance.
