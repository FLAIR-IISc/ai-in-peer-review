- Paper formulates supervised PageRank learning as a constrained, generally non-convex two-level optimization where node/edge features determine restart and transition probabilities.
- Proposes a gradient-free two-level method using an inexact zero-order oracle and randomized sphere-smoothing for projected updates.
- Proposes a gradient-based two-level method using an inexact first-order oracle with an adaptive projected gradient routine for composite, possibly non-convex objectives.
- Lower-level uses a linearly convergent Nesterov–Nemirovski-style scheme (instead of power method) to approximate PageRank stationary distributions and, for the gradient-based method, their derivatives.
- Upper-level controls inner accuracy explicitly to obtain convergence rate guarantees and complexity bounds showing the inner-accuracy vs runtime trade-off.
- Experimental comparison (in supplement) on a Yandex browsing-graph dataset contrasts the proposed methods with a previous gradient-based Supervised PageRank approach.
- Builds on supervised random walks/PageRank literature and on methods for PageRank computation; adapts random gradient-free and inexact-oracle frameworks to constrained, non-convex settings.
- Theory strength: modular analysis with convergence-rate guarantees and complexity accounting for both method variants.
- Practical modeling strength: integrates node and edge features for learning-to-rank on graphs.
- Methodological strength: extends random gradient-free methods to constrained problems with biased/noisy function values and develops an adaptive projected gradient with inexact first-order oracle.
- Implementation strength: addresses inner–outer accuracy trade-offs and specifies how to set inner iteration counts for outer tolerances.
- Weakness: key experimental results (loss curves, ranking quality, runtime, iteration counts) are only in the supplement and should be in the main text.
- Theorem 1 assumes convexity though the objective (squared hinge over pairwise PageRank differences) is generally non-convex; unclear how Φ ensuring convexity is chosen and its sensitivity.
- Need conditions or diagnostics on features/α that guarantee f is convex on Φ and practical guidance for selecting Φ.
- Theorem 3 provides a bound on MK‖xK − xK+1‖ resembling a projected gradient mapping, but it's unclear whether this ensures convergence to a first-order stationary point in the constrained non-convex setting.
- Empirical efficiency concern: gradient-free method appears slow in supplement; request wall-clock time, total ops, inner iterations (N, N1, N2), and outer iterations for fair comparison and ablations.
- Presentation issues: notation-heavy text with key assumptions/constants in the supplement; request a self-contained summary of constants and practical parameter choices.
- References and minor typos need fixing (e.g., “concider,” “probablities,” incomplete conference refs).
- Quality assessment: promising and technically sound given oracle bounds, but convexity assumption and stationarity implications need clearer connection to the non-convex objective; experiments should be more prominent and include efficiency.
- Clarity recommendation: include core empirical results, concise assumptions/constants, clarify selection/role of Φ, and specify inexact oracle definitions and tolerance settings.
- Originality: meaningful adaptation of random gradient-free methods to constrained inexact-oracle settings and development of an adaptive projected gradient for composite non-convex problems; solid synthesis applying linearly convergent stationary-distribution computations to supervised PageRank.
- Significance: potentially impactful for large-scale graph ranking if guarantees hold in practice and empirical gains are robust, but stronger in-paper empirical validation is needed.
- Pros for acceptance: solid theoretical contributions, clear inner–outer trade-offs, and promising empirical improvements over prior supervised PageRank.
- Cons for acceptance: core experiments absent from main text, unclear convexity justification for Theorem 1, insufficiently explicit stationarity guarantees for gradient-based method, and presentation/ref polishing needed.
- Suggested improvements: move key experimental figures/tables to main paper, add inner-accuracy ablations, state conditions for convexity on Φ and guidance to choose it, clarify stationarity implications of Theorem 3, consolidate assumptions/constants, and fix typos/references.
