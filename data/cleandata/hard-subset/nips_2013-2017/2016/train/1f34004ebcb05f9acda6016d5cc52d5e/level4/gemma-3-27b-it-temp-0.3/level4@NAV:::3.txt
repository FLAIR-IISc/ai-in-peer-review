This paper introduces two novel optimization approaches – a gradient-based method (GBN) and a gradient-free method (GFN) – designed to address the supervised PageRank problem. A key strength of this work lies in its theoretical foundations; the gradient-based method offers a guaranteed convergence rate to a specified accuracy, while the gradient-free method ensures a quantifiable decrease in the loss function. Importantly, both methods operate without requiring precise objective function values and provide an estimated convergence rate, demonstrating improved ranking quality compared to existing state-of-the-art techniques. The authors clearly detail the hyperparameter settings used in their experiments. While the datasets are not publicly available, the provided descriptions appear sufficient for reproduction.

The proposed two-level optimization framework, leveraging concepts from Nesterov and Nemirovski (ref:17), effectively avoids the computational burden of large matrix calculations without sacrificing performance. The methodology is thoroughly explained from Section 2 through Section 5, with comprehensive proofs included in the supplementary material. 

However, the density of notation throughout the paper presents a challenge. A symbol table would significantly enhance readability and reduce the potential for confusion. For instance, the variable *N* appears in Line 139 and Equation 3.1 without explicit definition until Line 193. Furthermore, the experimental evaluation primarily focuses on loss function comparisons with GBP, neglecting a direct assessment of ranking quality. Given that this work generalizes ref:17, a comparative analysis against the original method would be valuable in demonstrating the advancements achieved.
