- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank with two methods: gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN).
- Core contribution: provides theoretical convergence rate guarantees for both GBN and GFN.
- Methods handle inexact oracle information, making them practical for costly evaluations.
- Empirical results show superior ranking quality compared to the state-of-the-art [21].
- Builds on established techniques (power method) and adapts prior optimization frameworks [6,16,17].
- Paper is well-structured with extensive mathematical derivations and proofs in the supplement.
- Experiments use a real-world web browsing dataset and a reasonably detailed setup.
- Strength: strong theoretical guarantees addressing a gap in prior work.
- Strength: practical inexact-oracle operation.
- Strength: thorough empirical validation demonstrating improved ranking.
- Strength: clear problem definition and positioning within related work.
- Weakness: heavy and sometimes confusing notation; lacks a symbol table (e.g., meaning of 'N' unclear early).
- Weakness: experimental comparisons lack direct ranking metrics (NDCG, MAP) for stronger validation.
- Weakness: missing direct baseline comparison to Nesterov & Nemirovski’s foundational approach [17].
- Weakness: dataset unavailability hinders reproducibility; needs sharing or recreation instructions.
- Weakness: some lemma/theorem sections are overly dense and could be shortened or moved to supplement.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add ranking metrics, compare to [17], and include a symbol table.
