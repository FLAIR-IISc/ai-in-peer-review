- Summary: Studies learning in Supervised PageRank with feature-weighted random walks and a non-convex parameterized restart/transition model using a two-level optimization approach.

- Lower-level: Uses a linearly convergent method (Nesterov–Nemirovski style) to approximate the stationary distribution and its derivatives with explicit error control.

- Upper-level: Proposes two inexact-oracle optimization schemes — a constrained random gradient-free method and an adaptive gradient-based method for non-convex problems.

- Theoretical guarantees: Provides convergence results (ε-accuracy for gradient-free in convex/local-convex case; convergence to stationary points for gradient-based) and complexity bounds accounting for lower-level approximation.

- Empirical results: On real Yandex browsing data, methods are competitive or improve ranking quality vs. a prior gradient-based baseline.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Supervised PageRank and supervised random-walk models; adapts Nesterov–Spokoiny gradient-free techniques to constrained, inexact settings and replaces power method with a linearly convergent stationary approximation.

- Strengths: Clear two-level formulation with explicit inexactness control, convergence guarantees absent in prior work, complexity analysis, real-data experiments, and solid proofs.

- Weaknesses: Optimization contributions are mostly adaptations of existing methods; Supervised PageRank is not new; presentation could better frame the problem generally and needs a concise conclusion.

- Quality: Technical development is solid and claims are supported by theory and experiments, though not all proofs were checked in detail.

- Clarity: Generally well organized and clear, but would benefit from a more general optimization framing and an explicit conclusion.

- Originality: Moderate — well-executed application of known optimization techniques but limited algorithmic novelty.

- Significance: Valuable for learning graph-based ranking with inexact function/gradient information and provides practically useful convergence guarantees.

- Suggestions: Recast the problem in a general constrained inexact-oracle framework, add a concise conclusion, and explicitly state assumptions (e.g., local convexity) for the gradient-free method.

- Arguments for acceptance: Convergence-guaranteed methods for inexact-oracle Supervised PageRank, careful complexity analysis, and empirical gains on real data.

- Arguments against acceptance: Limited novelty in optimization methods and modeling, and the presentation does not fully highlight generality or distinctiveness.
