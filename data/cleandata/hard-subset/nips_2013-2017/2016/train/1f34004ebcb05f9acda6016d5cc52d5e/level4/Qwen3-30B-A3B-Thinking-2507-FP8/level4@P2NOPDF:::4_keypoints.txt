- Proposes gradient-based and derivative-free optimization for non-convex supervised PageRank with theoretical convergence analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks critical quantitative metrics necessary for assessing practical utility.  
- Major omission: no runtime or computational complexity analysis (no execution times reported).  
- No comparison of execution time against baseline methods (e.g., standard gradient descent, existing PageRank solvers).  
- Need justification of the trade-off between derivative-free per-iteration cost and gradient-based methods.  
- Unclear computational scalability to large graphs or increasing model complexity; runtime efficiency not demonstrated.  
- Authors should report average time per iteration, total optimization time, runtime comparisons under identical hardware/implementation, and sensitivity to parameters.  
- Missing convergence rate comparisons: iteration counts and function evaluations versus baselines are not provided.  
- Theoretical contributions are weakened by insufficient experimental rigor on computational efficiency.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent terminology ("random-gradient-free" vs "derivative-free"/"gradient-free") and ambiguous randomization description hurt reproducibility.  
- Overall: relevant approach but limited impact without comprehensive computational benchmarks; recommend rejection unless these gaps are addressed.
