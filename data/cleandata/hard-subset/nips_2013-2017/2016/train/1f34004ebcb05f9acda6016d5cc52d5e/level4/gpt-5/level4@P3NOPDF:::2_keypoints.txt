- Paper formulates supervised PageRank with node/edge features as a constrained, generally nonconvex optimization problem and proposes GFNM (zero-order) and GBNM (first-order) learning algorithms.  
- Strength: Addresses a relevant, practical problem of learning query-dependent PageRank with rich features under constraints.  
- Strength: Properly motivates the need to handle inexact oracles because each (sub)function/gradient requires solving a PageRank-like subproblem.  
- Strength: Provides theoretical analysis for both zero- and first-order methods under oracle inexactness.  
- Strength: The two algorithms are complementary, covering scenarios where derivatives are unavailable or available inexactly.  
- Weakness: Empirical results are only in the supplement; the main paper lacks dataset, baselines, metrics, and key quantitative findings.  
- Weakness: Theorem 1 assumes convexity while the stated objective (eq. 2.4) is nonconvex, creating an unclear assumption–reality gap.  
- Weakness: Convergence claims are unclear for the nonconvex setting—uncertain whether bounds imply convergence to a stationary point.  
- Weakness: GFNM appears to converge slowly (supplement Fig.1) and there is no quantitative analysis of efficiency or scalability.  
- Weakness: Clarity and notation issues (definition/tractability of feasible set Φ, inconsistent indices, cost of projections).  
- Requested: Add an experiment section in the main text summarizing datasets (sizes/graph stats), features, baselines, metrics (e.g., NDCG@k, MAP), and key quantitative improvements/statistical significance.  
- Requested: Include convergence plots (objective vs iterations and wall-clock), final ranking quality, ablations on oracle accuracy, and computational budgets (time/iteration, PageRank solves, total training time, memory).  
- Requested: Precisely state when the objective is convex — specify loss/regularizer/Φ choices that yield convexity and practical Φ examples enabling projection.  
- Requested: If convexity is not general, explicitly state Theorem 1 applies to a restricted case and provide separate results for the general nonconvex case.  
- Requested: For Theorem 3 clarify whether ||x^{k+1}-x^k||^2 is summable, whether expected gradient norms converge to zero (with rates like O(1/√K)), and state any additional conditions (e.g., KL) for full-sequence convergence.  
- Requested: Report wall-clock comparisons and compare GFNM vs GBNM under matched computational budgets; analyze sensitivity to inner-solve accuracy and total time.  
- Requested: Detail per-iteration complexity: number of PageRank subroutine calls, complexity per call, and acceleration techniques (warm starts, early stopping).  
- Requested: Run experiments on larger graphs to demonstrate scalability and discuss sparsity/parallelization strategies.  
- Requested: Define inexact oracle models explicitly (bias/variance, smoothing radius dependence), state dimension dependence for GFNM and iteration complexity for GBNM to reach ε-stationarity, and explain hyperparameter tuning.  
- Requested: Describe feasible set Φ concretely (boxes, simplex, norm balls), cost of projection, and implications of approximate projections on theory.  
- Requested: Improve clarity: provide pseudocode with oracle definitions and stopping criteria, consistent notation, and state theorem assumptions mapped to PageRank practicality.  
- Minor suggestions: Consider a hybrid scheme (start with GFNM, switch to GBNM), and explore variance reduction, momentum, or improved smoothing for better performance.  
- Overall: Paper is relevant with solid theoretical work but incomplete due to missing main-text experiments and unresolved gaps between assumptions and the nonconvex objective; major clarifications and added empirical/efficiency analyses are needed before strong acceptance recommendation.
