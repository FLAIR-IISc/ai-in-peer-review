This paper investigates the convergence properties of a non-convex loss minimization problem arising in the context of learning parameters for a general graph-based ranking model. The model is defined via a random walk process governed by node and edge weights, themselves determined by random walks on node and edge features. Due to the intractability of directly optimizing this loss function with standard methods requiring exact objective function values, the authors propose a two-level approach. The first level employs a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, demonstrating the ability to approximate the loss function to arbitrary precision.  Building upon this, they develop a gradient method tailored for general constrained non-convex optimization problems utilizing an inexact oracle, and establish its convergence to a stationary point. The core contribution lies in extending this framework to constrained optimization scenarios where function values are known only to a specified precision, proving convergence and applying it within the second level of their algorithm. The paper is clearly written and the proofs appear sound, although a complete verification was not performed. 

A primary concern is that the key optimization techniques presented are closely related to those found in Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015), and the concept of supervised PageRank has appeared in prior work. Furthermore, the paper lacks a concluding section, which would be beneficial given the analysis presented.  Finally, framing the optimization problem in a broader context, and illustrating how the proposed algorithm represents a specific instance within that context, would more effectively highlight the novelty of the contribution relative to the existing literature.
