The paper "Learning Supervised PageRank with Gradient-Based and Gradient-Free Optimization Methods" proposes two novel optimization methods for solving the supervised PageRank problem. The authors develop a gradient-based method with a theoretically guaranteed convergence rate and a gradient-free method with a guaranteed loss function decrease value. Both methods do not require the exact value of the objective function and have a proven estimate of the convergence rate.

The main contributions of the paper are the development of two two-level optimization methods, namely gradient-based (GBN) and gradient-free (GFN), to solve the supervised PageRank problem. The lower-level and upper-level optimization is based on Nesterov and Nemirovski's work. The proposed methods do not require an exact value of the objective function and have a proven estimate of the convergence rate given an accuracy. This allows the methods to avoid computing large matrices while still achieving good results.

The authors provide a detailed explanation of their methods in Sections 2 to 5, including all the proofs in the supplementary materials. However, the paper could benefit from a symbol table, as there are numerous symbols in the equations, making it easy to lose track of their meanings.

The experimental results in Section 6 demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, outperforming the state-of-the-art gradient-based method (GBP) in terms of the loss function. However, it would be beneficial to compare the ranking quality of the proposed methods with GBP, as this is the primary objective of the supervised PageRank problem. Additionally, since this work is a generalization of Nesterov and Nemirovski's work, a direct comparison with their method would be valuable in demonstrating the performance improvement.
