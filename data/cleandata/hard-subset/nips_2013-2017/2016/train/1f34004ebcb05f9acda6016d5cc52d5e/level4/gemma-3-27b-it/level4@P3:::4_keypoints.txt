- Addresses learning Supervised PageRank models with both gradient-based and gradient-free optimization for non-convex loss minimization.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees missing in prior Supervised PageRank work.  
- Uses an inexact-oracle framework to balance lower-level stationary distribution accuracy and overall computational complexity.  
- Builds on existing PageRank extensions that incorporate node/edge features and previous Supervised PageRank formulations.  
- Strength: formal convergence rate guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods.  
- Strength: practical inexact oracle approach suitable for large-scale graphs and analysis of accuracy/complexity trade-offs.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis of methods, parameter effects, and complexity estimations.  
- Strength: empirical validation shows improvement over the state-of-the-art gradient-based method on a real-world ranking task.  
- Strength: the paper is generally well-written and explains technical details clearly.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single dataset/task (Yandex web ranking), limiting generalizability.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to hyperparameters (e.g., L, ε) is not thoroughly explored.  
- Weakness: lacks comparisons to a broader set of gradient-free optimization methods beyond the adaptation of [16].  
- Weakness: complexity analysis is detailed but not intuitively or practically clarified (e.g., scaling with graph size/features).  
- Weakness: missing ablation studies to isolate contributions of method components.  
- For acceptance: addresses an important problem and provides novel, valuable theoretical contributions.  
- For acceptance: empirical results support effectiveness and the paper is clearly presented.  
- Against acceptance: experimental scope is limited and comparisons to other gradient-free algorithms are incomplete.  
- Against acceptance: complexity analysis could be made more interpretable for practical implications.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions; merits acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—expand experiments, clarify complexity implications, and compare to more gradient-free methods.
