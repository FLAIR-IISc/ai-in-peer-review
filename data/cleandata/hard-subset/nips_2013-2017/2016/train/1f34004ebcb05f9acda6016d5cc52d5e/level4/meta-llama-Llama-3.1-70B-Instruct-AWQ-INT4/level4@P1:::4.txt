This paper proposes gradient-based and random gradient-free methods to solve a non-convex loss-minimization problem of learning supervised PageRank models. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates and complexity bounds of the proposed methods, which is a significant contribution to the field.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors do an excellent job of explaining the technical details of the proposed methods. The writing is clear, and the notation is well-defined. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of the related work, which helps to situate their contributions within the broader context of the field.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for solving non-convex optimization problems with inexact oracles. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach by applying it to the problem of learning supervised PageRank models, which is a challenging problem in its own right.

The experimental results presented in the paper are also impressive, demonstrating the superiority of the proposed methods over the state-of-the-art gradient-based method. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of their methods, which is essential for understanding their practical implications.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, the authors could provide more insight into the choice of the Lipschitz constant L, which plays a crucial role in the convergence analysis of the proposed methods. Secondly, the authors could provide more details on the computational resources required to implement their methods, which would be helpful for practitioners who want to use these methods in practice.

Overall, this is a well-written paper that makes significant contributions to the field of optimization. The authors demonstrate a deep understanding of the technical details of the proposed methods, and their experimental results are impressive. With a few minor revisions to address the areas mentioned above, this paper has the potential to be a seminal contribution to the field.
