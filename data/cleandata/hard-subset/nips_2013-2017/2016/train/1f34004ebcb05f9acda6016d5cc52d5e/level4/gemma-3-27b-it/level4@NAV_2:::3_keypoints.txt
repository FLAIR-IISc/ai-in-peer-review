- Paper proposes two two-level optimization methods (gradient-based GBN and gradient-free GFN) for supervised PageRank.  
- Methods avoid computing exact objective values and provide estimated convergence rates for a given accuracy.  
- GBN offers a gradient-based approach with proven convergence rate; GFN provides a gradient-free method with guaranteed loss decrease.  
- Both methods build on Nesterov and Nemirovski’s optimization framework (ref:17).  
- Theoretical guarantees address limitations of prior work (e.g., ref:21) and are provided in the paper.  
- Experimental results claim superior ranking quality compared to state-of-the-art.  
- Hyperparameters for both methods are reported, and proofs are in the supplementary material.  
- Datasets are not publicly available, but dataset descriptions are clear enough to suggest reproducibility is feasible.  
- Paper contains many symbols and dense equations, reducing readability; a symbol table is recommended.  
- Some symbols are undefined early on (e.g., N is used before being defined), which harms clarity.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: comparisons focus on loss function rather than ranking quality metrics.  
- A direct empirical comparison with the generalized baseline (ref:17) is missing and would be valuable.
