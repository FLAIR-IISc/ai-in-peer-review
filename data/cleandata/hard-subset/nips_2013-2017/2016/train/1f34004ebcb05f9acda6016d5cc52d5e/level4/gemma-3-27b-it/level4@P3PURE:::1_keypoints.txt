- Paper studies convergence for a non-convex loss from learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by random walks with feature-dependent node/edge weights.  
- Direct optimization is intractable because existing methods require exact objective values, so authors propose a two-level approach.  
- Level 1: a linearly convergent method estimates the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk and shows the loss can be approximated to arbitrary precision.  
- Level 2: a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle is developed and proven to converge to a stationary point.  
- Core technical contribution: adapting convergence analysis to constrained optimization when function values are known only up to specified precision, and using this in the two-level algorithm.  
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear sound, though the reviewer could not fully verify all details.  
- Major concern: key optimization techniques heavily build on prior work (notably Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015).  
- Related-method concern: supervised PageRank-style algorithms have been explored previously, raising novelty questions.  
- Minor suggestions: add a concluding section summarizing findings and future directions, and present the optimization problem in a more general framework to better position the contribution.
