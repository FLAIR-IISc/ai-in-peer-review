- Problem & contribution: Proposes gradient-based and gradient-free methods for learning Supervised PageRank using an inexact oracle and provides convergence guarantees.  
- Theoretical guarantees: Offers convergence rate guarantees for both methods, improving over prior work [21] which lacked such guarantees.  
- Inexact oracle approach: Practical, well-motivated technique that enables analysis of the accuracy vs. computational complexity trade-off.  
- Experimental comparison: Empirical evaluation shows improved ranking quality over the state-of-the-art [21] on a real-world Yandex dataset.  
- Clear formulation & related work: Precise problem statement, solid mathematical formulation, and a thorough literature review.  
- Experimental detail weakness: Lacks detailed reporting of parameter tuning and runtimes; needs time-to-convergence data.  
- Complexity analysis weakness: Theoretical complexity bounds are dense and hard to interpret; require a more intuitive practical explanation.  
- Dataset limitation: Experiments only on a single Yandex dataset, raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Convexity assumption concern: Use of local convexity for Theorem 1 seems ad-hoc and needs justification or discussion of non-convex behavior.  
- Redundancy: Some repetitive descriptions (e.g., model description) across sections.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel approach with strong theory, convergence guarantees, and demonstrated empirical effectiveness.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficiently comprehensive experiments, opaque complexity analysis, and questionable convexity assumption.  
- Overall assessment: Solid paper with contributions that outweigh weaknesses but requiring clarifications.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve experimental detail, clarify complexity analysis, and address the convexity assumption.
