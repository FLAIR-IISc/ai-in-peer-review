- Problem: learns Supervised PageRank parameters via a non-convex two-level optimization using node/edge features and inexact oracle for the stationary distribution.  
- Methods: proposes two two-level algorithms — one gradient-based and one gradient-free — both using inexact oracles.  
- Main contributions: provides convergence-rate guarantees for both methods, a framework for constrained non-convex optimization with inexact oracles, and empirical improvements on a web-ranking task.  
- Analysis: studies the trade-off between lower-level approximation accuracy and overall computational complexity for large-scale settings.  
- Strength: gives theoretical convergence guarantees, advancing beyond prior work [21].  
- Strength: carefully analyzes computational trade-offs useful for practical deployment.  
- Strength: experiments show consistent, statistically significant ranking improvements over [21] (p<0.005).  
- Weakness: major technical details and most empirical results are in the supplement; main text lacks justification for choices (e.g., ϕ₀ and R) and omits key tables.  
- Weakness: inconsistent citations/claims — paper refers to "Supervised PageRank" but cites [21], which is "Supervised Nested PageRank."  
- Weakness: constraint Φ = {ϕ : ‖ϕ−ϕ̂‖₂ ≤ R} may be overly restrictive; selection and sensitivity of ϕ̂ and R are not discussed.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks detail on graph diversity and generalizability beyond the Yandex dataset.  
- Weakness: novelty claim about combining gradient-free/gradient methods with inexact-oracle large-scale optimization needs stronger justification versus related work [16,7].  
- Presentation issues: grammatical errors, unclear problem formulation, inconsistent related-work references, and imprecise mathematical notation/explanations.  
- Recommendation: theoretical results are sound but presentation, parameter-selection details, inclusion of key results in the main paper, and clearer positioning relative to prior work must be addressed before acceptance.
