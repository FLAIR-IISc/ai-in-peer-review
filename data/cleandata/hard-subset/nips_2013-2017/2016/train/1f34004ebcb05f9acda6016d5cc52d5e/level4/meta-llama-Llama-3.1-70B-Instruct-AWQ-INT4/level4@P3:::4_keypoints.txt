- Proposes two novel methods (gradient-based and random gradient-free) for non-convex loss minimization in Supervised PageRank.  
- Develops general frameworks for gradient methods and random gradient-free methods with inexact oracle and applies them to the problem.  
- Provides thorough mathematical derivations, convergence-rate analysis, and complexity bounds.  
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform existing methods on the presented dataset.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with detailed explanations.  
- Strength: significant methodological contribution via two novel algorithms.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis and complexity guarantees.  
- Strength: empirical evidence demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with the inexact oracle concept; lacks a brief introduction.  
- Weakness: no clear comparison of computational complexity and runtime vs. existing methods.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to a single problem/dataset, reducing evidence of generality.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, significant; rated 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel methods, thorough analysis, strong empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on reader's prior knowledge of inexact oracle, missing runtime/complexity comparisons, limited experimental scope.
