This paper analyzes the convergence of a non-convex loss-minimization problem for learning the parameters of a general graph-based ranking model. The model is based on a random walk where node and edge weights are determined by a secondary random walk over the features of the nodes and edges. A key challenge is that the objective function cannot be computed exactly, rendering standard optimization methods that require exact function values inapplicable.

To address this, the authors propose a two-level approach. At the first level, a linearly convergent method is used to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov random walk, which in turn allows the loss function to be approximated to any desired precision. At the second level, they develop a gradient method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems that uses an inexact oracle. They prove that this method converges to a stationary point of the problem. The primary contribution here is the adaptation of such an approach to constrained optimization problems where the function value can only be calculated with a known precision.

The paper is well-written, and the proofs appear to be correct, although I have not verified them in detail. My main concern, however, is that the core optimization techniques seem to be adaptations of methods proposed by Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015), and the supervised PageRank algorithm has also been proposed previously.

On a minor note, the paper is missing a conclusion, which would be a welcome addition. I would also suggest presenting the optimization problem in a more general context and then showing the studied algorithm as a specific instance. This would help to better position the paper's contribution with respect to the state-of-the-art.
