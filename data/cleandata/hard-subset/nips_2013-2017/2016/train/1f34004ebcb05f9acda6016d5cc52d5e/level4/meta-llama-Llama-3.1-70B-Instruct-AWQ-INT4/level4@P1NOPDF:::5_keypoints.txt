- Paper extends PageRank to learn node/edge weights from features, motivated by prior work.  
- Authors claim prior parameter-learning algorithm lacked guarantees and propose a new approach.  
- Proposed method combines stationary-distribution approximation for Markov walks with a gradient-free optimization suited to noisy loss estimates.  
- Framework is essentially a reformulation of prior work under new name/notation, with problematic citation to [21].  
- Paper states several technical assumptions, defines a loss, and argues the problem is challenging.  
- Lemmas bound the approximate gradient and a general biased gradient-free oracle is analyzed with a performance theorem.  
- Under additional assumptions the authors build a concrete algorithm and adapt the gradient-free method, providing further bounds.  
- Experiments are only outlined on a web graph; detailed results and analysis are relegated to the appendix.  
- Important experimental parameters (e.g., phi0, R) and whether multiple/different graphs were tested are not reported.  
- Claim of outperforming baselines lacks discussion of the magnitude or practical implications of the improvement.  
- Main paper omits proof outlines and full empirical results, placing most content in the appendix.  
- Reviewer doubts practical impact: assumptions may not hold in real data and the R-ball search restriction could limit applicability.  
- Writing quality is poor with grammatical errors and sloppy/misleading citations.  
- Overall, reviewer unconvinced of the paper’s significance despite strong theoretical bounds.
