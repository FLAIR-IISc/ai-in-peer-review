

This paper formulates Supervised PageRank models as constrained non-convex optimization problems by incorporating node and edge properties. The authors propose two solution approaches: a gradient-free method utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle, and a gradient-based method employing an inexact first-order oracle. My primary concern is that no experimental results appear in the main text, significantly undermining the paper's completeness. Detailed comments follow:  
1. Experimental results are relegated to the supplement, preventing meaningful evaluation within the main body.  
2. Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the objective function in (2.4) is explicitly non-convex. How does the choice of set $\Phi$ ensure convexity?  
3. Theorem 3 provides a bound for $M\|x_K - x_{K+1}\|$. Does this bound guarantee convergence of the sequence $\{x_k\}$ to a stationary point?  
4. Figure 1 (supplement) indicates slow convergence of GFNM; the authors should report algorithmic efficiency comparisons.
