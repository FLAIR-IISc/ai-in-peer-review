- Proposes a weighted PageRank framework with a feature model and aims to provide performance guarantees for parameter estimation.
- Combines stationary distribution approximation of a Markov random walk with a gradient-free optimization method for loss functions evaluated via estimation.
- Framework largely reproduces prior work ([21]) under different notation.
- Paper states multiple technical assumptions, defines a loss, and acknowledges solution difficulty.
- Lemmas provide approximate gradient bounds supporting the approach.
- Presents a general gradient-free method using a biased oracle and a corresponding performance theorem.
- Develops a tailored learning algorithm building on the gradient-free method with associated bounds.
- Experiments run on a web graph previously used in related studies, with detailed results placed in the appendix.
- Experimental reporting is incomplete; key parameters are missing.
- Claims of improved performance lack quantitative evidence and discussion of significance/magnitude.
- Unclear how many and what kinds of graph datasets were used for evaluation.
- Manuscript contains numerous grammatical errors and inconsistent citations (e.g., mislabeling “Supervised PageRank” vs. cited semi-supervised work).
- Practical impact is questioned: empirical validity of assumptions is uncertain and some assumptions seem restrictive.
- Restricting φ search to an R-ball around φ0 may severely limit applicability and requires discussion.
- Core contributions (proofs and detailed empirical results) are relegated to the appendix; main text lacks proof sketches and substantive experimental data.
- Specific experimental details missing: selection of φ0 and R, and whether evaluation extended beyond a single graph.
- Overall, despite theoretically strong bounds and a plausible algorithm, the paper fails to persuasively demonstrate practical contributions in its current form.
