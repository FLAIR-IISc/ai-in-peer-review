- Motivates PageRank and introduces node/edge weights from a feature model.  
- Notes prior parameter-learning work for this model lacks performance guarantees.  
- Proposes combining stationary-distribution approximation for Markov walks with a gradient-free optimizer for noisy-loss functions.  
- Reformulates the framework under new notation, closely mirroring [21].  
- States several technical assumptions, defines a loss function, and explains inherent problem difficulty.  
- Provides key lemmas that bound the error of the approximate gradient.  
- Presents a general biased gradient-free oracle method with an accompanying performance theorem.  
- Adds further assumptions to derive a tailored algorithm for the learning problem and additional bounds.  
- Reports experiments on a web graph from prior work but only outlines results briefly.  
- Omits critical experimental details in the main text (parameter choices and specific performance metrics).  
- Claims the algorithm outperforms competitors without reporting the magnitude or statistical significance of improvements.  
- Unclear whether experiments included diverse graph types or multiple datasets.  
- Writing has numerous grammatical errors and inconsistent citations (e.g., calling the problem "Supervised PageRank" while citing [21] on semi-supervised PageRank).  
- Questions the practical validity of restrictive technical assumptions (e.g., constraining φ to an R-ball around φ₀).  
- Main substantive content (proofs and full empirical results) is relegated to the appendix with no proof outlines in the main text.  
- Raises unanswered experimental questions: how were φ₀ and R chosen, and were multiple graphs tested?  
- Main paper omits tables, detailed results, and discussion of practical implications.  
- Conclusion: theoretical analysis appears strong but the paper fails to convincingly demonstrate practical value.
