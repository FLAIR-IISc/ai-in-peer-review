- Paper proposes PageRank framework with node/edge weights from a feature model and criticizes prior parameter-learning algorithm for lacking guarantees.
- Authors combine an approximate stationary-distribution method with a gradient-free optimization suited to noisy-loss estimation.
- Framework resembles [21] but uses different notation and terminology, raising concerns about novelty.
- Several technical assumptions are stated; a loss function and problem-solving challenges are described.
- Lemmas are provided that bound the approximate gradient.
- A general gradient-free method using a biased gradient-free oracle is presented, along with a performance theorem.
- Under extra assumptions the authors adapt the gradient-free method to their learning problem and derive further bounds.
- Experiments are briefly reported on a web graph, with details deferred to the appendix.
- Important experimental parameters are not reported, and the extent/implications of claimed improvements are not discussed.
- It is unclear whether experiments used multiple or diverse graphs.
- Writing contains grammatical errors and sloppy citations.
- The paper mislabels the problem as Supervised PageRank despite [21] addressing semi-supervised PageRank.
- Reviewer is unconvinced of the practical impact of the problem being solved.
- Doubts exist about the validity of technical assumptions for real data, particularly the R-ball restriction around phi0.
- Primary concern: major content (proof outlines and empirical results) is relegated to the appendix.
- Authors do not explain how phi0 and R were chosen in experiments or whether multiple graphs were tested.
- Main text lacks specific results, tables, and analysis of practical consequences, weakening the paper’s significance.
