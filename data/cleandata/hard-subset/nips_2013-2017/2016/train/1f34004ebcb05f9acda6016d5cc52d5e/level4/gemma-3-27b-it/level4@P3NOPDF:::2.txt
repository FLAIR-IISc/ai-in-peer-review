## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission: Supervised PageRank with Node and Edge Properties

This paper proposes a formulation of Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem, leveraging node and edge properties to enhance the ranking process. The authors present two algorithmic approaches for solving this optimization: a gradient-free method utilizing an inexact zero-order oracle, and a gradient-based method employing an inexact first-order oracle. While the proposed formulation and algorithmic approaches are potentially valuable contributions to the field, the current presentation suffers from a significant weakness: the complete absence of experimental results in the main text. This omission renders the paper incomplete and makes a thorough evaluation of the proposed methods impossible.

**Major Concerns:**

The most critical issue is the relocation of all experimental results to the supplementary material. Presenting results only in the supplement severely hinders the reader’s ability to quickly grasp the empirical performance and effectiveness of the proposed algorithms. The main text should include a concise but comprehensive overview of the key experimental findings, including comparisons to baseline methods, and a discussion of the observed trends. Without this information, it’s difficult to assess the practical value of the theoretical contributions.

**Specific Comments & Questions:**

1. **Convexity and Objective Function:** Theorem 1 assumes convexity, yet the function defined in equation (2.4) appears to be non-convex. The authors need to clarify how the choice of the set $\Phi$ influences the convexity of the objective function. A detailed discussion of the conditions under which $\Phi$ can be selected to guarantee convexity is crucial. Is there a trade-off between the expressiveness of node/edge properties incorporated (and thus the size of $\Phi$) and the ability to maintain convexity?

2. **Convergence Analysis (Theorem 3):** Theorem 3 provides a bound for $MK(xK - x{K+1})$. However, it’s unclear whether this bound is sufficient to guarantee convergence of the sequence $x_k$ to a stationary point. The authors should explicitly address this point and discuss the implications of the bound for the convergence properties of the proposed gradient-based method. Providing a more direct connection between the bound and the convergence criteria would significantly strengthen the analysis.

3. **Experimental Performance and Efficiency:**  The supplementary material (as referenced in the text) shows that the gradient-free method (GFNM) converges relatively slowly (Figure 1). The authors should explicitly discuss this observation in the main text. Furthermore, a quantitative assessment of the algorithms' efficiency – computational time, scalability with respect to graph size and number of features – is missing. Reporting running times, memory usage, and potentially the number of oracle evaluations would provide valuable insights into the practical applicability of the methods. The main text should include a comparison of the computational cost of both proposed approaches.

4. **Clarification of Notation:** While generally clear, a few points could benefit from clarification.  For example, the meaning of ‘M’ and ‘K’ in the context of Theorem 3 should be explicitly stated within the theorem’s description.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Formulation:** The formulation of Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem, incorporating node and edge properties, represents a potentially useful direction for research.
* **Algorithmic Contributions:** The proposal of both gradient-free and gradient-based methods offers flexibility in addressing the optimization problem, potentially catering to different characteristics of the dataset and computational resources.
* **Theoretical Analysis:** The provided theoretical analysis, including the convergence bound in Theorem 3, demonstrates a rigorous approach to understanding the properties of the proposed algorithms.



**Recommendations:**

I recommend **rejecting** this paper in its current form. The absence of experimental results in the main text is a critical flaw that prevents a proper evaluation of the proposed methods. 

To be reconsidered, the authors must:

* **Include a comprehensive summary of the experimental results in the main text.** This should include comparisons to relevant baselines and a clear discussion of the observed performance.
* **Address the questions regarding convexity and convergence analysis.** Clarify the conditions for convexity and explicitly state whether the provided bound guarantees convergence to a stationary point.
* **Provide a discussion of the algorithms’ efficiency and scalability.** Include computational time and resource usage in the experimental evaluation.



Addressing these concerns will significantly improve the quality and impact of the paper.
