1. Paper formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained, non-convex optimization using node/edge features and proposes a gradient-free method with an inexact zero-order oracle and a gradient-based method with an inexact first-order oracle.

2. Major weakness: experimental results are omitted from the main text and relegated to the supplement, leaving the paper feeling incomplete and preventing self-contained assessment of practical performance.

3. Placement of experiments: the main paper should include summaries of key empirical findings, datasets, and baseline comparisons to demonstrate effectiveness.

4. Convexity contradiction: Theorem 1 assumes convexity, but Equation (2.4) is generally non-convex; authors must clarify conditions on Φ (if any) that make the objective convex.

5. Convergence guarantee unclear: Theorem 3’s bound on successive iterates doesn’t explicitly show convergence to a stationary point; the authors should state and prove the algorithm’s convergence properties.

6. Algorithm efficiency concerns: GFNM appears to converge slowly (per supp. Figure 1); the paper should report wall-clock time or function-evaluation counts and compare to baselines.
