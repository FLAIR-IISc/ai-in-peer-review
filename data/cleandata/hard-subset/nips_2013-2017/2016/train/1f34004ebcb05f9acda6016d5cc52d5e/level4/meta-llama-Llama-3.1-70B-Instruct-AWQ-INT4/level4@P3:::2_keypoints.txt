- Formulates Supervised PageRank as a constrained non-convex optimization problem using node/edge features.  
- Proposes two solvers: a gradient-free method with inexact zero-order oracle (GFNM) and a gradient-based method with inexact first-order oracle.  
- Provides a thorough introduction and clear motivation, highlighting limits of existing methods and benefits of feature incorporation.  
- Presents clear, concise descriptions of the proposed algorithms.  
- Offers rigorous theoretical analysis with proofs of convergence rates and complexity bounds.  
- Experimental results (in the supplement) show the methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline.  
- Major concern: Theorem 1 assumes convexity despite objective (2.4) being non-convex; authors must justify this or explain how to choose Φ to guarantee convexity.  
- Major concern: Theorem 3 bounds MK(xK − x{K+1}) but does not make clear whether xk converges to a stationary point; additional analysis/discussion needed.  
- Practical concern: GFNM converges slowly; authors should report runtime and/or iteration counts to evaluate efficiency.  
- Usability concern: Key theoretical details are primarily in the supplement, which may hinder accessibility for non-experts.  
- Impact: Significant contribution to optimization for Supervised PageRank but potentially limited by convexity assumption and GFNM’s slow convergence.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the theoretical and empirical concerns.  
- Reviewer scores: Originality 8/10; Quality 8/10; Clarity 9/10; Significance 8/10.
