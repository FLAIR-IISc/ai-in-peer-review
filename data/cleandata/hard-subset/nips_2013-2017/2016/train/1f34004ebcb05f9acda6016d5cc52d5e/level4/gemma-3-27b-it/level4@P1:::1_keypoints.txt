- Paper studies convergence for learning parameters of a graph-based ranking model defined by a feature-dependent random walk (supervised PageRank).
- Main challenge: objective cannot be computed exactly, complicating optimization for a non-convex problem.
- Proposed two-level algorithm: inner level uses a linearly convergent method to approximate the Markov stationary distribution to any precision.
- Developed an inexact-oracle gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization and proved convergence to a stationary point.
- Adapted the inexact-oracle approach to cases where function values are known to specified accuracy and proved convergence for the upper-level problem.
- Theoretical proofs are well-presented and appear sound, though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Experimental results show improved performance over existing methods and a rigorous analysis of accuracy vs. computational complexity.
- Primary concern: core optimization techniques heavily build on prior work (notably Nesterov & Spokoiny 2015), making novelty incremental.
- Additional concern: the supervised PageRank framework itself is not new.
- Minor issue: paper lacks a concluding section summarizing contributions and future work.
- Suggestion: place the optimization problem in a broader general framework to better situate the contribution and applicability.
- Acceptance arguments: theoretical convergence guarantees, trade-off analysis, empirical improvements, and clear writing.
- Rejection arguments: reliance on adapted existing techniques, lack of novelty in the framework, and missing conclusion.
