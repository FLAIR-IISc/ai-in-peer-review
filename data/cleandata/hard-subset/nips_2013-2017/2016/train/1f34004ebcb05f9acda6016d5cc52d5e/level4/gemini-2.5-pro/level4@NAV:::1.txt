This paper presents a convergence analysis for a non-convex loss minimization problem aimed at learning parameters for a sophisticated graph-based ranking model. The model is characterized by a random walk governed by node and edge weights, which are themselves defined through random walks over node and edge features.

A key challenge is that the optimization problem is intractable for standard methods that require exact objective function evaluations. To address this, the authors propose a two-level approach. The first level involves a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the underlying Markov random walk. The authors validate this component and show that it can estimate the loss function to any desired degree of precision.

For the second level, they develop a gradient-based method for general constrained non-convex optimization problems that rely on an inexact oracle. They prove that this method converges to a stationary point. The main contribution lies in adapting this inexact oracle framework to the constrained optimization setting where the objective function can only be approximated with known precision. This method is then applied at the second level of their algorithm to solve the parameter learning problem.

The manuscript is well-written, and the proofs appear to be sound, although I did not conduct an exhaustive verification. My primary concern is regarding the novelty of the contributions. The core optimization techniques seem to be adaptations of those introduced by Nesterov and Spokoiny (2015), and the supervised PageRank model has also been previously established.

On a minor point, the paper would benefit from a conclusion section, for which there appears to be adequate space. I would also suggest framing the optimization problem in a more general context first, subsequently positioning the specific graph-ranking algorithm as a special case. This would help to better situate the paper's contributions within the current state-of-the-art.
