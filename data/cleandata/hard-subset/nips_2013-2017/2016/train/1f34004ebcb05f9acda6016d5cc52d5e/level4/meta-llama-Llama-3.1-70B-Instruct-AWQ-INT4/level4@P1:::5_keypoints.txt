- Paper motivates PageRank and proposes a learning framework with node/edge weights from a feature model, aiming to learn parameters with performance guarantees.  
- Authors combine a Markov stationary-distribution approximation method with an adaptation of a gradient-free optimizer for noisy loss estimates.  
- The paper reproduces a framework from prior work ([21]) under different notation and discusses technical assumptions and a loss function, stating why the problem is hard.  
- Several lemmas bounding the approximate gradient and a theorem on a biased gradient-free oracle’s performance are presented.  
- Authors construct a tailored algorithm for the learning problem, adapt gradient-free methods, and state additional theoretical bounds.  
- Strength: development of a gradient-free optimization method for general convex problems with an inexact zero-order oracle.  
- Strength: proposal of an adaptive gradient method for possibly nonconvex composite optimization with an inexact first-order oracle.  
- Strength: convergence rate analyses are provided for both methods.  
- Weakness: writing contains many grammatical errors and citations are sloppy/misleading (e.g., mislabeling prior work as “Supervised PageRank”).  
- Weakness: technical assumptions may not hold in real data and could limit applicability (e.g., constraining phi to an R-ball around phi0).  
- Weakness: most technical and empirical “meat” is confined to the appendix, with no proof outlines or main-body empirical results/tables.  
- Weakness: empirical section lacks explanation for choice of phi0 and R, unclear whether experiments used more than one graph, and practical impact is not discussed.  
- Recommendation: paper has promising theoretical contributions but requires revision to fix writing/citations, justify assumptions, and expand/clarify empirical results and analysis.
