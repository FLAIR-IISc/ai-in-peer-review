- Paper analyzes convergence for non-convex loss minimization in supervised PageRank where node/edge weights come from features and a random walk.
- Proposes a two-level approach: lower level approximates stationary distribution with a linearly convergent method, upper level uses gradient-based and gradient-free optimization with an inexact oracle.
- Provides convergence guarantees for both gradient-based and gradient-free methods, adapting existing techniques for constrained optimization.
- Uses inexact oracles to address practical difficulty of computing the objective and gradients on large graphs.
- Builds on prior work (Supervised PageRank and methods by Nesterov & Spokoiny) and acknowledges related PageRank computation methods.
- Theoretical analysis and proofs appear sound though not fully verified by the reviewer.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear mathematical derivations and structure.
- Empirical results show the proposed methods outperform or match state-of-the-art, demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Strength: provides important theoretical guarantees for a challenging non-convex problem.
- Strength: inexact oracle approach makes the methods practically applicable to large-scale graphs.
- Strength: comprehensive related work and clear presentation.
- Weakness: contribution is somewhat incremental, mainly adapting existing optimization techniques to this setting.
- Weakness: limited contextualization—could frame supervised PageRank as an instance of a broader optimization class.
- Weakness: missing a concluding section summarizing findings, limitations, and future work.
- Weakness: dataset description lacks detail on size and characteristics.
- Recommendation: solid contribution with minor revisions (add conclusion, broaden context); reviewer recommends acceptance with score 6/10.
