- Paper motivates PageRank and proposes learning node/edge weights from a feature-based model, noting prior algorithms lack guarantees.
- Approach combines an existing stationary-distribution approximation for Markov walks with an adapted gradient-free optimization for estimable loss functions.
- The framework essentially reintroduces the model from [21] under a different name and notation.
- Several technical assumptions are listed, a loss is defined, and the problem’s challenges are explained.
- Lemmas bound the approximate gradient; a general gradient-free method with a biased oracle is discussed and a performance theorem is given.
- With extra assumptions the authors construct a learning algorithm, adapt gradient-free methods, and state further bounds.
- Experiments are briefly described on a web graph, but detailed results and analyses are deferred to the appendix.
- Important experimental parameters are missing, and there is no discussion of the magnitude or practical significance of claimed improvements.
- It is unclear whether evaluations included diverse graphs or multiple datasets.
- Writing has numerous grammatical errors and citations are imprecise (e.g., “Supervised PageRank” vs. [21] which seems semi-supervised).
- The reviewer is unconvinced of the paper’s practical impact and doubts empirical validity of the technical assumptions.
- Some assumptions appear restrictive (e.g., constraining phi search to an R-ball around phi0) and lack discussion of consequences.
- Main substantive content (proofs, comprehensive empirical results) resides in the appendix rather than the main text.
- Key experimental choices are unexplained (How were phi0 and R chosen?), and the paper reports no tables or quantitative outcomes in the main body.
