- Paper studies convergence for non-convex loss minimization in a graph-based ranking model defined by a Markov random walk.
- Node and edge weights, determined by features, govern the walk dynamics and thus the ranking objective.
- Standard optimization methods fail because they require exact objective evaluations, which are unavailable here.
- Authors propose a two-level approach to handle inexact objective information.
- Level 1: use a linearly convergent method to approximate the stationary distribution of the Markov walk, enabling arbitrarily precise loss-value approximations.
- Level 2: develop a gradient method for constrained non-convex optimization with an inexact oracle and prove convergence to a stationary point.
- Main contribution: adapting the inexact oracle framework to constrained settings where function evaluations are known to within specified precision.
- Paper is well-written and proofs appear sound (not fully verified by reviewer).
- Primary concern: core optimization techniques closely adapt Nesterov & Spokoiny (2015) methods; novelty may be limited.
- Additional concern: supervised PageRank algorithm groundwork was previously proposed, reducing perceived novelty.
- Paper lacks a formal conclusion despite sufficient space.
- Recommendation: reframe the optimization problem within a broader theoretical framework and clarify how the algorithm generalizes or extends prior inexact-oracle methods to better situate the contribution.
