- Paper proposes a supervised variant of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a Mahalanobis ground metric over word embeddings and a histogram reweighting.  
- Authors derive NCA-style gradients for the objective and provide strong empirical validation at scale.  
- Contribution is a clean synthesis of known components; conceptually simple but nontrivial to implement; experiments are convincing.  
- Reviewer inclined to recommend acceptance; main merits are simplicity and practicality.  
- Request to better articulate the relationship between regularized and unregularized OT/Wasserstein formulations.  
- Suggest tightening mathematical exposition: address notational and derivational looseness.  
- Ask for a clear algorithmic summary to crystallize the method and implementation.  
- Minor: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean — recommend framing WMD as p-Wasserstein for arbitrary p to avoid mismatch.  
- Minor: Histogram “reweighting” is not novel — cite Lebanon’s metric learning for text and Le & Cuturi’s Aitchison-transform work.  
- Minor: Provide ablation/experimental analysis on the learned weight vector w to show its empirical contribution.  
- Minor: Clarify distinctions from prior work (Cuturi/Avis): emphasize entropy regularization for speed/scale and use of continuous linear ground-cost parameterization; clarify connection and differences with Wang et al.
