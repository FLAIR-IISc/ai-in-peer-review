This paper demonstrates a supervised approach to learning distances between documents, specifically Word Movers Distance (WMD). The proposal for WMD was inspired by Earth Movers Distance. More specifically, it calculates the optimal transport to obtain document distances from one word distribution to another. For transport distances between words, Word2Vec embedding is primarily used. In this paper, the authors propose learning (1) a linear transformation of embedded words and (2) a related word distribution, so that documents of different labels become more distinct. This paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The paper contains a thorough review of previously related work, and the authors have a good understanding of that literature. The authors provide experimental results of their algorithm for many new datasets. However, the purpose of the formulation needs further explanation, and its significance is low. 1) Why do the authors use the rewiring of word distribution using w? This is not well explained, and it seems that this rewiring can transform the document into a completely different document. If the authors have shown why this rewiring helps instead of transforming the document into a different document, then this rewiring can be supported. 2) The contribution of this work is not significant. NCA objective function with linear transformation `A' and rewiring `w' is a potential extension for WMD's supervised settings, but this formulation is not groundbreaking or novel. 3) How can this algorithm find distances for a new document? How can one find a rewit w for a completely new document? 4) It seems that the semantic difference in the abstract indicates the label of the document. The author's difference or difference of content does not seem to be a semantic difference. Does semantic difference mean something else? In general, the paper is well written, and I do not object to accepting this paper if the questions are properly explained.
