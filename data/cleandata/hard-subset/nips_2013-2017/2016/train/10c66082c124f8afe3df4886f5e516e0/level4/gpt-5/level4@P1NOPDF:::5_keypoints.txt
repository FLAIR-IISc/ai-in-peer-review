- Paper extends Word Mover’s Distance to supervised text classification via learned, word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance to accelerate computation.  
- Empirical evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines shows the best average performance.  
- Paper is very well written with clear presentation and thorough mathematical treatment.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance work.  
- Discussion comparing to Word Centroid Distance is confusing and arguably irrelevant; should be tightened or removed.  
- Major concern: treatment of the entropic regularization parameter λ is insufficient and potentially crucial.  
- Need to clarify whether the method operates in a near-Wasserstein regime or a distinctly entropic (Sinkhorn) regime.  
- Must report how λ is selected (per-dataset or global), the search range, and the tuning protocol.  
- Require a sensitivity analysis showing performance and convergence as a function of λ.  
- Should report whether performance gains over baselines persist across reasonable λ values and discuss trade-offs between accuracy and computational efficiency as λ varies.
