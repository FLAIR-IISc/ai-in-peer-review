- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance as an efficient approximation to WMD to enable practical supervised learning.  
- Evaluated on eight document classification datasets against 26 baselines, showing consistent improvements and lowest average error.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical foundation with derived gradient updates for both the linear transform and word weights.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive across diverse tasks (news categorization, sentiment, product identification).  
- Pseudocode and visualizations (learned word weights) improve clarity and reproducibility.  
- Well-written with clear exposition and strong connections to prior metric learning and document representation work.  
- Original in adapting WMD to a supervised setting and learning in low-dimensional embedding space rather than high-dimensional document space.  
- Learning word importance weights adds flexibility beyond previous metric learning approaches.  
- Significant practical impact: Sinkhorn efficiency and reported training times make the method viable for real-world use, especially with limited labeled data.  
- Primary weakness: insufficient analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ and its trade-off between approximation fidelity and convergence speed, including behavior for small λ.  
- Secondary weakness: lacks thorough explanation for underperformance on some datasets (e.g., CLASSIC) and whether initialization causes issues.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
