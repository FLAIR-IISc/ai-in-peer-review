This paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), a powerful metric for measuring the distance between textual documents. While the original WMD is unsupervised and relies on pre-trained word embeddings, this work introduces a method to learn a distance metric from data labeled with class information (i.e., pairs of documents that belong to the same or different classes). The proposed model learns two key components simultaneously: a linear transformation of the word embedding space and a set of word-specific importance weights. The authors have also committed to making their Matlab code publicly available.

The author's response to my initial questions was thorough and clarified several points, which I appreciate.

**Strengths**

1.  **Novel Contribution:** The extension of the WMD framework to a supervised setting is a valuable and well-motivated contribution. It provides a principled way to adapt the powerful semantic capabilities of WMD to specific classification tasks.

2.  **Methodology:** The approach of jointly learning a global geometric transformation (the matrix `A`) and word-level importance weights (`w`) is compelling. This allows the model to both reshape the entire semantic space and re-weight specific words to better suit the supervised task.

3.  **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results are strong, showing that the proposed method, Word Mover's Supervised Distance (WMSD), consistently outperforms the original WMD and other competing metric learning baselines on several text classification datasets.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement**

1.  **Intuition and Ablation:** The paper's primary contribution lies in learning the transformation matrix `A` and the word weight vector `w`. However, the paper could be significantly strengthened in two related areas:
    *   **Intuition:** A more detailed intuition behind the specific formulation of the word weights in Equation (5) would be beneficial. While the mathematical definition is clear, a conceptual explanation of why this particular form was chosen and what it represents would improve the paper's clarity.
    *   **Ablation Study:** It is currently difficult to assess the relative contribution of each of the two learned components (`A` and `w`) to the overall performance improvement. An ablation study would be highly informative. For instance, evaluating the performance of models trained with only the linear transformation (`A`, with all `w` fixed to 1) and only the word weights (`w`, with `A` fixed as the identity matrix) would provide valuable insight into which component is more critical for the tasks evaluated.

2.  **Justification of Claims:** The paper claims the method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" (line 316). While this property is inherited from the underlying WMD, the paper could better substantiate how the proposed supervised learning process specifically enhances this capability. A brief example or a more detailed argument would strengthen this claim.

**Minor Issues and Questions for Clarification**

1.  **Equation 2:** The role of Equation (2) in the overall framework is not entirely clear. Specifically, is the regularization parameter `lambda` introduced there the same `lambda` that appears in the main optimization problem in line 170? Clarifying this connection would be helpful.

2.  **Table and Figure References:**
    *   In line 257, the text states, "on the right we also show the average error across datasets." I was unable to locate this information in Table 3. Please clarify this reference or correct the table.
    *   In Table 2, the meanings of the column headers "n" and "ne" are not defined. Please add a clarification in the table caption or the main text.

3.  **Experimental Details:** The procedure for selecting the hyperparameter `k` for the k-NN classifier is not described. It would be helpful for reproducibility to know if `k` was chosen via cross-validation for each dataset/method combination or if a single value was used throughout.
