- Proposes supervised metric learning for WMD by jointly learning a linear projection of word embeddings and per-word document weights so transport costs become squared Euclidean in the projected space.  
- Presents an efficient, well-motivated optimization scheme enabling scalable learning of the parameterized transport metric.  
- Thorough experiments showing strong kNN classification performance across multiple text datasets and comparisons to many baselines.  
- Clear and readable presentation.  
- Main concern: empirical gains over a centroid-based Earth Mover’s Distance initialized with a simple metric-learning step are modest, questioning the practical benefit of the full joint OT learning.  
- Main concern: novelty is mainly in the optimization/implementation rather than fundamentally new modeling ideas (projection + reweighting are natural extensions).  
- Missing evaluation: compare a simple word-centroid document representation combined with standard supervised metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) to test whether OT adds value over a cheaper baseline.  
- Missing ablations: report results for (a) projection A only (no learned word weights) and (b) learned word weights only (no A) to isolate component contributions.  
- Minor clarity: specify which prior works are meant by “The authors” around Line 152 (cite [1],[7],[8] as appropriate).  
- Minor correction: Equation (11) likely should use α* and β* to denote optimal values consistently.  
- Minor clarification: explicitly state the scaling procedure around Line 170 is the Sinkhorn algorithm to link to the regularized OT discussion.  
- Overall assessment: solid, elegant technical contribution with efficient optimization and promising results, but impact is limited until centroid-baseline comparisons and component-wise ablations demonstrate clear, consistent advantages.
