- Contribution: Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance with a learnable linear projection A and word-reweighting vector w trained with a large-margin/NCA-style objective.  
- Clarity & presentation: Well-written, clearly structured, and explains WMD background effectively.  
- Literature review: Thorough coverage demonstrating strong command of relevant work in document distance and metric learning.  
- Experiments: Comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple datasets showing effectiveness.  
- Motivation for w: Insufficient justification for word reweighting—concern it can distort document semantics; authors should provide qualitative examples (e.g., up-weighting discriminative terms, down-weighting stopwords) and rationale.  
- Significance/novelty: Technical novelty appears incremental—applying standard metric-learning (A and w with large-margin loss) to WMD rather than a new methodological advance.  
- Inference on new documents: Missing practical detail—unclear whether w is global or document-specific and how to obtain w for unseen documents at test time.  
- Terminology (“semantic difference”): Ambiguous usage—should clarify whether they mean true semantic difference or class-discriminative distance (e.g., authorship/topic differences).  
- Overall: Promising and well-executed but limited impact due to unclear motivation for w, incremental novelty, and omission about test-time inference; reviewer is borderline and may raise score if concerns are addressed.
