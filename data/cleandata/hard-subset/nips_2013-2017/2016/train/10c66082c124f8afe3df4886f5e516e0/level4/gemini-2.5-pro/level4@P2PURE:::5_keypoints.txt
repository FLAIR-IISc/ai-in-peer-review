- Paper tackles supervised text classification by extending Word Mover's Distance (WMD).
- Two main contributions: learning word-specific weights in a supervised WMD framework and using Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy.
- Empirical evaluation on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear and deep mathematical exposition.
- Novelty appears incremental relative to the original WMD paper.
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance is misplaced and confusing rather than clarifying benefits.
- The role and choice of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ are insufficiently discussed.
- λ can dominate the Sinkhorn distance: large λ approximates Wasserstein (slower convergence) while small λ yields qualitatively different, entropically-regularized behavior.
- It is unclear whether the method operates in a Wasserstein-like regime or an entropic regime; sensitivity analysis of λ in experiments is needed.
