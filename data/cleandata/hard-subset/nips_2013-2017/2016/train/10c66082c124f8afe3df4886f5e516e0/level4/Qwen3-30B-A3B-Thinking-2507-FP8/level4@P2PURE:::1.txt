

This paper introduces a supervised metric learning approach leveraging the recent "word-movers distance" [19], a transport distance applied to word2vec embeddings. The method generalizes to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances after linear projection. It jointly learns a linear projection matrix and document-specific word weights—adjusting term frequencies via learned weights followed by re-normalization. The authors propose an efficient optimization procedure for the parameterized metric and demonstrate its effectiveness through extensive experiments on document classification datasets using a k-NN classifier. The approach extends the word-movers distance, representing a technically interesting and empirically effective enhancement for k-NN text classification tasks.  

However, the potential impact may be limited. As shown in Table 3, Earth Mover's Distance with metric learning initialized via "word centroid distance" achieves performance nearly identical to the full proposed method (last two rows), suggesting the primary technical contributions yield only marginal gains over this baseline initialization. The evaluation would benefit from:  
- Comparing "word centroid embedding" (weighted document mean of word embeddings) with existing metric learners (ITML, LMNN, NCA) in Table 3.  
- Assessing the relative importance of the word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A by reporting performance when each component is activated in isolation.  

The originality centers on the optimization strategy (Lines 156–175), though the core idea of linear metric learning within Earth Mover's Distance constitutes a limited contribution. The paper is well-written and clear. Minor points for clarification:
