- Contribution: Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a linear embedding transform and global word-importance weights to make transport distances reflect label similarity.
- Training objective: Optimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss, using entropically regularized Sinkhorn transport for differentiable, efficient distance computation.
- Initialization and training tricks: Uses supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) for initialization, mini-batching, and neighbor filtering for scalability.
- Computational complexity: Sinkhorn relaxation yields practical O(p^2) per-pair computation (p = unique words per document), much faster than exact WMD.
- Theoretical/technical soundness: Gradient derivations for both A and w follow from primal/dual OT properties; approximation and uniqueness issues are acknowledged.
- Experiments: Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines (unsupervised and supervised); S-WMD achieves best or near-best kNN performance and code is released.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD, NCA, and Sinkhorn/entropic OT; extends classic metric learning by operating in embedding space and reweighting histograms.
- Clarity: Paper is well written, organized, and provides clear explanations plus helpful visualizations (t-SNE, learned word weights).
- Originality: Novel, meaningful integration of known components to learn a supervised ground metric and histogram reweighting for WMD.
- Significance: Strong, consistent empirical gains make S-WMD a useful, data-efficient tool likely valuable to researchers and practitioners.
- Weakness—kNN focus: Objective is tied to NCA/kNN; needs evidence that improvements transfer to other classifiers.
- Weakness—ablations/sensitivity: Lacks ablations isolating A vs. w, impact of S-WCD initialization, and sensitivity to λ, batch size, and neighbor set N.
- Weakness—ground metric: Uses squared Euclidean cost which may be suboptimal; alternatives (L1, cosine, non-linear transforms) should be explored.
- Weakness—scalability limits: O(p^2) can still be expensive for very long documents; runtime/memory scaling curves and pruning strategies are needed.
- Weakness—baseline fairness/details: Clarify use of transductive LDA, add significance testing, consider recent neural baselines, and report stability over seeds.
- Weakness—model details: Clarify constraints/regularization on w, handling of OOV/rare words and stopwords, and regularization on A to prevent overfitting.
- Pros summary: Principled supervised extension of WMD, efficient Sinkhorn-based training, strong empirical gains, clear presentation, and released code.
- Cons summary: Limited component analysis and hyperparameter sensitivity study, linearity/ground metric limits, and evaluation centered on kNN.
- Overall recommendation: Solid, well-executed paper that advances supervised document distance learning; recommendation to accept.
