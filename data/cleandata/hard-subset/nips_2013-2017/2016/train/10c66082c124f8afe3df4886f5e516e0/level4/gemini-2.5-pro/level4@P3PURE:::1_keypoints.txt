- Proposes supervised metric learning for WMD by learning a linear projection of word embeddings and word-specific frequency weights with an efficient optimization procedure.  
- Thorough experiments show strong k-NN classification performance against many strong baselines on several datasets.  
- Strength: an interesting, technically sound extension to WMD that is effective for k-NN text classification.  
- Strength: paper is very well written and clearly structured.  
- Main concern: full optimization yields little improvement over a simpler initialization using a word-centroid distance (per Table 3).  
- Missing baseline: should compare to standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) applied to word-centroid embeddings.  
- Missing ablation study: need to evaluate the projection matrix A and the word-specific weights independently to assess their contributions.  
- Originality: idea is interesting but limited in novelty; main novelty is the specific optimization method for learning the metric.  
- Clarity issue: ambiguous reference at Line 152—specify which paper ([1], [7], or [8]) is meant.  
- Possible typo in Equation 11: alpha and beta should likely be denoted with stars to indicate optimal values.  
- Clarify that the algorithm section around Line 170 corresponds to the Sinkhorn scaling method mentioned earlier.
