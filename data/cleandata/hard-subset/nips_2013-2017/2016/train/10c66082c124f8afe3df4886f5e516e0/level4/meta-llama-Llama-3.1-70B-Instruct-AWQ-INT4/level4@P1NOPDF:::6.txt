The paper proposes a novel method for metric learning on textual documents, building upon the previous work known as "Word Mover's Distance". The original method is unsupervised, measuring the distance between word embeddings. This paper extends this approach to the supervised setting, where the supervision is of the type 'these documents belong to the same class'. The authors have promised to make the code (in MATLAB) available, which is appreciated.

I would like to thank the authors for their careful and clarifying responses to my questions. The authors propose two components to learn the distance: a linear transformation A (a traditional approach) and word importance weights w. I would have liked a clearer explanation of the intuition behind Eq (5), which defines the usage of w, and a better understanding of which component is responsible for the reported improvements. In the experimental section, the method is evaluated on a classification task. While I understand that evaluating a distance metric is not straightforward, it would be interesting to see how a simple classifier performs on the same datasets for comparison.

I have a few questions regarding the paper. Firstly, I did not understand when Eq 2 is used. The lambda in this equation appears to be different from the lambda in line 170. Could the authors clarify this discrepancy? Additionally, I would appreciate an explanation for the claim that the proposed method "is particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" (line 316).
