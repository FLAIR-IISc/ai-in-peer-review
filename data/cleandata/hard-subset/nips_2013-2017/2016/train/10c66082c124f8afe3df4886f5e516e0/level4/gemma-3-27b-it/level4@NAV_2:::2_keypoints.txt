- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a weighted metric and embedding transformation for WMD using supervision.
- Core objective: minimize leave-one-out kNN classification error to learn word importance and embedding transform.
- Key technical innovation: efficient gradient approximation for WMD via the Sinkhorn distance to avoid nested optimization.
- Empirically evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks, outperforming 26 competitive baselines consistently.
- Technical approach is sound, clearly explained, and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.
- Use of Sinkhorn distance is a clever and crucial practical contribution enabling training.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, with diverse datasets and a wide range of baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, mSDA, LSI, etc.).
- Results are consistently and significantly better than strong traditional and recent baselines.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and analyze factors like document length affecting performance.
- Minor weakness: connection to the broader, more recent metric learning literature could be strengthened.
- Minor weakness: sensitivity of the Sinkhorn parameter λ (chosen as 10) needs a more detailed ablation study.
- Minor weakness: computational trade-offs and memory requirements of the learned transformations should be discussed more explicitly.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, technically novel with extensive validation — recommend acceptance.
