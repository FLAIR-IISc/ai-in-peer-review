- Extends Word Mover’s Distance to supervised text classification by learning word-specific weights and using Sinkhorn distance for efficiency.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on eight datasets, outperforming 26 baselines on average.
- Paper is well-written with clear, consistent notation and a well-structured gradient derivation.
- Technically sound with a solid mathematical foundation and robust experimental validation.
- Efficient implementation leveraging the Sinkhorn (entropic) approximation makes the method practical.
- Contribution includes a novel supervised weighting scheme and an efficient gradient approximation.
- Thorough comparison against a large and diverse set of baselines.
- Primary weakness: novelty is incremental relative to the original WMD work.
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is tangential and sometimes confusing.
- Limited discussion and analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter lambda and its practical impact.
- Sensitivity to lambda noted; small lambda accelerates convergence but deviates from true Wasserstein behavior.
- Would benefit from clarifying whether lambda yields behavior closer to pure Wasserstein distance or retains entropic Sinkhorn characteristics.
