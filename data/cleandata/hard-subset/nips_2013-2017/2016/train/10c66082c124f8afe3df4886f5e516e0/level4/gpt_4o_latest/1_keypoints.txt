- Introduces supervised metric learning for word-mover’s distance, generalizable to transport distances using squared Euclidean after a linear projection.  
- Learns both a linear projection and document-specific word weights by re-weighting and re-normalizing word frequencies.  
- Proposes an efficient optimization procedure for training the parameterized metric.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance against many baselines on multiple document classification datasets using kNN with the learned metric.  
- Technically interesting and practically useful extension of WMD, and the paper is very well written and enjoyable to read.  
- Impact may be limited: earth-mover’s distance with metric learning via a word-centroid initialization achieves comparable performance to the fully learned method (Table 3).  
- Main technical contributions offer only marginal improvement over existing centroid-initialization techniques.  
- Missing evaluation: word-centroid embedding (weighted mean of word embeddings) is not tested with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) in Table 3.  
- Missing ablation: no analysis isolating the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Originality mostly resides in the optimization procedure (Lines 156–175); linear metric learning within the EMD framework is a modest standalone contribution.  
- Minor clarifications needed: specify which paper “The authors” refers to at Line 152; Equation 11 likely needs stars on α and β; explicitly remind readers that the reference around Line 170 is to Sinkhorn scaling mentioned earlier.
