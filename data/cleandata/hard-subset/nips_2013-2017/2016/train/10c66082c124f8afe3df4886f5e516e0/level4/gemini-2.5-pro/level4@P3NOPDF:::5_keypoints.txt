- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns word-specific weights for text classification.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance as a faster, differentiable approximation to the exact WMD.  
- Thorough evaluation on eight benchmark datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance.  
- Strength: exceptionally clear and well-written with rigorous, accessible mathematical presentation.  
- Strength: extensive and robust experimental setup providing strong empirical evidence.  
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is limited—an incremental supervised adaptation of existing WMD.  
- Weakness: motivation and treatment of the Word Centroid Distance (WCD) baseline are underdeveloped and confusing.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and analysis of the entropic regularization parameter (lambda) in Sinkhorn.  
- Request: provide a sensitivity analysis of model performance to different lambda values.  
- Request: clarify whether chosen lambda values yield Wasserstein-like behavior or are dominated by entropic (Sinkhorn) effects.  
- Request: describe how lambda was selected for experiments and include an ablation study on this parameter.
