This paper presents a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) that learns a task-specific distance metric. The proposed method is technically sound and well-explained, with the presentation providing just the right amount of detail. It is gratifying to see work that builds upon the WMD framework by applying discriminative training to learn a domain-specific metric.

The paper's primary strength lies in its extensive experimental evaluation. The authors test their method on several classification datasets against a wide variety of baseline methods and features. The results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms all baselines by a significant margin.

This is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution, and I would very much like to see it presented at NIPS.
