The paper proposes a novel method for metric learning on textual documents, building upon the existing "Word Mover's Distance" approach. The original Word Mover's Distance is an unsupervised method that measures the distance between word embeddings. This paper extends this concept to the supervised setting, where the supervision is based on document class labels. The authors have promised to make the MATLAB code available.

I appreciated the authors' careful responses to my questions, which provided clarification on several aspects of the paper. The proposed method learns two components to determine the distance: a linear transformation A (adopting the traditional approach) and word importance weights w. I would have liked a clearer explanation of the intuition behind Equation (5), which defines the usage of w, and a deeper understanding of which component is primarily responsible for the reported improvements.

In the experimental section, the method is evaluated on a classification task. While I understand that assessing a distance metric can be challenging, it would be interesting to compare the performance of a simple classifier on the same datasets for a more comprehensive evaluation.

I have a few questions regarding the paper. Firstly, I did not understand when Equation (2) is used, as the lambda value appears to be different from the lambda value in line 170. Secondly, could the authors provide more arguments to support the claim that their method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" (line 316)?

I also have some minor comments. On line 257, the text mentions "on the right we also show the average error across datasets," but I could not find this information; is it supposed to be in Table 3? In Table 2, it is unclear what the difference is between "n" and "ne." Additionally, the paper does not mention how the value of k (for kNN classification) is defined; is this dependent on the dataset or the method?
