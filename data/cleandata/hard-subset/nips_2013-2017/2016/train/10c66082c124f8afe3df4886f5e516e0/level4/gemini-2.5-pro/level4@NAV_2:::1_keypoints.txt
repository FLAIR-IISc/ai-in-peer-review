- Addresses WMD’s lack of supervision by learning a linear embedding transform and word-importance weights.
- Derives an efficient gradient-based optimization via an entropy-regularized Sinkhorn relaxation of WMD.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across eight datasets against 26 baselines using k-NN.
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly written.

- Much of the performance gain appears to come from the supervised WCD initialization (S-WMD init.), questioning the marginal benefit of the full S-WMD optimization.
- Missing direct comparisons of standard metric learning (e.g., LMNN, ITML) applied to word-centroid representations to better isolate benefits of transport-based learning.
- No ablation study separating the contributions of the linear transform A and the word-importance vector w.
- Conceptually related work exists applying linear metric learning to Earth Mover’s Distance in other domains, limiting novelty to the text application and optimization details.

- Clarify that the matrix-scaling algorithm used for the relaxed transport is the Sinkhorn scaling method.
- In the gradient w.r.t. w (Eq. 10), specify that the dual variables α and β should be the optimal ones (α*, β*).
- Specify the precise attribution for the result quoted on line 152 instead of citing multiple papers ambiguously.
