Review- The manuscript introduces a new metric learning approach for textual documents, extending prior work known as “Word Mover’s Distance.” The original method is unsupervised and measures distances between word embeddings. This paper moves to a supervised setting with supervision of the form “these documents are in the same class.” Matlab code is promised for release. [I appreciated the authors’ careful responses, which clarified my questions.] The authors propose learning two components for the distance: a linear transformation A (the standard approach) and word-importance weights w. I would have liked a clearer intuition for Eq. (5) (which defines how w is employed), and an understanding of which component contributes to the observed improvements. In the experiments, the method is evaluated via a classification task. I recognize that assessing a distance metric is challenging, but—for comparison—it would be informative to see how a simple classifier performs on the same datasets.
