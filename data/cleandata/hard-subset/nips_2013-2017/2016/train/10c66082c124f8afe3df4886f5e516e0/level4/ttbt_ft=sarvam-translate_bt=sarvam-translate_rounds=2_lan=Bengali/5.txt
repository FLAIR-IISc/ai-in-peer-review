The authors have analyzed the problem of classifying text into different categories. They have extended the previous work of Ward mover distance (Wassermann), which proposed to monitor this by learning word-specific weights. This contribution suggests using the Sinkhorn distance as an alternative to the Wassermann distance to accelerate it. It shows the best average performance on 8 datasets against 26 baselines. It is a very well-written paper that presents the subject well and deeply analyzes the mathematics behind the problem, but with very little clarity. The weaknesses are probably limited novelty compared to the previous Ward mover distance paper, and the references do not seem relevant, but rather misleading, in contrast to the Wassermann distance. I think the lambda (regularization factor) is poorly discussed. From my experience, the value of lambda has a huge impact, sometimes becoming the main reason for EQ (2). Theoretically, using a sufficiently large lambda should yield results similar to the pure Wassermann distance, but at the cost of slower convergence. However, most people use a very small lambda to improve the rate of convergence using the Sinkhorn, which creates a completely different behavior than Wassermann. So I am curious whether lambda determines a pure Wassermann behavior or a Sinkhorn (entropic) behavior in the case of this contribution.
