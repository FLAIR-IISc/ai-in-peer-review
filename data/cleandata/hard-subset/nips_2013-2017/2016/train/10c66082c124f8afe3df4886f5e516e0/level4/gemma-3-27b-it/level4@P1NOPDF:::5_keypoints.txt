- Introduces supervised refinement of word-specific weights building on Word Mover’s Distance for text classification.  
- Key contribution: uses Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient approximation to the Wasserstein distance.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: state-of-the-art on eight datasets versus 26 baselines.  
- Paper is very well-written, clear, and provides accessible, in-depth mathematical analysis.  
- Main weakness: novelty is incremental relative to the original WMD paper.  
- Inclusion of comparisons to World Centroid Distance is unhelpful and distracting.  
- Discussion of the regularization parameter lambda is insufficient.  
- Concern that lambda can dominate Eq. (2) and, when set small for faster convergence, yields behavior different from true Wasserstein.  
- Reviewer wants clarity on whether lambda in this work induces behavior closer to pure Wasserstein or to the entropic-regularized Sinkhorn.
