- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) to add supervision to WMD for document similarity/classification.
- Learns a linear transformation A for embeddings and a word-importance vector w jointly to improve task relevance.
- Optimizes a stochastic k-NN leave-one-out classification objective inspired by Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA).
- Replaces WMD with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to enable differentiability and efficient gradient-based learning.
- Uses a supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) initialization to aid convergence.
- Evaluated on eight text-classification datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art or highly competitive results.
- Strength: addresses a clear limitation of WMD and extends optimal-transport-based distances to supervised settings with strong empirical impact.
- Strength: technically sound and elegant—Sinkhorn approximation is the key practical insight; gradient derivations are clear.
- Strength: paper is very clear, well organized, and explains background and methods effectively.
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation and informative visualizations strengthen the empirical claims.
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is incremental—primarily a synthesis of WMD, NCA, and Sinkhorn ideas rather than a wholly new paradigm.
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ and its effect on performance, stability, and training dynamics.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical solution to an important problem, well-executed Sinkhorn-based learning, extensive strong results, and excellent presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: mainly an integration of existing techniques and incomplete hyperparameter (λ) analysis.
- Conclusion: overall a strong, practically useful contribution with minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
