- Proposes supervised metric learning that extends word-movers distance by learning a linear projection and word-specific weights (reweighted, renormalized frequencies).  
- Presents an efficient optimization method to learn the parameterized transport metric.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on multiple document classification datasets using kNN with the learned metric.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and compares against many reasonable baselines.  
- Main technical gains may be limited: earth-movers distance initialized from a learned "word centroid distance" performs similarly to full learning.  
- Missing ablation: no assessment of the relative importance of the word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A (should test each component in isolation).  
- Missing baseline evaluation: apply standard metric learning (ITML, LMNN, NCA) to the word-centroid embedding to compare fairly.  
- Originality mainly lies in the optimization approach; linear metric learning within earth-movers distance is a modest novelty.  
- Paper is very well written and easy to follow.  
- Minor clarifications needed: specify which paper is meant at Line 152, add stars to alpha and beta in Equation 11, and explicitly note that Line 170 refers to Sinkhorn scaling mentioned earlier.  
- Significance: effective approach for kNN text classification but impact may be constrained by comparable simpler initialization methods.  
- Quality: solid writing and well-executed experiments, but some evaluation gaps weaken claims about component contributions.
