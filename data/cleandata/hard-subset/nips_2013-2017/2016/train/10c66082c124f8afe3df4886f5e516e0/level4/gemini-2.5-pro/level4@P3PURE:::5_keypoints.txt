- Extends Word Mover’s Distance by learning supervised word-specific weights.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance as a computationally faster proxy for the exact Wasserstein distance.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance.  
- Exceptionally well-written with clear presentation and accessible mathematical analysis.  
- Primary contribution may have limited novelty since it’s an extension of the original WMD.  
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance is confusing and needs clearer motivation.  
- Lacks sufficient discussion of the regularization parameter lambda and the trade-off between entropic Sinkhorn behavior and approximation to true Wasserstein; clarify lambda’s effect in experiments.
