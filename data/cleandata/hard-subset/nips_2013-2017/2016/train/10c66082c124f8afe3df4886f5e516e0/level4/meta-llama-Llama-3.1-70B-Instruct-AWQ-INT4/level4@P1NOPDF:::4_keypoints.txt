- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear word embedding transform (A) and a per-document reweighting (w).  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and contains a thorough related-work review showing strong literature knowledge.  
- Provides experimental results on multiple novel datasets.  
- Motivation for reweighting w is weak: authors don’t explain why reweighting is beneficial or how it avoids changing a document’s meaning.  
- Reweighting could alter documents substantially (potentially creating a different document), which is not addressed.  
- Contribution’s significance is limited: using a linear transform A and reweighting w with an NCA objective is a straightforward extension rather than a novel formulation.  
- Unclear how to obtain or apply the reweighting w for completely new/unseen documents (generalization issue).  
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous (appears to mean label); authors should clarify whether it includes authorship, topic, or other notions.  
- Reviewer would not oppose acceptance provided the above concerns are adequately addressed.
