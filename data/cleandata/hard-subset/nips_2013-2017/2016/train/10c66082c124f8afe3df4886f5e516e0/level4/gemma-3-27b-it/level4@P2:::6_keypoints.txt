- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform and word-specific importance weights.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD computationally efficient.  
- Novelty: joint learning of embedding transformation (A) and word importance weights (w) within the WMD framework.  
- Strong empirical performance: outperforms 26 baselines across eight datasets.  
- Efficient and scalable alternative to many metric-learning methods for high-dimensional text.  
- Well-written paper with clear motivation, method description, and experimental setup.  
- Code is provided, improving reproducibility.  
- Weakness: insufficient intuition/explanation for the learned word importance weights and Eq. (5).  
- Weakness: unclear relative contribution of the linear transform (A) versus word weights (w).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of sensitivity to k in k-NN (no varying-k experiments reported).  
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous usage of parameter λ in different contexts and unsupported claim about WMD’s forgiveness of syntactic variation.  
- Formatting issue in original submission (table/text discrepancy) has been clarified by authors.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, effective, efficient, and reproducible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited insight into learned weights and missing ablation/sensitivity analyses.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, minor improvements suggested, recommend acceptance.
