- Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and a word importance vector w for document classification.
- Builds on metric learning and optimal transport literature and situates clearly relative to WMD and related methods.
- Technically sound and well-motivated: supervised WMD is a natural and valuable extension.
- Computationally mindful: proposes a clever gradient approximation to avoid WMD’s cubic complexity.
- Strong empirical evaluation: tested on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving lowest error on five datasets.
- Clear and well-organized presentation with good methodological detail.
- Unclear contribution breakdown: lacks an ablation study to quantify the separate effects of A versus w.
- Weak justification for the claim that S-WMD is “forgiving” of syntactically different but conceptually similar words; needs examples or deeper explanation.
- Experimental detail missing: value of k for kNN classification is not specified.
- Reporting inconsistency: references “average error across datasets” but Table 2 shows average rank, which should be clarified.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel supervised metric learning approach, strong empirical gains, attention to efficiency, solid theoretical grounding.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing ablation, insufficient support for a key qualitative claim, and omitted kNN hyperparameter specification.
