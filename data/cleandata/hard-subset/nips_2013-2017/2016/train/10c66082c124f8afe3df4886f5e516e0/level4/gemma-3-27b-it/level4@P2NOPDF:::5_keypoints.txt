- Extends Word Mover’s Distance by learning word-specific weights for supervised text classification.  
- Introduces the Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for the Wasserstein distance.  
- Rigorous evaluation on eight datasets showing superior average performance versus 26 baselines.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation and strong mathematical exposition.  
- Novelty is incremental relative to original WMD; proposed changes are extensions rather than a new direction.  
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance feels tangential and sometimes obscures core contributions; relevance is unclear.  
- Primary concern: insufficient treatment of the regularization parameter λ, which strongly influences behavior.  
- Unclear whether chosen λ values yield behavior close to true Wasserstein or are dominated by entropic Sinkhorn effects.  
- Recommends a sensitivity analysis or ablation study over λ values to clarify impact and strengthen the paper.
