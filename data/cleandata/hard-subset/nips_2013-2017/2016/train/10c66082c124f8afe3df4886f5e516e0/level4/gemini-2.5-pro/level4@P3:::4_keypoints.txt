- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a task-specific document distance by adapting WMD with supervision.  
- Learns a linear embedding transform A and a word importance vector w to adapt word representations and re-weight document bag-of-words.  
- Uses an NCA-style stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss to train A and w for classification.  
- Addresses intractable WMD gradients by using entropic regularization (Sinkhorn distance) for a differentiable, efficient approximation.  
- Employs a supervised Word Centroid Distance initialization and batch gradient descent for practical training.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets and outperforms 26 strong unsupervised and supervised baselines.  
- Strength: clear and significant advance by making a strong unsupervised baseline (WMD) supervised with state-of-the-art results.  
- Strength: technically sound—NCA formulation and Sinkhorn approximation are well-motivated and effective.  
- Strength: paper is exceptionally well-written and accessible with clear background and methods.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation across diverse baselines and datasets.  
- Weakness: motivation and interpretation of the learned word re-weighting w needs deeper discussion (risk of distorting document representation vs down-weighting stop-words).  
- Weakness: core components (linear transform A and NCA loss) are standard—novelty is mainly in adapting them to WMD and making it tractable.  
- Weakness: unclear how the learned w generalizes to unseen test documents—needs explicit clarification.  
- Weakness: minor terminology issue—“semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels” should be phrased as task-specific similarity.  
- Conclusion: high-quality, significant, and well-executed work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
