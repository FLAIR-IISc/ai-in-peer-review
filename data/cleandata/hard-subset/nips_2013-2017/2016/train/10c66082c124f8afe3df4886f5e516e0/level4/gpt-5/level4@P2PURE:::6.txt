The paper proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) for metric learning over textual documents. The original WMD is unsupervised and measures distances between documents via distances between word embeddings. This work brings in supervision of the form “these documents belong to the same class.” Matlab code is promised. [I appreciated the authors’ careful responses, which clarified several of my questions.]

The method learns two components: (1) a linear transformation A (as in traditional metric learning), and (2) word-importance weights w. I would like a clearer intuition for Eq. (5), which defines how w is used, and an analysis to determine which component—A or w—accounts for the reported improvements.

In the experiments, the learned distance is evaluated via a classification task. I understand that evaluating a distance metric directly is challenging, but—purely for context—it would be useful to see how a simple classifier performs on the same datasets.
