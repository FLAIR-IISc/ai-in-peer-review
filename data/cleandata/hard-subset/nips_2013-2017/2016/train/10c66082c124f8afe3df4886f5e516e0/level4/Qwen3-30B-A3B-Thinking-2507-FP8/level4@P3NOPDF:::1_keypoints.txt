- Extends WMD with supervised metric learning to jointly learn a linear projection and word-specific weights; evaluated on kNN text classification.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, exceptionally clear writing, efficient optimization, and strong empirical results against baseline metric learning and unsupervised WMD variants.  
- Main concern: full method shows marginal gains over a simple centroid-based initialization (WMD+centroid), questioning the benefit of the added complexity.  
- Missing comparison: metric learning applied to the centroid document representation (centroid + ITML/LMNN/NCA) is not evaluated.  
- Missing ablation: no experiments isolating contributions of word-specific weights (A = I) versus projection matrix (weights = 1).  
- Originality: idea is conceptually straightforward; novelty lies in optimization and weighting formulation—should be presented as a practical refinement rather than a major conceptual advance.  
- Minor issues: unclear citation at Line 152, Equation 11’s dual-variable notation likely should be starred, and explicitly reference Sinkhorn scaling around Line 170.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and useful but incremental due to small improvements over centroid baseline; needs additional comparisons to justify claims.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept / Borderline; addressing missing experiments would substantially strengthen the paper.
