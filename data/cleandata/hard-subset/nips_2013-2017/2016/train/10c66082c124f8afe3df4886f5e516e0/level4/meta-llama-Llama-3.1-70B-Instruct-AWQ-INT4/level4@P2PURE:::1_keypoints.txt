- Proposes a supervised metric learning approach extending word-movers distance, generalizable to any transport distance after a linear projection.  
- Learns both a linear projection and word-specific weights (reweighting and renormalizing word frequencies) for document applications.  
- Presents an efficient optimization method to learn the parameterized metric.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation shows the method is effective compared to many baselines on kNN text classification datasets.  
- Potential impact limited because earth-movers distance with metric learning via a word-centroid-distance initialization performs similarly to the full learned distance (Table 3).  
- Table 3 omits evaluation of the word-centroid embedding combined with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: no assessment of the relative importance of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A or performance of each component in isolation.  
- Originality is mainly in the optimization details; linear metric learning within earth-movers distance is interesting but a limited contribution.  
- Paper is very well written and pleasant to read.  
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous citation at Line 152 ("The authors" [1,7,8]); Equation 11 likely needs stars on alpha and beta; explicitly recall that the method at Line 170 is Sinkhorn scaling.
