This paper proposes a new method for learning the parameters of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), which is based on the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) between word embedding distributions. The authors parameterize the EMD ground metric using a Mahalanobis distance and additionally learn histogram reweighting coefficients. The paper derives the gradients for an NCA-style cost function and presents compelling experimental evidence for the proposed approach.

Overall, the paper presents a nice combination of existing techniques to solve an important problem. While the core idea is relatively straightforward, its implementation and validation are non-trivial, and the convincing experimental results are a strong component of the work. I support its acceptance.

**Strengths:**
*   The paper addresses a clear goal: learning a better metric for the WMD framework. The proposed solution is simple, well-motivated, and effective.
*   The experimental section is a significant strength, providing strong and convincing evidence for the method's utility.

**Suggestions for Improvement:**
*   The interplay between the regularized and non-regularized EMD formulations should be handled more clearly throughout the paper.
*   The mathematical presentation could be improved. Some notations and computations appear inconsistent or could be presented with greater rigor.
*   The clarity of the proposed method would be significantly enhanced by the inclusion of an algorithm box summarizing the training procedure.

**Detailed Comments:**
1.  **Inconsistent Distance Metrics:** There is an inconsistency in the definition of the cost function. Equation 1 uses the Euclidean distance between word embeddings (implying 1-Wasserstein distance), while Equation 6, for Mahalanobis metric learning, uses the squared Euclidean distance (implying 2-Wasserstein distance). To avoid this confusion, it would be simpler to frame WMD more generally as a p-Wasserstein distance between clouds of word embeddings for an arbitrary p.

2.  **Novelty of Reweighting:** The histogram reweighting approach proposed in Equation 5 is not new. It was previously introduced by Guy Lebanon in "Metric learning for text documents" for the same application. A similar idea was also explored in "Unsupervised Riemannian Metric Learning for Histograms Using Aitchison Transformations" by Le and Cuturi. The authors should acknowledge this prior work.

3.  **Impact of the Weight Vector:** The paper introduces a learned weight vector `w`, but its empirical contribution is not analyzed. Could the authors provide experimental insights or an ablation study on the importance of this component? Clarifying whether this innovation significantly impacts performance would be valuable.

4.  **Strengthening the Related Work Section:** The discussion of related work could be expanded to more clearly delineate the contributions of this paper from previous methods.
    *   **Comparison to Cuturi & Avis:** This paper clearly builds on their work on learning EMD. Two key differences should be highlighted: (1) the use of entropy regularization, which significantly improves the scalability of the EMD computation, and (2) the parameterization of the cost with a continuous linear map, whereas the cost matrix in Cuturi & Avis is discrete.
    *   **Comparison to Wang et al.:** The paper also draws from Wang et al. However, the discussion should note the critical limitations of that work, such as performing the OT optimization only a single time, which is an insufficient approach that this paper improves upon.
