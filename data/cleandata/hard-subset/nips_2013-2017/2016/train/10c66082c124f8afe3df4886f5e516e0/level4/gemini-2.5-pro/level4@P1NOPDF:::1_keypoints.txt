- Proposes a supervised metric-learning extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear projection and word-specific weights, with an efficient optimization method.
- Method generalizes to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean metric under a linear projection.
- Word-specific weights re-weight and re-normalize document word distributions for document applications.
- Thorough experimental evaluation on several document classification datasets shows effectiveness using k-NN and many strong baselines.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
- Main weakness: full method performs very similarly to a simpler baseline (EMD initialized with a centroid-learned metric), suggesting only marginal gains from joint learning.
- Missing comparison: combine the word-centroid embedding with other metric-learning baselines (ITML, LMNN, NCA) to isolate WMD’s benefits.
- Missing ablation study to separate the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.
- Originality: novelty mainly in the optimization procedure; applying linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but relatively incremental.
- Minor issues: ambiguous phrasing about "The authors" (Line 152), Equation 11 should use α*, β* for optimal dual variables, and explicitly note that Line 170 describes the Sinkhorn scaling algorithm.
