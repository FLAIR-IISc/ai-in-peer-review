- Proposes supervised metric learning for transport distances (word-movers) by learning a linear projection and word-specific weights applied to word2vec embeddings and frequencies.
- Method applicable to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances after linear projection.
- Introduces efficient optimization to learn the parameterized metric.
- Thorough experimental evaluation on several document classification datasets using kNN, comparing to many baselines.
- Empirical effectiveness shown, but impact limited because earth-mover distance with "word centroid distance" initialization performs similarly to full learned metric (Table 3).
- Main technical contributions may not add much beyond the centroid-based initialization from existing techniques.
- Missing evaluation: apply "word centroid embedding" (weighted mean of word embeddings) with all tested metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- Missing ablation: assess importance of word-specific weights versus projection matrix A by testing each component in isolation.
- Originality primarily in the optimization procedure (Lines 156–175); linear metric learning within earth-movers is interesting but a limited standalone contribution.
- Paper is well written and readable.
- Minor clarifications needed: specify which papers are referenced at Line 152 ([1,7,8]); denote alpha and beta with stars in Equation 11; explicitly note that the scaling around Line 170 is the Sinkhorn scaling mentioned at Line 56.
