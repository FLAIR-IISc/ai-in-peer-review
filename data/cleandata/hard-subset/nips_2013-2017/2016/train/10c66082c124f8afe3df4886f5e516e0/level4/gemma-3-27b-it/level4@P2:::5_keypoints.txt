- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD): learns word-specific weights to extend Word Mover’s Distance to supervised text classification using Sinkhorn distance and an efficient update rule, evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines.  
- Exceptionally well-written with clear presentation and thorough mathematical exposition.  
- Strong empirical results: S-WMD consistently outperforms many strong baselines.  
- Practical contribution: uses Sinkhorn approximation for computationally efficient supervised metric learning with word embeddings.  
- Solid theoretical foundation and good integration with prior WMD and metric learning literature.  
- Novelty is incremental, building directly on existing WMD work.  
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is poorly motivated and confusing.  
- Discussion of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ is too brief; its impact (Wasserstein vs entropic behavior and convergence trade-offs) is not sufficiently explored.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, but address λ analysis and clarify the WCD comparison to strengthen the paper.
