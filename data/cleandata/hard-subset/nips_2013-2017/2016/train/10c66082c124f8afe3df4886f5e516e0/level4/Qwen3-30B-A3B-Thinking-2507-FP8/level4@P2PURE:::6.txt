

The paper introduces a novel supervised metric learning approach for textual documents, extending the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance (WMD) framework. While the original WMD measures distances between document embeddings using word embeddings, this work adapts it to a supervised setting where supervision indicates "documents belong to the same class." The authors promise to release MATLAB code. I appreciate the authors' careful responses addressing my queries. The method proposes learning two components: a linear transformation matrix **A** (the conventional approach) and word importance weights **w**. I seek further clarification on the intuition behind Equation (5), which defines the role of **w**, and on which component (A or **w**) primarily drives the reported performance improvements. Regarding experiments, the method is evaluated on classification tasks. While evaluating distance metrics presents challenges, it would be informative to include a baseline comparison using a simple classifier on the same datasets (these points have been addressed in the authors' response). Specific concerns:  
- Line 2: Clarify the usage of λ in Equation (2) versus λ in line 170.  
- Line 316: Provide justification for the claim that the method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words."  
- Line 257: The phrase "on the right we also show the average error across datasets" appears unverified; confirm if this refers to Table 3.  
- Table 2: Explain the distinction between entries labeled "n" and "ne."  
- Clarify whether the k-value for kNN classification (used in experiments) is dataset-dependent or method-dependent.
