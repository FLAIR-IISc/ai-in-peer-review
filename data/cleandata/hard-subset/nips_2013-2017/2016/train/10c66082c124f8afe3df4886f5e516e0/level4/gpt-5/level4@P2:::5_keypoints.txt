- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform and per-word importance weights using a leave-one-out kNN/NCA-style loss.
- Uses entropic regularization (Sinkhorn) to obtain differentiable, efficient approximations of WMD for gradient-based learning.
- Accelerates training with WCD-based neighbor filtering, minibatching, and initialization via NCA on word centroids (S-WCD).
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving best average kNN performance and often lowest per-dataset error.
- Provides interpretability via learned word-importance weights and class-cluster visualizations; code is released.
- Builds on WMD, Sinkhorn/entropic OT, and supervised metric learning; novelty is incremental but practically useful for text.
- Clear, well-organized exposition with sound mathematical derivations and practical training details.
- Entropic OT optimization is well-justified: unique solutions and efficient approximate gradients.
- Strong empirical study with sensible ablations and initialization analyses.
- Moderate novelty since it composes known components (WMD + Sinkhorn + NCA-style objective).
- Key missing analysis: sensitivity to the entropic regularization parameter λ and its effect on distance behavior and runtime.
- Confusing presentation of WCD’s role; needs clearer separation between initialization/filtering and final metric use.
- Limited ablation separating the contributions of learning A versus w, and lacking comparison of ground cost norms (p=1 vs p=2).
- Some datasets favor TF-IDF+NCA, suggesting S-WMD may underperform when IDF weighting or long documents dominate; more analysis needed.
- Questions/suggestions: provide λ sensitivity plots, clarify λ tuning, report A vs w ablations, clarify WCD usage, analyze TF-IDF+NCA failure modes, and ensure fair hyperparameter tuning across baselines.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical performance, solid theoretical grounding, interpretability, and practical impact with released code.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited methodological novelty, insufficient λ analysis, and ambiguity around WCD and failure conditions.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on addressing λ sensitivity and clarifying WCD role.
