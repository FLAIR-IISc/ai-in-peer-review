- Proposes learning WMD parameters via a Mahalanobis ground metric and Lebanon-style histogram reweighting, with gradients for NCA-type objectives and strong experimental validation.  
- Core idea is simple but non-trivial to implement; thorough experiments are a primary strength.  
- Major concern: inconsistent mathematical treatment—Eq. (1) uses Euclidean distance while Eq. (6) uses squared Euclidean—authors should uniformly define WMD as the p-Wasserstein distance.  
- Major concern: algorithmic clarity is lacking; include explicit pseudocode for the optimization procedure.  
- Major concern: novelty/related work issues—Equation (5)’s reweighting was proposed by Lebanon (2005) and must be acknowledged; clarify distinctions from Cuturi & Avis (2013) (discrete costs) and from the unpublished Wang et al. (2015).  
- Minor: unclear empirical impact of the learned weight vector w—report ablation to show whether w significantly improves performance.  
- Minor: related work should explicitly contrast continuous parameterized cost here with Cuturi/Avis’s discrete cost and explain how entropy regularization enables scalability.  
- Overall: well-motivated and empirically strong paper that merits acceptance pending improvements in mathematical precision, algorithmic transparency, and clearer delineation of novelty.
