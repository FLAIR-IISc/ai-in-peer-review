This paper proposes a supervised method to learn the distance between documents based on the Word Mover's Distance (WMD). The WMD is a document metric that formulates the distance between two documents as an optimal transport problem between the embedded words. The authors extend this idea by learning a linear transformation of the embedded words and a reweighted word distribution to separate documents of different labels.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a thorough review of related works and a clear understanding of the literature. The authors provide experimental results for their algorithm on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness.

However, the motivation behind the formulation needs further explanation, and the significance of the contribution is low. Specifically, the use of reweighting the word distribution using w is not well-motivated and seems to change the document into a completely different one. The authors should provide a clear justification for this reweighting and explain how it helps without altering the document's meaning.

Moreover, the contribution of this work is not groundbreaking, as adopting a linear transformation A and reweighting w with the NCA objective function is a straightforward extension of WMD to the supervised setting. The authors should highlight what makes their approach unique and significant compared to other metric learning methods.

Additionally, the authors should clarify how their algorithm can find a distance for a new document. Specifically, how can one determine the reweighting w for a completely new document? This is crucial for understanding the practical applicability of the proposed method.

Finally, the authors should provide a clear definition of "semantic difference" in the abstract, as it seems to refer to the label of the document. Does this term imply something else, such as authorship or topic difference? Clarifying this will help readers understand the scope and implications of the proposed method.

In conclusion, while the paper is well-written, it requires further explanation and justification to strengthen its contribution and significance. Addressing the above questions and concerns will enhance the paper's clarity and impact.
