

The paper introduces a novel supervised metric learning approach for textual documents, extending the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance framework. While the original method measures word embedding distances without class information, this work incorporates supervision of the form "documents belong to the same class," with MATLAB code to be released. The authors propose two learning components: a linear transformation matrix **A** (standard in such settings) and word importance weights **w**. I request deeper clarification on the intuition behind Equation (5) governing **w**'s usage, particularly identifying which component (A or **w**) primarily drives the reported performance gains. Regarding experiments, while evaluating distance metrics presents inherent challenges, it would strengthen the work to include a baseline classifier performance on the same datasets for direct comparison.  

Key questions:  
- Equation (2) references a λ parameter; how does this differ from λ in line 170?  
- What evidence supports the claim that the method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" (line 316)?  
- Line 257 states "the average error across datasets" is shown on the right; this appears absent from Table 3.  
- In Table 2, what distinguishes "n" from "ne"?  
- How is k (for kNN classification) determined—is it dataset-dependent or method-dependent?
