- Introduces supervised metric learning extension of WMD that learns a linear projection and word-specific weights applicable to squared-Euclidean transport metrics.  
- Word-weighting component rescales document word frequencies via learned weights and renormalization for classification.  
- Proposes an efficient optimization procedure for jointly learning the projection and weights.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation using kNN across multiple datasets and many baselines (ITML, LMNN, NCA, WMD variants).  
- Paper is very well-written, technically clear, and the core idea is well-motivated.  
- Major concern: Table 3 shows "WMD + centroid initialization" performs nearly identically to the full method, implying minimal marginal benefit from learned projection and weights.  
- This similarity undermines the claimed impact—metric learning within WMD may be incremental if centroid initialization suffices.  
- Missing experiment 1: baseline of standard word centroid embedding combined with metric learners (ITML, LMNN, NCA) to assess necessity of the proposed learning.  
- Missing experiment 2: ablation study separating projection vs. word weights (weights-only, projection-only, both) to identify which component drives gains.  
- Minor issues: ambiguous reference "The authors" (specify citations), Equation 11 should denote optimized α*, β*, and explicitly link Sinkhorn scaling mention to the introduction.  
- Overall assessment: coherent and well-executed work but empirical evidence is insufficient to justify complexity; recommendation weak accept / borderline reject pending additional experiments.
