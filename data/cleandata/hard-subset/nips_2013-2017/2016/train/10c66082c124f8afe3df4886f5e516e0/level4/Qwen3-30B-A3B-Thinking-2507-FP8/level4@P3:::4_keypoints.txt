- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights to incorporate supervision into WMD for classification.  
- Uses stochastic leave-one-out nearest-neighbor loss and a relaxed optimal transport (Sinkhorn) to make training tractable.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over 26 baselines across eight text classification datasets, achieving best average performance among 27 methods.  
- Builds on Kusner et al.'s WMD and situates the work within metric learning and document representation literature.  
- Technically sound: clear theoretical justification for the Sinkhorn relaxation and a well-justified supervised formulation.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive and rigorous, including ablation studies and analysis of learned word weights.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized; mathematical formulations and algorithmic descriptions are precise and accessible.  
- Visualization (t-SNE) effectively shows improved document embedding separation with S-WMD.  
- Originality is modest: learning a linear transform and reweighting echoes established metric-learning ideas (NCA, LMNN, ITML).  
- Distinction from prior EMD-based metric learning is the histogram reweighting and specific integration with WMD, but novelty could be more strongly argued.  
- Significance is supported by empirical gains, but the paper should better explain why S-WMD is preferable to applying standard metric learning to precomputed document representations.  
- Motivation and interpretation of the word importance weights need clearer examples showing how reweighting improves representations without distorting semantics.  
- Generalization concerns: unclear handling of out-of-vocabulary words and whether weights are global or per-document; requires clarification for inference on new documents.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong empirical results, clear writing, and practical contribution; minor revisions suggested for clarifying novelty, word-weight motivation, and OOV handling.
