- Proposes using Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy for supervised Word Mover’s (Wasserstein) distance in text classification.  
- Empirical evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines shows best average performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition and thorough mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty is limited compared to the original Word Mover’s Distance work.  
- References to the World Centroid Distance are confusing and largely irrelevant.  
- Discussion of the entropic regularizer lambda is insufficient and potentially critical.  
- Lambda can dominate equation (2), affect convergence speed, and change behavior between pure Wasserstein and entropic Sinkhorn.  
- Most practitioners use small lambda for faster convergence, producing behavior different from Wasserstein—authors should clarify which regime their method operates in.  
- Strengths: clear writing, strong empirical results, and speed improvements from Sinkhorn.  
- Weaknesses: limited novelty, confusing/irrelevant references, and poor analysis of lambda’s impact.  
- Recommendation: accept, but require the authors to analyze and report the effects of different lambda values and compare Sinkhorn behavior to pure Wasserstein.
