- Contribution: Proposes S-WMD—learning an affine transform of word embeddings and word importance weights to minimize leave-one-out kNN error using Sinkhorn-approximated WMD.  
- Computational method: Uses Cuturi’s Sinkhorn distance to avoid cubic complexity of exact WMD and derives Sinkhorn-based gradient approximations.  
- Empirical evaluation: Tested on eight text classification tasks against 26 baselines, consistently outperforming competitors and showing strong gains on short-document tasks.  
- Quality: Technical approach is sound with rigorous derivations, reproducible details, and thorough experiments.  
- Theoretical concern: Insufficient justification of λ choice—unclear whether results reflect true Wasserstein behavior (λ→∞) or an entropically regularized approximation.  
- Clarity: Paper is clear and well-structured with precise equations and effective figures; motivation for word weighting and gradient computation are well explained.  
- Baseline critique: Inclusion of WCD is questionable without clarifying that it is a lower-bound approximation of WMD and not a metric-learning baseline.  
- Originality: Methodological novelty is limited—builds on Kusner et al. (WMD) and Cuturi (Sinkhorn); needs clearer differentiation from related EMD metric-learning work (e.g., Wang et al.).  
- Significance: Results are practically significant, with notable accuracy gains and demonstrated efficiency, making it useful for real-world classification.  
- Acceptance pros: Strong empirical validation, clear exposition, efficient implementation, and practical improvements over unsupervised baselines.  
- Acceptance cons: Limited methodological novelty, ambiguous theoretical treatment of λ, and weak justification for the WCD baseline.  
- Overall: High-quality, well-executed paper with practical impact; minor revisions addressing λ theory and baseline justification would suffice.
