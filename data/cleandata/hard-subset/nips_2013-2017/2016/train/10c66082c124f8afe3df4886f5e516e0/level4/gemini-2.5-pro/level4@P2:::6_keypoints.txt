- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-importance weights for a document distance metric.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain an efficient, differentiable approximation of WMD for gradient-based learning.  
- Trains the model by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out k-NN classification loss inspired by Neighborhood Components Analysis.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing state-of-the-art or highly competitive performance.  
- Novel contribution: extends WMD to incorporate supervision by jointly learning embedding geometry and word weights.  
- Technical strength: elegant use of Sinkhorn approximation to make WMD gradients tractable and efficient.  
- Empirical strength: comprehensive experiments with many baselines and datasets lend credibility to results.  
- Performance strength: achieves lowest k-NN test error on five of eight datasets and best average rank overall.  
- Missing ablation: no study isolating the contributions of the linear transform A versus the word weights w.  
- Limited comparisons: evaluation only uses k-NN; lacks comparisons to strong non-metric classifiers (e.g., logistic regression, SVM, CNN/LSTM).  
- Quality: technically sound with well-supported claims and rigorous experiments.  
- Clarity: generally well-written, though some experimental details need clarification.  
- Originality: highly original combination of WMD, NCA, and Sinkhorn for supervised document metric learning.  
- Significance: meaningful advance likely to be impactful given WMD’s importance and the strong empirical gains.  
- Ask for clarification: relationship and interpretation of λ between the entropy regularizer and dual variable updates.  
- Ask for clarification: explicitly state why S-WMD is forgiving for conceptually similar but syntactically different words (link to embeddings).  
- Ask for clarification: specify the value/tuning procedure for k in k-NN experiments.  
- Minor fixes requested: define `n` vs `ne` in Table 1 and correct the “average error” vs “Avg. Rank” label discrepancy in Table 2.
