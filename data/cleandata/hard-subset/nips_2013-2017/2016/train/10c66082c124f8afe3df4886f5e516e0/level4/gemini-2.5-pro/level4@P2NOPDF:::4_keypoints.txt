- Introduces a supervised extension to Word Mover's Distance by learning a linear transform A and a global word-reweighting vector w to improve document dissimilarity measures.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough, knowledgeable review of related work.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and covers multiple datasets.  
- Motivation for the global word reweighting w is insufficiently explained and may distort document semantics; needs justification or analysis showing it preserves core meaning.  
- Technical contribution seems incremental—learning A and w with a standard NCA-like objective is a straightforward extension rather than a novel method.  
- Unclear how distances are computed for unseen test documents and how the learned w is applied at inference time; clarification on generalization is needed.  
- The term "semantic difference" is ambiguous given the tasks (topic classification, authorship attribution) and should be clarified.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution likely acceptable if authors satisfactorily address the above concerns in rebuttal or revision.
