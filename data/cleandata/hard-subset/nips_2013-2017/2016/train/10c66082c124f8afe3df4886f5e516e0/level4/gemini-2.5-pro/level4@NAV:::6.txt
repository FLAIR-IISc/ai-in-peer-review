This paper presents a novel approach for supervised metric learning for textual documents, extending the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance framework. The method adapts the original distance measure, which operates on word embeddings, to a supervised context using class-label information to guide the learning process. The authors state that the corresponding Matlab code will be made publicly available.

The proposed learning objective incorporates two components: a linear transformation `A` of the word embeddings, which is a standard technique in metric learning, and a set of word importance weights `w`. The paper could be strengthened by providing a more detailed intuition for Equation (5), which governs the application of the word weights. Furthermore, an ablation study to disentangle the individual contributions of the transformation `A` and the weights `w` would offer valuable insight into which component is primarily responsible for the method's performance gains.

The experimental evaluation is based on a k-Nearest Neighbors classification task. The following points require clarification:

*   Could the authors elaborate on the specific role of Equation 2 within the overall method? The hyperparameter lambda in this equation appears to be distinct from the lambda introduced on line 170; a clarification would be helpful.
*   The paper asserts on line 316 that the method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words." Could the authors provide further argumentation or evidence to substantiate this claim?
*   On line 257, the text mentions showing "the average error across datasets." This information could not be located; please clarify if this is intended to be in Table 3 or elsewhere.
*   In Table 2, the distinction between the columns labeled "n" and "ne" is not explained.
*   The procedure for selecting the value of k for the kNN classifier is not specified. Please detail how k was determined and whether it varied across datasets or methods.
