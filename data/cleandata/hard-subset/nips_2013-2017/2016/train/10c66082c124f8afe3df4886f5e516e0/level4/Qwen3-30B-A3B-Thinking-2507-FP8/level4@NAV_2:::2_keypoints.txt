- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a discriminative document metric using label information.  
- Jointly learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights to optimize leave-one-out classification error.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance relaxation to approximate WMD gradients efficiently, avoiding cubic complexity of exact WMD.  
- Derivation of the relaxed gradient (Eq. 8–10) is clear and technically sound.  
- Achieves O(p²) computational complexity and scalable training.  
- Evaluated on 8 text classification datasets against 26 competitive baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, mSDA, NCA, LMNN, ITML, etc.).  
- S-WMD attains the lowest test error on 5/8 datasets and outperforms all baselines on average, with large gains on short documents (e.g., 42% relative improvement on CLASSIC).  
- Experimental design includes rigorous ablation, visualizations (t-SNE, word weights), 5-fold splits, and scalability analysis (Table 3).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear background, formulation, pseudo-code, and accessible results.  
- Novel contribution: supervised metric learning directly in the word embedding space and simultaneous histogram reweighting—distinct from prior document- or metric-level methods.  
- Practical significance: sets new state-of-the-art for kNN classification, maintains competitive training time, and offers interpretable word weights.  
- Minor gaps: limited analysis of failure cases (e.g., BBCSPORT) and little sensitivity analysis of the Sinkhorn parameter λ.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant, reproducible work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
