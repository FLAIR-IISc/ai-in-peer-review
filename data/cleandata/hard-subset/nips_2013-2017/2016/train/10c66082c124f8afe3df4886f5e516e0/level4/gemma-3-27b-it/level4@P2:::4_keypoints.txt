- Introduces a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) by learning a linear transformation of word embeddings and a re-weighting of word distributions.  
- Builds on WMD’s optimal transport framework and connects to Earth Mover’s Distance and metric learning.  
- Method uses a linear embedding transform and an NCA-style objective to improve class separation.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a comprehensive literature review demonstrating strong understanding of related work.  
- Empirically evaluated on eight datasets with substantial experimental analysis.  
- Motivation for the re-weighting vector w is under-explained and needs stronger justification.  
- Re-weighting may alter document semantics substantially; authors should show it preserves semantic meaning while emphasizing important words.  
- The contribution feels incremental; the paper needs a stronger argument for novelty and significance of this formulation.  
- Practical details are missing on how to determine/apply w for entirely new (test-time) documents.  
- The term “semantic difference” is vague and should be clarified (e.g., label-based, authorship, topic).  
- Overall solid work and acceptable pending satisfactory revisions addressing motivation, significance, and implementation concerns.
