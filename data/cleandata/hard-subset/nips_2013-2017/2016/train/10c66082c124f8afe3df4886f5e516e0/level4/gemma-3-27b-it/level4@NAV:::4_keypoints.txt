- Proposes a supervised refinement of WMD by learning a linear embedding transform (A) and a reweighted word distribution (w).
- Paper is well-written, comprehensible, cites prior work thoroughly, and provides extensive experiments on multiple novel datasets.
- Motivation for the proposed reweighting w is unclear; it may substantially alter document representations and needs justification that it improves separation without misrepresenting documents.
- Framing the method as applying a Nearest Centroid Algorithm (NCA) objective to learn A and w seems incremental and not a major novel contribution.
- Practical issue: the paper does not explain how to compute the reweighting w for entirely new, unseen documents.
- Terminology concern: the phrase "semantic difference" in the abstract is ambiguous and may conflate authorship/topical variation with semantic distinctions; clarification is needed.
- Reviewer is open to acceptance conditional on satisfactory responses to these concerns and a stronger statement of the work’s significance.
