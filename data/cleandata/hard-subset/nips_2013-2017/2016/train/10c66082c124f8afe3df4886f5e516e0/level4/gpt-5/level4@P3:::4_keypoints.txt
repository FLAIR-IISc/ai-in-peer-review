- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD learning a linear ground-metric A and a nonnegative word-importance vector w for reweighted histograms.  
- Training: Parameters optimized via a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss with Sinkhorn entropic OT for efficient gradient computation.  
- Initialization & training details: Initialized by NCA on word-centroid features (S-WCD) and trained with minibatches and nearest-neighbor filtering.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD (Kusner et al.), entropic OT/Sinkhorn (Cuturi), and supervised metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML); related to supervised EMD ground-metric learning in vision.  
- Strength — formulation: Clear problem statement on incorporating supervision into WMD without high-dimensional document features.  
- Strength — objective: NCA stochastic LOO loss aligns well with kNN evaluation protocol.  
- Strength — efficiency: Sinkhorn-based approximation yields unique plans/dual potentials enabling practical gradient computation and training speedups.  
- Strength — empirical breadth: Evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines with often best or competitive kNN accuracy and interpretable word weights.  
- Strength — reproducibility: Code available and complexity analysis reasonable; training appears fast with approximation and batching.  
- Weakness — motivation for w: Insufficient justification and need for constraints/regularization on w to prevent degenerate solutions or extreme reweighting.  
- Weakness — missing ablation: Need A-only vs w-only vs both to isolate the contribution of the word-importance vector.  
- Weakness — generalization clarity: Must explicitly state A and w are global and detail test-time complexity and memory usage.  
- Weakness — novelty: Components are known; novelty is moderate — combine elements for text and provide clearer positioning relative to supervised EMD/ground-metric learning.  
- Weakness — terminology: “Semantic difference” conflates task labels with inherent semantics and should be rephrased.  
- Weakness — evaluation scope: Experiments limited to kNN; suggest additional evaluations (retrieval, clustering, kernels) to show broader utility.  
- Weakness — missing sensitivity analyses: Report sensitivity to entropy λ, batch size, neighbor-filter size N, k in kNN, runtime vs accuracy, and Sinkhorn vs exact WMD comparisons.  
- Weakness — regularization/optimization details: Need explicit regularization on A (e.g., Frobenius, low-rank), learning rates, early stopping, and initialization sensitivity; discuss squared vs Euclidean cost choice.  
- Weakness — clarity/notation: Typos, inconsistent section numbering, reference issues, and unclear expressions (dimensionality) need fixing.  
- Quality — technical soundness: Formulation and Sinkhorn approximation are sound; gradient derivations plausible but dual-variable derivation for w needs tightening.  
- Quality — clarity & originality: Generally well written; originality moderate as a careful, practical integration with added w.  
- Significance: Empirically useful and likely adopted by practitioners, but conceptually incremental.  
- Specific clarifications requested: Justify w as supervised term weighting (akin to IDF), emphasize efficient gradient design, confirm global w usage with formula for test-time reweighting, and clarify “semantic difference” meaning.  
- Pros for acceptance: Practical, efficient supervised WMD extension with strong empirical results and available code.  
- Cons for acceptance: Incremental novelty, missing key ablations/sensitivity analyses, and clarity/notation shortcomings.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept if authors clarify/regularize w, fix notation/references, and add key ablations and sensitivity analyses.
