This paper introduces a supervised metric learning method built atop the recent “word-movers distance” [19], a transport metric applied to word2vec embeddings. The approach extends to any transport distance whose ground cost is a squared Euclidean distance after a linear projection. Beyond learning the linear projection to maximise performance, it also learns word-specific weights: each word’s frequency is reweighted by a learned coefficient and the resulting distribution is renormalised. The paper proposes an efficient optimisation scheme to fit the parameterised metric. A comprehensive empirical study demonstrates the method’s effectiveness by comparing against many reasonable baselines across multiple document classification datasets, using a kNN classifier defined by the learned metric.

+ This work extends the recent word-movers distance; the extension is technically interesting and practically useful (with demonstrated effectiveness on several kNN text classification tasks).
- The potential impact may be limited because earth-movers distance with metric learning initialised via a “word centroid distance” performs similarly to the fully learned metric. See the small gap between the last two rows in Table 3. In other words, the main technical contributions do not appear to add much beyond this initialisation based on existing techniques.
- Table 3 is missing an evaluation of the “word centroid embedding” (the weighted mean of word embeddings for a document) paired with all tested existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- The evaluation also lacks an analysis of the relative contributions of the word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A. It would be useful to report performance with each component enabled in isolation.
o The originality primarily resides in the metric optimisation procedure (Lines 156–175); the idea of linear metric learning within earth-movers distance is compelling but, by itself, a modest contribution.
+ The paper is very well written and a pleasure to read. Minor points that could be clarified:
