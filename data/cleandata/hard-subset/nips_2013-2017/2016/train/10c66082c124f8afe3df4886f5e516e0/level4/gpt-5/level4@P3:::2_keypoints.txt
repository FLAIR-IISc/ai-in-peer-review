- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised variant of WMD that learns task-specific document distances for kNN by learning a linear transform A and nonnegative per-word importance vector w.
- Uses an NCA-style objective minimizing stochastic leave-one-out kNN error under the learned distance.
- Approximates WMD with entropic-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) and derives practical gradients for A and w from relaxed primal and dual solutions.
- Accelerates training via initialization from NCA on word centroids (S-WCD), minibatch optimization, and fast WCD neighbor pruning.
- Well connected to prior metric-learning and entropic OT literature; differs by targeting text and jointly learning ground metric and histogram reweighting.
- Evaluated on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance; code released.
- Formulation is technically sound; Sinkhorn approximation is appropriate to avoid nested LPs and enables efficient gradients.
- Experiments are broad and competitive with reasonable reported training times and implementation details.
- Caveat: reliance on Sinkhorn introduces bias controlled by λ; lacking sensitivity analysis of λ’s effect on accuracy and convergence.
- Caveat: gradients for w depend on Sinkhorn duals; constraints/regularization on w (nonnegativity, sparsity, smoothing) and sensitivity are under-specified.
- Caveat: evaluation focused on kNN; missing experiments on retrieval or other nonparametric classifiers and comparisons to strong linear supervised baselines.
- Notational issues: overloaded symbols (e.g., p) and minor typos that need fixing.
- Clarity suggestions: disambiguate notation, define symbols at first use, state w constraints and regularization, and discuss initialization and convergence behavior.
- Originality: novel combination of learning linear ground metric, per-word weights, and efficient NCA-style Sinkhorn training applied to text.
- Significance: addresses WMD’s limitation by incorporating labels; practical impact for distance-based text methods and supervised OT in NLP.
- Pros: principled, simple method; efficient training with practical accelerations; strong empirical results; and public code.
- Cons/questions: missing ablations disentangling A vs w, limited sensitivity analyses (λ, batch size, neighbor size), and lack of comparisons to competitive supervised text classifiers.
- Suggestion: add ablations for A-only, w-only, and both; include sensitivity/convergence plots and compare to linear SVM/logistic on TF-IDF and simple supervised embedding baselines.
- Suggestion: test robustness across different pre-trained embeddings and vocabulary sizes; consider L1/L2 or entropy regularization on w for interpretability and generalization.
- Suggestion: clarify and enforce constraints on w, tighten notation, and fix editorial issues.
- Overall recommendation: solid, well-executed contribution with strong empirical gains and practicality; recommend acceptance pending minor clarifications and additional analyses.
