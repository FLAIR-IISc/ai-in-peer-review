- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform A and a word-importance vector w using a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss.  
- Differentiates through WMD by using Sinkhorn entropy-regularization to obtain an efficient O(q^2) approximation and approximate dual variables for gradients.  
- Training speedups include S-WCD initialization, mini-batching, and a WCD-based neighbor filter.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines; S-WMD has lowest kNN error on 5 datasets and is competitive on others, with reported efficient training and released code.  
- Sinkhorn relaxation is appropriate and provides unique, differentiable transport plans; gradients for A and w are clearly derived.  
- Experimental setup is broad with strong baselines and Bayesian hyperparameter tuning; kNN evaluation aligns with the method’s goal.  
- Missing analyses: no ablations for A-only vs w-only, sensitivity to the Sinkhorn regularization λ, or comparisons of p=1 vs p=2 ground costs.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, with sufficient background on WMD, Sinkhorn, and NCA.  
- Needs more intuition and a small example for Eq. (5) reweighting d̃ = (w ◦ d)/(w>d) to explain mass conservation and emphasis effects.  
- Notation issues: unify λ across equations, clarify chosen λ=10 and its sensitivity, and fix table/figure descriptions (average error vs average rank).  
- Table 2 needs clarification of terms “n” vs “ne”; specify how k in kNN is selected (fixed or tuned).  
- Recommend adding a controlled synonym replacement example in the supplement to support claims about semantic forgiveness.  
- Originality: novel supervised learning of both ground metric and histogram weights for document distances, and elegant integration of Sinkhorn for end-to-end learning.  
- Significance: advances state of the art on multiple datasets, keeps WMD interpretability, and is applicable beyond kNN; code availability increases impact.  
- Strengths: conceptually clean extension, sound use of Sinkhorn, strong empirical results, practical training pipeline, and code release.  
- Weaknesses: lack of ablation/sensitivity studies, minor presentation and notation issues, and limited discussion of alternative ground-distance choices.  
- Questions for authors: provide A-only/w-only ablations; confirm if λ in Eq.(2) is the same and report sensitivity to λ and Sinkhorn iterations; clarify k selection; define “n” and “ne”; confirm average error vs average rank; give intuitive example for Eq.(5).  
- Recommendation rationale: high-quality, impactful contribution with practical gains and solid grounding, but needs additional analyses and minor clarifications before final acceptance.
