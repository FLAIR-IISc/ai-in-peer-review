- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) using Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for Wasserstein distance.  
- Learns word-specific weights to adapt WMD for supervised text classification.  
- Evaluated on 8 benchmark datasets against 26 baselines, reporting superior average performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear and thorough mathematical exposition.  
- Practical adoption of Sinkhorn is well-motivated and technically sound, delivering speedups without significant accuracy loss.  
- Novelty is limited: core idea (supervised adaptation via word weights) builds directly on prior WMD work.  
- Comparison to World Centroid Distance is tangential and confusing, lacking clear relevance to the method or claims.  
- Major gap: insufficient discussion and analysis of the regularization parameter λ.  
- Unclear whether λ choices prioritize true Wasserstein behavior (large λ) or entropic Sinkhorn approximation (small λ), affecting fidelity vs. speed.  
- No empirical analysis of λ’s impact on Equation (2) or resulting model behavior, hindering reproducibility and interpretation.  
- Overall: solid empirical contribution and technical foundation, but weak treatment of λ and an irrelevant baseline limit scholarly impact.
