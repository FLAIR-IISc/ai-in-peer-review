- Proposes S-WMD: a novel supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-importance weights.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the proposed method.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on eight real-world text classification tasks.  
- Compares S-WMD against 26 competitive baselines and consistently outperforms nearly all.  
- Key contribution: jointly learning embedding-space linear transform and word-importance weights for better document distance.  
- Provides detailed analysis of results, including strengths and weaknesses of S-WMD.  
- Lacks clear intuition and explanation for Equation (5) defining word-importance weight usage.  
- Limited discussion/comparison of S-WMD’s relationship to other metric learning methods (e.g., NCA).  
- Acceptance arguments: novel method, strong empirical results, potential significant contribution to NLP.  
- Rejection arguments: need clearer intuition for Eq (5), more detailed comparisons to related metric learning, and additional experiments.
