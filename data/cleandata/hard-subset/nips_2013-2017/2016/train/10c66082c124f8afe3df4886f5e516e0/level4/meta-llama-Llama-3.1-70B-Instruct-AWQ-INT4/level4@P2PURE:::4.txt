This paper presents a supervised approach to learning the distance between documents based on the word mover's distance (WMD), which is itself an extension of the earth mover's distance. The WMD calculates the optimal transport from one distribution of words to another to obtain the document distance, initially using word2vec embeddings for the transport distance between words. The authors propose learning a linear transformation of the embedded words and a reweighted word distribution to increase the separation between documents of different labels. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, providing a thorough review of related works that demonstrates the authors' good understanding of the literature. Experimental results are also presented for several novel datasets.

However, the motivation behind the formulation requires further explanation, and the significance of the contribution is limited. Specifically, the use of reweighting the word distribution using w is not well-motivated and appears to potentially alter the document's meaning entirely. To justify this reweighting, the authors should provide a clear explanation of its benefits without changing the document's meaning.

The contribution of this work is not substantial, as adopting a linear transformation A and reweighting w with the Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA) objective function is a straightforward extension of WMD to the supervised setting. This formulation lacks novelty and innovation.

Furthermore, it is unclear how the algorithm can determine a distance for a new, unseen document. Specifically, how can the reweighting w be determined for a completely new document?

Additionally, the term "semantic difference" in the abstract appears to refer to the label of the document. However, differences in authorship or topic do not seem to constitute a semantic difference. The authors should clarify whether "semantic difference" has a different meaning in this context.

In general, the paper is well-written, and I do not object to its acceptance, provided that the authors adequately address these questions and concerns.
