- Paper extends supervised Word Mover’s Distance by learning word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy, evaluated on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines with best average performance.
- Well-written manuscript with clear and in-depth mathematical analysis.
- Using Sinkhorn distance as a proxy for Wasserstein is a useful contribution for speed.
- Limited novelty compared to the original Word Mover’s Distance paper.
- References to Word Centroid Distance are not relevant and are confusing.
- Poor discussion of the regularization parameter lambda and its large impact on results.
- Unclear whether reported behavior corresponds to pure Wasserstein (large lambda) or entropic Sinkhorn (small lambda).
- Evaluation is extensive (8 datasets, 26 baselines) and empirically strong.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.
