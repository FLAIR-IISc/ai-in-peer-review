- Proposes learning a Mahalanobis ground metric for Word Mover's Distance (WMD) and reweighting document histograms, with NCA-style gradient derivations and strong empirical results.  
- Combines existing techniques effectively; idea is simple and intuitive yet non-trivial to implement and validate—supports acceptance.  

Major points for improvement:
- Clarify the interplay between regularized and non-regularized EMD/WMD formulations.  
- Improve mathematical rigor and fix imprecise computations and notation.  
- Include pseudocode/algorithm box to provide a clear step-by-step overview of the method.  

Specific comments:
- Inconsistency in cost definition: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance but Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); recommend defining WMD as p-Wasserstein for generality.  
- Histogram reweighting (Eq.5) is not novel—previously introduced by Guy Lebanon ("Metric Learning for Text Documents") and related to work by Le & Cuturi on histogram metric learning.  
- Provide ablation or experimental analysis on the importance and impact of the learned weight vector w.  
- Strengthen related work: clearly distinguish from Cuturi & Avis (entropy regularization, discrete vs continuous cost, scalability) and discuss limitations of Wang et al. and how the paper’s iterative approach overcomes them.
