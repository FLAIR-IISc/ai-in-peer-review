- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised variant of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and nonnegative word-importance vector w to align WMD with labels.  
- Uses entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to obtain a smooth, efficient approximation for differentiable transport and reuses dual variables for gradients.  
- Training accelerations include supervised centroid initialization (S-WCD/NCA), minibatching, and neighbor pruning via a cheap WCD lower bound.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving best average rank and lowest error on 5/8 tasks with reasonable training times.  
- Connects metric learning (NCA/LMNN), entropic OT (Sinkhorn), learned OT ground metrics, and histogram reweighting/compositional text methods.  
- Strength: clear, practical idea that injects supervision into WMD while leveraging pre-trained embeddings.  
- Strength: solid engineering—Sinkhorn approximation, dual-variable reuse, and neighbor pruning for scalable training.  
- Strength: thorough experiments, fair protocols, hyperparameter tuning, and visualizations.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance across diverse datasets.  
- Weakness: unclear whether evaluation uses regularized or exact WMD; needs clarity on training/eval choices and λ role.  
- Weakness: distance definition inconsistencies (p-Wasserstein vs. squared Euclidean under A) cause notational confusion.  
- Weakness: under-analyzed word-weight w—needs ablations (A only, w only, both), sparsity/density analysis, and long vs. short document performance.  
- Weakness: insufficient detail on constraints/regularization for w and safeguards against concentrating on rare words.  
- Weakness: mathematical/derivation clarity is terse—needs uniqueness/subgradient discussion, separation of exact vs. relaxed objectives, and explicit α,β recovery justification.  
- Weakness: related work positioning needs clearer contrasts with Cuturi–Avis and Wang et al., and ties to histogram/text metric learning and Aitchison approaches.  
- Weakness: reporting omissions—initialization time excluded from training time; request full end-to-end time and memory, and sensitivity of neighbor-list size N.  
- Quality: method is technically sound and principled, but claims about arbitrary approximation require discussion of λ and iterations.  
- Clarity: generally readable but notation and toggling between exact/relaxed formulations should be tightened and an algorithm box added.  
- Originality: components are known but their joint application (learning A and w for supervised WMD with Sinkhorn gradients) is novel and practical.  
- Significance: addresses an impactful problem with likely practical adoption in kNN/retrieval systems and transferability to other embeddings/tasks.  
- Questions for authors: how much gain stems from A vs. w; are test-time distances regularized or exact; sensitivity to λ; constraints/regularization on w; sensitivity to neighbor set N and batch size B.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical and effective supervised WMD extension, scalable engineering, broad relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: presentation and mathematical inconsistencies, missing key ablations (w, λ, N) and end-to-end runtime reporting.  
- Recommendation: Accept, conditional on improving clarity about regularization, adding an algorithm summary, and providing ablations on w and λ.
