- Summary: Extends WMD by learning word-specific weights and using Sinkhorn regularization for speed; evaluated on eight datasets vs 26 baselines with best average performance; clear writing and math.

- Strength: Clear problem formulation and thorough, accessible mathematical analysis.

- Strength: Practical contribution—using Sinkhorn yields substantial computational speedups.

- Strength: Supervised, word-specific weighting is intuitive and well-motivated for classification.

- Strength: Broad and competitive empirical evaluation across many datasets and baselines.

- Weakness: Novelty is incremental relative to WMD—main advances are supervised weighting and Sinkhorn approximation.

- Weakness: Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is unclear and sometimes confusing; appropriateness as a baseline is not justified.

- Weakness: Role of entropic regularization parameter λ is under-discussed despite its strong influence on transport and classifier behavior.

- Weakness: Unclear whether performance gains come from learned weights, Sinkhorn regularization, or their combination—ablation study missing.

- Clarification requested: Precisely define λ and its relationship to the entropy term (convention varies).

- Clarification requested: Empirically characterize limiting regimes as λ varies—does it approach unregularized OT or a diffuse entropic transport?

- Clarification requested: Explain how λ is chosen (per dataset/fold, validation, fixed globally) and report selected values and any cost/embedding normalization.

- Clarification requested: Provide runtime comparisons across λ values and against exact OT where feasible to quantify speed–accuracy trade-offs.

- Clarification requested: Clarify why WCD is included as a baseline and what aspect of the method it tests.

- Suggestion: Add ablation studies: uniform vs learned weights; Sinkhorn-regularized vs unregularized OT; impact of λ on accuracy and transport sparsity/diffuseness.

- Suggestion: Discuss how cost scaling/embedding normalization interacts with λ, since scaling changes effective regularization.

- Suggestion: Fix terminology (e.g., “Word Centroid Distance” typo) and streamline references to avoid confusion.

- Suggestion: If possible, include qualitative examples showing how learned weights and Sinkhorn affect nearest neighbors or decision boundaries.

- Overall assessment: Well-executed and practically useful with strong empirical results; acceptance recommended contingent on clarifying λ’s role and adding targeted ablations.
