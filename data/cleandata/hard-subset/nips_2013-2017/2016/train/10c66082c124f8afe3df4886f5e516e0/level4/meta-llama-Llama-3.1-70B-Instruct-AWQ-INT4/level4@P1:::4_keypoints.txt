- Proposes a supervised method to learn document distances based on Word Mover’s Distance using a linear embedding transform and reweighted word distributions.  
- Uses pre-trained word2vec for word-wise transport distances and trains a linear transformation A and weights w with an NCA-style objective.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Experimental results are reported on several novel datasets.  
- Motivation for the formulation, especially the use of reweighting w, is insufficiently explained.  
- Reweighting w could alter a document’s meaning; the authors must justify why it helps without changing essence.  
- Contribution is limited—applying a linear transform and reweighting with NCA is an incremental extension rather than a major novelty.  
- Unclear how the method handles new documents, specifically how to determine or infer appropriate reweighting w at test time.  
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified (labels, authorship, topic, etc.).  
- Reviewer conditionally supports acceptance if the authors address these concerns and provide stronger explanations.
