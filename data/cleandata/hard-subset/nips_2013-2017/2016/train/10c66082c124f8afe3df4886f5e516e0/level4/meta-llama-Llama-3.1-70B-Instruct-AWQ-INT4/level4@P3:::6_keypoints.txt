- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of WMD for document metric learning.  
- S-WMD learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and per-word importance weights to improve distances.  
- Paper gives a clear, concise introduction and situates the work within related literature.  
- Method section provides a detailed optimization formulation and derivation of gradient updates.  
- Experimental evaluation compares against 26 baselines across 8 document corpora.  
- S-WMD achieves the lowest kNN test error on 5 of 8 datasets and the best average performance across datasets.  
- t-SNE visualizations illustrate that learned distances capture semantic document similarities.  
- Code is released, aiding reproducibility and adoption.  
- Main limitation: evaluation is restricted to kNN classification and lacks other tasks like clustering or retrieval.  
- Missing analysis: more intuition and explanation for the learned word importance weights.  
- Missing ablation: unclear which component (linear transform vs. importance weights) drives the improvements.  
- Paper could benefit from deeper analysis of learned distances and supervision impact.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, significant contribution to supervised metric learning for text.  
- Recommendation: accept.
