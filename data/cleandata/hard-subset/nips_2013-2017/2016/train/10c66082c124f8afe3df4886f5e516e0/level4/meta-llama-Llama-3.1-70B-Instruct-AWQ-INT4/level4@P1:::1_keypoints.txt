- Proposes supervised metric learning built on word-movers distance, applicable to transport distances after a linear projection.
- Learns both a linear projection and document-specific word-frequency re-weighting with renormalization.
- Presents an efficient optimization method for the parameterized metric (including Sinkhorn iterations).
- Thorough experimental evaluation on multiple document classification datasets using kNN.
- Demonstrates effectiveness compared to many reasonable baselines.
- Impact limited because earth-movers distance with a "word centroid distance" initialization achieves similar performance (Table 3).
- Missing comparison: evaluate word-centroid embeddings with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- Missing ablation: assess relative importance of word-specific weights versus projection matrix A by testing each component in isolation.
- Originality mainly in the optimization procedure; linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a limited contribution.
- Paper is very well written, clear, and well organized.
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous "The authors" reference (Line 152), Equation 11 notation for alpha/beta, and explicit mention of Sinkhorn scaling near Line 170.
- Strengths summarized: novel supervised metric learning approach, useful extension of WMD, efficient optimization, and comprehensive experiments.
- Weaknesses summarized: limited incremental impact over centroid initialization and incomplete experimental comparisons.
- Acceptance arguments focus on novelty and thorough evaluation; rejection arguments focus on limited additional benefit and missing baselines/ablations.
