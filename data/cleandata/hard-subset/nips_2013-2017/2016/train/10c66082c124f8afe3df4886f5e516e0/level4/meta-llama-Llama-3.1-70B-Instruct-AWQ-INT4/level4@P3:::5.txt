The authors propose a novel method for learning a supervised document distance, which they call the Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD). This approach builds upon the previously introduced Word Mover's Distance (WMD), a document metric that leverages word embeddings to compute distances between documents. The key contribution of this work is the incorporation of supervision into the WMD, allowing the method to learn a metric that is tailored to a specific task.

The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the mathematical formulation of S-WMD, including the use of the Sinkhorn distance as a proxy for the Wasserstein distance to improve computational efficiency. The evaluation of S-WMD on 8 real-world text classification tasks, against 26 competitive baselines, demonstrates its effectiveness and superiority over existing methods.

The paper is well-written, and the authors do an excellent job of presenting the topic and analyzing the math behind the problem in depth. The use of visualizations, such as the 2D embedding of the test split of each dataset using t-SNE, helps to illustrate the quality of the learned distance metric.

One potential weakness of the paper is the limited novelty compared to the previous WMD paper. While the incorporation of supervision into the WMD is a significant contribution, it is not entirely clear how much of an impact this has on the overall performance of the method. Additionally, the discussion of the regularizing factor, lambda, is somewhat limited. In the reviewer's experience, the value of lambda can have a significant impact on the behavior of the Sinkhorn distance, and it is not clear whether the chosen value of lambda (10) dictates a pure Wasserstein behavior or a Sinkhorn (entropic) behavior.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of document distance metrics and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method. However, further discussion of the impact of lambda and the novelty of the approach compared to previous work would strengthen the paper.

Strengths:

* Clear and thorough explanation of the mathematical formulation of S-WMD
* Effective evaluation of S-WMD on 8 real-world text classification tasks
* Superior performance compared to 26 competitive baselines
* Well-written paper with good use of visualizations

Weaknesses:

* Limited novelty compared to previous WMD paper
* Limited discussion of the regularizing factor, lambda
* Unclear impact of lambda on the behavior of the Sinkhorn distance

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of document distance metrics
* The proposed method demonstrates superior performance compared to existing methods
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the mathematical formulation of S-WMD

Arguments against acceptance:
