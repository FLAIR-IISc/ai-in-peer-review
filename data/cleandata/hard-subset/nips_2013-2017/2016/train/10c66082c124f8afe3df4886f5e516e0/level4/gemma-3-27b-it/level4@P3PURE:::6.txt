The paper proposes a novel metric learning method for textual documents, extending the previously established Word Mover’s Distance (WMD). WMD is an unsupervised approach that measures distance based on word embeddings; this work adapts it to a supervised setting utilizing class labels – specifically, indications of document similarity. The authors promise to release code (in Matlab). They propose learning the distance metric through two components: a linear transformation A, representing a standard approach, and word importance weights *w*. I would appreciate a clearer explanation of the intuition behind Eq (5), which defines the application of *w*, and a better understanding of the contribution of each component (A and *w*) to the reported performance gains. 

The experimental evaluation focuses on a document classification task. While acknowledging the challenges of evaluating distance metrics directly, it would be valuable to include, for comparative purposes, the performance of a simple classifier trained directly on the same datasets. 

Questions for the authors: The usage of Eq (2) remains unclear. The λ symbol in this equation appears to differ from the λ mentioned in line 170. Furthermore, the claim “which is particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words” (line 316) requires further justification. Other comments: Line 257 states, “on the right we also show the average error across datasets,” but I could not locate this information; is it intended to be presented in Table 3? Regarding Table 2, what is the distinction between “n” and “ne”? Finally, the definition of *k* for the k-nearest neighbors classification is not specified. Is this parameter determined based on the dataset or is it method-dependent?
