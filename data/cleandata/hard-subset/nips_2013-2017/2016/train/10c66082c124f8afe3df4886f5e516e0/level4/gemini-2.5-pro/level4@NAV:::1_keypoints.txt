- Introduces a supervised metric learning method that enhances Word Mover's Distance applied to word2vec embeddings.  
- Method generalizes to any transport distance using squared Euclidean distances after a linear projection.  
- Model jointly learns a linear projection and word-specific weights that re-weight and re-normalize document word frequencies.  
- Presents an efficient optimization algorithm for learning the metric parameters.  
- Comprehensive experiments on multiple document classification datasets show effectiveness versus many baselines in kNN classification.  
- Technically interesting and useful extension of Word Mover's Distance, with convincing empirical utility.  
- Impact may be limited: fully learned metric only marginally outperforms a simpler EMD baseline initialized with a "word centroid distance."  
- Table 3 should include a comparison of the "word centroid embedding" against all tested metric learning algorithms (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation study evaluating the individual contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Primary originality appears to be the optimization strategy (Lines 156–175), making the conceptual contribution somewhat limited.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to read.  
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous reference at Line 152 about which cited work is meant.  
- Minor notation fix: Equation 11 should consistently denote alpha and beta with stars.  
- Clarify around Line 170 that the described procedure is the Sinkhorn scaling method referenced earlier.
