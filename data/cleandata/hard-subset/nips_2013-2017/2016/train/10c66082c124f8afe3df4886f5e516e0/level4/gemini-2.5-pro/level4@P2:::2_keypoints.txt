- Proposal: Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of WMD that adapts document distance to classification tasks.  
- Model: Simultaneously learns an affine transformation of word embeddings (matrix A) and word-importance weights (vector w).  
- Objective: Optimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification loss to tailor the distance metric to labels.  
- Technical challenge: Direct WMD gradient is intractable due to a nested optimization; authors address this.  
- Solution: Approximate WMD with entropically regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain a strongly convex objective and efficient gradients.  
- Foundations: Builds on Kusner et al. (2015) WMD and uses a Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA)-style loss.  
- Empirics: Extensive evaluation vs 26 strong baselines on 8 datasets; S-WMD consistently and often substantially outperforms competitors.  
- Presentation: Paper is clear, well-organized, and provides detailed derivations and transparent experimental setup.  
- Missing comparison: Lacks evaluation/discussion relative to end-to-end deep learning classifiers (e.g., CNNs/LSTMs) that learn task-specific representations.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Several hyperparameters (λ, batch size B, neighbor set size N) are tuned but sensitivity analysis is lacking.  
- Initialization importance: Smart initialization via NCA on Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) contributes substantially; need clarification on performance with other initializations.
