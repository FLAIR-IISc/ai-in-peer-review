- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out classification error.
- Uses Cuturi’s Sinkhorn distance to make optimal-transport computations tractable and enable practical application to text classification.
- Empirically strong: outperforms 26 baselines across eight datasets (wins on 5/8 tasks) including WMD, WCD, and supervised metric-learning baselines.
- Experimental validation is thorough, covering diverse tasks (news, sentiment, product ID) with appropriate statistics and visualizations.
- Technical approach is sound with a solid theoretical foundation and well-justified approximations for computational feasibility.
- Mathematical presentation needs more rigor—specifically clarify the relationship between regularized (Sinkhorn) and unregularized formulations.
- Inconsistency between Euclidean vs squared-Euclidean distances (Eq.1 vs Eq.6) must be clarified, and the paper should state whether it targets 1-Wasserstein or 2-Wasserstein.
- Clarity is good overall, but the paper should include a formal algorithm box summarizing S-WMD and standardize mathematical notation for reproducibility.
- Originality: novel idea to supervise WMD via learned linear transforms and weights, addressing a key limitation of original WMD.
- Prior work overlap: the reweighting approach (Eq.5) is similar to Lebanon (2010) and needs explicit comparison to clarify novelty.
- The framing of Sinkhorn as a key practical innovation is well-motivated but should be emphasized more explicitly.
- Significance: provides a practical supervised WMD without retraining embeddings, with competitive training time (Table 3) suitable for real-world use.
- Visualization (Figure 1) supports that S-WMD yields more discriminative document representations.
- Weaknesses: mathematical rigor lacking, partial novelty in reweighting, and related work section should better distinguish contributions from prior metric-learning-for-text methods.
- Recommendation: accept, provided the authors address mathematical/notation issues and clarify relationship to prior reweighting work.
