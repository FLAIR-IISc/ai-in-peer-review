- Proposes supervised metric learning extending word-movers distance: learns a linear projection and document-specific word-frequency weights, with an efficient optimization and strong kNN classification results.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against many baselines on multiple document classification datasets.  
- Major concern: earth-movers distance with metric learning via "word centroid distance" initialization performs similarly to the full learned metric (Table 3), questioning the added benefit of the proposed contributions.  
- Suggestion: include results for the word-centroid embedding (weighted mean of word embeddings) combined with all tested metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) in Table 3.  
- Suggestion: perform an ablation to assess the relative importance of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A (components active in isolation).  
- Originality primarily lies in the optimization procedure (Lines 156–175); linear metric learning within earth-movers is interesting but a limited standalone contribution.  
- Writing quality is very high and the paper is a pleasure to read.  
- Minor clarification: specify which paper is meant at Line 152 ([1,7,8]?).  
- Minor correction: in Equation 11, alpha and beta should likely carry stars.  
- Minor clarification: explicitly state around Line 170 that this is the Sinkhorn scaling mentioned in the introduction (Line 56).
