- Extends Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) for supervised text classification by learning a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-specific importance weights.
- Introduces S-WMD using the Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD, enabling efficient gradient-based optimization and alleviating cubic scaling.
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, consistently outperforming almost all competitors.
- Builds on Kusner et al.’s WMD and connects to metric learning literature while operating directly on word embeddings rather than document representations.
- Technical soundness: correct gradient derivation and sensible batch optimization strategy.
- Strong empirical evidence: extensive experiments demonstrating clear performance gains.
- Clarity: exceptionally well-written with digestible mathematical derivations and clear motivation.
- Novelty: combination of learned linear transform, word importance weights, and Sinkhorn-based optimization is a novel contribution.
- Incremental novelty: applying metric learning to WMD may be seen as straightforward and not groundbreaking.
- Insufficient analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ; sensitivity and guidance for selection are lacking.
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) feels tangential and not well-justified.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, strong results, clear presentation, and practical Sinkhorn solution.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty and inadequate study of λ.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with practical impact; recommended for acceptance despite incremental novelty.
