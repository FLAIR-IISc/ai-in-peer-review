- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): incorporates supervision to learn word-specific weights for improved text classification.
- Builds on Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) and uses Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for the Wasserstein distance.
- Paper is well-written with thorough mathematical formulation and analysis.
- Strong empirical evaluation: tested on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing superior performance.
- Key contribution is supervision in WMD, but novelty over original WMD is limited.
- Unclear how S-WMD differs from or relates to other metric learning methods.
- References to World Centroid Distance (WCD) are confusing and need clarification regarding relation to S-WMD.
- Regularization parameter lambda needs deeper discussion; its value strongly affects whether behavior is Wasserstein-like or entropic Sinkhorn-like.
- Theoretical note: large lambda approximates pure Wasserstein at slower convergence; small lambda commonly used for faster, different behavior.
- With revisions addressing novelty, lambda discussion, and WCD relation, the paper could be a top-tier contribution.
- Acceptance arguments: novel supervised extension, strong empirical results, clear writing and analysis.
- Rejection arguments: limited novelty, unclear lambda treatment, and confusing WCD references.
