- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) for learning a task-specific document metric by adapting WMD with supervision.  
- Learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and word-importance weights for improved document distances.  
- Trains using a stochastic leave-one-out k-NN loss inspired by Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA).  
- Key technical contribution: efficient gradient computation via entropically regularized Sinkhorn approximation to avoid nested optimization.  
- Achieves state-of-the-art k-NN text classification performance across eight datasets and 26 baselines, outperforming unsupervised WMD and supervised baselines.  
- Technically sound, with well-justified approximation and rigorous experimental support.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization, with helpful background and transparent mathematical derivations.  
- Originality is incremental: combines existing components (WMD, NCA-style loss, Sinkhorn) rather than introducing a fundamentally new framework.  
- Insufficient analysis of the entropy regularization parameter λ and its effect on performance and approximation quality.  
- Role and motivation for using Word Centroid Distance (WCD) for initialization and neighbor selection need clearer justification.  
- Overall: high-quality, significant contribution that advances the state of the art despite being an incremental methodological combination.
