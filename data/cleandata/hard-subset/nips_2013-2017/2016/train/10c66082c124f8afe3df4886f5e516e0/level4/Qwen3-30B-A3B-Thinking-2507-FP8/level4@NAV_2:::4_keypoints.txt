- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance for document classification.
- S-WMD learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights to improve classification performance.
- Builds on metric learning (Neighborhood Components Analysis) while retaining pre-trained word embeddings instead of learning document representations.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes a thorough related-work review.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive (8 real-world tasks, 26 baselines) and shows consistent improvements over unsupervised WMD.
- Technical approach is sound, using Sinkhorn approximations for tractable optimization and providing clear gradient derivations.
- Motivation for the word importance reweighting (w) is weak and needs stronger justification about preserving document semantics.
- Contribution is incremental—applying standard metric learning techniques to WMD rather than offering a major conceptual advance.
- Needs more explicit comparison to related supervised metric-learning work for text (e.g., learning ground distances for EMD).
- Significance is limited: improvements are consistent but not dramatic and do not clearly demonstrate a broad advance beyond tested classification tasks.
- The claim of capturing "semantic differences" is ambiguous and requires clarification (e.g., topic vs authorship vs other semantics).
- Specific concerns: (1) stronger justification that reweighting preserves meaning; (2) how to determine word weights for new documents; (3) clearer link between semantic differences and labels.
- Overall: solid, well-executed, and potentially useful contribution but incremental; addressing the above concerns would strengthen the paper.
