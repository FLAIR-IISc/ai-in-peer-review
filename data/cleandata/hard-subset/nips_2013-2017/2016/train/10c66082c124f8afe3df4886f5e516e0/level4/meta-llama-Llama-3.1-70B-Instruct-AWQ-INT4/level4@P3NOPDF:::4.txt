This paper proposes a supervised approach to learning the distance between documents based on the word mover's distance (WMD). WMD is an extension of the earth mover's distance, which calculates the optimal transport between two distributions of words to obtain the document distance. Initially, word2vec embedding is used to calculate the transport distance between words. The authors introduce two key components to improve the separation of documents with different labels: (1) a linear transformation of the embedded words and (2) a reweighted word distribution.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, demonstrating a thorough understanding of related works in the field. The authors provide a comprehensive review of previous literature and present experimental results on various novel datasets. However, several aspects of the paper require further clarification and justification.

Firstly, the motivation behind the formulation needs more explanation, and the significance of the contribution is relatively low. Specifically, the use of reweighting the word distribution using 'w' is not well-motivated, and it appears that this reweighting could alter the document's meaning entirely. To justify this reweighting, the authors should provide a clear explanation of why it helps to improve the separation of documents without changing their fundamental meaning.

Secondly, the contribution of this work is not groundbreaking. Adopting a linear transformation 'A' and reweighting 'w' with the Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA) objective function is a possible extension of WMD to a supervised setting, but it does not represent a significant departure from existing methods.

Thirdly, it is unclear how the algorithm would handle a completely new document. Specifically, how would one determine the reweighting 'w' for a novel document? The authors should provide a clear explanation of how their method can be applied to new, unseen documents.

Lastly, the term 'semantic difference' in the abstract appears to refer to the label of the document. However, this interpretation may not align with the conventional understanding of semantic difference, which typically encompasses differences in meaning, authorship, or topic. The authors should clarify their intended meaning of 'semantic difference' to avoid confusion.

In general, the paper is well-written, and the reviewer does not object to its acceptance, provided that the authors address the above concerns and provide sufficient justification for their methodology.
