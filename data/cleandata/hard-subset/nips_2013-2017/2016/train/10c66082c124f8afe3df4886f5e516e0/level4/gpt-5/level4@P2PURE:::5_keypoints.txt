- Paper extends Word Mover’s Distance for text classification by learning supervised, word-specific weights.  
- Uses Sinkhorn (entropic) distance as a computationally faster proxy for Wasserstein.  
- Empirical evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving best average performance.  
- Writing and mathematical exposition are clear and thorough.  
- Main novelty is limited relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance paper.  
- Inclusion of critiques/references on Word Centroid Distance is seen as irrelevant and confusing.  
- Insufficient discussion of the regularization parameter λ — its large impact and whether results behave like pure Wasserstein or entropic Sinkhorn needs clarification.  
- Practical concern: many users choose small λ for faster convergence, which can make behavior diverge markedly from true Wasserstein; authors should clarify which regime their method targets.
