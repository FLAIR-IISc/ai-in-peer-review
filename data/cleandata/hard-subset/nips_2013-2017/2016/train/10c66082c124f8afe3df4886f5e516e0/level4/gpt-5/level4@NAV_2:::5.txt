The paper addresses supervised text categorization. It extends prior work on Word Mover’s Distance (a Wasserstein-based document metric) by making it supervised through learning word-specific importance weights. The approach adopts Sinkhorn-regularized transport as a proxy for the Wasserstein computation to gain efficiency, and is evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance.

The manuscript is very well written, clearly motivates the problem, and presents the mathematical analysis with both depth and clarity. Possible weaknesses include somewhat limited novelty relative to the original WMD paper, and a discussion of the Word Centroid Distance that feels tangential and potentially confusing. I also find the treatment of the regularization parameter λ underdeveloped: in practice λ often has a substantial—sometimes dominant—effect on Eq. (2). In theory, taking λ large should recover behavior close to the unregularized Wasserstein distance at the cost of slower convergence, whereas many practitioners use small λ for faster convergence, yielding dynamics that can differ markedly from true Wasserstein. It would be helpful to clarify whether, in this work, the chosen λ induces behavior closer to the pure Wasserstein metric or to the entropic (Sinkhorn) regime.
