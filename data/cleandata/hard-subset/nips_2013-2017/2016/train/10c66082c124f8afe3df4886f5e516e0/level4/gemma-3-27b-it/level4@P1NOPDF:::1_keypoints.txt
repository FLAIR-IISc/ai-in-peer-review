- Proposes supervised metric learning extending word-movers distance via a linear projection and learned word-specific weights, with an efficient optimization and strong kNN classification results.  
- Technically interesting and a useful extension of word-movers, demonstrated effective on several datasets.  
- Impact may be limited because earth-movers distance with metric learning initialized by the word-centroid distance performs comparably (Table 3).  
- Table 3 omits evaluation of the word-centroid embedding combined with the tested metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: no assessment of the separate contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A (performance with each component in isolation).  
- Originality mainly in the optimization method; linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a somewhat limited contribution.  
- Paper is very well written and readable.  
- Minor clarifications needed: specify which paper “The authors” refers to (Line 152); Equation 11 should superscript alpha and beta with stars; explicitly recall that the scaling at Line 170 is the Sinkhorn scaling mentioned earlier.
