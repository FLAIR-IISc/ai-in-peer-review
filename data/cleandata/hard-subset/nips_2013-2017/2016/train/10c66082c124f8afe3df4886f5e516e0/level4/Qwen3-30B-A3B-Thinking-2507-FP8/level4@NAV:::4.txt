

This paper introduces a supervised approach for learning document distances by extending Word Mover's Distance (WMD), which is inspired by Earth Mover's Distance (EMD). Specifically, WMD computes optimal transport between word distributions to derive document distances, initially using word2vec embeddings for word-level transport costs. The authors propose learning (1) a linear transformation of word embeddings and (2) a reweighted word distribution to enhance label separation between documents. The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and includes a comprehensive review of related work demonstrating strong literature understanding. Experimental results are presented across multiple novel datasets. However, the motivation for the proposed formulation requires deeper justification, and the contribution lacks substantial significance. Specifically:  
1) The rationale for reweighting word distributions using vector \( \mathbf{w} \) is unclear. This reweighting risks fundamentally altering a document's meaning, and the authors must demonstrate how it preserves document integrity while improving separation.  
2) The contribution is incremental: adapting linear transformation \( \mathbf{A} \) and reweighting \( \mathbf{w} \) via the NCA objective for supervised WMD represents a straightforward extension, not a novel or impactful advancement.  
3) The method's applicability to unseen documents is ambiguous: how is the reweighting vector \( \mathbf{w} \) determined for a completely new document?  
4) The abstract's reference to "semantic difference" appears to conflate label assignment with semantic distinctions (e.g., authorship or topic differences), requiring clarification on its intended meaning.  
Overall, the paper is well-written and merits acceptance upon sufficiently addressing these concerns.
