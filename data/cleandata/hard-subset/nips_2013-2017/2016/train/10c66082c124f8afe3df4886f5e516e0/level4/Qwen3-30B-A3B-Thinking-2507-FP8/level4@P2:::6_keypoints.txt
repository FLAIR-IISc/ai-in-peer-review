- Introduces S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform A and word importance weights w, uses Sinkhorn approximation for efficient gradient training minimizing stochastic leave-one-out kNN error.  
- Empirical results: evaluated on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines, achieves lowest error on 5 datasets and best average performance; large gains (e.g., 51% relative reduction on BBCSPORT vs. LSI); code released.  
- Strength — technical quality/significance: solid theoretical grounding in optimal transport and metric learning, with practical scalability via Sinkhorn (O(q²) vs O(q³ log q)).  
- Strength — originality: novel integration of word-level supervision (w) with embedding-space transformation (A), distinct from prior work that only learned ground distances.  
- Strength — clarity: well-structured paper with clear definitions, Sinkhorn relaxation, training algorithm, experiments, t-SNE visualizations, and word-weight analyses.  
- Weakness — intuition for w: paper needs a clearer, earlier explanation of how and why the word-weight vector w improves distances beyond A alone.  
- Weakness — unsupported claim: statement that S-WMD handles syntactically different but semantically similar words is not backed by concrete document-pair examples comparing S-WMD and WMD.  
- Weakness — minor technical omissions: conflate λ’s roles in Eq.2 and Eq.6 and omit the k value for kNN classification (should specify, e.g., k=5).  
- Pro acceptance: significant, scalable advance that leverages embeddings for supervised metric learning and shows strong empirical gains.  
- Con acceptance: partially incremental (A and w follow standard metric-learning ideas) and one key claim lacks direct empirical illustration.  
- Recommendation: recommend acceptance — strong contribution and reproducible results, with only minor clarifications needed.
