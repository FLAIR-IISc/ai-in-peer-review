- Proposes learning WMD/EMD ground cost via a Mahalanobis distance on word embeddings and jointly learning histogram reweighting.  
- Derives gradients for NCA-type objectives and provides strong empirical evidence supporting the method.  
- Overall assessment: thoughtful combination of existing techniques with strong results; supports acceptance.  
- Strength: idea is simple, clear, nontrivial to implement, and experimentally well validated.  
- Weakness: needs to better address the interplay between regularized and unregularized OT formulations in method and analysis.  
- Weakness: mathematical presentation is somewhat sloppy; several computations and notational elements need tightening.  
- Weakness: paper should include an algorithm box or pseudocode to make the procedure explicit and reproducible.  
- Minor: inconsistent use of distance (Euclidean in Eq.1 vs squared Euclidean/Mahalanobis in Eq.6); recommend framing as p-Wasserstein with a consistent p.  
- Minor: histogram reweighting in Eq.5 is not new—cite Lebanon’s “Metric Learning for Text Documents” and related work (Le & Cuturi).  
- Minor: request experimental analysis of the learned weight vector w—does it measurably improve performance and in what settings?  
- Minor: related work positioning should be clearer—clarify differences from Cuturi/Avis (entropy regularization and different ground cost) and comment on issues in Wang et al.  
- Summary recommendation: solid, well-motivated contribution with strong empirical validation; clarifications on regularization, math rigor, and an algorithmic description would substantially strengthen the paper.
