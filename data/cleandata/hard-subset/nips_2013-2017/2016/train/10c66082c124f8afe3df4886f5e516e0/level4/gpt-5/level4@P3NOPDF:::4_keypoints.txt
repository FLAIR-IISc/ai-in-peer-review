- Summary: Proposes supervised extension of WMD by learning a linear embedding transform A and a word-distribution reweighting w using an NCA-style objective over word2vec-based OT distances.

- Strength: Clear exposition and comprehensive literature positioning on WMD, optimal transport, and supervised metric learning.

- Strength: Practical, straightforward formulation adapting an unsupervised distance to supervised settings.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation on multiple datasets demonstrating feasibility.

- Concern (reweighting motivation): Rationale for introducing global/document-specific reweighting w is unclear and may change document semantics.

- Concern (w details): Unclear whether w is global or document-specific and how it relates to IDF or supervised term-weighting schemes.

- Concern (regularization): Missing constraints/regularization on w (nonnegativity, normalization, sparsity, proximity to IDF) to avoid pathological solutions.

- Concern (ablation): Need ablations isolating effects of w vs A to show w provides real benefit rather than memorizing labels.

- Concern (novelty/significance): Contribution (A + w with NCA) appears incremental; needs clearer novelty relative to prior supervised WMD/OT, classic metric learning, and heuristic WMD improvements.

- Concern (theory/empirics): Lacks theoretical insights on learned transport metric and stronger empirical evidence against strong supervised baselines or statistical significance.

- Concern (generalization): Unclear how distances are computed at test time for unseen documents, especially if w is document- or label-dependent.

- Concern (inference cost): Need precise inference procedure and discussion of per-test-document optimization and scalability.

- Concern (semantic language): “Semantic difference” is vague—clarify whether it means label differences and avoid conflating metadata (e.g., authorship) with semantics.

- Suggestion (ablations): Include experiments: WMD; WMD+IDF; WMD+A only; WMD+w only; WMD+A+w to attribute gains.

- Suggestion (regularization analysis): Report constraints/regularization on A and w and sensitivity analyses (e.g., bound deviations from IDF/uniform).

- Suggestion (interpretability): Provide learned high/low-weight term examples, nearest-neighbor changes, and qualitative transport plans to show semantics preserved.

- Suggestion (computational details): Describe gradient computation through transport solver, whether Sinkhorn/entropy regularization is used, per-iteration complexity, and training scalability.

- Suggestion (baselines/evaluation): Strengthen baselines with supervised metric learning and embedding approaches, ensure statistical significance testing, and detail hyperparameter selection for reproducibility.

- Overall assessment: Well-written, practically motivated approach with empirical promise, but requires stronger justification for w, clear test-time procedures, ablations, and more compelling comparisons to avoid feeling incremental.
