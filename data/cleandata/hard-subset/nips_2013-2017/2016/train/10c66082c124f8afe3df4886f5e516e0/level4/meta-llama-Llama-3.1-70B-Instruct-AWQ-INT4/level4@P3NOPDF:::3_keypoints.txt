- Proposes learning WMD/EMD parameters by parameterizing the cost matrix with a Mahalanobis distance between word embeddings.  
- Also proposes learning histogram coefficient reweighting, a concept previously explored (e.g., Lebanon).  
- Derives NCA-type gradients for the learned WMD and provides convincing experimental evidence.  
- Strength: simple, effective idea with thorough experimental evaluation and solid execution.  
- Weakness: need to better handle interplay between regularized and non-regularized OT formulations.  
- Weakness: mathematical notation and computations are somewhat sloppy and need tightening.  
- Suggestion: include an algorithmic box to clarify the proposed method.  
- Minor inconsistency: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean; should refer to WMD as a p-Wasserstein distance for consistency.  
- Reweighting in Eq.5 is not novel—prior work includes Lebanon’s metric learning for text and Le & Cuturi’s Aitchison-transform approach.  
- Request: analyze the importance and impact of the learned weight vector w and whether it materially improves results.  
- Clarify distinctions from previous work (e.g., Cuturi & Avis): this paper uses entropy regularization for scalability and a continuous, linear-map-parameterized cost.  
- Note on Wang et al.: cited inspiration, but that paper appears flawed (OT optimization run only once), so its relevance is limited.  
- Overall: solid paper with convincing results; with revisions addressing the above issues, it is strong candidate for acceptance.
