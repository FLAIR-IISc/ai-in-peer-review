- Proposes learning a Mahalanobis ground metric and a histogram reweighting vector for Word Mover's Distance (WMD), trained end-to-end with an NCA-style loss.  
- Intuitive, well-motivated idea that combines metric learning with histogram reweighting in a non-trivial implementation.  
- Strong and thorough experimental results demonstrating significant improvement over baselines; reviewer supports acceptance.  
- Mathematical presentation needs clearer, more rigorous notation and derivations to avoid imprecision.  
- Include an algorithm box or pseudo-code to clarify the training procedure and step-by-step parameter updates.  
- Clarify the role and implications of entropy regularization (Sinkhorn) versus unregularized WMD throughout the paper.  
- Inconsistency in cost definition: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 implies squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); recommend framing WMD as p-Wasserstein and keeping p consistent.  
- Reweighting scheme (Eq.5) is not novel and should be contextualized with prior work (Lebanon; Le & Cuturi).  
- Provide an ablation study or analysis quantifying the contribution and scenarios where the learned weight vector w helps.  
- Better position the paper relative to prior OT learning work (e.g., Cuturi & Avis), highlighting distinctions: use of entropy regularization for scalability and parameterization of a continuous ground metric.  
- Clarify differences from Wang et al., especially how OT optimization is integrated into the learning loop and why that matters for training.
