- Proposes supervised metric learning built on word-movers distance, applicable to transport distances after linear projection.  
- Learns both a linear projection and document-specific word weighting (re-weighted and renormalized word frequencies).  
- Presents an efficient optimization method to learn the parameterized metric.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and shows strong performance vs many baselines on kNN text classification.  
- Extends word-movers distance in a technically useful way with demonstrated effectiveness.  
- Limitation: earth-movers distance initialized with a word-centroid metric learning performs similarly to the full proposed method (Table 3), questioning added benefit.  
- Missing evaluation: compare the word-centroid embedding baseline with all tested metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: evaluate the impact of word-specific weights and projection matrix A in isolation.  
- Originality mainly in the optimization details (Lines 156–175); linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a limited contribution.  
- Paper is well written and readable.  
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous citation at Line 152 ([1,7,8] unclear), Equation 11 likely needs stars on alpha/beta, and explicitly recall that Line 170 refers to Sinkhorn scaling mentioned earlier.
