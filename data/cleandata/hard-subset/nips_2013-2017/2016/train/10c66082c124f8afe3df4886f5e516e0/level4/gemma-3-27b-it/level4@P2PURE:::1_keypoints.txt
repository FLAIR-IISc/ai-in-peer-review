- Introduces supervised metric learning based on word-movers distance, generalizing to transport distances via a linear projection.  
- Learns both a projection and word-specific weights that re-weight and renormalize document word frequencies.  
- Proposes an efficient optimization method for learning the parameterized metric.  
- Evaluates extensively on document classification using kNN with the learned metric and many baselines.  
- Technically interesting and useful extension of WMD, but potential impact may be limited.  
- An earth-movers distance initialized with metric learning from the “word centroid distance” matches full-method performance (Table 3), reducing the apparent gain.  
- Missing comparison of the word centroid embedding (weighted mean) against all tested metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Lacks an ablation study to quantify the separate contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Originality lies chiefly in the optimization procedure; linear metric learning within EMD is a relatively modest contribution.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and pleasant to read.  
- Minor clarification needed: ambiguous reference “the authors” on Line 152—specify which citation ([1,7,8]).  
- Minor correction: Equation 11’s α and β should likely include asterisks.  
- Suggest explicitly referencing the Sinkhorn scaling (introduced Line 56) around Line 170 for clarity.
