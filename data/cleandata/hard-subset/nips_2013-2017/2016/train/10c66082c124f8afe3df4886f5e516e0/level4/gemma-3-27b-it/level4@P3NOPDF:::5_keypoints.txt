- Introduces learned word-specific weights to make WMD supervised, addressing a key limitation.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for WMD.  
- Strong empirical evaluation across eight datasets and 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art average performance.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-written with rigorous mathematical exposition.  
- Thorough and diverse empirical comparison to existing methods.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental relative to prior WMD work.  
- Comparison to World Centroid Distance (WCD) appears tangential and potentially confusing.  
- Insufficient discussion of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ and its impact.  
- Primary concern: λ can dominate behavior—unclear whether chosen λ yields Wasserstein-like results or entropic Sinkhorn behavior.  
- Lacks ablation/analysis varying λ to show effects on performance and computational cost.  
- Recommendation: accept pending better discussion of λ and clarification of WCD relevance.  
- Questions for authors: rationale for chosen λ values, any λ ablation studies, and motivation for including the WCD comparison.
