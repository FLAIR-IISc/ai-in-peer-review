- Proposes Supervised WMD: learn linear transform A and word re-weighting vector w for WMD, optimized with a stochastic leave-one-out k-NN/NCA objective.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and demonstrates strong command of related literature.  
- Empirical evaluation is extensive and thorough, comparing S-WMD to many baselines across eight datasets.  
- Motivation for word re-weighting w is unclear; it could distort document content and needs analysis showing it preserves document fidelity and whether it mainly suppresses stop-words or learns task-specific importances.  
- Contribution may appear incremental (combining WMD and NCA); authors should emphasize specific technical challenges overcome, notably the efficient gradient approximation for WMD.  
- Clarify application to new documents: how is w used at test time and is it a global vector applied to unseen documents?  
- Terminology issue: “semantic differences” is imprecise—recommend “task-specific similarity” or “class-discriminative distance.”  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing re-weighting motivation and clarifying novelty and test-time application.
