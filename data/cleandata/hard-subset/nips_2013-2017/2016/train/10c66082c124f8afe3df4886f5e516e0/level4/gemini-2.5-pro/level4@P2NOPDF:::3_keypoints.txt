- Introduces a method to learn WMD parameters by parameterizing the EMD ground metric with a Mahalanobis distance (linear transform of embedding space).  
- Also learns document histogram weights and trains both sets of parameters with an NCA-style objective, with derived gradients.  
- Empirical results are strong and validate the practical utility of the approach; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Core idea is simple and elegant but non-trivial to implement and validate.  
- Paper needs clearer and more rigorous mathematical presentation; some notations and computations are imprecise.  
- Clarify and motivate the use of both regularized (entropic) and unregularized EMD formulations.  
- Strongly recommend including pseudocode that outlines the full training algorithm for clarity.  
- Related work section should better delineate contributions versus prior EMD/OT learning (e.g., Cuturi, Avis).  
- Emphasize distinctions: use of entropy regularization for scalability and parameterization of a continuous ground metric via linear map rather than a discrete cost matrix.  
- Clarify technical/optimization differences with Wang et al., highlighting the robustness advantage of the proposed optimization scheme.  
- Acknowledge parallels between the histogram reweighting (Eq. 5) and earlier work (Lebanon; Le & Cuturi) and discuss connections.  
- Minor inconsistency: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); recommend framing WMD as p-Wasserstein and specifying p.  
- Request experimental ablation to assess the contribution of the learned histogram weight vector w (performance with vs. without learning w).
