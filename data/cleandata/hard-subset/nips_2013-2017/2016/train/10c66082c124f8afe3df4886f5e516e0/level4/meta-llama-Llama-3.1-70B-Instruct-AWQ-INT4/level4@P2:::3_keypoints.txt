- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised document distance that learns a generalized Euclidean metric in word embedding space plus a word importance vector.  
- Incorporates supervision into Word Mover’s Distance, filling a prior gap.  
- Builds on and relates to BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA and metric learning methods (NCA, ITML).  
- Uses a relaxed optimal transport formulation and probabilistic filtering to substantially reduce computation.  
- Introduces clever initialization and batch gradient descent to speed up training.  
- Method is technically sound with a clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Achieves strong empirical results: lowest kNN test error on 5/8 datasets and best average versus 26 methods.  
- t-SNE visualization shows the learned metric captures semantic document relationships.  
- Strengths: simplicity, straightforward idea, and convincing experimental evidence.  
- Requests more rigorous mathematical derivations, clearer notation, and an algorithmic box for clarity.  
- Minor inconsistency: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean—needs clarification.  
- Asks for more insight into the learned weight vector w and its impact on results.  
- Suggests clearer differentiation from related work (e.g., Cuturi/Avis, Wang et al.), emphasizing entropy regularization and cost-function differences.  
- Overall recommendation: support for acceptance; rating 8/10, confidence 8/10.
