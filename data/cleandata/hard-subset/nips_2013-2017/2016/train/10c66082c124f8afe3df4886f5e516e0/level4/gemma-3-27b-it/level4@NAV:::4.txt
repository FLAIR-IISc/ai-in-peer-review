This paper presents a supervised learning approach to refine document distance estimation building upon word mover’s distance (WMD). WMD, inspired by earth mover’s distance, calculates optimal transport between document word distributions to quantify their dissimilarity, typically using word2vec embeddings for word-level distances. The authors propose learning both (1) a linear transformation of these word embeddings and (2) a reweighted word distribution to enhance separation between documents of differing classes. 

The manuscript is well-written and readily comprehensible, and demonstrates a strong grasp of relevant prior work through a comprehensive literature review. Extensive experimental evaluation is provided across multiple novel datasets. However, the rationale behind the proposed formulation requires further elaboration, and the overall contribution feels incremental. 

Specifically, the motivation for reweighting the word distribution using the proposed weights, *w*, is unclear. The potential for this reweighting to substantially alter the document's representation raises concerns; justification demonstrating how this reweighting improves performance *without* fundamentally changing the document is needed. Furthermore, while applying a Nearest Centroid Algorithm (NCA) objective to learn a linear transformation *A* and reweighting *w* represents a supervised extension of WMD, it does not constitute a particularly novel or impactful advancement. 

Practical considerations also need addressing. The method for determining the reweighting *w* for entirely new, unseen documents is not described. Finally, the use of "semantic difference" in the abstract is ambiguous. The connection to document labels is clear, but the term appears to conflate authorship or topical variations with semantic distinctions, and clarification is requested. 

Despite these concerns, the paper is presented clearly and I do not oppose acceptance pending satisfactory responses to these questions and a stronger articulation of the work’s significance.
