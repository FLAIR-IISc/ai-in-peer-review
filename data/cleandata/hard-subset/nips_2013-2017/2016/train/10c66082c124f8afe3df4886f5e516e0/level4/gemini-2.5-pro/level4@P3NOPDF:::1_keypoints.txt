- Paper introduces S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear projection of word embeddings and word-specific importance weights via an alternating optimization, showing improved k-NN classification.
- Strength: Timely and useful extension of the influential WMD to supervised metric learning for text.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance—comprehensive experiments show S-WMD outperforms WMD and many baselines on k-NN text classification benchmarks.
- Strength: Clear and well-written presentation with precise technical exposition.
- Weakness: Full end-to-end optimization offers only marginal improvement over a simpler WMD-MD baseline (good initialization seems to account for most gains).
- Weakness: Missing ablation study to disentangle contributions of the projection matrix A vs. the word-specific importance weights (need experiments: only A; only weights).
- Weakness: Missing important baselines—should compare word centroid document vectors combined with standard metric learners (e.g., ITML, LMNN, NCA) to isolate the contribution of the transport-based distance vs. the learning algorithm.
- Minor: Clarify which cited authors are meant in the phrase “The authors show…” (Line 152).
- Minor: Consider using stars to denote optimal dual variables (alpha*, beta*) in Equation 11 for consistency.
- Minor: State explicitly that the iterative optimization is an instance of the Sinkhorn scaling algorithm to improve readability and connection to the introduction.
