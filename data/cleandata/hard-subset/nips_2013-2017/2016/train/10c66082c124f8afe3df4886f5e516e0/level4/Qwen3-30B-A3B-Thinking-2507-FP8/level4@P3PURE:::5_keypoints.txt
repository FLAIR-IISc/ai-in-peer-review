- Extends Word Mover’s Distance for supervised text classification by learning word-specific weights.  
- Introduces Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for the Wasserstein distance.  
- Empirically evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear and thorough mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty is limited — the work is incremental relative to foundational WMD rather than a major conceptual advance.  
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance is tangential and confusing, detracting from the main contribution.  
- Treatment of the entropic regularization parameter λ is inadequate and underdiscussed.  
- λ crucially affects performance: large λ approximates Wasserstein (with slower convergence), while typical small-λ Sinkhorn regimes behave differently.  
- It is unclear which λ regime the authors used, creating ambiguity that undermines interpretation of results and claimed advantages.
