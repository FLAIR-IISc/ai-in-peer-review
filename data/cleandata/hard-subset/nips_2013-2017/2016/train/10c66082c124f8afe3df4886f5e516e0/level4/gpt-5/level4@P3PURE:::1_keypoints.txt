- Summary: Proposes supervised metric learning for WMD that learns a linear projection and per-word reweighting with an efficient optimization, evaluated via kNN on document classification tasks.

- Strength: Technically sound extension of WMD with demonstrated gains on multiple kNN text classification tasks.

- Strength: Applicability to any transport distance with squared Euclidean costs after a linear projection.

- Strength: Clear formulation and an efficient optimization procedure.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments and strong writing quality.

- Weakness: Limited potential impact—a centroid-based initialization (“word centroid distance”) plus metric learning gives similar performance to the full learned WMD.

- Weakness: Missing baseline—Table 3 omits word centroid embeddings combined with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).

- Weakness: Missing ablation—no evaluation isolating the contributions of per-word weights versus the projection matrix A.

- Originality: Main novelty is the optimization strategy; integrating linear metric learning into EMD is interesting but relatively incremental given the strong centroid baseline.

- Minor: Clarify which paper “The authors” refers to (Line 152: [1], [7], or [8]?).

- Minor: Equation 11 should consistently denote optimal dual variables (α*, β*).

- Minor: Around Line 170, explicitly state that the referenced procedure is the Sinkhorn scaling.

- Overall: Well-written and empirically solid, but incremental; adding centroid+ITML/LMNN/NCA baselines and ablations on weights vs A would strengthen the claims.
