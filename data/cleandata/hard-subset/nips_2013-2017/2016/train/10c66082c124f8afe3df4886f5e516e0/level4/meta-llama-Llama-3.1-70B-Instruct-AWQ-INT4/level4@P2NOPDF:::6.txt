The paper proposes a novel method for metric learning on textual documents, building upon the existing "Word Mover's Distance" approach. The original Word Mover's Distance is an unsupervised method that measures the distance between word embeddings. In contrast, this paper extends the approach to a supervised setting, where the supervision is provided in the form of document class labels. The authors plan to make the code (in Matlab) publicly available.

The authors have provided clear and detailed responses to my questions, which I appreciate. They propose learning two components to define the distance: a linear transformation A (a traditional approach) and word importance weights w. I would have liked a more in-depth explanation of the intuition behind Equation (5), which defines the usage of w, and a clearer understanding of which component contributes to the reported improvements.

The method is evaluated on a classification task in the experimental section. While I understand that assessing a distance metric can be challenging, it would be interesting to compare the performance of a simple classifier on the same datasets for reference.

I have a few questions regarding the paper. Firstly, I did not understand when Equation (2) is used, as the lambda value seems to be different from the one in line 170. Secondly, I would appreciate clarification on the claim that the method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" (line 316).

I also have some minor comments. On line 257, the text mentions "on the right we also show the average error across datasets," but I could not find this information. Is it supposed to be in Table 3? In Table 2, what is the difference between the "n" and "ne" columns? Additionally, the value of k for kNN classification is not clearly defined. Is this value dependent on the dataset or the method?
