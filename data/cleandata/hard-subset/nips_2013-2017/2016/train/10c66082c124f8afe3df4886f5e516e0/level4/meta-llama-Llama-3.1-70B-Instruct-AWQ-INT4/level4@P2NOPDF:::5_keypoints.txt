- Paper extends Word Mover's Distance by adding supervised, word-specific weights.
- Main proposal: use Sinkhorn (entropic) distance as a computationally efficient proxy for Wasserstein distance.
- Empirical evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines shows best average performance.
- Manuscript is well-written with clear, in-depth mathematical exposition.
- Limited novelty relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance work is a concern.
- Inclusion of references to "World Centroid Distance" is confusing and may be irrelevant.
- Discussion of the regularization parameter lambda is inadequate.
- Lambda can dominate equation (2) and materially affect results in practice.
- Theoretical point: large lambda approximates pure Wasserstein (with slower convergence), while small lambda yields faster convergence but deviates from Wasserstein behavior.
- Missing clarification on whether the chosen lambda produces behavior closer to pure Wasserstein or to the Sinkhorn (entropic) distance.
