- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that jointly learns an affine embedding transform A and a word-importance vector w.  
- Technical trick: Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD gradients, making learning efficient and differentiable.  
- Training objective: Optimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification loss inspired by Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA).  
- Empirical results: Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines and reports state-of-the-art performance across most tasks.  
- Strength — novelty/significance: Well-motivated extension of WMD to incorporate supervision with a novel joint A and w learning scheme.  
- Strength — technical quality: Clever, tractable solution via Sinkhorn approximation and sensible WCD+NCA initialization.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is very well written, organized, and easy to follow.  
- Strength — experimental rigor: Thorough comparisons across many baselines and datasets supporting the claims.  
- Weakness — marginal gains: Full S-WMD shows only marginal improvement over the simpler initialization (S-WMD init./S-WCD), questioning practical impact.  
- Weakness — missing ablation: No experiments isolating the contributions of A versus w (e.g., A only or w only).  
- Weakness — missing baselines: Lacks comparisons to standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN) applied to WCD representations.  
- Suggestions: Add ablation study for A and w, include ITML/LMNN baselines on WCD, and address minor clarifications (subgradient reference, consistent α*/β* notation, state that the alternating fixed-point is Sinkhorn scaling).
