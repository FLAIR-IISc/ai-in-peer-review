- Paper proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transform A and word-importance weights w; authors will release MATLAB code.  
- Reviewer appreciates the authors’ careful responses which clarified earlier questions.  
- Request clearer intuition and explanation for Eq. (5) defining how w is used.  
- Request an ablation or analysis disentangling the contributions of A versus w to reported performance gains.  
- Experiments use a classification task; reviewer suggests comparing against a simple classifier on the same datasets or justifying the experimental choice.  
- Clarify whether the two mentions of lambda (in Eq. (2) and around line 170) refer to the same hyperparameter or different roles.  
- Provide empirical or theoretical evidence for the claim that the method is “particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words” (line 316).  
- Fix or clarify the reference to “average error across datasets” mentioned at line 257—where should this appear?  
- Explain the difference between “n” and “ne” in Table 2.  
- Specify how k is selected for kNN classification: dataset-dependent, method-dependent, fixed, and whether a validation set was used.  
- Overall: supervised WMD via learning A and w is promising but needs clearer Eq. (5) intuition, component-wise attribution of improvements, and the requested clarifications.
