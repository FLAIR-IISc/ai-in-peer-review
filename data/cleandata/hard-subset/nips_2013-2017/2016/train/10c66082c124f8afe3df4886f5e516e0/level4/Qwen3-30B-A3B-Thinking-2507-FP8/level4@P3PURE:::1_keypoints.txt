- Proposes a supervised metric-learning framework built on WMD that jointly learns a linear projection matrix and word-specific re-weighting, with an efficient optimization and kNN classification experiments.  
- Method generalizes to any transport distance derived from squared Euclidean distances after linear projection.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on kNN-based text classification across multiple datasets and baselines.  

- Major concern: EMD with metric learning initialized by the word-centroid distance achieves comparable results, suggesting only marginal gains from the full method (Table 3).  
- Missing baseline: evaluation of word-centroid embeddings combined with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) is not reported.  
- Missing ablation: no analysis isolating the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  

- Novelty: the proposed optimization strategy is new, but the overall technical contribution is relatively modest.  
- Presentation: the paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.  
- Minor clarifications requested: specify which papers are meant at Line 152 (e.g., [1,7,8]); confirm α and β notation in Equation 11 (asterisks?); explicitly connect the Sinkhorn scaling referenced earlier to the optimization procedure.
