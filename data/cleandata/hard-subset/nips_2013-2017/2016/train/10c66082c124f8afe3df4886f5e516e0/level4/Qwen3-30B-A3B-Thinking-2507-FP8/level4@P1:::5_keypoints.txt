- Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), extending WMD to incorporate label information for better document classification.
- Learns a linear transformation of the word embedding space and word-specific importance weights to align distances with class labels.
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance as an efficient entropic-regularized proxy for the Wasserstein distance to avoid cubic WMD complexity.
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains over 26 baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, metric learning methods) across eight text classification datasets.
- Provides thorough mathematical foundations and a detailed derivation of gradients enabling efficient optimization.
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and technically rigorous, making the complex optimization accessible to readers.
- Includes sufficient implementation details and hyperparameter choices (e.g., λ=10) to aid reproducibility.
- Properly situates work within metric learning and optimal transport literature and addresses limitations of unsupervised WMD.
- Notes that S-WMD performs best on datasets with shorter documents where word vectors are more informative.
- Identifies sensitivity to initialization (e.g., S-WMD underperforms WMD on CLASSIC due to initialization issues).
- Main weakness: inadequate theoretical/discussion treatment of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ and its effect on approximation quality.
- Secondary weakness: potentially confusing reference and comparison between WCD and S-WCD; needs clearer distinction and role as initialization.
- Originality: meaningful and practical extension of WMD by applying supervised metric learning in the word embedding space with an efficient Sinkhorn approximation.
- Significance: advances document distance metrics with a practical, supervised optimal-transport-based approach relevant to retrieval and classification.
- Recommendation: acceptance based on strong technical contribution, empirical results, and clear writing.
