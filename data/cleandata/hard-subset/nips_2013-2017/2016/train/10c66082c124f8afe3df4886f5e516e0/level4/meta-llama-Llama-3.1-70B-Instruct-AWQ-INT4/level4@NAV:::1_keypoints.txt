- Proposes a supervised metric learning method that extends the word-movers distance via a learnable linear projection.  
- Method is applicable to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances after linear projection.  
- Learns both a projection matrix and a document-specific word weighting scheme that re-weights and re-normalizes word frequencies.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization technique to learn the parameterized metric.  
- Experimental results show effectiveness on document classification using kNN compared to multiple baselines.  
- Reviewer notes limited potential impact because EMD with metric learning initialized by the word-centroid distance achieves similar performance (Table 3).  
- Suggests the main contributions may not substantially improve over the initialization-based baseline.  
- Points out missing evaluations: apply word-centroid embeddings with all metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) in Table 3.  
- Recommends an ablation study to measure the individual contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Views originality as primarily coming from the optimization method; linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a limited standalone contribution.  
- Paper is well-written; requests minor clarifications (identify authors in Line 152, mark alpha/beta in Eq.11, explicitly recall Sinkhorn scaling near Line 170).
