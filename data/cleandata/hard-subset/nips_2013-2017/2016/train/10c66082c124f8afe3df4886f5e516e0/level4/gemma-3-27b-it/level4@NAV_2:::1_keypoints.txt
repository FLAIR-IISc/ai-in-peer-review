- Proposes supervised metric learning extending Word Mover’s Distance by learning a linear projection and task-specific re-weighting of word frequencies for transport distances based on squared Euclidean after projection.  
- Presents an efficient optimization procedure for learning the parameterized metric.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on several document classification datasets using kNN with the learned metric, outperforming many baselines.  
- The main technical contribution may be limited because earth-mover’s distance initialized with a word-centroid distance (S-WCD) achieves comparable performance to the full learned metric.  
- Missing ablation study: paper does not evaluate the individual contributions of the word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Missing comparison: lacks evaluation of the word centroid embedding (weighted mean of word embeddings) with the metric learning baselines (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Originality mainly in the optimization method (Lines 156–175); linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but incremental.  
- Paper is very well written and easy to read.  
- Minor clarifications needed: specify “The authors” reference (Line 152), check Equation 11 for α and β notation, and explicitly recall Sinkhorn scaling near Line 170.  
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound extension of WMD with strong empirical results and practical optimization.  
- Argument against acceptance: modest gain over simple S-WCD initialization and missing key ablations and baseline comparisons.
