- Proposes a novel supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transformation A and word importance weights w.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and notation.  
- Intuition for the word-importance weights w in Eq. (5) is unclear; need to know whether A or w drives improvements.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: comparison to 26 baselines across eight document corpora.  
- Suggestion to compare against simple classifier baselines to better contextualize metric performance.  
- Related work and metric-learning background are well-covered.  
- Claim that the method is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words lacks sufficient justification.  
- Minor notation issue: lambda in Eq. (2) appears inconsistent with lambda referenced in ln170 and should be clarified.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original extension of WMD, and significant empirical evaluation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, extensive experiments, and strong related-work discussion.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear role/intuition of w, unclear lambda notation, and weak support for the forgiveness claim.
