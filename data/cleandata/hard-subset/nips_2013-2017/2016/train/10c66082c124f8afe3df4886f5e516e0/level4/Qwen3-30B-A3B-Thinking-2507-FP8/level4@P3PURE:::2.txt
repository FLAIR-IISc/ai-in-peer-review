

This paper presents a well-motivated extension of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) that employs supervised learning to optimize weights for the distance metric, enabling domain-specific adaptation. The approach is methodologically sound, clearly articulated, and provides precisely the necessary technical details for reproducibility. The experimental evaluation is exceptionally thorough, encompassing multiple classification datasets, a comprehensive set of baseline methods, and diverse feature representations. The proposed method consistently and significantly outperforms all competing approaches across the evaluated benchmarks. This represents a strong contribution with clear empirical validation, and I strongly recommend acceptance for presentation at NIPS.
