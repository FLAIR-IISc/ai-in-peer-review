- Proposes a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform A and per-word importance weights w via an NCA-style objective.
- Paper is clearly written, contains a thorough related-work review, and provides comprehensive experiments on multiple datasets.
- Motivation for reweighting the word distribution (w) is weak — it can fundamentally alter document content and needs justification or analysis of learned weights to show it doesn't distort semantics.
- Technical contribution seems limited and a natural/straightforward extension of WMD rather than a novel breakthrough.
- Unclear how the method generalizes at test time: how are weights assigned to words not seen in the training vocabulary?
- The phrase "semantic difference" is ambiguous and should be clarified, since the paper uses it to mean label differences (e.g., authorship/topic), not necessarily semantic meaning.
- Overall sensible and well-written; reviewer would support acceptance if authors adequately address the motivation, novelty, and generalization concerns.
