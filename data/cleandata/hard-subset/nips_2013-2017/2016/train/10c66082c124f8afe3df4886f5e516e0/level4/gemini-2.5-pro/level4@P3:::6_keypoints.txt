- Proposal: S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that learns an affine transform A and word-importance weights w, trained by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective using a differentiable Sinkhorn approximation.

- Strength — Significance/Originality: Addresses WMD's lack of supervision with a novel task-adaptive metric learning approach (learns A and w).

- Strength — Technical Quality: Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain efficient, differentiable gradients; sensible optimization and S-WCD initialization.

- Strength — Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art on most datasets, with visualizations and timing analysis.

- Strength — Reproducibility: Authors will release code.

- Weakness — Ablation Study: Missing experiments isolating the effects of A-only and w-only to quantify each component's contribution.

- Weakness — Word-weighting Intuition: Need better motivation for applying w via Hadamard product and renormalization versus other formulations (e.g., modifying transport cost).

- Weakness — Baseline Comparison: Lacks comparison to strong non-metric classifiers (e.g., Linear SVM on TF-IDF) to contextualize kNN performance.

- Minor — Lambda Clarity: Clarify that the same lambda is used for Sinkhorn regularization and in the dual derivation, and comment on its role.

- Minor — WMD Property Justification: Briefly argue or show how learned A preserves or enhances WMD's semantic matching of conceptually similar words.

- Minor — Table/Text Inconsistencies: Fix wording about "average error" vs "Avg Rank" in Table 2 and correct "ne" typo to "n=".

- Minor — Experimental Detail: Report the k used in kNN experiments and whether k was tuned per method/dataset.
