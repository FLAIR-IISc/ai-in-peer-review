This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method for learning a task-specific document distance metric. It builds directly upon the recently introduced Word Mover’s Distance (WMD), which leverages pretrained word embeddings to calculate the "transportation cost" between the words of two documents. While WMD has shown state-of-the-art performance in unsupervised settings, it lacks a mechanism to incorporate label information. The authors address this gap by introducing a supervised learning framework that minimizes the leave-one-out k-Nearest Neighbor (kNN) classification error.

The core contributions are twofold: the model learns 1) an affine transformation of the word embedding space to better align word distances with the classification task, and 2) a word-importance vector to up-weight words that are more discriminative for the given task. A key technical challenge is that computing the gradient of the WMD objective is computationally prohibitive, as it requires solving a nested optimization problem. The authors cleverly circumvent this by replacing the exact WMD with its entropically regularized version (the Sinkhorn distance), which provides an arbitrarily close, differentiable approximation that can be computed much more efficiently. The model is optimized using batch gradient descent with a practical initialization strategy based on the Word Centroid Distance (WCD).

**Strengths:**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a complete, well-executed piece of research. The motivation is clear, and the proposed solution is elegant and practical. The use of the Sinkhorn distance to enable efficient gradient computation is a significant technical contribution that makes the entire approach feasible. The experimental evaluation is exceptionally thorough, comparing S-WMD against 26 competitive baselines across 8 diverse datasets. The authors are careful in their evaluation, using Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning and reporting standard errors, which lends strong support to their claims.

*   **Clarity:** The paper is extremely well-written, organized, and easy to follow. The background section provides all the necessary context on word embeddings, WMD, and Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA). The proposed method is laid out logically, with clear derivations for the loss function and gradients. The inclusion of Algorithm 1, visualizations of the learned embeddings (Figure 1), and a word cloud of important words (Figure 2) greatly enhances the reader's understanding.

*   **Originality:** The work is a novel and significant extension of WMD. While the idea of learning a ground metric for the Earth Mover's Distance has been explored in other domains (e.g., computer vision), this paper is the first to successfully formulate and solve this problem for document classification in the WMD framework. The combination of learning an embedding transformation and a word-reweighting vector, optimized via a relaxed transport problem, constitutes a novel and impactful contribution.

*   **Significance:** The results are highly significant. S-WMD achieves state-of-the-art or near state-of-the-art performance on all 8 datasets, demonstrably advancing the capability of distance-based document classification. Given the popularity and strong performance of the original WMD, this supervised extension is likely to be widely adopted by both researchers and practitioners. The work provides a powerful new tool for text classification and retrieval tasks where labeled data is available. The public release of the code further increases its potential impact.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**

The paper is very strong, and the weaknesses are minor. The following are primarily suggestions for further strengthening the manuscript:

1.  **Contextualizing Training Time:** Table 3 shows the training time for S-WMD, noting that it is efficient. To make this claim more concrete, it would be beneficial to include the training times for the main supervised baselines (e.g., running NCA, LMNN, or ITML on the LSI or TF-IDF representations) in the same table. This would provide a direct comparison and better contextualize the computational cost of the proposed method.

2.  **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The authors mention that they set the regularization parameter λ=10 as a trade-off between speed and accuracy. While the results are excellent, a brief discussion or a small experiment in the supplementary material showing the sensitivity of the model to this parameter would add value.

3.  **Performance on Specific Datasets:** The authors honestly note that S-WMD is not the top-performing method on a few datasets (e.g., BBCSPORT, CLASSIC). They provide a plausible hypothesis related to long document lengths for BBCSPORT. For the CLASSIC dataset, they suggest the squared Euclidean distance might be suboptimal. A slightly more detailed discussion on these cases could provide further insight into the method's operating characteristics and potential limitations. This is not a major flaw, but expanding on it would be interesting.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Proposes a novel, well-motivated, and significant extension to the highly successful Word Mover's Distance.
*   Presents a key technical innovation—using the Sinkhorn distance to create an efficient and differentiable approximation—that makes learning feasible.
*   The experimental evaluation is extensive, rigorous, and demonstrates state-of-the-art performance across a wide range of tasks.
*   The paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and easy to understand.
*   The work is likely to have a high impact on the fields of metric learning and natural language processing.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   There are no significant arguments against accepting this paper. The minor weaknesses identified are suggestions for improvement rather than fundamental flaws.

In summary, this is an excellent paper that presents a solid contribution with strong theoretical motivation and compelling empirical results. It addresses an important problem and provides a powerful, practical solution. I strongly recommend its acceptance for presentation at the conference.
