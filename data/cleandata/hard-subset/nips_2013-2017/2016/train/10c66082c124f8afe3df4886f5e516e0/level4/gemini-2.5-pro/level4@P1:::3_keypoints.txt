- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learn a linear transform of word embeddings (Mahalanobis ground metric) and per-word importance weights to make WMD task-specific.  
- Optimizes parameters with a Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) loss approximating leave-one-out k-NN classification error.  
- Uses entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn distance) to obtain a fast, differentiable approximation of WMD and its gradients, making optimization tractable.  
- Evaluated on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state- or near-state-of-the-art performance.  
- Strength: elegant, well-motivated incorporation of supervision into the WMD framework.  
- Strength: thorough and convincing experimental evaluation demonstrating practical utility.  
- Strength: key technical contribution is using entropy regularization to enable efficient gradient-based learning.  
- Clarity issue: need to clarify relationship between exact WMD (LP) and its entropy-regularized approximation and implications for optimizing the original distance.  
- Consistency issue: ground distance definition is inconsistent (Euclidean vs squared Euclidean); recommend framing WMD as a p-Wasserstein distance and sticking to one formulation.  
- Presentation: Algorithm 1 needs more detail (reference specific equations used in gradient updates) for clarity.  
- Prior work/novelty: must acknowledge related techniques for histogram re-weighting (e.g., Guy Lebanon) and works on learning EMD ground metrics (e.g., Cuturi & Avis, Le & Cuturi, Wang et al.), and better delineate differences.  
- Experimental suggestion: include an ablation study separating contributions of the transform A and word-importance vector w to quantify each component’s impact.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, well-executed paper with valuable technical and empirical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor clarifications and expanded related-work and ablation analyses.
