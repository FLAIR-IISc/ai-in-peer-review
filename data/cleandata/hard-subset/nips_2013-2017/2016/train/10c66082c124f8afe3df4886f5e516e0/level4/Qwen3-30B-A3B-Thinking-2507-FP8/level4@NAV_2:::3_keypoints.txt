- Proposes learning WMD parameters by parameterizing the EMD ground metric via a Mahalanobis distance on word embeddings and jointly learning histogram reweighting coefficients, with gradients for NCA-type losses and strong empirical validation on eight text classification tasks.  
- Strengths: conceptually simple yet non-trivial approach with thorough experiments showing consistent improvements over 26 baselines.  
- Improve treatment of the interplay between regularized and non-regularized OT formulations.  
- Improve mathematical rigor and clarity in derivations and notation, which are currently imprecise.  
- Add clear algorithmic pseudocode to elucidate the proposed method.  
- Technical inconsistency: Eq.(1) uses Euclidean distance while Eq.(6) uses squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); better to refer to WMD as p-Wasserstein with p as a hyperparameter.  
- Claim of novelty for histogram reweighting (Eq.(5)) is incorrect — similar ideas appeared in Guy Lebanon’s "Metric Learning for Text Documents" and Le & Cuturi’s Aitchison-transform work.  
- Provide experiments isolating the contribution of the weight vector w to final metric performance and explain why it helps.  
- Related work needs clearer differentiation: emphasize entropy regularization and continuous linear map parameterization advantages, and clarify differences from Wang et al. (their OT optimization runs once and differs substantially).
