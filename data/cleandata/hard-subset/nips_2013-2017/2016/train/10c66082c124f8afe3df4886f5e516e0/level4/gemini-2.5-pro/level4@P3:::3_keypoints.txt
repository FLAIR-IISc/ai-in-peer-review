- Paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a task-specific document distance that learns an affine transform of word embeddings and a word-importance vector.  
- S-WMD frames document distance via WMD (optimal transport) but introduces supervision by learning matrix A (Mahalanobis transform) and weight vector w (re-weighted bag-of-words).  
- Training minimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification loss akin to Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA).  
- Main technical contribution: efficiently differentiating through WMD by using the entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance as a differentiable, tractable proxy.  
- Additional efficiency from batching and nearest-neighbor filtering to make training practical.  
- Extensive experiments on eight text-classification datasets show S-WMD outperforms 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art kNN errors on most benchmarks.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance with thorough comparisons, making a compelling case for S-WMD as a leading document distance metric.  
- Strength: novel and effective synthesis of WMD geometry, supervised metric learning, and regularized optimal transport.  
- Strength: solves a significant technical challenge (differentiating through WMD) using Sinkhorn regularization for tractability.  
- Strength: clear and intuitive motivation for learning task-specific distances and word importance.  
- Weakness: notational/mathematical precision issues—ground metric framing should explicitly state p-Wasserstein (p=2) and squared-distance usage in supervised loss.  
- Weakness: needs clearer articulation of the trade-offs between exact WMD and entropy-regularized approximation, including the role of hyperparameter λ.  
- Weakness: prior work positioning incomplete—histogram re-weighting has precedents (e.g., Lebanon) and related metric-learning for histograms (Le & Cuturi) should be acknowledged.  
- Weakness: should more explicitly differentiate from prior EMD-ground-metric learning (e.g., Cuturi & Avis) and clarify improvements over methods like Wang et al.  
- Weakness: lacks an ablation study disentangling contributions of A versus w (e.g., A-only and w-only variants).  
- Weakness: presentation could be improved with a formal algorithm box summarizing training (initialization, batching, gradient computation, updates) to aid reproducibility.  
- Final recommendation: accept; overall strengths outweigh weaknesses and suggested revisions would further strengthen the paper.  
- Arguments for acceptance: state-of-the-art results, elegant synthesis of ideas, and a practical solution to differentiating through WMD.  
- Arguments against acceptance: some components aren’t entirely novel and the paper needs improved mathematical precision and ablation evidence.
