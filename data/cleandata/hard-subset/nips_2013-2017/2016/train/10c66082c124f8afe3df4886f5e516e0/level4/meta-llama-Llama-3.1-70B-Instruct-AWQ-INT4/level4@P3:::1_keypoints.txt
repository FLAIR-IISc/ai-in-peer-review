- Proposes S-WMD: supervised metric learning extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear projection and word-specific frequency weights.  
- S-WMD applies to transport distances based on squared Euclidean distances after linear projection.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization method for learning the parameterized metric.  
- Experiments are thorough, comparing to many baselines on multiple document classification datasets using kNN.  
- Results show S-WMD is effective on several kNN text classification tasks.  
- Potential impact limited because WMD with a "word centroid distance" initialization performs similarly to full S-WMD learning.  
- Missing evaluation: no ablation showing relative importance of learned word weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Missing comparison: evaluate word-centroid embeddings with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Originality mainly in the optimization approach (Section 3.1); linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a modest contribution.  
- Paper is very well written, clear, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Paper is technically sound; claims supported by theory and experiments.  
- Minor clarifications needed: specify reference at Line 152, add stars on alpha/beta in Eq.11, and explicitly note Sinkhorn scaling around Line 170.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, thorough experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited additional benefit over centroid-based initialization and missing important comparisons/ablations.
