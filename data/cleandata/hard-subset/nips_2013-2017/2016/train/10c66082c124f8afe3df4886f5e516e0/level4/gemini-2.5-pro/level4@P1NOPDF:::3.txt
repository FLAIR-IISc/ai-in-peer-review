This paper proposes a method for learning the parameters of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), which is an application of the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) to text documents where the ground distance is derived from word embeddings. The authors parameterize the EMD ground metric using a Mahalanobis distance between word vectors. In addition, they propose learning coefficients to reweight the word frequency histograms. The paper derives the gradients for Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA) style loss functions to solve this learning problem and presents compelling experimental evidence for the method's effectiveness.

Overall, this paper presents a valuable contribution by effectively combining several existing techniques. The core idea is simple and intuitive, yet its implementation and empirical validation are non-trivial. The strong experimental results are a key strength of the paper, and I support its acceptance.

Major points for improvement:
*   The interplay between the regularized and non-regularized formulations should be clarified.
*   The mathematical rigor could be improved, as some computations and notations are imprecise.
*   The authors should consider including a pseudocode algorithm (e.g., in an algorithm box) to provide a clearer, step-by-step overview of the proposed method.

Specific comments:
*   There appears to be an inconsistency in the definition of the cost function. In Equation 1, the cost is the Euclidean distance between word embeddings. However, in Equation 6, for the purpose of Mahalanobis metric learning, the cost becomes the squared Euclidean distance, which corresponds to the 2-Wasserstein distance. To avoid this, it would be clearer to define WMD more generally as the p-Wasserstein distance between clouds of word embeddings for a given p.

*   The histogram reweighting approach proposed in Equation 5 is not novel. It was previously introduced by Guy Lebanon in "Metric Learning for Text Documents" for the exact same context of text document classification. Related work can also be found in "Unsupervised Riemannian Metric Learning for Histograms Using Aitchison Transformations" by Le and Cuturi.

*   Could the authors provide experimental insights or an ablation study on the importance of the weight vector **w** in the learned metric? It is unclear how significant this component is to the model's overall performance.

*   The related work section could be strengthened by more clearly differentiating this work from prior art. For instance:
    *   The paper clearly builds on the work of Cuturi and Avis but introduces entropy regularization, which enables significant speed-ups and improved scalability. A further distinction is the cost function: their work uses a discrete cost, whereas this paper employs a continuous cost parameterized by a linear map. Highlighting these differences would better position the contribution.
    *   The paper also draws from Wang et al. It would be beneficial to discuss the limitations of that work which this paper overcomes. For instance, the Optimal Transport optimization in Wang et al. is performed only once and is not effective as proposed, a key issue that the iterative approach in this paper presumably solves.
