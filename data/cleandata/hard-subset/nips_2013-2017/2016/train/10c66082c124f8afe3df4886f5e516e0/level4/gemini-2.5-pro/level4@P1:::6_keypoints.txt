- Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear embedding transform A and word-importance weights w for task-specific document distance.  
- Builds on WMD by minimizing a stochastic, differentiable approximation of leave-one-out k-NN error and using a relaxed (Sinkhorn) WMD for gradient-based optimization.  
- Promises to release Matlab code for reproducibility.  
- Novelty/significance: meaningful extension of WMD to supervised metric learning tailored to classification tasks.  
- Technical soundness: uses Sinkhorn distance to obtain a differentiable, computationally efficient approximation enabling large-scale optimization.  
- Empirical evaluation: extensive comparisons (26 baselines, 8 datasets) showing consistent and often substantial improvements over unsupervised WMD and other methods.  
- Weakness: lacks an ablation study to separate the contributions of the linear transform A versus the word-importance vector w.  
- Weakness: evaluation limited to k-NN—should compare to standard supervised classifiers (e.g., SVM with TF-IDF) to contextualize performance.  
- Question: clarify the role and setting of the regularization parameter λ (is it the same in Sinkhorn and dual recovery?).  
- Question/claim: strengthen the claim that S-WMD is forgiving of syntactic variation by showing a concrete example or analysis demonstrating how A and w enhance semantic alignment.  
- Minor clarity issues: clarify whether "average error" vs "average rank" in Table 2, define column headers "n" and "ne", and state how k for k-NN was chosen/tuned for reproducibility.
