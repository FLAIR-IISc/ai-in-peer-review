- Proposes learning WMD/EMD ground metric via a Mahalanobis parameterization and learning histogram reweighting coefficients.  
- Provides gradients for NCA-type costs and presents strong, convincing experimental results.  
- Reviewer supports acceptance due to the solid empirical evidence and straightforward idea.  
- Strength: simplicity of the approach and a strong experimental section despite non-trivial implementation.  
- Improve handling of the interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations.  
- Improve mathematical rigor: clean up sloppy computations and notations.  
- Add an algorithmic box to clearly illustrate the proposed method.  
- Eq.1 vs Eq.6 inconsistency: Euclidean vs squared-Euclidean — suggest framing WMD as p-Wasserstein to avoid confusion.  
- Reweighting in Eq.5 is not novel; similar ideas appeared in Lebanon’s metric learning for text and Le/Cuturi’s work.  
- Need experiments or discussion on the importance and effect of the learned weight vector w.  
- Related work should more clearly differentiate this paper from Cuturi/Avis (use of entropy regularization, continuous vs discrete cost, linear map parametrization).  
- Clarify relation to Wang et al.: that prior work’s OT optimization was only run once and is problematic.  
- Overall clarity and flow are good; addressing the above points would strengthen the paper.
