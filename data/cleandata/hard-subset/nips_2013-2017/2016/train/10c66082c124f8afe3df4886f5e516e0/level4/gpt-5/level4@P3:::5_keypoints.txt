- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform A of word embeddings and a word-importance vector w for reweighting document histograms.  
- Trains with a stochastic leave-one-out kNN/NCA-style loss and uses entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) for smooth, efficient gradients.  
- Uses practical speedups: S-WCD initialization, minibatch training, and neighbor filtering based on WCD.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, BM25, LSI, LDA, mSDA, WCD/WMD, metric learning methods) and releases code.  
- Achieves best average rank and lowest error on 5/8 datasets, with competitive results elsewhere and improved class separation in t-SNE visualizations.  
- Technical formulation is sensible and grounded in optimal transport and metric learning; entropic regularization choice is well-motivated.  
- Empirical evaluation is broad with Bayesian hyperparameter tuning and reported training-time comparisons.  
- Need sensitivity analysis for the entropic regularization parameter λ (fixed at 10); report performance, convergence, and any annealing.  
- Require ablations to isolate contributions: “only A,” “only w,” both, and effects of neighbor set size N and batch size B.  
- Clarify ground metric choice (p=2); provide rationale and checks for p=1 or cosine-based costs.  
- Provide derivation/citation and precise constants for mapping Sinkhorn scaling vectors (u,v) to approximate dual potentials (α,β) and discuss approximation error vs λ.  
- Confirm fairness/stability: ensure PCA to 200 dimensions for some baselines does not disadvantage methods sensitive to dimensionality reduction.  
- Writing is clear and structured, but unify notation (vocabulary size vs histogram dimensionality) and fix typos (e.g., “imporance,” “Sinhorn”).  
- Clarify roles and interplay of WCD, S-WCD, and S-WMD to avoid reader confusion.  
- Originality: meaningful integration of metric learning into WMD with embedding transform and histogram reweighting plus scaling techniques, though building on known components.  
- Significance: practical recipe for supervised transport-based document metrics with strong empirical gains and useful code release; limitations include reliance on pre-trained embeddings and kNN focus.  
- Pros: well-motivated method, strong empirical results, practical training strategy, and code release.  
- Cons: limited discussion of λ and entropic bias, modest novelty relative to prior building blocks, and missing ablations/explanatory clarity.  
- Specific suggested experiments: λ sweep (e.g., 1,5,10,20,50) reporting accuracy, training time, and transport sparsity; ablations for A/w/initialization; compare to Paragraph Vector.  
- Also clarify computational/memory scaling for long documents and any rare-word or vocabulary filtering used during training.  
- Recommendation: overall positive/leaning-accept, contingent on addressing λ sensitivity and providing thorough ablations.
