- Proposes supervised metric learning that extends Word Mover’s Distance by learning a linear projection of word embeddings and task-specific word weights, with an efficient optimization algorithm and strong k-NN classification results.
- Experimental results show the method outperforms many baselines on several document classification datasets.
- Impact may be limited because a simple baseline (word centroid initialization + EMD) performs nearly as well, suggesting limited gains from the learned components (small gap in Table 3).
- The paper should include a baseline applying standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) directly to word centroid embeddings.
- Lacks an ablation study to measure the separate contributions of the word-specific weights and the projection matrix A.
- The main novelty appears to be the optimization method (Lines 156–175); learning a linear transformation for EMD is relatively incremental.
- The paper is very well written and easy to read.
- Minor clarification needed: ambiguous reference “The authors” on Line 152 (unclear which citation is meant).
- Minor clarification needed: Equation 11 should indicate dual variables alpha and beta are optimal (e.g., use stars).
- Minor clarification needed: explicitly state around Line 170 that the described optimization is the Sinkhorn scaling algorithm referenced earlier.
