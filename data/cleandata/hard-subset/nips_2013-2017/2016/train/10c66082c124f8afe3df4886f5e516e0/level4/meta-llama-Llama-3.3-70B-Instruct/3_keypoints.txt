- Novelty: paper parameterizes the WMD/EMD ground metric via a Mahalanobis distance on word embeddings and learns it from data.  
- Additional contribution: proposes learning histogram coefficient reweighting for documents.  
- Theory: provides gradients for NCA-type costs applied to this EMD parameter learning problem.  
- Experiments: presents compelling and convincing experimental evidence; implementation and testing are non-trivial.  
- Overall verdict: well-crafted combination of existing techniques with strong empirical results; reviewer supports acceptance.  
- Strengths: simplicity of the core idea and a strong experimental section.  
- Improve rigor: mathematical derivations and notation are somewhat sloppy and need better rigor and clarity.  
- Method presentation: add an algorithm box to clarify the proposed procedure.  
- Regularization issue: better handle and discuss the interplay between regularized (entropy-regularized OT) and non-regularized formulations.  
- Inconsistency: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while later using squared Euclidean (Mahalanobis), suggesting defining WMD as a p-Wasserstein distance would be clearer.  
- Novelty concern: the histogram reweighting in Eq.5 is not novel—previously proposed by Guy Lebanon and explored by Le/Cuturi.  
- Ablation requested: provide experiments assessing the importance and impact of the learned weight vector w.  
- Related work: more clearly delineate differences from Cuturi/Avis (entropy regularization, continuous linear-parameterized cost vs discrete cost) and clarify issues with Wang et al.’s approach and OT optimization.
