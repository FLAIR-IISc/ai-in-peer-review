- Introduces S-WMD: a supervised document metric learning method that learns an affine transform of embeddings and word-importance weights.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance as a differentiable, efficient proxy for WMD and derives gradients via the Sinkhorn dual.  
- Proposes an efficient training algorithm to make the supervised WMD optimization tractable.  
- Evaluated on 8 text classification datasets against 26 baselines, consistently outperforming them and achieving new kNN-based state-of-the-art.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation and strong positioning relative to prior work.  
- Technical presentation and derivation of the S-WMD loss/gradients are in-depth and very clear.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and convincing.  
- Conceptual novelty is somewhat incremental: mainly applies standard metric learning (NCA-style) ideas to WMD, with the key contributions being computational/engineering.  
- The role and sensitivity of the entropy regularization parameter (lambda) is under-discussed; its choice (lambda=10) needs analysis due to its large effect on bias vs. approximation and convergence.  
- The repeated comparisons to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) are confusing; WCD’s role as an initialization (S-WCD) is fine but its prominence as a primary baseline may detract from the main story.
