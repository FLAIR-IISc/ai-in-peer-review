- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform and per-word importance weights for task-specific document distances.  
- Uses an entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to enable efficient gradient-based optimization through the WMD transport problem.  
- Empirically evaluated on eight document classification datasets against 26 baselines using k-NN, showing strong results.  
- Strength: A technically interesting and useful extension that improves document distance metrics for k-NN text classification.  
- Strength: Paper is very well-written and provides a comprehensive experimental comparison against strong, relevant baselines.  
- Strength: The Sinkhorn-based optimization scheme is a significant contribution that makes supervised learning of this metric tractable.  
- Weakness: Full S-WMD often yields only marginal improvement over its initialization (S-WMD init. using NCA on WCD), calling into question the practical benefit of the full optimization.  
- Weakness: No ablation study to quantify the relative contributions of the projection matrix A versus the word importance weights w.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparison misses additional baselines, e.g., applying standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN) to Word Centroid Distance.  
- Originality: Main novelty is the Sinkhorn-based efficient optimization for supervised WMD; learning a linear metric in an EMD framework has precedents in other domains.  
- Minor: Clarify which cited paper [1,7,8] is meant by "The authors" at Line 152.  
- Minor: Equation 11 should probably use α* and β* for consistency with Equation 10.  
- Minor: Explicitly state around Line 170 that the alternating fixed-point algorithm is the Sinkhorn scaling algorithm.
