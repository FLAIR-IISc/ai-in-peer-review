

This paper presents a supervised approach for learning document distances by extending Word Mover's Distance (WMD). WMD, originally motivated by the Earth Mover's Distance, computes document dissimilarity via optimal transport between word distributions, using word2vec embeddings for word-level transport costs. The authors propose learning (1) a linear transformation of word embeddings and (2) a reweighted word distribution to enhance label separation between documents. The paper is clearly written, well-structured, and includes a thorough review of related work demonstrating strong familiarity with the literature. Experimental results are provided on multiple novel datasets. However, the motivation for the proposed formulation requires deeper justification, and the contribution lacks significant novelty. Specifically:  
1) The rationale for reweighting the word distribution using vector \( \mathbf{w} \) is insufficiently explained. This reweighting may fundamentally alter a document's content, potentially transforming it into a different semantic entity. The authors must clarify how reweighting preserves the document's original meaning while improving label separation.  
2) The contribution is incremental: adapting WMD to a supervised setting via linear transformation \( \mathbf{A} \) and reweighting \( \mathbf{w} \) with the NCA objective is a plausible extension but neither novel nor particularly innovative.  
3) The paper does not address how to compute the reweighting \( \mathbf{w} \) for a new, unseen document, which is critical for practical application.  
4) The phrase "semantic difference" in the abstract appears to refer to document labels, but semantic differences typically relate to meaning rather than authorship or topic. The authors should clarify whether "semantic difference" denotes label-based distinctions or another concept.  
Overall, the paper is well-written and merits acceptance pending satisfactory resolution of these points.
