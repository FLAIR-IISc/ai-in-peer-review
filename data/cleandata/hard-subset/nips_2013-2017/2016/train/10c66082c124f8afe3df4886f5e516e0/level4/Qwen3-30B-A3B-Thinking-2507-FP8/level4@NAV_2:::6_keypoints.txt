- Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that jointly learns a linear embedding transform A and word importance weights w for classification.
- Builds on WMD by minimizing stochastic leave-one-out kNN error and uses NCA-style metric learning principles.
- Addresses computational burden by approximating WMD with the Sinkhorn distance, reducing complexity from O(q^3 log q) to O(q^2).
- Proposes efficient batch gradient descent and a WCD-based initialization to speed training and improve practicality.
- Evaluated on 8 text classification datasets and consistently outperformed 26 baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, supervised metric learning).
- Achieved the lowest error on 5 of 8 datasets and the best average performance across tasks.
- Technical quality is strong with solid theoretical grounding in optimal transport and metric learning.
- Novelty in jointly learning both A and w offers clear advantages over unsupervised WMD and prior metric learning approaches.
- Implementation details (Sinkhorn approximation, WCD init) are well-justified and contribute to practicality.
- Needs clearer intuition and exposition for Eq. (5), the weighted document representation.
- Lacks ablation studies to determine whether gains come primarily from A, w, or their combination.
- Experimental reporting omission: k is not specified in Table 2 (it is k=1 in experiments).
- The claim about handling "syntactically different but conceptually similar words" needs concrete examples from results to substantiate it.
- Potential confusion between the λ in Eq. (2) and the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ should be clarified.
- Overall judgment: high quality, clear, original, significant empirical impact — recommendation to accept, with minor revisions requested.
