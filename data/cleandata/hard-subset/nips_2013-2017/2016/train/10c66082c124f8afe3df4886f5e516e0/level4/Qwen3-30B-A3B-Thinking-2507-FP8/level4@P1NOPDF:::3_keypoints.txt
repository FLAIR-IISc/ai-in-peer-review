- Novel contribution: Parameterizes WMD ground metric via a Mahalanobis distance on word embeddings and learns histogram reweighting, derives gradients for NCA-style objectives, with strong empirical validation — recommend acceptance.  
- Strength: Method is simple, clearly presented, nontrivial to implement, and supported by a robust experimental section.  
- Mathematical rigor: Clarify interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations, fix imprecise notation/gradient derivations, and add an algorithm box to improve readability.  
- Metric consistency: Resolve inconsistency between Euclidean vs. squared-Euclidean costs (Eq.1 vs. Eq.6); state WMD as p-Wasserstein with p as a parameter (e.g., p=2).  
- Novelty clarification: Acknowledge that histogram “reweighting” was proposed by Lebanon (2006) and further developed by Le & Cuturi (2015); explicitly state how the current method differs.  
- Experimental insights: Provide analysis of the learned weight vector w—its effect on performance, consistency of improvement, and conditions where it matters most.  
- Related work: Stronger emphasis on Cuturi’s entropy-regularized OT (Cuturi & Avis, 2013) and clarify differences (continuous ground metric via linear maps and histogram reweighting); explain why Wang et al.’s approach is an insufficient comparison.  
- Overall: Strong experiments and sound idea; addressing the above clarifications would elevate the paper further.
