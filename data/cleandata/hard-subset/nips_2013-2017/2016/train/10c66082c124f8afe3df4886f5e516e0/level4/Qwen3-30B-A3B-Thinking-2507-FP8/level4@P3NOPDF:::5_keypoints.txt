1. Extends WMD to supervised text classification using learned word-specific weights and Sinkhorn distance for efficiency.  
2. Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing strong average performance and significant improvements.  
3. Exceptionally well-written with clear, rigorous mathematical exposition and well-structured experiments.  
4. Main novelty concern: contribution is incremental and closely aligned with prior WMD work; novelty claim needs sharpening.  
5. Paper should better separate the supervised weighting contribution from the use of the Sinkhorn approximation in its claims.  
6. Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is tangential and confusing; recommend focusing on OT-based baselines.  
7. Major issue: inadequate analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ and its impact on results and metric behavior.  
8. Authors must clarify whether λ values approximate Wasserstein (large λ) or exploit entropic bias (small λ) and how that affects speed/accuracy.  
9. Overall: technically sound and suitable for acceptance pending minor revisions to clarify novelty, remove/justify WCD comparison, and address λ.
