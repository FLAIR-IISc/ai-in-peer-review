

The authors propose a novel method for learning the parameters of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), which computes the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) over text documents using word embeddings to define bin-to-bin dissimilarities. Specifically, they parameterize the EMD cost matrix (ground metric) via a Mahalanobis distance between vector representations and additionally learn histogram coefficients for reweighting, following Lebanon's approach for text document classification. The paper derives gradients for NCA-type objectives and presents compelling experimental results. Overall, the work constitutes a well-executed synthesis of established techniques, yielding strong empirical validation. The simplicity of the core idea—while non-trivial to implement and test—combined with the thorough experimental section forms the paper's primary strength.  

**Major concerns:**  
1. **Mathematical rigor:** The treatment of notation and computations is inconsistent. For instance, Equation (1) uses Euclidean distance as the cost, while Equation (6) employs squared Euclidean distance (effectively the 2-Wasserstein cost), creating confusion. To avoid this, the authors should uniformly define WMD as the *p*-Wasserstein distance between word embedding clouds for arbitrary *p*, rather than conflating cost definitions.  
2. **Algorithmic clarity:** An explicit algorithmic pseudocode box would greatly improve readability and help readers grasp the proposed optimization procedure.  
3. **Novelty and related work:** The reweighting mechanism in Equation (5) is not new; it was explicitly proposed by Lebanon (2005) for text classification ("Metric Learning for Text Documents"). This should be acknowledged, and the paper should more clearly distinguish its contributions from Cuturi and Avis (2013), who used discrete costs without parameterization or entropy regularization (which this work leverages for scalability). Additionally, the paper builds on Wang et al. (2015), but their approach is unpublished and lacks a working OT optimization procedure (as noted in the draft).  

**Minor comments:**  
- The empirical impact of the learned weight vector **w** (Equation 5) on performance is unclear. Does **w** significantly improve results, or is it redundant? This should be addressed.  
- The related work section should explicitly contrast the continuous, parameterized cost in this paper with the discrete cost in Cuturi/Avis (2013), and clarify how entropy regularization enables practical scalability.  

The paper is well-motivated and empirically strong, but requires greater mathematical precision, algorithmic transparency, and a clearer delineation of novelty to meet NIPS standards. The experimental results are convincing, and the work merits acceptance pending these revisions.
