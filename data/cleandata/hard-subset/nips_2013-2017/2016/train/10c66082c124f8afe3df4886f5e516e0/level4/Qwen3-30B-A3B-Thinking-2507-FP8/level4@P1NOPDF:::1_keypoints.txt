- Paper proposes supervised metric learning for Word Mover’s Distance, jointly learning a linear projection and document-specific word weights.  
- Method generalizes to transport distances from squared Euclidean distances after linear projection and includes an efficient optimization scheme.  
- Strong empirical performance on multiple text classification tasks using k-NN and the paper is well-written and clear.  
- Major concern: EMD initialized with word-centroid distance performs nearly identically to the full learned metric (Table 3), suggesting marginal incremental gain.  
- The small performance gap raises doubts about the necessity and practical impact of the proposed optimization framework.  
- Missing baseline comparison: evaluate the word-centroid embedding with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: separate the effects of learned word weights versus the projection matrix A to identify which component drives gains.  
- Originality concern: linear metric learning within EMD is not novel; primary novelty is the optimization procedure but empirical differentiation is limited.  
- Minor fixes requested: clarify which paper is meant by “The authors” (Line 152), add stars to α and β in Equation 11 if appropriate, and explicitly state Sinkhorn scaling is used (around Line 170).  
- Overall: technically coherent and well-executed but empirical evidence and missing studies weaken the argument for the method’s necessity and impact.
