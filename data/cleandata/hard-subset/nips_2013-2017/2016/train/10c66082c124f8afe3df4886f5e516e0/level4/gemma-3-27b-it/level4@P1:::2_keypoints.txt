- Proposes a supervised extension to Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns an affine embedding transformation and word-importance weights for text classification.  
- Optimizes leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error to produce a task-specific document metric.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance as an efficient approximation to address WMD’s computational bottleneck.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world datasets against 26 baselines, consistently outperforming competitors.  
- Technically sound and theoretically grounded, building on optimal transport and metric learning.  
- Paper is clearly written and sufficiently detailed for reproducibility.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive and demonstrates consistent superiority across diverse datasets.  
- Significant contribution: addresses a key limitation of original WMD by incorporating supervision.  
- Original idea: jointly learning embedding transformation and word re-weighting is novel and effective.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of shortcomings for longer documents and the squared Euclidean cost (e.g., CLASSIC dataset).  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters despite using Bayesian optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial improvement over existing metrics, technical merit, clarity, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: gains are not uniformly large across all datasets and computational complexity may still be problematic for very large datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, substantial contribution deserving acceptance to NIPS.
