- Proposes S-WMD: learn a linear transformation of embeddings and word importance weights to minimize leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error.  
- Uses Sinkhorn (entropy-regularized OT) as a relaxed WMD to enable efficient gradient computation.  
- Theoretically sound approach with strong empirical performance on eight text classification tasks, outperforming 26 baselines.  
- Strength: elegant, conceptually straightforward combination of techniques that is non-trivial to implement and optimize.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation and useful algorithmic details and complexity discussion.  
- Needs clearer discussion of trade-offs between regularized and non-regularized formulations.  
- Mathematical presentation could be more rigorous and notationally consistent, especially the Euclidean vs squared-Euclidean / Mahalanobis transition; frame WMD as p-Wasserstein.  
- Reweighting scheme (Eq. 5) is not entirely novel; should discuss prior related work (e.g., Lebanon; Le/Cuturi) to better position contribution.  
- Should analyze and report insights on the learned word importance weights (w): their contribution to performance and any emerging patterns.  
- Related work section should more explicitly contrast this method with Cuturi & Avis (continuous linear maps vs discrete optimization) and clarify entropy regularization’s role; mention limitations of Wang et al.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical gains, novel/effective technique combination, solid theoretical foundation, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: imprecise notation, insufficient related-work discussion, and lack of deeper analysis of learned weights.  
- Overall: high-quality paper with significant impact potential; merits acceptance to a top-tier conference despite minor revisions.
