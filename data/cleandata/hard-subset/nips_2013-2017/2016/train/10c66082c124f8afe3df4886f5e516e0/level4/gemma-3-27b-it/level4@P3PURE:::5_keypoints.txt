- Contribution: Learns supervised word-specific weights for text classification, extending Word Mover’s Distance.  
- Method: Proposes using the Sinkhorn (entropic-regularized) distance as a computationally efficient proxy for Wasserstein distance.  
- Results: Shows strong performance on eight datasets, achieving state-of-the-art average results versus 26 baselines.  
- Presentation: Paper is very well written with clear exposition and thorough mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty concern: Primary weakness is incremental novelty relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance.  
- Relevance issue: Inclusion of comparisons to the Word Centroid Distance feels irrelevant and confusing.  
- Lambda discussion: Treatment of the entropic regularization parameter λ is incomplete; λ can strongly affect performance and may dominate equation (2).  
- Unresolved question: Unclear whether the chosen λ yields behavior closer to the true (unregularized) Wasserstein distance or to the entropic Sinkhorn relaxation used in practice.
