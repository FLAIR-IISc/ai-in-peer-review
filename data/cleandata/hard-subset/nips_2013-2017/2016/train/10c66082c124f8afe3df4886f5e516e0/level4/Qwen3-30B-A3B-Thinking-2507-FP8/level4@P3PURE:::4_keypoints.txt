- Proposal: supervised extension to Word Mover’s Distance using a linear embedding transform A and per-document reweighting vector w optimized with an NCA objective to maximize label separation.  
- Strengths: well-written, clearly structured, comprehensive literature review, and experimental results on multiple novel datasets.  
- Concern—reweighting motivation: rationale for reweighting w is weak; it may change core document semantics and authors must show w preserves document integrity while aiding separation.  
- Concern—novelty: combining a linear transform A and reweighting w within NCA is a straightforward extension with limited technical novelty relative to existing supervised metric learning.  
- Concern—generalization: unclear how w is determined for unseen documents if w is learned per-document during training.  
- Concern—terminology: “semantic difference” is ambiguous and conflates label differences with semantic meaning; authors must clarify intended meaning.  
- Overall: technically sound but key issues (reweighting justification, novelty, and generalization) weaken impact; recommend acceptance pending thorough clarification.
