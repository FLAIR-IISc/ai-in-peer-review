- Introduces supervised metric learning for word-mover’s distance that jointly learns a linear projection and per-word weights with renormalization for document tasks.
- Method generalizes to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances after a linear projection.
- Proposes an efficient optimization procedure to learn the projection matrix and word weights.
- Empirically evaluated extensively against strong baselines using kNN on multiple document classification datasets.
- Technically sound extension of WMD with demonstrated empirical utility for kNN-based text classification.
- Impact appears limited because a “word centroid distance” initialization achieves comparable performance to the full learned distance (Table 3), questioning incremental benefit.
- Missing baseline: should compare weighted word-centroid embeddings with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) to clarify the contribution of representation vs. metric learning.
- Missing ablation: need analysis of the individual importance of learned word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A (evaluate each component in isolation).
- Optimization method is a notable contribution, but the core idea of applying linear metric learning within the earth-movers framework is relatively incremental.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.
- Minor clarifications requested: disambiguate “the authors” reference (line 152); mark α and β as intermediate variables in Eq. 11; explicitly state that the scaling around line 170 is the Sinkhorn scaling.
