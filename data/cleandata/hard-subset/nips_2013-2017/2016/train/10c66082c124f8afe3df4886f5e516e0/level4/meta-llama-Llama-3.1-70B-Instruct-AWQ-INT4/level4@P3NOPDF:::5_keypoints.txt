- Proposal: uses Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for Wasserstein and learns word-specific weights to make Word Mover’s Distance supervised.
- Evaluation: tested on eight datasets against 26 baselines and reports the best average performance.
- Writing quality: paper is well-written with clear, concise, and accessible mathematical explanations.
- Novelty concern: contribution’s novelty is limited relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance work.
- Baseline clarity: discussion of the “World Centroid Distance” baseline is unclear and appears out of place.
- Lambda analysis lacking: insufficient examination of the regularization parameter λ, which can heavily influence results and dominate equation (2).
- Behavior ambiguity: unclear whether λ yields behavior close to pure Wasserstein (large λ) or entropic Sinkhorn (small λ), noting that practical choices often produce entropic behavior.
- Recommendation: addressing the λ sensitivity and clarifying the baseline discussion would strengthen the paper.
