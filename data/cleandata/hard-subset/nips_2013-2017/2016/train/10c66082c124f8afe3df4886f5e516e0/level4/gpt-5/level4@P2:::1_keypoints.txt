- Introduces S-WMD: supervised metric learning for WMD that learns a linear transform A and word-importance weights w.  
- Trains via a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective (NCA-style) on entropy-regularized WMD (Sinkhorn) for efficient gradients.  
- Accelerates training with batch sampling and neighborhood pruning guided by Word Centroid Distance (WCD).  
- Uses S-WCD (NCA on centroids) as a strong initialization for full S-WMD.  
- Situates work at the intersection of metric learning and optimal transport, extending WMD-based kNN with supervision.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation to align transport ground metric and word weights with labels.  
- Strength: sound and efficient optimization using Sinkhorn, neighborhood filtering, and batching.  
- Strength: strong empirical study across eight datasets and 26 baselines, with public code.  
- Strength: well written and clearly presented with helpful connections to NCA and Sinkhorn.  
- Weakness: modest gains over the S-WCD initialization on several datasets, raising questions about added value of full transport learning.  
- Weakness: missing centroid-based baselines—ITML and LMNN on centroid features are omitted.  
- Weakness: no ablation isolating the effects of learning A alone versus w alone.  
- Weakness: little analysis of sensitivity to Sinkhorn λ, ground-distance p, or neighbor truncation M.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound but needs more ablations and centroid baselines to substantiate contributions.  
- Clarity assessment: generally clear with minor notation and referencing issues.  
- Originality assessment: combination is a useful and novel synthesis though individual ideas are not entirely new.  
- Significance assessment: promising practical significance but impact depends on consistent improvements over S-WCD and clearer component contributions.  
- Pros for acceptance: conceptually clean supervised extension, efficient training, strong empirical results, and public code.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited improvements vs centroid init, missing baselines and ablations, and minor clarity issues.  
- Suggestions: add ITML/LMNN centroids baselines; run ablations (A only, w only, full) across document lengths; report sensitivity to λ, p, and M; analyze cases where TF-IDF+NCA outperforms S-WMD.  
- Minor suggested clarifications: fix ambiguous antecedents, mark dual variables α*/β* in gradients, and explicitly cite Sinkhorn scaling when introducing u and v.  
- Overall recommendation: lean toward acceptance, contingent on adding the missing centroid-based baselines and ablation studies.
