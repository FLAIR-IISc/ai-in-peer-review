This paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance for learning a distance metric between textual documents. While the original WMD is unsupervised, this work introduces supervision via class labels to learn the metric. The authors propose learning two components for the distance function: a linear transformation A, which is a traditional approach in metric learning, and a set of word importance weights, w.

The manuscript would benefit from a more detailed intuition behind Equation (5), which governs the use of the word weights. Furthermore, it is unclear which of the two learned components—the linear transformation A or the weights w—is primarily responsible for the reported performance improvements. An ablation study analyzing the impact of each component individually would significantly strengthen the paper. The method's effectiveness is evaluated on a k-NN classification task. The authors are commended for their plan to make the Matlab code available.

Questions and Minor Comments:
*   The role of Equation (2) in the method is unclear. Is the regularization parameter lambda in this equation the same as the one mentioned on line 170?
*   The claim on line 316 that the method "is particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" requires further justification.
*   Line 257 states, "on the right we also show the average error across datasets." I was unable to locate this information. Is it intended to be in Table 3?
*   In Table 2, the distinction between the "n" and "ne" columns is not defined.
*   The paper does not specify how the value of k for k-Nearest Neighbors classification was selected. Was it chosen per dataset or per method, and how was it tuned?
