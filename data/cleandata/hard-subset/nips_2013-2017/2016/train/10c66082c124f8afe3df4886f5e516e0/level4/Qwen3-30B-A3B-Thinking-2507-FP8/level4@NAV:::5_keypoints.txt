- Extends Word Mover’s Distance to a supervised setting via learned word-specific weights.  
- Replaces Wasserstein distance with Sinkhorn distance as a computational proxy to improve speed.  
- Demonstrates superior average performance across 8 datasets versus 26 baselines.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with a thorough, accessible mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty is modest compared to the foundational Word Mover’s Distance work.  
- References to World Centroid Distance are irrelevant and confusing.  
- Discussion of the regularization parameter λ is insufficient, leaving unclear whether the method attains pure Wasserstein behavior (large λ, slow convergence) or Sinkhorn-entropic behavior (small λ, faster but divergent).
