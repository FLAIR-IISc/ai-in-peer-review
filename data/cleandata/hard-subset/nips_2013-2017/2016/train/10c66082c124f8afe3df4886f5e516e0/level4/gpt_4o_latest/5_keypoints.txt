- Task and approach: supervised text classification using a word-weighted variant of Word Mover’s Distance implemented with the Sinkhorn distance for computational efficiency.  
- Contribution: introduces learning word-specific weights and leverages entropic Sinkhorn approximation of Wasserstein.  
- Experiments: evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, claiming best average performance.  
- Presentation: paper is exceptionally well-written with clear exposition and thorough, clear mathematical treatment.  
- Novelty concern: perceived novelty is limited compared to the original Word Mover’s Distance paper.  
- Irrelevant/unclear material: references to the World Centroid Distance appear irrelevant and confusing.  
- Insufficient discussion of regularization: treatment of the entropic regularization parameter λ is inadequate.  
- Sensitivity of λ: reviewer notes λ can dominate Equation (2) and strongly affect results.  
- Theoretical behavior question: large λ should approach pure Wasserstein (with slower convergence) while small λ yields entropic Sinkhorn-like behavior — it’s unclear which regime the paper achieves.
