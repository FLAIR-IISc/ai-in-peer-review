This paper proposes a supervised method for learning the distance between documents based on the word mover's distance (WMD). The WMD, initially motivated by the earth mover's distance, calculates the optimal transport between two word distributions to obtain the document distance. The transport distance between words is initially computed using word2vec embeddings. The authors extend this approach by learning (1) a linear transformation of the embedded words and (2) a reweighted word distribution, with the goal of increasing the separation between documents of different labels. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a thorough review of related works that demonstrates the authors' solid understanding of the literature. The authors also provide experimental results for their algorithm on multiple novel datasets.

However, the motivation behind the formulation could be more thoroughly explained, and the significance of the contribution is somewhat limited. Several concerns arise from the proposed approach: 

Firstly, the use of reweighting the word distribution using w is not well-motivated. It appears that this reweighting could alter the document to the point of creating a completely different document. The authors should provide a clearer explanation of why this reweighting is beneficial and how it avoids changing the document's meaning.

Secondly, the contribution of this work is not particularly significant. The adoption of a linear transformation A and reweighting w with the Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA) objective function is a possible extension of WMD to the supervised setting, but it does not represent a groundbreaking or innovative formulation.

Thirdly, it is unclear how the algorithm can determine the distance for a completely new document. Specifically, how can one obtain a suitable reweighting w for a new document?

Lastly, the term "semantic difference" in the abstract appears to refer to the label of the document. However, differences in authorship or topic do not necessarily constitute a semantic difference. The authors should clarify whether the term "semantic difference" encompasses other meanings.

In general, the paper is well-written, and I do not object to its acceptance once these concerns are adequately addressed.
