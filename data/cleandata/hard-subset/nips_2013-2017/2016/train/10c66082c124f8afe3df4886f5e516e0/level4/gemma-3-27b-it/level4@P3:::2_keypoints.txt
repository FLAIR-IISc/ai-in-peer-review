- Introduces S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a word-embedding transformation and word importance weights.  
- Optimizes parameters by minimizing leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error.  
- Provides an efficient gradient approximation using the Sinkhorn distance to make training practical.  
- Gradient derivations for both transformation matrix and word weights are thorough and technically sound.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation on eight datasets showing substantial improvements over 26 competitive baselines.  
- Ablation study and initialization experiments clarify the contribution of the full training procedure.  
- Well-written, clearly organized paper with helpful pseudocode and reproducibility details.  
- Novel combination of supervised metric learning with WMD and introduction of word importance weighting.  
- Significant potential impact for document distance metrics and downstream IR/text mining tasks.  
- Main weakness: remaining computational complexity and scalability concerns for very large datasets.  
- Potential sensitivity to hyperparameters, indicated by extensive Bayesian optimization.  
- Limited error analysis—insufficient insight into where S-WMD succeeds or fails on specific cases.  
- Reviewer recommendation: strong accept; strengths outweigh weaknesses and likely to be influential.
