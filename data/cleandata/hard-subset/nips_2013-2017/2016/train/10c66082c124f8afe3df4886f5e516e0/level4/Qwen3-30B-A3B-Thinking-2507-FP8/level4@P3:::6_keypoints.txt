- Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that jointly learns a linear transformation A and word importance weights w to minimize leave-one-out kNN error.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance approximation and batch gradient descent to reduce computation to O(q²) versus WMD’s O(q³ log q).  
- Novel combination of affine transformation and histogram reweighting, distinguishing it from prior EMD-based metric learning that lacks word weighting.  
- Experiments cover 8 diverse datasets and 26 baselines with Bayesian hyperparameter tuning.  
- S-WMD achieves the lowest test error on 5/8 datasets and outperforms all baselines on average.  
- Ablation study (init vs full training) validates benefit of supervised learning; training is practically efficient (seconds per iteration).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly presented with helpful figures, pseudocode, and complexity analysis.  
- Minor clarity needed for Eq. (5): the intuition behind the Hadamard product (w ◦ d̃ₐ) should be expanded.  
- Original and significant: fills a supervised-learning gap for WMD while preserving use of pre-trained embeddings.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases on BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, and AMAZON; causes are suggested but not evidenced.  
- Weakness: ambiguous claims about robustness to syntactic variation lack concrete examples or analysis.  
- Weakness: missing experimental details critical for reproducibility (k value for kNN; unclear table labels like "n" vs "ne").  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, strong empirical results, and minor revisions would address the noted weaknesses.
