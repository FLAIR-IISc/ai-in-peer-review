This paper introduces a novel supervised metric learning approach for textual documents, extending the well-established Word Mover's Distance (WMD). While WMD is inherently unsupervised, operating on word embedding distances, this work incorporates supervision based on document class membership. The authors state that code will be released (Matlab). The proposed method learns the distance function through two components: a linear transformation matrix, A, and word importance weights, w. Further clarification of the motivation behind the formulation in Equation (5), particularly concerning the application of *w*, would be beneficial, as would a more detailed analysis of the individual contributions of A and *w* to the observed performance gains. The method is evaluated on a document classification task. Recognizing the challenges of distance metric evaluation, inclusion of a baseline classification result on the same datasets could provide valuable context. Several points require further clarification. The specific application of Equation (2) is unclear. The use of lambda in that equation appears inconsistent with its definition in line 170. The authors claim the method “is particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words” (line 316); justification for this claim would strengthen the paper. Regarding specific details, line 257 references “average error across datasets” which I could not locate – is this intended for presentation in Table 3? In Table 2, the distinction between “n” and “ne” remains undefined. Finally, the definition of *k* used for k-Nearest Neighbors classification is not specified; is this parameter dataset-dependent, method-dependent, or determined otherwise?
