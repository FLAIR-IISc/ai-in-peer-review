- Proposes S-WMD: learns an affine transform of embeddings plus word importance weights to supervise WMD by minimizing leave-one-out NN error, using Sinkhorn for tractability.  
- Bridges unsupervised word embeddings and task-specific metric learning, building on WMD and NCA-style approaches.  
- Sound technical approach with rigorous gradient derivation using Sinkhorn to make optimization practical.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation on 8 datasets against 26 baselines showing consistent superiority over WMD, WCD, and other metric-learning baselines.  
- Training time and efficiency are reasonable—comparable to or faster than standard metric learning methods.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and organized with clear background, derivations, and helpful pseudocode; minor clarity issue: WCD not defined at first mention.  
- Original contribution: novel application of metric learning to optimal transport and introduction of word importance weights, distinct from prior ECCV work.  
- Significant practical impact demonstrated (e.g., 51% relative error reduction on OHSUMED) and easy adoption using existing embeddings.  
- Strengths: thorough experiments, solid theoretical foundation with practical approximation, consistent empirical wins, and good implementation details.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of failures on BBCSPORT and CLASSIC, insufficient theoretical study of Sinkhorn approximation error, and no exploration of integration with learned deep embeddings.  
- Recommendation: strong accept for NIPS—well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically validated contribution.
