- Proposes learning WMD/EMD cost matrix via a Mahalanobis parameterization of word-embedding distances.  
- Introduces learning of histogram reweighting coefficients (weight vector w) for documents.  
- Derives gradients for NCA-type losses applied to this learned EMD/WMD setting.  
- Provides strong empirical evidence with extensive experiments across eight corpora.  
- Achieves lowest kNN test error on 5/8 datasets and best average performance versus 26 baselines.  
- Paper reads like a coherent combination of existing techniques but offers convincing results; reviewer supports acceptance.  
- Strength: simple and conceptually straightforward idea, with a solid experimental evaluation.  
- Implementation is nontrivial despite conceptual simplicity.  
- Requested improvement: clarify interplay between regularized (entropy-regularized OT) and non-regularized formulations.  
- Requested improvement: tighten and make mathematics, computations, and notations more rigorous.  
- Requested improvement: include an algorithmic box/pseudocode to illustrate the method clearly.  
- Minor comment: inconsistency between Eq.1 (Euclidean) and Eq.6 (squared Euclidean); recommend framing WMD as p-Wasserstein for consistency.  
- Minor comment: the reweighting approach is not novel—previously proposed by Guy Lebanon and related work (Le/Cuturi).  
- Question: provide experimental analysis on the practical importance and impact of the learned weight vector w.  
- Related-work critique: clarify differences from Cuturi/Avis (use of entropy regularization, continuous vs discrete cost, linear parameterization) and note issues with Wang et al.’s OT optimization.
