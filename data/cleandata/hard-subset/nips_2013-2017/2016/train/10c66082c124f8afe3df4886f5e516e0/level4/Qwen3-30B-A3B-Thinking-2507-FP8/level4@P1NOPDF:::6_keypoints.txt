- Introduces a supervised metric learning extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear transform A and word importance weights w for documents.  
- Reviewer appreciates authors’ responses but remains unclear on the intuition behind w in Equation (5) and which of A or w mainly drives performance gains.  
- Evaluation on classification tasks is appropriate, but baseline comparisons (e.g., kNN with Euclidean distance) are needed to contextualize improvements (note: reviewer says this was addressed in authors’ response).  
- Equation (2) is unclear: the λ there appears distinct from the λ in Equation (170); relationship between these parameters needs clarification.  
- The robustness claim (line 316) that the method handles syntactically different but conceptually similar words requires stronger empirical justification.  
- Text at line 257 references an average error “on the right” that does not match Table 3 or any table—correct table reference needed.  
- Table 2 is unclear: meanings of columns “n” and “ne” should be explained, and the k value used for kNN classification must be specified (fixed or dataset-dependent).  
- Availability of MATLAB code is welcome.  
- Overall: promising idea but paper needs stronger technical justification for Equation (5) and the role/effect of w to increase impact.
