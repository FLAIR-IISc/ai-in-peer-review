This paper tackles text classification by extending prior work on Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) to a supervised setting via learned, word-specific weights. The main technical contribution is to replace the exact Wasserstein computation with the entropically regularized Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy. The method is evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines and achieves the best average performance. The paper is very well written, with a clear and in-depth mathematical treatment that makes the approach easy to follow.

The primary weaknesses are: (1) the novelty relative to the original WMD paper appears limited; and (2) the discussion around the Word Centroid Distance baselines is not particularly relevant and comes across as confusing.

I am also concerned that the role of the regularization parameter λ is under-discussed. In my experience, λ can have a very large impact—sometimes becoming the dominant factor in Eq. (2). Theoretically, with a sufficiently large λ the results should approach those of the unregularized Wasserstein distance, albeit with slower convergence. In practice, however, many users choose very small λ to speed up convergence, which yields behavior that can differ substantially from true Wasserstein. It would be helpful to clarify, in this work, whether the chosen λ regime is intended to emulate the pure Wasserstein behavior or whether the method is effectively operating in the entropic (Sinkhorn) regime.
