The paper proposes a novel metric learning method for textual documents, extending the well-established Word Mover’s Distance (WMD). While the original WMD operates in an unsupervised manner by measuring the distance between word embeddings, this work introduces a supervised extension leveraging class label information – specifically, whether two documents belong to the same class. The authors promise to release code (in Matlab). [I appreciated the authors’ thorough responses to my questions, which helped clarify several points.]

The core of the proposed method involves learning two components to define the distance: a linear transformation, A (a common approach), and word importance weights, w. I would appreciate a more detailed explanation of the intuition behind Equation (5), which governs the application of *w*, and a better understanding of the relative contribution of each component – A and *w* – to the observed performance improvements. 

The experimental evaluation focuses on a document classification task. I recognize that evaluating a distance metric is inherently challenging, but for comparative purposes, it would be insightful to see the performance of a simple classifier (e.g., logistic regression) on the same datasets. [This point is now moot, having been addressed by the authors.]

Several questions remain. First, the usage of Equation (2) is unclear. The λ used in that equation appears different from the λ mentioned in line 170 – could this be clarified? Second, the claim that the method "is particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" (line 316) requires further justification. What specific mechanisms enable this forgiveness? 

Finally, some minor points: in line 257, the authors state, “on the right we also show the average error across datasets.” However, I was unable to locate this information; is it intended to be presented in Table 3? Regarding Table 2, what distinguishes “n” and “ne”? Additionally, the paper doesn’t define *k* for the k-Nearest Neighbors (kNN) classification. Is the value of *k* determined empirically for each dataset, or is it a parameter of the method itself?
