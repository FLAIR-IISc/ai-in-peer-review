- Tackles supervised text categorization by learning word-specific weights building on Word Mover's Distance.  
- Key contribution: using Sinkhorn distance as a fast proxy for Wasserstein distance.  
- Empirical evaluation: tested on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving best average performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation and thorough mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty may be limited compared to the original Word Mover's Distance work.  
- References to Word Centroid Distance are confusing and seem of limited relevance.  
- Discussion of the regularizer lambda is insufficient given its strong practical impact.  
- Unclear whether the chosen lambda yields behavior closer to pure Wasserstein (large lambda) or to entropic Sinkhorn (small lambda) — this should be reported.
