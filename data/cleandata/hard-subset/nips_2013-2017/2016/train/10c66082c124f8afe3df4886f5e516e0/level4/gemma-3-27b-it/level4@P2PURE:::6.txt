This paper introduces a novel supervised metric learning approach for textual documents, extending the previously established Word Mover’s Distance. While the original Word Mover’s Distance operates in an unsupervised manner by measuring the distance between word embeddings, this work leverages supervision in the form of document class labels. The authors have indicated that code (in Matlab) will be released. The proposed method learns the distance metric by optimizing both a linear transformation matrix A and word importance weights w. I would appreciate further clarification regarding the intuition behind Equation (5), which governs the application of these weights, and a clearer understanding of the individual contributions of A and w to the reported performance gains. The method’s evaluation focuses on a document classification task. Recognizing the challenges inherent in evaluating distance metrics, it would be informative to compare the results against those of a simpler classification baseline using the same datasets.
