- Proposes supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance with learned word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance for tractable supervised training.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets and reported superior performance against 26 competitive baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and situates the work within relevant literature.  
- Mathematical foundations and theoretical motivation are thorough and well-explained.  
- Practical efficiency achieved via the Sinkhorn approximation is a notable strength.  
- Novelty is viewed as incremental relative to the original WMD and needs clearer justification.  
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is unclear and sometimes confusing or tangential.  
- Insufficient analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ and its impact on approximation, performance, and convergence.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear derivations, motivated approach, and practical efficiency.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, unclear relevance of WCD comparisons, and inadequate discussion of λ.
