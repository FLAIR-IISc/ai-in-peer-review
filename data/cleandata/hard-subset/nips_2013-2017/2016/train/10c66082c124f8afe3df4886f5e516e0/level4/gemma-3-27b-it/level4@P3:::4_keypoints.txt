- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance by learning a linear embedding transform and a word importance weighting vector to improve k-NN classification.  
- Addresses a relevant problem: converting WMD from unsupervised to supervised to improve downstream document classification.  
- Solid technical foundation: combines optimal transport, metric learning (NCA), and Sinkhorn approximations for tractable gradient computation.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across eight datasets and 26 baselines showing significant improvements.  
- Clear writing and organization with thorough related-work coverage.  
- Motivation for learning the word importance vector w is under-explained; reweighting could alter document meaning and needs justification.  
- Contribution feels incremental: applying NCA to WMD is natural and not a major conceptual leap.  
- Authors should better highlight unique challenges of applying NCA to WMD and how their approximations specifically address them.  
- Unclear how the learned weighting w generalizes to entirely new documents or novel vocabulary; deployment on unseen data needs clarification.  
- The phrase “semantic difference” is vague and should be defined more precisely beyond label differences.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-executed, and backed by strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived incremental novelty and insufficient discussion of word-reweighting motivation and handling unseen documents.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on clarifying word reweighting motivation, handling of new documents, and articulating novelty beyond straightforward NCA application.  
- Rating: Accept (with minor revisions).
