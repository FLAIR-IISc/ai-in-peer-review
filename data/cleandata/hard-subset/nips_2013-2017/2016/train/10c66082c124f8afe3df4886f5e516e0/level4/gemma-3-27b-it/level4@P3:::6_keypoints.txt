- Paper introduces S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transformation A and word importance weights w, optimized via leave-one-out kNN error.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance to relax WMD for efficient gradient computation and scalability.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing consistently superior performance.  
- Novelty: combines supervised metric learning with WMD, learning both embedding transform and word weights.  
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation and practical approximation for gradients.  
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments and strong results across diverse datasets.  
- Efficiency: computationally practical for real-world use.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and explanations are generally clear.  
- Weakness — intuition for word weights w (Eq. 5) needs clearer explanation of how w modulates distance.  
- Weakness — lacks a focused ablation study isolating the contributions of A versus w.  
- Weakness — baseline selection should include simple classifiers (e.g., bag-of-words + logistic regression) for reference.  
- Weakness — missing discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning (e.g., Sinkhorn λ, learning rates).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, sound method with compelling empirical and practical contributions relevant to the community.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs clearer intuition for w, targeted ablation, and comparison to simple baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor issues; recommendation to accept.  
- Specific comments resolved: lambda in Eq. (2) clarified, authors addressed Eq. (5) intuition and table notations, and clarified dataset-dependent choice of k for kNN.
