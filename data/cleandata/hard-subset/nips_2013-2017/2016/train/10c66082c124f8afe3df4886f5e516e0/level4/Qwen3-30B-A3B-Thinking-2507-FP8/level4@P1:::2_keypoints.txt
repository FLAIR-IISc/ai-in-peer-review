- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): jointly learns an affine transform of word embeddings and per-word importance weights for supervised document classification.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance relaxation to efficiently approximate gradients of the nested WMD optimization, enabling scalable training.  
- Optimizes a leave-one-out kNN classification loss via stochastic neighborhood probabilities (NCA-style) for differentiable supervision.  
- Evaluated on 8 diverse text classification datasets against 26 baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, mSDA, WCD, WMD, etc.).  
- Strong empirical results: lowest test error on 5/8 datasets and best average rank, with statistically significant improvements over unsupervised WMD.  
- Preserves WMD’s semantic strengths (handling semantically similar but syntactically different words) while adding discriminative power through weighting and transformation.  
- Training complexity is practical and efficient (O(q²)), outperforming standard metric learning pipelines by operating in low-dimensional word space.  
- Technical approach is sound: Sinkhorn approximation addresses cubic complexity and the loss formulation enables differentiable supervision.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, with consistent hyperparameter tuning via Bayesian optimization across domains.  
- Clarity is generally strong with good literature context; minor issues include notation consistency and clarification of the S-WCD initialization step.  
- Visualizations (t-SNE) and word weight analyses provide intuitive evidence of improved class separation and learned importance weights.  
- Originality: first to integrate supervision directly into WMD without learning a new document representation; unique dual contribution (embedding transform + word weights).  
- Properly situates work relative to related literature and cites Sinkhorn distance; avoids overclaiming novelty.  
- Strengths summarized: practical solution, comprehensive validation, efficient training, and solid theoretical grounding in optimal transport and metric learning.  
- Weaknesses: reliance on pre-trained word2vec may limit domain adaptability; limited analysis of failure cases (e.g., CLASSIC) where WMD outperforms S-WMD.  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality, impactful contribution with strong support for acceptance.
