- Proposes using Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy for supervised Word Mover’s Distance and evaluates it on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines, showing best average performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, in-depth mathematical exposition.  
- Novelty is limited compared to prior supervised Word Mover’s Distance work.  
- Comparison to the World Centroid Distance is not relevant and may confuse readers.  
- Discussion of the regularization parameter lambda is lacking.  
- Reviewer notes lambda can significantly impact results and sometimes dominates equation (2).  
- Theoretically, with large lambda the Sinkhorn solution approaches pure Wasserstein but with slower convergence.  
- In practice, small lambda is often used for faster convergence, producing behavior distinct from Wasserstein.  
- Reviewer asks whether the paper’s lambda choice yields behavior closer to pure Wasserstein or to the entropic (Sinkhorn) distance.
