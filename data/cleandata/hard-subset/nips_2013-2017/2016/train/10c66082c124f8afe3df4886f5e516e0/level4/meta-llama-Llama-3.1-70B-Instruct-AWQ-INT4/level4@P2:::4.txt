This paper proposes a supervised method for learning the distance between documents based on the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), which was previously introduced as a document metric that leverages high-quality word embeddings. The authors extend WMD by learning a linear transformation of the embedded words and a re-weighted word distribution, aiming to better separate documents with different labels. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a thorough review of related works and a good understanding of the literature.

The authors provide experimental results on several novel datasets, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach. However, some aspects of the paper require further clarification. Firstly, the motivation behind re-weighting the word distribution using the vector w is not well-explained. It is unclear why this re-weighting is necessary and how it helps to improve the document distance without altering the document's meaning. A more detailed justification for this re-weighting is needed.

Furthermore, the contribution of this work may be considered incremental rather than groundbreaking. The proposed formulation, which combines a linear transformation and re-weighting with the Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) objective function, is a possible extension of WMD to the supervised setting, but it does not introduce a new paradigm or significantly advance the state-of-the-art.

Additionally, the paper does not clearly explain how the algorithm can be applied to find a distance for a new, unseen document. Specifically, it is unclear how the re-weighting vector w can be determined for a completely new document.

Lastly, the term "semantic difference" is used in the abstract, but its meaning is not explicitly defined. It appears to refer to the difference in labels between documents, but it is unclear whether it encompasses other aspects, such as authorship or topic differences.

In conclusion, while the paper is well-written and demonstrates some promising results, it requires further clarification and justification to fully appreciate its contributions. Addressing these concerns would strengthen the paper and provide a clearer understanding of its significance.

Strengths:

* Well-written and easy to follow
* Thorough review of related works
* Experimental results on novel datasets

Weaknesses:

* Motivation for re-weighting the word distribution is unclear
* Contribution may be considered incremental rather than groundbreaking
* Unclear how the algorithm can be applied to new, unseen documents
* Definition of "semantic difference" is unclear

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide a clearer motivation for re-weighting the word distribution
* Discuss the significance of the proposed formulation and its potential impact on the field
* Explain how the algorithm can be applied to new, unseen documents
* Clarify the definition of "semantic difference" and its relevance to the proposed approach

Acceptance: Conditional on addressing the above concerns.
