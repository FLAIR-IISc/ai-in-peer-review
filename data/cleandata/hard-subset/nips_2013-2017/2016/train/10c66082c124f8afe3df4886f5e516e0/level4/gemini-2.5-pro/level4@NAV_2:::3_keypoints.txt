- Introduces Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns a task-specific document distance by parameterizing the WMD ground metric via an affine embedding transformation and a word-importance weight vector.  
- Uses a stochastic leave-one-out kNN/NCA-style objective to learn parameters.  
- Employs entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to enable efficient gradient computation and avoid nested optimization.  
- Strength: novel and significant extension of WMD by combining metric learning and optimal transport into a supervised document distance.  
- Strength: technically sound use of Sinkhorn regularization to make gradient-based training practical at scale.  
- Strength: strong experimental evaluation — compared to 26 baselines on 8 tasks, achieving state-of-the-art on most datasets and best average performance.  
- Weakness: mathematical presentation inconsistent — Eq.1 uses a p-norm while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean (p-Wasserstein vs 2-Wasserstein); needs clarification and consistency.  
- Weakness: paper would benefit from a concise algorithm box summarizing the full training procedure for clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: relation to prior work is underdeveloped — re-weighting histograms has prior art (e.g., Lebanon; Le & Cuturi) and learning EMD ground metrics has predecessors (e.g., Cuturi & Avis) that should be explicitly acknowledged.  
- Weakness: should more clearly highlight key differences from prior work (notably entropy regularization speed-ups and continuous linear parameterization of the ground metric).  
- Weakness: lacks an ablation study quantifying contributions of the affine transform A versus the word-importance vector w; such analysis would clarify which component drives performance gains.
