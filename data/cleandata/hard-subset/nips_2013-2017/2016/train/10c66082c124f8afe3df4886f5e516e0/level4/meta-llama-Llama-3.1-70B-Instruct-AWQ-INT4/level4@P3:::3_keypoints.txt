- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised word mover's distance learning a linear embedding transform and a word importance vector.  
- Method leverages WMD and supervision to improve document classification.  
- Well-written paper with clear, concise method explanation.  
- Strong experimental results: outperforms 26 baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks.  
- t-SNE visualizations show quality of learned document embeddings.  
- Strength: simplicity and straightforward combination of existing techniques (NCA + WMD).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and well-executed.  
- Needs more rigorous mathematical treatment with clearer notation and computations.  
- Requires deeper analysis of the learned weight vector w and its benefit over unsupervised WMD.  
- Related work discussion should be clearer, explicitly contrasting with Cuturi & Avis and Wang et al.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: support acceptance; pros (novel method, strong results, clear writing), cons (math rigor, w analysis, related-work clarity).
