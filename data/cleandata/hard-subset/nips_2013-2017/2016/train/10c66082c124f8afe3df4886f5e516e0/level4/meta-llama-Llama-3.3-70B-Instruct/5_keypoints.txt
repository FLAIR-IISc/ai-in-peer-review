- Extends Word Mover’s Distance by learning supervised, word-specific weights for text categorization.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for Wasserstein distance.  
- Shows superior average performance across 8 datasets versus 26 baselines.  
- Paper is very well-written with clear, thorough mathematical exposition.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance.  
- Comparison to Word Centroid/“World” Centroid Distance seems unnecessary and confusing.  
- Discussion of lambda (entropic regularization) is inadequate given its strong practical influence.  
- Unclear whether the method behaves more like pure Wasserstein (large lambda) or entropic/Sinkhorn (small lambda) in this work.
