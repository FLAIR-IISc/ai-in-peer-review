- Extends Word Mover's Distance for text classification by learning word-specific weights in a supervised framework and using the Sinkhorn distance approximation.
- Core technical contribution: employ entropic Sinkhorn distance for computationally efficient approximation of the Wasserstein distance.
- Empirical evaluation: tested on eight datasets against 26 baselines and reports the best average performance.
- Paper quality: exceptionally well-written with a clear introduction and accessible yet deep mathematical analysis.
- Weakness: limited novelty compared to the original Word Mover's Distance.
- Weakness: comparisons to Word Centroid Distance are confusing and their relevance is not well-established.
- Major concern: insufficient discussion of the regularization parameter lambda and its potential to dominate Equation (2).
- Key open question: does the chosen lambda make the method behave like the true Wasserstein distance or reflect distinct properties of the entropically regularized Sinkhorn distance — the trade-off needs discussion.
