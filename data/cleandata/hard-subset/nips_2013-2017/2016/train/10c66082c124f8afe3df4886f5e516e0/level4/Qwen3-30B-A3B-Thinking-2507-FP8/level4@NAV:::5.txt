

The authors address text classification by extending prior work on Word Mover's Distance (Wasserstein) to a supervised setting through learned word-specific weights. Their key contribution replaces the Wasserstein distance with the Sinkhorn distance as a computational proxy, enhancing speed, and demonstrates superior average performance across 8 datasets against 26 baselines. The paper is exceptionally well-written, offering a thorough yet accessible mathematical analysis of the problem. However, two limitations warrant note: the novelty is modest relative to the foundational Word Mover's Distance work, and references to World Centroid Distance appear irrelevant and confusing. Furthermore, the discussion of λ (the regularization parameter) is insufficient. Given λ's critical influence—often dominating the behavior of Equation (2)—the paper should clarify whether the approach achieves pure Wasserstein behavior (requiring large λ, albeit with slower convergence) or Sinkhorn-entropic behavior (using small λ for faster convergence, which diverges significantly from Wasserstein). The current treatment leaves uncertainty on this fundamental aspect.
