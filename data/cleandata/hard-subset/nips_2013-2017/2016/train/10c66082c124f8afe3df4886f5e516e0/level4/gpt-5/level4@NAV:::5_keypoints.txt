- Task & approach: Supervised document categorization building on Word Mover’s Distance by learning word-specific weights.  
- Main contribution: Replace Wasserstein with Sinkhorn distance as a faster surrogate.  
- Experiments: Benchmarked on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, reporting best average performance.  
- Presentation: Paper is very well written with clear exposition and a lucid mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty concern: Contribution is modest relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance work.  
- Baseline relevance: Comparisons to Word Centroid Distance are not particularly relevant and are somewhat confusing.  
- Hyperparameter issue: The regularization parameter lambda is insufficiently discussed.  
- Regime ambiguity: Unclear whether lambda produces a pure Wasserstein regime or an entropic Sinkhorn regime; lambda choice can dominate results and critically affects convergence/behavior.
