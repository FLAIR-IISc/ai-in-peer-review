This is an extension of the term 'mover's distance,' which uses supervised learning to estimate the weight on distance. This paper features a very comprehensive experimental evaluation. I am pleased to see that someone has adopted the approach of 'mover's distance' and used differential training to learn domain-specific metrics. The method is solid, well-explained, and provides accurate information. The experiments are very comprehensive across several classification data sets and various types of baseline methods and features. The suggested method performs much better than all others. It is a solid paper that I would like to see presented at NIPS.
