- Paper proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance by learning a linear embedding transform A and a reweighting vector w for word distributions.  
- The manuscript is clearly written, well-structured, and shows strong understanding of related work.  
- Experiments are run on multiple novel datasets and results are presented comprehensively.  
- Motivation for reweighting word distributions with w is unclear and needs deeper justification.  
- Reweighting risks altering a document’s meaning; authors must show it preserves document integrity while improving label separation.  
- The contribution appears incremental: learning A and w with an NCA objective is a straightforward extension rather than a novel advance.  
- Applicability to unseen documents is ambiguous — it’s unclear how w is determined for completely new documents.  
- The phrase “semantic difference” in the abstract conflates label assignment with semantic notions (authorship/topic) and requires clarification.  
- Overall judgment: paper is well-written and merits acceptance if the above concerns are satisfactorily addressed.
