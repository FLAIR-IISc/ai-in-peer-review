- Paper proposes supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance learning a linear embedding transform and a global per-word reweighting vector to pull same-label documents closer and push different-label ones apart.  
- Manuscript is clearly written with comprehensive related work and multi-dataset experiments.  
- Motivation for histogram reweighting w is weak: needs stronger justification and empirical analysis showing it improves discrimination without distorting document semantics.  
- Contribution seems incremental (linear A + global w + NCA-style loss) and needs a stronger case that it materially advances the state of the art.  
- Test-time procedure is unclear: clarify how unseen documents are represented and whether w is global or adapted per document, and how distances are computed without retraining.  
- Ambiguity in “semantic difference”: clarify whether supervision equates semantic difference with label difference or a more nuanced notion.  
- Overall positive but would only support acceptance if the above concerns are convincingly addressed.
