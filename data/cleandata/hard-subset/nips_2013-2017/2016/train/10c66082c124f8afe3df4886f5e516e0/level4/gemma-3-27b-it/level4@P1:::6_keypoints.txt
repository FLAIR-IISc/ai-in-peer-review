- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD), a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns an affine transform A and word-specific importance weights w to minimize k-NN classification error.  
- Builds on WMD’s optimal-transport word-embedding distance by incorporating label supervision into the document distance.  
- Introduces an affine transformation of the embedding space and a word importance vector; reviewer asks for clearer intuition for Equation (5) and how A and w separately contribute to improvements.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance relaxation and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD computationally practical.  
- Provides Matlab code to promote reproducibility.  
- Strong empirical evaluation: consistently outperforms 26 competitive baselines across eight text classification datasets.  
- Baselines include unsupervised WMD, BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, mSDA, and metric learning combinations (NCA, ITML, LMNN).  
- Strength: novel supervised metric learning directly within the WMD framework.  
- Strength: compelling and consistent empirical gains across datasets.  
- Strength: efficient implementation addressing computational complexity.  
- Strength: clear presentation and available code.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation of the role and impact of word importance weights w (need to disentangle effects of A vs. w).  
- Weakness: sensitivity analysis for the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ is missing.  
- Weakness: paper does not specify how k for k-NN was chosen, reducing reproducibility.  
- Acceptance argument: technically sound, novel, empirically strong, and well-presented.  
- Rejection argument: limited insight into w’s contribution and missing analyses of λ and k choices.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contribution; recommend acceptance pending clarifications.
