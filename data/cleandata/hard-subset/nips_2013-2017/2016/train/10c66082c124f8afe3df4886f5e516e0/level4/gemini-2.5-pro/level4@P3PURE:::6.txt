This paper proposes a new method for supervised metric learning for textual documents, building upon the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance (WMD). While the original WMD measures the distance between documents based on their word embeddings, this work extends it to a supervised setting using class labels to identify documents that belong to the same class. The authors propose learning two components to define the distance: a linear transformation (A) of the word embeddings, which is a traditional approach in metric learning, and a set of word importance weights (w). The authors promise to make Matlab code for the method available.

The core of the proposed method involves learning both the linear transformation A and the word importance weights w. However, the intuition behind Equation (5), which defines how the weights w are utilized, could be explained more clearly. Furthermore, it is difficult to understand which of these two learned components is primarily responsible for the reported performance improvements. An ablation study analyzing the individual contributions of A and w would significantly strengthen the paper.

The method is evaluated on a classification task. While the evaluation is sound, there are several points that require clarification:

*   Could the authors clarify when Equation (2) is used? The parameter λ in this equation appears to be different from the λ introduced in line 170.
*   In line 316, the paper claims the method "is particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words." Could the authors provide further arguments to support this claim?
*   Line 257 states, "on the right we also show the average error across datasets." I was unable to find this in the tables; is this information supposed to be in Table 3?
*   In Table 2, what is the difference between the "n" and "ne" variants of the proposed method?
*   The paper does not mention how the value of k for the k-NN classifier was chosen. Is the selection of k dependent on the dataset or the method?
