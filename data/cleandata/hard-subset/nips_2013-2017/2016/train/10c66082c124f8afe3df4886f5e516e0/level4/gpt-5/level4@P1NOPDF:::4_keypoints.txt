- Proposes supervised WMD variant learning a linear transform A and a reweighting vector w to better separate labeled documents.  
- Paper is clearly written with a thorough related-work review and extensive experiments on multiple (including novel) datasets.  
- Motivation for reweighting w is weak: it may change a document’s identity and needs justification or constraints (normalization, sparsity, proximity).  
- Significance/novelty is limited: adding A and w to an NCA objective feels straightforward and should be better positioned against prior supervised metric-learning for transport distances.  
- Test-time procedure is unclear: how is w obtained for unseen documents—document-specific, global, or class-specific—and what inference is used?  
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be precisely defined (e.g., whether it refers to labels, topic, authorship, etc.).  
- Overall: would not oppose acceptance if the authors satisfactorily address reweighting motivation/impact, clarify novelty, and explain the test-time handling of w.
