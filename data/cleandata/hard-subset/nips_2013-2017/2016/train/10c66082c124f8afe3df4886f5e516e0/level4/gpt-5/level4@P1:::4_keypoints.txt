- Summary: Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a linear embedding transform A and a global word-importance vector w, trained with an NCA-style kNN loss using Sinkhorn-regularized WMD for differentiable, efficient gradients.
- Method details: Uses entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to approximate WMD and its gradients, initializes A via supervised WCD (S-WCD), and trains with minibatches and neighbor filtering.
- Empirical claim: Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieves best average rank and top performance on five datasets; code released.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD/entropic OT, supervised metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML), and learned ground metrics for EMD; novelty is combining A and global w with end-to-end OT optimization.
- Strength: Clarity and organization—well written with thorough literature review and clear exposition of Sinkhorn gradients.
- Strength: Technical soundness—appropriate use of entropic regularization for efficient, differentiable approximation.
- Strength: Empirical evaluation—broad, competitive baselines and consistent average gains.
- Strength: Practicality—initialization, minibatching, and neighbor filtering make training feasible.
- Weakness: Motivation for global reweighting w is weak; needs constraints and justification to avoid pathological reweighting.
- Weakness: Need analysis of how w relates to classical schemes (e.g., IDF) and empirical correlations or qualitative examples.
- Weakness: More ablations required to isolate effects of A vs w (A-only, w-only, both) and to show reweighting doesn't distort document semantics.
- Weakness: Novelty perceived as incremental—combines known components; deeper theoretical insight or extensive ablations would strengthen contribution.
- Weakness: Unclear test-time behavior—clarify whether w is a single global vector applied to new documents and how unseen documents are handled.
- Weakness: Terminology—“semantic difference” is vague; clarify that supervision defines task-specific similarity (topic/author/sentiment).
- Additional: Report sensitivity to ground metric choice (e.g., cosine, p=1) and Sinkhorn regularization λ; visualizations of weights should be tied to class labels.
- Evaluation summary: Quality and clarity are strong; originality moderate; significance empirical and practical despite conceptual incrementalism.
- Pros: Clear paper, efficient Sinkhorn-based training, strong empirical performance, open-source code.
- Cons: Underexplained motivation/constraints for w, limited conceptual novelty, unclear test-time usage and “semantic” wording.
- Overall: Well-executed, practical extension of WMD with strong results; recommend acceptance if authors address motivation/constraints for w, ablations, and clarify test-time behavior.
