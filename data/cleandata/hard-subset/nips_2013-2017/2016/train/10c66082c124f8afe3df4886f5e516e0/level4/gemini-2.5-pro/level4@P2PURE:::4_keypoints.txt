1. Proposes a supervised extension of WMD that jointly learns a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance reweighting vector w via an NCA-style objective.  
2. Paper is clearly written, has a thorough literature review, and presents comprehensive experiments on multiple datasets.  
3. Motivation for the reweighting vector w is insufficient; it could distort document representations and needs justification/demonstration that discriminability improves without losing core meaning.  
4. Novelty is limited — learning A and w with an NCA-like objective is a straightforward supervised extension rather than a major methodological advance.  
5. Test-time application is unclear — the paper does not explain how w is determined or applied to unseen documents.  
6. Terminology issue: the phrase “semantic difference” is ambiguous and appears to mean class-label differences; the authors should clarify or use more precise wording.  
7. Overall: would recommend acceptance only if the authors satisfactorily address the above concerns.
