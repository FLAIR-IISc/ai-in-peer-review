- Contribution: Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance using a linear transformation A and document-specific word weights w; authors promise to release MATLAB code.  
- Core technical innovation: Equation (5) formalizes weighting embeddings by w, but the intuition behind this formulation is not clearly explained.  
- Interaction ambiguity: Unclear how w and A interact and which component primarily drives performance; requires explicit analysis.  
- Ablation needed: Paper should include ablation comparing full model, A-only, and w-only variants to isolate contributions.  
- Evaluation concern: Uses kNN accuracy but lacks a baseline comparison to simple classifiers (e.g., SVM/logistic regression on raw embeddings).  
- kNN detail missing: Paper does not state the k-value (fixed or dataset-dependent), harming reproducibility.  
- λ inconsistency: Parameter λ appears inconsistent between Equation 2 and Line 170; need explicit definition and confirmation if they are the same.  
- “Forgiving” claim unsupported: Assertion that method handles syntactically different but conceptually similar words needs concrete examples or quantitative analysis relative to WMD.  
- Table/figure errors: Line 257 references “average error across datasets” on the right of Table 3 but this entry cannot be located; verify and correct.  
- Ambiguous table labels: Columns “n” and “ne” in Table 2 are unclear and must be defined.  
- Equation usage unclear: Specify when Equation 2 is applied during training or inference.  
- Strengths: Logical supervised extension of WMD, intuitive word-weight mechanism, and evaluation across multiple datasets.  
- Required fixes: Clarify hyperparameters and notation (k, λ, n/ne), add ablation study, provide illustrative example for the “forgiving” property, and correct table references/labels.  
- Overall verdict: Addresses a meaningful gap but needs substantial clarification and experiments to justify claimed contributions.
