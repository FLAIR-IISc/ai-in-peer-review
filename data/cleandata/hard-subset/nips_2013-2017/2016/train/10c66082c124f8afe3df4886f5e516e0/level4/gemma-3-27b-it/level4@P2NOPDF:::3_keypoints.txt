- Proposes learning WMD parameters by parameterizing the ground metric as a Mahalanobis distance and learning histogram reweighting.  
- Derives gradients for NCA-type costs within this framework and reports strong empirical performance.  
- Core idea is simple, intuitive, and required non-trivial implementation and extensive testing.  
- Experimental validation is a major strength and supports acceptance.  
- Clarify the interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations.  
- Improve mathematical rigor and notation; address inconsistency between Eq.1 (Euclidean) and Eq.6 (squared Euclidean) and frame WMD as a p‑Wasserstein distance.  
- Include a high-level algorithmic box to improve reader comprehension.  
- Acknowledge prior histogram reweighting (Guy Lebanon) and related work (e.g., Le & Cuturi); better delineate novel contributions.  
- Experimentally assess the impact and necessity of the learned weight vector w.  
- Explicitly compare to existing OT methods (Cuturi, Avis), emphasizing entropy regularization and the shift from discrete to continuous parameterized costs.  
- Note limitations/unpublished status of Wang et al. and that their single-iteration OT optimization is insufficient in practice.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution, recommend acceptance pending the above refinements.
