- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD): learn a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance weight vector w to improve WMD in a supervised NCA framework.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and shows strong understanding of related work.  
- Provides extensive experimental results across eight datasets demonstrating consistent performance gains over baselines.  
- Efficient implementation leveraging recent optimal transport approximations.  
- Motivation for reweighting word distributions with w is underexplained and requires justification that it preserves document semantics while improving discrimination.  
- The contribution appears incremental—applying a linear transform and reweighting within NCA is a natural extension rather than a novel algorithmic advance.  
- Unclear how to apply the learned reweighting w to entirely new/unseen documents; method’s generalization to new data is not addressed.  
- The term “semantic difference” is vague and needs clarification about whether it refers to label-based distinctions, topic, authorship, or other dissimilarities.  
- Reviewer is not strongly opposed to acceptance but urges addressing the above concerns to increase the paper’s impact.
