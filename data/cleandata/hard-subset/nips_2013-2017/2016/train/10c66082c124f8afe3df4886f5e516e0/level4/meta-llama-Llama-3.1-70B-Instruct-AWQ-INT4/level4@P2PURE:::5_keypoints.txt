- Contribution: proposes using the Sinkhorn (entropic) distance as a computationally efficient proxy for supervised Word Mover’s Distance with learned word-specific weights.  
- Aim: improve computational efficiency of Wasserstein-based text classification.  
- Empirical results: evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines and reports the best average performance.  
- Writing and exposition: paper is well-written with clear presentation and in-depth mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty concern: approach may offer limited novelty relative to the prior Word Mover’s Distance work.  
- Baseline relevance: comparison to the World Centroid Distance is questioned as not entirely relevant and potentially confusing.  
- Lambda discussion: treatment of the regularization parameter lambda is insufficient.  
- Lambda impact: reviewer highlights that lambda choice can make the method behave closer to pure Wasserstein or to entropic Sinkhorn, and requests clarification which regime the paper’s lambda falls into.
