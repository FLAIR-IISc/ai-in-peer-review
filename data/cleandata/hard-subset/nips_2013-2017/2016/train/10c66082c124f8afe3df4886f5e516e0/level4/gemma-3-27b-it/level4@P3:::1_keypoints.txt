- Proposes supervised metric learning extension of Word Mover’s Distance, learning a linear embedding transform and word-specific importance weights.  
- Weights re-normalize word frequencies to emphasize task-relevant words; optimization is designed to be efficient.  
- Evaluated comprehensively using k-NN on multiple document classification datasets and compared to many baselines.  
- Demonstrates consistent performance improvements over existing methods in experiments.  
- Builds on WMD in a technically sound and well-motivated way by adding supervision to an unsupervised distance.  
- Potential impact limited because S-WCD initialization performs comparably to the fully learned metric (Table 3), questioning added value.  
- Missing comparison of weighted word-centroid embedding combined with standard metric-learning algorithms (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Lacks an ablation study isolating the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Originality mainly resides in the optimization strategy; overall contribution is somewhat incremental.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written.  
- Minor clarifications requested: specify “the authors” in Line 152; denote optimal dual variables in Eq. 11 (α* and β*); explicitly reference Sinkhorn scaling near Line 170.  
- Strengths: strong technical foundation, clear presentation, efficient optimization, comprehensive evaluation, and empirical improvements.  
- Weaknesses: modest gain over simple initialization, missing ablation and baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add requested experiments (centroid+metric learning and ablation) and address clarifications.
