

The paper introduces a novel approach for learning parameters of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), specifically by parameterizing the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) cost matrix via a Mahalanobis distance over word embeddings. The authors additionally propose learning histogram coefficients to reweight document representations, building on Lebanon's work in metric learning for text. They derive gradients for NCA-style objective functions and present compelling empirical validation. The method effectively combines established techniques into a coherent framework, with the experimental evaluation serving as a significant strength. The core idea is simple yet non-trivial to implement and validate, making the results particularly convincing. I recommend acceptance.

**Strengths:** The approach demonstrates clear practical value through strong experimental results. The simplicity of the core formulation, coupled with its effective integration of existing concepts (Mahalanobis metric learning, histogram reweighting), is a notable asset. The experimental section thoroughly supports the claimed advantages over baselines.

**Key Improvements Needed:**

1.  **Mathematical Rigor and Notation:** The manuscript requires greater precision in mathematical presentation. Notable issues include:
    *   Inconsistent treatment of the ground metric: Equation (1) uses the Euclidean distance between embeddings, while Equation (6) employs the *squared* Euclidean distance (implying a 2-Wasserstein formulation). To avoid confusion, the paper should consistently define WMD as the *p*-Wasserstein distance between word embedding clouds (specifying *p*) or explicitly state the metric used throughout (e.g., "using the Euclidean ground metric, *p*=1").
    *   The notation and derivations for the reweighting coefficients (Equation 5) and the gradient computations need tightening. A clearer presentation of the optimization steps and assumptions is essential.

2.  **Algorithmic Clarity:** An explicit algorithmic box outlining the full optimization procedure (including initialization, updates for both the metric and reweighting coefficients, and convergence criteria) would significantly enhance the paper's accessibility and reproducibility. This is crucial given the non-trivial implementation aspects noted.

3.  **Related Work and Differentiation:** The paper should more explicitly contrast its contributions with prior work:
    *   While building on Cuturi & Avis (2014) for the EMD framework, it crucially *introduces entropy regularization* (a key factor enabling scalability) and *parameterizes the continuous ground metric* (using linear maps, unlike their discrete cost). These distinctions need highlighting.
    *   The connection to Lebanon (2007) for histogram reweighting (Section 3.1) is valid but should be more precisely positioned. The method is not novel in reweighting itself, but its *integration* within the WMD framework with metric learning is the contribution. Clarify this nuance.
    *   The reference to Wang et al. (2015) is problematic. The cited work ("Wang et al.") is not published and appears to be a preprint or internal report. Its relevance should be reconsidered; if the core idea is similar but unverified, the paper should focus on the established prior work (Lebanon, Cuturi & Avis) instead. The claim that their OT optimization "does not work as proposed" requires careful justification or removal.

4.  **Critical Analysis of Key Components:** The paper lacks analysis on the *impact* of the learned weight vector **w** (Equation 5). Specifically:
    *   How significant is the contribution of the reweighting compared to just learning the Mahalanobis metric?
    *   Are the learned weights interpretable or meaningful (e.g., do they emphasize specific semantic features)? Providing insights into the importance and utility of **w** is essential to justify this component.

**Minor Comments:**
*   Correct "Malahanobis" to "Mahalanobis" (e.g., in the description of Eq. 6).
*   Ensure consistent use of "WMD" versus "EMD" (WMD is a specific instance of EMD for text). Define WMD clearly early on.
*   The phrase "the authors propose also to learn" (referring to reweighting) is slightly awkward; rephrase for clarity (e.g., "the authors additionally propose learning...").
*   Clarify the role of regularization (e.g., entropy) in the context of the optimization, especially regarding the interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations, as hinted in the draft.

**Conclusion:** This is a strong paper with a clear, well-motivated contribution that delivers convincing empirical results. The core idea is valuable and well-executed, making it a worthy addition to the literature. Addressing the points above—particularly enhancing mathematical rigor, providing a clear algorithmic description, and offering deeper analysis of the reweighting component—will significantly strengthen the manuscript. The minor issues identified do not detract from the paper's overall merit. I strongly support acceptance.
