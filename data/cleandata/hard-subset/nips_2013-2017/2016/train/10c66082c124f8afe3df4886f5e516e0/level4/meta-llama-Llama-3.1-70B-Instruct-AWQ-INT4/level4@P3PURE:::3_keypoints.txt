- Proposes learning WMD/EMD ground metric via a Mahalanobis distance on word embeddings and also learns histogram reweighting coefficients.  
- Provides gradients for NCA-type costs and offers compelling experimental evidence.  
- Combines existing techniques in a simple, straightforward way that yields convincing results; reviewer supports acceptance.  
- Strength: simplicity and strong experimental section despite non-trivial implementation.  
- Improve handling of the interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations.  
- Improve mathematical rigor and notation.  
- Include an algorithmic box to clearly illustrate the proposed method.  
- Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); recommend stating WMD as p-Wasserstein to avoid inconsistency.  
- The reweighting approach in Eq.5 is not novel; similar ideas appear in Guy Lebanon’s work and Le/Cuturi.  
- Provide experimental analysis on the importance and impact of the learned weight vector w.  
- Related work should more clearly distinguish this paper from prior work (e.g., Cuturi/Avis: entropy regularization for scalability; continuous cost parameterized by linear maps).  
- Note issues with Wang et al.: their OT optimization is run only once and appears not to work as proposed.
