- Paper proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform (A) and a word-distribution reweighting (w).
- Manuscript is well-written, shows strong related-work knowledge, and evaluates the method on multiple (including novel) datasets.
- Motivation for learnable reweighting w is insufficiently explained; concern that reweighting may fundamentally alter document meaning and needs justification.
- Contribution seems incremental: applying a Non-convex Amortized Coordinate Ascent objective to learn A and w is a natural extension rather than a major novelty.
- The method’s deployment is unclear: the paper does not explain how w is determined for unseen/new documents.
- The term “semantic difference” is vague/misleading and should be explicitly defined and used consistently.
- Reviewer conditionally supports acceptance if authors address the above concerns, strengthen justification for reweighting and significance, clarify unseen-document applicability, and define “semantic difference.”
