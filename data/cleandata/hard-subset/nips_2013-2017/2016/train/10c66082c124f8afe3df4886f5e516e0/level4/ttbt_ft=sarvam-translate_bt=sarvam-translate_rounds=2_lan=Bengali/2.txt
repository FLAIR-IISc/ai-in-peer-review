Here is an extension of the term "mover distance" that uses supervised learning to learn weight in distance. This paper contains a very detailed experimental evaluation. I am glad that someone has adopted the "mover distance" method and used differential training to learn domain-specific metrics. The method is correct, well-explained, and includes accurate information. The tests are very extensive across various classification data sets and various baseline methods and features. The proposed method has surpassed all other methods by a significant margin. It is a powerful paper that I would like to see presented at NIPS.
