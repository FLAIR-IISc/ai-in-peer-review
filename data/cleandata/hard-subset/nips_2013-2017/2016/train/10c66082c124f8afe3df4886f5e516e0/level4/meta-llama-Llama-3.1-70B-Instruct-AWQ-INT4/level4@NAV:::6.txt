This paper introduces a novel approach to metric learning for textual documents, expanding on the existing "Word Mover's Distance" method. The original method is unsupervised, focusing on the distance between word embeddings. In contrast, this work adapts it to a supervised setting, where the supervision signal is based on document class membership. The authors have promised to make the MATLAB code available.

The authors propose two key components for learning the distance metric: a linear transformation A (a traditional approach) and word importance weights w. I would have appreciated a clearer explanation of the intuition behind Eq (5), which defines the usage of w, as well as insight into which component contributes to the reported performance improvements. The experimental evaluation focuses on a classification task. While assessing a distance metric can be challenging, a comparison with a simple classifier on the same datasets would have been informative for context.

Several aspects of the paper require clarification. Firstly, the application of Eq (2) is unclear to me. Additionally, the lambda in Eq (2) appears to differ from the lambda in line 170. The authors' claim that their method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" (line 316) lacks supporting evidence. 

Minor issues include the unclear reference to "the average error across datasets" on the right (line 257), which does not seem to be presented in Table 3. Furthermore, the distinction between "n" and "ne" in Table 2 is not explicitly stated. Lastly, the definition of k for kNN classification is not provided, and it is unclear whether this value is dataset-dependent or method-dependent.
