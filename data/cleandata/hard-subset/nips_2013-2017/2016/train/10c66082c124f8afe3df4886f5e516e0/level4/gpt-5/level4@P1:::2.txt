This paper presents a supervised extension of the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) that incorporates label information to learn task-specific weights for the distance, enabling discriminative training of a domain-specific metric. The approach is sound and clearly described, with an appropriate level of technical detail.

The experimental evaluation is very comprehensive: the authors test on several classification datasets and compare against a wide range of baseline features and methods. Across these settings, the proposed method consistently outperforms the baselines, often by a substantial margin. I am pleased to see WMD extended in this direction, and the results convincingly demonstrate the benefits of supervision for this metric.

Overall, this is a solid and well-executed contribution that advances the practical effectiveness of WMD through supervised learning. I would be happy to see it presented at NIPS.
