This paper proposes a supervised approach to learning the distance between documents based on the Word Mover's Distance (WMD). The WMD, inspired by the Earth Mover's Distance, calculates the optimal transport between two word distributions to obtain the document distance. Initially, word2vec embeddings are used to compute the transport distance between words. The authors extend this idea by learning a linear transformation of the embedded words and a reweighted word distribution, aiming to increase the separation between documents of different labels.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, demonstrating a thorough understanding of related literature through a comprehensive review. The authors provide experimental results on various novel datasets, showcasing the effectiveness of their algorithm. However, several aspects of the paper require further clarification and motivation.

Firstly, the formulation could benefit from a more detailed explanation. Specifically, the use of reweighting the word distribution using 'w' lacks clear motivation and may potentially alter the document's meaning entirely. It is essential to justify this reweighting and demonstrate its effectiveness in preserving the document's original meaning.

Furthermore, the significance of this contribution is relatively low. The extension of WMD to the supervised setting using a linear transformation and reweighting with a Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA) objective function, although a possible approach, does not present a groundbreaking or innovative idea.

Additionally, the authors should address how their algorithm can be applied to find a distance for a completely new document. Specifically, it is unclear how the reweight 'w' can be determined for an unseen document.

Lastly, the term 'semantic difference' in the abstract appears to refer to the label of the document. However, it is unclear whether this term encompasses other aspects, such as authorship or topic differences. The authors should provide a clearer definition of 'semantic difference' to avoid ambiguity.

In general, the paper is well-structured and clearly written. Addressing the above concerns and providing further explanations and justifications would strengthen the paper. With these clarifications, the paper's acceptance would be more justified.
