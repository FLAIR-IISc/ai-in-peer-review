- Introduces a supervised metric learning method built on word-movers distance, extending to transport distances with squared-Euclidean ground cost after a linear projection.  
- Learns both a linear projection and word-specific reweighting coefficients (frequency reweight + renormalisation).  
- Proposes an efficient optimisation scheme for fitting the parameterised metric.  
- Empirical study is comprehensive and shows effectiveness on multiple kNN text classification datasets against reasonable baselines.  
- Potential impact limited: WMD with metric learning initialized by a “word centroid distance” performs nearly as well as the fully learned metric (small gap in Table 3).  
- Missing evaluation: Table 3 does not test the word-centroid embedding paired with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Lacks ablation analysis separating the effects of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A; report isolated-component performance.  
- Originality is mainly in the metric optimisation procedure; linear metric learning within earth-movers distance is compelling but a modest contribution.  
- Paper is very well written and pleasant to read.  
- Minor clarifications requested: ambiguous reference at Line 152 (“The authors” — which paper?), Equation 11 should mark alpha and beta as starred, and explicitly state around Line 170 that this is the Sinkhorn scaling referenced earlier.
