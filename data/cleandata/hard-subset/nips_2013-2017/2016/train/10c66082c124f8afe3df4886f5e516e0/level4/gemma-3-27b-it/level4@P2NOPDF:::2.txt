This paper introduces a compelling extension to Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) by incorporating supervised learning to refine the weighting of distances between words. The core idea – learning a domain-specific metric through discriminative training applied to the WMD framework – is a valuable contribution, and it’s encouraging to see this direction explored. 

The method itself is clearly and concisely presented, providing an appropriate level of detail for reproducibility and understanding. The authors strike a good balance between theoretical explanation and practical implementation. A significant strength of this work lies in its exceptionally thorough experimental evaluation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach across multiple classification datasets, comparing against a comprehensive set of baselines utilizing a diverse range of features. 

Notably, the proposed method consistently and substantially outperforms all competing approaches. The margin of improvement is significant, suggesting a genuine advancement over existing techniques. This robust performance, coupled with the clear presentation and sound methodology, makes this a strong submission. 

I believe this is a solid and well-executed paper that would be a valuable addition to the NIPS program, and I recommend it for acceptance.
