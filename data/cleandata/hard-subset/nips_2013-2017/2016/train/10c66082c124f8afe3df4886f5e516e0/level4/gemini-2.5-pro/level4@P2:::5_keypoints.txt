- Proposal: S-WMD — a supervised document metric that learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and word-importance weights for text classification.  
- Objective: Components are jointly learned to minimize a stochastic approximation of leave-one-out kNN classification error.  
- Technical trick: WMD is approximated with entropic Sinkhorn distance, making optimization differentiable and reducing complexity from O(q³) to O(q²).  
- Empirics: Thorough evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing state-of-the-art average performance.  

Strengths:
- Significance: Addresses a clear limitation of WMD by incorporating supervision, producing a practically useful metric.  
- Technical quality: Sound method; Sinkhorn relaxation is an elegant, efficient solution and learning both transform and weights adds flexibility.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with clear background and derivations.  
- Evaluation: Comprehensive, diverse baselines and consistent superior performance.

Weaknesses / suggestions:
- Incremental novelty: Core idea (learning linear transform for ground metric) has precedents; novelty is mainly its application to WMD plus Sinkhorn use.  
- Hyperparameter analysis lacking: Insufficient discussion/ablation of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter lambda and its effect on approximation vs. speed.  
- Presentation of baselines: Overemphasis on WCD/S-WCD in tables is potentially confusing; key comparisons should highlight S-WMD vs WMD and top baselines.

- Overall: Well-executed work with strengths outweighing weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
