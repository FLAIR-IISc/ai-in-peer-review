This paper presents an extension to Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) that leverages supervision to learn weights within the distance calculation. The authors demonstrate a very extensive experimental evaluation, and I am pleased to see the WMD approach extended with discriminative training to learn a domain-specific metric. The method is sound and clearly explained, providing an appropriate level of detail. The experiments are comprehensive, covering several classification datasets and a wide variety of baseline methods and features. Notably, the proposed method consistently outperforms all baselines by a significant margin. This is a strong paper, and I would be very happy to see it presented at NIPS. 

I found the extensive experimentation particularly convincing. The ablation studies, for example, are well-designed and clearly demonstrate the contribution of each component of the proposed method. The choice of datasets is also appropriate, covering a range of complexities and application areas.

One minor point: while the paper thoroughly evaluates performance, a discussion of the computational cost associated with learning and applying the weighted WMD would be valuable. Although the performance gains are substantial, understanding the trade-off between accuracy and efficiency is important for potential adopters. This could be addressed with a brief analysis or perhaps reporting training/inference times.

Overall, this paper represents a substantial contribution to the field. The combination of a well-motivated method, thorough experimentation, and clear writing makes it a strong candidate for acceptance to NIPS. I recommend accepting this paper.
