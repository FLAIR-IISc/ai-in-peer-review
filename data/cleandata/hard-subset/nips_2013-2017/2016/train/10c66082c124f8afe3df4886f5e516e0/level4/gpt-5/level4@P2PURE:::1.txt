This paper introduces a supervised metric learning method built on the recent Word Mover’s Distance [19] (a transport distance defined over word2vec embeddings). The approach applies to any transport distance whose ground cost is a squared Euclidean distance in a linearly projected space. Beyond learning the linear projection to improve performance, it also learns per-word weights: each term frequency is reweighted by a learned coefficient and then renormalized. The paper presents an efficient optimization procedure to learn these parameters. A thorough experimental evaluation compares against many reasonable baselines on multiple document classification datasets, using a k-NN classifier with the learned metric, and demonstrates strong performance.

Strengths
- A meaningful extension of Word Mover’s Distance that is technically interesting and shown to be effective on several k-NN text classification tasks.

Limitations and concerns
- The overall impact appears limited because Earth Mover’s Distance with metric learning initialized via a “word centroid distance” performs similarly to the fully learned metric. See the difference between the last two rows in Table 3. This suggests the main technical contributions add little beyond this initialization based on existing techniques.
- Table 3 is missing an evaluation of the “word centroid embedding” (the weighted mean of word embeddings for a document) combined with all the tested metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- The evaluation does not disentangle the contributions of the word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A. An ablation showing performance with each component enabled in isolation would clarify their relative importance.

Originality
- The novelty primarily lies in the optimization strategy (Lines 156–175). While the idea of linear metric learning within Earth Mover’s Distance is interesting, it is a limited contribution on its own.

Presentation
- The paper is very well written and a pleasure to read.
