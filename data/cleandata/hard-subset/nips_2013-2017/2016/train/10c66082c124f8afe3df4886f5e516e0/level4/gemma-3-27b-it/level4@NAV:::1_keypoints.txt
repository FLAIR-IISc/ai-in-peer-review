- Proposes supervised metric learning that generalizes word-movers distance to transport distances after a linear projection, learning both the projection and word-specific weights to re-weight and renormalize word frequencies.
- Presents an efficient optimization strategy to learn this parameterized transport metric.
- Empirical results show the method is effective for kNN-based document classification across multiple datasets and baselines.
- Practical impact appears limited: performance is comparable to earth-movers distance initialized with a “word centroid distance” (Table 3), suggesting only modest improvement over that initialization.
- Recommends adding a comparison between the weighted word-centroid embedding and existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- Recommends an ablation study separating the effects of word-specific weights and the projection matrix A to quantify each component’s contribution.
- Claims of originality are mainly due to the optimization approach (Lines 156–175); integrating linear metric learning into earth-movers distance is interesting but modest.
- Paper is very well written and clear.
- Minor clarifications: specify which authors “[1,7,8]” refers to at Line 152; add asterisks to α and β in Equation 11 for consistency; explicitly link the discussion around Line 170 to the Sinkhorn scaling mentioned at Line 56.
