- Paper extends supervised WMD by learning word-specific weights and using Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy for Wasserstein.
- Main empirical claim: best average performance over eight datasets against twenty-six baselines.
- Manuscript is very well written with clear problem statement and accessible mathematical treatment.
- Limited novelty compared to the original supervised WMD approach.
- Discussion and relevance of the Word Centroid Distance (WCD) comparison is tangential and confusing.
- Role of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter lambda is under-discussed and can substantially affect the objective.
- Theoretically, larger lambda approaches true Wasserstein but slows convergence; small lambda for speed can produce markedly different behavior.
- Reviewer requests clarification whether the chosen lambda places the method closer to pure Wasserstein or entropically regularized (Sinkhorn) regime.
- Reviewer asks how lambda was selected (cross-validation or heuristic) and how sensitive results are to this choice.
- Reviewer asks whether larger lambda values empirically recover Wasserstein behavior and what the runtime/accuracy trade-off is.
- Overall assessment: strong, clearly presented paper with convincing results but modest incremental novelty and needs deeper clarification on lambda and WCD motivation.
