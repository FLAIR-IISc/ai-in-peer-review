This paper presents a supervised approach to learning document distances based on the word mover's distance (WMD), which was previously proposed as an adaptation of the earth mover's distance. Specifically, it calculates the optimal transport between two word distributions to determine the distance between documents, initially utilizing word2vec embeddings for the transport distance between words. The authors propose learning a linear transformation of embedded words and a reweighted word distribution to enhance separation between documents of different labels. The paper is well-written, easy to follow, and demonstrates a thorough understanding of related works through a comprehensive literature review. The authors also provide experimental results on various novel datasets. However, several aspects require further clarification. Firstly, the motivation behind formulating the reweighting of word distributions using 'w' needs more explanation, as it appears to potentially alter the document's meaning entirely. A clear justification for this reweighting, ensuring it does not change the document's essence, would be beneficial. Secondly, the significance of this contribution is relatively low, as adopting a linear transformation and reweighting with a neighborhood component analysis (NCA) objective function in a supervised WMD setting, although a possible extension, does not represent a groundbreaking innovation. Furthermore, it is unclear how the algorithm would determine the distance for a completely new document, particularly in terms of finding an appropriate reweight 'w'. Additionally, the term 'semantic difference' in the abstract appears to refer to label differences, but it is unclear whether it encompasses other aspects, such as authorship or topic differences. Overall, while the paper is well-written, addressing these concerns would strengthen the work, and I do not object to its acceptance pending suitable explanations.
