- Introduces supervised metric learning based on word-movers distance, generalizing to transport distances after linear projection.  
- Jointly learns a linear projection matrix and document-specific word weights (reweighting term frequencies with renormalization).  
- Proposes an efficient optimization procedure for the parameterized metric.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on document classification with k-NN, extending word-movers distance.  
- Impact may be limited: EMD with metric learning initialized by word-centroid distance performs nearly identical to the full method (Table 3).  
- Suggests comparing word-centroid embedding with established metric learners (ITML, LMNN, NCA) in Table 3.  
- Recommends ablation testing to measure the individual contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Originality mainly in the optimization strategy (Lines 156–175); core idea of linear metric learning within EMD is a modest contribution.  
- Paper is well-written and clear.  
- Minor clarifications requested: specify "the authors of [1,7,8]" (Line 152), check whether α and β should include stars in Equation 11, and confirm use of Sinkhorn scaling referenced near Line 56.
