- Paper proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform (A) and a word-distribution reweighting (w) to separate labeled documents.
- Writing and literature review are clear and thorough, and experiments cover multiple novel datasets.
- Motivation for the reweighting vector w is underdeveloped; reweighting may distort document identity and needs justification, constraints, or regularization.
- The contribution’s novelty is limited—combining A and w with an NCA-style objective seems incremental relative to prior supervised metric learning and supervised optimal transport.
- It is unclear how the method generalizes to unseen documents: if w is document-specific, test-time inference is not explained; if global, this must be stated and justified.
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified to align with labels (topic/authorship) versus embedding-space semantics.
- Overall: solid writing and empirical work, but the paper needs stronger justification for reweighting, clearer novelty claims, and a clarified inference procedure before endorsement.
