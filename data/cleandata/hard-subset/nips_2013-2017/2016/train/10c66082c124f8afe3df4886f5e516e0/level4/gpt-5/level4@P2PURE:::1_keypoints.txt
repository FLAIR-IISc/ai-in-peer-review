- Introduces supervised metric learning based on Word Mover’s Distance for transport distances with squared Euclidean ground cost in a projected space.
- Learns a linear projection and per-word weights (term frequencies reweighted and renormalized) with an efficient optimization procedure.
- Thorough experimental evaluation on multiple document classification datasets using k-NN with the learned metric, showing strong performance.
- Strength: Meaningful, technically interesting extension of WMD effective for k-NN text classification tasks.
- Concern: Limited impact because EMD with metric learning initialized by a “word centroid distance” performs similarly to the fully learned metric (Table 3, last two rows).
- Missing comparison: Table 3 does not evaluate the word centroid embedding combined with metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- Missing ablation: Evaluation does not disentangle contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A; need performance with each component alone.
- Originality: Main novelty is the optimization strategy; linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a limited standalone contribution.
- Presentation: Paper is very well written and a pleasure to read.

- Minor clarification: In Equation 11, alpha and beta should likely be starred.
- Minor clarification: Explicitly state around line 170 that this is the Sinkhorn scaling referenced in the introduction.
