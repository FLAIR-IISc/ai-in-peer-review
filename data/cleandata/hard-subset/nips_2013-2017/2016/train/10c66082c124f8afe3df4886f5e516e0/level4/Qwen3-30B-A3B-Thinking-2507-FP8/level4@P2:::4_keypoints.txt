- Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of WMD to learn document distance metrics.  
- Learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word importance weight vector to minimize leave-one-out kNN error.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance to efficiently approximate WMD gradients, making training practical.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear connection to metric learning frameworks like Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA).  
- Thorough literature review situating the work within related NIPS and metric-learning research.  
- Well-motivated extension of unsupervised WMD to supervised settings, addressing a known limitation.  
- Clear and well-organized mathematical formulation, including optimal transport, regularization, and gradient computation.  
- Extensive experiments on eight datasets and comparisons to 26 baselines, showing consistent improvements.  
- t-SNE visualizations effectively demonstrate improved class separation versus unsupervised baselines.  
- Originality: novel combination of linear embedding transform and word-specific weights within the WMD/Sinkhorn framework, distinct from prior EMD metric-learning work.  
- Limited significance: performance gains are modest (typically 1–5% error reduction) and the practical impact is not convincingly argued.  
- Weak motivation and explanation for the word importance weight vector w and how it preserves semantic content.  
- Unclear handling of new/unseen documents—whether w is global or per-document and how to generalize is not specified.  
- Ambiguous use of “semantic difference” in the abstract; should clarify that the method captures label-based dissimilarity, not necessarily semantic similarity.  
- Recommendation: technically sound and well-written; recommend acceptance pending revisions to justify significance and address w, generalization to new documents, and terminology clarifications.
