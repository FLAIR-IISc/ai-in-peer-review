- Proposes a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform of embeddings (A) and a reweighting of word distributions (w) to better separate labeled documents.  
- Paper is clearly written, with a thorough related work review and experiments on many (including novel) datasets.  
- Motivation for the reweighting w is weak: it could substantially alter a document and needs justification or demonstration that it preserves document meaning.  
- Contribution appears incremental: A + w with an NCA objective is a plausible but not clearly novel or groundbreaking extension.  
- Unclear test-time procedure: how is the reweight w computed for new, unseen documents?  
- Ambiguous use of “semantic difference” in the abstract—needs clarification whether it refers to labels, topic, authorship, or something else.  
- Overall significance seems limited; the paper could be acceptable if the above concerns are adequately addressed.
