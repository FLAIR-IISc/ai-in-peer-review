

The paper proposes a novel supervised metric learning approach for textual documents, extending the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance (WMD) framework. The authors introduce two key innovations: a linear transformation matrix **A** (the traditional approach) and word importance weights **w**. The method is evaluated on classification tasks, with the authors promising to release MATLAB code upon publication. The authors' response to prior queries has adequately addressed my initial concerns regarding the experimental setup and claims.  

I request clarification on two technical points:  
1. The intuition behind Equation (5), which defines the role of **w**, remains unclear. Specifically, the authors should elucidate which component (the linear transformation **A** or the word weights **w**) primarily drives the reported performance improvements.  
2. The usage of Equation (2) is ambiguous. The parameter λ in Equation (2) appears distinct from the λ referenced in line 170; the authors should explicitly justify this distinction.  

Regarding the claim in line 316—"which is particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words"—the authors should provide empirical or theoretical justification for this assertion.  

Additional concerns:  
- Line 257 states "on the right we also show the average error across datasets," but this claim is unsupported in Table 3 (or elsewhere in the paper).  
- Table 2 uses both "n" and "ne"; the distinction between these notations is not defined.  
- The value of *k* for kNN classification is not specified (e.g., whether it is dataset-dependent or method-specific), which is critical for reproducibility.  

While the evaluation focuses on distance-based classification, it would strengthen the work to include a baseline showing the performance of a simple classifier (e.g., linear SVM) on the same datasets, to contextualize the metric's contribution.
