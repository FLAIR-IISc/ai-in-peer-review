This paper introduces a supervised metric learning approach leveraging the word-movers distance [19] – a transport distance applied to word2vec embeddings – and generalizes it to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances following linear projection. The method learns both this linear projection for optimal performance and a weighting scheme for individual words, specifically re-weighting word frequencies within documents before re-normalization. An efficient optimization procedure is presented for learning this parameterized metric, and a comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrates its effectiveness. The approach is compared against numerous strong baselines on several document classification datasets, utilizing a kNN classifier based on the learned metric. 

This work represents a technically sound and valuable extension of the recent word-movers distance, proving effective in kNN text classification tasks. However, the potential impact may be somewhat constrained. The results indicate that an earth-movers distance initialized with a metric learning approach based on “word centroid distance” achieves comparable performance to the full learning process proposed in this paper, as evidenced by the last two rows of Table 3. Consequently, the core technical contributions do not appear to offer a substantial improvement over this existing initialization technique. 

The evaluation would benefit from including a comparison of the “word centroid embedding” (the weighted mean of word embeddings for a document) against all tested metric learning approaches (ITML, LMNN, NCA). Furthermore, assessing the individual contributions of the word-specific weights and the projection matrix A would be insightful; reporting performance with each component active in isolation would be valuable. The originality primarily lies in the optimization method (Lines 156–175), and while integrating linear metric learning within the earth-movers distance is interesting, it constitutes a relatively incremental contribution.
