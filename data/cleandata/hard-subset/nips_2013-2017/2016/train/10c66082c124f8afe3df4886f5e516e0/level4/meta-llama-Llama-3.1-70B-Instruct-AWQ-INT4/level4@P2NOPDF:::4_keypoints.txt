- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance by learning a linear transform of embeddings and a reweighted word distribution.
- Uses word2vec for initial embeddings and optimizes separation between labels with an NCA objective.
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and includes a comprehensive literature review.
- Experimental results on several novel datasets demonstrate the algorithm's effectiveness.
- Formulation needs clearer explanation, especially the motivation and implications of reweighting the word distribution 'w'.
- Reweighting 'w' may alter document meaning; authors must justify how meaning is preserved.
- Contribution is incremental; supervised linear transform + reweighting is not highly novel.
- Unclear how to compute the reweight 'w' or distance for unseen/new documents—needs a practical procedure.
- Term "semantic difference" is ambiguous (labels, authorship, topic, etc.) and should be clearly defined.
