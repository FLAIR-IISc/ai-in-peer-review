- Proposes learning WMD parameters by parameterizing the EMD ground metric with a Mahalanobis distance and learning histogram reweighting coefficients.  
- Derives gradients for an NCA-style cost and presents strong experimental validation.  
- Overall assessment: straightforward idea but non-trivial implementation and convincing results; reviewer supports acceptance.  

Strengths:
- Clear, well-motivated objective to learn a better metric for WMD.  
- Experimental section is strong and convincing.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Clarify the interplay between regularized and non-regularized EMD formulations throughout the paper.  
- Improve mathematical presentation and fix inconsistent notation/computations.  
- Add an algorithm box summarizing the training procedure for clarity.

Detailed issues:
- Inconsistent distance metrics: Eq.1 uses Euclidean (1-Wasserstein) while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); consider framing WMD as p-Wasserstein.  
- Reweighting novelty: histogram reweighting is not new and should cite prior work (Guy Lebanon; Le & Cuturi).  
- Unclear impact of learned weight vector w: request experimental ablation or analysis of this component.  
- Related work needs strengthening: explicitly contrast with Cuturi & Avis (entropy regularization and continuous cost parameterization) and clarify limitations of Wang et al. (single OT optimization).
