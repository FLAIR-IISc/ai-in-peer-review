This paper presents a supervised approach to learning inter-document distances built upon Word Mover’s Distance (WMD). WMD was introduced previously as an adaptation of Earth Mover’s Distance. Specifically, it computes optimal transport from one distribution of words to another to define a document distance, with pairwise word transport costs initially derived from word2vec embeddings. The authors propose to learn (1) a linear transformation of the embedded words and (2) a reweighting of the word distribution, with the aim of better separating documents with different labels.

The manuscript is clearly written and easy to follow. It offers a thorough review of related work, demonstrating a strong understanding of the literature. The authors provide experimental results on many datasets, including novel ones. However, the motivation for the formulation needs further clarification, and the overall significance seems limited.

1) Why do the authors reweight the word distribution with w? The motivation is not clearly articulated, and this reweighting appears capable of turning a document into a substantially different one. If the authors can show why such reweighting helps without effectively altering the document, the choice would be better justified.

2) The contribution appears modest. Using a linear transform A and reweighting w within an NCA objective is a reasonable supervised extension of WMD, but the formulation is neither particularly novel nor especially sophisticated.

3) How does the algorithm compute a distance for a new document? For a completely unseen document, how is the reweight vector w determined?

4) In the abstract, “semantic difference” seems to correspond to the document’s label. Differences in authorship or topic do not obviously constitute semantic differences. Does “semantic difference” mean something else here?

Overall, the paper is well written, and I would not oppose acceptance if these questions are satisfactorily addressed.
