- Introduces a supervised extension to Word Mover’s Distance that learns distance weights for domain-specific text classification.  
- Leverages discriminative training to adapt the unsupervised WMD metric, addressing its poor generalization across domains.  
- Method is theoretically grounded and described with sufficient detail for reproducibility without unnecessary complexity.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across multiple benchmarks (20 Newsgroups, IMDB, RCV1) and diverse baselines.  
- Tests robustness across feature configurations (GloVe, Word2Vec, fastText) and architectures, showing consistent improvements.  
- Reports accuracy gains of 3.2%–8.7% over competitors, often statistically significant and practically meaningful.  
- Achieves improvements with minimal computational overhead by efficiently refining embeddings instead of full retraining.  
- Ablation studies demonstrate learned weights capture domain-relevant semantics and provide interpretability, not mere overfitting.  
- Minor noted limitations: scalability to very large vocabularies and applicability to cross-lingual settings could be discussed.  
- Writing, figures, and tables are clear and effective; overall a strong, well-executed contribution recommended for acceptance.
