- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance (WMSD) that learns a linear transformation A and word-specific importance weights w; authors will release Matlab code.  
- Reviewer appreciated the authors’ thorough response to initial questions.  
- Strength: Novel contribution—well-motivated supervised adaptation of WMD for classification tasks.  
- Strength: Methodology—joint learning of a global transformation A and per-word weights w to reshape semantic space and re-weight words.  
- Strength: Empirical performance—WMSD consistently outperforms original WMD and other metric learning baselines on several datasets.  
- Weakness: Lacks intuition for the specific form of the word weights in Equation (5); needs clearer conceptual explanation.  
- Weakness: Missing ablation study to isolate contributions of A vs. w (e.g., A-only with w=1 and w-only with A=I).  
- Weakness: Claim that the method is “particularly forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words needs stronger justification or an example showing supervised improvement.  
- Minor: Clarify role of Equation (2) and whether its regularization parameter lambda is the same as in the main optimization.  
- Minor: Fix table/figure references—locate or correct the stated “average error across datasets” and define column headers “n” and “ne” in Table 2.  
- Minor: Describe hyperparameter selection for k in k-NN (cross-validation per dataset/method or a fixed value) for reproducibility.
