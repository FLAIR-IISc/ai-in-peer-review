- Proposal: learn WMD/EMD parameters by parameterizing the cost matrix with a Mahalanobis distance and learning histogram weights, with gradients for NCA-type costs and strong experimental support.  
- Strengths: conceptually simple, non-trivial implementation, and convincing experimental validation.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Clarify interplay between regularized and non-regularized OT formulations.  
- Improve mathematical rigor: computations and notations are imprecise and need tightening.  
- Add an algorithmic box to clearly present the proposed method.  
- Inconsistency: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); suggest framing WMD as p-Wasserstein to avoid confusion.  
- Novelty concern: the reweighting approach in Eq.5 is not new—similar to Guy Lebanon’s prior work on metric learning for text.  
- Missing related work: cite “Unsupervised Riemannian Metric Learning for Histograms Using Aitchison Transformations” (Le and Cuturi).  
- Need additional experiments: clarify the importance and practical impact of the learned weight vector w.  
- Related work should more explicitly distinguish this paper from Cuturi and Avis, highlighting entropy regularization and use of a continuous, linearly parameterized cost versus prior discrete costs.  
- Note on Wang et al.: it’s unpublished and appears ineffective due to a single, non-iterative OT optimization.
