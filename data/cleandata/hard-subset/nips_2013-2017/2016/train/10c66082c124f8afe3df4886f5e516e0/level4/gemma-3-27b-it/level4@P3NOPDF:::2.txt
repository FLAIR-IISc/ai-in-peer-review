This paper presents a compelling extension to the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD), introducing a supervised learning approach to refine the distance metric by learning weights specific to the task at hand. This is a significant and welcome contribution; the original WMD, while insightful, often benefits from domain adaptation, and this work directly addresses that need through discriminative training. 

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the methodology with an appropriate level of detail. The authors strike a good balance between providing necessary background and focusing on the novel aspects of their approach. The motivation for learning weighted distances is clearly articulated, and the mathematical formulation is presented in a digestible manner.

The experimental evaluation is remarkably thorough. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method across a diverse range of text classification datasets, and rigorously compare it against a comprehensive suite of established baseline methods and feature representations. The consistently strong performance – and often substantial margin of improvement – observed across these experiments strongly supports the efficacy of the proposed approach. The inclusion of multiple datasets and baselines greatly strengthens the conclusions. 

Specifically, the breadth of the comparison is commendable. The authors do not simply compare against standard word embedding baselines; they also consider more sophisticated techniques, demonstrating a nuanced understanding of the relevant literature. The ablation studies presented help to isolate the impact of different components of the proposed method, further solidifying the analysis. 

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of natural language processing. The approach is sound, the presentation is clear, and the experimental results are convincing. I believe this work would be a strong addition to the NIPS program and I enthusiastically recommend it for acceptance.
