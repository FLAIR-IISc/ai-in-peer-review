- Proposes supervised metric learning that extends WMD by learning a linear projection of embeddings and word-specific importance weights.  
- Framework generalizes to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances after linear projection.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization method for learning the parameterized metric.  
- Validated via extensive experiments: 26 baselines across eight datasets using kNN classification.  
- Shows strong empirical performance and effectively incorporates supervision into the WMD framework.  
- Impact may be limited because unsupervised word centroid distance (WCD) initialization performs comparably to fully learned S-WMD.  
- Lacks comparisons applying standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) directly to word centroid embeddings.  
- Needs an ablation study to quantify the individual contributions of learned word weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Originality is modest; main novelty lies in the optimization strategy rather than a fundamentally new concept.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.  
- Minor clarifications requested: identify "The authors" (Line 152), mark α and β with stars in Eq. 11, and clarify that the scaling near Line 170 refers to earlier Sinkhorn scaling.  
- Arguments for acceptance: extends WMD, strong empirical results, efficient/technical contribution, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest improvement over WCD, evaluation incomplete, and incremental novelty.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable and technically sound work; recommend acceptance with suggested additional evaluations in future revisions.
