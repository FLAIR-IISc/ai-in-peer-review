- Paper proposes S-WMD: supervised metric learning for WMD that jointly optimizes an affine transform of embeddings (A) and word importance weights (w), using Sinkhorn relaxation for efficient gradient estimation.
- Evaluated on eight text classification tasks against 26 baselines, showing consistent improvements over unsupervised WMD and other document representations.
- Strength: Technically sound adaptation of metric learning to optimal transport, building coherently on prior WMD and NCA work.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation with comprehensive baselines and diverse datasets.
- Strength: Sinkhorn-based approximation yields substantial computational efficiency gains (Table 3).
- Weakness: S-WMD yields only marginal gains over its initialization S-WCD on most datasets, questioning the added value of learning A and w.
- Weakness: Missing critical ablation studies—performance using only w or only A is not reported.
- Weakness: The full S-WCD baseline (word centroid + ITML/LMNN/NCA) is omitted, preventing assessment of relative contributions.
- Originality: Novel application of linear metric learning within WMD; main technical novelty is the efficient Sinkhorn gradient approximation.
- Significance: Offers a practical supervised extension to WMD but limited impact due to small improvements over simple initialization; authors should justify joint learning and add ablations.
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written with minor fixes suggested (clarify references [1,7,8], add stars on α*/β* in Eq.11, state Sinkhorn scaling explicitly).
