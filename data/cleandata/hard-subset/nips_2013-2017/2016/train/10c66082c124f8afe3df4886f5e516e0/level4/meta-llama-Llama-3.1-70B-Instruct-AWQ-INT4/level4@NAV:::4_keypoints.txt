- Proposes a supervised method to learn document distances based on Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) using a linear transformation of word embeddings and a reweighted word distribution.  
- Builds on prior WMD/earth mover’s distance work and initially uses word2vec for word transport costs.  
- Claims improvement by learning a linear embedding transform and per-word reweighting to better separate labeled documents.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and demonstrates comprehensive knowledge of related work.  
- Provides experimental results on several novel datasets.  
- Motivation for the reweighting vector w is insufficiently explained and may arbitrarily change document meaning—needs clearer justification that essence is preserved.  
- Contribution seems incremental: linear transform + reweighting optimized with an NCA objective is not a major conceptual advance.  
- Unclear how the model handles completely new documents—specifically how an appropriate reweight w would be determined for unseen texts.  
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous in the abstract (appears to mean label differences) and should clarify whether it includes authorship, topic, or other distinctions.  
- Reviewer has no objection to acceptance provided the above concerns are addressed with suitable explanations.
