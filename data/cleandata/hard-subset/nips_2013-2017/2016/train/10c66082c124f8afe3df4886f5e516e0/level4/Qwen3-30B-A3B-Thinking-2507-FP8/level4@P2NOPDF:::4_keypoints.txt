- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and a per-document word-distribution reweighting w to maximize label separation.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, grounded in related work, and supported by comprehensive experiments on multiple datasets.  
- Motivation for the reweighting vector w is weak: authors do not justify why reweighting is necessary or show it preserves a document’s semantic content.  
- Reweighting may fundamentally alter document meaning (amplifying irrelevant words or suppressing key terms); need evidence that learned w reflects meaningful characteristics rather than optimization artifacts.  
- Contribution is incremental: combining a linear transform and reweighting within an NCA objective is a straightforward, expected supervised adaptation of WMD rather than a major technical advance.  
- Lacks a clear generalization mechanism for unseen documents, since w is learned per training document and no procedure is given to infer w out-of-sample.  
- The phrase “semantic difference” is ambiguous and potentially misleading—results may mainly reflect label distinctions; authors should precisely define what they mean by semantic difference.  
- Overall significance is low for NIPS unless the authors strengthen motivation for reweighting, clarify novelty, provide an out-of-sample strategy, and define “semantic difference.”
