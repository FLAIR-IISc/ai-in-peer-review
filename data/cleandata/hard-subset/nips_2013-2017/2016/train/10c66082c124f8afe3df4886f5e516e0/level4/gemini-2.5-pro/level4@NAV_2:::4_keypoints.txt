- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance vector w for task-specific document distance.
- Key technical contribution: an efficient optimization that approximates WMD gradients using the entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance.
- Empirical strength: thorough evaluation showing consistently strong/state-of-the-art kNN classification across 8 datasets versus 26 baselines.
- Paper is technically sound, the optimization idea is clever, and the empirical results convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.
- Presentation is clear, well-written, and situates the work appropriately within related literature.
- Significance: meaningful improvement over WMD with likely practical impact for text classification and retrieval.
- Originality concern: conceptual contribution viewed as incremental—combines existing WMD with standard metric-learning (NCA) and learns A and w.
- Motivation for the word re-weighting vector w is underexplained; authors should justify principledness and analyze learned weights (e.g., down-weighting stopwords).
- Clarify application to new/unseen documents at test time (confirm A and w are global parameters and how they are applied).
- Minor terminology point: “semantic difference” is ambiguous—use more specific language tied to experiment labels (topic/sentiment).
