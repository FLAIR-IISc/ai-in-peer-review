

The paper introduces a novel supervised metric learning approach for textual documents, extending the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance (WMD) framework. While the original WMD measures semantic distance between documents using word embeddings, this work adapts it to leverage class-level supervision (i.e., "documents sharing the same class"). The authors propose learning two components: a linear transformation matrix **A** (a standard approach) and word importance weights **w**. I appreciate the authors' careful responses to my queries regarding the role of **w** in Equation (5), though I remain unclear on the precise intuition behind this formulation and which component—**A** or **w**—primarily drives the reported performance gains.  

The evaluation focuses on classification tasks, which is appropriate for a metric learning method. However, assessing metric quality requires careful baseline comparison. It would be valuable to include results from a simple classifier (e.g., kNN with Euclidean distance) on the same datasets to contextualize the improvements over existing metrics. *[Note: This point is now addressed in the authors' response.]*  

Specific concerns:  
1. **Equation (2)**: I am unclear about its usage. The λ in Equation (2) appears distinct from the λ in Equation (170). Could the authors clarify the relationship between these parameters?  
2. **Claim about robustness** (line 316): The assertion that the method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words" requires stronger justification. What specific evidence supports this claim?  
3. **Line 257**: The phrase "on the right we also show the average error across datasets" does not align with Table 3 (or any table in the paper). Could this be referenced to the correct table?  
4. **Table 2**: What distinguishes the columns labeled "n" and "ne"? Additionally, the value of *k* for kNN classification is unspecified—is it dataset-dependent or a fixed hyperparameter?  

The promise of MATLAB code availability is welcome. Overall, the core idea is promising, but the technical justification for key design choices (especially Equation 5 and the role of **w**) needs strengthening. Clarifying the above points would significantly enhance the paper's impact.
