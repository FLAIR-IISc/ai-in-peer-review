This paper presents a supervised learning approach to document distance, building upon word mover's distance (WMD). WMD, inspired by earth mover's distance, calculates an optimal transport cost between word distributions to quantify document dissimilarity, initially employing word2vec embeddings for word representation. The authors propose learning both (1) a linear transformation of these word embeddings and (2) a reweighted word distribution to enhance separation between documents belonging to different classes. The paper is well-written and clearly explains the methodology. It includes a comprehensive review of related work, demonstrating a strong understanding of the existing literature, and presents experimental results on several datasets. However, the motivation behind the proposed formulation requires further clarification, and the overall contribution appears incremental. 

Specifically: 1) The rationale for reweighting the word distribution using weights *w* is unclear. This reweighting has the potential to significantly alter a document's representation, and justification is needed to demonstrate that it improves performance *without* fundamentally changing the document's meaning. 2) The contribution of this work is limited. Applying a linear transformation *A* and reweighting *w* within a Noise Contrastive Estimation (NCA) objective represents a plausible extension of WMD to a supervised setting, but the formulation itself is not particularly novel or impactful. 3) The paper does not address how the algorithm computes distances for new, unseen documents. Determining the appropriate reweighting *w* for a document not present in the training data remains an open question. 4) The use of "semantic difference" in the abstract is ambiguous. While it appears to refer to document labels, terms like authorship or topic difference do not readily align with a semantic interpretation; clarification is needed regarding the intended meaning. 

Overall, this is a well-presented paper, and I do not oppose acceptance pending satisfactory responses to these questions.
