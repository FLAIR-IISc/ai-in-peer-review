The paper proposes a new method for metric learning for textual documents, building upon previous work known as "Word Mover's Distance". The original is unsupervised, measuring the distance between the embeddings of words. This paper extends this to the supervised setting, where the supervision is of the type 'these documents belong to the same class'. The authors propose two things to learn the distance: one is a linear transformation A (the traditional approach), and the other is the word importance weights w.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the background and related work, and the proposed method is well-motivated and described in detail. The experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, with a large number of baselines and datasets used for comparison.

One minor suggestion I have is to provide more intuition behind Eq (5), which defines the usage of w. It would be helpful to understand which of the two learned components (A and w) is responsible for the reported improvements. Additionally, it would be interesting to see how well a simple classifier does on the same datasets, for comparison.

The authors claim that the proposed method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words". It would be helpful to provide more evidence or arguments to support this claim.

Some minor comments on the writing: 

* In line 257, the authors mention that the average error across datasets is shown on the right, but I couldn't find this in Table 3.
* In Table 2, the difference between "n" and "ne" is not clear. It would be helpful to provide a brief explanation.
* The value of k for kNN classification is not defined. It would be helpful to clarify whether this is dataset-dependent or method-dependent.

Overall, the paper is well-written and the proposed method is well-motivated and evaluated. With some minor revisions to address the above comments, the paper is ready for publication.
