This paper presents an extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) that leverages supervision to learn weights on the distance metric. The authors have conducted an extensive experimental evaluation, which is a notable strength of the paper. It is encouraging to see the WMD approach being built upon, and the incorporation of discriminative training to learn a domain-specific metric is a sound methodological choice. The paper is well-written, providing a clear explanation of the method with an appropriate level of detail. The experimental results are comprehensive, covering several classification datasets and a wide range of baseline methods and features. The proposed method demonstrates a significant performance gain over existing approaches, outperforming them by a substantial margin. Overall, this is a solid paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field, and I strongly believe it deserves to be presented at NIPS.
