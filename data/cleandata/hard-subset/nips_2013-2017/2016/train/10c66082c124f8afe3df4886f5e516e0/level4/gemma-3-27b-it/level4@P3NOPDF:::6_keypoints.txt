- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transformation (A) and word importance weights (w).
- Public MATLAB code release and thorough author responses are positive aspects.
- The intuition and intended effect of the word-weighting scheme (Equation 5) need clearer explanation.
- The paper should quantify the relative contributions of the learned transformation A versus the word weights w to performance gains.
- Experimental evaluation uses document classification appropriately, but needs simpler baselines (e.g., logistic regression/SVM with TF-IDF) to isolate metric benefits.
- The usage of λ is unclear—Equation (2) and line 170 appear inconsistent and need clarification.
- The claim that WMD is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words requires a concrete example or stronger argument.
- Minor issues: Table 3’s advertised “average error across datasets” is not found; Table 2’s symbols "n" and "ne" are undefined.
- The k value for k-NN classification is not specified or justified (dataset-specific or cross-validated), harming reproducibility.
- Overall: a promising supervised WMD extension with encouraging results, but needs clearer intuition, component ablation, and fixes to technical/minor issues.
