- Proposes supervised text classification by learning word-specific weights, extending Word Mover's Distance (Wasserstein) approach.  
- Key technical contribution: replaces Wasserstein distance with Sinkhorn distance as a computational proxy, greatly improving speed.  
- Empirical evaluation: outperforms 26 baselines across 8 datasets, achieving the best average performance.  
- Paper quality: exceptionally well-written with clear exposition and thorough, accessible mathematical analysis.  
- Weakness: novelty is limited relative to the original Word Mover's Distance paper.  
- Weakness: comparisons with Word Centroid Distance are irrelevant and confusing.  
- Weakness: discussion of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ is inadequate.  
- Practical concern: λ strongly affects behavior in Equation (2) and often dominates outcomes in practice.  
- Theoretical note: large λ approximates pure Wasserstein (but slows convergence); small λ accelerates convergence and yields qualitatively different, entropic Sinkhorn behavior.  
- Open question: unclear whether the method attains pure Wasserstein behavior or remains in the entropic Sinkhorn regime given the λ choices.
