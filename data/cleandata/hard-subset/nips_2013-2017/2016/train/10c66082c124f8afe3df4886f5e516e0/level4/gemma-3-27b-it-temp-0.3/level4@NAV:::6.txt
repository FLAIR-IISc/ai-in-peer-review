This paper introduces a novel supervised metric learning approach for textual documents, extending the established Word Mover's Distance (WMD) framework. WMD, originally an unsupervised technique measuring distance based on word embeddings, is adapted here to leverage supervision indicating document class membership. The authors state that code will be released (matlab). The core contribution lies in learning both a linear transformation, A, and word importance weights, w, to refine the distance calculation. While the approach is promising, the rationale behind the formulation in Equation (5) – governing the application of the word weights – could benefit from further elaboration. It would also be valuable to understand the relative contribution of the learned transformation (A) and the word weights (w) to the observed performance gains. The method is evaluated on a document classification task. Recognizing the challenges of distance metric evaluation, including a comparison to a standard classifier’s performance on the same datasets would provide useful context. 

Several points require clarification. The usage of Equation (2) is unclear, particularly regarding the definition of lambda, which appears inconsistent with its use in line 170. The claim in line 316, regarding the method’s tolerance for syntactic variation, requires further justification. Specifically, what mechanisms enable this forgiving behavior? Regarding presentation, line 257 references an “average error across datasets” that I was unable to locate – is this intended for inclusion in Table 3? Finally, Table 2 lacks clarification on the distinction between “n” and “ne,” and the definition of *k* in the kNN classification experiments is missing. Is *k* determined based on the dataset or is it a parameter of the method itself?
