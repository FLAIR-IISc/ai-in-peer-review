- Proposes learning WMD ground metric by parameterizing EMD cost with a Mahalanobis distance on embeddings and jointly learning histogram reweighting coefficients.  
- Derives gradients for an NCA-style objective and reports strong empirical gains across multiple text classification benchmarks.  
- Experimental validation is the paper’s main strength: significant improvements over WMD and related baselines, thorough ablations, and demonstrated scalability.  
- Method is conceptually elegant and practical, leveraging standard optimization and avoiding full EMD computational burden.  
- Mathematical inconsistency: the paper mixes Euclidean and squared-Euclidean cost definitions (Eqn.1 vs Eqn.6); should frame the formulation as a p-Wasserstein distance with cost d(u,v)=||u−v||_M^p.  
- Novelty concern: histogram reweighting is not new—must cite Lebanon (ICML 2007) and Le & Cuturi (ICML 2015) and clarify how this work extends them (e.g., joint learning of w and M).  
- Related-work clarity: more clearly distinguish from Cuturi’s entropy-regularized EMD (cost structure and continuous parameterization) and either omit or critically address Wang et al.’s flawed approach.  
- Minor: analyze empirical impact of the weight vector w (how much it improves over uniform weights).  
- Minor: include pseudocode for the full optimization (gradient updates for M and w) to improve reproducibility.  
- Minor: fix inconsistent notation (e.g., x_i vs z_i in Eqn.4) for rigor.  
- Recommendation: accept pending revisions to clarify math (p-Wasserstein), cite prior reweighting work, and better contrast with entropy-regularized EMD.
