- Paper tackles text classification by learning supervised, word-specific weights building on Word Mover’s Distance (WMD).
- Main contribution: using Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient proxy for the Wasserstein distance.
- Empirical results: strong performance on eight datasets, achieving state-of-the-art against 26 baselines.
- Presentation: clearly written and well-explained mathematically.
- Weakness: novelty is relatively incremental compared to the original WMD paper.
- Criticism: inclusion of comparisons to Word Centroid Distance is unhelpful and distracting.
- Methodological concern: discussion of the regularization parameter lambda is incomplete.
- Practical issue: lambda can dominate behavior (and equation (2)); small lambda for faster convergence yields behavior different from pure Wasserstein.
- Open question: unclear whether reported results reflect pure Wasserstein behavior or the entropic relaxation induced by the chosen lambda.
