- Proposes a supervised extension to Word Mover’s Distance using a linear embedding transform (A) and a reweighted word distribution (w).  
- Paper is clearly written with a comprehensive literature survey and experiments on multiple novel datasets.  
- Reweighting procedure (w) is insufficiently justified and may distort document semantics by amplifying non-representative words.  
- Need clarification on why reweighting preserves original meaning while improving label separation.  
- Contribution appears incremental: applying a linear transform and reweighting with an NCA objective is a straightforward supervised extension lacking novel theoretical insight.  
- Unclear how the model handles new/unseen documents, since w seems to be learned per-document during training.  
- Abstract’s phrase “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified to indicate label-based distinctions rather than broader semantic shifts.  
- Methodological choices and implementation details must be better justified and specified before publication.
