- Introduces a supervised metric learning method extending WMD by learning a linear projection and word-specific reweighting, with an efficient optimization procedure.
- Applicable to transport distances using squared Euclidean costs after a linear projection and tested with kNN classification.
- Strong empirical performance across multiple datasets and broad baselines; kNN results are consistently competitive.
- Well-motivated idea, clear presentation, and high-quality writing.
- Concern that much of the gain may come from centroid-based initialization rather than joint learning; request deeper analysis of this effect.
- Missing baseline: apply standard metric learning (ITML, LMNN, NCA) to word centroid embeddings to compare against the transport formulation.
- Request an ablation study isolating contributions of (i) projection A only, (ii) word weights only, and (iii) both together.
- Novelty appears limited mainly to the optimization strategy; need stronger conceptual/theoretical insights showing why joint learning improves over strong initializations.
- Originality lies in integrating linear projection and word reweighting within WMD, but practical significance is uncertain without the above ablations/baselines.
- Minor clarity edits requested: specify referenced prior work at Line 152, adjust notation in Equation (11), and explicitly link Sinkhorn scaling mention around Line 170.
- Suggest adding runtime/complexity comparisons between the proposed optimization and baselines to contextualize practicality.
- Overall: promising, well-written empirical paper that needs missing baselines and ablations to better justify novelty and impact.
