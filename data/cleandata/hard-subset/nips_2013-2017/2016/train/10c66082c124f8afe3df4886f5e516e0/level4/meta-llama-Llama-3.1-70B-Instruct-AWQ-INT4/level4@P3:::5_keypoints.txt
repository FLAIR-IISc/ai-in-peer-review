- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) to learn a task-specific supervised document distance.  
- Incorporates supervision into WMD and uses Sinkhorn distance as an efficient proxy for the Wasserstein distance.  
- Provides a clear and thorough mathematical formulation of S-WMD.  
- Evaluated on 8 real-world text classification tasks against 26 competitive baselines, demonstrating superior performance.  
- Paper is well-written and includes helpful visualizations (t-SNE) to illustrate the learned metric.  
- Novelty is limited relative to the original WMD paper; the incremental contribution of supervision is unclear.  
- Discussion of the regularization parameter lambda is limited and underanalyzed.  
- Unclear whether the chosen lambda (10) produces pure Wasserstein behavior or entropic (Sinkhorn) behavior; its impact on results is not studied.  
- Overall, the paper makes a significant contribution but would be strengthened by deeper analysis of lambda and clearer articulation of novelty.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear exposition, and an important direction for document distance metrics.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty and insufficient analysis of the regularizer's impact.
