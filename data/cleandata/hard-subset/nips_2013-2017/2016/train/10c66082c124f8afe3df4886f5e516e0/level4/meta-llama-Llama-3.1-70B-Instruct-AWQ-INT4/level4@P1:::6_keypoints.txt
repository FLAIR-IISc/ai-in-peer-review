- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance learning a linear transform A and word importance weights w.  
- Method is well-motivated, clearly described, and builds on relevant prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, using many baselines and datasets.  
- Need more intuition behind Eq. (5) that defines how w is used.  
- Unclear which learned component (A or w) is primarily responsible for performance gains.  
- Suggestion to compare against a simple classifier baseline on the same datasets.  
- Claim that the method is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words needs additional evidence or argument.  
- Minor writing issue: line 257 references an average error on the right that isn’t found in Table 3.  
- Minor clarification: Table 2’s distinction between “n” and “ne” is not explained.  
- Minor clarification: the k value for kNN classification is not specified (dataset- or method-dependent?).  
- Originality: extends unsupervised WMD to a supervised metric-learning setting—novel adaptation.  
- Significance: potential impact on document classification, clustering, topic modeling, and retrieval.  
- Quality and clarity: paper is well-written, easy to follow, and experiments are solid.  
- Overall: ready for publication pending minor revisions to address the above clarifications and evidence requests.
