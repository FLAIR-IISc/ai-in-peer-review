- Proposes supervised metric learning built on Word Mover’s Distance, learning a linear transformation of embeddings and word-specific importance weights.  
- Key innovation: efficiently optimizes nested WMD parameters by approximating with the Sinkhorn distance for computational tractability.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation on eight text classification tasks against 26 baselines, showing consistent improvements over unsupervised WMD and other methods.  
- Technically sound extension combining metric learning and document representation, addressing label-dependent semantic similarity.  
- Correctly identifies and addresses the computational infeasibility of naive supervised WMD via the Sinkhorn approximation.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, comparing to a wide range of unsupervised and supervised baselines.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized with good background, implementation details, training times, and visualizations of learned word weights.  
- Originality: novel supervised WMD formulation with joint embedding transform and word-level reweighting; optimization novelty is limited given prior Sinkhorn use.  
- Significance: strong practical value for text classification; S-WMD outperforms baselines on average and visualizations support learned class-relevant features.  
- Major concern 1: Initialization S-WCD (NCA on Word Centroid Distance) performs nearly as well as final S-WMD, questioning the incremental benefit of the full optimization.  
- Major concern 2: Lacks ablation isolating contributions of the linear transform A and word weights w (need results for only-A, only-w, and both).  
- Minor technical fixes: mark dual variables in Eq.11 as optimal (stars), explicitly link “Sinkhorn scaling” to earlier Sinkhorn distance mention, and clarify ambiguous “the authors” reference.  
- Overall: strong, well-motivated, and empirically supported paper; address the initialization and component-contribution analyses to strengthen claims.
