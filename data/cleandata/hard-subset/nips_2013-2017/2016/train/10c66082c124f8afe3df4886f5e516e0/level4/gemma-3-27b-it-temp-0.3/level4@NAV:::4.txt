This paper presents a supervised learning approach to refine document distance calculations built upon Word Mover’s Distance (WMD), a method inspired by Earth Mover’s Distance and utilizing optimal transport between word distributions, initially with word2vec embeddings. The authors propose learning both a linear transformation of word embeddings and a reweighted word distribution to enhance separation between documents of differing classes. The paper is well-written and clearly presented, demonstrating a strong grasp of relevant prior work through a comprehensive literature review. Extensive experimental results are provided across several datasets. However, the rationale behind the proposed formulation requires further elaboration, and the overall contribution feels incremental. 

Specifically: 1) The motivation for reweighting the word distribution with the parameter *w* is unclear. It’s not adequately explained why this reweighting improves distance calculations *without* fundamentally altering the document’s representation. Justification for this reweighting would be strengthened by demonstrating its benefits without causing substantial shifts in document meaning. 2) The work’s contribution, while valid – adapting WMD to a supervised setting via a linear transformation and reweighting within a Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA) objective – is not particularly novel or impactful. 3) The method for determining a reweighting *w* for entirely new, unseen documents is unspecified.  4) The use of “semantic difference” in the abstract appears to equate to document label, which is potentially limiting. Differences in authorship or topic are not typically considered semantic differences; clarification of this terminology is needed. 

Overall, this is a well-executed paper, and I do not oppose its acceptance pending satisfactory responses to these questions.
