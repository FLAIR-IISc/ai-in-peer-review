This paper proposes a novel and effective extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) by incorporating supervision to learn a task-specific distance metric. The core idea is to introduce learnable, word-specific weights into the WMD formulation, allowing the model to emphasize words that are most discriminative for a given classification task.

**Strengths:**

1.  **Methodology and Clarity:** The proposed method is methodologically sound, elegant, and well-motivated. It builds upon the strong foundation of the Word Mover's Distance and introduces supervision in a principled and intuitive way. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. The authors provide just the right amount of detail to explain the problem formulation and optimization procedure, making a potentially complex topic accessible and easy to follow.

2.  **Novelty and Significance:** I am very pleased to see this line of work pursued. While WMD is a powerful unsupervised metric, its utility can be limited in supervised settings where not all words are equally important. This paper successfully bridges that gap by applying discriminative training to learn a domain-specific metric. This is a significant contribution that effectively combines the semantic richness of pre-trained word embeddings with the specific requirements of a downstream task.

3.  **Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation is a key strength of this paper. It is both extensive and convincing. The authors have validated their approach on a diverse set of standard text classification benchmarks. The comparison to a wide array of strong baseline methods—ranging from classic feature-based models to more recent deep learning architectures—is comprehensive and rigorous. The fact that the proposed method outperforms all baselines, often by a substantial margin, is highly impressive and speaks to the effectiveness of the approach.

**Minor Suggestions for Improvement:**

While the paper is strong, a brief discussion on a few points could further enhance it:

*   **Scalability:** The computational cost of the original WMD can be a bottleneck. A short discussion on the scalability of the proposed supervised variant, including its training time relative to the baselines, would be a valuable addition.
*   **Interpretability:** The learned word-specific weights are a key component of the model. A qualitative analysis or visualization of the most highly weighted words for a few example classes could offer valuable insights into what the model is learning. This would not only strengthen the paper's conclusions but also enhance its interpretability.

**Conclusion:**

This is a solid, high-quality paper that makes a clear and significant contribution to the field. The method is novel, the technical execution is sound, and the experimental results are compelling. This work provides a strong case for learning task-specific metrics on top of powerful unsupervised distances. It is well-suited for the NIPS audience, and I would very much like to see it presented at the conference. I strongly recommend acceptance.
