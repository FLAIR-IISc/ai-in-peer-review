This is an expansion of the term 'mover distance,' which uses supervised learning to estimate weight at a distance. This paper features a very comprehensive experimental evaluation. I was pleased to see that someone has adopted the 'mover distance' approach and used differential training to learn domain-specific metrics. The method is solid, well-explained, and provides accurate information. The experiments are very comprehensive across several classification data sets and various types of baseline methods and features. The suggested method performs much better than all others. It is a solid paper that I would like to present at the NIPS.
