- Proposes a discriminative supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance to learn task-specific weights.  
- Addresses the limitation of original unsupervised WMD by incorporating supervision for downstream classification.  
- Core idea is sound, clearly articulated, and presented with technical rigor.  
- Novelty lies in principled integration of discriminative learning into the WMD framework to optimize classification.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across multiple benchmarks (20 Newsgroups, IMDB, AG News).  
- Compares against a wide range of baselines, from TF-IDF SVMs to CNNs, RNNs, and other metric learning methods.  
- Demonstrates robustness across diverse feature settings (various word embeddings and dimensions).  
- Shows consistent, statistically significant improvements (e.g., ~3–5% absolute accuracy gains).  
- Presentation is clear with well-designed figures/tables and informative ablation studies.  
- Learned weights offer interpretable correlations with task semantics.  
- Makes a strong theoretical and empirical contribution and establishes a new strong baseline.  
- Strong recommendation for acceptance due to technical soundness, thorough evaluation, and practical impact.
