This paper introduces a supervised extension of the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) for textual documents. Whereas the original WMD is an unsupervised metric that measures distances via optimal transport over pre-trained word embeddings, the present work incorporates label information of the form “these documents share a class.” The authors learn an affine transformation of the embedding space together with word-importance weights to tailor the ground cost to the task. Code (Matlab) is stated to be available. [I appreciated the authors’ careful replies, which addressed my earlier concerns.] The method learns two components: (i) a linear map A on the word embeddings (a standard metric-learning approach), and (ii) a weight vector w that reweights the document histograms. I would have liked more intuition for Eq. (5) (the role and normalization of w), and an analysis clarifying which component—A, w, or their combination—drives the performance gains. The empirical evaluation uses kNN classification, which is a reasonable way to assess a learned distance; for context, it would still be informative to report how a simple classifier performs on the same datasets. [These points are now moot given the authors’ clarifications.]
