- Proposes a supervised extension of WMD using a linear embedding transform A and a learnable reweight vector w for document distributions.  
- Paper is well-structured with a thorough literature review showing strong familiarity with prior work.  
- Experimental results on multiple novel datasets indicate the method can improve label separation.  
- Motivation for reweighting via w is inadequately explained; reweighting may distort document semantics and needs concrete justification or examples.  
- Contribution appears incremental and lacking novelty; similar supervised adaptations exist and the specific combination’s advantage is not convincingly argued.  
- Missing comparisons to stronger, non-trivial baselines (e.g., other supervised WMD variants or metric learning methods).  
- Generalization to unseen documents is unclear: it’s unspecified whether w is global or per-document and how w is obtained for new data, raising feasibility and scalability concerns.  
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous and likely conflates semantic meaning with label-driven distinctions; authors should clarify scope.  
- To improve impact, authors should justify w’s semantic preservation, compare to stronger baselines, clarify generalization/inference for w, and align terminology with experiments.  
- Overall assessment: plausible technical step but limited novelty and theoretical grounding; recommend major revision.
