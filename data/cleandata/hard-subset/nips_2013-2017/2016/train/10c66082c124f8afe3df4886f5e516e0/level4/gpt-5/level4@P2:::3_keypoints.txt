- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a Mahalanobis ground cost A and a word-importance vector w for kNN document classification using entropy-regularized OT and a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss.  
- Uses Sinkhorn relaxation, dual-variable gradient reuse, minibatching, and neighbor filtering for scalable and efficient training.  
- Introduces S-WCD initialization (supervised centroid-based NCA) to improve stability and reduce training time.  
- Strong empirical evaluation on eight datasets and 26 baselines, achieving best average rank and state-of-the-art on most tasks.  
- Learned parameters are interpretable: w highlights class-discriminative words and A is inspectable as a linear transform.  
- Clear and useful problem framing: bringing supervision to semantically robust WMD while maintaining practical scalability.  
- Solid engineering: practical implementation choices and helpful visualizations and timing results.  
- Clarity/math issues: inconsistent use of p (Euclidean vs squared Euclidean) and needs tighter notation, assumptions, and subgradient treatment.  
- Must better explain relationship and bias between unregularized WMD and entropy-regularized Sinkhorn OT, including dependence on λ.  
- Novelty positioning needs improvement: histogram reweighting is not new and should be contrasted with Lebanon’s metric learning and Aitchison-based approaches.  
- Should more explicitly contrast with prior work on learning ground costs for EMD (Cuturi & Avis) and supervised EMD in vision (Wang et al.), emphasizing continuous linear ground cost and entropy regularization.  
- Missing ablations: quantify contribution of learned w (vs. fixed), and report sensitivity to λ, neighbor truncation parameters, batch size, and choice of squared vs unsquared distance.  
- Clarify how much further S-WMD improves over the strong S-WCD initialization and when full training is most beneficial.  
- Presentation suggestions: provide a concise algorithm box with hyperparameters/defaults/stopping criteria and fix minor typos/notation inconsistencies.  
- Quality: technically sound with standard OT approximations validated empirically; theoretical guarantees are limited but acceptable.  
- Originality: components are known individually, but their integration for supervised WMD over embeddings is novel and practically impactful.  
- Significance: high—offers a general-purpose, supervised, scalable document metric with strong empirical gains.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical results, efficient training, interpretability, and relevance to OT/NLP/metric-learning communities.  
- Cons against acceptance: moderate conceptual novelty, some mathematical/expository rough edges, and missing key ablations (w and λ sensitivity).  
- Overall recommendation: solid, well-executed paper with acceptance recommended after improved positioning, clearer math/algorithm presentation, and a few ablations.
