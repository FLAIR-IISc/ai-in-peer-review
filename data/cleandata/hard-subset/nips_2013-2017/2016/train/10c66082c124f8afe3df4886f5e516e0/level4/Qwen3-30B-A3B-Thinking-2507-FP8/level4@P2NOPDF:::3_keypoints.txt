- Introduces learning WMD parameters by parameterizing the EMD ground cost via a Mahalanobis distance over word embeddings and learning histogram reweighting coefficients.  
- Derives gradients for NCA-style objectives and provides strong empirical validation; implementation is non-trivial but results are convincing.  
- Strength: Clear practical value, simple core formulation, effective integration of Mahalanobis metric learning and histogram reweighting, and thorough experimental support.  
- Need clearer mathematical rigor and notation: inconsistent ground metric (Euclidean vs squared Euclidean) — explicitly specify p in p-Wasserstein or fix the metric choice.  
- Tighten notation and derivations for the reweighting coefficients (Eq.5) and gradients; clarify optimization assumptions and steps.  
- Add an explicit algorithmic box detailing initialization, update rules for metric and weights, and convergence criteria for reproducibility.  
- Better distinguish contributions from prior work: emphasize entropy regularization and continuous ground metric parameterization versus Cuturi & Avis (2014).  
- Clarify that reweighting is not novel per se (Lebanon 2007), but the novelty is its integration with WMD metric learning.  
- Reassess citation/usage of Wang et al. (2015) — avoid relying on unpublished/unverified work or justify claims that their OT optimization “does not work as proposed.”  
- Provide analysis of the learned weight vector w: quantify its contribution relative to metric learning and assess interpretability/meaningfulness.  
- Minor fixes: correct spelling “Mahalanobis,” use WMD vs EMD consistently and define WMD early, rephrase awkward sentences for clarity.  
- Clarify the role and interplay of regularization (e.g., entropy) in optimization, and differences between regularized and unregularized formulations.  
- Conclusion: Strong, well-motivated paper with convincing results; recommend acceptance after addressing the above clarity and analysis issues.
