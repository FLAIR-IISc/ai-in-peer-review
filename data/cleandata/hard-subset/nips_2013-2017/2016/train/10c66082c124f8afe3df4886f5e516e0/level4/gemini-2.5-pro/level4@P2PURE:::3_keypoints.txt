- Proposes learning WMD parameters by parameterizing the ground cost with a Mahalanobis metric and learning histogram reweighting coefficients, with gradients derived for an NCA-style loss and strong empirical results.
- Combines existing techniques into a straightforward core idea whose implementation and empirical validation are non-trivial and convincing.
- Strong experimental section is a key strength; reviewer supports acceptance.
- Clarify the relationship and interplay between the regularized and unregularized formulations of the method.
- Improve mathematical rigor and precision of computations and notation throughout the paper.
- Add an algorithm box or pseudocode to clearly and compactly present the proposed procedure.
- Inconsistency in ground metric: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean, effectively switching between 1- and 2-Wasserstein formulations; consider framing WMD as p-Wasserstein.
- Acknowledge prior work on histogram reweighting (Guy Lebanon’s "Metric Learning for Text Documents" and Le & Cuturi’s Aitchison-transform work) since Eq.5 is not novel.
- Provide an ablation study to evaluate the importance and contribution of the learned weight vector w.
- Related work should more clearly differentiate this paper by highlighting entropy regularization for scalability and the use of a continuous parameterized cost matrix.
- When comparing to Wang et al., note that their method performs OT optimization only once (a methodological limitation) and explain how the current approach overcomes this.
