Here is a paraphrased version of the review:

The authors introduce a novel approach for learning the parameters of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) by leveraging word embeddings to compute bin-to-bin dissimilarities, effectively applying the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) to text documents. They parameterize the EMD cost matrix using a Mahalanobis distance between vector representations, and further propose learning histogram coefficients reweighting. The authors derive gradients for NCA-type costs and provide convincing experimental results. Overall, the paper presents a nice combination of existing techniques, yielding a simple yet non-trivial idea, with a strong experimental section. The paper's strengths lie in its simplicity and straightforward idea, although its implementation and testing are non-trivial. To improve, the authors should better address the interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations, refine their mathematical derivations, and consider providing an algorithmic box to clarify their proposal. Minor comments include:

- The authors should clarify the inconsistency in using the Euclidean distance in Eq.1 and the squared Euclidean metric in Eq.6, and instead consider framing WMD as p-Wasserstein between clouds of word embeddings.
- The "reweighting" approach in Eq.5 is not novel, as it was previously proposed by Guy Lebanon in the context of text document classification, and also explored in "Unsupervised Riemannian Metric Learning for Histograms Using Aitchison Transformations" by Le/Cuturi.
- It would be interesting to gain insight into the importance of the weight vector w in the learned metric and its impact on the results.
- The authors should more clearly highlight the differences between their work and previous studies, such as Cuturi/Avis, which this paper builds upon but with notable differences, including the use of entropy regularization and a continuous, parameterized cost.

Overall, I would support the acceptance of this paper.
