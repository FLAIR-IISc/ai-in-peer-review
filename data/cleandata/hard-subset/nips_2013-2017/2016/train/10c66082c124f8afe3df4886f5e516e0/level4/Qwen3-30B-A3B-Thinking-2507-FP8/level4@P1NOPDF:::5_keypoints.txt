- Problem and approach: Extend Word Mover’s Distance to supervised text classification by learning word-specific weights.  
- Main contribution: Replace computationally intensive Wasserstein distance with Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy and report best average performance across 8 datasets vs 26 baselines.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with a clear topic and thorough, accessible mathematical analysis.  
- Novelty concern: Contribution seems limited relative to the original WMD; supervised word-weight extension does not meaningfully diverge from prior directions.  
- Baseline comparison issue: Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is unclear/misleading because WCD has fundamentally different assumptions (centroid-based, non-optimal transport).  
- Major technical omission: Insufficient discussion of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ; its choice critically affects whether behavior approximates pure Wasserstein or stays in an entropic-regularized regime, so λ values and implications must be clarified.
