- Paper proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learn linear embedding transform A and word-importance vector w to supervise WMD using Sinkhorn relaxation for tractable training.  
- Training optimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) objective with minibatching and neighbor pruning; A initialized via S-WCD.  
- Empirical evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines shows consistent kNN improvements, t-SNE visualizations, interpretable w, and competitive training time; code released.  
- Builds on WMD, metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML), and Sinkhorn distances; novel in combining ground-metric learning with histogram reweighting for text.  
- Strength: clear motivation to inject supervision into WMD while staying in low-dimensional embedding space.  
- Strength: sound NCA-style objective and sensible gradient derivations using Sinkhorn approximation.  
- Strength: practical efficiency via entropic regularization, fixed-point Sinkhorn updates, minibatching, and neighbor pruning.  
- Strength: extensive empirical comparisons, visualizations, and interpretability of learned word weights.  
- Weakness: limited ablations — need separate A-only, w-only, and combined results plus effect of Sinkhorn λ on accuracy and runtime.  
- Weakness: insufficient intuition for normalized reweighting d̃ = (w ◦ d)/(w^T d); request explanation/ toy example on transport behavior and stability.  
- Weakness: evaluation tied to kNN; recommend adding discriminative baselines (e.g., linear SVM/logistic on TF-IDF or mSDA) for context.  
- Weakness: notation/clarity issues — unclear where Eq. (2) is used in training and whether λ is consistent; request consistent symbols and cross-references.  
- Weakness: fixed squared-Euclidean ground cost — ask whether p=1 or cosine costs were tried or could be learned, given cases where WMD outperforms S-WMD.  
- Weakness: hyperparameter reporting unclear — clarify how k for kNN is chosen and meaning of “n” vs “ne” in Table 2.  
- Weakness: initialization sensitivity — S-WMD-init already strong; report whether training degrades S-WCD and sensitivity to initialization.  
- Quality: method is technically sound though nonconvex; approximation and training choices are plausible; more ablations needed to isolate effects.  
- Clarity: generally well written but needs better intuition for Eq. (5), unified λ usage, k-selection definition, corrected table/figure references, and an algorithm box.  
- Originality: novel and natural extension of WMD combining low-dimensional ground metric learning with histogram reweighting for NLP.  
- Significance: consistent improvements across datasets suggest practical utility for retrieval and kNN; interpretable w is a broader impact.  
- Specific requests: unify and state λ and stopping criteria, provide ablations (A-only/w-only/both, λ, neighbor set size, batch size), and give evidence for paraphrase/robustness claims.  
- Specific requests: explain k selection consistency across methods and clarify “n” vs “ne” in Table 2 and the missing “average error” reference.  
- Minor: define all symbols on first use, report memory footprint for K and T in Sinkhorn for typical q, and discuss sparsity or regularization of w.  
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated, novel, efficient, reproducible, and empirically strong with interpretability.  
- Cons against acceptance: missing ablations, reliance on kNN evaluation without discriminative baselines, and notational/reporting ambiguities.
