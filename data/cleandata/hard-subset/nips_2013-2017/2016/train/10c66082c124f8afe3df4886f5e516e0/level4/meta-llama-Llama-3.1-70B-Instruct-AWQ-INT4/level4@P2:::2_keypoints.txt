- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension to Word Mover's Distance that learns an affine transform of embeddings and a word-importance weight vector.  
- Method is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Paper is well-organized with thorough background on WMD and metric learning.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive: eight real-world text classification tasks and 26 competitive baselines.  
- Results show S-WMD consistently outperforms baselines, demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Strengths: sound methodology, clear exposition, thorough experiments, and detailed analysis highlighting benefits of supervision.  
- Weakness: may be viewed as an incremental extension rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Authors mitigate the incremental concern by demonstrating significant performance improvements from supervision.  
- Some readers may find the experimental evaluation overly extensive with too many baselines/features.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper, convincing results, reviewer supports acceptance/presentation at NIPS.
