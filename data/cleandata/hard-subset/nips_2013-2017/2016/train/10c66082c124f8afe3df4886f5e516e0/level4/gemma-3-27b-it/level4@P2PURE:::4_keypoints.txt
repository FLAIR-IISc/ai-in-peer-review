- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and a reweighted word distribution w.  
- Paper is well-written, explains methodology clearly, reviews related work comprehensively, and reports experiments on several datasets.  
- Motivation for reweighting w is unclear; needs justification that reweighting improves class separation without fundamentally changing document meaning.  
- Contribution appears incremental: using A and w within an NCA-style objective is a plausible but not especially novel or impactful extension of WMD.  
- Paper does not explain how to compute distances for unseen documents—how to determine w for new documents is unanswered.  
- The phrase “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified (e.g., whether it refers to labels, authorship, topic, etc.).  
- Reviewer would not oppose acceptance provided satisfactory responses to the above concerns.
