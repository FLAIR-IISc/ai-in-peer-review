- Introduces supervised metric learning on top of WMD, learning a linear projection and per-word importance weights for squared-Euclidean transport costs.  
- Proposes an efficient training strategy using a tractable approximation to avoid nested optimization.  
- Comprehensive empirical study shows clear gains versus strong baselines on multiple kNN text classification benchmarks.  
- Gains may be modest: an EMD approach initialized by the word-centroid-distance (WCD) performs comparably to the fully trained model.  
- Table 3 should include results for word-centroid embeddings paired with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: no isolated assessment of the contribution of per-word weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Novelty primarily lies in the optimization strategy; learning a linear metric within the EMD framework is a relatively limited technical advance.  
- Manuscript is clearly written; minor clarifications requested (specify which prior work “the authors” refers to, whether α/β should be starred in Eq.11, and explicitly name the Sinkhorn scaling procedure).
