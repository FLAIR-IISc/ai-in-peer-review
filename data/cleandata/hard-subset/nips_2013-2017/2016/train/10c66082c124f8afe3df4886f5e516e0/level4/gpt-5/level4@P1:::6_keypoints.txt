- Paper proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learn linear embedding transform A and word-importance weights w using a stochastic leave-one-out kNN/NCA-style loss.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance for efficient approximate OT gradients and proposes WCD-based initialization plus batch training with NN filtering.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines, with visualizations and runtime reporting; code promised.  

Strengths:
- Timely and clear problem: adding supervision to WMD.  
- Solid technical approach: Sinkhorn relaxation for efficiency and NCA-style objective aligning with kNN.  
- Joint learning of geometry (A) and vocabulary salience (w) is sensible.  
- Thorough empirical comparisons, visualizations, and training-time measurements.  

Weaknesses / Questions:
- Need clearer intuition for histogram reweighting d̃ = (w ◦ d)/(w>d) and how w interacts with transport vs A.  
- Missing ablation to separate contributions of A-only vs w-only to performance gains.  
- Unclear usage and notation of Eq. (2) and conflicting λ symbols; clarify which λ controls Sinkhorn regularization and the value used.  
- Claim that S-WMD is “forgiving” for conceptually similar but syntactically different words lacks empirical/theoretical support or examples.  
- Reporting/clarity issues: missing “average error across datasets” reference; ambiguous “n” vs “ne” in Table 2; unclear how k in kNN is chosen/tuned.  
- Suggest comparing with simple classifiers (linear SVM/LogReg on TF-IDF or averaged embeddings) for context.  
- Sensitivity observed on some datasets (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC) suggests dependence on initialization and ground metric; robustness analysis (p=1 vs p=2, different initializations) needed.  
- Need discussion of approximation error from Sinkhorn relaxation vs. λ and impact on nearest-neighbor rankings.  

Quality:
- Method is technically sound with reasonable gradient derivations and credible complexity claims, but notational ambiguities and missing experimental details reduce clarity.  

Clarity:
- Generally well organized but needs clearer distinction of roles for A vs w and precise use of Eq. (2); figures are helpful.  

Originality:
- Meaningful extension: supervised metric learning combined with WMD, jointly learning A and w beyond prior supervised EMD work.  

Significance:
- Likely useful in practice for document similarity/classification when embeddings and limited labels are available.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Practical supervised extension to a popular distance, efficient Sinkhorn-based training, strong empirical results, good visualizations, and code availability.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Lack of ablation disentangling A vs w, notation/reporting issues (λ, k, table labels), sensitivity/robustness not fully explored, limited discussion on Sinkhorn approximation error.  

Questions to authors:
- Clarify when Eq. (2) is used in training and reconcile λ notation (state the λ value used).  
- Provide intuition and an ablation for impact of w vs A in Eq. (5).  
- Support the “forgiving” claim with analysis or examples.  
- Indicate where the “average error” appears, define “n” vs “ne” in Table 2, and explain k selection/tuning for kNN.
