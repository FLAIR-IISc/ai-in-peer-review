- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learns an affine transform of word embeddings and word-importance weights to minimize stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification error.  
- Employs Sinkhorn distance as a practical, differentiable approximation to WMD for efficient gradient-based training.  
- Builds on Kusner et al.'s WMD and is well-motivated to introduce supervision for task-specific document distances.  
- Strong empirical performance: outperforms 26 baselines across 8 text classification datasets with detailed analyses and t-SNE visualizations.  
- Technically rigorous: clear mathematical derivations, correct gradient computations, and statistically validated experiments.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and presentation, with consistent notation and helpful supplementary material.  
- Original contribution: jointly learning a linear embedding transformation and word importance within the optimal-transport framework is novel.  
- Significant practical impact: consistent gains and training efficiency comparable to baseline metric-learning approaches.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental because the method directly extends the existing WMD framework.  
- Insufficient discussion of the Sinkhorn regularization λ (choice of λ=10), and lack of clarity whether behavior is closer to true Wasserstein or entropic Sinkhorn.  
- Confusing treatment of Word Centroid Distance (WCD); presentation risks implying S-WMD is built on WCD, which is misleading.  
- Lacks discussion of failure modes or settings where S-WMD may be inappropriate (e.g., extremely short documents).  
- Arguments for acceptance: supervised extension of influential WMD, consistent empirical improvements, practical Sinkhorn use, excellent presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, inadequate λ analysis, and confusing relation to WCD.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify λ choice, the relation to WCD, and limitations.
