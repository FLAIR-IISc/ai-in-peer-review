- Proposes supervised WMD (S-WMD) learning EMD/WMD parameters via Mahalanobis-parameterized cost and learned histogram reweighting.  
- Provides gradient derivations inspired by Neighborhood Components Analysis for end-to-end learning.  
- Empirical results are strong and convincing across diverse datasets, out-performing many baselines.  
- Strength: simple, straightforward idea with non-trivial implementation and robust experimental validation.  
- Area for improvement: clarify interplay between regularized and non-regularized OT formulations.  
- Area for improvement: increase mathematical rigor; some computations and notations are imprecise.  
- Area for improvement: include an algorithmic box to summarize the method and workflow.  
- Minor: Eq.1 vs Eq.6 inconsistency—recommend framing WMD as a p-Wasserstein distance to avoid ambiguity.  
- Minor: Reweighting in Eq.5 is not entirely novel; prior work (Guy Lebanon; Le & Cuturi) should be cited.  
- Minor: Need experimental analysis showing the impact and importance of the learned weight vector w.  
- Minor: Related work should better distinguish this work (entropy regularization, continuous/parameterized cost, linear maps) from prior discrete-cost formulations.  
- Minor: Clarify status and limitations of related unpublished work (Wang et al.) that had optimization issues.  
- For acceptance: tackles an important problem of supervised document distance learning within the WMD framework.  
- For acceptance: technical contributions (efficient gradients, metric learning with embeddings) are valuable.  
- For acceptance: empirical evaluation is thorough and supports claims.  
- Against acceptance: mathematical notation/rigor needs improvement.  
- Against acceptance: prior literature on histogram reweighting not fully acknowledged.  
- Against acceptance: insufficient investigation of the practical impact of learned word-importance weights.
