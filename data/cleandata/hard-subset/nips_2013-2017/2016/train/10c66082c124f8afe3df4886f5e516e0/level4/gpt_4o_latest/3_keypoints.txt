- Paper introduces a novel method to learn WMD/EMD ground metric by parameterizing the cost matrix with a Mahalanobis distance over word embeddings.  
- Authors also learn histogram coefficient reweighting jointly with the ground metric, inspired by Lebanon’s prior work.  
- Gradients for NCA-type objectives are derived to enable end-to-end learning of the metric and weights.  
- Entropy-regularized OT is used for scalability and computational efficiency in experiments.  
- Empirical results are compelling and strongly support the proposed approach.  
- Reviewer leans toward recommending acceptance based on simplicity, clear execution, and strong experiments.  
- Suggestion to better handle and discuss interplay between regularized and non-regularized OT formulations.  
- Request for increased mathematical rigor: some derivations and notations are sloppy and need clarification.  
- Recommend adding an algorithmic box/pseudocode to clarify the proposed method and implementation details.  
- Minor: Eq.1 uses Euclidean cost while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean — propose describing WMD as p-Wasserstein to avoid inconsistency.  
- Minor: The reweighting in Eq.5 is not novel — prior work by Guy Lebanon and others (Le & Cuturi) introduced similar ideas.  
- Ask for experimental analysis on the practical significance and impact of the learned weight vector w.  
- Related work should be clarified: emphasize differences from Cuturi/Avis (entropy regularization, continuous/parameterized cost) and contrast with Wang et al.’s approach.
