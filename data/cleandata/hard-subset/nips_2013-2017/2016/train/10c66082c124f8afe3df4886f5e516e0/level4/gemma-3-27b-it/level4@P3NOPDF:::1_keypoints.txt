- Proposes supervised metric learning that extends Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) via a linear projection and word-specific frequency weights.  
- Method generalizes to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances after projection.  
- Learns both the projection matrix and per-word weights, with an efficient optimization procedure.  
- Empirical evaluation shows strong performance on kNN-based document classification against competitive baselines.  
- Paper is very well written and technically sound.  
- Impact appears limited because full learning’s performance is very close to EMD initialized with a “word centroid distance.”  
- Missing baseline: evaluation of simple word-centroid document embeddings combined with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: need results isolating the contributions of the word-specific weights vs. the linear projection A.  
- Originality mainly lies in the optimization method; the core idea of linear metric learning within EMD is modest.  
- Minor clarifications requested: specify ambiguous “the authors” reference (Line 152).  
- Notation fix: Equation 11 should superscript α and β to denote optimal values.  
- Clarify that the scaling around Line 170 refers to Sinkhorn scaling for continuity.  
- Overall: solid, practical improvement with modest gains; addressing additional baselines and ablations would strengthen the paper.
