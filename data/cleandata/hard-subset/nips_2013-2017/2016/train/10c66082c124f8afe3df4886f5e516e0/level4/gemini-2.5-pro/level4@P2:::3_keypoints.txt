- Introduces S-WMD: supervised learning of a Mahalanobis transform A and per-word weights w to make WMD task-specific.

- Formulates training as minimizing stochastic k-NN leave-one-out classification loss using differentiable NCA loss.

- Key technical contribution: efficient approximation of gradients by replacing WMD with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance for differentiability and speed.

- Strong empirical results: extensive evaluation on eight datasets, achieving state-of-the-art on 5/8 benchmarks versus 26 baselines.

- Novel and elegant synthesis of WMD, metric learning, and Sinkhorn optimal transport; practical and scalable implementation.

- Paper lacks mathematical precision/clarity (e.g., p-norm vs squared Euclidean and 2-Wasserstein framing) and should state assumptions explicitly.

- Needs better positioning with prior work on histogram re-weighting (Lebanon), Riemannian metric learning for histograms (Le & Cuturi), and learned EMD ground distances (Cuturi & Avis); clarify differences with Wang et al.

- Missing ablation study to disentangle contributions of the linear transform A and the word-weight vector w.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective method with convincing experiments and practical gradient solution for OT-based distances.

- Arguments against acceptance: minor clarity/related-work issues and absence of component-wise analysis.

- Overall recommendation: accept.
