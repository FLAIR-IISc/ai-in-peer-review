- Proposes supervised learning of word-specific weights for text classification using the Sinkhorn distance as an efficient approximation to Word Mover’s (Wasserstein) distance.
- Shows computational efficiency and achieves state-of-the-art average performance across eight datasets versus 26 baselines.
- Paper is clearly written with a thorough and accessible mathematical exposition.
- Novelty is somewhat incremental relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance work.
- Comparison to Word Centroid Distance is unhelpful and distracting.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive (multiple datasets and many baselines).
- Treatment of the regularization parameter lambda is incomplete; lambda can strongly influence results and may dominate equation (2).
- Reviewer asks for clarification on whether the chosen lambda leads to Wasserstein-like behavior or typical entropic Sinkhorn behavior.
