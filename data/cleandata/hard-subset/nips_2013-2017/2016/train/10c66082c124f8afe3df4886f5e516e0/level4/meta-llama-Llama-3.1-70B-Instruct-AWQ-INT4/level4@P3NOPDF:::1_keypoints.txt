- Proposes supervised metric learning built on word-movers distance, applicable to transport distances after a linear projection and including learned word-frequency re-weighting and renormalization.
- Introduces an efficient optimization method to learn the parameterized metric.
- Provides a thorough experimental evaluation comparing to many baselines on document classification with kNN, showing effectiveness.
- Strength: natural and useful extension of the recent word-movers distance, effective on several kNN text-classification tasks.
- Limitation: earth-movers distance with a "word centroid distance" initialization performs similarly to the full learned metric, reducing the added impact of the proposed method (see last two rows of Table 3).
- Suggestion: evaluate the word-centroid embedding (weighted mean of word embeddings) with all tested metric-learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- Suggestion: ablate the two components (word-specific weights vs. projection matrix A) to assess their relative importance and performance in isolation.
- Originality: main novelty lies in the optimization (lines 156–175); linear metric learning within earth-movers distance is interesting but a limited standalone contribution.
- Writing: paper is well-written and clear, overall a pleasure to read.
- Minor issues to clarify: specify the cited paper on line 152 ([1,7,8]?); in Eq.11 alpha and beta should carry stars; explicitly note around line 170 that this is the Sinkhorn scaling referenced earlier.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-presented contribution with limited potential impact due to similar performance from existing initialization techniques.
