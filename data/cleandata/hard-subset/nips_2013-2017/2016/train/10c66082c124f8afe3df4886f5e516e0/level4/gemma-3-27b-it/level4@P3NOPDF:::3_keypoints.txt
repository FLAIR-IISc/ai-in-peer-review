- Proposes learning WMD’s ground metric via a Mahalanobis distance on linear transforms of word embeddings and a learned histogram reweighting; shows strong experimental results and recommends acceptance.

- Strength: simple, clear idea; significant contribution in parameterization and gradient derivation, with rigorous implementation and evaluation demonstrating practical benefit.

- Weakness: interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations is unclear and needs a more unified presentation.

- Weakness: mathematical notation and derivations are sometimes sloppy or inconsistent; require more rigorous, consistent treatment.

- Weakness: paper should include an algorithmic box outlining key implementation and optimization steps for clarity.

- Specific: potential inconsistency between using Euclidean cost in Eq.1 and squared Euclidean in Eq.6; suggest framing WMD as a p-Wasserstein distance to clarify.

- Specific: the reweighting scheme is not novel and prior work (e.g., Lebanon; Le & Cuturi) should be acknowledged.

- Specific: provide deeper experimental analysis of the learned weight vector w—its impact, consistency, and scenarios where it helps.

- Specific: related work needs clearer delineation of differences, especially highlighting entropy regularization for scalability and the move from discrete to continuous parameterized costs.

- Specific: discuss Wang et al. more fairly, explaining their limitations (instability from single OT run) and how this work overcomes them.

- Overall conclusion: well-written, impactful, and effective paper with convincing results; minor revisions would further strengthen the contribution.
