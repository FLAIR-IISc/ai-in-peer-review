- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that injects supervision into WMD to learn task-specific document distances.  
- Learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and per-word importance weights to improve distance quality.  
- Optimizes a stochastic leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification loss to directly target classification performance.  
- Uses a Sinkhorn-relaxed optimal transport formulation to approximate WMD gradients and enable efficient supervised training.  
- Technical formulation, loss, initialization, and gradient computations are clearly explained and well-justified.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical gains, outperforming 26 baselines across eight real-world text classification datasets.  
- Provides thorough experimental analysis including ablations, t-SNE visualizations, and runtime/complexity discussion.  
- Positions work effectively relative to prior metric-learning and WMD literature, highlighting novelty and significance.  
- Ablation study shows both learned weights and transformations contribute to improved performance.  
- Visualization evidence supports that S-WMD learns more semantically meaningful distances.  
- Main weakness: performance depends on the quality of input word embeddings (authors used word2vec; other embeddings not explored).  
- Additional concern: scalability to very large datasets needs further investigation despite provided complexity analysis.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, significant contribution with strong empirical support and a recommendation for acceptance.
