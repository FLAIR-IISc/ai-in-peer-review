This paper introduces a supervised approach to learning document distance, building upon the recently proposed Word Mover’s Distance (WMD). WMD leverages optimal transport to calculate the distance between documents based on the embeddings of their constituent words, initially using word2vec. The authors propose learning both a linear transformation of the word embeddings and a re-weighting of the word distributions to improve separation between documents of different classes. 

The paper is well-written and clearly presented, making it easy to follow the methodology. It includes a comprehensive review of relevant prior work, demonstrating a strong understanding of the field, including connections to Earth Mover’s Distance and metric learning. The authors evaluate their approach on eight datasets, presenting a substantial experimental analysis.

However, several aspects could benefit from further clarification. First, the motivation for re-weighting the word distribution (using vector *w*) requires more explanation. While the approach aims to emphasize important words, the potential for significantly altering the document's representation raises concerns. Justifying this re-weighting by demonstrating its impact on semantic similarity *without* fundamentally changing the document’s meaning would strengthen the paper. Second, while the proposed method represents a natural extension of WMD to a supervised setting – employing a linear transformation and NCA objective – the contribution feels incremental rather than groundbreaking. A more compelling argument for the novelty and significance of this specific formulation would be beneficial.

Further practical considerations should be addressed. The paper does not fully detail how the re-weighting vector *w* is determined for entirely new documents not present in the training set. Clarifying this process is crucial for demonstrating real-world applicability. Finally, the use of "semantic difference" in the abstract is vague.  The authors should clarify whether this refers solely to label-based differences or encompasses other types of distinctions, such as authorship or topic. 

Overall, this is a solid paper with a clear presentation and thorough empirical evaluation. Addressing the concerns regarding motivation, significance, and practical implementation would substantially enhance its contribution. I do not object to its acceptance, contingent upon addressing these points to a satisfactory degree.
