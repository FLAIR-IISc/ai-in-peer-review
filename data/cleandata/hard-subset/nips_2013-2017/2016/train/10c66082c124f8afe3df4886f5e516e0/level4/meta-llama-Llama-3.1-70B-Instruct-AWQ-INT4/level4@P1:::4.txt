This paper proposes a supervised method to learn the distance between documents based on the Word Mover's Distance (WMD). The WMD, introduced in previous literature, calculates the optimal transport between two distributions of words to obtain the document distance. Initially, word2vec embedding is used for the transport distance between words. The authors suggest learning a linear transformation of embedded words and a reweighted word distribution to separate documents with different labels. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a thorough review of related works, demonstrating the authors' understanding of the literature. Experimental results are provided for several novel datasets.

However, the motivation behind the formulation requires further explanation, and the significance of the work is limited. Specifically, the use of reweighting the word distribution using w needs more justification. It is unclear why this reweighting is beneficial, and it appears that it could alter the document's meaning entirely. If the authors can demonstrate why this reweighting is helpful without changing the document's essence, it would be more convincing.

Furthermore, the contribution of this work is not substantial. Adopting a linear transformation A and reweighting w with the NCA objective function is a possible extension for the supervised setting of WMD, but this formulation is not groundbreaking. Additionally, it is unclear how this algorithm can find a distance for a new document, particularly when it comes to determining a suitable reweighting w.

The term "semantic difference" in the abstract seems to refer to the label of the document. However, it is unclear if this term encompasses other aspects, such as authorship or topic differences. The authors should clarify the meaning of "semantic difference" in this context.

In general, the paper is well-written, and I do not object to its acceptance, provided that the authors address these concerns and provide adequate explanations.
