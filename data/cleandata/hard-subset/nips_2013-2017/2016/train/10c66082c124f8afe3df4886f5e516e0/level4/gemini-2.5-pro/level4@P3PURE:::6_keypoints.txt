- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance for documents, learning a linear embedding transform A and per-word importance weights w; Matlab code to be released.
- Core method jointly learns A and w, but the paper does not clearly explain the intuition behind Equation (5) that defines how w are used.
- It is unclear which learned component (A or w) drives the performance gains — an ablation study isolating A vs w is needed.
- Evaluation uses classification (k-NN) and is generally sound, but several methodological clarifications are required.
- Clarify when Equation (2) is applied and resolve the apparent mismatch between the λ in Eq.(2) and the λ referenced at line 170.
- Provide stronger justification or empirical evidence for the claim that the method is “particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words” (line 316).
- Line 257 refers to “average error across datasets” that is not found in the tables—clarify whether this belongs in Table 3 or elsewhere.
- Explain the difference between the "n" and "ne" variants reported in Table 2.
- State how the k value for the k-NN classifier was chosen and whether k selection depends on dataset or method.
