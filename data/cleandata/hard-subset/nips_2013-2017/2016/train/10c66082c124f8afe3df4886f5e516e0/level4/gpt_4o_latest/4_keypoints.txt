- Introduces a supervised method to learn document distances based on WMD by learning a linear embedding transform A and a reweighted word distribution w.
- Uses WMD/earth mover’s distance foundation with word2vec embeddings and extends it into a supervised setting.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Provides a comprehensive related-work review showing strong literature knowledge.
- Presents experimental results on several novel datasets.
- Rationale for reweighting words via w is not well-justified and may distort the original document representation.
- Contribution appears incremental—applying A and w in an NCA objective is a straightforward extension lacking strong novelty.
- Unclear how the method generalizes to unseen documents (how to determine w for new documents).
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous (unclear if it means labels, authorship, topic, or something else).
- Reviewer would not oppose acceptance if the authors adequately clarify the above concerns.
