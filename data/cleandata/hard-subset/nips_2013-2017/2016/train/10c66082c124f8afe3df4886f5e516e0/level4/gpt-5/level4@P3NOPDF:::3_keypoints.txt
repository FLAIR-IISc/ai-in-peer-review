- Proposes supervised learning of WMD ground metric via a Mahalanobis parameterization plus optional histogram reweighting, optimized with an NCA-style loss and entropic OT (Sinkhorn); shows strong empirical gains.

- Overall assessment: clear, compelling, practical extension combining known components; reviewer recommends acceptance.

- Strength: Clear motivation that task-adapted ground metrics significantly benefit WMD.

- Strength: Entropy-regularized OT enables efficient training and backpropagation.

- Strength: Strong, comprehensive experimental results.

- Strength: Mahalanobis parameterization provides interpretability and a principled inductive bias.

- Improve mathematical rigor and notation: define variables, use consistent symbols, and clearly separate unregularized EMD vs entropic OT derivations.

- Clarify regularized vs unregularized formulations: specify training/evaluation regularization, sensitivity to regularization strength, and effects on learned metric.

- Add algorithmic clarity: provide pseudocode/algorithm box for the full training loop (cost construction, Sinkhorn, NCA loss, gradients, updates).

- Ensure distance consistency: frame as a p-Wasserstein distance and explicitly state which p is used (derivations appear p = 2).

- Acknowledge prior work on histogram reweighting/metric learning (e.g., Lebanon; Le & Cuturi) and delineate the paper’s specific novelty.

- Provide ablations on the weight vector w: quantify its contribution relative to M, dataset consistency, and interpretability (discriminative terms/dimensions).

- Better position related work: clarify distinctions from past ground-metric learning and WMD-learning attempts and emphasize advantages of repeated end-to-end entropic OT optimization.

- Practical details: ensure M is PSD (e.g., parameterize M = L L^T) and discuss regularization choices on M and w and their effects on stability/generalization.

- Discuss gradients and stability: choice of entropic strength, number of Sinkhorn iterations, stabilization tricks (log-domain, epsilon scheduling), and include complexity analysis.

- Evaluation protocol: add ablations (fixed vs learned M; with/without w; entropic vs unregularized evaluation) and report runtime/memory overhead vs vanilla WMD.

- Interpretation: visualize or summarize learned metric (e.g., spectrum of M, top-weighted w features) to give insight into learned structure.

- Notation consistency: unify p-Wasserstein usage, avoid mixing Euclidean and squared-Euclidean without explicit statements, standardize symbols across equations.

- Minor fixes: tighten exposition around Eqs. 1, 5, 6 to avoid p/cost inconsistencies and update related work to acknowledge prior histogram reweighting/entropy-OT literature.

- Conclusion: A well-motivated, effective contribution to supervised WMD learning; acceptance recommended pending improved clarity, ablations (especially on w), and clearer related-work positioning.
