- Paper proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform A and word-importance weights w using an NCA-style leave-one-out kNN loss with Sinkhorn-regularized OT for tractable gradients.  
- Novelty: combines NCA-style supervision with Sinkhorn OT and joint learning of A and w in word-embedding space.  

Strengths
- Clear, principled supervised metric learning formulation with efficient Sinkhorn-based optimization.  
- Joint learning of ground metric A and word-importance w gives task-specific flexibility beyond prior EMD metric learning.  
- Strong empirical evaluation: 8 datasets, 26 baselines; best average rank and top on 5/8 datasets.  
- Sensible engineering (S-WCD init, batch training, neighbor pruning) and competitive runtime.  
- Well written, useful visualizations, and public code.

Weaknesses / Questions
- Incremental gains: S-WMD often yields modest improvements over the S-WCD centroid initialization, sometimes underperforming WMD on some datasets.  
- Missing ablations: no isolated results for “A only” vs. “w only” vs. both, nor comparisons using exact OT for small q.  
- Baselines: need centroid-based baselines with LMNN/ITML (not just NCA) to test whether transport adds value beyond centroid metric learning.  
- Sensitivity/stability: dependence on λ, neighbor size M, batch size, and k in kNN lacks analysis; convergence and seed variability not discussed.  
- Theory: limited discussion of approximation bias from Sinkhorn (T*λ), convergence conditions, and trade-offs in λ.

Assessment (review criteria)
- Quality: technically sound and well executed; main gap is limited analysis of when supervised OT refinement helps over S-WCD.  
- Clarity: well written with minor notational issues.  
- Originality: moderate — components are known but their combination is practical and coherent.  
- Significance: empirically significant but tempered by modest gains over S-WCD in some cases.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add ablations: A-only, w-only, vary λ, neighbor pruning M, batch size, and k sensitivity.  
- Strengthen centroid baselines: report centroid + LMNN/ITML (after PCA) to isolate transport benefit.  
- Report per-dataset deltas from S-WCD → S-WMD (absolute and relative improvements).  
- Test robustness to alternative embeddings (GloVe, fastText).  
- Analyze performance by document length and consider alternative ground costs (L1, unsquared L2).  
- Discuss theoretical bias from Sinkhorn approximation and when gradients approximate true WMD gradients.

Minor comments / clarifications
- Clarify ambiguous citations (“The authors”) and consistent dual-variable notation (α*/β* vs α*λ/β*λ).  
- Disambiguate symbol p and other notation (use d, n, q).  
- Remind reader of Sinkhorn scaling updates and state whether squared Euclidean cost is used consistently.  
- Report chosen k in kNN, tuning procedure, and include confidence intervals/significance tests.  
- Consider releasing learned A and w for reuse.

Pros for acceptance
- Practical, efficient method that improves over strong unsupervised distances and many supervised baselines with code available.  

Cons for acceptance
- Small improvements over S-WCD in places, missing key ablations and sensitivity/theoretical analysis.  

Overall recommendation
- Lean accept, contingent on adding requested ablations and clarifications.
