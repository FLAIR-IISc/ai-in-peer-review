- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that jointly learns a linear embedding transformation and word-importance weights for a task-specific document distance.
- Uses a differentiable Sinkhorn-regularized approximation to WMD to enable efficient end-to-end training.
- Initializes optimization with a supervised word centroid distance (S-WCD) to mitigate non-convexity.
- Promises to release code for reproducibility.
- Novel and well-motivated extension of WMD to incorporate supervision, addressing an important problem.
- Clever technical contribution: regularized Sinkhorn makes differentiating through WMD tractable.
- Thorough empirical evaluation: compared to 26 baselines across eight datasets, achieving state-of-the-art kNN performance on most tasks.
- Lacks an ablation study isolating the effects of the linear transform A versus the word-importance vector w.
- Needs more intuition and explanation for the re-weighting scheme in Eq. (4) and its expected effect.
- Would benefit from contextualizing metric results against standard supervised classifiers (e.g., SVM with TF-IDF).
- Clarify whether the Sinkhorn regularization λ is the same as the dual derivation λ and justify the chosen value (λ=10).
- Provide a supervised-context qualitative example to support the claim about handling conceptually similar but syntactically different words.
- Resolve inconsistency between text claiming "average error across datasets" and Table 2 reporting "average rank"; define Table 2 headers "n" and "ne."
- Describe the procedure for selecting k in kNN (whether tuned per method/dataset or fixed).
