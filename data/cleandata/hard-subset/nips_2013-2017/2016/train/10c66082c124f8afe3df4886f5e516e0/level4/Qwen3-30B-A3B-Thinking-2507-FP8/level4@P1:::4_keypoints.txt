- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word importance weight vector to improve document classification.  
- Optimizes a stochastic approximation of leave-one-out classification error under WMD using a Sinkhorn distance approximation to avoid cubic complexity.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over 26 baselines across eight text classification tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates work within document representation, metric learning, and optimal transport.  
- Strong connections drawn to Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) and recent efficient Wasserstein computation methods.  
- Primary strengths: elegant supervised integration with WMD, practical computational gains via Sinkhorn, and thorough experimental validation.  
- Weakness: Motivation and justification for the word importance weight vector (w) are insufficient; reweighting alters document representation and its semantic preservation is unexplained.  
- Weakness: Contribution appears incremental—largely a straightforward application of existing metric learning (NCA) to WMD with limited theoretical novelty.  
- Weakness: Fails to explain how to handle new/unseen documents at inference, since learned weights w depend on training distribution.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous use of “semantic difference” in the abstract—unclear if it refers to label-based differences or real semantic meaning.  
- Recommendation: Reject for NIPS due to incremental contribution and unresolved questions about reweighting motivation and practical applicability.  
- Revision suggestions: strengthen motivation for reweighting, clarify inference for new documents, and better articulate the theoretical significance of the approach.
