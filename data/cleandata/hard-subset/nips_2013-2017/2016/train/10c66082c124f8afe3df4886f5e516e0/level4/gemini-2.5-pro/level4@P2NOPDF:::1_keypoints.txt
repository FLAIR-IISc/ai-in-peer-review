- Proposes S-WMD: learn a linear projection of word2vec embeddings and word-specific weights with an efficient optimization algorithm, evaluated on k-NN document classification.
- Strength: Extending unsupervised WMD into a supervised metric learning framework is technically interesting and practically useful.
- Strength: Empirical results show S-WMD outperforms WMD and many baselines on several text classification benchmarks.
- Strength: Paper is exceptionally well-written and clear.
- Weakness: Fully-learned S-WMD shows only marginal gains over the WCD-initialized model, implying most improvement may come from initialization rather than the full transport-based optimization.
- Missing experiment: Ablation study needed to separate the effects of the projection matrix A and the word-specific weights.
- Missing experiment: Compare against stronger centroid-based baselines by applying standard metric learning methods (e.g., ITML, LMNN, NCA) to word-centroid embeddings.
- Originality: The main novel contribution is the optimization procedure, but limited empirical gains reduce the perceived practical impact.
- Minor comment: Clarify which paper is meant by "The authors" (line 152).
- Minor comment: Equation 11 should denote alpha and beta as optimal (α* and β*) for consistency.
- Minor comment: Explicitly connect the iterative update procedure (~line 170) to the Sinkhorn scaling algorithm mentioned in the introduction (line 56).
