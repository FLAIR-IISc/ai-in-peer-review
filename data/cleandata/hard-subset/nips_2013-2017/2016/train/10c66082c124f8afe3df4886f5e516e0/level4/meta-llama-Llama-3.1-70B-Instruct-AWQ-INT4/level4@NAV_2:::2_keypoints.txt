- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension to Word Mover's Distance that learns weights on the distance.  
- Method transforms word embeddings and introduces a histogram importance vector to re-weight word histograms.  
- Uses stochastic neighborhood relaxation to approximate leave-one-out classification error and minimizes a KL-divergence loss.  
- Provides a fast gradient computation algorithm, uses batch gradient descent, and initializes matrix A with Supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) for efficiency.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive across multiple classification datasets, baselines, and features.  
- Results show S-WMD outperforms all baselines by a large margin.  
- Leverages large unsupervised word2vec corpus to match conceptually similar but syntactically different words.  
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation, clear explanations, and well-organized figures and tables.  
- Presents a comprehensive related-work discussion, distinguishing the method from EMD/Wasserstein and other metric-learning approaches.  
- Technically sound with thorough experimental validation.  
- Significant contribution with potential applications in text classification, sentiment analysis, and information retrieval.  
- Could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the method's limitations.  
- Lacks deeper insights into the learned weights and their relationship to document classes.  
- Needs more detailed comparisons with other metric-learning approaches.
