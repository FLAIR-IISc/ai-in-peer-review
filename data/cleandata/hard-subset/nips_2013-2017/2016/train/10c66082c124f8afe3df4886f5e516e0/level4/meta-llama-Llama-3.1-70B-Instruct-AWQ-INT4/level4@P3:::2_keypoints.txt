- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), extending Word Mover's Distance by learning supervised weights on the distance.  
- Clear formulation of the optimization problem and a described algorithm to solve it.  
- Extensive experimental evaluation on eight real-world text classification tasks.  
- Comparison against 26 competitive baselines (unsupervised and supervised).  
- S-WMD consistently outperforms almost all baselines and achieves state-of-the-art on five of eight datasets.  
- Provides detailed analysis and visualizations of learned weights and the effect of supervision.  
- Thorough and well-executed experiments that strongly support the method's effectiveness.  
- Well-written paper with a comprehensive related-work survey, including metric learning methods.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of how learned weights relate to specific tasks and datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity and scalability to large datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, strong empirical results, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: the two weaknesses above, though considered minor.
