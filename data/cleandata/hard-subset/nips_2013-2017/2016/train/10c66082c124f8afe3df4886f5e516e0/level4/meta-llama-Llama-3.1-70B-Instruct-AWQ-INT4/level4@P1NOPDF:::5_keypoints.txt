- Builds on Word Mover’s Distance by learning supervised word-specific weights for text classification.  
- Proposes using the Sinkhorn (entropic) distance as a computationally efficient proxy for the Wasserstein distance.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, balancing technical rigor and clarity for a broad audience.  
- Limited novelty relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance paper.  
- Including the “World Centroid Distance” baseline seems unnecessary and may confuse readers.  
- Treatment of the regularizer lambda is insufficient; its choice can strongly affect results and behavior.  
- Reviewer questions whether lambda is set to approximate pure Wasserstein or to favor Sinkhorn (entropic) behavior, and notes practical trade-offs (convergence vs fidelity).
