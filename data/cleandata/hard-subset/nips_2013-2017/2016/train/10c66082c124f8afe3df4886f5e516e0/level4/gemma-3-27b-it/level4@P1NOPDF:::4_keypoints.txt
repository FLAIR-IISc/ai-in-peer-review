- Proposes supervised document-distance learning by extending WMD to learn a linear embedding transform A and a reweighted word distribution w to increase class separation.  
- Paper is clearly written, easy to follow, with a thorough and knowledgeable related-work review.  
- Provides experimental results on several datasets, including some novel ones.  
- Motivation for reweighting w is insufficiently explained; unclear why reweighting is beneficial.  
- Concern that reweighting could fundamentally alter document representation; need evidence it boosts performance without changing document meaning.  
- Contribution appears incremental: applying a linear transform and reweighting within an NCA objective is plausible but not highly novel.  
- Unclear procedure for handling unseen documents—how to determine reweighting w for new inputs.  
- The term “semantic difference” in the abstract is ambiguous and should be clarified (labels like authorship/topic may not be semantic).  
- Reviewer is generally positive and does not oppose acceptance, contingent on satisfactory responses to the above concerns.
