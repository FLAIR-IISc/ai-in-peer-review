- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance: learn a linear transform of word embeddings plus a re-weighted word distribution.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Experimental results on several novel datasets show the method’s effectiveness.  
- Motivation for re-weighting the word distribution (vector w) is unclear and not justified—uncertain how it improves distances without altering meaning.  
- The contribution appears incremental (combining linear transform + re-weighting with NCA) rather than a major new paradigm.  
- Unclear how to compute or apply the re-weighting vector w for new, unseen documents.  
- The term “semantic difference” is used ambiguously and not explicitly defined (labels? authorship? topic?).  
- Suggestions: clarify motivation for re-weighting, discuss broader significance/impact, explain application to unseen documents, and define “semantic difference.”  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending satisfactory responses to the above concerns.
