- Introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) to learn a task-specific document distance metric.  
- Learns a linear embedding transformation (A) and a word-importance vector (w) jointly to adapt WMD for supervision.  
- Uses a Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) objective to minimize stochastic k-NN leave-one-out classification error.  
- Addresses intractable WMD gradients by approximating WMD with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance for efficient differentiation.  
- Proposes a practical gradient-based algorithm enabled by the Sinkhorn approximation.  
- Validated on eight text-classification datasets, consistently outperforming 26 competitive baselines.  
- Significance: fills the unsupervised limitation of WMD, enabling task-adapted document distances for classification tasks.  
- Technical novelty: key contribution is making WMD differentiable/learnable via Sinkhorn regularization and gradient computation.  
- Experimental evaluation: comprehensive comparisons across many baselines and representations show consistent gains.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful background and visualizations.  
- Weakness: motivation for learning the word re-weighting vector (w) needs more explanation and discussion of potential distortions.  
- Weakness: lacks an ablation study isolating the contributions of A versus w.  
- Weakness: needs explicit clarification of how the learned w is applied to unseen test documents.  
- Minor point: term “semantic difference” is imprecise — suggest “task-specific similarity” or “label-based similarity.”  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept; high-quality, novel, and practically significant work with minor revisions suggested.
