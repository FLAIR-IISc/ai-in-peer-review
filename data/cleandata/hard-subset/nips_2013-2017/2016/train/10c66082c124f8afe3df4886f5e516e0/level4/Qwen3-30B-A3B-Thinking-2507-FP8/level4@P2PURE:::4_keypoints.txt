- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance by learning a linear embedding transform A and a reweighted word distribution w to improve label separation.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and shows strong familiarity with related work.  
- Experimental results are provided on multiple novel datasets.  
- Rationale for reweighting the word distribution w is insufficiently explained; reweighting may alter document semantics and the authors must clarify how meaning is preserved while improving label separation.  
- Contribution is incremental: adapting WMD with a linear transform and reweighting under an NCA objective is plausible but not particularly novel.  
- The paper fails to explain how to compute the reweighting w for new, unseen documents, which is crucial for practical use.  
- The term "semantic difference" is ambiguous—authors should clarify whether it refers to label-based distinctions (e.g., topic/authorship) or true semantic meaning.  
- Overall recommendation: paper merits acceptance pending satisfactory resolution of the above points.
