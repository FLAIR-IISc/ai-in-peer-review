- Paper proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform A and word-importance vector w to adapt WMD for supervised text classification.  
- Uses a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) objective and Sinkhorn entropy-regularized OT for differentiable, efficient training.  
- Training speedups include word-centroid initialization (S-WCD), mini-batching, and neighbor filtering.  
- Empirically evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance; code released.  
- Builds on WMD, Sinkhorn regularization, and classic metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML); main novelty is their integration for supervised document distances.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with solid mathematical derivations and explained gradients via Sinkhorn dual variables.  
- Practical and efficient training pipeline that meaningfully reduces computational cost.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation with strong baselines and proper tuning.  
- Sensible design choice to learn both A and w, increasing flexibility over prior approaches.  
- Novelty is incremental since components (WMD, Sinkhorn, NCA) are known, though the integration is useful.  
- Insufficient discussion and sensitivity analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization λ, which affects approximation vs. speed trade-offs.  
- Confusing or inconsistent discussion/references around Word Centroid Distance (WCD); role as lower bound and initialization needs clarity.  
- S-WMD underperforms NCA on TF-IDF for long-document datasets (BBCSPORT, OHSUMED) with limited analysis of causes or remedies.  
- Ground cost fixed to squared Euclidean distance may be suboptimal (e.g., CLASSIC); lack of ablations on alternative costs or constraints on A.  
- Theoretical guidance on the approximation error induced by Sinkhorn relaxation and its effect on gradients is lacking.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical gains, efficiency, clear integration of metric learning and OT, and reproducibility.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited conceptual novelty, insufficient λ analysis, and unclear WCD treatment and cost ablations.  
- Suggested experiments: sensitivity study for λ (accuracy and runtime), ablations isolating A vs. w and cost choices, comparison to exact WMD gradients on short docs, and analysis/fixes for long-document failures.  
- Overall: strong, practical, and well-written paper with meaningful empirical contribution; addressing λ and WCD issues would further strengthen it.
