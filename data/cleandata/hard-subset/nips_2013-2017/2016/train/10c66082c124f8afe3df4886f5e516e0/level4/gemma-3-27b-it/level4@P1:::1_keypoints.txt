- Proposal: supervised metric learning for transport distances (extending Word Mover's Distance) by learning a linear projection and per-word weighting with re-normalized frequencies.
- Applicability: method applies to any transport distance based on squared Euclidean distances after linear projection.
- Optimization: introduces an efficient optimization method to learn the parameterized metric (Lines 156–175).
- Empirical evaluation: thorough experiments comparing to many baselines on multiple document classification datasets using kNN with the learned metric.
- Strength: technically interesting and useful extension of WMD, showing consistent empirical improvements.
- Weakness: gains over a strong initialization (S‑WCD / word-centroid-based EMD) are sometimes modest (small difference in Table 3).
- Missing baseline comparison: does not evaluate word-centroid embeddings under existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- Ablation gap: lacks an analysis isolating the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.
- Originality: main novelty lies in the optimization procedure; linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a limited contribution.
- Significance: addresses WMD’s lack of task-specific supervision and offers a practical, effective approach.
- Clarity: paper is very well written; requests minor clarifications (which "The authors" refers to, α/β asterisks in Eq.11, explicit mention that line ~170 refers to Sinkhorn scaling).
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, addresses a clear limitation of WMD, efficient/practical method, excellent writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: modest improvement over S‑WCD, incomplete ablation study, missing metric-learning comparisons on centroid embeddings.
- Overall: a strong, valuable contribution to document similarity and metric learning with only minor, addressable weaknesses.
