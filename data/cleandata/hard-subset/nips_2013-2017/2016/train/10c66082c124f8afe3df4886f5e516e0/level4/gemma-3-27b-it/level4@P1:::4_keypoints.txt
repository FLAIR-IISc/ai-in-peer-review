- Paper proposes a supervised extension of WMD by learning a linear embedding transform and word-frequency re-weighting to improve label-based document separation.  
- Writing and organization are clear, with a thorough literature review and good understanding of prior work.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive across multiple datasets and technically sound.  
- Motivation for the word re-weighting vector w is insufficiently explained.  
- It is unclear why re-weighting the document distribution does not fundamentally alter the document’s representation or meaning.  
- Authors should demonstrate re-weighting improves class separation without drastically changing inherent document semantics.  
- The methodological contribution (linear transform + re-weighting in an NCA framework) seems incremental and not highly novel.  
- Practical concern: how to handle new/unseen documents that have no pre-computed re-weightings.  
- Terminology issue: “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified (e.g., labels vs. authorship or topic).  
- Reviewer has reservations about significance but would not oppose acceptance if concerns are addressed.
