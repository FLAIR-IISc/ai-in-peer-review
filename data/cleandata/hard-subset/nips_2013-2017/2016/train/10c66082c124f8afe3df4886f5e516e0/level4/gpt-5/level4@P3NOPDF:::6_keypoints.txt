- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that jointly learns a linear transform A of embeddings and per-word importance weights w for kNN document classification.  
- Timely and relevant idea: leveraging labels to adapt WMD to downstream tasks is well motivated.  
- Joint learning of A and w is appealing because it can capture global geometry and per-word relevance.  
- Empirical results show consistent gains over unsupervised WMD and baselines, and code is promised.  
- Lacks intuition for word-importance weights (Eq. (5)): need clarity on how w interacts with the transport plan and whether it reweights histograms or modifies ground costs, plus a small example.  
- Missing ablation to disentangle contributions of A vs w—must report results for only A, only w, and both.  
- Confusing usage of Eq. (2) and regularization parameters: clarify when Eq. (2) is used, whether the multiple λ’s are distinct, and provide sensitivity analyses.  
- The claim that the method is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words needs theoretical justification or targeted controlled experiments (e.g., synonym/paraphrase swaps).  
- Evaluation is limited to kNN; add baselines like linear SVM, logistic regression on BoW/tf-idf, averaged embeddings, or supervised encoders, or justify their omission.  
- Missing experimental details: how k in kNN is chosen and its sensitivity, clarify “n” vs “ne” in Table 2, and correct the pointer to the reported “average error.”  
- Need discussion of computational cost and memory overhead versus vanilla WMD and any approximations used (e.g., Sinkhorn, pruning) and their accuracy trade-offs.  
- Reproducibility requires full hyperparameter settings, data preprocessing, and exact validation protocol for selecting k and λ.  
- Concrete suggestions: provide the ablation study, expand Eq. (5) explanation (constraints, learning, gradients), clarify Eq. (2)/λs, substantiate forgiving claim qualitatively/quantitatively, report runtimes, and fix presentation issues.  
- Overall: well-motivated and promising approach, but acceptance should depend on clarifying the weighting mechanism, performing the ablation to attribute gains, and resolving several clarity and experimental issues.
