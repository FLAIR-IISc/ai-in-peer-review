- Summary: Proposes supervised WMD learning a linear ground metric A and word-importance vector w with an NCA-style kNN loss, using Sinkhorn relaxation and S-WCD initialization; shows strong kNN performance across datasets.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD, entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn), and NCA; relates to Cuturi & Avis, Lebanon, Le & Cuturi, and Wang et al., with novelty mainly in applying a continuous linear map and joint reweighting.  
- Quality: Conceptually simple and technically sound with extensive experiments, but mathematical exposition has notational inconsistencies (Euclidean vs squared Euclidean).  
- Clarity: Generally readable but needs clearer treatment of regularized vs unregularized OT (λ effects), consistent p/Wasserstein notation, and an algorithm box for reproducibility.  
- Originality: Incremental contribution—combines known components (learned linear metric, Sinkhorn, histogram reweighting) though the joint A+w in a WMD/NCA text setting is a useful instantiation.  
- Significance: Practically relevant supervised metric for kNN tasks; efficient and scalable due to Sinkhorn and neighbor pruning.  
- Strengths: Simple, effective idea with strong empirical gains; solid engineering (S-WCD init, batching, pruning); clear motivation for learning both metric and weights.  
- Weaknesses/Suggestions: Provide deeper analysis of λ and its gradient effects, fix notation rigor, cite prior reweighting work explicitly, include pseudo-code, and add ablation on w.  
- Questions for authors: How much does w contribute (A-only vs A+w ablation)? How sensitive are results to p and λ? Provide runtime comparisons with exact WMD-based learning.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong, consistent empirical results; practical use of Sinkhorn OT; method is adoptable and scalable.  
- Cons for acceptance: Mostly a synthesis of known techniques; exposition and citation gaps; missing ablation on the weight vector w.  
- Overall recommendation: Support acceptance, contingent on addressing clarity, prior-work positioning, and providing the requested ablation.
