- Task & contribution: Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns word-specific weights and uses the Sinkhorn distance for computational efficiency.
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments on eight datasets versus 26 baselines showing superior average performance.
- Writing & analysis: Paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear introduction and accessible mathematical treatment.
- Novelty concern: Contribution feels incremental relative to the original Word Mover’s Distance work.
- Relevance of baselines: Comparisons to Word Centroid Distance are confusing and seem less relevant to the main claims.
- Major technical concern: Insufficient discussion of the Sinkhorn regularization factor (lambda) and its practical impact on the objective.
- Clarification requested: Need analysis of the trade-off—whether chosen lambda yields behavior closer to true Wasserstein (accurate but slower) or to entropy-regularized Sinkhorn (faster but different).
