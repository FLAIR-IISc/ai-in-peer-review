This paper proposes a novel method for learning a supervised word mover's distance (S-WMD), which leverages the word mover's distance (WMD) and incorporates supervision to improve document classification. The authors' approach is to learn a linear transformation of the word embeddings and a word importance vector, which allows for a more accurate representation of the semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The experimental results are also impressive, showing that S-WMD outperforms 26 competitive baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks. The visualization of the document embeddings using t-SNE also provides a clear insight into the quality of the learned distance metric.

One of the strengths of the paper is its simplicity and straightforward idea. The authors have successfully combined existing techniques, such as NCA and the WMD, to create a novel method that is both effective and efficient. The experimental section is also well-executed, providing a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the authors could provide a more rigorous mathematical treatment of their method, including clearer notation and computations. Additionally, the authors could provide more insight into the importance of the weight vector w in the learned metric. It would be interesting to see if this innovation is relevant in any way and if it provides any benefits over the unsupervised WMD.

Furthermore, the authors could provide a clearer discussion of the related work, highlighting the differences between their approach and previous methods. For example, the authors could elaborate on how their method builds upon the work of Cuturi and Avis, and how it differs from other approaches such as Wang et al.
