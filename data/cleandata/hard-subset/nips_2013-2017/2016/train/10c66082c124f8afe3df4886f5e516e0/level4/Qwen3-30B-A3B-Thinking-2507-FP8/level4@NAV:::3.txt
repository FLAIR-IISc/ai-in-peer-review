

The authors introduce a novel approach for learning parameters of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), specifically the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) computed on text documents using word embeddings to define bin-to-bin dissimilarities. They parameterize the EMD cost (i.e., ground metric) matrix via a Mahalanobis distance between vector representations and additionally propose learning histogram coefficients reweighting (referencing Lebanon's prior work, as noted below). The paper derives gradients for NCA-type costs and presents compelling empirical validation. Overall, the work elegantly combines established techniques with highly convincing experimental results, warranting acceptance. Key strengths include methodological simplicity and a conceptually straightforward yet non-trivial implementation. The experimental section serves as a particularly strong component.  

Areas for improvement:  
1. Clarify the interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations.  
2. Enhance mathematical rigor (computations and notations are somewhat imprecise).  
3. Include a formal algorithmic description to improve clarity.  

Minor comments:  
- Equation (1) uses Euclidean distance between embeddings as the cost, while Equation (6) employs squared Euclidean (typically associated with 2-Wasserstein). To avoid inconsistency, explicitly state that WMD is p-Wasserstein between word embedding clouds for any p.  
- The reweighting strategy in Equation (5) is not novel; it was previously proposed by Lebanon for text classification ("Metric Learning for Text Documents") and further explored in Le/Cuturi ("Unsupervised Riemannian Metric Learning for Histograms Using Aitchison Transformations").  
- Provide experimental insights on the significance of the learned weight vector **w**—does this component substantially impact performance?  
- Strengthen the related work section by explicitly differentiating from Cuturi/Avis (who use discrete costs and entropy regularization for scalability, while this work employs continuous, linearly parameterized costs) and clarifying that Wang et al.'s approach is non-published and fails when the OT optimization is run once as proposed.
