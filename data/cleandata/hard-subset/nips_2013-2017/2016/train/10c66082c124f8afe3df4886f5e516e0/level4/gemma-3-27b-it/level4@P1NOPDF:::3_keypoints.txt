- Proposal: learn parameters of Word Mover’s Distance by parameterizing the ground metric via a Mahalanobis distance and learning histogram reweighting coefficients.  
- Method: provide gradients for NCA-type costs and use entropy-regularized OT for scalable computation.  
- Empirical result: experiments are compelling and provide strong validation; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Strength: idea is simple and conceptually straightforward, with a particularly strong experimental section.  
- Strength: combines existing techniques effectively to produce convincing results.  
- Weakness: interplay between regularized and non-regularized formulations is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Weakness: mathematical treatment and notation are somewhat sloppy and would benefit from greater rigor.  
- Suggestion: include an algorithmic box to clearly illustrate the proposed approach.  
- Minor comment: Equation inconsistency — Euclidean distance in Eq.1 vs squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein) in Eq.6; suggest stating WMD as a p-Wasserstein distance for generality.  
- Prior work: the reweighting in Eq.5 is not novel and was earlier introduced by Guy Lebanon (“metric learning for text documents”).  
- Related literature: mention and compare Le & Cuturi’s work on histogram metric learning (Aitchison transformations).  
- Experimental question: evaluate the importance and impact of the learned weight vector w — is it empirically significant?  
- Related work clarity: more clearly distinguish this paper from Cuturi/Avis (entropy regularization, discrete vs continuous cost) and from Wang et al.  
- Reproducibility note: Wang et al.’s one-time OT optimization is reported as not readily reproducible in its current form.
