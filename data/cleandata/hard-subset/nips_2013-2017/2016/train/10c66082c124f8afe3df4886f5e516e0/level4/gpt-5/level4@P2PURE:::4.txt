This paper presents a supervised method for learning document distances on top of word mover’s distance (WMD). WMD, inspired by earth mover’s distance, computes optimal transport between two word distributions to yield a document distance, with word2vec embeddings providing the ground metric between words. Here, the authors propose learning (1) a linear transformation of the word embeddings and (2) a reweighting of the word distribution, to better separate documents with different labels. The paper is clearly written and easy to follow, includes a thorough review of related work, and demonstrates strong familiarity with the literature. The experiments cover many datasets, including some novel ones. However, the motivation for the formulation needs more explanation, and the overall significance appears limited.

1) Why introduce the reweighting w over the word distribution? The motivation is not well developed, and such reweighting could effectively turn a document into a substantially different one. If the authors can demonstrate why this reweighting helps without altering the document into something different, then it would be justified.

2) The contribution seems incremental. Using a linear transformation A and a reweighting w with an NCA objective is a plausible supervised extension of WMD, but the formulation does not appear groundbreaking or particularly novel.

3) How is the distance computed for a new, unseen document? Specifically, how is the reweight w determined for a new document at test time?

4) In the abstract, “semantic difference” seems to correspond to document labels. Differences in authorship or topic do not obviously constitute “semantic difference.” Do the authors mean something else by this term?

Overall, the paper is well written, and I would not oppose acceptance if these questions are adequately addressed.
