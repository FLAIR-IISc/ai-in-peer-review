- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD): learns a linear transform of word embeddings and word-specific importance weights to improve WMD for classification.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD and stochastic neighborhood learning (inspired by NCA) for efficient optimization.  
- Evaluated on eight document classification tasks and compared against 26 baselines, consistently outperforming them in kNN error.  
- Technical approach is sound and well-motivated: addresses WMD’s lack of supervision and task-dependent similarity needs.  
- Strong experimental evaluation: diverse datasets, comprehensive baselines, and consistent empirical gains (best average performance; lowest error on 5/8 datasets).  
- Well-written paper with clear mathematical exposition and logical presentation.  
- Key technical strength: a rigorous, efficient gradient approximation enabling practical optimization of S-WMD.  
- Main weakness: only marginal improvement over a simpler Supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) baseline.  
- Reported average rank difference between S-WMD and S-WCD is minimal (S-WMD: 1.0, S-WCD: 1.1), questioning added value.  
- Lacks comparison of weighted word centroid embeddings combined with standard metric learning (ITML, LMNN, NCA) to isolate S-WMD’s contribution.  
- Missing ablation study to quantify the relative importance of the linear transform A versus word weights w.  
- Originality is limited: builds on existing metric learning and NCA ideas; main novelty is the efficient approximation method.  
- Significance is tempered: supervised improvement is demonstrated but marginal gains may not justify added complexity in practice.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on addressing evaluation gaps (additional comparisons and ablations) for camera-ready version.
