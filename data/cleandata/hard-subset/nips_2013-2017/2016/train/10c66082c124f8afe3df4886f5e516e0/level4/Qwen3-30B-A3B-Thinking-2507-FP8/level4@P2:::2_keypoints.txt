- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns an affine transform of embeddings and word importance weights to incorporate supervision for document distances.  
- Uses a stochastic leave-one-out classification loss to train the transformation and weights.  
- Introduces a practical gradient approximation for WMD using the Sinkhorn distance, reducing complexity from O(q^3 log q) to O(q^2).  
- Novel metric learning approach that jointly learns a linear embedding transformation and word-specific importance weights.  
- Empirically evaluated on eight diverse text classification datasets against 26 baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, mSDA, NCA, ITML, LMNN, etc.).  
- Consistently outperforms all baselines on the majority of datasets with several statistically significant improvements.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear mathematical formulation and efficient approximation techniques.  
- Thorough experimental design: Bayesian hyperparameter tuning and averaging over multiple train/test splits where needed.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with useful figures (t-SNE), pseudocode (Algorithm 1), and reproducibility details in supplements.  
- Originality: extends metric learning to operate directly on word embedding space rather than on document representations.  
- Practical significance: improved classification performance and competitive training times make it appealing for real-world use.  
- Minor weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to the Sinkhorn λ parameter.  
- Minor weakness: scalability to extremely large datasets is not fully explored.  
- Minor weakness: initialization procedure (S-WCD) needs more detailed analysis of its impact.  
- Minor weakness: limited discussion of performance with embedding types beyond word2vec.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, well-validated, and practically significant; could be improved by deeper analysis of limitations and hyperparameter sensitivity.
