This paper introduces a supervised variant of Word Mover’s Distance (WMD), i.e., Earth Mover’s Distance applied to documents with word-embedding-based ground costs. The approach learns a parametric ground metric via a Mahalanobis transformation over word vectors, and, in addition, estimates a reweighting of histogram coefficients. The authors derive NCA-style gradients for this objective and back them with strong empirical results. Overall, the contribution reads as a clean synthesis of known components that, while conceptually simple, is nontrivial to implement and validate at scale; the experiments are particularly convincing. I am inclined to recommend acceptance. The work’s primary merits are its simplicity and practicality. To strengthen the paper: better articulate the relationship between regularized and unregularized formulations, tighten the mathematical exposition (some notational/derivational looseness), and include a clear algorithmic summary to crystallize the method.

Minor comments:
- In Eq. 1 the ground cost uses the Euclidean distance between embeddings, whereas Eq. 6 switches to squared Euclidean for Mahalanobis learning (corresponding to the 2-Wasserstein case). To avoid this mismatch, it would be clearer to frame WMD generically as the p-Wasserstein distance over embedded word clouds, for an arbitrary p.
- The histogram “reweighting” in Eq. 5 is not novel; see Lebanon’s work on metric learning for text documents, and also Le & Cuturi’s “Unsupervised Riemannian Metric Learning for Histograms Using Aitchison Transformations.”
- Please provide experimental analysis on the contribution of the learned weight vector w. Does this component materially affect performance? An ablation would clarify its relevance.
- Related work: the distinctions from prior efforts should be made more explicit. This paper builds on Cuturi/Avis but leverages entropy regularization, which markedly improves speed/scale, and uses a continuous, linearly parameterized ground cost rather than a discrete one—these points are not currently emphasized. The connection to Wang et al. should also be clarified; that work, in its current form, is not ready for publication, and solves the OT once, which empirically is insufficient.
