- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns word-specific weights to prioritize important words for classification.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn (entropically regularized) distance as an efficient proxy for the exact Wasserstein distance in WMD.  
- Empirically evaluated on eight benchmark datasets against 26 baselines, showing state-of-the-art average performance.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear motivation, literature positioning, and balanced mathematical derivations.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation provides strong evidence of the method’s effectiveness across varied text classification tasks.  
- Novelty is incremental: main contribution is a supervised adaptation of existing WMD rather than a radical new concept.  
- Justification for the detailed comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is unclear and felt distracting.  
- Insufficient analysis of the entropic regularization parameter (lambda): missing reported values, justification, and a sensitivity/ablation study on lambda’s effect on fidelity to Wasserstein, performance, and convergence speed.
