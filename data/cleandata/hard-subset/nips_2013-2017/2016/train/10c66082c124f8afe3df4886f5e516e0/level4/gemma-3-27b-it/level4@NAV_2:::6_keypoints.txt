- Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transformation A and word-importance weights w.  
- Addresses WMD’s unsupervised limitation by incorporating supervision into the document distance metric.  
- Learns A and w by minimizing leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance to efficiently approximate the optimal transport problem and enable gradient computation.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across eight real-world text classification tasks, outperforming 26 baselines.  
- Provides useful visualizations and a detailed analysis of training time.  
- Core idea of jointly learning transformation and word weights is a compelling and principled extension.  
- Mathematical formulation and the Sinkhorn-based approximation are technically sound.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-written.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the individual contributions of A versus w.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to a simple classifier trained directly on word embeddings as a baseline.  
- Weakness: does not explicitly state the k used for k-nearest neighbors or whether k is tuned per dataset.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with high empirical validation; minor clarifications and extra baselines would strengthen the paper.
