- Proposes supervised learning of document distance by learning a linear transform of word embeddings and re-weighted word distributions on top of WMD.  
- Builds on Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) and uses Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) objective for supervision.  
- Paper is well-written, with a thorough literature review and clear organization.  
- Experimental results are presented on several novel datasets and are reproducible for expert readers.  
- Motivation for the proposed formulation is weak and not well-explained.  
- The choice and role of re-weighting the word distribution w is unclear and potentially harmful (could change document meaning).  
- The contribution is incremental — applying a linear transform A and re-weighting w with NCA is a straightforward extension of WMD.  
- Ambiguity about how to obtain a re-weighting vector w (and thus a distance) for completely new documents.  
- The term “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified (labels, authorship, topic, etc.).  
- Originality is limited; the paper does not offer a novel combination or new perspective beyond existing WMD/NCA work.  
- Significance is limited: improvements over state-of-the-art are small and not clearly impactful.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear writing, good literature understanding, and well-presented experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak motivation, limited novelty, no substantial performance gains, and unclear handling of new-doc scenarios.
