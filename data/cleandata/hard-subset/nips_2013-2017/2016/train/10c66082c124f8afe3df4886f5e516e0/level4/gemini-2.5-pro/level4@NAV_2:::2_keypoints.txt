- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that jointly learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and per-word importance weights.  
- Key technical contribution is an efficient gradient computation using an entropy-regularized (Sinkhorn) approximation of the WMD optimal transport problem.  
- Optimizes the metric to minimize a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification error.  
- Significance/originality: fills a gap by enabling supervision within the WMD framework, combining non-parametric matching with metric learning.  
- Technical quality: sound approach—differentiation through WMD via entropy smoothing is elegant; sensible initialization (S-WCD) and efficient batch optimization presented.  
- Clarity: paper is very well written, provides necessary background, clear derivations, and logical structure.  
- Empirical evaluation: extensive experiments on eight real-world datasets against 26 baselines, showing state-of-the-art performance on most tasks.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: several new hyperparameters (λ, batch size, neighbor set) need discussion or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — analysis of learned metric: visualization of word weights is good, but more insight into the learned affine transform A (scaling vs. rotation vs. complex mapping) is needed.  
- Weakness — ground metric choice: using squared Euclidean may be suboptimal for some datasets (e.g., CLASSIC); generalization to other ground metrics is a possible future direction.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-motivated, technically solid work with strong empirical support; recommended as a strong candidate for acceptance.
