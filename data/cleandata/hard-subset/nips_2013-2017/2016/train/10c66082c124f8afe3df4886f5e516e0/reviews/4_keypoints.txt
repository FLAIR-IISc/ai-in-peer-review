- Proposes supervised learning of document distance on top of WMD by learning a linear transform A and a reweighted word distribution w.  
- Uses WMD (optimal transport between word distributions with word2vec costs) as the base distance.  
- Paper is clearly written, easy to follow, with a thorough literature review.  
- Provides extensive experimental results on several novel datasets.  
- Motivation for reweighting w is weak and not well explained; it could change a document into a different document.  
- Needs justification that reweighting improves label separation without altering document semantics.  
- Contribution is limited: applying a linear transform and reweighting with an NCA objective is not seen as a significant novelty.  
- Practical concern: unclear how to compute the reweight w for a completely new/unseen document.  
- Terminology issue: “semantic difference” in the abstract is ambiguous and appears to mean label/topic/authorship rather than true semantics.  
- Reviewer would not object to acceptance provided the above questions are properly addressed.
