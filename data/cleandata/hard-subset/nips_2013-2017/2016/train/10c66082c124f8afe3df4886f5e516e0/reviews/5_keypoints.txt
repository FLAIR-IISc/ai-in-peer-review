- Proposes supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance by learning word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy for Wasserstein.
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, reporting the best average performance.
- Paper is very well written with clear, in-depth mathematical exposition.
- Limited novelty compared to the prior Word Mover's Distance work.
- References to Word Centroid Distance are not relevant and are confusing.
- The role and choice of lambda (entropic regularizer) are poorly discussed in the manuscript.
- Lambda can have a dominant effect on eq(2), and its value dramatically impacts behavior and results.
- Unclear whether the method behaves like pure Wasserstein (large lambda) or like entropic/Sinkhorn (small lambda); most users choose small lambda for speed, which yields different behavior.
