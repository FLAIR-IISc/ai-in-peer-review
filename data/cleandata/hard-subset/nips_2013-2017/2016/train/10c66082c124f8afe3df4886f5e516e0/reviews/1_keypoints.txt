- Proposes supervised metric learning built on Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear projection and document-specific word re-weighting, with an efficient optimization and strong kNN classification results.  
- Positive: interesting and useful extension of WMD, shown effective on several kNN text classification tasks.  
- Concern: limited impact because EMD with metric learning initialized via a "word centroid distance" performs similarly to full proposed learning (see Table 3).  
- Missing baseline: no evaluation of word-centroid document embeddings with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: no isolated assessment of the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Originality: main novelty lies in the optimization approach (lines 156–175); linear metric learning within EMD is interesting but a somewhat limited contribution.  
- Writing: paper is very well written and pleasant to read.  
- Minor clarifications needed: specify which paper “The authors” refers to (line 152); Equation 11 likely needs stars on α and β; explicitly note around line 170 that the method uses Sinkhorn scaling referenced earlier.
