- Proposes S-WMD: learn linear transform A on word embeddings and nonuniform word-importance vector w to improve WMD for document similarity.  
- Uses a leave-one-out kNN/NCA-style objective with OT distances where ground cost is ||A(xi−xj)||^2 and histograms are reweighted by w.  
- Employs entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to accelerate distance computation and obtain approximate gradients using primal T*λ for A and dual potentials α*,β* for w.  
- Introduces S-WCD initialization (learn A on word centroids) and a batch/neighbor-filtered training loop for scalability.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines, reporting strong average ranking and state-of-the-art on 5 datasets; code released.  
- Builds on WMD and Sinkhorn OT literature, extending supervised EMD ideas to text by learning both metric and word-importance weights with a kNN/NCA objective.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated formulation leveraging pretrained embeddings.  
- Strength: elegant computational strategy using entropic OT and envelope-theorem-style gradients.  
- Strength: practical training recipe (initialization, batching, neighbor filtering) likely important for scalability.  
- Strength: broad empirical comparisons, consistent gains, and interpretability via learned w.  
- Weakness: unclear constraints/parameterization for w (nonnegativity) and lack of discussed regularization on A or w to prevent overfitting.  
- Weakness: notational overload and typos (e.g., p used for multiple meanings; incorrect Sinkhorn kernel definition Kij = exp(−λ Cij) needed).  
- Weakness: ambiguous sign/centering of Sinkhorn dual potentials and unclear impact on gradients for w.  
- Weakness: objective/gradient mismatch — loss uses unregularized OT but gradients use entropic approximations, creating biased gradients unless reconciled or λ→∞.  
- Weakness: scalability and test-time cost concerns for kNN+OT; unclear whether Sinkhorn is used at inference and how neighbor-filtering affects latency.  
- Weakness: experimental scope missing strong text baselines (TF-IDF SVM/fastText) and ablations (A-only, w-only, initialization, λ sensitivity).  
- Weakness: insufficient reproducibility details (learning rates, iterations, regularization, variance across runs) and inconsistent BO usage.  
- Questions: How is w constrained/reparameterized to ensure w≥0 and valid histograms?  
- Questions: Do you regularize A or w, and how are penalties chosen to avoid overfitting?  
- Questions: Is the loss in Eq. (6) computed with entropic transport or unregularized WMD, and what λ is used at inference?  
- Questions: What exact mapping/sign convention from Sinkhorn scalings u,v to α,β is used for ∂/∂w?  
- Questions: Please provide ablations for A-only, w-only, and sensitivity analyses for λ, batch size B, and neighbor count N.  
- Questions: What is test-time cost per query on the largest dataset under your neighbor-filtering strategy?  
- Minor: fix section numbering, equation refs, consistent notation for support size vs embedding dim vs rank.  
- Minor: clarify squared cost (p=2) vs L1; evaluate both as p choice may affect datasets differently.  
- Minor: complete and correct citations (e.g., Cuturi & Peyré 2016; Wang et al. ECCV 2011) and move key tables/figures to main text.  
- Overall: timely, well-motivated, and practically useful combination of metric learning and OT for text, but clarity issues, missing constraints/regularization, and entropic vs unregularized loss mismatch must be addressed.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept conditional on clarifying optimization details (w constraints, dual potentials), aligning objective with entropic approximation, and adding ablations/sensitivity analyses.
