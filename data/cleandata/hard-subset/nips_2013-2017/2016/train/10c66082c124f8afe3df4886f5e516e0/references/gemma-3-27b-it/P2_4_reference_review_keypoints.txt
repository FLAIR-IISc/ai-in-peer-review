- Introduces S-WMD: a supervised method that adapts word embeddings and re-weights word frequencies within the WMD framework using Sinkhorn relaxation and probabilistic filtering.  
- Novelty: learns a metric inside the WMD computation itself rather than before or after representation learning.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms 26 baselines across 8 diverse datasets.  
- Theoretical justification & efficiency: uses Sinkhorn distance and nearest-neighbor filtering for a tractable and principled approximation of optimal transport.  
- Clear writing & structure: paper is well-written and accessible, with good background coverage.  
- Practical significance: potential impact on information retrieval, text classification, and related applications.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: lack of details on search space and computational cost raises concerns about tuning sensitivity.  
- Implementation complexity: non-trivial to implement; requires familiarity with optimal transport and metric learning.  
- Limited ablation study: insufficient analysis of individual contributions from the linear transformation (A) versus word re-weighting (w).  
- Discussion of limitations: acknowledges some issues (long documents, squared Euclidean) but lacks broader failure-mode analysis.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound with solid theoretical and experimental support.  
- Clarity assessment: exceptionally clear presentation.  
- Originality assessment: highly original and differentiates clearly from prior work.  
- Significance assessment: results are significant and likely to interest researchers and practitioners.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical performance, novelty, efficiency, clarity, and practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about hyperparameter sensitivity, implementation complexity, and limited ablations.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend accepting the paper.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
