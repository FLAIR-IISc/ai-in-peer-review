## Review of "Supervised Word Mover’s Distance"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method for learning a document distance metric by adapting word embeddings and re-weighting word frequencies within the framework of the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD). The authors address the challenge of incorporating supervision into WMD, which traditionally relies on unsupervised word embeddings. They achieve efficiency by leveraging a relaxed optimal transport problem (Sinkhorn distance) and a probabilistic filtering approach. The core idea is to learn a linear transformation of word representations and word importance weights to align WMD distances with semantic similarity defined by document labels.  Extensive experiments across 8 datasets demonstrate superior performance compared to 26 baseline methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of metric learning *within* the WMD framework, alongside word re-weighting, is a genuinely novel approach. Existing work largely focuses on learning document representations *before* distance calculation, or learning generalized metrics applied *after* representation learning. S-WMD learns to modulate the fundamental building blocks of WMD itself.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents compelling experimental results, consistently outperforming a large number of strong baselines across diverse datasets. The comprehensive comparison is a major strength.
* **Theoretical Justification & Efficient Implementation:** The use of the Sinkhorn distance provides a theoretically sound and computationally efficient way to approximate the optimal transport problem, addressing a key bottleneck in supervised WMD. The batch gradient descent with nearest neighbor filtering further enhances efficiency.
* **Clear Writing & Structure:** The paper is well-written, clearly outlining the motivations, methods, and results. The background section provides sufficient context for readers unfamiliar with WMD and metric learning.
* **Practical Significance:**  The potential to improve document distance metrics has wide-ranging applications in information retrieval, text classification, and related fields.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the authors mention tuning hyperparameters with Bayesian Optimization, the details of the search space and computational cost are not explicitly stated. The sensitivity of performance to hyperparameter settings could be a concern.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** Although the authors address computational efficiency, implementing S-WMD appears non-trivial, requiring familiarity with optimal transport, metric learning, and potentially specialized optimization techniques.
* **Limited Ablation Study:** While the initialization with S-WCD is shown to be helpful, a more detailed ablation study investigating the individual contributions of the linear transformation (A) and the word re-weighting (w) would strengthen the analysis. Is one component significantly more important than the other?
* **Discussion of Limitations:** The discussion acknowledges potential issues with long documents and the squared Euclidean distance, but a more general discussion of limitations and potential failure modes would be beneficial.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-supported. The theoretical foundations are solid, the implementation details are sufficiently described, and the experimental results are convincing. The authors are careful to present a comprehensive comparison with existing methods.
* **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly organized. The core concepts are explained in a concise and accessible manner.
* **Originality:** The approach is highly original, building upon existing techniques in a novel and impactful way. The authors clearly delineate how their work differs from previous contributions.
* **Significance:** The results are significant and likely to be of interest to researchers and practitioners in the field of information retrieval and natural language processing. The potential for improved document distance metrics is a valuable contribution.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For:**

* Strong empirical performance across diverse datasets.
* Novel and theoretically sound approach.
* Efficient implementation leveraging existing techniques.
* Clear and well-written presentation.
* Potential for significant impact in relevant fields.

**Against:**

* Potential hyperparameter sensitivity.
* Implementation complexity.
* Limited ablation study.



**Overall Assessment:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper. The authors present a novel, well-supported, and impactful contribution to the field of document distance metrics. While some areas (hyperparameter sensitivity, ablation study) could be further explored, the strengths of the paper far outweigh the weaknesses.  This work advances the state-of-the-art in a demonstrable way and is likely to inspire further research in this area.



**Confidence:** 9/10
