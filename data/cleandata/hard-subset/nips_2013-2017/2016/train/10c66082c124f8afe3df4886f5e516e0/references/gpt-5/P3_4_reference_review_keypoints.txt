- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, learning a linear transform A and nonnegative word-importance vector w for WMD, using entropic OT (Sinkhorn) and an NCA-style loss with S-WCD initialization and neighbor-pruning; strong empirical results on 8 datasets and code released.

- Strength: Injects supervision into WMD without learning document representations, keeping computations in low-dimensional embedding space.

- Strength: Methodologically coherent—combines metric learning (NCA), entropic OT (Sinkhorn), and histogram reweighting with sensible gradient handling for the ground metric.

- Strength: Practical contributions include careful initialization (S-WCD), batch training with neighbor pruning, complexity analysis, and public code.

- Strength: Broad empirical comparison to many baselines, yielding best average rank and top accuracy on 5/8 datasets.

- Strength: Learned word weights are interpretable and supported by qualitative visualizations.

- Weakness: Objective/gradient mismatch—paper mixes unregularized loss notation with gradients computed from entropic-regularized solutions; needs consistent definition of optimized surrogate D^λ_A,w.

- Weakness: Insufficient clarification/constraints on w—nonnegativity, regularization, and identifiability (scaling) are not specified or properly parameterized.

- Weakness: Dual potentials (αλ, βλ) and their centering are stated without derivation or clear references; impact of histogram normalization on gradients is unclear.

- Weakness: Notational slippages, typos, missing/malformed references, and undefined constants reduce clarity and reproducibility.

- Weakness: Limited ablations—no isolated A vs w comparisons, limited sensitivity analysis for λ, initialization, neighbor-pruning N, batch size B, or runtime/accuracy trade-offs.

- Weakness: Baselines omit modern supervised text classifiers (e.g., fastText, simple CNN/LSTM variants), weakening comparative claims.

- Weakness: Evaluation scope limited to kNN classification; retrieval/ranking metrics (MAP, nDCG) and domain-shift robustness are not reported.

- Quality: Overall technically sound with reasonable optimization and solid empirical evaluation, but main theoretical caveat is the surrogate vs. written objective mismatch and underspecified w derivation.

- Clarity: Generally readable but undermined by notation issues and insufficient detail on optimization and gradient derivations needed for reproducibility.

- Originality: Novel in NLP—joint learning of supervised ground metric and global histogram weights for WMD using Sinkhorn and NCA; related vision work noted but NLP application is new.

- Significance: Valuable contribution given WMD's popularity; supervised, efficient variant with public recipe likely to influence future supervised OT work for text.

- Suggestions: Explicitly define the optimized objective D^λ_A,w, provide λ sensitivity plots, and discuss λ→∞ trade-offs.

- Suggestions: Specify parameterization/enforcement and regularization for w (e.g., w = exp(u), ℓ1/ℓ2 penalties) and add regularization for A.

- Suggestions: Provide precise derivation/references for entropic-OT duals and the centering term, and include ablation studies (only-A, only-w, initialization, pruning, runtime).

- Suggestions: Clarify output dimension r of A, whether dimensionality reduction is learned, and add at least one modern supervised text baseline and retrieval metrics.

- Pros for acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a principled, efficient approach, strong empirical results, public code, and broad applicability.

- Cons for acceptance: Some theoretical/derivational details and constraints are underspecified, minor clarity/notation issues, limited ablations, and missing contemporary baselines.

- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on clarifying objective/gradients and detailing constraints/derivations for w; overall strong borderline accept with high reviewer confidence.
