## Review of "Supervised Word Mover’s Distance"

**Summary:**

This paper presents Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method for learning a document distance metric by adapting word embeddings and re-weighting word frequencies to improve the performance of the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD). The key contribution lies in efficiently incorporating supervision into the WMD framework, leveraging the Sinkhorn distance to mitigate computational costs. The authors demonstrate strong performance across eight datasets, outperforming numerous baselines including unsupervised WMD and other metric learning techniques.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The idea of learning a metric *within* the word embedding space, coupled with word re-weighting, is a valuable contribution. While metric learning is well-established, applying it specifically to refine WMD and leveraging the strengths of pre-trained word embeddings is novel.
* **Significance:** The results are compelling, consistently outperforming a wide range of baselines. The improvement over WMD, particularly on datasets where it was previously state-of-the-art, demonstrates the effectiveness of the supervised approach.  Document distance is a fundamental problem with applications across many NLP tasks, so improvements in this area are broadly impactful.
* **Technical Soundness:** The use of the Sinkhorn distance to approximate the optimal transport problem is a clever and effective optimization. The derivation of the gradients and the described optimization process appear sound. The authors address potential issues like gradient existence with the use of the relaxed transport problem.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The background section provides sufficient context for understanding WMD and related concepts. The explanation of the S-WMD algorithm and loss function is clear.
* **Completeness:** The paper provides extensive experimental results, including comparisons to a large number of baselines. The ablation study (showing results for the initialization step) is also helpful.  The included training time results are a positive addition.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the authors demonstrate efficiency gains, the overall approach appears complex to implement.  The reliance on multiple components (Sinkhorn distance, gradient calculations, batch optimization) may present a barrier to adoption.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The number of hyperparameters (learning rates, batch size, neighbor size, lambda for Sinkhorn regularization) could potentially make the method sensitive to tuning. While BO is used for tuning, a discussion of the sensitivity of the results to these parameters would be beneficial.
* **Limited Error Analysis:** The paper focuses on overall kNN error rates. A deeper analysis of *where* S-WMD succeeds and fails would strengthen the claims. Are there specific types of documents or relationships between documents where S-WMD performs particularly well or poorly?
* **Connection to Existing Metric Learning:** While related work is discussed, the connection to the broader metric learning landscape could be strengthened. Specifically, a more detailed discussion of how S-WMD differs from or builds upon existing methods like triplet loss or contrastive loss would be valuable.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Strong empirical results across multiple datasets.
* Novel approach to incorporating supervision into WMD.
* Technically sound and well-motivated optimization strategy.
* Clear writing and comprehensive experimentation.
* Addresses a fundamental problem in NLP.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Implementation complexity might limit adoption.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity is a potential concern.
* Limited error analysis.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of document distance learning. The proposed S-WMD method is both novel and effective, consistently outperforming a large number of baselines. While the implementation complexity and potential hyperparameter sensitivity are concerns, the benefits of improved accuracy and the clever use of the Sinkhorn distance outweigh these drawbacks.

**Recommendation:** **Strong Accept**

**Confidence:** 9/10
