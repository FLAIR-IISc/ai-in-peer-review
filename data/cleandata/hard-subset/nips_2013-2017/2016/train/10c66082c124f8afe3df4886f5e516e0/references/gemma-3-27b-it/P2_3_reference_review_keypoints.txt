- Presents S-WMD: a supervised method that adapts word embeddings and re-weights word frequencies to improve Word Mover’s Distance using Sinkhorn regularization for efficiency.
- Novelty: learning a metric within the embedding space and re-weighting words for WMD is a valuable and original contribution.
- Significance: consistently strong empirical gains across eight datasets, often outperforming unsupervised WMD and many baselines.
- Technical soundness: using Sinkhorn distance to approximate optimal transport and derived gradients appears correct and well-motivated.
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear background, algorithm, and loss explanations.
- Completeness: extensive experiments, ablation study, and training time reporting strengthen the evaluation.
- Implementation complexity: method relies on multiple components (Sinkhorn, gradient computation, batch optimization), making implementation nontrivial.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: many tunable hyperparameters (learning rates, batch size, neighbor size, Sinkhorn lambda) may require careful tuning; sensitivity analysis is lacking.
- Limited error analysis: evaluation focuses on kNN error rates without deeper analysis of when and why S-WMD succeeds or fails on specific document types.
- Connection to metric learning: discussion could better situate S-WMD relative to triplet/contrastive losses and other metric-learning approaches.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, novel supervised WMD approach, sound optimization, clear writing, and broad NLP relevance.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential adoption barriers due to complexity, hyperparameter concerns, and limited diagnostic analysis.
- Overall assessment: strong, effective contribution that substantially improves document distance learning despite some practical concerns.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
- Confidence: 9/10.
