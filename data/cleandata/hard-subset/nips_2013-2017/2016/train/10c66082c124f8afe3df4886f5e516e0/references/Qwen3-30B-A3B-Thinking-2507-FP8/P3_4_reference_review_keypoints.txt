- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-specific importance weights for supervised document distance.  
- Uses Sinkhorn approximation for efficient optimal transport computation and introduces Supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) for initialization.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art on 5 out of 8 datasets.  
- Correctly identifies the gap that WMD is unsupervised and motivates supervised metric learning for text.  
- Joint learning of embedding transformation and word weights is well-motivated and theoretically grounded.  
- Computational efficiency is strong via Sinkhorn, batch gradient descent, and nearest-neighbor filtering.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and diverse, with compelling t-SNE visualizations showing improved clustering.  
- Major weakness: lacks comparison to modern deep learning text classifiers (e.g., BERT, ELMo, transformers), weakening significance claims.  
- Major weakness: insufficiently differentiates from Wang et al. (2015); need to clarify added value of histogram re-weighting.  
- Unexplained methodological choice to use squared Euclidean distance (p=2) instead of Manhattan (p=1) used by WMD, without principled justification.  
- Minor weakness: no statistical significance testing (p-values or confidence intervals) reported.  
- Overall quality: methodology sound and well-executed; clarity and presentation are good.  
- Originality: novel combination of supervised metric learning with WMD but somewhat incremental relative to closely related prior work.  
- Significance: valuable to metric learning literature but limited by omission of comparisons to state-of-the-art neural methods; should be positioned as complementary to deep learning.  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance conditional on adding comparisons to at least two modern deep models, strengthening comparison to Wang et al., and justifying/exploring distance choice.
