- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD with learnable linear transform A (Mahalanobis ground cost) and word-importance vector w, trained via NCA-style stochastic LOO kNN loss using Sinkhorn OT.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn for fast differentiable distances and derives dual potentials from scaling vectors to compute gradients w.r.t. w.  
- Training accelerations: initialize A via S-WCD, use minibatching, and restrict NCA neighborhood to a WCD-based candidate set.  
- Experiments on 8 datasets vs. 26 baselines: S-WMD has best average rank, wins 5/8 datasets, releases code, and shows interpretable upweighted words and improved clustering.  
- Builds on WMD and Sinkhorn OT; novelty is supervised ground-metric learning plus histogram reweighting in the NLP/WMD setting.  
- Technical soundness: NCA objective and entropic OT use are appropriate; gradient w.r.t. A via regularized plan is standard; optimization is nonconvex but expected.  
- Efficiency: proposed accelerations are sensible and empirically effective.  
- Empirics: broad comparisons with tuning; reported gains are consistent and training times reasonable.  
- Missing details on constraints for w (must be nonnegative) and lack of discussion of regularization for A and w, risking negative weights or overfitting.  
- Neighborhood truncation (Na) is a practical heuristic whose effect and sensitivity to |Na| are not analyzed.  
- Unclear whether test-time kNN uses exact WMD with learned A,w or the Sinkhorn approximation; this affects accuracy and runtime reporting.  
- Notation issues and terse derivations: p is overloaded and some dual-formula details could be clarified.  
- Clarity: generally well-organized but some implementation choices, derivation details, and typos need fixing for reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel adaptation in NLP and a neat use of Sinkhorn duals to backpropagate through histogram reweighting.  
- Significance: advances supervised document distances for kNN and has wider applicability (retrieval, clustering, domain adaptation).  
- Requests: specify w constraints/regularization, value/rank r for A, test-time distance choice, sensitivity analyses for λ and |Na|, and ablation studies (A only, w only, baseline).  
- Pros for acceptance: well-motivated, elegant technical execution, strong empirical results, interpretable weights, and released code.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing constraint/regularization details, reliance on heuristic neighborhood truncation without sensitivity study, notation/clarity issues, and evaluation limited to kNN.  
- Minor comments: unify p notation, fix section/header and reference formatting, report k selection/BO tuning, and consider L1 regularization on w for sparsity.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely contribution; recommend accept contingent on addressing clarifications and adding small ablations/sensitivity analyses.
