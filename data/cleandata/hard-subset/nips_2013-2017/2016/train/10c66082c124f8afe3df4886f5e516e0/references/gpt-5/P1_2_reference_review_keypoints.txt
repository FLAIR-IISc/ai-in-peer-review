- Proposes S-WMD: learn linear transform A of word embeddings and nonuniform importance vector w, trained via NCA kNN loss with Sinkhorn (entropy-regularized OT) and initialized/accelerated by S-WCD and neighbor pruning.  
- Well-situated relative to WMD, metric learning, and entropic OT; novelty in jointly learning A and w for text embeddings.  
- Uses Sinkhorn relaxation to obtain differentiable, efficient approximations for WMD-like distances and gradients.  
- Gradient w.r.t. A uses current transport plan; gradients w.r.t. w use relaxed dual variables.  
- Broad empirical study: 8 datasets, outperforms 26 baselines on average and is best on 5/8 datasets; training-time measurements and code released.  
- Must clarify positivity/constraints on w (w ≥ 0) and how they enforce it (e.g., softplus, projection) and whether w is regularized to avoid overfitting.  
- Ambiguity whether the NCA probabilities use the regularized Dλ or the unregularized D and whether gradients are biased—needs consistency and explicit statement.  
- Dual potentials signs/constants appear inconsistent with standard entropic OT notation; request derivation or citation to resolve sign/constant issues.  
- Complexity claim O(d^2(p+r)) for assembling ∂/∂A may be pessimistic; suggest noting algebraic/implementation tricks to reduce cost.  
- Missing ablations: A-only vs. w-only variants, sensitivity to λ, embedding choice, and impact of WCD pruning parameter N on speed and accuracy.  
- Fairness of baselines: supervised baselines run after PCA to 200 dims—confirm tuning, embedding usage, and include simple supervised baselines on averaged embeddings (e.g., logistic regression/SVM).  
- Evaluation limited to kNN classification; recommend exploring retrieval/clustering metrics or adding a discussion of broader applicability.  
- Notational inconsistencies (p/q/n) and missing domain/constraint statements; minor typos and reference/formatting issues to fix.  
- Training details (learning rates, batch size, stopping criteria, neighborhood pruning procedure) need to be fully specified for reproducibility.  
- Originality: combination of supervised ground-metric learning, joint histogram reweighting, and Sinkhorn-based NCA for text is novel and nontrivial.  
- Significance: meaningful empirical gains on WMD, practical efficiency, modularity across embeddings, and code release increase impact.  
- Pros: principled formulation, efficient Sinkhorn-based training, strong empirical results, interpretability via learned w, and available code.  
- Cons: unclear constraints/regularization on w, ambiguity about regularized vs. unregularized objective and dual signs, limited sensitivity/ablation studies, and some clarity issues.  
- Suggestions: explicitly enforce w ≥ 0 (e.g., w = softplus(z)), add regularization for A and w, align/clarify objective vs. gradients and provide dual derivation or citation, add sensitivity analyses and A-only/w-only ablations, and include averaged-embedding supervised baselines.  
- Recommendation: reviewer leans toward acceptance, conditional on addressing technical clarifications and adding small ablations/sensitivity analyses.
