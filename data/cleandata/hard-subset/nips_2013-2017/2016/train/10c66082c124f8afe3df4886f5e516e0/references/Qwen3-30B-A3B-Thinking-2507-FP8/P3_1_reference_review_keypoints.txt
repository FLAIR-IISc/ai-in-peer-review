- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, learning a linear embedding transform (A) and word importance weights (w) within a Sinkhorn-approximated WMD framework for supervised document classification.

- Technical soundness: Method grounded in optimal transport and metric learning; Sinkhorn approximation is a practical choice.

- Empirical evaluation: Strong results—outperforms 26 baselines on 8 datasets in kNN classification.

- Implementation details: Clear description of initialization (S-WCD) and batch gradient descent.

- Computational approach: Correctly addresses WMD's cubic complexity via Sinkhorn to enable supervised learning.

- Lack of theoretical guarantees: No analysis of convergence or formal relationship between Sinkhorn relaxation and true WMD.

- Limited ablation study: Missing experiments isolating effects of A versus w to determine each component's contribution.

- Overfitting concerns: No discussion or evidence addressing potential overfitting from learning extra parameters on small datasets.

- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Uses λ=10 for Sinkhorn without exploring sensitivity or justification.

- Comparison with contextualized embeddings: Relies on word2vec and does not evaluate or discuss integration with BERT-like models.

- Clarity and organization: Paper is well-organized with clear mathematical notation and effective visualizations (t-SNE, word weights).

- Ambiguous notation: Uses d̃ for re-weighted document vectors without sufficient clarification versus normalized BOW.

- Initialization explanation: Insufficient rationale for using NCA on WCD as the initialization strategy.

- Missing implementation detail: Lacks pseudocode for gradient computations to ease reproducibility.

- Originality: Novelly combines metric learning and WMD with re-weighted histograms; Sinkhorn use is appropriate.

- Related-work differentiation: Insufficiently distinguishes from Wang et al. [35] and omits recent supervised embedding/EMD literature.

- Significance—practical value: Demonstrates clear empirical and computational benefits suitable for real-world tasks.

- Significance—limitations: Incremental advance over WMD, limited scope (word embedding space) and no broader-domain discussion.

- Recommendations—ablation: Add experiments isolating A vs. w contributions.

- Recommendations—theory: Provide analysis on Sinkhorn vs. true WMD and optimization convergence.

- Recommendations—hyperparameters: Study sensitivity to λ and other training hyperparameters.

- Recommendations—contextual embeddings: Evaluate or discuss integration with BERT and other contextual models.

- Recommendations—overfitting: Address and evaluate overfitting via train/validation comparisons or regularization.

- Recommendations—related work: Expand discussion to include recent supervised EMD and Wasserstein-learning papers.

- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated paper with solid empirical gains; minor revisions advised.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
