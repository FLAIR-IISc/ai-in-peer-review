Summary
The paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised metric for document comparison that builds on the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD). The method learns (i) a linear transform A applied to pre-trained word embeddings and (ii) a word-importance vector w that reweights document histograms. Training minimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification loss, following the NCA formulation, with distances computed by optimal transport. To make optimization practical, the paper uses the entropically-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) as a surrogate to compute transports T*, and derives gradients w.r.t. A and w using T* and (approximate) dual potentials from Sinkhorn scaling variables u, v. It further accelerates training with an initialization from supervised word centroid distance (S-WCD) via NCA on document centroids, mini-batching, and neighbor-list truncation based on WCD. Experiments on 8 datasets against 26 baselines report consistent gains in kNN error, visual improvements in t-SNE plots, and modest training times; code is provided.

Relation to prior work
The work is closely related to WMD (Kusner et al.), NCA, entropic OT/Sinkhorn (Cuturi), and supervised EMD ground-metric learning in vision (e.g., Cuturi & Avis; Wang & Guibas; Wang et al. on ITML for EMD). The novelty claimed is (a) learning both a linear ground metric and histogram reweighting jointly for text, (b) training via an NCA-style objective, and (c) practical gradient computation via Sinkhorn.

Strengths
- Addresses an important and active area (OT-based text distances) with a principled supervised extension.
- Sensible design: learn a task-specific ground metric A plus word weights w; uses pre-trained embeddings and retains OT interpretability.
- Practical training via entropic OT and stochastic neighbor truncation; initialization via S-WCD is reasonable.
- Broad empirical comparisons across many baselines and datasets, with apparently strong results; code release.

Weaknesses and detailed issues
Methodological and theoretical:
1) Objective/gradient mismatch: The loss uses DA,w from the unregularized WMD (Eq. 5), but gradients are computed by substituting the entropic solution T*λ (and λ-derived duals). This is an uncontrolled surrogate unless the training objective is explicitly switched to the entropic OT distance. Please state the exact training objective, justify the surrogate by Danskin/envelope theorems for the entropic case, and provide an ablation over λ (including λ→∞ vs. finite λ) and its effect on accuracy and convergence.

2) Constraints on w: d̃a must be a probability vector (nonnegative, sums to 1). The paper does not constrain w≥0. Negative entries would break feasibility of the transport constraints. Enforce positivity (e.g., w=exp(s) or projected gradient) and report whether such constraints were used.

3) Scale/pathologies in A and w: The NCA loss with exp(−D) encourages scaling A to large norms; similarly, joint scaling of A and w can lead to degenerate behavior. No regularization on A (e.g., Frobenius penalty) or explicit constraint (e.g., spectral norm bound) is described; r (the output dimension) is also not specified. Please clarify regularization, r, and whether early stopping alone suffices.

4) Dual potentials from Sinkhorn: The formulas α*λ=log(u)/λ−(log(u)^T1)/p·1 and similarly for β require a derivation and correct dimensions. The paper mixes symbols p (norm exponent), p (document length), and q (unique words) inconsistently. Provide a clear derivation or citation for recovering duals from u, v, fix the notation, and state the constant-offset handling precisely.

5) Ignoring ∂T*/∂A: The gradient w.r.t. A (Eq. 8) uses T* as a fixed weighting. This is correct under Danskin if the objective is the entropic OT with unique optimum. As written, the paper claims this for the unregularized problem and then “replaces” Tab by T*λ; the current justification is insufficient.

6) Neighbor truncation bias: Approximating the NCA neighborhood by the top-N under WCD introduces bias in the gradient. No analysis or sensitivity study for N is provided. Please quantify how N affects accuracy and training time; report whether this changes the final ranking.

7) Complexity: The complexity uses O(p^2) for Sinkhorn but omits the number of Sinkhorn iterations; the d^2(p+r) term is unexplained. Clarify the per-iteration costs, number of Sinkhorn iterations, and memory complexity. Unify notation for p/q/n (vocab size vs. unique words per document vs. vector dimension).

Experimental concerns:
8) Missing critical details: k in kNN, r (A’s output dim), λ, batch size, learning rates, number of iterations/epochs, convergence criteria, regularization strength(s), and preprocessing (tokenization, stopwords, OOV handling) are not specified. Without these, reproducibility is compromised.

9) Hyperparameter tuning: BO is used, but it is unclear whether validation is strictly on training folds (no test leakage). Please specify the validation protocol and number of BO evaluations.

10) Transductive LDA: Training LDA on combined train+test is not comparable to strictly inductive methods and may inflate LDA performance. Either avoid transductive baselines or allow transductive variants for all methods (including S-WMD via vocabulary building), and discuss fairness.

11) Alternative baselines: Missing comparisons to strong supervised baselines for text at the time (e.g., fastText, CNN/RNN classifiers), and to supervised EMD ground-metric learning adapted to text (e.g., Wang et al. [35]). Also, ablations on w (A-only, w-only) are important to isolate contributions.

12) Reported results: The paper references tables/figures (e.g., Table 2/3, Fig. 1/2) but key numbers are not visible here. Claims of “state-of-the-art” require statistical tests and full results; please include standard deviations and significance.

Presentation/formatting:
13) Numerous typos and inconsistencies: “th Conference …”, “[5, 21?]”, “.2 Optimization”, footnote URLs inline, inconsistent use of p/q, undefined symbols (p in 1p), missing years/pages in many references, grammar issues. These hinder clarity.

14) Notation: p is used for the norm exponent, the number of unique words, and the vector length in 1p; q is also used for unique words earlier. Unify symbols throughout.

15) Figures/links: Placeholder SVG link and footnote URLs appear in text; ensure proper placement and that all assets render in the camera-ready.

Pros
- Conceptually appealing supervised extension of WMD with joint ground-metric and word-weight learning.
- Practical training via entropic OT; sensible initialization and batching heuristics.
- Broad empirical comparison; code release.

Cons
- Objective/gradient inconsistency and insufficient theoretical justification for the surrogate.
- Missing constraints/regularization on w and A; potential degeneracies.
- Important experimental and preprocessing details omitted; some baselines unfair (transductive LDA).
- Numerous notation/formatting errors; clarity suffers.

Recommendation: Weak reject. The idea is promising and potentially impactful, but the current manuscript has too many methodological ambiguities, missing constraints, and presentation issues to meet the standard. Addressing the objective-consistency, constraints/regularization, experimental rigor, and clarity could make this a strong submission. Confidence: 4/5.
