

# Review of "Supervised Word Mover's Distance"

## Summary
This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), an extension of the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance (WMD) that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights to improve document classification. The authors claim S-WMD outperforms 26 baselines across 8 datasets, with improvements over WMD on 5/8 datasets.

## Strengths
The paper addresses an important gap in the literature by incorporating supervision into the WMD framework. The proposed method is well-motivated and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering multiple document classification tasks. The use of Sinkhorn distance for computational efficiency is appropriate, and the initialization strategy using NCA with WCD is clever.

## Weaknesses

### Formatting and Notation Issues
1. The paper contains numerous inconsistent mathematical notations (e.g., "d̃a" instead of "d̃_a", "d̃b" instead of "d̃_b", inconsistent use of subscripts).
2. Equation (4) is mathematically flawed: "d̃a = (w ◦ da)/(w>da)" should be "d̃a = (w ◦ da) / (w^T da)" to properly represent scalar division.
3. The paper states "the WCD scales asymptotically by O(dq)" (Section 2), but this is incorrect - the WCD computation scales as O(dq) for centroid calculation but O(d) for distance computation, making the total complexity O(dq).
4. The reference to [26] for WMD complexity (O(q^3 log q)) is inaccurate; [26] is a paper on "Fast and robust earth mover's distances" which does not discuss WMD complexity. The actual complexity is O(q^3).

### Technical Inaccuracies
1. The paper states "Word embeddings can learn hundreds of millions of parameters" (Section 2). This is misleading - word embeddings themselves are vectors (d×n), not parameters. The model may have hundreds of millions of parameters, but the embedding is the resulting vector representation.
2. The WCD is incorrectly defined as "‖Xd−Xd′‖2" (Section 2). The correct definition is ‖X da - X db‖_2, where da and db are BOW vectors.
3. The paper claims "the WCD always lower bounds the WMD" but fails to cite the original WMD paper [19] that makes this claim.
4. Equation (8) is missing a transpose: "∂/∂A DA,w(da,db) = 2A ∑_{i,j} Tabij (xi-xj)(xi-xj)" should be "2A ∑_{i,j} Tabij (xi-xj)(xi-xj)^T".
5. The paper cites Wang & Guibas [34] as [35] in the Related Work section (reference number mismatch).

### Methodological Concerns
1. The paper states "we use λ = 10, which leads to a nice trade-off between speed and approximation accuracy" without providing any empirical evidence or comparison with alternative λ values.
2. The weight learning in equation (4) normalizes the vector (w ◦ da)/(w^T da), which is not standard for weight learning. The standard approach would be to use w ◦ da without normalization.
3. The paper claims "S-WMD outperforms all other 26 methods" on average but fails to provide statistical significance tests for these comparisons.
4. The paper states "the vast majority of NCA probabilities pab near zero" but provides no evidence for this claim.

### Experimental Concerns
1. The paper claims "S-WMD achieves the lowest kNN test error on 5/8 datasets" but the table doesn't clearly show statistical significance.
2. The results for S-WCD and S-WMD init. are not clearly distinguished in the results section.
3. The paper states "On these datasets it achieves the 4th lowest on BBCSPORT and CLASSIC" but doesn't provide the actual error rates for comparison.

## Recommendation
While the paper presents a novel extension to WMD with promising experimental results, the numerous technical inaccuracies, inconsistent notation, and unverified claims significantly undermine the paper's credibility. The formatting issues and mathematical errors suggest a lack of careful proofreading that would be unacceptable for a NIPS submission. 

The paper's claims about the WCD complexity, WMD complexity, and mathematical formulations are factually incorrect, and these errors are too numerous to be merely typographical. The lack of statistical significance tests for the main claims further weakens the paper's contribution.

Although the core idea of supervised WMD is interesting and the experimental results are generally positive, the paper's technical flaws are too substantial to recommend acceptance. The authors should carefully revise the mathematical formulations, fix the citations, and provide proper statistical validation before resubmission.

I recommend rejection due to the substantial number of minor but significant technical flaws that compromise the paper's technical soundness.
