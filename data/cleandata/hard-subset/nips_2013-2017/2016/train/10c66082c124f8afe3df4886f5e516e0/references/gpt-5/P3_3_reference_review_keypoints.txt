- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised WMD variant that learns a linear transform A and global nonnegative word-importance vector w to reweight document histograms.  
- Training: Uses a Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA)-style stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss with entropically regularized Sinkhorn OT for tractable gradients.  
- Optimization detail: Derives gradients via Sinkhorn primal/dual (u,v) and envelope-theorem-style reasoning; accelerates training with minibatching and WCD-based neighbor filtering.  
- Empirics: Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, often achieving best average rank and state-of-the-art performance.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD, entropic OT, and NCA; novelty is jointly learning embedding metric and word weights for WMD with practical Sinkhorn-based training.  
- Strength — motivation: Well-motivated to adapt WMD for supervised tasks by learning ground metric and word importance.  
- Strength — methodology: Appropriate use of entropic OT and dual potentials to obtain efficient, computationally feasible gradients.  
- Strength — engineering: Practical solutions (WCD neighbor filtering, minibatching) mitigate OT’s quadratic/cubic costs.  
- Strength — empirical breadth: Wide dataset and baseline coverage strengthen claims.  
- Strength — interpretability: Learned word weights are interpretable and sensible.  
- Weakness — training vs. evaluation distance: Unclear whether training and test use exact WMD or Sinkhorn; need clarity and sensitivity to λ.  
- Weakness — constraints/regularization: Paper omits explicit constraints on w (must be ≥0) and regularization/penalties on A and w; risk of non-simplex histograms and overfitting.  
- Weakness — dual-potentials sign: Apparent sign/error in dual-potential expressions from Sinkhorn scaling; needs verification/correction.  
- Weakness — NCA approximation: Using restricted neighbor set Na is a nontrivial approximation; impact on results should be quantified by varying |Na|.  
- Weakness — ground cost choice: Uses squared Euclidean cost though standard WMD uses unsquared; ablation comparing squared vs. unsquared is needed.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters and initialization: Missing details on transform dimension r, its selection, initialization, learning rates, batch sizes, stopping criteria, and other reproducibility info.  
- Weakness — baselines: Could add standard supervised classifiers (e.g., linear SVM/logistic on TF-IDF) to contextualize utility beyond kNN-based methods.  
- Theoretical note: Should explicitly state gradients/updates are taken w.r.t. entropic OT and discuss λ→∞ limit approximating exact WMD and uniqueness/smoothness assumptions.  
- Reproducibility: Need clear k in kNN, validation protocols for hyperparameters (including λ), early stopping criteria, and full hyperparameter tables.  
- Suggestions: Enforce w≥0 (e.g., exponential parameterization), add regularization for A and w, report sensitivity to λ, |Na|, r, compare squared/unsquared costs, include SVM baseline, and expand supplementary details.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, well-motivated, and empirically strong submission but requires clarifications, derivation fixes, and additional ablations for full confidence.
