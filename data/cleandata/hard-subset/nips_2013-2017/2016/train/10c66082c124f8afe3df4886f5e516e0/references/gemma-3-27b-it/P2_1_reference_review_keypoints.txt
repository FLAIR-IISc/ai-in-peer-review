- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, learning a linear embedding transform and word-frequency reweighting for WMD, using Sinkhorn approximation; shows strong results on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance with notable improvements across diverse datasets.  
- Strength: Novel joint learning of embedding transformation and word reweighting within the WMD framework.  
- Strength: Uses Sinkhorn distance for an efficient, practical approximation of supervised WMD.  
- Strength: Clear presentation and public code; well-organized background and algorithm description.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation and useful ablation studies.  
- Weakness: Implementation is complex and may require significant engineering/optimization expertise.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity is not deeply analyzed or reported.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis and convergence guarantees for the method.  
- Weakness: Insufficiently thorough discussion of failure cases and why performance drops on some datasets.  
- Qualitative — Quality: 4/5 — technically sound but would benefit from more theory.  
- Qualitative — Clarity: 4/5 — generally well-written; notation could be streamlined.  
- Qualitative — Originality: 4.5/5 — novel combination of techniques.  
- Qualitative — Significance: 4.5/5 — impactful empirical results and practical approximation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong empirical results, novel/motivated method, practical Sinkhorn approximation, comprehensive baselines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Adoption limited by implementation complexity, limited theory, and brief hyperparameter/failure-case analysis.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with valuable contribution; weaknesses are minor relative to empirical gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
