- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised WMD variant learning a linear transform A (Mahalanobis metric) and nonnegative word-importance weights w for BOW histograms.  
- Uses an NCA-style loss approximating leave-one-out k-NN error with exp(−D) where D is the OT-based document distance.  
- Replaces exact WMD with entropic Sinkhorn relaxation for tractable learning.  
- Computes gradients w.r.t. A via Danskin’s theorem using the optimal transport plan, and w via dual Sinkhorn potentials.  
- Accelerates training with WCD pretraining, mini-batching, and neighborhood truncation using WCD.  
- Empirical evaluation across 8 datasets and 26 baselines; best average rank and state-of-the-art k-NN error on 5/8 datasets.  
- Builds on WMD and Sinkhorn OT literature and applies NCA-style supervised metric learning to text, novelizing joint ground-metric and histogram reweighting.  
- Keeps documents as sparse BOW while learning in low-dimensional embedding space, complementing representation learning methods.  
- Technical soundness: entropic OT yields unique solutions and gradients are principled under the relaxation.  
- Practical efficiency: Sinkhorn reduces per-pair complexity and heuristics make training seconds per iteration.  
- Clear algorithmic contribution: jointly learning A and w with a coherent NCA objective; histogram reweighting adapts marginals to the task.  
- Strong empirical breadth with many baselines and sensible ablations; code released and hyperparameters tuned via Bayesian optimization.  
- Missing clarity that the Sinkhorn kernel K must be recomputed as A changes (K = exp(−λ‖A(xi−xj)‖^2)); paper does not explicitly state this.  
- No stated constraints or parameterization for w (needs w ≥ 0); paper omits projection/regularization details to prevent upweighting rare words.  
- Omits numerical/stability details for entropic OT (log-domain stabilization, ε-additions) and lacks precise per-iteration complexity derivation (e.g., why d^2 term).  
- Evaluation limited to k-NN; lacks downstream tasks (retrieval, kernel classifiers) or comparisons to supervised text classifiers (fastText/doc2vec/CNN).  
- Notational and clarity issues: typos, inconsistent symbols for vocabulary/document support, and some imprecise formulas/citations.  
- Quality: technically solid objective and approximations; implementation and constraint details need clarification.  
- Clarity: generally readable but would benefit from tightened notation, explicit constraints, numerical details, and a training-loop figure.  
- Originality: novel combination of NCA supervision with OT over word embeddings and joint learning of metric and marginal reweighting for NLP.  
- Significance: useful for task-specific distances in short/medium texts, k-NN classification, and retrieval; likely to inspire supervised OT work in NLP.  
- Key questions for authors: do you recompute K each step and how efficiently; how enforce w ≥ 0 and regularize; sensitivity to λ, N, B; log-domain stabilization and stopping criteria; effect of A’s rank r.  
- Pros: principled Sinkhorn optimization, joint A and w learning with empirical gains, good initialization/accelerations, code released.  
- Cons: unclear dependence of Sinkhorn on A and constraints for w, limited numerical/hyperparameter discussion, evaluation confined to k-NN.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept, pending clarifications and fixes to implementation/notation details.
