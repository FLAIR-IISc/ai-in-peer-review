- Proposes S-WMD: supervised learning of a linear transform and word importance weights to improve Word Mover’s Distance for document classification.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD for computational efficiency, enabling practical training.  
- Provides clear mathematical formulation, loss, gradients, and an implementable optimization algorithm.  
- Experimental evaluation across 8 datasets and 26 baselines shows consistent improvements and state-of-the-art results on 5/8 datasets.  
- Implementation details and training-time measurements indicate the method is practically efficient.  
- Writing, background, and algorithm description are exceptionally clear and accessible to ML/NLP readers.  
- Novel contribution: supervised metric learning applied to WMD with joint learning of embedding transform and histogram re-weighting.  
- Combining Sinkhorn approximation with supervised learning is a novel and effective application of existing techniques.  
- Significant impact on text classification tasks, outperforming baselines on average and offering interpretable word-weight visualizations.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation addressing WMD’s computational bottleneck via Sinkhorn.  
- Strength: comprehensive, statistically sound evaluation with diverse datasets and many baselines.  
- Strength: practical efficiency demonstrated in training-time comparisons.  
- Strength: clear presentation and reproducible algorithmic details.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC) beyond brief hypotheses.  
- Weakness: insufficient in-depth analysis of learned word weights and their relation to class labels.  
- Weakness: comparisons to other metric learning methods are indirect, limiting isolation of S-WMD’s contribution.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — a technically sound, novel, and practically useful contribution with minor analysis gaps.
