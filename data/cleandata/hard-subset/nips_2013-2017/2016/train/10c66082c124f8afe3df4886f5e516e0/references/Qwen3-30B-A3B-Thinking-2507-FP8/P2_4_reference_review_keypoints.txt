- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transform and word weights to make WMD align with class labels for better document classification.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance approximation to enable efficient gradient computation and practical optimization.  
- Evaluated on 8 diverse datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art on 5 datasets and large relative error reductions (e.g., up to 51% on OHSUMED).  
- Demonstrates particularly strong gains on TWITTER, CLASSIC, and REUTERS compared to TF-IDF+NCA and other baselines.  
- Builds on WMD and metric learning literature by applying metric learning directly within the optimal transport framework.  
- Theoretical formulation is sound and well-justified with correct use of optimal transport and metric learning concepts.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, reports kNN error rates with standard errors, and includes visualizations (t-SNE, word weights).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides reproducibility details (pseudo-code, parameters, and code availability).  
- Novelty lies in learning the ground metric/word re-weighting inside WMD and leveraging Sinkhorn for scalability.  
- Significant practical impact: better classification performance and computational efficiency comparable to metric learning baselines.  
- Strengths: strong motivation, solid theory, broad experiments, computational efficiency, clear presentation, and practical relevance.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis for underperforming datasets (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON), ad-hoc initialization using NCA on WCD, lacks comparisons to newer supervised text representation methods, and little discussion on very long-document or low-resource scenarios.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, meaningful contribution that meets NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and is recommended for acceptance.
