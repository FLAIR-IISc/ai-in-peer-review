- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, which learns a linear transform A on word embeddings and a nonnegative word-importance vector w, using an NCA-style supervised kNN loss with OT distances approximated by Sinkhorn.  
- Implementation detail: Uses Sinkhorn to get transport plans and dual potentials for efficient O(q^2) per-pair computation, initializes A via WCD NCA, and uses neighbor filtering and batching.  
- Empirical claim: Evaluated on 8 datasets vs. 26 baselines, achieves best average rank and top accuracy on 5/8 datasets; code released.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD and entropy-regularized OT, uses NCA as objective, related to ground-metric learning in vision but novel for word-embedding space and joint w learning.  
- Advantage over classic text reps: Learns in low-dimensional embedding space, avoiding quadratic scaling with vocabulary size.  
- Strength — motivation: Clear goal to inject supervision into unsupervised word embeddings via ground-metric and word importance learning.  
- Strength — technical soundness: Principled use of Sinkhorn and dual potentials; envelope theorem justifies gradients under unique solutions.  
- Strength — practicality: Efficient training with batch sampling, neighbor filtering, strong initialization; competitive training times reported.  
- Strength — empirical and qualitative analysis: Strong results across datasets and insightful qualitative analyses (t-SNE, top words by w).  
- Notation/clarity issue: Overloading of symbol p (Lp norm exponent vs. vocabulary/word-count) is confusing and needs disambiguation.  
- Notation/clarity issue: Derivation of dual potentials α*, β* from Sinkhorn u, v subtracts a mean term without precise dimensionality or rationale for centering.  
- Presentation issue: Section numbering glitches, incomplete references (e.g., [21?]), and inconsistent symbols (M vs N for neighbor size).  
- Theoretical concern: Loss uses unregularized distance while gradients use Sinkhorn-regularized solution — need clarification on whether training/evaluation use same distance and approximation error.  
- Complexity concern: Complexity claim for gradient computation seems incorrect — computing ∑ij Tij(xi−xj)(xi−xj)T is O(q^2 d^2), not O(d^2(p+r)); explain exploited low-rank structure.  
- Regularization/constraints missing: Constraints on w (nonnegativity, normalization) and safeguards against degenerate solutions (e.g., zeroing words) are not specified.  
- Regularization/architecture details missing: Rank/regularization choices for A (choice of r, penalties) and sensitivity/ablation studies are absent.  
- Experimental fairness issue: LDA trained transductively on train+test vocabulary—risk of leakage; recommend training LDA only on training data.  
- Hyperparameter tuning concern: Baselines tuned with BO but S-WMD’s hyperparameter tuning protocol (λ, B, N, etc.) is unclear; sensitivity plots requested.  
- Missing ablations: No A-only / w-only / both ablations, no test of squared vs. unsquared ground costs, and no analysis of WCD neighbor filter budget effects.  
- Baselines scope concern: Important supervised baselines (linear SVM/logistic over TF-IDF, fastText) are missing; clarify focus on metric learning for kNN if applicable.  
- Practical reporting omissions: k for kNN not reported; sensitivity to k, λ, rank r, neighbor budget N not provided.  
- Sinkhorn runtime detail missing: Report number of Sinkhorn iterations and stopping tolerance since runtime depends on these.  
- Questions for authors: Are w constrained nonnegative or regularized? How avoid degenerate zeroing? Do train and eval use same regularized distance? Reconcile gradient complexity; report k and sensitivities; contribution of A vs. w?  
- Suggestions: Add ablations (A-only, w-only), enforce fair LDA training, include strong supervised baselines, clean notation/references, clarify dual variables, and report Sinkhorn iterations and runtime/accuracy trade-offs.  
- Minor issues: Fix typos, incomplete citations, section numbering, and define all symbols at first use (avoid overloading p).  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid, well-motivated, efficient and empirically strong; with clarified theory, ablations, and fairness fixes, suitable for acceptance.
