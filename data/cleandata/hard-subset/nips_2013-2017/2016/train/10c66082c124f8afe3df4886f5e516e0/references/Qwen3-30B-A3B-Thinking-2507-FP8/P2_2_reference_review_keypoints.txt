- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transform of embeddings and word importance weights to make WMD task-supervised.  
- Frames S-WMD as metric learning solved efficiently using the Sinkhorn (entropic OT) relaxation.  
- Demonstrates S-WMD outperforms 26 baselines across 8 datasets, achieving lowest test error on 5/8 and best average performance.  
- Technically sound with correct gradient derivations and justified use of Sinkhorn for efficiency.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across diverse datasets and many baselines; code released for reproducibility.  
- Clear, well-organized writing and consistent notation; effective visualizations (t-SNE, learned weights).  
- Novel contribution: first to incorporate supervision into WMD and introduce learnable word importance weights.  
- Significant empirical gains and practical computational efficiency, making the method applicable to real tasks.  
- Limited analysis of failure cases (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON); reasons are suggested but not systematically explored.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental relative to existing WMD and metric learning literature; stronger distinction from deep metric learning could help.  
- Uses only word2vec embeddings; no experiments with other embeddings (GloVe, ELMo, BERT) to test robustness.  
- Limited discussion and ablation of key hyperparameters (Sinkhorn λ, batch size, neighbor set size).  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong, well-motivated, and empirically effective work despite minor weaknesses.  
- Suggested improvements: deeper failure-case analysis, experiments with other embeddings, fuller hyperparameter study, and discussion of integration with end-to-end deep learning.
