- Proposes S-WMD: learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-specific weights to improve WMD, using Sinkhorn relaxation and probabilistic filtering; achieves state-of-the-art on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines.  
- Builds on WMD and metric learning, but is novel in operating directly on word embeddings and jointly learning embedding transforms and word importance; relates to prior ground-distance learning work.  
- Strength: Strong empirical performance across diverse datasets with extensive baseline comparisons.  
- Strength: Novel combination of linear metric learning and re-weighted word frequencies within the WMD framework.  
- Strength: Computational efficiency achieved via Sinkhorn approximation, enabling scaling to reasonably sized datasets.  
- Strength: Clear presentation, detailed mathematical derivations, and well-explained optimization.  
- Strength: Thorough experimentation and analysis including t-SNE visualizations, learned weight inspection, and ablation studies.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity not deeply analyzed despite Bayesian optimization usage.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis of the learned metric and convergence guarantees.  
- Weakness: Core quadratic complexity in vocabulary size remains a limitation despite Sinkhorn mitigation.  
- Weakness: Dependency on quality of pre-trained embeddings with limited analysis of different embedding types.  
- Weakness: S-WMD init.’s strong performance raises questions about the marginal benefit of full training in some cases.  
- For acceptance: Novel, effective method with strong empirical results, efficient implementation, and clear presentation.  
- Against acceptance: Limited theory, insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and potential scalability issues.  
- Overall: Strong paper with significant contributions to document similarity; reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Confidence: 8/10.
