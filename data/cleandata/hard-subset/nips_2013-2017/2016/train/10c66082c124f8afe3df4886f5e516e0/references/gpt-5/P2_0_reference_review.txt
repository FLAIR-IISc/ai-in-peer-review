Summary
The paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) for document similarity. Rather than learning a document representation, S-WMD learns to (i) linearly transform the pre-trained word embedding space (A) and (ii) reweight words via a multiplicative importance vector (w), and uses this supervised ground cost in the optimal transport between document histograms. The learning objective is a stochastic leave-one-out kNN criterion in the spirit of Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA). Because exact WMD is cubic in the number of unique words per document, the paper adopts the entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) relaxation to obtain fast, differentiable approximations: the transport plan T*λ for the gradient w.r.t. A, and the dual potentials (α, β) from the Sinkhorn scaling vectors (u, v) for the gradient w.r.t. w. Additional speedups include NCA neighbor pruning using the cheap Word Centroid Distance (WCD) and minibatching, with initialization via an NCA-trained supervised WCD (S-WCD). Across 8 datasets and many baselines (unsupervised representations and their supervised metric-learning variants), S-WMD achieves the best average rank and the best or near-best accuracy on most datasets.

Relation to prior work: The work builds on WMD (Kusner et al., ICML’15) and Sinkhorn distances (Cuturi, NIPS’13), and is inspired by NCA (Goldberger et al., NIPS’04). It also relates to learning ground metrics for EMD/OT in vision (e.g., Wang & Guibas; Cuturi & Avis), but tailors the learning to low-dimensional word embedding space and adds word-importance reweighting, which is novel in this context.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The formulation is reasonable and consistent with the literature. Using ||A(xi−xj)||^2 as ground cost ensures PSD via A^T A. The derivations of gradients are standard for entropic OT: ∂/∂A uses the (approximate) optimal transport plan; ∂/∂w uses dual potentials obtainable from Sinkhorn’s scaling. The learning objective is a well-established NCA-style stochastic kNN loss.
- Approximations: The main caveat is a mismatch between the unregularized distance in the objective and the use of entropically regularized solutions for gradients; the paper effectively optimizes a smoothed surrogate. This is practical, but theoretical guarantees (e.g., consistency as λ→∞, convergence properties, or bias introduced by finite λ) are not discussed.
- Empirical support: Experiments cover 8 datasets with a wide set of baselines (including unsupervised and supervised/metric-learning variants). Hyperparameters are tuned with Bayesian optimization. S-WMD wins on 5/8 datasets and is competitive on the rest. Training times are reported to be reasonable. However, ablations are limited: the paper would benefit from isolating the contributions of A vs. w, sensitivity to λ, neighbor set size N, and the effect of initialization.

Clarity
- The paper is generally clear and well-organized: background on WMD, Sinkhorn, and NCA; method; gradient computations; optimization; experiments; related work. The intuition for learning in word space instead of document space is well-motivated.
- Some presentation issues should be fixed: a missing section number (“.2 Optimization”), a few incomplete references and typos, and a brief, somewhat opaque derivation of α*, β* from u, v (a short derivation or explicit citation would help). Constraints on w (nonnegativity) and any regularization are not clearly stated.
- Reproducibility is helped by released code, but more implementation detail (e.g., exact λ, stopping criteria, constraints on w, regularization on A/w) would further aid replication.

Originality
- The combination is novel for NLP/document distances: learning an OT ground metric directly in pre-trained embedding space together with per-word reweighting under a supervised NCA objective, trained efficiently via Sinkhorn. Prior supervised-EMD works in vision learn ground costs but do not (to my knowledge) combine with word-importance learning nor exploit the low-dimensional embedding to sidestep high-dimensional document metrics. The initialization via supervised WCD is a neat, pragmatic contribution.

Significance
- Practical impact: A supervised distance that outperforms strong baselines on kNN classification and is applicable to retrieval/ranking has practical utility. The approach is modular (can use any pre-trained embeddings) and efficient enough for mid-scale datasets.
- Scientific impact: The paper pushes forward supervised OT in NLP by tying Sinkhorn differentiation with a kNN objective and by showing clear empirical gains over many alternatives. The work is likely to be adopted and extended (e.g., to other downstream tasks or with contextual embeddings).
- Limitations: The evaluation focuses on kNN; comparisons to modern supervised classifiers (e.g., CNN/RNN at the time) or to end-to-end metric learning with deep encoders are absent. Scalability to very long documents or very large corpora may still be challenging (O(p^2) per pair), although the neighbor pruning helps.

Pros
- Clear, well-motivated problem; aligns with WMD’s strengths while adding task supervision.
- Sound use of entropic OT to obtain efficient, differentiable training.
- Joint learning of ground metric and word importance is novel and intuitive.
- Strong empirical performance across multiple datasets and a large suite of baselines.
- Practical initialization and training heuristics; code released.

Cons
- Theoretical analysis of the surrogate/approximation is limited; no guarantees or sensitivity analysis for λ.
- Missing or unclear constraints/regularization on w; potential overfitting not analyzed.
- Limited ablation/sensitivity studies; unclear contributions of components.
- Minor clarity and citation issues; some derivations are terse.

Suggestions
- Add ablations: A-only, w-only, both; λ sensitivity; neighbor set size; effect of initialization.
- Clarify constraints on w (nonnegativity) and add regularization (e.g., L2 or entropy) if used.
- Provide more detail on α*, β* derivation from Sinkhorn scaling and discuss the approximation.
- Consider reporting retrieval metrics and runtime scalability vs. document length/corpus size.

Recommendation
I lean toward acceptance. The method is a well-executed and practically impactful extension of WMD, with solid empirical gains and a coherent optimization scheme using Sinkhorn OT. Addressing the noted clarity and analysis issues would further strengthen the paper.
