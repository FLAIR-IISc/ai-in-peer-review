- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, learning a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-frequency re-weighting to create a supervised document distance.
- Implementation: Uses Sinkhorn (relaxed optimal transport) and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD scalable.
- Empirical claim: Outperforms 26 baselines on 8 datasets, showing strong k-NN classification improvements.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD and metric learning, uniquely learning a metric in embedding space without changing document representations.
- Novelty: Simultaneous learning of embedding transformation and word importance weights is new and insightful.
- Scalability strength: Sinkhorn optimization, probabilistic filtering, and batch gradient techniques address computational bottlenecks.
- Empirical strength: Strong and consistent results across diverse datasets demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Evaluation quality: Thorough comparisons, ablation (S-WMD init.), and t-SNE visualizations support findings.
- Reproducibility: Code release is provided and praised.
- Writing: Paper is clear and well-explained with solid connections to related work.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lacks detailed analysis of sensitivity to λ (entropy regularizer) and learning rates; guidelines are needed.
- Distance choice concern: Using squared Euclidean distance is not fully justified; alternatives (e.g., cosine) should be considered.
- Theoretical limitation: Limited theoretical analysis on convergence and conditions guaranteeing metric improvement.
- Design choices: Batch size (32) and neighbor count (200) appear arbitrary without sensitivity justification.
- Overfitting risk: Learning word-importance weights may overfit, especially on small datasets; regularization/held-out analysis is limited.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, effective, scalable method with strong empirical support, clear writing, and released code.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient theoretical grounding, under-justified hyperparameter/components, and potential overfitting.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with significant contributions; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
- Rating: 8.5/10 (Strong Accept)
