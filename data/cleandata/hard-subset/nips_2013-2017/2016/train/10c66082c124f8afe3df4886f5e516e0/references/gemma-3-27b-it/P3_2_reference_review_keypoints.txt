Overall assessment: Proposes supervised WMD using Sinkhorn + probabilistic filtering, achieves strong results and is recommended for acceptance.

Summary & relation: Learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-specific importance weights, adapting metric learning to WMD and using Sinkhorn to make supervised learning tractable.

Strength — empirical results: Consistently outperforms a wide range of baselines across diverse datasets (26 baselines).

Strength — computational efficiency: Sinkhorn relaxation and probabilistic filtering substantially reduce WMD’s computational cost and training time.

Strength — novelty: Novel joint learning of embedding transform and word weights tailored to classification tasks.

Strength — theoretical soundness: Well-grounded in optimal transport and metric learning with clear gradient derivations.

Strength — reproducibility: Publicly available code provided.

Weakness — presentation clarity: Gradient derivation and some technical sections are dense and need more intuitive explanation.

Weakness — Sinkhorn Euclidean reliance: Using squared Euclidean distance in the Sinkhorn relaxation may be limiting for some applications.

Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Insufficient analysis of sensitivity and lack of best-configuration summaries per dataset.

Weakness — limited failure analysis: Little discussion of datasets or cases where S-WMD fails or underperforms and why.

Arguments for acceptance: Exceptional empirical performance, addresses a key challenge for supervised WMD, novel and theoretically sound, computationally efficient, reproducible.

Arguments against acceptance: Technical presentation could be more accessible, reliance on Euclidean Sinkhorn may be suboptimal, and limited analysis of failures and hyperparameter effects.

Specific suggestions: Expand gradient derivation with step-by-step intuition; add a table of best hyperparameters per dataset; show negatively weighted words in visualizations; discuss newer fast Wasserstein computation methods.

Final recommendation: Strong accept.
