Summary
The paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised variant of WMD that learns (i) a linear transformation A of the word embedding space and (ii) a word-importance vector w that reweights document histograms. The goal is to make optimal transport distances between documents align with class labels. Training minimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective à la NCA, but computes gradients efficiently by using the entropically regularized OT (Sinkhorn) relaxation to obtain unique transport plans and dual potentials, enabling O(q^2) per-pair complexity instead of cubic. The authors further accelerate training with an NCA-based initialization on word centroids (S-WCD), mini-batching, and neighbor filtering via a WCD-based candidate set. Across 8 datasets and 26 baselines, S-WMD achieves the best average rank and the lowest error on 5/8 datasets.

Relation to prior work
This work builds on WMD (Kusner et al.) by adding supervision directly to the ground metric and histogram weights, and on metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML) by moving the learning target from document feature space to the word-embedding ground cost of OT. It leverages Sinkhorn (Cuturi) for speed and differentiability and connects to supervised ground-metric learning for EMD in vision (e.g., Wang & Guibas; Cuturi & Avis), but applies and extends these ideas to text with reweighted histograms and an NCA-style objective. Relative to past supervised EMD works, learning both A and w in the word embedding space for document OT is a new combination that is well-motivated for NLP.

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty and direction: Moving supervision into the OT ground metric and per-word weighting for document distances is a bold, appealing idea with broad applicability.
- Technical soundness: The derivation of gradients via Sinkhorn (using T*λ and dual potentials α, β from scaling vectors u, v) is sensible and practical; the NCA-style loss fits the kNN use case.
- Practicality: The initialization (S-WCD), minibatching, and neighbor filtering make the approach trainable; complexity and runtime are discussed and reasonable.
- Empirical coverage: 8 datasets, 26 baselines (including metric learning on standard representations), and consistent gains in average rank; code availability.
- Interpretability: Learned w highlights task-relevant words; t-SNE visualizations suggest tighter class clusters.

Weaknesses and questions
- Clarity/notation: There are several typos and inconsistencies (e.g., “.2 Optimization,” reuse of p for both norm exponent and dimensionality, a dangling [21?], some equation referencing issues). Please clean up notation for α, β dimensions, and clarify r (output dimension of A), and stopping criteria.
- Ablations: While S-WMD init. is reported, a fuller ablation would help: A-only vs w-only vs both, sensitivity to λ, to M (neighbor set size), and to the choice of p-norm (p=1 vs 2).
- Baselines: Given the 2016 context, comparisons to doc2vec and fastText-style supervised text classifiers or simple linear SVM/LogReg on averaged embeddings would strengthen the case. Also include supervised embedding learning baselines (e.g., Siamese/WSABIE-style) if feasible.
- Objective scope: Optimizing kNN LOO error is natural for nearest-neighbor retrieval/classification, but implications for other downstream tasks (ranking, clustering) are not explored.
- Regularization/generalization: The paper does not discuss regularization on A and w (e.g., Frobenius, sparsity, or trace constraints) or overfitting analyses (learning curves, robustness to vocabulary size). Given the extra degrees of freedom, this is important.
- Fairness of neighbor filtering: Using WCD to preselect Na speeds training, but may bias gradient estimates if true nearest neighbors under S-WMD fall outside Na early in training. Empirical justification or a brief analysis of this trade-off would help.
- Complexity derivation: The stated O(TBN[p^2 + d^2(p + r)]) bound mixes terms in a way that is not fully justified in the text; please detail how the d^2(p + r) term arises from Eq. (8)/(10).

Quality
Overall technically solid and well-motivated. Using entropic OT to enable differentiable, fast training is appropriate and the gradient constructions are plausible. The experimental setup is broad and results are mostly strong, though additional ablations and a few stronger modern baselines would further solidify claims.

Clarity
Generally readable but needs careful editing for typos, consistent notation, and a more explicit training algorithm description (hyperparameters, regularization, rank r of A). A brief pseudo-code for Sinkhorn gradient flow and stopping criteria would aid reproducibility.

Originality
High. While supervised EMD has precedents, this paper’s combination—learning both ground metric in the embedding space and word weights with an NCA loss for document OT—is new in NLP and practically impactful.

Significance
High. Supervised OT distances for text are likely to be useful beyond kNN classification (retrieval, clustering, deduplication). Results suggest SOTA improvements over strong baselines and a promising direction for metric learning with OT.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add ablations (A-only vs w-only; λ sensitivity; neighbor set size M).
- Include at least one supervised embedding/text classification baseline (e.g., averaged embeddings + linear classifier, fastText) and doc2vec.
- Discuss/experiment with regularization for A and w; report the learned rank r or constrain A to be low-rank.
- Clarify complexity and provide memory usage; report wall-clock comparisons vs exact WMD when feasible.
- Fix notation and citation inconsistencies; ensure all references are complete.

Pros for acceptance
- Novel, well-argued idea with broad relevance; bridges metric learning and OT for text.
- Solid technical execution with Sinkhorn-based training.
- Strong empirical performance and code release.
- Interpretability via word weights and visualizations.

Cons for acceptance
- Missing ablations and some modern baselines.
- Minor clarity/notation issues; limited analysis of regularization/generalization.
- kNN-centric evaluation; broader task validation would strengthen impact.

Recommendation: Accept (7/10). The conceptual advance and empirical gains outweigh the minor shortcomings; this work opens a valuable direction for supervised OT in NLP.

Confidence: High.
