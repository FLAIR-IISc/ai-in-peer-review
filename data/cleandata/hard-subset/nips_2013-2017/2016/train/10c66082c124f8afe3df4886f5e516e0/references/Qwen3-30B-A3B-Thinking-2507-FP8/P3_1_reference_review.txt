

# Peer Review: Supervised Word Mover's Distance

## Summary

This paper proposes "Supervised Word Mover's Distance" (S-WMD), an extension of Kusner et al.'s Word Mover's Distance (WMD) that incorporates supervision to improve document distances for classification tasks. The key innovation is learning a linear transformation of word embeddings (A) and word-specific importance weights (w) to emphasize discriminative words. The authors leverage the Sinkhorn distance approximation to make the computation feasible, and train the model by minimizing a stochastic version of the leave-one-out classification error. The method is evaluated across 8 datasets and outperforms 26 baselines in kNN classification.

The paper builds on several key threads in the literature: WMD as a distance measure [19], metric learning (NCA, LMNN, ITML) [10, 15, 36], and the recent work on supervised Earth Mover's Distance [34, 35]. The novelty lies in combining these concepts with a novel approach to re-weighting word histograms and learning a linear transformation within the WMD framework.

## Quality

### Strengths

1. **Technical soundness**: The methodology is well-founded, with a solid theoretical basis in optimal transport and metric learning. The use of Sinkhorn distance to approximate the WMD computation is a practical and well-justified choice.

2. **Comprehensive empirical evaluation**: The paper evaluates against 26 baselines across 8 datasets, providing strong empirical evidence of the method's superiority. The results consistently show improvements over unsupervised WMD and other document representation methods.

3. **Clear implementation details**: The paper provides sufficient details about the implementation, including the initialization strategy (S-WCD) and batch gradient descent approach.

4. **Well-justified computational approach**: The authors correctly identify the cubic complexity of WMD as a barrier to supervised learning, and the Sinkhorn approximation is a good solution that has been validated in prior work [6].

### Weaknesses

1. **Lack of theoretical guarantees**: While the empirical results are strong, the paper lacks theoretical analysis of why the approach should work better than alternatives. There's no analysis of the convergence properties of the optimization or the relationship between the Sinkhorn relaxation and the true WMD.

2. **Limited ablation study**: The paper doesn't provide a thorough ablation study to isolate the contribution of the linear transformation (A) versus the word importance weights (w). It would be valuable to know which component contributes more to the performance gain.

3. **Overfitting concerns**: The paper doesn't discuss potential overfitting issues, particularly since the method involves learning additional parameters (A and w) on relatively small datasets (e.g., BBCSPORT has only 1,000 documents).

4. **Hyperparameter sensitivity**: The paper uses λ = 10 for the Sinkhorn distance without exploring the sensitivity of the method to this parameter. The choice seems arbitrary without empirical justification.

5. **Comparison with recent contextualized embeddings**: The paper uses word2vec embeddings but doesn't discuss how S-WMD might work with more recent contextualized embeddings like BERT, which have become standard for many NLP tasks.

## Clarity

### Strengths

1. **Well-organized structure**: The paper is well-organized, with clear sections covering background, methodology, experiments, and related work.

2. **Clear mathematical notation**: The mathematical formulations are presented clearly, with appropriate references to the underlying theory.

3. **Effective visualization**: The t-SNE visualizations (Figure 1) and word weight visualization (Figure 2) effectively demonstrate the quality of the learned embeddings.

### Weaknesses

1. **Ambiguous notation**: The paper uses "d̃" for the re-weighted document vector (equation 4) without sufficient explanation of how this differs from the normalized BOW vector.

2. **Insufficient explanation of initialization**: The initialization procedure using NCA on WCD is described, but the paper could better explain why this is a good starting point.

3. **Lack of pseudocode for key steps**: While Algorithm 1 is provided, the paper could include pseudocode for the gradient computation (equations 8-10) to make the implementation clearer.

## Originality

### Strengths

1. **Novel combination of ideas**: The paper successfully combines metric learning with WMD in a supervised setting, which is a meaningful extension of prior work.

2. **Appropriate use of Sinkhorn distance**: The application of Sinkhorn distance to make supervised WMD computationally feasible is a clever and appropriate solution.

### Weaknesses

1. **Limited differentiation from Wang et al. [35]**: The paper cites Wang et al. [35] as similar work but doesn't sufficiently differentiate its approach. Wang et al. also learn a metric within a generalized Euclidean EMD ground distance but don't consider re-weighting histograms, which is a key contribution of S-WMD.

2. **Lack of discussion on related supervised word embedding work**: The paper mentions Tang et al. [32] but doesn't discuss more recent supervised word embedding methods like those using attention mechanisms or contextualized embeddings.

3. **Not sufficiently novel in the context of recent NIPS work**: While the specific combination is novel, the core idea of learning a metric for WMD is similar to Wang et al. [35], which was presented at ECCV, and there's a growing body of work on supervised EMD in recent NIPS proceedings.

## Significance

### Strengths

1. **Strong empirical results**: The consistent improvement over 26 baselines across 8 datasets demonstrates clear practical value.

2. **Practical computational efficiency**: The method's efficiency (Table 3) makes it applicable to real-world document classification tasks.

3. **Good foundation for future work**: The approach provides a natural framework for incorporating supervision into other optimal transport-based document distances.

### Weaknesses

1. **Limited scope of application**: The method is limited to the word embedding space and doesn't address how to handle out-of-vocabulary words or integrate with more recent contextualized embeddings.

2. **Not a paradigm shift**: While the method is effective, it's an incremental improvement over WMD rather than a fundamental new approach to document representation.

3. **Limited discussion of broader impact**: The paper doesn't discuss how this method might be applied to other domains beyond text classification (e.g., image or video retrieval).

## Specific Recommendations for Improvement

1. **Add a thorough ablation study**: Include experiments that isolate the contribution of the linear transformation (A) versus the word importance weights (w), and show how each component affects performance.

2. **Provide theoretical analysis**: Add a brief theoretical discussion of the relationship between the Sinkhorn relaxation and the true WMD, and discuss the convergence properties of the optimization.

3. **Explore hyperparameter sensitivity**: Include experiments showing how performance varies with different values of λ in the Sinkhorn distance.

4. **Compare with recent contextualized embeddings**: Discuss how S-WMD might work with BERT or other contextualized embeddings, and provide preliminary results if possible.

5. **Address overfitting concerns**: Include a discussion of potential overfitting and how the method handles it, perhaps with a comparison of training vs. validation error.

6. **Expand related work**: Discuss more recent supervised EMD work (e.g., Frogner et al. [17] on learning with a Wasserstein loss) and clarify how S-WMD differs from these approaches.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of document representation and metric learning. The method is well-motivated, technically sound, and demonstrates clear empirical advantages over a wide range of baselines. The computational efficiency of the approach is particularly noteworthy.

While the paper has some weaknesses in theoretical analysis and ablation studies, these are relatively minor compared to the overall quality of the work. The method is a natural extension of WMD that addresses a clear limitation (unsupervised nature) and provides a practical solution for supervised document classification.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the theoretical and ablation study concerns mentioned above.

## Recommendation

Accept (with minor revisions)

## Strengths vs. Weaknesses Summary

**Strengths:**
- Strong empirical results across 8 datasets
- Practical computational approach using Sinkhorn distance
- Clear methodology with solid theoretical foundation
- Comprehensive comparison against 26 baselines
- Good visualization of results

**Weaknesses:**
- Lack of theoretical analysis and guarantees
- Limited ablation study to isolate key components
- Overfitting concerns not addressed
- Comparison with recent contextualized embeddings missing
- Hyperparameter sensitivity not explored

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field and is well-suited for publication at NIPS. The minor weaknesses can be addressed with straightforward revisions.
