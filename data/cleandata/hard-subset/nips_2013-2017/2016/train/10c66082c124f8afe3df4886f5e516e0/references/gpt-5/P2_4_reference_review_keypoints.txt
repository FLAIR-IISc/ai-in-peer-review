- Proposal: S-WMD — supervised WMD variant that learns a linear transform A and nonnegative word-importance vector w, optimizing an NCA-style kNN classification loss.  
- Method: Uses entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to approximate transport plans and dual potentials, enabling gradient computation and O(p^2) per-pair cost (p = unique words per doc).  
- Training: Initializes A via NCA on word-centroids (WCD) and trains with batch SGD plus nearest-neighbor truncation based on WCD.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD, entropic OT, and metric learning; novelty is adapting these to text and jointly learning A and histogram reweighting.  
- Technical soundness: Using Sinkhorn to obtain transport and dual potentials for gradients is appropriate for entropically regularized OT.  
- Loss/gradient mismatch: Paper appears to use unregularized WMD in loss but differentiates through the regularized Sinkhorn solution — needs explicit clarification and discussion of λ’s approximation error.  
- w constraints: w must be nonnegative to form valid histograms, but the algorithm uses unconstrained gradient updates without projection/reparameterization — correctness issue to fix/clarify.  
- Gradient complexity: Claimed O(d^2(p+r)) complexity is unclear; naive computation ∑ij T_ij(xi−xj)(xi−xj)ᵀ costs O(p^2 d) or O(p^2 d^2) if forming full matrices — provide derivation or structural tricks.  
- Scalability: Per-pair Sinkhorn is standard and datasets are handled, but more discussion of typical p, wall-clock times, and scaling to large p is needed.  
- Empirics: Strong evaluation across 8 datasets and 26 baselines; S-WMD wins 5/8 and has best average rank with hyperparameters tuned via BO.  
- Missing ablations: Need experiments for only-A vs only-w, effect of λ, neighbor truncation size (M/N), and impact of initialization.  
- Robustness checks: Sensitivity to choice of word embeddings and to different k values in kNN should be reported.  
- Fairness of baselines: Supervised baselines were PCA-reduced to 200 dims while S-WMD operates in embedding space — discuss whether dimensionality differences bias results and, if so, provide controlled comparisons.  
- Reproducibility: Code is promised and most algorithmic details given, but some hyperparameters (learning rates, regularization, early stopping) are not fully specified.  
- Clarity fixes: Explicitly state if the optimized distance is the entropic OT cost, enforce/parameterize constraints on A and w, and fix minor typos/notation collisions.  
- Originality: Moderate — metric learning for EMD exists in vision, but contribution is a nontrivial integration for text with learned word weights and Sinkhorn gradients.  
- Significance: High practical relevance for document similarity; interpretable word weights and consistent empirical gains make the approach useful.  
- Pros: Principled and efficient method, good integration of ideas, broad empirical evaluation, interpretability via word weights, and promised code.  
- Cons: Correctness concerns about w nonnegativity and loss/gradient consistency, insufficient detail on gradient complexity and scalability, limited ablations, and reliance on WCD pruning.  
- Suggested fixes/questions: Enforce w≥0 (projection/exp), clarify whether DA,w^λ is used in loss and λ selection, provide derivation for duals and gradient complexity, add ablations and embedding/k robustness tests.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing the clarity/correctness issues (especially w constraints and loss/gradient consistency).
