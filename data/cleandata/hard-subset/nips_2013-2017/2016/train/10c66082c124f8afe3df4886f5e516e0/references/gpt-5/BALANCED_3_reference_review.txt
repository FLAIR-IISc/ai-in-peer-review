Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) that learns a task-specific metric in the word embedding space and a set of word-importance weights to improve document distance estimation for kNN classification. Concretely: (i) it learns a linear transformation A applied to word embeddings and (ii) a nonnegative word-weight vector w reweighting document histograms, then uses the resulting ground costs in an optimal transport (OT) formulation of document distance. To make learning feasible, the paper employs the entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn distance) to obtain differentiable, O(q^2) approximations of transport plans and dual variables, enabling efficient stochastic optimization of a leave-one-out (LOO) NCA-style objective. Additional engineering includes an initialization via NCA on Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD), mini-batch training, and neighbor pruning using WCD.

This work builds directly on: WMD for unsupervised document distances (Kusner et al., ICML’15); entropy-regularized OT and Sinkhorn scaling (Cuturi, NIPS’13); and metric learning via NCA (Goldberger et al., NIPS’05), as well as prior supervised EMD/OT works in computer vision (e.g., Wang & Guibas, ECCV’09; Cuturi & Avis). The contribution is to combine these strands for text, with a practical learning algorithm and empirical gains across multiple datasets.

Quality
- Technical soundness: The use of Sinkhorn to approximate WMD makes the inner transport differentiable and efficient. The gradient w.r.t. A via the (relaxed) optimal transport plan is standard. The gradient w.r.t. w via dual potentials is a nice touch; recovering α, β from Sinkhorn scaling is a known relation in regularized OT and is used here to backprop through histogram reweighting. The stochastic NCA objective is reasonable for kNN performance.
- Some issues merit clarification: (1) Notation is occasionally inconsistent (e.g., reuse of p for the power in Lp and for dimensions; “.2 Optimization” typo; dimensions in α, β formulas include an unexplained division by p). (2) The paper assumes nonnegativity of w but does not explicitly state or enforce it; negative weights would break the histogram semantics and the transport constraints. (3) There is no explicit regularization on A or w; without constraints, w can become extremely peaky, and A can overfit, especially with small datasets. (4) The choice of squared Euclidean ground cost is fixed; it is unclear whether other costs (cosine, L1) might be superior. (5) The approximation error from using T*λ and approximate duals to compute gradients is not analyzed; how sensitive is learning to λ? (6) Complexity: the stated O(d^2(p + r)) term deserves derivation; r (rows of A) is not motivated—do you learn a low-rank A? If so, how is r chosen?
- Empirics: Experiments cover 8 datasets with 26 baselines (unsupervised and supervised with PCA+NCA/LMNN/ITML). S-WMD is strong overall, often best or near-best. The training-time table is helpful. However, ablation studies are missing: effect of A only vs w only; impact of λ, M (neighbor cap), batch size, initialization; sensitivity to embedding choice; effect of removing neighbor pruning; comparison to exact (unregularized) OT on small instances; robustness to document length and vocabulary size. A few datasets (e.g., BBCSPORT/CLASSIC) show anomalous behavior relative to WMD; a deeper analysis would help.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable and well-motivated, but could be improved:
- Clean up notation, define all symbols, and avoid overloading p.
- State constraints on w (w ≥ 0) and any normalization or regularization (e.g., L1 sparsity or L2 on A).
- Provide pseudo-code with clear inputs/outputs, convergence criteria, learning-rate schedules.
- Expand details on preprocessing (tokenization, stopwords, OOV handling), vocabulary size, and whether all documents are restricted to the embedding vocabulary.

Originality
Moderate. The components—WMD/OT, Sinkhorn regularization, and NCA-style metric learning—are known, and supervised EMD has prior art in vision. The novelty lies in: (i) bringing supervised OT-based metric learning to document distances with pretrained word embeddings; (ii) jointly learning A and per-word weights w; (iii) using Sinkhorn duals to differentiate through histogram reweighting efficiently for text. This combination for NLP is, to my knowledge, new and practically useful.

Significance
The problem—task-adaptive document distance—is important, and WMD has been influential. A supervised extension that preserves WMD’s semantic advantages and delivers consistent accuracy gains with reasonable training cost is likely to see adoption in retrieval and classification pipelines. While neural end-to-end text classifiers can outperform kNN in many settings, a strong, interpretable, distance-based method remains valuable, especially in low-label or domain-shifted regimes.

Pros
- Well-motivated supervised extension of WMD with clear formulation.
- Efficient training via Sinkhorn and neighbor pruning; code release.
- Strong empirical results across diverse datasets against many baselines.
- Interpretability via learned word weights and improved t-SNE structure.

Cons
- Missing ablations and sensitivity analyses; limited discussion of λ and approximation effects.
- Notational inconsistencies and some derivational omissions reduce clarity.
- Lack of explicit constraints/regularization on A and w; potential overfitting.
- Comparisons omit some modern supervised text baselines (e.g., fastText supervised, CNNs) and recent OT-based learning baselines beyond the cited EMD works.
- Some fairness concerns: LDA is trained transductively; dimensionality reduction choices for baselines could bias outcomes.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add constraints and regularization: enforce w ≥ 0, consider L1 on w for sparsity and L2/Frobenius on A; report their effects.
- Provide ablations: A-only, w-only, both; with/without WCD initialization; varying λ, M, batch size; different ground costs.
- Discuss/justify choice of r (rank of A); consider learning M = A^T A directly with PSD constraint.
- Include sensitivity to the underlying embeddings (word2vec vs GloVe/fastText) and OOV handling.
- Compare to fastText-supervised and a simple linear SVM over TF-IDF to contextualize absolute performance.
- Clarify derivations (dual potentials from Sinkhorn, gradient formulas) and fix notation.
- Analyze when S-WMD underperforms WMD and whether alternative ground metrics would help long documents.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper addresses a timely and relevant problem with a practical and effective method that integrates known ideas in a nontrivial way for NLP. Despite some clarity issues and missing analyses, the empirical gains and efficient training make it a valuable contribution to NeurIPS. With the suggested clarifications and ablations, the paper would be stronger.
