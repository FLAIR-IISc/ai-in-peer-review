- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform and word importance weights using a Sinkhorn-relaxed OT loss for kNN classification.

- Technical contribution: Learns transformation A and word weights w, derives gradients, and uses Sinkhorn approximation to make WMD training tractable.

- Experimental evaluation: Comprehensive tests on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, showing state-of-the-art on 5/8 datasets with ablations and visualizations.

- Quality: Mathematically sound with thorough complexity analysis and practical solutions; code released.

- Clarity: Well-written, organized, and reproducible in principle with clear figures, pseudocode, and explanations.

- Originality: Novel supervised integration into WMD combining linear embedding transform and histogram re-weighting; differentiates from prior work.

- Significance: Relevant for many text tasks, computationally efficient, and shows substantial empirical improvements over unsupervised baselines.

- Reproducibility weakness: Paper omits key hyperparameter details (learning rates, batch size, k, Sinkhorn λ) and Bayes-opt search settings.

- Failure-case analysis weakness: Insufficient investigation into why S-WMD underperforms on BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, and AMAZON.

- Comparison weakness: Lacks direct comparison or discussion with recent deep contextual models (e.g., BERT-based approaches).

- Initialization analysis weakness: S-WCD initialization is proposed but not thoroughly compared to alternatives like random init.

- Overall assessment: Strong, significant paper with solid theory, experiments, and practical impact despite some minor analysis and reproducibility gaps.

- Recommendation: Strong accept.

- Specific suggestion 1: Report all hyperparameter settings (η_A, η_w, B, N, λ) and Bayes-opt details.

- Specific suggestion 2: Provide deeper analysis of failure cases on the three underperforming datasets.

- Specific suggestion 3: Add brief comparisons or discussion involving modern deep contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT).

- Specific suggestion 4: Compare different initialization strategies to show impact on convergence/performance.

- Specific suggestion 5: Discuss scalability to very large corpora and integration with contextualized embeddings.

- Pro acceptance arguments: Novel supervised WMD, strong theoretical foundation, comprehensive experiments, practical Sinkhorn solution, SOTA results on majority of datasets.

- Con acceptance arguments: Missing hyperparameter detail hurting reproducibility, limited failure-case analysis, no comparison with recent deep learning text representations.
