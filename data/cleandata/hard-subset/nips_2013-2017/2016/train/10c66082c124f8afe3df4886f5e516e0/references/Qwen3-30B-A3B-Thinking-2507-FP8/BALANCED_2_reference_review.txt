

# Review: Supervised Word Mover's Distance

## Summary
This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), an extension of Kusner et al.'s Word Mover's Distance (WMD) that incorporates supervision to learn a more discriminative document distance. While WMD computes document distances as optimal transport costs between word embeddings, S-WMD learns a linear transformation of the word embeddings and word-specific importance weights to better align with class labels. The approach leverages the Sinkhorn distance approximation for computational efficiency and minimizes a stochastic leave-one-out classification error. The authors evaluate S-WMD on 8 document classification datasets, demonstrating consistent improvements over unsupervised WMD and 26 baseline methods.

## Strengths

1. **Technical Soundness and Quality**: The paper presents a well-motivated technical contribution that builds logically on prior work. The mathematical formulation of the loss function, gradients, and optimization procedure is clear and technically sound. The authors provide a thorough analysis of computational complexity, showing that S-WMD scales reasonably well (O(TBN[p² + d²(p + r)]).

2. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The experimental section is strong, with evaluation across 8 diverse document datasets (news categorization, sentiment analysis, etc.) against 26 baselines. The results consistently show S-WMD outperforming unsupervised WMD and other document representations, with the method achieving the lowest error on 5/8 datasets. The ablation study showing improvement from initialization to final model is particularly convincing.

3. **Novelty and Significance**: The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the intersection of metric learning and text representation. While metric learning has been applied to text before, this is the first to directly incorporate supervision into the WMD framework, which is a significant step beyond prior work. The ability to learn word importance weights provides valuable interpretability, as shown in Figure 2.

4. **Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The background section effectively contextualizes the work, and the technical details are presented with sufficient mathematical rigor. The experimental setup is transparent, with clear descriptions of datasets, baselines, and evaluation protocols.

## Weaknesses

1. **Lack of Theoretical Guarantees**: While the method is empirically effective, the paper lacks theoretical analysis of convergence properties or generalization bounds. This is particularly notable given the non-convex nature of the optimization problem.

2. **Hyperparameter Sensitivity**: The paper doesn't thoroughly explore how S-WMD's performance varies with key hyperparameters, particularly λ (the entropy regularization parameter). The choice of λ=10 is made without sufficient justification or ablation study.

3. **Limited Comparison to Recent Work**: The related work section omits several recent papers on metric learning for text (e.g., work by Wang et al. on supervised EMD learning). The authors also don't compare against more recent text embedding methods beyond word2vec.

4. **Initialization Justification**: While the initialization via NCA with WCD is effective, the paper doesn't provide strong theoretical justification for why this initialization is superior to alternatives, which would strengthen the method's credibility.

5. **Limited Analysis of Failure Cases**: The paper notes S-WMD underperforms on BBCSPORT and CLASSIC datasets but doesn't provide a deep analysis of why this occurs. A more detailed examination of these failure cases would provide valuable insights for future work.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field of document representation and metric learning. The S-WMD approach is well-motivated, technically sound, and demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over the state of the art. The paper's clarity, thorough evaluation, and significant empirical results make it a valuable contribution to NIPS.

While the paper has some limitations in theoretical grounding and hyperparameter analysis, these do not undermine the substantial empirical contribution. The authors' approach to incorporating supervision into WMD is novel and addresses an important gap in the literature. The method's efficiency and interpretability through learned word weights further enhance its significance.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of text representation learning, with strong empirical results and a well-executed approach. The minor weaknesses noted are common in applied machine learning papers and could be addressed in a revised version, but do not diminish the paper's overall quality and contribution.

**Pro**: Strong empirical results across diverse datasets, well-motivated extension of WMD, clear writing, efficient implementation, valuable interpretability through word weights.

**Con**: Limited theoretical analysis, insufficient exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity, some missing comparisons to recent work, limited analysis of failure cases.
