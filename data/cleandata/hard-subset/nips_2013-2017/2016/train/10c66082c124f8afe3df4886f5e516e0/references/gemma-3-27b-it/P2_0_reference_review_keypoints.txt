- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised metric learning method that learns a linear transformation of embeddings and re-weights word frequencies to improve Word Mover’s Distance for document similarity.
- Uses Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD for computational tractability and adopts WCD initialization plus SGD for efficient optimization.
- Strong empirical results across 8 datasets and 26 baselines, consistently outperforming competitors; includes informative ablations and visualizations.
- Novel idea combining metric learning inside the WMD framework and joint word re-weighting.
- Computational efficiency is significantly improved via the Sinkhorn approximation and practical optimization strategies.
- Clear, well-organized writing with accessible explanations of complex concepts.
- Detailed analysis includes t-SNE visualizations and inspection of learned word weights.
- Implementation is complex and may hinder reproducibility for practitioners unfamiliar with optimal transport and metric learning.
- Hyperparameter tuning details and sensitivity analysis are insufficiently reported, leaving robustness unclear.
- Limited theoretical analysis of the learned metric and its properties.
- Comparison lacks some recent baselines using contextualized embeddings (e.g., BERT/RoBERTa).
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, computationally efficient, strong empirical performance, well-written.
- Arguments against acceptance: implementation complexity, limited theory, missing comparisons to modern contextual baselines.
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with suggestions to strengthen theoretical analysis and include newer baselines in future work.
