This paper introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method for learning a metric to improve the Word Mover’s Distance by linearly transforming word embeddings and re-weighting word frequencies, all guided by supervised labels. The core idea – leveraging the strengths of WMD (operating in a low-dimensional space with pre-trained embeddings) while incorporating task-specific supervision – is genuinely interesting and potentially impactful. The authors address the computational challenges of supervised WMD using a relaxed optimization technique (Sinkhorn distance) and probabilistic filtering. This work builds upon Kusner et al.’s WMD and metric learning literature, cleverly combining them.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The combination of supervised learning with the WMD framework is a novel approach. While metric learning is well-established, applying it *within* the word embedding space, alongside word re-weighting, is a compelling and relatively unexplored idea. This isn't merely an incremental improvement; it’s a different angle on document distance calculation.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper presents strong results across 8 datasets and 26 baseline methods. Consistently outperforming a large number of competitors, including established metric learning approaches, is a significant achievement.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The authors demonstrate awareness of the computational challenges and propose a practical solution using the Sinkhorn distance and probabilistic filtering, making the approach feasible.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and results. The connection to related work (metric learning, optimal transport) is well-established.
*   **Ablation Study & Visualization:** Showing the results of the initialization (S-WMD init.) and visualizing the learned word weights (Fig. 2) provide valuable insights.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the paper motivates the approach well, the theoretical justification for the linear transformation and re-weighting is somewhat limited. A more in-depth analysis of the properties of the learned metric would be beneficial.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The reliance on Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning is noted, but the sensitivity of the results to these hyperparameters isn’t fully discussed.  A brief analysis of how performance changes with different hyperparameter settings would strengthen the work.
*   **Dataset Specificity:** The results seem quite variable across datasets. The discussion acknowledges this but could benefit from a deeper dive into *why* S-WMD performs better on some datasets than others. Are there particular characteristics of the data that favor this approach?
*   **Squared Euclidean Distance:** The choice of squared Euclidean distance in the Sinkhorn relaxation may be suboptimal for some datasets, as acknowledged. Exploring alternative distance metrics (e.g., L1) could potentially lead to further improvements.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The novelty and potential impact of the approach are high. This work opens up a promising new direction for research in document distance learning.
*   The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of S-WMD.
*   The authors have addressed the computational challenges effectively.
*   The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of deeper theoretical analysis is a limitation.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity and dataset-specific performance require further investigation.
*   While strong, the improvements are not overwhelming in every case.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a novel and promising approach to learning document distances. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths – particularly the novelty, strong empirical results, and practical efficiency – significantly outweigh them. The paper introduces a valuable contribution to the field and has the potential to inspire further research. I believe it meets the criteria for acceptance to NIPS, despite being not entirely theoretically grounded. I'm prioritizing the boldness of the approach and potential impact over absolute methodological perfection.



**Recommendation:** Accept.
