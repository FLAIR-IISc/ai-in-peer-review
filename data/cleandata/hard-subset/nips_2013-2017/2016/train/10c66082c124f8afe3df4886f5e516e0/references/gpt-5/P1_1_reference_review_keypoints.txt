- Proposal: S-WMD learns a linear transform A of word embeddings and per-word importance weights w, using a supervised WMD for document comparison.  
- Training objective: NCA-style stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss with a softmax over negative distances DA,w.  
- Efficiency: Uses entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to approximate transport and dual potentials, yielding O(p^2) scaling per pair, plus batching and top-N neighbor pruning.  
- Initialization: S-WCD (NCA on word centroids) provides a sensible starting A and already strong performance.  
- Empirical results: Evaluated on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines, best average rank and lowest error on 5/8 datasets; code released.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD and entropic OT; novelty is learning the OT ground metric in embedding space and histogram reweighting for text.  
- Distinction: Differs from vision OT metric learning and from representation learning methods by preserving OT alignment of semantically similar words.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple and practical—learns task-specific ground distance and term weights while staying low-dimensional.  
- Strength: Solid optimization design—NCA loss, Sinkhorn relaxation, reuse of dual potentials, batching, and neighbor pruning.  
- Strength: Strong, broad empirical study and sensible initialization strategy; reproducible code.  
- Main concern: Objective/gradient mismatch—training uses gradients from entropic-regularized transport but loss is written for unregularized DA,w; potential bias unless the regularized distance is the actual objective.  
- Missing analysis: No sensitivity study for entropic regularization parameter λ (fixed at 10) and its effect on accuracy and speed.  
- Word-weight issues: No explicit constraint or regularization for w; positivity enforcement and overfitting risks (e.g., upweighting rare words) are not addressed.  
- Neighbor pruning concern: Truncating the NCA softmax to top-N WCD neighbors changes the objective with no analysis of discarded mass, convergence, or sensitivity to N.  
- Notation and exposition: Reuse of p, section numbering glitches, incomplete references, and only sketched derivation of dual potentials hinder clarity and reproducibility.  
- Complexity claim: Reported gradient cost seems understated; accumulating Σij Tij (xi−xj)(xi−xj)^T is O(p^2 d), so exact computational costs need clarification.  
- Missing ablations: No isolated studies of A-only vs w-only vs joint, nor sensitivity analyses for neighbor cap N and batch size; test-time speed and exact distance used are unclear.  
- Baselines: Lacks comparison/discussion against strong supervised neural text encoders (e.g., CNN/RNN) to contextualize gains.  
- Quality verdict: Technically sound with appropriate choices, but key caveats around regularization/gradient fidelity and w constraints remain.  
- Clarity fixes requested: Explicitly state whether the trained objective is regularized or not, provide full derivation of dual potentials, and clarify test-time distance computation/runtime.  
- Originality: Novel and timely—supervised learning of OT ground metric plus word weighting for text is a distinct contribution.  
- Significance: High practical relevance as a drop-in supervised improvement to WMD likely useful for retrieval/classification and supervised OT research.  
- Questions for authors: Do you optimize Dλ or the exact DA,w? How is w≥0 enforced and regularized? What distance and prefiltering are used at test time and what are runtimes? Can you provide A vs w ablations and λ/N sensitivity? Have you tried other embeddings?  
- Recommendation: Lean accept, contingent on clarifying the objective vs approximation, λ sensitivity analysis, and addressing constraints/regularization for w.
