- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, which learns a linear transform of embeddings and re-weights word frequencies to improve WMD, using Sinkhorn relaxation and probabilistic filtering; shows strong empirical gains on eight datasets.

- Relation to Prior Work: Positions S-WMD between unsupervised WMD and metric learning, cites optimal transport and NCA literature, and provides comprehensive related work coverage.

- Strength — Novelty: Novel approach of learning a metric inside the WMD framework while preserving pre-trained word embeddings and re-weighting word frequencies.

- Strength — Computational Efficiency: Uses Sinkhorn distance and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD scalable and practical.

- Strength — Strong Empirical Results: Outperforms many strong baselines (26 compared) across diverse datasets.

- Strength — Clear Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear explanations of Sinkhorn distance and gradient computations.

- Strength — Analysis & Ablation: Includes initialization ablation (S-WMD init.) and visualizes learned word weights for insight.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter Sensitivity: Insufficient exploration of sensitivity to key hyperparameters (λ and nearest neighbor size N) despite Bayesian optimization.

- Weakness — Distance Choice: Uses squared Euclidean distance without full discussion of alternatives (e.g., L1) and their impact.

- Weakness — Limited Scalability Discussion: Lacks concrete guidance or empirical analysis for extreme-scale settings (e.g., billions of documents).

- Weakness — Local Optima Risk: Does not analyze potential optimization issues from non-convexity or alternatives in initialization/optimization.

- Weakness — Interpretation of Word Weights: Visualization shown but deeper explanations for why certain words are upweighted per task are missing.

- Argument For Acceptance: Technically sound, novel, and significantly improves state-of-the-art document distance learning with strong empirical evidence.

- Argument For Acceptance: Computational challenges are thoughtfully addressed via Sinkhorn and filtering, making supervised WMD practical.

- Argument For Acceptance: Well-written and thoroughly evaluated work with likely impact on IR and document analysis.

- Argument Against Acceptance: Hyperparameter sensitivity may limit practical applicability.

- Argument Against Acceptance: Insufficient detailed scalability analysis for very large datasets.

- Overall Assessment: Strong accept recommendation — significant, well-executed contribution with compelling results; weaknesses are minor.

- Confidence: Reviewer confidence 9/10.
