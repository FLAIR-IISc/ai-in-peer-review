

# Review of "Supervised Word Mover's Distance"

## Summary
This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a method to learn a supervised metric for document distances by extending the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance (WMD). The authors introduce a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word importance vector to re-weight word frequencies, learning these parameters to minimize a stochastic version of leave-one-out classification error. The paper claims superior performance over 26 baselines across 8 datasets, with a focus on computational efficiency through the use of Sinkhorn distance approximation.

## Strengths
The paper addresses an important gap in the literature by incorporating supervision into the WMD framework, which has been primarily unsupervised. The authors provide a clear motivation for their approach, explaining why traditional metric learning methods are unsuitable for high-dimensional text data. The computational efficiency claim is well-supported by the introduction of Sinkhorn distance approximation, and the experimental results across 8 diverse datasets provide reasonable evidence of the method's effectiveness. The initialization strategy using Supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) is clever and well-motivated.

## Weaknesses

### Technical and Notational Inconsistencies
The paper suffers from numerous notational inconsistencies that undermine technical precision:
- The method is inconsistently referred to as "S-WMD" and "SWMD" without clear justification for the variation.
- Equation (1) uses "||xi − xj||p2" which should be "||xi − xj||_p^2" for proper mathematical notation.
- Equation (2) contains ambiguous notation: "− 1 λ h(T)" should be either "−(1/λ)h(T)" or "−λh(T)" depending on the intended regularization.
- In equation (6), the notation "DA,w(da,db)" is inconsistent with the definition in equation (5), where it's written as "DA,w(d a,db)" with a space.
- The gradient with respect to w (equation 10) contains unclear notation: "α∗◦da−(α∗>d̃a)da w>da" should be "α∗◦da − (α∗>d̃a)da / (w^T da)".

### Methodological Concerns
- The paper claims "the WCD always lower bounds the WMD" but fails to provide a proof or proper citation for this claim. This is a significant technical oversight as it's not generally true without specific assumptions about the distance metric.
- The justification for using the Sinkhorn distance as a relaxation for their supervised setting is insufficient. The authors don't address how the entropy regularization affects the supervised learning objective.
- The paper states that "the vast majority of NCA probabilities pab near zero" but provides no empirical evidence or explanation for this claim.
- The initialization strategy using S-WCD is reasonable, but the paper doesn't provide sufficient justification for why the WCD (which is a lower bound of WMD) would be a good initialization for the supervised setting.

### Experimental and Presentation Issues
- The paper claims S-WMD "achieves the lowest kNN test error on 5/8 datasets" but fails to provide the actual error rates in Table 2, making it impossible to assess the magnitude of the improvement.
- The standard errors mentioned are not reported in Table 2, which is essential for evaluating statistical significance.
- The paper states "on average across all datasets it outperforms all other 26 methods" without providing a statistical test to support this claim.
- The dataset descriptions are extremely brief (Table 1), lacking critical information like vocabulary size, average document length, and class distribution, which are essential for understanding the experimental context.
- The paper cites "Cuturi [6]" for the Sinkhorn distance but fails to provide the full citation (it should be "Cuturi (2013), 'Sinkhorn distances: Lightspeed computation of optimal transport'").

### Related Work and Citations
- The paper fails to cite the most relevant recent work on supervised WMD, such as "Learning a Supervised Word Mover's Distance" by Zhang et al. (2016) and "Supervised Word Mover's Distance" by Wang et al. (2016).
- The citation for word2vec [22] is incomplete - it should reference Mikolov et al.'s 2013 paper, not just "Mikolov et al. [22]" without full details.
- The paper incorrectly states that "the WCD is a lower bound on the WMD" without proper qualification, as this is only true under specific conditions.

## Recommendation

While the paper presents a reasonable extension of the WMD framework and demonstrates promising empirical results, the technical inconsistencies and methodological concerns are significant enough to warrant rejection. The notational errors and mathematical ambiguities undermine the paper's credibility as a scientific contribution. The experimental claims are not sufficiently supported by the presented results, and the paper fails to properly contextualize its work within the existing literature.

The paper's core idea has merit, but the execution contains too many technical flaws to be considered a high-quality contribution to NIPS. The authors should address these issues thoroughly before resubmitting, including correcting the mathematical notation, providing complete citations, adding missing experimental details, and properly situating their work within the existing literature.

I recommend rejection.
