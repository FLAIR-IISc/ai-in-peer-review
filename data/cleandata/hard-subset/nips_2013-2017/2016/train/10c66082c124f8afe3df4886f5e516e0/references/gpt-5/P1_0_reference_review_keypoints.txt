- Proposal: S-WMD learns a linear transform A of pretrained embeddings and a global word-importance vector w to supervise WMD via an NCA-style leave-one-out kNN loss.  
- Optimization trick: replaces exact OT with entropic Sinkhorn for O(q^2) pairwise computation and derives gradients via Danskin’s theorem (A) and dual potentials from Sinkhorn scalings (w).  
- Practical speedups: uses WCD neighbor shortlisting and mini-batching; code released and hyperparameters tuned with Bayesian optimization.  
- Empirical result: evaluated on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines, best on 5/8 and competitive otherwise with improved average rank.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on WMD and Sinkhorn OT, follows NCA paradigm, and differs from prior OT metric learning by targeting pretrained word embeddings and jointly learning global vocabulary weights.  
- Strength — motivation: adding supervision to WMD is timely and relevant for document classification/retrieval.  
- Strength — formulation: jointly learning ground metric A and histogram weights w within OT is principled; gradient approach is appropriate with entropic regularization.  
- Strength — efficiency: Sinkhorn, neighbor shortlists, and mini-batches make training feasible with reasonable per-iteration runtimes.  
- Strength — reproducibility: code release and systematic hyperparameter tuning improve reproducibility.  
- Weakness — notation/clarity: inconsistent symbols (p vs q), incorrect/unclear kernel notation K vs K_A, confusing uses of p in formulas, and multiple typos/formatting glitches.  
- Weakness — unspecified constraints/regularization: no clear constraints (e.g., nonnegativity) or regularizers on w and A, risking degenerate solutions and overemphasis of rare words.  
- Weakness — modeling limitation: a single global w may be restrictive for multi-domain or multi-class datasets; per-class/group variants could help.  
- Weakness — optimization detail: derivative presentation should explicitly state Danskin’s applicability (dT*/dA ignored) and clarify the approximation of dual potentials from Sinkhorn scalings.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: limited discussion/analysis of λ (entropic regularization) and its impact on approximation quality, accuracy, and runtime.  
- Weakness — evaluation scope: results are limited to kNN; benefits for retrieval (mAP), clustering, or other classifiers (e.g., SVM) are not shown.  
- Weakness — ablations: missing ablations (learn A only vs w only vs full) and comparison of Sinkhorn vs exact OT on small problems.  
- Weakness — fairness in baselines: discrepancy in dimensionality treatment (PCA to 200D for baselines vs no rank constraint for A) raises fairness questions.  
- Quality: technically sound overall, but some theoretical details (constraints on w, rigorous link between Sinkhorn scalings and dual potentials) need fuller treatment or appendix proofs.  
- Clarity: generally readable but marred by notation overload, typos, and unclear optimization descriptions; algorithmic dependence of K on A should be explicit.  
- Originality: moderately novel—ground metric learning for OT is known, but applying it to pretrained embeddings with joint global weighting and NCA loss is a useful combination.  
- Significance: high practical significance given WMD’s popularity and demonstrated empirical/efficiency gains.  
- Suggestions: standardize notation, define K_A, state Danskin’s use, specify constraints/regularizers for w/A, add λ/N/batch sensitivity analyses, provide ablations, broaden evaluation, and fix typos.  
- Recommendation: Accept (weak accept).  
- Confidence: high in assessment of methodology and empirical claims; medium on the dual-potential approximation details.
