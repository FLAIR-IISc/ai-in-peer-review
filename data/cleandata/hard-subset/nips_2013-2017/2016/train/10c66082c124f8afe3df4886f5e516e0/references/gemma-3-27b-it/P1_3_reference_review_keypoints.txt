- Paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transform of embeddings and re-weights word frequencies, using Sinkhorn approximation and probabilistic filtering to minimize a stochastic leave-one-out classification error.
- Builds on WMD (Kusner et al.), efficient optimal transport (Cuturi), and metric learning ideas (e.g., NCA), adapting them to operate in embedding space without dimensionality reduction.
- Novelty: first to apply supervised learning directly to WMD, combining embedding transformation and word-frequency re-weighting.
- Technical soundness: uses Sinkhorn distance for efficiency, provides gradient derivations, and sensible initialization from S-WCD.
- Empirical results: extensive evaluation on 8 datasets versus 26 baselines shows S-WMD outperforms competitors; ablation study supports full model benefits.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, consistent notation, clear algorithmic description.
- Significance: improvements in document distance measures have broad NLP impact (IR, classification, recommendations).
- Efficient implementation: uses batch gradient descent and practical approximations to reduce computation.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks detailed reporting on ranges tried and computational cost of Bayesian tuning.
- Computational complexity: despite improvements, remaining complexity (e.g., O(q^2) Sinkhorn) and added cost of learning A and w are substantial and under-analyzed.
- Limited error analysis: paper shows accuracy gains but lacks analysis of where S-WMD succeeds or fails across document types/tasks.
- Word-weight interpretation: learned weights claimed to reflect class importance, but visualization limited to only 20NEWS dataset.
- Embedding comparison limited: experiments use word2vec; performance with modern embeddings (BERT/RoBERTa) is not evaluated.
- For acceptance: presents a novel, technically sound approach with strong experimental support and potential high impact.
- For rejection concerns: computational scalability and missing detailed hyperparameter/error analyses weaken conclusions.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution that effectively incorporates supervision into WMD and shows impressive empirical performance.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revision 1: provide detailed hyperparameter tuning ranges and computational cost of tuning.
- Requested revision 2: include more detailed runtime analysis across document and vocabulary sizes.
- Requested revision 3: add discussion of limitations, failure cases, and error analysis.
- Requested revision 4: expand learned word-weight visualizations/analyses to additional datasets.
- Requested revision 5: consider experiments with more recent word embeddings.
