## Review of "Supervised Word Mover’s Distance"

**Summary:**

This paper presents Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a novel approach to learning a metric for improving the Word Mover's Distance (WMD). The core idea is to learn a linear transformation of word embeddings *and* re-weight word frequencies simultaneously, tailored to a specific classification task. The authors address the computational challenges of incorporating supervision into WMD by leveraging the Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient relaxation of the optimal transport problem, combined with probabilistic filtering of the training set.  Extensive experiments on eight datasets demonstrate the superiority of S-WMD over a wide range of baselines, including WMD and various metric learning approaches.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds directly upon the foundational work of Kusner et al. (2017) on WMD, acknowledging its strength in leveraging pre-trained word embeddings. It appropriately positions itself within the broader context of metric learning (Yang & Jin, 2017), specifically drawing inspiration from Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) but extends it to operate within the word embedding space and incorporates word re-weighting. The authors also correctly acknowledge related work in efficient optimal transport computation (Cuturi, 2013) and supervised word embedding learning (Tang et al., 2014). They thoughtfully address how their approach differs from simultaneous ground distance learning (Wang & Guibas, 2017; Wang et al., 2018).

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The combination of learning a transformation of the word embeddings *and* re-weighting word frequencies within the WMD framework is a genuinely novel contribution. It goes beyond simply learning a metric on document representations and directly manipulates the underlying word space to improve document distance calculation.
* **Strong Empirical Results:** The experimental results are compelling.  S-WMD consistently outperforms a very large number of baselines across diverse datasets. The average rank across datasets is particularly convincing.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The authors intelligently address the computational bottleneck of supervised WMD by utilizing the Sinkhorn distance and probabilistic filtering.  The reported training times are reasonable, suggesting the method is practical.
* **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the limitations of unsupervised document distance metrics and motivates the need for a supervised approach tailored to specific tasks.
* **Detailed Analysis:** The visualization using t-SNE and the analysis of learned word weights provide valuable insights into the behavior of S-WMD.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is generally well-written, clearly organized, and logically structured. The background section provides sufficient context for understanding the core concepts.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Sensitivity to λ:** While the authors mention tuning λ (the regularization parameter in the Sinkhorn distance), the sensitivity analysis feels limited.  A more thorough exploration of the impact of λ on performance, and perhaps a principled method for choosing it (e.g., cross-validation) would strengthen the work.  Is the choice of λ=10 generalizable or dataset-specific?
* **Squared Euclidean Distance:**  The use of squared Euclidean distance within the WMD calculation feels somewhat arbitrary. While justified, exploring alternative distance metrics (e.g., cosine similarity) could potentially improve performance.
* **Batch Size & Nearest Neighbor Selection:** The choice of batch size (B=32) and number of nearest neighbors (N=200) seems empirically determined.  A more systematic investigation into the impact of these hyperparameters could be beneficial.
* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the authors present the gradients and optimization process, a deeper theoretical understanding of the convergence properties of the algorithm would be valuable.  Is the optimization landscape convex or non-convex?
* **Initialization Dependence:** The paper acknowledges the importance of initialization and describes a reasonable initialization strategy (S-WCD). However, a more comprehensive comparison of different initialization methods could be included.  How much does performance depend on the initial NCA step?



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to supervised document distance learning.
* The empirical results are strong and demonstrate a clear improvement over existing methods.
* The computational challenges are addressed effectively.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the core concepts.
* It has the potential to significantly impact research in text retrieval, document classification, and related fields.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The sensitivity analysis of the regularization parameter (λ) is limited.
* The choice of the squared Euclidean distance could be further justified.
* The theoretical analysis could be more in-depth.
* The hyperparameter choices of batch size and nearest neighbor selection aren't rigorously justified.




**Overall Assessment:**

I strongly recommend accepting this paper. Despite minor weaknesses, the novelty, strong empirical results, and clear presentation make it a significant contribution to the field. The authors have addressed a challenging problem and proposed a practical and effective solution. The insights gained from learning word-specific importance weights and adapting the word embedding space are particularly valuable.  Addressing the minor weaknesses mentioned above (particularly a more thorough analysis of λ and initialization methods) would further strengthen the paper.




**Confidence:** 9/10
