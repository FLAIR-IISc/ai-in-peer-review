- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD learning a linear embedding transform (A) and word importance weights (w), using Sinkhorn approximation for tractability.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines; S-WMD achieves the lowest average rank and best kNN error on 5/8 datasets.  
- Strength: strong empirical evaluation with diverse datasets and many baselines.  
- Strength: addresses computational costs via Sinkhorn distance, batch gradient descent, and neighbor filtering.  
- Strength: uses S-WCD initialization to improve convergence and provide a meaningful baseline.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance testing to validate claimed improvements.  
- Weakness: insufficient reporting of absolute error rates and magnitudes of improvement over second-best methods.  
- Weakness: theoretical gaps regarding existence of gradients and lack of conditions or practical subgradient treatment.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of limitations, scalability to very large datasets, and comparison to deep learning approaches.  
- Clarity strength: well-organized paper, consistent notation, and clear pseudocode (Algorithm 1).  
- Clarity weakness: unproven claim that WCD lower-bounds WMD and unclear distinction/relationship between S-WCD and standard WCD.  
- Clarity weakness: derivation of gradient w.r.t. w (Eq.10) and dual variable explanation need more detail.  
- Originality strength: novel supervised extension of WMD combining embedding transform and histogram re-weighting.  
- Originality weakness: insufficient differentiation from existing metric learning methods and limited comparison to closely related work (Wang et al. [35], supervised embedding literature).  
- Significance strength: provides a practical, efficient way to inject supervision into WMD with empirical gains over unsupervised WMD.  
- Significance weakness: unclear practical impact relative to state-of-the-art deep learning/document classification models.  
- Overall assessment: promising and well-motivated contribution but requires substantial revisions.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision.  
- Specific requested revisions: add statistical tests, report full error rates, expand theoretical analysis on gradients, clarify S-WCD vs WCD, compare thoroughly to Wang et al. and supervised embedding work, discuss limitations/scalability, and add interpretability discussion for learned weights.  
- Final thought: valuable contribution that needs more rigorous evaluation and clearer exposition to be publication-ready.
