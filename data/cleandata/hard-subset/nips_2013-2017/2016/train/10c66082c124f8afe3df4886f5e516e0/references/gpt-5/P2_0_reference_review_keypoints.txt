- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform A and per-word importance weights w, using a supervised ground cost in entropy-regularized OT for document kNN classification.  
- Uses an NCA-style stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss and Sinkhorn relaxation for differentiable, efficient training (gradients via transport plan and dual potentials).  
- Adds practical speedups: WCD pruning for candidate neighbors, minibatching, and initialization from supervised WCD (S-WCD).  
- Builds on WMD, Sinkhorn OT, and NCA; extends prior supervised EMD/OT work by learning word-importance and exploiting low-dimensional word embeddings.  
- Technical formulation is sound: PSD ground cost via A^T A and standard entropic OT gradient derivations.  
- Main approximation caveat: objective uses unregularized distance while optimizing with entropic Sinkhorn solutions, lacking theoretical guarantees or analysis of bias from finite λ.  
- Empirically strong: evaluated on 8 datasets with many baselines, S-WMD wins 5/8 and is competitive elsewhere; hyperparameters tuned with Bayesian optimization.  
- Ablation and sensitivity analyses are limited: unclear separate contributions of A vs w, sensitivity to λ, neighbor set size, and initialization effects.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-structured, with good motivation for learning in word space rather than document space.  
- Some presentation issues: missing section number, typos, incomplete references, terse derivation of dual potentials, and unclear constraints/regularization on w.  
- Reproducibility aided by released code but would benefit from more implementation details (exact λ, stopping criteria, constraints on w, regularization on A/w).  
- Novelty: joint supervised learning of OT ground metric and per-word reweighting in embedding space is a novel and pragmatic contribution.  
- Practical significance: modular, efficient supervised distance with strong kNN and retrieval potential; likely adoptable and extendable in NLP.  
- Limitations: evaluation focused on kNN without comparisons to modern supervised classifiers or end-to-end encoders; scalability to very long documents/large corpora remains a concern.  
- Pros: well-motivated problem, efficient differentiable training with entropic OT, novel joint learning of A and w, strong empirical results, practical initialization/training and released code.  
- Cons: limited theoretical analysis of the Sinkhorn surrogate and λ sensitivity, unclear regularization/constraints on w, limited ablations, and minor clarity/citation issues.  
- Suggestions: add ablations (A-only, w-only, both), λ sensitivity studies, neighbor-set experiments, clarify/regularize w constraints, detail dual-potential derivation, and report retrieval/runtime scalability.  
- Recommendation: leaning toward acceptance — solid, practically impactful work that would be strengthened by addressing clarity and analysis issues.
