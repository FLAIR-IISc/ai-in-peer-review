- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear embedding transform (A) and word importance weights (w) to supervise WMD for classification.  
- Optimizes kNN leave-one-out classification error under the WMD metric.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance approximation to make transport computation efficient.  
- Evaluated on 8 diverse datasets against 26 baselines, outperforming on 5/8 and achieving the best average performance.  
- Provides solid theoretical foundations with clear gradient derivations for A and w.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, including ablation studies and useful visualizations showing improved class clustering.  
- Training is efficient (batch gradient descent, neighborhood filtering) and practical for real-world use.  
- Original contribution: first to incorporate supervised metric learning and histogram re-weighting directly into the WMD framework.  
- Acknowledges limitations, notably underperformance on the CLASSIC dataset attributed to poor initialization and squared Euclidean distance.  
- Choice of squared Euclidean distance for word vectors is not well-justified; other metrics might be more appropriate.  
- Limited analysis on interpretability of learned word importance weights beyond visual inspection.  
- Lacks comparison to supervised approaches using modern contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT).  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, novel, well-evaluated, with minor addressable weaknesses.
