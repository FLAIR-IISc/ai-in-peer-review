- Proposes S-WMD: supervised WMD learning a linear transform A and per-word importance weights w via an NCA kNN loss.  
- Uses entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to obtain unique transport plans and dual potentials for efficient O(q^2) computation and approximate gradients.  
- Introduces S-WCD initialization (NCA on word centroids) and WCD-based neighbor pruning during training.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets versus 26 baselines; achieves lowest kNN error on 5/8 datasets and best average rank.  
- Builds on WMD, entropic OT, and NCA; novel combination of supervised OT ground metric learning and histogram reweighting for text.  
- Clear motivation: WMD is unsupervised and task-specific similarity should be learned.  
- Methodologically sound: NCA loss on pairwise OT distances and using entropic OT for tractable gradients is principled.  
- Practical engineering: effective initialization/pruning, complexity analysis, and released code.  
- Empirical breadth and qualitative visualizations strengthen the results.  
- Key ambiguity: loss in Eq. (6) is written with unregularized WMD but gradients use entropic OT—unclear whether training optimizes regularized cost or uses Sinkhorn only for gradient approximation.  
- Request clarification on which distance is used during training and testing and on the approximation error between regularized and unregularized objectives.  
- Implementation detail: K in Sinkhorn depends on A; unclear if K is recomputed per pair and per iteration and how this affects runtime.  
- Constraints on w: unclear if w is enforced nonnegative (e.g., softplus) or regularized to prevent peaky/unstable histograms.  
- Constraints/regularization on A: unclear whether A is constrained/parameterized (e.g., low-rank L with r<d) or regularized, and how r is chosen.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: λ, batch size B, neighbor count N, and r require sensitivity analyses; fixed λ=10 may be brittle.  
- Ablation requests: effects of only A vs only w, with/without S-WCD init, with/without neighbor pruning.  
- Baseline fairness: LDA trained transductively (train+test) is unusual—clarify if other methods had similar access.  
- Test-time efficiency: unclear whether WCD pruning is used at test time and what the end-to-end query time is for kNN.  
- Theoretical concern: need discussion of when entropic dual potentials give unbiased/sensible gradients for w and limitations of the entropic approximation.  
- Clarity/typos: several minor issues—missing/incorrect citations, section numbering errors, stray file path, and inconsistent notation; proofreading needed.  
- Suggest adding pseudocode explicitly stating whether regularized distances are used in training/testing and an ablation/sensitivity figure for reproducibility.  
- Overall quality: technically reasonable with supportive empirical results, but important implementation choices are under-specified.  
- Originality: not entirely new to learn OT ground metrics, but novel application to WMD with joint histogram reweighting and Sinkhorn-based gradients for text.  
- Significance: valuable and likely to be adopted given popularity of WMD; shows gains across diverse datasets.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled, efficient method; strong average empirical performance; reusable engineering and open-source code.  
- Cons for acceptance: ambiguity about exact objective and approximation gap; missing constraint/regularization details and limited ablations; clarity issues.  
- Questions for authors: Are w constrained nonnegative or regularized? Do you optimize/evaluate with entropic OT or use Sinkhorn only for gradients? Is K recomputed per iteration as A changes? Do you use neighbor pruning at test time and what is query time? Please provide ablations (only A vs only w; with/without S-WCD; sensitivity to λ and r).  
- Recommendation: Weak accept (high reviewer confidence).
