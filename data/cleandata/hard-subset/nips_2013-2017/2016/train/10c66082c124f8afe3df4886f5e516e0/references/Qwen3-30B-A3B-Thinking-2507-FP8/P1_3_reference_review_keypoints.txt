- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform and word-specific weights to make WMD task-specific for classification.  
- Uses Sinkhorn (entropy-regularized optimal transport) to enable efficient gradient computation and practical training.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across 8 datasets and 26 baselines in kNN classification.  
- Solid technical contribution: novel combination of supervised metric learning with optimal transport and practical optimization.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: diverse datasets, many baselines (including supervised variants) and useful visualizations and pseudo-code.  
- Practical impact: training times comparable to or better than baseline metric-learning methods, making S-WMD usable in practice.  
- Clear exposition of methodology, optimization, and implementation details.  
- Lacks sufficient analysis of failure cases where S-WMD underperforms (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON).  
- Insufficient justification for key design choices (use of squared Euclidean distance, interpretation of learned word weights).  
- Initialization heuristic (NCA on WCD) is not well motivated theoretically.  
- Limited discussion of scalability and constraints (vocabulary size, document length, embedding space).  
- Missing comparison to recent contextual/transformer-based supervised text representation methods (e.g., BERT).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-evaluated, and a valuable contribution; recommendation: accept.
