- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD learning a linear transform A on embeddings and per-word importance vector w, optimized with an NCA-style kNN loss.
- Uses entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) and its scaling vectors to obtain approximate dual variables for gradients w.r.t. A and w.
- Initializes via NCA on word centroids (S-WCD) and speeds training with mini-batches and WCD neighbor filtering.
- Empirical evaluation on 8 datasets against 26 baselines shows best kNN accuracy on 5/8 datasets and best average rank.
- Builds on prior work: WMD, Sinkhorn OT, and supervised metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML); novel application to text with joint histogram reweighting.
- Technical soundness: formulation is clear and using Sinkhorn for differentiability and speed is appropriate.
- Gradient for A follows standard OT results; gradient for w via Sinkhorn duals is reasonable but approximate.
- Empirical support is broad and careful, with many baselines, hyperparameter tuning, visualizations, and timing analyses.
- Missing ablation comparing contributions of A-only vs w-only vs full model to identify which component drives gains.
- No analysis of approximation quality or sensitivity for the Sinkhorn regularization λ beyond a single setting (λ=10).
- Potential unfairness in baselines: PCA reduction to 200 dims “if necessary” may disadvantage some supervised baselines; LDA trained transductively.
- No statistical significance tests reported; only some standard errors across splits.
- Convergence properties, regularization, and explicit constraints on w (e.g., positivity) are not discussed and could allow degeneracies.
- Paper is generally well written with useful pseudocode and complexity discussion; minor typos and notation glitches present.
- The link between Sinkhorn scaling and dual potentials could use a short derivation or explicit citation (e.g., Cuturi & Peyré).
- Reproducibility: code is available, but some hyperparameters and the k used in kNN are not fully specified.
- Originality: combination of supervised ground-metric learning for OT in text, joint per-word reweighting, and NCA objective is novel.
- Significance: promising and potentially impactful extension of WMD, though evaluated only on kNN tasks and scalability to very large corpora/documents remains a concern.
- Suggested experiments: clarify constraints on w, provide A-only/w-only ablation, compare learned w to IDF, and report sensitivity to λ and neighbor set size N.
- Suggested clarifications: state how k in kNN was chosen/tuned and ensure supervised baselines are comparably tuned (including PCA choices).
- Consider discussing or testing non-linear transforms and overfitting control for the learned metric.
- Pros: principled OT+NCA formulation, efficient gradient trick via Sinkhorn, pragmatic training heuristics, strong empirical results, and open-source code.
- Cons: missing ablations and sensitivity analyses, limited discussion on w regularization and potential degeneracies, evaluation confined to kNN, and minor clarity issues.
- Overall recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on addressing empirical ablations and clarifications.
