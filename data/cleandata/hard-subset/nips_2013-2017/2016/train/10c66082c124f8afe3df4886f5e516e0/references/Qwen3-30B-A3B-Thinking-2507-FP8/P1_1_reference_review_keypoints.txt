- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear embedding transform A and word importance weights w for supervised document distances.  
- Incorporates supervision into WMD by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out classification loss.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance to efficiently approximate the optimal transport problem and make computation feasible.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art on 5 of 8 datasets.  
- Builds on Kusner et al. (WMD) and leverages metric learning ideas similar to NCA.  

- Technically sound with clear mathematical formulations and gradient derivations.  
- Comprehensive experimental design with diverse datasets and multiple baselines.  
- Detailed ablation study showing the benefit of the supervised component over initialization.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations and analyze failure cases for some datasets.  
- Well-organized writing, consistent notation, clear experimental setup, and useful pseudo-code.  
- Visualization of learned word weights effectively shows discriminative term identification.  
- Novel idea: adding supervision to WMD and combining optimal transport with metric learning.  
- Practical computational efficiency demonstrated (Table 3), making the method usable in practice.  
- Potential applicability beyond document classification to other text-processing tasks.  

- Insufficient exploration of the Sinkhorn regularization hyperparameter λ and its effect on performance.  
- Limited analysis of why S-WMD underperforms on BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, and AMAZON datasets.  
- Lacks detailed empirical comparison of computational complexity with other metric learning methods.  
- Initialization (Section 3.2) and the connection between WCD and NCA are not explained clearly.  
- Relationship between relaxed dual variables and the dual problem needs clearer exposition.  
- More intuition needed for why Sinkhorn relaxation is appropriate for supervised WMD.  
- Not fully differentiated from Wang et al. (2015) on supervised EMD learning; comparison is sparse.  
- Does not explore extensions to contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT) or other embedding types.  
- Absolute error differences on the three underperformed datasets are not quantified in sufficient detail.  
- Practical implications for large-scale document classification beyond experiments are not discussed.  

- Recommendation: Accept — strong, novel contribution with solid empirical and theoretical support.  

- Arguments for acceptance: novel supervised integration into WMD, rigorous evaluation, theoretical soundness, computational efficiency, and insightful visualizations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited λ analysis, unclear initialization details, and insufficient differentiation from prior supervised EMD work.  

- Specific suggestion: add a small analysis of accuracy vs. λ for a representative dataset.  
- Specific suggestion: expand discussion of underperformance on BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, and AMAZON, comparing to initialization.  
- Specific suggestion: explicitly contrast with Wang et al. (2015), emphasizing histogram re-weighting differences.  
- Specific suggestion: discuss potential generalization to contextual embeddings (e.g., BERT) as future work.  
- Specific suggestion: clarify initialization in Section 3.2 and justify learning A via NCA on WCD.  

- Overall assessment: valuable, well-written, and technically strong paper that meaningfully advances supervised metric learning for document representation and is suitable for NIPS.
