- Proposes S-WMD: jointly learn a linear transform of word embeddings and re-weight word frequencies to improve Word Mover’s Distance for classification tasks.
- Uses Sinkhorn distance as a computationally efficient relaxation of optimal transport, plus probabilistic filtering to scale supervised WMD learning.
- Evaluated on eight datasets, showing consistent superiority and strong average rank versus many baselines.
- Builds on Kusner et al. (2017) WMD and extends metric learning (NCA-style) to the word space with word re-weighting.
- Appropriately situates itself among work on efficient OT (Cuturi) and supervised embedding learning; differs from ground-distance learning approaches.
- Novel contribution: simultaneously learning embedding transformation and word-frequency weights within WMD is new and impactful.
- Strong empirical results across diverse datasets provide convincing evidence of effectiveness.
- Computational efficiency is addressed well; reported training times indicate practicality.
- Clear motivation, problem framing, and informative analyses (t-SNE visualizations and learned word weight inspection).
- Paper is well-written, organized, and presents sufficient background for readers.
- Sensitivity to the Sinkhorn regularization λ is under-explored; need more analysis and principled selection strategy.
- Use of squared Euclidean distance in WMD feels arbitrary; alternatives like cosine similarity should be examined.
- Hyperparameters (batch size B=32, nearest neighbors N=200) are empirically chosen without systematic investigation.
- Limited theoretical analysis on optimization landscape and convergence; unclear convexity/non-convexity properties.
- Dependence on initialization (S-WCD/NCA) is acknowledged but requires broader comparison and assessment of impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical gains, practical scalability, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited λ sensitivity study, justification for squared Euclidean distance, shallow theoretical treatment, and unexamined hyperparameter choices.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—minor weaknesses do not outweigh novelty and empirical strength.
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
