- Proposal: Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform of embeddings and word importance weights to align WMD with class labels using a Sinkhorn approximation and NCA-style loss.  
- Strong empirical gains: Supervision consistently improves WMD performance across 8 datasets and vs. 26 baselines, especially on smaller-document tasks.  
- Practical contribution: Addresses the limitation of unsupervised WMD for task-specific classification and delivers a usable supervised distance metric.  
- Computational efficiency: Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance and batch gradient updates with nearest-neighbor filtering to make training practical.  
- Comprehensive evaluation: Thorough experiments across diverse datasets and many baselines demonstrate robust improvements.  
- Clear technical development: Provides detailed gradient derivations and a reasonable initialization strategy (S-WCD).  
- Theoretical gap: Lacks analysis of when and why supervision helps over unsupervised WMD and no statistical significance testing of claims.  
- Related work clarity: Insufficient differentiation from Wang et al. [35], especially regarding ground distance and use of pre-trained embeddings.  
- Limited interpretability: Analysis of learned word importance weights is superficial and limited to 20NEWS.  
- Overfitting risk: Does not address overfitting concerns or fully describe validation procedures on small datasets.  
- Complexity claims under-supported: Training-time and complexity comparisons to baselines are claimed but not rigorously quantified.  
- Overall assessment: Valuable and well-executed practical extension with strong empirical results but limited theoretical novelty.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Required revision 1: Add a brief theoretical analysis of conditions where supervision improves WMD.  
- Required revision 2: Expand related work to clearly differentiate from Wang et al. [35], focusing on embedding-based ground distance.  
- Required revision 3: Include statistical significance testing for main results (e.g., paired tests).  
- Required revision 4: Provide deeper analysis of learned word importance weights across multiple datasets.  
- Required revision 5: Clarify and quantify computational complexity comparisons with baseline methods.
