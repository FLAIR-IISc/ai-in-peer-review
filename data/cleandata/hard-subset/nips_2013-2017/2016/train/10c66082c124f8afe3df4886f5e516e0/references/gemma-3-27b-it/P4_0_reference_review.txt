The paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method for learning a metric to improve the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) by learning a linear transformation of word representations and re-weighted word frequencies. The core idea is to minimize a stochastic leave-one-out classification error under the WMD metric, utilizing a relaxed version of the optimal transport problem (Sinkhorn distance) for computational efficiency. The authors demonstrate superior performance across 8 datasets and 26 baselines.

This work builds upon the existing literature in document distance measures (e.g., BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA) and the recent advancements in WMD by Kusner et al. (2016). It also leverages the field of metric learning, citing several prominent algorithms like NCA, LMNN, and ITML. The paper positions itself as a novel approach by incorporating supervision directly into the WMD framework, which previously relied entirely on unsupervised methods. The use of the Sinkhorn distance to accelerate computation is inspired by Cuturi (2013).

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Combining supervised metric learning with the Word Mover’s Distance is a novel contribution. Directly optimizing a document distance for a specific task, rather than relying on unsupervised pre-training, is a valuable idea.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The paper demonstrates consistently strong performance across a diverse set of datasets, outperforming a large number of competitive baselines. The ablation study (S-WMD init.) is helpful for understanding the contribution of the full training procedure.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The authors address the computational challenges of incorporating supervision into WMD by employing the Sinkhorn distance, a crucial optimization that makes the approach practical.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed method. The mathematical derivations are reasonably clear, and the algorithm is described with sufficient detail.
*   **Thorough Evaluation:** The authors evaluate their method against a comprehensive set of baselines and provide a detailed analysis of the results.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the paper provides a clear description of the algorithm and its implementation, the theoretical justification for the proposed method could be strengthened. Further insights into the properties of the learned metric and its relationship to the underlying data distribution would be valuable.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The dependency on Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning is mentioned, but a deeper discussion of the sensitivity of the results to these hyperparameters would be beneficial. Understanding which hyperparameters are most critical and why would improve the reproducibility and generalizability of the work.
*   **Initialization Dependence:** The S-WMD init performs reasonably well, suggesting that the initialization significantly affects final performance. Exploring the implications of various initializations and their impact on convergence and the learned metric would be valuable.
*   **Complexity of Implementation:** While the Sinkhorn distance addresses the computational bottleneck, the implementation may be complex and require significant engineering effort. This could limit its adoption by researchers without strong computational resources.
*   **Comparison to Related Work:** The related work section acknowledges Wang et al. (2017) but lacks a detailed comparison highlighting the specific differences and advantages of S-WMD. Expanding this discussion could further emphasize the contribution of the current work.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to supervised document distance learning.
*   The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods.
*   The computational challenges are addressed effectively with the use of the Sinkhorn distance.
*   The paper is clearly written and thoroughly evaluated.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical analysis is limited.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity and the importance of initialization are not fully explored.
*   The implementation complexity could pose a barrier to adoption.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of document distance learning. The proposed method is novel, the empirical results are compelling, and the computational challenges are addressed effectively. While there are some areas for improvement, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance.
