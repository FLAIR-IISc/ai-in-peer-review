Summary and relation to prior work
This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised variant of WMD that learns a task-specific ground metric and per-word importance weights to improve document distance for kNN classification. Concretely, the authors (1) learn a linear transformation A of the word embedding space and (2) learn a nonuniform word-importance vector w that reweights document histograms, so that the WMD distances better reflect label-based semantic similarity. They optimize a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective (à la NCA) using entropic-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) to obtain efficient and differentiable approximations for both the transport plan T and the dual potentials, enabling gradients with respect to A and w. They initialize A by running NCA on word centroids (S-WCD) and then perform batch training with candidate-neighbor filtering based on WCD.

The work builds on: WMD (Kusner et al., ICML’15), entropic OT/Sinkhorn distances (Cuturi, NIPS’13), and linear metric learning (NCA, LMNN, ITML). In vision, supervised EMD learning has been explored (e.g., Cuturi & Avis; Wang & Guibas; Wang et al. on ITML + EMD). This paper adapts those ideas to text with word embeddings, adds word reweighting, and addresses scalability with Sinkhorn and neighbor sampling.

On 8 datasets and 26 baselines (unsupervised methods, plus NCA/LMNN/ITML on various representations), S-WMD achieves lowest error on 5/8 datasets and the best average rank.

Quality
- Method: The optimization objective is sensible, and the use of the Sinkhorn relaxation and dual potentials to obtain gradients is appropriate. The gradient expressions for A are standard; the use of the relaxed dual (via log u, log v) for w is reasonable and makes the approach practical.
- Efficiency: The training scheme (initialization with S-WCD, minibatches, and WCD-based neighbor filtering) is thoughtful. The complexity discussion is helpful; reported times seem practical.
- Empirics: The evaluation is broad and includes strong baselines, with hyperparameters tuned via Bayesian optimization. Reporting average ranks and multiple splits for some datasets is appreciated.
- Concerns:
  - Constraints/regularization on w are underspecified. Since d̃ = (w ◦ d)/(wᵀd) must be nonnegative, one must enforce w ≥ 0 (e.g., via softplus/exponentials) or project onto the nonnegative orthant. The algorithm description does not specify this; unconstrained SGD could produce negative entries and invalid histograms.
  - No explicit regularization on A or w is described. Without norm, orthogonality, or trace constraints, A may overfit or collapse scales. Please clarify if any penalties or early stopping are used.
  - Sensitivity to λ (entropic regularization) is not explored; λ is fixed at 10. Given the approximation–variance trade-off, an ablation on λ would strengthen the paper.
  - Evaluation focuses on kNN classification. Given the motivation (retrieval/ranking), retrieval metrics (e.g., MAP, nDCG) and efficiency comparisons at query time would be valuable.
  - Ablations: It would help to quantify the contribution of A vs. w (A-only, w-only), and the benefit of the Sinkhorn-based gradient vs. a straight-through approximation.
  - Some derivational details: The notation in Eq. (1) mixes p-norm with a squared norm (‖·‖_p^2). Clarify whether the ground cost is squared Euclidean or Lp. The dual variable expressions for α, β from u, v could use a brief derivation or a citation to a standard form in entropic OT duality.

Clarity
The paper is generally well written and organized, with a clear motivation and method. However:
- There are minor typos/formatting issues (e.g., “.2 Optimization” likely Section 3.2; broken references “[5, 21?]”; truncated tables/figures in the provided text).
- Please state constraints (w ≥ 0) and any normalization/projection steps explicitly in Algorithm 1, and specify r (the learned dimensionality) and whether A is full rank or low rank.
- Clearly state whether WMD cost uses L2 or L2-squared and keep notation consistent.

Originality
The combination of (i) learning a ground metric in word embedding space for WMD, (ii) learning word-importance weights, and (iii) making it trainable and efficient via entropic OT and NCA is novel in the NLP setting. While related EMD metric learning exists in vision, the adaptation to text with embeddings and the specific optimization scheme are new and well motivated.

Significance
The approach yields consistent gains over strong baselines across multiple datasets and should be of practical interest for distance-based text classification and retrieval. The method is general (can use any embedding), efficient enough for medium-scale problems, and code availability improves impact. Demonstrating retrieval improvements and broader comparisons to contemporary neural document encoders (e.g., CNN/LSTM classifiers) would further elevate significance.

Pros
- Solid, well-motivated extension of WMD with supervision.
- Clever and practical use of Sinkhorn to enable end-to-end training.
- Broad empirical evaluation with competitive baselines and tuning.
- Code released.

Cons
- Missing explicit constraints/regularization for w and A; risk of invalid histograms/overfitting.
- Limited analysis of sensitivity (λ, neighbor set size N, batch size).
- Focused on kNN classification; lacks retrieval evaluation and comparisons to supervised neural baselines.
- Some notational/formatting issues and incomplete references.

Questions and suggestions
- How do you enforce w ≥ 0 during optimization? Do you project or reparameterize w = exp(θ)?
- Do you regularize A or w (e.g., Frobenius norm, column norm constraints, trace constraint)? Please add ablations on regularization strength.
- Provide sensitivity plots for λ, N, and r (projection dimension).
- Add ablations: A-only, w-only, and no Sinkhorn (exact OT on small subsets) to show each component’s effect.
- Include retrieval metrics and query-time latency comparisons (WMD vs. S-WMD) on at least one dataset.
- Compare to at least one supervised neural document classifier (e.g., fastText/CNN/LSTM) for context.
- Clarify the ground cost choice and fix notation in Eq. (1); add a brief derivation or citation for the dual potentials from u, v.

Recommendation
Weak accept. The paper presents a technically sound and practically useful supervised extension of WMD with thoughtful optimization and strong empirical results. Addressing the constraints/regularization for w and A, adding sensitivity/ablation studies, and clarifying notation would strengthen the submission further. Confidence: moderate-high.
