## Review of "Supervised Word Mover’s Distance"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method for learning a document distance metric by learning a linear transformation of word embeddings and re-weighting word frequencies. The core idea is to incorporate supervision directly into the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) framework, a previously unsupervised approach, to improve its performance for document classification. The authors address the computational challenges of supervised WMD by leveraging the Sinkhorn distance and probabilistic filtering techniques. They demonstrate strong empirical results on eight datasets, outperforming numerous baselines, including WMD itself, and providing a comprehensive experimental evaluation.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty & Significance:** The paper tackles an important problem - improving document distance metrics - and proposes a novel approach by directly incorporating supervision into WMD. This is a significant contribution as WMD was previously limited by its unsupervised nature. The experimental results demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods, establishing the significance of the work.
* **Technical Soundness:** The authors provide a solid technical foundation for S-WMD, grounding it in optimal transport theory and leveraging the efficient Sinkhorn distance approximation. The gradient computation details and optimization strategy, including initialization and batch gradient descent, are well-explained. The use of the KL-divergence loss and the stochastic neighborhood relaxation are appropriate choices for metric learning.
* **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The paper presents a very thorough experimental evaluation, comparing S-WMD against a large number of baselines (26 in total) across diverse datasets. The inclusion of both unsupervised and supervised baselines provides a strong point of comparison. The ablation studies (S-WMD init.) and visualization (t-SNE plots, word importance weights) further strengthen the analysis.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The background section effectively introduces the necessary concepts (word embeddings, WMD, metric learning).  The method section is clearly structured and the mathematical notation is understandable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Complexity of Implementation:** While the authors demonstrate efficient gradient computation, the overall implementation and tuning of S-WMD appears complex. The reliance on multiple hyperparameters (learning rates, regularization parameters, batch size, etc.) and the need for Bayesian optimization can make the method less accessible to practitioners.  While the training time is comparable to other methods, the initial setup and hyperparameter tuning can be substantial.
* **Sensitivity to Initialization:** The results suggest that initialization (S-WCD) significantly impacts performance. This dependence highlights a potential sensitivity of the method to the choice of initial word embeddings and the quality of the unsupervised WCD approximation. More analysis on the robustness of the initialization process would be beneficial.
* **Limited Discussion of Failure Cases:** While the results are strong overall, the paper doesn’t deeply investigate the datasets where S-WMD performs less well (e.g., BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON). Understanding *why* S-WMD struggles in these scenarios could provide valuable insights for future work.  The authors mention potential reasons, but a more thorough analysis would be appreciated.
* **Theoretical Justification:** While the technical approach is sound, the paper lacks a strong theoretical justification for *why* learning word importance weights improves performance. A more rigorous analysis of the benefits of re-weighting the histograms could further strengthen the contribution.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Significant improvement over state-of-the-art on a majority of datasets.
* Novel approach to incorporating supervision into the WMD framework.
* Technically sound and well-explained methodology.
* Comprehensive experimental evaluation.
* Addresses a challenging and important problem in document distance learning.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Implementation complexity and sensitivity to initialization.
* Limited analysis of failure cases.
* Lack of strong theoretical justification for the word re-weighting strategy.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a strong contribution to the field of document distance learning.  The proposed S-WMD method is novel, technically sound, and demonstrates significant empirical improvements over existing approaches. While there are some weaknesses regarding implementation complexity and theoretical justification, the overall strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. 

**Suggestions for Improvement:**

* Provide more detailed instructions or code examples to ease the implementation process.
* Conduct a more thorough analysis of the datasets where S-WMD underperforms, investigating the reasons for the reduced performance.
* Explore the theoretical benefits of learning word importance weights, potentially through analysis of the learned weights or by relating them to information-theoretic measures.
* Consider including a discussion of potential limitations and future research directions.




**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** 8/10 - Technically sound, well-supported by experiments, but lacks a strong theoretical foundation.
* **Clarity:** 8/10 - Generally well-written and organized, but the complexity of the method can be challenging to follow.
* **Originality:** 9/10 - Highly original approach to incorporating supervision into WMD.
* **Significance:** 8/10 - The results are important and have the potential to impact the field of document distance learning.
