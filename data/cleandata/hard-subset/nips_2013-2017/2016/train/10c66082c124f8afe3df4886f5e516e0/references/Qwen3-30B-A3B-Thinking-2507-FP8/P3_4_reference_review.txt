

# Peer Review: Supervised Word Mover's Distance

## Summary and Context
This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a novel approach to learn a metric that improves upon the unsupervised Word Mover's Distance (WMD) for document classification. Building on Kusner et al.'s WMD (ICML 2015), which computes document distances through optimal transport in word embedding space, the authors introduce two key innovations: (1) a linear transformation of word embeddings, and (2) word-specific importance weights to emphasize discriminative words. The paper leverages Sinkhorn distance for efficient computation and introduces a clever initialization via Supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD). The method is evaluated across 8 datasets against 26 baselines, with S-WMD showing state-of-the-art performance on 5/8 datasets.

## Strengths

The paper makes several technically sound contributions. First, it correctly identifies a significant gap: WMD is unsupervised and not optimized for specific classification tasks, whereas supervised metric learning has been highly successful in other domains. The authors' approach to learning a linear transformation of word embeddings while simultaneously learning word weights is well-motivated and theoretically grounded.

The computational efficiency is a major strength. By using the Sinkhorn approximation (Cuturi, 2013), the authors avoid the cubic complexity of exact WMD computations, making the method feasible for large-scale applications. The batch gradient descent approach with nearest-neighbor filtering (Section 3.2) further accelerates training, with reported training times comparable to baseline metric learning methods.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, covering 8 diverse datasets (news categorization, sentiment analysis, etc.) with comprehensive comparisons against 26 baselines. The paper correctly acknowledges that S-WMD doesn't outperform on all datasets (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON), demonstrating appropriate scientific honesty. The t-SNE visualizations (Figure 1) compellingly demonstrate how S-WMD improves document clustering compared to WMD.

## Weaknesses

While the paper makes a solid contribution to metric learning for text, it suffers from two critical weaknesses:

1. **Lack of meaningful comparison with modern deep learning approaches**: The paper compares against traditional document representations (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA) and metric learning methods (NCA, LMNN, ITML), but fails to compare against state-of-the-art deep learning methods for text classification (e.g., BERT, ELMo, or recent transformer-based approaches). Given that these have largely surpassed traditional metric learning methods in NLP, this omission significantly weakens the paper's significance claim. The paper should acknowledge that while S-WMD is competitive with traditional methods, it may not be competitive with the current state-of-the-art in NLP.

2. **Insufficient differentiation from Wang et al. (2015)**: The paper cites Wang et al. [35] as "most similar" to their work but fails to adequately distinguish their contribution. Wang et al. learn a metric within a generalized Euclidean EMD ground distance using ITML, while the authors' key innovation is the histogram re-weighting (Eq. 4). The paper should explicitly clarify how the histogram re-weighting provides significant additional flexibility over Wang et al.'s approach, and why this matters for document classification.

The paper also makes an unexplained choice to use squared Euclidean distance in Eq. (5) rather than the Manhattan distance used in WMD (p=1). While the authors note that the squared distance "may be suboptimal" for CLASSIC, they don't provide a principled justification for this choice or explore alternative distances.

## Overall Assessment

**Quality**: The methodology is sound and well-executed. The experimental design is thorough, though the lack of statistical significance testing (p-values, confidence intervals) is a minor weakness.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-written and logically organized. The mathematical notation is consistent, and the algorithm is clearly described.

**Originality**: The core idea of combining supervised metric learning with WMD is novel, but the contribution is somewhat incremental given the strong relationship to Wang et al. [35]. The paper would be significantly stronger with a more detailed comparison to this prior work.

**Significance**: The work makes a valuable contribution to the metric learning literature for text, but its significance is limited by the lack of comparison to modern deep learning approaches. For NIPS, the paper would be significantly stronger if it demonstrated how S-WMD compares to or complements state-of-the-art neural text classification methods.

## Recommendation

This is a technically sound paper with solid methodology and thorough evaluation, but its significance is limited by two key omissions: (1) failure to compare with modern deep learning approaches for text classification, and (2) insufficient differentiation from Wang et al. [35]. I recommend **weak acceptance** with the following conditions:

1. The authors must add a comparison to at least two modern deep learning text classification approaches (e.g., BERT, ELMo) to properly assess the significance of their contribution.
2. The authors must substantially strengthen the comparison with Wang et al. [35] to clearly articulate the unique contributions of their histogram re-weighting approach.
3. The authors should justify their choice of squared Euclidean distance in Eq. (5) or explore alternative distances.

Without these revisions, the paper's claims about being "the best performing distance metric for documents to date" are unsubstantiated in the context of current NLP research. The paper would be significantly improved by positioning itself as a complementary technique to deep learning rather than a direct competitor.

The paper makes a valuable technical contribution to the metric learning literature, but its relevance to current NLP research needs strengthening before it can be considered for NIPS.
