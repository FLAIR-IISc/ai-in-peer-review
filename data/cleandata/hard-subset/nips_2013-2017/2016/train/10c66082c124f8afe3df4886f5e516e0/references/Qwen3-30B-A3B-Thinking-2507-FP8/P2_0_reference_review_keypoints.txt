- Proposal: S-WMD extends WMD by learning a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-specific importance weights, using Sinkhorn approximation for efficient training.
- Empirical result: Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art on 5 of 8 datasets.
- Technical soundness: Methodology and gradient derivations are carefully derived and computationally tractable.
- Computational advantage: Sinkhorn approximation makes training efficient with times comparable to or better than baseline metric learning methods.
- Reproducibility: Provides implementation details (e.g., λ=10, batch size 32, neighbor set N=200) and helpful pseudocode (Algorithm 1).
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized, with effective figures (e.g., t-SNE) and useful supplementary material.
- Originality: Novel combination of supervised metric learning with WMD plus histogram re-weighting (word importance), distinct from prior work.
- Significance: Substantial practical impact for document distance metrics and task-specific similarity; leverages unsupervised embeddings with supervision.
- Strength: Clear motivation and formulation, novel method, practical Sinkhorn approach, comprehensive experiments, strong empirical performance, excellent clarity.
- Weakness: No theoretical analysis of convergence or approximation guarantees for the learned metric or Sinkhorn approximation.
- Weakness: Limited sensitivity analysis for the Sinkhorn parameter λ (only λ=10 reported).
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation for underperformance on the CLASSIC dataset relative to WMD.
- Weakness: Limited exploration of alternative word embedding types (only word2vec used).
- Weakness: Initialization (NCA on WCD) described but its importance to performance not analyzed.
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, well-presented, and a meaningful contribution despite minor theoretical gaps.
- Final comment: Valuable, practical extension of WMD likely to influence future document distance methods; meets NIPS criteria at a high level.
