- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear ground-space transform A and per-word importance weights w.  
- Objective and training: Optimizes A and w with a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss using entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) for differentiable transport and dual potentials.  
- Practical tricks: Uses S-WCD initialization, mini-batching, and WCD-based neighbor pruning to speed and stabilize training; code is released.  
- Empirical results: Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines; achieves best average rank and lowest error on 5/8 datasets.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD and entropic OT literature; novel combination of learning ground metric and histogram weights specifically for text with pre-trained embeddings.  
- Strength: Clear, principled formulation that injects supervision into WMD while leveraging pre-trained embeddings.  
- Strength: Sound computational approach using Sinkhorn for efficient gradients and practical acceleration techniques.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical study with many baselines and Bayesian hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: Interpretability via learned word weights and t-SNE visualizations showing tighter class clusters.  
- Strength: Reproducibility supported by released code and methodological details.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether test-time kNN uses Sinkhorn-regularized distance or exact WMD with learned A,w; needs explicit statement and ablation.  
- Weakness: Missing constraints/regularization on w and A (e.g., nonnegativity for w, norm penalties) creating risk of degenerate solutions; sensitivity analysis needed.  
- Weakness: Gradient derivation for ∂/∂w via Sinkhorn duals is only sketched; a brief proof or citation would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness: Notation and clarity issues (overloaded p, section numbering, unspecified output dimension r) that should be fixed.  
- Weakness: Evaluation limited to kNN; lacks comparison to strong supervised neural classifiers and discussion of when distance-based methods are preferable.  
- Weakness: Limited ablations (sensitivity to λ, batch/neighbor sizes, learning w vs only A, ground metric choices) to attribute gains.  
- Weakness: Test-time kNN with OT distances remains expensive; more discussion of acceleration strategies for inference needed.  
- Quality: Technically sound overall; main gaps (constraints on w, dual-gradient detail) are addressable.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and well-organized but needs minor notation and detail fixes.  
- Originality and significance: Novel and meaningful adaptation of supervised OT learning for text that is likely useful for retrieval/kNN tasks.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — substantive contribution with solid empirical support, but needs fixes on theoretical/practical details and broader evaluation.
