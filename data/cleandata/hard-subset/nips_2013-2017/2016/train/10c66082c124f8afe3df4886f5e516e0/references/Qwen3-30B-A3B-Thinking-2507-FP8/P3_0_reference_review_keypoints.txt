- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, extending WMD with a learned linear transform A and word-specific weights w, optimized via a Sinkhorn-relaxed OT objective and evaluated on 8 datasets vs 26 baselines.  
- Claim: Authors report S-WMD outperforms all baselines on average and achieves SOTA on 5/8 datasets.  

- Technical soundness: Solid theoretical derivations, correct use of Sinkhorn relaxation, and clear gradient/optimization details.  
- Evaluation breadth: Thorough empirical study with 8 diverse datasets, 26 baselines, t-SNE visualizations, and analysis of learned word weights.  
- Practicality: Proposes batch gradient descent with neighbor filtering and provides a useful complexity analysis for practitioners.  

- No statistical tests: Paper reports standard errors but fails to perform formal significance testing to validate improvements.  
- Weak analysis of Sinkhorn relaxation: Limited theoretical investigation of how the relaxation and choice of λ affect learned metrics and convergence.  
- Unjustified design choices: No clear rationale for using squared Euclidean distance (vs. original Manhattan) or KL-divergence loss over other metric-learning losses.  
- Potential embedding distortion: Does not analyze whether linear transform A harms semantic structure of pre-trained embeddings.  

- Clarity and organization: Well-structured paper with a clear algorithm and effective visualizations.  
- Missing intuition and terminology clarity: Lacks intuitive explanations for key technical steps and does not clearly distinguish the word importance vector from traditional term-weighting schemes.  
- Weak related-work context: Fails to clearly differentiate S-WMD from closely related methods (e.g., Wang et al.) and to connect to supervised word-embedding literature.  

- Novelty: Novel combination of metric learning and optimal transport for text and introduction of word-specific importance weights within WMD.  
- Limited novelty concerns: Incremental relative to prior metric-learning (e.g., NCA) and insufficiently distinguished from Wang et al.; misses connections to related supervised embedding work.  

- Significance: Practical impact by improving document distances for classification, improved computational efficiency over exact WMD, and strong empirical wins on multiple datasets.  
- Limits of significance: Evaluation limited to small/medium datasets, lacks discussion of failure modes (long documents, huge vocabularies, word-order tasks), and unclear integration into broader NLP pipelines.  

- Suggested fixes: Add statistical significance tests, justify metric/loss choices, deepen analysis of Sinkhorn/λ and transform A, expand related work distinctions, and include a limitations section.  

- Recommendation: Weak reject — promising, well-executed extension but incremental novelty, missing statistical validation, and insufficient theoretical/related-work differentiation prevent acceptance.
