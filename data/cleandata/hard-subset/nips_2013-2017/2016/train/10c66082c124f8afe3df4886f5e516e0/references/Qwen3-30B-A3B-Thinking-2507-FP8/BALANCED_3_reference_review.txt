

# Review of "Supervised Word Mover's Distance"

## Summary
This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a novel method that learns a metric within the word embedding space to improve document classification performance. Building on Kusner et al.'s Word Mover's Distance (WMD) [19], the authors introduce two key innovations: (1) a linear transformation of word embeddings to better capture semantic relationships for classification tasks, and (2) word-specific importance weights to emphasize words that distinguish between document classes. The authors address computational challenges by using the Sinkhorn distance relaxation and probabilistic filtering, enabling efficient training. The method is evaluated across 8 document datasets against 26 baselines, demonstrating consistent improvements over unsupervised approaches and other metric learning methods.

## Strengths
**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with clear theoretical derivations for the gradient computations. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering 8 diverse datasets with thorough comparison against 26 baselines. The authors carefully analyze the results, noting both strengths (e.g., S-WMD outperforms all baselines on 5/8 datasets) and limitations (e.g., underperformance on CLASSIC dataset due to poor initialization). The paper provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, including implementation specifics and training time analysis.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-organized and clearly written. Mathematical notation is consistent and well-defined throughout. The authors effectively use figures (t-SNE visualizations, learned word weights) to illustrate key findings. The background section provides sufficient context for readers unfamiliar with WMD and metric learning.

**Originality:** The paper makes a meaningful contribution by bridging metric learning with the WMD framework, a novel combination that has not been previously explored. The authors clearly distinguish their work from related literature in Section 5, particularly noting how their approach differs from Wang et al. [35] by incorporating histogram re-weighting.

**Significance:** S-WMD achieves state-of-the-art results across multiple document classification tasks, demonstrating that supervision can significantly improve document distances. The method is efficient (training times comparable to baseline metric learning methods) and applicable to any word embedding. The insights about which words are most important for classification (Figure 2) provide valuable interpretability.

## Weaknesses
**Limited Analysis of Failure Cases:** While the paper acknowledges S-WMD underperforms on 3 datasets (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON), it lacks a thorough analysis of why these cases are challenging. A deeper investigation into these failure cases would strengthen the paper by providing more insight into the method's limitations.

**Restricted Word Embedding Exploration:** The paper exclusively uses word2vec embeddings without exploring how different embeddings (e.g., GloVe, FastText) might affect performance. This is a significant limitation since the quality of the base word embeddings can substantially impact WMD-based methods.

**Insufficient Parameter Analysis:** The choice of λ=10 for the Sinkhorn distance is made without justification or sensitivity analysis. A thorough exploration of how this parameter affects the trade-off between approximation accuracy and computational efficiency would be valuable.

**Lack of Scalability Discussion:** The paper doesn't address how S-WMD scales to very large document collections or its applicability to tasks beyond classification (e.g., retrieval or clustering).

## Suggestions for Improvement
1. Conduct a detailed analysis of the three datasets where S-WMD underperforms, identifying specific characteristics that make them challenging for the method.
2. Include experiments with alternative word embeddings (e.g., GloVe, FastText) to demonstrate the method's robustness across different embedding spaces.
3. Provide a sensitivity analysis for the λ parameter, showing how performance varies across different values.
4. Discuss potential extensions of S-WMD to other tasks beyond classification and analyze its scalability to larger datasets.

## Recommendation
This paper presents a strong, novel contribution to the field of document distances. The method is well-motivated, technically sound, and empirically validated across multiple datasets. While the paper has some limitations in scope and analysis, these are relatively minor compared to the significant contribution it makes to the literature. The method is both theoretically interesting and practically useful, with clear potential for impact in text classification tasks.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
- Novel combination of metric learning with WMD
- Comprehensive experimental validation across 8 datasets
- Strong empirical results demonstrating state-of-the-art performance
- Clear theoretical foundations and efficient implementation

**Arguments against acceptance:**
- Limited analysis of failure cases on certain datasets
- Exclusive use of word2vec embeddings without exploration of alternatives
- Lack of sensitivity analysis for key parameter λ

The paper meets all the quality criteria for NIPS. The contribution is significant, the methodology is sound, and the results are well-supported. With minor revisions addressing the weaknesses noted above, this paper would be an excellent fit for NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
