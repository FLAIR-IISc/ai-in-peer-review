- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transform of word embeddings and re-weights word frequencies to align WMD with class labels.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance relaxation and probabilistic filtering to enable efficient gradient computation and scalability.  
- Builds on WMD and metric learning literature, drawing inspiration from NCA and optimal transport work.  
- Novelty: learning the metric inside the WMD framework with word re-weighting rather than only learning document representations.  
- Technical soundness: Sinkhorn relaxation and gradient derivations are well-justified and appear correct.  
- Empirical performance: strong results across 8 datasets and 26 baselines, consistently outperforming state-of-the-art methods.  
- Computational efficiency: addresses cubic WMD complexity via efficient gradient approximation and complexity analysis.  
- Writing and organization: clear, logically structured, with sufficient background.  
- Initialization: S-WCD initialization helps with faster convergence and improved performance.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: BO was used but BO setup details and sensitivity analysis are lacking.  
- Limited error analysis: insufficient investigation of failure modes beyond overall kNN error; more per-class/length analysis needed.  
- Choice of squared Euclidean transport cost is somewhat arbitrary and not thoroughly evaluated against alternatives.  
- Dependence on pre-trained embeddings: impact of different embedding models (GloVe, FastText) not explored.  
- Lack of ablation study: need to isolate contributions of linear transform vs. word re-weighting.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically strong, and computationally feasible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited error/hyperparameter analysis, embedding dependence, and missing ablation.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, significant contribution to document similarity and metric learning; recommended for acceptance.  
- Suggestions: detail BO setup, perform deeper error analysis, test different embeddings, add ablation study, and discuss limitations/future work.
