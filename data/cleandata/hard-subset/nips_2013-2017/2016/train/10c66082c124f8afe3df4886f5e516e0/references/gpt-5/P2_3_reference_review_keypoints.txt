- Proposes S-WMD: learn a linear transform A on embeddings and word-importance weights w to adapt WMD for supervised document comparison using entropic OT (Sinkhorn) for differentiable training.
- Uses an NCA-style stochastic leave-one-out k-NN loss with Sinkhorn-relaxed WMD; gradients for A use the Sinkhorn plan, and gradients for w use approximate dual potentials from Sinkhorn scaling vectors.
- Training accelerations include WCD-based initialization for A, batch updates, and restricting pairwise computations to a WCD-based nearest-neighbor candidate set; code is released.
- Empirically evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving best average rank and top performance on 5/8 datasets, showing meaningful improvements.
- Technically sound: leverages known entropic OT properties; approximating WMD subgradients via Sinkhorn plan/duals is reasonable and yields a smooth surrogate.
- Experimental section is comprehensive with many baselines, initialization ablations, runtime comparisons, and qualitative visualizations.
- Under-specified constraints: unclear how nonnegativity of w is enforced and whether A has regularization (e.g., Frobenius norm or low-rank) to avoid degenerate solutions.
- Limited sensitivity and ablation analysis for λ, batch size, neighbor set size N, and separate contributions of A vs. w; dependence on WCD initialization not fully quantified.
- Evaluation is narrowly focused on k-NN accuracy; lacks retrieval metrics or demonstrations that S-WMD benefits other downstream tasks (e.g., linear classifiers).
- Minor technical clarity issues: typos/formatting, inconsistent notation for dimensions, and a complex/unclear computational complexity expression.
- Ambiguity about test-time distances: unclear whether k-NN uses exact WMD under learned A,w or the Sinkhorn approximation.
- Originality: builds on prior supervised EMD and OT work but novelty lies in combining Sinkhorn-based supervised learning of A and w for text, dual-based gradient for w, and WCD-neighborhood filtering.
- Significance: practically valuable for text applications of WMD and scientifically contributes a principled, trainable OT-based distance with useful training tricks.
- Pros: principled formulation, joint learning of metric and word weights, strong empirical results, training accelerations, and released code.
- Cons: unclear/unspecified regularization and constraints, limited sensitivity analyses, evaluation limited to k-NN, and a few presentation ambiguities.
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying constraints/regularization, test-time distance computation, and adding brief sensitivity/ablation analyses.
