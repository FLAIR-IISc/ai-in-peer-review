- Introduces S-WMD: learn task-specific ground metric (Mahalanobis A) and word-importance weights w to supervise WMD for classification.  
- Uses an NCA-inspired objective minimizing a soft kNN leave-one-out error to learn A and w.  
- Handles differentiation through optimal transport by using entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance for efficient gradients.  
- Strength: addresses a key limitation of unsupervised WMD with an elegant, practical method yielding strong empirical gains.  
- Strength: novel synthesis of WMD, metric learning, and regularized optimal transport.  
- Strength: thorough experiments on eight datasets vs 26 baselines, showing state-of-the-art or competitive performance.  
- Weakness: exposition/maths could be clearer—notation inconsistency between Euclidean distance in Eq.1 and squared Euclidean in Eq.6.  
- Suggestion: explicitly frame the distance as a p-Wasserstein metric and provide a more detailed algorithm box for reproducibility.  
- Related-work gap: cite histogram re-weighting work by Guy Lebanon and Le/Cuturi and better distinguish from prior ground-metric learning (e.g., Cuturi & Avis, Wang et al.).  
- Analysis gap: discuss interplay between unregularized EMD and entropy-regularized approximation in more depth.  
- Ablation needed: provide quantitative analysis showing the empirical contribution of the learned weight vector w beyond the visualization.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed contribution with addressable minor issues; recommendation to accept.
