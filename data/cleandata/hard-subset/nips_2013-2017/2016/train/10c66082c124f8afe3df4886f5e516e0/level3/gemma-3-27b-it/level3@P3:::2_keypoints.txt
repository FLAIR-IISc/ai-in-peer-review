- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD): a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns word importance weights and a linear transformation of embedding space.
- Training objective: minimize leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error to learn a discriminative document distance.
- Uses Sinkhorn (entropic OT) for efficient optimal transport and tractable gradient computation during training.
- Empirically strong: outperforms 26 baselines across eight datasets with substantial margins.
- Builds on WMD and metric learning, avoiding high-dimensional scalability issues by operating on word embeddings.
- Novelty: jointly learning transformed word embeddings and ground-cost importance weights, unlike prior EMD/ground-distance work.
- Extensive experimental evaluation including ablation ("S-WMD init") supporting the contributions.
- Clear presentation with derivations, complexity discussion, and helpful pseudocode.
- Practical considerations addressed: initialization strategies and batch gradient descent for scalability.
- Weakness: reliance on squared Euclidean distance in OT may limit generality; alternatives not explored.
- Weakness: hyperparameter tuning details and sensitivity analysis (ranges, robustness) are lacking.
- Weakness: limited error analysis—more analysis of failure cases would be valuable.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses WMD’s unsupervised limitation, technically novel, practical, and convincingly evaluated.
- Arguments against acceptance: possible limitation from squared Euclidean choice and insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity study.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, suitable for NIPS, reviewer recommends acceptance.
