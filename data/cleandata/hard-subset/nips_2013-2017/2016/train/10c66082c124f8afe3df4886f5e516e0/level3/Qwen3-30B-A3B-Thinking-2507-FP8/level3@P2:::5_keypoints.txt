- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns word-specific weights and a linear embedding transform to inject supervision into WMD.  
- Uses a Sinkhorn (entropic) approximation to the Wasserstein distance to enable efficient gradient-based optimization.  
- Evaluated on 8 text classification datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the lowest average error rate.  
- Mathematical derivations and gradient computations are sound and thoroughly presented.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with appropriate statistical reporting.  
- Major weakness: insufficient discussion and ablation of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter lambda; choice of lambda=10 is unjustified.  
- Clarity is high: well-written, clear exposition, logical algorithm presentation, and supportive figures (t-SNE, word weights).  
- Confusing/misleading treatment of Word Centroid Distance (WCD) as a baseline rather than a simple WMD approximation.  
- Originality is limited: using Sinkhorn is not novel and the paper does not sufficiently distinguish from prior metric-learning/EMD works (e.g., Wang et al.).  
- Fails to clearly articulate how S-WMD’s mathematical properties differ from unsupervised WMD, particularly regarding the entropic regularizer.  
- Significance: strong empirical gains and efficient training make a meaningful practical contribution.  
- Practical impact is tempered by lack of principled guidance for selecting lambda across datasets.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Pros: strong empirical results, clear exposition, efficient Sinkhorn-based implementation, outperforms many baselines.  
- Cons: limited novelty, inadequate analysis of lambda’s effects, confusing WCD references, insufficient differentiation from similar metric-learning approaches.
