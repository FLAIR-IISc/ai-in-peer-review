- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension to Word Mover's Distance that learns an affine transform of word embeddings plus a word-importance weight vector.  
- Metric is learned by minimizing stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification error at the per-document level.  
- Introduces an efficient approximation for the gradient of WMD to avoid an expensive nested optimization.  
- Method is sound, clearly explained, and well-related to prior work.  
- Extensive experiments on eight real-world text classification tasks against 26 baselines.  
- S-WMD consistently outperforms almost all baselines and achieves lowest kNN test error on 5 of 8 datasets.  
- t-SNE visualizations show improved class clustering under S-WMD.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and presents results clearly and concisely.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the learned word-importance weights and their effect on performance.  
- Weakness: insufficient insight/discussion about hyperparameter choices and their impact.  
- Argument for acceptance: significant empirical improvement over WMD and strong experimental evidence.  
- Argument against acceptance: the same gaps in analysis (word weights and hyperparameter sensitivity) limit interpretability/insight.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
