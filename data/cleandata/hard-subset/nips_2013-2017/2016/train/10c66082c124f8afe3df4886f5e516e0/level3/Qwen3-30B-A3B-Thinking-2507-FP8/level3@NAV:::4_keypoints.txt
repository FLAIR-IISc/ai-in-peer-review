- Proposal: S-WMD extends WMD by learning a linear transform A and word-importance weights w, optimized with a stochastic leave-one-out (NCA-style) objective and approximated via Sinkhorn.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and situates itself thoroughly within related metric-learning and WMD literature.  
- Empirical strength: Extensive experiments on eight datasets show consistent improvements over 26 baselines, demonstrating strong empirical performance.  
- Weak motivation for w: The rationale for reweighting word distributions is weak and poorly explained; reweighting may fundamentally alter document semantics.  
- Semantic preservation not justified: The paper fails to justify how reweighting improves label separation without distorting true semantic meaning.  
- Limited novelty: Core contribution—applying a linear transform and reweighting with an NCA objective—appears incremental relative to existing metric-learning work.  
- Practical gap: The method does not address how to compute or infer w for unseen documents, a critical deployment limitation.  
- Terminology problem: Use of “semantic difference” is ambiguous and conflates label/topic similarity with semantic meaning, undermining claims of preserving semantics.  
- Overall verdict: Technical execution and experiments are solid, but limited novelty and unresolved conceptual/practical issues prevent this from being a significant advancement as currently presented.
