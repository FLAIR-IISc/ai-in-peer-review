- Proposal: Introduces Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform A and a word importance vector w to improve WMD for classification.  
- Method: Adapts Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) to word-embedding space and uses a Sinkhorn-based gradient approximation for practical training.  
- Literature: Clearly situates work relative to metric learning (NCA, LMNN, ITML) and document representations, highlighting operation directly on word embeddings.  
- Experiments: Extensive evaluation on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines, including unsupervised WMD and multiple supervised methods.  
- Results: Consistent improvements; S-WMD achieves lowest error on 5/8 datasets and best average performance across methods.  
- Writing: Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and includes a thorough literature review.  
- Technical soundness: Methodology and gradient approximation are well-motivated and technically sound.  
- Weakness — reweighting motivation: The rationale for learning word-importance weights w is weak and insufficiently explained.  
- Weakness — semantic validity: No rigorous analysis or examples showing that reweighting preserves document semantics or corresponds to meaningful linguistic distinctions.  
- Weakness — novelty: Contribution is incremental; applying a linear transform and reweighting with NCA is a straightforward adaptation of existing metric-learning ideas.  
- Weakness — prior work comparison: Insufficient contrast with prior supervised EMD approaches (e.g., Wang et al.).  
- Weakness — practical deployment: Paper fails to explain how to compute the reweighting vector w for unseen documents, a critical practical gap.  
- Terminology: The phrase “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified to avoid conflating semantics with label/topic/authorship.  
- Overall: Technically sound and empirically strong, but needs minor revisions addressing reweighting justification, semantic validity, novelty clarification, and handling unseen documents.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the concerns about reweighting, semantic interpretation, and relation to prior supervised EMD work.
