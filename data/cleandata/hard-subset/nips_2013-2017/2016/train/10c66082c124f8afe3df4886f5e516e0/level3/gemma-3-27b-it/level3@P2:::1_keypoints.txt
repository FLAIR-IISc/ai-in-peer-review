- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transformation of embeddings and document-specific word importance weights to incorporate supervision into WMD.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization using Sinkhorn distance to approximate optimal transport and enable practical training.  
- Evaluated on eight text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines consistently.  
- Core idea of supervised extension to WMD is interesting and potentially impactful for task-specific document similarity.  
- Empirical evaluation is extensive and the paper is clearly written and well-situated in related work.  
- Improvement of full S-WMD over the S-WCD initialization is limited, raising questions about the added value of the complex optimization (Table 3).  
- Missing evaluation of word-centroid embeddings with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) as stronger baselines; NCA was only tested after PCA.  
- Lacks an ablation study to isolate contributions of learned word-specific weights versus the linear projection matrix A.  
- Originality is tempered because metric learning within EMD has prior work (e.g., Wang et al., ECCV 2017), though the optimization approach is novel.  
- Optimization contribution (lines 156–175) is significant and enables efficient training, but the overarching idea is not wholly groundbreaking.  
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous reference "The authors" (line 152) should name Cuturi et al., dual variables α/β in Eq.11 should have stars, and the use of Sinkhorn scaling should be explicitly connected to Section 2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear presentation, efficient optimization, and relevance to document similarity learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited performance gains over simpler initialization, missing strong baselines with standard metric learning, absent ablation study, and limited novelty beyond optimization.  
- Overall assessment: solid paper deserving acceptance with minor revisions to add baseline comparisons, ablation analyses, and clarify optimization details.
