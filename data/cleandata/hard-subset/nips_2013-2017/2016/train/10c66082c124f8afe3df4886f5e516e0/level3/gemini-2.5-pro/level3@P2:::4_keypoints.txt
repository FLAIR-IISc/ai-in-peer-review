- Paper proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns a linear embedding transform A and a global word-importance vector w to supervise Word Mover's Distance using an NCA-style k-NN loss.  
- Optimization made practical by approximating WMD with entropic-regularized Sinkhorn distance and deriving efficient gradients; sensible initialization via supervised WCD.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is very well-written, organized, and provides thorough background and clear motivation.  
- Strength — Empirical results: extensive evaluation on eight datasets vs. 26 baselines shows state- or near-state-of-the-art performance, plus useful visualizations and timing analysis.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: using Sinkhorn for a differentiable approximation is well-motivated and makes training tractable.  
- Weakness — Originality: the method is an effective but incremental combination of existing techniques (WMD, linear metric learning/NCA, feature re-weighting).  
- Weakness — Conceptual justification for w: insufficient theoretical justification that re-weighting preserves semantics vs. producing task-specific abstractions; relation to TF-IDF-like schemes is not discussed.  
- Weakness — Practicality/generalization of w: w is a single global, task-specific importance vector that may not generalize across domains/tasks and ignores context-dependent word importance.  
- Weakness — Terminology: claiming S-WMD captures "semantic meaning" is ambiguous; should use "task-specific similarity" for precision.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem (supervising WMD), provides strong empirical results, uses a technically sound and practical method, and is clearly presented.  
- Arguments for rejection: contribution is somewhat incremental and the word re-weighting component lacks deeper conceptual analysis.  
- Conclusion: solid, practical, and well-evaluated paper likely worthy of acceptance, but should better justify and analyze the global word-importance vector w in the final version.
