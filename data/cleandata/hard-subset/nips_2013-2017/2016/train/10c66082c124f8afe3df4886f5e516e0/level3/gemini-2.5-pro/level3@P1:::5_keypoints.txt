- Proposal: S-WMD learns a task-specific document metric by training a linear embedding transform (A) and a word-importance vector (w) to adapt WMD for classification.  
- Objective: Learning is framed as minimizing stochastic leave-one-out k-NN classification error using the Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) framework.  
- Technical tractability: The authors use the entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance as a differentiable, efficient proxy for WMD, enabling gradient-based optimization.  
- Core contribution: Efficient method for computing gradients of the (approximate) WMD via the Sinkhorn relaxation.  
- Empirical results: Comprehensive evaluation on eight text classification tasks against 26 baselines, achieving strong, state-of-the-art average performance.  
- Writing and clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and explains background and derivations clearly.  
- Weakness — lambda hyperparameter: Insufficient analysis of the entropy regularization parameter (lambda), with no sensitivity study or justification for the chosen value.  
- Weakness — novelty: Conceptually incremental, largely combining existing ideas (metric learning/NCA, ground-metric learning, word weighting) for documents.  
- Weakness — role of WCD: Repeated emphasis on Word Centroid Distance (WCD) as initialization/heuristic is potentially confusing and should be presented as an auxiliary training heuristic rather than a central contribution.
