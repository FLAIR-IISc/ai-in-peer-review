This paper proposes a novel method for learning a supervised word mover's distance (S-WMD) for document classification tasks. The authors build upon the existing Word Mover's Distance (WMD) by incorporating supervision through an affine transformation of the underlying word embedding space and a word-importance weight vector. The proposed method, S-WMD, directly minimizes a stochastic version of the leave-one-out classification error under the WMD metric.

The paper provides a clear and concise background on the WMD and its limitations, as well as the motivation for incorporating supervision. The authors also provide a detailed description of their proposed method, including the loss function, gradient computation, and optimization algorithm.

The experimental results presented in the paper are compelling, with S-WMD outperforming 26 competitive baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks. The authors also provide visualization of the learned word embeddings and document distances, which helps to illustrate the effectiveness of their method.

Overall, the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their proposed method and its advantages over existing approaches.
