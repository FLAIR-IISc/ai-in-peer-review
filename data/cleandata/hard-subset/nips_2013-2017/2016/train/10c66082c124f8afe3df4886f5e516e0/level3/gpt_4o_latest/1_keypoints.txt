- Proposal: Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) combining a linear projection of word embeddings and word-specific frequency weights, optimized to minimize leave-one-out kNN error.  
- Optimization: Uses a relaxed optimal transport formulation with Sinkhorn-type optimization for practical, efficient training.  
- Results: Evaluated on eight datasets and outperforms most of 26 competitive baselines in kNN text classification.  
- Strength — Technical soundness: Builds on established optimal transport, Sinkhorn scaling, and metric learning with an efficient optimization strategy.  
- Strength — Experimental rigor: Broad comparison across 8 datasets and 26 baselines showing consistent competitive or superior performance.  
- Strength — Clarity: Well-written, logically structured, with helpful visualizations (t-SNE, word clouds).  
- Strength — Practicality: Computationally efficient training times comparable or faster than baselines.  
- Weakness — Incremental contribution: Extension is novel but builds directly on existing WMD and supervised metric learning ideas.  
- Weakness — Limited evaluation: Lacks ablation isolating effects of word-specific weights and projection matrix.  
- Weakness — Missing baselines: No comparison to metric learning applied to word-centroid embeddings.  
- Weakness — Performance ceiling: Sometimes matches simpler initialization (S-WCD), questioning added value of full optimization.  
- Weakness — Minor clarity issues: Missing reference (Line 152), unclear notation in Eq.11, and need to recall Sinkhorn scaling (Line 170).  
- Pro argument: Addresses a relevant problem with competitive, efficient, and well-explained methodology—valuable contribution.  
- Con argument: Incremental novelty and incomplete component-level evaluation weaken the claim of advance.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add missing evaluations and clarify noted presentation issues.
