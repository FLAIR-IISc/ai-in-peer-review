- Proposal: S-WMD learns a Mahalanobis ground metric A and a word-importance vector w to create a supervised variant of WMD.  
- Training objective: Supervision via a stochastic leave-one-out kNN/NCA objective with pairwise distances from optimal transport.  
- Computational approach: Use entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to approximate WMD and obtain surrogate dual variables for efficient gradients.  
- Practical additions: Batch sampling and WCD-based neighbor filtering speed up training.  
- Empirical claim: Outperforms 26 baselines on 8 datasets, best average rank and wins on 5/8 datasets with reasonable training times.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD, Sinkhorn OT, and metric learning; related to supervised EMD work in vision and OT feature-weighting literature.  
- Technical soundness: Objective and parameterization are natural and well-motivated; entropic relaxation yields uniqueness and stable differentiation.  
- Theoretical gaps: Derivations are sketched; no analysis of approximation gap introduced by entropic OT or justification for λ choice.  
- Missing detail: Constraints/regularization on w and A are not fully specified.  
- Empirical evaluation: Strong experimental protocol, many baselines, Bayesian hyperparameter tuning, and useful visualizations.  
- Reproducibility: Code released and algorithmic details mostly present, but additional clarity is needed.  
- Notational issues: Inconsistent use of Euclidean vs. squared Euclidean costs and unclear p-Wasserstein notation; equations should be reconciled.  
- Presentation suggestions: Fix typos, add a self-contained algorithm box with variable shapes, constraints, and neighbor-filtering details.  
- Gradient derivations: Gradient w.r.t. w via dual variables needs more rigorous derivation and explicit assumptions.  
- Originality: Moderate novelty — combines known ideas but meaningfully adapts them to document distances using pretrained embeddings and joint A/w learning.  
- Significance: Practical improvement to WMD with likely adoption in NLP due to low computational footprint and empirical gains.  
- Strength: Clear parameterization, efficient Sinkhorn-based training, thorough experiments, public code, and sensible initialization (S-WCD).  
- Weakness — λ study: Need systematic empirical/ablation study of λ’s effect on accuracy, runtime, and bias vs. exact WMD.  
- Weakness — constraints/regularizers: Must clarify and justify constraints on w and A, and address potential degenerate solutions.  
- Weakness — prior work: Add missing citations (term weighting, OT feature-weight learning) and better distinguish contributions from related methods.  
- Ablation requests: Report A-only, w-only, and both; sensitivity to neighbors N and batch size B; statistics on learned w (sparsity, IDF correlation).  
- Scalability concerns: Discuss behavior on very long documents, large vocabularies, and robustness to different embeddings (e.g., GloVe).  
- Acceptance arguments: Practical, effective, open-source, and likely impactful; combines supervised metric learning with entropic OT effectively.  
- Rejection arguments: Limited theoretical depth, heuristic approximations without principled analysis, missing citations, and incomplete ablations.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept, with improvements to mathematical clarity, regularization discussion, and related-work positioning.
