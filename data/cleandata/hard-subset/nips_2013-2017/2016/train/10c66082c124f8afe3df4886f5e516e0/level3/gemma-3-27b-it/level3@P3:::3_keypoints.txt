- Proposes S-WMD: learn a supervised metric in embedding space by parameterizing WMD cost with a Mahalanobis distance and learning word importance weights.  
- Optimizes NCA-type objectives using gradients with an efficient Sinkhorn-based approximation of EMD.  
- Strong empirical validation: outperforms 26 baselines across eight datasets consistently.  
- Efficient implementation: uses Sinkhorn distances and probabilistic filtering to make training practical with reasonable runtimes.  
- Clear, focused contribution combining WMD, metric learning, and Sinkhorn regularization with learnable linear transform and word weights.  
- Well-motivated: addresses limitations of unsupervised document distance metrics with a supervised approach.  
- Mathematical derivations need more rigor and clearer links between approximations and theoretical guarantees.  
- Require clearer discussion of the trade-off introduced by Sinkhorn regularization and the role of the regularization parameter λ.  
- Notational inconsistency between Euclidean and squared-Euclidean formulations; should frame WMD as p-Wasserstein for consistency.  
- Reweighting scheme in Eq. 5 is not novel and should cite prior work (e.g., Lebanon, Le/Cuturi).  
- Lacks analysis/interpretability of learned weight vector w — need insights into which words drive class distinctions.  
- Needs clearer distinction from Cuturi/Avis and Wang et al., especially regarding entropy regularization and parameterization choices.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant empirical improvements, scalability, novel effective combination of techniques, and relevance to text retrieval.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient mathematical rigor, gaps in related work, and limited analysis of learned weights.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance pending revisions to improve clarity, rigor, and related-work/interpretability coverage.
