- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear transformation A and word importance weights w to optimize leave-one-out kNN under WMD.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance approximation to make the optimal transport optimization tractable.  
- Evaluated on eight diverse text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Empirical section is thorough with detailed analysis across news categorization, sentiment, and product identification datasets.  
- Writing, organization, and literature review are clear and comprehensive.  
- Strengths: clear exposition, extensive empirical validation, practical implementation details, and consistent supervised improvements over unsupervised WMD.  
- Technical approach is sound in practice, with appropriate use of Sinkhorn for computational efficiency.  
- Weak motivation and justification for the word importance weight vector w; no evidence that reweighting preserves semantics rather than arbitrarily altering documents.  
- Novelty is limited: learning a linear transformation is standard in metric learning and the method appears to be an application of NCA-style learning to WMD rather than a fundamental innovation.  
- Needs clearer differentiation from prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) that learns metrics in generalized EMD frameworks.  
- Terminology issue: claiming to capture "semantic difference" is misleading since the method optimizes label separation, not intrinsic semantic relations.  
- Practical concern: unclear how to compute or generalize word importance weights for unseen documents or out-of-vocabulary words.  
- Lacks theoretical justification for the reweighting mechanism and would benefit from ablation studies separating the effects of A and w.  
- Overall: strong empirical contribution but limited novelty and theoretical depth; address reweighting motivation, prior-work distinctions, and terminology for stronger acceptance prospects.
