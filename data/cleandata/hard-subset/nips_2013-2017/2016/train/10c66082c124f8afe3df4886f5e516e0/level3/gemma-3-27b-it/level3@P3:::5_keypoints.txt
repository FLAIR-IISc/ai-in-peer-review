- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD): learns word-specific weights and a linear embedding transformation, using Sinkhorn distance to approximate Wasserstein distance for supervised document similarity.
- Optimizes leave-one-out kNN classification error via batch gradient descent to learn re-weighted frequencies and embedding transform.
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance and strong empirical results.
- Builds on WMD and metric learning literature but directly learns a metric within the word embedding space, avoiding dimensionality-reduction bottlenecks.
- Uses the Sinkhorn (entropic) approximation for computational efficiency, with mathematically sound derivations and gradient explanations.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents informative figures and tables.
- Practical: computationally feasible with reasonable reported training times due to Sinkhorn approximation and batch optimization.
- Limited novelty: combination of supervised metric learning and Sinkhorn/WMD ideas is incremental rather than fundamentally new.
- Discussion of the Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is tangential and sometimes confusing; its connection to the method is not clearly articulated.
- Inadequate treatment of the entropic regularizer lambda (λ): lacks detailed discussion of trade-offs and sensitivity analysis.
- Fails to sufficiently emphasize that λ can dominate behavior, changing whether the learned metric approximates true Wasserstein distance or a different entropic-regularized distance.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical, theoretically sound method with extensive empirical support and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, insufficient λ discussion, and questionable relevance of WCD material.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify λ’s role, provide guidance on selecting λ, and streamline WCD discussion.
