- Paper extends WMD to supervised learning by learning a linear transform A for ground cost and a word-importance vector w, trained with a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective using Sinkhorn regularization.
- Clear, well-motivated formulation and sensible, computationally attractive entropy-regularized approximation.
- Strong empirical evaluation on eight datasets with many baselines and consistent, often substantial improvements.
- Good engineering choices (WCD/NCA initialization, neighborhood pruning) producing an efficient pipeline.
- Rebuttal was helpful and code release in Matlab aids reproducibility.
- Need clearer intuition for Eq. (5) and how w differs from TF-IDF/BM25, plus how normalization in Eq. (4) interacts with transport constraints.
- Clarify whether w is constrained nonnegative and whether there is regularization to prevent degenerate upweighting.
- Missing ablation study: report results for only A learned (w=1), only w learned (A=I), A+w, and S-WCD initialization alone to identify which component drives gains.
- Clarify use of entropy-regularized transport and λ: is it used at training only or also at test time, is the λ in Eq. (2) the same as ln170, and provide sensitivity analysis or default guidance.
- Provide empirical evidence that improvements are due to semantic alignment (A) vs lexical reweighting (w), e.g., class-conditional NN swaps attributable to each.
- Clarify where the reported “average error across datasets” is located (average rank vs Table 3) and correct reporting.
- Table 2 needs explanation of column labels “n” vs “ne.”
- Specify how k in kNN is chosen (validation, Bayesian optimization), whether it is dataset- or method-dependent, and report chosen k per dataset/method for reproducibility.
- Overall recommendation: solid and timely contribution with principled, efficient optimization and strong empirical gains; acceptance contingent on requested clarifications and ablations.
