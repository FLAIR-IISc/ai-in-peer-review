- Proposes a Residual Transfer Network (RTN) for domain adaptation that jointly learns adaptive classifiers and transferable features from labeled source and unlabeled target data.
- RTN relaxes the shared-classifier assumption by modeling the source and target classifiers as differing by a small residual function.
- Method is well-motivated, technically sound, and clearly and coherently presented.
- Thorough literature review and evaluation on novel datasets with comparisons to state-of-the-art transfer learning and deep learning methods.
- RTN outperforms competing methods on most transfer tasks in the reported experiments.
- Ablation study effectively isolates and demonstrates the contributions of different RTN modules.
- Paper lacks sufficient explanation and justification for the formulation, especially the reweighting of word distributions.
- The overall contribution may be limited—using linear transformation and reweighting with an NCA objective may be an incremental extension rather than a breakthrough.
- The term "semantic difference" is unclear and needs a precise definition (e.g., not clearly about label, authorship, or topic).
- Raises practical questions about how to compute distances and determine reweighting factors for entirely new documents.
