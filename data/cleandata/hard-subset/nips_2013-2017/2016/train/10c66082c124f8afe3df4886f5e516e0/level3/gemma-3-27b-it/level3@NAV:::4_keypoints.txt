- Proposes S-WMD: learns an affine transform of embeddings plus a word-importance reweighting on top of WMD, optimized with stochastic leave-one-out NN loss.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear explanation of concepts and math.
- Thorough literature review connecting metric learning and optimal transport.
- Extensive experiments on eight datasets comparing against 26 baselines with consistently strong empirical results.
- Builds on a successful existing method (WMD) and is well-motivated.
- Contribution is incremental: linear transform + reweighting is a straightforward extension of existing techniques.
- Major concern: weak justification for the word reweighting—insufficient analysis showing it improves label separation rather than harming semantics.
- No theoretical or empirical guarantees that reweighting won’t introduce noise or distort document meaning.
- Practical issue: paper fails to explain how to compute or apply the learned reweighting vector w for new/unseen documents.
- Terminology issue: phrase “semantic difference” is ambiguous and may imply topic/label/authorship similarity rather than true semantic understanding.
- Overall recommendation: solid and effective technique but acceptance should be conditional on addressing novelty, reweighting justification, and generalization to unseen documents.
