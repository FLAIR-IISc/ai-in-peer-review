This paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) called the Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD). The S-WMD learns a linear transformation of the word embeddings and a histogram re-weighting vector to improve the WMD distance for k-nearest neighbor (kNN) document classification tasks. The authors provide a novel method for efficiently computing the gradient of the S-WMD loss function, which is based on a relaxed version of the optimal transport problem. They also introduce a probabilistic filtering of the training set to reduce the computational complexity of the algorithm.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. The experimental results are compelling, showing that the S-WMD outperforms 26 competitive baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including a visualization of the learned weights and a discussion of the limitations of their method.

The strengths of the paper include the novelty of the proposed method, the efficiency of the algorithm, and the thorough experimental evaluation. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, making it easy to understand and implement.

One weakness of the paper is the lack of rigor in the mathematical derivations and notation. The authors use different notations and formulations throughout the paper, which can be confusing. For example, Eq. (1) uses the Euclidean distance, while Eq. (6) uses the squared Euclidean distance. The authors should clarify the notation and provide a consistent formulation throughout the paper.

Another weakness is the lack of discussion on the interplay between the regularized and unregularized optimal transport formulations. The authors should provide more insights on how the regularization affects the results and how it compares to the unregularized formulation.

The paper also lacks a clear discussion on the prior work related to the re-weighting of histograms. The authors should cite and discuss the related work by Guy Lebanon and Le/Cuturi, which also proposed a re-weighting scheme for histograms.

Finally, the paper could benefit from more insights on the empirical importance and relevance of the learned weight vector w. The authors should provide more analysis on how the learned weights affect the results and how they compare to other methods.

In terms of related work, the paper could benefit from a clearer distinction between the proposed method and the work by Cuturi/Avis and Wang et al. The authors should discuss the limitations of these methods and how their proposed method addresses these limitations.

Overall, I support the acceptance of this paper. The proposed method is novel and efficient, and the experimental results are compelling. However, the authors should address the weaknesses mentioned above to improve the clarity and rigor of the paper.
