- Proposal: Introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), learning a supervised metric via an affine transformation of word embeddings plus a word-importance weight vector.  
- Evaluation: Tested on eight real-world text classification tasks and consistently outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Writing: Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and background.  
- Motivation & explanation: Method is well-motivated and the optimization approach is thoroughly explained.  
- Strength: Effectively leverages WMD strengths while addressing its limitations by incorporating supervision.  
- Novelty: Main novelty lies in the optimization approach (lines 156–175); linear metric learning within EMD.  
- Empirical analysis: Experimental results are impressive with detailed dataset-level analysis.  
- Limited impact concern: EMD with metric learning initialized by a "word centroid distance" performs similarly to the full method (Table 3).  
- Missing comparison: No evaluation of word-centroid document embeddings with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation: No isolated assessment of the separate contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Contextualization: Could provide more context relating the work to existing metric learning and optimal transport research.  
- Quality assessment: Technically sound with clear explanations.  
- Clarity assessment: Presentation and motivation are clear and concise.  
- Significance assessment: Useful extension of WMD with supervised metric learning yielding strong empirical gains.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel extension, strong empirical performance, and good presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: Potentially limited novelty/impact and lacking key comparisons and ablations.
