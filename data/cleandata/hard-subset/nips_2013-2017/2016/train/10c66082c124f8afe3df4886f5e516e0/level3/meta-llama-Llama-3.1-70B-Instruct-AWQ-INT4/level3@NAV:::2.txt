**Paper Review: Supervised Word Mover's Distance**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD). The S-WMD learns a metric that incorporates supervision to improve the WMD by transforming the word embedding space and learning word-specific importance weights. The method is evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear explanation of the method and its components, including the loss function, gradient computation, and optimization process. The experimental evaluation is extensive and covers several classification datasets and a wide range of baselines and features.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear introduction to the background and related work, and the method is explained in a step-by-step manner. The notation is consistent, and the equations are well-formatted. The experimental results are presented in a clear and concise manner, making it easy to compare the performance of different methods.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel combination of techniques, including the use of supervision to improve the WMD and the learning of word-specific importance weights. The method is original and differs from previous contributions in the field of metric learning and natural language processing.

**Significance**

The results are important and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving the performance of text classification tasks. The method has the potential to be used in a wide range of applications, including document retrieval, sentiment analysis, and topic modeling. The paper also provides a unique perspective on the use of supervision in improving the performance of word embeddings.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and effective method for improving the performance of text classification tasks.
* The experimental evaluation is extensive and demonstrates the superiority of the proposed method over 26 competitive baselines.
* The method has the potential to be used in a wide range of applications and provides a unique perspective on the use of supervision in improving the performance of word embeddings.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper assumes that the word embeddings are given and does not provide a method for learning the embeddings themselves.
* The method requires a large amount of labeled data, which may not be available in all applications.
* The computational complexity of the method may be high, especially for large datasets.

**Conclusion**

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed method is novel and effective. The experimental evaluation is extensive, and the results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over 26 competitive baselines. The method has the potential to be used in a wide range of applications and provides a unique perspective on the use of supervision in improving the performance of word embeddings. I recommend accepting the paper for presentation at NIPS.
