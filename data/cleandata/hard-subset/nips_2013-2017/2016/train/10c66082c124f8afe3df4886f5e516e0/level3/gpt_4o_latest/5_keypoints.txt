- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns word importance weights and a linear embedding transform for classification.
- Uses Sinkhorn distance as a faster, scalable approximation to WMD to address computational inefficiency.
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines and achieves the best average performance.
- Strength: Well-written with detailed, reproducible mathematical derivations and optimization steps.
- Strength: Strong empirical results demonstrating robust performance across diverse datasets.
- Strength: Significant efficiency gains by leveraging Sinkhorn distance for practical scalability.
- Strength: Visualizations (learned word weights, t-SNE) enhance interpretability and illustrate method impact.
- Weakness: Conceptual novelty is incremental—adapts established supervised metric learning ideas to WMD.
- Weakness: Comparison to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is of unclear relevance given WCD’s simplicity.
- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of the Sinkhorn regularization parameter (λ) and its effect on convergence/behavior.
- Weakness: Sensitivity to initialization (reliance on S-WCD) raises robustness concerns; poor init can harm performance.
- Pro: Strong empirical performance, clear exposition, efficient WMD approximation, and practical classification utility.
- Con: Incremental contribution, limited hyperparameter study (notably λ), and sparse discussion of failure cases.
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing weaknesses—especially deeper analysis of λ and clarification of WCD comparisons.
