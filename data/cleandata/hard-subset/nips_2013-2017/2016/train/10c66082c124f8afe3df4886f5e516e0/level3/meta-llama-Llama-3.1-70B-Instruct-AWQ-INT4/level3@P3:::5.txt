This paper proposes a supervised extension of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) by learning word-specific weights and using Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy for Wasserstein distance. The authors evaluate their method, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), on eight datasets against 26 baselines and report the best average performance.

The paper is well-written, with a clear and in-depth mathematical exposition. The authors provide a thorough background on the Word Mover's Distance and its relation to the Earth Mover's Distance and the Wasserstein distance. They also provide a detailed description of their method, including the loss function, gradient computation, and optimization algorithm.

The strengths of the paper include its ability to leverage the power of word embeddings to improve document classification tasks. The authors demonstrate that their method can outperform state-of-the-art methods on several datasets, including those with small document lengths. The use of Sinkhorn distance as a proxy for Wasserstein distance is also a significant contribution, as it allows for faster computation of the distance.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The novelty of the method is limited compared to the prior Word Mover's Distance work. The authors do not provide a clear justification for the choice of lambda (entropic regularizer) and its impact on the behavior and results of the method. The role of lambda is poorly discussed in the manuscript, and it is unclear whether the method behaves like pure Wasserstein (large lambda) or like entropic/Sinkhorn (small lambda).

Additionally, the references to Word Centroid Distance are not relevant and are confusing. The authors should clarify the relationship between Word Centroid Distance and their method, and provide a clear explanation of why they use Word Centroid Distance as an initialization for their method.

The paper also lacks a clear discussion of the computational complexity of the method. While the authors provide some information on the time complexity of the algorithm, they do not provide a detailed analysis of the computational resources required to run the method.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing. However, the authors should address the limitations mentioned above to improve the clarity and impact of their work.
