This paper proposes a supervised learning method for document distance, building upon the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) by learning a linear transformation of the word embeddings and a reweighted word distribution. The authors leverage the optimal transport problem between word distributions with word2vec costs as the base distance. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a thorough literature review. The experimental results on several novel datasets are extensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The paper relates to previous work on document distances, such as the Word Centroid Distance (WCD) and the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD). The authors also draw connections to metric learning algorithms, including Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) and Information-Theoretic Metric Learning (ITML). The proposed method, Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), is a novel combination of these techniques.

The strengths of the paper include its clarity and organization. The authors provide a clear explanation of the WMD and its limitations, as well as the motivation for learning a supervised document distance. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, with a clear comparison to baseline methods.

However, there are some weaknesses to the paper. The motivation for reweighting the word distribution is not well-explained, and it is unclear how this reweighting improves label separation without altering document semantics. Additionally, the contribution of the paper is limited, as applying a linear transform and reweighting with an NCA objective is not seen as a significant novelty. There is also a practical concern regarding how to compute the reweighting for a completely new/unseen document.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the authors could provide more justification for the reweighting step and its impact on document semantics. The paper is a complete piece of work, but it could benefit from more discussion on the limitations and potential applications of the proposed method.

The originality of the paper is somewhat limited, as it builds upon existing techniques. However, the combination of WMD and supervised metric learning is novel, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The related work is adequately referenced, and the authors provide a clear comparison to existing methods.

The significance of the paper is moderate, as it addresses a specific problem in document classification. The results are important, as they demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving document classification accuracy. However, the impact of the paper is limited to the specific application of document classification, and it may not have broader implications for the field.

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
