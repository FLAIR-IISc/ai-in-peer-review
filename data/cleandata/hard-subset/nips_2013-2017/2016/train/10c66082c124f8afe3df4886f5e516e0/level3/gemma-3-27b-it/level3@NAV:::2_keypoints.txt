- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD), a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transformation of embedding space and word importance weights.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance for an efficient, scalable approximation of optimal transport in the supervised WMD framework.  
- Addresses a core limitation of original WMD by introducing discriminative training to make the metric domain-specific.  
- Methodology is technically sound, well-motivated, and builds logically from WMD and metric learning foundations.  
- Paper is clearly written and explains technical details with appropriate depth and clarity.  
- Carefully tackles computational challenges and offers a clever approximation to enable practical training.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive: eight real-world text classification datasets and comparisons to 26 diverse baselines.  
- Results consistently show S-WMD significantly outperforms all baselines, often by substantial margins.  
- Includes t-SNE visualizations that provide intuitive insight into the learned representations.  
- Authors thoughtfully discuss limitations and propose directions for future work.  
- Main weakness: experiments focus primarily on kNN classification; performance on other downstream tasks (e.g., IR, clustering) is not explored.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, significant contribution with strong empirical evidence; strongly recommended for acceptance.
