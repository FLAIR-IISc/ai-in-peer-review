- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear ground-embedding transform A and word importance weights w trained with a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA) objective.
- Uses entropically regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) and its dual potentials/transport plan to obtain efficient gradients for training.
- Builds on prior work: WMD, entropic OT/Sinkhorn, and supervised metric learning; related to supervised EMD/ground-metric learning in vision but applied to NLP with joint A and w learning.
- Technically careful and clearly written; using Sinkhorn to obtain unique T* and applying envelope/Danskin arguments is reasonable but needs explicit justification/citation.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: 8 datasets and 26 baselines with consistent average gains.
- Major methodological gap: insufficient analysis of the entropic regularizer λ—its effect on geometry, sensitivity, runtime-accuracy trade-offs, and behavior as λ→∞ (near-unregularized WMD) is under-discussed.
- Clarity issues: over-emphasis on Word Centroid Distance (WCD) risks conflating centroid methods with OT; WCD’s conceptual role should be deemphasized.
- Notational confusion: reuse of p for different meanings and statements about choosing small λ for speed need alignment and clearer guidance.
- Originality judged moderate: combination of ground-metric learning and histogram reweighting for WMD with Sinkhorn is incremental relative to supervised EMD/OT, but well executed.
- Significance: empirical gains and released code make the method practically useful and likely to be adopted.
- Pros: strong empirical results, clear/practical training, and interpretable joint learning of A and w.
- Cons: limited novelty versus prior supervised EMD/OT, inadequate treatment of λ, and confusing WCD positioning and notation.
- Suggested revisions: add λ sensitivity and large-λ experiments and report selection protocol; ablate A vs w vs both; clarify WCD’s role and fix notation; explicitly cite Danskin/envelope theorem in gradient derivations.
