- Paper proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) to learn a task-specific document metric by jointly learning a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance vector w.  
- Uses an entropy-regularized Sinkhorn approximation to WMD to obtain a differentiable objective and an efficient gradient for optimization.  
- Objective minimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification loss in the Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) framework.  
- Evaluated on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines, showing strong kNN performance.  
- Main technical contribution is the efficient differentiation through an approximate transport problem (Sinkhorn relaxation).  
- Paper is technically sound, well-justified, and the optimization approach is clever.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive and results generally show state-of-the-art kNN accuracy.  
- Clarity is excellent: well-written, organized, and with helpful background, figures, and tables.  
- Novelty lies in applying supervised metric learning to WMD, jointly learning A and w, and the Sinkhorn-based optimization.  
- Practical significance is questioned because S-WMD shows only marginal improvements over its simple initialization (S-WCD / NCA on word centroids).  
- Primary empirical weakness: lack of ablation study to isolate effects of learning A versus learning w.  
- Secondary empirical weakness: missing direct comparisons applying standard metric learners (e.g., LMNN, ITML) to the WCD/centroid representation.  
- Pro-acceptance points: principled supervision of WMD, clever optimization, strong empirical baselines, and excellent presentation.  
- Con-acceptance points: complex optimization yields minimal gains over initialization and missing ablation/complete baselines reduce interpretability and impact.  
- Minor suggestions: clarify reference on Line 152, denote dual variables consistently in Equation 10 (α*λ, β*λ), and explicitly link Sinkhorn scaling vectors (u,v) to dual variables for clarity.
