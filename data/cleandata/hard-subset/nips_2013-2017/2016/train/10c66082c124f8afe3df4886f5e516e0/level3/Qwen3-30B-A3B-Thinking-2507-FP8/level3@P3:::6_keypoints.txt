- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) extending WMD with a learned linear transform A and word importance weights w.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance approximation for efficient gradient-based optimization to avoid cubic WMD cost.  
- Learns A and w by minimizing stochastic leave-one-out k-NN classification error in a supervised metric-learning framework.  
- Evaluated on 8 document classification tasks and outperforms 26 baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, mSDA, NCA, ITML, LMNN).  
- Meaningful contribution: incorporates supervision into WMD to obtain task-specific document distances.  
- Technical innovation: well-motivated use of Sinkhorn approximation enabling efficient training.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and shows consistent, often statistically significant, improvements.  
- Code provided (Matlab) and builds effectively on prior NCA and WMD work.  
- Clarification needed on intuition and role of w (how it interacts with document representation and whether A or w drives improvements).  
- Confusion about λ: authors should confirm if λ in Eq(2) is the same as λ referenced elsewhere and clarify its role in optimization.  
- Claim that S-WMD is forgiving for syntactically different but semantically similar words needs concrete examples or analysis.  
- Ambiguity about "average error across datasets"—should state whether this appears in Table 3.  
- Table 2 lacks descriptions for columns "n" and "ne"; paper also omits how k for k-NN was chosen (dataset- or method-dependent).  
- Paper should better emphasize that S-WMD complements, not replaces, standard classifiers/pipelines.  
- Overall technically sound, significant, and well-motivated work with minor clarity/implementation details to fix; recommendation: Accept.
