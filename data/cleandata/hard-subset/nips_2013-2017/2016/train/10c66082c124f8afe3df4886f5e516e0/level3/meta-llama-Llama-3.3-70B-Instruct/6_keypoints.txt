- Proposes Residual Transfer Network (RTN) that bridges source and target classifiers via a residual function for unsupervised domain adaptation.  
- Enables end-to-end learning of adaptive classifiers and transferable features in deep networks.  
- Trains RTN with standard back-propagation and reports state-of-the-art performance on benchmark datasets.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and explains technical details and related work thoroughly.  
- Effectively addresses classifier mismatch by learning a residual between source and target classifiers.  
- Incorporates tensor MMD and entropy minimization to improve adaptation performance.  
- Experimental results demonstrate improved classification accuracy across domains.  
- Paper is technically sound with claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Original contribution: unified framework combining feature adaptation and classifier adaptation, novel use of residual transfer and tensor MMD.  
- Significant potential impact for real-world domain adaptation problems.  
- Minor issues: unclear notation in Table 2 and lack of reported average error across datasets.  
- Minor issues: need clearer justification for choice/value of lambda and the claim about forgiving syntactically different but conceptually similar words.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, pending clarification of the above minor issues.
