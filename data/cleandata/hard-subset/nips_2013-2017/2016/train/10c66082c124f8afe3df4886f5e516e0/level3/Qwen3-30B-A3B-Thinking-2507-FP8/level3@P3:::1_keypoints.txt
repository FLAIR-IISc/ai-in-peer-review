- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns a linear embedding projection and document-specific word importance weights to supervise Word Mover’s Distance for kNN classification.  
- Key technical innovation is an efficient approximation of the WMD gradient using Sinkhorn scaling, avoiding nested optimization.  
- Optimizes leave-one-out kNN classification error, building on WMD/EMD and inspired by metric learning (e.g., NCA).  
- Strong empirical results: consistently outperforms 26 baselines across 8 text classification datasets.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear exposition, good pseudo-code, and persuasive t-SNE visualizations.  
- Major omission: no comparison to word-centroid document embeddings coupled with standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Missing ablation study: does not isolate contributions of the projection matrix A versus the word-specific importance weights.  
- Table 3 shows the initialization (S-WCD) performs nearly as well as full S-WMD, undermining the claimed gains from the full method.  
- Originality is limited: main novelty is the optimization method rather than a fundamentally new metric-learning idea; related work (e.g., Wang et al.) not clearly distinguished.  
- Significance is uncertain because missing baselines and ablations reduce confidence that S-WMD adds value beyond simpler alternatives.  
- Strengths: well-motivated extension to WMD, clever and efficient optimization, comprehensive experimental scope, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: lacks critical baseline comparisons and ablation analyses, limited demonstrated impact over initialization, novelty mainly in optimization.  
- Recommendation: require additional experiments (missing baselines and ablation studies) before accepting, as current evidence is insufficient to confirm the method’s added value.
