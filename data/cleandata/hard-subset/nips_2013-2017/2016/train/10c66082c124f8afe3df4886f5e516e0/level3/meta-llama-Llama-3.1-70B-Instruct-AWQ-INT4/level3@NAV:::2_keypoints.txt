- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns an embedding transform and word-specific importance weights.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Technically sound with supporting theoretical analysis and experimental results.  
- Clear explanation of method components, loss function, gradient computation, and optimization.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive across datasets, baselines, and features.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with consistent notation and well-formatted equations.  
- Novel combination of supervision with WMD and learning of word-specific importance weights.  
- Significant empirical improvements with potential applications in retrieval, sentiment analysis, and topic modeling.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, effective method with strong experimental support and broad applicability.  
- Argument against acceptance: assumes given word embeddings and does not learn embeddings.  
- Argument against acceptance: requires large amounts of labeled data, limiting applicability.  
- Argument against acceptance: potential high computational complexity for large datasets.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept for presentation at NIPS.
