- Proposes S-WMD: discriminatively trained WMD that learns an affine transform of embeddings and a word-importance vector.  
- Uses an NCA-style stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss for training.  
- Computes gradients through optimal transport via Cuturi’s entropy-regularized Sinkhorn relaxation and dual variables.  
- Training accelerated with S-WCD (supervised centroid) initialization, batch optimization, and neighbor truncation.  
- Evaluated on eight classification datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average rank and large margins often.  
- Method connects WMD/OT, classic metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML), and supervised EMD variants, learning task-specific ground metric and histogram reweighting.  
- Technically sound with clear derivations of approximate gradients using Sinkhorn primal/dual solutions.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive, with careful baselines, reasonable engineering choices, and released code.  
- Paper is well written and organized with sufficient reproducibility details, complexity analysis, and training heuristics.  
- Combination of existing components is novel and well motivated (learning both A and w jointly).  
- Results are significant and consistent across datasets, likely useful in practice for document distances and retrieval.  
- Weakness: lacks ablations isolating the contributions of A vs. w and sensitivity analyses for λ, neighbor size N, and initialization.  
- Weakness: scalability/memory/runtime for long documents (T and K scaling O(p^2)) not fully quantified; peak memory not reported.  
- Suggestion: consider regularization on w (sparsity/boundedness) and report learned weight distributions to mitigate overfitting.  
- Suggestion: clarify robustness to different embeddings (GloVe/fastText) and to choice of k in kNN.  
- Suggestion: add statistical significance tests on key improvements and comment on LDA’s transductive setting.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical gains, sound and efficient method, elegant bridge between OT/WMD and supervised metric learning, and high practical relevance.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited ablation/sensitivity analysis, unresolved scalability concerns for very long documents, and narrow focus on kNN with limited downstream task exploration.  
- Overall recommendation: solid paper recommended for acceptance (suitable for NIPS).
