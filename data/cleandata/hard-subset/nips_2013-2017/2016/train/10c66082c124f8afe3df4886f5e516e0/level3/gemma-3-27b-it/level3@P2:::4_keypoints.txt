- Proposes S-WMD: learns an affine transform A and word-importance vector w to supervise Word Mover’s Distance using a leave-one-out NN objective and Sinkhorn relaxation for gradients.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: outperforms 26 baselines across eight real-world text classification tasks with statistically significant results.
- Builds on and is well-positioned within prior work (BoW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, WMD, metric learning, NCA).
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and logically organized with consistent notation.
- Conceptually sound integration of supervised metric learning into the WMD framework.
- Efficient training enabled by a gradient approximation of the Sinkhorn-relaxed OT problem.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation and practical discussion of computational complexity and optimization.
- Code release enhances reproducibility and practical impact.
- Weak justification for the word-importance reweighting w and limited explanation of how it affects document semantics.
- Lacks detailed insight into why certain words are emphasized for particular tasks and how weights contribute to gains.
- Unclear how to compute or apply w for completely new documents not seen during training.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental—linear transform and reweighting within an NCA-style objective rather than a fundamentally novel technique.
- Abstract uses ambiguous terminology (“semantic difference”) that should be clarified.
- Originality comes from integrating WMD with supervised metric learning and jointly learning transform and weights.
- Significance: advances state-of-the-art document distance metrics and is likely useful for classification, retrieval, and related tasks.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clarity, efficient implementation, relevance, and released code.
- Arguments against acceptance: weak reweighting justification, incremental novelty, unclear unseen-document procedure, and ambiguous abstract wording.
- Overall assessment: strong, polished paper with significant potential; recommend acceptance if authors better justify and analyze word reweighting and clarify unseen-document application and terminology.
