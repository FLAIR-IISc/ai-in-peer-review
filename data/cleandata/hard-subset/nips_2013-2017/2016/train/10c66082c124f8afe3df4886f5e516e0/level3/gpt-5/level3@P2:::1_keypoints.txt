- Proposes S-WMD: jointly learn linear projection A and nonnegative word-importance vector w to supervise Word Mover’s Distance via a stochastic leave-one-out kNN/NCA objective.  
- Uses entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn distance) to obtain efficient, differentiable approximations and gradients for training.  
- Introduces practical training recipe: initialize with S-WCD (NCA on centroids), batch optimization with WCD-based neighbor pruning, and approximate gradients from primal/dual OT solutions.  
- Evaluated on eight text classification datasets and reportedly outperforms 26 baselines including BOW/TF-IDF/LSI/LDA/mSDA, NCA/LMNN/ITML, WCD, and WMD.  
- Builds on prior work: WMD, entropic OT, and metric learning; novelty is combining entropic OT + NCA to learn A and w for text kNN.  
- Formulation is technically sound; Sinkhorn regularization makes gradients feasible and training acceleration via neighbor pruning is practical.  
- Gradient derivations for A (primal T) and w (dual α, β) are reasonable under entropic approximation and uniqueness assumptions.  
- Empirical results are strong but lack several important ablations and baselines to isolate sources of improvement.  
- Reproducibility is good: clear writing and promised code; key hyperparameters listed though sensitivity analyses are limited.  
- Manuscript is well organized and clearly explains connections among WMD, Sinkhorn, and NCA; training algorithm is concrete.  
- Originality: learning ground metric for EMD has precedent, but the supervised entropic-OT + NCA combination for A and w is a novel and interesting contribution.  
- Significance: practically valuable for adapting WMD to tasks, but impact limited by modest gains over centroid initialization and narrow evaluation (kNN only).  
- Strength: principled merge of metric learning with OT, efficient training, strong empirical performance, and interpretable learned word weights.  
- Weakness: missing baselines such as ITML/LMNN on centroids and simple supervised reweighting methods to contextualize w.  
- Weakness: missing ablations for A-only (w fixed) and w-only (A identity) to quantify component contributions.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of gains over S-WMD initialization—need per-dataset deltas and sensitivity studies on λ, neighbor set size, batch size.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical discussion of bias introduced by entropic regularization λ and its effect on learned A and w.  
- Weakness: evaluation confined to kNN; retrieval/clustering metrics (MAP, nDCG) would demonstrate broader utility.  
- Weakness: efficiency claims need wall-clock comparisons versus running WMD kNN at test time to quantify costs.  
- Minor comments: clarify ambiguous citation, mark dual variables in Eq.(11), specify constraints/regularization on w, state A’s dimensionality r, and fix notation/typos.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clean concept, practical supervised WMD extension, solid empirical gains, and likely adoption of Sinkhorn-based supervised OT approach.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete ablation/baseline analysis, modest improvements over centroid initialization, and narrow evaluation scope.  
- Overall recommendation: lean accept, contingent on addressing ablation, baseline, and evaluation gaps.  
- Reviewer confidence: high (familiar with OT, metric learning, and text representation literature).
