- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that jointly learns a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance vector w via minimizing stochastic leave-one-out k-NN error (NCA-inspired).  
- Uses entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn distance) to obtain an efficient, differentiable approximation of WMD and make gradient-based learning practical.  
- Evaluated on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines, achieving lowest error on 5/8 datasets and the best average rank.  
- Novel and significant contribution: introduces supervision into a strong unsupervised metric (WMD) with an elegant two-component learning approach (A and w).  
- Technically sound: provides gradient derivations, sensible initialization (supervised WCD), and a practical optimization strategy (batch descent with neighbor filtering).  
- Empirically thorough: extensive baselines (classic and modern representations plus metric learners) and promise to release code for reproducibility.  
- Missing intuition and ablation: need clearer explanation of the role of w and ablation results isolating the effects of A-only and w-only.  
- Ambiguity about regularization λ: clarify that the λ in the paper is the same as the Cuturi (2013) Sinkhorn regularizer and state its use explicitly.  
- Claim about being "forgiving" to synonymy needs concrete qualitative examples (document pairs) to support it.  
- Reporting clarity issues: text implies an average error but only Avg. Rank is shown; Table 2 columns "n" and "ne" are undefined; k selection for k-NN is not specified.  
- Appropriate evaluation scope: framed as learning a distance metric for k-NN classification, which justifies the chosen evaluation methodology.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, novel, and impactful work with minor, addressable clarity/completeness issues; recommend acceptance.
