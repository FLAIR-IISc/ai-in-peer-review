- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform and a histogram re-weighting vector for kNN document classification.  
- Introduces an efficient gradient computation for the S-WMD loss based on a relaxed optimal transport formulation.  
- Proposes probabilistic filtering of the training set to reduce computational complexity.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise method explanation and implementation clarity.  
- Strong experimental results: S-WMD outperforms 26 baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks.  
- Provides thorough analysis including visualization of learned weights and discussion of method limitations.  
- Strengths: novelty of method, algorithmic efficiency, and comprehensive empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: inconsistent and sometimes unclear mathematical notation and derivations (e.g., Euclidean vs squared Euclidean in equations).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of interplay between regularized and unregularized optimal transport and its effects on results.  
- Weakness: missing citation/discussion of prior histogram re-weighting work (e.g., Guy Lebanon, Le/Cuturi).  
- Weakness: limited empirical analysis of the learned weight vector w and its practical importance.  
- Related work: needs clearer distinction from Cuturi/Avis and Wang et al., and discussion of their limitations versus S-WMD.  
- Recommendation: support acceptance, contingent on addressing the noted clarity, notation, and related-work gaps.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
