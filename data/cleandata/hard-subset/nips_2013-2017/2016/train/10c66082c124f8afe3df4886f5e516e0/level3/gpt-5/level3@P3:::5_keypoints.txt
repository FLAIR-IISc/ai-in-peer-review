- Proposal: S-WMD extends WMD by learning a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance vector w with an NCA-style supervised kNN objective, using Sinkhorn (entropic OT) for tractable gradients.
- Practical speedups: uses S-WCD initialization and WCD-based neighborhood prefiltering to accelerate training versus exact WMD.
- Empirics: evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieves best average kNN rank and wins on 5/8 datasets; code released.
- Technical clarity: clean derivations for gradients and pragmatic description of Sinkhorn-based computation and batching.
- Interpretability: learned word weights w give task-relevant vocabulary insights.
- Novelty: moderate — combines known ingredients (WMD, entropic OT, NCA/metric learning) into an effective text-focused instantiation rather than introducing a fundamentally new method.
- Main technical concern: entropic regularization λ is under-discussed; λ strongly affects approximation bias, geometry, computational behavior, and was fixed at 10 without sensitivity analysis.
- Objective/approximation mismatch: paper optimizes an objective written in terms of WMD but uses Sinkhorn solutions for gradients; need explicit statement whether NCA distances are entropic or analysis of approximation impact.
- Word-weight constraints unclear: no statement whether w is constrained nonnegative; recommend enforcing w≥0 and reporting sparsity/regularization.
- Overemphasis on WCD: repeated framing of WCD/S-WCD risks distracting from the main contribution; suggest tightening presentation to emphasize WCD as an engineering tool.
- Missing ablations/diagnostics: need A-only vs w-only experiments, sensitivity to rank(A) and neighbor set size M, comparison to exact WMD gradients on small problems, and clarity on k selection in kNN.
- Quality judgement: technically sound and well-presented, but the λ issue and missing ablations reduce confidence.
- Clarity suggestions: align optimized distance with approximated gradients, detail λ trade-offs/selection, and clarify constraints on w.
- Significance: high practical impact for kNN document retrieval/classification and bridging OT with NLP.
- Pros for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear method and speedups, interpretability, and released code.
- Cons for acceptance: limited conceptual novelty, insufficient treatment of λ and entropic vs Wasserstein behavior, some implementation/objective details under-specified, and overemphasis on WCD.
- Recommended fixes: add λ sensitivity/time plots and guidance, run ablations for A vs w and rank/M/k, enforce/report constraints/regularization on w and A, validate Sinkhorn-gradient proxy against exact WMD on small problems, and streamline WCD discussion.
