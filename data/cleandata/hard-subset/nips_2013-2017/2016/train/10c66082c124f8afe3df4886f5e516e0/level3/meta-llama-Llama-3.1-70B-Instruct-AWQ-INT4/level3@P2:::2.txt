This paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), a document metric that has shown state-of-the-art performance in k-nearest neighbor (kNN) document classification. The authors introduce a technique to learn a supervised metric, called the Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), which incorporates supervision into the WMD by learning an affine transformation of the underlying word embedding space and a word-importance weight vector.

The S-WMD metric is learned by minimizing the stochastic leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error on a per-document level. The authors provide an efficient technique to approximate the gradient of the original WMD distance, which would result in an inefficient nested optimization problem. The proposed method is sound and clearly explained with appropriate detail.

The experimental evaluation is extensive, covering eight real-world text classification tasks and comparing the proposed method to 26 competitive baselines. The results show that S-WMD consistently outperforms almost all baselines, achieving the lowest kNN test error on five out of eight datasets. The authors also provide a visualization of the test split of each dataset using t-Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t-SNE), which demonstrates the improved clustering of points in the same class using S-WMD.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its relation to previous work. The experimental evaluation is thorough, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.
