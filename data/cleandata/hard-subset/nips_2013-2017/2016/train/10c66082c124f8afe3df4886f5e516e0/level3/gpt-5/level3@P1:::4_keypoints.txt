- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised document distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and a global vocabulary weight vector w to improve WMD for kNN classification.  
- Optimization: A and w are trained with a stochastic leave-one-out kNN/NCA-style objective.  
- Differentiable OT: Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn OT to avoid nested differentiation and obtain practical gradients for transport and dual potentials.  
- Initialization & engineering: Initializes A via supervised word-centroid NCA (S-WCD), uses minibatch training and a WCD-based neighbor filter for efficiency.  
- Empirics: Reports consistent improvements over 26 baselines on eight datasets, with t-SNE visualizations, timing analyses, and public code.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD/OT and supervised metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML); adapts known components for a supervised ground metric for OT.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written with a thorough literature review situating the contribution.  
- Strength — methodology: Learning a task-aligned ground metric A and word weights w directly targets kNN performance; Sinkhorn makes optimization practical.  
- Strength — engineering & reproducibility: Sensible initialization, minibatching, neighbor filtering, and public code aid reproducibility and efficiency.  
- Weakness — interpretation of w: The global reweighting w may change document semantics and lacks principled motivation or empirical justification.  
- Weakness — novelty: Contribution is incremental — known components combined rather than a deep conceptual advance.  
- Weakness — generalization/OOV: Unclear how w handles unseen vocabulary or domain shifts at test time and what defaults are for OOV tokens.  
- Weakness — terminology: “Semantic difference” is misleading; should be clarified to “task-aligned similarity.”  
- Weakness — limited ablations: Missing A-only vs w-only vs A+w comparisons, sensitivity to λ, neighbor count, and ground-cost choices.  
- Quality: Method is generally sound and gradients are derived cleanly, but needs justification of dual-potential approximations and discussion of regularization for A and w.  
- Clarity suggestions: Explicitly state w is global and nonnegative, define OOV handling, and discuss constraints/priors on w and A.  
- Originality: Moderate — natural supervised extension of WMD with Sinkhorn acceleration, but not highly novel.  
- Significance: Practically useful with improved kNN performance and efficiency; conceptual impact modest.  
- Key questions for authors: Are there constraints on w? Ablation results for A-only/w-only? OOV handling and robustness to domain shift? Sensitivity to λ and ground-cost p? Regularization and model selection? Inference-time cost vs baselines?  
- Suggested improvements: Provide principled justification or constraints for w (e.g., IDF prior or KL penalty), add ablations and sensitivity analyses, clarify test-time and OOV procedures, and compare alternative ground metrics and embeddings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Solid, well-executed supervised extension with strong empirical gains, useful engineering, and public code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Limited conceptual novelty and underspecified justification and generalization behavior of the global weight w.  
- Overall: Acceptable if authors address w-related concerns, add ablations, and clarify terminology/test-time behavior.
