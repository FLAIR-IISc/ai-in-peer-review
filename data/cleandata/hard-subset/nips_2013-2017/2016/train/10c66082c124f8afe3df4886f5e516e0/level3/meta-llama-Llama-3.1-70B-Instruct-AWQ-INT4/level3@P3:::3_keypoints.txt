- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word-importance vector to adapt WMD for supervised document classification.  
- Builds on Word Mover's Distance and linear metric learning to align WMD distances with label-based semantic similarity.  
- Introduces a regularized optimal transport formulation to reduce the cubic complexity of Wasserstein distance computation.  
- Derives gradients for the loss with respect to the linear transform and weight vector and proposes an efficient optimization algorithm.  
- Empirical results: S-WMD outperforms 26 competitive baselines across 8 real-world text classification tasks.  
- Provides t-SNE visualizations showing learned distances capture semantic document similarities.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes a thorough related work section clearly distinguishing the contribution.  
- Strength: novel supervised metric learning approach combining word embeddings and transport-based distances.  
- Strength: thorough background exposition and clear methodological explanation.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance and visualization evidence.  
- Weakness: needs more rigorous mathematical derivation of gradients and the optimization algorithm.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the empirical importance and interpretation of the learned weight vector w.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed discussion on the trade-offs and interplay between regularized and unregularized OT formulations.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing the derivation, weight-vector insights, and OT formulation discussion.
