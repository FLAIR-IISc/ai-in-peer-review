- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transform of embeddings and a reweighted word distribution on top of WMD.  
- Leverages optimal transport between word distributions with word2vec costs as base distance.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with a thorough literature review.  
- Experimental results are extensive on several novel datasets and demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Connects to prior document-distance work (WCD, EMD) and metric learning (NCA, ITML).  
- S-WMD is a novel combination of WMD and supervised metric learning techniques.  
- Strength: clear explanation of WMD, its limitations, and motivation for supervised distance learning.  
- Strength: thorough, well-presented comparisons to baseline methods.  
- Weakness: motivation for reweighting the word distribution is not well-explained.  
- Weakness: unclear how reweighting improves label separation without altering document semantics.  
- Weakness: contribution viewed as limited—linear transform plus NCA-style reweighting lacks strong novelty.  
- Practical concern: unclear how to compute reweighting for completely new/unseen documents.  
- Quality: technically sound; claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Originality: somewhat limited but the specific combination of methods is novel.  
- Significance: moderate—important for document classification but limited broader impact.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing reweighting justification and discussing limitations and applications.
