- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform and word-importance weights via a NCA-style k-NN loss.
- Uses entropically regularized Wasserstein (Sinkhorn) distance as a differentiable, efficient proxy to WMD to enable gradient-based learning.
- Derives and correctly presents gradients for the transform and word weights using the Sinkhorn approximation.
- Evaluates S-WMD on eight datasets against 26 baselines, claiming state-of-the-art average performance for k-NN text classification.
- Empirical methodology is rigorous, with extensive experiments and Bayesian hyperparameter tuning.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written, with detailed, easy-to-follow mathematical exposition and algorithm description.
- Major weakness: the critical entropic regularizer λ is barely analyzed, despite strongly affecting the objective, distance behavior, and gradients.
- Lack of ablation on λ leaves it unclear whether the method learns a metric for true WMD or for the Sinkhorn distance in practice.
- Confusing use of Word Centroid Distance (WCD) for initialization and approximate neighbor search without clear motivation linking it to the S-WMD objective.
- Novelty is limited: the work mainly combines existing techniques (WMD, NCA, Sinkhorn) rather than proposing a fundamental algorithmic advance.
- Nonetheless, the combination is a novel, practical synthesis that yields strong empirical results and is likely useful to others.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical supervised WMD extension, strong empirical results, excellent clarity and exposition.
- Arguments for rejection: ignored analysis of λ harms interpretability/reproducibility, limited conceptual novelty, and minor confusion about WCD’s role.
