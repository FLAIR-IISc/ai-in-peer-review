- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension to Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transform of embeddings (A) and word-importance weights (w).
- Addresses WMD’s limitation of being unsupervised by learning a task-specific transport-based distance.
- Training objective: minimize a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification loss inspired by Neighborhood Component Analysis.
- Key technical hurdle: differentiating the loss through the WMD optimization is computationally prohibitive and unstable.
- Main solution: approximate WMD with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance, yielding a strictly convex, differentiable objective and efficient gradients.
- Optimization enhancements: supervised Word Centroid Distance initialization and batch gradient updates using local document neighborhoods.
- Technical quality: sound derivations for gradients of A and w and a practical, scalable optimization pipeline.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, self-contained, and explains background and methods clearly.
- Experiments: extensive evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines, with S-WMD achieving state-of-the-art on most datasets.
- Additional empirical analysis: includes visualizations and training time comparisons to support claims.
- Weakness: slightly worse performance than unsupervised WMD on the CLASSIC dataset; potential issue with using squared Euclidean ground metric.
- Weakness: reliance on Sinkhorn regularization parameter (λ=10) without a sensitivity analysis.
- Overall: high quality, clarity, originality, and significance; strong candidate for NIPS.
