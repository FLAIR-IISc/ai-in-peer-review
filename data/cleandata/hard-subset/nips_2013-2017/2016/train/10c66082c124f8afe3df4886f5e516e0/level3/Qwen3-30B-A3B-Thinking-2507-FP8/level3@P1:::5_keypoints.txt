- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns word-specific importance weights and a linear transform of embeddings, using Sinkhorn approximation for efficiency.  
- Frames learning objective as minimizing leave-one-out kNN classification error via supervised metric learning.  
- Builds directly on Kusner et al.'s WMD and extends it with supervised metric-learning techniques.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: outperforms 26 baselines across 8 text classification tasks and achieves lowest average error.  
- Shows consistent improvements over unsupervised WMD, with error reductions up to 51% on some datasets.  
- Provides thorough evaluation and analysis of computational efficiency vs. approximation quality; training times comparable to other metric learning methods.  
- Mathematical exposition is clear and rigorous; gradient derivations and Sinkhorn details make the method reproducible.  
- Visualization of learned word weights effectively highlights class-relevant words.  
- Novelty is limited and largely incremental relative to prior WMD and existing metric learning approaches.  
- Confusing and unnecessary emphasis on Word Centroid Distance (WCD) / S-WCD as initialization, creating ambiguity.  
- Critical omission: poor justification and analysis of the entropic regularizer λ, whose value crucially affects behavior and efficiency.  
- Needs ablation studies and clarification on whether results approximate true WMD (large λ) or the Sinkhorn approximation (small λ).  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of how supervised learning affects the underlying optimal transport problem.  
- Overall significance is strong due to empirical gains and practicality, but tempered by limited novelty and parameter/analysis gaps; recommendation: accept with revisions.
