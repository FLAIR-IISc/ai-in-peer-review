- Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), extending WMD to incorporate supervision for domain-specific document metrics.  
- Learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights via discriminative training.  
- Optimization minimizes stochastic leave-one-out classification error.  
- Key technical contribution: efficient WMD gradient approximation using Sinkhorn distance to avoid cubic complexity.  
- Method is technically sound with clear theoretical motivation and derivation of gradient approximations.  
- Exceptionally comprehensive experimental evaluation across eight diverse text classification datasets.  
- Compared against 26 competitive baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, mSDA, NCA, ITML, LMNN, etc.).  
- S-WMD consistently outperforms all baselines, achieving state-of-the-art on five of eight datasets.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, accessible to readers familiar with WMD and metric learning.  
- Original contribution: uniquely combines optimal transport with discriminative metric learning for document distances.  
- Significant and practical: improves document metrics using existing word embeddings without extra training.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-executed, and suitable for presentation at NIPS.
