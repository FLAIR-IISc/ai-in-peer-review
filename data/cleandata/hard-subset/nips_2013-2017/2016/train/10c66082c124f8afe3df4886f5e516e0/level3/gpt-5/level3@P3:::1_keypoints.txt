- Proposal: S-WMD learns a linear ground metric A and a global word-importance vector w for WMD using Sinkhorn-regularized OT and an NCA-style kNN loss, with batching and neighbor pruning.  
- Technical soundness: Formulation and use of Sinkhorn for tractable gradients are sound; S-WCD initialization (NCA on centroids) is sensible.  
- Empirical support: Thorough evaluation on 8 datasets against 26 baselines showing competitive or state-of-the-art kNN accuracy and reasonable training times.  
- Key concern: S-WCD initialization often performs nearly as well as full S-WMD, calling into question the incremental benefit of learned OT.  
- Key concern: Missing ablations to isolate the individual contributions of A versus w.  
- Key concern: Missing baselines applying LMNN and ITML to word-centroid embeddings (S-WCD only covers NCA).  
- Clarity: Paper is well written, organized, and explains motivation and computational tricks clearly; code availability is a plus.  
- Originality: Modeling is incremental (linear A + global w); main novelty lies in efficient Sinkhorn-based optimization and scaling to documents.  
- Significance: Practically useful for supervised document distances, but impact depends on showing clear gains over simpler centroid+metric methods.  
- Pros: Clear extension of WMD to supervised settings, efficient optimization, strong empirical results, simple model leveraging pre-trained embeddings, interpretable weights.  
- Cons: Added OT complexity yields modest gains on some datasets; scope limited to linear/global parameters; missing ablations and baselines; hyperparameter analyses lacking.  
- Requested ablation: Train with (a) A learned, w fixed to 1; (b) w learned, A fixed to identity; (c) both — report accuracy and training times.  
- Requested baselines: Add LMNN and ITML run on word-centroid embeddings to compare with S-WCD.  
- Requested analysis: Quantify incremental gains of S-WMD over S-WCD per dataset and characterize when OT learning helps (e.g., doc length, vocab size, label granularity).  
- Requested sensitivity tests: Vary Sinkhorn λ, neighbor-pruning M, initialization quality, and embeddings (GloVe/fastText); report memory/time scaling with document length and vocabulary and show convergence plots.  
- Minor fixes: Clarify ambiguous citation, mark dual potentials α*,β*, explicitly link Sinkhorn outputs to duals, disambiguate symbols p vs q, fix typos, and state whether w is global or class-specific (and whether class-specific variants were tried).  
- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed, and useful work; borderline accept contingent on adding the missing centroid+metric baselines and ablations to demonstrate where OT-based learning gives clear gains.
