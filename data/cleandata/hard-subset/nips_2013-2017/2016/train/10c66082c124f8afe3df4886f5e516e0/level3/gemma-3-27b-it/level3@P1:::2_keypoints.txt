- Proposal: S-WMD — a supervised refinement of WMD that learns an affine transformation of word embeddings and word-importance weights to minimize leave-one-out k-NN error.
- Technical contribution: an efficient gradient approximation using the Sinkhorn distance to overcome WMD’s computational bottleneck.
- Framing: explicitly learns a transformation of the word embedding space (not a new document representation) and connects to EMD/metric learning literature.
- Motivation and soundness: clear rationale for supervised metric learning on top of WMD and well-justified simultaneous learning of transform and weights.
- Experimental strength: evaluated on 8 real-world text classification tasks and compared against 26 competitive baselines with consistent, substantial gains.
- Rigorous methodology: includes statistical significance (standard errors), Bayesian hyperparameter tuning, and t-SNE visualizations showing improved clustering.
- Reproducibility and clarity: thorough gradient derivations, sufficient detail for reproduction, and well-written presentation.
- Weakness: discussion of limitations is brief—insufficient exploration of document length effects and the squared Euclidean assumption.
- Weakness: complexity analysis could better address practical implications of the O(q^2) term with respect to vocabulary size.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed ablation study isolating the contributions of word-importance weighting versus embedding transformation.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, novel supervised approach to WMD, practical gradient approximation, comprehensive evaluation, and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited limitations/failure-case analysis, missing ablation, and incomplete complexity insights.
- Overall: strong, well-supported contribution with minor areas for improvement; reviewer recommends acceptance.
