- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns an affine transform of word embeddings and a word-importance weighting vector to align document distance with label similarity.
- Trains using a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective (NCA-style) and uses entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) for efficient, differentiable transport plans and dual variables.
- Initializes with a supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) and refines with batch updates using approximate neighborhoods filtered by the fast WCD lower bound.
- Uses Sinkhorn relaxation to avoid nested non-differentiable optimization and to obtain unique transport plans for gradient computation.
- Experiments on 8 text classification datasets against 26 baselines show consistent improvements; S-WMD achieves best average rank and top accuracy on most datasets.
- Visualizations and learned word weights support interpretability and task-adaptivity of the learned metric.
- Builds on WMD, Sinkhorn OT, and NCA, but novelly combines supervised ground-metric and word-weight learning for text with scalable, low-dimensional use of pre-trained embeddings.
- Methodological derivations, approximations, and gradient computations are clearly presented and well-motivated.
- Neighborhood filtering by WCD and S-WCD initialization are sensible practical contributions that improve efficiency.
- Empirical evaluation is extensive and includes many strong baselines (BOW, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, mSDA, WCD, WMD, supervised metrics).
- Limitations include reliance on entropy-regularized approximation (sensitivity to λ), use of squared Euclidean ground cost, and smaller gains on some long-document datasets.
- Regularization and constraints on A and w (to prevent degenerate scaling) are not thoroughly discussed.
- Paper clarity is high with good algorithmic detail, pseudo-code, complexity analysis, and gradient explanations.
- Minor clarity suggestions: explicitly state constraints/regularization on w and A, and summarize sensitivity to λ, batch size B, neighbor size N, and k.
- Originality: timely and novel combination of supervised metric learning, OT, and word reweighting for text; S-WCD initialization is a practical novel element.
- Significance: substantial improvements across datasets, useful for kNN classification, retrieval, clustering, with released code increasing impact.
- Pros: principled formulation, strong empirical gains, efficient training via approximations and filtering, interpretable word weights, released code.
- Cons: limited sensitivity analysis for λ and ground cost p, limited ablations separating A vs. w contributions, missing comparisons to contemporary neural text classifiers, and unclear regularization to avoid overfitting.
- Suggested ablations and analyses: only-A vs only-w, S-WCD vs full S-WMD, sensitivity plots for λ/B/N/r, runtime-accuracy trade-offs.
- Suggested extensions: evaluate alternative ground costs (cosine, L1), report constraints/regularization on w/A, test different embeddings (e.g., GloVe), and vary k in kNN and retrieval tasks.
- Recommendation: Accept — solid, well-executed contribution likely to influence supervised OT for NLP.
