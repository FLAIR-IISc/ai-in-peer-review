- Proposal: Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a Mahalanobis cost and reweights word histograms to supervise WMD for document classification.
- Loss & learning: Provides gradients for an NCA-inspired loss and uses entropy-regularized transport for computational efficiency.
- Empirical performance: Extensive experiments on eight datasets show S-WMD outperforms 26 competitive baselines.
- Conceptual strength: Method is simple yet non-trivial, combining optimal transport with supervised metric learning.
- Practicality: Fast gradient approximations make the approach scalable to large datasets.
- Interpretability: Visualization of learned word weights and t-SNE embeddings highlights model interpretability.
- Regularization clarity: Paper does not sufficiently explore trade-offs between regularized and non-regularized formulations.
- Notation and reproducibility: Gradient derivations and mathematical notation need more rigor to aid reproducibility.
- Algorithmic clarity: Lacks an algorithmic box summarizing S-WMD steps for reader clarity.
- Metric inconsistency: Uses squared Euclidean in the cost matrix inconsistently with other Euclidean metric usage.
- Generality: Does not discuss generalization to p-Wasserstein distances, limiting methodological flexibility.
- Reweighting novelty: The reweighting idea is not new in text classification and is insufficiently acknowledged in related work.
- Ablation/insights: Insufficient experimental analysis of the importance and role of the weight vector w.
- Related work: Related work section fails to clearly distinguish the paper from prior methods (e.g., entropy regularization, continuous cost parameterization).
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions, addressing metric inconsistencies and adding an algorithmic summary.
