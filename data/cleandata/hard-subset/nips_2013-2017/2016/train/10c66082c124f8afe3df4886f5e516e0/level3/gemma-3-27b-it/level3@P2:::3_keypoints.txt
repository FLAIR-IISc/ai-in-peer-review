- Proposes S-WMD: learns a linear transformation of embeddings and word-specific importance weights to adapt WMD via leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification loss.
- Uses entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) to enable efficient gradient computation and avoid nested optimization.
- Empirical evaluation on eight datasets shows consistently strong performance, outperforming 26 baselines.
- Includes ablation studies (S-WMD initialization) that clarify the contribution of components.
- Positions work within prior document-distance literature and metric learning (NCA, LMNN, ITML), extending WMD with supervision.
- Fails to explicitly cite and discuss related reweighting schemes in optimal transport (e.g., Lebanon & Cuturi).
- Strength: conceptually simple and intuitive, leveraging pre-trained embeddings while adapting to specific tasks.
- Strength: technical contribution of efficient gradient-based supervised WMD learning addresses computational bottlenecks.
- Strength: thorough empirical comparisons across diverse datasets support robustness of results.
- Weakness: limited exploration of the interplay between regularized (Sinkhorn) and unregularized OT formulations.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of how the regularization parameter affects approximation accuracy and computational cost.
- Weakness: mathematical derivations and gradient calculations need clearer, more rigorous treatment.
- Weakness: lacks an algorithmic box or concise procedural summary of the S-WMD learning algorithm.
- Weakness: notational inconsistency between Eq.1 (Euclidean) and Eq.6 (squared Euclidean); should reference p-Wasserstein notation.
- Weakness: missing analysis of the learned weight vector w (which words are upweighted and why).
- Needs clearer articulation of specific limitations of related methods (e.g., Wang et al.) relative to S-WMD.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated, and empirically effective paper with minor methodological and exposition issues; recommend acceptance.
