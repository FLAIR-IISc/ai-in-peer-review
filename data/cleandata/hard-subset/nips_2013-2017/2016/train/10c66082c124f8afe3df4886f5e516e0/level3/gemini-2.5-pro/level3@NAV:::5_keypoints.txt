- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns an affine transformation of embeddings and word-specific importance weights for task-specific document metric learning.  
- Uses entropically regularized Sinkhorn distance as a differentiable proxy to make WMD optimization tractable and trains with a stochastic nearest-neighbor classification loss.  
- Evaluated on eight text classification datasets and outperforms 26 competitive baselines on average.  
- Paper has exceptional clarity and thorough mathematical exposition, making the method easy to understand and reproduce.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation convincingly demonstrates empirical effectiveness.  
- Addresses a clear limitation of WMD by providing a supervised, practical solution.  
- Contribution is incremental—combines existing metric learning and optimization techniques with WMD rather than introducing a fundamentally new approach.  
- Major technical weakness: insufficient analysis and justification of the entropic regularizer lambda (chosen as 10), which crucially affects transport behavior.  
- Unclear whether success is due to learning a metric for true Wasserstein distance or the qualitatively different Sinkhorn distance; lacks ablation on lambda.  
- Initialization using Word Centroid Distance is pragmatic but feels ad-hoc and somewhat confuses the central contribution.  
- Overall: well-written with strong empirical results but limited novelty and weakened by the missing rigorous analysis of the lambda hyperparameter.
