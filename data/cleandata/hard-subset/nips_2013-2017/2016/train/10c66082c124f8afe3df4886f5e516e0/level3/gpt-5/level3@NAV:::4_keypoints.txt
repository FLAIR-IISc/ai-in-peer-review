- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns a linear transform A and a global word-importance vector w with an NCA-style leave-one-out objective.  
- Replaces exact WMD with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn for scalability and derives efficient gradients via primal transport T* and dual potentials (α, β).  
- Situates work relative to WMD, Sinkhorn distances, supervised metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML), and supervised EMD ground-metric learning in vision.  
- Contribution: instantiation of these ideas for document distances, with an efficient training pipeline and extensive evaluation.  
- Method is technically sound and gradient approximations are reasonable.  
- Experiments are broad (8 datasets, 26 baselines), generally support claims, and code is released.  
- Missing ablation isolating effects of A vs w and experiments on w-only, A-only, and combined variants.  
- Need sensitivity analysis for entropy regularizer λ and reporting of training/inference costs.  
- Inference pipeline unclear: is w fixed at test time and how are OOV words handled—make test-time deployment explicit.  
- Motivation and semantics of global reweighting w are underdeveloped; must justify why w improves label separation without corrupting document semantics and consider regularization (e.g., KL to uniform, positivity/box constraints).  
- Terminology: “semantic difference” is ambiguous—suggest “label-informed similarity” or similar.  
- Originality assessed as moderate: components are known, learning w is a natural incremental extension, but combination is well executed.  
- Significance: consistent and sometimes large empirical gains; practical for kNN-style retrieval/classification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear method, efficient Sinkhorn training, strong empirical results, released code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty and weak justification/empirical support for reweighting due to missing ablations and inference clarity; reviewer would accept if issues—especially role/deployment of w—are addressed.
