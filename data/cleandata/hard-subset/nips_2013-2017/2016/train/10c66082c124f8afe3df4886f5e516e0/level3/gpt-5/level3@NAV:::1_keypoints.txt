- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD that learns a supervised ground cost by jointly optimizing a linear projection A and document word re-weighting w using a stochastic LOO kNN objective.
- Optimization: Replaces exact EMD with entropy-regularized OT and uses Sinkhorn scaling to derive efficient gradients, yielding O(q^2) per-pair costs.
- Technical soundness: Careful derivation of approximate gradients for A (via relaxed transport plan) and w (via relaxed dual variables).
- Empirical results: Broad evaluation on eight kNN text classification tasks with generally strong results, but gains over the WCD/NCA initialization are modest on several datasets.
- Missing evaluations: Lacks baselines applying ITML/LMNN to word-centroid embeddings and an ablation isolating the effects of A vs. w.
- Clarity: Paper is well written and the optimization section is clear; needs minor clarifications about “The authors,” stars on α/β in Eq.11, and explicit note that gradients use Sinkhorn scaling.
- Originality: Interesting combination of learning a linear ground metric and word weights for text; main novelty is in efficient optimization and NCA-style integration; should better discuss prior EMD ground-metric learning (e.g., Wang & Guibas).
- Significance: Practical, reusable approach with released code, but impact is tempered by closeness to the initialization.
- Pros: Well-engineered extension of WMD, clear optimization likely to interest OT/NLP practitioners, broad evaluation, released code.
- Cons and recommendation: Missing baselines/ablations and limited incremental gains on some datasets; recommend acceptance contingent on adding these experiments and minor clarifications.
