- Proposes S-WMD: learns a linear transform A of word embeddings and a global word-importance vector w to supervise WMD via a stochastic LOO kNN/NCA objective.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD for forward passes and gradient computation.  
- Derives gradients w.r.t. A using relaxed optimal transport T*λ and w using relaxed dual variables from Sinkhorn scaling vectors.  
- Practical training pipeline: S-WCD initialization for A, batch training, and neighbor filtering to accelerate learning.  
- Empirical evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines shows consistent improvements or competitive performance; code released.  
- Strong literature grounding at intersection of WMD/OT, supervised metric learning, and Sinkhorn approximations.  
- Strength: clear exposition, solid literature review, and helpful visualizations and qualitative analysis of learned weights.  
- Strength: well-engineered, practical optimization choices and training heuristics that make supervised WMD feasible and fast.  
- Strength: extensive experiments, careful tuning, reporting of ranks/error rates, and reproducibility via released code.  
- Weakness: insufficient motivation and justification for global histogram reweighting w and its semantic implications vs. IDF/stopword schemes.  
- Weakness: perceived incremental novelty—combines existing ideas (NCA-style loss, linear transform, Sinkhorn) with modest conceptual advance.  
- Weakness: practical clarity lacking about w at test time and whether A and w are global vs. per-document parameters.  
- Weakness: ambiguous terminology (e.g., “semantic difference”); suggest using “task-specific similarity.”  
- Weakness: limited ablation studies isolating contributions of A vs. w, initialization effects, and hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Weakness: no discussion of theoretical properties—whether learned distance remains a metric and bounds on Sinkhorn-induced bias.  
- Quality: method is technically sound with coherent gradient derivations and effective heuristics.  
- Originality: moderate—useful integration into text domain but not a large conceptual leap.  
- Significance: practically valuable for kNN document classification/retrieval and likely to influence WMD usage in practice.  
- Questions for authors: clarify global vs per-document nature of w, constraints/regularization on w, test-time use of exact WMD vs Sinkhorn, required ablations, failure modes on datasets where S-WMD underperforms, and wording fixes.  
- Minor comments: discuss OOV handling and transfer robustness, state w≥0 and histogram normalization, and comment on whether the learned distance preserves metric properties.  
- Arguments for acceptance: clear, well-executed work with strong empirical gains and practical training advances.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited conceptual novelty, weak motivation/analysis for w, and missing ablations/clarifications.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept / weak accept, contingent on clarifying role/constraints of w, test-time usage, and adding ablation studies.
