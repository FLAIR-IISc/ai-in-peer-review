- Introduces S-WMD: learns a linear transform of word embeddings (A) and a word importance vector (w) to improve WMD for supervised tasks.  
- Optimization minimizes leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error using an efficient Sinkhorn-based approximation to WMD gradients.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms 26 baselines across 8 real-world text classification datasets.  
- Well-positioned in related work, relating to bag-of-words, LSI/LDA, metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML), EMD, and Cuturi’s regularized transport.  
- Writing is clear and paper is well-organized, making complex concepts accessible.  
- Thorough literature review that properly situates the contribution.  
- Extensive and convincing experimental evaluation with practical training-time analysis.  
- Code is available, enabling reproducibility.  
- Weak motivation and insufficient justification for the word importance weighting vector (w).  
- Concern that reweighting word frequencies may distort document semantics; unclear how semantic integrity is preserved versus simply removing words.  
- Contribution may be incremental since it applies existing metric-learning ideas to the WMD framework; novelty needs clearer articulation.  
- Practical issue: unclear how the learned w generalizes to entirely new, unseen documents.  
- Ambiguous use of the term “semantic difference,” conflating semantics with label/topic differences.  
- Recommendation: acceptable if authors address motivation/justification for w, generalization to unseen docs, clarify terminology, and discuss limitations.
