- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a task-specific document distance by learning a linear transformation A (Mahalanobis metric) and per-word importance weights w.  
- Incorporates supervision by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out k-NN classification loss following Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA).  
- Addresses intractable WMD gradients by using entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn distance) to obtain a fast, differentiable approximation for gradient-based learning.  
- Empirical evaluation on eight text classification datasets shows S-WMD consistently outperforms 26 strong baselines, including WMD and supervised/unsupervised alternatives.  
- Main technical strength: practical and effective use of Sinkhorn relaxation to make metric learning for WMD computationally feasible.  
- Experimental strength: thorough comparisons with many baselines and consistent state-of-the-art kNN classification performance.  
- Weakness: lacks an ablation study disentangling the contributions of learned metric A versus word weights w.  
- Clarity issue: needs a more rigorous discussion of how gradients of the regularized Sinkhorn distance relate to (sub)gradients of the original unregularized WMD.  
- Notational inconsistency: WMD is introduced with a general p-norm but the supervised objective uses squared Euclidean distance; clarity would improve by specifying 2-Wasserstein/squared form upfront.  
- Originality: novel and effective combination of WMD, metric learning, and Sinkhorn, but similar ideas exist for learning EMD ground metrics and histogram re-weighting.  
- Suggestion: better contextualize and cite related work (e.g., Guy Lebanon, Le & Cuturi, Cuturi & Avis, Wang et al.) to clarify distinctions.  
- Significance: provides a principled, efficient way to adapt WMD to supervised tasks and is likely to be adopted and inspire further work.  
- Recommendation: strong paper with minor fixable weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
