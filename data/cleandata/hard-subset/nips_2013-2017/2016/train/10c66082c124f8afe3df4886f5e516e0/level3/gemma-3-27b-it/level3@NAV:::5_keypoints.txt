- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns word-specific weights and a linear embedding transform, using Sinkhorn for speed.  
- Learns re-weighted word frequencies and a linear transformation to minimize k-NN classification error.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: best average across eight datasets and 26 baselines.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear mathematical exposition and detailed gradient derivations.  
- Establishes a solid connection to prior metric learning work, particularly NCA.  
- Includes an ablation study showing the effect of S-WMD initialization and the supervised component.  
- Contribution is incremental—largely builds on existing WMD research and standard metric learning techniques.  
- Major weakness: insufficient discussion and sensitivity analysis of the entropic regularizer parameter lambda and its impact.  
- References to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) are frequent, feel tangential, and can be confusing; clarify or remove them.  
- Efficient implementation leveraging the Sinkhorn approximation is a practical strength.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing lambda discussion and WCD clarity.
