This paper proposes an extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) by incorporating supervised metric learning for documents. The authors introduce a linear transform A and word importance weights w to improve the document distance metric. The method, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), is evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its relation to previous work. The introduction provides a thorough background on document distances and the WMD, making it easy to follow for readers unfamiliar with the topic. The authors also provide a detailed description of the proposed method, including the optimization process and the fast computation of the gradient.

The evaluation of the proposed method is thorough, with results on eight different datasets and comparisons to various baselines. The authors also provide a visualization of the results using t-SNE, which helps to illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper also includes a discussion on the related work, which provides a comprehensive overview of the field.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to leverage the large unsupervised corpus used to learn the word2vec embedding. This allows the proposed method to benefit from the high-quality word embeddings while still incorporating supervision to improve the document distance metric.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. The authors claim that the proposed method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words." While the results do show that the proposed method can capture semantic similarities between documents, it would be helpful to provide more insight into why this is the case. Additionally, the authors could provide more detail on how the word importance weights w are learned and how they contribute to the overall performance of the method.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its relation to previous work. However, there are a few areas where the notation could be improved. For example, the use of subscripts and superscripts could be clarified, and the authors could provide a table or list of notation to help readers keep track of the various symbols and variables used throughout the paper.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel extension to the WMD by incorporating supervised metric learning for documents. The use of a linear transform A and word importance weights w is a unique contribution to the field, and the authors provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in natural language processing and provides a significant improvement over existing methods. The proposed method has the potential to be widely adopted in various applications, including text classification, sentiment analysis, and information retrieval.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its relation to previous work. The evaluation is thorough, and the results are impressive. While there are a few areas that could be improved, the paper makes a significant contribution to the field and has the potential to be widely adopted.
