- Introduces S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear projection of word embeddings and word-importance weights, optimized by minimizing stochastic leave-one-out kNN error (NCA-inspired) with efficient Sinkhorn-based gradient computation.

- Strength: interesting and useful extension of WMD that achieves strong results on multiple kNN text classification benchmarks.

- Strength: paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and pleasant to read.

- Major weakness: full S-WMD optimization yields only marginal improvements over the simpler initialization S-WCD, questioning the practical value of the more expensive method.

- Missing baseline: no comparison to applying existing metric-learning methods (e.g., ITML or LMNN) to word-centroid document embeddings, which could be competitive.

- Missing ablation: no study isolating the contributions of the projection matrix A versus the word-specific weights w.

- Limited novelty: learning a linear metric within the Earth Mover’s Distance framework is interesting but somewhat limited as a core contribution.

- Minor clarifications requested: specify which paper "The authors" refers to (Line 152); denote optimal dual variables in Eq. 11 as α*, β*; explicitly state use of Sinkhorn scaling (Line 170).
