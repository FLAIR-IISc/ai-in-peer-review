- Proposes S-WMD: parameterizes WMD cost with a Mahalanobis distance and learns histogram reweighting coefficients to create a supervised metric.  
- Uses NCA-type objective and practical gradient updates to optimize parameterized EMD while avoiding cubic complexity of exact WMD.  
- Strong empirical validation: thorough evaluations on 8 real-world datasets against 26 competitive baselines, consistently outperforming them.  
- Methodological clarity: simple combination of existing techniques that preserves WMD’s low-dimensional advantage without per-document representation learning.  
- Notational inconsistency: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean (2-Wasserstein); recommend consistently describing WMD as p-Wasserstein.  
- Needs clearer discussion of the interplay between regularized (Sinkhorn) and unregularized OT formulations and implications for approximation quality.  
- Reweighting in Eq.5 is not novel; prior work by Guy Lebanon and Le/Cuturi on histogram reweighting should be cited.  
- Empirical analysis should probe the learned weight vector w more deeply to explain its importance beyond performance gains.  
- Related work should more clearly distinguish this approach from Cuturi/Avis (entropy regularization, continuous vs discrete cost) and from Wang et al. (limitations of their linear parameterization).  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-executed paper with meaningful contribution and strong empirical support; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
