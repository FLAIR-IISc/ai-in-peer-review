- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): jointly learns a linear transformation of embeddings and word importance weights to make WMD discriminative.  
- Key technical innovation: efficient gradient approximation of WMD using Sinkhorn distance to enable supervised training.  
- Builds on Kusner et al. (2015) by extending WMD from unsupervised to supervised metric learning.  
- Empirical results: outperforms 26 baselines across eight diverse text classification datasets.  
- Large empirical gains reported (e.g., 51% relative error reduction on OHSUMED).  
- Comprehensive experimental design with hyperparameter tuning and statistical significance testing.  
- Ablation studies confirm both the linear transform and word weighting contribute to performance.  
- Runtime analysis shows the method is computationally practical for large-scale tasks.  
- Visualizations (t-SNE, learned word weights) increase interpretability of the model.  
- Method is technically sound with clear mathematical exposition and reproducible pseudo-code.  
- Originality: novel adaptation of metric learning techniques to the WMD/EMD setting, distinct from prior work.  
- Significance: enables practical incorporation of supervision into WMD, with broad implications for retrieval/classification.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, convincing experiments, practical algorithm, clear comparisons to prior work.  
- Limitation acknowledged: performance depends on quality of pre-trained word embeddings and initialization.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of different embedding types (e.g., GloVe vs. word2vec).  
- Weakness: no explicit analysis of performance on extremely short texts (e.g., tweets).  
- Weakness: no discussion on integrating S-WMD with modern deep learning text classifiers.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, significant contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS 2016.
