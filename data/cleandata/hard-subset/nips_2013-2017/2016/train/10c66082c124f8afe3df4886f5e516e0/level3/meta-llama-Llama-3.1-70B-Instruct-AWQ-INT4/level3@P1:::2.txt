The paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), a document metric that has shown state-of-the-art results in kNN-based document classification. The original WMD is unsupervised and does not incorporate any task-specific information. The proposed method, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), learns a linear transformation of the word embedding space and a word importance vector to improve the distance metric.

The paper is well-motivated, and the reviewer appreciates the application of discriminative training to learn a domain-specific metric from WMD. The method is sound and clearly explained with appropriate detail. The experimental evaluation is extensive, covering several classification datasets and a wide range of baselines and features. The proposed method outperforms all baselines by a large margin, demonstrating its effectiveness.

The strengths of the paper include:

* A well-motivated and clearly explained method that builds upon existing work on WMD.
* An extensive experimental evaluation that covers several classification datasets and a wide range of baselines and features.
* The proposed method outperforms all baselines by a large margin, demonstrating its effectiveness.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The method may not be applicable to all types of text data, as it relies on the availability of labeled data.
* The computational complexity of the method may be high, especially for large datasets.
