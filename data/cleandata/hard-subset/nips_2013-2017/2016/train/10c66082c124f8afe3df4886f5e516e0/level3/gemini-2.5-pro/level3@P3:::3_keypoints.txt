- Proposal: Introduces Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a Mahalanobis ground metric A and histogram weights w using an NCA objective and Sinkhorn (entropy-regularized OT) for differentiability.  
- Empirical result: Strong experiments on eight datasets showing S-WMD outperforms 26 competitive baselines (state-of-the-art on five datasets).  

Strengths:
- Significance: Addresses a clear limitation of WMD by incorporating supervision, likely to be widely useful.  
- Novelty/soundness: Novel synthesis of WMD, metric learning (NCA), and entropy-regularized OT; learning A and w jointly is well-motivated.  
- Empirical strength: Comprehensive comparisons and consistently strong performance across datasets and baselines.

Weaknesses / suggestions — Mathematical rigor & clarity:
- Regularized vs unregularized OT: Need clearer discussion on using Sinkhorn gradients vs subgradients of unregularized WMD and how approximation depends on regularization λ.  
- Notational inconsistency: Clarify distance notation (p-Wasserstein vs squared 2-Wasserstein) and state explicitly that S-WMD focuses on squared 2-Wasserstein.  
- Algorithm clarity: Algorithm 1 omits key gradient details; include explicit gradient update rules (from Eqs. 8 and 10) for reproducibility.

Weaknesses / suggestions — Positioning & prior work:
- Histogram reweighting prior work: Cite and discuss prior ideas on learning histogram importance (e.g., Guy Lebanon, Cuturi & Doucet).  
- Comparison to related work: Sharpen Related Work—distinguish from Cuturi & Avis (2011) and contrast Wang et al. (2012)’s ITML framework with this NCA-based approach.

Question for authors:
- Ablation request: Provide ablation showing performance when learning only A (w=1) or only w (A=I) to quantify the relative contributions of each component.

Overall assessment:
- High-quality, well-executed paper that combines optimal transport and metric learning effectively; strong empirical evidence; recommend acceptance.
