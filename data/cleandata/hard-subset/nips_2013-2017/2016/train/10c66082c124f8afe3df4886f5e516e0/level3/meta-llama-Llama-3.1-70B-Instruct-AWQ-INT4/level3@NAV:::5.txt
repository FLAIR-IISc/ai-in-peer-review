**Paper Review: Supervised Word Mover's Distance**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a supervised extension of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) by learning word-specific weights and using Sinkhorn distance as a faster proxy for Wasserstein distance. The method, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), is evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance. The paper is well-written with a clear mathematical exposition.

**Strengths**

* The paper presents a novel approach to learning a supervised document distance metric, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving document classification performance.
* The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the mathematical derivations and experimental results.

**Weaknesses**

* The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited, as it builds upon previous work on Word Mover's Distance. While the authors do introduce a new supervised learning component, the core idea of using word embeddings and optimal transport is not entirely new.
* The references to Word Centroid Distance are not well-integrated into the paper and seem somewhat tangential to the main contribution.
* The role and choice of the entropic regularizer (λ) are not well-discussed in the manuscript, which is a crucial component of the Sinkhorn distance algorithm. The authors should provide more insight into the impact of λ on the behavior and results of the method.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their method. The experimental results are well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. However, the lack of discussion on the entropic regularizer is a notable omission.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the mathematical derivations and experimental results. The authors provide sufficient background information on the Word Mover's Distance and Sinkhorn distance algorithms, making the paper accessible to readers with a basic understanding of natural language processing and optimal transport.

**Originality**

While the paper builds upon previous work on Word Mover's Distance, the introduction of a supervised learning component is a novel contribution. However, the core idea of using word embeddings and optimal transport is not entirely new, which limits the overall originality of the paper.

**Significance**

The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing, as it introduces a new approach to learning a supervised document distance metric. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in improving document classification performance, which has important implications for applications such as text retrieval and sentiment analysis.

**Arguments for acceptance**

* The paper presents a novel approach to learning a supervised document distance metric, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of their method on multiple datasets, demonstrating its effectiveness in improving document classification performance.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the mathematical derivations and experimental results.

**Arguments against acceptance**

* The novelty of the paper is somewhat limited, as it builds upon previous work on Word Mover's Distance.
* The lack of discussion on the entropic regularizer is a notable omission, which may impact the behavior and results of the method.
* The references to Word Centroid Distance are not well-integrated into the paper and seem somewhat tangential to the main contribution.
