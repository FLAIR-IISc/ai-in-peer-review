- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD that learns a Mahalanobis ground cost and histogram reweighting for documents.  
- Builds on WMD (optimal transport between word embeddings) and minimizes leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error.  
- Main technical contribution: efficient gradient-based optimization for NCA-type objectives in WMD using Sinkhorn approximation.  
- Complexity reduced to O(q²) from O(q³ log q) by using regularized Sinkhorn distances.  
- Introduces a histogram reweighting vector w to emphasize classification-relevant words.  
- Strong empirical evaluation across eight datasets and 26 baselines; wins on 5/8 and best average error.  
- Visualization shows learned word weights capture semantically relevant words.  
- Method is technically sound, well-explained, and training times are competitive with baseline metric learners.  
- Notational inconsistency: mixing Euclidean and squared-Euclidean distances leaves p-Wasserstein setting unclear.  
- Missing rigorous derivations: gradient formulas (Eq. 8) and dual-variable proofs are not provided; an algorithm box would help.  
- Reweighting mechanism is not novel; prior work (Lebanon 2006; Le & Cuturi 2014) should be cited.  
- Need discussion of effects of using regularized (Sinkhorn) vs. unregularized OT on the optimization objective.  
- Empirical analysis of learned weight vector w is insufficient; authors should analyze which words get high weights and why.  
- Related work should better distinguish S-WMD from Cuturi & Avis (2014) and Wang et al. (2015), noting histogram reweighting as a key difference.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix notation, add derivations/citation clarifications, and deepen analysis of w.
