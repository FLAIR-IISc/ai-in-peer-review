- Introduces S-WMD: supervised metric learning for Word Mover’s Distance via affine word-embedding transformation and word importance weights.  
- Uses a relaxed optimal transport objective (Sinkhorn distance) for efficient optimization and practical parameter updates.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over 26 baselines on eight real-world text classification tasks.  
- Builds on prior document-distance and metric-learning literature, correctly situating contributions relative to WMD, EMD, and Sinkhorn approximations.  
- Strength: natural and valuable idea to incorporate supervision into WMD with a technically sound approach and clear exposition.  
- Strength: extensive empirical evaluation and public code release supporting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: impact may be limited because S-WCD initialization achieves performance comparable to full S-WMD in Table 3.  
- Missing baseline: does not compare metric learning applied to word-centroid embeddings (e.g., ITML, LMNN, NCA) to isolate benefits of joint WMD learning.  
- Missing ablation: no study disentangling contributions of word-specific weights versus the linear projection matrix A.  
- Novelty concern: main novelty centers on the optimization trick (lines 156–175), which is incremental given prior optimal transport approximations.  
- Minor clarifications needed: ambiguous reference “The authors” (line 152), missing stars on α and β in Equation 11, and explicit mention of using earlier-described Sinkhorn scaling around line 170.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, clear writing, relevant problem, sound/novel optimization, and released code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited improvement beyond initialization, missing centroid-metric-learning baselines, lack of ablation study, and incremental optimization novelty.  
- Recommendation: accept conditionally if authors add the missing baseline comparisons and ablation studies in a revision.
