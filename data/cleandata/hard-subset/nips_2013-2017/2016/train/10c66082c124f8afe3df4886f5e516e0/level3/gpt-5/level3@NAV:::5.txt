Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of WMD that learns (i) a linear transform A of the ground embedding and (ii) word-specific importance weights w, trained to minimize a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective (NCA). To make training practical, the authors replace exact Wasserstein/EMD with entropically regularized OT (Sinkhorn) and use its dual potentials and transport plan to derive efficient gradients. The work builds directly on WMD (Kusner et al.), entropic OT and Sinkhorn scaling (Cuturi), and supervised metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML). It is also connected to supervised EMD/ground-metric learning in vision (e.g., Wang & Guibas; ITML-based EMD), with the main difference being the NLP setting, joint learning of A and w, and extensive empirical evaluation.

Quality: The paper is technically careful and the exposition is clear. Using Sinkhorn to obtain unique T* and apply Danskin/envelope arguments is reasonable, though the manuscript should explicitly justify the differentiation (cite Danskin and clarify that strong convexity ensures validity). The empirical section is thorough (8 datasets, 26 baselines) with consistent average gains. However, the role of the entropic regularizer λ is under-discussed. Because λ fundamentally changes the geometry (from unregularized Wasserstein at large λ to highly entropic Sinkhorn at small λ), conclusions may depend on λ. Sensitivity curves for λ, runtime-accuracy trade-offs, and a discussion of whether the reported improvements persist as λ→∞ are needed.

Clarity: Writing is strong overall. Two sources of confusion: (1) repeated references to Word Centroid Distance (WCD). While useful as a lower bound and for initialization, its conceptual role in S-WMD should be deemphasized; the current narrative risks conflating centroid methods with OT. (2) Notational reuse of p for different concepts (norm power vs dimension in the dual) and the statement that “most users choose small λ for speed” should be aligned with guidance on acceptable behavior changes.

Originality: Moderate. The combination—learning a ground metric and histogram reweighting for WMD with Sinkhorn-based training in NLP—is incremental relative to prior supervised EMD/OT, but well instantiated.

Significance: Empirical gains and code release make this useful for practitioners. The method is likely to be adopted.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong empirical results across many baselines and datasets.
- Clear, well-explained method; practical and efficient training.
- Joint learning of A and w is impactful and interpretable.

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited novelty relative to supervised EMD/OT literature.
- Insufficient treatment of λ; behavior may differ substantially from WMD.
- Confusing positioning of WCD and some notation issues.

Suggestions:
- Add λ sensitivity and large-λ (near-EMD) experiments; report default λ and selection protocol.
- Ablate A vs w vs both.
- Clarify WCD’s role and clean up notation; explicitly cite Danskin/the envelope theorem in gradient derivations.
