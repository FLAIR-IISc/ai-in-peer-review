Summary: This paper extends Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) to a supervised setting by learning (i) a linear transform A of the word-embedding space that defines the ground cost, and (ii) a word-importance vector w that reweights document histograms. Training minimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective, with gradients made tractable via the Sinkhorn-regularized transport and dual-variable approximations. Compared to prior work, the paper sits at the intersection of WMD (Kusner et al.), Sinkhorn distances (Cuturi), and supervised metric learning (e.g., NCA/LMNN/ITML), and contributes a pragmatic recipe to inject supervision directly into the transport ground metric and the term weights.

Strengths (Quality, Originality, Significance, Clarity):
- Clear and well-motivated formulation; the approximation strategy via entropy-regularized transport is sensible and computationally attractive.
- Strong empirical evaluation across eight datasets with many baselines; improvements are consistent and often substantial.
- Good engineering choices (WCD/NCA-based initialization, neighborhood pruning) yield an efficient pipeline.
- Rebuttal clarifications were careful and helpful; code release in Matlab is appreciated for reproducibility.

Weaknesses and clarification requests:
- Intuition behind Eq. (5) and w: Please provide a clearer narrative for how w differs conceptually from TF-IDF/BM25 and how normalization in Eq. (4) interacts with transport constraints. Is w constrained to be nonnegative, and is there any regularization to avoid degenerate upweighting?
- Ablation: Which component drives gains? Please report results for: only A learned (w=1), only w learned (A=I), A+w, and the S-WCD initialization alone, to quantify contributions and the importance of fine-tuning beyond initialization.
- Use of Eq. (2) and λ: Clarify when the entropy-regularized transport is used (training only vs also at test time), and whether the λ in Eq. (2) is the same λ referenced at ln170; include sensitivity analyses or default guidance.
- “Forgiving when documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words” (ln316): provide empirical support, e.g., class-conditional nearest neighbor swaps attributable to A (semantic alignment) vs w (lexical reweighting).
- Results reporting: I could not find the “average error across datasets” mentioned at ln257—should this correspond to the average rank column, or is it in Table 3 (which appears to be training time)?
- Table 2: please clarify the meaning of columns “n” vs “ne”.
- k in kNN: How is k selected (validation, BO), and is it dataset- or method-dependent? State the chosen k per dataset/method for reproducibility.
- Prior suggestion to compare with simple classifiers on the same datasets is now moot per rebuttal clarification.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel supervised extension of WMD with principled, efficient optimization.
- Strong empirical gains with broad baselines; code release.

Cons for acceptance:
- Missing ablations obscure the source of improvements.
- Several presentation ambiguities (λ, Eq. (5) intuition, tables, k selection) hinder reproducibility.

Overall: A solid and timely contribution; with the requested clarifications and ablations, this would be a strong accept.
