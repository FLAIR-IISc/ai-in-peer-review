- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD learning a linear embedding transform A and nonnegative word-importance vector w for kNN classification via OT distances.

- Uses entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) to obtain differentiable, efficient transport plans and approximate gradients for A and w.

- Accelerates training via S-WCD initialization (NCA on Word Centroid Distance) and WCD-based neighbor filtering.

- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving best average rank and winning 5/8 datasets; code released.

- Methodologically sound: NCA objective and Sinkhorn relaxation are appropriate and derivations clear.

- Main approximation hinges on entropy regularization λ, but λ fixed at 10 with minimal analysis; choice can materially affect learned metric.

- Unclear whether evaluations use exact WMD with learned A,w or Sinkhorn distance and how results vary with λ.

- Heavy reliance on WCD for initialization and neighbor pruning may obscure the core S-WMD contribution; need ablations.

- Empirics are broad and strong, but novelty is incremental relative to prior OT/EMD metric learning literature.

- Requests for additional analyses: sensitivity to λ; effect of learned w vs fixed; exact WMD at test time vs Sinkhorn; sensitivity to k and neighbor count N; p=1 vs squared L2 ground costs.

- Paper is clearly written with solid math, but discussion of λ’s limiting behaviors and selection guidance is insufficient.

- Suggest moving WCD-related material to supplement and clearly separate its role (speed/initialization) from the learned metric.

- Minor clarity: specify constraints/regularization on A (e.g., Frobenius penalty, rank r) and w (nonnegativity, sparsity).

- Pros: principled Sinkhorn training, strong empirical results, joint learning of interpretable weights, clear writing, released code.

- Cons: limited novelty vs prior OT metric learning, insufficient λ analysis, overreliance on WCD, and unclear approximation gap between Sinkhorn and exact WMD.

- Suggestions: ablate λ, report test-time distance choice and both metrics, ablate w/initialization/filtering, regularize/report sensitivity to A’s rank, and streamline WCD narrative.

- Overall recommendation: Weak Accept (confidence medium-high) — technically sound, useful empirical gains, but needs λ analysis and clearer disentangling of WCD role.
