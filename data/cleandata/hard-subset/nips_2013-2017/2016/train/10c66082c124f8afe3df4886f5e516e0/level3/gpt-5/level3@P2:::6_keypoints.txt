- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform A and per-word importance weights w using a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA) objective.
- Uses entropically-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) to enable O(q^2) differentiable distance computation and efficient gradient estimates via approximate dual variables.
- Initialization via supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD), followed by mini-batch training with nearest-neighbor pruning.
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, showing consistent kNN classification gains and competitive training times; Matlab code promised.
- Builds on prior work: WMD (Kusner et al.), entropic OT (Cuturi), and NCA metric learning; extends ground-metric learning to text by transforming embeddings and learning histogram weights.
- Differs from prior OT/EMD metric learning (which learns Mahalanobis metrics) by learning A in embedding space and per-word weights w for text.
- Strength: clear, well-motivated idea to align OT distances with labels while keeping low-dimensional ground space.
- Strength: solid engineering — Sinkhorn relaxation, NCA objective, batching, and neighbor filtering for practical efficiency.
- Strength: comprehensive evaluation with careful hyperparameter tuning (Bayesian optimization) and multiple baselines.
- Strength: good initialization (S-WCD) and evidence that training improves over initialization on most tasks.
- Strength: code release increases reproducibility and practical impact.
- Weakness: missing ablation quantifying contributions of A vs w (need experiments: only A, only w, both).
- Weakness: not enough intuition or illustrative examples for Eq. (5) and the effect of w on reweighted histograms.
- Weakness: unclear treatment of entropic regularization λ — clarify that gradients use the entropic relaxation, confirm λ consistency, report sensitivity analysis and trade-offs.
- Weakness: claim about being forgiving for semantically similar words needs empirical/qualitative justification (e.g., nearest-neighbor examples).
- Weakness: ambiguous reference to “average error” and pointer to Table 3 — needs correction or proper summary statistics with CIs.
- Weakness: Table 2 uses columns “n” vs “ne” without clear definition; table captions/text should define them explicitly.
- Weakness: unclear selection/tuning of k for kNN — specify whether k is per-dataset/method and tuned via validation consistently.
- Weakness: missing specification of constraints/regularization on w (non-negativity?) and A (Frobenius penalty, rank r), and sensitivity to r.
- Weakness: minor clarity issues — typos, equation references, and explanation of when relaxed dual variables are used.
- Quality: method is technically sound; Sinkhorn for differentiable gradients and NCA objective are appropriate, but ablation and sensitivity analyses are needed.
- Clarity: paper is mostly clear and organized but requires fixes to notation, tables, and more intuition for w and λ.
- Originality: moderate but meaningful — novel combination of learned ground metric and per-word weights for WMD with NCA and entropic OT.
- Significance: high practical significance due to robust empirical results and applicability for practitioners.
- Pros for acceptance: effective method, elegant OT+metric learning use, relevance to topics, and code release.
- Cons for acceptance: missing ablation/sensitivity analyses, some clarity/table issues, and narrow focus on kNN without discussion of transfer to other tasks.
- Requested from authors: provide A vs w ablation, clarify λ usage and sensitivity, define “n”/“ne” and fix average-error pointer, specify k selection, state constraints/regularization on w and A, and add a short intuitive example for w’s effect.
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept (high reviewer confidence) pending minor clarifications, ablation, and sensitivity analyses.
