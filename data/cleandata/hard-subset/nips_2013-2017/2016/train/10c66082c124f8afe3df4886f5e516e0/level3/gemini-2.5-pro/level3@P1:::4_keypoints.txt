- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) to learn a task-specific document distance building on WMD.
- Learns a linear embedding transformation A and a word-importance vector w to reshape embedding space and re-weight document BOWs.
- Trains with a stochastic leave-one-out k-NN objective in the NCA framework.
- Addresses non-differentiability and high cost of WMD gradients by using an entropy-regularized Sinkhorn relaxation for a differentiable, efficient approximation.
- Evaluates S-WMD on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing consistently strong average performance.
- Strength: paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and accessible with strong motivation and explanation.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation across diverse datasets and many competitive baselines.
- Strength: technically sound solution (Sinkhorn relaxation), plus practical engineering like smart initialization and batching.
- Weakness: insufficient conceptual justification for the word re-weighting w and how it affects document semantics.
- Weakness: limited novelty—linear transform and feature weighting with NCA are established; main novelty is applying them to WMD and making gradients practical.
- Weakness: ambiguous use of "semantic" similarity; the method learns task-specific discriminative distance tied to labels, not general semantics.
- Quality: high, with claims supported by extensive experiments and honest discussion of approximations.
- Clarity: excellent writing and organization.
- Originality: moderate—valuable adaptation and engineering but constituent ideas are not new.
- Significance: high practical utility; advances WMD for supervised tasks and likely useful for classification/retrieval.
- Recommendation: generally favorable toward acceptance, contingent on stronger justification/ablation for w and clearer terminology about "semantic" vs. task-specific similarity.
