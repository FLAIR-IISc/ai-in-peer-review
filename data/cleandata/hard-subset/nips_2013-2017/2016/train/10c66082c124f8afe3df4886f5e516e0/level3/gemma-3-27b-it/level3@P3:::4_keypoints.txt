- Proposes S-WMD: learn a linear transform of embeddings and reweighted word distributions on top of WMD, optimizing k-NN error; uses Sinkhorn for computational efficiency.
- Well-written with a thorough literature review situating the work in metric learning, embeddings, and optimal transport.
- Strong empirical evaluation: experiments on eight datasets and comparisons to 26 baselines show consistent performance gains.
- Motivation is sound: supervised learning can make WMD capture task-specific notions of similarity better than unsupervised metrics.
- Practical contribution: a fast Sinkhorn-based approximation addresses WMD’s computational bottleneck.
- Weak justification for reweighting the word distribution w; unclear why it improves label separation without altering document semantics.
- Contribution appears incremental: applying linear transformations and reweighting within an NCA objective is not a major conceptual advance.
- Practical concern about generalization: unclear how to compute or apply the learned reweight vector w to unseen documents and how that affects performance.
- Terminology issue: the phrase “semantic difference” is ambiguous and should be clarified (label/topic/authorship vs true semantic change).
- Recommendation: paper is acceptable if authors strengthen the reweighting justification, provide a generalization procedure for w, and clarify wording.
