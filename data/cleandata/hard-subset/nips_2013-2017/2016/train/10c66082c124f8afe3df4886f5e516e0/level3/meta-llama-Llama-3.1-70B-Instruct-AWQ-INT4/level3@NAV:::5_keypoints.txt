- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learns word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance as a fast proxy for Wasserstein distance.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving the best average performance.  
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical exposition and organization.  
- Novel contribution: introduces a supervised component for document distance learning.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation demonstrating improved document classification performance.  
- Technically sound with solid theoretical analysis.  
- Limited originality: builds on existing WMD/optimal transport ideas, so core concept isn’t entirely new.  
- References to Word Centroid Distance are poorly integrated and seem tangential.  
- Missing discussion on the choice and impact of the Sinkhorn entropic regularizer (λ), a crucial algorithmic detail.  
- Clarity: accessible background on WMD and Sinkhorn, easy to follow derivations and results.  
- Significance: relevant for NLP tasks like text retrieval and sentiment analysis due to improved distance metric.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel supervised metric, comprehensive evaluation, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, omission of λ discussion, and weak integration of Word Centroid Distance references.
