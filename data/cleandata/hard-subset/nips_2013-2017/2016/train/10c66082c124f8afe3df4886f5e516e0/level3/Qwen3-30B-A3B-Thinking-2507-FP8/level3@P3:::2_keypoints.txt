- Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), learning an affine transform of embeddings and word-importance weights to minimize leave-one-out NN error.  
- Builds on Kusner et al.'s unsupervised WMD and fills the gap by incorporating task-specific supervision.  
- Develops an efficient approximation to the WMD gradient to avoid computationally prohibitive nested optimization.  
- Leverages Sinkhorn distance approximation, batch gradient descent, and neighbor filtering to address computational challenges.  
- Conducts extensive empirical evaluation on 8 text classification datasets against 26 competitive baselines.  
- S-WMD outperforms all baselines on 5 of 8 datasets and achieves the best average performance overall.  
- Visualizations show S-WMD yields more clustered class representations than WMD; learned word weights are task-relevant.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition of theoretical derivations and implementation details.  
- Uses careful experimental design and hyperparameter tuning via Bayesian optimization.  
- Approach is original: learning a linear embedding transform plus word-importance weights applies metric learning novelly to text.  
- Significant contribution: supervised learning substantially improves WMD, achieving state-of-the-art and practical training times.  
- Implementation is publicly available, facilitating reproducibility and adoption.  
- Technically sound with detailed derivations and empirical support for claims.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations: on some datasets (e.g., CLASSIC) unsupervised WMD slightly outperformed S-WMD (likely initialization).  
- Minor weakness: evaluation uses only word2vec embeddings; effect of other embeddings not explored.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance for NIPS 2016.
