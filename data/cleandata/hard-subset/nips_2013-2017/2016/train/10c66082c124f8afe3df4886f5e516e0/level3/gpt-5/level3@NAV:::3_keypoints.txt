- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a Mahalanobis ground metric A and per-word importance weights w on top of WMD.  
- Trains A and w end-to-end using a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) objective.  
- Differentiates through Sinkhorn entropy-regularized OT with gradients via primal transport plan and relaxed dual variables.  
- Reports strong empirical gains over 26 baselines on eight datasets, with sensible runtime and open-source code.  
- Builds on prior work: WMD, entropy-regularized OT, and metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML).  
- Similar ideas exist for learning EMD ground metrics and histogram reweighting; missing citations to prior supervised reweighting and OT reweighting literature (e.g., Guy Lebanon, Le & Cuturi, Cuturi & Avis, Wang & Guibas).  
- Method is technically sound and uses Sinkhorn cleverly for stable, fast gradients.  
- Mathematical development lacks rigor: need assumptions for differentiability, precise subgradient statements, constraints on w, and normalization details.  
- Notation inconsistencies: Eq.1 uses Euclidean cost while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean—paper should consistently treat WMD as p-Wasserstein and fix p/cost.  
- Clarity could be improved with tightened notation and an algorithmic box summarizing the full training pipeline.  
- Originality is modest but nontrivial: combining Mahalanobis metric and histogram reweighting under NCA is simple, useful, and practical.  
- Significance is high: results are strong and likely adopted by OT-based document distance practitioners.  
- Pros: simple, effective idea; strong empirical gains; efficient optimization; practical relevance and released code.  
- Cons: inadequate discussion of regularized vs unregularized OT (impact of λ, bias–variance tradeoff); missing/unclear citations on reweighting and supervised EMD.  
- Cons (cont.): Limited analysis of learned w—needs ablations (A-only vs w-only), interpretability, and stability studies.  
- Cons (cont.): Potential bias from WCD neighbor filtering—need sensitivity analysis to M/N and discussion of missed hard negatives.  
- Overall recommendation: support acceptance, with requested improvements in derivations, consistent notation, clearer positioning relative to Cuturi/Avis, and discussion of limitations of related work (e.g., Wang et al.).
