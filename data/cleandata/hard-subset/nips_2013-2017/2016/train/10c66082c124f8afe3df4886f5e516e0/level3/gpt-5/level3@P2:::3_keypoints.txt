- Proposes S-WMD: learn Mahalanobis ground metric A and nonnegative word-importance vector w for supervised WMD using an NCA-style kNN loss with Sinkhorn-regularized OT for efficient gradients.
- Builds on WMD, Sinkhorn OT, and metric learning literature; related to supervised EMD/OT ground metric learning and histogram reweighting work.
- Technical formulation is sensible; Sinkhorn-based gradient approximations for A (via primal T*) and w (via dual α*,β*) are reasonable though approximate to unregularized WMD.
- Empirical evaluation is strong: eight datasets, 26 baselines, careful tuning, consistent improvements, plus visualization and timing results.
- Caveat: fixed λ=10; paper lacks analysis of how regularization strength affects accuracy and faithfulness to unregularized WMD; needs ablation on A vs. w.
- Notational issues: inconsistent use of p and other symbols; recommend unifying notation (e.g., p-Wasserstein with distinct letters for exponents/dimensions).
- Must clearly distinguish when training/ testing uses Sinkhorn vs exact WMD and discuss bias introduced by entropy regularization and λ trade-offs.
- Algorithm 1 should be fully self-contained with inputs/outputs, constraints on A and w, learning schedules, stopping criteria, and neighbor-filtering details.
- Provide derivation or citation connecting Sinkhorn scaling vectors (u,v) to approximate dual variables (α*,β*); include appendix derivations for rigor.
- Originality: idea of learning OT ground cost is known, but novelty lies in applying it to word-embedding-based document distances, joint A and w learning, and integrating Sinkhorn gradients with NCA.
- Significance: practical, adoptable supervised variant of WMD that improves kNN classification and offers interpretability via w and A.
- Reasons to accept: practical extension of WMD, strong empirical evidence, efficient training, released code, and interpretable components.
- Reasons to reject: insufficient analysis of regularization vs accuracy trade-off, some notational/mathematical sloppiness, missing ablations isolating A vs w, and incomplete related-work comparisons.
- Suggestion 1: study impact of λ on accuracy, optimization stability/speed, and gap to unregularized WMD (vary λ and report accuracy/runtime).
- Suggestion 2: ablation study variants: (i) w fixed, (ii) A fixed, (iii) both learned to quantify contributions.
- Suggestion 3: specify constraints/regularization on w and A and discuss overfitting controls (e.g., nonnegativity, normalization, low-rank, penalties).
- Suggestion 4: provide evidence that WCD neighbor pruning does not harm performance (recall/sensitivity to N).
- Suggestion 5: fix overloaded notation (use distinct symbols for norm exponent, vocab size, doc support size, embedding dim).
- Suggestion 6: add related work on histogram reweighting and OT feature-weight learning (e.g., Lebanon) and clarify differences.
- Suggestion 7: clarify whether test-time uses learned A,w with exact WMD or Sinkhorn and report accuracy/runtime differences.
- Suggestion 8: report sensitivity to initialization (S-WCD), convergence plots, final gains, and k selection protocol in kNN.
- Suggestion 9: deeper analysis of learned w (top up/down-weighted words per class and error effects) beyond word clouds.
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation for document support size, clarify D′(da,db) as a lower bound to WMD under A, provide Sinkhorn iteration/stop details, and fix incomplete references.
- Overall: effective and simple supervised WMD extension with strong results; acceptance recommended pending improved rigor, clearer notation, and additional analyses.
