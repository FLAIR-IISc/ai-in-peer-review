- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance as a fast proxy for Wasserstein distance.
- Evaluation: Tested on eight datasets against 26 baselines, reporting the best average performance.
- Exposition: Well-written with clear, in-depth mathematical background, loss, gradient computations, and optimization details.
- Strength: Leverages word embeddings to improve document classification and outperforms state-of-the-art on several datasets, including short documents.
- Strength: Use of Sinkhorn distance provides a significant computational speedup.
- Limitation: Novelty is limited relative to prior WMD work.
- Limitation: Poorly justified and discussed entropic regularizer (lambda); unclear whether behavior approximates pure Wasserstein or entropic/Sinkhorn regimes.
- Limitation: References to Word Centroid Distance are irrelevant/confusing and require clarification, especially regarding initialization.
- Limitation: Lacks a clear, detailed analysis of computational complexity and resource requirements.
- Acceptance pros: Novel supervised approach, strong empirical performance, and practical speedup via Sinkhorn.
- Acceptance cons: Limited novelty, missing complexity discussion, and insufficient treatment of lambda’s role.
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
- Overall: Technically sound and significant, but needs fixes on lambda discussion, complexity analysis, and WCD references.
