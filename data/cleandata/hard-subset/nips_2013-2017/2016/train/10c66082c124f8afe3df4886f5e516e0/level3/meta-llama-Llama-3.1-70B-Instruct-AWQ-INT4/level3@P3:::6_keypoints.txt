- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), extending WMD with a linear transform A and word importance weights w.  
- Introduces supervised metric learning for documents to improve document distance measurement.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and thorough background on document distances and WMD.  
- Provides a detailed description of the method, optimization process, and fast gradient computation.  
- Includes comprehensive evaluation, t-SNE visualizations, and a thorough related-work discussion.  
- Leverages large unsupervised corpus (word2vec) to combine high-quality embeddings with supervision.  
- Claims S-WMD is forgiving of syntactically different but conceptually similar words, but lacks deeper explanation why.  
- Insufficient detail on how word importance weights w are learned and their impact on performance.  
- Notation could be clarified (subscripts/superscripts) and would benefit from a notation table.  
- Originality: novel and unique extension to WMD via supervised metric learning.  
- Significance: addresses an important NLP problem with potential for wide adoption.
