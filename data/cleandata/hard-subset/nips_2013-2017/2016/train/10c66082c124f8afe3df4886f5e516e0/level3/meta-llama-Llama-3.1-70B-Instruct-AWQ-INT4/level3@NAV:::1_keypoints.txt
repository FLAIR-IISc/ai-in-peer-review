- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear projection plus document-specific word re-weighting.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks, outperforming 26 baselines and achieving best results on 5/8 datasets and lowest average kNN error.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Provides a clear explanation of the optimization method and initialization technique.  
- Main contribution: a supervised metric-learning approach that adapts WMD to labeled classification tasks.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation with detailed comparisons against a wide range of competitive baselines.  
- Limitation: EMD with metric learning initialized by a "word centroid distance" performs similarly to the full method (Table 3), questioning added impact.  
- Missing comparisons to established supervised metric-learning methods (e.g., ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- No isolated/ablation study separating the contributions of word-specific weights vs. the projection matrix A.  
- Originality is limited: primary novelty is an optimization approximation to WMD rather than a major new insight.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel supervised approach, leverages semantic WMD, strong empirical gains, and comprehensive evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited impact/novelty, missing standard metric-learning baselines, and lack of component-level analysis.
