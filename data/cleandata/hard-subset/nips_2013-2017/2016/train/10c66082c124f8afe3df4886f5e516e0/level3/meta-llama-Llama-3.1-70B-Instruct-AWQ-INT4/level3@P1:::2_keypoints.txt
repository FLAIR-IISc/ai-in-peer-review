- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform and a word importance vector.  
- Addresses WMD’s lack of task-specific information by applying discriminative training.  
- Method is sound, clearly explained, and builds naturally on existing WMD work.  
- Provides sufficient detail for reproduction.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive across several classification datasets and many baselines/features.  
- S-WMD outperforms all baselines by a large margin.  
- Claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experiments; evaluation is careful and honest.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.  
- Novelty lies in a new supervised combination of familiar techniques for WMD.  
- Significance: advances state of the art and is likely useful to practitioners and researchers.  
- Weakness: requires labeled data, limiting applicability to some text domains.  
- Weakness: potentially high computational complexity on large datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution suitable for presentation at NIPS; strengths outweigh weaknesses.
