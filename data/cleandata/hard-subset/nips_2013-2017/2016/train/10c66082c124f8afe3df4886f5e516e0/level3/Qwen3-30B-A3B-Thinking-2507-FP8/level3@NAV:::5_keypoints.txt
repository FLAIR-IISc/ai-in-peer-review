- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD): learns word-specific weights and an affine embedding transform, using Sinkhorn distance to approximate Wasserstein distance.  
- Training framed as minimizing stochastic leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error.  
- Evaluated on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, reporting best average performance and consistent improvements over unsupervised WMD and other document representations.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written with clear mathematical exposition and thorough implementation details.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and results are strong.  
- Novelty is limited: supervision in optimal transport distances is incremental over prior WMD work.  
- Major methodological gap: poor discussion of the entropic regularizer λ (eq.2) despite its dominant effect on behavior.  
- Unclear whether the method behaves closer to pure Wasserstein (large λ) or Sinkhorn (small λ); no guidance on λ selection or impact on speed/accuracy.  
- References to Word Centroid Distance (WCD) are confusing and irrelevant, misleading readers about its relation to the method.  
- Quality: good empirical results but theoretical foundation weakened by missing λ analysis.  
- Clarity: excellent overall, but degraded by WCD references and lack of λ discussion.  
- Originality: limited incremental contribution; not substantially differentiated from prior WMD research.  
- Significance: moderate—practical and effective improvements but not a transformative advance.  
- Recommendation: add thorough discussion/analysis of λ and remove or clarify irrelevant WCD references.
