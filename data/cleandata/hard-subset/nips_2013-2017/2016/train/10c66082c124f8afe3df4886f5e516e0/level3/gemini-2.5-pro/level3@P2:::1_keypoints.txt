- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform A and word-importance weights w for a task-specific document metric.
- Uses a differentiable proxy for leave-one-out kNN loss inspired by Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA).
- Replaces intractable WMD differentiation with entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn distance) to enable efficient, differentiable optimization.
- Evaluated on kNN classification across eight datasets against 26 baselines, yielding state-of-the-art or highly competitive performance.
- Technically sound: Sinkhorn relaxation is a clever, practical solution and the experimental evaluation is thorough.
- Missing ablation study to disentangle the independent contributions of the transform A versus the word-weight vector w.
- Improvements over the strong initialization S-WCD are sometimes marginal, raising questions about the practical value of the full S-WMD optimization.
- Missing critical baselines: standard metric learning methods (LMNN, ITML) applied to word-centroid document representations are not compared.
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-written; figures and t-SNE visualizations are helpful.
- Minor clarity issues: ambiguous citation phrasing, inconsistent notation for dual variables, and not explicitly naming the Sinkhorn algorithm when introducing u and v.
- Originality: novel application of learned ground metric to text, introduction of word-importance weights, and a new optimization combining NCA-style loss with Sinkhorn-based gradients.
- Significance: addresses a key WMD limitation, with potential wide applicability and an optimization technique of independent interest.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method; clever technical contribution; strong results; excellent clarity.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of ablation and critical baselines, and limited gains over simpler S-WCD on some datasets.
- Overall: high-quality, important work but needs additional evaluations (ablation and LMNN/ITML comparisons) to fully validate contributions.
