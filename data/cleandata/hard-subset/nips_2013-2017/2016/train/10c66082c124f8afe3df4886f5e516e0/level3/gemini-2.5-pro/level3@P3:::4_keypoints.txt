- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learn a linear transform A on embeddings and a word importance vector w to improve WMD for kNN classification.  
- Objective inspired by Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA), minimizing a differentiable surrogate of leave-one-out kNN error.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD and enable efficient gradient computation.  
- Builds on prior work (WMD, NCA/metric learning) and adapts these ideas to the WMD framework.  
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains background, motivation, and method.  
- Technical quality: sound approach; Sinkhorn approximation is a clever, necessary practical solution.  
- Experimental rigor: evaluated on eight diverse datasets against 26 competitive baselines, with consistent outperformance of S-WMD.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem—injecting supervision into unsupervised document distances—and offers a scalable, principled solution.  
- Weakness: motivation and justification for learning word reweighting w are weak; reweighting could distort document semantics and needs analysis.  
- Weakness: unclear how w is applied to unseen test documents and how the normalization behaves out-of-sample; practical details need clarification.  
- Weakness: originality is moderate—combines established ideas (linear transform, NCA) with WMD rather than introducing a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Weakness: wording is imprecise about "semantic meaning" of labels; should use terms like "task-specific similarity" or "class-based dissimilarity."  
- Overall evaluation: high quality, excellent clarity, moderate originality, high significance; recommend acceptance if authors satisfactorily address w-related issues and clarify terminology.
