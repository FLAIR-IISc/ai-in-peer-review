- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a task-specific document distance via a linear embedding transform A and word-importance weights w.
- Extends Word Mover’s Distance by introducing supervision and connecting document representation with supervised metric learning (inspired by NCA).
- Uses a differentiable, tractable approximation via the regularized Sinkhorn distance to solve the nested optimization.
- Technical approach is sound and the relaxation is necessary for computational feasibility.
- Thorough experiments show state-of-the-art k-NN classification on eight datasets against 26 baselines.
- Authors plan to release code, aiding reproducibility and future work.
- Missing ablation study to disentangle the individual contributions of A versus w.
- Needs clearer intuition for the word-importance weights w and their interaction with A.
- Lacks experimental details on k selection for k-NN and ambiguous use of the regularization parameter lambda.
- Unsupported claim that S-WMD is especially robust to syntactically different but conceptually similar words without parameter-level justification.
- Author rebuttal satisfactorily clarified the focus on k-NN-specific metric learning.
