- Introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a task-specific document distance metric.
- Direct, well-motivated extension of Kusner et al.’s WMD that incorporates supervision.
- Learns an affine transformation of the word embedding space plus word-importance weights.
- Formulates learning as a metric learning problem, adapting stochastic leave-one-out error minimization from NCA to WMD.
- Addresses the key computational challenge (differentiating through WMD) by using an entropy-regularized transport formulation.
- Method is technically sound, clearly explained, and described with sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough (8 diverse datasets, 26 strong baselines) and shows S-WMD largely outperforming competitors for kNN text classification.
- Original contribution: discriminative training of WMD with learned word weights is novel and valuable for NLP.
- Minor weakness: S-WMD is not top on every dataset; more analysis of those failure cases would be useful.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-evaluated paper; suitable for presentation at NIPS.
