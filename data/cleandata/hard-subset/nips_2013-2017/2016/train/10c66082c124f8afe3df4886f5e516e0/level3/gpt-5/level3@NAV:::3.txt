This paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), which learns a task-adapted ground metric and document reweighting on top of WMD. Concretely, the ground cost is parameterized by a Mahalanobis metric A applied to word embeddings, and normalized histograms are reweighted by a per-word importance vector w. The parameters are trained end-to-end by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective (NCA-style), while differentiating efficiently through an entropy-regularized OT approximation (Sinkhorn), with gradients derived via the primal (transport plan) and relaxed dual variables. Empirically, the paper reports compelling gains over 26 baselines across eight datasets, with sensible runtime and open-source code.

Relation to prior work: The approach builds on WMD (Kusner et al.), entropy-regularized OT (Cuturi), and metric learning (NCA/LMNN/ITML). Learning EMD ground metrics has precedents (Cuturi & Avis; Wang & Guibas; Wang et al. on ITML + EMD for images). The reweighting of multinomial histograms is also not new and should cite earlier supervised term-reweighting and multinomial models (e.g., Guy Lebanon) as well as related reweighting in OT/transport contexts (e.g., Le & Cuturi).

Quality: The method is technically sound overall, with a clever use of Sinkhorn for stable, fast gradients and a reasonable NCA objective. The empirical section is a strong point. However, the mathematical development would benefit from more rigor (assumptions for differentiability, precise statements when gradients are subgradients, constraints on w, and normalization).

Clarity: Generally clear, but notation needs tightening. Eq. 1 uses Euclidean cost while Eq. 6 optimizes squared Euclidean (i.e., 2-Wasserstein). Please describe WMD as p-Wasserstein throughout and use a consistent p and cost (squared vs unsquared). An algorithmic box summarizing the full training pipeline would greatly improve readability.

Originality: The combination—learning both a Mahalanobis ground metric and histogram reweighting for WMD under an NCA loss—is simple yet nontrivial and practically useful, though it closely builds on existing techniques.

Significance: Results are strong and likely to be adopted by practitioners who use OT-based document distances.

Pros:
- Simple, effective idea; strong empirical gains; efficient optimization.
- Clear practical relevance; code available.

Cons:
- Insufficient discussion of regularized vs unregularized OT (impact of λ, bias vs variance).
- Missing/unclear citations on reweighting and supervised EMD.
- Limited analysis of the learned w: please quantify its contribution (ablations A-only vs w-only), interpretability, and stability.
- Potential bias from WCD neighbor filtering: discuss sensitivity to M/N and any missed hard negatives.

Overall: I support acceptance. With stronger derivations, consistent notation, clearer positioning vs Cuturi/Avis (entropy regularization, continuous vs discrete costs, linear parameterization) and a discussion of limitations of Wang et al., this will be a solid NIPS contribution.
