- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear embedding transformation and word importance weights using a leave-one-out NN loss and Sinkhorn-based gradient approximation.
- Key technical contribution is using Sinkhorn distance to efficiently approximate gradients and avoid cubic complexity of exact WMD.
- Well-motivated connection to prior metric learning (NCA, LMNN, ITML) and optimal transport (EMD, Sinkhorn) literature.
- Strong empirical evaluation on 8 text classification datasets, outperforming 26 baselines and achieving state-of-the-art results on multiple tasks.
- Paper is very clearly written with precise mathematical formulation and good exposition.
- Practical impact: improves WMD baseline with reasonable training time and computational benefits via Sinkhorn regularization.
- Major weakness: initialization method (S-WCD) performs nearly as well as full S-WMD, suggesting limited additional benefit from learned weights and projection.
- Lacks ablation studies to isolate contributions of word importance weights versus the projection matrix.
- Missing baseline: did not evaluate word-centroid document embeddings with standard metric learning (ITML, LMNN, NCA), undermining comparison strength.
- Originality limited: core idea of learning a metric for WMD is incremental, mainly an application of existing metric learning techniques with a novel optimization twist.
- Arguments for acceptance: well-executed extension of WMD, strong empirical results, clear writing, and practical, efficient supervised training.
- Arguments against acceptance: marginal gains over initialization, missing ablations and baseline comparisons, and an incremental rather than transformative contribution.
- Overall assessment: solid and technically sound work that merits acceptance with minor revisions to add the missing baseline and ablation analyses to clarify component contributions.
