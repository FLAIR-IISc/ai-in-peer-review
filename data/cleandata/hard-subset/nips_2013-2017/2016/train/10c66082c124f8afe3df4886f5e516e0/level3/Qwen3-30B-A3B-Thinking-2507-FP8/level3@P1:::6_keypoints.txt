- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD), extending WMD with a linear transformation A and word importance weights w to incorporate label supervision for document classification.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance to efficiently approximate gradients of the leave-one-out classification error, enabling practical optimization.  
- Evaluated on 8 diverse datasets and compared against 26 baselines, showing strongest average performance and lowest error on 5/8 datasets.  
- Empirical results are compelling and training efficiency is competitive with other metric learning methods (see Table 3).  
- Technically sound integration of supervision into WMD while retaining WMD’s conceptual appeal.  
- Clear connection and positioning relative to metric learning literature (NCA, LMNN, ITML).  
- Novel idea of jointly learning word importance weights w alongside transformation A, motivated by task-specific word relevance.  
- Unclear intuition and explanation for Equation (5) and the specific role of w in the distance definition.  
- Unclear whether performance gains come primarily from A or from w; ablation or analysis is needed.  
- Unsupported claim about being “forgiving” for syntactically different but semantically similar words; needs justification or examples.  
- Technical ambiguities: relationship between Equation (2) and λ (line 170) and λ’s role in gradient computation are not clearly explained.  
- Reporting/notation issues: “average error across datasets” is referenced but not in Tables 2/3; columns “n” and “ne” in Table 2 are not defined.  
- Method details missing: choice of k for k-NN classification (dataset- or method-dependent) is not specified.  
- Rebuttal clarified “n” vs “ne” (normalized vs non-normalized histograms); this should be explicitly stated in the paper.  
- Recommendation: high-significance paper with code release planned; accept with minor revisions to address the above clarifications.
