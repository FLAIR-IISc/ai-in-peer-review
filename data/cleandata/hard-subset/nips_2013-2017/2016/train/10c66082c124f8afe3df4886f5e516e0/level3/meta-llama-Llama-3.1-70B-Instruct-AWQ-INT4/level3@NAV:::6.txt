This paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) metric, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD). The authors introduce a linear transformation of the word embedding space and a word importance weight vector, which are learned to minimize the kNN-based classification error on a per-document level. The method is evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method. The use of a linear transformation and word importance weights is a novel approach to supervised metric learning for documents. The evaluation on multiple datasets and comparison to various baselines demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

However, there are a few areas that require clarification. The intuition behind Equation (5) and the usage of the word importance weights w could be further explained. Additionally, it is unclear which component (A vs. w) drives the reported improvements. The authors should provide a clearer justification for the claim that the method is "particularly forgiving when two documents use syntactically different but conceptually similar words."

The evaluation is primarily focused on classification tasks, and it would be interesting to see how the method performs on other tasks, such as clustering or information retrieval. The authors should also provide more details on the choice of hyperparameters, such as the value of λ in Equation (2) and the learning rates ηA and ηw.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their method. The use of figures and tables to illustrate the results is helpful. However, there are a few minor errors in the text, such as the missing description of how k is chosen for k-NN classification.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective approach to supervised metric learning for documents. With some minor revisions to address the areas mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
