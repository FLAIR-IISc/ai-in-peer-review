- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, adding an affine transform and word-importance weight to WMD and minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out classification error.
- Background: Provides clear motivation and explanation of WMD limitations and why supervision is needed.
- Method detail: Presents loss, gradient, and optimization steps in detail but lacks a fully rigorous mathematical derivation.
- Experiments: Empirical results are strong—S-WMD outperforms 26 baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks.
- Visualizations: Includes visualizations of learned word embeddings and document distances that illustrate effectiveness.
- Strength — novelty: Proposes a novel supervised WMD approach, a significant contribution to NLP.
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- Weakness — mathematical rigor: Needs more rigorous derivation of the loss function and gradient computations.
- Weakness — weight vector insight: Provides insufficient analysis of the empirical importance and interpretation of the learned weight vector w.
- Weakness — related work: Lacks a clear distinction from related methods such as Cuturi/Avis and Wang et al.
- Originality: Offers a new perspective on incorporating supervision into WMD, departing from existing approaches.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for document classification and other text tasks (sentiment, topic modeling, IR).
- Quality: High-quality presentation with compelling experimental support.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel method, strong empirical performance, and clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: Need for stronger mathematical justification, clearer analysis of w, and sharper comparisons to prior work.
