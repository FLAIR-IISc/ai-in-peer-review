- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns an affine transform of embeddings and word importance weights.  
- Uses a Sinkhorn-distance approximation to make supervised WMD training computationally efficient.  
- Optimizes a stochastic leave-one-out classification loss, directly targeting classification performance.  
- Novel contribution: first discriminative/task-specific metric learning applied to WMD.  
- Technically sound: clear approximation avoiding cubic complexity and detailed gradient derivations.  
- Extensive experiments on eight diverse datasets (news, sentiment, product ID) against 26 competitive baselines.  
- Strong empirical results: best average performance and lowest error on 5/8 datasets, with large relative gains (e.g., 51% on BBCSPORT, 22% on OHSUMED).  
- Well-written and reproducible, with organized sections and thorough methodological explanations.  
- Properly situates work relative to prior metric learning (NCA, LMNN, ITML) and WMD literature.  
- Visualizations (t-SNE, learned word weights) effectively illustrate learned improvements.  
- Practical strengths: particularly effective on shorter documents and training time comparable to baseline metric-learning methods.  
- Minor limitations: evaluation limited to word2vec embeddings and scalability to extremely large corpora not fully explored.  
- Overall judgment: technically rigorous, original, significant contribution with no major flaws—strong case for acceptance.
