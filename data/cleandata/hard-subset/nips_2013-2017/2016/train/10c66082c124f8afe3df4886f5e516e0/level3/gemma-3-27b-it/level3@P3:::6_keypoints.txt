- Introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear embedding transform (A) and word importance weights (w) for supervised document distance.
- Proposes an efficient approximation to the gradient of WMD enabling practical supervised optimization despite WMD’s computational cost.
- Well-motivated connection to prior work in WMD and metric learning.
- Strong empirical results: consistently outperforms 26 baselines across eight datasets.
- Promises Matlab code release to aid reproducibility.
- Authors responded helpfully during rebuttal, indicating responsiveness.

- Lacks intuitive explanation for learned word importance weights (w) and their relation to embedding biases.
- Does not disentangle the individual contributions of A (linear transform) versus w (word weights); no focused ablation provided.
- The claim that S-WMD is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words is asserted without sufficient justification or examples.
- Minor editorial issue: missing reference to “average error across datasets” — check Table 3.
- Table 2 needs clarification of the “n” and “ne” columns for experimental transparency.
- No discussion of how k (in k-NN) was selected or sensitivity analysis, introducing a potential confound.

- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper with clear contributions and compelling results.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant performance gains, practical algorithmic contribution, strong motivation, and planned code release.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient intuition for w, lack of A vs w dissection, weak justification for key claims, and minor clarity issues.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the listed explanatory and clarifying points.
