- Paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform A and word importance weights w for document similarity.
- Uses Sinkhorn distance approximation to make optimization tractable.
- Empirically evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, consistently outperforming most baselines.
- Provides t-SNE visualizations and analysis of learned word weights to support findings.
- Authors promise to release Matlab code, improving reproducibility.
- Rebuttal responses were thorough and addressed several reviewer concerns.
- Missing ablation study: unclear which component (A or w) drives improvements.
- Intuition for Eq. (5) and the role of w in re-weighting histograms is too concise and needs a clearer example.
- Connection between Eq. (2) and the lambda referenced in the text is not explicitly stated and should be clarified.
- Claim that S-WMD is “particularly forgiving” of syntactic variation lacks concrete evidence or justification.
- The “average error across datasets” referenced in the text is not found in Table 3 (confirm or remove).
- Table 2 column labels “n” and “ne” are unclear and need explanation.
- Selection of k for k-NN was initially unclear but is now satisfactorily explained after the rebuttal.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
