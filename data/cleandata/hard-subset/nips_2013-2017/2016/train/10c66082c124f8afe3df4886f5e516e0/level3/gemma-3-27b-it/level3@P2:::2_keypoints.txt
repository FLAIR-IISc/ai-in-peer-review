- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) to learn a supervised metric based on WMD.  
- Learns an affine transformation of the word embedding space and word-importance weights.  
- Optimizes parameters by minimizing leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error.  
- Introduces an efficient gradient approximation using Sinkhorn/optimal transport for practical training.  
- Comprehensive experiments across eight datasets and 26 baselines showing consistently strong performance.  
- Builds on prior work in document metrics, metric learning, and Cuturi’s efficient optimal transport.  
- Technically sound derivation and justified approximation for scalable training.  
- Novel combination of embedding transformation and word-frequency re-weighting for discriminative WMD.  
- Exceptionally clear writing, consistent notation, and accessible explanations.  
- Significant practical impact: improved accuracy, efficient training times, and improved clustering visualization.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of sensitivity to hyperparameters (learning rate, regularization, batch size).  
- Weakness: insufficient investigation into why S-WMD underperforms on the CLASSIC dataset.  
- Suggestion: briefly discuss potential effects of alternative transport distances (e.g., p=1).  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, strong empirical results, clarity, and practicality; caveats are deeper hyperparameter and dataset-specific analyses.
