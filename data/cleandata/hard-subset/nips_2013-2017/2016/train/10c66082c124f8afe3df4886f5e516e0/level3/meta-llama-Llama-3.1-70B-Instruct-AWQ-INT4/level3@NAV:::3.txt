**Paper Review: Supervised Word Mover’s Distance**

This paper proposes a novel approach to learning a supervised document distance metric, called Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD). The authors build upon the existing Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) metric, which has shown promising results in document classification tasks. The key contribution of this work is the introduction of a linear transformation of the word embedding space and a word importance vector, which are learned to improve the document distance metric.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a method for learning a supervised document distance metric by parameterizing the cost matrix with a Mahalanobis distance and learning histogram reweighting coefficients. The authors provide gradients for NCA-type objectives and optimize the parameterized EMD (Wasserstein) efficiently. The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, outperforming 26 competitive baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks.

**Strengths**

1. **Simple and Straightforward Idea**: The paper combines existing techniques in a novel way, making it easy to understand and implement.
2. **Compelling Experimental Evidence**: The authors provide extensive experimental results, demonstrating the effectiveness of their approach on various datasets.
3. **Efficient Optimization**: The proposed method uses a fast gradient approximation and batch gradient descent, making it computationally efficient.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of Rigorous Mathematical Derivations**: The paper could benefit from more detailed mathematical derivations and notation consistency.
2. **Insufficient Discussion of Interplay between Regularized and Unregularized OT Formulations**: The authors could provide more insights into the relationship between these two formulations.
3. **Notational Inconsistency**: The use of Euclidean distance in Eq. 1 and squared Euclidean distance in Eq. 6 may cause confusion.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

1. **Provide an Algorithmic Box for Clarity**: A clear and concise algorithmic box would help readers understand the proposed method.
2. **Discuss Limitations of Wang et al.**: The authors could provide more context on the limitations of Wang et al.'s work and how their approach addresses these limitations.
3. **Provide Insights on the Empirical Importance of the Learned Weight Vector w**: The authors could provide more analysis on the importance of the learned weight vector w in the proposed method.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper proposes a novel approach to learning a supervised document distance metric, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various datasets, making it a valuable contribution to the research community.

**Conclusion**

In conclusion, this paper proposes a novel approach to learning a supervised document distance metric, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing. While there are some areas for improvement, the paper provides compelling experimental evidence and efficient optimization techniques, making it a valuable contribution to the research community. I support the acceptance of this paper.
