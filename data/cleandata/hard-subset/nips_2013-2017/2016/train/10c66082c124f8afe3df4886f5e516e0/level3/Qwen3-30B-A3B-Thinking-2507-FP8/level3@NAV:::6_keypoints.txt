- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): supervised metric learning extension of WMD using linear transform A and word importance weights w.  
- Key technical contribution: efficient gradient approximation via Sinkhorn distance that preserves links to original WMD.  
- Empirical result: evaluated on 8 document classification tasks, showing consistent improvements over 26 baselines (including WMD, WCD, and supervised representations).  
- Builds on Kusner et al. (WMD) and metric learning (e.g., NCA); relevant for supervised document classification.  
- Strengths: clear motivation for supervised metric learning, elegant approximation for tractable training, and comprehensive experimental evaluation.  
- Authors demonstrated good understanding through careful clarifications in rebuttal.  
- Clarification needed: intuition behind Eq. (5) is unclear, especially the role and effect of w.  
- Ablation unclear: uncertain whether performance gains primarily come from A, from w, or their combination.  
- Unsupported claim: statement that S-WMD is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words lacks justification or evidence.  
- Reporting/notation issues: reference to “average error across datasets” (ln257) cannot be found—Table 3 seems to show average rank, not error.  
- Table 2 needs explanation of "n" vs "ne" columns; method for selecting k in k-NN is not specified.  
- Confusion about regularization: unclear if lambda in Eq. (2) is the same lambda referenced at ln170.  
- Overall verdict: strong, significant, and original contribution but requires addressing the above clarifications before acceptance.
