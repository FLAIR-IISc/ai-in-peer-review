- Paper proposes S-WMD: supervised metric learning on WMD by learning a linear projection A and word-importance vector w, using stochastic neighborhood relaxation and entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) for efficient gradient computation.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks; S-WMD achieves lowest kNN test error on 5/8 datasets and outperforms 26 baselines on average; t-SNE visualizations show improved class structure.  
- Strength: interesting and useful extension of WMD with a thorough empirical evaluation and clear writing.  
- Weakness: limited impact because EMD with metric learning initialized by a “word centroid distance” performs similarly to full method.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison of word-centroid document embeddings with existing metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).  
- Weakness: no isolated ablation separating contributions of word-specific weights versus projection matrix A.  
- Weakness: originality mainly in the optimization approach, which is a somewhat limited contribution.  
- Weakness: minor clarifications needed (ambiguous citation around line 152; explicitly note use of Sinkhorn scaling near line 170).  
- Overall ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and efficient optimization for supervised metric learning, strong empirical results, well-written presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty/impact relative to simpler baselines and missing key comparisons and ablations.
