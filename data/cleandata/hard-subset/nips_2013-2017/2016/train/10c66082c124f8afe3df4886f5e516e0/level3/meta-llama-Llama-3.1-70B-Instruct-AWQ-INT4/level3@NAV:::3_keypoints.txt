- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learns a Mahalanobis linear transform of embeddings and a word-importance vector to improve document distance.
- Parameterizes the EMD/Wasserstein cost matrix and histogram reweighting coefficients, with gradients provided for NCA-type objectives.
- Optimizes the parameterized WMD efficiently using a fast gradient approximation and batch gradient descent.
- Empirical results: outperforms 26 competitive baselines across 8 real-world text classification tasks.
- Strength: simple, straightforward combination of existing techniques that is easy to understand and implement.
- Strength: compelling and extensive experimental evidence supporting the approach.
- Strength: computationally efficient optimization strategy.
- Weakness: lacks rigorous mathematical derivations and consistent notation throughout the paper.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of the relationship and interplay between regularized and unregularized optimal transport formulations.
- Weakness: notational inconsistency (e.g., Euclidean vs. squared Euclidean distance between Eq. 1 and Eq. 6) may confuse readers.
- Suggestion: include an algorithmic box to clearly present the method and optimization procedure.
- Suggestion: discuss limitations of Wang et al.’s related work and clarify how this approach addresses them.
- Suggestion: provide empirical analysis on the importance and effect of the learned word-weight vector w.
- Originality/significance: presents a novel, meaningful contribution to supervised document distance learning in NLP.
- Conclusion: paper is a valuable contribution with room for clarity improvements; reviewer recommends acceptance.
