- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance for speed.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines and reports best average performance.  
- Clear and in-depth mathematical exposition throughout the paper.  
- Builds on WMD and Sinkhorn distance; relates to metric learning methods like NCA and ITML.  
- Strength: well-written with strong mathematical clarity.  
- Strength: achieves state-of-the-art average performance across multiple datasets.  
- Strength: introduces efficient gradient computation and batch gradient descent for faster training.  
- Weakness: limited novelty since method extends existing WMD/Sinkhorn ideas.  
- Weakness: role and choice of lambda (entropic regularizer) are poorly discussed despite strong impact on results.  
- Weakness: references to Word Centroid Distance are irrelevant and confusing.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough analysis of method behavior across different lambda settings.  
- Originality: novel supervised WMD extension but incremental given prior work.  
- Significance: significant empirical gains and potential for wide adoption in NLP.  
- Quality: high writing and exposition, but needs clearer lambda discussion and corrected references.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify lambda’s role and remove/fix confusing Word Centroid Distance references.
