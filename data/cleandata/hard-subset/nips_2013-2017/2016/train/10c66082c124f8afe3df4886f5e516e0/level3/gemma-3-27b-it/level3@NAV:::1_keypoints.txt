- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learn linear projection of word embeddings and document-specific word re-weighting.  
- Uses an efficient Sinkhorn approximation for gradient-based optimization, making the method practical for larger datasets.  
- Shows strong kNN classification performance across multiple text datasets, outperforming many baselines (26 compared).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and provides a solid theoretical explanation.  
- Extensive experimental evaluation is commendable and supports empirical claims.  
- Table 3 suggests EMD with metric learning initialized via word-centroid distance (S-WCD) performs similarly, questioning the added complexity of learning both A and word weights.  
- Missing comparison to standard metric learning methods (e.g., ITML, LMNN, NCA) applied directly to word-centroid document embeddings.  
- Lacks an ablation study to isolate the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.  
- Originality is strongest in the optimization approach; methodological novelty in metric learning within EMD is limited.  
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions: add missing baselines and ablation, and clarify references and equation notation.
