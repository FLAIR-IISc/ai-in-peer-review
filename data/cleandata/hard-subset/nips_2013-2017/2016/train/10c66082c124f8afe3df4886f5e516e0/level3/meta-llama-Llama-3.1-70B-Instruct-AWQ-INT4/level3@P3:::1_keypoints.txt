- Proposes S-WMD: supervised metric learning that learns a linear projection of word embeddings and word-importance weights to adapt WMD to labels.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough related-work review and technical exposition.
- Method leverages WMD’s semantic strengths while addressing its unsupervised limitation by incorporating supervision.
- Experimental results are comprehensive and show S-WMD outperforms unsupervised WMD, WCD, and multiple document representation baselines across datasets.
- Concern: impact may be limited because EMD with metric learning initialized by "word centroid distance" performs similarly to full S-WMD (Table 3).
- Concern: missing comparisons of word-centroid document embeddings using existing metric-learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA).
- Concern: insufficient analysis separating the contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.
- Originality is moderate: main novelty is the optimization approach (lines 156–175), but builds on existing metric learning and optimal transport work.
- Quality: 8/10 — technically sound with supported claims, but needs further analysis/evaluation.
- Clarity: 9/10 — clear exposition though some notation may be unfamiliar to non-experts.
- Originality score: 7/10 — useful extension but somewhat incremental.
- Significance: 8/10 — addresses an important NLP problem and is potentially widely applicable, though impact may be limited.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/effective supervised WMD, strong experimental validation, addresses important NLP problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited incremental impact, missing comparisons to ITML/LMNN/NCA on centroid embeddings, and need for deeper analysis of weight vs projection contributions.
