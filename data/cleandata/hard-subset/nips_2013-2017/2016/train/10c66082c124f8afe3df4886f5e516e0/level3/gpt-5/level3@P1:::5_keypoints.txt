- Proposal: S-WMD — a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform A of embeddings and a nonnegative word-importance vector w, trained with a leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss.  
- Optimization: Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn OT for efficient, differentiable transport plans and dual potentials to obtain gradients for A and w.  
- Efficiency tricks: Initializes A via NCA on Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) and prefilters neighbors with WCD to speed training.  
- Technical soundness: Gradient derivations via Sinkhorn are standard and plausible; uniqueness assumptions acknowledged but the impact of using regularized plans vs evaluation is unclear.  
- Empirical strength: Broad evaluation on 8 datasets and 26 baselines; best average rank, wins 5/8 datasets, Bayesian hyperparameter tuning, published code, and t-SNE visualizations.  
- Efficiency claim: Sinkhorn, batching, and WCD-based neighbor filtering are well-justified and empirically effective.  
- Clarity: Generally well written with clear notation; frequent WCD references can be distracting and should be consolidated.  
- Lambda concern: Entropic regularization parameter (lambda) is fixed at 10 without rationale or sensitivity analysis; needs investigation and guidance.  
- Train vs test ambiguity: Unclear whether gradients use the same lambda as test-time distances or whether test-time uses unregularized WMD — this must be clarified.  
- Behavior vs true WMD: Unclear whether learned metric approaches true WMD or remains in the entropic regime; practitioners need accuracy-speed tradeoff guidance.  
- Missing ablations/robustness: Needs A-only vs w-only comparisons, different embedding backbones, ground-cost norms, regularization of A, and sensitivity to neighbor/batch sizes.  
- w constraints and priors: Ensure nonnegativity, discuss regularizers or IDF-informed initialization for stability and performance.  
- Evaluation gaps: Lacks statistical significance tests and clarity on whether test-time distances are exact WMD or Sinkhorn approximations.  
- Originality: Moderate — combines known tools (WMD, Sinkhorn, NCA) but contributes a useful joint learning of ground metric and term weights for text.  
- Significance: High practical impact due to improved, interpretable, and efficient supervised document distance useful for kNN and other tasks.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong empirical results, practicality, interpretability, reproducible code, and clear exposition.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited conceptual novelty, insufficient treatment of lambda and its implications, missing ablations/sensitivity analyses, and some confusion around WCD’s role.  
- Overall: Well-executed and valuable work; addressing lambda sensitivity, test-time behavior, and ablations would substantially strengthen the paper.
