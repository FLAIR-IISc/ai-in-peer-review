- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transform of word embeddings plus a word-importance weight vector to make WMD supervised.  
- Builds on Kusner et al.’s WMD by introducing learned supervision to the ground distance.  
- Formulates an optimization with computed gradients; method is clearly explained and justified.  
- Extensive experiments on eight real-world text classification tasks.  
- Empirical results: S-WMD consistently outperforms unsupervised WMD and many baselines (TF-IDF, LSI, other document distances).  
- Strength: bridges unsupervised word embeddings and supervised document classification, capturing label-encoded similarity.  
- Strength: word-importance weights let the model emphasize discriminative words.  
- Related work: surveys metric learning methods (NCA, LMNN, ITML) and contrasts with Wang et al.’s EMD metric learning for images.  
- Weakness: requires substantial labeled data to learn the supervised metric effectively.  
- Weakness: optimization can be computationally expensive, especially on large datasets.  
- Weakness: may be unsuitable for tasks where similarity is not clearly encoded by labels.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS.
