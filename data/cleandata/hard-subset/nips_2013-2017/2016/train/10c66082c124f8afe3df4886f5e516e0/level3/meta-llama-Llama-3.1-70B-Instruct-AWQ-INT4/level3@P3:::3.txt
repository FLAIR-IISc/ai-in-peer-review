The paper "Supervised Word Mover's Distance" proposes a novel approach to learning a supervised metric for document classification. The authors build upon the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), a document metric that leverages word embeddings to compute distances between documents. The key idea is to learn a linear transformation of the word representations and a word-importance weight vector to make the WMD distances match the semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels.

The authors provide a thorough background on word embeddings, the WMD, and linear metric learning. They also introduce a regularized transport problem to alleviate the cubic time complexity of the Wasserstein distance computation. The proposed method, Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), learns a generalized Euclidean metric within the word embedding space and a word-importance vector. The authors derive the gradients of the loss function with respect to the linear transformation and the word-importance vector and propose an efficient optimization algorithm.

The experimental results are compelling, with S-WMD outperforming 26 competitive baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks. The authors also provide visualizations of the learned distances using t-SNE, which demonstrate the effectiveness of S-WMD in capturing semantic similarities between documents.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The related work section is thorough, and the authors clearly distinguish their work from previous contributions.
