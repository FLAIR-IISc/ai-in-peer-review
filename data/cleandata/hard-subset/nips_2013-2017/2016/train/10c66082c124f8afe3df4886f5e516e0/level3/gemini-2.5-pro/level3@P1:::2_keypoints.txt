- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of WMD for task-specific document similarity.  
- Jointly learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-importance weights to adapt document geometry and feature relevance.  
- Adapts the Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) objective to learn a metric that minimizes stochastic leave-one-out k-NN error.  
- Replaces the exact optimal transport with an entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain a differentiable, convex approximation enabling efficient gradients.  
- Provides gradient derivations for both the transformation matrix and word weights and uses WCD-based initialization and batch training for scalability.  
- Original and significant: fills an important gap by making WMD discriminative and task-specific.  
- Technically strong: elegant use of Sinkhorn relaxation and well-motivated optimization strategy.  
- Clear and well-written exposition with sufficient background and reproducibility details.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on eight diverse datasets against 26 baselines, showing state-of-the-art on five datasets and best average rank.  
- Includes qualitative visualizations supporting empirical results.  
- Weakness: performance can be sensitive to initialization and hyperparameter choices (e.g., CLASSIC dataset failure).  
- Weakness: higher computational cost per gradient step than simpler metric-learning on fixed document vectors, raising scalability concerns for very large vocabularies or datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—novel, impactful, well-executed work suitable for NIPS.
