- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns an affine transform of embeddings and word-importance weights.  
- Training objective minimizes leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error.  
- Introduces an efficient Sinkhorn-distance-based approximation to make supervised WMD computationally feasible.  
- Strong empirical results: evaluated on eight datasets and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Situates work well in prior literature (bag-of-words, TF-IDF, LSI, LDA, WMD, metric learning) with appropriate citations.  
- Technical presentation is clear and mathematically rigorous, accessible to readers familiar with optimal transport and metric learning.  
- Practical contributions include optimization details (initialization, batch gradient descent) and useful ablation studies (e.g., “S-WMD init.”).  
- Novelty is incremental: adding supervision to a distance metric is not a fundamentally new idea compared to original WMD.  
- Discussion of Word Centroid Distance (WCD) is tangential and sometimes confusing, with unclear relevance.  
- Major weakness: insufficient discussion and guidance on choosing the entropic regularizer λ and its significant impact on Sinkhorn behavior.  
- Unclear whether reported results prioritize accuracy (large λ) or speed (small λ), harming reproducibility and interpretability.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify the role/choice of λ and streamline or better motivate the WCD discussion.
