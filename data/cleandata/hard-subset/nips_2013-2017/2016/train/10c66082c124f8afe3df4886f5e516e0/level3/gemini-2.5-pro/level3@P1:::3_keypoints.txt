- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, a supervised document distance that learns a Mahalanobis transform A and a word-importance vector w for WMD using an NCA-like kNN leave-one-out loss and entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) for tractable gradients.

- Strength: Clear motivation and simple, well-motivated combination of WMD, NCA, and Sinkhorn regularization to incorporate supervision.

- Strength: Key technical contribution is deriving an efficient, differentiable approximation to WMD gradients via the Sinkhorn distance and its dual variables.

- Strength: Strong, thorough empirical evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing consistent superior performance.

- Weakness: Needs clearer discussion of relation between entropy-regularized Sinkhorn gradients used for learning and the unregularized WMD used at test time, and effects of the regularization parameter λ.

- Weakness: Mathematical exposition and algorithmic description need tightening; provide fuller derivations (e.g., gradient wrt w) and a detailed algorithm box for reproducibility.

- Weakness: Notational inconsistency around the distance norm/p-Wasserstein vs. squared Euclidean (p=2) should be resolved and clarified.

- Weakness: The idea of histogram re-weighting inside an EMD framework is not entirely novel; related prior work (e.g., Lebanon 2006, Le & Cuturi) should be cited and discussed.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of learned parameters—need quantitative ablation (e.g., learn A only vs. A+w) to assess contribution of w beyond a word cloud.

- Weakness: Related-work distinctions should be sharper (e.g., difference from learning unconstrained ground distances and from Wang et al. 2012), emphasizing novelty in histogram re-weighting.

- Overall assessment: Strong, practical, and effective contribution that addresses an important limitation of WMD; recommend acceptance subject to clarifications and stronger exposition.
