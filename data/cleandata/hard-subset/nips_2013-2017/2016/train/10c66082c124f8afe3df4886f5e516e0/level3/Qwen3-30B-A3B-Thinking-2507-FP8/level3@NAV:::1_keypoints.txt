- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear projection and document-specific word re-weighting for supervised text classification.
- Formulates supervised metric learning that minimizes stochastic leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error.
- Uses Sinkhorn scaling to efficiently approximate the WMD gradient for optimization.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across eight text classification tasks, outperforming 26 baselines.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides solid theoretical foundation and experimental validation.
- Technical approach is sound and experimental evaluation is broadly comprehensive.
- Major concern: limited novelty—initialization method (S-WCD) performs nearly as well as full S-WMD per Table 3.
- Missing critical baselines: no comparisons to standard metric learning methods (ITML, LMNN, NCA) on word-centroid document embeddings.
- Lacks ablation study isolating contributions of word-specific weights versus the projection matrix A.
- Optimization details (lines 156–175) are interesting, but the core contribution (linear metric learning within EMD) is somewhat narrow.
- Evaluation lacks depth in key areas, reducing confidence in the relative value of the approach.
- Clarity is excellent; significance is moderate—useful extension to WMD but not a breakthrough.
- Originality is limited—builds on established metric learning techniques with incremental modifications.
- Overall: solid technical contribution with constrained impact; should address missing comparisons and ablations before publication.
