- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform of embeddings and a word-importance weight vector by minimizing leave-one-out kNN error.  
- Computes gradients efficiently using Sinkhorn (entropy-regularized OT) and a probabilistic filtering technique.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: outperforms 26 baselines across eight real-world text classification datasets.  
- Operates in low-dimensional word-embedding space, avoiding scalability issues of conventional metric learning in high dimensions.  
- Positions work within prior literature (bag-of-words, TF-IDF, LSI/LDA, WMD) and metric learning; acknowledges related OT and reweighting methods.  
- Needs a deeper, more nuanced discussion of related work (e.g., entropy regularization, reweighting schemes) and clearer comparison to Wang et al.  
- Mathematical derivations are generally clear but would benefit from greater rigor and consistent notation.  
- Notational inconsistency: mixing Euclidean and squared Euclidean distances creates ambiguity; recommend defining WMD as a p‑Wasserstein distance upfront.  
- Suggest adding an algorithmic box summarizing S-WMD steps to improve clarity and reproducibility.  
- Recommend deeper analysis of learned word-importance weights (which words get high weight and dataset-specific variations).  
- Practical aspects like initialization and batch gradient descent are addressed, improving usability.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, novel combination of supervised metric learning with WMD, strong empirical evidence — merits acceptance with minor clarifications.
