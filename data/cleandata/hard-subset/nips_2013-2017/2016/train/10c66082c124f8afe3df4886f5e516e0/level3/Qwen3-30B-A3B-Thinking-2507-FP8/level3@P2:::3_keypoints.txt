- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform of embeddings and word importance weights for document classification.  
- Frames learning as minimizing stochastic leave-one-out classification error under the WMD framework, combining WMD with metric learning (e.g., NCA).  
- Uses entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) to approximate WMD gradients, making training practical.  
- Introduces Supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) as an initialization strategy to improve convergence.  
- Empirical evaluation: consistent improvements over 26 baselines across 8 diverse document classification datasets.  
- Strength: comprehensive, well-designed experiments demonstrating clear superiority of S-WMD.  
- Strength: clever Sinkhorn-based approximation that reduces computational cost of gradient computation.  
- Strength: paper is well-written and clarifies links between WMD, metric learning, and the proposed method.  
- Strength: S-WCD initialization is insightful and empirically helpful.  
- Weakness: notational inconsistency between Euclidean and squared Euclidean distances; needs clear p-Wasserstein specification.  
- Weakness: derivation of gradients (Eqs.8–10) lacks sufficient mathematical rigor and detail.  
- Weakness: reweighting approach (Eq.5) is not clearly distinguished from prior work (e.g., Lebanon, Cuturi); novelty there is questionable.  
- Weakness: lacks a formal algorithm box to clarify the optimization procedure and improve reproducibility.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of the learned word importance weights w and their semantic/classification relevance.  
- Suggestion: clarify theoretical relationship between regularized and unregularized optimal transport and justify the entropy-regularization approximation for the supervised objective.  
- Suggestion: include a formal algorithm box outlining S-WMD optimization steps.  
- Suggestion: expand related work to better contrast with Cuturi & Avis (2014), Wang et al. (2015), and other ground-distance parameterization methods.  
- Suggestion: add analysis of learned weight vector w with examples of high-weight words and task relevance.  
- Overall: strong, meaningful contribution with practical impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address math rigor, notation, and related-work clarity.  
- Meets NIPS criteria: quality (technically sound, strong experiments), clarity (well-written), originality (novel combo of WMD and metric learning, though reweighting has prior work), and significance (consistent empirical gains).
