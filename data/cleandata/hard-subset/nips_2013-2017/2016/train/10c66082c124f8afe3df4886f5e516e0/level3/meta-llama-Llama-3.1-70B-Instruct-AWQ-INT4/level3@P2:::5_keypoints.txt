- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns word-specific weights and uses Sinkhorn distance for speed.
- Evaluated on 8 real-world text classification tasks against 26 baselines; S-WMD outperforms almost all and achieves lowest kNN error on 5/8 datasets.
- Paper is well-written with clear, in-depth mathematical exposition and thorough background/related work coverage.
- Method is well-motivated and mathematically sound; theoretical analysis and experiments support the claims.
- Novelty is limited: learning word-specific weights is useful but not a major departure from prior WMD work.
- References to Word Centroid Distance are irrelevant/confusing and require clarification of their relationship to S-WMD.
- The role and choice of the entropic regularizer lambda are poorly discussed despite lambda having a dominant impact on behavior and results.
- It is unclear whether the method behaves like pure Wasserstein (large lambda) or entropic/Sinkhorn (small lambda); most users choose small lambda for speed, changing behavior.
- Authors should include more analysis and empirical insight into how different lambda values affect performance and method behavior.
- Overall significance is positive: a useful contribution with convincing empirical gains for text classification.
