- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear projection of embeddings and word-importance weights.  
- Replaces exact EMD with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn approximation to enable efficient, differentiable optimization.  
- Learns parameters by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-like) classification loss.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on eight datasets, outperforming 26 baselines and achieving SOTA on 5/8 datasets for kNN.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-written, and well-organized.  
- Main weakness: full S-WMD yields only marginal gains over its simple initialization S-WCD (NCA on word-centroid), questioning practical significance.  
- Missing baseline comparisons: ITML and LMNN applied to the word-centroid distance (WCD) are not included but are crucial given S-WCD’s strong performance.  
- Lacks an ablation study to isolate contributions of the projection matrix A versus the word-importance vector w.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental since similar linear metric learning within an EMD framework exists in vision (e.g., Wang et al. [35]); novelty mainly in adapting/optimizing for text.  
- Minor clarity fixes: specify that “The authors” refers to Kusner et al. [19] in Section 2.  
- Notational clarity: use α*, β* to denote optimal dual variables consistently in Equations 10–11.  
- Clarify that u and v used to obtain dual variables are computed by the Sinkhorn scaling algorithm.  
- Overall: technically sound and empirically strong, but needs additional experiments (ablation and missing baselines) to convincingly attribute performance gains to the full S-WMD model.
