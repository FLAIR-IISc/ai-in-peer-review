- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform of embeddings and reweights word distributions, trained via entropy-regularized OT for scalability.
- Well-written and organized with clear methodology, mathematical derivations, and pseudo-code aiding reproducibility.
- Strong empirical validation: outperforms 26 baselines across eight datasets, showing consistent performance gains.
- Efficient training enabled by entropy-regularized optimal transport and batch gradient descent, reducing computational overhead.
- Interpretability enhanced by t-SNE visualizations and word importance analyses.
- Insufficient justification for reweighting word distributions; potential to distort original document semantics without theoretical/empirical support.
- Contribution feels incremental, largely building on WMD, linear metric learning, and entropy-regularized transport rather than introducing fundamentally new ideas.
- Lacks discussion of how to determine reweighting for unseen documents, a critical issue for deployment.
- Terminology issue: “semantic difference” is vague and conflates labels with semantic meaning, needing clearer definition.
- Arguments for acceptance: well-executed supervised extension with significant performance gains, efficiency, and broad applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, unjustified key design choices (reweighting), and missing practical considerations (unseen documents).
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending revisions to justify reweighting, explain unseen-document handling, and clarify terminology.
