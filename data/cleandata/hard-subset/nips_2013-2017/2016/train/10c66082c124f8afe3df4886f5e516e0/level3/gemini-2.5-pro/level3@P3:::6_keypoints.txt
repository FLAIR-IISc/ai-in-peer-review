- Proposes S-WMD: supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform A and word-importance weights w for task-specific document distances.  
- Frames learning as minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out k-NN classification error using an NCA-style loss.  
- Uses Sinkhorn (entropy-regularized) distance as a differentiable, efficient approximation to WMD to enable gradient-based learning.  
- Empirically evaluated on eight datasets and consistently outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Originality/significance: fills the gap between unsupervised WMD and supervised metric learning by transforming embedding geometry and re-weighting words.  
- Technical soundness: elegant use of Sinkhorn approximation and a practical batch-based training scheme.  
- Thorough empirical validation across many baselines demonstrates strong performance.  
- Reproducibility: authors pledge to release Matlab code.  
- Need more intuition/explanation for the definition and role of word-importance weights w and why re-weighting normalized BoW is effective.  
- Missing ablation: unclear which component (A vs w) drives improvements; evaluate A-only and w-only variants.  
- Clarify that the λ in the dual variable computation is the same entropy-regularization parameter from Eq. (2).  
- Explain how supervised learning of A and w specifically enhances robustness to syntactically different but conceptually similar words beyond inherited WMD properties.  
- Text inconsistency: “average error across datasets” is referenced but not found—Table 2 shows “Avg. Rank”; clarify or correct.  
- Table 2: columns “n” and “ne” are undefined—add definitions to the caption.  
- Experimental detail missing: specify how k in k-NN was chosen (fixed vs. tuned per method/dataset) for fair reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality, well-motivated, and empirically strong paper; recommendation for acceptance.
