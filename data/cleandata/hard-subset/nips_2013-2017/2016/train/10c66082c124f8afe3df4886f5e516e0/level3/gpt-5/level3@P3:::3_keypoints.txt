- Summary: Proposes S-WMD to learn a Mahalanobis ground metric A and word-importance weights w for WMD, optimizing an NCA-style kNN objective using entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) gradients with practical training recipes and strong empirical gains.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD and entropic OT; related to ground-metric learning (Cuturi & Avis) and image EMD metric learning (Wang et al.), but differs by learning histogram reweighting and using a kNN/NCA objective.

- Strength — joint learning: Learning both ground cost A and word weights w is intuitive and effective for text tasks.

- Strength — method: Coherent NCA-style objective, efficient Sinkhorn-based gradients, S-WCD initialization, and neighbor subsampling for scalability.

- Strength — experiments: Broad evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines, consistent improvements, visualizations, and released code.

- Strength — practical relevance: Uses pre-trained embeddings and improves retrieval/classification without major pipeline changes.

- Weakness — regularization mismatch: Training uses entropy-regularized OT but the role of λ at test time and bias vs convergence trade-offs are inadequately analyzed.

- Weakness — sensitivity analysis missing: Needs ablations/sensitivity over λ, number of Sinkhorn iterations, and neighbor set size; consider reporting both regularized and unregularized OT at test time.

- Weakness — mathematical rigor: Some gradient derivations (especially for w) are sketched;需 tighten assumptions (uniqueness/subgradient) and state when relaxed Sinkhorn duals are used.

- Weakness — notation inconsistency: Clarify cost exponent (Euclidean vs squared Euclidean) and whether optimizing 1-Wasserstein with quadratic ground cost or true 2-Wasserstein.

- Weakness — prior work on reweighting: Eq.5 has precedents in supervised term reweighting and OT parameterizations (e.g., Lebanon; Le & Cuturi) and these should be cited and contrasted.

- Weakness — related-work clarity: More clearly differentiate from Cuturi & Avis and Wang et al. in formulation, regularization, objectives, and scalability.

- Weakness — empirical analysis of w: Need more than word clouds — ablations (fixing w or A), stability across folds, sparsity/concentration, and impact tests (permute/zero top words).

- Quality: Experiments are sound and comprehensive; main caveat is reliance on entropic approximations without deep analysis of their effect on the unregularized objective.

- Clarity: Readable and organized; would benefit from an algorithm box, tightened derivations, and fixed notation for reproducibility.

- Originality: Moderate conceptual novelty — combines existing components (NCA, Sinkhorn, Mahalanobis, reweighting) but the integrated, efficient pipeline for WMD on text is practically novel.

- Significance: Strong empirical gains and high practical impact for OT/WMD in text applications.

- Questions for authors: Sensitivity to λ, Sinkhorn iterations, neighbor size; whether evaluation uses regularized or unregularized OT; ablations for learning only A/only w; computational overhead of w; effective rank of A and benefit of low-rank constraints.

- Pros for acceptance: Strong empirical performance, practical and efficient method, broad evaluation, code release.

- Cons for acceptance: Incomplete theoretical discussion on regularization mismatch, sketchy derivations/notation, and incomplete citation/positioning of reweighting prior work.

- Overall assessment: Well-executed, practically valuable combination of known techniques with substantial empirical improvements; recommend acceptance pending clearer treatment of regularization, tighter derivations, and expanded related-work discussion.
