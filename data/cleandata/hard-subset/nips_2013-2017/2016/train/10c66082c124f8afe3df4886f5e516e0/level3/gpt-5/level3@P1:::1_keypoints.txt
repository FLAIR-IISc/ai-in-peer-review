- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised WMD variant that learns a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance vector w for reweighting histograms.  
- Uses an NCA-style leave-one-out kNN surrogate loss to pull same-label documents closer under the learned transport distance DA,w.  
- Differentiation through WMD LP is intractable, so the authors use entropy-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) to obtain unique transport T*λ and approximate duals, enabling closed-form gradients.  
- Initializes A by applying NCA to Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) and restricts gradient computation to a candidate neighbor set from WCD for efficiency.  
- Reports state-of-the-art kNN accuracy on 8 datasets and best average rank against 26 baselines, winning on 5/8 datasets.  
- Builds on WMD and entropy-regularized OT and relates to supervised metric learning; novelty lies in combining supervised ground metric learning, histogram reweighting, and a Sinkhorn-based training loop.  
- Technical derivations are sound, with clear gradient derivation, complexity analysis, and practical accelerations that match training-time claims.  
- Incremental gains over S-WCD are modest on several datasets; paper needs systematic analysis of when S-WMD outperforms S-WCD and why.  
- Missing ablation separating contributions of A vs. w, so unclear whether transform or reweighting drives improvements.  
- No sensitivity analysis for λ (Sinkhorn regularization), neighbor set size, or squared vs. unsquared Euclidean ground costs.  
- Using WCD to select candidate neighbors may bias training; empirical justification (e.g., neighbor recall under WCD) is requested.  
- Minor clarity issues: ambiguous antecedent of “the authors” around line ~152 and Eq. (11) should denote α, β as optimal (α*, β*λ).  
- Recommend explicitly stating that dual variables come from Sinkhorn scaling near gradient derivations and specifying constraints/regularization on w (e.g., non-negativity).  
- Originality: meaningful but incremental—novel adaptation of Sinkhorn-based gradients + NCA to WMD with joint A and w learning for text is practical and useful.  
- Significance: likely useful for kNN text classification and retrieval, but impact limited if S-WCD performs nearly as well; stronger evidence (short-text, cross-domain, low-resource) would help.  
- Missing baselines/analyses: ITML/LMNN on WCD, A-only (w=1) and w-only (A=I) ablations, supervised embedding fine-tuning + WMD comparisons, and hyperparameter sensitivity plots.  
- Additional requests: report constraints/regularization on w, inspect learned w vs. IDF, discuss failure cases (e.g., CLASSIC) and whether L1 ground distance helps.  
- Clarify whether neighbor sets for gradient computation are refreshed as A and w change and include convergence plots (loss vs. iterations).  
- Pros: well-motivated extension, elegant Sinkhorn use for differentiable training, strong empirical performance, public code, clear exposition.  
- Cons: modest improvements over strong S-WCD baseline, missing ablations and supervised baselines, limited sensitivity analysis, unclear contribution of w vs. A, scope focused on kNN.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed paper with practical value; lean toward acceptance provided authors add requested ablations, WCD+ITML/LMNN comparisons, and address minor clarity issues.
