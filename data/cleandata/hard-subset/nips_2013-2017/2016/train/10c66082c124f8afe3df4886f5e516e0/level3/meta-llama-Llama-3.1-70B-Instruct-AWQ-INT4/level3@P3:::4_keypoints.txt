- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear embedding transform and a word-importance weight vector via stochastic leave-one-out NN classification.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with a thorough literature review and clear organization.  
- Extensive experiments on several datasets show S-WMD outperforms unsupervised WMD and other baselines.  
- Key contribution: supervised learning approach to improve WMD using a linear transform plus importance weights.  
- Technical soundness: theoretical analysis and experiments support claims; enough detail for expert reproduction.  
- Originality: novel combination of familiar techniques; moderate novelty.  
- Significance: addresses a difficult problem and is likely useful to researchers/practitioners.  
- Motivation for reweighting the word-importance vector is insufficiently explained and its effect on document semantics is unclear.  
- Limited novelty concern: applying a linear transform and NCA-style reweighting may not be a substantial contribution.  
- Practical concern: unclear how to compute reweighting for completely new or unseen documents.  
- Terminology issue: abstract uses ambiguous terms (e.g., “semantic difference” appears to mean label/topic/authorship, not true semantics).  
- Recommendation: clearly explain motivation and semantic impact of reweighting.  
- Recommendation: describe how to handle reweighting for new/unseen documents in deployment.  
- Recommendation: clarify abstract terminology and provide more experimental/dataset details.  
- Overall: Reviewer would not object to acceptance if the above concerns are addressed.
