- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learn linear transform A and word importance weights w to minimize leave-one-out kNN classification error.  
- Key technical innovation: use Sinkhorn distance to efficiently approximate the costly WMD optimization, enabling practical training.  
- Provides an initialization strategy (S-WCD) that speeds up training and stabilizes optimization.  
- Thorough experiments on 8 diverse text classification datasets against 26 competitive baselines.  
- Empirical results: S-WMD achieves lowest error on 5/8 datasets and best average performance across datasets.  
- Claims the method is "forgiving" to syntactically different but conceptually similar words, but lacks concrete examples or analysis.  
- Unclear role and interpretation of word importance weights w (Eq. 5): whether they merely reweight frequencies or perform a deeper transformation.  
- Unclear which component (A vs. w) contributes most to performance gains; needs ablation/clarification.  
- Confusion around the lambda parameter in Eq. 2 and its relation to the lambda referenced elsewhere; these should be explicitly connected.  
- Paper mentions "average error across datasets" but does not clearly present it (Table 3 shows times, not errors); average performance should be shown explicitly.  
- Reproducibility concern: paper does not explain how k was chosen for kNN classification (dataset- or method-dependent?).  
- Writing and structure are generally clear; authors plan to release implementation code.  
- Originality: extends WMD to supervised metric learning in embedding space, sufficiently distinct from prior work.  
- Overall assessment/recommendation: technically sound, significant empirical gains, minor clarity issues; recommendation is to accept.
