- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a task-specific document distance metric built on WMD.
- Learns a linear embedding transform A and word-importance weights w to incorporate supervision into WMD.
- Uses an NCA-inspired objective to minimize stochastic leave-one-out k-NN classification error.
- Overcomes gradient intractability of WMD via an efficient regularized Sinkhorn-distance approximation for end-to-end training.
- High technical quality: method is sound and the Sinkhorn-based gradient technique is novel and effective.
- Strong empirical validation: evaluated on 8 real-world datasets against 26 competitive baselines, claiming state-of-the-art performance.
- Careful experimental setup: hyperparameters tuned with Bayesian optimization and includes a fast WCD-based initialization.
- Clarity: generally well-written with clear background and motivation; authors plan to release code for reproducibility.
- Clarity issue: intuition for the word-importance vector w and its interaction with A could be elaborated.
- Missing ablation: unclear which component (A or w) primarily drives performance gains; an A-only / w-only study is needed.
- Ambiguity about the regularization parameter lambda: unclear if lambda in Eq.(2) equals the lambda=10 referenced later.
- Experimental detail missing: procedure for selecting k in k-NN evaluation (per-dataset or per-method tuning) is not described.
- Minor wording correction: text should align "on average across all datasets" with Table 2's "average rank" wording.
- Originality: first work to integrate supervision directly into WMD with an NCA-style loss and tractable gradient method.
- Significance: establishes new state-of-the-art for k-NN document classification and is likely impactful for text tasks using labeled data.
- Authors’ rebuttal justification: k-NN is a natural evaluation for a new distance metric and is reasonable.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and significant method, strong empirical results, solid Sinkhorn-based technical contribution, clear writing and planned code release.
- Arguments for rejection: lacks ablation disentangling A and w effects and has minor clarity/experimental detail issues.
- Final assessment: strong paper with minor weaknesses; recommended for acceptance.
