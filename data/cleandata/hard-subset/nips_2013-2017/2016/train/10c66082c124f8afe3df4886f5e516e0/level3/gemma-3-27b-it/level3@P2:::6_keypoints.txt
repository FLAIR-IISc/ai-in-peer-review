- Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform (A) and word importance weights (w) to minimize k-NN classification error.  
- Uses a Sinkhorn relaxed optimal transport formulation and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD computationally practical.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms 26 baselines across eight real-world text classification datasets.  
- Technical soundness: clear mathematical formulation, gradient derivation, NCA initialization, and batch gradient descent.  
- Practicality: approximation yields reasonable training times and released code increases reproducibility.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized.  
- Weakness: insufficient intuition and analysis of what the learned word weights (w) capture and how they influence the metric.  
- Weakness: lacks ablation/sensitivity analysis quantifying the separate contributions of A and w.  
- Weakness: ambiguous use of lambda (λ) in Eq. (2) and later references—needs clarification if they are the same.  
- Weakness: claim that S-WMD is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words is under-justified beyond generic embedding synonymy.  
- Weakness: Table 2 contains undefined column labels (“n” and “ne”) that require definition.  
- Originality: novel combination of metric learning and optimal transport applied directly to word embeddings rather than new document representations.  
- Significance: method is a practical, scalable advance for task-specific document distances with strong empirical gains.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong contribution with minor clarifications requested.
