- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that learns an affine transform of word embeddings (A) and word-importance reweighting (w) via a stochastic LOO kNN/NCA-style objective.  
- OT optimization: Uses entropically-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) to obtain differentiable approximations and unique transport plans for gradient computation.  
- Gradients: Derives efficient gradients w.r.t. A and w by recovering dual variables from Sinkhorn scaling.  
- Initialization & training: Initializes A with NCA on Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) and trains with minibatching plus neighbor filtering using WCD to preselect candidates.  
- Efficiency: Practical via Sinkhorn, neighbor filtering, and minibatching; complexity analyzed in terms of per-document vocabulary and batch/neighbor sizes.  
- Empirical evaluation: Extensive experiments on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, showing consistent and often large improvements; code released for reproducibility.  
- Technical soundness: Formulation and derivations are coherent and appropriate; use of entropic OT and dual variables is sensible and elegant.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written, well-structured, with sufficient algorithmic detail and informative figures/tables.  
- Originality: Novel combination of supervised ground metric learning and histogram reweighting inside WMD for text, distinct from prior EMD/metric-learning work.  
- Significance: Advances kNN-based document classification/retrieval and provides a general recipe for task-adaptive OT over embeddings.  
- Strength: Conceptually clean integration of supervision into WMD via A and w.  
- Strength: Sound, efficient training approach using Sinkhorn and pragmatic approximations.  
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation with strong gains over robust baselines.  
- Weakness: Lacks ablation isolating contributions of A vs. w, impact of initialization, λ, and neighbor set size M.  
- Weakness: Assumes squared Euclidean ground metric; alternative ground distances (L2, cosine) not evaluated.  
- Weakness: Neighbor filtering introduces biased approximation to NCA objective; effect of M on performance not quantified.  
- Weakness: Dual variable recovery and choice of λ (e.g., λ=10) need sensitivity/stability analysis.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns remain for very long documents due to O(p^2) Sinkhorn complexity; need discussion of truncation/stop-word strategies.  
- Weakness: Some baselines use PCA to 200 dims before metric learning—possible fairness issue or need justification.  
- Questions for authors: How do learned weights and performance vary by task (topic vs. sentiment) and per-class/word analyses beyond top-100?  
- Questions: Would class-specific weights w_c or low-rank class-conditional A improve results?  
- Questions: How robust is S-WMD to domain shift between pretrained word2vec and downstream data?  
- Questions: Did you try fine-tuning embeddings jointly or using alternative embeddings (e.g., GloVe)?  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong, well-motivated methodological contribution, practical engineering, extensive empirical gains, clear presentation, and available code.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited sensitivity/ablation analyses, single ground-metric assumption, and remaining computational burden for very long documents.  
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance (NIPS) with a suggestion to address ablations and sensitivity analyses to strengthen the paper.
