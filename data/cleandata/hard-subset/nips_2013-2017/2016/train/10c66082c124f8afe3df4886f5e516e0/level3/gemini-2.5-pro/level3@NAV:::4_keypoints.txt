- Proposal: S-WMD extends Word Mover’s Distance by learning a linear transform (A) of embeddings and a word-importance vector (w) optimized with a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) objective.  
- Empirical strength: Exceptionally clear writing, thorough literature review, and strong experimental results across 8 datasets vs 26 baselines for kNN document classification.  
- Novelty concern: Contribution is incremental—applying a linear transform and NCA-style objective to WMD is a well-established paradigm.  
- Weak motivation for w: Justification for learning the word-reweighting vector w is weak; reweighting may alter semantic content and the paper lacks argumentation that it improves label separation without corrupting meaning.  
- Practical gap: No discussion on how to compute or handle w for new documents containing unseen words.  
- Terminology ambiguity: The phrase “semantic difference” in the abstract is ambiguous—method yields task-specific similarity (e.g., topic/author) rather than general semantic similarity.  
- Recommendation: Solid empirical work and acceptable for publication, but authors should address motivation, semantic implications, and practical application of the word reweighting vector w in the final version.
