- Extends WMD by learning a linear transform A of embeddings and a global word-importance vector w, optimized with an NCA-style leave-one-out kNN objective using Sinkhorn relaxation.  
- Novelty: jointly learns a low-dimensional ground metric in embedding space plus discriminative histogram reweighting, trained end-to-end.  
- Evaluated on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines, showing strong empirical performance.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn for tractable gradients and efficient training; smart neighbor filtering and S-WCD initialization.  
- Strength: clear, well-motivated model with interpretable components (A, w) and released code; training times provided.  
- Strength: sound optimization strategy with scalable Sinkhorn-based gradients and extensive empirical comparisons.  
- Requested clarification: expand intuition for w (acts like a task-specific discriminative IDF that downweights ubiquitous words).  
- Requested ablation: quantify contributions of A-only, w-only, and both to isolate improvements.  
- Requested clarification: specify when Eq.(2)/Sinkhorn is used, whether λ in the paper is the Sinkhorn parameter, if λ is fixed or tuned, and whether Sinkhorn is used at test time.  
- Requested quantitative evidence for claim that method is forgiving to synonyms (e.g., synonym/paraphrase experiment or transport-mass analysis).  
- Fix wording “average error” to “average rank” (or point to the correct table/column).  
- Define Table 2 column labels “n” vs “ne” clearly in caption or text.  
- Clarify k selection protocol for kNN (per-dataset vs per-method tuning and whether k is fixed across methods).  
- Request sensitivity analyses for λ, neighbor-filter size N, batch size B, squared vs unsquared Euclidean ground cost, and A’s output dimension r.  
- Request empirical check of approximation accuracy: correlation between Sinkhorn DA,w and exact WMD under learned A,w.  
- Suggest adding a retrieval (nearest-neighbor search) task to further showcase the learned distance beyond kNN classification.  
- Quality: technically sound method; relaxed primal/dual gradient use is standard and complexity/optimization analysis is reasonable.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but needs the noted clarifications (w intuition, λ/Eq.2 use, table labels, k-selection, test-time distance).  
- Originality: novel and distinct from prior supervised EMD work by operating in embedding space and adding learned histogram reweighting.  
- Significance: practically useful for distance-based document retrieval/classification, with interpretable outputs and state-of-the-art gains.  
- Cons: missing key ablations and sensitivity studies, several clarity issues, and lack of quantitative support for the “forgiving” claim.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed contribution likely acceptable pending the requested clarifications and ablation studies.
