- Proposal: Introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that parameterizes WMD’s cost matrix with a Mahalanobis distance and learns word importance weights.  
- Optimization: Uses Sinkhorn (entropy-regularized OT) to enable gradient-based learning with a k-NN classification loss.  
- Contribution framing: Builds on WMD, addressing its unsupervised limitation via supervised metric learning for text.  
- Practicality: Clever use of Sinkhorn approximation for efficient gradient computation.  
- Empirical strength: Strong empirical results on eight datasets, outperforming 26 baselines.  
- Missing analysis: Needs deeper discussion of the interplay between regularized and unregularized OT and how the regularization parameter (λ) affects performance and accuracy trade-offs.  
- Mathematical clarity: Derivations could be more rigorous and would benefit from an algorithm box summarizing S-WMD.  
- Notational confusion: Eq.1 uses Euclidean distance while Eq.6 uses squared Euclidean—should describe WMD as p-Wasserstein to avoid confusion.  
- Prior work omission: Overlooks prior reweighting work (e.g., Guy Lebanon, Le/Cuturi) that should be cited.  
- Learned weights analysis: Offers limited insight into the learned word-weight vector; needs analysis of which words are upweighted and why.  
- Comparison gaps: Should more explicitly contrast S-WMD with Cuturi/Avis (entropy regularization, continuous vs. discrete cost, linear parameterization) and clarify limitations of Wang et al.’s approach.  
- Review verdict: Supports acceptance—empirical gains and sound methodology outweigh listed theoretical and analysis shortcomings.
