- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear transform of word embeddings and a word-importance vector for task-specific document distances.  
- Frames learning as minimization of a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) classification error.  
- Addresses intractable WMD gradients by using entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance as an efficient, differentiable approximation.  
- Derives gradients for both the linear transformation A and the word-weight vector w and integrates them into the optimization.  
- Paper is technically sound, with a coherent combination of NCA loss and Sinkhorn-approximated WMD and clear derivations.  
- Well-written and self-contained: provides necessary background (word2vec, WMD, NCA), algorithmic details, and reproducible implementation choices.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: eight diverse datasets, 26 baselines (including classic, topic-model, neural, and metric-learning baselines).  
- S-WMD achieves best performance on 5/8 datasets and best average rank, often with large margins; includes t-SNE and word-weight visualizations for interpretation.  
- Weakness: S-WMD is outperformed by unsupervised WMD on CLASSIC; needs further analysis (initialization or alternative p-norms) to explain failure.  
- Weakness: Strong initialization (S-WCD) already performs well; the marginal benefit of full S-WMD optimization should be analyzed and discussed.  
- Weakness: Sensitivity to chosen hyperparameters (batch size, neighbor set size, regularization) is not explored; practitioners would benefit from sensitivity/training-time analysis.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: Novel, significant, and practical contribution with strong empirical support; strong accept.
