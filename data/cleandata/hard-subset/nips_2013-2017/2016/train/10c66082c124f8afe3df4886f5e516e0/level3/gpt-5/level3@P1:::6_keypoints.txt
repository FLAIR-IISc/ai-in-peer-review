- Proposal: Introduces Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform A on embeddings and a nonnegative word-importance vector w, trained with an NCA-style leave-one-out objective.  
- Differentiable training: Uses entropic OT (Sinkhorn) to obtain smooth transport plans and dual variables, enabling efficient approximate gradients (O(q^2) per pair).  
- Initialization & training: Initializes with a supervised WCD projection (S-WCD), uses mini-batches and neighbor filtering based on WCD for efficiency.  
- Empirical results: Evaluated on 8 text-classification benchmarks vs 26 baselines; best average rank and lowest error on 5 of 8 datasets; code promised.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD/EMD, entropic OT, and metric learning; novel in jointly learning ground metric A and histogram reweighting w for text with Sinkhorn-based gradients.  
- Technical soundness: Entropic OT use and S-WCD initialization are appropriate; training complexity and accelerations are well discussed and feasible in wall-clock time.  
- Extensive evaluation: Careful hyperparameter tuning (Bayesian optimization) and comparisons across many representations and supervised baselines.  
- Clarification needed — role of w: Explain renormalized reweighting d̃=(w◦d)/(w^T d), constraints/regularization on w, and ablation disentangling A vs w contributions.  
- Clarification needed — λ and Eq.(2): Confirm same λ controls primal entropic regularization and dual approximations for α*,β*; state how λ is chosen (fixed vs tuned) and provide sensitivity analysis.  
- Clarification needed — gradient notation: Correct and clarify formulas for α*,β* from u,v, define the all-ones vector and centering terms, and fix dimensionality/notation issues.  
- Empirical claim support: Substantiate the claim of semantic robustness (forgiving syntactic variation) with synonym/paraphrase experiments or qualitative nearest-neighbor examples showing A and w effects.  
- Reporting clarity: Add clear aggregate summaries (mean error and rank with standard errors), define “n” vs “ne” in Table 2, and clarify whether “average error” or “average rank” was intended.  
- k-NN details: State how k is chosen (per-dataset validation or fixed), whether k is tuned per method, and whether k was included in Bayesian optimization.  
- Training details requested: Specify early stopping/convergence criteria, whether neighbor set size N and batch size B were tuned, and sensitivity of results to N and neighbor filtering bias.  
- Preprocessing & embeddings: Report tokenization/stopword/stemming policies, word2vec source and dimension, OOV handling, and any preprocessing differences across datasets.  
- Notation/clarity issues: Tighten notation (e.g., p used for multiple meanings), explicitly state constraints/regularization on A and w, and distinguish exact WMD from entropic approximation in gradients.  
- Originality: The joint supervised learning of A and w inside WMD with Sinkhorn gradients is novel for NLP/OT and the histogram reweighting is a simple, effective addition.  
- Significance: Practical, efficient supervised document similarity with strong empirical gains is impactful for classification, retrieval, and clustering; code release increases utility.  
- Pros: Principled supervised extension of WMD, strong broad empirical results, sound use of entropic OT, sensible S-WCD initialization, and good training-time profile.  
- Cons: Lacks ablations separating A vs w and initialization vs training, needs clearer λ/notation and dual formulas, limited sensitivity analyses (λ, k, N, ground cost), and the semantic-robustness claim is under-supported.  
- Key requests for authors: Provide ablations (A-only, w-only, both), enforce/describe w constraints, confirm λ usage and sensitivity, define table columns and k selection, report N sensitivity, try alternative ground costs/affine A, and clarify preprocessing/embeddings.
