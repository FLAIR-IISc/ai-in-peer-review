- Proposes supervised document distance by learning a linear transform A and reweighted word distribution w on top of Word Mover’s Distance (WMD).  
- Uses Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) objective to learn the linear transformation A.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with a thorough literature review.  
- Extensive experimental results on several novel datasets demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Novelty is limited: applying a linear transform and reweighting with NCA is not seen as a major contribution.  
- Motivation for reweighting the word distribution w is weak and not well-explained.  
- Unclear how reweighting w improves label separation without altering document semantics.  
- Practical concern: unclear how to compute reweighted w for completely new or unseen documents.  
- Terminology issue: “semantic difference” is ambiguous and appears to mean label/topic/authorship rather than true semantics.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance conditional on addressing the listed concerns.  
- Requested fixes: justify reweighting w and its semantic implications, clarify terminology, and provide guidance for handling unseen documents.  
- Requested fixes: better contextualize the contribution and explain why this approach improves on existing methods.
