- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised document distance metric built on Word Mover’s Distance.
- S-WMD learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word importance vector to capture label-informed similarity.
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, with a thorough literature review providing clear context and motivation.
- Extensive experimental results on several datasets demonstrate improved document classification accuracy with S-WMD.
- Strength: clear and concise presentation of the method and underlying principles.
- Strength: comprehensive literature review framing the contribution.
- Strength: strong empirical validation across novel datasets.
- Weakness: motivation for reweighting the word importance vector w is not well explained and its semantic effect is unclear.
- Weakness: claimed contribution is limited—using a linear transform and reweighting with an NCA objective is not seen as highly novel.
- Weakness: practical procedure for computing the reweight vector w for new/unseen documents is not addressed.
- Weakness: ambiguous use of the term “semantic difference” in the abstract (likely refers to label/topic/authorship rather than true semantics).
- Suggestions: provide clearer justification and analysis of w reweighting, explore more significant novelties, and give guidance for computing w on unseen documents.
- Overall judgment: would not object to acceptance pending minor revisions addressing the above concerns.
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
