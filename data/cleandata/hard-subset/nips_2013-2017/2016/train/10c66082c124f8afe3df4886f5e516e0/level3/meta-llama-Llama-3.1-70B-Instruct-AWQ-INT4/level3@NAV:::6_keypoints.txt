- Proposes S-WMD: learns a linear transformation of embedding space and a word importance weight vector to supervise WMD via per-document kNN classification loss.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Method is technically sound, clearly presented, and uses figures/tables effectively.  
- The linear transformation and importance weights represent a novel supervised metric-learning approach for documents.  
- Clarify the intuition behind Equation (5) and the role/interpretation of the word importance weights w.  
- Unclear which component (transformation A vs weights w) mainly drives the performance gains—needs ablation or analysis.  
- The claim that the method is “forgiving” for syntactically different but conceptually similar words requires clearer justification or evidence.  
- Evaluation is focused on classification; recommend testing on other tasks (clustering, information retrieval) to show broader utility.  
- Provide more details on hyperparameter choices, especially λ in Eq.(2) and learning rates ηA and ηw.  
- Minor issues in the text (e.g., missing description of how k is chosen for k-NN) should be fixed.  
- Overall positive assessment: novel, effective method with minor revisions recommended.  
- Reviewer score 8/10 with confidence 8/10.
