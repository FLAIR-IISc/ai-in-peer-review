- Proposes a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance (learns word importance and linear transform) using the Sinkhorn approximation for tractable optimization.
- Goal: improve document classification by incorporating label supervision into WMD and minimizing leave-one-out classification error.
- Builds on Kusner et al.’s WMD and applies standard metric-learning ideas to a new distance metric.
- Strength: exceptionally clear writing and thorough mathematical exposition, including gradient derivations and Sinkhorn details.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, showing consistent and best-average performance.
- Strength: practical implementation details and efficient batch training with Word Centroid Distance initialization and timing analysis.
- Weakness: limited novelty — essentially standard metric learning applied to WMD without clearly differentiating from prior approaches.
- Weakness: poor and unjustified discussion of entropic regularizer λ; authors pick λ=10 without analyzing its impact.
- Weakness: confusing use and justification of Word Centroid Distance for initialization and comparison, muddling relation to prior work.
- Weakness: incomplete evaluation of method behavior — unclear whether it behaves like true Wasserstein (large λ) or Sinkhorn (small λ).
- Quality: technically sound with robust experiments, but theoretical foundation weakened by omission around λ’s role.
- Originality: limited; contribution is an expected supervised extension rather than a major conceptual advance.
- Overall: well-executed and practical with strong results, but limited novelty and failure to address λ sufficiently justify revisions before acceptance.
