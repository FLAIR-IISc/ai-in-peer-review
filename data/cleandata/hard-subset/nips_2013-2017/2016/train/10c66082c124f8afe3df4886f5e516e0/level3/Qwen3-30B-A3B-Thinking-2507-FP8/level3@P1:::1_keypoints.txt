- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear embedding transform and word importance weights via leave-one-out nearest-neighbor error minimization.
- Introduces a Sinkhorn distance approximation to efficiently handle the nested optimization and make supervised WMD training practical.
- Empirically evaluated on 8 text classification datasets, showing consistent improvements over 26 baselines and strong gains on small collections (e.g., TWITTER, CLASSIC), outperforming baselines on 5/8 datasets.
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical exposition and precise technical details; situates work appropriately with related metric-learning methods (NCA).
- Main concern: the initialization method (S-WCD) achieves performance very close to final S-WMD, suggesting supervised refinement yields only marginal gains.
- Missing critical baseline comparisons: standard metric-learning methods (NCA, LMNN, ITML) applied to word-centroid embeddings are not evaluated.
- Lacks an ablation study isolating the effects of the linear projection matrix A versus the learned word importance weights.
- Contribution is somewhat incremental: novelty is mainly practical/implementation (making supervised WMD feasible) rather than a new theoretical framework.
- Needs clearer differentiation from prior EMD metric-learning work (Wang et al., ECCV 2015), which also learns metrics within an EMD framework.
- Limited broader impact due to narrow focus on kNN classification and unclear applicability to other distance-based tasks (e.g., IR, clustering).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add the missing baseline comparisons and an ablation study to clarify component contributions.
- Pros summarized: technically sound, efficient Sinkhorn-based optimization, strong empirical results, reproducible implementation details.
