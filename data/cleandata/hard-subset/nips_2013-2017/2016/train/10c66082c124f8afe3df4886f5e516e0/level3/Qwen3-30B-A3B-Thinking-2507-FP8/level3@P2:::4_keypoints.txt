- Introduces Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learns a linear transform A and word importance vector w to improve classification via a supervised metric-learning objective.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn approximation and an efficient WMD gradient to avoid nested optimization and enable practical training.  
- Clear writing and thorough literature review; effectively connects to Neighborhood Components Analysis and metric learning.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation on eight datasets vs. 26 baselines, showing consistent improvements over unsupervised baselines and many supervised competitors.  
- Practical implementation details (S-WCD initialization, batch gradient descent, neighbor filtering) are well-explained and useful.  
- Strength: technically addresses computational challenges, making supervised WMD feasible.  
- Strength: compelling empirical results and broad experimental validation.  
- Weakness: weak motivation/justification for the word importance weight vector w; risks altering document semantics.  
- Weakness: contribution appears incremental—linear metric learning applied to WMD is not highly novel.  
- Weakness: does not explain how to compute word importance weights for unseen documents, a major practical concern.  
- Weakness: ambiguous use of “semantic difference” conflates label/topic differences with true semantics.  
- Pro: method is technically sound, experimentally validated, and computationally practical.  
- Con: limited novelty, insufficient justification for key component (w), and unclear applicability to unseen documents.  
- Overall: solid, well-executed incremental contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing w justification and handling of unseen documents.
