- Proposes Residual Transfer Network (RTN) for domain adaptation that jointly learns adaptive classifiers and transferable features from labeled source and unlabeled target data.  
- Relaxes prior shared-classifier assumption by modeling target and source classifiers as differing via a learned residual function.  
- Implements classifier adaptation by inserting layers into deep networks to explicitly learn the residual with respect to the target classifier.  
- Well-written and clearly organized, making the approach and arguments easy to follow.  
- Provides a thorough related-work review that motivates the need for RTN.  
- RTN architecture and training procedure are clearly described.  
- Extensive experiments on benchmark datasets show RTN outperforms DDC, DAN, and RevGrad.  
- Ablation study analyzes the contribution of each RTN component.  
- Strengths: novel approach, well-motivated assumptions, clarity of presentation, and strong empirical results.  
- Weakness: requires careful hyperparameter tuning with unclear practical feasibility.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: does not address semi-supervised domain adaptation scenarios.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions (address complexity analysis, semi-supervised case, and hyperparameter tuning/sensitivity).  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
