- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): extends WMD with a linear transform A and word importance weights w for supervised metric learning.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks, consistently outperforming 26 competitive baselines.  
- Main contribution is incorporating label information into WMD to improve document classification performance.  
- Method is clearly and well-structured with mathematical formulation and optimization procedure provided.  
- Thorough empirical comparison against unsupervised WMD, Word Centroid Distance, and six document representation methods (with/without supervised metric learning).  
- Empirical results: lowest k-NN test error on 5/8 datasets and best average performance across all datasets.  
- Insight: S-WMD appears forgiving to syntactically different but conceptually similar words due to semantic word embeddings capturing meaning.  
- Reviewer requests clearer intuition/explanation for Eq. (5) and how word importance weights w re-weight histograms and affect document distance.  
- Reviewer asks for additional evidence or analysis to justify the claim about forgiveness for semantically similar words.  
- Minor issues: no description of how k is chosen for k-NN classification and missing explanation of "n" vs "ne" columns in Table 2.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension of WMD, thorough evaluation, clear presentation of method and optimization.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need more explanation of w, more evidence for the forgiveness claim, and minor reporting clarifications.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
