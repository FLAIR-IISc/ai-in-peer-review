- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transformation of embeddings and word-importance weights to incorporate supervision into WMD.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn distance as a fast approximation to make supervised WMD computationally practical.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks against 26 baselines, showing strong empirical performance.  
- Core idea is well-motivated: adapting WMD to task-specific supervision fills a practical gap in WMD’s applicability.  
- Learning both embedding transformation and per-word weights provides flexible metric adaptation.  
- Mathematical exposition is clear and detailed, aiding understanding of the method and theory.  
- Comprehensive experiments, including ablation studies (initialization vs. full training), strengthen the empirical claims.  
- Publicly available code enhances reproducibility and value to the community.  
- Main novelty is incremental relative to foundational WMD—supervised adaptation is the primary innovation.  
- Insufficient analysis and guidance on the entropic regularization parameter λ and its effect on approximation vs. speed.  
- Unclear whether reported results correspond to large λ (Wasserstein-like) or small λ (more entropic/fast), and users need guidance.  
- Inclusion of Word Centroid Distance (WCD) baseline feels tangential and distracts from supervised metric learning comparisons.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add deeper analysis of λ’s impact and refocus comparisons on supervised metric learning methods.  
- Reviewer confidence: 7/10.
