- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform of word embeddings and a word-importance weight vector to inject supervision into WMD.  
- Method is clearly written with a concise mathematical formulation and an explained optimization procedure.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, outperforming 26 baselines on 8 real-world text classification tasks.  
- Addresses WMD’s main limitation (lack of supervision) while preserving its semantic matching strengths.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization approach (fast gradient computation and batch gradient descent) that scales to large datasets.  
- Provides a thorough related-work analysis and situates the contribution relative to existing methods.  
- Main weakness: performance depends on the quality of pre-trained word embeddings, which may not always be available or accurate.  
- Potential applicability limits for certain text types (e.g., short texts or limited-vocabulary corpora).  
- Originality: novel supervised document-distance formulation that is well-motivated by existing limitations.  
- Significance: likely impactful for text classification, sentiment analysis, information retrieval, and NLP/ML research.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze robustness to short/limited-vocabulary texts, compare with deep learning baselines, and provide more optimization/hyperparameter and convergence details.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with high rating (9/10) and relatively high reviewer confidence (8/10).
