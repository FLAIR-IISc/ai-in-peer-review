**Paper Review: Supervised Word Mover's Distance**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a supervised learning approach to improve the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) metric, which is a state-of-the-art document distance metric. The authors introduce a novel technique called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), which learns a linear transformation of the word representations and a word-importance weight vector to minimize the stochastic leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error. The proposed method is evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and consistently outperforms 26 competitive baselines.

**Claims**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. S-WMD is a more effective document distance metric than the original WMD.
2. S-WMD can be efficiently learned using a relaxed version of the optimal transport problem and a probabilistic filtering of the training set.
3. S-WMD achieves state-of-the-art performance on eight real-world text classification tasks.

**Support for Claims**

The claims are supported by:

1. Experimental results on eight real-world text classification tasks, which show that S-WMD consistently outperforms 26 competitive baselines.
2. A theoretical analysis of the relaxed optimal transport problem, which shows that it can be efficiently solved using a matrix-scaling algorithm.
3. A discussion of the limitations of the original WMD metric and how S-WMD addresses these limitations.

**Usefulness**

The proposed method is practically useful for several reasons:

1. It improves the performance of document classification tasks, which is a critical application in many fields, such as information retrieval and natural language processing.
2. It provides a more effective way to incorporate supervision into the WMD metric, which can be useful in scenarios where labeled data is available.
3. It can be efficiently learned using a relaxed version of the optimal transport problem, which makes it scalable to large datasets.

**Field Knowledge**

The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the field of document distance metrics and metric learning. The authors provide a clear overview of the related work and demonstrate a deep understanding of the strengths and limitations of existing methods.

**Novelty**

The proposed method is novel in several ways:

1. It introduces a supervised learning approach to improve the WMD metric, which is a new direction in the field of document distance metrics.
2. It uses a relaxed version of the optimal transport problem to efficiently learn the linear transformation of the word representations and the word-importance weight vector.
3. It provides a new way to incorporate supervision into the WMD metric, which can be useful in scenarios where labeled data is available.

**Completeness**

2. Experimental results on eight real-world text classification tasks, which demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
3. A discussion of the limitations of the proposed method and potential future work.

**Limitations**

The paper acknowledges several limitations, including:

1. The proposed method assumes that the word embeddings are fixed, which may not be optimal in all scenarios.
2. The method may not be effective in scenarios where the labeled data is limited or noisy.
