- Proposes S-WMD: learn a linear transformation of embeddings and word-importance weights to minimize k-NN classification error on top of WMD.  
- Addresses WMD’s computational bottleneck using Sinkhorn (entropic) relaxation and a probabilistic filtering scheme.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across eight text classification tasks, outperforming 26 baselines.  
- Includes an ablation study on initialization (S-WMD init.) that adds value to the evaluation.  
- Novelty: supervised metric learning for WMD is a meaningful and original extension.  
- Technical soundness: using Sinkhorn distance is a well-justified and effective approximation for tractability.  
- Practicality: method is computationally efficient and viable for real-world use.  
- Weakness: optimization details (learning rates, batch size, convergence criteria) are under-specified, hurting reproducibility.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ is not explored.  
- Weakness: interpretability of learned word-importance weights is limited—only one dataset visualized; no systematic analysis across datasets.  
- Weakness: impact of word embedding dimensionality is not investigated and embedding size is inconsistently reported.  
- Weakness: presentation suffers from dense notation and disorganized flow; some mathematical steps (e.g., dual variables from u and v) need clearer, more intuitive exposition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, is novel and technically sound, and shows state-of-the-art empirical results with extensive comparisons.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks key optimization details, interpretability analysis, and clarity in presentation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add hyperparameter table, deeper analysis of word weights, and clearer/simplified derivations.
