- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD) that learns a linear ground-cost transform A and a word-importance vector w to make WMD label-aware.
- Optimizes an NCA-style stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss to align document distances with labels.
- Replaces exact OT with Sinkhorn-regularized OT and uses transport plans and dual potentials for differentiable gradients.
- Uses WCD-based initialization, mini-batching, and neighbor filtering to make training tractable.
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines; achieves best average rank and lowest error on 5/8 datasets; code released.
- Builds on WMD, Sinkhorn OT, and NCA; novel in jointly learning ground metric and histogram reweighting for NLP.
- Clear motivation: making WMD supervised is a natural and useful extension.
- Principled formulation: jointly learning A and w within OT with a kNN-targeted objective is sound.
- Efficient optimization: Sinkhorn-based gradients and pragmatic heuristics make the approach scalable.
- Empirical strength: consistently competitive or superior across many datasets and baselines.
- Reproducibility: public implementation and described tuning procedure.
- Unclear constraints on w: nonnegativity and explicit regularization/details are not specified and should be clarified.
- Limited overfitting controls: little discussion of norm/rank regularization on A or regularizers for w given nonconvexity.
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters not explored: fixed λ, neighbor truncation, and batch size lack sensitivity analysis.
- Test-time complexity concerns: kNN with S-WMD still requires many OT computations; quantitative latency vs. baselines is missing.
- Missing ablations: no isolated experiments for (A only), (w only), and joint (A,w) to attribute gains.
- Scope of comparisons: lacks comparisons to supervised neural text classifiers (e.g., CNNs, doc2vec) for broader context.
- Minor clarity issues: typos, section/notation inconsistencies, and unclear terms in dual potential formula.
- Suggestions: enforce/clarify positivity and regularization for w and A, report test-time latency and hyperparameter sensitivity, add ablations, and compare to at least one supervised neural model.
- Acceptance arguments: fills an important gap with a principled, efficient method, strong empirical results, and practical contributions.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient analysis of test-time scalability, hyperparameter sensitivity, and missing ablations disentangling A vs. w.
