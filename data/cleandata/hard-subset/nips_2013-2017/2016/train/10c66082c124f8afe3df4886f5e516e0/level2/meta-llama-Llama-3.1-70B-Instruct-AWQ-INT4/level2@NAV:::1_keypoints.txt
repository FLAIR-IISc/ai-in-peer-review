- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised method learning a linear transform and word-importance weights to improve Word Mover’s Distance for document comparison.  
- Claims S-WMD is more effective than original WMD, learnable efficiently via a relaxed optimal-transport formulation, and achieves state-of-the-art on eight tasks.  
- Empirically supported by experiments showing S-WMD outperforms 26 baselines across eight real-world text classification datasets.  
- Theoretical support: relaxed optimal transport can be solved efficiently using a matrix-scaling algorithm.  
- Addresses known limitations of WMD by incorporating supervision and importance weighting.  
- Practically useful: improves document classification performance in IR/NLP applications.  
- Useful where labeled data exists, offering a principled supervised incorporation into WMD.  
- Claimed scalable via the relaxed optimal-transport approach, making learning feasible on larger datasets.  
- Demonstrates strong field knowledge with a clear related-work overview and understanding of metric learning issues.  
- Novel contributions: supervised learning for WMD, use of relaxed optimal transport to learn transforms and weights, and supervised integration into WMD.  
- Paper is reproducible with clear method description, experimental details, and discussion of limitations.  
- Limitations: assumes fixed word embeddings, may struggle with limited/noisy labels, and scalability to very large datasets is uncertain.  
- Constructive suggestions: provide more experimental hyperparameter details, analyze learned word-importance vectors across tasks, and discuss applications beyond classification.  
- Pro-accept: novel, effective method with strong empirical performance and reproducibility.  
- Con-accept: concerns about fixed embeddings, limited/noisy label scenarios, and potential scalability issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—novel, effective approach with clear presentation and sufficient reproducibility details.
