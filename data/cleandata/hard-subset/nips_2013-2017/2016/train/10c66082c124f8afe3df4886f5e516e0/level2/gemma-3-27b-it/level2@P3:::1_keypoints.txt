- Proposes S-WMD: learns a linear transformation of embeddings and word importance weights to minimize leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD computationally feasible.  
- Experiments on eight datasets (26 baselines) show S-WMD consistently outperforms many baselines.  
- Well-motivated: addresses the limitation of WMD being unsupervised for supervised tasks.  
- Technical strengths: Sinkhorn relaxation enables efficient gradient computation; word importance weights add model flexibility.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized with detailed mathematical derivations and algorithmic details.  
- Strong experimental evaluation: large number of baselines and an ablation study comparing S-WMD to its initialization (S-WCD).  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, e.g., squared Euclidean ground cost may be suboptimal for some datasets.  
- Weakness: the paper could better articulate why the Sinkhorn relaxation enables more efficient gradient computation than original WMD.  
- Weakness: related work discussion is limited and should more fully compare to other methods addressing optimal transport computational challenges.  
- Weakness: the regularization parameter λ (set to 10) appears arbitrary; a systematic evaluation or validation-based selection is needed.  
- Weakness: reliance on PCA for dimensionality reduction in some baselines is not thoroughly discussed and could confound comparisons.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with optimal transport and metric learning; some readers may need more introductory material.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and principled supervision for WMD, strong empirical results, technical soundness, and thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient explanation of Sinkhorn–gradient link, arbitrary λ choice, and incomplete related-work coverage.  
- Overall: high-quality paper recommended for acceptance with suggested revisions to clarify gradient computation, explore λ values, and expand related work.
