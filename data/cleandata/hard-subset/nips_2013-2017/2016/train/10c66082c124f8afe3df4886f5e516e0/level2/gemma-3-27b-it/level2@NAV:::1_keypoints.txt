- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD): learn a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word importance vector to minimize k-NN classification error.  
- Introduces an efficient gradient approximation for WMD using the Sinkhorn distance to enable supervised learning.  
- Well-motivated extension of WMD addressing its unsupervised limitation.  
- Technically sound approach; Sinkhorn-based approximation is a significant practical contribution.  
- Strong empirical performance: evaluated on eight datasets and compared to 26 baselines, consistently outperforming competitors.  
- Ablation study demonstrates the importance of initialization choices.  
- Mathematical notation and gradient derivations are dense and need clearer exposition.  
- Limited discussion of potential failure modes, e.g., when the linear transformation assumption breaks down.  
- No sensitivity analysis reported for the Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, fills a key gap in WMD, efficient supervised training, comprehensive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dense mathematical presentation, limited limitations discussion, missing λ sensitivity study.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and address the noted limitations.
