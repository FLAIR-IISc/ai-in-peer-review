- Proposes Supervised-WMD (S-WMD): learns a linear transform of embeddings and word importance weights for document classification.  
- Addresses limitation of unsupervised WMD by incorporating task-specific supervision.  
- Claims consistent outperformance of 26 baselines across 8 text classification tasks.  
- Strength: clear motivation and proper contextualization within related work.  
- Strength: sound theoretical foundation for gradient approximation using the Sinkhorn distance.  
- Strength: well-explained, practical implementation including efficient batch gradient descent.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on 8 datasets showing lowest average error.  
- Weakness: no statistical significance tests provided to validate claimed performance improvements.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of why S-WMD outperforms WMD and no investigation of dataset characteristics where it fails.  
- Weakness: unexplained switch to squared Euclidean distance (vs. original L1) and no discussion of its impact.  
- Weakness: initialization (S-WMD init.) often nearly as good as final model, but paper doesn't explore its sufficiency.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to Sinkhorn regularization parameter (λ=10) not analyzed, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: related work omits some relevant supervised embedding papers (e.g., Tang et al., 2015).  
- Suggestion: include statistical validation, deeper analysis of learned weights and dataset behavior, and discussion of training time vs. marginal gains.
