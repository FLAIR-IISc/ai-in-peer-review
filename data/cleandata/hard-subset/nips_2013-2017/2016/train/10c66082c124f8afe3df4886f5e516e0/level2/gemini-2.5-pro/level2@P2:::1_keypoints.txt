- Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a task-specific document distance that extends WMD with supervision.
- Key contributions: jointly learn an affine transform of embeddings and a word-importance weight vector.
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD, enabling differentiable and faster computation.
- Trains S-WMD by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss with neighbor-filtered batch gradient descent.
- Practical initialization via a supervised Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) speeds convergence.
- Extensive evaluation on eight datasets against 26 baselines shows consistent state-of-the-art kNN classification performance.
- High technical quality: well-motivated, principled solution to differentiating through optimal transport.
- Clever and practical optimization strategy that makes the method efficient and scalable.
- Thorough experimental setup: many strong baselines, metric-learning comparisons, visualizations, and learned-word analysis.
- Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful background, derivations, and algorithm pseudocode.
- Original: first to apply ground-metric learning to WMD for text and to jointly learn word re-weighting.
- Significant: extends WMD to supervised tasks and provides an efficient way to differentiate through WMD that can inspire follow-up work.
- Strengths summarized: novelty, technical soundness, convincing experiments, and clarity of exposition.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: include ablation showing effect of learning A vs w, discuss hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., Sinkhorn lambda, neighbor size), and justify choice of squared Euclidean distance norm.
