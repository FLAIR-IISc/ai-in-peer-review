- Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns an affine transformation of embeddings and word-importance weights for improved document classification.  
- Proposes efficient training by minimizing leave-one-out kNN error and using the Sinkhorn distance to approximate WMD gradients for scalable updates.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification datasets, outperforming 26 baselines and consistently reducing kNN classification error.  

Strengths:
- Novel supervised formulation that adds flexibility via learned linear transformation and word-importance weights.  
- Solid theoretical grounding with detailed gradient derivations and principled optimization.  
- Practical computational solution using Sinkhorn distance enabling efficient batch training.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across diverse datasets with strong, consistent gains.  
- Interpretability via t-SNE visualizations and word importance analyses.  

Weaknesses:
- Method is sensitive to initialization (relies on S-WCD), which can degrade performance on some datasets.  
- Not uniformly best across all datasets (e.g., fourth-best on BBCSPORT and CLASSIC), indicating dataset-specific limits.  
- Scalability concerns remain due to quadratic complexity in document size and large vocabularies.  
- Presentation is dense and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with optimal transport or metric learning.  

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance: significant contribution to supervised metric learning with strong empirical results, despite minor concerns.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, impactful supervised document distance; strong empirical performance; efficient computational approach.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Initialization sensitivity and dataset-specific variability; potential scalability issues for very large corpora.
