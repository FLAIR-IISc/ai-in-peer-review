- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that jointly learns an affine word-embedding transform and word importance weights, using Sinkhorn regularization for efficient gradient approximation.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements vs. 26 baselines on 8 text classification tasks, achieving lowest error on 5/8 datasets.  
- Fills a gap in WMD literature by incorporating supervision while preserving semantic richness of pre-trained embeddings.  
- Sinkhorn approximation is well-justified and enables practical scalability; empirical complexity validated (Table 3).  
- Experimental design is thorough, comparing diverse baselines (unsupervised and supervised) and including clear visualizations of cluster separation and word weights.  
- Initialization via NCA on WCD (S-WCD) meaningfully reduces training time.  
- Gradient derivation (Section 3.1) is dense; recommend clarifying the step from Eq.(9) to Eq.(10) (e.g., invoke envelope theorem/dual variables).  
- S-WMD underperforms WMD on the CLASSIC dataset; suggest systematic analysis of initialization sensitivity and testing alternative word distance metrics (e.g., cosine).  
- Runtime claims should include total training time comparisons to baseline methods (e.g., mSDA, LDA) for better context.  
- Scalability to very large vocabularies (>100k) is not discussed; recommend mentioning vocabulary pruning or other mitigation strategies.  
- Overall: technically sound, novel, clearly written, and impactful; minor clarifications would strengthen an already strong paper—recommend acceptance.
