- Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) to learn a task-specific document distance for kNN classification.  
- Learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and per-word importance weights from labeled data.  
- Uses a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective inspired by Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA).  
- Main technical challenge: differentiating through WMD’s nested optimization; solved via entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance.  
- Sinkhorn-based approximation yields a differentiable, efficient proxy to WMD and enables gradient computation.  
- Derives gradients for both embedding transform A and word weights w; derivations appear sound.  
- Practical optimizations include a strong initialization scheme and batching strategy to make training feasible.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation on eight datasets, comparing against 26 strong baselines; S-WMD often achieves state-of-the-art.  
- Baselines include classic (TF-IDF, LSI, LDA), representation learning (mSDA), original WMD, and metric learning methods (NCA, ITML, LMNN) applied fairly.  
- Qualitative analyses (t-SNE and word clouds) and training time reporting add useful insight and practicality evidence.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear background, notation, and a concise Algorithm 1 summarizing training.  
- The approach is highly original, combining WMD, NCA, and Sinkhorn distances and introducing learned word importance weights.  
- The gradient-computation solution is a notable technical contribution that could transfer to other EMD/WMD applications.  
- Work is significant: makes WMD adaptable to tasks and likely to be adopted for kNN-based text classification and other domains.  
- Pros: novel problem framing, elegant Sinkhorn-based solution, strong empirical results, clarity, and provided code for reproducibility.  
- Con: squared Euclidean distance choice in embedding space may be suboptimal; needs discussion/justification.  
- Con: sensitivity to Sinkhorn hyperparameter λ (set to 10) is unclear; suggested analysis of performance/stability w.r.t. λ.  
- Con: lacks comparison/discussion relative to end-to-end deep text classifiers (CNNs/RNNs) that also learn task-specific representations.
