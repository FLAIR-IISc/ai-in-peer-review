- Paper proposes S-WMD: jointly learn linear transform A on word embeddings and per-word importance weights w, training with an NCA-style kNN loss using Sinkhorn-regularized OT for gradients.
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain unique transport plans and dual potentials, enabling efficient, differentiable training; includes S-WCD initialization and neighbor truncation to speed training.
- Builds on WMD, entropy-regularized OT, and NCA; differs from prior supervised EMD by jointly learning embedding ground metric and histogram reweighting for text.
- Empirically strong: outperforms 26 baselines across eight datasets, best average rank and state-of-the-art on 5/8 datasets; consistent gains on short texts, weaker on some long-document sets.
- Technical soundness is good overall; gradients for A and w via Sinkhorn duals are sensible and practical.
- Major concern: possible training–evaluation mismatch — loss written with exact WMD but gradients use Sinkhorn; need clarification and sensitivity analysis for using exact vs. regularized distances at train/test.
- Need explicit constraints/regularization on w and A (positivity, L2, scale control) and empirical sensitivity to A’s norm and potential degenerate solutions.
- Notation issues and dual derivation need cleanup (overloaded symbol p, clearer derivation/citation for α*, β* and centering constants).
- Report Sinkhorn complexity details: typical iterations, tolerance, and sensitivity to regularization λ (λ fixed at 10 currently).
- Paper is well-organized but should explicitly state constraints on A and w, how r (reduced dimension) is chosen, and whether distances used in training/testing are exact or regularized.
- Original and significant: novel combination of supervised learning with WMD via joint A and w learning; likely to be adopted and inspire follow-up work.
- Strengths: clear motivation, sensible use of Sinkhorn, clever initialization/truncation, broad baselines with thorough tuning, and supporting qualitative analyses.
- Weaknesses/suggestions: include ablations (A only, w only, both), study effect of initialization, λ, neighbor size N, and dimensionality r; add regularization experiments and fairness/external-data discussion.
- Suggest exploring hybrid approaches for long documents (e.g., IDF-informed w, non-squared ground distances) and clarifying whether pretrained embeddings advantage baselines.
- Questions for authors to answer: do you enforce w ≥ 0 or regularize A/w; what is r and is A dimensionality-reducing; are training/test distances exact WMD or Sinkhorn; sensitivity to λ and neighbor truncation N?
- Pros for acceptance: addresses an important limitation of WMD with principled, efficient method and strong empirical results plus released code.
- Cons for acceptance: clarity gaps (training–evaluation mismatch), limited ablations/regularization analysis, and weaker results on some long-document datasets.
- Overall recommendation: strong, practical contribution leaning toward acceptance pending clarifications, added ablations, and minor fixes.
