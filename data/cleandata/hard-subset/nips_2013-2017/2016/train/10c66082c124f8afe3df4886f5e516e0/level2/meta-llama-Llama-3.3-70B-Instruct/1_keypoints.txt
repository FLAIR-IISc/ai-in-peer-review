- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a task-specific document distance and outperforms baselines on eight text classification tasks.  
- Builds on WMD and uses the Sinkhorn (relaxed optimal transport) formulation for efficient computation.  
- Paper is well-written with clear background and related work.  
- Technical contributions are sound and the method is convincingly evaluated.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation showing superiority over 26 competitive baselines.  
- Efficient and scalable computation suitable for large datasets due to Sinkhorn relaxation.  
- Weakness: depends on pre-trained word embeddings, which may be unavailable or unsuitable for some domains.  
- Weakness: potentially sensitive to hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, number of nearest neighbors), requiring careful tuning.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, strong empirical results, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability due to embedding reliance and hyperparameter sensitivity.
