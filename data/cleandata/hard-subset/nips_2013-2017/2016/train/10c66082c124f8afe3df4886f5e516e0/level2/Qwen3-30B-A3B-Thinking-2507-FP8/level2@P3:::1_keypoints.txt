- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), extending WMD with supervision for improved document classification.  
- Jointly learns an affine transformation of the embedding space and per-word importance weights.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance to approximate gradients efficiently and avoid WMD's cubic complexity.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains across eight datasets and 26 baselines, achieving best average performance.  
- Provides rigorous evaluation with statistical analysis, average ranks, and visualizations of learned word weights.  
- Well-motivated and technically sound; scalable via batch processing and practical training times.  
- Clear exposition with effective background, mathematical detail, and logical organization.  
- Novel approach by applying supervised learning directly on word embeddings rather than high-dimensional document vectors.  
- Significant practical value: leverages pre-trained embeddings while improving classification without heavy retraining.  
- Computationally efficient compared to original WMD and comparable to standard metric learning methods.  
- Suggests broader applicability in NLP and good alignment with conference topics.  
- Minor weaknesses: lacks detailed analysis of failure cases (e.g., CLASSIC dataset).  
- Hyperparameter λ is chosen without sufficient justification or sensitivity analysis.  
- Would benefit from deeper investigation of how learned word weights relate to task-specific semantics.  
- Overall strong contribution meeting criteria of technical quality, clarity, novelty, and significance.
