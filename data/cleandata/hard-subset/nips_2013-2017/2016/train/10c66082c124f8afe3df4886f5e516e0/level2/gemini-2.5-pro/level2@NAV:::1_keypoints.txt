- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that jointly learns a linear embedding transform and word-importance weights.
- Key technical advance: differentiates through WMD by using an entropy-regularized Sinkhorn approximation for efficient gradient-based learning.
- Integrates metric learning (NCA loss) with computational optimal transport techniques.
- Method is novel, well-motivated, and technically sound.
- Evaluation is thorough: tested on 8 datasets against 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art kNN error on most tasks.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases where S-WMD is outperformed (e.g., CLASSIC).
- Weakness: lacks a sensitivity analysis for the regularization parameter λ and its speed–accuracy trade-off.
- Overall verdict: high-quality, significant contribution with strong empirical support; strongly recommended for acceptance.
