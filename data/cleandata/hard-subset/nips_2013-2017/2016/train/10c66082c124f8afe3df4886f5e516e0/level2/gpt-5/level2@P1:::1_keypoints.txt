- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a Mahalanobis ground metric A and nonnegative word-importance vector w for kNN document classification.
- Uses a Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA)-style stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss where neighborhood probabilities are computed from WMD.
- Replaces exact WMD with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn OT to obtain differentiable, efficient gradients for A and w.
- Computes gradients for A from the relaxed transport plan T*λ and for w via dual potentials (α, β) from Sinkhorn scaling vectors (u, v).
- Training速度ups: initialize A via NCA on Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD), use minibatching, and restrict neighbor computations to WCD-based nearest neighbors.
- Empirically evaluated on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines; S-WMD achieves best average performance and top test error on 5/8 datasets.
- Provides qualitative analysis (t-SNE with S-WMD distances, high-weight word visualizations) and releases code.
- Builds on prior work: WMD, entropy-regularized OT (Cuturi), NCA, and supervised EMD/metric learning literature, but novel in jointly learning ground metric and histogram reweighting for text.
- Strength: principled combination of OT-based document distance and supervised metric learning with practical training techniques.
- Strength: learning both A and w increases adaptability to task semantics compared to fixed WMD.
- Strength: thorough empirical comparisons and runtime/complexity analysis.
- Weakness: approximation gap—unclear whether training and forward distances use regularized OT or exact WMD and how λ affects bias/variance and accuracy.
- Weakness: insufficient clarity on constraints/regularization for w (nonnegativity, L1/L2) and no ablation separating A-only vs w-only vs both.
- Weakness: potential identifiability/scaling ambiguity between A and λ; normalization or regularization of A not fully discussed.
- Weakness: evaluation scope limited to kNN—missing sensitivity analyses to k, impact of A on other classifiers, and per-query inference scalability comparisons.
- Weakness: baselines omit contemporary neural document encoders (CNN/RNN) that could be competitive on some tasks.
- Weakness: derivation clarity—needs appendix derivation for w-gradient and conditions under which Sinkhorn dual potentials approximate true gradients; minor notation issues.
- Key questions for authors: are w entries constrained nonnegative and regularized? How is rank r of A chosen?
- Key questions: is regularized OT used at test time or is exact WMD computed? Sensitivity to λ, neighbor set size N, and batch size?
- Key questions: scalability to very long documents (large q) and large retrieval pools (per-query latency)?
- Evaluation summary: technically sound method with practical contributions; some theoretical/experimental clarifications needed for reproducibility.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid empirical gains and a practical advance over WMD, but needs additional analyses/ablations and clarification on approximation, constraints, and baselines.
