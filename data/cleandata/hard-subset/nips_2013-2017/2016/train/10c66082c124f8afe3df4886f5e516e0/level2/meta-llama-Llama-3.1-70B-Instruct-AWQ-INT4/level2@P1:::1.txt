**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a supervised extension to the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) metric, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD). The WMD metric, introduced by Kusner et al., leverages word embeddings to compute distances between documents. However, the WMD is entirely unsupervised and lacks a mechanism to incorporate supervision when available. The authors propose an efficient technique to learn a supervised metric by minimizing the stochastic leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error on a per-document level. The S-WMD metric learns a linear transformation of the word embedding space and a word-importance weight vector. The authors evaluate S-WMD on eight real-world text classification tasks and demonstrate its superiority over 26 competitive baselines.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel extension to WMD**: The paper proposes a novel extension to the WMD metric, which is a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing.
2. **Efficient optimization**: The authors propose an efficient optimization technique to learn the supervised metric, which is a significant improvement over the original WMD optimization problem.
3. **State-of-the-art results**: The paper demonstrates state-of-the-art results on eight real-world text classification tasks, outperforming 26 competitive baselines.
4. **Well-written and organized**: The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow and understand.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited theoretical analysis**: The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the proposed S-WMD metric, which would provide deeper insights into its properties and behavior.
2. **Dependence on word embeddings**: The S-WMD metric relies heavily on the quality of the word embeddings used, which may not always be optimal.
3. **Computational complexity**: Although the authors propose an efficient optimization technique, the computational complexity of the S-WMD metric may still be high for very large datasets.
