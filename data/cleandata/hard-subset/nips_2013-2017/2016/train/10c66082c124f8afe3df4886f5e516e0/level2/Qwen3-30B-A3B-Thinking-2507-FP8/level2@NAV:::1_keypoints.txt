- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear transform of embeddings and word importance weights.  
- Training minimizes leave-one-out classification error and uses Sinkhorn distance for computational tractability.  
- Addresses the key limitation of WMD being entirely unsupervised by introducing supervision into the distance.  
- Technical approach is sound with justified Sinkhorn approximation and efficient gradient computations.  
- Demonstrates consistent superiority across 8 text-classification datasets, outperforming 26 baselines.  
- Achieves lowest error on 5/8 datasets and the best average rank; results are statistically significant.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive with appropriate baselines, multiple datasets, and detailed analyses.  
- Visualization of learned word weights shows the method identifies discriminative terms effectively.  
- Paper provides clear algorithmic details and implementation notes, aiding reproducibility.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations (e.g., suboptimal performance on CLASSIC due to initialization) and speed vs. approximation trade-offs.  
- Suggests the paper needs more discussion on scenarios where S-WMD is inappropriate and clearer computational complexity comparisons to alternatives.  
- Recommends explicit differentiation from Wang et al.'s EMD metric learning work.  
- Builds on prior work (Kusner et al.'s WMD and NCA-like metric learning) and makes a meaningful, novel contribution.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, well-written, practical utility for document classification, recommended for acceptance.
