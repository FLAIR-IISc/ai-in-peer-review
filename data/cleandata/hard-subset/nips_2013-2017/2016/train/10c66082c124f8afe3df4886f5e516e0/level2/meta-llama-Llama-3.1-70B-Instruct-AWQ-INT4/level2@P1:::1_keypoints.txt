Summary: Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transform and word-importance weights by minimizing stochastic leave-one-out NN error; evaluated on 8 tasks, outperforming 26 baselines.

Strength — Novelty: Introduces a novel supervised extension to WMD.

Strength — Optimization: Presents an efficient optimization technique for learning the supervised metric.

Strength — Empirical results: Achieves state-of-the-art performance on eight real-world text classification tasks.

Strength — Presentation: Paper is well-written and well-organized.

Weakness — Theory: Lacks thorough theoretical analysis of S-WMD’s properties and behavior.

Weakness — Embedding dependence: Performance depends heavily on the quality of input word embeddings.

Weakness — Scalability: Computational complexity may remain high on very large datasets.

Originality: Combines word embeddings and supervised learning into a unique supervised WMD variant.

Significance: Has potential to improve various NLP tasks (text classification, sentiment analysis, IR) given strong empirical gains.

Quality: Clear, concise explanation and solid experimental evaluation.

Clarity: Writing and presentation are clear and easy to follow.

Pro acceptance: Novel contribution, strong empirical performance, and clear exposition.

Con acceptance: Limited theoretical understanding, reliance on embeddings, and potential scalability concerns.

Recommendation: Recommend accept.
