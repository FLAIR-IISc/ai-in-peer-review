- Introduces S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and word-importance weights w for task-specific document distance.
- Uses an NCA-inspired objective to minimize a differentiable proxy of leave-one-out kNN error for metric learning.
- Main technical contribution: replaces WMD with entropically regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain a differentiable, efficiently solvable transport objective.
- Derives gradients for both A and w via the dual variables of the regularized transport problem.
- Practical improvements include WCD-based initialization and a batch training scheme that restricts to a small nearest-neighbor subset.
- Empirical evaluation is extensive: compares to 26 baselines across 8 datasets, showing best performance on 5/8 datasets and best average rank.
- Provides training-time analysis and qualitative visualizations (t-SNE, word clouds) to support empirical claims.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides accessible background on relevant methods.
- Scalability concern: gradient computation scales O(p^2) with unique words per document, which may hurt very long documents.
- Method fixes ground metric to squared Euclidean in transformed space; lack of other norms (e.g., L1) may explain poorer performance on some datasets.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity is underexplored (e.g., λ, neighbor set size N, batch size B, learning rates), and guidance for practitioners is limited.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, technically solid, and empirically convincing work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel supervised extension of WMD, principled Sinkhorn-based solution, state-of-the-art results, and excellent presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance (minor): potential scalability limits and limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and ground-metric choices.
