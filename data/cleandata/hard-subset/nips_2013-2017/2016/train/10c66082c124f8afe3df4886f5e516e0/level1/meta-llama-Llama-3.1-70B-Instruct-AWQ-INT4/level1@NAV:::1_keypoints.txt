- Proposal: Introduces Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word-importance weights to supervise WMD.
- Motivation: Addresses WMD's lack of supervision despite its strong kNN classification performance.
- Methodology: Formulates a sound mathematical approach for learning embeddings transform and importance weights to improve document distance.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and thorough explanation of related work.
- Experiments: Comprehensive evaluation against 26 baselines on eight real-world text classification tasks.
- Results: Empirical results show S-WMD outperforms competing methods.
- Strength: Novel, well-motivated, and effective supervised document distance metric.
- Strength: Thorough experimental validation supporting claims.
- Weakness: Assumes reader familiarity with word embeddings and document distance metrics.
- Weakness: Some mathematical derivations may be challenging for non-expert readers.
- Weakness: Lacks visualizations that could clarify the method and results.
- Originality: Distinct supervised approach to WMD, providing a new perspective on incorporating supervision.
- Significance: Addresses an important NLP problem with potential impact on classification and clustering tasks.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, well-motivated method with strong empirical support and clear writing.
- Rejection arguments: Accessibility concerns due to assumed background and potentially hard-to-follow derivations.
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance for publication.
