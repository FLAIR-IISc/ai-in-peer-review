- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) to learn a task-specific document distance metric.  
- Jointly learns a linear transformation A of word embeddings and a word-importance vector w to tailor WMD for classification.  
- Replaces intractable WMD gradient with entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain a differentiable, efficiently solvable objective.  
- Derives approximate gradients w.r.t. A and w using the Sinkhorn relaxation, enabling practical gradient-based optimization.  
- Improves efficiency with WCD-based initialization and batch/local-neighborhood training.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on eight datasets, comparing against 26 strong baselines and supervised metric-learning combinations.  
- Uses Bayesian optimization for fair hyperparameter tuning across all methods.  
- Demonstrates consistent state-of-the-art performance on the majority of benchmarks.  
- Paper is clearly written, self-contained, and well-organized with helpful figures and an algorithm overview.  
- Minor clarity issue: derivation of dual variables from Sinkhorn scaling vectors lacks citation or brief justification.  
- Highly original synthesis of WMD, NCA, and Sinkhorn distances, plus simultaneous learning of geometry and word weights.  
- Significant contribution: adds supervision to WMD, scalable learning in embedding space, likely to inspire follow-up work.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: well-motivated problem, elegant technical solution, extensive convincing experiments, clear presentation.  
- Minor weakness: relies on an approximation to true WMD gradients; more analysis of the regularization parameter λ trade-offs would strengthen the paper.
