- Proposal: Introduces S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear embedding transformation and a word importance vector.  
- Empirical result: Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and consistently outperforms 26 competitive baselines.  
- Motivation: Addresses WMD's lack of supervision by learning a metric that captures label-encoded semantic similarity.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD and draws from metric learning literature (e.g., NCA, LMNN, ITML) but operates within the word embedding/OT framework.  
- Method/optimization: Proposes an efficient learning technique using a relaxed regularized optimal transport formulation.  
- Strength — supervision: Effectively incorporates supervision to improve semantic similarity modeling.  
- Strength — optimization: Offers an efficient optimization approach for the supervised metric.  
- Strength — performance: Achieves state-of-the-art results across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness — computation: Requires solving a regularized transport problem, which can be computationally expensive.  
- Weakness — hyperparameters: Relies on Bayesian optimization for tuning, which is time-consuming and not guaranteed optimal.  
- Weakness — interpretability: Learned transformation of embedding space is complex and hard to interpret.  
- Originality: Novel combination of WMD and metric learning to enable supervised document distances.  
- Significance: Potentially impactful for text classification and applications like search, recommendation, and news categorization.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized, though some derivations (e.g., gradient) may be hard for non-experts.  
- Quality: Technically sound with thorough evaluation and detailed analysis.  
- Acceptance pros: Strong empirical results, effective use of supervision, and efficient learning method.  
- Acceptance cons: Computational cost, interpretability concerns, and expensive hyperparameter tuning.
