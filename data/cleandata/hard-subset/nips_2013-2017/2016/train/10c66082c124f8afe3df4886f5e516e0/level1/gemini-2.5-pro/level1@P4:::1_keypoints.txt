- Proposes S-WMD: supervised Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transformation A and a word-importance vector w to adapt WMD for classification.  
- Learns A and w by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-like) classification loss.  
- Main technical bottleneck is differentiating through the WMD (a linear program) required for gradient-based learning.  
- Core contribution: replace WMD with entropically regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain a differentiable, strictly convex proxy.  
- Derives gradients w.r.t. A and w using the dual of the transport problem and computes the transport plan in O(q^2) time (q = unique words).  
- Accelerates training via pre-training A with Word Centroid Distance and batching/neighborhood-limiting strategies.  
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation on 8 datasets versus 26 strong baselines, with Bayesian hyperparameter tuning.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art or near state-of-the-art kNN classification performance, showing significant practical impact.  
- Paper is clearly written, with solid background, clear formulations, interpretable visualizations, and publicly released code.  
- Originality: first successful metric learning application to WMD for documents and novel joint learning of embedding transform and word weights.  
- Significance: effectively combines unsupervised word embeddings with supervised metric learning using differentiable optimal transport, with broader research applicability.  
- Weakness: uses squared Euclidean ground metric for compatibility with NCA without thorough analysis of this modeling choice or its effects.  
- Weakness: evaluation focuses on kNN; lacks comparisons or discussion relative to end-to-end deep learning classifiers (CNNs/RNNs).
