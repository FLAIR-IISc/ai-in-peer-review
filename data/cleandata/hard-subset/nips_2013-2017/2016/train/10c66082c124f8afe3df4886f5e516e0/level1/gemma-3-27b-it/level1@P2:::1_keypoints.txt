- Proposes S-WMD: learns a linear transformation of embeddings and a word-importance weight vector to supervise WMD.  
- Objective: minimize leave-one-out nearest-neighbor (LOO-NN) classification error.  
- Computational approach: uses Sinkhorn (entropic) relaxation of optimal transport plus probabilistic filtering.  
- Optimization: employs a stochastic approximation inspired by NCA to handle LOO non-differentiability.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms 26 baselines across 8 text classification datasets.  
- Includes extensive experiments and ablation studies (initialization vs full training).  
- Presentation and related work coverage are generally clear and appropriate.  
- Technical soundness: method and Sinkhorn approximation are reasonable and well-motivated.  
- Weakness: stochastic approximation explanation is thin; potential biases not fully analyzed.  
- Weakness: connection to NCA is mentioned but not deeply justified.  
- Weakness: heavy reliance on Sinkhorn λ (λ=10) without comprehensive sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness: complexity analysis is abstract; actual runtimes across datasets/dimensions are missing.  
- Weakness: unclear which component (linear transform vs word weighting) drives improved clustering.  
- Overall assessment: significant, novel, practically valuable contribution — reviewer recommends acceptance.
