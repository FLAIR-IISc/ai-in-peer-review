This paper proposes the Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method for learning a task-specific document metric. It builds directly upon the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) by Kusner et al. (ICML 2015), which introduced a powerful unsupervised metric based on the optimal transport cost between word embeddings. The key limitation of WMD is its inability to incorporate supervision. This work addresses this gap by learning two components: an affine transformation of the word embedding space and a word-importance weight vector. The optimization objective is inspired by Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA), minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification error. The authors' main technical contribution is to circumvent the intractable gradient computation of the original WMD by using the entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance, which provides an efficient and differentiable approximation.

**Arguments for acceptance:**
*   **Significance:** The paper addresses a clear and important limitation of a highly influential document distance metric. Injecting supervision into the WMD framework is a valuable contribution with the potential for broad impact in text classification and retrieval. The strong empirical results demonstrate a significant advance over an extensive set of baselines.
*   **Originality and Quality:** The method is technically sound and novel. Using the Sinkhorn distance to enable gradient-based optimization for a WMD-like metric is a clever and practical solution to a difficult nested optimization problem. The proposed initialization strategy using the Word Centroid Distance and the batched gradient descent with neighbor filtering further enhance the method’s practicality.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is very well-written and organized. It clearly explains the background, motivates the problem, and details the proposed solution and its technical underpinnings, making the work easy to follow and understand.
*   **Thorough Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, testing the method on eight diverse datasets against 26 strong baselines, including various document representations paired with different metric learning algorithms. This provides convincing evidence of S-WMD's effectiveness.

**Arguments against acceptance (minor weaknesses):**
*   **Analysis of Failure Cases:** The paper notes that S-WMD is occasionally outperformed by unsupervised WMD or other methods. While potential reasons are briefly mentioned, a more thorough analysis of when S-WMD might be suboptimal (e.g., due to dataset characteristics or initialization sensitivity) would strengthen the paper and provide better guidance for practitioners.

Overall, this is a high-quality paper that presents a novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated method for an important problem. The strengths decisively outweigh the minor weaknesses.
