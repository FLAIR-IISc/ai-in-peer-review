- Introduces S-WMD: a supervised extension of WMD that learns a linear transformation of embeddings and word importance weights optimized via leave-one-out nearest neighbor error.  
- Efficient gradient approximation using a Sinkhorn relaxation overcomes WMD’s computational bottleneck, enabling practical training.  
- Builds logically on Kusner et al.’s WMD and metric learning (NCA, LMNN), addressing the limitation of unsupervised WMD.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across eight datasets and 26 baselines shows consistent, strong performance gains.  
- Novel contribution: injecting supervision into WMD for improved document similarity in classification tasks.  
- Technical soundness: the proposed approximation for gradient computation is well-motivated and effective.  
- Efficiency: the method mitigates the high computational complexity of original WMD.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written, though mathematical details could be expanded.  
- Weakness: limited analysis and interpretation of learned word importance weights; lacks illustrative examples.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity despite using Bayesian optimization.  
- Weakness: using squared Euclidean distance in the transport problem may not be optimal for all datasets.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with minor revisions to address weight interpretation and hyperparameter sensitivity.
