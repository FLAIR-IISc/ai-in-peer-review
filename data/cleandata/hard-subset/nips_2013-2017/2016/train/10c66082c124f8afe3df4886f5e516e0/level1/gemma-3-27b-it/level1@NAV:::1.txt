## Review of “Supervised Word Mover’s Distance”

This paper introduces Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a method to incorporate supervision into the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) framework for document similarity. WMD, a recent and powerful approach leveraging word embeddings, was previously limited by its unsupervised nature. The authors address this by learning a linear transformation of the word embedding space and word importance weights, optimized to minimize leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error. A key contribution lies in efficiently approximating the gradient calculation, overcoming the computational bottleneck of the original WMD formulation, and enabling practical training.

The work builds logically on Kusner et al.’s WMD and prior metric learning research (e.g., NCA, LMNN). It correctly identifies the limitations of unsupervised approaches and offers a principled way to inject label information. The relaxation using the Sinkhorn distance for gradient computation is a smart move, effectively addressing a significant computational challenge. The thorough empirical evaluation across eight datasets and comparison against 26 baselines demonstrates the effectiveness of S-WMD, consistently achieving strong performance.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Adapting the WMD to a supervised setting is a valuable contribution, offering a compelling way to improve document similarity for classification tasks.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed approximation for gradient calculation is well-motivated and appears to be effective, enabling practical implementation.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:** The consistent outperformance of a large number of baselines across diverse datasets highlights the effectiveness of the method.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the methodology, although the mathematical details could be expanded upon.
*   **Efficiency:** The authors address and mitigate the computational complexity concerns associated with WMD.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Insight into Weight Interpretation:** While the learned word importance weights are used, there’s limited analysis of *why* certain words are deemed important for specific tasks. Providing examples could strengthen the contribution.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper mentions Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning, but doesn’t delve into the sensitivity of performance to these parameters.
*   **Squared Euclidean Distance:** The choice of using the squared Euclidean distance in the transport problem might not be optimal for all datasets as suggested in the results section.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   S-WMD is a technically sound and novel contribution that addresses a key limitation of WMD.
*   The extensive empirical evaluation demonstrates a clear and consistent performance improvement over strong baselines.
*   The efficient gradient approximation makes the method practical for real-world applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of detailed analysis regarding the learned word weights.
*   The limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.



**Overall:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. The contribution is significant, the technical approach is well-reasoned, and the experimental results are compelling. Addressing the minor weaknesses regarding weight interpretation and hyperparameter sensitivity in a revised version would further strengthen the paper.
