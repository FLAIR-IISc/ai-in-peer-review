- Proposes S-WMD: supervised WMD that learns a linear embedding transform A and nonnegative word-importance vector w, optimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) loss.  
- Uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn OT to obtain unique transport plans and duals, enabling efficient approximate gradients for A and w.  
- Training is sped up via S-WCD initialization (NCA on centroids), mini-batching, and neighbor filtering using the cheap WCD lower bound.  
- Evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, showing best average rank and lowest error on 5/8 datasets with competitive performance elsewhere.  
- Methodology is sound, combining established ideas (NCA, Mahalanobis ground cost learning, Sinkhorn) and correct application of OT gradients.  
- The paper replaces exact WMD with entropic OT for gradients, introducing bias; clarity is needed on whether forward distances use smoothed OT or exact WMD.  
- Claims that approximation bias vanishes as λ→∞ but omits discussion of the associated computational trade-offs and practical λ choices.  
- The w reweighting requires w≥0, but the paper does not specify constraints or parameterization to enforce nonnegativity.  
- No explicit regularization on A or w is reported, raising potential identifiability and overfitting concerns, especially on small datasets.  
- Complexity analysis matches Sinkhorn costs and gradient assembly; neighbor filtering via WCD is a practical acceleration.  
- Experimental setup is broad and tuned via Bayesian optimization, but lacks ablations (A-only vs w-only, initialization effects, λ and neighborhood sensitivity).  
- Results are strong but S-WMD is not universally best (e.g., BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON), and test-time distance computation (exact vs smoothed) is unclear.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, but has notational overload (p used twice), a section-numbering typo, and minor dimensional ambiguities.  
- Originality is incremental: combines known components (WMD, Sinkhorn, NCA) but novel in jointly learning ground metric and per-word reweighting for text.  
- Significance is practical: adapts pretrained embeddings to supervised tasks, fits kNN pipelines, and is likely useful where labels exist but deep models are unnecessary.  
- Pros: clear supervised OT formulation, efficient Sinkhorn-based training, broad empirical comparison, and public code.  
- Cons: ambiguity about forward vs smoothed distances, missing constraints/regularization for A and w, limited ablations, and minor reproducibility issues (k, r, positivity).  
- Suggestions: clarify whether forward distances use entropic OT, report λ effects, enforce w≥0 (e.g., softplus or projection), add l1/l2 regularization, include A-only/w-only and initialization ablations, and fix notation/typos.  
- Overall: solid, well-motivated supervised extension of WMD with efficient training and strong empirical results; suitable for NeurIPS after clarifications and minor additions.
