- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform A and a word-importance vector w.  
- Replaces intractable WMD gradients by using the entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance, enabling efficient, differentiable optimization.  
- Uses practical optimization heuristics: WCD-based initialization via NCA and batch updates limited to local neighborhoods for speed.  
- Evaluated on eight diverse text classification datasets and compared to 26 baselines, achieving state-of-the-art performance on most and best average rank.  
- High significance and originality: addresses WMD’s unsupervised limitation by jointly learning A and w tailored to tasks.  
- Technical quality: elegant, sound use of Sinkhorn distances with valid gradient derivations and effective optimization strategies.  
- Empirical strength: comprehensive, convincing experiments against many strong baselines and metric-learning variants.  
- Presentation: clear, well-organized paper with helpful background, visualizations, and reproducible details.  
- Missing ablation study to separate the contributions of A versus w and understand their interaction.  
- Limited discussion of the ground metric choice: reliance on squared L2 may be suboptimal and extension to other distances is unclear.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity not thoroughly explored—λ fixed to 10 without a sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, well-executed contribution with minor analyses missing; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
