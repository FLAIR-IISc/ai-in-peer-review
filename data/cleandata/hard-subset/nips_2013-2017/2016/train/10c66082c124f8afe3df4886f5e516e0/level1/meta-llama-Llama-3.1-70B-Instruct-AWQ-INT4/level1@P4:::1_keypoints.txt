- Proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word importance vector to supervise WMD distances.  
- Builds on Word Mover’s Distance by incorporating label supervision to align distances with semantic similarity.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear, concise writing and well-defined mathematical notation.  
- Provides a detailed description of the optimization process and computational complexity.  
- Evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks and outperforms nearly all of 26 competitive baselines.  
- Visualization of learned embeddings demonstrates meaningful semantic relationships captured by the method.  
- Strength: novel supervised document distance metric with thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Strength: wide-ranging baseline comparisons across multiple datasets.  
- Weakness: assumes reader familiarity with WMD; background on WMD is brief.  
- Weakness: optimization and computational complexity explanation could be more intuitive for non-experts.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of results and insights beyond embedding visualizations.  
- Originality: novel and innovative combination of WMD with supervision for metric learning.  
- Significance: potential impact for text classification, information retrieval, sentiment analysis, and topic modeling.  
- Quality: overall high—well-written, clear notation, extensive evaluations.  
- Clarity: generally clear presentation of background, related work, and methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient WMD background, need for clearer complexity intuition, and deeper result analysis.  
- Overall: strong candidate for acceptance with some areas for improvement.
