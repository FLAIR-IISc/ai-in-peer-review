- Proposes S-WMD, a supervised variant of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights via leave-one-out NN classification loss.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance and a probabilistic filtering technique to address WMD’s computational challenges and enable efficient gradient computation.  
- Novelty lies in applying metric learning directly in the high-dimensional word embedding space without dimensionality reduction, combined with learned word weights.  
- Builds on prior work (WMD, metric learning, supervised EMD) but adapts and extends these ideas specifically for document distances.  
- Strong empirical performance: outperforms 26 baselines across eight real-world text classification tasks.  
- Technical contributions are sound: gradient derivations, optimization procedure, and use of Sinkhorn distance are well-motivated and correctly implemented.  
- Paper is clearly written with well-outlined algorithmic steps and adequate discussion of motivation and related work.  
- Demonstrates practical efficiency and reports training-time comparisons (Table 3) supporting real-world applicability.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters is insufficiently explored despite tuning with Bayesian optimization.  
- Choice of squared Euclidean distance in the transport cost is not rigorously justified; alternative metrics were not investigated.  
- Error analysis is limited; paper lacks detailed examination of failure cases and types of instances where S-WMD underperforms.  
- Could better situate S-WMD within broader metric learning literature, clarifying differences and advantages over existing methods beyond NCA.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound method with strong empirical gains and practical efficiency.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor issues with hyperparameter sensitivity, limited error analysis, and weaker discussion of connections to prior metric learning work.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution to supervised document distance learning; recommended for acceptance.
