Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Supervised Word Mover’s Distance (S-WMD), a supervised variant of the Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) [19] for document comparison. WMD measures document distance via optimal transport over pretrained word embeddings. S-WMD incorporates supervision by learning (i) a linear transformation A of the embedding space (ground cost) and (ii) a nonnegative word-importance vector w that reweights document histograms before transport. The objective minimizes a stochastic leave-one-out kNN loss (as in NCA [15]) where distances are computed via WMD with the transformed ground cost and reweighted histograms. Because exact WMD gradients are expensive and may be nondifferentiable, the paper adopts the entropy-regularized Sinkhorn transport [6] to obtain a unique transport T*λ and dual potentials, which are used to form efficient approximate gradients for A and w. Training is further sped up with a good initialization (NCA on word centroids, S-WCD), mini-batching, and restricting the NCA neighborhood to the closest candidates under the cheap WCD lower bound.

Experiments on eight datasets compare S-WMD to 26 baselines (unsupervised and supervised, including BOW/TF-IDF/LSI/LDA/mSDA, WCD/WMD, and metric learning with NCA/ITML/LMNN). S-WMD achieves the best average rank and the lowest error on 5/8 datasets, with competitive performance on the remainder. The authors report training times and qualitative visualizations (t-SNE) indicating improved clustering.

Quality (soundness, correctness, evaluation)
- Methodology: The learning setup is sound and builds on established ideas: NCA-style loss for kNN, learning a Mahalanobis-like ground cost for EMD/OT, and using Sinkhorn for tractable computation. The gradient w.r.t. A using the transport plan, and the w-gradient via dual potentials, are standard in OT; using Sinkhorn to ensure uniqueness and differentiability is appropriate.
- Approximation: The paper replaces the exact WMD plan and dual variables with their entropic counterparts for gradient computation. This introduces bias relative to the unregularized DA,w used in the loss. The paper claims the approximation can be made arbitrarily close as λ→∞, but computational trade-offs remain. It would help to clarify whether the forward distances in the NCA probabilities are also computed with the smoothed OT, or exact WMD; as written, the loss uses DA,w from Eq. (5) but gradients use T*λ and α*λ,β*λ.
- Constraints and regularization: The reweighted histogram d̃a=(w◦da)/(w>da) implies w≥0 to maintain a valid distribution; explicit constraints or parameterization ensuring nonnegativity are not specified. There is also no stated regularization on A (e.g., Frobenius norm, low-rank) or w (e.g., l1/l2), which is atypical for NCA-style training and may risk overfitting, especially on small datasets. Empirically, generalization appears reasonable, but explicit regularization and an ablation would strengthen the case.
- Complexity: The stated complexity O(TBN[p2 + d2(p + r)]) is consistent with Sinkhorn’s O(p2) and the cost of assembling gradients. The neighbor filtering via WCD is a practical and justified acceleration.
- Experiments: Comparisons are broad and hyperparameters are tuned via Bayesian optimization. Including both unsupervised and supervised baselines is fair. Results show consistent gains, though S-WMD is not always best (e.g., BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON). An ablation (A-only vs w-only vs both; effect of initialization; λ, M, N sensitivity) would be valuable. Clarify the choice of k in kNN and whether distances at test time are computed with exact WMD or Sinkhorn.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized, with helpful background. Notational issues to fix:
- The symbol p is used for both the norm exponent and the document sparsity; avoid overloading.
- Section numbering typo (“.2 Optimization”).
- Clearly state constraints on w (nonnegativity) and whether A is full or low-rank. Indicate regularization (if any).
- Specify whether the forward pass uses smoothed OT, and the exact λ used in all experiments.
- Minor dimensional ambiguities in the expressions for α*λ,β*λ derived from u,v should be clarified.

Originality
The work combines known components—WMD [19], Sinkhorn OT [6], and NCA [15]—in a novel way to learn a supervised ground metric and histogram reweighting for document distances. Prior EMD metric learning in vision (e.g., [35]) learned ground metrics but did not consider document-specific reweighting of word histograms. Applying these ideas to text with pretrained embeddings and demonstrating consistent empirical gains is a meaningful incremental advance.

Significance
The approach is practically relevant: it leverages large unsupervised embeddings while adapting to task-specific similarity, fits within kNN pipelines, and shows strong empirical performance across diverse datasets with competitive training times. The contribution is likely to be used and extended, especially in settings where label information is available but full supervised deep models are unnecessary or data-limited.

Pros
- Clear formulation of supervised OT-based document distance with both ground metric learning and word-importance reweighting.
- Efficient training via Sinkhorn-based gradients, good initialization, and neighbor filtering.
- Broad, carefully tuned empirical comparison; consistent improvements and state-of-the-art results on multiple datasets.
- Public code release.

Cons
- Ambiguity about whether the training objective uses exact or smoothed distances; potential mismatch between forward loss and gradient approximation.
- Lack of explicit constraints/regularization for w and A; potential identifiability and overfitting concerns.
- Limited ablations; unclear contribution of A vs w individually, and sensitivity to λ and neighborhood truncation.
- Minor notational and editorial issues that hinder reproducibility (e.g., kNN k, rank r, positivity constraints).

Suggestions and questions for authors
- Clarify the forward distance used in Eq. (6): exact WMD or entropic OT? Report the effect of λ on accuracy, speed, and convergence.
- Specify and enforce w≥0 (e.g., parameterize w=softplus(v) or project onto the nonnegative orthant) and consider l1/l2 regularization on w and A; report ablations.
- Report A-only and w-only variants, and the gain from S-WCD initialization vs random.
- Provide k for kNN, r for A, and test-time distance computation details.
- Resolve notation overload and fix section/typo issues; include a short derivation of α*λ,β*λ from u,v for completeness.

Overall assessment
A solid, well-motivated, and effective supervised extension of WMD with efficient training and strong empirical results. With clarifications and minor additions, the paper meets NeurIPS standards on quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
