- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learns an affine transform and word importance weights to align WMD with class labels.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance and an efficient gradient update to address computational challenges of learning the metric.  
- Evaluated on 8 text classification datasets and compared against 26 baselines, showing consistent empirical gains.  
- Technically sound with a rigorous gradient derivation and comprehensive experimental design.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations on BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, and AMAZON and provide plausible explanations.  
- Implementation details and released code enhance reproducibility.  
- Well-organized and clearly written; mathematical notation consistent and t-SNE visualizations effective.  
- Gradient derivation (Section 3.1) may be difficult for non-expert readers.  
- Related work section is long and should better highlight novelty relative to Wang et al. (2016).  
- The supervised combination of WMD and metric learning is novel; distinguishes from prior EMD metric learning by histogram re-weighting.  
- Use of Sinkhorn approximation is not novel but is applied effectively to enable learning.  
- Significant empirical impact: lowest error on 5/8 datasets and best average performance across baselines.  
- Method is efficient in training time and broadly applicable across domains.  
- Strengths: clear motivation, rigorous derivation, comprehensive evaluation, efficient implementation, effective visualizations, code availability.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient analysis of poor-performing datasets, weak motivation for initialization strategy (S-WCD), unclear distinction from Wang et al., and unclear significance of word importance weights.  
- Arguments for acceptance: meaningful extension of WMD, theoretically sound and practical, strong empirical gains, reproducible implementation, high adoption potential.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, limited explanation for dataset performance variability, and insufficient emphasis of unique contributions versus related work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty vs. related work and better explain performance variations.
