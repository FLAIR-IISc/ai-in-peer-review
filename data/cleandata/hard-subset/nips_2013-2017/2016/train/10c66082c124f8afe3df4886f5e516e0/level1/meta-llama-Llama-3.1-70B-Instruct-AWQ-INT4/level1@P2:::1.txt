This paper proposes a supervised version of the Word Mover's Distance (WMD), called the Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), which learns a linear transformation of the word representations and a word importance vector to improve the document distance. The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method.

The main idea of the paper is to leverage the strengths of the WMD, which has been shown to be effective in document classification tasks, and incorporate supervision to further improve its performance. The authors propose to learn a linear transformation of the word representations and a word importance vector, which are learned to make the WMD distances match the semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a detailed description of the optimization process and the computational complexity of the algorithm. The authors also provide an extensive evaluation of the proposed method on eight real-world text classification tasks, comparing it to 26 competitive baselines.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The proposed method is well-motivated and builds upon the strengths of the WMD.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including a detailed description of the optimization process and the computational complexity of the algorithm.
* The evaluation of the proposed method is extensive and compares it to a large number of competitive baselines.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The paper assumes that the word embeddings are given and does not provide a method for learning the word embeddings from scratch.
* The paper does not provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed method, such as a proof of convergence or a bound on the error.

Overall, the paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method. The evaluation of the proposed method is extensive and compares it to a large number of competitive baselines. However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough theoretical analysis and a method for learning the word embeddings from scratch.
