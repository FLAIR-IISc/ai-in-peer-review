- Summary: Proposes S-WMD, a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance that learns a linear transform and word importance vector; outperforms 26 baselines on eight text classification tasks.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on WMD/WCD and linear metric learning (e.g., NCA); distinct in jointly learning a transform and word importance.
- Strength — performance: Achieves state-of-the-art results across eight text classification datasets.
- Strength — efficiency: Uses Sinkhorn approximation to speed WMD from O(q^3 log q) to O(q^2).
- Strength — supervision: Learns a task-specific document distance via supervised training.
- Strength — flexibility: Applicable across varied text classification tasks and datasets.
- Weakness — complexity: Multiple components (transform, importance vector, Sinkhorn) make implementation and optimization challenging.
- Weakness — hyperparameter tuning: Relies on Bayesian optimization, which may be impractical for some users.
- Weakness — interpretability: Learned distance metric is complex and hard to interpret.
- Quality: Technically sound with theoretical analysis, thorough experiments, and reproducible algorithm description.
- Clarity: Well-written, organized, and clearly explained with appropriate notation and experimental details.
- Originality: Novel combination of metric learning and word embeddings to create a supervised WMD.
- Significance: Potentially impactful for NLP/text classification due to task-specific distances and computational gains.
- Pro acceptance: Strong empirical results, efficiency, flexibility, and supervised metric learning.
- Con acceptance: Algorithmic complexity, tuning burden, and limited interpretability.
- Recommendation: Accept for publication.
