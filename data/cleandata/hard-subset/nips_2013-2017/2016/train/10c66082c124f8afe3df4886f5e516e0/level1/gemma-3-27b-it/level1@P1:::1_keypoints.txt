- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised metric learning method that learns a linear transform of word embeddings and word importance weights on top of Word Mover’s Distance.  
- Optimizes leave-one-out nearest-neighbor classification error to adapt embeddings for a target task.  
- Uses Sinkhorn distance (relaxed optimal transport) and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD computationally tractable.  
- Demonstrates consistently strong empirical performance, outperforming 26 baselines across eight text classification datasets.  
- Novelty: adapts pre-trained word embeddings for supervised document distance rather than learning document representations from scratch.  
- Leverages metric learning ideas in a lower-dimensional embedding space to address scalability for text.  
- Clear motivation, algorithm description, and mathematical derivations.  
- Thorough evaluation with many competitive baselines and public code for reproducibility.  
- Limited theoretical analysis — lacks convergence guarantees and generalization bounds.  
- Potential sensitivity to hyperparameters; more analysis of hyperparameter impact would help.  
- Dependence on initialization is acknowledged but not fully investigated; WCD initialization needs stronger justification.  
- Choice of squared Euclidean distance in WMD is potentially suboptimal and not rigorously explored.  
- Insufficient analysis of failure cases where S-WMD performs poorly.  
- Overall judgment: strong, novel, and efficient contribution with compelling empirical results; recommended for acceptance despite theoretical and sensitivity concerns.
