**Summary**

The paper proposes a supervised word mover's distance (S-WMD) metric, which extends the word mover's distance (WMD) by incorporating supervision. The S-WMD metric learns a linear transformation of the word embedding space and a word importance vector to capture the semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels. The authors evaluate S-WMD on eight real-world text classification tasks and show that it consistently outperforms 26 competitive baselines.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon the WMD metric, which was introduced by Kusner et al. [19]. The WMD metric computes the distance between two documents as the minimum cumulative word distance of moving one document to another in the word embedding space. However, the WMD metric is entirely unsupervised and lacks a mechanism to incorporate supervision when available. The S-WMD metric addresses this limitation by learning a supervised metric that captures the semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels.

The paper also relates to the field of metric learning, which has been extensively studied in the literature. The authors mention several popular metric learning algorithms, including Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA) [15], Large Margin Nearest Neighbors (LMNN) [36], and Information-Theoretic Metric Learning (ITML) [10]. However, these algorithms are designed for learning a generalized Euclidean metric, whereas the S-WMD metric learns a supervised metric within the word embedding space.

**Strengths**

1. **Effective use of supervision**: The S-WMD metric effectively incorporates supervision to capture the semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels.
2. **Efficient optimization**: The authors propose an efficient technique to learn the S-WMD metric, which exploits a relaxed version of the underlying optimal transport problem.
3. **State-of-the-art results**: The S-WMD metric achieves state-of-the-art results on eight real-world text classification tasks.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Computational complexity**: The S-WMD metric requires solving a regularized transport problem, which can be computationally expensive.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors use Bayesian optimization to tune the hyperparameters, but this can be time-consuming and may not always lead to optimal results.
3. **Limited interpretability**: The S-WMD metric learns a complex transformation of the word embedding space, which can be difficult to interpret.

**Originality**

The S-WMD metric is a novel combination of familiar techniques, including the WMD metric and metric learning algorithms. The authors' contribution lies in extending the WMD metric to incorporate supervision and proposing an efficient technique to learn the supervised metric.

**Significance**

The S-WMD metric has significant implications for text classification tasks, as it achieves state-of-the-art results on eight real-world datasets. The metric can be used in various applications, including web-search ranking, book recommendation, and news categorization.
