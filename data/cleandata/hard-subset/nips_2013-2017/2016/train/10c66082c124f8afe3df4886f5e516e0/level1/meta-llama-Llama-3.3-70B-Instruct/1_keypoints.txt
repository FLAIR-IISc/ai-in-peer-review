- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), a supervised extension of Word Mover's Distance for document similarity.
- Motivates S-WMD by noting WMD's limitation of being unsupervised and not task/label-aware.
- Learns a linear transformation of word embeddings plus a word importance vector to re-weight word frequencies.
- Trains S-WMD by minimizing stochastic leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error at the document level.
- Provides full derivation of loss, gradients, and optimization algorithm for the model.
- Introduces an efficient gradient approximation via a relaxed optimal transport formulation, reducing complexity from O(q^3 log q) to O(q^2).
- Evaluates on eight real-world text classification tasks against 26 baselines.
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art performance on most datasets and significant gains over unsupervised WMD.
- Paper is well-written with clear mathematical and algorithmic exposition.
- Strength: novel supervised metric addressing WMD's limitations.
- Strength: principled training objective (leave-one-out NN) and thorough derivations.
- Strength: strong empirical results across multiple datasets and baselines.
- Weakness: assumes fixed pre-trained word embeddings rather than joint learning.
- Weakness: substantial computational/resource demands for large datasets due to optimal transport.
- Weakness: lacks in-depth analysis of the learned word importance weights and their impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, SOTA results, detailed derivations and experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on fixed embeddings, computational cost, missing analysis of learned weights.
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address embedding assumption, computational complexity, and analyze learned weights.
