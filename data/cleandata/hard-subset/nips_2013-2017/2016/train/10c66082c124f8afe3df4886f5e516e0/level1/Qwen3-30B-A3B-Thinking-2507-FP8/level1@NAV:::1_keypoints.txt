- Proposal of S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns an affine transform of embeddings and word importance weights for document classification.  
- Optimization: minimizes leave-one-out kNN classification error using a learned distance.  
- Efficient gradient: uses Sinkhorn distance to approximate gradients, avoiding cubic WMD complexity.  
- Empirical results: consistently outperforms 26 baselines across 8 text classification tasks.  
- Theoretical/technical lineage: builds on NCA and optimal transport literature, extending EMD/WMD to a supervised setting.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: paper is well-written with sufficient implementation detail.  
- Strength: practical and efficient gradient approximation enabling scalability.  
- Strength: strong, consistent empirical improvements over unsupervised baselines.  
- Significance: fills an important gap by incorporating task supervision into WMD, with broad applicability.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical justification for the Sinkhorn-based gradient approximation beyond empirical speed.  
- Weakness: lacks explanation for underperformance on three datasets (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON).  
- Weakness: insufficient differentiation from related EMD-based metric learning work (Wang et al., ECCV 2015).  
- Overall judgment: technically sound, original, significant, and recommended for acceptance with minor theoretical concerns.
