- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and a word-importance vector to improve document distances.  
- Well-written paper with clear explanation of method.  
- Motivation: leverages WMD strengths and uses supervision to align distances with label-encoded semantic similarity.  
- Describes optimization procedure and analyzes computational complexity in detail.  
- Extensive evaluation on eight real-world text classification tasks, compared to 26 competitive baselines.  
- Empirical results show S-WMD outperforms many baselines, indicating strong practical significance.  
- Strength: method is well-motivated and builds on proven WMD foundations.  
- Strength: thorough methodological analysis and clear presentation.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation against many baselines.  
- Weakness: assumes pre-existing word embeddings; does not learn embeddings from scratch.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis (no convergence proof or error bounds).  
- Ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: effective, well-analyzed method with extensive comparisons.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing embedding-learning method and theoretical guarantees.
