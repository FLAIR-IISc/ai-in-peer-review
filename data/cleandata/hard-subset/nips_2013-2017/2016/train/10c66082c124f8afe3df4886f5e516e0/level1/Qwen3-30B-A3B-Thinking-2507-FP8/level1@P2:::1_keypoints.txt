- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD) that learns a linear transform of embeddings and word importance weights to improve document classification.
- Builds on Kusner et al.'s WMD and minimizes leave-one-out classification error via learned parameters.
- Introduces an efficient gradient approximation using Sinkhorn distance to avoid cubic-time exact WMD computation.
- Paper is technically sound with clear mathematical formulation and derived gradients for optimization.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough: 8 diverse datasets and 26 baselines (unsupervised and supervised).
- Empirical results show S-WMD achieves lowest test error on 5/8 datasets and outperforms baselines on average.
- Computational efficiency improved (approx. O(q^2) vs O(q^3 log q) for exact WMD), making it practical.
- Authors acknowledge and analyze limitations (e.g., S-WMD underperforms WMD on CLASSIC, possibly due to initialization).
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; pseudocode and visualizations aid reproducibility.
- Minor clarity issue: abrupt transition to gradient approximation; needs an intuitive justification for Sinkhorn sufficiency.
- Originality: novel application of supervised metric learning within WMD and introduction of word importance weights.
- Significance: demonstrates practical value, especially for short-document classification using pre-trained embeddings.
- Strengths include methodological novelty, Sinkhorn use, comprehensive experiments, theoretical grounding, and code availability.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of semantic interpretation of word importance weights, insufficient justification for S-WCD initialization, and lack of exploration of different embedding models.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept with high confidence; significant advancement in document distance metrics.
