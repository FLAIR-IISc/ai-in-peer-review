- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised variant of Word Mover's Distance that learns an affine transformation of embeddings and word-importance weights via minimizing stochastic leave-one-out nearest neighbor error.
- Uses a Sinkhorn-relaxed optimal transport objective to enable efficient gradient computation and scalable optimization.
- Demonstrates S-WMD outperforms 26 baselines across eight real-world text classification tasks, achieving state-of-the-art on most datasets.
- Strength: technically sound and well-motivated, with a clever use of Sinkhorn distance to address WMD’s optimization challenges.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with mathematical details, pseudo-code, initialization strategies, and batch gradient descent for reproducibility.
- Strength: novel combination of supervised metric learning and optimal transport by jointly learning linear embedding transforms and word-specific importance weights.
- Strength: significant practical utility—consistent improvements, efficiency, and scalability for text classification.
- Weakness: sensitive to initialization, causing suboptimal performance on some datasets (e.g., CLASSIC).
- Weakness: underperforms on datasets with longer documents (e.g., BBCSPORT, OHSUMED), suggesting limited generalization to all text types.
- Weakness: lacks in-depth analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., Sinkhorn λ, batch size).
- For acceptance: addresses a key limitation of WMD, presents compelling empirical results, and provides reproducible code.
- Against acceptance: reliance on good initialization and variable cross-dataset performance; needs more thorough hyperparameter tuning analysis.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address initialization robustness and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.
