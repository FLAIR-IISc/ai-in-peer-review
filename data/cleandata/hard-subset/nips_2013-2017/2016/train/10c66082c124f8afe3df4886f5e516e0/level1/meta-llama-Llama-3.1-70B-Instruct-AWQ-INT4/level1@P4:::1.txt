The paper "Supervised Word Mover's Distance" proposes a novel method for learning a supervised document distance metric. The authors build upon the recently introduced Word Mover's Distance (WMD) and incorporate supervision into the metric learning process. The proposed method, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD), learns a linear transformation of the word representations and a word importance vector to make the WMD distances match the semantic meaning of similarity encoded in the labels.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the background, related work, and the proposed method. The writing is concise, and the mathematical notation is well-defined. The authors also provide a detailed description of the optimization process and the computational complexity of the algorithm.

The proposed method is evaluated on eight real-world text classification tasks, and the results show that S-WMD consistently outperforms almost all of the 26 competitive baselines. The authors also provide a visualization of the learned word embeddings and demonstrate that the method can capture meaningful semantic relationships between words.
