- Proposes S-WMD: jointly learn a linear embedding transform A and a nonnegative word-importance vector w to make WMD supervised for kNN classification.  
- Trains by minimizing a stochastic leave-one-out kNN objective using the NCA loss with document distances computed by an entropically regularized OT (Sinkhorn) approximation.  
- Uses Sinkhorn to obtain efficient, differentiable WMD-like distances; gradients for A use the optimal plan and for w use dual potentials from Sinkhorn scaling.  
- Trains with mini-batches, neighbor subsampling via a cheap WCD filter, and initializes from NCA on word centroids (S-WCD).  
- Experiments on eight datasets against 26 baselines show S-WMD achieves best kNN error on 5/8 datasets and best average rank; code is released.  
- Builds on WMD, entropic OT/Sinkhorn, and supervised metric learning, and is related to prior ground-metric learning work in vision but novel in jointly learning histogram weights for text.  
- Strength: clear and well-motivated extension that brings supervision into WMD while preserving semantic OT alignment.  
- Strength: sound optimization — entropic OT yields efficient, smooth approximations and gradient derivations via envelope/duality are appropriate.  
- Strength: compact parameterization — learning A in embedding space is tractable, and w provides task-specific salience.  
- Strength: strong empirical results with broad, carefully tuned baseline coverage and reproducible code.  
- Strength: sensible S-WCD initialization that aligns with WMD lower bounds and is empirically effective.  
- Weakness: constraints/regularization on w are not clearly specified; nonnegativity enforcement and regularization (L1/L2, sparsity) should be reported and ablated.  
- Weakness: choice of squared Euclidean ground cost may overweight outliers; need ablation between ℓ2 and ℓ2-squared or consider unsquared Mahalanobis cost.  
- Weakness: evaluation fairness — relying solely on kNN ignores that many document representations are typically paired with linear classifiers; include standard linear classifier baselines (TF-IDF, mSDA).  
- Weakness: hyperparameter sensitivity underexplored — λ, batch/neighborhood sizes, rank r of A, learning rates, and stopping criteria need sensitivity analyses.  
- Weakness: notation and clarity issues (overloaded symbols, minor typos); clarify dimensions, dual potential notation, and envelope theorem justification.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns for long documents due to O(p^2) pairwise complexity; report runtimes and discuss scaling strategies (vocab pruning, stochastic OT, low-rank kernels).  
- Weakness: missing some contemporary supervised text baselines (doc2vec, fastText); include simple bag-of-n-grams + linear models for context.  
- Quality: methodology is technically sound; Sinkhorn approximation and gradient derivations are principled, but more details on constraints/regularization and sensitivity needed.  
- Clarity: generally clear and organized, but fix notation inconsistencies and explicitly state constraints/hyperparameters for reproducibility.  
- Originality: moderate — learning ground metrics for OT exists, but the joint learning of A and w with NCA+Sinkhorn for text is a novel and useful combination.  
- Significance: high for distance-based text retrieval/classification and OT in NLP; practical impact would be strengthened by comparisons to standard supervised classifiers.  
- Questions for authors: are w constrained nonnegative and how enforced/regularized? what rank r is used and do low-rank constraints help? sensitivity to λ and neighbor size N? ablation of A-only vs w-only vs both? handling of OOV words at test time?  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a clear gap, shows strong empirical gains with extensive baselines, and provides open-source code likely to be adopted.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited analysis of constraints/regularization and sensitivity, evaluation restricted to kNN without standard supervised baselines, and some clarity/notation issues.  
- Recommendation: solid, timely contribution recommended for acceptance after addressing clarity and experimental concerns.
