- Proposes Supervised WMD (S-WMD): learn linear embedding transform A and nonnegative word-importance vector w to supervise Word Mover’s Distance.  
- Trains metric with a leave-one-out kNN objective inspired by NCA, using entropic-regularized OT (Sinkhorn) for differentiable, efficient gradients.  
- Uses WCD-based initialization and neighbor pruning to speed training; reports O(q^2) per pair complexity (q = unique words per doc).  
- Empirically evaluated on eight datasets against 26 baselines, achieving best average rank and top performance on most datasets.  
- Novelty: first supervised ground-metric + histogram reweighting applied to WMD in NLP, leveraging Sinkhorn duals for gradients.  
- Technical soundness: entropic OT surrogate and gradient derivations are appropriate and practical.  
- Concern: mismatch between training objective (regularized OT) and claimed S-WMD; unclear whether evaluation uses exact WMD or surrogate.  
- Lack of theoretical guarantees on bias introduced by finite entropic regularization (λ) and its impact on kNN accuracy.  
- Efficiency: training is feasible with provided tricks, but quadratic dependence on per-document vocab may limit very long-document scalability.  
- Empirical support is strong with careful tuning, but ablations only partially disentangle contributions (A vs. w).  
- Missing sensitivity analyses for λ, neighbor-set size, and initialization robustness.  
- Potential overfitting risk due to absent explicit regularization/constraints on A and w; w’s nonnegativity and any regularizers are not clearly enforced or discussed.  
- Clarity: generally well written with clear derivations, but has minor notation issues, typos, and unclear test-time metric/constraints on w.  
- Originality: solid adaptation of supervised EMD concepts to word-embedding WMD with novel training/gradient scheme.  
- Significance: practical, interpretable, general method likely useful for retrieval/classification, but lacks comparisons to strong supervised document models (doc2vec, fastText, CNN/RNN).  
- Pros: clear formulation, efficient Sinkhorn-based training, code released, strong empirical gains, interpretable word weights.  
- Cons: objective/evaluation mismatch, missing regularization, absent comparisons to major supervised baselines, limited ablation/sensitivity studies, scalability concerns.  
- Suggestions: clarify test-time metric and report λ sensitivity; enforce and ablate constraints/regularizers on A and w; disentangle A vs. w contributions; add comparisons to doc2vec/fastText/CNNs; discuss memory/time scaling and sparse Sinkhorn variants.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and practically significant work with some missing analyses—leaning toward acceptance.
