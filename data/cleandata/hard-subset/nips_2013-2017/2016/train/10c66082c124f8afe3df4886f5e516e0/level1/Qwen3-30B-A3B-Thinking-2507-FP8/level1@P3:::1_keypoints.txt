- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that learns a linear embedding transform and word importance weights to improve document classification.
- Uses a loss that minimizes stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification error to train the metric.
- Addresses nested optimization with a Sinkhorn distance approximation, reducing transport complexity from O(q³ log q) to O(q²).
- Demonstrates comprehensive experiments on 8 datasets (news, sentiment, product) against 26 baselines.
- Empirical results: S-WMD yields the lowest kNN error on 5/8 datasets and outperforms all methods on average.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, with precise algorithm description and a compelling visualization showing improved clustering.
- Authors acknowledge limitations and discuss three datasets (BBCSPORT, CLASSIC, AMAZON) where S-WMD underperforms.
- Strength: novel supervised extension of WMD with practical efficiency and solid empirical validation.
- Strength: effective computational solution (Sinkhorn relaxation) with clear justification.
- Weakness: insufficient empirical analysis of failure cases on BBCSPORT and AMAZON; CLASSIC explanation (initialization) is unverified.
- Weakness: initialization method (S-WCD) is described but not rigorously compared to alternatives.
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis/bounds on the approximation error introduced by the Sinkhorn relaxation.
- Weakness: no comparison with more recent metric learning methods post-WMD to contextualize improvements.
- Overall verdict: significant, well-executed contribution with recommendation for acceptance, but would benefit from deeper failure-case analysis, stronger theory, and broader comparisons.
