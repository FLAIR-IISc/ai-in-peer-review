**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a supervised technique to learn a document distance metric, called Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD). The method builds upon the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) algorithm, which leverages word embeddings to compute distances between documents. The S-WMD technique learns a linear transformation of the word representations and a word importance vector to incorporate supervision into the WMD. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of S-WMD on eight real-world text classification tasks, outperforming 26 competitive baselines.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper relates to previous work on document distance metrics, such as the Word Mover's Distance (WMD) and the Word Centroid Distance (WCD). The authors also draw inspiration from linear metric learning algorithms, such as Neighborhood Components Analysis (NCA). The S-WMD technique is distinct from previous work in that it learns a supervised metric that incorporates both a linear transformation of the word representations and a word importance vector.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved performance**: S-WMD achieves state-of-the-art results on eight text classification tasks, outperforming 26 competitive baselines.
2. **Efficient computation**: The authors propose a fast approximation of the WMD computation using the Sinkhorn distance, which reduces the time complexity from O(q^3 log q) to O(q^2).
3. **Supervised learning**: S-WMD incorporates supervision into the WMD, allowing the model to learn a task-specific document distance metric.
4. **Flexibility**: The technique can be applied to various text classification tasks and datasets.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Complexity**: The S-WMD algorithm involves multiple components, including the linear transformation of word representations, the word importance vector, and the Sinkhorn distance approximation. This complexity may make it challenging to implement and optimize the algorithm.
2. **Hyperparameter tuning**: The authors use Bayesian optimization to tune the hyperparameters, which may not be feasible for all users.
3. **Limited interpretability**: The S-WMD technique is a complex algorithm, and it may be challenging to interpret the learned document distance metric.
