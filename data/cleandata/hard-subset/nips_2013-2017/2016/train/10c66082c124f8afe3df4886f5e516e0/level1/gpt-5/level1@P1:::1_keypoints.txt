- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised extension of Word Mover’s Distance that jointly learns a linear embedding transform A and nonnegative word-importance weights w under a stochastic leave-one-out kNN (NCA-style) objective.  
- Uses entropy-regularized optimal transport (Sinkhorn) to obtain differentiable, efficient approximations of WMD and derive gradients via dual potentials/envelope theorem.  
- Initializes A via NCA on Word Centroid Distance (S-WCD) and trains with mini-batch SGD plus WCD-based neighborhood filtering for scalability.  
- Empirical evaluation: strong, consistent gains on eight text classification datasets against 26 baselines including WMD/WCD, classical text models, and metric learning methods.  
- Methodological strengths: well-motivated formulation, sensible use of Sinkhorn for differentiability, and thoughtful computational strategies to make training practical.  
- Empirical strengths: broad evaluation, Bayesian hyperparameter tuning, public code, interpretable learned word weights, and improved class clustering visualizations.  
- Main technical concern: no quantification or sensitivity analysis of the bias introduced by Sinkhorn regularization parameter λ and lack of discussion on convergence to unregularized WMD.  
- Under-specified constraints/regularization: paper does not state how nonnegativity of w is enforced or whether A and w receive regularization to prevent overfitting or extreme scaling.  
- Evaluation limited to kNN: unclear if learned A and w transfer to downstream classifiers or retrieval tasks (e.g., linear SVM or other models).  
- Comparative gaps: missing experimental comparisons to strong supervised neural baselines (CNNs, doc2vec, fastText) and no empirical comparison to related supervised ground-metric learning works.  
- Some derivational and notational details are terse or unclear (use of envelope theorem, gradients w.r.t. w, uniqueness/stability of dual potentials, overloaded notation).  
- Clarity issues: minor typographical/notation errors, missing subsection numbering, and omission of learned embedding dimensionality r and explicit regularizers; ablation studies (A-only, w-only) are absent.  
- Originality: novel combination of supervised ground-metric learning in embedding space with joint histogram reweighting, applied end-to-end with Sinkhorn in NLP/WMD context.  
- Significance: practical, interpretable supervised variant of WMD with consistent empirical gains and likely adoption potential, though placement relative to neural classifiers is not fully established.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear motivation, efficient training, strong empirical results, interpretability, public code, and practical impact.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical analysis of approximation gap, under-specified constraints/regularization, evaluation focused on kNN, missing neural baselines, and absent ablations.  
- Recommendation: lean accept, with suggested revisions to add sensitivity analyses, clarify constraints/regularization, include ablations, and broaden comparisons.
