- Proposes S-WMD: learns an affine transform of embeddings and word importance weights to supervise Word Mover’s Distance for document similarity.
- Uses Sinkhorn (relaxed optimal transport) and probabilistic filtering to make supervised WMD computationally tractable.
- Optimizes parameters by minimizing leave-one-out nearest neighbor classification error.
- Extensive experiments on eight datasets, comparing to 26 baselines, show consistent outperforming of many competitors.
- Includes ablation/initialization studies that strengthen empirical claims.
- Clear connection to related work in metric learning and optimal transport; gradient derivations are helpful.
- Writing is sometimes dense with many equations, hurting readability and concision.
- Non-convex loss and optimization landscape are noted but lack deeper discussion of local optima and training challenges.
- Choice of Sinkhorn regularization λ=10 is asserted as a trade-off but lacks sensitivity analysis or thorough justification.
- Probabilistic filtering is mentioned without sufficient detail on procedure or its impact on training.
- Improvements are often marginal (e.g., 1–2%)—statistically significant but possibly limited practical impact.
- Use of squared Euclidean distance in relaxed transport may limit performance on some datasets (e.g., CLASSIC) and alternatives are not explored.
- Code is provided, but reproducing results demands substantial computational resources due to many experiments and WMD complexity.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong empirical results, novel supervised extension of WMD, technical soundness, practical relevance, comprehensive presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: marginal gains in some cases, high computational cost, and insufficient analysis/detail on key methodological choices.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add λ sensitivity analysis, detail probabilistic filtering, discuss squared Euclidean limitations, and improve concision.
