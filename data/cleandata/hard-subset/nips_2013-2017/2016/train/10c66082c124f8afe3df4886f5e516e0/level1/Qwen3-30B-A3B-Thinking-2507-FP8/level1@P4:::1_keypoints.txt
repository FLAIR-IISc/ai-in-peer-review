- Proposes Supervised Word Mover's Distance (S-WMD): learns a linear transformation of word embeddings and word importance weights to incorporate supervision into WMD.  
- Uses the Sinkhorn entropy-regularized approximation to WMD to enable efficient gradient computation and end-to-end training.  
- Provides clear theoretical derivation of gradients for both the transformation matrix and word weights, addressing nested optimization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical improvements across 8 text classification datasets against 26 baselines, often achieving state-of-the-art performance.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive with thorough details on datasets, baselines, hyperparameters, and reproducibility.  
- Visualization and examples (Figures 1–2) effectively illustrate improved document clustering and learned word importances.  
- Method is efficient in practice (Table 3) and applicable to any pre-trained word embeddings without requiring additional labeled-data training.  
- Novelty lies in applying supervision to WMD and combining it with the Sinkhorn approximation, distinct from prior metric-learning on document representations.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and accessible with clear mathematical formulations and pseudocode.  
- Acknowledges limitations: sensitivity to initialization (notably CLASSIC dataset) and potential suboptimality of squared Euclidean distance for some datasets.  
- Does not deeply analyze why squared Euclidean fails on certain datasets nor explore alternative distance metrics.  
- Initialization strategy (NCA on WCD) may not be optimal across all datasets, affecting robustness.  
- Lacks detailed computational complexity comparison of full training versus other metric-learning approaches.  
- Does not explicitly compare to other supervised extensions of WMD or newer supervised document distance metrics post-2015.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically useful, and significant improvement over unsupervised WMD; recommended for acceptance despite somewhat incremental novelty.
