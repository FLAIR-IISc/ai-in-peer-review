- Proposes S-WMD: a supervised, task-specific document metric extending WMD by learning an affine embedding transform and per-word importance weights.
- Objective: NCA-inspired stochastic leave-one-out kNN classification loss to learn the metric end-to-end.
- Main technical contribution: uses entropy-regularized Sinkhorn distance to obtain an efficient, differentiable approximation of WMD for gradient-based learning.
- Practical enhancements: initialization via Word Centroid Distance and batched gradient descent with neighbor filtering to improve training practicality.
- Significance: addresses WMD’s lack of supervision, with potential broad impact on text classification and retrieval.
- Originality and quality: technically sound and novel; clever practical solution to a challenging nested optimization.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
- Evaluation: thorough empirical study on eight diverse datasets against 26 strong baselines, showing significant improvements.
- Minor weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of failure cases and when S-WMD is outperformed (e.g., dataset traits or initialization sensitivity).
- Overall verdict: high-quality, well-motivated, empirically validated work whose strengths outweigh minor weaknesses.
