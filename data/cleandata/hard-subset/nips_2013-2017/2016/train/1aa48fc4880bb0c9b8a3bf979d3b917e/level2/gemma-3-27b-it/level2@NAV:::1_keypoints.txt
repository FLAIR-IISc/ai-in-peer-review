- Tackles scaling convex-concave saddle-point problems by extending SVRG/SAGA methods.
- Claims existing SVR approaches don’t directly apply and proposes specific adaptations for saddle-point settings.
- Provides a novel convergence analysis based on monotone operator theory.
- Emphasizes two notions of “splits”: function-based vs. derivative-based decompositions.
- Demonstrates non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency, with theoretical and empirical support.
- Adds catalyst-style acceleration that outperforms accelerated batch algorithms.
- Theoretical development is strong, establishing linear convergence.
- Experimental evaluation is limited, using relatively small datasets.
- Comparisons with primal methods and modern optimizers are insufficient.
- Practical guidance on choosing between splitting strategies is underexplored.
- The connection to variational inequalities is mentioned but not well integrated.
- Writing could be more concise; the introduction is overly lengthy.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and improve clarity.
- Pros: novelty, rigorous theory, and demonstrated practical improvements.
- Cons: limited experiments and presentation/writing issues.
