- Proposes and analyzes SVR methods (SVRG, SAGA) for large-scale convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides the first linear convergence analysis for SVR in the saddle-point setting.  
- Leverages monotone operator theory for a rigorous, generalizable convergence proof.  
- Presents a unified analysis that extends beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Investigates different data splitting and sampling strategies, including non-uniform sampling.  
- Introduces an acceleration scheme based on the catalyst framework.  
- Highly original and significant: fills a gap by extending SVR to saddle-point formulations common in ML.  
- Strong technical quality: avoids restrictive co-coercivity assumptions and offers elegant theory.  
- Clear writing and complete presentation with experiments showing accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling outperforms baselines.  
- Main weakness: requires knowledge of strong convexity–concavity constants (λ, γ) to set step-sizes.  
- Practical concern: catalyst acceleration adds tuning parameter τ and would benefit from a practical guide or ablation study.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper, strong candidate for acceptance and likely to inspire further research.
