- Summary: Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, presenting the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms with theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Strength: Novel extension of SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems.  
- Strength: Thorough theoretical analysis including convergence rates and complexity bounds.  
- Strength: Experiments on real-world datasets demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Strength: Explores extension to monotone operators, broadening applicability.  
- Weakness: Assumes strong convexity–concavity constants, which may not be available in practice.  
- Weakness: Limited empirical and methodological comparison to existing saddle-point methods.  
- Weakness: Experimental evaluation needs more detailed analysis and additional experiments.  
- Originality: Novel adaptation of variance-reduction methods to saddle-point and monotone operator settings.  
- Significance: Potentially high impact for ML applications involving saddle-point problems, enabling more efficient solutions.  
- Quality: Well-written paper with solid theoretical presentation and clear experimental results.  
- Clarity: Well-organized, concise presentation with well-defined notation.  
- Related work: Comprehensive literature review but lacks deeper comparisons to competing methods.  
- Conclusion: Significant contribution with some limitations, worthy of publication.  
- Rating: 8.5/10.  
- Recommendation: Recommend for NIPS publication.  
- Minor comments: Request more detailed experimental analysis, disclosure of computational resources, fuller comparisons to existing methods, and more details on the monotone-operator extension.
