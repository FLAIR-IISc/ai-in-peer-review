- Novel extension of stochastic variance reduction (SVRG/SAGA) methods to convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- First large-scale algorithms for saddle-point problems with provable linear convergence.  
- Algorithms can be accelerated via a simple extension of the "catalyst" framework.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis including convergence rates and running-time complexities.  
- Experimental evaluation on multiple datasets demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Assumes known strong convexity/concavity constants, which may be impractical.  
- Focus is limited to saddle-point problems, not the most general ML settings.  
- Comparisons to existing methods are presented but lack some state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., primal–dual coordinate descent).  
- The approach is original, building on recent advances in variance reduction.  
- Contributions are significant and potentially widely applicable in ML where objectives decompose.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow with good figures/tables.  
- High-quality work overall; recommendation to accept for publication.
