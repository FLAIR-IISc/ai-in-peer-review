- Extends stochastic variance reduction methods to convex-concave saddle-point problems, a significant contribution.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including convergence rates and computational complexities.
- Writing is clear, concise, and the paper is well-structured.
- Theoretical claims are well-supported by experimental results.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on supervised learning with non-separable losses/regularizers, robust optimization, and convex relaxations of unsupervised learning.
- Empirical results outperform existing batch and stochastic algorithms, especially on ill-conditioned problems.
- Offers a comprehensive review of related work and uses standard notation with clear explanations.
- Novel contribution: applying variance reduction to saddle-point problems.
- Introduces a simple catalyst-style extension to accelerate the proposed algorithms.
- Catalyst extension leads to improved convergence rates.
- Experiments are thorough and well-presented with detailed analysis.
- Limitation: assumes known strong convexity–concavity constants, which may be unrealistic.
- Limitation: lacks a detailed practical analysis of computational complexity.
- Could benefit from more detailed experimental comparisons with other state-of-the-art algorithms.
- Overall assessment: well-written paper with significant potential to improve large-scale machine learning methods.
