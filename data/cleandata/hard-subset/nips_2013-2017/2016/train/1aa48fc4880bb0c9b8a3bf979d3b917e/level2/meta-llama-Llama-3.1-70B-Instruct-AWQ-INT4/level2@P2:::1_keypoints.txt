- Proposes first linearly convergent incremental gradient algorithms for saddle-point problems by extending SVRG and SAGA.  
- Shows algorithms apply to a broader class, including variational inequalities.  
- Identifies non-uniform sampling as essential for efficient convergence.  
- Uses monotone-operator framework to prove convergence.  
- Provides detailed analysis of convergence rates and running-time complexities.  
- Presents experiments demonstrating algorithm effectiveness.  
- Well-written and clearly organized with thorough explanations.  
- Places work in context with a comprehensive review of existing batch and stochastic methods.  
- Original contribution: extending SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point problems and novel use of monotone operators.  
- High significance: applicable to supervised learning with non-separable losses, robust optimization, and convex relaxations.  
- Limitation: requires knowledge of strong convexity–concavity constants.  
- Lacks clear adaptation strategy for non-strongly convex-concave problems.  
- Suggests future work on adaptivity to unknown strong-convexity/concavity constants.  
- Suggested improvements: more experiments across problem types and more background on monotone operators.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for publication.
