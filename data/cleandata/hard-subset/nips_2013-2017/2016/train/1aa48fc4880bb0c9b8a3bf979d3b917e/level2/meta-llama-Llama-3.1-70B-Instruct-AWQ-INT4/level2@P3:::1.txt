This paper presents a novel extension of stochastic variance reduction methods to saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors provide the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems, building upon recent advances in stochastic variance reduction methods.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper focuses on convex-concave saddle-point problems, where the objective functions can be split into many components. The authors extend stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) to this setting, providing a linearly convergent algorithm for saddle-point problems. They also show that these incremental algorithms can be easily accelerated using a simple extension of the "catalyst" framework.
