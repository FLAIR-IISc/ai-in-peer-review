- Studies min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) where M is strongly convex-concave with easy prox and K is smooth, extending SVRG/SAGA to this saddle-point setting.  
- Proves linear convergence for SVRG, SAGA, and accelerated SVRG under strong convexity-concavity using monotone operator theory.  
- Analysis departs from standard convex minimization by leveraging monotone operator/V.I. theory, enabling extension beyond saddle points.  
- Formalizes two natural operator splits for the smooth part B: by functions or by partial derivatives.  
- Demonstrates the importance of non-uniform sampling and derives tight L̄(π) constants for bilinear problems.  
- Introduces storage-efficient “factored” SAGA variants to reduce memory costs.  
- Adapts Catalyst/proximal-point acceleration to the saddle-point setting, yielding improved complexities.  
- Experiments on two datasets show gains over batch baselines and highlight benefits of non-uniform sampling and acceleration.  
- Well positioned relative to SAG/SVRG/SAGA and primal-dual/forward-backward methods; handles non-separable losses unlike some stochastic primal-dual coordinate methods.  
- Connects to Rockafellar’s monotone operator theory and generalized forward-backward/SMART methods; argues analysis avoids co-coercivity assumptions.  
- Reviewer suggests adding discussion and baselines for extragradient/Mirror-Prox and their linear rates under strong monotonicity.  
- Theorems 1–3 give explicit linear rates and step-sizes depending on L and L̄(π); non-uniform sampling and splitting analyses are carefully derived.  
- Assumptions (strong convexity-concavity, prox_M access, monotonicity/Lipschitz B) are standard; need to know/bound L and L̄, with practical workarounds noted.  
- SVRG/SAGA algorithm variants are straightforward and thoughtfully adapted; Catalyst adaptation is elegant and avoids extra Nesterov machinery.  
- Empirical results align with theory, but experiments are narrow (two datasets) and omit comparisons with extragradient/Mirror-Prox and some accelerated primal-dual methods.  
- Clarity is generally good, but there is an inconsistency about whether splits must be convex-concave vs. allowing partial-derivative splits—needs clarification.  
- Request for a concise practical tuning paragraph on estimating L/L̄, choosing minibatch size m, and setting τ for Catalyst.  
- Originality rated high: first rigorous linear-convergence variance-reduction framework for general convex-concave saddle-point problems (circa NIPS 2016).  
- Significance rated high: likely influential for ML problems expressed as saddle points, with practical contributions like factored SAGA and sampling guidance.  
- Pros: novel extension of SVRG/SAGA to saddle points, insightful splitting/sampling analysis, implementable algorithms, Catalyst acceleration, supportive empirical results.  
- Cons: limited empirical evaluation and baselines, brief practical tuning guidance, resampling issue in non-uniform SAGA needs more discussion/ablation, minor clarity contradiction.  
- Suggestions: add extragradient/Mirror-Prox baselines and complexity discussion, provide practical tuning and sensitivity analysis, expand experiments (datasets, wall-clock, splits, minibatch, resampling ablation).  
- Further suggestions: clarify split requirement language, include brief proof sketch showing monotonicity suffices without co-coercivity, discuss limitations and adaptivity prospects.  
- Minor comments: fix typos/consistency (split wording), cite extragradient/Mirror-Prox, and add pseudocode for accelerated SVRG outer loop for readability.  
- Overall judgment: strong and timely contribution with solid theory and promising empirical evidence; recommend acceptance pending additional baselines and clarifications.
