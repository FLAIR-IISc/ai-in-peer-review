## Review of “Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems”

This paper tackles the important problem of scaling up algorithms for solving convex-concave saddle-point problems, common in machine learning, by extending Stochastic Variance Reduction (SVR) methods like SVRG and SAGA. The authors convincingly argue that existing approaches don’t directly apply to this setting and propose adaptations along with a novel convergence analysis based on monotone operator theory. This is a valuable contribution, as saddle-point problems arising in many ML applications (e.g., regularized learning, robust optimization) lack the separability required for standard stochastic gradient techniques.

The core strength of the paper lies in its theoretical development. Extending SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems isn’t trivial, and the authors’ use of monotone operator theory provides a robust framework for proving linear convergence.  Highlighting the two notions of “splits” – in terms of functions vs. partial derivatives – is insightful.  The demonstration that non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency, both theoretically and empirically, is also a significant contribution. The acceleration via a "catalyst" framework is a useful addition and outperforms accelerated batch algorithms. 

However, the paper could benefit from some improvements. While the theoretical analysis is strong, the experimental section, while demonstrating the effectiveness of their methods, feels somewhat limited. The chosen datasets are relatively small and the comparison with primal methods could be more comprehensive, including more modern optimizers. The practical implications of choosing between different splitting strategies (functional vs. derivative-based) aren’t sufficiently explored. The connection to variational inequalities, while mentioned, feels somewhat tacked on and could be better integrated. Also, the writing could be more concise; the introduction, in particular, is a bit lengthy.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Extends SVRG/SAGA to a challenging and relevant problem setting.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** Provides a rigorous convergence analysis based on monotone operator theory.
*   **Practical Relevance:** Demonstrates empirical improvements over existing methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Limited Experiments:** Datasets are relatively small; comparisons could be more thorough.
*   **Writing:** Could be more concise and focused.



**Overall:**  This is a strong paper with a valuable theoretical contribution. Despite the minor weaknesses in the experimental evaluation and writing, the novelty and soundness of the work justify acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, specifically encouraging the authors to expand the experimental section and improve the clarity of the presentation.
