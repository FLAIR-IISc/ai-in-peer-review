- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, yielding the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Notes convex minimization analysis is inapplicable and uses monotone operator theory, extending results to variational inequalities.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis including convergence rates and complexity bounds.  
- Distinguishes two split notions (functional vs. derivative-based) and identifies non-uniform sampling as critical for efficiency.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear exposition and connections to existing convex optimization work.  
- Experiments show substantial practical gains over batch methods and uniform sampling, especially on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Framework is successfully accelerated via the catalyst approach, outperforming accelerated batch methods.  
- Limitation: requires knowledge of strong convexity–concavity constants, which may be unavailable in practice.  
- Authors acknowledge this limitation but do not explore adaptive estimation of those constants.  
- Experimental comparisons should include more specialized saddle-point solvers from the literature.  
- Overall: significant, novel contribution with theoretical and practical value; recommended for acceptance.
