- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, providing the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Develops convergence analysis using monotone operator theory, extending results to variational inequalities.  
- Identifies two distinct splitting notions (by functions vs. by partial derivatives) specific to saddle-point problems.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling is crucial for theoretical efficiency and practical performance.  
- Introduces a simple catalyst-based acceleration that yields methods outperforming accelerated batch algorithms.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and technically sound, with detailed complexity results for sampling schemes.  
- Empirical results on non-separable losses and structured regularizers strongly support theoretical claims and outperform baselines.  
- Original contribution: requires a fundamentally different analysis from convex minimization and achieves linear rather than sublinear rates.  
- Significant impact: fills a gap for large-scale saddle-point optimization relevant to many ML applications, especially ill-conditioned problems.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and connects theory to implementation choices.  
- Minor weakness: limited practical detail on implementing the factored split and how O(n+d) storage is managed.  
- Would benefit from more explicit comparisons of computational overhead versus existing methods on specific problems.  
- Overall recommendation: accept (novel, technically sound, and significant contribution).
