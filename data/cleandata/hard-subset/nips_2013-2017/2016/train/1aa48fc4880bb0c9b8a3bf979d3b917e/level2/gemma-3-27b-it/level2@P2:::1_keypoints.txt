- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems with convergence analysis based on monotone operators.  
- Highlights importance of non-uniform sampling and catalyst-like acceleration for saddle-point methods.  
- Provides first linear convergence guarantees for these stochastic methods in the saddle-point setting.  
- Thorough comparison to related batch and stochastic algorithms; extensions to variational inequalities broaden applicability.  
- Experimental results show effectiveness, especially from non-uniform sampling and acceleration.  
- Convergence proofs appear rigorous and technically sound; appendix proof sketches are reassuring.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and uses consistent notation with accessible algorithm descriptions.  
- Original contributions include adapting SVRG/SAGA to saddle points, emphasizing sampling, and applying catalyst acceleration.  
- Significant due to ubiquity of saddle-point problems and demonstrated practical improvements.  
- Assumptions (A)-(C) are abstract and could use concrete examples for common ML settings.  
- Analysis depends on mini-batch size `m`; practical trade-offs and impacts on prox computation are underexplored.  
- Catalyst acceleration section needs more detail on choosing and tuning parameter `τ` in practice.  
- Experimental scope is limited to two datasets; broader evaluation across problem types/dimensions is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, rigorous analysis, effective experiments, and broader applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: abstract assumptions, insufficient practical tuning guidance, and limited experimental breadth.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify assumptions, discuss mini-batch trade-offs/τ tuning, and expand experiments.
