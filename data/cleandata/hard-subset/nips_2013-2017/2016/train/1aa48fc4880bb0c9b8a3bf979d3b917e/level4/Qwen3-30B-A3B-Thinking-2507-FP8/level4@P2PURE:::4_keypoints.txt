- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence rates.
- Theoretical and empirical evidence shows variance reduction effectively addresses saddle-point problems.
- Algorithms apply to a broad class of non-separable saddle-point problems (non-separable loss and regularizer).
- Convergence analysis uses monotone-operator theory to handle the more complex saddle-point setting versus prior convex-only work.
- Analysis implies the methods cover a wider problem class, including variational inequalities arising in game theory.
- Proposes two gradient operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Applies variance reduction to stochastic forward-backward algorithms and considers non-uniform sampling.
- Both theory and experiments indicate non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experiments in main text and appendix support theoretical claims and show effectiveness on non-separable loss/regularizer settings.
- Acceleration technique (Section 5) follows prior work by adding a regularizer to align updates with a reference iterate (\bar{x},\bar{y}), but how to adaptively update this iterate for speed-up is unclear.
- Convergence and acceleration proof is provided for SVRG, but it is uncertain whether identical theoretical guarantees hold for SAGA.
- Notational ambiguity: the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K should use distinct symbols to avoid confusion.
