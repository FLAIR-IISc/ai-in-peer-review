- Tackles convex-concave saddle-point problems using stochastic variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA).  
- Extends SVRG and SAGA to the saddle-point setting with a tailored convergence analysis.  
- Proves linear convergence under suitable (strong convexity) assumptions.  
- Applies the “catalyst” acceleration framework, yielding superior performance to accelerated batch methods.  
- Extends analysis beyond saddle-points to general monotone operators.  
- Empirical results validate theoretical findings and show practical effectiveness.  
- Builds on prior SVRG/SAGA work but requires nontrivial adaptations for saddle-point problems.  
- Leverages monotone operator theory for a more general and rigorous convergence analysis.  
- Appropriately references related work on accelerated saddle-point methods, robust optimization, and variational inequalities.  
- Novel extension fills a gap in the literature by bringing variance-reduction to saddle-point problems.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and broadens applicability via monotone-operator perspective.  
- Catalyst-based acceleration is a clever and effective contribution.  
- Addresses a practically important set of problems in supervised, robust, and unsupervised learning.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and include meaningful comparisons to existing methods.  
- Reliance on strong convexity for both primal and dual problems is a restrictive assumption.  
- Paper lacks detailed discussion of practical considerations like parameter tuning and computational costs.  
- Notation is sometimes unclear, especially regarding different splitting notions (functions vs. partial derivatives).  
- Choice and justification of the catalyst acceleration parameter τ need further investigation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, experimentally validated, well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability due to strong convexity assumption, insufficient practical guidance, and notation clarity issues.  
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address strong convexity relaxation and practical parameter guidance.
