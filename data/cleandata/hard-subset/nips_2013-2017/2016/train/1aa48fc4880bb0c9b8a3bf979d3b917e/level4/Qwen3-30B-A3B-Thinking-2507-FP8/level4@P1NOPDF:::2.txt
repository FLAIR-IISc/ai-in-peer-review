

This paper addresses the minimization of convex-concave saddle problems by extending recent variance reduction techniques. The authors provide a convergence analysis for their proposed methods and demonstrate that acceleration can be achieved through a straightforward extension of the Catalyst framework. The work also includes an extension to monotone operators and presents numerical results. However, the assumption of strong convexity for both the primal and dual problems is overly strong; it suffices for only one of the two to be strongly convex. Additionally, the numerical experiments would be strengthened by including more examples to better support the theoretical claims.
