- Adapts SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Provides both theoretical analysis and empirical validation demonstrating efficacy of the variance-reduced approach.  
- Algorithms handle non-separable saddle-point problems where loss and regularizer may be non-separable (e.g., structured output, fusion terms).  
- Uses monotone operator framework to manage saddle-point complexity and establish convergence guarantees.  
- Shows methods extend to a broader class including variational inequalities in game-theory settings.  
- Introduces two stochastic gradient-splitting strategies: element-wise split for SAGA and factored split for SVRG.  
- Integrates variance-reduction schemes into the stochastic forward–backward algorithm for saddle-point solving.  
- Analyzes and empirically demonstrates that non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Experiments (main paper + appendix) comprehensively validate theoretical claims and performance on non-separable problems.
