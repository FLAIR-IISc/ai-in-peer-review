- Adapt SVRG/SAGA to produce what appear to be the first algorithms with linear convergence for saddle-point problems.  
- Main contribution: convergence analysis via monotone-operator framework (finding zeros), extending to variational inequalities.  
- Approach allows handling non-separable losses and regularizers by formulating them as saddle-point problems.  
- Claimed novelty: first saddle-point methods with provable linear rates — an important advance.  
- Proof strategy (casting as zeros of a monotone operator) is compelling and likely broadly applicable.  
- Paper also gives simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic counterpart.  
- Empirically, proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform existing methods.  
- Primary novelty is analytical; authors should consider adding a proof sketch in the main text.  
- Some clarity issues: unclear how non-uniform sampling distribution π is defined beyond bilinear cases.  
- Unclear how step size σ was chosen in experiments and why non-uniform sampling matters more for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos: line 78 (k -> d) and line 121 (extra y_{t-1} term).
