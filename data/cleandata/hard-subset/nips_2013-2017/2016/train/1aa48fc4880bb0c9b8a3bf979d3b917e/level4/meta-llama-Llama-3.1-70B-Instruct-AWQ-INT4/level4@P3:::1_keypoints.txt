- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Proposes an accelerated variant that improves convergence rates.  
- Introduces a non-uniform sampling scheme to potentially boost practical performance.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis and claims first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Novelly handles non-separable functions, extending existing variance-reduction techniques.  
- Reports experimental results on two datasets that demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Presentation is unclear, especially the connection to monotone operator theory.  
- Experiments are limited to only two datasets and need broader evaluation.  
- Lacks comparison to accelerated baselines, e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock variants.  
- Strength of assumptions A–C (particularly A) requires clarification.  
- The distinction between stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed approach is insufficiently explained.  
- The constant μ in Theorem 2 is ambiguous relative to the monotonicity constant in the appendix.  
- Minor notational issue: confusion between L as condition number versus Lipschitz constant.  
- Forward–Backward algorithm is not referenced where relevant.  
- Recommendations: clarify assumptions and operator connections, improve presentation, expand experiments, and compare to accelerated methods.
