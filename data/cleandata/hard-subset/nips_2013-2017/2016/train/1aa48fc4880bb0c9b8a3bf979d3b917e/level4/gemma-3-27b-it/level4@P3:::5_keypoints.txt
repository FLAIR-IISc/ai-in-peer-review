- Paper extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, achieving first linear convergence for large-scale instances.  
- Key methodological insight: use of monotone operator theory for convergence analysis beyond convex minimization.  
- Results apply beyond saddle-point problems to general variational inequalities.  
- Addresses practical need for algorithms handling non-separable losses and regularizers in modern ML.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and broadly applicable.  
- Presentation is clear and accessible.  
- Empirical results validate effectiveness, especially with non-uniform sampling.  
- Connection to the “catalyst” framework improves efficiency and practicality.  
- Inclusion of proofs for simplified forward-backward splitting is a helpful addition.  
- Unclear explanation of how the sampling distribution π is chosen for non-bilinear problems.  
- Insufficient detail and rationale for the step-size σ used in experiments.  
- Lacking deeper intuition on why non-uniform sampling is more critical for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Suggestion to include a proof sketch to improve accessibility of core theoretical contributions.  
- Minor typos: Line 78 ("k -> d") and Line 121 (extra "y_{t-1}" term).  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing π, step-size, and sampling intuition.
