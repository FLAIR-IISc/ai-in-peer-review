This work addresses the problem of minimizing a convex-concave saddle point problem and extends recent variance reduction methods to solve it. The authors provide convergence analysis for the proposed methods, demonstrating their effectiveness. Furthermore, they show that the methods can be easily accelerated by leveraging the extension of the Catalyst framework. The authors also provide extensions to monotone operators and numerical results, which support the theoretical findings.

However, the strong convexity assumptions imposed on both the primal and dual problems are overly restrictive. A more realistic assumption would be to require strong convexity on either the primal or dual problem, but not necessarily both. This relaxation would make the results more applicable to a broader range of problems.

Additionally, the numerical examples provided are limited, and it would be beneficial to include more examples to demonstrate the robustness and efficiency of the proposed methods across different problem settings. This would strengthen the paper's contribution and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the methods' performance.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of convex-concave saddle point problems, and with some revisions to address the above concerns, it has the potential to make a more significant impact.
