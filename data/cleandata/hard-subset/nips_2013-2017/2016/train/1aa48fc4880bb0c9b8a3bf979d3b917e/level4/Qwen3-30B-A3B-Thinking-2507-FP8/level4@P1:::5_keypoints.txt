- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent large-scale stochastic algorithms for this class.  
- Algorithmic extension from convex minimization is straightforward, but convergence analysis requires monotone operator theory instead of convexity-based techniques.  
- The monotone-operator framework generalizes beyond saddle-point problems to variational inequalities.  
- Key contributions: first convergence analysis for stochastic variance reduction on saddle-points using monotone operators.  
- Key contributions: clarifies distinction between splits (functions vs partial derivatives) and necessity of convex-concave splits.  
- Key contributions: demonstrates critical importance of non-uniform sampling for theoretical and practical efficiency.  
- Key contributions: shows acceleration via the catalyst framework.  
- Significance: fills an important literature gap—saddle-point problems in ML (non-separable losses/regularizers, robust and unsupervised settings) now have linearly convergent stochastic methods.  
- Theoretical strength: monotone-operator perspective is a notable generalization and theoretical advancement.  
- Practical strength: clear ML application examples and strong empirical validation showing non-uniform sampling and acceleration outperform alternatives.  
- Paper quality: well-structured, clearly written, technically detailed, reproducible, and with sound proofs and useful comparisons to batch methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient guidance on choosing sampling probabilities π for general (non-bilinear) saddle-point problems.  
- Weakness: limited intuitive explanation of why non-uniform sampling is essential for saddle-point problems but not necessarily for convex minimization.  
- Minor issues: typos—line 78 "k" should be "d" and line 121 contains an extra "y_{t-1}" term.  
- Suggested improvement: add discussion on sampling-probability selection for general problems and a more intuitive comparison of variance-reduction behavior between saddle-point and convex minimization settings.
