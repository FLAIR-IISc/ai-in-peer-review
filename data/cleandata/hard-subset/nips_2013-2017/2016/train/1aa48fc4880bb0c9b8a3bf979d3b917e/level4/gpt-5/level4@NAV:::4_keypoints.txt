- Generalizes SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Algorithms handle non-separable losses and regularizers (e.g., structured output, fusion terms).  
- Convergence analysis is more intricate than standard convex cases and leverages monotone operator theory.  
- Extended methods encompass a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities from game theory.  
- Introduces two gradient decompositions within the stochastic framework: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
- Integrates variance-reduced schemes into stochastic forward–backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems.  
- Studies non-uniform sampling and shows theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling with variance reduction.  
- Experiments in the paper and appendix validate the theory and demonstrate effectiveness on non-separable losses and regularizers.  
- Minor concern: acceleration approach adds a regularizer toward a reference iterate (x̄,ȳ) but it’s unclear how to choose/update this reference adaptively.  
- Question: convergence and acceleration guarantees are given for SVRG—are analogous results available for SAGA?  
- Notational issue: using the same symbol K for both a smooth function and a design matrix is potentially confusing; recommend distinct notation.
