- Contribution: Solves convex–concave saddle-point problems with stochastic variance-reduced methods + proximal operators, using a monotone operator framework, proving linear convergence and proposing acceleration and non-uniform sampling.  
- Novelty: Substantial theoretical contributions and new insights for saddle-point optimization, notably handling non-separable functions.  
- Presentation issue: Monotone operator framework is central to the analysis but is presented too late (Section 6); it should be introduced earlier for clarity.  
- Experiments: Empirical validation is limited to two datasets and should be broadened.  
- Baseline comparison: Lacks comparison to accelerated stochastic primal–dual baselines (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock variant); add the Zhang et al. (2015) reference and comparisons.  
- Clarification 1: Strength of Assumptions A–C (especially A) is unclear; provide concrete examples (e.g., SVM hinge-loss with regularization) showing whether they hold.  
- Clarification 2: Relationship to stochastic primal–dual methods is insufficiently detailed—clarify algorithmic differences, variance-reduction mechanisms, proximal use, required assumptions, and convergence rates.  
- Clarification 3: Theorem 2’s μ should be explicitly identified (is it the strong monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and its rate compared to Theorem 1 under the same conditioning and sampling.  
- Clarification 4: The appendix transition from Assumptions A–C to (strong) monotonicity is confusing—make the logical implications and any extra assumptions explicit.  
- Notation minor: Ambiguous use of L as both Lipschitz constant and condition number; disambiguate (suggest κ for condition number, L for Lipschitz).  
- Reference minor: Add a standard reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Overall strengths: Rigorous theoretical development, linear convergence in monotone operator setting, acceleration and non-uniform sampling extensions, and applicability to non-separable problems.  
- Overall weaknesses: Exposition hides the monotone operator perspective, narrow experiments, missing key baseline comparisons, and unclear mapping of assumptions/constants between main text and appendix.  
- Recommendation: Improve exposition of monotone operator framework, expand experiments and baseline comparisons, and clearly tie assumptions and constants across text and appendix to make the paper a strong contribution.
