- First algorithms using variance reduction (SVRG/SAGA) to achieve linear convergence for saddle point problems.  
- Novel use of monotone-operator framework to establish convergence where convex-minimization analysis fails.  
- Analysis generalizes to a broader class including variational inequality problems.  
- Provides a pathway to handle non-separable loss functions and regularizers via saddle-point reformulation.  
- Proof technique (viewing saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator) is insightful and likely broadly applicable.  
- Offers simpler proofs for forward-backward splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Proposed algorithms use non-uniform sampling, show competitive performance, and generally outperform existing methods.  
- Main contribution is the proof technique; algorithms are relatively straightforward extensions of existing methods.
