- Adapts SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems using a proximal operator and monotone-operator framework, proving linear convergence rates.  
- Extends the core method to non-separable objectives, introduces an accelerated variant, and analyzes non-uniform sampling.  
- Strong theoretical contributions that broaden applicability and provide novel analysis for variance-reduced methods on saddle-point problems.  
- Presentation issue: monotone-operator framework is introduced too late (Section 6); it should be introduced earlier to frame the main algorithm and analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; needs experiments on more datasets to demonstrate practical effectiveness and robustness.  
- Empirical comparisons are incomplete; include baselines such as Zhang & Xiao (2015) stochastic primal–dual coordinate method for separable cases.  
- Clarify the strength and restrictiveness of Assumptions (A)–(C), particularly Assumption (A), and whether they hold for common ML problems (e.g., SVM saddle-point).  
- Discuss in detail how the proposed method differs from and improves upon existing stochastic primal–dual methods (theoretical and practical advantages).  
- In Theorem 2, clarify the definition of μ (is it the strong monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and explicitly compare the convergence rate to Theorem 1.  
- Make explicit whether Assumptions (A)–(C) imply the strong monotonicity used in the appendix and clearly state the relationship between them.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of the constant L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) — clarify notation; add a proper citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
