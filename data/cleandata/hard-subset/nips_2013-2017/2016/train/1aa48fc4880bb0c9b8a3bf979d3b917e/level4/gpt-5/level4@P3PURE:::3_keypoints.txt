- Extends SVRG, SAGA, and Catalyst-accelerated variants to strongly convex–concave finite-sum saddle-point problems with individual and factored splits.  
- Provides thorough complexity analysis across splits and sampling strategies (uniform and nonuniform).  
- Experiments (binary classification) show nonuniform sampling and acceleration are critical to outperform strong batch baselines.  
- Results are novel and the technical development is nontrivial despite apparent straightforwardness.  
- For bilinear saddle-point problems, gives a clear comparison of batch, stochastic, and incremental FB algorithms with conclusions mirroring convex minimization.  
- Major concern: each iteration requires an O(n + d) proximal cost, creating a bottleneck that can eliminate theoretical advantages over accelerated batch methods.  
- When n >> d (common in ML), complexity under factored splits can be worse than batch FB (as shown in Table 2).  
- Requests clarification/discussion on: (i) whether factored split is theoretically worse than individual split, (ii) conditions where accelerated SVRG beats batch FB, and (iii) tightness of n and d dependences.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer was satisfied with the authors’ response but kept scores unchanged.
