- Combines SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator to solve convex–concave saddle-point problems, analyzed via monotone operators with linear convergence.  
- Proposes an accelerated variant and a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Significant theoretical contributions with thorough analysis and extension to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation is weak; the connection to monotone operators is unclear despite being central to the analysis.  
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets); more datasets are needed.  
- Lacks comparison to accelerated methods for separable functions (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock, Zhao et al. 2015); add this reference.  
- Clarify the strength of assumptions (A)–(C), especially (A); do they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems?  
- Explain differences between stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed method.  
- Clarify the constant μ in Theorem 2 (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and compare Theorem 2 to Theorem 1.  
- The transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing—do (A)–(C) imply monotonicity?  
- Minor: inconsistent use of L (referred to both as condition number and Lipschitz constant).  
- Minor: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
