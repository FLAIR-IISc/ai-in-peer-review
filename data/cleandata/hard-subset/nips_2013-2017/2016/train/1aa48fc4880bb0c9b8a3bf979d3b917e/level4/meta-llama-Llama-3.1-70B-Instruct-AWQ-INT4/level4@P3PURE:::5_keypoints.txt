- Proposes the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems using variance reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA).  
- Main novelty is using monotone-operator theory to prove convergence, allowing extension to variational inequality problems.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers by formulating them as saddle point problems.  
- Proof technique (interpreting saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator) is intriguing and potentially broadly applicable.  
- Provides simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform prior alternatives.  
- Primary contribution is the proof technique rather than novel algorithmic design; suggest adding more proof detail (e.g., a proof sketch) in the main paper.  
- Clarification needed on the form of non-uniform sampling π for problems beyond bilinear saddle-point problems.  
- Experimental setup lacks description of how the step size σ was chosen and discussion of why non-uniform sampling seems essential for saddle points but not for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos: line 78 ("k" should be "d") and line 121 (extra y_{t-1} in the second equality).
