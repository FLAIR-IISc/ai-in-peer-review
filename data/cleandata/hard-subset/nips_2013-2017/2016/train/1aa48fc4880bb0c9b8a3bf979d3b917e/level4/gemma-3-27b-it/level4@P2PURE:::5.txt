The authors present the first linearly convergent algorithms for solving saddle point problems by leveraging variance reduction techniques like SVRG and SAGA. A key contribution of this work is the development of a convergence analysis based on monotone operators, necessitated by the inapplicability of standard convex minimization analysis to saddle point problems. This approach extends the analysis to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities. The proposed framework effectively addresses non-separable loss functions and regularizers by reformulating them as saddle point problems. Consequently, the paper introduces the first algorithms achieving a linear convergence rate for saddle point problems – a significant advancement given the prevalence of non-separable terms in many machine learning models. The authors’ technique of interpreting saddle point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator is both novel and potentially broadly applicable. Furthermore, the paper offers simplified proofs for algorithms such as FB splitting and its stochastic counterpart. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed algorithms, employing non-uniform sampling, perform competitively and often outperform existing methods. Overall, this work represents a valuable extension to the field, presenting an insightful analytical approach. Given that the core innovation lies in the proof technique, rather than the algorithms themselves (which are straightforward extensions of existing work), the authors could strengthen the paper by providing additional details on the proof, potentially including a sketch, if space allows. The paper is generally well-written and clearly presented, although certain aspects could benefit from further clarification. Specifically: 1. For problems beyond bilinear saddle-point problems, what specific non-uniform sampling strategy is employed, and how is π defined? 2. How was the step size σ determined in the experiments? The authors might also consider discussing the intuition behind why non-uniform sampling appears crucial for saddle point problems, but less so for convex minimization. Addressing this point could add significant insight. Minor comments: 1. Line 78 contains a typo: ‘k’ should be ‘d’. 2. Line 121, the second equality contains an extra ‘y_{t-1}’ term.
