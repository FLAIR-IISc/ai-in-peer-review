This paper extends stochastic variance-reduction techniques (SVRG and SAGA) to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence. The work is well-motivated and technically solid: both the theoretical analysis and the empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of variance reduction for saddle-point optimization, including settings with non-separable losses (e.g., structured output prediction) and non-separable regularizers (e.g., fusion terms).

A key contribution is the convergence analysis based on monotone operator theory, which is substantially more involved than standard analyses for purely convex objectives. This framework not only yields linear rates for the proposed methods on saddle-point problems but also shows that the extended SVRG and SAGA algorithms apply to a broader class of problems, such as variational inequalities arising in game theory.

On the algorithmic side, the authors consider a stochastic approach with two distinct decompositions of the gradient operator: an element-wise split for SVRG and a factored split for SAGA. The variance-reduced updates are embedded within stochastic forward–backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems. The paper also studies non-uniform sampling; both the theory and experiments indicate that non-uniform sampling yields superior performance compared to uniform sampling.

The experimental evaluation (in the main paper and the appendix) provides sufficient evidence supporting the theoretical claims and demonstrates the benefits of the proposed methods on problems with both non-separable losses and non-separable regularizers.

Minor comments and questions:
- Acceleration (Section 5): Following [8], the authors add a regularization term that nudges the iterates toward a reference point, denoted (x̄, ȳ). It is unclear how to adaptively update this reference iterate to achieve practical speed-ups. Clarification on a principled adaptive scheme would be helpful.
- The paper provides convergence and acceleration proofs for SVRG. Do analogous theoretical guarantees hold for SAGA under the same assumptions?
- Notation: The smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should use different symbols to avoid confusion.
