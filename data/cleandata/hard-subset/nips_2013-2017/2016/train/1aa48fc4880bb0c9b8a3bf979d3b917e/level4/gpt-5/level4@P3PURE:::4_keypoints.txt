- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence.
- Analysis based on monotone operator theory, enabling application to a broader class (e.g., variational inequalities in game theory).
- Handles settings with non-separable losses and non-separable regularizers; theoretical and empirical results are technically solid.
- Algorithmic design uses two gradient decompositions: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Variance-reduced updates are embedded in stochastic forward–backward algorithms for saddle-point problems.
- Non-uniform sampling is studied; both theory and experiments indicate it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experimental evaluation (main paper + appendix) provides sufficient evidence supporting theoretical claims and practical benefits.
- Minor comment: unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate in the acceleration scheme—needs a principled adaptive method.
- Minor question: do analogous convergence and acceleration guarantees hold for SAGA under the same assumptions?
- Notation issue: smooth function K(·) and design matrix K should use different symbols to avoid confusion.
