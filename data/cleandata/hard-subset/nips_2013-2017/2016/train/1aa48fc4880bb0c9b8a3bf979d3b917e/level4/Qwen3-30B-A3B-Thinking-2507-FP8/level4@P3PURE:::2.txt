

This work addresses the minimization of convex-concave saddle problems by extending recent variance reduction methods. The convergence analysis of the proposed methods is provided, and the authors demonstrate that acceleration can be achieved through an extension of the Catalyst framework. Additional contributions include an extension to monotone operators and supporting numerical results. However, the strong convexity assumption imposed on both the primal and dual problems is overly restrictive; it should apply to either the primal or dual problem, not both. Furthermore, additional numerical examples should be provided to strengthen the empirical validation.
