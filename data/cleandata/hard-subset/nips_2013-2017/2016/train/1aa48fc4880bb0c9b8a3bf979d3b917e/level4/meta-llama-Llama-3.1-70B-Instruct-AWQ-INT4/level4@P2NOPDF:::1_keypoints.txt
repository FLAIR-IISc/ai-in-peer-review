- Paper studies convex-concave saddle-point problems, combining SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator.  
- Theoretical analysis in the monotone-operator setting establishes linear convergence and presents an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.  
- Significant contributions, including extension to non-separable functions.  
- The connection to monotone operators is central but not made explicit early; it appears only as an extension in Section 6.  
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets); need results on more datasets.  
- For separable functions, compare against accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock, Zhang et al. 2015) and cite that work.  
- Clarify strength and applicability of assumptions (A)–(C) — e.g., do they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems?  
- Explain differences between stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed variance-reduced proximal approach.  
- The constant μ in Theorem 2 is ill-defined (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and should be related to Theorem 1.  
- The link from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is unclear — do these assumptions imply monotonicity?  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs Lipschitz constant) and missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
