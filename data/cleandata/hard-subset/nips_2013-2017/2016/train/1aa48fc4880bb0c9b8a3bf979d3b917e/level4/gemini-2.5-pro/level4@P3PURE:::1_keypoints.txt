- Paper applies SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to convex-concave saddle-point problems and analyzes them under a monotone-operator framework, proving linear convergence.  
- Authors introduce an accelerated variant and analyze a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Contributions are significant: thorough theoretical analysis and important extensions, notably applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation issues: the monotone-operator connection is introduced late (Section 6), making the main analysis hard to follow; this should be presented earlier.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: results are limited to two datasets and need expansion.  
- For separable functions, include comparisons to relevant accelerated methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock [Zhang & Lan 2015]).  
- Question 1: Clarify the strength of Assumptions (A)–(C), especially (A); do they hold for standard SVM saddle-point formulations?  
- Question 2: Clarify the key differences between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual methods.  
- Question 3: In Theorem 2, clarify the definition of constant μ (is it the same monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and how Theorem 2’s convergence compares to Theorem 1.  
- Question 4: Explain the relationship between Assumptions (A)–(C) and the strong monotonicity assumption in the appendix — do (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity?  
- Minor: inconsistent notation for L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) — clarify and standardize.  
- Minor: add a citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
