- Extends SVRG and SAGA to large-scale convex-concave saddle-point problems, combining a proximal "simple" term M and a decomposable smooth term K.  
- Provides the first linear convergence analysis for these variance-reduction methods in the saddle-point setting.  
- Uses monotone operator theory for analysis, offering a more general framework than standard convex optimization techniques.  
- Shows applicability beyond convex-concave problems, including variational inequalities, without algorithmic changes.  
- Demonstrates practical improvements: non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency.  
- Integrates a Catalyst-like acceleration yielding theoretical rates better than accelerated batch methods.  
- Empirical results on binary classification with non-separable losses/regularizers validate theoretical gains over batch and stochastic baselines.  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and the complexity table effectively summarizes theoretical advantages.  
- Main limitation: Assumption (A) requires M to be strongly convex in x and strongly concave in y, which is restrictive for many ML formulations.  
- Experiments are limited in scope; more domains (e.g., robust optimization, unsupervised relaxations) would strengthen empirical claims.  
- Algorithms and rates depend on Lipschitz and strong convexity/concavity parameters (λ, γ); practical sensitivity or estimation strategies are not discussed.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — substantial, original contribution with strong theory and practical promise despite the restrictive assumption.
