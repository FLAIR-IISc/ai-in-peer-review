

This paper addresses convex-concave saddle-point problems using stochastic variance reduced methods (SVRG, SAGA) combined with proximal operators. The authors establish linear convergence within the monotone operator framework and propose an accelerated variant alongside a non-uniform sampling scheme. Novelty/originality: The contributions are substantial, offering thorough theoretical analysis that reveals novel perspectives for saddle-point problems. The most significant extension is the applicability to non-separable functions. Technical quality: While the theoretical contributions are strong, the presentation could be enhanced for accessibility, particularly by clarifying the connection to monotone operators earlier (currently framed as an extension in Section 6 despite being foundational to the analysis). The experimental section would benefit from results on more than two datasets. For separable cases, a comparison with accelerated methods like the stochastic Chambolle-Pock algorithm (Zhang & Lin, 2015) should be included (reference: http://www.jmlr.org/proceedings/papers/v37/zhanga15.pdf). Clarifications needed:  
1.  Assess the strength of assumptions A–C, especially A, regarding applicability to SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
2.  Explicitly contrast the proposed method with stochastic primal-dual approaches.  
3.  Clarify the constant μ in Theorem 2: does it correspond to the monotonicity constant in the appendix? How does this result relate to Theorem 1?  
4.  Resolve the confusion in the appendix regarding whether assumptions A–C imply strong monotonicity.  
Minor issues:  
-  The constant L is confusingly conflated as both the condition number ("L represents the condition number") and Lipschitz constant ("Lipschitz constant L").  
-  The Forward-Backward algorithm requires a citation.
