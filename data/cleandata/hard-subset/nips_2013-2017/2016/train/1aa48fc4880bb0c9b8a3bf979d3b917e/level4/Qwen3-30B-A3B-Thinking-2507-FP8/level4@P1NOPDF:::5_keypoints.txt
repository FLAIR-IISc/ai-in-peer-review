- Introduces the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems using variance reduction (SVRG, SAGA).  
- Main contribution is a novel proof technique using monotone operator theory, departing from convex minimization analysis.  
- Establishes linear convergence for saddle point problems and extends analysis to variational inequalities.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers common in machine learning.  
- Core innovation: interpreting saddle point problems as finding zeros of monotone operators—elegant and broadly applicable.  
- Provides simplified proofs for forward-backward splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Empirically, algorithms with non-uniform sampling outperform prior methods in most cases.  
- Since the primary novelty is the proof technique (algorithms are natural extensions), include a concise proof sketch in the main paper for accessibility.  
- Clarification needed: specify the non-uniform sampling distribution π for non-bilinear saddle point problems.  
- Clarification needed: explain selection and rationale for the experimental step size σ and why non-uniform sampling matters more for saddle points than for convex minimization.  
- Minor issues: Line 78 typo ('k' should be 'd'); Line 121 second equality has an extraneous y_{t-1} term.  
- Overall: significant and promising theoretical contribution; minor technical and exposition clarifications would strengthen the paper.
