- Paper studies convex–concave saddle-point problems using SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator, analyzed via monotone operators, proving linear convergence and proposing an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.  
- Novelty is substantial, with thorough theory and an important extension to non-separable functions.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong but the presentation needs clearer exposition.  
- The connection to monotone operators should be introduced earlier rather than appearing as an extension in Section 6.  
- Experiments should include more than two datasets to strengthen empirical claims.  
- For separable functions, compare to accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock) and add that reference.  
- Clarify the strength and applicability of assumptions (A)–(C), particularly whether (A) holds for the SVM-induced saddle-point.  
- Explain how the authors’ method differs from existing stochastic primal–dual methods.  
- In Theorem 2, clarify the constant μ (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and state how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1.  
- The appendix transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing—state explicitly whether (A)–(C) imply monotonicity.  
- Minor: Avoid reusing L for both condition number and Lipschitz constant; clarify notation.  
- Minor: Add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
