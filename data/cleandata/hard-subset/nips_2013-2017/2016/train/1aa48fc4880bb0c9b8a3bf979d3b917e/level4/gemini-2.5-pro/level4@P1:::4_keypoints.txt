- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with the first linear convergence proofs for this setting.  
- Demonstrates theoretical and empirical advantages of variance reduction for non-separable saddle-point problems.  
- Applicable to ML tasks with non-separable losses or regularizers (e.g., structured output, total variation, grouped norms).  
- Novel convergence analysis using monotone operator theory, enabling broader applicability (including variational inequalities).  
- Proposes two gradient splitting schemes—element-wise and factored—and applies variance reduction to forward-backward methods.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling is theoretically and empirically superior to uniform sampling.  
- Adapts the Catalyst acceleration framework to SVRG, yielding a provably superior accelerated method versus batch accelerations.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments validating theoretical claims and showing improvements over batch and stochastic baselines.  
- Suggests providing more intuition or concrete methods for more aggressive/adaptive updates of the Catalyst center iterate.  
- Asks whether an accelerated SAGA can receive similar convergence and acceleration guarantees as SVRG.  
- Recommends using distinct notation for the smooth function K(·,·) and the design matrix K to avoid reader confusion.
