- Extends incremental algorithms (SVRG, SAGA, and Catalyst-accelerated variants) to strongly convex–concave finite-sum saddle-point problems.  
- Provides a thorough complexity study across different data splits and uniform/nonuniform sampling strategies.  
- Experiments (binary classification) show nonuniform sampling and acceleration are critical to outperform competitive batch algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with a comprehensive related work review.  
- Main contribution: non-trivial extension of incremental methods to saddle-point problems, including bilinear cases, with detailed theoretical analysis.  
- Practicality concern: overall computational complexity may not improve over accelerated batch methods when n ≫ d due to O(n + d) proximal operator cost per iteration.  
- Requests for clarification: is the factored split theoretically worse than individual splits?  
- Requests for clarification: under what conditions is accelerated SVRG preferable to batch forward–backward (FB)?  
- Requests for clarification: is the dependence on n and d in the complexity bounds tight?  
- Presentation could be improved by adding more intuition and explanation for key analysis steps.  
- Overall assessment: good but not excellent; recommend accept with revisions.
