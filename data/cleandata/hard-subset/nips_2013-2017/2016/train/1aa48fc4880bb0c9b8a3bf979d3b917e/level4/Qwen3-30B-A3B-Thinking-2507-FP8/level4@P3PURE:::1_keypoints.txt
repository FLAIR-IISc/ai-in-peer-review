- Paper adapts SVRG/SAGA with proximal operators for convex–concave saddle-point problems, proving linear convergence in a monotone-operator framework and proposing an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.  
- Novelty: significant theoretical contributions with notable extension to non-separable functions, broadening prior work.  
- Technical quality: strong theory but presentation weakly connects to monotone operator theory (introduced only in Sec. 6); experiments are limited (only two datasets).  
- Empirical comparison: for separable functions, must compare against accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock (Zhang & Xiao, JMLR 2015).  
- Clarification needed 1: assess strength of assumptions (A)–(C), particularly (A), and whether they hold for standard SVM saddle-point formulations.  
- Clarification needed 2: clarify distinction between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual approaches.  
- Clarification needed 3: in Theorem 2 specify whether constant μ is the monotonicity constant (Appendix) and explain how its convergence rate compares to Theorem 1.  
- Clarification needed 4: explain the transition in the Appendix from (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity — do (A)–(C) imply monotonicity?  
- Minor issue: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) — clarify.  
- Minor issue: include a standard reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm (e.g., Combettes & Pesquet, 2011).
