- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.
- Provides both theoretical analysis and empirical results showing effectiveness of variance reduction for saddle-point problems.
- Applicable to non-separable saddle-point problems where both loss and regularizer can be non-separable (e.g., structured output, fusion terms).
- Uses monotone operator theory to handle the added complexity of saddle-point convergence analysis.
- Analysis shows methods extend to a broader class including variational inequalities from game theory.
- Proposes two gradient operator splits to handle stochasticity: element-wise split (SVRG) and factored split (SAGA).
- Applies variance reduction within stochastic forward-backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems.
- Considers non-uniform sampling and shows both theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experimental results in the paper and appendix strongly support the theoretical findings on non-separable problems.
- Minor concern: unclear how to adaptively update the acceleration iterate (bar{x}, bar{y}) used in Section 5.
- Question whether the provided convergence and acceleration proof for SVRG also applies to SAGA.
- Notational confusion: the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should use different symbols.
