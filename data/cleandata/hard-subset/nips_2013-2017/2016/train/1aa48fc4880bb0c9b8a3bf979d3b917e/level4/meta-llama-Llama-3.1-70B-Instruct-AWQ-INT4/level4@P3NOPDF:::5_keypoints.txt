- Proposes the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems using variance reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA).  
- Main contribution: uses monotone-operator framework to prove convergence where convex-minimization analysis does not apply.  
- Framework extends to broader problem classes, including variational inequalities and non-separable loss/regularizer formulations via saddle-point reformulation.  
- Claims first algorithms achieving linear convergence for saddle-point problems, important for ML models with non-separable terms.  
- Proof technique (viewing saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator) is novel, potentially widely applicable, and yields simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform prior alternatives.  
- Novelty centers on the proof technique rather than algorithmic innovation; recommend adding a proof sketch or more proof details in the main paper.  
- Request clarification: specify the non-uniform sampling distribution π for problems beyond bilinear saddle points.  
- Request clarification: describe how step size σ was set in experiments and provide intuition why non-uniform sampling matters for saddle points but not necessarily for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos: line 78 (k -> d) and line 121 (extra y_{t-1} term).  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution that, with minor revisions, can have substantial impact.
