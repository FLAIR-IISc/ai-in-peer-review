This paper tackles the convex-concave saddle point problem and builds upon recent variance reduction techniques to solve it. The authors provide a convergence analysis of their proposed methods and demonstrate how they can be efficiently accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. Furthermore, they extend their approach to monotone operators and provide numerical results. However, the assumption of strong convexity on both the primal and dual problems is overly restrictive and could be relaxed to only require strong convexity on either the primal or dual problem. To strengthen the paper, additional numerical examples would be beneficial.
