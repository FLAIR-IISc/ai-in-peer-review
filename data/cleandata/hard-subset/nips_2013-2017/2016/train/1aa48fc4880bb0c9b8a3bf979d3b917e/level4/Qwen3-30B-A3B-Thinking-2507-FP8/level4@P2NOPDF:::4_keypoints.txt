- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence guarantees.
- Supports non-separable saddle-point problems (non-separable loss and regularizer), broadening applicability beyond prior separable-only methods.
- Uses monotone operator theory for a novel, more intricate convergence proof suitable for saddle-point/variational-inequality settings.
- Proposes two gradient operator splits: element-wise split for SAGA and factored split for SVRG, enabling variance reduction in forward–backward schemes.
- Incorporates non-uniform sampling, with theory and experiments showing it outperforms uniform sampling for variance reduction and speed.
- Comprehensive experiments validate the theoretical claims, including effectiveness on non-separable configurations and the benefit of non-uniform sampling.
- Minor comment: The adaptive update mechanism for the acceleration anchor point (x̄,ȳ) is not clearly explained and needs clarification.
- Minor comment: It’s unclear whether the SAGA variant admits the same acceleration and linear-convergence proof as SVRG; authors should clarify or provide analysis.
- Minor comment: Notational confusion between the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K; distinct symbols are recommended.
