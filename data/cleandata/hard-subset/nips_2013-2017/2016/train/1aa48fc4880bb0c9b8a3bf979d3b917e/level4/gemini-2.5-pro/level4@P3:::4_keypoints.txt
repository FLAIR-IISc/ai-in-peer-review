- Extends SAGA and SVRG to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and provides the first linear convergence guarantees.  
- Uses a monotone-operator-based analysis, which is more general than standard SVRG/SAGA proofs and applies to variational inequalities.  
- Studies practical design choices: compares "factored" vs "element-wise" operator splitting and emphasizes the importance of non-uniform sampling for optimal performance.  
- Proposes a catalyst-like acceleration yielding improved theoretical complexity and demonstrates empirical speedups.  
- Strong originality and significance: fills a gap enabling fast variance-reduced methods for many saddle-point ML problems.  
- Solid technical contribution: novel, general convergence analysis via monotone operators.  
- Thorough and comprehensive: analyzes multiple splitting strategies, proves benefits of non-uniform sampling, and develops a simple acceleration scheme.  
- Clear practical implications and strong experiments: shows superiority of accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling on non-separable objectives and regularizers.  
- Missing details on how to adaptively update the proximal center (bar{x}, bar{y}) in the acceleration scheme.  
- Acceleration proof provided only for SVRG; extension to SAGA is claimed but not formally justified.  
- Minor notational ambiguity: symbol K denotes both a function K(x,y) and a design matrix K, which can confuse readers.
