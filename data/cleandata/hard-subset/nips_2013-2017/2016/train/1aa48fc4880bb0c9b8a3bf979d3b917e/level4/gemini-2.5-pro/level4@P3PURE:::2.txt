This work addresses the problem of minimizing convex-concave saddle-point problems by extending recent variance reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide a convergence analysis for the proposed methods and show that they can be accelerated using an extension of the catalyst framework. The paper also presents an extension to monotone operators and includes numerical results.

However, the assumption of strong convexity in both the primal and dual problems is a significant limitation. This requirement is quite strong, as it is typically sufficient for only the primal or the dual problem to be strongly convex. Additionally, the empirical evaluation would be strengthened by the inclusion of more comprehensive numerical examples.
