- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, introducing the first incremental algorithms with linear convergence using monotone operator theory.
- Original and significant: first linearly convergent variance-reduced methods for composite decomposable saddle-point problems.
- Practically relevant: handles non-separable losses/regularizers relevant to structured prediction and robust optimization.
- Novel proof technique: leverages monotone operator theory to obtain general convergence results applicable beyond saddle-point problems.
- Strong empirical results: proposed methods outperform batch and stochastic baselines, with clear gains from non-uniform sampling and acceleration.
- Clear presentation of contributions, including simplified proofs for related methods like forward-backward splitting.
- Weakness: main paper lacks high-level intuition or a proof sketch explaining the necessity and workings of the monotone operator framework.
- Weakness: practical implementation details missing—how to choose non-uniform sampling distribution π for non-bilinear problems is unspecified.
- Weakness: experimental setup omits how step-size σ was chosen, hindering reproducibility.
- Weakness: needs deeper discussion/intuition for why non-uniform sampling is more critical in saddle-point problems than in convex minimization.

- Minor: possible extra y_{t-1} term inside prox in the Arrow-Hurwicz update on Line 121.
