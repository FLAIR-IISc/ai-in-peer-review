

This paper extends stochastic variance reduction techniques SVRG and SAGA to solve strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems, establishing linear convergence guarantees. Theoretical and empirical results confirm the effectiveness of the proposed variance reduction approach for these problems. The methodology applies to a broad class of non-separable saddle-point problems, where both the loss (e.g., structured output prediction) and regularizer (e.g., fusion term) may lack separability. A key contribution lies in the convergence analysis: unlike prior work on convex problems, the saddle-point analysis is significantly more complex, and the authors employ monotone operators to establish convergence. This analysis further demonstrates that the extended SVRG and SAGA algorithms encompass a wider problem class, including variational inequalities in game theory. For the stochastic approach, the authors propose two distinct gradient operator splits—element-wise for SVRG and factored for SAGA—to facilitate variance reduction within stochastic forward-backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems. Non-uniform sampling is also incorporated, with both theoretical and empirical results showing superior performance compared to uniform sampling. Comprehensive experimental validation in the main text and appendix provides strong support for the theoretical claims, demonstrating the proposed methods' efficacy on problems with non-separable loss and regularizer.  

Minor comments:  
1. In Section 5, the acceleration technique follows [8] by adding a regularization term to align updates with an iterate point (\bar{x}, \bar{y}). It is unclear how to adaptively update this iterate point to achieve acceleration.  
2. The convergence and acceleration proof is provided for SVRG. It remains uncertain whether identical theoretical results extend to SAGA.  
3. The smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should use distinct notation to avoid confusion.
