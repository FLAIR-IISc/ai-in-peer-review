- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with established linear convergence guarantees.
- Adapts convex-optimization algorithms to saddle-point settings using monotone operator theory instead of convex minimization techniques.
- Shows broader applicability of the analysis to variational inequalities and problems from game theory.
- Introduces two gradient splitting strategies for SVRG/SAGA: element-wise and factored.
- Emphasizes and analyzes the benefits of non-uniform sampling for improved performance.
- Provides theoretical results (with proof for SVRG) and empirical validation supporting linear convergence and practical gains.
- Experiments (including supplemental results) demonstrate effectiveness on problems with non-separable losses and regularizers.
- Clear articulation of challenges in extending stochastic methods to saddle-point problems and a rigorous analytical approach.
- Acceleration scheme (Section 5) builds on prior work but lacks clarity on adaptive updating of regularization parameters and iterates.
- Convergence proof is given for SVRG, but it is not explicitly stated whether identical guarantees hold for SAGA.
- Minor stylistic issue: notation for smooth function K(.) conflicts with the design matrix K and could be clarified.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, practical relevance, empirical validation, and generality.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient clarity on adaptive acceleration and missing explicit SAGA convergence statement.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommended for acceptance pending minor clarifications.
