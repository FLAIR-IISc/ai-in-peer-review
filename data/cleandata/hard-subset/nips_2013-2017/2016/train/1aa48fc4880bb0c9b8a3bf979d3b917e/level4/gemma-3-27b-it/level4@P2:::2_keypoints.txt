- Addresses solving convex-concave saddle-point problems by adapting SVRG/SAGA variance-reduction methods.  
- Provides a rigorous convergence analysis showing linear convergence for the adapted algorithms.  
- Extends algorithms with a “catalyst” acceleration framework for further speedups.  
- Connects the approach to monotone operator theory, broadening applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Non-uniform sampling and two distinct problem-splitting notions are key technical insights.  
- Novelty: variance reduction techniques originally for convex minimization are non-trivially extended to saddle-point problems.  
- Strength: Theoretical soundness via monotone operator theory and linear convergence proofs.  
- Strength: Practical relevance for large-scale machine learning with potential speedups over existing methods.  
- Strength: Catalyst acceleration is effectively applied to enhance performance.  
- Strength: Experiments provide empirical support for theoretical claims and performance gains.  
- Weakness: Assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual sides, which is restrictive.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited; more datasets and non-separable losses/regularizers needed.  
- Weakness: Some notation (especially for splitting options) lacks clarity and could be made more accessible.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of practical considerations like parameter tuning and proximal operator cost.  
- Argument for acceptance: Important problem, novel and theoretically sound approach with broad applicability.  
- Argument against acceptance: Limited generality due to strong convexity assumption and limited experimental validation.  
- Overall: Strong contribution with solid theory and encouraging experiments, but minor revisions recommended.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address convexity assumptions, expand experiments, and improve clarity.
