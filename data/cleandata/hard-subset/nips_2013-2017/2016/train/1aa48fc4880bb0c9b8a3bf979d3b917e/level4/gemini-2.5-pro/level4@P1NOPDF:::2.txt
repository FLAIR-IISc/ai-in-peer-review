This paper considers the problem of solving convex-concave saddle-point problems and extends recent variance reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide a convergence analysis for the proposed methods and show that they can be accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. The work also includes an extension to the more general setting of monotone operators, supported by numerical results.

While the paper presents interesting theoretical contributions, there are two main areas for improvement:

1.  The assumption that the objective is strongly convex in the primal variable and strongly concave in the dual variable is very restrictive. A more common and practical setting in the literature is when strong convexity holds for only the primal *or* the dual problem, but not both simultaneously. The impact of this work would be significantly greater if the analysis could be extended to this less restrictive and more general case.

2.  The numerical evaluation is somewhat limited. The paper would be strengthened by the inclusion of more comprehensive experiments on a wider range of problems. This would provide a more convincing demonstration of the practical advantages of the proposed methods compared to existing state-of-the-art algorithms.
