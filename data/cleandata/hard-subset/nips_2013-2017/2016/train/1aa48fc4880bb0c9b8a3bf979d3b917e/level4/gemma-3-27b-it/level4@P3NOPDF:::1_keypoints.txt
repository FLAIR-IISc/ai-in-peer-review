- Adapts SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to solve convex–concave saddle-point problems, analyzed via monotone operator theory, with linear convergence, an accelerated variant, and non-uniform sampling.  
- Strong theoretical contribution and originality, notably convergence analysis and extension to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation issue: connection to monotone operator theory should be introduced up front rather than appearing as an extension in Section 6.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to two datasets and requires a broader empirical study to support practical claims.  
- Missing empirical comparison to accelerated proximal-gradient/stochastic Chambolle–Pock methods for separable functions.  
- Clarify how restrictive assumptions (A)–(C) are in practice, with concrete examples (e.g., SVMs).  
- Explain distinctions between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual methods, both algorithmically and theoretically.  
- In Theorem 2, clarify the constant μ (is it the appendix monotonicity constant?) and directly compare its convergence rate to Theorem 1.  
- Clarify whether assumptions (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity and under what conditions.  
- Ambiguity in the use of L (both condition number and Lipschitz constant) — needs clarification.  
- Forward–Backward algorithm mentioned without citation; add a standard reference (e.g., Beck & Teboulle, 2009).  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions, contingent on addressing the above presentation, theoretical clarifications, and experimental concerns.
