The paper "Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems" proposes the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems using variance reduction techniques like SVRG and SAGA. The authors' main contribution is the use of monotone operators to prove convergence, which extends the analysis to a wider class of problems, including variational inequality problems. This method enables the tackling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers, a common issue in machine learning models.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed algorithms, including their convergence rates and computational complexities. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on binary classification problems with non-separable losses and regularizers. The results show that the proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform previous alternatives.

The paper is well-written and clearly presented, with a good balance between theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a detailed discussion of the related work and the significance of their contributions. The use of monotone operators to prove convergence is an interesting and potentially widely applicable technique.

However, there are a few issues that could be clarified or expanded upon. For example, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the non-uniform sampling scheme used for problems other than bilinear saddle-point problems. Additionally, the authors could provide more intuition on why non-uniform sampling is essential for saddle-point problems, but not necessarily for convex minimization problems.

Minor comments include a typo on line 78 (k -> d) and an extra y_{t-1} term in the second equality on line 121.

Overall, the paper makes an important contribution to the field of machine learning and optimization, and the analysis approach is interesting and well-presented.
