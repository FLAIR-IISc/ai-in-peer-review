- Introduces first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems using variance reduction (SVRG, SAGA).  
- Main contribution: convergence analysis based on monotone operators because standard convex minimization analysis does not apply.  
- Analysis extends to a broader class including variational inequalities.  
- Framework handles non-separable loss functions and regularizers by reformulating them as saddle point problems.  
- Presents the first algorithms achieving linear convergence for saddle point problems, important for models with non-separable terms.  
- Novel technique: view saddle point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator, with potentially broad applicability.  
- Provides simplified proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic counterpart.  
- Experiments show the proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform existing methods.  
- Core innovation is the proof technique rather than new algorithmic ideas; reviewer suggests adding more proof detail or a sketch.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear but some aspects require further clarification.  
- Clarification requested: for non-bilinear problems, specify the non-uniform sampling strategy and how π is defined.  
- Clarification requested: how was the step size σ chosen in the experiments?  
- Suggestion: discuss intuition why non-uniform sampling seems crucial for saddle point problems but less so for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos: Line 78 'k' should be 'd'; Line 121 second equality has an extra 'y_{t-1}' term.
