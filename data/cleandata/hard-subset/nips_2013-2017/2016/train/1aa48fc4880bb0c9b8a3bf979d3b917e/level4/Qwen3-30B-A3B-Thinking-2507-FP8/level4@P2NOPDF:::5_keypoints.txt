- Establishes the first linearly convergent SVRG/SAGA algorithms for saddle-point problems using variance reduction.  
- Core innovation: employs monotone operator theory to prove convergence, extending to variational inequalities.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers without requiring separability.  
- Proof technique: interprets saddle points as zeros of monotone operators; elegant and widely applicable.  
- Provides simpler proofs for existing methods (e.g., forward-backward splitting and its stochastic variant).  
- Empirical results: proposed algorithms, especially with non-uniform sampling, outperform prior alternatives in most experiments.  
- Main novel contribution is the proof framework rather than algorithmic design, so the paper should emphasize and expand the proof sketch in the main text.  
- Clarify definition and computation of non-uniform sampling distribution π for general (non-bilinear) saddle-point problems.  
- Clarify selection and tuning of step size σ in experiments—whether theoretically derived, tuned per problem, or heuristic—and provide intuition.  
- Discuss why non-uniform sampling is essential for saddle-point problems but not for convex minimization, relating to monotone operator structure.
