This paper presents a novel extension of stochastic variance reduction methods to solve convex-concave saddle-point problems. The authors provide the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems, which are common in machine learning. The proposed algorithms, SVRG and SAGA, are shown to be efficient and effective in solving these problems.

The paper starts by introducing the problem of convex-concave saddle-point problems and the existing algorithms for solving them. The authors then present their proposed algorithms, SVRG and SAGA, and provide a detailed analysis of their convergence rates. The paper also discusses the extension of the algorithms to monotone operators and provides numerical results to demonstrate their effectiveness.

The strengths of the paper include its clear and concise presentation of the proposed algorithms and their analysis. The authors provide a thorough discussion of the related work and the advantages of their proposed methods. The numerical results are also well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms.

However, there are some weaknesses in the paper. The strong convexity assumptions on both the primal and dual problems are very strong and may not be satisfied in many practical scenarios. It would be more realistic to assume that only one of the problems is strongly convex. Additionally, more numerical examples should be added to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms in different scenarios.
