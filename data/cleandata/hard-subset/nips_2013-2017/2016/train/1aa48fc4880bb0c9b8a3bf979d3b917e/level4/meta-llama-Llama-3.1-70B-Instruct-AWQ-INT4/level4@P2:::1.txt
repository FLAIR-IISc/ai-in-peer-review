This paper proposes stochastic variance reduction methods for saddle-point problems, extending existing techniques such as SVRG and SAGA to this setting. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, including linear convergence guarantees, and demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on two datasets.

The paper's main contributions are significant, particularly in its extension to non-separable functions. The authors' analysis is based on monotone operators, which provides a novel perspective on saddle-point problems. However, the connection to monotone operators could be made clearer, and the presentation could be improved to make the paper more accessible.

The experimental results are promising, but more datasets should be included to demonstrate the robustness of the approach. Additionally, comparisons to accelerated methods, such as the stochastic variant of the Chambolle-Pock algorithm, would be beneficial.

There are a few points that require clarification:

1. The assumptions A-C, particularly assumption (A), seem strong. It would be helpful to discuss the applicability of these assumptions to specific problems, such as SVM.
2. The differences between stochastic primal-dual methods and the proposed approach are not entirely clear. A more detailed comparison would be useful.
3. The constant μ in Theorem 2 is not well-defined. It would be helpful to clarify its relationship to the monotonicity constant mentioned in the appendix and to compare the result with Theorem 1.
4. The transition from assumptions (A)-(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing. It would be helpful to clarify whether assumptions (A)-(C) imply monotonicity.

Minor issues:

* There is a mix-up between the condition number and the Lipschitz constant, which should be corrected.
* A reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm should be added.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a thorough theoretical analysis of stochastic variance reduction methods for saddle-point problems.
* The authors extend existing techniques to non-separable functions, which is a significant contribution.
* The experimental results are promising and demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.

Weaknesses:

* The presentation could be improved to make the paper more accessible.
* More datasets should be included to demonstrate the robustness of the approach.
* Comparisons to accelerated methods would be beneficial.

Acceptance:

* The paper is well-suited for NIPS, as it presents a novel approach to saddle-point problems and provides a thorough theoretical analysis.
* The experimental results are promising, and the authors' extension to non-separable functions is a significant contribution.
* However, the paper could benefit from additional experiments and comparisons to accelerated methods.

Rating: 8/10

Confidence: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept, but encourage the authors to address the minor issues and provide additional experiments and comparisons.
