- Paper addresses convex-concave saddle-point problems and adapts SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator.  
- Analysis framed via monotone operators and establishes linear convergence guarantees.  
- Authors propose an accelerated variant and study a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Contributions are substantial and theoretically thorough, with notable applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation needs clarity; stronger, more explicit connection to monotone operator theory is required.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to two datasets; broader experiments are requested.  
- For separable functions, authors should compare to accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock).  
- Clarification requested on the strength of assumptions (A)-(C), especially (A), and whether they hold for problems like SVMs.  
- Request clearer distinction between existing stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed approach.  
- In Theorem 2, clarify whether μ is the same monotonicity constant from the appendix and compare its convergence rate to Theorem 1.  
- Explain the transition from assumptions (A)-(C) to strong monotonicity—do (A)-(C) imply strong monotonicity?  
- Minor: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant).  
- Minor: include a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Include the suggested reference: Zhang & Chan (2015) on stochastic primal–dual coordinate methods.
