1. Proposes solving convex–concave saddle-point problems with SVRG/SAGA plus a proximal operator, analyzed in the monotone operator framework, proving linear convergence and presenting an accelerated variant and non-uniform sampling.

2. Contributions are significant and original, notably extending to non-separable functions.

3. Presentation problem: the connection to monotone operator theory is insufficiently emphasized and should be introduced earlier.

4. Experiments are limited to two datasets and need broader evaluation.

5. Missing comparison to accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock); please add this baseline and reference.

6. Clarify the strength and practicality of assumptions A–C, especially (A); state whether they hold for standard SVM saddle-point formulations.

7. Explain the relation to stochastic primal–dual methods: algorithmic differences, theoretical distinctions, and regimes where this method is preferable.

8. Theorem 2’s constant μ is unclear—state whether it is the strong monotonicity constant from the appendix and how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1 under that definition.

9. The appendix transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to (strong) monotonicity is confusing—explicitly state whether (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity and provide a clear derivation or extra conditions required.

10. Notation ambiguity: L is used both as a condition number and as a Lipschitz constant—disambiguate with distinct symbols.

11. Add an appropriate reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.

12. Reorganize the paper by moving monotone operator definitions and assumptions from the appendix/Section 6 to earlier sections to guide readers.

13. Overall: strong theoretical results (linear convergence, acceleration, non-uniform sampling), but needs clearer exposition of monotone-operator role, precise assumptions, and stronger empirical comparisons.
