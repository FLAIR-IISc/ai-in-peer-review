- Novel extension of stochastic variance reduction methods to convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- First large-scale, linearly convergent algorithms for this problem class.  
- Proposes SVRG and SAGA variants shown to be efficient and effective.  
- Paper presents problem setup, related work, algorithm descriptions, and detailed convergence analysis.  
- Also extends methods to monotone operators and provides numerical experiments.  
- Strength: clear, concise presentation and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Strength: well-presented numerical results demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Weakness: requires strong convexity on both primal and dual problems, which is often unrealistic.  
- Weakness: needs more numerical examples across different scenarios.  
- Quality: technically sound, claims supported by theory and experiments, well-organized writing.  
- Clarity: analysis and results are easy to follow and clearly presented.  
- Originality: novel application of SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point problems with new convergence analysis.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to large-scale ML, providing practical algorithms and improved convergence insights.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, efficient algorithms, rigorous convergence analysis, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overly strong assumptions and limited diversity of numerical validation.
