This work addresses the problem of minimizing convex-concave saddle point problems and extends recent variance reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide a convergence analysis of their proposed methods and demonstrate that they can be readily accelerated using an extension of the catalyst framework. They also present extensions to the monotone operator setting and supporting numerical results. However, the strong convexity assumption imposed on *both* the primal and dual problems is overly restrictive; strong convexity should be required for only the primal *or* the dual problem. Furthermore, the inclusion of more comprehensive numerical examples would strengthen the paper.
