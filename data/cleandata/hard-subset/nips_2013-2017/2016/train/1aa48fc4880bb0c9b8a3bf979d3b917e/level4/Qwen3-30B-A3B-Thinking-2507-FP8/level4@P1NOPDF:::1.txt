

This paper addresses saddle-point problems with convex-concave structure, demonstrating how existing stochastic variance-reduced techniques (e.g., SVRG, SAGA) combined with proximal operators can solve such problems. The authors analyze these methods within the monotone operator framework, establishing linear convergence. They further propose an accelerated variant and investigate non-uniform sampling schemes.  

**Novelty/Originality:** The contributions are significant. The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides new insights into saddle-point problems. The most notable extension is the applicability to non-separable functions, a substantial advancement over prior work.  

**Technical Quality:** I appreciate the strong theoretical contributions but was disappointed that the presentation could have been clearer. The connection to monotone operators is introduced as an extension in Section 6, yet the core analysis relies on this framework—this should be integrated earlier for better accessibility. On experiments, the authors should include results on more than two benchmark datasets. For separable cases, a comparison to accelerated methods like the stochastic Chambolle-Pock algorithm (Zhang et al., 2015) would strengthen the evaluation; please include this reference: [Zhang et al., "Stochastic Primal-Dual Coordinate Descent," JMLR 2015].  

**Key Clarifications Needed:**  
1. **Assumption Strength:** To what extent do assumptions (A)–(C) hold? Specifically, do they apply to the saddle-point problem arising from SVMs?  
2. **Method Distinction:** Please clarify how the proposed stochastic primal-dual method differs from existing stochastic primal-dual approaches.  
3. **Theorem 2 Constant:** In Theorem 2, is the constant $\mu$ the monotonicity constant referenced in the appendix? How does this result compare to Theorem 1?  
4. **Assumption Implication:** The transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing. Do (A)–(C) imply monotonicity?  

**Minor Issues:**  
- **Confusion over $L$:** The text states "The quantity $L$ represents the condition number" but later refers to "the Lipschitz constant $L$." These are distinct concepts—please clarify or correct this inconsistency.  
- **Forward-Backward Algorithm:** A reference to the Forward-Backward algorithm should be added for completeness.  

Overall, the paper makes valuable theoretical contributions, particularly regarding non-separable problems and monotone operator analysis. Addressing the presentation clarity, expanding experimental validation, and resolving the technical queries above would substantially improve the work. The core ideas are promising, and with these revisions, the paper would be a strong candidate for publication.
