- Paper extends variance-reduction methods to convex–concave saddle-point problems and provides convergence analysis.  
- Shows compatibility with and acceleration via the catalyst framework.  
- Presents extensions to monotone operators and includes numerical results.  
- Major concern: requiring strong convexity in both primal and dual problems is overly restrictive and needs justification or relaxation.  
- Suggest exploring algorithm variants or analysis under weaker assumptions (strong convexity in only primal OR dual).  
- Numerical evaluation is limited and should include comparisons to relevant baselines (existing saddle-point solvers and other variance-reduction methods).  
- Recommend broader experiments across problem sizes, structures, and application domains (e.g., distributed optimization, game theory) to demonstrate practical benefits.
