- First contribution: proposes the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems using variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA).  
- Main novelty: uses monotone-operator framework to establish convergence, extending analysis to variational inequality problems.  
- Practical advantage: saddle-point formulation allows handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers.  
- Proof technique: interpreting saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator is intriguing, potentially widely applicable, and yields simpler proofs (e.g., FB splitting and its stochastic variant).  
- Empirical result: algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform previous alternatives.  
- Suggestion: include more details or a proof sketch of the main proof technique in the main paper since the proof is the primary contribution.
