- Proposes variance-reduction methods for convex-concave saddle point problems.  
- Provides convergence analysis and shows methods can be accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework.  
- Extends the approach to monotone operators.  
- Presents supporting numerical experiments.  
- Major concern: assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual, which is overly restrictive.  
- Recommends discussing or relaxing to only primal-or-dual strong convexity (or weaker conditions).  
- Numerical evaluation is limited; needs more problem instances and comparisons to state-of-the-art solvers (e.g., proximal/accelerated gradient methods).  
- Overall technically sound but impact limited by the strong-assumption and insufficient empirical validation.
