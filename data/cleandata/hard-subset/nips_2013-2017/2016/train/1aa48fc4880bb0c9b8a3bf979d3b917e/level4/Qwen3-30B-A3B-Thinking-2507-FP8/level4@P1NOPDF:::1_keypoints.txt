- Paper studies convex–concave saddle-point problems using variance-reduced stochastic methods (SVRG/SAGA) with proximal operators, analyzed via monotone-operator theory, proving linear convergence and proposing an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.  
- Novelty: significant theoretical contributions, notably extending applicability to non-separable functions beyond prior work.  
- Technical strength: strong theoretical analysis, but presentation is unclear—monotone-operator framework is introduced late despite being central.  
- Experiments: limited to two benchmarks; need broader empirical validation and comparison to accelerated methods for separable cases.  
- Missing reference/comparison: include stochastic Chambolle–Pock (Zhang et al., JMLR 2015) for separable-case baselines.  
- Clarification 1: Do assumptions (A)–(C) hold in practical problems such as SVM-derived saddle-point formulations?  
- Clarification 2: Precisely how does the proposed stochastic primal–dual method differ from existing stochastic primal–dual approaches?  
- Clarification 3: In Theorem 2, confirm whether constant μ is the monotonicity constant from the appendix and explain how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1.  
- Clarification 4: The appendix’s transition from (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing—do (A)–(C) imply monotonicity?  
- Minor issue: Inconsistent use of L—stated as "condition number" in one place and "Lipschitz constant" in another; clarify/correct.  
- Minor issue: Add a reference to the Forward–Backward algorithm for completeness.  
- Overall recommendation: Valuable and promising work; address presentation, expand experiments, and resolve technical queries to make the paper a strong candidate for publication.
