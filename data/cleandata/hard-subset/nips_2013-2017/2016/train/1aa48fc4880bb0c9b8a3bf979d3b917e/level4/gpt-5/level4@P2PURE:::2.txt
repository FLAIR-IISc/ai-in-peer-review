This paper studies convex–concave saddle-point optimization and extends recent variance-reduction techniques to this setting. The authors present convergence analyses for the proposed methods and show that they can be readily accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework. The paper also includes an extension to monotone operators and provides numerical results.

However, the requirement of strong convexity on both the primal and the dual sides is too restrictive. It would be more appropriate to assume strong convexity on only one side (either the primal or the dual). In addition, more numerical examples should be included.
