The authors propose the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems by leveraging variance reduction algorithms such as SVRG and SAGA. Notably, the conventional convex minimization analysis does not directly apply to saddle point problems, making the primary contribution of this paper the utilization of monotone operators to establish convergence. This approach has the added benefit of extending the analysis to a broader class of problems, including variational inequality problems. By formulating the problem as a saddle point problem, the method can effectively tackle non-separable loss functions and regularizers. The paper's significance lies in its provision of the first algorithms with a linear convergence rate for saddle point problems, which is crucial for addressing non-separable loss functions and regularizers prevalent in various machine learning models. The proof technique, which interprets saddle point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator, is intriguing and potentially widely applicable. Additionally, the authors provide simpler proofs for algorithms such as FB splitting (as well as its stochastic version). The proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and, in most cases, outperform previous alternatives. Overall, the paper makes a substantial contribution to the field by extending the analysis in an important direction, and the approach is noteworthy.

However, since the primary contribution of the paper lies in the proof technique (with the algorithms being relatively straightforward extensions of the original algorithms), the authors may consider including more details about the proof technique in the main paper, such as a proof sketch, if space permits. The paper is generally well-presented and clearly written, but there are some areas that require clarification. For instance: 1. The authors could provide more information on the type of non-uniform sampling used for problems other than bilinear saddle-point problems, specifically the definition of π. 2. The paper does not specify how the step size σ is set in the experiments. Furthermore, it would be beneficial to include a discussion on why non-uniform sampling appears to be essential for saddle point problems, but not necessarily for convex minimization problems, and whether the authors have any intuition on this matter.
