- Paper extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems and provides the first linear convergence analysis in this setting.  
- Uses monotone operator theory to enable convergence proofs and broaden applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Discusses practical techniques like "factored" splits and non-uniform sampling that improve efficiency.  
- Proposes a simple Catalyst-based acceleration that is theoretically justified and empirically outperforms standard accelerated batch methods.  
- Original and significant: fills a gap by adapting variance-reduction methods to saddle-point problems relevant in ML.  
- Theoretical soundness and generality: rigorous analysis that extends beyond saddle-points to other monotone problems.  
- Practical relevance: includes implementable enhancements and shows strong empirical performance, especially on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Clarity: well-written, well-motivated, and clearly organized exposition.  
- Main weakness: relies on a strong convexity-concavity assumption (strong in both x and y), which is restrictive for many applications.  
- Experimental scope limited: evaluations on only two classification datasets with one regularizer/loss; broader experiments (robust optimization, convex relaxations) would strengthen claims.
