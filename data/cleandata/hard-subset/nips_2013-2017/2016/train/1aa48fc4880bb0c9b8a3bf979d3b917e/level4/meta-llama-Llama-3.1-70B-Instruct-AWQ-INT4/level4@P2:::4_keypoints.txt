- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with proven linear convergence.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis using monotone operator theory to handle saddle-point complexity.  
- Handles a wide range of saddle-point problems, including non-separable losses and regularizers.  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits: element-wise for SVRG and factored for SAGA.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness of variance reduction with non-uniform sampling over uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results align with theory and show effectiveness on non-separable loss and regularizer cases.  
- Well-written and clearly explains algorithms and analysis.  
- Main novelty: applying stochastic variance reduction to saddle-point problems common in ML and game theory.  
- Assumes known strong convexity–concavity constants, which may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Lacks a clear method for adaptively updating the iterate point (\bar{x}, \bar{y}) to obtain speed-ups.  
- Notational confusion: smooth function K(.) and design matrix K should use different symbols.  
- Suggests exploring extensions to non-strongly convex–concave objectives and broader saddle-point settings.  
- Recommends a more detailed computational complexity analysis of the proposed algorithms.
