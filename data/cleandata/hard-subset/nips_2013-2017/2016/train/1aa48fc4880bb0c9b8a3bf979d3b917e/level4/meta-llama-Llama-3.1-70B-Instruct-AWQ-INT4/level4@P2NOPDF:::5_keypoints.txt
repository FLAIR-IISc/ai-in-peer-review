- Proposes first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems using variance reduction (SVRG, SAGA) and monotone-operator analysis.  
- Monotone-operator approach is the primary novel proof technique, extending beyond convex minimization and applicable to variational inequalities.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers by formulating them as saddle-point problems.  
- Provides simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform prior methods.  
- Paper is generally well-written and makes a substantial contribution by extending analysis to saddle-point/VI settings.  
- Suggests adding a proof sketch or more proof details in the main paper if space allows.  
- Request to clarify the definition of non-uniform sampling (π) for problems beyond bilinear saddle points.  
- Ask to explain how the step size σ was chosen in experiments.  
- Recommend providing intuition why non-uniform sampling seems essential for saddle-point problems but not for convex minimization.
