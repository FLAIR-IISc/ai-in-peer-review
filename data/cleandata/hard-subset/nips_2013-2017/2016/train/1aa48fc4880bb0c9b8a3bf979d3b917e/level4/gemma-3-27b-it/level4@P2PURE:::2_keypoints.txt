- Tackles minimization of convex–concave saddle-point problems by extending recent variance-reduction methods to this setting.  
- Provides theoretical convergence analysis for the proposed algorithms.  
- Shows the methods can be accelerated via an extension of the catalyst framework.  
- Gives extensions of the approach to the monotone operator setting.  
- Includes supporting numerical results validating the methods.  
- Criticism: imposing strong convexity on both primal and dual is overly restrictive.  
- Recommendation: strong convexity should be required on only the primal or the dual (not both).  
- Suggestion: include more comprehensive numerical experiments to strengthen the paper.
