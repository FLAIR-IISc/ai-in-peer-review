- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing the first large-scale algorithms with proven linear convergence for this class.  
- Main algorithmic ideas mirror convex minimization counterparts; core novelty is in the convergence analysis.  
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence proofs, enabling broader applicability (e.g., variational inequalities).  
- Demonstrates practical enhancements: non-uniform sampling and acceleration via the catalyst framework.  
- Empirically, accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling outperforms existing batch and stochastic methods on relevant tasks.  
- Provides simplified convergence proofs for some batch methods (e.g., forward-backward splitting), aiding clarity and pedagogy.  
- Paper would benefit from a proof sketch or more intuition about the monotone operator analysis in the main text.  
- Unclear how non-uniform sampling probabilities (πi) should be chosen beyond the bilinear case; guidance is needed for general saddle-point problems.  
- Experimental reproducibility would improve with details on hyperparameter selection (e.g., step-size σ).  
- Lacks discussion on why non-uniform sampling appears more critical for saddle-point problems than for standard convex minimization.  
- Minor typo: in the "factored" split, the sum index should go up to d (dimension of x), not k (Line 78).  
- Minor typo in proximal update for y: extraneous "yt-1" inside prox argument; should be yt = proxσg(yt−1 + γ−1σ∂yK(xt−1, yt−1)) (Line 121).
