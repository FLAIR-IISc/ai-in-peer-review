This work addresses the problem of minimizing convex-concave saddle point problems and extends recent variance reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide a convergence analysis of the proposed methods and demonstrate that they can be readily accelerated using an extension of the catalyst framework. An extension to monotone operators and supporting numerical results are also presented. However, the strong convexity assumption imposed on both the primal and dual problems is quite restrictive; it would be more common and often sufficient to assume strong convexity for only the primal or dual problem. Furthermore, additional numerical examples would strengthen the empirical evaluation of the proposed methods.
