- Proposes the first linearly convergent incremental gradient algorithms for saddle-point problems.  
- Extends SVRG and SAGA to non-separable losses/regularizers by casting problems as saddle-point formulations.  
- Main technical novelty: using monotone-operator theory to prove convergence, differing from convex minimization analyses.  
- Proof technique (interpreting saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator) is interesting and potentially widely applicable.  
- Algorithms use non-uniform sampling and are competitive, often outperforming previous alternatives.  
- Paper is well-written and provides a clear, thorough explanation of the approach.  
- Main contribution is primarily the proof technique; reviewer suggests adding a proof sketch in the main paper if space allows.  
- Needs a discussion on why non-uniform sampling appears essential for saddle-point problems but not for convex minimization.  
- Must clarify what form of non-uniform sampling is used for non-bilinear saddle-point problems.  
- Should explain how step sizes were chosen in the experiments.  
- Minor typos to fix: line 78 (k -> d) and line 121 (extra y_{t-1} term).  
- Overall scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
