- Paper proposes the first linearly convergent variance-reduced algorithms (SVRG/SAGA) for saddle-point problems.  
- Key innovation: using monotone operator formulation to prove convergence, extending analysis to variational inequality problems.  
- Reformulates saddle-point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator, enabling linear rates for non-separable losses and regularizers.  
- Method effectively handles non-separable loss functions and regularizers common in ML models.  
- Provides simplified proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Proposed algorithms use non-uniform sampling and are competitive or outperform prior methods.  
- Analysis approach is intriguing and likely broadly applicable, expanding the field’s direction.  
- Main contribution is mainly the proof technique (a modest extension of existing algorithms); suggest adding a proof sketch in the main paper.  
- Presentation is generally clear but some clarifications are needed (see below).  
- Clarify what non-uniform sampling is for non-bilinear problems (precise definition of π).  
- State how the step size σ was chosen in experiments and discuss why non-uniform sampling is crucial for saddle-point vs. convex minimization.  
- Minor corrections: line 78 typo ("k" → "d"); line 121 second equality has an extraneous y_{t-1} term.
