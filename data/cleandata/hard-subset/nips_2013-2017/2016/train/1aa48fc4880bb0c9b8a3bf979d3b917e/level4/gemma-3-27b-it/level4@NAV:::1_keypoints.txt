- Problem and contribution: Applies SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to convex-concave saddle-point problems and analyzes them via monotone-operator theory, proving linear convergence.
- Extensions: Introduces an accelerated variant and studies non-uniform sampling.
- Novelty: Significant theoretical contribution with notable breadth, especially extending to non-separable functions.
- Presentation weakness: Overall clarity is lacking; the connection to the monotone-operator framework should be emphasized earlier.
- Experiments: Evaluation is limited (only two datasets); broader empirical validation is needed.
- Baseline comparison: For separable functions, should compare to accelerated proximal algorithms (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock).
- Assumptions clarity: Need discussion of the strength and implications of assumptions A–C, particularly assumption (A) and its applicability to problems like SVMs.
- Method differentiation: Require a clearer explanation of how the proposed method differs from existing stochastic primal–dual approaches.
- Theorem 2: The constant μ needs further elaboration—clarify its relation to the monotonicity constant in the appendix and compare Theorem 2’s convergence rate to Theorem 1’s.
- Strong monotonicity: Clarify the transition from assumptions A–C to strong monotonicity and whether A–C imply strong monotonicity.
- Notational issue: Inconsistent use of L (sometimes condition number, sometimes Lipschitz constant) should be fixed.
- Missing reference: Include a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
