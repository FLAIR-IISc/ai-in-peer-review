- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Provides first linear-convergence analysis for these variance-reduced methods in the saddle-point setting.  
- Frames analysis using monotone operator theory, extending applicability to variational inequalities beyond saddle points.  
- Demonstrates relevance to ML problems with non-separable losses and regularizers (e.g., structured output prediction).  
- Examines two gradient splitting strategies (element-wise and factored) and analyzes their storage and computational trade-offs.  
- Shows importance of non-uniform sampling for theoretical efficiency and empirical performance.  
- Empirical results validate theoretical findings and show practical performance benefits.  
- Well-motivated and builds on recent advances in stochastic convex optimization.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation that is accessible to a broad audience.  
- Lacks practical guidance on adaptively updating the additional regularization parameter/center (bar{x}, bar{y}) used for acceleration.  
- Claims SAGA acceleration should hold but omits explicit theoretical proof for SAGA acceleration.  
- Notational ambiguity: uses 'K' for both a general function and the design matrix, which can confuse readers.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution with rigorous analysis and convincing experiments; recommended for acceptance.  
- Minor weaknesses (adaptive tuning detail, missing SAGA proof, notation) could be addressed without undermining the main contributions.
