- Paper develops SVRG/SAGA with proximal operators for convex-concave saddle-point problems, proving linear convergence in a monotone-operator framework and proposing an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.  
- Major novelty: applicability to non-separable objective functions, a notable advance over prior work.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and provides novel insights into saddle-point optimization.  
- Main weakness: presentation—monotone operator theory is not introduced early, causing a disconnect between Sections 2–4 and the extension in Section 6.  
- Recommendation to explicitly frame the problem within monotone operator theory early (e.g., in Section 2) to align presentation with the analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to two datasets and insufficient to demonstrate robustness; add at least one additional (larger/diverse) benchmark.  
- For separable objectives, empirical comparisons are incomplete; include stochastic Chambolle–Pock (Zhang et al., JMLR 2015) and cite it in related work.  
- Technical: Assumptions A–C (particularly A) need justification and should be checked on standard cases (e.g., SVM saddle-point) to validate practicality.  
- Technical: Clarify relationship to existing stochastic primal–dual methods (e.g., Chen et al., ICML 2015) with a concise comparison of convergence guarantees, assumptions, and applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Technical: Explicitly define the constant μ (monotonicity constant) and clarify how Theorem 2’s rate compares to Theorem 1, including the role of strong convexity vs strong monotonicity.  
- Technical: Clarify whether Assumptions A–C imply strong monotonicity or if strong monotonicity is an additional requirement—add a lemma or explicit statement.  
- Minor: Notational inconsistency—“L” is ambiguously used for condition number and Lipschitz constant; use κ for condition number and L for Lipschitz.  
- Minor: Add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm (e.g., Combettes & Pesquet, 2011).  
- Overall recommendation: Major Revision—resubmit after restructuring to integrate monotone operator theory early, expanding experiments to ≥3 datasets with Zhang et al. (2015) comparison, and clarifying assumptions, μ, L, and notation.
