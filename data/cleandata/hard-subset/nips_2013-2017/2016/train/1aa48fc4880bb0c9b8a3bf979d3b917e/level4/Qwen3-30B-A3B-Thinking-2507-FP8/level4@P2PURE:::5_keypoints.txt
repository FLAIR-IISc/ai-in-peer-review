- Authors apply variance reduction (SVRG, SAGA) to obtain the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems.  
- Standard convex minimization analyses don’t extend; primary novelty is using monotone operator theory to prove convergence.  
- Monotone-operator framework broadens applicability to variational inequality problems.  
- Framework allows handling non-separable losses and regularizers by reformulating them as saddle point problems.  
- Proposed algorithms achieve linear convergence rates for saddle point problems, a significant advance.  
- Proof technique—viewing saddle points as zeros of monotone operators—is novel and broadly useful.  
- Paper provides simpler proofs for forward-backward splitting (including stochastic variants).  
- Non-uniform sampling variants perform competitively in experiments and often outperform prior methods.  
- Main novelty is in the proof/analysis rather than algorithmic design; recommend adding a proof sketch in the main text for accessibility.  
- Requests clarification: exact form of non-uniform sampling distribution π for non-bilinear saddle point problems.  
- Requests clarification: how step size σ was determined in experiments and why non-uniform sampling is crucial for saddle points but less so for convex minimization.
