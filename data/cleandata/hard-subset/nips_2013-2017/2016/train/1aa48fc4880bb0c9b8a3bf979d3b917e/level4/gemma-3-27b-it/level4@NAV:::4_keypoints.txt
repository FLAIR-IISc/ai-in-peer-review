- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with a proof of linear convergence.  
- Provides both theoretical analysis and empirical validation showing variance reduction is effective for saddle-point problems.  
- Algorithms handle non-separable saddle-point problems, including non-separable losses (e.g., structured output) and regularizers (e.g., fusion terms).  
- Convergence analysis relies on monotone operator theory, a notable departure from prior convex-only analyses.  
- Analysis indicates applicability beyond saddle-point problems to broader classes like variational inequalities relevant to game theory.  
- Introduces and analyzes two gradient operator splitting strategies—element-wise and factored—within the stochastic forward-backward framework.  
- Shows theoretically and empirically that non-uniform sampling yields better variance-reduction performance than uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results in the paper and appendix robustly corroborate theoretical claims and demonstrate method efficacy on non-separable losses and regularizers.  
- Minor: Acceleration technique in Section 5 (extra regularization inspired by [8]) lacks clarity on adaptive updating of the reference point (\bar{x}, \bar{y}).  
- Minor: Convergence/acceleration proof is provided for SVRG, but it’s unclear whether similar guarantees hold for SAGA.  
- Minor: Suggest clarifying notation to avoid confusion between the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K.
