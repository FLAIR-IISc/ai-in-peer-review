- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing first linearly convergent algorithms for large-scale instances.  
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence analysis, a departure from standard convex minimization proofs.  
- Analysis shows methods apply to a broader class including variational inequalities.  
- Addresses saddle-point–specific challenges: need for convex–concave splits, importance of non-uniform sampling, and two gradient operator splits (element-wise and factored).  
- Strong technical contribution with careful, rigorous theoretical analysis.  
- Algorithms cover bilinear saddle-point problems and more general convex–concave cases (e.g., non-separable losses).  
- Experiments show non-uniform sampling improves performance over uniform sampling.  
- Empirical results: proposed methods outperform existing batch and stochastic approaches, especially on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Experimental validation includes both synthetic and real-world benchmarks across separable and non-separable problems.  
- Fills a literature gap by extending variance reduction techniques to saddle-point problems with theoretical and empirical support.  
- Practical relevance: applications to structured output prediction, grouped norms, total variation, etc.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly presents problem, algorithms, and results.  
- Strengths: novelty, rigorous monotone-operator analysis, broad applicability, strong experiments.  
- Weaknesses: notational confusion using K for two meanings; unclear if acceleration extends to SAGA; adaptive update mechanism for auxiliary iterate (x̄,ȳ) needs clarification.  
- Recommendation: accept.
