This paper extends the stochastic variance reduction techniques SVRG and SAGA to deal with strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems, where linear convergence is proved. The proposed algorithms can apply to a number of non-separable saddle-point problems, where both the loss (e.g., structured output prediction) and regularizer (e.g., fusion term) can be non-separable.

The paper provides a thorough analysis of the algorithms, including their convergence rates and computational complexities. The authors also provide experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the variance reduction method on the saddle-point problems.

The main contribution of the paper is the extension of the stochastic variance reduction techniques to saddle-point problems. The authors adopt the monotone operators to prove the convergence, which is more complex than the previous convergence analysis on convex problems. The analysis also shows that the extended SVRG and SAGA algorithms apply to a wider class of problems, such as the variational inequality in game theory.

The paper considers a stochastic approach and proposes two different splits of the gradient operator, i.e., the element-wise split and factored split, for the SVRG and SAGA respectively. The variance reduction works on the stochastic forward-backward algorithms, which are commonly used for saddle-point problems. Non-uniform sampling is also considered, and both theoretical and empirical results show that variance reduction with non-uniform sampling is superior to that with uniform sampling.

Experimental results in both the main article and the appendix provide sufficient evidence of the theoretical results and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods on both non-separable loss and regularizer.
