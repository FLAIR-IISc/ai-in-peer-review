- Adapts SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to solve convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Analyzes these algorithms via monotone-operator framework and proves linear convergence.  
- Proposes an accelerated variant and examines a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Contributions are substantial with thorough theoretical analysis and extension to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation is unclear; the connection to monotone operators should be foregrounded rather than deferred to Section 6.  
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets) and need broader empirical validation.  
- Missing comparison to accelerated stochastic methods for separable functions (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock, Zhang et al. 2015).  
- Need clarification on the strength and applicability of assumptions (A)–(C), especially whether (A) holds for SVM-induced saddle points.  
- The distinction between proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual methods is not clearly explained.  
- Theorem 2’s constant μ is not clearly defined—clarify if it is the monotonicity constant—and compare Theorems 1 and 2.  
- The transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing; state whether (A)–(C) imply monotonicity.  
- Minor: inconsistent use of L as both condition number and Lipschitz constant—reconcile terminology.  
- Minor: Forward–Backward algorithm is mentioned without citation—add an appropriate reference.
