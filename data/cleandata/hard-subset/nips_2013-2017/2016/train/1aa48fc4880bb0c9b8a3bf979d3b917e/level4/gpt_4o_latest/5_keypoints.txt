- Authors use SVRG/SAGA to obtain the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems.  
- Main novelty is leveraging monotone-operator theory to prove convergence, extending beyond standard convex minimization.  
- Approach applies to a broader class including variational inequality problems.  
- Method can handle non-separable loss functions and regularizers common in ML.  
- Proof strategy (viewing saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator) is innovative and broadly applicable.  
- Paper provides simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Algorithms with non-uniform sampling show competitive or superior empirical performance.  
- Primary novelty is the proof technique rather than algorithmic design; consider adding a proof sketch in the main paper.  
- Clarify which non-uniform sampling distribution π is used for non-bilinear problems.  
- Report how the step size σ was chosen in the experiments.  
- Discuss why non-uniform sampling seems critical for saddle-point problems but not for convex minimization.
