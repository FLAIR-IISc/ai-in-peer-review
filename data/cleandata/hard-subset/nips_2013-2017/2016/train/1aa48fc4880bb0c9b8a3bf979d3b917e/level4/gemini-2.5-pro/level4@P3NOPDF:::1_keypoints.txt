- Adapts SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator for large-scale convex-concave saddle-point problems, analyzed via monotone operator theory with linear convergence proofs.  
- Extends the methods to include an accelerated variant and analyzes a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Novelty: strong theoretical contributions, especially extending applicability to non-separable objective functions.  
- Presentation/organization: monotone operator framework is introduced too late (Section 6); it should appear earlier to motivate the analysis and algorithms.  
- Experiments: empirical validation is weak—results on only two datasets are insufficient to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock / Zhang & Xiao 2015) for separable problems.  
- Assumptions: clarify how restrictive assumptions (A)–(C) are and whether standard examples (e.g., SVM saddle-point) satisfy them.  
- Positioning: explain key differences between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal-dual methods.  
- Constant μ: clarify what μ denotes (is it the strong monotonicity constant?) and directly compare the convergence rates in Theorems 1 and 2.  
- Logical implication: clarify whether (A)–(C) directly imply strong monotonicity and make the appendix transition clearer.  
- Notation: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs Lipschitz constant)—standardize notation.  
- Reference: add a citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm at first mention.
