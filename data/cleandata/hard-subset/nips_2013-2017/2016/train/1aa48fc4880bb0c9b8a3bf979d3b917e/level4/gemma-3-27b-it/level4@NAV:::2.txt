This paper addresses the challenging problem of minimizing convex-concave saddle points, building upon recent advances in variance reduction techniques. The authors provide a convergence analysis for their proposed methods and demonstrate their compatibility with, and acceleration via, a catalyst-inspired framework. Furthermore, they extend the methods to handle monotone operators and support their theoretical findings with numerical results. However, the requirement of strong convexity for *both* the primal and dual problems appears overly restrictive; strong convexity should reasonably be assumed for either the primal *or* the dual problem alone. The manuscript would benefit from the inclusion of additional numerical examples to further validate the proposed approaches.
