- Studies variance-reduced methods for convex-concave saddle-point problems and provides convergence analysis.  
- Shows the proposed methods can be accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework.  
- Includes extensions to monotone operators and presents numerical results.  
- Major concern: assuming strong convexity on both primal and dual problems is overly restrictive.  
- Suggests it would be more reasonable to assume strong convexity on either the primal or the dual, not necessarily both.  
- Recommends additional numerical experiments to better demonstrate effectiveness and robustness.
