- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Shows theoretical and empirical effectiveness of variance reduction in the saddle-point setting.  
- Key contribution: handles non-separable saddle-point problems (non-separable losses and regularizers).  
- Leverages monotone operator theory for convergence proofs, broadening applicability to variational inequalities and game-theory problems.  
- Develops two gradient operator splitting strategies: element-wise splits for SVRG and factored splits for SAGA within stochastic forward–backward schemes.  
- Analyzes and empirically demonstrates that non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Experiments (main + supplement) strongly support theoretical claims and practical performance on non-separable problems.  
- Clarification needed: how the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) is adaptively updated in Section 5 to maximize speed-up.  
- The convergence/acceleration proof is provided for SVRG only; it’s unclear whether analogous results hold for SAGA.  
- Suggestion: use distinct notation for the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K to avoid confusion.
