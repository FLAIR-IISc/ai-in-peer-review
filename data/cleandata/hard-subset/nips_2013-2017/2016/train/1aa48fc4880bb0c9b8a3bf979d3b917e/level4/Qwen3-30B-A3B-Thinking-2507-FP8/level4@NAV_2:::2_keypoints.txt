- Introduces first large-scale, linearly convergent SVRG/SAGA algorithms for convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- Novel convergence analysis based on monotone operator theory, extending applicability to variational inequalities beyond saddle-points.  
- Identifies two practical splitting approaches (function-wise and derivative-wise) important for implementation.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling is theoretically and empirically essential for efficiency.  
- Demonstrates acceleration via a simple Catalyst extension, yielding consistently better performance than accelerated batch methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound with thorough proofs addressing saddle-point-specific challenges.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, effectively presenting contributions.  
- Concern: analysis assumes strong convexity in primal AND strong concavity in dual, which is stronger than typical minimal conditions and needs clarification.  
- Experiments show clear practical benefits, especially on ill-conditioned problems, but would be stronger with more diverse ML problem evaluations.  
- Overall: high-quality, meaningful contribution suitable for the conference pending minor clarification on assumptions.
