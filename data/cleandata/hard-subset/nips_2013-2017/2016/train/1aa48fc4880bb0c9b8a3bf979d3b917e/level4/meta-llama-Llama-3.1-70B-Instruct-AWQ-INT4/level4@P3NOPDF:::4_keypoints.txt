- Proposes extensions of SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Provides both theoretical and empirical evidence showing effectiveness of the proposed variance-reduction methods.  
- Algorithms handle a wide class of non-separable saddle-point problems where loss and regularizer can be non-separable.  
- Convergence analysis is nontrivial: uses monotone-operator framework to prove convergence, extending beyond standard convex analyses.  
- Shows applicability to broader problems including variational inequalities and game-theoretic settings.  
- Introduces two gradient splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA, used with stochastic forward-backward algorithms.  
- Studies non-uniform sampling and demonstrates both theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results in the paper and appendix substantiate theoretical findings on non-separable loss and regularizer cases.  
- Minor concern: the adaptive update strategy for the accelerated iterate (\bar{x},\bar{y}) in Section 5 is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Minor concern: convergence and acceleration proof is provided for SVRG but it should be confirmed whether the same holds for SAGA.  
- Minor suggestion: distinguish notation between the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K to avoid confusion.
