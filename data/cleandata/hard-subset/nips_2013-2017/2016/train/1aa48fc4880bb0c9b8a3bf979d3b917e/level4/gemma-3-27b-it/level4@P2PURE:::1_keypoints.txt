- Paper applies SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to convex–concave saddle-point problems, analyzes them via monotone-operator framework, and proves linear convergence; also introduces an accelerated variant and non-uniform sampling.  
- Contributions are substantial: thorough theoretical analysis and several extensions, notably applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Technical strength is high, but presentation needs improvement—especially clearer exposition of the connection to monotone operators (currently treated as an extension in Sec. 6).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets); more datasets are needed.  
- For separable problems, include comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock methods (Zhang & Peyré, 2015).  
- Clarify the strength and scope of assumptions (A)–(C), particularly whether (A) holds for SVM-derived saddle-point problems.  
- Explain differences between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual approaches.  
- In Theorem 2, clarify the constant μ (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and compare the result to Theorem 1.  
- Explain how assumptions (A)–(C) relate to (or imply) strong monotonicity; the transition in the appendix is unclear.  
- Minor issues: ambiguity in the use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and add a reference to the Forward–Backward algorithm.
