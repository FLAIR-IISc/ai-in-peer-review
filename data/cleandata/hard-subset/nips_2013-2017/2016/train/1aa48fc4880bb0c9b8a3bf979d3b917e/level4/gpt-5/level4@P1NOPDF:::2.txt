This paper addresses convex–concave saddle-point optimization and extends recent variance-reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide convergence analyses for the proposed methods and show that they can be readily accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. They also present extensions to monotone operators and include numerical results.

However, the assumption of strong convexity on both the primal and dual problems is very restrictive. Typically, it is sufficient for either the primal or the dual problem to be strongly convex. The paper would be stronger if the methods and guarantees were developed under this weaker and more standard assumption, or if the authors clarified how their results change when only one side is strongly convex.

In addition, the experimental section is limited. More numerical examples should be included to better substantiate the claims, ideally across a broader range of problem instances and datasets, with more comprehensive comparisons to relevant baselines.
