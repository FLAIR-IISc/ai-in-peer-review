- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems, addressing non-separable losses/regularizers.
- Proposes SVRG- and SAGA-style algorithms tailored for saddle-point problems.
- Provides the first linear convergence analysis for these methods using monotone operator theory instead of potential-function arguments.
- Generalizes convergence results to broader problems like variational inequalities.
- Analyzes different decomposition strategies and emphasizes the practical and theoretical importance of non-uniform sampling.
- Demonstrates that the new algorithms can be accelerated using a catalyst-like framework.
- High technical quality: rigorous theoretical analysis with proofs supporting linear convergence.
- Use of monotone operator theory is a well-chosen and appropriate analytical tool for saddle-point structure.
- Presented as a complete contribution covering algorithm design, theoretical guarantees, acceleration, and experiments; assumptions (e.g., strong convexity–concavity) are clearly stated.
- Very well written and organized with clear motivation, formulation, and algorithm descriptions.
- Insightful discussion on sampling schemes and their effect on complexity bounds.
- Highly original: first successful linear-convergence analysis for composite saddle-point setting and novel extension of variance-reduction analysis to monotone operators.
- Significant potential impact for ML problems with structured non-separable penalties, robust optimization, and structured prediction.
- Strengths: novelty and impact, strong theoretical foundation, practical relevance, and strong empirical performance (especially accelerated SVRG).
- Weakness: relies on strong convexity–concavity assumption; lacks detailed discussion on extending results or obtaining sublinear rates when concavity is not strong.
- Weakness: experimental scope is limited; should include additional distinct problem classes to validate general applicability.
