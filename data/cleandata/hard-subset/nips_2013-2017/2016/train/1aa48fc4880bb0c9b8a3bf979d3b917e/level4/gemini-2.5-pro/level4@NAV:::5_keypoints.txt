- Presents first variance-reduction (SVRG/SAGA) algorithms with linear convergence for saddle point problems.
- Central novelty is a new convergence proof using monotone operator theory rather than convex minimization analysis.
- Proof technique is necessary and enables applicability to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities.
- Useful for ML models with non-separable losses/regularizers by framing them as zeros of a monotone operator.
- Paper also yields simpler proofs for existing methods (Forward–Backward splitting and its stochastic version).
- Empirical results show the proposed algorithms, especially with non-uniform sampling, are competitive and often superior to prior methods.
- Core innovation is mainly the proof technique; paper impact would increase by elaborating this contribution (e.g., add a proof sketch in the main text).
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.
- Clarification needed: definition of non-uniform sampling distribution π beyond the bilinear case.
- Clarification needed: how the step size σ was chosen in the experiments.
- Suggest adding intuition on why non-uniform sampling is particularly important for saddle point problems versus convex minimization.
- Minor fixes: Line 78 typo ("k" should likely be "d"); Line 121 appears to have an extraneous y_{t-1} term.
