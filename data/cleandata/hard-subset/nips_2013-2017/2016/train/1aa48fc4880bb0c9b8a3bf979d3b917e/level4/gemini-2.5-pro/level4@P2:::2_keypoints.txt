- Extends SVR methods (SVRG, SAGA) to finite-sum convex-concave saddle-point problems, enabling variance reduction for non-separable losses/regularizers.  
- Provides the first linear convergence analysis for SVR-type algorithms in the saddle-point context.  
- Analysis uses monotone operator theory, broadening applicability to variational inequalities beyond standard convex minimization.  
- Incorporates non-uniform sampling, shown to be important for efficiency.  
- Integrates a catalyst-style acceleration scheme, yielding theoretically faster convergence than accelerated batch methods.  
- Experiments on binary classification with non-separable objectives demonstrate practical gains, especially for the accelerated variant.  
- Strength: novel and significant contribution that makes powerful optimization tools available for broader ML problems (structured outputs, robust optimization, relaxations).  
- Strength: strong technical quality and rigorous, non-trivial proofs using monotone operator framework.  
- Strength: clear exposition and good motivation; explains different problem splits and sampling choices well.  
- Weakness: relies on strong convexity in x and strong concavity in y, limiting applicability to problems lacking dual strong concavity.  
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is narrow (only supervised classification); additional tasks (e.g., robust optimization) would strengthen claims of broad applicability.  
- Overall: high-quality, timely work likely to inspire follow-up research despite assumptions and limited experimental scope.
