- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence.  
- Theoretical and empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of variance reduction for saddle-point problems.  
- Algorithms apply to non-separable saddle-point problems where both loss and regularizer can be non-separable.  
- Convergence analysis uses monotone operator theory, addressing greater complexity than standard convex analysis.  
- Analysis shows applicability to a broader class including variational inequalities and game-theoretic problems.  
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
- Variance reduction is applied to stochastic forward-backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems.  
- Non-uniform sampling is studied and shown (theoretically and empirically) to outperform uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results (main text and appendix) substantiate theoretical claims and demonstrate practical effectiveness.  
- Minor concern: Section 5 adds a regularization term to push updates toward an iterate (\bar{x},\bar{y}), but how to adaptively update this iterate for acceleration is unclear.  
- Acceleration and convergence are proved for SVRG, but it is unclear whether the same results hold for SAGA.  
- Notational issue: the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K should use distinct symbols to avoid confusion.
