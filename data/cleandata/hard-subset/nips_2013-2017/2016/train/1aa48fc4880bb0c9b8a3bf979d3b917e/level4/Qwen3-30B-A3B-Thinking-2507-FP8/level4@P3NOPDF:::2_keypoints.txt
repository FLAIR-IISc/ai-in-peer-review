- Contribution: extends variance reduction techniques to convex-concave saddle point problems, gives convergence analysis, Catalyst acceleration, and an extension to monotone operator inclusions.  
- Major technical flaw: assumes both primal and dual are strongly convex, which is unnecessarily restrictive.  
- Theory impact: two-sided strong convexity limits applicability and may invalidate derived convergence rates for common problems with only one-sided strong convexity (e.g., robust optimization, GANs).  
- Fix required: either relax to one-sided strong convexity with adapted proofs or provide a convincing justification for the two-sided assumption.  
- Empirics insufficient: numerical experiments are too few and do not test cases with only primal or only dual strong convexity.  
- Suggested benchmarks: include logistic regression with adversarial noise, matrix games, and other non-strongly convex minimax problems to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Catalyst extension: well-executed, but the paper should better explain why Catalyst is suitable (e.g., connection to minimizing a composite objective with a smooth term).  
- Monotone operator extension: technically sound but needs a concise explanation of how it generalizes beyond saddle points and potential applications (distributed optimization, online learning, variational inequalities).  
- Presentation: generally clear but contains minor grammatical errors that should be corrected.  
- Recommendation: major revision to address the strong convexity oversight and expand empirical validation before acceptance.
