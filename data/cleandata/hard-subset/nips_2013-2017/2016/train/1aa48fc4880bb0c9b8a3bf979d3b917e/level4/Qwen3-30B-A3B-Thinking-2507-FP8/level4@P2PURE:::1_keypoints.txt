- Paper integrates SVRG/SAGA with proximal operators for convex-concave saddle-point problems and analyzes them in the monotone operator framework, showing linear convergence.
- Authors propose an accelerated variant and study non-uniform sampling.
- Main novelty: significant theoretical contributions, notably extending applicability to non-separable objective functions.
- Technical strength: thorough theoretical analysis, but presentation needs improvement.
- The monotone-operator connection is introduced too late (Section 6) despite being central to the analysis.
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets); more datasets would strengthen empirical claims.
- For separable cases, the paper must compare to accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock, Zhang et al. 2015) and add that reference.
- Clarification needed on assumptions A–C, especially how strong assumption (A) is and whether it holds for SVM-induced saddle-point problems.
- Clarify the distinction between the stochastic primal–dual method and the proposed variance-reduced approach.
- In Theorem 2, specify the constant μ (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and explain how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1.
- Explain the transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix; do (A)–(C) imply monotonicity?
- Minor issue: ambiguous use of L—sometimes called condition number, other times Lipschitz constant—needs consistent definition.
- Minor issue: provide a proper reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
