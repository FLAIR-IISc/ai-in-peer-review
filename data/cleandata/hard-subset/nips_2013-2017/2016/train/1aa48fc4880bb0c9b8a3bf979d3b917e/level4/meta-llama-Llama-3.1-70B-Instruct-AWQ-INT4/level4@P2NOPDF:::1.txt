This paper explores saddle-point problems with a convex-concave property, demonstrating how existing stochastic variance reduced techniques, such as SVRG and SAGA, can be combined with a proximal operator to address these problems. The authors analyze these algorithms in the monotone operator setting, establishing linear convergence, and propose an accelerated variant as well as a non-uniform sampling scheme.

The contributions of this paper are significant, offering thorough theoretical analysis that reveals new insights for saddle-point problems. A notable extension is the applicability of their method to non-separable functions. However, the presentation of the paper could be improved by making the connection to monotone operators more explicit. Although this connection appears as an extension in Section 6, the analysis relies heavily on monotone operators.

From an experimental perspective, it would be beneficial to include results on more than two datasets. For separable functions, comparisons with accelerated methods, such as the stochastic variant of the Chambolle-Pock algorithm (Zhang et al., 2015), would be valuable. The authors should consider adding this reference to their submission.

Several points require clarification from the authors:

1. The strength of assumptions A-C, particularly assumption (A), should be discussed. For instance, do these assumptions hold for solving the saddle-point problem induced by SVM?
2. The authors should elaborate on the differences between stochastic primal-dual methods and their suggested method, as these are not entirely clear.
3. The constant μ in Theorem 2 is not well-defined. Is it the monotonicity constant mentioned in the appendix? How can this result be compared to the result of Theorem 1?
4. The transition from assumptions (A)-(C) to strong monotonicity (in the appendix) is unclear. Do assumptions (A)-(C) imply monotonicity?

Minor issues include the inconsistent use of the constant L, which is referred to as both the condition number and the Lipschitz constant. Additionally, a reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm should be provided.

Overall, the paper presents significant theoretical contributions, but the presentation and experimental evaluation could be improved with further clarification and additional results.
