This work considers the problem of reducing convex-concave saddle problems and extends recent gradient descent methods to solve them. Convergence analysis of the proposed methods is provided. The authors show that the methods can be easily accelerated using catalyst structure expansion. Expansion of the monotonic operator and numerical results are also provided. Strong convex assumptions are strong enough for both primal and dual problems. Only the primal problem or dual problem should be strong convex. More numerical examples should be added.
