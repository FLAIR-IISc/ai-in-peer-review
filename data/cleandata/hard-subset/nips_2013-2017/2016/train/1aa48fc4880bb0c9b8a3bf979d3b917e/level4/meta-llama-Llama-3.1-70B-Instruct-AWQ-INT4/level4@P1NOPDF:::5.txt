The authors propose the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems by leveraging variance reduction algorithms such as SVRG and SAGA. Notably, the conventional convex minimization analysis does not apply to saddle point problems, making the paper's primary contribution the use of monotone operators to establish convergence. This approach has the added benefit of generalizing to a broader class of problems, including variational inequality problems. By formulating the problem as a saddle point problem, this method can effectively handle non-separable loss functions and regularizers, which are common in numerous machine learning models. The paper's significance lies in its provision of the first algorithms with a linear convergence rate for saddle point problems, a crucial problem that enables the tackling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers.

The proof technique, which interprets saddle point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator, is intriguing and potentially widely applicable. Furthermore, the paper provides simpler proofs for algorithms such as FB splitting (as well as its stochastic version). The proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling demonstrate competitiveness and, in most cases, outperform previous alternatives. Overall, the paper extends the field in an important direction, and its analysis approach is compelling.

Since the primary contribution of the paper lies in the proof technique (the algorithms themselves being relatively simple extensions of the original algorithms), the authors may consider providing additional details about the proof technique, such as a proof sketch, in the main paper, if space permits. The paper is generally well-presented and clearly written. However, I encountered some unclear points, including:

1. The type of non-uniform sampling used for problems other than bilinear saddle-point problems, specifically the definition of π.
2. The method for setting the step size σ in the experiments. Additionally, the authors may want to provide insight into why non-uniform sampling appears to be essential for saddle point problems, but not necessarily for convex minimization problems. A discussion on this topic could enhance the paper.
