- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, achieving linear convergence.
- Applicable to non-separable loss and regularizer functions, broadening practical use (e.g., structured prediction, fusion terms).
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence analysis, addressing more complex saddle-point settings than convex cases.
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Integrates variance reduction into stochastic forward–backward algorithms for saddle-point problems.
- Studies non-uniform sampling and shows theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experimental results (main paper and appendix) support the theoretical findings and demonstrate method effectiveness.
- Significant contribution: extends variance-reduction methods to variational inequalities and game-theory settings.
- Minor concern: unclear how to adaptively update the iterate (x̄, ȳ) for the acceleration in Section 5.
- Minor concern: convergence and acceleration proof is given for SVRG but it is unclear if analogous results hold for SAGA.
- Minor suggestion: avoid notation confusion by using different symbols for the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K.
