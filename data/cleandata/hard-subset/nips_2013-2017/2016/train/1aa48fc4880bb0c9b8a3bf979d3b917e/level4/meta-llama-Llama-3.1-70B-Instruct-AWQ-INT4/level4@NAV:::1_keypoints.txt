- Proposes combining SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator to solve convex–concave saddle-point problems under a monotone operator framework, proving linear convergence and giving an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.  
- Strong novelty: thorough theoretical analysis and a key extension to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation needs clearer explanation of the connection to monotone operators, especially within the analysis.  
- Experiments are limited; add more datasets and, for separable functions, compare with accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock).  
- Clarify the strength and applicability of assumptions A–C, particularly assumption (A), and whether they cover the SVM-induced saddle-point.  
- Explain the distinction between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual methods.  
- Clarify the role of the constant μ in Theorem 2 and how it relates to the monotonicity constant in the appendix; provide a comparison between Theorems 1 and 2.  
- The transition in the appendix from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing—state explicitly whether those assumptions imply monotonicity.  
- Minor: L is used inconsistently (both as condition number and Lipschitz constant)—resolve notation.  
- Minor: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
