- Novel contribution: extends variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) to saddle-point problems, bridging a gap between convex optimization tools and saddle-point applications.  
- Main result: proposes algorithms for strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Theoretical approach: leverages monotone operator theory for rigorous convergence analysis and potential extension to variational inequalities.  
- Applicability: handles non-separable losses and regularizers with two gradient-splitting strategies (element-wise and factored).  
- Sampling: introduces and analyzes non-uniform sampling, showing theoretical and empirical improvements over uniform sampling.  
- Empirical validation: thorough experiments (main text and appendix) that convincingly support theoretical claims and outperform baselines.  
- Acceleration question: acceleration scheme (adding regularization toward a reference point) lacks clear practical guidance on how to adaptively update the reference point.  
- SAGA acceleration: unclear whether the provided acceleration/convergence proof for SVRG extends to SAGA; discussion or proof is requested.  
- Notation issue: ambiguity in using K for both a smooth function and a design matrix—recommend distinct symbols.
