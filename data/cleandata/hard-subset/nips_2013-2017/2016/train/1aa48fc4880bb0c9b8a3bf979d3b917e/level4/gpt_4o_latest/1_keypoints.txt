- Paper studies convex-concave saddle-point problems using SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator, analyzed via monotone operators, proving linear convergence and proposing an accelerated variant and non-uniform sampling.  
- Contributions are substantial with rigorous theoretical analysis and notable extension to non-separable functions.  
- Exposition is unclear; the connection to monotone operators should be made more explicit since the analysis relies on it.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets) and lacks comparisons to accelerated methods for separable functions (e.g., stochastic Chambolle-Pock).  
- Reviewer recommends adding the stochastic Chambolle-Pock reference (Zhang & Xiao 2015).  
- Clarification needed: how restrictive are assumptions A–C, especially (A); do they hold for SVM saddle-point formulations?  
- Clarification needed: distinction between the stochastic primal-dual method and the proposed approach.  
- Clarification needed: ambiguity of constant μ in Theorem 2 (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1.  
- Clarification needed: the transition in the appendix from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing—do A–C imply monotonicity?  
- Minor issue: inconsistent use of L (referred to both as condition number and as Lipschitz constant).  
- Minor issue: include a reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm.
