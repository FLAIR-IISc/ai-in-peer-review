- Proposes the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for convex-concave saddle-point problems by extending SVRG and SAGA.
- Provides a thorough analysis in the monotone operator setting and proves linear convergence.
- Introduces a novel accelerated variant and a non-uniform sampling scheme.
- Significantly advances handling of non-separable losses and regularizers.
- Well-structured paper with clear writing and detailed technical explanations.
- Empirical validation on two datasets demonstrates effectiveness compared to batch and stochastic baselines.
- High-quality analysis with detailed comparisons to existing methods.
- Novelty is strong: extension of SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point problems and new algorithmic variants.
- Clarify strength and role of assumptions A–C, particularly assumption (A).
- Explain differences between stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed approach more clearly.
- Clarify the transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix.
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contributions, solid analysis, effective algorithms, and novel extensions.
- Arguments against acceptance: need clarifications on assumptions, method comparisons, appendix transition, and minor notation/references.
- Overall rating 9/10 with recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
