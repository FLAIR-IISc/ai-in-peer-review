This paper extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence. The proposed methods handle non-separable saddle-point formulations in which both the loss (e.g., structured output prediction) and the regularizer (e.g., fusion terms) are non-separable. A key contribution is the convergence analysis: unlike prior work on purely convex problems, the saddle-point setting is substantially more complex, and the authors use monotone operator theory to establish convergence. The analysis further shows that the extended SVRG and SAGA algorithms apply to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities in game theory. From a stochastic standpoint, the paper proposes two ways to split the gradient operator—an element-wise split and a factored split—for SVRG and SAGA, respectively. Variance reduction is incorporated into stochastic forward–backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems. Non-uniform sampling is also considered, and both theory and experiments indicate that it outperforms uniform sampling. Experimental results in the main paper and the appendix provide strong evidence for the theory and demonstrate effectiveness on problems with non-separable losses and regularizers.

Minor comments:
- In Section 5 on acceleration, following [8], an additional regularization term is introduced to pull the update toward a reference iterate. It is unclear how to adaptively update this reference point, (x̄, ȳ), to achieve speed-ups.
- Convergence and acceleration guarantees are provided for SVRG. Do analogous results hold for SAGA?
- The smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should be denoted with different symbols to avoid confusion.
