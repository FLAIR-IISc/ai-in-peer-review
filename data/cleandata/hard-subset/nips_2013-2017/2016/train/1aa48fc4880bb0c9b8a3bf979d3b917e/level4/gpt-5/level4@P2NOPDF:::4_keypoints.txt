- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Handles non-separable losses and regularizers (e.g., structured output prediction, fusion penalties).  
- Uses monotone operator theory to analyze stochastic forward–backward splitting with variance reduction, beyond standard convex optimization techniques.  
- Analysis indicates applicability to a broader class of problems, including certain variational inequalities in game theory.  
- Introduces two stochastic gradient decompositions: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA, integrating variance reduction into forward–backward schemes.  
- Considers non-uniform sampling; both theory and experiments show well-chosen non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Empirical results (main paper + appendix) substantiate theoretical claims and show strong performance on non-separable problems.  
- Acceleration: unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (\bar{x},\bar{y}); a principled update rule or practical guidance is needed.  
- Theory coverage: convergence and acceleration guarantees are given for SVRG; it’s unclear whether analogous results hold for SAGA—clarification or partial guarantees required.  
- Notation: using K(·) for a smooth function and K for a design matrix is confusing and should be disambiguated.  
- Overall assessment: solid, technically nontrivial contribution with linear rates and convincing experiments.
