- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.
- Can handle broad non-separable saddle-point problems where both loss and regularizer are non-separable.
- Leverages monotone operator theory to handle the added intricacy of the saddle-point setting versus purely convex problems.
- Analysis shows the methods cover a wider class of problems, including certain variational inequalities from game theory.
- Proposes two gradient operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Integrates variance reduction into stochastic forward–backward algorithms and analyzes non-uniform sampling.
- Theory and experiments show non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform sampling.
- Empirical results (main paper and appendix) support the theory and demonstrate practical effectiveness on non-separable losses and regularizers.
- Minor/commentary: unclear how to adaptively update the acceleration reference iterate (x̄,ȳ); guidance on tuning would help.
- Minor/question: do the convergence and acceleration guarantees for SVRG extend to SAGA under comparable assumptions?
- Minor/commentary: notation clash—smooth function K(·) and design matrix K should use different symbols.
