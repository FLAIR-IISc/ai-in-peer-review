This paper tackles convex–concave saddle-point optimization and adapts recent stochastic variance-reduction methods (e.g., SVRG/SAGA) to this setting. Convergence analyses for the proposed algorithms are provided. The authors further show that these methods can be readily accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. Extensions to general monotone operators and empirical results are also presented. The assumption of strong convexity–concavity (strong convexity in the primal and strong concavity in the dual) is quite restrictive; it would be preferable to require strong curvature on only one side (either primal or dual). Additional experimental examples would strengthen the paper.
