- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with proven linear convergence.
- Provides theoretical analysis using monotone operator theory to handle the more intricate saddle-point convergence.
- Demonstrates applicability to non-separable losses and regularizers (e.g., structured output, fusion terms).
- Shows the methods extend to a broader class including variational inequalities and game-theory problems.
- Proposes two gradient-operator splitting strategies: element-wise splitting for SVRG and factored splitting for SAGA.
- Integrates variance reduction into stochastic forward-backward algorithms for saddle-point problems.
- Develops and analyzes non-uniform sampling, with theory and experiments showing it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Empirical results in main text and appendix strongly support the theoretical claims and effectiveness on non-separable problems.
- Section 5 adds a regularization term to accelerate convergence but leaves unclear how to adaptively update the iterate point (ȳ, x̄).
- Acceleration and convergence proof provided for SVRG, but it is unspecified whether similar guarantees hold for SAGA.
- Notational confusion: recommend using distinct symbols for the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K.
