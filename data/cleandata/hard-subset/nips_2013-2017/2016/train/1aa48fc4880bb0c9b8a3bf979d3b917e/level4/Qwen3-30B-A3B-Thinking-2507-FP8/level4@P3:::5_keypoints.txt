- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent incremental algorithms for this setting.  
- Convergence analysis cannot use standard convex-minimization techniques and instead leverages monotone operator theory.  
- Monotone-operator analysis shows the algorithms apply to a broader class, including variational inequalities.  
- Provides the first convergence proofs for stochastic variance-reduction methods on saddle-point problems, framing them via monotone operators.  
- Identifies two distinct notions of function splits for saddle-point problems (functions vs partial derivatives), a key difference from convex minimization.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling is theoretically and empirically essential for efficiency in saddle-point problems.  
- Adapts the "catalyst" acceleration framework to yield accelerated incremental methods that outperform accelerated batch methods.  
- Targets important ML scenarios (robust optimization, structured prediction, non-separable regularizers) where existing methods fail due to non-separability.  
- Provides a comprehensive set of ML examples demonstrating applicability when proximal operators are available.  
- Technically sound with careful convergence analysis and experiments showing non-uniform variants outperform uniform and batch baselines.  
- Paper is generally clear and reproducible, but would benefit from a brief proof sketch in the main text to convey monotone-operator insights.  
- Original and well-situated relative to prior work; extension to monotone operators broadens applicability beyond saddle points.  
- Weakness: lacks guidance on choosing non-uniform sampling probabilities π for general (non-bilinear) problems.  
- Weakness: experimental description omits practical details on selecting step size σ beyond stating theoretical bounds.  
- Weakness: needs deeper explanation of why non-uniform sampling matters more for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos to fix: line 78 ("k" → "d"); line 121 (extra "y_{t-1}").  
- Recommendation: accept — significant contribution advancing optimization for ML with practical and theoretical impact.
