- Extends SVRG/SAGA to develop the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- Algorithmic extensions are straightforward, but standard convex minimization analysis does not directly apply.  
- Key technical contribution: uses monotone operator theory to provide rigorous convergence analysis.  
- Shows broader applicability to variational inequalities and non-separable losses/regularizers.  
- Highlights the importance of non-uniform sampling for efficiency, with theoretical and empirical support.  
- Presents an acceleration technique based on the “catalyst” framework.  
- Interprets saddle-point problems as finding zeros of monotone operators—an insightful, broadly applicable perspective.  
- Offers simplified proofs for methods like forward-backward splitting.  
- Experimental results are competitive; non-uniform sampling generally yields superior performance.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor and extension of variance-reduction methods to a new problem class.  
- Strength: practical relevance for machine learning problems with non-separable components.  
- Weakness: need clearer elaboration of the non-uniform sampling strategy beyond bilinear problems (clarify definition of π).  
- Weakness: lack of discussion on how step size σ was chosen in experiments.  
- Weakness: should provide more intuition why non-uniform sampling is particularly crucial for saddle-point problems.  
- Minor issues: typos on line 78 ("k" → "d") and line 121 (extraneous y_{t-1} term).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with acceptance recommended if clarity points are addressed.
