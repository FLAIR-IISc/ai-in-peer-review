- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence.  
- Presents both theoretical and empirical evidence showing variance reduction is effective for these saddle-point problems.  
- Algorithms handle non-separable saddle-point problems (non-separable loss and regularizer), e.g., structured output prediction and fusion regularizers.  
- Uses monotone operator theory for the more complex convergence analysis of saddle-point problems.  
- Analysis shows the methods apply to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities in game theory.  
- Proposes two gradient operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
- Applies variance reduction to stochastic forward–backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems.  
- Studies non-uniform sampling and shows theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Experiments (main text and appendix) sufficiently support theoretical claims and demonstrate method effectiveness.  
- Minor concern: Section 5’s additional regularization term uses an iterate (x̄,ȳ), but it’s unclear how to adaptively update this iterate to achieve acceleration.  
- Minor concern: Convergence/acceleration proof is provided for SVRG but it’s uncertain whether analogous results hold for SAGA.  
- Minor notation issue: the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K should use distinct symbols to avoid confusion.
