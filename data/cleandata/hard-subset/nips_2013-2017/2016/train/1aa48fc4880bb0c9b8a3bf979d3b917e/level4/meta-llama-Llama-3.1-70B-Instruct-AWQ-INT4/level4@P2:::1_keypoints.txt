- Proposes stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG/SAGA) for saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Extends existing techniques to non-separable functions, a significant contribution.  
- Uses a monotone-operator-based analysis offering a novel perspective on saddle-point problems.  
- Experimental validation on two datasets shows promising results.  
- Connection to monotone operators is unclear and should be made more explicit.  
- Presentation needs improvement for better accessibility.  
- Experiments should include more datasets to demonstrate robustness.  
- Provide comparisons to accelerated methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock).  
- Assumptions A–C (especially A) seem strong; discuss applicability to concrete problems like SVM.  
- Clarify differences between stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed approach.  
- The constant μ in Theorem 2 is not well-defined; clarify its link to the monotonicity constant and compare to Theorem 1.  
- The appendix transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing; state whether (A)–(C) imply monotonicity.  
- Minor: fix mix-up between condition number and Lipschitz constant.  
- Minor: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Overall strengths: thorough theoretical analysis, non-separable extension, and promising experiments.  
- Overall weaknesses: presentation clarity, limited experiments, and missing comparisons to accelerated methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept for NIPS (rating 8/10, confidence 8/10) with encouragement to address minor issues and add experiments/comparisons.
