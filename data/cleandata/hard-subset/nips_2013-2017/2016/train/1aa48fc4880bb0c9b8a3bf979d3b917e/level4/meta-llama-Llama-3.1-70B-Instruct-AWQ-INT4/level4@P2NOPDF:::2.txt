This work addresses the problem of minimizing a convex-concave saddle point problem and extends recent variance reduction methods to solve it. The authors provide a convergence analysis of the proposed methods and demonstrate that they can be easily accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. Furthermore, they provide extensions to monotone operators and present numerical results. However, the assumption of strong convexity on both the primal and dual problems is overly restrictive; it would be more reasonable to assume strong convexity on either the primal or dual problem, but not necessarily both. Additionally, including more numerical examples would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the methods' performance.
