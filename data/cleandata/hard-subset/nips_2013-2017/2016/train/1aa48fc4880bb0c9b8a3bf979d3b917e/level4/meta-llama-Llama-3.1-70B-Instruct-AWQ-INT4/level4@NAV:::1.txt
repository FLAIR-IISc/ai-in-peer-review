This paper explores saddle-point problems with a convex-concave property, addressing them through the integration of existing stochastic variance reduced techniques, such as SVRG and SAGA, with a proximal operator. The authors conduct their analysis within the monotone operator setting, demonstrating linear convergence. Additionally, they propose an accelerated variant and examine a non-uniform sampling scheme.

The paper's novelty and originality are substantial, offering a thorough theoretical analysis that reveals new insights into saddle-point problems. Notably, the authors extend their method to non-separable functions, a significant contribution.

From a technical standpoint, while the theoretical contributions are commendable, the presentation could be improved. Clarifying the connection to monotone operators, particularly in the analysis, would enhance the paper's accessibility. Furthermore, the experimental section would benefit from results on additional datasets. For separable functions, comparisons with accelerated methods, such as the stochastic variant of the Chambolle-Pock algorithm, would be valuable.

Several points require clarification:

1. The authors should assess the strength of assumptions A-C, particularly assumption (A), and discuss their applicability to solving the saddle-point problem induced by SVM.

2. The distinction between the stochastic primal-dual method and the authors' suggested method is unclear and warrants further explanation.

3. The role of the constant \mu in theorem 2, specifically its relation to the monotonicity constant mentioned in the appendix, needs clarification. Additionally, a comparison between the results of theorems 1 and 2 would be beneficial.

4. The transition from assumptions (A)-(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing and requires clarification on whether these assumptions imply monotonicity.

Minor issues include:

- Inconsistent usage of the constant L, representing both the condition number and the Lipschitz constant.

- The addition of a reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm would be appreciated.
