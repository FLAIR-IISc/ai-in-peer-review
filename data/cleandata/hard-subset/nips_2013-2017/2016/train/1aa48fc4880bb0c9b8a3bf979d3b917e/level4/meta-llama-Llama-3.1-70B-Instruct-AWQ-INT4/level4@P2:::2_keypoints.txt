- Proposal: Introduces stochastic variance reduction methods for convex–concave saddle-point problems, with convergence analysis, catalyst-based acceleration, extension to monotone operators, and numerical results.  
- Writing/organization: Well-organized and clearly written; introduction motivates the problem and technical sections are easy to follow.  
- Main contributions: Extends SVR to saddle-point problems, develops catalyst acceleration for these methods, and provides convergence analysis plus experiments.  
- Strengths: Clear, accessible introduction; well-structured technical presentation; thorough convergence analysis.  
- Weaknesses: Relies on strong convexity assumptions for both primal and dual problems, which is restrictive.  
- Weaknesses: Numerical validation is limited to a single example and needs more diverse experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions.  
- Requested revisions: Relax the strong convexity assumptions (weaker conditions) and provide more numerical examples across different scenarios.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10; Originality 7/10; Impact 8/10; Clarity 9/10.
