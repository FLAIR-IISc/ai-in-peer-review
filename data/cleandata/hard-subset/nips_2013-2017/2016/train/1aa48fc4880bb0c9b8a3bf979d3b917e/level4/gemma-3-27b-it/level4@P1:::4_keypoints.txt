- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with provable linear convergence.  
- Presents both theoretical analysis and empirical results showing effectiveness in the saddle-point setting.  
- Tackles non-separable optimization problems common in ML (non-separable losses and regularizers).  
- Provides a more complex convergence analysis than standard convex optimization as a key contribution.  
- Leverages the monotone-operator framework to prove convergence.  
- Shows algorithms generalize to variational inequalities, with applications in game theory.  
- Analyzes nuances of gradient-operator splitting: element-wise versus factored approaches in SVRG/SAGA.  
- Builds on stochastic forward–backward methods and uses non-uniform sampling to achieve superior performance.  
- Experiments (main text and appendix) convincingly support theoretical claims, including for non-separable cases.  
- Minor point: adaptive updating of acceleration parameters (x̄, ȳ) in Section 5 is unclear and needs elaboration.  
- Minor point: unclear whether the SVRG convergence/acceleration results also hold for SAGA—should be stated.  
- Minor point: reuse of notation K(.) and matrix K is potentially confusing and should be disambiguated.
