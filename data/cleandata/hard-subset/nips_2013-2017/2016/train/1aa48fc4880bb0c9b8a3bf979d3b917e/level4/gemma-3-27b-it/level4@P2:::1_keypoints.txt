- Paper extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems and shows linear convergence under a monotone operator framework.  
- Proposes an accelerated variant using non-uniform sampling.  
- Method applies beyond saddle-point problems to a broader class including variational inequalities.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough and provides novel insights into saddle-point structure.  
- Extending variance reduction techniques to this setting is a significant contribution.  
- Demonstration of linear convergence is valuable.  
- Non-uniform sampling and catalyst-style acceleration increase practical relevance.  
- Connection to monotone operator theory is powerful but not emphasized early enough in presentation.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to only two datasets, weakening empirical validation.  
- Missing comparison to related accelerated primal–dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock [Zhang & Chambolle, 2015]).  
- Assumptions (A)–(C) may be strong; their practical applicability (e.g., to SVMs) needs clarification.  
- Request clarification on how the method relates to stochastic primal–dual approaches and what advantages it offers.  
- Ask for clarification of the constant μ in Theorem 2 and comparison between Theorems 1 and 2 in practice.  
- Unclear derivation in appendix: do assumptions (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity?  
- Minor ambiguity in the use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) — needs clarification.  
- Missing citation: Zhang & Chambolle (2015) should be included.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, linear convergence, practical acceleration, broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation, limited experiments, lack of comparison to relevant methods.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and acceptably strong paper; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing assumptions, experiments, and comparisons.
