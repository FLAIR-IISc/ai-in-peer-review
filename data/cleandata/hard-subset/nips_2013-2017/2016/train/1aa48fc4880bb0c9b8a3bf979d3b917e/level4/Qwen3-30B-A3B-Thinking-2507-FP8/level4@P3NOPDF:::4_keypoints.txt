- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, proving linear convergence under appropriate conditions.
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence proofs, providing a more robust and general analysis than standard convex tools.
- Targets non-separable problems (e.g., structured output prediction, fusion regularizers) that prior variance-reduction work did not handle.
- Introduces two gradient operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Integrates variance reduction into stochastic forward–backward algorithms for saddle-point settings.
- Analyzes and empirically validates non-uniform sampling, showing it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Strong empirical results across diverse non-separable loss and regularizer scenarios, supported by extensive appendix material.
- Technical depth is high and the approach avoids ad hoc extensions, situating the work well within existing literature.
- Minor concern: acceleration mechanism in Section 5 lacks specification of the adaptive update for the auxiliary iterate (bar{x}, bar{y}).
- Minor concern: theoretical guarantees for accelerated SAGA are missing while accelerated SVRG is analyzed.
- Minor concern: notational inconsistency between the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K could cause confusion.
- Overall recommendation: paper is well-suited for NeurIPS pending minor revisions (clarify acceleration, SAGA theory, and notation; fix small grammatical issues).
