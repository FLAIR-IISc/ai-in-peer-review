

This work addresses the minimization of convex-concave saddle problems by extending recent variance reduction methods for their solution. The convergence analysis of the proposed methods is provided. The authors demonstrate that these methods can be efficiently accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. Extensions to monotone operators and additional numerical results are also included. However, the assumption that both the primal and dual problems are strongly convex is overly strong; it suffices for only one of the problems (primal or dual) to be strongly convex. Additional numerical examples should be provided to further support the claims.
