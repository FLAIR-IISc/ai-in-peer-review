This paper proposes the first linearly convergent incremental gradient algorithms for saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors extend recent stochastic variance reduction methods, such as SVRG and SAGA, to tackle non-separable losses or regularizers by considering the saddle-point problem. The main contribution of the paper is using monotone operators to prove convergence, which differs significantly from the associated convex minimization set-up.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of their approach. The proof technique of interpreting saddle-point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator is interesting and potentially widely applicable. The proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive, and in most cases, better than previous alternatives.

The paper extends the field in an important direction, and the analysis approach is interesting. However, the main contribution of the paper lies in the proof technique, and the authors could consider providing more details about it, such as a proof sketch, in the main paper, if space permits.

There are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. For example, the paper could benefit from a discussion on why non-uniform sampling seems so essential for saddle-point problems, and not necessarily for convex minimization problems. Additionally, the authors should clarify what kind of non-uniform sampling is used for problems other than bilinear saddle-point problems and how the step size is set in the experiments.

Minor comments include a typo on line 78 (k -> d) and a typo on line 121 in the second equality (extra y_{t-1} term).
