- Paper studies convex-concave saddle-point problems using SVRG/SAGA plus a proximal operator and analyzes them in the monotone operator framework, proving linear convergence.  
- Authors present an accelerated variant and a non-uniform sampling scheme, and show applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Contributions are substantial and the theoretical analysis provides new insights for saddle-point problems.  
- The link to monotone operators should be made more explicit since the analysis relies on that framework.  
- Presentation is disappointing in places; the monotone-operator foundation is not clearly highlighted despite being central.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited; experiments on more than two datasets are needed.  
- For the separable case, compare against accelerated stochastic primal–dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock) and include the referenced JMLR paper.  
- Clarification needed on strength and applicability of assumptions (A)–(C), particularly whether they hold for SVM saddle-point formulations.  
- Clarify differences between the stochastic primal–dual method and the proposed approach.  
- In Theorem 2, clarify the constant μ (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1.  
- Explain the transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix and whether (A)–(C) imply monotonicity.  
- Minor issues: confusion between L as condition number vs. Lipschitz constant, and add a citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
