The authors extend stochastic variance reduction methods, such as SVRG and SAGA, to develop the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for convex-concave saddle-point problems, common in machine learning. While the algorithmic extensions are straightforward, the work addresses significant challenges, notably that the standard convex minimization analysis does not directly apply. The key contribution lies in leveraging the theory of monotone operators to provide a convergence analysis, demonstrating the broader applicability of the proposed algorithms to problems like variational inequalities. The paper also highlights the importance of non-uniform sampling for efficiency, both theoretically and empirically, and presents an acceleration technique based on the “catalyst” framework. This is a valuable contribution, as it provides tools for tackling non-separable losses and regularizers that frequently arise in machine learning applications. The interpretation of saddle-point problems as finding zeros of monotone operators is insightful and potentially broadly applicable, and the simplified proofs offered for algorithms like the forward-backward splitting are a welcome addition. The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance, with non-uniform sampling generally yielding superior results. 

The paper’s strengths lie in its theoretical rigor, extending existing variance reduction techniques to a new problem class, and its practical relevance to machine learning. The analysis using monotone operators is a significant step forward. The extension to variational inequalities is also notable, broadening the applicability of the presented methods.

However, the paper could benefit from increased clarity in certain areas. Specifically: 1) Further elaboration on the non-uniform sampling strategy for problems beyond bilinear saddle-point problems would be helpful – clarifying the definition of π in those cases. 2) A discussion of how the step size σ is selected in the experiments would be appreciated. 3) The authors could strengthen the paper by offering more intuition regarding *why* non-uniform sampling appears so crucial for saddle-point problems, a distinction not always observed in convex minimization. Addressing this point could deepen the understanding of the proposed methods.

Minor issues include: 1) A typo on line 78 ("k" should be "d"). 2) A typo on line 121 in the second equality (an extraneous y_{t-1} term). 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The paper presents the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems using stochastic variance reduction.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The convergence analysis, based on monotone operators, is rigorous and extends the applicability of the methods.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The algorithms address a significant challenge in machine learning – handling non-separable losses and regularizers.
*   **Significance:** The work advances the state-of-the-art in solving saddle-point problems and offers a new perspective through the monotone operator framework.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Clarity:** Certain aspects of the non-uniform sampling strategy and step size selection could be clarified.
*   **Depth of Discussion:** A more detailed discussion of the reasons behind the importance of non-uniform sampling for saddle-point problems would be beneficial.



Overall, this is a strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions. Addressing the points raised above would further enhance its quality. I recommend acceptance.
