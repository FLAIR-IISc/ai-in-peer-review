- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Adapts variance-reduction techniques using a monotone-operator framework, extending results to variational inequalities.  
- Provides convergence analysis that addresses key differences between convex minimization and saddle-point problems.  
- Examines two notions of splits (functions vs partial derivatives) and argues for the necessity of convex-concave splits.  
- Develops and analyzes non-uniform sampling strategies that yield theoretical and practical improvements.  
- Presents a simple catalyst-based acceleration extension that outperforms accelerated batch methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound with clear linear convergence guarantees and comprehensive complexity results.  
- Experiments validate theoretical claims, showing significant improvements, especially on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; monotone-operator material may require reader familiarity.  
- Original and novel contribution by bringing variance reduction to overlooked saddle-point problems, distinct from prior SVRG/SAGA work.  
- Significant practical impact for ML applications (robust optimization, non-separable losses, convex relaxations).  
- Limitations: relies on strong convexity-concavity assumptions, potentially restricting applicability.  
- Could include more practical examples of non-separable losses/regularizers and stronger discussion of related NIPS saddle-point work.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — original, significant, technically sound, and valuable to the community.
