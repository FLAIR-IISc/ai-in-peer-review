- Paper studies convex-concave saddle-point problems using SVRG/SAGA with proximal operators, proving linear convergence in a monotone operator framework and proposing an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.
- Core novelty: extends stochastic variance reduction methods to non-separable saddle-point problems, advancing beyond prior separable-function work.
- The monotone operator analysis is thorough and central to proofs but is presented late and should be integrated earlier in the paper.
- Presentation should explicitly frame the problem within monotone operator theory up front to improve accessibility and coherence.
- Experiments are too limited (only two datasets); add at least one more benchmark to strengthen empirical validation.
- For separable cases, compare against accelerated stochastic methods like stochastic Chambolle–Pock (Zhang et al., JMLR 2015) and cite that work.
- Clarify validity and strength of assumptions (A)–(C), especially whether they hold for SVM-derived saddle-point problems, with examples or counterexamples.
- Explain how the proposed method relates to and differs from stochastic primal–dual approaches in structure, assumptions, and convergence guarantees.
- In Theorem 2, explicitly state whether μ is the strong monotonicity constant and relate it to Theorem 1’s convergence rate for direct comparison.
- Clarify whether assumptions (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity or if additional structure is needed, and provide a concise proof sketch linking them to monotonicity.
- Fix notation confusion between L as condition number and L as Lipschitz constant (e.g., use κ for condition number).
- Add a citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm (e.g., Combettes & Wajs, 2005).
- Overall assessment: strong technical contributions and important advances for non-separable settings, but presentation, clarity of assumptions, and experimental breadth need improvement.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the above clarifications, expanding experiments, and improving exposition.
