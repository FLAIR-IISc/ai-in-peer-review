- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Presents the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and motivates the problem thoroughly.  
- Proposed algorithms are carefully explained and analyzed.  
- Provides thorough convergence analysis showing variance reduction improves rates.  
- Experiments on non-separable loss and regularizer show strong empirical performance and outperform existing methods.  
- Strength: novel extension of stochastic variance reduction to saddle-point optimization.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Strength: empirical validation across relevant settings.  
- Weakness: assumes known strong convexity–concavity constants, which may be unrealistic.  
- Weakness: lacks clear guidance on adaptively updating the reference iterate (¯x,¯y) for speed-ups.  
- Weakness: notational confusion between smooth function K(.) and design matrix K.  
- Originality: new contribution applying SVRG/SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle points with theoretical analysis.  
- Significance: potential to substantially improve convergence in many machine-learning applications.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address adaptive-update discussion and notation clarification.
