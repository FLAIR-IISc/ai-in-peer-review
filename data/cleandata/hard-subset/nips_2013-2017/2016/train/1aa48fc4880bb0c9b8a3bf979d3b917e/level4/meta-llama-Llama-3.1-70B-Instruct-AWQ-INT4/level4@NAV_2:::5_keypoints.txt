- Proposes the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems via extensions of SVRG and SAGA.  
- Main novelty: using monotone-operator analysis for convergence, differing from convex minimization proofs.  
- Analysis extends to a broader class including variational inequality problems.  
- Tackles non-separable loss functions and regularizers by formulating saddle-point problems.  
- Provides simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Introduces non-uniform sampling variants that are competitive and often outperform prior methods.  
- Unclear definition/type of non-uniform sampling π for non-bilinear saddle-point problems.  
- Experimental protocol omits how step size σ was chosen.  
- Lacks intuition/explanation why non-uniform sampling is crucial for saddle-point problems but not for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos/errors: line 78 (k -> d) and extra y_{t-1} term in second equality on line 121.  
- Strengths: novel approach, broader applicability, simpler proofs, and empirical competitiveness.  
- Weaknesses: clarity issues on sampling and step-size, missing intuition, and minor technical errors.  
- Overall: significant contribution with potential for greater impact after addressing the stated clarifications.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, monotone-operator technique, and broader applicability; rejection arguments: clarity gaps and missing intuition/experimental details.
