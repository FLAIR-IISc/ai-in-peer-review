- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing first linearly convergent algorithms for large-scale non-separable losses/regularizers.  
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence analysis, enabling application to variational inequalities beyond standard convex optimization.  
- Introduces two gradient operator splits (element-wise and factored) tailored to saddle-point structures.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling significantly improves theoretical and empirical performance.  
- Adapts the catalyst acceleration framework to these algorithms, achieving improved complexity bounds over batch methods.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis with well-structured proofs addressing saddle-point-specific challenges.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized; examples (e.g., structured output prediction, robust optimization) effectively illustrate applicability.  
- Originality: fills a gap by extending stochastic variance reduction from convex minimization to saddle-point problems.  
- Empirical results validate superiority over batch methods and uniform-sampling variants; acceleration and non-uniform sampling give large gains.  
- Well-positioned for NIPS with clear connections to prior SVRG/SAGA literature and broad ML applicability.  
- Weakness: inconsistent notation for K (smooth function vs. design matrix).  
- Weakness: limited discussion on how to adaptively update the acceleration iterate (\bar{x},\bar{y}).  
- Weakness: does not explicitly prove SAGA shares the same convergence guarantees as SVRG.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — significant theoretical and empirical contribution to optimization and machine learning.
