- Presents first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems by extending variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA).
- Central novelty is a proof technique based on monotone operator theory rather than standard convex-minimization analysis.
- The monotone-operator approach is necessary and generalizable to other problems (e.g., variational inequalities).
- Saddle-point formulation is well-motivated for handling non-separable losses and regularizers in ML.
- Recasting the problem as finding zeros of a monotone operator is elegant and potentially widely applicable.
- Uses the framework to give simpler proofs for existing methods (Forward-Backward splitting and stochastic variants).
- Experiments show the proposed algorithms are competitive and often outperform existing methods, especially with non-uniform sampling.
- Primary novelty lies in the analysis/proof technique rather than the algorithmic updates, so the paper should include more detailed exposition and a proof sketch in the main text.
- Clarify the non-uniform sampling distribution π for non-bilinear problems.
- Describe how the step size σ was chosen in the experiments.
- Discuss intuition for why non-uniform sampling matters more for saddle-point problems than for standard convex minimization.
