- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with proven linear convergence.
- Methods handle non-separable saddle-point formulations where both loss and regularizer are non-separable.
- Main technical contribution: convergence analysis using monotone operator theory to handle saddle-point complexity.
- Analysis shows algorithms apply to a broader class including variational inequalities in game theory.
- Proposes two stochastic gradient splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Incorporates variance reduction into stochastic forward–backward algorithms for saddle-point problems.
- Considers non-uniform sampling; theory and experiments indicate it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experimental results (main paper and appendix) strongly support the theoretical claims and effectiveness on non-separable problems.
- Minor: In Section 5 on acceleration, it is unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) to gain speed-ups.
- Minor: Acceleration and convergence guarantees are provided for SVRG—question whether analogous results hold for SAGA.
- Minor: Notational confusion between the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K; recommend different symbols.
