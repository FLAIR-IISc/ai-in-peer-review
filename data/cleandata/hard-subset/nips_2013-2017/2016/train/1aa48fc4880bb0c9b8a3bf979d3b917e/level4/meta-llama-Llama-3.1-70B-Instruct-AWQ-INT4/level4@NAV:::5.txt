This paper presents a significant breakthrough by proposing the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems, leveraging variance reduction techniques such as SVRG and SAGA. The key innovation lies in employing monotone operators to establish convergence, thereby extending the applicability of the analysis to a broader class of problems, including variational inequality problems. This approach enables the effective handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers, commonly encountered in various machine learning models. By reformulating saddle point problems as the search for zeros of a monotone operator, the authors devise the first algorithms to achieve a linear convergence rate for such problems. This contribution is substantial, as it facilitates the tackling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers, prevalent in numerous machine learning applications. Furthermore, the paper offers simplified proofs for algorithms such as FB splitting and its stochastic variant. The proposed algorithms, incorporating non-uniform sampling, demonstrate competitiveness and often outperform previous alternatives. The analysis approach adopted in this work is intriguing and has the potential for widespread applicability, thereby expanding the field in a vital direction. However, since the primary contribution of the paper resides in the proof technique, which is a relatively simple extension of existing algorithms, the authors may consider providing additional details about the proof technique, such as a proof sketch, in the main paper if space permits. The paper is generally well-presented and clearly written, although certain aspects could be clarified. Specifically: 1. The authors could elaborate on the type of non-uniform sampling employed for problems other than bilinear saddle-point problems, i.e., the definition of \pi. 2. The process for setting the step size \sigma in the experiments is not explicitly stated. Additionally, the authors might discuss their intuition regarding the essential role of non-uniform sampling in saddle point problems, as opposed to convex minimization problems. Minor corrections: 1. A typo is present on line 78, where "k" should be replaced with "d". 2. On line 121, the second equality contains an extraneous y_{t-1} term.
