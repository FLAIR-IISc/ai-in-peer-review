This paper addresses the challenging problem of minimizing convex-concave saddle points, building upon recent advances in variance reduction techniques. The authors present novel methods for this problem and provide a convergence analysis to support their efficacy. They further demonstrate the potential for acceleration via an extension of the catalyst framework, and extend their approach to monotone operators, validating it with numerical results. However, the requirement of strong convexity for *both* the primal and dual problems appears overly restrictive; strong convexity should ideally be required for only one of the problems. Additionally, the manuscript would benefit from a more comprehensive set of numerical examples to further demonstrate the methods’ performance and robustness.
