- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Adopts monotone operator framework for a novel and more complex convergence analysis.  
- Applicable to non-separable losses and regularizers and broader problems like variational inequalities/game theory.  
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits (element-wise and factored) for SVRG and SAGA, respectively.  
- Integrates variance reduction into stochastic forward–backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems.  
- Considers non-uniform sampling and shows both theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results (main text and appendix) support theoretical claims and show method effectiveness.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Minor issue: unclear how to adaptively update the iterate point (x̄, ȳ) in Section 5 to obtain speed-up.  
- Minor issue: convergence and acceleration proof is given for SVRG but not for SAGA—request to provide similar results for SAGA.  
- Minor issue: notation conflict between the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K—suggest using different symbols.  
- Recommendation: accept; reviewer scores Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Impact 9/10.
