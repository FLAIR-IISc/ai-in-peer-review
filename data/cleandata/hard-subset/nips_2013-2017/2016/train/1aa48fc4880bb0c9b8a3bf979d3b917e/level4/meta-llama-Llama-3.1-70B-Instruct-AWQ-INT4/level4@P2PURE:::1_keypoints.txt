- Applies SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to convex-concave saddle-point problems and analyzes them in the monotone operator framework.  
- Establishes linear convergence, proposes an accelerated variant, and analyzes a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Major contribution: thorough theoretical analysis that extends to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation issue: the connection to monotone operators is not made explicit early and should be clarified (currently only in Section 6).  
- Experimental limitation: only two datasets tested; needs broader evaluation.  
- For separable functions, compare to accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle-Pock, Zhang et al., 2015) and include that reference.  
- Clarify strength and applicability of assumptions A–C, especially assumption (A) (e.g., do they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems?).  
- Explain differences between the stochastic primal-dual method and the proposed method more clearly.  
- In Theorem 2, define the constant μ precisely (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and compare this result to Theorem 1.  
- Clarify whether assumptions A–C imply (strong) monotonicity and streamline the transition to strong monotonicity in the appendix.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
