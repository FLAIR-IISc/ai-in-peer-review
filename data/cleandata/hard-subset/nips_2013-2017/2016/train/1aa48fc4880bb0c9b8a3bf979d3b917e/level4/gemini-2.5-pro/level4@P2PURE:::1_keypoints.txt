- Paper adapts SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator for convex-concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence via monotone operator analysis.  
- Introduces an accelerated variant and analyzes a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Novelty: strong theoretical contributions and a notable generalization to non-separable functions beyond prior work.  
- Presentation issue: connection to the monotone operator framework is central but not made clear early, being deferred to Section 6.  
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets) and lack comparisons to related accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock / Zhang & Lin 2015).  
- Clarification needed: how restrictive are assumptions (A)–(C), particularly (A), and do they hold for SVM saddle-point formulations?  
- Clarification needed: clarify key differences between the proposed method and standard stochastic primal–dual methods.  
- Clarification needed: the constant μ in Theorem 2 is ambiguous—confirm if it equals the monotonicity constant in the appendix and compare Theorem 2’s rate to Theorem 1.  
- Clarification needed: whether assumptions (A)–(C) imply the strong monotonicity property used in the appendix.  
- Minor: inconsistent use of L (called both condition number and Lipschitz constant); clarify notation.  
- Minor: provide a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
