- Paper adapts SVRG/SAGA with proximal operators to convex-concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence using a monotone operator framework.  
- Authors propose an accelerated variant and analyze a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Reviewers judge the contributions significant and the theoretical analysis thorough.  
- Key novel extension: applicability to non-separable functions, broadening scope.  
- Presentation critique: connection to monotone operators should be made explicit early, not relegated to Section 6.  
- Experimental critique: empirical validation is weak, relying on only two datasets.  
- Reviewer requests comparison with accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., Zhang & Lin 2015 stochastic Chambolle–Pock) and asks that the reference and experiments be added.  
- Question 1: Clarify how restrictive assumptions (A)-(C) are and whether they hold for SVM saddle-point formulations.  
- Question 2: Clarify the distinction between the proposed method and other stochastic primal-dual methods.  
- Question 3: Clarify definition of constant μ in Theorem 2 (is it the strong monotonicity constant?) and explain comparability with Theorem 1’s rate.  
- Question 4: Clarify relationship between assumptions (A)-(C) and the strong monotonicity assumption in the appendix—do (A)-(C) imply strong monotonicity?  
- Minor issue: notation confusion for L used both as condition number and as a Lipschitz constant—use distinct symbols.  
- Minor issue: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
