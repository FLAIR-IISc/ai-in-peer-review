- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.
- Provides both theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrating effectiveness of variance reduction for saddle-point problems.
- Algorithms handle non-separable saddle-point problems where both loss and regularizer can be non-separable (e.g., structured output, fusion terms).
- Major technical contribution: convergence analysis for saddle-point problems using monotone operator theory, which is substantially more complex than convex-only analyses.
- Analysis shows methods apply to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities from game theory.
- Introduces two gradient operator splits to handle stochasticity: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Integrates variance reduction into a stochastic forward–backward algorithm commonly used for saddle-point problems.
- Considers non-uniform sampling and shows both theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experimental results (main paper and appendix) strongly support theoretical claims and show effectiveness on non-separable loss and regularizer tasks.
- Minor comment: unclear how to adaptively update the acceleration iterate (x̄, ȳ) in Section 5 for speed-up.
- Minor comment: acceleration proof is provided for SVRG — unclear whether similar theoretical results hold for SAGA.
- Minor comment: avoid symbol confusion by using distinct notation for the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K.
