This paper extends the stochastic variance reduction techniques SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems, establishing a linear convergence rate. Both theoretical and empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of this approach.

The proposed algorithms are applicable to a range of non-separable saddle-point problems, where both the loss function (e.g., in structured output prediction) and the regularizer (e.g., a fusion term) can be non-separable. A key contribution lies in the convergence analysis; in contrast to prior work on convex problems, the analysis for saddle-point problems is considerably more complex. The authors successfully leverage monotone operator theory to prove convergence. This analysis also shows that the extended algorithms apply to a broader class of problems, such as variational inequalities in game theory.

The authors integrate variance reduction into the stochastic forward-backward algorithm, a standard framework for saddle-point problems. To facilitate this, they propose two distinct splits of the gradient operator: an element-wise split and a factored split. The paper also considers non-uniform sampling, presenting both theoretical and empirical evidence that it is superior to uniform sampling in this context. The experimental results, provided in both the main paper and the appendix, sufficiently corroborate the theoretical findings and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods on problems with non-separable losses and regularizers.

Minor Comments:
*   In Section 5, the proposed acceleration technique, following [8], incorporates an additional regularization term to guide the update towards an auxiliary iterate point, $(\bar{x}, \bar{y})$. It is not clear, however, how this point should be adaptively updated to achieve the desired speed-up.
*   A convergence and acceleration proof is provided for SVRG. Do the same theoretical results hold for SAGA?
*   To avoid confusion, the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K should be denoted by different symbols.
