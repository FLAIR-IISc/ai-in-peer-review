- Extends SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point problems, providing first linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Main contribution: use of monotone operators to prove convergence since convex minimization analysis fails for saddle points.  
- Analysis shows methods apply beyond saddle points to broader classes, including variational inequalities.  
- Handles non-separable losses and regularizers by formulating them as saddle-point problems—important for many ML models.  
- Novel proof technique: interpret saddle-point problems as finding zeros of monotone operators, offering a general theoretical framework.  
- Provides simplified proofs for forward-backward splitting and its stochastic variant, valuable to the community.  
- Algorithms with non-uniform sampling show competitive empirical performance, often outperforming alternatives.  
- Overall, an important advancement: theoretical extension of variance reduction methods with practical effectiveness.  
- Suggestion: include a more detailed proof sketch in the main paper to clarify the monotone-operator approach.  
- Clarification needed: how sampling probabilities π are chosen for non-bilinear saddle-point problems beyond the bilinear case.  
- Clarification needed: experimental step size σ is not specified and more intuition is needed on why non-uniform sampling is crucial for saddle-point problems but not necessarily for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos: line 78 "k" should be "d"; line 121 has an extra "y_{t-1}" in the second equality.
