- Paper addresses convex-concave saddle-point problems using SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator.  
- Analysis in the monotone operator setting shows linear convergence; includes an accelerated variant and non-uniform sampling.  
- Major contribution: thorough theoretical analysis with new insights and applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation issue: connection to monotone operators is unclear and presented too late (Section 6) despite being central to the analysis.  
- Experimental limitation: only two datasets tested; broader empirical validation requested.  
- Missing comparison: should compare to accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock variant) and add the referenced JMLR paper.  
- Clarification needed on strength and applicability of assumptions A–C, especially (A) and whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle problems.  
- Clarification needed on differences between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual methods.  
- Theorem 2: unclear meaning of constant μ and how this result compares to Theorem 1.  
- Confusing transition in the appendix from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity; need to state whether A–C imply monotonicity.  
- Minor notation issue: inconsistent use of L as condition number vs. Lipschitz constant.  
- Minor request: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Strengths summarized: novel approach, thorough theoretical analysis, supports non-separable functions.  
- Weaknesses summarized: presentation clarity, limited experiments, unclear assumptions, missing method comparison, unclear monotonicity transition.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the above points.
