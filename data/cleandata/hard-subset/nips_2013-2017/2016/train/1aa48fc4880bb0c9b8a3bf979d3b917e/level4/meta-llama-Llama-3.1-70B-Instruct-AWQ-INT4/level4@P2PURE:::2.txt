This work addresses the problem of minimizing a convex-concave saddle point problem and extends recent variance reduction methods to solve it. The authors provide a convergence analysis of the proposed methods and demonstrate that they can be easily accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. The paper also explores extensions to monotone operators and presents numerical results. However, the assumption of strong convexity on both the primal and dual problems is overly restrictive; it would be more realistic to assume strong convexity on either the primal or the dual problem, but not necessarily both. To further strengthen the paper, additional numerical examples would be beneficial.
