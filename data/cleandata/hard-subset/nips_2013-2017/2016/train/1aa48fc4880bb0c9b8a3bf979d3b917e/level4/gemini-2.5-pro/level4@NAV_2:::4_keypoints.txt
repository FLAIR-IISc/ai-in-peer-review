- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems, enabling large-scale, linearly convergent algorithms for structured (non-separable) objectives.
- Core theoretical contribution: novel convergence analysis using monotone-operator theory, applicable beyond convex minimization (including variational inequalities).
- Explores practical choices: operator splitting strategies (factored vs. element-wise), importance of non-uniform sampling, and a catalyst-based acceleration scheme.
- Originality/significance: novel and important extension of variance-reduction methods to saddle-point formulations often encountered in ML.
- Technical quality: rigorous monotone-operator proofs and complexity bounds that highlight advantages of variance reduction and non-uniform sampling.
- Clarity/completeness: well-written, logically structured presentation with systematic algorithm descriptions and practical investigations.
- Empirical validation: experiments show SVRG/SAGA with non-uniform sampling outperform baselines, and accelerated SVRG excels on ill-conditioned problems.
- Weakness: Acceleration scheme’s adaptive update suggestion lacks concrete implementation details or heuristics for practitioners.
- Weakness: Claim that the catalyst acceleration extends to SAGA is not formally justified; needs a detailed argument or reference.
- Weakness: Notational ambiguity (symbol K used for both a function and a matrix); recommend distinct notation to avoid confusion.
