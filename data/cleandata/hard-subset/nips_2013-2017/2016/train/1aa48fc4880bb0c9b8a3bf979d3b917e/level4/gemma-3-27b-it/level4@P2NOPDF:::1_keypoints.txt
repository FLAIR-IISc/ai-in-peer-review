- Paper studies convex-concave saddle-point problems using variance-reduced SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator and proves linear convergence via monotone operator theory, plus an accelerated variant and non-uniform sampling analysis.
- Contributions are substantial and theoretically rigorous, with valuable insights and notable extensions (including non-separable functions).
- Presentation needs improvement: the connection to monotone operator theory is underdeveloped and should be introduced earlier and more explicitly.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to two datasets and is insufficient to fully assess performance.
- For separable functions, include comparisons to accelerated proximal algorithms (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock) and add the Zhang & Peyré (2015) reference.
- Clarify the practical strength of assumptions A–C, especially (A), and whether they hold in common applications (e.g., SVM saddle-point formulations).
- Explain more clearly how the proposed approach differs from existing stochastic primal–dual methods.
- In Theorem 2, clarify the constant μ (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and directly compare Theorem 2’s convergence rate to Theorem 1 to show the acceleration effect.
- Make explicit whether assumptions A–C imply strong monotonicity or if additional conditions are required; the transition in the appendix is unclear.
- Fix inconsistent notation for L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) to avoid ambiguity.
- Add a citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
