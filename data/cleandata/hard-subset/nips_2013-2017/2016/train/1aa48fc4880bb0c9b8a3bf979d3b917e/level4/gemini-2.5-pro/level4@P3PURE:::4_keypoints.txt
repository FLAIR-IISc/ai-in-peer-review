- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence.
- Provides both theoretical analysis and empirical results showing variance reduction is effective for these saddle-point problems.
- Applicable to non-separable saddle-point problems where both loss and regularizer can be non-separable.
- Key theoretical contribution: convergence proof using monotone operator theory for the more complex saddle-point setting.
- Analysis indicates applicability to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities in game theory.
- Algorithmically integrates variance reduction into the stochastic forward–backward framework.
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split for SAGA and factored split for SVRG.
- Incorporates non-uniform sampling and shows theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experimental results (main paper and appendix) support the theoretical findings and demonstrate effectiveness on non-separable problems.
- Minor comment: unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) in the acceleration scheme to achieve speed-up.
- Minor question: do the convergence and acceleration results proven for SVRG also hold for the SAGA variant?
- Minor suggestion: avoid confusion by using different symbols for the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K.
