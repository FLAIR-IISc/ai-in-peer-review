This work addresses the problem of minimizing convex-concave saddle point problems and extends recent variance reduction methods to solve them. The authors provide a convergence analysis of the proposed methods, demonstrating that they can be easily accelerated by leveraging the extension of the Catalyst framework. The paper also explores extensions to monotone operators and presents numerical results. However, the strong convexity assumptions imposed on both the primal and dual problems are overly restrictive; it would be more reasonable to assume strong convexity on either the primal or dual problem, but not necessarily both. Furthermore, additional numerical examples would be beneficial to strengthen the paper's claims and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed methods' performance.
