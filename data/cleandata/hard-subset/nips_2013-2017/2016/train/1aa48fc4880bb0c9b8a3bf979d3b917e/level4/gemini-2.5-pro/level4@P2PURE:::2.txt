This work addresses the problem of solving convex-concave saddle-point problems by extending recent variance reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide a convergence analysis for the proposed methods and show that they can be accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework. An extension to monotone operators and supporting numerical results are also presented.

However, the assumption of strong convexity in both the primal and dual problems is a significant limitation. This condition is very restrictive; a more standard assumption is that only the primal or the dual problem is strongly convex, not both. Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by the inclusion of additional numerical examples to better validate the proposed methods.
