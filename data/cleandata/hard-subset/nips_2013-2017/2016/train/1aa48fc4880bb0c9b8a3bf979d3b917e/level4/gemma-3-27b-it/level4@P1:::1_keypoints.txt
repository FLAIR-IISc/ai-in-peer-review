- Paper studies convex-concave saddle-point problems using stochastic variance reduction (SVRG, SAGA) with a proximal operator in the monotone operator setting.  
- Authors prove linear convergence, propose an accelerated variant, and analyze a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Major contribution: thorough theoretical analysis with new insights and applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation is hard to follow; the role and connection to monotone operator theory should be made more explicit.  
- Experiments are limited; results on a wider range of datasets are needed.  
- For separable cases, comparisons to accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock) are missing.  
- Clarification needed on the strength and applicability of assumptions (A)–(C), especially whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle problems.  
- Request to elaborate differences between stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed approach.  
- The constant μ in Theorem 2 is unclear—need confirmation if it is the monotonicity constant from the appendix and how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1.  
- The transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing; clarify whether (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity.  
- Minor issue: ambiguous use of L for both condition number and Lipschitz constant.  
- Minor request: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Suggested reference: Zhang & Pock (2015) for a stochastic primal–dual algorithm comparison.
