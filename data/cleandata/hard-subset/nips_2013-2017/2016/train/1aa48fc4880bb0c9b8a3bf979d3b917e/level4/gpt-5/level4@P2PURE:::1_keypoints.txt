- Paper studies convex–concave saddle-point problems and applies stochastic variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) with a proximal operator.  
- Analysis is done in a monotone-operator framework and establishes linear convergence.  
- Authors propose an accelerated variant and analyze a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Method extends to non-separable functions — notable extension.  
- Theoretical contributions are substantial and provide new insights into saddle-point problems.  
- Presentation needs improvement, especially clearer connection to monotone operators (core analysis relies on them despite being presented as an extension).  
- Experiments are limited; results on more than two datasets would strengthen empirical support.  
- For separable functions, the paper should compare against accelerated stochastic methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock) and add the referenced JMLR paper.  
- Clarification needed on strength and applicability of Assumptions (A)–(C), particularly whether they hold for SVM saddle-point formulations.  
- Clarify differences between standard stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed approach.  
- In Theorem 2, explain the constant μ (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and how Theorem 2 compares to Theorem 1.  
- Clarify whether Assumptions (A)–(C) imply (strong) monotonicity — the transition in the appendix is confusing.  
- Minor: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant).  
- Minor: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
