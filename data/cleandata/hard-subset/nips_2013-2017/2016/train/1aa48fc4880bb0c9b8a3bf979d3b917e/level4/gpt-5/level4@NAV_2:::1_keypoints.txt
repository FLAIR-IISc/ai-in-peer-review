- Paper adapts SVRG/SAGA with proximal updates to convex-concave saddle-point problems, proving linear convergence via monotone-operator analysis.  
- Introduces an accelerated variant and studies non-uniform sampling schemes.  
- Connects variance-reduction literature for finite-sum convex minimization to primal-dual/forward-backward methods, notably extending to non-separable objectives.  
- Novelty is substantial; the extension to non-separable objectives is particularly noteworthy.  
- Theoretical development is careful and offers new insights for saddle-point optimization.  
- Exposition needs improvement: the monotone-operator connection is central but presented late and should be clarified earlier.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (only two datasets); broader experiments would strengthen the paper.  
- For separable cases, the paper should compare to accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock) and add the referenced JMLR paper.  
- Clarify strength and practicality of assumptions A–C, especially (A); do they hold for SVM saddle-point formulations?  
- Explain algorithmic and guarantee differences between this approach and stochastic primal-dual schemes.  
- In Theorem 2, clarify the constant mu (is it the strong-monotonicity parameter from the appendix?) and compare the convergence rate to Theorem 1 under the same parameters.  
- Make the derivation from (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix clearer: do (A)–(C) suffice or are extra conditions needed?  
- Resolve ambiguity in the use of L (Lipschitz constant vs. “condition number”) and correct/confine terminology.  
- Add an explicit citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
