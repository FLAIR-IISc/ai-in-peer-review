

This paper addresses convex-concave saddle point problems by extending recent variance reduction techniques. The authors provide a convergence analysis and demonstrate how their methods can be accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework. They also generalize the approach to monotone operators and include numerical results. However, the assumption of strong convexity for both the primal and dual problems is unnecessarily strong; it suffices to assume strong convexity in only one of the two (primal or dual). This relaxation is standard in saddle point optimization and would strengthen the theoretical contribution. Additionally, the numerical experiments would benefit from more comprehensive examples to better illustrate the method's performance across diverse problem structures, including cases where strong convexity holds in only one variable. The current results, while supportive, lack sufficient breadth to fully validate the claimed advantages over existing approaches. The technical content is generally sound, but the over-restrictive convexity assumption weakens the paper's novelty and applicability. Addressing these points would significantly improve the paper's impact.
