- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence guarantees.  
- Employs monotone operator theory for a more complex saddle-point convergence analysis than prior convex-only work.  
- Methodology applies to a broad class of non-separable saddle-point problems (non-separable loss and regularizer).  
- Analysis shows the extended algorithms cover a wider problem class, including variational inequalities from game theory.  
- Proposes two gradient operator splits—element-wise for SVRG and factored for SAGA—to enable variance reduction in stochastic forward-backward algorithms.  
- Incorporates non-uniform sampling and provides theoretical and empirical evidence that it outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Presents comprehensive experiments (main text and appendix) that support the theoretical claims and demonstrate efficacy on non-separable problems.  
- Minor: Unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (\bar{x},\bar{y}) used in the acceleration technique following [8].  
- Minor: Convergence and acceleration proofs are provided for SVRG; it is uncertain whether identical theoretical results extend to SAGA.  
- Minor: Notational clash—the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should use distinct notation to avoid confusion.
