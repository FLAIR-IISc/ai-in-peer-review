**Summary of the Paper**

This paper proposes stochastic variance reduction methods for solving convex-concave saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors extend recent stochastic variance reduction methods, such as SVRG and SAGA, to provide the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems. They also show that these algorithms can be easily accelerated using a simple extension of the "catalyst" framework.

**Strengths and Weaknesses**

The paper has several strengths:

1. **Novel extension of variance reduction methods**: The authors provide a novel extension of variance reduction methods to convex-concave saddle-point problems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Linear convergence analysis**: The authors provide a thorough convergence analysis of the proposed methods, which is essential for understanding their performance.
3. **Acceleration technique**: The authors show that the proposed algorithms can be easily accelerated using a simple extension of the "catalyst" framework, which is a significant improvement over existing methods.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses:

1. **Strong convexity assumptions**: The authors assume strong convexity on both the primal and dual problems, which is a strong assumption. In practice, it is common to have strong convexity on only one of the problems.
2. **Limited numerical examples**: The paper only provides a few numerical examples, which may not be sufficient to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in various scenarios.

**Originality and Significance**

The paper is original in its extension of variance reduction methods to convex-concave saddle-point problems.
