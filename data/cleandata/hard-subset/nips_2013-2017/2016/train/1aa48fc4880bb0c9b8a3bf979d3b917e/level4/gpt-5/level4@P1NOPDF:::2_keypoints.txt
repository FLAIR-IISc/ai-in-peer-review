- Addresses convex–concave saddle-point optimization, extends variance-reduction methods, provides convergence analyses, Catalyst-style acceleration, extensions to monotone operators, and includes numerical results.  
- Major methodological concern: the analysis assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual, which is overly restrictive.  
- Recommendation: develop guarantees under the weaker/standard assumption that only one side is strongly convex, or clarify how results change when only one side is strongly convex.  
- Experimental concern: the empirical section is limited and needs more numerical examples across a broader range of problem instances and datasets.  
- Experimental recommendation: include more comprehensive comparisons to relevant baselines to better substantiate the claims.
