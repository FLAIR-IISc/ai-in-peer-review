- Paper extends SVRG/SAGA with a proximal operator to convex-concave saddle-point problems and analyzes them via monotone operators.
- Shows linear convergence, proposes an accelerated variant, and analyzes a non-uniform sampling scheme.
- Novelty: significant contributions and thorough theoretical analysis, notably applicability to non-separable functions.
- Technical quality: strong theoretical results but presentation and accessibility need improvement, especially the monotone-operator connection central to Section 6.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and should include more datasets.
- Missing comparison for separable functions to accelerated primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock [Zhang & Chambolle, 2015]).
- Clarification needed: how restrictive are assumptions (A)-(C), particularly (A), and do they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems?
- Clarification needed: precise distinction and relationship between the proposed method and stochastic primal-dual methods.
- Clarification needed: the constant μ in Theorem 2—is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix, and how does Theorem 2 compare to Theorem 1?
- Clarification needed: whether assumptions (A)-(C) imply strong monotonicity, as the transition in the appendix is confusing.
- Minor issue: notation ambiguity—L is used for both condition number and Lipschitz constant.
- Minor issue: missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm discussion.
- Pro-acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach with broadened applicability via monotone-operator extension.
- Pro-acceptance: accelerated variant and non-uniform sampling provide practical improvements.
- Pro-acceptance: experimental results, though limited, support the method's effectiveness.
- Con-acceptance: manuscript clarity, especially regarding monotone operators, needs improvement.
- Con-acceptance: experimental breadth is insufficient (only two datasets).
- Con-acceptance: incomplete comparison to related work, missing relevant accelerated primal-dual baseline.
- Con-acceptance: several technical details and assumptions require further clarification.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing clarity, experimental validation, and comparisons to related work.
