This paper proposes the extension of stochastic variance reduction techniques SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems, where linear convergence is proved. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed algorithms, demonstrating their effectiveness on a wide range of saddle-point problems, including those with non-separable losses and regularizers.

The paper's main contribution lies in its ability to tackle saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning, using stochastic variance reduction techniques. The authors show that their proposed algorithms can be applied to a variety of problems, including variational inequalities in game theory. The analysis of the algorithms is more complex than that of convex problems, and the authors use monotone operators to prove convergence.

The paper also explores two different splits of the gradient operator, namely element-wise and factored splits, for SVRG and SAGA respectively. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of variance reduction with non-uniform sampling, which is shown to be superior to uniform sampling. Experimental results provide sufficient evidence of the theoretical results and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods on both non-separable loss and regularizer.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their proposed algorithms and analysis. The use of monotone operators to prove convergence is a significant contribution, and the authors' ability to tackle saddle-point problems using stochastic variance reduction techniques is a notable achievement.

* The paper proposes a novel extension of stochastic variance reduction techniques to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems.
* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed algorithms, demonstrating their effectiveness on a wide range of saddle-point problems.

Weaknesses:
