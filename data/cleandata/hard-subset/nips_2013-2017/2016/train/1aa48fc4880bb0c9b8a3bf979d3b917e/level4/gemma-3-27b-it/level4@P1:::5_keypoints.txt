- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, achieving the first linearly convergent algorithms for large-scale saddle-point settings.  
- Recognizes that standard convex minimization analysis fails for saddle-points and leverages monotone operator theory to establish convergence.  
- Framework naturally extends to variational inequality problems, broadening applicability.  
- Addresses efficiently solving problems with non-separable loss functions and regularizers, an important ML challenge.  
- Interpreting saddle-point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator is a novel, widely applicable perspective.  
- Provides simpler proofs for forward-backward splitting and its stochastic variants.  
- Experiments show the proposed algorithms (especially with non-uniform sampling) are competitive and often outperform alternatives.  
- Overall a strong extension to the field with compelling analytical contributions.
