The authors propose the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems by leveraging variance reduction algorithms such as SVRG and SAGA. The primary contribution of the paper lies in its use of monotone operators to establish convergence, as the traditional convex minimization analysis does not apply to saddle point problems. This approach has a broader applicability, extending to variational inequality problems. By formulating non-separable loss functions and regularizers as saddle point problems, this method provides a means to tackle these complex problems. The paper's significance stems from its ability to address non-separable loss functions and regularizers, which are prevalent in numerous machine learning models.

The authors' proof technique, which interprets saddle point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator, is noteworthy and potentially widely applicable. Additionally, they provide simpler proofs for algorithms such as FB splitting and its stochastic version. The proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling demonstrate competitiveness and often outperform previous alternatives. The paper makes a substantial contribution to the field by extending the analysis approach in an important direction.

The authors' main contribution lies in the proof technique, and they may consider providing more details, such as a proof sketch, in the main paper if space permits. The paper is generally well-written and clear, but some aspects could be improved. For instance, the authors could clarify the type of non-uniform sampling used for problems other than bilinear saddle-point problems, specifically the definition of π. Furthermore, they could elaborate on how the step size σ is set in the experiments and provide intuition on why non-uniform sampling appears to be essential for saddle point problems, but not necessarily for convex minimization problems. A discussion on this issue would be beneficial.

Minor comments include correcting the typo on line 78, where "k" should be replaced with "d," and the typo on line 121, where the extra y_{t-1} term should be removed in the second equality.
