This work considers the problem of minimizing a convex-concave saddle point problem and extends the recent variance reduction methods for solving it. The authors provide convergence analysis for the proposed methods and demonstrate that they can be easily accelerated using the extension of the Catalyst framework. Extensions to monotone operators and numerical results are also provided.

However, the strong convexity assumptions on both the primal and dual problems are overly restrictive. It would be more reasonable to assume strong convexity on either the primal or dual problem, but not necessarily both. Furthermore, additional numerical examples would be beneficial to demonstrate the effectiveness and robustness of the proposed methods.
