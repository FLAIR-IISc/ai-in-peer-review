- Paper tackles convex-concave saddle-point problems using SVRG/SAGA with proximal operators, proving linear convergence in a monotone-operator framework.  
- Introduces an accelerated variant and a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Main novelty: extends applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong but presentation needs improvement for accessibility.  
- The connection to monotone operators should be introduced earlier, not deferred to Section 6.  
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets); more datasets are needed to strengthen empirical claims.  
- For separable cases, include comparison with accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock (Zhang & Lin, 2015).  
- Clarification needed on strength and practicality of assumptions A–C, especially A’s applicability to SVM-induced saddle points.  
- Explicitly contrast the proposed method with existing stochastic primal–dual approaches.  
- Clarify the constant μ in Theorem 2 (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and explain its relation to Theorem 1.  
- Resolve whether assumptions A–C imply strong monotonicity (appendix currently confusing).  
- Minor issues: the symbol L is used ambiguously as both condition number and Lipschitz constant.  
- Minor issue: add a citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
