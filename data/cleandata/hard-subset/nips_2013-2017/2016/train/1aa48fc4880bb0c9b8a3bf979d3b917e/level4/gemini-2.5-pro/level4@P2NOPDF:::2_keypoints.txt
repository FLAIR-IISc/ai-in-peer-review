- Contribution: Extends variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) to convex-concave saddle-point problems with convergence analysis.
- Contribution: Adapts the Catalyst framework to accelerate the proposed algorithms.
- Contribution: Generalizes results to monotone operator problems and provides initial numerical experiments.
- Strength: Well-motivated, timely problem with sound theoretical analysis.
- Strength: Catalyst-based acceleration broadens practical utility.
- Weakness: Strong assumption of strong convexity in the primal and strong concavity in the dual is restrictive and limits applicability.
- Suggestion: Analysis should be relaxed to the common setting of one-sided strong convexity (only primal or only dual).
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is limited and serves only as a proof of concept.
- Suggestion: Expand experiments to compare against more state-of-the-art saddle-point methods and evaluate on real-world ML tasks (e.g., regularized AUC max, adversarial training, robust optimization).
