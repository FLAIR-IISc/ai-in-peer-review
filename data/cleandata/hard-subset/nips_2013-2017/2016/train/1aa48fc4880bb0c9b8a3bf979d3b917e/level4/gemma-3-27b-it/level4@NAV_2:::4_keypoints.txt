- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Provides both theoretical analysis and empirical results showing variance reduction is effective for saddle-point problems.  
- Addresses non-separable losses and regularizers (e.g., structured output, fusion penalties), broadening applicability beyond separable objectives.  
- Adapts convergence analysis using monotone operator theory, a more complex extension from standard convex optimization analyses.  
- Framework applies to a wider class of problems, including variational inequalities relevant to game theory.  
- Investigates gradient splitting strategies (element-wise and factored) within SVRG and SAGA implementations.  
- Analyzes non-uniform stochastic sampling and demonstrates theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Comprehensive experiments (main text and appendix) corroborate theoretical findings and show effectiveness on non-separable problems.  
- Acceleration (Section 5) adds an extra regularization/momentum-like term (building on [7]) but lacks an adaptive update strategy for the momentum variables (bar{x}, bar{y}).  
- Convergence proof is provided for SVRG, but it is unclear whether the same results hold for accelerated SAGA.  
- Notational ambiguity: reuse of K for both the smooth function K(x,y) and the design matrix K could cause confusion.
