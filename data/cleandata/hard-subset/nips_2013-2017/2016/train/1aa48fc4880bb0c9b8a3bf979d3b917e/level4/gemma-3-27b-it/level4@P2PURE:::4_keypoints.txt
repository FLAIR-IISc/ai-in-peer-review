- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.
- Uses monotone operator theory for the complex convergence analysis, a departure from prior convex-only analyses.
- Shows applicability to non-separable losses and regularizers (e.g., structured output prediction, fusion terms).
- Demonstrates broader applicability to variational inequalities and game-theoretic problems.
- Proposes two gradient operator splitting strategies: element-wise splits for SVRG and factored splits for SAGA, within a stochastic forward–backward framework.
- Analyzes and empirically validates non-uniform sampling, showing it reduces variance better than uniform sampling.
- Provides comprehensive experiments (main text and appendix) that support the theoretical claims and method effectiveness.
- Minor: Acceleration technique (Section 5) lacks clarity on how the iterate point (x̄, ȳ) is adaptively updated.
- Minor: Convergence/acceleration proof is shown for SVRG but it is unclear whether it extends to SAGA.
- Minor: Recommends different notation for the smooth function K(.) versus the design matrix K to avoid confusion.
