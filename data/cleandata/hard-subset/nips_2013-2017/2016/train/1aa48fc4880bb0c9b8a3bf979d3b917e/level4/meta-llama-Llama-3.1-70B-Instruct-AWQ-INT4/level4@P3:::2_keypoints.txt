- Proposes stochastic variance reduction methods (extending SVRG/SAGA) for convex-concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence and catalyst-style acceleration.
- Novel extension of variance reduction methods to saddle-point problems, representing a significant contribution.
- Provides thorough linear convergence analysis to characterize algorithm performance.
- Demonstrates that the algorithms can be easily accelerated using a simple extension of the catalyst framework.
- Assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual problems, which is a strong and potentially unrealistic assumption.
- Provides only a few numerical examples, limiting empirical validation across scenarios.
- Original and significant work, advancing variance reduction techniques in machine learning.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and accessible with clear notation and derivations.
- Minor typos and formatting issues (e.g., Table 1 caption not centered).
- Could include more references to related work.
- Arguments for acceptance: original contribution, significant algorithmic improvement, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive strong-convexity assumptions and limited empirical evaluation.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix minor issues and add additional numerical examples.
