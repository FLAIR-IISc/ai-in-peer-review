- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence rates.
- Theoretical and empirical results both demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed variance-reduced methods.
- Applicable to non-separable saddle-point problems where both loss and regularizer can be non-separable (e.g., structured output prediction, fusion terms).
- Convergence analysis is a key contribution and is considerably more complex than convex-only cases.
- Leverages monotone operator theory to prove convergence and generalize applicability.
- Shows the algorithms extend to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities in game theory.
- Integrates variance reduction into the stochastic forward-backward algorithm, a standard saddle-point framework.
- Proposes two gradient operator splits: element-wise split and factored split.
- Considers non-uniform sampling and provides theory and experiments showing it outperforms uniform sampling.
- Experiments in the paper and appendix corroborate theoretical findings and demonstrate effectiveness on non-separable problems.
- Minor concerns: unclear how to adaptively update the auxiliary iterate (bar{x},bar{y}) in the proposed acceleration.
- Minor concerns: convergence and acceleration proof provided for SVRG—unclear if the same theoretical results hold for SAGA.
- Minor suggestion: avoid using the same symbol K for both the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K to prevent confusion.
