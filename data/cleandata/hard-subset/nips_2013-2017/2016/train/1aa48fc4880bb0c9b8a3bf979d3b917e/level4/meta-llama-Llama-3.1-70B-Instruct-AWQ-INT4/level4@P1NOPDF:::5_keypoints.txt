- Proposes first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems using variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA).  
- Main novelty is using monotone-operator theory to prove linear convergence, since standard convex-minimization analyses do not apply.  
- Approach generalizes to broader problem classes, including variational inequalities.  
- Formulation handles non-separable loss functions and regularizers common in ML.  
- Proof technique (viewing saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator) is intriguing, potentially widely applicable, and yields simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform previous methods.  
- Because the primary contribution is the proof technique, the paper should consider adding a proof sketch or more proof details in the main text if space allows.  
- Unclear: the definition of the non-uniform sampling distribution π for problems beyond bilinear saddle-point cases.  
- Unclear: how the step size σ was chosen in experiments; add details and rationale.  
- Suggestion: discuss why non-uniform sampling seems essential for saddle-point problems but not necessarily for convex minimization.  
- Minor issues: typo on line 78 ("k" should be "d"); extra y_{t-1} term in the second equality on line 121.
