- Proposes the first incremental SVRG/SAGA-style algorithms for large-scale convex-concave saddle-point problems with provable linear convergence.  
- Uses monotone operator theory to analyze convergence by viewing saddle-point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator.  
- Analysis generalizes beyond saddle-point problems to variational inequalities and yields simplified proofs for batch forward-backward splitting.  
- Addresses problems with non-separable losses/regularizers (e.g., AUC maximization, structured-output prediction) that are difficult for primal/dual variance-reduced methods.  
- Empirical results show strong performance; algorithms with non-uniform sampling and catalyst acceleration often outperform batch and stochastic baselines.  
- Paper is generally well-written and positions its contributions clearly within the literature.  
- Request to improve exposition: include a high-level proof sketch to build intuition for why the monotone-operator analysis succeeds where standard convex analysis does not.  
- Question on non-uniform sampling: need guidance on choosing sampling probabilities (π_i) for general saddle-point problems beyond the bilinear case.  
- Experimental detail needed: clarify whether the step-size (σ) was tuned or set to the theoretical value in experiments.  
- Request for intuition: explain why non-uniform sampling is particularly critical for saddle-point problems, unlike many convex minimization settings.  
- Minor typo: in the factored split for bilinear problems, the sum index should read k=1,...,d (not k=1,...,k).  
- Minor issue: the accelerated forward-backward update for y_t appears to include an extra y_{t-1} inside the prox.
