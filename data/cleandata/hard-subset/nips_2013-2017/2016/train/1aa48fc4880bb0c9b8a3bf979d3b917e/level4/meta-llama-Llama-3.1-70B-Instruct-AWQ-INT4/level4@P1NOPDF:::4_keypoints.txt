- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence.  
- Algorithms handle non-separable saddle-point problems (non-separable loss and regularizer).  
- Convergence analysis is complex and different from convex cases; uses monotone operators.  
- Analysis extends applicability to variational inequalities in game theory.  
- Proposes two gradient splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
- Applies variance reduction to stochastic forward–backward algorithms; non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform.  
- Experiments (main text and appendix) support theory and demonstrate effectiveness on non-separable settings.  
- Minor issues: unclear how to adaptively update the iterate (x̄, ȳ) for acceleration; acceleration proof given for SVRG but unclear for SAGA.  
- Suggestion to avoid symbol confusion by using different notations for smooth function K(.) and design matrix K.  
- Overall: significant contribution with comprehensive theoretical and empirical evaluation.
