- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Fills a gap in the literature: previous variance-reduction methods were limited to convex minimization.  
- Builds on recent stochastic optimization advances (SAG, SVRG, SAGA) and generalizes them to saddle-point settings.  
- Identifies two distinct notions of splitting (function-based vs. derivative-based) specific to saddle-point formulations.  
- Requires splits into convex-concave terms and shows non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency.  
- Extends methods to monotone operators, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point problems (e.g., variational inequalities).  
- Presents a simple, effective acceleration using the catalyst framework that outperforms accelerated batch methods.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: first convergence analysis for variance-reduced methods in the saddle-point setting using monotone operator theory.  
- Demonstrates that the algorithms apply to a wider class of problems than convex minimization alone.  
- Carefully distinguishes splitting strategies and theoretically/practically justifies non-uniform sampling over uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results on synthetic and real-world problems are convincing and support theoretical claims.  
- Major concern: assumption (A) requires strong convexity-concavity in both primal and dual, which is overly restrictive compared to standard assumptions (strong convexity in one variable suffices).  
- Authors should clarify whether both-variable strong convexity is necessary or if analysis can be relaxed to one-variable strong convexity.  
- Experiments include only a limited set of non-separable examples; more diverse non-separable losses/regularizers would strengthen empirical validation.  
- Presentation is generally clear with helpful pseudocode, but some technical transitions (especially to monotone operators in Section 6) could be smoother.  
- Recommendation: valuable contribution; accept with minor revisions pending clarification/relaxation of the strong convexity assumption and expanded non-separable experiments.
