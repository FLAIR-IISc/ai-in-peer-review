- Brings variance-reduction techniques to convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees for the proposed methods.  
- Shows how to accelerate the algorithms using a Catalyst-style scheme.  
- Extends the approach to monotone operators and includes numerical experiments.  
- Assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual sides; authors should clarify or relax to one-sided strong convexity or justify two-sided assumption and discuss regularization effects.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited; needs more datasets, diverse problem types, stronger baselines (non–VR primal–dual, extragradient, accelerated), wall-clock and oracle-complexity reporting, ablations, sensitivity analysis, and scalability studies.  
- Presentation lacks clarity: add precise assumptions (Lipschitz/monotonicity), self-contained pseudocode with all parameters, a proof sketch, and explicit complexity bounds with condition-number dependence.  
- For the monotone-operator extension, specify required operator properties and whether convergence refers to last iterates or ergodic averages.  
- Clarify compatibility with proximal terms and inexact inner solves in the Catalyst framework and how inexactness affects overall convergence rates.  
- Overall: theoretically promising with appealing acceleration, but overly strong assumptions and insufficient experiments need addressing to strengthen the paper.
