

This paper addresses saddle-point problems with convex-concave structure by adapting stochastic variance-reduced techniques (SVRG, SAGA) with proximal operators. The authors establish linear convergence in the monotone operator framework and propose an accelerated variant alongside a non-uniform sampling scheme.  

**Novelty/Originality:** The contributions are significant. The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides new insights for saddle-point problems. The most notable extension is the applicability to non-separable functions, which broadens the scope beyond prior work.  

**Technical Quality:** While the theoretical contributions are strong, the presentation lacks clarity in connecting the work to monotone operator theory. This foundational framework is introduced only in Section 6, though the analysis fundamentally relies on it. Regarding experiments, additional datasets beyond the two reported would strengthen the empirical validation. For separable functions, the authors should compare against accelerated methods like the stochastic Chambolle-Pock algorithm (Zhang & Xiao, 2015; JMLR 2015). Please include this reference.  

**Clarifications Needed:**  
1.  Assess the strength of assumptions (A)–(C), particularly (A). Do they hold for the standard SVM saddle-point problem?  
2.  Clarify the distinction between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal-dual approaches.  
3.  In Theorem 2, specify whether constant $\mu$ refers to the monotonicity constant (Appendix) and explain how its convergence rate compares to Theorem 1.  
4.  The transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the Appendix is unclear. Do (A)–(C) imply monotonicity?  

**Minor Issues:**  
-  Inconsistent use of $L$: "The quantity $L$ represents the condition number" conflicts with "we need the Lipschitz constant $L$." Clarify this distinction.  
-  For the Forward-Backward algorithm, include a standard reference (e.g., Combettes & Pesquet, 2011).
