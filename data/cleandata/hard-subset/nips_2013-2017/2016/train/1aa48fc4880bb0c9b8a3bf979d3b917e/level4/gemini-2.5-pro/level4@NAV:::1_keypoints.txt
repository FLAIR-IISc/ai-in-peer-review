- Method: Adapts SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to convex-concave saddle-point problems, analyzes in the monotone operator framework, proves linear convergence, and adds an accelerated variant plus non-uniform sampling.
- Novelty: Substantial, rigorous theoretical contributions; key impactful extension is applicability to non-separable functions.
- Presentation: Strong theory but accessibility suffers; the monotone-operator connection is introduced too late and should be brought forward.
- Experiments: Evaluation is limited (only two datasets); need more datasets and comparisons to accelerated methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock, Zhang et al. 2015) for separable cases.
- Clarification 1: Strength of assumptions (A)–(C) needs elaboration—unclear if they hold for common cases like SVM saddle-point formulations.
- Clarification 2: Differences between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual methods are not clearly explained.
- Clarification 3: Theorem 2’s constant μ is not clearly defined (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?); provide a direct comparison between Theorems 1 and 2.
- Clarification 4: The link from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is unclear—state whether (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity and explain the transition.
- Minor: Inconsistent usage of L (referred to both as condition number and as a Lipschitz constant).
- Minor: Add a citation for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
