- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Proposed algorithms achieve linear convergence rates.  
- Convergence analysis leverages monotone operator theory, diverging from standard convex analyses.  
- Framework applies to non-separable losses/regularizers and broader variational-inequality problems.  
- Introduces two gradient-splitting strategies: element-wise split and factored split.  
- Studies non-uniform sampling, showing theoretical and empirical improvements over uniform sampling.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical proofs and convergence guarantees (explicitly for the SVRG variant).  
- Thorough empirical validation demonstrating practical effectiveness on challenging tasks.  
- Acceleration technique is presented but lacks details on how the anchor iterate (̄x, ̄y) is adaptively updated.  
- Unclear whether the SAGA-based algorithm admits the same acceleration/convergence guarantees as SVRG—needs clarification or proof.  
- Notational ambiguity: symbol "K" used for both a smooth function and a design matrix; distinct symbols recommended.
