- Extends variance reduction (SVRG/SAGA) to saddle point problems, providing the first linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Core innovation: leverages monotone operator theory to prove convergence, a necessary shift from convex minimization analysis.  
- Framework generalizes to variational inequalities, broadening applicability beyond saddle points.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers relevant to modern ML (e.g., adversarial training, robust optimization).  
- Proof technique (viewing saddle points as zeros of monotone operators) is elegant and potentially influential beyond this work.  
- Yields simpler analyses for existing methods like forward-backward splitting (and its stochastic variant).  
- Empirically, non-uniform sampling algorithms consistently outperform prior alternatives across benchmarks.  
- Two key technical clarifications needed: definition/derivation of the non-uniform sampling distribution π for general (non-bilinear) problems.  
- Two key technical clarifications needed: details and rationale for step size σ selection in experiments (fixed/adaptive/tuned) and why non-uniform sampling is essential for saddle points.
