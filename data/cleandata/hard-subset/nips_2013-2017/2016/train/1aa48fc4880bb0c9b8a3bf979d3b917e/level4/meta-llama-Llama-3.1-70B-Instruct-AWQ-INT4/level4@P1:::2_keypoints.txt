- Proposes the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- Extends stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) to saddle-point settings.  
- Provides a novel convergence analysis that differs substantially from convex minimization setups.  
- Shows saddle-point formulation permits two notions of splitting: by functions or by partial derivatives.  
- Demonstrates non-uniform sampling is crucial for algorithmic efficiency.  
- Extends applicability to a larger class of problems, including variational inequalities / monotone operators.  
- Shows incremental algorithms can be accelerated via a simple "catalyst" extension.  
- Numerical results indicate significant improvements over existing batch and stochastic methods.  
- Finds uniform sampling for SAGA offers no improvement over batch methods, while non-uniform SAGA and accelerated SVRG perform much better.  
- Relies on strong convexity assumptions for both primal and dual problems, which is arguably unrealistic.  
- Recommends relaxing assumptions (e.g., only one-sided strong convexity) for greater realism.  
- Calls for more numerical experiments to better demonstrate method effectiveness.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, technically sound, and presents an original, significant contribution.
