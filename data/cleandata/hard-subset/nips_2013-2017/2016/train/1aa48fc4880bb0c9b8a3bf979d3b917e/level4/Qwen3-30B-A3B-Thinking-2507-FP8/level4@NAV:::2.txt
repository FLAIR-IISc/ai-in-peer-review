

This work addresses the problem of minimizing convex-concave saddle points and extends recent variance reduction techniques for its solution. The convergence analysis of the proposed methods is provided. The authors demonstrate that the methods can be efficiently accelerated using an extension of the catalyst framework. Extensions to monotone operators and supporting numerical results are also included. However, the assumption of strong convexity on both the primal and dual problems is overly strong; it should require strong convexity on either the primal or dual problem, but not necessarily both. Additional numerical examples should be provided.
