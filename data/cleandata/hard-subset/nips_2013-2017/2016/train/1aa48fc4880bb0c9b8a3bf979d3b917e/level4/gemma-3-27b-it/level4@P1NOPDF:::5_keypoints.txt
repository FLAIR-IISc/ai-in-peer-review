- Introduces SVRG/SAGA-based algorithms yielding the first linear-convergence guarantees for saddle point problems.
- Key conceptual contribution: frames convergence analysis using monotone operators since convex minimization analysis doesn't directly apply.
- Approach extends naturally to broader problem classes, including variational inequality problems.
- Provides a pathway to handle non-separable loss functions and regularizers by reformulating them as saddle point problems.
- Demonstrates the first algorithms with linear rates for saddle point problems common in ML with non-separable terms.
- Proof technique (viewing saddle points as zeros of a monotone operator) is insightful and potentially widely applicable.
- Offers simpler proofs for Forward-Backward splitting and its stochastic counterpart.
- Empirically, algorithms with non-uniform sampling achieve competitive performance and generally outperform alternatives.
- Main contribution is the proof technique; authors should consider adding more proof details or a sketch in the main paper.
- Open questions needing clarification: the form of non-uniform sampling distribution π for non-bilinear saddle problems.
- Experimental clarifications requested: how step size σ was chosen and intuition why non-uniform sampling is more crucial for saddle points than for convex minimization.
- Minor typos to fix: Line 78 ‘k’ should be ‘d’; Line 121 contains an extra ‘y_{t-1}’ in the second equality.
