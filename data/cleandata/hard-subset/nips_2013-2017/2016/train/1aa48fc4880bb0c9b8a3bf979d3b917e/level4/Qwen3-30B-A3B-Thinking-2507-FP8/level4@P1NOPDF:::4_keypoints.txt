Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and establishes linear convergence.  
Both theoretical analysis and empirical results support the efficacy of these methods for saddle-point problems.  
Proposed algorithms handle a broad class of non-separable saddle-point problems (non-separable loss and regularizer).  
Convergence analysis relies on monotone operator theory, a notably more complex framework than standard convex analysis.  
Methods apply beyond standard convex problems, including variational inequalities in game theory.  
Introduces two gradient operator splits tailored to each method: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
Integrates variance reduction with stochastic forward–backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems.  
Non-uniform sampling is considered and shown (theoretically and empirically) to outperform uniform sampling.  
Experimental results (main text and appendix) convincingly support the theoretical claims on non-separable problems.  
Minor concern: unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) used for acceleration.  
Minor concern: convergence and acceleration proof is provided for SVRG but it is unclear whether identical guarantees hold for SAGA.  
Minor concern: notation confusion between smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should be clarified.
