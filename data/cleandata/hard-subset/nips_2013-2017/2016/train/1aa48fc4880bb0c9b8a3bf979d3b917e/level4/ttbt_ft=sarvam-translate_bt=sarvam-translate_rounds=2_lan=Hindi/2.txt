This work addresses the problem of reducing convex-concave saddle problems and expands on recently developed variance reduction methods to solve them. Convergence analysis of the proposed methods is provided. The authors state that the methods can be easily accelerated by using the expansion of the catalyst structure. The expansion of the monotone operator and numerical results are also provided. Strong convex notions are robust in both primal and dual problems. It should only be the primal problem or the dual problem that is strongly convex. More numerical examples should be added.
