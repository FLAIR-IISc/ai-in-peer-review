- Paper extends SVRG/SAGA-style variance reduction to convex–concave saddle-point and monotone operator problems with linear convergence under strong convexity–concavity.
- Uses a monotone operator viewpoint for convergence analysis, avoiding co-coercivity-based proofs from minimization literature.
- Considers two decomposition types (by functions and by partial derivatives) and emphasizes non-uniform sampling and mini-batch options.
- Introduces a simple catalyst-style regularization to obtain accelerated methods provably better than accelerated batch algorithms.
- Framework generalizes to finding zeros of strongly monotone operators, covering certain variational inequalities.
- Empirical results show gains over batch methods and over uniform-sampling variants on supervised learning tasks with non-separable and separable losses.
- Builds on SAG/SVRG/SAGA literature and complements primal–dual/forward–backward methods like Chambolle–Pock/PDHG.
- Targets non-separable losses/regularizers and broader monotone operator inclusions unlike SPDC and separable-coordinate methods.
- Technical novelty: convergence proofs rely on monotone operator theory rather than minimization-specific properties.
- Provides explicit complexity bounds tied to operator/matrix norms and discusses sampling-dependent rates.
- Strength: clear treatment of splitting strategies, non-uniform sampling, and practical insights for implementation.
- Strength: catalyst-style acceleration tailored to saddle-point problems with a simple analysis.
- Weakness: requires strong convexity in x and strong concavity in y and knowledge of constants λ, γ, which is restrictive.
- Weakness: algorithms depend on L and L̄; paper lacks practical guidance on estimating these constants and robustness to misspecification.
- Weakness: limited experimental scope (two datasets); lacks wall-clock, more datasets, variational inequality examples, and ablations.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to relevant baselines like SPDC, stochastic/variance-reduced PDHG, and accelerated PDHG.
- Practical concern: SAGA memory overhead (O(nd) for individual split) and cost of computing/maintaining non-uniform sampling probabilities.
- Practical concern: non-uniform SAGA resampling overhead not quantified or empirically analyzed.
- Clarity: overall well organized; distinction between individual and factored splits is helpful.
- Clarity: accelerated scheme presentation could use more intuition on τ trade-off and explicit pseudocode in the main text.
- Originality: novel extension of variance reduction to general convex–concave saddle-point problems with monotone operator analysis.
- Significance: fills a gap for scalable, linearly convergent methods for non-separable saddle-point problems, with potential impact on variational inequalities and games.
- Suggestions: relax to single-sided strong convexity or discuss feasibility; expand experiments and baselines; provide practical guidance for parameter estimation and adaptive step-sizes.
- Suggestions: quantify memory/time trade-offs for SAGA vs SVRG, cost of non-uniform sampling, and consider releasing code for reproducibility.
- Recommendation: promising and timely; lean toward acceptance if empirical section and assumption/parameter clarifications are strengthened.
