- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems, achieving linear convergence.
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis using monotone operator theory to handle the harder saddle-point setting.
- Algorithms apply to broad non-separable saddle-point problems, including structured losses and fusion regularizers.
- Introduces two gradient operator splitting strategies: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.
- Integrates variance reduction within stochastic forward-backward algorithms for saddle-point optimization.
- Shows both theoretically and empirically that non-uniform sampling consistently outperforms uniform sampling.
- Empirical evaluation (main text + appendix) convincingly validates theoretical claims and practical effectiveness.
- Proposes an acceleration technique via regularization toward a reference point but the adaptive update mechanism for (x̄, ȳ) is unclear.
- Convergence and acceleration proof is given for SVRG, but analogous theoretical results for SAGA are not shown and would be useful.
- Notational confusion risk: smooth function K(.) and design matrix K should use distinct symbols.
- Overall assessment: strong, well-motivated paper with rigorous analysis and comprehensive experiments; recommend acceptance.
