This paper studies the minimization of convex–concave saddle-point problems and extends recent variance-reduction methods to this setting. Convergence analyses for the proposed methods are provided. The authors show that the methods can be readily accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework. Extensions to monotone operator settings and numerical results are also presented. The assumption of strong convexity on both the primal and dual problems is overly strong. It should suffice for only the primal or only the dual problem to be strongly convex. More numerical experiments should be added.
