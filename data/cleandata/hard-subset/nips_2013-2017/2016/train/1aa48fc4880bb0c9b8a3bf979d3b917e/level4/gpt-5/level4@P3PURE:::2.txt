This paper studies convex–concave saddle-point problems and extends recent variance-reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide convergence analyses for the proposed methods and argue that they can be readily accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework. They also discuss extensions to monotone operator problems and present numerical results.

Strengths:
- Extends variance-reduction techniques to convex–concave saddle-point problems.
- Provides theoretical convergence guarantees.
- Outlines a straightforward acceleration via the Catalyst framework.
- Includes an extension to the monotone operator setting and some empirical validation.

Concerns and suggestions:
- The assumption of strong convexity on both the primal and the dual sides is overly restrictive. It would be more appropriate to assume strong convexity on only one side (either the primal or the dual).
- The experimental section is limited; more numerical examples should be included to better substantiate the claims.
