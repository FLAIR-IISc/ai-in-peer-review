- Adapts SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
- Provides theoretical guarantees and empirical evidence that variance reduction is effective in this setting.  
- Develops methods applicable to non-separable formulations where both loss and regularizer are non-separable.  
- Key proof technique leverages monotone-operator framework, implying extensions to broader problems (e.g., variational inequalities/game theory).  
- Introduces two stochastic gradient decompositions: component-wise split (for SVRG) and factored split (for SAGA).  
- Embeds variance-reduced estimators within stochastic forward–backward iterations for saddle-point problems.  
- Analyzes and empirically demonstrates that non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Experiments (main text and appendix) support theory and show strong performance on non-separable losses and regularizers.  
- Minor: Acceleration via nudging toward a reference iterate lacks guidance on how to adaptively update the reference (x̄, ȳ) in practice.  
- Minor: Unclear whether the provided convergence and acceleration guarantees for SVRG also hold for SAGA.  
- Minor: Notational ambiguity—symbol K used for both a smooth function and a design matrix; use distinct notation to avoid confusion.
