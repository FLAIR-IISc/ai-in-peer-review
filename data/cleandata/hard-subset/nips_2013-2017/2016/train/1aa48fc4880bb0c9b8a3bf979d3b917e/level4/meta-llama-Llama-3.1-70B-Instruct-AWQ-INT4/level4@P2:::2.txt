This paper proposes stochastic variance reduction methods for solving convex-concave saddle-point problems, extending recent advances in this area. The authors provide a convergence analysis for these methods and demonstrate that they can be easily accelerated using an extension of the catalyst framework. The paper also explores the extension of these methods to monotone operators and presents numerical results to support the proposed approach.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the problem and effectively sets the stage for the rest of the paper. The technical sections are well-structured, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed methods.

The main contributions of the paper are the extension of stochastic variance reduction methods to convex-concave saddle-point problems and the development of an acceleration technique using the catalyst framework. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the convergence properties of the proposed methods and demonstrate their effectiveness through numerical experiments.

The paper has several strengths. First, the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, making it accessible to a broad audience. Second, the technical sections are well-organized and easy to follow, making it easy for readers to understand the proposed methods and their analysis. Third, the authors provide a thorough analysis of the convergence properties of the proposed methods, which is essential for establishing their effectiveness.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. One of the main limitations of the paper is the strong convexity assumption on both the primal and dual problems.
