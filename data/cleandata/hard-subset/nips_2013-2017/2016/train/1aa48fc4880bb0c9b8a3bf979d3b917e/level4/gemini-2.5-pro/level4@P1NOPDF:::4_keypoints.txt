1. Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems and proves linear convergence.  
2. Applicable to challenging non-separable problems (e.g., structured-output losses and fusion regularizers).  
3. Convergence analysis leverages monotone operator theory, making results more general (covers variational inequalities/game-theory settings).  
4. Integrates variance reduction into the stochastic forward–backward algorithm for saddle-point optimization.  
5. Proposes two gradient-splitting schemes: element-wise split (for SVRG) and factored split (for SAGA).  
6. Studies non-uniform sampling and shows both theoretically and empirically that it outperforms uniform sampling.  
7. Comprehensive experiments (main paper + appendix) strongly support the theoretical claims and practical effectiveness.  
8. Minor: unclear how the reference iterate (bar{x}, bar{y}) should be updated adaptively in the acceleration scheme.  
9. Minor: acceleration and convergence proof is provided for SVRG—unclear whether similar guarantees hold for SAGA.  
10. Minor: notation ambiguity between smooth function K(.) and design matrix K; recommend using distinct symbols.
