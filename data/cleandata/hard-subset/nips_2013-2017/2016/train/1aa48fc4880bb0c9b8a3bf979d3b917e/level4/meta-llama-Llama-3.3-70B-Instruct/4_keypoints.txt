- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, achieving linear convergence.  
- Theoretical and empirical evidence show variance reduction is effective for saddle-point problems.  
- Methods handle non-separable saddle-point problems where both loss and regularizer may be non-separable.  
- Main technical contribution: convergence analysis using monotone operator theory to address saddle-point complexity.  
- Analysis demonstrates applicability to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities and game-theoretic settings.  
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA in a stochastic framework.  
- Applies variance reduction to stochastic forward–backward algorithms with non-uniform sampling; non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform.  
- Experimental results (main text and appendix) validate theory and effectiveness on non-separable loss and regularizer.  
- Minor issue: acceleration scheme in Section 5 is unclear on how to adaptively update the reference iterate (\bar{x}, \bar{y}).  
- Minor issue: convergence and acceleration proofs are given for SVRG but it’s unclear if analogous results hold for SAGA.  
- Minor issue: potential symbol confusion—use distinct symbols for the smooth function K(.) and the design matrix K.
