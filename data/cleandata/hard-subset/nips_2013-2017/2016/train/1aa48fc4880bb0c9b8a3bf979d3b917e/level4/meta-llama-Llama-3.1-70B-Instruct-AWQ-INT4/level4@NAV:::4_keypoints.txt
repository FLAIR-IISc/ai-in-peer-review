- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, achieving linear convergence.  
- Theoretical analysis and empirical results validate the efficacy of variance reduction for saddle-point problems.  
- Method handles non-separable losses and regularizers (e.g., structured output prediction, fusion terms).  
- Convergence analysis is more complex than convex case and leverages monotone operator theory.  
- Results extend to broader problems such as variational inequalities in game-theory settings.  
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
- Applies variance reduction to stochastic forward–backward algorithms commonly used for saddle-point problems.  
- Non-uniform sampling is analyzed and shown (theoretically and empirically) to outperform uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results in main paper and appendix substantiate the theoretical claims on non-separable problems.  
- Minor concern: unclear how to adaptively update the auxiliary iterate (x̄, ȳ) in Section 5 to obtain acceleration.  
- Minor concern: convergence and acceleration proof provided for SVRG, but unclear if the same holds for SAGA.  
- Minor suggestion: avoid notation confusion by distinguishing the smooth function K(·) from the design matrix K.
