- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees.
- Methods apply to non-separable losses and regularizers and extend to broader problems (e.g., variational inequalities in game theory).
- Convergence analysis uses monotone operator theory, which is more complex than standard convex analyses.
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits: element-wise for SVRG and factored for SAGA; variance reduction is applied to stochastic forward–backward algorithms.
- Non-uniform sampling is analyzed and empirically shown to outperform uniform sampling.
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including convergence rates and computational complexity.
- Experimental results (main text and appendix) corroborate theory and show effectiveness on non-separable loss and regularizer cases.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and notation is generally clear.
- Technically sound and well-organized; claims are well-supported by theory and experiments.
- Original and significant contribution to stochastic variance reduction literature.
- Methods are practically significant and broadly applicable to ML problems, useful to researchers and practitioners.
- Main downside: paper may be too technical for non-experts.
- Practical limitation: assumes known strong convexity–concavity constants, which may not hold in practice.
- Minor issues: unclear how to adaptively update the extra regularization iterate (x̄, ȳ) for speed-up.
- Minor issue: unclear whether the same convergence/acceleration results proven for SVRG hold for SAGA.
- Minor issue: notation conflict—smooth function K(.) and design matrix K should use different symbols to avoid confusion.
