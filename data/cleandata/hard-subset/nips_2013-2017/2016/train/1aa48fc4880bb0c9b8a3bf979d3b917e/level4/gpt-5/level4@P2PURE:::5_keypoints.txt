- Adaptation of SVRG and SAGA yields the first algorithms with linear convergence for saddle-point problems.  
- Main contribution is a convergence analysis based on monotone operators, since standard convex-minimization analyses do not apply.  
- Analysis extends to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities.  
- Casting problems as saddle-point formulations allows handling non-separable losses and regularizers important for many ML models.  
- Proof technique (viewing saddle-point problems as zeros of a monotone operator) is interesting, broadly applicable, and simplifies proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Empirically, the proposed algorithms with non-uniform sampling are competitive and typically outperform prior methods.  
- Paper advances the field and presents a compelling analysis approach, though the algorithms themselves are straightforward extensions of existing variance-reduction methods.  
- Suggestion: include more analysis details or a proof sketch in the main paper since the main novelty is the proof technique.  
- Clarification requested: what form of non-uniform sampling is used beyond bilinear instances; specifically, what is the distribution π?  
- Clarification requested: how was the step size σ chosen in the experiments?  
- Clarification requested: provide intuition why non-uniform sampling seems essential for saddle-point problems but not necessarily for convex minimization.
