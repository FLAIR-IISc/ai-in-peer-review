- Proposes variance-reduced algorithms (SVRG, SAGA) with a proximal operator for convex-concave saddle-point problems, analyzed in a monotone-operator framework.  
- Proves linear convergence for these methods and presents an accelerated variant plus a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Significant theoretical contributions, including extensions to non-separable objective functions.  
- Presentation is unclear: the connection to monotone operators is relegated to Section 6 and needs clearer exposition earlier.  
- Experiments are limited: evaluate on more than two datasets and include comparisons to accelerated methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock / Zhao et al., 2015).  
- Clarify the strength and applicability of assumptions (A)–(C); indicate whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
- Explain differences between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual methods.  
- Define the constant μ in Theorem 2 (is it the monotonicity constant from the appendix?) and compare Theorem 2 with Theorem 1.  
- Clarify whether assumptions (A)–(C) imply (strong) monotonicity used in the appendix.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Overall: paper has strong potential but requires clearer exposition, additional experiments, and resolution of the listed technical clarifications.
