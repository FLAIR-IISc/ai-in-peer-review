- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, a non-trivial adaptation of variance-reduction methods.  
- Provides convergence analysis showing linear convergence for the adapted algorithms.  
- Leverages monotone operator theory, broadening results to variational inequalities and monotone operators.  
- Highlights the importance and theoretical/empirical benefits of non-uniform sampling for efficiency.  
- Demonstrates potential acceleration via the catalyst framework.  
- Explores different problem “splits” (functions vs. partial derivatives), offering insight into problem structure.  
- Thorough and rigorous theoretical analysis, with clear comparisons to related work.  
- Empirical results validate the methods and confirm benefits of non-uniform sampling and acceleration.  
- Novel contribution: variance-reduction methods applied to saddle-point settings.  
- Practical relevance: addresses an important optimization problem in machine learning with promising efficiency gains.  
- Limitation: requires strong convexity on both primal and dual problems, which is restrictive.  
- Limitation: experimental evaluation is limited in scope and could compare to more state-of-the-art baselines.  
- Suggestion: discuss implementation challenges of non-uniform sampling in high dimensions and parameter tuning for λ and γ.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with valuable contributions; recommended for acceptance if limitations are addressed.
