- Proposes first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle point problems by extending variance-reduction methods (SVRG/SAGA) using a monotone-operator-based proof technique.
- Novel analytical framework interprets saddle-point problems as zeros of monotone operators, enabling broader applicability (e.g., variational inequalities).
- Framework also yields simpler proofs for existing methods like Forward–Backward splitting and stochastic variants.
- Methods are useful for machine learning models with non-separable loss functions and regularizers.
- Significance: addresses an important efficiency problem and delivers the first linear-rate algorithms for this class.
- Empirical performance: proposed algorithms (especially with non-uniform sampling) are competitive and often superior to alternatives.
- Suggestion: include a proof sketch in the main paper to better highlight the core theoretical contribution.
- Clarification needed: the specific non-uniform sampling distribution (π) used for non-bilinear problems is not stated.
- Clarification needed: the procedure for setting the step size (σ) in experiments is not specified.
- Discussion requested: intuition for why non-uniform sampling appears essential for saddle-point problems, unlike some convex minimization cases.
