This paper addresses convex-concave saddle-point problems by adapting stochastic variance reduced techniques, such as SVRG and SAGA, for use with a proximal operator. The authors analyze these algorithms within the monotone operator framework and establish their linear convergence. Furthermore, the paper introduces an accelerated variant and analyzes a non-uniform sampling scheme.

**Novelty/Originality**
The contributions of this paper are significant. The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides new insights into solving saddle-point problems. A key strength is the extension of the proposed framework to non-separable functions, which represents a notable generalization over prior work.

**Technical Quality**
While the theoretical contributions are strong, the presentation could be significantly improved. The paper would be more accessible if the connection to monotone operators were made clearer from the outset. The core analysis relies on the monotone operator framework, yet this connection is relegated to an extension in Section 6, which may confuse the reader.

The experimental evaluation is limited, with results presented on only two datasets. For the case of separable functions, the paper would be strengthened by a comparison to related accelerated methods, such as the stochastic variant of the Chambolle-Pock algorithm presented in:

Zhang, Y. and Lin, Y. "Stochastic Primal-Dual Coordinate Method for Regularized Empirical Risk Minimization." JMLR, 2015.

**Points for Clarification**
I would like the authors to clarify the following points:

1.  How restrictive are assumptions (A)-(C), particularly assumption (A)? For instance, do these assumptions hold for the saddle-point formulation of Support Vector Machines (SVMs)?
2.  The distinction between the proposed method and standard stochastic primal-dual methods is unclear. Could the authors elaborate on the key differences?
3.  In Theorem 2, the definition of the constant μ is ambiguous. Is this the same monotonicity constant defined in the appendix? Furthermore, how does the convergence rate in Theorem 2 compare to the one presented in Theorem 1?
4.  The connection between assumptions (A)-(C) and the strong monotonicity property used in the appendix is not clearly established. Do assumptions (A)-(C) imply strong monotonicity?

**Minor Issues**

*   There appears to be inconsistent notation for the constant L, which is variously referred to as both the condition number ("The quantity L represents the condition number of the problem") and a Lipschitz constant ("...we need the Lipschitz constant L"). Please clarify this.
*   Please provide a reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm.
