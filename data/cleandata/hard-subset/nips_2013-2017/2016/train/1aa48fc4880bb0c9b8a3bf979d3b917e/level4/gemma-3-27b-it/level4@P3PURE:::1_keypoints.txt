- Proposes applying SVRG and SAGA with a proximal operator to convex–concave saddle-point problems, analyzed via monotone-operator theory.  
- Establishes linear convergence guarantees for these variance-reduced methods.  
- Introduces an accelerated variant and studies a non-uniform sampling scheme.  
- Contributions are significant and thorough, with notable applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation weakens impact: the role of monotone-operator theory should be made more explicit rather than relegated to Section 6.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets) and needs broader empirical validation.  
- Lacks comparison to accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock / Zhang & Peyré 2015).  
- Question on assumptions A–C: need clarification on how restrictive they are and whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
- Request to clarify differences between the proposed method and existing stochastic primal–dual algorithms.  
- Theorem 2 uses a constant μ that is not fully defined—clarify whether this is the monotonicity constant and how Theorem 2’s rate compares to Theorem 1.  
- Unclear whether assumptions (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity; the transition in the appendix needs explanation.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and missing reference/discussion of the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Recommend adding the Zhang & Peyré (2015) JMLR reference on stochastic variance reduction.
