- Presents first linearly convergent incremental SVRG/SAGA algorithms for general convex-concave saddle-point problems, addressing an important gap for ML applications.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and based on monotone operator theory, yielding linear convergence and novel insights beyond convex minimization.
- Extends methods with Catalyst-style acceleration and non-uniform sampling, offering practical and theoretical improvements.
- Applicable to non-separable objectives, a notable strength not handled by many existing variance-reduced methods.
- Paper organization and presentation could be improved by introducing the monotone-operator framework earlier to better motivate the analysis.
- Empirical evaluation is limited to two datasets and should be broadened to demonstrate general applicability and robustness.
- Needs stronger comparisons to related stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock); include Zhang & Xiao (2015) as a relevant reference and baseline.
- Clarify the scope and restrictiveness of assumptions (A)–(C), especially assumption (A) on strong convex-concavity, and whether canonical ML problems (e.g., SVM saddle-point) satisfy them.
- Better position the proposed methods relative to existing stochastic primal-dual literature, highlighting conceptual and algorithmic differences.
- Define the constant µ explicitly in the main text (likely the monotonicity constant) and compare theoretical rates of Theorems 1 (SVRG) and 2 (SAGA) more directly.
- Explain briefly in the main text how assumptions (A)–(C) imply strong monotonicity used in proofs to improve readability.
- Ensure consistent and precise use/definition of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) throughout the paper.
- Add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm in Section 2.2.
