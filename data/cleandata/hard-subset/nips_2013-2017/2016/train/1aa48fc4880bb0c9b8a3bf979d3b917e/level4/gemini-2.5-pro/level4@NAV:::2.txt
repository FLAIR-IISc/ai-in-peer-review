This paper addresses the convex-concave saddle point problem, extending recent variance reduction methods to this setting. The authors provide a theoretical convergence analysis for the proposed algorithms and demonstrate that they can be effectively accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework. The work also includes a generalization to monotone operators and presents supporting numerical results.

A key limitation is the strong convexity assumption on both the primal and dual problems, which is a very restrictive condition. Typically, the standard assumption in the literature is that only the primal or the dual problem is strongly convex. Furthermore, the paper would be strengthened by including a more comprehensive set of numerical examples to better validate the methods.
