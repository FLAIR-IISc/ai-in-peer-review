- Proposes first linearly convergent stochastic variance-reduction algorithms (SVRG, SAGA) for saddle-point problems.  
- Main technical contribution: use of monotone operator framework to prove convergence, extending analysis to variational inequalities.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers common in ML.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis: convergence rates and computational complexity.  
- Empirical evaluation on binary classification with non-separable losses/regularizers shows competitiveness, often outperforming alternatives.  
- Non-uniform sampling variant is effective and emphasized in experiments.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Related work discussion is detailed and the approach is original and significant to optimization/ML.  
- Weakness: needs more detailed discussion of non-uniform sampling for problems beyond bilinear saddle-point cases.  
- Weakness: lacks intuition on why non-uniform sampling is crucial for saddle-point problems but not for convex minimization.  
- Minor issues: typo on line 78 (k -> d) and extra y_{t-1} term in the second equality on line 121.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions; overall rating 8/10 and confidence 8/10.
