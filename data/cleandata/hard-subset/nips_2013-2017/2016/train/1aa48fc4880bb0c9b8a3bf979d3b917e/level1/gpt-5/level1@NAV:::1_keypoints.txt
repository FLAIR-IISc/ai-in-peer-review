- Extends SVRG/SAGA to composite convex–concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence under strong convexity–concavity.  
- Key technical move: analyze forward–backward splitting in the monotone operator framework, avoiding co-coercivity and covering general saddle-point and variational inequality problems.  
- Proposes two decompositions (by functions and by partial derivatives/factored operators); factored splits and non-uniform sampling give clear complexity gains.  
- Introduces proximal-point/Catalyst-style outer-loop acceleration that dominates accelerated batch alternatives.  
- Positions the work against batch primal–dual/forward–backward methods (e.g., Chambolle–Pock), stochastic FB, and separable-coordinate methods, clarifying relations.  
- Theory is careful and technically sound: linear rates for SVRG/SAGA, explicit step sizes, and complexity bounds in terms of L and sampling-dependent smoothness L̄(π).  
- Practical aspects covered: mini-batching, storage/computation trade-offs for factored vs individual splits, and resampling for non-uniform SAGA.  
- Experiments on non-separable losses/regularizers corroborate theory, especially for ill-conditioned problems, but are limited (two datasets, few baselines).  
- Missing empirical comparisons to extra-gradient/mirror-prox style methods.  
- Clarity issues: dense notation (Ω, L vs L̄), the “splits” discussion could be tightened, and guidance on choosing π, m, and step sizes is lacking.  
- A brief intuitive explanation and computation guidance for L̄(π) would improve readability.  
- Method requires known strong convexity parameters and lacks adaptive schemes.  
- Originality: high — likely the first linearly convergent variance-reduced framework for general non-separable saddle points and a novel catalyst-style acceleration in this setting.  
- Significance: strong — broadens scalable, provable methods to many ML problems and general monotone inclusions.  
- Overall assessment: strong, significant contribution within NeurIPS scope; recommend acceptance.
