- Main contribution: Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, giving the first linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Theoretical innovation: Uses monotone operator theory for rigorous convergence analysis, addressing nontrivial technical challenges beyond convex minimization.  
- Structural insight: Identifies two distinct split notions (functions vs. partial derivatives) and the necessity of convex-concave splits for saddle-point problems.  
- Sampling result: Shows non-uniform sampling is critical for efficiency and improved complexity.  
- Acceleration: Extends the catalyst framework to accelerate variance-reduced methods for saddle-point settings.  
- Complexity: Provides theoretical complexity bounds demonstrating improvement over batch algorithms, especially for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments on non-separable ML problems (e.g., AUC optimization, grouped regularizers) show substantial gains, with accelerated SVRG + non-uniform sampling performing best.  
- Clarity and presentation: Exceptionally well-organized paper with clear formulations, detailed algorithms, and relevant ML examples.  
- Generality: Extension to monotone operators broadens applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Weakness — assumptions: Analysis requires known strong convexity-concavity constants, limiting practicality in some settings.  
- Weakness — experiments: Limited experimental scope focused on a single ML problem; broader empirical validation is needed.  
- Weakness — accessibility: Heavy reliance on monotone operator theory may be hard for non-specialist readers.  
- Acceptance case: First rigorous variance-reduction convergence for saddle-points, theoretical and practical superiority, novel structural insights, and broad potential impact.  
- Rejection risks: Strong assumptions, limited experimental diversity, and potential accessibility issues.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — substantial, rigorous contribution enabling large-scale linearly convergent optimization for important ML problems.
