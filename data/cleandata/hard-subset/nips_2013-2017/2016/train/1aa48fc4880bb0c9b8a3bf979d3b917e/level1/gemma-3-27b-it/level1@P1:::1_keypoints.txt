- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, adapting variance-reduction methods from convex minimization to min-max settings.  
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence analysis, generalizing results to variational inequalities.  
- Identifies key technical challenges: adapting convergence proofs, choosing split formulations, and ensuring convex-concave decompositions.  
- Emphasizes the importance of non-uniform sampling for performance.  
- Proposes acceleration via a catalyst framework for faster convergence in the saddle-point context.  
- Builds on and positions itself relative to substantial prior work in stochastic and batch saddle-point optimization.  
- Novelty: valuable contribution despite straightforward algorithmic extensions, due to theoretical insights and practical considerations.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous, complete convergence analysis with clear rate bounds.  
- Practical relevance: addresses common ML problems and shows promising empirical performance.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, consistent notation, and good connection to related work.  
- Comprehensive analysis: explores split formulations, sampling strategies, and acceleration effects.  
- Experimental weaknesses: dataset choices and setup need stronger justification and comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Assumptions: strong convex-concavity conditions may limit applicability; robustness to assumption violations is not fully explored.  
- Complexity analysis: detailed but hard to interpret practically due to many parameters (L, L̄, T(B), T(Bi)).  
- Catalyst discussion: acceleration presented but explanation is brief and would benefit from more detail.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments and clarify the catalyst method.
