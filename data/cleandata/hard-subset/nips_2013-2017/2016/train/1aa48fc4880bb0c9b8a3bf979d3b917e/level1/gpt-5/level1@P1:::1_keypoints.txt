- Studies convex-concave saddle-point problems min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with proximable, strongly convex-concave M and smooth, possibly nonseparable K, extending SVRG/SAGA to this setting.
- Key algorithmic idea: replace full gradients of K by variance-reduced stochastic estimates of operator B=(∂xK, -∂yK) and analyze via monotone operator theory.
- Main technical contributions: formalize two split notions (by functions or partial derivatives), require splits to preserve convex-concave structure, and handle Bi that are not gradients.
- Derives importance sampling rules and a weighted Lipschitz constant L̄(π) that sharpens complexity bounds.
- Provides storage-efficient SAGA updates for factored (row/column) splits of bilinear K with O(n+d) storage.
- Proposes a Catalyst-style proximal-point acceleration for SPs that improves complexity and is claimed superior (up to constants) to accelerated batch methods.
- Analysis: careful forward-backward operator-splitting with resolvent of A=∂M and forward steps on B=∇K in a weighted norm, avoiding cocoercivity assumptions.
- Proves linear convergence: SVRG per-epoch linear rate, SAGA per-iteration linear rate, and geometric contraction for accelerated proximal outer loop.
- Complexity bounds reflect T(B), T(Bi), Tprox(M) and use mini-batching to offset proximal costs; bilinear K analysis leverages operator/Frobenius/max-norm inequalities.
- Accelerated complexity scales like L̄√T(B) max_i T(Bi) (up to logs), consistent with Catalyst intuition.
- Experiments (two datasets) show importance sampling matters, SAGA/SVRG beat batch FB, acceleration helps for ill-conditioned problems, and primal SAGA is best when separability holds.
- Paper is dense but well-organized; problem setup, operator definitions, and pseudocode are clear, with helpful discussion of splits and SAGA storage.
- Clarity could improve with more intuition on why FB suits strongly convex-concave SPs vs extragradient, why cocoercivity fails, and practical estimation of L and L̄.
- Originality: novel monotone-operator extension of SVRG/SAGA to SPs where Bi need not be gradients; factored split and importance sampling for bilinear K; new proximal-point acceleration for monotone operators.
- Significance: broadens variance reduction and acceleration to nonseparable SPs with proximable M, likely useful for large-scale problems and variational inequalities.
- Pros: first linear-convergent VR methods for general convex-concave SPs, concrete importance sampling bounds, storage trick for SAGA, and effective acceleration with matching theory.
- Cons: limited experimental scope (two datasets, missing EG/Mirror-Prox baselines), assumes known strong-convexity parameters, and lacks empirical analysis of non-uniform sampling overhead and comparisons to SPDC.
- Suggestions: add extragradient/Mirror-Prox baselines, practical guidance and sensitivity analysis for L and L̄ estimation, clarify non-uniform SAGA resampling, include VI/robust optimization experiments, and discuss artificial strong convexity trade-offs.
- Overall: high-quality theoretical contribution with promising practical implications; experiments are aligned but could be broadened; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
