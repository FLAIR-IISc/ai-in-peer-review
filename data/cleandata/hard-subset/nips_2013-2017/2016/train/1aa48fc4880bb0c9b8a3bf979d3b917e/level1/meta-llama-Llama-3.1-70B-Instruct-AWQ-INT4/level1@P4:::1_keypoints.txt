- Summary: Introduces stochastic variance reduction (SVRG/SAGA) for saddle-point problems, giving the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms and a distinct convergence analysis; highlights two split notions (functions vs partial derivatives) and the importance of non-uniform sampling.

- Strength: Novel extension of SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, filling a gap in optimization methods.

- Strength: Provides a thorough and rigorous convergence analysis tailored to the saddle-point setting.

- Strength: Demonstrates improved convergence rates over existing batch and stochastic methods.

- Strength: Extends the approach to monotone operators, covering a broader class of problems beyond saddle-points.

- Weakness: Analysis assumes strong convexity–concavity, which may not hold in many practical problems.

- Weakness: Requires knowledge of strong convexity–concavity constants, which are not always available.

- Weakness: Limited experimental evaluation, leaving empirical effectiveness under-explored.

- Originality: Proposes a new algorithmic framework for saddle-point optimization.

- Originality: Offers novel analytical techniques specific to the proposed algorithms.

- Significance: Potential to impact ML tasks (e.g., supervised learning, robust optimization) by enabling scalable saddle-point solvers.

- Significance: Broader implications for optimization and ML through a new solution framework.

- Quality: Technically sound with clear presentation and solid theoretical support.

- Quality: Well-written and easy to follow.

- For acceptance: Novel contribution, thorough analysis, and potential ML impact argue in favor.

- For rejection: Limited experiments, reliance on strong convexity–concavity, and need for problem constants argue against.
