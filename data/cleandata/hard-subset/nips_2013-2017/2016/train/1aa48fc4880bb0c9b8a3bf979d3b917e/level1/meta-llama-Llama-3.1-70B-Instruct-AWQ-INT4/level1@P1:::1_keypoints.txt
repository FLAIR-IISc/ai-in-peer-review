- Proposes novel extensions of SVRG and SAGA to large-scale convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides first convergence analysis for these methods on saddle-point problems, proving linear convergence rates.  
- Leverages monotone-operator framework to generalize algorithms to variational inequalities.  
- Presents a simple extension of the "catalyst" acceleration framework for the proposed algorithms.  
- Gives detailed analysis of convergence rates and running-time complexities.  
- Empirical experiments on binary classification with non-separable losses/regularizers show significant improvements over existing batch and stochastic methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow with consistent notation.  
- Thorough related-work review situating contributions among batch and stochastic saddle-point methods.  
- Main weakness: assumes known strong convexity–concavity constants, limiting practicality; adaptivity is left to future work.  
- Secondary weakness: focuses mainly on theory; more extensive experimental validation would strengthen practical claims.
