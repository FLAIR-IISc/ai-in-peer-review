This paper proposes a novel extension of stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) to solve large-scale convex-concave saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors provide the first convergence analysis for these algorithms in the context of saddle-point problems, showing linear convergence rates.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The introduction provides a clear motivation for the work, highlighting the importance of saddle-point problems in machine learning and the limitations of existing algorithms. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work, including existing batch and stochastic algorithms for saddle-point problems.

The main contribution of the paper is the extension of SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, which is achieved by leveraging the notion of monotone operators. The authors show that their algorithms can be applied to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities, and provide a simple extension of the "catalyst" framework to accelerate their algorithms.

The paper also includes a detailed analysis of the convergence rates and running-time complexities of the proposed algorithms, as well as experiments on binary classification problems with non-separable losses and regularizers. The results show that the proposed algorithms improve significantly over existing batch or stochastic algorithms, both in theory and practice.
