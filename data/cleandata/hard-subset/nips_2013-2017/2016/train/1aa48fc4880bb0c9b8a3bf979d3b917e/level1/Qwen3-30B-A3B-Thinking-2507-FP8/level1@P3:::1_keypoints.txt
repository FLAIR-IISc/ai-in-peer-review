- Extends stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) to saddle-point problems, addressing a notable gap in the literature.  
- Provides the first convergence analysis for these algorithms in the saddle-point setting using monotone operator theory.  
- Introduces the novel distinction between function-based and partial-derivative-based splits.  
- Emphasizes and analyzes the critical role of non-uniform sampling.  
- Demonstrates acceleration of the proposed methods via the "catalyst" framework.  
- Correctly notes convex minimization convergence theory is inapplicable and leverages monotone operator theory to prove linear convergence.  
- Extends results to general monotone operators, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous; algorithms are precisely described with consistent notation.  
- Experiments convincingly show superiority over batch and stochastic alternatives, particularly on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Main limitation: assumption of strong convexity-concavity is restrictive and requires known constants.  
- Comparison to other stochastic saddle-point or adaptive sampling methods is not comprehensive.  
- Overall significance is high for ML and related fields; reviewer recommends acceptance.
