- Tackles scaling algorithms for convex-concave saddle-point problems common in ML.
- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point setting, providing first linearly convergent large-scale algorithms.
- Adaptation idea is straightforward but analysis and extensions are substantial.
- Uses monotone operators as a unifying framework, broadening applicability beyond standard saddle points.
- Identifies two types of problem “splits” (function-wise and partial derivative-wise) and highlights importance of non-uniform sampling.
- Includes acceleration via the “catalyst” framework for practical speedups.
- Technical analysis is rigorous and addresses differences between convex minimization and saddle-point problems.
- Original contribution: novel extension of variance-reduction methods to saddle-point problems with proven linear convergence.
- Significant potential impact for scaling many machine learning and variational inequality applications.
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and clearly motivates the work.
- Experiments are presented but lack detailed dataset descriptions, hyperparameter tuning, and broader method comparisons.
- Practical challenges of implementing non-uniform sampling are not sufficiently discussed.
- Assumptions (A)-(C) are fairly general but their practical implications and sensitivity to violations are underexplored.
- Theoretical complexity analysis is detailed but hard to parse; needs more intuitive explanation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/significant contribution, rigorous guarantees, and broad practical potential.
- Arguments against acceptance: empirical validation insufficient and complexity discussion could be clearer.
- Overall assessment: strong paper; strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
