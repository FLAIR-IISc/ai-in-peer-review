- Main contribution: Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, yielding the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this setting.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction, motivation, and related work.  
- Technical depth: Substantial technical contributions with thorough analysis of convergence rates and computational complexities.  
- Novelty: Novel extension of variance-reduction methods to saddle-point problems, a significant advancement.  
- Comparative analysis: Detailed comparison with existing batch and stochastic methods showing superiority of the proposed approach.  
- Experiments: Convincing empirical results demonstrating practical effectiveness of the algorithms.  
- Weakness — parameter knowledge: Assumes known strong convexity–concavity constants, which may be unavailable in practice (authors plan to explore adaptivity).  
- Weakness — problem scope: Primarily addresses bilinear saddle-point problems; generalization to broader saddle-point problems is unclear.  
- Recommendation: Strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
