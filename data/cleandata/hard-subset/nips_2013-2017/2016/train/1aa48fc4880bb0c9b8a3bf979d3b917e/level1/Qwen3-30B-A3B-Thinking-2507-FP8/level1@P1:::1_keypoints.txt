- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, filling a gap in variance-reduction literature for non-minimization problems.  
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence analysis, a novel departure from standard convex minimization techniques.  
- Extends the "catalyst" acceleration framework to saddle-point problems, yielding the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for these problems.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical results (Theorems 1–3) with detailed proofs, showing strong technical quality.  
- Highlights and analyzes two problem-splitting approaches (function-based vs. partial-derivative-based) and prefers convex-concave splits.  
- Demonstrates the theoretical and empirical importance of non-uniform sampling for efficiency.  
- Experiments are well-designed and validate theoretical claims, comparing to batch methods, uniform sampling, and primal methods.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and uses consistent mathematical notation.  
- Clarifies scope and contributions relative to prior work on variance reduction and saddle-point methods.  
- Original contributions include first convergence analysis for variance reduction on saddle-points and broader applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Results have substantial significance for many ML problems (non-separable losses, robust optimization, convex relaxations).  
- Strengths: novel analysis, monotone-operator approach, catalyst extension, clear empirical validation, practical relevance.  
- Weaknesses: requires known strong convexity–concavity constants (λ, γ), limited intuition on necessity of monotone operators, assumes strong convex-concave structure.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, empirically validated, and broadly relevant to ML applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on strong convexity–concavity knowledge and lack of extension to non-strongly convex-concave settings.  
- Conclusion: technically strong, well-presented, and significant work with clear recommendation for acceptance.
