- Extends SVRG/SAGA-style variance reduction to composite convex–concave saddle-point problems using monotone-operator theory.  
- Provides linear-convergence analyses for forward–backward splitting in the saddle-point setting (avoids cocoercivity assumptions).  
- Introduces two finite-sum splitting notions, including “factored” splits that align with saddle-point structure.  
- Develops principled non-uniform sampling with refined smoothness constants L̄(π) for improved complexity.  
- Proposes an acceleration via a proximal-point/Catalyst-style outer loop tailored to saddle points with better complexity.  
- Extends analysis to finding zeros of sums of monotone operators and variational inequalities beyond saddle points.  
- Builds on existing variance-reduction and primal–dual literature but is novel in handling non-separable composite saddle points.  
- Technical analysis is strong, with explicit algorithms, stepsizes, mini-batch sizes, and oracle-cost-aware complexity bounds.  
- Monotone-operator viewpoint is natural and necessary for saddle-point problems where cocoercivity fails.  
- Bilinear case treated in detail, with factored vs. elementwise splits and interpretable norm-based complexities.  
- Caveat: SAGA’s non-uniform-sampling guarantee requires a resampling step; practical variant used is not theoretically covered.  
- Caveat: Some bounds depend on L and L̄; paper lacks guidance or adaptive/backtracking strategies for estimating them.  
- Caveat: Acceleration analysis is provided only for SVRG; accelerated SAGA is claimed but not proved.  
- Caveat: Baseline discussion could better compare to extragradient/mirror-prox methods traditionally used for monotone VIs.  
- Empirically shows SAGA/SVRG outperform batch and stochastic FB and acceleration helps on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Empirical section is limited (two tasks) and missing important ablations (sampling sensitivity, mini-batch size, split strategies) and extragradient baselines.  
- Paper is dense but readable; monotone-operator framing and sampling discussion are clear, though some notation and practical guidance could be improved.  
- Originality: first to provide variance-reduced linear rates for general composite saddle points via monotone-operator analysis and to identify factored splits.  
- Significance: applicable to many ML problems with non-separable structure; offers practical sampling/acceleration tools but requires strong convex–concavity and proximal access.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid general theory, practical non-uniform sampling and factored splits, provable acceleration, extension to monotone inclusions, supportive experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited experiments and baselines, reliance on known problem constants, theoretical gap for practical SAGA variant and SAGA acceleration.  
- Suggestions: add extragradient/mirror-prox comparisons, provide/adapt backtracking or robustness analysis for L and L̄, include empirical ablations and clearer plotting of primal–dual gaps.  
- Overall: technically strong, novel, and broadly applicable work with limited experiments — reviewer recommends acceptance.
