The paper "Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems" proposes an extension of stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) to solve convex-concave saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors provide the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems and demonstrate their effectiveness through experiments.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem, related work, and their contributions. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the authors provide a detailed proof of the convergence rates of their algorithms.

The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the convergence rates of their algorithms and demonstrate their effectiveness through experiments.
