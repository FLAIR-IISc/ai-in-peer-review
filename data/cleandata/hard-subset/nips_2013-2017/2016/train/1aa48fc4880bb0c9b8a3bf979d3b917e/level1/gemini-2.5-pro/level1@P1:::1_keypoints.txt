- Paper extends SVRG and SAGA to finite-sum convex-concave saddle-point problems of form min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with K smooth finite-sum and M proximal but possibly non-smooth.  
- Provides first linear convergence analysis for SVRG and SAGA in the saddle-point setting using monotone operator theory instead of co-coercivity.  
- Analysis generalizes to variational inequalities and a broader class of monotone operators.  
- Studies saddle-point-specific issues: different operator splits (functional vs. factored) and shows non-uniform sampling is crucial for best rates.  
- Introduces an accelerated variant via a catalyst-like framework that is provably superior to accelerated full-batch methods.  
- Significance: enables efficient large-scale optimization for important ML problems with non-separable losses/regularizers (e.g., TV, overlapping group lasso, AUC).  
- Originality/quality: technically strong, novel convergence proofs, comprehensive theoretical results summarized in Table 1 and proved in the appendix.  
- Empirical validation: experiments support theory, showing accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling outperforms competitors (but scope is narrow).  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and presents assumptions, algorithms, and complexity comparisons clearly.  
- Weakness — limited experimental scope: empirical evaluation is confined to one binary classification task despite broader motivations.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter practicality: tuning-sensitive parameters (step-size σ, catalyst τ) depend on constants L and L̄ that may be unknown or costly to compute; no practical tuning guidance or sensitivity analysis.  
- Weakness — minor clarity in Algorithm 2: resampling step for non-uniform sampling labeled “(optional)” despite being required for theoretical guarantees.  
- Pro acceptance: first to achieve linear-convergent variance-reduced algorithms for finite-sum saddle-point problems, strong and general theoretical contribution, practical speedups, and excellent exposition.  
- Con acceptance: limited empirical validation across motivated application domains and lack of practical guidance for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Overall recommendation: technically outstanding and practically important work; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
