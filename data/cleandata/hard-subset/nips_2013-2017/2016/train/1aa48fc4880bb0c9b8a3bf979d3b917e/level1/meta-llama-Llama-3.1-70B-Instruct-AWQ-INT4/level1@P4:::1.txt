**Summary**

This paper introduces stochastic variance reduction methods for solving saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors extend recent stochastic variance reduction methods, such as SVRG and SAGA, to provide the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems. The paper provides a convergence analysis for these algorithms, which differs significantly from the associated convex minimization set-up. The authors also show that the saddle-point formulation allows for two different notions of splits, in terms of functions or in terms of partial derivatives, and that non-uniform sampling is key to an efficient algorithm.
