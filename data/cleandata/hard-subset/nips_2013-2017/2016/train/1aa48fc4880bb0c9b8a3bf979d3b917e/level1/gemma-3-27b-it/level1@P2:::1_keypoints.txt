- Introduces SVRG and SAGA for convex-concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Extends variance-reduction methods to saddle-point formulations and proves their linear convergence.  
- Demonstrates acceleration via the catalyst framework.  
- Properly situates work among SGD, SAG/SVRG/SAGA, and prior saddle-point/variational-inequality literature.  
- Connects analysis to monotone operator theory and justifies why saddle-point analysis differs from convex minimization.  
- Insightful comparison of function-level vs. derivative-level splitting and the role of non-uniform sampling.  
- Technically strong and rigorous convergence analysis, largely complete.  
- Practical motivation is clear; algorithms are scalable and relevant for non-separable losses and regularizers.  
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Some theoretical sections lack clarity and smoother transitions from assumptions to proofs.  
- Heavy reliance on assumptions (A)-(C); robustness to violations is not fully discussed.  
- Empirical evaluation could be broadened to more datasets and settings.  
- Extension to monotone operators broadens applicability and impact.  
- Overall assessment: strong, original, and impactful work recommended for acceptance.
