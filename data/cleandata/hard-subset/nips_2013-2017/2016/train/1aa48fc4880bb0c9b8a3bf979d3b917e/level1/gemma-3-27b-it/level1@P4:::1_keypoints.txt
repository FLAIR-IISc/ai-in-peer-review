- Proposes stochastic variance reduction (SVRG and SAGA) for convex-concave saddle-point problems and extends them to monotone operators with convergence analysis.  
- Adapts variance reduction techniques to saddle-point settings, highlighting differences from standard convex minimization.  
- Provides the first linearly convergent stochastic variance-reduced algorithms for large-scale saddle-point problems.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and generalizes via monotone operator theory.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and uses consistent mathematical notation.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness, especially for the accelerated variant, with thorough comparisons to batch and stochastic baselines.  
- Detailed discussion of split types, non-uniform sampling, and Catalyst acceleration adds practical and theoretical insight.  
- Assumptions (A)-(C) are relatively strong and their practical limitations are not thoroughly discussed.  
- Convergence rates depend on constants (L, L̄) that may be hard to estimate in practice, with limited discussion of implications.  
- Practical implementation likely requires careful hyperparameter tuning (step size, sampling probabilities) that is underexplored.  
- The Catalyst-accelerated variant’s overhead and practical benefits need clearer empirical justification.  
- Experiments are limited to a few datasets; broader empirical evaluation would strengthen claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel, and theoretically sound contribution with supporting experiments and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: strong assumptions, dependence on hard-to-estimate constants, and limited experimental scope.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contributions and minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
