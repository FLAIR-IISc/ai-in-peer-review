- Extends SVRG and SAGA to general convex–concave saddle-point problems, yielding the first large-scale, linearly convergent algorithms for this class.
- Addresses important ML applications (non-separable losses/regularizers, robust optimization, unsupervised relaxations).
- Provides a novel convergence analysis based on monotone operator theory, extending beyond standard convex optimization and covering variational inequalities.
- Introduces an acceleration scheme using the "catalyst" framework and highlights the practical benefit of non-uniform sampling.
- Significance: closes an important gap by enabling variance reduction for finite-sum saddle-point problems.
- Originality: theoretical analysis is novel and elegant despite straightforward algorithmic adaptations.
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous proofs and empirical results showing improvements over batch and stochastic baselines.
- Clarity: manuscript is well-written, well-organized, and clearly motivates and explains contributions.
- Weakness: step-size choices depend on problem constants (strong convexity/concavity, Lipschitz) that may be unknown in practice.
- Weakness: experiments, while convincing, are limited in scope and could be broadened for stronger practical validation.
