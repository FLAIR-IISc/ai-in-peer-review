- Extends SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point problems using a monotone-operator framework.  
- Well-motivated adaptation to convex-concave structure; highlights importance of splitting choices and non-uniform sampling.  
- Includes catalyst acceleration, improving practical performance.  
- Introduction clearly motivates relevance with ML examples (supervised learning, robust optimization).  
- Theoretical foundations are solid and broadly applicable beyond pure saddle-point problems.  
- Analysis is rigorous but dense; correctness seems plausible though full proof verification needed.  
- Thorough treatment of subtleties (two notions of splitting, sampling strategies) with extensive experiments supporting claims.  
- Generally clear and well-organized writing.  
- Heavy notation and complex analysis may reduce accessibility; could use more intuitive explanations.  
- Experiments focus mainly on binary classification with one regularizer; broader problem/dataset coverage is needed.  
- Limited comparison to primal methods and to recent accelerated saddle-point literature.  
- No exploration of adaptive step-size strategies; analysis assumes known constants (L, etc.).  
- Lacks in-depth discussion of practical implementation challenges (parameter tuning, ill-conditioning).  
- Should explicitly compare/contrast with related work (e.g., Necoara & Talmage 2019).  
- Pro-accept: addresses significant ML optimization problem, rigorous analysis, effective experiments, broad applicability.  
- Con-accept: dense theory, limited experimental scope, insufficient practical guidance.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept.  
- Suggested improvements: add intuitive theory explanations, expand experiments, discuss implementation/tuning, compare to recent work, investigate adaptive step-sizes.
