- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, giving the first linearly convergent large-scale algorithms for this class.
- Proofs use monotone operator theory, showing applicability beyond saddle-point problems to variational inequalities.
- Handles non-separable functions, a major advance for problems like structured prediction and robust optimization.
- Proposes non-uniform sampling strategies and demonstrates acceleration via the catalyst framework.
- Connects to prior stochastic optimization work (SAGA, SVRG) but crucially extends analysis to settings where convex minimization arguments fail.
- Theoretical novelty and significance are clear, with acceleration showing advantages over batch methods.
- The monotone-operator connection is buried in Section 6 despite being fundamental, causing clarity issues.
- The constant μ in Theorem 2 is undefined and the link from assumptions (A)-(C) to strong monotonicity is unclear.
- Authors should explicitly state whether assumptions A–C imply monotonicity and clarify transitions in the analysis.
- Presentation omits a key reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm (e.g., [20]) and uses L inconsistently for condition number and Lipschitz constant.
- Experiments are limited to two datasets (sido, rcv1) and lack comparisons to relevant methods like accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock (Zhang & Xiao 2015).
- Paper should verify whether assumptions hold for common SVM-induced saddle-point problems and clarify differences from standard stochastic primal–dual methods.
- Due to presentation weaknesses, limited experiments, and theoretical ambiguities, the paper lacks the completeness expected for NIPS.
- Suggested fixes: clearer exposition of monotone-operator framework, additional experimental validation, and proper comparison to accelerated stochastic primal–dual baselines.
