- Introduces SVRG and SAGA extensions for large-scale saddle-point problems with first linear convergence guarantees in this setting.  
- Extends analysis to monotone operators, broadening applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Well-motivated: addresses limitations of existing methods on non-separable losses/regularizers.  
- Strong connection to related work in SGD and variance reduction (SAG, SVRG, SAGA) and convex/monotone operator theory.  
- Introduces two notions of “splits” (functions vs partial derivatives), clarifying algorithm adaptation for saddle points.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and based on monotone operator theory to establish convergence.  
- Emphasizes non-uniform sampling with theoretical support and experimental validation for practical efficiency.  
- Uses catalyst-style acceleration to improve algorithm performance.  
- Experimental evaluation (in appendix) shows compelling empirical gains over batch and stochastic baselines.  
- Clear identification of benefits of factored splits versus element-wise splits for bilinear problems.  
- Accessibility concern: reliance on monotone operator theory may limit readership comprehension.  
- Notational ambiguity: reuse of symbol ‘K’ for different concepts could confuse readers.  
- Incomplete discussion of practical/adaptive updates for the reference iterate in the acceleration section.  
- Claim that acceleration extends to SAGA lacks a formal proof and requires strengthening.  
- Assumes strong convexity-concavity; relaxing this assumption is not explored but would be valuable.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practically relevant work recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
