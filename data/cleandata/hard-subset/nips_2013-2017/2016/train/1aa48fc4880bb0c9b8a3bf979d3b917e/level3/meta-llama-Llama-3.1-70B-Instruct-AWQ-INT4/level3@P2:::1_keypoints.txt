- Novel extension of SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems using proximal operators, achieving linear convergence.  
- Comprehensive theoretical analysis including monotone-operator framework, acceleration techniques, and non-uniform sampling.  
- Significant contribution for applying variance-reduction methods to non-separable functions.  
- Connection to monotone operators is important but introduced too late; should be made explicit earlier.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets); needs more datasets and stronger baseline comparisons.  
- Missing comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock–type methods (e.g., Zhang & Xiao 2015).  
- Need to clarify strength/applicability of assumptions A–C—especially assumption A—and whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
- Unclear whether assumptions A–C imply monotonicity; the transition to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing.  
- Differences between standard stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed method require clearer exposition.  
- In Theorem 2 the constant μ is unclear—confirm if it is the monotonicity constant from the appendix and compare to Theorem 1.  
- Notational inconsistency: L is used ambiguously (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant); standardize notation.  
- Missing citation/reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Overall quality: technically sound with well-supported claims and careful evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing presentation issues, theoretical clarifications, expanded experiments, and missing comparisons.
