Summary and relation to prior work: The paper studies convex–concave saddle-point problems with proximal structure and extends SVRG/SAGA to this setting, proving linear convergence in a monotone operator framework, proposing acceleration via a Catalyst-like outer loop, and advocating non-uniform sampling. A key insight is to work with operators rather than separable functions, enabling applicability to non-separable losses/regularizers and even beyond saddle points to general monotone inclusions/variational inequalities. This relates to variance-reduced methods in finite-sum convex minimization (SAG/SVRG/SAGA), primal–dual methods (Chambolle–Pock), and accelerated stochastic primal–dual schemes such as SPDC (Zhang & Xiao, JMLR 2015). The theoretical treatment via monotone operators is novel for variance reduction in saddle-point settings.

Quality: The technical contribution is strong: linear rates for SVRG/SAGA in a strongly monotone operator setting, attention to non-uniform sampling, and an acceleration strategy that yields improved complexities. However, clarity of assumptions and constants needs improvement. Assumptions (A)–(C): please state explicitly whether (A)–(C) imply monotonicity of B and strong monotonicity of A (A provides strong monotonicity via the proxable part; B is monotone for convex–concave K). In Theorem 2, the constant μ is not clearly defined—confirm it is the strong monotonicity parameter (from (A)/appendix) and explain how rates compare to Theorem 1 when expressed in L/μ. Also clarify the transition from (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix. The experimental section is limited (two datasets) and lacks comparisons to accelerated stochastic primal–dual methods (e.g., SPDC; accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock-type variants). Add Zhang & Xiao (2015, JMLR) and compare empirically.

Clarity: Presentation is uneven. The connection to monotone operators underpins the analysis but appears late; move this framing to the main text and sharpen the mapping between saddle-point assumptions and operator assumptions. Unify notation: L is used both as a Lipschitz constant and a “condition number”; please use κ = L/μ (or L²/μ² given your normalization) for condition number and reserve L for Lipschitz. Add a standard reference for Forward–Backward splitting (e.g., Bauschke & Combettes, 2011).

Originality: High. Extending variance reduction to non-separable saddle points and general monotone operators with linear rates is novel.

Significance: Potentially high impact for large-scale non-separable problems; acceleration and non-uniform sampling are practical contributions.

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel linear-convergence analysis for SVRG/SAGA in monotone operator settings
- Applicability to non-separable functions and variational inequalities
- Acceleration and non-uniform sampling with clear complexity gains

Cons for acceptance:
- Presentation/notation issues; unclear constants/assumptions
- Experiments are limited; missing baselines (SPDC, accelerated stochastic PDHG/Chambolle–Pock)
- Need clearer differentiation from standard stochastic primal–dual methods and where assumptions (A)–(C) hold (e.g., SVM hinge-loss settings)
