- Extends stochastic variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) to convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides the first linearly convergent large-scale algorithms for this problem class.  
- Highly relevant to ML applications involving duality, robust optimization, and structured prediction.  
- Builds on foundational variance-reduction work (Johnson & Zhang 2013; Defazio et al. 2014).  
- Adapts the Catalyst acceleration framework to improve convergence rates.  
- Theoretical analysis uses monotone operator theory, extending applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Strong originality and rigorous theoretical contributions.  
- Acceleration scheme theoretically outperforms accelerated batch methods.  
- Includes non-uniform sampling and analysis of different data-splitting strategies.  
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized.  
- Main weakness: requires simultaneous strong convexity in the primal and strong concavity in the dual—a restrictive assumption.  
- Suggestion: relaxing to strong convexity in only one variable would be a more significant contribution.  
- Empirical results are promising but limited; broader experimental evaluation across datasets and problem types is needed.  
- Overall: high-quality theoretical work whose practical impact is tempered by the restrictive assumption and limited experiments.
