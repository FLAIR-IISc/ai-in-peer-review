- Proposes the first linearly convergent SVRG- and SAGA-style algorithms for saddle-point problems.  
- Extends variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) from convex minimization to the saddle-point setting.  
- Main theoretical contribution is a convergence proof based on a monotone-operator framework that generalizes to variational inequality problems.  
- Paper is well-written and organized with a clear introduction, motivation, and related-work comparison.  
- Technical quality is high with a rigorous convergence proof, but the proof is relegated to the appendix.  
- Reviewer requests more proof details or a proof sketch in the main paper to clarify the novel proof technique.  
- More information needed on non-uniform sampling beyond bilinear problems, including the choice of π and intuition for its importance in saddle-point problems.  
- Approach enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers via the saddle-point formulation, increasing practical applicability.  
- Significant potential impact on machine learning due to providing first linear convergence guarantees for this problem class.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity and include more main-paper proof details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel monotone-operator proof technique, broader problem applicability, and practical significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: key proofs missing from main text and insufficient clarity on non-uniform sampling choices.
