This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization by extending stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG/SAGA) to convex-concave saddle-point problems. The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis, proving linear convergence in a monotone operator setting, and propose acceleration and non-uniform sampling techniques. The paper's contributions are substantial, offering new insights into the applicability of these methods to non-separable functions.

The presentation of the paper is somewhat weak, with the connection to monotone operators being introduced relatively late as an extension, despite being a crucial aspect of the analysis. Furthermore, the experiments are limited to only two datasets, and more comparisons with other methods, such as accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock-type methods, would be beneficial.

The assumptions A-C, particularly assumption A, could be clarified in terms of their strength and applicability. It is also unclear whether these assumptions hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems. Additionally, the differences between standard stochastic primal-dual methods and the authors' proposed method could be more clearly explained.

In Theorem 2, the constant μ is not clearly defined, and it would be helpful to confirm whether it is the monotonicity constant from the appendix. The transition from assumptions A-C to strong monotonicity in the appendix is also confusing, and it would be beneficial to state whether A-C imply monotonicity.

Minor issues include the inconsistent use of L (condition number vs Lipschitz constant) and the missing reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field, but could benefit from improvements in presentation, clarity, and comparisons with other methods.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the presentation could be improved, and some clarifications are needed.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized, but the connection to monotone operators could be introduced earlier, and some notation and terminology could be clarified.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel extension of SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, and the theoretical analysis and experimental results are original and significant.

**Significance:** The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of optimization, offering new insights and techniques for solving convex-concave saddle-point problems. The results have the potential to impact various applications in machine learning and beyond.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization.
* The theoretical analysis is thorough and provides new insights into the applicability of SVRG/SAGA to non-separable functions.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The presentation could be improved, with earlier introduction of the connection to monotone operators.
* More comparisons with other methods, such as accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock-type methods, would be beneficial.
* Clarifications are needed regarding the assumptions A-C and their applicability to SVM-induced saddle-point problems.
