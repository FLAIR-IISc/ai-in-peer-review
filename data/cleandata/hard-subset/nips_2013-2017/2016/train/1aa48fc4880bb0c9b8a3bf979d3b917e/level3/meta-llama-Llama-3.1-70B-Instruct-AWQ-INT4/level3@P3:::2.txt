This paper proposes stochastic variance reduction methods for solving convex-concave saddle-point problems. The authors extend recent variance-reduction methods, such as SVRG and SAGA, to this setting, providing the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems. They also show that these methods can be accelerated via an extension of the Catalyst framework. Furthermore, the approach is extended to monotone operators, and numerical results are provided to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

The main contribution of this paper is the provision of the first convergence analysis for these algorithms in the context of saddle-point problems. The authors use the notion of monotone operators to prove convergence, showing that the same algorithm applies to a larger class of problems, such as variational inequalities.
