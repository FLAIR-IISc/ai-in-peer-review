- Introduces stochastic variance reduction (SVRG/SAGA) for convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Extends algorithms with Catalyst-style acceleration and provides convergence analysis showing linear rates.  
- Generalizes approach to monotone operators, covering saddle-point, convex minimization, and variational inequality problems.  
- Presents numerical results comparing proposed methods to batch and stochastic baselines, showing improved convergence and runtime.  
- Clear, concise presentation and well-organized, thorough theoretical analysis.  
- Provides a thorough review of existing batch and stochastic methods and highlights their limitations.  
- Novel contribution: applying stochastic variance reduction to saddle-point problems.  
- Significant potential impact by improving efficiency of ML algorithms that involve saddle-point formulations.  
- Main weakness: assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual, which is overly strong and unrealistic in many applications.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to few examples; more numerical experiments are needed to demonstrate robustness.  
- Recommendation to relax the dual/primal strong-convexity assumption to a single-side assumption.  
- Recommendation to include more numerical experiments and additional examples of monotone operators.  
- Overall quality: technically sound with well-supported claims; clarity: clearly written and easy to follow.
