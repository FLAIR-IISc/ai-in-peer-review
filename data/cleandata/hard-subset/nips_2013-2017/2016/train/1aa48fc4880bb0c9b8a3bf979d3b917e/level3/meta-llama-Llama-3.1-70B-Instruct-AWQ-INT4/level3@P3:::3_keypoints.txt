- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave finite-sum saddle-point problems — novel and technically non-trivial.  
- Provides thorough complexity analysis for different splits and uniform/non-uniform sampling.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Experiments show the methods are effective; non-uniform sampling and acceleration are critical to beat batch algorithms.  
- Accelerated SVRG shows significant gains on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Major concern: incremental iterations require an O(n + d) cost for the proximal operator, creating a bottleneck that may prevent improvement over accelerated batch FB when n >> d.  
- Reviewer requests deeper discussion on (i) whether factored splits are theoretically worse than individual splits, (ii) when accelerated SVRG outperforms batch FB, and (iii) whether the n, d dependence is tight.  
- Authors’ responses were satisfactory but did not change the reviewer’s score.  
- Originality: clear differentiation from prior work by combining familiar techniques in a new setting.  
- Significance: high; likely to influence future optimization research and provide a useful tool for saddle-point problems.  
- Pros summary: novel extension, thorough analysis, strong empirical performance, addresses a difficult problem better than prior work.  
- Cons summary: proximal-cost bottleneck, unclear theory on factored splits, remaining unanswered questions.  
- Overall rating/recommendation: 8/10 — Accept, with encouragement to address raised concerns and provide more detailed analysis of limitations.
