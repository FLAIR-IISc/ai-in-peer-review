- Proposes stochastic variance-reduced extensions of SVRG and SAGA for strongly convex–concave finite-sum saddle-point problems with individual and factored splits.
- Provides thorough complexity analysis under different splits and uniform/non-uniform sampling strategies.
- Experiments (binary classification) show non-uniform sampling and acceleration are key to outperforming batch methods.
- Novel and technically non-trivial extension of SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point settings.
- Detailed complexity results clarify algorithm behavior across problem decompositions and sampling schemes.
- Clear comparisons for bilinear saddle points that align with convex minimization intuition.
- Each incremental iteration requires O(n + d) cost for the proximal operator, creating a fundamental bottleneck.
- Overall complexity may not improve over accelerated batch forward–backward (FB); factored splits can be worse when n >> d.
- Lacks deeper theoretical discussion on whether factored splits are inherently worse than individual splits.
- Insufficient analysis of when accelerated SVRG truly outperforms batch FB and whether the n, d dependence is tight.
- Paper is significant, high-quality, and well-written with clear presentation and experiments.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance pending revisions to address theoretical gaps and improve clarity/presentation.
