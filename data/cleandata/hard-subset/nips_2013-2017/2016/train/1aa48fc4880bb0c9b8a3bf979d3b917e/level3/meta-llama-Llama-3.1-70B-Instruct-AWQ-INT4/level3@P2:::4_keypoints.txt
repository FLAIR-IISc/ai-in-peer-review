- Proposes extending SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides linear convergence analysis for these variance-reduced algorithms.  
- Reviews saddle-point problems and limitations of existing batch and stochastic methods.  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits (element-wise and factored) to derive variance-reduced algorithms.  
- Uses the monotone-operator framework for a more complex but rigorous convergence proof.  
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform existing ones in convergence rate and complexity.  
- Demonstrates the effectiveness of non-uniform sampling in the algorithms.  
- Minor concern: unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) for acceleration speed-up.  
- Notational issue: the same symbol K is used for both a smooth function K(·) and a design matrix K, risking confusion.  
- Originality and significance are high; the extension and analysis are new and important for ML applications.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper; recommendation to accept with minor revisions (rating 9/10).
