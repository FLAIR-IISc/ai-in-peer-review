- Contribution: Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems and provides the first linear convergence analysis for these stochastic methods in that setting.

- Problem setup: Considers min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with K smooth and decomposable, M proximable and assumed strongly convex–strongly concave.

- Key idea: Apply variance reduction to the gradient operator of K; algorithmic extension is simple but analysis is nontrivial.

- Analysis technique: Uses monotone operator theory (more general than descent analysis) to prove convergence and cover variational inequalities.

- Operator design: Discusses multiple ways to split B(x,y)=(∇_xK,−∇_yK) and highlights the role of non-uniform sampling for better rates.

- Acceleration: Extends the catalyst framework to accelerate SVRG for saddle-point problems, yielding theoretically superior complexity to accelerated batch methods.

- Theoretical results: Derives linear convergence rates, complexity bounds, and careful treatment of non-uniform sampling.

- Experiments: Numerical tests on binary classification with non-separable losses/regularizers show practical benefits of the proposed methods.

- Strength — originality/significance: Novel and important extension bridging variance reduction and saddle-point optimization; first linearly convergent stochastic algorithms for this class.

- Strength — technical quality: Solid, elegant use of monotone operators and rigorous convergence and complexity analysis.

- Strength — clarity: Well-written, organized, clear positioning relative to prior work and precise algorithm/theorem presentation.

- Strength — acceleration: Catalyst extension is a valuable addition producing state-of-the-art theoretical guarantees.

- Weakness — assumption scope: Requires M to be strongly convex in x and strongly concave in y, which significantly limits applicability to many ML problems.

- Implication of limitation: Excludes common cases (e.g., Fenchel duals for SVMs/Lasso) where only one side is strongly convex/concave.

- Suggested remedy: Authors should analyze or discuss the single-sided strong convexity/concavity case (even with sublinear rates) or explicitly state implications for applications.

- Weakness — experiments narrow: Experimental evaluation is limited to custom problems; should include standard benchmarks and sensitivity to (λ,γ) parameters.

- Overall verdict: Strong, novel, and technically solid paper worthy of acceptance, but should address the strong-convexity–strong-concavity limitation and broaden experiments.
