**Paper Review: Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems**

This paper proposes the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems using stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA). The authors extend the existing SVRG and SAGA algorithms to handle non-separable loss functions and regularizers through saddle-point formulation. They provide a convergence proof via monotone-operator framework, which generalizes to a wider class of problems, including variational inequality problems.

**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper focuses on convex-concave saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors extend recent stochastic variance reduction methods to provide the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems. They use the notion of monotone operators to prove convergence, showing that the same algorithm applies to a larger class of problems, such as variational inequalities.

**Strengths**

1. **First linearly convergent algorithms**: The paper proposes the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems using SVRG and SAGA.
2. **Extension to non-separable loss functions and regularizers**: The authors extend the existing SVRG and SAGA algorithms to handle non-separable loss functions and regularizers through saddle-point formulation.
3. **Convergence proof via monotone-operator framework**: The paper provides a convergence proof via monotone-operator framework, which generalizes to a wider class of problems, including variational inequality problems.
4. **Acceleration technique**: The authors propose an acceleration technique using a simple extension of the "catalyst" framework, leading to an algorithm that is always superior to accelerated batch algorithms.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited clarity in some sections**: The paper could benefit from improved clarity in some sections, particularly in the proofs and algorithm descriptions.
2. **Minor typos**: There are a few minor typos in the paper, such as the incorrect use of "k" instead of "d" in line 78.
3. **Lack of detailed proof**: The paper could benefit from a more detailed proof of the convergence theorem, particularly for readers who are not familiar with monotone-operator theory.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel extension of SVRG and SAGA to handle saddle-point problems, which is a significant contribution to the field of machine learning. The use of monotone-operator framework to prove convergence is also a new and interesting approach.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for machine learning, as it provides the first linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems. The algorithms proposed in the paper can be used to solve a wide range of problems in machine learning, including supervised learning, robust optimization, and convex relaxation of unsupervised learning.
