- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and variational inequalities using monotone-operator theory.  
- Provides convergence analysis and detailed complexity bounds under various assumptions.  
- Discusses two splitting notions (function-level and partial-derivative-level) and necessity of convex-concave splits.  
- Analyzes non-uniform sampling theoretically and empirically, showing practical efficiency gains.  
- Introduces acceleration via a catalyst framework for the saddle-point setting.  
- Thorough exploration of sampling strategies and splitting techniques with performance implications.  
- Connects the methods to monotone operator theory, broadening theoretical applicability.  
- Addresses the cost of the proximal operator in terms of n and d.  
- Major weakness: O(n + d) per-iteration proximal cost is a significant bottleneck not fully analyzed.  
- Claim that accelerated SVRG always outperforms accelerated batch methods is unsubstantiated given proximal costs.  
- Lacks clear conditions delineating when accelerated SVRG outperforms batch Forward–Backward.  
- Factored splits’ potential higher complexity versus individual splits (especially when n >> d) is insufficiently investigated.  
- Unclear whether the n and d dependencies in the complexity bounds are tight or can be improved.  
- Experiments (synthetic) support theory, but broader empirical validation and practical implications need strengthening.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing proximal-cost implications, comparisons with batch algorithms, and factored vs. individual split analysis.
