- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, delivering the first linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Uses monotone operator framework to analyze saddle-point problems as zero-finding, enabling rigorous convergence proofs.  
- Proof technique is novel and yields a simpler analysis for forward-backward splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Approach generalizes to variational inequality problems, broadening applicability.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers via saddle-point formulation.  
- Demonstrates theoretically and empirically that non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency.  
- Experiments show non-uniform SAGA and accelerated SVRG outperform batch and uniform-sampling variants, especially on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Paper is technically sound with rigorous theoretical analysis and well-supported claims.  
- Well-organized with useful machine-learning examples (Section 2.1) and convincing experiments (Section 7).  
- Main novelty is the monotone-operator-based proof; including a detailed proof sketch in the main paper would strengthen the contribution.  
- Clarity issues: insufficient detail on the general non-uniform sampling strategy (what π is in general).  
- Clarity issues: need to explain step-size (σ) selection used in experiments.  
- Clarity issues: should clarify why non-uniform sampling matters more for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos noted (lines 78 and 121) that should be fixed.  
- Review rates: high quality and significance, clear originality, but clarity could be improved.  
- Acceptance arguments: first linear-convergence results, novel framework, generalization to VIs, handles non-separable problems, strong empirical validation, simpler proofs for existing methods.  
- Rejection/concern points: lingering clarity on sampling probabilities and step-size choice, minor manuscript errors.
