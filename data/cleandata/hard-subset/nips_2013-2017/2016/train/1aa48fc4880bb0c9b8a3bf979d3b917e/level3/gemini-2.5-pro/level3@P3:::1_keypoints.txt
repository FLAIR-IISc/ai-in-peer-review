- Major contribution: first stochastic variance-reduced SVRG/SAGA adaptations with provable linear convergence for a broad class of convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Theoretical novelty: uses monotone operator theory to prove convergence, extending applicability to variational inequalities beyond saddle points.  
- Practical reach: framework handles non-separable losses/regularizers (when proximal operators are available), enabling variance reduction for problems like AUC maximization and structured prediction.  
- Algorithmic extensions: introduces a Catalyst-style acceleration scheme and uses non-uniform sampling to improve practical performance.  
- Presentation issue: organization buries the monotone-operator viewpoint (Section 6), obscuring the main theoretical insight and narrative flow.  
- Experimental limitation: empirical validation is weak—only two datasets—insufficient to demonstrate general effectiveness across problem sizes and sparsity patterns.  
- Baseline omission: fails to compare against accelerated stochastic primal–dual methods (e.g., Zhang & Xiao), leaving unclear how it stacks up against state-of-the-art.  
- Strong assumptions: Assumption (A) requires M(x,y) to be strongly convex–concave, which is restrictive and needs discussion about applicability to common ML problems (e.g., SVM duals).  
- Clarity on methods: should more explicitly compare update rules and mechanics with standard stochastic primal–dual algorithms to clarify novel aspects.  
- Unclear constants: constants like μ in Theorem 2 are not defined in the main text and must be stated and explained for rate comparisons.  
- Logical gap: the paper does not clearly show how Assumptions (A)–(C) imply the strong monotonicity used in proofs; this connection should be explicitly stated.  
- Notation inconsistency: symbol L is used ambiguously for both a Lipschitz constant and a condition number—use distinct notation.  
- Missing citation: add a reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm (e.g., Bauschke & Combettes 2011).
