- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent methods for large-scale saddle-point settings.  
- Well-motivated, clearly written, and thoroughly evaluated work that addresses a notable gap in the literature.  
- Builds on SAG/SVRG/SAGA to handle non-separable objectives and the non-separability inherent in many saddle-point formulations.  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits (element-wise and factored), an insightful algorithmic contribution.  
- Uses the monotone operator framework for theoretical analysis, enabling generalization to variational inequalities.  
- Demonstrates the benefit of non-uniform sampling both theoretically and empirically.  
- Novelty and significance: substantially advances stochastic variance reduction methods for a broader class of problems.  
- Theoretical rigor: convergence analysis is thorough, well-justified, and clearly presented.  
- Experimental validation: comprehensive experiments support theoretical claims across datasets and baselines.  
- Clarity and organization: paper is well-structured and accessible, with concise technical presentation.  
- Practical relevance: applicable to supervised learning with non-separable losses/regularizers, robust optimization, and convex relaxations in unsupervised learning.  
- Weakness: unclear practical details for adaptive updating of the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) and how this yields acceleration.  
- Weakness: convergence proof for SAGA is not fully resolved or justified by the presented analysis.  
- Weakness: notational ambiguity (reuse of ‘K’ for different objects) may cause confusion.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant problem addressed, rigorous theory and experiments, broader applicability, and high-quality presentation.  
- Rejection considerations: unclear adaptive acceleration implementation, unresolved SAGA proof, and minor notational issues — though these are minor relative to strengths.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution with strengths far outweighing minor weaknesses; strong recommendation for acceptance.
