- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this setting.
- Theoretical analysis gives convergence rates, computational complexity, and new insights for non-separable functions.
- Extension to monotone operators is a notable theoretical contribution.
- Strengths: originality, technical soundness, significance, and thorough algorithmic analysis.
- Experiments show practical effectiveness and superiority over existing batch and stochastic methods.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to only two datasets and needs more datasets and comparisons.
- Missing empirical comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock-type methods.
- Presentation weakness: connection to monotone operators is unclear and introduced too late despite underpinning analysis.
- Assumptions need clarification (especially assumption A), and the transition from (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing.
- Theorem 2’s constant μ is unclear and its relationship to Theorem 1 should be explained.
- Notational issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
- Recommendation: accept, but require authors to address presentation, clarity, and experimental/comparative gaps.
