- Paper studies min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) and extends SVRG/SAGA with proximal steps using a monotone-operator A+B formulation to get linear rates without co-coercivity, plus importance sampling, factored splits, and Catalyst-style acceleration.  
- Novelty: handles non-separable K/M and proves global linear rates in a general monotone inclusion setting, extending beyond saddle-points to variational inequalities.  

Strengths:
- First linearly convergent variance-reduced methods for general (non-separable) saddle-point problems with a clean monotone-operator proof.  
- Practical algorithmic contributions: non-uniform sampling, factored vs elemental splits, and explicit complexity expressions showing when variance reduction helps.  
- Simple, effective Catalyst-style acceleration with theoretical and empirical support for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Broad applicability to non-separable losses/regularizers with efficient prox operators.  

Weaknesses / high-level questions:
- Monotone-operator perspective is presented late (Section 6), making the analysis feel disconnected from earlier sections.  
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets) and lack comparisons to state-of-the-art stochastic primal–dual/accelerated CP methods.  
- Assumptions (A)–(C) need clearer discussion about practical applicability, especially when (A) (strong concavity in y) holds.  
- Notational inconsistencies (use of L) and missing standard references for forward–backward splitting.  

Key detailed requests:
- Bring monotone inclusion viewpoint earlier and explicitly state needed properties (maximal µ-strongly monotone A with resolvent; Bi Lipschitz; B monotone) to clarify why co-coercivity is not required.  
- Clarify assumption (A): discuss practical scenarios for γ-strong concavity in y, smoothing strategies and dependence on added γ, or alternative conditions like restarting/local growth.  
- Unify and clarify L: specify it's the Lipschitz constant of B in the Ω→Ω* norm, distinguish from condition number κ=L/µ, and fix σ choices (e.g., σ=1/L^2) consistently.  
- Provide practitioner guidance on when factored vs elemental splits are beneficial and heuristics for choosing p,q beyond row/column norms.  
- Define μ explicitly in Theorem 2 (strong monotonicity constant of A+B in the Ω-norm) and add a short comparison paragraph on dependency differences between Theorems 1 and 2.  
- State explicitly that (A) implies strong monotonicity of A (with µ tied to λ,γ in the Ω-norm) and that (B) makes B monotone so A+B is strongly monotone; spell out any extra required conditions.  
- Position and (if possible) compare empirically to SPDC (Zhang & Xiao) and accelerated stochastic CP-type methods, explaining when the new bounds dominate (e.g., non-separable settings).  
- Expand experiments: more datasets/tasks showcasing non-separability/efficient prox (overlapping group sparsity, TV, structured AUC), include SPDC/CP and primal SAGA/SDCA baselines, report wall-clock time and PD-gap, and ablate sampling, batch size m, and split choices; report SAGA memory overheads.  
- Add a concise subsection contrasting variance-reduced FB updates vs classical primal–dual/CP methods, clearly listing differences in assumptions and update formulas.  
- Fix minor notation issues (reserve κ for condition number, L for Lipschitz) and add forward–backward splitting references (e.g., Combettes & Wajs 2005; Bauschke & Combettes 2011).  

Evaluation summary:
- Quality: strong theoretical contributions but some definition gaps (μ) and presentation issues; experiments too limited.  
- Clarity: generally readable but needs reorganization to foreground monotone-operator idea and tighten notation.  
- Originality & significance: high — likely influential for theory and practice on non-separable models.  

Pros for acceptance:
- First linear-rate variance-reduced methods for general saddle-point problems, with acceleration and non-uniform sampling; broad applicability.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited experiments and missing key comparisons (SPDC/accelerated CP); presentation/notation and practicality of assumption (A) need addressing.  

Recommendation:
- Borderline accept, leaning accept conditional on clarifying μ and monotone connection, improving related work (include SPDC), and expanding experiments.
