- Proposes first linearly convergent stochastic variance reduction methods for saddle-point problems, extending SVRG and SAGA.  
- Main contribution: convergence proof via the monotone-operator framework, generalizing to variational inequality problems.  
- Enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers through the saddle-point formulation.  
- Provides simpler proofs for forward-backward (FB) splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Demonstrates competitiveness of non-uniform sampling, which often outperforms prior methods.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized.  
- Thorough analysis of algorithms and their convergence rates.  
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness of the proposed methods.  
- Lacks sufficient proof details or a proof sketch in the main paper despite the novelty of the proof technique.  
- Clarity issues: definition of non-uniform sampling beyond bilinear problems and choice of step size in experiments need clarification.  
- Needs more discussion/intuition on why non-uniform sampling is more crucial for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Originality: novel approach and novel use of monotone-operator framework.  
- Significance: addresses an important ML problem with potentially impactful improvements.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address weaknesses.  
- Minor typos: Line 78 replace "k" with "d"; Line 121 remove extra "y_{t-1}" in second equality.
