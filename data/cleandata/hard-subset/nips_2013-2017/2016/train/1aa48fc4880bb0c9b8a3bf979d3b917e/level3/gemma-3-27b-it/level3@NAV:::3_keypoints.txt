- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave finite-sum saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Adapts algorithms for non-separable losses/regularizers and connects results to monotone operator theory for broader applicability.  
- Provides theoretical analysis for different split types (individual vs factored) and sampling strategies (uniform and non-uniform).  
- Novel and detailed analysis of factored splits, highlighting sensitivity to problem dimensions.  
- Experimental validation on binary classification shows importance of non-uniform sampling and acceleration.  
- Paper is technically sound, novel, well-written, and clearly organized.  
- Major practical concern: O(n + d) cost of computing the proximal operator per incremental iteration is a significant bottleneck.  
- This proximal cost may negate theoretical benefits, especially when n >> d, potentially making methods worse than accelerated batch FB.  
- Reviewer requests formal justification for when factored splits can be worse than individual splits in the n >> d regime.  
- Requests concrete demonstrations of when accelerated SVRG outperforms batch forward-backward (FB) methods.  
- Asks whether the n, d dependence in complexity bounds is tight and if algorithmic refinements could reduce this dependence.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/strong extension of SVR/SAGA, thorough convergence analysis, clear presentation, and valuable theoretical foundation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: proximal operator cost bottleneck, unclear outperforming conditions vs batch FB, insufficient discussion of factored split limitations.  
- Overall score: 6/10 (strong reject), with reviewer maintaining score after rebuttal due to unresolved practical limitation.
