- Studies SVRG and SAGA for convex–concave saddle-point problems and extends convergence analysis to this setting, proving linear convergence under certain assumptions.  
- Proposes an acceleration technique and non-uniform sampling to improve algorithmic efficiency.  
- Contributions are significant: theory extends to non-separable functions and variational inequalities.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and well supported.  
- Connection to monotone-operator theory is unclear and introduced late despite underpinning the analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to only two datasets; more datasets and comparisons are needed.  
- Missing comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock–type methods.  
- Assumptions A–C need clearer statement and justification, especially assumption A and its applicability to SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
- Paper should clarify differences between standard stochastic primal–dual methods and the proposed method.  
- In Theorem 2 the constant μ is unclear—confirm if it is the monotonicity constant from the appendix and explain relation to Theorem 1.  
- The transition from assumptions A–C to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing; state whether A–C imply monotonicity.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on improved presentation, expanded experiments, and additional comparisons.
