- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems with thorough theoretical analysis and linear convergence in a monotone operator setting.  
- Proposes acceleration and non-uniform sampling techniques and addresses non-separable functions.  
- Major contribution and originality: novel extension of variance-reduction methods to saddle-point problems with significant theoretical and empirical results.  
- Presentation weakness: connection to monotone operators is introduced late despite being central to analysis.  
- Experiments are limited to two datasets and lack comparisons with other methods (e.g., accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock).  
- Assumptions A–C (especially A) need clearer statement regarding strength, applicability, and whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
- Differences between standard stochastic primal-dual methods and the proposed method are not clearly explained.  
- Theorem 2: constant μ is not clearly defined; should confirm if it is the monotonicity constant referenced in the appendix.  
- Transition from assumptions A–C to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing; clarify whether A–C imply monotonicity.  
- Notational inconsistency: L used ambiguously (condition number vs Lipschitz constant).  
- Missing reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound with well-supported claims, but needs improved presentation and clarifications.  
- Significance: results are potentially impactful for optimization and machine learning applications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: substantial theoretical contribution, novel methods, and supportive experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation issues, insufficient empirical comparisons, and unclear assumptions.
