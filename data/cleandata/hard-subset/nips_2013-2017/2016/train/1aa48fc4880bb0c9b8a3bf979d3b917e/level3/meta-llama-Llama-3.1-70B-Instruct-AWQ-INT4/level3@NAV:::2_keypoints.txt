- Summary: Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, yielding the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms and Catalyst-based acceleration.  
- Strength: Novel extension of SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point settings.  
- Strength: Proposed methods achieve linear convergence.  
- Strength: Methods can be accelerated using the Catalyst framework.  
- Strength: Approach is extended to the broader class of monotone operators.  
- Weakness: Assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual problems, which may be overly restrictive.  
- Weakness: Numerical experiments are limited and could be expanded.  
- Recommendation: Relax the strong convexity requirement (e.g., to only primal or only dual).  
- Recommendation: Provide more numerical experiments across diverse problems.  
- Originality: Presents an original contribution as the first SVRG/SAGA extensions to saddle-point problems.  
- Significance: Addresses an important ML optimization problem with potential practical impact.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and easy to follow with clear explanations.  
- Quality: Technically sound with thorough theoretical analysis and supporting results.  
- For acceptance: Novel methods, linear convergence, and thorough analysis support acceptance.  
- Against acceptance: Restrictive assumptions and limited empirical validation argue for revisions.  
- Overall: Recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the noted limitations.
