- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems of form min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) where K is smooth/decomposable and M is strongly convex-concave with an efficient proximal.
- Proposes SVRG- and SAGA-type algorithms for this saddle-point setting with linear convergence guarantees.
- Uses monotone operator theory for convergence analysis, providing a unified framework applicable beyond saddle-points (e.g., variational inequalities).
- Introduces factored gradient splits and non-uniform sampling to improve theoretical rates and practical performance.
- Presents a Catalyst-style acceleration scheme that improves complexity over accelerated batch methods.
- Claims novelty and significance by handling non-separable losses/regularizers (e.g., AUC, total variation) where primal/dual SVRG/SAGA fail.
- Theoretical analysis is strong, elegant, and yields state-of-the-art complexity bounds including non-uniform sampling and acceleration.
- Potential high impact: algorithms likely useful for structured prediction and robust optimization in practice.
- Presentation and organization are poor; monotone-operator connection is introduced too late and should appear earlier.
- Transition from problem assumptions (A)-(C) to the strong monotonicity used in proofs is unclear and needs explicit linkage.
- Missing discussion/comparison to stochastic primal-dual methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock variants, Zhang & Xiao 2014/2015).
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets) and lack baselines that reflect related stochastic primal-dual methods; broader empirical validation is needed.
- Assumption (A) requiring computable joint proximal for M(x,y) is strong and should be clarified with concrete ML examples (e.g., SVM).
- Notation issues: parameter μ in Theorem 2 is undefined in the main text; L is used inconsistently (Lipschitz constant vs condition number).
- Citation gap: linear convergence rate of forward-backward should cite Chen & Rockafellar where first mentioned.
- Request to clarify whether (A)-(C) imply strong monotonicity of A+B required by proofs.
- Ask authors to explicitly differentiate their methods from standard stochastic primal-dual methods beyond variance reduction.
- Overall recommendation: lean accept for substantial theoretical novelty, contingent on major revisions to clarity, related work comparisons, and experimental validation.
