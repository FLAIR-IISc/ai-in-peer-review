- Presents extensions of SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, claiming first linear convergence for this class.
- Core theoretical contribution: adapts convergence analysis using monotone operator theory, extending applicability to variational inequalities.
- Well-motivated problem with clear relevance to non-separable losses, robust optimization, and convex relaxations in ML.
- Strong theoretical rigor with linear convergence guarantees and generalization beyond convex minimization.
- Practical relevance: offers scalable algorithms addressing common saddle-point structures in ML.
- Experimental validation on synthetic and real datasets demonstrates effectiveness, especially with non-uniform sampling.
- Non-uniform sampling empirically outperforms existing techniques in several scenarios.
- Introduction clearly articulates problem, relevance, and contributions.
- Unclear specification of the non-uniform sampling probability vector π beyond the bilinear case.
- Step size selection (σ) used in experiments is not detailed, hurting reproducibility and understanding.
- Key proof techniques are relegated to appendices; more detailed proof sketches in the main paper would improve accessibility.
- Limited discussion and intuition on why non-uniform sampling is more crucial for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.
- Minor typographical errors (e.g., line 78 k→d, line 121 extra y_{t-1}) present.
- Overall quality: technically sound and complete, but some clarity issues in implementation details.
- Highly original combination of SVR methods with monotone operator analysis.
- Significant potential impact by providing new scalable tools for prevalent ML problems.
- Arguments for acceptance: first linear-convergent algorithms for saddle-points, novel analysis framework, strong experiments, practical importance.
- Arguments for rejection: insufficient clarity on sampling and step-size, lack of main-paper proof detail, limited intuition on non-uniform sampling importance.
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to addressing clarity and detail concerns in a revision.
