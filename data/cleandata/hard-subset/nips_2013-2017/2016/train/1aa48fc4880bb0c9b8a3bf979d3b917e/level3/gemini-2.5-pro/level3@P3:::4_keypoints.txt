- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linear convergence guarantees in this setting.  
- Analyzes algorithms via the monotone operator framework, generalizing beyond convex analysis and covering variational inequalities.  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits (element-wise and factored) tailored to saddle-point structure.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling schemes can significantly outperform uniform sampling, both theoretically and empirically.  
- Applies a Catalyst-style acceleration to achieve provably faster algorithms in certain regimes.  
- Empirical results validate theory: accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling outperforms batch and simple stochastic baselines on non-separable losses/regularizers.  
- Original and significant contribution addressing a practical gap for ML problems (e.g., AUC, total variation) cast as saddle-point problems.  
- Technically rigorous and sophisticated convergence analysis that unifies related problem classes.  
- Well-written and well-organized presentation that ties theoretical constants to experimental setup.  
- Thorough and compelling experimental validation demonstrating practical utility.  
- Suggest clarifying how to implement the proposed "adaptive" acceleration updates (heuristic, detail, or reference).  
- Recommend justifying or adapting the acceleration proof for SAGA rather than only asserting it should work.  
- Minor notational ambiguity: reuse of symbol K for both function K(x,y) and design matrix K could be clarified.
