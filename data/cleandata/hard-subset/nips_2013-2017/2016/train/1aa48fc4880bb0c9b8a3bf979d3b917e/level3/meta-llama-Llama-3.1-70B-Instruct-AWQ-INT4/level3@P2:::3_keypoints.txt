- Summary: Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave finite-sum saddle-point problems with individual/factored splits and uniform/non-uniform sampling; experiments show non-uniform sampling and acceleration help beat batch methods.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on SVRG/SAGA for convex minimization and links to monotone operators/variational inequalities, extending these techniques to saddle-point problems.

- Strength 1: Novel, technically non-trivial extension of SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point settings.

- Strength 2: Thorough complexity analysis across different splits and sampling strategies.

- Strength 3: Experiments convincingly demonstrate effectiveness versus batch algorithms.

- Weakness 1: Does not address the proximal operator bottleneck, which incurs O(n + d) cost per incremental iteration.

- Weakness 2: Overall complexity may not improve over accelerated batch Forward–Backward algorithms, particularly for factored splits when n >> d.

- Weakness 3: Needs deeper theoretical comparison between factored and individual splits.

- Quality: Technically sound with clear presentation and comprehensive analysis.

- Clarity: Well-written, consistent notation, and helpful figures.

- Originality: Significant and original contribution by extending variance-reduction methods to saddle-point problems.

- Significance: Potentially impactful for ML applications involving saddle-point formulations.

- Pro arguments: Novel extension, solid theoretical analysis, and well-designed experiments.

- Con arguments: Proximal computation bottleneck and limited complexity gains in some regimes.

- Recommendation: Accept, with revisions requested to address proximal operator cost and theoretical comparison of split types.
