- Proposes the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, extending SVRG and SAGA.  
- Handles non-separable saddle-point problems where loss and regularizer may be non-separable.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow for experts.  
- Provides complete description of algorithms, convergence analysis, and experiments.  
- Main contribution: extension of SVRG and SAGA to this problem class with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split (SVRG) and factored split (SAGA).  
- Demonstrates that non-uniform sampling is crucial for theoretical and practical efficiency.  
- Shows incremental algorithms can be accelerated via a simple extension of the "catalyst" framework.  
- Convergence analysis uses a monotone-operator framework, which is more complex but clearly explained.  
- Discusses broader applicability, including variational inequalities in game theory.  
- Experimental results support theoretical claims and show superiority over existing batch and stochastic methods.  
- Minor concern: adaptation of the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) for acceleration is unclear.  
- Unclear whether the convergence and acceleration proof for SVRG also applies to SAGA.  
- Notational issue: confusion between smooth function K(·) and design matrix K — should use distinct symbols.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound, original, significant, and well-presented (scores ~9/10 across metrics).
