- Proposes first linearly convergent stochastic algorithms for convex–concave saddle-point problems by extending SVRG and SAGA.  
- Main technical contribution is a novel convergence proof reframing the problem as finding the zero of a monotone operator.  
- Proof technique is powerful and generalizable, extending to variational inequalities and simplifying proofs for methods like forward–backward splitting.  
- Fills an important gap for large-scale optimization with non-separable losses/regularizers common in machine learning.  
- Achieves the first linear convergence rate for stochastic methods in this domain, improving over batch or sub-linear stochastic methods.  
- Experimental results validate theory: proposed methods with non-uniform sampling are competitive and often outperform prior work.  
- Suggestion to include a high-level proof sketch in the main paper to emphasize the core novelty (the proof technique).  
- Clarify the non-uniform sampling strategy (π) used for problems beyond the bilinear case.  
- Describe how the experimental step size σ was chosen to ensure reproducibility.  
- Provide intuition for why non-uniform sampling seems more critical for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos to fix: line 78 (k should be d) and line 121 (extra y_{t-1} term).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance provided the authors address the clarity points.
