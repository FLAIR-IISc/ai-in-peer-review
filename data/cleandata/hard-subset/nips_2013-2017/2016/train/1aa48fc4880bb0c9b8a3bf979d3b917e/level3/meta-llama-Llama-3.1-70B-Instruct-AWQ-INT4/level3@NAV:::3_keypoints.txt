- Presents novel extensions of SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with individual and factored splits.  
- Provides thorough complexity analysis for different splits and for uniform vs. non-uniform sampling.  
- Experiments show non-uniform sampling and acceleration are critical to beat batch algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction, related work, and contributions.  
- Technical content is sound; proofs are well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Main strength: technically non-trivial and original extension of variance-reduction methods to saddle-point settings.  
- Comparison with bilinear saddle points matches convex minimization intuition.  
- Major concern: per-iteration cost O(n + d) for proximal computation creates a fundamental bottleneck.  
- Consequently, overall complexity (even accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling) may not improve over accelerated batch forward–backward; factored splits can be worse when n >> d.  
- Authors should clarify: (1) whether factored splits are theoretically worse than individual splits, (2) when accelerated SVRG outperforms batch FB, and (3) whether the n,d dependence is tight.  
- Conclusion: significant contribution but needs deeper discussion of computational complexity.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8.5/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the computational-complexity concerns.
