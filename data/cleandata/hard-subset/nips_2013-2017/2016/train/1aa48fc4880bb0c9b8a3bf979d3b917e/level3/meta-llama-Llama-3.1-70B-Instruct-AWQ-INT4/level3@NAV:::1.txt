This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization, specifically in the context of convex-concave saddle-point problems. The authors extend stochastic variance reduction methods, such as SVRG and SAGA, to this class of problems, providing the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this setting. The theoretical analysis reveals new insights, particularly in the applicability of these methods to non-separable functions.

The paper's strengths lie in its originality, technical soundness, and significance. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithms, including convergence rates and computational complexities. The extension of these methods to monotone operators is also a notable contribution. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithms in practice, showcasing their superiority over existing batch and stochastic algorithms.

However, the presentation of the paper is weak in some areas. The connection to monotone operators is unclear and appears late in the paper as an extension, despite underpinning the analysis. The experiments are limited to only two datasets, and more datasets and comparisons are needed to fully assess the performance of the algorithms. Additionally, the paper is missing a comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock-type methods, which would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed algorithms' performance.

Furthermore, some assumptions, particularly assumption (A), require clarification on their strength and applicability. The authors should also clarify the differences between standard stochastic primal-dual methods and the proposed method. In Theorem 2, the constant μ is unclear, and its relationship to Theorem 1 should be explained. The transition from assumptions (A)-(C) to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing and should be clarified.

Minor issues include the inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and the missing reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm.

In conclusion, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization, but some areas of the presentation require improvement. The authors should address the mentioned weaknesses to strengthen the paper's overall quality.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* Originality: The paper presents a novel extension of stochastic variance reduction methods to convex-concave saddle-point problems.
* Technical soundness: The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithms, including convergence rates and computational complexities.
* Significance: The proposed algorithms have the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in optimization for machine learning problems.

Arguments con acceptance:

* Presentation: The connection to monotone operators is unclear, and the experiments are limited to only two datasets.
* Clarity: Some assumptions and notations require clarification, and the transition from assumptions (A)-(C) to strong monotonicity is confusing.
* Comparison: The paper is missing a comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock-type methods.

Overall, I recommend accepting the paper, but the authors should address the mentioned weaknesses to strengthen the paper's overall quality.
