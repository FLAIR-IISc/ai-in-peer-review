- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent algorithms for large-scale instances.  
- Tackles a practically important setting where standard convex minimization analyses do not apply.  
- Leverages monotone operator framework to prove convergence, extending applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Proposes careful gradient-operator splits: element-wise approach for SVRG and factored approach for SAGA.  
- Offers comprehensive theoretical analysis with linear convergence guarantees.  
- Empirical validation shows effectiveness of non-uniform sampling and benefits of the proposed acceleration scheme.  
- Experimental details are thorough and supplemented in the appendix.  
- Acceleration section lacks clarity on how the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) is adaptively updated in practice.  
- Authors claim SVRG convergence proof extends to SAGA but do not provide an explicit SAGA-proof.  
- Notational ambiguity: ‘K’ used for both a smooth function and the design matrix can confuse readers.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution worth acceptance, conditional on clarifying acceleration implementation and supplying the SAGA convergence proof.
