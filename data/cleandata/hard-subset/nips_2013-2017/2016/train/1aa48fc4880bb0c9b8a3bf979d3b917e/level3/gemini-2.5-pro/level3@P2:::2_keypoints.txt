1. Introduces SVRG and SAGA extensions for finite-sum convex-concave saddle-point problems with the first linear convergence analysis.  
2. Uses monotone operator theory to provide a general theoretical foundation applicable beyond saddle-point problems.  
3. Discusses practical algorithmic choices: functional vs. factored operator splitting, importance of non-uniform sampling, and Catalyst acceleration.  
4. Empirical results show practical benefits over batch and standard stochastic baselines on chosen non-separable problems.  
5. High originality and significance: fills a gap by enabling variance-reduced methods for many non-separable ML problems (e.g., AUC, total variation, overlapping group lasso).  
6. Strong theoretical contribution: elegant, general linear-convergence proofs via monotone operator framework.  
7. Practical enhancements: analyzes splitting strategies, proves and empirically validates non-uniform sampling benefits, and provides provable acceleration superior to accelerated batch methods.  
8. Clear and well-structured presentation with concrete motivations and systematic exposition.  
9. Major weakness: assumes strong convexity in x and strong concavity in y (double strong), which is restrictive and excludes common single-strong settings.  
10. Limited experimental scope: few datasets and lack of tests on singly-strongly-convex-concave problems to assess empirical behavior beyond theory.  
11. Key suggestion: relax or discuss extending analysis to single strong convexity (even if only yielding sublinear rates) and outline associated challenges.  
12. Additional suggestion: broaden experiments to canonical problems (e.g., L2-regularized SVM, logistic regression) and more datasets to demonstrate robustness.  
13. Overall assessment: novel, significant, and theoretically strong work with a key limitation on assumptions; recommendation for acceptance.
