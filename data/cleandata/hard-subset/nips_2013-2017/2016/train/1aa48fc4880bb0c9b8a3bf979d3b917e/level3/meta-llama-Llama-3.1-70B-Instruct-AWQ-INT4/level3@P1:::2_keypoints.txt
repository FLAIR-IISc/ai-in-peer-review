- Extends stochastic variance reduction methods (e.g., SVRG/SAGA) to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.
- Provides convergence analysis and shows acceleration via an extension of the Catalyst framework.
- Handles non-separable losses and regularizers, enabling application to supervised learning, robust optimization, and convex relaxations of unsupervised learning.
- Extends the approach to monotone operators, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point problems.
- Numerical experiments show significant improvement over existing batch and stochastic methods, especially on ill-conditioned problems.
- Main weakness: assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual problems, which is an overly strong and potentially unrealistic assumption.
- Recommendation to relax assumptions to require strong convexity on only the primal or only the dual.
- Suggestion to include more numerical experiments to demonstrate robustness across diverse problem settings.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and presents technical details clearly and accessibly.
- Reviewer scores high: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant contribution with demonstrated effectiveness.
