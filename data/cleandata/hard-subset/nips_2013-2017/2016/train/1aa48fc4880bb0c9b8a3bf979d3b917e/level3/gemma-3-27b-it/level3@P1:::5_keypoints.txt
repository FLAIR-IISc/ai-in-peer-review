- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, claiming first linear convergence for this class.
- Uses monotone operator theory to prove convergence, generalizing beyond convex minimization.
- Framework also applies to variational inequalities and common ML tasks (robust optimization, non-separable regularizers).
- Highlights importance of convex-concave splitting and non-uniform sampling for efficiency.
- Proposes catalyst-based acceleration that outperforms accelerated batch algorithms.
- Builds on recent stochastic optimization advances and fills a gap for saddle-point formulations.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and a key strength of the paper.
- Algorithms show improved empirical performance, particularly with non-uniform sampling and acceleration.
- Clarity issues: the two notions of splits (functions vs. partial derivatives) need clearer exposition.
- Lacks implementation detail on constructing the non-uniform sampling probability vector π beyond the bilinear case.
- Experimental details are incomplete: step-size σ selection and sensitivity analysis are not justified.
- Needs more discussion of why non-uniform sampling specifically benefits saddle-point problems versus convex minimization.
- Major proofs are relegated to appendices; including proof sketches in the main text would improve accessibility.
- Minor typographical errors noted (e.g., line 78 k→d; line 121 extra y_{t-1}).
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, theoretically sound work with significant potential impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid convergence analysis, relevant applications, promising empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity, experimental detail, and omission of key proof sketches may hinder reproducibility and understanding.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address clarity, sampling construction, experimental setup, and include key proof sketches.
