- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent large-scale algorithms for this class.  
- Fills a critical gap: prior stochastic variance-reduction methods were limited to separable convex minimization, leaving non-separable saddle-point problems without scalable solvers.  
- Uses monotone operator theory to generalize beyond convexity, enabling applicability to variational inequalities and broadening the theoretical foundation.  
- Novelty is clear: variance reduction with linear convergence for non-separable saddle-point structures is a new contribution.  
- High significance: addresses practical ML settings (e.g., AUC surrogates, grouped regularizers) where existing methods fail.  
- Strong theoretical quality: Theorems 1–3 are technically sound, handle element-wise vs. factored splits, and give explicit complexity bounds.  
- Empirical results support theory: non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform and batch methods; accelerated SVRG shows superior runtime complexity.  
- Catalyst acceleration framework is effectively adapted to saddle-point problems.  
- Minor weakness: adaptive update for the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) in acceleration lacks practical detail on setting τ without knowing L̄ or T(B).  
- Minor exposition gap: SAGA convergence proof is not explicitly extended from SVRG, though it is claimed to generalize.  
- Notational issue: reusing K for both the smooth function and design matrix can be confusing; a distinct symbol is recommended.  
- Overall clarity and presentation are strong, with well-structured sections, clear examples, and thorough experiments.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: technically sound, impactful work that substantially advances scalable optimization for non-separable saddle-point problems; strongly recommended for acceptance.
