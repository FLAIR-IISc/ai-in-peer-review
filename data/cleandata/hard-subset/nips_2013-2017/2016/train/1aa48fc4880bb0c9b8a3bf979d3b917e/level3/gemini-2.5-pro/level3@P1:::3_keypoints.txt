- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing first linearly convergent large-scale algorithms for this class.  
- Analysis uses monotone operator theory instead of standard co-coercivity-based SVRG/SAGA proofs (significant technical contribution).  
- Proposes two problem-splitting strategies: "individual" splits and "factored" splits, with factored splits tailored to bilinear problems.  
- Provides thorough convergence-rate analysis, showing benefits of non-uniform sampling for improved performance.  
- Integrates a Catalyst-style acceleration scheme achieving the best theoretical overall complexity.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly relates new methods to prior variance-reduction and batch saddle-point literature.  
- Originality/significance: fills an important gap by bringing variance reduction to saddle-point problems common in ML.  
- Technical quality: proofs and complexity analysis are sound, non-trivial, and comprehensive (Table 1 comparisons).  
- Clarity/experiments: organized exposition and experiments effectively validate non-uniform sampling and acceleration predictions.  
- Major weakness: practical bottleneck—requires computing the full prox of M(x,y) at every incremental iteration, often costing O(n+d).  
- This per-iteration O(n+d) prox cost undermines the incremental method’s motivation and may negate runtime gains over batch methods.  
- Comparison to accelerated batch methods is unclear because Table 1 appears to omit prox cost, so total runtime advantage is not convincing.  
- Factored splits complexity/storage concerns: SAGA storage O(n+d) but iteration complexity may scale with max{|J|,|K|} (potentially O(n)), hurting asymmetric problems (n >> d).  
- Questions for authors: clarify precise conditions (n, d, condition numbers, T(Bi)) where accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling outperforms accelerated batch methods when prox cost is included.  
- Questions for authors: is the n and d dependency for factored splits tight or an artifact of analysis, and provide deeper trade-off comparison between individual and factored splits.  
- Overall: strong and novel theoretical contribution, but practical significance is questionable unless the prox-cost issue and factored-split trade-offs are more thoroughly addressed.
