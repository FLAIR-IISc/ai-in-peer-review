Paper extends SVRG and SAGA to large-scale convex-concave saddle-point problems using a monotone-operator framework to prove linear convergence.  
Introduces acceleration and non-uniform sampling schemes and handles non-separable functions common in ML.  
Major theoretical contribution: first linearly convergent large-scale algorithms for this saddle-point class, with applicability to variational inequalities.  
Monotone-operator analysis is a powerful insight that broadens the methods' applicability beyond standard convex minimization.  
Presentation flaw: the monotone-operator connection is introduced late as an "extension" rather than the central analytical tool, hurting logical flow.  
Weak experimental validation: only two datasets used, insufficient to support broad empirical claims.  
Missing comparison to relevant accelerated stochastic primal-dual methods, e.g., accelerated Chambolle–Pock-type work (Zhang & Xiao, JMLR 2015).  
Unclear practical validity of Assumptions (A)–(C); need justification and examples (e.g., SVM-induced saddle-point problems).  
Relationship between the proposed methods and standard stochastic primal-dual methods is not clearly detailed.  
The constant μ in Theorem 2 is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
Transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to the strong monotonicity property in the appendix is confusing and requires clearer exposition.  
Minor issues: inconsistent notation for L and a missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
Overall recommendation: core ideas are original and significant but the paper needs major revision to improve clarity, experiments, and address technical ambiguities.
