- Studies composite convex–concave saddle-point problems min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with proximable strongly convex–concave M and smooth non-separable K.  
- Extends variance-reduced incremental methods (SVRG/SAGA) to this saddle-point setting and provides first linear-convergence analyses.  
- Frames analysis via monotone operator theory rather than cocoercivity, broadening applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Introduces two splitting notions for B = ∑_i B_i: function-level splits and partial-derivative/factored row–column splits (useful for bilinear K).  
- Identifies necessity and benefits of non-uniform sampling and describes practical storage for SAGA under factored splits.  
- Proposes a Catalyst-style proximal-point outer loop tailored to accelerate saddle-point methods.  
- Experiments on two datasets show benefits of non-uniform sampling and acceleration but are limited in scope.  
- Related to variance-reduction literature (SAG/SVRG/SAGA) and primal–dual splitting (Chambolle–Pock), but targets non-separable losses/regularizers and derivative-only splits.  
- Complementary to stochastic forward–backward and mirror-prox methods; variance reduction yields improved linear rates under strong monotonicity.  
- Theory: linear convergence proofs for SVRG/SAGA under strong convex–concavity/monotonicity are technically solid and novel.  
- Algorithms: clean extension of SVRG/SAGA with careful treatment of split types and non-uniform sampling; acceleration is simpler than Nesterov-style machinery.  
- Assumptions: need clarity on how often Assumption (A) (strong (λ,γ)-convex–concave M with efficient prox) holds in common ML models (e.g., hinge SVM requires smoothing).  
- Constants/notation: define μ (strong monotonicity) in main text and clarify relation to λ, γ; avoid calling L a “condition number” and unify L vs L̄(π).  
- Theorem 2: clarify the role of resampling with non-uniform sampling—text conflicts with theorem requirement.  
- Empirics: require more datasets, wall-clock times, ablations (uniform vs non-uniform sampling, individual vs factored splits), and comparisons to SPDC, accelerated PDHG, and mirror-prox variants.  
- Assumption (C): clarify precise requirements for factored splits and implications for SAGA storage/update rules.  
- Originality: first variance-reduced, linearly convergent large-scale method for non-separable saddle-point structures allowing derivative-only splits; Catalyst-style acceleration adaptation is a neat contribution.  
- Significance: high potential impact for ML problems with prox-available M (AUC surrogates, overlapping group penalties, TV, robust optimization); broadens reach to variational inequalities.  
- Pros: sound monotone-operator analysis, linear rates for SVRG/SAGA, handles non-separable prox M, flexible splitting, non-uniform sampling guidance, practical acceleration.  
- Cons: clarity issues about assumptions and constants, limited experimental comparisons, confusion over resampling necessity in SAGA, misleading use of “condition number.”  
- Key questions for authors: define μ and relate to (λ,γ); list common setups where (A) holds or smoothing is needed; state whether SAGA resampling is required and what weaker guarantees exist; advise on choosing individual vs factored splits and memory/runtime trade-offs.  
- Minor requests: unify L and L̄(π) early, state per-iteration cost model including T_prox(M), add SPDC reference and consider as baseline, and add standard FB splitting references.  
- Overall: technically strong and impactful paper; recommend acceptance after clarifying assumptions/constants and strengthening empirical comparisons to accelerated primal–dual baselines.
