- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, a significant contribution.  
- Provides rigorous convergence analysis via monotone operator theory, extending results to variational inequalities.  
- Elegantly adapts the Catalyst acceleration framework, showing theoretical gains over batch methods.  
- Formalizes two split notions (function-based vs. partial-derivative-based) and demonstrates importance of non-uniform sampling.  
- Strong technical quality with well-supported theoretical results building on recent stochastic optimization advances.  
- Generally clear exposition of algorithms and analysis, though monotone operator theory could be more accessible to ML readers.  
- Highly original: first to obtain linear convergence guarantees for variance-reduction on saddle-point problems.  
- High significance given prevalence of saddle-point formulations in ML (robust optimization, structured prediction, duals).  
- Numerical results demonstrate practical advantages, but experiments should include more problem types and comparisons to state-of-the-art solvers.  
- Main weakness: assumption of strong convexity-concavity on both primal and dual is overly restrictive; suggest relaxing to one-sided strong convexity.  
- Overall strong paper; with minor revisions on assumptions and expanded experiments, suitable for NIPS acceptance.
