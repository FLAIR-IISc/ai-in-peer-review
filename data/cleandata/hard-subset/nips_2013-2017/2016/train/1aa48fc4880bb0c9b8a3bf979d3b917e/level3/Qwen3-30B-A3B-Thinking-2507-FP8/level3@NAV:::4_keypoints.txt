- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with proven linear convergence.  
- Fills an important gap: variance-reduction methods previously focused on convex minimization, not saddle-point problems.  
- Uses the monotone-operator framework for a sophisticated, broadly applicable convergence analysis.  
- Introduces two novel gradient-operator splits (element-wise and factored) to handle non-separable saddle-point problems.  
- Provides comprehensive theoretical analysis supported by extensive empirical evidence.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling significantly outperforms uniform sampling both in theory and practice.  
- Demonstrates strong technical quality with clear algorithm presentation and careful analysis of saddle-point structure.  
- Experimental results across multiple ML applications (non-separable regularizers/losses) strongly support the theoretical claims.  
- High originality: extends variance-reduction to new problem class and enables efficient large-scale solutions.  
- Significant practical impact for applications like robust optimization, structured prediction, and matrix factorization.  
- Minor concern: adaptive update mechanism for the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) in the acceleration section is unclear.  
- Minor concern: unclear whether SVRG convergence and acceleration proofs extend to SAGA.  
- Minor concern: notational confusion between smooth function K(·) and design matrix K should be resolved.  
- Overall: technically sound contribution with clear theory, compelling experiments, and easily addressable minor issues.
