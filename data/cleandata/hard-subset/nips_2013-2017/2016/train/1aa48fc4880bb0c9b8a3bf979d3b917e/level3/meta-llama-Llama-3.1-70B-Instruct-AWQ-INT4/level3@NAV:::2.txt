**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper "Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems" presents a novel approach to solving convex-concave saddle-point problems using stochastic variance reduction methods. The authors extend recent methods such as SVRG and SAGA to this setting, providing the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems. They also show that these methods can be accelerated using an extension of the Catalyst framework.

**Strengths**

1. **Novel extension of SVRG and SAGA**: The authors successfully extend these methods to saddle-point problems, which is a significant contribution to the field.
2. **Linear convergence**: The proposed methods achieve linear convergence, which is a desirable property in optimization algorithms.
3. **Acceleration**: The authors show that their methods can be accelerated using the Catalyst framework, leading to improved performance.
4. **Extension to monotone operators**: The paper also extends the approach to monotone operators, which is a broader class of problems.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Strong convexity assumption**: The authors assume strong convexity on both primal and dual problems, which may be too restrictive in practice.
2. **Limited numerical experiments**: While the paper includes some numerical results, more experiments would be beneficial to demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed methods.

**Recommendations**

1. **Relax strong convexity assumption**: Consider relaxing the strong convexity assumption to only require strong convexity on either the primal or dual problem.
2. **More numerical experiments**: Include more numerical experiments to demonstrate the performance of the proposed methods on a variety of problems.
