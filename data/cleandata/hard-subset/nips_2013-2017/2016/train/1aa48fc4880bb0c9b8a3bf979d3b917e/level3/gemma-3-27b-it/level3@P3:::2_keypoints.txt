- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, achieving linear convergence with simple modifications.  
- Further generalizes methods to monotone operators, broadening applicability.  
- Convergence analysis is rigorous and leverages monotone operator theory effectively.  
- Derives convergence rates for both SVRG and SAGA, showing substantial improvements over batch algorithms.  
- Highlights importance of two notions of splits and non-uniform sampling for algorithm performance.  
- Includes Catalyst acceleration to further speed convergence.  
- Well-motivated, clearly written, and organized with helpful examples linking theory to applications.  
- Numerical experiments show effectiveness versus baselines but are limited to two datasets.  
- Strong convexity assumed on both primal and dual is overly restrictive and limits generality.  
- Experimental scope should include more datasets, non-separable losses/regularizers, and comparisons to recent solvers.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of practical issues: parameter tuning, proximal cost, and high-dimensional implementation challenges.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address relaxing strong convexity, expand experiments, and discuss practical considerations.
