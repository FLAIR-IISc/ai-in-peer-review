- Problem: Extends SVRG/SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with M proximable and B = (∂xK, −∂yK) decomposed into many components.  
- Algorithmic idea: Stochastic forward–backward scheme with variance reduction, non-uniform sampling, and optional catalyst-style proximal regularization.  
- Analytical contribution: Reframes convergence via monotone-operator theory, obtaining linear rates without requiring co-coercivity.  
- Scope extension: Analysis and methods apply beyond saddle points to general monotone variational inequalities.  
- Operator splits: Proposes two decomposition schemes—element-wise and factored—tailored to bilinear/saddle structure.  
- Sampling: Uses non-uniform sampling with explicit tight constants L̄(π) and analyzes its benefits.  
- SVRG vs SAGA: Provides storage vs runtime trade-offs and complexity breakdowns for SAGA and SVRG.  
- Acceleration: Adapts a simple catalyst-style proximal regularization to the saddle-point setting, improving theoretical complexity and empirical performance on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Convergence results: Proves linear convergence with explicit dependence on smoothness constants L and L̄(π) and strong convex–concavity parameters.  
- Practical performance: Experiments show non-uniform sampling outperforms uniform, variance reduction beats batch and plain stochastic methods, and accelerated SVRG performs best.  
- Novelty: First large-scale linearly convergent incremental methods for general convex–concave saddle points with monotone-operator analysis.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends finite-sum acceleration beyond separable convex minimization and addresses non-separable losses/regularizers not handled by prior primal–dual/coordinate methods.  
- Technical strength: Careful monotone-operator analysis avoids co-coercivity assumptions and broadens applicability.  
- Clarity: Generally well written; explains roles of L vs L̄(π) and the two splits with interpretable bounds in bilinear cases.  
- Weakness — acceleration tuning: Section 5 lacks concrete guidance on adaptive updates for the anchor (x̄,ȳ) and schedules/safeguards for practitioners.  
- Weakness — SAGA acceleration: Catalyst theory given for SVRG only; no proof or clear conditions for carrying it over to SAGA.  
- Weakness — parameter knowledge: Step-sizes require knowing λ, γ, L, L̄; robustness to misspecification and adaptive strategies are not discussed.  
- Weakness — experiments: Experimental breadth is limited (more datasets, wall-clock times, sparsity/conditioning sensitivity, and ablations needed).  
- Weakness — SAGA resampling: Theory requires resampling after iterate updates, while empirical practice mixes sampling without resampling; theoretical–practical gap not discussed.  
- Weakness — notation: Overloads K for both function and design matrix in bilinear case; distinct symbols would improve clarity.  
- Suggestions: Clarify/adaptively specify anchor update schedule and include an ablation; provide proof or conditions for SAGA acceleration; discuss adaptive estimation or backtracking for constants; expand experiments (wall-clock, splits, sampling); fix notation overload.
