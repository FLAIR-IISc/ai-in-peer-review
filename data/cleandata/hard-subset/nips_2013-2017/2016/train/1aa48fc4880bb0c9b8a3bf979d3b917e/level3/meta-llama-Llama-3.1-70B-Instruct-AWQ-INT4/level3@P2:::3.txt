**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes an extension of Stochastic Variance Reduction (SVRG) and Stochastic Average Gradient (SAGA) methods to strongly convex-concave finite-sum saddle-point problems. The authors consider two types of splits: individual and factored, and provide a thorough complexity analysis under different splits and uniform/non-uniform sampling strategies. The paper also presents experiments on a binary classification example, demonstrating the importance of non-uniform sampling and acceleration in beating batch algorithms.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on SVRG and SAGA methods for convex minimization problems. The authors extend these methods to saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The paper also draws connections to monotone operators and variational inequalities, highlighting the broader applicability of the proposed methods.

**Strengths**

1. The paper provides a novel and technically non-trivial extension of SVRG and SAGA methods to saddle-point problems.
2. The authors present a thorough complexity analysis, considering different splits and sampling strategies.
3. The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods in beating batch algorithms.

**Weaknesses**

1. The paper does not address the fundamental bottleneck of computing the proximal operator, which incurs an O(n + d) cost per incremental iteration.
2. The overall complexity of the proposed methods may not improve over accelerated batch Forward-Backward (FB) algorithms, especially for factored splits when n >> d.
3. The paper could benefit from a deeper discussion on the theoretical comparison between factored and individual splits.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their methods and results. The complexity analysis is thorough, and the experiments are well-designed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their methods and results. The notation is consistent, and the figures are helpful in illustrating the key concepts.

**Originality**

**Significance**

The paper has the potential to impact the field of machine learning, particularly in applications where saddle-point problems are common. The proposed methods could lead to more efficient and effective solutions for these problems.
