- Extends SVRG and SAGA from convex minimization to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides the first linear convergence guarantees for these variance-reduced methods in this setting.  
- Leverages the monotone-operator framework, broadening applicability to variational inequalities and game-theoretic problems.  
- Novel and impactful contribution enabling large-scale, linearly convergent algorithms for non-separable saddle-point problems.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound and complete, with rigorous proofs of linear convergence.  
- Empirical results are thorough and show practical effectiveness, including benefit of non-uniform sampling.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well-organized, with useful gradient-operator split variants (element-wise and factored).  
- Acceleration technique is promising but lacks guidance on adaptively updating the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) for best practical speed-up.  
- Acceleration and convergence proofs are given for SVRG, but it’s unclear if the same analysis directly extends to SAGA.  
- Minor notational confusion: the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K share the same symbol.
