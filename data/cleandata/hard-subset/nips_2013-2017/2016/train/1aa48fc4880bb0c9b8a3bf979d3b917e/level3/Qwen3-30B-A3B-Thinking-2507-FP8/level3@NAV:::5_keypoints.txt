- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent algorithms for this problem class.  
- Key insight: using the monotone operator framework for convergence analysis, enabling generalization to variational inequalities rather than relying on convex-minimization techniques.  
- Theoretical approach is elegant and broadly applicable, yielding simpler proofs for forward-backward splitting and its stochastic variant.  
- Main strength: enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers via saddle-point formulation—important for many ML applications.  
- Experiments show non-uniform sampling algorithms are competitive and often outperform prior methods, especially on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Weakness: main novelty is in the proof technique, so the paper needs more detailed proof sketches in the main text.  
- Clarity issues to address: specify non-uniform sampling probabilities beyond bilinear problems (define π); explain intuition for step-size choices in experiments; justify why non-uniform sampling is more crucial for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Minor typos to correct (lines 78 and 121).  
- Overall assessment: well-written, technically sound, high originality and substantial significance; would be improved with more detailed technical and experimental explanations.
