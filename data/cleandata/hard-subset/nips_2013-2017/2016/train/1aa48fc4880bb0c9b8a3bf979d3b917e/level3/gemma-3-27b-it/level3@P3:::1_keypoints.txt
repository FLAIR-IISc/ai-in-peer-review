- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems with theoretical linear convergence and acceleration techniques.  
- Novel: addresses non-separable ML objectives and frames algorithms via monotone operator theory, extending to variational inequalities.  
- Identifies non-uniform sampling as crucial for theoretical and empirical efficiency.  
- Catalyst-based acceleration shows clear benefits over standard batch methods.  
- Strong technical contributions overall, but presentation and experiments need improvement.  
- Connection to monotone operators is introduced too late (Section 6) and should be integrated earlier.  
- Assumptions (A)-(C) are insufficiently explained; unclear whether they imply strong monotonicity and how they relate to constants (e.g., μ).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets) and insufficient to establish robustness or generalizability.  
- Missing comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock-type methods (important baseline omission).  
- Lacks a clear distinction and detailed comparison to standard stochastic primal-dual methods.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation for ‘L’ and missing reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, novelty, practical relevance, and shown empirical gains.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems, limited experiments, unclear assumptions, and missing key comparisons.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending major revisions—reorganize to emphasize monotone operator framework, clarify assumptions/constants, expand experiments and baselines (include Chambolle-Pock), distinguish from primal-dual methods, and fix notation/references.
