## Review of "Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems"

This paper tackles the important problem of scaling up optimization for saddle-point problems, a common occurrence in many machine learning applications. The authors extend SVRG and SAGA to this setting, providing the first linearly convergent algorithms for large-scale saddle-point problems. This is a significant contribution, as existing methods often struggle with the non-separable nature of these problems. The core idea of adapting variance reduction techniques is sound and well-motivated, building upon the success of SVRG/SAGA in convex minimization. 

The paper’s strength lies in its thoroughness. The authors not only present the algorithmic extensions but also provide a rigorous convergence analysis using a monotone operator framework – a more general and appropriate tool than standard convex analysis in this context. This broader framework is a key contribution, highlighting the applicability of their methods to a wider class of problems like variational inequalities. The exploration of different gradient-operator splits (element-wise and factored) for SVRG and SAGA is insightful, and the empirical validation, including the demonstration of the benefits of non-uniform sampling, strengthens the claims. The experimental results, while presented concisely in the main text, appear robust and support the theoretical findings. 

However, the paper isn’t without its weaknesses. The discussion of acceleration (Section 5) feels somewhat incomplete. It’s unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) to achieve speed-up, leaving a practical implementation detail unresolved. Furthermore, the authors state that the convergence proof for SVRG should also hold for SAGA, but don’t explicitly demonstrate this, raising a question about the generality of their analysis. A minor notational issue exists with the use of ‘K’ for both the smooth function and the design matrix, potentially causing confusion.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Extends successful variance reduction methods to a challenging and important class of problems.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** Rigorous convergence analysis using a suitable mathematical framework.
*   **Practical Relevance:** Demonstrates effectiveness through experiments, including the benefit of non-uniform sampling.
*   **Generality:** The monotone operator framework broadens the applicability of the proposed methods.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Incomplete Acceleration Discussion:** Lack of clarity on adaptive reference iterate updates.
*   **Unproven SAGA Extension:** The claim of shared convergence proof with SVRG requires further justification.
*   **Notational Ambiguity:** Potential confusion due to the dual use of ‘K’.



**Overall:** Despite these minor concerns, this is a strong paper with significant contributions to the field. The theoretical and empirical results are compelling, and the work addresses a relevant and challenging problem. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
