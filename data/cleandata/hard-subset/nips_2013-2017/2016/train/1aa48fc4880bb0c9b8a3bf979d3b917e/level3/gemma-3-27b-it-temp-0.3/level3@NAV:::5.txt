## Review of "Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems"

This paper tackles the important problem of developing linearly convergent algorithms for saddle-point problems, a common occurrence in machine learning, particularly when dealing with non-separable loss functions or regularizers. The authors extend SVRG and SAGA to this setting, recognizing that standard convex minimization analyses do not directly apply. A key contribution is framing the problem as finding zeros of a monotone operator, allowing for a more general convergence proof that also extends to variational inequalities. This is a clever and broadly applicable proof technique.

The work is significant because achieving linear convergence for saddle-point problems is a notable advance. The authors correctly identify the importance of non-uniform sampling for efficiency, both theoretically and empirically, and demonstrate that their algorithms, coupled with a catalyst-like acceleration, outperform existing batch methods. The paper builds upon established stochastic variance reduction techniques in a logical and well-motivated manner. The extension of simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant is also a valuable addition.

However, while the core ideas are strong, the paper suffers from some clarity issues and a lack of detailed proofs. The authors state the analysis generalizes, but providing a sketch of the monotone operator proof, even in the appendix, would significantly strengthen the contribution. The choice of the non-uniform sampling distribution (π) beyond the bilinear case is not clearly explained – more detail here is crucial. Similarly, the experimental section lacks specifics regarding the step size (σ) selection. A discussion of *why* non-uniform sampling is more critical for saddle-point problems compared to convex minimization would also be beneficial. 

There are minor typos (line 78, 121) that should be addressed. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel application of monotone operator theory to analyze SVRG/SAGA for saddle-point problems.
*   First linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems.
*   Demonstrated empirical performance improvements.
*   Broad applicability beyond saddle-point problems.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of detailed proofs, particularly for the monotone operator framework.
*   Insufficient clarity regarding the non-uniform sampling distribution and step size selection.
*   Clarity issues in several sections.



**Overall:** This is a solid contribution with the potential to be a significant advancement in the field. Addressing the concerns regarding proof details and clarity would substantially improve the paper and warrant a strong acceptance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions, contingent on the authors providing more detailed explanations and proof sketches.
