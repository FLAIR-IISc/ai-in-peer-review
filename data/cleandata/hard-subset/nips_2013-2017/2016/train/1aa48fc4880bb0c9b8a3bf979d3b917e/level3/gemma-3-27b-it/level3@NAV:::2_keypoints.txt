- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent algorithms for large-scale instances.  
- Demonstrates acceleration via the catalyst framework and generalizes the approach to monotone operators.  
- Strong theoretical analysis with convergence proofs based on monotone operator theory.  
- Empirical results show performance gains over batch and stochastic baselines.  
- Key weakness: requires strong convexity on both primal and dual problems, limiting real-world applicability.  
- Authors note remedy (strong convexity on only one side) — this relaxation should be addressed in future revisions.  
- Numerical evaluation is limited (two datasets, narrow parameter range); needs broader datasets and exploration of sampling strategies.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing convexity assumptions and expanding empirical evaluation.
