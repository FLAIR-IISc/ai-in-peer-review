- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, delivering the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.  
- Fills a literature gap: prior variance-reduction techniques targeted convex minimization, not saddle-point problems.  
- Builds on SAG/SVRG/SAGA principles with appropriate algorithmic modifications for saddle-point settings.  
- Provides a novel convergence analysis using monotone operator theory, extending applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Identifies two distinct notions of splits (functions vs partial derivatives) and shows non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency.  
- Successfully extends the Catalyst acceleration framework; accelerated variants consistently outperform batch methods.  
- Theoretical analysis is thorough with well-justified assumptions and detailed proofs in the appendix.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with effective motivation and relation to prior NIPS work.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated for bilinear saddle-point problems and supervised learning with non-separable losses/regularizers.  
- Experiments show clear improvements over existing methods but are limited to only two datasets (sido and rcv1).  
- No experiments on non-bilinear saddle-point problems or variational inequalities despite claiming broader applicability.  
- Major weakness: assumes strong convexity-concavity on both primal and dual variables, which is overly strong for many practical problems.  
- Recommendation to relax assumption to strong convexity on one side and analyze algorithm behavior under weaker conditions.  
- Suggestion to add more extensive empirical validation across diverse problem types and comparisons with state-of-the-art.  
- Overall: technically sound and original with substantial potential impact; recommend accept with minor revisions.
