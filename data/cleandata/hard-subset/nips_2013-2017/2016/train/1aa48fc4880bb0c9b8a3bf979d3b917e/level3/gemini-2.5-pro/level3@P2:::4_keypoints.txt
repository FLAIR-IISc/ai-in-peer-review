- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent large-scale algorithms for this class.  
- Leverages the monotone operator framework for convergence proofs, yielding a general and robust theoretical analysis beyond standard convex minimization techniques.  
- Analyzes practical/theoretical considerations unique to saddle-point problems, including "factored" vs. "element-wise" gradient splitting, the importance of non-uniform sampling, and a Catalyst-based acceleration scheme.  
- Provides complete results: algorithms, convergence rates (including non-uniform sampling and accelerated variants), and regimes where acceleration improves over batch methods.  
- Well-written and organized; empirical results validate theoretical claims, with accelerated SVRG + non-uniform sampling outperforming batch and stochastic baselines on real datasets.  
- Minor clarity issue: no concrete details on how to implement the suggested adaptive updating of the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ).  
- Acceleration is formally developed for SVRG only; the claim that the same scheme should work for SAGA lacks justification or discussion of potential analysis challenges.  
- Notational ambiguity: symbol K is used both for the smooth component K(x,y) and for a design matrix in examples, which could be clarified.  
- Overall judgment: minor weaknesses only; strong, timely contribution deserving acceptance.
