- Proposes multinomial dropout that samples features/neurons with feature-dependent probabilities.  
- Main contribution: risk-bound analysis for stochastic optimization with multinomial dropout.  
- Derived distribution-dependent dropout that yields faster convergence and smaller generalization error.  
- Introduces evolutional dropout that adapts sampling probabilities to evolving layer output distributions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear background and thorough related work.  
- Technical contributions are rigorous and significant.  
- Experimental results on benchmarks show improved convergence and reduced test error.  
- Strength: theoretically grounded distribution-dependent dropout.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation across datasets.  
- Strength: clear and concise presentation.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of relationship to other regularization methods (e.g., batch normalization).  
- Weakness: limited insight/justification for hyperparameter choices (k, step size).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the above weaknesses.
