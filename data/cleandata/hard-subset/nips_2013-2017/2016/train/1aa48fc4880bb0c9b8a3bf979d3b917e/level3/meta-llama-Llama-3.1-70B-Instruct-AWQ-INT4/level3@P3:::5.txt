This paper proposes the first linearly convergent stochastic variance reduction methods for saddle-point problems, a common class of problems in machine learning. The authors extend recent stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) to provide large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems.

The main contribution of this paper is the convergence proof via the monotone-operator framework, which generalizes to a wider class of problems, including variational inequality problems. The approach enables handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers through saddle-point formulation.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the algorithms and their convergence rates, as well as experimental results that demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods.

* The proposal of the first linearly convergent stochastic variance reduction methods for saddle-point problems, which is an important advance in machine learning.
* The use of the monotone-operator framework to prove convergence, which is an interesting and broadly applicable proof technique.
* The generalization of the analysis to a wider class of problems, including variational inequality problems.
* The provision of simpler proofs for FB splitting and its stochastic variant.
