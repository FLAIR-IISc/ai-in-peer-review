- Proposes the first linearly convergent incremental gradient algorithms for convex-concave saddle-point problems using SVRG and SAGA.  
- Extends the monotone-operator framework to prove convergence, generalizing to variational inequality problems.  
- Main technical contribution is a novel proof technique interpreting saddle-point problems as finding zeros of a monotone operator.  
- Approach enables handling non-separable losses and regularizers via a saddle-point formulation.  
- Claims of first linear convergence for saddle-point problems represent an important advance in ML.  
- Paper is mostly clear, well-written, and logically organized with a good relation to prior work.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments support the claims; the work is technically sound and reproducible for experts.  
- Main novelty is the proof technique, so more proof details or a sketch should be included in the main paper.  
- Clarify non-uniform sampling beyond bilinear problems, specifically the form of the probability vector π.  
- Explain how experiment step size σ was chosen and why non-uniform sampling is more crucial for saddle-point problems than for convex minimization.  
- Several minor typos to fix (e.g., line 78 k -> d; extra y_{t-1} in second equality on line 121).  
- Originality and significance are high; results are likely to be used or extended by researchers and practitioners.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity, additional proof details, sampling explanation, and minor corrections.  
- Pro-accept arguments: novel approach, new proof technique, broad applicability, and demonstrable advancement over prior work.  
- Con-accept arguments: clarity and exposition issues, missing proof detail in main text, incomplete description of sampling and experimental choices, and minor typos.
