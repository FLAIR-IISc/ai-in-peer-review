- Proposes first linearly convergent stochastic variance-reduction algorithms (SVRG/SAGA) for convex-concave saddle-point problems.
- Provides a novel convergence proof using monotone operator theory, differing from convex minimization analyses.
- Analysis generalizes beyond saddle-point problems to variational inequalities, broadening applicability.
- Algorithms handle non-separable losses and regularizers that prior methods cannot address.
- Correctly emphasizes that saddle-point problems require different treatment than convex minimization (convex-concave splits, non-uniform sampling).
- Non-uniform sampling is identified as critical; experiments show it significantly outperforms uniform sampling and batch methods.
- Accelerated variants show particularly strong empirical performance on ill-conditioned problems.
- Theoretical analysis is sound, with clear problem setup, appropriate assumptions, and proven convergence under those conditions.
- Experiments are well-designed and support the theoretical claims with appropriate baselines.
- Paper is generally well-written and structured, but some sections need more detailed explanations.
- Key insight about monotone-operator-based convergence proof is somewhat buried and would benefit from a concise presentation.
- Originality is strong: application of variance-reduction to saddle-point problems plus the novel convergence analysis is a significant advance.
- Insufficient explanation of how to choose non-uniform sampling probabilities beyond the bilinear π_jk = K_jk^2/||K||_F^2 example.
- Limited justification and explanation for step-size (σ) selection in the experiments.
- Minor typos and technical errors (e.g., line 78: "k" vs "d"; line 121: extra y_{t-1} term) should be corrected.
- Overall assessment: significant, high-quality, and original contribution with minor clarifications needed—strong candidate for acceptance.
