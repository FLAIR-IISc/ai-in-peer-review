- Proposes multinomial dropout that samples features/neurons with feature-specific probabilities instead of uniform dropout.
- Provides rigorous risk-bound analysis for shallow learning showing distribution-dependent dropout yields faster convergence and smaller generalization error.
- Introduces evolutional dropout that adapts sampling probabilities to layers’ evolving output distributions to address internal covariate shift.
- Empirical results on benchmark datasets show markedly faster convergence and reduced test error with the proposed methods.
- Theoretical analysis is sound and well-integrated with empirical validation.
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and provides a clear, concise explanation of the approach.
- Gives a good related-work review and clearly distinguishes the proposed method from existing dropout variants.
- Assumes sampling probabilities are known, which may not hold in practice and is a potential weakness.
- Could benefit from more numerical examples to further support the proposed methods.
- May not be suitable for all deep learning model types; limitations and applicability could be discussed more.
- Strong arguments for acceptance: novel dropout formulation, rigorous analysis, convincing experiments, and clarity of presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on known probabilities, limited numerical examples, and unclear generalizability to all models.
