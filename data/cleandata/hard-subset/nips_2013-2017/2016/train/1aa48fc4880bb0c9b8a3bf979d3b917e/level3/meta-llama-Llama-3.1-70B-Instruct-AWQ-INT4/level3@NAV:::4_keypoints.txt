- Proposes stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) tailored to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Extends variance-reduction to non-separable saddle-point problems where loss and regularizer may be non-separable.  
- Provides first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class of problems.  
- Uses a monotone-operator framework for convergence proofs, which is more complex than standard convex analyses.  
- Analysis suggests applicability beyond saddle-points to broader variational inequalities, including game-theoretic settings.  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
- Applies variance reduction to stochastic forward–backward algorithms common in saddle-point optimization.  
- Considers and demonstrates benefits of non-uniform sampling over uniform sampling in theory and experiments.  
- Empirical results support theoretical claims and show practical effectiveness.  
- Strengths: novelty, technical soundness, clarity, and thorough convergence analysis.  
- Concern: unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) in the acceleration section to realize speed-ups.  
- Concern: unclear whether the convergence and acceleration proofs provided for SVRG also hold for SAGA.  
- Concern: notational clash between smooth function K(·) and design matrix K — distinct symbols needed.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the notation and clarity/validity of acceleration and SAGA-related proofs.
