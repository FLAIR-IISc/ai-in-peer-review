- Addresses large-scale finite-sum convex-concave saddle-point problems relevant to ML (robust optimization, non-separable losses/regularizers).  
- Main contribution: extend SVRG and SAGA to the saddle-point setting with novel theoretical analysis.  
- Proves linear convergence under a strong convexity–concavity assumption.  
- Analysis framed in monotone operator theory, extending applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Extends the Catalyst acceleration framework to these stochastic methods, yielding theoretically superior algorithms to accelerated batch methods.  
- Studies sampling strategies and highlights benefits of non-uniform sampling.  
- Numerical experiments on binary classification with non-separable objectives support theoretical claims.  
- Strength: first linearly convergent stochastic algorithms for finite-sum saddle-point problems—significant and original.  
- Strength: technically strong and elegant convergence analysis that generalizes SVRG/SAGA beyond minimization.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with helpful complexity summary (Table 1).  
- Weakness: restrictive strong convexity–concavity assumption limits applicability (e.g., L1 regularization, hinge loss).  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to custom tasks, lacking standard saddle-point benchmarks and broader baselines.  
- Suggestion: extend analysis to one-sided strong convexity (or obtain sublinear rates) to broaden practical impact.  
- Suggestion: expand experiments to canonical saddle-point problems, comparisons where primal/dual forms are solvable by SVRG/SAGA, and a detailed study of acceleration vs condition number.  
- Overall: strong, novel, and technically solid paper whose main limitation is the restrictive assumption, but it lays good groundwork for future work.
