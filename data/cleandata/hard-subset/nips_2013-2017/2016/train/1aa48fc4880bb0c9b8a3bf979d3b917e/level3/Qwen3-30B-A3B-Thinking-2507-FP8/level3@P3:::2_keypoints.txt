- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, yielding the first large-scale linearly convergent algorithms for this class.
- Leverages monotone operator theory to handle lack of convexity and provide unified convergence analysis that applies to variational inequalities.
- Provides first convergence analysis of variance-reduced methods for saddle-point problems, proving linear convergence under strong convexity-concavity.
- Identifies two splitting strategies (by functions or by partial derivatives) and emphasizes the importance of non-uniform sampling for efficiency.
- Adapts the Catalyst acceleration framework to the proposed methods, achieving better performance than accelerated batch methods.
- Generalizes the approach from saddle-point problems to monotone operators, broadening applicability.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with strong connections to prior variance-reduction work (SAGA, SVRG).
- Theoretical analysis is careful and sound, accounting for different scaling factors for primal and dual variables.
- Numerical experiments validate theoretical claims and show significant improvements over batch methods and uniform sampling variants.
- Demonstrates advantages on synthetic and real-world datasets, including a challenging non-separable classification problem.
- Methods are particularly effective for ill-conditioned problems.
- Major weakness: requires strong convexity on both primal and dual sides, which is overly restrictive for many ML applications.
- Authors note but do not explore relaxing the assumption to strong convexity on only one side.
- Empirical evaluation is limited (results on two datasets); needs broader baselines, problem types, and deeper analysis of sampling/parameter choices.
- Extension to monotone operators is theoretically interesting but lacks comprehensive empirical validation.
- Overall assessment: high technical quality, originality, and significance; makes a valuable contribution to stochastic optimization for saddle-point problems.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the strong convexity assumption and expand experimental validation.
