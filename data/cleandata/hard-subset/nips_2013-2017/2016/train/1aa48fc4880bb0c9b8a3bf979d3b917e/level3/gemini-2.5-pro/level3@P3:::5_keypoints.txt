- Summary: Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, giving the first stochastic algorithms with linear convergence using a monotone-operator framework; supports non-uniform sampling, catalyst acceleration, and shows strong empirical gains on non-separable losses.

- Strength 1 (Significance): First linearly convergent stochastic methods for general saddle-point problems—high impact for applications like robust optimization, AUC, structured prediction, and non-separable regularizers.

- Strength 2 (Originality): Novel, elegant convergence proof using monotone-operator theory that broadens applicability (e.g., variational inequalities) and simplifies some existing analyses.

- Strength 3 (Practical performance): Methods handle non-separable objectives; non-uniform sampling and acceleration yield superior empirical performance, especially in ill-conditioned settings.

- Weakness 1 (Non-uniform sampling detail): Paper lacks guidance on how to construct the importance sampling distribution π for general (non-bilinear) saddle-point problems.

- Weakness 2 (Hyperparameters): Missing details on hyperparameter selection—particularly how step size σ was chosen—hindering reproducibility and assessment of robustness.

- Weakness 3 (Intuition for sampling): Requests more intuition for why non-uniform sampling is potentially more critical in saddle-point problems versus standard convex minimization, especially regarding dual-variable structure.

- Weakness 4 (Presentation of proof): Recommend adding a high-level proof sketch of the monotone-operator analysis in the main text, since the novel proof is the core contribution but currently resides in the appendix.

- Minor typo 1: Line 78 — summation index in the "factored" split appears incorrect (∑ k∈K vs expected ∑_{i=1}^d); please clarify.

- Minor typo 2: Line 121 — Forward-Backward update for y_t seems to contain an extra y_{t-1} term; please verify.

- Overall evaluation: Excellent, timely, and technically strong paper with novel analysis and solid experiments; recommend acceptance pending clarity revisions.
