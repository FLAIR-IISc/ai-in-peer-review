This paper studies the application of stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) to convex-concave saddle-point problems, which are common in machine learning. The authors extend the convergence analysis of these methods to the saddle-point setting, proving linear convergence under certain assumptions. They also propose an acceleration technique and non-uniform sampling, which can improve the efficiency of the algorithms.

The contributions of this paper are significant, and the theoretical analysis provides new insights into the applicability of these methods to non-separable functions. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be applied to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities.

However, the presentation of the paper could be improved. The connection to monotone operators is unclear and appears late in the paper as an extension, despite underpinning the analysis. The experiments are limited to only two datasets, and more datasets and comparisons are needed to fully evaluate the performance of the proposed methods.

Additionally, the paper is missing a comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock-type methods, which are relevant to the topic. The authors should also clarify the strength and applicability of assumptions A-C, especially assumption A, and question whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems.

The paper could also benefit from a clearer explanation of the differences between standard stochastic primal-dual methods and the authors' proposed method. In Theorem 2, the constant μ is unclear, and the authors should confirm whether it is the monotonicity constant from the appendix and explain the comparison to Theorem 1.

Furthermore, the transition from assumptions A-C to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing, and the authors should state whether A-C imply monotonicity. There are also minor issues with the inconsistent use of L (condition number vs. Lipschitz constant) and a missing reference for the Forward-Backward algorithm.

Overall, this paper provides a valuable contribution to the field of stochastic optimization, but could benefit from improvements in presentation, experimentation, and comparison to other relevant methods.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis. However, the experimental evaluation is limited, and more datasets and comparisons are needed to fully evaluate the performance of the proposed methods.

Clarity: The paper is not clearly written, and the connection to monotone operators is unclear. The presentation could be improved by providing a clearer explanation of the differences between standard stochastic primal-dual methods and the authors' proposed method.

Originality: The paper provides a novel application of stochastic variance reduction methods to convex-concave saddle-point problems. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be applied to a broader class of problems, including variational inequalities.

Significance: The results of this paper are important, and the proposed methods have the potential to be widely used in machine learning. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.
