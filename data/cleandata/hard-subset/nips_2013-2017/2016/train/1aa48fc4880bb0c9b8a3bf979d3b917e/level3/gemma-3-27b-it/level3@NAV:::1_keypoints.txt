- Extends SVRG/SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems using a monotone operator framework.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis proving linear convergence for SVRG/SAGA in the monotone operator setting.  
- Proposes a “catalyst” acceleration scheme that could yield practical speedups.  
- Analysis offers novel insights on two notions of splitting and highlights the importance of non-uniform sampling.  
- Broadens applicability by extending results to general monotone operators.  
- The monotone-operator connection feels tacked on and should be motivated earlier as it underpins the convergence analysis.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited (only two datasets) and doesn’t convincingly show advantages across diverse problems.  
- Missing crucial comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle-Pock–type methods (e.g., Zhang & Xiao 2015).  
- Unclear whether assumptions A–C are realistic or hold for common cases like SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
- Differences between the proposed method and standard stochastic primal–dual methods are not explained explicitly enough.  
- Theorem 2’s constant μ needs clarification—unclear if it is the monotonicity constant from the appendix.  
- The transition from assumptions A–C to strong monotonicity in the appendix is confusing; authors should state explicitly whether A–C imply monotonicity.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation for ‘L’ and a missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.  
- Overall assessment: weak accept—strong theoretical contributions but presentation and experimental validation require substantial improvement.
