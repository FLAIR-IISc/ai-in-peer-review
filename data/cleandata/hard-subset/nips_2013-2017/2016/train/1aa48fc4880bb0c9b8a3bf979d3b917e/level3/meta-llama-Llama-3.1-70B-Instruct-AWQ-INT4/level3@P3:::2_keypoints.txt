- Proposes stochastic variance-reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) extended to convex-concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence for large-scale settings.  
- Shows acceleration via an extension of the Catalyst framework.  
- Extends the approach to monotone operators, covering variational inequalities and a broader problem class.  
- Provides the first convergence analysis for these variance-reduced algorithms in the saddle-point setting.  
- Uses monotone operator theory to prove convergence and generalize applicability.  
- Explores non-uniform sampling, showing it is important for theoretical and practical efficiency.  
- Presents convergence rates and running-time complexity analyses.  
- Includes numerical experiments demonstrating the methods’ effectiveness.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, well-organized, and accessible to experts and non-experts.  
- The extension of variance-reduction to saddle-point problems is novel and significant.  
- Empirical results support the theoretical claims, strengthening the contribution.  
- Main weakness: assumption of strong convexity on both primal and dual is overly strong and may be impractical.  
- Difficulty in determining strong convexity constants could degrade algorithm performance if misestimated.  
- Recommends future work to relax strong-convexity assumptions and develop adaptivity to unknown constants.  
- Suggests more extensive numerical experiments across datasets and settings to better assess practical performance.  
- Paper would benefit from more discussion of limitations and concrete future research directions.
