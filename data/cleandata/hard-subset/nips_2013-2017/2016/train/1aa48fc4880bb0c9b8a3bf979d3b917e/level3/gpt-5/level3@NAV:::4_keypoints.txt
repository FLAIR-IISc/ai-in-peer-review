- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems, proving linear convergence and showing practical gains.
- Recasts stochastic forward–backward variance-reduced schemes under a monotone-operator framework, enabling analysis beyond convex minimization and covering variational inequalities.
- Handles non-separable losses/regularizers via two operator splits (element-wise and factored row/column) with rigorous complexity bounds and justification for non-uniform sampling.
- Proposes catalyst-style acceleration via regularization of saddle-point subproblems.
- Novelty: first to obtain linearly convergent incremental methods for general strongly convex–concave non-separable saddle points using a monotone-operator analysis.
- Technical quality is high: careful, nontrivial monotone-operator analysis; solid linear convergence and non-uniform sampling theory.
- Empirical results (including appendices) align with theory and demonstrate the benefit of non-uniform over uniform sampling.
- Clarity issue: acceleration section does not specify how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ); needs a concrete schedule or heuristic.
- Clarity issue: unclear whether the convergence and acceleration guarantees for SVRG directly extend to SAGA; proofs/limitations should be clarified.
- Clarity issue: notation conflict between the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K — should use distinct symbols.
- Significance: high potential impact for large-scale saddle-point and variational-inequality problems in ML and game theory.
- Pros for acceptance: novel first results for non-separable saddle points, solid analysis, practical non-uniform sampling, and consistent empirical support.
- Cons for acceptance: acceleration lacks actionable guidance; unclear SAGA extension and practical resampling/storage trade-offs; missing VI experiments and more wall-clock/memory metrics.
- Overall: strong and timely contribution; addressing the noted clarifications would further strengthen the paper.
