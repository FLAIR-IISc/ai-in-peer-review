- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing the first linearly convergent algorithms for this class.
- Main novelty is a convergence analysis via monotone operator theory, which generalizes beyond saddle-point problems to variational inequalities.
- Algorithmic extensions are straightforward; the key contribution is the new proof technique rather than novel algorithm design.
- Shows linear convergence where previous stochastic methods for saddle-point problems had only sublinear rates.
- Carefully analyzes different operator splits (function-based vs partial-derivative-based) and the role of non-uniform sampling.
- Non-uniform sampling is shown to be crucial for efficiency both theoretically and empirically.
- Uses the catalyst acceleration framework to produce algorithms that outperform accelerated batch methods, especially on ill-conditioned problems.
- Enables handling of non-separable losses and regularizers via saddle-point formulations, expanding practical applicability.
- Provides compelling ML examples (supervised with non-separable losses, robust optimization, unsupervised learning) where prior methods fail.
- Experimental results consistently show superiority of non-uniform sampling and strong gains from accelerated variants.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, but would benefit from including proof sketches in the main text.
- Needs clarification on how to choose non-uniform sampling probabilities π for non-bilinear problems.
- Requires clearer explanation of step size selection σ in experiments and correction of minor typos (e.g., lines 78, 121).
- Overall assessment: technically strong, significant and original contribution with recommendation for acceptance pending minor revisions.
