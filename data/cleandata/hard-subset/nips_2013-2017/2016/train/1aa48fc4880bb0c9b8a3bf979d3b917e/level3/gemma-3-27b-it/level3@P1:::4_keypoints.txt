- Extends SVR methods (SVRG, SAGA) to saddle-point problems, providing first large-scale, linearly convergent algorithms for this setting.  
- Uses monotone operator framework to prove convergence, since convex minimization analysis does not directly apply.  
- Addresses subtleties in splitting the gradient operator and emphasizes necessity of convex-concave splits.  
- Demonstrates broader applicability to variational inequalities and non-separable problems common in ML.  
- Provides insightful theoretical and empirical analysis showing non-uniform sampling is crucial for performance.  
- Integrates the "catalyst" acceleration framework, achieving consistent speedups over accelerated batch methods.  
- Experimental validation is thorough on synthetic and real datasets, supporting theoretical claims and showing benefits on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Comprehensive related-work positioning, including links to SGD, SVRG, SAGA, accelerated methods, and monotone operator literature.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and clearly written, with detailed results in the appendix for reproducibility.  
- Notational ambiguity: ‘K’ is used both for a smooth function and a design matrix, which could confuse readers.  
- Weakness: brief treatment of adaptive updating of the reference iterate in the acceleration section; needs clearer practical strategy.  
- Weakness: unclear whether the SVRG acceleration proof extends to SAGA; requires clarification or separate proof.  
- Strengths summarized: novelty, theoretical rigor, practical relevance, comprehensive evaluation, and clarity.  
- Minor weaknesses do not undermine the contribution; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
