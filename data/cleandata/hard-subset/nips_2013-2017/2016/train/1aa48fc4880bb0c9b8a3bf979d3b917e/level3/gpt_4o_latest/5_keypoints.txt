- Introduces first linearly convergent variance-reduced algorithms (SVRG/SAGA) for convex-concave saddle-point problems.
- Novel proof technique: interprets saddle-point problems as zeros of monotone operators to prove convergence and extend to variational inequalities.
- Extends methods to handle non-separable loss functions and regularizers by formulating them as saddle-point problems.
- Incorporates non-uniform sampling to improve theoretical guarantees and empirical performance.
- Simplifies proofs for existing algorithms (e.g., forward-backward splitting) and their stochastic variants.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and broadens applicability beyond traditional convex minimization settings.
- Experimental results convincingly show superiority over existing approaches.
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and clear in exposition.
- Weakness: proofs could use a sketch or more details to make monotone operator analysis more accessible.
- Weakness: the role and general applicability of non-uniform sampling in non-bilinear problems is not fully clarified.
- Weakness: experiments lack sufficient discussion of step-size settings and practical implications of non-uniform sampling.
- Minor issues: typographical errors on lines 78 and 121 need correction.
- Arguments for acceptance: fills a significant gap, offers novel and rigorous theory with broad impact, and strong empirical validation.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient explanation of proof techniques and non-uniform sampling, plus underexplored experimental details.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and experimental detail.
