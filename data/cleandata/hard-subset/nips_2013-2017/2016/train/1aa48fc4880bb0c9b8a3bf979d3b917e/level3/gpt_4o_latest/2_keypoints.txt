- Extends stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG, SAGA) to convex-concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides first convergence analysis for these variance-reduced methods in this context, showing linear rates.  
- Shows acceleration via an extended catalyst framework, improving convergence over existing batch and stochastic algorithms.  
- Generalizes the methods to monotone operators, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and clearly presented for readers familiar with optimization and monotone operator theory.  
- Numerical experiments validate theoretical findings but are limited in scope and diversity.  
- Relies on strong convexity-concavity assumptions, which are restrictive and exclude many practical problems.  
- Lacks extensive empirical validation on diverse or real-world datasets to strengthen practical claims.  
- Needs more detailed comparisons with related work, especially practical performance on benchmarks.  
- Omits discussion of practical implementation challenges (e.g., SAGA memory costs, non-uniform sampling overhead).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing assumptions, experiments, and implementation details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretical contributions, provable acceleration, and extension to monotone operators.  
- Arguments against acceptance: overly restrictive assumptions and limited empirical evaluation.
