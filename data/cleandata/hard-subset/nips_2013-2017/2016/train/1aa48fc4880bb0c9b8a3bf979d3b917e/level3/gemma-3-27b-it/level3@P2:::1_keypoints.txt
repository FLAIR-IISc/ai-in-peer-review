- Presents SVRG and SAGA extensions to convex-concave saddle-point problems with convergence analysis via monotone operator theory and an acceleration technique.  
- Core idea: adapt SVRG/SAGA to min-max structure using careful objective splitting and non-uniform sampling.  
- Analysis uses monotone operator framework to obtain linear convergence and apply to variational inequalities.  
- Proposes a catalyst-based acceleration scheme improving performance, especially for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Situates work within prior stochastic optimization literature (SAG, SVRG, SAGA) and convex analysis, highlighting novelty.  
- Strength — Theoretical soundness: rigorous convergence proofs and extension beyond standard saddle-point settings.  
- Strength — Novelty: first convergence-guaranteed application of variance reduction to saddle-point problems with analysis of splitting and sampling.  
- Strength — Practical relevance: addresses frequently occurring ML saddle-point structures.  
- Strength — Acceleration: practical performance gains via catalyst extension.  
- Weakness — Clarity: presentation sometimes unclear, with delayed/missing motivation for monotone operator approach.  
- Weakness — Limited experiments: only two datasets tested; needs broader benchmarks and comparisons to methods like stochastic Chambolle–Pock.  
- Weakness — Assumptions (A)-(C): require clearer statement and discussion of practicality (e.g., applicability to SVMs and possible relaxations).  
- Weakness — Comparison to primal-dual methods: distinction and trade-offs versus stochastic primal-dual algorithms are inadequately detailed.  
- Weakness — Notational issues: overloading 'L' for condition number and Lipschitz constant causes confusion.  
- Weakness — Missing citation: Forward–Backward algorithm referenced without initial citation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, theoretically sound contribution with broadened applicability and promising empirical support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs improved presentation, expanded experiments, and deeper discussion of assumptions and related-method comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with substantial theoretical contributions; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, experiments, and assumption explanations.
