1. Novel extension of SVRG/SAGA and Catalyst to strongly convex–concave saddle‑point problems using monotone operator theory.  
2. Broadens applicability to variational inequalities and ML problems with non‑separable losses or regularizers.  
3. Technically non‑trivial adaptation and convergence analysis beyond standard convex minimization.  
4. Provides comprehensive complexity bounds for individual vs factored splits and uniform vs non‑uniform sampling.  
5. Empirical results show non‑uniform sampling and acceleration can outperform batch algorithms on ill‑conditioned problems.  
6. Results for bilinear saddle‑point problems align with known convex minimization insights.  
7. Major practical limitation: O(n + d) proximal operator cost per iteration can negate theoretical gains when n ≫ d.  
8. Accelerated SVRG with non‑uniform sampling does not consistently beat batch methods in all settings, especially when n ≫ d.  
9. Factored splits used in simulations may yield worse complexity than individual splits, limiting generalizability.  
10. Theoretical claims need clearer articulation: dependence on n and d and conditions under which accelerated SVRG outperforms batch methods are unclear.  
11. Recommendation: accept if authors address theoretical tightness and clarify practical regimes favoring their methods; overall a valuable contribution likely to spur further research.
