- Introduces stochastic variance reduction (SVRG/SAGA) for convex-concave saddle-point problems, with convergence analysis.
- Extends algorithms via monotone operator theory, broadening applicability beyond standard convex minimization.
- Demonstrates acceleration using the catalyst framework, showing improved performance over accelerated batch methods.
- Highlights importance and benefits of non-uniform sampling for efficiency, supported theoretically and empirically.
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and general, moving beyond minimization-only frameworks.
- Experimental validation supports claims but is limited in scope (two datasets, specific setup).
- Novel contribution enabling large-scale optimization for common ML saddle-point structures.
- Clear and well-written presentation that explains technical details accessibly.
- Assumes strong convexity on both primal and dual, which is overly restrictive for many applications.
- Limited empirical comparisons — needs broader datasets and comparisons with specialized saddle-point solvers.
- Sensitivity and practical tuning of the catalyst parameter (τ) are under-discussed.
- Lacks implementation details (software, parameter settings), hindering reproducibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: relevant problem, solid theory, promising empirical results, and generality via monotone operators.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive strong convexity assumption, limited experiments, and missing implementation details.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant theoretical and practical contributions despite noted weaknesses.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to discuss relaxed convexity assumptions, expand experiments, detail catalyst tuning, and add implementation specifics.
