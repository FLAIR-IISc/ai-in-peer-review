- Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees under suitable conditions.  
- Provides first convergence analysis of SVRG/SAGA for saddle-point problems using monotone operator theory.  
- Analyzes different splitting strategies (factored vs. individual) and sampling schemes, highlighting the value of non-uniform sampling.  
- Proposes an acceleration technique based on the catalyst framework for saddle-point variance-reduced methods.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-structured, and clearly written with a thorough related-work review.  
- Novelty: applying SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point problems with rigorous analysis is a notable contribution.  
- Theoretical soundness: monotone operator framework broadens applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Practical relevance: methods target common ML saddle-point structures, making them useful in practice.  
- Comprehensive analysis: careful study of splits and sampling impacts.  
- Experimental validation: results show benefits of non-uniform sampling and acceleration.  
- Weakness: O(n + d) proximal operator cost per iteration is a significant practical bottleneck that may negate incremental gains.  
- Weakness: theoretical comparison between factored and individual splits is unclear, especially when n >> d.  
- Weakness: conditions under which accelerated SVRG outperforms accelerated batch FB are not precisely characterized.  
- Weakness: tightness of complexity bounds (dependence on n and d) is not rigorously justified.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant extension of optimization techniques, rigorous analysis, strong experiments, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: practical proximal cost, insufficient split/acceleration characterization, and unresolved complexity-tightness questions.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with valuable contributions; concerns do not outweigh merits.  
- Score summary: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 7/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
