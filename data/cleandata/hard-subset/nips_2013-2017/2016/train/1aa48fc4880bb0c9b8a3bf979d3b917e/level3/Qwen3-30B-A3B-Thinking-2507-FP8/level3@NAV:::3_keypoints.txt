- Extends SVRG, SAGA, and Catalyst-accelerated methods to strongly convex–concave finite-sum saddle-point problems with individual and factored splits.  
- Provides thorough complexity analysis for different splits and for uniform vs. non-uniform sampling.  
- Experiments show non-uniform sampling and acceleration are critical to outperform batch algorithms.  
- Theoretical extension is technically non-trivial with clear connections to convex minimization intuition for bilinear saddle points.  
- Analysis is extended to monotone operators, increasing applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Original and significant: first linearly convergent incremental gradient algorithms for saddle-point problems in ML, useful for non-separable losses/regularizers.  
- Experimental validation is strong, particularly for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Major quality concern: computational bottleneck — each incremental iteration requires O(n + d) to compute the proximal operator, which can make overall complexity worse than accelerated batch FB when n >> d.  
- The claim that accelerated SVRG always outperforms batch FB is questionable without resolving the proximal-operator cost.  
- Reviewer requested deeper discussion on (i) factored vs. individual split theory, (ii) precise conditions where accelerated SVRG beats batch FB, and (iii) tightness of n,d dependence; rebuttal helped but issues remain.  
- Well-written and well-organized with strong links to prior convex optimization work.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the computational/proximal-operator concerns.
