- Proposes multinomial dropout that samples features/neurons with feature-specific probabilities.
- Provides rigorous risk-bound analysis for shallow learning showing distribution-dependent dropout yields faster convergence and smaller generalization error.
- Introduces evolutional dropout for deep nets that adapts sampling probabilities to evolving layer output distributions.
- Paper is well-written with clear methodology and results.
- Experiments on several benchmarks show improved convergence speed and lower test error versus standard dropout.
- Strength: adaptation to evolving layer output distributions.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis for the shallow case.
- Strength: empirical validation across multiple datasets.
- Weakness: unclear how to adaptively update the iterate point for acceleration.
- Weakness: no convergence or acceleration proof for evolutional dropout.
- Weakness: limited comparison with batch normalization (only one dataset).
- Overall: significant contribution improving convergence and test error, supported by theory and experiments.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
