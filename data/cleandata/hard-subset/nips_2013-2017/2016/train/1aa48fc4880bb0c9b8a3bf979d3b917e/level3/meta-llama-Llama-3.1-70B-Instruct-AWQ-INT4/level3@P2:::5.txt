This paper proposes the first linearly convergent incremental gradient algorithms for saddle-point problems, leveraging stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) to tackle convex-concave saddle-point problems. The authors extend the monotone-operator framework to provide a convergence proof, which generalizes to a wider class of problems, including variational inequality problems.

The paper's main contribution is the convergence analysis for these algorithms, which differs significantly from the associated convex minimization setup. By interpreting saddle-point problems as finding the zeros of a monotone operator, the authors provide a novel proof technique that is broadly applicable. This approach enables the handling of non-separable loss functions and regularizers through the saddle-point formulation.

The authors claim that their algorithms are the first to achieve linear convergence for saddle-point problems, which is an important advance in machine learning. The paper meaningfully extends the field and presents an interesting analysis approach. However, since the main novelty is the proof technique, it would be beneficial to include more proof details or a proof sketch in the main paper.

The paper is mostly clear and well-written, but there are some clarity issues that need to be addressed. For instance, the authors should provide more details on the non-uniform sampling used beyond bilinear problems, including the specific form of the probability vector π. Additionally, the authors should explain how the step size σ was chosen in the experiments and provide intuition on why non-uniform sampling is more crucial for saddle-point problems than convex minimization.

The paper's organization is logical, and the authors provide a clear summary of the main ideas and their relation to previous work. The strengths and weaknesses of the paper are well-discussed, and the authors provide a list of arguments pro and con acceptance. However, some minor typos need to be corrected, such as the mistake on line 78 (k -> d) and the extra y_{t-1} term in the second equality on line 121.

The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work. The paper is a complete piece of work, and the authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce their results.

The organization is logical, but some sections could be reorganized for better flow.
