- Extends variance reduction (SVRG, SAGA) to saddle-point problems, filling a gap where prior methods targeted convex minimization only.  
- Uses monotone operator theory to provide a rigorous theoretical framework tailored to saddle-point structures.  
- Proves linear convergence for the proposed variance-reduced methods in the saddle-point setting (with sophisticated analysis different from standard convex tools).  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits (element-wise and factored) specifically designed for non-separable saddle-point problems.  
- Demonstrates empirical superiority—especially for ill-conditioned problems—and shows non-uniform sampling beats uniform and batch methods.  
- Applies broadly to problems like robust optimization, structured output prediction, non-separable regularizers, and variational inequalities/game-theory settings.  
- Provides the first large-scale, linearly convergent algorithms for non-separable saddle-point formulations, with practical acceleration gains.  
- Experimental evidence is comprehensive (main text and appendix) and supports theoretical claims.  
- Minor concern: the adaptive update of the reference iterate (τ) in the acceleration section is not clearly explained for general problems.  
- Minor concern: unclear whether the SVRG convergence proof (Theorem 1) fully extends to SAGA (Theorem 2); no detailed proof provided.  
- Minor issue: notational confusion—K is used for both the smooth function and the design matrix.  
- Overall judgment: paper is original, technically strong, practically relevant, and recommended for acceptance.
