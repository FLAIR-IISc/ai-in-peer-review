- Proposes novel extensions of SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems.  
- Provides thorough analysis of convergence rates and running-time complexities for the proposed algorithms.  
- Demonstrates both theoretical and empirical effectiveness of variance-reduction methods on non-separable saddle-point problems.  
- Introduces two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split for SVRG and factored split for SAGA.  
- Considers non-uniform sampling and shows it outperforms uniform sampling in theory and experiments.  
- Experimental results support theoretical claims and show practical effectiveness.  
- Unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) to obtain acceleration speed-ups.  
- Unclear whether the SVRG convergence and acceleration proofs extend to SAGA.  
- Notational confusion: the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should use distinct symbols.  
- Significant contribution with potential impact on machine learning, game theory, and optimization.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and of high quality overall.
