- Paper addresses convex-concave saddle-point problems using stochastic variance-reduced methods (SVRG/SAGA) with proximal operators.
- Extends variance-reduced methods to non-separable functions, relevant for structured prediction and robust optimization.
- Introduces an accelerated variant and non-uniform sampling; proves linear convergence in the monotone operator setting and extends to variational inequalities.
- Theoretical contribution: provides rigorous convergence guarantees, including linear rates, via monotone operator theory.
- Novelty: significant advance by handling non-separable objectives and adding non-uniform sampling and acceleration.
- Practical relevance: methods suited for large-scale ML and demonstrated on binary classification with non-separable losses/regularizers.
- Weakness: connection to monotone operators is not clearly articulated; Assumptions A–C need clearer, more accessible presentation.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation limited to two datasets and lacks comparisons to other accelerated methods (e.g., stochastic Chambolle–Pock).
- Weakness: notational inconsistencies for constant L (condition number vs Lipschitz) and missing Forward–Backward algorithm references.
- Argument for acceptance: fills a literature gap with novel theory and linear convergence for non-separable saddle-point problems.
- Argument against acceptance: presentation clarity and limited experiments/comparisons undermine practical assessment.
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance subject to clearer exposition and expanded experimental comparisons.
