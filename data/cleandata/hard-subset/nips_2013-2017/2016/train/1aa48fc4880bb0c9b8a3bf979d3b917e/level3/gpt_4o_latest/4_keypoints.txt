- Main contribution: Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees and empirical support.  
- Theoretical contribution: First convergence analysis of variance-reduction methods for saddle-point problems using monotone operator theory.  
- Algorithmic innovation: Introduces gradient operator splits (element-wise for SVRG, factored for SAGA) and non-uniform sampling, shown to outperform uniform sampling.  
- Generality: Methods apply to a broad class of problems, including variational inequalities, game theory, robust optimization, and structured prediction.  
- Empirical validation: Real-world experiments demonstrate effectiveness, with accelerated SVRG providing strong gains on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Clarity of analysis: Detailed proofs and discussions, especially leveraging monotone operators, support the convergence claims.  
- Weakness — adaptive updates: Adaptive update for acceleration (Section 5) is not well explained, creating implementation ambiguity.  
- Weakness — SAGA acceleration: No formal proof that the acceleration framework for SVRG extends to SAGA, leaving a theoretical gap.  
- Weakness — notation: Reusing K(.) for both the smooth function and the design matrix is confusing and should be fixed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Solves a challenging, important problem with novel theoretical advances and strong empirical validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Lack of clarity on adaptive updates may hurt reproducibility; missing SAGA acceleration proof limits completeness.  
- Suggestions: Clarify the adaptive update (pseudocode/examples), provide a formal proof or discussion for SAGA acceleration, and use distinct symbols for function vs. design matrix.  
- Conclusion/recommendation: High-quality, impactful work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity and theoretical completeness.
