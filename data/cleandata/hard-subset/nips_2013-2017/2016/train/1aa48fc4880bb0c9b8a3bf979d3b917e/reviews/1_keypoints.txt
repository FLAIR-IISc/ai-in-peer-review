- Paper studies convex-concave saddle-point problems using SVRG/SAGA with proximal operators, proving linear convergence in a monotone operator setting and proposing acceleration and non-uniform sampling.  
- Contributions are significant and theoretical analysis reveals new insights, notably applicability to non-separable functions.  
- Presentation is weak; connection to monotone operators is unclear and appears late as an extension despite underpinning the analysis.  
- Experiments are limited (only two datasets); more datasets and comparisons are needed.  
- Missing comparison to accelerated stochastic Chambolle–Pock-type methods (add Zhang & Xiao 2015 JMLR reference).  
- Clarify strength and applicability of assumptions A–C, especially (A); question whether they hold for SVM-induced saddle-point problems.  
- Clarify differences between standard stochastic primal–dual methods and the authors’ proposed method.  
- In Theorem 2, the constant μ is unclear—confirm if it is the monotonicity constant from the appendix and explain comparison to Theorem 1.  
- Transition from assumptions (A)–(C) to strong monotonicity (in appendix) is confusing; state whether A–C imply monotonicity.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent use of L (condition number vs Lipschitz constant) and missing reference for the Forward–Backward algorithm.
