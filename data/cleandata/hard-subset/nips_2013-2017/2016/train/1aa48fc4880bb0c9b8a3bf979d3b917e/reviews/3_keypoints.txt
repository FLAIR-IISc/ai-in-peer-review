- Extends SVRG, SAGA, and Catalyst-accelerated variants to strongly convex–concave finite-sum saddle point problems with two split types (individual and factored).  
- Thorough complexity analysis under different splits and uniform/non-uniform sampling strategies.  
- Experiments on a binary classification example show nonuniform sampling and acceleration are critical to beat batch algorithms.  
- The extension is novel and technically non-trivial; clear comparisons for bilinear saddle points that align with convex minimization intuition.  
- Major concern: each incremental iteration incurs an O(n + d) cost to compute the proximal operator, creating a fundamental bottleneck.  
- Consequently, overall complexity (even accelerated SVRG with nonuniform sampling) may not improve over accelerated batch FB, and factored splits can be worse when n >> d.  
- Reviewer requests deeper discussion addressing: (i) whether factored splits are theoretically worse than individual splits, (ii) when accelerated SVRG outperforms batch FB, and (iii) whether the n, d dependence is tight.  
- Post-rebuttal: reviewer is satisfied with the authors’ response but leaves scores unchanged.
