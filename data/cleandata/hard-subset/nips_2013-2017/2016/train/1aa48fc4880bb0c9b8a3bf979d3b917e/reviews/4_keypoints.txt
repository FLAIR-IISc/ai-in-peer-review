- Extends SVRG and SAGA to strongly convex–concave saddle-point problems with proven linear convergence.  
- Provides both theoretical and empirical evidence of the variance-reduction methods’ effectiveness.  
- Algorithms handle non-separable saddle-point problems where loss and regularizer can be non-separable.  
- Convergence analysis uses monotone-operator framework, which is more complex than standard convex analyses.  
- Analysis indicates applicability to a wider class of problems, including variational inequalities in game theory.  
- Proposes two gradient-operator splits: element-wise split and factored split for SVRG and SAGA respectively.  
- Applies variance reduction to stochastic forward–backward algorithms common in saddle-point problems.  
- Considers non-uniform sampling; theory and experiments show it outperforms uniform sampling.  
- Experimental results (main text and appendix) support theoretical claims and show practical effectiveness.  
- Minor concern: in acceleration (Section 5) it’s unclear how to adaptively update the reference iterate (x̄, ȳ) to achieve speed-up.  
- Question whether the convergence and acceleration proof given for SVRG also holds for SAGA.  
- Notational issue: the smooth function K(·) and the design matrix K should use distinct symbols to avoid confusion.
