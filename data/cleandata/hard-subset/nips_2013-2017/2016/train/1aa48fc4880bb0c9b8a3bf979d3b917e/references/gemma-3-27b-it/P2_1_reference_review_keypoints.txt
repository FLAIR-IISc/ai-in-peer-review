- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems with first convergence analysis in this setting.  
- Shows saddle-point formulation enables non-uniform sampling for improved efficiency.  
- Proposes catalyst-like acceleration for the stochastic variance reduction methods.  
- Connects saddle-point problems to monotone operator theory, broadening applicability.  
- Empirical evaluation on binary classification demonstrates practical improvements, especially with acceleration.  

- Novel contribution: adapting SVR methods to saddle-points is non-trivial and original.  
- Theoretical soundness: rigorous convergence analysis using monotone operator framework and practical parameter guidance.  
- Practical relevance: addresses common ML problems (regularization, robust optimization) and explores sampling strategies.  
- Clarity: well-written, organized, and relates clearly to prior proximal/SVRG/SAGA work.  
- Significance: potential high impact on efficient optimization methods for ML.  

- Assumptions (Lipschitz gradients, computable proximal of M) can be restrictive and may not hold broadly.  
- Accelerated algorithm sensitive to catalyst parameter τ; robustness to tuning is unclear.  
- Experiments limited to binary classification; broader tasks (robust optimization, matrix factorization) are missing.  
- Comparisons to alternative saddle-point solvers and recent methods are not comprehensive.  

- For acceptance: novel, rigorous, empirically validated, and broadly applicable due to monotone operator extension.  
- Against acceptance: restrictive assumptions, limited experiments, and parameter sensitivity need more investigation.  

- Overall: strong paper that meets evaluation criteria and makes a significant contribution.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address assumption robustness, expand experiments, and add more comparisons.
