- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and provides the first convergence analysis for this setting.  
- Considers problems min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with K smooth convex-concave and M nonsmooth but proximable.  
- Analyzes uniform vs. non-uniform sampling strategies and accelerates methods using the catalyst framework.  
- Provides rigorous convergence guarantees (Theorems 1 and 2) with careful theoretical analysis.  
- Extension to monotone operators broadens applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Experiments demonstrate clear improvements over existing methods, particularly on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and self-contained for readers familiar with convex optimization.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: add a summary table comparing algorithm complexities at the end of Section 2.  
- Original contribution: first to bring variance reduction techniques to general saddle-point problems and monotone operator analysis.  
- Significant impact for ML: enables linearly convergent incremental algorithms for non-separable losses, robust optimization, and related problems.  
- Strengths: novel convergence analysis, rigorous proofs, strong empirical results, broad applicability, and well-motivated examples.  
- Weaknesses: requires known strong convexity–concavity constants, limited experimental scope (two datasets), and less-detailed complexity analysis for factored splits.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — high-quality, original, and significant contribution suitable for NIPS.
