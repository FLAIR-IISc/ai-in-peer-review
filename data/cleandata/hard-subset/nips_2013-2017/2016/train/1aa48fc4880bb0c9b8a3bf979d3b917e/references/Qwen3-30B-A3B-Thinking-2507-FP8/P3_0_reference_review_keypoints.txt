- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and provides the first convergence analysis showing linear convergence under appropriate assumptions.
- Reframes saddle-point problems as zeros of monotone operators, highlighting analysis differences from convex minimization.
- Theoretical novelty and rigor: first convergence proofs for variance-reduced methods in the saddle-point setting with careful mathematical treatment.
- Practical relevance: targets non-separable losses/regularizers common in ML and shows empirical advantages, especially for ill-conditioned problems.
- Comprehensive analysis of sampling schemes, showing theoretical and practical benefits of non-uniform sampling and proposing efficient factored splits.
- Extends methods to monotone operators and variational inequalities, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point problems.
- Presents clear experiments that validate theoretical claims and demonstrate superiority over existing batch and stochastic algorithms.
- Relies on strong convexity–concavity assumptions (restrictive); discussion and treatment of weaker settings are limited.
- Experimental scope is narrow (mainly binary classification with specific losses); broader problem types and comparisons to other stochastic saddle-point methods are needed.
- Lacks concrete implementation details for proximal operators of non-separable regularizers that would aid practitioners.
- Comparison with recent related stochastic saddle-point work could be more thorough and some claims may be overstated.
- Complexity analysis for factored splits needs more detail relating matrix dimensions to computational cost.
- Overall assessment: strong, timely theoretical contribution with solid experiments and clear writing.
- Recommendation: Accept.
