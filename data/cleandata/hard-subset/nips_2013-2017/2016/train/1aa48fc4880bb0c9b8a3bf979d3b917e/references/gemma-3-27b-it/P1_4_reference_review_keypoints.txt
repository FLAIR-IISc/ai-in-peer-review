- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and provides the first convergence analysis in this setting.  
- Highlights that saddle-point structure enables advantageous problem splits and that non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency.  
- Shows incremental algorithms can be accelerated via a catalyst-like framework, improving over accelerated batch methods.  
- Connects the methods to monotone operator theory, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Builds on and correctly situates itself within prior work on stochastic gradient and variance reduction methods and forward-backward splitting.  
- Cites relevant literature in convex optimization, saddle-point methods, and sketching, and compares to accelerated batch algorithms.  
- Novel contribution: extending SVR methods to saddle-point problems with convergence proofs.  
- Theoretical rigor: well-developed convergence analysis and solid monotone-operator framing.  
- Practical insight: emphasizes non-uniform sampling and provides acceleration strategies useful for implementations.  
- Provides analysis of different split strategies (function-based vs. partial-derivative-based).  
- Broad applicability: results extend to variational inequalities and other monotone operator problems.  
- Empirically validated: comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real datasets showing improvements, especially for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Clear presentation with logical flow and proofs included in the appendix.  
- Weakness: assumptions (A)-(C) on K and M may be hard to verify in practice and could be restrictive.  
- Weakness: practical parameter tuning (e.g., step-size, catalyst τ) may be challenging and needs more guidance.  
- Weakness: limited insight into why uniform sampling can underperform and limited comparison to recent proximal stochastic methods (e.g., Xiao & Zhang).  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, practical relevance, novelty, clear presentation, and thorough experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: parameter sensitivity and potentially restrictive assumptions limiting practical usability.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution, minor weaknesses, recommended as Strong Accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: 9/10.
