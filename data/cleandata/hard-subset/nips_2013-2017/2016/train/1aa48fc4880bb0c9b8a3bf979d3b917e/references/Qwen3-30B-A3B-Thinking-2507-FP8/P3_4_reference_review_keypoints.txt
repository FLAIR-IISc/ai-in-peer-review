- Extends SVRG and SAGA from convex minimization to saddle-point problems with first convergence analysis in that setting.  
- Shows non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency and uses the catalyst framework to accelerate methods.  
- Extends analysis to monotone operators, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- Provides ML examples where approach is useful (e.g., non-separable losses/regularizers).  
- Significant contribution filling a gap: meaningful extension of variance-reduction methods to saddle-point optimization.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound, using monotonicity to establish linear convergence and connecting to monotone operator theory.  
- Practical relevance demonstrated by well-chosen examples highlighting cases where existing methods fail.  
- Experiments support theoretical claims, showing improvements especially for ill-conditioned problems and convincing primal comparisons for separable cases.  
- Novelty claim is unclear; insufficient distinction from prior monotone-operator work (e.g., [29]).  
- Reliance on strong convexity-concavity is a significant practical limitation that requires better acknowledgment and discussion of relaxations.  
- Experimental scope is limited to a single ML problem; more diverse benchmarks would strengthen claims.  
- Insufficient comparison/explanation of when saddle-point formulation outperforms primal/dual alternatives, especially for separable problems.  
- Overall: well-written, technically solid, valuable contribution but should clarify novelty, address convexity-concavity limitation, and expand experiments.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
