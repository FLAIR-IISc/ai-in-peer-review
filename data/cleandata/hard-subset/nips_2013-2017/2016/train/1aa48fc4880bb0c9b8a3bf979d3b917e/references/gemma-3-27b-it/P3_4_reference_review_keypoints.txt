- Overall: Strong, well-executed extension of SVR techniques to saddle-point problems with rigorous analysis and practical gains; recommend accept.  
- Problem & approach: Adapts SVRG and SAGA to finite-sum stochastic saddle-point problems and frames analysis via monotone operator theory.  
- Novelty: First rigorous extension of SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point/monotone-operator settings.  
- Theoretical contribution: Solid convergence analysis, including effects of splitting strategies and non-uniform sampling.  
- Practical relevance: Addresses common ML saddle-point instances; experiments show clear benefits, especially with acceleration and non-uniform sampling.  
- Relation to prior work: Well-situated among convex/SVR and saddle-point/primal-dual literature; could mention more recent stochastic primal-dual developments.  
- Splitting strategies: Investigates function-wise vs. partial-derivative-wise splits and a factored split for bilinear cases.  
- Experimental evaluation: Detailed comparisons to baselines demonstrating performance gains.  
- Readability: Notation is dense and can be hard to follow; more intuitive explanations needed.  
- Intuition gap: Lacks clear intuition for why some splitting strategies outperform others and for the factored split.  
- Practical considerations: Sparse discussion of implementation details like step-size tuning and numerical stability.  
- Catalyst acceleration: Promising but presented too briefly; needs clearer intuition and exposition.  
- Acceptance pros: Significant theory, rigorous guarantees, compelling experiments, high relevance.  
- Acceptance cons: Heavy notation and limited intuition for key design choices.  
- Final verdict: Accept, with revision suggested to improve readability and clarify intuitions.
