- Proposes stochastic variance-reduced methods (SVRG/SAGA) for composite convex-concave saddle-point problems and more generally for finding zeros of A+B with A resolvent-accessible and B=sum B_i Lipschitz/monotone.
- Extends SVRG/SAGA from finite-sum minimization to saddle-points and monotone operator settings without requiring B_i to be gradients or monotone.
- Provides linear convergence analyses under strong convexity-concavity of M and Lipschitz monotonicity of B, using weighted-norm step sizes that handle differing x/y curvature.
- Introduces a sampling smoothness constant L̄(π) and shows non-uniform sampling (leverage-like probabilities) improves complexity, with bilinear K analyzed via elementwise and factored splits.
- Adapts catalyst/proximal-point acceleration to obtain an outer linear rate and balances cost of backward and forward evaluations via an optimized τ.
- Empirical results on non-separable and separable problems show non-uniform sampling and accelerated SVRG outperform batch FB/accelerated FB; uniform sampling can be worse.
- Builds on SAG/SVRG/SAGA, primal-dual forward–backward splitting, and catalyst ideas, but differs from SPDC/APCG (which need separability) and methods requiring co-coercivity.
- Uses a monotone operator perspective (Rockafellar) to generalize beyond saddle-points to variational inequalities.
- Theory is solid: global linear convergence proofs, explicit step sizes, and insightful bilinear-case bounds using ‖K‖_F, ‖K‖_op, ‖K‖_max.
- Catalyst-style acceleration is cleanly adapted; proximal-point contraction simplifies analysis compared to Nesterov-style acceleration.
- Paper explicitly states needed assumptions (λ,γ for strong convexity-concavity and Lipschitz constants L, L̄) and ties sampling to structure.
- Potential confusion: FB linear rate and stepsize are expressed in terms of L^2; clarifying L’s definition and why L^2 appears would help readers.
- Empirics are relevant but limited: only two datasets, distance-to-optimum metrics, no wall-clock time plots, and missing variance bands.
- Practical details (resampling for SAGA with non-uniform sampling, minibatch sizes, τ selection) are mentioned but not fully detailed.
- Paper is well-organized with clear notation; weighted norm Ω and the role of λ,γ are well explained.
- Suggestions: define constants where used (e.g., μ), make L vs L̄ dependence clearer, include main complexity table in the paper, and sketch key lemmas for linear convergence.
- Originality: novel extension of VR methods to saddle-points/monotone operators without co-coercivity, plus fresh sampling and factored decomposition analyses.
- Significance: broad applicability to non-separable saddle-point problems and potential influence on both theory and practice.
- Pros: general theory without co-coercivity, careful sampling treatment, practical acceleration, clear algorithms, supporting experiments.
- Cons: limited empirical breadth and wall-clock comparison, reliance on known λ,γ,L without adaptive tuning, some notation/constant clarity issues, overhead and robustness of non-uniform sampling not quantified, SAGA resampling practicalities unclear.
- Key questions for authors: clarify μ and L vs L^2 issue; practical choices/adaptation for τ and m; overhead of computing/maintaining non-uniform sampling and wall-clock results; empirical impact of omitting SAGA resampling; comparisons to SPDC/APCG on separable instances.
- Recommendation: Accept (strong) — significant theoretical and algorithmic contribution, with suggested empirical/practical clarifications to strengthen the work.
- Reviewer confidence: High.
