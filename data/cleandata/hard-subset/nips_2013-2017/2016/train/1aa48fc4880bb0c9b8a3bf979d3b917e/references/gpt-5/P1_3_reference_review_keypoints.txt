- Problem studied: finite-sum composite saddle-point problems min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with M strongly convex-concave (prox available) and K smooth, non-separable.  
- Main idea: extend variance-reduced methods (SVRG/SAGA) to saddle-point problems and zeros of monotone operators, achieving linear rates.  
- Analysis: linear convergence of SVRG/SAGA without requiring each component to be a gradient of a scalar function.  
- Operator splits: exploit two natural splits of B=(∂xK, −∂yK), including a factored split tailored to bilinear problems.  
- Sampling: develop non-uniform sampling schemes that improve constants (interpretable via ‖K‖F, ‖K‖op, ‖K‖max).  
- Acceleration: adapt Catalyst/proximal-point wrapper to obtain improved complexities and a method that can dominate accelerated batch methods.  
- Extension: analysis covers strongly monotone inclusions A+B with A maximal monotone (prox) and B a sum of Lipschitz operators, beyond co-coercive settings.  
- Experiments: classification with non-separable loss/regularizer shows practical benefits, especially non-uniform sampling and acceleration.  
- Technical novelty: first linear-rate analyses of SVRG/SAGA for saddle-points and general monotone operators without co-coercivity.  
- Algorithmic contributions: practical SVRG/SAGA variants, mini-batching, factored vs individual splits, and concrete sampling schemes.  
- Complexity accounting: explicit complexity expressions that include prox and operator costs.  
- Scope/impact: monotone-operator viewpoint broadens applicability to variational inequalities and ML problems (robust optimization, AUC surrogates, structured regularizers).  
- Key weakness: requires strong convexity-concavity; no current adaptivity to unknown strong convexity.  
- Step-size/constants concern: depend on L and L̄ (hard to estimate); analysis uses conservative L^2-type constants versus 1/L-type in minimization.  
- SAGA practical/theory gap: theory needs resampling under non-uniform sampling but experiments omit it; storage O(nd) for individual splits, O(n+d) for factored—guidance on trade-offs needed.  
- Experimental limitations: only two datasets and specific setup; missing comparisons to PDHG/Chambolle-Pock, Mirror-Prox/extra-gradient and their stochastic variants; more ablations desired (uniform vs non-uniform, factored vs individual, mini-batch effects).  
- Acceleration limitation: proven only for SVRG; extension to SAGA left as future work.  
- Quality: technically sound and careful; some proofs in appendix—main-text proof sketches would improve readability.  
- Clarity suggestions: clarify L vs L̄, summarize assumptions in a compact table, and illustrate factored split/storage with a schematic.  
- Originality: high—novel extension of variance reduction to non-separable saddle-points and monotone operators; factored split and tailored sampling are original.  
- Significance: high—methods and theory likely influential for research and practice in ML and optimization.  
- Questions for authors: heuristics for estimating L̄/choosing σ; sensitivity to λ,γ mis-specification; comparisons to PDHG/Mirror-Prox; empirical impact of SAGA resampling; progress on accelerating SAGA.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, well-founded extension of variance reduction to saddle-points/monotone operators; strong explicit theory and practical algorithm design.  
- Cons for acceptance: strong convexity requirement, limited experimental baselines, need more practical guidance on constants/resampling, acceleration only for SVRG.  
- Minor comments: add citations/discussion of extra-gradient/Mirror-Prox/Korpelevich, fix notation inconsistencies (L 2 vs L^2), and justify mini-batch size choices in bilinear settings.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept.
