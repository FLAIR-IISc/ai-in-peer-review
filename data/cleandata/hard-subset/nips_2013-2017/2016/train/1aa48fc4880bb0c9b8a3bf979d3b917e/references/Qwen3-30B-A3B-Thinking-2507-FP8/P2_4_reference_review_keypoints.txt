- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and provides the first convergence analysis for these methods in that setting.  
- Tackles problems of form min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with smooth convex-concave K and possibly non-smooth convex-concave M.  
- Analysis relies on monotonicity properties rather than co-coercivity, which is crucial for saddle-point problems.  
- Thorough investigation of split structures (factored vs. individual) and demonstration that non-uniform sampling is critical.  
- Proposes and analyzes an acceleration technique using the catalyst framework adapted to saddle-point problems.  
- Extends results to monotone operators, broadening applicability beyond saddle-point formulations.  
- Theoretical contributions are rigorous, with clear convergence rates, complexity bounds, and sampling strategy effects.  
- Detailed analysis of the bilinear saddle-point case, including comparisons across operator, Frobenius, and max norms.  
- Empirical results support theory: non-uniform sampling and accelerated SVRG outperform batch and uniform variants on real ML tasks.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, with precise assumptions and reproducible appendix proofs.  
- Original contributions include sampling insights, catalyst adaptation for saddle points, and extension to monotone operators.  
- Significant because it provides the first linearly convergent methods for many non-separable ML saddle-point problems.  
- Strengths: novel convergence analysis, comprehensive theory, practical sampling guidance, strong experiments, and broader applicability.  
- Weaknesses: assumes strong convexity-concavity (λ,γ>0), limited comparison to some specialized saddle-point methods, and no treatment for unknown strong-convexity parameters.  
- Recommendation: Accept — technically sound, original, significant, and well-presented.
