- Studies variance-reduced SVRG/SAGA for convex-concave min_x max_y K+M with prox-friendly M and smooth K, proving linear convergence under strong convexity-concavity.  
- Recasts the problem as zeros of a strongly monotone operator plus Lipschitz operators, enabling analysis beyond saddle points to monotone inclusions/variational inequalities.  
- Develops importance sampling and “factored” splits (especially for bilinear K) to tighten complexity bounds and storage/runtime trade-offs.  
- Provides explicit iteration and runtime complexity bounds in terms of L and a sampling-dependent smoothness constant L̄(π).  
- Applies a proximal-point (“catalyst”-style) outer acceleration that provably improves complexities and is simpler than some minimization-only accelerations.  
- Technical contribution: extends SVRG/SAGA without relying on co-coercivity, deriving step sizes and linear rates via the monotone-operator view.  
- Proofs: core theorems are in the appendix with sketches in the main text, but some notation gaps remain (notably µ undefined in Theorem 2).  
- Assumptions (prox access to M, strong convexity-concavity) are standard and clearly stated; Lipschitz constants for Bi and aggregate L may be hard to know in practice.  
- Discusses substituting L̄ for L and trade-offs, but practical guidance on estimating L̄(π) and step-size tuning is limited.  
- Experiments show accelerated SVRG/SAGA outperform batch and stochastic FB and validate benefits of non-uniform sampling, but only on two datasets.  
- Experimental limitations: no comparisons to extragradient/Mirror-Prox/PDHG, limited ablations/sensitivity analyses, and little evaluation on non-bilinear K or broader monotone inclusions.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-organized with interpretable Ω-norming and useful discussion of splits' storage/runtime impacts.  
- Suggested clarity improvements: explicitly define µ when first used, summarize monotone-operator proof ideas in the main text, and give more guidance on estimating L̄(π).  
- Originality: first linear-rate variance-reduced methods for general (non-separable) convex-concave saddle problems and novel factored splits/non-uniform sampling tailored to bilinear structure.  
- Significance: meaningful for ML applications with non-separable prox-friendly structure; monotone-operator perspective broadens applicability beyond ML.  
- Pros: first linear-rate SVRG/SAGA for general saddle problems, thoughtful sampling/split strategies, practical proximal-point acceleration, and solid theoretical-experimental alignment.  
- Cons: modest experimental coverage, step-size dependence on hard-to-estimate constants, potentially prohibitive SAGA storage for individual splits, and minor notational clarity issues.  
- Reviewer suggestions: clarify µ and its relation to λ,γ and the Ω-norm; add Mirror-Prox/PDHG baselines; provide heuristics for step-size selection and robustness to mis-specification; show empirical results on broader monotone inclusions; discuss factored-split existence beyond bilinear cases.  
- Overall recommendation: Strong accept — technically solid, original, and significant, with minor clarifications and stronger experiments suggested.
