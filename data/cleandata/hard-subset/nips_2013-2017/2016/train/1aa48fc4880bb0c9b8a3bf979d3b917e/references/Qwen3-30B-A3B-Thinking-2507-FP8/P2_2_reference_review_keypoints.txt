- Summary: Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems with a convergence analysis based on monotone operator interpretation, highlighting function- vs derivative-based splits and the importance of non-uniform sampling, and shows catalyst acceleration and experiments on non-separable ML problems.  
- Quality: Theoretical analysis is rigorous with standard monotone-operator assumptions, convergence rates derived without co-coercivity, experiments support claims, and limitations are acknowledged.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized with clear notation and reproducible algorithms; however, intuitive explanation for why saddle-point analysis differs from convex minimization and more details on the non-separable loss in experiments are needed.  
- Originality: Novel extension of variance reduction to saddle-point problems with linear convergence guarantees, new distinction between split types, and catalyst acceleration for saddle-points targeting non-separable cases.  
- Significance: Substantial practical and theoretical impact by filling a gap for non-separable saddle-point problems common in ML and extending applicability to monotone operators, with clear empirical benefits for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Strength 1: First convergence analysis of variance reduction methods for saddle-point problems using monotone operator theory.  
- Strength 2: Clear identification of function-based vs derivative-based splits and demonstration that non-uniform sampling is important.  
- Strength 3: Practical demonstrations on machine learning problems with non-separable components.  
- Strength 4: Extension of the catalyst acceleration framework to saddle-point problems.  
- Strength 5: Strong theoretical and experimental validation of the proposed methods.  
- Weakness 1: Requires strong convexity–concavity, limiting applicability and not addressed adaptively in the current work.  
- Weakness 2: Experiments focus on a single ML problem; broader empirical evaluation would strengthen claims.  
- Weakness 3: Computational cost of computing non-uniform sampling probabilities is not discussed.  
- Weakness 4: Connections to recent related saddle-point optimization work are not fully explored.  
- Overall recommendation: Recommend acceptance — strong theoretical contribution with practical relevance and sufficient novelty for NIPS.  
- Final note: Well-written, technically sound, and a meaningful contribution that advances optimization for machine learning.
