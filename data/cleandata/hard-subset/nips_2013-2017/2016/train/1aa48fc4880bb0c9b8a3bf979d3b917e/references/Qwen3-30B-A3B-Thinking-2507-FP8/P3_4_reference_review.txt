

# Peer Review: "Stochastic Variance Reduction Methods for Saddle-Point Problems"

## Summary
This paper extends stochastic variance reduction methods (SVRG and SAGA) from convex minimization to saddle-point problems, providing the first convergence analysis for these algorithms in the saddle-point setting. The authors demonstrate that non-uniform sampling is crucial for efficiency and show how to accelerate these methods using the "catalyst" framework. The paper also extends the analysis to monotone operators, broadening the applicability beyond saddle-point problems. The authors provide examples of machine learning problems where their approach is particularly useful, such as supervised learning with non-separable losses or regularizers.

## Strengths

1. **Significant Contribution:** The paper addresses a genuine gap in the literature by extending stochastic variance reduction methods to saddle-point problems, which is a meaningful step for optimization in machine learning. The authors clearly articulate why existing methods for convex minimization cannot be directly applied to saddle-point problems.

2. **Theoretical Soundness:** The convergence analysis is technically sound and carefully developed. The authors correctly identify that the saddle-point setting requires different analysis than convex minimization, particularly in how they use monotonicity properties to establish linear convergence. The connection to monotone operator theory is particularly elegant.

3. **Practical Relevance:** The examples in Section 2.1 (non-separable losses/regularizers) are well-chosen and demonstrate clear practical relevance. The paper correctly identifies that for non-separable problems, existing methods cannot be applied, making this work particularly valuable.

4. **Experimental Validation:** The experiments support the theoretical claims, showing clear improvements over existing methods, particularly for ill-conditioned problems. The comparison with primal methods on separable problems is especially convincing.

## Weaknesses

1. **Novelty Claim:** The paper claims to be the first to provide convergence analysis for SVRG/SAGA for saddle-point problems, but it's not clear why this wasn't done before. The connection to monotone operators is made, but the paper doesn't sufficiently distinguish their analysis from previous work on monotone operators (e.g., [29] which also considers monotone operators). The authors should better clarify the novelty of their analysis.

2. **Strong Convexity-Concavity Assumption:** The paper assumes strong convexity-concavity, which is a significant limitation for practical applications. While the authors mention future work on adaptivity, this assumption is a major barrier to practical use. The paper should better acknowledge this limitation and discuss potential ways to relax it.

3. **Limited Experimental Scope:** The experiments are limited to a single machine learning problem (binary classification with a non-separable regularizer). More diverse examples would strengthen the paper's claims about practical relevance. The comparison with primal methods is good but could be expanded.

4. **Insufficient Comparison with Alternative Approaches:** The paper doesn't adequately explain why the saddle-point formulation is better than the primal or dual approaches for the same problems, especially for separable cases where existing methods already exist. This is particularly important given the examples provided in Section 2.1.

## Overall Assessment

This is a well-written and technically sound paper that makes a valuable contribution to the literature on optimization methods for machine learning. The extension of stochastic variance reduction to saddle-point problems is meaningful, and the theoretical analysis is solid. The connection to monotone operator theory is a nice theoretical contribution that broadens the applicability.

The paper would be significantly strengthened by better clarifying the novelty of the work, addressing the strong convexity-concavity limitation, and providing more diverse experimental results. However, these are relatively minor issues for a high-quality paper.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The paper makes a clear contribution to the field, the analysis is sound, and the experimental results support the theoretical claims. The identified weaknesses are important but do not undermine the paper's overall quality.

**Pro for Acceptance:**
- Makes a clear and important extension of stochastic variance reduction methods to saddle-point problems
- Provides a sound theoretical analysis with proper convergence guarantees
- Demonstrates practical relevance through well-chosen examples
- Includes clear experimental validation of the theoretical claims

**Con for Acceptance:**
- Strong convexity-concavity assumption limits practical applicability
- Novelty claim is somewhat overstated
- Experiments could be more diverse
- Doesn't sufficiently explain why saddle-point formulation is better than primal/dual approaches for the same problems

**Final Recommendation:** Accept (with minor revisions)
