- Studies finite-sum convex-concave saddle-point problems min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with prox-available M and smooth K.  
- Main contributions: SVRG/SAGA extensions to monotone operator/saddle-point setting with linear convergence under strong convex-concavity.  
- Introduces importance sampling and two decompositions (function-based and factored/operator splits) with corresponding complexity bounds.  
- Proposes a catalyst-style proximal acceleration for improved oracle complexity in the monotone-operator setting.  
- Extends analysis to general monotone inclusions A+B where A is maximal strongly monotone and B is a sum of Lipschitz operators with monotone sum.  
- Positioned against SAG/SVRG/SAGA (separable objectives), batch primal-dual methods (Chambolle-Pock, forward-backward, Nesterov), and SPDC (requires separability).  
- Algorithms do not require component gradients of scalar functions and can exploit operator factorizations (e.g., row/column sampling).  
- Technical approach uses variance-reduced estimates of B with SVRG snapshot or SAGA table and applies prox steps scaled by the weighted norm from λ and γ.  
- Analysis relies on a monotone operator viewpoint rather than co-coercivity, important since B may not be co-coercive in saddle-point problems.  
- Shows linear convergence under strong convex-concavity with rates depending on B’s Lipschitz L and sampling-dependent smoothness L̄(π).  
- Acceleration scheme regularizes around a moving center and yields multiplicative complexity improvements at optimal τ, with batch variant as a special case.  
- Derives L̄(π) for different splits in bilinear problems and advocates importance sampling proportional to squared norms, yielding reasonable bounds (e.g., L̄ ≤ ||K||_F/√(λγ)).  
- Acknowledges forward-backward with general Lipschitz monotone B does not converge in general and restricts to strongly convex-concave settings for linear rates.  
- Notes some complexity claims are asymptotic and hide constants/log factors; “always superior to accelerated batch” needs qualification (depends on τ, inner solve accuracy, prox cost).  
- Non-uniform SAGA analysis requires resampling updated components; practical variant used in experiments lacks theoretical coverage.  
- Paper is dense but organized; operator-norm scaling and decomposition trade-offs are clearly explained.  
- Suggests adding intuition/short sketch of linear convergence of batch FB in main text to aid readers.  
- Recommends clarifying Algorithm 2’s resampling step necessity for non-uniform sampling bounds and providing practical guidance for choosing p, q, and mini-batch size m.  
- Novelty: adapting SVRG/SAGA to non-separable saddle-point/monotone inclusions without co-coercivity and operator-level decompositions is original and useful.  
- Significance: fills an important gap for large-scale non-separable saddle-point problems and likely to influence VR methods for variational inequalities and games.  
- Experiments show benefits of non-uniform sampling and SVRG/SAGA over batch methods, especially on ill-conditioned problems, and handle non-separable prox.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: only two datasets/problem classes, no non-bilinear K tests, no variational inequality tasks, and missing comparisons to extragradient/Mirror-Prox or SPDC.  
- Missing wall-clock time comparisons and memory/time reporting for SAGA’s O(nd) storage in individual splits; factored variant only helps specific models.  
- Pros: clean and general extension of VR methods to saddle points/monotone inclusions, solid linear convergence analysis, simple proximal acceleration, practical sampling/factored-split insights.  
- Cons: narrow experimental evaluation, some overstated claims, prohibitive SAGA storage except in factored settings, requires known strong convexity constants and lacks adaptive schemes.  
- Requests for authors: include extragradient/Mirror-Prox and SPDC comparisons, add wall-clock and memory plots, clarify batch FB linear convergence, discuss leverage-score or other sampling, and address adaptivity to unknown λ, γ.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept, subject to strengthening experiments and tempering a few claims.
