- Extends SVRG and SAGA from convex minimization to saddle-point problems with a novel convergence analysis.  
- Uses monotone operator theory instead of co-coercivity, reflecting key differences from convex minimization.  
- Introduces non-uniform sampling strategies that significantly improve convergence, with specific schemes for bilinear problems.  
- Proposes an accelerated variant using the "catalyst" framework that achieves better complexity than batch methods.  
- Provides rigorous linear convergence proofs for SVRG and SAGA in the saddle-point setting (Theorems 1 and 2).  
- Extends the analysis beyond saddle-point problems to general monotone operators, widening applicability.  
- Experiments on sido and rcv1 with non-separable losses/regularizers show practical speed-ups, especially for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Accelerated method and non-uniform sampling outperform batch methods, uniform sampling, and primal methods in experiments.  
- Paper is technically sound, exceptionally well-written, well-organized, and reproducible (complete proofs in appendix).  
- Clearly positions itself relative to prior work and highlights novelty versus previous bilinear-only analyses.  
- Main limitation: requires knowledge of strong convexity–concavity constants, restricting applicability.  
- Experimental diversity is limited (focused on binary classification), could include other problem types like matrix factorization or robust optimization.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—significant theoretical and practical contribution to stochastic optimization for ML.
