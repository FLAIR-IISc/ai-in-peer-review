- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and provides the first convergence analysis in this setting.  
- Proves linear convergence rates for SVRG and SAGA under clear assumptions (A–C).  
- Shows saddle-point analysis relies on monotonicity properties rather than convexity.  
- Demonstrates the theoretical and practical importance of non-uniform sampling for efficiency.  
- Incorporates acceleration via the "catalyst" framework, yielding notable theoretical and empirical gains.  
- Generalizes methods to monotone operators, extending applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Technical contributions are rigorous with well-structured proofs and reproducible algorithm descriptions.  
- Experiments validate theory on non-separable learning problems, with accelerated SVRG + non-uniform sampling outperforming baselines.  
- Clear presentation, consistent notation, and useful pseudocode make the paper accessible to NIPS audience.  
- Cites prior work appropriately and distinguishes its novel contributions from convex optimization results.  
- Weakness: relies on knowledge of strong convexity–concavity constants (λ, γ) without detailed adaptive strategies.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of the computational cost of computing Lipschitz constants L and L̄(π).  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons could include more specialized saddle-point methods.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, significant contribution with high quality and practical impact — recommend acceptance.
