- Extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems with convergence analysis tailored to the saddle-point setting.  
- Frames the problem as finding zeros of a monotone operator, broadening applicability to variational inequalities.  
- Highlights importance of splitting strategies and non-uniform sampling; introduces a catalyst-based acceleration technique.  
- Novel contribution: adapts stochastic variance reduction methods to the structurally different saddle-point context.  
- Theoretical rigor: provides thorough convergence proofs and insightful operator-based analysis.  
- Practical relevance: addresses ML scenarios like robust optimization, non-separable regularizers, and structured prediction.  
- Detailed and useful analysis of splitting strategies (function-wise, derivative-wise, factored splits).  
- Empirical validation: accelerated SVRG with non-uniform sampling shows strong performance in experiments.  
- Extension to general monotone operators increases the paper’s potential impact.  
- Clear and well-organized writing that makes complex theory accessible.  
- Main weakness: reliance on restrictive assumptions (A)-(C), notably strong convexity–concavity.  
- Insufficient guidance on practical parameter tuning (step-sizes, regularization choices).  
- Experiments are narrowly focused (primarily binary classification); broader benchmarks are needed.  
- Catalyst acceleration: implementation details, practical overhead, and scaling with problem dimensions are under-explained.  
- Lacks comparison and discussion relative to dual ascent methods and SDCA.  
- Open question: can results be extended to weaker conditions like mere convexity–concavity?  
- Open question: practical guidelines for choosing between function-wise and derivative-wise splits based on K’s structure.  
- Open question: sensitivity of non-uniform sampling performance to inaccuracies in probability estimates.  
- Suggestion: empirically justify whether acceleration overhead is worthwhile in practice.  
- Suggestion: include confidence intervals or significance tests in experimental results.  
- Suggestion: strengthen connections to SDCA and related dual methods.  
- Pro acceptance arguments: fills a theoretical gap, rigorous analysis, empirically effective, extends to monotone operators, well-written.  
- Con acceptance arguments: strong assumptions, limited experiments, lacking parameter/implementation detail, missing comparisons to dual methods.  
- Overall: strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to expand experiments, provide parameter/acceleration guidance, and discuss dual-ascent relations.  
- Review confidence: 8/10.
