- Main contribution: First extension and convergence analysis of variance-reduction methods (SAG, SVRG, SAGA) to saddle-point problems.  
- Problem setup: Targets min_x max_y K(x,y)+M(x,y) with smooth convex-concave K and simple proximal M.  
- Theoretical soundness: Rigorous proofs for SVRG and SAGA adapted to saddle-point structure, accounting for scaling with λ and γ.  
- Generality: Analysis extends beyond saddle-points to monotone operators.  
- Novel insights: Identifies two notions of splits (functions vs partial derivatives) unique to saddle-points.  
- Sampling: Shows non-uniform sampling is crucial for theoretical and empirical efficiency.  
- Acceleration: Applies the catalyst framework to achieve accelerated convergence.  
- Experiments: Empirical validation on two real datasets with non-separable losses; methods outperform batch and stochastic baselines, especially on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-written paper with clear pseudocode, consistent notation, helpful tables, and detailed appendix proofs.  
- Strengths summary: Novel first analysis, rigorous theory, practical algorithms, strong empirical results, and broader theoretical impact.  
- Weaknesses summary: No practical guidance for estimating strong convexity/concavity constants (λ, γ); limited experimental diversity; weaker connection to concrete ML applications.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept.  
- Suggested improvements: Add discussion/experiment on estimating λ and γ, move primal-dual gap comparison into main text, and provide more detailed application examples.
