- Extends SVRG and SAGA from convex minimization to saddle-point problems with rigorous convergence analysis.  
- Proves linear convergence under strongly convex-concave and Lipschitz-gradient assumptions.  
- Shows acceleration via the "catalyst" framework and extends analysis to monotone operators.  
- Emphasizes that saddle-point problems require different analysis than convex minimization (zeros of monotone operators vs. minimization).  
- Connects theory to ML applications: non-separable losses, robust optimization, and convex relaxations of unsupervised learning.  
- Comprehensive experiments on real datasets showing significant improvements, especially for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Experimental comparisons are thorough and include relevant baselines and demonstration of non-uniform sampling benefits.  
- Technical analysis is sound and appropriately adapts variance-reduction methods to the saddle-point setting.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and includes clear definitions, pseudocode, and precise theorems.  
- Figures effectively communicate empirical performance gains.  
- Novel contribution: applying SVRG/SAGA and catalyst acceleration to saddle-point problems is new and nontrivial.  
- Extension to monotone operators broadens applicability beyond saddle-point problems.  
- High significance due to prevalence of saddle-point problems in practical ML tasks.  
- Main limitation: reliance on strong convexity-concavity assumptions; non-strongly convex-concave case not explored.  
- Could strengthen connections to existing saddle-point/primal-dual literature (e.g., Arrow-Hurwicz and recent works).  
- Minor suggestion: add more intuition on why analysis differs from convex minimization.  
- Recommend acceptance (NIPS) given technical, experimental, and practical contributions.  
- Additional suggestion: discuss adaptations for weaker assumptions (adaptive step-sizes) and compare to more primal-dual algorithms.
