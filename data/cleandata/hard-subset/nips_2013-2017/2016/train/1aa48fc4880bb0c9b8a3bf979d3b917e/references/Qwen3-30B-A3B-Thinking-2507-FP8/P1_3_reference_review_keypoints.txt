- Summary: Extends SVRG and SAGA to convex-concave saddle-point problems, providing first convergence analysis showing linear rates with appropriate sampling and extensions to monotone operators and catalyst acceleration.

- Strength: Theoretical novelty and rigor — first convergence analysis for variance-reduced methods on saddle-point problems using monotone operator theory.

- Strength: Clear distinction from prior work — highlights different split structures and need for non-uniform sampling in saddle-point vs. convex minimization.

- Strength: Strong practical motivation — identifies real ML settings (non-separable losses/regularizers) where existing stochastic methods fail.

- Strength: Comprehensive experiments — shows proposed methods outperform batch and uniform-sampling variants, especially on ill-conditioned problems.

- Strength: Extension to monotone operators — broadens applicability beyond saddle-point problems.

- Weakness: Lacks practical guidance for choosing non-uniform sampling probabilities πi for general (non-bilinear) saddle-point problems.

- Weakness: Comparison with recent stochastic saddle-point methods is limited and could better contextualize assumptions and guarantees.

- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited to a single non-separable example (ROC surrogate); more diverse problems would strengthen claims.

- Weakness: Acceleration justification is thin — paper doesn’t clearly explain why their catalyst-based approach is preferable to other acceleration techniques (e.g., Nesterov).

- Overall assessment: High-quality, novel, and well-motivated theoretical contribution with solid empirical validation that fills an important gap.

- Recommendation: Accept.

- Suggestion: Provide concrete guidance in Section 2.4 for choosing πi and estimating L̄(π) in practice.

- Suggestion: Expand Section 2.2 to more thoroughly compare and position against recent saddle-point methods (e.g., [29]).

- Suggestion: Add a second non-separable experimental problem in Section 7 to strengthen empirical claims.

- Suggestion: In Section 5, briefly explain why their acceleration avoids Nesterov machinery and why this is advantageous.
