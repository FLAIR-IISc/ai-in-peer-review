This paper presents a novel extension of stochastic variance reduction (SVRG & SAGA) methods to saddle-point problems, a common formulation in machine learning arising from duality. The core idea – adapting these algorithms to handle the min-max structure – is a strong contribution, and the convergence analysis, framed through monotone operator theory, is particularly impressive. The authors demonstrate the applicability beyond standard saddle-point problems to variational inequalities, broadening the impact. The identification of non-uniform sampling as key for efficiency, both theoretically and empirically, is a valuable insight. The acceleration technique, extending the "catalyst" framework, further strengthens the work.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** Applying SVRG/SAGA to saddle-point problems is a genuinely novel contribution. Existing methods often treat the min and max problems separately or rely on batch algorithms, making this a significant step forward.
*   **Theoretical Depth:** The convergence analysis, leveraging monotone operator theory, is rigorous and provides a solid foundation for the proposed algorithms. The extension beyond saddle-points to variational inequalities demonstrates the generality of the approach.
*   **Practical Relevance:** The paper explicitly targets problems prevalent in machine learning (supervised learning with non-separable components, robust optimization, convex relaxation of unsupervised learning), enhancing its practical impact.
*   **Insightful Analysis:** The observations regarding the importance of different splitting strategies (function vs. partial derivatives) and non-uniform sampling are crucial for effective implementation.
*   **Experimental Validation:** The empirical results, though focused on a specific setup, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed methods, particularly the accelerated variant, and compare favorably to existing techniques.
*   **Catalyst Extension:** Applying the catalyst framework provides an additional performance boost, showcasing the authors’ ingenuity.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Assumptions:** The assumptions (A)-(C) are stated clearly, but their verification in real-world scenarios might be challenging. The strong convexity-concavity assumption (A) is particularly restrictive.
*   **Parameter Tuning:** The paper doesn't delve deeply into practical parameter tuning (step sizes, mini-batch size, etc.). While theoretical guidance is provided, more detailed guidelines for real-world applications would be beneficial.
*   **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experiments are somewhat limited in scope. Expanding the evaluation to a wider range of datasets and problem settings would further strengthen the empirical validation.
*   **Complexity of Algorithm:** While theoretically sound, the implementation complexity of the accelerated SVRG/SAGA algorithms might be higher compared to simpler alternatives, potentially hindering adoption.
*   **Primal vs. Dual comparison**: While the authors perform comparisons with the primal form of SAGA, the performance gap might be dependent on the specific dataset and structure of the problem. More in-depth investigation is required to understand where the methods shine.

**Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   **High Novelty:** The core idea is genuinely new and addresses a significant gap in existing optimization methods.
*   **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The analysis is rigorous and provides a solid guarantee of convergence.
*   **Potential Impact:** The method has the potential to significantly improve the efficiency of solving saddle-point problems in various machine learning applications.

**Con:**

*   **Assumptions could be restrictive:** The strong convexity-concavity assumption may limit applicability.
*   **Implementation Complexity**:  More complex compared to simpler methods.
*   **Limited Experiments:** Scope of the experimental evaluation could be broadened.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a significant contribution to the field of optimization. The novelty of the approach, the rigor of the theoretical analysis, and the potential practical impact outweigh the minor weaknesses. While the assumptions and implementation complexity are concerns, they are not deal-breakers, especially given the demonstrated performance gains. The authors successfully extend established techniques to a challenging problem setting, providing valuable insights and opening up new avenues for research.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept**. While some further refinement of the experimental evaluation and a discussion of practical parameter tuning would be beneficial, the overall quality and originality of the work warrant acceptance to NIPS. The paper introduces a fundamentally new perspective on solving saddle-point problems, and its potential impact on the field is substantial. The authors demonstrate a clear understanding of the underlying theory and a commitment to rigorous analysis.
