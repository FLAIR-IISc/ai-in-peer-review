- Paper extends SVRG and SAGA to saddle-point problems and provides the first convergence analysis showing linear rates under strong convexity-concavity.  
- Highlights unique saddle-point splits (functions or partial derivatives), importance of non-uniform sampling, and acceleration via the catalyst framework.  
- Demonstrates theoretical and empirical advantages over batch and existing stochastic methods, with applications to non-separable losses and structured regularizers.  
- Theoretical contribution: first rigorous convergence analysis for SVRG/SAGA in the saddle-point/monotone operator setting.  
- Practical relevance: fills a gap by addressing variance reduction for non-separable losses and regularizers with clear machine-learning applications.  
- Comprehensive analysis: studies uniform vs. non-uniform sampling and shows factored splits can significantly improve convergence bounds.  
- Empirical validation: experiments show clear advantages, especially on ill-conditioned problems, with thorough comparisons.  
- Weakness: relies on strong convexity-concavity assumption, limiting applicability to many practical problems.  
- Weakness: acceleration via catalyst is presented but lacks clarity on how it differs from existing saddle-point accelerations and connection to accelerated forward-backward methods.  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with other standard saddle-point methods (e.g., Chambolle & Pock), weakening broader claims of novelty.  
- Weakness: analysis of factored splits is less rigorous and lacks theoretical justification for practical superiority.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, technically sound contribution that extends variance reduction to saddle-point problems and provides a solid foundation for future work.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant contribution with clear theoretical and empirical merits despite noted limitations.
