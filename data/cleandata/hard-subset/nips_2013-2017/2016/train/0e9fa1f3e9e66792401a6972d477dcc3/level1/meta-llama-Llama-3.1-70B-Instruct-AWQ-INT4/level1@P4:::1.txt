The paper "Measuring the reliability of MCMC inference with bidirectional Monte Carlo" presents a novel approach to evaluating the accuracy of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference algorithms. The authors extend the bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) technique to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples. The proposed method, called Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD), uses annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains to upper bound the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.

The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, including a thorough background on AIS, BDMC, and the challenges of evaluating MCMC inference algorithms. The technical contributions of the paper are well-motivated and clearly explained.
