- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to upper-bound Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and MCMC/ AIS/SMC-derived samples.
- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) methodology from marginal likelihood estimation to posterior inference evaluation.
- Theoretical analysis provides guarantees (for simulated data) and is mathematically sound.
- Builds on prior work in MCMC diagnostics, importance sampling, and marginal likelihood estimation; distinguishes recent related work [CTM16].
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, making the method practically accessible.
- Experimental validation is thorough, showing bound accuracy and usefulness for model representation choices.
- Demonstrates practical utility by discovering a bug in WebPPL.
- Reliance on simulated data limits the rigor of guarantees for real-world problems.
- Sensitivity to the hyperparameter fitting protocol (Section 3.2) is a concern and needs deeper analysis.
- Presentation issues: use of quartiles in figures is driven by outliers and warrants further investigation.
- Potentially valuable for debugging and improving probabilistic programming systems.
- Recommendation: accept — strong, well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and practically useful despite noted limitations.
