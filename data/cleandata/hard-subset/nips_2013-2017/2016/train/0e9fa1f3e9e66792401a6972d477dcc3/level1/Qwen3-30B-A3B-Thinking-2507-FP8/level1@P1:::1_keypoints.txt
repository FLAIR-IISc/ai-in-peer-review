- Main contribution: Introduces BREAD, an extension of BDMC that upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence to evaluate MCMC posterior inference quality.  
- Theoretical soundness: Provides rigorous derivation (Equations 6–12) showing BDMC can bound Jeffreys divergence, with toy-distribution validation (Appendix B).  
- Practical protocol: Proposes applying the method to real datasets by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters (Section 3.2).  
- Implementation and validation: Integrated with WebPPL and Stan and validated on five common models (Section 5.1).  
- Demonstrated utility: Used to compare matrix factorization representations (Section 5.2.1) and to discover a bug in WebPPL (Section 5.2.2).  
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized; background and derivations are clear and reproducible with appropriate figures.  
- Originality: Novelly extends BDMC from marginal likelihood estimation to rigorous MCMC reliability measurement, distinguished from CTM16’s subjective divergence.  
- Significance: Addresses an important need for automatic, rigorous MCMC diagnostics in probabilistic programming with potential wide impact.  
- Strengths: Novel theoretical contribution, rigorous toy and real-model validation, practical application protocol, useful demos, WebPPL/Stan integration, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses: Limited discussion of computational overhead from running forward and reverse AIS chains.  
- Weaknesses (cont.): Reliance on fitted hyperparameters to produce representative posteriors is only validated on five models; needs broader validation.  
- Weaknesses (cont.): Lacks thorough empirical comparison to other MCMC diagnostic methods beyond brief discussion.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept—technically sound, practically useful, and appropriate for NIPS 2016.
