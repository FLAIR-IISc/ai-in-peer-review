- Proposes BREAD, a method using reverse annealing to bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Provides an upper bound on divergence via bidirectional Monte Carlo and demonstrates effectiveness on several models, including matrix factorization.  
- Builds on BDMC (Grosse et al., 2015) by extending stochastic marginal-likelihood sandwiching to bound Jeffreys divergence.  
- Related to concurrent work (Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka, 2016) on unbiased subjective divergence estimators.  
- Technically sound with thorough theoretical analysis and experimental support.  
- Claims are well-supported and authors give an honest assessment of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and uses clear, consistent notation.  
- Presents a novel combination of existing techniques, offering a new perspective on evaluating MCMC accuracy.  
- Addresses an important problem and provides a rigorous, potentially widely applicable evaluation tool for probabilistic inference.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, technical rigor, clarity, and potential to advance inference algorithm design.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited to MCMC algorithms interpretable as importance sampling on extended spaces.  
- Practical limitations: requires exact samples from the target distribution and lacks extensive empirical evaluation across diverse models/datasets.  
- Suggested improvements: broaden experimental evaluation, extend method to other MCMC types, and provide more implementation details or software.
