- Proposes extending Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC posterior inference using Jeffreys divergence bounded via reverse annealing — theoretically sound and practical.  
- Implements method in WebPPL and Stan, providing empirical validation and guidance for model representation choices.  
- Rigorous derivation connecting BDMC to a Jeffreys divergence bound, building on prior work [GGA15, Nea01].  
- Acknowledges limitation that exact bounds require simulated data and proposes a pragmatic hyperparameter-estimation protocol for real data.  
- Empirical results show consistency between real and simulated data and revealed a bug in WebPPL, demonstrating practical utility.  
- Comparison of collapsed vs. uncollapsed representations illustrates how BREAD can guide modeling and algorithm design.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and positions the contribution well, including a fair comparison to [CTM16].  
- Upper bound can overestimate true divergence; paper should explore when and why this happens in more detail.  
- Empirical evaluation is focused on Stan and WebPPL; broader experiments would improve generalizability.  
- Hyperparameter-fitting protocol (Section 3.2) is somewhat hand-wavy; the claim that few MCMC steps suffice to initialize the reverse chain needs stronger justification.  
- Recommend more thorough sensitivity analysis to the number of MCMC steps used for initialization.  
- Figure 1 relies on quartiles due to outliers; the paper should analyze the causes and impact of those outliers.  
- Code for experiments should be released to facilitate reproducibility and further research.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of BDMC, rigorous theory, practical implementations, potential high impact, and reasonable handling of limitations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited generalizability, weakly justified hyperparameter protocol, and insufficient analysis of outliers.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-presented contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing hyperparameter protocol, outlier analysis, and broader applicability.
