- Introduces BREAD, a novel method (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) for evaluating MCMC posterior inference accuracy.  
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the symmetrized KL divergence between true posterior and approximate sample distributions.  
- Demonstrates BREAD by integrating it into WebPPL and Stan and validating on multiple models and datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and motivation.  
- Technical contributions are significant and include a thorough analysis of strengths and limitations.  
- Experiments are well-designed and effectively demonstrate BREAD’s effectiveness.  
- Relates to and builds upon prior work in probabilistic inference and MCMC, with appropriate citations.  
- Strength: novel evaluation method for MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Strength: comprehensive analysis of method including pros and cons.  
- Strength: practical demonstration and integration into two probabilistic programming languages.  
- Weakness: limited to certain MCMC algorithms (e.g., AIS and SMC).  
- Weakness: requires an exact posterior sample, which can be difficult to obtain in practice.  
- Weakness: could use a more detailed discussion of potential applications and implications.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel contribution with strong experiments and clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: methodological limitations (algorithm class and exact-sample requirement) and limited application discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance.
