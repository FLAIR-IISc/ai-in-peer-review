**Summary**

The authors propose a method, called Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD), to evaluate the accuracy of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference algorithms using bidirectional Monte Carlo. The method provides an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of BREAD on various models and datasets, including a matrix factorization model, and show that it can be used to guide the design of inference algorithms.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The authors build upon the bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) method proposed by Grosse et al. (2015), which provides a way to sandwich the log marginal likelihood between stochastic upper and lower bounds. The authors extend this method to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples. The work is also related to the independent work of Cusumano-Towner and Mansinghka (2016), which defines an unbiased estimator for the subjective divergence.
