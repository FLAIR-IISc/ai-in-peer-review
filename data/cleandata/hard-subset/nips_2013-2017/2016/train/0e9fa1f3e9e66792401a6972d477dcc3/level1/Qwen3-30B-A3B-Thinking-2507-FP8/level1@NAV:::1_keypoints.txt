- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate MCMC samples and the true posterior.  
- Builds on Grosse et al. (2015) BDMC marginal likelihood estimator and gives a rigorous upper bound on symmetrized KL in expectation.  
- Requires exact posterior samples, obtained here by simulating data from the model; proposes a protocol for applying this to real-world data via simulation from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Integrates the technique into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical applicability and uncovering a WebPPL bug.  
- Strong theoretical contribution with a clear derivation showing the BDMC estimator upper-bounds Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Thorough experimental validation across multiple models and datasets, examining bound accuracy and protocol validity.  
- Clearly written and well-organized presentation of math and experiments.  
- Appropriately cites and distinguishes related work (e.g., Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka 2016).  
- Original and significant: novel extension of BDMC to evaluate MCMC inference quality and a practical real-world protocol.  
- Addresses a fundamental gap: traditional MCMC diagnostics can’t confirm convergence, whereas this method can rigorously demonstrate it in simulated-data settings.  
- Main limitation: rigorous bounds require simulated data, limiting direct applicability to real observed datasets (mitigated by proposed protocol).  
- Algorithmic limitation: method applies to algorithms expressible as importance sampling over extended state spaces (e.g., AIS, SMC).  
- Practical concern: paper could discuss computational cost of running both forward and reverse AIS chains in more detail.  
- Overall assessment: valuable, general, and practical contribution to evaluating MCMC inference quality; recommendation to accept.
