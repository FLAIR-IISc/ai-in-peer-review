- Introduces BREAD, extending bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC posterior inference by bounding Jeffreys divergence.  
- Key insight: running AIS forward (prior→posterior) and reverse (posterior→prior) yields an expected upper bound on the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Derivation of the upper bound is rigorous and builds correctly on the BDMC framework.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical implementation.  
- Validated on toy distributions (where true divergence is known) and on real-data protocols using fitted hyperparameters for simulation.  
- Demonstrated utility for guiding model representation choices (e.g., collapsed vs. uncollapsed) and for debugging inference implementations.  
- Exceptionally well-written and organized; background and derivations are clear and reproducible, with effective figures.  
- Original contribution by applying BDMC to measure posterior sample quality and providing rigorous guarantees compared to prior work.  
- Significant practical impact for probabilistic programming users by offering an automatic procedure to monitor inference quality.  
- Limitations: requires exact posterior samples (so relies on simulated-data protocol for real problems).  
- Limitations: running both forward and reverse AIS chains can be computationally expensive, potentially prohibitive at scale.  
- Does not deeply explore trade-offs between bound tightness and computational cost.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, practically useful, and recommended for acceptance.
