- Introduces BREAD, extending BDMC to bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence for MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Leverages BDMC’s upper and lower log marginal likelihood bounds to derive a theoretically sound Jeffreys divergence bound.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility and practical utility.  
- Empirically validates BREAD on multiple models and datasets, including consistency checks between real and simulated data.  
- Demonstrates BREAD guiding modeling decisions (e.g., collapsed vs. uncollapsed representations).  
- Discovers a real bug in WebPPL, showcasing BREAD’s use for implementation verification.  
- Builds on related work in convergence diagnostics and approximation error quantification, positioning BREAD as a rigorous alternative.  
- Theoretical derivation is rigorous and clearly connected to prior BDMC/AIS/SMC frameworks.  
- Computational cost is high, posing a barrier to widespread adoption and not deeply addressed in mitigation strategies.  
- Dependence on simulated data and exact posterior samples limits direct applicability to some real-world problems.  
- Proposed protocol for real data (fit hyperparameters then simulate) may introduce biases and needs more discussion.  
- Experiments focus on relatively small models, leaving scalability to high-dimensional problems insufficiently explored.  
- Quartile-based visualization for noisy estimates may obscure information and doesn’t fully explain large outliers.  
- Sensitivity of results to hyperparameter fitting choices is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with solid theory, good implementation, and convincing validation; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss computational cost reduction strategies (e.g., importance sampling variants) and expand on potential biases from hyperparameter fitting.
