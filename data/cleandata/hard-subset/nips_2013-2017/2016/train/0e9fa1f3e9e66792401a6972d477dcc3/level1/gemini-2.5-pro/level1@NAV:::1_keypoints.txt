- Introduces BREAD, a method to quantitatively evaluate MCMC approximate posterior samples via reverse annealing.  
- Builds on BDMC and AIS and shows the gap between forward/reverse AIS bounds upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Requires an exact posterior sample to initialize the reverse chain, so theoretical guarantees hold only for simulated data.  
- Proposes a practical protocol for real data: fit hyperparameters, simulate data, and apply the method heuristically.  
- Implements and demonstrates BREAD in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Strength: addresses a critical need for quantitative MCMC convergence diagnostics beyond heuristics.  
- Strength: novel and elegant connection between BDMC bound gap and Jeffreys divergence.  
- Strength: technically sound, clearly written, with comprehensive experimental validation.  
- Weakness: applicability to real-world data is heuristic rather than rigorously guaranteed.  
- Weakness: computationally intensive due to multiple AIS chains in both directions.  
- Overall: high-quality, influential contribution with a strong recommendation for acceptance.
