- Introduces BREAD, a novel method (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) for evaluating MCMC inference reliability.
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo to bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate sample distribution.
- Applicable to certain MCMC algorithms, specifically annealed importance sampling (AIS) and sequential Monte Carlo (SMC).
- Provides clear, well-written exposition and thorough theoretical analysis, including an expected upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence.
- Demonstrates BREAD’s effectiveness on several models and datasets, including matrix factorization in WebPPL and Stan.
- Offers a practical protocol for diagnosing inference quality on real-world data.
- Strength: provides a rigorous, general method for evaluating MCMC reliability with implications for probabilistic programming languages.
- Limitation: only applicable to specific MCMC methods (AIS, SMC), limiting broader applicability.
- Limitation: assumes the true posterior is known, which is not always realistic in practice.
- Suggestion: paper could benefit from additional experiments on a wider range of models and datasets.
- Originality: builds on bidirectional Monte Carlo but offers a novel extension to bound Jeffreys divergence and a new diagnostic protocol.
- Significance: important contribution to probabilistic inference and evaluation of MCMC methods.
- Recommendation: accept for publication.
