This paper presents a novel method, Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD), for evaluating the reliability of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference algorithms. The authors extend the bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples. The method is applicable to certain MCMC algorithms, such as annealed importance sampling (AIS) and sequential Monte Carlo (SMC).

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method. They also provide a thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of BREAD, including its ability to upper bound the Jeffreys divergence in expectation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of BREAD on several models and datasets, including a matrix factorization model in both WebPPL and Stan.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a rigorous and general method for evaluating the reliability of MCMC inference algorithms. However, there are some limitations to the paper. One of the main limitations is that BREAD is only applicable to certain MCMC algorithms, such as AIS and SMC.
