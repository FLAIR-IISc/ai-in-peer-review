- Proposes AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo (AIS and reverse AIS) to assess MCMC convergence via stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood.  
- Shows the gap between the upper and lower bounds upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate sample distribution, giving a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Method is limited to analysis on synthetic data sampled from the joint, but authors provide experiments showing synthetic-data analyses predict real-data behavior.  
- Demonstrates additional uses for model selection and debugging.  
- Paper is readable, well-motivated, and presents a useful method with reasonably compelling experiments.  
- Section 3 (especially transition into 3.1) is hard to follow; variable/equation definitions are introduced without sufficient motivation.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, this should be explicitly stated since it is a central contribution.  
- “AutoBDMC” is never explicitly defined; the manuscript should state precisely what AutoBDMC denotes (appears to be the use of AIS and rAIS for MCMC analysis).  
- By Section 3.2 the paper refers to challenges “for AutoBDMC” before AutoBDMC is clearly defined.  
- Minor issues: potential typo on Line 149 (p(theta, z | y) vs p(theta, z, y)) and inconsistent notation for data (y vs D).  
- Supplemental WebPPL code needs (a) stated compatible WebPPL versions and integration instructions, and (b) ideally be released as an npm-installable WebPPL package; similarly release the Stan code.
