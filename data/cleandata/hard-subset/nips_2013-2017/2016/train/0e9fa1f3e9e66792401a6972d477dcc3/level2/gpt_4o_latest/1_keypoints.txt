- Proposes BREAD, a novel method extending BDMC to bound Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence for evaluating MCMC-based posterior inference.
- Provides mathematically sound derivation of bounds and validates tightness on toy distributions.
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical applicability to probabilistic programming systems.
- Validates method across multiple experiments, showing consistency between simulated and real-data behavior and robustness to approximate hyperparameter sampling.
- Offers a protocol for applying BREAD to real-world datasets and shows utility for guiding model representation choices and debugging implementations.
- Strength: addresses a critical need for quantitative evaluation of approximate posterior samples in black-box inference.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical foundation combined with empirical validation on simulated data.
- Weakness: requires exact posterior samples for rigor, limiting direct applicability to many real-world datasets; proposed real-data protocol is heuristic.
- Weakness: computationally expensive due to running both forward and reverse AIS chains, posing scalability concerns.
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparison to alternative diagnostics and related methods, leaving relative advantages unclear.
- Weakness: dense presentation that assumes substantial prior knowledge of MCMC/AIS, reducing accessibility.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested improvements to reduce computational overhead, clarify guidance for real-data use, and include more comparisons to related work.
