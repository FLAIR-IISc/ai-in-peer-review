- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between approximate MCMC samples and the true posterior.
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to provide an upper bound in expectation on Jeffreys divergence by running AIS forward and backward.
- Applicable to inference algorithms that can be framed as importance sampling over an extended state space (e.g., AIS, SMC).
- Derives a theoretical connection translating a sandwich bound on log marginal likelihood into a bound on Jeffreys divergence with solid analysis.
- Develops a practical protocol for applying the method to real-world data including hyperparameter estimation and simulation-based validation.
- Implements BREAD in two probabilistic programming languages (WebPPL and Stan) and releases open-source code for practitioners.
- Thorough experimental validation on toy distributions (where exact divergence is known) and multiple real models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization).
- Demonstrates robustness via sensitivity analysis of the hyperparameter sampling procedure and consistent simulated vs. real-data behavior.
- Provides practical insights by comparing collapsed vs. uncollapsed representations and exposing a WebPPL bug through failed bounds.
- Appropriately situates work relative to related literature, including concurrent Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (2016), highlighting distinct contributions.
- Primary limitation: restricted to AIS/SMC-like algorithms and thus does not directly cover all common MCMC methods.
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, original in extending BDMC to Jeffreys divergence, and significant for validating inference in probabilistic programming.
