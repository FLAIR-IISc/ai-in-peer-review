- Introduces BREAD, an extension of BDMC that uses reverse annealing to evaluate MCMC posterior inference quality.  
- Core idea: apply AIS forward and reverse to upper bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true and approximate posteriors.  
- Extends applicability to AIS and SMC-style algorithms common in MCMC practice.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility and practical impact.  
- Proposes a protocol for real data by simulating data from inferred hyperparameters to sidestep the need for exact posteriors.  
- Validates the bound on toy distributions and conducts extensive experiments on WebPPL and Stan models.  
- Demonstrates BREAD's practical utility by identifying and helping debug a bug in WebPPL.  
- Uses quartiles to visualize AIS estimate distributions, appropriately handling outliers.  
- Shows how BREAD can inform model design choices via analysis of a matrix factorization model.  
- Addresses an important gap: provides a mathematically grounded diagnostic for approximate posterior quality versus heuristic checks.  
- Presentation issues: Section 3 has a disjointed flow and Section 3.1 derivation is dense and needs more intuition.  
- Needs more discussion on sensitivity and robustness of the hyperparameter-fitting protocol to model misspecification.  
- Experiments are concentrated on Stan and WebPPL; demonstrating applicability to other frameworks would strengthen generalizability.  
- Related work: connection to CTM16 is discussed but would benefit from a deeper comparative analysis of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Notation inconsistencies throughout the paper should be cleaned up for clarity.  
- Recommendation: strong paper with strengths outweighing weaknesses; minor revision recommended.
