- Proposes BREAD, an extension of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between approximate samples and the true posterior.  
- Provides a sound theoretical derivation showing BDMC gives an upper bound in expectation on the Jeffreys divergence.  
- Validates the bound on toy distributions, demonstrating its accuracy.  
- Proposes a practical protocol for real-world use by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters to evaluate inference quality.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, showing practical integration into probabilistic programming systems.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by detecting an implementation bug in WebPPL via inconsistent reverse AIS estimates.  
- Paper is clear, well-structured, and situates itself correctly with respect to related work (e.g., Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka 2016).  
- Addresses an important problem: lack of reliable metrics for assessing MCMC inference quality in probabilistic programming.  
- Main limitation: applicability is restricted to algorithms like AIS or SMC, not general MCMC.  
- Main limitation: real-world protocol depends on the assumption that simulated data from fitted hyperparameters is representative, and the paper could further explore failure conditions.  
- Overall assessment: novel theoretical contribution with practical implementation and validation; recommendation for acceptance with minor revisions to clarify protocol limitations.
