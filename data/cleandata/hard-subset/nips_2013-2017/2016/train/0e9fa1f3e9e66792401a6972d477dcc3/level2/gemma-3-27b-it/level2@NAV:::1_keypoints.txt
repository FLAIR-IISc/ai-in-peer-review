- Introduces BREAD, a method to upper-bound the Jeffreys divergence between true and MCMC approximate posteriors, extending BDMC to AIS/SMC.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and builds rigorously on prior BDMC work [GGA15].  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical implementation and accessibility.  
- Validation experiments show consistency between results on real and simulated data.  
- Demonstrates utility for informing model representation choices and uncovered a bug in WebPPL.  
- Well-motivated need for quantitative MCMC diagnostics beyond traditional convergence checks.  
- Major limitation: method relies on generating exact posterior samples for simulated data, which is challenging for complex models.  
- Extension to real-world data depends on a heuristic hyperparameter-fitting procedure that may be sensitive to priors and optimization.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited in scope; broader exploration across model types and complexities is needed.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, citing novelty, theoretical soundness, practical implementation, and potential impact.  
- Requests authors address limitations: reduce reliance on exact posterior samples, expand experiments, and discuss computational cost.
