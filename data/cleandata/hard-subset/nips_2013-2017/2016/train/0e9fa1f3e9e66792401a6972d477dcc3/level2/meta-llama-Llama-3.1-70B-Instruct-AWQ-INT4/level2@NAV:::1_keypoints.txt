- Proposes extending bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior and MCMC sample distribution.  
- Introduces a protocol to evaluate posterior inference on real-world data by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Implements the method in WebPPL and Stan and validates it on multiple models and datasets.  
- Provides theoretical analysis including derivation of bias of the stochastic lower bound (log ˆR) and an upper bound on Jeffreys divergence.  
- Presents experimental validation on simulated data and demonstrates use for guiding modeling and algorithmic decisions.  
- Method is potentially highly useful for rigorously evaluating MCMC reliability and is made accessible via implementations.  
- Authors demonstrate strong understanding of MCMC and probabilistic programming literature.  
- Contribution is novel: applying BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence is a new application.  
- The work could have significant impact on probabilistic inference practice.  
- Paper is clear and reasonably complete, with theory, experiments, and implementation details.  
- Major limitation: requires an exact posterior sample and is limited to certain types of MCMC algorithms and models.  
- Practical concern: running forward and reverse AIS chains may be computationally expensive.  
- Suggested improvement: explore ways to reduce computational cost (e.g., parallelization or approximations).  
- Pros for acceptance: rigorous method, accessible implementations, potential community impact.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited applicability, need for exact samples, and computational expense.
