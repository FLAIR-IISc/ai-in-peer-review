- Paper extends BDMC to upper bound, in expectation, the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and AIS/SMC approximate samples using forward/reverse AIS on simulated data.
- Key contributions: formalize the E[log R̂ − log R̂rev] ≥ DJ(q||p) connection, derive one-sided KL bounds, propose a fitted-hyperparameter protocol, and provide WebPPL and Stan implementations.
- Builds on BDMC and AIS; complements convergence diagnostics and Stein-discrepancy methods.
- Correctly relates to concurrent “subjective divergence” work and clarifies equivalence when reverse chain starts at the true posterior.
- Theoretical derivation is sound using the AIS extended-state-space view.
- Guarantee holds only in expectation over AIS randomness; no concentration or high-probability finite-sample bounds are provided.
- One-sided KL bounds with auxiliary upper/lower bounds are valid.
- Fitted-hyperparameter simulation protocol is reasonable and empirically supported by checks comparing forward AIS on real vs. simulated data.
- Experiments cover five standard models plus a matrix factorization case study and reveal practical utility (e.g., finding a WebPPL bug).
- Important implementation subtleties: AIS transition kernels must leave each intermediate p_t invariant; adaptive NUTS/HMC must have adaptation disabled during AIS transitions.
- Numerical integrator error, step-size schedules, and Metropolis-correction/reversibility details can affect correctness and need explicit discussion.
- Paper is well written and organized; background and estimator/protocol are clearly presented.
- Recommend reporting means and standard errors for log-weights/gap estimates because the theory concerns expectations.
- More reproducibility detail requested: intermediate temperature schedules, transitions per temperature, and computational budgets.
- Novelty lies in repurposing BDMC’s two-sided log-ML bounds as an expected upper bound on Jeffreys divergence and integrating into PPL toolchains.
- Method is significant as a principled, interpretable diagnostic that can catch real issues and aid model-representation comparisons.
- Limitations: strongest applicability to AIS/SMC samplers and simulated-data scenarios; fitted-hyperparameter approach is heuristic; expectation-only guarantee limits per-run certification.
- Pros: clear theoretical link to DJ, extensions to one-sided KLs, practical protocol, multi-PPL implementations, and useful empirical case studies.
- Cons: lacks finite-sample/high-probability bounds and guidance on number of AIS runs; relies on heuristic reverse-chain initialization for real data; needs explicit AIS/NUTS kernel correctness details; limited exploration of multimodal posteriors and SMC variants.
- Suggested improvements: provide finite-sample uncertainty quantification (means and confidence intervals over multiple runs) and discuss bias/variance trade-offs.
- Suggested improvements: explicitly clarify AIS kernel construction with HMC/NUTS (disable adaptation, Metropolis correction, reversibility) and report any practical deviations.
- Suggested improvements: analyze computational cost vs. bound tightness and give guidelines for temperatures, transitions, and number of chains.
- Suggested improvements: expand experiments to multimodal posteriors and include SMC-based implementations to support broader claims.
- Suggested improvements: discuss using the diagnostic to tune annealing schedules or transition kernels automatically (close the loop).
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance, contingent on addressing kernel correctness details and uncertainty quantification.
