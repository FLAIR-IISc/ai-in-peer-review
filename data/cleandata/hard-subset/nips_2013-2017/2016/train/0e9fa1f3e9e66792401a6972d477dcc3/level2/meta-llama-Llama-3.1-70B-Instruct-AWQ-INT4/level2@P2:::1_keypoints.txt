- Introduces a method to measure MCMC inference reliability using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC).
- Extends BDMC to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.
- Paper is well-written with clear background and related work explanations.
- Technical contributions are significant and clearly explained.
- Method is integrated into WebPPL and Stan for practical use.
- Validated on several models and datasets, showing effectiveness in practice.
- Provides a rigorous, general approach for monitoring posterior inference quality.
- Results give insights into model representation choices and guide inference algorithm design.
- Experiments are well-designed and show consistency between real and simulated data.
- Discusses limitations, notably the need for an exact sample from the target distribution.
- Notes the upper bound can overestimate the true Jeffreys divergence in some cases.
- Authors provide a protocol to apply the method on real-world data despite limitations.
- Overall recommendation: accept; paper makes a significant contribution to probabilistic inference.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, rigorous/general method, demonstrated effectiveness.
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on exact target sample, potential overestimation of divergence.
