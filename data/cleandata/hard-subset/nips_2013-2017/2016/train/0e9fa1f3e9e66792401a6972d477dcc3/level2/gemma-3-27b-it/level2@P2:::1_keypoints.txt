- Introduces BREAD: extends BDMC to upper bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples via forward and reverse AIS/SMC chains.  
- Core idea: sandwich the log marginal likelihood using reverse annealing to derive a divergence bound.  
- Practical contributions: integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and demonstrates use for guiding model representation.  
- Well-motivated: addresses hard problem of evaluating MCMC reliability and fits needs of probabilistic programming.  
- Technical soundness: derivation appears rigorous and builds cleanly on BDMC foundations.  
- Novelty: new application of BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence and a pragmatic protocol for real-data use.  
- Clear presentation: background, method, and experiments are organized and readable.  
- Debugging utility: can reveal implementation issues through inconsistencies between forward/reverse bounds.  
- Computational cost concern: running AIS chains is expensive and scaling strategies are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Reliance on simulated data: method needs simulated or accurately fitted hyperparameters, and sensitivity to fit quality is underexplored.  
- Limited validation: experiments cover only a few models and don’t test highly multimodal or high-dimensional cases.  
- Missing comparisons: lacks empirical comparison to standard MCMC diagnostics (R-hat, ESS) in accuracy and cost.  
- Assumption limitation: requires exact posterior samples (or strong approximations) for reverse chain initialization, which is often infeasible.  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses critical problem, technically solid, practically applicable, and useful for debugging.  
- Rejection arguments: high cost, dependence on simulated/fit hyperparameters, and limited empirical validation.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address scaling, sensitivity to hyperparameter fitting, and comparisons to existing diagnostics.
