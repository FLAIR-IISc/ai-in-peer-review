- Summary: Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing), using AIS to bound symmetrized KL between true posterior and approximate samples; implemented in WebPPL and Stan and validated on several models/datasets.
- Strength — Technical contribution: Proposes a novel, practical method for assessing MCMC reliability in probabilistic programming.
- Strength — Theoretical foundations: Provides solid derivations, including bias analysis of the stochastic lower bound on log partition function ratios.
- Strength — Empirical validation: Demonstrates effectiveness across multiple models and real-world datasets.
- Strength — Implementation: Integrates the method into WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility.
- Weakness — Limited scope: Method applies mainly to AIS/SMC-style algorithms and may not generalize to other MCMC types.
- Weakness — Assumptions: Relies on knowing the true posterior, which is not always realistic in practice.
- Weakness — Computational cost: Requires running AIS chains forward and reverse, which can be expensive.
- Weakness — Interpretation: Guidance on interpreting KL bounds is limited; implications need clearer exposition.
- Originality: Builds on bidirectional Monte Carlo and AIS but presents a new application for measuring MCMC inference reliability.
- Significance: Has potential to materially impact probabilistic programming by providing a rigorous evaluation tool for posterior accuracy.
- Relationship to prior work: Extends previous bidirectional Monte Carlo and AIS techniques to a new evaluation role.

- Clarity — Organization: Well-structured sections and logical flow.
