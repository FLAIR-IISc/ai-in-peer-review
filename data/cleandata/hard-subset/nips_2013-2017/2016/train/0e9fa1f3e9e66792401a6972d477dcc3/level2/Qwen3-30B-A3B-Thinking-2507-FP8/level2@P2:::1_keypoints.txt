- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to extend BDMC and measure reliability of MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Main theoretical contribution: shows BDMC can upper bound the expected Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Derivation in Section 3.1 rigorously extends BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence with clear mathematical justification.  
- Authors acknowledge related work (e.g., Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka 2016) and clarify differences, especially reverse chain initialization.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, making the method immediately applicable to probabilistic programming systems.  
- Validation on toy examples (Appendix B) shows the upper bound closely matches true Jeffreys divergence in simple cases.  
- Experimental protocol addresses simulated vs. real data, showing inference behavior on simulated data matches real data across five models.  
- Demonstrates that approximate posterior initialization for the reverse chain has negligible impact on the upper bound.  
- Provides practical scientific findings: guides model representation choices (e.g., collapsed vs. uncollapsed matrix factorization) with differing optimal choices for WebPPL vs. Stan.  
- Debugging example illustrates BREAD's practical utility for uncovering implementation bugs.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and especially relevant to probabilistic programming and NIPS audience.  
- Limitations: empirical validation limited to relatively small-scale models, leaving scalability to larger problems unclear.  
- Does not explore sensitivity of results to choice of hyperparameters used for simulation.  
- Lacks a thorough comparison of BREAD's computational cost versus other convergence diagnostics (e.g., Gelman-Rubin).  
- Minor suggestions: quantify computational overhead, discuss impractical scenarios (e.g., when AIS is too expensive), and more explicitly compare guarantees to Gorham & Mackey (2015).  
- Significance: substantial and practically valuable; reviewer recommends acceptance.
