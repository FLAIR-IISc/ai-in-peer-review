This paper introduces a method—dubbed AutoBDMC—for gauging MCMC convergence. The approach leverages the recently introduced Bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to obtain stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood (via annealed importance sampling). The authors argue that the gap between these bounds serves as a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, yielding a single scalar to summarize MCMC convergence. Because the method can only evaluate convergence on synthetic data drawn from the joint model, the authors present experiments indicating that such analyses predict behavior on real datasets. They also showcase applications to model selection and debugging. Overall, the paper is readable and well-motivated; the method appears genuinely useful, and the empirical results are reasonably convincing.

I found Section 3 substantially harder to follow than the rest. The transition into 3.1 is particularly abrupt, jumping into variables and equations without sufficient motivation. If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel (as I believe), this should be stated clearly, since the paper’s contribution seems to hinge on it.

What exactly is AutoBDMC? The paper never explicitly defines it. My understanding is that AutoBDMC is the practice of using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence; if so, please say this plainly. By the time Section 3.2 asserts that “…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC,” the term should already be clearly defined.

Some other minutiae: Line 149: Should p(theta, z | y) be p(theta, z, y)? Notational inconsistencies: the paper alternates between y and D for data/observations, which was occasionally confusing. WebPPL code in the supplement: please include (a) the WebPPL version(s) known to work and (b) instructions for integrating into the WebPPL distribution. Even better would be releasing a WebPPL package installable via npm. If possible, a comparable packaged release for the Stan code would also be appreciated.
