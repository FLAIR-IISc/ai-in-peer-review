This paper proposes the use of bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a particular focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). I appreciate the idea of utilizing bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose failures of a sampler to converge, as this concept seems potentially quite useful given the existing limitations of MCMC diagnostics.

Two highly relevant references, [1,2], also assess the performance of MCMC samplers by running them on data sampled from the prior. These references provide a valuable context for understanding the proposed method.

However, I found the derivation in Section 3.1 to be challenging to follow. Specifically, the notation q(.) is used before its definition. Although the context suggests that q(.) represents the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampler (AIS) after T steps, this should be explicitly stated. While the supplement provides some clarification, the main text should be self-contained, allowing the reader to understand the method without needing to consult the supplement. In general, it would be beneficial to include a clear, step-by-step explanation of how to apply the method from start to finish, as the reader currently needs to piece this together.

Some minor suggestions for improvement include simplifying the notation by eliminating the distinction between unobserved variables z and theta, as they are always used together in this general setting. Additionally, there is a missing close parenthesis on line 165.
