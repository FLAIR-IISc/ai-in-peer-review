The paper primarily synthesizes previously published results on bidirectional Monte Carlo and demonstrates their application in two probabilistic programming languages. The contribution appears to be an implementation of established techniques in WebPPL and Stan, rather than a novel methodological or theoretical advance. I could not identify any original ideas or results beyond what is already known in the literature. While such implementations may be useful to practitioners, the novelty and significance seem limited for the NIPS audience. A more suitable venue might be an MCMC-focused workshop or a software-oriented track where implementation details are the primary focus.
