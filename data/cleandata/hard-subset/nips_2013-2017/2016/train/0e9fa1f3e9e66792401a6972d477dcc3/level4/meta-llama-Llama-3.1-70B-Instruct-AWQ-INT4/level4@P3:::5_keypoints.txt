- Proposal: use Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) upper/lower stochastic bounds on log marginal likelihood to assess MCMC reliability and compare schemes by bound tightness per compute.
- Strength: well-motivated and clearly explains the challenges of assessing MCMC reliability.
- Contribution: novel use of BMC stochastic lower bounds as an MCMC convergence diagnostic.
- Technical quality: paper is technically sound and well-written.
- Major assumption 1: requires a known representative hyperparameter η_real obtained from prior MCMC, which itself assumes convergence and that a single value summarizes the posterior.
- Major assumption 2: assumes synthetic-data posterior from η_real has MCMC convergence properties similar to the original posterior, which is unjustified, especially under model misspecification.
- Major assumption 3: assumes initializing MCMC at η_real leads to rapid near-exact sampling, an optimistic and not generally valid claim.
- Implementation drawback: the above assumptions reduce the practical appeal of the proposed diagnostic.
- Empirical evaluation: examples in Sec. 5.1 are not sufficiently convincing that the identified issues won’t be critical in more complex problems.
- Missing comparisons: lacks simulations comparing the method to standard diagnostics and demonstrating advantages in challenging cases (e.g., multimodal posteriors, weak summary statistics).
- Clarity issues: overall clear writing but several implementation assumptions are not well explained and need more detail.
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential wide applicability, but impact is weakened by poorly justified assumptions.
- Required improvements: provide stronger empirical evidence, clearer justification of assumptions, and comparisons with common diagnostic tools.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel methodology, clear exposition, potential applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: problematic assumptions, implementation drawbacks, and insufficient supporting evidence.
