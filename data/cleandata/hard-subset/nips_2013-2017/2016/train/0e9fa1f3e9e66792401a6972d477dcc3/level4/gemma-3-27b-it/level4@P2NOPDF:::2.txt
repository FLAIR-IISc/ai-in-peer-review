This paper presents an investigation into the theoretical properties of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)-based posterior inference using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC). The authors derive lower bounds on the Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence between the true posterior distribution and the approximate distribution obtained via MCMC. A significant contribution is the practical implementation and evaluation of this analysis within established probabilistic programming systems, WebPPL and Stan. 

Overall, this is a strong and valuable paper. The application of BMC to analyze MCMC convergence and accuracy addresses an important and actively researched problem in Bayesian statistics. The core idea is insightful and offers a novel perspective on understanding the limitations of MCMC methods. By providing theoretical bounds, the work moves beyond purely empirical assessments of MCMC performance. 

The integration with WebPPL and Stan is particularly noteworthy. This demonstrates the practicality of the proposed methodology and allows for concrete assessment on real-world models. While the paper focuses on theoretical analysis, the practical demonstration strengthens the impact and relevance of the findings.

I recommend acceptance of this paper. The work is well-motivated, technically sound, and offers a useful contribution to the field of Bayesian inference.
