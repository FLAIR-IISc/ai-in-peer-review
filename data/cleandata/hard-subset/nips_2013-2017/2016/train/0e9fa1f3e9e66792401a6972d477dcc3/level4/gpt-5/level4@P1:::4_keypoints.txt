- Proposes AutoBDMC: use bidirectional AIS (forward and reverse) to obtain stochastic lower/upper bounds on log marginal likelihood for diagnosing MCMC sample quality.  
- Main theoretical claim: the expected gap between forward and reverse AIS bounds upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between the true posterior and the sampler’s approximate sample distribution.  
- Reverse AIS requires an exact posterior sample, so the method gives rigorous guarantees on simulated data from the joint model.  
- Practical real-data protocol: fit hyperparameters to real data, simulate datasets from the fitted model, and check that AIS behavior on simulated and real data agrees.  
- Implemented and integrated into WebPPL and Stan, with case studies comparing model representations and a real debugging example.  
- Builds directly on prior BDMC and AIS work, and relates to reverse AIS/thermodynamic integration and recent posterior-assessment methods.  
- Strength: clear motivation—diagnosing MCMC reliability in black-box systems is important and challenging.  
- Strength: conceptual simplicity and a single scalar diagnostic that is easy to interpret.  
- Strength: practical relevance and useful case studies demonstrating actionable insights and a real debugging win.  
- Strength: thoughtful and pragmatic real-data validation protocol.  
- Weakness: Section 3 is hard to follow; needs a high-level roadmap and smoother introduction of variables/equations.  
- Weakness: the novelty of the divergence-bound result should be stated explicitly in the introduction and Section 3 if it is new.  
- Weakness: “AutoBDMC” is used without a precise early definition—needs a concise definition before later references.  
- Weakness: inconsistent notation (y vs D) and possible typo (p(θ, z | y) vs p(θ, z, y))—notation should be standardized and checked.  
- Weakness: reproducibility details lacking—specify supported WebPPL versions, provide installation/packaging (npm) and Stan instructions.  
- Empirical evaluation: experiments are well chosen and convincing—validation of simulated-vs-real behavior, sensitivity to hyperparameter-approximation, representation comparisons, and a compelling debugging case.  
- Recommendation: accept after revisions addressing Section 3 clarity, explicit novelty claim, precise terminology, notation consistency, and improved reproducibility packaging.
