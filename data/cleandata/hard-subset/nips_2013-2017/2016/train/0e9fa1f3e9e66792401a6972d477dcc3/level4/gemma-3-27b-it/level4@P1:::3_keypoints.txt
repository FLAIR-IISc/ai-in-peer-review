- Paper proposes using BDMC-derived gap between stochastic lower and upper bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC transition operators.  
- Main technical contributions are BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, easy to implement, potentially high impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main concern: novelty and depth are modest; much of BDMC is rehashed from prior work.  
- Notable theoretical remark: authors show the bound gap upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence, but this observation may not be entirely novel.  
- Missing related work: fails to cite Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related and shares reverse/forward sweeps and acceptance-ratio interpretation.  
- Authors should discuss connections to Tempered Transitions and implications for choosing β_t schedules (Δβ step sizes).  
- Practical concern: Δβ choices strongly affect BDMC bound gap; suggest iteratively decreasing Δβ (AutoBDMC) until bounds are sufficiently tight (e.g., gap < ~1 nat).  
- Experiments are incomplete: no systematic study of how bounds vary with step size or clear description of how β_t is chosen.  
- Clarity issues: loose/ambiguous definition of vector v and inconsistent naming conventions (rev/fwd vs for/back).  
- Math clarity problems: estimator being averaged in eqs. (5-7),(9-11) is not defined; eq. (6) appears incorrect as written (KL arguments identical).  
- AutoBDMC is never unambiguously defined (is it bound-difference checking, hyperparameter fitting, or both?).  
- Experimental details unclear: interpretation of “HMC / No-U-Turn steps”, RAIS and (u)c(R)AIS acronyms/variants are undefined, and figure labels/ticks are too small.  
- Typos/errors: missing closing parentheses in D_JS expression and mis-citation “Homan” should be “Hoffman.”  
- Overall recommendation: method is useful and potentially acceptable if authors fix clarity, related-work discussion, β-schedule/AutoBDMC definition, and expand experiments.
