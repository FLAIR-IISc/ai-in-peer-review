Introduces AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo (AIS + reverse AIS) to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood.  
The gap between these bounds yields an upper bound on the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the MCMC approximate posterior, providing a single scalar convergence metric.  
Method is designed for synthetic data sampled from the model joint, and authors present evidence that synthetic performance predicts real-world performance.  
Demonstrates utility for model selection and debugging with a compelling experimental evaluation.  
Paper is generally well-motivated and clearly written overall.  
Section 3 is unclear and harder to follow than other sections, with an abrupt transition into 3.1 introducing variables/equations without sufficient motivation.  
If the divergence bound in Section 3 is novel, the paper should state this explicitly since it appears to be a core contribution.  
“AutoBDMC” is never precisely defined; the paper should explicitly define the term/framework early on.  
Minor potential typo at Line 149: `p(theta, z | y)` may be intended as `p(theta, z, y)`.  
Inconsistent notation for observed data: both `y` and `D` are used.  
Supplemental WebPPL code lacks versioning and integration instructions; recommend releasing as an installable WebPPL npm package and likewise packaging the Stan implementation.
