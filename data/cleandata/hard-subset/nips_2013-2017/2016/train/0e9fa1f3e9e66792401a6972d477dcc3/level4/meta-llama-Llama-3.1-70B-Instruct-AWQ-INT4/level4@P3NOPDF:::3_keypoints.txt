- Paper proposes using BDMC (two AIS sweeps) to evaluate MCMC transition operators by the gap between stochastic lower and upper bounds on marginal likelihood.  
- Authors implement BDMC in Stan and WebPPL and propose a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- Strength: idea is simple, elegant, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming.  
- Weakness: novelty and depth are limited; much of the paper reiterates existing BDMC/arXiv material.  
- Sections 3.1 and 3.2 feel rushed and lack clarity, weakening the technical contribution.  
- Main novelty (gap gives an upper bound on Jensen–Shannon divergence) is modest and not entirely new.  
- Important related work missing: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions, which performs analogous reverse/forward sweeps.  
- Choice of βt (Δβ) is crucial; too large steps hurt acceptance, too small waste compute, and the BDMC gap depends on Δβ.  
- Practical guideline: comparisons are unreliable if log upper–lower gap > ~1 nat; Δβ should be reduced in such cases.  
- AutoBDMC is underexplained; authors should detail the “auto” procedure and propose an iterative schedule to decrease Δβ.  
- Experiments should include a systematic study of how lower/upper bounds vary with step size (Δβ).  
- Several clarity/notation issues: loose definition of vector v, undefined estimator ĥp(y) in equations (5–7, 9–11), and equation (6) appears incorrect.  
- AutoBDMC must be clearly defined and βt selection procedure should be explicitly stated in experiments.  
- Figures: define RAIS and variants in captions and increase axis/tick label sizes for readability.  
- Minor issues: typos (missing closing parentheses in DKL terms) and a misspelling in the NUTS citation.  
- Overall: method is potentially useful and paper could be acceptable pending substantive clarifications, added references, and stronger experiments.
