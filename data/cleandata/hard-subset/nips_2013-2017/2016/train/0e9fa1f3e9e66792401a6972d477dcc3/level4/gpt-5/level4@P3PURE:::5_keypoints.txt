- Paper tackles assessing MCMC reliability and comparing MCMC schemes using BMC upper/lower stochastic bounds on log marginal likelihood.  
- Core idea: pick the MCMC scheme that gives the tightest BMC stochastic bounds for a fixed computational budget.  
- Main technical challenge: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to obtain a valid stochastic upper bound.  
- Proposed solution: heuristics using a posterior induced by synthetic data (generated from a representative hyperparameter η_real) so approximate exact samples are easier to obtain.  
- Empirical evaluation: method tested on several examples; Section 5.1 shows simple cases where heuristics work.  
- Positive: using BMC-based stochastic lower bounds for MCMC diagnostics is an interesting, theoretically principled direction, especially for AIS/SMC methods.  
- Major drawback: method assumes prior knowledge of a representative hyperparameter η_real, implying an already-converged algorithm and that the posterior is well summarized by a single value.  
- Major drawback: assumes the synthetic-data posterior has similar convergence properties to the original posterior, which may fail under model misspecification.  
- Major drawback: optimistic assumption that initializing at η_real yields an almost exact sample quickly is not generally guaranteed.  
- Because these assumptions relate directly to the convergence question, the overall reliability of the diagnostic is uncertain.  
- Limited demonstrations do not convincingly show the heuristic will work in more complex or realistic settings.  
- Recommendation: authors should explain why their method would handle multimodality or insufficient summary statistics better than existing diagnostics and provide comparative simulations against common diagnostics.
