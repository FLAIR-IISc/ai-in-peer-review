- Task: assess MCMC reliability and compare sampler efficiency using stochastic bounds from Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC).
- Proposal: select MCMC scheme that yields the tightest BMC bounds under a fixed computational budget.
- Core challenge: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound.
- Workaround: authors introduce heuristics, notably running diagnostics on a posterior induced by synthetic data.
- Heuristic 1 issue: method assumes knowledge of a representative hyperparameter value (eta_real), presuming prior MCMC used to find it has already converged.
- Heuristic 2 issue: assumes posterior from synthetic data has convergence properties similar to the original posterior, which is unjustified—especially under model misspecification.
- Heuristic 3 issue: assumes initializing at eta_real quickly produces an almost-exact posterior sample, which is not guaranteed.
- Conceptual critique: the procedure effectively changes the problem by diagnosing a different posterior, tying assumptions to the very convergence being assessed.
- Empirical limitation: examples in Section 5.1 are simple and do not demonstrate robustness in more challenging settings.
- Comparative critique: authors should explain why their method outperforms existing diagnostics in multimodal or hard-to-summarize cases and provide simulation comparisons with standard diagnostics.
