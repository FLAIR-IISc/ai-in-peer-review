This work introduces a method using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target distribution. The stated goal is to aid in the debugging and evaluation of MCMC algorithms, particularly within probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The core idea of applying BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence issues is compelling and potentially very valuable, addressing known limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics. However, the paper should acknowledge two highly relevant prior works, [1] and [2], which also validate MCMC samplers by testing them on data simulated from the prior.

The derivation in Section 3.1 is difficult to follow. For instance, the notation q(.) is used before being defined. While context implies it may be the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after T steps, this needs to be explicitly stated. The main text should be understandable without relying on the supplementary material, which currently offers necessary clarification. A significant improvement would be the inclusion of a clear, step-by-step description of the complete method, as the reader is currently required to piece together the full procedure from various sections.

Minor points: The notational distinction between unobserved variables z and theta adds unnecessary clutter; since they always appear together in this general formulation, one of them could be eliminated to simplify the presentation. Additionally, there is a missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
