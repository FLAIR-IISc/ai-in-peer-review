

This paper introduces a novel application of bidirectional Monte Carlo to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference methods. The authors derive lower bounds on the KL-divergence between the true posterior and the approximate distribution, providing a theoretically grounded framework for assessing approximation quality. The proposed methodology is successfully integrated into both WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical utility. The problem addressed is significant in Bayesian inference, and the use of bidirectional Monte Carlo represents an interesting and timely contribution. The technical approach is well-executed and the implementation enhances the accessibility of the method. I recommend acceptance of this paper.
