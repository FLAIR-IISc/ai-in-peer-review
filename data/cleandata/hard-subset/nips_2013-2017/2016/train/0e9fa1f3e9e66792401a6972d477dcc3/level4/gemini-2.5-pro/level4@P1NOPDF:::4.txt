This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence. The approach leverages Bidirectional Monte Carlo to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood via annealed importance sampling (AIS). The authors' key theoretical contribution is to show that the gap between these bounds also provides an upper bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the approximate sample distribution and the true posterior. This yields a single, interpretable scalar quantity for diagnosing MCMC convergence. As the method requires generating synthetic data from the joint distribution, the authors provide compelling experimental evidence that its performance on synthetic data is predictive of its behavior on real-world data. They further demonstrate its utility for model selection and debugging MCMC implementations.

Overall, the paper is well-motivated and clearly written. The proposed method is demonstrably useful, and the experimental evaluation is compelling. However, there are several areas that could be improved for clarity and impact.

**Major Comments:**

1.  **Clarity of Section 3:** While the paper is generally clear, Section 3 was significantly harder to follow. The transition into Section 3.1 is particularly abrupt, as it introduces new variables and equations without sufficient motivation or context. Providing more introductory text to frame this section would greatly improve readability.

2.  **Explicit Statement of Contribution:** The divergence bound in Section 3.1 appears to be the central theoretical contribution of this work. If this result is indeed novel (as I believe it is), the authors should state this explicitly, as it is fundamental to the paper's contribution.

3.  **Definition of AutoBDMC:** The term "AutoBDMC" is used throughout the paper but is never formally defined. My understanding is that it refers to the overall approach of using AIS and reverse AIS to analyze MCMC convergence, but this should be stated clearly for the reader. The method should be well-defined before it is referenced, for example, in Section 3.2 ("…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC").

**Minor Comments & Suggestions:**

*   **Notational Inconsistency:** The paper alternates between using `y` and `D` to represent the data, which can be confusing at times. Sticking to one convention would improve consistency.
*   **Potential Typo:** On Line 149, should `p(theta, z | y)` instead be the joint distribution `p(theta, z, y)`?
*   **Code and Reproducibility:** The provided supplementary code would be more useful with the following additions:
    *   For the WebPPL code, please specify which version(s) it is compatible with and provide instructions for integration. Releasing it as a WebPPL package (e.g., installable via npm) would be even better.
    *   A similar packaged release for the Stan implementation would also be a welcome addition, improving the accessibility of this useful tool.
