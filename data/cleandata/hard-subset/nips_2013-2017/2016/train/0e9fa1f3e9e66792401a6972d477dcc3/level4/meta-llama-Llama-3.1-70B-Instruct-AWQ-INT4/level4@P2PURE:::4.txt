This paper proposes a novel method, termed AutoBDMC, for assessing the convergence of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithms. The method leverages the Bidirectional Monte Carlo approach to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood, relying on annealed importance sampling. The authors demonstrate that the gap between these bounds serves as a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples, providing a single scalar quantity to evaluate MCMC convergence behavior. Although the method is limited to analyzing convergence on synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors provide empirical evidence suggesting that its analysis on such synthetic data is predictive of its behavior on real-world data. They also showcase the method's utility in model selection and debugging applications.

Overall, I found the paper to be well-motivated and readable, with the presented method appearing to be clearly useful. The experimental evaluation is reasonably compelling. However, I encountered difficulties in following Section 3, particularly the transition into Section 3.1, which abruptly introduces variable and equation definitions without providing sufficient motivation. If the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 is a novel result (as I believe it to be), this should be explicitly stated, as the paper's contribution and novelty appear to hinge on this result. Furthermore, the definition of AutoBDMC is not explicitly stated anywhere in the paper. Based on my understanding, AutoBDMC seems to refer to the approach of using annealed importance sampling (AIS) and reverse AIS (rAIS) to analyze MCMC convergence. I would expect a clear statement to this effect.

Additionally, by the time Section 3.2 mentions the challenges posed to AutoBDMC, the definition of AutoBDMC should be clearly established. Some minor issues include: Line 149, where "p(theta, z | y)" should be "p(theta, z, y)"; notational inconsistencies, as the paper switches between using "y" and "D" to denote data/observations, which I found occasionally confusing; and the supplemental WebPPL code, which should be accompanied by information on compatible WebPPL versions and integration instructions. Ideally, releasing this code as a WebPPL package installable via npm would be beneficial. A similar release for the Stan version of the code would also be appreciated.
