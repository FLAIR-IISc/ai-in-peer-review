

This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, with a focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages. The idea of using BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures appears highly valuable, as existing MCMC diagnostics are indeed problematic. Two highly relevant references are [1,2], which similarly assess MCMC samplers by running them on data drawn from the prior. Section 3.1: The derivation is difficult to follow. Specifically, $q(\cdot)$ is used before its definition; from context, it appears to denote the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after $T$ steps, but this should be explicitly stated. While the supplement clarifies some points, the main text should be self-contained and not require supplement reading. In general, a clear step-by-step description of how to apply the method from start to finish would be beneficial—the reader must infer this from scattered details. Minor note: Separating unobserved variables into $z$ and $\theta$ is unnecessarily cluttered, as in a general setting there is no distinction between them—they always appear together; thus, one should be eliminated.
