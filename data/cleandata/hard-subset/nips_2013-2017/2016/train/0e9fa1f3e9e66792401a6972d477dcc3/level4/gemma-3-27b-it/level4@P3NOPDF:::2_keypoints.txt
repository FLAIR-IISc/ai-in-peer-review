- Presents a compelling analysis applying bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) to diagnose MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Uses BMC to establish lower bounds on KL divergence between true and MCMC-approximate posteriors.  
- Provides theoretical insights and a principled framework for assessing MCMC convergence and approximation quality.  
- Addresses the important problem of distinguishing MCMC algorithm limitations from model intractability.  
- Integrates the method practically with WebPPL and Stan, enabling empirical validation and practitioner adoption.  
- Clearly explains how to incorporate BMC into existing MCMC workflows as a diagnostic tool.  
- Does not introduce new sampling algorithms but offers a powerful diagnostic for existing methods.  
- Lacks detailed quantification of BMC’s computational cost and how it scales with dimensionality.  
- Needs more concrete practical guidelines for interpreting KL lower bounds across applications.  
- Would benefit from clearer articulation of BMC’s assumptions and implications if they are violated.  
- Overall judged a significant, rigorous contribution with recommendation for acceptance.
