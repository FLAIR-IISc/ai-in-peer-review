This paper presents a method for evaluating MCMC-based posterior inference, leveraging the recently developed bidirectional Monte Carlo technique. The authors' approach enables the analysis of lower bounds on the KL-divergence between the true posterior and the approximate distribution generated by an MCMC algorithm. The method has been successfully integrated into the popular probabilistic programming frameworks WebPPL and Stan.

Overall, this is a strong and well-executed paper. The application of a state-of-the-art technique, bidirectional Monte Carlo, to the challenging and important problem of assessing posterior inference quality is a valuable contribution. The idea is compelling, as it provides a principled and quantitative tool for a task that often relies on heuristics. The integration with WebPPL and Stan is a significant practical strength, making the proposed method readily available to the wider community.

Given its novelty, practical relevance, and clear presentation, I recommend this paper for acceptance.
