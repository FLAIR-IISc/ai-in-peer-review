- Proposes using BDMC (two AIS sweeps) to produce stochastic lower and upper bounds on the marginal likelihood and uses their gap to evaluate MCMC transition operators.  
- Strength: The idea is simple, easy to implement, and practically useful for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Novelty and depth are modest; much of the paper reiterates prior BDMC material and new sections (3.1–3.2) feel rushed and unclear.  
- Main new theoretical observation is that the lower–upper gap bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence, but this may not be entirely novel.  
- The paper omits a key related work: Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which uses the same forward/reverse sweeps and is closely connected to BDMC.  
- Important connections to Tempered Transitions are missing: TT’s acceptance ratio corresponds to the BDMC bound ratio and TT discusses related upper/lower thermodynamic integration estimates.  
- The choice of annealing schedule β_t (step sizes Δβ) is crucial; large Δβ harms performance and the BDMC gap should guide selecting smaller Δβ.  
- AutoBDMC is insufficiently specified; automating the schedule by reducing Δβ until bounds converge is a clear opportunity.  
- Experiments should systematically study how the lower and upper bounds depend on step size to strengthen the paper.  
- Overall: potentially useful work; acceptance could be supported if clarity and related-work issues (especially TT) are addressed in rebuttal/camera-ready.  
- Clarity issues: definition of the sampled-variable vector v⃗ is ambiguous given two AIS sweeps and inconsistent labeling (“rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”).  
- Mathematical clarity problems: p̂(y) is not defined in several equations, Equation (6) appears incorrect as written (identical distributions inside KL), and some KL terms seem misstated.  
- AutoBDMC is never unambiguously defined; the meaning should be explicitly stated.  
- Experimental detail questions: how β_t is chosen (code suggests β_t = t/T), whether BDMC uses the reported HMC/NUTS step counts, and undefined acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) need clarification.  
- Presentation fixes: enlarge figure axes/tick labels and correct typos (missing parentheses in D_JS expression; citation typo “Homan” → “Hoffman”).
