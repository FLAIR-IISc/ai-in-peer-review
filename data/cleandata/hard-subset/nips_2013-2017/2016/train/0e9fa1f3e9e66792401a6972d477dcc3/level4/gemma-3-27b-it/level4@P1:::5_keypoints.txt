- Problem addressed: assessing MCMC reliability and comparing efficiency of MCMC schemes using BDMC stochastic bounds on the log marginal likelihood.  
- Core idea: choose the MCMC scheme that yields the tightest BDMC upper/lower bounds for a given computational budget.  
- Main challenge: BDMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound.  
- Proposed workaround: use heuristics—run diagnostics on posteriors generated from synthetic data where good approximate/known samples are easier to obtain.  
- Evaluation: methodology tested on several examples with preliminary results showing promise in simple cases.  
- Positive note: using BDMC stochastic bounds as an MCMC diagnostic is novel and theoretically motivated, especially for AIS/SMC-type samplers.  
- Major concern 1: relies on prior knowledge of representative hyperparameters (ηreal), implying the very convergence problem is already solved.  
- Major concern 2: assumes the synthetic-data posterior has similar convergence properties to the real posterior, which may fail under model misspecification.  
- Major concern 3: assumes initializing at ηreal quickly yields an almost exact posterior sample, which is optimistic and not generally guaranteed.  
- Conceptual critique: the approach may effectively “change the problem” by diagnosing a different posterior than the real one.  
- Empirical critique: experiments are limited and simple, not sufficiently compelling for complex scenarios; more extensive validation needed.  
- Recommendation: authors should justify how their method overcomes multimodality and summary-statistic failures, and include simulations comparing against standard diagnostics.
