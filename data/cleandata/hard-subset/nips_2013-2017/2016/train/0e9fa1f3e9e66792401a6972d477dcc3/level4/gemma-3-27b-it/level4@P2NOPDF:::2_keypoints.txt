- Investigates theoretical properties of MCMC-based posterior inference using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC).
- Derives lower bounds on the KL divergence between the true posterior and MCMC approximations.
- Applies BMC to analyze MCMC convergence and accuracy, tackling an important Bayesian inference problem.
- Presents a novel perspective on the limitations of MCMC methods.
- Provides theoretical bounds that go beyond purely empirical assessments of MCMC performance.
- Implements and evaluates the methodology in WebPPL and Stan, a significant practical contribution.
- Integration with WebPPL and Stan demonstrates practicality and enables real-world model assessment.
- Paper is well-motivated and technically sound.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, calling it a strong and valuable contribution to Bayesian inference.
