- Proposal: use BDMC lower/upper bound gap as a generic performance measure for MCMC operators, with Stan/WebPPL implementations and an evaluation protocol.  
- Strength: idea is simple, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main critique: technical novelty is modest; the main new observation (gap bounds Jensen-Shannon divergence) may not be entirely novel.  
- Writing/clarity: sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed and suffer from reduced writing quality and clarity.  
- Important related work omitted: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is highly relevant and appears unacknowledged.  
- Conceptual connection: TT performs the same reverse/forward sweeps as BDMC; BDMC’s bound ratio appears equivalent to TT’s acceptance ratio.  
- β schedule concern: choice of β_t (Δβ) critically affects acceptance rates and BDMC bounds; authors give insufficient attention to schedule design.  
- AutoBDMC unclear: unclear what “auto” entails (e.g., automated Δβ reduction until bounds close).  
- Experiments lack systematic study of how bounds vary with step size (Δβ) and how that affects operator comparisons.  
- Ambiguity in notation: definition of vector v (variables sampled in AIS) is imprecise and inconsistent across labels (rev/fwd vs. for/back).  
- Missing definitions: estimator \hat{p}(y) and intended expectations (e.g., E[log \hat{R}_{rev}], E[log \hat{R}_{fwd}]) are not defined in equations (5–7, 9–11).  
- Mathematical error: Equation (6) is nonsensical as written (identical distributions inside KL); RHS likely misstated and should involve q_back(v | z, θ, y).  
- AutoBDMC purpose ambiguous: unclear if it means (a) using bound difference to assess convergence and/or (b) using the fixed hyperparameter scheme in §3.2.  
- Experimental details unclear: β_t selection seems to be step = 1/options.steps (i.e., T steps), but this should be stated explicitly.  
- Figure/notation issues: unclear what “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” counts, RAIS and variants (u)c(R)AIS are undefined in captions, and axis/tick labels are too small.  
- Typos: missing closing parentheses in D_KL terms in D_JS expression; “Homan” should be “Hoffman” in the NUTS citation.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is promising but acceptance hinges on fixing clarity issues and engaging with related work.
