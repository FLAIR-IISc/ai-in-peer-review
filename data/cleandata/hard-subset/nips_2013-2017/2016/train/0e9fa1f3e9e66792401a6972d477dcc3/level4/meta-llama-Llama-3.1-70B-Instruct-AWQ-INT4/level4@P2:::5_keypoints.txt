- Proposes BREAD: extending bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound symmetrized KL between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Demonstrates BREAD on several models/datasets and integrates it into WebPPL and Stan.  
- Problem is well-motivated and important; introduction is clear and concise.  
- Technical contributions are sound with a thorough analysis of properties and limitations.  
- Major practical concern: BDMC requires an exact sample from the target to give a valid stochastic upper bound.  
- Proposed heuristics to obtain that exact sample rely on strong, potentially unjustified assumptions.  
- Assumes availability of a representative hyperparameter η_real from prior MCMC runs, which is circular if convergence is uncertain.  
- Assumes the posterior from synthetic data generated at η_real resembles the original posterior, an unjustified leap especially under model misspecification.  
- Assumes starting from η_real lets MCMC quickly produce an almost exact sample, an optimistic and unproven claim.  
- Provided simple examples are unconvincing and may not generalize to more complex or pathological cases.  
- Paper lacks evidence that the method handles multimodal posteriors or cases where limited summary statistics are insufficient.  
- Needs simulation comparisons against common diagnostic methods to demonstrate improved reliability.  
- Writing could be improved with more background/context, clearer technical detail, and a stronger conclusion.  
- Paper would benefit from more visualizations and examples to illustrate method behaviors and limitations.  
- Originality: novel application of BDMC to MCMC reliability, but novelty would be clearer with more contextual framing.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem, but practical impact is uncertain without real-world evidence and broader validation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear exposition, and technically sound analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: relies on strong assumptions, insufficient empirical evidence for challenging cases, and needs more illustrative results and context.
