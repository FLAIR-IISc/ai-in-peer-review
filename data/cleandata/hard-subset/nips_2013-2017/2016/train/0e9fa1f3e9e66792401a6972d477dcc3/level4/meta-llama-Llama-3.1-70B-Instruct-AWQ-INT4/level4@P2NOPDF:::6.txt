The paper provides a summary of previously published results on bidirectional Monte Carlo and presents its application using two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan. Upon reviewing the content, it appears that the paper does not introduce any novel concepts, instead focusing on the implementation of established techniques in these languages. The work may be more suitable for presentation at a specialized workshop focused on Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, rather than a general conference like NIPS.
