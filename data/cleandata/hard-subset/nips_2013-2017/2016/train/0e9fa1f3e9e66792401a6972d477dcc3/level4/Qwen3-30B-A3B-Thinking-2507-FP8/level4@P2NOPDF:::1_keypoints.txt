- Proposes bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target for debugging/validating MCMC in PPLs.  
- Core idea is compelling and aligns with prior joint-distribution tests (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2012).  
- Section 3.1 is hard to follow: the marginal distribution q(·) is used before being defined—define q(·) on first use in the main text.  
- Provide a concise, step-by-step workflow in the main paper (data preparation → run sampler → compute divergence) to improve reproducibility.  
- The separate notations z and θ are redundant and add clutter; unify to a single latent-variable symbol.  
- The claim that BDMC “diagnoses failures” needs concrete examples of failure modes (e.g., multimodality, slow mixing) that BDMC can detect.  
- Expand how BDMC would integrate into PPL developer toolchains and practical debugging workflows.  
- Fix minor errors and tighten prose (e.g., missing closing parenthesis after “log” on line 165).  
- Reword vague phrases (e.g., replace “a little cluttered” with explicit critique) and clarify "test the ability of MCMC samplers" to something like "evaluate sampler performance on prior-sampled data."  
- Format citations in-text as (Geweke, 2004; Cook et al., 2012) for clarity.  
- Overall: promising technical contribution but currently obscured by presentation; prioritize clarity and accessibility for PPL developers.
