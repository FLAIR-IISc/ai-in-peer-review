- Proposes AutoBDMC, a novel method using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (AIS and reverse AIS) to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood for assessing MCMC convergence.
- Shows the gap between bounds bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate-sample distribution, providing a single scalar convergence measure.
- Well-motivated, clearly written overall, with a compelling experimental evaluation on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- Demonstrates practical utility for model selection and debugging.
- Section 3 is harder to follow; the transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and lacks motivation before introducing variables/equations.
- The divergence bound in Section 3.1 appears to be a novel contribution and should be explicitly stated as such.
- A concise, clear definition of “AutoBDMC” is missing and should state it is the AIS + reverse AIS approach to analyze MCMC convergence.
- Minor notation issues: Line 149 likely has p(theta, z | y) but should be p(theta, z, y), and the paper inconsistently uses y and D for data.
- Supplemental WebPPL code needs specified compatible WebPPL version(s) and integration instructions, or preferably be released as an npm-installable WebPPL package.
- Request to similarly release the Stan code/package for easier use and reproducibility.
- With revisions addressing clarity, explicit novelty claims, notation fixes, and code packaging, the paper could make a valuable contribution.
