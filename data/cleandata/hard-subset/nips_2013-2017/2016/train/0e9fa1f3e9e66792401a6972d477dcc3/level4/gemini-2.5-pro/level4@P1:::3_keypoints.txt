- Proposes using BDMC with forward and reverse AIS to produce stochastic lower/upper bounds on the marginal likelihood and uses their gap as a measure of MCMC performance; implemented for Stan and WebPPL with a real-data evaluation protocol.

- Strength: elegant, simple, intuitive idea that is easy to implement and valuable for probabilistic programming systems.

- Concern: novelty is modest since much of the paper repeats BDMC background, leaving Sections 3.1–3.2 rushed and unclear.

- Main new theoretical claim: the bound gap upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors, but the novelty of this observation is debatable.

- Major related-work omission: Radford Neal’s 1996 Tempered Transitions (TT) is highly relevant and closely connected to BDMC/acceptance-ratio interpretations.

- Connection to TT implies annealing schedule choice (βt and Δβ) is critical; the paper gives little guidance and the “automatic” claim is unsubstantiated.

- Recommendation: provide a procedure for iteratively reducing Δβ (increasing T) until the bound gap is acceptably small.

- Experiments are insufficiently thorough; need systematic study of how lower/upper bounds vary with Δβ or number of steps T.

- Clarity issue: definition of v (“variables sampled in AIS before the final stage”) is imprecise and ambiguous across the two AIS sweeps.

- Notation inconsistency: forward/reverse chains labeled inconsistently (fwd/rev vs for/back), causing confusion.

- Missing specification of which estimators are used in the expectations in Eqs. (6–11) (e.g., E[log R̂]?), making derivations hard to follow.

- Equation (7) appears incorrect as written; the KL divergence terms likely should be qfwd(v,xT) vs pT(xT) qrev(v|xT).

- Experimental detail unclear: annealing schedule βt appears linear (var step = 1/options.steps) — this should be explicitly stated and justified.

- Figure ambiguities: x-axis labels (“AIS steps” or “HMC / No-U-Turn steps”) unclear whether they denote annealing steps T or MCMC transitions per temperature.

- Figure captions omit acronym definitions (e.g., RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) and axes/tick fonts are too small for readability.

- Typo: in the NUTS citation [HG14], the first author is Hoffman, not “Homan.”
