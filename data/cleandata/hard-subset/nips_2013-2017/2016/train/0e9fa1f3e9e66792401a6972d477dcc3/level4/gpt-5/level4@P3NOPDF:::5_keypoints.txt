- Summary: Proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo bounds on the log marginal likelihood as an MCMC convergence diagnostic, using synthetic-data posteriors from a representative parameter η_real to obtain an approximate upper bound; includes empirical demonstrations.

- Strength: Tackles an important problem with a theoretically grounded, single comparative metric (bound width) and a clear practical motivation.

- Concern (η_real circularity): Method assumes access to a representative η_real that is already effectively converged and that a single point summarizes the posterior—both problematic, especially for multimodal or heavy-tailed posteriors.

- Concern (synthetic-data surrogate): Evaluating on synthetic-data posteriors may not reflect the real-data posterior’s mixing or geometry; model misspecification or atypical data can make the surrogate misleading.

- Concern (near-exact draws assumption): Counting on rapid mixing and near-exact posterior draws from synthetic-data initialized near η_real is optimistic and can fail in funnels, strong correlations, or non-identifiable problems, invalidating the upper bound.

- Collective critique: The workaround converts a principled bound into a heuristic tied to the very convergence properties it seeks to diagnose, making its accuracy hard to certify.

- Scope limitation: Method is most natural for AIS/SMC and focuses on marginal likelihood, so it may not apply well to standard MCMC workflows (HMC/NUTS, Gibbs) or detect local pathologies affecting expectations.

- Empirical limitations: Experiments are limited in scope/difficulty and do not convincingly show robustness under misspecification or superiority over standard diagnostics.

- Suggestion (validate surrogate): Provide theoretical/empirical conditions for surrogate validity, sensitivity analyses across choices of η_real, and tests under known misspecification.

- Suggestion (calibrate upper bound): Quantify bias from approximate reverse samples (coupling diagnostics, R-hat/ESS, TV bounds) and use exact-draw methods in small problems to calibrate the heuristic.

- Suggestion (comparative evaluation): Benchmark against standard diagnostics under matched budgets and run stress tests (multimodal mixtures, funnels, heavy tails, sparse models).

- Suggestion (practical guidance): Offer decision rules for bound gaps, document failure modes and safeguards (repeats, cross-checks), and give recipes for integrating non-AIS/SMC MCMC.

- Suggestion (clarity/presentation): Explicitly define η_real and its source, mark when upper bounds are heuristic, and report computational budgets and ablations showing schedule/sample effects.

- Overall assessment: Conceptually compelling but currently relies on strong, problem-dependent assumptions; requires stronger justification, calibration, and broader experiments to be a reliable convergence diagnostic.
