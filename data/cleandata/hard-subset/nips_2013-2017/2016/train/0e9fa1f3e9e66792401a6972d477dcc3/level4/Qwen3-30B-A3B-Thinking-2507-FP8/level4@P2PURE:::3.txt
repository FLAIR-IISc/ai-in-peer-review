

The authors propose a general method for evaluating MCMC transition operators using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC), a recently introduced technique. BDMC performs two annealed importance sampling (AIS) sweeps—structurally identical to those in Tempered Transitions (TT) [Neal, 1996]—but reinterprets the accumulated probabilities as lower and upper bounds on the marginal likelihood. This paper leverages the gap between these bounds as a general performance metric for MCMC transition operators. Technical contributions include BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL probabilistic programming systems and a protocol for assessing posterior inference quality. The work has notable strengths and weaknesses. Its primary strength is the simplicity and implementability of using BDMC to assess MCMC convergence, which, in my view, confers high potential impact, particularly for probabilistic programming systems (the intended use case). All such systems would benefit from implementing this method. However, concerns arise regarding novelty and technical depth. While the authors cite an arXiv submission on BDMC and dedicate substantial space to re-explaining it (a prudent choice), the main technical contributions in Sections 3.1 and 3.2 are underdeveloped, and writing clarity suffers in these sections. The technical contributions are modest relative to existing BDMC work. The key novelty—recognizing that the difference between the bounds provides an upper bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence—is insightful, though arguably not entirely novel. This leads to a related work concern: the authors omit Radford Neal's TT work [Statistics and Computing, 1996], which is highly relevant. This citation is also absent from the BDMC manuscript, raising questions about awareness. If known, this omission misses an opportunity to highlight connections: TT performs identical reverse "heating" and forward "annealing" sweeps as BDMC but uses tempering MCMC moves to improve mixing in multimodal distributions. In TT, the reverse sweep's final sample initializes the forward sweep, and the forward sweep's final sample is accepted/rejected based on the ratio of importance weights accumulated in both sweeps. This acceptance ratio precisely equals the ratio of the stochastic lower and upper bounds. Section 3.3 of Neal's TT paper interprets this ratio via two thermodynamic integration estimates bounding log Z_T - log Z_1, noting TT accepts with probability 1 when the bounds converge. The TT analogy also questions the authors' focus on β_t selection. In TT, suboptimal Δβ = β_{t+1} - β_t steps cause poor acceptance (too large) or wasted computation (too small). The BDMC bound gap similarly depends on Δβ, offering clear intuition: if bounds are insufficiently close, rerun BDMC with smaller Δβ. Similarly, comparisons between MCMC operators likely become unreliable when the log-bound gap exceeds 1 nat, necessitating reduced Δβ. It remains unclear what "auto" signifies in AutoBDMC; an opportunity exists to develop an iterative Δβ schedule for reliable inference. The experiments would benefit from a systematic study of how bounds vary with step size. Despite these concerns, the proposed method is potentially valuable. If the authors' response convincingly addresses clarity and related work in the camera-ready version, I would support acceptance.  

**Clarity Issues:**  
- Definition of $\vec{v}$ as "all variables sampled in AIS before the final stage" is ambiguous. The authors likely intend ${z_{1:T-1}, \theta_{1:T-1}}$, but inconsistent labels ("rev"/"fwd" vs. "for"/"back") obscure whether this distinction is meaningful or coincidental.  
- Equations (5–7) and (9–11) lack definition of estimator $\hat{p}(y)$ for the expectation. Clarification is needed on whether the intended terms are $E[\log \hat{R}_{\text{rev}}]$ and $E[\log \hat{R}_{\text{fwd}}]$.  
- Equation (6) is inconsistent (identical distributions inside KL divergence). Based on Equations (4) and (10), the terms should likely be $p(z, \theta \mid y) \parallel q_{\text{back}}(v \mid z, \theta, y)$.  
- AutoBDMC is not unambiguously defined. It presumably entails: (a) using the stochastic bound gap to assess convergence, and (b) applying the fixed hyperparameter scheme from Section 3.2.  

**Experiments:**  
- β_t selection is unclear. The appendix code uses `var step = 1/options.steps`, suggesting AutoBDMC parameterizes β_t by steps $T$ (from β_T = 1 to β_1 = 1/T). This should be explicitly stated.  
- Figures 2–3: Does "HMC / No-U-Turn steps" indicate the number of steps used in BDMC?  
- RAIS (reverse AIS) and variants (u)c(R)AIS (likely (un)collapsed) are undefined in captions.  
- Axes and tick labels should be enlarged for readability.  

**Typos:**  
- Missing closing parentheses in D_KL terms within the D_JS expression.  
- "Homan" in NUTS citation should be "Hoffman."
