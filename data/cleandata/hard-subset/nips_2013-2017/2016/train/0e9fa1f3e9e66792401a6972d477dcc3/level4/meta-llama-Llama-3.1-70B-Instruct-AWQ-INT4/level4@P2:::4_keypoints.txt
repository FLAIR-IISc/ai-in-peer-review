- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) using BDMC/AIS to assess MCMC convergence and shows the bound gap upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Provides a single scalar quantity to evaluate MCMC convergence behavior.  
- Well-motivated and clearly useful; experiments are reasonably compelling and show utility for model selection and debugging.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow, with a jarring transition into 3.1 that launches into variables and equations without motivation.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, that novelty should be stated explicitly since it is central to the paper’s contribution.  
- Authors should explicitly define BREAD and its components (e.g., that it uses AIS and reverse AIS to analyze MCMC convergence).  
- Notation is inconsistent (e.g., switching between y and D for data), which causes confusion.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and provides sufficient background and thorough analysis.  
- Significant contribution: a rigorous, general method for evaluating posterior inference accuracy in probabilistic programming languages.  
- Reliance on simulated data for convergence analysis is a potential weakness because it may not represent real-world data, though authors attempt to justify this via joint-distribution simulation.  
- Recommend acceptance with revisions to improve clarity and consistency.  
- Requested revisions: define BREAD and components, motivate variables/equations in Section 3.1, standardize notation, and provide WebPPL/Stan code details (versions and integration instructions).  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
