- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the symmetrized KL divergence between the true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Builds on prior marginal likelihood estimation work and applies to AIS and SMC-based inference.  
- Proposes the BREAD method and integrates it into WebPPL and Stan.  
- Validated on several models and datasets; used to guide comparisons of model representations.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by uncovering a bug in WebPPL, showing implementation-verification power.  
- Provides a practical, theoretically grounded diagnostic for MCMC sample quality in black-box settings.  
- Represents a significant engineering effort with useful tooling for probabilistic programming users.  
- Limited theoretical novelty: core contributions rely heavily on existing work [GGA15].  
- Primarily an application/engineering contribution rather than a methodological breakthrough in inference.  
- The protocol/heuristic for applying BREAD to real-world data needs stronger theoretical justification.  
- Paper assumes reader familiarity with AIS and SMC and offers only brief explanations of these methods.  
- Reviewer suggests the work may be more appropriate for a workshop on MCMC implementations than for core NIPS.  
- Overall: well-executed and useful implementation but limited novelty raises concerns about suitability for NIPS.
