- Introduces AutoBDMC: uses BDMC (AIS + reverse AIS) to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood and a scalar Jensen-Shannon divergence bound to diagnose MCMC convergence.  
- Diagnostic requires synthetic data from the model joint; authors show empirical evidence that synthetic-data behavior predicts real-data sampler performance.  
- Demonstrates practical utility of AutoBDMC for model selection and debugging MCMC implementations.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and experimentally well-supported.  
- Section 3 needs clearer framing: abrupt transition, new notation without context, and the divergence bound’s novelty should be stated explicitly if it’s a main theoretical contribution.  
- Define “AutoBDMC” precisely and early (e.g., one-sentence definition in the introduction or start of Section 3) to avoid confusion.  
- Notation inconsistency for observed data: `y` vs `D` should be unified.  
- Possible typo at Line 149: joint distribution should likely be written `p(theta, z, y)` rather than `p(theta, z | y)`.  
- Supplementary code suggestions: specify WebPPL versions supported, provide clear integration instructions, and consider releasing WebPPL and Stan implementations as installable packages (npm) for easier adoption.
