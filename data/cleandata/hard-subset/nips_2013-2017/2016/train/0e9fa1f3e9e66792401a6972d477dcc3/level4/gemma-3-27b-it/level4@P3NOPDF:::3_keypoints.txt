- Paper proposes evaluating MCMC operators using BDMC by measuring the gap between AIS-derived lower and upper marginal likelihood bounds.
- Implementation provided for Stan and WebPPL, plus a protocol for posterior inference quality evaluation.
- Strength: simple, easily implemented method with potential high impact and wide adoption in probabilistic programming.
- Concern: limited novelty and technical depth; heavy emphasis on BDMC background crowds out authors’ original contributions.
- Insight: the bound gap gives an upper bound on Jensen-Shannon divergence, but this observation is not entirely novel.
- Major omission: failure to cite Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related conceptually and mathematically.
- Important connection: BDMC’s reverse/forward sweeps and bound ratios align with TT acceptance ratios and thermodynamic integration interpretations.
- Key sensitivity: performance depends on the β_t schedule and step size Δβ; too-large Δβ harms acceptance, too-small wastes computation.
- Recommendation: explore adaptive schedules (e.g., iteratively decreasing Δβ based on the bound gap) as an extension of AutoBDMC.
- Experiments are insufficiently thorough; need a systematic study of how bounds vary with step size Δβ.
- AutoBDMC description is unclear—uncertain whether it refers only to bound-difference computation or also includes the hyperparameter scheme in §3.2.
- Documentation/parameterization issues: role of the “steps” parameter and relation to β_t should be explicitly stated (code suggests β_T=1 to β_1=1/T).
- Figure/notation issues: unclear whether “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” refers to steps within BDMC evaluation; RAIS and variant acronyms are undefined in captions.
- Specific clarity problems: ambiguous definition of vector v and inconsistent use of rev/fwd vs for/back terminology.
- Mathematical clarity issues: Equations (5–7) and (9–11) do not define estimator \hat{p}(y); Equation (6) appears incorrect due to identical distributions in a KL term.
- AutoBDMC’s precise meaning and algorithmic details remain ambiguously defined.
- Presentation fixes requested: increase axis/tick label sizes in figures and define acronyms in captions.
- Minor errors: missing closing parentheses in D_JS expression and incorrect NUTS citation (Homan → Hoffman).
- Overall judgment: promising and worthwhile paper; camera-ready should address clarity, Tempered Transitions relation, and a deeper experimental analysis of Δβ to strengthen the manuscript.
