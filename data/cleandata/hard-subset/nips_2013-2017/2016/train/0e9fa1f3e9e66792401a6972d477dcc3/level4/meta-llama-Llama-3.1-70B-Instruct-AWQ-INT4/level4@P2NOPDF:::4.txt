This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence that leverages the Bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood. By utilizing annealed importance sampling, the authors establish that the gap between these bounds serves as a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples. This provides a single scalar quantity to evaluate MCMC convergence behavior. Although the method is limited to analyzing convergence on synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors provide experimental evidence that its analyses on such synthetic data are predictive of behavior on real-world data. They also demonstrate the method's utility in model selection and debugging.

Overall, the paper is well-motivated and readable, presenting a clearly useful method with a reasonably compelling experimental evaluation. However, Section 3 is more challenging to follow than the rest of the paper, particularly the transition into Section 3.1, which introduces variable and equation definitions without proper motivation. If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is a novel result, this should be explicitly stated, as it appears to be a crucial contribution of the paper.

A clear definition of AutoBDMC is lacking throughout the paper. Based on the content, it seems that AutoBDMC refers to the approach of using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence. A statement to this effect would be beneficial. By the time Section 3.2 mentions the challenges posed to AutoBDMC, a clear definition of AutoBDMC should have been established.

Some minor issues were also noted. On Line 149, it is unclear whether p(theta, z | y) should be p(theta, z, y). Additionally, the paper inconsistently uses y and D to denote data/observations, which may cause confusion. In the supplemental material, the WebPPL code should be accompanied by information on which version(s) of WebPPL it is compatible with and instructions on how to integrate it into the WebPPL distribution. Ideally, the code should be released as a WebPPL package that can be installed via npm. A similar release for the Stan version of the code would also be appreciated.
