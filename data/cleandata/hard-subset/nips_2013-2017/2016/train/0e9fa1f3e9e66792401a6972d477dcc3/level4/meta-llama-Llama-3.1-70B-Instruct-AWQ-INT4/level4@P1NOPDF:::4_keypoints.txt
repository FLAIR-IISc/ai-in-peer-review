- Proposes AutoBDMC, using Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS/rAIS to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood and bound the Jensen–Shannon divergence for MCMC convergence assessment.  
- Demonstrates that the bound gap provides a single scalar measure of divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Method is limited to analyzing convergence on synthetic data from the joint distribution, but authors present evidence that results predict real-world behavior.  
- Shows utility of the method for model selection and debugging; paper is readable, well-motivated, and experiments are reasonably compelling.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow; transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces variables/equations without sufficient motivation.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, it should be explicitly stated because the paper’s contribution depends on it.  
- The paper never explicitly defines “AutoBDMC”; authors should clearly state that it refers to using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence.  
- References to AutoBDMC’s challenges (e.g., in Section 3.2) appear before its definition is established.  
- Minor issues: typo on Line 149 (p(theta, z | y) likely should be p(theta, z, y)) and inconsistent notation for data (y vs. D).  
- For supplemental WebPPL code, provide compatible WebPPL version(s) and integration instructions; ideally release as an npm-installable WebPPL package and provide a similar release for the Stan code.
