- Proposes using BDMC (two AIS sweeps) to bound the marginal likelihood and uses the gap between lower/upper bounds as an MCMC performance measure.  
- Implements BDMC within Stan and WebPPL and presents a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- The idea is conceptually simple, implementable, and potentially impactful for probabilistic programming debugging and assessment.  
- Novelty/technical depth is modest and should be carefully weighed.  
- The paper insufficiently connects to Tempered Transitions (Neal 1996), which uses similar annealing/heating and should be discussed in detail.  
- The observation that the bound gap upper-bounds Jeffreys divergence is insightful but not entirely novel.  
- Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed and suffer from unclear writing and notation.  
- Definition of vector v (“all variables sampled in AIS before the final stage”) is imprecise—unclear if it means {z_{1:T-1}, θ_{1:T-1}}.  
- Inconsistent terminology ("rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back") adds confusion.  
- Expectations in equations (5–7) and (9–11) lack a clear definition of the estimator \hat{p}(y).  
- Equation (6) appears to contain identical distributions inside the KL divergence (possible error).  
- The meaning and scope of “AutoBDMC” are ambiguous—unclear if it denotes just convergence assessment or also the fixed hyperparameter scheme in Sec. 3.2.  
- Experiments need more systematic investigation, particularly how bounds vary with step size Δβ and sensitivity to small Δβ.  
- Experimental details are unclear about the choice of β_t and the meaning of "HMC / No-U-Turn steps" in Figures 2–3.  
- The RAIS acronym is undefined in figure captions, and figure axes/tick labels need to be larger for readability.  
- Several minor typos and notation mistakes (e.g., missing parentheses in Jeffreys divergence, typo in NUTS citation).  
- Overall recommendation: worthwhile paper with practical value; likely to be accepted if clarity, related-work discussion (esp. Tempered Transitions), and experimental rigor are improved.  
- Pros summarized: simple, impactful method; useful Stan/WebPPL implementations; novel application of BDMC to MCMC quality assessment.  
- Cons summarized: modest technical contribution, unclear writing in key sections, incomplete related-work discussion, and experiments that could be more thorough.
