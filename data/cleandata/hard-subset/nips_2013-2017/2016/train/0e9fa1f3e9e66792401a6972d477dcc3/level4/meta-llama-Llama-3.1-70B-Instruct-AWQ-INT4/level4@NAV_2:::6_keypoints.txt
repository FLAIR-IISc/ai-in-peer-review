- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC inference accuracy.  
- Extends BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and AIS-generated sample distribution.  
- Proposes a practical protocol for applying the technique to real-world data.  
- Integrates the method into probabilistic programming languages WebPPL and Stan.  
- Validates the approach on several models and datasets with thorough evaluation.  
- Provides clear background on AIS, BDMC, and probabilistic programming for accessibility.  
- Compares the method to other techniques and analyzes its limitations.  
- Relates work to prior studies on MCMC diagnostics and automatic configuration.  
- Notes independent related work building on BDMC for probabilistic programming.  
- Technically sound and well-presented, with useful practitioner applicability.  
- Main weakness: perceived lack of originality—method is an extension of prior work.  
- Conclusion: well-written and evaluated but may lack sufficient novelty for a top-tier conference.
