This paper introduces a method for estimating the Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler’s output and the target distribution, with a focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations within Probabilistic Programming Languages. The core idea of leveraging bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose sampler convergence issues is appealing, as current MCMC diagnostics often prove unreliable. The approach relates to existing work such as [1,2], which similarly assess sampler performance by evaluating them on data drawn from the prior distribution. Section 3.1 presents a derivation that I found difficult to follow. Specifically, the function q(.) is used before its definition; while the context suggests it represents the marginal distribution of the Approximate Incremental Sampler (AIS) after T steps, this should be stated explicitly. While the supplementary material clarifies this point, the main text should be self-contained. A clearer, step-by-step explanation of the method’s application would be beneficial, as the reader currently needs to synthesize this information from various parts of the paper. A minor point: the separation of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* feels unnecessary in this general setting, as they consistently appear together; eliminating either *z* or *θ* would simplify the notation.
