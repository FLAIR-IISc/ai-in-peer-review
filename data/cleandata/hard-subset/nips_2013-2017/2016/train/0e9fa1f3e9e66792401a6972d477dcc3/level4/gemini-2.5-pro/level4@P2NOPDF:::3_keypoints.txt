- Proposes using the BDMC stochastic upper–lower bound gap to evaluate MCMC transition operator quality, with implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for assessing posterior inference.  
- Strength: simple, elegant, easily implementable diagnostic with high potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Concern about novelty: heavy reliance on prior BDMC work and the new insight (bound gap upper-bounding JS divergence) may be of limited novelty.  
- Major omission: fails to cite/discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (TT), which closely parallels the BDMC reverse/forward sweeps and acceptance-ratio interpretation.  
- Clarify technical details: ambiguous definition of v, inconsistent labeling ("rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back"), and undefined estimator \hat{p}(y) in key equations.  
- Mathematical error: Equation (6) appears incorrect (KL between identical distributions); suggested correction to the second term is needed.  
- AutoBDMC is never explicitly defined and should be clearly stated as the protocol/hyperparameter scheme it denotes.  
- Missed discussion on annealing schedule: step sizes Δβ_t critically affect acceptance/bound gap (as in TT); recommend discussing/adapting schedule and making AutoBDMC iterative/adaptive.  
- Experimental clarity issues: choice of β_t not stated in main text, ambiguity about what "HMC/No-U-Turn steps" refers to, undefined RAIS acronym and (u)c(R)AIS variants, and small figure font sizes.  
- Reviewer stance: paper has a strong core idea and could be accepted if the TT connection and clarity issues are addressed.  
- Minor typos: missing parentheses in D_JS expression and "Homan" should be "Hoffman" in the NUTS citation.
