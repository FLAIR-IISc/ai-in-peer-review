

This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence using the Bidirectional Monte Carlo approach to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood (relying on annealed importance sampling). The authors demonstrate that the gap between these bounds serves as a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, providing a single scalar metric for evaluating convergence behavior. While the method is inherently limited to synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors present experimental evidence indicating that its analyses on such data predict behavior on real-world data. The paper further illustrates applications for model selection and debugging. Overall, the paper is well-written, well-motivated, and presents a clearly useful method with reasonably compelling experimental evaluation.  

However, several issues require attention. Section 3 is notably more difficult to follow than the rest of the paper. The transition into Section 3.1 is particularly jarring, as it immediately introduces variables and equations without justification. Crucially, if the divergence bound in Section 3.1 constitutes a new contribution (which appears to be the case), this novelty should be explicitly stated, as the paper’s core contribution hinges on this result. Furthermore, the paper never explicitly defines AutoBDMC. Based on the context, it appears to refer to the approach of using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence; this should be clearly articulated early in the paper. The phrase "this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC" in Section 3.2 assumes prior definition of AutoBDMC, which is absent.  

Additional minor concerns:  
- Line 149: The expression $p(\theta, z \mid y)$ likely requires $p(\theta, z, y)$ (the joint distribution).  
- Notational inconsistencies: The paper alternates between $y$ and $D$ to denote data/observations, causing confusion.  
- WebPPL code in the supplement should specify the required WebPPL version(s) and provide clear instructions for integration (ideally, release as a standalone WebPPL package via npm). A similar package for the Stan implementation would be highly desirable.
