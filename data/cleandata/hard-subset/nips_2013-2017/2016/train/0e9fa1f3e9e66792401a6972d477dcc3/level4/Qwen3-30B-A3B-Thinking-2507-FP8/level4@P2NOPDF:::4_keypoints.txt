- Proposes AutoBDMC: uses BDMC (AIS/rAIS) to get stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood, with their gap provably upper-bounding the Jensen–Shannon divergence as a scalar MCMC convergence diagnostic.  
- Missing explicit definition of “AutoBDMC”; paper never clearly states it is the application of BDMC/AIS to MCMC convergence assessment.  
- Section 3 (esp. transition into 3.1) is poorly motivated and disjointed, launching into variables/equations without adequate setup.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, that novelty must be stated and emphasized; currently it’s presented as a routine consequence, undermining the contribution.  
- Notational inconsistency: uses both y and D for observed data/likelihood (e.g., Eq.3 vs. Line 149); likely typo on Line 149 (should be p(theta, z, y) not p(theta, z | y)).  
- Supplementary code issues: WebPPL implementation lacks version compatibility and integration instructions; recommend releasing an npm-installable WebPPL package and a similar Stan package for reproducibility.  
- Empirical evaluation is reasonably compelling: shows AutoBDMC assessments on synthetic data predict performance on real datasets.  
- Demonstrated practical applications for model selection and MCMC debugging, indicating clear utility.  
- Overall assessment: promising, well-motivated paper with solid experiments, but major revisions required to clarify AutoBDMC’s definition and the theoretical presentation before acceptance (recommend major revision).
