

This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence. The approach leverages the Bidirectional Monte Carlo method to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood (which relies on annealed importance sampling). The authors demonstrate that the gap between these bounds constitutes a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, thereby providing a single scalar metric for evaluating MCMC convergence behavior. Since the method is restricted to synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors provide experimental evidence that its analysis on such synthetic data predicts performance on real-world data. They further demonstrate applications of the method for model selection and debugging. Overall, the paper is well-written and well-motivated, with the presented method appearing clearly useful and the experimental evaluation reasonably compelling. However, Section 3 is difficult to follow, particularly the transition into Section 3.1, which immediately introduces variables and equations without motivation. If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 represents a novel contribution (which appears to be the case), this novelty should be explicitly stated, as the paper's core contribution hinges on this result. Crucially, the paper never explicitly defines what AutoBDMC is. Based on my understanding, AutoBDMC refers to the approach of using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence; this should be clearly stated early in the paper. By Section 3.2's statement that "…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC," the term must already be unambiguously defined. Additional minor issues: Line 149: Should p(θ, z | y) be p(θ, z, y)? Notational inconsistencies: The paper alternates between using y and D to denote data/observations, which is occasionally confusing. WebPPL code in the supplement: This should include (a) the specific WebPPL version(s) it is known to work with, and (b) instructions for integrating it into the WebPPL distribution. Ideally, this should be released as a WebPPL package installable via npm. A similar release for a Stan implementation would also be highly appreciated.
