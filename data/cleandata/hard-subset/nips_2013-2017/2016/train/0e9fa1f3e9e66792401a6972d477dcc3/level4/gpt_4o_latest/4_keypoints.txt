- Introduces AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS/rAIS to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood.  
- Shows the gap between bounds estimates the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the approximate sample distribution, yielding a single scalar MCMC-convergence metric.  
- Method is restricted to analyzing convergence on synthetic data generated from the joint, though authors claim synthetic performance predicts real-data behavior.  
- Demonstrates applications of the method for model selection and debugging.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and proposes a practical, valuable method with reasonably convincing experiments.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow; transition to Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces variables/equations without adequate context or motivation.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, this should be explicitly stated since it appears to be a primary contribution.  
- Definition of “AutoBDMC” is unclear and should be explicitly and early clarified (the term is used before being defined).  
- Line 149 likely contains an expression error: p(theta, z | y) vs. p(theta, z, y).  
- Notational inconsistency between using y and D for data/observations, which is confusing.  
- Supplemental WebPPL code needs compatible WebPPL version(s) and integration instructions; recommend releasing as an npm-installable WebPPL package and providing a Stan release if feasible.
