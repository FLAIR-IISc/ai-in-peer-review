This paper presents a novel method for evaluating the quality of MCMC-based posterior inference by leveraging the recently developed Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) framework. The core idea is to compute rigorous lower bounds on the KL-divergence between the true posterior and the approximate distribution produced by an MCMC sampler. The authors demonstrate the practical utility of their approach by integrating it into two popular probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan.

The key strengths of this work are:

1.  **Novelty and Significance:** The paper tackles the important and challenging problem of MCMC diagnostics. Applying a state-of-the-art technique like Bidirectional Monte Carlo to quantitatively assess the accuracy of posterior approximations is a clever and insightful contribution. This provides a more principled alternative to existing heuristic-based convergence diagnostics.

2.  **Practical Relevance:** The successful integration of the proposed evaluation method into widely used frameworks like Stan and WebPPL is a significant strength. This demonstrates the feasibility of the approach and provides a clear path for its adoption by practitioners in the machine learning and statistics communities.

3.  **Clarity and Execution:** The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the problem, the proposed solution, and its implementation.

Overall, this is a strong and well-executed paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian inference. The idea is interesting, the methodology is sound, and the work addresses a topic of high relevance to the NIPS community. I recommend this paper for acceptance.
