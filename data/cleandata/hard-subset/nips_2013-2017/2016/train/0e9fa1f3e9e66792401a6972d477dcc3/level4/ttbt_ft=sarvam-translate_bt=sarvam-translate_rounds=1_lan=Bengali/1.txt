This paper proposes the use of a two-way Monte Carlo to estimate the J-S divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a particular focus on debugging and evaluating the MCMC implementation in PPL. I liked the idea of using a BCMC to determine the reason for a sampler's failure to converge—this idea seems quite useful, as the existing MCMC diagnostics are really problematic. Two very relevant references are [1,2], which test the capabilities of MCMC samplers by taking samples from prior data. Section 3.1: This derivation was very difficult for me to follow. Firstly, q(.) is used before it is defined. From the context, it seems to represent the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps, but this should be explicitly stated. Reading the appendix will clarify things a bit, but the reader will not need to read the appendix to understand the main text. In general, it would be good to have a clear statement of how to apply the method from start to finish—the reader needs to connect it a bit. Small note: In general, if there is no difference between z and θ, it is a bit more complicated to divide the uninitialized variables into z and θ—they are always together, so what if z or θ is omitted?
