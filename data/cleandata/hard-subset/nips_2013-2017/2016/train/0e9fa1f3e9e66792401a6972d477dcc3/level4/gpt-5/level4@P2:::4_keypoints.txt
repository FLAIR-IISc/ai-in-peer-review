- Main contribution: extends BDMC to show the expected BDMC gap upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and AIS/SMC approximate samples.  
- Methodology: uses forward (prior→posterior) and reverse (posterior→prior) AIS to obtain stochastic lower/upper bounds on log marginal likelihood.  
- Practical implementations: integrated into WebPPL and Stan with empirical validation on toy and realistic models.  
- Practical protocol: propose fitting hyperparameters on real data, simulating from fitted model, and evaluating bounds on simulated data to relate guarantees to real-data use.  
- Conceptual strength: provides an algorithm- and task-agnostic scalar measure of sample quality for AIS/SMC samplers.  
- Practical impact: demonstrates usefulness for model representation choices (collapsed vs uncollapsed) and debugging (found a WebPPL bug).  
- Relation to prior work: refines BDMC (Grosse et al. 2015) and complements Stein-discrepancy and classical MCMC diagnostics; concurrent work on “subjective divergence” noted.  
- Theoretical soundness: derivation appears correct linking expected BDMC gap to Jeffreys divergence.  
- Clarity issue: Section 3 is hard to follow; variables and distributions introduced abruptly and need better motivation and roadmap.  
- Recommendation for Section 3: motivate extended-state view, state assumptions explicitly (ergodicity, exact posterior sample, independent forward chains), and give pseudocode for forward/reverse AIS.  
- Terminology issue: method naming inconsistent (BDMC vs BREAD); recommend a single explicit name and clear definition at introduction.  
- Scope limitation: guarantees require an exact posterior sample (usually only for simulated data); real-data protocol is heuristic and depends on reverse-chain initialization.  
- Need for quantified analyses: quantify reverse-chain initialization bias, report variability/uncertainty of bounds (multiple runs, CIs), and analyze computational cost vs bound tightness.  
- Practical guidance missing: provide advice and sensitivity analyses for choosing annealing schedules and transition kernels.  
- Comparisons: empirical section would be stronger with direct comparisons to alternative sample-quality metrics (e.g., Stein discrepancy).  
- Notation/presentation issues: inconsistent use of y vs D for data; unclear where log R and log R_rev are stochastic bounds and whether inequalities are in expectation or high probability.  
- Supplementary/code requests: specify supported WebPPL versions, installation instructions, npm packaging, and analogous packaging/instructions for Stan.  
- Significance: high practical relevance for probabilistic programming and MCMC users; useful diagnostic for AIS/SMC samplers.  
- Grounds for acceptance: rigorous, interpretable measure with theoretical grounding, implementations, and convincing demonstrations.  
- Grounds against acceptance: relies on simulated-data exact samples, bound is only in expectation with finite-sample uncertainty uncharacterized, and exposition/notation need refinement.
