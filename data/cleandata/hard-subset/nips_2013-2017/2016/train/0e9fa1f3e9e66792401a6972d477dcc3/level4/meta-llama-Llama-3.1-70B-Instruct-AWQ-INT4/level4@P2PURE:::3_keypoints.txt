- Proposes using the gap between BDMC's stochastic lower and upper bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC transition operators; implements BDMC for Stan and WebPPL and gives an evaluation protocol.  
- Strength: simple, elegant, easy to implement, potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Weakness: limited novelty and depth; much of the paper reiterates existing BDMC material and sections 3.1–3.2 are rushed and unclear.  
- Main technical novelty claimed is that the bound gap upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence — a nice observation but arguably not entirely novel.  
- Important related work omitted: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is highly relevant and not cited.  
- Tempered Transitions performs similar reverse/forward sweeps and its acceptance ratio corresponds to the ratio of stochastic lower/upper bounds; connections to that method are not discussed.  
- The choice of βt (Δβ) is under-discussed; step size affects acceptance/gap and thus reliability, and should be analyzed and tuned.  
- “Auto” in AutoBDMC is not clearly defined; opportunity exists to describe an adaptive schedule that decreases Δβ until bounds are reliable.  
- Experiments are insufficiently thorough; missing systematic study of how lower and upper bounds vary with step size and β schedule.  
- Ambiguity in notation: definition of vector v (variables sampled in AIS) is loose and labels for sweeps are inconsistent (rev/fwd vs for/back).  
- Missing definitions in equations (5–7) and (9–11): the estimator \hat{p}(y) and the expectations taken are not clearly specified.  
- Equation (6) appears incorrect as written (KL divergence uses identical distributions); likely wrong terms are presented.  
- AutoBDMC’s precise meaning (e.g., whether it is just computing the bound gap and using a fixed hyperparameter scheme) is never unambiguously specified.  
- Experimental details unclear: how βt is chosen (code hints step = 1/options.steps but not documented) and whether reported HMC/NUTS steps refer to BDMC inner steps.  
- Acronyms and variants in figures/captions (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) are not defined; figures need larger axes and tick labels for readability.  
- Typos: missing parentheses in D_JS expression; NUTS citation typo “Homan” should be “Hoffman.”  
- Overall verdict: method is potentially useful and could be accepted if authors fix clarity, add related-work discussion (Neal TT), and strengthen experimental analysis.
