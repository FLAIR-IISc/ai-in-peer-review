- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to rigorously evaluate MCMC by bounding the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Extends the BDMC framework to provide stochastic upper and lower bounds on the Jeffreys divergence, not just the marginal likelihood.  
- Shows the gap between these bounds gives an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence, yielding a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Validated on simulated data where exact Jeffreys divergence is computable.  
- Proposes a protocol to apply BREAD to real-world data by fitting hyperparameters and generating synthetic data from estimates.  
- Integrated BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical applicability.  
- Demonstrated uses: model selection (collapsed vs uncollapsed matrix factorization) and debugging (discovered a WebPPL multivariate Gaussian sampling bug).  
- Builds on and conceptually advances BDMC and differs from Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka [CTM16] by directly assessing posterior approximation quality.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and experiments on toy distributions support claims.  
- Experimental validation spans five models: linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization, AR time series, and mixture-of-Gaussians clustering.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, though Section 3 (esp. 3.1) is hard to follow due to abrupt notation changes and limited motivation.  
- Key theoretical novelty (BDMC gap bounds Jeffreys divergence) should be explicitly stated as the main contribution.  
- Draft mistakenly refers to the method as "AutoBDMC" in title/abstract and should consistently use "BREAD."  
- Suggested clarity improvements: define BREAD early, use consistent data notation (y vs D), explicitly contrast with [CTM16], and state limitations (applicability mainly to AIS/SMC-like algorithms).  
- Significance is high for probabilistic programming users lacking reliable inference-quality tools; BREAD fills a critical need.  
- Main strengths: clear problem statement, significant theoretical extension, and compelling multi-framework experimental validation.  
- Primary weaknesses: lack of explicit novelty statement in Section 3.1 and potential confusion between BREAD and BDMC in title/abstract.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, well-validated contribution with minor exposition revisions needed; recommendation: accept.
