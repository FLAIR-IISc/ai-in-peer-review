- Proposes estimating Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC).
- Main application is debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations, especially in probabilistic programming languages.
- Idea is promising and useful because existing MCMC diagnostics are often inadequate for detecting convergence failures.
- Reviewer recommends citing two highly relevant prior works that test samplers using data drawn from the prior (Geweke 2004; Cook, Gelman & Rubin 2006).
- Primary weakness: clarity of presentation—derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow and uses q(.) before it is defined.
- Supplement clarifies some points, but the main text should be self-contained and understandable without it.
- Paper needs a clear, step-by-step algorithm or procedural description showing how to apply the proposed method in practice.
- Minor: unnecessary notational distinction between unobserved variables z and theta—merge into a single variable to improve clarity.
- Minor: typographical error—missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
