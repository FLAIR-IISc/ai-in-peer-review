- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence (JSD) between an MCMC sampler and the target for debugging/validation in PPLs.  
- Builds on prior sampler-checking work (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2012) and offers a concrete, computationally feasible diagnostic tool.  
- Major clarity issue: Section 3.1’s derivation of the JSD estimator is confusing and needs significant refinement.  
- Notation is unclear—e.g., the marginal q(·) of the AIS sampler after T steps is used without explicit definition, forcing reliance on the supplement.  
- Logical flow omits key steps (role of bidirectional coupling, justification for estimator bias), making correctness hard to verify.  
- Paper should include a concise, self-contained step-by-step derivation and pseudocode/algorithm box for computing the JSD from MCMC samples.  
- Unnecessary partitioning of unobserved variables into z and θ; merging them would simplify notation without loss of generality.  
- Minor presentation errors (e.g., missing closing parenthesis on Line 165) and ambiguous phrasing detract from readability.  
- The manuscript does not clearly articulate how BDMC advances beyond Geweke’s joint test or Cook et al.’s posterior quantile validation.  
- Experiments demonstrate utility but lack depth—results are qualitative (JSD trends) without quantitative failure thresholds.  
- Need quantitative benchmarks linking JSD to sampler performance metrics (e.g., effective sample size, bias) to validate practical usefulness.  
- AIS ablation is useful but should better contextualize BDMC’s sensitivity to AIS parameters for debugging workflows.  
- Overall: compelling and potentially valuable idea, but presentation, derivation, and experimental justification require major revision.  
- Recommendation: Major Revision with concrete fixes—clarify Section 3.1, simplify notation, strengthen experiments/benchmarks, and correct minor errors.
