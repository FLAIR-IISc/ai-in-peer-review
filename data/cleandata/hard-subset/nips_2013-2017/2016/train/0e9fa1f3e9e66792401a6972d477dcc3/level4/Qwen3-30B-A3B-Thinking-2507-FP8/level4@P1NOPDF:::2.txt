

This paper applies bidirectional Monte Carlo methods to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference approaches. The authors derive lower bounds on the KL-divergence between the true posterior and the approximate distribution. The proposed technique is implemented in WebPPL and Stan. The work addresses an important problem in Bayesian inference through a novel application of bidirectional Monte Carlo, which is both timely and theoretically sound. The experimental validation is thorough, and the integration into widely used probabilistic programming frameworks significantly enhances practical utility. I recommend acceptance.
