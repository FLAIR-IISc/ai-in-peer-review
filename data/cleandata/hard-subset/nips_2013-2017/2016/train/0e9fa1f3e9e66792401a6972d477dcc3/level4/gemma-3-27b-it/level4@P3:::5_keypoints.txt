- Extends BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors (BREAD).
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and demonstrates it on multiple models/datasets.
- Leverages BDMC’s log marginal likelihood bounds to assess MCMC sample quality.
- Provides a theoretically grounded upper bound on posterior discrepancy.
- Addresses the need for quantitative MCMC diagnostics beyond common heuristics.
- Requires exact posterior samples, forcing reliance on simulated data and strong assumptions.
- Protocol of estimating hyperparameters from real data, simulating, then inferring may fail under model misspecification or complex posteriors.
- Assuming few MCMC steps from estimated hyperparameters approximate exact samples lacks strong justification.
- Validation is limited (only five models), insufficient to demonstrate robustness across diverse scenarios.
- Lacks comprehensive empirical comparison to established diagnostics (e.g., Gelman-Rubin, trace plots), especially for multimodal posteriors.
- Needs a more detailed comparison with related BDMC-based work (Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka).
- Practical implementation in WebPPL and Stan is a valuable contribution for practitioners.
- Demonstrated utility for comparing model representations suggests potential to guide modeling choices.
- Overall: technically solid and promising but weakened by reliance on heuristics, strong assumptions, and limited validation; reviewer rates weak accept.
- Recommendation: stronger, broader simulations and direct comparisons to existing diagnostics to improve the paper.
