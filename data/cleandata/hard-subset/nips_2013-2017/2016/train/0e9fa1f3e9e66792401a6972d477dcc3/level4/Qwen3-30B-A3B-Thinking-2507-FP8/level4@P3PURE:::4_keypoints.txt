- Introduces AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS/rAIS to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood.  
- Shows the gap between these bounds bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate-sample distribution, giving a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Method is inherently limited to synthetic data drawn from the joint, but experiments indicate analyses on synthetic data predict behavior on real-world data.  
- Demonstrates applications for model selection and debugging.  
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and provides a useful method with reasonably compelling experiments.  
- Major issue: Section 3 is harder to follow than the rest; the transition into Section 3.1 is jarring and introduces variables/equations without justification.  
- Crucial clarity needed: if the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper must explicitly claim this contribution.  
- Major omission: the paper never explicitly defines “AutoBDMC”; the term and the precise procedure (e.g., using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence) should be defined early.  
- Inconsistent phrasing: sentences like “this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC” presume AutoBDMC was defined earlier, which it was not.  
- Minor error: Line 149 likely uses p(θ, z | y) but should refer to the joint p(θ, z, y).  
- Notational inconsistency: the paper alternates between y and D for data/observations, causing confusion.  
- Supplement concerns: WebPPL code should specify required versions and integration instructions (ideally released as an npm package); a similar packaged Stan implementation would be desirable.
