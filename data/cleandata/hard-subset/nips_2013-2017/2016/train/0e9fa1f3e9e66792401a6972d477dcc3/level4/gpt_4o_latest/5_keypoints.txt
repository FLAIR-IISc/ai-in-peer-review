- Paper proposes selecting MCMC schemes by choosing the one that yields the tightest Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds on the log marginal likelihood for a fixed computational budget.
- BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound; authors introduce heuristics to bypass this requirement.
- Heuristic: run diagnostics on posteriors from synthetic data generated using a presumed representative hyperparameter η_real, since high-quality samples are easier to obtain for synthetic posteriors.
- Methodology is mainly tailored to AIS and SMC samplers and focuses exclusively on marginal-likelihood–based diagnostics.
- The idea of using BMC lower bounds for MCMC convergence is intriguing and theoretically motivated.
- Major concern 1: assuming prior knowledge of a representative η_real presumes a previously converged MCMC and that the posterior can be summarized by a single value—strong, unjustified assumptions.
- Major concern 2: assuming synthetic-data posteriors have similar MCMC convergence properties to the original posterior is unjustified and vulnerable to model misspecification, effectively shifting the original problem.
- Major concern 3: assuming initializing at η_real quickly yields an almost exact sample is optimistic and unsubstantiated.
- Overall, the method rests on assumptions closely tied to the very convergence properties it aims to diagnose, and the limited toy examples do not demonstrate robustness to complex scenarios.
- Reviewer requests clarification and empirical evidence showing why the method handles multimodality or summary-statistic limitations better than existing diagnostics.
- Reviewer recommends including simulations comparing the proposed method to standard diagnostics (e.g., those in Section 4) to highlight cases where it provides more reliable assessments.
