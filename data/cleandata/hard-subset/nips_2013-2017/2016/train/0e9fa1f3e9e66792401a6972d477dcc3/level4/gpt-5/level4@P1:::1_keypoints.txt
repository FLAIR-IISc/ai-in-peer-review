- Paper extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC)/AIS to bound MCMC-based posterior sample quality via stochastic forward/reverse AIS producing bounds whose gap upper-bounds expected Jeffreys divergence.
- Method implemented in WebPPL and Stan, validated on toy and realistic models, with a fitted-hyperparameter protocol for real data.
- Builds on BDMC and AIS and relates to SMC, convergence diagnostics, sample-quality measures, and concurrent subjective-divergence work.
- Conceptually appealing: provides a principled, quantitative measure tied to an expected upper bound on Jeffreys divergence.
- Practical and useful: integrated into PPLs, guides modeling/algorithm choices, and can reveal implementation bugs.
- Useful for debugging: forward/reverse sandwiching serves as a clear invariant and exposed a real WebPPL bug.
- Fitted-hyperparameter protocol is thoughtful and empirically checked for consistency between simulated and real data.
- Technical soundness: clear derivation linking BDMC bounds to an expected Jeffreys divergence bound.
- Fitted-hyperparameter approach is heuristic but appropriately validated empirically.
- Major clarity issue: Section 3.1 is hard to follow; q(·) and related notation are introduced before definition.
- The paper lacks an explicit end-to-end procedure (data simulation, forward AIS, reverse initialization, computing/interpreting the bound).
- Notational clutter: splitting latent variables into z and θ is unnecessary and increases cognitive load.
- Minor typographical error (missing closing parenthesis on Line 165).
- Suggested revisions: define all qfwd/qrev distributions up front and intuitively explain marginalization inequalities.
- Suggested revisions: add a succinct algorithm box/protocol for simulated-data BREAD and fitted-hyperparameter workflow, and consolidate latent-variable notation.
- Relation to prior validation methods: should cite/discuss Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2006); methods are complementary.
