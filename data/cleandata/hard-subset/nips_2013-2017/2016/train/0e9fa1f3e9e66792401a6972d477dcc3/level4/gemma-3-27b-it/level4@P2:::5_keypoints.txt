- Addresses reliable assessment of MCMC quality by upper-bounding symmetrized KL divergence using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC).
- Core idea: use tightest BDMC bounds achievable under a computational budget as a proxy for MCMC efficiency and reliability.
- Key challenge: BDMC requires an exact sample from the target to provide a valid upper bound.
- Proposed workaround: heuristics that run diagnostics on data simulated from hyperparameters estimated from real data.
- Implemented the approach in WebPPL and Stan and validated on several models.
- Novel contribution: applying BDMC to MCMC diagnostics and integrating it into popular probabilistic programming languages.
- Practical limitation: method applies mainly to algorithms amenable to importance sampling (e.g., AIS, SMC).
- Major assumption risk: initial hyperparameter estimates and simulated-data behavior must represent the true posterior, which may fail under misspecification or multimodality.
- Circularity concern: assessing convergence depends on an already-converged or presumed-converged MCMC run to provide hyperparameter estimates.
- Validation is limited in scope; consistency between real and simulated data is shown for tested cases but not broadly generalizable.
- Lacks thorough comparative analysis with established MCMC diagnostics and limited discussion of advantages in challenging scenarios (e.g., multimodality).
- Positive evidence: theoretical grounding is sound, practical utility demonstrated (including finding a WebPPL bug).
- Recommendation: weak reject; encourage more extensive validation, comparative experiments, and stronger justification of key assumptions.
