- Paper extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference by bounding symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Method (BREAD) runs annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains forward and reverse on simulated data to obtain an expected upper bound.  
- BREAD is implemented in WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility to practitioners.  
- Validated on multiple models/datasets and used to compare model representations in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Theoretical contribution: extension of BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence with rigorous mathematical analysis.  
- Practical relevance: provides a quantitative, information-theoretic diagnostic for black-box inference quality.  
- Experiments show consistency between real and simulated data and demonstrate BREAD’s utility; a WebPPL bug was discovered via the method.  
- Authors acknowledge limitation that BREAD requires simulated data and propose a protocol for applying it to real datasets.  
- Main weakness: protocol for real data depends on an approximate posterior sample; sensitivity to its quality needs deeper investigation.  
- Related work discussion is limited and should include more recent convergence diagnostics and posterior evaluation techniques.  
- Experimental presentation could be improved with more detailed statistical comparisons and error bars.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with theoretical and practical impact; strengths outweigh minor weaknesses; recommendation to accept.
