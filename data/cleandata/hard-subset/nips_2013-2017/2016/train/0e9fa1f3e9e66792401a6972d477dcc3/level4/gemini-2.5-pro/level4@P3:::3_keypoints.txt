- Paper proposes BREAD: uses forward/reverse AIS (BDMC) gap to upper-bound expected Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors and implements it in WebPPL and Stan.

- Strength: Simple, elegant idea linking BDMC gap to posterior divergence, offering a practical diagnostic for MCMC inference quality.

- Strength: Broadly applicable and easy to implement, with potential high impact for probabilistic programming developers and users.

- Strength: Demonstrated utility by comparing collapsed vs. uncollapsed matrix factorization parameterizations.

- Strength: Successfully used to detect a real bug in WebPPL, showing practical validation power.

- Weakness: Novelty and technical depth are limited — main theoretical contribution (BDMC gap → Jeffreys divergence) is brief and much of the paper reviews existing AIS/BDMC.

- Weakness: Fails to cite/discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related conceptually and technically to the forward/reverse AIS procedure.

- Weakness: Does not analyze the critical dependence on the annealing schedule (choice/number of τ_t); lacks methods for adaptively choosing schedules to balance tightness vs. cost.

- Weakness: Experiments are illustrative but not systematic; missing studies showing how bounds tighten with more annealing steps and corresponding computational cost.

- Comment: Section 3.1 needs clearer definitions (e.g., precise meaning of v as sampled AIS variables) and clearer statements of which estimators appear inside expectations.

- Comment: Equations (6)–(11) should explicitly state the distribution/estimator used in expectations; clarify the step using marginalization to reduce KL by naming the property.

- Experimental detail: Must state how annealing schedules τ_t were chosen (appendix hints linear schedule but this should be in main text).

- Experimental detail: Clarify what "AIS steps" or "HMC / No-U-Turn steps" on x-axes represent — number of intermediate distributions T or number of MCMC transitions per temperature.

- Experimental detail: Figure captions should be self-contained, define acronyms like RAIS, and explain collapsed/uncollapsed conventions (e.g., (u)c(R)AIS).

- Presentation: Improve figure readability (axis label/tick font sizes).

- Typo/citation: Correct No-U-Turn Sampler citation to Hoffman and Gelman, 2014.

- Conclusion: Core idea is valuable and promising, but paper needs deeper technical analysis, inclusion of Tempered Transitions discussion, clearer derivations, and more rigorous experimental evaluation before strong acceptance.
