- Novel method: applies bidirectional Monte Carlo to compute tight, efficient lower bounds on KL divergence for evaluating MCMC posterior inference quality.
- Theoretical soundness: BMC-based lower bounds provide a principled, quantitative measure of discrepancy between target posterior and MCMC approximation.
- Practical relevance: Implemented in WebPPL and Stan, making the technique immediately usable by practitioners.
- Computational concerns: Needs more detail on computational complexity and scalability with parameter dimensionality and dataset size.
- Comparison gap: Lacks direct empirical comparisons to standard diagnostics (e.g., Gelman-Rubin, ESS); demonstrating cases where KL detects issues they miss would strengthen the claim.
- Practitioner guidance: Needs recommendations on interpreting KL lower-bound magnitudes (what values indicate acceptable convergence).
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance based on significance, novelty, technical quality, and utility.
