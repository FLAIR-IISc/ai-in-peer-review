- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target for debugging/assessing MCMC in PPLs.  
- The idea is compelling and potentially valuable because existing MCMC diagnostics have notable limitations.  
- BDMC appears useful for identifying sampler convergence issues in probabilistic programming implementations.  
- Prior works by Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2006/2012) are closely related and should be acknowledged and connected to this approach.  
- Section 3.1 is hard to follow: key elements like q(.) are introduced without explicit definition (likely the AIS marginal) and need clarity.  
- The main text should be self-contained and include a concise, step-by-step implementation description rather than forcing readers to consult the supplement.  
- Minor suggestion: combining z and θ into a single latent variable would simplify presentation since they always appear together.  
- Minor typo: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
