The paper primarily reviews previously published results on bidirectional Monte Carlo and demonstrates their implementation in two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan. It does not appear to introduce any substantive new ideas or methodological advances; the contribution is mainly the application of established techniques within these systems. While these implementations may be useful to practitioners, the work feels incremental and below the novelty threshold expected for a NIPS main-track paper. A specialized MCMC or probabilistic programming workshop would likely be a more suitable venue.
