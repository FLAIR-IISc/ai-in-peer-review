- Paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target, aimed at debugging/evaluating MCMC in probabilistic programming languages.  
- Reviewer supports the idea, noting current MCMC diagnostics are problematic and the proposal is valuable for diagnosing convergence failures.  
- References [1,2] (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2006) are highly relevant because they also evaluate samplers by running them on prior-sampled data.  
- The derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow; key functions (e.g., q(.)) are used before being defined and should be explicitly stated.  
- The supplement clarifies parts of the method, but the main text should be self-contained and include a step-by-step explanation of the method’s application.  
- Minor suggestion: the distinction between unobserved variables z and theta appears unnecessary and could be simplified to reduce clutter.  
- Minor editorial issue: a missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
