- Paper extends BDMC to assess MCMC posterior inference quality by comparing forward (AIS) and reverse (RAIS) runs; the forward–reverse gap upper-bounds the expected symmetrized KL between AIS sample distribution and true posterior.  
- Integrates method into WebPPL and Stan and proposes a fitted-hyperparameter simulation protocol to transfer insights to real data.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple, broadly applicable diagnostic that is easy to implement and impactful for probabilistic programming.  
- Strength: Practical Stan and WebPPL implementations increase likelihood of adoption.  
- Strength: The fitted-hyperparameter simulation strategy and consistency checks are pragmatic and reassuring.  
- Strength: Experiments (matrix factorization, debugging) provide actionable insights and show real utility.  
- Concern: Main novelty is recognizing the bound on symmetrized KL; contribution is arguably incremental over prior BDMC work and Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed/unclear.  
- Important omission: No discussion of Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (TT), which is closely related and would clarify connections to acceptance ratios and thermodynamic integration.  
- Annealing schedule issue: The bound strongly depends on βt; large Δβ harms performance and comparisons across operators can be unreliable if gap is large.  
- Request: Explain how βt is chosen in experiments and clarify whether AutoBDMC adapts T/βt or only computes gaps.  
- Request: Provide systematic study of how bounds vary with Δβ and T and how this affects operator comparisons.  
- Quality: Technically sound overall; fitted-hyperparameter protocol and experiments are reasonable; note caveat that using approximate posterior samples for reverse initialization breaks strict bound.  
- Clarity: Definition of v is loose—explicitly define intermediate states and xT as the approximate sample; avoid overloading notation across sweeps.  
- Clarity: Use consistent labels for forward/reverse (fwd/rev vs for/back) and state conventions clearly.  
- Clarity: Make estimator notation explicit in Eqs. (5–7),(9–11) by writing expectations of log R̂ for each sweep and defining R̂.  
- Clarity: Correct Equation (6) where KL arguments appear identical; use intended distributions from Eqs. (4) and (10).  
- Clarity: Define AutoBDMC clearly (what is automated: bound computation vs schedule/step adaptation).  
- Clarity: Define acronyms in figures (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS), increase axis/tick label sizes, and state experimental details (βt schedule, meaning of HMC/NUTS steps, number of temperatures).  
- Originality: Moderate—neat observation about BDMC gap but broader framework and reverse AIS are prior work; fitted-hyperparameter protocol pragmatic but not novel.  
- Significance: High practical significance for probabilistic programming and MCMC diagnostics despite limited theoretical novelty.  
- Pros summary: Simple, general diagnostic; Stan/WebPPL integrations; clear protocol and empirical validation.  
- Cons summary: Limited novelty beyond BDMC, missing TT connection, and underdeveloped discussion of annealing schedules/AutoBDMC.  
- Minor: Typos—missing closing parentheses in D_KL terms in D_JS expression and NUTS citation should be “Hoffman” not “Homan.”  
- Overall: Useful, practical contribution with modest theoretical novelty; recommend acceptance pending clearer exposition, explicit TT discussion, β-schedule details, and minor fixes.
