- Introduces AutoBDMC, using Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood for assessing MCMC convergence.  
- Shows the gap between these bounds upper-bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate samples, yielding a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Method is limited to analyzing convergence on synthetic data drawn from the model joint.  
- Experimental evidence suggests synthetic-data analysis predicts MCMC performance on real-world data.  
- Demonstrates practical utility for model selection and debugging.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written overall, and experimentally convincing.  
- Section 3 (especially transition to 3.1) is hard to follow and lacks high-level motivation before introducing variables/equations.  
- The divergence bound in Section 3.1 appears to be the main contribution and should be explicitly stated as novel if so.  
- The term “AutoBDMC” is never formally defined and is used before being introduced, causing confusion.  
- Notation issues: possible error on line 149 (`p(theta, z | y)` vs `p(theta, z, y)`).  
- Inconsistent use of `y` and `D` for data/observations is confusing.  
- Supplemental code needs clearer instructions and compatibility info: specify WebPPL versions, integration steps, and consider releasing as an npm WebPPL package.  
- Provide similar packaged release or detailed usage instructions for the Stan implementation.
