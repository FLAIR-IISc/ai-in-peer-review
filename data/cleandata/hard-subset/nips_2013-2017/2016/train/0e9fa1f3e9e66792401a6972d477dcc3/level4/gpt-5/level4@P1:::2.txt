This paper leverages the recently introduced bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) framework to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference. By running annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains forward (prior to posterior) and in reverse, the authors obtain bounds on the divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples. They integrate the method into WebPPL and Stan, and demonstrate its use on several models and datasets. The work targets a timely and important problem: assessing the reliability of black-box, MCMC-based inference. The idea is interesting and well motivated, and the empirical results suggest practical value.

Strengths:
- Uses BDMC to provide quantitative, principled diagnostics for sample quality in MCMC-based posterior inference, a recognized challenge in probabilistic programming.
- Integration into WebPPL and Stan increases the potential impact and demonstrates practicality.
- The experiments illustrate that the method can inform algorithmic and modeling choices (e.g., alternative model representations).

Overall, this is a nice paper with a clear contribution to evaluating the quality of MCMC-based inference, and it addresses a hot and relevant problem. I recommend acceptance.
