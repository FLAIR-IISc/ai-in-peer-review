This paper introduces a novel approach leveraging bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampler and its target distribution. The focus on using this divergence for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations, particularly within Probabilistic Programming Languages (PPLs), is a valuable contribution. The concept of employing BDMC to identify sampler convergence issues is promising, as current MCMC diagnostics often exhibit limitations. The work builds upon and relates to existing methods for evaluating sampler performance via prior predictive checking, such as those described in [1, 2]. 

However, the derivation presented in Section 3.1 requires significant clarification. The notation `q(.)` is introduced without prior definition, and while its meaning can be inferred from context as the marginal distribution of the Approximate Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after *T* steps, this connection should be explicitly stated within the main text. While the supplementary material provides some clarification, readers should not be required to consult it for a complete understanding of the core methodology.  A more streamlined, step-by-step description of how to apply the method would greatly improve clarity and accessibility. 

Additionally, the separation of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* feels unnecessarily complex given the general setting. Since these variables consistently appear together, simplifying the notation by eliminating either *z* or *θ* could reduce clutter. Finally, a minor typographical error exists on line 165 with a missing closing parenthesis.
