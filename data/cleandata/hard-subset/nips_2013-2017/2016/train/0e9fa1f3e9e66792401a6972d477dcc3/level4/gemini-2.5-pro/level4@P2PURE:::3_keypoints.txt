- Proposes using the gap between BDMC stochastic lower and upper bounds as a metric to evaluate MCMC transition operators.  
- Implements BDMC for Stan and WebPPL and presents a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- Strength: the BDMC-gap idea is simple, elegant, easy to implement, and has high potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Weakness: the paper spends too much space on existing BDMC background, leaving novel contributions in sections 3.1–3.2 feeling rushed and underdeveloped.  
- Novelty concern: the main new insight (BDMC-gap upper-bounds Jensen-Shannon divergence to AIS posterior) is useful but not clearly a strong, original contribution.  
- Major omission: fails to cite or discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which closely relates to BDMC and shares the same forward/reverse annealing structure.  
- Important connection: Neal’s TT acceptance ratio equals the ratio of BDMC’s stochastic bounds and Section 3.3 of Neal interprets these as bounding log partition function differences.  
- Annealing schedule critique: the paper understates the critical role of Δβ (step size); large Δβ inflates the BDMC gap while tiny Δβ is wasteful—this sensitivity must be discussed.  
- AutoBDMC ambiguity: “Auto” in AutoBDMC is not defined—unclear if it’s just the evaluation protocol or includes an adaptive annealing scheme.  
- Suggestion: introduce/adopt an adaptive scheme to tighten Δβ until the BDMC gap is acceptably small and study its effect systematically.  
- Experimental weakness: no systematic study of how lower/upper bounds vary with annealing step size; experiments should include this analysis.  
- Clarity issue: definition of v (“all variables sampled in AIS before the final stage”) is ambiguous—should specify whether it means {z_{1:T-1}, θ_{1:T-1}} from one or both sweeps.  
- Terminology issue: inconsistent labels for AIS sweeps (“rev”/“fwd” vs. “for”/“back”) create confusion—use consistent notation.  
- Notational/derivation issues: p̂(y) is undefined in equations (5–7) and (9–11), making the derivations hard to follow; clarify whether these are expectations of log R̂_rev and log R̂_fwd.  
- Possible error: Equation (6) seems incorrect—KL is computed between identical distributions; the right-hand distribution in the KL term likely should be q_back(v | z, θ, y).  
- Experimental method omission: annealing schedule βt is not described in the main text; appendix suggests a uniform schedule (β step = 1/options.steps)—this should be stated up front.  
- Figure clarifications needed: “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” in Figures 2–3 is unclear (is it BDMC T?), RAIS acronym and variants (u)c(R)AIS are undefined, and axis fonts are too small.  
- Typos/corrections: D_JS expression is missing two closing parentheses for D_KL terms; NUTS citation should be “Hoffman,” not “Homan.”  
- Overall recommendation: idea is valuable and likely acceptable if authors fix clarity, include Neal/TT related work, address annealing schedule issues, and strengthen experiments.
