- Paper introduces a general framework using BDMC (two AIS sweeps) to evaluate MCMC transition operators via the gap between stochastic bounds on the marginal likelihood.  
- Technical contributions: implementations of BDMC for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for assessing posterior inference quality.  
- Strength: method is simple, practical, and potentially impactful for probabilistic programming systems as a standard evaluation tool.  
- Weakness: limited novelty and technical depth; much space is spent reiterating BDMC from an arXiv submission rather than new contributions.  
- Presentation: Sections 3.1 and 3.2 are rushed and suffer from clarity and writing-quality issues.  
- The observation that the bound gap upper-bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence is insightful but not entirely novel.  
- Important missing citation and discussion of Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related to BDMC’s forward/reverse sweeps.  
- Neal’s TT connection could inform choice of β_t (Δβ) and draw parallels between acceptance ratios and stochastic bounds.  
- Step size Δβ critically affects BDMC performance; if bounds are far apart, Δβ should be reduced—this deserves systematic study.  
- “AutoBDMC” is not clearly defined; unclear whether it means adaptive Δβ, fixed hyperparameter scheme, or something else.  
- Clarity issues: definition of vector v (“variables sampled in AIS before the final stage”) is vague and ambiguous given two AIS sweeps.  
- Notation inconsistency: labels like “rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back” are confusing and possibly accidental.  
- Equations (5–7) and (9–11) lack a clear definition of the estimator hat p(y); expectations of log R estimators should be clarified.  
- Equation (6) appears incorrect as written—the KL divergence terms are nonsensical; correct distributions likely involve p(z,θ|y) and q_back(v|z,θ,y).  
- Experimental setup: choice and parameterization of β_t (number of steps T) is unclear and should be explicitly stated.  
- Figures: unclear whether “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” refers to BDMC HMC steps; axes/tick labels are too small and reduce readability.  
- Terminology: acronym RAIS (reverse AIS) and variants (u)c(R)AIS are undefined and confusing; clarify (un)collapsed variants.  
- Recommendation: authors should address clarity issues, related-work omissions, and provide systematic experiments on Δβ to strengthen the paper; reviewer would consider acceptance if fixed.  
- Typos: missing closing parentheses in DKL terms in the DJS expression; NUTS citation misspells “Hoffman” as “Homan.”
