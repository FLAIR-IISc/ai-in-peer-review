This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a particular focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). I appreciate the idea of utilizing bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose failures of a sampler to converge, as this concept appears to be potentially quite useful given the limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics.

Two relevant references that also test the ability of MCMC samplers by running them on data sampled from the prior are [1,2]. In Section 3.1, I found the derivation to be challenging to follow, primarily because the notation q(.) is used before its definition. Based on the context, it seems to represent the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after T steps; however, this should be explicitly stated. While the supplement provides some clarification, the main text should be self-explanatory, and the reader should not need to consult the supplement to understand the core concepts.

In general, it would be beneficial to have a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the method from start to finish. Currently, the reader must piece together the information, which can be cumbersome. A minor suggestion is to eliminate the distinction between unobserved variables z and theta, as they always appear together in a general setting. This would help reduce clutter and improve readability. Additionally, there is a minor typographical error on Line 165, where a close parenthesis is missing.
