This paper presents an application of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate the reliability of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference in probabilistic programming languages. The authors extend the BDMC technique to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples. They integrate their method, called Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD), into two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan, and validate it on several models and datasets.

The main idea of the paper is to use BDMC to evaluate the accuracy of MCMC-based posterior inference algorithms. The authors provide a thorough review of the background material, including annealed importance sampling (AIS), stochastic lower bounds on the log partition function ratio, and reverse AIS. They also discuss the relationship between their method and previous work on diagnosing Markov chain convergence.

The paper has several strengths. First, the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the BDMC technique and its application to MCMC inference. Second, they demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on several models and datasets, including a matrix factorization model and a mixture-of-Gaussians clustering model. Third, they show that their method can be used to guide the design of inference algorithms and to debug implementations.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. First, the authors do not provide any new theoretical insights or contributions to the field of probabilistic programming. The BDMC technique has been previously published, and the authors' contribution is primarily in implementing and applying it to probabilistic programming languages. Second, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the results and a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the method.
