- Introduces AutoBDMC using BDMC/AIS to produce stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood and uses their gap to upper-bound Jensen–Shannon divergence as a scalar MCMC convergence diagnostic.
- Experimental evidence shows performance on synthetic data drawn from the joint is predictive of behavior on real-world data; method demonstrated for model selection and debugging.
- Overall assessment: well written, well motivated, genuinely useful method with a reasonably compelling experimental evaluation.
- Key strength: reduction of convergence assessment to a single, interpretable scalar bound via the BDMC gap.
- Applications to model selection and debugging are practical and persuasive.
- Section 3 is substantially harder to follow than the rest of the paper; transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces variables/equations without sufficient motivation or context.
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper should state this explicitly as it is central to the contribution.
- The paper never explicitly defines “AutoBDMC”; it should be defined clearly and early (using AIS and rAIS within BDMC to analyze MCMC convergence).
- Reliance on synthetic data drawn from the joint is a real limitation; experiments partially address this but the caveat and scope of predictive claims should be emphasized.
- Minor correction: Line 149 likely should read p(θ, z, y) instead of p(θ, z | y).
- Notation inconsistency: alternate use of y and D for observed data—standardize to a single symbol.
- Reproducibility: WebPPL supplement should specify compatible versions and installation/integration instructions; consider releasing as an npm-installable WebPPL package and provide a similar packaged release for the Stan implementation.
- Suggested revisions: clarify AutoBDMC definition, smooth exposition in Section 3 (especially 3.1), explicitly state novelty of divergence bound, standardize notation, and improve code packaging/documentation.
