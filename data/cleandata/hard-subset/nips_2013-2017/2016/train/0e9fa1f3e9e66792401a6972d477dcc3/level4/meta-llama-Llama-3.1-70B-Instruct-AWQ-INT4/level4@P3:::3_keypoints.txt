- Proposes BREAD: extends BDMC to evaluate reliability of MCMC by upper-bounding symmetrized KL between true posterior and approximate samples using AIS reverse/forward chains.
- Uses annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains to obtain upper bounds on divergence and assesses posterior-sample accuracy.
- Implements BDMC/BREAD for Stan and WebPPL and provides an evaluation protocol for posterior inference quality.
- Experimental results validate the method and demonstrate practical usefulness in realistic settings.
- Strength: method is simple, elegant, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact.
- Strength: thorough related-work discussion, including some comparison to Tempered Transitions (TT).
- Clarity issues: some variables (e.g., v vector) are undefined and AIS sweep labels are inconsistent.
- Missing definitions: estimator hat{p}(y) is not defined in equations, making derivations hard to follow.
- Methodological gap: connection to Tempered Transitions is noted but not fully explored.
- Important omission: no discussion of choice of beta_t (temperature schedule), which affects performance.
- Originality concern: main novelty (difference between lower and upper bounds bounding Jensen–Shannon divergence) is helpful but not entirely new.
- Overall judgment: promising and useful paper but needs clearer exposition and deeper exploration of novelty and TT connections.
- Scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 6/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 8/10.
- Recommendations: fix clarity/notation, explore TT connection, discuss beta_t selection, and provide additional experimental validation.
