This paper proposes a method to measure the reliability of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference using bidirectional Monte Carlo. The authors extend the recently introduced bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference algorithms. By running annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains both from prior to posterior and vice versa on simulated data, they upper bound in expectation the symmetrized KL divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples.

The main idea of the paper is related to previous work on bidirectional Monte Carlo [GGA15] and importance sampling [Nea01]. However, the authors provide a new application of these techniques to evaluate the accuracy of MCMC-based posterior inference methods. The method is integrated into two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan, which is a significant contribution.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its relation to previous work. The experiments are also well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The strengths of the paper are:

* The authors propose a new method to evaluate the accuracy of MCMC-based posterior inference methods using bidirectional Monte Carlo.
* The method is integrated into two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan.
* The experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

The weaknesses of the paper are:

* The method is limited to certain algorithms such as AIS or sequential Monte Carlo (SMC), which may not be applicable to all MCMC-based posterior inference methods.
* The authors do not provide a comprehensive comparison with other methods for evaluating the accuracy of MCMC-based posterior inference methods.
