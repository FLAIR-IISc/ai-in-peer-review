- Proposes AutoBDMC, using BDMC and AIS/rAIS to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood for MCMC convergence assessment.
- Shows gap between bounds connects to Jensen–Shannon divergence (JSD) between true posterior and MCMC sample distribution, yielding a scalar convergence metric.
- Demonstrates empirically that AutoBDMC analysis on synthetic data predicts performance on real-world datasets; useful for model selection and MCMC debugging.
- Paper is overall clear, well-motivated, and experimentally convincing.
- Section 3 is hard to follow; transition to Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces variables/equations without sufficient context or motivation.
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, it should be explicitly highlighted as the paper’s core contribution.
- Critical omission: no clear, early definition of “AutoBDMC”; readers need a precise statement of what the term encompasses before Section 3.2.
- Notational error: Line 149 likely should use p(θ, z, y) instead of p(θ, z | y).
- Notational inconsistency: paper switches between y and D for observed data; maintain consistent notation.
- Reproducibility issue: WebPPL supplemental code needs stated compatible versions and clear integration instructions; packaging as an npm WebPPL package recommended.
- Reproducibility issue: Stan code should also be packaged and documented for community use.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying Section 3, formally defining AutoBDMC, and packaging/documenting supplementary code.
