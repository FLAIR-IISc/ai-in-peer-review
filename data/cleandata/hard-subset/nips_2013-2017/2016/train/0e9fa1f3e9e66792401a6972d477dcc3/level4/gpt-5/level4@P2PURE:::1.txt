This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the distribution induced by an MCMC sampler and the intended target distribution, with the goal of debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages. I find the idea of using BDMC to diagnose non-convergence compelling and potentially very useful, given the well-known limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics. Two closely related references are [1,2], which likewise assess MCMC performance by running samplers on data drawn from the prior.

Section 3.1: I found the derivation difficult to follow. In particular, q(·) is used before it is defined. From context, it appears to denote the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps, but this should be stated explicitly. While the supplement helps, the main text should be understandable without it. More generally, it would be helpful to include a concise, end-to-end description of how to apply the method; as written, the reader must piece together the procedure.

Minor notes:
- Splitting the unobserved variables into z and theta feels needlessly cluttered in this general setup—there is no substantive distinction between them here, and they always appear together. Consider eliminating one of z or theta.
- Line 165: missing closing parenthesis.
