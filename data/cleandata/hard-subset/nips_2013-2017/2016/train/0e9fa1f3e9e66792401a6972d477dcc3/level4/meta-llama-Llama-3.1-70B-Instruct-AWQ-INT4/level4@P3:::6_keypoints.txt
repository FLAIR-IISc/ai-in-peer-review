- Applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC posterior inference and extends BDMC to bound the symmetrized KL divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of concepts and techniques.  
- Provides thorough background on BDMC, annealed importance sampling (AIS), and stochastic lower bounds on log partition function ratios.  
- Discusses related work on diagnosing MCMC convergence and automated MCMC parameter configuration.  
- Main contribution: integrates BDMC-based method (BREAD) into WebPPL and Stan and proposes a protocol to diagnose inference quality on real-world data.  
- Experiments are thorough and well-designed; validate simulated data as a proxy, show hyperparameter fitting yields representative samples, and demonstrate BREAD’s effectiveness and model-representation tradeoffs.  
- Criticism: lacks original ideas—techniques are largely based on previously published results.  
- Criticism: applying BDMC to WebPPL and Stan, while useful, may not be a substantial enough contribution for NIPS; possibly more suitable for an MCMC workshop or probabilistic programming venue.  
- Summary scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 4/10, Significance 6/10.
