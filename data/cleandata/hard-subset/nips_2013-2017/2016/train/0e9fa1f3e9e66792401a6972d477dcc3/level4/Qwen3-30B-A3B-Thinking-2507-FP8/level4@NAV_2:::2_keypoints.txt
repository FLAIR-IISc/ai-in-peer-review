- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Introduces BREAD, which uses reverse annealed importance sampling to produce rigorous upper bounds on that divergence.  
- Method applies to AIS and SMC-based MCMC algorithms, covering many common inference schemes.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan for practical validation and tooling.  
- Four main contributions: theoretical Jeffreys divergence bound, empirical accuracy on toy distributions, a protocol for applying BREAD to real data via simulation/hyperparameter fitting, and validation across multiple probabilistic models.  
- Strong theoretical derivation with empirical validation where exact calculations are possible.  
- Presents a well-justified experimental protocol for extending bounds to real-world data.  
- Demonstrates practical utility, including insights on model representation (collapsed vs uncollapsed) and discovery of a WebPPL bug.  
- Clearly written and organized with sufficient technical detail for reproducibility.  
- Properly situates BREAD relative to prior BDMC work and related MCMC diagnostics, emphasizing fewer assumptions about the posterior than some previous methods.  
- High significance: provides an automatic, rigorous way to assess black-box inference quality, valuable for probabilistic programming and non-expert users.  
- Limitations: requires access to exact posterior samples (so primarily applies to simulated-data cases despite the proposed protocol for real data).  
- Limitation: computational overhead from running both forward and reverse AIS chains may be prohibitive for very large models.  
- Limitation: paper would benefit from more detailed comparisons with alternative convergence diagnostics.  
- Overall strong recommendation for acceptance due to theoretical novelty, practical integrations, and demonstrated impact.
