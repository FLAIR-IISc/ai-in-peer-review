- Proposes AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS/rAIS to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood and bound JS divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- The gap between bounds yields a single scalar quantifying MCMC convergence.  
- Validated on synthetic data from the joint distribution; authors present evidence that synthetic analysis predicts real-world behavior.  
- Demonstrates utility for model selection and debugging with reasonably compelling experiments.  
- Overall paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and the method appears valuable.  
- Section 3 is harder to follow; the transition to Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces definitions/equations without sufficient context.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, this novelty should be explicitly stated since it is central to the paper.  
- The paper lacks a clear, explicit definition of “AutoBDMC”; it should state precisely that it is the application of AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence.  
- The term “AutoBDMC” should be well-defined before discussing challenges in Section 3.2.  
- Minor error: line 149 likely has p(theta, z | y) but should be p(theta, z, y).  
- Notational inconsistencies (switching between y and D for data) cause occasional confusion.  
- Supplementary WebPPL code needs compatible WebPPL version info and integration instructions; releasing as an npm-installable WebPPL package would be ideal.  
- A similar package release for the Stan implementation would be highly appreciated.
