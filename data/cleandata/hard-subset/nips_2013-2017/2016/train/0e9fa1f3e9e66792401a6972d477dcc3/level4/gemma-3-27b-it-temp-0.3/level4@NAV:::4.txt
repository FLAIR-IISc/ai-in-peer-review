This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a novel method for evaluating MCMC convergence. The approach leverages the Bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to calculate stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood, employing annealed importance sampling as a core component. The authors demonstrate that the difference between these bounds provides a quantifiable limit on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution represented by the MCMC samples, yielding a single scalar metric for assessing convergence. While the method is initially validated on synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the paper provides evidence suggesting its analyses on synthetic datasets correlate with observed behavior in real-world applications. Furthermore, the authors illustrate the utility of AutoBDMC for model selection and diagnostic purposes. 

Overall, I found the paper to be well-written and clearly motivated, presenting a potentially valuable contribution to the field. The experimental results are generally convincing. However, I experienced some difficulty following the technical details in Section 3, particularly the transition to Section 3.1, which begins with definitions of variables and equations without sufficient contextualization. If the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 represents a novel result – as I believe it does – this should be explicitly highlighted, as the paper’s core contribution appears to center on this finding. 

A key ambiguity is the lack of a clear definition of “AutoBDMC” itself. While I interpret it as the application of annealed importance sampling (AIS) and reverse AIS (rAIS) to the problem of MCMC convergence analysis, the paper never explicitly states this. Consequently, the reference to “AutoBDMC” in Section 3.2 feels premature without a prior, formal definition. 

Minor points include: Line 149, where p(theta, z | y) may be more accurately represented as p(theta, z, y). Additionally, the inconsistent use of ‘y’ and ‘D’ to denote data/observations introduced some confusion. Regarding the supplemental WebPPL code, it would be beneficial to specify the compatible WebPPL version(s) and provide instructions for integration with WebPPL distributions. Ideally, releasing the code as a readily installable WebPPL package via npm would greatly enhance usability. A similar package release for the Stan implementation would also be highly valuable.
