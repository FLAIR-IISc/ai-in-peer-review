- Paper proposes using the gap between BDMC’s AIS lower and upper bounds as a performance measure for MCMC transition operators.  
- Technical contributions: BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol to evaluate posterior-inference quality.  
- Strength: simple approach with potentially high impact for diagnosing convergence in probabilistic programming systems.  
- Concern about novelty and depth: the Jensen–Shannon upper-bound observation may not be entirely new.  
- Important related work missing: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (TT) performs similar reverse/forward sweeps and should be cited.  
- TT analogy raises methodological questions about choice of β_t schedule and the reliability of comparisons when the bound gap is large.  
- Clarity problems: vector v is undefined, labels are inconsistent, and some estimators are not defined.  
- Experimental weaknesses: need systematic study of how lower/upper bounds change with MCMC step size.  
- “Auto” in AutoBDMC is unclear; propose a clear schedule (e.g., iteratively decreasing Δβ) to ensure reliable inference.  
- Overall: method is promising but requires clarification, related-work fixes, and stronger experiments before acceptance.
