- Introduces BREAD, a method using BDMC and AIS to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds for log marginal likelihood to assess MCMC convergence.
- Shows the gap between bounds upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate samples, yielding a single scalar convergence diagnostic.
- Clear, concise summary and relation to prior MCMC convergence diagnostics and probabilistic programming.
- Detailed description and application of BREAD to simulated and real-world data with reasonably compelling experiments.
- Paper is well-written, readable, and well-organized, making technical details accessible.
- Major weakness: authors never explicitly define BREAD, obscuring novelty and contribution.
- Notation inconsistency: switches between y and D for data/observations, causing confusion.
- Supplemental WebPPL code lacks version/integration instructions and should be packaged (e.g., npm) for easier use.
- Qualitative scores: strong on Clarity and Originality, could improve on Quality due to definitional and notational issues.
- Significance is high, with potential impact on probabilistic programming and MCMC diagnostics.
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on revisions addressing definitions, notation, and code distribution.
- Suggested revisions: provide an explicit definition of BREAD, enforce consistent notation, and improve/release WebPPL code as an installable package.
