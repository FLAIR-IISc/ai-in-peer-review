

This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, with a focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The core idea of leveraging BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures is promising, as existing MCMC diagnostics remain problematic. The approach aligns closely with relevant prior work [1, 2], which similarly tests MCMC samplers by running them on data generated from the prior.

Section 3.1 contains a derivation that is difficult to follow. Specifically, the notation $q(\cdot)$ is introduced without definition; from context, it appears to denote the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after $T$ steps, but this must be explicitly stated. While the supplement clarifies some points, the main text should be self-contained and not require supplementary reading for comprehension. Additionally, the paper would benefit from a clear, step-by-step exposition of how to apply the method end-to-end—readers must currently infer the procedure from scattered details.

Minor issues: The partitioning of unobserved variables into $z$ and $\theta$ is unnecessarily cluttered, as $z$ and $\theta$ represent identical latent variables in a general setting (they always co-occur), making the distinction superfluous. Line 165 contains a missing closing parenthesis.
