This paper introduces a novel application of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) for estimating the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampler and its target distribution. The focus on utilizing this approach for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations within Probabilistic Programming Languages (PPLs) is a valuable contribution, as current MCMC diagnostics often present limitations. The proposed method aligns with and builds upon related work, such as the approaches in [1,2] which assess sampler performance by evaluating their behavior on prior samples. 

However, Section 3.1 contains a derivation that is difficult to follow. Specifically, the notation q(.) is introduced without prior definition; while the context suggests it represents the marginal distribution obtained after T steps of an Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler, this connection should be explicitly stated. Although clarification can be found in the supplementary material, the main text should be self-contained. A more comprehensive, step-by-step description of the method’s application would significantly improve clarity and accessibility. 

A minor point regarding notation: the separation of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* feels unnecessary given the general setting. As these variables are always considered jointly, simplifying the notation by eliminating either *z* or *θ* could reduce clutter. Finally, a closing parenthesis is missing on line 165.
