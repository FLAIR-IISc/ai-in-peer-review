This manuscript introduces AutoBDMC, a novel approach for evaluating MCMC convergence. By leveraging the Bidirectional Monte Carlo method, the authors derive stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood, utilizing annealed importance sampling. A key insight is that the discrepancy between these bounds serves as an upper bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the approximate sample distribution, providing a scalar metric for assessing convergence. While the method is primarily applicable to synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors provide empirical evidence suggesting its analysis on such data can be predictive of real-world data behavior. Furthermore, they demonstrate the method's utility in model selection and debugging. Overall, the paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with the proposed method appearing to be a valuable contribution. The experimental evaluation is also reasonably convincing. However, Section 3 is somewhat more challenging to follow, particularly the transition to Section 3.1, which introduces variables and equations without adequate motivation. If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is indeed a novel result, this should be explicitly stated, as it seems to be a crucial contribution of the paper. A clear definition of AutoBDMC is also lacking; based on the content, it appears to refer to the approach of using AIS and rAIS for MCMC convergence analysis, but this should be explicitly stated. Minor issues include a potential typo in Line 149, where p(theta, z | y) might be more accurately written as p(theta, z, y). Additionally, the paper's notation is occasionally inconsistent, switching between y and D to denote data or observations. The supplemental WebPPL code would benefit from information on compatible versions and integration instructions. Ideally, releasing the code as a WebPPL package or a similar release for the Stan version would be highly appreciated.
