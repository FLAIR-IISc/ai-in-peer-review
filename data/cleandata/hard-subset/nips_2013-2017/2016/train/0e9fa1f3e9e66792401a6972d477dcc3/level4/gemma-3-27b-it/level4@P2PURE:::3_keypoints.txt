- Paper proposes using BDMC (bidirectional AIS) gap as a generic performance measure for MCMC transition operators, with implementations for Stan and WebPPL and an evaluation protocol for posterior inference quality.
- Strength: method is simple and easy to implement, likely high impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Core technical contribution: gap between BDMC lower and upper bounds gives an upper bound on Jensen–Shannon divergence, a useful but not entirely novel observation.
- Major related-work omission: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is not cited despite strong conceptual overlap.
- Tempered Transitions (TT) uses reverse and forward sweeps similar to BDMC and links the acceptance ratio to the ratio of BDMC stochastic bounds, suggesting deeper connections.
- Choice of βt (Δβ steps) is critical: large steps hurt acceptance/mixing, small steps waste computation, and BDMC bound gap depends on Δβ—authors should study this systematically.
- Comparisons between MCMC operators may be unreliable when the log bound gap exceeds ~1 nat; a schedule to reduce Δβ (AutoBDMC) would be helpful.
- “AutoBDMC” is never clearly defined—its functionality and whether it adaptively adjusts Δβ are unclear.
- Clarity issue: definition of vector v (“variables sampled in AIS before the final stage”) is ambiguous and inconsistent with notation across forward/reverse sweeps.
- Clarity issue: equations (5–7) and (9–11) omit definition of the estimator ĥp(y) and unclear expectations (e.g., E[log R̂_rev] vs E[log R̂_fwd]).
- Equation (6) appears incorrect as written (KL divergence uses identical distributions); the correct RHS likely involves p(z,θ|y) and q_back(v | z,θ,y).
- Experiments lack clarity on β schedule: appendix suggests step = 1/options.steps but the intended parametrization of AutoBDMC and T is not stated explicitly.
- Experimental details unclear: “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” ambiguous (are these steps within BDMC?), RAIS and variants (u)c(R)AIS are undefined in captions, and axis labels/ticks are too small.
- Minor issues/typos: missing closing parentheses in D_JS expression and Hoffman misspelled as Homan in the NUTS citation.
- Overall judgment: worthwhile paper with significant potential, but acceptance should depend on addressing clarity problems and providing a more thorough related-work discussion and empirical study of β/Δβ effects.
