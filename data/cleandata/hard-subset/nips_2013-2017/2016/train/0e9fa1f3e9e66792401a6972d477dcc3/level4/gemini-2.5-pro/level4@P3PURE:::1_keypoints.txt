- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler’s distribution and the target, aimed at debugging/evaluating MCMC in probabilistic programming languages.
- Core idea of using BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures is promising and potentially very useful given limitations of existing diagnostics.
- Method is highly relevant to prior work; authors should discuss its relationship to existing literature, specifically Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012).
- Exposition needs significant improvement: Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow and uses notation (e.g., q(.)) before definition.
- Main text should be self-contained; supplement clarification is insufficient and readers currently must piece together the full procedure.
- Paper would benefit from a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the proposed method.
- Minor: distinction between unobserved variables z and theta is unnecessary—use a single variable to simplify presentation.
- Minor: typographical error — line 165 is missing a closing parenthesis.
