- Proposes AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS/rAIS to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood for MCMC convergence assessment.
- Shows the gap between bounds provides a bound on the Jensen–Shannon divergence between true posterior and approximate samples, yielding a single scalar convergence metric.
- Method is limited to analyzing convergence on synthetic data drawn from the joint distribution.
- Empirical evidence suggests synthetic-data analysis is predictive of behavior on real-world data.
- Demonstrates utility of the method for model selection and debugging applications.
- Paper is well-motivated, readable, and experimentally reasonably compelling.
- Section 3 (especially the transition to 3.1) is hard to follow: variables and equations are introduced abruptly without sufficient motivation.
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, that novelty should be explicitly stated, as it is central to the paper’s contribution.
- The term “AutoBDMC” is never explicitly defined; it should be clearly stated (apparently refers to using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence).
- AutoBDMC’s definition should be established before discussing challenges in Section 3.2.
- Minor issues: typo on Line 149 — "p(theta, z | y)" should be "p(theta, z, y)".
- Notational inconsistencies: the paper switches between using "y" and "D" for data/observations, causing occasional confusion.
- Supplementary WebPPL code lacks compatible version and integration instructions; releasing it as an npm-installable WebPPL package is recommended.
- A similar packaged release is recommended for the Stan version of the code.
