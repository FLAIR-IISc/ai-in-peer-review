- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing), extending BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence between AIS approximate posterior and true posterior via forward/reverse AIS on simulated data.

- Key theoretical claim: the expected gap between stochastic upper and lower bounds on log marginal likelihood upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence.

- Implements the diagnostic in two popular probabilistic programming systems (WebPPL and Stan), making the method practically accessible.

- Demonstrates practical utility for model selection and debugging (e.g., comparing collapsed vs. uncollapsed matrix factorization representations).

- Addresses an important, hard problem: diagnosing MCMC convergence and sample quality in black-box inference systems.

- Provides a thoughtful protocol for applying a simulated-data technique to real problems (fit hyperparameters) and empirically validates consistency between real and simulated data.

- Strength of the paper lies in solid engineering, practical implementation, and careful experiments rather than novel theory.

- Main weakness: limited conceptual novelty—an incremental extension of prior BDMC analysis rather than a new foundational method.

- Questionable fit for a general NIPS audience: contribution reads more like a strong systems/application paper and may be better suited to a specialized workshop.

- Overall: well-executed, useful application with careful experiments, but primarily an implementation/validation of existing ideas rather than a novel methodological advance.
