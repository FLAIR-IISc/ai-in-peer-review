- Proposes a method to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler’s output and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating MCMC, especially in PPLs.  
- Using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose sampler convergence failures is a compelling, practically useful idea with strong motivation.  
- Main weaknesses are poor clarity in presentation and inadequate connection to prior work.  
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow: `q(.)` is used before being defined and should be explicitly defined in the main text.  
- The paper lacks a clear, step-by-step procedural overview or algorithm box describing how to apply the proposed method end-to-end.  
- The authors should situate their method relative to prior “joint distribution test” approaches (e.g., Geweke 2004; Cook et al.) and explain similarities/differences.  
- Notational clutter: treat `z` and `theta` jointly (unify into a single variable) unless a distinction is necessary.  
- Minor typo: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
