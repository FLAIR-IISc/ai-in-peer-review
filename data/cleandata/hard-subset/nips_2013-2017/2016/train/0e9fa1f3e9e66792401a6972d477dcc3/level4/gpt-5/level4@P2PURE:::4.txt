This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence. It leverages the recently developed Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) technique to produce stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood via annealed importance sampling (AIS). The authors show that the gap between these bounds upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, yielding a single scalar metric for diagnosing MCMC convergence. Because the approach can only analyze convergence on synthetic data generated from the joint model, the paper provides experimental evidence that results obtained on such synthetic data predict behavior on real-world datasets. The authors also illustrate how the method can be used for model selection and debugging. Overall, the paper is clear and well-motivated; the method appears useful, and the empirical evaluation is reasonably convincing.

I found Section 3 more difficult to follow than the rest of the paper. The transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt, launching into variable and equation definitions without sufficient motivation. If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel (as I believe it is), this should be stated explicitly, since much of the paper’s contribution seems to depend on it.

It is also unclear what, precisely, “AutoBDMC” denotes. The paper never explicitly defines it. My understanding is that AutoBDMC refers to using AIS and reverse AIS (rAIS) to analyze MCMC convergence. If so, this should be clearly stated early on. By the time Section 3.2 claims that “…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC,” the term should already be unambiguously defined.
