- Paper implements bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Implementation is a useful engineering contribution that lowers the barrier to using BMC.  
- The manuscript largely summarizes existing BMC methodology with no novel theoretical contributions.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and lacks depth and rigor.  
- Missing systematic comparisons to state-of-the-art samplers (e.g., NUTS) within WebPPL and Stan under comparable budgets.  
- Performance metrics are incomplete; should report R-hat, effective sample size, and runtime.  
- Benchmark selection appears arbitrary; should include models known to challenge standard MCMC.  
- Paper does not adequately discuss limitations (e.g., sensitivity to forward kernel, computational cost of KL estimator).  
- Implementation section lacks specific details on integration challenges (gradient estimation in Stan, stochastic computations in WebPPL).  
- Background could be more concise and focused on key differences from standard MCMC.  
- Presentation has minor grammatical errors and needs proofreading.  
- Recommendation: reject for NIPS; suggest a software/workshop venue or a software-description submission unless substantial theoretical or empirical improvements are added.
