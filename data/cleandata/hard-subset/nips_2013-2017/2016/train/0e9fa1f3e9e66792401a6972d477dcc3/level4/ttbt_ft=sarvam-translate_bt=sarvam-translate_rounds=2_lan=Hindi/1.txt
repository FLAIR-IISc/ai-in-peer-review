This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the J-S deviation between the MCMC sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a particular focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in PPL. I like the idea of using BDM to diagnose failure of the sampler's convergence – this idea seems potentially quite useful because existing MCMC diagnoses are actually problematic. Two very relevant references [1,2] also test the ability of the MCMC sampler by running it on pre-sampled data. Section 3.1: I found this derivation very difficult to understand. One thing is that q(.) is used even before it is defined. From the context, it seems that it represents the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after the T steps, but this should be clarified. Things become a little clearer if one reads the supplement, but the reader should not need to read the supplement to understand the main text. Generally, it would be good if it were clearly stated somewhere how to apply the method from start to finish – the reader would have to add it a little. Small notes: Breaking the unused variables into z and theta is a bit confusing, whereas in such a general setting there is no difference between z and theta – they always appear together, so why not remove z or theta?
