- Contribution: Extends BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and MCMC samples, introduces BREAD (reverse AIS) for rigorous bounds on simulated data, provides a protocol for real data, and integrates into WebPPL and Stan with validation on multiple models.

- Motivation: Fills a gap where existing diagnostics cannot rigorously quantify accuracy of approximate MCMC samples.

- Strength 1: Novel and impactful idea of using BDMC to diagnose MCMC convergence.

- Strength 2: Strong practical applicability via integration into WebPPL/Stan and thorough validation across models and datasets.

- Strength 3: Well-motivated, carefully evaluated protocol for applying BREAD to real-world data showing consistent behavior.

- Strength 4: Clear differentiation from related work with thoughtful analysis of connections.

- Weakness 1: Section 3.1 theoretical derivation is hard to follow due to unclear notation (e.g., q(.) used before definition).

- Weakness 2: Paper lacks a concise, step-by-step how-to for applying BREAD end-to-end.

- Weakness 3: Redundant/confusing notation using both z and theta for unobserved variables; should unify latent variable notation.

- Weakness 4: Minor typo (missing closing parenthesis at line 165).

- Quality: Method is technically sound with proper theoretical justification and thorough experimental validation.

- Clarity: Strong technical foundation but Section 3.1 and main-text presentation need clearer explanation so supplement isn’t required.

- Originality: Novel extension of BDMC to evaluate MCMC, clearly distinguished from prior work.

- Significance: Addresses a fundamental problem with immediate practical applications for probabilistic programming systems.

- Recommendation: Minor issues are fixable; recommend acceptance.
