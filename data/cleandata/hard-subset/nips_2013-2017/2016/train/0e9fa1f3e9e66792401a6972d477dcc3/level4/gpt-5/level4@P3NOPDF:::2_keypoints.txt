- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) to evaluate MCMC posterior inference via computable lower bounds on KL divergence; integrated into WebPPL and Stan; recommends acceptance.
- Addresses an important problem: assessing MCMC accuracy without ground truth.
- Frames evaluation clearly in terms of KL divergence to the true posterior.
- Provides a theoretical contribution: derivation and analysis of KL lower bounds that can serve as conservative, model-aware diagnostics.
- Practical impact: tooling integrated into popular probabilistic programming systems increases accessibility and adoption potential.
- Appears broadly applicable across models and MCMC variants implemented in WebPPL and Stan.
- Question about tightness and calibration: need evidence/guidance on when bounds are informative versus vacuous and how they scale with computation budget.
- Concern about computational overhead: BMC requires forward and reverse chains (e.g., AIS) and can be expensive; quantify additional cost and runtime vs. tighter bounds.
- Sensitivity to design choices: robustness to annealing schedules, intermediate distributions, and proposal kernels is unclear; request defaults that work across model classes.
- Scalability concerns: unclear behavior in high-dimensional, strongly correlated, or multimodal posteriors and whether diagnostics can detect degraded bounds.
- Applicability to nonstandard algorithms: ask how method handles adaptive MCMC, non-reversible dynamics, or heavily tuned algorithms (e.g., NUTS with adaptation) and whether corrections are needed.
- Generality beyond MCMC: question whether framework extends to SMC, VI, or other samplers or requires additional assumptions.
- Requests validation on tractable cases with known posteriors/normalizing constants to calibrate and demonstrate bounds reflect true error.
- Suggests adding a concise algorithmic summary or pseudocode for the BMC-based evaluation procedure to improve clarity.
- Recommend explicitly stating assumptions required for bounds (e.g., ergodicity, absolute continuity).
- Ask for practical guidance: default schedules, number of intermediate steps, diagnostics for convergence of the bound, and recommended compute budgets.
- Request discussion of failure modes and mitigation strategies, e.g., variance diagnostics and repeated runs.
- Integration details: describe required user changes to Stan/WebPPL models and whether implementations reuse compiled code and gradients to minimize overhead.
- Empirical evaluation should include varied MCMC configurations, runtime-normalized metrics, and variability across runs.
- Recommend ablation studies on schedule length and intermediate distributions to show sensitivity.
- Include examples where the bound meaningfully distinguishes well- and poorly-tuned samplers.
- Overall: principled, practical, and novel approach with useful theoretical and tooling contributions; acceptance recommended but paper would be strengthened by more analysis of tightness, cost, and sensitivity.
