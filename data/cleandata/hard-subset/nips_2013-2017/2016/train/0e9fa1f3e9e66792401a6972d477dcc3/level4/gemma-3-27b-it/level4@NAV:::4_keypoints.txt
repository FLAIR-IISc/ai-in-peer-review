- Introduces AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo with annealed importance sampling to get stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood for MCMC convergence evaluation.
- Shows the gap between bounds bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate MCMC samples, yielding a single scalar convergence metric.
- Validated on synthetic data sampled from the joint; authors report correlation between behavior on synthetic vs. real-world data.
- Demonstrates utility of AutoBDMC for model selection and MCMC debugging.
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and presents convincing experimental results.
- Section 3 is hard to follow; transition to Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces notation/equations without sufficient context.
- The divergence bound in Section 3.1 appears to be a novel contribution and should be explicitly highlighted.
- AutoBDMC is not clearly defined; should state explicitly that it applies AIS and reverse AIS to analyze MCMC convergence.
- By Section 3.2, the method should already be clearly defined before discussing challenges “for AutoBDMC.”
- Minor notational issues: possible typo p(theta, z | y) vs. p(theta, z, y) and inconsistent use of ‘y’ vs. ‘D’ for data.
- Supplementary WebPPL code needs compatible-version and integration instructions; recommend releasing as an npm-distributable WebPPL package and similarly packaging the Stan implementation.
