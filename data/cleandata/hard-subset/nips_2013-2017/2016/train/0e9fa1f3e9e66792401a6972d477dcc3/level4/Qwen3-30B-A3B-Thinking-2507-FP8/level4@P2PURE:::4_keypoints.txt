- Introduces AutoBDMC: using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (AIS and reverse AIS) to assess MCMC convergence via stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood.
- Shows that the gap between these bounds bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate sample distribution, giving a single scalar convergence metric.
- Method is limited to synthetic data drawn from the joint, but experiments indicate synthetic analysis predicts performance on real-world data.
- Demonstrates practical applications for model selection and debugging.
- Paper is generally well-written, well-motivated, and experimentally compelling.
- Section 3 (especially the transition into 3.1) is hard to follow; variables and equations are introduced without sufficient motivation.
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, that novelty should be explicitly stated because it is central to the contribution.
- The paper never explicitly defines “AutoBDMC”; it should be unambiguously defined early (before references like “this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC”).
- Notational issues: possible typo Line 149 (p(θ, z | y) vs p(θ, z, y)) and inconsistent use of y vs D for data.
- Supplement WebPPL code should state working WebPPL version(s) and integration instructions; ideally released as an npm-installable WebPPL package.
- A similar packaged implementation for Stan would be highly appreciated.
