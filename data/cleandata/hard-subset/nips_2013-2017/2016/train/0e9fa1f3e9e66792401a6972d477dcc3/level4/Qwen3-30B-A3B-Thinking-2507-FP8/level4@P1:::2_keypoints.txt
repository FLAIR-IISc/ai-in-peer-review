- Introduces BREAD, a method for rigorously evaluating the quality of MCMC-based approximate posterior samples.  
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between the true posterior and approximate sample distribution.  
- Derives an estimator by running AIS both forward and reverse on simulated data that upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Provides a theoretically grounded, quantitative measure that complements existing heuristic convergence diagnostics.  
- Main contributions: (1) proof that BDMC yields an expected upper bound on Jeffreys divergence; (2) shows the bound is accurate on small, tractable examples; (3) proposes a protocol for real-data evaluation via simulation from inferred hyperparameters; (4) integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and validates across models.  
- Addresses the challenge of assessing inference quality in black-box probabilistic programming systems, enabling automatic monitoring for non-expert users.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and carefully extends BDMC; empirical results demonstrate effectiveness across multiple models and PPLs.  
- Authors explicitly discuss limitations—chiefly the need for exact posterior samples—and propose a validated simulation-based workaround.  
- Practical utility demonstrated by discovering a bug in WebPPL, showing value for debugging inference implementations.  
- Paper is well-written, reproducible, and situates itself clearly relative to related work (acknowledging concurrent contributions).  
- Meets NIPS review criteria: high quality, clear, original extension of BDMC, and significant practical impact; reviewer recommends acceptance.
