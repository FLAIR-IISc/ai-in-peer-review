- Proposes using the gap between BDMC’s stochastic lower and upper AIS bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC transition operators.  
- Main technical contributions: BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- Strength: simple, readily implementable idea with potentially high impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Concern: novelty and depth are modest; primary new observation (gap bounds JS divergence) may not be entirely novel.  
- Major related-work omission: fails to cite Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which closely connects to BDMC and uses the same annealing sweeps.  
- Important connection: TT’s acceptance ratio appears to be the ratio of BDMC’s stochastic bounds and TT discusses upper/lower thermodynamic integration estimates.  
- Missing discussion on β_t schedule: choice of Δβ strongly affects performance/acceptance and should be addressed and tuned.  
- Suggestion: AutoBDMC should include an iterative schedule to decrease Δβ until bounds converge; experiments lack a systematic study of step-size effects.  
- Clarity problems in Sections 3.1–3.2: rushed writing, inconsistent labeling (“rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”), and imprecise definitions reduce readability.  
- Notation issues: definition of vector v is ambiguous (likely z_{1:T-1}, θ_{1:T-1} but unclear).  
- Missing estimator definition: \hat{p}(y) is not defined in equations (5–7) and (9–11), making derivations hard to follow.  
- Mathematical error: Equation (6) is nonsensical as written (KL has identical distributions); likely a wrong RHS and needs correction.  
- AutoBDMC undefined: unclear whether it means using the bound gap only or also a fixed hyperparameter schedule from section 3.2.  
- Experimental details unclear: β_t selection is not stated (code implies uniform steps), RAIS and variants (u)c(R)AIS are not defined in captions, and axis/tick legibility could be improved.  
- Typos: missing parentheses in D_JS expression and “Homan” should be “Hoffman” in NUTS citation.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is promising and potentially useful; could be acceptable if authors fix clarity, related-work discussion, and expand experiments on β scheduling.
