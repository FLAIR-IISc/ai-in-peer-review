- Paper proposes using the gap between BDMC lower and upper bounds as a generic performance metric for MCMC transition operators.  
- Technical contributions: BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- Main strength: simple, elegant, easy-to-implement idea with high potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main weakness: modest novelty relative to prior BDMC work; key new observation (gap bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence) may not be entirely novel.  
- Significant clarity issues, especially in Sections 3.1 and 3.2, where writing quality declines and explanations feel rushed.  
- Omission of Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) citation and failure to discuss strong connections between TT and BDMC.  
- TT performs the same reverse/forward sweeps as BDMC; BDMC’s bound ratio corresponds to TT’s acceptance ratio and should be discussed.  
- Annealing schedule (β_t) choice is insufficiently addressed; gap between bounds depends on Δβ and affects reliability of comparisons.  
- Suggestion to design an adaptive annealing schedule (AutoBDMC) that decreases Δβ until bounds are sufficiently tight; experiments lack a systematic study of Δβ effects.  
- Unclear what “AutoBDMC” refers to—needs unambiguous definition (protocol vs. fixed hyperparameter scheme).  
- Ambiguous definition of vector v and inconsistent labeling of forward/reverse sweeps ("rev"/"fwd" vs. "for"/"back").  
- Equations (5–7) and (9–11) take expectations over an undefined estimator \hat{p}(y), making derivations hard to follow.  
- Equation (6) appears incorrect as written; KL divergence term seems to reference identical distributions and likely has a missing conditional.  
- Experimental details unclear: how β_t is chosen (code suggests linear schedule), whether AutoBDMC is parameterized by number of steps T.  
- Unclear x-axis labeling in Figures 2–3: does "HMC / No-U-Turn steps" mean MCMC steps per temperature level?  
- Undefined acronyms/variants in captions (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) and small figure font sizes harming readability.  
- Minor typos: missing parentheses in D_JS expression and "Homan" should be "Hoffman" in NUTS citation.  
- Overall recommendation: promising work that could merit acceptance if clarity issues, related-work omissions, and experimental shortcomings are convincingly addressed.
