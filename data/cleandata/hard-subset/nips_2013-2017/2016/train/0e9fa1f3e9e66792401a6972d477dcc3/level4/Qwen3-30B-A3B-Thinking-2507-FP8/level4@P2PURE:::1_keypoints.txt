- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target for debugging/evaluating MCMC in probabilistic programming languages.  
- Using BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures is highly valuable given known problems with existing MCMC diagnostics.  
- Two highly relevant prior works (Geweke 2004; Cook, Gelman & Rubin 2006/2012) that run samplers on prior-generated data should be cited and discussed.  
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow: q(·) is used before being defined—seems to be the marginal of the AIS sampler after T steps but this must be explicit.  
- The main text relies on the supplement for clarification; the paper should be self-contained and include a clear step-by-step description of how to apply the method.  
- Separating unobserved variables into z and θ is unnecessary clutter since they always appear together—one should be eliminated.  
- Minor typo: missing closing parenthesis at line 165.
