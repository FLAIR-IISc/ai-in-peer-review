- Proposes bidirectional Monte Carlo methods to derive tighter lower bounds on KL divergence between true posterior and MCMC approximations.
- Addresses a key scalability challenge by providing theoretically grounded, computationally efficient diagnostics for evaluating MCMC approximations.
- Technique avoids bias inherent in standard importance sampling bounds while remaining computationally tractable.
- Derivations are rigorous and connect clearly to existing variational inference and MCMC convergence theory.
- Shows bounds remain informative even when the approximate distribution lacks full support over the posterior.
- Empirical validation across multiple Bayesian models demonstrates consistent correlation between bounds and inference quality.
- Integrates diagnostics into WebPPL and Stan, enabling immediate practical adoption and reproducibility.
- WebPPL integration shows ease of embedding into probabilistic programming without rearchitecture; Stan interface leverages high-performance MCMC.
- Comparisons to baselines (importance sampling, variational inference) show improved bound tightness with modest computational cost.
- Acknowledges limitations—bounds can be conservative in high-dimensional settings—and presents them transparently.
- Figures and clear writing effectively convey results; implementation discussion (e.g., multimodal posteriors) adds practical nuance.
- Overall, the work meaningfully advances theoretical and practical Bayesian inference and is recommended for acceptance.
