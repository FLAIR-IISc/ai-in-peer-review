- Introduces AutoBDMC, using Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS to estimate stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood for MCMC convergence evaluation.
- Shows the gap between these bounds bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the approximate sample distribution, yielding a scalar convergence metric.
- Method is limited to analyzing convergence on synthetic data from the joint distribution, though authors provide empirical evidence it reflects real-data performance.
- Demonstrates applicability to model selection and debugging; experiments are reasonably convincing and the method appears useful.
- Paper is overall well-structured and clearly motivated.
- Section 3 is difficult to follow; the transition to Section 3.1 abruptly introduces variables and equations without sufficient motivation.
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper should explicitly state this, as it is central to the contribution.
- The definition of “AutoBDMC” is not explicitly provided and should be clearly stated (appears to mean using AIS and rAIS for MCMC convergence analysis).
- AutoBDMC should be clearly defined before Section 3.2, which discusses challenges to the method.
- Minor error: Line 149 may incorrectly state p(θ, z, y) instead of p(θ, z | y).
- Notational inconsistencies (alternating use of y and D for data/observations) create confusion.
- Supplemental WebPPL code needs compatible-version and integration instructions or, ideally, an npm-installable WebPPL package; similar release requested for the Stan code.
