- Paper proposes using BDMC gap (difference between stochastic lower and upper AIS bounds) as a generic performance measure for MCMC transition operators.  
- Technical contributions: BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- Main strength: simple, easy-to-implement idea with potentially high impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty and modest technical depth beyond existing BDMC work.  
- Paper repeats existing BDMC material extensively, making Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed and hurting writing quality.  
- Novel observation: the bound gap provides an upper bound on Jensen–Shannon divergence, but this may not be entirely novel.  
- Crucial related work missing: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is highly relevant and not cited or discussed.  
- Strong analogy to Tempered Transitions suggests missed opportunities to connect BDMC acceptance ratios and tempering intuition.  
- Authors give insufficient attention to βt schedule choice; Δβ affects bound gap and reliability and needs systematic treatment.  
- “Auto” in AutoBDMC is undefined and the paper does not explain any automated schedule or iterative Δβ reduction.  
- Experiments are thin: need systematic study of how bounds vary with step size and clearer description of experimental settings.  
- Experimental clarity issues: unclear how βt is chosen, what “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” refers to, and undefined acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS).  
- Presentation/clarity problems: ambiguous definition of vector v, undefined estimators in equations (5–7,9–11), and a nonsensical Eq. (6) as written.  
- Potential practical guideline: comparisons unreliable when bound gap > ~1 nat; suggests rerunning with smaller Δβ.  
- Minor issues/typos: missing parentheses in D_JS expression and wrong name in NUTS citation (Homan -> Hoffman).  
- Overall verdict: method is useful and potentially acceptable if authors fix clarity, related-work discussion, and experimental thoroughness by camera-ready deadline.
