- Introduces AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS/rAIS to get stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood and uses their gap to bound Jensen-Shannon divergence as a scalar MCMC-convergence measure.  
- Method evaluates convergence only on synthetic data drawn from the joint model; authors provide experiments suggesting this predicts behavior on real datasets and show uses in model selection/debugging.  
- Overall assessment: paper is readable, well-motivated, method appears useful, and empirical results are reasonably convincing.  
- Section 3 is substantially harder to follow than the rest; the transition into 3.1 is abrupt and lacks sufficient motivation.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, that should be explicitly stated, since the paper’s contribution hinges on it.  
- AutoBDMC is never explicitly defined; the paper should plainly state that it refers to using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence and define the term before Section 3.2.  
- Notational issues: possible error at Line 149 (p(theta, z | y) vs p(theta, z, y)) and inconsistent use of y versus D for data, which is sometimes confusing.  
- Supplement/code requests: include WebPPL version(s) that work and integration instructions; prefer releasing a WebPPL npm package and a comparable packaged Stan release.
