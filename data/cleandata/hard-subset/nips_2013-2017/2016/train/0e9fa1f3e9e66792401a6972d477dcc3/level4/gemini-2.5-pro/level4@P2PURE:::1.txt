This paper proposes a method based on bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (JS) divergence between the distribution produced by an MCMC sampler and its target distribution. The primary application is for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations, particularly within probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The proposal to use BDMC for diagnosing MCMC convergence failures is compelling, as it addresses known limitations of existing diagnostic tools.

The authors should, however, discuss the relationship of their work to two highly relevant references, [1] and [2]. These works also propose methods for testing MCMC samplers by running them on data generated from the model's prior distribution.

The derivation in Section 3.1 is difficult to follow. For instance, the notation q(·) is used before it is formally defined. While context suggests it represents the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after T steps, this should be stated explicitly. Although the supplement provides some clarification, the main text should be self-contained and understandable on its own. More broadly, the paper would benefit from a clear, step-by-step description or algorithm outlining how to apply the proposed method. Currently, the reader must piece together the procedure from different parts of the text.

Minor notes:
*   The distinction between unobserved variables z and θ seems unnecessary and clutters the notation. Since they always appear together in the general setting described, the presentation could be simplified by using a single variable.
*   There is a missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
