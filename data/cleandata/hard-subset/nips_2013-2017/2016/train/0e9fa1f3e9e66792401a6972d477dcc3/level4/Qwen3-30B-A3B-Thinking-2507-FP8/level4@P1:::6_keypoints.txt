- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to bound Jeffreys divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior using BDMC.  
- Extends the BDMC framework to evaluate MCMC-based inference specifically for AIS and SMC algorithms.  
- Derives an estimator that upper-bounds the expected Jeffreys divergence.  
- Validates the divergence bound on toy distributions.  
- Proposes a protocol to evaluate inference on real-world data via hyperparameter-fitted simulations.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and provides empirical validation across multiple models.  
- Builds on prior BDMC work [GGA15] and relates to subjective divergence [CTM16] and classic MCMC diagnostics.  
- Theoretical contribution (Section 3.1) is sound and practically useful.  
- The real-data validation protocol (Section 3.2) is innovative and addresses prior reliance on simulated data.  
- Empirical implementation is thorough, demonstrates real-world utility, and uncovered a WebPPL bug.  
- Comparison of collapsed vs. uncollapsed model representations gives actionable practitioner insights.  
- Paper is clear with precise definitions and well-structured experiments.  
- Scope limited to AIS/SMC-based inference, excluding many traditional MCMC methods.  
- Claim that insights generalize to traditional MCMC needs stronger justification or additional experiments.  
- Real-data validation depends on fitted hyperparameters and may not generalize to highly misspecified models.  
- Comparison to [CTM16] could better clarify how BREAD’s guarantees differ from subjective divergence assumptions.  
- Overall assessment: technically rigorous, novel, implementable, and significant for probabilistic programming; suitable for NIPS.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
