This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence. The approach leverages the Bidirectional Monte Carlo method to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood, utilizing annealed importance sampling. The authors demonstrate that the gap between these bounds provides a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, yielding a single scalar value to quantify MCMC convergence. While the method is initially validated on synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors present evidence suggesting that its analysis of this synthetic data predicts behavior observed with real-world data. They further illustrate the utility of their method for model selection and debugging. Overall, the paper is well-written and clearly motivated, and the proposed method appears valuable, supported by reasonably compelling experimental results. Section 3 was more difficult to follow than the remainder of the paper; the transition to Section 3.1 is particularly abrupt, presenting definitions and equations without sufficient context. If the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 is a novel contribution—which I believe it is—this should be explicitly stated, as the paper’s novelty appears to center on this result. The paper lacks a clear definition of “AutoBDMC”; while I understand it to be the application of AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence, an explicit statement clarifying this would be beneficial. By Section 3.2, when the text references challenges for “AutoBDMC,” the term should be well-defined. Several minor issues also warrant attention: on line 149, it appears that p(theta, z | y) should be p(theta, z, y). Furthermore, the paper exhibits notational inconsistencies, switching between *y* and *D* to represent data/observations, which occasionally caused confusion. The WebPPL code in the supplementary materials should be accompanied by (a) information regarding compatible WebPPL versions and (b) instructions for integrating the code with WebPPL distributions for use. Ideally, releasing this as an installable WebPPL package via npm would be even more useful. A similar package release for the Stan implementation would also be highly appreciated.
