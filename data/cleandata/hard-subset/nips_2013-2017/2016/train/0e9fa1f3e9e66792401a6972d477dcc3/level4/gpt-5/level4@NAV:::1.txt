This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the intended target distribution, with the goal of debugging and assessing MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming systems. I find the idea of deploying BDMC to detect non-convergence appealing—this could be quite practical given the well-known limitations of standard MCMC diagnostics. Two closely related works are [1,2], which likewise evaluate MCMC performance by running on datasets drawn from the prior.

Section 3.1: I found the derivation difficult to follow. In particular, q(.) is introduced before being defined. From context, it appears to denote the marginal distribution of the AIS procedure after T steps; if so, this should be stated explicitly. The supplement helps somewhat, but the main text should be self-contained. More broadly, it would help to include a concise, end-to-end recipe for applying the method—the reader currently has to infer several steps.

Minor notes: Splitting the latent variables into z and theta feels unnecessarily cluttered; in this general presentation there is no functional distinction between them—they always occur together—so consider collapsing z and theta. Line 165: missing closing parenthesis.
