- Proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) gap between lower and upper AIS bounds as a generic performance metric for MCMC operators.  
- Implements BDMC in Stan and WebPPL and presents a protocol for assessing posterior inference quality.  
- Strength: simple, easy-to-implement approach with potential high impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Concern: limited novelty—main technical novelty (gap bounds Jensen-Shannon divergence) is useful but not entirely new.  
- Major omission: fails to cite and sufficiently connect to Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related.  
- Important conceptual link: BDMC gap behavior depends on the annealing schedule (Δβ), mirroring step-size concerns in Tempered Transitions.  
- AutoBDMC is unclear in definition and behavior; needs explicit description and possibly an iterative Δβ schedule.  
- Experiments are insufficiently comprehensive; should systematically study how bounds vary with step size (Δβ/T).  
- Presentation is rushed in parts (especially §§3.1–3.2), harming clarity and emphasis on the authors’ core contributions.  
- Notation/labeling issues: ambiguous definition of vector v and inconsistent “rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back” labels.  
- Missing definition of estimator \hat{p}(y) in key equations, making E[log \hat{R}_{rev}] and E[log \hat{R}_{fwd}] unclear.  
- Equation (6) appears incorrect—the KL divergence shows identical distributions and likely needs p(z,θ|y) q_back(v|z,θ,y).  
- AutoBDMC ambiguity: unclear whether it only computes bound differences for convergence or also uses the hyperparameter scheme from §3.2.  
- Experimental procedure unclear: β_t choice is not stated in text though code suggests β scheduling by number of steps T.  
- Figure labeling/readability problems: ambiguous “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” phrasing, undefined RAIS/u(c)R AIS acronyms, and small axes/tick labels.  
- Typos: missing closing parentheses in the D_JS expression and incorrect NUTS citation (“Homan” → “Hoffman”).  
- Overall recommendation: promising contribution contingent on improved clarity, stronger related-work discussion, and more systematic experiments for camera-ready version.
