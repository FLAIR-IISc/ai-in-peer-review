1. Proposes using BDMC (two AIS sweeps) and the gap between its bounds as a generic performance metric for MCMC operators.  
2. Demonstrates implementations for Stan and WebPPL and proposes a protocol for posterior-inference quality assessment.  
3. Method is simple, implementable, and highly relevant to probabilistic programming systems.  
4. Paper spends excessive space re-explaining BDMC, reducing perceived novelty.  
5. Fails to cite and contextualize Neal’s Tempered Transitions (TT), which performs identical forward/reverse AIS sweeps and is directly related.  
6. The BDMC bound-ratio equals TT’s acceptance ratio; Neal shows this ratio bounds log Z_T − log Z_1 and implies perfect acceptance when bounds converge.  
7. Observation that the bound gap upper-bounds Jensen-Shannon divergence is insightful but not entirely novel.  
8. Lacks systematic analysis of how the bound gap depends on temperature schedule Δβ; Δβ critically affects performance as in TT.  
9. Authors should warn that comparisons are unreliable when log-bound gap > 1 nat and smaller Δβ may be required.  
10. “Auto” in AutoBDMC is unclear—paper does not state whether Δβ scheduling is adaptive or how AutoBDMC works.  
11. Definition of vector v is ambiguous; should explicitly state v = (z_{1:T-1}, θ_{1:T-1}).  
12. Inconsistent terminology ("rev/fwd" vs "for/back") creates confusion and should be standardized.  
13. Estimators in Equations (5–7) and (9–11) reference ˆp(y) without defining the estimator; likely meant E[log ˆR_rev] and E[log ˆR_fwd].  
14. Equation (6) is incorrect as written—the KL divergence uses identical distributions; it should involve p(z,θ|y) and q_back(v|z,θ,y).  
15. AutoBDMC’s definition is never clearly stated; appears to combine bound-gap convergence checks with a fixed hyperparameter scheme.  
16. Experimental details unclear: β_t schedule ambiguous (appendix implies β_t = t/T); this must be specified in main text.  
17. Figure captions omit acronym definitions (e.g., RAIS, (u/c)(R)AIS) and axis/tick labels are too small for readability.  
18. Minor typos: missing closing parentheses in D_JS expressions and "Homan" should be "Hoffman" in the NUTS citation.  
19. Overall: compelling, practical idea with potential high impact, but needs clearer technical context (esp. relation to TT), clearer AutoBDMC definition, Δβ analysis, and presentation fixes before strong acceptance.
