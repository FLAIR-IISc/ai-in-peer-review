This paper proposes an innovative approach to estimating the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a specific focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The idea of utilizing bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose convergence failures in samplers is particularly noteworthy, as existing MCMC diagnostics are indeed problematic. The authors' approach bears some resemblance to the methods proposed in [1] and [2], which assess the performance of MCMC samplers by running them on data sampled from the prior.

However, there are some areas in the paper that require clarification. Specifically, Section 3.1 contains a derivation that is difficult to follow. The notation q(.) is introduced before its definition, which may cause confusion. Although the context suggests that q(.) represents the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampler (AIS) after T steps, this should be explicitly stated. Furthermore, the reader should not need to consult the supplement to understand the main text. It would be beneficial to provide a clear, step-by-step explanation of how to apply the proposed method from start to finish, as the reader currently needs to piece this information together.

Additionally, some minor suggestions can be made to improve the clarity of the paper. The distinction between unobserved variables z and θ is not necessary in the general setting presented, as they always appear together. Therefore, it may be more concise to eliminate one of these variables. Finally, a minor typographical error is present in Line 165, where a close parenthesis is missing.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach to evaluating MCMC implementations in PPLs. With some revisions to address the aforementioned issues, the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
