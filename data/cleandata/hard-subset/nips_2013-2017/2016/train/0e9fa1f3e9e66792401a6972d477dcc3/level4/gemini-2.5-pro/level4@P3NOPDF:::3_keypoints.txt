- Core idea: Use BDMC forward/reverse AIS bound gap as a simple, practical performance measure for MCMC transition operators; implemented in Stan and WebPPL.
- Strength: Elegant, easy-to-implement diagnostic with high potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Contribution: Protocol for evaluation and software implementations are the main deliverables.
- Novelty concern: The main insight (BDMC gap bounds JS divergence) is useful but its novelty is debatable given prior BDMC work.
- Major omission: Fails to cite or discuss Radford Neal's Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related and explains the same reverse/forward sweep mechanics.
- Important connection missed: Neal’s TT acceptance ratio corresponds to the BDMC upper/lower bound ratio and informs annealing schedule choices.
- Annealing schedule issue: Choice of β_t is critical; a large BDMC gap indicates too-coarse scheduling and can invalidate operator comparisons.
- Clarity problems: Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed with ambiguous definitions (e.g., v), inconsistent notation, and undefined/unclear estimators (p̂(y)).
- Technical error: Equation (6) appears incorrect (KL between identical distributions); other derivations need correction and clearer statements.
- "AutoBDMC" undefined: Unclear what "Auto" entails and missed opportunity to propose automated schedule refinement (e.g., iteratively reducing Δβ).
- Experimental shortcomings: Main paper omits how β_t is chosen (appendix shows linear schedule); lacks systematic study of bounds vs annealing step size.
- Reliability concern: Comparisons may be unreliable when log-bound gap is large (>~1 nat); experiments should show gap behavior with step size.
- Minor issues: Typos (missing parentheses in D_JS), incorrect citation (Homan → Hoffman), small/unclear figure labels, and undefined acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS).
- Conclusion/recommendation: Valuable idea but paper needs to address related work (Neal 1996), improve technical clarity/fixes, and add experimental detail; acceptance should be conditional on these fixes.
