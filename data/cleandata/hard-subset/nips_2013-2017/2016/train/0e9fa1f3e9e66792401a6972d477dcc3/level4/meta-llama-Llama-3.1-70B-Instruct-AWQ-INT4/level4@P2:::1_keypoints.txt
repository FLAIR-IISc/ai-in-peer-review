- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.  
- Motivated by shortcomings of existing MCMC diagnostics; BDMC-based diagnosis could be practically useful.  
- Paper gives a clear summary, situates work relative to prior literature, and is well-written.  
- Main technical contribution: extend BDMC to MCMC-based posterior inference by running AIS forward/backward to upper-bound the symmetrized KL on simulated data.  
- Implements the method in WebPPL and Stan and validates on multiple models and datasets.  
- Strength: provides a rigorous, general method to evaluate approximate posterior samples and guide inference algorithm design.  
- Strength: potential to improve accuracy/reliability of probabilistic inference and increase practitioner confidence in PPL outputs.  
- Weakness: derivation in Section 3.1 is complex and may be hard for some readers to follow.  
- Weakness: lacks a clear, end-to-end how-to; application steps are scattered and need consolidation.  
- Originality: builds on prior BDMC work but offers a new application and a protocol for analyzing sampler accuracy on real data.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with significant potential impact.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (clarify Section 3.1 derivation and provide fuller application guidance); rating 8/10.
