- Proposes BREAD, a novel method using bidirectional Monte Carlo to evaluate MCMC inference reliability.  
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo to measure accuracy of approximate posterior samples from MCMC algorithms.  
- Runs AIS chains forward (prior→posterior) and reverse to upper bound the expected symmetrized KL divergence between true posterior and approximate sample distribution.  
- Situates work relative to existing MCMC diagnostics and automatic configuration, addressing limitations of prior methods.  
- Claims BREAD is rigorous and general compared to method-specific, non-confirmatory diagnostics.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly written, with thorough exposition of bidirectional Monte Carlo applied to MCMC.  
- Experimental evaluation is well-executed, showing BREAD’s effectiveness on simulated and real data.  
- Demonstrates potential uses of BREAD for model selection and debugging.  
- Notes minor notation/terminology inconsistencies (e.g., y vs D for data/observations).  
- Technically sound approach with solid theoretical and experimental support.  
- Original contribution: development of BREAD as a new tool for evaluating MCMC reliability.  
- Significant contribution: offers a rigorous, general procedure for assessing posterior inference accuracy.  
- For acceptance: novelty, practical usefulness (model selection/debugging), clear motivation, and strong experiments.  
- Against acceptance: minor notation issues, needs clearer explanation of the divergence bound in Section 3.1.  
- Against acceptance: should provide more implementation details (WebPPL and Stan code) used in experiments.
