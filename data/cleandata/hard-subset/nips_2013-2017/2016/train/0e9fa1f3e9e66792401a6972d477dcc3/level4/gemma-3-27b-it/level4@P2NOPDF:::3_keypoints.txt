- Proposes using BDMC gap (difference between AIS lower and upper bounds) as a performance metric for MCMC transition operators, with implementations in Stan and WebPPL.  
- Main strength: simple, broadly applicable method for assessing MCMC convergence in probabilistic programming systems.  
- Key technical insight: the bounds gap provides an upper bound on the Jensen–Shannon divergence between forward and reverse chains, but this may not be entirely novel.  
- Novelty and technical depth are modest; sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed and lack clarity.  
- Major related-work omission: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is not discussed despite strong conceptual connections to BDMC.  
- Important technical question: the choice of β_t (Δβ step sizes) strongly affects bounds reliability and should be analyzed and discussed.  
- Suggestion: AutoBDMC should include or explain an iterative schedule to decrease Δβ until bounds converge.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak on systematically studying the relationship between bounds and step size (Δβ).  
- Clarity issue: definition of vector v is imprecise and ambiguous given two AIS sweeps; notation (rev/fwd vs for/back) is inconsistent.  
- Clarity issue: estimator \hat{p}(y) used in multiple equations is undefined, obscuring interpretation of expectations.  
- Potential error: Equation (6) seems incorrect because the KL divergence appears to use identical distributions.  
- Ambiguity: AutoBDMC’s exact scope (bound calculation vs fixed hyperparameter scheme) is not clearly specified.  
- Experimental details unclear: β_t selection and the meaning of “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” in figures need explicit explanation.  
- Presentation issues: acronyms like RAIS and variants (u)c(R)AIS are undefined in captions; axes and tick labels are too small.  
- Typos: missing parentheses in the D_JS expression and “Homan” should be “Hoffman” in the NUTS citation.  
- Acceptance depends on authors addressing clarity problems and expanding the related-work discussion for the camera-ready version.
