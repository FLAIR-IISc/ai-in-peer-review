This paper applies the recently developed bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference methods. The authors derive lower bounds on the KL-divergence between the true posterior and the approximate distribution generated by an MCMC sampler. The method's practical utility is demonstrated through its integration into the popular probabilistic programming frameworks WebPPL and Stan.

The problem of assessing posterior inference quality is highly relevant, and the application of bidirectional Monte Carlo for this purpose is a novel and interesting contribution. Overall, this is a well-executed paper, and I recommend it for acceptance.
