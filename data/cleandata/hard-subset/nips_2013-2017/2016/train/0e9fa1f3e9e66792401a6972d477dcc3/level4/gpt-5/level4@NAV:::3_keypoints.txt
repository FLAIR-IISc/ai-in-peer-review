- Proposes using BDMC’s stochastic lower–upper bound gap as a generic performance metric for MCMC kernels; provides Stan and WebPPL implementations and a posterior-assessment protocol.  
- Idea is elegant, easy to implement, and potentially high impact for probabilistic programming.  
- Novelty is modest: main new insight is that the bound gap upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence, which may not be entirely new.  
- Fails to cite / discuss Neal (1996) Tempered Transitions (TT), despite strong technical connections (same forward/reverse sweeps; TT acceptance ratio ≈ bound ratio).  
- Paper omits careful discussion of β_t scheduling (Δβ tradeoffs) and how the BDMC gap depends on step-size/schedule.  
- AutoBDMC is not precisely defined—unclear what is automated and how the procedure is specified.  
- Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed and unclear, reducing technical clarity.  
- Notation and labels are ambiguous: vector v’s definition is vague and rev/fwd vs for/back labels are inconsistent.  
- Several equations are unclear or incoherent: unspecified estimator in (5–7),(9–11), and Equation (6) appears incorrect.  
- Experimental details are unclear: how β_t is chosen, interpretation of “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” in BDMC, and undefined acronyms RAIS and (u)c(R)AIS in captions.  
- Experiments should more systematically study how bounds behave with step size and Δβ and suggest automating schedule reduction when log-gap is large (>~1 nat).  
- Presentation issues: small figure fonts and a few typos (missing parentheses in D_JS expression; Homan → Hoffman in NUTS citation).
