- Proposes using BDMC (reverse “heating” + forward “annealing”) to get stochastic lower/upper bounds on the marginal likelihood and use their gap to evaluate MCMC transition operators.  
- Implements BDMC in Stan and WebPPL and presents a practical posterior-inference assessment protocol.  
- Strength: the BDMC-gap-as-performance-measure idea is elegant, practical, easy to implement, and impactful for probabilistic programming.  
- Strength: Working implementations in Stan and WebPPL increase accessibility and adoption potential.  
- Strength: Framing the bound gap as an upper bound on Jensen–Shannon divergence is a useful interpretive link.  
- Concern: Technical novelty is modest relative to prior BDMC/AIS work; the JS divergence observation is not entirely new.  
- Concern: Large portions of the paper reiterate BDMC, leaving Sections 3.1–3.2 compressed and unclear.  
- Prior work gap: The paper should explicitly discuss Tempered Transitions (Neal 1996) and the connection between TT acceptance ratios, thermodynamic integration, and BDMC bounds.  
- Temperature schedule issue: β_t choice is crucial—large Δβ harms ratios; the BDMC gap depends on Δβ and suggests adaptive schedule refinement.  
- AutoBDMC ambiguity: Clarify whether AutoBDMC adaptively refines β_t/transition kernels or merely computes the bound gap with a fixed schedule.  
- Clarity: Define v unambiguously across the two sweeps and clearly index which sweep each variable belongs to.  
- Clarity: Use consistent labeling for sweeps (don’t mix “rev/fwd” and “for/back”) and explain any meaningful distinctions.  
- Notation: Specify the exact stochastic estimators used (e.g., E[log R̂rev], E[log R̂fwd]) and how they are constructed from AIS weights in Eqs. (5–7, 9–11).  
- Equation error: Eq. (6) appears to state a KL between identical distributions; intended terms likely involve p(z,θ|y) and the reverse path q_back—correct derivations.  
- AutoBDMC definition: Explicitly define whether it means (a) using the gap as diagnostic, (b) fixed hyperparameter schedule, and/or (c) automatic adaptation of β_t or kernels.  
- Experimental clarity: State explicitly how β_t is chosen (appears to be β_t = t/T) and clarify what “HMC/No-U-Turn steps” count refers to.  
- Experiments: Include a systematic study of BDMC gap vs T (or Δβ) across models and operators and provide rules of thumb (e.g., gap > 1 nat indicates unreliable comparisons).  
- Terminology: Define RAIS and c/u prefixes (collapsed/uncollapsed) in text and captions.  
- Reporting: Add error bars/confidence intervals for gaps and report runtime or effective sample size to contextualize computational trade-offs.  
- Visualization: Improve plot readability (larger axis/tick fonts).  
- Suggested improvements: Strengthen related-work discussion (TT/AIS), provide precise definitions of path distributions/weights/estimators, and include a schematic of the two sweeps with indices.  
- Suggested improvement: Propose an AutoBDMC procedure that adaptively increases T until a BDMC-gap quantile falls below a threshold with a runtime cap.  
- Minor fixes: Add missing parentheses in D_JS expression, correct “Homan” → “Hoffman,” define all acronyms on first use, and ensure consistent notation for log marginal likelihood and sweep directionality.  
- Overall: Useful, practical contribution with good implementations but limited novelty; recommend acceptance if definitions/derivations, connection to Tempered Transitions and schedule design, and empirical studies are clarified and strengthened.
