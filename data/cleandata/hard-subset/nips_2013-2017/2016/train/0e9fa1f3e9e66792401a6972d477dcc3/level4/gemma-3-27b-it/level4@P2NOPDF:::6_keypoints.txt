- Paper implements bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) in WebPPL and Stan, mainly summarizing existing BMC methodology.  
- Work is primarily an engineering implementation rather than a novel theoretical contribution.  
- Implementation may be practically useful but lacks new algorithmic or theoretical insights.  
- Core contribution is demonstrating BMC functionality within these two probabilistic programming ecosystems.  
- Paper does not analyze BMC’s specific behavior or offer modifications/improvements for these languages.  
- Empirical evaluation fails to demonstrate compelling advantages of BMC over existing inference methods (e.g., standard MCMC).  
- Main concern is limited novelty—reads like an engineering report inappropriate for NeurIPS.  
- Recommends stronger comparative analysis (runtime, convergence, accuracy) against existing routines like NUTS.  
- Suggests detailing implementation challenges, bottlenecks, and solutions to provide practical insights.  
- Conclusion: technically sound but lacks the significant contribution required for a NeurIPS submission; better suited for a focused workshop.
