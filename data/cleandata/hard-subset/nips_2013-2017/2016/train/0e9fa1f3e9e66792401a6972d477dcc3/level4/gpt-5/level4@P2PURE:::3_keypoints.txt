- Paper proposes using the gap between BDMC’s stochastic lower and upper bounds as a generic performance metric for MCMC transition operators, with implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for posterior-quality assessment.
- Strength: The BDMC-based convergence assessment is simple, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Main novelty is modest; primary new point is that the bound gap upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence, which may not be entirely new.
- Major related-work omission: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is highly relevant and not cited, despite close technical connections.
- The Tempered Transitions analogy suggests the BDMC bound gap depends on the inverse-temperature schedule {β_t} and that acceptance/convergence behavior parallels TT results.
- Choice of β_t (step size Δβ) is unclear; large Δβ harms acceptance and small Δβ wastes compute, so a systematic study of Δβ’s effect is needed.
- “AutoBDMC” is not unambiguously defined; authors should clarify what is automatic and whether an iterative schedule-adjustment scheme is intended.
- Sections 3.1 and 3.2 feel rushed and suffer clarity issues and writing quality drops, making technical arguments hard to follow.
- Notational ambiguities: definition of v (“all variables sampled in AIS before final stage”) is too loose, and inconsistent labeling (rev/fwd vs for/back) confuses which sweep is meant.
- The estimator p̂(y) used in equations is never defined, leaving derivations hard to follow; expectations referenced are unclear.
- Equation (6) appears incorrect as stated because the KL divergence’s distributions are identical; the intended terms likely involve p(z, θ | y) and q_back(v | z, θ, y).
- Experiments lack clarity on β_t selection; appendix suggests β_t = t/T but this should be stated explicitly and linked to reported “HMC/No-U-Turn steps.”
- Acronyms and variants in figures/captions (e.g., RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) are undefined and should be explained.
- Figures need improved readability (larger axis and tick-label fonts).
- Experimental protocol could be more thorough, e.g., systematic evaluation of how bound gaps vary with Δβ and practical thresholds for reliable comparisons (e.g., log-gap ~1 nat).
- Paper would benefit from discussing how to automatically adjust Δβ (AutoBDMC) to ensure sufficiently tight bounds for reliable inference.
- Typos: missing closing parentheses in D_KL terms within D_JS expression, and “Homan” should be “Hoffman” in the NUTS citation.
- Overall recommendation: work is promising and potentially useful; acceptance supported if clarity issues and related-work discussion are convincingly addressed for the camera-ready version.
