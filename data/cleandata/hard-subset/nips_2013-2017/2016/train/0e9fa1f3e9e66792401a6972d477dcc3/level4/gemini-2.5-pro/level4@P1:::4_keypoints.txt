- Introduces BREAD, a method to assess MCMC quality by bounding divergences using reverse annealing.
- Theoretical result: the gap between forward/reverse AIS bounds upper-bounds the expected Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.
- The bound gap provides a single scalar metric summarizing convergence quality.
- BREAD is rigorously applicable only with simulated data; authors propose a protocol to extend it to real data by fitting hyperparameters and generating synthetic datasets.
- Experiments show inference behavior on synthetic data is predictive of behavior on the real data.
- Demonstrated practical utility for comparing model representations and debugging inference in WebPPL and Stan.
- Overall judgment: well-motivated, useful method with a compelling experimental evaluation.
- Strength: addresses the difficult problem of diagnosing MCMC convergence with a principled, quantitative measure.
- Strength: useful for researchers and practitioners for model comparison and implementation debugging.
- Strength: experimental evaluation and validation of the real-data protocol are thoughtfully designed.
- Weakness: Section 3 is hard to follow; needs a clearer introductory overview and smoother transition into technical derivations.
- Weakness: the paper should more clearly emphasize the Jeffreys divergence bound as its primary theoretical novelty.
- Weakness: the BREAD method name is introduced late and should be defined more formally and earlier.
- Minor: check notation (p(theta,z|y) vs p(theta,z,y)) and use consistent symbols for observations (y vs D).
- Minor: supplementary code should list compatible software versions, provide clear integration/use instructions, and ideally be released as a standalone package.
