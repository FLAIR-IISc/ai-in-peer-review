- Paper introduces BREAD: Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing, extending BDMC/AIS to produce stochastic upper and lower bounds on log marginal likelihood for assessing MCMC sample quality.
- Main theoretical result: the expected gap between forward and reverse AIS bounds upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate samples and the true posterior.
- Practical protocol: estimate hyperparameters on real data, simulate synthetic data from these hyperparameters, and run BREAD on synthetic data where exact posterior samples are available.
- Implementation provided in WebPPL and Stan, used to validate inference and compare model representations; discovered a bug in WebPPL.
- Strength: provides a principled, quantitative diagnostic tied to a formal statistical distance rather than heuristic indicators.
- Strength: novel and elegant adaptation of BDMC to sample-quality assessment, with a clear derivation linking log-likelihood gap to Jeffreys divergence.
- Strength: practical utility demonstrated via implementations and a useful experiment comparing collapsed vs uncollapsed matrix factorization.
- Weakness: protocol relies on an initial approximate inference run to produce hyperparameters, introducing circularity because convergence of that run is assumed but unverified.
- Weakness: assumes inference dynamics on synthetic data match those on real data, which can fail under model misspecification or differing posterior geometries.
- Weakness: reverse chain initialization uses a short "warm start" from data-generating parameters, which may be overly optimistic for complex or multimodal posteriors.
- Empirical validation is limited to relatively simple models, leaving open whether BREAD will handle complex, real-world scenarios.
- Suggestion: include direct empirical comparisons where standard diagnostics fail but BREAD succeeds (e.g., multimodal or highly correlated posteriors).
- Suggestion: analyze and present failure cases where synthetic-data diagnostics do not reflect real-data convergence behavior.
- Suggestion: demonstrate BREAD on well-known multimodal examples to substantiate claims about handling multimodality.
