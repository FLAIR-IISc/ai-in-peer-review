This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the intended target distribution, with the goal of debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages. I find the core idea compelling: using BDMC to diagnose non-convergence could be quite useful, given the known limitations of standard MCMC diagnostics. Two closely related works are [1,2], which also assess MCMC by generating data from the prior; it would be helpful to clarify the relationship to these approaches.

Section 3.1 was difficult to follow. In particular, q(·) is used before it is defined. From context, it appears to denote the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps; if so, please state this explicitly when q(·) first appears. While the supplement provides some clarification, the main text should be self-contained. More generally, a concise, end-to-end description of how to apply the method would help. For example, a short “recipe” outlining the required inputs, the construction of the forward and reverse AIS paths, how the estimates are combined to bound the J–S divergence, and how to interpret the outcome would greatly improve readability and reproducibility.

Minor comments:
- The split of unobserved variables into z and θ feels unnecessarily complicated in this general presentation. Since they always appear together, consider consolidating the notation to reduce clutter.
- Line 165 appears to be missing a closing parenthesis.
