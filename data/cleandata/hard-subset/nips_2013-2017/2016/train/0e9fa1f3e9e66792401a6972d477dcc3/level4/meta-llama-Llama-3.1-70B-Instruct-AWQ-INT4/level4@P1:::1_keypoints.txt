- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating PPL implementations.
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo by running AIS chains forward and reverse to upper-bound (in expectation) the symmetrized KL divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.
- Method named BREAD and implemented/integrated into WebPPL and Stan, with validation on several models and datasets.
- Reviewer finds the idea promising and potentially very useful given limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics.
- Technical contributions are sound and experiments demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.
- Derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow and needs clearer exposition and notation.
- Notation issues include an unnecessary split of unobserved variables into z and θ; simplify to reduce clutter.
- Paper needs a clearer, step-by-step statement of how to apply the method end-to-end.
- Related work discussion should be expanded, particularly comparisons to Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012).
- Minor errors (e.g., missing closing parenthesis at Line 165) should be corrected.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, notation, related work coverage, and fix minor errors.
