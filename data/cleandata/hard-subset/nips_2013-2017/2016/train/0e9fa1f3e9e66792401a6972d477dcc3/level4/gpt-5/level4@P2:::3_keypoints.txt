- Paper extends BDMC/AIS to bound, in expectation, the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate MCMC samples and the exact posterior via forward and reverse AIS gap.
- Method implemented in WebPPL and Stan, validated on simulated and real datasets, with a practical protocol: fit hyperparameters on real data, simulate from model, and compare inference behavior.
- Demonstrates usefulness for comparing model representations (collapsed vs uncollapsed) and for debugging (found a WebPPL bug).
- Builds directly on BDMC and AIS and relates to thermodynamic integration and non-equilibrium work identities.
- The connection to Neal’s Tempered Transitions (TT) is particularly relevant and should be discussed explicitly.
- Core novelty is modest: main contribution is observing the forward–reverse gap bounds expected Jeffreys divergence plus practical protocol and PPL integrations.
- Strengths: conceptually simple, general, easy to implement, and broadly useful for probabilistic programming diagnostics.
- Strengths: practical integrations and real-data protocol increase impact and demonstrate feasibility.
- Strengths: can guide modeling/algorithmic choices and detect implementation errors.
- Weakness: technical additions feel incremental relative to BDMC and some derivations are terse.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of related tempering literature (especially TT) and differences in objectives/guarantees.
- Weakness: lack of guidance and empirical study on inverse-temperature schedule (βt) choice and its effect on gap tightness.
- Suggestion: consider an adaptive “auto” β schedule that refines βt until the gap meets a tolerance.
- Exposition issues: vague definition of v (variables sampled before final stage) in presence of two sweeps—needs precise specification.
- Exposition issues: inconsistent forward/reverse notation (“fwd”/“for”, “rev”/“back”) should be unified.
- Exposition issues: expectations in Eqs. (5–7, 9–11) should explicitly state the estimators (e.g., E[log R̂fwd], E[log R̂rev]) for clarity.
- Equation concern: Eq. (6) appears to place identical distributions inside a KL; likely intended to involve pT(xT) qrev(v|xT) and qfwd(xT) qfwd(v|xT).
- “AutoBDMC” is undefined; clarify what is automated (gap computation, hyperparameter fitting, adaptive β, stopping criteria).
- Quality: approach is sound but derivations need clearer notation and corrections; experiments sensible but would benefit from systematic study of gap dependence on Δβ and transition kernels.
- Clarity: tighten notation, fix equation issues, define acronyms/variants at first use, and improve figure readability and captions (define RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS).
- Originality: moderate—useful application and deployment but incremental conceptual advance over BDMC.
- Significance: high practical significance for PPLs and MCMC users as a quantitative diagnostic tool.
- Pros for acceptance: simple, general, practical method; solid PPL integrations; useful debugging example; pragmatic real-data protocol.
- Cons for acceptance: modest technical novelty, terse/unclear derivations, incomplete tempering discussion, limited guidance on β schedules.
- Specific requests: state βt schedule in main text (appendix suggests linear), clarify “HMC/No-U-Turn steps” vs BDMC steps, and report results varying T and schedule shape.
- Minor issues: missing closing parenthesis in a divergence expression and incorrect NUTS citation (“Homan” → “Hoffman”).
- Overall: useful, timely contribution with strong practical impact; recommend acceptance if derivations are clarified, tempering links addressed, and schedule guidance improved.
