- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to rigorously assess reliability of MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Extends BDMC to directly bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Key insight: BDMC’s stochastic gap on log marginal likelihood upper-bounds expected Jeffreys divergence.  
- Provides a single interpretable metric for evaluating inference quality, useful for non-expert users of probabilistic programming.  
- Theoretical contribution: novel connection showing BDMC can bound posterior approximation quality (Jeffreys divergence).  
- Practical protocol: simulating datasets from estimated hyperparameters and validating consistency with real data.  
- Implementation: integrated BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, showing utility for model selection and debugging.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments across five models in Stan and demonstrated debugging (found a WebPPL Gaussian sampling bug).  
- Writing and presentation: generally clear, strongest in Section 3, though transition into Section 3.1 and some notational inconsistencies need smoothing.  
- Recommend explicitly stating the Jeffreys divergence bound as a novel contribution.  
- Related work: comparison to Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka helpful but should more clearly differentiate theoretical guarantees from "subjective divergence."  
- Applicability concern: paper ambiguously states applicability to algorithms "viewed as importance sampling over extended state space"—needs precise characterization of applicable MCMC classes.  
- Practical guidance gap: paper should provide more implementation details (version compatibility, installation) and discuss how BREAD can guide choice between different MCMC algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, significant for probabilistic programming, recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to improve theoretical clarity and implementation guidance.
