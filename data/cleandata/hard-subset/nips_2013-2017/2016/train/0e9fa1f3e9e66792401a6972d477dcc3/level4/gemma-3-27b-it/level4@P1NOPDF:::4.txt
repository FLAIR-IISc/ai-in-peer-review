This paper presents a method, termed AutoBDMC, for assessing MCMC convergence. The method leverages the recently developed Bidirectional Monte Carlo approach to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood (relying on annealed importance sampling). The authors demonstrate that the gap between these upper and lower bounds provides a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, yielding a single scalar quantity for evaluating MCMC convergence. While the method can only be directly applied to synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors provide empirical evidence suggesting that analyses performed on this synthetic data are predictive of behavior observed with real-world data. They also illustrate the utility of their method for model selection and debugging. Overall, I found this paper to be well-written and clearly motivated. The proposed method appears genuinely useful, and the experimental evaluation is reasonably convincing.

I found Section 3 more challenging to follow than the rest of the paper. The transition into Section 3.1 is particularly abrupt, launching directly into variable definitions and equations without sufficient motivation. If the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 is a novel result—which I believe it is—this should be explicitly stated, as the paper’s novelty and contribution seem to hinge on it. 

Critically, what precisely *is* AutoBDMC? This is never explicitly defined within the paper. My understanding is that AutoBDMC represents the approach of using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC convergence. A clear statement to this effect would be beneficial. By the time Section 3.2 mentions that “…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC,” the method should have already been clearly defined.

A few minor points: Line 149: Should p(theta, z | y) be p(theta, z, y)? There are also notational inconsistencies; the paper switches between using *y* and *D* to denote data/observations, which occasionally caused confusion.

Regarding the WebPPL code in the supplementary materials: it should be accompanied by (a) information regarding which version(s) of WebPPL it is known to work with, and (b) instructions on how to integrate it into a WebPPL distribution for practical use. Ideally, releasing this as a WebPPL package installable via npm would be highly beneficial. If feasible, a similar release for the Stan version of the code would also be greatly appreciated.
