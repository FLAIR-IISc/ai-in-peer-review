- Paper proposes using BDMC (two AIS sweeps) and the gap between stochastic bounds as a universal metric to evaluate MCMC transition operators.  
- Contributions include BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL, a protocol for posterior-inference quality assessment, and theoretical discussion.  
- Strength: elegant, simple, highly implementable approach with significant potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main novelty claim (bound gap upper-bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence) is insightful but not fully novel or technically deep.  
- Critical omission: fails to cite Neal (1996) Tempered Transitions (TT) and misses the close connection between TT’s reverse/forward sweeps and BDMC bounds/acceptance ratio.  
- Sections 3.1 and 3.2 present rushed technical content with reduced clarity.  
- Analysis of β_t scheduling (Δβ step sizes) is underdeveloped; bound gap depends on Δβ and can cause poor acceptance or wasted computation.  
- AutoBDMC is ambiguously defined and seems to conflate bound-gap checking with a fixed hyperparameter scheme.  
- Experiments lack systematic analysis of how the bound varies with Δβ and do not clearly define β_t choice (e.g., "step = 1/options.steps").  
- Clarity issue: definition of vector v is ambiguous and notation/labels ("rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back") are inconsistent.  
- Equations (5–7) and (9–11) omit the estimator for p̂(y) and likely need expectations like E[log R̂_rev]/E[log R̂_fwd].  
- Equation (6) is incorrect as written (it wrongly shows identical distributions in a KL term); it should reference p(z,θ|y) and q_back(v|z,θ,y).  
- AutoBDMC never receives an unambiguous formal definition in the paper.  
- Experimental presentation problems: β_t scheduling and "HMC / No-U-Turn steps" are not clearly explained.  
- Figures/captions: acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) are undefined and axes/tick labels are too small.  
- Typos: missing closing parentheses in D_KL/D_JS expressions and "Homan" should be "Hoffman" in the NUTS citation.  
- Overall verdict: method is potentially valuable; recommend acceptance if clarity, related-work citations (Neal 1996), Δβ analysis, AutoBDMC definition, and experimental presentation are fixed.
