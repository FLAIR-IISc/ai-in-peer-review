- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target, aimed at debugging and evaluating MCMC in PPLs.
- The idea is compelling because existing MCMC diagnostics often cannot definitively confirm convergence.
- Method builds on and complements prior BDMC-based sampler assessment work (e.g., GGA15, CTM16).
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow; q(.) is introduced without definition — clarify it as the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps.
- The supplement clarifies details, but the main text should be more self-contained.
- Provide a concise, step-by-step guide for applying the method, since the procedure is currently scattered across the paper.
- Notational critique: separating unobserved variables into z and θ seems unnecessary—consolidate to simplify notation.
- Minor editorial issue: missing closing parenthesis at line 165.
- Overall assessment: promising approach that could be a valuable tool for PPL developers and MCMC researchers.
