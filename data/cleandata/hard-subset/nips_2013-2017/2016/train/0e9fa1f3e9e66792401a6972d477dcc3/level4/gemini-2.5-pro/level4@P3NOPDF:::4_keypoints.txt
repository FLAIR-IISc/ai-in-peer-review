- Introduces AutoBDMC, using BDMC (AIS + reverse AIS) to bound log marginal likelihood and shows the bound gap upper-bounds the JS divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Provides a single, interpretable scalar (the BDMC gap) for diagnosing MCMC convergence.  
- Requires generating synthetic data from the model joint, but experiments show synthetic-data diagnostics predict real-data MCMC performance.  
- Demonstrates practical utility for model selection and debugging MCMC samplers.  
- Strength: well-written, clearly motivated, addresses an important MCMC convergence problem.  
- Strength: method is practically useful and grounded in information theory (JS divergence) rather than heuristics.  
- Strength: thorough, persuasive experimental evaluation and convincing case studies.  
- Weakness: Section 3’s exposition is hard to follow with abrupt transitions and insufficient context before equations.  
- Weakness: the novelty and significance of the divergence bound (Section 3.1) should be explicitly stated and highlighted.  
- Weakness: “AutoBDMC” is never formally defined and should be concisely defined early, before technical use.  
- Minor: possible typo at line 149—`p(theta, z | y)` likely should be `p(theta, z, y)`.  
- Minor: inconsistent notation for observed data (`y` vs `D`)—use one consistently.  
- Minor: supplement code should specify WebPPL version and installation/use instructions (npm package suggested) and provide user-friendly Stan usage guidance.  
- Conclusion: strong, well-validated contribution; improving clarity around theoretical contributions would enhance impact.
