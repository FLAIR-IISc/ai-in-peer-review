This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The idea of leveraging bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose convergence failures of a sampler is particularly appealing, as existing MCMC diagnostics are indeed problematic. Notably, this approach bears some resemblance to the methods proposed in [1,2], which assess the performance of MCMC samplers by running them on data sampled from the prior.

However, some aspects of the paper require clarification. Specifically, the derivation in Section 3.1 is challenging to follow. The notation q(.) is introduced before its definition, which appears to represent the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after T steps. This interpretation is inferred from context, but it would be beneficial to provide an explicit definition to avoid confusion. While the supplement offers some additional clarity, the main text should be self-contained, and the reader should not need to consult the supplement to understand the method.

Furthermore, it would be helpful to provide a clear, step-by-step explanation of how to apply the method from start to finish. Currently, the reader must piece together the various components of the approach, which can be somewhat cumbersome.

On a minor note, the distinction between unobserved variables z and theta seems unnecessary in this general setting, as they are always used together. Consider eliminating one of these variables to simplify the notation. Additionally, there is a missing close parenthesis on line 165.

Overall, the paper presents an intriguing approach to evaluating MCMC implementations in PPLs. With some revisions to address the clarity and presentation of the method, it has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
