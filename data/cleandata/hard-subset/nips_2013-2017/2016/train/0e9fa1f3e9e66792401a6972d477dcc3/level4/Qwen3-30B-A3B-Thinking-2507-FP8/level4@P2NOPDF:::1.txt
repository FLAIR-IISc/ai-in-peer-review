

This paper introduces bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) for estimating the Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, with a focus on debugging and validating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The core contribution—using BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures—is compelling, as existing MCMC diagnostics remain limited. This approach aligns well with prior work by Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012), which similarly assess MCMC performance by testing samplers on data drawn from the prior.  

However, the paper requires significant revisions for clarity and completeness. Section 3.1’s derivation is difficult to follow due to unclear notation. Specifically, the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps (denoted *q*(·)) is used before its definition. While the supplement clarifies this, the main text must stand alone without requiring supplemental reading. The authors should explicitly define *q*(·) upon first use and provide a concise, step-by-step guide to applying the method—from data preparation to divergence computation—within the main body. This would greatly enhance reproducibility and accessibility.  

The distinction between unobserved variables *z* and *θ* introduces unnecessary complexity. In a general setting, these variables are indistinguishable and always appear jointly; eliminating one notation (e.g., using *θ* exclusively) would streamline the presentation. Additionally, a missing closing parenthesis appears after "log" on line 165.  

The paper’s strengths lie in its practical motivation for MCMC validation and alignment with established diagnostic frameworks. Yet, the current exposition risks obscuring the method’s utility. For instance, the claim that BDMC "diagnoses failures" lacks concrete examples of failure modes it addresses (e.g., multimodality, slow mixing), which would strengthen the argument. The connection to PPL debugging could also be expanded: how would practitioners integrate this into existing toolchains?  

Minor improvements would significantly enhance readability. Replace "a little cluttered" with "the distinction between *z* and *θ* is redundant," and clarify ambiguous phrasing like "test the ability of MCMC samplers" (e.g., "evaluate sampler performance on prior-sampled data"). The references [1,2] are appropriately cited but should be formatted as (Geweke, 2004; Cook et al., 2012) in the text.  

**Summary of Required Revisions:**  
1. **Clarify notation:** Define *q*(·) explicitly in Section 3.1.  
2. **Add application guide:** Include a high-level workflow for method deployment.  
3. **Simplify notation:** Eliminate redundant *z*/*θ* distinction.  
4. **Fix minor errors:** Correct line 165 typo and tighten prose.  
5. **Strengthen motivation:** Specify concrete failure cases BDMC diagnoses.  

With these revisions, the paper would make a valuable contribution to MCMC validation. As written, the technical merits are promising but obscured by presentation issues. The authors should prioritize making the method accessible to PPL developers, who are the intended audience.
