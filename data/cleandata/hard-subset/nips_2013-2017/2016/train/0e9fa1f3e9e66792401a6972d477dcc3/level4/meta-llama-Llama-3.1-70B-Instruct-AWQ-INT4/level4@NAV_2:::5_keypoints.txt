- Proposal: Use Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds to assess MCMC reliability and choose the sampler that yields the tightest bounds for the same compute.
- Core challenge: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound.
- Heuristic solution: Run diagnostics on posteriors from synthetic data (generated from chosen hyperparameters) where approximate/near-exact samples are easier to obtain.
- Technical contribution: Extend BMC to bound the symmetrized KL divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate MCMC samples.
- Implementation: Integrated the method into WebPPL and Stan.
- Empirical evaluation: Validated on several models/datasets; experiments show the method can evaluate posterior inference accuracy in realistic settings.
- Key assumption 1: Hyperparameters used to generate synthetic data are representative of the whole posterior — may fail for multimodal or complex posteriors.
- Key assumption 2: Posterior from synthetic data has properties similar to the original posterior — may not hold under model misspecification.
- Optimistic assumption concern: Starting from hyperparameters yields an almost exact sample quickly — questionable for complex or highly multimodal posteriors.
- Evidence limitation: Current evidence is mostly on simple examples; more simulations needed comparing against standard diagnostics.
- Suggested improvements: Provide more experiments on multimodal/misspecified models and head-to-head comparisons with common diagnostic methods.
- Clarity and quality: Paper is well-written with clear explanation of BMC and application to MCMC diagnostics.
- Originality and significance: Novel extension of BMC to MCMC diagnostics with potential impact if shown effective in complex scenarios.
- Overall verdict: Good contribution but requires additional work to demonstrate robustness in more complex settings.
- Rating: 7/10.
