

This paper introduces bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) for estimating the Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, specifically targeting the debugging and evaluation of MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The core idea of leveraging BDMC to diagnose sampler non-convergence is promising and addresses a genuine gap, as current MCMC diagnostics remain inadequate. The approach aligns closely with highly relevant work in [1,2], which similarly assesses sampler performance by testing on data generated from the prior.  

Section 3.1 presents significant clarity issues: the notation $q(\cdot)$ is employed before definition, and its intended meaning (the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after $T$ steps) requires explicit clarification. While the supplement offers marginal improvement, the main text must stand independently without supplement dependency. Furthermore, a concise, step-by-step procedural guide for applying the method would greatly enhance accessibility—readers should not need to reconstruct the workflow from scattered details.  

Minor points: The separation of unobserved variables into $z$ and $\theta$ is unnecessarily cluttered, as they are functionally indistinct in general settings and should be consolidated. Line 165 contains a missing closing parenthesis.
