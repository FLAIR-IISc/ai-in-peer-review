This paper presents an implementation of the bidirectional Monte Carlo method and reports on its application within two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan. The work serves to summarize this previously published technique and demonstrate its integration into existing frameworks.

While the implementation of a sophisticated sampling method in popular languages like WebPPL and Stan is a valuable practical exercise, the primary concern with this submission is its limited novelty. The paper focuses on describing the application of a known technique rather than introducing new algorithms, theoretical insights, or a significant empirical investigation that would advance the state of the art. The core ideas of bidirectional Monte Carlo are not original to this work, and the contribution appears to be confined to the software engineering effort of its implementation.

For a top-tier conference like NIPS, a substantial methodological or theoretical contribution is expected. A paper centered on the implementation of existing methods, without a rigorous, large-scale empirical comparison or novel theoretical analysis, typically falls outside the scope of the main conference.

In its current form, this work reads more like a technical report or an application note. It would likely be a much stronger and more appropriate contribution for a specialized venue, such as a workshop on probabilistic programming or MCMC methods. In that context, the focus on practical implementation details would be highly relevant and could foster valuable discussion among practitioners.
