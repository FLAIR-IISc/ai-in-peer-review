- Paper proposes using the BDMC stochastic upper/lower bound gap as a universal metric for MCMC operator quality and implements BDMC in Stan and WebPPL.  
- Main strength: elegant, simple diagnostic (BDMC gap) that is easy to implement and valuable for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty and technical depth beyond the original BDMC work; Sections 3.1–3.2 feel compressed and poorly written.  
- Key technical observation (possibly not fully novel): the BDMC gap upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between approximate and true posteriors.  
- Major related-work omission: no citation or discussion of Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely connected to BDMC.  
- Important connection: the Tempered Transitions acceptance ratio appears to equal the ratio of BDMC’s stochastic bounds; this link should be explored and cited.  
- Temperature schedule ({βt}) discussion is insufficient; step size Δβ trade-offs (acceptance vs. efficiency) are well-known from TT and apply to BDMC.  
- Suggestion: need for adaptive/refinement scheme (AutoBDMC) to iteratively tighten temperature schedule until bounds are reliable.  
- Experimental shortcomings: no systematic study showing how bounds vary with step size; comparisons may be unreliable if log-gap > ~1 nat.  
- AutoBDMC is never explicitly defined; the paper should state precisely what “AutoBDMC” entails.  
- Clarity issue: definition of vector v ("all variables sampled in AIS before final stage") is ambiguous and should be explicit (e.g., z1:T-1, θ1:T-1) for both AIS sweeps.  
- Notation inconsistency: inconsistent use of "rev"/"fwd" vs. "for"/"back" creates confusion.  
- Mathematical clarity: estimator ĥp(y) is not defined in expectations in equations (5–7) and (9–11); expectations likely refer to log-ratios but this must be clarified.  
- Equation error: Equation (6) is incorrect as written—the KL divergence term uses identical distributions and should be corrected (likely involves p(z,θ|y) and q_back(v|z,θ,y)).  
- Experiments: choice of βt is not explained in main text (appendix code suggests uniform steps = 1/options.steps); this should be stated.  
- Figure/plot issues: unclear what “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” refers to (is it BDMC T?), acronyms like RAIS and variants (u)c(R)AIS are undefined, and plot font sizes should be increased.  
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance if authors address clarity, related work (Neal/TT), and experimental completeness in the final version.  
- Typos: missing closing parentheses in D_JS expression and NUTS citation author should be “Hoffman” (not “Homan”).
