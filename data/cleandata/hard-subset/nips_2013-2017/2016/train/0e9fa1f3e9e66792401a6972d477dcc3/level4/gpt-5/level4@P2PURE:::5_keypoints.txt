- Proposes using BMC upper/lower stochastic bounds on log marginal likelihood to assess MCMC reliability and compare schemes by tightness under equal computational budgets.  
- Main obstacle: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound.  
- Heuristic solution: run diagnostics on the posterior induced by synthetic data generated from a representative hyperparameter (eta_real) instead of the observed-data posterior.  
- Tests on examples; approach is interesting and offers a theoretically grounded diagnostic, but is tailored to AIS/SMC and marginal-likelihood–based checks.  
- Implementation relies on heuristics that undermine the core BMC requirement, making the diagnostic less compelling.  
- Concern 1: Method assumes eta_real is known and representative, which presumes an already-converged MCMC and that a single parameter summarizes the posterior—strong, unjustified assumptions.  
- Concern 2: Assumes the synthetic-data posterior has convergence behavior similar to the original posterior; this is unjustified, especially under model misspecification, and effectively changes the problem.  
- Concern 3: Assumes initializing at eta_real lets MCMC quickly produce an almost exact sample; this optimistic assumption is not guaranteed.  
- Consequently, the methodology depends on assumptions about the very convergence behavior it aims to evaluate, undermining validity.  
- Section 5.1’s simple examples do not suffice to demonstrate robustness in more complex settings.  
- Authors should explain and provide evidence why their method handles multimodality or insufficiency of summary statistics better than existing diagnostics.  
- Recommend comparative simulations against common diagnostics to illustrate scenarios where the proposed method is more reliable.
