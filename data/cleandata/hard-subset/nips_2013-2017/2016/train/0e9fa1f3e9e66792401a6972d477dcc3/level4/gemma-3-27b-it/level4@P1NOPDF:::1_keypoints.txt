- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target, aimed at debugging/evaluating MCMC in probabilistic programming languages.

- Reviewer finds leveraging BDMC for diagnosing sampler convergence failures compelling and potentially valuable given limits of existing diagnostics.

- Paper appropriately cites prior works ([1,2]) that validate MCMC performance using data drawn from the prior.

- Section 3.1 is difficult to follow: notation q(.) is used before being defined and should be explicitly stated (likely the AIS marginal after T steps).

- Main text is not self-contained; readers must consult the supplement—authors should provide a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the method in the main paper.

- Minor clarity suggestion: splitting unobserved variables into z and θ is arbitrary and could be simplified (e.g., use θ only) to reduce visual clutter.

- Typo: missing closing parenthesis on Line 165.
