- Paper proposes using the gap between BDMC's stochastic lower and upper AIS bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC transition operators.  
- Strength: idea is simple, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact, especially for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main concern: technical contribution and novelty are modest relative to existing BDMC work.  
- Key theoretical observation: the bound gap is an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence — nice but arguably not entirely novel.  
- Missing related work: authors do not cite Radford Neal's Tempered Transitions (TT), which is highly relevant.  
- Strong connection to TT: TT uses the same reverse/forward sweeps and its acceptance ratio appears to equal the ratio of BDMC's stochastic bounds.  
- Beta schedule issue: paper underemphasizes choice of β_t and Δβ, though these strongly affect bounds and reliability.  
- Practical suggestion: authors could develop an iterative schedule to decrease Δβ until bounds are sufficiently tight (AutoBDMC unclear on this).  
- Experiments are insufficiently thorough; need systematic study of how bounds vary with step size and schedule.  
- Ambiguity in notation: definition of vector v is loose and unclear given two AIS sweeps.  
- Inconsistent labels: uses "rev"/"fwd" and "for"/"back" inconsistently, adding confusion.  
- Missing estimator definition: \hat{p}(y) (or which estimator is used) is not defined in equations (5–7) and (9–11).  
- Equation error: Equation (6) as written is nonsensical (KL between identical distributions) and likely contains a typo.  
- AutoBDMC is never unambiguously defined; unclear if it means gap measurement plus fixed hyperparameter scheme.  
- Experimental details unclear: how β_t is chosen is not stated in the paper (code hints at step=1/options.steps but not documented).  
- Figure/caption problems: ambiguous labels (e.g., "HMC / No-U-Turn steps"), undefined acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS), and small axis/tick labels.  
- Minor typos: missing parentheses in D_JS expression and citation typo "Homan" → "Hoffman."  
- Overall verdict: promising and useful approach but needs clearer presentation, stronger related-work discussion, corrected equations/notation, and more thorough experiments before acceptance.
