This paper proposes a novel approach to estimating the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The idea of utilizing bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose convergence failures in samplers is particularly appealing, as existing MCMC diagnostics have been shown to be problematic.

The authors' approach is reminiscent of previous work by Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012), which also assess the performance of MCMC samplers by running them on data sampled from the prior. These references are highly relevant to the proposed method and provide a useful context for understanding the contributions of this paper.

However, the derivation in Section 3.1 is somewhat difficult to follow. The notation q(.) is introduced before its definition, which may cause confusion for readers. From the context, it appears that q(.) represents the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after T steps. It would be beneficial to explicitly define q(.) to avoid ambiguity. While the supplement provides some clarification, it is essential to ensure that the main text is self-contained and does not require the reader to refer to the supplement for understanding.

A clear, step-by-step explanation of how to apply the proposed method from start to finish would be helpful. Currently, readers must piece together the various components of the approach, which may lead to confusion. Providing a concise summary or a flowchart illustrating the method's application would enhance the paper's clarity.

Some minor suggestions for improvement include simplifying the notation by eliminating the distinction between unobserved variables z and θ, as they are always used together in the general setting. Additionally, there is a missing close parenthesis on line 165.

Overall, the proposed approach has the potential to be a valuable tool for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in PPLs. With some revisions to address the mentioned issues, the paper can become more accessible and easier to understand for readers.
