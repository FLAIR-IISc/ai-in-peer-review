- Proposes BREAD: combines forward and reverse AIS to bound Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors.  
- Extends BDMC analysis to derive an estimator that upper-bounds Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Shows the bound closely matches true Jeffreys divergence on toy examples.  
- Proposes a protocol to apply the method to real-world data by simulating from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan and validates across multiple models.  
- Demonstrates practical utility, including debugging (found a WebPPL multivariate Gaussian bug).  
- Theoretical derivations and experimental validation are rigorous and reproducible.  
- Main limitation: requires exact posterior samples (simulated data), restricting real-world applicability.  
- Originality concern: builds directly on Grosse et al. (2015) and does not clearly distinguish novel vs. extended contributions.  
- Related work is cited (including Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka 2016) but relationships could be clearer.  
- Significance is limited because applicability to many real problems is constrained by the simulation requirement.  
- Useful for probabilistic programming users and model-representation decisions, but not a major advance in MCMC methodology.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, accessible to the NIPS audience.  
- Recommendation: better suited to probabilistic programming/MCMC-focused venues rather than NIPS due to modest novelty and constrained impact.
