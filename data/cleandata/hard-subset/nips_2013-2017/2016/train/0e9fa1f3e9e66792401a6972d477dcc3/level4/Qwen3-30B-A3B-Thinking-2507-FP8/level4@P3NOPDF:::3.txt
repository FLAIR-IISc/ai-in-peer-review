

Review  

This paper proposes a simple yet potentially impactful method for evaluating MCMC transition operators using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC), which computes lower and upper bounds on the marginal likelihood via two annealed importance sampling (AIS) sweeps. The authors leverage the gap between these bounds as a generic convergence metric, implement it for Stan and WebPPL, and propose a protocol for assessing posterior inference quality. The core idea is elegant and straightforward to deploy, making it highly relevant for probabilistic programming systems (PPS)—a key use case the paper rightly emphasizes. If adopted widely, this method could significantly improve MCMC diagnostics in PPS, representing a strong point for potential high impact.  

However, the paper’s technical contributions are modest and suffer from insufficient novelty justification and clarity issues. The primary novelty—recognizing that the bound gap upper-bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence—is insightful but arguably not entirely new; it closely parallels Neal’s Tempered Transitions (TT) [1996], which the authors omit from related work. This omission is critical: TT performs identical reverse/forward AIS sweeps (termed "heating" and "annealing" in TT) to improve mixing in multimodal targets, using the ratio of accumulated weights as an acceptance probability. Crucially, Neal’s Section 3.3 explicitly frames this ratio as bounding the log-marginal likelihood gap, with acceptance probability 1 when bounds converge. The authors’ BDMC gap is thus directly analogous to TT’s acceptance ratio, yet the connection remains unaddressed. This oversight weakens the novelty claim and misses an opportunity to contextualize their work.  

Clarity issues compound these concerns. Key definitions are ambiguous:  
- The vector $\vec{v}$ is defined as "all variables sampled in AIS before the final stage," but this is unclear given two AIS sweeps (e.g., whether it includes both reverse and forward samples). The paper should specify $\{z_{1:T-1}, \theta_{1:T-1}\}$ and resolve inconsistent labels (e.g., "rev/fwd" vs. "for/back").  
- Equations (5–7) and (9–11) reference $\hat{p}(y)$ without defining the estimator, causing confusion. The intended expressions likely involve $\mathbb{E}[\log \hat{R}_{\text{rev}}]$ and $\mathbb{E}[\log \hat{R}_{\text{fwd}}]$.  
- Equation (6) as written is invalid (KL divergence over identical distributions), and the correct form appears to require $p(z, \theta | y)$ vs. $q_{\text{back}}(v | z, \theta, y)$.  
- The term "AutoBDMC" lacks a precise definition. It seems to combine (a) bound-gap assessment and (b) the fixed $\beta_t$ schedule in Section 3.2, but this is not explicitly stated.  

The experimental section also requires refinement. The choice of $\beta_t$ (e.g., $\beta_t = 1 - (t-1)/T$) is not clearly motivated, though the appendix reveals a linear schedule. A systematic study of how bound gaps scale with $\Delta\beta$ (e.g., showing gaps $>1$ nat necessitate smaller steps) would strengthen the argument for adaptive scheduling. The "auto" aspect of AutoBDMC could be enhanced by proposing an iterative $\Delta\beta$ reduction protocol until convergence, a natural extension the paper overlooks. Additionally:  
- The caption for Figures 2–3 should define acronyms (e.g., RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS).  
- Axis labels in figures need larger font sizes for readability.  
- A typo ("Homan" in the NUTS citation) requires correction.  

Despite these issues, the core idea holds value. The simplicity of the bound-gap metric—requiring minimal implementation beyond existing AIS—makes it highly accessible for PPS developers. If the authors address the missing TT connection, clarify definitions, and expand the experimental analysis (e.g., bound sensitivity to $\Delta\beta$), the paper would be significantly stronger.  

**Recommendation**: **Weak Accept** pending camera-ready revisions. The method is promising and practical, but the current version’s lack of novelty context and technical ambiguities must be resolved. Specifically:  
1. Cite Neal’s TT paper and explicitly discuss its relationship to BDMC.  
2. Clarify all definitions (e.g., $\vec{v}$, AutoBDMC, $\hat{p}(y)$) and correct equation errors.  
3. Add a subsection analyzing bound gaps across $\Delta\beta$ values and propose an adaptive scheme.  
4. Fix typos and figure readability.  

The authors’ response should convincingly demonstrate that these gaps are minor and resolvable. Given the method’s potential utility for the PPS community, this paper deserves acceptance if these points are addressed. The current draft is a solid foundation but requires tightening to fully realize its impact.
