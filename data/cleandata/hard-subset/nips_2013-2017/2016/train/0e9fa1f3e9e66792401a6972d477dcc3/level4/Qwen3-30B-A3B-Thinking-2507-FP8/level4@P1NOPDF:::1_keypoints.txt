- Introduces BDMC to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target, aimed at debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.
- Core idea is compelling and addresses a gap because existing MCMC diagnostics often lack robustness.
- Connection to prior work [1,2] using prior-sampled data is relevant and well-motivated.
- Main text has significant clarity issues; Section 3.1’s derivation is difficult to follow.
- Symbol q(·) appears before definition and is ambiguous; needs explicit statement (likely the AIS marginal after T steps).
- The paper should be self-contained; readers shouldn’t need the supplement to understand the core method.
- Provide a concise, step-by-step guide for applying the method (data preparation through divergence estimation).
- Minor: unnecessary distinction between unobserved variables z and θ—use a single variable to improve readability.
- Minor: typographical error—missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
- Overall assessment: promising approach with a sound technical foundation and strong potential for improving MCMC validation in practice.
