This paper presents a method—termed AutoBDMC—for assessing MCMC convergence. The method leverages the recently-developed Bidirectional Monte Carlo method to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood, relying on annealed importance sampling. The authors demonstrate that the gap between these upper and lower bounds provides a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, yielding a single scalar quantity to assess MCMC convergence behavior. While the method can currently only analyze convergence on synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors provide experimental evidence that analyses performed on such synthetic data are predictive of behavior on real-world data. They also demonstrate applications of their method for model selection and debugging. Overall, I found this paper to be quite readable and well-motivated. The presented method appears clearly useful, and the experimental evaluation is reasonably compelling. 

I found Section 3 more challenging to follow than the rest of the paper. The transition into Section 3.1 is especially abrupt, launching into variable and equation definitions without sufficient motivation. If the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 is a novel result – which I believe it is – this should be explicitly stated, as the novelty and core contribution of the paper seem to hinge on it. Furthermore, the paper lacks a precise definition of “AutoBDMC.” In my understanding, AutoBDMC is the approach of using annealed importance sampling (AIS) and reverse AIS (rAIS) to analyze MCMC convergence, and I would expect to see a clear statement to that effect. By the time Section 3.2 states that “…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC,” the method should have been explicitly defined. 

Several minor points also require attention: Line 149: Should *p(θ, z | y)* be *p(θ, z, y)*? Notational inconsistencies exist, with the paper switching between *y* and *D* to denote data/observations, which occasionally caused confusion. The WebPPL code in the supplemental material should be accompanied by (a) information regarding which version(s) of WebPPL it is known to work with, and (b) instructions on how to integrate it into a WebPPL distribution for use. Even better, releasing this as a WebPPL package installable via npm would be highly beneficial. If possible, a similar package release for the Stan version of the code would also be much appreciated.
