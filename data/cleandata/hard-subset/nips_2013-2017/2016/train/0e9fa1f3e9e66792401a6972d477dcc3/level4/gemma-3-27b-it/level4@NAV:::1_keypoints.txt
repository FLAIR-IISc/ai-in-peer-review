- Introduces a novel use of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target.  
- Targets debugging and evaluation of MCMC implementations in Probabilistic Programming Languages (PPLs), a valuable practical contribution.  
- Using J-S divergence via BDMC to detect sampler convergence issues is promising given limitations of current diagnostics.  
- Situates the method relative to prior predictive checking and validation methods (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2006).  
- Derivation in Section 3.1 is unclear and needs significant clarification.  
- The notation q(.) is introduced without definition; it should be explicitly defined as the AIS marginal after T steps.  
- Core method should be fully explained in the main text (supplementary material shouldn’t be required).  
- Provide a streamlined, step‑by‑step description of how to apply the method for better accessibility.  
- Separating unobserved variables into z and θ is unnecessarily complex and could be simplified.  
- Minor typographical error: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
