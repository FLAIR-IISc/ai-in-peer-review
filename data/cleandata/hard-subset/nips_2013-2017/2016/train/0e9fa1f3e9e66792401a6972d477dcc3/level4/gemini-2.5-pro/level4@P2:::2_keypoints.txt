- Introduces BREAD, extending BDMC/AIS to bound Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors via the expected forward–reverse log-likelihood gap.  
- Theoretical result: the AIS forward–reverse gap provides an upper bound on the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Rigorous guarantees require exact posterior samples, so theoretical validity is limited to simulated data.  
- Proposes a practical real-data protocol: infer hyperparameters, simulate dataset from them, and apply BREAD to the simulated data.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating real PPL integration.  
- Empirical validation shows forward AIS behavior on simulated data mirrors behavior on real data under the proposed protocol.  
- Shows robustness to using approximate posterior samples to initialize the reverse chain’s hyperparameters.  
- Uses BREAD to compare collapsed vs uncollapsed matrix factorization representations, providing actionable modeling guidance.  
- Discovered a bug in WebPPL’s sampler, illustrating utility for software debugging and verification.  
- Strength: novel, principled alternative to heuristic convergence diagnostics, addressing a fundamental inference-quality problem.  
- Strength: practical utility demonstrated via implementations, case studies, and experimental rigor.  
- Strength: clear exposition and well-organized presentation, with straightforward derivations.  
- Weakness: direct applicability is limited to AIS/SMC-style samplers, with only indirect implications for standard MCMC (e.g., HMC).  
- Weakness: real-data protocol is heuristic and may fail under severe model misspecification; potential failure modes need discussion.  
- Weakness: computationally intensive—requires many forward/reverse AIS runs; cost vs. benefit analysis is lacking.  
- Overall assessment: significant, well-validated contribution with practical impact; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
