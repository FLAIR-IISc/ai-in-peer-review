- Paper proposes using BDMC/AIS to estimate/bound Jeffreys divergence between MCMC output and the true posterior for debugging and evaluation.
- Core idea: extend BDMC to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence by running AIS/SMC chains forward and reverse.
- Motivated by shortcomings of existing MCMC diagnostics and builds on prior work (Grosse et al. 2015; Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2012).
- Method integrated into WebPPL and Stan, making it practically accessible to practitioners.
- Validated on several models and datasets, including matrix factorization examples that show BREAD can guide modeling choices.
- Practical payoff demonstrated by detecting a bug in WebPPL, illustrating debugging utility.
- Strength: novel application of BDMC to assess sampler accuracy rather than just marginal likelihoods.
- Strength: provides rigorous expected upper bounds on Jeffreys divergence for quantitative assessment.
- Strength: concrete implementation in popular PPLs increases impact and usability.
- Weakness: derivation (Section 3.1) is hard to follow; notation (especially q(.)) is unclear and needs clarification or appendix details.
- Weakness: the paper lacks a concise, step-by-step exposition of how to apply the method in practice.
- Weakness: method relies on access to exact posterior samples for the reverse chain, limiting general applicability to many real-world MCMC scenarios.
- Weakness: distinction between latent variables z and parameters θ is unnecessary and complicates notation; a unified variable would streamline exposition.
- Minor issue: typographical error (missing closing parenthesis on line 165).
- Argument for acceptance: novel, technically sound approach addressing a long-standing challenge with rigorous guarantees and practical tooling.
- Argument against acceptance: clarity and derivation presentation need improvement; reliance on exact posterior samples and limited validation reduce immediate applicability.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with substantial contribution and practical impact; recommend acceptance conditional on addressing clarity and limitation discussions.
