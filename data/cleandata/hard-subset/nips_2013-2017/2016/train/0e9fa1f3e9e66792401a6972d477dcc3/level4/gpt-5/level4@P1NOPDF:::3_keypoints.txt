- Paper proposes using BDMC (bidirectional AIS) gap as a general performance measure for MCMC transition operators and implements BDMC in Stan and WebPPL with an evaluation protocol.
- Strength: the idea is simple, elegant, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Main novelty is limited: primary new observation is that the lower–upper bound gap upper-bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence, which may not be entirely new.
- Related work is incomplete: the paper (and cited BDMC manuscript) omits Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions, which closely relates to BDMC.
- There is a close analogy between TT acceptance ratios and the BDMC bound ratio that the paper fails to highlight.
- The choice of β_t (annealing schedule) is unclear; Δβ affects bounds and acceptance, and an adaptive schedule to reduce Δβ until bounds are tight is suggested but not explored.
- Experiments lack a systematic study of how the BDMC bounds vary with step size (Δβ) or number of steps.
- Clarity issues in Sections 3.1–3.2 with rushed writing and resulting derivation confusion.
- Ambiguous notation: the variable v is loosely defined (confusion about {z_{1:T−1}, θ_{1:T−1}} and across two AIS sweeps).
- Inconsistent labels (rev/fwd vs for/back) create confusion about sweep semantics.
- The estimator p̂(y) is not defined in equations (5–7) and (9–11), leaving unclear what expectations like E[log R̂_rev] refer to.
- Equation (6) appears incorrect because the KL divergence is written with identical distributions; the correct RHS should involve p(z, θ | y) and q_back(v | z, θ, y).
- AutoBDMC is never unambiguously defined—unclear whether it means using the gap to assess convergence or the fixed hyperparameter scheme in Section 3.2.
- Experimental details unclear: how β_t is chosen (appendix suggests step = 1/options.steps), and whether reported HMC/NUTS steps correspond to BDMC steps needs explicit statement.
- Acronyms and labels in figures/captions are undefined or unclear (e.g., RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS), and plot readability needs larger axes/tick labels.
- Minor typos: missing closing parentheses in D_KL terms in the D_JS expression and a NUTS citation typo (Homan → Hoffman).
- Overall recommendation: useful paper that could be accepted if clarity and related-work discussion are improved and experiments expanded/clarified.
