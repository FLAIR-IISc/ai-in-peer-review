- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BREAD) to bound the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target, aimed at debugging/evaluating samplers in PPLs.
- Well-motivated approach addressing shortcomings of existing MCMC diagnostics.
- Technique (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) uses bidirectional Monte Carlo on simulated data and provides a protocol for real data analysis.
- Technical contributions are sound with a thorough theoretical analysis.
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow; notation and presentation need clarification.
- The paper should include a clear, step-by-step statement of how to apply the method end-to-end.
- Notation is confusing (splitting unobserved variables into z and θ); a single unified notation is recommended.
- Experiments are thorough, well-designed, and compare results to other methods, demonstrating effectiveness.
- Related work discussion is good but should explicitly cite Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012) for prior-sampled-data MCMC tests.
- Recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address notation, clarity, and related-work citations.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution to probabilistic programming; Rating 8/10, Confidence 8/10.
