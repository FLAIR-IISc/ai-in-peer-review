- Proposes AutoBDMC: uses AIS and reverse AIS to get stochastic upper/lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood, with the gap giving an upper bound on Jensen–Shannon divergence as a single scalar MCMC convergence diagnostic.
- Method requires synthetic data from the joint model; paper evaluates whether diagnostics on synthetic data predict behavior on real datasets.
- Experiments support the synthetic-to-real predictive claim and demonstrate applications to model selection and debugging.
- Overall paper is readable, well-motivated, and the method appears practically useful with a reasonably convincing empirical evaluation.
- Section 3 is substantially harder to follow than the rest; the transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces variables/equations without sufficient motivation or intuition.
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, this should be stated explicitly and highlighted as a central contribution.
- The paper needs a clear, early definition of “AutoBDMC” (whether it means the AIS procedure, the divergence diagnostic, or the full automated pipeline) and an algorithmic summary near the start.
- By the time Section 3.2 refers to “this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC,” the term should already be unambiguous.
- Notation inconsistency: alternate use of y and D for observed data—use a single symbol consistently.
- Minor correction requested: verify whether p(θ, z | y) on Line 149 should be p(θ, z, y).
- Reproducibility: specify WebPPL version(s), provide integration instructions, and ideally release the WebPPL and Stan code as npm-installable / packaged releases.
