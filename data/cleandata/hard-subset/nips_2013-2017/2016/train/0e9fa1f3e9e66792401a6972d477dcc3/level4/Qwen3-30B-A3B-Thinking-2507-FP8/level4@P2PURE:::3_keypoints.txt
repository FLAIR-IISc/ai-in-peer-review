- Paper proposes using BDMC gap (difference between bidirectional AIS bounds) as a general metric to evaluate MCMC transition operators and provides Stan/WebPPL implementations and a protocol for posterior assessment.  
- Major strength: method is simple, implementable, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty and modest technical contributions relative to existing BDMC work; Sections 3.1–3.2 are underdeveloped and unclearly written.  
- Key technical observation (upper bound on Jensen-Shannon divergence) is insightful but may not be entirely novel.  
- Significant omission: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is not cited or discussed despite close methodological connections to BDMC.  
- Neal’s TT provides the same reverse/forward sweeps and relates the acceptance ratio to the ratio of stochastic bounds, a connection the paper misses.  
- The paper’s focus on β_t selection is questioned because TT already addresses Δβ trade-offs and suggests reducing Δβ when bounds are too wide.  
- AutoBDMC is ambiguously defined; the authors should clarify whether it means an iterative Δβ schedule or a fixed hyperparameter scheme.  
- The experimental section lacks a systematic study of how bound gaps vary with step size Δβ or other BDMC parameters.  
- Experiments: β_t parameterization is unclear (appendix hints at step-based β_t but not stated), and figure captions/labels (e.g., HMC/NUTS steps, RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) are undefined or ambiguous.  
- Presentation issues: small axes/tick labels in figures reduce readability.  
- Clarity problems in notation and equations: ambiguous definition of vector v, inconsistent forward/reverse labeling, missing definition of estimator \hat{p}(y), and apparent errors in Equations (5–7), (9–11), and (6).  
- Suggestion: when log-bound gap exceeds ~1 nat, comparisons between MCMC operators become unreliable and Δβ should be reduced or BDMC rerun with finer steps.  
- If authors address clarity and related-work omissions in a revision, the reviewer would support acceptance.  
- Minor typos: missing closing parentheses in D_KL terms within the D_JS expression and "Homan" should be "Hoffman."
