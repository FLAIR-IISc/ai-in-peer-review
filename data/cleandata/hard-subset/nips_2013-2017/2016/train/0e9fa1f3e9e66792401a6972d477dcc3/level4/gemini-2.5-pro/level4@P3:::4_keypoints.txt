- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing), extending BDMC by running AIS forward and reverse to produce stochastic lower/upper bounds on log marginal likelihood.  
- Shows theoretically that the gap between forward and reverse bounds upper-bounds, in expectation, the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate samples and the true posterior.  
- Reverse pass requires an exact posterior sample, so the method is rigorous only on simulated data; authors propose fitting hyperparameters on real data and simulating from them to evaluate real-world performance.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical utility for comparing model representations and debugging inference implementations (including finding a WebPPL bug).  
- Significance: addresses the important, difficult problem of diagnosing MCMC/sample quality and offers a quantitative, interpretable metric beyond common heuristics.  
- Theoretical quality: elegant, well-founded derivation connecting BDMC bound gap to Jeffreys divergence.  
- Empirical quality: thorough experiments across five models that validate simulated-to-real predictive assumption and demonstrate practical use cases.  
- Clarity/originality: generally well-written and a novel extension of BDMC to posterior-approximation diagnostics.  
- Weakness — exposition: Section 3.1 (Jeffreys divergence derivation) needs a clearer introductory statement and explicit linking of Equations (8) and (11).  
- Weakness — notation: inconsistent use of y vs. D for observed data and a potentially confusing presentation of Equation (3) that could use brief clarification.  
- Weakness — reproducibility/usability: code is provided but needs more detailed instructions, version compatibility info, and ideally packaging (e.g., npm) to ease adoption.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality contribution with minor presentation and usability issues; recommend acceptance.
