- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.  
- Idea is appealing because existing MCMC diagnostics are problematic and BDMC can diagnose convergence failures.  
- Method resembles prior approaches that evaluate MCMC by running samplers on data sampled from the prior (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2006).  
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow; notation q(.) is introduced before being defined.  
- q(.) likely denotes the AIS sampler’s marginal after T steps but needs an explicit definition in the main text.  
- The supplement helps but the main paper should be self-contained and not require supplement consultation.  
- Provide a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the method from start to finish.  
- The distinction between unobserved variables z and theta seems unnecessary; simplify notation by eliminating one.  
- Minor edit: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.  
- Overall: intriguing and potentially valuable contribution conditional on improved clarity and presentation.
