- Proposes using the BDMC lower/upper marginal likelihood bounds gap as a simple, practical convergence metric for MCMC, implemented for Stan and WebPPL.  
- The idea is elegant, easy to deploy in probabilistic programming systems (PPS), and could substantially improve MCMC diagnostics if widely adopted.  
- Technical novelty is modest and under-justified; the key insight (bound gap upper-bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence) is insightful but not clearly novel.  
- The paper omits a critical related work: Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which uses equivalent forward/reverse AIS sweeps and discusses the same log-marginal gap.  
- Fails to contextualize BDMC relative to Neal’s TT, weakening the novelty claim and missing an important conceptual connection.  
- Several clarity issues in definitions: ambiguous specification of vector v (which samples it includes) and inconsistent labeling (rev/fwd vs. for/back).  
- Mathematical presentation problems: estimator \hat{p}(y) is not defined in key equations, several equations (e.g., Eq. 6) appear invalid or incorrectly formed.  
- The term "AutoBDMC" is not precisely defined and seems to conflate bound-gap assessment with a fixed β_t schedule.  
- Experimental design lacks motivation for the chosen β_t schedule (linear) and should systematically study how bound gaps scale with Δβ.  
- The paper should propose an adaptive/iterative Δβ reduction protocol to realize the “auto” aspect; current experiments miss this natural extension.  
- Presentation issues: figure captions should define acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS), axis fonts need enlarging, and there is a typo in the NUTS citation (“Homan”).  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept pending camera-ready fixes, specifically to cite Neal TT, clarify definitions and equations, analyze bound sensitivity to Δβ and propose adaptivity, and fix typos/figure readability.
