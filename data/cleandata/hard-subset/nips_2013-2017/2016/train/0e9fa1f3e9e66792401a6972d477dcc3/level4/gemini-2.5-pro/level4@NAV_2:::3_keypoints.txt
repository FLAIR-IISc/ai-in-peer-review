- Core idea: extends BDMC by using the gap between forward and reverse AIS bounds (BREAD) to quantify inference quality.
- Theoretical claim: the expected bound gap upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posterior.
- Implementation: BREAD is implemented and evaluated in Stan and WebPPL, with a protocol for real datasets.
- Strength: elegant, practical, automated diagnostic for MCMC in probabilistic programming; potentially high impact.
- Originality concern: novelty over BDMC is limited—main step is interpreting the bound gap as a Jeffreys divergence bound.
- Major omission: fails to cite/discuss Radford Neal’s 1996 Tempered Transitions, which is closely related algorithmically and conceptually.
- Clarity issue: excessive BDMC review leaves novel contributions (Sec. 3) feeling rushed and under-explained.
- Ambiguity: definition of v (“variables sampled in AIS before final stage”) is unclear which variables/chains are included.
- Derivation problems: Equations (6–11) are hard to follow; estimator inside expectations (e.g., E[log R̂]) is not specified.
- KL term confusion: Equation (7)’s DKL expression and its relation to later Jeffreys divergence bounds are not clearly explained.
- Annealing schedule: choice of intermediate distributions/temperatures is critical but poorly discussed; no guidance on selecting schedule or interpreting large gaps.
- Experimental details lacking: number of annealing steps not specified in main text and appears fixed in appendix code.
- Figure ambiguities: “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” label is unclear (temperatures vs leapfrog steps), acronyms in captions are undefined, and axis/tick labels are too small.
- Recommendation: paper has strong potential but needs to address Tempered Transitions, clarify technical derivations, and improve experimental methodology.
- Minor corrections: missing parentheses in Jeffreys divergence expression and typo in NUTS citation (should be Hoffman and Gelman).
