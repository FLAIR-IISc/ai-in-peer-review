- Proposal: use BDMC’s lower/upper marginal likelihood bounds gap as a performance measure for MCMC transition operators; implemented for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for posterior-evaluation.

- Strength: simple, easy-to-implement idea with potentially high impact in probabilistic programming systems.

- Novelty/depth concern: technical contribution is modest; much of the paper reiterates existing BDMC material, making main contributions feel rushed.

- Theoretical note: authors observe the gap upper-bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence — a nice observation but not wholly novel.

- Missing related work: fails to cite or discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions and possible connections to BDMC/tempering moves.

- β_t scheduling issue: choice of β_t values (crucial in TT) is not justified; need analysis of how β_t and step size affect the bound gap and acceptance.

- AutoBDMC unclear: what “auto” does is not defined; appendix/code imply T parameterization, but no algorithmic schedule (e.g., iteratively decreasing Δβ) is provided.

- Notation and clarity problems: ambiguous definition of vector v, inconsistent labels (“rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”), and unclear definitions throughout.

- Missing/incorrect definitions and equations: estimator p̂(y) is not defined, Equation (6) appears incorrect, and AutoBDMC is not precisely defined.

- Experimental issues: β_t choices not explained, RAIS and its variants are not defined, and figures need larger axes/tick labels for readability.

- Presentation errors: typos (e.g., missing parentheses in DKL terms) and an incorrect citation.

- Overall: paper has promise, but acceptance hinges on addressing clarity, related-work omissions, methodological details (β_t, AutoBDMC), and experimental presentation.
