- Introduces bidirectional Monte Carlo for evaluating MCMC-based posterior inference, a significant contribution to Bayesian computation.  
- Derives and analyzes computable lower bounds for the KL divergence between true and approximate posteriors, providing a rigorous theoretical foundation.  
- Addresses the key challenge of quantifying approximation error without access to the true posterior.  
- Theoretical framework is well-motivated and clearly presented, showing how bidirectional Monte Carlo yields tight KL lower bounds.  
- Enables systematic, practical evaluation of MCMC fidelity, improving on heuristic or costly alternatives.  
- Implemented in WebPPL and Stan, facilitating integration into existing probabilistic programming workflows.  
- Elegantly synthesizes recent Monte Carlo advances with the need for reliable posterior diagnostics, bridging theory and practice.  
- Experimental results across diverse models validate the bounds as reliable and computationally feasible.  
- Minor limitations: focus on continuous latent variables and limited comparison to other error metrics beyond KL.  
- Clear, reproducible writing; technically appropriate and original work with strong recommendation for acceptance.
