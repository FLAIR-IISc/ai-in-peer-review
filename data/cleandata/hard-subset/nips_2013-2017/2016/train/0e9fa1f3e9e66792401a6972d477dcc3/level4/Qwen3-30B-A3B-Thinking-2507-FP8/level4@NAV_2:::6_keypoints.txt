- Extends BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence for evaluating MCMC inference (BREAD).  
- Builds on prior BDMC work and applies to AIS/SMC viewed as importance sampling on extended state spaces.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and validates on several probabilistic models.  
- Theoretical analysis is rigorous and shows how BDMC upper-bounds the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Estimator derivation is clear and accurate on toy problems with exact posteriors.  
- Method is limited to simulated data where exact posterior samples exist; real-data protocol is heuristic and assumes representative simulated data.  
- Experimental validation shows consistency between real and simulated data but does not explore model misspecification scenarios.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and experimentally reproducible with effective figures.  
- Novel application of BDMC to MCMC inference, but the conceptual innovation is incremental and follows naturally from BDMC.  
- Insufficient differentiation from related work (Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka, 2016) regarding technical contributions.  
- Practical value for probabilistic programming users is clear, but broader significance is limited by applicability constraints.  
- Recommendation: useful and sound work but better suited to a probabilistic programming or MCMC-focused venue than a top-tier general ML conference.
