- Paper proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds on log marginal likelihood to assess MCMC reliability and compare MCMC schemes.  
- Selection rule: prefer MCMC schemes that produce the tightest BMC stochastic bounds for equal computational effort.  
- Core challenge: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to yield a valid stochastic upper bound.  
- Authors introduce heuristics to avoid the need for an exact posterior sample.  
- Heuristic in Bayesian context: perform diagnostics on a posterior from synthetic data generated using a representative hyperparameter η_real.  
- Rationale: synthetic-data posterior makes it easier to obtain good approximate samples for BMC.  
- Method is demonstrated on several examples.  
- Strength: interesting, theoretically motivated approach to MCMC diagnostics, potentially useful despite being specific to AIS/SMC and marginal-likelihood-based.  
- Major concern 1: method assumes knowledge of a representative η_real from prior MCMC, which presumes the original algorithm had converged and that the posterior is well summarized by one value.  
- Major concern 2: assumes the synthetic-data posterior has similar MCMC convergence behavior to the real posterior, an unjustified and risky assumption, especially under model misspecification.  
- Major concern 3: assumes starting from η_real enables rapid near-exact sampling, an optimistic and unguaranteed premise that reintroduces the original convergence question.  
- Empirical evidence (Section 5.1) uses simple examples that are not convincing for more complex or realistic problems.  
- Authors should explain and provide evidence why their method handles multimodality or insufficient summary statistics better than existing diagnostics.  
- Recommendation: include simulations comparing the proposed diagnostic to standard methods to demonstrate scenarios where it yields more reliable assessments.
