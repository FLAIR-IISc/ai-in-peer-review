This paper presents a method that leverages bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the J-S divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target distribution, with the goal of facilitating debugging and assessment of MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages. I find the basic idea of using BDMC to detect sampler failures to converge quite appealing—this seems potentially valuable given the known limitations of standard MCMC diagnostics. Two closely related and pertinent references are [1,2], which also assess MCMC by running on data generated from the prior.

Section 3.1: I found the derivation difficult to parse. In particular, q(.) appears before it is defined. From context, it seems intended to denote the marginal distribution of the AIS procedure after T steps; this should be stated explicitly. While the supplement helps somewhat, the main text should stand on its own. More broadly, a concise, end-to-end description of how to apply the method would help; as written, the reader must piece together the workflow.

Minor notes: Splitting unobserved variables into z and theta is unnecessarily cluttered in this general presentation—since they always enter jointly, consider using a single symbol. Line 165: missing a closing parenthesis.
