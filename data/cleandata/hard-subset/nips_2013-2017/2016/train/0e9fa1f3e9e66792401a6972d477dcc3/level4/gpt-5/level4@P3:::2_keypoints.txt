- Extends BDMC to bound expected Jeffreys divergence for AIS/SMC-like samplers using forward and reverse AIS; method named BREAD.  
- Practical protocol: fit hyperparameters on real data, simulate data, run reverse AIS initialized from approximate posterior; integrated into WebPPL and Stan and validated on several models.  
- Theoretical claim: forward AIS lower and reverse AIS upper bounds sandwich the marginal likelihood; their gap upper-bounds expected Jeffreys divergence — derivations are sound but the guarantee is in expectation.  
- Practical protocol appears robust in experiments varying MCMC steps and simulated-data behavior mirrors real data across tested models.  
- Experiments cover multiple models and systems; BREAD helps choose model representations and uncovered a real WebPPL bug, but SMC is not empirically evaluated.  
- Main caveat: rigorous guarantees hold only for simulated data with an exact posterior draw; the real-data protocol uses approximations without formal bounds.  
- Estimator variance can be high with outliers; paper reports quartiles but gives limited guidance on budget vs reliability or confidence intervals for the divergence bound.  
- Sensitivity to annealing schedule, number of intermediate distributions, and transition kernels is not systematically studied; practical guidelines are missing.  
- Clarity: paper is well written; suggestions include adding pseudocode, stating expectation-only guarantee earlier, offering practical annealing/diagnostic guidance, reporting runtimes, and fixing minor typos.  
- Originality: modest but meaningful repurposing of BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence and a practical real-data protocol; distinguishes itself from concurrent "subjective divergence" work.  
- Significance: provides a valuable quantitative evaluation tool for MCMC in probabilistic programming, useful for representation choice and debugging despite limitations (simulatable data requirement, expectation-only bound, AIS focus).  
- Pros: clear theoretical link to BDMC, practical simulated-to-real protocol, integration into popular PPLs, diverse experiments, and real debugging success.  
- Cons: guarantees limited to simulated data, high-variance estimators, lack of SMC experiments, and results reported by steps rather than compute-normalized metrics.  
- Requests/suggestions: provide annealing/schedule guidelines, stress-tests on multimodal posteriors, quantify bound uncertainty (e.g., multiple reverse initializations), extend guarantees for fitted-hyperparameter protocol, include at least one SMC experiment, and report wall-clock/ESS.  
- Recommendation: Accept — solid, principled contribution with practical utility, despite empirical scope and formal-limitations.
