- Paper proposes BREAD: using BDMC to bound inference error, showing the BDMC gap upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence in expectation.
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan and proposes a protocol for evaluating inference on real-world data via simulation from fitted hyperparameters.
- Four claimed contributions: (1) BDMC bounds Jeffreys divergence, (2) empirical accuracy on toy examples, (3) real-data evaluation protocol, (4) implementations in two PPLs.
- Strength: elegant, simple, and practical method that provides rigorous bounds for simulated data and is easy to integrate into probabilistic programming systems.
- Strength: convincing empirical validation across multiple models/datasets and practical utility demonstrated (e.g., found a WebPPL bug).
- Weakness: novelty claim underdeveloped—connection to Neal (1996) Tempered Transitions and related bounds is not discussed and seems closely related.
- Weakness: technical presentation in Sections 3.1–3.2 is unclear; definitions (e.g., vector v, "rev/fwd" vs "for/back") and terms like "AutoBDMC" are ambiguous.
- Weakness: important implementation details missing for reproducibility, notably how β_t values are chosen (only vague appendix code cited).
- Weakness: experimental analysis insufficient—no statistical significance testing for claims about indistinguishability across S, and no systematic study of gap behavior vs. Δβ.
- Weakness: technical errors and notational inconsistencies (e.g., Equation (6) with identical distributions in KL, unclear E[log R] vs E[log R_rev], undefined acronyms like RAIS and (u)c(R)AIS).
- Writing: generally good but contains awkward phrasing and unclear technical points that should be clarified.
- Recommendation: revise to address related work (Neal), clarify technical derivations and notation, provide reproducibility details and stronger experimental analysis before acceptance.
