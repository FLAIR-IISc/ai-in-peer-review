This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, with the aim of debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). I like the idea of using BDMC to diagnose failures of a sampler to converge; this seems potentially quite useful given that existing MCMC diagnostics are indeed problematic. Two very relevant references are [1,2], which also test the ability of MCMC samplers by running them on data sampled from the prior.

Section 3.1: I found this derivation very hard to follow. In particular, q(.) is used before it is defined. From context, it appears to represent the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps, but this should be made explicit. Things are somewhat clearer in the supplement, but the main text should be self-contained so that a reader does not need to consult the supplement to understand the core method. More generally, it would help to include a clear, end-to-end description of how to apply the method—from setup through estimation—so the reader does not have to piece the procedure together.

Minor notes:
- It feels cluttered to split the unobserved variables into z and θ when, in this general setting, there is no meaningful distinction between them—they always appear together. Consider eliminating one of z or θ to simplify exposition.
- Line 165: missing closing parenthesis.
