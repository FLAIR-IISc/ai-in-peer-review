This paper presents AutoBDMC, a novel method for assessing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) convergence. The approach leverages the recently developed Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) method to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood, employing annealed importance sampling (AIS) as a key component. The authors demonstrate a connection between the gap between these bounds and the Jensen-Shannon divergence (JSD) between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of MCMC samples, ultimately providing a scalar metric for evaluating convergence. While directly applicable to synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors present empirical evidence suggesting that AutoBDMC’s analysis on synthetic data reliably predicts performance on real-world datasets. They further illustrate the method’s utility in model selection and MCMC debugging. 

Overall, I found this paper to be clearly written and well-motivated. The proposed method appears genuinely useful, and the experimental evaluation provides a reasonably compelling argument for its effectiveness. However, Section 3 proved more challenging to follow than the remainder of the paper. The transition to Section 3.1 is particularly abrupt, immediately introducing variables and equations without sufficient context or motivation. If the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 represents a novel contribution—as I believe it does—this should be explicitly highlighted, as the paper’s core novelty appears to reside in this result.

A critical omission is a clear definition of “AutoBDMC.” Although I infer from the context that AutoBDMC refers to the application of AIS and reverse AIS (rAIS) to analyze MCMC convergence, this is never explicitly stated. By the time Section 3.2 references challenges for “AutoBDMC,” the reader should already have a precise understanding of what this term encompasses. A concise definition early in the paper would greatly improve clarity.

Here are some further, more minor points:

*   **Line 149:** The notation *p(θ, z | y)* should likely be *p(θ, z, y)*.
*   **Notational Inconsistency:** The paper inconsistently uses *y* and *D* to represent the observed data. Maintaining a consistent notation throughout would reduce potential confusion.
*   **Supplemental Code (WebPPL):** The provided WebPPL code in the supplementary materials should be accompanied by information specifying (a) the WebPPL version(s) with which it is confirmed to be compatible, and (b) clear instructions on how to integrate the provided code into a standard WebPPL distribution. Ideally, releasing this functionality as a readily installable WebPPL package via npm would significantly enhance reproducibility and usability.
*   **Supplemental Code (Stan):** A similar, packaged release of the Stan code would also be highly appreciated by the community.

The authors convincingly demonstrate that AutoBDMC provides a useful diagnostic for assessing MCMC convergence. The connection to JSD is a strong theoretical result. Addressing the clarity issues in Section 3 and providing a formal definition of AutoBDMC would strengthen the paper considerably. Furthermore, packaging and documenting the supplementary code would greatly improve the accessibility and impact of this work. I recommend acceptance contingent upon these revisions.
