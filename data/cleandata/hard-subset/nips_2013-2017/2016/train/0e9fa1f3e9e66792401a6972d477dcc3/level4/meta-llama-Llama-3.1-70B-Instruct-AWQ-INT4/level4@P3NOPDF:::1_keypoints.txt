- Proposes a novel method to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating PPL samplers.  
- Uses bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose sampler convergence failures, addressing limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics.  
- Approach is closely related to Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012); these references are highly relevant and provide important context.  
- Derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow; notation q(.) is introduced before being defined, causing reader confusion.  
- q(.) appears to be the AIS marginal after T steps but should be explicitly defined in the main text rather than relying on the supplement.  
- Paper needs a clear, step-by-step procedure or flowchart showing how to apply the proposed method end-to-end.  
- Suggest simplifying notation by removing the unnecessary distinction between unobserved variables z and θ, since they are always used together.  
- Minor typographical error: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.  
- Overall the approach is promising for debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs but requires clarity and presentation revisions to be more accessible.
