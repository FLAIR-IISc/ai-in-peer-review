This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a novel method for evaluating Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) convergence. The approach leverages the Bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to estimate stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood, employing annealed importance sampling for this purpose. The authors establish that the difference between these bounds provides a quantifiable limit on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution derived from approximate MCMC samples, yielding a single scalar metric for assessing convergence. While currently validated on synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the paper provides evidence that AutoBDMC’s performance on synthetic datasets correlates with its behavior when applied to real-world data. Furthermore, the authors demonstrate the utility of their method for model selection and MCMC debugging. 

Overall, the paper is well-written and effectively motivated, presenting a method that appears genuinely valuable. The experimental results are convincing. However, I found Section 3 more challenging to follow than the remainder of the manuscript. The transition to Section 3.1 is abrupt, introducing notation and equations without sufficient contextualization. If the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 represents a novel contribution – as I believe it does – this should be explicitly highlighted, given that the paper’s novelty appears to center on this result. 

A clear definition of AutoBDMC itself is lacking. My understanding is that AutoBDMC constitutes the application of annealed importance sampling (AIS) and reverse annealed importance sampling (rAIS) to analyze MCMC convergence, and I recommend a statement to that effect be included. By Section 3.2, when the authors state a challenge “for AutoBDMC,” the method should already be clearly defined. 

Minor points for consideration: Line 149, the notation p(theta, z | y) may be a typo and should be checked against p(theta, z, y). There are also notational inconsistencies, specifically the interchange between ‘y’ and ‘D’ to represent data, which caused some confusion. The WebPPL code provided in the supplementary materials should be accompanied by details regarding (a) compatible WebPPL versions, and (b) instructions for integrating the code into existing WebPPL distributions. Ideally, releasing the code as a distributable WebPPL package via npm would be highly beneficial. A similar package release for the Stan implementation would also be greatly appreciated.
