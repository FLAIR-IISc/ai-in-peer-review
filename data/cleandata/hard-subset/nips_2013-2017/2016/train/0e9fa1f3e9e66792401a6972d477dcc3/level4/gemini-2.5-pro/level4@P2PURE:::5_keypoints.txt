- Proposes using BMC stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood to compare MCMC efficiency — tightest bounds indicate best sampler.  
- Circumvents BMC's requirement for an exact sample by running diagnostics on a surrogate posterior generated from synthetic data.  
- Method is limited to AIS/SMC samplers and focuses solely on marginal likelihood, reducing general applicability.  
- Requires a “representative” hyperparameter η_real from a prior MCMC run, creating a circular dependency and assuming that prior run has converged.  
- Assumes the synthetic-data posterior has convergence properties similar to the true posterior, an unsubstantiated claim especially under model misspecification.  
- Assumes initializing MCMC at η_real yields a near-exact sample from the surrogate posterior, which is not guaranteed.  
- These intertwined heuristics effectively change the problem and undermine the method’s practical reliability.  
- Demonstrations use simple examples that are insufficient to alleviate concerns about performance in complex, realistic scenarios.  
- Paper criticizes existing diagnostics for multimodality but fails to empirically demonstrate superior robustness in such challenging cases.  
- Lacks direct comparisons with standard diagnostic methods; such comparisons and targeted simulations are needed to justify the proposed approach.
