- Proposes a novel method extending bidirectional Monte Carlo to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Uses AIS chains run forward and reverse on simulated data to upper bound the expected symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Tackles an important and challenging problem: reliably evaluating posterior inference accuracy.  
- Method development and exposition are clear, well-motivated, and easy to follow.  
- Provides theoretical analysis of strengths and weaknesses of the bidirectional approach.  
- Demonstrates the upper bound is accurate on toy distributions and presents a protocol for real-data analysis.  
- Integrates the technique into WebPPL and Stan and validates on multiple models and datasets.  
- Offers a rigorous and general framework for assessing posterior inference quality.  
- Strength: clever use of bidirectional Monte Carlo with thorough empirical and theoretical support.  
- Weakness: directly applicable only to algorithms like AIS or sequential Monte Carlo, not all MCMC methods.  
- Weakness: requires an exact sample from the target distribution, which may not always be available.  
- Suggests more discussion needed on limitations and directions for future work.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with impressive results; recommendation to accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
