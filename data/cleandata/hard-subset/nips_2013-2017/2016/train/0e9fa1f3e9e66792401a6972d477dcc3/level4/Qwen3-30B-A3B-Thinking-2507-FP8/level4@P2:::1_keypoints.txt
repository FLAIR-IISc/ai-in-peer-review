- Proposes extending BDMC to upper bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior.  
- Integrates the method into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating utility for debugging and guiding model representation choices.  
- Extends Grosse et al. (2015) BDMC framework from marginal likelihood estimation to direct assessment of posterior sample quality.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a method to upper bound the Jeffreys divergence in expectation using BDMC.  
- Practical protocol: simulates data from fitted hyperparameters to apply the method to real-world datasets.  
- Demonstrates how the technique can inform modeling decisions (e.g., whether to collapse latent variables in matrix factorization).  
- Empirical validation across multiple probabilistic models; method can detect implementation bugs (example: uncovered WebPPL bug).  
- Well-situated in literature, relating to BDMC and probabilistic programming diagnostics and offering more rigorous bounds than traditional MCMC diagnostics.  
- Strengths: addresses an important problem, offers a theoretically grounded quantitative measure, integrates into probabilistic programming, and has practical validation.  
- Major weakness: Section 3.1 is unclear—ambiguous notation (q(·) undefined) and insufficient explanation, forcing readers to consult supplementary material.  
- Lacks a clear, step-by-step user guide (e.g., how many AIS chains, choosing intermediate distributions, interpreting bounds).  
- Exposition issues: redundant variable notation (distinguishing z and θ unnecessarily) and a minor typographical error (missing parenthesis on line 165).  
- Theoretical novelty is moderate—method builds on existing BDMC work rather than introducing fundamentally new mathematics.  
- Experimental quality is strong; authors validate consistency between simulated and real data and support the protocol for fitted hyperparameters.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on significantly revising Section 3.1 for clarity, adding a practical algorithmic description, eliminating redundant notation, and fixing the grammatical/typo.
