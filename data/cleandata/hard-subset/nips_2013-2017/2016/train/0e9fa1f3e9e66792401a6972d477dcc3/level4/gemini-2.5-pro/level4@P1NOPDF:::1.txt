This paper proposes estimating the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target distribution using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC). The primary application is for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations, particularly within probabilistic programming languages. The core idea of using BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures is promising and potentially very useful, as existing MCMC diagnostics are often inadequate.

The authors should also consider two highly relevant works, [1] and [2], which propose similar methods for testing MCMC samplers by running them on data sampled from the prior.

The primary weakness of the paper is the clarity of its presentation. The derivation in Section 3.1, in particular, was difficult to follow. For instance, the notation q(.) is used before it is formally defined. While context suggests it represents the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after T steps, this should be stated explicitly. Although the supplementary material provides some clarification, the main text should be self-contained and understandable on its own.

More broadly, the paper would be significantly improved by including a clear, step-by-step description or algorithm outlining how to apply the proposed method in practice. As it stands, the reader must piece together the complete procedure from different parts of the text.

Minor notes:
*   The notational distinction between unobserved variables `z` and `theta` seems unnecessary. In the general setting presented, they are treated identically and always appear together. Simplifying the notation by using a single variable would improve clarity.
*   There appears to be a missing closing parenthesis on line 165.

[1] Geweke, J. (2004). Getting it right: Joint distribution tests of posterior simulators. *Journal of the American Statistical Association, 99*(467), 799–804.

[2] Cook, S. R., Gelman, A., & Rubin, D. B. (2006). Validation of software for Bayesian models using posterior quantiles. *Journal of Computational and Graphical Statistics, 15*(3), 675–692.
