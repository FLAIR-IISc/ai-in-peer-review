- Extends BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and MCMC-produced approximate samples using forward and reverse AIS.
- Integrates the BREAD technique into WebPPL and Stan for practical use in probabilistic programming.
- Validates BREAD across multiple models and datasets, showing empirical effectiveness.
- Demonstrates BREAD guiding model-design choices, e.g., comparing representations for matrix factorization.
- Discovers a bug in WebPPL, highlighting BREAD’s utility as a testing and debugging tool.
- Provides a clear theoretical derivation and thorough empirical evaluation.
- Builds on prior work in convergence diagnostics and sample-quality measures, repurposing BDMC innovatively.
- Requires access to exact posterior samples, so rigorous use relies on simulated data.
- Proposed protocol for real data depends on accurate hyperparameter estimation, which may limit validity.
- Running forward and reverse AIS chains is computationally expensive, limiting scalability to complex models.
- Performance may be sensitive to initialization of the reverse AIS chain.
- The derivation linking BDMC to Jeffreys divergence can be difficult for readers unfamiliar with the theory.
- Strong case for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method with practical implementations and validated experiments.
- Counterarguments against acceptance: dependence on simulated data and computational cost may limit immediate applicability.
- Overall assessment: valuable and well-executed contribution to Bayesian inference evaluation; recommended for acceptance.
