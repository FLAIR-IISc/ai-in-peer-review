- Proposes using BDMC bidirectional bound gap as a general performance metric for MCMC; includes Stan/WebPPL implementations and an evaluation protocol.  
- Strength: idea is elegant, simple, practical, and especially relevant for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Strength: focus on tooling and evaluation protocols for real systems is useful to the community.  
- Main concern: novelty is incremental—primary new observation is the bound gap upper-bounding Jensen–Shannon divergence.  
- Main concern: exposition issues—Sections 3.1 and 3.2 feel rushed and unclear.  
- Missing related work: does not cite Neal (1996) Tempered Transitions (TT), which is highly relevant.  
- Connection to TT: TT uses reverse/forward sweeps and an acceptance ratio equal to the BDMC lower/upper weight ratio; thermodynamic-integration links are omitted.  
- Temperature schedule importance: bound gap depends strongly on {β_t} and Δβ, mirroring TT’s acceptance trade-offs.  
- Practical implications: if bounds are far apart, rerun with smaller Δβ; comparisons unreliable when log-gap ≳ 1 nat.  
- "Auto" in AutoBDMC is not clearly defined; an adaptive schedule that decreases Δβ until a criterion is met is suggested.  
- Overall: methodology is potentially useful; acceptability contingent on addressing clarity and related-work issues.  
- Clarity issue: define v precisely (sequence of intermediate latent/parameter states across both sweeps).  
- Notation inconsistency: standardize rev/fwd vs for/back or clarify distinctions.  
- Unspecified estimator: clearly define the estimator (e.g., R̂) whose expectation is taken in equations (5–7, 9–11).  
- Equation (6) problem: currently states a KL between identical distributions; RHS should involve posterior and the backward path distribution.  
- AutoBDMC ambiguity: explicitly state whether it means using the bound gap for convergence assessment plus the fixed hyperparameter scheme.  
- Experimental detail: clarify how {β_t} is chosen—code suggests linear schedule with T steps; document this.  
- Experimental labeling: clarify whether “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” refers to BDMC temperature steps.  
- Acronyms: define RAIS and u/c(R)AIS (uncollapsed/collapsed variants) in captions.  
- Figures: improve axis and tick label sizes for readability.  
- Minor typos: missing parentheses in the JS expression and NUTS citation typo (“Homan” → “Hoffman”).  
- Suggested additions: add TT citation and explicitly connect BDMC bound gap to TT acceptance ratio and thermodynamic integration.  
- Suggested additions: provide practical heuristics or an adaptive scheme for choosing {β_t} (e.g., target bound gaps).  
- Suggested experiments: controlled study of bound gap vs T and different schedules (linear, geometric) and how this impacts kernel comparisons.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify all estimators, path distributions, expectations; standardize notation; precisely define AutoBDMC components and defaults.
