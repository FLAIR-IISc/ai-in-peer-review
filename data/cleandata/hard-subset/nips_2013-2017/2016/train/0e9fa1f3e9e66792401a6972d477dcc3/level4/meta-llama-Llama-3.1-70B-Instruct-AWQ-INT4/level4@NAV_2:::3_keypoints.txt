- Proposes BREAD: extending BDMC to evaluate MCMC transition operators by bounding symmetrized KL between approximate samples and true posterior.  
- Uses gap between stochastic lower and upper bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC operators.  
- Main strength: simple, easy-to-implement idea with potential high impact in probabilistic programming.  
- Authors wisely reiterate BDMC background, aiding understanding of the proposed method.  
- Main technical contributions (sections 3.1–3.2) feel rushed and modest in novelty.  
- Observation that bound gap gives an upper bound on Jensen–Shannon divergence is useful but arguably not novel.  
- Missing citation: Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions is highly relevant and not referenced.  
- Writing/clarity issues in sections 3.1–3.2 (loose definition of vector v, unclear derivations).  
- Equations (5–7) and (9–11) lack definition of the estimator \hat{p}(y) being averaged, making them hard to follow.  
- Experiments are insufficiently thorough: need systematic study of how bounds vary with step size.  
- Choice of annealing schedule β_t is not explained; should be stated explicitly.  
- Presentation issues: RAIS acronym and (u)c(R)AIS variants are not defined in captions/text.  
- Overall: technically sound and potentially useful, but needs clearer exposition, more related-work discussion, and stronger experiments.  
- Acceptance tradeoff: elegant idea and potential impact vs. modest novelty, missing citations, clarity, and experimental gaps.
