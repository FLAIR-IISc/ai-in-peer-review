This paper presents a summary of bidirectional Monte Carlo methods and reports on their implementation within two probabilistic programming languages: WebPPL and Stan.

The primary contribution of this work appears to be the practical application of these previously published techniques. The authors have successfully demonstrated the use of an established algorithm within modern software frameworks, which may be of interest to practitioners working with these specific tools.

However, the paper's main weakness is its limited methodological novelty. The work does not introduce new algorithms, theoretical insights, or a fundamentally new analysis. Instead, it describes the implementation of known techniques. While such application-focused studies have their place, this submission lacks the significant scientific or theoretical contribution typically expected for a publication at this conference. To be a better fit for this venue, the authors would need to demonstrate, for example, how their implementation led to new insights into the method itself, solved non-trivial and generalizable technical challenges, or enabled new state-of-the-art performance on important benchmark problems.

Given that the contribution is centered on implementation rather than novel research, this work may be better suited for a more specialized venue. A workshop on MCMC methods or probabilistic programming, for instance, would provide a more suitable audience that could directly benefit from these practical findings.
