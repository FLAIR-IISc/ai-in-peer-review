This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, with a particular focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). I find the idea of leveraging BDMC for diagnosing sampler convergence issues compelling, as existing MCMC diagnostics are often limited in their ability to definitively confirm convergence. The approach builds on and complements related work such as [GGA15] and [CTM16], which also utilize BDMC to assess sampler quality. Section 3.1 presents a derivation that I initially found challenging to follow. For instance, the distribution q(.) is introduced without a clear definition; clarifying that it represents the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps would improve readability. While the supplement provides additional clarity, the main text should ideally be self-contained. A concise, step-by-step guide on applying the method would also be beneficial, as currently the reader needs to synthesize the procedure from different parts of the paper. A minor point: the separation of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* seems unnecessary in this general setting, as they always appear together; consolidating them would simplify the notation. Line 165 contains a missing closing parenthesis. Overall, the work offers a promising approach to evaluating MCMC performance and could be a valuable tool for developers of PPLs and researchers employing MCMC methods.
