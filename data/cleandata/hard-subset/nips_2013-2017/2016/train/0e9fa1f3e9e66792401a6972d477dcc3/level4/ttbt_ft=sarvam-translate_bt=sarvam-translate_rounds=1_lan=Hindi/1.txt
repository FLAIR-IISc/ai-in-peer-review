This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the J-S deviation between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a particular focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in PPL. I like the idea of using BDMC to diagnose failure of the sampler's convergence – this idea seems potentially quite useful because existing MCMC diagnoses are actually problematic. Two very relevant references [1,2] also test the MCMC sampler's ability by running them on pre-sampled data. Section 3.1: I found this derivation very difficult to understand. One thing is that q(.) is used even before it is defined. From the reference, it seems that it represents the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps, but this should be clarified. Things become a little clearer if one reads the supplement, but the reader should not need to read the supplement to understand the main text. Generally, it would be good if there was a clear description of how to implement the method from start to finish somewhere – the reader would have to add it a little. Minor comments: Breaking unused variables into z and theta is a little messy, whereas there is no difference between z and theta in such a general setting – they always appear together, so why not remove z or theta?
