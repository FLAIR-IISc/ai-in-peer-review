- Proposes an innovative method to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.  
- Noteworthy use of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose sampler convergence failures; addresses limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics.  
- Method is similar to prior approaches (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2012) that evaluate samplers using data drawn from the prior.  
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow; notation q(.) is introduced before being defined and should be explicitly stated (e.g., marginal of AIS after T steps).  
- The main text relies on the supplement; provide a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the method without requiring the supplement.  
- Simplify notation by removing the unnecessary distinction between unobserved variables z and θ, since they always appear together.  
- Minor typographical error: missing closing parenthesis on Line 165.  
- Overall assessment: interesting approach with potential to make a valuable contribution pending the suggested clarifications and fixes.
