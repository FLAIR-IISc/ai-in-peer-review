- Proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence for diagnosing MCMC samplers in probabilistic programming languages.  
- Promising approach because existing MCMC diagnostics are unreliable and often miss subtle sampler failures.  
- Method can effectively diagnose sampler failures in complex PPL models and offers a more direct divergence estimate than some prior techniques.  
- Conceptually related to prior joint-distribution recovery tests (e.g., Geweke, Cook et al.) but provides a direct divergence-based evaluation.  
- Core idea is sound and addresses a genuine need in Bayesian computation.  
- Presentation lacks clarity and needs substantial improvement to convey method details and enable adoption.  
- Section 3.1 (BDMC estimator derivation) is difficult to follow; key function q(.) is used before being defined and requires explicit explanation.  
- Reliance on supplementary material for core derivation is problematic; the main text should include a clear, step-by-step derivation with all variables introduced beforehand.  
- Paper should include a concrete start-to-finish application workflow (e.g., numbered steps or pseudocode) to improve usability.  
- Notation choice splitting unobserved variables into z and θ is unnecessary and complicates presentation; consolidate into a single variable (e.g., x).  
- Minor typographical error: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.  
- With clarity improvements and a more comprehensive guide to application, the approach could be a valuable contribution.
