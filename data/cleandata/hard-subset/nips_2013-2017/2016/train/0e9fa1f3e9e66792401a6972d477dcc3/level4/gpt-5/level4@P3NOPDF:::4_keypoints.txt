- Paper proposes AutoBDMC: automated BDMC using AIS/rAIS to get stochastic bounds on log marginal likelihood and a scalar MCMC convergence diagnostic from their gap.  
- Experiments test whether diagnostics on synthetic data predict behavior on real datasets and demonstrate model selection/debugging uses.  
- Request: Define “AutoBDMC” clearly and early, specifying what is automated vs. user-specified (schedule, base dist, temps, kernels, particles).  
- Request: Clarify Section 3 with a roadmap and intuitive explanations before formal definitions, especially the transition into 3.1.  
- Request: Make the divergence bound status explicit—state as a theorem with assumptions and proof sketch if novel, or cite sources if not.  
- Request: Emphasize scope and limitations—AutoBDMC evaluates synthetic-data convergence and discuss when this proxy transfers or fails (misspecification, heavy tails, multimodality, atypical real-data regions).  
- Request: Quantify computational cost and sensitivity of AIS/rAIS vs baseline MCMC and analyze dependence on annealing schedule, number of temps, kernels, and particles; include an ablation on bound tightness in high dimensions/complex posteriors.  
- Request: Compare clearly to existing diagnostics (R-hat, ESS, Geweke, SBC), discuss complementary strengths/weaknesses, and provide empirical comparisons where feasible.
