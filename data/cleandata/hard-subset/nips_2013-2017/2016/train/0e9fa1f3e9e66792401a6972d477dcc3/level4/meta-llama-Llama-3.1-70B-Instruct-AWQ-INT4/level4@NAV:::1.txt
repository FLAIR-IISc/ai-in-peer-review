This paper introduces the use of bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate the Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution of interest, with a focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages. I appreciate the proposal to utilize bidirectional Monte Carlo for diagnosing convergence failures in samplers, as existing MCMC diagnostics are indeed problematic. Notably, references [1,2] also assess MCMC samplers by running them on prior-sampled data, making them highly relevant to this work. 

However, I found the derivation in Section 3.1 challenging to follow. Specifically, the function q(.) is used before its definition, which appears to represent the marginal distribution of the AIS sampler after T steps, based on context. This should be explicitly stated. While the supplement provides some clarification, the main text should be self-contained, eliminating the need for supplemental reading. A step-by-step explanation of the method's application would be beneficial, as the reader currently needs to piece this together.

Some minor suggestions: the distinction between unobserved variables z and theta seems unnecessary, as they always appear together in this general setting. Consider eliminating one of these variables to reduce clutter. Additionally, a close parenthesis is missing on line 165.
