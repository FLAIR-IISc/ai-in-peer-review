- Proposes AutoBDMC: uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo (AIS + reverse AIS) to get stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood and a scalar bound on JS divergence for MCMC convergence.
- Method is applicable only to synthetic data from the joint, but authors present empirical evidence that performance on synthetic data predicts real-data MCMC behavior.
- Demonstrates practical applications in model selection and debugging MCMC implementations.
- Paper is generally well-written, well-motivated, and the empirical validation is convincing.
- Section 3 is hard to follow; the transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and introduces variables/equations without sufficient context or motivation.
- If the JS-divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper should explicitly claim this central contribution.
- The term “AutoBDMC” is never formally defined; a clear definition should be provided before its usage (e.g., in Section 3.2).
- Minor: Line 149 likely uses p(theta, z | y) incorrectly and should clarify whether p(theta, z, y) was intended.
- Minor: Inconsistent notation for data (uses both y and D) — standardize notation.
- Supplemental code: WebPPL code should state compatible WebPPL versions and integration instructions; ideally release as an npm-installable WebPPL package, and provide a similar distributable package for the Stan implementation.
