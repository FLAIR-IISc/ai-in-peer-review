- Proposes using the BDMC forward/reverse AIS bound gap as a generic convergence metric, with Stan/WebPPL implementations and a protocol for posterior inference assessment.  
- Strength: elegant, simple, and broadly applicable idea with high potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Major weakness 1: Lack of novelty—closely related to Neal (1996) Tempered Transitions/Annealed Importance Sampling; paper omits this connection and citation, undermining originality.  
- Major weakness 2: Insufficient technical treatment—Sections 3.1–3.2 are rushed, notation and definitions (e.g., vec(v), AutoBDMC) are ambiguous, and the “auto” aspect is not clearly defined.  
- Major weakness 3: Superficial experiments—no systematic study of dependence on Δβ (β_{t+1}-β_t), unexplored large-Δβ failure modes, undefined acronyms in captions, and missing β_t scheduling details.  
- Clarity issues in math: Equations 5–7 and 9–11 lack definition of ĥp(y); Equation 6 is incorrect and KL targets are mis-specified.  
- Presentation problems: tiny axes/tick labels in Figures 2–3, missing parentheses in D_JS terms, typos and a citation name error (Homan → Hoffman).  
- Recommendation: Method has merit but paper should cite/engage Neal (1996), clarify AutoBDMC, and add a Δβ sensitivity analysis; currently a weak accept contingent on these revisions.
