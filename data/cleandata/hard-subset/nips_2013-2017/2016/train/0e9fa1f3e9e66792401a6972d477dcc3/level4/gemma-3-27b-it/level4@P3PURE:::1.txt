This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, with a specific focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations within probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). I find the idea of leveraging BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures compelling; this approach appears potentially valuable, as existing MCMC diagnostics are known to be problematic. The work builds upon and is relevant to previous efforts such as [1, 2], which also assess MCMC sampler performance by evaluating their ability to recover samples from the prior distribution. 

Section 3.1 presents a derivation that I found difficult to follow. Notably, the notation *q(.)* is used prior to its definition. While context suggests it represents the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after *T* steps, this should be explicitly stated. Clarity improves somewhat when consulting the supplementary material, but understanding the main text should not require reference to the supplement. Generally, a clear, step-by-step description of the method’s application from beginning to end would be beneficial; currently, the reader must synthesize this information. 

Minor comments: The separation of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* feels unnecessarily cluttered, especially given the general setting where these variables typically appear together. Eliminating either *z* or *θ* would streamline the notation. Line 165 contains a missing closing parenthesis.
