- Leverages bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC posterior inference and derive upper bounds on the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Integrates the method (BREAD) into WebPPL and Stan and validates it on multiple models and datasets.  
- Addresses an important problem by providing a principled, non-heuristic way to quantify MCMC sample accuracy.  
- Extends BDMC analysis to produce a clear estimator for the Jeffreys divergence.  
- Technical analysis is sound, well-motivated, and clearly explained.  
- Implementation in popular probabilistic programming languages increases practical impact and accessibility.  
- Empirical validation supports the method’s effectiveness across tested examples.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by using BREAD to compare model representations and identify a bug in WebPPL.  
- Limitation: relies on availability of exact posterior samples for simulated data, restricting direct applicability.  
- Limitation: proposed protocol for applying BREAD to real-world data needs further validation in complex scenarios.  
- Limitation: accuracy of the bounds in high-dimensional, realistic settings requires more extensive exploration.  
- Minor issue: related work discussion could be more concise.  
- Overall: well-written, technically solid contribution to Bayesian inference evaluation; recommends acceptance.
