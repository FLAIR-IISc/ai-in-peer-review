The authors introduce a generic approach to evaluating MCMC transition operators based on the bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) method. BDMC involves two annealed importance sampling (AIS) sweeps, similar to those used in Tempered Transitions (TT) [Neal Stat & Comp 1996], but interprets the accumulated probabilities as lower and upper bounds on the marginal likelihood. This paper proposes utilizing the gap between these bounds as a performance measure for MCMC transition operators. The key technical contributions include implementing BDMC in Stan and WebPPL probabilistic programming systems and developing a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.

This paper has both strengths and weaknesses. One of its strong points is the simplicity and ease of implementation of using BDMC to assess MCMC operator convergence, making it potentially high-impact, particularly in probabilistic programming systems. However, concerns lie in the novelty and depth of the technical contribution. The authors cite existing work on BDMC but devote considerable space to reiterating its description, leaving the main technical contributions somewhat rushed and lacking in clarity.

The technical contributions, while modest, include the realization that the difference between the lower and upper bounds provides an upper bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence. Notably, the authors fail to cite Radford Neal's work on Tempered Transitions, which is highly relevant and shares similarities with BDMC. The connection between the two methods could have been explored further.

The paper's clarity suffers from issues such as ambiguous definitions, inconsistent labeling, and undefined estimators. Additionally, the experiments could be more thorough, including a systematic study of how the lower and upper bounds vary with step size. Despite these criticisms, the proposed method has potential, and the authors' response to addressing clarity and related work issues will be crucial in determining the paper's acceptance.

Specific issues with clarity include:

* Ambiguous definition of $\vec{v}$, which should be clarified as ${z{1:T-1}, \theta{1:T-1}$.
* Inconsistent labeling, such as "rev" and "fwd" versus "for" and "back," which should be standardized.
* Undefined estimator $\hat{p}(y)$ in equations (5-7) and (9-11), which should be explicitly defined.
* Equation (6) appears to be incorrect and should be revised.
* The definition of AutoBDMC is unclear and should be explicitly stated.

Experiments could be improved by:

* Clarifying the choice of βt and its relationship to the number of steps T.
* Stating the parameterization of AutoBDMC in terms of the number of steps T.
* Defining the acronym RAIS and its variants in the caption.
* Increasing the size of axes and tick labels in figures 2 and 3.

Typos include:

* Missing closing parentheses in the DKL terms.
* Incorrect citation of Homan instead of Hoffman in the NUTS reference.
