## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission: [Paper Title - *Please replace with actual title*]

This paper introduces a novel approach to diagnosing and evaluating Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) samplers, particularly within the context of Probabilistic Programming Languages (PPLs). The authors propose utilizing Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between an MCMC sampler’s output and the target distribution. This is a promising direction, as existing MCMC convergence diagnostics are known to be unreliable and often fail to detect subtle, yet critical, issues. The potential to effectively diagnose sampler failures, especially within complex PPL models, is a significant contribution. The method shares some conceptual ground with existing techniques for evaluating MCMC samplers by testing their ability to recover known data distributions (e.g., simulating data from the prior, as in [1, 2]), but offers a more direct divergence estimation approach.

The core idea is sound and addresses a genuine need in the Bayesian computation community. However, the presentation requires significant clarity improvements to fully convey the method's intricacies and facilitate its application by others. 

My primary concern lies with Section 3.1, the derivation of the BDMC estimator. I found this section difficult to follow, largely due to insufficient explanation and notation. Specifically, the function *q(.)* is used before it is formally defined. While the context suggests it represents the marginal distribution obtained after *T* steps of an Approximate Inference Sampler (AIS), this connection needs to be explicitly stated. The supplementary material clarifies this point, but readers should not be required to consult the supplement to understand the core mathematical derivation presented in the main text. A clearer, step-by-step presentation of the derivation, with each variable introduced before its use, would substantially improve comprehension. 

Furthermore, the paper would benefit from a more concrete “start-to-finish” explanation of how to apply the proposed method. Currently, the reader must synthesize information from various parts of the paper to understand the practical workflow. Providing a clear algorithmic description, perhaps as a numbered list or a pseudocode snippet, would significantly enhance usability.

I also have a minor comment regarding notation. The separation of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* feels arbitrary and unnecessarily complicates the presentation, especially given the general setting. In a typical Bayesian model, *z* and *θ* are jointly unobserved and always appear together in calculations. Consolidating these into a single variable (e.g., *x*) would simplify the notation without loss of generality and improve readability.

Finally, there is a minor typographical error on line 165, where a closing parenthesis is missing.

Despite these concerns, the underlying idea of leveraging BDMC for MCMC diagnostics is compelling. Addressing the clarity issues in the derivation and providing a more comprehensive guide to application would greatly strengthen the paper. I believe with revisions, this work has the potential to be a valuable contribution to the field.
