- Paper applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC inference in probabilistic programming languages, extends BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence, and implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan with empirical validation.
- Main idea: use BDMC/AIS/reverse-AIS techniques to assess accuracy of MCMC-based posterior inference and relate to convergence diagnostics.
- Background coverage is thorough, reviewing AIS, stochastic lower bounds, reverse AIS, and relevant diagnostics literature.
- Strength: clear and concise explanation of BDMC and its application to MCMC inference.
- Strength: empirical effectiveness demonstrated on multiple models/datasets (e.g., matrix factorization, mixture-of-Gaussians).
- Strength: method aids design of inference algorithms and debugging of implementations.
- Weakness: no new theoretical contributions—BDMC itself is not novel and the paper is mainly an implementation/application.
- Weakness: analysis of results is not detailed enough and limitations of the method are not thoroughly discussed.
- NIPS criteria assessment: scores well on clarity and significance, but lower on originality and overall quality.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to provide deeper result analysis, fuller limitations discussion, and clearer statement of novelty relative to prior BDMC work.
- For acceptance: clear exposition, useful empirical demonstrations, practical debugging/design utility.
- For rejection: limited originality, primarily implementation work, and inadequate analysis of limitations/results.
