This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between the distribution of an MCMC sampler and its target distribution. The primary application is for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations, particularly within probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The core idea of using BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures is promising and potentially very useful, as existing MCMC diagnostics are often inadequate.

The proposed method is highly relevant to prior work on validating MCMC samplers, and the authors should discuss its relationship to existing literature. Two particularly relevant references are Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012), which also propose testing MCMC samplers by running them on data simulated from the model's prior distribution.

The exposition of the method could be significantly improved. The derivation in Section 3.1 is very difficult to follow. For instance, the notation q(.) is used before it is defined. From context, it appears to represent the marginal distribution of the Annealed Importance Sampling (AIS) chain after T steps, but this should be stated explicitly. While the supplement provides some clarification, the main text should be understandable on its own. More broadly, the paper would benefit from a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the proposed method. As written, the reader must piece together the complete procedure from different sections.

Minor comments:
*   The notational distinction between unobserved variables `z` and `theta` seems to add unnecessary clutter. In the general setting presented, they are not functionally different and always appear together. The presentation could be simplified by using a single variable for all unobserved parameters.
*   Line 165 is missing a closing parenthesis.
