- Paper introduces using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and its target, aimed at debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.  
- Core idea of applying BDMC for sampler convergence diagnosis is compelling and addresses limits of existing MCMC diagnostics.  
- Authors should acknowledge two closely related prior works that validate MCMC via data simulated from the prior: Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2012).  
- Derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow; key notation (e.g., q(.)) is used before being defined and must be explicitly stated.  
- Main text currently depends on the supplementary material for crucial clarification; the paper should be self-contained.  
- Recommend adding a clear, step-by-step description of the complete method instead of scattering details across sections.  
- Notational clutter: separating unobserved variables z and theta is unnecessary since they always appear together—one could be eliminated.  
- Minor typographical error: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
