- Paper proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) gaps from AIS/rAIS to bound Jeffreys divergence and provide a scalar metric for MCMC convergence.
- Authors introduce a protocol using synthetic data from fitted hyperparameters to approximate real-world inference quality and demonstrate uses in model selection and debugging.
- Theoretical result linking BDMC gap to Jeffreys divergence appears novel and is the paper’s main contribution.
- Experimental evaluation is compelling and shows practical utility on real problems.
- Paper is generally well written and mathematically grounded.
- Section 3 (especially the transition into 3.1) is dense, abruptly introduces variables/equations, and lacks sufficient motivation or clarity.
- The term “AutoBDMC” is used repeatedly but never explicitly defined, reducing clarity.
- Notational inconsistencies (e.g., switching between y and D; possible typo p(theta, z | y) vs p(theta, z, y)) cause minor confusion.
- Supplemental WebPPL code lacks versioning, integration instructions, and would benefit from npm packaging; Stan code release is also desired.
- Strength: novel application of BDMC to bound posterior approximation error.
- Strength: rigorous theoretical backing providing quantifiable inference bounds.
- Strength: practical diagnostic tool for MCMC, model selection, and probabilistic programming debugging.
- Weakness: clarity and exposition issues in key theoretical section hinder accessibility.
- Acceptance is recommended given novelty, theory, empirical support, and practical importance, conditional on clarifying Section 3, defining AutoBDMC, and improving code accessibility.
