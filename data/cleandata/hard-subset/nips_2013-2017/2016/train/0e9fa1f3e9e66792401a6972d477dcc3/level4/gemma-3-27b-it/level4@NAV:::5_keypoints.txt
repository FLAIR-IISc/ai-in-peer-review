- Addresses MCMC reliability assessment and scheme comparison using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds on the log marginal likelihood.  
- Core idea: select the most efficient MCMC scheme by minimizing BMC stochastic bound widths for a given computational cost—promising concept.  
- Fundamental challenge: BMC requires exact target samples to guarantee valid upper bounds; authors resort to heuristics to bypass this.  
- Methodology: run diagnostics on posteriors from synthetic data generated using initial MCMC-informed hyperparameter estimates.  
- Demonstrated the approach on several examples to illustrate feasibility.  
- BMC-derived stochastic lower bounds for convergence diagnostics are interesting but may be limited to AIS/SMC samplers and diagnostics based on marginal likelihoods.  
- Heuristic fixes for the exact-sample requirement significantly weaken the proposed diagnostic’s validity and appeal.  
- Questionable assumption 1: presumes known representative hyperparameters from potentially unconverged preliminary MCMC and that a single value can summarize the posterior.  
- Questionable assumption 2: assumes the synthetic-data posterior reflects the original posterior, which is unreliable, especially under model misspecification.  
- Questionable assumption 3: assumes initializing MCMC from the estimated hyperparameters yields near-exact samples, which is not guaranteed.  
- These assumptions all rely on the very MCMC convergence properties the method is trying to assess.  
- The examples (Section 5.1) are limited and insufficient to demonstrate robustness in more complex or challenging settings.  
- The paper does not clearly explain how the method overcomes issues with multimodality or poor posterior summaries, nor does it include comparative simulations against common diagnostics.
