This paper presents a method for MCMC convergence evaluation – known as AutoBDMC. This method calculates the stochastic upper and lower bounds for log-probability using the recently invented two-way Monte Carlo method (which relies on annealed importance sampling). The authors show that the difference between the upper and lower bounds is itself a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the actual posterior and approximate sample distributions, providing a single scalar quantity for evaluating MCMC convergence behavior. Since this method can only perform convergence analysis on synthetic data generated from a joint distribution, the authors provide experimental evidence that its analysis on such synthetic data can predict the behavior of real-world data. They also demonstrate the use of their method for model selection and debugging. Overall, I found this paper to be quite readable and well-motivated. The presented method seems clearly useful, and the experimental evaluation is reasonably compelling. I found section 3 to be the hardest to follow compared to the rest. The conversion in 3.1 is particularly painful, as it enters the definition of variables and equations without any motivation. If the divergence bound in section 3.1 is a new result (which I believe it is), it should be clearly stated, as the novelty/contribution of the paper depends on this result. What exactly is AutoBDMC? This is not clearly stated anywhere in the paper. In my understanding, AutoBDMC is a method for using AIS and rAIS for MCMC convergence analysis. I hope to see such a statement. When it is said in section 3.2 that "...it poses a challenge to AutoBDMC," AutoBDMC should be clearly defined. A few more details: Line 149: Should p(theta, z | y) be p(theta, z, y)? Symbolic inconsistency: The paper switches between using y and D to represent data/observations, which sometimes seems a bit confusing. Supplemental WebPPL code: It should be mentioned with (a) which versions of WebPPL it works with, and (b) how to integrate it into a WebPPL distribution for use. Or even better, it should be published as a WebPPL package that can be installed via npm. If possible, a similar publication for a Stan version of the code would also be greatly appreciated.
