- Introduces AutoBDMC: using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (AIS + reverse AIS) to get stochastic upper/lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood for MCMC convergence assessment.  
- Shows the gap between those bounds bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the approximate sample distribution, yielding a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Method requires synthetic data from the joint, but authors provide empirical evidence that synthetic-data analyses predict real-world behavior.  
- Demonstrates useful applications for model selection and debugging with reasonably compelling experimental results.  
- Paper is overall well-written and well-motivated.  
- Major clarity issue: Section 3 is harder to follow; the transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and launches into variable definitions and equations without justification.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper must explicitly state this—it's central to the method’s novelty.  
- The paper never explicitly defines “AutoBDMC”; this definition should appear early (by Section 3.2) so references to AutoBDMC are unambiguous.  
- Minor correction: Line 149’s p(θ, z | y) likely should be p(θ, z, y) to denote the joint.  
- Notational inconsistencies: the paper alternates between y and D for data/observations, which is confusing.  
- Supplementary WebPPL code should state compatible WebPPL versions and integration instructions; ideally release as an npm-installable WebPPL package, and provide a similar release for Stan code.
