- Presents BREAD (Bounding Divergences with REverse Annealing), a method using AIS and reverse AIS (rAIS) to bound MCMC inference quality via bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC).  
- Shows stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood and uses their gap to upper-bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Provides protocol and experiments arguing that analyses on simulated data can indicate behavior on real-world datasets.  
- Demonstrates practical applications: guiding model design, debugging implementations, and applicability to WebPPL and Stan.  
- Novel contribution: extending BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence, offering a scalar metric for MCMC performance.  
- Theoretical analysis appears rigorous and well-supported.  
- Practicality: gives a concrete, quantifiable metric for assessing MCMC convergence, addressing a long-standing challenge.  
- Experimental evaluation is reasonably compelling and the paper is generally well-written.  
- Major clarity issue: Section 3 (particularly transition to 3.1) is hard to follow, with abrupt variable/equation introductions and insufficient contextualization.  
- BREAD is never explicitly defined; paper should clearly state that BREAD is the use of AIS and rAIS via BDMC to analyze MCMC convergence.  
- Limitation: rigorous bounds require exact posterior samples, so method relies on simulated data which limits direct applicability to real problems.  
- Minor issues: notation inconsistencies (e.g., p(θ, z | y) vs p(θ, z, y), y vs D), and supplemental WebPPL code needs version info, integration instructions, and preferably an npm package (and similarly for Stan).  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with novel, sound, and practical elements; clarity improvements and addressing the simulated-data limitation would strengthen the paper.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
