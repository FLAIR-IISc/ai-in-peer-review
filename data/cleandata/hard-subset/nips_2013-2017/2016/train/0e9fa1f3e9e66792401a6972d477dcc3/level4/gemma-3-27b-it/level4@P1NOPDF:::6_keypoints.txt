- Paper implements bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) in WebPPL and Stan but presents no novel theoretical or algorithmic contributions.  
- Core contribution is a practical case study showing BMC applied within probabilistic programming frameworks.  
- Work mainly summarizes previously published BMC results rather than extending them.  
- More suitable venue would be an MCMC or probabilistic programming workshop than NeurIPS.  
- Evaluation is weak: lacks rigorous, quantitative comparisons to standard MCMC methods (e.g., HMC) in WebPPL/Stan.  
- Empirical results rely on qualitative observations and a single example; a broader range of models is needed.  
- Paper should include quantitative metrics (convergence speed, mixing, accuracy) to demonstrate BMC’s performance trade-offs.  
- Background on BMC is cursory; readers would benefit from more detailed explanation of core concepts and the weighting scheme’s impact on convergence.  
- Authors should consider and report any optimizations for BMC implementations in these environments.  
- Potential limitations of applying BMC in WebPPL and Stan are not fully discussed.  
- Writing is generally clear but needs careful editing to fix awkward phrasing and improve readability.  
- Recommendation: reject for NeurIPS but consider submitting a revised, expanded version to a specialized MCMC/probabilistic programming workshop.
