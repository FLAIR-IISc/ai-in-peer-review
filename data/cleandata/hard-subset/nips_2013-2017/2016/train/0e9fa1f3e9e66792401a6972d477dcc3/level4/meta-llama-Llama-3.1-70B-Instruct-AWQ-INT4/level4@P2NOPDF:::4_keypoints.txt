- Introduces AutoBDMC, using Bidirectional Monte Carlo and AIS to compute stochastic upper/lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood.  
- Shows the gap between bounds bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate-sample distribution.  
- Provides a single scalar metric for assessing MCMC convergence.  
- Method is limited to analyzing convergence on synthetic data sampled from the joint distribution.  
- Experimental evidence indicates synthetic-data analyses predict behavior on real-world data.  
- Demonstrates utility of the method for model selection and debugging.  
- Paper is well-motivated and readable overall.  
- Section 3 (especially transition to 3.1) is hard to follow; variables and equations are introduced without sufficient motivation.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, it should be explicitly stated as a key contribution.  
- A clear, early definition of “AutoBDMC” is missing; it appears to mean using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC, and this should be stated upfront.  
- Minor notation issues: unclear use of p(theta, z | y) vs p(theta, z, y) on Line 149 and inconsistent use of y vs D for data.  
- Supplementary code notes: WebPPL code needs compatible version info and integration instructions; ideally release as an npm-installable WebPPL package.  
- Recommend also releasing the Stan code in a similar easy-to-install form.
