- Proposes BREAD: using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to assess MCMC convergence by bounding symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between true posterior and approximate sample distribution.  
- BREAD applies to algorithms viewed as importance sampling over extended state spaces (e.g., AIS, SMC).  
- Reviews problem motivation: traditional MCMC convergence heuristics are unreliable; need quantitative measures.  
- BDMC produces stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood.  
- The gap between BDMC bounds yields a bound on the Jeffreys divergence between posterior and approximate samples.  
- Experiments on synthetic data drawn from the joint distribution show the method analyzes convergence effectively.  
- Results on synthetic data are predictive of behavior on real-world datasets.  
- Demonstrates utility for model selection and debugging.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and presents a useful method for MCMC convergence assessment.  
- Some difficulty following Section 3, especially the transition into 3.1 and its variable/equation definitions.  
- Needs clearer statement of novelty/contribution, particularly regarding the divergence bound in Section 3.1.  
- Minor notation/terminology issues (e.g., inconsistent use of y vs D for data) that should be standardized.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel useful method, well-motivated and clear, reasonably compelling experiments.  
- Rejection concerns: sections needing more motivation/explanation, minor notation problems, and need for clearer standardization/clarification.
