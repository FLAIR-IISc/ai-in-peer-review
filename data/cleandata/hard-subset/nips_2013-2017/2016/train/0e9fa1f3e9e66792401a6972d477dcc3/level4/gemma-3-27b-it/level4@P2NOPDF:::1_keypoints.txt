- Introduces a novel method to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between MCMC outputs and the target distribution for debugging MCMC in PPLs.  
- Core idea of using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to diagnose sampler convergence is strong and promising as an alternative to existing diagnostics.  
- Appropriately relates to prior work ([1],[2]) that assesses samplers by their ability to recover prior samples.  
- Presentation impedes clarity: Section 3.1 contains a difficult derivation that is hard to follow.  
- Notation issue: q(.) is introduced without definition — it should be explicitly stated as the AIS sampler’s marginal after T steps.  
- Supplementary material clarifies the derivation, but the main text should be self-contained.  
- Paper needs a clear, step-by-step exposition of the full procedure (initialization → execution → final divergence estimation).  
- Splitting unobserved variables into z and θ is unnecessary; use a single combined variable for simplicity.  
- Minor typo: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
