- Paper proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to assess MCMC sample quality by bounding Jeffreys divergence using BDMC/AIS log-likelihood gaps.
- Key theoretical claim: expected gap between AIS upper and lower bounds upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between AIS samples and the true posterior.
- Reverse AIS requires exact posterior samples, so BREAD is limited to simulated data; authors propose a protocol (fit hyperparameters on real data → simulate dataset → apply BREAD) to bridge to real-world use.
- Implementation and validation provided in WebPPL and Stan, with experiments across five Stan models and use-cases comparing sampler representations.
- Empirical results show inference behavior on simulated data (from inferred hyperparameters) is a good proxy for behavior on real data.
- Practical utility demonstrated by using BREAD to compare collapsed vs. uncollapsed samplers and by uncovering a bug in WebPPL.
- Strength: addresses a significant, longstanding problem—providing a principled quantitative metric beyond heuristics like Gelman-Rubin.
- Strength: original insight repurposing BDMC/AIS log-likelihood gaps to bound a divergence metric.
- Strength: technically sound derivation and thorough experimental validation across multiple models and platforms.
- Weakness: the central theoretical contribution (Jeffreys divergence derivation) is not explicitly highlighted as the main novel result.
- Weakness: Section 3’s exposition is abrupt and hard to follow; needs a motivating intro and smoother transitions into equations.
- Weakness: the term “BREAD” and its exact scope (theory vs. protocol) should be defined more explicitly early in the paper.
- Detailed comment: notation is inconsistent (uses both y and D for data); standardize symbols.
- Detailed comment: encourage releasing code/packages (e.g., WebPPL npm package) and clear reproducibility instructions including language versions and integration steps.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with minor clarity revisions; recommend acceptance.
