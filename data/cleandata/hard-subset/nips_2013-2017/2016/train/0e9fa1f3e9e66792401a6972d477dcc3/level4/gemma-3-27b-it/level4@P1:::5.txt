The authors address the challenging problem of assessing the reliability of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) outputs and comparing the efficiency of different MCMC schemes. They propose leveraging the upper and lower stochastic bounds on the log marginal likelihood provided by the Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) procedure. The core idea is to select, among available MCMC schemes, the one that produces the tightest BDMC stochastic bounds for a given computational effort. A key challenge is that BDMC requires an exact sample from the target distribution to produce a valid stochastic upper bound. The authors propose heuristics to circumvent this limitation. Their approach, in the context of Bayesian posterior sampling, involves performing the diagnostic on the posterior generated from synthetic data rather than real data, exploiting the relative ease of obtaining good approximate samples for synthetic posteriors. Finally, the proposed methodology is evaluated on several examples.

The idea of using stochastic lower bounds from Bidirectional Monte Carlo as an MCMC convergence diagnostic is interesting. Even though the methodology appears specific to AIS and SMC samplers and relies on assessing the marginal likelihood, it represents a valuable attempt to develop theoretically justified MCMC diagnostic tools. However, the implementation has several drawbacks that, in my opinion, limit the appeal of the proposed diagnostic methodology. These drawbacks stem from the heuristic approach taken to address the requirement of an exact sample from the posterior for BDMC to be valid. 

I identify three key concerns. First, the authors assume prior knowledge – obtained, for example, from a previously run MCMC – of hyperparameters, denoted as ηreal, which is representative of the entire posterior distribution. This assumption implies both that the algorithm used to find ηreal had already converged (which is precisely the original problem of interest) and that the posterior can be adequately summarized by a single value. Both assumptions are strong and generally lack justification. Second, they assume that the posterior induced by synthetic data generated from ηreal exhibits similar MCMC convergence properties to the original posterior. This assumption also requires careful justification, particularly in scenarios involving model misspecification. It seems the authors address the difficulty posed by BDMC by effectively “changing the problem,” performing diagnostics on a different posterior. Third, they optimistically assume that starting from ηreal allows the MCMC algorithm to quickly obtain an almost exact sample from the posterior – a scenario that may not always hold. Therefore, the proposed methodology appears to rely on assumptions closely tied to the very thing it aims to assess: the convergence properties of the original MCMC algorithm.

I appreciate the authors acknowledging these limitations and presenting preliminary results in Section 5.1 demonstrating that, in simple examples, these issues do not critically affect their conclusions. However, these limited examples are not sufficiently compelling to claim that these issues will not be critical in more complex scenarios.

The authors critique existing convergence diagnostic tools for their potential failures in multimodal cases or when using insufficiently expressive summary statistics (lines 203-205). They should explain how their method overcomes these limitations and provide evidence to support this claim. More generally, it would be beneficial to include simulations comparing their method with commonly used diagnostics (e.g., those described in Section 4) to demonstrate scenarios where it provides more reliable assessments.

In summary, this work proposes an interesting, though not entirely convincing, approach to MCMC diagnostics. While the theoretical foundations are solid, the practical implementation relies on assumptions that need further investigation and justification. The experimental validation, while promising, is limited in scope. The work could be significantly strengthened by addressing the concerns raised above and providing a more thorough empirical evaluation.
