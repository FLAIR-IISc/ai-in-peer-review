- Proposal: use Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds on log marginal likelihood to assess MCMC reliability by choosing methods that yield the tightest bounds for equal computation.

- Critical requirement: BMC needs an exact sample from the target posterior to provide valid stochastic upper bounds.

- Authors’ workaround: run diagnostics on a synthetic posterior generated from a pre-specified hyperparameter η_real, assuming it’s easier to sample and mirrors the true posterior.

- Fundamental flaw 1: method assumes η_real is known and representative, which is circular because it effectively requires a prior converged MCMC run.

- Fundamental flaw 1 (cont.): summarizing a complex or multimodal posterior with a single η_real is unjustified and likely invalid.

- Fundamental flaw 2: assumes synthetic and true posteriors share convergence behavior; this fails under model misspecification and lacks theoretical or empirical support.

- Fundamental flaw 3: assumes MCMC initialized at η_real quickly produces near-exact samples of the synthetic posterior—an unverified, scenario-dependent condition that can fail.

- Consequence: the diagnostic effectively tests convergence on a synthetic surrogate rather than the true target posterior.

- Authors acknowledge limitations but do not sufficiently address their implications or mitigate risks.

- Experimental weaknesses: provided examples are overly simple and do not test multimodality, high dimensionality, or model misspecification where diagnostics typically fail.

- Missing comparative validation: no benchmarking against standard diagnostics (e.g., Gelman–Rubin, ESS) on controlled simulations with known ground-truth convergence.

- Risk example: if synthetic posterior is unimodal but true posterior is multimodal, BMC bounds may falsely indicate convergence.

- Remedies recommended: either rigorously justify synthetic/posterior equivalence or develop a framework avoiding that assumption (e.g., integrate BMC with adaptive MCMC); also run comparative simulations and clarify dependence on η_real.

- Overall verdict: promising idea but undermined by unverified assumptions and limited validation, making the diagnostic currently impractical for real-world MCMC analysis.
