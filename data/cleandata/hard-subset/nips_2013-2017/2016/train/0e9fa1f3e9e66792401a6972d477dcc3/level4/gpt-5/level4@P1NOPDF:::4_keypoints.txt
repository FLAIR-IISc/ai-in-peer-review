- Proposes AutoBDMC: uses BDMC with AIS/rAIS to obtain stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood for assessing MCMC convergence.
- Shows the gap between BDMC bounds upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate-sample distribution, giving a single scalar convergence diagnostic.
- Demonstrates that behavior on synthetic data sampled from the joint predicts performance on real-world datasets and shows applications to model selection and debugging.
- Paper is readable, well-motivated, and experimentally compelling; the method appears practically useful.
- Section 3 is significantly harder to follow than the rest; transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and lacks motivation/context.
- The divergence bound in Section 3.1 appears to be a novel theoretical result and should be stated explicitly as such.
- “AutoBDMC” is never precisely defined; the paper should clearly state early that it means using AIS and rAIS within BDMC to assess MCMC convergence.
- Section 3.2 refers to challenges for AutoBDMC before a crisp definition of the method is provided.
- Typo/question: Line 149 — check whether p(theta, z | y) should be p(theta, z, y).
- Inconsistent notation for observed data (y vs D); standardize to a single symbol.
- Recommend adding a short motivating paragraph before Section 3.1 explaining the derivation goal, high-level argument, and relation to BDMC/AIS/MCMC convergence.
- Be explicit about the assumptions under which synthetic-data diagnostics predict real-data performance and identify settings where this may fail.
- Request clearer code release: specify WebPPL version(s), integration instructions, preferably release as an npm-installable WebPPL package, and provide a similar packaged Stan release.
