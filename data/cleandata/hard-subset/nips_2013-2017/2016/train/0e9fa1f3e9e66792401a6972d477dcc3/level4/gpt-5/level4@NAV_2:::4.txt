This paper proposes a method—referred to as AutoBDMC—for evaluating MCMC convergence. The approach leverages the recently introduced Bidirectional Monte Carlo framework to obtain stochastic lower and upper bounds on the log marginal likelihood via annealed importance sampling. The authors argue that the gap between these bounds serves as a bound on the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the exact posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, yielding a single scalar indicator of MCMC convergence quality. Because the technique evaluates convergence only on synthetic data sampled from the joint model, the paper presents empirical evidence that performance on such simulated data predicts behavior on real datasets. The method is further demonstrated for tasks such as model selection and debugging. Overall, the paper is clearly written and well-motivated; the method appears practically valuable, and the empirical results are reasonably persuasive.

I found Section 3 less accessible than the rest of the manuscript. The transition into 3.1 is abrupt, introducing variables and equations without sufficient context. If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel (as I believe it is), this should be stated explicitly, since the paper’s contribution seems to rest on that result.

It is also unclear what precisely is meant by AutoBDMC, as the term is never formally defined. My interpretation is that AutoBDMC refers to using AIS and reverse AIS to assess MCMC convergence, but the paper should state this explicitly. By the point where Section 3.2 notes that “…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC,” the term should already be clearly defined.
