- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen-Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler’s output and the target distribution, aimed at debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.  
- The idea to use BDMC for diagnosing MCMC convergence failures is compelling and addresses limitations of existing diagnostics.  
- Authors should discuss and relate their method to Geweke (2004) and Cook, Gelman & Rubin (2006/2012), which also test MCMC by running samplers on prior-generated data.  
- Derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow; notation q(·) appears before being defined and should be explicitly stated (e.g., as the AIS marginal after T steps).  
- Main text is not fully self-contained—clarifications in the supplement should be moved into the paper so readers aren’t forced to piece the procedure together.  
- Paper needs a clear, step-by-step algorithm or procedural description of how to apply the proposed method rather than distributed explanations.  
- Minor: distinction between unobserved variables z and θ is unnecessary and clutters notation; use a single variable.  
- Minor: missing closing parenthesis on line 165.
