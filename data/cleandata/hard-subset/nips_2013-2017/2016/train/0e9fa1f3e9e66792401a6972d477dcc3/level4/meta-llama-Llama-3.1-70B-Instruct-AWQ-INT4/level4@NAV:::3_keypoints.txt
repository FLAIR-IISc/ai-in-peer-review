- Proposes using the gap between BDMC lower and upper bounds as a performance measure for MCMC transition operators.
- Implements BDMC in Stan and WebPPL and proposes a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.
- Strength: simple, easy-to-implement method with potential high impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Weakness: technical novelty is modest and main contributions are presented too briefly after reiterating existing BDMC material.
- Key technical note: the bound gap provides an upper bound on the Jensen–Shannon divergence.
- Missed related work: fails to cite and connect to Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions, which is highly relevant.
- Clarity problems: ambiguous definition of vector v (should be z_{1:T-1}, θ_{1:T-1}).
- Clarity problems: inconsistent labeling ("rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back") needs standardization.
- Clarity problems: estimator \hat{p}(y) in equations (5–7) and (9–11) is undefined and must be specified.
- Clarity problems: Equation (6) appears incorrect and should be revised.
- Clarity problems: definition of AutoBDMC is unclear and should be explicitly stated.
- Experimental shortcomings: need systematic study of how lower/upper bounds vary with step size and clearer choice/role of β_t relative to T.
- Experimental details missing: state AutoBDMC parameterization in terms of number of steps T and define RAIS and its variants in captions.
- Presentation fixes: increase axis and tick label sizes in Figures 2 and 3.
- Typos/citations: missing closing parentheses in D_KL terms and mis-citation of Hoffman as Homan in the NUTS reference.
