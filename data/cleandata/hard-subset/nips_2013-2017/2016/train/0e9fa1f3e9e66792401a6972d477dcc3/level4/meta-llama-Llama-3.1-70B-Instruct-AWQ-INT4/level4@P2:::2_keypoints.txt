- Proposes extending bidirectional Monte Carlo to measure reliability of MCMC posterior inference.  
- Runs AIS chains forward (prior→posterior) and backward (posterior→prior) on simulated data to upper-bound the expected symmetrized KL between true and approximate posteriors.  
- Builds on prior bidirectional Monte Carlo and importance sampling work but offers a new application for evaluating MCMC accuracy.  
- Integrated the method into two probabilistic programming languages: WebPPL and Stan.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Experiments are well-designed and demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Strength: introduces a novel evaluation technique for MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Strength: practical contribution via integration into WebPPL and Stan.  
- Weakness: method is limited to algorithms like AIS or SMC and may not apply to all MCMC methods.  
- Weakness: lacks comprehensive comparison with other existing MCMC evaluation methods.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; overall recommendation: accept with suggestion to add broader comparisons.
