- Proposes using the BDMC lower–upper bound gap as a metric for MCMC operator quality and implements BDMC for Stan and WebPPL with an evaluation protocol.  
- Strength: simple, elegant, easy-to-implement idea with potentially high impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main technical novelty is modest; the observation that the bound gap upper-bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence may not be entirely new.  
- Major omission: fails to cite or discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is highly relevant.  
- Tempered Transitions performs the same forward/reverse sweeps as BDMC and its acceptance ratio corresponds to the ratio of BDMC’s stochastic bounds.  
- Neal’s interpretation links the TT acceptance ratio to two thermodynamic integration estimates that bound log partition function differences—this connection is missing.  
- Paper neglects discussion of annealing schedule (βt) choice; step sizes Δβ are critical for acceptance/gap trade-offs.  
- Lack of adaptive schedule discussion despite “AutoBDMC” name—AutoBDMC is never clearly defined (adaptive vs fixed hyperparameters unclear).  
- Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed and under-detailed, causing clarity issues throughout the paper.  
- Notational ambiguities: imprecise definition of v and inconsistent labels for AIS sweeps (“rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”).  
- Mathematical clarity issues: expectations taken over an undefined estimator p̂(y) and likely incorrect expression in Eq. (6) (KL term appears wrong).  
- Experiments lack detail on annealing schedule (code suggests linear β but not stated) and do not systematically study how bounds vary with step size.  
- Figure and caption issues: unclear labels (e.g., “HMC / No-U-Turn steps”), undefined acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS), and small font sizes hurting readability.  
- Minor typos: missing parentheses in D_JS expression and “Homan” should be “Hoffman” in the NUTS citation.  
- Overall conclusion: idea is valuable and potentially impactful, but paper needs clearer exposition, more thorough related-work discussion, and stronger experimental study to justify acceptance.
