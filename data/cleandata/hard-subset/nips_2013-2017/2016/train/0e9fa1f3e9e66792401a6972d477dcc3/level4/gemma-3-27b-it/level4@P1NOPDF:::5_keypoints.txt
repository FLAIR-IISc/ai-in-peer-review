- Paper proposes selecting MCMC schemes by choosing the one that yields the tightest Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds for a given computational effort.

- Core obstacle: BMC requires an exact target sample to produce a valid stochastic upper bound; authors introduce heuristics to avoid this.

- Main heuristic: run the diagnostic on a posterior induced by synthetic data (from a chosen η_real) where good approximate samples are easier to obtain.

- Evaluation: methodology tested on several examples; concept of using BMC lower bounds for convergence diagnostics is interesting and theoretically motivated.

- Scope limitation: approach appears specific to AIS and SMC samplers and focuses only on marginal likelihood estimation.

- Major implementation drawback: reliance on heuristics to replace the exact-sample requirement reduces the method’s appeal.

- Key concern 1: assumes prior knowledge of a representative hyperparameter η_real (often from a previous MCMC), which presumes the original algorithm has already converged and reduces the posterior to a single value.

- Key concern 2: assumes the synthetic-data posterior has similar MCMC convergence properties to the original posterior, an unsupported and fragile assumption (especially under model misspecification).

- Key concern 3: assumes initializing MCMC at η_real yields an almost exact sample quickly, which is not guaranteed in general.

- Overall critique: the method depends on assumptions intrinsically tied to the very convergence properties it aims to diagnose, undermining its validity.

- Empirical weakness: Section 5.1’s simple examples do not demonstrate robustness of the approach in more complex, realistic scenarios.

- Requested clarifications: authors should explain how their method overcomes known failures of existing diagnostics in multimodal cases or with poor summary statistics.

- Suggested experiments: include simulations comparing the proposed diagnostic to standard diagnostics (those in Section 4) to show cases where it provides more reliable assessments.
