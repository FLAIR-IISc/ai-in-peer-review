- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating MCMC in probabilistic programming languages.  
- Core idea is promising because existing MCMC diagnostics are problematic.  
- Approach closely aligns with prior joint-distribution/posterior-quantile validation work (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2006).  
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow: q(·) is introduced without definition and should be explicitly defined (apparently the AIS marginal after T steps).  
- Paper relies on the supplement; the main text should be self-contained and include a clear, step-by-step end-to-end procedure.  
- Minor issue: splitting unobserved variables into z and θ is unnecessary/clutters the presentation since they co-occur.  
- Minor issue: missing closing parenthesis at line 165.
