This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen-Shannon (J-S) divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution, with a specific focus on debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations within probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). I find the idea of leveraging BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures compelling – this approach appears potentially quite valuable, given the known limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics. The paper appropriately references [1, 2], which also assess MCMC sampler performance by evaluating them on data drawn from the prior distribution.

Section 3.1: I found the derivation in this section difficult to follow. Specifically, the notation *q(.)* is used before being explicitly defined. While the context suggests it represents the marginal distribution of the Approximate Importance Sampling (AIS) sampler after *T* steps, this should be stated directly. The explanation becomes somewhat clearer upon consulting the supplementary material, but the main text should be self-contained and not require reference to the supplement for understanding. More generally, a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the method would be beneficial; currently, the reader needs to synthesize this information from various parts of the text. 

Minor comment: The separation of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* feels somewhat arbitrary, especially in this general setting. Since *z* and *θ* always appear together, it might be clearer to eliminate one of the variables – using *θ* alone, for instance – to reduce visual clutter. Line 165: A closing parenthesis is missing.
