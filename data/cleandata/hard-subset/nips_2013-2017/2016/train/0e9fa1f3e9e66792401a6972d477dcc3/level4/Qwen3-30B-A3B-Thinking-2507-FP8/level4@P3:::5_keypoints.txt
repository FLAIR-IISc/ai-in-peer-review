- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo to bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate MCMC samples and the true posterior using simulated-data ancestral sampling (BREAD).  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and proposes a protocol for real data: fit hyperparameters, simulate data from them, and evaluate inference on the synthetic data.  
- Validated on five models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization, etc.), showing consistency between real/synthetic data and discovering a WebPPL bug.  

Strengths:  
- Novel repurposing of BDMC for MCMC diagnostics, addressing a gap in prior work.  
- Practical utility demonstrated by implementations in two major probabilistic programming systems and a useful debugging use case.  
- Rigorous empirical validation: consistency checks and insensitivity to approximate reverse initialization.  
- Clear, mathematically sound derivation of the Jeffreys divergence bound.  

Weaknesses:  
- Protocol circularity: relies on fitted hyperparameters from MCMC that may not have converged, an assumption untested in complex or misspecified/multimodal settings.  
- Limited applicability: only works for AIS/SMC-style inference (extended state spaces), excluding many common MCMC methods like Metropolis–Hastings.  
- Lacks comparisons with standard diagnostics (e.g., Gelman–Rubin, Geweke) on challenging/multimodal problems to demonstrate superior performance.  

Pro/Con summary:  
- Pros: first theoretically grounded diagnostic for probabilistic programming, validated and integrated, provides actionable modeling insights.  
- Cons: circular protocol assumptions, narrow method scope, and no evidence of superiority in hard cases.  

Recommendation and required revisions:  
- Recommend weak acceptance (score 3): valuable contribution but needs significant clarification and evaluation.  
- Authors should explicitly discuss protocol limitations for misspecified/multimodal settings, compare BREAD to standard diagnostics on challenging models, and clarify that BREAD applies to AIS/SMC-based inference only.
