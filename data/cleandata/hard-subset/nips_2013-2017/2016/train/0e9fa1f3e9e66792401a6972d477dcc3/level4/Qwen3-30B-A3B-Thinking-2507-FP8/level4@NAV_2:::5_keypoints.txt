- Proposes using BMC's stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood to evaluate and choose MCMC schemes by selecting the one with the tightest bounds under a compute budget.
- Core technical challenge: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce valid stochastic upper bounds.
- Heuristic solution: perform diagnostics on posteriors from synthetic data generated using a representative hyperparameter η_real obtained from a prior MCMC run.
- Validated on several examples; idea of leveraging BMC for MCMC convergence diagnosis is promising and theoretically motivated.
- Method appears restricted to AIS and SMC samplers and centers on marginal-likelihood–based diagnostics.
- Major flaw: assumes η_real from a previous MCMC is representative, which requires that prior MCMC already converged (circular and unjustified).
- Assumes the synthetic-data posterior induced by η_real has similar MCMC convergence behavior to the real posterior—this lacks justification and can fail under model misspecification.
- Assumes starting from η_real lets MCMC rapidly produce near-exact samples—an optimistic, non-guaranteed assumption critical to the method.
- The simple examples (Section 5.1) do not demonstrate these assumptions hold in more complex, realistic scenarios.
- Claims of outperforming existing diagnostics in multimodal or summary-statistic–poor settings are not sufficiently explained or evidenced.
- Paper would be significantly strengthened by comparative simulations against common diagnostic methods to show cases where the proposed approach is more reliable.
