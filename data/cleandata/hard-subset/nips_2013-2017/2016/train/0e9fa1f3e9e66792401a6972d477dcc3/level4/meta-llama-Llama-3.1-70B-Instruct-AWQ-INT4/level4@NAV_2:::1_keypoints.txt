- Proposes BREAD: a novel method extending bidirectional Monte Carlo to bound symmetrized KL between true posterior and MCMC approximate samples.  
- Well-motivated and clearly explains challenges of measuring MCMC posterior sample quality.  
- Method is rigorously described with detailed analysis of properties and limitations.  
- Experiments are thorough, well-designed, and demonstrate BREAD’s effectiveness in realistic settings.  
- Paper is well-written with an informative background and good positioning within MCMC diagnostics and probabilistic programming.  
- Strengths: novelty, technical soundness, generality, and potential impact for probabilistic programming and Bayesian statistics.  
- Demonstrated utility for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations in probabilistic programming languages.  
- Main weakness: Section 3.1 derivation is complex and may be hard for some readers to follow.  
- Suggestion: provide a clearer end-to-end application recipe plus more explanations or illustrative examples.  
- Minor issues: unnecessary notation (e.g., z vs. theta distinction) and a missing close parenthesis on line 165.  
- Related-work gap: recommend citing additional MCMC diagnostics/probabilistic programming references (e.g., Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2012).  
- Acceptance pros: rigorous, novel method; clear motivation; solid analysis; convincing experiments.  
- Acceptance cons: derivation clarity concerns; need for more examples, clearer application steps, and additional contextual background.
