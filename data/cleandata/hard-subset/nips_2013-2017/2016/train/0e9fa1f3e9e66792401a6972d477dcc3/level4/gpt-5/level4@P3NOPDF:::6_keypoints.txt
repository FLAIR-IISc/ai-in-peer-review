- Paper implements and evaluates bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) in WebPPL and Stan, positioned as a practical guide rather than novel research.
- Strength: Practical implementations lower the barrier for practitioners to apply BDMC in common PPL workflows.
- Strength: Consolidates prior BDMC work and clarifies deployment steps, aiding standardization and reproducibility.
- Strength: Cross-language comparison highlights tooling differences, defaults, diagnostics, and language-specific pitfalls.
- Strength: Planned release of code/scripts would contribute useful reference implementations for the community.
- Weakness: Limited novelty—no new algorithms, theory, or substantial empirical insights beyond existing BDMC literature.
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation is shallow, demonstrating feasibility rather than providing new conclusions or robust comparisons.
- Weakness: Lacks explicit, evidence-based guidance on when BDMC is practical in WebPPL or Stan (costs, convergence, robustness).
- Weakness: Missing clear comparison to alternative marginal likelihood estimators (AIS, bridge sampling, TI, SMC) in accuracy and cost.
- Weakness: Contribution is primarily software/implementation, making the paper a better fit for a workshop or tools/reproducibility venue than NeurIPS.
- Clarity: Writing and high-level BDMC description are clear, but more engineering details (initialization, schedules, proposals, diagnostics) are needed for reuse.
- Clarity: Terminology should be standardized and prior work clearly separated from language-specific adaptations.
- Suggestions: Add a concrete novel contribution (e.g., BDMC-specific diagnostic, adaptive scheme, theoretical improvement, or generalizable language optimizations).
- Suggestions: Strengthen experiments with head-to-head baselines, diverse models, variance/bias metrics, compute-cost reporting, and sensitivity analyses/ablations.
- Suggestions: Provide actionable guidance on when to prefer BDMC, diagnostics for failure modes, scalability limits, and support for discrete/multimodal cases.
- Suggestions: Improve reproducibility by releasing well-documented code, reproduction scripts, and a minimal API with examples for both languages.
- Suggestions: Better position the work relative to prior BDMC papers and competing estimators, summarizing theoretical guarantees and practical trade-offs.
- Recommendation: Recommend rejection for NeurIPS due to insufficient novelty/significance; encourage submission to a specialized workshop or reproducibility-focused venue after strengthening contributions.
