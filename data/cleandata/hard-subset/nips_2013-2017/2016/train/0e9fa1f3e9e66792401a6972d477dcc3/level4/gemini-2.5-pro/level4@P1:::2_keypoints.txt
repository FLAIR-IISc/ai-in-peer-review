- Proposes BREAD: extends BDMC by running AIS forward and reverse to obtain stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood and bound posterior quality.  
- Theoretical result: the expected gap between AIS bounds upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between AIS approximate and true posterior.  
- Key practical challenge: reverse chain requires an exact posterior sample, unavailable on real data.  
- Practical protocol: infer hyperparameters from real data, simulate a dataset with those hyperparameters, then apply BREAD to the simulated data.  
- Empirical validation: experiments show inference behavior on simulated data closely mirrors real data for several models.  
- Implementation: BREAD integrated into Stan and WebPPL, demonstrating practical usability.  
- Strength — significance/originality: provides a principled, quantitative measure of MCMC sample quality beyond standard diagnostics.  
- Strength — technical quality: elegant, sound derivation and thoughtful treatment of limitations with convincing empirical support.  
- Strength — practical impact: useful for guiding modeling choices (e.g., collapsed vs uncollapsed MF) and debugging inference systems (found a WebPPL bug).  
- Weakness — reliance on simulated data: rigorous guarantees hold only for simulated data; real-data protocol is a heuristic dependent on initial hyperparameter estimates.  
- Weakness — sampler applicability: directly applies to AIS/SMC-like samplers framed as importance sampling over extended space; not directly applicable to standard MH/HMC/NUTS.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-validated contribution with clear limitations acknowledged; strongly recommended for acceptance.
