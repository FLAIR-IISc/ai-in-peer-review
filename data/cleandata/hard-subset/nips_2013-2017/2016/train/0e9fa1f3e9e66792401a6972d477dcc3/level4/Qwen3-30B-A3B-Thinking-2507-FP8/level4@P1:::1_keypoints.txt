- Introduces BREAD, extending BDMC to upper-bound the expected Jeffreys divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior.  
- Core method: run forward and reverse AIS chains to obtain rigorous divergence bounds.  
- Proposes a protocol to transfer insights from simulated data to real-world applications by fitting hyperparameters, simulating data, and comparing inference behavior.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and validates empirically across multiple models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization).  
- Validates theory on toy distributions where exact Jeffreys divergence is computable.  
- Theoretical contribution: derivation showing BDMC upper-bounds expected Jeffreys divergence (Section 3.1).  
- Practical utility demonstrated by discovering a WebPPL bug using BREAD.  
- Open-sourced implementations enhance reproducibility.  
- Strength: provides rigorous, quantitative bounds addressing a key gap in MCMC convergence diagnostics.  
- Strength: relevant to scalable, reliable probabilistic inference and complements prior MCMC diagnostic work.  
- Weakness: theoretical derivation is dense and unclear in places; key notation (e.g., q(·)) is introduced ambiguously.  
- Weakness: inconsistent notation (use of z vs. θ) and minor typographical errors reduce clarity.  
- Weakness: reliance on the supplement for key definitions detracts from readability of the main text.  
- Suggested improvement: include a concise, step-by-step procedure for applying BREAD to improve accessibility.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify exposition and notation.
