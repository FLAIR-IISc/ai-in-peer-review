- Proposal: use the gap between BDMC stochastic lower and upper bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC operators; implementations for Stan and WebPPL and an evaluation protocol are provided.

- Strength: the idea is simple, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.

- Main concern: limited novelty and depth; much of BDMC is reiterated from existing work and technical contributions are modest.

- Novel technical claim: the gap upper-bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence — a nice but arguably not wholly novel observation.

- Missing related work: the paper omits Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is highly relevant and closely related.

- TT connection: BDMC’s reverse/forward sweeps and the acceptance ratio are analogous to TT; this connection and implications are not discussed.

- β schedule (Δβ) under-discussed: step sizes affect the gap and reliability; guidance on choosing Δβ is lacking.

- AutoBDMC undefined: the paper never unambiguously explains what “auto” entails or how it automates scheduling.

- Experiments insufficient: no systematic study of how lower/upper bounds vary with Δβ or of adaptive decreasing of Δβ to reach reliability.

- Sections 3.1–3.2 rushed: writing quality slips and several clarity issues arise in the technical exposition.

- Ambiguity in notation: definition of v is loose/ambiguous (unclear which variables are meant across two AIS sweeps).

- Missing/unclear estimators: the estimator p̂(y) is not defined in expectations (are they E[log R̂rev] and E[log R̂fwd]?).

- Equation errors: Equation (6) as written is nonsensical (KL between identical distributions); intended terms appear to be different.

- AutoBDMC purpose unclear: unclear whether it means (a) using the bound gap to assess convergence and (b) using a fixed hyperparameter scheme from §3.2.

- Experimental detail gaps: βt choice not stated (code hints step = 1/options.steps but paper omits this); unclear whether “HMC/No-U-Turn steps” refer to BDMC steps.

- Notation/label issues: RAIS and variants (u)c(R)AIS are not defined in captions; figure axes and tick labels are too small.

- Typos/errors: missing closing parentheses in DKL terms in D_JS expression; NUTS citation typo (Homan → Hoffman).

- Overall recommendation: method is promising and useful; acceptance contingent on addressing clarity, related-work, and experimental thoroughness in the camera-ready.
