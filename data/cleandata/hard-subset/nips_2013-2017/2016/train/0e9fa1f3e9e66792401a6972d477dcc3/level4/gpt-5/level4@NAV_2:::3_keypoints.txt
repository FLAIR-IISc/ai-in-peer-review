- Proposes using the gap between BDMC’s stochastic lower and upper AIS bounds as a general metric of MCMC sampler performance.  
- Implements BDMC for Stan and WebPPL and provides a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.  
- Method is elegant, straightforward, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Main technical novelty is modest; principal new observation is that the bound gap upper-bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence.  
- Sections 3.1–3.2 feel compressed and lack clarity; writing and exposition need improvement.  
- Fails to cite and discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related and would strengthen the narrative.  
- Tempered Transitions uses the same reverse/forward sweep and its acceptance ratio corresponds to the BDMC lower/upper weight ratio; temperature schedule {βt} matters similarly.  
- The BDMC gap depends on Δβ; large gaps suggest using a finer schedule and comparisons are unreliable when the log-gap is large (e.g., >1 nat).  
- “AutoBDMC” is not well-defined in the paper; needs explicit definition and explanation of what is automated.  
- Experiments omit details about βt schedule (apparently βt = t/T) and whether reported HMC/NUTS steps correspond to BDMC steps; this should be stated.  
- No definition for acronyms like RAIS or notation for (u)c(R)AIS in captions; clarity needed.  
- Recommend systematic experiments studying how bounds vary with step size and adaptive refinement of Δβ.  
- Specific clarity issues: ambiguous definition of v, inconsistent rev/fwd vs for/back labels, unclear expectations in Eqs. (5–7,9–11), and puzzling Eq. (6) formulation.  
- Presentation/formatting fixes: enlarge axis/tick fonts in figures and fix typos (missing parentheses in D_JS expression; Homan → Hoffman citation).
