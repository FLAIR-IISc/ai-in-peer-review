- Paper introduces BREAD: uses BDMC with AIS and reverse AIS to bound log marginal likelihoods and upper-bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.
- Main theoretical claim: the gap between stochastic upper and lower log-evidence bounds upper-bounds the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence, yielding a scalar MCMC-convergence metric.
- Rigorous bounds apply to simulated data; authors provide empirical evidence that the method predicts convergence behavior on real-world data.
- Demonstrated practical applications in model selection and debugging probabilistic programs.
- Overall assessment: well-written, well-motivated, and experimentally convincing.
- Section 3 is technically dense and difficult to follow; the transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and under-motivated.
- If the divergence bound derivation in Section 3.1 is novel, it should be explicitly highlighted as a central contribution.
- The term “BREAD” is never formally defined; the paper should clearly state that BREAD is the use of AIS + reverse AIS (BDMC) to assess MCMC convergence.
- Notational inconsistencies (e.g., y vs. D) and possible typo at Line 149 (p(θ, z | y) vs p(θ, z, y)) cause confusion.
- Reproducibility concern: WebPPL supplemental code lacks version info and installation/integration instructions; recommend releasing as an npm package and providing a Stan package.
- Arguments for acceptance: apparent novelty of bounding Jeffreys divergence with BDMC, solid theoretical grounding, practical utility for MCMC convergence assessment, empirical validation, and useful debugging application.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity/presentation issues in Section 3, missing formal definition of BREAD, minor notation problems, and limited immediate reproducibility due to packaging/documentation gaps.
