This paper presents AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence. It leverages Bidirectional Monte Carlo to obtain stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood via annealed importance sampling (AIS). The authors show that the gap between these bounds itself upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, yielding a single scalar diagnostic of MCMC convergence. Because the method can only analyze convergence on synthetic data generated from the joint distribution, the authors provide experiments indicating that behavior on such synthetic data predicts performance on real-world datasets. They also demonstrate applications to model selection and debugging.

Overall, the paper is readable, well-motivated, and proposes a clearly useful tool. The experimental evaluation is reasonably compelling.

That said, I found Section 3 significantly harder to follow than the rest. The transition into 3.1 is especially abrupt: it launches into variable and equation definitions without sufficient motivation or context. If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is a new theoretical result (as I believe it is), this should be stated explicitly, since the paper’s novelty appears to hinge on it.

Relatedly, the paper never precisely defines “AutoBDMC.” My understanding is that AutoBDMC refers to using AIS and reverse AIS (rAIS) within BDMC to assess MCMC convergence. If that is correct, please state this clearly early on. By the time Section 3.2 says “…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC,” the reader should already have a crisp definition of the method.

Minor comments and suggestions:
- Line 149: Should p(theta, z | y) be p(theta, z, y)? Please check for correctness.
- Notation: The paper alternates between y and D to denote the observed data, which is confusing. Please standardize on one symbol throughout.
- Clarity in Section 3.1: Before introducing the detailed notation and equations, add a short motivating paragraph explaining the goal of the derivation, the high-level structure of the argument, and how the bound relates to BDMC, AIS/rAIS, and MCMC convergence.
- Scope and claims: Since AutoBDMC evaluates convergence only on synthetic data from the joint, it would help to be more explicit about the assumptions under which synthetic-data diagnostics are predictive for real data, and to delineate any settings where this might fail.
- Code release: For the WebPPL code in the supplement, please include (a) the specific WebPPL version(s) with which it is known to work, and (b) clear instructions on how to integrate it into the WebPPL distribution. Even better, release it as a WebPPL package installable via npm. If possible, a similar packaged release for the Stan code would be very helpful to practitioners.

In summary, this is a promising and practical contribution. Clarifying the definition of AutoBDMC, explicitly stating the novelty of the divergence bound, and improving the motivation and exposition in Section 3 would substantially strengthen the paper. Standardizing notation and improving code packaging/documentation would further increase its accessibility and impact.
