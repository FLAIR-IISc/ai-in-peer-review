This paper introduces AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence. The approach leverages the Bidirectional Monte Carlo method to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood using annealed importance sampling (AIS). The authors show that the gap between these bounds itself provides an upper bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples. This yields a single scalar quantity for evaluating MCMC convergence.

A key limitation is that the method can only analyze convergence on synthetic data generated from the model's joint distribution. To address this, the authors provide compelling experimental evidence suggesting that the analysis on synthetic data is predictive of MCMC performance on real-world data. They further demonstrate the utility of their method for model selection and debugging.

Overall, the paper is well-motivated and clearly written, and the proposed method appears to be of significant practical utility. The experimental evaluation is convincing.

However, there are several areas that could be improved for clarity and impact:

**Major Points:**

*   **Clarity of Section 3:** I found Section 3 more difficult to follow than the rest of the paper. The transition into Section 3.1 is particularly jarring, as it immediately introduces variables and equations without providing sufficient motivation or high-level context for the reader.
*   **Novelty and Contribution:** The paper's primary contribution appears to be the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1. If this is indeed a novel result, this should be stated explicitly, as it is central to the paper's contribution.
*   **Definition of AutoBDMC:** The term "AutoBDMC" is never formally defined. My understanding is that it refers to the overall approach of using AIS and reverse AIS to analyze MCMC convergence via the JS-divergence bound. The paper would be significantly improved by a clear, concise definition of the term early on. For instance, the term is used in Section 3.2 ("…this poses a challenge for AutoBDMC") before the reader has been given a formal definition, which creates unnecessary confusion.

**Minor Points and Suggestions:**

*   **Notation:**
    *   On line 149, should `p(theta, z | y)` be the joint `p(theta, z, y)`?
    *   The paper inconsistently uses `y` and `D` to denote data/observations, which can be confusing for the reader.
*   **Supplemental Code:** The provided code would be more useful with the following additions:
    *   **WebPPL:** Please specify which version(s) of WebPPL the code is known to work with and provide clear instructions on how to integrate it. Even better would be to release it as a WebPPL package that can be installed via npm.
    *   **Stan:** A similar packaged release or detailed usage instructions for the Stan version of the code would also be greatly appreciated.
