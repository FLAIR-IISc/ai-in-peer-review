- Proposes AutoBDMC: leverages Bidirectional Monte Carlo/AIS to obtain stochastic lower and upper bounds on the log marginal likelihood for evaluating MCMC convergence.  
- Claims the gap between these bounds bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the exact posterior and the approximate-sample distribution, giving a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Evaluates convergence using synthetic data sampled from the joint model and presents empirical evidence that simulated performance predicts behavior on real datasets.  
- Demonstrates additional applications such as model selection and debugging.  
- Overall assessment: clearly written, well-motivated, practically valuable method with persuasive empirical results.  
- Section 3 is less accessible: transition into 3.1 is abrupt and introduces variables/equations without sufficient context.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, this should be stated explicitly as a paper contribution.  
- The term “AutoBDMC” is never formally defined; the paper should define it early (presumably as using AIS and reverse AIS to assess MCMC).  
- Minor technical typo: Line 149’s p(theta, z | y) likely should be p(theta, z, y).  
- Notational inconsistency: manuscript alternates between y and D to denote the data.  
- Supplemental WebPPL code: include the WebPPL version(s) known to work and integration instructions; ideally release as an npm-installable WebPPL package.  
- If feasible, provide a similarly packaged release for the Stan implementation.
