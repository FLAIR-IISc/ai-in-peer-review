- Introduces AutoBDMC, using Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood for assessing MCMC convergence.  
- Shows the gap between those bounds bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate samples, yielding a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Method can only be directly applied to synthetic data drawn from the joint, though authors provide empirical evidence that synthetic analyses predict real-data behavior.  
- Demonstrates utility of the approach for model selection and debugging.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and experimentally convincing.  
- Section 3 is difficult to follow; the transition into Section 3.1 is abrupt and lacks sufficient motivation for variables and equations.  
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper should explicitly state this, as it is central to the contribution.  
- AutoBDMC is never explicitly defined in the paper; readers need a clear statement of what the method entails (e.g., using AIS and rAIS to analyze MCMC).  
- Minor issues: possible typo (p(theta, z | y) vs p(theta, z, y)) and inconsistent use of notation for data (y vs D).  
- Supplementary WebPPL code needs supported-version information and integration instructions; recommend releasing as an npm-installable WebPPL package and, if possible, a similar Stan release.
