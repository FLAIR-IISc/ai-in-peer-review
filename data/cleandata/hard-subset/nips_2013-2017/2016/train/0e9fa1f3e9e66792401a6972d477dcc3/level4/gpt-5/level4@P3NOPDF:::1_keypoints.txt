- Proposal: use bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC-induced distribution and the target posterior to debug/evaluate MCMC in PPLs.  
- Strength: clear, pragmatic goal to provide a quantitative measure for when an MCMC implementation fails to target the intended posterior.  
- Strength: BDMC and JS divergence are natural, intuitive, and interpretable choices for this evaluation.  
- Strength: addresses a real need in probabilistic programming where subtle implementation or modeling bugs are hard to diagnose with standard diagnostics.  
- Relation to prior work: must situate relative to Geweke’s “Getting it right” and Cook et al.’s posterior-quantile tests—clarify which failure modes each detects and how JS-based checks complement distributional/quantile checks.  
- Notation/Section 3.1: q(·) is used before being defined—define it precisely (initialization, transition kernels, annealing path, and whether it is a pushforward or stationary distribution).  
- Derivation clarity: present assumptions explicitly, define all distributions/kernels before use, show how BDMC provides estimators/bounds and how these map to a JS estimate.  
- Algorithmic exposition: provide a self-contained algorithm box or checklist (inputs, outputs, recommended hyperparameters: schedule, T, number of forward/reverse runs).  
- Self-contained paper: move key definitions and justifications from the supplement into the main text so readers can implement the method without the supplement.  
- Practical guidance: give heuristics for choosing annealing schedules, number of intermediates, chain lengths, forward/reverse runs; discuss estimator variance, computational cost, and practical interpretation of JS magnitudes.  
- Scope and limitations: clarify applicability—whether exact prior sampling is required, sensitivity to dimensionality, poor schedules, multimodality, and transferability from prior-synthetic to real datasets.  
- Minor notation: consider collapsing z and θ into a single latent block to reduce clutter.  
- Minor fixes: correct line 165 parenthesis, define symbols at first use (q, p_T, K_t, β_t), and keep notation consistent between main text and supplement.  
- Illustration suggestion: include a small running example (e.g., a simple conjugate model with known ground truth) to anchor the derivation.  
- Overall assessment: promising and useful contribution, but needs clearer exposition (especially Sec. 3.1), a self-contained algorithmic description, and stronger positioning relative to prior work before acceptance.
