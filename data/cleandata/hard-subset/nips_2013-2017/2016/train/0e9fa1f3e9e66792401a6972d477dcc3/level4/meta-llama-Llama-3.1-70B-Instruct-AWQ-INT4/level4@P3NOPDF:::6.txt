This paper summarizes previously published results on bidirectional Monte Carlo and reports on its application using two probabilistic programming languages. However, upon reviewing the content, it appears that there are no original ideas presented in this paper, as it primarily describes the implementation of known techniques in WebPPL and Stan languages. The work seems to be more focused on demonstrating the applicability of existing methods rather than introducing novel concepts or contributions to the field.

Given the nature of the paper, it may be more suitable for a workshop focused on Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods, where the presentation of implementation details and applications of established techniques is more commonly accepted. In contrast, the NIPS conference tends to prioritize papers that introduce innovative ideas, algorithms, or theoretical results that significantly advance the state-of-the-art in the field.

While the authors' effort to implement and demonstrate bidirectional Monte Carlo in WebPPL and Stan is appreciated, the lack of originality and contribution to the field may make it less competitive for inclusion in the NIPS conference.
