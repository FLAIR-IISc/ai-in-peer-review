The paper "Measuring the reliability of MCMC inference with bidirectional Monte Carlo" applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate the accuracy of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) based posterior inference algorithms. The authors extend the BDMC technique to bound the symmetrized KL divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the concepts and techniques used. The authors provide a thorough background on BDMC, annealed importance sampling (AIS), and stochastic lower bounds on the log partition function ratio. They also discuss related work on diagnosing Markov chain convergence and automatically configuring parameters of MCMC algorithms.

The main contribution of the paper is the application of BDMC to two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan. The authors integrate their method, called Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD), into the tool chains of these languages and validate it on several models and datasets. They also propose a protocol for using BREAD to diagnose inference quality on real-world data.

The experiments are thorough and well-designed, and the results are clearly presented. The authors validate the use of simulated data as a proxy for real data and show that the fitted hyperparameter procedure yields a sample sufficiently representative of the true posterior. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of BREAD in evaluating the accuracy of posterior inference and provide insights into the tradeoffs between different model representations.

However, it is worth noting that the paper does not present any original ideas, and the techniques used are based on previously published results. The application of BDMC to WebPPL and Stan is a significant contribution, but it may not be substantial enough to warrant a paper at NIPS.
