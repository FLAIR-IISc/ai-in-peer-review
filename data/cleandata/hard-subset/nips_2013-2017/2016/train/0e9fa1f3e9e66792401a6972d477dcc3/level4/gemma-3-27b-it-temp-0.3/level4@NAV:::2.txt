This paper presents a valuable analysis of MCMC-based posterior inference methods through the lens of bidirectional Monte Carlo. The authors rigorously investigate lower bounds on the KL-divergence between the true posterior and its approximate counterpart, and demonstrate a practical implementation within established probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan. This work addresses a significant and actively researched problem in Bayesian statistics, and the application of bidirectional Monte Carlo offers a novel and interesting approach. I recommend acceptance of this manuscript.
