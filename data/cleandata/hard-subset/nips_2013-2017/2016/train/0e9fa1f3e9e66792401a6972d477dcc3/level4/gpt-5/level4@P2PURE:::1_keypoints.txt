- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jensen–Shannon divergence between an MCMC sampler’s induced distribution and the target to debug/evaluate MCMC in probabilistic programming languages.  
- The idea of using BDMC to diagnose non-convergence is compelling and potentially very useful given limitations of existing MCMC diagnostics.  
- Two closely related references (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2006/2012) also assess MCMC performance by running samplers on data drawn from the prior.  
- Section 3.1’s derivation is difficult to follow: q(·) is used before being defined and should be explicitly stated (appears to be the AIS marginal after T steps).  
- The main text should be understandable without the supplement; include a concise, end-to-end description of how to apply the method.
