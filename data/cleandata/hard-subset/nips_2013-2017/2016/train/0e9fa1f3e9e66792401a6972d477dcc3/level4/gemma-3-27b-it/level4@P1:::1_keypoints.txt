- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target, aimed at debugging/evaluating PPLs.
- Novel application: BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence for MCMC samplers is a clever and original contribution.
- Practical implementation: integrated BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, making the method accessible to practitioners.
- Theoretical strength: BDMC gives rigorous bounds (for simulated data) that improve on heuristic MCMC diagnostics.
- Demonstrated usefulness: used to compare model representations and to guide modeling decisions in WebPPL and Stan.
- Bug detection: BREAD successfully identified a bug in WebPPL, showing practical verification potential.
- Related work: connects to prior methods that assess samplers using prior-sampled data (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2012).
- Section 3.1 derivation is hard to follow; key quantities (e.g., q) are used before being defined.
- Main text lacks self-containment; needs a clear, step-by-step description of how to apply the method.
- Notation could be simplified (separating z and θ is unnecessary); minor typographical error (missing parenthesis at line 165).
- Real-world data protocol is heuristic (fit hyperparameters then simulate) and may not be reliable without further validation.
- Scalability concern: computational cost of BDMC may limit applicability to very large or complex models.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a hard problem, theoretically sound, practical implementations and experiments demonstrate utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear derivation, reliance on a heuristic for real data, and potential computational limitations.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with novel impact; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and discuss limitations.
