- Paper proposes selecting MCMC schemes by comparing tightness of Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds on the log marginal likelihood for equal compute effort.
- Core challenge: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound.
- Authors circumvent this by running diagnostics on posteriors from synthetic data, using heuristics to obtain (approximate) exact samples.
- Novel idea: using BMC stochastic lower bounds as a theoretically motivated MCMC convergence diagnostic, mainly for AIS and SMC samplers.
- Limitation: methodology is specialized to AIS/SMC and relies solely on marginal likelihood diagnostics.
- Major concern 1: method assumes prior knowledge of a representative hyperparameter η_real, typically from a previously run MCMC, which presumes that prior MCMC had converged and that the posterior is well summarized by one value.
- Major concern 2: assumes the posterior from synthetic data generated with η_real has similar convergence behavior to the real posterior, an unsupported and fragile assumption (especially under model misspecification).
- Major concern 3: assumes initializing from η_real lets the MCMC quickly produce an almost exact sample—possible but not guaranteed.
- Overall critique: the approach depends on the very convergence properties it intends to diagnose, undermining robustness.
- Empirical insufficiency: examples in Section 5.1 are simple and do not demonstrate robustness in complex scenarios.
- Recommendation: clarify how the method handles multimodality and insufficient summary statistics, and provide comparisons to standard diagnostics to show where it outperforms existing tools.
