- Proposal: Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with REverse Annealing) to measure reliability of MCMC-based inference.  
- Theory: Shows the BDMC forward–reverse AIS gap gives an expected upper bound on the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence to the true posterior.  
- Real-data protocol: Proposes fitting hyperparameters, simulating data from them, then applying BREAD as a practical evaluation heuristic.  
- Implementation: Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Experiments: Validates method across five Stan models and shows simulated-data behavior matches real-data behavior under the protocol.  
- Use cases: Demonstrates utility by comparing collapsed vs. uncollapsed matrix factorization representations.  
- Bug discovery: Uses BREAD to uncover a bug in the WebPPL implementation.  
- Strength — significance: Addresses a critical problem in assessing posterior-sample quality for black-box PPLs.  
- Strength — originality/quality: Novel theoretical insight connecting BDMC gap to Jeffreys divergence beyond prior AIS/BDMC work.  
- Strength — empirical validation: Thorough experiments and practical demonstrations reinforce usefulness.  
- Weakness — theoretical scope: Rigorous guarantees hold only for simulated data where exact posterior samples are available.  
- Weakness — real-data heuristic: The proposed protocol for real data is not foolproof, especially under severe model misspecification.  
- Weakness — algorithm applicability: Directly applicable to AIS/SMC-type algorithms, not standard Metropolis-Hastings or HMC/NUTS.  
- Novelty concern: Builds heavily on BDMC/AIS, so conceptual novelty is incremental.  
- Acceptance pro: Principled solution to an important problem with a novel theoretical result and practical implementations.  
- Acceptance con: Limited fundamental originality and restricted guarantees to synthetic settings.  
- Overall: Strengths (significance, theory, experiments) outweigh weaknesses; provides a valuable tool for evaluating inference.
