- Proposal: use the gap between BDMC stochastic lower and upper bounds (via two AIS sweeps) as a performance measure for MCMC transition operators.
- Implementation: authors provide BDMC implementations for Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality.
- Strength: conceptually simple, readily implementable, and likely high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Novelty: core idea is useful but has limited novelty and technical depth.
- Presentation: sections 3.1 and 3.2 feel rushed and reduce clarity of the technical contributions.
- Definition issues: “v” is imprecisely defined and labeling (“rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”) is confusing.
- Notation problems: the estimator p̂(y) is not defined and expectation/ratio notation (e.g., E[log R_rev]) is ambiguous.
- Mathematical error: equation (6) is nonsensical as written (KL uses identical distributions); a corrected form likely involves p(z, θ | y) q_back(v | z, θ, y).
- AutoBDMC undefined: unclear whether it means (a) using the bound gap to assess convergence and (b) using the fixed hyperparameter scheme in §3.2.
- Experiments: β_t schedule is not specified in the paper (code suggests βT=1 to β1=1/T); this should be clarified.
- Experimental reporting: “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” in figures is unclear about BDMC step counts; RAIS and (u)c(R)AIS acronyms are undefined; axis labels and ticks are too small.
- Sensitivity: paper lacks a systematic study of step-size (Δβ) influence on bounds and how to auto-adjust it.
- Originality gap: close relationship to Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) is not sufficiently acknowledged or discussed.
- Insight suggestion: authors should explore connections between Δβ, acceptance ratios in TT, and sensitivity of BDMC bounds.
- Significance: method is practically valuable for automatic posterior quality monitoring and debugging in PPLs.
- Quality: technical analysis appears sound but presentation issues weaken understanding; experiments are adequate but could be more systematic.
- Typos/citations: D_JS expressions missing closing parentheses; NUTS citation wrongly lists Homan instead of Hoffman.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical utility, implementations in Stan/WebPPL, and demonstrated bug-detection capability.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient related-work discussion, clarity problems, and inadequate sensitivity analysis.
