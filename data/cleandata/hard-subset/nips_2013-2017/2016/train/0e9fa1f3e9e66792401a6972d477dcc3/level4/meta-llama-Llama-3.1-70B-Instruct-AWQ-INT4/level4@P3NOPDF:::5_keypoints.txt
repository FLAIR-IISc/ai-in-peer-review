- Paper tackles evaluating MCMC reliability and comparing MCMC scheme efficiency using BMC stochastic bounds on log marginal likelihood.
- Proposal: choose the MCMC scheme that yields the tightest BMC stochastic bounds for equal computational effort.
- Core obstacle: BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound.
- Authors’ heuristic: run diagnostics on posteriors generated from synthetic data (using a representative hyperparameter η_real) where approximate exact samples are easier to obtain.
- Methodology is demonstrated on several examples.
- Approach is largely specific to AIS and SMC schemes and bases diagnostics solely on the marginal likelihood.
- The idea is valuable as an attempt at theoretically justified MCMC diagnostics, but the implementation has significant drawbacks.
- Drawback 1: Requires prior knowledge of a representative η_real, assuming the algorithm that found it has already converged and that a single value summarizes the posterior.
- Drawback 2: Assumes the synthetic-data posterior has similar MCMC convergence properties to the real posterior, an unjustified leap especially under model misspecification.
- Drawback 3: Assumes initializing at η_real lets the MCMC quickly produce an almost exact sample, which may not hold in practice.
- These assumptions make the diagnostic circular, tying its validity to the very convergence properties it aims to assess.
- Section 5.1’s simple examples do not convincingly show the method will generalize to more complex, realistic problems.
- Authors criticize existing diagnostics for multimodality and weak summaries but do not explain or demonstrate why their method overcomes these issues.
- Reviewer requests comparative simulations against standard diagnostics (e.g., those in Section 4) to show scenarios where the proposed method is more reliable.
- Overall: interesting concept but needs further development and stronger empirical/theoretical support to address key assumptions and limitations.
