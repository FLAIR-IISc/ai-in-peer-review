

This paper presents a summary of previously published results on bidirectional Monte Carlo methods, along with their implementation in WebPPL and Stan probabilistic programming languages. The core contribution lies in applying established techniques rather than introducing novel theoretical or methodological advances. The work primarily reiterates known approaches without significant innovation in algorithm design, analysis, or empirical evaluation. While the implementation details are clearly described, the paper fails to demonstrate how this application advances the state of the art in probabilistic inference or provides new insights beyond existing literature. The absence of original theoretical contributions, comparative analysis with alternative methods, or compelling empirical results that justify the specific choice of languages further diminishes the paper's novelty. Given the focus on reimplementation rather than innovation, this work appears better suited for a venue dedicated to practical applications or methodological case studies, such as an MCMC workshop or a probabilistic programming-focused workshop. NIPS prioritizes high-impact theoretical or empirical advances in machine learning; this submission does not meet that standard. The authors are encouraged to identify a more appropriate forum where the incremental nature of the contribution aligns with the venue's scope.
