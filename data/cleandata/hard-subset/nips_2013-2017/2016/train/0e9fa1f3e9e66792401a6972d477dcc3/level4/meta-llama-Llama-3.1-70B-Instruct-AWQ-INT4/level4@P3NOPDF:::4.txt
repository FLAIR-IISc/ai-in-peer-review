This paper presents a novel method, termed AutoBDMC, for assessing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) convergence. The method leverages the recently-developed Bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds for the log marginal likelihood, relying on annealed importance sampling. The authors demonstrate that the gap between these bounds serves as a bound on the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the distribution of approximate samples, providing a single scalar quantity to evaluate MCMC convergence behavior.

The paper is well-motivated and clearly written, making it easy to follow for the most part. The authors provide a compelling experimental evaluation, showcasing the method's effectiveness in assessing convergence on both synthetic and real-world data. Furthermore, they illustrate the method's utility in model selection and debugging, highlighting its potential for practical applications.

However, Section 3 is somewhat more challenging to follow than the rest of the paper. The transition into Section 3.1 is particularly abrupt, as it delves into variable and equation definitions without providing sufficient motivation. It appears that the divergence bound presented in Section 3.1 is a novel result, and this should be explicitly stated, as it seems to be a crucial contribution of the paper.

A clear definition of AutoBDMC is lacking throughout the paper. Based on the content, it appears that AutoBDMC refers to the approach of utilizing annealed importance sampling (AIS) and reverse AIS to analyze MCMC convergence. A concise statement to this effect would be beneficial in clarifying the method's core concept.

Some minor issues were also noted. On Line 149, it seems that p(theta, z | y) should be p(theta, z, y). Additionally, the paper exhibits notational inconsistencies, switching between y and D to denote data/observations, which may cause confusion at times.

Regarding the supplemental WebPPL code, it would be helpful to provide information on the specific version(s) of WebPPL that the code is compatible with, as well as instructions on how to integrate it into the WebPPL distribution. Alternatively, releasing the code as a WebPPL package that can be installed via npm would be more convenient. A similar release for the Stan version of the code would also be appreciated.

Overall, the paper presents a useful method for assessing MCMC convergence, and the experimental evaluation is reasonably compelling. With some revisions to address the mentioned issues, the paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
