- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to upper-bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and MCMC sample distribution via reverse annealing (BREAD).
- Achieves the bound by running annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains forward and reverse, leveraging BDMC log-marginal-likelihood estimation.
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, enabling practical use in popular probabilistic programming systems.
- Validates the method on several models and datasets, including consistency checks with real and simulated data.
- Addresses the important problem of quantitatively assessing MCMC posterior sample quality in black-box inference systems.
- Theoretical contribution is rigorous with a clear derivation of the Jeffreys divergence upper bound.
- Novel application of BDMC beyond marginal likelihood estimation to assess MCMC accuracy.
- Significant practical impact by giving practitioners a principled accuracy measure and revealing representation tradeoffs between WebPPL and Stan.
- Paper is generally clear and well-written, connecting theory to implementation.
- Implementation in two languages increases accessibility and practical relevance.
- Validation experiments strengthen credibility and demonstrate utility.
- Main weakness: BREAD (AIS-based) can be computationally expensive, raising scalability concerns.
- Reliance on simulated data and hyperparameter estimates for real-data application may limit validity if simulations are unrepresentative.
- Applicability is limited to algorithms expressible as importance sampling over extended state spaces (e.g., AIS, SMC), not all MCMC methods.
- Potential error source: using approximate posterior samples for hyperparameter fitting is not fully quantified.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with practical implementations; recommend acceptance despite computational and applicability limitations.
