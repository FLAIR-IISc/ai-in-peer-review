- Proposal: Use BDMC (two AIS sweeps) to evaluate MCMC transition operators by interpreting accumulated probabilities as stochastic lower/upper bounds on marginal likelihood.  
- Core contribution: Use the gap between BDMC bounds as a performance metric for MCMC operators, with implementations for Stan and WebPPL and an inference-quality protocol.  
- Strength: The BDMC-based evaluation framework is simple, implementable, and has high potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Concern: Novelty and technical depth are limited; much of the paper reiterates prior BDMC description, leaving little space for new technical exposition.  
- Missing citation/connection: The paper omits Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which performs essentially identical reverse/forward sweeps and links acceptance ratios to thermodynamic integration bounds.  
- Insight but not novel: The observation that the bound gap upper-bounds Jensen–Shannon divergence is interesting but not entirely new given Neal’s related analysis.  
- AutoBDMC undefined: The “auto” aspect is not explicitly defined; it likely involves using the gap to assess convergence and a fixed β schedule, but needs formalization.  
- Missed opportunity: The authors could develop an iterative Δβ–reduction schedule (refining annealing steps until bounds converge) but do not explore this experimentally.  
- Clarity issues: Ambiguous definition of vector v and inconsistent labeling ("rev"/"fwd" vs. "for"/"back") create confusion.  
- Equation problems: Equations (5–7) and (9–11) omit definition of the estimator êp(y); Equation (6) appears invalid and should reference the correct distributions.  
- Experimental issues: β_t scheduling is unclear (appendix implies step = 1/options.steps but this must be stated explicitly).  
- Figure/legend issues: Figure captions omit definitions for RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS, and NUTS/HMC step counting; axes/tick labels are too small.  
- Typos: Missing closing parentheses in D_JS expressions and "Homan" should be "Hoffman" in the NUTS citation.  
- Recommendation: If clarity, related-work citations, and experimental deficiencies are addressed in the camera-ready version, the paper is worthy of acceptance.
