- Paper proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) stochastic bounds on the log marginal likelihood to evaluate reliability and compare efficiency of MCMC schemes.
- Main idea: select the MCMC scheme that gives the tightest BMC upper/lower bounds for a given computational budget.
- BMC requires an exact sample from the target to produce a valid stochastic upper bound; authors introduce heuristics to bypass this requirement.
- Authors’ practical solution: run diagnostics on posteriors induced by synthetic data (generated from a chosen hyperparameter η_real) instead of the original data.
- Method tested on several examples, and framed as a theoretically motivated MCMC diagnostic specifically applicable to AIS and SMC samplers using marginal likelihood bounds.
- Major drawback: reliance on heuristics to produce exact-like samples undermines theoretical guarantees and reduces practical appeal.
- First primary concern: assumes prior knowledge of a representative hyperparameter η_real, implying (a) the algorithm used to find η_real has already converged and (b) the posterior can be summarized by one value—both unjustified.
- Second primary concern: assumes the synthetic-data posterior (from η_real) has convergence properties similar to the true posterior, an unsupported and potentially false claim especially under model misspecification.
- Third primary concern: assumes starting MCMC from η_real yields an almost exact sample quickly, which may not hold and ties the diagnostic’s validity to the very convergence it aims to assess.
- Examples in Section 5.1 are simple and do not convincingly demonstrate robustness of the approach for more complex or multimodal posteriors.
- Authors criticize existing diagnostics for multimodality and limited summaries but fail to explain or provide evidence why their method would outperform in such scenarios.
- Recommended improvements: provide careful justification of assumptions, more challenging experiments, and direct comparisons with standard diagnostic methods to show where the proposed method is more reliable.
