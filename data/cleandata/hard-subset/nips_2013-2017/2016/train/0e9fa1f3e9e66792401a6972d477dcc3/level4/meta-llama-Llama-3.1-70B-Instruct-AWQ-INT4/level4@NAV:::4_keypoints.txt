- Introduces AutoBDMC, which uses Bidirectional Monte Carlo with AIS/rAIS to produce stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood.
- Key theoretical insight: the gap between those bounds upper-bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and the approximate sample distribution, giving a scalar convergence metric.
- Method mainly applies to synthetic data drawn from the joint, with empirical evidence suggesting such analyses can predict behavior on real data.
- Demonstrates practical utility for model selection and debugging of MCMC workflows.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written overall, and the experiments are reasonably convincing.
- Section 3 (especially the transition to 3.1) is hard to follow; variables and equations are introduced without sufficient motivation.
- If the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper should explicitly claim and highlight that contribution.
- AutoBDMC is not clearly defined; it appears to mean using AIS and rAIS for MCMC convergence analysis but should be stated explicitly.
- Minor typo: Line 149’s p(theta, z | y) likely should be p(theta, z, y).
- Notation is inconsistent at times, switching between y and D for data/observations.
- Supplemental WebPPL code lacks version and integration instructions for reproducibility.
- Recommend releasing code as a WebPPL package or similar distribution for the Stan implementation.
