- Introduces BREAD, an extension of BDMC that runs AIS forward and reverse to assess MCMC sample quality.
- Shows the gap between stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood gives an expected upper bound on the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence.
- Builds on Grosse et al. (2015) BDMC framework and repurposes it for sample-quality assessment.
- Provides a clear derivation and validates the tightness of the divergence bound on toy examples.
- Proposes a practical protocol for applying BREAD to real-world data by fitting hyperparameters and simulating data.
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical tooling and applicability.
- Thorough experimental validation, including consistency checks between real and simulated data and robustness analyses.
- Demonstrates utility by comparing model representations (collapsed vs uncollapsed) and uncovering a WebPPL bug.
- Main limitation: high computational overhead from running many forward and reverse AIS chains.
- Theoretical guarantees hold only for simulated-data setting; the real-data protocol is a pragmatic heuristic without formal guarantees.
- Suggestion: need more discussion of computational cost scaling and potential failure modes under severe model misspecification.
- Suggestion: clarify handling of multiple AIS chains—whether bounds extend to mixtures/averages or how to aggregate results.
