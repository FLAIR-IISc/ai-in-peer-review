- Introduces BREAD, a method using bidirectional Monte Carlo to bound the Jeffreys divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior for AIS/SMC-style algorithms.
- Extends BDMC to provide an expected upper bound on Jeffreys divergence for MCMC viewed as importance sampling over an extended state space.
- Proposes a protocol for applying the method to real-world data by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters.
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan for practical use.

- Theoretical foundation is sound with a clear derivation showing forward–reverse AIS gap bounds Jeffreys divergence in expectation.
- Validation experiments on toy distributions show the bound is reasonably tight.
- The real-data protocol addresses BDMC’s need for exact posterior samples and is well-justified.
- Novel extension of BDMC to bounding posterior divergence (Jeffreys), not just marginal likelihoods.
- Addresses a significant problem—rigorous assessment of MCMC sample quality—and offers practical utility for probabilistic programming users.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and includes thorough background, transparent experiments, and tool integrations.

- Method only applies to algorithms representable as AIS/SMC, excluding many common MCMC methods like standard Metropolis–Hastings.
- Real-world protocol relies on simulated data from fitted hyperparameters accurately reflecting real data; validation is limited to five models and lacks guidance for mismatch cases.
- Requires running both forward and reverse AIS, roughly doubling computational cost; scalability to large models/datasets is not discussed.
- Comparison to related work (Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka 2016) is superficial and doesn’t fully explore theoretical implications of using true posterior initialization.

- Overall: technically strong, meaningful contribution with practical integrations, but limited by algorithmic scope and needing broader validation of the real-data protocol.
- Recommendation: Accept.

- For acceptance: rigorous Jeffreys divergence bounds, well-motivated theoretical extension of BDMC, practical WebPPL/Stan integration, useful insights on model representation, and clear presentation.
- For rejection: restricted to AIS/SMC algorithms, real-data protocol needs more diverse validation, and increased computational cost concerns.
