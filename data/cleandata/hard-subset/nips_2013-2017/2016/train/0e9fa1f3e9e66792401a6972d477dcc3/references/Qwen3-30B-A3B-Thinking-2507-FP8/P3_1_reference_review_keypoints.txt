- Introduces BREAD, a method to bound the Jeffreys divergence between MCMC (AIS/SMC) approximate samples and the true posterior.  
- Builds on BDMC/BDMC-like bidirectional Monte Carlo and AIS theoretical foundations.  
- Main theoretical result: the gap between forward and reverse AIS estimates upper bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Proposes a protocol for applying BREAD to real data by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Extends WebPPL and Stan to implement BREAD, demonstrating practical accessibility.  
- Validates that inference behavior on simulated data matches real data across several model types.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by uncovering a bug in WebPPL.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear background, derivations, and effective figures.  
- Theoretical guarantees are limited to simulated data; real-data protocol relies on an unproven assumption about fitted hyperparameter representativeness.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of how approximation error in reverse-chain initialization affects the Jeffreys divergence bound.  
- Insufficient comparison to existing MCMC diagnostics (e.g., Gelman-Rubin, ESS) in cost, reliability, and interpretability.  
- Scalability concerns: running both forward and reverse AIS chains may be computationally expensive for large problems; scaling not analyzed.  
- No specific experiments or analysis on multimodal posteriors, despite claiming distributional generality.  
- Technical derivation in Section 3.1 is sound and the connection to BDMC is novel and valuable.  
- Practical implementation concerns: protocol assumptions may fail for other model classes or dataset sizes; conditions for success should be discussed.  
- Comparison to CTM16 is noted but superficial; implications of using a reference distribution vs. true posterior need deeper exploration.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and uses appropriate visualizations for heavy-tailed AIS estimates.  
- Matrix factorization case shows actionable insights and representation-dependent behavior across WebPPL and Stan.  
- Debugging case is a strong practical demonstration but lacks detail on the specific bug and fix.  
- Overall assessment: solid and valuable contribution with strong theory and useful implementations; recommend acceptance.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze limitations of the real-data protocol, compare to other diagnostics, study multimodal behavior, evaluate computational cost/scalability, and clarify relation to CTM16.
