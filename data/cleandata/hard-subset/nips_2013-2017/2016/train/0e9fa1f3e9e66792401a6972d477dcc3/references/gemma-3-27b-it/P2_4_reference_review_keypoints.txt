- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the Jeffreys divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior.
- Key theoretical result: BDMC provides an upper bound in expectation on the Jeffreys divergence.
- Validated on toy problems and packaged as BREAD with a protocol for applying to real data.
- Integrated BREAD into WebPPL and Stan for practical use.
- Empirical demonstration includes model-choice guidance (matrix factorization) and discovered a WebPPL bug.
- Novel and potentially impactful extension of BDMC to quantify posterior accuracy.
- Solid theoretical guarantees for the simulated-data setting where exact posterior samples are available.
- Practical implementation into popular probabilistic programming languages increases accessibility.
- Addresses an important need for reliable assessment of MCMC/approximate inference quality.
- Applicability is limited to algorithms expressible as importance sampling on an extended state space (e.g., AIS, SMC).
- Strong guarantees rely on simulated data; the real-data protocol introduces approximations not fully accounted for.
- Computational cost of running BDMC/reverse chains can be high and is not thoroughly discussed.
- Jeffreys divergence may be unintuitive for practitioners and could be better connected to interpretable metrics (e.g., parameter bias).
- Empirical evaluation is limited (more models/datasets would strengthen conclusions).
- Paper is technically sound and well-written, though some background sections are dense.
- Original contribution with significant potential impact on probabilistic inference reliability.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve background clarity and discuss computational costs.
