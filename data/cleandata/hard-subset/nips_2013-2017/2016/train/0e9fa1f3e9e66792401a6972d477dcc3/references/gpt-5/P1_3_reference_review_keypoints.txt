- Main theoretical contribution: shows the expected gap between reverse and forward AIS log normalizing constant estimates upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posterior.  
- Protocol (BREAD): propose simulating data from fitted hyperparameters and running bidirectional AIS/SMC, with reverse chains initialized from brief MCMC on hyperparameters.  
- Implementation: integrated BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and evaluated across several models, revealing practical issues (e.g., a WebPPL bug).  
- Relation to prior work: builds on BDMC and reverse-AIS/Jarzynski arguments and is closely related to contemporary subjective divergence work.  
- Theory quality: derivations are sound and correctly tie AIS bias to KL terms.  
- Scope limitation: the bound holds only in expectation, so single-run gaps do not guarantee divergence bounds.  
- Practical-protocol limitation: fitted-hyperparameter procedure for real data is heuristic and removes rigorous guarantees.  
- Empirical evidence: experiments cover diverse models and PPLs, but tightness is demonstrated rigorously only on toy problems; larger models lack ground-truth divergence.  
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written; extended-state derivations are clear but some assumptions and practical settings should be stated explicitly.  
- Suggested clarity additions: specify reversibility/ergodicity assumptions, temperature schedules, kernel settings, and guidance on number of temperatures.  
- Originality: novel repurposing of BDMC/AIS sandwiching to estimate posterior sample quality via Jeffreys divergence.  
- Significance: fills a gap in quantitative assessment of approximate posterior samples, useful for PPL users and algorithm designers.  
- Strengths: clear theoretical link, practical protocol and integration, empirical validation, and demonstrated debugging utility.  
- Weaknesses: expectation-only bound, limited applicability to AIS/SMC samplers, and heuristic real-data protocol reduce rigor.  
- Suggestions: provide guidance on estimating E[gap] with uncertainty, analyze sensitivity to misspecified reference distributions, and correlate bound with downstream metrics (predictive likelihood, ESS/R-hat).  
- Suggestions (variance reduction): discuss strategies to tighten bounds (more temperatures, schedule design, symmetrized AIS, Rao-Blackwellization, combining runs with tail bounds).  
- Applicability concern: clarify when AIS/SMC framing is valid in Stan, and how adaptation (e.g., NUTS) interacts with invariance.  
- Reporting suggestion: report confidence intervals on E[gap] and consider concentration bounds to make expectation guarantees actionable.  
- Pros for acceptance: solid theory, practical tool likely to be adopted, relevant experiments, advances beyond BDMC.  
- Cons for acceptance: reliance on simulated data for guarantees, expectation-only bound, and limited quantitative tightness on complex models.  
- Recommendation: lean accept.
