- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound Jeffreys divergence and quantify MCMC posterior reliability for importance-sampling framed algorithms (AIS, SMC).  
- Derives a theoretical upper bound (BREAD) using reverse AIS and stochastic bounds.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, validating on toy examples and showing consistency between real and simulated data.  
- Applies BREAD to model-representation choices in matrix factorization and uncovers a WebPPL bug.  
- Novel application of BDMC that provides a more rigorous MCMC diagnostic than heuristic methods.  
- Strong theoretical foundation linking Jeffreys divergence, stochastic bounds, and reverse AIS.  
- Practical implementation in widely used probabilistic programming languages increases accessibility and impact.  
- Validation experiments are useful but limited in scope (only five models tested).  
- Method is restricted to algorithms amenable to importance-sampling framing (AIS/SMC), limiting broad applicability.  
- Computationally expensive; the paper does not adequately address scalability to complex models and datasets.  
- Real-world protocol depends on accurate hyperparameter estimation and representativeness of simulated data, creating potential errors.  
- Lacks a detailed comparison to existing diagnostics like R-hat, effective sample size, and traceplots.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, original, and potentially significant for improving trust in probabilistic inference.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically rigorous, practical implementations, demonstrable benefits and debugging utility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited applicability, high computational cost, and dependence on hyperparameter estimation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with caveats—address scalability concerns, expand empirical comparisons, and more explicitly discuss broader implications beyond AIS/SMC.
