- Introduces BREAD, extending BDMC to rigorously bound the Jeffreys divergence between approximate MCMC posterior samples and the true posterior using the forward–reverse AIS gap.  
- Targets MCMC algorithms that can be seen as importance sampling on an extended state space (e.g., AIS, SMC).  
- Theoretical result: the gap between forward and reverse AIS estimates upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation, with clear mathematical derivation.  
- Validated on toy problems where true divergence and bound can be computed exactly (Appendix B).  
- Empirical validation across five Stan models shows simulated-data behavior is consistent with real data, supporting the proposed protocol for real-world use.  
- Paper is well-written and organized; background, derivations, figures, and reproducibility details are clear and accessible.  
- Novelty: repurposes BDMC from marginal likelihood estimation to measuring posterior-sample reliability and distinguishes itself from related work by initializing the reverse chain from the true posterior.  
- Significance: provides rigorous error bounds where typical diagnostics are heuristic, and is practically useful for probabilistic programming systems (WebPPL, Stan).  
- Main weakness: requires exact posterior samples (only available for simulated data) and depends on the assumption that simulated datasets from fitted hyperparameters resemble real datasets, which may fail under misspecification.  
- Additional practical drawback: computational cost of running both forward and reverse AIS can be prohibitive for very large models.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — technically sound, empirically validated, novel, and practically impactful; benefits outweigh limitations.
