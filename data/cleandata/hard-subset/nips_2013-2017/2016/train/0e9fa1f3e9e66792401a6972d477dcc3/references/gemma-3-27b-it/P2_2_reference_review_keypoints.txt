- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors via a method called BREAD.
- Proposes a protocol for applying BREAD to real data by simulating datasets from estimated hyperparameters.
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility.
- Demonstrates practical utility on matrix factorization, guiding representation choices and uncovering a WebPPL bug.
- Theoretical connection to bounding Jeffreys divergence is clearly presented and novel.
- Provides rigorous guarantees on inference quality for simulated-data scenarios.
- Practical implementation in popular probabilistic programming languages enhances impact.
- Reveals tradeoffs between collapsed and uncollapsed model representations in practice.
- Core theoretical guarantee requires access to exact posterior samples, limiting direct application to real-world data.
- The simulation-based workaround depends on quality of hyperparameter estimation and representativeness of simulated data.
- BDMC/BREAD is computationally intensive, potentially limiting adoption versus traditional diagnostics.
- Method directly applies to AIS/SMC-style algorithms; applicability to general MCMC methods is not fully established.
- Empirical validation of the hyperparameter estimation/simulation protocol is limited and needs broader evaluation.
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, but sometimes mathematically dense for non-experts.
- Contribution is original and significant: a reliable metric for posterior accuracy could improve probabilistic programming trustworthiness.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions (expand discussion of computational cost, strengthen protocol validation, clarify scope).
