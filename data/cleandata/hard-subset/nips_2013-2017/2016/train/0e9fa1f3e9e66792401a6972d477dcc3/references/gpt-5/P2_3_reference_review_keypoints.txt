- Extends BDMC to upper-bound, in expectation, the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and AIS/SMC-derived approximate samples (method called BREAD).
- Key idea: expected gap between forward AIS stochastic lower bound and reverse AIS stochastic upper bound yields E[DKL(q||p)+DKL(p||q)].
- Implemented BREAD and protocol in WebPPL and Stan, and applied to multiple models.
- Proposes a practical real-data diagnosis: fit hyperparameters on real data, simulate data from them, and evaluate inference on simulated data.
- Empirical validation shows forward AIS behavior matches between real and simulated datasets across five models.
- Short MCMC runs to initialize reverse chains produce reverse bounds stable across many steps.
- Case study (matrix factorization) provided actionable guidance (collapsed vs. uncollapsed) and uncovered a WebPPL bug.
- Theoretical derivation is clear, correct, and a clean extension of BDMC’s log-ML sandwiching using extended-state AIS view.
- Guarantees are explicit but limited: bound holds in expectation, requires exact posterior sample (thus simulated data), and applies only to AIS/SMC-like algorithms.
- Empirics are sensible but limited: tightness vs. true Jeffreys shown only on toy distributions; more quantitative analyses would strengthen claims.
- Paper is well written with clear notation and sufficient background for reproduction by experts; minor typos and reference formatting issues noted.
- Conceptually novel: connects bidirectional log-ML gap to expected upper bound on Jeffreys divergence and provides a practical protocol and PPL integrations.
- Significant for researchers/practitioners evaluating samplers and PPLs, though scope is reduced by AIS/SMC restriction and expectation-only bounds.
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical link to Jeffreys divergence, validated protocol bridging simulated and real data, and concrete implementations yielding real debugging value.
- Weaknesses: only expected bounds (no per-run high-probability guarantees); dependence on exact posterior samples limits real-data guarantees.
- Suggestion: provide procedures for empirical confidence intervals (e.g., multiple runs) to quantify uncertainty around E[B̂].
- Suggestion: include stress tests for prior/hyperparameter misspecification to assess protocol robustness.
- Suggestion: perform ablations on annealing schedule, intermediate distributions, and transition kernels with guidance for tuning under compute budgets.
- Suggestion: discuss prospects for extending diagnosis to standard MCMC (e.g., coupling to AIS or bridging techniques).
- Reporting suggestion: add quantitative metrics (wall-clock vs. bound tightness, run variability) and release code/recipes.
- Questions for authors: how to form empirical confidence intervals for the Jeffreys bound using multiple runs?
- Questions for authors: how sensitive are conclusions to the annealing schedule and were adaptive schedules tested?
- Questions for authors: in Stan, which transition kernels were used at intermediate temperatures and how was adaptivity handled?
- Overall assessment: solid, technically sound, and impactful extension of BDMC with practical value for PPLs; recommendation — accept.
