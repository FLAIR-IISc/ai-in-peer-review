- Introduces BREAD, extending BDMC to bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate and true posteriors.  
- Theoretical result: BDMC yields an estimator that upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Provides a method to compute exact Jeffreys divergence and its upper bound on small toy examples.  
- Proposes a protocol for evaluating inference on real data by simulating from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, enabling practical use in probabilistic programming.  
- Empirically validates BREAD across multiple models, including matrix factorization.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by using BREAD to guide representation choices and detect a WebPPL bug.  
- Positions the work relative to prior convergence diagnostics and inference-quality methods.  
- Strength: rigorous, clear theoretical derivations building on BDMC.  
- Strength: immediate practical utility via integration into two major PPLs.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments and a real-world validation protocol.  
- Strength: clear differentiation from Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (2016) via use of the true posterior in reverse chains.  
- Strength: provides actionable scientific insights (e.g., representation differences between WebPPL and Stan).  
- Weakness: only applies to algorithms representable as importance sampling on extended state spaces (e.g., AIS/SMC), excluding many MCMC methods.  
- Weakness: computationally intensive to run forward and reverse AIS chains; scalability concerns for large/high‑dimensional problems.  
- Weakness: real-data protocol may fail for models with very broad priors; limited guidance on diagnosing failures.  
- Weakness: lacks systematic empirical comparison to other inference-reliability methods (e.g., subjective divergence).  
- Weakness: limited discussion and examples of failure cases or when the bound is loose.  
- Overall: high-quality, significant, technically sound contribution with immediate practical applicability despite scope and cost limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly compare theoretical guarantees to Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (2016).  
- Suggested improvements: discuss computational cost relative to standard MCMC for larger models.  
- Suggested improvements: include a case study demonstrating a loose bound to illustrate limitations.  
- Suggested improvements: analyze how the number of MCMC steps affects reverse AIS estimate accuracy.
