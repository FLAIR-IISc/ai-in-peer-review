- Introduces BREAD, an extension of BDMC that provides rigorous bounds on the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors.  
- Theoretical derivation shows the gap between forward and reverse AIS estimates upper bounds the expected Jeffreys divergence (Equation 12).  
- Validated on toy distributions with exact values and on real models in WebPPL and Stan; includes a case study where BREAD found a WebPPL bug.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains background, method, and experiments with effective figures.  
- Original contribution: extends BDMC from marginal likelihood bounds to a more informative Jeffreys divergence bound and presents a practical protocol for real data.  
- Significant practical impact: integration into WebPPL and Stan and potential to improve reliability of probabilistic programming inference.  
- Scope limitation: method applies directly only to importance-sampling-over-extended-state-space algorithms (AIS, SMC), not all MCMC methods.  
- Lacks detailed implementation specifics (e.g., number of AIS steps, exact MCMC kernels) needed for reproducibility.  
- Insufficient empirical comparison against alternative inference diagnostics (e.g., Gelman–Rubin, effective sample size).  
- The real-world data protocol depends on approximate posterior initialization for reverse chains; more theoretical justification would strengthen the claim.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and carefully presented, including appendices with exact-computation checks.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and significant contribution with high quality and clarity.  
- Recommendation: Accept — valuable addition to the conference proceedings.
