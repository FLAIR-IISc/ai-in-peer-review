- Overall: Valuable extension of BDMC to assess MCMC accuracy; rigorous but computationally intensive; recommend accept with minor revisions.
- Contribution: Extends BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence for MCMC methods like AIS/SMC, offering a provable error bound.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on BDMC and situates well among MCMC diagnostics and automatic inference evaluation; connects to parametric bootstrap.
- Theoretical strength: Mathematically sound derivation providing an upper bound on Jeffreys divergence (BREAD).
- Novelty: Creative application of BDMC beyond log-marginal likelihood estimation to posterior accuracy assessment.
- Implementation: Practical integration into WebPPL and Stan increases accessibility and impact.
- Real-data protocol: Proposes a protocol to apply BREAD to real datasets to mitigate simulated-data limitations.
- Practical utility: Demonstrated ability to find a bug in WebPPL, showing debugging potential.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and easy to follow despite technical complexity.
- Major weakness — cost: BDMC/BREAD is computationally expensive and scales poorly to complex models and large datasets.
- Major weakness — simulated-data reliance: Fundamental guarantees require exact posterior samples (typically only for simulated data); real-data protocol’s effectiveness is underexplored.
- Empirical weakness: Limited number and diversity of models/datasets in experiments; needs broader validation.
- Metric concern: Jeffreys divergence, while theoretically appealing, may be unintuitive for practitioners and alternatives are not discussed.
- Tone concern: Claims of being "rigorous, general, and automatic" are somewhat overstated given cost and data limitations.
- Acceptance arguments: Theoretically sound, accessible implementation, novel BDMC use, practical bug-finding, clear exposition.
- Rejection arguments: High computational cost, reliance on simulated data, limited empirical evaluation, and possibly overstated claims.
- Questions for authors: Clarify BREAD’s computational complexity and optimizations; sensitivity to hyperparameter priors; limitations/alternatives to Jeffreys divergence; more runtime and bound-tightness results; potential broader applications.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, simulated-data reliance, and limited empirical evaluation.
