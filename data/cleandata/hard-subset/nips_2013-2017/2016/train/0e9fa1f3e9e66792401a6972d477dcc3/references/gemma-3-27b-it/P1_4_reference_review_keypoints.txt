- Overall assessment: Novel, theoretically sound extension of BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence for MCMC, recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Main contribution: Uses BDMC to provide quantitative bounds on the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors.  
- Theoretical soundness: Derivation of the upper bound on Jeffreys divergence is rigorous and well-motivated.  
- Practical implementation: BREAD integrated into WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility and demonstrating utility.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments (including simulated vs. real-data checks and matrix factorization) convincingly support BREAD’s effectiveness.  
- Diagnostic potential: Method offers a concrete tool for evaluating inference quality and guiding modeling decisions.  
- Bug detection: BREAD discovered a bug in WebPPL, showing usefulness for probabilistic programming validation.  
- Limited applicability: Method applies only to algorithms interpretable as importance sampling on extended state spaces (e.g., AIS, SMC), excluding many MCMC methods.  
- Simulated-data requirement: Requires exact posterior samples for tight guarantees, limiting direct application to simulated settings; real-data protocol depends on good hyperparameter estimation.  
- Computational cost: BDMC/BREAD can be expensive for complex models; scalability and runtime trade-offs need fuller discussion.  
- Clarity concerns: Some sections use dense notation and abrupt transitions; more intuitive explanations would improve readability.  
- Interpretation of bounds: Paper should better explain what constitutes practically meaningful or acceptable Jeffreys divergence bounds.  
- Recommendations — limitations: Authors should more explicitly discuss computational and scalability limitations.  
- Recommendations — interpretability: Provide guidance on practical interpretation of divergence bounds and acceptable thresholds.  
- Recommendations — scalability: Include an analysis or discussion of scalability with model complexity and data size.  
- Recommendations — real-world use: Elaborate strategies to mitigate reliance on exact posteriors for applying BREAD to real-world datasets.  
- Recommendations — presentation: Streamline mathematical notation and add intuitive explanations for key concepts.
