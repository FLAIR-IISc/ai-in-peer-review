- Proposes BREAD, extending BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior in expectation.  
- Applies to AIS and SMC by viewing them as importance sampling on an extended state space.  
- Provides a rigorous derivation showing the gap between forward and reverse AIS estimates upper bounds the Jeffreys divergence.  
- Introduces a validation protocol for real-world data: fit hyperparameters, simulate data, and compare inference consistency.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical utility for evaluation and debugging.  
- Empirically validates the method on toy and real examples and uses it to find a bug in WebPPL.  
- Thorough related-work discussion, distinguishing this work from prior BDMC and reverse-chain approaches.  
- Limitation: requires exact posterior samples (only available for simulated data), restricting direct applicability to real datasets.  
- Scalability concern: experiments are on relatively small models and computational cost of forward+reverse chains may be prohibitive for large problems.  
- Limited analysis of bound tightness across diverse models or conditions; more systematic evaluation is needed.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel theoretical contribution with practical implementation and clear utility; recommended for acceptance despite noted limitations.
