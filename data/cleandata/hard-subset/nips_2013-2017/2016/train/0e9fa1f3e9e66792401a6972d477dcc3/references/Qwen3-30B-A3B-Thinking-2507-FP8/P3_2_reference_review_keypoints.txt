- Paper introduces BREAD: extends BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence between MCMC approximate posteriors and the true posterior.  
- Shows the gap between forward and reverse AIS estimates upper bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Proposes a protocol for applying BREAD to real-world data by simulating from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, enabling practical use and debugging.  
- Theoretical derivation linking BDMC and Jeffreys divergence is sound and novel.  
- Practical implementation is valuable, demonstrated by finding a WebPPL sampling bug.  
- Experiments on simulated data validate that simulated-data behavior matches real-data inference for tested models.  
- Clear writing, well-structured background, informative figures, and detailed derivations.  
- Core bound applies rigorously only to simulated data; real-data application is heuristic without strong theory.  
- No comparison to standard MCMC diagnostics (Gelman–Rubin, ESS, Geweke), leaving unclear practical advantage.  
- Choice of Jeffreys divergence is not convincingly justified over other divergences or metrics.  
- Computational cost and scalability of running both forward and reverse AIS chains are not discussed.  
- Protocol’s theoretical guarantees for real-world use are weak; impact of fitted hyperparameter quality is unaddressed.  
- Comparison to related BDMC work is appropriate but broader MCMC diagnostics literature engagement is limited.  
- Suggested improvements: compare BREAD to standard diagnostics, justify Jeffreys divergence, analyze computational cost, strengthen theoretical support for real-data protocol, and clarify limitations.  
- Overall: technically sound, practical contributions, but limited by scope and lacking comparisons; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Key revisions requested: add diagnostics comparison, better justify Jeffreys divergence, address runtime/scalability, and provide stronger theory for real-data protocol.
