Overall assessment: strong, above-average paper extending BDMC to assess MCMC (AIS/SMC) accuracy with recommendation for acceptance subject to minor revisions.

Core contribution: extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo to provide an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence between AIS/SMC approximate posteriors and the true posterior.

Practical implementation: integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility and practical impact.

Real-data challenge: method requires simulated data from true hyperparameters; authors propose a heuristic protocol to apply BREAD to real datasets.

Theoretical rigor: clear derivation and sound mathematical justification for the Jeffreys divergence bound built on BDMC.

Diagnostic utility: offers a quantitative diagnostic for inference reliability that goes beyond qualitative convergence checks.

Case study value: demonstrated usefulness in guiding model representation choices (matrix factorization) and diagnosing inference issues.

Bug discovery: BREAD uncovered a bug in WebPPL, showing value as a testing tool for PPLs.

Related work: comprehensive literature review, including links to MCMC diagnostics, Stein-based measures, and historical context like Jarzynski’s work.

Main weakness — simulated-data dependence: requirement to know or estimate hyperparameters can introduce bias and limits applicability to real-world problems.

Main weakness — computational cost: BDMC can be expensive and scalability to high-dimensional models is not fully addressed.

Main weakness — heuristic real-data protocol: protocol is useful but acknowledged as heuristic and needs more robustness checks.

Main weakness — limited experiments: experimental validation is on a small set of models; broader evaluation would strengthen claims.

Presentation issues: some figures (e.g., Fig. 2) need clearer labeling and presentation.

Suggested revisions — scalability: add discussion of computational complexity and strategies (approximations, parallelization) to scale BREAD.

Suggested revisions — validate real-data protocol: expand experiments testing poor hyperparameter estimates, model misspecification, and different MCMC step counts.

Suggested revisions — clarify assumptions: explicitly state assumptions behind the real-data protocol (e.g., closeness of estimated hyperparameters).

Suggested revisions — improve figures and discuss alternatives: enhance figure clarity and briefly compare BREAD to other MCMC assessment methods.

Acceptance arguments — pro: rigorous quantitative measure, practical diagnostic tool, theoretical novelty, and PPL integrations justify acceptance.

Acceptance arguments — con: reliance on simulated data, heuristic real-data protocol, and computational burden warrant further validation.
