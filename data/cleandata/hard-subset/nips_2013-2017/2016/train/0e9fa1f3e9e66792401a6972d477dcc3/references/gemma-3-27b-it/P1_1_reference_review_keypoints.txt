- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC-based inference quality (AIS/SMC viewed as importance sampling on extended state spaces).  
- Provides an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior.  
- Demonstrates technique on toy examples and measures tightness of the bound by computing the true Jeffreys divergence.  
- Proposes a protocol (hyperparameter inference → data simulation → consistency checks) to apply the method to real-world data.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, showing practical utility and uncovering a real bug.  
- Positions work relative to BDMC (Grosse et al. 2015) and related MCMC diagnostics and tuning literature.  
- Clarifies differences from Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (2016) regarding reverse-annealing reference distributions.  
- Highlights broader applicability by connecting AIS and SMC frameworks.  
- Novel contribution: directly assesses sample quality rather than only marginal likelihoods.  
- Provides a rigorous, quantifiable guarantee (upper bound) for simulated-data inference accuracy.  
- Practical protocol for real data addresses the challenge of lacking exact posterior samples.  
- Well-written and clearly organized with solid mathematical derivations.  
- Applicability limited to algorithms expressible as importance sampling over extended state spaces (AIS, SMC).  
- Computationally expensive for complex models; practical costs not fully addressed.  
- Real-data protocol sensitive to hyperparameter estimation; robustness to misspecification not thoroughly explored.  
- Relies on strong assumption that fitted hyperparameters reflect true data-generating process.  
- Focuses on Jeffreys divergence without discussing implications or alternatives.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, rigorous, addresses key inference assessment problem, practical implementations and insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited algorithmic applicability, high computational cost, real-data protocol assumptions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Suggested revisions: discuss computational cost and possible reductions, analyze sensitivity to hyperparameter fitting, consider alternative divergence measures, and note limits for complex/non-identifiable models.
