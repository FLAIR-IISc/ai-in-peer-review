- Novel contribution: extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the Jeffreys divergence for MCMC-like inference (AIS/SMC).  
- Practical impact: implements the method ("BREAD") in WebPPL and Stan, making it usable in probabilistic programming systems.  
- Theoretical soundness: derivation of the divergence bound is solid and grounded in BDMC/AIS theory.  
- Addresses an important problem: provides a more rigorous, quantitative assessment of MCMC accuracy than standard diagnostics.  
- BREAD protocol: proposes a protocol to apply BDMC to real-world data via hyperparameter fitting and simulated data generation.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on Neal’s AIS analysis and acknowledges related independent work by Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (CTM16).  
- Empirical results: experiments on toy models and a debugging case study are encouraging but limited in scope.  
- Limited applicability: method only applies to inference algorithms expressible as importance sampling, excluding many standard MCMC algorithms.  
- Computational cost: BDMC (especially reverse AIS) can be expensive, potentially limiting use on complex models or large datasets.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: real-data protocol depends on accurate hyperparameter estimation and sensitivity is not fully explored.  
- Dependence on exact posterior sample: reverse chain requires an exact posterior sample; using approximate initialization introduces another approximation that needs analysis.  
- Clarity and presentation: paper is well-written, well-organized, and uses clear notation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, given novelty, soundness, and practical utility.  
- Suggested revisions: expand discussion of computational trade-offs and mitigation (e.g., approximations/parallelization).  
- Suggested revisions: analyze sensitivity to hyperparameter misspecification and report results across varied settings.  
- Suggested revisions: explore alternative reverse-chain initialization strategies when exact samples are unavailable.  
- Suggested revisions: broaden empirical validation across more models, datasets, and MCMC algorithms.  
- Suggested revisions: provide theoretical analysis of the divergence bound when the reverse chain is initialized with an approximate posterior sample.
