The paper proposes extending bidirectional Monte Carlo to evaluate MCMC posterior inference by running AIS chains forward and reverse to upper-bound the symmetrized KL in expectation.

Contributions: simple, easy-to-implement method for evaluating MCMC transition operators and a protocol for posterior inference quality using BDMC; applicable to AIS/SMC and integrated into WebPPL and Stan with validation on models/datasets.

Strength: simplicity, ease of implementation, and high potential impact for probabilistic programming systems.

Weakness: modest technical novelty—Jensen-Shannon divergence bound observation is not clearly novel.

Related work omission: lacks discussion of closely related Tempered Transitions (Radford Neal, 1996) and other prior work.

Main methodological concern: choice of β schedule affects bounds and comparisons but is under-discussed and not systematically treated.

AutoBDMC unclear: the meaning and mechanism of "auto" are not clearly defined and experiments do not explore it.

Clarity problems: sections 3.1–3.2 have slipping writing quality causing ambiguities.

Notation issues: vector v is loosely defined and confusing given two AIS sweeps; inconsistent use of "rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back".

Specific corrections requested: define the estimator inside expectations in eqs. 5–7 and 9–11, fix the error in eq. 6, and clarify AutoBDMC.

Suggested evaluation improvement: explore iterative schemes to decrease Δβ until bounds are sufficiently tight and provide systematic treatment of β scheduling.

Overall assessment: valuable practical contribution for probabilistic programming but requires revisions on clarity, related work, and β-schedule/AutoBDMC issues before acceptance.
