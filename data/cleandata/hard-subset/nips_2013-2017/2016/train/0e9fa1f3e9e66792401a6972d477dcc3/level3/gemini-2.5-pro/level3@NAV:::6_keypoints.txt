- Proposes BREAD to bound the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors for MCMC inference.
- Uses BDMC framework (Grosse et al., 2015) with AIS chains run forward (prior→posterior) and reverse (posterior→prior).
- Main contribution is implementing and applying this existing technique in WebPPL and Stan.
- Validated across several models, comparing model representations and checking consistency between real and simulated data.
- Demonstrated practical diagnostic value by discovering a bug in WebPPL.
- Strengths: clear exposition, practical utility, and accessibility to practitioners via popular PPL implementations.
- Weakness: limited originality—theoretical basis is largely prior BDMC work and the Jeffreys-divergence bounding is a minor extension.
- Overall: technically sound and useful but not a significant methodological advance; borderline for a top-tier conference.
- Suggested venue: more appropriate for a specialized workshop on MCMC methods or probabilistic programming.
