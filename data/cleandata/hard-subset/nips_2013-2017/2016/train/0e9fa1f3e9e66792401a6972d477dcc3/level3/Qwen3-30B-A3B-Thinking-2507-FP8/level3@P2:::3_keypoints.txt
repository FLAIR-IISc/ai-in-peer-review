- Main contribution: extends BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior and approximate MCMC samples; shows the BDMC gap upper-bounds this divergence in expectation.  
- Implementation: integrates the method into WebPPL and Stan and proposes a protocol for evaluating inference on real-world data by simulating from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Core technical result (Section 3.1) is the Jeffreys divergence derivation, but the derivation contains errors and ambiguities (Eq. (6) appears incorrect, vector v undefined, estimator p̂(y) not defined).  
- Missing related work: fails to connect to Neal (1996) Tempered Transitions, which is conceptually similar and would strengthen the theoretical foundation.  
- Experiments: validated on five models and shown useful for debugging and comparing model representations.  
- Critical parameter omission: insufficient detail on the β schedule choice, which crucially affects bounds and comparisons.  
- Undefined concepts: "AutoBDMC" is never clearly defined or explained.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation (e.g., "rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back"), undefined acronyms (RAIS, u/cRAIS), and poor equation presentation.  
- Mathematical errors: critical equations contain mistakes or missing parentheses (e.g., D_JS expression), and Sections 3.1–3.2 read rushed and ambiguous, hindering reproducibility.  
- Presentation: figures have poor labeling and small axis/tick labels, reducing readability.  
- Originality concern: the core insight (BDMC gap bounding Jeffreys divergence) is not made clearly novel and reads as a straightforward application of existing BDMC techniques.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem with high potential impact for probabilistic programming users, but applicability is limited to specific algorithms (AIS, SMC) and relies on simulated data.  
- Strengths summary: rigorous general evaluation method, practical utility demonstrated, integrations with major PPLs, and careful experiments.  
- Weaknesses summary: missing key related work, technical and notation errors, poor discussion of β_t and AutoBDMC, insufficient experimental detail, and various typos/presentation issues.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; require substantial revision to (1) connect to Neal’s work, (2) fix derivations and ambiguities, (3) provide implementation/β-schedule details, and (4) clarify AutoBDMC before resubmission.
