- Paper extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound Jeffreys divergence for evaluating MCMC, proposing BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing).  
- BREAD is implemented in WebPPL and Stan and validated on multiple models and datasets.  
- Main contribution: using BDMC to compute upper bounds on divergence between true posterior and MCMC sample distributions.  
- Strength: clear and concise introduction to the problem and BDMC-based approach.  
- Strength: BREAD method is well-motivated and clearly explained.  
- Strength: Empirical validation shows BREAD can provide useful insights into MCMC algorithm design.  
- Weakness: Limited originality—BDMC is previously published and paper lacks significant novel theoretical contributions.  
- Weakness: Paper focuses mainly on implementing known techniques rather than introducing new methods.  
- Weakness: Insufficient explanation or motivation for why BREAD is necessary or uniquely useful in practice.  
- Clarity score: 8/10 — generally well-written but some technical details may challenge non-experts.  
- Originality score: 4/10 — little novel contribution beyond prior BDMC work.  
- Significance score: 6/10 — provides useful but unsurprising insights; practical necessity unclear.  
- Arguments for acceptance: well-written, well-explained method, and provides practical insights.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of novelty, implementation-centric, and unclear practical necessity.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions — clarify usefulness, add practical guidance, and include more original contributions.
