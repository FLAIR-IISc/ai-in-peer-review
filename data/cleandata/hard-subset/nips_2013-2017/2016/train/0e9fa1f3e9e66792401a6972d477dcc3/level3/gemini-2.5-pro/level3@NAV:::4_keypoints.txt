- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to quantitatively evaluate MCMC inference quality using forward and reverse AIS.  
- Builds on BDMC to compute stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood for synthetic data.  
- Main theoretical claim: the gap between these bounds upper-bounds in expectation the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Provides a single-scalar convergence metric that goes beyond common MCMC diagnostic heuristics.  
- Method is well-motivated, technically sound, and supported by strong experiments in Stan and WebPPL.  
- Authors are transparent about the limitation that rigorous bounds apply only to synthetic data and propose a protocol to predict real-data performance.  
- Demonstrates practical utility beyond convergence checks, e.g., comparing model representations and debugging (found a WebPPL bug).  
- Major clarity issue: derivation of the divergence bound in Section 3 is difficult to follow and lacks intuition/motivation for key equations and variables.  
- Originality concern: if the divergence bound in Section 3.1 is novel, the paper should explicitly state and highlight this.  
- Minor presentation issues: possible typo (Line 149 p(theta, z | y) vs p(theta, z, y)) and inconsistent data notation (y vs D).  
- Reproducibility concern: supplemental code needs better documentation, compatible WebPPL versions, integration instructions, and ideally installable packages for both platforms.
