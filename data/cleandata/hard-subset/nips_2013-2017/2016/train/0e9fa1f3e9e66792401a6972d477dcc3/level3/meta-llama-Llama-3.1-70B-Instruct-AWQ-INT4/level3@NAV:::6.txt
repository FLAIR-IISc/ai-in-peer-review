This paper presents an extension of the bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) technique to evaluate the accuracy of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference algorithms. The authors propose a method called Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD) to upper bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples produced by MCMC. The method is integrated into two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan, and validated on several models and datasets.

The main idea of the paper is to use BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence, which is a measure of the difference between two distributions. The authors show that the BREAD method can be used to evaluate the accuracy of MCMC inference algorithms, and that it can provide insights into the design of these algorithms.
