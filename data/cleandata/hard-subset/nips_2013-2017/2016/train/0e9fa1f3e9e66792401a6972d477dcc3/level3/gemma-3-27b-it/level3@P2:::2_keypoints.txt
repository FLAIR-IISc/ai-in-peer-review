- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with REverse Annealing) to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Extends BDMC to provide a rigorous expectation-based upper bound applicable to black-box inference methods.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical integration into probabilistic programming systems.  
- Experimental validation uses simulated and real data; protocol for real data (hyperparameter inference + simulation) is a clever workaround for needing exact samples.  
- Shows practical utility by guiding model representation choices and uncovering a WebPPL bug.  
- Addresses an important problem: rigorous quantitative assessment of MCMC sample quality where diagnostics often fail.  
- Theoretical analysis is sound and builds appropriately on prior work.  
- Presentation is clear, well-organized, and accessible.  
- Main weakness: computational cost of running forward and reverse AIS chains and requirement for exact samples can be high.  
- Scalability concerns: more evaluation needed on much larger and more complex models.  
- Suggested improvements: expand discussion of runtime/scalability and explore approximations to reduce computational burden.  
- Validation suggestion: test on a broader, more diverse set of models and datasets to strengthen robustness claims.  
- Notes limitations of using approximate posterior samples for hyperparameter fitting and suggests exploring alternative strategies.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address computational cost, scalability, and broader validation.
