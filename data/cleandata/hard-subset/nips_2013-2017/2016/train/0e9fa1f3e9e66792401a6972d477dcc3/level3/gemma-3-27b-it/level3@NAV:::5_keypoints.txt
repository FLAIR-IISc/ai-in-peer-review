- Paper proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) to assess MCMC reliability and compare sampler efficiency via tightness of BMC log-marginal likelihood bounds.  
- Extends framework to estimate Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate samples as an MCMC convergence diagnostic.  
- Builds on recent theoretical work (e.g., GGA15) and differs from heuristic diagnostics by aiming for a theoretically grounded approach.  
- Related to Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (2016) but with a different reverse-chain initialization; implemented in WebPPL and Stan demonstrating practical applicability.  
- Major limitation: method assumes access to a pre-existing, converged hyperparameter value (η_real), which undermines the diagnostic if that initialization is not truly converged.  
- Assumes synthetic-data posterior reflects convergence properties of the real posterior; this is unjustified, especially under model misspecification.  
- Claim that starting from η_real yields an “almost exact” sample is optimistic and lacks empirical verification.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (Section 5.1) with simple examples that do not demonstrate robustness to complex scenarios.  
- Finds a WebPPL bug (useful), but this does not validate the general efficacy of the diagnostic.  
- Paper does not address performance on multimodal posteriors or with poor summary statistics—situations where standard diagnostics already struggle.  
- Reviewer recommendation: lean toward rejection; requests stronger treatment of core assumptions, more comprehensive simulations comparing to established diagnostics, and demonstrations of robustness to multimodality and poor summaries.
