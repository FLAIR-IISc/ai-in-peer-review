- Proposes BREAD, using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to upper bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and MCMC approximate samples.  
- Leverages BDMC (originally for marginal likelihood) to quantify MCMC sample quality, aimed at “black-box” PPL settings.  
- Implements BREAD in WebPPL and Stan and demonstrates feasibility through validation experiments.  
- Practical demonstration uncovered a bug in WebPPL, highlighting real-world utility.  
- Strong motivation: existing MCMC diagnostics lack rigorous guarantees and PPL users need reliable inference checks.  
- Builds naturally on prior BDMC and AIS literature (Grosse et al. 2015; Neal 2001).  
- Theoretical derivation appears sound and the estimator for bounding Jeffreys divergence is well-founded.  
- Integration with popular PPLs is a significant practical contribution.  
- Experiments are limited in scope and could cover more models and datasets.  
- Section 3.1 is difficult to follow: dense notation, premature introduction of distributions (q(.)), and confusing presentation.  
- Notation clutter: using both z and θ for unobserved variables is unnecessary and reduces readability.  
- Important derivation steps are pushed to the supplement; essential steps should appear in the main text for clarity/reproducibility.  
- Minor typographical error noted (missing closing parenthesis on line 165).  
- Related-work discussion on MCMC diagnostics is insufficient; needs comparison to Geweke and Cook/Gelman/Rubin approaches.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel application of BDMC, technical soundness, practical significance for PPLs, and demonstrated ability to detect implementation errors.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: unclear theoretical presentation, limited experimental evaluation, and incomplete related-work comparison.  
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying Section 3.1 and expanding the related-work discussion.
