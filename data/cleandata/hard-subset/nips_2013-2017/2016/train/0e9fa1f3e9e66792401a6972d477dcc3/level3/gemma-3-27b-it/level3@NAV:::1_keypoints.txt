- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between MCMC output and the true target distribution — a significant, theoretically grounded contribution.  
- Targets the important problem of reliable MCMC diagnostics in “black box” probabilistic programming settings.  
- Builds on GGA15 and convincingly adapts the framework to bound Jeffreys divergence.  
- Integrated implementations in WebPPL and Stan, increasing practical relevance.  
- Experimental validation across diverse models and datasets supports the method’s applicability.  
- Acknowledges the simulated-data requirement and proposes a pragmatic protocol to apply the method to real-world data.  
- Properly relates to and acknowledges concurrent work (CTM16).  
- Clarity issue: Section 3.1’s derivation is unclear—q(.) is used before being defined (should be defined as the AIS marginal after T steps).  
- Important derivation details are pushed to the supplement; the main text should be more self-contained with step-by-step exposition.  
- Minor presentation issues: unnecessary split of unobserved variables into z and θ, and a typographical error on line 165.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant challenge, presents a novel/theoretically sound method, provides practical implementations, includes validation, and suggests a real-data protocol.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems in Section 3.1, over-reliance on supplemental material, and minor presentational flaws.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and completeness of the main text.
