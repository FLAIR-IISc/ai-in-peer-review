- Proposes BREAD, a novel method extending BDMC to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Demonstrates BREAD’s effectiveness on simulated data and offers a protocol for analyzing sampler accuracy on real-world data.  
- Potentially very useful for debugging and evaluating MCMC implementations and diagnosing sampler convergence failures.  
- Provides a clear, detailed derivation of an estimator that upper bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.  
- Derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow, with undefined notation and reliance on the supplement for clarity.  
- Paper lacks a clear, end-to-end statement of how to apply the method in practice; readers must piece steps together.  
- Minor notation and formatting issues (unnecessary variables, missing parentheses) need fixing.  
- Meets conference criteria for quality and originality but requires improved clarity.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance, conditional on revisions to improve clarity and address minor issues.
