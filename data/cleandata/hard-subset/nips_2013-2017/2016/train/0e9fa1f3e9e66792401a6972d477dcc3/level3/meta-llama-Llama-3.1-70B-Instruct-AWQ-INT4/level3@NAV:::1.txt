This paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging and evaluating MCMC in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The idea is interesting and potentially useful, as existing MCMC diagnostics are problematic.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its application to PPLs. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work and discuss the limitations of their approach.

One potential issue with the paper is that it does not adequately cite and discuss relevant prior works, such as Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2006), which validate MCMC by running on data sampled from the prior.

Additionally, Section 3.1 is hard to follow, as the notation q(.) is used before being defined. It would be helpful to explicitly state that q(.) is the marginal of the AIS sampler after T steps.

The paper also pushes important derivation details to the supplement, which makes it difficult to follow the main text. It would be better to present a clear step-by-step application of the method in the main text.

Minor issues include the unnecessary splitting of unobserved variables into z and theta, and a typographical error at line 165 (missing closing parenthesis).

Overall, the paper is well-written and proposes an interesting idea, but could benefit from more thorough citations and clearer explanations of the methodology.

**Quality:** 8/10
The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the lack of clear explanations in some sections and the omission of relevant prior works detract from the overall quality.

**Clarity:** 7/10
The paper is generally well-written, but some sections, such as Section 3.1, are hard to follow. The notation and terminology are not always clearly defined, which can make it difficult for non-experts to understand the material.

The paper proposes a novel application of BDMC to evaluate MCMC in PPLs, which is a significant contribution to the field. However, the idea of using BDMC to estimate the Jeffreys divergence is not entirely new.
