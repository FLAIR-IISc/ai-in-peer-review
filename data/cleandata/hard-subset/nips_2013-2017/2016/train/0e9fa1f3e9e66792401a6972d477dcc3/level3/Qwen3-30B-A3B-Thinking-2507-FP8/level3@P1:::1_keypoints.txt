- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to rigorously bound the Jeffreys divergence between MCMC approximate posterior samples and the true posterior, extending BDMC to MCMC (AIS and SMC).  
- Provides expected upper bounds on divergence by running AIS chains forward and backward to sandwich the true log marginal likelihood.  
- Integrates the method into WebPPL and Stan and validates it across multiple models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization, time series, Gaussian mixtures).  
- Strong theoretical contribution: derivation of the upper bound on Jeffreys divergence is sound, though presentation could be clearer.  
- Demonstrates practical utility: uncovered a bug in WebPPL and guided model representation decisions (collapsed vs. uncollapsed) with measurable effects on convergence.  
- Experimental protocol for using simulated data to evaluate real-world inference is well-justified and validated; fitted hyperparameters make simulated behavior match real data.  
- Matrix factorization case study effectively shows differing impacts of collapsed representations in WebPPL versus Stan.  
- Original and novel application of BDMC to bounding posterior divergence; distinct from prior MCMC diagnostics and from methods with strong assumptions (e.g., Gorham & Mackey).  
- High significance for probabilistic programming: provides a rigorous, general, automatic procedure for monitoring inference quality.  
- Clarity weaknesses: key derivation hard to follow, unclear notation (e.g., q(.) used before definition), important details relegated to supplement, unnecessary splitting of variables, and a typographical error.  
- Missing related work discussion: should cite and discuss prior protocols for validating MCMC on prior-sampled data (Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2006).  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor presentation revisions needed; recommendation to accept.
