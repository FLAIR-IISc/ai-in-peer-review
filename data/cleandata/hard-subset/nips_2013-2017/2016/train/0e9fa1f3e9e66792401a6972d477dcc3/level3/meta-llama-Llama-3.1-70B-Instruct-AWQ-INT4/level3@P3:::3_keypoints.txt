- Proposal: use the gap between BDMC stochastic lower and upper bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC transition operators.  
- Extension: adapt BDMC to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference by bounding symmetrized KL divergence between true posterior and approximate-sample distribution.  
- Contributions: implementations of BDMC for Stan and WebPPL and a practical evaluation protocol for posterior inference.  
- Contribution: technique for bounding the Jeffreys divergence between target and AIS-produced sample distributions.  
- Strength: method is simple, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Strength: paper provides a clear explanation of BDMC technique and its application to posterior inference.  
- Strength: experimental results support the method’s effectiveness in evaluating posterior inference accuracy.  
- Novelty concern: technical contributions are modest and the Jensen–Shannon divergence bound observation may not be novel.  
- Related work omission: does not cite or discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which closely relates to BDMC.  
- Clarity issues: Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed, writing quality slips, and several ambiguities remain.  
- Notation problems: ambiguous notation and inconsistent use of labels ("rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back").  
- Experimental detail missing: unclear how β_t is chosen in practice; code implies step = 1/options.steps but this isn’t stated.  
- Figure problems: unclear captions and figures with axes/tick labels that are too small.  
- Typos/terminology errors: missing closing parenthesis in D_JS expression and misspelling of Hoffman as "Homan"; some acronyms undefined.  
- Acceptance arguments: proposes useful new evaluation method, simple to implement, and experimentally validated.  
- Rejection arguments: modest novelty, incomplete experiments, clarity and notation problems.  
- Recommendation: potentially acceptable conditional on fixing clarity, expanding related-work discussion, improving experimental detail, addressing novelty concerns, and correcting typos for camera-ready.
