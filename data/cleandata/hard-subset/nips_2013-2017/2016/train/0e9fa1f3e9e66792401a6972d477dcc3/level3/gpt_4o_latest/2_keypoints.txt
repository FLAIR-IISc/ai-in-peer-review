- Proposes BREAD: an extension of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate MCMC samples and the true posterior.
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and validates it on simulated and real-world datasets.
- Tackles the important problem of quantitatively evaluating the quality of MCMC-based posterior inference.
- Strength: Theoretical derivation and bounds on Jeffreys divergence are rigorous and empirically validated.
- Strength: Method is significant and could impact both inference algorithm developers and probabilistic programming practitioners.
- Strength: Practical contribution through integration with popular probabilistic programming frameworks.
- Strength: Original idea—extending BDMC to Jeffreys divergence and proposing a simulated-data protocol for real-data proxying.
- Strength: Paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes useful validation and debugging examples.
- Weakness: Requires access to exact posterior samples, limiting direct applicability to simulated data; real-data heuristic’s generality is unclear.
- Weakness: Computationally expensive due to running both forward and reverse AIS chains; runtime tradeoffs not thoroughly analyzed.
- Weakness: Limited detailed comparison to alternative MCMC convergence diagnostics and related evaluation methods.
- Recommendation: High-quality paper recommended for acceptance, with strengths outweighing the noted limitations.
