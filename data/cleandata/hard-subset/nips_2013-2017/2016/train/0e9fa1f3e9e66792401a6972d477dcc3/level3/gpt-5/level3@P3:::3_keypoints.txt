- Paper extends BDMC/AIS to bound symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) between MCMC sampler marginal and true posterior using forward/reverse AIS on simulated data.
- Implements method in Stan and WebPPL and proposes a protocol transferring simulated-data guarantees to real data via fitted hyperparameters.
- Relates to AIS, BDMC, Jarzynski work relations, SMC, and concurrent "subjective divergence" work.

- The forward/reverse annealing idea connects to Neal’s AIS (2001), Jarzynski (1997), and BDMC (Grosse et al., 2015).
- Missing citation/discussion of Tempered Transitions (Neal, 1996) and the acceptance-ratio viewpoint; needs positioning relative to subjective divergence work.

Strengths:
- Simple, broadly applicable metric for sample quality for AIS/SMC-style transitions.
- Practical implementations in popular PPLs with potential impact on workflows.
- Sensible simulated-vs-real protocol useful for users and developers.
- Demonstrated utility for model choice and debugging (e.g., WebPPL Gaussian bug).

Technical correctness & clarity issues:
- Sections 3.1–3.2 are rushed with hard-to-follow derivations, missing definitions, and notation inconsistencies.
- The AIS estimator ĥp(y) used in expectations is never defined; must define forward and reverse estimators and specify expectation measures.
- Equation (6) appears incorrect (identical KL arguments); needs correction to show intended decomposition and marginalization to (8).
- Ambiguous definition of v (variables sampled in AIS) — clarify for forward and reverse directions and whether it includes intermediate states and auxiliary randomness.
- Inconsistent labels (rev/fwd vs for/back); standardize terminology throughout.
- "AutoBDMC" is undefined — clarify whether it means stopping based on bound gap, fixed hyperparameter protocol, or automated tuning/schedule.

Related work & novelty:
- Precisely state novelty relative to concurrent subjective divergence work; clarify any Jensen–Shannon divergence claims or reframe as known consequence.

β-schedule and protocol shortcomings:
- βt schedule choice is under-discussed though Δβ directly affects bounds; state schedules used and analyze sensitivity to Δβ and number of stages T.
- Provide practical guidance for schedule selection and consider proposing adaptive Δβ refinement (e.g., equalize incremental log-weight variance, ESS thresholds, place more β where log-likelihood curvature is high).
- Clarify whether AutoBDMC includes automated Δβ refinement and stopping criteria based on stochastic gap.

Experiments & presentation issues:
- Explicitly report βt, number of AIS stages, and HMC/NUTS steps per stage; clarify whether reported HMC/NUTS steps are per stage or total.
- Define RAIS and acronyms like (u)c(R)AIS in-text and in captions.
- Provide numerical experiment details (number of chains, seeds, time budgets) and discuss how many AIS runs are needed given bounds are in expectation and how estimates are aggregated.
- Improve figure readability: larger axis titles and tick labels.

Typos & minor fixes:
- Fix missing parenthesis in DJS expression and confirm whether references mean JSD or Jeffreys.
- Correct NUTS citation spelling (Hoffman misspelled).
- Ensure consistent Jeffreys divergence notation and definition.

Quality & clarity summary:
- Conceptually sound and valuable, but derivational slips, missing estimators, and β-schedule ambiguities reduce reproducibility; Sections 3.1–3.2 need tightening and notation standardization.

Originality & significance:
- Moderate originality (builds on BDMC/AIS); overlap with concurrent work reduces novelty, but implementations and PPL protocol are useful.
- High potential impact for PPL users and developers as a practical diagnostic for inference reliability.

Pros:
- Simple, general, practical idea with real implementations and demonstrated debugging utility.

Cons:
- Modest novelty, missing Tempered Transitions citation, clarity problems (derivations, undefined estimators, inconsistent labels), under-specified β schedule, and experimental detail deficits.

Recommendation:
- Borderline accept conditional on fixing clarity issues (Sections 3.1–3.2, eq. 6, estimator definitions), adding Tempered Transitions to related work, systematically addressing β-schedule choice (ideally with adaptive Δβ study), and clarifying AutoBDMC.
