- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to evaluate approximate posterior sample quality from MCMC.  
- Extends BDMC to provide an expected upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Offers a rigorous theoretical derivation building on Grosse et al. (2015).  
- Addresses the critical problem of measuring MCMC inference quality, especially for black-box probabilistic programming.  
- Demonstrates the estimator on simulated data where exact posterior samples are available.  
- Presents a practical protocol for applying the method to real data by simulating from inferred hyperparameters.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, making it immediately applicable to practitioners.  
- Validates the method on multiple models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization, mixture-of-Gaussians).  
- Shows practical utility by diagnosing and uncovering a bug in WebPPL’s multivariate Gaussian sampling.  
- Distinguishes itself from related work (e.g., subjective divergence) by providing mathematical guarantees on the bound.  
- Significant contribution for probabilistic inference and probabilistic programming, offering an automatic, rigorous monitoring tool.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and experimentally thorough.  
- Minor weakness: method requires cases where exact posterior samples can be obtained (relies on simulated data), though a reasonable real-data protocol is provided.  
- Minor weakness: focus is primarily on AIS-based inference, but authors argue insights generalize to MCMC more broadly.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, practically useful, and recommended for acceptance.
