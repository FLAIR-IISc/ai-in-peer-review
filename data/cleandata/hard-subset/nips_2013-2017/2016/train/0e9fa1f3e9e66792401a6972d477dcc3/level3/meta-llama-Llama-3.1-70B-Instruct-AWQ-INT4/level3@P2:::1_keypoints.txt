- Proposes extending bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to measure MCMC sampler accuracy by estimating the Jeffreys divergence and integrates this into WebPPL and Stan with validation on several models and datasets.  
- Builds on Grosse et al. (2015) BDMC and mentions prior MCMC diagnostics (Geweke, Cook et al.) but fails to adequately cite or discuss these related works.  
- Technically sound with clear BDMC explanation, but key derivation details are relegated to the supplement, leaving the main text not fully self-contained.  
- Evaluation is thorough across models/datasets but would benefit from more detailed results and comparisons to alternative methods.  
- Some sections (notably Section 3.1) have unclear notation; authors should define q(.) and clarify the AIS marginal after T steps.  
- Minor clarity/format issues: unnecessary split of unobserved variables into z and theta and a typographical error (line 165).  
- Original contribution: novel extension of BDMC to estimate Jeffreys divergence and practical integration into two probabilistic programming languages.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem in probabilistic programming and could be widely adopted due to tooling integration.  
- Pros: novel method, language integrations, and comprehensive empirical validation.  
- Cons: lacks a clear, detailed main-text derivation, needs stronger comparative evaluation, and contains minor notation/typo issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve exposition, expand evaluation details, and fix minor issues.
