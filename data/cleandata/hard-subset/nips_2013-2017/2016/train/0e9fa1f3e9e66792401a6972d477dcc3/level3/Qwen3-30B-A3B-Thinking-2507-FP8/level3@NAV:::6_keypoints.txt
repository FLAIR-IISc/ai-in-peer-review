- Proposes extending bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound Jeffreys divergence between MCMC approximate samples and the true posterior.
- Applies the technique to two probabilistic programming languages (WebPPL and Stan) and validates on several models.
- Core idea directly builds on Grosse et al. (2015) BDMC framework, focusing on measuring inference quality instead of marginal likelihoods.
- Implementation is technically sound, clearly described, and validated on simulated data.
- Practical utility: provides tools for practitioners to assess inference quality in probabilistic programming.
- Demonstrates how BREAD can guide model representation choices (e.g., matrix factorization experiments).
- Main limitation: lacks original contribution—extension is a minor technical refinement of existing BDMC.
- Fails to clearly distinguish itself from related work, notably Cusumano-Towner and Mansinghka (2016).
- Significance is limited: no new theoretical insights or substantial methodological improvements.
- Meets quality standards and is useful for practitioners, but lacks sufficient novelty for NIPS; recommend rejection and placement in more appropriate venues (e.g., MCMC workshops).
