- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate MCMC samples and the true posterior.  
- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing), an estimator that upper-bounds this divergence in expectation.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and validates it on multiple models.  
- Demonstrates practical uses: comparing model representations and debugging inference implementations.  
- Adapts existing BDMC framework rather than introducing new theoretical machinery.  
- Shows BDMC bounds can be interpreted as upper bounds on Jeffreys divergence and provides a protocol for real-data use via simulated data from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Notes related work (CTM16) and differences (uses exact posterior samples for reverse initialization), but this is not a major theoretical advance.  
- Implementation and experiments are technically sound, thorough, and well executed.  
- Presentation and explanations are clear and accessible to readers familiar with MCMC and probabilistic programming.  
- Main weakness: limited originality—core idea is a straightforward extension of BDMC.  
- Limitation: requires exact posterior samples (practical mainly for simulated-data scenarios), restricting real-world applicability.  
- Significance is limited—useful diagnostic for probabilistic programming practitioners but not a broad advance in inference methodology.  
- Pros: solid implementations in two frameworks, useful diagnostic tool, practical utility demonstrated, clear validation and presentation.  
- Cons: lacks novel theory or methodological innovation, not sufficiently novel for a top-tier conference, better suited to a workshop on MCMC/probabilistic programming.
