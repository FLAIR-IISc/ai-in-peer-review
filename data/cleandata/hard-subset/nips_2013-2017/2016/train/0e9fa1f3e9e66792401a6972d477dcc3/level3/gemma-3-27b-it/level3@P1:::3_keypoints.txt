- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference, bounding the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Core idea: run AIS chains forward (prior→posterior) and reverse to provide an upper bound on Jeffreys divergence.  
- Implemented in WebPPL and Stan; validated on multiple models/datasets and used to analyze model representations.  
- Significance: tackles the hard problem of validating MCMC in “black box” probabilistic programming systems with a principled quantitative measure.  
- Integration with two widely used PPLs increases immediate practical usability and impact.  
- Strength: simple, easy to implement and integrate; practical potential for practitioners and researchers.  
- Demonstrated ability of BDMC to guide modeling decisions (e.g., matrix factorization) is compelling.  
- Technical contribution is solid but incremental; novelty is limited.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete—omits important connections such as Neal’s Tempered Transitions and other marginal likelihood bounding methods.  
- Sections 3.1–3.2 lack clarity; notation is imprecise and labeling inconsistent (e.g., definition of ‘v’, “rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”).  
- Key estimator \hat{p}(y) is undefined in several equations; Equation (6) appears incorrect.  
- “AutoBDMC” is not fully explained—mechanism and meaning are unclear.  
- Choice of annealing schedule (β_t) and step size Δβ is under-discussed; need systematic exploration or iterative scheme to tighten bounds.  
- Experimental details insufficient: how β_t is chosen, and relation between HMC/No-U-Turn steps and BDMC steps are ambiguous.  
- Presentation issues: figures have small axis labels; typos and undefined acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS) reduce polish.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important problem, provides principled/simple evaluation approach, practical PPL integration, useful for model development/debugging.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, missing relevant prior work, clarity and completeness problems, insufficient experimental detail.  
- Overall: valuable and practically impactful but requires significant revisions on related work, technical clarity, and experiments; currently borderline (leans toward rejection) pending fixes.
