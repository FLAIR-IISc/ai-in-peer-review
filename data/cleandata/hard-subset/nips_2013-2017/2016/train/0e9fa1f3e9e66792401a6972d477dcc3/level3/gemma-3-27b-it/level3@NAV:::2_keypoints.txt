- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to provide upper bounds on the KL divergence between the true posterior and MCMC approximations.  
- Novel estimator for quantifying inference reliability, addressing a critical need for black-box probabilistic programming systems.  
- Builds on prior work ([GGA15]) and positions the contribution within existing MCMC diagnostics literature.  
- Correctly identifies limitations of traditional convergence diagnostics and motivates a quantitative measure of approximation quality.  
- Related work section is thorough and compares the method to recent efforts like [CTM16].  
- Practical implementation (BREAD) integrated into WebPPL and Stan demonstrates versatility and accessibility.  
- Validation experiments give tangible evidence of BREAD’s utility and inform model representation choices.  
- Proposes a protocol for applying BREAD to real-world data by simulating from fitted hyperparameters and validates its consistency.  
- Discovery and reporting of a WebPPL bug shows BREAD’s potential as a robust testing/debugging tool.  
- The theoretical analysis is sound and well-presented.  
- Paper lacks a detailed discussion of computational cost and scaling to complex models.  
- Needs clearer articulation of assumptions for applying the method to algorithms beyond AIS/SMC.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical problem, offers a theoretically grounded approach, practical implementations, and compelling experiments.  
- Main concerns against acceptance: potential computational cost for complex models and unclear generalizability outside AIS/SMC.
