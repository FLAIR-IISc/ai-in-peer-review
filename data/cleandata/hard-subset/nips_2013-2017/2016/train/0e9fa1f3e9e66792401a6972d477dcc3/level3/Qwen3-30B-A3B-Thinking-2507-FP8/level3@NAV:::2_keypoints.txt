- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) for rigorous evaluation of MCMC posterior inference quality.
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to provide an upper bound on the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between approximate samples and the true posterior.
- Uses forward and reverse annealed importance sampling (AIS) on simulated data to obtain a theoretically grounded expected upper bound.
- Contribution 1: Refined theoretical analysis proving the upper bound property.
- Contribution 2: Exact computation of true divergence and upper bound on toy examples.
- Contribution 3: A protocol for applying the method to real-world data.
- Contribution 4: Practical integration into WebPPL and Stan.
- Builds on Grosse et al. (2015) BDMC but applies it novelly to posterior evaluation and is distinct from related work (e.g., Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka 2016).
- Demonstrates practical utility by guiding model representation choices (e.g., collapsed vs uncollapsed) and detecting implementation bugs (found a WebPPL sampling error).
- Quality: Sound theory, careful derivations, and comprehensive experimental validation across models and PPLs.
- Clarity and reproducibility: Well-organized presentation with sufficient implementation detail.
- Originality and significance: Novel, well-contextualized method addressing a fundamental challenge in probabilistic modeling with high practical impact.
