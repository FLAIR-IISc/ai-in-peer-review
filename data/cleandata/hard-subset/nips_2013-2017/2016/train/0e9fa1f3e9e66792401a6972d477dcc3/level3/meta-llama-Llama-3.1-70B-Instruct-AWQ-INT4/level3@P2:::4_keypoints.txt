- Proposes measuring MCMC reliability using Bidirectional Monte Carlo, extending BDMC to MCMC by running AIS forward and backward to upper bound the expected symmetrized KL between true posterior and approximate samples.
- Connects to prior work on MCMC convergence diagnostics, automatic MCMC configuration, thermodynamic integration, and sample-quality estimation.
- Experiments across diverse models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization, autoregressive time series, mixture-of-Gaussians) convincingly demonstrate effectiveness and utility for algorithm design and debugging.
- Section 3 (especially transition to 3.1) is hard to follow; variables and equations are introduced without sufficient motivation.
- The divergence bound in Section 3.1 appears central but is not explicitly stated or highlighted as a novel contribution.
- The term "AutoBDMC" is used before being clearly defined, causing clarity issues.
- Abstract, introduction, background, and methods are clear and well-written; experiments are well-organized with clear results and discussion.
- Minor notation and formatting issues throughout the manuscript.
- The method is novel and potentially significant for evaluating posterior inference in probabilistic programming.
