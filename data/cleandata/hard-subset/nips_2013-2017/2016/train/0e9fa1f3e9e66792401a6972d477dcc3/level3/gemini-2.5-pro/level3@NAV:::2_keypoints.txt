- Introduces BREAD, a novel method for evaluating MCMC-based posterior inference quality.  
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) by running AIS chains forward and reverse on simulated data to obtain an estimator that upper-bounds (in expectation) the Jeffreys divergence between approximate samples and the true posterior.  
- Demonstrates practical utility by integrating BREAD into WebPPL and Stan.  
- Tackles the important problem of reliably diagnosing MCMC sample quality for black-box probabilistic inference systems.  
- Significance & originality: provides a principled, quantitative measure beyond common convergence heuristics; using the AIS forward/reverse gap to bound symmetrized KL is elegant and novel.  
- Technical quality: derivation is sound, extends prior work, and experiments are comprehensive across several models.  
- Practical validation: useful for comparing model representations and debugging (e.g., found a bug in WebPPL).  
- Clarity & impact: paper is well-written, well-organized, and likely to be adopted by practitioners.  
- Limitation: rigorous guarantees require an exact posterior sample, so formal bounds apply only to simulated data; real-data protocol is heuristic without theoretical backing.  
- Limitation: method applies directly to AIS/SMC; applicability to standard MCMC diagnostics is indirect.  
- Overall: strengths outweigh limitations; careful presentation and convincing results; strong recommendation for acceptance.
