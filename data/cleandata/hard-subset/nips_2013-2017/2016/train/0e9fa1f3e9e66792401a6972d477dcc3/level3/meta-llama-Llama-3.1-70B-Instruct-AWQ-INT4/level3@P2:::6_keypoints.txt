- Paper applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC inference accuracy.  
- Extends BDMC to bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and AIS sample distribution.  
- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) as the proposed method.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan probabilistic programming languages.  
- Validates method on multiple models and datasets, demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Uses BREAD to study impact of model representation on MCMC performance.  
- Shows BREAD can help debug probabilistic programming implementations.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and provides a thorough literature review.  
- Technically sound with detailed explanations of methods and results.  
- Main contribution is application and implementation of existing BDMC techniques, not novel algorithms.  
- Novelty limited to extending BDMC to Jeffreys divergence and engineering integration.  
- Significance/impact considered modest; may be more appropriate for MCMC or probabilistic programming venues.

- Recommendation: accept with reservations due to limited novelty and fit for NIPS.
