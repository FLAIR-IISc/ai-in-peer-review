- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with REverse Annealing), an extension of BDMC for evaluating MCMC inference reliability.  
- Core idea: use forward and reverse AIS to upper bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Integrates the method into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical applicability.  
- Validated on multiple models and datasets, showing utility for model and algorithmic design.  
- Addresses the important problem of quantitatively assessing approximate posterior quality in black-box inference systems.  
- Positions contribution clearly as enabling posterior evaluation (not just marginal likelihood estimation), with a clear connection to BDMC.  
- Theoretical grounding and derivation of the Jeffreys divergence upper bound are sound and significant.  
- Novelty could be more explicitly stated despite the solid theoretical contribution.  
- Practical implementations and experiments (including finding a WebPPL bug) demonstrate diagnostic power and real-world impact.  
- Method is principled but computationally intensive.  
- Section 3 is hard to follow; transitions and introduction of variables/equations are abrupt and need more motivation.  
- The term “AutoBDMC” is used without an explicit definition, causing confusion.  
- Minor notation inconsistencies (e.g., ‘y’ vs ‘D’) detract from clarity.  
- Reliance on simulated data is a limitation; more discussion is needed on assumptions for transferring results to real data.  
- Reproducibility is weak: need clear instructions, compatible WebPPL/Stan versions, and ideally packaged code.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant, practical, impactful, and generally clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: poor clarity in Section 3, limited applicability to simulated data, and reproducibility concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on clarifying Section 3, defining AutoBDMC, and improving reproducibility (code/instructions).
