## Review of "Measuring the reliability of MCMC inference with bidirectional Monte Carlo"

This paper proposes a valuable extension of Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler’s output and the true target distribution. This is a significant contribution, as reliable MCMC diagnostics remain a challenging problem, particularly within the “black box” setting of probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The authors correctly identify the limitations of existing convergence diagnostics and present a theoretically grounded approach to quantifying the accuracy of approximate posterior samples. The idea of leveraging BDMC for this purpose is well-motivated and potentially very impactful.

The paper builds upon the work of [GGA15] and convincingly demonstrates how the framework can be adapted to bound the Jeffreys divergence. Integrating this method into WebPPL and Stan further enhances its practical relevance. The experimental validation, demonstrating the method’s application to diverse models and datasets, is a strong point.  Importantly, the authors acknowledge and address the limitation of requiring simulated data, proposing a pragmatic protocol to extend its utility to real-world problems. This work is related to, and acknowledges, the concurrent work of [CTM16] on similar themes. 

However, the presentation of the derivation in Section 3.1 is somewhat unclear. The variable *q(.)* is used before being explicitly defined, hindering comprehension. Defining it as the marginal of the AIS sampler after T steps would improve clarity. Furthermore, crucial derivation details are relegated to the supplement; while conciseness is appreciated, the main text should strive for self-containment and present a step-by-step application of the method. 

A minor point is that the splitting of unobserved variables into *z* and *θ* seems unnecessary as they always appear together, adding clutter without clear benefit. Finally, a typographical error exists on line 165 (missing closing parenthesis).

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian inference - reliable MCMC diagnostics.
*   Presents a theoretically sound and novel application of BDMC.
*   Demonstrates practical implementation within popular PPLs (WebPPL and Stan).
*   Includes validation on diverse models and datasets.
*   Proposes a reasonable protocol for applying the method to real-world data.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Clarity issues in Section 3.1 hinder understanding of the derivation.
*   Excessive reliance on supplemental material for crucial details.
*   Minor presentational issues (variable naming, typo).



**Overall:**  Despite minor shortcomings in presentation, this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field. The proposed method has the potential to significantly improve the reliability of MCMC-based inference, and the authors’ work is well-executed and thoughtfully presented. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the clarity and completeness of the main text.
