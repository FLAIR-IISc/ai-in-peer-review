- Revisits BDMC and uses forward/reverse AIS to produce stochastic lower/upper bounds on log p(y) and upper-bound the expected Jeffreys divergence between AIS samples and the true posterior.
- Integrates the diagnostic into WebPPL and Stan, proposes a fitted-hyperparameter protocol to approximate reverse initialization on real data, and presents case studies (regression, mixtures, matrix factorization) including a bug-finding example.
- Analysis is sound for simulated data, but the fitted-hyperparameter reverse initialization breaks the rigorous guarantee on real data and its empirical validation is limited.
- Lacks comparisons against standard diagnostics (R-hat/ESS) and Stein discrepancies; tightness-of-bound experiments are small-scale.
- Paper is clearly written and organized; needs a clearer statement of relationship to BDMC and CTM16 and explicit novelty beyond reinterpreting BDMC bias as a Jeffreys bound.
- Originality is limited: main ideas are in BDMC or are straightforward corollaries, and CTM16 presents an equivalent formulation; primary contribution is implementation/application.
- Significance is practical/engineering for PPL users (debugging, model choices) but modest as a NeurIPS main-track research contribution.
- Pros: clear exposition, good integration into WebPPL/Stan, practical case studies, reproducible work, useful for practitioners.
- Cons: limited novelty and overlap with CTM16, guarantees only hold on simulated data (real-data protocol is heuristic), limited empirical breadth, no head-to-head against established diagnostics.
- Recommendation: Reject for main track; consider submitting to an MCMC/Probabilistic Programming workshop after clarifying novelty, broadening benchmarks, and adding comparisons to R-hat/ESS and Stein-based diagnostics.
