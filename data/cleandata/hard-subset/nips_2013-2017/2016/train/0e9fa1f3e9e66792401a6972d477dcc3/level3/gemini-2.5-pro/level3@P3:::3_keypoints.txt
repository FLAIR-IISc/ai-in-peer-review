- Summary: Extends BDMC/AIS to bound Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors, applicable directly to simulated data and extended heuristically to real data; implemented in Stan and WebPPL.

- Strength: Addresses a practical need for automated, quantitative MCMC sample-quality diagnostics for probabilistic programming users.

- Strength: Method is simple, elegant, easy to implement, and useful in practice (e.g., found a WebPPL sampler bug).

- Strength: Provides a quantitative bound on Jeffreys divergence, enabling interpretable comparison of inference choices.

- Weakness: Section 3 has major clarity problems—key definitions and derivations are ambiguous or missing.

- Weakness: Several undefined terms/notations (e.g., estimator \hat{p}(y), vector v, acronyms like RAIS/uc(R)AIS) and inconsistent terminology.

- Weakness: Equation (6) appears incorrect or ill-defined, hindering theoretical correctness.

- Weakness: Important related work omitted—specifically Neal (1996) on tempered transitions.

- Weakness: Methodological gaps around annealing schedule choice and lack of formal treatment of its impact.

- Weakness: "AutoBDMC" is not clearly defined; its meaning is ambiguous.

- Weakness: Experimental details are insufficient for reproducibility (e.g., annealing schedules, ambiguous figure captions/labels).

- Suggestion: Major revision of Section 3 to define all terms, fix equations, and clarify notation.

- Suggestion: Add discussion of Neal (1996) and relate tempered transitions to BDMC framework.

- Suggestion: Provide systematic treatment/heuristics for selecting the annealing (β) schedule and clarify "AutoBDMC".

- Suggestion: Add full experimental hyperparameters, improve figure legibility, and correct typos (e.g., Hoffman→Homan, missing parenthesis).

- Recommendation: Borderline accept conditional on resolving clarity, related-work, and experimental-detail issues.
