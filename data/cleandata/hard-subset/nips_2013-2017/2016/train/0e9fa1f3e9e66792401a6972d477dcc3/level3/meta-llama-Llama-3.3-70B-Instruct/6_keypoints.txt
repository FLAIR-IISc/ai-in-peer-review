- Applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BREAD) to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo with Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing to monitor MCMC inference.  
- Presents a rigorous, general, and automatic procedure for assessing posterior inference quality.  
- Well-written and clearly explains background and methodology.  
- Provides detailed analysis and experiments validating BREAD on multiple models and datasets.  
- Explores tradeoffs between different representations of a matrix factorization model in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Demonstrates practical usefulness (e.g., uncovered a bug in WebPPL).  
- Main weakness: lacks originality and primarily implements existing techniques.  
- Limited scope and potentially of interest mainly to MCMC-focused audiences; may suit a workshop better.  
- Meets technical quality and clarity but has limited significance for the conference; recommends acceptance with revisions to address originality and scope.
