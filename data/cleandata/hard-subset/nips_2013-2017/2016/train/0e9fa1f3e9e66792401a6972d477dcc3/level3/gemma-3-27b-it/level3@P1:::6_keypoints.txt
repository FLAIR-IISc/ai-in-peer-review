- Paper extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference and upper-bounds symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.
- Uses AIS and SMC to apply BDMC, refining analysis from prior work to produce an estimator that upper-bounds Jeffreys divergence.
- Integrates the method (BREAD) into WebPPL and Stan, increasing practitioner accessibility.
- Applies BREAD to study the effect of model representation on inference effectiveness.
- Well-structured and clearly explains necessary background and related work.
- Appropriately contextualizes BDMC among existing MCMC diagnostics and automated tuning methods.
- Demonstrates awareness of related sample-quality estimation and BDMC variants.
- Validation on toy distributions and consistency checks between real and simulated data increase confidence in the method.
- Provides a rigorous approach to bounding inference error, stronger than typical diagnostics.
- Limited novelty: primarily an extension rather than a fundamentally new methodological contribution.
- Applicability restricted to algorithms amenable to importance sampling (AIS, SMC), limiting broader use.
- Reliance on simulated data in the protocol may introduce biases if simulations poorly match real-world data.
- Computationally expensive to run AIS chains, especially in reverse, which may hinder use on complex models.
- Lacks detailed comparison to standard diagnostics (e.g., R-hat, effective sample size).
- Arguments for acceptance: practical, useful tool; integration with mainstream probabilistic programming systems; rigorous error bounds.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution, limited applicability, likely better suited for a specialized workshop.
- Overall assessment: solid and technically sound but limited novelty and applicability lead to a recommendation of weak reject; suggest clearer limitations and more diagnostic comparisons.
