- Paper extends BDMC by running AIS in both directions to sandwich the log marginal likelihood and interprets the expected forward–reverse gap as an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence between approximate samples and the true posterior.
- Method (BREAD) applies when the inference algorithm can be cast as IS on an extended space (AIS/SMC) and is implemented in WebPPL and Stan.
- Proposes a pragmatic real-data protocol: fit hyperparameters on real data, simulate data, and run reverse AIS from an approximate posterior sample.
- Validated on multiple models (linear/logistic regression, AR, matrix factorization, MoG) and used to compare collapsed vs uncollapsed representations.
- Demonstrated practical utility for debugging; found a multivariate Gaussian sampling bug in WebPPL via inconsistent bounds.
- Builds directly on BDMC and AIS; conceptually related to Jarzynski relations, SMC, and complements existing convergence diagnostics and sample-quality measures.
- Close to concurrent work on subjective divergence in probabilistic programming and should compare more deeply to that work.
- Novelty is modest: main inequality follows from BDMC/AIS identities; contribution is a careful repurposing for sampler evaluation.
- The connection to tempered transitions/thermodynamic integration and acceptance-ratio interpretations is under-discussed and should be expanded.
- β-schedule is under-specified; authors should state schedules used, how T is chosen, and study schedule sensitivity (linear vs adaptive) and effect on the forward–reverse gap.
- Recommend reporting results as functions of both number of transitions and wall-clock time to assess practical tradeoffs.
- “AutoBDMC” is not defined; clarify whether it adapts β, steps, kernels, or stopping criteria and if it refers to the fitted-hyperparameter protocol.
- Sections 3.1–3.2 need clearer notation and definitions (introduce AIS weight estimator \hat{p}(y), index forward vs reverse chains, standardize labels, and correct Eqs. (6)–(11) with derivations).
- Real-data protocol should discuss failure modes when fitted hyperparameters are poor, consider multiple reverse-chain initializations, and quantify bias via S and confidence intervals.
- Experimental details are incomplete: specify βt and number of intermediates per figure, clarify axes/labels (what “HMC / No-U-Turn steps” means), and define RAIS and u/c(R)AIS in captions.
- Improve figure readability and make captions self-contained.
- Minor presentation fixes: typos, missing parentheses, and correct NUTS author name.
- Theoretical argument is sound given BDMC/AIS foundations but the bound is in expectation and relies on exact posterior initialization; empirical validation is reasonable but could be strengthened with schedule analysis and toy problems with known Jeffreys divergence.
- Clarity is generally good but derivations, notation, and figures need tightening; define all acronyms on first use.
- Originality is incremental but useful: reframes BDMC bounds as a sample-quality measure and integrates into PPLs.
- Significance is high for practitioners and MCMC developers as a concrete, quantitative diagnostic for black-box samplers.
- Pros for acceptance: practical, implementable diagnostic with real integrations, empirical utility for representation choice and debugging.
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty relative to BDMC, under-specified schedule/protocol, and bounds only rigorous on simulated data.
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending clearer derivations, fuller related-work discussion (tempering/subjective divergence), β-schedule design, and improved experimental reporting.
