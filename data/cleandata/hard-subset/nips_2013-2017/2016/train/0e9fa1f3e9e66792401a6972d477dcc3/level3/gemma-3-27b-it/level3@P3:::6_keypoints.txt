- Applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound Jeffreys divergence and evaluate MCMC inference quality for AIS/SMC-style algorithms.  
- Accurately summarizes prior BDMC and related theoretical foundations.  
- Implements BDMC within two probabilistic programming systems (WebPPL and Stan), demonstrating feasibility.  
- Implementation is meticulous and engineering effort is commendable.  
- Experiments on simulated data and model representations provide practical validation but are limited in scope.  
- Discovery of a WebPPL bug shows practical utility for testing and debugging.  
- Primary contribution is engineering/implementation rather than new theory or algorithms.  
- Lacks significant novelty or methodological innovation beyond prior BDMC work.  
- Paper reads more like an engineering report than a major scientific contribution suitable for NIPS.  
- Method applicability is restricted to algorithms amenable to AIS-style analysis.  
- Some sections (e.g., background) are overly detailed and could be condensed.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are generally supported by experiments.  
- Writing and organization are clear but occasionally verbose.  
- Overall significance is limited and unlikely to broadly impact MCMC diagnostics or probabilistic programming.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection; suggests the work is better suited for a workshop or technical report on implementation and validation.
