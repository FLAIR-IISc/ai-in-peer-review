- Core idea: use the gap between BDMC’s stochastic lower and upper bounds on log marginal likelihood as a generic performance measure for MCMC, implemented in Stan and WebPPL with an evaluation protocol.  
- Strength: conceptually simple, potentially high-impact, directly applicable to probabilistic programming systems.  
- Strength: demonstrated utility by identifying a bug in WebPPL and providing a practical protocol for real data via simulation from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Weakness: technical contributions are modest; the BDMC→Jensen-Shannon divergence connection isn’t shown to be novel or fully explored.  
- Related-work omission: fails to sufficiently engage prior work, notably Neal (1996) on Tempered Transitions.  
- Beta-schedule concern: choice of β_t (Δβ) is under-discussed despite large impact on bounds and comparisons; needs systematic treatment.  
- Ambiguity: “AutoBDMC” is not clearly defined (automated convergence assessment vs. fixed-hyperparameter scheme).  
- Clarity issues: Sections 3.1–3.2 feel rushed; definition of vector v is loose given two AIS sweeps; inconsistent labeling (rev/fwd vs for/back).  
- Missing/incorrect math: \hat{p}(y) estimator inside expectations is undefined; Equation (6) appears incorrect with identical KL arguments.  
- Experimental detail gaps: practical β_t choice not documented in paper (code implies step = 1/options.steps) and reproducibility is limited.  
- Figure/presentation problems: RAIS and variants undefined in captions; axes/tick labels too small; minor typos (missing parenthesis, “Homan” typo).  
- Originality: presents a novel application of existing techniques but not a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Significance: could become a standard PPL tool if refined and adopted, but currently reads as a proof-of-concept.  
- Arguments for acceptance: provides a practical evaluation tool, debug utility, and a path toward more automated reliable inference.  
- Arguments against acceptance: modest novelty, insufficient related work discussion, clarity/reproducibility shortcomings, and unresolved β-schedule issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions—address clarity and presentation, add Neal (1996) and related work discussion, and expand experiments exploring iterative Δβ reduction.
