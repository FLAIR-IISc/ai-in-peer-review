- Proposes BREAD: an extension of BDMC that bounds the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and MCMC sample distribution using AIS chains run forward and reverse on simulated data.
- Uses BDMC/AIS background to obtain stochastic lower bounds on log partition function ratios and to bound divergences in expectation.
- Introduces a protocol for applying BREAD to real-world datasets by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters.
- Evaluates BREAD on two probabilistic programming systems (WebPPL and Stan), validating consistency between real and simulated data.
- Demonstrates BREAD’s effectiveness at diagnosing issues in MCMC samplers.
- Paper provides thorough, accessible background material on AIS and BDMC.
- Experiments and results are detailed and reproducible.
- Main weakness: perceived lack of originality—primarily an implementation/application of existing BDMC techniques.
- Authors do not clearly justify why BREAD is superior to existing methods for evaluating MCMC inference.
- Paper lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and potential drawbacks of BREAD.
- Ratings: Originality 2/5, Quality 4/5, Clarity 4/5, Significance 3/5.
- Recommendation: Conditional acceptance if authors address originality concerns and better justify BREAD’s advantages; suggest emphasizing novel aspects (BDMC applied to probabilistic programming).
