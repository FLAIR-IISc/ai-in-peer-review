- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to evaluate MCMC reliability via bidirectional Monte Carlo, estimating the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Clearly explains background on MCMC, AIS, and bidirectional Monte Carlo and discusses convergence-diagnosis limitations.  
- Main contribution: development and rigorous analysis of BREAD, including derivation of the bias of the stochastic lower bound on the log partition function ratio.  
- Provides a protocol for applying BREAD to real-world data to diagnose inference quality.  
- Empirical validation: experiments showing BREAD effectively evaluates posterior inference accuracy in realistic settings.  
- Implementation: extended WebPPL and Stan to run forward and reverse AIS using the proposed sequence of distributions.  
- Demonstrates practical use by using BREAD to investigate model representation choices in Stan and WebPPL.  
- Strength: tackles a timely and important problem in probabilistic inference.  
- Strength: introduces a novel and interesting extension of bidirectional Monte Carlo for MCMC evaluation.  
- Strength: offers rigorous theoretical analysis and bias derivation.  
- Strength: supports claims with experiments and tool extensions.  
- Weakness: method limited to algorithms expressible as AIS or sequential Monte Carlo (importance sampling over an extended state space).  
- Weakness: requires an exact sample from the target distribution, which can be difficult to obtain in practice.  
- Weakness: paper could include more discussion of limitations and potential extensions.  
- Overall: well-written and organized; reviewer recommends acceptance, judging strengths to outweigh weaknesses.
