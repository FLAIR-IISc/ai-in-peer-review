- Introduces a novel method using bidirectional Monte Carlo to evaluate reliability of MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo to derive lower bounds on KL divergence between true and approximate posteriors.  
- Addresses a timely and important problem in probabilistic inference.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of method and theoretical foundations.  
- Thorough analysis of strengths and limitations of the proposed approach.  
- Demonstrates practical applicability by integrating the method into WebPPL and Stan.  
- Provides a protocol for diagnosing inference quality on real-world data.  
- Original contribution that builds on and improves previous related work.  
- High potential significance for rigorously evaluating approximate posterior samples.  
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis and supporting experimental results.
