- Proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jeffreys (J-S) divergence for debugging and evaluating MCMC in probabilistic programming languages (PPLs).
- Bounds the Jeffreys divergence by running annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains in both forward and reverse directions.
- Provides a quantitative measure of approximate posterior sample accuracy, addressing limits of common MCMC diagnostics.
- Integrates the method into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical feasibility.
- Validates the approach on multiple models and datasets and uncovers a real bug in WebPPL, showing debugging utility.
- Builds on prior work that uses prior-sampled data, extending BDMC to diagnose sampler convergence failures and proposing a real-data protocol.
- Strength: novel and significant approach that fills an important gap in MCMC evaluation and PPL debugging.
- Strength: practical implementation in widely used PPLs increases relevance to practitioners.
- Strength: experimental validation and demonstrated ability to find implementation bugs.
- Weakness: derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow and uses undefined terms (e.g., q(.))—needs clearer, stepwise explanation.
- Weakness: unnecessary complexity from distinguishing unobserved variables z and θ when they appear together.
- Weakness: cluttered notation and minor proofreading errors (e.g., missing parenthesis) reduce readability.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem with a novel, well-motivated, and practically validated method.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear derivations and notation complexity hinder accessibility for a broader audience.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and presentation.
