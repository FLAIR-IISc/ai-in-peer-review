**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper "Measuring the reliability of MCMC inference with bidirectional Monte Carlo" proposes a method for evaluating the accuracy of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) samplers using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC). The authors extend the BDMC technique to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between the target distribution and the distribution of approximate samples produced by MCMC. The method is integrated into two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan, and validated on several models and datasets. The authors also propose a protocol for using BDMC to diagnose inference quality on real-world data.

**Relationship to Previous Work**

The paper builds on previous work on BDMC, which was introduced by Grosse et al. (2015) as a method for estimating the log marginal likelihood. The authors also mention related work on MCMC diagnostics, such as Geweke (2004) and Cook et al. (2006), which validate MCMC by running on data sampled from the prior. However, these works are not directly cited and discussed in the paper. It would be beneficial to include a more detailed discussion of these prior works and how they relate to the proposed method.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the BDMC technique and its extension to estimate the Jeffreys divergence. However, some derivation details are pushed to the supplement, which makes the main text not self-contained. It would be beneficial to include a clear step-by-step application of the method in the main text.

The authors also provide a thorough evaluation of the method on several models and datasets, including a validation of the method on real-world data. However, the evaluation could be improved by including more detailed results and comparisons with other methods.

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written, but some sections are hard to follow. For example, Section 3.1 is difficult to understand because the notation is not clearly defined. The authors should explicitly state the definition of q(.) and provide a clear explanation of the marginal of the AIS sampler after T steps.

Additionally, some minor issues, such as the unnecessary splitting of unobserved variables into z and theta, and a typographical error at line 165, should be addressed.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel method for evaluating the accuracy of MCMC samplers using BDMC. The extension of BDMC to estimate the Jeffreys divergence is a significant contribution to the field. The integration of the method into two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan, is also a notable achievement.

**Significance**

The paper addresses an important problem in the field of probabilistic programming, which is the evaluation of the accuracy of MCMC samplers. The proposed method has the potential to be widely used in the field, and the integration into WebPPL and Stan makes it easily accessible to practitioners.
