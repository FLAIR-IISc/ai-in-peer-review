- Proposes AutoBDMC, extending BDMC to bound Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and approximate sample distribution using bidirectional AIS.
- Runs AIS chains forward (prior→posterior) and reverse on simulated data to upper-bound the expected symmetrized KL divergence.
- Provides clear motivation, a well-described method, and detailed analysis of its properties.
- Produces a single scalar convergence metric, improving over existing MCMC diagnostics.
- Demonstrates additional uses for model selection and debugging.
- Experiments are thorough and show effectiveness of the proposed method.
- Limited to synthetic data sampled from the joint; may not generalize to real-world data despite some supporting experiments.
- Some notation and variable definitions lack sufficient motivation and are hard to follow in places.
- AutoBDMC is never explicitly defined, which could confuse readers unfamiliar with the approach.
- Minor issues: potential typos and inconsistent notation for data.
- Builds on BDMC and AIS but offers a novel application for evaluating MCMC convergence; well-referenced.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to clarify notation, explicitly define AutoBDMC, and fix small errors.
