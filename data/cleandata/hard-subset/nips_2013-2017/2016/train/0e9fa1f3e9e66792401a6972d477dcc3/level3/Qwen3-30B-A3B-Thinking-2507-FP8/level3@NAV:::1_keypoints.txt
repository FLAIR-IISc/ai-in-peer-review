- Proposes BREAD, an extension of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between approximate posterior samples and the true posterior for MCMC in PPLs.  
- Shows BDMC provides rigorous upper bounds on the Jeffreys divergence for simulated data and introduces a protocol to apply the method to real data by simulating datasets using fitted hyperparameters.  
- Integrates the approach into WebPPL and Stan and validates it across multiple models.  
- Makes a valuable, theoretically grounded contribution to MCMC diagnostics, offering an alternative to common convergence diagnostics that lack rigorous guarantees.  
- Well-motivated for evaluating "black box" inference systems and practically useful—demonstrated utility includes debugging a WebPPL bug.  
- Omits citation/discussion of key prior works (Geweke 2004; Cook, Gelman & Rubin 2006) on validating MCMC with prior-sampled data, weakening contextualization.  
- Section 3.1 uses q(·) without defining it, making derivations hard to follow; q should be explicitly defined (e.g., marginal of the AIS sampler after T steps).  
- Important derivation details are relegated to the supplement; the main text should be more self-contained with clear step-by-step exposition.  
- Unnecessarily splits unobserved variables into z and θ when they always appear together.  
- Minor issues: typographical error at line 165 (missing closing parenthesis).  
- With fixes for missing citations, clearer technical presentation, and minor corrections, the paper could make a significant impact.
