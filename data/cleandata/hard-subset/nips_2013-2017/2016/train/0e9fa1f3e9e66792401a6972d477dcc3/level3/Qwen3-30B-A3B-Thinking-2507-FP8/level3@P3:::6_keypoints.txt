- Extends BDMC to bound Jeffreys divergence for MCMC, naming the method BREAD and integrating it into WebPPL and Stan.
- Uses BDMC’s stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood to bound symmetrized KL when exact posterior samples are available via simulation.
- Primary contribution is applying and implementing existing BDMC technique to evaluate MCMC inference rather than new theoretical advances.
- Proposes a protocol for applying BREAD to real-world data by simulating from fitted hyperparameters.
- Validated on toy examples and matrix factorization models; implementation uncovered a bug in WebPPL.
- Technical development is sound and implementation appears correct and reproducible, though more documentation would help.
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope and lacks extensive real-world validation.
- No new theoretical guarantees beyond Grosse et al. (2015); originality is limited.
- Related work (e.g., Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka 2016) is acknowledged but the relationship could be clarified.
- Significance is modest: a useful diagnostic tool for probabilistic programming practitioners but unlikely to broadly advance MCMC or inference methods.
- Recommended venue: more appropriate for an MCMC or probabilistic programming workshop than NIPS due to limited novelty.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical implementations in popular PPLs, useful diagnostic application of BDMC, bug discovery, clear protocol.
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks novel theory, core technique already published, limited evidence of broad adoption or impact.
