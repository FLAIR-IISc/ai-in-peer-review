- Introduces BREAD, a method to quantitatively evaluate MCMC samplers by bounding divergence to the true posterior.
- Extends BDMC to estimate a stochastic upper bound on the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence for AIS-based samplers.
- Rigorous guarantees require an exact sample from the true posterior, so guarantees apply only to simulated data.
- Proposes a practical protocol for real data: fit hyperparameters on real data, simulate from fitted parameters, then evaluate the sampler.
- Implemented in WebPPL and Stan; practical utility demonstrated by debugging (found a WebPPL bug).
- Originality/significance: provides a principled, quantitative diagnostic for MCMC beyond heuristic checks and ESS.
- Clever repurposing of BDMC offers a single summary divergence metric for sampler quality.
- Practical utility for PPLs: enables automated, rigorous quality control for developers and practitioners.
- Compelling experiments: simulation from fitted hyperparameters matched real-data behavior and guided modeling choices (e.g., parameterizations).
- Missing relevant literature: should cite/discuss Geweke (2004) and Cook, Gelman & Rubin (2006) and contrast simulate-and-fit validation approaches.
- Clarity issues in derivations: Section 3.1 hard to follow; notation like qfwd(xT) is not properly defined.
- Paper not self-contained: relies heavily on the supplement; main text needs a clearer algorithmic description or walkthrough.
- Notational clutter: separating z and θ is unnecessary and complicates equations.
- Minor typographical errors (e.g., missing parenthesis); needs proofreading.
- Overall: strong, novel, and impactful paper with addressable clarity/positioning issues; recommend acceptance.
