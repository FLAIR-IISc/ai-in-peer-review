- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing), extending Bidirectional Monte Carlo to bound the Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors.  
- Shows BMC gives stochastic upper bounds on log marginal likelihood and uses these to bound Jeffreys divergence when exact posterior samples are available.  
- Addresses lack of exact samples by proposing synthetic data from fitted hyperparameters, assuming synthetic posterior convergence mirrors real posterior convergence.  
- Theoretical foundation and derivation are sound and clearly presented.  
- Practical implementation provided in WebPPL and Stan and empirical validation on toy models shows reasonable accuracy.  
- Novel application of BMC to MCMC diagnostics, distinguishing from prior work by focusing on divergence bounds.  
- Major flaw 1: Assumes a known representative hyperparameter η_real from prior MCMC runs, which is circular when diagnosing convergence.  
- Major flaw 2: Assumes synthetic-data posteriors reflect real-data posterior convergence, an unjustified assumption under model misspecification.  
- Major flaw 3: Assumes initializing from η_real yields near-exact samples quickly, an optimistic and unverified claim.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited to simple examples and lacks robustness checks for complex settings, multimodality, or poor summary statistics.  
- Lacks comparisons to standard diagnostics (e.g., Gelman-Rubin) and does not address handling of multimodal posteriors.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting but practically limited by unverified assumptions; recommended rejection with encouragement to address these limitations.
