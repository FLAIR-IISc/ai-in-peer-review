- Proposes BREAD, extending BDMC/AIS to bound Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posteriors.
- Core idea: use forward and reverse AIS to produce stochastic lower/upper bounds; their gap upper-bounds Jeffreys divergence in expectation.
- Practical protocol for real data: fit hyperparameters approx., simulate data from the fitted model to obtain exact posterior samples, then apply BREAD.
- Implemented and validated in WebPPL and Stan; used to compare model parameterizations and found a WebPPL bug.
- Key theoretical contribution: derivation that the BDMC gap bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.
- Overall technically sound but hampered by clarity issues and incomplete experimental rigor.
- Major missing experimental detail: choice of annealing schedule (β_t) is not specified despite its large impact on results and comparisons.
- Section 3.1 is unclear, rushed, and contains ambiguous notation and apparent errors, reducing trust in the derivation.
- Specific issues in Section 3.1: ambiguous definition of v, estimator for partition function ratio not explicitly defined in terms of qfwd/qrev, and Equation (6) appears incorrect.
- Inconsistent terminology (e.g., "fwd"/"rev" vs. "forward"/"reverse") and poorly legible figures with undefined acronyms (RAIS, ucRAIS).
- Originality is modest—mainly extends BDMC by linking gap to Jeffreys divergence and proposing a practical simulation-based protocol.
- Omits important related work, notably Neal (1996) on Tempered Transitions and its connection to annealing and partition function ratios.
- Significance is high: BREAD could provide a principled, general diagnostic for MCMC quality in probabilistic programming.
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, general, quantitative metric; practical implementations; useful for model representation decisions and PPL debugging.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, missing Neal citation, flawed/unclear theoretical section, and omitted annealing schedule details harming reproducibility.
- Recommendation: Marginal Accept, contingent on addressing clarity, related work, and experimental detail concerns.
- Requested revisions: add Neal (1996) discussion; fully revise Section 3.1 with explicit estimator definitions and corrected equations; specify annealing schedule and number of steps; fix typos and improve figure readability and acronym definitions.
