- Paper proposes BREAD: using the expected bidirectional AIS (BDMC) log-gap to upper bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between AIS-produced samples and the true posterior.
- Introduces a fitted-hyperparameter heuristic to apply BREAD to real datasets by simulating data from hyperparameters fitted on real data.
- Implements tooling by integrating BREAD into WebPPL (MH over traces) and Stan (NUTS/HMC).
- Validates the bound on toy problems where exact divergences are available and experiments on multiple canonical models.
- Demonstrates practical uses: guiding modeling choices (e.g., collapsed vs. uncollapsed MF) and discovering a WebPPL Gaussian-sampling bug.
- Clear, careful exposition of BDMC/AIS mechanics and derivation of the expected Jeffreys-divergence bound.
- Practical contribution: tooling and case studies that are useful for practitioners and debugging.
- Polite, thorough discussion of related work, including concurrent and classical diagnostics.
- Main theoretical result is not highly novel—largely a direct consequence of BDMC/AIS and close to concurrent CTM’16 work.
- Strong guarantees require exact posterior samples, restricting rigorous application to simulated data.
- The fitted-hyperparameter real-data protocol is heuristic, unproven, and only empirically validated in limited settings.
- Unclear tightness of the bound beyond toy problems; lacks systematic assessment, error bars, and conditions for meaningfulness.
- Limited empirical comparison to alternative diagnostics (R̂, ESS, Stein discrepancies) to contextualize benefits.
- Minor issues: bibliographic/citation formatting and notational rough edges.
- Derivations appear correct; experiments are sensible and reproducible in principle but lack thorough tightness quantification.
- Writing and organization are good; would benefit from explicit computational-cost summaries and schedule-design guidance.
- Suggested improvements: systematic tightness studies, comparisons to other diagnostics, clarify reverse-AIS overhead and scheduling, discuss applicability to SMC/other samplers, and fix citations.
- Recommendation: Reject (borderline) due to limited novelty and scope; confidence 0.7.
