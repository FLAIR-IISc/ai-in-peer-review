## Review of “Measuring the reliability of MCMC inference with bidirectional Monte Carlo”

This paper proposes a novel application of bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler’s output and the true posterior distribution. The motivation is strong: existing MCMC diagnostics are often insufficient for reliable assessment of inference quality, particularly within the “black box” setting of probabilistic programming languages (PPLs). The idea of leveraging BDMC to provide a quantifiable upper bound on the divergence is promising and addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian inference. This work builds on prior work by GGA15 and extends it to the context of MCMC evaluation, which is a valuable contribution. 

The paper is generally well-written and clearly articulates the problem and proposed solution. The authors demonstrate the method’s integration into WebPPL and Stan and provide validation through experiments. The application to guide model representation choices in both PPLs is a compelling example of the method’s utility. However, the presentation of the core derivation in Section 3.1 is somewhat unclear. The variable `q(.)` is used before being explicitly defined, which hinders understanding. A more explicit statement of what `q(.)` represents (e.g., the marginal of the AIS sampler after T steps) would improve clarity. 

A significant weakness is the reliance on supplementary material for crucial derivation details. While space is limited, the main text should strive for self-containment and present a more step-by-step application of the method. This would make the paper more accessible and allow for easier verification of the results. The splitting of unobserved variables into `z` and `theta` feels unnecessarily cluttered, as they always appear together. Finally, a minor typographical error exists at line 165 (missing closing parenthesis).

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** Applying BDMC to assess MCMC quality is a novel and potentially impactful approach.
*   **Significance:** Reliable MCMC diagnostics are crucial for trustworthy Bayesian inference, and this work offers a promising avenue for improvement.
*   **Soundness:** The theoretical foundation appears solid, and the experimental validation provides initial support for the method’s effectiveness.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Clarity:** Section 3.1 requires improvement for better readability.
*   **Completeness:** Important derivation details are relegated to the supplement, hindering understanding and reproducibility.



**Overall:** I believe this paper has merit and addresses an important problem. Addressing the clarity and completeness issues would significantly strengthen the contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should focus on clarifying Section 3.1 and incorporating more of the key derivations into the main text. Additionally, citing Geweke (2004) and Cook, Gelman & Rubin (2006) – which discuss validation of MCMC by sampling from the prior – would contextualize the work within existing diagnostic approaches.
