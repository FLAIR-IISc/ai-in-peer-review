- Proposes BREAD: using BDMC to upper bound Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.
- Integrates BREAD into two probabilistic programming languages, WebPPL and Stan.
- Idea is simple, elegant, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Demonstrates effectiveness in evaluating posterior inference and comparing model representations.
- Technical contributions are modest and feel rushed.
- Clarity issues: sections 3.1 and 3.2 are difficult to follow.
- Paper lacks thorough exploration of connections between BDMC and Tempered Transitions.
- Insufficient attention to the choice of β_t values in BDMC; selection procedure is unclear.
- Ambiguous definitions, inconsistent labels, and unexplained notation reduce readability.
- Experiments need more thoroughness and clearer explanations, particularly regarding AutoBDMC.
- Figures should improve readability (larger axes and tick labels).
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address clarity, technical depth, and experimental details.
