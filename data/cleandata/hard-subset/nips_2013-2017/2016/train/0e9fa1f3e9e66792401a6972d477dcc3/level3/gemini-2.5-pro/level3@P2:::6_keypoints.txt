- Paper tackles assessing MCMC sample quality using Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD), extending BDMC.
- Core idea: run AIS forward and reverse to obtain stochastic lower and upper bounds on log marginal likelihood.
- Main theoretical claim: expected gap between bounds upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate samples and true posterior.
- Theoretical guarantees require exact posterior samples for the reverse chain, so they only hold on simulated data.
- Proposed practical protocol: estimate hyperparameters on real data, simulate data from the model, then apply BREAD to the simulated dataset.
- Implemented BREAD in WebPPL and Stan, used for validation and model-representation comparisons.
- Strong experimental validation across five models shows inference behavior on simulated data matches real-data behavior under the protocol.
- Compelling application: comparing collapsed vs. uncollapsed matrix factorization and uncovering a WebPPL bug.
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and well-organized with sufficient AIS/BDMC background.
- Main weakness: limited originality — core machinery derived from prior BDMC work with a relatively incremental theoretical refinement.
- Significance is meaningful for MCMC diagnostics and probabilistic programming but limited by novelty and simulated-data-only guarantees.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses critical diagnostic problem, solid implementations, and practical use cases demonstrating utility.
- Arguments for rejection: methodological novelty is small, reads more like a systems/applications paper, and guarantees don’t extend to most real-world settings.
