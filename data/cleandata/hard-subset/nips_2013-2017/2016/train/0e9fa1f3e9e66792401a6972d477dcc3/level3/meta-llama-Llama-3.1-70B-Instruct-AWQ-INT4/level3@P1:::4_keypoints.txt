- Proposes AutoBDMC, extending bidirectional Monte Carlo to assess MCMC posterior inference by running AIS forward and reverse to upper-bound expected symmetrized KL divergence.
- Method is well-motivated, novel, and addresses a long-standing problem in probabilistic inference.
- Theoretical analysis and experiments support the technical soundness of the method.
- Demonstrated integrations into WebPPL and Stan, with validation on multiple models and datasets.
- Shows utility for guiding inference algorithm design and analyzing model representations.
- Paper is generally well-written and provides sufficient background for non-experts.
- Section 3 (transition to 3.1) is hard to follow; variable and equation definitions lack motivation.
- AutoBDMC is referenced before being clearly defined, and data notation is inconsistent.
- Paper would benefit from more explanations and examples to aid non-expert readers.
- Supplemental WebPPL code should be released as an npm-installable package and Stan code should be published.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10, confidence 8/10.
