- Proposes using the gap between BDMC stochastic lower and upper bounds to measure MCMC transition operator performance by bounding the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence.  
- Extends Grosse et al. (2015) BDMC framework to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference and relates to broader MCMC diagnostics and sample-quality estimation literature.  
- Simple to implement and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Implemented BREAD/AutoBDMC in WebPPL and Stan and demonstrated utility across multiple models, including debugging a WebPPL issue.  
- Provides a well-motivated validation protocol for applying BDMC to real-world data.  
- Technical novelty is modest; key insight (Jensen-Shannon/Jeffreys bound) not clearly novel.  
- Fails to cite/discuss closely related prior work (notably Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions, 1996).  
- Choice and scheduling of β_t (Δβ) are under-discussed despite critically affecting bounds and comparisons.  
- “Auto” in AutoBDMC is not clearly defined and no iterative scheme is provided to reduce Δβ until bounds tighten.  
- Several clarity issues: rushed Sections 3.1–3.2, ambiguous notation (vector v), inconsistent terms (“rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”), and missing estimator definitions.  
- Equation errors and omissions (e.g., equation (6) KL argument issue; \hat{p}(y) undefined in multiple equations).  
- Experiments lack detail on β_t choice (code implication not stated), ambiguous figure labels (e.g., “HMC / No-U-Turn steps”), undefined acronyms (RAIS, (u)c(R)AIS), and small/unclear figure axes.  
- Recommendation: substantial revisions needed — address clarity, add missing related work, and provide systematic β_t scheduling (including an iterative tightening scheme) before acceptance.
