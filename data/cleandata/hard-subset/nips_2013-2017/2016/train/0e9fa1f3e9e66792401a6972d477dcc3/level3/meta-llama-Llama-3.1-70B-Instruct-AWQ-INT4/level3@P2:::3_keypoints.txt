- Proposes measuring MCMC inference reliability using bidirectional Monte Carlo by bounding the symmetrized KL via the gap between stochastic lower and upper BMC bounds.
- Extends BMC to evaluate MCMC posterior inference and provides implementations for Stan and WebPPL plus an evaluation protocol.
- Well-motivated with a clear explanation of the difficulty of assessing approximate posterior samples.
- Method is simple, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.
- Concern about novelty: the Jensen–Shannon divergence bound observation is nice but not clearly novel.
- Fails to cite/discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which closely relates to BMC and its acceptance-ratio interpretation.
- Lacks a systematic treatment of the choice of β_t; authors should discuss β_t selection and propose an iterative scheme to reduce Δβ until bounds are tight.
- Clarity problems in Sections 3.1–3.2: writing slips cause ambiguities and reduced readability.
- Notation issues (e.g., undefined vector v) and inconsistent terminology ("rev"/"fwd" vs "for"/"back").
- Experiments lack detail; unclear how β_t is chosen in practice (code implies step = 1/options.steps but this isn’t stated).
- Figures have presentation issues (small axes and tick labels).
- Needs to clarify the meaning and mechanism of "auto" in AutoBDMC.
- Overall evaluation: Quality 6/10, Clarity 5/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: new practical measurement method, easy implementation, addresses an important problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: modest technical contribution, incomplete β_t treatment, clarity and related-work gaps.
