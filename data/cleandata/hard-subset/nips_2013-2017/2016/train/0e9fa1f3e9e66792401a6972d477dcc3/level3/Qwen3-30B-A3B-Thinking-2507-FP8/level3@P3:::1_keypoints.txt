- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to estimate Jeffreys divergence using bidirectional AIS/BDMC on simulated data.  
- Provides a rigorous expected upper bound on divergence by running AIS from prior→posterior and posterior→prior.  
- Particularly valuable for probabilistic programming languages (PPLs) as an automatic diagnostic for black-box MCMC.  
- Should better acknowledge and contrast with prior validation approaches by Geweke (2004) and Cook, Gelman & Rubin (2006) that use prior-sampled data.  
- Novel application of BDMC for MCMC diagnostics addressing a real need in PPLs.  
- Theoretical derivation supports that BDMC gives an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence.  
- Practical implementations in WebPPL and Stan and validation on multiple models.  
- Demonstrated practical utility by exposing a bug in WebPPL.  
- Provides a clear protocol for applying the method to real-world data.  
- Weakness: Section 3.1 is unclear—q(.) is used before being defined and should be explicitly defined (e.g., marginal of AIS after T steps).  
- Weakness: Key derivation details are pushed to the supplement; main text should include the essential steps.  
- Weakness: Unnecessary splitting of unobserved variables into z and theta; simplify to a single notation.  
- Weakness: Typographical error (missing closing parenthesis at line 165).  
- Weakness: Insufficient discussion/contrast with Geweke (2004) and Cook, Gelman & Rubin (2006); add explicit citations and discussion.  
- Overall recommendation: Significant, original, and well-supported work that meets quality criteria and should be accepted after minor clarity/citation revisions.
