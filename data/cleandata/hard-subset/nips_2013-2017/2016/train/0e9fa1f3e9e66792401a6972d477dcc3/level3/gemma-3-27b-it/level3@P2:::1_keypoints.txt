- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to evaluate MCMC quality in PPLs by upper-bounding Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Core idea: leverage bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to obtain an upper bound on the symmetrized KL divergence.  
- Strong motivation: rigorous MCMC validation is hard in black-box PPLs, and existing diagnostics lack guarantees.  
- Novelty: extends BDMC use from marginal likelihood estimation to posterior accuracy assessment.  
- Theoretical foundation: provides an expected upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence.  
- Practical implementation: integrated BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, making it accessible to practitioners.  
- Demonstrated utility: experiments show BREAD helps choose model representations and uncovered a WebPPL bug.  
- Clarity issue: derivation in Section 3.1 is hard to follow—notation q(.) used before definition; qfwd(xT) should be explicitly stated as the AIS marginal.  
- Dependence on simulated data: method requires generating data with known posterior samples, limiting real-world applicability and relying on accurate hyperparameter estimation for resimulation.  
- Limited applicability: currently applies to algorithms that are importance sampling on extended state spaces (AIS, SMC), not all MCMC methods.  
- Missing related work: should discuss prior validation-by-simulation approaches (e.g., Geweke 2004; Cook, Gelman & Rubin 2006).  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and potentially impactful paper despite clarity and scope limitations.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve derivation clarity and add related-work discussion.  
- Rating: Accept (with minor revisions).
