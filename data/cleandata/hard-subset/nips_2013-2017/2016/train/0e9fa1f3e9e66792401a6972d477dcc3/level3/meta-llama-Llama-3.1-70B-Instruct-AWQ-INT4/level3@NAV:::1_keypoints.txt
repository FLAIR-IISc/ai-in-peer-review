- Proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target for debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.  
- Idea is interesting, potentially useful, and addresses problematic existing MCMC diagnostics.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow overall, with clear explanation and good related-work review.  
- Thorough theoretical analysis and experimental results support the method.  
- Fails to adequately cite/discuss relevant prior validation work (e.g., Geweke 2004; Cook et al. 2006).  
- Section 3.1 is hard to follow: notation q(.) is used before being defined; should state q(.) is the AIS marginal after T steps.  
- Important derivation details are pushed to the supplement, making the main text harder to follow; needs a clear step-by-step application in the main text.  
- Minor issues: unnecessary splitting of unobserved variables into z and theta.  
- Minor typographical error at line 165 (missing closing parenthesis).  
- Claim that using BDMC to estimate Jeffreys divergence is novel is somewhat overstated—idea not entirely new.  
- Overall quality rated 8/10: technically sound but hampered by clarity and citation omissions.  
- Clarity rated 7/10: generally well-written but some sections and notation are unclear for non-experts.  
- Originality rated 9/10: novel application to PPLs, though parts of the idea preexist.  
- Significance rated 9/10: addresses an important problem and could have wide impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/useful method, strong theory and experiments, tackles an important PPL evaluation problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear explanations in some sections, missing prior-work citations, and minor notation/terminology issues.
