- Applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference by bounding the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Introduces Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD) and integrates it into WebPPL and Stan.  
- Validates BREAD on multiple models and datasets, showing it can bound Jeffreys divergence and diagnose inference quality.  
- Demonstrates practical uses: comparing collapsed vs. uncollapsed model representations and revealing trade-offs in efficiency vs. convergence.  
- Shows BREAD’s utility for debugging probabilistic programming implementations.  
- Clear and thorough exposition of BDMC and its extension to posterior inference evaluation.  
- Practical, working integration into widely used probabilistic programming languages.  
- Rigorous experimental validation across simulated and real-world datasets.  
- Primary weakness: limited originality, largely building on prior BDMC work without substantive new theoretical contributions.  
- Contribution is implementation/validation-focused and may be more appropriate for an MCMC workshop than a general AI conference.  
- Related work section is insufficiently comprehensive regarding alternative MCMC evaluation methods.  
- Recommendation: technically sound but not novel enough for NeurIPS; recommend rejection for this venue and submission to a specialized MCMC workshop.
