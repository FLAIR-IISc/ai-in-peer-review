- Proposes a novel method for measuring MCMC inference reliability by extending bidirectional Monte Carlo to posterior inference.  
- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and approximate samples.  
- Uses annealed importance sampling (AIS) within the bidirectional Monte Carlo framework to obtain rigorous divergence bounds.  
- Demonstrates BREAD’s effectiveness on several models and datasets, showing applicability in realistic settings.  
- Experiments are well-designed, results clearly presented, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains relation to prior work clearly without excessive technicality.  
- Theoretical analysis and experimental evidence support the authors’ claims; method is technically sound.  
- Authors provide a thorough discussion of limitations and potential future directions.  
- Strength: tackles a timely, important problem in probabilistic inference with a novel and interesting technique.  
- Potential impact: could guide inference algorithm design and evaluation of model representations.  
- Limitation: approach may be restricted to certain model classes and dataset types.  
- Concern: reliance on simulated data raises questions about generalization to diverse real-world scenarios.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
