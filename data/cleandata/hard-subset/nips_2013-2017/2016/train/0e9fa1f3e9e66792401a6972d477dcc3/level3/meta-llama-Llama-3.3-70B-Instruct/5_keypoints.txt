- Proposes BREAD, extending bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate Jeffreys divergence for MCMC-like algorithms (e.g., AIS, SMC).  
- Derives an estimator that upper-bounds in expectation the Jeffreys divergence between approximate samples and the true posterior.  
- Introduces a protocol to apply BDMC to real datasets by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Provides a rigorous and general framework for evaluating accuracy of MCMC-based posterior inference.  
- Demonstrates the method on multiple models/datasets, including matrix factorization in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Shows potential of BREAD as a debugging tool for probabilistic programming implementations.  
- Core limitation: BDMC requires an exact sample from the target, which is hard to obtain in practice.  
- Authors mitigate the exact-sample requirement with heuristics and diagnostics on synthetic-data posteriors.  
- These heuristics rely on strong, often unjustified assumptions (known representative hyperparameter; synthetic posterior similar to real posterior).  
- Assumptions may fail in challenging settings (multimodality, insufficient summary statistics), limiting applicability.  
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, reproducible for expert readers, and properly references prior work.  
- Lacks sufficiently convincing examples and simulations comparing BREAD to existing diagnostic tools.  
- Needs more evidence that the method succeeds where existing methods fail.  
- Overall: novel and promising but requires further empirical validation and weaker assumptions to be broadly useful.
