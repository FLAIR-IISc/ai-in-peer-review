- Proposes BREAD: extending BDMC to bound the symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and validates it on multiple models.
- Theoretical derivation is well-motivated and builds logically on prior BDMC work.
- Clearly positions the work relative to existing convergence diagnostics and related divergence estimation literature.
- Clear problem statement: need quantitative, rigorous measures for MCMC validation beyond heuristics.
- Practical implementation demonstrates applicability and uncovered a WebPPL bug.
- Experiments cover a reasonable range of models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization) and show the method’s utility.
- Demonstrates diagnostic use for comparing model representations (e.g., collapsed vs. uncollapsed).
- Limited novelty: adapting BDMC for divergence bounding is incremental rather than a fundamental advance.
- Requires simulated data to obtain rigorous bounds, limiting direct applicability to real-world problems.
- Proposed protocol using fitted hyperparameters to simulate data is plausible but validation is inconclusive.
- Computational cost is high (running AIS chains in both directions) and the paper lacks guidance on cost vs. benefit.
- Quartile-based visualizations may obscure central tendencies due to outliers.
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations (sensitivity to AIS intermediate choices, reverse-chain initialization from approximate posteriors).
- Arguments for acceptance: provides a rigorous MCMC evaluation tool, practical implementations, novel BDMC application, and debugging utility.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental idea, reliance on simulated data, potential prohibitive computation, and not clearly transformative.
- Overall assessment: solid and well-executed but limited novelty and practical concerns; reviewer rates Weak Accept (borderline reject).
