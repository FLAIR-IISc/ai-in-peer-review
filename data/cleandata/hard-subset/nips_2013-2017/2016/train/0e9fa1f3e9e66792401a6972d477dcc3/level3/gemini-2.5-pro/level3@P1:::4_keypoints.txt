- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to assess MCMC/AIS sample quality, building on BDMC.  
- Main theoretical result: the gap between BDMC forward and reverse bounds upper-bounds the expected Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence to the true posterior.  
- Produces a single scalar metric for quantitative evaluation of approximate inference.  
- Major limitation: reverse AIS requires an exact posterior sample, which is generally intractable for real data.  
- Authors restrict rigorous guarantees to simulated data and propose a protocol for real data: fit hyperparameters, simulate data, then run BREAD.  
- Experiments validate the protocol, showing simulated-data inference behavior closely matches real-data behavior and testing sensitivity to initialization.  
- Implemented BREAD in WebPPL and Stan and demonstrated practical uses for model-representation choices and debugging (including finding a WebPPL bug).  
- Paper is technically sound and well-supported by experiments; the Jeffreys-divergence bound is a valuable theoretical contribution.  
- Clarity issues: abrupt transition to theory (Section 3.1), dense derivations (eqs. 4–11), and insufficient explanation of notation (e.g., v and xT).  
- Presentation problems: inconsistent notation for data (y vs D) and minor typographical issues that should be fixed.  
- Originality: novel reframing of BDMC as a diagnostic for MCMC convergence; appropriately cites related and concurrent work.  
- Significance: addresses a major need for principled, quantitative MCMC diagnostics with high impact for PPL developers, MCMC researchers, and practitioners.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel principled method, thorough validation, high potential impact, and practical demonstrations.  
- Cons for acceptance: theoretical clarity needs improvement, rigorous guarantees limited to simulated data and protocol may be model-dependent, plus minor presentation issues.  
- Recommended revisions: clarify Section 3.1 with an extended-state-space explanation, explicitly highlight the Jeffreys-divergence contribution in the intro, and release accessible code/packages for WebPPL and Stan.
