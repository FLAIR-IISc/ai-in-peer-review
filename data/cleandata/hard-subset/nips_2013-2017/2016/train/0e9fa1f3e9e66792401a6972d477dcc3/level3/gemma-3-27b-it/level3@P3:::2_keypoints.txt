- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with REverse Annealing), using BDMC to upper-bound the KL between the true posterior and MCMC approximations — theoretically sound and impactful.  
- Leverages BDMC to derive an estimator for the Jeffreys divergence, refining Grosse et al. (2015) and providing a rigorous quantitative measure of inference quality.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, increasing accessibility and practical utility for practitioners.  
- Validates the method on diverse models and datasets, showing consistency between simulated and real-data behavior.  
- Demonstrates practical value by uncovering a bug in WebPPL, highlighting BREAD’s use as a testing tool.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates the work within MCMC diagnostics, marginal likelihood estimation, and probabilistic programming literature; acknowledges concurrent related work.  
- Mathematical derivations are dense but necessary; helpful appendices and code snippets are provided.  
- Main limitation: reverse AIS requires sampling from the true posterior, restricting direct application to simulated-data scenarios.  
- Proposed protocol for real data (using fitted hyperparameters and simulated data) is reasonable but depends on accurate hyperparameter estimation and representative simulations.  
- Needs further investigation into robustness under model misspecification and exploration of other divergence/error measures beyond Jeffreys divergence.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, novelty, significance, practical impact, and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reliance on simulated data limits direct real-world applicability; mathematical complexity may hinder accessibility for some readers.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-validated contribution to Bayesian inference reliability; recommendation — Accept.
