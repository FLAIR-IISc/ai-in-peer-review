- Proposes BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to evaluate MCMC-based inference in PPLs by extending BDMC to quantify sampler performance.
- Core idea: run AIS forward and reverse on simulated data to derive an estimator that upper-bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence in expectation.
- Method requires exact posterior samples, so is primarily applicable to simulated data.
- Proposes a protocol to apply the method to real datasets by fitting hyperparameters and generating simulated data from the posterior predictive.
- Implemented and validated in Stan and WebPPL.
- Strength: provides a principled, quantitative measure for MCMC diagnostics compared to heuristic existing methods.
- Strength: theoretical connection between BDMC bound gap and Jeffreys divergence is novel and elegant.
- Strength: experiments are compelling and practically useful (e.g., comparing Stan vs WebPPL representations) and BREAD found a bug in WebPPL.
- Weakness: related work discussion is incomplete; should cite prior validation techniques (e.g., Geweke 2004; Cook, Gelman, Rubin 2006).
- Weakness: Section 3.1 lacks clarity—qfwd(xT) used before definition and key derivation details are relegated to the supplement, making the main text hard to follow.
- Minor issues: cluttered notation from splitting always-paired latent variables into z and theta; typographical error (missing closing parenthesis) around line 165.
- Overall: high-quality paper with significant contribution; weaknesses are addressable through revision.
