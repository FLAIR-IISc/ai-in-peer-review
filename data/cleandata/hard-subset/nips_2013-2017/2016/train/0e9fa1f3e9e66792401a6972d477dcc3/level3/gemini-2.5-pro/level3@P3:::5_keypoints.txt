- Proposes extending BDMC into a diagnostic: the gap between BDMC stochastic lower/upper bounds is an expected upper bound on Jeffreys divergence between approximate and true posterior.  
- Criterion for comparing MCMC samplers: given equal compute, the sampler with the smallest BDMC bound gap is superior.  
- Core challenge: BDMC’s reverse procedure requires an exact posterior sample, which is infeasible on real data.  
- Introduces BREAD protocol: estimate hyperparameters via MCMC on real data, generate synthetic data from that estimate, and run BDMC on synthetic data using an approximate posterior to initialize the reverse chain.  
- Implemented in Stan and WebPPL and applied to compare model parameterizations and debug samplers for matrix factorization.  

Strengths:
- Novel and theoretically grounded link between BDMC bound gap and Jeffreys divergence.  
- Practical potential demonstrated by implementations and experiments, useful for model comparison and sampler debugging.

Weaknesses:
- BREAD depends on an initial MCMC run having already converged to produce η_real, creating circular reliance on a successful inference.  
- Assumes posterior geometry on synthetic data matches that on real data, which is unjustified under model misspecification and may fail for complex posteriors.  
- Initialization of the reverse chain from η_real after a few MCMC steps is optimistic and may produce biased samples if mixing is slow.  
- Empirical validation is limited to simple models and does not demonstrate robustness on high-dimensional, multimodal, or funnel-shaped posteriors.

Qualitative evaluation:
- Technical derivation is sound given the exact-sample assumption, but the practical protocol rests on questionable assumptions.  
- Paper is clear and well-organized, with effective background and derivations.  
- The idea is original, building creatively on prior BDMC work.  
- Significance is limited by practical flaws; users may not trust a diagnostic that requires the problem to be “easy enough.”

Recommendations:
- Provide justification or theoretical/empirical evidence that MCMC behavior on synthetic data mirrors real-data behavior, especially under misspecification.  
- Study sensitivity of results to the choice and uncertainty of η_real, including multimodal or diffuse hyperparameter posteriors.  
- Run harder experiments on known difficult posteriors and compare BREAD directly to standard diagnostics (R-hat, ESS) to demonstrate added value.
