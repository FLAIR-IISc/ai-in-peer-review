This paper proposes a method for measuring the reliability of Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference using Bidirectional Monte Carlo. The authors extend the recently introduced Bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference algorithms. They show that by running annealed importance sampling (AIS) chains both from prior to posterior and vice versa on simulated data, they can upper bound in expectation the symmetrized KL divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples.

The authors relate their work to previous research in the field of probabilistic inference, including the diagnosis of Markov chain convergence and the automatic configuration of MCMC algorithms. They also compare their method to other approaches, such as the use of thermodynamic integration and the estimation of the quality of a set of approximate samples.

The paper is well-motivated and presents a useful method with reasonably compelling experiments. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method on several models and datasets, including linear regression, logistic regression, matrix factorization, autoregressive time series modeling, and mixture-of-Gaussians clustering. They also show that their method can be used to guide the design of inference algorithms and to debug implementations.

However, there are some issues with the presentation of the paper. Section 3, especially the transition into 3.1, is hard to follow, and variable and equation definitions are introduced without sufficient motivation. The divergence bound in Section 3.1 appears to be a central contribution, but it is not explicitly stated as a novel result. Additionally, the term "AutoBDMC" is not explicitly defined, and the manuscript refers to challenges "for AutoBDMC" before it is clearly defined.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors provide a clear and concise abstract that effectively summarizes the main contributions of the paper. The introduction provides a good motivation for the research and clearly states the problem that the authors are trying to solve. The background section provides a good overview of the relevant research in the field, and the methods section clearly describes the proposed method.

The experiments section is well-organized and effectively demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method. The results are clearly presented, and the authors provide a good discussion of the implications of their findings. The related work section provides a good comparison to other approaches in the field, and the authors clearly state the contributions of their paper.

In terms of the originality of the paper, the authors propose a new method for measuring the reliability of MCMC inference using Bidirectional Monte Carlo. The method is novel and provides a useful contribution to the field of probabilistic inference. The authors also provide a good comparison to other approaches in the field and clearly state the advantages of their method.

In terms of the significance of the paper, the authors provide a method that can be used to evaluate the accuracy of posterior inference in probabilistic programming languages. The method has the potential to be widely used in the field of probabilistic inference and could have a significant impact on the development of more accurate and reliable inference algorithms.

Overall, I would rate the quality of the paper as 8/10, the originality as 9/10, and the significance as 9/10.
