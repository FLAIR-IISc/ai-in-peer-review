- Proposal: apply bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between an MCMC sampler and the target distribution for debugging/evaluating MCMC in PPLs.  
- Motivation: potentially useful because existing MCMC diagnostics are problematic.  
- Contribution: clear explanation of BDMC technique and its application to bounding the Jeffreys divergence.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on Grosse et al. (2015) and extends BDMC to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference algorithms.  
- Related work: discusses MCMC diagnostics and automatic MCMC configuration.  
- Readability issue: Section 3.1 is hard to follow; notation q(.) is used before being defined.  
- Notation suggestion: explicitly state q(.) is the AIS marginal after T steps.  
- Presentation issue: important derivation details are pushed to the supplement; main text needs a step-by-step application.  
- Notational simplification: splitting unobserved variables into z and theta seems unnecessary since they appear together.  
- Minor error: typographical mistake at line 165 (missing closing parenthesis).  
- Originality: novel application of BDMC to evaluate MCMC in PPLs—technique isn’t new but application is significant.  
- Organization: paper is well-organized but notation/terminology could be improved for non-experts.  
- Results: significant and demonstrate method effectiveness in realistic settings.  
- Quality: technically sound; claims supported by theory and experiments; authors are careful and honest.  
- Recommendations: clarify notation/terminology, move key derivations into the main text, and fix the typo.  
- Verdict: overall positive; arguments for and against acceptance provided; rating 8/10.
