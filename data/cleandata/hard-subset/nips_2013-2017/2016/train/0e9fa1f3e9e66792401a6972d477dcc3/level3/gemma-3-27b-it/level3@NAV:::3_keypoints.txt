- Proposes using the gap between BDMC stochastic lower and upper bounds as a general performance measure for MCMC transition operators.
- Provides BDMC implementations in Stan and WebPPL and a protocol for evaluating posterior inference quality via hyperparameter estimation and simulated-data validation.
- Core idea is simple and potentially impactful for assessing black-box inference systems.
- Strength: practical contributions lower adoption barrier and offer a reasonable real-world evaluation protocol despite requiring exact posterior samples.
- Novelty is modest; the Jensen–Shannon divergence observation is interesting but not a major theoretical advance.
- Major related-work omission: no discussion of Radford Neal’s 1996 Tempered Transitions, which is closely related to BDMC and annealing interpretations.
- Under-discusses the choice of β_t (Δβ) in the annealing schedule and its impact on bounds; needs systematic exploration and possibly iterative tightening schemes.
- “AutoBDMC” is vaguely defined—unclear whether it denotes a convergence criterion based on the bound gap or the fixed hyperparameter scheme from Section 3.2.
- Sections 3.1–3.2 lack clarity: vector v is loosely defined and terminology (rev/fwd vs for/back) is inconsistent and confusing.
- The estimator \hat{p}(y) used in equations 5–7 and 9–11 is never defined, preventing proper understanding of derivations.
- Equation (6) appears incorrect as written, showing identical arguments in the KL divergence terms.
- Experimental section lacks detail on practical β_t choices and contains ambiguous figure labels (e.g., “HMC / No-U-Turn steps”).
- Minor issues: undefined acronyms (RAIS), typos, and other polish problems that detract from readability.
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance if authors fix clarity issues, expand related work (include Neal 1996), and provide a more thorough exploration of the β schedule and AutoBDMC.
