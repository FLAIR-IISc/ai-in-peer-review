- Proposes "AutoBDMC" using bidirectional Monte Carlo (AIS + reverse AIS) to give stochastic upper/lower bounds on log marginal likelihood.  
- Key theoretical claim: the gap between bounds upper-bounds the Jensen–Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate sample distribution, yielding a single scalar convergence metric.  
- Validated on synthetic data and shown that synthetic analyses predict real-data behavior.  
- Demonstrated practical applications for model selection and debugging in WebPPL and Stan.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, useful method with compelling experiments and clear practical utility in probabilistic programming.  
- Useful for identifying optimal model representations (e.g., collapsed vs. uncollapsed factorization).  
- Major clarity issues: "AutoBDMC" is never explicitly defined and is referenced before definition.  
- Section 3.1 is hard to follow—insufficient motivation for variable definitions and no explicit statement about the novelty of the divergence bound.  
- Minor issues: possible typo on Line 149 and inconsistent data notation (y vs D).  
- Limitation: primarily synthetic-data experiments, though real-data validation protocol is reasonably justified.  
- Suggestion: supplemental code needs clearer documentation and should be released as npm-installable packages for WebPPL and Stan.  
- Overall: meaningful contribution to MCMC diagnostics but requires substantial exposition revisions before publication.
