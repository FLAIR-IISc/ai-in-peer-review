- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to upper bound the symmetrized KL divergence between the true posterior and approximate MCMC sample distributions (AIS/SMC).  
- Integrates BDMC-based posterior assessment into WebPPL and Stan with working implementations.  
- Validates the method empirically on several models, demonstrating its functionality.  
- Provides clear background and connections to AIS, stochastic log-partition bounds, and prior BDMC work.  
- Appropriately situates the contribution relative to existing MCMC diagnostics and sample-quality measures.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-written, useful for evaluating and debugging probabilistic programming systems.  
- The main novelty is limited: approach is a straightforward extension of existing BDMC techniques.  
- Primary contribution is implementation and application rather than new theoretical or methodological advances.  
- Experiments largely validate known theory instead of showing improvements in inference accuracy or efficiency.  
- The discovered WebPPL bug is an interesting implementation artifact but not a core scientific contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: functional multi-language implementation, clear exposition, practical debugging/evaluation use case.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited originality, focus on implementation over new methods, experimental results are mainly confirmatory.  
- Overall recommendation: technically solid but incremental; better suited to a specialized workshop than the main conference—recommend rejection with encouragement to submit to a more focused venue.
