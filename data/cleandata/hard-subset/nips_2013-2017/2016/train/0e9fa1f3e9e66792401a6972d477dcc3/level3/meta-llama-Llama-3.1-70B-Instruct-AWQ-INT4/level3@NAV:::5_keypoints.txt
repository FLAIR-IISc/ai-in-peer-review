- Proposes using Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior and approximate samples from AIS/SMC.  
- Shows BMC yields an estimator that upper-bounds in expectation the Jeffreys divergence of approximate samples.  
- Provides a technique to compute the true Jeffreys divergence and the upper bound exactly on small examples, finding the bound often matches in practice.  
- Proposes a protocol for evaluating inference on real datasets by simulating data from fitted hyperparameters.  
- Implements the method (BREAD) in WebPPL and Stan and validates it across multiple probabilistic models.  
- Paper is well-written and clear, with solid theoretical foundations and impressive experimental results.  
- Major assumptions: synthetic-data posterior has similar MCMC convergence to the real posterior, and starting from true hyperparameters yields near-exact MCMC samples—both may be unjustified.  
- These assumptions can undermine the diagnostic’s validity under model misspecification, multimodality, or poor summary statistics.  
- Empirical evidence is limited; simple examples (Section 5.1) are not convincing for more complex settings.  
- Novelty: extends BMC/AIS to bound Jeffreys divergence and proposes a new real-data evaluation protocol.  
- Recommendation: address listed limitations, provide more extensive robustness experiments, and explain behavior under multimodality/bad summary-statistic cases.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
