- Proposes BREAD: using BDMC to upper bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples.  
- Leverages AIS/SMC and extends BDMC to assess approximate posterior quality.  
- Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practical applicability and model-comparison use.  
- Provides a theoretically grounded diagnostic as an alternative to heuristic MCMC convergence checks.  
- Uses simulated-data protocol where hyperparameters are estimated from initial MCMC runs to obtain an “exact” sample.  
- Strength: connects BDMC to MCMC diagnostics and offers a quantitative bound on divergence.  
- Strength: integration into popular probabilistic programming languages increases accessibility and impact.  
- Major weakness: relies on hyperparameters from initial MCMC runs, creating a circular assumption that those runs have converged.  
- Major weakness: assumes simulated data behavior reflects real data despite potential model misspecification, with limited justification.  
- Major weakness: optimistic assumption that MCMC can quickly converge to an almost exact sample from estimated hyperparameters lacks validation.  
- Empirical validation is limited to simple examples and insufficient to demonstrate performance on complex/high-dimensional models.  
- Focuses mainly on marginal likelihood, giving an incomplete picture of posterior quality (issues with multimodality and dependencies).  
- Paper does not adequately compare to established diagnostics or address limitations under multimodality and misspecification.  
- Overall recommendation: reject; requires stronger justification of assumptions, broader empirical validation, and comparisons to standard methods.
