- Paper proposes using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound Jeffreys divergence between approximate MCMC posterior samples and the true posterior, extending BDMC to evaluate MCMC transition operators.  
- Authors implement the method in WebPPL and Stan and propose a protocol for assessing inference quality on real data.  
- Core idea builds on Grosse et al. (2015); applying it to MCMC inference quality is interesting but technically modest.  
- Main strength: simple, easy-to-implement method with potential high impact for probabilistic programming practitioners.  
- Claim of a Jensen–Shannon divergence bound is not clearly novel.  
- Paper fails to cite Radford Neal’s 1996 Tempered Transitions, which is closely related and should be discussed.  
- Critical omission: inadequate discussion of β schedule (Δβ), despite its major effect on bounds and comparisons.  
- The meaning and role of “auto” in AutoBDMC remain ambiguous and under-explained.  
- Sections 3.1–3.2 are rushed and contain notation ambiguities (e.g., undefined vector v, inconsistent labels “rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”).  
- Equation (6) appears incorrect (KL arguments identical), and the estimator \hat{p}(y) is never defined, making derivations hard to follow.  
- Experimental section lacks details on β_t selection; figures have small axes and undefined acronyms like RAIS.  
- The proposed real-data evaluation protocol is valuable but marred by presentation and technical omissions.  
- Required revisions: systematically address β schedule, clarify AutoBDMC, fix equation and notation errors, define estimators, and properly contextualize against Neal (1996).  
- Current recommendation: reject unless major revisions address the above concerns; with fixes the work could make a meaningful contribution.
