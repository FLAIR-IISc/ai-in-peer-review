This paper proposes AutoBDMC, a method for assessing MCMC convergence using bidirectional Monte Carlo to estimate stochastic upper and lower bounds on the log marginal likelihood. The authors show that the gap between these bounds upper-bounds the Jensen-Shannon divergence between the true posterior and approximate sample distribution, providing a single scalar convergence metric.

The paper is well-motivated and readable, and the authors provide a useful method with reasonably compelling experiments. The technique is limited to analysis on synthetic data sampled from the joint, but the authors provide experiments showing that synthetic-data analyses can predict real-data behavior.

The method has additional uses for model selection and debugging, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness in these areas. However, there are some minor issues with the paper, such as a potential typo on Line 149 and inconsistent notation for data.

The supplemental WebPPL code needs to be released as an npm-installable WebPPL package, and the Stan code should also be released. Additionally, the authors should state precisely what AutoBDMC denotes, as it is not explicitly defined in the manuscript.

In terms of the conference guidelines, the paper meets the criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance. The method is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The paper is clearly written and well-organized, and it adequately informs the reader.

The method is original and builds upon previous work in a novel way. The authors provide a useful combination of familiar techniques, and it is clear how this work differs from previous contributions. The related work is adequately referenced, and the authors provide a unique theoretical or pragmatic approach.

The results are important, and other people are likely to use these ideas or build upon them. The paper addresses a difficult problem in a better way than previous research and advances the state of the art in a demonstrable way.
