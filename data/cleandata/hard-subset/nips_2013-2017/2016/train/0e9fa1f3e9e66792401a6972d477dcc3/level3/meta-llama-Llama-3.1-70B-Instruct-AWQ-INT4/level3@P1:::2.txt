This paper proposes a novel method, Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing (BREAD), to evaluate the reliability of Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) inference using bidirectional Monte Carlo. The method extends the recently introduced bidirectional Monte Carlo technique to estimate the symmetrized KL divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples.

The authors provide a clear and concise background on MCMC inference, annealed importance sampling (AIS), and bidirectional Monte Carlo. They also discuss the limitations of existing methods for diagnosing convergence in MCMC algorithms and the challenges of evaluating the quality of approximate posterior samples.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of BREAD, which uses bidirectional Monte Carlo to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior distribution and the distribution of approximate samples. The authors provide a rigorous analysis of the method, including a derivation of the bias of the stochastic lower bound on the log partition function ratio. They also discuss the application of BREAD to real-world data, including a protocol for using the method to diagnose inference quality on real-world data.

The paper also presents experiments that validate the effectiveness of BREAD in evaluating the accuracy of posterior inference in realistic settings. The authors extend both WebPPL and Stan to run forward and reverse AIS using the sequence of distributions defined in the paper. They also apply BREAD to investigate the choice of model representation in Stan and WebPPL, demonstrating its potential usefulness in guiding modeling and algorithmic decisions.
