- Proposes extending Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference and integrates it into WebPPL and Stan.  
- Provides a way to bound the Jeffreys divergence between the true posterior and approximate sample distribution.  
- Well-motivated and clearly explains the problem of evaluating approximate posterior samples.  
- Method is simple, easy to implement, and potentially high-impact for probabilistic programming systems.  
- Technical contributions are modest and the novelty of the approach is not clearly articulated.  
- The Jensen–Shannon divergence bound is noted as nice but its novelty is uncertain.  
- Fails to cite/discuss Radford Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996), which is closely related.  
- Sections 3.1–3.2 suffer from clarity issues, rushed exposition, ambiguous notation, and inconsistent labels.  
- The estimator \hat{p}(y) is not clearly defined, making derivations hard to follow.  
- Experiments lack detail, including unclear selection of β_t values and poorly labeled figures with small axes/ticks.  
- Related work discussion is limited and does not clearly distinguish the proposed approach from existing methods for evaluating MCMC.  
- Overall, interesting ideas but requires significant improvements in clarity, novelty discussion, related work, and experimental details.  
- Acceptance arguments: new practical evaluation method, ease of implementation, and interesting experimental comparisons.  
- Rejection arguments: modest technical novelty, clarity and notation problems, incomplete experiments, and weak related-work coverage.  
- Recommended revisions: clarify novelty and related work, fix sections 3.1–3.2 and notation, define \hat{p}(y), detail experimental choices (β_t), and improve figure readability.
