- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC-based posterior inference, providing a rigorous framework for bounding symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence.  
- Derives the bias of the stochastic lower bound and an upper bound on the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate sample distribution.  
- Method is technically sound with theoretical analysis supporting the claims.  
- Integration into popular probabilistic programming platforms (WebPPL and Stan) improves accessibility and practical impact.  
- Experiments on simulated and real-world datasets demonstrate effectiveness and validate the method in realistic settings.  
- Authors provide a clear, well-written exposition and thorough discussion of limitations and challenges.  
- Offers a general procedure for monitoring posterior inference quality useful to non-expert users.  
- Limitation: applicability restricted to certain algorithms (e.g., AIS, SMC) and not all MCMC variants.  
- Limitation: the BDMC evaluation protocol on real-world datasets may not always yield representative results.  
- Overall recommendation: accept due to strong contribution, sound methodology, and broad relevance to probabilistic inference.
