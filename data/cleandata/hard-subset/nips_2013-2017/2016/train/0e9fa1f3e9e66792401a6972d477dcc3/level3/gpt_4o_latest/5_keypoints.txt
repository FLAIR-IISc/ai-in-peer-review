- Contribution: Extends BDMC to evaluate MCMC posterior inference by introducing BREAD, which bounds the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between approximate and true posteriors.
- Implementation: Integrates BREAD into WebPPL and Stan and validates it on several models and datasets.
- Heuristic: Proposes using synthetic-data-based approximation of posterior samples to apply BREAD to real-world datasets.
- Strength — Novelty/Relevance: Addresses the important problem of measuring MCMC sample quality, a meaningful advance for Bayesian inference and PPLs.
- Strength — Integration/Validation: Practical applicability demonstrated through PPL integrations and experiments that diagnose inference quality.
- Strength — Scientific Insights: Provides insight into trade-offs between model representations, aiding modeling and algorithmic decisions.
- Strength — Debugging Utility: Can identify implementation bugs, showing value as a diagnostic tool for probabilistic programming systems.
- Weakness — Strong Assumptions: Relies on access to a representative hyperparameter (η_real) and assumes synthetic-data posteriors mirror real posterior convergence, which may fail under misspecification or poor priors.
- Weakness — Limited Applicability: Tailored to AIS/SMC samplers and requires exact posterior samples, limiting generalizability to other MCMC methods and real data.
- Weakness — Insufficient Evidence: Experiments are limited in diversity and do not convincingly demonstrate robustness on complex, multimodal, or high-dimensional posteriors.
- Weakness — Lack of Direct Comparisons: Does not present direct evidence that BREAD outperforms existing diagnostic tools in challenging scenarios.
- Weakness — Experimental Gaps: Missing simulations comparing BREAD with standard diagnostics weakens claims of advantage.
- Pros: Novelly tackles MCMC diagnostics, practical PPL integration, and provides actionable debugging/modeling insights.
- Cons: Restrictive assumptions and synthetic-data reliance reduce real-world applicability; validation scope and comparative analysis are insufficient.
- Recommendation: Weak reject; encourage stronger experimental validation, broader benchmarks, and direct comparisons with existing diagnostic methods before resubmission.
