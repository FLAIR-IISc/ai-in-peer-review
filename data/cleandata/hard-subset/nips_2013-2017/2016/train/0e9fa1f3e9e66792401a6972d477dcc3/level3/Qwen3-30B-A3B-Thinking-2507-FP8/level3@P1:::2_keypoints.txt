- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to bound the Jeffreys (symmetrized KL) divergence between true posterior and MCMC approximations.
- Extends Bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to provide rigorous upper bounds on divergence using forward and reverse AIS.
- Shows the gap between forward and reverse AIS log marginal likelihood estimates upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence in expectation.
- Main contributions: (1) estimator that upper-bounds Jeffreys divergence in expectation; (2) accuracy demonstrated on toy examples.
- Main contributions cont.: (3) protocol for applying BREAD to real data by simulating from fitted hyperparameters; (4) integrations into WebPPL and Stan.
- Experimental validation is comprehensive, covering five models and showing consistency between simulated and real-data protocols.
- Practical impact demonstrated: guides modeling decisions (e.g., collapsed vs uncollapsed representations) and uncovered a WebPPL Gaussian-sampling bug.
- Theoretical derivation (Section 3.1) is rigorous and correctly extends prior BDMC analysis.
- Paper clarity is excellent with clear background, derivations, experimental setup, and reproducibility details.
- Originality is strong: novel application of BDMC to posterior inference bounds, distinct from heuristic convergence diagnostics.
- Discusses related work appropriately, including differences from Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka and classical diagnostics/configuration methods.
- Primary limitation: requires exact posterior samples (i.e., simulated-data settings); real-data protocol via fitted hyperparameters is an approximation.
- Validation suggests the fitted-hyperparameter approximation has negligible practical impact, making the limitation manageable.
- Overall verdict: significant, well-executed contribution with strong theoretical and practical value; recommendation for acceptance.
