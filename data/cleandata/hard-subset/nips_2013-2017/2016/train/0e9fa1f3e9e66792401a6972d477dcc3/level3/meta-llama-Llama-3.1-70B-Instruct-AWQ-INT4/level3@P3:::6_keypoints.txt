- Paper applies bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to evaluate MCMC and extends BDMC to estimate the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and MCMC samples.
- Implementation integrated into two probabilistic programming systems: WebPPL and Stan, with validation on several models and datasets.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of background, methodology, and prior BDMC work.
- Provides a thorough analysis of method strengths, weaknesses, and limitations.
- Includes detailed descriptions of experiments that aid reproducibility and understanding of results.
- Technically sound and methodologically careful.
- Main contribution is an extension of existing BDMC results rather than a novel methodological advance.
- Results are not particularly surprising, insightful, or impactful across evaluated models and datasets.
- Lacks comparisons to other methods for evaluating MCMC, limiting assessment of relative strengths and weaknesses.
- Clarity is generally good but sections may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with BDMC.
- Recommendation: acceptable for workshop or conference proceedings but not for a top-tier journal due to limited originality and significance.
