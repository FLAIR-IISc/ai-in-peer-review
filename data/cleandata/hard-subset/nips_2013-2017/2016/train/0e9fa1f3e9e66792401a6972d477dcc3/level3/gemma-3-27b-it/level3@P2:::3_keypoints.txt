- Proposes BREAD: bounding symmetrized KL (Jeffreys) divergence for MCMC via reverse annealing to assess inference reliability.  
- Extends BDMC by running AIS both prior→posterior and posterior→prior to upper-bound divergence and quantify MCMC accuracy.  
- Implemented in WebPPL and Stan, demonstrating practicality and integration with probabilistic programming systems.  
- Experiments show BREAD can guide modeling choices (e.g., collapsed vs. uncollapsed representations) and uncovered a WebPPL bug.  
- Conceptually simple and potentially impactful for evaluating “black box” MCMC without relying solely on standard convergence diagnostics.  
- Technical contribution is incremental; using BDMC gap as a performance measure is not entirely novel.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete, notably missing Neal’s Tempered Transitions (1996) and other relevant literature.  
- Critical parameters like the annealing schedule (β_t) and step size (Δβ) are under-discussed; their effect on bound tightness needs systematic study.  
- Presentation is unclear in places (Sections 3.1–3.2), with rushed exposition and ambiguous definitions (e.g., vector v).  
- Notation is inconsistent (e.g., “rev”/“fwd” vs “for”/“back”), and key estimator \hat{p}(y) is never defined, impeding understanding.  
- Equation (6) appears incorrect as written, and the term “AutoBDMC” is ambiguously described.  
- Experimental details are insufficient: how β_t is chosen, interpretation of HMC/No-U-Turn steps, undefined acronyms (RAIS), and small/unclear figures.  
- Minor errors and typos (missing parenthesis in Jeffreys divergence, misspelling of Hoffman) reduce polish.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical tool, successful implementations, useful for modeling decisions, found real software bug.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited novelty, unclear presentation, incomplete related work, and insufficient experimental thoroughness.  
- Overall recommendation: promising idea but currently a reject pending major revisions addressing clarity, related work, and experimental detail.
