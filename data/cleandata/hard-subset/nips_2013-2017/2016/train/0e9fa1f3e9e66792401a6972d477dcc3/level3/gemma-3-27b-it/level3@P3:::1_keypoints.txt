- Proposes BREAD, a novel method using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound the Jeffreys divergence between MCMC output and the true posterior.  
- Strong motivation: existing MCMC diagnostics often fail, especially in black-box probabilistic programming systems.  
- Extends BDMC (GGA15) beyond marginal likelihood estimation to provide an upper-bounding estimator for Jeffreys divergence.  
- Demonstrates accuracy on toy distributions and gives a protocol for real-data application via hyperparameter estimation and simulated data.  
- Practical integration into WebPPL and Stan, showing feasibility and discovering a WebPPL Gaussian sampling bug.  
- Correctly situates the work relative to Neal’s AIS and prior BDMC literature, and distinguishes itself from methods needing strong convexity assumptions (e.g., GM15).  
- Reviewer recommends citing and discussing Geweke (2004) and Cook, Gelman & Rubin (2006) as related validation approaches using prior predictive checks.  
- Clarifies connection to Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (CTM16) and credits foundational work appropriately.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid and validation on toy problems is encouraging.  
- Major clarity issue: Section 3.1 is hard to follow; q(.) is used before definition and should be explicitly stated as the AIS marginal after T steps.  
- Overreliance on the supplement for derivations makes the main text less self-contained and hinders accessibility and reproducibility.  
- Suggests a more step-by-step explanation of how to apply BREAD for readers unfamiliar with BDMC.  
- Notational nitpick: splitting unobserved variables into z and θ is unnecessary—use a single variable (e.g., x) to simplify.  
- Minor typographical error noted (missing closing parenthesis on line 165).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, significance, soundness, and demonstrated practicality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity and incomplete discussion of related validation literature.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, completeness, and related-work citation.
