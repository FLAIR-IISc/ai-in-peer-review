- Proposal: use the gap between BDMC stochastic lower and upper bounds as a generic performance measure for MCMC inference.  
- Theory: authors show the BDMC gap provides an upper bound on the Jensen–Shannon divergence between approximate and true posteriors.  
- Implementation: implementations provided for Stan and WebPPL.  
- Strength: method is simple, theoretically grounded, and potentially high-impact for diagnosing sampler quality.  
- Practicality: integrating into popular frameworks and a protocol for fitting hyperparameters makes the approach usable on real data.  
- Novelty concern: technical novelty is modest—approach is a direct application of existing BDMC/AIS ideas.  
- Missing prior work: important related work (e.g., Neal 1996 on Tempered Transitions) is omitted.  
- Clarity issues: Sections 3.1–3.2 are rushed and ambiguous, hindering comprehension.  
- Undefined terms/notation: key terms like “AutoBDMC”, vector v, and the estimator \hat{p}(y) are not clearly defined.  
- Possible error: equation (6) appears incorrect as written.  
- Experimental weaknesses: sparse experimental details, especially choice of annealing schedule (β_t), which critically affects results.  
- Presentation problems: figures have small labels and undefined acronyms, making them hard to interpret.  
- Overall verdict: valuable idea but manuscript needs substantial revision on clarity, literature grounding, and experimental rigor before publication.
