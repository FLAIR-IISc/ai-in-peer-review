- Proposes BREAD: using bidirectional Monte Carlo (BMC) to bound the Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and approximate MCMC samples by estimating log marginal likelihoods.  
- Well-motivated problem statement with clear explanation of MCMC evaluation challenges.  
- The approach is theoretically justified and the BMC technique is clearly described.  
- Demonstrates BREAD on several models (linear/logistic regression, matrix factorization, autoregressive time series, mixture-of-Gaussians).  
- Shows BREAD can validate consistency between real and simulated-data inference and assess effects of approximate posterior samples.  
- Novel contribution: applying BMC to bound divergences for MCMC reliability evaluation.  
- Potentially significant impact for probabilistic programming and Bayesian inference evaluation.  
- Major limitation: assumes synthetic-data posterior has similar MCMC convergence properties to the original posterior (unjustified, especially under model misspecification).  
- Major limitation: assumes starting from a known representative hyperparameter η_real yields an almost exact MCMC sample (optimistic and unverified).  
- Empirical evidence is limited and mostly on simple examples; generalization to complex settings is unclear.  
- Builds on prior BMC work but needs more context and citations to related literature.  
- Recommendation: provide more extensive experiments on complex/realistic settings.  
- Recommendation: explicitly address and justify convergence and initialization assumptions.  
- Recommendation: add more references and discussion of prior work on BMC and MCMC evaluation.
