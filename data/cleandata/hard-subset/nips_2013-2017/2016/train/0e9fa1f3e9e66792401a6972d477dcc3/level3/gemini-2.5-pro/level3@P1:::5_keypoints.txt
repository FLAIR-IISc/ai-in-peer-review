- Proposes BREAD: extends BDMC to form an estimator that upper-bounds the Jeffreys divergence between AIS approximate posterior and true posterior.  
- Protocol for real data: run approximate inference to get hyperparameters, simulate data from the model, run BREAD on synthetic data where exact posterior samples are available.  
- Implemented in WebPPL and Stan; used to compare parameterizations and debug a WebPPL bug.  

Strengths:
- Novel theoretical link between the BDMC log-likelihood gap and an upper bound on Jeffreys divergence.  
- Potentially significant: could provide a principled, quantitative diagnostic for MCMC in probabilistic programming.  
- Paper is clear and well-written; key derivations are easy to follow.  

Weaknesses — methodological:
- Circularity: protocol requires successful approximate inference on real data to estimate hyperparameters, undermining its role as a diagnostic.  
- Unjustified assumption that synthetic-data posterior convergence mirrors real-data posterior convergence, vulnerable to dataset-specific geometry and model misspecification.  
- Relies on short MCMC runs to produce approximate posterior samples for reverse AIS, assuming good mixing where mixing is under question.  

Weaknesses — empirical:
- Empirical validation limited to five standard, well-behaved models and does not test challenging multimodal or highly correlated posteriors.  
- Fails to demonstrate robustness in scenarios where core assumptions are most likely violated (multimodality, misspecification).  
- No empirical comparison to standard diagnostics (e.g., R-hat, ESS) to show complementary or superior performance.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles an important problem (reliable MCMC diagnosis).  
- Presents an interesting and original theoretical contribution.  
- Practical demonstration (debugging WebPPL) shows potential utility.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Real-data protocol depends on strong, unverified assumptions that compromise its rigor.  
- Limited and unconvincing empirical evaluation for hard inference problems.  
- Lacks comparisons to existing diagnostics to justify added value.  

Recommended revisions:
- Explicitly acknowledge and analyze the circularity in the real-data protocol.  
- Provide stronger evidence or arguments that synthetic-data inference is a reliable proxy for real-data inference, addressing multimodality and misspecification.  
- Evaluate on more challenging models and compare BREAD against R-hat and ESS to demonstrate relative strengths and weaknesses.
