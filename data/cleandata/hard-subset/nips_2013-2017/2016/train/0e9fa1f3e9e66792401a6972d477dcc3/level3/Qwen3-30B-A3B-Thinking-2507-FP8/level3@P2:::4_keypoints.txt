- Introduces BREAD (Bounding Divergences with Reverse Annealing) to measure quality of MCMC posterior samples.
- Extends bidirectional Monte Carlo (BDMC) to bound Jeffreys divergence between true posterior and AIS/SMC approximate samples.
- Key insight: gap between forward and reverse AIS chains upper-bounds expected Jeffreys divergence, giving a single scalar metric.
- Builds on Grosse et al. (2015) and Cusumano-Towner & Mansinghka (2016) but novelly applies BDMC to bound divergence for MCMC convergence assessment.
- Theoretical derivation of the Jeffreys divergence bound is correct and meaningful.
- Experimental validation across multiple models in WebPPL and Stan is thorough and convincing.
- Synthetic-data analysis predicts real-data behavior; method aids model selection and debugging.
- Practical impact demonstrated by revealing a bug in WebPPL and guiding representation choices.
- Provides a practical protocol for applying the method to real-world data but requires simulated data as a limitation.
- Paper is generally well-written and organized but Section 3.1 is hard to follow and abruptly presented.
- Terminology and notation issues: "AutoBDMC" used before definition, inconsistent data notation (y vs D), and a potential typo in the posterior expression.
- Limited discussion of failure modes or situations where the method might mislead.
- Pro arguments: rigorous general method, applied to major PPLs, practical utility, strong experimental validation, fills a significant gap.
- Con arguments: poor exposition in theory section, undefined/inconsistent terms, notation errors, limited failure-mode discussion.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Section 3, define "AutoBDMC" early, and standardize notation.
