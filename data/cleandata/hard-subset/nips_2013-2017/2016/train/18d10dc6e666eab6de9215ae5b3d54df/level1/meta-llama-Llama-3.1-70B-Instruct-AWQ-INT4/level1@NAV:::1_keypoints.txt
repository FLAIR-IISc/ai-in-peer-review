- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function measuring information gain for evaluating q points, derived via decision-theoretic analysis.  
- Introduces an efficient Bayes-optimal batch computation strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with consistent notation.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis showing technical soundness of q-KG.  
- Experimental results show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art methods in both noise-free and noisy settings on several synthetic and practical ML problems.  
- Results are well-presented with detailed experimental setup and comparisons.  
- q-KG is an original contribution with potential to improve BO efficiency in ML and materials science.  
- Strengths supporting acceptance: novelty, thorough analysis, strong experimental evidence, and clear exposition.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: assumption of expensive objective evaluations may not hold universally.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis of the q-KG algorithm.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: experiments are limited in scope, leaving generalization to other domains unclear.  
- Suggestion: include a more detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Suggestion: evaluate q-KG on additional application domains (e.g., materials science, engineering design).  
- Suggestion: compare q-KG directly with other BO methods such as knowledge gradient and expected improvement.
