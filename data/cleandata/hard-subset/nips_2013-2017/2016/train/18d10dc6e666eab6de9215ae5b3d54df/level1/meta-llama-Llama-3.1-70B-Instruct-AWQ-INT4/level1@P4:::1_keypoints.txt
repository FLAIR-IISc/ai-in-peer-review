- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function that measures information gain for evaluating q points in parallel.  
- Develops an efficient gradient computation for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA), enabling practical optimization of the acquisition.  
- Empirically outperforms other parallel BO methods on synthetic and real-world tasks, particularly under noisy evaluations.  
- Positions the work relative to prior parallel BO methods (parallel EI, UCB) and connects to the sequential knowledge gradient (KG) framework.  
- Technically sound with clear derivations and thorough experimental comparisons, though some mathematical steps lack detail.  
- Well-written with consistent notation and helpful figures, but some sections need better organization and transitions.  
- Novel contribution of a batch BO algorithm and an IPA-based gradient method, while building on existing Bayesian optimization ideas.  
- Significant practical implications for parallel BO across applications like hyperparameter tuning and materials science.  
- Pros: significant novel algorithm, clear derivation, strong empirical results, and practical significance for parallel settings.  
- Cons: mathematical derivations could be more detailed, computational complexity discussion is limited, some organizational issues, and partial overlap with existing ideas.  
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS 2016.
