- Proposes q-KG, a batch acquisition function that maximizes expected one-step reduction in the GP posterior mean minimum, extending sequential KG to batch settings.
- Allows final recommendation anywhere in the domain (unlike EI) and reduces to parallel EI in the noise-free / restricted-recommendation case.
- Introduces a Monte Carlo estimator for q-KG and an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) via a Cholesky-based reparameterization.
- Approximates inner minimizations over the domain by a dynamically updated discrete set of candidate minimizers plus past samples.
- Empirically compares to batch EI variants, GP-BUCB, GP-UCB-PE, and parallel predictive entropy search, showing improved simple regret especially with observation noise.
- Demonstrates gains on synthetic benchmarks and two real hyperparameter tuning tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, small CNN on CIFAR-10); code released.
- Strength: Clear decision-theoretic derivation; q-KG is Bayes-optimal for a single remaining batch w.r.t. posterior-mean minimum.
- Strength: Technically sound Monte Carlo formulation and practical pathwise (IPA) gradient enabling gradient-based optimization of a complex batch acquisition.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance, particularly in noisy regimes where information-based methods excel.
- Strength: Sensible connection to existing methods and public implementation aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness: Central discretization of inner minimization is under-analyzed—no quantitative error, sensitivity to M, or convergence study as M increases.
- Weakness: Incomplete computational reporting—missing Monte Carlo sample counts, runtimes, scaling with q/d/M, and gradient variance; no fair runtime comparisons to baselines.
- Weakness: IPA gradient details and unbiasedness conditions are relegated to supplement; practical handling of nondifferentiable points and Cholesky differentiation stability not discussed in main text.
- Weakness: Experimental setup may be confounded by using different libraries across methods, leaving GP priors, hyperparameter learning, and optimization tolerances inconsistently controlled or documented.
- Weakness: Real-task experiments have few repetitions (e.g., 5 runs) and no confidence intervals; lacks ablations on batch size q and noise misspecification and omits comparisons to methods like local penalization.
- Quality: Methodological core is sound and correctly formulated, but reliance on unquantified discretization and limited computational detail weakens robustness and scalability assessment.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-organized with explicit notation, but omits important technical details (IPA conditions, Cholesky derivative, Monte Carlo sizes, hyperparameter protocols) from the main text.
- Originality: Novel and meaningful extension of KG to continuous batch settings with an efficient differentiable estimator; reduction to EI is a useful theoretical link.
- Significance: Batch BO method with strong noisy-performance is practically and scientifically relevant if computational costs are acceptable.
- Suggested improvements: add complexity/runtime analysis, report Monte Carlo/gradient sample counts and wall-clock times, and include fair runtime baselines.
- Suggested improvements: analyze discretization sensitivity (vary M), batch size q, and noise misspecification; discuss convergence as M→∞.
- Suggested improvements: summarize conditions for IPA unbiasedness in main text and explain handling of nondifferentiability and Cholesky stability.
- Suggested improvements: standardize and document GP priors/hyperparameter training across baselines, increase repetitions, and report uncertainties on final recommendations.
- Suggested improvements: compare to local penalization and consider asynchronous batch selection variants.
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually clean, practical optimizer for batch KG with empirical gains in noisy settings and shared code.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discretization/error analysis, limited computational and statistical rigor in experiments, and crucial technical conditions left out of the main text.
