- Introduces parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), a Bayes-optimal acquisition for one remaining batch under a GP posterior.  
- Extends KG from sequential to parallel batch selection without restricting final recommendation to sampled points.  
- Derives an efficient stochastic gradient estimator using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to jointly optimize the batch.  
- Approximates the inner minimization via a dynamically updated discretization inspired by sampling near posterior optima (ES/PES-like).  
- Empirically compares q-KG to batch EI (heuristic and exact) and batch UCB variants, showing consistent improvements, especially with noisy evaluations and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Decision-theoretic formulation and Monte Carlo/IPA methodology are technically sound with appropriate references.  
- Bayes-optimality claim is correct but limited to the single-batch horizon.  
- Gradient estimator motivation is solid, but required regularity (differentiability, unique argmin) and effects of hyperparameter re-estimation are only briefly discussed.  
- Experiments are solid, include synthetic and practical tasks, and code is available.  
- Runtime and scalability analyses as batch size q and dimension d grow are limited.  
- Notation ambiguity: dual use of A (feasible set vs. discretized set) remains somewhat confusing.  
- Novelty: extending KG to parallel batches and using IPA-based optimization is a meaningful and original contribution.  
- Insightful connection to EI in the noiseless, recommend-from-samples setting.  
- Significance: provides a principled, non-greedy, information-directed batch BO method that excels under noise and is likely to be adopted.  
- Pros: decision-theoretic batch acquisition, efficient IPA stochastic optimization, strong empirical gains and open-source code.  
- Cons: no theoretical regret/convergence guarantees beyond one-batch, dependence on discretization with limited sensitivity/MC budget analysis, missing comparisons to batch Thompson sampling and asynchronous settings, limited wall-clock/runtime scaling study.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Suggestions: clarify IPA unbiasedness assumptions, report computational cost vs. q and d, analyze discretization sensitivity, and include asynchronous and batch Thompson baselines.
