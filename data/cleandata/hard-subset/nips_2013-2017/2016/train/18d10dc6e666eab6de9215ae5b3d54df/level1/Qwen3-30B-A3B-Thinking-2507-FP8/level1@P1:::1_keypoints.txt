- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that generalizes the knowledge gradient from sequential to batch settings.  
- q-KG jointly optimizes a batch of q points rather than adding points greedily, measuring expected incremental value to minimize the predictor's expected minimum.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients of q-KG, enabling efficient optimization of the acquisition.  
- Empirically shows consistent improvements over existing batch BO methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.  
- Builds on prior KG and batch BO literature and clearly differentiates from related work (notably [26]) by focusing on joint optimization and computational efficiency.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and well-motivated, presenting q-KG as a Bayes-optimal acquisition for batch problems.  
- IPA-based computational technique is novel and effectively addresses a major computational challenge in batch BO.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering multiple benchmarks (Branin2, Rosenbrock3, Ackley5, Hartmann6) and practical tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN) with appropriate statistics.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear mathematical derivations, precise algorithm descriptions, and reproducible experimental details.  
- Original contribution: meaningful extension of KG to batch settings and a new gradient-estimation approach for the batch acquisition.  
- Significance: addresses parallel hyperparameter tuning for expensive models, demonstrates practical utility, and provides code to improve reproducibility and impact.  
- Strengths: solid decision-theoretic foundation, novel IPA computation, thorough experiments, clear superiority in noise, and excellent presentation.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational scaling with batch size q and no explicit runtime comparisons with baselines.  
- Additional weaknesses: discretization strategy (M=1000) not fully justified, no treatment of constrained optimization, and omission of more recent post-2016 batch BO work.  
- Recommendation: strong accept—contribution is meaningful and empirically validated; minor revisions suggested to address complexity scaling and constraint handling.
