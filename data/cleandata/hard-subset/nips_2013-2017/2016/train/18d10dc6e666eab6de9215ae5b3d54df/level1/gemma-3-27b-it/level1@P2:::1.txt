This paper introduces the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian Optimization (BO). The core idea is to extend the knowledge gradient acquisition function to the parallel setting, aiming to select a batch of points that maximizes information gain. The authors derive this acquisition function and propose an efficient gradient-based optimization strategy, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing parallel BO algorithms on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks, particularly in noisy settings.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. It begins with a solid introduction to Bayesian Optimization and its parallel extensions, effectively motivating the need for the proposed method. The theoretical derivation of the q-KG function is presented with reasonable detail, and the explanation of the infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient computation is clear. The experimental setup is comprehensive, including both synthetic benchmarks and real-world hyperparameter tuning problems, and the results convincingly demonstrate the advantages of q-KG, especially in noisy evaluations. The inclusion of a code repository is also a strong positive aspect.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The q-KG method provides a theoretically grounded approach to parallel BO, extending the knowledge gradient concept to batch optimization. This is a significant contribution as it considers the batch of points collectively, unlike many previous methods that operate greedily.
*   **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of the acquisition function is based on a decision-theoretic analysis, making it a principled approach. The efficient gradient computation using IPA addresses a major computational challenge.
*   **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results consistently show that q-KG outperforms existing methods, particularly in noisy settings. The inclusion of practical applications (tuning logistic regression and CNNs) enhances the relevance of the work.
*   **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-written and logically organized, making it easy to follow the ideas and contributions.
*   **Reproducibility:** The availability of the code repository promotes reproducibility and facilitates further research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Computational Complexity:** While IPA improves computational efficiency, the optimization of q-KG can still be computationally demanding, especially for high-dimensional problems or large batch sizes (q). This aspect could be further elaborated, potentially including a more detailed analysis of the computational cost.
*   **Discretization of Search Space:** The discretization of the search space (A) for gradient estimation introduces an approximation. The authors mention using 1000 samples, but a more thorough investigation of the sensitivity of the results to this discretization level would be beneficial. A discussion of adaptive discretization strategies could also be considered.
*   **Comparison Depth:** While the comparison to several benchmark algorithms is good, a more detailed analysis of *why* q-KG outperforms them would strengthen the paper. For example, analyzing the exploration-exploitation trade-off achieved by q-KG compared to other methods could provide valuable insights.
*   **Limited Exploration of Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the results are strong, the paper could benefit from a brief discussion of the sensitivity of the q-KG method to its own hyperparameters, such as the parameters used in the IPA optimization.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound method for parallel Bayesian Optimization.
*   The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of q-KG over existing methods.
*   The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes a code repository.
*   The work addresses a relevant and challenging problem in machine learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The computational complexity of the method, while addressed by IPA, could still be a limitation.
*   The discretization of the search space introduces an approximation that needs further investigation.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The q-KG method is a significant advance over existing techniques, and the paper is well-executed in terms of theory, implementation, and experimentation. Despite minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper clearly outweigh them. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions, specifically addressing the points raised regarding computational complexity, discretization sensitivity, and deeper comparison analysis.
