- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm generalizing knowledge gradient to parallel queries.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis and shows Bayes-optimality when one decision remains.  
- Develops an efficient method to compute the q-KG acquisition function and its gradient for optimization.  
- Experimental results show q-KG is effective in both noise-free and noisy settings, often outperforming benchmarks.  
- Assumes the feasible domain A is easy to evaluate, which may not hold in many practical problems.  
- Lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity, raising concerns for large-scale applications.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to a few synthetic and practical problems; broader benchmarks are needed.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear derivations of the acquisition function and gradient.  
- Well-written and accessible, but some notation/terminology may be unfamiliar to readers without BO background.  
- Original contribution in extending knowledge gradient to parallel/batch settings, though building on existing BO work.  
- Potentially significant impact for batch optimization tasks (e.g., hyperparameter tuning, experimental design).  
- Would benefit from more discussion of applications, limitations, and future directions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated method with strong empirical performance.  
- Arguments for rejection: unrealistic domain assumptions, missing complexity analysis, and limited experiments.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to address complexity analysis, broader experiments, and clearer notation/explanations.
