This paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient method (q-KG), which generalizes the knowledge gradient method to the parallel setting. The algorithm is derived using a decision-theoretic analysis and is shown to be Bayes-optimal for minimizing the minimum of the predictor of the GP if only one decision is remaining. The authors also develop an efficient strategy for computing the q-KG acquisition function and its gradient, allowing for efficient optimization.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated algorithm for batch Bayesian optimization, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The authors provide a clear and concise derivation of the q-KG acquisition function and its gradient, making it easy to follow and understand.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the q-KG algorithm in both noise-free and noisy settings, outperforming or being competitive with state-of-the-art benchmark algorithms.

* The paper assumes that the feasible domain A is easy to evaluate, which may not always be the case in practice.

* The experimental results are limited to a few synthetic functions and practical problems, and it would be beneficial to see more extensive evaluations on a wider range of problems.
