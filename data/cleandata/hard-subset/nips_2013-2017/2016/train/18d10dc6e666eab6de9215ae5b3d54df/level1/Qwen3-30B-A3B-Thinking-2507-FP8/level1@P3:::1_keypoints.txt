- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method that selects q points in a decision-theoretically optimal way (for the one-iteration remaining problem).  
- Extends the sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to the parallel/batch setting.  
- Introduces an IPA-based method to efficiently estimate and optimize the gradient of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance over existing batch BO methods on synthetic benchmarks and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noise.  
- Builds on prior KG work (Frazier et al.) and distinguishes from parallel EI and batch UCB approaches.  
- Correctly contrasts with Parallel Predictive Entropy Search (Shah & Ghahramani 2015) but needs clearer differentiation from Marmin et al. (2016).  
- Theoretical strength: provides a clear Bayes-optimal criterion for the one-iteration remaining problem.  
- Computational innovation: IPA addresses the main computational bottleneck, enabling practical implementation.  
- Experimental strength: comprehensive evaluation across multiple synthetic functions and real ML tasks (MNIST, CIFAR10).  
- Practical relevance: well-suited to real-world parallel evaluation scenarios like hyperparameter tuning.  
- Theoretical weakness: "Bayes-optimal" claim is limited to one-step remaining and lacks a formal proof and broader discussion of limitations.  
- Missing detailed computational complexity analysis for the IPA approach, particularly scaling with batch size q and dimension d.  
- Insufficient comparison with the most recent batch BO methods (notably Shah & Ghahramani 2015 and Marmin et al. 2016).  
- Discretization scheme (M=1000 posterior samples) lacks justification and sensitivity analysis on M.  
- Computational cost is not clearly quantified versus alternatives, which matters when evaluations are expensive.  
- Overall assessment: strong, relevant contribution with solid theory, novel computation, and convincing experiments; minor but addressable weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Accept; code availability increases practical impact.
