- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm called parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG).  
- Algorithm derived from a decision-theoretical perspective.  
- Shown to be Bayes-optimal for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when only one decision remains.  
- Generalizes the sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel/batch setting.  
- Provides a computational method to implement q-KG efficiently.  
- Compares q-KG to parallel EI and parallel UCB and reports better performance on several synthetic and real ML tuning tasks.  
- Strength: novel, well-motivated batch BO algorithm from first principles.  
- Strength: developed an efficient implementation approach.  
- Strength: thorough empirical comparisons against state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strength: convincing demonstrations on synthetic functions and practical ML tuning.  
- Weakness: potentially high computational complexity for large batches or high-dimensional problems.  
- Weakness: relies on a Gaussian process prior, limiting applicability for some problems.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical performance guarantees beyond the single-decision Bayes-optimality result.  
- Recommendation: accept — strengths outweigh weaknesses; paper is clear, well-written, and makes a significant contribution.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
