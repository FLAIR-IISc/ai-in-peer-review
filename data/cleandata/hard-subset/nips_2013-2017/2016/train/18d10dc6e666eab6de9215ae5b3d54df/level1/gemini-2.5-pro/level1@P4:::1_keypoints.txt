- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch acquisition function that defines a batch’s value as the expected improvement in the final solution (minimum of the GP posterior mean).
- q-KG is one-step Bayes-optimal under that criterion and generalizes sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel setting.
- Jointly optimizes all q points in the batch rather than using greedy heuristics (e.g., constant liar or UCB variants).
- Derives an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator for q-KG using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to enable gradient-based maximization.
- Employs a dynamic discretization strategy for the inner continuous minimization, adapted from entropy search methods.
- Empirically evaluated on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning tasks (logistic regression and CNNs) in both noisy and noise-free regimes.
- Shows strong empirical performance, often outperforming parallel EI and UCB baselines, particularly in noisy settings.
- Technical derivation is principled and sound; experimental methodology is rigorous with multiple runs and open-source code for reproducibility.
- Paper is clearly written and well-organized, with good positioning in related work.
- Does not discuss computational complexity or provide wall-clock time comparisons against faster heuristic methods.
- Scalability with respect to batch size q is not analyzed; optimization happens in a q*d dimensional space and may incur matrix costs that grow with q.
- Reliance on a discretization heuristic (choice of M) is adapted from prior work and not novel, and its sensitivity is unexplored.
- Minor clarity issue: the interchange of derivative and expectation and differentiation through the min operator should be briefly justified in the main text (e.g., via Danskin’s theorem).
- Overall claim: the method is novel, technically strong, empirically effective, and likely to be impactful.
- Main arguments against acceptance: limited scalability analysis and dependence on discretization hyperparameters.
