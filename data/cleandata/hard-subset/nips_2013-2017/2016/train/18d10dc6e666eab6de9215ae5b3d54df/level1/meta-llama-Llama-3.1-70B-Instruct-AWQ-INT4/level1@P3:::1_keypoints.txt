- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm with an acquisition function measuring information gain from evaluating q points.  
- Derives a decision-theoretic, Bayes-optimal selection rule for a single remaining batch.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute gradients of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world problems, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Compares q-KG against parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with pure exploration, showing consistent outperformance or competitiveness.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible with a strong related-work overview.  
- Provides a rigorous theoretical foundation grounded in decision theory.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across noise-free and noisy settings.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity and runtime comparisons with other batch BO methods.  
- Insufficient analysis of q-KG behavior across different settings, notably the effect of batch size.  
- Missing comparisons to other optimization families (e.g., gradient-based or evolutionary algorithms).  
- Review ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10; overall recommendation: accept.
