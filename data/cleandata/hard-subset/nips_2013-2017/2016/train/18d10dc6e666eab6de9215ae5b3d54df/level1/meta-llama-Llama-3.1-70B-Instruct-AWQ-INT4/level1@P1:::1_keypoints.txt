- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm for parallel evaluation of multiple points.
- q-KG is derived via decision-theoretic analysis to choose the batch optimal in the average-case when one batch remains.
- Provides an efficient computation strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic functions and practical ML algorithms, particularly under noisy evaluations.
- Extends the sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel setting and relates to parallel EI and UCB, but selects batches jointly.
- Strength: novel contribution with a thorough derivation and analysis.
- Strength: practical computation via IPA makes the method scalable compared to naive implementations.
- Strength: convincing empirical results on both synthetic and real-world tasks.
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis—no detailed convergence proofs or regret bounds.
- Weakness: still computationally intensive for large-scale problems despite IPA improvements.
- Weakness: comparisons to other state-of-the-art parallel BO methods are not comprehensive.
- Quality: paper is well-written, clearly presents the method and results.
- Clarity: well-organized with consistent notation and clear explanations.
- Originality: method is an original contribution to Bayesian optimization.
- Significance: potentially impactful for large-scale noisy optimization and practical applications.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical performance, and efficient computation approach.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical guarantees, computational cost concerns, and limited empirical comparisons.
