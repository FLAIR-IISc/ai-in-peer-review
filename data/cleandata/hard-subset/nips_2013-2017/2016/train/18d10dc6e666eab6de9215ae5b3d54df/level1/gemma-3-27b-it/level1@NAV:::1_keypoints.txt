- Proposes q-KG, a parallel extension of the knowledge gradient to choose Bayes-optimal batches in Bayesian optimization.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients and enable efficient optimization of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNNs) show q-KG outperforms several baselines, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Clearly situates the work within prior parallel BO and sequential KG literature, highlighting differences from greedy batch construction.  
- Theoretical derivation is decision-theoretic and well-motivated.  
- IPA-based gradient estimation substantially improves computational scalability.  
- Paper is well written and implementation/code is provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Weakness: discretization of domain A (M = 1000) appears arbitrary and needs sensitivity analysis or adaptive strategies.  
- Weakness: other hyperparameter sensitivities are not thoroughly analyzed, raising potential robustness concerns.  
- Weakness: benchmark suite is limited; broader evaluations (e.g., high-dimensional, multi-modal problems) would strengthen claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically strong, and computationally practical.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate justification for discretization choice and insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address discretization and sensitivity analyses.
