- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm generalizing the sequential knowledge gradient to parallel settings.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis to select a batch that maximizes expected improvement in the GP-predictor minimum.  
- Provides a one-step Bayes-optimal batch solution for the special case of a single remaining iteration.  
- Develops an efficient optimization approach using infinitesimal perturbation analysis to reduce computational cost of maximizing q-KG.  
- Builds on prior KG work (Frazier et al.) and extends recent parallel BO methods like parallel EI and GP-UCB-PE.  
- Theoretical contribution: formulates a batch acquisition function that selects points jointly rather than iteratively.  
- Strong empirical validation on synthetic functions and real hyperparameter tuning tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10).  
- Demonstrates superiority over state-of-the-art methods, particularly in noisy evaluation settings.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational scalability for large batch sizes (q).  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with very recent batch BO methods (e.g., Parallel Predictive Entropy Search, Shah & Ghahramani 2015).  
- Weakness: discretization strategy in the method could be more thoroughly explained.  
- Overall assessment: significant, practically impactful contribution to batch Bayesian optimization; recommended for acceptance.
