- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) to select batches for simultaneous evaluation by maximizing expected incremental value of a batch.
- Provides an efficient gradient-based optimizer for the q-KG acquisition function using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Well-motivated and clearly presented, addressing the common scenario of parallel function evaluations.
- Extends sequential Knowledge Gradient to the parallel setting, contrasting with greedy/iterative parallel EI and UCB approaches.
- Correctly positions contribution relative to prior work, noting differences with [25] (different gradient approach) and [26] (nested batch KG) in efficiency and setting.
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in decision-theoretic, Bayes-optimal principles.
- IPA-based gradient computation significantly improves computational efficiency for optimizing q-KG.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over existing parallel BO methods, especially in noisy settings and real-world hyperparameter tuning.
- Paper is well-written, mathematically clear, and includes code to support reproducibility.
- Discretization of continuous search space via posterior sampling (M=1000) may bottleneck scalability, especially in high dimensions.
- Gradient computation complexity likely grows with batch size and discretization points; more analysis of computational cost is needed.
- Limited experimental exploration of sensitivity to q-KG’s hyperparameters, such as GP kernel parameters.
- Lacks theoretical regret bounds to quantify performance guarantees.
- Minor redundancy in explanations relating q-KG to parallel EI across multiple sections.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand discussion of discretization, analyze computational complexity, and mention potential future work on regret bounds.
