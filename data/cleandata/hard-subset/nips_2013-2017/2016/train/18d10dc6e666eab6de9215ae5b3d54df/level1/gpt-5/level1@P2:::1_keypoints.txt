- Introduces q-KG, a batch KG acquisition for GP-based BO defined as expected reduction in the posterior-mean minimum for a batch of q evaluations, Bayes-optimal for one remaining batch under standard KG assumptions.
- Provides a reparameterization of the posterior update with a standard normal vector Zq and derives an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to optimize batches jointly.
- Approximates the continuous inner minimization by discretizing the candidate set using high-posterior-probability samples plus past/candidate points, since exact computation is intractable.
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR-10) show consistent improvements over parallel EI and UCB-based batch policies, especially under noisy evaluations.
- Connects and extends prior work: sequential KG to batch setting, contrasts with multi-point EI and entropy-based batch methods, and complements cumulative-regret methods like BUCB/GP-UCB-PE.
- Technical derivation and IPA-based gradient estimator are sound under reasonable assumptions (differentiable mean/kernel, finite discretization); Bayes-optimality holds only relative to the discretized set.
- Implementation pipeline (sampling Zq, finite minimization, backprop through Cholesky, multi-start stochastic ascent) is practical but computationally intensive for larger q or discretization sizes.
- Paper lacks explicit complexity analysis (dependence on n, q, |An|, MC samples) and wall-clock runtime comparisons to baselines.
- Empirics are sensible and demonstrate noise robustness, but mainly use q=4; missing scalability tests for larger q (e.g., q=8,16), runtime, and sensitivity analyses.
- Omitted comparisons to batch entropy-search methods (PPES/PES) and limited ablations on discretization size M, Monte Carlo sample counts, and hyperparameter learning.
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized; notation confusion between domain A and discretization An should be clarified earlier.
- More main-text details on IPA unbiasedness conditions and a brief complexity discussion would improve readability.
- Novelty: meaningful and novel extension of KG to batch with a tractable unbiased gradient estimator; shows reduction to parallel EI in noise-free, restricted-recommendation cases.
- Significance: practically important for batch BO, particularly in noisy ML tuning; code release increases impact.
- Pros: principled decision-theoretic batch acquisition, efficient unbiased IPA gradient enabling joint optimization, strong empirical noise performance, clear prior-work linkage and reduction to q-EI.
- Cons: discretization approximation without error analysis, missing runtime/complexity and scalability results, limited ablations, and missing entropy-based baselines.
- Suggested improvements: add wall-clock and asymptotic complexity analysis, include PPES/entropy and fantasizing baselines, sensitivity studies for M and MC samples, tests for larger batch sizes and higher dims, and clarify IPA unbiasedness and GP hyperparameter re-estimation effects.
- Overall assessment: solid, timely contribution with clear empirical benefits in noisy settings; recommend accept.
