- Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch Bayesian optimization method generalizing the sequential knowledge gradient to select batches collectively.
- Derives a one-step Bayes-optimal acquisition function for batches under the assumption of a single remaining batch of evaluations.
- Develops an efficient gradient estimation strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to optimize the q-KG acquisition function.
- Positions the work within existing BO literature and distinguishes it from iterative batch construction methods.
- Theoretical derivation is sound, clear, and technically rigorous.
- IPA-based computational approach is novel and effectively addresses a key practical challenge in parallel BO.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering synthetic benchmark functions and real ML tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN).
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over state-of-the-art baselines, with especially strong gains in noisy settings.
- Paper is well-written, includes clear pseudocode and notation, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility (open-source code provided).
- Lacks detailed comparison of computational runtime versus baselines, focusing mainly on function-evaluation efficiency.
- Provides limited theoretical explanation for why q-KG outperforms others in noisy settings.
- Related work discussion could better contextualize connections to recent parallel BO papers (e.g., nested batch KG, Shah & Ghahramani, Marmin et al.).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded parallel BO method with an efficient optimization approach and strong empirical gains applicable to hyperparameter tuning.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient runtime comparison, incomplete theoretical justification for noisy-case performance, and incomplete linkage to some recent related work.
- Overall assessment: strong, technically sound contribution with clear novelty and practical significance; reviewer recommends acceptance.
