- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch BO acquisition function that is one-step Bayes-optimal for the final recommended solution.  
- Generalizes sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) to the parallel setting for selecting q points jointly.  
- Proposes a Monte Carlo estimator and an IPA-based unbiased stochastic gradient to enable practical gradient-based optimization of q-KG.  
- Uses a dynamic discretization scheme to handle continuous domains.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation against strong baselines on synthetic functions and real hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic, CIFAR-10 CNN).  
- Demonstrates strong performance, especially in noisy evaluation settings.  
- High technical quality: principled derivation, sound decision-theoretic foundation, and clear algorithmic presentation.  
- Solid experimental methodology, honest reporting of results, and provided code for reproducibility.  
- Suggests adding a brief discussion of regularity conditions for interchange of expectation and differentiation used in the IPA gradient.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and situates related work effectively; figures are clear.  
- Original contribution: non-trivial parallelization of KG and novel use of IPA for KG-style acquisition in continuous BO.  
- Significant practical impact for parallel BO and noisy real-world optimization tasks.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled approach, novel optimization strategy, strong empirical results, noise robustness, and reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential computational overhead and unclear scalability with batch size q and dimension d.  
- Additional concern: sensitivity to the discretization heuristic (choice of M) is not fully analyzed.  
- Overall recommendation: strong recommendation for acceptance.
