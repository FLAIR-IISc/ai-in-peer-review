- Summary: Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic, one-step Bayes-optimal batch BO acquisition that maximizes expected improvement of the GP posterior mean min_x µ(x), and uses IPA to obtain unbiased stochastic gradients for efficient batch selection with dynamic discretization for continuous domains.

- Strength — Theoretical grounding: q-KG is derived from a clear one-step Bayes-optimal principle and targets the posterior mean over the whole domain, explaining advantages in noisy settings.

- Strength — Technical contribution: Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis to produce unbiased stochastic gradients, enabling tractable gradient-based optimization of a complex, non-myopic acquisition.

- Strength — Empirical evaluation: Thorough experiments on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks show q-KG outperforms strong baselines, especially under noise, including notable gains on CIFAR-10 CNN tuning.

- Strength — Clarity and reproducibility: Well-written presentation and provided open-source code enhance understanding and reproducibility.

- Weakness — Computational overhead: Lacks empirical analysis of wall-clock time and practical overhead of maximizing q-KG versus baselines, which may negate BO savings.

- Weakness — Sensitivity to discretization: Relies on sampled discretization of the domain (An) without ablation on discretization size M or robustness to missed regions.

- Weakness — Scalability with q: Experiments use q=4; complexity involves O(q^3) Cholesky steps and optimization in q*d dimensions, raising concerns for larger batch sizes.

- Argument for acceptance: Proposes a principled Bayes-optimal batch acquisition, introduces a significant IPA-based optimization technique, demonstrates state-of-the-art empirical performance (notably in noisy settings), and provides clear, reproducible work.

- Argument against acceptance: Insufficient treatment of practical computational cost scaling and limited analysis of discretization approximation quality.

- Conclusion: Strong, novel, and well-validated contribution with mainly practical/scale-related issues; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
