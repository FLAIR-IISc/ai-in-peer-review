- Proposes q-KG: a parallel extension of the Knowledge Gradient acquisition to select Bayes-optimal batches.
- Develops an efficient gradient-based maximization of q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Positions work relative to sequential KG and existing parallel BO methods (parallel EI, batch UCB), noting clear connections.
- Notes equivalence to parallel EI under noise-free and restricted solution-space conditions.
- Empirically validates q-KG on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, showing consistent improvement, especially in noisy evaluations.
- Code is released (https://github.com/wujian16/qKG), supporting reproducibility.
- Novelty: significant contribution by extending KG to the batch/parallel setting.
- Technical soundness: mathematically rigorous derivation and clever IPA-based gradient decomposition.
- Clarity: well-written, clear notation, and accessible derivations.
- Weakness: discretization of the domain A is under-explained; choice and impact of M=1000 samples may be sensitive to dimensionality.
- Weakness: lacking detailed computational cost and scalability analysis for higher dimensions and larger batch sizes.
- Weakness: no systematic study of sensitivity to hyperparameters (GP kernel, etc.) or robustness to their choices.
- Weakness: limited discussion of potential failure cases or scenarios where q-KG might underperform.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, justifies acceptance despite areas needing further analysis (discretization, cost, robustness).
