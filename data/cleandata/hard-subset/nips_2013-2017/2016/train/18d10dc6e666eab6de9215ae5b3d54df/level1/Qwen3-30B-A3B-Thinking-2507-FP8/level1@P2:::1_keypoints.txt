- Proposes q-KG, a Bayes-optimal acquisition function that selects a batch of q points jointly for parallel Bayesian optimization rather than greedily.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients of q-KG, enabling efficient gradient-based batch optimization.  
- Provides a rigorous, decision-theoretic derivation of q-KG with a strong theoretical foundation.  
- Presents an algorithmic implementation (Algorithm 1) and a dynamic discretization strategy for practical computation.  
- Evaluates on synthetic benchmarks (Branin2, Rosenbrock3, Ackley5, Hartmann6) and real ML tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNNs on CIFAR10).  
- Shows consistent empirical improvements over batch EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, especially under noisy observations, with statistical analysis.  
- Exceptionally clear and well-organized presentation that facilitates reproducibility; code is released.  
- Novel contribution: principled extension of sequential KG to the parallel setting and novel application of IPA in batch BO.  
- High practical significance for parallel hyperparameter tuning and reducing expensive evaluations in ML.  
- Weakness: computational cost increases with batch size; scalability to very large q (e.g., >20) is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Weakness: the dynamic discretization strategy (construction of An) is described briefly and could use more detail.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of scenarios where q-KG may underperform, such as extremely high-dimensional problems.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong theoretical and empirical contribution with minor weaknesses.
