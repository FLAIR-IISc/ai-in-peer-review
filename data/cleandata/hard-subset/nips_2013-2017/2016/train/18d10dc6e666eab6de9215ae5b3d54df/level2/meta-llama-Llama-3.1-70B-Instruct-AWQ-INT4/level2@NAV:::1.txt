This paper presents a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method, for optimizing expensive-to-evaluate black-box functions. The authors claim that their method provides the one-step Bayes optimal batch of points to sample, and demonstrate its effectiveness in both synthetic and real-world problems, especially when function evaluations are noisy.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-structured presentation of their method. The theoretical analysis is thorough, and the empirical results are convincing. The authors also provide a comprehensive review of related work, which helps to situate their contribution in the context of existing research.

**Clarity:** The writing is clear and concise, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments. The notation is well-defined, and the mathematical derivations are straightforward. The authors also provide a clear explanation of their algorithm and its implementation.

**Originality:** The q-KG method is a novel contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The authors' decision-theoretic analysis and the use of infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to optimize the acquisition function are innovative and effective.

**Significance:** The results presented in the paper demonstrate the potential of the q-KG method to improve the efficiency of Bayesian optimization, especially in problems with noisy function evaluations. The authors' contribution has the potential to impact various fields, including machine learning, optimization, and engineering design.
