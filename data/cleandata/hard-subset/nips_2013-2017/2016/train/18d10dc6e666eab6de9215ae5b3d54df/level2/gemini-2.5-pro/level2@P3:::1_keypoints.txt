- Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic acquisition function extending knowledge gradient to jointly select a batch of q points for parallel Bayesian optimization.
- Derives a tractable gradient-based optimizer using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to obtain unbiased gradient estimates of the stochastic q-KG function.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic functions and two real hyperparameter tuning tasks shows competitive or superior performance, especially in noisy settings.
- Provides open-source code, supporting reproducibility.
- Strong theoretical grounding and principled joint-batch formulation distinguish it from greedy or heuristic parallel BO methods.
- IPA-based gradient estimation is a notable technical contribution enabling joint optimization of all batch points.
- Experimental results demonstrate robustness and particular advantage when evaluations are noisy, relevant for many real-world tasks.
- Computational cost concerns: relies on Monte Carlo estimation and multi-start stochastic gradient ascent, potentially intensive; wall-clock time comparisons to baselines are missing.
- Dependence on discretizing the domain to compute the posterior-mean minimum is critical; sensitivity analysis to the number of discretization points (M) is not provided.
- Reproducibility could be improved by reporting q-KG optimization hyperparameters (MC samples, number of random starts, optimizer settings).
- Scalability questions remain: experiments are limited to q=4 and do not evaluate performance or computational scaling for larger batch sizes.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled acquisition, effective IPA computational technique, strong empirical performance, and clear benefits in noisy environments.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of explicit computational complexity/ wall-clock comparisons and unexplored scaling to larger q.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, minor issues only, clear candidate for acceptance.
