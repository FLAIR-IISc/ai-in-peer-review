- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm for parallel evaluation of multiple points.  
- Derives q-KG via a decision-theoretic analysis, optimal in the average-case when one batch remains.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise explanation of the method.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and thorough.  
- Evaluated on synthetic functions and real-world problems, including ML hyperparameter tuning.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Assumes evaluations are noise-free or have additive i.i.d. Gaussian noise, which may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Requires computation of the acquisition-function gradient, which can be computationally expensive.  
- Lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of q-KG.  
- Originality: novel batch BO method and novel use of decision-theoretic derivation.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution with potential for wide practical adoption and improved parallel efficiency.  
- Quality and clarity are high and empirical results are convincing.  
- Recommendation: reviewer recommends acceptance to NIPS 2016.
