- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), extending sequential KG to batch Bayesian optimization to maximize expected incremental value per iteration.
- Derives q-KG mathematically and proposes an infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient-based batch selection.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance versus parallel EI, GP-BUCB, GP-UCB-PE on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates the work within recent parallel BO literature.
- Code release provided, aiding reproducibility.
- Strength: novel, decision-theoretically grounded extension of KG to parallel BO.
- Strength: IPA-based gradient computation makes the method practically scalable.
- Strength: consistent empirical gains, notably under noise.
- Weakness: discretization of search space A relies on M=1000 posterior samples without sensitivity analysis of M vs. cost or dimensional dependence.
- Weakness: limited analysis of q-KG’s sensitivity to its own hyperparameters (e.g., ARD Matern 5/2 kernel params).
- Weakness: lacks direct computational-cost comparison (e.g., wall-clock time) against baselines despite gradient optimization overhead.
- Weakness: noisy experiments use a single fixed noise level (σ = 0.5); broader noise-level evaluation is missing.
- Weakness: insufficient explicit discussion of limitations (e.g., scalability to very high dimensions or very large batch sizes).
- Recommendation: overall strong contribution; accept with minor revisions addressing discretization sensitivity and limitations discussion.
