- Introduces q-KG, extending the knowledge gradient acquisition to parallel (batch) Bayesian optimization to maximize expected incremental improvement for one remaining batch.  
- Derives an efficient gradient-based optimization strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for the q-KG acquisition.  
- Mathematical formulation is clear and grounded in decision theory (Bayes optimality for a single remaining batch).  
- Empirical results show consistent performance gains over baselines, especially in noisy settings and hyperparameter tuning tasks; code is provided.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and makes technical details accessible.  

Strengths:  
- Novel, theoretically sound extension of KG to the batch setting; significant methodological contribution.  
- IPA-based gradient estimator improves practical efficiency of optimizing q-KG.  
- Strong empirical performance across synthetic and practical problems, particularly under noise.  

Weaknesses:  
- Discretization of search space A (selection of M initial samples via posterior optima) is somewhat arbitrary and potentially sensitive to GP initialization; lacks sensitivity analysis or alternatives.  
- No detailed computational complexity analysis (e.g., scaling with batch size q and dimensionality d) relative to baselines.  
- Comparisons omit some more recent or state-of-the-art batch BO methods (e.g., diversity-promoting approaches, deep GPs).  
- Limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., number of Monte Carlo samples for q-KG estimation).  

Arguments for acceptance:  
- Novel, theoretically grounded, empirically validated, and practically implementable method; well-written and reproducible.  

Arguments against acceptance:  
- Needs deeper investigation of discretization strategy, computational cost, and comparisons to newer competitive methods.  

Overall assessment:  
- Significant contribution deserving acceptance with minor revisions to address discretization, complexity analysis, and hyperparameter sensitivity.
