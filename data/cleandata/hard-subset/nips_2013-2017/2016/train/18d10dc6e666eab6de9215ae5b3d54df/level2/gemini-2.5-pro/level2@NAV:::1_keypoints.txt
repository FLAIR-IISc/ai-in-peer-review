- Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch acquisition function that jointly selects q points to maximize expected final solution quality.  
- Extends sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel setting, building on prior KG, EI, and UCB work.  
- Main technical contribution: efficient optimization of q-KG using an IPA-based gradient estimator.  
- Theoretically well-motivated as a one-step Bayes-optimal batch selection policy.  
- IPA-enabled gradient optimization makes the approach practical and computationally tractable.  
- Strong empirical evaluation showing superior performance on synthetic functions and real-world hyperparameter tuning.  
- Particularly strong results in noisy evaluation settings, demonstrating practical significance.  
- Paper is clearly written and code is provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Potential drawback: overall computational complexity of optimizing q-KG may still be high despite efficient gradient estimation.  
- Lacks discussion on wall-clock time scaling with batch size q and dimensionality d compared to benchmarks.  
- Discretization strategy for approximating posterior minimum (Section 5.3) is a key heuristic and needs more justification versus alternatives.  
- Experiments limited to q=4; analysis of performance across varying q values is missing.  
- Overall verdict: high-quality, novel, well-founded, and empirically validated method with minor but addressable weaknesses.
