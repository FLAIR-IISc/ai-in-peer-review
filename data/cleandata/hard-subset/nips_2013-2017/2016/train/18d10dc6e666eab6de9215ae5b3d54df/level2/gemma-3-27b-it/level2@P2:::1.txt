This paper introduces the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian Optimization (BO). The core idea is to extend the knowledge gradient acquisition function to the parallel setting, selecting a batch of points that maximizes the expected incremental improvement in the solution quality given a single remaining batch of samples. The authors provide an efficient gradient-based optimization strategy for maximizing this function, and demonstrate its effectiveness on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks, particularly when evaluations are noisy.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction provides good motivation, and the related work section adequately positions the contribution within the existing literature on parallel BO. The mathematical formulation of q-KG is clear, and the derivation of the gradient estimator using infinitesimal perturbation analysis is a significant technical contribution. The experimental results are comprehensive, demonstrating consistent performance gains over several baselines, particularly in noisy settings, and across various problem types. The inclusion of code availability is also a positive aspect.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The q-KG method offers a theoretically sound and novel approach to parallel BO, extending the well-established knowledge gradient framework to the batch setting. The derivation of the acquisition function and its efficient optimization strategy are significant contributions.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** Grounding the method in decision theory (Bayes optimality for a single remaining batch) provides a strong theoretical justification.
* **Efficiency:** The IPA-based gradient estimation is crucial for practical applicability, addressing the computational challenges associated with maximizing q-KG.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the superiority of q-KG, particularly in noisy environments, which are common in real-world applications like hyperparameter tuning. The consistent improvements across both synthetic and practical problems are compelling.
* **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, making the technical details accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Discretization of A:** While the method for discretizing the search space A is described, the choice of *how* to select the M initial samples (via the global optima of the posterior) seems somewhat arbitrary and potentially sensitive to the GP’s initial state. A more thorough discussion of the sensitivity of this choice, or comparison to alternative discretization strategies, would be beneficial.
* **Computational Complexity:** Although the IPA technique significantly improves efficiency, the paper lacks a detailed analysis of the computational cost of q-KG compared to the baselines. Providing a theoretical or empirical complexity analysis (e.g., scaling with batch size *q* and dimensionality *d*) would strengthen the argument for its practical utility.
* **Limited Comparison:** While the comparison to several existing methods is thorough, it would be beneficial to include comparisons with more recent or state-of-the-art batch BO algorithms that were not available at the time of writing. (e.g., methods leveraging diversity promotion or deep Gaussian processes).
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper does not extensively explore the sensitivity of the method to its own hyperparameters (e.g., the number of samples used in the Monte Carlo estimation for q-KG). A discussion of the robustness of the method to different hyperparameter settings would increase confidence in its practical applicability.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound method for parallel BO.
* The proposed q-KG algorithm consistently outperforms existing methods in the experiments.
* The efficient gradient-based optimization strategy makes the method practical.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The discretization strategy for the search space could be more thoroughly investigated.
* The computational complexity analysis is lacking.
* Comparisons to more recent and competitive methods are missing.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The q-KG method is a well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated approach to parallel BO. While there are some minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper clearly outweigh them. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above, specifically regarding the discretization strategy, computational complexity analysis, and a brief discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.
