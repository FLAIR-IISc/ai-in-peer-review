- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm aimed at parallel optimization of expensive functions, especially under noise.  
- Well-structured introduction that motivates the need for a new batch BO method.  
- q-KG is derived from a decision-theoretic perspective and is clearly explained with implementation details.  
- Extensive empirical evaluation on synthetic benchmarks and real-world problems (including hyperparameter tuning).  
- Results indicate q-KG consistently outperforms or matches state-of-the-art batch BO methods, particularly in noisy settings.  
- Strength: algorithm is novel and supported by a strong theoretical foundation.  
- Strength: thorough experimental validation across multiple benchmarks.  
- Strength: clear explanation of algorithm and implementation.  
- Weakness: assumes function evaluations are i.i.d., which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: optimizing the q-KG factor is computationally intensive and may limit practical use.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of computational complexity.  
- Overall assessment: paper is well-written and makes a significant contribution with potential for wide adoption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and extensively evaluated with strong empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: problematic i.i.d. assumption, high computational cost, and insufficient complexity analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address limitations and provide a more detailed complexity analysis.
