- Proposes q-KG: a parallel knowledge gradient method extending sequential KG to batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Claims q-KG yields the one-step Bayes-optimal batch of points to sample.  
- Introduces an IPA-based computational strategy that enables practical implementation of q-KG.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods (parallel EI, batch UCB, GP-UCB-PE) on synthetic and real ML hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Shows particularly strong performance in noisy evaluation settings.  
- Strength: clear theoretical foundation for q-KG.  
- Strength: rigorous experimental validation across multiple domains (4 synthetic functions and 2 practical ML applications).  
- Strength: well-organized presentation and sufficient implementation details with code availability for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: novelty claim should be better differentiated from related works [25] and [18] on parallel KG.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis comparing q-KG to other methods for practitioners.  
- Weakness: missing discussion of limitations when batch size q is very large or in high-dimensional settings.  
- Overall: significant, high-quality contribution that advances batch Bayesian optimization; recommend acceptance with minor improvements.
