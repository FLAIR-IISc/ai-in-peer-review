- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that maximizes expected reduction in the posterior mean’s minimum (parallel extension of knowledge gradient).  
- Derives an acquisition amenable to gradient-based batch optimization via an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Connects to prior work: generalizes sequential KG and relates/reduces to parallel EI in the noiseless sampled-point recommendation setting; contrasts with greedy “fantasizing” EI, GP-BUCB, GP-UCB-PE, joint multi-point EI, and PPES.  
- Empirically evaluated on standard synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression on MNIST, CNNs on CIFAR-10), with and without noise.  
- Shows consistent empirical improvements over strong baselines, especially in noisy settings where EI-based methods struggle.  
- Principled decision-theoretic objective: Bayes-optimal one-step lookahead for minimizing the posterior-mean minimum.  
- Sound computational approach: IPA-derived unbiased stochastic gradients under mild conditions, with careful derivations.  
- Reproducibility supported by provided code and use of established GP modeling (ARD Matérn-5/2 with MLE).  
- Practical Bayes-optimality weakened by discretization of the inner minimization domain (A); sensitivity to discretization size M is not reported.  
- AM_n sampling procedure (samples from posterior global optima) is under-specified and lacks precise citation/algorithmic detail.  
- Lacks wall-clock/runtime or complexity comparisons and breakdowns (MC samples, Cholesky costs, gradient steps); scalability with q and d is unquantified.  
- Empirical comparisons omit joint acquisition baselines like PPES and closed-form multi-point EI in applicable settings.  
- Noise modeling assumes homoscedastic Gaussian noise (constant σ^2); heteroscedastic noise common in ML tuning is not addressed.  
- IPA relies on differentiability and unique minimizers; conditions and proofs need clearer statement and referencing to supplement.  
- No theoretical convergence/regret guarantees are provided; such results would strengthen the contribution.  
- Methodological development is technically sound, but the impact of discretization approximation is not quantified.  
- Paper is generally well written and organized but needs clarification on AM_n construction, acquisition complexity, gradient through Cholesky, experimental budgets, and wall-clock times.  
- Contribution is original in extending KG to batch with an efficient gradient estimator, distinct from multi-point EI/PPES approaches.  
- Significance is high for practical batch BO, with notable gains under noise and available open-source implementation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled objective, solid computational solution, strong noisy-case empirical gains, clear relation to baselines, and released code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: dependence on discretization, missing runtime/scaling analyses, omitted relevant baselines (PPES), and limited discussion of higher-dimensional/larger-batch scalability.  
- Essential suggestions: add runtime/scaling results versus baselines and evaluate sensitivity to discretization M and the AM_n construction.  
- Important suggestions: include or justify omission of PPES and multi-point EI in comparisons.  
- Optional suggestions: evaluate larger batch sizes q, state theoretical conditions for IPA unbiasedness, and discuss heteroscedastic noise or repeated-evaluation strategies.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos (e.g., “Main Stange”) and expand implementation details for gradient computation and optimization settings.
