- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition function for tractable optimization.  
- Targets practical need for efficient parallel evaluations such as hyperparameter tuning.  
- The method is decision-theoretically motivated and theoretically sound.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and ML tasks (logistic regression, CNN tuning) show q-KG consistently outperforms existing parallel BO methods, especially under noise.  
- Provides a code repository, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Presentation is dense; the jump from theory to implementation (notably the discretization strategy in Section 5.3) is abrupt and unclear.  
- Computational complexity is mentioned but lacks detailed comparison to alternative methods.  
- Experimental reporting is limited to means and standard deviations; no statistical significance tests are presented.  
- Comparisons to other methods are uneven, relying on different implementations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and experimental rigor.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify discretization details, add statistical significance tests, and compare against unified implementations of baselines.
