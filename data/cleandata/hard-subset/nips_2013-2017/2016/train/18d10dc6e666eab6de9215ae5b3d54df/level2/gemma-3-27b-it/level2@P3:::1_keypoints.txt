- Proposes q-KG: a decision-theoretic batch Bayesian optimization method selecting batches optimal in average-case posterior assuming one batch remains.  
- Employs infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute q-KG gradients efficiently and enable gradient-based optimization.  
- Generalizes sequential knowledge gradient (KG) to the parallel setting and addresses limitations of greedy parallel EI/UCB methods.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation with explicit mathematical formulation and algorithmic details.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world hyperparameter tuning tasks, notably in noisy settings.  
- Provides code availability, aiding reproducibility.  
- Insufficient detail on search-space discretization (M=1000 posterior samples) and its implementation.  
- Lacks sensitivity analysis on M and comparison to other discretization strategies.  
- GP hyperparameter handling in noisy experiments (MLE) is underspecified, including which hyperparameters are estimated and runtime impact.  
- Missing detailed computational-cost (e.g., wall-clock time) comparisons against benchmarks to evaluate efficiency trade-offs.  
- Related-work section is thorough but should more explicitly differentiate the contribution from nested batch KG [26] and similar methods.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing discretization details, computational-cost analysis, and clearer connections to related work.
