- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), a novel acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization.
- q-KG generalizes the sequential Knowledge Gradient to jointly evaluate a batch of q points.
- Acquisition quantifies expected improvement in the final recommended solution via the posterior-mean minimum.
- Provides a one-step Bayes-optimal selection criterion for batch selection aimed at minimizing simple regret.
- Naive maximization is intractable; authors derive an efficient optimization strategy using an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator.
- Uses Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to obtain the stochastic gradient for q-KG.
- Enables use of standard gradient-based optimizers for acquisition maximization.
- Empirical evaluation on four synthetic benchmarks and two real hyperparameter tuning tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR-10 CNN).
- Demonstrates competitive or superior performance to state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.
- Positions contribution clearly relative to EI- and UCB-based parallel BO methods and prior KG work.
- High technical quality: sound decision-theoretic derivation and non-trivial IPA application.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes code for reproducibility.
- Originality: novel batch extension of KG and novel use of IPA for scalable gradient-based optimization.
- Significance: practical importance for parallel optimization and hyperparameter tuning; robust in noisy real-world scenarios.
- Strength: principled, one-step Bayes-optimal approach compared to greedy heuristics.
- Strength: efficient stochastic-gradient optimization makes the method practical.
- Strength: strong empirical performance across benchmarks and noise regimes.
- Weakness: lacks wall-clock computational cost analysis and discussion of trade-offs vs. baselines.
- Weakness: sensitivity to discretization (An, M=1000) not fully analyzed, which may hurt high-dimensional problems.
- Weakness: experimental plots need explicit definition of the "log10 immediate regret" and clarification of which x_n* is used.
