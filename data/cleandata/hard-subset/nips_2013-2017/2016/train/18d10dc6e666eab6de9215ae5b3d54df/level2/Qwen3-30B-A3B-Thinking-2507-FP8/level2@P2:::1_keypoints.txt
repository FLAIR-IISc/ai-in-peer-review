- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects multiple points via a decision-theoretic acquisition function.
- Derives a Bayes-optimal acquisition for batch selection that maximizes expected improvement in the GP predictor's minimum (optimality holds for one-batch-remaining).
- Develops an efficient gradient estimator using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to make q-KG computationally practical.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic functions and real hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN), outperforming parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE.
- Shows consistent advantages in noisy evaluation settings, with empirical evidence of improved robustness to noise.
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation and clear connection to sequential knowledge gradient.
- Strength: clever and well-justified IPA-based computational approach addressing a key practical bottleneck.
- Strength: comprehensive experiments with appropriate baselines and noisy evaluation scenarios.
- Strength: well-written and clearly organized presentation.
- Weakness: “Bayes-optimal” claim is limited to the one-batch-remaining case and needs clearer qualification to avoid misleading readers.
- Weakness: insufficient computational complexity analysis—no bounds for gradient estimation or scalability discussion for large q/high dimensions.
- Weakness: lacks theoretical explanation for why q-KG excels in noisy settings; only empirical claims provided.
- Weakness: comparisons to related work (including authors’ prior gradient-based parallel EI) are insufficiently differentiated; need clearer contrast and novelty of IPA approach.
- Weakness: inadequate discussion of discretization approach for infinite domains (choice of M=1000 is arbitrary; no analysis of its impact).
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem (parallel BO) with theoretical grounding and potential impact for hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy real-world scenarios.
- Overall: solid, impactful contribution with fixable weaknesses; recommendation: Accept.
