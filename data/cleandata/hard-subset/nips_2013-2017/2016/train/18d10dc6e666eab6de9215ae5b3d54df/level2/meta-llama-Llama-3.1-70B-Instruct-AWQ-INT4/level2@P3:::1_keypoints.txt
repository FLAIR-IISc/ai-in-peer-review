- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm derived via decision-theoretic analysis for parallel evaluations.  
- Designs an efficient strategy to compute Bayes-optimal batches and shows q-KG finds global optima faster than prior batch BO methods.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is technically sound with thorough theoretical analysis and convincing empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Well-written and organized; clear notation and useful figures/tables, though GP background could be shortened or moved to an appendix.  
- Original contribution: introduces the parallel knowledge gradient acquisition function distinct from previous methods.  
- Significant implications for parallel Bayesian optimization and practical applications like hyperparameter tuning and experimental design.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, significant, technically solid, clearly presented, and empirically convincing.  
- Arguments for rejection: could be more concise; some derivations/details may be better placed in an appendix; limited discussion of method limitations.  
- Suggestions for improvement: condense/move background to appendix, add discussion of limitations and future work, include more empirical figures/tables, and provide implementation details.
