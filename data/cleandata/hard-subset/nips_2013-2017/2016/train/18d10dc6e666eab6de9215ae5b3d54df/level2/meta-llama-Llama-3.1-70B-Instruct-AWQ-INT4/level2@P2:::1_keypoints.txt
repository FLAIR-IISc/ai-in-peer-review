- Proposes the parallel knowledge gradient (KG) batch Bayesian optimization algorithm derived from a decision-theoretic analysis.  
- Provides a one-step Bayes-optimal batch selection and an efficient strategy to compute that batch.  
- Empirically finds global optima significantly faster than prior batch BO methods on synthetic problems and real hyperparameter tuning.  
- Shows particular advantage when function evaluations are noisy.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and explains the method and advantages clearly.  
- Includes a thorough related-work review situating the method within Bayesian optimization.  
- Main contribution is the parallel KG method, a notable improvement over previous batch BO algorithms.  
- Decision-theoretic derivation is a key strength, giving a principled basis for batch selection.  
- Evaluation is thorough with comparative experiments demonstrating substantial performance gains.  
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis and more explicit comparison of runtime costs.  
- Does not provide an in-depth discussion of the method’s limitations or mitigation strategies.  
- Reviewer ratings: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant, novel contribution; strong empirical validation; clear presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: missing complexity analysis; limited discussion of limitations; potentially too specialized for a general audience.
