This paper introduces the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian Optimization (BO). The core idea is to extend the Knowledge Gradient (KG) approach, which is Bayes-optimal for single-point selection, to the parallel setting where *q* points can be evaluated simultaneously. The authors derive an acquisition function, q-KG, and develop an efficient gradient-based method (using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis - IPA) to optimize it, addressing the computational challenges that arise with larger batch sizes. The experimental results demonstrate that q-KG consistently outperforms existing parallel BO algorithms, particularly in noisy evaluations, on both synthetic functions and practical machine learning hyperparameter tuning problems.

**Strengths:**

* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The method is grounded in decision theory, specifically aiming for the Bayes-optimal solution in the final iteration. This provides a strong justification for the proposed acquisition function.
* **Novelty:** Extending the KG method to the parallel setting is a valuable contribution. While parallel BO has been explored, framing it through the lens of the knowledge gradient is a novel and insightful approach.
* **Computational Efficiency:** Recognizing the computational cost of maximizing the q-KG function and developing an efficient gradient-based optimization technique (IPA) is a significant strength. This makes the method practical for higher-dimensional problems and larger batch sizes.
* **Empirical Validation:** Thorough experiments on synthetic functions *and* practical machine learning tasks (logistic regression and CNN tuning) provide strong evidence of the effectiveness of q-KG. The performance gains, especially in the presence of noise, are compelling.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The notation is clear, and the explanations are accessible. The algorithm is explicitly stated.
* **Code Availability:** The public release of the code facilitates reproducibility and encourages further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Discretization of A:** While the authors address the infinite dimensionality of the search space *A* with discretization, the choice of M=1000 samples, and specifically *how* these samples are chosen (global optima of the GP posterior) might be sensitive to the problem. A more detailed discussion of the impact of this discretization and alternatives would be beneficial.  The paper doesn't fully articulate the trade-offs between discretization granularity and computational cost.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of BO algorithms is known to be sensitive to GP hyperparameters. While the authors estimate these using MLE, a more detailed analysis of how these estimates impact the performance of q-KG, particularly in comparison to the benchmark algorithms, would be valuable.
* **Comparison Depth:**  While the comparisons are comprehensive, a deeper analysis of *why* q-KG outperforms the benchmarks, beyond simply stating it does, would be helpful. For example, are there specific characteristics of the problem where q-KG shines?
* **Limited Scalability Discussion:** While the IPA method improves computational efficiency, the paper doesn't explicitly address the scalability limitations for very high-dimensional problems or extremely large batch sizes *q*.  A discussion of potential bottlenecks and future directions for addressing scalability would strengthen the paper.
* **Related Work Nuances:** The discussion of related work, while generally good, could be sharpened. For example, the connection to “nested batch knowledge gradient” [26] feels somewhat dismissive. Acknowledging that both approaches leverage the knowledge gradient but differ in computational tractability would be more nuanced.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to parallel Bayesian optimization.
* The proposed method consistently outperforms existing algorithms in a range of experiments.
* The authors address a key challenge – computational efficiency – with a practical and well-motivated technique.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and future research.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The discretization strategy for the search space could be a limiting factor.
* The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the algorithm’s behavior and limitations.
* The discussion of related work could be more nuanced.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The q-KG method is well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated. The weaknesses identified are relatively minor and do not detract substantially from the overall quality of the work.  I believe this paper is suitable for acceptance to NIPS, though addressing the comments regarding discretization and a deeper analysis of the results would further strengthen it.

**Recommendation:** **Accept**
