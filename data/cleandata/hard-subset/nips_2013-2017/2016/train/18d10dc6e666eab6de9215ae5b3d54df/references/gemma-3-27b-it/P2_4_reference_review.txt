## Review of "The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a novel batch Bayesian Optimization (BO) method, the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), designed for scenarios with parallel function evaluations. The core idea is to extend the sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) approach to the batch setting by maximizing the expected incremental value of a batch of samples, rather than relying on iterative greedy selection like many existing parallel BO algorithms. The authors address the computational challenge of optimizing the q-KG acquisition function by employing infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate its gradient. They demonstrate through experiments on synthetic functions and practical machine learning tuning tasks that q-KG consistently outperforms existing parallel BO methods, especially in noisy evaluation settings.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The q-KG method provides a principled approach to parallel BO that considers the batch of points collectively, a departure from many existing methods that optimize single-point acquisitions iteratively. This is a significant contribution.
* **Theoretical Foundation:**  The method is grounded in decision theory, aiming for Bayes-optimality in the single-batch remaining scenario, giving it a strong theoretical basis.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The use of IPA to enable gradient-based optimization of the q-KG function is a clever and crucial contribution, allowing for practical application of the method, especially for larger batch sizes.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate the superior performance of q-KG compared to several benchmark algorithms, particularly in noisy settings.  The results on both synthetic and real-world problems (logistic regression & CNN tuning) strengthen the practical relevance of the work.
* **Code Availability:** Providing publicly available code is a significant positive aspect, promoting reproducibility and facilitating further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Discretization of Search Space:** The reliance on discretizing the search space (A) for gradient calculation, while necessary for practical implementation, introduces an approximation that could impact performance.  The impact of the discretization parameter *M* is not deeply explored in the experiments.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper demonstrates strong performance, it lacks a detailed analysis of q-KG’s sensitivity to its own hyperparameters (e.g., the choice of the kernel, the learning rate used in the gradient ascent optimization of the acquisition function).
* **Comparison Depth:** While compared to several methods, the comparisons could be more exhaustive. For example, comparing against more recent parallel BO algorithms introduced *after* the methods cited in the related work section would be beneficial.
* **Clarity of Notation:** Some notation, particularly in Section 5, could benefit from clearer explanation. While technically correct, it might be challenging for readers unfamiliar with IPA to follow.



**Addressing the Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The derivation of q-KG and the IPA-based gradient estimation are mathematically well-defined and justified.  The experiments provide convincing evidence for the effectiveness of the proposed method. The authors do acknowledge limitations of the method (discretization).
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, though, as mentioned above, certain sections (particularly Section 5) could benefit from greater clarity in notation and explanation. The flow of the argument is logical.
* **Originality:** The approach of extending KG to a parallel batch setting with a principled decision-theoretic framework *is* original. While building upon existing work on KG and parallel BO, the combination is novel and the IPA-based optimization is a significant contribution. The authors adequately cite related work.
* **Significance:**  The potential impact of q-KG is significant. Parallel BO is crucial for expensive black-box optimization, and a method that demonstrably outperforms existing approaches, especially in noisy environments, is valuable.  The applicability to tuning machine learning algorithms further underscores the practical significance of this work.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundation and novelty of the q-KG approach.
* Demonstrated superior empirical performance compared to existing methods.
* Efficient gradient-based optimization via IPA.
* Availability of code for reproducibility.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Dependence on discretization of the search space.
* Limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity.
* Relatively shallow comparison to recent parallel BO algorithms.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a **strong contribution** to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The q-KG method is a novel, theoretically sound, and empirically validated approach to parallel BO. While there are minor weaknesses regarding clarity and experimental thoroughness, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept** with minor revisions. I recommend the authors:

1.  Provide a more detailed analysis of the impact of the discretization parameter *M* on performance.
2.  Include a discussion of q-KG's sensitivity to its own hyperparameters.
3.  Expand the comparison to include more recent parallel BO algorithms.
4.  Improve the clarity of the notation and explanations in Section 5.
