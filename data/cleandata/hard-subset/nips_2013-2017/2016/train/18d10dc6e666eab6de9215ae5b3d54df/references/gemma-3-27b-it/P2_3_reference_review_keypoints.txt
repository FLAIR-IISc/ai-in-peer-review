- Summary: Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method extending sequential Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations, with IPA-based gradient optimization and strong empirical gains, especially under noise.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation, deriving q-KG from decision-theoretic and KG Bayes-optimality principles.

- Strength: Computational efficiency via infinitesimal perturbation analysis for scalable gradient estimation of the q-KG acquisition function.

- Strength: Empirical performance consistently improves over existing parallel BO methods on synthetic and real tasks, notably in noisy settings.

- Strength: Clear presentation with a well-described algorithm (Algorithm 1) and detailed computational explanation.

- Strength: Code released, aiding reproducibility and community adoption.

- Weakness: Search-space discretization approach (sampling GP optima + past points) is ad-hoc and its sensitivity to sample count (M=1000) is not explored.

- Weakness: Limited analysis of method hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., learning rates, kernel choices).

- Weakness: Related work section omits some recent parallel BO advances (e.g., deep GPs, advanced exploration-exploitation methods).

- Weakness: Experimental scope is limited; more complex/high-dimensional tasks (e.g., NAS) would better demonstrate scalability and generality.

- Assessment — Quality: Technically sound with well-justified derivations and convincing experimental results (Strong).

- Assessment — Clarity: Well-written and organized; concepts and algorithm are clear (Strong).

- Assessment — Originality: Novel extension of KG to batch setting and IPA-based gradient estimation (Strong).

- Assessment — Significance: Potentially impactful for efficient BO in expensive evaluation settings, especially noisy problems (Strong).

- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded method with efficient optimization and strong empirical results that benefit BO practice.

- Against acceptance: Needs improved discretization strategy, more thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and broader comparisons to recent state-of-the-art.

- Overall recommendation: Strong Accept — high-quality, significant contribution suitable for NIPS.
