- Proposes q-KG: a parallel knowledge gradient method that jointly selects a batch of points using IPA-based gradient estimates, showing strong performance especially in noisy settings.

- Theoretical strength: grounded in decision-theoretic framework and Bayes-optimal when only one batch remains.

- Computational innovation: uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis to efficiently estimate gradients, enabling practical optimization for larger batch sizes.

- Empirical strength: thorough experiments on synthetic and real ML tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN) demonstrating consistent improvements over baselines, notably in noisy regimes.

- Clear differentiation: highlights collective batch optimization versus iterative batch constructions (e.g., parallel EI, GP-BUCB).

- Theoretical limitation: lacks guarantees for the multi-batch setting and unclear if sequential use of q-KG retains any optimality.

- Discretization concern: the posterior-optima sampling discretization in Section 5.3 lacks theoretical justification and analysis of convergence or discretization error.

- Insufficient comparison with related work [26]: need clearer distinctions from nested batch KG, especially for continuous vs. discrete domains.

- Missing complexity analysis: no detailed account of how IPA-based gradient estimation scales with batch size q and input dimensionality d.

- Overall assessment: strong, practically valuable contribution with solid one-batch theory and compelling empirical results; main gaps are theoretical (multi-batch and discretization).

- Recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to address multi-batch theoretical guarantees and discretization analysis.
