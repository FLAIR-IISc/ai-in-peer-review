This paper introduces the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian Optimization (BO). The core idea is to extend the Knowledge Gradient (KG) acquisition function to the parallel setting, allowing for the simultaneous evaluation of multiple points. The authors derive this function using a decision-theoretic analysis, aiming to optimize for the average-case performance when a single batch of points remains. To address the computational complexity of maximizing q-KG, they propose an efficient gradient-based optimization method utilizing infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA). The method is evaluated on both synthetic functions and real-world machine learning hyperparameter tuning tasks, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing parallel BO algorithms, particularly in noisy settings.

The paper is well-structured and logically presented. It clearly outlines the motivation, contributions, and methodology. The related work section is comprehensive, referencing relevant publications in parallel BO and KG-based optimization. The mathematical derivations, while dense, appear correct and are generally well-explained, and the link to prior work on KG is clearly established. The introduction of IPA to address the computational challenge is a significant contribution, enabling the practical application of the q-KG function. The empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing q-KG against several established baselines on a variety of problems. The code availability is also a positive aspect.

However, the paper lacks some clarity in a few areas. While the motivation for extending KG to the parallel setting is clear, the practical benefits of this approach over simpler alternatives (e.g., parallel EI) aren't fully elucidated beyond the empirical results. The explanation of the discretization method in Section 5.3 is somewhat brief, and the choice of M=1000 feels arbitrary; a justification or sensitivity analysis would be helpful. The presentation of the experimental results, particularly in the tables, could be improved with clearer formatting and statistical significance tests. While the authors state the advantages of q-KG in noisy settings, a more detailed analysis of *why* it performs better would be valuable. Further, the paper relies heavily on established baselines, but a brief discussion on why those baselines may struggle in certain cases would enhance understanding.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The extension of the KG acquisition function to the parallel setting is a significant contribution.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of q-KG and the use of IPA for efficient optimization appear mathematically sound.
*   **Empirical Performance:** The results demonstrate that q-KG consistently outperforms existing methods on both synthetic and real-world problems.
*   **Clarity (Overall):** The paper is well-written and logically organized.
*   **Code Availability:** Providing code fosters reproducibility and encourages further research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity (Specific Areas):**  Discretization method and justification of parameter choices could be more detailed.
*   **Limited Explanation:** A deeper understanding of *why* q-KG excels in noisy settings is lacking.
*   **Presentation of Results:**  Tables could benefit from clearer formatting and statistical significance.
*   **Lack of comparative analysis:** Discussion on why the baselines perform poorly.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound method for parallel Bayesian Optimization.
*   The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of q-KG across a range of problems.
*   The introduction of IPA addresses a crucial computational challenge, making the method practical.
*   The paper is well-written and contributes to a growing body of research in parallel BO.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Some areas of the paper lack sufficient clarity and detail.
*   The paper could benefit from a deeper analysis of the method’s strengths and weaknesses.
*   The presentation of results could be improved with statistical significance testing and better formatting.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. While there are some areas for improvement, the novelty, technical soundness, and empirical performance of q-KG are compelling. The paper addresses a relevant and challenging problem and offers a promising solution. Therefore, I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the clarity and presentation issues outlined above.
