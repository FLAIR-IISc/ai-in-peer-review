- Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch BO method extending sequential KG to select batches collectively rather than iteratively.  
- Proposes an IPA-based gradient estimator to efficiently maximize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, showing consistent improvements over parallel EI/UCB variants on synthetic and real ML tuning tasks, especially in noisy settings.  
- Well-written and clearly organized paper with precise algorithm description and helpful figures.  
- Claims Bayes-optimality for the parallel policy when one decision remains, but provides no formal proof or rigorous justification.  
- Computational complexity analysis is limited: no concrete complexity bounds or runtime comparisons with other methods.  
- Treatment of noise is insufficiently explained—unclear how noise variance is handled in the q-KG acquisition during optimization.  
- Discretization strategy in Section 5.3 is vague about how samples from the posterior optima are drawn.  
- Originality is strong: first application of KG to batch BO and a novel continuous-domain IPA gradient technique.  
- Could better contextualize differences from parallel EI methods and related batch BO literature.  
- Scalability with larger batch sizes (q) is not thoroughly discussed or evaluated.  
- Practical significance is high: notable real-world gains (e.g., CIFAR10 hyperparameter tuning improvement).  
- Strengths: well-motivated theory, novel gradient method, broad empirical validation, clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: insufficient theoretical proof, limited complexity/scalability analysis, vague discretization and noise handling.  
- Recommended revisions: provide a formal Bayes-optimality justification, add complexity/runtime comparisons, clarify discretization and noise handling, and discuss scaling with q.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
