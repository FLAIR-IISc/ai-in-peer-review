## Review of "The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of parallel Bayesian Optimization (BO). The proposed Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method offers a principled, decision-theoretic approach to batch acquisition, demonstrably outperforming existing methods, particularly in noisy settings. While the technical details are dense and require careful reading, the paper is generally well-written and provides convincing empirical evidence for its effectiveness. I recommend acceptance, with minor revisions to improve clarity and potentially expand on the discussion of limitations.

**Summary of Main Ideas:**

The paper addresses the challenge of efficiently exploring the objective function in parallel BO, where multiple evaluations can be performed simultaneously.  Existing parallel BO methods often rely on iteratively adding points based on single-evaluation acquisition functions (like EI or UCB).  q-KG departs from this greedy approach by formulating a batch acquisition function that directly optimizes the expected improvement in solution quality resulting from evaluating a *batch* of q points.  This is rooted in a Bayes-optimal decision-theoretic framework, considering the remaining single batch of samples.  Crucially, the authors address the computational intractability of maximizing q-KG by leveraging infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute its gradient.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper positions itself effectively within the existing BO literature, acknowledging the shift from sequential to parallel optimization and correctly identifying limitations of previous parallel BO approaches. The authors clearly differentiate q-KG from existing methods such as parallel EI, batch UCB, and GP-UCB-PE. They also highlight the connection to the sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) method, pointing out the generalization to the parallel setting and the necessary adaptations for computational feasibility.  The comparison to recent work on nested batch knowledge gradient [26] is particularly insightful, highlighting the differences in computational approach and applicable problem settings.  The referencing is thorough, and demonstrates a good understanding of the field.

**Strengths:**

* **Principled Approach:** The foundation of q-KG in decision theory and the KG framework provides a strong theoretical justification for its effectiveness.  Considering the batch as a whole, rather than iteratively selecting points, is a significant advantage.
* **Strong Empirical Results:**  The experiments on both synthetic functions and practical problems (logistic regression and CNN tuning) consistently demonstrate q-KG’s superior performance, especially in the presence of noise.  The fact that it improves upon well-established benchmarks is compelling.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The use of IPA to efficiently compute the gradient of q-KG is a key contribution. Without this, maximizing the batch acquisition function would be prohibitively expensive.
* **Clear Code Availability:** Providing code promotes reproducibility and encourages further research in this area.
* **Thorough Comparison:** The paper compares against a solid set of baselines, including well-maintained implementations (MOE, Gpoptimization).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Technical Density:** The mathematical details, particularly in Section 5, are quite dense and could benefit from more intuitive explanations or intermediate steps.  Readers unfamiliar with IPA may struggle to follow the derivation.
* **Discretization of A:** While the discussion of discretizing the search space A is present, more detail on the impact of the number of samples (M=1000) on performance could be helpful. Is this a sensitive parameter? What strategies could be used to dynamically adapt M?
* **Limited Discussion of Limitations:**  The paper could benefit from a more explicit discussion of the limitations of q-KG.  For example, does it perform well in very high-dimensional spaces? Are there specific types of objective functions where it might struggle?  What are the computational trade-offs associated with increasing the batch size (q)?
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the results are strong, it would be informative to briefly discuss the sensitivity of q-KG’s performance to its own hyperparameters (e.g., the choice of kernel parameters, the optimization algorithm used to maximize q-KG).



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* **Novelty:** q-KG introduces a genuinely novel approach to parallel BO with a strong theoretical foundation.
* **Impact:** The method has the potential to significantly improve the efficiency of expensive optimization tasks in various domains, particularly in machine learning hyperparameter tuning.
* **Soundness:** The technical arguments are well-supported by theoretical analysis and compelling empirical results.
* **Reproducibility:** The availability of code promotes reproducibility and encourages further research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* **Technical Complexity:** The mathematical details may be challenging for some readers. (However, this is mitigated by the clear presentation and strong results).
* **Limited Discussion of Limitations:**  A more thorough discussion of limitations would strengthen the paper.



**Specific Suggestions for Improvement:**

* **Section 5 (IPA):** Consider adding more intermediate steps or intuitive explanations to clarify the derivation of the gradient estimator.  Perhaps a brief example illustrating how IPA works in a simpler context would be helpful.
* **Section 5.3 (Discretization):** Discuss the impact of the number of samples (M) on performance.
* **Section 6:**  Include a brief analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity.
* **Conclusion:** Expand the discussion of limitations and future work.


**Overall, I strongly recommend accepting this paper. It presents a significant advance in the field of parallel Bayesian Optimization, with a sound theoretical foundation, strong empirical results, and practical relevance.**  Addressing the minor suggestions above will further enhance its clarity and impact.
