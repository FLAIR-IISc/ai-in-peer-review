- Introduces q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method that selects batches by maximizing expected incremental solution quality collectively.
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition for optimization.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance of q-KG over parallel EI, batch UCB, and related methods, especially in noisy settings.
- Positions the work within parallel BO literature, differentiating batch-wise optimization from greedy iterative approaches.
- Clearly connects to the original Knowledge Gradient and cites related gradient-based parallel EI and nested KG work, noting prior limitations.
- Novelty: q-KG is a principled, batch-wise acquisition that optimizes information gain across the whole batch.
- Theoretical foundation: decision-theoretic derivation and Bayes-optimality argument for single-batch case are solid.
- Computational contribution: IPA-based gradient estimation significantly improves practical optimization of q-KG.
- Empirical strength: consistent improvements on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, with code released for reproducibility.
- Writing and organization are clear, with consistent notation and an explicitly described algorithm.
- Discretization concern: reliance on sampling posterior optima (M=1000) may be sensitive to GP accuracy and lacks justification.
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper omits analysis of q-KG’s sensitivity to its own tuning parameters beyond M.
- Scalability concern: limited discussion of applicability to very high-dimensional problems and covariance inversion costs.
- Limited empirical comparisons: should include more recent parallel BO baselines to strengthen evaluation.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and well-motivated contribution with minor issues that do not undermine the work.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled approach, strong theory, effective IPA technique, compelling empirical results, clear writing, and available code.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient justification of discretization, missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and incomplete scalability discussion.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Required revisions: justify and analyze discretization strategy and M sensitivity, discuss hyperparameter robustness, expand scalability discussion and mitigation strategies, and consider comparisons to newer parallel BO methods.
