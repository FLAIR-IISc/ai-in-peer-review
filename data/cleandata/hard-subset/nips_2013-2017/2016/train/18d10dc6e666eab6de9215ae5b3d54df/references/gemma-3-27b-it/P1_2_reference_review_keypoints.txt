- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic batched BO acquisition that maximizes expected information gain for a batch of q points.  
- Derives q-KG from the sequential Knowledge Gradient principle, considering the optimal final action.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to obtain efficient gradient estimates for optimizing q-KG.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms existing parallel BO methods on synthetic and real ML hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noise.  
- Positions work relative to parallel BO literature and contrasts joint batch optimization with iterative single-point batch construction.  
- Clearly connects to sequential KG and highlights limitations of prior nested KG approaches.  
- References are comprehensive and relevant.  
- Strong theoretical foundation via decision-theoretic derivation of the acquisition function.  
- IPA-based gradient optimization addresses computational challenges and enables scalability to larger batches.  
- Consistent empirical superiority across noisy and noiseless experiments strengthens practical claims.  
- Well-written paper with clear notation and a straightforward algorithm presentation.  
- Code is provided, aiding reproducibility.  
- Discretization of the domain A is somewhat ad-hoc and lacks sensitivity analysis on the number of samples M.  
- Limited exploration of q-KG’s sensitivity to its own hyperparameters (GP kernel and optimization settings).  
- Baseline comparisons lack detailed hyperparameter tuning descriptions, raising fairness concerns.  
- No rigorous computational complexity analysis of q-KG versus baselines as q increases.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; effective IPA solution; strong empirical performance; code availability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weaker justification/analysis of discretization, incomplete hyperparameter sensitivity study, and missing computational-complexity comparisons.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with minor weaknesses that do not substantially detract from quality.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address discretization, hyperparameter sensitivity, and computational-complexity/baseline-tuning details.
