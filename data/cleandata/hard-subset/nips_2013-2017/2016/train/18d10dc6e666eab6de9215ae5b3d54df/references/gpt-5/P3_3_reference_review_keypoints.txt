- Proposes q-KG, a batch BO acquisition that generalizes KG to parallel setting by valuing expected decrease in posterior mean minimum and uses a reparameterized Monte Carlo estimator plus IPA-based gradient.

- Discretizes the inner argmin over a dynamic finite set An composed of posterior-minimizer samples, past points, and the candidate batch.

- Empirically outperforms batch EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, particularly in noisy settings; code provided.

- Extends classical KG to q>1 and reduces to q-EI in the noiseless, restricted-recommendation case.

- Related to batch ES/PPES and batch UCB methods but optimizes a distinct one-step Bayes-optimal objective.

- Employs gradient estimators in the spirit of q-EI optimization, adapted to the q-KG functional.

- Strength: conceptually clean, decision-theoretic derivation with clear relation to q-EI.

- Strength: sound Gaussian reparameterization and IPA-based stochastic gradient enabling practical optimization.

- Strength: compelling empirical results under noise and reproducible implementation.

- Weakness: discretization of An is crucial—An depends on z, making the objective piecewise constant and complicating gradients; unclear if An is held fixed during optimization.

- Weakness: no ablation or sensitivity analysis on M (size of An) or on the posterior-minimizer sampler; lacks practical guidance tradeoffs.

- Weakness: Bayes-optimality only holds for the myopic one-batch objective; no discussion of multi-round regret or horizon effects.

- Weakness: scalability concerns—complexity at least O(q^3) per batch and O(Mq) per MC sample; needs concrete complexity and wall-clock comparisons at larger q and d.

- Weakness: missing direct comparisons to PPES and other information-theoretic batch methods; unclear whether q-EI baselines use state-of-the-art implementations.

- Weakness: gradient estimator assumptions (differentiability, unique argmin) and variance-reduction strategies are under-specified; tie-handling needs clarification.

- Weakness: GP hyperparameter fitting by MLE each iteration can be unstable in low-data/noisy regimes; priors/MAP or sensitivity analysis recommended.

- Clarity: overall well written but with minor section numbering issues; posterior minimizer sampling needs concise algorithmic description or precise citation.

- Suggest adding complexity notes and describing implementation choices for An during optimization to improve reproducibility.

- Impact: batch BO is important; q-KG is a principled, noise-robust alternative likely to be adopted by practitioners.

- Pros summary: principled derivation, practical gradient estimator, strong noisy performance, and code availability.

- Cons summary: reliance on discretization without sensitivity analysis, missing PPES comparisons, limited theoretical guarantees beyond one-step optimality, and insufficient scalability/runtime analysis.

- Reviewer requests: clarify whether An is fixed during gradient steps; ablate M and An strategies; compare to PPES and state-of-the-art q-EI with wall-clock times; discuss variance-reduction and MC sample guidance; provide per-iteration complexity in q, M, and n.

- Recommendation: Accept (strong); Confidence: High.
