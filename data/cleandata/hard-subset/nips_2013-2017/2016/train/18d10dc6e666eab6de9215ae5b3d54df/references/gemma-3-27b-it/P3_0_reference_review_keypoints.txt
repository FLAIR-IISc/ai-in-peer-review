- Overall assessment: Strong, well-motivated contribution to parallel Bayesian Optimization with recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  
- Summary/related work: Proposes q-KG, a collective batch acquisition extending Knowledge Gradient to parallel BO with IPA-based gradient estimation and situates it clearly among greedy and collective batch methods.  
- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation linking q-KG to decision theory and KG optimality for one remaining batch.  
- Strength: Novelty in jointly optimizing batch information gain rather than using greedy, iterative point selection.  
- Strength: Efficient gradient estimation via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) addressing a key practical bottleneck.  
- Strength: Empirical results show consistent superior performance versus parallel EI and UCB variants, notably under noisy evaluations.  
- Strength: Publicly available code enhances reproducibility.  
- Strength: Clear and well-written exposition of technical details.  
- Weakness: Computational cost remains a concern for large batch sizes and high-dimensional problems; scalability details are lacking.  
- Weakness: Discretization of the search space A introduces approximation; sensitivity to discretization resolution and choice of M is not fully explored.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter sensitivity (kernels, noise levels, optimization settings) is not analyzed; robustness is unclear.  
- Weakness: Limited exploration of theoretical implications such as regret bounds or convergence guarantees for q-KG.  
- For acceptance: Method is theoretically grounded, novel, empirically strong, and accompanied by reproducible code.  
- Against acceptance: Remaining computational and discretization concerns for large-scale problems warrant further study.  
- Question: Provide detailed computational complexity analysis of maximizing q-KG as a function of q and dimensionality.  
- Question: How does performance vary with M (discretization samples), and is there a heuristic for choosing M?  
- Question: What is q-KG’s sensitivity to kernel hyperparameters, noise levels, and optimization algorithm choices?  
- Question: Have further optimizations to the IPA-based gradient estimation been considered to improve efficiency?  
- Question: Can you discuss potential theoretical regret bounds or convergence properties for q-KG?
