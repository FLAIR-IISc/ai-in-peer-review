- Presents q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method that extends Knowledge Gradient to select batches by maximizing expected incremental batch value and uses IPA for gradient estimation.  
- Demonstrates q-KG outperforms existing parallel BO methods, especially in noisy settings, on synthetic and ML tuning tasks.  

Strengths:
- Novelty: principled batch-aware KG approach rather than iterative single-point selection.  
- Theoretical foundation: decision-theoretic grounding aiming for Bayes-optimality in the single-batch remaining scenario.  
- Computational efficiency: IPA enables practical gradient-based optimization of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical performance: consistently strong results on synthetic and real-world problems (logistic regression, CNN tuning).  
- Code availability: publicly released code improves reproducibility.

Weaknesses:
- Discretization: relies on discretizing the search space for gradient calculation, introducing approximation; impact of discretization parameter M not deeply explored.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks analysis of sensitivity to kernel choices and optimization hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate).  
- Comparison depth: comparisons omit some more recent parallel BO algorithms and could be more exhaustive.  
- Clarity: some notation (notably Section 5) is hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with IPA.

Review criteria summary:
- Quality: technically sound derivation and IPA-based gradient estimation with convincing experiments.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but certain sections need clearer notation/explanations.  
- Originality: original combination of KG extension to batch setting with IPA-based optimization.  
- Significance: high potential impact for expensive black-box optimization and ML hyperparameter tuning.

For acceptance:
- Pros: strong theory, novelty, empirical superiority, efficient IPA, reproducible code.  
- Cons: dependence on discretization, limited hyperparameter analysis, relatively shallow comparison to recent methods.

Overall assessment:
- Strong contribution to Bayesian Optimization; recommend accept with minor revisions.

Recommended revisions:
- Provide detailed analysis of discretization parameter M’s impact.  
- Include discussion/analysis of q-KG’s hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Expand experimental comparisons to include more recent parallel BO algorithms.  
- Improve clarity and notation in Section 5.
