- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that generalizes the Knowledge Gradient to select q points by maximizing expected decrease in the GP posterior mean minimum.  
- Proves q-KG reduces to q-EI in the noiseless case when final recommendation is restricted to previously sampled points.  
- Uses standard-normal reparameterization plus a Monte Carlo estimator for intractable q-KG evaluations.  
- Introduces an IPA-based stochastic gradient estimator to efficiently optimize batched points jointly.  
- Discretizes continuous domains adaptively by sampling posterior optima plus previously sampled and candidate points.  
- Empirical results on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR-10) show q-KG is competitive or superior to q-EI, BUCB, GP-UCB-PE, especially under noisy evaluations; code released.  
- Positions work relative to sequential KG, q-EI, batch ES/PES, and BUCB/PE, and distinguishes from discrete nested batch KG.  
- IPA gradient estimator is analogous to reparameterization estimators for q-EI but adapted for the q-KG functional.  
- Decision-theoretic formulation is sound and the q-EI reduction argument is correct.  
- Monte Carlo reparameterization approach is standard and appropriate.  
- Technical issue: Section 5.2 incorrectly includes a derivative of the “before” term—μ(n) does not depend on z; this must be removed or justified.  
- Technical issue: when the discretized set A depends on z (Section 5.3) the unbiasedness/interchange argument for IPA is invalid unless handled explicitly.  
- The unbiasedness of the IPA gradient requires a.s. unique minimizers and smoothness of μ and K; precise conditions and measure-zero non-unique cases should be stated, and Cholesky differentiation handled carefully.  
- Discretization strategy can bias the objective and produce non-smooth dependence on z; analysis of approximation error vs. M and sensitivity to how A_n is constructed is needed.  
- Missing experimental details: wall-clock acquisition optimization cost as q and d vary, and number of MC samples used.  
- Missing ablations: sensitivity to M, MC budget, batch size q, and noise level.  
- Statistical reporting is incomplete: small number of runs for CIFAR-10 (5) and lack of consistent confidence intervals/variance bands.  
- Notational confusion around A (continuous domain vs discretized set); suggest distinct symbols and minor typo/grammar fixes.  
- Bayes-optimality claim should be formalized (finite vs continuous A) with a proof sketch or supplement pointer.  
- Novelty: extending KG to batch selection with a practical differentiable Monte Carlo estimator and IPA-based joint optimization is a meaningful and novel contribution.  
- Significance: relevant and potentially impactful for batch BO in hyperparameter tuning and simulation, with practical value due to released implementation.  
- Pros: principled KG-based foundation, practical gradient-based algorithm, code release, and strong empirical performance under noise.  
- Cons: gradient derivation gap, lack of runtime/scalability and sensitivity analyses, limited runs/CIs, and no theoretical consistency guarantees.  
- Suggestions for authors: fix gradient derivation and unbiasedness conditions, report wall-clock times and MC details, run ablations on M/MC budget, compare to batch PES/Thompson sampling variants, evaluate higher dimensions and larger q, and add confidence intervals/increase runs.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — valuable, principled contribution with strong empirical signals but needs clarifications and additional computational/sensitivity analysis.
