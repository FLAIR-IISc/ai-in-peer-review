This paper introduces the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian Optimization (BO). The core idea is to extend the knowledge gradient acquisition function to the parallel setting, allowing for simultaneous evaluation of multiple points, thereby improving efficiency, especially when function evaluations are expensive. The authors derive the q-KG acquisition function using a decision-theoretic analysis and propose an efficient gradient-based optimization method leveraging infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to address the computational challenges of maximizing q-KG, particularly as the batch size (q) increases. The method is evaluated on synthetic functions and practical machine learning hyperparameter tuning tasks, demonstrating superior performance compared to existing parallel BO algorithms, particularly in noisy settings.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper grounds its approach in solid decision-theoretic principles, extending the well-established knowledge gradient concept to the parallel domain. This provides a clear justification for the proposed method.
* **Novel Acquisition Function:** The q-KG acquisition function is a novel contribution, offering a principled way to balance exploration and exploitation in parallel BO. The consideration of the batch as a whole, rather than iteratively adding points, is a significant advantage.
* **Efficient Optimization:** The use of IPA to compute the gradient of q-KG is a key innovation, enabling practical optimization of the acquisition function even for larger batch sizes. This is crucial for real-world applications.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of q-KG across a range of problems, including both synthetic benchmarks and practical machine learning tuning tasks.  The results consistently show improvements over existing methods, particularly in noisy environments.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the methodology and experimental setup.  The algorithm is presented concisely.
* **Code Availability:** Providing the code is highly commendable and facilitates reproducibility and further research.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Discretization of A:** While the paper addresses the challenge of an infinite domain A through discretization, the method described (drawing M samples from the posterior) could be sensitive to the choice of M and may not be optimal for all problem structures. A more detailed discussion of alternative discretization strategies or an analysis of the impact of M would be beneficial.
* **Computational Complexity:** Although IPA improves efficiency, the computational cost of evaluating and optimizing q-KG, especially for high-dimensional problems and large batch sizes, remains a concern.  A more detailed discussion of the computational scaling of the method and potential avenues for further optimization would strengthen the paper.
* **Limited Comparison:** While the paper compares against several relevant baselines, including parallel EI, UCB, and ES, a comparison to more recent parallel BO methods (appearing *after* the NIPS 2016 submission date) would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the proposed method’s current standing in the field. For example, methods utilizing diversity promotion or multi-objective acquisition functions.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of q-KG may be sensitive to the hyperparameters of the Gaussian process and the choice of optimization algorithm used to maximize the acquisition function. A more thorough sensitivity analysis would be valuable.
* **Assumptions of Gaussian Noise:** The method assumes Gaussian noise, which may not hold for all real-world applications. Discussing the potential impact of non-Gaussian noise or suggesting extensions to handle other noise distributions would broaden the applicability of the method.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to parallel Bayesian Optimization.
* The proposed method demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods in challenging scenarios (e.g., noisy evaluations).
* The use of IPA for efficient gradient computation is a key contribution.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and future research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational complexity of the method could limit its scalability to high-dimensional problems.
* The discretization strategy for infinite domains may require careful tuning.
* The comparison to recent parallel BO methods is limited.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The proposed q-KG method is theoretically well-motivated, empirically validated, and addresses a practical challenge in parallel optimization. While there are some weaknesses related to computational complexity, discretization, and comparison to recent work, these are outweighed by the strengths of the paper.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept.**  I recommend accepting this paper with the caveat that the authors consider addressing the weaknesses outlined above in future work. Specifically, exploring more sophisticated discretization methods, discussing computational scaling in more detail, and providing a comparison to more recent parallel BO techniques would further enhance the contribution of this work.
