- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition as a one-step Bayes-optimal policy minimizing the posterior minimum when one batch remains.  
- Generalizes sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) to the parallel (batch) setting.  
- Tackles intractability by expressing the updated posterior via a standard-normal draw Zq and Cholesky factorization.  
- Estimates q-KG with Monte Carlo over Zq and derives an unbiased IPA-based stochastic gradient for joint batch optimization.  
- Approximates the inner minimization in continuous domains by discretization using M samples from the posterior over the global minimizer plus sampled points.  
- Empirically compared to parallel EI (MOE, Spearmint), GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic functions and two ML tuning tasks.  
- Shows q-KG typically outperforms or matches baselines, with particularly strong gains under noisy observations.  
- Reduces to parallel EI in the noiseless case when the final recommendation is restricted to sampled points, serving as a consistency check.  
- Technical soundness: decision-theoretic derivation is clear and correct within the GP model; Monte Carlo and IPA approaches are plausible and claimed unbiased.  
- Needs brief clarification on handling ties and non-smoothness from the min operator (measure-zero argument mentioned but terse).  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of optimization convergence (e.g., to stationary points) and provides no regret or finite-sample guarantees.  
- Empirical omissions: no wall-clock/runtime or scalability analysis despite heavier acquisition cost.  
- Empirical omissions: only q=4 evaluated; scalability to larger batch sizes is untested.  
- CNN tuning experiment averages only 5 runs, which is a low number for robust conclusions.  
- Discretization strategy is under-described; no sensitivity analysis for M (e.g., M=1000) or for including z(1:q) in the candidate set.  
- Clarity: overall well written and organized, with helpful links to KG and EI; minor typos and notation inconsistencies remain.  
- Originality: novel extension of KG to batch continuous domains combined with an IPA-based stochastic gradient for joint optimization.  
- Related work: broadly covered, but empirical comparisons omit parallel entropy search (PES), an important competitor.  
- Significance: practically valuable for noisy batch BO and hyperparameter tuning; public code aids adoption, but lack of runtime/scalability data tempers impact.  
- Key requested clarifications: report wall-clock times and scaling with q and d; sensitivity to M and discretization; results for larger q and higher dimensions; handling non-unique argmins in gradients; comparisons to parallel PES; behavior under heteroskedastic/non-Gaussian noise.  
- Minor fixes requested: correct typos (e.g., “Main Stange”), ensure consistent Cholesky/matrix factor notation, and clarify the posterior-optimizer sampling procedure.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid, well-motivated contribution with empirical benefits in noisy settings but needs more runtime/scalability data and broader comparisons.
