## Peer Review: The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to parallel Bayesian Optimization (BO) – the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG). The method is theoretically sound, builds on existing work in sequential BO (Knowledge Gradient), and demonstrates promising empirical results, particularly in noisy evaluation scenarios. While the computational complexity is addressed through the use of IPA, the implementation details are somewhat terse, and the experimental evaluation could be broadened. I recommend **Accept** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper tackles the problem of efficient global optimization of expensive black-box functions using parallel BO.  Traditional BO methods often assume sequential evaluation.  This work extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method to the parallel setting, proposing q-KG, which aims to maximize information gain from a batch of *q* evaluations. The key idea is to choose the batch that optimizes the expected improvement in the solution quality if this batch were the final evaluation.  The authors clearly position their work within the existing literature on parallel BO, differentiating it from methods based on iterative greedy selection (e.g., parallel EI, UCB) and other batch methods that value points collectively. The relationship to the sequential KG method is also well-explained.  The authors correctly point out how q-KG recovers the parallel EI algorithm under specific conditions (noise-free, restricted solution space). The literature review is comprehensive and well-organized.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation of q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective is strong and well-motivated.  The goal of optimizing for the final solution quality provides a compelling justification for the proposed acquisition function.
* **Novelty:**  The q-KG approach represents a genuinely novel contribution to parallel BO, moving beyond simple extensions of sequential methods.  Jointly optimizing over the batch using the knowledge gradient is a significant departure from greedy strategies.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The use of Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute the gradient of q-KG is a crucial contribution. This addresses a major practical challenge associated with maximizing the acquisition function, especially for larger batch sizes.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results demonstrate that q-KG consistently outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art parallel BO algorithms, particularly in noisy evaluation settings. The improvement on noisy problems is particularly noteworthy.
* **Code Availability:** Providing publicly available code is excellent and facilitates reproducibility and further research.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Implementation Details:** While the paper outlines the approach to computing q-KG and its gradient, the practical implementation details are somewhat lacking.  For example, the discretization of the domain A (Section 5.3) relies on sampling from the global optima of the posterior distribution, but the specifics of this sampling process (e.g., number of samples, criteria for identifying global optima) aren’t fully described.
* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** The experimental evaluation, while thorough, could be broadened.  While synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks are valuable, it would be beneficial to evaluate q-KG on a wider range of real-world optimization problems.  Consider including problems from different domains (e.g., robotics, material science) to assess its generalizability.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't discuss the sensitivity of q-KG’s performance to its own hyperparameters (e.g., the number of samples used in the Monte Carlo estimation of q-KG, the parameters controlling the discretization of A). A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the results.
* **Comparison with Nested Batch KG:** The comparison to the nested-batch knowledge gradient policy [26] feels slightly dismissive. While the authors point out differences in computation, a more detailed comparison of their approaches, specifically regarding the challenges of gradient estimation, would be valuable.



**4. Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the empirical results are well-supported. The authors acknowledge the limitations of their method and discuss potential areas for future work.  The derivation of the gradient estimator using IPA is a strong point.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. However, as noted above, the implementation details could be more explicit.  The notation is consistent, and the explanations are generally clear.
* **Originality:** The q-KG method represents a novel contribution to the field of parallel BO.  The authors clearly differentiate their work from existing approaches.
* **Significance:** The potential impact of q-KG is significant.  Efficient parallel BO is crucial for many real-world applications, and q-KG offers a promising approach for improving optimization performance, particularly in noisy settings.  The method could be widely adopted by researchers and practitioners.

**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundation and novel approach.
*  Demonstrated superior performance compared to established methods, particularly in noisy settings.
*  Efficient gradient estimation via IPA.
*  Code availability promotes reproducibility.

**Against Acceptance:**

*  Lack of detailed implementation specifics.
*  Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
*  Missing sensitivity analysis of hyperparameters.



**6. Recommendation:**

**Accept** with minor revisions.  The authors should:

* **Expand on the implementation details**, particularly regarding the discretization of the domain A and the sampling process used to identify global optima.
* **Consider adding experiments** on a broader range of real-world optimization problems.
* **Include a sensitivity analysis** to assess the impact of q-KG’s hyperparameters on its performance.
* **Revisit the discussion of the nested-batch KG** and provide a more detailed comparison of its strengths and weaknesses relative to q-KG.
