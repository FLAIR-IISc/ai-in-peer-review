- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch BO method extending sequential KG to jointly optimize q points.  
- Key technical contribution: using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute gradients of the q-KG acquisition.  
- Thorough experiments on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks showing q-KG outperforms parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, especially in noisy settings.  
- Solid theoretical foundation by building on sequential KG with clear decision-theoretic justification.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance (e.g., ~3% test error improvement on CIFAR10 CNNs) and is well-written and organized.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of convergence properties for the batch q-KG extension of sequential KG.  
- Baseline comparisons are limited; missing comparisons to recent collective batch methods (e.g., gradient-based parallel EI [15], parallel entropy search [18]).  
- Insufficient discussion and analysis of computational complexity and scaling with batch size q versus alternatives.  
- Experimental results report means and stds but lack statistical significance testing to validate improvements.  
- Ambiguous/missing mathematical derivation of how q-KG reduces to parallel EI under noise-free and restricted recommendation conditions.  
- Limited discussion of method limitations: scaling with large q, sensitivity to discretization (M=1000), and failure scenarios.  
- Request to clarify why q-KG's objective (expected improvement in GP predictor minimum) is preferable to other batch objectives.  
- Need to address potential bias/unbiasedness of the IPA gradient estimator and its implications for optimization.  
- Clarify the noise model used in the noisy logistic regression experiment and why small test-set error is characterized as a "noisy approximation."  
- Paper should better position and compare itself to recent work that optimizes batches collectively, especially methods using gradient-based batch acquisition.  
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem (parallel evaluations for hyperparameter tuning) but would be strengthened by concrete time/resource savings examples.  
- Overall assessment: strong and useful contribution with solid experiments, but critical gaps in theory, baselines, and complexity discussion; reviewer recommends Weak Accept.  
- Suggested revisions: add batch convergence theory, compare with recent collective-batch methods ([15], [18]), provide detailed complexity analysis (as function of q), include statistical tests, discuss limitations and discretization sensitivity, derive q-KG → parallel EI relationship, and explain practical computational implications.
