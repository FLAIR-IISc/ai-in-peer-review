- Introduces q-KG, a principled acquisition function that extends the sequential Knowledge Gradient to select batches in parallel BO.  
- Formulates q-KG as maximizing the expected incremental value of an entire evaluation batch.  
- Develops an IPA-based gradient estimator to make optimizing q-KG computationally practical.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over existing parallel BO methods, especially in noisy settings, on synthetic and real tasks.  
- Positions the work well in relation to prior BO literature, clarifying differences from greedy parallel EI/UCB and nested batch KG.  
- Novelty: principled, theoretically grounded parallel generalization of KG.  
- Computational efficiency: IPA-based gradient estimation is a significant practical contribution.  
- Empirical performance: strong results across noise-free and noisy experiments, including hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Clarity and presentation: well-written derivations with supplementary technical details; code released for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to discretization of the search space A (number of samples M) is not thoroughly analyzed.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion and analysis of q-KG’s own hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., optimizer learning rates).  
- Weakness: computational cost remains significant for high-dimensional problems and large batch sizes; detailed complexity scaling is missing.  
- Weakness: limited comparisons to the most recent parallel BO algorithms post-2016.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, effective handling of computational challenges, strong empirical evidence, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved discretization sensitivity, remaining computational concerns, and limited hyperparameter/complexity analysis.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to parallel BO; strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommend acceptance.  
- Review rating and confidence: Strong Accept (8/10), Confidence 8/10.
