- Proposes q-KG, a batch BO acquisition generalizing Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations by maximizing expected reduction in posterior mean minimum.
- q-KG equals parallel EI in noiseless case when final recommendation is restricted to sampled points, but allows arbitrary recommendations and handles noise.
- Uses reparameterization with standard normal Zq for Monte Carlo evaluation and derives an IPA-based unbiased stochastic gradient estimator for batch optimization.
- Inner minimization over A is approximated by a data-dependent discretization sampling putative global minimizers (as in ES/PPES).
- Empirically outperforms parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning, with strong gains under observational noise.
- Decision-theoretic objective is well-motivated and Bayes-optimal for one remaining batch, properly generalizing classical KG.
- IPA gradient estimator is plausible but requires precise regularity conditions (uniqueness of argmin a.s., kernel/mean differentiability) and justification for differentiating through Cholesky.
- Paper lacks detailed optimization implementation: batch initialization, step sizes, stopping criteria, and MC sample counts are underspecified.
- Complexity analysis is missing; need per-iteration cost in terms of q, M, N_MC and cost of constructing kernel blocks over discretization.
- Experiments are broad and relevant but should include wall-clock acquisition optimization time and sensitivity to batch size q.
- Ablations on discretization size M and Monte Carlo budget are needed to assess runtime/accuracy trade-offs.
- Robustness to GP hyperparameter learning (MAP vs. marginalization) and heteroskedastic noise is not evaluated.
- Presentation is mostly clear but Eq. (4.1) notation and the dual role of A should be clarified; discretization sampling scheme should be explicitly described.
- Minor typos and notation inconsistencies (e.g., “Main Stange”, σ̃n vs D(n)) should be fixed.
- Novelty: extends KG to parallel setting with joint gradient-based optimization over continuous spaces, distinct from prior q-EI and entropy-based approaches.
- Significance: a noise-aware, jointly-optimized batch acquisition is valuable and likely impactful if computational overhead is manageable.
- Pros: principled Bayes-optimal extension, natural noise handling, practical stochastic gradient estimator, joint batch optimization, and code release.
- Cons: theoretical justification of IPA and Cholesky differentiation deferred, discretization underspecified, missing comparisons (PPES, parallel TS, local penalization), limited q values, and ignored hyperparameter uncertainty.
- Suggested improvements: add a theorem on unbiasedness of IPA gradients, cite/demonstrate Cholesky differentiation, detail discretization and sampling, include runtime/scalability results, add baselines (PPES, TS), and evaluate hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Recommendation: solid contribution with strong empirical noise performance; lean accept pending clearer theory and more thorough experimental characterization.
