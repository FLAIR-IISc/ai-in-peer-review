- Overall: Novel, well-motivated q-KG for parallel BO; theoretically sound with promising empirical results; recommend Accept with minor revisions.  
- Summary/Related Work: Extends Knowledge Gradient to batch setting by selecting batches that maximize expected final solution quality; clearly positioned against greedy batch methods and recovers parallel EI in special cases.  
- Strength: Theoretical soundness — decision-theoretic derivation that targets final solution quality.  
- Strength: Novelty — joint-batch KG is a substantive departure from greedy or simple extensions of sequential methods.  
- Strength: Computational efficiency — uses Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to compute q-KG gradients efficiently.  
- Strength: Empirical performance — outperforms or matches state-of-the-art, notably in noisy evaluation settings.  
- Strength: Code availability — authors provide public code for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Implementation details sparse — specifics of domain discretization and sampling for posterior optima are not fully described.  
- Weakness: Limited experimental scope — needs broader real-world benchmarks across domains (e.g., robotics, materials).  
- Weakness: Missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis — no study of Monte Carlo sample counts or discretization parameters.  
- Weakness: Comparison to nested-batch KG is brief — requires a deeper discussion, especially about gradient estimation challenges.  
- Qualitative: Paper is rigorous, clear, and significant, but would benefit from clearer implementation and expanded experiments.  
- For acceptance: Strong theory, improved noisy-performance, efficient gradient estimation, and public code.  
- Against acceptance: Insufficient implementation specifics, limited experiments, and absent sensitivity analysis.  
- Recommendation / Requested revisions: Accept with minor revisions — expand implementation details, add broader experiments, include sensitivity analysis, and provide a deeper comparison to nested-batch KG.
