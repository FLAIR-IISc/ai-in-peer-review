- Proposes q-KG, a parallel extension of the knowledge gradient acquisition function using a decision-theoretic formulation and IPA for gradient estimation.
- Evaluated on synthetic problems and real hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10), showing consistent improvements, especially in noisy settings.
- Novelty: collectively optimizes batches (q points) rather than using iterative greedy batch selection.
- Strength — Theoretical soundness and novelty: clear decision-theoretic derivation and novel use of IPA for gradient estimation.
- Strength — Comprehensive experiments: multiple synthetic and real tasks, comparisons to several state-of-the-art baselines, consistent superiority.
- Strength — Practical impact: demonstrable improvements on real ML hyperparameter tuning, e.g., CNN test error reduction.
- Strength — Clarity and presentation: well-written, clear mathematical derivations and algorithmic description.
- Strength — Code availability: authors provide public code, aiding reproducibility.
- Weakness — Scalability: lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity scaling with batch size q and dimensionality d.
- Weakness — Discretization choice: M=1000 sampling strategy is ad-hoc without rigorous justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness — Missing comparisons: does not compare against some recent batch BO methods (e.g., Parallel Predictive Entropy Search, multipoint EI).
- Weakness — High-dimensional limitations: experiments capped at 6D, unclear performance for typical higher-dimensional hyperparameter tuning (10+ dims).
- Weakness — Theoretical guarantees: no regret bounds or convergence guarantees for the full algorithm beyond one-batch Bayes-optimality.
- Quality assessment: technically sound, complete work with well-justified claims supported by experiments.
- Clarity assessment: exceptionally clear and well-organized, accessible to BO researchers.
- Originality assessment: significant contribution by extending KG to parallel setting and using IPA for gradient estimation.
- Significance assessment: both theoretically and practically significant, with real-world impact and reproducible code.
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS.
- Pro-accept points: novel method, IPA-based efficient computation, thorough experiments, practical gains, reproducible code.
- Con-reject points: scalability and discretization issues, missing recent baselines, limited theoretical guarantees.
- Author suggestions: add scalability/time analysis for q and d, justify and sensitivity-test M=1000, compare to Parallel PES and gradient-based multipoint EI, include higher-dimensional experiments, and make the reduction to parallel EI in the noise-free case more explicit.
