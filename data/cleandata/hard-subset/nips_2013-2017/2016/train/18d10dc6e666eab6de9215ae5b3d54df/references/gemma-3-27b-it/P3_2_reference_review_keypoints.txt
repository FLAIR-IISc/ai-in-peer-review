- Overall assessment: valuable contribution; q-KG is principled, outperforms existing methods (especially with noise); recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Main idea: formulate a batch acquisition (q-KG) that optimizes expected solution improvement for the whole batch using a Bayes-optimal decision-theoretic framework.  
- Key technical innovation: use infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute gradients of the intractable q-KG objective.  
- Problem addressed: moves beyond greedy iterative batch selection to directly optimize batches in parallel BO.  
- Positioning: clearly situated within BO literature and differentiated from parallel EI, batch UCB, GP-UCB-PE, and sequential KG.  
- Comparison to recent work: insightful contrast with nested batch KG [26], highlighting computational/setting differences.  
- Strength — principled foundation: decision-theoretic KG framework gives strong theoretical justification for batch optimization.  
- Strength — empirical performance: strong results on synthetic and real problems (logistic regression, CNN tuning), notably robust to noise.  
- Strength — computational tractability: IPA makes maximizing q-KG practical where it would otherwise be prohibitively expensive.  
- Strength — reproducibility: code release supports replication and further research.  
- Strength — thorough baselines: compared against solid, well-maintained implementations (MOE, Gpoptimization).  
- Weakness — technical density: Section 5 and IPA derivations are dense and need more intuition or intermediate steps for broader accessibility.  
- Weakness — discretization: treatment of search-space discretization (choice of M=1000) lacks analysis on sensitivity and adaptive strategies.  
- Weakness — limited limitations discussion: more explicit discussion needed on performance in high dimensions, failure modes, and computational trade-offs with increasing q.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: absence of analysis on sensitivity to kernel choices and optimization settings for maximizing q-KG.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, potential impact on expensive optimization tasks, sound theoretical and empirical evidence, and reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: mathematical complexity may challenge readers and the paper insufficiently discusses limitations.  
- Specific suggestions: add intuitive IPA examples/steps, analyze effect of M in discretization, include hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and expand limitations/future work in the conclusion.
