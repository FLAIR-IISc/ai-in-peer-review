## Review of "The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian Optimization (BO) method, the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), based on a decision-theoretic analysis.  The core idea is to maximize the expected incremental value of evaluating a batch of `q` points, extending the sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) approach to the parallel setting.  The authors address the computational challenges of maximizing this new acquisition function via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate its gradient. They demonstrate empirically that q-KG outperforms existing parallel BO algorithms on both synthetic functions and practical machine learning hyperparameter tuning tasks, particularly in noisy evaluation scenarios.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Extending the KG policy to the parallel setting is a logical and valuable step, offering a theoretically grounded approach to batch BO.  The q-KG acquisition function represents a genuine contribution.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The derivation from decision theory is sound and well-motivated, building upon the existing KG framework.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The use of IPA to address the gradient computation challenge is a clever and crucial contribution.  Without this, the method would be intractable for many applications.
* **Empirical Results:** The experiments demonstrate consistent performance gains over existing methods, including parallel EI and UCB variants, on both synthetic and real-world problems. The robustness to noise is a particularly noteworthy strength.
* **Code Availability:** Providing code is excellent practice and encourages reproducibility.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, method, and results.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Discretization of A:** While the authors acknowledge the need to discretize the search space A for practical computation, the chosen method (based on global optima of the posterior) feels somewhat *ad hoc*. A more thorough discussion of the impact of discretization on performance, and perhaps exploration of alternative discretization strategies, would be beneficial.  The fixed size (M=1000) throughout experiments also isn’t justified.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't extensively analyze the sensitivity of q-KG to its own hyperparameters (e.g., the discretization parameter M).  A discussion of how these parameters affect performance would strengthen the analysis.
* **Comparison Depth:** While comparisons are made to several baselines, the parameter tuning for these baselines isn't fully detailed.  Ensuring fair comparisons is critical, and more specifics here would be appreciated.
* **Limited Scalability Discussion:** Although IPA improves computational efficiency, the paper lacks a detailed discussion about the scalability of q-KG to very high-dimensional problems.



**Addressing Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a complete piece of work. The theoretical analysis is well-developed, and the empirical results provide strong support for the claims. The authors are generally careful about evaluating the strengths and weaknesses of their method.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The authors provide sufficient detail to understand the method and reproduce the results (aided by the code availability).
* **Originality:** The approach is novel, extending KG to the parallel setting. The IPA-based gradient estimation is a significant contribution to making the method practical. The paper adequately references related work, though a few more citations in the discussion of gradient estimation techniques (beyond just L’Ecuyer) might be helpful.
* **Significance:**  The results are important. A more efficient and effective batch BO method has the potential to significantly speed up the optimization of expensive black-box functions in various applications, especially those involving noisy evaluations. The improvement over existing methods, particularly in the noisy setting, is a notable advance.

**Arguments For/Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundation and novelty.
* Empirically demonstrated performance improvements over existing methods.
* Practical contribution through efficient gradient estimation.
* Code availability promotes reproducibility.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Discretization strategy could be more thoroughly justified and explored.
* Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and scalability.
* Parameter tuning details for baselines could be more comprehensive.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The q-KG method is theoretically sound, computationally efficient, and empirically effective. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.  Specifically, I suggest the authors address the concerns about discretization, hyperparameter sensitivity, and baseline parameter tuning in a revised version.
