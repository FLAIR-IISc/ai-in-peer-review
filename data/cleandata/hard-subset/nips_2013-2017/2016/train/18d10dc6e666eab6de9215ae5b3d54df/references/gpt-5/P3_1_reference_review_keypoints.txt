- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that generalizes knowledge gradient to select q points jointly by minimizing expected posterior-mean minimum after observing a batch.  
- Uses Monte Carlo to estimate the acquisition, derives an unbiased stochastic gradient via IPA/Danskin, and discretizes the inner minimization by sampling posterior argmin candidates.  
- Shows q-KG reduces to q-EI in the noise-free, recommend-from-sampled-points setting.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR-10) show improvements over parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, especially under observation noise.  
- Code is released, improving reproducibility prospects.  
- Extends classical sequential KG to batch selection, positioning q-KG as a one-step Bayes-optimal, noise-aware alternative to regret-oriented UCB and (q-)EI.  
- Adapts IPA-based differentiable estimators (previously used for q-EI) to the new q-KG objective.  
- Discretization of the inner minimization follows standard ES/PES practice and is considered reasonable.  
- Strength: clear decision-theoretic grounding as a natural one-step Bayes-optimal batch rule.  
- Strength: better handling of noisy observations than (q-)EI, with meaningful empirical gains.  
- Strength: joint batch optimization (not greedy) aligns with modern batch BO advances.  
- Strength: practical unbiased stochastic gradient estimator enabling gradient-based optimization of a Monte Carlo acquisition.  
- Strength: solid empirical performance and early-iteration gains for expensive evaluations.  
- Weakness: theoretical details are light; interchange of gradient and expectation and non-differentiability/ties need explicit conditions and discussion.  
- Weakness: lacks analysis of computational complexity and scalability w.r.t. q, n, |A_n|, and Monte Carlo samples S, and no wall-clock comparisons.  
- Weakness: discretization/sampling of M argmin candidates is expensive and under-described; sensitivity to M and sampling procedure is unclear.  
- Weakness: gradient presentation is potentially misleading—µ^(n) terms should not depend on z per Danskin; exact gradient used in code should be clarified.  
- Weakness: fairness concerns in empirical comparisons due to different codebases and GP/hyperparameter settings; shared GP setup recommended.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to other batch BO methods (e.g., local penalization, PPES) or at least discussion of them.  
- Weakness: experimental details (S, M, optimizer settings, restarts, ablations, larger q and higher dimensions) are missing and needed.  
- Weakness: “Bayes-optimality” claim only strictly holds on finite discretized A; this approximation should be clearly stated.  
- Clarity: generally clear but heavy notation, minor typos, and should explicitly show q=1 reduction and q-EI reduction.  
- Reproducibility: code release is positive but paper must include all hyperparameters, seeds, and runtime budgets to enable full reproduction.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — technically sound and practically useful with promising results, but needs theoretical clarifications, fairer baselines, and scalability/ablation reporting.
