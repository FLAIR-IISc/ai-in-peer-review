- Proposes q-KG: a decision-theoretic acquisition function for joint selection of q points in batch BO, Bayes-optimal for one remaining batch and reduces to parallel EI in the noiseless/sampled-points limit.  
- Reparameterizes the GP posterior with a standard normal Zq and derives an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for gradient-based batch optimization.  
- Handles continuous-domain inner minimizations by discretizing with M candidate points drawn from posterior-minimum samples, augmented with previous and candidate points.  
- Empirically outperforms parallel EI (closed-form and heuristic), GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic benchmarks and ML hyperparameter tuning, with especially strong gains under observation noise.  
- Builds on prior KG, batch EI, batch UCB, and entropy-based batch methods; novelty lies in continuous-domain, gradient-based batch KG versus prior discrete-batch KG variants.  
- Acquisition formulation is principled and technically sound; IPA/pathwise derivatives are appropriate under differentiability and unique-argmin assumptions, though a more explicit proof sketch for one-step optimality would help.  
- Notational/derivational concern: gradient in Sec. 5.2 includes ∂µ(n)/∂z terms that should vanish—derivative should flow through the argmin and σ̃ term; clarification needed.  
- Differentiating through the Cholesky of the q×q covariance is feasible but nontrivial; more detail on numerical stability, autodiff, and explicit complexity/wall-clock overheads is needed.  
- Complexity per gradient step appears roughly O(|An| q d + q^3), but the paper lacks explicit complexity analysis and runtime comparisons to baselines.  
- Discretization introduces bias relative to the continuous objective; sensitivity to M and the An construction is not analyzed—ablation or alternative discretizations (e.g., Sobol) are recommended.  
- Experimental evaluation is strong (good baselines, repetitions, realistic tasks) but should report acquisition optimization time, total wall-clock, and results across different batch sizes q.  
- Presentation is generally clear and organized but has overloaded notation (A), minor typos/formatting issues, and would benefit from more detail on gradient derivation and one-step optimality for reproducibility.  
- Original contribution: a novel, practical extension of KG to continuous, noisy, batch BO with an efficient stochastic gradient estimator distinct from q-EI and batch ES.  
- Significance: high practical value for batch BO in noisy settings; open-source code increases impact; missing theoretical convergence/regret guarantees is a limitation.  
- Pros: principled decision-theoretic objective; reduces to q-EI in the right limit; efficient unbiased stochastic gradients; strong empirical gains; open-source implementation.  
- Cons: missing runtime/complexity analysis and wall-clock comparisons; limited study of discretization bias and sensitivity to M/An; minor derivational and presentation issues; no theoretical guarantees.  
- Suggestions for authors: clarify the gradient derivation (Danskin justification), report runtime and complexity as functions of q, |An|, d, provide sensitivity analyses for M and An, show scalability with q, and outline conditions for consistency as M→∞.  
- Recommendation: Accept (strong).
