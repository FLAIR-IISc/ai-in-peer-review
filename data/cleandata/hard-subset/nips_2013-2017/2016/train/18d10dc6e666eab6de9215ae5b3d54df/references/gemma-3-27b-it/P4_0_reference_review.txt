This paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian Optimization (BO) method, the parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), designed for parallel function evaluations. The method leverages a decision-theoretic analysis to determine the optimal batch of points to evaluate, aiming to maximize information gain. A key contribution lies in the development of an efficient gradient-based optimization method for q-KG, utilizing infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of q-KG on synthetic functions and practical machine learning hyperparameter tuning tasks, showing improvements over existing parallel BO algorithms, particularly in noisy settings.

**Quality:** The paper demonstrates technical soundness. The derivation of q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective is well-motivated, and the IPA-based gradient estimation offers a practical solution to the computational challenges. The experimental results, presented on both synthetic and real-world problems, support the claims of improved performance. The authors appropriately acknowledge the limitations (discretization of A) and provide clear explanations of the methodology. The inclusion of a code repository is a strong positive. However, the theoretical analysis, while sound, is dense and could benefit from clearer explanations of key steps. The paper would be strengthened by a more detailed error analysis or discussion of the convergence properties of the optimization process. The empirical evaluation, while comprehensive, lacks statistical significance testing (e.g., paired t-tests) to rigorously demonstrate the improvements reported.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, but its density and the technical nature of the derivations make it challenging to follow in places. The introduction clearly outlines the problem and the contribution of the paper. However, sections 3 and 4 are mathematically intensive and could benefit from more intuitive explanations. Algorithm 1 provides a clear outline of the procedure. The notation is consistent throughout the paper. The description of the experiments is clear, but some details about the hyperparameter settings used for the algorithms being compared would be helpful.

**Originality:** The paper presents a novel contribution to the field of parallel Bayesian optimization. While building upon existing concepts like Knowledge Gradient and expected improvement, the generalization to the parallel setting and the efficient gradient estimation technique are original. The paper effectively positions its work within the existing literature, accurately citing relevant publications and highlighting the differences between the proposed method and existing approaches. The use of IPA for gradient estimation appears to be a novel application within the context of parallel BO.

**Significance:** The proposed q-KG method addresses a significant challenge in Bayesian optimization – efficient batch acquisition in parallel settings. The demonstrated improvements over existing algorithms, particularly in noisy environments, suggest that this work could have a substantial impact on applications where function evaluations are expensive, such as hyperparameter tuning in machine learning. The availability of the code further enhances the significance, allowing other researchers to build upon this work. The method’s potential for wider applicability to other optimization problems beyond machine learning should be mentioned.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel and technically sound approach to parallel Bayesian optimization.
*   Effective gradient estimation technique enables practical implementation.
*   Demonstrated improvements over existing algorithms on both synthetic and real-world problems.
*   Clear presentation of the methodology and experimental results.
*   Availability of source code promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical analysis is dense and could benefit from clearer explanations.
*   The lack of statistical significance testing weakens the empirical validation.
*   The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential future directions.
*   The impact of the discretization of the search space (A) isn't thoroughly investigated or discussed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The proposed q-KG method offers a promising approach to parallel batch acquisition, and the authors have demonstrated its effectiveness through rigorous experimentation. While some aspects of the paper could be improved in terms of clarity and statistical rigor, the overall quality of the work is high. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above. Specifically, adding statistical significance testing to the experimental results and providing a more intuitive explanation of the theoretical derivations would significantly strengthen the paper.
