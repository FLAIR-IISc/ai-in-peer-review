- Proposes q-KG (Parallel Knowledge Gradient), extending sequential KG to jointly optimize batches of q evaluations.  
- Provides a decision-theoretic justification: q-KG measures expected incremental value of a batch and is Bayes-optimal when one batch remains.  
- Introduces an IPA-based method for efficient gradient computation to make direct optimization of q-KG tractable.  
- Empirically validated on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10), showing consistent improvements, especially under noise.  
- Distinguishes from prior parallel BO methods (e.g., parallel EI, parallel UCB) by optimizing batches collectively rather than iteratively.  
- Quality: technically sound extension with solid theoretical motivation and comprehensive experiments with proper statistical reporting.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, with consistent notation and clear algorithm/experiment descriptions.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of KG to batches and novel application of IPA for gradient computation.  
- Significance: relevant for real-world parallel hyperparameter tuning, demonstrates practical gains, and provides public code.  
- Weakness: Lacks formal theoretical analysis such as regret bounds or guarantees comparing q-KG to other batch BO methods.  
- Weakness: No detailed computational complexity analysis showing how the IPA approach scales with batch size q; experiments use q=4.  
- Weakness: Does not compare or discuss methods published after 2016 that are relevant to batch BO.  
- Weakness: Missing deeper analysis explaining why q-KG performs particularly well in noisy settings.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant, well-executed contribution with immediate practical value.  
- Suggestion: Include discussion or future work on theoretical regret bounds for q-KG.  
- Suggestion: Add a computational complexity analysis detailing scaling with batch size q.  
- Suggestion: Provide deeper experimental/analytic explanation for q-KG’s robustness to noise.  
- Suggestion: Add brief comparison or discussion of more recent (post-2016) batch BO methods in related work.
