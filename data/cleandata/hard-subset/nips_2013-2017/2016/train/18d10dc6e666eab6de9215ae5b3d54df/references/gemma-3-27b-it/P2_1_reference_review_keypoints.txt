- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function derived from decision theory that optimizes batches jointly rather than greedily.
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients for maximizing the q-KG acquisition function.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance: q-KG outperforms benchmarks on synthetic functions and practical ML hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.
- Provides publicly available code to facilitate reproducibility.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear mathematical notation and logical organization.
- q-KG is theoretically well-founded, connecting to the sequential knowledge gradient and offering rigorous justification.
- IPA-based gradient estimation addresses major computational challenges, making the method practical.
- Results are significant for scenarios with parallel evaluations and noisy objectives.
- Discretization strategy for continuous domains (sampling based on posterior optima) appears ad-hoc and needs more justification.
- Lacks thorough analysis of the effect of the number of discretization samples M on performance and alternative discretization methods.
- Does not thoroughly analyze sensitivity to q-KG’s hyperparameters (e.g., M and batch size q).
- Comparisons omit some recent or specialized parallel BO methods (e.g., diversity-promoting or active-learning based approaches).
- Limited discussion of method limitations and scalability, particularly IPA’s behavior in very high-dimensional problems.
- Some technical sections (notably the IPA derivation) could be clearer or more illustrative.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original, and impactful work that merits acceptance.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept for NIPS 2016.
