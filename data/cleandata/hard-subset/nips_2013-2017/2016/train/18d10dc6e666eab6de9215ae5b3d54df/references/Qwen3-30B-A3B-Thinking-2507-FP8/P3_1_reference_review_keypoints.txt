- Summary: Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch extension of the knowledge gradient using IPA to efficiently compute gradients for jointly optimizing q evaluation points.  
- Context: Addresses gap where existing parallel BO methods build batches iteratively rather than jointly optimizing them.  

Strengths:
- Theoretical contribution: Principled extension of KG to the parallel setting with a sound derivation of q-KG.  
- Computational innovation: Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute q-KG gradients efficiently, making joint optimization practical.  
- Experiments: Thorough evaluation on synthetic functions and real ML hyperparameter tasks with statistical analysis showing strong performance, especially in noisy settings.  
- Practical relevance: Demonstrates tangible improvements in hyperparameter tuning (e.g., CIFAR10 error reduction), relevant to parallel ML workflows.  
- Presentation: Clear organization, notation, and concise algorithm description (Algorithm 1).  

Weaknesses:
- No formal proof: Lacks a formal Bayes-optimality proof for the parallel q-KG claim.  
- Complexity analysis missing: Does not provide detailed computational scaling of the IPA-based method with batch size q and dimensionality d.  
- Limited comparisons: Omits comparisons to recent gradient-based parallel EI methods and other relevant baselines.  
- Discretization justification unclear: The sampling-based discretization strategy is not well-justified or compared to alternatives.  
- Dimensionality concerns: No discussion or experiments on scaling to higher-dimensional problems (d beyond 6).  
- No theoretical noise explanation: Claims of superior performance in noisy settings lack theoretical justification.  

Specific suggestions:
- Add a formal or clarified Bayes-optimality proof for the parallel case.  
- Include computational complexity analysis versus naive and existing methods.  
- Expand experiments to include gradient-based parallel EI and parallel ES comparisons.  
- Justify or empirically compare the discretization strategy against alternatives.  
- Add experiments on higher-dimensional problems (d ≈ 10–20) to assess scalability.  
- Provide theoretical discussion on why q-KG is robust to noise.  

Overall assessment:
- Significant, well-motivated contribution with strong empirical support, but needs additional theoretical and empirical completeness on optimality, complexity, comparisons, discretization, and scalability.  

Recommendation:
- Strong accept.
