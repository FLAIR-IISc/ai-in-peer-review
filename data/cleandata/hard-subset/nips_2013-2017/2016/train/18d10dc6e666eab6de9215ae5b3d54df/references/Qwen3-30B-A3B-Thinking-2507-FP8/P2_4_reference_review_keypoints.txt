- Introduces q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method that jointly optimizes a batch of q points rather than selecting points iteratively.
- Derives a decision-theoretic acquisition function (q-KG) suitable for the parallel setting.
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients of the q-KG acquisition function efficiently.
- Demonstrates q-KG outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and other baselines on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning tasks.
- Shows particularly strong improvements in noisy settings.
- Technically sound with a clear mathematical formulation and rigorous justification of the IPA-based computational approach.
- Comprehensive experimental validation: four synthetic functions, logistic regression on MNIST, and CNN tuning on CIFAR10, with both noisy and noise-free evaluations.
- Provides appropriate statistical analysis (means and standard deviations) and fair baseline comparisons.
- Exceptionally well-written and organized, with clear background, consistent notation, and reproducible implementation details.
- Novel contribution: joint-batch optimization (Bayes-optimal for the one-batch setting) and novel use of IPA to enable practical optimization.
- Significant practical impact for parallel hyperparameter tuning, e.g., reducing CNN test error on CIFAR10 from ~14% to ~11%.
- Strengths include strong theory, novel computation via IPA, extensive experiments, robust performance in noise, and clarity of presentation.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of computational scaling with batch size q, under-justified discretization for infinite domains, no theoretical guarantees beyond one-batch, and possible omission of post-2016 related work.
- Recommendation: strong accept — high-quality, original, significant contribution suitable for NIPS.
