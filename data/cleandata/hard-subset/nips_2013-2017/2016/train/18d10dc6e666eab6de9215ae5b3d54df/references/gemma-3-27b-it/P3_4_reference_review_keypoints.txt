- Overall: Strong contribution — q-KG is a principled, decision-theoretic batch BO method that outperforms existing approaches (especially in noisy settings) and is made practical by IPA gradient estimation; recommend accept.  
- Summary/Related Work: Moves beyond greedy parallel EI/UCB by jointly optimizing a batch KG acquisition, clearly positioned against nested-batch methods and prior parallel BO work.  
- Strength: Principled derivation from Bayes-optimal decision theory gives a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Strength: Consistently superior empirical performance across synthetic and real tasks, with a pronounced advantage in noisy evaluations.  
- Strength: IPA-based gradient estimation is an effective, scalable solution to the computational bottleneck for batch optimization.  
- Strength: Algorithm 1 is clear, code is open source, and the method connects well to sequential KG theory.  
- Weakness: Notation is dense and confusing in places (e.g., multiple uses of "A"), reducing readability.  
- Weakness: Discretization strategy (M=1000) is under-justified; lacks sensitivity analysis on M.  
- Weakness: Insufficient exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity for q-KG/IPA optimization.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion and complexity analysis of computational cost for larger batches and higher dimensions.  
- Weakness: Presentation is dense and would benefit from clearer, more pedagogical exposition.  
- Originality: Highly original — novel extension of KG to batch BO with IPA-based gradient computation.  
- Significance: High practical impact for parallel BO (e.g., hyperparameter tuning), particularly in noisy, real-world scenarios.  
- Arguments for Acceptance: Significant empirical gains, principled approach, IPA innovation, thorough experiments, and open-source release.  
- Arguments Against Acceptance: Dense notation/writing, limited hyperparameter and computational-cost analyses.  
- Final Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity (esp. Section 5) and to address hyperparameter sensitivity and computational-cost details.
