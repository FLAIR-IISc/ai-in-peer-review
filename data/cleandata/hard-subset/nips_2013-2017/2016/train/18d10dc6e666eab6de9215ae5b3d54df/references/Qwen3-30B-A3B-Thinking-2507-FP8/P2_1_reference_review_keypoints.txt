- Introduces q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function extending Knowledge Gradient to select q points jointly.  
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective to maximize expected improvement in the GP predictor minimum.  
- Addresses optimization of q-KG via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients efficiently.  
- Empirically compares q-KG to parallel EI, batch UCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic functions and ML tasks.  
- Results show q-KG consistently outperforms other batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is technically sound with correct derivations and justified use of IPA.  
- Experimental design is comprehensive and statistically significant, with multiple runs and error bars.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations, note reduction to parallel EI under specific conditions, and provide code for reproducibility.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and notation and algorithms are presented consistently.  
- Related work is thorough and the paper clearly differentiates q-KG from iterative batch methods.  
- Novel contribution: jointly optimizing batches rather than constructing them iteratively.  
- Proposes a dynamic discretization approach for infinite domains based on the posterior.  
- High significance due to practical relevance for parallel/distributed evaluations and improved performance in noisy real-world tasks.  
- Strengths include strong theory, clever computational approach, comprehensive experiments, consistent empirical gains, and clear presentation.  
- Weaknesses: computational cost may scale poorly with large q and lacks detailed scaling analysis.  
- Sensitivity to discretization sample size M (set to 1000) is not thoroughly explored.  
- Does not sufficiently examine limitations of the Gaussian process assumption for complex real-world tasks.  
- Comparison with related nested batch KG ([26]) is mentioned but not deeply analyzed.  
- Recommendation: accept — contribution is significant and well-supported despite the noted weaknesses.
