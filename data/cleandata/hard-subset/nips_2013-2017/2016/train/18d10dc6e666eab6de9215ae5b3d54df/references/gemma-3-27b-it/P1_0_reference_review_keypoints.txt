- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function extending single-point KG to evaluate q points simultaneously.  
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic analysis and employs infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients efficiently.  
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance over parallel EI, batch UCB, and other baselines, notably in noisy evaluation settings.  
- Validated on synthetic benchmarks and real-world hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression and CNN), strengthening practical claims.  
- Places work correctly within prior literature, building on sequential KG and differentiating from nested batch KG and other parallel BO methods.  
- Novel contribution with a strong theoretical foundation emphasizing information-gain for batch selection.  
- IPA-based gradient estimation makes maximization of q-KG computationally practical for larger batch sizes.  
- Paper is well-written, mathematically consistent, and presents the algorithm clearly.  
- Publicly available code is provided, enhancing reproducibility.  
- Discretization of continuous domain A via posterior sampling (M = 1000) may be a bottleneck and lacks sensitivity analysis.  
- Limited analysis of q-KG’s sensitivity to its own hyperparameters (GP kernel and optimization settings).  
- Comparisons could include more recent parallel BO advances that may have emerged since submission.  
- Does not address practical parallel-evaluation issues (communication overhead, worker failures).  
- Overall technically sound, original, and potentially significant for expensive, noisy evaluations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretical novelty, strong empirical results, IPA efficiency, clear presentation, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: concerns about discretization, limited hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and practical parallelism details.  
- Reviewer’s overall recommendation: strong accept; q-KG is a significant, well-executed contribution likely to spur follow-up work.
