- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending the Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients of the batch acquisition function.  
- Empirically outperforms several parallel BO methods, especially in noisy evaluation settings, on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Situates work within existing parallel BO literature, distinguishing from greedy batch and collective batch strategies and connecting to single-point KG.  
- Highlights computational differences from nested batch KG due to use of smooth gradient estimation.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: decision-theoretic derivation and Bayes-optimal final-batch objective.  
- Major methodological contribution: IPA significantly improves efficiency of maximizing batch acquisition functions.  
- Clear presentation and consistent notation make the algorithm easy to follow.  
- Public code availability enhances reproducibility and adoption.  
- Discretization of continuous domain A via sampling from posterior optima may miss important regions and is potentially fragile early on.  
- Sensitivity to hyperparameters (IPA step size, discretization parameters) is not thoroughly investigated.  
- Lacks deeper analysis explaining why q-KG outperforms competitors (exploration vs. exploitation behavior).  
- Missing detailed computational cost/complexity analysis as batch size q and dimensionality increase.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; significant IPA contribution; strong empirical gains; available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential limitations from discretization; insufficient hyperparameter sensitivity analysis; inadequate explanation of performance advantage.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution, empirically validated, recommended for acceptance.  
- Recommendations: discuss/improve discretization strategy, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, visualize comparison patterns to explain performance, and provide computational complexity analysis.
