- Proposes q-KG: a novel extension of the Knowledge Gradient policy to batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization.
- Provides a sound decision-theoretic derivation building on the KG framework.
- Introduces IPA-based gradient estimation to make q-KG computationally tractable.
- Shows empirical gains over parallel EI/UCB baselines on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noise.
- Releases code, aiding reproducibility.
- Paper is generally well written and clear.

- Relies on an ad hoc discretization of the search space A (M=1000) without sufficient justification or exploration of alternatives.
- Lacks thorough analysis of q-KG’s hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., discretization parameter M).
- Baseline parameter tuning details are insufficient, raising fairness concerns in comparisons.
- Limited discussion of scalability to very high-dimensional problems despite IPA improvements.

- Quality: technically sound with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Clarity: well-organized and reproducible (code provided).
- Originality: novel parallel KG and practical IPA-based gradient estimator.
- Significance: meaningful improvement for batch BO, particularly for noisy evaluations.

- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, empirical improvements, practical gradient method, and available code.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient treatment of discretization, hyperparameter sensitivity, scalability, and baseline tuning.

- Overall: recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address discretization methodology, hyperparameter sensitivity, and baseline tuning details.
