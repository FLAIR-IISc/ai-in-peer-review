- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic acquisition function that generalizes the knowledge gradient to select batches of q points jointly for Bayesian optimization.  
- q-KG evaluates expected reduction in the GP-predicted minimum after simultaneously evaluating a batch.  
- Introduces an IPA-based Monte Carlo gradient estimator to efficiently optimize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic benchmarks and real ML tuning tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10) show q-KG consistently outperforms existing parallel BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Code is provided for reproducibility (https://github.com/wujian16/qKG).  
- Paper is technically sound with a clear theoretical foundation and well-motivated derivations.  
- Writing and organization are excellent; methodological and experimental details are clearly presented.  
- The approach is original: jointly optimizing batches rather than iteratively constructing them represents a meaningful advancement.  
- Significant practical impact: improves optimization speed for parallel evaluations and shows tangible gains (e.g., CIFAR10 test error reduced from 14% to 11%).  
- Main weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational trade-offs (e.g., wall-clock time or per-iteration cost) relative to cheaper methods.  
- Second weakness: choice of discretization parameter M = 1000 is not thoroughly justified or analyzed with respect to dimensionality.  
- Recommendation: strong accept for NeurIPS; high quality, originality, clarity, and significance.
