- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that jointly selects q points by expected decrease in the posterior mean minimizer.
- Shows q-KG reduces to parallel EI in the noiseless case when the final recommendation is restricted to observed points.
- Uses a reparameterized Monte Carlo estimator with standard normal Zq to approximate q-KG for q>1.
- Derives an unbiased stochastic gradient via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) on a discretized candidate set.
- Discretizes the inner minimization by sampling likely posterior minima augmented with candidate points.
- Empirically outperforms or matches parallel EI and UCB-based batch methods on synthetic and hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noise.
- Extends sequential KG to joint batch selection rather than greedy selection, relating to prior batch EI, UCB, and ES methods.
- Employs a fantasization-style reparameterization similar to parallel EI optimizers but uses IPA for gradients.
- The inner minimization discretization idea follows entropy-search-style sampling of likely minima.
- Strength: principled one-step Bayes decision objective aligned with simple-regret minimization and robust to observation noise.
- Strength: coherent Monte Carlo formulation and IPA gradient that avoids score-function variance for continuous domains.
- Strength: compelling empirical results and realistic hyperparameter tuning applications demonstrating practical impact.
- Weakness: no theoretical quantification of discretization bias or guidance on choosing M and its scaling with dimension.
- Weakness: Bayes-optimality claims rely on assumptions (single remaining batch, same A) that don’t hold with adaptive discretization and MLE hyperparameter re-estimation.
- Weakness: differentiability assumptions for IPA (unique inner minimizers, smooth kernels) are under-discussed; nondifferentiability of min needs more treatment or subgradient arguments.
- Weakness: computational complexity and practical runtime scaling with q, |An|, and d are not made explicit or evaluated.
- Weakness: hyperparameter uncertainty is ignored by using MLE at each iteration, which can bias acquisition especially early or under high noise.
- Weakness: missing comparisons (e.g., PPES) or justification for omission and lack of runtime-normalized experimental comparisons.
- Clarity: generally well written with clear motivation and algorithm outline, but has typos, incomplete references, and needs more detail on gradient computation and numerical stability.
- Reproducibility: should report Monte Carlo sample counts, optimizer hyperparameters, and acquisition optimization budgets.
- Originality: novel extension of KG to joint batch selection with reparameterized MC estimator and IPA gradient; connection to parallel EI is insightful.
- Significance: practical utility for batch BO, particularly under noise, though reliance on discretization and lack of broader guarantees limit adoption in very high-d or large-batch settings.
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to M, number of MC samples, q, and d; use of variance reduction; effect of marginalizing hyperparameters; role of including z(1:q) in discretization.
- Pros: principled batch acquisition, unbiased stochastic gradients, strong empirical performance, and available code.
- Cons: lacks theoretical analysis of discretization error and complexity, limited discussion of gradient assumptions and numerical stability, and missing runtime-normalized baselines.
- Recommendation: Accept (weak accept) conditional on stronger experimental analysis and clearer computational/technical clarifications.
