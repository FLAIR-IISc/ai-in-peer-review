

# Peer Review: "The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization"

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization method called "Parallel Knowledge Gradient" (q-KG), which extends the sequential knowledge gradient (KG) method to the parallel setting. The authors propose a decision-theoretic acquisition function that evaluates the expected incremental value of evaluating a batch of q points simultaneously, rather than sequentially. The key innovation is the derivation of q-KG as a generalization of the sequential KG method to the batch setting, with a computational approach using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute its gradients. The paper demonstrates that q-KG consistently outperforms existing batch BO methods (parallel EI, GP-BUCB, GP-UCB-PE) on both synthetic functions and practical machine learning tuning tasks, with particularly strong performance in noisy settings.

The paper is well-positioned in the Bayesian optimization literature, building upon the sequential KG method [7, 17] and extending it to the parallel setting, which is a significant practical consideration for real-world optimization problems where multiple evaluations can be performed simultaneously. The authors clearly articulate how their approach differs from previous batch BO methods that construct batches iteratively by optimizing single-point acquisition functions.

## Strengths

1. **Strong Theoretical Foundation**: The paper provides a solid decision-theoretic justification for the q-KG acquisition function, showing it is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the minimum of the GP predictor when only one batch remains. This is a significant improvement over existing batch methods that often optimize sequentially.

2. **Novel Computational Approach**: The use of infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute gradients of q-KG is clever and addresses a major computational challenge in batch BO. This approach makes the method practical for real-world applications.

3. **Comprehensive Empirical Evaluation**: The paper includes extensive experiments on both synthetic functions (Branin, Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann) and practical machine learning problems (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10). The results consistently show that q-KG outperforms baselines, especially in noisy settings, which is a significant contribution.

4. **Clear Writing and Organization**: The paper is well-written, logically organized, and provides sufficient technical detail for reproducibility. The experimental setup and results are clearly presented.

5. **Practical Impact**: The method addresses a real-world problem (parallel evaluation of expensive functions) with direct applications in hyperparameter tuning for machine learning, where parallel computation is common.

## Weaknesses

1. **Computational Complexity Analysis**: While the paper demonstrates that IPA makes the gradient computation efficient, it lacks a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of q-KG compared to other batch BO methods. It would be helpful to have theoretical complexity bounds or empirical comparisons of runtime.

2. **Sensitivity Analysis**: The paper uses M=1000 for discretization (Section 5.3) but doesn't discuss how sensitive the method is to this parameter choice. A sensitivity analysis would strengthen the paper by showing how robust the method is to this parameter.

3. **Comparison with Other Recent Batch Methods**: The paper compares to several batch methods but doesn't discuss recent work like [15, 25] that also uses gradient-based methods for parallel EI. A more comprehensive comparison would strengthen the claims of superiority.

4. **Lack of Theoretical Convergence Guarantees**: While the paper shows q-KG is Bayes-optimal for one remaining batch, it doesn't provide theoretical guarantees about convergence properties or regret bounds for the full algorithm, which would be valuable for a theoretical contribution.

5. **Limited Experimental Diversity**: While the synthetic functions are standard, the experiments could be strengthened by including more diverse test functions or problems that better showcase the method's strengths.

## Quality

The paper is technically sound and well-supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results. The claims are carefully evaluated, with the authors acknowledging that q-KG is slightly worse than benchmarks on Branin2, showing appropriate honesty about limitations. The method is complete as a piece of work, not just a position paper. The authors clearly distinguish their approach from previous work and provide a solid foundation for future research.

## Clarity

The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical derivations are clear and well-motivated, and the experimental results are presented with sufficient detail for reproducibility. The authors provide a clear description of their implementation (including GitHub code) and the experimental setup. The writing is accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian optimization but would also be understandable to machine learning researchers.

## Originality

This paper makes a significant original contribution by extending the knowledge gradient method to the batch setting in a decision-theoretically sound way. While there are other batch BO methods, they typically construct batches iteratively, whereas q-KG considers the batch collectively. The paper clearly articulates how this differs from previous work, including the related work on parallel EI [2, 19, 25] and parallel UCB [3, 5]. The extension from sequential to parallel is novel and addresses a practical gap in the literature.

## Significance

The significance of this work is high. Batch Bayesian optimization is an important practical problem, as many real-world optimization tasks can be parallelized (e.g., multiple machines evaluating hyperparameters simultaneously). The paper demonstrates that q-KG provides consistent improvements over existing methods, especially in noisy settings where many practical optimization problems reside. The results on real-world machine learning tuning tasks (logistic regression, CNNs) show clear practical impact. This work advances the state of the art in batch Bayesian optimization in a demonstrable way.

## Recommendation

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS. The paper makes a significant contribution to the Bayesian optimization literature with a well-motivated method, solid theoretical foundation, and strong empirical results. The approach is novel, well-executed, and addresses an important practical problem.

## Suggestions for Improvement

1. Include a computational complexity analysis of q-KG compared to other batch BO methods.

2. Add a sensitivity analysis of the discretization parameter M.

3. Expand the comparison to include more recent batch BO methods like those in [15, 25].

4. Discuss theoretical convergence properties or regret bounds for the full algorithm.

5. Consider adding more diverse test functions to the experimental section.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Pro Acceptance:**
- Clear theoretical contribution extending knowledge gradient to batch setting
- Novel computational approach using IPA that makes the method practical
- Strong empirical results showing consistent improvements over baselines
- Well-written, well-organized paper with clear contributions
- Addresses a significant practical problem in machine learning

**Con Acceptance:**
- Limited theoretical analysis of computational complexity
- Lacks sensitivity analysis for discretization parameter
- Could include more comprehensive comparison with recent batch BO methods

## Final Assessment

This is a high-quality paper that makes a significant contribution to the Bayesian optimization literature. The method is well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically validated. The paper clearly articulates its contribution and demonstrates its superiority over existing methods in a variety of settings. The authors have provided a complete and reproducible implementation, which is valuable for the community. The paper is well-suited for NIPS, addressing a significant problem in machine learning with a novel and impactful solution.
