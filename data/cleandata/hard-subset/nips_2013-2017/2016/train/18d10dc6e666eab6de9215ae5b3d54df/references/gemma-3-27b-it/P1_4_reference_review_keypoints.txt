- Introduces q-KG: a parallel extension of Knowledge Gradient that selects batches jointly and uses IPA-based gradient estimation to make optimization practical, showing strong empirical gains especially under noise.  
- Clearly positions the work within parallel BO literature, distinguishing from greedy EI/UCB and nested-batch KG approaches and citing relevant prior work.  
- Novelty: extends KG principle to batch selection, treating the batch collectively rather than greedily.  
- Theoretical foundation: derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective, giving a clear rationale.  
- Computational efficiency: develops an IPA-based gradient estimator that enables practical maximization of the q-KG acquisition for larger batches.  
- Empirical performance: consistently outperforms existing parallel BO methods on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, notably in noisy settings.  
- Presentation and reproducibility: paper is well-organized, clearly written, and the authors provide code.  
- Weakness — discretization: insufficient detail on the discretization strategy (choice of M=1000 and AMn sampling) and its impact on performance.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: lacks analysis of sensitivity to optimization hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate) for q-KG.  
- Weakness — computational cost: no detailed quantitative comparison of computational cost versus methods like parallel EI.  
- Weakness — noise robustness: experiments use a single noise level (σ = 0.5); broader noise-level exploration is needed.  
- Weakness — experiment scope: real-world experiments could cover more diverse and challenging hyperparameter tuning problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, made practical via efficient gradient estimation, and backed by compelling empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing methodological details (discretization, hyperparameter sensitivity) and somewhat limited experimental breadth.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contribution to BO; strengths outweigh weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept, with suggestion to add more detail on discretization and hyperparameter sensitivity in revision.
