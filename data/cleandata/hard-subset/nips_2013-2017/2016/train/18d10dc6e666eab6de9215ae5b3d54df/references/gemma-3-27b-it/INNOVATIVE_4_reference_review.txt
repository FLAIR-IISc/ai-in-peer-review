This paper introduces q-KG, a novel parallel Bayesian Optimization (BO) method grounded in decision theory. The core idea – extending the knowledge gradient concept to a batch setting and optimizing for the expected incremental value of a batch of evaluations – is a strong one. The paper clearly articulates the motivation for parallel BO and positions q-KG within the existing landscape of parallel acquisition functions. The derivation, while potentially complex, is well-explained and the introduction of infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to enable efficient gradient computation is a clever and significant contribution.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The q-KG acquisition function represents a genuinely new approach to parallel BO, moving beyond iterative greedy strategies. The decision-theoretic foundation and global optimization of the batch are compelling.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The derivation from the knowledge gradient policy provides a strong theoretical justification for the method. The use of IPA to address the computational challenge is well-motivated and appears effective.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results across both synthetic and real-world problems consistently demonstrate q-KG’s superiority, particularly in noisy evaluation scenarios.  The consistent gains over established methods like parallel EI and UCB are noteworthy.
* **Implementation & Availability:** The authors provide code, promoting reproducibility and facilitating future research.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear and the explanations are generally accessible.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Discretization Details:** While the discretization strategy for continuous search spaces is mentioned (using samples from the GP posterior), the practical impact of the number of samples (M=1000) on performance isn't thoroughly investigated. A sensitivity analysis of M would be valuable.
* **Computational Cost:** Though IPA helps, the computational burden of optimizing q-KG likely remains substantial, especially in high-dimensional problems. The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of scalability limitations and potential future optimizations.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance may be sensitive to GP hyperparameters. While MLE is used for noise estimation, a broader discussion of hyperparameter tuning for the GP itself would be helpful.
* **Limited Scope of Real-World Problems:** While the logistic regression and CNN tuning are good examples, expanding the range of practical applications would further strengthen the paper.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to parallel Bayesian Optimization.
* The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of q-KG, particularly in challenging noisy settings.
* The introduction of IPA is a significant technical contribution that enables practical implementation.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.
* The paper addresses a core problem in automated machine learning and offers a potentially impactful solution.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost might limit applicability to high-dimensional or very expensive function evaluations.
* The discretization strategy could introduce approximations that affect performance.
* While good, the empirical evaluation could be broadened with more diverse real-world applications.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that introduces a genuinely novel and promising approach to parallel Bayesian Optimization. While there are minor weaknesses regarding computational cost and the details of discretization, the strengths – particularly the novelty, theoretical grounding, and empirical performance – significantly outweigh these concerns. This work presents a clear advancement in the field and has the potential to be highly influential.  I am willing to overlook the incomplete validation and minor flaws in favor of the significant new concept introduced. 

**Recommendation:** Accept.
