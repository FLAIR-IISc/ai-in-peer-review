- Proposes q-KG, a Bayes-optimal (one-step) batch acquisition generalizing knowledge gradient to jointly select q points, estimated by Monte Carlo and optimized via an IPA-derived unbiased stochastic gradient.  
- Uses adaptive discretization for the inner minimization by sampling candidate locations concentrated near posterior optima, augmented with past samples and the candidate batch.  
- Empirically outperforms or matches parallel EI and UCB variants on synthetic and hyperparameter tuning tasks, with particularly large gains under noise.  
- Technical strengths: principled decision-theoretic objective and a substantive IPA-based stochastic gradient estimator enabling gradient-based batch optimization.  
- Technical weaknesses: no regret/asymptotic guarantees, no analysis of GP hyperparameter re-estimation effects, and potential differentiability issues around the min operator only argued in the supplement.  
- Discretization concerns: adaptive scheme is reasonable but its accuracy, bias/variance impact, and exact sampling procedure from the “global optima” distribution are under-specified and unanalyzed.  
- Computational concerns: no analysis of complexity or scaling with q, dimension d, or discretization size M, and no wall-clock runtime comparisons.  
- Empirical limitations: experiments fix q=4, lack studies of performance vs q, M, or noise level, and omit ablations on discretization and gradient-estimator parameters.  
- Clarity: generally readable and well-organized, but has presentation issues (section numbering, minor typos) and needs clearer notation/conditioning (e.g., Eq. 4.1) and more concrete discretization details for reproducibility.  
- Originality: novel combination of joint batch KG, IPA gradient, and adaptive discretization; incremental relative to prior joint-batch methods like q-EI, PPES, and ES-inspired approaches.  
- Significance: practically valuable for batch BO, especially under noise, and the IPA estimator may be broadly useful, but practical impact needs stronger scaling/runtime evidence.  
- Suggested improvements: add per-iteration complexity and wall-clock runtimes, include PPES baseline, vary q and test higher-dimensional tasks, clarify/discuss PES-style discretization, and analyze effect of GP hyperparameter re-fitting.  
- Pros: principled Bayes-optimal acquisition, novel practical stochastic gradient estimator, strong empirical gains under noise, and released code.  
- Cons: missing runtime/scaling analysis, no PPES comparison, under-specified discretization and IPA details in main text, and lack of theoretical guarantees beyond one-step optimality.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — solid and useful contribution but needs clearer exposition and expanded experiments (scaling, PPES, q-variation) to strengthen the case.
