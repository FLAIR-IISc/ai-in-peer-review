- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and claims superior performance.
- Paper is well-written; parallelizing BO is important and the Bayes-optimal batch idea is elegant and promising.
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors (GP assumption), which may be unrealistic in HPO due to dependent measurement errors and structural bias with small samples.
- Theoretical contribution is strong.
- Experimental criterion (test set error) is problematic for HPO; threshold-based metrics are suboptimal—metrics like AUC or log loss are preferable.
- Choice of datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10) is limited; better to evaluate on established HPO benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib).
- Benchmark is too small and narrow: only two datasets, a single task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), lacking diversity to assess generality.
- Overall: valuable theoretical work but the empirical evaluation is too limited to be fully convincing.
