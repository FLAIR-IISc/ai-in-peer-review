- Paper proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) method using Monte Carlo gradient estimation and reports better performance than other batch BO methods.  
- Concern that batch KG itself is not new; prior works (e.g., [1], [2]) have explored similar concepts and should be acknowledged.  
- Request that authors explicitly state the novel contribution relative to existing batch KG studies.  
- Reviewer believes the main novelty is the proposed gradient estimation for finding optimal batches and asks authors to confirm this.  
- Recommend experimental or mathematical comparison of the proposed gradient estimator against previous batch KG approaches for efficiency and effectiveness.  
- Section 5.2 is crucial and should be expanded with more detail to improve reader understanding.  
- Expected Improvement (EI) typically assumes noiseless observations; clarify whether a noise-aware EI variant was used or discuss the fairness of noisy comparisons.  
- Abbreviation "IPA" is redefined (line 162); avoid redefinition or use a different abbreviation to prevent confusion.  
- Rating: pending revisions — authors should address the above concerns to strengthen the paper.
