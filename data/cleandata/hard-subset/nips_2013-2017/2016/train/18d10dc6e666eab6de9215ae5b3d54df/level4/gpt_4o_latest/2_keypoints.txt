- Introduces a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and shows superior performance to existing methods.  
- Manuscript is well-written and easy to understand.  
- Parallelizing Bayesian optimization is important for practical hyperparameter tuning; the proposed approach is intriguing and more refined than many existing methods.  
- Ability to determine a Bayes-optimal batch is particularly promising.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (standard for GP-based BO), but this may be unrealistic since measurement errors often reflect generalization-vs-empirical discrepancies and structural biases in hyperparameter space.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong.  
- Experimental evaluation is underwhelming because it uses test set error as the optimization criterion, which is suboptimal for hyperparameter tuning; metrics like AUC or log loss would be preferable.  
- Benchmarks are limited to MNIST and CIFAR10; using HPOlib benchmarks would be more appropriate.  
- Benchmark scope is narrow—only two datasets, one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN)—so results may not generalize across tasks and models.  
- Overall: compelling and theoretically valuable paper, but experimental evaluation is too narrow to be fully convincing.
