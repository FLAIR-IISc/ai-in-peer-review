- Introduces a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects Bayes-optimal batches and reportedly outperforms existing BO methods.
- Manuscript is clearly written and the approach is elegant and practically important for hyperparameter optimization.
- The ability to determine Bayes-optimal batches is particularly promising.
- Method assumes independent Gaussian noise, which may be unrealistic for hyperparameter optimization due to structural bias and dependent measurement errors.
- Theoretical results are strong.
- Experiments are underwhelming because they optimize test-set error, which is problematic for HPO and thresholded metrics; metrics like AUC or log loss would be preferable.
- Evaluation is limited to MNIST and CIFAR-10 instead of broader HPO benchmarking suites like HPOlib.
- Benchmark is small and narrow: only two datasets, one task (classification), and two learning algorithms (logistic regression and a CNN), limiting generalizability.
- Overall positive about the paper and theory, but the limited experimental benchmark reduces the paper’s convincingness.
