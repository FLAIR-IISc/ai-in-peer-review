- Proposes selecting Bayes-optimal parallel batches using the batch knowledge gradient (q-KG) and estimates its gradient via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Empirically, q-KG is competitive on noise-free benchmarks and outperforms other parallel BO methods under observation noise.
- Main motivation is enabling multi-core parallel hyperparameter optimization, but the paper lacks end-to-end wall-clock time or practical speedup measurements.
- Batch size q is fixed at 4 in all experiments without justification or sensitivity analysis for other q values.
- Methodological novelty is unclear: both KG and IPA are established, and prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) already uses IPA for batch selection with EI — clarify differences and contributions.
- Experiments include standard artificial benchmarks and CNN/logistic regression on CIFAR-10/MNIST, but experimental protocol needs more clarity.
- Request comparisons against Wang et al. (2015) and explicit labeling/distinction of q-EI baselines (avoid ambiguous references to “parallel EI in MOE”).
- Need analyses on scalability with larger batch sizes (practical gains, computational/statistical limits) and justification given real systems often support far more than q=4.
- Ask whether the synchronous method can be extended to asynchronous settings and how q-KG/IPA would need to change.
- Require wall-clock comparisons with random search and sequential BO, quantification of batch-selection overhead, and discussion of its growth with q.
- Clarify plotting: what “iterations” means (evaluations or batches), whether initial design is counted, and why methods don’t all start from the same initial value.
- Minor issues: possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammatical fixes and correct citation of IPA’s foundational sources rather than Wang et al. (2015).
- Overall: promising and relevant approach, but needs stronger experimental evaluation (runtime, q justification, scaling) and clearer distinction from closely related work.
