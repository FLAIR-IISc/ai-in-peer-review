- Proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using the q-KG acquisition and an infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) method to maximize it.
- Experiments indicate q-KG is competitive with existing parallel BO in noise-free problems and outperforms them under observation noise.
- Overall approach is appealing for multi-core hyperparameter tuning, but some aspects are unclear and results are not fully convincing.
- Technical evaluation uses standard benchmark functions and experiments on CNN/logistic regression with CIFAR-10/MNIST.
- Lacks wall-clock time measurements or analysis of real speedup versus random search or sequential optimization.
- The fixed choice of batch size q = 4 across all experiments is not justified.
- Combination of knowledge gradient and IPA is notable, though neither component is individually novel.
- Needs explicit comparison/clarification with Wang et al. (2015) and the "parallel EI in MOE" approach.
- Scalability questions: unclear performance and gains when increasing the number of parallel runs beyond 4.
- Unclear whether the method can be extended to asynchronous execution similar to Spearmint.
- Missing comparison of overhead for computing batches and impact on wall-clock time.
- Plot labeling needs clarification: what “iterations” represent and whether the initial design is included on the x-axis.
- Minor issues: potential missing term in Eq. (4.1), grammatical errors, and incorrect reference formatting.
- Incorrectly attributes the introduction of infinitesimal perturbation analysis to Wang et al. (2015).
