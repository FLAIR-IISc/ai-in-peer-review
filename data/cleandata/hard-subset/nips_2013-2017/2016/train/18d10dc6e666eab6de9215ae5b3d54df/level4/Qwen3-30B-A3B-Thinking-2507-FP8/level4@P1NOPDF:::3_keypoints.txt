- Introduces a batch-optimization variant of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm for Bayesian optimization over finite domains, enabling parallel sampling.  
- Core contribution: extends KG to select multiple points simultaneously instead of sequentially.  
- Addresses computational intractability by proposing Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the batch KG criterion.  
- Gradient estimation via IPA enables scalable optimization of the information-gain objective.  
- Empirical results on benchmarks show competitive or superior performance compared to state-of-the-art confidence-bound methods.  
- Paper is well-structured and presents a practical, usable method for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Novelty judged incremental rather than transformative, building directly on established KG principles.  
- Primary technical novelty (IPA-based gradient estimation) aligns with standard gradient-based optimization techniques and is not viewed as a fundamental advance.  
- Notational ambiguity in Equation (3.1): unclear whether x^{(1:n)} denotes a sequence or set; μ(·) and K(·,·) are defined for individual vectors, creating undefined expressions.  
- Authors should clarify notation (apply μ and K per point or index individual x^{(i)}) to ensure reproducibility and technical accuracy.  
- Paper should better distinguish how batch KG differs fundamentally from prior batch methods (e.g., Thompson sampling, batch EI).  
- Need clearer justification for preferring IPA over alternative gradient estimators (e.g., finite differences) in this application.
