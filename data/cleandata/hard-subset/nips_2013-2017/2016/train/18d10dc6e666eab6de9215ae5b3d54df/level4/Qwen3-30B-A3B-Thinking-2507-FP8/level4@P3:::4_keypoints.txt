- Introduces q-KG, a novel parallel Bayesian optimization method that selects batches and is Bayes-optimal when one batch remains.  
- Extends the knowledge gradient (KG) acquisition function from sequential to batch settings and jointly optimizes batches.  
- Key computational innovation: uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently evaluate q-KG and its gradient, enabling practical implementation.  
- Builds on prior BO work and compares to parallel EI, batch UCB, and related methods, showing clear empirical advantages.  
- Theoretical strength: decision-theoretic foundation establishing Bayes-optimality for the parallel setting.  
- Computational strength: IPA-based approach yields significant speedups over naive evaluation.  
- Empirical strength: comprehensive experiments on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR-10), with consistent improvements, especially in noisy settings.  
- Presentation strength: well-structured and clearly organized paper.  
- Weakness: GP assumption not thoroughly justified for high-dimensional problems; needs discussion/testing in higher dimensions.  
- Weakness: Limited theoretical analysis—no error bounds or rigorous complexity/dimensionality scaling provided.  
- Weakness: Needs more detailed comparison/explanation relative to parallel EI (q-KG reduces to parallel EI under certain conditions).  
- Weakness: Notational inconsistencies (e.g., multiple uses of "A", unclear definition of input to μⁿ).  
- Significance: Substantial practical impact for parallelizing hyperparameter tuning and expensive ML pipelines.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, original, and empirically compelling; reviewer recommends acceptance despite some theoretical and notational gaps.
