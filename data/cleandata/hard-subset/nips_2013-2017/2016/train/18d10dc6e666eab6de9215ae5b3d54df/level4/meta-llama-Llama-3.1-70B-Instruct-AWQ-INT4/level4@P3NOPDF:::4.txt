This paper proposes the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization, focusing on scenarios where evaluating the function value is time-consuming. The authors introduce a new acquisition function, q-KG, which can output multiple new points for parallel evaluation. They also provide an efficient approach to evaluate the q-KG function. Empirical results are presented to validate their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed in this paper is important, as it enables the distribution of work in a way that is beneficial for the performance of data-driven applications. Instead of evaluating one point at a time, the algorithm considers the q knowledge gradient, which proposes q points to evaluate at the next iteration, significantly improving the algorithm's efficiency. This is an interesting idea worth exploring.

However, it is unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is suitable, particularly in neural network architectures where the input dimension is very large. It would be helpful if the authors could provide some theoretical results on the error bound of their method and a quantitative analysis of the complexity with regard to dimension. Nevertheless, this is a solid work in the field of Bayesian optimization. The method is sound, and the experiments are well-designed. The paper is well-written overall.

There are a few minor issues that need attention. On line 36, the phrase "the set of points to evaluate next that is" seems incomplete. On line 95, the definition of A is not provided. On line 104, the input of mu^n is not specified. It would be helpful to briefly discuss the parallel EI algorithm on line 128 to provide a clearer comparison. Finally, using different notations for A on line 140 would improve clarity.

Overall, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization, and with some minor revisions, it has the potential to make a significant impact.
