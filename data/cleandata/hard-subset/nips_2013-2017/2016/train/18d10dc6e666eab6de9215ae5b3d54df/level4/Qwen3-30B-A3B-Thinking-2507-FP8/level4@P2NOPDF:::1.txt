

This paper introduces a batch-sequential extension of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) criterion for Bayesian optimization, termed q-KG. After establishing necessary background on Gaussian processes and related work, the authors formally define the q-KG criterion and detail its computation, following the structure of the sequential KG framework. The method is evaluated through numerical experiments demonstrating superior performance over state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian optimization approaches. The work presents a significant contribution with clear societal relevance due to the potential efficiency gains from parallelizing optimization workflows.  

The paper is well-motivated and technically sound, making it a strong candidate for publication at NIPS. However, three minor issues require attention:  
1. **Literature precision**: The claim that "integrating EI with respect to posterior distributions" was novel is inaccurate. This concept was explicitly addressed in the Kriging parallel optimization paper (alongside Constant Liar), while Chevalier et al. [2] introduced CL-mix. Additionally, q-EI maximization via natural gradient is covered in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design."  
2. **Computational efficiency**: The manuscript does not quantify speed-ups achieved by transitioning from sequential to batch-sequential execution, a key practical consideration.  
3. **Technical clarifications**: Several points need elaboration:  
   - Assumptions on the domain \( \mathcal{A} \) (e.g., compactness) and objective function \( f \) (e.g., continuity) should be explicitly stated.  
   - The rationale for restricting initial sampling to Latin Hypercube (LHS) requires justification.  
   - Algorithm 1 omits hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating steps during batch acquisition.  
   - In Section 5.2, the definition of \( g \) uses "max" but the context suggests "min"; this must be clarified.  
   - The smoothness of \( g \) and the differentiability of Cholesky factor derivatives are not justified.  
   - The Matérn kernel is specified as 5/2 (not 2/5), which should be corrected.  
   - Figure 1’s experiments: It would be valuable to confirm whether identical Gaussian process fits could be replicated using alternative software (e.g., GPy, scikit-learn) to ensure methodological reproducibility.  

These points are minor and do not undermine the paper’s core contributions. The q-KG criterion is a meaningful advancement, and the experimental validation is robust. The societal impact of enabling efficient parallel Bayesian optimization—critical for applications like hyperparameter tuning in deep learning or experimental design—is substantial.  

The paper is well-written overall, with clear exposition of the method and compelling results. Addressing the minor technical and literature points would further strengthen the manuscript. I recommend acceptance with these revisions.
