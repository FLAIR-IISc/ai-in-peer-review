- Proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using q-KG acquisition and IPA-based gradient estimation to select batches.
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noiseless problems and outperforms others with observation noise; tested on synthetic functions, CNN (CIFAR-10), and logistic regression (MNIST).
- Batch size q=4 is used in all experiments but not justified.
- Requests wall-clock time experiments or analysis comparing parallel q-KG to random search and sequential BO, and asking about computational overhead per batch.
- Asks for comparison to Wang et al. (2015)/parallel EI in MOE, and clear statement of differences from existing IPA-based batch selection methods.
- Questions scalability to larger numbers of parallel runs and expected gains from increased parallelism.
- Asks whether the method can be extended to asynchronous execution (e.g., like Spearmint).
- Requests clarification of the x-axis “iterations” in plots (evaluations vs. batches), whether initial design is included, and why methods don’t all start at the same value.
- Notes novelty concern: both knowledge gradient and IPA are existing techniques, so novelty should be clarified relative to prior work.
- Praises clarity: technical background and experimental details are well described.
- Notes impact/usefulness: parallel hyperparameter tuning is highly relevant across disciplines.
- Minor comments: Eq. (4.1) seems to miss a z^(1:n) term; grammar fix “achieves”; rewrite parenthetical references as noun phrases; correct attribution that Wang et al. (2015) applied IPA rather than introduced it.
