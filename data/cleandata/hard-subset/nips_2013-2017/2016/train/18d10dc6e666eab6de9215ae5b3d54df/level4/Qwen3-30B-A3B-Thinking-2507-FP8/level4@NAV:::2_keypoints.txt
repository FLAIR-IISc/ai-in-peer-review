- Introduces a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Claims superior performance over existing parallel BO approaches.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and accessible.  
- Parallelization of BO is a critical practical advancement for hyperparameter optimization.  
- Proposed method is a compelling and elegant alternative to existing techniques.  
- The derivation of a Bayes-optimal batch is particularly promising.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors, which is questionable for HPO because measurement errors reflect generalization vs. empirical estimates and can exhibit structural bias, especially with small samples.  
- Theoretical foundation is robust.  
- Experimental evaluation is underwhelming because optimization uses test-set error, an inappropriate criterion for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Experimental benchmarks are narrow (MNIST, CIFAR10); inclusion of HPOlib would strengthen evaluation.  
- Benchmarks lack diversity—only two datasets and two classifiers (logistic regression and CNN)—so results may not generalize across algorithms and tasks.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution, but limited experimental scope undermines practical impact.
