- Introduces parallel batch Bayesian optimization using the q-KG acquisition function and an IPA-based estimator for its gradient.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noise-free benchmarks and outperforms alternatives under observation noise; includes CNN and logistic regression on CIFAR10/MNIST.  
- Lacks wall-clock time comparisons (vs random search and sequential BO) to demonstrate real parallelization benefits.  
- Batch size q=4 is used without justification or sensitivity analysis; this choice needs explanation.  
- Novelty limited: method combines established KG and IPA techniques; contribution is applying IPA to maximize q-KG.  
- Related work insufficiently discussed—must compare and distinguish from Wang et al. (2015), which uses IPA with EI for batch selection.  
- Recommend clearer labeling/distinction between methods (e.g., “parallel EI in MOE” vs “q-EI”) to avoid confusion.  
- Experiments use small parallelism (q=4); scalability to larger numbers of parallel evaluations (e.g., tens/hundreds) is not evaluated.  
- Method is synchronous; authors should address whether an asynchronous extension is possible.  
- Need to report computational overhead for batch selection and wall-clock speedups versus baselines.  
- Plots are unclear: define “iterations” (evaluations vs batches), clarify whether initial design is included, and explain why methods don’t start at the same value.  
- Questions for authors: comparison to Wang et al. (2015), scalability to larger q, support for asynchronous execution, wall-clock/time overhead, and clarify plot x-axis semantics.  
- Minor issues: missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammatical correction (“achieve” → “achieves”); improve citation phrasing; verify correct IPA citation (Wang et al. may apply rather than introduce IPA).
