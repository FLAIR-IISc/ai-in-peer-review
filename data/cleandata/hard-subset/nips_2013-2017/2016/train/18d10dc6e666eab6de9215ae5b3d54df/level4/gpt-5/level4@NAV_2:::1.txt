This paper addresses batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization. It introduces a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient acquisition function, q-KG. After reviewing related work and Gaussian process modeling, the authors define the parallel q-KG criterion, show how to compute it by extending the standard KG derivation, and present a suite of experiments in which q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch BO methods. The work is strong, and in my view has the qualities needed for NIPS. Given the practical relevance of parallel evaluations, the potential impact is high. Minor concerns: the paper does not analyze speed-ups from sequential to batch operation, and parts of the literature review are a bit imprecise. For example, integrating EI over posterior distributions already appears in “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” (alongside Constant Liar), with Chevalier et al. also proposing CL-mix [2]. In addition, maximization of q-EI via (natural) gradient methods is treated in “Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design.”
