- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization method that selects q points by maximizing expected reduction in the minimum posterior mean, generalizing KG and Bayes-optimal for one remaining batch.  
- Introduces an efficient Monte Carlo approach with an unbiased IPA gradient estimator and a practical discretization strategy for continuous domains.  
- Empirically competitive or superior to parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic tasks and HPO (MNIST logistic, CIFAR-10 CNN), especially under noisy evaluations; code released.  
- Builds on GP acquisition literature; contribution differs from prior work by jointly optimizing batches in continuous domains with IPA gradients rather than greedy/discrete approximations.  
- Strength: principled decision-theoretic, jointly-evaluated batch acquisition that is one-step Bayes-optimal.  
- Strength: efficient IPA-based stochastic gradient estimator and clear Monte Carlo optimization scheme.  
- Strength: consistent empirical improvements in noisy regimes and clear exposition with released code.  
- Weakness: assumes independent Gaussian observation noise, which may be unrealistic for HPO where noise can be biased, heteroscedastic, or correlated.  
- Weakness: uses test-set error as HPO objective; recommends proper scoring rules (log loss/AUC) and nested CV or validation/hold-out to avoid leakage.  
- Weakness: limited HPO experiments (two datasets, two classifiers), reducing generality; suggests broader benchmarks (SVMs, GBMs, regression, HPOlib/BOB).  
- Weakness: missing additional batch-BO baselines and ablations (q-EI with gradients, local penalization, q-Thompson, parallel ES; vary q, M, MC samples, GP re-estimation).  
- Weakness: lacks runtime/scalability analysis—need wall-clock time, CPU/GPU usage, scaling with q and dimension versus faster heuristics.  
- Weakness: notation confusion (reuse of A), minor typos, and insufficient detail on constructing AM_n and sensitivity to its choice.  
- Quality: derivation and IPA estimator are sound for one-step optimality but lack multi-step theoretical guarantees (regret or asymptotic consistency).  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized, but some notation and discretization details need clarification.  
- Originality: meaningful and novel extension of KG to batch selection in continuous domains with an efficient stochastic gradient method.  
- Significance: potentially impactful for noisy, parallel BO, but stronger empirical breadth would increase significance.  
- Pros summary: principled Bayes-optimal batch acquisition, efficient gradient estimation, strong noisy performance, reproducible code.  
- Cons summary: limited HPO evaluation, possibly unrealistic noise assumptions, missing runtime/scalability and broader baselines.  
- Overall: strong and elegant methodological contribution; recommend acceptance if authors broaden HPO evaluation and address noise realism and computational scaling, otherwise borderline accept.
