- Introduces q-KG, a parallel knowledge gradient acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization to speed up time-consuming evaluations.  
- Provides a scalable algorithm to evaluate q-KG and empirically validates its effectiveness, showing alignment with theoretical claims.  
- Highly relevant work: distributed/batch evaluation is important for scaling data-driven applications and q-KG offers a compelling solution.  
- Method is well-motivated, experiments are sound, and the paper is clearly written overall.  
- Concern: Assumes Gaussian processes (GPs); applicability to high-dimensional neural-network settings is questionable and needs justification or discussion of GP approximations.  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees on error bounds and on scalability with input dimension; needs explicit analysis.  
- Missing explicit computational-complexity analysis (e.g., as functions of dimensionality d and batch size q) to assess practical viability.  
- Presentation issues: redundant phrasing at line 36 that should be streamlined.  
- Notation issues: symbol "A" referenced without definition at line 95 and used inconsistently later (line 140); use distinct, defined symbols.  
- Clarify domain/input to μⁿ at line 104 (specify input points x).  
- Suggest briefly comparing q-KG with parallel expected improvement (EI) to contextualize advantages (line 128).  
- Minor typos (e.g., "fasten" → "accelerate" in the abstract) that should be corrected.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and practically useful contribution; addressing theoretical gaps and notation/typo fixes would make it a strong NIPS submission.
