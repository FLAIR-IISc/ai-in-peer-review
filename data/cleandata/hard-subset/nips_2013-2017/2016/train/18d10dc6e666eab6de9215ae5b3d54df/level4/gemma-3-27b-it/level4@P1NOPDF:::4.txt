This paper considers the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, addressing the scenario where function evaluations are computationally expensive. The authors propose a novel acquisition function, q-KG, designed to output multiple points for parallel evaluation. They also present an efficient approach for evaluating this q-KG function. Empirical results are included to validate their theoretical findings. The problem addressed is important; the ability to distribute computation is crucial for scaling data-driven applications. Instead of sequentially evaluating one point at a time, the algorithm considers a ‘knowledge gradient’ of size *q*, proposing *q* points for evaluation in each iteration, significantly improving efficiency. This is an interesting and worthwhile direction for exploration.

However, the assumption of a Gaussian process (GP) is not fully justified, especially when applied to neural network architectures with high-dimensional input spaces. It would be beneficial if the authors could provide theoretical results regarding the error bounds of their method, along with a quantitative analysis of its computational complexity as it scales with input dimension. 

Despite this, the work is solid overall and represents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The method is well-founded, and the experiments are well-executed. The paper is generally well-written. A few minor points require attention: on line 36, the phrase “the set of points to evaluate next that is” is somewhat redundant; on line 95, the definition of *A* is missing; on line 104, clarification is needed regarding the input of  μ̂ⁿ; line 128 would benefit from a brief discussion of the parallel Expected Improvement (EI) algorithm for context; and on line 140, using a different notation for *A* might avoid confusion.
