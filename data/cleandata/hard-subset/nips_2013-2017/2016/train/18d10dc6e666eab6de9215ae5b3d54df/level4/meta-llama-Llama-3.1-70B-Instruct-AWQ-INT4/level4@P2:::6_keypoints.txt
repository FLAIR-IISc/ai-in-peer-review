- Introduces q-KG, a novel decision-theoretic batch Bayesian optimization method.  
- q-KG uses the knowledge gradient to measure utility, generalizing expected improvement.  
- Proposes using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate q-KG gradients for efficient acquisition maximization.  
- Background and related work are clearly and concisely presented.  
- Experiments are well-organized and sufficiently described.  
- q-KG performs competitively on noiseless problems and outperforms alternatives on noisy problems.  
- Method is technically sound with thorough analysis of properties and behavior.  
- IPA-based gradient estimation is a key contribution enabling efficient implementation.  
- Algorithm and implementation are clearly described.  
- Strengths: novelty, originality, impact, ability to handle noisy observations, and readable writing.  
- Weakness: unclear rationale for fixing q = 4 in all experiments.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability to larger batch sizes.  
- Recommends acceptance overall, with minor questions and requested clarifications.  
- Question: comparison to other parallel BO methods (e.g., "Parallel Bayesian global optimization of expensive functions").  
- Question: how q-KG scales with larger batch sizes and its computational limits.  
- Question: can q-KG be extended to asynchronous batch evaluations (e.g., like Spearmint)?  
- Question: have you measured wall-clock time versus random search or sequential BO?  
- Minor: missing z^(1:n) in equation (4.1).  
- Minor: grammatical correction—“achieve” → “achieves.”  
- Minor: parenthetical citations should be phrased as noun phrases (e.g., “Snoek et al. [16] suggest…”).  
- Minor: Wang et al. (2015) may not be the correct original reference for IPA.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, solid analysis, clear writing, and experimental validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient complexity/scalability analysis, unexplained q choice, and limited comparison with other parallel BO methods.
