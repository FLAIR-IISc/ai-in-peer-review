- Proposes q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), a novel acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization that selects q points for simultaneous evaluation.  
- Provides an efficient optimization approach for the q-KG acquisition function and reports empirical results demonstrating effectiveness.  
- Addresses an important, relevant problem: parallelizing expensive function evaluations to improve optimization efficiency.  
- Extends the knowledge gradient principle to the batch setting, which is a well-motivated and interesting idea.  
- Overall contribution is solid: methodology appears sound, experiments are well-conducted, and the paper is generally well-written.  
- Limitation: heavy reliance on the Gaussian Process assumption, which may not suit all settings, especially high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural network hyperparameter tuning).  
- Would benefit from more theoretical analysis, such as error bounds and quantitative computational-complexity results with respect to input dimension.  
- Minor/clarity issues: grammatically awkward phrase at l.36.  
- Minor/clarity issues: notation 'A' should be explicitly defined when first introduced (l.95).  
- Minor/clarity issues: input argument for μⁿ is unclear (l.104).  
- Minor/clarity issues: provide brief discussion of related methods like parallel Expected Improvement (l.128).  
- Minor/clarity issues: inconsistent reuse of notation 'A' (l.140) — use distinct notations for clarity.
