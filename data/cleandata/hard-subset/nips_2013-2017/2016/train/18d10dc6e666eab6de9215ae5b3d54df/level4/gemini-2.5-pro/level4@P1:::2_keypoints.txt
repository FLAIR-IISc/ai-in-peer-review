- Addresses the important problem of parallel Bayesian optimization, highly relevant for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Proposes a novel parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) algorithm that selects a Bayes-optimal batch.  
- Decision-theoretic batch selection is more principled than many greedy heuristics.  
- Derives an efficient gradient-based method to maximize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art parallel BO methods, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and theoretically elegant.  
- Key modeling assumption—independent, normally distributed measurement errors—may be invalid in hyperparameter tuning and needs discussion.  
- Experimental metric choice (test set error) is suboptimal; threshold metrics can be less sensitive than alternatives like AUC or log-likelihood.  
- Lacks use of standardized BO benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib), limiting comparability to prior work.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (two datasets, one task, two models); broader tasks and model types are needed to demonstrate generality.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but empirical validation is too limited to fully establish practical superiority.
