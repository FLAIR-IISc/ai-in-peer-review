- Proposes q-KG, a parallel extension of the Knowledge Gradient policy that selects a batch of q points maximizing one-step expected improvement of the GP posterior mean.  
- Main technical contribution: an efficient gradient-based optimizer for q-KG using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to produce an unbiased Monte Carlo gradient estimator.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning show q-KG is competitive with or outperforms parallel EI and UCB, with stronger advantage in noisy settings.  
- Provides open-source code, supporting reproducibility.  
- Potential unfairness in experiments: comparison in noisy settings may use a noise-agnostic EI baseline; authors should clarify which parallel EI variant was used.  
- Clarity issue: Section 5.2 (IPA-based gradient derivation) is overly condensed and needs a more detailed exposition for reader understanding and reproducibility.  
- Originality concern: the parallel KG concept is not clearly distinguished from prior batch KG work (e.g., Wang et al., 2015); novelty seems mainly the IPA-based gradient estimator.  
- Missing comparison: no experimental or analytical comparison to prior batch KG methods (notably Wang et al., 2015), weakening claims of superiority.  
- Significance: addresses a practical, impactful problem and makes non-myopic batch acquisition practical via efficient gradient optimization.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled decision-theoretic formulation, meaningful technical contribution (IPA gradient), strong empirical performance, and released code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient novelty positioning, lack of direct comparison to prior batch KG, potentially unfair EI baseline in noisy tests, and insufficient technical detail in gradient derivation.  
- Minor comment: redundant redefinition of the abbreviation "IPA" in the paper.
