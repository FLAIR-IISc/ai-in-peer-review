- Introduces q-KG, a parallel extension of the Knowledge Gradient for batch-sequential Bayesian optimization.  
- Defines q-KG, derives its computation by extending standard KG, and provides numerical experiments showing q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Paper is well-written, technically solid, addresses an important problem, and has substantial practical impact; suitable for NIPS.  
- Missing empirical analysis of wall-clock speedups from batching versus sequential evaluations.  
- Literature review imprecise: integration of EI over posteriors and Constant Liar/CL-mix were previously discussed in prior work.  
- Prior work studied maximization of q-EI using natural gradients and should be cited.  
- Clarify assumptions: is domain A compact and is the objective f continuous?  
- Initialization choice: why restrict initial design to a Latin hypercube sample, could other designs be used, and does this affect performance?  
- Algorithm 1: unclear how GP hyperparameters are handled—periodic MLE/MAP re-estimation or full Bayesian updates—and what the computational cost/schedule is.  
- Section 5.2: sign convention for g (maximization vs minimization) needs verification.  
- Section 5.2: the smoothness of g is not immediate; provide justification or conditions.  
- Gradients through Cholesky factorization are nontrivial—add a citation or short derivation.  
- Typo/error: Matérn kernel order appears to be 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Experimental fairness: confirm whether the same GP modeling choices and software were used across methods or discuss potential discrepancies.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, timely contribution extending KG to parallel settings; addressing the above clarifications would improve clarity and impact.
