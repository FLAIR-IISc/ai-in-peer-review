

This paper addresses the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization, focusing on scenarios where function evaluations are time-intensive. The authors propose a novel acquisition function, q-KG, designed to output multiple points for simultaneous evaluation, alongside an efficient computational approach for evaluating q-KG. Empirical results support the theoretical claims. The problem is significant: distributed evaluation is crucial for scaling data-driven applications, and the q-KG approach substantially improves efficiency over sequential methods by enabling parallel function evaluations at each iteration. This represents a valuable direction for future work.  

However, the assumption of Gaussian processes (GPs) as the underlying model warrants scrutiny, particularly in high-dimensional settings like neural network training, where GPs often struggle with scalability and accuracy. The authors should clarify whether their method remains robust in such contexts or provide theoretical justification for the GP assumption. Additionally, the paper lacks theoretical guarantees on error bounds for the proposed algorithm and a quantitative analysis of computational complexity relative to input dimensionality—critical gaps for assessing scalability in real-world applications.  

Despite these concerns, the work is methodologically sound, with a well-motivated approach and thorough experimental validation. The paper is clearly written and makes a solid contribution to Bayesian optimization. Minor issues include:  
- Line 36: "the set of points to evaluate next that is" → "the set of points to evaluate next, which is"  
- Line 95: Clarify the definition of set $A$  
- Line 104: Specify the input to $\mu^n$  
- Line 128: Briefly compare with parallel EI as a baseline  
- Line 140: Use distinct notation for $A$ (e.g., $\mathcal{A}$) to avoid confusion  

Overall, this is a strong submission that advances batch Bayesian optimization. Addressing the theoretical gaps and notational clarity would further strengthen the paper.
