- Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch Bayesian optimization method selecting one-step Bayes-optimal batches.
- Proposes an efficient strategy to maximize the q-KG acquisition by estimating its gradient via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).
- Paper is well-structured, presenting background, method, computational approach, and experiments clearly.
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art batch methods, especially under noisy evaluations.
- Significance: Addresses practical need for parallel Bayesian optimization in large-scale applications.
- Originality/quality: Principled extension of Knowledge Gradient to batch setting and practical IPA-based gradient computation.
- Empirical evaluation: Thorough comparisons on synthetic and real tasks demonstrating robustness to noise.
- Related-work positioning could be improved by citing Ginsbourger et al. on averaging EI and Marmin et al. on differentiating multipoint EI.
- Experimental scope lacks wall-clock speed-up analysis comparing batch-sequential to standard sequential approaches.
- Clarify formal assumptions on domain A (e.g., compactness) and objective f (e.g., continuity) used in analysis.
- Justify choice of initial Latin Hypercube Design over other space-filling designs in Algorithm 1.
- Confirm whether GP hyperparameters are re-trained every iteration and discuss the computational overhead.
- Provide justification or reference for interchanging gradient and expectation in the gradient derivation involving an argmin.
- Add a reference for computing derivatives of the Cholesky factor to aid readers unfamiliar with the technique.
- Explain measures taken to ensure fair comparisons across different open-source benchmark packages and GP/hyperparameter settings.
