1. Problem addressed: batch Bayesian optimization—selecting q points to evaluate in parallel to optimize expensive black-box functions.  
2. Main contribution: proposal of the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) acquisition function that is one-step Bayes-optimal for batch selection.  
3. Optimization technique: express q-KG as a Monte Carlo expectation and use Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis to obtain an unbiased stochastic gradient for gradient-based optimization.  
4. Empirical evaluation: tested on synthetic benchmarks and two hyperparameter tuning tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR-10 CNN) under noise-free and noisy settings.  
5. Performance result: q-KG is competitive with or outperforms state-of-the-art parallel BO methods, especially robust in noisy evaluation scenarios.  
6. Strength — principled approach: jointly optimizes the entire batch with a decision-theoretic, one-step Bayes-optimal derivation (vs. heuristic or greedy baselines).  
7. Strength — technical novelty: non-trivial application of Monte Carlo + IPA to derive an unbiased gradient makes the principled q-KG practical for continuous domains.  
8. Strength — empirical rigor and reproducibility: thorough experiments across settings and provided open-source code.  
9. Weakness — incremental originality: q-KG extends existing KG from q=1 to q>1, so conceptual novelty is mainly an extension rather than a radically new paradigm.  
10. Weakness — computational overhead: Monte Carlo sampling and stochastic gradient ascent per BO iteration can be much more expensive than simpler heuristics, raising trade-off concerns.  
11. Weakness — reliance on discretization: approximating the inner min via domain discretization may make performance sensitive to the chosen discrete set size and quality.  
12. Specific suggestion: clarify notation (e.g., explicitly state µ(x(1:n)) and K(...) are vector and matrix quantities) for broader readability.  
13. Overall verdict: well-executed, impactful paper with solid technical and empirical contributions; reviewer recommends acceptance.
