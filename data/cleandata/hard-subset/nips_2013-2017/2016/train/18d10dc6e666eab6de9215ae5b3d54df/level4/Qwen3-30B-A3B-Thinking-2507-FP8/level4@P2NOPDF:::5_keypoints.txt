- Proposes a batch extension of Knowledge Gradient (KG) using a Monte‑Carlo-based gradient estimator and reports superior performance over existing batch BO methods.  
- Core novelty appears to be the gradient estimation technique rather than the batch KG framework, which prior work (Wang et al. [1], Peng [2]) has already explored.  
- Authors must explicitly clarify how this work advances beyond [1] and [2], especially in the gradient estimation aspect.  
- Provide direct comparisons (experiments or analysis) with prior batch KG approaches to substantiate claimed novelty.  
- Section 5.2 lacks rigor: expand the derivation, justify the sampling strategy, provide convergence guarantees, and analyze computational complexity versus alternative gradient estimators.  
- The current presentation of Section 5.2 is procedural; it needs deeper theoretical and algorithmic substance.  
- Comparison with Expected Improvement (EI) in noisy experiments is inappropriate because EI assumes noiseless observations; use a noise-aware EI variant or explicitly justify and contextualize the baseline choice.  
- Experiments must include noise-aware baselines and direct comparisons to prior batch KG methods to validate robustness and effectiveness.  
- Minor fixes: introduce "IPA" (Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis) at first use and format the thesis citation [2] with year and institution.  
- Overall: the gradient estimation idea is promising but requires clearer positioning against prior work, stronger theoretical justification, corrected baselines, and improved experimental validation.
