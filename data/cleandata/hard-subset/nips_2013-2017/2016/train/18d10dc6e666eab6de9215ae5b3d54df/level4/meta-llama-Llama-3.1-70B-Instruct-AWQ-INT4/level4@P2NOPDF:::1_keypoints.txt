- Proposes a batch-sequential Bayesian optimization method (q-KG), defines the parallel KG criterion, details its computation, and shows experiments where q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods.
- Paper is well-written, has significant potential impact, and merits publication.
- Does not analyze computational speed-ups from moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.
- Literature review contains imprecise/missing citations: integrating EI over posteriors was explored in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization" and "Constant Liar"; Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix in [2]; q-EI with natural gradient was studied in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design."
- Unclear mathematical assumptions: is the domain A compact and is f continuous?
- Why is the initial candidate set A restricted to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS)?
- Algorithm 1 lacks discussion of hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating of GP hyperparameters.
- In Section 5.2, unclear whether g is defined for maximization or minimization; smoothness of g is not stated and the derivative of the Cholesky factor is not justified.
- Typographical/parameter error: Matérn parameter is 5/2, not 2/5.
- Experiments: could the same Gaussian processes be fitted with different software to validate results and increase confidence?
