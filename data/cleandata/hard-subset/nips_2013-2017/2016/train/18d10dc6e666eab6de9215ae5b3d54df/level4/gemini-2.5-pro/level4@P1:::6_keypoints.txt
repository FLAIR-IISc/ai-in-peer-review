- Addresses the important problem of parallelizing Bayesian optimization for multi-core environments.
- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic, batch extension of the knowledge gradient acquisition function.
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presents technical details and experiments.

- Lacks wall-clock time/runtime comparisons and discussion of batch selection computational overhead versus simpler baselines.
- Uses fixed batch size q=4 without justification and provides no scalability analysis for larger q.
- Plots use "iterations" on x-axis unclearly — unclear if this means batches or total evaluations, and whether initial design points are included (curves don’t start uniformly).

- Novelty appears incremental: KG is known and IPA-based gradient optimization was used previously (e.g., Wang et al., 2015).
- Relationship to Wang et al. (2015) and the specific parallel EI implementation compared (MOE/q-EI) is unclear and needs explicit clarification.

- Method focuses on synchronous batches; no discussion of asynchronous settings or how q-KG could be extended for practical asynchronous use.

- Minor wording/grammar: "achieve" -> "achieves".
- Citation style: avoid parenthetical citations used as nouns; name authors with citations.
- Notation: make En[·] explicit as expectation over the upcoming measurement y(z(1:q)).
