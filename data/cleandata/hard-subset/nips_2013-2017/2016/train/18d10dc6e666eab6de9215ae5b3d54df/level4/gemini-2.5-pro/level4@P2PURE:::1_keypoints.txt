- Paper introduces q-KG, a parallel Knowledge Gradient criterion for batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization, with computation analogous to standard KG.  
- Numerical experiments claim q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Reviewer views the contribution as valuable and recommends publication at NIPS.  
- Parallelizing Bayesian Optimization is an important problem with significant potential impact.  
- Primary criticism: no analysis of computational speed-ups of batch-sequential vs purely sequential approaches.  
- Literature review contains imprecise attributions: integrating EI over the posterior was already in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization," which also introduced Constant Liar.  
- CL-mix heuristic attribution should credit Chevalier et al. in [2].  
- Prior work on maximizing q-EI with the natural gradient (“Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement…”) should be acknowledged.  
- Question 1: Are there implicit assumptions that domain A is compact and f is continuous — these should be stated explicitly.  
- Question 2: Why restrict initial design points to a Latin Hypercube Sample — what motivates this choice?  
- Question 3: Does Algorithm 1 include re-estimation or Bayesian updating of GP hyperparameters during optimization?  
- Question 4: In Section 5.2, should the definition of g involve maximization or minimization (clarify sign/opt direction)?  
- Question 5: Smoothness of g is assumed in Section 5.2 but not justified — provide brief justification.  
- Question 6: Use of the derivative of the Cholesky factor is non-trivial — include a reference or brief explanation.  
- Question 7: Possible typo in Matérn kernel parameter — should it be 5/2 instead of 2/5?  
- Question 8: For Figure 1 experiments, were GP models for all competing methods fitted using the same software framework to ensure fair comparison?
