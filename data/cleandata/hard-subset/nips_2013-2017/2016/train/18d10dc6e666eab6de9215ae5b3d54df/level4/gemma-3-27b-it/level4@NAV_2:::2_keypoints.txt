- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal evaluation batches.  
- Method consistently outperforms existing batch BO algorithms on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong and more grounded than many existing methods; derivation of Bayes-optimal batch selection is promising.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed evaluation errors, a common but simplifying GP-based assumption.  
- The independent-Gaussian noise assumption can be problematic in HPO, where noise often arises from cross-validation variance and may exhibit structural biases.  
- Empirical evaluation uses test set error as the optimization criterion, which can be a suboptimal and potentially misleading metric for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Benchmarks are limited to MNIST and CIFAR10 and two learning algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), reducing diversity and generality of results.  
- Recommends evaluation on a broader suite (e.g., HPOlib) to provide a more robust assessment.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical advance and important practical direction, but empirical scope is limited and may not demonstrate broad applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, clear presentation, promising empirical gains, and relevance to practical parallelization of BO.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited and narrow empirical evaluation, questionable evaluation metric, and simplifying noise assumption.
