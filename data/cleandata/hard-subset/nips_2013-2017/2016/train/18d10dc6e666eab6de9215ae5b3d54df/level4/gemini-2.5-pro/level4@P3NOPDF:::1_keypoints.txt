- Novelty & significance: Introduces q-Knowledge Gradient, extending Knowledge Gradient to batch selection for parallel Bayesian Optimization.  
- Technical soundness: Thorough derivation of q-KG and its gradient with a clear Monte Carlo-based computational strategy.  
- Empirical validation: Strong experiments on synthetic and benchmark functions showing q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch methods (e.g., q-EI, Constant Liar variants).  
- Potential impact: Makes Bayesian Optimization more practical for parallel evaluation settings (hyperparameter tuning, experiments, industrial design).  
- Computational cost analysis: Lacks discussion of computational trade-offs and wall-clock speed-up from sequential to batch-sequential execution.  
- Literature precision: Some citations need refinement—prior work on q-EI and Constant Liar variants should be more accurately credited.  
- Specific citation notes: q-EI’s integration over the posterior incumbent and Chevalier et al.’s CL-mix contribution should be properly cited.  
- Additional related work: Should cite work on differentiating multipoint Expected Improvement and natural-gradient maximization of q-EI.  
- Assumptions: Authors should state theoretical assumptions on domain A (e.g., compactness) and properties of f (e.g., continuity).  
- Initial design choice: Clarify why Latin Hypercube Sampling was chosen over other space-filling designs.  
- GP hyperparameters: Algorithm 1 omits whether GP hyperparameters are fixed or re-estimated after each batch—clarify for reproducibility.  
- Definition of g: Clarify whether the operation defining g in Section 5.2 is a max or a min (possible formulation error).  
- Smoothness of g: Address differentiability concerns of g, since it involves a max over posterior samples but gradient-based optimization is used.  
- Cholesky derivative: Provide a reference or brief note on computing derivatives of the Cholesky factor used in the gradient.  
- Typo in kernel description: Kernel likely Matérn 5/2, not “2/5”; correct this typo.  
- GP implementation reproducibility: Confirm whether results were cross-validated across GP software to rule out implementation-specific effects.  
- Overall recommendation: Review recommends acceptance, deeming the paper a significant and well-executed contribution.
