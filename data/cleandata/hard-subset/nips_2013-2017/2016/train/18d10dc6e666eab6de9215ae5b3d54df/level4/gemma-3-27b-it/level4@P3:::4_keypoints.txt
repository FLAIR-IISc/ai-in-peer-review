- Proposes q-KG, a Bayes-optimal acquisition function to select a batch of q points for parallel Bayesian Optimization.
- Introduces an IPA-based gradient estimator to efficiently optimize q-KG, crucial when q is large.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance versus baselines on synthetic problems and hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations.
- Provides code to enable reproducibility and community adoption.
- Addresses an important practical problem: accelerating expensive function evaluations via parallelization.
- The method is grounded in decision theory and moves beyond greedy batch selection approaches.
- Presentation is clear and logically structured.
- Reliance on a Gaussian Process surrogate may limit applicability in high-dimensional settings (e.g., neural architecture search).
- Lacks detailed theoretical analysis: convergence proofs, error bounds, and quantification of discretization impact are missing.
- Experimental scope is limited; broader hyperparameter tuning tasks/datasets would strengthen generalizability claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically motivated approach; strong empirical gains; practical via efficient optimization; code release.
- Arguments against acceptance: GP assumption limits applicability; insufficient rigorous theoretical guarantees.
- Specific manuscript suggestions: reword l.36; clarify definition of A (l.95) and input of µ^n(x) (l.104); discuss parallel EI (l.128); use distinct notation for A and its discretization (l.140).
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with convincing results; recommend acceptance despite noted limitations.
