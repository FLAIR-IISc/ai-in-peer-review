- Introduces q-KG: a novel batch Bayesian optimization criterion extending Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Provides a theoretical foundation and computational details for q-KG.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient estimation—a key practical contribution.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presenting motivation and method.  
- Minor omission: no explicit analysis of speed-ups from batch-sequential vs. purely sequential optimization.  
- Literature review imprecision: iterative batch EI averaging and Constant Liar (Ginsbourger et al.) and q-EI natural-gradient work (Marmin et al.) should be cited/positioned more precisely.  
- Query: clarify assumptions on feasible domain A (compactness) and continuity of objective f to strengthen theory.  
- Query: justify choice of Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS) for initial design.  
- Query: provide details on GP hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating in Algorithm 1 for reproducibility.  
- Query: confirm definition of g in Section 5.2 involves a minimization.  
- Concern: justify smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor more thoroughly.  
- Typo/correction: kernel is Matérn 5/2, not 2/5, in Section 6.  
- Suggestion: verify Figure 3 experiments by fitting same GPs with different software implementations to ensure consistency.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, IPA contribution, clear exposition, novel parallel KG extension, and significant practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor literature imprecisions, lack of quantified speed-up analysis, and missing implementation details (hyperparameter updates, LHS justification).  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept if minor points are addressed, as contributions are significant and results compelling.
