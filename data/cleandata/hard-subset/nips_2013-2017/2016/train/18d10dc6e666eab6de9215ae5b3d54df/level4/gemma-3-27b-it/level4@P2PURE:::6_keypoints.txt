- Introduces a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method using the knowledge gradient (q-KG) and infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for acquisition optimization.  
- Empirical results: q-KG is competitive with existing methods on noiseless problems and outperforms them under observation noise.  
- Experimental shortcomings: lacks wall-clock time measurements or comparisons quantifying parallel gains versus random or sequential search.  
- Hyperparameter choice concern: rationale for fixing batch size q=4 across experiments is not explained.  
- Novelty concern: combining q-KG and IPA is incremental; prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) already applies IPA for batch selection using expected improvement.  
- Impact: parallel hyperparameter tuning is broadly useful across ML and other fields.  
- Presentation: technical background and experiments are well-described and reproducible.  
- Comparison request: need explicit comparison to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI (q-EI) and clearer distinction from Spearmint’s implementation.  
- Scalability question: unclear how the method scales to large q (dozens to hundreds) as used in other works.  
- Asynchrony question: unclear if or how the method could be extended to asynchronous batch execution.  
- Overhead question: missing evaluation of computational overhead for batch selection and overall wall-clock performance.  
- Plot clarity: x-axis “iterations” is ambiguous—need to state whether it counts evaluations or batches and whether initial design is included.  
- Minor corrections: missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammar fixes; citation phrasing; IPA attribution may be incorrect.
