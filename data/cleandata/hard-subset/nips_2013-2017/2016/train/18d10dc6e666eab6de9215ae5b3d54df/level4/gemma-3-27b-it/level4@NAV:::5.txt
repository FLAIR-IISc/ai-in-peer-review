This paper presents a batch implementation of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm, employing a Monte-Carlo based gradient estimation technique to identify promising batches of design points. Experimental results demonstrate performance gains over existing batch Bayesian Optimization (BO) methods. 

1. The claim of novelty regarding the batch KG approach requires clarification. Existing works, such as [1] and [2], have previously explored batch KG. The primary contribution of this paper appears to be the proposed gradient estimation method for batch selection. If this is the case, a comparative analysis – either experimental or analytical – against these prior batch KG implementations is crucial to substantiate the method's efficiency and effectiveness.

2. Section 5.2, which details the core gradient estimation process, should be expanded upon. This section appears central to the paper's contribution and warrants a more thorough explanation.

3. The use of Expected Improvement (EI), which inherently assumes noiseless observations, creates a potential imbalance in the comparison presented in Section 6.2 (noisy setting). Is a noise-aware variant of EI being utilized? If the standard EI is employed, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed acknowledging the limitations of EI in the presence of noise.

4. In line 162, the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined, which is stylistically unusual.
