- Proposes a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method using the knowledge gradient (q-KG) and IPA to estimate its gradient for acquisition maximization.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance on noiseless problems and superior performance under observation noise.  
- Positive focus on parallel hyperparameter optimization for multi-core environments.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks wall-clock time comparisons and analysis of real speedup versus simpler methods (random search, sequential BO).  
- Unclear justification for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Novelty is limited to combining known techniques (knowledge gradient and IPA) rather than introducing fundamentally new methods.  
- Needs clearer comparison and distinction from prior work, especially Wang et al. (2015) on parallel EI with IPA.  
- Scalability to larger batch sizes (many-core systems) is not evaluated or discussed.  
- Method appears synchronous; extension to asynchronous parallel evaluation is not addressed.  
- Computational overhead per batch and its impact on wall-clock performance are not reported.  
- Plots/x-axis ambiguity: unclear whether "iterations" means function evaluations or batches and why curves start at different x-values.  
- Minor presentation issues: missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1), grammar errors, and citation phrasing; appropriate citation for IPA's origin questioned.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising (especially for noisy settings) but weakened by missing comparisons to related work and lack of wall-clock/time-scalability analysis.
