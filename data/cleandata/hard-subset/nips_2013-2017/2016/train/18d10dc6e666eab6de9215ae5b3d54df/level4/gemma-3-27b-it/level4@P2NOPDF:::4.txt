This paper presents a parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian Optimization, specifically addressing scenarios where function evaluations are computationally expensive. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, q-KG, designed to suggest multiple points for simultaneous evaluation, and detail an efficient approach for its evaluation. The empirical results presented support the theoretical claims made in the paper.

The problem addressed – accelerating Bayesian Optimization through parallelization – is both important and practically relevant. Distributing the computational load across multiple evaluations is crucial for scaling data-driven applications. The proposed approach, which considers *q* candidate points in each iteration (as opposed to sequential evaluation), has the potential to significantly improve efficiency. This is a promising direction for research.

However, the reliance on a Gaussian Process (GP) prior warrants further discussion. The suitability of GPs decreases with increasing input dimensionality, a common characteristic of many modern applications, particularly those involving neural network architectures. Exploring the method's performance or limitations in high-dimensional spaces would strengthen the work.  Furthermore, while the paper demonstrates empirical success, providing theoretical guarantees on the error bound of the proposed method, and an analysis of its computational complexity as a function of dimensionality, would significantly enhance its contribution.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The method appears well-founded, and the experimental validation is thorough. The paper is generally well-written and clear.
