Summary:
The paper studies the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization in settings where function evaluations are time-consuming. It introduces a new acquisition function, q-KG, that proposes multiple points for parallel evaluation, along with an efficient procedure to compute q-KG. Empirical results are provided to support the theoretical development.

Strengths:
- The problem is important, and enabling parallel or distributed evaluations can substantially improve the performance of data-driven applications.
- By selecting q points per iteration, the proposed q-KG approach can significantly accelerate the optimization process relative to sequential methods.
- The idea is interesting and worth exploring further. The method appears solid, the experiments are sound, and the paper is generally well written.

Concerns and suggestions:
- It is unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is appropriate in high-dimensional settings, such as neural network architectures with very large input spaces. Some discussion or justification of this modeling choice in such regimes would be valuable.
- It would strengthen the paper to provide theoretical results on error bounds for the proposed algorithm and a quantitative analysis of computational and statistical complexity as a function of dimension.

Minor comments and typos:
- l.36: Consider revising “the set of points to evaluate next that is …” for clarity.
- l.95: What is the definition of A?
- l.104: What is the input to μ^n?
- l.128: It would be helpful to briefly discuss the parallel EI (Expected Improvement) algorithm for context and comparison.
- l.140: Consider using different notation for A to avoid ambiguity.

Overall assessment:
This is a solid contribution to batch Bayesian optimization. The proposed q-KG acquisition function and its efficient evaluation procedure address a practical and important setting. Addressing the concerns about GP assumptions in high dimensions and adding theoretical guarantees (error bounds and dimension-dependent complexity) would further strengthen the work.
