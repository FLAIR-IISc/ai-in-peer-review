This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG). A gradient estimation method based on Monte-Carlo simulation is proposed to find an optimal batch of design points. Experiments show that the proposed method performs better than other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

1. As far as I know, batch KG is not a new method proposed by this paper. Please clarify the contribution of this paper in relation to previous batch KG papers such as [1] and [2]. In my opinion, the contribution is estimating the gradient to find the optimal batch. If so, the efficiency/effectiveness of the proposed method should be compared to previous batch KG papers through experiments or possibly mathematical analysis.

2. Section 5.2 should be explained in more detail, as I think this section is the main point of this paper.

3. Basically, EI assumes noise-free observations. Therefore, it is inappropriate to compare in a noise setting. Is any modification of EI used in relation to noise? If simple EI is used, the results in section 6.2 should be discussed for the reason that EI does not consider noise.
