- Introduces a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and shows superior performance; paper is well-written and clear.
- Parallelizing Bayesian optimization is important for practical HPO; the approach is interesting, elegant, and the Bayes-optimal batch idea is promising.
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (standard GP assumption), but this can be problematic for HPO where measurement errors (e.g., CV vs. generalization) exhibit structural bias and dependence.
- Theoretical foundation is strong.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: using test-set error as the optimization criterion is questionable—metrics like AUC or log loss would be preferable.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: benchmarks are narrow (only MNIST and CIFAR10); should include HPOlib or broader benchmark suites.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: scope is small (two datasets, one task—classification, and two learners), yet optimizer performance can vary across algorithms, so a more diverse benchmark is needed.
- Overall, valuable theoretical contribution but experimental results are not entirely convincing due to the limited benchmark.
