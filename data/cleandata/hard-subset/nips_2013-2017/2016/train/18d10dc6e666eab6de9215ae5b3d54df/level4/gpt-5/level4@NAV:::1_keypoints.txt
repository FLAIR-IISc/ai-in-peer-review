- Paper introduces a batch-sequential variant of the Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for Bayesian optimization.
- Background on related work and Gaussian processes is provided; q-KG is defined and computed following standard KG methodology.
- Numerical experiments show q-KG often outperforms certain state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods.
- Reviewer recommends publication and notes significant societal impact of enabling parallel BO.
- Minor critique: paper does not analyze speed-ups gained from moving from sequential to batch-sequential execution.
- Literature review contains imprecise claims: integrating EI over the posterior and Constant Liar approaches were already discussed in prior work; Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix.
- Related work omission: maximization of q-EI using a natural gradient was studied in “Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design.”
- Theoretical/assumption question: is the domain A compact and is the objective f continuous?
- Initialization question: why is A restricted to a Latin hypercube sample (LHS) at initialization?
- Algorithmic question: how are GP hyperparameters handled during Algorithm 1—re-estimated or updated in a Bayesian way?
- Clarification needed in Sec. 5.2: is g defined as a max or a min, and is its smoothness justified?
- Technical concern: the derivative of the Cholesky factor used in derivations may not be straightforward.
- Possible typo/clarification: Matérn kernel order likely 5/2, not 2/5.
- Experimental detail: for Figure 1, could the same GPs be fit using different software (reproducibility/implementation differences)?
