- Introduces a stochastic-gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multi-dimensional objective over a discrete search space.  
- Main contribution: algorithm can identify a batch of points for parallel objective evaluations each iteration.  
- Core challenge: selecting sampling points according to an optimality criterion for batch decisions.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected gain from additional samples to guide selection.  
- Extends KG to a batch (q-KG) setting where multiple points are chosen concurrently.  
- Because exact optimization is intractable, proposes Monte Carlo simulation plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients.  
- Algorithm maximizes information gain over candidate batches and outperforms or matches benchmarks, particularly methods using confidence bounds.  
- Manuscript is well-presented and method appears practically valuable.  
- Reviewer questions the novelty: primarily an extension of existing KG to batch processing, with limited novelty beyond applying IPA to estimate q-KG gradients.  
- Specific clarification requested: ambiguous notation in Eq. (3.1) for x^(1:n) and definitions of mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)).
