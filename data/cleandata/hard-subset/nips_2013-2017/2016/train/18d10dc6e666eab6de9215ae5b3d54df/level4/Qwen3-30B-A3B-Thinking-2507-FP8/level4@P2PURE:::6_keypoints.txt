- Proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using q-KG (knowledge gradient) and an IPA-based gradient estimator to maximize the batch acquisition function.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noise-free problems and outperforms baselines under observation noise.  
- Reviewer appreciates the motivation for parallel hyperparameter optimization but finds several aspects unclear and the experiments unconvincing.  
- Technical: uses standard synthetic benchmarks plus CNN/logistic regression on CIFAR-10/MNIST but lacks wall-clock time comparisons and practical speedup analysis versus random search or sequential BO.  
- Experimental: choice of fixed batch size q=4 across experiments is unexplained.  
- Novelty: q-KG and IPA-based maximization are not novel; prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) already applies IPA for batch selection and should be compared.  
- Impact: parallel hyperparameter tuning is highly relevant for many real-world applications.  
- Clarity: technical background and experimental details are well-described and understandable.  
- Question: Have you compared against Wang et al. (2015)/q-EI (e.g., MOE implementation), and can you clearly state differences?  
- Question: How does the method scale to larger parallelism (>>4 workers) and what gains are expected from increased parallelism?  
- Question: Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous execution like Spearmint?  
- Question: Provide wall-clock time comparisons and report computational overhead per batch versus random/sequential methods.  
- Question: Clarify what “iterations” on plot x-axes represent (function evaluations or batches) and whether initial design is included and why starting values differ.  
- Minor: Equation (4.1) seems to miss z^{(1:n)}; fix grammatical and citation phrasing; correct attribution that Wang et al. (2015) applied IPA rather than introduced it.
