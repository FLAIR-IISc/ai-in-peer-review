This paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient method, which is particularly useful when function evaluations are time-consuming. The authors introduce a new acquisition function, q-KG, that can output multiple new points for parallel evaluation, allowing for significant efficiency gains. They also provide an efficient approach to evaluate the q-KG function, making it a practical solution for large-scale optimization problems.

The problem addressed in this paper is important, as it enables the parallelization of Bayesian optimization, which can greatly improve the performance of data-driven applications. The idea of considering q knowledge gradients that propose q points to evaluate at each iteration is interesting and has the potential to accelerate the optimization process.

The authors provide a thorough background on Gaussian processes and Bayesian optimization, making the paper accessible to readers who may not be familiar with these topics. The mathematical derivations are clear and well-explained, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, it is unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is suitable for all problems, particularly those with high-dimensional input spaces, such as neural network architectures. Providing some theoretical results on the error bounds of the method and the quantitative analysis of the complexity with regard to dimension would strengthen the paper.
