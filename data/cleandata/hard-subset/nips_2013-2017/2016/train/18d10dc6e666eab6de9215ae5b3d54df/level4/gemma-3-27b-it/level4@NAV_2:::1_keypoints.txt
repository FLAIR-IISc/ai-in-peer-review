- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending the knowledge gradient to the parallel setting with a decision-theoretic derivation.  
- Proposes an efficient computational implementation using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Empirically outperforms existing batch BO methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear introduction and appropriate background on Gaussian processes and related work.  
- Derivation of q-KG and IPA-based computation is detailed and technically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, covering noise-free and noisy settings and multiple baselines.  
- Strong theoretical grounding: method is explicitly Bayes-optimal in formulation.  
- IPA-based computational approach is a significant practical contribution enabling efficient acquisition optimization.  
- Code availability enhances reproducibility and impact.  
- Lacks explicit analysis/reporting of parallel speed-ups achieved in practice.  
- Related work contains minor imprecisions (e.g., prior iterative batch EI, CL-mix, gradient-based parallel EI references).  
- Several technical clarifications requested: domain compactness and continuity of f; reason for using Latin Hypercube Sampling for initialization; GP hyperparameter re-estimation vs Bayesian updating in Algorithm 1.  
- Additional technical questions: whether 'g' is minimized or maximized in Section 5.2; justification of smoothness and derivative assumptions for 'g'; correct kernel notation is Matérn 5/2 (not 2/5).  
- Question about experimental reproducibility: whether GP fitting consistency was verified across different software implementations for Figure 3.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong decision-theoretic foundation, novel and efficient IPA computation, convincing empirical gains (esp. noisy), clear presentation, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing parallel speed-up analysis, minor related-work inaccuracies, and some technical details needing clarification.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper that would be strengthened by addressing the minor technical and literature clarifications.
