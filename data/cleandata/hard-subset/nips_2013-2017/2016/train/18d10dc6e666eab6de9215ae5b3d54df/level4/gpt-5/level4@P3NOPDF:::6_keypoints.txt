- Proposes q-KG acquisition for parallel batch Bayesian optimization with an IPA-based gradient estimator to enable scalable stochastic optimization.  
- Strength: tackles the practical setting of parallel BO under observation noise.  
- Strength: Knowledge Gradient gives a principled batch value-of-information metric.  
- Strength: IPA-based gradient estimator is technically sound and enables optimization of a challenging acquisition.  
- Strength: Paper is generally well written with clear technical background.  
- Major concern: No wall-clock time comparisons or analysis of computational overhead versus simpler baselines.  
- Major concern: Batch size fixed at q=4 in all experiments; no study of scaling with q or guidance on choosing q.  
- Major concern: Novelty relative to prior work is unclear—KG and IPA are known; relation to IPA-applied EI (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) needs clarification.  
- Major concern: Method appears synchronous only; lacks discussion/extension to asynchronous parallelization.  
- Major concern: Some experimental details and figure axes are unclear (e.g., definition of “iterations”, initial design inclusion).  
- Technical quality: Methodology and IPA optimization are well motivated; experiments include standard synthetic functions and two ML tasks.  
- Missing technical analysis: No reporting of computational cost per batch, number of Monte Carlo samples, variance of the gradient estimator, or optimization vs. evaluation time.  
- Missing baselines: Unclear whether comparisons include most relevant baselines (q-EI in MOE, Wang et al. 2015, Spearmint heuristic).  
- Novelty nuance: Main contribution appears to be formulating/optimizing KG for noisy batches but needs clearer articulation and direct empirical comparison to related methods.  
- Impact: Potentially significant if wall-clock competitiveness and scalability to larger q are demonstrated.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and self-contained, but some figures and minor writing issues need fixing.  
- Author questions: Did you compare to Wang et al. (2015) and q-EI (MOE), and can you clarify methodological differences and naming?  
- Author questions: How does performance and computational difficulty scale with q in terms of optimization, overhead, and results?  
- Author questions: Can q-KG be extended to asynchronous settings and how would the gradient estimator change?  
- Author questions: Provide wall-clock comparisons to random search and sequential BO and report batch computation overhead scaling with dimension, q, and pending points.  
- Author questions: Clarify what “iterations” means in plots and whether initial design points are included and why methods start at different values.  
- Minor comments: Fix equation notation, grammar, IPA attribution, justify q=4, and report MC sample counts, optimization tolerances, restarts, variance-reduction details.  
- Suggestion: Add an ablation comparing q-KG to q-EI and sequential KG across noise levels to isolate acquisition benefits.  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid and promising for noisy settings but needs clearer positioning vs. prior work, wall-clock and scalability evaluations, and experimental clarifications.
