- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm derived from decision-theoretic analysis.
- Defines the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) acquisition function as the expected incremental value (information gain) of evaluating q points.
- Shows q-KG is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when only one decision remains.
- Presents an efficient method to compute q-KG and its gradient using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Enables gradient-based optimization of the q-KG acquisition function for practical use.
- Experiments show q-KG outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with exploration in multiple settings.
- q-KG is especially effective on noisy function evaluation problems.
- Strength: clear decision-theoretic derivation and Bayes-optimality property.
- Strength: efficient IPA-based computation of acquisition and gradient.
- Strength: well-designed experiments demonstrating empirical superiority.
- Weakness: lacks clear comparison to prior batch KG work (e.g., cited [1], [2]).
- Weakness: Section 5.2 is underspecified and needs elaboration.
- Weakness: comparison to EI in noisy settings may be unfair since EI assumes noiseless observations.
- Overall assessments: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
