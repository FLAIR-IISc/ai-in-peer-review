- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function that selects the one-step Bayes-optimal batch of q points.
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective and develops an IPA-based gradient optimization to maximize the acquisition efficiently.
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNNs), often outperforming baselines, especially in noisy settings.
- Novelty/significance: principled, non-greedy joint batch selection versus existing greedy or heuristic batch methods.
- Technical quality: sound derivation and a practical, gradient-based maximization method suitable for continuous domains.
- Empirical evaluation: comprehensive comparisons with strong baselines (parallel EI, UCB) and convincing results.
- Weakness: Relies on Gaussian Processes, so scalability to high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural architecture search) is unclear.
- Weakness: Paper lacks theoretical analysis such as convergence guarantees or computational complexity bounds in d and q.
- Overall assessment: solid, well-executed contribution to batch BO; recommended for acceptance.
- Minor: awkward phrasing at l.36; suggest rephrasing for clarity.
- Minor: notation A is reused/confusing between overall domain and discretized inner minimization; recommend distinct notation (e.g., A_d).
- Minor: clarify that µ^(n) denotes the GP posterior mean when first used.
- Minor: when stating q-KG reduces to parallel EI, briefly define or describe parallel EI for completeness.
