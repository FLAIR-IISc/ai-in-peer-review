- Paper proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient method with a Monte‑Carlo gradient estimator and reports better performance than other batch BO methods.
- Novelty concern: batch KG is not new—prior works (e.g., [1],[2]) have explored this; the paper must clarify its contribution versus these.
- The main novel claim appears to be the gradient estimation method; authors should empirically or analytically compare its efficiency and effectiveness to prior batch KG approaches.
- Section 5.2 is central to the paper and needs further elaboration and detail to improve clarity and understanding.
- Experimental comparison with Expected Improvement may be unfair in noisy settings—clarify whether a noise-aware EI variant was used and discuss EI’s limitations if plain EI was used.
- The abbreviation “IPA” is redefined in the manuscript; clarify the distinction between the proposed method and the existing IPA method.
- Provide and discuss relevant related work (e.g., the cited [1],[2]) to situate contributions and differences more clearly.
