This paper explores the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on scenarios where function evaluations are computationally expensive. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, q-KG, which facilitates parallel evaluations by proposing multiple new points simultaneously. They also present an efficient approach for evaluating the q-KG function. Empirical results are provided to validate their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed in this paper is significant, as it enables the distribution of workload, thereby enhancing the performance of data-driven applications. By considering the q knowledge gradient, which suggests multiple points for evaluation in each iteration, the algorithm substantially improves efficiency. This concept is intriguing and warrants further exploration.

However, it is unclear whether the assumption of a Gaussian process is suitable, particularly in neural network architectures with high-dimensional inputs. Providing theoretical results on the error bounds of the proposed method and a quantitative analysis of its complexity with respect to dimension would be beneficial. Nevertheless, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization, with a well-designed method and sound experimental framework. The paper is well-written overall.
