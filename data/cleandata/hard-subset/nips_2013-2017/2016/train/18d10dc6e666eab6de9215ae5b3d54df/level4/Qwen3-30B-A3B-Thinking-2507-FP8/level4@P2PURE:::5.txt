

This paper introduces a batch variant of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) acquisition function, proposing a Monte-Carlo-based gradient estimation method to identify an optimal batch of design points. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior performance compared to existing batch Bayesian optimization (BO) approaches.  

1.  The batch KG framework is not novel in this work, as prior contributions exist (e.g., [1, 2]). The authors must explicitly clarify the specific contribution of this paper relative to these references. If the core advancement lies in the gradient estimation technique for batch optimization, the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method must be rigorously compared against the batch KG methods in [1] and [2] via experiments or mathematical analysis.  
2.  Section 5.2 requires substantial elaboration, as it constitutes the central technical contribution of the paper.  
3.  Expected Improvement (EI) assumes noiseless observations. Consequently, the comparison with EI under noisy settings in Section 6.2 is unjustified. The authors must specify whether a noise-aware variant of EI was employed. If plain EI was used, Section 6.2 must discuss the implications of this choice given EI's noise-agnostic nature.  
4.  The abbreviation "IPA" is redundantly redefined in line 162.
