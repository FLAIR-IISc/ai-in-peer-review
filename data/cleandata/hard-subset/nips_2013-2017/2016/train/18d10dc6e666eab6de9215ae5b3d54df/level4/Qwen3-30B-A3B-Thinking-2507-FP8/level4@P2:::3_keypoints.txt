- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending sequential Knowledge Gradient to jointly optimize q points for parallel evaluation.  
- Key technical contribution: an efficient gradient estimator for the q-KG acquisition using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to make optimization tractable.  
- Contrasts with prior parallel BO methods (parallel EI, GP-UCB) by optimizing the entire batch jointly rather than iteratively via single-point acquisition functions.  
- Theoretical development is sound with clear decision-theoretic justification (Bayes-optimal for minimizing simple regret).  
- Experimental validation is comprehensive: synthetic benchmarks (Branin, Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann) and ML tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10).  
- Results show q-KG matches or outperforms state-of-the-art, with notably larger gains in noisy settings (e.g., 11–14% test error improvement on CIFAR10).  
- Implementation details are thorough and code is provided on GitHub, improving reproducibility.  
- Paper clarity is strong: well-organized, carefully defined notation, and accessible exposition for BO researchers.  
- Originality is somewhat incremental (builds on established KG), but the IPA-based gradient estimation is a meaningful and non-trivial computational advance.  
- Significance is substantial for practical hyperparameter tuning in parallel compute environments, with potential to reduce time and resources for complex models.  
- Main weakness: some reviewers may view novelty as incremental; reviewer requests clarification of ambiguous notation in Eq. (3.1) for μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)).  
- Overall assessment: strong paper meeting NIPS quality standards; recommendation for acceptance.
