- Introduces parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending sequential KG to parallel evaluations.  
- Derives a decision-theoretic acquisition function that is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the expected minimum when one batch remains.  
- q-KG treats the batch collectively rather than selecting points iteratively, providing a more principled batch strategy.  
- Develops an efficient optimization strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis to compute gradients, avoiding costly Monte Carlo sampling.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance, consistently outperforming state-of-the-art batch BO methods on synthetic and real ML hyperparameter tasks.  
- Shows particular advantage in noisy evaluation settings; gains are larger with noise than in noise-free scenarios.  
- Experimental design is thorough, with comparisons to parallel EI, batch UCB, and variants, and results reported with mean and standard deviation.  
- Paper is technically sound with a rigorous theoretical foundation and clear mathematical formulations allowing reproducibility.  
- Well-written and organized, though discretization strategy (Section 5.3) could use more detail for clarity.  
- Original contribution: significant extension of KG to batch setting with a decision-theoretic basis distinguishing it from prior parallel EI/UCB methods.  
- Related work is cited appropriately, but the paper could more explicitly acknowledge earlier ideas about integrating acquisition functions over posteriors.  
- Demonstrates clear practical significance for parallel hyperparameter tuning and distributed evaluation scenarios.  
- Authors acknowledge computational complexity and limitations, and focus benefits primarily in noisy settings.  
- Pros: theoretically grounded method, novel IPA-based computational approach, and convincing empirical results.  
- Cons: lacks explicit analysis of parallel speed-up (wall-clock or resource-based), some smoothness/gradient assumptions need stronger justification, and the choice of initial Latin hypercube design is not well motivated.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper suitable for acceptance with minor revisions to address technical justifications and literature/contextual clarifications.
