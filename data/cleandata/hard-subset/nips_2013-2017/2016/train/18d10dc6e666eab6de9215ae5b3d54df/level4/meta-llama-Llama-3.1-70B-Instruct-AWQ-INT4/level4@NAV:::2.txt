The paper proposes a novel parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches, offering a promising solution for machine learning tasks such as hyperparameter optimization. The authors demonstrate the superiority of their method over existing Bayesian optimization approaches, and the paper is well-organized and easy to comprehend. The parallelization of Bayesian optimization is a crucial topic for practical hyperparameter optimization, and the proposed approach is both interesting and more elegant than most existing methods. The determination of a Bayes-optimal batch is particularly noteworthy. 

However, the authors' assumption of independent normally distributed errors, although common in most Gaussian process-based Bayesian optimization methods, may be problematic in hyperparameter optimization due to the potential for structural bias in certain regions of the hyperparameter space, especially with small sample sizes. This assumption of independence may not always be realistic and could be worth mentioning.

The paper's theoretical contribution is strong, but the experimental evaluation falls short in several aspects. Firstly, the use of test set error as an optimization criterion is suboptimal for hyperparameter tuning, as it introduces issues with model selection and hyperparameter optimization. More suitable metrics, such as area under the ROC curve or log loss, would be more effective.

Furthermore, the experimental benchmark is limited to the MNIST and CIFAR10 datasets, and it would be beneficial to include benchmarks from HPOlib, a widely-used library specifically designed for benchmarking hyperparameter optimization algorithms. Additionally, the benchmark consists of only two datasets and a single machine learning task (classification) with two learning algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), which is insufficient to demonstrate the robustness of the proposed method. A more comprehensive benchmark with a variety of datasets, tasks, and learning algorithms would be more convincing.

In summary, while the paper's theoretical contribution is valuable, the experimental evaluation is limited, and a more extensive benchmark is necessary to fully demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm.
