- Proposes a Bayesian stochastic gradient algorithm for optimizing multivariate objectives over finite domains.  
- Key innovation: parallelizable/batch evaluation by selecting sets of points each iteration for simultaneous computation.  
- Main challenge: optimal selection of sampling points in a batch setting.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected improvement and guide sampling decisions.  
- Generalizes KG to operate on batches (q-KG).  
- q-KG is computationally intractable in general; authors propose Monte Carlo estimation plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients efficiently.  
- Empirical results on test problems show performance comparable to or better than existing methods, particularly confidence-bound-based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and likely useful in practice.  
- Reviewer questions the level of novelty: extension to batch KG is incremental, and IPA-based gradient estimation is relatively standard.  
- Specific technical issue: equation (3.1) unclear—definitions of μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) on sequences are not explained.
