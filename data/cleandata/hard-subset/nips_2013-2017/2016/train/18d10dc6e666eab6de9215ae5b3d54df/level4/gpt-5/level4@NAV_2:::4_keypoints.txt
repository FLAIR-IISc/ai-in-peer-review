- Proposes a parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization with an efficient computation/optimization procedure.  
- Selects multiple points per iteration to enable concurrent evaluations and accelerate expensive function optimization.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and hyperparameter-tuning tasks show strong performance, especially under observation noise, outperforming parallel EI and UCB variants.  
- Addressing parallel evaluations is important and timely for speeding data-driven workflows compared with strictly sequential designs.  
- Concern: Gaussian process priors may be inappropriate in high-dimensional settings like neural network hyperparameter spaces.  
- Suggestion: Add theoretical analysis—error bounds and complexity guarantees with respect to dimensionality to clarify scalability and performance regimes.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution, sound methodology, careful and convincing experiments, and clear writing.
