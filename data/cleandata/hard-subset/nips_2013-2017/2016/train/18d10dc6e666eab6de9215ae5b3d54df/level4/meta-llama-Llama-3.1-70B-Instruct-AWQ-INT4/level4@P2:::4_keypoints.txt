- Proposes the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- q-KG outputs multiple points per iteration to enable parallel evaluations and speed up optimization.  
- Introduces an efficient method to evaluate the q-KG function.  
- Empirical results show the proposed method is effective.  
- Addresses an important problem: parallelizing evaluations can significantly improve performance in data-driven applications.  
- The idea of using q knowledge gradients to propose multiple points is novel and useful for acceleration.  
- Paper is well-written and presents sound experiments, constituting a solid contribution.  
- Unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is suitable for high-dimensional input spaces (e.g., neural network architectures).  
- Lacks theoretical results on error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to dimension.  
- Requests minor clarifications and notation fixes (rephrase line 36; define set A at line 95; specify input of mu^n at line 104; discuss parallel EI at line 128; use distinct notation for A at line 140).  
- Strengths: novel acquisition function, efficient evaluation approach, strong empirical validation, solid contribution.  
- Weaknesses: unclear GP suitability in high dimensions, missing theoretical bounds and complexity analysis, minor presentation/notation issues.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the points above.
