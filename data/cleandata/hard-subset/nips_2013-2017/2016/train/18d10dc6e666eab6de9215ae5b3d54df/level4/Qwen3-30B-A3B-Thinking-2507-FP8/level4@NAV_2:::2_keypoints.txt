- Introduces q-KG, a Bayes-optimal parallel BO algorithm that jointly selects evaluation batches, extending sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel setting.  
- Uses Gaussian process modeling with gradient estimation via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to address computational challenges in parallel BO.  
- Empirically outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Originality: provides a principled joint-batch acquisition distinct from greedy iterative methods.  
- Quality: rigorous theoretical derivation, validated IPA gradient computation, and reduction to parallel EI in noise-free cases.  
- Clarity: well-written with clear exposition, detailed algorithm (Algorithm 1), reproducible experimental setup, and available code.  
- Significance: addresses scalable hyperparameter optimization needs; shows practical gains in noisy settings.  
- Weakness — Experimental scope: limited benchmarks (MNIST, CIFAR10; logistic regression and CNN); broader validation (e.g., HPOlib, varied tasks) warranted.  
- Weakness — Evaluation metrics: optimizing test set error is suboptimal for HPO; metrics like log loss or AUC are preferable.  
- Weakness — Noise assumption: assumes independent measurement errors, which may not hold for cross-validation–induced correlated noise in HPO; needs discussion.  
- Overall: technically sound and impactful contribution with strong theory and experiments; accept, but recommend expanding empirical validation.
