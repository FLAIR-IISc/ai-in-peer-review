This paper introduces a stochastic-gradient-based algorithm within a Bayesian optimization framework for maximizing a multivariate objective over a finite domain. The key algorithmic feature is the selection, at each iteration, of multiple evaluation points to enable parallel objective computation. The central challenge is to choose these sampling points optimally. To address this, the paper adopts the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework, which quantifies expected improvement from additional samples, and extends it to a batch setting that selects multiple points at once.

Because the resulting optimization over batches is computationally intractable, the authors develop a Monte Carlo method combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the batch KG (q-KG) objective, enabling stochastic gradient optimization of information gain across candidate batches. Empirical results on several benchmarks indicate that the method often outperforms or is highly competitive with approaches based on confidence bounds.

The paper is clearly written and the approach appears useful for practical, parallel evaluation settings. However, the level of novelty seems modest. The primary contribution is an extension of the established KG methodology to batch selection; while this necessitates a more sophisticated optimization procedure over sets of points, the core idea is incremental relative to prior KG work. Moreover, the main technical component—estimating the q-KG gradient via IPA—is itself fairly standard.
