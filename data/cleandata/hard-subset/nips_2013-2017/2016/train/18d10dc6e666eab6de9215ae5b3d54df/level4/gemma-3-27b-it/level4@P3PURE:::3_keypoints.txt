- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm that combines Bayesian modeling to optimize multivariate objectives over finite domains.
- Key innovation: parallelizable objective evaluation by selecting a set of points each iteration.
- Central challenge: optimally selecting sampling points, addressed using the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework.
- KG is used to estimate expected improvement and guide whether and which points to evaluate.
- Generalizes KG to a batch (q-KG) setting to choose multiple sampling points simultaneously.
- Recognizes computational intractability and develops a Monte Carlo + Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) method to estimate q-KG gradients efficiently.
- Empirical results show the method outperforms or competes with existing approaches, especially confidence-bound–based methods.
- Paper is well-written and likely useful in practice.
- Novelty is incremental; primary contribution is the IPA-based q-KG gradient estimation, which is a relatively standard technique.
- Specific concern: notation in Eq. (3.1) is unclear—μ and K are defined on vectors, so μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) need clarification.
