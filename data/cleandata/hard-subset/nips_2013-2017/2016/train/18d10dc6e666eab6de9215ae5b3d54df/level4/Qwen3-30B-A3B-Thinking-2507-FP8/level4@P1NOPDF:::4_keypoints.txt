- Proposes q-KG, a novel acquisition function for batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization with an efficient method to evaluate it.  
- Empirical results support the theoretical claims and show substantial efficiency gains over sequential methods for parallel evaluations.  
- Problem is significant: enabling distributed/parallel evaluations is important for scaling data-driven applications.  
- Concern: reliance on Gaussian processes may not be robust or scalable in high-dimensional settings (e.g., neural network training); authors should clarify robustness or justify the GP assumption.  
- Missing theoretical guarantees: no error bounds for the algorithm are provided.  
- Missing computational analysis: no quantitative complexity analysis relative to input dimensionality to assess scalability.  
- Methodologically sound and well-written; makes a solid contribution to batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Suggestion: include a brief comparison with parallel Expected Improvement (parallel EI) as a baseline.  
- Notation/clarity issues to fix: grammatical fix at Line 36; clarify definition of set A (Line 95) and input to μ^n (Line 104); use distinct notation for A (e.g., 𝒜) to avoid confusion (Line 140).  
- Overall verdict: strong submission that advances batch BO, but would be strengthened by addressing theoretical gaps and notational clarity.
