- Proposes q-KG, a novel acquisition function to select a batch of q points for parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Provides a stochastic gradient-based algorithm to efficiently optimize the non-trivial q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical benchmarks show q-KG outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization strategies.  
- Tackles the practical problem of reducing wall-clock time via parallel evaluations.  
- q-KG is a principled extension of the sequential Knowledge Gradient, promoting diverse, jointly informative selections.  
- Method is technically sound and the paper is generally well-written with clear presentation of core ideas.  
- Reliance on Gaussian Processes may limit applicability in very high-dimensional settings (e.g., tuning large neural networks).  
- Paper lacks formal theoretical analysis (e.g., convergence guarantees or error bounds) and quantitative complexity results.  
- Needs discussion on GP scalability and behavior of q-KG with increasing input dimensionality.  
- Should include computational complexity analysis, especially dependence on batch size q and input dimension.  
- Minor issues: incomplete sentence at l.36; undefined set A at l.95; unspecified input for posterior mean at l.104; suggest discussing alternative parallel acquisition functions at l.128; reuse of notation A at l.140 is ambiguous.  
- Overall: a solid, novel, and empirically effective contribution to parallel Bayesian optimization despite gaps in theory and scalability discussion.
