- Proposes computing Bayes-optimal parallel batches via q-KG and maximizes q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Experiments show competitive performance on noiseless benchmarks and superior results on noisy problems.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks wall-clock time analysis and comparisons to random search and sequential Bayesian optimization.  
- The fixed choice q=4 is unexplained; no experiments evaluate larger batch sizes or scalability.  
- Novelty is unclear: IPA with parallel acquisition resembles prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015), and the paper fails to clearly distinguish or credit that prior approach.  
- Potential impact is high for real-world parallel hyperparameter tuning, but depends on demonstrated scalability and efficiency.  
- Technical exposition and methodology are well-explained, but practical contribution is weakened by missing time-to-solution metrics.  
- Ask why Wang et al. (2015) q-EI (using IPA) was not compared or explicitly contrasted with q-KG.  
- Ask for analysis of scalability ceiling and how performance degrades or improves as q increases beyond 4.  
- Ask whether the synchronous method can be adapted to asynchronous evaluations for heterogeneous/unreliable compute.  
- Request wall-clock comparisons to random search and sequential BO, and reporting of computational overhead for batch generation.  
- Request clarification of plot x-axis (“iterations” = evaluations or batches?) and whether initial design is included and consistent across methods.  
- Minor: Equation (4.1) may omit a term (z^{(1:n)}).  
- Minor: several wording/grammar and citation phrasing fixes suggested.  
- Minor: correct attribution for IPA — Wang et al. applied IPA but did not introduce it; cite original IPA source (e.g., Glynn & Whitt, 1989).  
- Summary: q-KG+IPA is reasonable, but lack of key comparisons, scalability analysis for larger q, and wall-clock evidence leave practical significance and novelty unclear.
