- Introduces q-KG, a novel parallel Bayesian optimization method computing Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong with clear explanations.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited: only MNIST and CIFAR10 datasets used.  
- Experiments cover a single task (classification) and only two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN).  
- Using test-set error as the optimization criterion is problematic for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Recommend using more robust metrics (e.g., AUC or log loss) instead of test-set error.  
- The common GP assumption of independent normally distributed observation noise may be unrealistic in HPO.  
- Structural bias in measurement errors (especially with small samples) should be acknowledged.  
- More diverse datasets and learning tasks are needed to convincingly demonstrate generality.  
- Overall positive: novel, promising approach with solid theory, but limited experiments weaken significance.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
