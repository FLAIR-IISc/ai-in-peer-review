This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) policy for Bayesian optimization. To find an optimal batch of design points, the authors propose a gradient estimation method based on Monte-Carlo simulation. The experiments show that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

1.  The concept of a batch Knowledge Gradient policy is not novel to this work. The authors should clarify the specific contributions of this paper in relation to prior work on batch KG, such as [1] and [2]. It appears the primary contribution is the proposed gradient estimation technique for optimizing the batch selection. If this is the case, the efficiency and effectiveness of this optimization method should be benchmarked against the approaches used in previous batch KG papers, either through direct experimental comparison or mathematical analysis.

2.  Section 5.2, which appears to contain the core technical contribution of the paper, requires further elaboration to improve clarity.

3.  The standard Expected Improvement (EI) acquisition function is designed for noiseless observations. Consequently, comparing the proposed method against standard EI in a noisy setting may be an unfair benchmark. The authors should clarify whether a noise-aware variant of EI was used in the experiments. If the standard, noiseless version of EI was used, the discussion of the results in Section 6.2 should acknowledge this mismatch and consider that EI's formulation does not account for observation noise.

4.  A minor point: the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162.
