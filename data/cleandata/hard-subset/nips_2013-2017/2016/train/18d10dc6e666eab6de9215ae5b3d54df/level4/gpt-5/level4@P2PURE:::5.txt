This paper derives a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG). To select an optimal batch of design points, the authors propose a gradient estimation method based on Monte Carlo simulation. The experiments indicate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

1. To my knowledge, batch KG is not introduced for the first time in this paper. Please clarify the paper’s contribution relative to prior batch KG work such as [1] and [2]. In my view, the main contribution here is the gradient estimator used to optimize the batch. If so, the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed approach should be compared against prior batch KG methods, either empirically or, if possible, via mathematical analysis.

2. Section 5.2 should be expanded, as it appears to be the key technical component of the paper.

3. EI generally assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, comparisons in a noisy setting may be unfair. Was a noise-aware variant of EI used? If plain EI was used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed with the explicit acknowledgement that EI does not account for observation noise.

4. On line 162, the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined.
