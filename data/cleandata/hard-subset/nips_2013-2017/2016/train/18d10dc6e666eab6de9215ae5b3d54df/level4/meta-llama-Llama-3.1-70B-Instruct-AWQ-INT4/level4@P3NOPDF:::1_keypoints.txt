- Proposes a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion for Bayesian optimization and details its computation.  
- Introduces a parallel variant q-KG and provides numerical experiments showing it outperforms some state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Paper is well-structured with thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes.  
- Contribution is significant: parallelizing Bayesian optimization has substantial potential impact and justifies publication.  
- Missing analysis of wall-clock speed-ups when moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Literature review contains imprecise statements—prior work on integrating EI over posteriors, Constant Liar, CL-mix, and multipoint EI differentiation should be cited/acknowledged.  
- Unclear mathematical assumptions: is domain A compact and is f continuous? these should be stated.  
- Rationale for restricting A to an initial Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) is not explained.  
- Algorithm 1 omits discussion of hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating during optimization.  
- In Section 5.2 the definition of g is ambiguous (max vs min) and its smoothness assumption is not justified.  
- Derivation/assumption about the derivative of the Cholesky factor needs further clarification.  
- Possible typo: Matérn kernel parameter listed as 2/5 likely should be 5/2.  
- Experiments: clarify whether identical Gaussian process fits were reproducible across different software implementations to rule out implementation dependence.
