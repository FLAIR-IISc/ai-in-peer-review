- Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch acquisition function for one-step Bayes-optimal selection of q points in parallel Bayesian optimization.
- Derives an unbiased stochastic gradient for q-KG using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA), enabling gradient-based optimization in q*d dimensions.
- Addresses computational challenges of optimizing the acquisition over a high-dimensional batch space and provides an efficient solver.
- Empirically evaluated on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR-10).
- Demonstrates superior performance over state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy evaluation settings.
- Builds on the sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework and extends it to the parallel setting, contrasting with EI/UCB-based approaches.
- Well-written, clearly described method with open-source code for reproducibility.
- Lacks theoretical analysis (convergence or regret bounds) and formal complexity characterization in q and d.
- Inherits GP limitations: cubic scaling with observations and potential degradation in very high-dimensional search spaces.
- Suggests clearer notation and phrasing: reword awkward sentences, restate feasible domain A in Section 4, clarify µ^(n) inputs, and distinguish continuous domain A from discretized set (e.g., A_disc).
- Recommends briefly reminding readers how parallel EI is typically implemented (greedy/constant liar) when making comparisons.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practically impactful contribution with empirical strengths outweighing theoretical and scalability limitations.
