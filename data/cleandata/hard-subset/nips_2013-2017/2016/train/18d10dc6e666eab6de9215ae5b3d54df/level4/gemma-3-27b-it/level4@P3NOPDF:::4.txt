This paper presents a parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian Optimization, addressing the challenge of expensive function evaluations. The authors propose a novel acquisition function, q-KG, designed to suggest multiple points for parallel evaluation, coupled with an efficient evaluation approach for this function. The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of their method and align with the theoretical findings. 

The problem addressed is both important and practically relevant. Distributing the evaluation workload is crucial for scaling data-driven applications, and moving beyond sequential, single-point evaluation promises significant efficiency gains. The core idea of considering *q* potential evaluation points per iteration – effectively leveraging *q* knowledge gradients – is compelling and a worthwhile exploration within Bayesian Optimization.

The proposed q-KG method appears sound, and the experimental validation is well-executed. However, the reliance on a Gaussian Process (GP) prior warrants further discussion. While GPs are common in Bayesian Optimization, their suitability can diminish when applied to high-dimensional input spaces typical of many neural network architectures. Investigating the method’s performance, or limitations, in such scenarios would strengthen the paper. 

Further, while the empirical results are encouraging, the inclusion of theoretical error bounds for the proposed method would provide a more rigorous understanding of its performance characteristics. A quantitative analysis of the algorithm's computational complexity, particularly with respect to input dimension (*d*), would also be valuable. Such analysis could illuminate scalability concerns and guide future algorithmic refinements.

Specifically, I have the following minor points and requests for clarification:

*   **Line 36:** The phrasing "the set of points to evaluate next that is" is redundant. Suggestion: "the set of points to evaluate next."
*   **Line 95:** The definition of matrix *A* is missing. Please provide this definition for clarity.
*   **Line 104:** Clarify the input to μ<sup>n</sup>. What does this represent, and what is its dimensionality?
*   **Line 128:** A brief discussion of the parallel Expected Improvement (EI) algorithm, as a related approach, would provide useful context and help highlight the advantages of q-KG.
*   **Line 140:** Consider using alternative notation for matrix *A* to avoid potential confusion with other uses of *A* in the paper or related literature.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The method is well-motivated, the experimental results are convincing, and the paper is generally well-written. Addressing the concerns regarding the GP assumption, theoretical guarantees, and computational complexity, along with the minor clarifications requested, would significantly enhance the paper’s impact. I recommend acceptance, pending minor revisions.
