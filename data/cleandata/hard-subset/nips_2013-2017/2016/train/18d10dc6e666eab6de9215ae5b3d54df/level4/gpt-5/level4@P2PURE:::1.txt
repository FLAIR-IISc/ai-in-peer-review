This paper addresses batch-sequential Bayesian optimization and proposes a batch-sequential version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) criterion. Following a concise review of related work and Gaussian processes, the authors define the parallel (q-KG) criterion. Its computation is developed analogously to the standard KG criterion, and the paper presents a series of numerical experiments in which q-KG appears to outperform several state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian optimization methods.

Overall, this is a very strong paper with qualities that, in my view, warrant acceptance at NIPS. The potential impact on society of parallelizing Bayesian optimization algorithms is paramount.

I have a few minor critiques. First, the paper does not study speed-ups achieved when moving from sequential to batch-sequential settings. Second, some statements in the literature review are slightly imprecise. For example, integrating Expected Improvement (EI) with respect to posterior distributions was already discussed in “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” (together with Constant Liar), and Chevalier et al. notably introduced CL-mix in [2]. In addition, maximization of q-EI using the natural gradient is treated in “Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design.”

Additional remarks and questions:
- Are the assumptions that A is compact and f is continuous in place?
- Why restrict A to a Latin hypercube sample (LHS) at initialization?
- In Algorithm 1, how are model hyperparameters handled—do you re-estimate them (or perform Bayesian updating) during the process?
- In Section 5.2, is the definition of g based on a maximization or a minimization?
- Still in Section 5.2, the smoothness of g does not seem immediate; could you clarify the required conditions?
- The derivative of the Cholesky factor is also non-obvious; some explanation or a reference would help.
- I believe the Matérn kernel is ν = 5/2, not 2/5.
- Regarding Figure 1 and the associated experiments: was it possible to fit the same Gaussian processes using different software packages?
