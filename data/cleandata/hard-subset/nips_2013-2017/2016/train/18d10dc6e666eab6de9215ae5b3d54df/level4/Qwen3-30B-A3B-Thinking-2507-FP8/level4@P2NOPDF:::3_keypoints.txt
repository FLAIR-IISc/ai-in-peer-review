- Introduces a stochastic gradient algorithm combining Bayesian modeling for optimizing multivariate objectives over finite domains.  
- Core innovation: selects multiple points for parallel evaluation each iteration via a batch extension of the Knowledge Gradient (KG).  
- Generalizes KG to identify an optimal set of simultaneous samples that maximize expected information gain.  
- Addresses batch optimization intractability using Monte Carlo sampling and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the q-KG criterion.  
- Enables scalable optimization over candidate point sets without exhaustive enumeration.  
- Empirical evaluation on benchmarks shows competitive or superior performance, especially against GP-UCB and other confidence-bound methods.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly explains technical contributions, and provides thorough implementation and experimental details aiding reproducibility.  
- Novelty is modest: builds on well-studied KG framework and uses established IPA techniques rather than offering a fundamentally new theoretical insight.  
- Theoretical contribution is incremental, with the main advance being an effective adaptation of existing tools to the batch setting.  
- Technical concern: notation in Equation (3.1) is ambiguous—μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) need clarification whether they are element-wise vectors/matrices or aggregated statistics.  
- Overall assessment: practical, well-executed extension of KG for batch Bayesian optimization with strong empirical support; suitable for publication after minor revisions to clarify notation.
