- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches, showing strong theoretical contribution.  
- Paper is well-written and theoretically sound; determination of Bayes-optimal batches is a promising advancement.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (common in GP-based BO), which may be an unrealistic simplification for hyperparameter optimization.  
- Measurement errors in HPO often reflect generalization-vs-empirical discrepancies (e.g., cross-validation), so the independence assumption should be acknowledged as potentially invalid.  
- Structural bias in hyperparameter space, especially with small samples, further undermines the independence/error assumptions.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited: using test set error as the optimization criterion is suboptimal and can introduce known issues; metrics like AUC or log loss are preferable.  
- Benchmarking is narrow: only MNIST and CIFAR-10 are used instead of standard HPO libraries like HPOlib for broader comparison.  
- Scope of experiments is too small—only two datasets, two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), and a single task (classification)—limiting claims of broad practical superiority.  
- Optimizer performance often varies across algorithm families, so the narrow experimental scope weakens generalizability claims.  
- Recommendation: augment experiments with diverse datasets, tasks, models, and metrics to substantiate practical impact; current evaluation does not convincingly demonstrate real-world applicability.
