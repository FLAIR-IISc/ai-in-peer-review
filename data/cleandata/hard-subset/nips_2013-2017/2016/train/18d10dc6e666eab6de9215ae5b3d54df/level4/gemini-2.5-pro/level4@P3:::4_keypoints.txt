- Introduces q-KG, a one-step Bayes-optimal acquisition function for batch Bayesian Optimization.  
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective to select the batch that maximally improves final solution quality after one parallel step.  
- Uses Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to obtain an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator for q-KG.  
- Enables efficient gradient-based optimization of the otherwise intractable q-KG acquisition function.  
- Generalizes sequential Knowledge Gradient to the parallel setting and jointly optimizes entire batches rather than using greedy heuristics.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic benchmarks and real ML hyperparameter tasks, especially in noisy settings and the CIFAR-10 CNN tuning.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and reproducible (code provided).  
- Method inherits Gaussian Process (GP) limitations and may not scale well to high-dimensional input spaces (experiments up to 8 dimensions).  
- Lacks theoretical long-term guarantees (e.g., cumulative regret bounds).  
- Computationally intensive: relies on Monte Carlo sampling, Cholesky decompositions, and inner gradient-based optimization loops.  
- Needs more explicit discussion of computational complexity relative to batch size q, number of Monte Carlo samples, and discretization size M.  
- Suggested clarifications: rephrase awkward sentence at l.36 and distinguish notation for continuous domain A vs. discretized set (e.g., A_n or A_d).  
- Suggested exposition: emphasize greedy nature of parallel EI when comparing methods.
