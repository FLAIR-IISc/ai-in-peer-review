This paper presents a batch implementation of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm, employing a Monte Carlo-based gradient estimation technique to identify optimal batches of design points. Experimental results demonstrate competitive performance against existing batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods. 

1. The claim of novelty regarding the batch KG approach requires clarification. Prior work, such as [1] and [2], has already explored batch KG formulations. The paper’s primary contribution appears to be the proposed gradient estimation method for batch selection. If this is the case, a comparative analysis – either experimental or analytical – against these existing batch KG methods is necessary to demonstrate the advantages of the proposed approach.

2. Section 5.2, detailing the gradient estimation process, is central to the paper’s contribution and should be expanded upon for greater clarity and detail.

3. The comparison presented in Section 6.2, utilizing Expected Improvement (EI) in a noisy environment, may be biased as EI fundamentally assumes noiseless observations. Please clarify whether a noise-aware variant of EI was employed. If the standard EI was used, the results should be discussed acknowledging this limitation and the impact of EI’s noiseless assumption.

4. The abbreviation “IPA” is redefined in line 162, which is inconsistent.
