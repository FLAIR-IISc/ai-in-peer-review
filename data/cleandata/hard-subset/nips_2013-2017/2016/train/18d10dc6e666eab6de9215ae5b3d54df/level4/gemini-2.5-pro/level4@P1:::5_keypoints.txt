- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization method using Knowledge Gradient to select q points maximizing one-step-ahead expected solution quality.
- Main technical contribution: a computationally efficient Monte Carlo estimator for q-KG and an unbiased gradient estimator via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).
- Enables gradient-based optimization of the q-KG acquisition function, avoiding prohibitive naive optimization.
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, covering synthetic benchmarks and real-world hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNN).
- q-KG shows strong performance, especially in noisy function-evaluation settings.
- Well-motivated practical problem (parallel Bayesian optimization) and clear decision-theoretic derivation aiming for Bayes-optimality for one remaining batch.
- Need clearer comparison to prior batch KG work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015 / Peng 2010); direct theoretical or empirical comparison would clarify novelty and efficiency gains.
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) is too condensed; key derivation details and intuition should be moved into the main paper for clarity.
- Unclear whether baseline parallel EI was noise-aware; experiments may be unfair if a noise-free EI variant was used—authors should clarify and contextualize results.
- Minor issue: "IPA" is redundantly defined twice in the paper.
- Overall: strong, practical contribution with promising results, but would be strengthened by direct comparison to prior work and clearer presentation of the gradient estimator and experimental fairness.
