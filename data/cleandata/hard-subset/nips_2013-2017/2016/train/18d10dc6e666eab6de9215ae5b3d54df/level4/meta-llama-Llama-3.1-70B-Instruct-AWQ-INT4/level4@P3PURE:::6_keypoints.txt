- Paper proposes q-KG (knowledge gradient for batches) with IPA-based gradient estimation for parallel batch Bayesian optimization and reports competitive/noise-robust performance.  
- Reviewer appreciates parallel HPO motivation but finds some aspects unclear and experimental evidence unconvincing.  
- Technical: Experiments use standard benchmarks and CNN/logistic tasks but lack wall-clock time or analysis of parallel gains versus random/sequential search.  
- Concern about why batch size q is fixed to 4 in all experiments.  
- Novelty: Using KG and IPA is not new; prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) already applies IPA for batch EI—needs clearer differentiation.  
- Impact: Parallel hyperparameter tuning is broadly useful across ML, vision, biology, robotics.  
- Clarity: Technical background and experimental descriptions are well written and adequate.  
- Q1: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015)/parallel EI (q-EI) and clearly distinguished it from Spearmint’s parallel method?  
- Q2: How does the method scale to larger q (dozens/hundreds) and what are expected gains from more parallel runs?  
- Q3: Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous execution like Spearmint?  
- Q4: Have you measured wall-clock time, overhead per batch, and comparisons to random or sequential BO in real time?  
- Q5: Clarify the x-axis “iterations”: is it function evaluations or batches, does it include the initial design, and why do methods not start at the same value?  
- Minor: Possible missing z^(1:n) in equation (4.1).  
- Minor: Grammar/usage fixes (e.g., “achieves”; parenthetical references should be nouns).  
- Minor: Correctly attribute IPA—Wang et al. (2015) applied IPA rather than introduced it.
