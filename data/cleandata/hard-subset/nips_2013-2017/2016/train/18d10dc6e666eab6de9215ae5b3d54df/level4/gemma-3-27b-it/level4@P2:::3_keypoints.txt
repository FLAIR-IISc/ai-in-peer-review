- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending Knowledge Gradient for parallel evaluations.  
- Addresses computational intractability of batch selection using Monte Carlo sampling and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) for gradient estimation.  
- Empirically outperforms or matches state-of-the-art methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Grounded in a sound decision-theoretic Knowledge Gradient framework.  
- Practical and efficient implementation enabling application in higher-dimensional, real-world problems.  
- Strong empirical results demonstrating consistent improvements in noisy settings.  
- Clear, well-structured presentation of methodology and experiments.  
- Code is provided, supporting reproducibility and further research.  
- Novelty is incremental—mainly an adaptation of KG to batch settings with IPA-based gradients rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Practical implementation depends on discretizing the search space, introducing approximation and potential sensitivity to the discretization parameter M.  
- Limited theoretical analysis—lacks detailed convergence or regret bounds.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-implemented, convincing empirical gains, and useful for practitioners with parallelizable expensive evaluations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution, discretization reliance, and need for stronger theoretical guarantees.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with notable practical impact; recommend acceptance with a minor clarification requested on how µ and K are applied to sequences in equation (3.1).
