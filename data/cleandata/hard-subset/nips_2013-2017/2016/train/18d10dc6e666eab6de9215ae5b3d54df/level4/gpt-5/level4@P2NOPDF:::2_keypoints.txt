- Proposes a conceptually elegant parallel BO algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and shows improvements over prior methods.  
- Assumes independent Gaussian observation noise, which may be unrealistic in HPO where noise reflects generalization discrepancies and can be structurally biased; authors should discuss this assumption and its impact.  
- Using test-set error as the optimization criterion is problematic because threshold metrics can obscure model differences; more informative metrics (e.g., AUC, log loss) are recommended.  
- Experiments are limited to MNIST and CIFAR-10; evaluation would be stronger using broader, standardized benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) for fair, reproducible comparison.  
- Benchmark scope is narrow (two datasets, one task, two algorithms); needs more datasets, tasks (e.g., regression), and model classes to validate practical benefits.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution and promising method, but empirical evidence is currently too limited—improve experiments with better metrics, broader benchmarks, and more diverse tasks/models.
