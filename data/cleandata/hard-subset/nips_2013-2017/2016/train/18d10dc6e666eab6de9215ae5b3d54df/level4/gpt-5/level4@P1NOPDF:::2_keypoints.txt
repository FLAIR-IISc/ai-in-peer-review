- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and appears more elegant than existing methods.  
- Paper is well written and easy to follow.  
- Determining Bayes-optimal batches is a particularly promising contribution.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors, which may be unrealistic in hyperparameter optimization due to structural bias and correlated measurement errors.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and not convincing.  
- Using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; threshold-based metrics are suboptimal—prefer AUC or log loss.  
- Experiments use only MNIST and CIFAR-10; should include broader HPO benchmarks such as HPOlib.  
- Benchmark is narrow: only two datasets, only classification tasks, and only two learning algorithms (logistic regression and a CNN); needs more variety.  
- Overall: theory is valuable but empirical validation is too limited to be fully convincing.
