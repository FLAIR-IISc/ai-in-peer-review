- Proposes a batch-selection variant of a stochastic, gradient-based Bayesian optimization method for multivariate objectives over finite domains.  
- Key feature: selects a batch of points each iteration to enable parallel evaluations.  
- Main challenge addressed: optimally choosing sampling locations; extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to jointly select multiple points (q-KG).  
- Computational issue: direct optimization of the batched KG criterion is prohibitive.  
- Solution: combine Monte Carlo estimation with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to obtain gradients for maximizing information gain over candidate batches.  
- Empirical results: tested on benchmarks and practical tasks, outperforming or remaining competitive with existing methods, especially UCB-based approaches.  
- Assessment: paper is clearly written and practically useful, but conceptual novelty is moderate—chief contribution is applying IPA to q-KG gradient estimation, seen as a standard technique.  
- Specific suggestion: clarify notation in Eq. (3.1) for µ(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n), x(1:n))—explicitly indicate vectorization of µ and construction of the Gram matrix for K.
