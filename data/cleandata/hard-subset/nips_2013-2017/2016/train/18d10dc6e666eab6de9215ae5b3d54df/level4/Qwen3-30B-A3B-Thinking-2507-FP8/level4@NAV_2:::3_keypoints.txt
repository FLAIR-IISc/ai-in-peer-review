- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending the sequential knowledge gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- q-KG is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the predictor minimum when one batch remains.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Optimizes the batch jointly rather than constructing it iteratively, distinguishing it from many prior parallel methods.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over state-of-the-art on synthetic functions and ML tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN), especially in noisy settings.  
- Code is publicly available and the paper provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical foundation and experimental validation are strong, demonstrating high technical quality.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents results effectively.  
- Novelty is incremental: extends an existing sequential framework to batch settings rather than introducing a fundamentally new concept.  
- The paper should better differentiate itself from closely related joint-parallel acquisition work (e.g., [2,15,25]).  
- Technical issue noted in Eq. (3.1) concerning definitions of μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) for sequences vs. vectors.  
- Method is practically significant for parallel evaluations and hyperparameter tuning, particularly under noise.  
- Argument for acceptance: well-executed extension with clear empirical advantages and efficient IPA-based computation.  
- Argument against acceptance: contribution may be viewed as primarily computational/implementation-focused and not highly novel.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with practical value; reviewer recommends acceptance.
