- Introduces q-KG, a novel acquisition function for batch (q) Bayesian Optimization with an optimization strategy for parallel evaluations.  
- Targets expensive function evaluations by leveraging parallel computation.  
- Extends the knowledge gradient framework to the parallel setting with a decision-theoretic formulation.  
- Proposes infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient estimation of q-KG.  
- Empirical results show strong performance on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning, especially under noise.  
- Builds on sequential KG literature and compares to parallel EI and UCB approaches.  
- Novel contribution to parallel Bayesian Optimization.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and Bayes-optimal in motivation.  
- Computationally efficient approach suitable for larger batch sizes.  
- Clear writing and available code enhance reproducibility.  
- Reliance on Gaussian Processes may limit applicability in high-dimensional or non-GP settings.  
- Would benefit from deeper theoretical analysis of error bounds and computational complexity with respect to input dimension.  
- Minor notation clarity issues (e.g., definition/use of A and input of µ^n) and ambiguous reuse of A.  
- Related work discussion should more explicitly cover parallel EI within the main text.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to address theoretical and clarity concerns.
