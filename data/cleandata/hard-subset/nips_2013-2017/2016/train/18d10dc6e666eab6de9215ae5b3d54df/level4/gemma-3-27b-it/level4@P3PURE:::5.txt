This paper derives a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm. To identify an optimal batch of design points, the authors propose a gradient estimation method based on Monte-Carlo simulation. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods. 

1. As far as I am aware, batch KG is not a novel concept introduced by this paper. Please clarify the contribution of this work with respect to prior batch KG publications, such as [1] and [2]. In my assessment, the primary contribution lies in the gradient estimation technique for selecting the optimal batch. If this is the case, the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed gradient estimation method should be directly compared to existing approaches in previous batch KG papers, ideally through experiments or mathematical analysis.

2. Section 5.2 should be expanded upon, as I believe it represents a key aspect of this paper. Further elaboration on this section is crucial for a complete understanding of the methodology.

3. Expected Improvement (EI) typically assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, the comparison presented in the noisy setting (Section 6.2) may be unfair. Was a noise-robust variant of EI employed? If the standard EI acquisition function was used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed with a clear acknowledgement that EI does not explicitly account for observational noise.

4. In line 162, the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined, which is potentially confusing.
