The paper proposes a new stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling for optimizing a multivariate objective function over a finite domain. The key innovation of the algorithm lies in its ability to identify multiple points for objective function computation in each iteration, allowing for parallelization. The challenge is to determine the optimal sampling points, and the authors address this by adopting the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework. This approach estimates the expected improvement in the objective value when an additional sampling point is used, informing the decision on whether and which sampling point to utilize. The authors extend this approach to make decisions on multiple sampling points in batch, necessitating the development of a novel algorithm.

To tackle the computational intractability of this problem, the authors employ a Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) based algorithm to estimate the relevant gradients, aiming to maximize information gain over the set of candidate sampling sets. The algorithm is evaluated on several test problems, demonstrating competitive performance with existing approaches, primarily those based on Confidence Bounds.

The paper is well-written and has potential for practical applications. However, the level of novelty is debatable, as the authors build upon a previously studied approach (KG) by expanding it to a batch setting. This requires a more sophisticated method for optimizing the criterion over multiple points, but the core idea remains an extension rather than a fundamental departure. In my opinion, the primary novelty of the paper lies in the estimation of the q-KG gradient using an IPA approach, which, although a valuable contribution, is also a relatively standard technique.

One specific comment regarding equation (3.1) is that the definitions of μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) are unclear, as μ and K are functions of vectors (or pairs of vectors), not sequences. The authors may want to provide additional clarification or notation to address this point.

Overall, while the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field, its impact would be strengthened by a clearer articulation of its novelty and a more detailed explanation of the mathematical formulations.
