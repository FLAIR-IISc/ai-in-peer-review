- Proposes a novel parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches for hyperparameter optimization.  
- Shows superiority over existing Bayesian optimization methods; paper is well-organized and easy to follow.  
- Parallelization of Bayesian optimization is important in practice; the proposed approach is interesting and more elegant than many existing methods.  
- The method for determining Bayes-optimal batches is a particularly noteworthy contribution.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors, which may be unrealistic due to structural bias in hyperparameter spaces, especially with small samples.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong, but experimental evaluation is insufficient.  
- Using test-set error as the optimization criterion is suboptimal for hyperparameter tuning; metrics like AUC or log loss would be preferable.  
- Experimental benchmark is limited to MNIST and CIFAR10; should include HPOlib benchmarks.  
- Benchmark uses only two datasets, one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), which is inadequate to demonstrate robustness.  
- A more comprehensive benchmark across varied datasets, tasks, and learning algorithms is necessary to convincingly validate the method.
