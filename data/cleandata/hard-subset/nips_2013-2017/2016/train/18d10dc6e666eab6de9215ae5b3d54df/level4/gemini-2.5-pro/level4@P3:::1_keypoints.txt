- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch acquisition function extending Knowledge Gradient to select batches that maximize expected final solution quality.  
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective, grounding the method theoretically.  
- Proposes an IPA-based unbiased gradient estimator to enable efficient gradient-based optimization of the Monte Carlo acquisition.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance versus state-of-the-art batch BO methods across synthetic and real hyperparameter-tuning tasks, especially in noisy settings.  
- Addresses an important and impactful problem (parallelizing BO) with a principled approach.  
- Presents original and technically solid contributions (q-KG formulation and IPA gradient estimator).  
- Experimental validation is thorough, compares against strong baselines, and includes released code for reproducibility.  
- Needs explicit statements of technical assumptions (e.g., compactness of domain A, continuity of f) used in theoretical development.  
- Requires justification/citation for interchanging derivative and expectation in the IPA step and the differentiability of g given argmin and Cholesky dependencies.  
- Related-work discussion should more precisely acknowledge prior batch EI integration and Constant Liar (Ginsbourger et al., 2010).  
- Should cite prior gradient-based multipoint EI work (e.g., Marmin et al., 2015) to contextualize the optimization contribution.  
- Reproducibility detail needed: clarify GP hyperparameter retraining frequency/method in Algorithm 1 (e.g., after every batch via marginal likelihood).  
- Confirm that GP model configurations (hyperparameter optimization, priors) are made comparable across benchmark methods for fair experimental comparison.  
- Minor corrections: clarify the definition/sign in Section 5.2 for g, fix "ARD Matérn 2/5" to "ARD Matérn 5/2", and briefly justify using Latin Hypercube over random initial design.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor clarifications needed; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
