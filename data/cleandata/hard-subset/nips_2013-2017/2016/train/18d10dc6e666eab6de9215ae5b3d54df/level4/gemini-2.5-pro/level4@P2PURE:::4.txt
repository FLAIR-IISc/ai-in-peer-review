This paper presents a parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on scenarios where function evaluations are time-consuming. The authors propose a novel acquisition function, the q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), to select a batch of `q` points for parallel evaluation at each iteration. A key contribution is an efficient approach to optimize this q-KG function. The effectiveness of the method is demonstrated through empirical results.

The problem of parallelizing Bayesian Optimization is highly relevant, as distributing the evaluation workload is crucial for improving the performance of many data-driven applications. The proposed q-KG method, which selects multiple points per iteration instead of the sequential one-at-a-time approach, is a compelling strategy that can significantly accelerate the optimization process.

A potential limitation is the reliance on a Gaussian Process (GP) model, whose suitability can be questionable in high-dimensional settings, such as the optimization of neural network architectures. The paper would be strengthened by the inclusion of theoretical analysis, such as error bounds for the proposed method, and a quantitative analysis of its computational complexity, particularly with respect to the input dimension.

Despite these points, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The proposed method is well-motivated, the experiments are sound, and the paper is well-written.
