- Proposes a parallel batch Bayesian optimization method using the knowledge gradient (q-KG) to choose Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition function for efficient optimization.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive with existing parallel BO methods on noise-free problems and outperforms them on noisy problems.  
- Reviewer finds the experimental results unconvincing and certain methodological aspects unclear.  
- No wall-clock time or computational-overhead analysis is provided to quantify practical gains over random search or sequential BO.  
- Batch size q is fixed at 4 across experiments with no justification or analysis of sensitivity to q.  
- Novelty is limited because knowledge gradient and IPA are established methods and prior work (e.g., parallel EI with IPA) overlaps closely.  
- Method could be impactful for real-world parallel hyperparameter tuning across many domains.  
- Technical exposition and background are generally clear, but some details require further clarification.  
- Asks whether the method was compared to Wang et al. (2015)/parallel q-EI and requests clearer distinctions among parallel EI implementations.  
- Requests analysis or evidence on scalability to much larger batch sizes (e.g., tens to hundreds of parallel runs).  
- Questions whether the synchronous method can be extended to asynchronous evaluations.  
- Requests wall-clock comparisons to random search and sequential BO, and asks about the computational cost of generating each batch.  
- Asks for clarification on what “iterations” on the x-axis denotes and whether initial designs are treated consistently across methods.  
- Minor comments: possible missing term in Eq. (4.1), a grammatical error, improper parenthetical references, and misattribution of IPA origin to Wang et al. (2015).
