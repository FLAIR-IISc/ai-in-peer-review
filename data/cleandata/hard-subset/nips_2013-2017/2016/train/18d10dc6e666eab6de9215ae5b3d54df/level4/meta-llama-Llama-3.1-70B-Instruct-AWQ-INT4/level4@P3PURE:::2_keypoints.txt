- Proposes an elegant parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and shows superior performance; paper is well-written.  
- Parallelization of Bayesian optimization is important for practical hyperparameter optimization and Bayes-optimal batch determination is promising.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed observation errors; this is common but potentially unrealistic in HPO because measurement errors (finite-sample/generalization gaps) can have structural bias and dependencies.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong.  
- Experiments problematic: using test set error as the optimization criterion is suboptimal—threshold-based metrics introduce selection issues; prefer AUC or log loss.  
- Experimental benchmark limited to MNIST and CIFAR10; should include established HPO benchmarks such as HPOlib.  
- Benchmark uses only two datasets and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), lacking diversity across tasks and learners, which limits conclusions about optimizer performance.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical advance but empirical evaluation is too limited to be fully convincing.
