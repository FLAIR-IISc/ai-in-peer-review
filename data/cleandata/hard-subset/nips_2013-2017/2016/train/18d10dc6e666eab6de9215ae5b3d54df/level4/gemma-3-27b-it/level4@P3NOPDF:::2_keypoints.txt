- Proposes a novel, well-written parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and is a conceptually elegant advancement over existing methods.  
- Uses GP assumption of independent, normally distributed errors but should discuss limitations since HPO error estimates (e.g., from cross-validation) can be dependent and biased.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and raises robustness concerns, primarily because test set error is used as the sole optimization metric, which can be suboptimal and prone to overfitting.  
- Recommends evaluating on standardized HPO benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) to strengthen empirical claims.  
- Experiments lack diversity: only two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10) and two classifiers (logistic regression, CNN); needs regression tasks and additional algorithms (SVMs, random forests) to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Theoretical contribution and parallel batch acquisition are valuable, but a more comprehensive benchmark is required to fully validate the method’s practical significance.
