This paper studies the parallel knowledge gradient (KG) method for batch Bayesian optimization in settings where function evaluations are time-consuming. The authors propose a new acquisition function, q-KG, which selects multiple points for parallel evaluation, and they provide an efficient approach to compute q-KG. Empirical results are presented that support their theoretical claims.

The problem addressed is important: enabling parallel or distributed evaluations can significantly improve the performance and wall-clock efficiency of data-driven applications. Rather than evaluating a single point per iteration, the proposed q-KG selects q points to evaluate in parallel at each step, which can substantially accelerate the optimization process when resources allow. This is an interesting and timely idea to explore.

That said, it remains unclear whether the Gaussian process prior is an appropriate modeling assumption in all relevant applications, especially for neural network–related tasks where the input dimension is very large. It would strengthen the paper to include theoretical results characterizing the error or regret of the proposed algorithm, as well as quantitative analysis of its computational and statistical complexity as a function of dimensionality. Such results would help clarify when q-KG is expected to perform well and when it may struggle.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to the Bayesian optimization literature. The method appears sound, and the experimental evaluation is convincing. The paper is generally well written.
