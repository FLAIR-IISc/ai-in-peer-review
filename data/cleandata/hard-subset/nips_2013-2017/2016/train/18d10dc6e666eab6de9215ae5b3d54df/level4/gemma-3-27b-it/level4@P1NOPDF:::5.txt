This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm. A gradient estimation method, based on Monte Carlo simulation, is proposed to identify an optimal batch of design points. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) approaches.

1. To the best of my knowledge, batch KG is not a novel concept introduced in this paper. Please clarify the specific contribution of this work relative to existing batch KG literature, such as [1] and [2]. I believe the primary contribution lies in the gradient estimation method used to determine the optimal batch. If this is the case, the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method should be rigorously compared to previous batch KG approaches, either through experiments or, ideally, through mathematical analysis.

2. Section 5.2 should be expanded upon, as it represents a crucial aspect of this paper. Providing more detail would significantly strengthen the presentation.

3. Expected Improvement (EI) typically assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, the comparison presented in the noisy setting (Section 6.2) may be somewhat unfair. Was a noise-aware variant of EI employed? If the standard EI was used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed acknowledging that EI does not account for observational noise.

4. In line 162, the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined. This is potentially confusing and should be addressed.
