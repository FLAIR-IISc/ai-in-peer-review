- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches, a significant contribution for hyperparameter optimization.
- Shows superior performance compared to existing Bayesian optimization methods.
- Writing is clear and concise and easy to follow.
- Parallelizing Bayesian optimization is important in practice, and the proposed method is interesting and more elegant than most existing approaches.
- Determining a Bayes-optimal batch is a particularly promising and notable aspect of the work.
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors (common in GP-based BO), but this may be unrealistic in HPO due to structural bias and correlated measurement errors.
- Theoretical foundations and analysis are strong.
- Experimental evaluation is weak: using test set error as the optimization criterion is suboptimal; metrics like AUC or log loss would be more appropriate.
- Recommends benchmarking with HPOlib for a more convincing and standardized evaluation.
- Experiments are limited to two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10), one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN); broader benchmarks are needed as optimizer performance can be task- and model-dependent.
- Overall, the paper makes a valuable theoretical contribution, but the limited experimental validation reduces the persuasiveness of its claims.
