- Paper proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) method with a Monte Carlo-based gradient estimator and reports empirical improvements over other batch BO methods.
- Novelty is unclear relative to prior batch KG work (e.g., [1], [2]); the paper should explicitly state its specific contributions versus these works.
- The apparent main contribution—an efficient gradient estimation technique—needs rigorous comparison (empirical or theoretical) to previous batch KG methods to demonstrate efficiency and effectiveness.
- Section 5.2 (infinitesimal perturbation analysis, IPA) is crucial and requires more detailed explanation and implementation details.
- The Expected Improvement (EI) baseline comparison may be unfair in noisy settings; clarify whether a noise-aware EI was used, or discuss EI’s limitations under noise.
- Minor presentation issue: the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined at line 162 and should be fixed.
- General manuscript improvements were made for flow, clarity, precision, politeness, conciseness, consistency, and grammar.
