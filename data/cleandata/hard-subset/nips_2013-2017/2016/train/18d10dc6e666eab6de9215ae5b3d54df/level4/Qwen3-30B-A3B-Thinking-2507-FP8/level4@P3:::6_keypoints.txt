- Introduces q-KG, a novel parallel Bayesian optimization method that selects batches of q points via a decision-theoretic acquisition function.  
- Proposes an efficient computation of the acquisition function using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to address batch-selection costs.  
- Extends the knowledge gradient (KG) framework to the parallel setting, building on prior Bayesian optimization work.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and shows Bayes-optimality for the parallel setting.  
- IPA-based approach effectively addresses computational challenges of evaluating the batch acquisition function.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic benchmarks (Branin, Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann) and real ML hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN).  
- Results show consistent improvements over existing methods, especially in noisy evaluation settings.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation, appropriate GP/BO background, and well-organized experiments.  
- Related work is acknowledged, but relationship with Wang et al. (2015) (IPA for expected improvement) needs clearer clarification.  
- Should clarify distinction between their q-KG and "parallel EI" (MOE); recommend using "q-EI" to avoid confusion with Spearmint's implementation.  
- Experimental section lacks wall-clock time comparisons to assess real-world time-to-solution and overheads.  
- Choice of q=4 across experiments is unjustified; authors should evaluate scaling to larger batch sizes (e.g., q=10,20,40) and discuss practical parallel limits.  
- Paper does not address asynchronous operation, which is important for distributed implementations.  
- Plot x-axis labels (Figures 3 and 4) are ambiguous—clarify whether they represent number of batches or function evaluations.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical and practical contribution to parallel Bayesian optimization with strong experimental support; recommendation: Accept.
