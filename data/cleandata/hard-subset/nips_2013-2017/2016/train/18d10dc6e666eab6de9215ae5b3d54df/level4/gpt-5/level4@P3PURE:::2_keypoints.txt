- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches to address BO's sequential nature.  
- Method is clearly presented, conceptually appealing, elegant, and reportedly outperforms several existing BO techniques.  
- The idea of determining a Bayes-optimal batch is promising and practically relevant for hyperparameter optimization.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (standard for GP-based BO), but this can be unrealistic for HPO due to structural bias and non-independent measurement errors; limitations should be acknowledged.  
- Strong theoretical contribution.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: using test set error as the optimization metric is problematic and suboptimal for hyperparameter tuning (prefer AUC, log loss, etc.).  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: benchmark selection is limited; should include HPOlib for fairer, more comprehensive comparisons.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak: narrow breadth of tasks/models (only MNIST, CIFAR-10; logistic regression and CNN); needs more diverse datasets and learning algorithms.  
- Overall: paper is valuable theoretically but needs broader, more appropriate empirical validation (better metrics, wider benchmarks, more tasks/models) to be fully convincing.
