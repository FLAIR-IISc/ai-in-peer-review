- Introduces a batch-sequential variant of the Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for Bayesian optimization and details its computation analogous to standard KG.  
- Numerical experiments show q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian optimization algorithms.  
- Strong contribution with significant potential impact due to importance of parallelizing Bayesian optimization.  
- Minor limitation: no analysis of wall-clock or speed-up benefits from moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Literature review contains imprecise statements: EI integration over the posterior and Constant Liar were presented in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization."  
- Chevalier et al. previously introduced CL-mix (reference [2]), which the paper should acknowledge.  
- q-EI maximization via natural gradient has been addressed in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design."
