- Proposes a novel, well-explained parallel BO algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal evaluation batches and shows superior performance.  
- The problem is highly relevant for hyperparameter optimization and the Bayes-optimal batch idea is elegant and promising.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed measurement errors; this may be unrealistic for hyperparameter tuning where empirical estimates introduce structural bias, and the paper should acknowledge/discuss this limitation.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: using test set error (accuracy/error rate) as the optimization criterion is problematic; more robust metrics like AUC or log-loss should be used.  
- Experiments omit standard benchmarking suites (e.g., HPOlib), which would enable more direct, standardized comparisons.  
- Evaluation lacks diversity: only two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10), one task (classification), and two model types; broader datasets, tasks (e.g., regression), and models are needed.  
- Summary: strong theoretical contribution but empirical evidence is insufficient; authors should strengthen experiments to better support claims.
