- Paper studies parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) for batch Bayesian optimization in expensive-evaluation settings.
- Introduces a novel acquisition function q-KG to propose multiple evaluation points simultaneously.
- Presents an efficient method to evaluate the q-KG function.
- Provides empirical results supporting the theoretical claims.
- Problem is significant because batching enables workload distribution and improves performance of data-driven applications.
- q-KG’s multi-point suggestion substantially improves optimization efficiency and is an intriguing direction.
- Concern about whether the Gaussian process assumption is appropriate for high-dimensional neural network settings.
- Suggestion to provide theoretical error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to dimension.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with a well-designed method, sound experiments, and clear writing.
- Minor writing and clarity suggestions: rephrase Line 36, define A at Line 95, specify input of mu^n at Line 104, discuss parallel EI at Line 128, and use distinct notations for A at Line 140.
