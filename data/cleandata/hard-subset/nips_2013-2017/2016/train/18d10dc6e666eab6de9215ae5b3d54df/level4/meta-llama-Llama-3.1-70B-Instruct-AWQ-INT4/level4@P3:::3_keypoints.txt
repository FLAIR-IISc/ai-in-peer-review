- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm: the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) for optimizing multivariate objectives over a finite domain, selecting points for parallel evaluation.  
- Provides clear background on Bayesian optimization, Gaussian processes, and the knowledge gradient, and positions the work relative to related methods.  
- Derives the parallel KG method via a decision-theoretic analysis, presented as a novel contribution.  
- Develops an efficient strategy to compute the Bayes-optimal batch using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate the acquisition-function gradient.  
- Presents extensive experiments on synthetic and real-world problems showing q-KG consistently outperforms or matches state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, detailed description of the algorithm and its implementation.  
- Novelty is limited insofar as the work extends existing KG approaches to the batch setting rather than introducing an entirely new paradigm.  
- Notable technical contribution is the IPA-based estimation of the q-KG gradient, enabling efficient multi-point acquisition optimization.  
- Minor issue: notation in equation (3.1) for mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) should clarify dependence on sequences of vectors.  
- Strengths summarized: novel batch algorithm, efficient batch computation, thorough evaluation, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses summarized: incremental novelty and a small notation clarity issue.  
- Acceptance arguments: significant, efficient algorithm with strong empirical support and clear presentation.  
- Rejection arguments: primarily limited novelty and the minor notation ambiguity.  
- Overall recommendation: the paper is a significant, well-presented contribution and is recommended for acceptance.
