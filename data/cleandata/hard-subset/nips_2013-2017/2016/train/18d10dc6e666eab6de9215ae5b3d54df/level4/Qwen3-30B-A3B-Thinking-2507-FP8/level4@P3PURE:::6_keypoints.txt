- Proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using the q-KG acquisition and IPA-based gradient estimation.  
- Empirical claim: q-KG is competitive on noiseless benchmarks and outperforms others with observation noise.  
- Experimental section lacks wall-clock time measurements and analysis of practical parallelization gains versus random or sequential search.  
- The fixed choice q=4 for all experiments is unexplained and unjustified.  
- Novelty is limited: q-KG and IPA-based maximization are not new and overlap with prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015); the paper must differentiate from q-EI.  
- Scalability concerns: no evaluation of performance as parallelism increases (q > 4).  
- Method runs synchronously; unclear if it can be extended to asynchronous execution.  
- Missing comparison to publicly available parallel-EI implementations (Wang et al. 2015 / q-EI).  
- Need wall-clock comparisons to quantify computational overhead per batch and end-to-end runtime versus baselines.  
- Ambiguity in plots: unclear whether x-axis is evaluations or batches, and whether initial design is included.  
- Presentation strengths: technical background is well-explained and experiments are reproducible.  
- Minor issues: Equation (4.1) likely missing a z^{(1:n)} term and several grammatical/phrasing errors.  
- Correction: Wang et al. [2015] applies IPA rather than introducing it; citation/attribution should be fixed.
