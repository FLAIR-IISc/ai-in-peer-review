- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that yields the one-step Bayes-optimal parallel sampling batch.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis and proposes an efficient computation strategy for the Bayes-optimal batch.  
- Main technical contribution: an efficient method to estimate the gradient of the q-KG acquisition function, enabling gradient-based optimization.  
- Experimental results show q-KG outperforms other batch BO methods in both noise-free and noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-organized with clear explanations and assumptions.  
- Thorough related-work section that situates the method within prior parallel Bayesian optimization literature.  
- Needs a more detailed comparison with previous batch KG papers to clarify advantages and limitations.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) requires more elaboration to justify efficiency and effectiveness.  
- Comparison with EI in noisy settings may be unfair; authors should discuss noise assumptions or use a noise-aware EI variant.  
- The abbreviation “IPA” is redefined in the manuscript (line 162), causing potential confusion—use a consistent definition.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing the above clarifications.
