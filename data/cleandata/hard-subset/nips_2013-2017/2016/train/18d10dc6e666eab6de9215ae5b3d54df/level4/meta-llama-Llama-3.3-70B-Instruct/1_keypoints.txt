- Paper proposes a batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization method and a novel parallel q-KG (Knowledge Gradient) criterion.  
- Provides a thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes.  
- Defines the parallel q-KG criterion and details its computational implementation, following the standard KG approach.  
- Numerical experiments claim q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Reviewer judges the paper excellent and recommends publication at NIPS, citing potential societal impact from parallelizing BO.  
- Minor criticism: manuscript does not evaluate actual speed-ups from sequential to batch-sequential methods.  
- Literature review contains slightly inaccurate or incomplete statements—prior work on integrating EI over posteriors (e.g., “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” and Chevalier et al.’s CL-mix) is overlooked.  
- Prior work on q-EI maximization with natural gradients (“Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design”) is not cited.  
- Question: Are the domain A and objective f assumed compact and continuous?  
- Question: Why restrict the initial set A to a Latin Hypercube Sample—what is the rationale?  
- Question: Does Algorithm 1 include hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating of the GP?  
- Question: In Section 5.2, is g defined as a maximization or minimization problem?  
- Suggestion: Clarify the smoothness of g and the differentiability of the Cholesky factor used in derivatives.  
- Typo/correction: Matérn kernel parameter likely 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Question about Figure 1/experiments: were Gaussian processes fitted using different software packages, and could that affect results?
