- Proposes a stochastic gradient optimization algorithm combining Bayesian modeling with batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) for finite domains.  
- Core idea: intelligently select a batch of points per iteration via KG to estimate expected improvement, enabling parallel evaluations.  
- Addresses intractable batch selection using Monte Carlo sampling plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for maximizing information gain.  
- Paper navigates combinatorial sampling sets to prioritize evaluations that reduce uncertainty about the optimum.  
- Experiments show the method is competitive with or outperforms existing approaches, especially Confidence Bounds methods.  
- Well-written, technically sound, and motivated by costly objective evaluations on finite domains.  
- Primary contribution is the q-KG gradient estimation implementation using IPA.  
- Novelty is incremental—extending KG to batches is natural and IPA use is a standard technique in the field.  
- Notational ambiguity in Eq. (3.1): unclear how μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) apply to sequences of vectors; needs clarification.  
- Strengths: clear presentation, solid KG foundation, empirical performance, and parallelization potential.  
- Weaknesses: incremental novelty and the noted notational issue.  
- Recommendation: accept with low priority (weaker acceptance); fix notation in Eq. (3.1).
