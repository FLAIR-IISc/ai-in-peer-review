- Proposes a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method using a Bayes-optimal q-KG acquisition and IPA to maximize it.  
- Shows competitive performance on noiseless benchmarks and better results on noisy problems vs existing parallel methods.  
- Lacks wall-clock time comparisons to random search and sequential BO, so practical speedups are unclear.  
- Fixes batch size q=4 without justification; scalability to larger q (e.g., 10, 50, 100) is not analyzed.  
- Core components (q-KG and IPA maximization) are not novel and the paper fails to clearly differentiate from prior IPA/q-EI work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015; Glynn & Whitt 1989).  
- Impact is potentially high for hyperparameter tuning, but utility remains theoretical without time-to-solution evidence.  
- Technical exposition and experimental detail are clear and reproducible, but missing time-based metrics undermines conclusions.  
- Requests comparison to Wang et al. (2015) q-EI and clearer explanation of differences between q-KG and q-EI.  
- Asks for scalability experiments varying q and discussion/extension to asynchronous execution.  
- Requests wall-clock time measurements and reporting of batch computation overhead.  
- Seeks clarification on plot axes: whether "iterations" means evaluations or batches, inclusion of initial design, and differing starting points.  
- Minor fixes: Eq.(4.1) likely omits z^(1:n); grammar correction ("achieve" → "achieves"); correct IPA attribution; use author citations instead of bracketed references.
