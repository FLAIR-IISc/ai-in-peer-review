This paper studies the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization in settings where function evaluations are expensive. The authors propose a new acquisition function, q-KG, that selects multiple points for parallel evaluation, and present an efficient procedure to compute q-KG. Empirical results are provided that support their theoretical developments.

The problem addressed is important: distributing evaluations across multiple workers can substantially improve the performance of data-driven applications. By selecting q points per iteration rather than one, the proposed q-KG can significantly improve efficiency. This is an interesting direction to explore.

However, it is unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is appropriate in practice, especially for high-dimensional inputs common in neural network architectures. It would be helpful if the authors could provide theoretical guarantees, such as error bounds for their method, as well as quantitative complexity analysis with respect to dimension.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to Bayesian optimization. The method is sound, the experiments are convincing, and the paper is generally well written.
