- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm combining Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Core innovation: selects a set of points per iteration for objective evaluation, enabling parallelization.  
- Main challenge: how to determine the optimal sampling points each iteration.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected improvement and decide whether and which points to sample.  
- Generalizes KG to batch (multiple-point) decision-making, which is computationally intractable.  
- Develops an algorithm leveraging Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate q-KG gradients and maximize information gain over candidate sets.  
- Empirical results: outperforms or closely matches existing confidence-bound–based approaches on several test problems.  
- Paper is well-written and potentially useful in practice.  
- Reviewer questions the level of novelty: mainly extends KG to batches; IPA-based q-KG gradient estimation is the primary novelty but IPA is a standard technique.  
- Specific technical comment: equation (3.1) is unclear—how are μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) defined when μ and K are functions of single vectors/pairwise vectors, not sequences.
