- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method for parallel evaluations based on the knowledge gradient aiming for Bayes-optimal batch selection.
- Introduces an IPA-based gradient estimation technique to efficiently optimize the otherwise expensive q-KG acquisition function.
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms competing batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings and on complex tasks (e.g., CNN tuning); code is provided.
- Extends sequential Knowledge Gradient to batch setting and emphasizes collective (non-greedy) batch selection under a one-batch-optimal decision-theoretic assumption.
- Situates work among recent gradient-based parallel EI and parallel entropy search methods, indicating active interest in collective batch optimization.
- Strength: principled, decision-theoretic novelty grounded in the knowledge gradient framework.
- Strength: solid theoretical justification linking q-KG to Bayes optimality.
- Strength: IPA gradient estimator is a significant computational contribution enabling practical use.
- Strength: strong empirical performance and reproducibility thanks to released code.
- Strength: generally clear writing and well-structured presentation.
- Weakness: related work needs clearer differentiation from prior batch KG methods, particularly Wang et al. (2015), and explicit comparisons.
- Weakness: Section 5.2 (IPA) lacks sufficient detail and an intuitive explanation of how IPA is applied here.
- Weakness: comparing standard EI in noisy settings may be unfair; should use a noise-aware EI variant or justify the comparison.
- Weakness: unnecessary and confusing redefinition of "IPA" at line 162.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty vs. prior batch KG, expand IPA explanation, and address fairness of noisy comparisons.
