- Proposes q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), a principled extension of the Knowledge Gradient acquisition function for selecting batches of q points in parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Introduces a practical stochastic gradient-based algorithm to optimize the non-trivial q-KG acquisition function.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world problems, outperforming relevant batch BO baselines.  
- Addresses the important and practical problem of accelerating Bayesian optimization via parallelization.  
- q-KG is a natural, well-motivated generalization that quantifies expected value of information from evaluating sets of points, avoiding heuristic batch constructions.  
- The stochastic-gradient approach makes optimization of the q-KG acquisition computationally feasible and scalable in practice.  
- Empirical evaluation is well-designed and provides convincing evidence of practical effectiveness.  
- Reliance on standard Gaussian Process models may limit applicability in very high-dimensional settings (e.g., neural architecture search); authors should discuss scaling with input dimension.  
- Paper lacks theoretical analysis—no convergence results, error bounds, or formal complexity analysis with respect to batch size q and dimension d.  
- Minor clarity issues: incomplete sentence at l.36; set A undefined at l.95; specify input for μ^n (e.g., μ^n(x)) at l.104.  
- Suggest adding a brief comparison between q-KG and parallel Expected Improvement (EI) to better position the method.  
- Notation ambiguity: `A` appears to be used in multiple contexts (e.g., l.140); consider using distinct symbols.  
- Overall assessment: solid, impactful contribution with a methodologically sound approach, practical algorithm, and strong results, but would be strengthened by discussion of high-dimensional limitations and theoretical guarantees.
