- Proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using the q-KG acquisition and IPA-based gradient estimation to compute Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noise-free benchmarks and outperforms others on noisy problems.  
- No wall-clock time analysis or comparisons to random search/sequential BO to demonstrate real-world efficiency gains.  
- Choice of batch size q=4 across experiments is unjustified.  
- Use of q-KG and IPA is not clearly novel—prior work (Wang et al. 2015) applies IPA for parallel EI; distinctions should be clarified.  
- Paper should explicitly differentiate q-KG from prior q-EI/MOE/Spearmint methods and clarify novelty/original contribution.  
- Parallel hyperparameter tuning is highly relevant and impactful across ML, CV, biology, and robotics.  
- Technical background and experimental setup are well explained and provide sufficient comparison context.  
- Ask whether the authors compared to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI (q-EI) and whether that is the MOE approach referenced.  
- Question scalability to much larger parallelism (dozens to hundreds of parallel runs) and impact on performance.  
- Ask if the synchronous method can be extended to asynchronous execution like Spearmint.  
- Request wall-clock time comparisons and reporting of computational overhead for batch computation versus random/sequential methods.  
- Clarify the x-axis “iterations”: does it count function evaluations or batches, and does it include the initial design—ensure same baseline across methods.  
- Minor comments: possible missing term z^{(1:n)} in Eq. (4.1), a typo (“achieve”→“achieves”), citation grammar issues, and correct attribution for IPA’s origin versus its application in Wang et al. (2015).
