- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending the sequential Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Provides a decision-theoretic derivation of q-KG and uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient acquisition-gradient computation.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNNs) show q-KG outperforms existing batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Code is available, enhancing reproducibility and impact potential.

Strengths:
- The method is theoretically sound, grounded in decision theory.  
- IPA enables computationally efficient, gradient-based optimization of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical results are comprehensive and convincingly favorable to q-KG.  
- Demonstrated practical relevance via real-world hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Reproducibility is supported by released code.

Weaknesses:
- No explicit analysis or quantification of speedups from parallelization.  
- Literature review contains minor inaccuracies (misattribution of EI batch construction/Constant Liar; omission of Marmin et al. (2017) on parallel EI optimization).  
- Assumptions about the domain A and function f (e.g., compactness, continuity) are not clearly stated; LHS initialization is not justified.  
- Several technical details need clarification (precise definition and objective of g in §5.2, smoothness of g, differentiability of the Cholesky factor).  
- Minor typo: kernel should be Matérn 5/2, not 2/5.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, theoretically grounded contribution to batch BO.  
- Demonstrated empirical superiority across settings, including noisy evaluations.  
- Computational efficiency makes it practical for real applications.  
- Code availability promotes reproducibility and follow-up work.  
- Addresses an important problem in optimization and parallel computing.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Missing speedup analysis is a notable omission.  
- Literature inaccuracies need correction.  
- Some technical clarifications are required before publication.

Overall assessment:
- Strong accept recommendation for NIPS 2016; significant contribution with clear presentation and compelling results, pending minor revisions.

Specific requests for authors:
- Provide quantitative analysis of speedups from parallel q-KG versus sequential BO.  
- Revise the literature review to correctly attribute Ginsbourger et al. (2010) and include Marmin et al. (2017).  
- Explicitly state assumptions on domain A and function f.  
- Justify using Latin Hypercube Sampling for initial samples.  
- Clarify whether g in §5.2 is a maximization or minimization problem.  
- Provide detailed justification for g’s smoothness and Cholesky differentiability.  
- Correct the Matérn kernel typo (5/2).  
- Indicate whether the same GPs were fit using different software to verify consistency.
