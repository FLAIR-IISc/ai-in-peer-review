This paper investigates the parallel knowledge gradient method within the framework of batch Bayesian Optimization, specifically addressing scenarios where function evaluation is computationally expensive. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, q-KG, designed to suggest multiple points for simultaneous evaluation, alongside an efficient evaluation strategy for this function. The effectiveness of their approach is demonstrated through empirical validation, aligning with their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed is significant; distributing the evaluation workload offers substantial benefits for the performance of data-driven applications. By considering *q* knowledge gradients to propose *q* evaluation points per iteration, the proposed algorithm substantially improves efficiency compared to sequential approaches. This is a promising direction for research. However, the reliance on a Gaussian process assumption warrants further consideration, particularly when applied to high-dimensional input spaces common in neural network applications. Strengthening the paper with theoretical guarantees regarding error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to dimensionality would be valuable. 

Nevertheless, this represents a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The methodology is well-founded and the experimental evaluation is rigorous. The paper is generally well-written. Specific suggestions for improvement include: clarifying the phrasing on line 36 ("the set of points to evaluate next that is"), defining the variable *A* on line 95, specifying the input to μ<sup>n</sup> on line 104, briefly discussing the parallel Expected Improvement (EI) algorithm on line 128, and considering alternative notation for *A* on line 140.
