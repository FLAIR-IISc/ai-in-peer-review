

This paper introduces a parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, addressing scenarios where function evaluations are time-consuming. The authors propose a novel acquisition function, q-KG, which outputs multiple points for simultaneous evaluation, alongside an efficient computational approach for q-KG evaluation. Empirical results corroborate the theoretical findings. The problem addressed is significant, as distributed execution capability substantially enhances performance in data-driven applications. By evaluating q points per iteration instead of one, the method accelerates optimization efficiency—a compelling advancement. However, the suitability of Gaussian processes as an assumption, particularly for high-dimensional neural network inputs, remains questionable. The authors should provide theoretical error bounds for their approach and quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension. Despite this, the work is robust and well-positioned within Bayesian Optimization. The methodology is sound, experiments are thorough, and the paper is clearly written. Minor issues include: l.36: "the set of points to evaluate next that is" (redundant phrasing); l.95: Definition of set A is unclear; l.104: Input specification for μⁿ is missing; l.128: Brief discussion of parallel EI would be beneficial; l.140: Distinct notations for A are recommended.
