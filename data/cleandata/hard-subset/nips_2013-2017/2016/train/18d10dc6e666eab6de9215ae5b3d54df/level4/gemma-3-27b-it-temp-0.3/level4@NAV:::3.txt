The paper introduces a novel stochastic gradient algorithm integrating Bayesian modeling for optimizing multivariate objective functions defined on a finite domain. A key contribution lies in its parallelizable iterative process, where the algorithm strategically selects a batch of points for objective function evaluation. This selection is guided by a Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework, adapted to handle multiple sampling points simultaneously, aiming to maximize expected objective improvement. Addressing the computational intractability of this multi-point selection, the authors present an algorithm leveraging Monte Carlo methods and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients for maximizing information gain across candidate sampling sets. Empirical evaluation on several test problems demonstrates competitive or superior performance compared to existing methods, particularly those utilizing Confidence Bounds. The manuscript is clearly written and holds promise for practical applications. 

While the work is valuable, the level of novelty appears incremental. The authors extend a known approach (KG) to a batch setting, necessitating a more complex optimization strategy for the selection criterion. The primary novelty, in my assessment, resides in the IPA-based estimation of the q-KG gradient, though this technique is relatively established. 

I have one specific comment: In equation (3.1), the definitions of  mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) are unclear, as mu and K are defined for single vectors or pairs of vectors, not sequences of vectors like x^(1:n).
