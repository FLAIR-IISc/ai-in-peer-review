This paper derives a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method for Bayesian optimization. To find an optimal batch of design points, a gradient estimation method based on Monte Carlo simulation is proposed. The experiments demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods. 

1. As far as I understand, a batch KG method is not entirely novel; this paper builds upon prior work. Please clarify the specific contributions of this paper concerning existing batch KG approaches, such as [1] and [2]. The key contribution appears to be an efficient gradient estimation technique for finding the optimal batch. If so, the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method should be rigorously compared with previous batch KG methods, ideally through experiments or mathematical analysis.

2. Section 5.2 is crucial to this paper and would benefit from further elaboration. Providing more details on the infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and its implementation would strengthen the paper.

3. Expected Improvement (EI) typically assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, the comparison in the noisy setting might be considered somewhat unfair. Was a noise-aware variant of EI employed? If the standard EI was used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed in the context of EI’s limitations when dealing with noisy evaluations.
