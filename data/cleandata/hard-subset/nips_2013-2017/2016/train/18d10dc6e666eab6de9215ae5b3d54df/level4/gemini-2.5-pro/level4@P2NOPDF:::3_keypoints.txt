- Presents a Bayesian optimization algorithm for maximizing a multivariate objective over a finite domain with parallel batch evaluations.  
- Main contribution: extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) policy to the batch setting via a new q-KG acquisition criterion.  
- q-KG aims to select a batch of q points that jointly maximize expected improvement.  
- Direct optimization of q-KG is intractable, so a stochastic gradient-based search is proposed.  
- Technical novelty: uses Monte Carlo simulation combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate the q-KG gradient efficiently.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms or is competitive with existing approaches, notably Confidence Bounds methods.  
- Paper is well-written and addresses the practical need for parallelization in Bayesian optimization.  
- Main reservation: limited conceptual novelty—primarily an extension of KG and application of standard stochastic gradient/IPA techniques rather than a fundamental breakthrough.  
- Specific comment: notation in Eq. (3.1) is ambiguous—μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) should be clarified as the mean vector [μ(x1),…,μ(xn)]^T and the n×n covariance matrix with entries K(xi,xj).
