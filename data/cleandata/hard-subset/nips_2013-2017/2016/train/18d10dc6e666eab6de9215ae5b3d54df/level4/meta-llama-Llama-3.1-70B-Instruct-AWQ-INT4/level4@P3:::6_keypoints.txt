- Proposes q-KG: a novel parallel extension of the knowledge gradient policy for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis optimizing average-case posterior utility with one batch remaining.  
- Introduces a new acquisition function that measures information gain from evaluating q points jointly.  
- Provides an efficient estimator and gradient computation for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Proposes domain discretization to approximate q-KG for continuous/infinite domains.  
- Experiments show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art baselines on synthetic benchmarks and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noise.  
- Paper is well-written with clear background on Gaussian processes and knowledge gradient, aiding reproducibility.  
- Experimental results are clearly presented with effective plots and tables.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and comparison of runtime/cost versus benchmarks.  
- Needs more experiments to demonstrate scalability as the number of parallel evaluations increases.  
- Little guidance or analysis on how to choose the batch size q (fixed to 4 in experiments).  
- Novelty: significant contribution extending KG to parallel setting; IPA use for gradient is notable.  
- Caveat: IPA-based batch selection has been applied previously for expected improvement, so this aspect is not entirely new.  
- Impact: q-KG could meaningfully speed up hyperparameter tuning and other black-box optimization tasks in parallel settings.  
- Minor clarity issues: provide more experimental setup details (hyperparameters) and interpretation of noisy results.  
- Recommend adding more citations to related Bayesian optimization and batch selection literature.  
- Pro-accept: novel parallel KG extension, efficient IPA-based computation, strong empirical performance.  
- Con-accept: missing complexity/scalability analysis and guidance on batch-size selection.  
- Questions for authors: comparisons to other IPA-based batch-EI methods?  
- Questions for authors: how does q-KG scale with large parallelism and can it run asynchronously?  
- Questions for authors: comparison to random search or sequential BO in wall-clock time?  
- Questions for authors: clarify x-axis “iterations” — evaluations or batches, and whether it includes initial design?
