This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm. A gradient estimation method, utilizing Monte-Carlo simulation, is proposed to identify an optimal batch of design points. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior performance compared to other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods. 

1. The claim of novelty for batch KG appears overstated. Please clarify the paper’s contribution relative to prior work on batch KG, specifically addressing papers such as [1] and [2]. If the primary contribution lies in the gradient estimation technique for batch selection, then a comparative analysis—either experimental or mathematical—against existing batch KG approaches is necessary to demonstrate its efficiency and effectiveness.

2. Section 5.2 should be expanded upon, as it appears to be central to the paper’s core methodology and contribution.

3. Expected Improvement (EI) typically assumes noiseless observations. Consequently, the comparison presented in the noisy setting (Section 6.2) may be biased. Was a noise-aware variant of EI employed? If the standard EI was used, the results in Section 6.2 require discussion acknowledging the limitations of EI when applied to noisy data.

4. The abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162, which is potentially confusing.
