## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission

This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm for Bayesian optimization. The authors propose a gradient estimation method, leveraging Monte Carlo simulation, to identify an optimal batch of design points for evaluation. Experimental results demonstrate the proposed method’s performance exceeding that of several other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) approaches.

However, the paper’s contribution requires further clarification. While the presented work builds upon existing batch KG approaches, it is not immediately clear how it advances the state-of-the-art. Specifically, the authors should address the relationship to prior work, such as Wang et al. [1] and the work described in [2], which also explore batch KG. The core contribution appears to be the proposed gradient estimation technique for batch selection. If this is the case, a more direct comparison—either through experiments or theoretical analysis—with these existing batch KG methods is crucial to establish the method’s novelty and efficacy. Demonstrating an improvement over these existing approaches would significantly strengthen the paper.

Section 5.2, detailing the gradient estimation method, is central to the paper’s contribution and requires substantial elaboration. Currently, the explanation feels somewhat cursory. A more detailed explanation of the methodology, including any assumptions made or potential limitations, would significantly improve understanding. Providing a more intuitive explanation of *why* this particular gradient estimation technique is effective for batch KG would also be beneficial.

The experimental evaluation in Section 6.2, which compares the method against others in a noisy setting, raises a concern regarding fairness. Expected Improvement (EI), the acquisition function used for comparison, inherently assumes noiseless observations. Comparing against EI when noise is present presents a potential bias. It is important to clarify whether a noise-aware variant of EI was employed, or if the observed performance difference is attributable to EI's inherent limitations in noisy environments. If the standard EI was used, this should be explicitly acknowledged and discussed as a potential reason for the observed results, rather than presented as a direct performance comparison. 

Finally, a minor point: the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162. This is unnecessary and should be removed.

**Strengths:**

* The paper addresses an important problem in Bayesian optimization – efficiently selecting batches of points.
* The experimental results demonstrate promising performance compared to existing batch BO methods.
* The use of Monte Carlo simulation for gradient estimation is a reasonable approach.

**Weaknesses:**

* The novelty of the contribution is unclear and requires better contextualization within the existing literature on batch KG.
* Section 5.2 lacks sufficient detail regarding the core gradient estimation method.
* The comparison with EI in the noisy setting is potentially unfair without a noise-aware EI variant or a clear discussion of the limitations of standard EI.
* A minor inconsistency exists with the redefinition of “IPA” on line 162.

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents an interesting approach to batch KG, but requires significant revision to clearly articulate its contribution and address the concerns outlined above. Addressing the clarity of the gradient estimation method and justifying the experimental setup will substantially improve the paper's impact. I recommend rejection in its current form, but encourage resubmission after these issues are addressed.
