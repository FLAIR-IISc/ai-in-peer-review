- Paper proposes a batch KG with a Monte‑Carlo gradient estimator and shows empirical gains, but the core contribution is not clearly articulated.  
- Novelty unclear: prior batch KG work (Wang et al. 2015; Peng 2010) must be explicitly compared to show what is new—particularly demonstrate how the proposed gradient estimator differs and improves on those methods.  
- Require rigorous comparison of the gradient estimation’s efficiency and effectiveness (via analysis or direct experiments) against Wang et al. (2015) and Peng (2010), not just general baselines.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) is the central technical contribution and needs much more detail: derivation, implementation steps/algorithm, justification for the Monte‑Carlo approach, and a small illustrative example.  
- Noisy‑setting comparison is unfair if standard (noiseless) EI was used: authors must state whether a noise‑aware EI was used, and if not, justify the comparison or re-run with noise‑aware baselines.  
- Minor: define the abbreviation "IPA (Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis)" at first use in the main text; cite Peng (2010) as a thesis if the URL is unavailable.
