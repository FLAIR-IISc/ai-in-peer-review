- Introduces a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and reports superior performance.  
- Paper is well-written and accessible.  
- Method is interesting and more elegant than alternatives; derivation of a Bayes-optimal batch is particularly promising.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (standard in GP-based BO).  
- Independence/normality assumption is problematic for HPO because measurement errors reflect generalization-vs-empirical discrepancies and structural bias in hyperparameter regions, especially with small sample sizes.  
- Theoretical contribution is strong but experimental evaluation is limited.  
- Using test set error as the optimization criterion is suboptimal; threshold-based metrics cause issues—metrics like AUC or log loss would be more appropriate.  
- Experiments rely only on MNIST and CIFAR10 and would benefit from benchmarking against HPOlib.  
- Benchmark is too narrow: only two datasets, one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), so optimizer generality is unproven.  
- Overall: promising theoretical work but current experiments are insufficient to convincingly demonstrate the method’s advantages.
