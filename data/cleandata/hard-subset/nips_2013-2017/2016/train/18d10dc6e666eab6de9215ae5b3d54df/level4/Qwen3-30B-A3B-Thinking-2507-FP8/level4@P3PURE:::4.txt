

This paper proposes a parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization, addressing the challenge of time-consuming function evaluations by introducing a novel acquisition function, q-KG, which outputs multiple points for parallel evaluation. The authors present an efficient computational approach for evaluating q-KG and provide empirical results supporting their theoretical claims. The problem addressed is significant, as enabling distributed evaluation accelerates data-driven applications by avoiding sequential point evaluations. The q-KG approach, which considers q points simultaneously in each iteration, substantially improves algorithmic efficiency compared to sequential methods, representing a valuable contribution to the field.  

However, the assumption of a Gaussian process (GP) model warrants scrutiny, particularly in high-dimensional settings such as neural network architectures, where GP scalability and appropriateness are questionable. The authors should address this by providing theoretical error bounds for their method and a quantitative analysis of computational complexity with respect to input dimensionality.  

Overall, this is a well-executed work in Bayesian optimization. The method is sound, the experiments are thorough, and the paper is clearly written. Minor suggestions:  
- Line 36: "the set of points to evaluate next that is" → "the set of points to evaluate next, which is"  
- Line 95: Define the set \( \mathcal{A} \)  
- Line 104: Clarify the input to \( \mu^n \)  
- Line 128: Briefly discuss the parallel EI algorithm for context  
- Line 140: Use distinct notation for \( \mathcal{A} \) to avoid ambiguity
