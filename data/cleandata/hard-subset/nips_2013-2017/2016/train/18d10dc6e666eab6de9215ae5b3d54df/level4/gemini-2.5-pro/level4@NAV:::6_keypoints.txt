- Proposes a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method that selects Bayes-optimal batches using the q-Knowledge Gradient acquisition function and IPA to estimate its gradient.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noiseless problems and outperforms alternatives under observation noise.  
- Goal of enabling efficient parallel hyperparameter optimization is valuable but the paper lacks clarity and persuasive experimental evidence.  
- Evaluation uses standard benchmarks plus CNN on CIFAR-10 and logistic regression on MNIST, but omits wall-clock time measurements.  
- No justification is given for fixing the batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Core components (knowledge gradient and IPA) are established techniques, raising concerns about the paper’s incremental novelty.  
- Prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) has used IPA for batch selection with EI, so distinctions from that work need clarification.  
- Parallel hyperparameter tuning is highly impactful across many application domains.  
- Manuscript is well-written with clear technical background and experimental setup.  
- Request 1: Compare explicitly against Wang et al. (2015); if using parallel EI baseline, rename it to q-EI and clarify differences from prior methods.  
- Request 2: Provide analysis of scalability to larger q (modern systems support far more than q=4) and expected performance at higher parallelism.  
- Request 3: Discuss potential extension to asynchronous evaluation settings (e.g., Spearmint-style asynchronous BO).  
- Request 4: Report wall-clock comparisons versus random search and sequential BO and quantify the computational overhead per batch selection.  
- Request 5: Clarify the x-axis "iterations" in plots—batches or total evaluations—and whether initial design points are included and why curves do not start at the same value.  
- Minor: Check Equation (4.1) for a missing z^(1:n) term.  
- Minor: Fix grammatical error (“achieve” → “achieves”).  
- Minor: Avoid using citations as nouns (rephrase accordingly).  
- Minor: Verify and correct the citation for IPA — Wang et al. applied IPA but did not originate it.
