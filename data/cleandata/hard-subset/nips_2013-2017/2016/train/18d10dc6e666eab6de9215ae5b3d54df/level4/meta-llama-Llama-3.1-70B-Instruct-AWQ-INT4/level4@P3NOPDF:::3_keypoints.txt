- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Key innovation: selects multiple sampling points per iteration to enable parallel evaluation.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected improvement from additional sampling points.  
- Extends KG to a batch (multi-point) setting, requiring a novel optimization algorithm.  
- Addresses computational intractability by using Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients.  
- Optimization objective is to maximize information gain over candidate sampling sets.  
- Empirical evaluation on test problems shows competitive performance versus Confidence Bound–based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and has potential practical applications.  
- Novelty is debatable: mainly an extension of KG to batches rather than a fundamentally new approach.  
- Primary technical contribution: estimation of the q-KG gradient using IPA, which is useful but a relatively standard technique.  
- Specific issue: equation (3.1) has unclear notation—μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) should be defined more precisely.  
- Suggested improvement: clearer articulation of novelty and more detailed explanations of mathematical formulations.
