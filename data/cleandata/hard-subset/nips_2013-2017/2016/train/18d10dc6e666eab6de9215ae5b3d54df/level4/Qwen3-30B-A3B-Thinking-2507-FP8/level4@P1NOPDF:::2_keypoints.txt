- Introduces a parallel BO algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches to overcome sequentiality in existing BO methods.  
- Strong theoretical contribution and an elegant alternative to heuristic parallelization.  
- Paper is clearly written and compelling about the importance of parallelization for practical HPO.  
- Assumes independent Gaussian observation noise—this is questionable for HPO where errors reflect generalization vs. empirical estimates and structural bias; authors should explicitly acknowledge this limitation.  
- Experimental evaluation supports theory but has three major shortcomings that reduce persuasiveness.  
- Optimization metric issue: using test set error for HPO is suboptimal; metrics like AUC or log loss would be more robust.  
- Benchmark scope issue: experiments only use MNIST and CIFAR-10 and do not leverage HPOlib, weakening empirical validity.  
- Benchmark diversity issue: evaluation uses only two datasets and two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN), lacking task and algorithm diversity (e.g., regression, clustering, varied architectures).  
- Overall: theory merits publication, but experiments need substantial expansion (better metrics and broader benchmarks, ideally via HPOlib) to demonstrate practical superiority.
