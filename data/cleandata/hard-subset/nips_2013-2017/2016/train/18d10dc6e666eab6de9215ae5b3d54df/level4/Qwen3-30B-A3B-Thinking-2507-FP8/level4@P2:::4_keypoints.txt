- Introduces q-KG, a novel decision-theoretic parallel Bayesian optimization acquisition function for batch evaluations.  
- Generalizes sequential Knowledge Gradient to jointly optimize entire batches rather than building them iteratively.  
- Provides Bayes-optimality for minimizing expected simple regret when one batch remains.  
- Develops an IPA-based computational method to efficiently optimize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR10) show q-KG outperforms parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and includes a helpful Algorithm 1 for implementation.  
- Strong experimental design with appropriate baselines and statistical significance testing.  
- Original contribution: jointly optimized batch acquisition differs fundamentally from prior iterative batch methods.  
- Significant practical relevance for distributed hyperparameter tuning and noisy evaluation scenarios.  
- Weakness: lacks error bounds or convergence guarantees, particularly for the discretization approach.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of Gaussian process limitations in higher-dimensional hyperparameter spaces; experiments focus on moderate dimensions (d=2–6).  
- Weakness: minor notational inconsistencies (e.g., reuse of A) that may confuse readers.  
- Weakness: needs clearer contrast with closely related work, notably Wang et al.'s nested batch KG.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify definition of A, explain input to μ^n, use distinct notation for domain vs. discretization, add justification for GP assumption in hyperparameter tuning, and explicitly relate to Wang et al. (2016).  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach with practical computational solution and strong empirical gains.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited theoretical analysis of error bounds/complexity, need deeper GP limitation discussion, and minor notation issues.
