- Extends Knowledge Gradient to q-point batch selection for finite-domain Bayesian optimization with parallel evaluations.  
- Introduces Monte Carlo stochastic gradient ascent using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to optimize the intractable q-KG objective.  
- Empirical results often outperform or are competitive with UCB-type baselines on several benchmarks.  
- Strength: clear problem formulation and strong motivation for parallel BO.  
- Strength: principled value-of-information objective rather than heuristic batch methods.  
- Strength: IPA-based Monte Carlo gradient estimator is sensible and practically implementable.  
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is incremental—mainly a batch generalization of an existing acquisition function.  
- Weakness: IPA application is standard and not a substantial theoretical contribution.  
- Needs explicit computational complexity analysis: per-iteration cost vs q, domain size, GP state, and Monte Carlo samples.  
- Notation issue: clarify μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) as vectors/matrices to avoid dimensional confusion (eq. 3.1).  
- IPA estimator: state assumptions for unbiasedness, characterize any bias, and provide variance-control guidance (e.g., CRN, antithetic).  
- Clarify how candidate batches are represented/optimized (continuous relaxation vs discrete search) and how duplicate points are prevented.  
- Reproducibility gaps: report initialization strategy, number of initial evaluations, MC sample counts/schedules, stopping criteria, step-size/tuning, and GP hyperparameter treatment.  
- Empirical suggestion: include wall-clock time comparisons versus q and baselines to support parallelization claims.  
- Overall: solid, well-written practical extension useful to practitioners favoring VOI, but moderate novelty.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clearer notation, complexity/estimator discussion, and additional implementation details.
