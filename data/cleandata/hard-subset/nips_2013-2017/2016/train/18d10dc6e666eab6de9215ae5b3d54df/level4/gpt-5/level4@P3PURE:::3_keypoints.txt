- Introduces a stochastic-gradient-based algorithm within Bayesian optimization for maximizing a multivariate objective over a finite domain with parallel evaluations.  
- Algorithm selects multiple evaluation points per iteration; central challenge is optimal batch sampling.  
- Adopts the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework and extends it to a batch setting (q-KG) to quantify expected improvement from additional samples.  
- Because batch optimization is intractable, develops a Monte Carlo method combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of q-KG, enabling stochastic gradient optimization over batches.  
- Empirical results on benchmarks show the method often outperforms or is highly competitive with confidence-bound-based approaches.  
- Paper is clearly written and appears practically useful for parallel evaluation settings.  
- Novelty is modest: batch extension of KG is incremental and the IPA-based gradient estimation is fairly standard.  
- Request to clarify notation in Eq. (3.1): specify whether μ(x^(1:n)) denotes the vector of means at the sequence and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) the corresponding Gram matrix.
