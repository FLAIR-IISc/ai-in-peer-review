- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic, one-step Bayes-optimal batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to derive an efficient gradient estimator for optimizing q-KG.  
- Empirically competitive with state-of-the-art in noise-free settings and shows superior performance under noisy evaluations.  
- Strength: addresses a highly relevant problem (parallelizing BO) with clear applications to expensive black-box optimization.  
- Strength: q-KG is theoretically well-founded, generalizing the sequential knowledge gradient to the batch setting.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written with clear GP background and derivations.  
- Weakness: Novelty unclear relative to prior work—IPA-based gradient optimization was used by Wang et al. (2015) for parallel EI.  
- Weakness: Experiments use a single fixed batch size (q=4), limiting assessment of scalability and performance as q increases.  
- Weakness: Lacks wall-clock time comparisons; q-KG acquisition optimization may be computationally intensive versus practical baselines.  
- Weakness: Method is synchronous; no discussion or extension to asynchronous batch selection which is often more practical.  
- Question: Clarify relation to Wang et al. (2015) and whether the "parallel EI in MOE" baseline is their q-EI implementation.  
- Question: Provide insights or experiments on scalability of q-KG (optimization cost and regret) as q grows beyond 4.  
- Question: Can q-KG be extended to an asynchronous setting and what challenges would that entail?  
- Question: Provide wall-clock time comparisons to sequential BO and parallel random search to quantify practical overhead.  
- Question: Clarify x-axis "iterations" in plots (batches vs. evaluations) and whether initial design points are included and why curves don’t start at the same value.  
- Minor: Fix typo (“achieve” → “achieves”) and improve parenthetical citation style in Section 2.  
- Minor: Clarify IPA citation—credit L’Ecuyer (1990) for IPA and avoid implying Wang et al. (2015) introduced it.
