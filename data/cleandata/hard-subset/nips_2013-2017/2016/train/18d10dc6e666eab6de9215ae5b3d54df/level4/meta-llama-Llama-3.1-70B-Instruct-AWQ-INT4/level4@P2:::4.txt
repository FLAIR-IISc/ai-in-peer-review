This paper proposes the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization, addressing the challenge of time-consuming function evaluations. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, q-KG, which outputs multiple points for parallel evaluation. They also develop an efficient approach to evaluate the q-KG function. Empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of their method.

The problem considered is important, as parallelizing evaluations can significantly improve the performance of data-driven applications. The idea of using q knowledge gradients to propose multiple points for evaluation at each iteration is interesting and useful for accelerating the algorithm.

The authors provide a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization, with a well-written paper and sound experiments. However, some clarifications and improvements would be beneficial. For instance, it is unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is suitable for high-dimensional input spaces, such as those encountered in neural network architectures. Theoretical results on the error bounds of their method and a quantitative analysis of its complexity with respect to dimension would be valuable additions.

Minor suggestions include:

* Line 36: Consider rephrasing "the set of points to evaluate next that is" for improved clarity.
* Line 95: Define the set A explicitly.
* Line 104: Specify the input of mu^n.

Overall, the paper is well-structured, and the method is solid. With some revisions to address the mentioned points, the paper has the potential to make a more significant impact in the field of Bayesian optimization.
