- Introduces q-KG, a parallel/batch extension of the Knowledge Gradient for Bayesian optimization.  
- Derives a Bayes-optimal batch acquisition function for minimizing the expected GP minimum when one batch remains.  
- Develops an efficient IPA-based gradient estimator to optimize the batch acquisition function.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms existing batch BO methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noise.  
- Builds on prior KG and parallel BO literature; emphasizes a decision-theoretic, collective point selection rather than iterative batching.  
- Theoretical derivation and IPA computational approach are sound and represent a significant computational advance.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, reproducible, and includes practical ML tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN).  
- Code is publicly available, improving reproducibility and practical utility.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear math and detailed experimental setup.  
- Originality concern: insufficiently clarifies differences from prior batch KG work (e.g., Wang et al., Peng thesis).  
- Experimental fairness concern: comparing standard EI in noisy settings is problematic; should use/noisy EI variant or justify choice.  
- Technical detail concern: Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) needs more mathematical detail and intuition.  
- Pro arguments: theoretically grounded, novel IPA computational method, strong noisy-case empirical gains, code release.  
- Con arguments: novelty relative to prior batch KG unclear, potentially misleading EI comparison, insufficient detail on the key technical method.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
