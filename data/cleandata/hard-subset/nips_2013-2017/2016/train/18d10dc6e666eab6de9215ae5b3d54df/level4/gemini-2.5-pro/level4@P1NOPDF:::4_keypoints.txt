- Introduces q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), an acquisition function for selecting a batch of q points for parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Targets scenarios with time-consuming function evaluations to reduce wall-clock time via batch evaluations.  
- Presents an efficient computational approach to optimize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirically demonstrates the method's effectiveness across experiments.  
- Addresses a relevant and important problem with potential to accelerate many data-driven applications.  
- Moving from sequential to batch proposals (q points) is a promising way to speed optimization.  
- Reliance on Gaussian Process models may limit applicability in very high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural architecture search); discussion needed.  
- Paper would be strengthened by theoretical results (e.g., error bounds) for the algorithm.  
- Needs a quantitative analysis of computational complexity with respect to input dimension.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution—well-motivated, sound experiments, and well-written.  
- Minor issues to fix: incomplete phrase at l.36; define set A (l.95); clarify arguments of µ^n (l.104); compare to parallel Expected Improvement (l.128); avoid reusing notation A for multiple concepts (l.140).
