- Proposes a batch (parallel) Knowledge Gradient acquisition function (q-KG) for one-step-ahead Bayes-optimal batch selection in Bayesian optimization.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) with Monte Carlo to efficiently estimate gradients for maximizing the q-KG acquisition.  
- Demonstrates empirical superiority of q-KG over several batch BO methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Extends sequential KG to the parallel setting and situates the work within existing batch BO literature, including parallel EI and UCB extensions.  
- Theoretical derivation of q-KG and the IPA-based gradient estimator is a key strength, enabling scalability to higher dimensions and larger batch sizes.  
- Robust experimental results and publicly available code enhance reproducibility and practical impact.  
- Novelty needs clearer articulation relative to prior batch-KG work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015 and a Princeton thesis referenced).  
- The paper should provide a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity of the IPA-based gradient estimation.  
- Section 5.2 should be expanded to give greater intuition and derivation details for the IPA approach.  
- Comparison with EI in noisy settings is potentially unfair; should consider and compare to noise-aware EI variants.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically grounded approach, strong empirical performance (noisy settings), significant IPA-based optimization contribution, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear novelty versus prior batch KG, insufficient detail on IPA derivation, and incomplete comparisons to noise-handling baselines.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with minor addressable issues; recommendation for acceptance.
