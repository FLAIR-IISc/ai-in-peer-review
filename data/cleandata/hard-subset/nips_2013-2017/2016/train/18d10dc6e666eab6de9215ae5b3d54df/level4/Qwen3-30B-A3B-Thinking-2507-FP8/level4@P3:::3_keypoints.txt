- Introduces q-KG, a novel decision-theoretic extension of the sequential knowledge gradient to jointly optimize batches in parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Addresses the practical problem of selecting an optimal batch of q points when evaluations can be run in parallel.  
- Derives q-KG as the batch acquisition function that maximizes expected improvement in solution quality from one additional batch.  
- Distinguishes from prior parallel BO methods by jointly optimizing the acquisition over the entire batch rather than iteratively selecting single points.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for Monte Carlo gradient estimation, enabling practical optimization of the parallel KG.  
- Theoretical foundation and algorithm (Algorithm 1) are clear and technically sound.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic benchmarks (Branin, Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann) and ML tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10).  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or matches baselines, with particularly strong gains in noisy settings; code is publicly available.  
- Writing and organization are good, with well-documented experimental setup; minor clarity improvement suggested for domain discretization.  
- Main weakness: limited discussion and analysis of computational complexity and scaling with batch size q.  
- Secondary weakness: ambiguity in the relationship between feasible domain A and discretized set An and how discretization size M affects solution quality.  
- Minor issues: a few grammatical/errors (e.g., equation 3.1) and a requested brief discussion of limitations such as high-dimensional scaling.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add complexity analysis, clarify discretization, and note limitations.
