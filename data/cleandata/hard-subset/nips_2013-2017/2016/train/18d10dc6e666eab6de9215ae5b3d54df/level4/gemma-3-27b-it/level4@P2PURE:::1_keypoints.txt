- Introduces a batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization method using a parallel Knowledge Gradient criterion (q-KG) with computation similar to standard KG.
- Numerical experiments show q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.
- Overall assessment: strong paper worthy of NIPS; parallelizing BO has significant societal impact.
- Missing analysis of runtime speed-ups achieved by batch-sequential processing.
- Literature review contains imprecise claims: EI integration over the posterior was previously explored in “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” (and Constant Liar); Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix.
- Prior work on maximizing q-EI using natural gradients exists (“Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design”).
- Clarify mathematical assumptions: is the domain A assumed compact and is f continuous?
- Why is A restricted to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) during the initial stage?
- Does Algorithm 1 include hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating of hyperparameters?
- In Section 5.2, clarify whether g is defined as a maximization or a minimization objective.
- Smoothness of g is not obvious; the differentiability of the Cholesky factorization used is nontrivial and should be justified.
- The Matérn kernel parameter appears misstated—likely 5/2 rather than 2/5.
- For Figure 1 experiments, clarify whether the same Gaussian Processes could be fitted using different software packages.
