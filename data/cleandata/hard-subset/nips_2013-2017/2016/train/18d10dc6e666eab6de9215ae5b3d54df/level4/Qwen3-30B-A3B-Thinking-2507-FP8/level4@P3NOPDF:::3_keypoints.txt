- Proposes a batch Bayesian optimization algorithm extending Knowledge Gradient (KG) to select multiple points for parallel evaluation.  
- Core technical contribution: efficient gradient estimation for the q-KG acquisition via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) combined with Monte Carlo sampling.  
- Uses IPA to approximate the gradient of the q-KG criterion, addressing intractability of direct optimization over candidate sets.  
- Empirical evaluation on benchmarks shows competitive or superior performance vs. state-of-the-art methods (e.g., Confidence Bound approaches).  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and emphasizes practical applicability; technical exposition is accessible.  
- Novelty is modest: applying IPA for gradient estimation in this setting is incremental since IPA has been used similarly before.  
- Primary contribution is the application of established techniques (IPA + KG) to batch optimization rather than a fundamentally new algorithmic paradigm.  
- Significant technical issue: ambiguous notation in Equation (3.1) for μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n))—unclear if these denote vectors/matrices or scalar summaries.  
- Recommended fix: explicitly define μ(x^(1:n)) as a vector of means and K as the corresponding kernel matrix (or clarify intended meaning).  
- Overall assessment: useful and well-executed extension suitable for publication (NIPS) after revisions that clarify notation and more explicitly acknowledge the incremental novelty.
