- Introduces q-KG, a parallel extension of the knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Acquisition derived decision-theoretically to maximize expected improvement in the posterior minimum after a batch.  
- Proposes infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients of the q-KG objective for scalable joint batch optimization.  
- IPA-based gradient estimation is a practical advance over prior iterative greedy batch selection methods.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic benchmarks and ML tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN) shows consistent superiority over parallel EI, batch UCB, and GP-UCB-PE, especially in noisy settings (e.g., 11% CIFAR10 vs 14% expert).  
- Reports substantial speedups in noisy problems (e.g., ~40% faster convergence on noisy Rosenbrock3).  
- Provides open-source code and detailed experimental setup, improving reproducibility.  
- Situates work well within prior literature and avoids overstating novelty.  
- Weakness: overall novelty is incremental—main idea is extending KG to batch, with IPA as the main innovation.  
- Weakness: IPA itself is not entirely novel; paper lacks clear justification for preferring IPA over alternatives (e.g., stochastic gradient methods).  
- Weakness: Notational ambiguity in Eq.(3.1) — need to clarify vectorization of μ and K for sequences of points.  
- Weakness: Claim that q-KG reduces to parallel EI in a specific case is slightly misleading and not explicitly validated experimentally.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound, well-executed, and practically impactful with minor caveats.  
- Recommendation: Strong accept.
