- Proposes a batch KG acquisition with a Monte‑Carlo gradient estimator for batch selection; experiments claim superior performance but contribution is unclear.  
- Claim of novelty is misleading because batch KG was previously studied (Wang et al. 2015; Peng 2010); the real novelty appears to be the gradient estimation method.  
- Authors must explicitly contrast their method with prior batch KG work and either add direct experiments vs [1],[2] or provide mathematical/computational superiority analysis.  
- Section 5.2 is underdeveloped: the Monte‑Carlo gradient derivation omits key steps, batch dependency handling, and convergence properties.  
- Need theoretical justification for the estimator (e.g., unbiasedness, variance analysis) and empirical cost comparison against alternatives (e.g., finite differences).  
- Clarify and quantify how the estimator scales with batch size m and input dimension d.  
- Experimental inconsistency: Section 6.2 uses standard EI in noisy settings—authors must state whether a noise‑aware EI was used, justify the choice, or add noise‑aware EI results for fair comparison.  
- Minor technical issues: IPA is redefined instead of first‑defined, Peng’s thesis should be replaced or cited as a stable report, and the claim of a “closed‑form” gradient is incorrect (it’s Monte‑Carlo/approximate).  
- Overall assessment: promising approach but fails to establish novelty, lacks critical technical details, and contains methodological inconsistencies.  
- Recommendation: Reject with strong encouragement to resubmit after major revisions addressing prior‑work comparison, Section 5.2 expansion, noise handling, and minor corrections.
