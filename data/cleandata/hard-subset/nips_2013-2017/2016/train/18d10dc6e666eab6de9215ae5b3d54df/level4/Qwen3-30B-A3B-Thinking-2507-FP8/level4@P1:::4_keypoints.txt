- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), a Bayes-optimal acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization.
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective and optimizes batches collectively rather than greedily.
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to make optimization of the acquisition function computationally efficient.
- Empirically outperforms existing batch BO methods, especially in noisy evaluation settings.
- Evaluation includes multiple synthetic functions (Branin, Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann) and real ML tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10).
- Code is publicly available, improving reproducibility.
- Well-situated in BO literature, extending knowledge gradient from sequential to parallel settings.
- Novelty lies in the collective batch optimization vs. prior iterative/greedy batch methods.
- Main theoretical weakness: reliance on Gaussian process priors without justification for high-dimensional hyperparameter tuning.
- Lacks theoretical guarantees—no error bounds or complexity analysis with respect to input dimensionality.
- Related-work discussion could be improved, specifically comparing to other methods that optimize batches collectively (e.g., refs [2,15,25]).
- Some notational and clarity issues: undefined feasible domain A (l.95), unclear input to posterior mean μ^(n) (l.104), confusing notation for candidate set z^(1:q) (l.140).
- Overall quality: technically strong method with rigorous development and comprehensive empirical validation.
- Clarity: generally good but reduced by notational inconsistencies and missing definitions.
- Originality and significance: meaningful, novel contribution with practical impact for distributed/parallel optimization.
