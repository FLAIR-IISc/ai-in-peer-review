- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Key innovation: identifies multiple evaluation points per iteration to enable parallelization.  
- Main challenge: choosing optimal sampling points; authors adopt the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework.  
- KG framework estimates expected improvement from an extra sample and decides whether and which point to sample.  
- Extends KG to batch (multiple-point) decisions, requiring a computationally efficient optimization method.  
- Uses Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients and maximize information gain over candidate sets.  
- Empirical evaluation on test problems shows competitive performance against Confidence-Bound–based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and has potential practical applications.  
- Novelty concern: extension from KG to batch is incremental and may not be highly novel.  
- Primary technical novelty: estimation of the q-KG gradient via IPA, which is useful but a relatively standard technique.  
- Specific issue: equation (3.1) is unclear—definitions of mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) for sequences need clarification.
