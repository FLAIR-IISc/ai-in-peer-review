- Introduces the parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) acquisition function to select Bayes-optimal batches under a one-step lookahead for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Extends the sequential Knowledge Gradient policy to the parallel/batch setting, generalizing an established decision-theoretic framework.  
- Direct maximization of q-KG is intractable, so the paper develops an efficient optimization strategy using Monte Carlo sampling.  
- Uses Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to derive an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator, enabling gradient-based batch selection.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic problems and hyperparameter tuning, often outperforming EI- and UCB-based parallel methods.  
- Shows particular advantages in noisy evaluation settings, highlighting practical utility for stochastic objectives.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly structured, making the method and derivations easy to follow.  
- Conceptual contribution is largely an extension of KG to batches, which may be viewed as an incremental advance rather than a fundamentally new framework.  
- The technical novelty is somewhat tempered because IPA is a standard technique in stochastic optimization.  
- Minor clarity issue: notation in Eq. (3.1) for μ(x(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) should be explicitly defined as the mean vector and n×n covariance matrix.  
- Overall assessment: solid, theoretically motivated, and practically effective contribution to Bayesian optimization despite limited novelty.
