- Proposes a novel parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches; elegant and promising.  
- Authors report the proposed method outperforms existing BO approaches.  
- Paper is well written, easy to follow, and theoretically strong with clear relation to prior work.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors, which may be unrealistic due to structural bias in hyperparameter optimization measurements.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing and too limited to be convincing.  
- Using test-set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; threshold-based metrics are suboptimal for HPO (prefer AUC or log loss).  
- Experiments use MNIST and CIFAR10 but should include HPOlib benchmarks designed for hyperparameter optimization.  
- Benchmark uses only two datasets and two learning algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), lacking variety across tasks and models.  
- Overall benchmark limitations weaken empirical claims despite strong theory.  
- Reviewer would not accept in current form but would reconsider if experiments are substantially expanded and concerns addressed.  
- Rating: 6; Confidence: 4.
