- Addresses batch Bayesian optimization by proposing the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) acquisition function for selecting a batch of q points.
- q-KG is derived from a decision-theoretic framework and is one-step Bayes-optimal assuming the batch is the last evaluation.
- Optimizing q-KG directly is computationally hard; the authors introduce an efficient gradient-based optimizer using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Empirical evaluation compares q-KG to parallel EI and UCB on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR-10) for Logistic Regression and a CNN.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and situates itself well in related work.
- Strength: originality and theoretical soundness—q-KG provides a principled, non-greedy batch selection strategy.
- Strength: technical quality—derivation of an unbiased gradient estimator via IPA makes optimization feasible.
- Strength: significance—effective parallel BO is important for practical hyperparameter tuning and accelerates real-world workflows.
- Weakness: choice of optimization metric—using test set error/accuracy is suboptimal; continuous metrics like log-loss or AUC would be more appropriate.
- Weakness: narrow experimental scope—only two datasets and two model types limit generalizability across tasks and algorithms.
- Weakness: lack of standardized benchmark usage—should evaluate on HPO benchmark suites (e.g., HPOlib) for broader comparison.
- Weakness: unaddressed noise assumption—the i.i.d. Gaussian noise assumption may be violated in hyperparameter tuning due to structured validation noise.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but empirical validation is too limited; expanding experiments and metrics would strengthen the paper.
