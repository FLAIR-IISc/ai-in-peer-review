- Proposes parallel Bayesian optimization using q-KG optimized via IPA, with competitive/noisy benchmark results but overall weak validation.  
- Experimental evaluation relies heavily on synthetic benchmarks and only two real tasks (CNN on CIFAR-10, logistic regression on MNIST).  
- Fails to report wall-clock time comparisons versus random search or sequential BO, so claimed practical efficiency is unverified.  
- Uses a fixed batch size q = 4 with no justification or analysis of batch-size impact or scalability.  
- Does not demonstrate scaling to larger q (e.g., q ≥ 10) common in prior work and modern multi-core settings.  
- Novelty is overstated: IPA and parallel BO methods (e.g., Wang et al. 2015 for q-EI) already exist; the paper fails to clearly differentiate q-KG+IPA from prior q-EI+IPA.  
- Lacks explicit comparison to Wang et al. (2015) and clarification whether contribution is a new acquisition/analysis or a reimplementation.  
- Practical impact is undermined by missing analysis of batch-computation overhead versus gains in parallel evaluations (time-to-solution tradeoff).  
- Clarity issues in presentation: undefined x-axis in Figure 3 (iterations = evaluations or batches?), inconsistent starting points between methods.  
- Mathematical/typographical errors: Equation (4.1) likely omits z^(1:n); minor grammar and citation-style errors throughout.  
- Key methodological questions left unanswered: relation to Wang et al., scalability beyond q=4, support for asynchronous execution, and wall-clock runtime comparisons.  
- Suggested revisions: include wall-clock/time-to-solution baselines, justify or vary q, explicitly contrast with prior q-EI work, and fix experimental and notation ambiguities.
