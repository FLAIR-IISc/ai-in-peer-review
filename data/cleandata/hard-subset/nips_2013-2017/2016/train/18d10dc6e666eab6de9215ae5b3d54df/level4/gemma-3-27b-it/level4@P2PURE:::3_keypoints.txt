- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm using Bayesian modeling to optimize multivariate objectives over finite domains.  
- Main innovation: parallelizable selection of multiple sampling points per iteration.  
- Adopts the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework, extending it to a batch (q-KG) setting to choose multiple points simultaneously.  
- Batch KG is computationally intractable; authors develop a Monte Carlo algorithm with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients and maximize information gain.  
- Experiments on several test problems show competitive performance, often matching or surpassing Confidence-Bound-based methods.  
- Paper is well-written and appears practically valuable.  
- Novelty is limited: primarily an extension of existing KG to batch rather than a fundamentally new framework.  
- Principal technical contribution is IPA-based q-KG gradient estimation, but IPA is a relatively standard technique.  
- Specific issue: Equation (3.1) is unclear about mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) definitions, since mu and K are defined for one/two vectors while x^(1:n) denotes a sequence.
