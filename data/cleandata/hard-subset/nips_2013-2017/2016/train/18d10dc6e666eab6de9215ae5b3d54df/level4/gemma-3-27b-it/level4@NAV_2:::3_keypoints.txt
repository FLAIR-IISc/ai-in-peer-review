- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method extending the knowledge gradient to select points for parallel evaluation.
- Makes the batch KG tractable by estimating acquisition gradients using Monte Carlo sampling and infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Evaluated on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks, showing competitive or superior performance, especially under noisy evaluations.
- Paper is well-written with a clear logical flow from problem setup to solution and experiments.
- Effectively situates q-KG within prior Bayesian optimization and parallel acquisition literature.
- Detailed explanation of the q-KG factor and computational techniques is a valuable contribution.
- Demonstrates robustness to noise in empirical results.
- Primary novelty is incremental: extending KG to batch settings; main innovation is efficient gradient estimation using established techniques.
- Notational clarity issue: Equation (3.1) should better specify how µ and K apply to the vector sequence x^(1:n).
- Lacks a detailed computational complexity analysis comparing q-KG to alternative methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, practical through efficient gradient estimation, and strong empirical gains in noisy environments.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty and insufficient analysis of computational cost.
