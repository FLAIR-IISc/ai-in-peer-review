The paper proposes a new stochastic gradient algorithm that incorporates Bayesian modeling for optimizing a multivariate objective function over a finite domain. The key innovation of the algorithm lies in its ability to identify multiple points for objective function computation in each iteration, allowing for parallelization. The challenge is to determine these sampling points in an optimal manner. To address this, the authors employ the Knowledge Gradient framework, which estimates the expected improvement in the objective value when an additional sampling point is utilized, and use this information to decide whether and which sampling point to select. This approach is generalized to accommodate batch decision-making for multiple sampling points. However, due to the computational intractability of this problem, the authors develop an algorithm leveraging Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis to estimate the relevant gradients, thereby maximizing information gain over the set of candidate sampling sets. The algorithm is applied to several test problems, demonstrating competitive performance with existing approaches, primarily those based on Confidence Bounds. The paper is well-written and has potential practical applications.

While I acknowledge the paper's contributions, I am not entirely convinced by the level of novelty presented, as the authors essentially extend a previously studied approach (Knowledge Gradient) to a batch setting. This extension necessitates a more sophisticated method for optimizing the criterion over multiple points. In my opinion, the primary novelty of the paper lies in the estimation of the q-KG gradient using an Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis approach, which, although useful, is a relatively standard technique.

One specific comment I have is regarding equation (3.1): since x^(1:n) represents a finite sequence of vectors, it is unclear how mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) are defined, given that mu and K are functions of single or paired vectors, not sequences.
