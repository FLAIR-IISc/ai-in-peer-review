- Introduces a batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization method based on a parallelized Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion with a clear definition and computation.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods.  
- The approach is a strong contribution with broad applicability and important practical implications due to parallelization.  
- Experiments do not quantify speed-ups of batch-sequential vs. sequential optimization.  
- Related work section contains imprecisions: prior EI-with-respect-to-posterior work by Lizotte et al. (and Constant Liar) and CL-mix by Chevalier et al. are not properly cited.  
- Prior work on differentiating multi-point Expected Improvement for optimal batch design is missing or not cited.  
- Theoretical assumptions need clarification: is the domain A compact and is f assumed continuous?  
- Initial design choice needs justification: why use Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS) and what alternatives were considered?  
- Algorithm 1 should state whether GP hyperparameters are re-estimated or updated (and how); discuss implications if they are fixed.  
- Clarify Section 5.2 whether g is defined as a maximization or minimization problem.  
- Provide justification or proof for the claimed smoothness of g in Section 5.2.  
- Provide details/proof for differentiability and derivative computation of the Cholesky factor.  
- Typographical error: Matérn kernel parameter should be ν = 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Consider verifying results with multiple GP software implementations (e.g., GPy, scikit-learn) to assess robustness and reproducibility.
