This paper introduces a novel parallel Bayesian optimization method called the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) algorithm. The proposed method computes Bayes-optimal batches, which is an interesting and promising approach to parallelizing Bayesian optimization. The authors show that q-KG outperforms existing Bayesian optimization approaches, and the paper is well-written and easy to follow.

The theoretical aspect of the paper is strong, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method. However, the experiments are somewhat limited. The use of test set error as an optimization criterion is problematic, as it is a suboptimal score-function for hyperparameter tuning. It would be better to use metrics like area under the ROC curve or log loss. Additionally, the authors only used two data sets (MNIST and CIFAR10) and a single machine learning task (classification) with only two learning algorithms (logistic regression and CNN). It would be beneficial to see more variety in the benchmark, as some optimizers perform well for certain types of learning algorithms but poorly for others.

Furthermore, the assumption of independent normally distributed errors is common in most BO methods based on Gaussian processes, but it may not be realistic in hyperparameter optimization. Measurements errors represent the difference between generalization performance and empirical estimates, and structural bias is common in certain regions of hyperparameter-space, especially given small sample sizes. It would be worth mentioning this limitation in the paper.

Overall, I like the paper, and the theoretical contribution is valuable. However, the current benchmark is too limited to be truly convincing. To strengthen the paper, the authors could consider using more diverse data sets and machine learning tasks, as well as more robust evaluation metrics.
