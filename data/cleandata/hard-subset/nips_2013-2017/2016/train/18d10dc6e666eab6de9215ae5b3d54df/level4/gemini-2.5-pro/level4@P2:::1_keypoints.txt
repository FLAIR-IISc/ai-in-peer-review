- Introduces q-KG, a principled decision-theoretic extension of Knowledge Gradient for parallel (batch) Bayesian Optimization.
- Proposes an acquisition function that selects one-step Bayes-optimal batches by maximizing expected value of information.
- Develops an IPA-based unbiased stochastic gradient estimator to make q-KG optimization computationally feasible.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings, outperforming state-of-the-art batch BO methods.
- Significant and original contribution: principled parallelization versus heuristic batch methods.
- Technical quality is high: clear derivation of q-KG, sound gradient estimator, and thorough empirical comparisons.
- Paper is well-written and accessible with systematic algorithmic presentation and GP/BO background.
- Related work section should better acknowledge prior parallel EI ideas (Ginsbourger et al. 2010) and gradient-based multipoint EI (Marmin et al. 2015).
- Experiments lack analysis of computational cost; need wall-clock/time-per-iteration comparisons and discussion of trade-off between overhead and sample efficiency.
- Missing study of batch speed-up: compare q>1 performance to sequential KG (q=1) to quantify parallel gains.
- Technical assumptions need clearer statement in main text: smoothness for IPA, interchange of expectation and differentiation, and differentiability of Cholesky factor.
- Questions/minor remarks: state formal assumptions on f and domain A (continuity, compactness).
- Clarify choice of Latin Hypercube for initial design and confirm consistent GP/hyperparameter fitting across baselines to avoid implementation artifacts.
- Check formulation of g (min(mu) - min(mu_new)) for correctness, fix kernel typo (likely Matérn 5/2), and note GP retraining is properly mentioned.
