- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending knowledge gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Core idea: select batches that jointly maximize expected improvement (KG) for expensive black-box functions.  
- Direct maximization is intractable; authors use Monte Carlo sampling and infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients and enable optimization.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art batch BO on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides necessary GP background and related work summary.  
- Main novelty is the scalable IPA-based gradient estimation for the parallel KG acquisition function.  
- Contribution is valuable but incremental—adapts established KG/IPA techniques rather than introducing a fundamentally new paradigm.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sound decision-theoretic foundation, strong empirical validation, practical relevance for parallelizable costly evaluations, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty and reliance on standard IPA techniques.  
- Specific comment: notation in Eq. (3.1) for x^(1:n), µ, and K is ambiguous; recommend explicitly writing the vector/matrix forms for clarity.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—technically sound, empirically validated, and practically useful despite incremental novelty.
