- Proposes a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method using q-KG acquisition and IPA to maximize it.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noiseless benchmarks and outperforms others when observations are noisy.  
- Overall experimental evidence is not fully convincing; key metrics and justifications are missing.  
- Technical critique: experiments lack wall-clock time measurements and comparisons to random search or sequential BO.  
- Technical critique: no rationale provided for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Novelty: combining knowledge gradient with IPA is a contribution, but overlaps with prior work (e.g., parallel EI + IPA) and differences are not clearly delineated.  
- Impact: Parallel hyperparameter tuning is broadly useful across ML and other fields.  
- Clarity: technical details and background are well described; experiments are comprehensive in reported information.  
- Question: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI implementations and clarified notation (q-EI vs Spearmint)?  
- Question: How does the method scale to larger q (many-core systems) and what are expected benefits for larger parallel runs?  
- Question: Can the method be extended to asynchronous batch selection like Spearmint?  
- Question: What is the computational overhead for computing batches and how does wall-clock performance compare to alternatives?  
- Question: Clarify x-axis “iterations” (batches vs evaluations) and whether initial design is included and why starting values differ.  
- Minor comments: missing z^(1:n) in Eq.(4.1); grammatical/citation phrasing fixes; Wang et al. likely applied IPA rather than introduced it.
