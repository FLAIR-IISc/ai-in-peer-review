This paper investigates the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on scenarios where function evaluations are computationally expensive. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, q-KG, designed to suggest multiple points for parallel evaluation and present an efficient method for evaluating this function. Empirical results validate their theoretical findings. The problem addressed is important; distributing the computational workload is crucial for improving the performance of data-driven applications. Instead of sequential evaluation, the algorithm leverages a knowledge gradient that proposes *q* points for evaluation in each iteration, significantly enhancing efficiency. This is an interesting and worthwhile direction for research. However, the assumption of a Gaussian process may be limiting, particularly when applied to neural network architectures with high-dimensional input spaces. Providing theoretical guarantees regarding error bounds and a quantitative analysis of the algorithm’s complexity with respect to input dimension would strengthen the work. Nevertheless, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization, with a sound methodology and well-executed experiments. The paper is generally well-written. Specific comments include: line 36, rephrase "the set of points to evaluate next that is"; line 95, define the variable A; line 104, clarify the input of μ<sup>n</sup>; line 128, briefly discuss the parallel Expected Improvement (EI) algorithm for context; and line 140, consider using distinct notation for A.
