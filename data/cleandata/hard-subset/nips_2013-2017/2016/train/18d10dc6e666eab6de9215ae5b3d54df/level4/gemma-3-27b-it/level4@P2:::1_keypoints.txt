- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization criterion for parallel function evaluations derived from decision theory.  
- Proposes an efficient computational method for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and ML hyperparameter tasks show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Highlights the practical importance of parallelizing Bayesian optimization for expensive black-box functions.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and uses consistent notation.  
- Derivation of q-KG is technically sound and well-motivated.  
- IPA-based computational approach effectively addresses optimization efficiency challenges.  
- q-KG is an original extension of the Knowledge Gradient to the parallel/batch setting.  
- Potentially significant impact for hyperparameter tuning and other costly evaluation scenarios.  
- Lacks explicit analysis of parallel speed-up compared to sequential Bayesian optimization.  
- Literature review has imprecise attributions regarding prior batch/parallel BO work (e.g., Ginsbourger et al., Marmin et al.).  
- Some problem assumptions (compactness of A, continuity of f) are not explicitly stated.  
- Implementation choices (e.g., initial Latin Hypercube Sampling) need further justification.  
- Requires deeper discussion of smoothness/differentiability assumptions for gradient estimates and Cholesky factor in Section 5.2; minor Matérn kernel typo noted.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical results, high potential impact, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of speed-up analysis, minor literature imprecisions, and unclear technical details needing clarification.  
- Questions for authors: quantify computational speed-up vs sequential BO; clarify literature attributions; state assumptions on f and A; justify Latin Hypercube initialization; detail smoothness assumptions for gradients; report GP fits using different software if feasible.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality paper with significant contribution; recommended for acceptance pending addressing the noted clarifications.
