- Introduces q-KG, a batch extension of the Knowledge Gradient acquisition function for selecting q points to maximize expected final solution quality.
- Tackles the computational challenge by deriving an unbiased, Monte Carlo-based gradient estimator for q-KG using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).
- Proposes a gradient-based optimization algorithm that makes q-KG computationally feasible.
- Empirically evaluates q-KG on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, showing competitive or superior performance to batch EI and UCB, especially with noisy evaluations.
- Strength: Addresses a practical problem (parallel BO) with convincing empirical gains, notably in noisy settings.
- Strength: Technically sound, principled one-step Bayes-optimal derivation, and an effective stochastic gradient estimator.
- Strength: Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and the method and derivations are easy to follow.
- Weakness: Main novelty is extending KG to batches and applying IPA; IPA is established, so the contribution is more in application than a fundamentally new paradigm.
- Suggestion: Clarify GP notation in Eq. (3.1) by explicitly defining µ(x(1:n)) as the prior mean vector and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) as the covariance matrix at observation points.
