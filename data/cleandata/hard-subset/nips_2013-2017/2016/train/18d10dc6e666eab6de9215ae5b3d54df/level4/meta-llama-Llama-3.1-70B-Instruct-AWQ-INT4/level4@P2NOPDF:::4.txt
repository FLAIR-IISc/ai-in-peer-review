This paper explores the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on scenarios where evaluating function values is time-consuming. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, q-KG, capable of proposing multiple new points for parallel evaluation, and provide an efficient approach to evaluate this function. Empirical results are presented to validate their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed in this paper is significant, as the ability to distribute work in a parallel manner can substantially enhance the performance of data-driven applications. Instead of evaluating one point at a time, the algorithm leverages q knowledge gradients to propose q points for evaluation in the next iteration, significantly accelerating the algorithm's efficiency. This concept is intriguing and warrants further exploration.

However, it remains unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is suitable, particularly in neural network architectures where the input dimension is extremely large. Providing theoretical results on the error bounds of their method and a quantitative analysis of the complexity with respect to dimension would be beneficial. Nevertheless, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization, with a well-founded method and sound experimental design. The paper is well-written overall.

* Line 104: Please specify the input for μ^n.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field, and addressing these minor suggestions will further improve its quality.
