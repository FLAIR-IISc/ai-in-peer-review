- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization method extending the Knowledge Gradient to parallel settings with IPA-based gradient estimation for efficient optimization.
- Strong decision-theoretic motivation: selects one-step Bayes-optimal batches, providing a principled alternative to heuristic or greedy batch methods.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance in noisy evaluation settings compared to existing parallel BO methods.
- Addresses a practically important problem (parallel hyperparameter tuning) with clear presentation and well-written technical exposition.
- Unclear novelty relative to Wang et al. (2015): both use IPA for batch acquisition optimization, so need to clarify contribution beyond applying IPA to KG vs. EI.
- Comparison to "parallel EI in MOE" is ambiguous—must state whether this implements Wang et al.'s q-EI or a heuristic EI to avoid confusion.
- Experiments use only q=4; scalability and computational overhead as q increases (e.g., q=8,16,32) are not evaluated.
- Lacks wall-clock time comparisons; current plots using "iterations" do not reflect acquisition optimization overhead versus real-time speedups.
- Missing baseline comparisons such as parallel random search and sequential BO in wall-clock terms to quantify practical gains.
- Method appears synchronous; paper does not discuss extending q-KG to asynchronous evaluation scenarios common in practice.
- Experimental reporting needs clarity: define "iterations" (batches vs. function evaluations) and explain why curves don't share initial design starting points.
- Summary pro: well-motivated, technically sound, and strong for noisy problems; presentation is clear.
- Summary con: insufficient experimental validation (scalability, wall-clock, key baseline q-EI) and unclear novelty reduce practical assessment of impact.
- Minor: possible notation error in Equation (4.1) regarding y(z(1:q)) conditioning.
- Minor: grammar fix on Page 7 ("achieve" -> "achieves") and citation phrasing in related work.
- Minor: cite Wang et al. (2015) as a more direct reference for IPA applied to batch BO.
