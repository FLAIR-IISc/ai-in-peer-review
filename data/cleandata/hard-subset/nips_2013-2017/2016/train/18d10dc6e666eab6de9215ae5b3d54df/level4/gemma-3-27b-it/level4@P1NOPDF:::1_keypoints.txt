- Introduces q-KG, a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient criterion for Bayesian optimization with detailed computation analogous to standard KG.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Paper is strong and merits NIPS publication; parallelizing BO has significant potential impact.  
- Missing analysis of actual speed-ups achieved by moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Literature review needs more precision: integrating EI over posteriors was explored in “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” and CL-mix was presented by Chevalier et al.  
- Prior work on differentiating multipoint Expected Improvement using the natural gradient should be cited/discussed.  
- Question: Is the domain A assumed compact and is f assumed continuous?  
- Question: Why restrict the initial design A to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS); what motivated this choice?  
- Question: Does Algorithm 1 include GP hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating of the GP?  
- Clarification needed in Section 5.2: is g defined as a maximization or minimization problem?  
- Concern: Smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor are not self-evident and need further justification.  
- Correction: paper states Matérn is 2/5, but it should be 5/2.  
- Suggestion: For Figure 1 experiments, consider fitting the same GPs using different software packages to verify consistency and robustness.
