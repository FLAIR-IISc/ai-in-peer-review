- Proposes a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method selecting Bayes-optimal batches using the q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG).  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients and maximize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noiseless benchmarks and outperforms others under observation noise.  
- Experimental evaluation lacks wall-clock time comparisons to quantify practical gains over simpler baselines (random search, sequential BO).  
- No justification is given for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Core components (knowledge gradient and IPA) are not novel; paper should better distinguish contributions from prior work.  
- Needs clearer comparison and distinction from Wang et al. (2015) and related parallel EI methods (recommend renaming baseline to q-EI).  
- Method is potentially impactful for parallel hyperparameter tuning in many domains.  
- Technical background and experiment descriptions are generally clear and well-presented.  
- Requests comparison to Wang et al. (2015) and clarification whether the "parallel EI in MOE" baseline corresponds to q-EI.  
- Asks about scalability to larger parallelism (e.g., q = 10, 25, 800) and expected performance with increased parallel runs.  
- Questions whether the synchronous method can be extended to an asynchronous setting to handle staggered worker completion.  
- Requests wall-clock time comparisons and quantification of computational overhead per batch selection.  
- Asks for clarification of the x-axis "iterations" (batches vs individual evaluations) and whether initial design is included and why methods start at different performance.  
- Minor comments: possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); several grammar issues; inappropriate citation for origin of IPA.
