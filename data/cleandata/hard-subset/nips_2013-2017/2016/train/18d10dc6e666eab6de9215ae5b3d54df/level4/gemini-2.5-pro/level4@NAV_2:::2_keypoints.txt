- Proposes q-KG, a novel parallel extension of the knowledge gradient that picks a Bayes-optimal batch of q points for one-step lookahead.
- Contrasts the decision-theoretic q-KG with heuristic parallelizations of EI and UCB, and situates it among joint batch optimization methods.
- Introduces an efficient gradient-based optimizer for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to make batch selection practical.
- Originality/significance: principled, theoretically grounded approach to parallel Bayesian optimization relevant for large-scale hyperparameter tuning.
- Technical quality: sound theoretical development and a meaningful technical contribution in the IPA-based gradient estimator.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, clearly structured, and explains motivation and prior work relationships effectively.
- Weakness: assumes independent, normally distributed measurement noise, which may be inappropriate for hyperparameter tuning with structured or biased validation noise.
- Weakness: uses test set error as the optimization metric, which can be suboptimal for hyperparameter tuning compared to log-loss or AUC.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is narrow—only MNIST and CIFAR-10, two models (logistic regression, CNN), and one task (classification).
- Suggestion: broaden experiments (e.g., HPOlib benchmarks) and test more tasks/models to better demonstrate generality and practical impact.
- Overall: theoretically elegant and promising method, but empirical validation needs significant expansion to fully substantiate practical advantages.
