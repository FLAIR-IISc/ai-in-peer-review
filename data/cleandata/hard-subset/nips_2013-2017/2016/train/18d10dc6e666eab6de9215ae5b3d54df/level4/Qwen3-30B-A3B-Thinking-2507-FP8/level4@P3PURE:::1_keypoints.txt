- Introduces a batch-sequential Bayesian optimization method based on a parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion.  
- Formally defines q-KG and details its computation following the standard KG framework.  
- Extensive numerical experiments show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian optimization methods.  
- Paper is a strong contribution with significant potential societal impact by enabling efficient parallelization of Bayesian optimization.  
- Minor concern: no analysis of computational speedups from sequential to batch-sequential execution.  
- Minor concern: literature review contains inaccuracies—EI integration with respect to posterior was previously presented in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization," CL-mix was introduced by Chevalier et al., and q-EI via natural gradient has been studied.  
- Clarification requested: is the domain A assumed compact and is f continuous?  
- Question: why restrict A to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) at initialization?  
- Question: does Algorithm 1 include hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating?  
- Question: in Section 5.2, is the definition of g for maximization or minimization?  
- Comment: the smoothness of g requires justification and does not follow immediately.  
- Comment: the derivative of the Cholesky factor is non-trivial and merits discussion.  
- Correction: the Matérn kernel should be specified as 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Question: for Figure 1 experiments, could the same Gaussian processes have been fitted using alternative software?  
- Overall recommendation: high-quality paper deserving publication at NIPS.
