This paper presents a batch formulation of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) acquisition function. To optimize the selection of a batch of design points, the authors introduce a Monte-Carlo based gradient estimation technique. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that the proposed method shows superior performance compared to other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) baselines.

1.  The primary concern is the positioning of this work with respect to existing literature. The concept of batch KG is not novel, with prior art such as [1] and [2] having explored this area. The authors should explicitly clarify the distinct contributions of their work in light of these previous publications. It appears the main contribution lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for finding the optimal batch. If this is the case, the paper's claims would be significantly strengthened by a direct empirical or analytical comparison against the optimization approaches used in the prior batch KG literature to validate the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed estimator.

2.  Section 5.2, which describes the core methodology, seems to be the central technical contribution of the paper. This section is currently underdeveloped and would benefit from significant elaboration to provide readers with a clearer and more comprehensive understanding.

3.  The experimental comparison with Expected Improvement (EI) in the noisy setting (Section 6.2) raises a concern about fairness, as the standard EI formulation assumes noiseless observations. Please clarify whether a noise-aware variant of EI was used. If the standard, noiseless version of EI was used, this comparison is potentially misleading. The results should then be discussed with the explicit acknowledgment that EI is not designed to handle observation noise, which would explain its underperformance.

4.  A minor point: The abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162. Please ensure abbreviations are defined only at their first appearance.
