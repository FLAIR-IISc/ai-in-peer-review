- Introduces q-KG, a batch acquisition function derived from one-step Bayes-optimality for minimizing the posterior mean.  
- Develops an efficient computational strategy using Monte Carlo estimation and an unbiased stochastic gradient via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Enables gradient-based optimization of a non-greedy batch acquisition, extending sequential KG to the parallel setting.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, particularly outperforming baselines in noisy settings.  
- Code released, aiding reproducibility.  

- Positioning versus prior batch KG work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) is not clearly distinguished; needs clearer statement of novelty and comparative applicability.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation via IPA) is terse; needs more intermediate steps and intuition for self-containedness.  
- Baseline comparisons in noisy experiments may be unfair—should compare to EI variants that account for noise and clarify which EI was used.  
- Minor readability issue: "IPA" is redefined in Section 5.2; remove duplicate definition.  

- Overall: solid, principled, and effective contribution whose impact would increase with clearer prior-work positioning and fairer noisy baselines.
