

This paper develops a batch version of Knowledge Gradient (KG). To identify an optimal batch of design points, it proposes a gradient estimation technique utilizing Monte-Carlo simulation. Experimental results indicate the proposed method surpasses existing batch Bayesian optimization (BO) approaches.  
1. The batch KG framework is not novel as presented in this paper. Clarify the specific contribution relative to prior batch KG works such as [1] and [2]. The core contribution appears to be the gradient estimation method for batch optimization; if so, the efficiency and effectiveness of this method must be rigorously compared against previous batch KG methods through experiments or mathematical analysis.  
2. Section 5.2 requires substantial elaboration, as it constitutes the paper's key technical contribution.  
3. Expected Improvement (EI) fundamentally assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, comparisons in noisy settings are inherently unfair. Specify whether a noise-aware variant of EI was employed; if plain EI was used, Section 6.2 must address this limitation and discuss why EI's noise insensitivity impacts the results.  
4. The abbreviation "IPA" is unnecessarily redefined in line 162.
