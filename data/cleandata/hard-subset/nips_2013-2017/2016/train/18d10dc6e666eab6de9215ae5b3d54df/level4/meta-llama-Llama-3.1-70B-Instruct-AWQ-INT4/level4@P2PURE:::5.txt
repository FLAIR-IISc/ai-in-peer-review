This paper develops a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method. To identify an optimal batch of design points, the authors propose a gradient estimation approach based on Monte-Carlo simulation. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

However, several concerns need to be addressed to strengthen the paper. Firstly, the concept of batch KG is not novel, as it has been explored in previous studies such as [1] and [2]. Therefore, it is essential to clarify the specific contribution of this paper relative to existing batch KG literature. In my opinion, the primary contribution lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for finding the optimal batch. To solidify this claim, the authors should compare the efficiency and effectiveness of their approach with previous batch KG methods through experiments or mathematical analysis.

Secondly, Section 5.2 appears to be a crucial component of the paper, and I recommend expanding on this section to provide more insight. 

Thirdly, since Expected Improvement (EI) assumes noiseless observations, the comparison in the noisy setting may be unfair. I would like to know whether a noise-aware variant of EI is employed in the experiments. If the standard EI is used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed in the context of EI's limitation in handling noisy observations.

Lastly, the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162, which may cause confusion. To maintain consistency, the authors should ensure that the definition is consistent throughout the paper.
