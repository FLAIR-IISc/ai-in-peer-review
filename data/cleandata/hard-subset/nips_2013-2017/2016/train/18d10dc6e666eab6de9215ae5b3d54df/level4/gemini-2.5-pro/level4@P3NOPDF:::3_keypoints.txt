- Addresses optimizing multivariate objectives over a finite domain in Bayesian optimization, focusing on parallel/batch evaluations.
- Proposes q-KG: an extension of the Knowledge Gradient policy to select multiple points per iteration using stochastic gradient optimization.
- Core challenge: selecting a batch of q points to maximize expected improvement is computationally intractable.
- Solution uses Monte Carlo simulation and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition function.
- Empirical results show the algorithm is competitive with, and sometimes outperforms, state-of-the-art methods (notably UCB-based approaches).
- Strength: practically relevant for leveraging parallel computing in Bayesian optimization.
- Strength: paper is well-written with clear problem formulation and motivation.
- Strength: convincing experimental evaluation demonstrating strong performance.
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is incremental—main idea is an extension of existing KG to batch settings.
- Weakness: technical use of IPA and stochastic gradient estimation is standard, making the contribution more a skillful combination of known methods than a new paradigm.
- Specific suggestion: clarify notation in Equation (3.1)—explicitly define μ(x^(1:n)) as the vector of means and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) as the corresponding n×n covariance matrix.
