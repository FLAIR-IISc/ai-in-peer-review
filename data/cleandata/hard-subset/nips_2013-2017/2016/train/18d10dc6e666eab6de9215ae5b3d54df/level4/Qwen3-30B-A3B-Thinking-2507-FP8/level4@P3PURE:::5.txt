

This paper proposes a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) policy, introducing a Monte Carlo-based gradient estimation method to identify optimal batches of design points. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method achieves superior performance compared to existing batch Bayesian optimization (BO) approaches. However, the following concerns require clarification:

1.  **Contribution Clarification:** The batch KG framework has been previously explored in works such as [1] and [2]. The authors must explicitly delineate the specific contribution of this paper relative to these prior studies. If the core contribution lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for batch selection, the paper must provide either experimental comparisons (with the prior batch KG methods) or a mathematical analysis demonstrating the efficiency or effectiveness gains of this estimation approach.

2.  **Section 5.2 Elaboration:** Section 5.2, which details the proposed gradient estimation method, constitutes the paper's key technical innovation. This section requires significant elaboration to clearly explain the methodology, its theoretical justification, and how it specifically addresses the challenges of batch KG optimization.

3.  **Noise Assumption in EI Comparison:** The standard Expected Improvement (EI) acquisition function assumes noiseless observations. Consequently, comparing the proposed method against standard EI in noisy experimental settings is potentially misleading. The authors must clarify whether a noise-aware variant of EI (e.g., EI for noisy observations) was used in the experiments. If the standard noiseless EI was employed, the results in Section 6.2 should be explicitly discussed, acknowledging that the comparison may disadvantage the proposed method due to EI's lack of noise modeling.
