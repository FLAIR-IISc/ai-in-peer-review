- Proposes a new Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects multiple sampling points per iteration for parallel evaluation on a finite multivariate domain.  
- Generalizes the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to a batch setting (q-KG) to choose sets of sampling points.  
- Addresses computational intractability by using Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for optimizing the batch criterion.  
- Algorithm empirically outperforms or strongly competes with competing methods (mainly Confidence-Bound approaches) on several test problems.  
- Paper is well written, technically sound, clear, and well organized.  
- Main novelty is limited: primarily an extension of KG to batches, with the gradient-estimation via IPA being the main technical contribution.  
- IPA-based q-KG gradient estimation is standard, so overall methodological novelty is not very high.  
- Raises a clarity issue: equation (3.1) needs explanation of how mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) are defined for sequences of vectors.  
- Significance: useful contribution for expensive-to-evaluate objectives with parallelization potential.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address the noted clarity/notation issues.
