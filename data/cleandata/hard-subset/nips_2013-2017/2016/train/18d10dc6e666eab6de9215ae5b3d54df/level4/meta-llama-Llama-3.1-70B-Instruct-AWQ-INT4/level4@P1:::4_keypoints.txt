- Proposes q-KG, a new acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization that outputs q points for parallel evaluation and gives an efficient method to evaluate q-KG.  
- Provides empirical results that support the theoretical claims.  
- Addresses an important problem: enabling distributed/parallel evaluations to speed up expensive function measurements.  
- q-KG’s strategy of proposing q points at each iteration can significantly accelerate optimization versus sequential selection.  
- Concerns about the suitability of the Gaussian process assumption for high-dimensional inputs, e.g., neural network architectures.  
- Requests theoretical error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.  
- Overall assessment: method is sound, experiments are well-designed, and the paper is well-written.  
- Minor clarity and notation issues: awkward phrasing (line 36), undefined A (line 95), unclear input for mu^n (line 104).  
- Suggest briefly discussing the parallel EI algorithm and using different notation for A (lines 128 and 140).  
- Recommendations for improvement: add theoretical error/complexity results, consider alternative assumptions to GP, and fix notation/clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: accept for publication, conditional on addressing minor issues and strengthening theoretical analysis.
