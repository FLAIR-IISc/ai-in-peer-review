- Proposes a novel parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization that generalizes knowledge gradient to parallel settings.  
- Strong theoretical contribution and elegant parallelization compared to existing approaches.  
- Method computes Bayes-optimal batches, which is promising for practical optimization.  
- Demonstrates improved performance over existing Bayesian optimization methods, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors, which may be problematic due to structural bias in hyperparameter optimization.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and not fully convincing.  
- Uses test set error as the optimization criterion, which is suboptimal for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Recommends using more robust metrics (e.g., AUC or log loss) instead of threshold-based test error.  
- Benchmarks are limited to MNIST and CIFAR10 and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), lacking diversity.  
- Suggests benchmarking with HPOlib and expanding the variety of datasets and learning algorithms.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to strengthen experiments and address the error-distribution assumption.
