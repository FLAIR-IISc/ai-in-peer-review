- Proposes q-KG, a new parallel knowledge gradient acquisition function that selects q points per batch and an efficient method to compute it; empirical results support theoretical claims.
- Addresses an important problem: enabling parallel/distributed evaluations to improve optimization performance and wall-clock efficiency.
- q-KG can substantially accelerate optimization by evaluating multiple points in parallel when resources permit.
- Concern that Gaussian process priors may be inappropriate in some real-world settings, notably high-dimensional neural network tasks.
- Recommends adding theoretical guarantees (error/regret) and quantitative analysis of computational and statistical complexity vs. dimensionality to clarify applicability.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution, method appears sound, experiments convincing, and paper is generally well written.
