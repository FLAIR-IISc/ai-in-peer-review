- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects Bayes-optimal batches and empirically outperforms existing BO methods.  
- Manuscript is clear, well organized, and the method is practically important and more principled than many alternatives.  
- The ability to choose a Bayes-optimal batch is a particularly compelling contribution.  
- Method assumes independent, normally distributed observation noise; this assumption can be violated in hyperparameter optimization where empirical estimates (e.g., CV) induce systematic, non-independent bias, especially with small samples—should be acknowledged.  
- Theoretical development is strong and substantial.  
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing for three reasons: using test-set error as the optimization target is problematic—metrics like AUC or log loss would be more appropriate.  
- Experimental evaluation should include HPOlib, a standard HPO benchmarking suite.  
- Benchmarks are limited (only MNIST and CIFAR-10, one task—classification, and two algorithms—logistic regression and CNN); greater dataset, task, and model diversity is needed.  
- Overall assessment: strong theory and appealing method, but empirical evidence is too limited to be fully persuasive.
