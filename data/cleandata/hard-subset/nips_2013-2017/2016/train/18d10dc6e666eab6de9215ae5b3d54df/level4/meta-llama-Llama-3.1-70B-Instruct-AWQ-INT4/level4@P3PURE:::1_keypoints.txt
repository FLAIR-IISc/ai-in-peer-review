- Introduces a novel batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion for Bayesian optimization.
- Provides thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes, and defines/computes q-KG mirroring standard KG.
- Numerical experiments show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods.
- Paper is well-written, impactful, and suitable for publication.
- Missing evaluation of runtime/speed-ups gained by moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.
- Literature review needs clarification: prior work integrated EI over posteriors (e.g., "Kriging is well-suited..." and "Differentiating the multipoint EI...") and Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix in [2].
- Question: Is the objective function f assumed compact and continuous over the domain A?
- Question: Why is the initial set A restricted to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS)?
- Question: Does Algorithm 1 perform hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating?
- Question: In Section 5.2, is g defined as a maximization or minimization problem?
- Concern: Smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor are assumed but not justified.
- Typo/notation: Matérn kernel parameter should be 5/2, not 2/5.
- Experimental detail: Could the same Gaussian processes for Figure 1 be fitted using different software packages?
