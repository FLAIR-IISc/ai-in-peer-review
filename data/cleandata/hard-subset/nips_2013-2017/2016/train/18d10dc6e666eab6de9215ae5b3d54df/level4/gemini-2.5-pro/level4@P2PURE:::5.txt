This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) policy for Bayesian optimization. To optimize the selection of points in a batch, the authors propose a gradient estimation method based on Monte-Carlo simulation. The experiments show that this approach outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization methods.

1.  The novelty of this work requires clarification. To my knowledge, the batch KG policy is not new and has been explored in prior work (e.g., [1, 2]). The authors should explicitly state the contributions of this paper in relation to this previous literature. My understanding is that the primary contribution is the proposed method for estimating the gradient to find the optimal batch. If this is correct, the paper must compare the efficiency and effectiveness of this optimization approach against the methods used in previous batch KG papers, providing either experimental evidence or mathematical analysis.

2.  Section 5.2, which appears to describe the core technical contribution, should be elaborated upon. More detail is needed for the reader to fully understand the proposed method.

3.  The experimental comparison with Expected Improvement (EI) in the noisy setting (Section 6.2) seems unfair, as the standard EI policy assumes noiseless observations. Was a noise-aware variant of EI used in the experiments? If the standard version of EI was used, the results in Section 6.2 must be discussed in the context of this limitation, acknowledging that EI is not designed to handle observation noise.

4.  A minor correction: the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162.
