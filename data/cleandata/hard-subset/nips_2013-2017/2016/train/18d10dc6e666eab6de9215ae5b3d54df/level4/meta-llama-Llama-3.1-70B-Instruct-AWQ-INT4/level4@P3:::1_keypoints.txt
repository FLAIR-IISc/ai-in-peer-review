- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), derived from decision-theoretic analysis.  
- Provides thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes and clearly defines the q-KG criterion.  
- Details computation of q-KG and presents numerical experiments where q-KG often outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods.  
- Paper is well-written; authors show strong understanding and appropriately use GPs with an ARD Matérn 5/2 kernel.  
- Experiments cover synthetic and real-world problems and compare against multiple leading algorithms.  
- q-KG is especially competitive in noisy settings.  
- Does not study actual parallel speed-ups gained by batching the BO algorithm.  
- Some literature review statements are slightly imprecise; prior work on integrating EI, CL-mix, and differentiating multipoint EI should be acknowledged.  
- Clarify mathematical assumptions: is domain A compact and is f continuous?  
- Justify restriction of initial design A to an LHS (Latin hypercube sample).  
- Algorithm 1 omits discussion of hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating.  
- Ambiguity in Section 5.2: unclear whether g is a max or a min; need to clarify smoothness of g and derivative of the Cholesky factor.  
- Reproducibility concern: provide details on GP fitting and whether the same GPs could be fit using different software for Figure 1.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution with strong results; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.
