- Proposes a batch optimization algorithm combining stochastic gradient methods with Bayesian modeling for multivariate objectives over finite domains.  
- Key innovation: parallelizable selection of multiple sampling points per iteration, extending the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to batches.  
- Generalizes KG to select sets simultaneously by estimating expected improvement from adding multiple points.  
- Addresses computational intractability using Monte Carlo sampling and infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for the q-KG criterion.  
- Empirical benchmarks show the method outperforms or competes strongly with existing approaches, particularly Confidence Bounds.  
- Paper is well-structured and presents a method with clear practical relevance.  
- Main critique: limited novelty—batch extension of KG is a natural generalization and IPA-based gradient estimation is standard, so the adaptation may not be a substantial advance.  
- Technical concern: notation in Eq. (3.1) is ambiguous—unclear how μ(x^{(1:n)}) and K(x^{(1:n)}, x^{(1:n)}) are defined for sequences; request clarification or adjusted notation.
