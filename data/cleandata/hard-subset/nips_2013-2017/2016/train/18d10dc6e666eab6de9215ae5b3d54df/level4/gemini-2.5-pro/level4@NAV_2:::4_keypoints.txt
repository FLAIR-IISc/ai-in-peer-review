- Proposes q-KG, a new batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function that selects q points by maximizing one-step expected improvement in final solution quality.
- Extends sequential Knowledge Gradient and contrasts with greedy batch methods (e.g., EI, UCB) by jointly optimizing the entire batch.
- Introduces an efficient gradient-based optimizer using infinitesimal perturbation analysis to make q-KG computationally feasible.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic benchmarks and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations; code is released for reproducibility.
- Addresses a significant problem (accelerating expensive black-box optimization) with clear practical relevance for large-scale hyperparameter tuning.
- Novelty: principled, decision-theoretic batch extension of KG and non-greedy joint batch optimization.
- Quality: technically sound method with comprehensive comparisons to state-of-the-art parallel BO algorithms.
- Weakness: primarily empirical—lacks theoretical analysis of complexity, convergence, or regret guarantees.
- Scalability concern: reliance on Gaussian Processes and search-space discretization may not scale well to very high-dimensional problems.
- Clarity issues: some minor points could be clarified to improve readability and presentation.
- Suggestion: discuss computational overhead of optimizing q-KG versus baselines as batch size q increases.
- Suggestion: acknowledge GP limitations in high dimensions and mention possible remedies (e.g., random embeddings, structured kernels).
- Minor editorial suggestions: briefly describe parallel EI when first mentioned and use distinct notation for the discretized domain An versus the full domain A.
