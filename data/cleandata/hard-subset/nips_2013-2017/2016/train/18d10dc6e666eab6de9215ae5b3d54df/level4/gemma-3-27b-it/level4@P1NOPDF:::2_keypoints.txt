- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches, showing improved performance and presenting an elegant, well-written approach.  
- Determination of a Bayes-optimal batch is particularly promising.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors — a common GP assumption but potentially unrealistic for HPO due to measurement noise reflecting generalization vs empirical estimates and structural bias, especially with small samples.  
- Theoretical results are strong.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing because: (a) it optimizes test set error, which is a suboptimal criterion for HPO (prefer AUC, log loss, etc.); (b) uses only MNIST and CIFAR-10 instead of broader benchmarks like HPOlib; (c) relies on only two datasets, one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), lacking diversity.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution but the limited benchmark makes the empirical claims less convincing.
