This paper presents a novel batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization approach based on a parallelized Knowledge Gradient criterion (q-KG). The authors provide necessary background on related work and Gaussian processes before detailing the formulation and computation of q-KG, which builds upon the standard KG framework.  The empirical evaluation demonstrates promising performance, with q-KG outperforming several existing state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization algorithms across a range of experiments. Overall, this is a strong contribution with the potential to significantly impact the field, given the importance of efficient parallelization in Bayesian optimization. 

A minor point for consideration is the lack of analysis regarding the speed-ups achieved through batch-sequential optimization compared to sequential approaches. Additionally, the literature review contains a few imprecisions; for example, the integration of Expected Improvement (EI) with respect to posterior distributions was previously explored in the work by Frazier et al. ("Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization") alongside Constant Liar, and Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix in [2]. Furthermore, the use of natural gradients for q-EI maximization is discussed in Hernández-Lobato et al. ("Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design").
