1. Addresses parallelizing Bayesian optimization with a novel algorithm for constructing Bayes-optimal batches.  
2. Paper is clearly written and the batch-construction approach is elegant and promising.  
3. Method appropriately assumes Gaussian-process errors with independent normal noise.  
4. That GP/independent-noise assumption may be unrealistic in hyperparameter optimization where CV estimates show dependencies and structural biases—this mismatch should be discussed.  
5. Theoretical contributions are strong.  
6. Experimental evaluation is problematic: using test-set error as the sole optimization criterion is inappropriate for hyperparameter tuning.  
7. More robust evaluation metrics (e.g., AUC, log loss) should be used in addition to or instead of test error.  
8. Benchmarks are narrow—only MNIST and CIFAR-10—so broader HPO benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) would enable fairer comparisons.  
9. Scope is limited (two datasets, one task type, two learning algorithms), restricting generalizability across learning algorithms and search spaces.  
10. Overall: valuable and promising contribution, but experimental breadth must be expanded to substantiate claims of superiority.
