- Proposes a batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization method centered on a novel parallel (q-KG) Knowledge Gradient criterion.  
- Provides background on Gaussian processes and related BO work.  
- Formally defines and details computation of the q-KG criterion as an extension of standard KG.  
- Numerical experiments show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods.  
- Paper is technically rigorous, relevant, and merits NIPS publication; highlights societal benefits of parallelizing BO.  
- Minor critique: no analysis of computational speed-up when moving from sequential to batch execution.  
- Minor critique: literature review has imprecisions—EI with posterior integration and CL-mix were previously addressed (e.g., “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” and Chevalier et al.); q-EI natural-gradient work also exists.  
- Q1: Is the domain A assumed compact and is f continuous?  
- Q2: Why restrict A to a Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS) at initialization?  
- Q3: Algorithm 1 omits hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating—how are GP hyperparameters handled?  
- Q4: In Section 5.2, is the function g defined for maximization or minimization?  
- Q5: The claimed smoothness of g requires justification—this is not obvious.  
- Q6: The derivative of the Cholesky factor is non-trivial—has this been derived or explained?  
- P7: Matérn kernel parameter is misstated as “2/5”; it should be 5/2.  
- Q8: For Figure 1 experiments, could equivalent Gaussian Processes be fitted using alternative GP software?
