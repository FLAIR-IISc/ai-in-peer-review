- Proposes a stochastic optimization algorithm using Bayesian modeling that selects batches of points for parallel evaluation over a finite domain.  
- Extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework from single-point to batch selection.  
- Direct maximization of the batch KG criterion is intractable.  
- Develops a Monte Carlo + Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) method to estimate the gradient of the batch KG.  
- Uses the gradient estimates to enable gradient-based optimization for selecting batch candidates.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms or is competitive with existing approaches, especially UCB-based methods.  
- Paper is well-written and appears practically promising.  
- Main reservation: degree of novelty is limited—extension of KG and use of IPA are non-trivial but rely on established techniques.  
- Clarity suggestion: notation mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) is ambiguous; explicitly define them (e.g., posterior mean vector and covariance matrix at the points).
