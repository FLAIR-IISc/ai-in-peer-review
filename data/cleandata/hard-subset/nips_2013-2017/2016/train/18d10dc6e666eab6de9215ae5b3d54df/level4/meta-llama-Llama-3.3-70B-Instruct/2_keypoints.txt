- Proposes a novel parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches, showing clear improvement over sequential approaches.  
- Determination of a Bayes-optimal batch is particularly promising and theoretically appealing.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written.  
- Parallelizing Bayesian optimization is practically important, and the approach is more refined than many existing methods.  
- The assumption of independent normally distributed errors (common in GP-based BO) may be problematic due to measurement error structure and small-sample bias in HPO.  
- Theoretical foundation is robust.  
- Experimental methodology is weak: using test-set error as the optimization objective is questionable; metrics like AUC or log loss would be more appropriate.  
- Benchmark choices are limited (only MNIST and CIFAR10); using HPOlib would provide more comprehensive benchmarking.  
- Benchmark scope is narrow—single task (classification) and only two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN); need more diverse tasks and models since optimizer performance varies.  
- More extensive and varied experiments are needed to fully demonstrate the method’s effectiveness; prior work supports the need for nuanced evaluation metrics and benchmarking.
