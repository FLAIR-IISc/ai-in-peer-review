- Proposes a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method using the q-KG acquisition and infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate its gradient for batch selection.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive with existing parallel BO in noise-free settings and outperforms them under observation noise.  
- Motivating application (multi-core hyperparameter tuning) is compelling, but several methodological and experimental clarifications are needed.  
- Technical validation uses standard benchmark functions and CNN/logistic regression on CIFAR10 and MNIST.  
- Lacks wall-clock time measurements or comparisons against simpler baselines (random search or sequential BO) to quantify practical gains.  
- Unclear rationale for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Novelty limited: combines established KG with IPA, and prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) already applied IPA for batch selection with parallel EI.  
- Questions about scalability to large numbers of parallel runs and empirical behavior as q increases.  
- Current method is synchronous; authors should discuss feasibility of asynchronous execution.  
- Need to report computational overhead for selecting new batches and wall-clock performance relative to alternatives.  
- Plots have ambiguous x-axis labeling (iterations: evaluations or batches? includes initial design?) and inconsistent starting points across methods.  
- Paper is well-written with clear technical details and comprehensive experimental comparisons.  
- Minor issues: missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammatical fixes; avoid using parenthetical references as nouns; clarify correct citation for IPA origin vs application.
