- Proposes a novel parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Paper is well written, clear, and presents the approach elegantly.  
- Parallelization of BO is practically important and the Bayes-optimal batch determination is promising.  
- Theoretical contributions are strong and well-developed.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed observation errors, which may be unrealistic given structural bias and dependent errors in hyperparameter tuning.  
- Experiments are disappointing and limited in scope.  
- Using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; metrics like AUC or log loss would be better.  
- Benchmarks use only MNIST and CIFAR10 instead of broader HPOlib-style suites.  
- Only two datasets and two learning algorithms (logistic regression, CNN) restrict generality and task diversity.  
- Some optimizers vary by task/algorithm, so the limited benchmark may not reveal true performance differences.  
- Overall recommendation: theoretical value is high but empirical evaluation is too narrow to be fully convincing.  
- Suggested improvements: expand benchmarks across more datasets and algorithms, use alternative performance metrics, and discuss limitations of the independent Gaussian error assumption.
