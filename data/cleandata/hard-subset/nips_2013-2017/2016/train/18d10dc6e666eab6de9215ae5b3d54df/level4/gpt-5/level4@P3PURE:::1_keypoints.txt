- Paper introduces q-KG, a batch-sequential (parallel) extension of the Knowledge Gradient for Bayesian optimization; technically solid and well written.
- Strength: Principled extension of KG to the batch setting with computation aligned to classical KG.
- Strength: Strong empirical study showing q-KG outperforms established batch BO baselines.
- Strength: Clear practical impact due to importance of parallel experimentation.
- Weakness: No study of wall-clock speed-ups or scaling with batch size — practical runtime benefits are unassessed.
- Weakness: Literature review contains imprecisions/omissions that should be corrected to properly position the contribution.
- Related work: Integration of EI over posteriors was already discussed in “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” (and Constant Liar).
- Related work: Chevalier et al. presented CL-mix and should be acknowledged.
- Related work: Multipoint EI maximization via natural gradient is studied in “Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design.”
- Question: State assumptions on domain A and continuity of f (e.g., compactness, continuity) explicitly.
- Question: Why restrict initial design to Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS); could other space-filling or adaptive schemes change results?
- Question: How are GP hyperparameters treated in the optimization loop — re-estimated (MLE/MAP), Bayesian-updated, and at what frequency?
- Question: In Section 5.2, should g be a maximum or a minimum? Check sign/convention and correct.
- Question: Provide conditions or references ensuring g is differentiable (a.s. or in expectation) to justify gradient-based q-KG optimization.
- Question: Clarify how derivatives of the Cholesky factor are computed (explicit formula, reverse-mode through Cholesky, or citation/implementation).
- Question: Were the same GP models and hyperparameter learning procedures used across all methods to avoid implementation confounds?
- Minor: Matérn kernel parameter is 5/2, not 2/5 — correct the typo.
- Minor: Ensure consistent max/min notation for g in Section 5.2.
- Overall: Very good paper with principled contribution and strong results; recommend acceptance after revisions addressing related work, assumptions, differentiability, hyperparameter treatment, and runtime reporting.
