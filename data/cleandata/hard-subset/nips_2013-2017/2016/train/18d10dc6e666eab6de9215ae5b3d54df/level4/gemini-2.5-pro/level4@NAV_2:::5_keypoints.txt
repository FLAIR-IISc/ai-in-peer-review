- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function that is one-step Bayes-optimal by maximizing expected final-solution improvement for the last batch.  
- Derives an efficient gradient-based optimization of q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and Monte Carlo sampling to make batch KG practical.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning shows q-KG often outperforms state-of-the-art parallel BO methods, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Code is publicly available, aiding reproducibility.  
- Strength: principled decision-theoretic derivation gives a strong theoretical foundation for the acquisition function.  
- Strength: primary technical contribution—efficient gradient computation for a Monte Carlo-estimated batch acquisition function—is significant and practically important.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across noise-free and noisy settings convincingly demonstrate strong performance, with notable gains in noisy scenarios.  
- Weakness: insufficient positioning and comparison to prior batch KG work (e.g., Wang et al., 2015), leaving novelty relative to existing batch KG unclear.  
- Weakness: Section 5.2 (IPA gradient derivation) is too condensed and needs more intermediate steps for clarity and reproducibility.  
- Weakness: experimental comparison to parallel EI in noisy settings may be unfair if a noise-agnostic EI variant was used; paper should clarify or compare to noise-aware EI.  
- Minor issue: the abbreviation "IPA" is redundantly redefined in the text.  
- For acceptance: principled Bayes-optimal acquisition with a practical optimization method and strong empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: unclear novelty vis-à-vis prior batch KG, insufficient technical detail in the core derivation, and potentially unfair experimental comparisons.
