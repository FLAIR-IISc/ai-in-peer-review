- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Key innovation: identifies multiple evaluation points per iteration to enable parallelization.  
- Main challenge: optimally selecting which sampling points to evaluate.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected improvement and guide sampling decisions.  
- Extends KG to make batch (multi-point) sampling decisions.  
- Addresses computational intractability by using Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients and maximize information gain.  
- Empirical results: competitive performance against confidence-bound-based methods on several test problems.  
- Paper quality: well-written and potentially practically useful.  
- Concern about novelty: essentially a batch extension of KG; main novel part (q-KG gradient estimation via IPA) is useful but relatively standard.  
- Specific technical issue: equation (3.1) notation unclear — mu and K are defined for vectors, not sequences x^(1:n).
