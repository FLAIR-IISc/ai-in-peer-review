This paper proposes the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on scenarios where evaluating the function value is time-consuming. The authors introduce a new acquisition function called q-KG, capable of outputting several new points for parallel evaluation. They also provide an efficient approach to evaluate the q-KG function. Empirical results are presented to verify their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed in this paper is important, as it enables the distribution of work in a way that can significantly improve the performance of data-driven applications. Instead of evaluating one point at a time, the algorithm considers the q knowledge gradient, which proposes q points to evaluate at the next iteration, thereby substantially increasing the algorithm's efficiency. This is an interesting idea worth exploring.

However, it is unclear whether the assumption of a Gaussian process is suitable, particularly in neural network architectures where the input dimension is very large. It would be helpful if the authors could provide some theoretical results on the error bound of their method and a quantitative analysis of the complexity with regard to dimension. Nevertheless, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The method is well-founded, and the experiments are sound. The paper is well-written overall.
