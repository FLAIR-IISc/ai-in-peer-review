- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Method selects multiple evaluation points per iteration to enable parallel computation.  
- Central challenge is optimally choosing sampling points for batch evaluations.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected improvement and guide sampling.  
- Extends KG to a batch setting (q-KG) to select multiple points simultaneously.  
- Exact optimization of the q-KG acquisition is intractable, so they develop a Monte Carlo method with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients.  
- IPA-based gradient estimates are used to maximize information gain over candidate batches.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms or is competitive with existing approaches, particularly confidence-bound methods.  
- Paper is clearly written and appears practically useful.  
- Concern about novelty: extending KG to batches is incremental; the IPA-based gradient estimation, while appropriate, is fairly standard.  
- Specific comment: clarify notation in Eq. (3.1) for μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) since μ and K are defined on vectors, not sequences.
