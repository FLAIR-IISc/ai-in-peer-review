- Proposes a parallel algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal evaluation batches and reportedly outperforms existing parallel Bayesian optimization methods.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation of contributions.  
- Parallelizing Bayesian optimization is practically important, and the proposed method is notably elegant.  
- The ability to compute Bayes-optimal batches is a particularly promising contribution.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (standard for GPs), which may be problematic in hyperparameter tuning due to structural bias from empirical performance estimates.  
- Authors should acknowledge that the independence/error assumptions may not hold in practice, especially with small sample sizes.  
- Experimental flaw: using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic and can bias model selection; metrics like AUC or log-likelihood would be more appropriate.  
- Evaluation should include established hyperparameter optimization benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) for fairer, more rigorous comparisons.  
- Experimental scope is limited (only MNIST and CIFAR-10, one task, two model types); broader tasks, datasets, and algorithms are needed to validate generality.  
- Summary: strong theoretical contribution but empirical validation is too limited; more robust experiments are required to be fully convincing.
