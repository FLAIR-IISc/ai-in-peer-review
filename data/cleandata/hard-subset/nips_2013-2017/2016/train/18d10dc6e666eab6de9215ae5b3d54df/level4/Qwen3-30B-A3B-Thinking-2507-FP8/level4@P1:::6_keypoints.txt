- Paper proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function that minimizes the expected GP minimum.  
- Authors develop an IPA-based gradient estimator to efficiently optimize q-KG for batch selection.  
- Experiments show q-KG outperforms parallel EI and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic and ML hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Strength — Quality: method is theoretically sound and IPA addresses a key computational bottleneck; experiments cover noise-free and noisy benchmarks and practical ML tasks.  
- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-organized with precise definitions, clear algorithm description, and reproducible computational details.  
- Strength — Originality: novel combination of knowledge gradient with IPA for batch optimization, advancing over iterative batch heuristics.  
- Strength — Significance: addresses practical parallel evaluation bottlenecks in hyperparameter tuning with demonstrated real-world gains.  
- Weakness — Quality: lacks wall-clock time and scalability comparisons; unclear how batch computation overhead affects real speedup.  
- Weakness — Originality: insufficient distinction from Wang et al. (2015) which used IPA for batch selection; need explicit clarification of extensions.  
- Weakness — Significance: unexplained choice of q=4; scalability and performance for larger q (e.g., 8–1000) not evaluated.  
- Weakness — Clarity: x-axis in Figures 3–4 is ambiguous (iterations vs. evaluations) and initial design handling differs between methods.  
- Key question 1: How does q-KG differ from or extend Wang et al. (2015), and why wasn’t that work adequately discussed?  
- Key question 2: What scalability results or insights exist for q>4, and how does speedup vs. sequential BO change with q?  
- Key question 3: Can q-KG be adapted to asynchronous evaluation settings, and what is the expected impact on performance?  
- Key question 4: Provide wall-clock runtime comparisons including batch computation overhead versus sequential BO and random search.  
- Key question 5: Clarify the x-axis "iterations" in Section 6.1.2 — does it count batches or function evaluations, and is the initial design included?  
- Minor correction: change "achieve" to "achieves" in Section 6.1.2.  
- Minor correction: cite Chevalier et al. (2014) as the IPA reference rather than Wang et al. (2015).  
- Minor correction: Equation (4.1) appears to be missing z^{(1:n)} — please correct.  
- Overall assessment: strong technical contribution with convincing noisy-case results but needs wall-clock/time-scaling analysis and clearer differentiation from prior IPA work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
