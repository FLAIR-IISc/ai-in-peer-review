- Paper proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using the q-KG acquisition function and uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate q-KG gradients.  
- Experiments show competitive performance on noiseless benchmarks and superior performance under observation noise.  
- Reviewer appreciates parallel hyperparameter optimization but finds some aspects unclear and is unconvinced by the experimental evidence.  
- Technical concern: experiments lack wall-clock time measurements or analysis of practical gains over random search or sequential optimization.  
- Technical concern: no justification for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Novelty concern: method applies known tools (knowledge gradient and IPA); paper does not clearly distinguish itself from prior work such as Wang et al. (2015).  
- Request for explicit comparison to or differentiation from Wang et al. (2015), which uses IPA for batch selection.  
- Scalability concern: reviewer asks about performance and gains for much larger parallelism (e.g., dozens to hundreds of workers).  
- Implementation question: can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous parallel evaluations?  
- Wall-clock/overhead question: what is the runtime overhead for computing each new batch compared to random or sequential methods?  
- Plotting/clarity question: clarify what "iterations" on the x-axis denotes (evaluations vs batches) and whether initial design is included and why starting values differ.  
- Presentation: technical background and experiment descriptions are generally clear and sufficient.  
- Minor comments: possible missing term z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammar fix ("achieves"); rephrase parenthetical references; correct attribution for IPA (Wang et al. (2015) applied IPA but did not introduce it).
