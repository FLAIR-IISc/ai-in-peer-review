- Problem: addresses batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization for expensive-to-evaluate functions.  
- Contribution: proposes q-KG, a novel acquisition function that selects q points jointly for parallel evaluation.  
- Extension: generalizes the knowledge gradient policy to the parallel/batch setting.  
- Main idea: q-KG measures the collective information gain from evaluating a batch of q points.  
- Computational approach: uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to evaluate q-KG efficiently.  
- Empirical results: demonstrates strong performance on synthetic functions and real-world problems versus state-of-the-art.  
- Strength: method is novel.  
- Strength: experimental evaluation is thorough.  
- Strength: writing and mathematical exposition are clear.  
- Weakness: no theoretical error bounds provided for the method.  
- Weakness: lacks quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.  
- Weakness: assumes a Gaussian process prior, which may not suit all (especially high-dimensional) applications.  
- Overall: well-written and solid empirical contribution but limited by missing theory and scalability analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong experiments, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theoretical guarantees, GP assumption, insufficient complexity analysis.
