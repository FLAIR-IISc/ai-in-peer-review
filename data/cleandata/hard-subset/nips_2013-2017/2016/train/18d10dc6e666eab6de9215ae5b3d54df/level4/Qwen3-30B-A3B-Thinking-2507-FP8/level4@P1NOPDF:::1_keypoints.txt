- Introduces q-KG, a batch-sequential variant of the Knowledge Gradient for Bayesian optimization.
- Provides background on Gaussian processes and related work, and formally defines the q-KG criterion and its computation.
- Experimental results show q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods across multiple benchmarks.
- Paper is technically sound, well-motivated, and has strong practical potential for parallelizing optimization.
- Recommendation: paper merits acceptance at NIPS due to rigor and relevance.
- Minor issue: paper does not quantify runtime speedup from parallelization (sequential vs. batch).
- Literature corrections: prior work explored EI integration over posteriors and CL-mix/Constant Liar methods should be cited; q-EI maximization via natural gradients was previously studied and should be referenced.
- Clarification needed: are domain A and objective f assumed compact and continuous for theoretical guarantees?
- Clarification needed: rationale for restricting initial design to Latin hypercube sampling—necessary assumption or practical choice?
- Clarification needed: how and when are GP hyperparameters re-estimated or updated during batch optimization (Algorithm 1 omission)?
- Clarification needed: inconsistency in definition of g (text uses "max" but equation uses "min")—confirm optimization direction.
- Clarification needed: justify the claimed smoothness of g under the stated assumptions.
- Technical detail: provide brief mention of the method/formula used to differentiate the Cholesky factor w.r.t. parameters.
- Typo: Matérn kernel parameter should be 5/2 (not 2/5).
- Reproducibility: state whether GP models were fitted using different software packages to rule out implementation-specific artifacts (e.g., GPy, GPflow, scikit-learn).
