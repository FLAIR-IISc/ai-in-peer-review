This paper develops a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) acquisition function for Bayesian optimization. The proposed method uses a Monte Carlo-based gradient estimation technique to find the optimal batch of points to evaluate simultaneously. The experimental results suggest that this approach outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods. While the paper addresses an important problem and the proposed method shows promise, there are several key areas that require clarification and improvement.

1.  The concept of a batch Knowledge Gradient policy is not new to this work (see e.g., [1, 2]). The authors should more clearly position their contribution in relation to this prior art. The main contribution appears to be the specific Monte Carlo method for estimating the gradient to find the optimal batch. If this is the case, it is crucial to compare the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed gradient estimation method against those used in previous batch KG approaches, either through empirical experiments or mathematical analysis.

2.  Section 5.2, which seems to contain the paper's central technical contribution, should be expanded significantly. As written, it lacks the necessary detail for the reader to fully understand the proposed gradient estimation method and its implementation.

3.  The experimental comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) in the noisy setting (Section 6.2) may be unfair, as the standard EI formulation assumes noiseless observations. Could the authors clarify if a noise-aware variant of EI was used for this comparison? If the standard, noiseless EI was used, this should be stated explicitly, and the results should be discussed in this context. Specifically, the authors should acknowledge that EI's underperformance is expected in this setting, as it is not designed to handle observation noise.

4.  The abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162.
