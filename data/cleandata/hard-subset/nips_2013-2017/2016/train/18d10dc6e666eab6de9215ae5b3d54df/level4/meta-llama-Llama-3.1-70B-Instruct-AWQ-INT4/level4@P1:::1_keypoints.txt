- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm generalizing the knowledge gradient to parallel settings.  
- Provides thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes, defines the q-KG criterion, and details its computation.  
- Presents numerical experiments where q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian optimization methods.  
- Paper is well-written and demonstrates clear understanding; contribution is important given societal impact of parallel BO.  
- Does not evaluate actual speed-ups from parallelization, an important omission.  
- Literature review contains imprecise claims: EI integration, Constant Liar, CL-mix, and q-EI natural gradient prior work should be acknowledged.  
- Unclear whether feasible domain A is compact and whether f is assumed continuous.  
- Questioned rationale for restricting initial A to a Latin hypercube design (LHS).  
- Algorithm 1 does not discuss hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating of GP hyperparameters.  
- In Section 5.2, ambiguity whether g is defined as a maximum or minimum.  
- Smoothness of g and differentiation through the Cholesky factorization appear non-trivial and need justification.  
- Kernel in experiments is actually Matérn 5/2, not “2/5” as stated.  
- Suggests verifying Figure 1 results by fitting the same GPs with different software to rule out implementation dependence.  
- Conclusion: strong paper with minor revisions; suitable for NIPS after addressing comments.
