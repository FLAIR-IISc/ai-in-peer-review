- Paper proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using q-KG (knowledge gradient) and uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for acquisition optimization.  
- Evaluated on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning for CNN/logistic regression (CIFAR-10/MNIST).  
- Experiments report q-KG competitive on noise-free problems and superior under noisy observations, but empirical evidence is not fully convincing.  
- Experiments lack wall-clock time measurements or analysis of practical speedups and do not justify fixing batch size q=4.  
- Novelty limited: using KG and IPA is interesting but both components are known, and prior work (Wang et al. 2015) applied IPA to parallel EI.  
- Parallel hyperparameter tuning is practically important across many domains.  
- Presentation and technical derivations are clear and experimental details are sufficient.  
- Question: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015)/q-EI and explicitly stated differences from that method?  
- Question: How does the method scale with larger q (modern systems often use many more than 4), and what are the practical gains of increased parallelism?  
- Question: Can the synchronous approach be extended to an asynchronous setting?  
- Question: Have you evaluated wall-clock performance versus random search or sequential BO and reported computational overhead to construct a batch?  
- Question: Clarify what “iterations” on the x-axis denotes (batches or evaluations), whether initial design is included, and why methods do not all start at the same value.  
- Minor: Possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq.(4.1); grammar/citation wording fixes (e.g., “achieve”→“achieves”, parenthetical references); Wang et al. (2015) is not the primary IPA reference.
