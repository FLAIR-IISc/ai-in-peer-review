Paper proposes a stochastic-gradient + Bayesian method to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
Method selects multiple evaluation points per iteration to enable parallel sampling.  
Framework builds on the Knowledge Gradient (KG) and generalizes it to the batch (q-KG) setting.  
Because exact optimization is intractable, the authors use Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate q-KG gradients.  
Empirical results show the algorithm outperforms or is competitive with existing methods (especially confidence-bound approaches); paper is well written and practically useful.  
Reviewer questions the level of novelty, viewing this mainly as an extension of KG to batches with a fairly standard IPA-based gradient estimate.  
Specific technical comment: equation (3.1) is unclear about how μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) are defined for sequences of vectors.
