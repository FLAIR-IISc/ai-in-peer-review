- Proposes q-KG, a batch extension of Knowledge Gradient that jointly optimizes a batch to maximize expected reduction in the posterior mean’s minimum under a GP prior.  
- Shows q-KG reduces to parallel EI in the noiseless / sampled-points final recommendation setting.  
- Develops an IPA-based unbiased stochastic gradient estimator and uses Monte Carlo fantasizing to enable gradient-based joint batch optimization.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR-10) show q-KG is competitive or superior to parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Positions work relative to sequential KG and prior parallel acquisition functions, but existing batch/nested-batch KG literature (e.g., Wang et al. 2015; Peng 2010) is not sufficiently compared.  
- Main claimed novelty appears to be an efficient differentiable q-KG objective with an unbiased IPA gradient estimator for continuous domains and a practical discretization scheme; this should be stated explicitly.  
- Reviewer requests empirical comparison or complexity/accuracy analysis against prior discrete batch KG methods to demonstrate computational advantage.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimator) needs much more detail: assumptions for exchanging gradient and expectation, differentiability, uniqueness of minimizer, and tie-handling.  
- Correctness note: ∂µ(n)(x)/∂z should be zero, so two derivative terms in the displayed expression are misleading and must be fixed.  
- Clarify handling of non-differentiability at argmin ties (subgradients vs. measure-zero tie-breaking) and provide convergence behavior of the stochastic optimizer.  
- Report computational complexity per MC sample (e.g., O(q^3) Cholesky), dependence on |A_n|, number of MC samples used, variance reduction strategies, and Cholesky differentiation stability/implementation.  
- Discretization of A (drawing M samples from global optima) is unclear—specify whether sampling from posterior over minimizer (as in PES/PPES), detail procedure, cite relevant work, and report sensitivity to M.  
- Discuss implications of including z(1:q) in A_n on bias/variance of q-KG estimate and gradient stability.  
- Baselines: EI is noise-unaware; use a noise-aware EI variant or explicitly justify/interpret comparisons under noise; ensure baselines share GP priors, hyperparameter fitting, and comparable optimization budgets.  
- Empirical reporting needs wall-clock/acquisition optimization time per iteration, training budgets and fairness for CNN/MNIST experiments, and ablations on q, M, and MC samples; report learned noise variance in noisy settings.  
- Exposition/editorial fixes needed: expand Section 5.2 with a self-contained derivation/algorithm summary, fix typos/numbering, avoid redefining IPA, and use consistent notation (suggest Ã_n for discretized set).  
- Strength: principled decision-theoretic batch acquisition and practical IPA-based gradient making joint optimization tractable; strong empirical gains under noise.  
- Weakness: moderate novelty given prior batch KG work, key technical section lacks detail and may contain derivative error, and lacks noise-aware baselines and runtime analysis.  
- Assessment: technically sound conceptually but needs clearer assumptions, corrected derivatives, more computational detail, and improved experiments for full evaluation.  
- Recommendation: Borderline accept pending clarifications to Section 5.2, stronger comparison/experiments vs. prior batch KG, noise-aware baselines, and runtime/ablation reporting.
