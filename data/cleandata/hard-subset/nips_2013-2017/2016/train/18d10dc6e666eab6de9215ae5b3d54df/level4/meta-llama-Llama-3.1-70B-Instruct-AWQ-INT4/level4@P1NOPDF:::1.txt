This paper introduces a batch-sequential version of the Knowledge Gradient criterion, a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The authors provide a thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes, followed by a clear definition of the parallel (q-KG) criterion. They detail its computation, closely mirroring the standard KG criterion, and present a number of numerical experiments demonstrating the superior performance of q-KG over state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization algorithms.

Overall, this is an excellent paper that possesses important qualities that justify its publication in NIPS. The potential impact on society of parallelizing Bayesian optimization algorithms is substantial. However, I do have a few minor criticisms. Firstly, the paper does not study the speed-ups achieved by transitioning from sequential to batch-sequential optimization, which would be a valuable addition. Secondly, some statements in the literature review are slightly imprecise. For instance, the idea of integrating Expected Improvement (EI) with respect to posterior distributions was already explored in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization" (along with Constant Liar), and Chevalier et al. presented the CL-mix approach in [2]. Additionally, q-EI maximization using natural gradient is studied in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design".

I also have a few remarks and questions. Firstly, is the set A compact, and is the function f continuous? Secondly, why is A restricted to be a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) at the initial stage? In Algorithm 1, what about hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating? In Section 5.2, is the definition of g a maximization or minimization problem? Furthermore, the smoothness of g is not immediately apparent, and the derivative of the Cholesky factor may not be obvious. Additionally, I believe the Matérn parameter is 5/2, not 2/5. Regarding Figure 1 and the corresponding experiments, was it possible to fit the same Gaussian processes using different software packages?
