This paper presents a batch extension of the Knowledge Gradient (KG). To select an optimal batch of design points, it introduces a Monte Carlo–based gradient estimator. The empirical results indicate that the proposed approach outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

1. To my knowledge, batch KG is not novel to this work. Please clarify the specific contributions relative to prior batch KG literature such as [1] and [2]. My interpretation is that the main contribution lies in estimating the gradient to optimize the batch. If so, the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed estimator should be compared against previous batch KG approaches, either empirically or via mathematical analysis.

2. Section 5.2 should be expanded, as it appears to be the core technical contribution of the paper.

3. Standard EI assumes noiseless evaluations, so comparisons under observation noise may be unfavorable to EI. Did you use a noise-aware variant of EI? If plain EI was used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed in light of EI’s insensitivity to noise.

4. On line 162, the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined.
