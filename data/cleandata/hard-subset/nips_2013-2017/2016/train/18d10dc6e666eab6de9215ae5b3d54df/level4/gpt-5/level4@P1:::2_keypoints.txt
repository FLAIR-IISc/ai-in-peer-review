- Introduces q-KG, a parallel extension of the knowledge gradient acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- q-KG is Bayes-optimal for the one-step batch decision problem, selecting batches that maximize expected decrease in posterior mean minimum.  
- Under noise-free evaluations and recommendation restricted to sampled points, q-KG reduces to parallel expected improvement (q-EI).  
- Exact q-KG evaluation is intractable; authors use Monte Carlo estimation and develop an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- They use discretization and gradient-based optimization to make joint batch optimization computationally feasible.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and HPO (logistic regression on MNIST, CNNs on CIFAR-10) show strong performance, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Compared against parallel EI (including heuristics), GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, q-KG performs notably well in noisy settings.  
- Strength: principled, decision-theoretic derivation extending KG to batch settings with an efficient stochastic gradient method.  
- Strength: clear derivation, elegant approach, good empirical gains, and publicly available code.  
- Weakness: assumes independent Gaussian observation noise, which may be unrealistic in hyperparameter optimization due to bias and dependence.  
- Weakness: evaluation metric choice (test set error) is suboptimal for HPO; metrics like AUC or log loss are preferable.  
- Weakness: narrow benchmark—only two datasets, one task type (classification), and two models—limiting empirical generality; recommends using standardized suites like HPOlib.  
- Quality: technical development and computational strategy are sound and well motivated, though empirical evidence is limited.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized, readable, connects to prior work, and provides useful reductions and implementation details.  
- Originality and significance: novel extension of KG to batch settings with practical gradient optimization; promising for parallel BO but broader evaluation needed to confirm general applicability.
