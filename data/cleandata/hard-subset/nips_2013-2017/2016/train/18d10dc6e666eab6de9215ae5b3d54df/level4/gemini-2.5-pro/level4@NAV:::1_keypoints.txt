- Introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for Bayesian Optimization and defines it after reviewing related literature and Gaussian processes.  
- Proposes a computational approach for q-KG that closely mirrors the standard KG methodology.  
- Provides numerical experiments showing q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution, strong candidate for publication with substantial societal impact for parallel BO.  
- Missing analysis quantifying computational speed-ups of the batch-sequential approach versus the sequential counterpart.  
- Literature review has minor inaccuracies/omissions: prior work on EI over posterior and Constant Liar, CL-mix by Chevalier et al., and multipoint EI differentiation via natural gradients are not properly cited.  
- Clarify theoretical assumptions: is domain A assumed compact and is f assumed continuous?  
- Justify the choice to restrict the initial design to a Latin Hypercube Sample.  
- Explain how hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating is handled within Algorithm 1’s optimization loop.  
- In Section 5.2, clarify whether the definition of g should involve a maximization or minimization.  
- Provide justification for the assumed smoothness of g in Section 5.2.  
- Expand on the derivation of the derivative of the Cholesky factor, which may be non-trivial.  
- Minor typo: Matérn kernel parameter likely 5/2 rather than 2/5.  
- For Figure 1 experiments, state whether GP models were cross-validated or compared across software implementations to ensure consistency.
