This paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization (BO) method based on the Knowledge Gradient (KG) acquisition function, specifically designed for parallel evaluation of objective functions. The core contribution lies in developing an efficient gradient-based approach, utilizing infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and Monte Carlo simulation, to optimize the selection of a batch of points for simultaneous evaluation. Experimental results demonstrate the proposed method, termed parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), outperforms several existing batch BO algorithms on both synthetic test functions and in practical hyperparameter tuning scenarios, particularly when function evaluations are noisy.

The paper begins by framing the problem of batch BO and motivating the use of the KG framework for informed decision-making. The proposed q-KG method extends the single-point KG policy to a batch setting by maximizing the expected incremental value of a simultaneous evaluation of *q* points. The authors acknowledge the computational challenges of directly maximizing the q-KG acquisition function and present a method for efficient gradient estimation using IPA. The paper concludes with comprehensive experimental evaluation showcasing the superiority of q-KG compared to competing methods.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The extension of the KG method to a parallel batch setting is a valuable contribution. While batch BO is a well-studied area, the application of the KG framework, with its decision-theoretic grounding, offers a different perspective.
*   **Computational Efficiency:**  The IPA-based gradient estimation technique addresses a key bottleneck in maximizing the q-KG acquisition function, making the method practical for higher-dimensional problems.
*   **Strong Empirical Performance:** The results presented consistently demonstrate that q-KG outperforms other batch BO algorithms, especially in noisy evaluation settings, suggesting its robustness and effectiveness.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the core concepts and methodology. The algorithm is well-defined and the experimental setup is described in sufficient detail.
* **Practical Implementation:** The availability of code fosters reproducibility and encourages further research.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Related Work Clarification:** The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of related work on batch KG methods. Specifically, addressing the relationship to Wang et al. (2015) and other potentially relevant works is crucial to clearly establish the novelty of the proposed approach. A direct comparison of efficiency or effectiveness with these existing methods, either through experiments or theoretical analysis, would strengthen the paper.
*   **IPA Section Detail:** Section 5.2, detailing the IPA-based gradient estimation, is a central aspect of the contribution and warrants further elaboration. Providing more intuition behind the derivation and potentially including intermediate steps would enhance clarity.
*   **Fairness of Comparison in Noisy Settings:** The comparison against standard Expected Improvement (EI) in noisy environments may be somewhat unfair, as EI is inherently designed for noiseless observations. The authors should discuss this limitation and consider comparing against noise-aware variants of EI, or provide a rationale for using the standard EI as a baseline.
*   **Redefinition of IPA:** The redefinition of the abbreviation "IPA" on line 162 is unnecessary and slightly confusing.  It should be removed.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to batch Bayesian optimization.
*   The proposed method demonstrates significant performance improvements over existing methods, particularly in challenging noisy evaluation scenarios.
*   The efficient gradient estimation technique makes the method practical for real-world applications.
*   The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the approach needs to be more clearly articulated in relation to existing batch KG methods.
*   The IPA section could benefit from increased detail and clarity.
*   The fairness of the comparison in noisy settings should be addressed.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with significant potential. The proposed q-KG method represents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above, particularly the clarification of related work and elaboration of the IPA section, would significantly strengthen the paper and make it a compelling addition to NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
