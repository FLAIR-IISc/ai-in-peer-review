This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method, which aims to find an optimal batch of design points. A gradient estimation approach based on Monte-Carlo simulation is proposed to achieve this goal. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

However, I have several concerns and suggestions for improvement. Firstly, the concept of batch KG is not new, and previous works such as [1] and [2] have already explored this idea. Therefore, it is essential to clarify the contribution of this paper in relation to these existing works. In my opinion, the primary contribution of this paper lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for finding the optimal batch. If this is the case, it would be beneficial to compare the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method with previous batch KG methods through experiments or mathematical analysis.

Secondly, I believe that Section 5.2 is a crucial part of this paper, and it would be helpful to elaborate on this section further. This section appears to be the key point of the paper, and providing more details would enhance the reader's understanding of the proposed method.

Thirdly, I would like to point out that the Expected Improvement (EI) method assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, the comparison in the noisy setting may be unfair. I would like to know if a variant of EI that takes into account noise is used in the experiments. If the plain EI method is used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed in the context of EI's limitation in handling noise.

Lastly, I noticed that the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined in line 162. It would be helpful to clarify the distinction between the proposed method and the existing IPA method.
