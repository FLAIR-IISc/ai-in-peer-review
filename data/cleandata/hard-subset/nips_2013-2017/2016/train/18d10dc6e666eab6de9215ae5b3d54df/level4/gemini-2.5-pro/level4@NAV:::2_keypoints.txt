- Introduces a novel parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm to identify Bayes-optimal batches for simultaneous evaluation.  
- Shows superior performance of the proposed method compared to established BO techniques.  
- Manuscript is clear, well-structured, and accessible.  
- Contribution is timely and significant; parallelization of BO is important and the formulation is conceptually appealing and elegant.  
- Formal determination of a Bayes-optimal batch is a particularly compelling theoretical contribution.  
- Minor concern: assumption of independent, normally distributed errors may be invalid in hyperparameter optimization due to structured measurement biases; discuss this limitation.  
- Major experimental weakness: using test-set error as the optimization objective is problematic; prefer metrics like AUC or log loss.  
- Experimental benchmark choice is weak: only MNIST and CIFAR10 used instead of dedicated HPO benchmark suites (e.g., HPOlib).  
- Empirical validation is limited in scope (two datasets, one ML task, two architectures); needs more diverse and comprehensive benchmarks to support generality claims.  
- Summary: strong theoretical contribution but current experimental evidence is insufficient; a more thorough empirical evaluation is needed to substantiate superiority claims.
