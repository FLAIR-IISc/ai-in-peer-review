- Introduces q-KG, a batch-sequential variant of the Knowledge Gradient for parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Provides a clear, KG-aligned definition and derivation of the parallel criterion.  
- Empirical evaluation shows consistent improvements over state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Paper is well-structured with a concise related work overview and GP refresher.  
- Presentation and derivation are logical and accessible to readers familiar with KG.  
- Lacks explicit quantification of wall-clock speed-up from parallelization; reporting this would strengthen impact.  
- Related work citations need greater precision—prior work on EI variants and parallelization (Lizotte et al. 2008, Chevalier et al. 2013, and multipoint EI differentiation 2014) should be acknowledged.  
- Clarify assumptions: is domain A compact and is f continuous; justify using LHS for initial design.  
- Algorithm 1 should state whether GP hyperparameters are re-estimated or updated within the algorithm.  
- Section 5.2: definition of g needs clarification—whether it is a maximization or minimization.  
- Smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor are not self-evident and require further discussion.  
- Typographical error: Matérn kernel parameter should be ν = 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Reproducibility: report whether GP fits were consistent across different software packages to assess robustness.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with acceptance suggested pending minor revisions addressing the above points.
