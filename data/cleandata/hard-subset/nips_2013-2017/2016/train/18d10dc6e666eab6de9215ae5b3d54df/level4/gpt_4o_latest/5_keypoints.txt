- Paper proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient method using Monte Carlo-based gradient estimation and reports superior performance to other batch BO methods.
- Novelty concern: batch KG is not new; authors must clarify specific contributions versus prior works (e.g., [1],[2]) and justify that the main novelty is the gradient estimator.
- Evaluation gap: the proposed gradient estimation should be rigorously compared to previous batch KG methods either experimentally or via theoretical analysis.
- Section 5.2 is central and underdeveloped; it needs more detail and clarification.
- Experimental fairness: EI assumes noiseless observations—if standard EI was used in noisy experiments, results are unfair; authors should state if a noise-aware EI was used and discuss noise effects.
- Presentation issue: the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined at line 162, which may confuse readers.
