- Introduces q-KG, a batch-sequential extension of the Knowledge Gradient for Bayesian optimization, with formal definition, computation details, and strong empirical performance.  
- Demonstrates superior performance over state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods and shows clear societal relevance via parallelization efficiency.  
- Paper is well-motivated, technically sound, well-written, and a strong candidate for acceptance.  
- Literature precision: claim of novelty for “integrating EI with respect to posterior distributions” is inaccurate—previous work (Kriging parallel optimization, CL-mix by Chevalier et al.) and multipoint EI differentiation/natural-gradient papers cover related ideas.  
- Computational efficiency: manuscript does not quantify speed-ups from sequential to batch-sequential execution, an important practical metric.  
- Technical clarification needed: explicitly state assumptions on domain A (e.g., compactness) and objective f (e.g., continuity).  
- Technical clarification needed: justify restricting initial sampling to Latin Hypercube (LHS).  
- Technical clarification needed: Algorithm 1 omits hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating during batch acquisition—needs specification.  
- Technical clarification needed: in Section 5.2 the definition of g uses “max” but likely should be “min”; clarify and correct.  
- Technical clarification needed: justify smoothness of g and differentiability of Cholesky factor derivatives used in analysis.  
- Technical correction: Matérn kernel parameter stated as 2/5 should be 5/2.  
- Reproducibility: confirm whether GP fits in Figure 1 can be replicated using other GP libraries (e.g., GPy, scikit-learn).  
- Overall: the issues are minor and do not undermine the contribution; recommend acceptance subject to these revisions.
