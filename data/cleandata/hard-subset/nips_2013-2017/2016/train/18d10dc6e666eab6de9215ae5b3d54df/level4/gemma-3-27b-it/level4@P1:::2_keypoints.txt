- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and outperforms existing methods.
- Paper is well-written, elegant, and addresses an important practical problem (parallel BO for HPO).
- Determining a Bayes-optimal batch is a promising contribution.
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors (common in GP-based BO), which can be unrealistic due to structural bias in hyperparameter evaluation.
- Theoretical contributions are strong.
- Experimental evaluation is limited: using test-set error as the optimization criterion is potentially inappropriate; threshold-based metrics can be suboptimal and alternatives like AUC or log loss should be considered.
- Experiments rely only on MNIST and CIFAR-10; should include standardized HPO benchmarks like HPOlib.
- Benchmark scope is narrow: only two datasets, one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN); more variety is needed to show broad applicability.
- Overall: solid theoretical work and promising method, but current empirical validation is insufficient to be fully convincing.
