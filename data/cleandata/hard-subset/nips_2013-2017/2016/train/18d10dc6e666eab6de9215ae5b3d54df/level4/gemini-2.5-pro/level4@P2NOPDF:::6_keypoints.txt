- Introduces q-KG: a batch Bayes-optimal acquisition function using the knowledge gradient and IPA for gradient computation.  
- Empirical results on synthetic benchmarks and two ML tasks (CNN on CIFAR-10, logistic regression on MNIST) show q-KG competitive in noiseless settings and superior with observation noise.  
- Missing wall-clock time analysis and no comparison of real-time optimization speed against sequential BO or parallel random search.  
- Overhead of computing q-KG (batch selection cost) is not reported, leaving real-world utility unclear.  
- Experiments fix batch size q=4 without justification; scalability and performance with larger q are unexplored.  
- Novelty concern: applying KG to batch BO and using IPA are incremental given prior work (e.g., Wang et al. used IPA for q-EI); need clearer delineation of novel contributions.  
- Request to clarify specific advantages of q-KG over q-EI and explicit comparison to parallel EI methods.  
- Problem importance and potential impact are high—parallel hyperparameter optimization is practically valuable.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear background and methodological exposition.  
- Ask authors to compare explicitly to Wang et al.'s parallel EI (rename baseline to q-EI if appropriate) and discuss differences.  
- Ask for analysis of theoretical/practical limitations and computational scaling of q-KG as q increases.  
- Suggest extending or discussing asynchronous batch evaluation (dispatching jobs as workers free).  
- Request concrete wall-clock timing: time to find a batch (q=4) and comparisons to sequential/parallel baselines.  
- Clarify what the x-axis "iterations" denotes (evaluations vs. batches) and whether initial design points are counted and why curves start differently.  
- Minor fixes: possible missing term z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammar correction in abstract/introduction; prefer non-parenthetical citation phrasing; correct citation for original IPA reference.
