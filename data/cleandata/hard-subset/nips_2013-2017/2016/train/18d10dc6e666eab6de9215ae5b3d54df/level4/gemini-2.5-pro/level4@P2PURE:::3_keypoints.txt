- Presents a Bayesian optimization algorithm for finite-domain multivariate functions that selects a batch of points per iteration.  
- Key novelty: extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to jointly select multiple points (batch KG).  
- Maximizing the batch KG criterion is intractable, so authors use Monte Carlo estimation plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to obtain gradients for optimization.  
- Experimental results on several test problems show the method outperforms or is competitive with existing approaches, especially Confidence Bound methods.  
- Paper is well-written and the method appears practically promising.  
- Concern about novelty: extending KG to batches is a natural step and IPA is a standard technique, so the main contribution may be application of known methods to a straightforward extension.  
- Specific notation issue: Equation (3.1) is unclear about how μ(x¹:ⁿ) and K(x¹:ⁿ, x¹:ⁿ) are defined when x¹:ⁿ is a sequence but μ and K are defined on single vectors/pairs — authors should clarify.
