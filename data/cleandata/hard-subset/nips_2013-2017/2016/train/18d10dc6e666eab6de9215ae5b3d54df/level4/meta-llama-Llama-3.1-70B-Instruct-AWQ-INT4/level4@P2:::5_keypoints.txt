- Introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm (q-KG) with a decision-theoretic foundation.  
- Proposes a new acquisition function, parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), and an IPA-based strategy to compute Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Provides clear background, related work, and careful description of the q-KG algorithm and its components.  
- Details computation of the q-KG factor and its gradient, but the derivation explanation is brief.  
- Experimental results show q-KG outperforms or is competitive with parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB+exploration in noisy and noise-free settings.  
- Contribution relative to prior batch KG papers ([1], [2]) is unclear and needs a more detailed comparison.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimator derivation) needs more detailed exposition.  
- Comparison with EI in noisy settings may be unfair; authors should discuss this limitation or use a noise-aware EI variant.  
- Avoid redefining abbreviations (e.g., "IPA"); maintain consistent notation throughout.  
- Overall well-written and promising; with revisions it could make a significant contribution.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, solid exposition, and strong empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparison to prior work, incomplete gradient derivation, and potentially unfair EI comparison.  
- Rating: 8/10.
