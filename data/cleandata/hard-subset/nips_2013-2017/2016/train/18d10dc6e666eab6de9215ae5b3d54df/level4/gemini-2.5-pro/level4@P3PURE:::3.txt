This paper proposes a stochastic, Bayesian optimization algorithm for multivariate objective functions over a finite domain. A key feature of the algorithm is its ability to select a batch of points for parallel evaluation in each iteration. The core problem addressed is how to select these sampling points in an optimal manner.

To this end, the authors adopt and extend the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework. Whereas standard KG estimates the expected improvement from evaluating a single additional point, this work generalizes the approach to a batch setting (q-KG), enabling the simultaneous selection of multiple points. As the problem of selecting the optimal batch is computationally intractable, the authors develop an algorithm that uses Monte Carlo simulation and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate the gradient of the batch KG acquisition function. This allows for efficient optimization of the batch selection criterion.

Experimental results on several test problems demonstrate that the proposed algorithm either outperforms or is highly competitive with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly those based on upper confidence bounds. Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a method with clear potential for practical applications.

My main reservation concerns the level of novelty. The work is primarily an extension of the existing Knowledge Gradient framework to a batch setting. While this extension is non-trivial and requires a more sophisticated optimization procedure, the main technical contribution appears to be the estimation of the q-KG gradient via IPA. However, using IPA for gradient estimation is a relatively standard technique.

I have one specific question regarding the notation in Equation (3.1). The terms `μ(x^(1:n))` and `K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n))` are used, where `x^(1:n)` is a finite sequence of vectors. It is unclear how these are defined, as the mean function `μ` and covariance function `K` are typically defined over single vectors and pairs of vectors, respectively, not over sequences. Clarification on this notation would be helpful.
