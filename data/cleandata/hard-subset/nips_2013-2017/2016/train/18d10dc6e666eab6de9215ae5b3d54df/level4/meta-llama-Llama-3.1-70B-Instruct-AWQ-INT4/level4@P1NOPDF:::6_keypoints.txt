- Proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using the q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) acquisition and IPA-based gradient estimation to select batches.  
- Empirical claim: q-KG is competitive on noiseless problems and outperforms others on noisy problems.  
- Reviewer appreciates parallel HPO motivation but finds some aspects unclear and experimental evidence unconvincing.  
- Technical experiments: uses synthetic benchmarks plus CNN/logistic on CIFAR10/MNIST, but lacks wall-clock time comparisons or analysis of gains vs random/sequential search.  
- No justification for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Novelty concern: q-KG and IPA are not new; prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) uses IPA for parallel EI—differences should be clearly stated.  
- Impact: parallel hyperparameter tuning is important and broadly useful across domains.  
- Clarity: technical background and experimental details are generally well-described.  
- Question: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI (q-EI) and clarified differences?  
- Question: How does the method scale to many more parallel runs (>>4) and what gains are expected?  
- Question: Can the approach be extended to asynchronous execution like Spearmint?  
- Question: Any wall-clock time comparisons vs random search or sequential BO, and what is the overhead to compute a new batch?  
- Question: Clarify x-axis “iterations” — batches or evaluations, does it include initial design, and why methods start at different values?  
- Minor comments: possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammar fixes (“achieves”, parenthetical reference usage); Wang et al. (2015) did not originate IPA.
