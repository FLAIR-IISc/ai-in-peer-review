- Proposes a batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization method using a parallelized Knowledge Gradient (q-KG).  
- Provides concise background (Gaussian processes) and formally defines the q-KG criterion and its computational treatment.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch BO algorithms across experiments.  
- Reviewer considers the paper a strong contribution with publication merit due to practical importance of efficient BO parallelization.  
- Missing analysis of actual speed-ups achieved by batch-sequential processing.  
- Related work discussion lacks precision; prior work on EI over posterior (Gelbart et al.), CL-mix (Chevalier et al.), and natural-gradient q-EI differentiation should be acknowledged.  
- Question: Is the domain A assumed compact and is f continuous on A?  
- Question: Why restrict initial design points to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS)?  
- Question: Does Algorithm 1 include hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating during the optimization?  
- Clarification needed: In Section 5.2, is g defined as a maximization or minimization problem?  
- Clarification needed: Smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor require further justification.  
- Typographical error: Matérn kernel parameter should be 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Reproducibility question: Could Figure 1 results be reproduced by fitting the same GPs with alternative software implementations?
