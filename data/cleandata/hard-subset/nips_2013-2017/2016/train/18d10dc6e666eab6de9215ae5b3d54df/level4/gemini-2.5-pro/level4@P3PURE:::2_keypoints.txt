- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches, addressing BO’s sequential limitation and outperforming existing techniques.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.  
- Parallelizing BO is important for practical applications like hyperparameter optimization, and the proposed approach is elegant compared to many existing methods.  
- The ability to determine Bayes-optimal batches is particularly promising and theoretically strong.  
- Minor theoretical concern: assumption of independent, normally distributed errors may be unrealistic in hyperparameter tuning due to structural bias and non-independent measurement errors.  
- Experimental evaluation is disappointing overall.  
- Issue 1: Using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; threshold-based scores are suboptimal—metrics like AUC or log loss would be more appropriate.  
- Issue 2: Experiments rely on MNIST and CIFAR-10 rather than standardized HPO benchmarking libraries (e.g., HPOlib), limiting comparability.  
- Issue 3: Experimental scope is very limited—only two datasets, a single task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN), hindering assessment of generality.  
- Summary: Strong and elegant theoretical contribution, but empirical evaluation is too narrow; broader experiments are needed to be fully convincing.
