- Introduces a stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling for optimizing multivariate objectives over finite domains.  
- Main innovation: iteratively identifies multiple points for evaluation to enable parallel computation.  
- Extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to batch settings (q-KG) to select optimal batches of points.  
- Uses Monte Carlo methods and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to approximate gradients of the q-KG criterion for batch optimization.  
- Algorithm maximizes information gain across candidate point sets to guide batch selection.  
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance to existing methods, especially those based on Confidence Bounds.  
- Paper is well-written and presents practical utility.  
- Novelty is limited: core contribution is the KG-to-batch extension, and the IPA-based gradient estimation is a standard technique.  
- Technical concern: Equation (3.1) is ambiguous because x^{(1:n)} is treated as a set while μ and K are defined for vectors/sequences, making μ(x^{(1:n)}) and K(x^{(1:n)}, x^{(1:n)}) unclear.
