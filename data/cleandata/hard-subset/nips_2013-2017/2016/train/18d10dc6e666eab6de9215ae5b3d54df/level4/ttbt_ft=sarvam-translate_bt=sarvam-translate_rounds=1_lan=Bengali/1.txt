This paper is about batch-sequential Bayesian optimization. A batch-sequential version of the knowledge gradient criterion is presented here. After some background on related work and Gaussian processes, the parallel (q-KG) criterion is defined. Its calculation is discussed in detail, following the path of the standard KG criterion, and several numerical tests are presented where the q-KG seems to outperform some state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian optimization algorithms. It is a very good paper, and I believe it has some important qualities that will justify its publication in NIPS. The impact of paralyzing a Bayesian optimization algorithm on society is very important. A minor criticism is that speed-up (from sequential to batch-sequential) research has not been done, and some statements in the literature review are slightly ambiguous; for example, the idea of combining EI against a posterior distribution was already present in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization" (with Constant Lauer), where Chevalier and others presented CL-mix [2]. Also, research has been done on q-EI maximization using natural gradients in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design". Finally, I have some comments and questions: Is A compact, f continuous? Why is A initially limited to an LHS? In algorithm 1: What is said about hyperparameter re-estimation (or Bayesian update)? In 5.2: Is it max or min in the definition of g? Again about 5.2: The smoothness of g goes without saying. Similarly, the derivative of the Cholesky factor may be ambiguous. Matérn is 5/2, not 2/5. Regarding Figure 1 and the related tests: Was it possible to fit the same GP using different software?
