This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method, which is used to find an optimal batch of design points. A novel gradient estimation approach based on Monte-Carlo simulation is proposed to achieve this goal. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

However, to better understand the contribution of this paper, it is essential to clarify its novelty with respect to previous work on batch KG, such as [1] and [2]. Upon reviewing the paper, it appears that the primary contribution lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for finding the optimal batch. To strengthen this claim, it would be beneficial to compare the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method with previous batch KG methods through experiments or mathematical analysis.

Furthermore, Section 5.2 is crucial to understanding the paper's key contribution and could be elaborated upon. This section provides valuable insights into the proposed method and its advantages over existing approaches.

The comparison with the Expected Improvement (EI) method in the noisy setting may be unfair, as EI typically assumes noiseless observations. It is unclear whether a variant of EI that accounts for noise is used in the comparison. If the plain EI method is employed, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed in the context of EI's limitations in handling noisy observations.

Additionally, the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined in line 162, which may cause confusion. It would be helpful to maintain consistency in terminology throughout the paper.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting approach to batch KG, and with some clarifications and additional analysis, it has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field.
