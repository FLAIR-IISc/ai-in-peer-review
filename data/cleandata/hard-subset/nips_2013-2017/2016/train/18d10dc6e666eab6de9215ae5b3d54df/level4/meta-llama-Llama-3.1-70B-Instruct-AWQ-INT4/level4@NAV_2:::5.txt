This paper derives a batch-version of Knowledge Gradient (KG) and proposes a novel method for computing the gradient of the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) acquisition function, which is essential for finding an optimal batch of design points. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments, showing that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of an efficient method for estimating the gradient of q-KG, which enables the optimization of the acquisition function. While batch KG is not a new concept, the proposed gradient estimation method based on infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) is a significant contribution. The authors should clarify how their approach compares to previous batch KG papers, such as [1] and [2], in terms of efficiency and effectiveness.

Section 5.2, which describes the computation of the gradient of q-KG, is a crucial part of the paper and could be elaborated further. The authors should provide more details on the derivation of the gradient estimation formula and its implementation.

The comparison of the proposed method with other BO methods in the noisy setting is not entirely fair, as Expected Improvement (EI) assumes noiseless observations. The authors should either use a variant of EI that accounts for noise or discuss the results in the context of EI's limitations.

The paper is well-organized, and the experiments are thorough. However, some minor issues need to be addressed. For instance, the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined in line 162, which is unnecessary.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of batch Bayesian optimization, and the proposed method has the potential to be widely adopted. With some minor revisions, the paper can be further improved.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel method for computing the gradient of q-KG, which is essential for finding an optimal batch of design points.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments, showing that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.
* The paper is well-organized, and the experiments are thorough.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The comparison of the proposed method with other BO methods in the noisy setting is not entirely fair.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with previous batch KG papers.
* Some minor issues, such as the redefinition of the "IPA" abbreviation, need to be addressed.
