This paper considers the Parallel Knowledge Gradient method for Batch Bayesian Optimization. The authors consider a situation where function value evaluation is time-consuming. They propose a new acquisition function called q-KG, which can provide several new point outputs for parallel evaluation. They also provide an efficient method for evaluating the q-KG function. Experimental results are shown to verify their theoretical results. This paper considers the Parallel Knowledge Gradient method for Batch Bayesian Optimization. The problem they consider is important. It is necessary to be able to divide the work in a distributed way for the performance of data-driven applications. Instead of evaluating one point at a time, the algorithm considers q knowledge gradients that propose q points for evaluation in the next iteration, which greatly increases the efficiency of the algorithm. This is an interesting concept that should be explored. However, it is not yet clear whether creating a Gaussian process is a good approximation, especially in neural network architectures where the input dimension is very large. It would be helpful if the authors could provide some theoretical results of their algorithm on the error bound of their method and a quantitative analysis of the amount of complexity related to the dimension. However, this is a difficult task overall in the field of Bayesian optimization. The method is difficult and the test is reliable. Overall, the paper is well written.
