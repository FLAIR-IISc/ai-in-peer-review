- Proposes q-KG, a batch-sequential extension of the Knowledge Gradient for Bayesian optimization.  
- Provides background on BO and GPs, formally defines the parallel q-KG criterion, and details its computation following KG methodology.  
- Empirical results on benchmarks show q-KG often outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Review praises clear derivations, compelling experiments, and views the contribution as valuable and publication-worthy.  
- Experimental evaluation: missing analysis of wall-clock computational speed-up from batch q-KG versus sequential KG.  
- Literature review: should acknowledge Ginsbourger et al. on integrating EI over the posterior max and their Constant Liar variant.  
- Literature review: when discussing Chevalier et al., explicitly note their CL-mix heuristic.  
- Literature review: mention Marmin et al. on maximizing q-EI via natural gradient methods.  
- Clarify theoretical assumptions on domain A (e.g., compactness) and objective f (e.g., continuity).  
- Clarify initialization choice: why Latin Hypercube Sampling was used over other space-filling designs.  
- Algorithm 1: state whether GP hyperparameters are fixed or re-estimated (e.g., via marginal likelihood) during iterations.  
- Section 5.2: confirm whether function g uses a max or min operator and correct the formulation if needed.  
- Section 5.2: justify or reference the assumed smoothness of g despite its definition involving a maximization.  
- Cholesky derivative: include a reference or brief appendix note on computing derivatives of the Cholesky factor.  
- Experiments/Figure 1: ensure comparisons aren’t confounded by differing GP implementations—consider a unified GP software framework.  
- Typo: verify Matérn kernel parameter—likely Matérn-5/2 rather than Matérn-2/5.  
- Overall recommendation: excellent paper; recommend acceptance.
