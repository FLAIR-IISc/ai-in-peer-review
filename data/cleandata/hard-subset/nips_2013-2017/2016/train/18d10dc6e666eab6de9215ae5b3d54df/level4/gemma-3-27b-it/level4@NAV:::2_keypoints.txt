- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes batches approximating Bayes-optimality.  
- Demonstrates improved empirical performance over existing Bayesian optimization methods.  
- The paper is clearly written and accessible.  
- Parallelization and the Bayes-optimal batch determination are important, refined contributions.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors typical of Gaussian-process BO.  
- This independence/normality assumption needs discussion because hyperparameter measurement errors reflect generalization vs empirical estimates (e.g., cross-validation).  
- Structural bias in hyperparameter spaces and small sample sizes suggest relaxing the independence assumption could be beneficial.  
- Theoretical foundations are strong.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and has shortcomings.  
- Concern 1: Uses test-set error as the optimization criterion, which is suboptimal for hyperparameter tuning; metrics like AUC or log loss are preferable.  
- Concern 2: Benchmarks are limited to MNIST and CIFAR10; should include the HPOlib benchmark suite.  
- Concern 3: Scope is narrow—only two datasets and two model types (logistic regression and CNNs); a broader set of datasets and algorithms is needed.  
- Overall assessment: Significant theoretical contribution, but experimental results are not fully convincing due to limited benchmarking.
