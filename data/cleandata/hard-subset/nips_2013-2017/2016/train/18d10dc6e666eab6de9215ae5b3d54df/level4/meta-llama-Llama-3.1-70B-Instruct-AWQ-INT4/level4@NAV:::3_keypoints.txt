- Introduces a stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Algorithm selects multiple sampling points per iteration, enabling parallel evaluations; optimally choosing these points is challenging.  
- Adopts the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework and extends it to batch decisions (q-KG) for selecting multiple points.  
- Addresses computational intractability by developing a Monte Carlo + Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) method to estimate required gradients.  
- The algorithm aims to maximize information gain across candidate sampling sets.  
- Empirical evaluation on several test problems shows competitive performance, mainly versus Confidence-Bound–based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and appears practically useful.  
- Novelty is somewhat limited: primarily an extension of existing KG methods to the batch setting, requiring more complex optimization.  
- Primary technical contribution is the q-KG gradient estimation via IPA, which is valuable but a relatively standard technique.  
- Specific clarity issue: equation (3.1) is unclear—definitions of mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) are ambiguous since mu and K are defined on single or paired vectors, not sequences.
