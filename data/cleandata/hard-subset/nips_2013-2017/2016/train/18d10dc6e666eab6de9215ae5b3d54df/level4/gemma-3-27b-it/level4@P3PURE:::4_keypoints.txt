- Proposes q-KG, a new acquisition function for batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization, with an efficient evaluation approach.
- Targets expensive function evaluations by proposing q points per iteration to distribute computational workload and improve efficiency.
- Interesting and worthwhile research direction that meaningfully extends knowledge gradient methods to parallel settings.
- Empirical results are provided and support the theoretical claims; experiments are well-designed.
- Method is sound and the paper is generally well written; overall a solid contribution to Bayesian optimization.
- Concern about the appropriateness of assuming a Gaussian process, especially for neural-network models and high-dimensional inputs.
- Recommends adding theoretical error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.
