- Introduces q-KG, a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient criterion for efficient Bayesian Optimization.  
- Provides GP background and formally defines the parallel criterion; computation closely follows standard KG methodology.  
- Comprehensive numerical experiments show q-KG compares favorably to state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Reviewer considers the paper strong, of significant merit, and suitable for NIPS publication.  
- Highlights the considerable potential impact of parallelizing Bayesian optimization algorithms.  
- Minor criticism: no analysis of actual speed-ups achieved by moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Literature review needs greater precision: EI integrated over posterior was explored in “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization” (with Constant Liar).  
- Chevalier et al. presented CL-mix and should be cited/acknowledged.  
- Prior work “Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design” addresses q-EI maximization using the natural gradient.  
- Specific question: Is the domain A assumed compact and is f continuous on A?  
- Specific question: What motivated restricting the initial design of A to Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS)?  
- Specific question: Does Algorithm 1 include hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating of the Gaussian process?  
- Specific question: In Section 5.2, is the definition of g a maximization or a minimization problem?  
- Concern: Smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor in Section 5.2 are not obvious and need further justification.  
- Typo/correction: The Matérn kernel likely has parameter 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Reproducibility question: Could the Figure 1 experiments be replicated by fitting the same GPs with different software implementations?
