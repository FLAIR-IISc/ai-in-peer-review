This paper explores the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization, focusing on scenarios where evaluating function values is time-consuming. The authors propose a new acquisition function, q-KG, capable of outputting multiple points for parallel evaluation, and provide an efficient approach to evaluate this function. Empirical results are presented to validate their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed in this paper is significant, as distributing work in a parallel manner can substantially enhance the performance of data-driven applications. By considering the q knowledge gradient, the algorithm can propose q points for evaluation at each iteration, thereby accelerating the algorithm's efficiency. This concept is worth exploring further.

However, it remains unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is suitable, particularly in neural network architectures with high-dimensional inputs. To strengthen the paper, the authors could provide theoretical results on the error bounds of their method and a quantitative analysis of its complexity with respect to dimensionality.

Overall, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization, with a well-designed method and sound experimental results. The paper is well-written, although some minor improvements can be made. Specifically, the authors may want to address the following points:

- Line 36: Consider rephrasing "the set of points to evaluate next that is" for clarity.
- Line 95: Define the term "A" to avoid confusion.
- Line 104: Specify the input of "mu^n" for better understanding.
- Line 128: A brief discussion on the parallel EI algorithm would be beneficial for comparison.
- Line 140: Using distinct notations for "A" might enhance readability.
