- Proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm using Monte Carlo gradient estimation for selecting optimal batches and reports superior empirical performance.  
- Novelty claim is unclear; prior batch KG work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015, Peng thesis) is not adequately contrasted.  
- The primary novel component appears to be the gradient estimation for batch selection and should be explicitly highlighted.  
- Needs comparative analysis (experimental or theoretical) against existing batch KG methods to substantiate novelty and performance claims.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) is crucial and must be expanded with a more formal, detailed presentation for clarity and reproducibility.  
- Comparing to standard Expected Improvement (EI) in a noisy setting is problematic because EI assumes noiseless observations.  
- Must clarify whether a noise-aware EI variant was used (and provide implementation details) or acknowledge the limitation if standard EI was used.  
- Minor issue: the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined (line 162); ensure consistent abbreviation usage throughout.  
- Overall: the gradient estimation approach is promising but the paper needs clearer novelty claims, stronger comparisons to related work, and more detailed methodological exposition.
