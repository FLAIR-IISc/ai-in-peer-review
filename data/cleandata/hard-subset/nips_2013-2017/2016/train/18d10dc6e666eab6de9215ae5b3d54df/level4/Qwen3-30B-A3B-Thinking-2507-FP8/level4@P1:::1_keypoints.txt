- Introduces the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization, decision-theoretically optimal when one batch remains.  
- q-KG jointly optimizes an entire batch of q points rather than selecting points iteratively.  
- Provides an efficient optimization method for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis to estimate gradients.  
- Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods on synthetic functions and real ML hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN), especially with noisy evaluations.  
- The approach is theoretically grounded and different from prior parallel EI/Constant Liar and GP-BUCB-style methods.  
- Related work is correctly cited, distinguishing q-KG from parallel EI variants and gradient-based multipoint EI.  
- Experimental design is thorough with appropriate baselines, multiple test functions, and real-world applications.  
- Implementation is released, aiding reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and presents sufficient methodological detail (Section 5) for readers.  
- Minor weakness: lacks explicit analysis of wall-clock speed-up from parallelization versus sequential methods.  
- Minor weakness: initial Latin hypercube sampling is used without strong justification or comparison to alternatives.  
- Minor weakness: some technical details on smoothness assumptions for gradient estimation need clarification for non-expert readers.  
- Minor correction: typo regarding the Matérn kernel (should be Matérn 5/2, not 2/5).  
- Suggested improvements: discuss theoretical guarantees (e.g., regret bounds), clarify relation to expected improvement when final solution is restricted to evaluated points, and provide explicit computational complexity comparisons with baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — strong theoretical contribution, clear empirical validation, and high practical relevance to modern ML.
