- Proposes q-KG, a parallel Knowledge Gradient acquisition for batch Bayesian optimization with decision-theoretic Bayes-optimal motivation.  
- Provides a gradient-based, computationally efficient maximization method for q-KG using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).  
- Empirically shows q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially under noisy evaluations, across synthetic and real tasks.  
- Paper is technically solid, novel, and well-written with clear motivation, methodology, and experiments.  
- Missing experimental comparison to a strong sequential baseline (e.g., running sequential KG q times) to quantify parallel speed-up and efficiency gain.  
- Literature review has precision issues: prior work on averaging EI over posterior (Ginsbourger et al. 2010) and Constant Liar was not fully credited.  
- Should mention CL-mix heuristic in the discussion of Chevalier and Ginsbourger.  
- Related work by Marmin et al. (2015) on differentiating q-EI with gradients is closely related and should be discussed.  
- Clarify theoretical assumptions on domain A (e.g., compactness) and properties of f (e.g., continuity).  
- Comment on sensitivity to the initial Latin hypercube design versus other space-filling designs.  
- Specify whether GP hyperparameter MLE is retrained from scratch each iteration or warmed-started from previous values.  
- Confirm the definition of g in Section 5.2 (difference between two minima) and whether a maximization term should appear.  
- Justify interchange of derivative and expectation for g given nondifferentiability from min/argmin and address potential non-uniqueness of argmin.  
- Provide a reference or brief outline for computing derivatives of the Cholesky factor used in gradient calculations.  
- Explain to what extent GP model choices and hyperparameter fitting were standardized across compared implementations (MOE, Spearmint, Gpoptimization) for fair comparison.
