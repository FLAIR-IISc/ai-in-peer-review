- Introduces a stochastic gradient algorithm with Bayesian modeling that selects multiple points per iteration to enable parallel objective evaluations.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to evaluate expected improvement and guide whether and which points to sample.  
- Extends KG to the batch setting, addressing the optimal selection of multiple sampling points.  
- Problem is computationally intractable; the authors propose Monte Carlo methods combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for maximizing information gain.  
- Main technical contribution is the q-KG gradient estimation via IPA, which the reviewer finds effective but relatively standard.  
- Algorithm evaluated on benchmarks and shows competitive or superior performance, particularly against Confidence Bounds methods.  
- Paper is well-written and has potential practical relevance.  
- Reviewer is not fully convinced about novelty, viewing the work mainly as an extension of KG to batches requiring more advanced optimization.  
- Specific comment: equation (3.1) is unclear—how mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) are defined for sequences rather than individual vectors/pairs.
