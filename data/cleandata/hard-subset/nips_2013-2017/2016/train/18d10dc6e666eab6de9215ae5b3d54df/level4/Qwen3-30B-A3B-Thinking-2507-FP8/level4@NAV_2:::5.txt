

This paper develops a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method for Bayesian optimization. The authors propose an efficient gradient estimation approach using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute the optimal batch of points, and demonstrate that their parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method outperforms existing batch Bayesian optimization algorithms. 1. The contribution of this work appears to be the efficient gradient estimation technique for batch KG, rather than the batch KG formulation itself, as similar batch KG approaches have been proposed previously (e.g., Wang et al. [1] and Peng's thesis [2]). The authors should clarify the specific novelty of their contribution compared to these prior works and provide a direct comparison of their gradient estimation method with previous approaches in terms of efficiency and effectiveness. 2. Section 5.2, which details the IPA-based gradient estimation method, is critical to the paper's technical contribution but lacks sufficient elaboration. The authors should provide more detailed explanation of this key technical component, including how it differs from previous gradient estimation techniques. 3. The comparison with Expected Improvement (EI) in noisy settings appears problematic, as standard EI assumes noiseless observations. The authors should clarify whether they used a noise-aware variant of EI or explain why the plain EI is appropriate for noisy evaluation settings, as this comparison may be misleading without proper context. 4. The abbreviation "IPA" is redefined in line 162, which is inconsistent with standard academic practice where acronyms are defined upon first use.
