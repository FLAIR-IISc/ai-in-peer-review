- Paper proposes q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) acquisition and uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to optimize it for parallel batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive on noiseless benchmarks and outperforms others under observation noise.  
- The paper addresses an important, practically relevant problem: efficient parallel hyperparameter optimization for multi-core environments.  
- Missing wall-clock time comparisons make it hard to judge practical speedups versus parallel random search or sequential BO.  
- The computational overhead of optimizing q-KG is not analyzed or reported.  
- Batch size q is fixed at 4 with no justification or sensitivity analysis, leaving scalability with larger q unclear.  
- Similar ideas (parallel EI with IPA) have prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015); the novelty over existing parallel EI methods is not clearly articulated.  
- The naming and relation to other "q-EI" variants should be clarified to avoid confusion with prior parallel EI implementations.  
- The method appears synchronous; the paper does not discuss possible asynchronous extensions.  
- Important experimental details are ambiguous: the x-axis "iterations" meaning (batches vs. individual evaluations) and inclusion of initial design are unclear.  
- Suggest comparing directly to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI implementations and clarifying differences.  
- Minor issues: possible missing term in Equation (4.1), a grammar error, citation style problems, and an inappropriate IPA citation choice.
