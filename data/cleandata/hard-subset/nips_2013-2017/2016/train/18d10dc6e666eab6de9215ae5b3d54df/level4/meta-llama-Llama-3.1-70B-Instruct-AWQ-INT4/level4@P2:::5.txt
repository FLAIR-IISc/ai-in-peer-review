This paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient method (q-KG), derived from a decision-theoretic analysis. The authors propose a new acquisition function, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), to guide the selection of the next batch of points to evaluate. They also develop an efficient strategy for computing the Bayes-optimal batch of points using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the background and related work in Bayesian optimization (BO). The q-KG algorithm is carefully described, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of how to compute the q-KG factor and its gradient.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the q-KG algorithm in both noise-free and noisy settings. The authors compare their method to several state-of-the-art batch BO algorithms, including parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with pure exploration. The results show that q-KG outperforms or is competitive with these algorithms on several synthetic functions and practical machine learning problems.

However, I have some concerns and suggestions for improvement:

1. Clarify the contribution of this paper: While the authors mention that the q-KG algorithm is a novel contribution, it is not entirely clear how it differs from previous batch KG papers, such as [1] and [2]. The authors should provide a more detailed comparison of their method with these previous works and clarify the specific contributions of this paper.

2. Elaborate on Section 5.2: This section is crucial to the paper, as it describes the computation of the q-KG factor and its gradient. However, the explanation is somewhat brief, and the authors could provide more details on the derivation of the gradient estimator.

3. Discuss the comparison with EI in the noisy setting: As the authors note, EI assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, the comparison with EI in the noisy setting may not be entirely fair. The authors should discuss this limitation and consider using a variant of EI that takes into account the noise.

4. Avoid redefining abbreviations: The abbreviation "IPA" is redefined in line 162, which can be confusing for readers. The authors should avoid redefining abbreviations and use consistent notation throughout the paper.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and results. With some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm with a clear decision-theoretic foundation.
* The authors provide a detailed explanation of the q-KG algorithm and its components.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the q-KG algorithm in both noise-free and noisy settings.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with previous batch KG papers.
* The explanation of the gradient estimator in Section 5.2 could be more detailed.
* The comparison with EI in the noisy setting may not be entirely fair.

Rating: 8/10
