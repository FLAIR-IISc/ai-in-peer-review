This research paper explores the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization. The authors consider a situation where evaluating the function value is time-consuming. They propose a new acquisition function called q-KG, which can output multiple new points for parallel evaluation. They also provide an efficient approach for evaluating the q-KG function. Empirical results are shown to verify their theoretical results. This research paper explores the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization. The problem they considered is significant. Being able to divide the work in a distributed manner is useful for the performance of data-driven applications. Instead of evaluating one point at a time, the algorithm considers q knowledge gradients that propose q points to evaluate at the next iteration, which greatly speeds up the algorithm's efficiency. This is an interesting idea to explore. However, it is not clear whether the Gaussian process is a good assumption, especially in neural network architectures where the input dimension is very large. It would be helpful if the authors could provide some theoretical results of their algorithm on a quantitative analysis of the error range and complexity of their methods. But overall, this is a solid work in the field of Bayesian optimization. The method is solid, and the experiment is correct. The research paper is well-written overall.
