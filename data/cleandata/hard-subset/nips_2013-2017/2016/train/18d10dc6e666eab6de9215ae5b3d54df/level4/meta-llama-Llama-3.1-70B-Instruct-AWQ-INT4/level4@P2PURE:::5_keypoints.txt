- Paper proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) method using Monte Carlo gradient estimation to identify optimal batches and shows empirical improvement over other batch BO methods.

- Novelty concern: batch KG itself has prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015 and Peng thesis), so the manuscript must clarify its specific contribution relative to existing batch KG literature.

- The reviewer believes the main novel contribution is the proposed gradient estimation method and requests empirical or theoretical comparison of its efficiency and effectiveness against previous batch KG approaches.

- Section 5.2 is important but underdeveloped; the reviewer asks for expansion and more insight into this section.

- Experimental comparison may be unfair: Expected Improvement (EI) assumes noiseless observations, so the authors should state whether a noise-aware EI variant was used and discuss EI’s limitations if standard EI was applied in noisy settings (Section 6.2).

- Editorial consistency issue: the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162 and should be made consistent throughout the paper.
