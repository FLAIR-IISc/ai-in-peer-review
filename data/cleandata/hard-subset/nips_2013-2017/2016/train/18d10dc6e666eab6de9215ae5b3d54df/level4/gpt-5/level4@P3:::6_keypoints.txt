- Contribution: Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method maximizing expected one-step reduction in GP posterior minimum, with Monte Carlo objective estimation and an IPA-based stochastic gradient for joint batch optimization.  
- Relationship to prior work: Positions q-KG relative to q-EI, UCB-based batch policies, and entropy-search methods; differentiates by joint batch decision-theoretic derivation.  
- Theoretical note: q-KG reduces to q-EI in the noise-free, sampled-recommendation limit.  
- Empirical summary: Evaluated on standard synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR-10 CNN); competitive overall and notably stronger under observation noise; code released.  
- Technical soundness positives: KG formulation, Monte Carlo estimator, IPA gradient, and Cholesky-based sampling/gradient computation are appropriate and sound.  
- Technical gaps: Lacks clear complexity analysis per batch step (dependence on q, Monte Carlo samples, discretization M), variance-reduction strategies for stochastic gradients, and explicit conditions guaranteeing unbiasedness/differentiability (deferred to supplement).  
- Experimental methodology gaps: Missing wall-clock time comparisons with random/ sequential BO, scaling behavior for larger batch sizes, asynchronous execution discussion, unexplained choice q=4, and unexplored effect of discretization size M=1000.  
- Claim clarification: Bayes-optimality holds for one remaining batch under the GP posterior and discretized domain; practical algorithm optimizes an approximation due to discretization and Monte Carlo.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear and organized, but needs notation/reference cleanup and clearer figure x-axis/“iterations” interpretation.  
- Originality: Meaningful extension of KG to continuous-domain batch setting and novel application of IPA to KG objective; novelty is incremental given prior IPA/q-EI work.  
- Significance: Promising practical value, especially under noise, but currently limited by empirical scope (scalability, overhead, asynchronous scenarios).  
- Pros for acceptance: Decision-theoretic formulation, reduction to q-EI, efficient stochastic gradient enabling joint optimization, robust noisy performance, and released implementation.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited exploration of larger q, no wall-clock overhead reporting, synchronous-only experiments, unexamined sensitivity to discretization M, and some ambiguity in baseline nomenclature.  
- Requested clarifications: Identify which baseline corresponds to MOE’s parallel EI (exact/approx q-EI), provide computational complexity per evaluation/gradient and larger-q results or ablations, discuss asynchronous adaptation (fantasizing), report wall-clock times and batch selection overhead, and clarify figure axes/initialization.  
- Suggested improvements: Ablation on discretization size M, report Monte Carlo sample counts and variance-reduction techniques, sensitivity analyses to GP hyperparameters/noise variance, and at least one higher-dimensional and larger-q benchmark.  
- Minor comments: Fix equation (4.1) conditioning/notation, grammar corrections, cite foundational IPA work (e.g., L’Ecuyer 1990), and standardize capitalization/nomenclature and URLs.  
- Overall evaluation: Solid, well-presented extension of KG to batch BO with an efficient estimator and strong noisy-results; recommend strengthening empirical evaluation (scalability, wall-clock, asynchronous) prior to acceptance.
