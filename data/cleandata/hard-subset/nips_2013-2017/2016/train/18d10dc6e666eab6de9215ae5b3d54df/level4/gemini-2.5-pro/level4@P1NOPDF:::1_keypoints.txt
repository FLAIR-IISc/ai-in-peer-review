1. Proposes q-KG, a parallel Knowledge Gradient acquisition function for batch-sequential Bayesian optimization.  
2. Presents computation details largely following the standard KG methodology.  
3. Empirical results show q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
4. Overall assessment: strong paper with significant contributions that merit NIPS publication.  
5. Main critique: paper does not report computational speed-ups of the batch approach versus fully sequential optimization.  
6. Literature review imprecision: EI integrated over the posterior of the optimum was previously introduced in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization," which also proposed Constant Liar.  
7. Review should acknowledge that Chevalier and Ginsbourger introduced the CL-mix heuristic.  
8. Prior work studied maximization of q-EI using natural gradients ("Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design").  
9. Question about assumptions on domain A and objective f (e.g., compactness, continuity) required for theoretical guarantees.  
10. Request justification for restricting the initial design to a Latin Hypercube Sample.  
11. Ask whether Algorithm 1 re-estimates or performs Bayesian updates of GP hyperparameters during the optimization loop.  
12. Clarification needed on whether the definition of g in Section 5.2 involves a maximization or a minimization.  
13. Request justification for the implicit smoothness assumption on g in Section 5.2.  
14. Note reliance on derivatives of the Cholesky factor; ask for discussion or citation since this computation is nontrivial.  
15. Typo: kernel likely intended to be Matérn-5/2, not 2/5.  
16. Reproducibility concern: were GPs implemented with a standard public package and are results sensitive to the specific GP implementation?
