- Paper proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) algorithm using Monte-Carlo gradient estimation and reports experimental gains over other batch Bayesian optimization methods.

- Claim that batch KG is treated as novel is questionable; authors should clarify contributions relative to prior batch KG work (e.g., [1],[2]); main novelty appears to be the gradient estimation method and it should be directly compared to existing techniques.

- Section 5.2 is important and underdeveloped; it needs substantial expansion for full methodological clarity.

- The comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) in noisy settings may be unfair—clarify whether a noise-robust EI variant was used or explicitly acknowledge standard EI’s noiseless assumption.

- Line 162 redefines the abbreviation “IPA,” which is potentially confusing and should be fixed.
