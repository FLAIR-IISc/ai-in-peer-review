- Proposes q-KG for parallel batch Bayesian optimization with an IPA-based gradient estimator; competitive on noiseless benchmarks and better under noise.  
- Evaluations on standard synthetic benchmarks and two ML tasks (CNN on CIFAR-10, logistic regression on MNIST) are appropriate.  
- Experimental section lacks wall-clock performance analysis; practical speedups vs random search and sequential BO are not reported.  
- The choice q=4 is unexplained; no ablation or sensitivity analysis over different q values is provided.  
- Novelty is limited: knowledge gradient and IPA are established techniques, and prior work has used IPA for batch q-EI.  
- The paper needs a clearer statement of what is new relative to prior batch BO methods and why q-KG outperforms q-EI theoretically/practically.  
- Potential impact is high if the method scales and yields measurable wall-clock gains across ML, vision, biology, and robotics.  
- Key practical details are missing: computational overhead per batch, scaling with q, and synchronous vs asynchronous operation.  
- Question: Did you compare to Wang et al. (2015) and explicitly distinguish q-EI (MOE) from Spearmint’s parallelization?  
- Question: How does computation and performance scale as q increases to larger values used in practice?  
- Question: Can the synchronous q-KG method be adapted to asynchronous evaluations, and how would benefits be preserved?  
- Question: Provide wall-clock runtime comparisons to random search and sequential BO and characterize overhead as a function of n, q, and dimensionality.  
- Question: Clarify what “iterations” on the plots denotes (function evaluations or batches) and whether initial design is included.  
- Minor: Equation (4.1) may be missing z^(1:n).  
- Minor: Rephrase “This architecture has been carefully tuned…” for clarity.  
- Minor: Avoid using parenthetical references as nouns; fix citation grammar.  
- Minor: Wang et al. (2015) is not the original IPA source—cite a foundational IPA reference as well.  
- Overall: Method is promising and well-presented, but needs stronger empirical validation (wall-clock, scaling with q, and comparisons to related batch BO methods).
