- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and empirically outperforms existing approaches.  
- Paper is well written and method is elegant and practically important for hyperparameter optimization.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (standard for GP BO) but this independence assumption can be unrealistic due to measurement noise and structural bias in HPO.  
- Experimental objective uses test set error, which is problematic for hyperparameter tuning; metrics like AUC or log loss would be more appropriate.  
- Experiments rely only on MNIST and CIFAR-10 and should include broader HPO benchmarks such as HPOlib.  
- Benchmark is limited to two datasets, one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and a CNN), reducing generality since optimizers can vary by algorithm family.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution but the limited experimental evaluation undermines the empirical claims.
