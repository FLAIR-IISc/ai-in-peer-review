- Introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) acquisition function extending KG to parallel evaluations.  
- Provides a rigorous derivation of q-KG using Gaussian processes and techniques analogous to sequential KG.  
- Empirical results show consistent superiority of q-KG over state-of-the-art batch BO methods on benchmarks.  
- Work is highly relevant to NIPS due to potential to accelerate high-dimensional scientific and engineering optimization.  
- Paper is well-structured and technically sound overall.  
- Literature claim about integrating EI with respect to posterior distributions is inaccurate; correct attribution needed (Constant Liar and CL-mix distinctions).  
- Missing citation/context: natural gradient method for q-EI maximization should be referenced (Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement).  
- Assumptions (compactness of search space and continuity of f) are not explicitly justified or referenced.  
- Rationale for restricting initial design to Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) is not provided.  
- Algorithm 1 omits hyperparameter re-estimation for the GP; clarify whether this is intentional or an oversight.  
- Equation 5.2 contains a max/min inconsistency for g that needs correction.  
- Claim that g is smooth requires justification, especially regarding derivatives of the Cholesky factor used in gradients.  
- Typo/notational issue: "Matérn is 5/2" should be corrected to standard "Matérn 5/2".  
- Experimental gaps: paper does not report wall-clock or computational speed-ups from parallelization.  
- Reproducibility concern: unclear whether identical GP models/implementations (e.g., GPflow vs. GPy) were used across experiments.  
- Overall assessment: q-KG is a significant, well-validated contribution; minor clarifications would improve clarity and reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
