- Addresses the important problem of parallelizing Bayesian optimization (BO) for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Proposes a novel, elegant algorithm to compute Bayes-optimal batches for parallel BO.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presented.  
- Proposed method outperforms existing parallel BO strategies in the reported experiments.  
- Work is notable for its principled, non-heuristic foundation.  
- Formulation of Bayes-optimal batches is a significant theoretical contribution.  
- Relies on the common Gaussian-process assumption of independent, normally distributed observation noise, which may not hold in practice.  
- Should acknowledge and discuss potential implications of biased or non-iid measurement error (e.g., from cross-validation or small datasets).  
- Empirical evaluation is weak due to choice of optimization criterion (uses test error; should consider metrics like AUC or log loss).  
- Benchmarking is limited; experiments should use standardized HPO suites (e.g., HPOlib) for better comparison.  
- Experimental scope is narrow (only MNIST and CIFAR-10, classification, two algorithms); needs more tasks, datasets, and models to demonstrate generality.  
- Overall: strong theoretical novelty but insufficient empirical validation; nevertheless worth consideration.
