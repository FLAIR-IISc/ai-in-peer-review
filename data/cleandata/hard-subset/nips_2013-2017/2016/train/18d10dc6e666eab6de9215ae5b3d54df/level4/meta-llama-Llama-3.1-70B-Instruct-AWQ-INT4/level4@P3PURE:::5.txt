This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method, aiming to find an optimal batch of design points. The authors propose a gradient estimation approach based on Monte-Carlo simulation to achieve this goal. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

However, I have several concerns and suggestions to strengthen the paper. Firstly, as the batch KG method is not novel to this paper, it is essential to clarify the contribution of this work with respect to previous studies on batch KG, such as [1] and [2]. In my opinion, the primary contribution lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for finding the optimal batch. To substantiate this claim, I recommend comparing the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method with previous batch KG approaches through experiments or mathematical analysis.

Secondly, Section 5.2 appears to be a crucial part of the paper, and I suggest expanding on this section to provide more insight into the proposed method. This would enhance the reader's understanding of the key concepts and ideas presented.

Thirdly, I would like to point out that the Expected Improvement (EI) method assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, the comparison in the noisy setting may be unfair. I would appreciate it if the authors could clarify whether a variant of EI that accounts for noise is used in the experiments. If the plain EI method is employed, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed in the context of EI's limitation in handling noisy observations.

Lastly, I noticed that the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162. To maintain consistency, it would be helpful to use the same definition throughout the paper.
