- Paper proposes a batch-sequential adaptation of the Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for Bayesian optimization.  
- Authors provide background on Gaussian processes and related work before defining the parallel q-KG criterion.  
- The paper details the computation of q-KG, following methodology from the standard KG criterion.  
- Numerical experiments show q-KG outperforms certain state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Reviewer rates the paper excellent and recommends acceptance to NIPS.  
- Notes that parallelizing BO has substantial societal impact.  
- Main critique: paper lacks analysis of speed-ups from moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Literature-review imprecisions: integrating EI over posteriors and Constant Liar ideas were previously discussed in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization."  
- Literature-review imprecision: Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix in [2], which should be acknowledged.  
- Literature-review imprecision: q-EI maximization using the natural gradient is covered in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design."  
- Question: Is the domain A compact and is the objective f continuous?  
- Question: Why is the initial design restricted to Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS)?  
- Question: In Algorithm 1, how are hyperparameters re-estimated or is Bayesian updating used?  
- Question: In Section 5.2, is the function g defined as a maximum or a minimum?  
- Concern: The smoothness of g in Section 5.2 is not immediately evident.  
- Concern: The derivative of the Cholesky factor used in derivations may not be straightforward.  
- Typo/question: The Matérn kernel is likely 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Question: For Figure 1 experiments, were the same GPs fitted using different software tools to check consistency?
