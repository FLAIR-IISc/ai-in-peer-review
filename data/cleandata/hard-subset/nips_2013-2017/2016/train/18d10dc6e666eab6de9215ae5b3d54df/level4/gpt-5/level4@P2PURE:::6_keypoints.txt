- Proposes parallel batch Bayesian optimization using the knowledge gradient (q-KG) and IPA-based gradient estimation for acquisition maximization.  
- Reports q-KG is competitive on noiseless benchmarks and outperforms others on noisy observations.  
- Reviewer appreciates the goal of parallel hyperparameter optimization but finds several aspects unclear and the experimental evaluation unconvincing.  

Technical quality:
- Experiments use common synthetic benchmarks plus CNN and logistic regression on CIFAR-10/MNIST.  
- Requests wall-clock performance or end-to-end efficiency analysis comparing parallelization to random search or sequential BO.  
- Questions why batch size q is fixed to 4 in all experiments.  

Novelty/Originality:
- Notes that using knowledge gradient and IPA is not new; prior work already applies IPA for parallel batch selection (e.g., parallel EI).  

Impact/Usefulness:
- Affirms that parallel hyperparameter tuning is highly relevant for many real-world applications.  

Clarity/Presentation:
- Finds technical background and methodological details well presented and experimental descriptions sufficient.  

Questions to authors:
- Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015)/parallel EI (q-EI), and can you clarify naming/distinctions from Spearmint’s parallel method?  
- How does the method scale with larger q (modern systems often use far more than 4 parallel evaluations)?  
- Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous execution?  
- Have you compared wall-clock time to random search or sequential BO, and what is the computational overhead per batch?  
- In plots, what does “iterations” denote (evaluations or batches), does it include the initial design, and why don’t all methods start from the same value?  

Minor comments:
- Possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1).  
- Typo: “achieve” → “achieves.”  
- Parenthetical references should be rephrased as nouns (e.g., “Snoek et al. [16] suggest…”).  
- Wang et al. (2015) applied IPA but did not introduce it; citation remark.
