- Proposes q-KG, a batch/parallel extension of the knowledge gradient that is Bayes-optimal (one-step) for selecting a final batch.  
- Introduces a Monte Carlo estimator and an IPA-based unbiased stochastic gradient estimator to jointly optimize batches via gradient methods.  
- Empirically outperforms or matches state-of-the-art batch BO methods on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR-10 CNN), with strong gains under noise.  
- Code for the method is released.  
- Builds on classical KG and recent parallel acquisition work; related prior ideas (batch-EI integration, Constant Liar, CL-mix, gradient-based q-EI) need clearer citation and positioning.  
- Strength: Strong decision-theoretic foundation—q-KG is Bayes-optimal and reduces to parallel EI in the noise-free recommend-from-evaluated case.  
- Strength: Computational contribution—unbiased IPA gradients and practical treatment of the Cholesky factor enable joint batch optimization without greedy construction.  
- Strength: Solid empirical evaluation including real ML tuning tasks and open-source code.  
- Strength: High practical impact by enabling parallel BO to leverage modern compute.  
- Weakness: No wall-clock or throughput analysis—missing time-to-target regret and overhead discussion as batch size q increases.  
- Weakness: Related-work precision lacking; must credit early batch-EI integration (Ginsbourger et al.), CL-mix, and gradient-based q-EI work.  
- Weakness: Missing explicit assumptions (e.g., A compact, f continuous) needed for existence/continuity of inner minima and IPA justification.  
- Weakness: Initial design restricted to Latin hypercube—no justification; alternative initializations might affect performance.  
- Weakness: Hyperparameter strategy unclear—paper states MLE re-training each iteration but does not discuss Bayesian hyperparameter averaging or sensitivity to hyperparameter uncertainty.  
- Weakness: Ambiguity in Section 5.2—need to confirm whether g is defined as a min or a max (sign/convention).  
- Weakness: Smoothness of g and conditions for interchanging derivative and expectation under IPA need precise regularity assumptions and proofs.  
- Weakness: Differentiation of the Cholesky factor is non-trivial—requires citation or derivation to justify used expressions.  
- Weakness: Typo/error—Matérn kernel reported as 2/5 but should be 5/2.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparability—need to confirm identical GP kernels and hyperparameter fitting across software; GP implementation differences can affect results.  
- Quality: Technically sound overall with principled derivation and reasonable stochastic gradient scheme; theoretical assumptions should be stated more explicitly.  
- Clarity: Generally well-written; fix Matérn order, clarify min/max in g, expand on differentiability and Cholesky derivatives, and refine related-work attributions.  
- Originality: Novel decision-theoretic Bayes-optimal q-KG framing and IPA-based gradient estimator, though related parallel acquisition and gradient-based q-EI work exists.  
- Significance: High practical relevance and likely adoption given empirical gains under noise and released code.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled contribution, efficient implementation, strong empirical performance, practical impact, and open-source code.  
- Cons for acceptance: Lacks wall-clock speed-up analysis, has some related-work imprecision, and needs clearer exposition of technical assumptions and derivative details.  
- Questions for authors: Are A compact and f continuous? Why choose LHS for initial design? Do you use MLE only or Bayesian hyperparameter treatment? Is g a min or max? Please justify smoothness and Cholesky derivative (references/derivations), confirm Matérn 5/2, and confirm identical GP setups across experiments.
