- Introduces q-KG, a parallel Knowledge Gradient acquisition function for batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization, with optimization procedure akin to sequential KG.  
- Provides numerical experiments showing q-KG outperforms several state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant, publishable contributions and practical importance for parallelizing Bayesian optimization.  
- Suggestion: include a study comparing wall-clock computational speed-ups of the batch approach versus the sequential counterpart.  
- Literature imprecision: integrating EI over posteriors and the Constant Liar heuristic were previously introduced in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization."  
- Literature imprecision: Chevalier et al. [2] specifically presented the CL-mix heuristic and should be noted as such.  
- Literature imprecision: optimization of q-EI with natural gradients was explored in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design."  
- Question 1: Clarify any implicit assumptions that domain A is compact and objective f is continuous.  
- Question 2: Explain why Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS) is used for initialization and whether other space-filling designs could be used.  
- Question 3: Describe how GP hyperparameters are handled—re-estimated each iteration (e.g., marginal likelihood) or treated fully Bayesian.  
- Question 4: In Section 5.2, clarify whether the definition of function g involves maximization or minimization.  
- Question 5: Provide a brief justification for the claimed smoothness of g used for optimization.  
- Question 6: Since computing derivatives of the Cholesky factor is nontrivial, include a reference or brief explanation for reproducibility.  
- Comment 7: Minor typo—Matérn kernel parameter likely 5/2 rather than 2/5.  
- Question 8: For Figure 1 experiments, confirm whether GP models for all methods were fitted with the same software/configuration to rule out implementation differences.
