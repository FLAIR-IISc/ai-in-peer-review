This paper introduces a parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, designed for scenarios where function evaluations are time-consuming. The authors propose a novel acquisition function, the q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), which identifies a batch of `q` points for simultaneous evaluation in parallel. The paper also presents an efficient computational approach for optimizing this acquisition function. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through a series of empirical results.

The problem addressed is highly relevant and important. The ability to parallelize expensive function evaluations can significantly improve the performance and wall-clock time of many data-driven applications. By moving from a sequential, one-point-at-a-time evaluation to a batch approach that proposes `q` points per iteration, the q-KG method offers a promising way to accelerate the optimization process. This is an interesting and valuable idea for the community to explore.

While the proposed method is compelling, there are a few points that could be addressed to strengthen the paper. The reliance on a Gaussian Process model, while standard in Bayesian Optimization, may have limitations in very high-dimensional settings, such as the optimization of neural network architectures. A discussion of this assumption would be welcome. Furthermore, the paper would be significantly strengthened by the inclusion of theoretical results, such as error bounds for the proposed algorithm, and a quantitative analysis of its computational complexity with respect to the input dimension.

Despite these suggestions, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The method is well-motivated, the experimental validation is sound, and the paper is well-written overall.
