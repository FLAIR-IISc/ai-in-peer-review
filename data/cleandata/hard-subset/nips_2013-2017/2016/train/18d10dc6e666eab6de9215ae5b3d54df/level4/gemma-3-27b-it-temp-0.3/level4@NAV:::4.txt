This paper investigates the parallel knowledge gradient method within the context of batch Bayesian Optimization, specifically addressing scenarios where function evaluation is computationally expensive. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, q-KG, designed to suggest multiple points for simultaneous evaluation, and present an efficient method for its evaluation. The empirical results effectively demonstrate the validity of their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed is significant; distributing computational workload is crucial for enhancing the performance of data-driven applications. By considering *q* knowledge gradients to propose *q* points for evaluation in each iteration, the proposed algorithm substantially improves efficiency compared to sequential point-by-point evaluation. This is a promising direction for research. 

However, the reliance on a Gaussian process assumption warrants further consideration, particularly when applied to high-dimensional input spaces common in neural network architectures. Strengthening the paper with theoretical guarantees regarding error bounds and a quantitative analysis of computational complexity with respect to input dimension would be beneficial. 

Overall, this is a strong contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The methodology is sound, the experimental validation is thorough, and the paper is generally well-written. Minor revisions are suggested: line 36, rephrase "the set of points to evaluate next that is"; line 95, define the variable A; line 104, clarify the input of μ<sup>n</sup>; line 128, briefly discuss the parallel Expected Improvement (EI) algorithm; and line 140, consider alternative notation for A.
