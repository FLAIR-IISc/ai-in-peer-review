- Introduces q-KG for parallel batch Bayesian optimization using IPA to estimate gradients and select Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive without observation noise and outperforms others with noise.  
- Reviewer appreciates leveraging parallel HPO but finds aspects unclear and experimental evidence unconvincing.  
- Technical: uses common benchmarks plus CNN/logistic experiments but lacks wall-clock time comparisons and justification for fixing q=4.  
- Novelty: combines established ideas (knowledge gradient and IPA); closely related prior work (Wang et al. 2015) applies IPA to parallel EI.  
- Impact: parallel hyperparameter tuning is highly relevant across ML, CV, biology, and robotics.  
- Clarity: technical background and experiments are generally well-described.  
- Question: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015)/parallel EI (q-EI) and clarified differences from existing methods?  
- Question: How does the method scale to much larger q (tens or hundreds of parallel runs) as used in prior work?  
- Question: Can the approach be extended to asynchronous parallel execution?  
- Question: Have you evaluated wall-clock time vs. random search or sequential BO, and what is the per-batch computational overhead?  
- Question: Clarify x-axis "iterations" — does it mean evaluations or batches, are initial designs included, and why do starting values differ across methods?
