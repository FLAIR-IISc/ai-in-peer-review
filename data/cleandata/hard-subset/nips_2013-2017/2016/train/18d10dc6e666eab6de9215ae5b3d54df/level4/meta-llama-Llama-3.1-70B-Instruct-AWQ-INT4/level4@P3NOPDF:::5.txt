This paper proposes a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method, which aims to find an optimal batch of design points through a gradient estimation approach based on Monte-Carlo simulation. The experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

However, I have some concerns regarding the novelty and contribution of this paper. Specifically, the batch KG method is not a new concept, and previous studies, such as [1] and [2], have explored similar ideas. To clarify, I would like the authors to explicitly state the contribution of their work in relation to these existing studies. In my opinion, the key innovation lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for finding the optimal batch. If this is indeed the case, I believe it would be beneficial to compare the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed method with previous batch KG approaches through experiments or mathematical analysis.

Furthermore, I found Section 5.2 to be crucial to the understanding of this paper, and I suggest expanding on this section to provide more details. This would enable readers to better comprehend the underlying concepts and appreciate the significance of the proposed method.

Additionally, I would like to point out that the Expected Improvement (EI) method typically assumes noiseless observations. Therefore, the comparison in the noisy setting may be unfair. I would appreciate it if the authors could clarify whether a variant of EI that accounts for noise is used in the experiments. If the plain EI method is employed, I suggest discussing the results in Section 6.2 with the caveat that EI does not consider noise.

Finally, I noticed that the abbreviation "IPA" is redefined on line 162. To maintain consistency and avoid confusion, I recommend using a different abbreviation or avoiding redefinition altogether.
