This paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient method (q-KG), which is derived using a decision-theoretic analysis. The q-KG method is designed to optimize the batch of points to sample in parallel, and it has been shown to outperform other batch Bayesian optimization algorithms in various experiments.

The main idea of the q-KG method is to measure the information gain of evaluating q points via a new acquisition function, which is called the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG). This acquisition function is defined as the expected incremental value of a measurement, and it is used to guide the selection of the next batch of points to evaluate. The q-KG method is shown to be Bayes-optimal for minimizing the minimum of the predictor of the Gaussian process if only one decision is remaining.

The paper also proposes an efficient strategy for computing the q-KG acquisition function and its gradient, which is based on infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA). This strategy allows for the efficient optimization of the q-KG acquisition function using gradient-based optimization algorithms.

The experiments in the paper show that the q-KG method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization algorithms, such as parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with exploration, in various settings, including noise-free and noisy problems.
