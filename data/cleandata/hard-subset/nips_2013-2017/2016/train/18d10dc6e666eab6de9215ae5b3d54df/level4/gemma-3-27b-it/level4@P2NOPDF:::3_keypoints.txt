- Proposes a stochastic gradient optimization algorithm using Bayesian modeling for multivariate objectives over finite domains.
- Main contribution: a parallelizable strategy for selecting batches of evaluation points each iteration.
- Extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) acquisition function from single-point to batch selection.
- KG guides sampling by estimating expected improvement from adding samples.
- Batch KG introduces computational intractability due to optimizing over multiple points.
- Addresses intractability with Monte Carlo estimation and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to approximate gradients.
- Empirical results show competitive or superior performance to existing methods, notably confidence-bound approaches.
- Paper is clearly written and potentially useful in practice.
- Doubts about novelty: extension is logical and builds on prior KG work; IPA is a standard technique—innovation is mainly their combination.
- Technical issue: equation (3.1) unclear—need clarification on how μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) are defined for sequences of vectors.
