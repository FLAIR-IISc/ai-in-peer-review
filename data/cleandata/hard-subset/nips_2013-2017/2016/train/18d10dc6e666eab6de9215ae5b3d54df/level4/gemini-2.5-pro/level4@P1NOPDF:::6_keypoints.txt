- Proposes q-KG: a parallel batch Bayesian optimization method using the knowledge gradient and IPA to compute gradients for tractability.
- Empirical results claim q-KG is competitive on noiseless benchmarks and outperforms baselines on noisy problems, but evaluation needs strengthening.
- Lacks wall-clock time comparisons; plotting performance vs. number of evaluations fails to capture optimization overhead.
- Batch size fixed at q=4 without justification; no sensitivity or ablation study on q.
- Limited novelty: KG + IPA are known techniques; contribution mainly applying them together to KG and clarifying distinction from prior work is needed.
- Prior work (Wang et al., 2015) used IPA for parallel EI; relationship/differences to that work must be discussed and compared directly.
- Baselines should be clearly labeled (e.g., “q-EI”) to distinguish parallel EI variants and ensure fair comparison.
- Need experiments and analysis on scalability with respect to batch size q (performance and computational scaling for larger q).
- Question whether q-KG can be extended to asynchronous parallel settings common in practice.
- Must quantify computational overhead of selecting batches (impact on wall-clock runtime vs. sequential BO and parallel random search).
- Clarify plotting: define “iterations” (batches vs. total evaluations), state whether initial design points are included, and explain why curves don’t start from the same value.
- Minor issues: possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1) conditioning; cite original IPA source (Wang et al. applied but did not introduce IPA); typo ("achieve"→"achieves"); fix citation grammar.
