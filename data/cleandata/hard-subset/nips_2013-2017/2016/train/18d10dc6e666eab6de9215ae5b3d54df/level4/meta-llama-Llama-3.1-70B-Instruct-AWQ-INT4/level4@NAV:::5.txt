This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) method. A Monte Carlo simulation-based gradient estimation approach is proposed for identifying the optimal batch of design points. Experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method over other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) techniques. 

1. While batch KG is not a novel concept introduced by this paper, it is essential to clarify its contribution relative to existing batch KG literature, such as references [1] and [2]. In my view, the primary contribution lies in the proposed gradient estimation method for optimal batch selection. A thorough comparison of the proposed method's efficiency and effectiveness with previous batch KG approaches through experiments or mathematical analysis would be beneficial.

2. Section 5.2 requires more elaboration, as it appears to be a crucial aspect of this paper.

3. Given that Expected Improvement (EI) typically assumes noiseless observations, the comparison in the noisy setting may be unfair. It would be helpful to clarify whether a noise-considering variant of EI is employed. If the standard EI is used, the results in Section 6.2 should be discussed in the context of EI's noise-agnostic nature.

4. The abbreviation "IPA" is redefined in line 162; consider adding a brief explanation or reference to maintain clarity.
