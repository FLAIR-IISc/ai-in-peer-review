- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic, one-step Bayes-optimal acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling and infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to obtain an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator for optimizing q-KG.  
- Enables joint gradient-based optimization over the entire batch (q points) rather than greedy/sequential construction.  
- Empirically compares q-KG to strong baselines on synthetic and real ML tasks, showing competitive or superior performance, especially in noisy settings and on CIFAR-10 CNN tuning.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient implementation and derivation details.  
- Conceptually extends sequential Knowledge Gradient to batches—this generalization is valuable but could be viewed as incremental; main novelty is the computational tractability via IPA.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability with batch size q, dimension d, and Monte Carlo samples; empirical timing/complexity results are needed.  
- Relies on discretizing domain A; sensitivity to discrete set size M and its impact on performance should be analyzed.  
- Minor notation clarity issue: make µ(x(1:n)) and K(x(1:n), x2) explicit as mean vectors and covariance matrices for broader readability.  
- Overall assessment: solid, significant contribution with strong empirical support and practical utility, despite limited conceptual novelty and some open practical analysis questions.
