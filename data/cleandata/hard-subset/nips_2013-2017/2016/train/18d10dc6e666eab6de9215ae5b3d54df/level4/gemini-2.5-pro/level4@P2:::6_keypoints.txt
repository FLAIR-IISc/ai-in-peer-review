- Addresses an important practical problem: parallelizing Bayesian Optimization for expensive tasks like hyperparameter tuning.  
- Proposes q-KG: a principled, decision-theoretic one-step Bayes-optimal acquisition for batch selection.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to obtain an efficient gradient estimator for optimizing the q-KG acquisition.  
- Experimental results are promising, especially showing q-KG outperforms other batch methods in noisy settings.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear technical details and GP background.  
- Missing wall-clock time analysis to quantify computational overhead vs. total time-to-target and comparison with simpler baselines (e.g., parallel random search, sequential BO).  
- Fixed batch size q=4 across experiments seems arbitrary; an ablation study on q is needed to assess scalability and performance with varying parallelism.  
- Experimental plots lack clarity: unclear whether x-axis "iterations" means batches or evaluations and whether initial design points are included; starting-performance discrepancies need explanation.  
- Need clearer discussion of relation to prior work, especially Wang et al. (2015) using IPA for parallel EI; explicitly distinguish q-KG from q-EI and other heuristic parallel EI methods.  
- Scalability concerns: experiments limited to small q; must evaluate how performance and acquisition optimization overhead scale with larger degrees of parallelism.  
- Method is synchronous; paper should discuss extensions or implications for asynchronous execution where runtimes are heterogeneous.  
- Notation in Eq. (4.1) should be clarified (expectation over y(z(1:q)) given past data).  
- Minor fixes: grammar in Section 6.1.2, citation style (avoid parenthetical nouns), and correct foundational citation for IPA (e.g., L’Ecuyer 1990) rather than implying Wang et al. introduced it.
