- Proposes q-KG: a knowledge-gradient acquisition for parallel batch Bayesian optimization with IPA-based gradient estimation.  
- Shows competitive performance on synthetic and ML hyperparameter tuning; outperforms others in noisy settings.  
- Leveraging multi-core environments for HPO is valuable, but method and results need clarification.  
- Missing wall-clock time analysis and comparisons to random search or sequential BO to demonstrate practical speedup.  
- Fixed batch size q=4 is unjustified; a sensitivity analysis over different q values is needed.  
- Technical derivations are generally sound but would benefit from clearer, more concise notation and explanations.  
- Novelty lies in integrating KG with IPA for parallel BO, but relation to prior IPA-based parallel EI (Wang et al., 2015) is not fully clarified.  
- Paper does not clearly articulate why KG is preferable to expected improvement in the parallel setting.  
- Impact is potentially high for expensive evaluations, but stronger evidence of scalability and real-world applicability is required.  
- Experimental plots lack clarity: ambiguous x-axis ("iterations") and unclear inclusion of the initial design causing inconsistent starting points.  
- Minor grammatical/stylistic errors and a likely missing term in Equation (4.1).  
- Question: Have you directly compared to Wang et al. (2015) or specified which "parallel EI in MOE" was used?  
- Question: How does the method scale to larger q (hundreds of parallel runs) and what are expected gains?  
- Question: Is asynchronous execution feasible or supported as an extension?  
- Question: What is the wall-clock performance and computational overhead for computing each new batch?  
- Question: Clarify what "iterations" denotes on plots and why methods do not start from the same value.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of KG to parallel BO, efficient IPA-based optimization, strong noisy-performance, well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of wall-clock/simple-baseline comparisons, unjustified q choice, unclear advantage over parallel EI, limited scalability/asynchrony discussion, minor issues.  
- Overall assessment: weak accept — solid technical contribution but weakened by missing practical performance analysis and design justifications.
