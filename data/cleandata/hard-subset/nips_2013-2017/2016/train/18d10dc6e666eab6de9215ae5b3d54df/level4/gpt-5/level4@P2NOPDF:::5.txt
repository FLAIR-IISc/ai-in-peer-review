This paper presents a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) for Bayesian optimization. To select an optimal batch of design points, the authors propose a Monte Carlo–based gradient estimator. Empirically, the method appears to outperform existing batch BO baselines.

1) Clarify contribution relative to prior batch KG work. Batch KG itself is not novel. Please clearly position the paper with respect to prior batch KG methods, particularly [1] and [2]. My understanding is that the key contribution is a Monte Carlo gradient estimator to optimize the batch acquisition. If so, the paper should compare the efficiency and effectiveness of the proposed estimator against prior batch KG approaches, either through head-to-head experiments (including runtime and sample efficiency) or through mathematical analysis (e.g., unbiasedness/consistency of the estimator, variance properties, convergence guarantees, and computational complexity per iteration).

2) Expand Section 5.2. This section appears central to the paper and is currently underdeveloped. Please provide more detail on:
- The derivation of the gradient estimator and any assumptions required.
- Its variance reduction or control, and any bias–variance trade-offs.
- Practical implementation details (e.g., pathwise vs. likelihood-ratio estimators, handling of discontinuities/nondifferentiability, reparameterization, common random numbers).
- Algorithmic steps or pseudocode for batch selection, including initialization, step sizes, and stopping criteria.
- Sensitivity to key hyperparameters (number of Monte Carlo samples, batch size) and computational cost.

3) Fairness of EI comparisons under noise. Standard Expected Improvement (EI) assumes noiseless observations, so comparisons in noisy settings can be misleading if plain EI is used. Please clarify whether a noise-aware EI variant is employed (e.g., EI under observation noise or a suitable surrogate like predictive entropy search for noisy settings). If plain EI is used, the results in Section 6.2 should explicitly acknowledge that EI is disadvantaged under noise, and the discussion should explain how this impacts the observed performance gaps. Ideally, include a noise-aware EI baseline or additional commentary to ensure a fair comparison.

4) Acronym consistency. In line 162, the abbreviation “IPA” appears to be redefined. Please ensure that “IPA” is introduced once, defined consistently, and used uniformly throughout the paper.

Overall, the paper addresses an important problem in batch Bayesian optimization and shows promising empirical results. Strengthening the positioning relative to prior batch KG work, expanding the core methodological exposition, and ensuring fair baselines under noise would substantially improve clarity and impact.
