- Novel extension of Knowledge Gradient (KG) to parallel batch Bayesian optimization (q-KG) with decision-theoretic grounding.  
- IPA-based gradient estimation enables efficient, gradient-based maximization of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Strong empirical performance: q-KG outperforms several batch BO baselines, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Clear presentation and well-defined algorithm with a detailed experimental setup.  
- Code availability enhances reproducibility and practical adoption.  
- Needs clearer discussion and positioning relative to prior batch KG work (e.g., Wang et al., 2015) to establish novelty.  
- Section 5.2 (IPA derivation) lacks sufficient intuition and intermediate steps; requires elaboration for clarity.  
- Comparison against standard EI in noisy settings may be unfair; should consider noise-aware EI variants or justify baseline choice.  
- Unnecessary redefinition of the abbreviation "IPA" creates minor confusion and should be removed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, shows significant practical improvements, and is made reproducible.  
- Arguments against acceptance: novelty relative to existing batch KG methods is insufficiently clarified, IPA exposition is thin, and some baseline comparisons may be unfair.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with significant potential; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing related work, IPA detail, and noisy-baseline comparisons.
