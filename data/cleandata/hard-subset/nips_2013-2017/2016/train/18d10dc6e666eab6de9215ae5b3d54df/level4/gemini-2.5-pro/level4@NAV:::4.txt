This paper addresses the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on applications where function evaluations are computationally expensive. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, the q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), designed to select a batch of `q` points for simultaneous evaluation. Furthermore, they present an efficient computational approach for optimizing this acquisition function. The efficacy of the proposed method is demonstrated through a series of empirical results.

The problem investigated is of significant practical importance, as the ability to distribute workloads is crucial for the performance of modern data-driven applications. By proposing a batch of `q` points at each step, the algorithm substantially accelerates the optimization process compared to traditional sequential methods. This represents a promising and interesting direction for research in the field.

A potential limitation is the reliance on the Gaussian Process assumption, which may not be well-suited for very high-dimensional input spaces, such as those found in neural network architecture search. The paper would be strengthened by the inclusion of theoretical results, particularly regarding error bounds for the method and a quantitative analysis of its computational complexity as a function of the input dimension.

Overall, however, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The methodology is sound, the experiments are well-designed, and the paper is clearly written.
