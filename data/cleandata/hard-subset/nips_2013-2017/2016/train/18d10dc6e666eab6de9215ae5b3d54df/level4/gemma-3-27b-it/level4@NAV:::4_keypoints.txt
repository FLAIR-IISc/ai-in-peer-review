- Paper proposes q-KG, a novel acquisition function for batch Bayesian Optimization and an efficient method to evaluate it, with empirical results matching theory.
- Addresses an important problem: reducing wall-clock time by proposing q points per iteration for parallel evaluations.
- q-KG substantially improves efficiency over sequential approaches in experiments.
- Main methodological reliance on Gaussian process models may limit applicability, especially in high-dimensional settings like neural networks.
- Paper would benefit from theoretical guarantees (error bounds) and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to dimensionality.
- Overall contribution is solid: well-founded methodology, rigorous experiments, and generally clear writing.
