- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method that computes Bayes-optimal batches for parallel evaluations.  
- Addresses a practical hyperparameter optimization problem where parallel evaluations are available.  
- Derives a theoretically grounded, decision-theory-based approach to batch selection.  
- Distinguishes itself from prior parallel BO methods by explicitly targeting Bayes optimality for batches.  
- Develops an efficient gradient-based optimization strategy using an IPA-based gradient estimator to handle q-KG’s computational complexity.  
- Paper is clearly written and well organized, making the method and derivation accessible.  
- Empirical comparisons use strong, relevant baselines and show competitive performance.  
- Assumes Gaussian process with independent, normal noise, which may be unrealistic for HPO where noise is biased or correlated (e.g., CV estimates).  
- Empirical evaluation is limited in scope, relying mainly on test error as the performance metric, which can be problematic.  
- Benchmark diversity is limited to two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10) and two models (logistic regression, CNN); broader HPOlib benchmarks are recommended.  
- Lacks an ablation study to isolate the contributions of q-KG components, particularly the IPA gradient estimator.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, practically relevant, competitive results, and public code for reproducibility.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive noise assumptions, limited and potentially suboptimal evaluation metrics, and unclear practical advantage over simpler methods.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand empirical evaluation (metrics and benchmarks) and discuss/extend beyond the Gaussian noise assumption.
