- Introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion, a significant contribution to Bayesian optimization.  
- Provides thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes and a clear definition of the parallel q-KG criterion.  
- Details computation of q-KG closely mirroring the standard KG criterion.  
- Presents numerical experiments showing q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Overall assessment: excellent paper with strong justification for publication and substantial potential societal impact from parallelizing BO.  
- Missing analysis of actual speed-ups achieved by moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Literature review contains imprecise claims: EI integration over posteriors and Constant Liar were already explored in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization"; CL-mix presented by Chevalier et al.; q-EI with natural gradient also studied previously.  
- Question whether the domain A is compact and whether the objective f is assumed continuous.  
- Why is the initial design restricted to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS)?  
- Algorithm 1: no discussion of hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating of GP hyperparameters.  
- In Section 5.2, unclear whether g is defined as a maximization or minimization problem.  
- Concerns about the smoothness of g and the nontrivial derivative of the Cholesky factor used in gradients.  
- Typo or mistake: Matérn parameter likely 5/2, not 2/5.  
- For Figure 1 experiments, asks whether the same Gaussian processes could be fitted using different software packages (reproducibility/software consistency).
