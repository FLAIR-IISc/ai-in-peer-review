- Proposes q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), a novel acquisition function for selecting a batch of q points in parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization approach for the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical results demonstrate the method's effectiveness on time-consuming evaluation tasks.  
- Addresses the highly relevant problem of parallelizing Bayesian optimization to speed up evaluations.  
- q-KG’s batch selection (multiple points per iteration) can significantly accelerate optimization versus sequential methods.  
- Potential limitation: reliance on Gaussian Process models, which may be unsuitable in high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural architecture search).  
- Missing theoretical analysis: no error bounds or formal guarantees are provided.  
- Lacks quantitative analysis of computational complexity, especially scaling with input dimension.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated contribution with sound experiments and clear writing.
