This paper presents a novel batch Bayesian optimization (BO) algorithm, the parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method, designed for scenarios with parallel function evaluations. The core idea is to derive a batch acquisition function based on the knowledge gradient, aiming for Bayes-optimal batch selection. To address the computational challenges of maximizing this function, the authors propose an efficient gradient estimation technique leveraging infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA). Experiments demonstrate that q-KG outperforms existing batch BO algorithms on both synthetic and real-world problems, particularly in noisy evaluation settings.

The paper builds upon the established field of Bayesian optimization, specifically extending the sequential Knowledge Gradient method to a batch setting. Prior work in batch BO, such as parallel Expected Improvement (EI) and Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) approaches, often relies on iterative or greedy selection strategies. This work distinguishes itself by considering the batch of points collectively during optimization, aiming for a more globally optimal solution. The connection to decision-theoretic analysis, choosing the batch that’s optimal assuming only one batch remains, is a strong point. Several recent papers have explored similar ideas, including those leveraging gradient-based optimization of parallel EI ([15, 25] as cited in the paper) and parallel entropy search ([18]), highlighting the current interest in collective batch optimization. 

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The q-KG method offers a principled approach to batch Bayesian optimization, grounded in decision theory and the knowledge gradient framework.
* **Theoretical Justification:** The derivation of the q-KG acquisition function and its connection to Bayes optimality are well-motivated.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The proposed IPA-based gradient estimation technique is a significant contribution, enabling efficient optimization of the otherwise computationally expensive q-KG function.
* **Empirical Performance:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate the superiority of q-KG over competing algorithms, particularly in noisy environments and on complex problems like CNN tuning. The code availability is a definite plus.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and structured, facilitating understanding of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Related Work Clarification:** The paper acknowledges prior work on batch KG but could benefit from a more detailed discussion of how this work differs from and builds upon those previous approaches, specifically addressing the question of novelty. Specifically, clarifying the contribution beyond simply estimating the gradient would strengthen the paper. A direct comparison (experimental or theoretical) to [26] (Wang et al., 2015) is needed, as it also addresses batch KG.
* **IPA Section Detail:** While the importance of Section 5.2 (IPA-based gradient estimation) is recognized, the explanation could be more detailed.  A more intuitive explanation of how IPA is applied in this specific context would be valuable.
* **Fairness of Noisy Comparison:** The comparison against standard EI in noisy settings is potentially unfair, as EI is designed for noiseless evaluations. The paper should either employ a noise-aware variant of EI or provide a discussion explaining the limitations of comparing against standard EI in the presence of noise.
* **Redefinition of IPA:** The redefinition of "IPA" in line 162 is unnecessary and slightly confusing.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound method for batch Bayesian optimization.
* The proposed gradient estimation technique is a significant contribution, enabling practical application of the q-KG method.
* The experimental results provide strong evidence of the effectiveness of q-KG, particularly in challenging scenarios.
* The code availability promotes reproducibility and further research.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The novelty of the approach relative to existing batch KG methods needs to be more clearly articulated.
* The explanation of the IPA-based gradient estimation could be more detailed.
* The comparison in noisy settings requires further justification or modification.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The q-KG method offers a principled and efficient approach to batch optimization, and the experimental results are compelling. Addressing the weaknesses related to related work clarification, IPA detail, and the noisy comparison would significantly strengthen the paper.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The authors should address the points raised above to provide a more comprehensive and convincing presentation of their work.
