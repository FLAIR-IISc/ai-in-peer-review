Addresses important problem of parallel Bayesian optimization for efficient hyperparameter tuning.  
Proposes q-KG: a novel, theoretically grounded extension of the knowledge gradient to compute Bayes-optimal evaluation batches.  
Provides an efficient computation strategy for q-KG via infinitesimal perturbation analysis.  
Paper is well-written and clearly explains core ideas.  
Experimental evaluation is limited and could be stronger.  
Using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; suggest metrics like AUC or log-loss.  
Benchmarking is narrow (only MNIST, CIFAR-10; logistic regression and CNN); recommend broader suites (e.g., HPOlib) and more varied tasks/algorithms.  
Assumption of independent normally distributed GP observation noise may be unrealistic for HPO and should be discussed.  
Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong theoretical contribution, practical impact, clear presentation.  
Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments, simplifying noise assumption, lack of broader comparisons.  
Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand evaluation and address the noise-assumption discussion.
