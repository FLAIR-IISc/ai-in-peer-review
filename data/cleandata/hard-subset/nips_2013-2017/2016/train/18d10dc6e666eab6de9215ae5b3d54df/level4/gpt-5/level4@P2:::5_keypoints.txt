- Proposes q-KG: a batch (parallel) knowledge-gradient acquisition for GP-based Bayesian optimization with an IPA-based stochastic gradient estimator and domain discretization.
- Method selects q points by maximizing expected one-step reduction in the GP posterior minimum and handles noisy observations.
- Strength: Principled decision-theoretic (Bayes-optimal one-step) objective for batches.
- Strength: Efficient, unbiased IPA gradient estimator enabling joint batch optimization.
- Strength: Naturally accounts for observation noise and shows substantial empirical gains in noisy settings.
- Strength: Broad empirical evaluation on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning; code released.
- Weakness: Novelty unclear relative to prior batch KG work (Wang et al. 2015; Peng 2010); needs explicit comparison and clarification of contributions.
- Request: Provide a side-by-side comparison (conceptual/empirical) with [1,2], specifying what is new in formulation, computation, and theory.
- Request: Expand Section 5.2 to state conditions for differentiation under expectation and give a clear unbiasedness proof (beyond supplement).
- Request: Discuss gradient-estimator variance, variance-reduction options, and practical MC-sample choices per gradient step.
- Request: Provide per-iteration computational complexity in terms of q, d, and |A_n| (e.g., Cholesky costs) and contrast with q-EI optimizers.
- Request: Detail optimizer settings (step sizes, restarts, stopping criteria) and sensitivity to these choices.
- Concern: Fairness under noise—clarify which EI variants were used and whether noise-aware EI or PPES baselines were included or feasible.
- Notation: Fix inconsistent acronym/notation use (define IPA once; clarify A vs A_n and other symbols).
- Suggestion: Report discretization sensitivity (dependence on M and sampling strategy for A_n) with ablations or plots.
- Suggestion: Include wall-clock timings and per-iteration costs for varying q and d, compared to q-EI and PPES.
- Suggestion: Improve reproducibility by reporting number of MC samples, restarts, hardware, and average runtime per iteration.
- Minor edits: fix typos, standardize capitalization/notation for Gaussian processes and vectors/sets.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound, but Section 5.2 lacks rigor on assumptions/variance/complexity and noisy baselines may be unfair.
- Overall appraisal: Moderately-to-highly original and highly significant if computational advantages and fair noisy comparisons are substantiated; several clarifying experiments and analyses needed.
