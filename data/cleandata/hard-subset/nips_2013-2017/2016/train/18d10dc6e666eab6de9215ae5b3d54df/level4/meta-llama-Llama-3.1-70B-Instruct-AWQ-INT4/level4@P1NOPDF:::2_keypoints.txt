- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and reportedly outperforms existing approaches.  
- Paper is well-written, easy to follow, and presents an elegant solution to parallelizing Bayesian optimization.  
- Determining a Bayes-optimal batch is a promising and novel contribution.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors, which is common but can be problematic for hyperparameter optimization.  
- Independence assumption may be unrealistic because measurement errors often reflect structural bias (e.g., generalization vs. empirical estimates, small sample effects).  
- Theoretical development is strong and convincing.  
- Experiments are disappointing: using test set error as the optimization criterion is suboptimal and problematic for HPO.  
- Experimental benchmarks are limited: only MNIST and CIFAR-10 used instead of broader suites like HPOlib.  
- Benchmark diversity is too low (only 2 datasets, one task—classification—and two learning algorithms), reducing confidence in empirical claims.  
- Recommendation: strengthen the paper with more diverse benchmarks, tasks, datasets, and algorithms to better validate the approach.
