- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm that combines Bayesian modeling with multivariate optimization over a finite domain.  
- Key innovation: selecting multiple evaluation points per iteration to enable parallelization.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected improvement and guide point selection.  
- Generalizes KG to batch (q-KG) decision-making for selecting multiple sampling points.  
- Computational intractability addressed by an algorithm that uses Monte Carlo sampling and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance against existing methods, particularly Confidence Bound–based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and has promising practical applications.  
- Novelty concern: primarily an extension of existing KG methods to batch settings, so overall novelty may be limited.  
- Main claimed novelty is the q-KG gradient estimation via IPA, but IPA is viewed as a relatively standard technique.  
- Technical clarification needed: unclear notation in equation (3.1) — how mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) are defined for sequences of vectors.
