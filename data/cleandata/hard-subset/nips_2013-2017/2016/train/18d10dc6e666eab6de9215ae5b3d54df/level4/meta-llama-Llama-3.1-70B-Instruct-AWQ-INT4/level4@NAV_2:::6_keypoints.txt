- Proposes a method for parallel batch Bayesian optimization using a parallel extension of the knowledge gradient (q-KG) to choose Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis to efficiently estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Experiments show q-KG is competitive with existing methods on noise-free problems and outperforms them on noisy problems.  
- Technically sound and well-motivated; method and derivations are clearly explained.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear background and explanation of key concepts.  
- Original contribution: extending knowledge gradient to parallel batches and using IPA for gradient estimation.  
- High potential impact for parallel hyperparameter tuning in ML, vision, biology, and robotics.  
- Significant contribution to Bayesian optimization by improving efficiency of batch selection.  
- For acceptance: novel method for parallel q-KG, practical impact on hyperparameter tuning, technical soundness, and well-designed experiments.  
- Against acceptance: lacks clear comparison to other parallel BO methods (e.g., Wang et al., 2015).  
- Against acceptance: no analysis of the computational overhead of the proposed method.  
- Against acceptance: unclear how the method extends to asynchronous evaluation.  
- Minor issues: typo in equation (4.1) missing z^(1:n).  
- Minor issues: uses parenthetical references incorrectly as nouns.  
- Minor issues: x-label "iterations" in plots is not clearly defined.
