- Introduces q-KG, a Bayes-optimal batch acquisition function that generalizes the sequential knowledge gradient to parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis to efficiently maximize q-KG, addressing scalability.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms parallel EI and batch UCB on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound.  
- Implementation is well-documented and code is publicly released.  
- Major theoretical contribution: formalizes Bayes-optimal batch decision-making for parallel BO.  
- Computational contribution: elegant, scalable IPA-based optimization strategy.  
- Strong empirical performance on Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann and on MNIST/CIFAR10 tuning tasks.  
- Clarity of exposition is excellent, aiding reader comprehension and reproducibility.  
- Experimental flaw: using test set error as the optimization objective is suboptimal due to high variance and bias; metrics like log loss or AUC are preferred.  
- Benchmarking is limited and lacks diversity—only two classification tasks/datasets and no HPOlib benchmarks.  
- Scope is narrow: only two datasets and two learning algorithms; omits evaluations on other model families (e.g., tree-based models) and tasks.  
- Noise model assumption (independent Gaussian) is standard but may not hold for HPO; paper acknowledges but doesn’t assess practical impact.  
- Recommendation: strengthen experiments by using robust metrics, adding HPOlib and diverse tasks (including regression/RL); current results are promising but not fully convincing for acceptance.
