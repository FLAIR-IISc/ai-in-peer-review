This paper presents a batch (parallel) variant of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) acquisition function for Bayesian optimization. To select an optimal batch of design points, the authors propose a Monte Carlo–based gradient estimation approach, enabling efficient optimization of the batch KG objective. Empirically, the method is shown to outperform other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) baselines.

1. To my knowledge, batch KG itself is not introduced here for the first time. Please clarify the paper’s contributions relative to prior batch KG work such as [1] and [2]. My reading is that the main novelty lies in the gradient estimation scheme used to optimize the batch selection. If that is correct, please provide a direct comparison of the computational efficiency and/or optimization effectiveness of the proposed approach against these earlier batch KG methods, either via experiments or, where possible, via theoretical analysis.

2. Section 5.2 appears to be the crux of the paper, yet it is relatively terse. This section should be expanded to more fully detail the gradient estimator, its unbiasedness, variance properties, and any practical stabilization techniques, as these are central to the contribution.

3. Standard EI assumes noise-free observations. As a result, comparisons in the noisy setting may be unfavorable to EI. Did you employ a noise-aware EI variant (e.g., using the GP posterior over the latent function rather than noisy observations)? If plain EI was used, the results in Section 6.2 should explicitly discuss that EI does not account for observation noise, and how that affects the comparison.

4. At line 162, the abbreviation “IPA” appears to be redefined. Please standardize its first occurrence and avoid redefining it later.
