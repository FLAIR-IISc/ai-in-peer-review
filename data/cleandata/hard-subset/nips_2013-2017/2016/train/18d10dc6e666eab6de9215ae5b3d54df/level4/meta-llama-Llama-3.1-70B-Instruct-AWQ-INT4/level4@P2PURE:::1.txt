This paper introduces a batch-sequential version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) criterion for Bayesian Optimization, presenting a parallel (q-KG) criterion that builds upon the standard KG criterion. Following a thorough background on related work and Gaussian processes, the authors define the q-KG criterion and provide a detailed computation of its value. The paper is complemented by a series of numerical experiments demonstrating the potential of q-KG to outperform state-of-the-art batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization algorithms. Overall, this is a high-quality paper with important contributions that justify its publication in NIPS. The potential impact of parallelizing Bayesian optimization algorithms on society is significant.

A minor criticism is that the paper does not investigate the speed-ups achieved by transitioning from sequential to batch-sequential Bayesian Optimization. Additionally, a few statements in the literature review could be improved for accuracy. For instance, the concept of integrating Expected Improvement (EI) with respect to posterior distributions was previously explored in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization" (along with Constant Liar), and Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix in [2]. Furthermore, q-EI maximization using natural gradient is studied in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design".

I have several remarks and questions regarding the paper. Firstly, is the set A compact, and is the function f continuous? Secondly, why is A restricted to be a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) at the initial stage? In Algorithm 1, what is the approach for hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating? In Section 5.2, is the optimization problem defined as a maximization or minimization of g? Additionally, the smoothness of g is not immediately apparent, and the derivative of the Cholesky factor may not be straightforward. I also note that the Matérn kernel is typically denoted as 5/2, not 2/5. Regarding Figure 1 and the corresponding experiments, was it possible to fit the same Gaussian processes using different software packages?
