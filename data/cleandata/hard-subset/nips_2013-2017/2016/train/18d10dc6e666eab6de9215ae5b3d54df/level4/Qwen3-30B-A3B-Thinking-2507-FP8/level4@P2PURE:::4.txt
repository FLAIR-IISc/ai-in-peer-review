

This paper introduces a parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, addressing the challenge of time-consuming function evaluations. The authors propose a novel acquisition function, q-KG, which outputs multiple points for parallel evaluation, alongside an efficient computational approach for evaluating q-KG. Empirical results validate the theoretical claims. The problem addressed is significant, as distributed evaluation capabilities substantially improve scalability for data-driven applications. By evaluating q points simultaneously in each iteration (rather than sequentially), the q-KG method significantly accelerates optimization efficiency. This represents a valuable and timely contribution. However, the suitability of the Gaussian process assumption, particularly for high-dimensional neural network architectures, remains questionable and warrants further justification. The authors should provide theoretical error bounds for their method and a quantitative analysis of computational complexity with respect to input dimension. Despite this, the paper presents a solid contribution to Bayesian Optimization: the methodology is sound, the experiments are well-designed, and the writing is clear. Minor issues include: l.36: "the set of points to evaluate next that is" → should read "the set of points to evaluate next, which is"; l.95: Define set A; l.104: Clarify the input to μⁿ; l.128: Briefly discuss the parallel EI algorithm; l.140: Use distinct notation for A to avoid ambiguity.
