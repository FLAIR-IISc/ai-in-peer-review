- Proposes a parallel batch Bayesian optimization method using the q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) and IPA-based gradient estimates to select Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Empirical claims: q-KG is competitive with existing parallel BO on noise-free problems and outperforms them with observation noise.  
- Paper demonstrates results on artificial benchmark functions and hyperparameter tuning for CNN and logistic regression (CIFAR-10/MNIST).  
- Reviewer requests wall-clock time measurements or analysis of real speedups versus random search or sequential BO.  
- The choice of batch size q = 4 across experiments is not justified.  
- Novelty concern: both KG and IPA are existing techniques; related prior work (e.g., parallel EI using IPA) may overlap substantially.  
- Suggests clarifying differences from Wang et al. (2015) and from Spearmint’s parallel EI (refer to q-EI to avoid confusion).  
- Impact: parallel hyperparameter tuning is highly relevant and useful across many application domains.  
- Clarity: technical background and experimental details are generally well-described and sufficient to understand comparisons.  
- Question 1: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI implementations, and can you clearly distinguish your method from those?  
- Question 2: How does the method scale beyond q=4 (e.g., to dozens or hundreds of parallel runs), and what are the expected benefits?  
- Question 3: Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous parallel evaluations (like Spearmint)?  
- Question 4: What is the wall-clock overhead for computing each batch, and how does total wall-clock time compare to random or sequential methods?  
- Question 5: Clarify what the x-label “iterations” denotes (evaluations or batches) and whether the initial design is included—explain why curves do not all start at the same value.  
- Minor: Possible missing z^(1:n) in equation (4.1).  
- Minor: Grammar correction—“achieve” → “achieves.”  
- Minor: Rephrase parenthetical citations into noun form (e.g., “Snoek et al. [16] suggest…”).  
- Minor: Wang et al. (2015) likely apply IPA rather than introduce it—correct the citation/context.
