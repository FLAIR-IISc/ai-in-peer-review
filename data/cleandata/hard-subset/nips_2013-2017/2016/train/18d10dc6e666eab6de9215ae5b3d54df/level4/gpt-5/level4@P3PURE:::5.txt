Summary
The paper derives a batch version of the Knowledge Gradient (KG) and proposes a Monte Carlo–based gradient estimation method to select an optimal batch of design points. Empirically, the proposed method appears to outperform other batch Bayesian optimization (BO) methods.

Major comments
1) Novelty and relation to prior work: To my knowledge, batch KG has been studied previously. Please clarify the contribution of this paper relative to prior batch KG work, such as [1] and [2]. In my view, the main contribution here is the gradient estimation procedure for finding the optimal batch. If that is correct, please evaluate the efficiency and effectiveness of your approach against prior batch KG methods, either through empirical comparisons or, if feasible, mathematical analysis.

2) Elaboration of Section 5.2: Section 5.2 appears to be the key technical component of the paper, yet it is under-explained. Please expand this section to make the approach, assumptions, and practical implementation details clearer.

3) Comparison with EI under observation noise: Expected Improvement (EI) is typically defined for noiseless observations. As a result, comparisons in noisy settings may be unfair. Did you use a noise-aware variant of EI? If plain EI was used, please discuss the Section 6.2 results explicitly in light of the fact that EI does not account for observation noise.

4) Notation/terminology: On line 162, the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined. Please standardize the definition to avoid confusion.
