This work provides a method to perform parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization by computing a Bayes-optimal batch of configurations to evaluate next. The proposed method measures the utility of evaluating a batch of configurations using the knowledge gradient (q-KG). To efficiently maximize the acquisition function they present a method based on infinitesimal perturbation analysis to estimate the gradient of q-KG. In 3 experiments they show, that q-KG performs competitive with existing parallel Bayesian optimization methods on problems without observation noise and it outperforms these methods on problems with observation noise.

Quality: The paper is technically sound and the proposed method is well-motivated. The authors provide a clear explanation of the knowledge gradient and its extension to the parallel setting. The use of infinitesimal perturbation analysis to estimate the gradient of q-KG is also well-explained. The experiments are well-designed and the results are clearly presented.

Clarity: The paper is well-written and the technical details are clearly explained. The background section provides a good overview of the relevant literature and the authors do a good job of explaining the key concepts and techniques used in the paper.

Originality: The paper proposes a new method for parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization, which is an extension of the knowledge gradient method to the parallel setting. The use of infinitesimal perturbation analysis to estimate the gradient of q-KG is also a new contribution.

Impact: Parallel hyperparameter tuning methods are highly relevant for real-world applications in many disciplines, such as machine learning, computer vision, biology, and robotics. The proposed method has the potential to improve the efficiency of these applications by allowing for parallel evaluation of multiple configurations.

Significance: The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization by proposing a new method for parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization. The method has the potential to improve the efficiency of hyperparameter tuning in many applications.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a new method for parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization, which is an extension of the knowledge gradient method to the parallel setting.
* The method has the potential to improve the efficiency of hyperparameter tuning in many applications.
* The paper is technically sound and the proposed method is well-motivated.
* The experiments are well-designed and the results are clearly presented.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper does not provide a clear comparison with other parallel Bayesian optimization methods, such as the method proposed by Wang et al. (2015).
* The paper does not provide an analysis of the computational overhead of the proposed method.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of how the method can be extended to run asynchronously.

Minor Comments:

* There is a typo in equation (4.1), where z^(1:n) is missing.
* The paper uses parenthetical references, which are not nouns.
* The paper does not provide a clear explanation of what "iterations" means in the x-label of the plots.
