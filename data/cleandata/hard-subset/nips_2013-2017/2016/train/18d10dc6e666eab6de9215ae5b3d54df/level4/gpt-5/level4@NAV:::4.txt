This paper studies the parallel knowledge gradient approach for batch Bayesian optimization, focusing on scenarios where function evaluations are costly. The authors introduce a new acquisition function, q-KG, that selects multiple candidate points for parallel evaluation, and they present an efficient procedure to compute q-KG. Empirical results are provided to support their theoretical developments.

The manuscript addresses the parallel knowledge gradient for batch Bayesian optimization. The problem is important, as distributing evaluations can significantly benefit data-driven applications. Rather than selecting a single point per iteration, the algorithm uses the q-knowledge gradient to propose q points for the next round, substantially improving efficiency. This is an intriguing direction. However, it is unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is appropriate, especially for neural network settings with very high-dimensional inputs. It would be helpful to include theoretical error bounds for the method and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to dimension. Overall, this is a solid contribution to Bayesian optimization. The method is sound, the experiments are convincing, and the paper is generally well written.
