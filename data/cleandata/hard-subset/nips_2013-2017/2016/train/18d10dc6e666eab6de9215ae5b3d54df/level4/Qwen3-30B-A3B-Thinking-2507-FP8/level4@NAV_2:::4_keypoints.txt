- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretically optimal batch acquisition function extending knowledge gradient to select q points jointly for parallel BO.  
- Leverages infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients efficiently, enabling scalable optimization of the acquisition.  
- Empirically outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic functions and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Strong theoretical foundation: Bayes-optimal batch selection with thorough computational analysis.  
- Original contribution: directly tackles collective batch optimization rather than iterative greedy batching, clearly differentiated from prior work.  
- High practical significance: scalable solution for parallel hyperparameter tuning; noise robustness is valuable for real-world applications.  
- Clear presentation: well-structured paper, clear Algorithm 1, and effective figures illustrating convergence.  
- Concern: Gaussian process modeling assumptions may not be justified for high-dimensional neural architecture tuning; GP scalability needs discussion.  
- Lacks theoretical error/regret bounds for q-KG despite strong empirical results.  
- Notational clarity issues (e.g., ambiguous use of "A" and missing domain specification) and the parallel EI comparison could be elaborated.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and statistically significant (100/20/5 runs) with code available for reproducibility.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
