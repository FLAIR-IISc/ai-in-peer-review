- Paper proposes batch Bayesian optimization using q-KG as the acquisition function and an IPA-based stochastic gradient method to optimize it.
- Empirical results claim competitiveness on noiseless problems and superiority under observation noise, but the experiments are unclear and unconvincing.

- Addresses the practical problem of accelerating optimization via parallel resources.
- Proposes a principled, Bayes-optimal batch selection instead of heuristic batching.
- Technical exposition and background are clear.
- Demonstrates notably strong performance in noisy evaluation settings.

- No wall-clock time analysis: overhead of computing batches may negate practical speedups; need wall-clock comparisons to baselines.
- Novelty concerns: q-KG and IPA exist in prior work; lacks direct comparison to Wang et al. (2016) q-EI and clearer advantages of q-KG over q-EI.
- Scalability and practicality unclear: experiments only use q=4, no analysis of overhead scaling with q, and no discussion of asynchronous evaluation to improve resource utilization.

- Have you compared directly to Wang et al. (2016)'s q-EI and is "parallel EI in MOE" that method?
- How does the method scale to larger q and how does batch-selection overhead grow with q?
- Can the method be extended to an asynchronous setting to improve wall-clock efficiency?
- Have you measured wall-clock time versus parallel random search or sequential BO, and what is the batch-selection overhead?
- Clarify the x-axis "iterations": batches or cumulative individual evaluations, and whether initial design points are included (explain differing starting values).

- Possible missing z^(1:n) term in the conditioning of Equation (4.1).
- Grammar fix: "achieve" should be "achieves".
- Citation style: avoid using parenthetical references as nouns.
- IPA reference may not be foundational; suggest citing a more appropriate foundational source.
