- Proposes q-KG (knowledge gradient) for parallel batch Bayesian optimization, optimized via IPA-estimated gradients; shows competitive/noisy-improved results.
- Reviewer likes the goal of efficient parallel hyperparameter optimization but finds several unclear aspects and unconvincing experiments.
- Experiments mix standard benchmarks and realistic CNN/logistic tasks, but experimental analysis is incomplete.
- Missing wall-clock/time-to-solution comparisons to random search and sequential BO; practical overhead may dominate.
- Batch size q fixed at 4 without justification; need experiments or rationale for varying q.
- Novelty limited: using KG and IPA is incremental since both components and IPA-for-q-point selection exist previously.
- Need to clarify incremental contribution relative to prior work (e.g., IPA-based q-EI) and other q-point acquisition methods.
- Parallel BO is impactful if it yields practical wall-clock gains and scales with available parallelism.
- Presentation and background are clear, but experimental strengthening and clearer positioning vs related methods are needed.
