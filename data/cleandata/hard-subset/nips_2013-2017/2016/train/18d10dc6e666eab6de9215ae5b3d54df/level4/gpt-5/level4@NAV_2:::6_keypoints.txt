- Paper proposes a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition (q-KG) using IPA to obtain stochastic gradients for optimizing batch evaluations.
- Empirical results show competitiveness with baselines on synthetic and hyperparameter tuning tasks, and superior performance under observation noise.
- Missing experiments measuring wall-clock time or practical speedups from batching, which are crucial for real-system assessment.
- Unjustified choice of fixed batch size q = 4; sensitivity to q is not analyzed.
- Novelty is incremental: combines established KG acquisition with IPA-based gradient optimization; relationship to prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) not clearly differentiated.
- Potential high impact if method demonstrably reduces end-to-end time-to-good-solutions and scales to larger parallelism.
- Background and technical exposition are clear and experiments appear reproducible given implementation notes.
- Question: Did you directly compare to Wang et al. (2015) / q-EI and clearly distinguish q-EI from Spearmint’s heuristic parallelization?
- Question: How does q-KG scale with increasing q both computationally (acquisition optimization overhead) and in optimization performance; what benefits do larger batches bring?
- Question: Can the synchronous algorithm be adapted to an asynchronous setting, and what modifications would that require?
- Question: Have you compared wall-clock time-to-target against random search and sequential BO, and what is the overhead per batch computation in your implementation?
- Question: In plots, clarify whether the x-axis “iterations” denotes batches or total evaluations, whether initial design points are included, and why methods don’t start at the same value.
- Minor: Possible missing z(1:q) term in Eq. (4.1).
- Minor: Typo/grammar fixes and citation style issues (e.g., “achieve” → “achieves”; parenthetical citations used as nouns).
- Minor: Wang et al. (2015) applied IPA but is not the canonical IPA reference; clarify citation.
