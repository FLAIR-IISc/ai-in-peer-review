- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending Knowledge Gradient to select batches for parallel evaluation.  
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Targets costly function evaluations (e.g., hyperparameter tuning) and handles noisy settings well.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and real ML tasks (logistic regression, CNN tuning) show q-KG matches or outperforms state-of-the-art parallel BO methods.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and demonstrates strong understanding of related work (parallel EI, UCB, ES).  
- Public code is provided, enhancing reproducibility and impact.  

Strengths:
- Novel extension of the Knowledge Gradient framework to the parallel batch setting.  
- Effective and practical gradient estimation using IPA enabling larger batch optimization.  
- Strong empirical performance, particularly in noisy evaluation scenarios.  
- Clear presentation that facilitates understanding and adoption.  
- Code availability supports reproducibility.  

Weaknesses:
- Incremental novelty since it builds directly on KG and uses a relatively standard IPA-based optimization technique.  
- Dependence on discretization of the search space; sensitivity to number of GP posterior samples (M) not fully explored.  
- Limited theoretical analysis (convergence or regret bounds) of q-KG.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Presents a practical, effective algorithm addressing a relevant problem in parallel BO.  
- Compelling experimental evidence of advantage over existing methods.  
- The KG extension combined with IPA is a valuable, implementable contribution.  
- Code availability encourages broader use and validation.  

Arguments for rejection:
- Novelty may be viewed as incremental rather than fundamentally new.  
- Insufficient theoretical guarantees leave open questions about algorithmic properties.  

Overall assessment:
- Solid contribution with strong empirical support; novelty is modest but practical benefits warrant acceptance.  

Specific comment:
- Clarify notation in Eq. (3.1): define μ(x^(1:n)) as the vector of values and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) as the covariance matrix to avoid ambiguity.  

Recommendation:
- Accept.
