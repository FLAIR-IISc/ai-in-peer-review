- Introduces q-KG, a Bayes-optimal one-step-lookahead batch BO acquisition that generalizes KG to select q points jointly.  
- Develops an unbiased stochastic IPA-based gradient estimator to enable gradient-based optimization of the batch objective.  
- Well connected to BO literature, contrasting with parallel EI, UCB-based batch policies, and entropy-search methods.  
- Strength: principled decision-theoretic foundation; reduces to parallel EI in the noise-free recommend-from-sampled-points regime.  
- Strength: solid computational contribution — practical continuous optimization with adaptive discretization and released code.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance on synthetic problems and two HPO tasks, notably under noise.  
- Weakness: limited real-world evaluation — only MNIST and CIFAR-10 with logistic regression and a CNN; broader tasks and model classes needed.  
- Suggestion: benchmark on standardized HPO suites (e.g., HPOlib) for more representative comparisons.  
- Weakness: missing important batch BO baselines (e.g., PPES, differentiated/joint qEI optimizers) in comparisons.  
- Major concern: evaluation protocol appears to optimize on test-set error; should use train/validation/test splits or nested CV and report held-out test results.  
- Metric concern: accuracy/error can be insensitive; recommend proper scoring rules (log loss) and other metrics (AUC/PR-AUC) or justification for chosen metrics.  
- Noise-modeling weakness: assumes i.i.d. Gaussian noise with constant variance; HPO often exhibits heteroscedasticity and correlated noise — discuss limitations or use advanced models.  
- Clarify how noise variance is estimated and whether replications are used to detect mis-specification.  
- Computational issue: q-KG is more expensive than greedy heuristics — report wall-clock time, time-to-target, Monte Carlo sample counts, gradient steps, multi-starts, and dependence on q/dimension.  
- Suggest sensitivity analyses for discretization size M and adaptive candidate set construction impacting performance and runtime.  
- Ask for discussion of stability/variance of the IPA gradient estimator and any variance-reduction techniques used.  
- Theoretical clarity needed: state differentiability assumptions for unbiased gradient prominently and give intuition why they hold in practice.  
- Suggest discussing multi-batch horizons, myopic performance, and any possible guarantees or regret bounds beyond one-step optimality.  
- Overall: technically sound, original extension of KG to batch with useful estimator; recommend acceptance conditional on improved validation protocol, runtime reporting, and broader baselines/tasks.
