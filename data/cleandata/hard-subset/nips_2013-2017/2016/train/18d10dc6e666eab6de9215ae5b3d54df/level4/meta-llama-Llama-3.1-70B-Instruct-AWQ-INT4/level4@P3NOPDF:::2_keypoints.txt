- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches to overcome sequential limitations.
- Contribution is noteworthy and addresses an important practical problem in hyperparameter optimization.
- Paper is well-written, accessible, and presents an elegant parallelization approach.
- Determination of Bayes-optimal batches is promising and could improve hyperparameter optimization efficiency.
- Assumes independent normally distributed errors, a common but potentially unrealistic assumption in hyperparameter optimization due to measurement noise and structural bias.
- Theoretical development is strong and provides a solid foundation for the method.
- Experimental evaluation is limited and disappointing relative to the theoretical contribution.
- Using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; threshold-based metrics can be suboptimal—prefer AUC or log loss.
- Experiments rely on MNIST and CIFAR10; should include HPOlib benchmarks for more comprehensive evaluation.
- Evaluation is restricted to two datasets, one task (classification), and two algorithms (logistic regression and CNN); needs more varied benchmarks.
- Conclusion: valuable theoretical advance but insufficient experimental evidence to convincingly demonstrate practical effectiveness.
