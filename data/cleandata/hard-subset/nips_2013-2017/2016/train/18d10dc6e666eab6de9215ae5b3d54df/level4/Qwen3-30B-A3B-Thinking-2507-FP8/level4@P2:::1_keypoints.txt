- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method extending the knowledge gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Uses a decision-theoretic, one-step Bayes-optimal approach that selects batches by maximizing expected incremental value rather than greedy iteration.  
- Derives q-KG for the single-batch-remaining setting and provides an efficient optimization method using infinitesimal perturbation analysis for the acquisition function.  
- Addresses an important gap: optimizes batches collectively rather than constructing them greedily or restricting to discrete settings.  
- Shows q-KG reduces to parallel EI under noise-free evaluations with final recommendation limited to sampled points, connecting to existing theory.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic benchmarks and real ML hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN).  
- Empirical results consistently outperform or match state-of-the-art batch BO methods, with notable advantages in noisy-evaluation settings.  
- Provides thorough experimental validation with appropriate baselines, multiple random initializations, clear results, and public code for reproducibility.  
- Theoretical and technical contributions are sound and rigorously derived, with justified computational approaches.  
- Acknowledges practical limitation: computational complexity can grow for large batch sizes q.  
- Paper is well-structured and clear overall, though section 5.2 (gradient estimation) could use more intuitive explanation.  
- Algorithm descriptions and notation are precise and accessible to the intended audience.  
- Method is original and distinct from prior parallel BO work that uses greedy or different acquisition strategies.  
- Significant practical impact potential for parallel hyperparameter tuning and noisy evaluation scenarios in ML.  
- Minor clarifications suggested: domain compactness/continuity assumptions, brief justification for initial Latin hypercube sampling, hyperparameter re-estimation is in Algorithm 1 step 5.  
- Fixes/corrections noted: g in Section 5.2 is a minimization, smoothness assumptions for derivatives are standard (but could be briefly justified), and the Matérn 5/2 kernel is correctly referenced.  
- Overall assessment: strong, high-quality, original, and significant work; recommendation for acceptance.
