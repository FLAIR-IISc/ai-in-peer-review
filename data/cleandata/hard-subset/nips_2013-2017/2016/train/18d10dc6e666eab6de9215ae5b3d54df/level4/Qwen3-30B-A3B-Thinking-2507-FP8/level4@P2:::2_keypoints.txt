- Proposes q-KG, a novel parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects Bayes-optimal batches of q points via a decision-theoretic acquisition function.  
- Extends the sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel setting, addressing a gap where most BO work assumes sequential evaluation.  
- Derives a theoretically grounded acquisition function maximizing expected incremental solution quality for batch selection.  
- Develops an efficient optimization method using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute q-KG.  
- Empirically shows q-KG consistently outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and related baselines on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Demonstrates particularly strong performance in noisy evaluation scenarios.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides detailed algorithmic and experimental descriptions.  
- Original contribution: decision-theoretic batch selection differs from prior greedy/iterative batch BO approaches.  
- Significance: practical relevance for parallel evaluations (e.g., hyperparameter tuning across machines) with immediate applicability.  
- Weakness: benchmarking is limited—only MNIST and CIFAR10 with two algorithms—reducing generalizability.  
- Weakness: evaluation uses test-set error, an arguably suboptimal metric for hyperparameter tuning compared to AUC or log loss.  
- Weakness: assumes independent Gaussian noise in observations, which may not hold for hyperparameter optimization (structural biases, cross-validation effects).  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions—expand benchmarking to broader HPO libraries (e.g., HPOlib) and justify or switch evaluation metrics.
