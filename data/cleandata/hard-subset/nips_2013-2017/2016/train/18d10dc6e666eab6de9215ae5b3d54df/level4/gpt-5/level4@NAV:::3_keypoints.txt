- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Method selects multiple evaluation points per iteration to enable parallel computation.  
- Main challenge is optimally choosing sampling locations.  
- Uses the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to estimate expected improvement and guide sampling decisions.  
- Extends KG to a batch (q-KG) setting to choose multiple points simultaneously.  
- The batch optimization is intractable, so the authors develop a Monte Carlo algorithm with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients.  
- Empirical results on test problems show the method outperforms or competes strongly with existing methods, especially Confidence Bound approaches.  
- Paper is clearly written and appears useful for practical applications.  
- Reviewer questions the level of novelty: primarily a batch extension of KG, with IPA-based gradient estimation being fairly standard.  
- Specific notation concern: equation (3.1) is unclear about how mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) are defined for sequences versus single-vector inputs.
