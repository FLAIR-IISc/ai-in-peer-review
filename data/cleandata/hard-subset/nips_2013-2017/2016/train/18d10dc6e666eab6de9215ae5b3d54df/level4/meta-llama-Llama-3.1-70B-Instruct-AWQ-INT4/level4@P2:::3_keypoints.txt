- Proposes a batch stochastic-gradient Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects multiple parallel sampling points per iteration for a multivariate objective over a finite domain.
- Extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to a batch setting (q-KG) to choose sampling sets by expected improvement.
- Uses Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of q-KG and optimize the sampling-set criterion.
- Empirical results show the method outperforms or competes with confidence-bound based approaches on several test problems.
- Paper is well written and potentially useful for practical applications.
- Main novelty is incremental: generalizing KG to batch selection; IPA-based gradient estimation is fairly standard.
- Specific request: clarify the definitions of mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) in equation (3.1) since mu and K are functions of vectors, not sequences.
- Quality: technically sound and algorithm appears correct, but novelty is not very high.
- Originality: builds on existing work; contribution significant but overall limited in novelty.
- Significance: promising for Bayesian optimization; would benefit from more theoretical analysis and discussion of limitations.
- Arguments for acceptance: clear exposition, practical potential, and strong empirical performance.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, incremental extension of prior work, and non-novel IPA usage.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — clarify notation in (3.1) and provide more theoretical insights and limitations.
