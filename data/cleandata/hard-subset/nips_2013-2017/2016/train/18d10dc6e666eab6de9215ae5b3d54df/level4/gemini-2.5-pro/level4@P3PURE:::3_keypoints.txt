- Proposes a stochastic Bayesian optimization algorithm for multivariate objectives over a finite domain with batch (parallel) evaluation capability.  
- Core problem: how to optimally select a batch of sampling points each iteration.  
- Extends the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to a batch setting (q-KG) to select multiple points simultaneously.  
- Batch selection is computationally intractable; authors use Monte Carlo simulation plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate the q-KG gradient.  
- IPA-based gradient estimation enables efficient optimization of the batch acquisition function.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art methods, notably UCB-based approaches.  
- Paper is well-written and appears practically useful.  
- Main reservation: limited novelty — mainly an extension of KG and IPA for gradient estimation is relatively standard.  
- Specific concern: notation in Eq. (3.1) unclear — definitions of μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n), x^(1:n)) over sequences need clarification.
