- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization method using a knowledge-gradient acquisition and IPA-based gradient estimation.  
- Experiments on synthetic functions and real ML tasks (CNN, logistic regression on CIFAR10/MNIST) show competitive/noisy-setting advantages.  
- Missing wall-clock time or speedup comparisons versus random search and sequential BO, so practical gains are unclear.  
- Uses a fixed batch size q=4 across experiments without justification; sensitivity to q is not analyzed.  
- Evaluation moves beyond cheap benchmarks, but experimental setup and results need further scrutiny.  
- Novelty is incremental: combining KG and IPA is a contribution, but constituent techniques are not new.  
- Must clearly differentiate and compare to Wang et al. (2015), which applied IPA to parallel EI.  
- Scalability to much larger batch sizes (e.g., 10–800) is not discussed; limitations and expected gains are unclear.  
- Method appears synchronous; authors should address whether and how it can be made asynchronous.  
- Need to report computational overhead per batch and compare wall-clock performance to baselines.  
- Plots’ x-axis “iterations” is ambiguous—clarify whether it means evaluations or batches and whether initial design is included.  
- Minor fixes: missing z^(1:n) in eq. (4.1); grammatical/citation phrasing issues; clarify that Wang et al. applied rather than introduced IPA.  
- Overall: promising and potentially impactful approach but evaluation weaknesses justify a weak accept pending revisions.
