- Introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion for Bayesian Optimization, extending standard KG to parallel/batch settings.  
- Presents background on related work and Gaussian processes, defines q-KG, and details its computation.  
- Numerical experiments indicate q-KG can outperform state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Overall judgment: high-quality paper with significant contributions that justify NIPS publication; parallelizing BO has notable societal impact.  
- Minor criticism: paper does not evaluate speed-ups or wall-clock benefits from moving from sequential to batch-sequential BO.  
- Literature review inaccuracies: integrating EI over posterior was previously explored in "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization" and Constant Liar approaches.  
- Literature note: Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix (cited as [2] here).  
- Prior work: q-EI maximization via natural gradient is studied in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design."  
- Question: Is the domain A assumed compact and is the objective function f continuous?  
- Question: Why is A restricted to be a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) at initialization?  
- Question: In Algorithm 1, how are GP hyperparameters handled—re-estimated via point estimates or updated in a fully Bayesian manner?  
- Clarification requested: In Section 5.2, is the optimization of g posed as a maximization or a minimization?  
- Concern: Smoothness of g is not obvious, and differentiating the Cholesky factor may be nontrivial.  
- Notation correction: Matérn kernel is typically denoted 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Reproducibility question: For Figure 1, could the same Gaussian processes be fitted using different software packages?
