- Proposes parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization using a Bayes-optimal batch acquisition (q-KG) and estimates its gradient via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Empirical results: q-KG is competitive on noiseless problems and outperforms existing methods on noisy problems.
- Reviewer appreciates the parallel hyperparameter optimization idea but finds some aspects unclear and is not fully convinced by experiments.
- Technical quality concern: experiments use standard synthetic benchmarks plus CNN/logistic on CIFAR10/MNIST but lack wall-clock time comparisons or analysis versus random search/sequential BO.
- Technical concern: rationale for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments is not justified.
- Novelty concern: using knowledge gradient and IPA is not fundamentally new; related prior work (Wang et al. 2015) applies IPA for parallel EI.
- Impact: parallel hyperparameter tuning is broadly useful across ML, CV, biology, and robotics.
- Clarity: background and technical sections are well explained; experiments provide necessary comparison details.
- Question: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015) / q-EI (and clarified differences vs. Spearmint’s parallel method)?
- Question: How does the method scale to larger q (many-core systems) and what gains are expected for larger degrees of parallelism?
- Question: Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous evaluation?
- Question: What is the wall-clock overhead for computing batches and how does total wall-clock performance compare to random or sequential BO?
- Question: What does “iterations” on the x-axis denote (evaluations or batches), does it include the initial design, and why don’t methods start from the same initial value?
- Minor comments: possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); grammar fixes; parenthetical citations used incorrectly as nouns; incorrect attribution of IPA origination to Wang et al. (2015).
