- Proposes a batch extension of a stochastic gradient algorithm within Bayesian optimization to maximize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Method selects multiple evaluation points per iteration to enable parallel computation.  
- Adopts and generalizes the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to select batches (q-KG).  
- Full optimization of the q-KG objective is intractable, so the authors use a Monte Carlo procedure with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate q-KG gradients.  
- Gradient estimates are used to search candidate batches and maximize expected information gain.  
- Experiments on benchmarks show the method typically outperforms or is competitive with confidence-bound–based approaches.  
- Paper is clearly written and appears useful for practical parallel evaluation settings.  
- Novelty is modest: batch KG is a natural extension and mainly differs by optimizing sets of points instead of single points.  
- Main technical contribution is the IPA-based q-KG gradient estimator, but IPA is a standard tool in stochastic optimization.  
- Overall a solid engineering/algorithmic contribution but not a fundamentally new concept beyond adapting KG to batches and devising a gradient estimator.  
- Specific suggestion: clarify Equation (3.1) notation—define mu(x^(1:n)) as the n-vector [mu(x^1),…,mu(x^n)] and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) as the n×n Gram matrix, and state dimensions.  
- Summary: well-presented, practically relevant, strong empirical results, but limited perceived novelty.
