This paper presents a parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on applications where function evaluations are computationally expensive. The authors introduce a novel acquisition function, the q-Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), which selects a batch of `q` points for simultaneous evaluation in each iteration. The paper also provides an efficient approach for optimizing this q-KG function and presents empirical results to demonstrate the method's effectiveness.

The problem addressed is important and highly relevant, as the ability to parallelize optimization is critical for the performance of many modern data-driven applications. By moving from a sequential, one-point-at-a-time evaluation to a batch approach, the proposed algorithm can significantly improve efficiency. The extension of the knowledge gradient principle to the batch setting is an interesting and well-motivated idea. Overall, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The methodology is sound, the experiments are well-conducted, and the paper is generally well-written.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. The reliance on the Gaussian Process (GP) assumption is a potential limitation, as its suitability is unclear in all settings, especially for high-dimensional problems like hyperparameter tuning for large neural network architectures. The paper would also be strengthened by the inclusion of more theoretical analysis, such as error bounds for the proposed method or a quantitative analysis of its computational complexity with respect to the input dimension.
