- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm generalizing knowledge gradient to parallel settings.  
- Provides thorough background on related work, Gaussian processes, and defines the q-KG criterion clearly.  
- Details computation of q-KG and gives numerical experiments showing competitive performance vs state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Paper is well-written and presents a valuable contribution with potentially substantial societal impact from parallelizing BO.  
- Does not evaluate runtime speed-ups from parallelization, an important omission.  
- Some literature-review statements are imprecise (e.g., prior work on EI integration, Constant Liar variants, and q-EI gradient methods).  
- Needs clarification whether feasible domain A is compact and function f is continuous.  
- Unclear why initial feasible domain A is restricted to a Latin hypercube design (LHS).  
- Algorithm 1 omits discussion of hyperparameter re-estimation or Bayesian updating.  
- Section 5.2 ambiguity: definition of g should state whether it is a max or min.  
- Smoothness of g is not guaranteed and the derivative of the Cholesky factorization is non-obvious and should be addressed.  
- Typo/inconsistency in Matérn kernel parameter: referenced as 5/2 but written as 2/5.  
- Question about reproducibility: could the same GPs in Figure 1 be fit using different software?  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to address the above technical and literature clarifications.
