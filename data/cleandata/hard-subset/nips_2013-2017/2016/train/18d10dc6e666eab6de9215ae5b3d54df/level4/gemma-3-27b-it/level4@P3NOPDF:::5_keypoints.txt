- Paper proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) method using Monte Carlo gradient estimation for selecting evaluation batches.  
- Experimental results claim the proposed method outperforms several existing batch Bayesian optimization approaches.  
- The novelty is unclear; authors must clarify how this work advances prior batch KG methods (e.g., Wang et al. [1] and [2]).  
- A direct comparison (empirical or theoretical) with existing batch KG approaches is needed to establish novelty and efficacy.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) is under-explained and requires much more detail on methodology, assumptions, limitations, and intuition.  
- Provide an intuitive explanation of why the proposed gradient estimator is effective for batch KG.  
- The noisy-setting comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) may be unfair because standard EI assumes noiseless observations.  
- Clarify whether a noise-aware EI variant was used, or explicitly discuss EI’s limitations in noisy environments if the standard EI was used.  
- Minor editorial issue: “IPA” is redefined on line 162 and should be removed.  
- Strength: addressing efficient batch selection in Bayesian optimization tackles an important problem.  
- Strength: empirical results are promising relative to other batch BO methods.  
- Strength: using Monte Carlo simulation for gradient estimation is a reasonable approach.  
- Weakness: insufficient contextualization in existing literature on batch KG.  
- Overall assessment: interesting approach but requires substantial revision and clearer justification; recommend rejection in current form with encouragement to resubmit after addressing concerns.
