- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects multiple sampling points per iteration (batch KG).
- Extends Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to make joint decisions over batches of points.
- Uses Monte Carlo and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the q-KG criterion for optimization.
- Demonstrates competitive or superior empirical performance versus confidence-bound-based methods on several test problems.
- Paper is well-written and appears practically useful.
- Main novelty is limited: primarily an extension of existing KG to batch setting rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- The claimed novelty (q-KG gradient estimation via IPA) is useful but relatively standard.
- Notation/clarity issue: mu(x^(1:n)) and K(x(1:n),x(1:n)) in Eq. (3.1) are not defined for sequences and need clarification.
- Strengths: clear theoretical motivation, practical parallel algorithm, solid experimental validation.
- Weaknesses: limited conceptual novelty, reliance on standard IPA technique, and a specific clarity/notation problem.
- Arguments for acceptance: practical parallel algorithm, clear framework, and strong experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient novelty and standardness of the main technical contribution, plus clarity issues with notation.
