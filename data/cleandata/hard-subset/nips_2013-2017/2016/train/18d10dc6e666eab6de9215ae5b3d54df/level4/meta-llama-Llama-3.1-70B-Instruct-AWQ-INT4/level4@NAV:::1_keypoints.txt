- Introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion for parallel Bayesian optimization with formal definition and computational details.  
- Provides comprehensive background on related work and Gaussian processes, drawing parallels to standard KG.  
- Numerical experiments demonstrate q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO methods.  
- Paper judged high quality with significant merits and suitable for NIPS; notes substantial potential societal impact from parallelizing BO.  
- Minor critique: does not study speed-ups gained when moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Minor critique: contains a few literature-review inaccuracies—prior work on integrating EI over posterior (e.g., "Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization") and Chevalier et al.'s CL-mix are not cited or acknowledged.  
- Minor critique: omits reference to prior work on q-EI maximization using natural gradients ("Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design").  
- Question: Is the domain A assumed compact and the objective f assumed continuous?  
- Question: Why restrict the initial design A to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS)?  
- Question: How are GP hyperparameters re-estimated or Bayesian-updated in Algorithm 1?  
- Clarification needed in Section 5.2: is g being maximized or minimized?  
- Concern: Smoothness of g is not justified; differentiability (and derivatives of the Cholesky factor) may be nontrivial.  
- Possible error: Matérn kernel parameter reported as 2/5 but appears to be 5/2.  
- Experimental question: For Figure 1, was it feasible to fit the same Gaussian processes using different software packages, and was this done?
