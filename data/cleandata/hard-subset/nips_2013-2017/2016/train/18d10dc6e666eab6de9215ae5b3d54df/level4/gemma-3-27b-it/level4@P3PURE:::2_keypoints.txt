- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes batches in a Bayes-optimal manner.  
- Empirically outperforms existing BO methods; paper is well-written and the Bayes-optimal batch determination is elegant and promising.  
- Assumes independent, normally distributed errors (standard GP assumption), which may be unrealistic for HPO due to measurement noise from empirical estimates and structural bias with small samples.  
- Theoretical foundations are strong.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak because using test-set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; threshold-based scoring is suboptimal—prefer AUC or log loss.  
- Experiments rely on MNIST and CIFAR-10 rather than established HPO benchmarks like HPOlib.  
- Benchmark scope is limited (only two datasets, one task—classification—and two algorithms), so results may not generalize across tasks and learners.  
- Overall: significant theoretical contribution but an insufficiently comprehensive experimental benchmark.
