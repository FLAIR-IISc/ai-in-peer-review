- Introduces a stochastic gradient algorithm combined with Bayesian modeling to optimize multivariate objectives over finite domains.  
- Core innovation: iteratively identifies multiple evaluation points to enable parallel objective evaluations.  
- Frames point selection within the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework and generalizes KG to batch (q-KG) selection.  
- Proposes a practical algorithm using Monte Carlo methods and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for maximizing batch information gain.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms or competitively matches Confidence-Bounds-based baselines on benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-written and has clear practical relevance.  
- Novelty is limited: main contribution is extending established KG to the batch setting, requiring more sophisticated optimization but not radical conceptual advances.  
- The IPA-based q-KG gradient estimation is the primary technical novelty but is viewed as relatively standard.  
- Specific clarity issue: notation in Eq. (3.1) — definitions of μ(x^(1:n)) and K(x^(1:n),x^(1:n)) for sequences are unclear.
