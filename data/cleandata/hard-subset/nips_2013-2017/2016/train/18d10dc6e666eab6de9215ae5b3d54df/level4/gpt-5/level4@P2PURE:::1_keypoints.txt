- Paper proposes a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion for Bayesian optimization and defines its parallel version with GP background.  
- q-KG computation is developed analogously to standard KG, and experiments show q-KG outperforming several state-of-the-art batch BO methods.  
- Reviewer rates the paper very strong and recommends acceptance, noting high societal impact of parallelizing BO.  
- Paper does not study empirical speed-ups from moving from sequential to batch-sequential settings.  
- Some literature-review statements are imprecise: EI integration and Constant Liar were discussed in “Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization,” Chevalier et al. introduced CL-mix, and multipoint EI natural-gradient work exists.  
- Question: Are assumptions that domain A is compact and f is continuous specified?  
- Question: Why restrict A to a Latin hypercube sample (LHS) at initialization?  
- Question: In Algorithm 1, how are GP hyperparameters handled—re-estimated or Bayesian-updated during the process?  
- Question: In Section 5.2, is the function g defined for maximization or minimization?  
- Request: Clarify required smoothness conditions for g in Section 5.2, as smoothness is not immediate.  
- Request: Provide explanation or reference for the derivative of the Cholesky factor, which is non-obvious.  
- Correction: Matérn kernel parameter should be ν = 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Question: For Figure 1 experiments, was it possible to fit the same GPs using different software packages?
