- Proposes a parallel Batch Bayesian Optimization method using the Bayes-optimal q-KG acquisition and IPA-based gradient estimates to maximize it; shows competitive results on noise-free tasks and superior results with observation noise.  
- Evaluation uses standard benchmarks plus CNN/logistic regression on CIFAR10/MNIST, but lacks wall-clock time measurements and direct comparisons to random search or sequential BO to quantify practical gains.  
- The choice of batch size q=4 is unexplained; a sensitivity analysis or justification for this fixed value is needed.  
- The combination of q-KG and IPA is incremental—prior work has applied IPA with EI for batch selection—so novelty is limited.  
- Parallel hyperparameter tuning is impactful across domains; the method could be a useful tool if validated further.  
- Paper is generally clear with good technical detail, though there are minor stylistic and presentation issues.  
- Q1: Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015)/parallel EI (MOE)? Clarify distinctions from Spearmint and adopt consistent notation (e.g., q-EI) to avoid confusion.  
- Q2: How does the method scale beyond q=4 and what performance gains are expected for larger numbers of parallel runs?  
- Q3: Can the synchronous implementation be extended to an asynchronous setting like Spearmint?  
- Q4: Provide wall-clock time comparisons to random search and sequential BO and report computational overhead per batch selection.  
- Q5: Clarify the x-axis “iterations”: does it count function evaluations or batches, does it include the initial design, and why do methods start from different values?  
- Minor comments: missing z^(1:n) in Eq. (4.1); fix “achieve” → “achieves”; avoid using parenthetical references as nouns; check attribution—Wang et al. (2015) applied IPA rather than introduced it.
