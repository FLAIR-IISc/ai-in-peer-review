Proposes q-KG: a Bayes-optimal parallel BO method extending the knowledge gradient to jointly select batches.  
Introduces IPA-based gradient estimation to efficiently maximize q-KG, avoiding Monte Carlo approximations.  
Shows empirical superiority over parallel EI and GP-UCB on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations.  
Builds on NIPS 2012–2015 BO literature, advancing from greedy batch selection to decision-theoretic joint optimization.  
Strength: technically sound and well-motivated with a rigorous decision-theoretic derivation.  
Strength: IPA-based gradient computation is computationally efficient and well-justified.  
Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation across multiple benchmarks and real ML tasks, with code available for reproducibility.  
Strength: Clear exposition, precise notation, and accessible algorithm description.  
Weakness: No direct comparison to Wang et al. (2015)/MOE parallel EI—unclear which parallel EI baseline was used.  
Weakness: Missing wall-clock time analysis and time-to-target speedup measurements for practical parallel utility.  
Weakness: Scalability to large batch sizes (q≫4) is not addressed; computational scaling and optimal q are unclear.  
Weakness: Does not consider asynchronous execution scenarios common in distributed evaluations.  
Weakness: Underemphasizes practical significance of noise robustness for real-world constraints (e.g., limited GPU hours).  
Recommendation: Novel and impactful; merits acceptance for joint KG+IPA contribution and noisy-performance gains but requires experimental strengthening.  
Final assessment: Solid contribution with high quality and significance, but experimental gaps make the current submission borderline/weak accept.  
Minor corrections: Fix Eq.(4.1) domain, small grammatical edits, clarify citations (cite L’Ecuyer 1990 for IPA), and specify "iterations" in plots.
