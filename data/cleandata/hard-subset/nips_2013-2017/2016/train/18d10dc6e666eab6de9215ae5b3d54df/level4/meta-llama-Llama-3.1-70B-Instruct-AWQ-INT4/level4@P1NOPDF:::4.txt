This paper explores the parallel knowledge gradient method for batch Bayesian Optimization, focusing on scenarios where evaluating the function value is time-consuming. The authors propose a new acquisition function called q-KG, which can output several new points for parallel evaluation, and provide an efficient approach to evaluate this function. Empirical results are presented to validate their theoretical findings.

The problem addressed in this paper is important, as the ability to distribute work in a parallel manner can significantly enhance the performance of data-driven applications. Instead of evaluating one point at a time, the algorithm considers the q knowledge gradient, which proposes q points to evaluate at the next iteration, thereby substantially improving the algorithm's efficiency. This is an interesting idea worth exploring.

However, it is unclear whether the Gaussian process assumption is suitable, particularly in neural network architectures where the input dimension is very large. It would be beneficial if the authors could provide some theoretical results on the error bound of their method and a quantitative analysis of the complexity with respect to dimension. Nevertheless, this is a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian Optimization. The method is well-founded, and the experiments are sound. The paper is well-written overall.

- Line 95: A definition of A would be helpful.
- Line 104: It would be beneficial to specify the input of mu^n.
- Line 128: A brief discussion on the parallel EI algorithm might provide additional context.
