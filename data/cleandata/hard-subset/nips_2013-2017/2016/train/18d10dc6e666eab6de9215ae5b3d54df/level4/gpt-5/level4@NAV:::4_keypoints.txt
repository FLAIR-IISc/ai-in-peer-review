- Paper proposes q-KG, a new acquisition function for batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization, and an efficient procedure to compute it, with empirical support.

- Tackles an important problem: distributing costly function evaluations via selecting q points per iteration.

- q-KG offers substantial efficiency gains over single-point selection; direction is intriguing.

- Concern that the Gaussian process assumption may be inappropriate for neural-network models or very high-dimensional inputs.

- Requests for additional theory: include error bounds for the method.

- Requests for complexity analysis: provide quantitative complexity scaling with respect to input dimension.

- Overall assessment: solid contribution—method is sound, experiments convincing, and paper generally well written.

- Minor issues/typos: l.36 awkward phrase; l.95 definition of A missing; l.104 clarify argument of μ^n.

- Minor suggestions: brief discussion of parallel EI at l.128 and use different notation for A at l.140.
