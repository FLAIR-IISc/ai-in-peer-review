- Introduces q-KG, a batch-sequential extension of Knowledge Gradient for Bayesian optimization with solid theory and promising empirical results.  
- Novelty/significance: Extending KG to batch settings is nontrivial and valuable for parallel experimental design and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Technical clarity: Derivation and computation follow KG framework, making the method interpretable and connected to prior KG work.  
- Empirical performance: q-KG often outperforms strong batch BO baselines across several test problems.  
- Practical relevance: A principled batch KG can substantially impact real-world synchronous-parallel optimization.  
- Missing wall-clock analysis: Paper does not quantify end-to-end speedups from sequential to batch-sequential optimization under realistic parallel overheads.  
- Literature positioning needs tightening: Several prior works on EI parallelization, CL-mix, and q-EI differentiation should be cited and discussed.  
- Differentiability assumptions unclear: Several smoothness/differentiability assumptions (e.g., Sec. 5.2) need clearer statements, conditions, or citations.  
- Domain/regularity clarification requested: State whether action set A is compact and what regularity (continuity/smoothness) is assumed for f.  
- Initialization rationale requested: Explain why the initial design is restricted to Latin Hypercube Sampling and whether other designs affect q-KG performance.  
- Hyperparameter learning details requested: Specify how GP hyperparameters are handled (re-estimation schedule vs. Bayesian marginalization) and its computational impact.  
- Sign convention ambiguity: Clarify whether g in Sec. 5.2 is defined for maximization or minimization and ensure consistency of signs.  
- Smoothness of g justification requested: Provide brief argument or references establishing differentiability (a.e.) and unbiasedness of gradients.  
- Cholesky derivative justification requested: Cite standard results or provide derivation and assumptions (positive definiteness, stabilization) for differentiating Cholesky.  
- Experimental comparability question: Confirm whether all methods used the same GP kernels, hyperparameter learning, and software settings for fair comparison (Fig. 1).  
- Minor typo: “Matérn 2/5” likely should be Matérn 5/2.  
- Notation inconsistencies: Verify maxima/minima notation in Sec. 5.2 and across acquisition definitions.  
- Terminology consistency: Ensure consistent use of “batch-sequential,” “parallel,” and “q-” prefixes.  
- Additional suggestion on complexity: Add discussion of computational complexity and scalability with batch size q, dataset size n, and dimension d.  
- Optimization/robustness details suggested: Report optimization settings (random restarts, Monte Carlo sample counts, tolerances) and include sensitivity analyses for kernel choice, hyperparameter frequency, and batch size.  
- Overall evaluation: Very good paper proposing a principled q-KG with practical estimators and strong empirical performance; leans toward acceptance pending clarifications and tighter positioning.
