- Addresses the important problem of parallelizing Bayesian Optimization to make BO practical for real-world hyperparameter tuning.
- Proposes a novel, principled algorithm to select Bayes-optimal batches for parallel evaluation.
- Core contribution is elegant and theoretically strong, offering a more principled alternative to ad-hoc parallel BO heuristics.
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and easy to follow.
- Assumes independent, normally distributed measurement errors; this may be unrealistic for HPO where cross-validation estimates can be biased and structured.
- Experiments are not comprehensive enough to be fully convincing.
- Choice of optimization metric (test error) is potentially misleading; should consider more informative metrics like log-loss or AUC.
- Benchmarking only on MNIST and CIFAR-10 is limited; should evaluate on standardized HPO suites (e.g., HPOlib) for comparability.
- Empirical scope is narrow: two datasets, one task (classification), and two model families; needs broader tasks, datasets, and algorithms (e.g., regression, GBMs, SVMs).
- Overall promising theoretical contribution but requires stronger and more diverse experimental validation to substantiate superiority claims.
