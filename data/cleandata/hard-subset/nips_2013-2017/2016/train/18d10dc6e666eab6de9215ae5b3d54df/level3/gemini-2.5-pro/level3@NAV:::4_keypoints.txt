- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch acquisition function generalizing the knowledge gradient to parallel (q) evaluations as a one-step Bayes-optimal policy.
- Derives an efficient gradient-based optimizer for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to obtain an unbiased gradient estimator.
- Correctly positions q-KG relative to prior batch BO methods (e.g., parallel EI, UCB).
- Addresses the practical and significant problem of accelerating optimization via parallelization.
- Offers a principled, non-greedy batch construction that advances beyond heuristic methods.
- Technical development makes the method practical by enabling efficient optimization of the acquisition function.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNNs).
- Results show q-KG is competitive with or outperforms state-of-the-art, with notable gains in noisy settings.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Main concern: reliance on Gaussian process priors may limit applicability in very high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural architecture search).
- Main concern: lacks theoretical analysis—no error bounds or formal complexity results (dependence on dimension d and batch size q).
- Minor clarity issues: awkward grammar l.36; clarify domain A in q-KG (l.95); correct mu^(n)(x) notation (l.104); discuss relation to parallel EI (l.128); avoid reusing A for domain and discretization (l.140).
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with convincing experiments despite missing theoretical guarantees; recommendation to accept.
