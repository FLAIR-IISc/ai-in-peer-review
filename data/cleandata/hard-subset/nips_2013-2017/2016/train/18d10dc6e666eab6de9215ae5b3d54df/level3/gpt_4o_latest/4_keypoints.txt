- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch BO acquisition function enabling simultaneous evaluation of multiple points for parallel/distributed settings.  
- Derives q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective, providing a principled and theoretically grounded approach.  
- Proposes an efficient computational strategy for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and domain discretization to reduce computational cost.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art methods, especially in noisy settings and high-dimensional tasks like hyperparameter tuning.  
- Extensive experiments on synthetic and practical ML tasks, with open-source code provided for reproducibility.  
- Method relies on Gaussian Processes, which may limit scalability to very high-dimensional problems (e.g., complex neural network hyperparameter spaces).  
- Paper lacks theoretical error bounds and complexity analysis with respect to input dimensionality, leaving questions about robustness and scalability.  
- Some notations and explanations are unclear and a few typos are present, reducing readability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled acquisition function with strong empirical performance and practical computational efficiency.  
- Arguments against acceptance: GP scalability concerns, insufficient theoretical guarantees, and minor presentation issues.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to resolve clarity issues and add additional theoretical insight.
