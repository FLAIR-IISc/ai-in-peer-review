- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function extending knowledge gradient to select multiple points jointly for parallel evaluation.
- Derives q-KG as Bayes-optimal for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when only one batch remains.
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients for optimizing q-KG.
- Theoretical development is rigorous and the paper is technically sound.
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over benchmarks, particularly on noisy problems and real ML tasks (logistic regression, CNN tuning on MNIST/CIFAR10).
- Algorithm description and experiments are clearly presented, with good use of supplementary materials and open-source code.
- Main novelty is extending KG to batch settings and using IPA; IPA itself is a standard technique so novelty is somewhat limited.
- Notation ambiguity in equation (3.1) (K(x, x^(1:n)) vs. sequence/vector interpretation) needs clarification.
- Paper lacks a thorough discussion of computational complexity and how performance scales with batch size q.
- Could better distinguish and compare with closely related recent work (e.g., nested batch KG [26]).
- Strengths: well-motivated practical problem, strong decision-theoretic foundation, comprehensive experiments, clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: limited methodological novelty, unclear notation, insufficient scalability analysis, and incomplete comparisons to recent batch BO methods.
- Pro acceptance arguments: practical relevance, theoretical grounding, strong empirical validation, and useful open-source implementation.
- Con acceptance arguments: limited theoretical advance, non-novel computational approach, and need for more comparisons and complexity discussion; overall recommendation: accept.
