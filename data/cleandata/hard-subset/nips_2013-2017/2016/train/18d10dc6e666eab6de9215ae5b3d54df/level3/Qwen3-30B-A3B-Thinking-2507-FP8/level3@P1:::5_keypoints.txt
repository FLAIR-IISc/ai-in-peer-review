- Introduces parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) for batch Bayesian optimization, extending sequential KG to parallel evaluations.
- Derives a decision-theoretic, Bayes-optimal acquisition function for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when one batch remains.
- Proposes using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of q-KG, enabling gradient-based optimization over batches.
- Claims IPA yields an efficient computational method but does not provide theoretical complexity bounds or detailed implementation guidance.
- Prior work on batch KG [26] is acknowledged, but the distinction (continuous domains and gradient-based optimization) is not clearly emphasized.
- Experimental evaluation is strong: q-KG consistently outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with exploration on synthetic and hyperparameter tasks.
- q-KG shows especially pronounced advantages in noisy evaluation settings.
- Comparison to Expected Improvement is ambiguous because it’s unclear whether a noise-aware EI variant was used in noisy experiments.
- Section 5.2 (the IPA-based gradient estimator) is central yet underdeveloped and lacks sufficient mathematical rigor for reproducibility.
- The paper provides open-source code and generally sufficient experimental detail aside from IPA specifics.
- Writing, organization, and overall technical approach are clear and well-presented.
- Contribution is practically significant for hyperparameter tuning where batch evaluations are common.
- Strengths: decision-theoretic acquisition, empirical improvements (esp. with noise), practical implementation, comprehensive experiments.
- Weaknesses: insufficient novelty clarification relative to prior batch KG, underdeveloped IPA technical details, ambiguous EI baseline, minor IPA definition issue.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses important practical problem, theoretically sound, computationally efficient, empirically superior.
- Arguments against acceptance: novelty claims need stronger justification, central technical contribution lacks detail, EI comparison needs clarification.
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions to clarify novelty and expand technical/complexity details for the IPA method.
