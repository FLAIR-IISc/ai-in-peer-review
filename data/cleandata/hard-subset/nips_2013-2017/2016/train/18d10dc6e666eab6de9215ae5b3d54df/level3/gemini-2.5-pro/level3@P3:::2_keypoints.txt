- Proposes parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), a Bayes-optimal batch selection criterion extending sequential KG to parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Introduces an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to make q-KG computationally feasible.  
- Novel and significant: provides a principled, decision-theoretic foundation for batch BO beyond greedy or heuristic approaches.  
- Strong theoretical analysis: q-KG derived from first principles with sound technical treatment.  
- Clear exposition: well-written, well-motivated, and accessible presentation of a complex method.  
- Unrealistic noise assumption for HPO: assumes independent Gaussian observation noise, which may not hold for correlated, non-Gaussian errors in practical HPO.  
- Weak experimental evaluation: empirical results do not convincingly demonstrate general superiority of q-KG.  
- Poor choice of optimization metric: optimizing test set 0-1 error is ill-suited for continuous optimization; smoother surrogates (log-loss, AUC) would be more appropriate.  
- Limited benchmarks: experiments only on MNIST and CIFAR-10 with two model types, lacking broader HPO benchmark suites like HPOlib.  
- Lack of algorithm and dataset variety: need evaluations across diverse models (e.g., GBDTs, SVMs) and datasets with varied characteristics.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution and clarity, but empirical validation is insufficient; recommend encouraging expanded experiments though paper is a strong candidate for acceptance.
