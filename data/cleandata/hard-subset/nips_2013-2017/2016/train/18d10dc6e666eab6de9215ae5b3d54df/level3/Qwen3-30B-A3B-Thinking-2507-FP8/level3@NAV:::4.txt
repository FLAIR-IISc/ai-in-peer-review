

This paper introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method that outputs q points simultaneously for parallel evaluation. The authors propose a decision-theoretic acquisition function that provides the one-step Bayes optimal batch of points, and develop an efficient computational approach using infinitesimal perturbation analysis to evaluate and optimize it. The method is theoretically grounded in extending the knowledge gradient policy to the parallel setting, and the authors demonstrate its superiority over existing batch BO methods on synthetic functions and practical machine learning hyperparameter tuning tasks.

The paper makes a solid contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. The problem addressed is important, as parallel evaluation can significantly accelerate expensive optimization tasks like hyperparameter tuning for neural networks. The theoretical foundation is sound, and the empirical results convincingly demonstrate q-KG's superiority, particularly in noisy evaluation scenarios. The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

However, the paper has notable limitations. The authors assume a Gaussian process prior, which may be inappropriate for high-dimensional inputs common in modern machine learning applications like neural architecture search. This is a significant concern given the paper's focus on tuning neural networks. The authors also lack theoretical results including error bounds for the algorithm and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension, which would strengthen their claims.

The paper would benefit from addressing these theoretical gaps and clarifying the scalability concerns. Minor issues include grammatical errors (l.36), undefined notation (l.95), and inconsistent notation reuse (l.140) that should be corrected.

Overall, this is a technically sound contribution with convincing empirical results that advances batch Bayesian optimization. While the high-dimensional applicability concern needs addressing, the method is well-motivated and likely to be valuable for practitioners. The paper meets all quality criteria and provides significant value to the field, though the theoretical limitations somewhat temper its significance. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the theoretical gaps and clarify the notation issues.
