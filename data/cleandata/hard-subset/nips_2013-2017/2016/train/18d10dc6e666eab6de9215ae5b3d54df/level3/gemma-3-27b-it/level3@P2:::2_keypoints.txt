- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method that extends Knowledge Gradient to identify Bayes-optimal batches of q points.  
- Formulates batch selection as a decision-theoretic problem and maximizes expected incremental value of a batch.  
- Introduces an IPA-based gradient estimation strategy to efficiently optimize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and principled approach to parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Elegant, computationally efficient solution to acquirement-function optimization via infinitesimal perturbation analysis.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with released code for reproducibility.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak due to using test set error as the optimization criterion, which is noisy and non-smooth.  
- Recommends more robust HPO metrics (e.g., AUC, log-loss) instead of test error to better reflect generalization.  
- Benchmark scope is limited (MNIST, CIFAR10; logistic regression and one CNN), raising concerns about generalizability.  
- Suggests expanding experiments to broader HPO benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) and diverse ML algorithms.  
- Claims about q-KG in noisy settings are under-supported; noise levels should be systematically varied and analyzed.  
- Assumes independent Gaussian observation noise, which may be violated in practice (e.g., cross-validation yields correlated/biased errors).  
- Recommends discussing and testing robustness to violations of the independent-noise assumption.  
- Overall assessment: significant theoretical contribution but empirical work needs major strengthening; recommendation: weak accept with major revisions.
