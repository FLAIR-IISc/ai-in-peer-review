- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) to select Bayes-optimal batches for parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Extends sequential KG to the parallel setting with a gradient-based acquisition optimization.  
- Positions work within parallel BO and emphasizes importance for expensive tasks like hyperparameter tuning.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient computation of the acquisition function.  
- Strong theoretical framing: decision-theoretic approach and Bayes-optimal batch selection.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation and logical derivations.  
- Theoretical analysis appears solid and provides insight into q-KG behavior.  
- Empirical evaluation is weak and undermines practical impact.  
- Primary experimental flaw: optimizes test set error (suboptimal), risking overfitting and unstable optimization.  
- Limited benchmarks: only MNIST and CIFAR10, and only two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN), reducing generalizability.  
- Lacks use of broader HPO benchmark suites (e.g., HPOlib) and diverse tasks/optimizers.  
- Recommendation: reject due to unconvincing empirical evidence despite strong theory.  
- Suggested fixes: re-evaluate with smoother metrics (AUC, log loss), expand benchmark suite and algorithms, and test sensitivity to q-KG hyperparameters.
