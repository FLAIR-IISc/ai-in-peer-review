- Introduces q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method that generalizes the Knowledge Gradient to quantify expected improvement in the posterior-mean minimum from evaluating q points.  
- Maximizing q-KG is high-dimensional; authors use gradient-based optimization with Monte Carlo sampling and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis to obtain an unbiased stochastic gradient.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR-10 CNN) shows q-KG is competitive or superior to batch EI and UCB, especially under observation noise.  
- Principled, decision-theoretic derivation extending one-step Bayes-optimal KG to the batch setting, offering a more grounded approach than many heuristics.  
- Strong empirical performance across benchmarks and a practical improvement on a human-tuned CNN demonstrate real-world utility.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes released code for reproducibility.  
- Limited novelty: core ideas are natural extensions and standard techniques (Monte Carlo + IPA), making the contribution a synthesis rather than fundamentally new theory.  
- Computational overhead: Monte Carlo-based stochastic gradient ascent can be expensive; wall-clock selection time vs. evaluation time trade-offs are not discussed.  
- Notation ambiguity in Eq. (3.1) for µ and K with vector arguments should be clarified.  
- Discretization for final recommendation (An sampled from GP optima) may affect q-KG quality; sensitivity to discretization size M and risk of missing the true minimum should be examined.  
- Lacks comparison to parallel Entropy Search (ES), which would be a natural and valuable baseline given similar goals and computational demands.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical contribution with convincing results; recommended for acceptance despite relative lack of deep novelty.
