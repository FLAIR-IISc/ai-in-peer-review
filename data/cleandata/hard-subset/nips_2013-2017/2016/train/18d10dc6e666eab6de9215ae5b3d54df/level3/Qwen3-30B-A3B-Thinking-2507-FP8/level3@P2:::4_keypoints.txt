- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects q points jointly via a decision-theoretic acquisition function.
- Derives the one-step Bayes-optimal batch under the single-remaining-batch assumption.
- Introduces an efficient computational strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to evaluate and optimize the q-KG acquisition function.
- Demonstrates significant empirical improvements over parallel EI, batch UCB, and related baselines on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning tasks.
- Shows particularly strong gains in noisy evaluation settings.
- Positions work clearly relative to prior sequential KG and parallel BO methods, emphasizing joint batch optimization versus greedy selection.
- Technically sound with clear theoretical foundation and precise algorithm description.
- Experimental design is thorough, includes noise-free/noisy tests and practical ML tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN), and code is open-source.
- Lacks theoretical guarantees such as error bounds and complexity scaling with input dimension.
- Notes the potential limitations of the Gaussian process prior for high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural architecture search).
- Paper is well-written and organized, but contains minor notational confusions (reuse of A, unclear definition of μ^(n) input).
- Originality: decision-theoretic batch acquisition and IPA-based gradient evaluation are meaningful advances over prior work.
- Significance: addresses practical need for parallel hyperparameter tuning and is valuable in noisy real-world settings.
- Strengths: theoretically grounded, computationally efficient, empirically superior, reproducible code.
- Weaknesses: missing theoretical analysis, GP applicability concerns for high-dimensional inputs, and minor notation issues.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix notation and acknowledge GP limitations.
