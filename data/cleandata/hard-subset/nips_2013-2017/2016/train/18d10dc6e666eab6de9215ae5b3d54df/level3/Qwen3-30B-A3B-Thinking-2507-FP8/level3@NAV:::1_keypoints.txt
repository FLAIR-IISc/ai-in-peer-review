- Introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion, extending KG to select q points jointly for Bayesian optimization.
- Derives a decision-theoretic acquisition function providing a Bayes-optimal batch selection strategy.
- Develops an efficient gradient computation method via infinitesimal perturbation analysis for optimizing the q-KG acquisition.
- Demonstrates superior empirical performance over state-of-the-art batch BO methods on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning, notably in noisy settings.
- Strength: strong experimental validation showing consistent improvements over benchmarks.
- Strength: clear theoretical foundations building on prior KG work.
- Strength: high practical relevance for parallel computing and accelerating resource-intensive hyperparameter tuning (notable societal impact).
- Weakness: literature review contains imprecise claims—fails to acknowledge Ginsbourger et al. (2010) on integrating EI and prior work by Chevalier et al. (2014) on CL-mix and q-EI optimization.
- Weakness: missing theoretical assumptions—unclear whether domain A is compact and whether f is assumed continuous.
- Weakness: unjustified restriction to Latin Hypercube Sampling for initial design choices.
- Weakness: lacks empirical analysis of computational speed-ups from batch-sequential versus sequential approaches.
- Weakness: technical concerns about smoothness of function g in Section 5.2 and differentiability of the Cholesky factor used in gradients.
- Correction needed: Matérn kernel parameter reported as 2/5 should be 5/2.
- Overall verdict: core contribution is significant and well-supported by experiments, but paper needs major revisions to address literature omissions, theoretical clarifications, and technical concerns before publication.
