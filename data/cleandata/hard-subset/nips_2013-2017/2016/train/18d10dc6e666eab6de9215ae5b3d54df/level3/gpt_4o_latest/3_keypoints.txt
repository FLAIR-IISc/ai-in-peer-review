- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm extending Knowledge Gradient to the parallel setting.  
- q-KG identifies multiple sampling points per iteration by maximizing a q-KG acquisition function measuring expected batch improvement.  
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling and Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients for optimizing the acquisition function.  
- Technically sound with solid theoretical foundation and rigorous empirical evaluation.  
- Provides a clear derivation of the q-KG acquisition function and an efficient computational strategy for practical use.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art, especially in noisy settings and hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNNs).  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and reproducible.  
- Novelty is somewhat limited—extension of KG to parallel setting and use of IPA are incremental over existing work.  
- Concern about clarity in the definition of mean µ and kernel K in equation (3.1) for finite sequences of vectors.  
- Could explore a broader range of benchmarks and provide deeper analysis of computational trade-offs versus other methods.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a relevant challenge, strong empirical performance in noisy settings, and well-executed theoretical/computational contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty and minor clarity issues in specific equations.  
- Conclusion: High-quality contribution with practical significance; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
