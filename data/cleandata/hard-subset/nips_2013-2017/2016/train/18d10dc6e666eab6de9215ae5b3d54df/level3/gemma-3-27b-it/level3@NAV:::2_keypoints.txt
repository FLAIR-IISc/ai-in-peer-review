- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for selecting Bayes-optimal evaluation batches in parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- The q-KG acquisition function extends sequential Knowledge Gradient to the parallel setting, focusing on maximizing information gain.  
- Provides a computationally efficient optimization method for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Paper is well-written and explains technical methodology clearly.  
- Major strength: strong theoretical foundation and an elegant approach to parallelizing BO.  
- Appropriately situates the method relative to parallel EI and UCB in prior work.  
- Major weakness: empirical evaluation is weak and uses test set error as the optimization criterion, which is inappropriate and highly variable in early optimization.  
- Recommends more suitable metrics (e.g., AUC, log loss) and broader benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) rather than only MNIST/CIFAR10 and two learning algorithms.  
- Limited dataset and algorithm diversity reduces generalizability to real-world HPO scenarios.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed observation noise, which may be unrealistic given structural bias and correlated errors (e.g., from cross-validation); impact not fully explored.  
- Recommendation: accept with major revisions contingent on addressing experimental weaknesses and demonstrating broader practical performance.
