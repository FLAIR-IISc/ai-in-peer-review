- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that selects a batch of q points as a one-step Bayes-optimal decision for the final evaluation.  
- Extends the sequential knowledge gradient (KG) framework to the parallel setting, considering joint value of entire batch rather than greedy heuristics.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to derive an unbiased gradient estimator for the Monte Carlo q-KG, enabling efficient gradient-based optimization.  
- Theoretically principled and novel: a decision-theoretic, Bayes-optimal formulation for batch selection that advances batch-mode active learning/optimization.  
- Strong theoretical development and technically solid application of IPA to make q-KG tractable.  
- Paper is very clear, well-written, well-organized, with consistent notation and a clearly presented algorithm.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited (only Logistic Regression and CNN on MNIST and CIFAR-10), reducing confidence in general-purpose claims.  
- Choice of test-set accuracy as optimization objective is suboptimal (piecewise constant, non-smooth); smoother metrics like log-loss/AUC would be more appropriate.  
- Noise model assumptions (i.i.d. Gaussian additive noise) may not reflect real HPO noise (e.g., binomial validation noise, correlated/stochastic training noise), so empirical gains may not generalize.  
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution warranting acceptance, but experiments need expansion and discussion of objective/noise choices to better substantiate practical superiority.
