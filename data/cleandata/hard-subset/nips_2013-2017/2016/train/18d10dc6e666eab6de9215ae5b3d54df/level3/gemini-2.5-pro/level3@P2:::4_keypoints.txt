- Introduces q-KG, a decision-theoretic acquisition function for selecting a batch of q points jointly in parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to derive an efficient, unbiased gradient estimator for q-KG, enabling gradient-based optimization of the batch.  
- Generalizes sequential Knowledge Gradient (KG) to the parallel setting and relates to parallel Expected Improvement (EI) under simplifying assumptions.  
- Addresses a timely and significant problem (parallelizing expensive evaluations), relevant for large-scale hyperparameter tuning.  
- Principled, one-step Bayes-optimal batch selection rather than heuristic or greedy batching.  
- Novel technical contribution making q-KG computationally practical via IPA-based gradients.  
- Strong, comprehensive empirical validation on synthetic benchmarks and real ML tuning tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR-10 CNN), with especially good performance under noise.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis: no formal convergence or regret bounds and no quantitative complexity characterization in d, q, n.  
- Relies on standard GP priors; may struggle in high-dimensional or complex search spaces without specialized kernels, embeddings, or input warping.  
- Minor clarity issues and suggested edits: awkward phrasing (Line 36), clarify minimization over continuous domain A (Line 95), correct “random vector” vs random field phrasing (Line 104).  
- Suggest adding brief explanation of how parallel EI is typically computed to contextualize the comparison (Line 128).  
- Recommend using distinct notation for continuous domain A vs finite discretization to avoid confusion (Line 140).  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated, practically effective contribution with strong experiments; recommend acceptance despite theoretical gaps.
