- Introduces q-KG, a parallel extension of the Knowledge Gradient policy for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients and enable efficient optimization of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirically outperforms competing batch BO algorithms on synthetic functions and real-world hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.  
- Builds on established BO literature and contributes a decision-theoretic, collective batch-selection approach rather than iterative batch construction.  
- Paper is well-written, logically organized, and presents the algorithm clearly (Algorithm 1).  
- High practical relevance: parallel BO addresses expensive evaluations common in ML, and code is publicly available.  
- Literature review has omissions and imprecise claims (e.g., Ginsbourger et al. 2010, missing Chevalier et al. 2014 on CL-mix and q-EI).  
- The paper fails to state important assumptions about domain A (e.g., compactness) and function f (e.g., continuity).  
- Rationale for restricting initial design to Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS) is not justified.  
- Algorithm 1 mentions hyperparameter re-training but lacks details on frequency and updating method.  
- Smoothness of the function g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor in IPA derivations are not clearly guaranteed.  
- Missing analysis of actual speed-ups from sequential to batch-sequential optimization despite parallelization motivation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions — address related-work gaps, state assumptions, justify LHS, clarify hyperparameter updates, address smoothness/Cholesky issues, fix Matérn typo, and discuss computational complexity/scaling with batch size q.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled method, strong empirical results, efficient IPA-based computation, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete literature review, some unjustified design choices, and lack of parallelization speed-up analysis.
