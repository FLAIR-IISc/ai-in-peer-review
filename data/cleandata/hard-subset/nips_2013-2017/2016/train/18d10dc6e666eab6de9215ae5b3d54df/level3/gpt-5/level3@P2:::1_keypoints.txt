- Paper introduces q-KG: a decision-theoretic batch BO acquisition selecting q points to maximize expected reduction in GP posterior minimum.
- q-KG reduces to q-EI in the noiseless case when final recommendation is constrained to sampled points.
- Main technical contribution: an efficient Monte Carlo stochastic gradient estimator for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Practical discretization strategy proposed for continuous feasible sets.
- Empirically, q-KG matches or outperforms parallel EI, GP-BUCB, GP-UCB-PE, and PPES, especially under observation noise.
- Review notes the paper should better acknowledge prior batch EI heuristics, constant liar methods, closed-form q-EI, and gradient-based q-EI work.
- Decision-theoretic formulation is well-motivated and technically sound, but several smoothness/differentiability assumptions need explicit statement.
- IPA estimator requires conditions (e.g., unique minimizer almost surely; ties measure-zero) for unbiasedness—these should be stated/cited.
- Differentiability of Cholesky factors wrt inputs holds for SPD matrices and smooth kernels but should be justified and referenced; numerical stability near ill-conditioned covariances must be discussed.
- Clarify sign/definition consistency for g in Eq. (5.2) vs. Eq. (4.1) (min vs max).
- Experiments are strong and include noisy and noiseless regimes; advantage of q-KG under noise is convincing.
- Missing: wall-clock speedup analysis comparing batch vs sequential BO under fixed parallel budget.
- Clarify assumptions on domain A (compactness) and function f (continuity), and conditions for Bayes-optimality claims.
- Algorithmic details need clarification: frequency of hyperparameter re-fitting, priors/regularization, and whether hyperparameter uncertainty is marginalized.
- Initial design choice (LHS) should be noted as practical, not required; other space-filling designs are acceptable.
- Provide sensitivity analysis of discretization size M and sampling measure for An; discuss scaling with dimension d and batch size q.
- Ensure consistent notation for σ̃n and D(n) and explicitly define tensor/vector shapes to avoid ambiguity.
- Originality: extension of KG to parallel setting and IPA-based stochastic gradients is novel and valuable.
- Significance: likely useful for practitioners with parallel resources, particularly for noisy expensive optimization like hyperparameter tuning.
- Key questions for authors: formal conditions for IPA unbiasedness; stability/references for Cholesky differentiation; domain/function assumptions; sensitivity to M; hyperparameter treatment comparisons; wall-clock speedups; GP implementation cross-checks.
- Pros: well-posed Bayes-optimal last-batch acquisition, strong empirical gains (noise), scalable stochastic gradients, clear links to prior KG/q-EI.
- Cons: implicit theoretical conditions, incomplete literature acknowledgment of batch EI work, missing wall-clock/overhead analysis, minor notational/clarity issues.
- Minor fixes: correct Matérn kernel notation to 5/2; ensure min/max consistency in Sec. 5.2; cite Smith (1995) and Murray (2016) for Cholesky differentiation.
- Recommendation: accept (8/10) pending the clarifications above; reviewer confidence 4/5.
