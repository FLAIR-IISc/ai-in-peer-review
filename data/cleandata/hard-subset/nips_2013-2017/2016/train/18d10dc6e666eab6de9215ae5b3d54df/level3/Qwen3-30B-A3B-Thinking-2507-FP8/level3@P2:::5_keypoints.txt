- Proposes q-KG, a parallel extension of the knowledge gradient acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization.
- Derives q-KG as the Bayes-optimal batch acquisition when only one batch remains, minimizing the GP predictor's expected minimum.
- Introduces an infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) estimator to compute gradients of q-KG for optimization.
- Empirically, q-KG consistently outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and related methods on synthetic and hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially in noisy settings.
- Builds on prior sequential KG (Frazier et al. 2009) and relates to parallel BO work (Wang et al. 2016; Contal et al. 2014).
- Correctly notes most prior batch BO methods form batches greedily, while q-KG optimizes the whole batch simultaneously.
- Paper is technically sound with a clear decision-theoretic foundation for the acquisition function.
- Section 5.2 lacks sufficient technical detail, making it hard to assess the IPA estimator's merits and implementation.
- Missing a thorough computational-complexity comparison between IPA/q-KG and prior batch KG methods (e.g., Wang et al. 2016).
- Comparison to Expected Improvement is problematic because EI's noise-free assumption was not clearly addressed or a noise-aware variant specified.
- Clarity issues: IPA is redefined mid-paper (line 162), and some implementation choices (e.g., M=1000 discretization) are not justified.
- Originality concern: the main novelty appears to be the computational approach, but differences from Wang et al.'s nested batch KG are not sufficiently clarified.
- Significance: addresses an important practical problem (parallel BO) with promising empirical gains for practitioners.
- To improve, authors should explicitly compare their computational method to prior batch KG approaches and clarify noise assumptions in experiments.
- Recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions — substantial clarification of novelty and more detailed exposition of the computational contribution are required.
