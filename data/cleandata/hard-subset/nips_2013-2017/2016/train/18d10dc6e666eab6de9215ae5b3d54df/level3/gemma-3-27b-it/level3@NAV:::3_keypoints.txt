- Introduces q-KG, extending the Knowledge Gradient to batch Bayesian optimization for parallel evaluations.
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient estimation of the q-KG acquisition function.
- Well-structured, clear mathematical foundations and detailed description of the gradient estimation technique.
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning shows q-KG consistently outperforms or matches existing batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.
- Code availability is provided.
- Strength: principled theoretical foundation with a practical IPA-based solution to computational challenges.
- Weakness: limited novelty—mainly an extension of KG and use of a standard IPA technique.
- Minor technical issue: Equation (3.1) is unclear in how μ and K are applied to sequences x^(1:n).
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Equation (3.1).
