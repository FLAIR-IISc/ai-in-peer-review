## Review of "The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization"

This paper introduces a batch Bayesian Optimization (BO) algorithm, the parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), aiming to efficiently select batches of points for evaluation, particularly in parallel computing environments. The core idea is to extend the sequential Knowledge Gradient approach to the batch setting, leveraging a decision-theoretic analysis to identify the Bayes-optimal batch. The authors also propose an infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) based method for efficient gradient computation of the q-KG acquisition function. Experiments on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning demonstrate improved performance compared to existing batch BO methods, especially in noisy evaluation scenarios.

While the problem addressed – efficient batch BO – is relevant and important, the novelty of this work is somewhat unclear. The paper acknowledges prior work on batch KG (e.g., [26] which is not cited, and [1, 2] which are), but doesn’t sufficiently articulate how this contribution advances beyond them. Simply extending KG to a parallel setting isn’t necessarily novel; the key lies in *how* it’s done and the benefits achieved. The authors claim the IPA-based gradient estimator is a significant contribution, but a direct comparison to gradient estimation techniques used in previous batch KG methods (like those in [15, 25]) is missing. A theoretical or empirical analysis of the efficiency/effectiveness of their estimator would significantly strengthen the paper.

Section 5.2, detailing the gradient estimation, is indeed crucial and lacks sufficient detail. The explanation feels rushed, and the connection between the equations and the practical implementation is not fully clear. Expanding this section with more intuitive explanations and potentially pseudo-code would greatly improve understanding. 

The comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) in Section 6.2 is problematic as highlighted. The authors need to explicitly state whether a noise-aware variant of EI was used and thoroughly discuss the impact of noise on the results. Simply stating that q-KG performs better in noisy settings isn't enough; a deeper analysis of *why* is needed.

Finally, a minor issue: the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined at line 162 and should be corrected for consistency.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a relevant problem in BO.
*   Demonstrates promising empirical results, particularly in noisy environments.
*   The proposed IPA-based gradient estimator *could* be a valuable contribution if its efficiency is convincingly demonstrated.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of clear novelty and insufficient differentiation from prior work on batch KG.
*   Insufficient detail and explanation in Section 5.2.
*   Problematic comparison to EI without clarifying the noise-awareness of the EI variant used.



**Overall:** This paper presents an interesting approach to batch BO, but requires significant revisions to address the concerns regarding novelty, clarity, and experimental rigor. I would recommend **rejection** in its current form, but encourage resubmission after addressing these points.
