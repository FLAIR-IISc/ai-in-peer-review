- Proposes q-KG: a Bayes-optimal one-step batch BO acquisition that selects q points by maximizing expected reduction in posterior predictive minimum.  
- q-KG generalizes the knowledge gradient to parallel selection and allows final recommendation outside sampled points.  
- Shows q-KG reduces to q-EI in the noise-free case when the final recommendation is restricted to evaluated points.  
- Develops a Monte Carlo estimator and an unbiased IPA-based stochastic gradient for practical optimization of q-KG.  
- Introduces a practical domain discretization for the inner minimization to make the algorithm tractable.  
- Positions work relative to sequential KG/PI/EI/UCB/ES and prior batch methods including q-EI heuristics and GP-UCB variants.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR-10) show q-KG matches or outperforms baselines, especially under noise.  
- Derivation and IPA gradient estimator are technically sound under stated differentiability assumptions.  
- Experiments convincingly support noise robustness of q-KG, aligning with theoretical motivations.  
- Lacks finite-sample or asymptotic guarantees for the iterative policy and no quantitative complexity analysis.  
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, but suffers some notational and grammatical issues.  
- Novelty: extending KG to batch selection with an efficient unbiased gradient is original and important.  
- Significance: practical, noise-robust batch BO is timely and likely to be adopted/extended.  
- Concern: GP with standard ARD Matérn kernels may not scale or be appropriate for high-dimensional structured inputs.  
- Request: discuss practical dimensionality limits, alternative kernels/embeddings, and effect of discretization size M and batch size q on runtime/performance.  
- Request theoretical results: convergence/error bounds for iterative q-KG and conditions for such guarantees.  
- Request complexity analysis detailing dependence on q, |A_n|, Monte Carlo samples, and input dimension d (e.g., Cholesky, gradient, optimizer costs).  
- Ask to compare and contrast with local penalization methods and analytic q-EI approximations in wall-clock time and solution quality.  
- Questions for authors: sensitivity to discretization M, scaling with q (timing and quality), robustness to mis-specified noise variance, and batch optimizer warm-starting/local minima.  
- Minor fixes: several notation/grammar points (define A, distinguish domain vs discretization, clarify μ(n) notation, typos like “Main Stange”), and report computational budgets.  
- Pros: principled one-step batch rule, efficient unbiased gradient, strong empirical results under noise, clear connection to q-EI, released code.  
- Cons: no regret/error bounds for iterative use, scalability vs dimension and q not quantified, GP prior suitability for high-dimensional spaces unaddressed, some editorial issues.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution, recommend acceptance after minor revisions addressing theoretical clarifications and complexity analysis.
