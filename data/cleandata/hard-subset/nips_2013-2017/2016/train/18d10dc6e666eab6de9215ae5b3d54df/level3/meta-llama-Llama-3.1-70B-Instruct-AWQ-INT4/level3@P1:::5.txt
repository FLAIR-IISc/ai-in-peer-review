This paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method, which is a generalization of the knowledge gradient method to the parallel setting. The q-KG method is derived using a decision-theoretic analysis and is shown to be Bayes-optimal for minimizing the minimum of the predictor of the Gaussian process if only one decision is remaining. The authors also develop a computationally efficient approach to maximizing q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and demonstrate its effectiveness in experiments on synthetic functions and practical machine learning algorithms.

The main contribution of this paper is the development of a batch-version of the knowledge gradient method, which is a well-known acquisition function in Bayesian optimization. The authors claim that their method outperforms other batch Bayesian optimization algorithms, such as parallel expected improvement (EI) and batch upper confidence bound (UCB). However, the reviewer questions the novelty of the batch knowledge gradient method, citing prior work in this area. The authors should clarify their contribution relative to prior batch knowledge gradient work and provide a more detailed comparison of their method to existing approaches.

The paper also proposes a Monte Carlo gradient estimator to find optimal batches, which is a key component of the q-KG method. The reviewer suggests that the authors should provide more detail and elaboration on this estimator, including empirical or theoretical comparisons to previous batch knowledge gradient methods. Additionally, the authors should clarify whether a noise-aware expected improvement variant was used in their comparisons and discuss how noise affects the results in Section 6.2.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its advantages. However, the reviewer raises some minor issues, such as the redefinition of the abbreviation "IPA" at line 162, which should be fixed.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that is derived using a decision-theoretic analysis.
* The authors develop a computationally efficient approach to maximizing q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
* The paper provides a clear explanation of the method and its advantages.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their method in experiments on synthetic functions and practical machine learning algorithms.

Weaknesses:

* The novelty of the batch knowledge gradient method is questioned by the reviewer.
* The paper lacks a detailed comparison of the proposed method to existing approaches.
* The Monte Carlo gradient estimator is not fully explained and compared to previous batch knowledge gradient methods.
* The comparison to expected improvement (EI) is problematic because EI assumes noiseless observations.
