- Introduces a novel batch-sequential Bayesian optimization algorithm (q-KG) derived from a decision-theoretical perspective.  
- q-KG outperforms state-of-the-art batch methods on synthetic benchmarks and practical ML problems, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a thorough related-work review.  
- Provides a clear derivation of the q-KG factor and an explicit computation of its gradient.  
- Strength: strong empirical demonstration of effectiveness across tasks.  
- Strength: method is theoretically motivated and potentially widely useful in practice.  
- Weakness: no empirical study of speed-ups from sequential to batch-sequential BO.  
- Weakness: some imprecise statements in the literature review that need clarification or removal.  
- Technical concerns: questions about compactness/continuity of A, restriction to LHS designs, and hyperparameter re-estimation in Algorithm 1.  
- Minor errors: incorrect specification of the Matérn parameter and potential reproducibility issues across software.  
- Overall recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and is recommended for acceptance despite listed issues.
