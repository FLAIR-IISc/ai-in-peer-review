- Introduces parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) for batch Bayesian optimization that selects q points by maximizing expected improvement in the GP predictor's minimum.  
- Derives a decision-theoretically optimal acquisition function for batch selection.  
- Presents an efficient computational strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to evaluate the acquisition gradient.  
- Extends the sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel/batch setting, contrasting with prior greedy iterative batch methods.  
- Shows q-KG consistently outperforms existing batch BO methods, particularly in noisy settings.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: synthetic functions (Branin2, Rosenbrock3, Ackley5, Hartmann6) and ML tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR10).  
- Demonstrates practical gains on real tasks (e.g., reducing CIFAR10 test error from ~14% to ~11%).  
- Provides detailed implementation information and releases code, improving reproducibility.  
- Theoretical foundation and derivations are clear and sound; Algorithm 1 is well described.  
- Writing is precise, well-organized, and accessible to the NIPS audience.  
- Originality is strong: formulates a Bayes-optimal one-step batch KG that differentiates from prior work.  
- Significance is substantial for parallel BO and expensive hyperparameter tuning.  
- Weakness: scalability to higher-dimensional problems is unclear; GP priors may be inappropriate for very high-dimensional inputs.  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.  
- Minor issues: several typographical and notation clarifications needed and a brief discussion of parallel EI would help.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution and should be accepted for publication at NIPS 2016.
