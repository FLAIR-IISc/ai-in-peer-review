- Proposes Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), a one-step Bayes-optimal acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Extends the sequential KG decision-theoretic framework to the parallel setting, providing a principled alternative to heuristic batch methods.  
- Defines q-KG as expected improvement in final solution quality after evaluating a batch of q points.  
- Uses Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis to obtain an unbiased gradient estimator for efficient optimization of the Monte Carlo acquisition function.  
- Builds on prior KG work and contributes a theoretically elegant, decision-theoretic advance for parallel BO.  
- Derivation and optimization method are technically sound with strong theoretical underpinnings.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-organized, and situates the contribution relative to prior work.  
- Experiments use test accuracy for HPO, which is a noisy, suboptimal objective; recommends re-running with smoother metrics like log-loss or AUC.  
- Empirical validation is limited (only logistic regression and CNN on MNIST/CIFAR10); needs broader benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) to demonstrate generality.  
- Assumes independent Gaussian observation noise; lacks discussion on robustness to non-Gaussian or correlated noise in real HPO settings.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and significant theoretical contribution, practical IPA-based optimization, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak and insufficient empirical evaluation, poor choice of HPO metrics, and limited benchmark diversity.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for strong theoretical impact but require improved experimental validation in the final version.
