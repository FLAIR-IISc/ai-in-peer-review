- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method selecting one-step Bayes-optimal batches via expected improvement and uses IPA for an unbiased stochastic gradient.  
- Extends sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel setting and relates to parallel acquisition functions like q-EI (Wang et al., 2015).  
- Strength: addresses a practical problem (parallel hyperparameter optimization) with clear presentation and well-explained derivations.  
- Major concern: novelty unclear since IPA-based optimization of parallel acquisition functions was used by Wang et al. (2015); authors must clearly state what is new.  
- Ask whether q-KG was compared experimentally against IPA-based q-EI from Wang et al. (2015).  
- Experimental evaluation incomplete: no wall-clock time comparisons to measure computational overhead of batch selection versus baselines (e.g., parallel random search, sequential BO).  
- Batch size fixed at q=4 without justification; unclear how performance and overhead scale for larger q (tens or hundreds).  
- Plots unclear: x-axis “iterations” ambiguous—does it mean batches or cumulative function evaluations?  
- Practicality question: can the synchronous q-KG be extended to an asynchronous setting?  
- Minor issues: possible missing term in Eq.(4.1), typo (“achieve”→“achieves”), and incorrect attribution of IPA to Wang et al. (2015).  
- Overall: technically sound but claims of superiority are not sufficiently supported and novelty/experiments need major revisions.
