- Introduces parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), extending KG to select Bayes-optimal batches for parallel Bayesian optimization.
- Provides a principled alternative to greedy batch heuristics used with EI or UCB.
- Proposes an efficient gradient-based method to maximize the q-KG acquisition function.
- Theoretical formulation and analysis are elegant, sound, and well-presented.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly relates to prior work.
- Empirical evaluation assumes independent, Gaussian observation noise, which can be unrealistic for HPO with correlated or structured errors.
- Experimental objective (test set error) and threshold-based metrics are noisy and less informative than alternatives like AUC or log loss.
- Benchmarks are narrow (only MNIST and CIFAR-10 with logistic regression and CNN), lacking diverse HPO suites (e.g., HPOlib), limiting assessment of generalizability.
- Theoretical novelty strongly supports acceptance, but limited and narrow experiments weaken the demonstration of practical significance and call for broader empirical validation.
