- Introduces q-KG, a batch generalization of the Knowledge Gradient that selects a Bayes-optimal batch via one-step lookahead.  
- Main technical contribution: efficient maximization of q-KG using Monte Carlo estimation and an unbiased gradient estimator via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).  
- Empirical results: comprehensive benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning show q-KG is competitive or superior to state-of-the-art, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Quality/originality: high—non-trivial extension of KG to batches with a practical gradient-based optimization scheme; technically sound.  
- Writing/clarity: generally well-written, but some areas require clarification.  
- Literature review: contains imprecise claims and misses prior work (e.g., Ginsbourger et al. 2010, Chevalier’s CL-mix, Marmin et al. 2015 on q-EI gradients).  
- Technical assumptions: does not state standard assumptions on domain A (e.g., compactness) or objective f (e.g., continuity).  
- Algorithm details: unclear how often and by what method GP hyperparameters are retrained (e.g., after every batch with MLE?).  
- Gradient derivation: needs brief justification for interchanging expectation and differentiation given the argmin non-smoothness and Cholesky differentiability concerns.  
- Definition of g: should note g = min(current) − min(future) represents positive improvement to be maximized.  
- Significance: high practical impact—leverages parallel compute for expensive black-box optimization and is likely to be adopted.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/principled acquisition function, significant technical contribution (IPA-based gradient), strong empirical performance, high practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lacks experiments quantifying speed-up from batch vs sequential; literature review errors/omissions.  
- Minor points/questions: rationale for using Latin Hypercube initial design; fix Matérn kernel parameter typo (5/2); consider validating GP fits with alternative software to rule out implementation-specific effects.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommend acceptance; identified weaknesses are minor and addressable.
