- Proposes a parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) algorithm for batch Bayesian optimization derived from a decision-theoretic perspective.  
- Introduces a new acquisition function measuring information gain for evaluating q points and selects the batch optimal in the average-case with one batch remaining.  
- Claims Bayes-optimality for minimizing the GP predictor's minimum when only one decision remains.  
- Develops an infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) based, computationally efficient gradient estimator to maximize q-KG.  
- Empirical comparisons show q-KG outperforms or matches parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with pure exploration on synthetic and ML tuning tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear notation, figures, and thorough explanation of method and advantages.  
- Technical soundness and thorough analysis of q-KG properties; experiments are well-designed and results clearly presented.  
- Main novelty concern: batch KG is not new, so the authors must clarify their contribution relative to prior batch KG work.  
- If the gradient estimator is the main contribution, the paper should empirically/theoretically compare its efficiency and effectiveness to prior batch KG methods.  
- Section 5.2 (IPA estimator) is central and needs more detail and elaboration on the estimator and its properties.  
- Comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) is problematic because EI assumes noiseless observations; authors should state whether a noise-aware EI was used and discuss noise effects in Section 6.2.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, addressing novelty clarification, more IPA details, and clarification about the EI/noise comparison.
