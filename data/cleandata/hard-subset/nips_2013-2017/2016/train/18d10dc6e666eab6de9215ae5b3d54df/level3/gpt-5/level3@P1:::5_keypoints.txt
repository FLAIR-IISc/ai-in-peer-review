- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that generalizes KG to select q points by maximizing expected reduction in the posterior minimum.  
- Shows q-KG reduces to parallel EI in the noiseless case when the final recommendation must be among queried points.  
- Develops a Monte Carlo estimator and an IPA-based stochastic gradient to optimize q-KG over a continuous domain with adaptive discretization.  
- Empirical results show strong performance vs. parallel EI and UCB-based batch methods, especially under observation noise, on synthetic and hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Code is released, and established baselines are used to enhance reproducibility.  
- Main technical contribution (IPA gradient) is under-specified: interchange of gradient/expectation, nondifferentiable min, optimizer dependence on samples, and differentiation through Cholesky need clearer regularity conditions.  
- Unclear whether EI baselines in noisy experiments are noise-aware (e.g., NEI-style); comparisons may unfairly favor q-KG if not.  
- No wall-clock runtime or scaling analysis is provided; MC, Cholesky, and gradient costs vs. q, d, M, and sample count need reporting.  
- Convergence guarantees and step-size/SGA conditions for the stochastic optimization are not discussed beyond unbiasedness of the estimator.  
- Section 5.2 lacks detail on Cholesky derivative computation, handling argmin nondifferentiability/ties, MC sample sizes, restart counts, and stopping criteria.  
- Discretization scheme (Section 5.3) is high-level: how posterior sample minima are drawn, computational cost, sensitivity to M, and whether including z(1:q) in An biases optimization are not specified.  
- Notational overload/confusion between feasible set A and internal discretization A; suggests consistent renaming (e.g., S) to reduce confusion.  
- Minor clarity issues and typos (e.g., “Main Stange”, inconsistent IPA introduction and phrasing).  
- Novelty needs clearer positioning vs. prior batch KG and joint batch acquisition work; emphasize continuous-domain KG + differentiable MC estimator + IPA gradients as distinct contributions.  
- Reduction to parallel EI is a useful connection but not fully novel; paper should highlight unique elements beyond that link.  
- Empirical gains in noisy settings are practically significant, but adoption depends on manageable computational overhead.  
- Requests: precisely define EI baselines used in noisy experiments and/or add a noise-aware q-EI comparison.  
- Requests: report wall-clock times and scaling with q, d, M, and MC samples, compared to q-EI and PPES.  
- Requests: expand Section 5.2 with assumptions ensuring unbiased gradient estimation and justify exchanging min/expectation/derivatives.  
- Requests: detail the discretization procedure, how posterior minima are generated, sensitivity analyses over M, and provide ablations (q, M, MC sample count).  
- Requests: discuss impact of re-estimating GP hyperparameters by MLE vs. marginalization and consider reporting marginalization results.  
- Suggest direct comparison to nested batch KG on discrete domains to clarify computational and theoretical benefits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled simple-regret objective, strong noisy-regime empirical performance, practical code and implementation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty vs. prior batch KG, insufficient methodological exposition of IPA gradient, and potentially unfair/noisy baselines.  
- Overall: solid and relevant work that leans toward acceptance if authors address novelty clarification, methodological detail, fairness of baselines, and runtime/ablation analyses.
