- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm for parallel derivative-free expensive evaluations.  
- Generalizes the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to batch selection using Monte Carlo plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) for gradient estimation.  
- Algorithm derived from a decision-theoretic analysis optimizing the average-case posterior when one batch remains.  
- Close relation to sequential KG, extended to parallel setting with new computational techniques for acquisition optimization.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of the GP prior, acquisition function, and optimization method.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or competes with confidence-bound-based methods on synthetic and real-world problems.  
- Concern about novelty: IPA for gradient estimation is a standard technique, so main contribution may be limited.  
- q-KG reduces to parallel Expected Improvement (EI) in the noise-free, restricted-recommendation setting, suggesting limited difference from existing methods.  
- Notation issue: equation (3.1) unclear because mu and K are defined for vectors, not sequences x^(1:n).  
- Quality score: 8/10.  
- Clarity score: 9/10.  
- Originality score: 6/10.  
- Significance score: 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical, novel batch BO formulation; clear presentation; strong empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, potential similarity to existing methods, and some unclear notation.
