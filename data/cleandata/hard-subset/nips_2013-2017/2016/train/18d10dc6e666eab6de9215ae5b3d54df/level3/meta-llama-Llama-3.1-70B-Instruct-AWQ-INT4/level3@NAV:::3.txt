This paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method, which selects multiple sampling points per iteration to allow parallel objective evaluations. The algorithm adopts and generalizes the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to batch selection (q-KG) and uses Monte Carlo plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients, making the batch KG selection computationally tractable.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed method. The authors also provide empirical results showing that the q-KG method outperforms or competes with confidence-bound-based approaches on several test problems, especially when function evaluations are noisy.

Strengths of the paper:

* The proposed method is novel and provides a new approach to batch Bayesian optimization.
* The paper provides a clear explanation of the method and its components.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the q-KG method.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The novelty of the paper is limited, as it mainly extends the KG framework to batch settings and uses a standard IPA-based gradient estimator.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other batch Bayesian optimization methods.
* Equation (3.1) is unclear, as mu and K are defined for vectors, not sequences x^(1:n).

Quality: 8/10

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the novelty of the paper is limited, and the method is not significantly different from previous contributions.
