- Introduces q-KG: a decision-theoretic, Bayes-optimal acquisition function for selecting a batch of q points in parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- Significant theoretical contribution: extends sequential KG to the parallel setting and provides one-step Bayes-optimality for a single remaining batch.  
- Distinguishes from prior parallel BO methods by optimizing the batch collectively rather than using greedy iterative approaches.  
- Strong theoretical foundation with clear derivation and rigorous justification of q-KG’s Bayes-optimal properties.  
- Addresses the computational challenge via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently compute gradients for q-KG optimization.  
- Well-written paper with clear organization, precise algorithm description, and accessible mathematical derivations.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.  
- Major experimental weakness: using test set error as the optimization objective for hyperparameter tuning is inappropriate; metrics like AUC, log loss, or F1 would be better.  
- Limited experimental scope: only two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10) and two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN), lacking broader benchmarks to demonstrate generalizability.  
- Modeling limitation: assumption of independent Gaussian observation noise may not hold in real HPO where noise can be correlated (e.g., from cross-validation).  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical and technical contribution with practical promise, but empirical evaluation limitations weaken the evidence of broad significance.
