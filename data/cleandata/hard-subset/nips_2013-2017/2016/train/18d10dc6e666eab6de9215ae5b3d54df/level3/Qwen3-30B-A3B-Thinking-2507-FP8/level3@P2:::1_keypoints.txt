- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition extending Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Derives a decision-theoretic acquisition function maximizing expected incremental value of a batch.  
- Develops an efficient optimization method using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real ML hyperparameter tasks shows consistent improvements over state-of-the-art, especially in noisy settings.  
- Theoretical foundations are sound and the IPA computational approach is clever and well-justified.  
- Paper should better analyze the extra optimization cost of the acquisition function versus wall-clock speed-up from parallel evaluations.  
- Algorithm 1’s hyperparameter re-training schedule (when GP hyperparameters are updated) is not clearly specified.  
- Clarity issues: ambiguity in Section 5.2 whether g uses max or min, and need better justification for smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor.  
- Typos and minor errors: Matérn kernel parameter written as 2/5 instead of 5/2; unclear justification for restricting initial design to LHS.  
- Original contribution is meaningful, but literature review omits and misattributes related prior work (should cite Chevalier et al. for Constant Liar and Marmin et al. for q-EI differentiation).  
- Significance is high due to relevance for distributed hyperparameter tuning; q-KG shows larger gains in noisy problems and improves test error on logistic regression and CNN tuning.  
- Pro-accept: theoretically grounded, IPA-based computation, strong empirical gains (notably in noise), and clear practical applications.  
- Con-accept: incomplete literature acknowledgment, technical clarifications needed (smoothness/Cholesky), lack of parallel speed-up analysis, and some inaccurate literature claims.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance; issues are minor and fixable for camera-ready version.
