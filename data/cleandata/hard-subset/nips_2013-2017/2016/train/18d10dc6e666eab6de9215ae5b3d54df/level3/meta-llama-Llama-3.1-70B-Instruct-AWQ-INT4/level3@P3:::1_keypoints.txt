- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method derived via decision-theoretic analysis.  
- q-KG selects Bayes-optimal batches for the average-case when one batch remains.  
- Authors provide an efficient algorithm to compute the Bayes-optimal batch.  
- Empirical results show q-KG finds global optima faster than prior batch BO methods on synthetic tasks.  
- q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art on hyperparameter tuning of practical ML algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written and explanations of q-KG and its advantages are clear.  
- Numerical experiments are thorough and evaluate various settings.  
- Strong literature review positioning q-KG within batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Strengths: clear method description, effective experiments, and contextual literature coverage.  
- Criticism 1: paper does not measure actual speed-ups from moving from sequential to batch-sequential BO.  
- Criticism 2: some imprecise claims in the literature review and careless citations.  
- Criticism 3: omits relevant prior work (e.g., Chevalier et al. on CL-mix; Marmin et al. on q-EI natural-gradient).  
- Overall: original and potentially significant contribution; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance.
