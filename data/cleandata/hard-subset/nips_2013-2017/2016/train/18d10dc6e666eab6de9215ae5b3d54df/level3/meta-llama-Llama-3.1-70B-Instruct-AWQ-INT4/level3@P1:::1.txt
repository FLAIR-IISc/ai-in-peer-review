This paper introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm called the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method. The q-KG method is derived using a decision-theoretic analysis that chooses the set of points to evaluate next that is optimal in the average-case with respect to the posterior when there is only one batch of points remaining. The authors provide an efficient strategy for computing this Bayes-optimal batch of points and demonstrate that the q-KG method finds global optima significantly faster than previous batch Bayesian optimization algorithms on both synthetic test functions and when tuning hyperparameters of practical machine learning algorithms, especially when function evaluations are noisy.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the q-KG method and its advantages over existing batch Bayesian optimization algorithms. The authors also provide a thorough literature review and compare their method to several state-of-the-art algorithms. The experimental results are impressive, showing that the q-KG method often outperforms existing algorithms, especially in the presence of noise.

One of the strengths of the paper is its clarity and organization. The authors do a great job of explaining the q-KG method and its underlying assumptions, and the paper is well-structured and easy to follow. The experimental results are also well-presented and provide a clear comparison of the q-KG method to existing algorithms.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it does not provide a detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the q-KG method. While the authors mention that the method is computationally efficient, they do not provide a detailed breakdown of the computational costs involved. This could be an important consideration for practitioners who need to implement the method in practice.

Another potential weakness is that the paper does not study the speed-ups gained by moving from sequential to batch-sequential Bayesian optimization. This could be an interesting area of future research, as it could provide insights into the benefits of parallelizing Bayesian optimization.

In terms of originality, the q-KG method is a novel contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. While the idea of using a knowledge gradient criterion is not new, the authors' extension of this idea to the batch-sequential setting is a significant contribution. The paper also provides a thorough comparison of the q-KG method to existing algorithms, which helps to establish its novelty and impact.

In terms of significance, the q-KG method has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of machine learning and optimization. Bayesian optimization is a widely used technique in many areas of machine learning, and the q-KG method provides a new and efficient way to perform batch-sequential Bayesian optimization. The method's ability to handle noisy function evaluations also makes it a promising approach for many real-world applications.
