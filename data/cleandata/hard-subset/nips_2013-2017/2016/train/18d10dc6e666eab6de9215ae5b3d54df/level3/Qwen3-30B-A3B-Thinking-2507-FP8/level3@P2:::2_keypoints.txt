- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method extending the knowledge gradient to select q points jointly under a GP prior.  
- Derives a decision-theoretic acquisition function that is one-step Bayes-optimal for batch selection.  
- Introduces an efficient optimization strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to make q-KG computationally practical.  
- Novelty: jointly optimizes the batch rather than constructing it greedily, representing a meaningful theoretical advance over prior parallel BO methods.  
- Strong theoretical foundation and clear, reproducible mathematical exposition.  
- Experimental results show consistent improvements over baselines (parallel EI, GP-UCB, GP-UCB-PE), particularly in noisy settings.  
- Major experimental flaw: uses test set error as the optimization objective, which is inappropriate for hyperparameter tuning (should use metrics like AUC or log loss).  
- Benchmarking is narrow (only MNIST and CIFAR10; logistic regression and CNN), insufficient to support broad empirical claims.  
- Lacks comparisons to comprehensive HPO benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib) and more diverse tasks/algorithms.  
- Modeling weakness: assumption of independent Gaussian observation noise is unrealistic for HPO, where errors can be structured or correlated.  
- Quality assessment: theory and computation are strong, but empirical evaluation lacks breadth and depth.  
- Clarity assessment: paper is exceptionally well-written with consistent notation and clear algorithm descriptions.  
- Originality assessment: novel and meaningful extension of KG to the parallel setting, well-contextualized with prior work.  
- Significance assessment: important and practically relevant problem, but limited experiments reduce demonstrated applicability.  
- Recommendation: accept, provided the authors substantially expand and improve the experimental evaluation and use appropriate HPO metrics.
