- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch acquisition function that selects a Bayes-optimal batch for one remaining parallel evaluation round.
- Novelty: generalizes the sequential Knowledge Gradient to the parallel setting and optimizes the batch collectively rather than iteratively.
- Technical contribution: derives q-KG from decision-theoretic principles and uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient computation.
- Empirical strength: strong performance on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noise.
- Practicality: presents an efficient gradient-based optimizer for q-KG and provides code for reproducibility.
- Situates work well in related literature and compares favorably to parallel EI and batch UCB approaches.
- Original and significant: meaningful advance for accelerating expensive evaluations in Bayesian optimization.
- Main weakness: reliance on Gaussian Process priors may limit applicability in high-dimensional problems like neural architecture search.
- Theoretical gap: lacks convergence rates, error bounds, or complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.
- Minor clarity/typo issues to fix (line references): grammar at line 36; define 'A' at line 95; clarify input µ^n in Eq. (3.1) at line 104; discuss pros/cons of parallel EI at line 128; avoid reusing 'A' at line 140.
- Acceptance pros: strong technical foundation, convincing empirical gains, and practical computational strategy.
- Acceptance cons: missing theoretical guarantees and potential GP limitations in high dimensions.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing clarity issues and discussion of strategies for high-dimensional inputs.
