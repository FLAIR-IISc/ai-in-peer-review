- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches via a decision-theoretic formulation.  
- Derives an efficient computation strategy for Bayes-optimal batches using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Demonstrates q-KG outperforms prior batch BO methods on synthetic functions and HPO tasks.  
- Strength: elegant and valuable theoretical contribution to parallelizing Bayesian optimization.  
- Strength: strong theoretical analysis with a clear and well-written derivation.  
- Strength: experimental results show effective performance improvements.  
- Weakness: assumes independent Gaussian observation noise, which may be unrealistic for HPO due to structural bias and correlated errors.  
- Weakness: limited experimental evaluation—only two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10) and two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN).  
- Weakness: uses test set error as the benchmark metric; prefers metrics like AUC or log loss over threshold-based criteria.  
- Weakness: should include broader HPO benchmarks (e.g., HPOlib), more datasets, and more algorithm variety to demonstrate generality.  
- Originality: novel Bayes-optimal batch formulation and use of IPA are innovative contributions.  
- Significance: addresses an important BO problem—efficient parallelization with potential to improve BO/HPO performance.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, accessible, and provides sufficient GP and BO background.  
- Quality of writing: clear, concise, well-organized, and free of grammatical errors.  
- Overall: valuable theoretical contribution with promising results but needs broader experiments to validate generality.  
- Recommendation: accept pending minor revisions and encourage expanded experimental validation across more benchmarks, datasets, and algorithms.
