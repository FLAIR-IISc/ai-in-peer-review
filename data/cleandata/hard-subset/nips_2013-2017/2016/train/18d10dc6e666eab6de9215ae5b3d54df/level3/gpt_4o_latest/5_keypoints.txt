- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm extending the knowledge gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Introduces an IPA-based Monte Carlo gradient estimator to optimize the computationally intensive q-KG acquisition function.  
- Shows superior empirical performance in both noise-free and noisy settings, notably for hyperparameter tuning of ML models.  
- Provides comprehensive experiments on synthetic and real-world tasks demonstrating competitiveness with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Releases open-source code, improving reproducibility and adoption potential.  
- Claims a strong decision-theoretic foundation by extending KG to the parallel setting.  
- Novelty relative to prior batch KG methods is not clearly articulated or contrasted with existing work.  
- Experimental comparisons lack direct mathematical or computational baselines against earlier batch KG approaches.  
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) is under-detailed and needs clearer explanation and examples.  
- Comparison with EI may be unfair because a noise-aware EI variant was not used.  
- Terminology inconsistency: IPA is redefined mid-paper, causing confusion.  
- Recommendation: clarify q-KG’s novelty versus prior batch KG methods.  
- Recommendation: include explicit mathematical/computational comparisons with prior batch KG approaches.  
- Recommendation: expand and clarify Section 5.2 with more details and examples.  
- Recommendation: reassess comparisons with EI by incorporating a noise-aware variant and ensure consistent terminology throughout.  
- Acceptance arguments: theoretically grounded method, strong empirical results (especially in noisy settings), and practical relevance for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Rejection concerns: insufficient clarification of novelty, lack of direct prior-method comparisons, and insufficient detail in key methodological sections.
