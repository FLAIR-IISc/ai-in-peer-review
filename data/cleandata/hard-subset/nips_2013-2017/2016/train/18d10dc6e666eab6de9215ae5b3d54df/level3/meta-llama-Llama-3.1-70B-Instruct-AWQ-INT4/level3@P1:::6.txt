**Paper Review: "The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization"**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method, which computes the Bayes-optimal parallel batch of points to sample. The authors derive the q-KG method using a decision-theoretic analysis and develop an efficient strategy for computing the Bayes-optimal batch of points using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA). The method is evaluated on both synthetic test functions and real-world problems, including tuning hyperparameters of machine learning algorithms. The results show that q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art benchmark algorithms, especially in the presence of noisy function evaluations.

**Strengths**

1. **Novelty**: The paper proposes a new batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that is specifically designed for parallel evaluations.
2. **Theoretical foundations**: The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for the q-KG method, deriving it from a decision-theoretic analysis.
3. **Efficient computation**: The use of IPA allows for efficient computation of the Bayes-optimal batch of points.
4. **Empirical evaluation**: The paper presents a thorough empirical evaluation of the q-KG method on various test functions and real-world problems.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Lack of comparison to similar methods**: The paper does not provide a clear comparison to similar methods, such as parallel EI (q-EI), which uses a similar IPA-based approach.
2. **Limited scalability analysis**: The paper does not provide a thorough analysis of the scalability of the q-KG method to larger batch sizes (e.g., tens or hundreds).
3. **No wall-clock time measurements**: The paper does not provide wall-clock time measurements, which would be useful for evaluating the practical speedups of the q-KG method.
4. **Unclear experimental design**: Some aspects of the experimental design, such as the choice of batch size q = 4, are not clearly justified.

**Clarity and organization**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The background and technical details are clearly described, and the experiments are well-organized and easy to understand.

**Originality and significance**

The paper presents a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that is specifically designed for parallel evaluations. The q-KG method has the potential to be highly relevant to many application domains, including hyperparameter optimization of machine learning algorithms.

**Minor comments**

1. Possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq.(4.1).
2. Grammar typo: "achieve" → "achieves".
3. Fix citation phrasing.
4. Wang et al. (2015) should not be credited with introducing IPA.

**Major questions**

1. Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI (q-EI), and can you clearly state differences?
2. How does the method scale to larger q (e.g., tens/hundreds) and what benefits are expected for more parallel runs?
3. Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous parallel evaluations?
4. What is the wall-clock performance vs random search or sequential BO, and what is the overhead to compute each new batch?
5. Clarify the x-axis “iterations” in plots — batches or function evaluations, does it include initial design, and why do starting values differ across methods?
