- Proposes q-KG, a parallel Knowledge Gradient method for batch Bayesian optimization using IPA to compute acquisition gradients efficiently.
- Shows q-KG is competitive and often outperforms existing parallel BO methods on synthetic benchmarks and HPO (CIFAR10/MNIST), especially in noisy settings.
- Extends sequential KG to the parallel setting to address multi-core optimization of expensive functions.
- Decision-theoretic grounding (KG) and IPA-based gradient computation are well-motivated and technically well-described.
- Presentation and experimental setup are clear and compare the method against relevant baselines.
- Novelty is incremental: KG and IPA usage is not groundbreaking and similar IPA-based parallel selection (e.g., parallel EI) exists.
- Lacks direct comparison to prior IPA-based parallel methods (e.g., Wang et al., 2015) to clarify differences and advantages.
- Experiments omit crucial practical metrics such as wall-clock time and speedup comparisons to simpler baselines like random search and sequential BO.
- Batch size is fixed at q=4 without justification; no analysis of scaling behavior as q increases.
- Method appears synchronous; extensions to asynchronous parallel evaluations are not addressed.
- The x-axis “iterations” in plots is not clearly defined and needs clarification.
- Overall: technically solid and relevant but concerns about novelty, experimental validation, scalability, and asynchrony prevent a strong endorsement without revisions.
