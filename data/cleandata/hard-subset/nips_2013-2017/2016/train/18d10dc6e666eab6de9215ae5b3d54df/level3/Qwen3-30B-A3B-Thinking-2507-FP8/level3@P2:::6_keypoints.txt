- Summary: Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch Bayesian optimization method using IPA to compute gradients, evaluated on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning.

- Strength: Sound theoretical decision-theoretic foundation extending sequential KG to parallel batches.

- Strength: Clear, technically rigorous IPA-based computational approach for estimating q-KG gradients.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation on synthetic and real ML tasks shows consistent improvements, especially in noisy settings.

- Strength: Implementation released publicly, aiding reproducibility.

- Strength: Well-organized paper with clear technical exposition.

- Weakness (novelty): Insufficient differentiation from Wang et al. (2015) and other IPA-based parallel EI methods.

- Weakness (experiments): No wall-clock time measurements to demonstrate real speedups over sequential BO or random search.

- Weakness (experiments): Unjustified fixed batch size q=4 and no tests with larger q values.

- Weakness (experiments): No direct comparison to sequential BO as a baseline for parallel methods.

- Weakness (experiments): No discussion of computational overhead per batch evaluation.

- Weakness (clarity): Plots' x-axis ("iterations") ambiguous—unclear whether it denotes batches or total evaluations; inconsistent starting values suggest differing initial designs.

- Weakness (clarity): Minor grammatical and notation errors (e.g., tense, missing z^(1:n) in Eq. 4.1).

- Weakness (scalability): No analysis or discussion of scalability to larger batch sizes (e.g., q=10 or q=100+).

- Major question: How does q-KG concretely differ from Wang et al. (2015) in acquisition function and IPA computational approach?

- Major question: What are expected benefits and limitations when scaling to larger batch sizes (e.g., q=10, 100)?

- Major question: Can the synchronous q-KG method be extended to support asynchronous parallel evaluations?

- Major question: What is the wall-clock time performance versus random search and sequential BO, and what is the per-iteration overhead?

- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to clarify novelty, provide wall-clock comparisons, justify and explore larger q, and address scalability/asynchronicity.
