- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) to extend KG to batch Bayesian optimization for Bayes-optimal batch selection.  
- Develops an IPA-based gradient estimator to optimize the q-KG acquisition function efficiently.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning show q-KG outperforms existing batch BO methods, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Builds on prior BO literature (GPs, EI, UCB) and recent interest in parallel BO for hyperparameter tuning.  
- Claims novelty by formulating a globally optimal batch selection criterion rather than greedy batch construction.  
- Needs clearer positioning relative to prior batch KG and parallel BO works (e.g., [26], [1], [2]).  
- Strength: sound decision-theoretic derivation of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Strength: IPA-based gradient estimator is a promising approach to scale the method.  
- Strength: empirical performance is competitive in noisy settings and code is available for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: Section 5 (especially 5.2) lacks sufficient detail on IPA derivations and implementation.  
- Weakness: missing discussion of computational cost, memory requirements, and numerical stability of the IPA estimator.  
- Weakness: no comparison of the IPA gradient estimator with alternatives (e.g., finite differences).  
- Weakness: unclear whether EI comparisons used a noise-aware variant, raising fairness concerns.  
- Weakness: empirical analysis lacks a hyperparameter sensitivity study to assess robustness.  
- Minor issue: inconsistent use of the IPA abbreviation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretically grounded batch BO method, promising IPA estimator, competitive empirical results, and available code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient computational detail, potentially misleading EI comparison under noise, limited empirical/sensitivity analysis, and imprecise positioning relative to other batch KG methods.  
- Overall assessment/recommendation: promising work but recommend rejection pending major revisions—expand Section 5, clarify EI comparisons, and provide more thorough empirical evaluation before resubmission.
