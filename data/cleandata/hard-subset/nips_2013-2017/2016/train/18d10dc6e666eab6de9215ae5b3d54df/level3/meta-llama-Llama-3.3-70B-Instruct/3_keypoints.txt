- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that selects a one-step Bayes-optimal batch of points using the Knowledge Gradient framework.
- Generalizes Knowledge Gradient to make joint decisions for multiple points and optimizes expected improvement in objective value for batches.
- Main technical contribution: estimating the q-KG gradient using Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) for efficient acquisition optimization.
- Presents an algorithm to estimate gradients and maximize information gain over candidate sampling sets.
- Empirically evaluated on synthetic benchmarks and real-world problems, including hyperparameter tuning of ML algorithms.
- Results show q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy observation settings.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and technically high-quality with a thorough, well-structured presentation of methodology and experiments.
- Authors demonstrate good knowledge of related work and place their contributions into context.
- Significance: improves efficiency of parallel Bayesian optimization, addressing an important practical problem in many applications.
- Originality: introduces a new acquisition function variant and a novel gradient-estimation approach for batch BO.
- Concern: novelty may be viewed as an extension of existing Knowledge Gradient work to the batch setting rather than a wholly new direction.
- Limitations: paper could include a more detailed discussion of q-KG’s limitations, potential extensions, and sensitivity to hyperparameter choices.
