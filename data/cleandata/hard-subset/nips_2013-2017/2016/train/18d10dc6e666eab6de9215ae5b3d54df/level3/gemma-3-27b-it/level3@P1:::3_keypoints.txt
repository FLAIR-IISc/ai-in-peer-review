- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) to extend KG for batch Bayesian optimization in parallel settings.  
- Selects batches by maximizing expected incremental value of measurements (most informative batch).  
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition efficiently.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real tasks (logistic regression, CNN tuning) show q-KG matches or outperforms existing batch BO, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Positions work within prior BO literature (EI, UCB, prior KG) and emphasizes collective batch optimization versus greedy selection.  
- Technical derivation from a decision-theoretic perspective is sound and IPA application is well-justified.  
- Gradient estimation explanation is detailed and appears rigorous.  
- Experiments are convincing, diverse (synthetic + real), and strengthened by code availability for reproducibility.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with accessible algorithm and math explanations.  
- Method addresses a practical need: efficient parallel hyperparameter tuning.  
- Novelty is limited: generalization to batch KG and IPA use rely on established techniques rather than radical innovation.  
- Equation (3.1) suffers from inconsistent notation (µ and K usage with sequences) and needs clearer formulation.  
- Discretization of domain A (sampling from posterior optima) requires further justification and discussion of its impact.  
- Scalability analysis is limited; more detail needed on q-KG behavior with increasing batch size q and input dimension d.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically solid, practically relevant, strong empirical performance, and reproducible code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited core novelty, notation clarity issue, and insufficient discussion of discretization and scalability.  
- Overall: solid, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify equation (3.1) and expand discussion on discretization and scalability.
