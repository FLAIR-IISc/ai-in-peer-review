- Summary: Introduces q-KG, a one-step Bayes-optimal batch BO acquisition (reduces to q-EI in noiseless sampled-recommendation limit) and a Monte Carlo pathwise/IPA gradient estimator for joint batch optimization; reports strong empirical gains, especially under noise.

- Strength — Conceptual: Principled decision-theoretic batch acquisition extending KG to continuous domains.

- Strength — Algorithmic: Practical MC pathwise (IPA) gradient estimator enabling joint (non-greedy) batch optimization.

- Strength — Empirical: Consistent improvements over common batch BO baselines, with largest gains under observation noise.

- Strength — Positioning: Clear relation drawn to q-EI/UCB/ES and sequential KG literature.

- Weakness — Prior work positioning: Need deeper contrast with prior batch-KG/nested-batch KG methods (objectives, domain assumptions, computational approaches, guarantees) and citation of related discrete-batch KG variants.

- Weakness — Gradient theory: Must state conditions (smoothness/boundedness) for interchange of derivative and expectation, formally prove unbiasedness, and discuss variance and bias from finite discretization.

- Weakness — Differentiation stability: Clarify stability and computation of derivatives through Cholesky/Σ (closed-form triangular solves vs autodiff).

- Weakness — Complexity: Provide per-iteration complexity (e.g., O(q^3) Cholesky, O(|An| q^2) σ̃n), MC sample counts for value vs gradient, and scaling with q and |An|.

- Weakness — Empirical estimator comparisons: Compare sample efficiency and wall-clock to alternatives (gradient-free optimizers like CMA-ES, score-function/REINFORCE, finite-difference/SPSA) and report gradient variance/convergence; ablate CRN and antithetic variates.

- Weakness — Section 5.2 detail: Provide self-contained gradient derivation, handle argmin nondifferentiability (uniqueness/ties or subgradients), and explain practical differentiation through post-argmin x*(after).

- Weakness — Implementation details: Include pseudo-code for stochastic gradient ascent (step sizes, restarts, MC batch sizes, stopping criteria).

- Weakness — Discretization of A: Clarify how M samples of global optima are drawn (PES-style p(x*|data)? Thompson sampling?), describe approximation error and impact on gradient unbiasedness, and ablate M and resampling schedule and inclusion of evaluated points/z.

- Weakness — EI under noise comparison: State whether a noise-aware q-EI variant (e.g., fantasy observations / MC qNEI) was used; if not, include noise-aware baselines or explicitly discuss tilted comparison and sensitivity to noise misspecification.

- Weakness — Experimental reporting: Report wall-clock time per batch (including hyperparameter refits), number of MC samples used, performance vs wall-clock, results for larger q (e.g., 8,16), and provide seeds/configs for reproducibility.

- Minor: Define IPA (Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis) once and use consistently.

- Quality assessment: Decision-theoretic formulation and pathwise estimator are appropriate, but core computation (Section 5.2) and noise-aware baseline comparisons need significant elaboration.

- Clarity assessment: Writing is generally clear, but Section 5.2 and the An discretization procedure require more detail, pseudo-code, and complexity analysis.

- Originality: Moderate to good — meaningful extension of KG to continuous joint-batch selection with a scalable gradient estimator, but prior-batch KG distinctions should be strengthened.

- Significance: High practical relevance for parallel noisy BO if computational scaling and noise-aware comparisons are addressed.

- Pros: Principled batch acquisition, practical gradient-based optimization, solid link to KG theory and relation to q-EI.

- Cons: Insufficient gradient/discretization detail, limited estimator variance/complexity analysis, and potentially unfair EI baselines under noise.

- Recommendation: Promising and likely impactful; lean toward acceptance conditional on clarifying prior work distinctions, expanding Section 5.2 with theory/implementation, and strengthening noise-aware baselines.
