- Paper proposes q-KG, a batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization acquisition extending knowledge gradient to select q points jointly by expected reduction in the GP posterior minimum.  
- q-KG reduces to q-EI in the noise-free, recommendation-in-samples setting, linking to prior parallel EI work.  
- Intractability handled via Monte Carlo "fantasies" and an unbiased stochastic gradient derived by infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) enabling multi-start stochastic gradient ascent.  
- Continuous-domain inner minimization is approximated by discretizing the domain using posterior-near-minima samples, prior points, and candidates (similar to ES/PES).  
- Empirical results show q-KG matches or outperforms strong baselines on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, with notable gains under observation noise.  
- Strength: principled decision-theoretic formulation—Bayes-optimal for one remaining batch under simple-regret.  
- Strength: practical IPA-based stochastic gradient optimization with clear derivations, implementation details, and released code.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance, especially in noisy settings where EI and UCB struggle.  
- Strength: clear connections to prior work (q-EI, ES/PES) and generally clear writing with thorough experiments.  
- Weakness: incremental novelty—natural extension of KG combined with known stochastic gradient techniques parallels prior batch EI work.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees limited to one-step Bayes optimality; lacks finite-time, regret, or error analysis for discretization/MCMC/gradient estimation.  
- Weakness: experiments omit recent information-theoretic batch methods (e.g., parallel PES/PPES), weakening empirical positioning.  
- Weakness: scalability concerns—per-acquisition complexity involves cubic Cholesky in q and O(M q^2) per MC sample; wall-clock overhead not reported.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to discretization (M) and sampling scheme; no ablation provided to assess robustness.  
- Weakness: notational issues around Eq. (3.1) and dual use of A; suggest clearer matrix/vector definitions and renaming discretized set.  
- Weakness: gradient interchange/differentiability through min requires assumptions (uniqueness of minimizers, smooth kernels); these should be stated and ties handled.  
- Quality: technically sound construction and estimator, but would benefit from stronger regularity assumption justification and broader empirical scaling tests.  
- Clarity: generally well written and organized, but improve notation around discretization and Eq. (3.1).  
- Originality: moderate—useful but natural extension of KG with established Monte Carlo/IPA techniques; the q-EI connection is nice.  
- Significance: practical impact strong for noisy parallel BO if computational overhead is acceptable; likely useful to practitioners.  
- Requests: report computational costs (time per acquisition, scaling with q and M) and wall-clock comparisons versus baselines.  
- Requests: provide MC sample/gradient-step counts and sensitivity plots versus MC budget and restarts.  
- Requests: ablation on discretization M and sampling strategy (posterior-minima vs random/grid).  
- Requests: discuss extensions to constraints, heteroscedastic noise, and give CNN experiment hyperparameter search details (ranges, log-transforms).  
- Pros for acceptance: principled myopic acquisition, strong noisy-performance gains, public code, bridges KG and batch BO with workable optimization.  
- Cons for acceptance: incremental novelty, missing comparisons to PPES, limited theoretical analysis, unclear scalability/overhead.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept.  
- Reviewer confidence: high on BO/acquisition design; moderate on implementation scalability.
