- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that yields the one-step Bayes-optimal batch of points.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis and develops an efficient computation strategy for the Bayes-optimal batch.  
- Demonstrates q-KG finds global optima faster than prior batch BO methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Builds on prior knowledge gradient work and compares against parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with exploration.  
- Strength: sound methodological contribution with convincing experiments and clear presentation.  
- Strength: addresses an important problem of speeding up expensive evaluations via distributed/batch sampling.  
- Weakness: Gaussian process prior assumption may be inappropriate for high-dimensional inputs (e.g., neural network architectures).  
- Weakness: lacks theoretical results (error bounds) and quantitative complexity analysis w.r.t. input dimension.  
- Minor clarity issues and typos (grammar on line 36; clarify mu^n input on line 104).  
- Reviewer rates the paper strong overall but requests addressing theoretical concerns before publication.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel efficient algorithm, strong empirical results, well-written and accessible.  
- Rejection arguments: GP prior suitability and missing theoretical guarantees; minor presentation fixes required.  
- Scores: Originality 8/10, Quality of Writing 9/10, Impact 9/10.
