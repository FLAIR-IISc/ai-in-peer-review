- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function that generalizes the sequential Knowledge Gradient to jointly select q points.  
- Targets parallel optimization of expensive black-box functions to find the global minimum.  
- Direct optimization of q-KG is intractable; main technical contribution is a stochastic gradient-based optimization approach.  
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling and derives an unbiased gradient estimator via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).  
- Evaluated on synthetic benchmarks and two hyperparameter tuning tasks (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR-10).  
- Compared against several batch BO baselines (parallel EI, parallel UCB variants); shows competitive or superior performance.  
- Shows notable advantage in noisy evaluation settings, common in practical applications.  
- Method is decision-theoretic and Bayes-optimal under a one-step lookahead assumption.  
- High technical quality: sound derivations, practical algorithm, and thorough experimental validation with means and standard deviations.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with good motivation and positioning relative to prior work.  
- Minor clarity issue: GP notation (e.g., x(1:n) used as both set and matrix) should be explicitly clarified.  
- Originality: novel and non-trivial synthesis of KG and IPA for continuous batch BO, distinct from concurrent discrete work.  
- Significance: practically important for parallel hyperparameter tuning; authors release code to aid adoption.  
- Main weakness/concern: limited novelty since it combines existing components (KG and IPA), though the execution and application are significant.
