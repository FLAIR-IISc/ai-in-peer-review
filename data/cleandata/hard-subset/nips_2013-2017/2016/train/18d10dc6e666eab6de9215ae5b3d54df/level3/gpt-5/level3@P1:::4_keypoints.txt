- Introduces q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function that jointly selects q points by maximizing expected one-step improvement in the GP posterior minimum.  
- Decision-theoretic motivation: Bayes-optimal for the final batch when the final recommendation is the posterior-mean minimizer over a continuous domain.  
- Reduces to parallel EI in the noiseless setting when final recommendation is restricted to sampled points.  
- Develops an efficient Monte Carlo estimator for q-KG.  
- Derives an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to enable gradient-based batch optimization.  
- Approximates the inner continuous minimization by a dynamically updated discrete candidate set built from posterior samples plus evaluated/candidate points.  
- Empirical evaluation on synthetic functions (Branin, Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann) and hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression MNIST, CNNs CIFAR-10) shows consistent improvements over parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE, especially with noisy observations.  
- Public code release is a practical plus.  
- Extends knowledge gradient literature from sequential to batch settings; distinct from parallel EI, UCB, and entropy search approaches.  
- Novelty: nontrivial extension of KG to continuous batch selection with IPA-based gradients.  
- Formulation is sound and clearly connected to Bayes-optimal one-step decision under a GP prior.  
- Conditions for unbiasedness are discussed (differentiable kernel, measure-zero tie events).  
- Discretization strategy is standard and practically sensible.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and uses reasonable baselines and implementations.  
- Lacks theoretical finite-sample error bounds for Monte Carlo and discretization approximations (dependence on M, S, posterior smoothness).  
- Missing explicit complexity analysis: per-iteration computational and memory costs as functions of n, d, q, M, S (e.g., Cholesky costs, gradient amortization).  
- Unclear scalability and sensitivity: behavior in higher dimensions and under GP mis-specification (e.g., structured or neural-architecture spaces).  
- Clarity issues and minor corrections requested (grammar, define A at first use, clarify posterior mean notation and hyperparameter re-estimation, recap parallel EI variants, distinct notation for discretization, Algorithm 1 typo, specify MC sample counts and optimizer settings).  
- Requests for authors: provide finite-sample estimator error bounds, add quantitative complexity/runtime scaling and wall-clock comparisons, discuss applicability to high-dimensional/structured spaces and possible extensions, include sensitivity analyses (GP hyperparameters, noise, discretization M), and test larger batch sizes (q ≥ 8).  
- Pros summarized: principled Bayes-optimal foundation, practical IPA gradient estimator, strong empirical results under noise, joint batch optimization vs greedy heuristics, code released.  
- Cons summarized: no theoretical error bounds, limited complexity/scalability analysis, dependence on GP prior and discretization may limit applicability.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel, and empirically convincing contribution for moderate-dimensional noisy batch BO; recommend acceptance after minor revisions addressing clarity and adding complexity/estimator analyses.
