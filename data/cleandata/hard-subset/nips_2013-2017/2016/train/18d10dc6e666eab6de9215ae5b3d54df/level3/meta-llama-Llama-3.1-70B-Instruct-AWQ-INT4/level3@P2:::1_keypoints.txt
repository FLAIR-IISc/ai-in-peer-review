- Introduces the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition function derived via decision-theoretic analysis.
- Develops an efficient infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA)-based strategy to compute Bayes-optimal batches.
- Generalizes the sequential knowledge gradient to the parallel setting; q-KG is Bayes-optimal when one decision remains.
- Shows q-KG reduces to parallel expected improvement (EI) in the noise-free, restricted-recommendation case.
- Provides clear derivations for q-KG and its gradient, discusses domain discretization, and proposes gradient-based maximization.
- Empirical results on synthetic and ML benchmark tasks show q-KG often outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods.
- Emphasizes the importance of parallelizing BO, especially for noisy function evaluations.

Strengths:
- Novel, well-motivated batch BO algorithm.
- Clear and detailed explanation of algorithm and computations.
- Strong empirical performance across varied settings.
- Highlights practical relevance of parallelization under noise.

Weaknesses:
- Does not study computational speed-ups from sequential to batch-sequential BO.
- Literature review contains imprecise claims and omissions.
- Assumptions (compact domain A, continuity of f) are not explicitly stated.
- Unclear why the initial design A is restricted to Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS).
- Hyperparameter re-estimation/updating procedure is not clearly explained.
- Smoothness/differentiability claims (g in Sec. 5.2 and Cholesky differentiability) are not convincingly justified.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel method with solid empirical validation and potential significant impact.
- Well-written and clearly presented work.

Arguments against acceptance:
- Missing analysis of batch speed-ups and gaps in literature/assumption clarity.

- Quality: 8/10

- Originality: 9/10
