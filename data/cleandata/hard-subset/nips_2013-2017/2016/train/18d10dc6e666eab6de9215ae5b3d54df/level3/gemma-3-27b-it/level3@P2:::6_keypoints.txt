- Novelty: Proposes q-KG, a principled parallel batch BO method combining knowledge gradient with IPA for gradient computation.  
- Theoretical soundness: Derivation based on information gain and IPA is solid and well-explained.  
- Empirical performance: Competitive results on synthetic and real tasks, with stronger gains in noisy settings and CNN tuning.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and accessible to BO researchers.  

- Missing baselines: No comparison to random search and insufficient comparison to sequential BO, limiting practical assessment.  
- Scalability concerns: Experiments only use q=4 and the paper lacks discussion/analysis of computational complexity and bottlenecks as q grows.  
- No wall-clock times: Reporting iterations but not wall-clock runtimes prevents evaluation of practical efficiency and overhead per batch.  
- Relation to prior work: Incomplete discussion of IPA-based parallel q-EI (Wang et al., 2015); claim of introducing IPA is incorrect and differences between q-KG and q-EI are unclear.  
- Experimental clarity: X-axis “iterations” is ambiguous, starting values differ across methods without justification, and initial design inclusion is unclear.  

- For acceptance: Novel, theoretically grounded approach with reproducible code and promising empirical results.  
- Against acceptance: Lacks random/sequential baselines, scalability analysis, wall-clock benchmarks, and clearer relation to prior IPA methods.  

- Key author questions: Clarify comparisons to Wang et al. (2015)/q-EI; explain computational scaling with q and benefits of larger q; discuss asynchronous extension; provide wall-clock comparisons and batch overhead; clarify plot x-axis and starting values.  
- Minor fixes: Equation (4.1) likely missing z^(1:n); typo "achieve"→"achieves"; refine Wang et al. (2015) citation phrasing; remove inaccurate IPA credit.  

- Overall assessment: Promising contribution but insufficient comparative and scalability analysis; current recommendation: weak reject pending major revision addressing these issues.
