

This paper introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion for Bayesian optimization, extending the sequential knowledge gradient approach to the parallel setting. The authors derive a decision-theoretic acquisition function that optimizes a batch of q points simultaneously, providing a Bayes-optimal batch selection strategy. They develop an efficient computational method using infinitesimal perturbation analysis to compute gradients for optimization, and demonstrate superior performance over state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks, particularly in noisy settings.

The paper's strengths include strong experimental validation showing consistent improvements over benchmarks, clear theoretical foundations building on prior KG work, and significant practical relevance for parallel computing environments in machine learning. The societal impact of accelerating hyperparameter tuning is substantial, especially given the resource-intensive nature of modern machine learning.

However, several weaknesses require attention. The literature review contains imprecise claims: integrating EI over the posterior and Constant Liar methods were previously described by Ginsbourger et al. (2010), and Chevalier et al. (2014) presented CL-mix and natural-gradient maximization for q-EI, which should be acknowledged. The paper fails to address whether domain A is assumed compact or if f is continuous, which are important theoretical considerations. The restriction to Latin Hypercube Sampling for initial design lacks justification, and the paper does not study computational speed-ups from batch-sequential over sequential approaches. There are also concerns about the smoothness of function g in Section 5.2, the differentiability of the Cholesky factor, and the Matérn parameter should be 5/2 not 2/5.

Despite these issues, the core contribution is significant and well-supported by experiments. With revisions addressing the literature gaps, theoretical clarifications, and the mentioned concerns, this would be a strong contribution to the field. The paper demonstrates clear advancement in batch Bayesian optimization, making it suitable for publication with major revisions.
