**Summary of Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient method (q-KG), which computes Bayes-optimal batches for parallelizing Bayesian optimization (BO). The algorithm is derived using a decision-theoretic analysis that chooses the set of points to evaluate next that is optimal in the average-case with respect to the posterior when there is only one batch of points remaining. The paper provides an efficient strategy for computing the Bayes-optimal batch of points using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and demonstrates that q-KG finds global optima significantly faster than previous batch Bayesian optimization algorithms on both synthetic test functions and when tuning hyperparameters of practical machine learning algorithms.

**Strengths**

* The paper presents an elegant solution to parallelizing BO, which is a valuable theoretical contribution.
* The theoretical analysis is strong, and the authors provide a clear and well-written explanation of their approach.
* The experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness of q-KG on synthetic test functions and practical machine learning algorithms.

**Weaknesses**

* The assumption of independent normally distributed observation noise may be unrealistic in hyperparameter optimization (HPO) due to structural bias and correlated errors from empirical estimates.
* The experimental evaluation is limited, using only two datasets (MNIST and CIFAR10) and two learning algorithms (logistic regression and CNN).
* The benchmarks are limited to test set error, which is a problematic optimization criterion; threshold-based metrics are suboptimal, and AUC or log loss would be preferred.
* The paper could benefit from a broader range of HPO benchmarks, such as HPOlib, and more datasets and algorithm variety to demonstrate the generality of the approach.

**Originality**

* The paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches, which is a unique contribution to the field.
* The use of IPA to efficiently compute the Bayes-optimal batch of points is a new and innovative approach.

**Significance**

* The paper addresses an important problem in BO, which is the need for efficient parallelization methods to speed up the optimization process.
* The proposed algorithm has the potential to significantly improve the performance of BO in various applications, including HPO.

**Clarity**

* The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the theoretical approach and experimental evaluation.
* The authors provide sufficient background information on Gaussian processes and BO to make the paper accessible to a broad audience.

**Quality of Writing**

* The writing is clear, concise, and well-organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the technical details.
* The paper is free of grammatical errors and typos, demonstrating a high level of professionalism.

**Overall Evaluation**

* The paper presents a valuable theoretical contribution to the field of BO, with a novel algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches.
* While the experimental evaluation is limited, the paper demonstrates the effectiveness of q-KG on synthetic test functions and practical machine learning algorithms.
* With some additional work to address the limitations of the experimental evaluation, this paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of BO and HPO.

**Recommendation**

* Accept the paper, pending minor revisions to address the limitations of the experimental evaluation.
* Encourage the authors to consider additional experiments with a broader range of HPO benchmarks, datasets, and algorithm variety to demonstrate the generality of the approach.
