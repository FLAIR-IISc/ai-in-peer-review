- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization method extending Knowledge Gradient to select one-step Bayes-optimal parallel evaluation batches.  
- Main technical contribution: an IPA-based Monte Carlo gradient estimator that makes optimizing the q-KG acquisition practical.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms other batch BO methods, notably under noisy function evaluations.  
- Strength: tackles a practical batch BO problem relevant to parallel computing environments.  
- Strength: q-KG is theoretically motivated to maximize expected information from parallel evaluations.  
- Strength: IPA gradient estimator is a valuable, practical advance for optimizing q-KG.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation on synthetic and real-world hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Weakness: novelty claim under-supported—batch KG generalizations exist (e.g., Scott et al., 2011; Wang & Powell, 2016); need clearer positioning.  
- Weakness: if novelty is primarily the gradient approach, authors should compare their estimator’s efficiency/effectiveness against prior batch KG computational methods.  
- Weakness: Section 5.2 (IPA derivation) is overly condensed and needs more detail and intuition for reproducibility and soundness assessment.  
- Weakness: EI comparison in noisy settings may be unfair—paper must clarify whether a noise-aware EI variant was used and discuss noise effects on acquisition functions.  
- Minor issue: “IPA” is redundantly defined twice; definitions should be consolidated.  
- Overall: promising, well-motivated work with strong empirical results, but acceptance depends on addressing novelty framing, technical clarity, and fairness of experiments.
