- Proposes parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for batch Bayesian Optimization, extending sequential KG to maximize expected information gain in parallel.
- Method is theoretically grounded and uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient estimation.
- Experiments (synthetic and real-world) show q-KG often outperforms existing batch BO methods, especially under noise.
- Paper is technically sound with logical derivation from sequential KG; GP hyperparameter choices are standard.
- Computational cost and runtime comparisons between q-KG and simpler methods are not fully explored.
- Minor error: Matérn kernel parameter misstated as 2/5 instead of 5/2.
- Writing and organization are mostly clear, but gradient sections are dense and require careful reading.
- Algorithm 1 is helpful but omits crucial details about hyperparameter re-estimation/updating.
- Ambiguity in Section 5.2 about the definition of ‘g’ (max vs. min) and unclear smoothness guarantees for ‘g’.
- Notation could better distinguish the two different sets denoted by ‘A’ (discretization vs. feasible domain).
- Originality: novel extension of KG to parallel setting with decision-theoretic basis; IPA use is a valuable contribution.
- Related work section is incomplete, omitting relevant prior work (e.g., Chevalier et al. and EI integration literature).
- Potential differentiability concerns regarding the Cholesky factor used in derivations.
- Strengths: strong theory, effective gradient estimation, empirical gains, clear algorithm, and open-source code availability.
- Weaknesses: incomplete related work, lack of computational-overhead analysis, missing hyperparameter-update details, and some technical/clarity issues.
- High potential impact for parallel BO applications (e.g., hyperparameter tuning) and societal implications noted.
- Acceptance arguments: novel, theoretically sound, strong empirical results, reproducibility via code.
- Rejection arguments: incomplete related work, insufficient treatment of computational overhead, and need for clarifications/corrections.
- Specific question: are domain assumptions (compactness, continuity of f) explicit and satisfied for theoretical guarantees?
- Specific question: rationale for restricting initial design to Latin Hypercube Sampling and its impact vs. other samplings.
- Specific question: when and how GP hyperparameters are re-trained (frequency, optimization method) in Algorithm 1?
- Specific question: clarify whether ‘g’ uses max or min and state conditions ensuring its smoothness.
- Specific question: could Cholesky differentiability issues affect gradient-based optimization?
- Specific question: suggest verifying GP fits (e.g., Figure 1) using alternative packages (GPy) to confirm implementation correctness.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing related work, hyperparameter updating details, assumptions, and smoothness/clarity issues.
