- Summary: Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic batch BO acquisition function with an IPA-based unbiased stochastic gradient estimator, evaluated on synthetic and HPO tasks, claiming strong performance in noisy settings.

- Strength: Addresses a relevant problem—parallelizing BO for modern multi-core/multi-machine environments.

- Strength: q-KG is well-motivated as a one-step Bayes-optimal batch policy, giving a principled theoretical basis.

- Strength: Paper is clearly written and organized; derivations and experimental setup are comprehensible to experts.

- Strength: Empirical results are promising, especially for noisy optimization problems.

- Weakness: Novelty unclear—IPA-based gradient estimation is similar to Wang et al. (2015) for q-EI, and the paper insufficiently distinguishes its contributions.

- Weakness: Experiments omit wall-clock time comparisons, so practical speedups from parallelization are unproven.

- Weakness: Experiments use only a fixed batch size q=4 with no analysis of scaling or justification for this choice.

- Weakness: Plot labeling is ambiguous ("iterations" unclear), and initial-performance differences suggest inconsistent initial designs.

- Weakness: Limited discussion of practical considerations—method is synchronous and lacks treatment of asynchronous, heterogeneous evaluation scenarios.

- Question: How does this work differ technically and conceptually from Wang et al. (2015) beyond changing the acquisition function?

- Question: How does performance and computational overhead scale with larger q (tens or hundreds), and what are the bottlenecks?

- Question: Can q-KG be extended to an asynchronous setting and what challenges would that entail?

- Question: What are typical wall-clock times for computing q-KG batches and how does total time-to-solution compare to sequential BO and parallel random search?

- Question: In plots, does "iterations" mean batches or cumulative evaluations, and why do methods appear to start at different performances?

- Minor: Eq. (4.1) conditioning appears incorrect—should clarify conditioning variables.

- Minor: Typo in Section 6.1.2 ("achieve" → "achieves").

- Minor: Citation phrasing ("in [2, 19, 25]") is awkward; integrate citations into text.

- Minor: Wang et al. (2015) should not be credited with originating IPA; cite earlier IPA work (e.g., L'Ecuyer, 1990).

- Conclusion: q-KG is principled and promising but lacks clear novelty separation from prior work and needs stronger experimental validation (wall-clock, scalability, asynchronous discussion) before acceptance.
