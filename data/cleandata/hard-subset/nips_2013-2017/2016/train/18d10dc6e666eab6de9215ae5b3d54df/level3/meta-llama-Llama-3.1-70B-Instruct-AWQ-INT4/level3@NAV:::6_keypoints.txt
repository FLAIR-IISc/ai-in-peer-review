- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), from a decision-theoretic perspective.  
- Introduces an acquisition function measuring information gain for evaluating q points and jointly optimizes the batch.  
- Develops an efficient optimization strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients for acquisition maximization.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear background on Gaussian processes and Bayesian optimization.  
- Experiments show q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art benchmarks, particularly in noisy settings.  
- Reviewer not fully convinced by experiments: results are not consistently better than benchmarks across all tests.  
- Paper lacks wall-clock time measurements and comparisons to random search or sequential BO to demonstrate practical speedups.  
- Justification missing for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments.  
- Novelty concerns: use of KG and IPA is not entirely new given existing IPA-based parallel selection (parallel EI / q-EI).  
- Authors should clearly state differences between q-KG and existing methods.  
- Major question 1: Have the authors compared to Wang et al. (2015) / parallel EI (q-EI), and what are the clear differences?  
- Major question 2: How does the method scale to larger q (tens/hundreds) and what benefits are expected from more parallel runs?  
- Major question 3: Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous parallel evaluations?  
- Major question 4: What is wall-clock performance vs random search or sequential BO, and what is the overhead to compute each new batch?  
- Major question 5: Clarify the x-axis “iterations” in plots—are these batches or function evaluations, do they include the initial design, and why do starting values differ across methods?  
- Overall assessment: promising approach; recommend acceptance pending revisions addressing the above concerns.
