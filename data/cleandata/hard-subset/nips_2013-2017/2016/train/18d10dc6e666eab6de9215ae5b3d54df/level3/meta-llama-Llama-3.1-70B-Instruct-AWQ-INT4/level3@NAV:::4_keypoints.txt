- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm for parallel evaluation of expensive black-box functions.  
- Derives a decision-theoretic acquisition function measuring collective information gain of evaluating q points.  
- Develops an efficient computation strategy for the q-KG acquisition function and its gradient using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Addresses the important problem of speeding up expensive evaluations via distributed/batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Proposing q points per iteration is interesting and can substantially improve optimization efficiency.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness through extensive experiments on synthetic functions and real-world tasks, including hyperparameter tuning.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanation of method and theoretical foundations.  
- Experimental results are convincing and comparisons with state-of-the-art methods are thorough.  
- Concern: Gaussian process prior assumption may be inappropriate for high-dimensional inputs (e.g., neural network architectures); authors should discuss limitations and extensions.  
- Concern: Lacks theoretical results such as error bounds and quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.  
- Minor issues/typos to fix (grammar line 36, definition of A line 95, input of mu^n line 104, discussion of parallel EI line 128).  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution, technically sound and well-evaluated; with revisions addressing concerns it has strong acceptance potential.
