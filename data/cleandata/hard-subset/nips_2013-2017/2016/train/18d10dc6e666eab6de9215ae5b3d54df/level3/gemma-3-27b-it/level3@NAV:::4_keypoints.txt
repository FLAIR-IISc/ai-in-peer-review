- Proposes q-KG, a novel decision-theoretic acquisition function for batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization that maximizes information gain for a batch of q points.  
- Extends the knowledge gradient framework to the parallel setting and provides a practical IPA-based gradient estimator for optimizing the acquisition.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, with logical presentation from background to experiments.  
- Correctly situates work relative to parallel EI and UCB, emphasizing collective batch selection rather than iterative heuristics.  
- Strong empirical results on synthetic functions and real-world hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNNs), especially robust in noisy settings.  
- Publicly released code improves reproducibility and impact.  
- Main limitation: reliance on Gaussian process priors, which may be restrictive for high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural architecture search).  
- Lacks theoretical guarantees beyond single-batch Bayesian optimality; missing error bounds and convergence analysis.  
- Missing complexity analysis: unclear how computational cost scales with input dimension d and batch size q.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/sound method, strong empirical performance, clear presentation, reproducible code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: GP dependence limiting applicability to high dimensions, and insufficient theoretical/complexity analysis.  
- Minor issues to fix: rephrase l.36, define A at first use (l.95), clarify input of μ^n (l.104), discuss how parallel EI is compared (l.128), avoid reusing notation A (l.140).  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution that justifies acceptance if theoretical aspects can be addressed in future work.
