- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that yields the one-step Bayes-optimal batch and an efficient way to compute it via decision-theoretic analysis.  
- Goal: speed up expensive function evaluations by proposing q points per iteration for distributed/batch evaluation.  
- Empirically, q-KG outperforms prior batch BO methods on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Strengths: clear derivation, efficient evaluation strategy, convincing experiments, detailed implementation, and open-source benchmarking.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear GP background and consistent notation overall.  
- Originality: interesting and useful idea to propose q points, though it builds on existing BO work.  
- Minor issues: typos and notation problems needing fixes (grammar at line 36; define A at line 95; clarify mu^n input at line 104; discuss parallel EI at line 128; avoid reusing A at line 140).  
- Major concern: assuming a Gaussian process prior may be inappropriate for high-dimensional inputs (e.g., neural network architectures); discuss limitations and extensions.  
- Missing theoretical results: no error bounds or quantitative complexity analysis w.r.t. input dimension.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with potential significant impact after minor revisions addressing concerns.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- For acceptance: tackles an important problem, efficient q-point strategy, strong empirical support, comprehensive comparisons.  
- Against acceptance: GP prior suitability in high dimensions, lack of theoretical guarantees, and minor errors.
