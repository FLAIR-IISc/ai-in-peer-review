- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method that proposes q points simultaneously for parallel evaluation.
- Proposes a decision-theoretic acquisition function that yields the one-step Bayes-optimal batch.
- Develops an efficient computational approach using infinitesimal perturbation analysis to evaluate and optimize the acquisition.
- Theoretically grounded by extending the knowledge gradient policy to the parallel setting.
- Empirically outperforms existing batch BO methods on synthetic problems and ML hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially under noise.
- Addresses an important problem—parallel evaluation to accelerate expensive optimization like neural network tuning.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and makes a solid technical contribution.
- Major limitation: assumes a Gaussian process prior, which may be inappropriate for high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural architecture search).
- Lacks theoretical results such as error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.
- Needs clarification on scalability and more discussion of applicability to high-dimensional settings.
- Minor issues: grammatical error (l.36), undefined notation (l.95), and inconsistent notation reuse (l.140) to be fixed.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical gaps and notation issues.
