- Proposes parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), extending sequential KG to batch selection.  
- Uses Monte Carlo estimation combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to efficiently optimize the intractable batch acquisition function.  
- Evaluated on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning tasks, showing consistent improvements.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms parallel EI, batch UCB, and other benchmarks, especially in noisy settings.  
- Writing is clear, well-organized, and experiments justify practical utility.  
- The IPA-based computational approach is sound and appropriately motivated.  
- Novelty is limited: extension is conceptually straightforward and lacks deep theoretical advancement.  
- Core technique (IPA for gradient estimation) is well-established and not strongly differentiated from prior work.  
- Related work (e.g., [2,5,18,25]) is not adequately distinguished from the proposed method.  
- Technical notational concern in equation (3.1): misuse of μ and K on sequences x^(1:n) creates confusion and could undermine foundations.  
- Overall assessment: modest but useful contribution with strong empirical impact; recommend acceptance if notation is fixed and distinctions from related work are clarified.
