- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm: the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) that computes Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Main contribution is a new acquisition function q-KG that measures information gain from evaluating q points.  
- Strong theoretical analysis: shows q-KG is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when only one decision remains.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow; algorithm presentation is clear and elegant.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: only two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10) and two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN).  
- Use of test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; threshold-based metrics are suboptimal.  
- Recommends using more robust metrics such as AUC or log loss.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed observation noise, which can be unrealistic due to structural bias and correlated empirical-estimate errors.  
- Authors should discuss limitations of the noise assumption and potential remedies.  
- Technically sound and claims are well-supported theoretically (Quality: 8/10).  
- Originality is solid but incremental since knowledge gradient policies exist (Originality: 8/10).  
- Potentially significant impact for Bayesian/hyperparameter optimization if experiments are strengthened (Significance: 8/10).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel batch method, strong theory, clear exposition, potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experiments, problematic metrics, and unrealistic noise assumptions.
