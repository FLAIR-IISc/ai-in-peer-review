- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), extending sequential KG to batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Proposes a novel acquisition function measuring expected information gain for evaluating a batch of q points, Bayes-optimal for the final iteration.  
- Key computational innovation: Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients and optimize the acquisition.  
- Strong empirical performance on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially in noisy evaluation scenarios, outperforming parallel EI, batch UCB, and variants.  
- Implementation and code provided; experiments cover noise-free and noisy settings across multiple dimensions and seeds.  
- Theoretical derivation and justification for the IPA-based gradient estimator are sound.  
- Comprehensive experimental suite: Branin2, Rosenbrock3, Ackley5, Hartmann6, MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN, with appropriate statistical reporting.  
- Well-structured, clearly written paper with thorough positioning relative to prior BO and parallel BO work.  
- Limitation: computational complexity grows for large q; authors propose a discretization strategy to mitigate this.  
- Minor clarity issue: inconsistent notation in Equation (3.1) regarding posterior mean/kernel and sequence vs vector notation.  
- Limited novelty in the computational technique itself (IPA is standard); paper could better explain why q-KG outperforms parallel EI in noisy settings.  
- Significant practical impact for parallel hyperparameter tuning (e.g., CNN test error improved from ~14% to ~11%).  
- Overall recommendation: accept — meaningful extension of KG to batch setting, well-executed theory, implementation, and experiments.
