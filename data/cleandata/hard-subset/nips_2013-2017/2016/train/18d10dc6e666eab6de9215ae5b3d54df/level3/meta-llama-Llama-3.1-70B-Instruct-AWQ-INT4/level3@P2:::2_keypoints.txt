- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization method that computes Bayes-optimal batches and reportedly outperforms existing methods.  
- Paper is clear and well-written, making the approach and concepts easy to follow.  
- q-KG is derived via decision-theoretic analysis, optimizing the average-case posterior when one batch remains.  
- Empirically finds global optima faster than prior batch BO methods on synthetic tests and hyperparameter tuning, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong, with clear explanations of assumptions and mathematical derivations.  
- Notation and exposition are well-defined and accessible.  
- Method is original and significant, addressing limitations of previous batch BO approaches.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and could be more comprehensive.  
- Using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic; more robust metrics (AUC, log loss) should be considered.  
- Benchmarks are narrow (MNIST, CIFAR10); broader HPO benchmarks like HPOlib are missing.  
- Only two datasets and two learning algorithms (logistic regression, CNN) were used; more dataset/algorithm variety is needed.  
- Authors responsibly discuss strengths and limitations and provide warranted claims.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing experimental evaluation shortcomings in future work.
