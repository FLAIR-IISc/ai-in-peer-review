## Review of "The Parallel Knowledge Gradient Method for Batch Bayesian Optimization"

This paper introduces the Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm designed for scenarios where multiple function evaluations can be performed simultaneously. The core idea is to extend the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to a batch setting, aiming for the Bayes-optimal selection of points for parallel evaluation. To address the computational intractability of directly maximizing the q-KG acquisition function, the authors leverage Monte Carlo sampling combined with Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient estimation. 

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the mathematical foundations of q-KG, along with a detailed description of the gradient estimation technique. The experimental evaluation on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning tasks demonstrates that q-KG consistently outperforms or matches the performance of existing batch Bayesian optimization algorithms, particularly in noisy evaluation settings. The code availability is also a positive aspect.

The strength of this work lies in its principled approach to batch Bayesian optimization. By extending the KG framework, the authors build upon a solid theoretical foundation. The proposed IPA-based gradient estimator offers a practical solution to the computational challenges associated with maximizing the q-KG acquisition function. The empirical results provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of the method. 

However, the novelty of the work is somewhat limited. The paper primarily extends an existing method (KG) to a batch setting and employs a standard technique (IPA) for gradient estimation. While the combination is effective, it doesn’t represent a radical departure from existing approaches.  A minor technical issue exists in Equation (3.1), where the definitions of *µ* and *K* as functions of vectors are applied to sequences *x^(1:n)*, making the equation unclear.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Sound theoretical foundation based on the KG framework.
*   Practical and effective gradient estimation technique using IPA.
*   Strong empirical results demonstrating performance improvements.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.
*   Practical relevance due to increasing availability of parallel computing resources.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited novelty – primarily an extension and application of existing methods.
*   Minor technical issue in Equation (3.1) hindering clarity.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid and well-executed work that makes a valuable contribution to the field of Bayesian optimization. While the novelty may not be groundbreaking, the method is theoretically sound, practically implementable, and empirically effective. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify Equation (3.1).
