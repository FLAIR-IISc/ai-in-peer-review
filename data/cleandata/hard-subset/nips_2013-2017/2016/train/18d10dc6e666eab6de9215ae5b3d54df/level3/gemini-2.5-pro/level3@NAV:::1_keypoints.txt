- Introduces q-KG, a parallel Knowledge Gradient acquisition function extending sequential KG to a one-step Bayes‑optimal batch BO criterion.  
- Derives an efficient gradient-based strategy to maximize the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Distinguishes itself from greedy/heuristic EI and UCB extensions by jointly optimizing the value of the entire batch.  
- Principled approach with significant practical importance for parallelizing Bayesian optimization.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks show q-KG is competitive and often outperforms state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Reviewer strongly recommends publication.  
- Literature review contains imprecise claims and omits key prior work (e.g., Ginsbourger et al.; Chevalier et al. on CL‑mix; Marmin et al. on natural‑gradient q‑EI).  
- Paper should explicitly state assumptions (e.g., whether domain A is compact and f is continuous).  
- Need justification/discussion for smoothness of g and differentiability of the Cholesky factor used in derivations.  
- Algorithmic details unclear: why restrict the initial design to a Latin Hypercube Sample and how GP hyperparameters are updated during the optimization loop.  
- Recommend studying computational speed‑ups achieved by the batch‑sequential method versus a purely sequential approach.  
- Minor points: clarify whether g is defined with a min or max; correct the Matérn parameter typo (likely 5/2, not 2/5); and verify GP fits in Figure 1 using different software for reproducibility.
