- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch acquisition function for Bayesian optimization derived from a decision-theoretic (Bayes-optimal) perspective.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients and enable practical optimization of the q-KG acquisition.  
- Jointly optimizes entire evaluation batches rather than selecting points greedily or sequentially.  
- Empirical results show consistent outperformance of existing parallel BO methods on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.  
- Strong theoretical grounding in the Knowledge Gradient framework and clear derivation.  
- Technical soundness: correct GP/BO methodology and effective use of IPA for computation.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-written, clear notation for the most part, and code available.  
- Original contribution: meaningful generalization of Knowledge Gradient to parallel/batch settings with an efficient computation strategy.  
- Limitation: heavy reliance on Gaussian Process priors, which may not scale or perform well in high-dimensional problems (e.g., complex neural network tuning).  
- Missing rigorous theoretical analysis: no error bounds, regret/convergence rates, or quantitative complexity analysis as functions of dimension, batch size, and data.  
- Minor presentation issues: grammatical awkwardness and unclear/ reused notation (lines 36, 95, 104, 128, 140) needing clarification.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong empirical evidence, and reproducible code.  
- Arguments for rejection: GP reliance and lack of rigorous theoretical guarantees limit applicability and generalizability.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing minor clarifications and adding discussion or directions on scalability to high-dimensional problems.
