- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm generalizing sequential KG to parallel settings.  
- Provides clear background on Bayesian optimization and Gaussian processes, situating the work in prior research.  
- Derives q-KG via a decision-theoretic framework and details computation strategies, including IPA for gradient estimation and domain discretization.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art batch methods, especially in noisy settings and hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Technical soundness: rigorous derivation of acquisition function and gradient computation; IPA justification is strong.  
- Empirical validation is comprehensive, covering synthetic benchmarks and real ML tasks (logistic regression, CNNs).  
- Clarity and organization: well-written, logically structured, with open-source code for reproducibility.  
- Societal impact: enables efficient parallel evaluations, accelerating resource-intensive optimization tasks.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of computational speed-ups from sequential to batch-sequential optimization, limiting practical deployment insights.  
- Literature review contains imprecise statements regarding prior work on EI and q-EI maximization; needs more nuanced discussion.  
- Technical assumptions (domain compactness, function continuity), hyperparameter re-estimation, and acquisition function smoothness are under-addressed.  
- Inconsistent software use for GP fitting in experiments raises fairness concerns; a unified implementation would be preferable.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, impactful algorithm with rigorous methodology and compelling empirical results, especially for noisy problems.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing speed-up analysis, imprecise literature treatment, and underexplored technical/implementation details affecting reproducibility.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the noted weaknesses.
