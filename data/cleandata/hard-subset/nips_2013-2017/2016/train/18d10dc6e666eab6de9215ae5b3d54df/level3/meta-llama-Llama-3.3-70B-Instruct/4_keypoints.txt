Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG), for optimizing multiple points in parallel.

Provides a clear introduction to parallel Bayesian optimization and a thorough explanation of q-KG and its theoretical foundations.

Paper is well-written with a solid methodological approach and sound experiments demonstrating q-KG’s effectiveness.

Includes detailed comparisons to state-of-the-art batch BO methods (parallel EI, UCB), showing q-KG is competitive or superior on synthetic and practical ML problems.

Strength: introduces a novel and effective algorithm capable of parallel evaluation of multiple points.

Strength: offers a thorough theoretical analysis including derivation and computational implementation details.

Strength: presents a comprehensive experimental evaluation across synthetic functions and practical machine learning tasks.

Weakness: assumes a Gaussian process prior, which may not be appropriate for all functions or problem domains.

Weakness: lacks theoretical results on error bounds and complexity analysis for q-KG.

Weakness: requires minor revisions to fix typos and clarify certain notations and definitions.

Pro-acceptance arguments: novelty, strong theoretical treatment, and comprehensive experiments indicate wide applicability and potential efficiency gains.

Con-acceptance arguments: GP assumption, missing error/complexity theory, and minor editorial/notation issues could limit confidence.

Overall assessment: meets conference criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance and is a strong candidate for acceptance.
