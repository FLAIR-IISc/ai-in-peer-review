- Paper proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that selects q points by maximizing expected reduction in the posterior mean minimum, and uses IPA-based Monte Carlo gradient estimates to enable gradient optimization.

- Evaluated on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression on MNIST, CNN on CIFAR-10) in noise-free and noisy settings, claiming competitive performance and advantages in noisy cases.

- Strength: Problem is significant and relevant for parallel hyperparameter optimization on multi-core/distributed systems.

- Strength: Paper is generally well-written with clear derivations for q-KG and the IPA-based gradient estimation.

- Strength: Method has a principled decision-theoretic one-step Bayes-optimal foundation.

- Weakness: Limited novelty — KG and IPA are existing techniques, and applying IPA to parallel acquisition was done for q-EI by Wang et al. (2015); the paper does not clearly differentiate its contribution.

- Weakness: Experimental evaluation is unconvincing due to missing wall-clock time comparisons, obscuring real-world speedups given acquisition optimization overhead.

- Weakness: Missing baselines — no comparison to parallel random search or sequential BO to quantify practical gains from parallelism.

- Weakness: All experiments use fixed batch size q=4 with no study of scalability or computational cost as q increases.

- Weakness: Plots are ambiguous (x-axis labeled "iterations"); different methods appear to start at different post-initial-design performance which is unexplained.

- Major question: Clarify differences from Wang et al. (2015) and provide direct experimental comparison to q-EI, especially under noise.

- Major question: Provide analysis and experiments on scalability with batch size q (e.g., q=2,8,16) and practical limits given Cholesky and Monte Carlo costs.

- Major question: Discuss synchronous vs asynchronous parallelism and how the method could be extended to asynchronous settings used in practice.

- Major question: Report wall-clock overhead for acquisition optimization versus evaluation time (e.g., how long to select a batch in the CNN experiment) and provide performance vs total wall-clock time plots.

- Question/clarification: Explain the x-axis labeling and whether iterations count batches or evaluations, and why algorithms show differing initial regrets after the shared initial design.

- Minor: Clarify notation in Eq.(4.1) regarding the dependency of µ(n+q) on observed outcomes.

- Minor: Wording fix in Section 6.1.2 ("achieves a test error of around 11%").

- Minor: Cite papers for open-source implementations and acknowledge IPA as an older general technique beyond Wang et al.
