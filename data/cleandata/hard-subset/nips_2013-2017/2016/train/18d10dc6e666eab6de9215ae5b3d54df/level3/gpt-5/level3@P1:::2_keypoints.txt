- Introduces q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization acquisition that selects the Bayes-optimal set of q points to minimize the GP posterior predictive minimum when one batch remains.  
- Generalizes the classical knowledge gradient (KG) to parallel evaluations and shows reduction to parallel EI in the noiseless, restricted-solution setting.  
- Uses Monte Carlo to evaluate q-KG and develops an IPA-based stochastic gradient estimator for efficient gradient-based batch optimization.  
- Implements discretization of the candidate set for the inner minimum, regenerating candidates from samples near posterior optima.  
- Empirical comparisons (parallel EI variants, GP-BUCB, GP-UCB-PE) on synthetic functions and HPO for logistic regression (MNIST) and a CNN (CIFAR-10) show clear gains, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Builds on prior KG and GP BO literature and contrasts with EI, entropy-based, and UCB-based batch policies; focuses on optimizing simple regret rather than cumulative regret.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized decision-theoretic formulation that cleanly generalizes KG to batch settings.  
- Strength: elegant, seemingly unbiased and practical IPA-based stochastic gradient estimator.  
- Strength: sound theoretical derivations and correct reduction to parallel EI in a special case.  
- Strength: explicitly addresses noisy evaluations where many batch BO methods struggle; code released for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: assumes independent, homoscedastic Gaussian observation noise; lacks discussion or experiments on heteroscedastic/structured noise robustness.  
- Weakness: choice of classification metric (test error) is threshold-dependent and non-smooth; should justify or include log loss/AUC results.  
- Weakness: narrow empirical benchmarks (two datasets/algorithms); needs broader HPO suites and more diverse learners to support generality.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to some strong batch BO baselines (e.g., PPES, recent q-EI optimizers).  
- Weakness: computational concerns—relies on discretization and repeated Choleskies; needs complexity analysis, wall-clock timings, and sensitivity to discretization size M, MC samples, and gradient variance.  
- Weakness: practical issues around refitting GP hyperparameters each batch and robustness to mis-specified kernels/noise variance not explored.  
- Quality/clarity: high theoretical quality and clear exposition; minor editorial issues and should clarify dual role of A earlier.  
- Originality/significance: novel and nontrivial extension of KG to batch with unbiased stochastic gradients; potentially high impact if empirical scope is broadened.  
- Key questions: sensitivity to discretization M, typical MC sample sizes and scaling with q, applicability to heteroscedastic/correlated noise, and performance on log loss/AUC.  
- Pros for acceptance: strong theory, principled Bayes-optimal batch acquisition, addresses noisy batch BO, available code.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited empirical validation, simplistic noise assumptions for HPO, missing baseline comparisons and scaling analysis.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — solid theoretical contribution and promise, but needs broader experiments, metric justification, and computational analysis.
