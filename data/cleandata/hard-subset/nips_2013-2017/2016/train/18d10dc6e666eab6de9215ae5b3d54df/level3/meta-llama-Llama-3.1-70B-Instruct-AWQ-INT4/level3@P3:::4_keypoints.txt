- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm (q-KG) that outputs q points per iteration to speed up expensive evaluations.
- Tackles an important problem of distributed/batch evaluation and can substantially improve optimization efficiency.
- Provides thorough background on Gaussian processes and clear notation for the method.
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis and gives an efficient strategy to compute the Bayes-optimal batch.
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients of q-KG, enabling acquisition optimization.
- Presents empirical results on synthetic functions and real ML tasks showing q-KG finds global optima faster than prior batch BO, especially under noisy evaluations.
- Offers a detailed implementation description and comparisons to open-source benchmark methods.
- Paper is well-written with convincing experiments and solid contribution to Bayesian optimization.
- Minor issues and typos to fix (grammar at line 36; define A on line 95; clarify mu^n input on line 104; discuss parallel EI on line 128; avoid reusing notation A on line 140).
- Concern that assuming a Gaussian process prior may be inappropriate for high-dimensional inputs (e.g., neural network architectures); needs more discussion and potential extensions.
- Requests for theoretical results: provide error bounds and quantitative complexity analysis w.r.t. input dimension.
- Originality is significant—the idea of proposing q points per iteration is novel and impactful.
- Significance is high—results demonstrate potential for broad application in ML hyperparameter tuning and other fields.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, decision-theoretic derivation, strong empirical results, clear exposition.
- Arguments against acceptance: GP prior limitations in high-dimensions, lack of theoretical guarantees/complexity analysis, and minor presentation issues.
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
