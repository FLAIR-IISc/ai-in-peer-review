- Problem addressed: batch Bayesian Optimization — selecting multiple points per iteration to optimize expensive black-box functions.
- Proposed method: q-KG, a parallel Knowledge Gradient acquisition function that is one-step Bayes-optimal for entire batches.
- Computational challenge: direct optimization of q-KG is intractable.
- Optimization solution: Monte Carlo estimation of q-KG with an unbiased stochastic gradient derived via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).
- Optimization benefit: IPA gradient enables use of standard gradient-based optimizers to jointly select batch points.
- Comparison to prior work: contrasts with EI/UCB and greedy batch methods by jointly optimizing the batch rather than constructing it greedily.
- Empirical evaluation: tested on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning for logistic regression and CNNs.
- Empirical findings: competitive or superior performance to baselines, with notable robustness and gains in noisy settings.
- Quality assessment: strong theoretical motivation, correct IPA derivation, thorough experiments, and complete usable algorithm.
- Clarity assessment: exceptionally well-written and organized; minor notation ambiguity for x(1:n) should be defined explicitly.
- Originality assessment: novel extension of KG to batch BO, but relies on established IPA technique — somewhat incremental.
- Significance: high practical importance for parallel BO and hyperparameter tuning; released code increases impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, provides a principled joint acquisition, makes it computationally feasible via IPA, shows strong empirical results (especially with noise), and is clearly presented.
- Argument against acceptance: perceived limited novelty because it combines existing KG and IPA ideas.
- Overall verdict: strengths (practical relevance, theory, experiments, clarity) outweigh minor originality concerns; valuable contribution.
