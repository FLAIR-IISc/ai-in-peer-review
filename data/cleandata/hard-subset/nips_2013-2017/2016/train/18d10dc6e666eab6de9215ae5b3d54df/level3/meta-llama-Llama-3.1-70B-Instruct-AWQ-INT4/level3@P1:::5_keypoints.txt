- Proposes q-KG, a batch/generalized knowledge gradient method derived via decision-theoretic analysis and claimed Bayes-optimal when one decision remains.  
- Develops a computationally efficient maximization approach using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Introduces a Monte Carlo gradient estimator to find optimal batches (key component of q-KG).  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on synthetic functions and practical ML algorithm tuning.  
- Paper is well-written and explains the method and advantages clearly.  
- Reviewer questions the novelty of the batch KG contribution, citing prior batch KG work.  
- Requests clarification of the contribution relative to prior batch KG literature and more detailed comparisons.  
- Asks for more detail and elaboration on the Monte Carlo gradient estimator, including empirical/theoretical comparisons to prior methods.  
- Notes the comparison to expected improvement (EI) is problematic because EI assumes noiseless observations.  
- Requests clarification whether a noise-aware EI variant was used and discussion of noise effects (especially for Section 6.2).  
- Points out minor fixes (e.g., IPA abbreviation redefined at line 162).  
- Recommendation: authors should address novelty claims, expand comparisons and estimator details, clarify noise treatment, and fix minor issues.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10, confidence 8/10.
