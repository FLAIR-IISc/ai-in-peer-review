- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm derived from decision-theoretic analysis for parallel settings.
- Proposes an efficient strategy to compute Bayes-optimal batches and demonstrates strong empirical performance, especially with noisy evaluations.
- Provides clear background on Gaussian processes and Bayesian optimization; paper is well-organized and well-written.
- Thorough explanation of q-KG and detailed derivation of the q-KG factor and its gradient using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Experimental results show q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art batch BO on synthetic and real-world problems.
- Strength: novel and effective approach to parallel Bayesian optimization with solid empirical evidence.
- Strength: clear presentation and thorough related-work review.
- Concern: lacks wall-clock time measurements and comparisons to random search or sequential BO to demonstrate practical speedups.
- Concern: no justification for fixing batch size q = 4 across all experiments; impact of q is unexplored.
- Concern: missing clear comparisons to other parallel BO methods (e.g., parallel EI / parallel UCB) and explanations of differences.
- Novelty: builds on prior work, but using IPA for acquisition-gradient estimation is a new contribution; needs clearer differentiation from past work.
- Major question: Have authors compared to Wang et al. (2015) / q-EI, and can they state precise differences?
- Major question: How does q-KG scale to larger q (tens/hundreds) and what benefits are expected with more parallelism?
- Major question: Can the synchronous q-KG be extended to asynchronous parallel evaluations?
- Major question: What is the wall-clock performance vs random search or sequential BO, and what is the overhead to compute each batch?
- Major question: Clarify x-axis “iterations” in plots — are these batches or function evaluations, do they include initial design, and why starting values differ across methods?
- Minor comments: possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq.(4.1); grammar typo “achieve”→“achieves”; fix citation phrasing.
- Minor comment: Wang et al. (2015) should not be credited with introducing IPA.
