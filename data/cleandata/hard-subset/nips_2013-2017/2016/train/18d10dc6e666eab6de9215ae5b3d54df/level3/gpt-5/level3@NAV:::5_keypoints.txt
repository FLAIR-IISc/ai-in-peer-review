- Paper proposes a batch (q-KG) acquisition function for Bayesian optimization plus an IPA-based Monte Carlo gradient estimator for optimization.  
- Method motivated by a one-step Bayes-optimal batch criterion estimated under a GP and optimized with stochastic gradients.  
- Experiments on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning show improvements over q-EI variants and batch UCB, notably under observation noise.  
- Relation to prior work presented, but novelty over existing batch KG and related extensions is not clearly delineated.  
- Unclear whether the main contribution is the Bayes-optimal batch derivation for continuous A or the stochastic gradient estimator and optimizer.  
- The decision-theoretic objective and reparameterization are sound, but Section 5.2 (IPA gradient estimator) is too terse.  
- Need formal assumptions for unbiasedness, justification for interchanging gradient and expectation, and treatment of ties/nonsmooth minima.  
- Require variance and complexity analyses of the gradient estimator and discussion of variance control.  
- If gradient estimator is the main novelty, include empirical/theoretical comparisons to prior batch KG and alternative estimators (reparameterization/score-function).  
- Report wall-clock time, optimizer convergence, and sensitivity to number of MC samples M, batch size q, and input dimension.  
- The discretization of A is central; discuss its bias/variance tradeoffs and impact on Bayes-optimality claims.  
- Clarity issues: Section 5.2 needs expansion, the dual use of “A” (domain vs discretization) is confusing, and “IPA” is redefined erroneously.  
- Originality is not fully established; better position the contribution relative to earlier batch KG variants.  
- Significance: a noise-robust joint batch rule would be valuable if substantiated, but EI comparisons may be unfair unless a noise-aware q-EI baseline was used—clarify and analyze noise modeling effects.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled batch objective, strong noisy-BO results, and practical stochastic gradient optimization with code.  
- Cons for acceptance: unclear novelty vs prior batch KG, insufficient detail/analysis in Sec. 5.2, and potentially unfair EI comparisons without noise-aware baselines.
