- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for batch Bayesian optimization, using Monte Carlo + IPA for tractable gradient estimation.  
- Builds on solid prior work (Frazier & Powell, Contal et al., Shah et al.) and relates clearly to parallel EI and UCB.  
- Strength: sound decision-theoretic derivation extending KG to the batch setting.  
- Strength: IPA-based gradient estimation yields practical computational efficiency.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation on synthetic problems and real hyperparameter tuning (logistic regression, CNN), especially in noisy settings.  
- Strength: Clear presentation, mostly clear notation, concise algorithm, and available code.  
- Strength: High practical relevance for tuning complex ML models with batch evaluations.  
- Weakness: Novelty is somewhat incremental—combines existing KG and IPA ideas without a groundbreaking conceptual leap.  
- Weakness: Equation (3.1) is unclear—notation for µ and K on sequences needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Discretization strategy for domain A is brief; sensitivity to sample count (M) and alternatives are not explored.  
- Weakness: Comparisons lack depth—insufficient analysis of why q-KG outperforms baselines (e.g., exploration-exploitation behavior).  
- For acceptance: tackles an important problem, shows competitive results, makes method practical via IPA, is well-written, and provides reproducible code.  
- Against acceptance: limited novelty, some unclear technical details, and insufficient behavioral analysis relative to baselines.  
- Overall assessment: Valuable though incremental contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify Eq. 3.1 and expand discretization/analysis.
