- Introduces batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) and an IPA-based differentiable gradient estimator to optimize it.  
- Defines q-KG as the expected one-step reduction in the GP posterior predictive minimum after a batch of size q.  
- Proves q-KG reduces to parallel EI in the noise-free setting when final recommendation is restricted to sampled points.  
- Empirical results: q-KG typically outperforms parallel EI variants, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic benchmarks and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially with noise.  
- Paper fits conference scope and connects to prior batch BO work (q-EI, PES) and sequential KG.  
- Decision-theoretic formulation is sound and the Monte Carlo/IPA approach for differentiable q-KG is well motivated.  
- Missing analysis: does not report wall-clock or throughput speed-ups from batching versus sequential policies (only iteration-based regret).  
- Clarity: generally clear and well organized with helpful algorithmic detail, but several points need clarification.  
- Literature review has imprecise claims and omissions (e.g., Constant Liar variants, CL-mix by Chevalier et al., and natural-gradient q-EI differentiation).  
- Originality: novel and significant extension of KG to the batch setting with a practical differentiable estimator.  
- Significance: high — efficient parallel BO is impactful, with especially compelling gains in noisy settings.  
- Pros: principled decision-theoretic foundation, efficient gradient-based optimization, strong empirical results, high practical impact.  
- Request to report actual wall-clock or sample-throughput speed-ups for batch vs. sequential approaches.  
- Ask to cite and position prior work on integrating EI over the posterior and Constant Liar (including CL-mix) and on differentiating multipoint EI.  
- Ask to state assumptions: is the domain A compact and is f continuous to ensure existence of minima in (4.1)/(4.2)?  
- Question why initial design is restricted to LHS; request sensitivity analysis to other space-filling designs.  
- Algorithm 1 needs clarification on hyperparameter updates: frequency, use of warm starts, priors, and whether pure MLE is used every batch.  
- Sec. 5.2: clarify whether g uses min or max — sign convention is confusing.  
- Smoothness/differentiability: justify differentiability through Cholesky (SPD covariance, non-degenerate K) and discuss corner cases (duplicate points, measure-zero non-differentiabilities).  
- Typo: Matérn parameter should be 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Reproducibility: ask whether the Figure 1 GPs can be refit with an independent toolbox for verification.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance.
