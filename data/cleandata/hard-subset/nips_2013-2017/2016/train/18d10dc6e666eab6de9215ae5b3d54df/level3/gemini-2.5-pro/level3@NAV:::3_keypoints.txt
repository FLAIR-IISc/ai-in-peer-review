- Proposes q-KG: a batch Bayesian optimization algorithm generalizing one-step KG to select q points in parallel.  
- Uses Monte Carlo simulation plus an Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) estimator to compute and optimize the stochastic q-KG acquisition function.  
- Positions the work within existing efforts to parallelize Bayesian optimization (extensions of EI and UCB).  
- Strength: addresses practical, important problem (parallel black-box optimization) relevant to large-scale hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: well-motivated extension of a principled, one-step optimal policy to the parallel setting.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation showing q-KG is competitive and often outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, especially in noisy settings.  
- Strength: paper is clearly written and easy to follow.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — essentially a straightforward combination of batch KG and standard IPA-based gradient estimation, so the conceptual leap is modest.  
- Weakness: would benefit from clearer articulation of any unique technical challenges or insights specific to this combination.  
- Minor issue: notation in Eq. (3.1) (µ(x(1:n)), K(x,x(1:n))) is unclear and should explicitly denote the vector of mean evaluations and the kernel cross-covariance matrix.
