- Summary: Proposes q-KG, a batch extension of Knowledge Gradient using a Monte Carlo gradient estimator via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis to enable gradient-based batch selection, with empirical gains in noisy settings.

- Strength: Tackles the practical problem of parallel/batch Bayesian optimization.

- Strength: q-KG is well-motivated decision-theoretically as a one-step Bayes-optimal policy for improving the posterior mean optimum.

- Strength: The stochastic (IPA-based) gradient estimator makes optimizing an otherwise intractable acquisition function practical.

- Strength: Empirical results show competitive or superior performance versus baselines, notably in noisy evaluation scenarios.

- Weakness 1 (originality): The novelty claim is weakly framed—batch KG has prior work (e.g., [26]) and the paper needs a more thorough comparison to existing batch KG formulations.

- Weakness 1 cont.: It is unclear whether the main contribution is the q-KG formulation or the gradient-based optimization method; clearer positioning against prior work is required.

- Weakness 2 (technical detail): Section 5.2 lacks sufficient detail; key derivations are relegated to the supplement, hindering reader verification.

- Weakness 2 cont.: The paper should state the regularity conditions justifying interchange of expectation and differentiation and include a high-level sketch of the gradient derivation.

- Weakness 3 (estimator evaluation): The claimed efficiency of the IPA estimator is not substantiated—no comparison of computational cost or solution quality versus non-gradient or other optimization methods.

- Weakness 4 (EI comparison): The EI baseline may be unfair in noisy experiments; the paper must clarify which EI variant was used and preferably compare to a noise-aware EI.

- Minor issue: The acronym "IPA" is defined twice; it should be defined once at first use.
