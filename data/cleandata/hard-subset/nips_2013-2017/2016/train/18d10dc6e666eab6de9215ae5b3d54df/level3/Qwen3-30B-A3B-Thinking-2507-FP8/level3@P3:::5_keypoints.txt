- Proposes q-KG, a Bayes-optimal batch extension of sequential KG with an IPA-based Monte Carlo gradient estimator.  
- Claims q-KG outperforms prior batch BO methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.  
- Builds on prior KG and parallel acquisition work but does not clearly distinguish its contribution from related methods ([2], [15], [25]).  
- Strength: provides a theoretically grounded Bayes-optimal acquisition for parallel settings.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on synthetic benchmarks and practical ML tasks (MNIST logistic regression, CIFAR10 CNN).  
- Strength: demonstrates practical advantage in noisy problems and releases code for reproducibility.  
- Weakness: novelty is unclear—insufficient theoretical/empirical comparison to prior parallel KG/EI approaches, particularly [25].  
- Weakness: Section 5.2 (IPA gradient estimation) is too brief and lacks implementation details and complexity analysis.  
- Weakness: no empirical or theoretical comparison of the proposed gradient estimator’s computational efficiency to alternatives.  
- Weakness: comparison to Expected Improvement is problematic unless a noise-aware EI variant was used; authors must clarify.  
- Minor issue: the acronym IPA is redefined late in the paper and should be introduced earlier.  
- Additional concern: the claim that q-KG reduces to parallel EI in noise-free/discrete cases is not well justified.  
- Recommendation: promising but borderline—requires clearer novelty evidence, expanded technical detail on the gradient estimator, and corrected EI comparisons before strong acceptance.
