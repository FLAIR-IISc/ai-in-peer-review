- Proposes a batch Bayesian Optimization algorithm (q-KG) that extends Knowledge Gradient to select optimal batches via decision-theoretic analysis.
- Introduces a Monte Carlo gradient estimator for the q-KG acquisition function, implemented using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).
- Shows promising empirical performance, especially in noisy evaluation settings and for hyperparameter tuning tasks.
- Novelty over prior batch KG work is not convincingly established; comparisons to related work (e.g., [1], [2]) are lacking.
- The paper should more clearly state that the primary technical contribution may be the IPA-based gradient estimator.
- Section 5.2 (gradient estimation) lacks sufficient detail on practical implementation, computational complexity, and specific challenges overcome.
- Comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) is flawed or unclear because EI’s noiseless assumption isn’t addressed; need to state whether a noise-aware EI was used.
- Minor editorial issue: the abbreviation “IPA” is redefined later in the paper and should be consistent.
- Arguments for acceptance: consistent empirical gains in noisy scenarios, potentially efficient IPA-based gradient estimation, and relevance to batch BO.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear differentiation from prior batch KG, insufficient technical detail on the gradient estimator, and an unfair EI comparison.
- Recommendation: reject in current form but encourage resubmission after clarifying contributions, expanding technical details, and improving experimental rigor.
