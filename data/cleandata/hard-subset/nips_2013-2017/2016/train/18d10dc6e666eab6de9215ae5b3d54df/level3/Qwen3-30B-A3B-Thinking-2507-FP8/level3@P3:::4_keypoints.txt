- Introduces q-KG, a novel acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization that selects q points jointly to leverage parallel evaluations.  
- Uses a decision-theoretic formulation yielding a Bayes-optimal batch when one batch remains.  
- Develops an efficient IPA-based method to evaluate and optimize the otherwise intractable acquisition function.  
- Improves over prior batch BO methods by jointly optimizing the entire batch rather than selecting points iteratively.  
- Clearly positions the work relative to knowledge gradient, parallel EI, and UCB prior work, highlighting distinctions.  
- Method is technically sound with clear theoretical motivation for the acquisition function.  
- Empirical results demonstrate consistent improvements over benchmarks on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, notably in noisy settings.  
- Experiments cover 2–6D problems, both noise-free and noisy evaluations, with thorough baseline comparisons and public code for reproducibility.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and accessible to readers familiar with Bayesian optimization.  
- Major limitation: reliance on Gaussian process priors may be questionable for high-dimensional inputs (e.g., neural network architectures).  
- Major limitation: lacks theoretical error bounds and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.  
- Minor issues: several notational and phrasing problems needing fixes (lines 36, 95, 104, 128, 140).  
- Overall: strengths outweigh weaknesses — high quality, clarity, significance, and moderate–high originality.  
- Recommendation: accept.
