- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that returns the one-step Bayes-optimal batch of q points.
- Derives the q-KG acquisition function via decision-theoretic analysis and develops an efficient computation strategy.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning, especially under noisy evaluations.
- Builds on prior BO and parallel BO work by measuring information gain for q-point evaluations with a new acquisition function.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with thorough background and technical details useful for implementation.
- Strength: experiments are understandable and show q-KG performs well in noisy settings.
- Weakness: some paper aspects are unclear and the reviewer is not fully convinced by the experimental evidence.
- Weakness: lacks wall-clock time measurements and comparisons to random search or sequential BO to demonstrate practical speedups.
- Weakness: unjustified choice of fixed batch size q = 4 across all experiments.
- Weakness/novelty concern: similar IPA-based parallel selection methods (e.g., parallel EI/q-EI) exist in prior work, questioning novelty.
- Evaluation: scores high on clarity and originality, lower on significance and quality due to experimental and novelty concerns.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — add comprehensive experiments (wall-clock, baselines), justify batch size choice, and clarify differences from prior work.
