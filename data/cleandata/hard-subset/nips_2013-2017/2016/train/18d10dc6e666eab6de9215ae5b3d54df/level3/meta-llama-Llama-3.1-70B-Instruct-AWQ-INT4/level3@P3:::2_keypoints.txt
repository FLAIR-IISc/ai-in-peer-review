- Proposes q-KG, a novel parallel Bayesian optimization acquisition function that computes Bayes-optimal batches.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis optimizing average-case posterior when one batch remains.  
- Provides an efficient computation and gradient via infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for gradient-based optimization.  
- Demonstrates q-KG outperforms existing parallel BO methods on synthetic and practical ML problems, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Theoretical analysis is strong and the methodology is clearly and concisely presented.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to two datasets (MNIST, CIFAR10) and two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN).  
- Assumes independent normally distributed observation noise, which may be unrealistic due to structural bias and correlated errors in hyperparameter tuning.  
- Authors should include more datasets and algorithm variety to demonstrate robustness.  
- Authors should discuss and explore more realistic, potentially correlated noise models.  
- Originality is high: novel acquisition function and new parallelization approach with clear comparisons to prior work.  
- Significance is high: potential to improve efficiency of expensive parallel evaluations, but further experiments are needed to confirm impact.
