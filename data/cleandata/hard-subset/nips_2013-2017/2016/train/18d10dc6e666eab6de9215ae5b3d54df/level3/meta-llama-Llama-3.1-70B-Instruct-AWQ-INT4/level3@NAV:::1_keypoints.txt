- Introduces the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method for batch Bayesian optimization to evaluate multiple points simultaneously in parallel environments.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis and shows it is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when one decision remains.  
- Presents an efficient computation strategy for q-KG and its gradient using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Demonstrates practicality by enabling efficient optimization of the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Provides extensive numerical experiments on synthetic and real-world problems.  
- Empirical results show q-KG consistently outperforms or matches state-of-the-art batch methods, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.  
- q-KG is a significant and novel contribution to batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Strength: novel and effective algorithmic contribution.  
- Strength: clear presentation and organization.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance, notably with noisy functions.  
- Weakness: implicit assumptions (compact domain A, continuity of f) are not explicitly stated.  
- Weakness: does not quantify speed-ups from moving from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Weakness: literature review contains imprecise claims and lacks sufficient prior-work references.  
- Weakness: unclear how hyperparameters are re-estimated or updated during optimization.  
- Recommendation: strongly recommend publication pending minor revisions to address weaknesses.  
- Qualitative scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
