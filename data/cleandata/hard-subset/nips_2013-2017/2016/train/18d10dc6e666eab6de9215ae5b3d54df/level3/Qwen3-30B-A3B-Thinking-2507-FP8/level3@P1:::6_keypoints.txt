- Proposes q-KG, a parallel extension of the Knowledge Gradient acquisition function using IPA to optimize batches of q points.  
- Claims theoretical Bayes-optimality for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when one batch remains.  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic and hyperparameter tuning tasks, especially in noisy settings.  
- Technical exposition is clear, background is thorough, experiments cover standard benchmarks and practical tasks (MNIST, CIFAR10).  
- Provides a well-maintained open-source implementation, aiding reproducibility.  
- Fails to adequately distinguish from Wang et al. (2015), which applied IPA to parallel EI—novelty claims are overstated.  
- The computational IPA approach is not shown to be novel since it was used in prior parallel EI work.  
- Lacks wall-clock time measurements, so practical speedups and computational overhead of batch selection are unknown.  
- Omits comparisons to random search and sequential BO, leaving the value of parallelization unquantified.  
- Fixes batch size q=4 across experiments without justification and provides no scaling analysis for larger q.  
- Figures are ambiguous (x-axis labeled "iterations" unclear if batches or evaluations) and methods start from different points, complicating comparisons.  
- Does not address extension to asynchronous parallel evaluations, limiting practical applicability.  
- Overall contribution is technically sound but primarily an application of existing techniques with insufficient novelty and practical validation.  
- Recommendation: reject due to insufficient novelty differentiation and missing evidence of practical impact.
