- Paper proposes q-KG, a batch extension of the knowledge gradient acquisition function for one-step Bayes-optimal batch Bayesian optimization.  
- Main technical contribution is an IPA-based gradient estimator for q-KG, enabling gradient-based optimization of batches.  
- Empirical results show q-KG often outperforms parallel EI and UCB variants, especially in noisy settings.  
- Quality: overall well-executed with extensive experiments, but comparison to EI in noisy experiments may be inappropriate if standard (noise-free) EI was used.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear, but Section 5.2 (gradient estimator) is too concise and omits key derivation steps and assumptions.  
- Missing discussion in main text on conditions permitting interchange of expectation and differentiation for the gradient estimator.  
- Minor clarity issue: "IPA" is redefined later in the paper; redundant definition should be removed.  
- Originality: insufficient positioning against prior batch/parallel KG work; novelty of q-KG formulation is unclear.  
- If novelty is limited to the optimization method, the paper should explicitly emphasize the IPA-based optimization as the main contribution.  
- Significance: batch BO is important and results look impactful, particularly for noisy real-world problems, but significance depends on resolving originality and experimental fairness.  
- Argument for acceptance: tackles a practical problem, principled Bayes-optimal formulation, and strong empirical gains on synthetic and real tasks.  
- Argument against acceptance: unclear novelty, insufficiently detailed gradient derivation in main text, and potentially unfair noisy-EI baseline.  
- Request for authors: clarify novelty relative to prior batch KG literature and highlight what is fundamentally new.  
- Request for authors: provide theoretical/empirical comparison of IPA gradient estimator versus prior optimization strategies for batch KG.  
- Request for authors: expand Section 5.2 with derivation details (interchange conditions, Cholesky gradient intuition) and specify which EI variant was used in noisy experiments.
