- Proposes a batch Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) method for parallel Bayesian optimization, claiming Bayes-optimal batch selection.  
- Derives a Monte Carlo gradient estimator using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to optimize the acquisition function.  
- Reports superior empirical performance over batch EI, batch UCB, and other methods, especially in noisy settings and on real-world tasks.  
- Strength: clear theoretical derivation of the batch KG acquisition function.  
- Strength: practical implementation for parallel BO with comprehensive experiments on synthetic and hyperparameter tuning problems.  
- Weakness: novelty claim is questionable because prior batch KG methods (e.g., [26]) exist and the paper does not clearly differentiate its contribution.  
- Weakness: Section 5.2 describing the IPA-based gradient estimator lacks sufficient mathematical detail and rigor to assess theoretical properties.  
- Weakness: Experimental comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) is problematic since EI assumes noiseless observations and the paper does not clarify use of a noise-aware EI variant.  
- Minor issue: IPA is redefined unnecessarily at line 162 and should be fixed.  
- Overall: useful incremental contribution to parallel BO but fails to convincingly establish novelty or provide adequate technical justification for its core computational method.
