- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic, one-step Bayes-optimal acquisition function for selecting a batch of q points in parallel Bayesian optimization, optimized via an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA).

- Extends sequential knowledge gradient (KG) to the parallel setting and jointly optimizes entire batches rather than using greedy heuristics.

- Main technical contribution: deriving an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator for q-KG via IPA, enabling practical gradient-based optimization of the acquisition function.

- Empirical results: thorough comparisons on synthetic functions and two hyperparameter tuning tasks show q-KG is often superior or competitive with state-of-the-art parallel BO methods, especially under noisy evaluations.

- Strength: addresses an important practical problem with a principled and novel extension of KG to batches, offering conceptual advantages over heuristic batch constructions.

- Strength: technically sound derivation and a clever, essential use of IPA to make joint batch optimization computationally feasible.

- Strength: strong empirical evaluation with multiple baselines and real-world tasks, demonstrating notable noise robustness.

- Strength: clear, well-organized writing, sufficient methodological detail, and released code to aid reproducibility.

- Weakness: lacks theoretical analysis (e.g., regret bounds or convergence rates) to formally characterize performance and sample complexity.

- Weakness: insufficient quantitative complexity analysis of optimizing q-KG — unclear scaling with batch size q, input dimension d, and discretization size M.

- Weakness: inherits Gaussian Process limitations and needs more discussion about challenges in higher-dimensional settings common in neural network tuning.
