This paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method, which is derived from a decision-theoretical perspective. The q-KG method measures the information gain of evaluating q points via a new acquisition function and chooses the batch of points to evaluate next that is optimal in the average-case with respect to the posterior when there is only one batch of points remaining. The authors also develop a computationally efficient approach to maximize q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).

The main idea of the paper is to generalize the concept of the knowledge gradient from the sequential setting to the parallel setting. The authors claim that their method is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the minimum of the predictor of the GP if only one decision is remaining. The q-KG algorithm is compared to other batch Bayesian optimization algorithms, such as parallel EI, batch UCB, and parallel UCB with pure exploration, and is shown to outperform or be competitive with these methods on several synthetic functions and in tuning practical machine learning algorithms.

The paper is well-written and well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its advantages. However, the reviewer has some concerns regarding the novelty of the paper. Batch KG is not a new concept, and the authors should clarify their contribution relative to prior batch KG work. If the main contribution of the paper is the gradient estimator, the authors should empirically or theoretically compare its efficiency/effectiveness against previous batch KG methods.

Section 5.2 is central to the paper and needs more detail and elaboration. The authors should provide more information about the IPA estimator and its properties. Additionally, the comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) is problematic because EI assumes noiseless observations. The authors should clarify whether a noise-aware EI variant was used and discuss how noise affects the results in Section 6.2.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its advantages. The q-KG algorithm is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its properties. The experiments are well-designed, and the results are clearly presented.

However, the paper could be improved by providing more detail and elaboration on the IPA estimator and its properties. Additionally, the authors should address the concerns regarding the novelty of the paper and clarify their contribution relative to prior batch KG work.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written and well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their method and its advantages. The notation is clear, and the figures are well-designed and easy to understand.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, the parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG) method. However, the reviewer has some concerns regarding the novelty of the paper, as batch KG is not a new concept.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field of Bayesian optimization. The q-KG algorithm is well-motivated, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of its properties. The experiments are well-designed, and the results are clearly presented.
