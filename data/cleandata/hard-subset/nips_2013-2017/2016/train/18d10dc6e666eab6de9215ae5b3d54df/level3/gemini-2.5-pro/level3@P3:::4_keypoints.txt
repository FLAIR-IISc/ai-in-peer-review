- Proposes q-KG, a decision-theoretic acquisition function for selecting a batch of q points in parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- q-KG generalizes the sequential knowledge gradient to jointly optimize the entire batch by maximizing one-step expected improvement of the final recommendation (minimum of posterior mean).  
- Naive optimization of q-KG is intractable; main technical contribution is an efficient stochastic gradient optimization using an unbiased IPA-based gradient estimator.  
- The method contrasts with prior parallel BO approaches that use greedy or heuristic batch construction.  
- Strength: Addresses an important, practical problem for accelerating BO in distributed/hyperparameter tuning settings.  
- Strength: Principled Bayesian decision-theoretic motivation that naturally handles noisy evaluations.  
- Strength: Significant technical contribution in deriving a practical IPA-based stochastic gradient and a sensible discretization scheme for continuous domains.  
- Strength: Convincing empirical evaluation on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tasks, showing strong performance, especially with noise.  
- Strength: Well-written and well-organized presentation with clear motivation and connections to prior work.  
- Weakness: Relies on Gaussian Process priors, so scalability and reliability in high-dimensional problems (d >> 8) is questionable and should be discussed.  
- Weakness: Lacks formal theoretical analysis (e.g., regret bounds) and a quantitative complexity analysis in terms of d, q, and discretization size M.  
- Minor issues: Several notation and clarity suggestions (grammar at l.36; define A and µ(n) more clearly; contrast IPA with gradient methods for parallel EI; avoid overloading A vs An).  
- Overall assessment: High-quality, significant contribution with practical and empirical strengths; recommended for acceptance despite theoretical and scalability limitations.
