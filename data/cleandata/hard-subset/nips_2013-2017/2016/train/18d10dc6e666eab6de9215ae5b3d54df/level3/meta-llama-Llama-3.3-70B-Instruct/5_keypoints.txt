- Proposes a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm, q-KG, derived from a decision-theoretical perspective.  
- Designed for parallel evaluation, optimizing the expected incremental value of measurements when multiple points are evaluated simultaneously.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and real ML tasks show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; introduction and related work provide good background and motivation.  
- Technical contributions include a new acquisition function (q-KG) and an efficient gradient computation strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA).  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness in both noise-free and noisy problems.  
- The paper must clarify its contribution relative to existing batch Knowledge Gradient (KG) methods and explain why it is more effective.  
- Comparison with Expected Improvement (EI) in noisy settings may be unfair because EI assumes noiseless observations; a more appropriate or thorough comparison is needed.  
- Section 5.2 requires more elaboration on how the q-KG gradient is estimated via IPA and how that estimate is used in optimization.  
- The abbreviation “IPA” is redefined later in the paper (line 162), which may cause confusion and should be fixed.  
- Paper is technically sound, claims are supported by theory and experiments, and the work is original and significant.  
- With minor revisions addressing the points above, the paper is a strong candidate for acceptance.
