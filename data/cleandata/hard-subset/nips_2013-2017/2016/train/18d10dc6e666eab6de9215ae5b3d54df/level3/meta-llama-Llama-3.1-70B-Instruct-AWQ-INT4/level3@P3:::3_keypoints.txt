- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm extending knowledge gradient to the parallel setting.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis, optimal in the average-case when only one batch remains.  
- Shows Bayes-optimality for minimizing the GP predictor minimum when a single decision is left.  
- Develops an efficient computation strategy using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to estimate q-KG gradients.  
- IPA-based gradient estimation enables standard gradient-based maximization of the acquisition function.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real problems (including hyperparameter tuning) show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, practically useful, and technically sound.  
- Novelty concern: mainly an extension of existing KG framework to batch setting and uses standard IPA, so contributions may be incremental.  
- Technical criticism: equation (3.1) notation is unclear—μ and K are defined for vectors, not sequences x^(1:n); needs clarification.  
- Recommendation: accept, but ask authors to clarify notation in (3.1) and better articulate the novelty/contributions.
