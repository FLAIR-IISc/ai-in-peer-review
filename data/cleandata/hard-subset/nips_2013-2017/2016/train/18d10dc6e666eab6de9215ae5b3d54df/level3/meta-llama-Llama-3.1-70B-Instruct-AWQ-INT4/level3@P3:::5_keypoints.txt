- Proposes q-KG, a batch BO acquisition from a decision-theoretic analysis optimizing the average-case for one remaining batch.  
- Contributions: (1) q-KG acquisition function, (2) IPA-based efficient gradient estimator, (3) empirical improvement over parallel EI/UCB variants, especially with noisy evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and presents thorough experiments with impressive results on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning.  
- Concern: novelty of the batch KG method is unclear — may be an extension of prior batch KG work; authors should clarify differences.  
- The gradient estimator might be the main novel contribution; authors should compare its efficiency/effectiveness against previous batch KG estimators (empirically or theoretically).  
- Section 5.2 is central and needs more detail and elaboration.  
- EI comparison unclear: state whether a noise-aware EI variant was used and discuss how noise affects results (Section 6.2).  
- Minor issue: "IPA" is redefined at line 162 and should be fixed.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound, claims supported by theory and experiments, and results appear reproducible for expert readers.  
- Originality: good — proposes a new batch BO method and a new gradient estimator with appropriate related-work citations.  
- Significance: high — addresses a difficult problem and demonstrably advances the state of the art.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel method, efficient gradient estimator, thorough/impressive experiments.  
- Cons for acceptance: questionable novelty relative to prior batch KG, unclear EI/noise handling, and minor editorial issues.
