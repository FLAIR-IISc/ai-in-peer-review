- Relevance/impact: Addresses parallel Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning and shows strong performance in noisy settings.  
- Technical clarity: Well-written with clear derivations, implementation details, and open-source code for reproducibility.  
- Experimental results: Competitive or superior performance on synthetic benchmarks and real ML tasks (e.g., CIFAR-10, MNIST), especially under noise.  
- Efficient gradient estimation: Uses IPA to efficiently estimate gradients of the q-KG acquisition function.  

- Originality: Relies on known techniques (knowledge gradient, IPA) and lacks clear advancement over prior work (e.g., Wang et al. 2015).  
- Experimental limitations: No wall-clock time comparisons, unjustified batch size choice (q=4), and no scalability analysis to larger batches (q=10,25).  
- Asynchronous execution: Does not address extension to asynchronous settings, limiting applicability in distributed systems.  
- Plot clarity: “Iterations” is undefined and it’s unclear if methods share the same initial design, reducing interpretability.  
- Minor issues: Grammar mistakes, missing terms in equations, and incorrect IPA references need correction.  

- Pro: Tackles a practical, impactful problem in parallel BO.  
- Pro: Demonstrates strong performance in noisy scenarios.  
- Pro: Provides a clear, reproducible implementation.  
- Con: Limited novelty due to reliance on existing methods.  
- Con: Lacks wall-clock and scalability analyses.  
- Con: Unclear applicability to asynchronous execution.  

- Recommendation: Accept with major revisions to strengthen novelty claims, add wall-clock/scalability experiments, and discuss asynchronous extensions.
