- Introduces q-KG: a parallel Knowledge Gradient acquisition function for batch BO with an IPA-based unbiased gradient estimator and stochastic gradient ascent, evaluated on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Significance: addresses practical need to leverage parallel computing for expensive black-box optimization.  
- Originality/Quality: principled, decision-theoretic batch acquisition (one-step Bayes-optimal) that values batches collectively rather than using heuristics.  
- Technical contribution: derives an efficient, unbiased gradient estimator via Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis enabling practical gradient-based optimization of q-KG.  
- Empirical validation: thorough comparisons to strong baselines show q-KG is often superior, especially under noisy evaluations, and performs well on real ML tuning tasks.  
- Related work: literature review is imprecise and should credit Ginsbourger et al. (2010) for parallel EI ideas and cite Chevalier & Ginsbourger (2013) and Marmin et al. (2015) on CL-mix and q-EI gradient methods.  
- Assumptions: paper omits explicit assumptions on domain A (e.g., compactness) and objective f (e.g., continuity) needed for GP models and guarantees.  
- Smoothness of g: needs justification for exchanging expectation and derivative since g involves a min/argmin that can introduce discontinuities.  
- Cholesky differentiability: must state and discuss the assumption of continuous positive definiteness required to differentiate the Cholesky factor.  
- Definition clarity: clarify that g represents improvement in a minimization problem to avoid reader confusion with maximization-oriented acquisition conventions.  
- Hyperparameter updates: Algorithm 1 is vague about how often GP hyperparameters are retrained (every iteration/batch?), which affects cost and implementation.  
- Initial design: using LHS is reasonable but should be justified and alternatives briefly discussed.  
- Performance metrics: paper reports fewer iterations but lacks per-iteration computational cost or wall-clock time comparisons to assess practical trade-offs.  
- Minor correction: kernel stated as "ARD Matérn 2/5" likely a typo and should be "ARD Matérn 5/2".  
- Reproducibility: discuss sensitivity to GP implementation (e.g., results with GPy or GPflow) to aid verification.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution with addressable weaknesses; strongly recommend acceptance.
