- Introduces a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm called parallel knowledge gradient (q-KG).
- Derives q-KG using a decision-theoretic, average-case analysis for the one-batch-remaining setting.
- Presents an efficient strategy for computing the Bayes-optimal batch of points.
- Demonstrates q-KG finds global optima significantly faster than prior batch BO methods on synthetic tests and hyperparameter tuning.
- Shows especially strong performance in the presence of noisy function evaluations.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with a good explanation of assumptions.
- Provides a thorough literature review and compares against several state-of-the-art algorithms.
- Experimental results are impressive and well-presented, offering clear algorithm comparisons.
- Does not provide a detailed computational complexity analysis despite claiming computational efficiency.
- Does not study empirical speed-ups gained by moving from sequential to batch-sequential Bayesian optimization.
- Literature review contains some imprecise claims and omissions.
- Originality: significant contribution by extending the knowledge gradient idea to the batch-sequential setting.
- Significance: method has strong potential impact for ML and optimization, especially for noisy real-world problems.
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend for publication.
- Rating: 9/10; Confidence: 8/10.
