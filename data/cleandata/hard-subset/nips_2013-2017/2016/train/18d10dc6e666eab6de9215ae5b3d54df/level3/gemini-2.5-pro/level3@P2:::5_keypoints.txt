- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization method that selects q points to be one-step Bayes-optimal for improving the posterior GP mean minimum.  
- Main technical contribution: an unbiased stochastic gradient estimator for the q-KG acquisition using Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to enable gradient-based batch optimization.  
- Presents thorough experiments on synthetic and real hyperparameter tuning problems in both noise-free and noisy settings.  
- Empirical results show q-KG is competitive with or outperforms parallel EI and parallel UCB, with particularly strong gains under observation noise.  
- Strength: technically solid derivation, natural extension of sequential Knowledge Gradient to the batch setting.  
- Strength: gradient-based optimization is a sophisticated solution to the high-dimensional batch selection problem.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental methodology including practical ML tasks (logistic regression, CNNs).  
- Weakness: Section 5.2 (gradient estimator) is too condensed and omits key derivation details needed to assess correctness and complexity.  
- Weakness/novelty concern: prior work has considered batch KG in other settings; the paper should better situate its novelty and compare against previous batch KG optimization methods.  
- Experimental clarification needed: comparisons in noisy settings should specify which EI variant was used; comparing to a noise-aware EI is necessary to validate the advantage.  
- Minor issue: redundant redefinition of the abbreviation “IPA” in the manuscript.
