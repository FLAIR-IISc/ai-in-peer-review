- Proposes q-KG, a novel acquisition function for batch Bayesian optimization with an efficient computation method.  
- Core idea: select q points in parallel to maximize information gain, useful for expensive evaluations like hyperparameter tuning.  
- Empirical results show q-KG outperforms existing batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains concepts and technical details.  
- Extends the Knowledge Gradient framework to the parallel setting, differentiating from greedy parallel EI and batch UCB approaches.  
- q-KG jointly optimizes batches instead of selecting points sequentially, a conceptually strong approach.  
- Connections to existing work are well-established and appropriately cited.  
- Novelty: represents a significant and theoretically grounded advance in batch BO.  
- Soundness: rigorous, decision-theoretic derivation of q-KG.  
- Efficiency: uses Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to make q-KG computationally practical.  
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments on synthetic and real problems confirm effectiveness.  
- Clarity: logical structure and clear exposition facilitate understanding and implementation.  
- Limitation: reliance on a Gaussian process prior may hinder applicability in high-dimensional settings; no alternatives explored.  
- Theoretical gaps: lacks formal error bounds and quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.  
- Minor technical issues: several notation/clarity and grammatical errors (lines 36, 95, 104, 128, 140) that should be fixed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: sound method, strong empirical results, clear writing, and available open-source code.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theoretical guarantees, limited discussion of GP prior limitations, and minor technical flaws.  
- Overall: strong contribution; recommend acceptance if minor technical issues are addressed and discussion of scalability/alternative priors and theoretical gaps is added.
