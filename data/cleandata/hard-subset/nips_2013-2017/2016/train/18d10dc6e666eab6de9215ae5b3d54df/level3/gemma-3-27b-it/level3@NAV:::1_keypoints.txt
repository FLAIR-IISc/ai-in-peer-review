- Introduces q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm extending Knowledge Gradient to parallel evaluations.  
- Presents an IPA-based method to efficiently compute gradients for optimizing the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical results on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning show competitive performance, often outperforming state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Correctly situates q-KG among parallel acquisition functions like parallel EI and UCB.  
- Literature review contains inaccuracies: misattributes integration of EI w.r.t. posterior and Constant Liar to prior work rather than Ginsbourger et al. (2016).  
- Omits relevant prior work, notably Chevalier et al. (2015) on CL-mix and Marmin et al. (2016) on differentiating EI for batch design.  
- Strength: theoretical soundness, clear motivation, and empirically promising results; IPA gradient computation is a notable contribution.  
- Weakness: no analysis quantifying speed-ups from sequential to batch-sequential optimization.  
- Weakness: key assumptions (compactness of domain A, continuity of f) are not explicitly stated.  
- Weakness: unjustified restriction to a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS) for initial design A.  
- Weakness: Algorithm 1 lacks clarity on GP hyperparameter re-estimation frequency and method.  
- Weakness: unclear definition and smoothing properties of 'g' in Sec. 5.2 (is it max or min?), raising differentiability concerns.  
- Weakness: potential theoretical issue about differentiability of the Cholesky factor in derivations.  
- Minor error: Matérn parameter reported as 2/5; should be 5/2.  
- Reproducibility concern: GP fits in Figure 1 should be verified with different software.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance, contingent on addressing clarity, assumptions, and literature-review issues.
