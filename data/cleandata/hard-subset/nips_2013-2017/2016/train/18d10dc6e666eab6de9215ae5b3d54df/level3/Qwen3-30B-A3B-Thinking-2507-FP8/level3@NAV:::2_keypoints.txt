- Proposes Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) to extend sequential KG to batch Bayesian optimization with a decision-theoretic acquisition function.  
- Provides a theoretically sound, principled approach for selecting Bayes-optimal evaluation batches versus greedy parallel methods.  
- Builds on prior BO work (sequential KG, parallel EI) and positions q-KG as a more principled batch selection strategy.  
- Derivation of the q-KG acquisition function is elegant and the theoretical analysis is rigorous.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis to efficiently optimize the acquisition function, with solid computational methodology.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex concepts.  
- Empirical evaluation is limited and undermines claimed advantages due to choice of suboptimal optimization criterion (test set error instead of AUC/log loss for HPO).  
- Benchmarks are narrowly focused (MNIST, CIFAR10) and only test two algorithms (logistic regression, CNN), lacking broader HPO benchmarks like HPOlib.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed observation noise, which is unrealistic for real-world HPO with correlated cross-validation errors and structural biases.  
- Conclusion: strong theoretical contribution but insufficient empirical validation; recommends expanding benchmarks, adopting appropriate metrics, and addressing noise-model limitations.
