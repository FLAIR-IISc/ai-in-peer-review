- Proposes q-KG: a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm for selecting multiple parallel evaluation points.  
- Generalizes the knowledge gradient framework to the batch setting.  
- Derives q-KG via decision-theoretic analysis and shows Bayes-optimality when only one decision remains.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and Monte Carlo sampling for efficient q-KG acquisition optimization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on synthetic and real-world problems, often outperforming benchmarks.  
- Provides clear, concise background on Bayesian optimization, Gaussian processes, and knowledge gradient.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and practically useful.  
- Novelty is limited: mainly an extension of KG to batches and uses a standard IPA-based gradient estimator.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of algorithmic limitations and directions for future work.  
- Insufficient insight on hyperparameter choices and sensitivity analysis.  
- Significance: potentially useful for practitioners in hyperparameter tuning, experimental design, and expensive-function optimization.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10, Clarity 9/10; recommendation: accept with suggested improvements.
