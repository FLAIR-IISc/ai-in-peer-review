- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian Optimization method extending Knowledge Gradient to select a batch maximizing expected incremental value.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to compute gradients efficiently for maximizing the q-KG acquisition function.  
- Empirical results show q-KG consistently outperforms existing batch BO methods, especially under noisy evaluations.  
- Builds on prior BO work (EI, UCB) and parallelization strategies, positioning q-KG as Bayes-optimal for a single batch versus greedy iterative approaches.  
- Strong theoretical foundation deriving q-KG from a decision-theoretic perspective.  
- Practical viability due to efficient gradient computation and availability of code.  
- Thorough empirical validation on synthetic benchmarks and real-world hyperparameter tuning tasks.  
- Strength: particularly strong performance in noisy evaluation scenarios.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit quantification of time/speedups gained from parallel evaluations.  
- Related work contains imprecisions and misses citations (e.g., Ginsbourger et al. (2010) and Chevalier et al. (2016) / CL-mix and q‑EI natural-gradient work).  
- Clarification needed on modeling assumptions: whether domain A is compact and f is continuous.  
- Clarification needed on choice/rationale for Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS) for initial design.  
- Algorithmic detail missing on frequency and mechanism for re-training GP hyperparameters in Algorithm 1.  
- Ambiguity in Section 5.2 regarding definition and minimization role of g.  
- Technical concerns: smoothness of g in Section 5.2 requires stronger justification.  
- Technical concerns: differentiability of the Cholesky factor used in derivations needs careful treatment.  
- Minor correction: Matérn parameter listed as 2/5 should be 5/2.  
- Suggestion to strengthen reproducibility by fitting GPs in Figure 1 using different software implementations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel theoretically sound method, empirical improvements, efficient computation, code availability, and high practical impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: related work imprecisions, missing parallel speed analysis, clarifying questions, and technical smoothness/differentiability concerns.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper with recommendation to accept subject to minor revisions addressing clarifications and related-work fixes.
