- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization algorithm extending knowledge gradient to select parallel evaluation points.  
- Derives a decision-theoretic acquisition function that is Bayes-optimal for minimizing the GP predictor's expected minimum when one batch remains.  
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling combined with infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to efficiently estimate gradients for acquisition optimization.  
- Builds on prior BO work (sequential KG, parallel EI, GP-BUCB, GP-UCB-PE) and distinguishes itself from nested batch KG.  
- Technically sound derivation with careful analysis of computational challenges.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive (Branin, Rosenbrock, Ackley, Hartmann; logistic regression on MNIST; CNN on CIFAR10).  
- Results show q-KG outperforms or competes with state-of-the-art batch BO methods, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; mathematical derivations and algorithm description are accessible.  
- Provides sufficient implementation details and hyperparameter choices for reproducibility; code is publicly available.  
- Originality is moderate: principled batch KG derivation is valuable but primarily an extension of existing KG concepts.  
- IPA application is a useful practical contribution but uses a standard gradient estimation technique.  
- Notational issue in Equation (3.1): µ and K defined for vectors rather than sequences, which may confuse readers.  
- Theoretical guarantees on the quality of the IPA approximation are limited and could be strengthened.  
- Comparison with some recent batch BO methods (e.g., Shah & Ghahramani 2015) is insufficient.  
- Overall recommendation: accept—method is practically relevant, empirically strong, and technically solid, with minor issues addressable in revision.
