- Proposes q-KG, a novel extension of the sequential knowledge gradient to select batches of q points for parallel Bayesian optimization.  
- q-KG is derived decision-theoretically and measures expected improvement in the GP predictor minimum, making it Bayes-optimal when one batch remains.  
- Introduces an IPA-based method to compute gradients of q-KG, enabling efficient optimization for larger batch sizes.  
- Empirical results show q-KG consistently outperforms parallel EI, GP-BUCB, and GP-UCB-PE on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning.  
- Demonstrates particularly strong performance in noisy evaluation settings, which is important for real-world applications.  
- Theoretical derivation is sound and grounded in first principles.  
- Experiments are comprehensive (four synthetic functions; logistic regression on MNIST; CNN tuning on CIFAR10) and code is open-sourced.  
- Literature review contains inaccuracies and omissions: fails to cite Ginsbourger et al. (2010) on Constant Liar/parallel EI and omits Chevalier et al. (2013) and Marmin et al. (2015).  
- Paper does not state key assumptions explicitly (e.g., compact domain A and continuity of f).  
- Choice of Latin Hypercube Sampling for initial design is not justified.  
- Algorithm 1 is unclear about GP hyperparameter updates (frequency and details of MLE retraining not specified).  
- Technical errors/confusions: Section 5.2 uses max where g should use min, and smoothness/differentiability of g and the Cholesky factor are not properly justified.  
- Typo/error in reporting Matérn parameter (listed as 2/5 in abstract; should be 5/2).  
- Paper fails to quantify wall-clock speed-up from parallel evaluations, leaving practical runtime benefits unclear.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with solid theory and empirical support, but needs minor revisions to fix literature citations, clarify technical assumptions/derivations, and report parallelization speed-up.
