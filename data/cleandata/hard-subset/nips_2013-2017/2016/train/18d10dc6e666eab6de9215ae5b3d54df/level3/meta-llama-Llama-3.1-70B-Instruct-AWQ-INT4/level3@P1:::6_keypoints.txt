- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal parallel batches using a decision-theoretic derivation.  
- Uses infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient computation of the Bayes-optimal batch.  
- Empirical results on synthetic and real tasks (incl. hyperparameter tuning) show q-KG outperforms or matches state-of-the-art, especially with noisy evaluations.  

Strengths:
- Novel batch BO algorithm tailored for parallel evaluations.  
- Solid theoretical foundation derived from decision theory.  
- IPA-based approach enables efficient batch computation.  
- Thorough empirical evaluation across test functions and real problems.  

Weaknesses:
- Lacks clear comparison to similar IPA-based methods like parallel EI (q-EI).  
- Insufficient analysis of scalability to large batch sizes (tens/hundreds).  
- No wall-clock time measurements to assess practical speedups.  
- Experimental design choices (e.g., q = 4) are not clearly justified.  

Clarity and organization:
- Paper is well-written, background and technical details are clear, experiments are well-organized.  

Originality and significance:
- Presents a novel method with potential high relevance for applications such as ML hyperparameter optimization.  

Minor comments:
- Possible missing z^(1:n) in Eq.(4.1).  
- Grammar typo: "achieve" → "achieves".  
- Fix citation phrasing.  
- Do not credit Wang et al. (2015) with introducing IPA.  

Major questions:
- Have you compared to Wang et al. (2015)/parallel EI (q-EI), and can you state differences?  
- How does the method scale to larger q and what benefits are expected with more parallel runs?  
- Can the synchronous method be extended to asynchronous parallel evaluations?  
- What is the wall-clock performance vs random search or sequential BO, and what is the per-batch computation overhead?  
- Clarify the x-axis “iterations” in plots: batches or function evaluations, inclusion of initial design, and why starting values differ across methods.
