- Proposes q-KG, a batch Bayesian optimization algorithm extending knowledge gradient to parallel evaluations by selecting batches that maximize expected incremental value.  
- Uses Monte Carlo sampling and infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) for efficient gradient estimation of the acquisition function.  
- Demonstrates q-KG outperforms or matches existing batch BO methods on synthetic benchmarks and real hyperparameter tuning, especially in noisy settings.  
- Paper is well-written with clear methodological explanations and thorough connections to prior BO and parallel acquisition literature.  
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, results are clearly presented, and code is made available for reproducibility.  
- Practical utility is high: q-KG can speed up hyperparameter optimization in parallel computing environments.  
- IPA application is standard but effectively enables scalable batch acquisition optimization within the q-KG framework.  
- Main novelty is incremental: extending KG to batches and applying established IPA/Monte Carlo techniques rather than introducing new theory.  
- Technical ambiguity in equation (3.1): unclear notation for µ(n) and K(n) when applied to a sequence x(1:n), needs clarification (vectorized vs. looped computation).  
- Strengths for acceptance: practical relevance, sound decision-theoretic basis, strong empirical validation, code availability, and clarity.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical novelty and the noted technical ambiguity in equation (3.1).  
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify equation (3.1).
