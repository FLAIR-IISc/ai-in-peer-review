- Introduces Parallel Knowledge Gradient (q-KG), a Bayes-optimal batch BO acquisition derived from decision-theoretic analysis.
- Proposes an efficient gradient estimation method using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) to address computational complexity.
- Builds on sequential KG and situates work within parallel BO literature (parallel EI, batch UCB, etc.).
- Strong theoretical foundation: derives a Bayes-optimal batch policy for a single remaining batch.
- Computational efficiency: IPA-based gradient estimator makes maximizing q-KG practical.
- Clear, well-structured, and mathematically rigorous presentation with consistent notation.
- Novelty: jointly optimizes entire batches instead of iteratively adding points.
- Potential significance: could substantially improve parallel BO efficiency in costly evaluation settings (e.g., HPO).
- Limiting assumption: assumes independent Gaussian noise, which may not hold in real HPO with biased or correlated errors.
- Weak empirical evaluation: choice of test set error as optimization criterion is suboptimal versus metrics like AUC or log loss.
- Limited benchmarks: experiments only on MNIST and CIFAR10 with logistic regression and one CNN, reducing generalizability.
- Lack of dataset and algorithm variety makes robustness assessment difficult.
- Missing comparisons to some recent parallel BO methods, weakening empirical claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theory, practical IPA technique, clear exposition, and promising initial results.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive noise assumptions, insufficient and flawed empirical validation, and limited comparisons.
- Overall recommendation: reject but encourage revision with broader benchmarks, better metrics, sensitivity to noise assumptions, and comparisons to recent methods.
