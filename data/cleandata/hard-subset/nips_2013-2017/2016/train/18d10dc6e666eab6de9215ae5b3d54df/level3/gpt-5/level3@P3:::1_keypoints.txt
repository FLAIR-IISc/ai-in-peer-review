- Introduces parallel (batch) Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) for Bayesian optimization, derived from a one-step Bayes‑optimal decision rule for choosing a final batch.
- Proposes an efficient stochastic gradient estimator for q-KG using infinitesimal perturbation analysis (IPA) and a reparameterization with standard Normal Zq and Cholesky factors.
- Shows q-KG reduces to q‑EI in the noise‑free case when recommendations are restricted to previously sampled points.
- Uses discretization of continuous domain A via M candidate points sampled from a posterior “global optima” scheme (augmented with past/candidate points).
- Empirical results on synthetic benchmarks and hyperparameter tuning (MNIST, CIFAR‑10) show q‑KG is competitive or superior to parallel EI and batch‑UCB, with strong gains in noisy settings.
- Strength: principled, Bayes‑optimal criterion for a single remaining batch.
- Strength: practical computational contribution—scalable stochastic gradient estimator that handles meaningful q and d.
- Strength: clear theoretical positioning via reduction to known special case (q‑EI).
- Strength: strong empirical performance under noise and public implementation.
- Strength: high practical/societal impact by enabling parallelized BO on modern hardware.
- Weakness: lacks wall‑clock speedup / time‑to‑target analysis for moving from sequential to batch workflows.
- Weakness: related‑work section omits or imprecisely attributes prior ideas (e.g., constant liar, CL‑mix, differentiable multipoint EI, PPES variants, natural‑gradient q‑EI work).
- Weakness: some technical/smoothness assumptions are under‑specified or not stated explicitly.
- Weakness: limited sensitivity analysis on discretization (choice of M), sampling strategy, and optimization restarts.
- Technical Q: State assumptions on A and f (compactness/continuity) needed to guarantee existence of posterior mean minimizers.
- Technical Q: Why require Latin Hypercube Sampling for initial design; is LHS essential or just practical?
- Technical Q: Provide details on GP hyperparameter retraining frequency, optimizer, priors/regularization, noise treatment, and whether hyperparameters are marginalized vs MLE.
- Technical Q: Clarify sign/optimization conventions in the definition of g and the q‑KG expectation to avoid min/max confusion.
- Technical Q: Justify IPA unbiasedness—state conditions (e.g., unique minimizers a.s., differentiability of µ and K), handling of ties, and cite supporting proofs.
- Technical Q: Discuss differentiability of Cholesky factor D(n)(·), cite literature (e.g., Smith 1995), and explain handling of numerical rank issues.
- Technical Q: Provide computational complexity in terms of q, d, M, MC samples, gradient steps/restarts and empirical runtimes vs q (e.g., q=2,4,8,16).
- Technical Q: Report sensitivity of performance to M and candidate sampling strategy, and whether samples are reused across iterations.
- Technical Q: Assess robustness to mis‑specified or heteroscedastic noise and the interaction with MLE noise re‑estimation.
- Technical Q: Recommend cross‑checking GP fits with other libraries (GPML/GPy/GPyTorch) for reproducibility.
- Minor issues: Matérn parameter typo (should be 5/2), Algorithm 1 typo “Main Stange” → “Main Stage”, and define symbols (Zq, D(n), σ̃n) at first use.
- Overall quality: derivation is sound, IPA estimator is a solid contribution, experiments thorough but would benefit from explicit assumptions and complexity analysis.
- Clarity: generally well written; needs stronger assumptions, differentiability discussion, and more details on hyperparameter learning and computational costs.
- Originality: novel extension of KG to batch setting with efficient gradient estimator, distinct from q‑EI/PPES/BUCB lines.
- Significance: high practical impact for parallel BO on modern compute.
- Pros for acceptance: principled Bayes‑optimal criterion, efficient/implementable IPA optimization, strong empirical gains (esp. with noise), public code.
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete related work and comparisons, limited runtime/scaling analysis, and some theoretical/smoothness details missing.
- Recommendation: Strong accept, pending addressing related‑work gaps, clarifying assumptions/differentiability, and adding runtime/speedup analyses.
