- Proposes q-KG, a novel batch Bayesian optimization algorithm generalizing the knowledge gradient to parallel settings.  
- Derives a batch-version of the knowledge gradient and introduces a Monte Carlo gradient estimator to optimize batches.  
- Shows q-KG finds global optima faster than prior batch BO methods on synthetic functions and ML hyperparameter tuning.  
- Demonstrates especially strong performance in noisy evaluation settings.  
- Paper is well-written with clear background on Gaussian processes and notation.  
- Provides a detailed description of the q-KG algorithm and its computational implementation.  
- Empirical evaluation is extensive and shows q-KG outperforms or is competitive with state-of-the-art benchmarks.  
- Paper is technically sound; claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Authors provide sufficient detail for expert readers to reproduce results.  
- Concern: novelty is unclear because batch knowledge gradient policy has prior work; authors must clarify their contribution relative to prior batch KG.  
- Concern: comparison to Expected Improvement (EI) is problematic since EI assumes noiseless observations; authors should state whether a noise-aware EI was used and discuss noise effects.  
- Some sections (notably Section 5.2) require more detail and elaboration.  
- Overall originality is good but somewhat diminished by existing batch KG literature.  
- Significance is high given practical hyperparameter tuning improvements and noisy-case benefits.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address novelty clarification, EI comparison/noise, and under-detailed sections.
