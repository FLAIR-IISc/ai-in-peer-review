- Proposed method: parallel Bayesian optimization using q-KG acquisition with IPA for efficient gradient estimation.  
- Claims: method yields Bayes-optimal parallel batches and shows competitive/noisy-performance gains on synthetic functions and hyperparameter tuning for CNNs/logistic regression.  
- Strength: clear technical description of q-KG formulation and IPA implementation.  
- Strength: experiments well-organized and relevant to practical multi-core optimization.  
- Strength: solid background on Gaussian processes and Bayesian optimization; experimental setup understandable.  
- Concern: novelty is questionable because similar IPA-based parallel selection (e.g., Wang et al. 2015) exists and the paper fails to clearly differentiate itself.  
- Major omission: no wall-clock time comparisons to random search or sequential BO, so practical speedups are unassessed.  
- Experimental choice: fixed batch size q=4 is used without justification, despite potential for larger q.  
- Evidence depth: experiments do not convincingly explain why q-KG outperforms existing methods in noisy settings beyond reported results.  
- Unanswered questions: scalability to larger q is not addressed.  
- Unanswered questions: actual wall-clock performance versus sequential BO is not provided.  
- Unclear reporting: relationship between the "iterations" axis in plots and function evaluations is not explained.  
- Attribution error: IPA is incorrectly attributed to Wang et al. (2015), which is misleading.  
- Overall: technical contributions are incremental and lack comparative runtime and novelty justification, limiting acceptance likelihood.  
- Minor issues: grammar errors and citation inaccuracies undermine paper quality.
