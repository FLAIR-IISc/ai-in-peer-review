- Proposes a parallel Bayesian optimization algorithm that computes Bayes-optimal batches and reportedly outperforms existing methods.  
- Paper is well written, easy to follow, and presents an elegant solution to parallelizing BO.  
- Determining Bayes-optimal batches is promising and a valuable theoretical contribution.  
- Assumes independent normally distributed observation noise; this assumption can be unrealistic in HPO due to structural bias and correlated errors from empirical estimates (e.g., CV).  
- Theoretical analysis is strong.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: using test set error as the optimization criterion is problematic—threshold-based metrics are suboptimal; prefer AUC or log loss.  
- Experimental benchmarks are limited to MNIST and CIFAR10; should include broader HPO benchmarks such as HPOlib.  
- Only two datasets and two learning algorithms (logistic regression and CNN) were used; need more datasets and algorithm variety since optimizers vary by task.  
- Overall positive on theory and approach, but the limited benchmark undermines empirical convincingness.
