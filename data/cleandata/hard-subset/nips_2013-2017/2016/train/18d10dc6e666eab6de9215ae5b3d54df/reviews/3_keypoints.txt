- Proposes a stochastic gradient algorithm with Bayesian modeling to optimize a multivariate objective over a finite domain.  
- Algorithm selects multiple sampling points per iteration to allow parallel objective evaluations.  
- Adopts and generalizes the Knowledge Gradient (KG) framework to batch selection (q-KG).  
- Batch KG selection is computationally intractable, so authors use Monte Carlo plus Infinitesimal Perturbation Analysis (IPA) to estimate gradients.  
- Empirical results show the method outperforms or competes with confidence-bound–based approaches on several test problems.  
- Paper is well written and appears practically useful.  
- Concern that novelty is limited—mainly an extension of KG to batch settings and a standard IPA-based gradient estimator.  
- Specific technical comment: equation (3.1) is unclear because mu and K are defined for vectors, not sequences x^(1:n).
