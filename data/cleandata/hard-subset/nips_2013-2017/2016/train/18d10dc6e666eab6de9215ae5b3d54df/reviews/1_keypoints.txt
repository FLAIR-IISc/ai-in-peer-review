- Introduces a batch-sequential Knowledge Gradient (q-KG) criterion for Bayesian optimization.  
- Defines and details computation of q-KG following the standard KG approach.  
- Numerical experiments show q-KG often outperforms state-of-the-art batch-sequential BO algorithms.  
- Strong recommendation for publication; notes high societal impact of parallelizing BO.  
- Minor criticism: paper does not study speed-ups gained by moving from sequential to batch-sequential.  
- Literature review has imprecise claims: integrating EI w.r.t. posterior and Constant Liar appeared in prior work ("Kriging is well-suited to parallelize optimization").  
- Literature omission: Chevalier et al. presented CL-mix and q-EI natural-gradient maximization was studied in "Differentiating the multipoint Expected Improvement for optimal batch design."  
- Question about assumptions: Is the domain A assumed compact and is f assumed continuous?  
- Question: Why restrict the initial design A to be a Latin Hypercube Sample (LHS)?  
- Algorithm 1 query: how and when are hyperparameters re-estimated or updated (Bayesian updating)?  
- Clarification needed in Sec. 5.2: is g defined using a max or a min?  
- Concern: smoothness of g in Sec. 5.2 is not obviously guaranteed.  
- Concern: differentiability of the Cholesky factor used in derivations may be non-obvious.  
- Typo/correction: Matérn parameter should be 5/2, not 2/5.  
- Experimental question: could the same GPs used in Figure 1 be fitted using different software for verification?
