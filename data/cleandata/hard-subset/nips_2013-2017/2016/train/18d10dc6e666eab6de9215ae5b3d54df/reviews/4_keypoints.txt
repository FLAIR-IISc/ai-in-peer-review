- Proposes q-KG, a new acquisition function for batch (parallel) Bayesian optimization that outputs q points and an efficient way to evaluate it, with empirical results supporting the theory.  
- Tackles an important problem: speeding up expensive function evaluations via distributed/batch evaluation.  
- The idea of proposing q points at each iteration is interesting and can substantially improve efficiency.  
- Concern that assuming a Gaussian process prior may be inappropriate for high-dimensional inputs such as neural network architectures.  
- Requests theoretical results: error bounds for the algorithm and a quantitative complexity analysis with respect to input dimension.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution — method is sound, experiments are convincing, and paper is well written.  
- Minor issues/typos and clarifications needed: l.36 grammar ("the set of points to evaluate next that is"), l.95 define A, l.104 clarify input of mu^n, l.128 briefly discuss parallel EI, l.140 avoid reusing notation A.
