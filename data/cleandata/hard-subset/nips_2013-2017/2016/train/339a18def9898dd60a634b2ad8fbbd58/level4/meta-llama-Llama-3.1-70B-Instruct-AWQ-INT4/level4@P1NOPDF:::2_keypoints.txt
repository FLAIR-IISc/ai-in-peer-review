- Models crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and derives asymptotic limits on queries per item.  
- Paper is well-written and a promising first step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.  
- Uses the source–channel separation theorem but needs a proof or citation that separation holds when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder (and possibly known at the decoder).  
- Taskmaster/encoder and crowdsourcer/decoder are modeled as seeing only labels, which omits possible side information from the original items; authors should consider encoder/decoder side information scenarios.  
- In Sec. 4.2, saying kIC lower bounds “improve” with k and q is misleading—bounds becoming smaller does not itself demonstrate improvement without corresponding upper bounds.  
- Provides a useful lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding under a specific crowd model.  
- Recommends including non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) analysis—cite Kostina & Verdú (2013) for finite-blocklength JSCC converses.
