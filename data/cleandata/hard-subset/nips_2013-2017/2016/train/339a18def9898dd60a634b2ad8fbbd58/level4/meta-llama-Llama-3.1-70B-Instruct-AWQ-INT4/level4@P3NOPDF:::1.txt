The paper presents an interesting approach to modeling the problem of labeling data from crowdsourcing, where each worker is represented as a noisy channel. Specifically, the authors consider two types of channels: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer channel model. By framing the problem as a coding problem for the chosen channel, the authors are able to study the tradeoffs between distortion and budget, providing a novel perspective on the crowdsourcing process. The budget is mapped onto the rate of the code for analysis, allowing for a more nuanced understanding of the relationship between the two. Furthermore, the authors propose a basic coding scheme, termed k-incidence coding, which involves requiring crowdsourced tasks to label a subset of k items at a time. While I found the paper to be engaging and thought-provoking, I must admit that it falls outside of my area of expertise, and therefore, I do not have any detailed comments to offer.
