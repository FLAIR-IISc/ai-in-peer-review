- Paper maps crowdsourcing to joint source-channel coding, with taskmaster as transmitter, workers as noisy channel, and crowdsourcer as receiver.  
- Main theoretical contribution: an information-theoretic lower bound on budget required to achieve a target inference fidelity, independent of specific query or aggregation schemes.  
- Authors analyze a specific k-ary incidence coding scheme and compare its performance to the derived lower bound under a chosen channel model.  
- The rate-distortion/crowdsourcing parallel is insightful and could inspire further application of information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing.  
- Modeling concern: worker skill and reliability are conflated into a single channel parameter, and the model assumes uniform query allocation regardless of individual skill.  
- Practical systems often use worker ratings or skill profiles enabling adaptive allocation, which the current model does not capture.  
- Channel model concern: it does not account for adversarial worker behavior or malicious degradation of fidelity; realism of assumed channels needs justification.  
- Presentation issue: numerical results explanation is unclear; Figure 2 curve labels need clarification (e.g., clarify meaning of k=1).  
- Presentation issue: the assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) (page 6, line 246) should be stated earlier in the paper.  
- Presentation issue: incorrect figure references on page 4, lines 135 ("Figure 1.a" → "Figure 1.b", "Figure 1.b" → "Figure 1.c").  
- Presentation issue: inconsistent use of capitalized "Theorem," "Lemma," and "Corollary" without numbering; should use lowercase or provide numbering for consistency.  
- Overall: theoretical framework is novel but authors should address modeling realism and presentation flaws to strengthen practical relevance.
