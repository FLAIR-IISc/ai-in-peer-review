- Frames crowdsourcing as joint source–channel coding (JSCC) with random channel state and derives asymptotic query-rate limits (Theorems 2–3).  
- Models item labels as i.i.d. source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses with skill as channel state, and crowdsourcer as decoder.  
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding under a specific worker model.  
- Manuscript is clearly written and a promising initial step toward JSCC-based analysis of crowdsourcing.  
- Needs justification or reference that source–channel separation holds when the channel has a random state unknown to the encoder and possibly known/unknown at the decoder.  
- Modeling that only the taskmaster sees labels and only the crowdsourcer sees worker outputs may be overly restrictive; decoder or shared side information (raw items) should be considered.  
- Claim that kIC lower bounds “improve” with larger k and q is misleading without an accompanying upper bound; should clarify that the lower bound value decreases (implying better performance).  
- Recommend adding nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterizations; cite Kostina & Verdú (2013) for finite-blocklength JSCC converses.
