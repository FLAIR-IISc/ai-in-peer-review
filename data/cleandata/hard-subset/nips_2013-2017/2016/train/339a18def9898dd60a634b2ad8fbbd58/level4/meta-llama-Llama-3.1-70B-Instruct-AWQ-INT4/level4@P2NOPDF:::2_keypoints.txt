- Novel contribution: models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem and derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item.
- Modeling details: maps item labels → i.i.d. source, taskmaster → encoder, workers → channel uses, worker skill → random channel state, crowdsourcer → decoder.
- Provides a lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific crowdsourcing model.
- Technical concern: reliance on the standard source–channel separation theorem—authors must justify or cite its validity when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder but possibly known at the receiver (affects Theorems 2 and 3).
- Model limitation: assumes encoder sees only labels and decoder sees only worker labels; should consider side information (original items) at encoder and/or decoder to increase realism.
- Clarification on Figure 2: saying kIC bounds “improve” as k and q grow is misleading—more accurate to state the lower bound decreases, since no upper bound is provided to show actual performance improvement.
- Practicality suggestion: pursue non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterizations of crowdsourcing limits; recommend citing Kostina & Verdú (2013) on non-asymptotic JSCC converses.
- Overall assessment: well-written and promising first step, but needs addressing of separation-theorem justification, model extensions, and non-asymptotic analysis.
