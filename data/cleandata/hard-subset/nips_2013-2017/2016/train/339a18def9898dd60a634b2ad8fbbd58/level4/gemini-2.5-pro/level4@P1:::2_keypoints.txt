- Paper frames crowdsourcing as joint source-channel coding (JSCC), modeling true labels as an i.i.d. source and workers as noisy parallel channels with random skill states.  
- Derives asymptotic lower bounds on queries-per-item vs. fidelity using the source-channel separation theorem.  
- Analyzes two scenarios: worker skill levels unknown to the decoder and skill levels known to the decoder.  
- Introduces and bounds performance of a specific scheme, k-ary incidence coding (kIC), under a spammer-hammer worker model.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and novel application of information-theoretic methods to crowdsourcing.  
- Major technical concern: need justification or citation that source-channel separation holds for channels with random states unknown at the encoder/decoder.  
- Modeling concern: current formulation omits item features (side information); decoder/encoder side information could significantly change fundamental limits and should be discussed.  
- Clarification requested: ambiguous phrasing that kIC bounds “improve” — should state bounds decrease (error lower bound becomes smaller) to indicate better potential performance.  
- Post-rebuttal critique: asymptotic results have limited practical utility; non-asymptotic/finite-blocklength analysis is more useful for system designers.  
- Suggested direction/reference for non-asymptotic analysis: Kostina & Verdú, “Lossy Joint Source-Channel Coding in the Finite Blocklength Regime” (IEEE Trans. IT, 2013).
