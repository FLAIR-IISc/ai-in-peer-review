1) Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding (JSCC) problem mapping items, taskmaster, workers, and crowdsourcer to source, encoder, channel with random state, and decoder.  
2) Authors derive asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item using coding-rate results and source–channel separation.  
3) Paper presents a lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding under a specific crowdsourcing/channel model.  
4) Overall assessment: well written and a promising first step toward JSCC-based modeling of crowdsourcing.  
5) Concern: the paper must justify that source–channel separation holds for channels with random state unknown at the encoder (and possibly known at the decoder) or cite an explicit proof.  
6) Concern: the model omits realistic side information (access to original items) at encoder/decoder; the authors should incorporate and analyze its impact.  
7) Concern: claims that lower bounds “improve” with k and q are potentially misleading—lower-bound decreases do not prove code optimality without matching upper bounds and should be clarified.  
8) Suggestion: include nonasymptotic/finite-blocklength analysis or discuss recent finite-blocklength JSCC results (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) for greater practical relevance.
