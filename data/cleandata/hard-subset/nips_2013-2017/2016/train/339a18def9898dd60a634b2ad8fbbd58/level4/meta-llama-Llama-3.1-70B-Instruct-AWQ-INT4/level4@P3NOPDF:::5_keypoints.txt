- Proposes a novel information-theoretic framework giving lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability in digital crowdsourcing.  
- Models workers as parallel independent channels using symmetric discrete memoryless channels (SDMC) and a spammer-hammer channel SHC(q).  
- Assumes nonadaptive coding and analyzes two cases: crowdsourcer knows worker skills vs does not.  
- Taskmaster is assumed unaware of individual skills, leading to uniform task assignment across workers.  
- Derives a necessary condition for the minimum required rate (queries per task) for k-ary incidence coding with an SHC(q) worker pool.  
- Paper is well-organized and well-written, making the material easy to follow.  
- Lacks experimental or simulation results to illustrate the gap between current schemes and the theoretical limits (e.g., AMT comparison).  
- Recommends shortening/omitting Sections 2.4 and 3.1 and adding proof sketches rather than those sections.  
- Novel contribution: first information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing, useful for designers to determine minimum rates and pricing.  
- Reviewer did not read the appendix, so no assessment of the proofs’ validity was provided.
