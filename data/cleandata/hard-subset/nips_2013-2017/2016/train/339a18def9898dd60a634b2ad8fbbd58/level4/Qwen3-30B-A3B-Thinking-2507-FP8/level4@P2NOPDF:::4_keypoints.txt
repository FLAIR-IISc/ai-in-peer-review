- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding problem: taskmaster = transmitter, workers = noisy channels, crowdsourcer = receiver.  
- Main theoretical contribution: scheme-independent information-theoretic lower bound on queries (budget) needed for a target fidelity.  
- Proposed practical scheme: k-ary incidence coding, analyzed against the derived bound under a specific channel model.  
- Insightful parallel drawn between rate-distortion theory and crowdsourcing, motivating future information-theoretic work.  
- Concern: model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel parameter, ignoring distinct worker skill levels.  
- Concern: taskmaster allocates identical query volumes to all workers, contradicting real-world practices that use worker-specific allocation based on skill.  
- Concern: channel model may not capture real worker behaviors, notably missing modeling or robustness against adversarial/malicious workers.  
- Clarity issue: Fig. 2 and explanation unclear—meaning of k=1 and comparisons across k-values need clarification and better curve labeling.  
- Missing justification: assumption of known P(B(X)) (page 6, line 246) is introduced without prior definition or motivation.  
- Presentation errors: incorrect figure references (Figure 1.a/b/c) and inconsistent use of "Theorem/Lemma/Corollary" without numbering; suggest using "result/claim/observation" or numbered statements.  
- Overall critique: technically sound and stimulating theoretically, but practical applicability is limited by unrealistic assumptions; authors should explicitly discuss limits and incorporate worker heterogeneity and adversarial scenarios.
