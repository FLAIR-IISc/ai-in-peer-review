

This work establishes an information-theoretic bound on the budget-fidelity tradeoff in label crowdsourcing using rate-distortion theory and the channel coding theorem. The derivation of the information-theoretic bound is technically sound. If this constitutes the first application of rate-distortion theory and the channel coding theorem to crowdsourcing, it represents a potentially significant contribution. However, the novelty and practical value of the paper remain questionable, as the derivation is relatively straightforward and the bound risks being overly loose. The primary issue lies in the fundamental disparity between the channel coding problem and the crowdsourcing problem regarding design freedom. In channel coding, extensive freedom exists in constructing codebooks (e.g., repetition codes, Hamming codes, LDPC codes), whereas for crowdsourcing, it is unclear whether such freedom applies to the sequence of channel inputs $u^n$. Specifically, in Equation (3), nearly all mappings from $\mathcal{B}(X)^n$ to $U^{\sum m_i}$ (except degenerate mappings) should be valid or implementable codebooks; otherwise, the channel capacity may provide a loose bound. While repetition-based schemes (with majority voting as the natural decoding method) are feasible, it is unclear how to design $u^n$ to enhance error correction beyond this. Crucially, a key difference exists: in crowdsourcing, reassigning the same query to the same worker violates the iid assumption in Equations (1) or (2), as the worker would likely produce identical responses. In contrast, communication channels assume iid behavior and permit repeated use of the same query. This is one example of a fundamental constraint absent in channel coding—where an encoding mapping $\{0,1\}^{nR} \to \{0,1\}^n$ faces minimal constraints—yet pervasive in crowdsourcing. Consequently, the set of feasible encoding schemes in Equation (3) is likely highly restricted, implying the proposed bound may be loose. A comparison of the derived bound against state-of-the-art methods' performance would strengthen the paper.
