- Studies crowdsourcing where a taskmaster decomposes tasks and a crowdsourcer aggregates worker responses, focusing on fidelity vs. query budget trade-off.
- Establishes a connection to joint source-channel coding, mapping taskmaster to transmitter and crowdsourcer to receiver with workers as noisy channels.
- Derives a scheme-agnostic information-theoretic lower bound on required budget for a target fidelity.
- Analyzes the performance of k-ary incidence coding against the theoretical lower bound under a specified channel model.
- Draws an insightful analogy between rate-distortion theory and the crowdsourcing framework, with potential to inspire further research.
- Concern: simplifying to a point-to-point communication model may omit important real-world crowdsourcing nuances.
- Concern: model conflates worker reliability and skill into a single channel, ignoring differential task assignment based on worker ratings.
- Concern: channel models need stronger justification and should acknowledge limitations, including the possibility of adversarial workers.
- Concern: numerical results presentation is unclear—Figure 2 curve labels need improvement and the meaning of k=1 must be clarified.
- Presentation issue: assumption of known P(B(X)) is introduced too late and should be stated earlier for clarity.
- Minor errors: incorrect figure references (Figure 1.a → 1.b and 1.b → 1.c).
- Presentation issue: use of ‘Theorem,’ ‘Lemma,’ and ‘Corollary’ without numbering; these should be used as descriptive labels or properly numbered.
