- Presents an information-theoretic bound for budget–fidelity tradeoff in crowdsourcing using rate-distortion and channel coding concepts; could be significant if novel.
- Novelty and practical utility are questionable: derivation is straightforward and the bound may be too loose to matter.
- Flawed analogy to channel coding: crowdsourcing lacks the broad freedom to design input sequences u^n like codebooks, undermining the capacity analogy.
- For the channel-capacity bound to be tight, nearly all mappings in Equation (3) must be implementable as coding schemes, which is not shown.
- The paper only considers simple repetition codes and fails to explore designing more complex u^n sequences for improved error correction.
- Assigning the same query to the same worker repeatedly violates i.i.d. assumptions due to correlated answers, unlike repeated transmissions in communication channels.
- These fundamental constraints on feasible encoding schemes imply the proposed bound is likely loose.
- To show practical value, the paper should compare the derived bound to empirical performance of state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods.
