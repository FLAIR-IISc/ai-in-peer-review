- Models digital crowdsourcing in an information-theoretic framework and derives lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.  
- Workers modeled as parallel, independent channels using symmetric discrete memoryless channels (DMCs).  
- Uses the spammer-hammer channel model SHC(q) to capture workers who respond randomly with probability 1−q.  
- Analysis assumes nonadaptive query strategies.  
- Considers two crowdsourcer knowledge scenarios: known worker skills and unknown worker skills.  
- Taskmaster is assumed unaware of individual worker capabilities and assigns the same number of tasks to each worker.  
- Provides a necessary condition on the minimum required rate (queries per task) for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Lacks experimental evaluation or simulations to compare real-world schemes (e.g., AMT) to the theoretical limits.  
- Recommends shortening section 2.4 and removing or condensing section 3.1; prioritize including proof sketches for theorems.  
- Work appears novel as the first information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing and is potentially useful for project design and pricing.  
- Reviewer did not check the appendix and cannot comment on the contained proofs.
