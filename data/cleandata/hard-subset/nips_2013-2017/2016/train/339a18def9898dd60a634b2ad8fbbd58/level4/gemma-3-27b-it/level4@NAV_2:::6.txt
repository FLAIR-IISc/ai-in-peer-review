This work provides an information-theoretic bound on the budget-fidelity tradeoff for label crowdsourcing, leveraging rate-distortion theory and channel coding theorem. The information-theoretic bound is well-derived within this framework. If this is the first attempt to apply these tools to the crowdsourcing problem, it represents a potentially valuable contribution to the field. However, the novelty and practical relevance of this paper are somewhat limited, as the derivation of the bound is relatively straightforward and may be loose in practice. A core challenge lies in the inherent differences between traditional channel coding and crowdsourcing. While channel coding offers substantial flexibility in codebook design (e.g., repetition codes, Hamming codes, LDPC codes), the design space for query sequences in crowdsourcing – represented by the mappings in equation (3) – appears significantly constrained. Almost all mappings from B(X)^n to U^(∑mi) (excluding degenerate cases) should be implementable codebooks; otherwise, the resulting channel capacity may be a loose upper bound. Specifically, while repetition-style coding (akin to majority voting) is plausible, it’s unclear how to design sequences of queries, u^n, to substantially improve error correction capabilities. A key constraint, not present in standard channel coding, is the inability to repeatedly query the same worker with identical tasks due to violations of the i.i.d. assumption in equations (1) or (2); a worker is likely to provide the same answer to the same query, invalidating this assumption. This is just one example, and more generally, the crowdsourcing problem likely possesses fundamental constraints on the encoding schemes in equation (3) that are not present in typical channel coding scenarios. Consequently, the proposed information-theoretic bound carries a risk of being overly optimistic. Finally, a stronger evaluation would involve comparing the performance of state-of-the-art crowdsourcing algorithms with the derived information-theoretic bound.
