

The paper addresses the problem of labeling data in crowdsourcing settings by modeling workers as noisy channels, specifically considering two channel types: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channel models. This framing transforms the problem into a channel coding problem, where the budget constraint is mapped to the code rate for analysis. The authors investigate distortion versus budget tradeoffs and propose a basic coding scheme—k-incidence coding—which requires each crowdsourced task to involve labeling a subset of k items. I find the paper quite interesting, though as it falls outside my area of expertise, I cannot provide detailed technical commentary on the methodology or results.
