This paper addresses the problem of aggregating worker output in crowdsourcing using the language and tools of information theory, establishing the fundamental limits of the budget-fidelity tradeoff. While I did not fully understand all the technical details, I found the model and its resulting insights to be an interesting and potentially useful perspective on the design of crowdsourcing aggregation techniques.

The primary weakness of the paper is its accessibility. While I appreciate the authors' efforts to make the material accessible to a broader audience, the paper was still quite difficult to follow for a reader not deeply familiar with information theory. I strongly recommend that the authors reframe many of the results in the concrete terms of the crowdsourcing application, rather than retaining the abstract language of channels, codes, and distortion. This would significantly improve clarity and help readers understand the practical implications of the work.

Furthermore, the high-level intuition and key takeaways are not highlighted prominently enough. For example, the insights at the end of Section 3 could be given more emphasis. The paper would also benefit greatly from a conclusion that summarizes what a reader should learn from these results.

A few additional points:

*   **Related Work:** The related work section should mention the alternate line of research on robust economic incentives for workers, often discussed under the terms "crowdsourcing mechanism" or "mechanism design for crowdsourcing." Some of this work also considers varying worker abilities and, more recently, how to combine these incentives with sophisticated aggregation methods.
*   **Line 84:** "is reviewed in" should probably be "are reviewed in".
*   **Line 106:** The distinction between the "crowdsourcer" and "taskmaster" was confusing, particularly since "crowdsourcing" is a portmanteau of "crowd" and "outsourcing," which would presumably occur before workers are involved.
*   **Line 122:** There appears to be a typo in "A A(X1,X2)".
*   **Lines 275-276:** The sentence beginning "In case..." was very difficult to parse and should be rewritten for clarity.
*   **Figure 2:** I could not glean much from this figure. A more detailed caption explaining its components and the main takeaway would be very helpful.
