- Models crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic framework to derive lower bounds on queries per task, treating workers as parallel independent channels.
- Considers symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and the spammer–hammer channel SHC(q) to model worker errors.
- Analyzes nonadaptive coding in two cases: crowdsourcer knows worker skills vs crowdsourcer does not; taskmaster assigns equal tasks to all workers.
- Derives a necessary condition on minimum rate for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).
- Strength: Clear problem formulation and informative lower bounds on query complexity for crowdsourced labeling.
- Strength: Use of MSC and SHC(q) is appropriate and the nonadaptive coding assumption is explicit.
- Strength: Novel information-theoretic perspective on crowdsourcing with no obvious prior work.
- Strength: Results could help estimate minimum query rates and inform pricing in practice.
- Weakness: No experiments or simulations to evaluate tightness or practical relevance of the bounds.
- Weakness: Exposition is at times longer than necessary.
- Suggestion: Add experiments/simulations comparing common methods (e.g., AMT) to the theoretical limits.
- Suggestion: Include proof sketches of main theorems in the main text for reader accessibility.
- Suggestion: Streamline Section 2.4 and remove or shorten Section 3.1 to make room for proofs/experiments.
- Clarity: Generally well organized; roles of “crowdsourcer” and “taskmaster” and modeling assumptions are clearly stated.
- Novelty/impact: Novel and valuable approach that quantifies fundamental limits to guide design and pricing.
