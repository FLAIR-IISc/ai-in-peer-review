- Models digital crowdsourcing in an information-theoretic framework to derive lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.
- Workers are modeled as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) for errors and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q) for spammer behavior.
- Assumes a nonadaptive coding scheme.
- Analyzes two crowdsourcer knowledge settings: (1) full knowledge of worker skills and (2) no knowledge of worker skills.
- Assumes the taskmaster has no skill information and assigns equal numbers of tasks to all workers.
- Derives a necessary condition on the minimum required rate (queries per task) for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Lacks experimental validation or simulations comparing theoretical limits to real crowdsourcing platforms (e.g., Amazon Mechanical Turk).
- Recommends shortening Section 2.4 and condensing or removing Section 3.1; prioritize including proof sketches for the theorems.
- Work appears novel — no prior information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing is known.
- Results are practically useful for determining minimum query rates and informing crowdsourcing pricing strategies.
- Appendix was not reviewed, so the correctness of proofs is not assessed.
