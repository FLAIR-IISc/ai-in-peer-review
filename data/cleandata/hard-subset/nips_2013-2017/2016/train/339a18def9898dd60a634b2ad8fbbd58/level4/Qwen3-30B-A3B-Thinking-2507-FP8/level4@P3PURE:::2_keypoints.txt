- Presents a JSCC information-theoretic model of crowdsourcing and derives asymptotic limits on queries per item.  
- Models: i.i.d. item labels (source), taskmaster as encoder observing labels, workers as channel uses with random state (worker skills) unknown at encoder, crowdsourcer as decoder (may know state).  
- Uses existing JSCC results to derive asymptotic fundamental query-complexity limits.  
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific worker model.  
- Paper is well-written and a promising first step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.  
- Major technical gap: applies source–channel separation theorem without justifying it when channel state is unknown at the encoder; needs proof or citation.  
- Model omits potential side information from original item data at encoder/decoder; including this would be more realistic and could improve performance.  
- Wording in Section 4.2 is misleading: the presented lower bound decreases as k and q increase (implying better achievable performance), so phrasing should be corrected.  
- Recommendation: include nonasymptotic/finite-blocklength analysis (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) for more practical characterization.
