The paper models crowdsourced labeling as communication over noisy channels, treating each worker as a channel. It analyzes two channel types: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer model. Casting the task as a channel-coding problem allows the annotation budget to be interpreted as the code rate. The authors study the distortion–budget trade-off and evaluate a basic coding scheme, k-incidence coding, in which each task asks a worker to label a subset of k items at a time. I found the paper fairly interesting, but because it is outside my area of expertise, I do not have detailed comments.
