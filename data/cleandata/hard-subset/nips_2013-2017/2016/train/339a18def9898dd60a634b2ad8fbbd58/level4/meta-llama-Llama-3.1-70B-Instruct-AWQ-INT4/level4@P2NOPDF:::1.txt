The paper proposes a novel approach to modeling the problem of labeling data from crowdsourcing by representing each worker as a noisy channel. Two types of channels are considered: the symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer channel model. By framing the problem as a coding problem for the chosen channel, the authors investigate the trade-offs between distortion and budget. Specifically, the budget is mapped onto the rate of the code, enabling the analysis of the optimal allocation of resources. The paper also introduces a basic coding scheme, termed k-incidence coding, which involves requiring crowdsourced tasks to label a subset of k items at a time.
