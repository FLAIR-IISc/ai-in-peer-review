- Proposes an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion theory and channel coding.  
- Bound is well-derived and could be a valuable contribution if this is the first application to crowdsourcing.  
- Introduces and analyzes k-ary incidence coding.  
- Novelty is questionable: the derivation is relatively straightforward.  
- Practical value is uncertain: the bound may be loose in real crowdsourcing settings.  
- Core challenge: crowdsourcing differs from channel coding, so standard coding freedoms may not apply to designing u^n.  
- Many implementable encoding mappings may be constrained, making channel-capacity-based bounds potentially loose.  
- Repetition coding fits majority voting, but other error-correcting designs may be infeasible because re-querying the same worker breaks independence assumptions.  
- Channel model mismatch: channel coding assumes i.i.d. repeated queries, which is unrealistic for human workers.  
- Paper lacks empirical comparison with state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods to assess bound tightness and practicality.  
- Needs expanded discussion on limitations and tightness of the proposed bound.  
- Minor fixes requested: clarify minimization in (8) over C and C' with E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t.  
- Proof of Theorem 4 should be more detailed; clarify the ln(1−q)<0 implication and whether ε_S > ˆε.  
- In equation (17), justify or cite the inclusion of H_M(E(ε)).
