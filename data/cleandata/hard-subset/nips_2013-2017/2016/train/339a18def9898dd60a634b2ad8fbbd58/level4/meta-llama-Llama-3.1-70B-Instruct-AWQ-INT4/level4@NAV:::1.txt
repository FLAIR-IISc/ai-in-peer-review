This paper presents a model for addressing the issue of labeling data obtained through crowdsourcing, where each worker is represented as a noisy channel, specifically examining two types of channels: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channel models. By framing the problem as a coding issue for the chosen channel, the authors conduct an in-depth analysis, mapping the allocated budget to the code rate. The study delves into the trade-offs between distortion and budget, as well as a fundamental coding scheme known as k-incidence coding, which involves labeling a subset of k items simultaneously. Although I found the paper to be intriguing, it falls outside my area of expertise, and therefore, I am unable to provide detailed comments.
