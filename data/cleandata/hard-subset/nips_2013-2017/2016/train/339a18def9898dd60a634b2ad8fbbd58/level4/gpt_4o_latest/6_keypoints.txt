- Paper derives an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate‑distortion theory and the channel coding theorem.
- The derivation is rigorous and well-presented.
- Novelty and practical significance are debatable: derivation seems straightforward and the resulting bound may be overly loose.
- Key concern: unlike channel coding, it’s unclear whether one has the flexibility to design the sequence u^n (codebooks) in crowdsourcing.
- In Eq. (3), many mappings from B(X)^n to U^{∑m_i} may not be implementable, so channel capacity could be a loose bound.
- Repetition codes (with majority voting) are feasible for crowdsourcing, but beyond repetition it’s unclear how to design u^n to improve error correction.
- Assigning the same query to the same worker violates the i.i.d. assumptions in Eqs. (1)–(2), unlike communication channels where repeated uses remain i.i.d.
- Such implementation constraints likely make the proposed information-theoretic bound excessively loose for practical crowdsourcing.
- The paper would be strengthened by comparing the derived bound to the performance of state‑of‑the‑art methods.

- Minor: The proof of Theorem 4 needs more detail.
- Minor: In Theorem 4, clarify whether ε_S is larger than ĥε given ln(1−q) < 0.
