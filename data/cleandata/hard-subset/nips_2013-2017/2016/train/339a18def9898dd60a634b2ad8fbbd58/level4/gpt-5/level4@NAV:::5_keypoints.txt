- Paper formulates digital crowdsourcing in an information-theoretic framework and derives lower bounds on queries per task to reach a target error probability.  
- Workers modeled as parallel, independent channels.  
- Two channel models analyzed: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and spammer–hammer channel SHC(q).  
- Coding is assumed nonadaptive.  
- Two knowledge cases: crowdsourcer knows all worker skills, and crowdsourcer knows nothing.  
- Taskmaster is unaware of worker skills so each worker receives the same number of tasks.  
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).  
- Paper is well organized and clearly written.  
- Lacks experiments or simulations comparing current practical schemes (e.g., AMT) to derived limits.  
- Suggests shortening Section 2.4 and condensing or removing Section 3.1; recommends adding sketch proofs of theorems instead.  
- Contribution appears novel and practically useful for determining minimum rates and pricing crowdsourcing projects.  
- Reviewer did not read the appendix and cannot comment on the formal proofs.
