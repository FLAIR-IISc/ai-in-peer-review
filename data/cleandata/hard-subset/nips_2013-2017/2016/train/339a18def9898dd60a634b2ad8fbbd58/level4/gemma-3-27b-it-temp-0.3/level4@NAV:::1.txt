This paper investigates the problem of data labeling using crowdsourcing, framing it as a channel coding problem. The authors model individual crowdworkers as noisy channels – specifically, both symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channel models – and analyze the resulting coding challenges. They connect the crowdsourcing budget to the achievable coding rate, allowing for an examination of distortion versus budget tradeoffs. A specific coding scheme, k-incidence coding (where workers label subsets of k items), is also explored. Overall, I found the work to be conceptually interesting; however, due to the topic residing outside my primary expertise, I am unable to provide a detailed technical assessment.
