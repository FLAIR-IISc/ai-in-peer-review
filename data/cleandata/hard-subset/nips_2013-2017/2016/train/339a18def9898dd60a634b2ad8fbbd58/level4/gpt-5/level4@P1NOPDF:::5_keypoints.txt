- Models crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic framework and derives lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability.  
- Workers modeled as parallel, independent channels with two types: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and spammer–hammer channel SHC(q); coding/assignment is nonadaptive.  
- Analyzes two regimes for the crowdsourcer: full knowledge of worker skills vs no knowledge; taskmaster is assumed ignorant and assigns equal tasks to all workers.  
- Provides a necessary condition on the minimum required query rate for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).  
- Paper is well organized, clearly written, and presents a novel information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing limits.  
- Results are potentially useful for guiding crowdsourcing system design, pricing, and budgeting decisions.  
- Lacks empirical or simulation results comparing real-world schemes (e.g., AMT) to the theoretical bounds, reducing practical impact.  
- Presentation could be improved: shorten Section 2.4, condense or remove Section 3.1, and add brief proof sketches for main theorems to build intuition.  
- Reviewer did not read the appendix and therefore cannot vouch for the correctness or completeness of the proofs.
