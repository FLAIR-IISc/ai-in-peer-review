- Proposes an information-theoretic framework giving lower bounds on queries per task to reach a target error probability in digital crowdsourcing.  
- Models workers as parallel independent channels, using symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and the spammer-hammer channel SHC(q).  
- Assumes nonadaptive coding and analyzes two scenarios: known worker skills and unknown worker skills.  
- Assumes the taskmaster is uninformed about individual skills, leading to uniform task assignment.  
- Derives a necessary condition for the minimum required rate in k-ary incidence coding with an SHC(q) worker pool.  
- Claims novelty in applying information-theoretic bounds to inform crowdsourcing design and project pricing.  
- Paper is well-organized and well-written.  
- Lacks experimental or simulation-based comparisons to existing practical schemes (e.g., AMT workflows).  
- Recommends condensing Sections 2.4 and 3.1 and adding proof sketches for main theorems.  
- Appendix (and thus formal proof verification) was not reviewed, so proof validity is unconfirmed.
