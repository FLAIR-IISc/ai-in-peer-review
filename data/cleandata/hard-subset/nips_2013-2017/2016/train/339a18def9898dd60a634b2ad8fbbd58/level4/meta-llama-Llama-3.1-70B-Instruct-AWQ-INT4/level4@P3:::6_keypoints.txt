- Paper derives an information-theoretic lower bound on queries per item for desired fidelity using rate-distortion and channel coding theory.  
- Introduces and analyzes a novel query scheme: k-ary incidence coding.  
- Information-theoretic bound is well-derived but its novelty and practical value are questionable.  
- Bound derivation is relatively straightforward and may be very loose in practice.  
- Crowdsourcing imposes constraints (e.g., cannot assign same query to same worker repeatedly) absent in traditional channel coding, limiting codebook/design freedom.  
- These crowdsourcing constraints make it unclear how to design the sequence of channel inputs u^n to improve error correction.  
- Paper lacks clear empirical or theoretical comparison between the derived bound and state-of-the-art methods.  
- Minor issue: in Eq. (8) minimization is over C, C' with E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t.  
- Minor issue: proof for Theorem 4 needs more elaboration.  
- Minor issue: in Theorem 4, ln(1-q) < 0 raises question whether εS > ε̂.  
- Minor issue: Eq. (17) needs explanation or citation for H_M(E(ε)).  
- Strength: thorough application of rate-distortion and channel coding theory to crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: k-ary incidence coding is an original contribution.  
- Significance: provides a fundamental limit on crowdsourcing rate that could inform system design.  
- Acceptance arguments: rigorous analysis and novel query scheme; offers fundamental rate limit.  
- Rejection arguments: derivation may be trivial/loose, missing comparison to SOTA, and practical constraints limit applicability.
