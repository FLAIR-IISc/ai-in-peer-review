- Proposes an information-theoretic framework giving lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability.  
- Models workers as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (SDMC) and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q).  
- Assumes non-adaptive coding and equal task assignment because the taskmaster is unaware of individual worker skills.  
- Analyzes two cases: crowdsourcer knows worker skill levels and crowdsourcer does not.  
- Provides a necessary condition for minimum required rate for k-ary incidence coding with an SHC(q) worker pool.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Novel contribution: first information-theoretic analysis applied to crowdsourcing, useful for system designers.  
- Practical utility: helps determine minimum required rate and aids in pricing crowdsourcing projects.  
- Lacks experimental or simulation results to demonstrate gap between current practice (e.g., AMT) and proposed theoretical limits.  
- Recommends condensing/removing Sections 2.4 and 3.1; suggests focusing on theorem proof sketches.  
- Appendix (and thus full proof validation) was not reviewed, so proof correctness remains unconfirmed.
