The paper formulates the problem of labeling data from crowdsourcing by modeling each worker as a noisy channel, specifically considering two types of channels: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channel models. This formulation transforms the problem into a coding problem for the considered channel, which the authors investigate. By mapping the budget to the code rate, the paper examines the trade-offs between distortion and budget, as well as a basic coding scheme known as k-incidence coding. This scheme corresponds to requiring crowdsourced tasks to label a subset of k items at a time. I found the paper fairly interesting, although as it falls outside my area of expertise, I do not have any detailed comments to offer.
