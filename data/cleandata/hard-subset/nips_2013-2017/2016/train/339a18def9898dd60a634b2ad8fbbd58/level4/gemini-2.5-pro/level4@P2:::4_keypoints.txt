- Introduces an information-theoretic framework mapping crowdsourcing to joint source-channel coding, with budget = coding rate and fidelity = distortion (labeling error).
- Applies rate-distortion theory to derive fundamental lower bounds on budget needed for a target fidelity under unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skill scenarios.
- Bounds are general and scheme-independent, providing theoretical performance limits for crowdsourcing systems.
- Proposes k-ary Incidence Coding (kIC), where workers compare k items, and analyzes its performance under a spammer-hammer worker model.
- Strength: novel, principled formulation that offers a new lens and benchmarks for human-in-the-loop computation research.
- Strength: potential to inspire further work applying information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing and to evaluate practical algorithms against fundamental limits.
- Weakness: model realism—merges worker skill and reliability into a single channel parameter and assumes equal query allocation, which may not reflect adaptive or reputation-based task assignment.
- Weakness: limited worker behavior models—standard channel models (MSC, SHC) may not capture adversarial, colluding, or stateful workers that violate independence/memoryless assumptions.
- Weakness: assumption that source distribution P(B(X)) is known is critical but only stated late; should be justified much earlier given its practical importance.
- Weakness: clarity/presentation issues—numerical results (Section 4.2) and Figure 2 need clearer labels and explanation, including what k=1 represents for kIC.
- Weakness: minor presentation errors (incorrect figure references, unconventional capitalization of Theorem/Lemma/Corollary) that should be fixed.
- Arguments for acceptance: originality, significance in establishing fundamental limits, and potential high impact on the field.
- Arguments for rejection: strong modeling assumptions limit practical applicability, and clarity issues reduce the paper's accessibility and immediate value.
- Overall: interesting and original theoretical contribution, but needs better motivation of assumptions and clearer presentation to bridge gap with practical crowdsourcing systems.
