- Paper maps a crowdsourcing decomposition problem to joint source-channel coding and derives a budget–fidelity lower bound independent of query/decoding schemes.  
- Focuses on k-ary incidence coding and analyzes its performance under a specific worker-channel model.  
- The rate–distortion perspective is insightful and could stimulate further information-theoretic work on crowdsourcing.  
- Model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel representation, which is unrealistic given observable worker ratings.  
- Assumes equal query allocation across workers, ignoring rational non-uniform allocation based on worker ratings.  
- Adopted channel models may not capture adversarial or strategic worker behavior; richer channel models are needed.  
- Numerical results are poorly explained and Figure 2 curve labeling should be improved for clarity.  
- The meaning of the k=1 case is unclear and needs explicit clarification (e.g., does it mean direct correct labels).  
- The assumption that P(B(X)) is known is stated too late and should be declared earlier in the paper.  
- Minor errors in figure references (Figure 1.a→1.b, 1.b→1.c) and inconsistent use of “Theorem/Lemma/Corollary” without numbering or proper casing.
