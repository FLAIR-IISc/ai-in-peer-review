- Paper provides an information-theoretic bound on budget–fidelity tradeoff for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion and channel coding; derivation is solid and potentially novel if first such application.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable: derivation is straightforward and the bound may be very loose.  
- Main conceptual issue: unlike channel coding, crowdsourcing lacks freedom to design input sequences u^n, so the channel-coding analogy may not hold.  
- Crucial difference: you cannot assign the same query to the same worker repeatedly (violates iid assumption), unlike repeated uses of a communication channel.  
- More generally, crowdsourcing imposes significant constraints on encoding mappings in equation (3) that likely weaken the proposed bound.  
- Paper should discuss these encoding constraints and their implications for the bound’s tightness.  
- Empirical comparison to state-of-the-art methods is needed to demonstrate bound tightness and practical relevance.

- Minor: expand the proof of Theorem 4 for clarity.  
- Minor: in Theorem 4 note ln(1−q) < 0 and clarify whether εS > \hat{ε}.
