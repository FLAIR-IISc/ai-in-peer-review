- Models crowdsourcing workers as noisy channels, considering symmetric memoryless channels and spammer/hammer models.
- Reformulates labeling from crowdsourcing as a coding problem for the specified channel models.
- Maps the available budget to code rate to analyze performance limits.
- Studies trade-offs between labeling distortion and budget/rate.
- Proposes and analyzes k-incidence coding, where each task requires labeling a subset of k items.
