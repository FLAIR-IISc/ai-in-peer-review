- Presents a JSCC model for crowdsourcing, deriving asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item and a lower bound on k-ary incidence coding error; well written and a first step toward JSCC-based crowdsourcing modeling.

- Must justify or cite that source–channel separation holds when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder and possibly known at the decoder (provide proof or reference).

- The encoder/decoder model (taskmaster sees only labels; crowdsourcer sees only worker labels) is overly restrictive—practical systems can have side information (original items) at encoder and/or decoder, which should be modeled and analyzed.

- Claim that kIC lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow is misleading: a decreasing lower bound does not imply actual improvement without corresponding upper bounds for comparison.

- Recommend adding nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) analysis of JSCC limits—e.g., consider Kostina & Verdú (2013) for finite-blocklength converses.
