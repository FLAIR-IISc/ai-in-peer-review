- Models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and applies JSCC coding rate results.  
- Derives fundamental asymptotic limits on queries per item, providing two theorems for unknown and known worker skill levels.  
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific crowdsourcing model.  
- Paper is well-written and presents an interesting, novel information-theoretic approach to crowdsourcing.  
- Theorems give useful performance bounds for crowdsourcing systems.  
- Needs justification or citation that source-channel separation holds with random channel state unknown at the encoder (and possibly known/unknown at receiver).  
- Model is potentially too restrictive by ignoring side information from the taskmaster and crowdsourcer having access to original items.  
- Should extend/model side information for encoder/decoder to make results more realistic and convincing.  
- Language around Fig. 2 should be more precise: say the lower bound becomes smaller as k and q grow, implying potential code performance improvement.  
- Should consider recent nonasymptotic JSCC converse results (e.g., Kostina and Verdú) for a more useful finite-blocklength characterization.  
- Strengths: novel treatment, solid derivations, clear writing.  
- Weaknesses: restrictive modeling assumptions, missing separation-theorem justification, and need for more precise wording and nonasymptotic references.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10; Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
