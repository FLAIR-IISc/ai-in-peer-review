- Paper models crowdsourcing as a human-in-the-loop computation and analyzes budget-fidelity tradeoffs via an information-theoretic rate-distortion framework.
- Query and inference are represented as joint source-channel (de)coding over parallel noisy channels, each channel modeling a worker with imperfect skill.
- Provides performance bounds and analyzes a specific query scheme, k-ary incidence coding, with optimized query pricing.
- Modeling crowdsourcing as an information transmission problem is well-motivated and draws useful parallels to communication theory.
- The rate-distortion framework naturally captures the tradeoff between query cost (budget) and label accuracy (fidelity).
- Considers multiple worker models (MSC and SHC), enhancing realism and applicability.
- k-ary incidence coding offers a concrete, potentially practical query scheme and design principle.
- Builds on prior crowdsourcing work (e.g., majority voting, Dawid-Skene) and connects to bandit surveys and worker reliability literature.
- Strength: rigorous mathematical treatment and clear articulation of assumptions.
- Strength: derivation of information-theoretic bounds provides fundamental limits beyond heuristic methods.
- Strength: analysis includes both known and unknown worker skill scenarios, increasing generality.
- Weakness: connection to practical crowdsourcing systems and real-world datasets is limited.
- Weakness: query pricing discussion (Appendix E) feels disconnected and under-justified.
- Weakness: numerical results for k-ary incidence coding are sparse and need expansion to show practical benefits.
- Weakness: paper restricts attention to non-adaptive codes; more discussion of adaptive strategies is needed.
- Weakness: notation is dense and clarity could be improved for readability.
- Weakness: uses a simple Hamming distortion metric; exploring other metrics might yield additional insights.
- Overall: solid theoretical contribution with significant novelty and potential impact, but practical validation and clarity issues warrant improvements; reviewer recommends weak accept.
