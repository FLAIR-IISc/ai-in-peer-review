- Establishes an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion theory and the channel coding theorem; derivation is technically sound.  
- If this is the first application of these theories to crowdsourcing, it could be a meaningful contribution.  
- Novelty and practical significance are questionable because the derivation is relatively straightforward and the bound may be overly loose.  
- Core concern: limited design freedom for the channel input sequence u^n compared to standard channel coding, raising doubts about the applicability of channel capacity as a tight bound.  
- Unclear whether most mappings from B(X)^n to U^{Σ m_i} are valid/implementable codebooks; if not, capacity-based bound may be loose.  
- While repetition codes (majority voting) are feasible in crowdsourcing, it is unclear how to design u^n to achieve stronger error correction as in channel coding.  
- Fundamental distinction: re-assigning the same query to the same worker violates iid assumptions (workers tend to repeat answers), unlike iid channel uses in communication theory.  
- Crowdsourcing likely imposes significant constraints on valid encoding schemes (eq. (3)) that are not accounted for, causing looseness in the proposed bound.  
- Empirical comparison between the derived bound and performance of state-of-the-art methods is needed to strengthen the paper.
