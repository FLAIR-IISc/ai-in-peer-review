This paper addresses the problem of data labeling using crowdsourcing, framing it as a coding problem over noisy channels. The authors model each worker as a noisy channel – specifically, either a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback or a spammer/hammer channel. They then analyze the problem in terms of coding rate, relating it to the crowdsourcing budget. The work explores tradeoffs between labeling distortion and budget, and introduces a simple coding scheme, termed k-incidence coding, which corresponds to assigning workers the task of labeling subsets of k items. I found the paper interesting, though lacking detailed expertise in this area, I am unable to provide in-depth commentary.
