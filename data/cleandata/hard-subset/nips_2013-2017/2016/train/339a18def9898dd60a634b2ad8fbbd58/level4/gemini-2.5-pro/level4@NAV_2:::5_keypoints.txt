- Frames crowdsourcing as an information-theoretic communication problem to analyze budget–fidelity trade-offs.
- Models workers as parallel noisy channels and query/inference as joint source-channel coding.
- Main contribution: lower bounds on queries per item required to achieve a target error probability.
- Analyzes two worker channel models: M-ary Symmetric Channel (MSC) and Spammer-Hammer Channel (SHC).
- Derives fundamental limits for both unknown and perfectly known worker skill scenarios.
- Proposes and analyzes k-ary Incidence Coding (kIC) under the SHC, giving necessary rate conditions for target error.
- Strength: novel, principled information-theoretic approach providing foundational benchmarks for crowdsourcing systems.
- Strength: results are theoretically significant and practically informative for budget and pricing decisions.
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly written; communication-system analogy is effective.
- Weakness: lacks experimental validation or simulations on real-world datasets to assess practical gap to bounds.
- Weakness: background section on information theory is overly long; main text would benefit from proof sketches/high-level intuitions for key theorems.
- Overall: strong theoretical contribution with high originality and significance, but needs empirical validation and clearer main-text exposition.
