- Paper derives an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate–distortion and channel coding; derivation is sound and could be valuable if novel.  
- Novelty and practical significance are questionable because the derivation seems straightforward and the bound may be overly loose.  
- Fundamental difference: crowdsourcing lacks the flexible codebook/codeword design available in channel coding (e.g., repetition, Hamming, LDPC codes).  
- Unclear whether one can design the sequence u^n freely as implied by (3); if not, channel-capacity-based bound may be loose.  
- Repetition-like schemes (majority voting) are feasible, but it’s unclear how to design u^n to achieve stronger error correction in crowdsourcing.  
- You cannot repeatedly assign the same query to the same worker without violating i.i.d. assumptions, unlike reuse in communication channels.  
- Crowdsourcing’s additional constraints likely restrict allowable encoding mappings, undermining the applicability/tightness of the bound in (3).  
- A stronger evaluation would compare state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods against the derived information-theoretic bound.  
- Minor: In (8), clarify that minimization is over C and C' with E[d(Z, \hat{Z})] ≤ D^t.  
- Minor: Theorem 4’s proof needs more elaboration.  
- Minor: In Theorem 4, since ln(1−q)<0, clarify whether ε_S is larger than ħε.
