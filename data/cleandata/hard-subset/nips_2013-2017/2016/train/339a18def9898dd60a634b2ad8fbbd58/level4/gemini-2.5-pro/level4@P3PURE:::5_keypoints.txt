- Paper models crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic framework to derive lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.  
- Workers modeled as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (DMCs) and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q); analysis assumes nonadaptive coding.  
- Two scenarios analyzed: crowdsourcer knows worker skills vs. no prior knowledge; taskmaster is unaware and assigns equal tasks to each worker.  
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).  
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Strength: novel application of information theory to crowdsourcing—apparently the first such analysis.  
- Strength: derived theoretical bounds are potentially useful for determining minimum rates and pricing.  
- Weakness: lacks experiments or simulations comparing bounds to practical schemes (e.g., Mechanical Turk).  
- Weakness: presentation would benefit from proof sketches in the main text; suggests shortening Section 2.4 and condensing/removing Section 3.1.  
- Note: reviewer did not check the appendix, so cannot vouch for correctness of proofs.
