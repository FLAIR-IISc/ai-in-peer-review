- Derives information-theoretic lower bounds on the minimum number of queries per task needed to achieve a target error probability in crowdsourcing.  
- Models workers as parallel independent channels using symmetric discrete memoryless channels (SDMCs) and a spammer-hammer channel SHC(q) for spammers.  
- Assumes a nonadaptive coding scheme for task assignment.  
- Analyzes two taskmaster scenarios: full knowledge of worker skills and no prior knowledge (uniform task assignment).  
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum rate for k-ary incidence coding under a worker pool following SHC(q).  
- Claims novelty as the first information-theoretic treatment of crowdsourcing and offers precise bounds useful for task pricing and resource allocation.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written overall.  
- Lacks experimental validation or simulations comparing real crowdsourcing systems to the theoretical limits, weakening practical impact.  
- Section 2.4 is overly detailed and could be condensed; Section 3.1 is largely redundant and should be shortened or removed.  
- Recommends including concise proof sketches for main theorems instead of lengthy exposition; reviewer did not check the appendix proofs.
