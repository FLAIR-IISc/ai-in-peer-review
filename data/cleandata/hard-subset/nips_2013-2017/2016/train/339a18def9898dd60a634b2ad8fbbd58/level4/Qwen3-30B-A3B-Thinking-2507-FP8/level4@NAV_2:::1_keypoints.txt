- Paper formulates crowdsourcing performance limits using an information-theoretic rate–distortion framework.  
- Models workers as parallel noisy channels (M-ary symmetric channel and spammer-hammer channel).  
- Treats taskmaster query design and crowdsourcer inference as a joint source–channel coding scheme.  
- Derives budget–fidelity tradeoff bounds for unknown worker skills (SL-UK) and known skills (SL-CS).  
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) to query k items at once and exploit error-correcting properties.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical results with formal theorems and complete proofs (Theorems 2–4, Corollary 1).  
- Advances beyond prior NIPS work by shifting from algorithmic aggregation to fundamental performance limits.  
- Framework unifies various crowdsourcing scenarios and offers implications for system design (e.g., query pricing).  
- Assumes independent workers and specific channel models, which may be overly simplistic for real-world behavior.  
- Relies on large-sample (L → ∞) and iid assumptions that may not hold in practical settings.  
- Lacks empirical validation on real datasets to support theoretical bounds and kIC performance.  
- Could better contextualize and differentiate contributions relative to recent NIPS crowdsourcing papers.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen real-world connections and literature context.
