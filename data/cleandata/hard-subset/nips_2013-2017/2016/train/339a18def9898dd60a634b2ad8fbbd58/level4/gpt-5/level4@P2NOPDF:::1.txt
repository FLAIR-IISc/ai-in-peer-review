The paper models crowdsourced labeling as communication over noisy channels, treating each worker as a stochastic channel. It analyzes two worker models: (i) a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and (ii) the spammer–hammer model. Under this framing, the labeling task becomes a channel-coding problem, with the annotation budget mapped to the code rate. The authors study the trade-off between distortion and budget and examine a simple coding strategy, k-incidence coding, in which each task asks a worker to label a subset of k items simultaneously. I found the paper interesting and well-motivated; however, because the topic lies outside my core expertise, I do not have detailed technical comments.
