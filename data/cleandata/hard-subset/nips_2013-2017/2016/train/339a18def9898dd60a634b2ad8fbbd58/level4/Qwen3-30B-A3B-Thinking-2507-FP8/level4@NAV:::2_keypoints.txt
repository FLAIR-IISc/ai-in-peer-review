- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and derives fundamental asymptotic query-rate limits.  
- Item labels treated as i.i.d. source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses, worker skills as random channel states, and crowdsourcer as decoder.  
- Uses existing JSCC coding-rate results to obtain asymptotic limits and gives a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific model.  
- Paper is well written and a promising initial step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.  
- Major gap: authors apply source–channel separation (Theorems 2 and 3) without proving or citing its validity for random channel states unknown to the encoder.  
- Model limitation: ignores potential side information from original items available to encoder and/or decoder, which could improve practical realism and performance.  
- Misleading statement in Section 4.2: the lower bound for kIC decreases as k and q grow (indicating possibly better codes) but this does not constitute an improvement of the bound itself without an upper bound for comparison.  
- Suggestion: include nonasymptotic JSCC converse results (e.g., Kostina and Verdú, IEEE Trans. IT, 2013) to strengthen practical relevance.
