- Novel framework: model each worker as a noisy channel and transform crowdsourcing into a coding problem to study fundamental limits.  
- Channel models: analyzes a symmetric memoryless channel (no feedback) and a spammer/hammer channel.  
- Budget-rate mapping: maps budget onto code rate to analyze distortion versus budget tradeoffs.  
- Comprehensive analysis: includes detailed system model, query schemes, inference methods, and distortion/design analysis.  
- k-ary incidence coding: introduces and analyzes a basic coding scheme labeling subsets of k items, compared to information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Lower bounds: derives information-theoretic minimum queries per item for unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skill scenarios.  
- Dependence factors: shows required queries depend on Hamming distortion, number of response choices, and worker skill levels.  
- Numerical results: demonstrates k-ary scheme achieves high fidelity with few queries and proposes an approximate pricing rule.  
- Strengths: rigorous information-theoretic approach, technically sound, well-supported by analysis and simulations.  
- Clarity: well-written and clear presentation but uses heavy technical notation and math.  
- Originality/significance: novel contribution with important implications for crowdsourcing system design.  
- Concerns: may have limited interest outside crowdsourcing, unfamiliar terminology for non-experts, and requires high mathematical sophistication.
