The paper models crowdsourced data labeling by treating each worker as a noisy channel. Two channel models are considered: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer model. Under this perspective, the labeling problem becomes a channel-coding problem, which the authors analyze accordingly. The annotation budget is mapped to the code rate in the analysis, enabling study of the distortion–budget trade-off. The paper also examines a basic coding scheme, k-incidence coding, in which each task asks a worker to label a subset of k items at a time.

I found the paper fairly interesting, and the coding-theoretic perspective on crowdsourcing is appealing. However, as this area is outside my primary expertise, I do not have detailed technical comments.
