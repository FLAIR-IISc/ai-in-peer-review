- Derives an information-theoretic bound on the budget–fidelity trade-off for crowdsourcing using rate–distortion and channel coding; derivation is clear.
- If this is the first application of these ideas to crowdsourcing, the contribution could be meaningful.
- Novelty and practical value are uncertain: derivation seems straightforward and the bound may be loose.
- Core mismatch: classical channel coding permits flexible codebook design, but crowdsourcing likely lacks comparable freedom for constructing encoder mappings u^n.
- For the capacity bound to be tight, almost all mappings in Eq.(3) would need to be valid/implementable encoders, which seems doubtful.
- While repetition/majority voting is available, constructing richer error-correcting “codes” under realistic crowdsourcing constraints is unclear.
- Assigning the same query multiple times to the same worker breaks i.i.d. assumptions due to worker memory and strategic effects, inducing dependence.
- Crowdsourcing imposes strong structural constraints on the encoder class, so classical capacity-based bounds may substantially overestimate achievable performance.
- The paper should characterize feasible encoding/assignment schemes in Eq.(3) under realistic constraints (per-worker reuse limits, dependence, heterogeneity, task types) and analyze effects on tightness.
- Empirical comparisons with state-of-the-art aggregation/assignment methods are needed to show how tight the bound is across budget, worker quality, and task difficulty regimes.
