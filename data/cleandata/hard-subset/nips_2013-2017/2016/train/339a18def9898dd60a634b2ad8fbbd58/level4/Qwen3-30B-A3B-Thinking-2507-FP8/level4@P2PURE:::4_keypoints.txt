- Paper maps crowdsourcing task decomposition and aggregation to joint source-channel coding, deriving an information-theoretic lower bound on query budget vs. fidelity.
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding scheme and compares its performance to the lower bound under a specific channel model.
- Conceptually interesting connection between rate-distortion theory and crowdsourcing; potential to inspire further research.
- Main critique: mapping to point-to-point communication may sacrifice realism to fit existing information-theoretic results.
- Model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel parameter and assumes uniform query allocation, ignoring practical skill-based assignment.
- Channel models do not account for adversarial workers who might intentionally degrade results using task knowledge.
- Numerical results and figure labeling are unclear—Figure 2 curve labels need clarification, especially the interpretation of the k=1 case.
- Assumption that P(B(X)) is known is introduced too late (should be stated earlier).
- Figure reference errors on page 4 (Figure 1.a → 1.b, Figure 1.b → 1.c) need correction.
- Manuscript inconsistently capitalizes "Theorem," "Lemma," and "Corollary"; use lowercase when not numbered.
