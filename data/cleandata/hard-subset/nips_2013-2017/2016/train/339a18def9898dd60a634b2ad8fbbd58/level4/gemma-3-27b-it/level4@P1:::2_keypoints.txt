- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item.  
- Item labels are i.i.d.; taskmaster = encoder, crowd workers = channel uses, crowdsourcer = decoder; limits obtained via established information-theoretic results.  
- Paper provides a lower bound on the error probability of “k-ary incidence coding” (kIC) under a specific crowdsourcing model.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and a useful first step toward a JSCC framework for crowdsourcing.  
- Technical concern: Need justification (proof or citation) for applying source–channel separation when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder but possibly known at the decoder.  
- Modeling concern: Assumes taskmaster only sees labels and crowdsourcer only sees responses; omits side information (original items) that encoder/decoder may have, which could change performance and realism.  
- Clarification requested: Claim that lower bounds for kIC “improve” as k and q grow should be clarified—lower bound decreasing does not by itself prove improved code performance without an upper bound.  
- Suggestion: Include or discuss non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterizations; cite Kostina and Verdú (2013) on non-asymptotic JSCC converses.
