- Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC framework for crowdsourcing, modeling taskmaster as encoder, workers as a random-state channel, and crowdsourcer as decoder to derive asymptotic query-per-item limits.  
- Uses the source–channel separation theorem for Theorems 2 and 3 but must justify its validity when the channel state is random and unknown at the encoder (possibly known at the decoder).  
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for a specific scheme ("k-ary incidence coding") under a particular crowdsourcing/channel model.  
- Modeling restricts encoder/decoder to observing only labels and omits potential side information from original items; including side information at encoder and/or decoder would be more realistic and could change results.  
- Clarify Fig. 2 wording: saying lower bounds "improve" as k and q increase is misleading—bounds decrease (performance improves), but without matching upper bounds "improvement" is ambiguous.  
- Recommend addressing nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) limits — cite/consider Kostina & Verdú (2013) on lossy JSCC finite-blocklength converses.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and a promising first step toward applying JSCC to crowdsourcing.
