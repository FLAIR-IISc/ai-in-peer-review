- Paper models crowdsourcing as joint source-channel coding (JSCC) with a random channel state to derive asymptotic limits on queries per item.  
- Specific setup: i.i.d. item labels, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses, worker skill as channel state, crowdsourcer as decoder; state unknown to encoder and possibly to decoder.  
- Uses existing JSCC coding-rate results to establish fundamental limits and gives a lower bound on error probability for a k-ary incidence coding (kIC) scheme.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and introduces a novel JSCC-based modeling approach to crowdsourcing.  
- Major technical concern: applying the source–channel separation theorem for channels with random state unknown to the encoder needs a formal proof or citation to justify validity.  
- Model limitation: assumes encoder/decoder see only labels, ignoring side information (original items) that—especially at the decoder—could significantly improve performance.  
- Misleading language: stating kIC lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow is incorrect without an accompanying upper bound or tightness argument.  
- Suggested extension: include non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) converses; cite Kostina & Verdú (2013) for finite-blocklength JSCC results.
