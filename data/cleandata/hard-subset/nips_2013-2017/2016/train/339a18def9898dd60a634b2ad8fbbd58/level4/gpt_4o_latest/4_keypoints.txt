- Studies a crowdsourcing framework that decomposes a large task into subtasks, assigns them to workers of varying skill/reliability, and aggregates responses to achieve target fidelity.
- Defines budget as total number of queries and analyzes the budget–fidelity trade-off by mapping the system to joint source–channel coding.
- Derives an information-theoretic lower bound on queries required for a given fidelity that is independent of specific query or aggregation schemes.
- Proposes and analyzes a specific taskmaster scheme (k-ary incidence coding) and compares its performance to the derived lower bound for a particular channel model.
- Reviewer finds the rate–distortion analogy intriguing and potentially inspiring for future work.
- Reviewer questions how accurately the model reflects real-world crowdsourcing, noting the mapping to point-to-point communication may be overly restrictive.
- Concern: paper conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel model and assumes equal query allocation to all workers, unlike real systems with ratings and adaptive assignment.
- Concern: realism of channel models is unclear, especially for workers with partial knowledge who may act adversarially to degrade fidelity.
- Numerical results need clearer explanations and better figure labeling (e.g., clarify meaning of k=1 and improve Fig. 2 labels).
- Specific technical/notation issues: assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) (p.6, l.246) should be stated earlier.
- Typographical/figure references: incorrect figure labels on p.4, l.135 (Figure 1.a→1.b, 1.b→1.c).
- Presentation issue: use of “Theorem/Lemma/Corollary” without numbering—suggest numbering or using lowercase terms for unnumbered statements.
