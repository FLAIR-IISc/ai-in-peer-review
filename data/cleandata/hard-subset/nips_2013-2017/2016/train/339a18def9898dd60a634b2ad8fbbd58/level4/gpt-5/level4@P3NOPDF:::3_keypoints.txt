- Novel, principled framing: casts aggregation as a rate–distortion-like problem with explicit fidelity constraints, yielding clear theoretical limits.  
- Generality: framework can model heterogeneous workers and multiple aggregation strategies.  
- Potential impact: results could guide practical redundancy and budget allocation if translated into design guidelines.  
- Accessibility issue: exposition is too technical and relies on channels/codes/distortions, obscuring crowdsourcing implications for non-information-theory readers.  
- Limited high-level intuition: qualitative insights are buried; the paper needs concise takeaways for practitioners.  
- Missing incentives connection: insufficient discussion of mechanism design and incentive-aware crowdsourcing literature (peer prediction, payments, calibration).  
- Restate theorems in crowdsourcing terms: map “distortion” to task error and “rate” to labels/dollars per task.  
- Add a succinct design recipe: actionable steps for computing redundancy, adaptive labeling, and stopping rules under budget constraints.  
- Clarify model/terminology early: precisely define and motivate roles like “crowdsourcer” vs. “taskmaster.”  
- Provide a running example: walk through a simple binary labeling scenario with two worker classes to illustrate predictions.  
- Expand on robustness: discuss sensitivity to misspecified reliabilities, adversarial behavior, and task difficulty heterogeneity, and suggest estimation methods.  
- Strengthen related work: cite and contrast with incentive-compatible and mechanism-design approaches that blend incentives with aggregation.  
- Improve figures/captions: label axes/parameters, explain curves, and tie visuals to specific theorems and takeaways.  
- Key open questions: must the tradeoff assume known worker reliabilities or can it be learned online; how do limits change with adaptive task assignment; can strategic, payment-sensitive workers be incorporated?  
- Minor edits: grammatical fixes (Line 84), clarify Line 106 distinction, fix typo Line 122, rewrite Lines 275–276 for clarity, and expand Figure 2 caption.  
- Overall: promising and potentially influential theoretical contribution, but requires clearer exposition, practical translation, and integration with incentives work for greater impact; acceptance recommended pending substantial revisions.
