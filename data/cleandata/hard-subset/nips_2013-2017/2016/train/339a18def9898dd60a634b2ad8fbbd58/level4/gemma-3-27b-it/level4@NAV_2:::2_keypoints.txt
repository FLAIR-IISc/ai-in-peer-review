- Paper models crowdsourcing as a JSCC problem with random channel state (worker skill) and derives asymptotic rate–distortion limits, considering known/unknown skills and analyzing k-ary incidence coding (kIC) with an error-probability lower bound.  
- The use of the source–channel separation theorem for JSCC with random channel state unknown at the encoder needs explicit justification (proof or citation).  
- The model omits side information (original data) at the taskmaster/crowdsourcer; including it could tighten bounds and should be discussed.  
- Clarify Section 4.2: state precisely that the kIC lower bound decreases as k and q increase (implying potentially better performance), rather than saying the bound “improves.”  
- Asymptotic analysis is insightful but limited in practice; recommend exploring nonasymptotic/finite-blocklength JSCC results (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel principled information-theoretic framework, sound technical analysis using coding theory, practical design insights, and a concrete kIC example.  
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive model excluding side information, limited practical relevance of asymptotic results, and insufficient articulation of the separation-theorem justification.  
- Overall recommendation: accept pending revisions addressing the separation-theorem justification, inclusion/discussion of side information, and clarification of kIC performance claims.  
- Reference suggested for finite-blocklength analysis: Kostina & Verdú (2013).
