- Paper models digital crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic framework to derive lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.
- Workers are modeled as symmetric discrete memoryless channels (SDMCs) and as spammer-hammer channels (SHC(q)) to capture worker errors and spammers.
- Coding scheme is assumed non-adaptive.
- Analysis covers two crowdsourcer knowledge cases: full knowledge of worker skill levels and no knowledge of worker skills.
- Taskmaster is assumed unaware of worker skills, so each worker receives the same number of tasks.
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum required rate for k-ary incidence coding with an SHC(q) worker pool.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Lacks experimental or simulation results to compare derived limits with current practical schemes (e.g., AMT).
- Sections 2.4 and 3.1 are considered expendable or could be shortened.
- Including proof sketches for the theorems would be more valuable than the removed sections.
- Claims novelty as the first information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing; reviewer finds this plausible and not previously explored.
- Results are useful for determining minimum query rates and informing pricing of crowdsourcing projects.
- Reviewer did not check the appendix and therefore cannot comment on the correctness of the formal proofs.
