The paper addresses the problem of aggregating worker output in crowdsourcing using the language and tools of information theory, providing fundamental limits on the budget-fidelity tradeoff. Although I did not fully grasp the paper's content, I found the insights and model presented to be interesting and potentially useful for designing crowdsourcing aggregation techniques.

The high-level intuition and insights stemming from these results, such as those at the end of Section 3, could be highlighted more prominently. Additionally, a conclusion summarizing the key takeaways would be beneficial, despite the space constraints. The authors' efforts to make the paper accessible to those unfamiliar with information theory, like myself, are appreciated. However, I still found the paper challenging to follow.

To improve clarity, I recommend rephrasing many of the results in terms of the crowdsourcing application itself, rather than retaining the language of channels, codes, and distortion. This would facilitate a more intuitive understanding of the concepts presented.

In the related work section, it would be beneficial to mention the alternate line of research on robust economic incentives for workers, often referred to as "crowdsourcing mechanism" or "mechanism design for crowdsourcing." This body of work also explores varying worker abilities and the combination of incentives with sophisticated aggregation methods.

Several minor corrections and suggestions for improvement are noted. On line 84, "is reviewed in" should be replaced with "are reviewed in." The distinction between the crowdsourcer and taskmaster, particularly in the initial explanation, was unclear, possibly due to the term "crowdsourcing" being a portmanteau of "crowd" and "outsourcing." A minor typo is present on line 122, where "A A(X1,X2)" should be corrected. The text on lines 275-276 was difficult to parse, and a rephrasing may be necessary. Figure 2 could benefit from a more detailed caption to enhance its clarity.

Overall, the paper presents an interesting and potentially useful perspective on crowdsourcing aggregation techniques. With some revisions to improve clarity and accessibility, it has the potential to make a more significant impact on the field.
