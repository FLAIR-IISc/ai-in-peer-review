This paper investigates the problem of data labeling using crowdsourcing, framing it as a coding problem over noisy channels. The authors model individual workers as either symmetric memoryless channels without feedback or as spammers/hammerheads, and analyze the resulting communication problem. They connect the crowdsourcing budget to the rate of the employed code, focusing on the trade-offs between distortion and budget. A specific coding scheme, k-incidence coding – where workers label subsets of *k* items – is also examined. I found the work to be generally interesting; however, due to its location outside my primary research area, I am unable to provide a detailed technical assessment.
