- Presents an information-theoretic framework giving lower bounds on queries per task to meet a target error probability.  
- Models workers as parallel independent channels using symmetric DMC and spammer-hammer SHC(q) to capture errors and spammers.  
- Assumes nonadaptive coding and analyzes two scenarios: crowdsourcer knows all worker skills vs. no skill information.  
- Assumes the taskmaster is unaware of skills, leading to uniform task assignment.  
- Derives a necessary condition for minimum required rate for k-ary incidence coding with an SHC(q) worker pool.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Novel, pioneering application of information theory to crowdsourcing with practical implications for project pricing.  
- Lacks experimental or simulation comparisons to existing practical schemes (e.g., AMT).  
- Recommends condensing Sections 2.4 and 3.1 and replacing detailed exposition with theorem proof sketches.  
- Appendix not reviewed, so proof validity cannot be confirmed.
