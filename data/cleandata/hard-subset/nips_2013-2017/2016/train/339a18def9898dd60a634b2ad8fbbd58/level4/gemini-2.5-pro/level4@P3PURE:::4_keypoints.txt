- Models crowdsourcing as decomposing a large inference task into smaller queries answered by unreliable workers, with the goal of guaranteeing a target fidelity.  
- Defines the system budget as the total number of queries and studies the trade-off between budget and fidelity.  
- Maps the crowdsourcing problem to joint source-channel coding and uses information-theoretic results to derive a budget lower bound for a desired fidelity that is independent of specific query/aggregation strategies.  
- Proposes and analyzes a specific query scheme, "k-ary incidence coding," and compares its performance to the information-theoretic lower bound for a particular channel model.  
- Novel contribution: drawing a parallel between the rate-distortion framework and crowdsourcing with unreliable workers, which could spur further information-theoretic work in crowdsourcing.  
- Concern: the model conflates worker reliability and skill into one channel parameter and assumes equal query allocation to all workers, unlike practical systems that use worker ratings and adaptive allocation.  
- Concern: proposed channel models for worker unreliability need more justification and do not consider adversarial workers or how the framework would handle them.  
- Minor: explanation of numerical results is unclear and the curve labeling in Figure 2 needs improvement.  
- Minor: unclear interpretation of the k=1 case—does it correspond to a worker directly providing an item's label?  
- Minor: the critical assumption that P(B(X)) is known should be stated earlier, in the problem formulation.  
- Minor: apparent incorrect figure references on page 4 ("Figure 1.a" and "Figure 1.b" seem swapped).  
- Minor: inconsistent capitalization of "Theorem", "Lemma", and "Corollary" — use lowercase in general references.
