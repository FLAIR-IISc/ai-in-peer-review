- Presents an information-theoretic bound on budget-fidelity tradeoff for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion and channel coding; derivation is sound and potentially valuable if novel.  
- Novelty and practical significance are questionable; derivation seems straightforward and the bound may be excessively loose.  
- Major concern: crowdsourcing differs from classical channel coding in allowable encoding designs, so the assumed freedom to choose u^n may not hold.  
- Equation (3) implies nearly all mappings from B(X)^n to U^(∑m_i) are valid codebooks, but many such mappings may be unimplementable in crowdsourcing.  
- Repetition coding (majority voting) is plausible, but it is unclear how to design u^n to enable stronger error correction beyond repetition.  
- Cannot generally query the same worker repeatedly with the same task without violating the i.i.d. assumptions in (1) and (2); repeated queries likely produce correlated responses.  
- More broadly, crowdsourcing constraints (reflected in (3)) are likely far more restrictive than unconstrained encoding in channel coding, which can loosen the bound.  
- A comparison between the derived bound and performance of state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods is needed to assess tightness and practical value.
