- Studies crowdsourcing where a taskmaster decomposes a large task into queries, aggregating unreliable workers’ responses to guarantee a target fidelity under a budget constraint.  
- Maps the crowdsourcing problem to a joint source–channel coding framework, yielding an information-theoretic lower bound on required budget independent of query/decoder design.  
- Proposes and analyzes k-ary incidence coding at the taskmaster and compares its performance to the derived lower bound under a specific channel model.  
- The rate–distortion/information-theory parallel is interesting and could encourage further theoretical work in crowdsourcing.  
- Concern that the modeling choices (strict point-to-point mapping) may not capture real crowdsourcing systems with sufficient realism.  
- Worker heterogeneity issue: the paper merges skill and reliability into a single channel and assumes equal query allocation; needs extension for rating-informed or adaptive/targeted assignment.  
- Channel realism and adversaries: unclear if chosen channel models capture real worker unreliability and adversarial behavior; request extension to adversarial or semi-adversarial models.  
- Numerical results require clearer explanation and better labeling of curves in Figure 2.  
- Clarify the k = 1 case: does it represent single-item label queries and how it fits within k-ary incidence coding.  
- State the assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) earlier in the paper (currently first used on p.6, line 246).  
- Correct figure references on p.4 (swap “Figure 1.a”→“Figure 1.b” and “Figure 1.b”→“Figure 1.c”).  
- Use lowercase “theorem/lemma/corollary” when not referring to numbered results instead of capitalizing them.  
- Overall: mapping and comparisons are appealing; addressing realism of worker models, allocation policies, adversarial scenarios, and presentation issues would substantially strengthen the paper.
