- Maps crowdsourcing to joint source-channel coding and models taskmaster as transmitter and crowdsourcer as receiver.
- Derives an information-theoretic lower bound on query budget for a target fidelity that is scheme-independent.
- Analyzes a specific taskmaster scheme (k-ary incidence coding) and compares its performance to the lower bound for a given channel model.
- Results are interesting and could motivate further information-theoretic study of crowdsourcing.
- Concern: model merges worker reliability and skill into a single channel parameter, ignoring individual worker ratings/skills.
- Concern: assumes equal number of queries allocated to all workers regardless of skill, which is unrealistic.
- Concern: channel models do not capture adversarial or informed workers who may intentionally degrade fidelity.
- Request: authors should discuss model limitations and possible realistic extensions.
- Concern: numerical results lack clear explanation; Fig. 2 curve labeling should be improved, especially the k=1 case.
- Issue: assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) is not stated early enough in the paper.
- Presentation: repeatedly uses "Theorem", "Lemma", and "Corollary" without numbering; suggests using lowercase or numbering appropriately.
- Strengths reiterated: novel approach, useful lower bound, potential significance for system design.
- Recommendation: paper is interesting and potentially publishable pending addressing realism concerns and improving clarity of results and figures.
