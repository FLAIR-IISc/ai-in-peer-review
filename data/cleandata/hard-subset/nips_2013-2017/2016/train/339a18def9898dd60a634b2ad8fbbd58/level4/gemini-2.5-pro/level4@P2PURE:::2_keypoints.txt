- Paper introduces a novel JSCC perspective on crowdsourcing, deriving asymptotic limits and a lower bound for k-ary incidence coding (kIC).
- Overall well-written and interesting, but several issues weaken the contribution.
- Major: Focus on asymptotic characterization limits practical utility; authors should consider non-asymptotic (finite blocklength) converses (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013).
- Major: Model ignores that taskmaster/crowdsourcer often have access to item data (side information), which could change limits if available at decoder or both ends.
- Technical: Theorems 2 and 3 use source–channel separation for a channel with random state unknown at the encoder without justification; a proof or citation is needed.
- Technical: Statement that kIC lower bounds "improve" as k and q grow is ambiguous—lower bound reduction doesn't imply the bound is tighter without an upper bound for comparison.
