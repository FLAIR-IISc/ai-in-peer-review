- Paper gives a well-derived information-theoretic bound on budget–fidelity tradeoff for crowdsourcing using rate–distortion and channel coding theorems.  
- If this is the first application of these theories to crowdsourcing, it could be a meaningful contribution.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable since the derivation is relatively straightforward and the bound may be very loose.  
- Key concern: unlike channel coding, crowdsourcing lacks freedom to design arbitrary codebooks/inputs u^n, so the channel-capacity-based bound may not be tight.  
- Repetition (majority-vote) is feasible, but it’s unclear how to design u^n to achieve stronger error correction in crowdsourcing.  
- Constraint example: cannot assign the same query repeatedly to the same worker without violating the iid assumptions, unlike repeated uses of an iid communication channel.  
- More broadly, practical constraints on feasible encoding schemes (eq. (3)) likely make the theoretical bound loose.  
- Empirical comparison to state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods and the derived bound is recommended.  
- Minor issues: clarify minimization in (8) and expand the proof of Theorem 4.  
- Minor issues: explain sign/relationship involving ln(1−q) and whether ε_S > ε̂ in Theorem 4.  
- Minor issues: justify or cite reason for H_M(E(ε)) in equation (17).
