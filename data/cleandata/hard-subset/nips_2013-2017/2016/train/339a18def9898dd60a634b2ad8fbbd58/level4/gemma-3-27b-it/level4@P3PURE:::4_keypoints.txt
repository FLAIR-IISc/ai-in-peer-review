- Paper maps crowdsourcing with unreliable workers to joint source-channel coding and derives an information-theoretic lower bound on queries required for target fidelity.  
- The bound is notable for being independent of specific query designs or processing algorithms.  
- Authors analyze a specific query scheme, k-ary incidence coding, and compare its performance to the derived lower bound under a particular channel model.  
- Reviewer praises the insight of applying rate-distortion and information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing problems.  
- Reviewer questions realism of the model due to simplifying assumptions made to fit a point-to-point communication framework.  
- Concern that worker reliability and skill are conflated into a single channel model, reducing realism.  
- Issue with assumption that the taskmaster allocates the same number of queries to all workers regardless of skill/rating.  
- Channel models may not capture adversarial or strategically malicious workers who intentionally degrade fidelity.  
- Numerical results explanation is unclear and Figure 2 curve labeling needs improvement.  
- Ambiguity about the meaning of the k=1 case (whether it denotes direct label provision versus comparisons).  
- Statement on page 6 about knowledge of P(B(X)) is introduced too late and should appear earlier.  
- Incorrect figure references on page 4 (Figure 1.a→1.b and Figure 1.b→1.c).  
- Repeated use of capitalized ‘Theorem,’ ‘Lemma,’ and ‘Corollary’ without numbering is inappropriate; lowercase or numbered labels should be used.
