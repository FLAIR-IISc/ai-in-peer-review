- Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC model for crowdsourcing and derives asymptotic query-complexity bounds, including a lower bound for k-ary incidence coding; paper is well-written and a useful first step.
- Theorems 2 and 3 rely on source–channel separation without justifying its validity for JSCC with random, encoder-unknown channel states; a proof or citation is needed.
- The model may be overly restrictive by assuming the taskmaster and crowdsourcer only see labels; allowing original item data as encoder/decoder side information could change results and should be considered.
- The Fig. 2 claim that kIC “improves” with increasing k and q is misleading; clarify that the lower-bound value decreases with k and q and avoid implying improvement relative to an unavailable upper bound.
- Recommend pursuing a non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterization of crowdsourcing limits and considering recent work such as Kostina and Verdú (2013) on finite-blocklength JSCC.
