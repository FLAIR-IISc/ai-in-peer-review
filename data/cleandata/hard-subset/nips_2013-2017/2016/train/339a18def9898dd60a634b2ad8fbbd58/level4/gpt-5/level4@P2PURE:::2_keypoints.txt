- Paper casts crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and derives asymptotic query-complexity limits.  
- Mapping: item labels = i.i.d. source, taskmaster = encoder, workers = channel uses, worker skills = random channel state, crowdsourcer = decoder.  
- Uses known JSCC coding-rate results to characterize asymptotic limits and gives a lower bound on k-ary incidence coding (kIC) error under a specific model.  
- Theorems 2 and 3 invoke source–channel separation, but the paper must justify (proof or citation) that separation holds with random channel state unknown at the encoder and possibly unknown at the decoder.  
- Modeling the taskmaster/ crowdsourcer as observing only labels/worker outputs is restrictive; real settings often include side information at encoder and/or decoder which should be incorporated.  
- The claim that kIC lower bounds “improve” with larger k and q is misleading: a smaller lower bound does not by itself indicate a meaningful performance improvement absent matching upper bounds.  
- Reviewer suggests adding nonasymptotic/finite-blocklength analysis (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) since asymptotic results may be of limited practical use.  
- Overall assessment: well written and an interesting first step toward a JSCC-based treatment of crowdsourcing.
