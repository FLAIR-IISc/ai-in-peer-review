This paper models the problem of labeling data from crowdsourcing by representing each worker as a noisy channel, specifically considering two types of channels: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channel models. By framing the problem as a coding problem for the chosen channel, the authors study the resulting implications. The budget is then mapped to the rate of the code for analysis purposes. The paper explores the trade-offs between distortion and budget, and also examines a basic coding scheme, termed k-incidence coding, which corresponds to requiring crowdsourced tasks to involve labeling a subset of k items at a time. I found the paper fairly interesting, although it falls outside of my area of expertise, and therefore I do not have any detailed comments to offer.
