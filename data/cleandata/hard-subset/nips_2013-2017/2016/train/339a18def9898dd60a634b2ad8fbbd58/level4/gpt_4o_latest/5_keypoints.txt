- Models digital crowdsourcing in an information-theoretic framework and derives lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability.
- Workers modeled as independent parallel channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q).
- Analysis assumes nonadaptive coding schemes.
- Examines two crowdsourcer knowledge scenarios: full knowledge of worker skills vs no knowledge.
- Taskmaster assumed unaware of worker skills, so tasks are equally distributed among workers.
- Derives a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).
- Paper is well-organized and well-written.
- Lacks experiments or simulations comparing practical schemes (e.g., AMT) to the theoretical limits.
- Suggests condensing Section 2.4 and shortening or removing Section 3.1; adding proof sketches would increase value.
- Work appears novel; no prior information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing found by reviewer.
- Results are potentially useful for designers to determine minimal query rates and for pricing crowdsourcing projects.
- Reviewer did not check the appendix and cannot comment on the validity of the proofs.
