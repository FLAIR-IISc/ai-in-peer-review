This paper presents an information-theoretic analysis of the budget-fidelity tradeoff in label crowdsourcing, using rate-distortion theory and channel coding theorem. The authors derive a fundamental limit on the crowdsourcing rate, providing a lower bound on the number of queries per item required to achieve a certain fidelity. The paper also introduces a novel query scheme, k-ary incidence coding, and analyzes its performance.

The information-theoretic bound is well-derived, and the paper provides a thorough analysis of the crowdsourcing problem using rate-distortion theory and channel coding theorem. However, the novelty and practical value of the paper are still questionable. The derivation of the information-theoretic bound is relatively straightforward, and the bound has a risk of being very loose.

One of the main concerns is that the crowdsourcing problem has fundamental constraints that are not present in traditional channel coding problems. For example, in crowdsourcing, it is not possible to assign the same query to the same worker multiple times, as this would ruin the iid assumption. This constraint limits the design of the sequence of channel input u^n, making it unclear how to design u^n to improve the error-correcting capability. In contrast, in traditional channel coding problems, there is more freedom in designing codebooks or codewords.

Furthermore, the paper does not provide a clear comparison between the derived information-theoretic bound and the performance of state-of-the-art methods. It would be beneficial to include such a comparison to demonstrate the practical value of the bound.
