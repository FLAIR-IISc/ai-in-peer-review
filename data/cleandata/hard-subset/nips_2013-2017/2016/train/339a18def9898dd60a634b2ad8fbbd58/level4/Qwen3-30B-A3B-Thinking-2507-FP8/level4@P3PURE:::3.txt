

The paper addresses the problem of aggregating worker output in crowdsourcing using information theory, establishing fundamental limits for the budget-fidelity tradeoff. While I found the core insights and model to be highly interesting and potentially valuable for designing crowdsourcing aggregation techniques, I did not fully grasp the paper due to significant clarity issues (detailed below). The authors should emphasize the high-level intuition and actionable takeaways from their results—particularly at the end of Section 3—more prominently, as these are crucial for readers without deep information theory expertise. Although the authors made commendable efforts to make the paper accessible, the heavy reliance on channel/coding theory terminology (e.g., "distortion," "codes," "channels") rather than direct crowdsourcing application language made the results difficult to follow. I recommend restating most key results in terms of crowdsourcing practice (e.g., worker reliability, task design) to enhance accessibility.  

The related work section should cite the emerging line of research on robust economic incentives for workers (typically termed "crowdsourcing mechanisms" or "mechanism design for crowdsourcing"), which also addresses varying worker abilities and increasingly integrates with sophisticated aggregation methods.  

Minor issues:  
- Line 84: "is reviewed in" → "are reviewed in" (subject: "papers").  
- Line 106: The distinction between "crowdsourcer" and "taskmaster" remains unclear; this is confusing given "crowdsourcing" as a portmanteau of "crowd" and "outsourcing" (implying outsourcing precedes worker involvement).  
- Line 122: Correct typo: "A A(X1,X2)" → "A(A(X1,X2))".  
- Lines 275–276: "In case..." is poorly phrased and hard to parse.  
- Figure 2: The figure provides limited insight; a more detailed caption clarifying axes and key observations would be beneficial.  

Despite these challenges, the paper’s theoretical foundation is promising and merits consideration. Addressing the clarity and accessibility issues would significantly strengthen its impact.
