- Paper reframes crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem to derive query budget vs. label fidelity limits.  
- Models: true labels = i.i.d. source, taskmaster = encoder, workers = noisy channel with random state (skill), crowdsourcer = decoder.  
- Applies JSCC/source-channel separation to find asymptotic minimum queries per item for a target error rate.  
- Considers cases where worker skills are unknown to encoder and either known or unknown to decoder.  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and derives a lower bound under a spammer-hammer worker model.  
- Strength: well-written and offers a novel, rigorous information-theoretic perspective on crowdsourcing limits.  
- Major concern: use of source-channel separation for channels with random state unknown to the encoder lacks formal justification or citation.  
- Modeling limitation: assumes encoder/decoder only see labels (not item content); omitting item-side information may be unrealistic and change limits.  
- Practicality issue: asymptotic analysis may not apply to finite datasets/budgets; recommends non-asymptotic/finite-blocklength analysis (e.g., Kostina & Verdú).  
- Minor clarity: phrase “improve” for kIC lower bounds is ambiguous — should state the bound becomes smaller (better) and clarify lack of an upper bound for comparison.
