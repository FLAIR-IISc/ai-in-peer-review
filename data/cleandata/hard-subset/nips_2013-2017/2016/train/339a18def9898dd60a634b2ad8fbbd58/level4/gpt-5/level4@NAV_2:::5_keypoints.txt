- Frames digital crowdsourcing in an information-theoretic paradigm to derive lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.  
- Models workers as independent parallel channels.  
- Uses M-ary symmetric channel (MSC) to capture worker errors.  
- Uses spammer–hammer model SHC(q) where a worker is a hammer with prob. q (spammer otherwise).  
- Assumes non-adaptive coding/query policies.  
- Analyzes two settings: crowdsourcer knows all worker reliabilities vs. crowdsourcer has no reliability information.  
- Assumes taskmaster lacks knowledge of worker skills, so each worker gets the same number of tasks.  
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum rate (queries per task) for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).  
- Paper is well organized and clearly written.  
- No experiments or simulations to show how practical schemes (e.g., AMT) compare to the theoretical limits.  
- Section 2.4 should be tightened; Section 3.1 is unnecessary or should be shortened; include concise proof sketches of main theorems.  
- Work appears novel as an information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing.  
- Potentially useful in practice for determining minimum query rates and informing pricing.  
- Reviewer did not read the appendix and therefore cannot comment on the proofs.
