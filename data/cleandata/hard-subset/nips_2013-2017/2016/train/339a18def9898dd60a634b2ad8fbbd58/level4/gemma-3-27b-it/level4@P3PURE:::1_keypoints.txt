Models crowdsourcing workers as noisy channels to address the data labeling problem.  
Considers two channel models: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer channel.  
Reframes labeling as a coding problem and analyzes the relationship between crowdsourcing budget and achievable coding rate.  
Investigates distortion–budget tradeoffs for the proposed framework.  
Proposes k-incidence coding, where workers label subsets of k items at a time.
