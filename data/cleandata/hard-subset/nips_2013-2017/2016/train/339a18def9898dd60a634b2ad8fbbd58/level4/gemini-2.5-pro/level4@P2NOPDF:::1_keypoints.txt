- Models crowdsourced workers as noisy channels and frames labeling as a coding-theory problem.
- Studies two worker models: symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer model.
- Maps crowdsourcing financial budget to code rate, recasting aggregation as a coding-rate problem.
- Centers on the tradeoff between distortion (labeling error) and budget.
- Proposes k-incidence coding: assigning workers to label subsets of k items.
- Analyzes the distortion–budget tradeoff for the proposed coding scheme.
- Reviewer finds the crowdsourcing–information-theory connection valuable and the formulation well-motivated.
- Reviewer cannot judge technical novelty in coding theory but considers the application and framework sound.
