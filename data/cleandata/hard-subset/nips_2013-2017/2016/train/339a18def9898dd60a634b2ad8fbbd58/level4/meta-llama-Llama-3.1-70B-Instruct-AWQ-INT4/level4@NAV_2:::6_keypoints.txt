- Derives an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion theory and channel coding; derivation appears correct.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable — approach may be straightforward and not clearly the first application.  
- Main concern: crowdsourcing lacks the freedom to design arbitrary codebooks/mappings like in channel coding, so the coding analogy may not hold.  
- It's unclear that mappings in Eq.(3) are implementable; if many mappings are infeasible, the capacity-based bound may be loose.  
- Key difference: you cannot assign the same query to the same worker repeatedly (breaks iid), unlike repeated uses of an iid communication channel.  
- Therefore important constraints on allowable encodings likely exist, increasing risk that the bound is overly optimistic/loose.  
- Recommendation: empirically compare the derived bound to state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods.
