- Paper gives a well-derived information-theoretic bound for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion and channel coding; technically solid and potentially novel if first application.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable: derivation is straightforward and the bound may be very loose.  
- Key concern: unlike channel coding, crowdsourcing lacks freedom to design input sequence u^n, so the channel-capacity-style bound may not be achievable.  
- Simple schemes (e.g., repetition code with majority voting) are possible, but it is unclear how to design u^n to improve error-correcting capability beyond trivial schemes.  
- Important structural constraints (e.g., cannot query the same worker repeatedly without breaking iid assumptions) differentiate crowdsourcing from standard channel models and may invalidate encoding assumptions.  
- Due to those constraints, the set of feasible encoding schemes is restricted and the proposed bound risks being loose in practice.  
- Recommendation: compare the derived information-theoretic bound with state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods to assess tightness and practical relevance.
