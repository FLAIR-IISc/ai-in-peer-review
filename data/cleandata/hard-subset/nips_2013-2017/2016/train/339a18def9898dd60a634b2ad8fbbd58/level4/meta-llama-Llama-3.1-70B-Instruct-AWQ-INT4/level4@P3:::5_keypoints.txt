- Proposes a novel information-theoretic framework to analyze budget–fidelity trade-offs in label crowdsourcing.  
- Models digital crowdsourcing as a human-in-the-loop computation problem.  
- Derives lower bounds on the number of queries per task to achieve a target error probability.  
- Considers two scenarios: crowdsourcer knows worker skills and crowdsourcer does not.  
- Paper is well-organized and well-written, making the arguments easy to follow.  
- Use of information-theoretic tools is innovative and provides a fresh perspective.  
- Analysis is rigorous and thorough, with clear derivations of fundamental limits.  
- Provides a fundamental performance limit that can benchmark and guide crowdsourcing algorithm design.  
- Discusses practical implications for pricing crowdsourcing projects.  
- Lacks experimental or simulation results to validate how current schemes compare to the derived limits.  
- Recommends shortening or removing less valuable sections (e.g., Sec. 2.4 and 3.1) and adding proof sketches instead.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and novel contribution; rating 8/10; accept with minor revisions.
