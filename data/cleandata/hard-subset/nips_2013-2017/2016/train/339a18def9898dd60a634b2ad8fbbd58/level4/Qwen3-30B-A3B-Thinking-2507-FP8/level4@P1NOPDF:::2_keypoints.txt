- Proposes a JSCC information-theoretic framework for crowdsourcing, modeling worker skills as a random channel state unknown to the encoder and deriving asymptotic query complexity bounds plus a lower bound for k-ary incidence coding (kIC).

- Major concern 1: The paper invokes source–channel separation (Theorems 2–3) without justifying its validity when the channel state is unknown at the encoder; an explicit citation or proof for this extension is required.

- Major concern 2: The model omits side information (e.g., images/text) available to taskmaster and/or crowdsourcer; including such side information can reduce required query rates and should be discussed or incorporated.

- Major concern 3: Section 4.2 misstates results by saying lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow; in fact the lower bound value decreases (becomes looser) even if actual code performance may improve—rephrase to avoid confusion.

- Additional note: The asymptotic focus limits practical relevance; authors should consider nonasymptotic/finite-blocklength JSCC results (e.g., Kostina & Verdu 2013) for tighter, practical bounds.

- Summary: A promising JSCC viewpoint on crowdsourcing, but needs stronger theoretical justification (separation, side information) and clearer exposition of bounds to be convincing.
