- Models crowdsourcing where a taskmaster breaks a large inference task into smaller queries for a heterogeneous, potentially unreliable crowd.  
- Objective is the trade-off between total queries (budget) and final outcome fidelity (goodness) under worker inaccuracies.  
- Maps crowdsourcing to joint source–channel coding: taskmaster as transmitter, crowdsourcer as decoder, unreliable workers as communication channel.  
- Derives an information-theoretic lower bound on the budget required for a target fidelity that is independent of specific query-design or decoding schemes.  
- Analyzes a specific scheme (k-ary incidence coding) and compares its performance to the information-theoretic lower bound for a chosen channel model.  
- The rate-distortion / information-theory parallel is interesting and may inspire further theoretical study of crowdsourcing.  
- Concern: worker skill and reliability are merged into a single channel model, while real systems often have worker ratings and nonuniform allocation.  
- Concern: channel models may not realistically capture adversarial or informed workers who could intentionally degrade fidelity.
