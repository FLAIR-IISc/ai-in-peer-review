- Models crowdsourcing workers as noisy channels to formalize labeling errors.
- Considers two channel types: symmetric memoryless channel without feedback, and spammer/hammer channel.
- Frames crowdsourcing as a coding problem to analyze distortion versus budget tradeoffs.
- Maps budget to code rate for analytical study of the budget–distortion relationship.
- Proposes k-incidence coding: tasks require labeling a subset of k items at a time.
