- Derives an information-theoretic bound on budget–fidelity tradeoff in crowdsourcing using rate–distortion and channel coding; derivation is technically sound and could be meaningful if novel.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable: derivation is straightforward and the bound may be overly conservative due to unaddressed practical constraints.  
- Fundamental mismatch between channel coding and crowdsourcing: codebook freedoms in channel coding (reusing identical queries) violate crowdsourcing iid assumptions because workers will likely repeat answers.  
- Repetition coding with majority voting is feasible, but it’s unclear how to design input sequence u^n to achieve error correction beyond trivial repetition.  
- The set of permissible encoding schemes is far more constrained in crowdsourcing than in communication, e.g., mappings from B(X)^n to U^{∑m_i} must avoid repeated queries per worker.  
- These practical encoding constraints likely make the proposed channel-capacity-based bound loose; the paper should explicitly analyze their effect on tightness.  
- The paper needs empirical validation: compare the derived bound to performance of state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods (e.g., Dawid–Skene, Bayesian approaches).  
- Minor: In Eq. (8), the minimization should be over C, C' satisfying E[d(Z, Ẑ)] ≤ D^t.  
- Minor: Proof of Theorem 4 needs more detail on handling ln(1−q)<0 and clarification/justification of the condition ε_S > ħε.  
- Minor: In Eq. (17), the term H_M(E(ε)) requires explanation or citation to prior entropy-bound work.
