- Models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state, mapping items, taskmaster, and workers to source, encoder, and channel.  
- Uses existing JSCC coding-rate results to derive fundamental limits on asymptotic number of queries per item.  
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific crowdsourcing model.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, consistent mathematical notation and an intuitive model.  
- Modeling labels as i.i.d. and worker skills as random channel states is reasonable and intuitive.  
- Primary technical concern: authors rely on source-channel separation theorems without proving or citing validity when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder.  
- Model may be too restrictive by ignoring possible side information (original items) available to the taskmaster and crowdsourcer; side information should be considered.  
- The statement that kIC lower bounds “improve” with larger k and q is misleading; the bound becomes smaller, indicating potential performance gains, not improvement of the bound itself.  
- Contribution is novel in applying information-theoretic limits to crowdsourcing.  
- Results are significant for designing and optimizing crowdsourcing systems, giving fundamental performance limits.  
- Quality of work is high, leveraging existing JSCC results effectively to make a significant contribution.  
- Recommendation: paper is a good start but should address the separation-theorem justification and incorporate or discuss side information to strengthen realism.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
