- Models crowdsourced labeling as communication over noisy channels, treating each worker as a noisy channel.  
- Considers two worker models: symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer model.  
- Formulates labeling as a coding problem and maps annotation budget to code rate for theoretical analysis.  
- Studies the trade-off between distortion (label error) and annotation budget.  
- Proposes and analyzes a basic coding scheme, k-incidence coding, where each task requires labeling a subset of k items at once.
