- Models crowdsourcing as JSCC with random channel states and derives asymptotic query-complexity limits, including a lower bound on k-ary incidence coding error.  
- Well-written paper and a promising initial application of JSCC to crowdsourcing.  
- Use of the source–channel separation theorem in Theorems 2–3 needs justification (proof or citation) when the channel state is random and unknown to the encoder.  
- Model unrealistically restricts the taskmaster and crowdsourcer to observing only labels/responses; should incorporate possible side information (items) at encoder and/or decoder.  
- Interpretation of Section 4.2/Fig.2 is ambiguous: lower bound values decrease with k and q, but calling this an “improvement” is misleading without an upper bound.  
- Recommend pursuing nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterizations; consult Kostina & Verdú (2013) for nonasymptotic JSCC converses.
