- Contribution: Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC model for crowdsourcing (labels as source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel with random skill-state, crowdsourcer as decoder) and derives asymptotic query-complexity limits plus a k-ary incidence coding (kIC) error lower bound; clearly written and promising.

- Separation justification: Theorems 2–3 rely on source–channel separation despite a random channel state unknown at the encoder (possibly known at decoder); the paper must explicitly justify or cite that separation holds in this state-dependent setting.

- Missing side information: The model omits original-item side information (e.g., images/text) available to encoder/decoder in practice; decoder or shared side information can change limits and should be incorporated or discussed.

- kIC lower-bound wording: Saying kIC bounds “improve” as k and q increase is misleading—bounds merely decrease; without matching upper bounds this should be phrased to avoid overinterpreting code performance.

- Finite-blocklength relevance: Recommend connecting asymptotic results to finite-blocklength JSCC converses (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) to provide nonasymptotic, practical accuracy–query tradeoffs and increase impact.
