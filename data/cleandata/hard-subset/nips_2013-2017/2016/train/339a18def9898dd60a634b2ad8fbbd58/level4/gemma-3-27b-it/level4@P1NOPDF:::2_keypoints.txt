- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state to derive asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item.  
- Authors leverage existing JSCC coding-rate results and provide asymptotic bounds (Theorems 2 and 3).  
- Paper also gives a lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific crowdsourcing/channel model.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and a valuable first step toward JSCC-based crowdsourcing modeling.  
- Technical concern 1: authors must justify or prove the applicability of the source–channel separation theorem when the channel has a random state unknown to the encoder but possibly known to the decoder, or cite an appropriate result.  
- Technical concern 2: the encoder/decoder access model is restrictive—omitting possible side information (original items) at encoder and/or decoder; including side information could change performance and should be considered.  
- Technical concern 3: wording in Section 4.2 is misleading—showing a decreasing lower bound as k and q grow does not by itself imply an “improved” bound without corresponding upper bounds for comparison.  
- Edited/added comment: recommend investigating nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) JSCC converses, e.g., Kostina and Verdú (2013), since nonasymptotic results are more practically relevant.
