- Presents an information-theoretic (rate-distortion/channel coding) bound on the budget–fidelity tradeoff in crowdsourcing; potentially valuable if genuinely first application.  
- Novelty and practical relevance are questionable: derivation seems straightforward and the bound may be loose in real settings.  
- Core concern: channel coding concepts may not apply because crowdsourcing severely constrains the design space for codebooks (mappings in equation (3)).  
- Authors may be implicitly assuming many valid mappings from B(X)^n to U^(∑m_i); if false, the bound will be overly optimistic.  
- Repetition-coding analogy breaks down: repeating the same query to the same worker violates i.i.d. assumptions, so redundancy may not improve error correction as in channel coding.  
- General crowdsourcing constraints (unlike unconstrained mapping {0,1}^{nR}→{0,1}^n) likely make the proposed bound loose; need justification for applying channel-coding principles under these constraints.  
- Key recommendation: empirically compare the information-theoretic bound to state-of-the-art crowdsourcing algorithms to assess tightness and practical value.
