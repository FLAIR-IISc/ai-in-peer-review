This paper addresses the problem of data labeling via crowdsourcing by framing it as a channel coding problem. The authors model each worker as a noisy channel, specifically considering two variants: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer model. This approach allows the analysis to map the available budget to the rate of the code. The paper investigates the resulting trade-off between distortion and budget and also studies a basic coding scheme, termed "k-incidence coding," which corresponds to tasking workers with labeling a subset of k items at a time.

While I found the paper fairly interesting, it falls outside my primary area of expertise, and I am therefore unable to provide detailed technical comments.
