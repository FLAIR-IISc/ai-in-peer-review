This paper addresses the problem of crowdsourced data labeling by modeling each worker as a noisy channel. The authors consider two types of channels: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer model. This formulation transforms the labeling task into a coding problem, where the crowdsourcing budget is mapped to the rate of the code for the analysis.

The paper investigates the trade-off between distortion and budget and introduces a basic coding scheme, termed 'k-incidence coding,' which requires crowdsourced tasks to involve labeling a subset of k items at a time.
