The paper frames the problem of labeling data from crowdsourcing. Each worker is modeled as a noisy channel (two channel types are considered: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer model). The problem is thus cast as a coding problem for the chosen channel, which the authors analyze. The budget is mapped to the code rate for the purposes of this analysis. The paper examines distortion versus budget tradeoffs and also investigates a basic coding scheme (called k-incidence coding), which corresponds to asking workers to label a subset of k items at a time. I found the paper quite interesting, but as it falls outside my area of expertise, I do not have detailed comments.
