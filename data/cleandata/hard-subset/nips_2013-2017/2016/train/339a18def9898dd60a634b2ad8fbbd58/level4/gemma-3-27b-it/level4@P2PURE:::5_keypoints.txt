- Introduces an information-theoretic framework deriving lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability.  
- Models workers as parallel, independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels for errors and the spammer-hammer channel SHC(q) for spammers.  
- Analyzes nonadaptive coding schemes under two scenarios: crowdsourcer knows worker skills vs. skills unknown.  
- Assumes the taskmaster lacks skill knowledge and assigns an equal number of tasks to each worker.  
- Derives a necessary condition linking the minimum required rate (queries per task) to k-ary incidence coding for a worker pool modeled by SHC(q).  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Major omission: no experimental evaluation or simulations to show the gap between practical schemes (e.g., AMT) and the theoretical limits.  
- Section 2.4 could be condensed.  
- Section 3.1 appears unnecessary or could be significantly shortened.  
- Recommend prioritizing proof sketches rather than lengthy detailed expansions.  
- Claims a novel information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing (no prior work known to reviewer).  
- Results are useful for designers to estimate minimum query rates and to inform project pricing.  
- Reviewer did not examine the appendix and cannot attest to the completeness of the proofs.
