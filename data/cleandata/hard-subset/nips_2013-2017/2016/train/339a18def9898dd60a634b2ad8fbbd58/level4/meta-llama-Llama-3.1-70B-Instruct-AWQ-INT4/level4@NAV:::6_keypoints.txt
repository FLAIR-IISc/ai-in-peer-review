- Derives an information-theoretic bound on budget–fidelity tradeoff for crowdsourcing via rate–distortion and channel coding; derivation is technically sound.  
- Could be significant if first such application, but originality and practical impact are uncertain because the derivation is straightforward and the bound may be loose.  
- Key concern: channel-coding assumptions (freedom to design codebooks) may not hold in crowdsourcing — unclear if mappings u^n are implementable, so channel capacity may be a loose bound.  
- Repetition codes/majority voting are feasible, but the paper does not show how to design u^n to improve error-correction beyond trivial schemes.  
- Fundamental mismatch: cannot query the same worker repeatedly (breaks iid assumptions), whereas communication channels can reuse inputs; this constraint is not modeled and may loosen the bound.  
- Empirical comparison to state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods is needed to assess the bound’s practical tightness.  
- Minor: Equation (8) minimization should explicitly be over C, C' with E[d(Z, \hat{Z})] ≤ D^t.  
- Minor: Theorem 4’s proof needs more detail; sign issue ln(1−q) < 0 creates ambiguity about whether ε_S > \hat{ε}.  
- Minor: In Eq. (17), the use/rationale of H_M(E(ε)) should be explained or supported by a citation.
