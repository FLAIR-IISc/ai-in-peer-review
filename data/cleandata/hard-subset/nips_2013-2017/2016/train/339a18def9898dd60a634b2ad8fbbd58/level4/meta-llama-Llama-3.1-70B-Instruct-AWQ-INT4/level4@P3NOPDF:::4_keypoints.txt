- Maps crowdsourcing to joint source–channel coding to study budget (queries) vs. fidelity trade-off and derives an information-theoretic lower bound independent of specific schemes.

- Proposes and analyzes k-ary incidence coding, comparing its performance to the information-theoretic lower bound for a specific channel model.

- Positive note: the rate–distortion framing of crowdsourcing with unreliable workers is interesting and may stimulate further information-theoretic work.

- Major concern: modeling merges worker skill and reliability into a single channel and assumes equal query allocation to all workers, which may not reflect real systems with worker ratings and adaptive allocation.

- Major concern: channel models may not capture realistic adversarial or partially informed workers who could deliberately degrade fidelity.
