- Proposes modeling each crowd worker as a noisy communication channel to represent labeling errors.
- Considers two channel models: symmetric memoryless channel (no feedback) and the spammer/hammer channel.
- Frames crowdsourcing labeling as a coding problem, mapping budget to code rate to study distortion–budget trade-offs.
- Analyzes optimal resource allocation under the coding framework.
- Introduces k‑incidence coding: tasks require workers to label subsets of k items simultaneously.
- Reviewer found the paper interesting but outside their expertise and offered no detailed comments.
