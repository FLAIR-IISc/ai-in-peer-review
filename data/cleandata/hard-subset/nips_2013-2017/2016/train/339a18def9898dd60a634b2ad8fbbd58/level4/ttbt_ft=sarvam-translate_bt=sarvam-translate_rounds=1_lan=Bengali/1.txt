This paper models the problem of data labeling from crowdsourcing. Each worker is modeled as a noisy channel (two types of channels are considered: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback, and spammer/hammer channels models). The problem then becomes a coding problem for studying the considered channels and the authors. The rate of the code is then mapped based on the budget analysis used. This paper considers the distortion versus budget trade-off and also studies a fundamental coding scheme (known as K-incidence coding), which is consistent with the requirement of crowdsourced tasks to label a subset of k items at a time. I found this paper quite interesting, but since it is outside my area of expertise, I have no detailed comment on it.
