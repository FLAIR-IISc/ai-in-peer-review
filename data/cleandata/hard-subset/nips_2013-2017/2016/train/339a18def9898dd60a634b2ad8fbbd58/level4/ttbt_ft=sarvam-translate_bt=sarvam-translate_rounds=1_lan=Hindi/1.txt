This paper models the problem of labeling data obtained from crowd-sourcing. Each worker is represented as a noisy channel (two types of channels are considered: symmetric memory-less channels without feedback, and spammer/hammer channel models). The problem then becomes a coding problem for the channel that the authors studied. The used budget is mapped to the rate of code for analysis. This paper considers the distortion versus budget tradeoff and also studies a basic coding scheme (called k-incidence coding), which corresponds to the requirement of labeling a subset of k items at a time of crowd-sourced tasks. I found this paper quite interesting, but since it is outside my area of expertise, there is no detailed comment on it.
