- Paper maps crowdsourcing budget–fidelity trade-off to a joint source–channel coding problem, yielding an information-theoretic lower bound on required queries.
- The mapping and bound are interesting and potentially influential for applying information theory to crowdsourcing.
- Concern: worker heterogeneity is conflated (skill vs. reliability) and the model assumes uniform query allocation across workers.
- Request: discuss incorporating worker-specific channels and non-uniform or adaptive task allocation.
- Concern: the assumed channel models may be unrealistic and do not address adversarial or strategic workers.
- Request: clarify whether and how the framework handles adversarial behavior and state the assumptions needed.
- Numerical/experimental issues: explanations and curve labeling (Fig. 2) are unclear, and the meaning/instantiation of k=1 is ambiguous.
- Presentation issue: assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) is stated late and should be declared earlier.
- Typos/mislabels: Figure 1 references are incorrect (Figure 1.a→1.b, 1.b→1.c).
- Formatting/notation: theorem/lemma/corollary are used without numbers and capitalization is inconsistent—use lowercase when not referencing a numbered result.
- Suggestion: define “queries” and “budget” precisely at the outset rather than informally.
- Overall recommendation: strengthen realism by separating skill and reliability, broaden/justify channel models (including adversaries), and clarify numerical results and key assumptions.
