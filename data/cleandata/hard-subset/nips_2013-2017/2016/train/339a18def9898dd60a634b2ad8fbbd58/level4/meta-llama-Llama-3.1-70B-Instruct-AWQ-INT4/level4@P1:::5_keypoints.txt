- Paper develops an information-theoretic model giving lower bounds on queries per task to reach a target error probability in crowdsourcing.
- Workers modeled as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) to capture errors and spammer-hammer channel (SHC) to model spammers.
- The coding/query strategy is assumed non-adaptive (fixed, no feedback-based changes).
- Two analysis cases: crowdsourcer knows worker skill levels vs. crowdsourcer unknown; taskmaster is assumed ignorant and assigns equal tasks to all workers.
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding with an SHC(q) worker pool.
- Manuscript is well-organized and clearly explains problem and approach.
- Lacks experimental or simulation results (e.g., AMT) to show how practical schemes compare to theoretical limits.
- Recommends shortening/removing general information-theory background (Section 2.4, 3.1) and adding proof sketches instead.
- Overall novel and practically useful for designers to determine minimum rates and price crowdsourcing tasks.
