The paper addresses the problem of data labeling via crowdsourcing by modeling each worker as a noisy channel. Two channel models are considered: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback, and a spammer/hammer channel. This framing transforms the labeling problem into a coding problem, which the authors then analyze, relating the crowdsourcing budget to the achievable coding rate. The work investigates distortion-budget tradeoffs and proposes a basic coding scheme, termed k-incidence coding, corresponding to requiring workers to label subsets of k items at a time. I found the paper to be fairly interesting; however, as the topic falls somewhat outside my primary area of expertise, I am unable to offer detailed technical comments.
