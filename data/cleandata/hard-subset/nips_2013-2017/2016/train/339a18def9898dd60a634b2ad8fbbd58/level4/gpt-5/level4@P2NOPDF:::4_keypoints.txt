- Paper frames crowdsourcing with unreliable workers as a joint source–channel coding problem to derive scheme-agnostic lower bounds on budget for a target fidelity.
- Strength: Elegant, insightful mapping to rate–distortion/joint source–channel coding that yields clean, general lower bounds.
- Strength: Provides a concrete instantiation and empirical comparison via k-ary incidence coding.
- Concern: Model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel and assumes equal query allocation; needs analysis for partial side information or adaptive allocation across workers.
- Concern: Channel models assume random errors; framework should consider strategic/adversarial workers, worker-specific confusion matrices, noisy-or mixtures, and worst-case/Byzantine guarantees.
- Clarify experiments: improve Fig. 2 labeling, specify which curve is which, and state exact channel parameters and mapping to theoretical quantities.
- Clarify k=1: explain whether k=1 corresponds to direct single-item labeling, define query/answer format, and specify the error model.
- Assumption issue: knowledge of P(B(X)) is a strong assumption—state it earlier, explain how it would be estimated/learned, and analyze effects of misspecification.
- Editorial: fix incorrect cross-references between Figure 1 panels.
- Editorial: use numbered Theorem/Lemma/Corollary references or switch to lowercase when not numbering; ensure consistency.
- Overall: core idea is compelling and broadly interesting, but the paper needs stronger modeling of worker heterogeneity/adversaries and clearer experiments and assumptions to increase practical impact.
