- Paper models crowdsourcing as taskmaster decomposing tasks, workers return responses, crowdsourcer aggregates to meet a fidelity goal under budget constraints.  
- Main technical contribution: maps the crowdsourcing problem to a joint source-channel coding framework, yielding an information-theoretic lower bound on required budget for a given fidelity.  
- Authors analyze a concrete k-ary incidence coding scheme and compare its performance to the derived lower bound under a specific channel model.  
- The mapping to rate-distortion and information-theoretic tools is intellectually compelling and could inspire further research.  
- Core bound and comparison to the k-ary scheme are technically sound.  
- Major concern: the model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel parameter and assumes identical query allocation to all workers, which ignores practical worker heterogeneity and adaptive allocation.  
- Channel model realism is questioned: the paper does not address workers with task knowledge or adversarial/malicious behavior that could intentionally degrade fidelity.  
- Numerical results (Section 5) lack clarity—Figure 2 labeling is ambiguous, particularly what the k=1 curve represents and whether k=1 implies direct labeling rather than pairwise comparisons.  
- Fundamental assumption that P(B(X)) is known (page 6, line 246) should be stated earlier because it underpins the analysis.  
- Presentation errors: incorrect figure references ("Figure 1.a" should be 1.b, "Figure 1.b" should be 1.c) and inconsistent use of "Theorem/Lemma/Corollary" without numbering.  
- These model simplifications and presentation issues reduce practical relevance and clarity; the reviewer urges explicit discussion of limitations and clearer explanations of numerical results.
