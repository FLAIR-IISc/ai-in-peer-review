- Paper proposes an information-theoretic framework deriving lower bounds on queries per task to meet a target error probability.  
- Workers modeled as parallel, independent channels; symmetric discrete memoryless channels represent worker errors.  
- Spammer-hammer channel (SHC(q)) used to model workers who respond randomly with probability 1−q.  
- Analysis restricted to non-adaptive coding schemes.  
- Studies two knowledge scenarios: crowdsourcer knows all worker skills, and crowdsourcer has no prior skill knowledge.  
- Taskmaster is assumed unaware of individual skills and assigns each worker the same number of tasks.  
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under an SHC(q) worker pool.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Lacks experimental validation or simulations comparing real-world schemes (e.g., Mechanical Turk) to theoretical limits.  
- Recommends streamlining section 2.4 and removing or significantly shortening section 3.1.  
- Suggests prioritizing proof sketches over detailed derivations to improve readability.  
- Approach appears novel and is likely the first information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing, with potential practical value for task design and pricing.  
- Reviewer did not check the appendix and cannot vouch for the validity of the proofs there.
