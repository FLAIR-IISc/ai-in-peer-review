- Models crowdsourced labeling by treating each worker as a noisy communication channel.
- Considers two channel types: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channels.
- Reframes the labeling task as a coding problem for the given channel models.
- Maps the labeling budget to the code rate to analyze distortion–budget tradeoffs.
- Proposes and analyzes a basic k-incidence coding scheme where tasks require labeling k items at once.
- Reviewer found the paper interesting but outside their expertise and had no detailed comments.
