The paper models the problem of labeling data from crowdsourcing by representing each worker as a noisy channel, specifically considering two types of channels: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channel models. By framing the problem in this way, the authors effectively transform it into a coding problem for the channel in question. They then map the budget to the rate of the code for analysis, allowing them to explore tradeoffs between distortion and budget. Additionally, the authors examine a basic coding scheme, referred to as k-incidence coding, which involves requiring crowdsourced tasks to label a subset of k items at a time. I found the paper to be fairly interesting, although it falls outside of my area of expertise, and therefore I do not have any detailed comments to offer.
