- Paper derives an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate–distortion theory and the channel coding theorem.  
- The derivation is technically sound and could be significant if this is the first such application to crowdsourcing.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable because the derivation is relatively straightforward and the resulting bound may be loose.  
- Fundamental mismatch: channel coding allows extensive design freedom in codebooks, but it is unclear that analogous freedom exists for selecting crowdsourcing inputs u^n.  
- If many mappings in Equation (3) are not implementable in crowdsourcing, using channel capacity may yield a loose bound.  
- Repetition-based schemes and majority voting are feasible in crowdsourcing, but it is unclear how to design u^n to achieve stronger error correction beyond repetition.  
- Key constraint: reassigning the same query to the same worker breaks the iid assumption (workers would repeat responses), unlike communication channels that permit iid reuse.  
- This per-worker constraint likely severely restricts the set of feasible encoding schemes, undermining the bound’s tightness.  
- A comparison of the derived bound to state-of-the-art practical methods is needed to demonstrate relevance.  
- Minor: In Equation (8), clarify that the minimization is over C, C' satisfying E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t.  
- Minor: Expand the proof of Theorem 4 and clarify the relationship involving ln(1−q) and whether ε_S > ε̂.  
- Minor: Justify the presence of H_M(E(ε)) in Equation (17) or cite relevant prior work.
