- Frames crowdsourced labeling as a channel coding problem, mapping labeling budget to code rate.  
- Models individual workers as noisy channels: symmetric memoryless and spammer/hammer variants.  
- Uses information-theoretic tools to analyze the distortion–budget trade-off.  
- Proposes k-incidence coding, where workers label subsets of k items simultaneously.  
- Conceptually novel and potentially fruitful connection between crowdsourcing and coding theory.  
- Considering two worker models shows attention to real-world complexity.  
- Reviewer lacks the specific expertise to evaluate technical proofs and algorithmic analyses.  
- Requests comparison of k-incidence to existing methods (e.g., majority voting, Dawid–Skene) and guidance on choosing k.  
- Questions sensitivity of results to deviations from assumed channel models and real-world representativeness.  
- Raises concerns about scalability with number of items and workers.  
- Calls for empirical validation on real or synthetic datasets to support theoretical claims.  
- Overall view: compelling core idea that needs rigorous expert review and empirical support to confirm its contribution.
