- Proposes a novel information-theoretic framework for crowdsourced labeling.
- Models workers as noisy channels (symmetric memoryless and spammer/hammer).
- Reformulates labeling as a channel coding problem to study distortion–budget trade-offs.
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC), asking workers to label k items jointly.
- Derives information-theoretic bounds on minimum queries per item for target fidelity (known/unknown worker skills).
- Theoretical models and proofs provide rigor; numerical results serve as performance benchmarks.
- kIC demonstrates better performance than traditional schemes, especially with limited queries per item.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explained, with helpful figures, tables, and up-to-date references.
- Main limitation: analysis is largely theoretical with limited real-world experiments.
- Lacks sufficient discussion of practical implications and how to design/optimize crowdsourcing systems.
- Reviewer rates paper positively (8/10) citing novelty and theoretical contributions; confidence 7/10.
