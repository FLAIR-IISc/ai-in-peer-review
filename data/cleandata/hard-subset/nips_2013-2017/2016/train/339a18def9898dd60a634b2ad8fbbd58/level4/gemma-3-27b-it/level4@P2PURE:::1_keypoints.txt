- Frames crowdsourced data labeling as a coding problem over noisy channels.  
- Models individual workers as noisy channels: symmetric memoryless channel (no feedback) or spammer/hammer channel.  
- Analyzes the problem using coding rate concepts to relate labeling performance to crowdsourcing budget.  
- Studies tradeoffs between labeling distortion (error) and budget constraints.  
- Proposes a simple coding scheme, "k-incidence coding," assigning workers to label subsets of k items.
