- Presents an information-theoretic framework to establish lower bounds on query complexity for crowdsourcing tasks with target error probability.  
- Models workers as parallel, independent channels.  
- Considers two worker channel models: Symmetric Discrete Memoryless Channels (for honest errors) and Spammer-Hammer Channel SHC(q) (worker is spammer with probability 1−q).  
- Assumes nonadaptive coding schemes for task assignment and querying.  
- Analyzes two aggregator knowledge scenarios: aggregator knows individual worker skills, and aggregator has no skill information.  
- Assumes an oblivious taskmaster who assigns tasks uniformly across workers.  
- Derives a necessary condition on the minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under the SHC(q) model.  
- Strength: paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Strength: application of information theory to fundamental crowdsourcing limits appears novel.  
- Strength: bounds have practical implications for setting redundancy, pricing, and resource allocation on crowdsourcing platforms.  
- Weakness: lacks experiments or simulations comparing theoretical bounds to performance of practical schemes (e.g., AMT).  
- Suggestion: shorten Section 2.4 and condense/remove Section 3.1 to make room for proof sketches of main theorems.  
- Note: reviewer did not assess correctness of proofs in the appendix.
