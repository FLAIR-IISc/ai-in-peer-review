- Maps crowdsourcing to joint source–channel coding and derives a budget (query count) vs. fidelity trade-off via an information-theoretic lower bound independent of specific schemes.

- Proposes and analyzes a taskmaster scheme called k-ary incidence coding and compares its performance to the information-theoretic bound for a specific channel model.

- Reviewer finds the rate–distortion analogy and application of information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing interesting and promising for future work.

- Concerns that the model may not reflect real crowdsourcing because worker skill and reliability are merged into a single channel parameter.

- Criticism that the taskmaster allocates the same number of queries to all workers regardless of skill, which is unrealistic given worker ratings in practice.

- Questions about realism of the channel models in capturing worker unreliability, especially for adversarial or knowledgeable workers who may actively degrade fidelity.

- Numerical results and Fig. 2 explanations are unclear; curve labeling needs improvement and the interpretation of the k=1 case is ambiguous.

- The assumption of knowing P(B(X)) is stated late (p.6, l.246) and should be declared earlier in the paper.

- Incorrect figure references on p.4, l.135: Figure 1.a and 1.b should be relabeled to Figure 1.b and 1.c respectively.

- Terminology: use lowercase 'theorem', 'lemma', 'corollary' when not accompanied by numbering rather than capitalized forms.
