- Proposes a crowdsourcing framework where a taskmaster decomposes tasks, workers with varying skill/reliability respond, and a crowdsourcer aggregates responses to meet fidelity vs budget trade-off.  
- Maps the crowdsourcing problem to joint source-channel coding and derives an information-theoretic lower bound on budget for a target fidelity that is independent of query design and decoding.  
- Analyzes performance of a specific scheme (k-ary incidence coding) and compares it to the derived lower bound for a particular channel model.  
- Reviewer finds the rate-distortion analogy intriguing and the work potentially inspiring for information-theoretic approaches to crowdsourcing.  
- Major realism concern: the model merges worker skill and reporting reliability into a single channel and assumes equal query allocation, which may not reflect real heterogeneous worker populations.  
- Major realism concern: channel models may not capture adversarial workers who intentionally degrade responses.  
- Numerical results and Figure 2 are unclearly explained; curve labeling needs improvement and the k=1 case (what “directly provide the correct label” means) is not well explained.  
- The assumption that P(B(X)) is known should be stated earlier in the paper.  
- Minor errors: incorrect figure references and inconsistent/misused capitalization of "Theorem/Lemma/Corollary"; suggestion to use descriptive lowercase terms when not citing numbered results.
