

The paper models crowdsourcing data labeling as a channel coding problem, where each worker is represented as a noisy channel. Two channel models are considered: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer channel model. The authors analyze the problem as a coding task for these channels, mapping the budget constraint to the code rate for analysis. The work examines distortion versus budget tradeoffs and proposes a basic coding scheme—termed k-incidence coding—which corresponds to requiring workers to label subsets of k items simultaneously. The approach is interesting, though as the topic falls outside my area of expertise, I cannot provide detailed technical feedback.
