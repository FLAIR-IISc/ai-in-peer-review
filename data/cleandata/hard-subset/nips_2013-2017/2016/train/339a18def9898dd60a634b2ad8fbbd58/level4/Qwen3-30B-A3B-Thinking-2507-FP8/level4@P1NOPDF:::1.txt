

The paper models the crowdsourcing labeling problem by representing each worker as a noisy channel, considering two channel types: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer channel models. The problem thus reduces to a channel coding problem, where the budget constraint maps to the code rate. The authors analyze distortion-budget tradeoffs and propose a basic coding scheme—k-incidence coding—which requires tasks to involve labeling subsets of k items. While I found the approach interesting, this topic falls outside my expertise, so I have no detailed technical comments.
