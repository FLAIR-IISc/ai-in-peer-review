- Frames crowdsourcing budget–fidelity trade-offs as a joint source–channel coding problem, modeling workers as parallel noisy channels.
- Derives fundamental performance bounds and analyzes scenarios with known and unknown worker skill levels.
- Introduces and evaluates k-ary incidence coding as a concrete query scheme and analyzes optimized query pricing.
- Leverages classical information theory (rate–distortion and joint source–channel coding) to provide a rigorous analytical framework.
- Appropriately models worker heterogeneity using channels like M-ary symmetric and spammer–hammer.
- Strength: rigorous, information-theoretic treatment yielding fundamental limits rather than heuristic algorithms.
- Strength: exploration of both known and unknown skill scenarios adds valuable insight into trade-offs.
- Weakness: dense, technical presentation that may be inaccessible to readers without information-theory background.
- Weakness: limited discussion of practical implementation and applicability of theoretical bounds to real-world systems.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to more recent related work (e.g., active learning approaches for crowdsourcing).
- Weakness: query pricing discussion in Appendix E is abrupt and needs further elaboration.
- Recommendation: technically strong and novel; worthy of acceptance pending minor revisions to improve clarity and practical contextualization.
