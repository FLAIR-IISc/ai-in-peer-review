- Frames crowdsourced labeling as an information-theoretic problem by modeling each worker as a noisy channel.  
- Studies two worker-channel variants: symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer model.  
- Transforms the labeling task into a channel coding problem.  
- Analyzes the relationship between available budget and code rate to study the distortion–budget tradeoff.  
- Proposes a simple coding scheme called "k-incidence coding," assigning tasks as k-item subsets.
