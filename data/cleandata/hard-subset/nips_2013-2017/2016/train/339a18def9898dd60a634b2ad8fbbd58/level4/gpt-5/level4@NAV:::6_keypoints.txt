- Derivation is sound and could be significant if this is the first application of rate–distortion and channel coding to crowdsourcing.  
- Novelty and practical impact are uncertain: derivation seems straightforward and the resulting bound may be quite loose.  
- Central concern: unlike channel coding, crowdsourcing may not allow freedom to design the codebook/input sequence u^n, so many mappings in (3) may be infeasible.  
- Because of those feasibility constraints, channel capacity could be an optimistic/loose upper bound for crowdsourcing performance.  
- Concrete issue: you cannot present the same query to the same worker multiple times without violating the i.i.d. assumptions, so repetition/error-correction strategies from channel coding do not directly transfer.  
- More generally, the crowdsourcing setting likely imposes substantial restrictions on the mapping in (3) that must be modeled for tight bounds.  
- Recommendation: evaluate/compare the derived bound against state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods to assess tightness and practical relevance.

- Minor: In Theorem 4, since ln(1 − q) < 0, clarify whether ε_S is larger than \hat{ε}.
