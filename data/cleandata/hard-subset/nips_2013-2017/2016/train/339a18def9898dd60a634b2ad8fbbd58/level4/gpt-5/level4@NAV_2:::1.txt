This paper approaches crowdsourced data labeling from an information-theoretic perspective. It models each annotator as a noisy channel—specifically considering an M-ary symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer–hammer channel model. Framed this way, the problem becomes one of coding over the selected channel, with the crowdsourcing budget mapped to the code rate. The work examines the trade-off between distortion and budget and analyzes a simple query design, k-ary incidence coding, which has workers label subsets of k items per query. I found the paper quite interesting, but as it is outside my primary expertise, I do not have detailed comments.
