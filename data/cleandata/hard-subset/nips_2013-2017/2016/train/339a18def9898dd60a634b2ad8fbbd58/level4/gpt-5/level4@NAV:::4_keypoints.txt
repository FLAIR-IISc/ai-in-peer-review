- Paper maps crowdsourcing to joint source–channel coding and derives a budget–fidelity trade-off with an information-theoretic lower bound independent of query design or processing.
- Authors evaluate a specific scheme (k-ary incidence coding) and compare its performance to the theoretical lower bound for a chosen channel model.
- Reviewer finds the rate–distortion analogy compelling and potentially useful for further research.
- Concern: worker reliability and skill are conflated into a single channel model, ignoring distinct worker proficiency.
- Concern: paper assumes uniform query allocation to all workers, whereas real systems use ratings and differentiated assignments.
- Concern: realism of the channel models for worker unreliability is unclear, especially treatment of knowledgeable adversarial workers.
- Numerical results and Fig. 2 are not clearly explained; curve labels need improvement and the meaning of k=1 is ambiguous (possibly single-item label queries?).
- The assumption that P(B(X)) is known is introduced late (page 6, line 246) and should be stated earlier.
- Figure reference errors on page 4, line 135: “Figure 1.a” should be “Figure 1.b” and “Figure 1.b” should be “Figure 1.c.”
- Stylistic issue: use of “Theorem,” “Lemma,” and “Corollary” without numbering; use lowercase or add numbering for clarity.
