- Models crowdsourced labeling as communication over noisy channels, treating each worker as a stochastic channel.  
- Analyzes two worker models: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer–hammer model.  
- Frames the labeling task as a channel-coding problem, mapping the annotation budget to the code rate.  
- Studies the trade-off between distortion (labeling error) and annotation budget.  
- Examines k-incidence coding, a simple strategy where each worker labels a subset of k items simultaneously.
