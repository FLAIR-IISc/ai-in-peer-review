- Derivation is technically sound and may be meaningful if this is the first application of rate-distortion and channel coding to crowdsourcing.  
- Novelty and practical significance are questionable because the derivation is relatively straightforward.  
- The bound risks being overly loose due to unmodeled constraints in crowdsourcing.  
- Key issue: lack of equivalent design freedom for the channel input sequence u^n compared to channel coding codebook design.  
- It is unclear whether the mapping from B(X)^n to U^{∑m_i} in Eq.(3) admits the same wide range of implementable encodings as channel coding.  
- Degenerate mappings are excluded, but many valid mappings may not be implementable, which could make channel-capacity-based bounds loose.  
- Example: repetition coding (majority voting) is feasible, but no clear method to design u^n for improved error correction beyond repetition.  
- Fundamental distinction: reassigning the same query to the same worker breaks the iid assumption (answers likely identical), unlike iid channel repetitions in coding.  
- Such worker-repeat constraints and other practical limits likely impose significant restrictions on feasible encoding schemes in Eq.(3).  
- Authors should compare the theoretical bound against state-of-the-art methods' empirical performance to strengthen the paper.
