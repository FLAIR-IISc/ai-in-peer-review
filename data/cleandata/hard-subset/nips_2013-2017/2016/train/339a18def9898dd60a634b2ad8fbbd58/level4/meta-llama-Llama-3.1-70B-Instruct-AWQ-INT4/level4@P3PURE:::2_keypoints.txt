- Presents a novel JSCC information-theoretic model of crowdsourcing, mapping taskmaster/workers/crowdsourcer to encoder/channel/decoder and deriving asymptotic limits on queries per item; also gives a lower bound on error for k-ary incidence coding.

- Well-written and a promising first step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.

- Must justify or cite that the source-channel separation theorem holds when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder but possibly known at the decoder.

- Model is overly restrictive by assuming the encoder/decoder only see labels; authors should incorporate possible side information from the original items at encoder and/or decoder.

- Clarify Sec.4.2 wording: stating that kIC lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow should note the lower bound value decreases, and that a lower bound alone does not demonstrate actual code improvement without an upper bound.

- Recommend exploring nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) JSCC results—e.g., Kostina and Verdú (2013)—to provide more practically relevant fundamental limits.
