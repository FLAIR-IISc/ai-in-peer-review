- Presents a novel information-theoretic JSCC framework modeling crowdsourcing as encoding dataset into queries sent through noisy worker channels to reconstruct labels.  
- Establishes fundamental limits on minimum queries per item for target fidelity in two scenarios: skill-level known to crowdsourcer only (SL-CS) and unknown to both (SL-UK).  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a concrete query scheme and derives performance implications.  
- Originality: introduces a new theoretical perspective connecting crowdsourcing to information theory and provides the first fundamental limits.  
- Technical quality: Theorems 2 and 3 are technically sound and correctly model worker skill as channel states.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured with clear definitions and accessible exposition linking crowdsourcing and information theory.  
- Weakness: Fails to justify applicability of source-channel separation theorem when channel state is unknown at the encoder; needs proof or citation.  
- Weakness: Omits modeling of side information (original items X) available to both taskmaster and crowdsourcer, which could materially affect results.  
- Weakness: Ambiguous wording in Section 4.2 about "lower bounds improving"—should clarify that achievable error decreases as k and q grow.  
- Weakness: Results are asymptotic (L → ∞) and may have limited practical relevance; should discuss non-asymptotic/finite-blocklength implications.  
- Overall: Important theoretical contribution that requires addressing separation-theorem justification, side information inclusion, bound interpretation, and non-asymptotic discussion.  
- Recommendation: Major revision.
