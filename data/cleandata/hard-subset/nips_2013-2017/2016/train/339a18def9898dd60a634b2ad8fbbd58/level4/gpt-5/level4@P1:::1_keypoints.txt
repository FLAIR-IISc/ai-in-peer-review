- Models crowdsourced labeling in an information-theoretic framework.  
- Represents each worker as a noisy channel with two specific models: M-ary symmetric memoryless (no feedback) and spammer–hammer.  
- Recasts labeling as a coding problem, equating crowdsourcing budget with code rate.  
- Analyzes the trade-off between budget (rate) and fidelity (distortion) and derives bounds on achievable performance.  
- Studies a specific query scheme, k-ary incidence coding, where each query asks a worker to jointly label a subset of k items.  
- Characterizes k-ary incidence coding performance within the same rate–distortion perspective.  
- Reviewer found the paper interesting and praised the clean mapping to joint source–channel coding and use of rate–distortion tools.  
- Reviewer is outside the paper’s primary area of expertise and cannot assess technical details, proof tightness, or how k-ary incidence coding compares to alternative methods.
