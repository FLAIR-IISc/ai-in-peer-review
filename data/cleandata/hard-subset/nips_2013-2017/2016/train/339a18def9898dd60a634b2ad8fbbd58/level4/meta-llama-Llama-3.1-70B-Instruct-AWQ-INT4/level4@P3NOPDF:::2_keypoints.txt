- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item using coding-rate results.  
- Labels modeled as i.i.d. source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses, worker skills as random channel states, and crowdsourcer as decoder.  
- Provides a lower bound on the error probability of the k-ary incidence coding (kIC) scheme under a specific crowdsourcing model.  
- Must explicitly justify or cite that the source-channel separation theorem holds when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder but possibly known at the receiver.  
- Model is restrictive by excluding original data as side information for encoder/decoder; incorporating side information would make the model more realistic and could improve performance.  
- Clarify wording in Section 4.2: saying the lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow is misleading—bounds become smaller, indicating potentially better code performance.  
- Recommend pursuing non-asymptotic characterizations (e.g., Kostina and Verdú’s non-asymptotic JSCC converses) for more practically useful limits.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and an interesting first step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.
