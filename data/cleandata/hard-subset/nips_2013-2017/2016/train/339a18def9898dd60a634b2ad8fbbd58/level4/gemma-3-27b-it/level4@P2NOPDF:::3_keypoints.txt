- Paper investigates crowdsourcing response aggregation with an information-theoretic model, deriving fundamental budget–fidelity limits.  
- Core ideas and proposed model are interesting and potentially valuable, but presentation details are hard to follow.  
- Authors' effort to explain information-theoretic concepts is good, yet overall technicality remains a significant barrier to accessibility.  
- High-level insights (e.g., end of Section 3) are a clear strength but are not emphasized enough.  
- Recommend adding a concise concluding section that summarizes practical implications and clear takeaways for practitioners.  
- Recast results in crowdsourcing terms (worker responses, budget allocation, accuracy) instead of channel-coding/distortion language to improve accessibility.  
- Related work should cite the mechanism-design/crowdsourcing-incentives literature that addresses variable worker abilities and ties incentives to aggregation.  
- Minor grammar fix: line 84 “is reviewed in” → likely “are reviewed in.”  
- Clarify the roles of “crowdsourcer” vs “taskmaster” (confusing descriptions around line 106 and earlier).  
- Fix typesetting error on line 122 (“A A(X1,X2)”).  
- Rephrase the hard-to-parse sentence beginning “In case…” on lines 275–276.
