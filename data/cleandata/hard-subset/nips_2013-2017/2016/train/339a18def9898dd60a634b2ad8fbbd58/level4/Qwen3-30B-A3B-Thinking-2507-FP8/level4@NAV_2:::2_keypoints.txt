- Presents a JSCC information-theoretic framework modeling crowdsourcing with random channel states for worker skills and derives asymptotic query-rate limits under SL-CS and SL-UK.  
- Extends prior aggregation work by introducing a rate-distortion perspective and deriving closed-form bounds (Theorems 2–3).  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under the spammer-hammer channel and provides novel combinatorial error results (Lemma 1, Theorem 4).  
- Paper is well-structured, technically rigorous, and relevant to practical platforms like Amazon Mechanical Turk.  
- Originality: first application of JSCC to crowdsourcing with random channel states and a novel kIC scheme.  
- Significance: establishes fundamental trade-offs between query budget (rate) and fidelity (error probability).  
- Clarity: modeling and derivations are logical and supported by intuitive examples.  
- Quality: Theorems 2–3 are theoretically sound, appropriately adapting standard JSCC results.  
- Theoretical gap: uses source-channel separation without justifying validity for channels with random states unknown at the encoder—needs proof sketch or citation.  
- Model restriction: assumes no side information at the taskmaster/crowdsourcer, which is unrealistic and could materially change bounds.  
- Misstatement: claim that "kIC bounds improve as k and q grow" is misleading and requires clarification about lower-bound behavior.  
- Limited practical impact: asymptotic analysis lacks guidance for finite-budget deployments; nonasymptotic bounds are needed.  
- Pro: rigorous foundation, novel analysis, clear exposition of limits.  
- Con: unresolved theoretical issue, unrealistic model simplification, and limited real-world applicability due to asymptotic focus.
