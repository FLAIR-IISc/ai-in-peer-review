This paper establishes an information-theoretic lower bound on the budget-fidelity tradeoff in label crowdsourcing, leveraging rate-distortion theory and the channel coding theorem. The derivation of this bound appears sound. If this represents the first application of these tools to crowdsourcing, it could represent a valuable contribution to the field. However, the practical significance and novelty remain uncertain, as the derivation is relatively direct and the resulting bound may be quite loose. 

A key concern is the applicability of the channel coding framework to crowdsourcing. While channel coding enjoys substantial flexibility in codebook design (e.g., repetition, Hamming, LDPC codes), it’s unclear if comparable freedom exists in designing the input sequence *u<sup>n</sup>* in this context. Specifically, equation (3) implies a wide range of valid encoding mappings from B(X)<sup>n</sup> to U<sup>(∑mi)</sup>, but it’s questionable whether most of these mappings are realistically implementable. For example, while repetition-based schemes with majority voting are feasible, extending this to more sophisticated error correction is not obvious. 

A fundamental difference between channel coding and crowdsourcing lies in the independence assumption. Unlike communication channels where repeated transmission of the same message is permissible, the i.i.d. assumption in equations (1) and (2) is violated if the same query is presented to the same worker multiple times – their responses are likely correlated. This is just one illustration of the potentially significant constraints inherent in crowdsourcing that are absent in standard channel coding, where encoding mappings from {0,1}<sup>nR</sup> to {0,1}<sup>n</sup> face fewer restrictions. Consequently, the set of feasible encoding schemes in equation (3) is likely more limited in crowdsourcing, potentially rendering the proposed bound overly optimistic. 

To strengthen the work, a comparison of the derived bound with the performance of existing state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods would be highly beneficial.
