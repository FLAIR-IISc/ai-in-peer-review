- Paper studies crowdsourcing where a taskmaster partitions a large inference task into queries, aggregates heterogeneous and unreliable worker responses, and measures budget as total queries to guarantee a target fidelity.
- Authors map the problem to joint source–channel coding, deriving an information-theoretic lower bound on budget needed for a desired fidelity that is independent of specific query/decoding schemes.
- The paper analyzes a specific taskmaster scheme (k-ary incidence coding) and compares its performance to the derived lower bound under a particular channel model.
- The rate–distortion / information-theoretic parallel is interesting and could motivate further use of information-theoretic tools in crowdsourcing.
- Concern: enforcing an exact point-to-point communication mapping may reduce realism and limit applicability to real-world crowdsourcing systems.
- Major issue 1: the model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel abstraction and unrealistically assumes equal query assignment regardless of worker ratings or past performance.
- Major issue 2: the channel models’ realism is unclear, especially regarding workers who understand the task but behave adversarially to degrade fidelity.
- Numerical results lack clear explanation and labeling (Figure 2); authors should clarify what the k=1 case denotes (e.g., direct labeling vs pair comparisons).
- The assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) (page 6, line 246) should be stated earlier in the paper.
- Figure reference errors on page 4: “Figure 1.a” should be “Figure 1.b” and “Figure 1.b” should be “Figure 1.c.”
- Presentation issue: repeated capitalized “Theorem,” “Lemma,” and “Corollary” without numbering—use lowercase or provide numbering.
