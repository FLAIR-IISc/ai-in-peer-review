- Clean, insightful mapping of crowdsourcing to joint source–channel coding (JSCC) yielding interpretable asymptotic query-complexity limits.  
- Random-state channel model naturally captures heterogeneous worker skills.  
- Provides a model-specific lower bound on the error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC).  
- Main concern: Theorems rely on source–channel separation for channels with random state, but the channel/state model and separation assumptions (stationarity, memorylessness, encoder/decoder state knowledge) are not explicitly stated or justified.  
- Suggestion: Add a lemma/appendix or cite standard results to rigorously establish separation for the treated channel-state knowledge cases.  
- Concern: The model omits side information (original item features) at encoder/decoder; side information at decoder or both can strictly improve performance.  
- Suggestion: Discuss or outline extensions to JSCC with correlated side information and cite relevant literature.  
- Concern: Claim that “lower bounds for kIC improve as k and q grow” is ambiguous; clarify that numerical lower bounds decrease with k and q and avoid implying bound tightness without matching upper bounds.  
- Nonasymptotic perspective: Finite-blocklength JSCC results could yield practical query–error tradeoffs; discuss applicability of such bounds (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013).  
- Overall: Paper is a promising first step but should (i) make separation arguments rigorous for the specified state-knowledge regimes and (ii) broaden modeling to include side information and finite-blocklength considerations to increase practical relevance.
