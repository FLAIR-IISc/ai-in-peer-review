- Paper models crowdsourced labelers as noisy channels to analyze labeling performance.
- Considers two channel types: symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and spammer/hammer model.
- Reframes labeling as a coding problem, mapping crowdsourcing budget to code rate.
- Studies the trade-off between distortion (labeling accuracy) and budget.
- Proposes "k-incidence coding," requiring tasks that label subsets of k items simultaneously.
