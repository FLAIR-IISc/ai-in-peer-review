

The paper formulates the crowdsourcing data labeling problem as a channel coding task, modeling each worker as a noisy channel. Two channel models are examined: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer channel model. The problem is thereby framed as a channel coding problem, with the budget constraint translated into the code rate for analysis. The authors analyze distortion-budget tradeoffs and propose a fundamental coding scheme (termed k-incidence coding), which requires workers to label subsets of k items simultaneously. While I find the paper conceptually intriguing, it lies outside my area of expertise, and I cannot provide specific technical feedback.
