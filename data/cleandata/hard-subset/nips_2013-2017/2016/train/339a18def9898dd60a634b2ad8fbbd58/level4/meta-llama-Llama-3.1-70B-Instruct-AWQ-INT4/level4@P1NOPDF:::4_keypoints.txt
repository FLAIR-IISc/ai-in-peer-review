- Paper maps a crowdsourcing setup to joint source-channel coding and studies the budget–fidelity trade-off using information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Lower bound on required queries is independent of specific taskmaster query design or crowdsourcer algorithms.  
- Authors analyze a specific taskmaster scheme, k-ary incidence coding, and compare its performance to the information-theoretic bound.  
- The rate–distortion parallel to crowdsourcing with unreliable workers is interesting and could spur further research.  
- Concern that the modeling may be unrealistic because it forces an exact point-to-point communication mapping.  
- Worker skill and reliability are merged into a single channel model, ignoring per-worker heterogeneity and ratings.  
- Assumes equal number of queries allocated to all workers, which may not reflect real systems that adapt to worker skill.  
- Questionable realism of the channel models for unreliable workers and lack of treatment for adversarial workers.  
- Numerical results and Figure 2 labeling are unclear; need clarification and better curve labels, including what k=1 represents.  
- The assumption of known P(B(X)) is introduced late (page 6, line 246) and should be stated earlier.  
- Incorrect figure transitions on page 4 (Figure 1.a → 1.b and 1.b → 1.c) and inconsistent capitalization/numbering of Theorem/Lemma/Corollary usages.
