- Paper models crowdsourcing as parallel noisy channels and provides information-theoretic lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.  
- Considers two cases: known worker skill levels and unknown skill levels.  
- Analyzes two worker models: M-ary symmetric channel (MSC) and spammer-hammer channel (SHC).  
- Uses a joint source-channel (de)coding framework to represent query schemes and inference.  
- Derives bounds on the minimum number of queries per item needed to achieve given fidelity.  
- Introduces and analyzes k-ary incidence coding, showing it provides error-correction benefits.  
- Studies optimized query pricing within the proposed framework.  
- Paper is well-organized, well-written, and explains the framework and results clearly.  
- Contributions are novel and useful for designing and pricing crowdsourcing projects.  
- Technical results appear sound and accessible even to non-experts in information theory.  
- Weakness: lacks experiments or simulations to demonstrate practical gap between current schemes and theoretical limits.  
- Weakness: Sections 2.4 and 3.1 are overly long or contain nonessential background and could be shortened or removed.  
- Suggestion: include proof sketches for theorems instead of lengthy background sections.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with potential impact on crowdsourcing system design; merits acceptance if practical validation is added.
