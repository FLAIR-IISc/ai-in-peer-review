- Models crowdsourced workers as noisy channels for labeling tasks.
- Examines two channel models: symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and spammer/hammer model.
- Reformulates labeling as a coding problem tied to the chosen channel model.
- Analyzes the mapping between budget constraints and code rate.
- Explores the tradeoff between distortion (label error) and budget.
- Proposes k-incidence coding: assign tasks that require labeling subsets of k items simultaneously.
