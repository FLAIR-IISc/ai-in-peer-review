- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and derives asymptotic limits on queries per item.  
- Items are treated as i.i.d. source, taskmaster = encoder, workers = channel uses with random skills as channel state, crowdsourcer = decoder.  
- Authors apply existing JSCC coding-rate results to obtain fundamental asymptotic query-rate limits.  
- Provide a lower bound on error probability for a specific scheme ("k-ary incidence coding") under a particular crowdsourcing model.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and a promising first step toward JSCC-based crowdsourcing modeling.  
- Technical concern 1: The use of the source-channel separation theorem needs justification (or citation/proof) for channels with random state unknown to the encoder but possibly known to the decoder.  
- Technical concern 2: The model is overly restrictive by denying encoder/decoder access to the original items (side information), which is common in practice and could affect results.  
- Technical concern 3: Claim that kIC lower bounds "improve" with k and q is ambiguous—smaller lower bounds do not necessarily indicate a better or tighter bound without corresponding upper bounds.  
- Post-response suggestion: A non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterization would be more practical; cite/consider Kostina & Verdú (2013) on finite-blocklength JSCC.
