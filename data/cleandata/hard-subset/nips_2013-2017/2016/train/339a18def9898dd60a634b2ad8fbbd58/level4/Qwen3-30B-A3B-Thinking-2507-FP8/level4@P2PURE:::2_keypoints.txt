Paper proposes an information-theoretic JSCC model of crowdsourcing, mapping labels/taskmaster/workers to source/encoder/channel/state/decoder and deriving asymptotic query-rate limits.  
Authors derive a lower bound on error probability for a k-ary incidence coding (kIC) scheme under a specific crowdsourcing model.  
Paper is well-written and a promising initial step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.  
Technical concern: must justify using the source–channel separation theorem when the channel state is unknown at the encoder, since the standard theorem assumes a fixed channel.  
Modeling concern: restricting encoder/decoder to observe only item labels ignores available item-side information; incorporating side information (at encoder, decoder, or both) would be more realistic and could improve performance.  
Clarification: claim that "lower bounds for kIC improve as k and q grow" is misleading—the lower bounds decrease as k and q increase, indicating potentially better code performance, not an improvement relative to an upper bound.  
Suggestion after response: a nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterization would be more practically useful; consider recent finite-blocklength JSCC results (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013).
