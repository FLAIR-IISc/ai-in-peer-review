- Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC framework for crowdsourcing, treating labels as source, workers as channel uses, worker skills as channel states, and deriving asymptotic query complexity bounds plus a lower bound for k-ary incidence coding.

- Overall assessment: well-written and a promising first step toward JSCC modeling for crowdsourcing.

- Major concern 1: Direct use of source–channel separation (Theorems 2 & 3) is unjustified for unknown encoder-side channel states; authors must prove or cite validity of separation in this setting.

- Major concern 2: Model omits side information (original items) at encoder/decoder; including such side information can materially affect JSCC performance and realism.

- Major concern 3: Misleading wording in Section 4.2 — saying lower bounds “improve” with k and q is incorrect; the lower-bound value decreases, and the authors should clarify the intended meaning.

- Recommendation: Include non-asymptotic/finite-blocklength analysis (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) to make results more practically useful.
