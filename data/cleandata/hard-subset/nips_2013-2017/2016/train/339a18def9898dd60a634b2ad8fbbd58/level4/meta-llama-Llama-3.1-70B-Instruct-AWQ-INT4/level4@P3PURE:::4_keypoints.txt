- Paper maps crowdsourcing with unreliable workers to joint source-channel coding, treating taskmaster as transmitter and crowdsourcer as receiver.  
- Derives an information-theoretic lower bound on budget (number of queries) needed for a target fidelity; bound is scheme- and decoder- independent.  
- Introduces and analyzes a specific taskmaster scheme: k-ary incidence coding, and compares it to the derived lower bound for a chosen channel model.  
- Reviewer finds the rate-distortion analogy and information-theoretic viewpoint interesting and potentially stimulating for future work.  
- Concern: worker skill and reliability are merged into a single channel model, which may oversimplify real crowdsourcing where workers have individual ratings.  
- Concern: paper assumes equal query allocation to all workers regardless of skill, which is unrealistic.  
- Concern: realism of the channel models is questionable, especially regarding adversarial or partially knowledgeable workers who might intentionally degrade results.  
- Numerical results and Fig. 2 are not clearly explained; curve labeling needs improvement and the meaning of k=1 is unclear.  
- Methodological issue: assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) is stated late (page 6, line 246) and should be declared earlier.  
- Minor editorial fixes: incorrect figure cross-references on page 4 (Figure 1.a→1.b, 1.b→1.c).  
- Stylistic consistency: authors misuse capitalized "Theorem"/"Lemma"/"Corollary" without numbering; should use lowercase or provide numbers.
