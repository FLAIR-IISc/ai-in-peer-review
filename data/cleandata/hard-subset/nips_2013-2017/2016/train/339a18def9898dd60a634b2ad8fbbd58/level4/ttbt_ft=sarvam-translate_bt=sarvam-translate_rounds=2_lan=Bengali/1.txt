This paper models the problem of data labeling from crowdsourcing. Each worker is modeled as a noisy channel (two types of channels are considered: symmetric memory-less channels without feedback, and spammer/hammer channels models). The problem then becomes a coding problem for studying the considered channels and the authors. The rate of code is mapped based on the budget analysis used. This paper considers the distortion versus budget trade-off and also studies a fundamental coding scheme (known as K-incidence coding), which is consistent with the requirement of labeling k items at a given time for a crowdsourced task. I found this paper quite interesting, but since it is outside my area of expertise, I have no detailed comment on it.
