The paper derives information-theoretic lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability in crowdsourcing.

Workers are modeled as parallel, independent channels using symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and the spammer–hammer channel SHC(q).

The coding scheme is assumed nonadaptive and the taskmaster is assumed to have no knowledge of worker skills, so every worker is assigned the same number of tasks.

Two decision scenarios are analyzed: the crowdsourcer either knows all worker skill levels or knows none.

Provides a necessary condition on the minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under an SHC(q) worker pool.

Paper is well organized and clearly written.

Work appears novel—no prior information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing is known to the reviewer.

Results are practically useful for determining minimum query rates and informing pricing decisions.

No experiments or simulations are provided to show how close common practical schemes (e.g., AMT) are to the derived limits.

Section 2.4 should be shortened; Section 3.1 is unnecessary or should be condensed, replaced by proof sketches for main theorems.

Reviewer did not read the appendix and therefore cannot comment on the proofs.
