- Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC framework mapping crowdsourcing (items, taskmaster, workers, crowdsourcer) to source, encoder, channel with random state, and decoder.  
- Derives fundamental asymptotic limits on the required number of queries per item using established JSCC coding-rate results.  
- Provides a lower bound on the error probability for a specific "k-ary incidence coding" (kIC) instance.  
- Novel and well-articulated application of JSCC to crowdsourcing.  
- Concern: validity of the source–channel separation theorem for channels with random state unknown to the encoder is not justified—needs proof or citation.  
- Concern: model omits side information (original items) at encoder/decoder; including decoder-side or both-sided side information could substantially change results.  
- Concern: wording that kIC bounds "improve" with increasing k and q is ambiguous and should be clarified (lower bound becoming smaller vs. actual code performance).  
- Suggestion: address non-asymptotic (finite blocklength) limits, e.g., cite Kostina & Verdú (2013) on finite-blocklength JSCC converses.
