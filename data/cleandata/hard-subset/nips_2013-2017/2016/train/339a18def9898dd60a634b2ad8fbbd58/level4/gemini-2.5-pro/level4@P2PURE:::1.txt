This paper approaches the problem of crowdsourced data labeling from a coding theory perspective. The authors model each worker as a noisy channel, specifically considering both a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer model. Within this framework, the crowdsourcing budget is mapped to the code rate, allowing the authors to analyze the fundamental tradeoff between distortion and budget. They also introduce and study a basic coding scheme, termed "k-incidence coding," where each task consists of labeling a subset of k items. While I found the paper to be an interesting contribution, it lies outside my core area of expertise, and thus I am unable to provide detailed technical comments.
