- Paper frames crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem and derives asymptotic limits on queries per item; includes a lower bound on k-ary incidence coding (kIC) error probability.  
- Well-written and a potentially valuable first step toward JSCC-based crowdsourcing modeling.  
- Authors apply the source-channel separation theorem despite a random channel state unknown to the encoder; they must justify or cite results that validate separation in this setting.  
- Model restricts the taskmaster and crowdsourcer to observing only labels; this ignores realistic side information (access to items) at encoder/decoder, which should be incorporated.  
- Claim in Section 4.2 that lower bounds for kIC “improve” with increasing k and q is ambiguous—lowering a converse bound isn’t an improvement without corresponding achievability/upper bounds.  
- A nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterization of fundamental limits is needed for practical relevance.  
- Recommend the authors consider Kostina & Verdú (2013) on nonasymptotic JSCC converses for finite-blocklength analysis.
