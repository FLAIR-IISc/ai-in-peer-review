- Models crowdsourcing in an information-theoretic framework to give lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.  
- Workers modeled as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q).  
- Assumes nonadaptive coding and analyzes two cases: crowdsourcer knows worker skills vs. does not; taskmaster assigns equally without skill knowledge.  
- Main contribution: fundamental limits on queries per task and a necessary condition on minimum rate for k-ary incidence coding with SHC(q).  
- Paper is well organized, well written, and the analysis is technically sound.  
- High originality and significance; useful for designers and pricing of crowdsourcing projects.  
- Lacks experiments or simulations (e.g., AMT) to show how current schemes compare to the theoretical limits.  
- Section 2.4 and Section 3.1 are overly long or unnecessary and could be shortened or removed.  
- Missing proof sketches for the theorems; these are more important than some lengthy exposition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant contribution, clear writing, high significance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: no empirical validation, some sections need trimming, and proofs are not sketched.
