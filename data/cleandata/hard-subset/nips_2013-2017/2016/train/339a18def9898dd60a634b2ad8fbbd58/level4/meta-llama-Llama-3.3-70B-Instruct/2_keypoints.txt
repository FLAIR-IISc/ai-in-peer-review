- Introduces an information-theoretic JSCC framework for crowdsourcing and derives asymptotic limits on queries per item.
- Models: item labels as i.i.d. source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as multiple channel uses, worker skill as random channel state, crowdsourcer as decoder.
- Leverages existing JSCC coding-rate results to establish fundamental asymptotic query limits.
- Provides a lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific crowdsourcing model.
- Well-written and potentially pioneering application of JSCC to crowdsourcing.
- Authors rely on source-channel separation for channels with random states unknown to the encoder (possibly known to decoder); this assumption needs a proof or citation.
- Current model ignores side information (original data) available to taskmaster or crowdsourcer; incorporating encoder/decoder side information would increase realism.
- Interpretation of Fig.2: lower bounds for kIC become smaller with increasing k and q; calling this an “improvement” is misleading without an upper bound.
- A non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterization would be more practical; recommend consulting Kostina and Verdu’s finite-blocklength JSCC results.
