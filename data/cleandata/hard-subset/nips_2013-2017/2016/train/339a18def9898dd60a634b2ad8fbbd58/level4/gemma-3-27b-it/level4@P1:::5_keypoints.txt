- Models crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic framework to derive fundamental limits on queries per task for a target error probability.
- Represents workers as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels for errors and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q) for malicious/consistently accurate contributors.
- Assumes non-adaptive coding and analyzes two cases: crowdsourcer knows worker skills vs. unknown skills; taskmaster assigns equal tasks to each worker.
- Derives a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under the SHC(q) model.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Theoretical contributions are strong, mathematically rigorous, and appear novel.
- Results have potential practical impact for crowdsourcing system design and pricing.
- Lacks empirical validation or simulations comparing real platforms (e.g., MTurk) to the theoretical limits.
- Section 2.4 should be streamlined; Section 3.1 is somewhat long and could be abbreviated.
- Important proof details are relegated to the appendices, which may hinder readability for a broad audience.
- Reviewer believes the paper is suitable for acceptance, recommending empirical validation be addressed in future work.
