- Presents an information-theoretic JSCC framing of crowdsourcing and derives asymptotic query-per-item bounds.
- Models items as source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses with random skill (channel state), and crowdsourcer as decoder.
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC) in a specific crowdsourcing model.
- Well written and potentially impactful as an initial application of JSCC principles to crowdsourcing.
- Over-relies on the source–channel separation theorem without justifying its applicability when channel states are random and unknown to the encoder; proof or citation needed.
- The model is restrictive by excluding possible side information (items) available to the taskmaster or crowdsourcer, which could materially affect results.
- The claim that kIC “improves” with larger k and q is misleading since only a decreasing lower bound is shown without corresponding upper bounds for comparison.
- Recommends pursuing non-asymptotic (finite-query) characterizations—cites Kostina & Verdú (2013) as a possible avenue for practical relevance.
