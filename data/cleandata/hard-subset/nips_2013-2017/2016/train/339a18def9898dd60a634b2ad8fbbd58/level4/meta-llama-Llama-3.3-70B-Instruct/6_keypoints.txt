- Paper derives an information-theoretic bound on the budget–fidelity tradeoff in crowdsourcing using rate-distortion theory and channel coding; derivation is technically sound.  
- If this is the first application of these theories to crowdsourcing, it could be a significant contribution.  
- Novelty and practical impact are uncertain because the derivation is straightforward and the bound may be loose.  
- Major concern: channel coding allows flexible codebook design, but crowdsourcing cannot freely design repeated queries to the same worker (violates iid), so encoding constraints may make the bound overly loose.  
- Recommend empirical comparison between state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods and the derived bound.

- Minor: provide more detailed proof for Theorem 4.
