- Proposes an information-theoretic framework giving lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability in digital crowdsourcing.  
- Models workers as parallel, independent channels and analyzes two channel types: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and spammer-hammer channels SHC(q).  
- Studies two aggregator-information scenarios: aggregator knows individual worker skills, and aggregator has no knowledge of worker skills.  
- Assumes the taskmaster is unaware of worker abilities, leading to a uniform task assignment strategy.  
- Derives a necessary condition on the minimum rate for k-ary incidence coding under the SHC(q) model.  
- Strength: paper is well-organized, clearly written, and offers a novel, valuable information-theoretic perspective on crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: theoretical bounds can guide practitioners/designers on minimum required rates and pricing/design trade-offs.  
- Weakness: lacks experiments or simulations to compare theoretical bounds with practical systems (e.g., Mechanical Turk).  
- Presentation suggestion: shorten Section 2.4 and significantly condense or remove Section 3.1 to save space.  
- Presentation suggestion: include proof sketches for main theorems in the paper body to improve accessibility instead of relegating details to the appendix.  
- Note: reviewer did not check the appendix and therefore cannot vouch for correctness of full proofs.
