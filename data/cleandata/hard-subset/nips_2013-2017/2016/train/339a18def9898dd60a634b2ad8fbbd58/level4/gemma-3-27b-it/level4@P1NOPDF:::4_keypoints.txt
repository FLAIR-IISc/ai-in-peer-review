- Paper maps crowdsourcing with unreliable workers to rate-distortion/joint source-channel coding and derives a budget–fidelity lower bound independent of specific query or decoding schemes.
- Analysis of a specific taskmaster scheme (k-ary incidence coding) and comparison to the information-theoretic lower bound.
- Reviewer finds the rate-distortion parallel interesting and potentially encouraging for further information-theoretic study of crowdsourcing.
- Concern that the model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel, which may be unrealistic.
- Criticism that assuming equal numbers of queries per worker ignores real systems where workers have ratings and varying allocations.
- Question about realism of the channel models for worker unreliability and lack of treatment of adversarial/strategic workers who deliberately degrade fidelity.
- Numerical results and Fig. 2 are unclearly explained; curve labeling needs improvement.
- Specific question: what does k=1 represent in Fig. 2—how are queries/labels interpreted in that case?
- Assumption of known P(B(X)) is introduced too late (page 6, line 246) and should be stated earlier.
- Typographical/figure references errors: on page 4, line 135 “Figure 1.a” → “Figure 1.b” and “Figure 1.b” → “Figure 1.c”.
- Style/format issue: authors use ‘Theorem’, ‘Lemma’, ‘Corollary’ without numbers; suggest using lowercase or numbered references.
