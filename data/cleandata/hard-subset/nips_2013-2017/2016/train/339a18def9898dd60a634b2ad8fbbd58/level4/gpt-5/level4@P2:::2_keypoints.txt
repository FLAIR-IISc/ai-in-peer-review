- Paper frames crowdsourcing labeling as joint source–channel coding (JSCC) with random channel states and derives asymptotic budget–fidelity tradeoffs.
- Two regimes analyzed: worker skills unknown to encoder/decoder (SL-UK) and known to decoder (SL-CS).
- Uses separation-based arguments to give lower bounds on queries per item under an M-ary symmetric channel (MSC) worker model.
- Studies k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and, under a spammer–hammer channel (SHC), derives a converse for kIC and proposes a pricing heuristic.
- Strength: principled, interpretable JSCC formulation yielding budget–fidelity limits.
- Strength: clear distinction of SL-UK vs SL-CS showing impact of skill knowledge on rates.
- Strength: kIC+SHC analysis offers insight on query design and a simple pricing rule.
- Strength: paper is generally well written and well organized.
- Weakness 1: Separation with random channel state is not fully justified; need citation/conditions where separation holds.
- Weakness 2: Model omits encoder/decoder side information (original items), which can change rate–distortion (Wyner–Ziv) results.
- Weakness 3: All results are asymptotic; finite-blocklength analysis (e.g., Kostina–Verdú) is needed for practical guidance.
- Weakness 4: Ambiguous wording about “improve as k and q grow”; should clarify that achievable performance improves for fixed budget, not bound tightness.
- Weakness 5: Strong modeling assumptions (i.i.d. labels, independent workers/tasks, non-adaptive querying, uniform assignment, N=2 for kIC) should be stated and discussed.
- Weakness 6: Lacks empirical/achievability baselines (majority vote, Dawid–Skene, spectral/EM) to gauge gap to limits and kIC practicality.
- Quality: Technical arguments are sound in spirit but need rigorous treatment of separation and explicit assumptions.
- Clarity: Generally clear; should explicitly list modeling assumptions, clarify state information role, and note where N=2 is assumed.
- Originality: Novel perspective framing crowdsourcing as JSCC and deriving high-level limits distinct from prior inference/task-allocation work.
- Significance: Provides conceptual limits to guide design, but practical impact depends on finite-blocklength analysis and empirical validation.
- For acceptance: clean unified IT framework, highlights role of skill knowledge and query design, potential theoretical/practical influence.
- Against acceptance: unjustified reliance on asymptotic separation with random state, missing side-information modeling, limited kIC scope and no achievability comparisons.
- Actionable recommendations: justify/cite separation results with channel state; discuss/analyze decoder side information; add finite-blocklength perspective and empirical baselines; clarify Fig.2 language and enumerate assumptions.
