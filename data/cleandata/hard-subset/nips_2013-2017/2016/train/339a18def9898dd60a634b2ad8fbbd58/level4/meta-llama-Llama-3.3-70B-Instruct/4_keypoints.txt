- Paper maps crowdsourcing (taskmaster → workers → crowdsourcer) to joint source–channel coding, treating workers as a communication channel.
- Main result: an information-theoretic lower bound on the query budget needed to achieve a target fidelity, independent of specific query/decoding schemes.
- Authors analyze k-ary incidence coding and compare its performance to the derived lower bound for a particular channel model.
- Notable contribution: connecting rate–distortion frameworks to crowdsourcing with unreliable workers, potentially inspiring further work.
- Major concern: model realism—merging worker skill and reliability into a single channel parameter may oversimplify real-world heterogeneity.
- Unrealistic assumption that the taskmaster assigns the same number of queries to all workers, ignoring worker-specific skills/ratings.
- Channel models may not capture adversarial or strategic worker behaviors that intentionally degrade fidelity.
- Numerical results need clearer explanation—Fig. 2 curve labels and the k=1 case are ambiguous and require clarification.
- The assumption that P(B(X)) is known should be stated earlier in the paper.
- Minor issues: swapped figure references on page 4, line 135, and inconsistent use/numbering of “Theorem,” “Lemma,” and “Corollary” (suggest lowercase or consistent numbering).
