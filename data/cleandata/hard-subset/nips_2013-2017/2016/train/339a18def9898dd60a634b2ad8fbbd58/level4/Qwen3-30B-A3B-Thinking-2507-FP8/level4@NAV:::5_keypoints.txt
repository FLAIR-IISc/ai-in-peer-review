- Paper frames digital crowdsourcing in an information-theoretic model to derive lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.  
- Workers are modeled as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) for generic errors and spammer-hammer channel (SHC(q)) for spammer behavior.  
- The coding scheme is assumed nonadaptive.  
- Two analytic cases: (i) crowdsourcer knows all worker skill levels, and (ii) crowdsourcer has no prior knowledge of skills.  
- Taskmaster is assumed unaware of worker skills, so tasks are assigned uniformly to workers.  
- Derives a necessary condition for the minimum rate (queries per task) for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).  
- Paper is well organized and clearly written.  
- Lacks experiments or simulations to quantify the gap between theoretical limits and practical schemes (e.g., Amazon Mechanical Turk).  
- Recommend condensing Section 2.4 and shortening/reducing redundancy in Section 3.1; include proof sketches for theorems.  
- Work appears novel—no prior information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing was found.  
- Potentially useful: enables designers to compute minimum query rates for guaranteed error probabilities, informing pricing and task design.  
- Appendix was not reviewed, so the correctness of proofs was not assessed.
