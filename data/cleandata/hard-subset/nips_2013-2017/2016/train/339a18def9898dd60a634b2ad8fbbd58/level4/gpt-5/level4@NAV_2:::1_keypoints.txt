- Models crowdsourced annotators as noisy channels, specifically M-ary symmetric memoryless (no feedback) and spammer–hammer channel models.
- Reframes crowdsourced labeling as a coding problem, mapping the crowdsourcing budget to the code rate.
- Analyzes the distortion–budget trade-off (accuracy versus budget).
- Proposes and analyzes k-ary incidence coding: queries where workers label subsets of k items.
