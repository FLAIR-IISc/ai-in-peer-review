This submission addresses the problem of crowdsourced data labeling by framing it within an information-theoretic model. The core of this model conceptualizes each worker as a noisy channel, with the authors specifically investigating two variants: the symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer model. Consequently, the labeling task is transformed into a channel coding problem. The paper's analysis connects the available budget to the code's rate, enabling an examination of the fundamental tradeoff between distortion and budget. A basic coding scheme, termed "k-incidence coding," is also proposed, which involves assigning tasks composed of labeling k-item subsets. While I find the paper's approach to be interesting, the topic falls outside my direct expertise, precluding me from offering more detailed feedback.
