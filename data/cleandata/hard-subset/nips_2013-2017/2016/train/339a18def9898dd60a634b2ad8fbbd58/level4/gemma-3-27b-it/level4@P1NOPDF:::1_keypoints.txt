- Frames crowdsourced labeling as a channel coding problem, modeling each worker as a noisy channel.  
- Considers two worker models: symmetric memoryless channel and spammer/hammer channel.  
- Relates crowdsourcing budget to the achievable coding rate to formalize cost–accuracy tradeoffs.  
- Analyzes tradeoffs between labeling distortion and budget.  
- Proposes k-incidence coding, where workers label subsets of k items to exploit item relationships.  
- k-incidence coding is clearly described and intuitively motivated to improve accuracy.
