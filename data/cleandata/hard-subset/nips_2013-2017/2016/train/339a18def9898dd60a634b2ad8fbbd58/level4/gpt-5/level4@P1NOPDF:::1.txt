The paper models crowdsourced data labeling as communication over noisy channels. Each worker is treated as a noisy channel; two worker models are considered: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and the spammer/hammer model. Under this formulation, the labeling task becomes a coding problem for the channel in question, and the annotation budget is mapped to the code rate for analysis. The paper studies the trade-off between distortion and budget and also examines a basic coding scheme, termed k-incidence coding, which requires each crowdsourcing task to involve labeling a subset of k items at once.
