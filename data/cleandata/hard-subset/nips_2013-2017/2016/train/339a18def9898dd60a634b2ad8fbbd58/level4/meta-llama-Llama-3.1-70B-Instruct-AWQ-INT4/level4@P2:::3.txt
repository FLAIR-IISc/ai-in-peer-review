This paper addresses the problem of aggregating worker output in crowdsourcing using the language and tools of information theory, providing fundamental limits of the budget-fidelity tradeoff. The insights and model presented are interesting and potentially useful for designing crowdsourcing aggregation techniques.

The authors have made efforts to make the paper accessible to non-experts in information theory, but the paper remains challenging to follow. Additionally, the authors may consider mentioning the related line of research on robust economic incentives for workers, which also discusses varying worker abilities and combining these incentives with sophisticated aggregation methods.
