- Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC formulation of crowdsourcing and derives asymptotic limits on queries per item.  
- Models labels as i.i.d. source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as multiple channel uses, worker skills as random channel state, and crowdsourcer as decoder.  
- Leverages existing JSCC coding rate results to obtain asymptotic bounds and gives a lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specific model.  
- Paper is well-written and may be a pioneering application of JSCC to crowdsourcing.  
- Technical concern: needs justification or reference that source–channel separation holds when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder but possibly known at the decoder.  
- Modeling concern: assuming the taskmaster and crowdsourcer lack access to original data may be overly restrictive—side information at encoder/decoder should be considered.  
- Clarification needed: claim that kIC lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow is ambiguous—smaller lower bounds don’t necessarily indicate actual performance improvement without upper bounds for comparison.  
- Suggestion: consider non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) converses for JSCC for practical relevance, e.g., Kostina & Verdú (2013).
