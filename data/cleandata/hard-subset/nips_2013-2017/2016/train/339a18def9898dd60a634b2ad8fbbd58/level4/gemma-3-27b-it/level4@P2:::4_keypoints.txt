- Paper models crowdsourcing as human-in-the-loop computation and analyzes ultimate fidelity vs. query budget using an information-theoretic joint source-channel coding framework.

- Authors derive fundamental budget–fidelity bounds treating workers as noisy channels and evaluate k-ary incidence coding against these limits.

- Strength: novel application of information theory to crowdsourcing that provides rigorous benchmarks and may inspire future optimization work.

- Strength: clear problem statement and mathematically rigorous analysis of fundamental limits.

- Limitation: model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel characteristic, ignoring heterogeneous skills and reputation systems.

- Limitation: assumes uniform query allocation across workers, which is unrealistic for platforms that use worker ratings or targeted assignment.

- Limitation: analysis does not account for adversarial or malicious workers who deliberately degrade fidelity.

- Suggestion: extend or discuss model extensions to incorporate worker ratings, non-uniform query allocation, and heterogeneous skill levels.

- Suggestion: acknowledge and sketch defenses or modeling approaches for adversarial worker behavior.

- Presentation issue: numerical results (Figure 2) are unclear; curve labels should be improved and the k=1 case needs clearer explanation relative to k-ary incidence coding.

- Presentation issue: the assumption that P(B(X)) is known (page 6, line 246) should be stated earlier in the paper.

- Presentation issue: minor figure reference errors (page 4, lines 135–136) — Figure 1a/1b references are swapped.

- Presentation issue: use of “Theorem,” “Lemma,” and “Corollary” without numbering is distracting—either number them or use descriptive statements.

- Arguments for acceptance: novel perspective, useful benchmark of fundamental limits, and rigorous formulation.

- Arguments against acceptance: simplifying assumptions limit real-world applicability, lack of adversarial analysis, and presentation/clarity issues.

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address worker-skill modeling, adversarial behavior, and clarity of numerical results.
