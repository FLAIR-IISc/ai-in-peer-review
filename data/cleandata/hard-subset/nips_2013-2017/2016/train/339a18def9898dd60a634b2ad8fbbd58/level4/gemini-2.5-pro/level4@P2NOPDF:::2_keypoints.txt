- Novel application: Models crowdsourcing as a Joint Source-Channel Coding (JSCC) problem, deriving asymptotic query limits and a lower bound for k-ary incidence coding.  
- Strength: Provides a principled information-theoretic perspective and is well-written with an interesting model.  
- Major concern 1: The use of the source-channel separation theorem in Theorems 2–3 is not justified for channels with unknown random states and requires proof or citation.  
- Major concern 2: The model omits realistic side information (the items) available to encoder/decoder, which could fundamentally change the limits and should be incorporated.  
- Major concern 3: Results are asymptotic; a finite-blocklength (non-asymptotic) analysis is needed for practical relevance (suggested reference: Kostina & Verdú 2013).  
- Minor issue: Wording in Figure 2 is ambiguous—state that the error-probability lower bound decreases with k and q rather than saying the bound “improves.”  
- Overall: Promising direction but theoretical claims need stronger justification and the model should be extended to include side information and non-asymptotic analysis.
