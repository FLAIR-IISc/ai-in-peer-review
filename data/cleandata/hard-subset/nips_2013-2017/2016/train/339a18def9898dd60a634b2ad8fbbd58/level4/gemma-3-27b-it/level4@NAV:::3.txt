This paper tackles the problem of aggregating results from crowdsourcing workers through the lens of information theory, establishing fundamental limits on the budget-fidelity tradeoff. Despite finding the core insights and the proposed model to be a compelling and potentially valuable contribution to the design of crowdsourcing aggregation methods, I admittedly struggled with fully understanding the detailed technical development. 

I believe the high-level implications of the results – such as those articulated at the end of Section 3 – could be made more impactful by emphasizing them more explicitly throughout the paper. A concluding section summarizing key takeaways for practitioners would also be beneficial, even acknowledging space constraints. The authors have made commendable efforts to explain the concepts for readers less familiar with information theory, but the paper remains challenging to follow for those without a strong background in the field. 

I recommend re-expressing the core results in the context of the crowdsourcing application itself, moving away from the technical language of channels, codes, and distortion. Furthermore, the related work section should acknowledge the growing body of research on robust economic incentives for workers in crowdsourcing, often framed as “crowdsourcing mechanisms” or “mechanism design for crowdsourcing.” This related work often addresses worker heterogeneity and increasingly explores combining incentive structures with advanced aggregation techniques.

Several minor points should be addressed: On line 84, “is reviewed in” should likely be “are reviewed in.” Line 106 presented a point of confusion regarding the roles of the “crowdsourcer” and “taskmaster,” potentially stemming from the inherent ambiguity of the term “crowdsourcing” itself. On line 122, please insert a space between the two “A”s in “A A(X1,X2)”. The phrasing on lines 275-276 (“In case…”) was difficult to parse and should be clarified. Finally, Figure 2 was not readily interpretable; a more detailed caption would significantly improve its value.
