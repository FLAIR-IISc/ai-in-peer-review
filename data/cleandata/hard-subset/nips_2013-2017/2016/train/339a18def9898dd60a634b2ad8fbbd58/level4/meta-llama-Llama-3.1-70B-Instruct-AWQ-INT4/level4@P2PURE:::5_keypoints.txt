- Proposes an information-theoretic framework for digital crowdsourcing, deriving lower bounds on queries per task to achieve a target error probability.
- Models workers as parallel independent channels using two channel types: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (SDMC) and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q).
- Assumes a nonadaptive coding scheme for task assignment.
- Analyzes two cases: crowdsourcer knows all worker skill levels, and crowdsourcer does not know them.
- Assumes the taskmaster is unaware of worker skills, leading to equal task assignment across workers.
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum required rate for k-ary incidence coding with an SHC(q) worker pool.
- Paper is well-organized and well-written.
- Lacks experimental or simulation results to demonstrate the gap between current practical schemes (e.g., AMT) and the proposed limits.
- Sections 2.4 and 3.1 could be condensed; inclusion of theorem proof sketches would be more valuable.
- Appears novel as the first analysis of crowdsourcing from an information-theoretic perspective.
- Framework could help designers determine minimum rates for tasks and inform pricing decisions.
- Reviewer did not read the appendix and therefore cannot comment on the formal proofs.
