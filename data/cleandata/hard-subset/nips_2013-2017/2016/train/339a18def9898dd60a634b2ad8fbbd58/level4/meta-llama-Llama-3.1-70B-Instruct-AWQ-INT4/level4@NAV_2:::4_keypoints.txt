- Novel mapping of crowdsourcing to a joint source–channel coding / rate–distortion framework.  
- Derives a lower bound on query budget for a target fidelity that is independent of query design and aggregation algorithm.  
- The mapping is interesting and could spur further information-theoretic work on crowdsourcing.  
- Concern that forcing an exact point-to-point communication mapping reduces realism for real crowdsourcing systems.  
- Assumes equal query allocation to all workers, ignoring worker skill levels or ratings.  
- Worker channel models are simplistic and do not capture partially informed or adversarial worker behavior.  
- Numerical results are not clearly explained; Fig. 2 curve labeling needs improvement and the k=1 case is mischaracterized.  
- Assumes knowledge of P(B(X)) but does not state this early enough in the paper.  
- Contains minor errors (incorrect figure references) and inconsistent use of “Theorem/Lemma/Corollary” capitalization.  
- Technical quality: sound methodology but questionable realism of assumptions.  
- Clarity: generally well written but hampered by unclear explanations and figure issues.  
- Originality: novel application of information theory, but limited by simplistic models of worker behavior.  
- Significance: has potential impact if realism and clarity concerns are addressed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear exposition, potential contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unrealistic assumptions, inadequate channel/worker models, and unclear numerical presentation.
