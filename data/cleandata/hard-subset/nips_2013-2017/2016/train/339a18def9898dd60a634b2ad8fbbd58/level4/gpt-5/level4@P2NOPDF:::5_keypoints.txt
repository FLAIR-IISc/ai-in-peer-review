- Paper models crowdsourcing via information theory and derives lower bounds on queries per task to hit a target error probability.
- Workers are modeled as independent parallel channels with two channel types analyzed: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) and spammer–hammer channel SHC(q).
- Coding scheme analyzed is nonadaptive.
- Two information regimes considered for the crowdsourcer: complete knowledge of worker skills vs no knowledge.
- Taskmaster is assumed unaware of worker skills and assigns the same number of tasks to each worker.
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under SHC(q).
- Paper is well organized, clearly written, and presents a novel, principled formulation with potential practical guidance for system design and pricing.
- Major weakness: no empirical validation or simulations to assess tightness of bounds or performance gaps with real-world schemes.
- Exposition suggestions: shorten Section 2.4 and condense or remove Section 3.1; add brief proof sketches or intuitive explanations for main theorems.
- Overall assessment: strong and timely contribution, but proofs’ completeness/technical correctness not evaluated (appendix not read).
