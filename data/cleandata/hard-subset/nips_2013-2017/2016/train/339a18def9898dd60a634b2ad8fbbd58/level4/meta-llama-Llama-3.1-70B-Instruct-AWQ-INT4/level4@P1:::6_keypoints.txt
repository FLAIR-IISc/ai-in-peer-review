- Provides an information-theoretic bound on budget–fidelity tradeoff for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion and channel coding; derivation is solid and potentially novel.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable since the derivation is relatively straightforward and the bound may be loose.  
- Main concern: crowdsourcing lacks the encoding freedom of channel coding, so capacity-based bounds may not be applicable or tight.  
- Specific issue: you cannot query the same worker repeatedly (breaks i.i.d. assumption), unlike repeating symbols over a communication channel.  
- There are likely fundamental constraints on the set of allowable encodings in Eq.(3) that the paper does not model, risking loose bounds.  
- The paper should compare the derived bound against state-of-the-art methods empirically.  
- Minor: In (8) the minimization constraint over C, C' with E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t should be clarified.  
- Minor: The proof of Theorem 4 needs more elaboration.  
- Minor: In Theorem 4 clarify signs (ln(1−q)<0) and whether ε_S is larger than ĥε.  
- Minor: In (17) justify or cite why H_M(E(ε)) appears.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured introduction and good overview of relevant theory.  
- Weakness: potentially loose bound, lack of SOTA comparison, and missing discussion of encoding constraints.  
- For acceptance: offers a new theoretical perspective and a well-derived information-theoretic bound.  
- For rejection: limited novelty, risk of loose/inactionable bounds, and insufficient empirical/theoretical constraints analysis.  
- Overall: interesting direction but needs stronger novelty, practical validation, and treatment of encoding constraints.
