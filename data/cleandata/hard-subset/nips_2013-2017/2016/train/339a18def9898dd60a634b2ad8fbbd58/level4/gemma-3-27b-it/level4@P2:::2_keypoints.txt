- Novel framing: models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with randomly skilled workers, a new and valuable perspective.  
- Main result: derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item to achieve given fidelity for known/unknown worker skill.  
- Modeling clarity: maps items → source, workers → noisy channels, queries → signals, aggregation → decoding, enabling use of information-theoretic tools.  
- Leverages theory: uses source-channel separation theorem and JSCC results to establish lower bounds on query complexity.  
- Specific scheme: analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and provides a lower bound on its error probability.  
- Strength — worker skill: explicitly accounts for imperfect and random worker skill levels in the analysis.  
- Strength — presentation: paper is generally well-written and provides a solid foundation for future work.  
- Weakness — separation reliance: heavy reliance on source-channel separation without explicit justification when channel state (worker skill) is unknown to the encoder.  
- Weakness — missing side information: model ignores using original item data as side information for encoding/decoding, limiting realism and potential tightness of bounds.  
- Weakness — asymptotic focus: results are asymptotic (large number of items/workers), which may limit practical applicability to finite-resource settings.  
- Clarity issue: claim that kIC bounds “improve” with larger k and q should state the lower bound decreases (i.e., performance improves) to avoid misinterpretation.  
- Non-asymptotic gap: lacks finite-blocklength/non-asymptotic analysis; suggesting incorporation of Kostina & Verdu (2013) style results would increase practical relevance.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, theoretically sound, provides fundamental insights, opens new research directions, and is well-presented.  
- Rejection concerns: asymptotic limitations, simplified model (no side information), and missing justification for applying separation theorem.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — address source-channel separation justification, acknowledge asymptotic limitations, and consider side information in future work.
