- Proposes a model for crowdsourced labeling treating each worker as a noisy communication channel.
- Analyzes two channel types: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer models.
- Frames crowdsourcing labeling as a coding problem to study optimal strategies.
- Maps the labeling budget to code rate, linking resource allocation with coding theory.
- Studies trade-offs between distortion (labeling error) and budget (cost/queries).
- Introduces and analyzes k-incidence coding, where k items are labeled jointly.
