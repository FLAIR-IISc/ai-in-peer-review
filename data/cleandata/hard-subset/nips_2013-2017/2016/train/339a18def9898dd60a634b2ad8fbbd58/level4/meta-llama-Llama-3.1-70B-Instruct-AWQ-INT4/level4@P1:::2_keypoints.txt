- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and maps labels/taskmaster/workers/crowdsourcer to source/encoder/channel/state/decoder.  
- Authors use existing JSCC coding rate results to derive asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item.  
- Paper provides a lower bound on the error probability of a "k-ary incidence coding" scheme under a specific crowdsourcing model.  
- The paper is well-written and the JSCC modeling approach is a promising first step toward fundamental limits of crowdsourcing.  
- The authors should better justify applying the standard source-channel separation theorem when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder.  
- The model is potentially too restrictive because it ignores that the taskmaster and crowdsourcer may have access to the original data (side information).  
- The authors should incorporate side information for encoder and decoder into the model to make it more realistic.  
- Rephrase Fig. 2 commentary: the lower bound becomes smaller as k and q grow, indicating potential performance improvement.  
- The work is original in using JSCC for crowdsourcing, but would be more useful with non-asymptotic characterizations.  
- The authors should consider recent non-asymptotic JSCC converses (e.g., Kostina and Verdú) to strengthen results.  
- Strength: novel information-theoretic treatment of crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: clear use of JSCC results to establish asymptotic query limits.  
- Weakness: restrictive model that ignores possible side information at encoder/decoder.  
- Weakness: insufficient justification for separation theorem under random-state channels.  
- Recommendation: accept but require the authors to address technical comments, incorporate side information, and explore non-asymptotic converses.
