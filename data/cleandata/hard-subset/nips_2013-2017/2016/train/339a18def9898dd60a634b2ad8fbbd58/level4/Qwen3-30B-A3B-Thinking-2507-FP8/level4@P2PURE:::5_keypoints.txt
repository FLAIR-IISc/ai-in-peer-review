- Models crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic framework to derive lower bounds on queries per task for a target error probability.
- Workers are modeled as parallel independent channels: symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) for errors and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q) for spammer behavior.
- Assumes nonadaptive coding schemes and that the taskmaster is unaware of worker skills, so tasks are equally assigned.
- Considers two crowdsourcer knowledge cases: (1) knows all worker skills, (2) knows nothing about worker skills.
- Derives a necessary condition on the minimum required rate (queries per task) for k-ary incidence coding under an SHC(q) worker pool.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.
- Lacks experimental or simulation results comparing derived bounds to practical crowdsourcing platforms (e.g., Mechanical Turk).
- Suggests condensing Section 2.4 and shortening or omitting Section 3.1; recommends including proof sketches rather than full proofs in the main text.
- Work appears novel and correctly claims no prior information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing.
- Results are practically useful for designers to determine minimum query rates and inform project pricing.
- Reviewer did not check the appendix and therefore cannot comment on the proofs.
