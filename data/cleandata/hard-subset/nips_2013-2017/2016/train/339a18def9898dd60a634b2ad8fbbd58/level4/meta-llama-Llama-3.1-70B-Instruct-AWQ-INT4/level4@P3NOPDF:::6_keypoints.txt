- Paper derives an information-theoretic bound on the budget–fidelity tradeoff for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion theory and channel coding; derivation is sound and could be important if this is the first such application.

- Novelty and practical value are questionable because the derivation is relatively straightforward and the bound may be very loose.

- Major concern: unlike channel coding, crowdsourcing may lack freedom to design the channel inputs u^n, so capacity-based bounds may not apply tightly.

- Specific issue: you cannot assign the same query to the same worker repeatedly (it would break the iid assumption), limiting error-correcting strategies available in standard channel coding.

- There are likely fundamental constraints on valid encoding mappings in equation (3), so many theoretical codebooks may be infeasible in practice, risking a loose bound.

- Recommendation: empirically compare the derived bound with state-of-the-art crowdsourcing methods to assess tightness and practical relevance.

- Minor: clarify that the minimization in equation (8) is over C and C' subject to E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t.

- Minor: expand and clarify the proof of Theorem 4 to improve readability.

- Minor: clarify whether ε_S is larger than ĥε given ln(1−q) < 0 and explain the relation.

- Minor: justify or cite why H_M(E(ε)) appears in equation (17).
