- Models crowdsourced labeling by representing each worker as a noisy channel.
- Considers two channel types: symmetric memoryless channels without feedback and spammer/hammer models.
- Frames the labeling problem as a coding problem for the chosen channel models.
- Maps the crowdsourcing budget to the code rate for analysis.
- Studies trade-offs between distortion (labeling error) and budget (rate).
- Proposes and analyzes a basic k-incidence coding scheme where tasks ask workers to label subsets of k items.
