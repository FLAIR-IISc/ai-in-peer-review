- Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC framework for crowdsourcing, modeling labels as an i.i.d. source, workers as channel uses, and worker skill as random channel state, and derives asymptotic queries-per-item limits via JSCC coding-rate results.
- Derives a lower bound on the error probability of the k-ary incidence coding (kIC) scheme under a specific crowdsourcing/channel model.
- Paper is clearly written and represents an initial, interesting step toward JSCC-based modeling of crowdsourcing.
- Technical concern: the paper invokes source–channel separation (Theorems 2 and 3) but must justify or cite that separation holds when the channel has a random state unknown at the encoder and possibly known/unknown at the decoder.
- Modeling concern: treating the taskmaster as observing only labels and the crowdsourcer only worker outputs ignores possible side information from original items at encoder/decoder, which can affect performance and should be incorporated.
- Clarification needed: stating that kIC lower bounds “improve” with larger k and q is misleading—lower bounds decreasing does not imply absolute performance improvement without accompanying upper bounds for comparison.
- Post-review suggestion: include nonasymptotic/finite-blocklength analyses or cite relevant finite-blocklength JSCC converses (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013).
