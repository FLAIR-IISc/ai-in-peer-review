- Introduces an information-theoretic framework to derive lower bounds on queries per task for digital crowdsourcing.
- Models workers as parallel independent channels using Symmetric DMCs and a Spammer-Hammer Channel (SHC).
- Analyzes non-adaptive, uniformly distributed task assignments where the taskmaster lacks worker skill knowledge.
- Derives performance bounds for the crowdsourcer under known and unknown worker skill scenarios.
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum query rate for k-ary incidence coding under the SHC model.
- Strength: Novel application of information theory to establish fundamental performance limits for crowdsourcing.
- Strength: Results can guide practitioners on trade-offs between cost, reliability, and task complexity.
- Strength: Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making complex theory accessible.
- Weakness: No empirical validation or simulations to compare theoretical bounds with practical heuristics or real platforms.
- Weakness: Presentation could be improved—some sections should be condensed and proof sketches added to the main text.
- Final assessment: A strong, novel theoretical contribution that opens promising directions despite lacking empirical comparison.
- Reviewer note: Detailed proofs in the appendix were not verified by the reviewer.
