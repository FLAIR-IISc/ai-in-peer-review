- Novel JSCC-based formulation for crowdsourcing with clear mapping (i.i.d. labels, taskmaster=encoder, workers=channel with skill-based states, crowdsourcer=decoder); well-structured and promising.
- Main theoretical issue: invoking source–channel separation (Theorems 2–3) without justification for the case where the encoder lacks channel state information — needs a proof or literature citation.
- Model omission: encoder/decoder are assumed to observe only labels and not original item data; ignoring side information (especially at the decoder) can substantially overestimate query complexity and should be addressed.
- Terminology error: Section 4.2’s claim that “lower bounds … improve as k and q grow” is misleading — lower bounds on error probability decrease (indicating better performance), and this should be stated precisely.
- Practicality gap: analysis is purely asymptotic; the paper should incorporate nonasymptotic/finite-blocklength results (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) to characterize trade-offs for finite datasets.
- Recommendation: explicitly justify separation, incorporate/Discuss side information, correct bound interpretation, and add nonasymptotic analysis to strengthen contribution and relevance.
- Writing/readability: generally clear, but the above technical clarifications and revisions are necessary for full impact.
