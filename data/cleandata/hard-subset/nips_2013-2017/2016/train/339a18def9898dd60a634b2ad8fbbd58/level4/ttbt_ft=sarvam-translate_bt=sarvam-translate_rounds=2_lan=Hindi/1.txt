This research paper models the problem of labeling data obtained from crowd-sourcing. Each worker is represented as a noisy channel (two types of channels are considered: symmetric memory-less channels without feedback, and spammer/hammer channel models). The problem then becomes a coding problem for that channel, which the authors studied. The budget used is mapped to the rate of code for analysis. This research paper considers the distortion versus budget tradeoff and also studies a basic coding scheme (called k-incidence coding), which is consistent with the need to label a subset of k items over time of crowd-sourced tasks. I found this research paper quite interesting, but since it is outside my area of expertise, there is no detailed comment on it.
