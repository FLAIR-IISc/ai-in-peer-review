- Models crowdsourced labeling as communication over noisy channels, treating each worker as a channel.  
- Analyzes two channel types: a symmetric memoryless channel without feedback and a spammer/hammer model.  
- Casts the annotation task as a channel-coding problem, interpreting the annotation budget as the code rate.  
- Studies the distortion–budget trade-off for the crowdsourcing setup.  
- Evaluates a basic coding scheme, k‑incidence coding, where each worker labels a subset of k items.
