- Paper presents an information-theoretic JSCC model of crowdsourcing and derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item.  
- Models: item labels as i.i.d. source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses, worker skill as random channel state, crowdsourcer as decoder.  
- Uses existing JSCC coding rate results and the source–channel separation theorem to obtain main theorems (Theorems 2 and 3).  
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under the proposed channel model.  
- Reviewer finds the paper well-written and a valuable initial JSCC-based approach to crowdsourcing.  
- Technical concern: authors must justify applicability of the source–channel separation theorem when the channel has a random state unknown to the encoder but possibly known to the decoder (provide proof or citation).  
- Model limitation: restricting taskmaster/crowdsourcer to observe only labels ignores potential side information from original items that could be available to encoder/decoder and improve performance.  
- Clarification needed in Section 4.2: claiming kIC lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow requires comparison to an upper bound to demonstrate actual improvement.  
- Suggestion: pursue nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) characterizations; reviewer recommends citing Kostina & Verdú (2013) on finite-blocklength JSCC converses.
