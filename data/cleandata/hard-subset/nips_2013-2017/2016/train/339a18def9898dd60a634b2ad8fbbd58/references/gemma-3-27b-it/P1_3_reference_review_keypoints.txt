- Paper formulates crowdsourcing as a communication system and derives information-theoretic lower bounds on budget vs. fidelity.  
- Considers both known and unknown worker skill scenarios and introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a query scheme.  
- Builds on prior CS aggregation and worker-selection work, differing by focusing on fundamental limits rather than algorithm design.  
- Explicitly connects to classical information theory and extends incidence coding literature.  
- Theoretical rigor provides fundamental benchmarks for CS performance.  
- The communication-modeling is clear and captures key CS components (source, workers as channels, queries, decoding).  
- Derives novel budget-fidelity bounds for both known- and unknown-skill cases.  
- kIC offers practical potential through error-correction advantages and a discussion of query pricing.  
- Analysis covers multiple worker models (MSC and SHC), increasing robustness.  
- Paper is well-structured and logically organized.  
- Relies on simplifying assumptions (independent workers, i.i.d. data, perfect knowledge in one case) that limit realism.  
- Asymptotic (n → ∞) analysis limits direct applicability to finite datasets.  
- Lacks empirical validation or simulations to demonstrate bound tightness on real or synthetic data.  
- kIC analysis is complex, uses an oracle decoder, and practical decoding/implementation challenges are under-addressed.  
- Focuses on non-adaptive schemes and does not investigate potentially beneficial adaptive querying.  
- Query-pricing discussion is informal and needs a more detailed cost analysis.  
- Overall assessment: solid theoretical contribution that advances understanding of CS limits, despite practical and validation gaps.  
- Arguments for acceptance: provides fundamental limits, proposes kIC, presents clear analysis, and extends existing theory.  
- Arguments against acceptance: simplifying assumptions, no empirical validation, limited practical kIC discussion, and non-adaptive focus.  
- Recommendation: revise to discuss relaxed assumptions, add empirical validation, expand kIC implementation details, explore adaptive coding, and refine pricing analysis.  
- Reviewer decision: Accept with Minor Revisions.
