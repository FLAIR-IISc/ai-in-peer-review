- Overall: Valuable information-theoretic analysis of budget–fidelity trade-offs in crowdsourcing; recommend accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and contextualization.

- Summary: Models crowdsourcing as noisy communication channels, analyzes limits with known/unknown worker skills, introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and a query-pricing strategy, and situates work within prior aggregation and information-theory literature.

- Strength — Novelty & Rigor: Novel application of information theory with mathematically sound derivations (Theorems 2 and 3).

- Strength — Fundamental Insight: Provides a principled framework distinguishing limits when worker skill is known vs unknown.

- Strength — kIC: Proposes k-ary incidence coding as a promising error-correcting query design.

- Strength — Query Pricing: Considers query-pricing strategies, linking theoretical results to practical concerns.

- Weakness — Clarity & Accessibility: Paper is dense and heavily information-theoretic; needs more intuitive explanations and contextualization of equations.

- Weakness — Practical Relevance: Limited exploration of how theoretical bounds apply to real platforms (e.g., AMT) with more complex worker behavior.

- Weakness — Model Assumptions: MSC/SHC and i.i.d. source assumptions are simplifying and may limit result validity; impact of these assumptions should be discussed.

- Weakness — Empirical Validation: Primarily theoretical with limited numerical/empirical demonstration of kIC’s practical benefits.

- Weakness — kIC Presentation: kIC description needs clearer explanation (e.g., why certain responses are “invalid”).

- Evaluation Scores: Quality 8/10 (technically sound), Clarity 6/10 (needs improvement), Originality 9/10, Significance 7/10 (theoretically important; practical impact uncertain).

- Pro Arguments: Offers a new perspective, rigorous bounds, a novel query scheme, and deeper understanding of crowdsourcing trade-offs.

- Con Arguments: Technical density, limited empirical validation, and possibly unrealistic model assumptions.

- Questions for Authors: Request intuitive channel-model explanation; sensitivity of Theorems 2/3 to model deviations; computational challenges of kIC at scale; details on query-pricing (time/cost differences); plans for real-world evaluation.

- Final Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve readability, discuss model limitations, and pursue empirical validation.
