## Review of "Fundamental Limits of Budget-Fidelity Trade-off in Label Crowdsourcing"

**Summary:**

This paper investigates the fundamental limits of crowdsourcing, framing it as a communication problem with a taskmaster (transmitter) querying a crowd of workers (noisy channels) to determine the value of a function associated with a dataset. It establishes theoretical bounds on the trade-off between the crowdsourcing budget (number of queries) and fidelity (accuracy) under two scenarios: unknown worker skill levels (SL-UK) and perfectly known skill levels (SL-CS).  The authors also introduce and analyze *k*-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a potential query scheme, particularly focusing on its performance with spammer-hammer channels (SHC) and proposing a pricing strategy based on *k*.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds on a strong foundation of previous work in crowdsourcing, referencing key papers on aggregation schemes like majority voting and Dawid-Skene (DS) algorithms, as well as recent work on bandit approaches and worker skill estimation. The framing of crowdsourcing as an information-theoretic problem is a compelling and less explored avenue, distinguishing it from most empirical and algorithmic work. The connection to classical information theory concepts like rate-distortion theory and channel capacity is well-motivated. While the paper acknowledges related work in clustering with crowdsourcing, its focus on fundamental limits offers a more general perspective.  The kIC scheme connects to earlier work on incidence matrix based labeling but extends it to the k-ary case and analyzes its theoretical properties.

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Theoretical Foundation:** The paper’s strength lies in its rigorous information-theoretic analysis. Establishing fundamental limits provides valuable insight into the inherent constraints of crowdsourcing, regardless of specific algorithmic choices.
* **Novel Framing:** Viewing crowdsourcing through the lens of communication theory is a significant contribution, offering a different and potentially powerful perspective.
* **Clear Mathematical Formulation:** The model and derived theorems are clearly presented and mathematically sound. The derivations appear careful and well-justified (although a deeper dive into the proofs would be beneficial – see weaknesses).
* **kIC Analysis:** The introduction and analysis of kIC is a valuable contribution. The analysis with the SHC model is particularly insightful and provides a concrete example of how the theoretical bounds translate to a practical scenario. The associated pricing suggestion is a good, albeit preliminary, direction.
* **Comprehensive Related Work:** The introduction provides a thorough overview of relevant crowdsourcing literature.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Practical Implications (Currently):** While the theoretical bounds are valuable, their practical utility is not fully demonstrated. The bounds are often expressed in terms of entropy and channel capacity, which can be difficult to estimate in real-world crowdsourcing scenarios.  A discussion of how these bounds could be used to *guide* practical system design would be helpful.
* **Assumptions and Simplifications:** The analysis relies on several simplifying assumptions (e.g., independent workers, memoryless channels, uniformly distributed data). While necessary for theoretical tractability, the paper could benefit from a discussion of the impact of relaxing these assumptions.  For example, how does the analysis change with correlated worker responses or non-uniform data distributions?
* **Proof Details:**  The paper refers to proofs in the appendices, but these are not included in the submitted version.  A complete paper *must* include the proofs for a thorough review. The complexity of the proofs is understandable, but their absence hinders a complete assessment of the theoretical validity.
* **kIC Numerical Results:** While the numerical results for kIC are interesting, the comparisons between the theoretical bounds and the performance of kIC are not entirely convincing. The plots are somewhat limited in scope and the benefits of kIC aren't drastically shown over baseline (which is currently missing).  A more extensive empirical evaluation, perhaps including comparisons with existing crowdsourcing algorithms, would strengthen this aspect of the work.
* **Discussion of Worker Skill Estimation:** The SL-CS scenario assumes *perfect* worker skill estimation. This is a strong assumption, and the paper would benefit from a discussion of the practical challenges of skill estimation and how imperfect estimation would affect the bounds.



**Arguments for and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Groundbreaking theoretical approach to a crucial problem in machine learning.
* Rigorous mathematical analysis and well-defined model.
* Novel contribution in the form of kIC and its analysis.
* Strong grounding in relevant literature.

**Against Acceptance:**

* Limited discussion of practical implications and real-world applicability.
* Several simplifying assumptions that may limit the generalizability of the results.
* Missing proofs in the submission.
* Numerical results for kIC are not sufficiently compelling.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong theoretical paper with significant potential to impact the field of crowdsourcing.  The framing of the problem in terms of information theory is novel and insightful, and the derived bounds provide fundamental limits on performance. However, the limited discussion of practical implications and the absence of proofs currently weaken the paper. 

**Recommendation:**

**Conditional Acceptance.** I recommend acceptance *contingent on* the authors providing complete proofs in the final version and expanding the discussion of practical implications.  Strengthening the numerical results for kIC and addressing the points raised regarding assumptions and worker skill estimation would further enhance the paper’s quality. Addressing these points would elevate this work from a theoretically interesting contribution to one with significant practical relevance.
