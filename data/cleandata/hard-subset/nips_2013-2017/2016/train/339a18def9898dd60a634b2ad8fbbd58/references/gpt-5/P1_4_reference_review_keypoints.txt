- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding problem to derive budget–fidelity trade-offs using Shannon separation.  
- Items are a source B(X); workers are noisy channels (MSC or SHC); queries are codes; the decoder is the crowdsourcer’s inference.  
- Derives lower bounds on queries per item (rate) to achieve target Hamming distortion using source R(D) over channel capacity.  
- Treats two cases: SL-UK (unknown worker skill) and SL-CS (skill known to crowdsourcer), yielding different capacity formulations.  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC), exploiting invalid adjacency patterns as built-in error-correction.  
- For SHC workers, derives spammer error probability under kIC and a necessary condition on query rate with an oracle hammer detector.  
- Provides a corollary specializing the information-theoretic limit to the SHC model and briefly discusses kIC pricing.  
- Builds on Dawid–Skene, EM aggregation, worker-selection, and spectral/convex aggregation literature, offering a channel-agnostic lower-bound perspective.  
- Technical approach using separation is appropriate and asymptotically sound; SL-CS and SL-UK capacity expressions are plausible for symmetric mixtures.  
- Source R(D)=H(B)−H_N(D) is valid under stated distortion thresholds, and kIC lower bound via “no hammer labels” is a clean necessary condition.  
- Main proofs are deferred to appendices; key steps (mixture-channel capacity, R(D) conditions, Lemma 1) need clearer exposition in the main text.  
- No empirical validation or simulations provided to assess bound tightness or practical benefits of kIC.  
- Manuscript has numerous typos and notation glitches (missing ε, inconsistent M vs N, H_M vs H_N, reference errors) that hinder readability.  
- Specific clarity fixes needed: define H_M(·) and E(ε) in bounds, state R(D) equality conditions, explain Lemma 1 intuition, and define R′ = kR in Theorem 4.  
- Pricing rule discussion likely contains a typo (π(k1)/π(k2) ≈ k1/k2) and is underdeveloped.  
- Originality: separation-based limits for SL-UK/SL-CS and SHC kIC analysis are novel and provide useful scheme-agnostic bounds.  
- Significance: offers conceptual guidance on limits with/without skill knowledge and benefits of structured queries, informing CS system design.  
- Weaknesses: no simulations, idealized modeling assumptions (iid, non-adaptive, no item difficulty), presentation and notation issues, and weak pricing discussion.  
- Suggested improvements: add simulations comparing bounds to majority/DS/spectral-EM and kIC decoders; fix notation/equations; discuss adaptive querying, item difficulty, partial state estimation, and finite-blocklength effects.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject pending clearer exposition and empirical validation; with fixes and experiments the work could become a strong contribution.
