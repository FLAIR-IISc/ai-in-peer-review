- Summary: Proposes an information-theoretic framework modeling workers as noisy channels, derives bounds on queries vs. error, analyzes SL-UK and SL-CS scenarios, and introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC).
- Strength — Quality: Technically rigorous with formal proofs, solid derivations, and numerical results that validate theoretical bounds.
- Strength — Originality: Novel application of information theory to crowdsourcing and the new kIC scheme that leverages query design for error correction.
- Strength — Significance: Provides fundamental limits with clear practical implications for budget-fidelity trade-offs and crowdsourcing system design.
- Strength — Clarity: Well-organized and consistently notated; figures aid understanding, though Sections 2–3 may be dense for non-information-theory readers.
- Weakness — Limited empirical validation: No experiments on real crowdsourcing data to confirm theoretical predictions.
- Weakness — Narrow channel models: Analysis restricted to MSC and SHC, which may not capture complex real worker behaviors.
- Weakness — Underdeveloped query pricing: Appendix E’s pricing rule-of-thumb lacks sufficient justification or analysis.
- Overall assessment: Strong, novel theoretical contribution that advances understanding of crowdsourcing limits and is well-executed.
- Recommendation: Accept — theoretical strengths and significance outweigh the lack of empirical validation.
