- Studies budget–fidelity trade-off in crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic communication-system model and derives bounds for unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skills.
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) scheme and discusses implications for query pricing and spammer-hammer workers.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical framework providing fundamental limits rather than algorithmic tweaks.
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated problem formulation modeling CS as a communication system.
- Strength: Novel kIC analysis with insightful bounds, especially under spammer-hammer assumptions.
- Strength: Considers two scenarios (SL-UK and SL-CS), clarifying the impact of skill knowledge on performance.
- Strength: Properly situates work within related literature on aggregation, inference, and worker modeling.
- Weakness: Limited practical guidance — assumptions (large block length, perfect channel estimation) may not hold in real systems.
- Weakness: Results assume uniform source distribution; sensitivity to non-uniform datasets is not explored.
- Weakness: Practical implementation and computational complexity of kIC (large k) are not thoroughly addressed.
- Weakness: Lacks empirical validation or simulations to demonstrate tightness of theoretical bounds.
- Weakness: Some sections (3.1, 3.2) are dense with information-theory background and may be hard for non-experts.
- Quality: Mathematically sound and logically presented but weakened by absence of experiments and limited practical translation.
- Clarity: Generally well-written with helpful figures, though parts are dense for readers without an information-theory background.
- Originality: Novel combination of information-theoretic analysis with crowdsourcing and original kIC scheme.
- Significance: Establishes important fundamental limits and benchmarks that can guide future CS algorithm design.
- Pro acceptance: Provides valuable theoretical insights, novel kIC, rigorous analysis, and addresses an important problem.
- Con acceptance: Limited real-world applicability, no empirical validation, potentially unrealistic assumptions, and kIC complexity concerns.
- Overall: Reviewer recommends strong accept for NIPS, urging addition of simulations in future versions.
- Confidence: 8/10.
