- Frames crowdsourcing as a communication system and applies information theory to study budget–fidelity trade-offs.  
- Derives theoretical bounds on minimum budget for given fidelity using entropy and channel-capacity concepts.  
- Analyzes scenarios with unknown and perfectly known worker skill levels (SL-CS vs SHC).  
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a novel potential query/coding scheme.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical formalism and a clear problem formulation.  
- Demonstrates good knowledge of related work and identifies fundamental limits.  
- Main weakness: significant gap between theoretical results and practical crowdsourcing systems.  
- Bounds are abstract (entropy/capacity) and hard to translate into actionable guidance for practitioners.  
- Model relies on strong simplifying assumptions (worker independence, perfect channel estimation, SHC, binary focus) that limit realism.  
- kIC analysis is underdeveloped (mainly N=2 case), with missing complexity analysis and limited trade-off discussion.  
- Query pricing discussion is brief and lacks rigorous justification.  
- No empirical validation—no experiments or simulations to support bounds or kIC benefits.  
- Notationally dense and difficult to follow; readability needs improvement.  
- Questions about tightness of Theorems 2 & 3 and comparison to performance of DS or majority voting.  
- Concern about sensitivity of results to deviations from SHC and more realistic worker skill distributions.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject, requiring substantial revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: add empirical validation, connect theory to existing algorithms, relax assumptions, expand kIC analysis to N>2 and analyze complexity, and streamline notation.
