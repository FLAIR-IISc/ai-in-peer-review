- Frames crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding problem, models workers as MSC/SHC, and derives info-theoretic lower bounds on queries-per-item vs error for SL-UK and SL-CS; analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and proposes a pricing rule-of-thumb.  
- Properly situates work relative to aggregation/inference and worker-selection literature; applies classical source–channel separation ideas to crowdsourcing.  
- Separation-theorem-based rate lower bounds are technically sound under iid source, memoryless channels, infinite blocklength, and non-adaptive coding assumptions.  
- Correct and insightful distinction between “capacity of the average worker” (unknown state) and “average capacity” (state known at decoder) explained via entropy concavity.  
- Notation and statement accuracy issues: ambiguous definitions (e.g., HM(·), ε), confusing M vs N, unclear thresholds (e.g., min{1−pmax,1−1/N}), and some theorem statements appear inconsistent.  
- kIC bound concerns: relies on an oracle for detecting hammers, likely gives a loose necessary bound, seems to omit the target error in the statement, and Lemma 1’s closed form lacks in-text derivation.  
- No achievability results, constructive schemes, or practical algorithms are provided to approach the derived bounds.  
- Numerical/experimental support is missing—only a single schematic figure is shown, no empirical comparisons to DS/spectral baselines.  
- Several references are incomplete (missing years) and formatting glitches obscure equations and the pricing formula.  
- Clarity issues: inconsistent notation, malformed/missing equations, excessive reliance on appendices, and insufficiently precise description of kIC codebook and invalid-response sets.  
- Originality is moderate: clean synthesis of known information-theoretic theorems applied to crowdsourcing and an interesting invalid-response-as-error-correction angle for kIC, but largely a direct application of classical results.  
- Significance: useful conceptual guidance on budgets and value of skill estimation; practical impact limited without algorithms, empirical validation, or tighter bounds.  
- Strengths: principled information-theoretic framing, insightful SL-UK vs SL-CS comparison, identification of kIC redundancy, and qualitative guidance for query/pricing design.  
- Weaknesses: presentation and technical clarity problems, heavy reliance on asymptotic/idealized assumptions, likely loose kIC bounds, no finite-sample analysis, and limited treatment of adaptivity, heterogeneity, or adversarial behaviors.  
- Suggestions: fix notation/equations and references, correct Theorem 4 and pricing formula, add constructive schemes and simulations vs baselines, provide finite-sample/tightness analysis, and clarify mapping between M and N and heterogeneous query costs.  
- Recommendation: Reject (borderline) — promising conceptual framing but needs clearer statements, achievable constructions, and empirical validation to be publishable.
