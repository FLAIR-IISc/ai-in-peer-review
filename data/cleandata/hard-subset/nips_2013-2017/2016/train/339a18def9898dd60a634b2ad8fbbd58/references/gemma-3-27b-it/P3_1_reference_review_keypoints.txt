- Frames crowdsourcing as a communication problem and derives fundamental budget–fidelity bounds for unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skills.  
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC), analyzes its performance under the spammer-hammer channel (SHC), and suggests a pricing strategy based on k.  
- Builds on prior crowdsourcing work (majority voting, Dawid–Skene, bandits) and uniquely applies information-theoretic concepts (rate–distortion, channel capacity).  
- Extends incidence-matrix labeling to the k-ary case and provides theoretical analysis of kIC.  
- Strength: strong, rigorous information-theoretic foundation giving fundamental limits independent of specific algorithms.  
- Strength: novel and compelling framing that offers a new perspective on crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: clear mathematical formulation and apparently careful derivations (proofs not included).  
- Strength: insightful kIC analysis with a concrete SHC example and preliminary pricing idea.  
- Strength: comprehensive and well-situated related-work discussion.  
- Weakness: limited demonstration of practical utility—bounds are in terms of entropy/capacity which are hard to estimate in real systems.  
- Weakness: simplifying assumptions (independent workers, memoryless channels, uniform data) may limit generality; lacks discussion of relaxing these assumptions.  
- Weakness: proofs are missing from the submission, preventing full validation of theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: kIC numerical results are limited in scope, lack baseline comparisons, and do not strongly demonstrate advantage.  
- Weakness: SL-CS assumes perfect skill estimation; lacks discussion of practical skill-estimation challenges and effects of imperfect estimates.  
- For acceptance: novel theoretical approach, rigorous analysis, kIC contribution, and strong literature grounding.  
- Against acceptance: limited practical applicability, strong assumptions, missing proofs, and unconvincing empirical results.  
- Overall assessment: strong theoretical contribution with high potential but weakened by missing proofs and insufficient practical evaluation.  
- Recommendation: conditional acceptance pending inclusion of complete proofs, expanded discussion of practical implications/assumptions, and stronger empirical evaluation of kIC.
