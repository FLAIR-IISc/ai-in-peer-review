- Proposes an information-theoretic framework for crowdsourcing treating task assignment + aggregation as joint source–channel coding.  
- Derives two fundamental budget–fidelity lower bounds (Theorems 2 & 3) for required queries per item under unknown vs known worker skills.  
- Shows expressions reduce to R ≥ [H(B) − HN(ε̂)] / [log M − HM(·)] with appropriate averaging over worker reliabilities.  
- Analyzes k-ary Incidence Coding (kIC), arguing structured queries provide intrinsic error-correction by invalidating many response configurations.  
- For SHC workers and binary labels, provides spammer-only error rate (Lemma 1) and a non-adaptive kIC budget lower bound (Theorem 4); corollary yields capacity q log M per query.  
- Suggests a rule-of-thumb pricing/effort ratio across different k values for kIC.  
- Connects to Dawid–Skene, spectral/EM methods, and reliability-aware task allocation literature, claiming complementary, code-agnostic limits.  
- Needs clearer positioning relative to sample-complexity and minimax-optimality results (e.g., Karger–Oh–Shah, Zhang et al.) and adaptive vs non-adaptive assignments.  
- Strength: elegant conceptualization as source–channel coding, distinguishing SL-UK vs SL-CS (“capacity of average worker” vs “average of capacities”).  
- Strength: generality—lower bounds apply to arbitrary non-adaptive query schemes and non-uniform N-ary sources.  
- Strength: kIC insight—explicit view of structured-query error-correction and intuitive q log M corollary.  
- Weakness: achievability not established—results read as lower bounds but rely on separation assumptions unrealistic for crowdsourcing (encoder cannot tailor codes to workers).  
- Weakness: ambiguous notation (M vs N, HM(·) vs HN(·)) and inconsistent use of E[HM(ε)] vs HM(E[ε]) that must be fixed.  
- Weakness: likely error in Theorem 4’s stated bound (missing ε̂, sign/log-base issues); needs correction and constants check.  
- Weakness: restrictive assumptions—i.i.d. labels, i.i.d. worker states, no task difficulty, non-adaptive assignments—limit practical relevance.  
- Weakness: no experiments or simulations comparing common algorithms (majority, DS, spectral/EM) to the bounds or validating kIC/pricing.  
- Weakness: presentation problems—typos, missing refs/figures, unclear appendices—hinder readability and credibility.  
- Significance: formalization and SL-UK vs SL-CS comparison are novel and valuable; kIC is interesting but currently limited to binary/SHC/oracle cases.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify coding constraints and achievability vs converse, include proof sketches, and explicitly state coding assumptions.  
- Suggested fixes: correct and extend Theorem 4 to N>2/non-uniform priors, and precisely define all notation (M, N, HM, HN, R, R′).  
- Suggested fixes: add numerical comparisons to DS/spectral/majority, discuss adaptive assignments, and evaluate kIC pricing/practicality (UI, cognitive load).  
- Suggested fixes: improve writing, fix references, and ensure all figures and appendices are clear and present.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending revisions—conceptually promising but needs technical fixes, clearer positioning, and empirical/algorithmic evidence.
