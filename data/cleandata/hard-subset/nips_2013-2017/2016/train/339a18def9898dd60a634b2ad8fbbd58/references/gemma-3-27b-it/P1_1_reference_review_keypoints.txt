- The paper provides a theoretical, information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing, deriving budget–fidelity lower bounds but remains abstract with limited practical instantiation.  
- Models crowdsourcing as a communication system: workers = noisy channels, tasks = source to encode; analyzes SL-UK (unknown skills) and SL-CS (known skills).  
- Introduces and analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a specific query scheme and briefly discusses query pricing.  
- Related work coverage is comprehensive, situating the paper beyond algorithmic improvements toward fundamental limits.  
- Strength: rigorous, well-defined information-theoretic framework that complements empirical/algorithmic studies.  
- Strength: derives fundamental lower bounds on queries required for target fidelity, providing theoretical benchmarks.  
- Strength: kIC is an interesting proposed coding scheme with potential error-correction benefits.  
- Strength: clear and justified modeling choices using standard information-theoretic tools.  
- Strength: demonstrates strong understanding of existing literature in crowdsourcing and related fields.  
- Weakness: results are highly abstract and give limited actionable guidance for real-world system design (e.g., choosing k in kIC).  
- Weakness: no empirical validation or simulations on real datasets to demonstrate practical relevance.  
- Weakness: strong simplifying assumptions (i.i.d. data, memoryless channels) that may not hold in practice and lack robustness discussion.  
- Weakness: query pricing discussion is brief and the proposed rule-of-thumb is inadequately justified.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of how worker skill knowledge (SL-CS) would be acquired or used in dynamic settings.  
- Quality: technically sound and rigorous, but practical relevance is weakened by lack of empirical support.  
- Clarity: generally well-written but dense mathematical notation and limited intuitive interpretation of results.  
- Originality: novel application of information theory to crowdsourcing fundamental limits.  
- Significance: moderate—importance hinges on translating theoretical bounds into practical system improvements.  
- Pro: offers valuable theoretical perspective, benchmarks, and a promising coding idea (kIC).  
- Con: too abstract, lacks experiments, rests on potentially unrealistic assumptions, and omits practical implementation details.  
- Key questions for authors: how to apply bounds to practical design; how to estimate channel/skill parameters; robustness to non-i.i.d./non-memoryless settings; justification and detail for kIC-based pricing; and computational complexity/scaling of kIC.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, conditional on substantially expanding practical implications, providing illustrative examples or experiments, and addressing noted assumptions and implementation concerns.
