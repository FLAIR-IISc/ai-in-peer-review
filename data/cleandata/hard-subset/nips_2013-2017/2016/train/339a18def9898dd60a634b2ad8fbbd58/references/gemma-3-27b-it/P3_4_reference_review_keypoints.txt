- Overall assessment: Rigorous information-theoretic analysis of crowdsourcing but disconnected from practice; reviewer recommends weak reject and major revisions to address practical implications.
- Summary of ideas: Models crowdsourcing as a communication system, derives budget–fidelity bounds for known/unknown worker skills, introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and a pricing idea.
- Relation to previous work: Positions itself relative to Dawid–Skene and aggregation literature but insufficiently connects to related information-theoretic fields (noisy sensor networks, active learning) and modern inference approaches.
- Strength — Rigorous theoretical framework: Solid information-theoretic foundation and correct mathematical derivations.
- Strength — Clear problem formulation: Insightful communication-system framing that formalizes budget–fidelity trade-offs.
- Strength — kIC contribution: Introduces kIC as a potentially useful query scheme with error-correction properties.
- Strength — Trade-off identification: Clearly shows benefit of knowing worker skill levels on required query budget.
- Weakness — Limited practical relevance: Bounds are too general and not tied to realistic scenarios, with optimistic i.i.d. and known-statistics assumptions.
- Weakness — Theory–practice disconnect: Little guidance on how real systems can approach the theoretical limits or estimate worker skills.
- Weakness — Insufficient kIC discussion: Practical implementation, advantages over simpler schemes, and pricing rationale are not convincingly demonstrated.
- Weakness — Lack of empirical validation: No simulations or real-data experiments to support theoretical claims.
- Weakness — Presentation clarity: Dense exposition and cumbersome notation make the paper hard to follow for non-experts.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel theoretical framework, derivation of fundamental limits, and introduction of kIC.
- Arguments against acceptance: Poor practical connection, no empirical support, unclear how to reach limits, and overly general/opaque bounds.
- Specific suggestions: Narrow scope to a concrete task, add a practical-implications section, discuss worker-skill estimation methods, compare kIC to existing strategies, include simulations/real-data validation, and simplify presentation.
- Conclusion: Solid theoretical contribution but limited impact without substantial revisions; reviewer recommends rejection in current form.
