- Paper frames crowdsourcing budget–fidelity trade-offs via information theory, modeling labels as a discrete source and workers as noisy channels to derive query-rate lower bounds.
- Considers two worker models: heterogeneous M-ary symmetric channels (MSC) and spammer–hammer channel (SHC), and two scenarios: skills unknown (SL-UK) vs known at decoder (SL-CS).
- Main result: R_min = [H(B) − H_N(ε̂)] divided by an effective per-query information term, with SL-UK using log M − H_M(E[ε]) and SL-CS using log M − E[H_M(ε)].
- Proposes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) which queries k items jointly and restricts answers to valid partitionings to provide implicit error correction.
- For SHC and binary labels, gives a lemma for spammer error under kIC and a converse (Theorem 4) necessary-rate condition for an oracle decoder; also provides a SHC-specialized corollary.
- Briefly proposes a pricing heuristic for kIC designs, but the rule appears mistyped and unvalidated.
- Relates to Dawid–Skene/EM, spectral/convex aggregation, adaptive allocation, and Karger–Oh–Shah; suggests more thorough positioning against prior bounds is needed.
- Strength: clear, principled separation-based framing yielding intuitive bounds separating source uncertainty and per-query channel information.
- Strength: insightful contrast between SL-UK and SL-CS capacities and practical benchmark utility independent of aggregation algorithms.
- Strength: kIC is an appealing structured query idea that formalizes structural constraints as error correction.
- Weakness: presentation issues—typos, missing references/figures/appendices, inconsistent notation (N vs M), and unclear formulas.
- Weakness: limited technical novelty—Theorems 2–3 follow directly from standard rate–distortion and channel capacity arguments without detailed proofs for mixtures or state knowledge handling.
- Weakness: kIC analysis is only a converse using an oracle decoder, lacks achievability, tightness, and has opaque/possibly incorrect inequalities.
- Weakness: no empirical or synthetic validation comparing practical algorithms to the bounds or comparing kIC to simpler query designs.
- Weakness: scope limited to non-adaptive schemes and asymptotic analysis; finite-length effects and adaptive allocation are ignored.
- Quality: technically sound at a high level for MSC mixtures and rate–distortion, but kIC derivation and several formulas need clarification and correction.
- Clarity: manuscript needs significant editing, consistent notation, explicit assumptions, and inclusion of referenced figures/appendices and proof sketches.
- Originality: moderate—useful application of separation and SL-UK vs SL-CS insight, but not highly novel without achievability or adaptive designs.
- Significance: potentially useful as a benchmark but limited currently by lack of achievability results, empirical validation, and tighter connections to existing optimality results.
- Suggestions: provide detailed proofs/sketches, fix notation/formulas, add achievability or constructive schemes with experiments, analyze finite-length/adaptive settings, and validate pricing heuristics.
- Recommendation: reject in current form; encourage resubmission after corrections, added proofs/experiments, and stronger positioning versus prior work.
