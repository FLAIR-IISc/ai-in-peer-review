- Summary: Models crowdsourcing as a communication system using information theory, derives budget–fidelity bounds for unknown vs. known worker skills, introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC), and uses source–channel separation to give minimum queries per item.

- Strength (Quality): Technically rigorous framework with correct application of source–channel separation and well-motivated derivations (Theorems 2 and 3); kIC analysis provides a concrete example.

- Strength (Originality): Novel application of information theory to characterize fundamental crowdsourcing limits and a new kIC query scheme with error-correction properties.

- Strength (Clarity): Well-structured, clear notation and figures, distinct treatment of SL‑UK vs. SL‑CS, and adequate appendix detail for verification.

- Weakness (Quality): Relies on strong assumptions (iid workers, discrete memoryless channels) and lacks empirical validation or simulations against real crowdsourcing data.

- Weakness (Significance): Insufficient linkage to practical systems (e.g., Mechanical Turk) and unclear guidance on using bounds to improve real-world crowdsourcing design.

- Weakness (Clarity, minor): kIC exposition needs a concrete task example and smoother transition from the theoretical model to kIC analysis.

- Overall assessment: Valuable and novel theoretical contribution with sound results, but limited practical impact due to missing empirical validation and implementation guidance.

- Suggested revisions: add simulations comparing theoretical bounds with kIC performance, discuss implications for real platforms and design decisions, and analyze the impact of relaxed/realistic assumptions.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.

- Pro acceptance: Establishes fundamental info‑theoretic limits, provides a rigorous budget‑fidelity framework, introduces kIC as a novel scheme, and is well written with clear derivations.

- Con acceptance: No empirical validation, limited practical implementation discussion, and potentially restrictive assumptions for real-world crowdsourcing.
