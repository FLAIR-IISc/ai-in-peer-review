- Paper frames crowdsourced labeling as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem and studies fundamental budget-fidelity trade-offs.
- Derives asymptotic query-rate limits (Theorems 2 and 3) for achieving a desired fidelity with known and unknown worker skill.
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding and provides a lower bound under a spammer-hammer worker model.
- Novel and well-motivated application of information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing.
- Mathematical derivations are rigorous, notation is consistent, and presentation is clear and well-organized.
- Relevant literature is cited and connections to existing work are apparent; practical considerations like query pricing are discussed.
- Major technical concern: the paper implicitly assumes source-channel separation holds with random/unknown channel state at the encoder, which is nonstandard and requires justification or citation.
- Modeling concern: encoder is assumed not to have access to original items as side information, which is unrealistic and may make bounds overly conservative.
- Wording issue: phrasing that "lower bounds improve as k and q grow" is misleading; more precise language is needed to state that bound values decrease.
- Paper focuses on asymptotic limits and lacks analysis of non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) performance, limiting practical applicability.
- Suggest citing finite-blocklength JSCC work (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) to contextualize and suggest future directions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear rigorous analysis, well-motivated problem, provides valuable fundamental limits.
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable separation assumption, oversimplified model ignoring side information, imprecise wording, and lack of finite-blocklength analysis.
- Overall recommendation: weak reject pending addressing the separation assumption, incorporation of side information, clarification of wording, and discussion of finite-blocklength issues.
