This paper presents a novel approach to modeling crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic framework. The authors provide a comprehensive analysis of the fundamental limits of budget-fidelity trade-offs in label crowdsourcing. The paper is well-organized and well-written, making it easy to follow.

The key contributions of this paper are:

1. Modeling crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic framework to give lower bounds on queries per task for target error probability.
2. Considering symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) for worker errors and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q) for spammers.
3. Analyzing two crowdsourcer knowledge cases: full knowledge of worker skills and no knowledge.
4. Providing a necessary condition on minimum rate for k-ary incidence coding with SHC(q) worker pool.

The strengths of this paper are:

1. The approach is novel and practically useful for designers and pricing.
2. The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the fundamental limits of budget-fidelity trade-offs in label crowdsourcing.
3. The authors consider various scenarios, including unknown worker skills and spammer-hammer channels.

The weaknesses of this paper are:

1. The paper lacks experiments or simulations to compare practical schemes (e.g., AMT) to the theoretical limits.
2. Some sections, such as Section 2.4 and Section 3.1, could be shortened or removed to improve the paper's flow.

Arguments for acceptance:

1. The paper presents a novel and comprehensive analysis of crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic framework.
2. The approach is practically useful for designers and pricing.
3. The paper provides a necessary condition on minimum rate for k-ary incidence coding with SHC(q) worker pool.

Arguments against acceptance:

1. The paper lacks experiments or simulations to compare practical schemes to the theoretical limits.
2. Some sections could be shortened or removed to improve the paper's flow.

Recommendations for improvement:

1. Add experiments or simulations to compare practical schemes to the theoretical limits.
2. Shorten or remove sections that are not essential to the paper's main contributions.
3. Provide proof sketches for the theorems to improve the paper's clarity.

Overall, this paper presents a novel and comprehensive analysis of crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic framework. While it has some weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses, and it is a valuable contribution to the field.
