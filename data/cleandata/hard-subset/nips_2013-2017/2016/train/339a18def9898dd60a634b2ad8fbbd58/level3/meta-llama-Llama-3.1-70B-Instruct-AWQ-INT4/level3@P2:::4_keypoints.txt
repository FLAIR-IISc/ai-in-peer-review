- Paper maps crowdsourcing budget-fidelity trade-offs to joint source-channel coding and derives information-theoretic lower bounds using a rate-distortion framing.
- Main contribution: communication-system model for crowdsourcing with analyses for unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skill levels.
- Derives lower bounds on minimum queries per item to achieve target fidelity in both scenarios.
- Concern: model merges worker skill and reporting reliability into a single channel, losing distinction between skill/rating and reporting noise.
- Concern: taskmaster allocates same number of queries to all workers, which is unrealistic given known ratings and worker heterogeneity.
- Concern: worker channel models (MSC, SHC) may not capture adversarial or informed malicious workers; realism needs justification.
- k-ary incidence coding (kIC) analysis is presented but numerical results and explanations are unclear.
- Figure issues: curve labeling in Figure 2 needs improvement and authors should explicitly state what k=1 corresponds to.
- Methodological issue: assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) is introduced late (page 6) and should be declared earlier.
- Presentation issue: misuse of 'Theorem', 'Lemma', and 'Corollary' without numbering; should use lowercase or add numbers for clarity.
- Strength: comprehensive related-work overview and clear, well-organized writing.
- Strength: rate-distortion framing is interesting and could inspire future work; figures and tables are helpful.
- Suggested improvements: clarify numerical results, introduce more realistic worker/channel models, and discuss real-world implications.
- Rating/recommendation: 6/10 — interesting ideas but requires significant revisions before acceptance.
