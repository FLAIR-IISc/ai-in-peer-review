- Presents an information-theoretic framework deriving fundamental budget-fidelity trade-off bounds for label crowdsourcing.
- Models two worker-knowledge scenarios: skill levels unknown (SL-UK) and skill levels known to crowdsourcer (SL-CS), with Theorems 2 and 3 giving minimum queries per item.
- Theorem 2 (SL-UK) bound depends on source entropy, number of classes, and maximum class probability.
- Theorem 3 (SL-CS) bound depends on source entropy, number of classes, and expected entropy of worker skill levels.
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) queries that ask workers to identify same-label items within sets of k items.
- Derives a lower bound for kIC under a spammer-hammer worker model and shows performance improves with larger k and higher hammer probability.
- Paper is well-organized, clearly presented, and provides a concise introduction and solid related-work discussion.
- Novel modeling and theoretical bounds are significant and relevant to practical crowdsourcing design.
- Main weakness: technical presentation is hard for non–information-theory readers to follow.
- Needs more high-level intuition and practical takeaways translating bounds to real crowdsourcing scenarios.
- Recommend restating results in crowdsourcing/application terms instead of channel/code/distortion language.
- Clarify terminology (e.g., distinction between “crowdsourcer” and “taskmaster”) and address minor copyediting issues.
- Reviewer scores high on quality, originality, and significance; rates paper 8/10 and recommends accept with minor revisions.
