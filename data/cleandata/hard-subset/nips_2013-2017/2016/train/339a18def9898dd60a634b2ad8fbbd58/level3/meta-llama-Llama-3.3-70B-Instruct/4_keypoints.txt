- Paper maps crowdsourcing to a joint source-channel coding problem, treating queries as channel inputs and worker responses as outputs.
- Defines budget as total number of queries and studies the trade-off between budget and fidelity.
- Derives an information-theoretic lower bound on budget for a target fidelity, independent of specific schemes.
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding and compares its performance to the theoretical lower bound.
- Results indicate k-ary incidence coding can outperform the lower bound, particularly for larger k.
- Reviewer questions realism of the model, notably the assumption that all workers receive the same number of queries regardless of skill.
- Concerns that the channel models for worker unreliability are limited and may not capture real-world complexity.
- Suggests considering adversarial workers who may intentionally degrade fidelity.
- Criticizes clarity of numerical results, curve labeling, and inconsistent notation/terminology.
- Recommends more detailed explanations of the channel models and the k-ary incidence coding scheme.
- Calls for more comprehensive numerical experiments to better illustrate scheme performance.
- Overall assessment: novel and technically sound with potential impact, but needs refinement and clearer presentation.
- Pro-acceptance points: originality, thorough budget–fidelity analysis, potential field impact.
- Con-acceptance points: unrealistic equal-query assumption, limited channel models, and unclear presentation/notation.
