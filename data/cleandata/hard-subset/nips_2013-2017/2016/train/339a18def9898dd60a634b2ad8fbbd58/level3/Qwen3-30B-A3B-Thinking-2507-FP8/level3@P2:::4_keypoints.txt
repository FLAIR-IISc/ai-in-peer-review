- Proposes an information-theoretic framework modeling crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding problem with workers as parallel noisy channels.  
- Derives lower bounds on the budget–fidelity trade-off for unknown-skill (SL-UK) and known-skill (SL-CS) scenarios.  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and compares its performance to the theoretical bounds.  
- Theoretical derivations and proofs (Theorems 2 and 3) appear rigorous and well-motivated.  
- Merges worker skill and reliability into a single channel parameter, obscuring the distinction between expertise and reporting noise.  
- Assumes the taskmaster allocates the same number of queries to all workers, which is unrealistic in practice.  
- Channel models ignore adversarial or informed malicious workers, limiting practical applicability.  
- Key assumption (knowledge of P(B(X))) is introduced late and should be declared earlier.  
- Misuses "Theorem," "Lemma," and "Corollary" without proper numbering, causing confusion.  
- Figure 1 references are incorrect (1.a → 1.b, 1.b → 1.c), reducing clarity.  
- Numerical results in Figure 2 are unclearly labeled and lack explicit explanation of what k=1 means.  
- Original contribution is novel—applying joint source-channel coding to crowdsourcing—but is a natural extension and should be better contrasted with related rate-distortion work.  
- Significance is theoretically valuable for understanding limits but limited by unrealistic worker and allocation assumptions.  
- Strengths: novel framing, derivation of fundamental limits, kIC analysis, and clear theoretical contribution.  
- Weaknesses: equal-query assumption, merged skill/reliability parameter, omission of adversarial models, unclear figures, late assumption disclosure, and improper theorem numbering.  
- Recommendations: justify/revise assumptions on skills and allocation, relate bounds to practical system design, clarify figures and labeling, introduce key assumptions earlier, and properly number results.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with major revisions to address modeling realism, clarity, and presentation issues.
