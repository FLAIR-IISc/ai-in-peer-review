- Models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel decoding problem using rate-distortion and channel coding to derive budget–fidelity bounds.
- Analyzes scenarios with unknown and known worker skills and studies a k-ary incidence coding query scheme and pricing.
- Novel application of information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing, contrasting with prior practical-algorithm literature.
- Theoretical derivations appear sound under the paper’s assumptions.
- Core weakness: applicability of channel coding framework is questionable because the i.i.d. assumption is violated when re-querying the same worker.
- Authors note this limitation but do not fully quantify or mitigate its impact on the bounds’ usefulness.
- Paper lacks a convincing mapping from the theoretical encoding scheme u^n to practical query assignment strategies under non-i.i.d. workers.
- Discussion of error correction mechanisms beyond repetition codes is underdeveloped.
- No empirical comparison to state-of-the-art practical methods, making practical relevance and bound tightness unclear.
- Derived bounds may be loose, but there is insufficient comparison or validation to assess tightness.
- Reviewer leans toward rejection but encourages addressing practical instantiation or explicitly acknowledging limitations.
- Minor fixes requested: clarify minimization in (8); expand motivation and proof details for Theorem 4; clarify ε_S relationship in Theorem 4; justify or cite H_M(E(ε)) in (17).
