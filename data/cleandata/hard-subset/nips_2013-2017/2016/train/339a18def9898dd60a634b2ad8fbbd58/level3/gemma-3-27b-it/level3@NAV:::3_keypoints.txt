- Novel framing: models crowdsourcing as an information-theoretic joint source-channel coding problem.  
- Main contribution: derives fundamental budget–fidelity limits and analyzes k-ary incidence coding with provable bounds.  
- Strength: rigorous formalization that can guide optimized algorithms and resource allocation.  
- Weakness: heavy technicality and information-theory jargon reduce accessibility for the broader ML community.  
- Presentation gap: many results are given in channel/code/distortion terms without translating to practical crowdsourcing implications.  
- Suggestion: expand end of Section 3 to provide more intuitive, practitioner-oriented takeaways.  
- Suggestion: add a concluding section that restates core findings in practical terms (e.g., budget → expected accuracy).  
- Related work: paper needs a more thorough discussion of incentive mechanisms and mechanism-design literature linked to aggregation.  
- Clarification needed: define and distinguish “crowdsourcer” vs. “taskmaster.”  
- Minor edits: fix line 84 (“are reviewed in”), typo on line 122 (“A A(X1,X2)”), and rephrase lines 275–276.  
- Figure 2: improve caption and visual clarity to better convey results.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel framing, rigorous theoretical analysis, potentially high impact.  
- Rejection arguments: limited accessibility, overreliance on jargon, insufficient practical/connective discussion on incentives.  
- Overall: valuable contribution that would be significantly strengthened by clearer exposition and stronger ties to practical crowdsourcing.
