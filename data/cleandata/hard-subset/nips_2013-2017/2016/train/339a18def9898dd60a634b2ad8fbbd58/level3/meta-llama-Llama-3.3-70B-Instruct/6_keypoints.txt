- Proposes a novel method to deconvolve feedback loops in recommender systems, a significant contribution.
- Develops a mathematical framework to recover the true ratings matrix from observed ratings under reasonable assumptions.
- Uses SVD-based approach to recover true ratings and identify items heavily influenced by the recommender.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains problem, method, and results.
- Thorough empirical analysis on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrating effectiveness at identifying high recommender-effect items.
- Strength: Builds on a well-established matrix factorization technique (SVD).
- Weakness: Assumptions about the recommender system may not hold in real-world settings, limiting applicability.
- Weakness: Insufficient detailed comparison with existing deconvolution methods to demonstrate relative advantages.
- Weakness: Some notation and equations require clarification to improve accessibility for non-experts.
- Overall assessment: Strong paper with minor revisions needed; good candidate for acceptance.
