- Novel modeling: represents each worker as a noisy communication channel to analyze crowdsourcing.  
- Channel types: analyzes symmetric memoryless and spammer/hammer worker models.  
- Coding perspective: reframes labeling as a coding problem to study distortion vs. budget tradeoffs.  
- k-incidence coding: proposes a new scheme requiring workers to label subsets of k items at a time.  
- Theoretical analysis: provides a thorough, information-theoretic analysis of k-incidence performance.  
- Clarity and writing: paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and exposition.  
- Technical strength: approach is original, technically sound, and uses rigorous information-theoretic tools.  
- Literature context: gives a thorough review and clearly differentiates from prior work.  
- Limited empirical validation: analysis is largely theoretical with only limited numerical results.  
- Robustness concern: numerical experiments cover a specific scenario, leaving robustness to parameter changes unclear.  
- Assumption limitation: assumes unknown worker skill levels, which may not hold in all practical settings.  
- Recommendation: overall positive — reviewer recommends acceptance despite minor practical concerns.
