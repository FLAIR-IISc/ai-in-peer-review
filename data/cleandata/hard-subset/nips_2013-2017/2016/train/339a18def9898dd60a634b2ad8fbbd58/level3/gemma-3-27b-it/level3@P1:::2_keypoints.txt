- Proposes an information-theoretic JSCC framework modeling items as source, workers as noisy channels, and taskmaster as encoder/decoder, deriving asymptotic query-rate limits and analyzing k-ary incidence coding.
- Novel application of rate-distortion and channel coding to crowdsourcing, providing fundamental performance benchmarks.
- Theorems 2 and 3 give clear asymptotic lower bounds on minimum queries per item for given fidelity.
- Analysis of k-ary incidence coding offers a concrete scheme and performance bound.
- Paper is well-written and logically organized.
- Crucial assumption: source-channel separation with unknown channel state at the encoder is made without justification and may be invalid.
- Model ignores encoder side information (original items), which can be exploited in practice and may render bounds conservative.
- Misleading phrasing in Section 4.2 claiming “lower bounds improve as k and q grow”; should clarify that bound values decrease (implying lower required rate), not that bounds become tighter.
- Results are asymptotic (large blocklength), limiting practical applicability; finite-blocklength analysis (e.g., Kostina & Verdú 2013) should be discussed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, fundamental limits useful for benchmarking, and illustrative coding analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: questionable separation assumption, limited practical relevance, and clarity issues.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing the separation assumption (justify or acknowledge limitations), clarifying Section 4.2 wording, and discussing finite-blocklength performance and side information.
