- Paper derives information-theoretic lower bounds on queries per task for desired error probability in crowdsourcing.
- Models crowdsourcing as joint source-channel coding with workers as parallel noisy channels (symmetric DMC and spammer-hammer channel).
- Analyzes two settings: full knowledge of worker skills and no knowledge (taskmaster has no prior skill info).
- Studies k-ary incidence coding scheme and discusses potential for error correction via query structure.
- Touches on implications for query pricing in crowdsourcing systems.
- Novel and valuable framing that provides a rigorous foundation for fundamental limits and system design.
- Modeling of standard worker errors and malicious spammers (SHC) is a strong aspect.
- Lack of experimental validation or comparison to platforms (e.g., AMT) and realistic worker simulations limits practical significance.
- Suggests condensing Section 2.4 and replacing full proofs in Section 3.1 with proof sketches to improve readability.
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound, novel framework with potential impact on design and pricing.
- Arguments against acceptance: no experiments and some sections are overly long/detailed.
- Overall recommendation: accept conditional on addressing length/clarity and ideally adding preliminary experiments or simulations.
