- Frames crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel problem and derives an info-theoretic budget–fidelity trade-off via rate–distortion (source) and channel capacity (worker noise); analyzes MSC and SHC worker models and k‑ary incidence coding (kIC) with an oracle lower bound and pricing heuristic.

- Mapping connects to classical information theory (Cover–Thomas; El Gamal–Kim) and to crowdsourcing aggregation methods (Dawid–Skene, spectral/EM, convex methods).

- Derivations appear sound and the separation-type mapping is correct; this may be the first explicit rate–distortion/capacity formulation for crowdsourcing.

- Main bounds are likely loose in practice because they inherit the freedom to design u^n and random codebooks assumed in channel-coding converses.

- Practical crowdsourcing constraints (few queries per worker, heterogeneous item exposures, no repeated queries to same worker) are not modeled, making many encodings inapplicable.

- It is unclear which encoder mappings C: B(X)^n → U^(Σ m_i) are implementable beyond trivial repetition codes; stronger error-correcting encodings are not addressed.

- These implementability issues likely undermine the tightness of capacity-based lower bounds and limit Theorem 4’s practical usefulness beyond order-of-magnitude guidance.

- Exposition is generally clear but some statements need more precision and proof details; several derivation steps are terse.

- kIC analysis is interesting and may stimulate query-design work but is incremental relative to the main theoretical framework.

- Originality is in the formal rate–distortion framing; the mathematical derivations are mostly standard once the mapping is fixed.

- Significance is limited without (a) formal modeling of implementable encodings and (b) empirical/theoretical comparisons to practical methods (Dawid–Skene, spectral/EM, Karger–Oh–Shah).

- Pros: clean, correct information-theoretic formulation; provides unifying vocabulary and baseline bounds; kIC offers a concrete query-design direction.

- Cons: likely loose/unrealistic bounds due to encoding freedom, limited novelty in derivations, unclear practical impact, and missing comparisons to modern algorithms and datasets.

- Suggested revisions: formalize admissible encodings (per-worker/item constraints, no-repeat conditions) and derive constrained bounds; compare sample complexity to DS/spectral/KOS both theoretically and via simulations; discuss implementable encoders beyond repetition (e.g., combinatorial designs for kIC).

- Minor technical comments to fix: in (8) clarify minimization over P(Ẑ|Z) (equivalently C,C′) subject to E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t; in Theorem 4 state sign conditions for ln(1−q) and clarify inequalities involving ε̄_S and ẑε and expand the proof; in (17) justify the H_M(E(ε)) term or cite prior work.
