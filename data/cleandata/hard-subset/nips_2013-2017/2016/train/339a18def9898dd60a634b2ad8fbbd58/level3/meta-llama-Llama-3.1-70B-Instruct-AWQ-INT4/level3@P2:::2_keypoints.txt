- Proposes a novel JSCC formulation of crowdsourcing, modeling labels as source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as random-state channel, and crowdsourcer as decoder.
- Derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item via source-channel separation (Theorems 2 and 3).
- Provides a lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding under a specific crowdsourcing/channel model.
- Paper is well-written, technically solid, and offers a promising first step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.
- The JSCC perspective is new and gives a fresh viewpoint compared to prior crowdsourcing work.
- Must justify or cite that source-channel separation holds when the channel has random state unknown to the encoder (and possibly known to the decoder).
- Assumes encoder/decoder only see labels and ignore original items as side information; this may be unrealistic and its impact should be analyzed.
- Wording in Sec.4.2/Fig.2 is misleading—clarify that lower bound values decrease (implying potential performance gain), not that the bound itself is unambiguously improved.
- Technical quality and exposition are high, and related work is comprehensively reviewed.
- Results are significant for understanding fundamental query limits and for designing more efficient crowdsourcing systems.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel perspective, fundamental limits, strong writing and technical quality.
- Arguments against acceptance: need to justify separation theorem assumptions, address side-information assumption, and clarify misleading wording.
- Rating: 8/10; recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address the technical comments and clarifications.
