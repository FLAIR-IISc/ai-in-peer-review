- Paper frames crowdsourced label aggregation as a joint source-channel coding problem and analyzes budget–fidelity trade-offs via rate-distortion theory.  
- Main theoretical contribution: information-theoretic lower bounds on queries needed for a target error in two scenarios — skill-level unknown (SL-UK) and skill-level known (SL-CS).  
- Proposes k-ary Incidence Coding (kIC) and derives performance bounds under a spammer-hammer (SHC) worker model.  
- Strength: novel, elegant formulation that provides fundamental limits and benchmarks beyond specific aggregation algorithms.  
- Strength: perspective can inspire new theoretical analyses and system designs in crowdsourcing.  
- Weakness: worker models (MSC and SHC) are overly simplistic and conflate different aspects of worker behavior.  
- Weakness: MSC cannot capture systematic biases (e.g., confusion between specific classes) or distinguish skill from momentary errors.  
- Weakness: SHC’s binary perfect-or-random model fails to represent informed adversaries, strategic collusion, or non-random malicious behavior.  
- Suggestion: discuss realism of chosen channel models and outline extensions to more nuanced models separating skill, reliability, and adversarial actions.  
- Weakness: query allocation assumption (equal queries per worker under SL-UK) is unrealistic given available reputation/history on platforms like MTurk.  
- Suggestion: consider or discuss heterogeneous task allocation leveraging imperfect worker reputation.  
- Clarity issue: assumption of known source (label) distribution P(B(X)) is strong and should be stated prominently in problem formulation, not late in the paper.  
- Clarity issue: Figure 2 is hard to interpret—label kIC curves clearly, explain k=1 baseline and its derivation.  
- Minor corrections: check and fix figure references (Figure 1a/1b/1c) and use consistent theorem phrasing (e.g., “Theorem 4” vs “a Theorem 4”).  
- Overall recommendation: justify modeling choices or clearly delineate limitations to improve practical significance and impact.
