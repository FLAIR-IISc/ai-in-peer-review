- Frames crowdsourcing as an information-theoretic communication problem and derives fundamental budget–fidelity limits.  
- Models workers as parallel noisy channels (MSC and SHC) and analyzes unknown-skill (SL-UK) and known-skill (SL-CS) scenarios.  
- Establishes lower bounds on queries per task for desired error probabilities.  
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and derives theoretical performance limits for it.  
- Novel departure from prior algorithmic/EM work by focusing on ultimate limits achievable by any scheme.  
- Strong originality and theoretical contribution, clarifying how knowledge of worker skill affects performance.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization; communication-system framing (Figure 1a) is effective.  
- Results have practical relevance (e.g., link to query pricing via Theorem 4) for system design.  
- Major weakness: no experiments or simulations to evaluate tightness of bounds in real-world settings.  
- Sections 2.4 and 3.1 are overly long and should be substantially shortened.  
- Missing brief proof sketches for central Theorems 2 and 3 in the main text reduces accessibility.  
- Overall analysis is sound; lacks empirical validation but offers substantial theoretical contribution warranting acceptance with revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: shorten Sections 2.4 and 3.1, add proof sketches for Theorems 2 and 3, and include a brief simulation study.
