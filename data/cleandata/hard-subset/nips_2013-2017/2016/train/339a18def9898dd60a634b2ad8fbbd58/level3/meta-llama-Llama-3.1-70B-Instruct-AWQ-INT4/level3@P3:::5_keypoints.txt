- Presents a novel information-theoretic framework to model crowdsourcing and derive lower bounds on queries per task for target error probability.  
- Models workers as parallel independent channels, a reasonable and useful abstraction.  
- Provides a unified framework covering two scenarios: workers' skill levels unknown (SL-UK) and perfectly known (SL-CS).  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding and gives a necessary condition on its minimum query rate.  
- Paper is well-organized and well-written, making the arguments easy to follow.  
- Use of information-theoretic tools offers a fresh, significant perspective for crowdsourcing design and pricing.  
- Lacks experiments or simulations to compare practical systems (e.g., Mechanical Turk) against the theoretical limits.  
- Some sections (e.g., Section 2.4 and 3.1) are overly long and could be shortened or removed.  
- Paper would benefit from including proof sketches to make the results more self-contained.  
- Overall technical quality is high (Quality: 8/10).  
- Clarity is excellent (Clarity: 9/10).  
- Originality is strong (Originality: 9/10).  
- Significance is notable but tempered by lack of empirical validation (Significance: 8/10).
