- Frames crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem and derives asymptotic query-per-item limits.
- Uses a rate-distortion viewpoint: labels as i.i.d. source, taskmaster = encoder, workers = channel with random skill-level state, crowdsourcer = decoder.
- Applies source-channel separation to obtain Theorems 2 and 3 for two scenarios: unknown worker skill-level (SL-UK) and skill-level known to crowdsourcer (SL-CS).
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding under a specific channel model.
- Paper is well-written and a promising first step applying JSCC to crowdsourcing, correctly situating itself relative to majority voting and Dawid–Skene approaches.
- Critical flaw: no justification for applying source-channel separation when the channel has random state unknown to the encoder—this undermines the main results.
- Authors should cite and justify the separation assumption with relevant information-theory literature.
- Modeling ignores original items as side information, which may be important in practical crowdsourcing and should be incorporated or discussed.
- Wording in Section 4.2 and Figure 2 is misleading—clarify that "lower bounds improve as k and q grow" means the bound value decreases (potential performance gain), not that the bound tightens without an upper bound comparison.
- Missing nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) converses; should include/compare to Kostina & Verdú (2013) to strengthen practical relevance.
- Overall assessment: promising but technically incomplete; requires significant revisions to address the theoretical gaps before publication.
