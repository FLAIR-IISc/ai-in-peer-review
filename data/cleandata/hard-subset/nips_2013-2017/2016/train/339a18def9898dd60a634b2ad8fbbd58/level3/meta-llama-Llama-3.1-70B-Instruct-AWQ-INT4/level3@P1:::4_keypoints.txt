- Novel mapping of crowdsourcing with unreliable workers to joint source-channel coding is an interesting and original contribution.  
- Derivation of a budget–fidelity trade-off via information-theoretic lower bounds is valuable and potentially impactful.  
- Analysis and comparison of k-ary incidence coding against the information-theoretic bound for a specific channel model is a useful case study.  
- Model conflates worker skill/rating and reporting noise into a single channel, oversimplifying real-world distinctions and dynamics.  
- The assumption that the taskmaster allocates the same number of queries to all workers ignores worker heterogeneity and known ratings.  
- Channel models used may not capture adversarial or informed malicious workers; more sophisticated adversary models are needed.  
- Numerical results and explanations need improvement; curve labels in Figure 2 are unclear and k=1 should be explicitly defined.  
- The assumption that P(B(X)) is known is introduced late (page 6) and should be stated and justified earlier in the paper.  
- Figure reference errors (Figure 1.a → 1.b, 1.b → 1.c) need correction.  
- Inconsistent use of “Theorem”, “Lemma”, and “Corollary” (unnumbered) should be fixed by numbering or using lowercase.  
- Authors should clarify and justify the realism of the assumed worker/channel models.  
- With the above methodological clarifications and minor fixes, the paper could make a significant contribution to the field.
