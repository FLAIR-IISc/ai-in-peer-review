- Novel application of information theory: models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem.  
- Novelty and motivation: JSCC connection is novel and provides a powerful analytical framework.  
- Main theoretical results: Theorems 2 and 3 give asymptotic query-rate limits under different knowledge of worker skill.  
- Concrete coding example: Introduces k-ary incidence coding and provides a lower bound on its error probability.  
- Practical relevance: Effectively connects theory to real-world models like spammer/hammer workers.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and well-organized.  
- Key limitation 1: Assumes source-channel separation with unknown/random channel state without sufficient justification or citation.  
- Key limitation 2: Modeling choice ignores item side information (e.g., the image), which may be restrictive and suboptimal.  
- Presentation issue: Wording in Section 4.2 and Figure 2 is misleading—“lower bounds improve as k and q grow” should clarify that the bound value decreases (implying better performance), not tightening.  
- Suggestion for strengthening: Explore non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) converses and cite Kostina & Verdú (2013) for finite-blocklength JSCC results.  
- Acceptance arguments: Novel theory application, clear presentation, establishes fundamental budget–fidelity limits, and opens avenues for further research.  
- Rejection/concern arguments: Unjustified separation assumption, restrictive omission of item side information, and misleading wording on coding bounds.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the technical justification and clarifications.
