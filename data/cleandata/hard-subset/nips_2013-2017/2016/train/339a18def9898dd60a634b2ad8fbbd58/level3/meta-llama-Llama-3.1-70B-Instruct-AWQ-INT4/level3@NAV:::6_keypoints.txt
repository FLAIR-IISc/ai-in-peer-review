- Main contribution: derives an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion and channel coding.  
- Modeling: frames crowdsourcing as human-in-the-loop computation analyzed within a rate–distortion framework.  
- Specific scheme analyzed: investigates and thoroughly analyzes k-ary incidence coding performance.  
- Strength: novel application of information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation.  
- Strength: useful comparison between the theoretical bound and state-of-the-art practical methods.  
- Weakness: derivation appears straightforward and the resulting bound may be very loose.  
- Weakness: assumption that the taskmaster can freely design the input sequence u^n may be unrealistic in practice.  
- Weakness: worker responses likely depend on skill levels, contrary to independence assumptions in the paper.  
- Weakness: analysis of k-ary incidence coding is limited to a specific scenario and lacks generality.  
- Weakness: unclear how to design u^n to achieve stronger error-correction in crowdsourcing.  
- Weakness: simplistic treatment of worker skill levels (either unknown or perfectly known) may not reflect real scenarios.  
- Suggestions: add discussion on practical implications and methods to design query schemes for stronger error-correction.  
- Suggestions: extend analysis to more realistic settings with varying worker skills and task difficulties.  
- Overall recommendation metrics: Rating 6/10, Confidence 8/10.
