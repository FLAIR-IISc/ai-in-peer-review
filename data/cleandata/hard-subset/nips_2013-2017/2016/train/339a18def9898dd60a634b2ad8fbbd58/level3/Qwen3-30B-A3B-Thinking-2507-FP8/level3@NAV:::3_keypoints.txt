- Presents an information-theoretic framework modeling crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding problem to derive budget-fidelity limits.  
- Establishes Theorems 2 and 3 characterizing minimum query rate for target error under unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skills.  
- Introduces and analyzes k-ary incidence coding as a query scheme, showing error-correction benefits.  
- Strong theoretical rigor and original contribution providing fundamental performance bounds not seen before.  
- Information-theoretic perspective yields useful insights on budget vs. fidelity trade-offs for system design.  
- k-ary incidence coding analysis is particularly interesting and practically relevant.  
- Major accessibility issue: heavy use of information-theoretic terminology makes practical implications hard for non-specialists to grasp.  
- Recommend restating results in crowdsourcing terms (e.g., "minimum workers per item") and expanding Section 3 for high-level intuition and takeaways.  
- Conclusion lacks a clear summary of actionable insights for practitioners.  
- Manuscript issues: unclear distinction between "crowdsourcer" and "taskmaster."  
- Minor copyediting errors (e.g., line 84, line 122) and unclear phrasing at lines 275–276.  
- Figure 2 is unclear and needs a more detailed caption.  
- Related work is incomplete; should include recent work on incentives and mechanism design combining incentives with aggregation.  
- Overall: strong, original theoretical work that would be much more impactful if translated into practical design principles and made more accessible.
