- Frames crowdsourcing as JSCC with worker skill as channel state and derives asymptotic query-rate limits via source–channel separation (Theorems 2 & 3 for unknown vs decoder-known states).
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a spammer–hammer channel (SHC(q)); Theorem 4 and a lemma give necessary query-rate conditions and spammer-induced error probabilities.
- Main technical gap: lack of explicit i.i.d./independence assumptions for channel state and missing citations/justification for applying separation with random channel state.
- Recommend explicitly stating SL-UK reduces to the averaged DMC and SL-CS to capacity C = maxP(x) I(X;Y|S), with citations (Cover & Thomas; El Gamal & Kim).
- Suggest adding finite-blocklength converses/achievability (Kostina & Verdú 2013; Polyanskiy–Poor–Verdú) to quantify practical penalties for realistic dataset sizes.
- Writing is clear and the JSCC mapping is good; ensure uniform notation for alphabets and explicitly state log base.
- Sec. 4.2/Fig. 2 wording is misleading: the bound is a necessary condition; larger k or q reduce required rate (achievable performance), not “improve lower bounds.”
- Modeling limitation: encoder/decoder use only B(X) and ignore item features/difficulty; discuss extensions (side information at encoder/decoder, Wyner–Ziv/Heegard–Berger, input-dependent state).
- Explicitly state independence (or lack thereof) between source labels and worker states; dependence (e.g., hard items) alters capacity and separation results.
- Distinguish universal information-theoretic limits from kIC-specific lower bounds with an oracle decoder; avoid overinterpreting kIC “improvement” absent matching achievability.
- Caveat the pricing rule-of-thumb as model-dependent (SHC(q) and uniform label assumptions).
- Pros: novel JSCC framing, clean asymptotic limits as benchmarks, concrete kIC insights, well written and situated relative to prior NIPS work.
- Cons: missing rigorous separation justification/citations, asymptotic-only focus without finite-blocklength bounds or empirical validation, omission of item-side information and source–state coupling.
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on adding separation-with-state justification, clarifying assumptions and Sec.4.2 wording, and preferably including finite-blocklength bounds or a discussion with proper citations.
