- Paper models crowdsourced labeling as joint source–channel coding (JSCC), treating labels as a source, queries as encoder, workers as parallel DMCs with random skill state, and inference as decoder.  
- Considers two worker channel families: M-ary symmetric channels (MSC) with error ε and spammer–hammer channel (SHC(q)) where workers are perfect with prob. q or random otherwise.  
- Uses source–channel separation to derive asymptotic lower bounds on query rate for target Hamming error under two regimes: skill unknown (SL-UK) and skill known (SL-CS).  
- Shows the difference between SL-UK and SL-CS via entropy ordering HM(E[⋅]) vs E[HM(⋅)], reflecting CSIR increasing effective capacity.  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC): query of k items asking which belong to same cluster, excluding invalid partitions for implicit error correction.  
- For binary labels under SHC(q), Lemma 1 gives spammer error vs k and Theorem 4 gives a converse: if R < (1/k) ln(1−q)/ln ε̄S no decoder can achieve error ≤ ε.  
- Corollary 1 provides a specialization of the information-theoretic lower bound as an asymptotic benchmark for SHC with CSIR.  
- Sketches a pricing rule of thumb that scales approximately with k.  
- Situates work relative to aggregation algorithms (majority vote, Dawid–Skene EM), spectral/graph models, bandit task assignment, and prior information-theory treatments.  
- Strength: conceptual novelty — framing crowdsourcing as JSCC yields interpretable lower bounds separating skill uncertainty from coding design.  
- Strength: clear asymptotic limits — Theorems crisply distinguish SL-UK vs SL-CS and tie to channel capacity intuition; kIC converse targets a practical query primitive.  
- Strength: practical insights — dependence of required rate on alphabet size M, k, and q informs query design and pricing.  
- Weakness: reliance on source–channel separation with random state needs rigorous justification and citations (e.g., El Gamal & Kim) and precise CSI assumptions.  
- Weakness: encoder/decoder ignore raw items X, but item-dependent worker reliability or kIC tuple effects create source-dependent channel state where separation may fail — needs clarification or bounds.  
- Weakness: lacks finite-blocklength results — non-asymptotic converses/approximations (e.g., Kostina & Verdú) would improve practical relevance.  
- Weakness: wording in Sec.4.2 (“lower bounds improve as k and q grow”) is misleading; should clarify that required rate decreases as k,q increase.  
- Weakness: modeling details incomplete — define M for each query, clarify item sampling into kIC, coverage uniformity, integrality of R′, and independence across queries; discuss worker fatigue/correlated errors.  
- Weakness: missing empirical/algorithmic comparison — show gap between kIC converse and achievable performance (DS/EM, ML with CSIR) and compare kIC to unary/pairwise queries at equal effort.  
- Quality: asymptotic bounds and kIC converse are sound under stated assumptions but separation step needs precise statement and citation; proofs in appendix helpful but main-text sketches recommended.  
- Clarity: generally well-written; suggest glossary/table for notation (HM, HN, M) and emphasize SL-UK vs SL-CS in corollaries.  
- Originality: novel framing of crowdsourcing as JSCC and kIC analysis in that lens.  
- Significance: provides principled baseline for achievable budgets and can influence query/pricing design; non-asymptotic results or experiments would broaden impact.  
- Accept arguments: introduces foundational framework, bridges crowdsourcing and information theory, and offers actionable kIC insights.  
- Reject arguments: separation with random state not fully justified, ignores item-dependent/adaptive phenomena where separation may fail, and lacks finite-blocklength and achievability benchmarks.  
- Suggestions: add precise theorem/citations for separation under CSI models and state all assumptions (memoryless, i.i.d. selection, ergodicity).  
- Suggestions: discuss extensions to source-dependent channel state and adaptivity, noting where separation may fail or is open.  
- Suggestions: include finite-blocklength references/short non-asymptotic bounds, clarify M for query types, rephrase Sec.4.2 language, and provide experiments comparing achievable performance to limits.  
- Suggestions: justify pricing rule with a behavioral effort model and consider nonlinear cognitive load effects with k.
