- Presents an information-theoretic framework for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion and channel coding, analyzing budget–fidelity trade-offs for unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skill scenarios and introducing k-ary incidence coding (kIC).
- Builds on Dawid & Skene and recent spectral+EM work; novel use of rate-distortion and channel-coding ideas in crowdsourcing.
- Strength: Sound derivation of the information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound.
- Strength: Novel application of rate-distortion theory and channel coding to crowdsourcing.
- Strength: Introduces and analyzes a new query scheme, k-ary incidence coding (kIC).
- Weakness: Derived bound may be very loose, limiting practical relevance.
- Weakness: Channel-coding-style bounds may be inappropriate because they ignore platform constraints of crowdsourcing.
- Weakness: No comparison to state-of-the-art practical methods, making practical value hard to assess.
- Weakness: Minor errors and insufficient elaboration in parts of the paper (e.g., proof of Theorem 4).
- Originality: Application of rate-distortion/channel coding is original though derivations are straightforward; kIC is novel.
- Significance: Potentially significant theoretical contribution, but practical impact is questionable due to loose bounds.
- Clarity: Overall well-written and easy to follow, but some proofs need more detail.
- Quality: Good quality overall but could be improved by addressing the listed weaknesses.
- For acceptance: Novel theoretical framework, new kIC scheme, and sound derivations.
- Against acceptance: Potentially loose/inapplicable bounds and lack of empirical/state-of-the-art comparisons.
