- Maps crowdsourcing to a joint source–channel coding problem to analyze fundamental limits.
- Derives information-theoretic lower bounds on queries per item for a target fidelity (budget–fidelity trade-off).
- Models taskmaster assigning tasks to imperfect workers and the crowdsourcer inferring labels.
- Proposes and analyzes a specific query scheme: k-ary incidence coding.
- Strength: novel perspective applying information theory to crowdsourcing.
- Strength: rigorous, theoretically grounded lower bounds on performance limits.
- Strength: provides insights into designing efficient crowdsourcing via the analyzed scheme.
- Weakness: simplistic worker reliability model that merges skill and reliability into one channel.
- Weakness: assumes equal number of queries allocated to all workers, which may be unrealistic.
- Weakness: uses discrete channel models that may not capture adversarial or informed malicious workers.
- Weakness: numerical results and Figure 2 labeling are unclear, and k=1 is not explicitly defined.
- Methodological issue: assumes knowledge of P(B(X)) without stating this earlier in the paper.
- Minor issues: contains small errors such as incorrect figure references and some convoluted sentences.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify model assumptions, fix figure labels, state P(B(X)) earlier, and correct minor errors.
