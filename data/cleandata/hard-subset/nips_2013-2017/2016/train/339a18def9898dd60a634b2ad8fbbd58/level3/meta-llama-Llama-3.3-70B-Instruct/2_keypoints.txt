- Models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with a random channel state and derives fundamental limits on queries per item for a given fidelity.  
- Uses existing JSCC coding rate results to establish those fundamental limits.  
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and provides a lower bound on its error probability under a spammer-hammer channel model.  
- Novel application of information-theoretic principles to crowdsourcing, offering a new perspective on fundamental limits.  
- The kIC analysis gives useful insight into the performance of a concrete query scheme.  
- Leveraging existing JSCC results to bound crowdsourcing performance is a clever methodological approach.  
- Derivation of a lower bound on kIC error probability is a significant technical contribution.  
- Model may be too restrictive by assuming the taskmaster and crowdsourcer lack access to original data (no side information for encoder/decoder).  
- Use of the source-channel separation theorem may be questionable for channels with random state and requires a proof or literature reference.  
- Comment that lower bounds “improve” as k and q grow is misleading—bounds shrink, implying potentially better code performance rather than a stronger lower bound.  
- The paper lacks a non-asymptotic characterization of fundamental limits, which would be more practically useful.  
- Should consider and cite recent non-asymptotic converses for JSCC to strengthen the analysis.  
- Overall well-written and valuable, but needs additional analysis and clarification on model assumptions and theoretical justifications.
