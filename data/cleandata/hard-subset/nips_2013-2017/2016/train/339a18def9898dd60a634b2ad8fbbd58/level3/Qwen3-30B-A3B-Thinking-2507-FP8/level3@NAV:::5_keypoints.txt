- Presents an information-theoretic framework modeling workers as noisy channels (MSC and SHC(q)) to derive lower bounds on queries per task for target error rates.  
- Analyzes two scenarios: crowdsourcer with full knowledge of worker skills (SL-CS) versus no knowledge (SL-UK).  
- Studies k-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a practical query scheme and derives associated limits.  
- Provides a novel theoretical foundation distinct from prior algorithmic-focused crowdsourcing work.  
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and develops the theory rigorously.  
- Results are theoretically sound and offer useful budget–fidelity trade-off insights for system designers.  
- Offers a practically relevant pricing insight for kIC (π(k1)/π(k2) ≈ k1 k2).  
- Major weakness: no experimental validation or simulations to assess tightness and real-world applicability of the bounds.  
- Recommends shortening Sections 2.4 and 3.1 and adding proof sketches for key theorems to improve readability.  
- Overall: strong and novel theoretical contribution worth acceptance, but limited practical significance without empirical validation.
