- Paper frames crowdsourced labeling as an information-theoretic coding problem, deriving fundamental budget–fidelity trade-offs.  
- Workers are modeled as symmetric memoryless channels (MSC) or spammer/hammer channels (SHC), linking worker noise to channel noise.  
- Crowdsourcing budget is mapped to code rate and analyzed for resulting distortion limits.  
- Introduces and analyzes a specific coding scheme called k-incidence coding.  
- Considers optimized query pricing and connects coding parameters to pricing strategies.  
- Novel perspective: applies classical source and channel coding ideas to crowdsourcing.  
- Strength: rigorous, information-theoretic derivation of bounds on required query rates for target fidelity.  
- Strength: k-incidence coding offers a theoretically grounded potential practical scheme.  
- Strength: effort to bridge theory and application via query-pricing analysis.  
- Weakness: MSC and SHC channel models oversimplify complex human behavior.  
- Weakness: Limited connection to practical implementation and real-world validation.  
- Weakness: Numerical results and simulations are sparse; more extensive experiments are needed.  
- Reviewer recommendation: overall positive—recommend acceptance with suggestion to expand experimental validation.
