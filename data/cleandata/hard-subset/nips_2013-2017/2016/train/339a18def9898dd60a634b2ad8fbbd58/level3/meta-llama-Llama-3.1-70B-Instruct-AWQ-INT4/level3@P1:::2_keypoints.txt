- Models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random worker skill-level states and derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item.
- Derives a lower bound on the error probability of k-ary incidence coding under a specific crowdsourcing/channel model.
- Builds on prior crowdsourcing and information-theory work (e.g., Dawid & Skene 1979, Kostina & Verdú 2013) and related clustering literature.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized.
- Provides a promising first step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing with potential for new insights.
- Offers asymptotic fundamental limits that serve as useful benchmarks for crowdsourcing performance.
- Assumes source-channel separation with random channel state but lacks clear justification or citation for this assumption.
- Ignores original items as side information in encoder/decoder modeling, which may be important and should be considered.
- Wording in Sec. 4.2/Fig. 2 is misleading about how the lower bound behaves as k and q vary.
- The contribution is original in framing crowdsourcing as a JSCC problem and deriving limits.
- The work could be significant by providing a new framework for evaluating crowdsourcing systems.
- Clarity is high: the paper uses clear and concise language.
- Pros: promising JSCC framing, derived asymptotic limits, and strong exposition.
- Cons: unjustified source-channel separation assumption, omission of item side information, and misleading figure/wording.
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to justify source-channel separation, incorporate or discuss item side information, and rephrase Sec. 4.2/Fig. 2 for clarity.
