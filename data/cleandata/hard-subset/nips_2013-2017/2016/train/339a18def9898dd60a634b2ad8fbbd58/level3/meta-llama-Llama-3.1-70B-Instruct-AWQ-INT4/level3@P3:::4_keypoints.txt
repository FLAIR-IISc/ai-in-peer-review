- Maps crowdsourcing to joint source-channel coding and derives a budget-fidelity trade-off via information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Models crowdsourcing in a rate-distortion framework to identify ultimate fidelity achievable for a given budget across all queries and decoders.  
- Main contribution: derivation of fundamental limits on crowdsourcing rate for a target fidelity.  
- Analyzes two scenarios: unknown worker skill (SL-UK) and known worker skill (SL-CS).  
- SL-UK yields overall work quality equal to an average worker; SL-CS yields overall quality equal to the average of worker qualities.  
- Proposes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) and analyzes its performance theoretically.  
- Derives lower bounds for kIC performance under a spammer-hammer worker pool; performance improves with larger k and q.  
- Strength: novel, rigorous, and technically sound use of information-theoretic tools with well-supported theoretical claims.  
- Weakness: model conflates worker skill and reliability into a single channel, losing the distinction between inherent skill and reporting noise.  
- Weakness: assumes equal query allocation to all workers, which is unrealistic given heterogeneous and rated workers.  
- Weakness: worker/channel models may not capture adversarial or informed malicious workers; realism needs clarification and justification.  
- Presentation issue: numerical results and explanations are unclear; Figure 2 curve labeling needs improvement and k=1 should be explicitly stated.  
- Methodological issue: assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) is introduced late and should be declared earlier.  
- Minor corrections: fix figure references on page 4 (Figure 1.a → 1.b, 1.b → 1.c).  
- Writing/style: improve referencing of theorems/lemmas/corollaries (use lowercase or numbering) for clarity.  
- Clarity: reorganize sections and add more explanations of technical terms and concepts.  
- Originality: high — presents a novel perspective and new theoretical results with adequate related-work positioning.  
- Significance: high — advances the state of the art on crowdsourcing limits.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to address modeling realism, presentation clarity, and justificatory explanations.
