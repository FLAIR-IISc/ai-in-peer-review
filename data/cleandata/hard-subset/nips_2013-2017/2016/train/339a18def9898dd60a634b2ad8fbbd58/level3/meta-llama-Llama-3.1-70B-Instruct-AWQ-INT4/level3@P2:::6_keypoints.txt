- Paper models budget–fidelity trade-off in crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic rate-distortion framework.  
- Main contribution: derivation of an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound using rate-distortion and channel coding.  
- Considers two scenarios: unknown worker skill levels (SL-UK) and known worker skill levels (SL-CS).  
- Derives minimum number of queries per item to achieve overall error probability ≤ ε.  
- Analyzes performance of a specific query scheme: k-ary incidence coding (kIC).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly structured, and provides thorough theoretical analysis with theorems and lemmas.  
- Strength: provides a new theoretical perspective and fundamental limits for crowdsourcing system design.  
- Concern: derivation appears straightforward and it is unclear whether the bound is tight.  
- Concern: practical value is limited due to lack of comparison with state-of-the-art practical methods.  
- Requests: authors should compare derived bounds to existing methods to demonstrate relevance.  
- Minor issues: clarify equation (8) minimization over C, C' with E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t.  
- Minor issues: expand and clarify the proof of Theorem 4 and whether ε_S > ε.  
- Minor issues: explain or cite justification for H_M(E(ε)) appearing in equation (17).  
- Recommendation: revise to address novelty, practical relevance, comparisons, and the listed minor clarifications.  
- Overall rating: 6/10.
