- Models crowdsourcing as a communication system (taskmaster → workers as channels → crowdsourcer), enabling information-theoretic analysis.
- Derives lower bounds on queries-per-item for given fidelity; separate theorems for unknown and known worker skill levels.
- Analyzes k-ary incidence coding, including a lower bound for a spammer-hammer worker pool and supporting numerical results.
- Novel information-theoretic framing provides a fresh perspective on crowdsourcing problems.
- Offers a theoretical foundation to inform design and performance limits of crowdsourcing systems.
- Provides insights into the fundamental budget–fidelity trade-off in crowdsourcing.

- Related work coverage is insufficient, notably lacking discussion of economic incentives and mechanism design in crowdsourcing.
- Some clarity issues—e.g., distinction between “crowdsourcer” and “taskmaster” and other terminology need tightening.
