- Proposes modeling crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state.  
- Derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item, characterizing the budget–fidelity trade-off.  
- Main theoretical contributions: Theorems 2 and 3 giving asymptotic query-rate limits for unknown and known worker skill levels.  
- Provides a lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding under a specific crowdsourcing/channel model.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, original, technically sound, and significant.  
- Major technical concern: need to justify or cite that source–channel separation holds when the channel has a random state unknown to the encoder (possibly known to the decoder); this underpins Theorems 2 and 3.  
- Modeling concern: encoder/decoder assume access only to labels and ignore original items as side information—this may be unrealistic and needs discussion/extension.  
- Wording issue in Sec. 4.2/Fig. 2: phrase “lower bounds improve as k and q grow” is misleading; should state the lower bound value decreases (implying potential performance gain).  
- Suggestion: include non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) converses (e.g., cite Kostina & Verdú 2013) for a more complete analysis.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
