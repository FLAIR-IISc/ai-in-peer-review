- Novel information-theoretic approach to modeling and analyzing crowdsourcing systems.
- Framework views crowdsourcing as human-in-the-loop computation with a taskmaster breaking projects into tasks for imperfect workers.
- Thorough analysis of fundamental performance limits, including budget vs. fidelity tradeoffs.
- Main contribution: joint source-channel coding scheme representing query design and inference in crowdsourcing.
- Derived information-theoretic bounds on the minimum number of queries per item for given fidelity, for known and unknown worker skills.
- Introduced k-ary incidence coding query scheme and analyzed its performance using a spammer-hammer worker model.
- Well-organized, clearly written paper with good balance of theory and numerical results.
- Comprehensive related-work review and clear explanation of significance.
- Use of information theory provides novel insights into crowdsourcing limits and design.
- Paper may be hard for non-experts; needs more high-level intuition and accessibility.
- Paper would benefit from a clearer conclusion summarizing key takeaways and implications.
- Lacks sufficient discussion of related work on economic incentives and mechanism design for crowdsourcing.
- Minor issues with notation and terminology (e.g., clearer distinction between "worker" and "channel") and some unclear sentences.
- Overall valuable contribution with potential to improve crowdsourcing system design, contingent on revisions.
