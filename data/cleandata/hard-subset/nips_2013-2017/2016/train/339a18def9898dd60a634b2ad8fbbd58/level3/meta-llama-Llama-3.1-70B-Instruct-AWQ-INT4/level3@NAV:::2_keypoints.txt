- Novel modeling of crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state.  
- Derivation of asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item, providing query-rate bounds.  
- Communication-system abstraction: labels as i.i.d. source, taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses with random skill states, crowdsourcer as decoder.  
- Application of source-channel separation to obtain Theorems 2 and 3 for SL-UK and SL-CS scenarios.  
- Lower bound on error probability for k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a specified crowdsourcing/channel model.  
- kIC lower bounds depend on k, q, and fidelity and improve as k and q increase.  
- Paper is well-written with clear, concise explanation and novel use of information-theoretic tools.  
- Technical concern: need to justify or cite correctness of source-channel separation when the channel has random state unknown to the encoder.  
- Technical concern: assumption that encoder/decoder ignore original items as side information may be unrealistic and could affect results.  
- Presentation issue: wording in Sec. 4.2/Fig. 2 is misleading about the meaning of the lower bound decreasing versus being “tightened.”  
- Missing analysis: lack of non-asymptotic (finite-blocklength) converses or discussion of finite-sample behavior.  
- Overall assessment: promising first step with valuable contributions, but requires revisions to address technical and presentation concerns.  
- Review rating: 8/10; confidence: 8/10.
