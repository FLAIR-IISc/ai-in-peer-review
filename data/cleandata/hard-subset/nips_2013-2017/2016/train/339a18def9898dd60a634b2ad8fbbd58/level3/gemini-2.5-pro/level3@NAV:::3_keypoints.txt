- Proposes an information-theoretic framework modeling crowdsourcing as a communication system and derives the budget–fidelity trade-off (budget-fidelity function).  
- Applies the analysis to settings with known/unknown worker skills and evaluates a specific query scheme (k-ary Incidence Coding).  
- Strength: original, significant contribution providing a rate-distortion perspective and fundamental performance benchmarks beyond prior algorithmic work.  
- Strength: model and insights are potentially very useful for gauging efficiency of practical algorithms.  
- Weakness: paper is hard to access for a general ML audience due to heavy information-theory jargon that obscures practical implications.  
- Weakness: omits relevant literature on economic aspects of crowdsourcing (mechanism design and incentive schemes) that also study budget–quality trade-offs.  
- Revision 1: restate key results in concrete crowdsourcing terms, expand intuition (e.g., end of Section 3) and add a concluding takeaway section.  
- Revision 2: broaden related work to include mechanism design and robust incentives and clarify how the information-theoretic approach complements/differs from them.  
- Revision 3: fix specific readability issues—clarify "crowdsourcer" vs "taskmaster," improve Figure 2 caption/visualization, and copyedit typos and unclear sentences.  
- Overall: promising theoretical paper; addressing clarity and positioning issues will substantially increase its NIPS impact.
