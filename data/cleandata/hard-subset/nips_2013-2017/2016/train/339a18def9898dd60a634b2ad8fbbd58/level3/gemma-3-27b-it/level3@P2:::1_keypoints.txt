- Paper studies budget–fidelity limits in crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic rate–distortion framework, modeling workers as noisy channels.
- Main contributions: fundamental bounds on achievable fidelity given query budget, analysis for unknown vs. known worker skill, and a constructive k-ary incidence coding (kIC) scheme with analysis under the SHC model.
- Novelty: applying classical information theory (rate–distortion, channel capacity) to crowdsourcing is a fresh, rigorous perspective.
- Fundamental bounds (Theorems 2 and 3) provide algorithm-agnostic benchmarks for achievable performance.
- k-ary incidence coding is a concrete query scheme offering potential error-correction and efficiency benefits, with theoretical analysis (Theorem 4).
- Paper situates itself well within prior work (majority voting, Dawid–Skene, spectral methods, bandit surveys) and clearly connects to joint source–channel coding.
- Mathematical presentation is clear and rigorous, accessible to readers with information-theory background.
- Considering both unknown and known worker skills gives a nuanced view and highlights benefits of skill estimation.
- Lacks empirical validation; numerical results only illustrate bounds and do not test kIC on real crowdsourcing data.
- Relies on simplifying assumptions (worker independence, memoryless channels, uniform source) with limited discussion of their practical impact.
- Practicality concerns for kIC, especially for large k, due to potential increased cognitive load and unaddressed effects on data quality.
- Query pricing discussion (Appendix E) is brief and under-justified; the provided rule-of-thumb needs more elaboration and practical grounding.
- Notation inconsistency (e.g., R vs. Rt) could be clarified for reader clarity.
- For acceptance: novel, rigorous theoretical contribution; useful benchmarks; a constructive query scheme that could influence future work.
- Against acceptance: limited practical validation, questionable generalizability due to assumptions, and insufficient treatment of kIC practicality and pricing.
- Overall: solid theoretical paper that needs empirical validation and deeper discussion of assumptions and practical implications.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing experimental validation and more thorough discussion of assumptions and real-world applicability.
