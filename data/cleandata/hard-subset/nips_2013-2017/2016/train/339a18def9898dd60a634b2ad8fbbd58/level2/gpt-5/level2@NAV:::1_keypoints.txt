- Models crowdsourcing as joint source–channel coding and derives information-theoretic budget–fidelity limits.
- Main claims: lower bounds on queries per item for SL-UK and SL-CS under the MSC worker model.
- Additional claim: analysis of k-ary incidence coding (kIC) under a spammer–hammer (SHC) model with lower bounds and a pricing heuristic.
- Connects to classic rate–distortion/channel capacity theory and to crowdsourcing aggregation literature (Dawid–Skene, Karger–Oh–Shah, spectral/EM).
- kIC idea ties to pairwise/graph-based clustering constraints, emphasizing error-correction from ruling out invalid incidence patterns.
- Theorems for Rmin under MSC and kIC lower bound under SHC are technically sound with outlined proofs.
- Use of separation principle yields principled, clean limits.
- Framing via rate–distortion is original and clarifies when “average worker” vs “average capacity” governs performance; kIC and the pricing rule are insightful.
- Establishing ultimate limits is significant for experimental design, platform pricing, and benchmarking algorithms.
- Strong/restrictive modeling assumptions: MSC/SHC, iid items, non-adaptive coding, worker independence, and perfect skill estimation in SL-CS; no adversarial/difficulty-dependent noise.
- No simulations or experiments to validate bound tightness or finite-sample behavior; pricing heuristic lacks empirical support.
- Notational ambiguities (e.g., M vs N, U=V), missing citation years, and incomplete direct comparison to minimax and allocation results (e.g., KOS).
- Suggested additions: simulations comparing kIC vs binary queries across q, k, and costs to show finite-length gaps.
- Suggested exploration: adaptive/query-dependent designs and intermediate (partial) skill-estimation regimes between SL-UK and SL-CS.
- Suggested clarifications: fix notation/citations, discuss non-uniform class priors and multiclass kIC, and compare more directly to Karger–Oh–Shah and spectral/EM theory.
- For acceptance: clear, principled information-theoretic framing with novel kIC and pricing insights.
- Against acceptance: lacks empirical/finite-length evidence, relies on strong assumptions, and has incomplete related-work positioning.
- Overall recommendation: solid theoretical contribution; accept if authors add simulations and strengthen discussion of assumptions and prior theory.
