**Summary of the Paper**

The paper "Fundamental Limits of Budget-Fidelity Trade-off in Label Crowdsourcing" presents a novel approach to modeling and analyzing the crowdsourcing problem in an information theoretic setting. The authors aim to identify the ultimate performance bounds of crowdsourcing in terms of budget (number of queries per item) and fidelity (accuracy of the results). They propose a communication system model for crowdsourcing, where the crowd is represented by a set of parallel noisy channels, and the taskmaster and crowdsourcer are modeled as an encoder and decoder, respectively.

**Claims and Contributions**

The main claims of the paper are:

1. The authors establish a fundamental limit on the minimum number of queries per item required to achieve a target fidelity in crowdsourcing, which is oblivious to the type of code used.
2. They present a novel query scheme, k-ary incidence coding (kIC), which poses a query as a set of k items and inquires the workers to identify those with the same label.
3. They analyze the performance of kIC with a spammer-hammer worker pool and establish a lower bound on the performance assuming an Oracle decoder.

The contributions of the paper are:

1. A novel information theoretic framework for modeling and analyzing crowdsourcing.
2. A fundamental limit on the minimum number of queries per item required to achieve a target fidelity.
3. A new query scheme, kIC, which improves the performance of crowdsourcing.

**Support for Claims**

They derive the fundamental limit on the minimum number of queries per item required to achieve a target fidelity using information theoretic tools, such as the joint source channel coding theorem.
