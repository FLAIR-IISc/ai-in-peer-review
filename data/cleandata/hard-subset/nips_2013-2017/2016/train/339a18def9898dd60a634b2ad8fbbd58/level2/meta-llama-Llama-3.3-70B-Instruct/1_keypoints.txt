- Novel approach: models crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic rate-distortion framework to identify ultimate fidelity given a query budget.  
- System model: represents the crowd as parallel noisy channels and queries/inference as a joint source-channel coding scheme.  
- New method: introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a novel query scheme.  
- Theoretical results: derives information-theoretic bounds on minimum queries per item to achieve target fidelity.  
- Worker modeling: analyzes worker skill levels and the impact of spammer-hammer behavior on system performance.  
- Empirical validation: provides numerical results and simulations comparing kIC to existing approaches.  
- Clarity and positioning: paper is well-written, well-organized, with clear notation and a thorough related-work review.  
- Strengths: novel modeling framework, thorough analysis, and useful guidance for optimized crowdsourcing system design.  
- Limitation: assumes worker skill levels are either unknown or perfectly known, which may be unrealistic.  
- Limitation: focuses mainly on binary labeling tasks; generalization to more complex tasks is unclear.  
- Recommendation: reviewer rates the paper as a strong accept based on contributions and supporting results.
