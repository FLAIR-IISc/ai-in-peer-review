- Main contribution: Proposes an information-theoretic framework modeling crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel system and derives rate-distortion bounds characterizing minimum queries per item for a target error rate; analyzes k-ary Incidence Coding (kIC).
- Originality/significance: Applying rate-distortion theory to crowdsourcing is novel and provides principled, problem-agnostic performance limits and benchmarks.
- Practical insight: Framework quantifies the value of information (e.g., knowing worker skill) and can guide more efficient system design.
- Technical quality: Theoretical formulation is rigorous, well-justified, and yields clear, interpretable theorems under stated assumptions.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and the communication-system analogy makes complex concepts accessible.
- Weakness: Entirely theoretical with no empirical validation or simulations to compare practical algorithms (e.g., Majority Voting, Dawid-Skene EM) against the bounds.
- Weakness: Results rely on asymptotic assumptions (large datasets/block lengths), with limited discussion of implications for finite-sized, real-world tasks.
- Recommendation: Recommend acceptance for novelty and significance, but strongly encourage adding simulation-based experiments in future versions to connect theory to practice.
