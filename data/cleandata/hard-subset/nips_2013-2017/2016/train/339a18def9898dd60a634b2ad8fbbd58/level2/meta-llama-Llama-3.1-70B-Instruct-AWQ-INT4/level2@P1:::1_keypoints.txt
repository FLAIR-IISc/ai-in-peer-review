- Proposes a novel information-theoretic framework to model and analyze crowdsourcing.  
- Models crowdsourcing as a communication system with a source (dataset), channels (workers), and a decoder (crowdsourcer).  
- Derives information-theoretic bounds on the minimum number of queries per item needed to achieve a target fidelity.  
- Analyzes two worker-skill scenarios: skill levels unknown (SL-UK) and skill levels known (SL-CS).  
- Introduces and analyzes a new query scheme, k-ary incidence coding (kIC).  
- Provides a thorough literature review and highlights limitations of existing crowdsourcing approaches.  
- Presents clear, well-organized writing and exposition.  
- Includes numerical results and comparisons that illustrate framework performance.

- Weakness: insufficient context/motivation about specific practical problems the framework addresses.
