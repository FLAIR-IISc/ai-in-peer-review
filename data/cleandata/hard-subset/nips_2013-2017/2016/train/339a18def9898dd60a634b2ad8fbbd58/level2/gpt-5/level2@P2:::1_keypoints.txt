1. Paper frames crowdsourced labeling as a joint source–channel coding problem and studies budget–fidelity trade-offs.  
2. Considers N-ary latent labels, queries answered via noisy worker channels under MSC and SHC models.  
3. Analyzes two information regimes: skill levels unknown to both (SL-UK) and known to crowdsourcer (SL-CS).  
4. Uses source–channel separation to derive lower bounds on queries per item Rmin for target Hamming distortion.  
5. Proposes k-ary incidence coding (kIC) where workers partition k items by label-equivalence, embedding redundancy via transitivity.  
6. Derives spammer error probability under kIC for SHC and necessary R conditions with an oracle decoder for binary labels.  
7. Sketches an approximate pricing rule for kIC.  
8. Novelty: information-theoretic treatment yielding closed-form lower bounds and kIC analyzed as an error-correcting code.  
9. Lacks quantitative comparison of bounds to DS-type sample-complexity results and minimax rates in crowdsourcing literature.  
10. Strength: clean, unifying information-theoretic framework with explicit rate–distortion lower bounds for two regimes.  
11. Strength: clear intuition that SL-CS improves performance by leveraging skill estimation versus SL-UK.  
12. Strength: kIC is implementable and parameterized by k and worker mix; analysis under SHC is interpretable.  
13. Weakness: strong modeling assumptions (iid labels, independent workers, MSC/SHC, non-adaptive querying, oracle decoder).  
14. Weakness: limited validation—no experiments on real or synthetic data comparing to majority vote, DS, or spectral methods.  
15. Weakness: clarity/notation issues (confusion between N and M, unclear definitions of H_M, H_N, E(·), typos).  
16. Weakness: bounds are mainly converse/asymptotic via separation and ignore practical constraints and achievability.  
17. Weakness: kIC pricing heuristic insufficiently justified; cognitive cost likely scales with C(k,2), not k.  
18. Quality concern: capacity expressions for mixtures with/without decoder side information may understate composite-channel subtleties.  
19. Clarity suggestion: provide consolidated notation, explicit definitions of H_M/H_N/E, and fix editorial errors.  
20. Empirical suggestion: compare lower bounds to performance of majority vote, DS, and spectral–EM under synthetic mixtures to assess tightness.  
21. kIC suggestion: quantify invalid incidence patterns for general k and N and relate to error-correcting capability and optimal k.  
22. Pricing suggestion: justify cost scaling with k vs C(k,2) using user-time or platform data.  
23. Extension suggestion: consider intermediate SL-EST with imperfect skill estimation and analyze impact on Rmin.  
24. Pros for acceptance: principled info-theoretic framing, insightful SL-UK vs SL-CS comparison, and concrete kIC analysis.  
25. Cons for acceptance: strong assumptions, asymptotic bounds without empirical grounding, notation issues, and lack of realistic achievability.  
26. Overall: promising conceptual contribution but needs clearer derivations, empirical validation, and connections to prior theory; recommend rejection now with revision path.
