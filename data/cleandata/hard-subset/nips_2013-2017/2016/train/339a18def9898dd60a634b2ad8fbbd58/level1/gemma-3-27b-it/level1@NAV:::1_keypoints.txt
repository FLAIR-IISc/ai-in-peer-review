- Models crowdsourcing as a joint source-channel coding problem to study budget (queries) vs. fidelity trade-offs.
- Aims to determine ultimate fidelity achievable given budget and worker skill levels.
- Derives fundamental bounds for unknown skill levels (SL-UK) and perfectly estimated skill levels (SL-CS).
- Proposes and analyzes a k-ary incidence coding (kIC) scheme for query design and pricing insights.
- Frames workers as noisy communication channels, enabling application of information-theoretic tools.
- Builds on and cites prior crowdsourcing and information-theory work (e.g., Dawid & Skene; Cover & Thomas).
- Theorems 2 and 3 provide rigorous mathematical bounds on achievable performance.
- Strong mathematical rigor but dense notation and limited intuitive explanations hinder clarity.
- Results are largely theoretical and asymptotic; empirical validation (simulations/experiments) is limited.
- Query pricing discussion (Appendix E) feels tacked on and lacks detailed analysis.
- kIC is an interesting concrete scheme linking theory to practice, but practical impact needs demonstration.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity and add empirical validation.
