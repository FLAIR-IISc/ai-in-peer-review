- Models crowdsourcing as an information-theoretic rate–distortion problem linking budget to labeling fidelity.  
- Frames dataset as source, workers as parallel noisy channels (MSC and SHC), queries as encoding, and inference as decoding.  
- Extends prior crowdsourcing work by deriving fundamental information-theoretic limits instead of heuristics.  
- Proves two main theorems giving minimum query rates for target fidelity under unknown (SL-UK) and known (SL-CS) worker skill levels.  
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding (kIC) as a novel query scheme with error-correcting properties.  
- Strength: strong theoretical foundations and clear problem formulation within information theory.  
- Strength: kIC is a novel query-design idea that could improve crowdsourcing efficiency.  
- Strength: well-structured presentation with clear definitions and logical progression.  
- Weakness: no empirical validation—bounds are not tested on real crowdsourcing data or practical algorithms.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of how theoretical limits translate to practical system design and implementation.  
- Weakness: some technical components (e.g., SHC model) lack accessible motivation for non‑information‑theory readers.  
- Overall: technically sound and original, appropriate for NIPS scope, but impact reduced by lack of practical evaluation and implementation discussion.
