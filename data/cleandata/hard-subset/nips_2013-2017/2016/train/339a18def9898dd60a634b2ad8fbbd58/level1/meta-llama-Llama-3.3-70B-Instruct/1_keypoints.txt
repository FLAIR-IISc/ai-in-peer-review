- Proposes a novel information-theoretic (rate-distortion) framework to model and analyze digital crowdsourcing (CS) systems.
- Develops a communication-system model treating the crowd as parallel noisy channels representing workers with imperfect skills.
- Aims to identify fundamental fidelity limits achievable by any query and decoding algorithm under a given budget.
- Introduces a new query scheme, k-ary incidence coding, which outperforms traditional query schemes in the authors' analysis.
- Provides clear, concise writing and a well-presented introduction to CS challenges.
- Offers a thorough theoretical analysis of CS performance limits, including numerical results and comparisons with existing methods.
- Demonstrates the potential of the framework to guide design and optimization of CS systems for applications like ML and data labeling.
- Main limitation: assumes a simplistic worker skill model that may not reflect real-world worker heterogeneity.
- Limited practical insight: paper focuses largely on theory with little detail on implementation or real-world deployment.
- Recommendation: accept, but with suggested revisions to address limitations.
- Suggested improvements: refine worker models to better match real scenarios and provide more guidance on practical implementation.
