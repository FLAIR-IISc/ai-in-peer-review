- Studies budget-fidelity trade-offs in label crowdsourcing using an information-theoretic joint source-channel coding framework.  
- Derives bounds on achievable fidelity given a query budget and vice versa for unknown and known worker skill scenarios.  
- Introduces and analyzes a k-ary incidence coding scheme as a practical query design with error-correction properties.  
- Discusses optimized query pricing and its potential practical implications.  
- Novel application of information theory to crowdsourcing, shifting focus to fundamental limits rather than algorithms.  
- Theoretical derivations and bounds are mathematically rigorous and well-justified.  
- Modeling as a joint source-channel coding problem is clear and intuitively captures worker uncertainty and budget-accuracy trade-offs.  
- Connects to rate-distortion theory and prior crowdsourcing/worker error literature, situating the work in existing research.  
- k-ary incidence coding offers a potentially practical query scheme building on existing concepts.  
- Assumes large numbers of i.i.d. workers and simplified channel models (MSC, SHC), which may not hold in real settings.  
- Lacks empirical validation or simulations to demonstrate tightness of bounds and real-world applicability.  
- Practical implementation details and computational complexity of k-ary incidence coding (including optimal k) are not sufficiently addressed.  
- Some notation is unclear or dense (e.g., HN(.) in Theorem 2), reducing readability.  
- Mentions feedback as an extension but does not analyze its impact on rate-distortion or coding strategies.  
- Paper is technically sound and well-organized but would benefit from empirical results and clearer notation.  
- Original and significant contribution that provides benchmarks for crowdsourcing system design and pricing strategies.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on adding empirical validation or simulations and improving notation clarity in future versions.
