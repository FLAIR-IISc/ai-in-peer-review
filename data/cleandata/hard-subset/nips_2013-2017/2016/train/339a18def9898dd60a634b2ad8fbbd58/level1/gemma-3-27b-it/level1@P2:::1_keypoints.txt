- Frames crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding problem and studies budget–fidelity trade-offs via rate–distortion theory.  
- Models workers as imperfect channels (MSC, SHC) and derives information-theoretic bounds (Theorems 2 & 3).  
- Introduces k-ary incidence coding as a constructive query scheme and provides initial performance analysis.  
- Explores optimized query pricing but the connection to the core theoretical results is weak and feels tacked on.  
- Technical framework is solid and derivations appear mathematically sound, though proofs are in appendices and need careful verification.  
- Relies on strong simplifying assumptions (i.i.d. data, specific channel models) that limit applicability to real-world crowdsourcing.  
- Practical implications and robustness of the bounds under assumption violations are unclear and underexplored.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized but dense; mathematical notation and volume of variables can be overwhelming.  
- Figures (notably Figure 1) help visualize the model; appendices lack sufficient detail for straightforward reproduction.  
- Novel application of classical information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing, offering a new theoretical perspective beyond prior algorithmic work.  
- k-ary incidence coding represents a novel query design idea that could be useful with further study.  
- Would benefit from more thorough discussion of related work in active learning, which relates to query design.  
- Limited empirical validation and practical evaluation reduce immediate impact for practitioners.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept — valuable theoretical contribution but needs stronger connection to practice and robustness analysis.
