- Presents a comprehensive information-theoretic analysis of budget–fidelity trade-offs in label crowdsourcing.  
- Models crowdsourcing as a rate–distortion problem with the crowd as parallel noisy channels and query+inference as joint source–channel coding.  
- Derives information-theoretic bounds on the minimum number of queries per item needed to achieve a target fidelity.  
- Introduces a novel query scheme, k-ary incidence coding, that outperforms traditional query schemes.  
- Provides a thorough analysis of k-ary incidence coding including a lower-bound on performance and a practical pricing rule-of-thumb.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with logical flow and technical derivations supported in appendices.  
- Technical sections are rigorous, mathematically sound, and include detailed proofs.  
- Well-related to prior work with a comprehensive literature discussion.  
- Numerical results support and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.  
- Strengths: rigorous theoretical contributions, novel coding scheme, clear exposition, and supportive experiments.  
- Weaknesses: highly technical and may be difficult for non-experts to follow.  
- Weaknesses: assumes substantial mathematical sophistication, limiting accessibility for some readers.  
- Weaknesses: lacks a clear discussion of practical implications, which may constrain real-world impact.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept—significant contributions and solid presentation despite accessibility concerns.
