- Frames crowdsourcing budget–fidelity trade-offs as a human-in-the-loop communication problem using information theory.
- Models workers as parallel noisy channels and uses joint source–channel coding to derive fundamental limits.
- Derives mathematically rigorous bounds on achievable fidelity given a budget (Theorems 2 and 3).
- Analyzes a specific query scheme, k-ary incidence coding (kIC), and provides performance analysis (Theorem 4).
- Introduces query pricing considerations linked to the derived bounds, though treatment is preliminary.
- Novelty lies in applying classical information-theoretic tools to crowdsourcing and proposing kIC.
- Paper is technically sound and well-motivated, with consistent notation and clear system model visuals.
- Lacks sufficient empirical validation or simulations to demonstrate tightness of theoretical bounds.
- Query pricing discussion is cursory and would benefit from concrete examples or comparisons to existing strategies.
- Reliance on the i.i.d. source assumption may limit real-world applicability; relaxing it should be discussed.
- Some exposition issues: abrupt transitions (Lemma 1 to Theorem 4), dense equations, and a need for smoother introduction of kIC.
- Insightful distinction between SL-UK and SL-CS, highlighting gains from worker skill estimation.
- Overall significant contribution with potential practical impact, but needs more empirical work and clearer explanations.
