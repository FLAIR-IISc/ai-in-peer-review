- Paper maps crowdsourcing with unreliable workers to joint source–channel coding and derives a budget–fidelity trade-off via information-theoretic lower bounds.  
- Reviewer finds the rate–distortion framing interesting and potentially inspiring for future work.  
- Concern: model merges worker skill and reliability into a single channel, losing distinction between skill/rating and reporting noise.  
- Concern: taskmaster allocates the same number of queries to all workers, which is unrealistic given workers often have known ratings and heterogeneous capabilities.  
- Concern: the channel models for worker unreliability may not realistically capture adversarial or informed malicious workers.  
- Authors should clarify and justify realism of the assumed worker/channel models.  
- Specific scheme (k-ary incidence coding) is analyzed and compared to the information-theoretic lower bound for a particular channel model.  
- Numerical results/explanations are unclear: improve labeling of curves in Fig.2 and explicitly state what k=1 corresponds to.  
- Methodological issue: assumption of knowledge of P(B(X)) is stated late (page 6, line 246) and should be declared earlier in the paper.  
- Minor corrections: fix figure references on page 4 (Figure 1.a → 1.b, 1.b → 1.c).  
- Writing/style: authors misuse 'Theorem', 'Lemma', and 'Corollary' without numbering; use lowercase or add numbers for clarity.
