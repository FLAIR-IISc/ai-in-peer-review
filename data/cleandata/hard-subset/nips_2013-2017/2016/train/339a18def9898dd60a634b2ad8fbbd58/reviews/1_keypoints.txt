- Models crowdsourcing workers as noisy channels (symmetric memoryless channel and spammer/hammer channel).
- Reformulates the labeling problem as a coding problem for the considered channels.
- Maps the crowdsourcing budget to the code rate for analytical purposes.
- Analyzes distortion versus budget tradeoffs.
- Proposes and studies a basic coding scheme called k-incidence coding (labeling subsets of k items).
