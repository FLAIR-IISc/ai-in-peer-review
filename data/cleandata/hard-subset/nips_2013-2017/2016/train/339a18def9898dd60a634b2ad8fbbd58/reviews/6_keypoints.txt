- Paper derives an information-theoretic budget–fidelity bound for crowdsourcing using rate-distortion theory and channel coding; derivation is sound.  
- If this is the first application of those tools to crowdsourcing, it could be a meaningful contribution.  
- Novelty and practical value are questionable because the derivation is straightforward and the bound may be very loose.  
- Main concern: unlike channel coding, crowdsourcing may not allow wide freedom to design the input sequence u^n (encoding), so channel-coding-style bounds may be inappropriate.  
- It’s unclear which mappings from B(X)^n to U^(Σ m_i) are implementable as codebooks in crowdsourcing; this could make the capacity bound loose.  
- While repetition codes (majority voting) are possible, it’s unclear how to design u^n to achieve stronger error-correction in crowdsourcing.  
- A key difference: you cannot query the same worker multiple times without violating the iid assumptions, unlike repeated uses of an iid communication channel.  
- Consequently, crowdsourcing likely imposes fundamental constraints on allowed encodings in (3), undermining the tightness of the proposed bound.  
- The paper should compare the derived information-theoretic bound to state-of-the-art practical methods.  
- Minor: In (8) clarify that the min is over C, C' with E[d(Z,Ẑ)] ≤ D^t.  
- Minor: Proof of Theorem 4 needs more elaboration.  
- Minor: In Theorem 4, since ln(1−q) < 0, clarify whether ε_S is larger than \hat{ε}.  
- Minor: In (17), explain why H_M(E(ε)) appears or cite prior work justifying it.
