- Paper models crowdsourcing with an information-theoretic framework to give lower bounds on queries per task for target error probability.
- Workers are modeled as parallel independent channels.
- Considers symmetric discrete memoryless channels (MSC) for worker errors and spammer-hammer channel SHC(q) for spammers.
- Assumes nonadaptive coding (task assignments fixed ahead of answers).
- Analyzes two crowdsourcer knowledge cases: full knowledge of worker skills and no knowledge.
- Taskmaster assumed ignorant of worker skills and assigns equal tasks to all workers.
- Provides a necessary condition on minimum rate for k-ary incidence coding with SHC(q) worker pool.
- Paper is well organized and well written.
- Lacks experiments or simulations to compare practical schemes (e.g., AMT) to the theoretical limits.
- Recommends shortening Sec. 2.4 and removing or shortening Sec. 3.1; adding proof sketches for theorems instead.
- Reviewer believes the approach is novel and practically useful for designers and pricing; did not check appendix proofs.
