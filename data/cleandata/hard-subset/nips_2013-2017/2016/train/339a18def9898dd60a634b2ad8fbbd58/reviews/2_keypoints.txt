- Paper models crowdsourcing as a joint source–channel coding (JSCC) problem with random channel state and derives asymptotic fundamental limits on queries per item.  
- Labels are modeled as an i.i.d. source, the taskmaster as encoder, workers as channel uses with random skill-level states, and the crowdsourcer as decoder.  
- Authors apply standard source–channel separation results to obtain Theorems 2 and 3 giving asymptotic query-rate limits.  
- The paper also gives a lower bound on the error probability of "k‑ary incidence coding" under a specific crowdsourcing/channel model.  
- Overall assessment: well-written and a promising first step toward JSCC modeling of crowdsourcing.  
- Major technical comment: the authors must justify or cite that source–channel separation holds when the channel has random state unknown to the encoder (and possibly known to the decoder).  
- Modeling concern: the encoder/decoder are assumed to only see labels and ignore original items as side information, which can be important and should be considered.  
- Clarification requested: wording in Sec.4.2/Fig.2 is misleading — “lower bounds improve as k and q grow” should state that the lower bound value decreases (implying potential performance gain), not that the bound itself is tightened without an upper bound for comparison.  
- Suggestion: include nonasymptotic (finite-blocklength) converses; cite Kostina & Verdú (2013) on finite-blocklength JSCC converses.
