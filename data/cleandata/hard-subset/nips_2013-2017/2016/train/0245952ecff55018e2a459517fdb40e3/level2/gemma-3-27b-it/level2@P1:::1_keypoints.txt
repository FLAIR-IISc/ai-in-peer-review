- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion to learn conditional distributions.  
- CMMD enables training via backpropagation and extends GMMNs to conditional modeling.  
- Theoretical foundation is strong, building on MMD and kernel embeddings with formal theorems.  
- Practical implementation details for efficient gradient computation are provided.  
- Empirical evaluation spans predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Results are competitive with state-of-the-art on tested datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Faces).  
- Paper clearly motivates conditional modeling and situates contributions within related work.  
- Network architectures used (MLP, CNN) are standard and lack architectural novelty tailored to CGMMN.  
- Paper omits detailed hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analysis (e.g., kernel bandwidth, kernel choice).  
- CMMD computation via kernel matrices can be computationally expensive; mini-batch approach is insufficiently detailed.  
- Lacks a direct, comprehensive empirical comparison to conditional GANs.  
- Assumptions required for CMMD are acknowledged but not thoroughly discussed for practical impact.  
- Reviewer recommends acceptance but requests revisions: hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, direct conditional GAN comparison, fuller description/complexity of mini-batch CMMD, and deeper discussion of CMMD assumptions.
