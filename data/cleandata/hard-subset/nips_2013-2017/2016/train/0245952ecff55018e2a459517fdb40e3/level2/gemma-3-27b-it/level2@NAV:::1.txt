## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) as an extension of existing Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional distribution setting. The core idea is to leverage the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to train a deep network that generates samples conditioned on input variables. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on three tasks: predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, achieving competitive performance in each.

The paper builds logically upon prior work in generative modeling, particularly GMMN and MMD, clearly articulating the motivation for extending the MMD approach to the conditional case. The CMMD formulation appears sound, and the authors appropriately address the computational considerations and provide a mini-batch training algorithm. The experimental evaluation is commendable in its breadth, encompassing various tasks and datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face. The results consistently demonstrate that CGMMN achieves performance comparable to state-of-the-art methods, particularly in predictive modeling and generation tasks. 

A strength of the paper is its clear presentation and thorough explanation of the theoretical underpinnings. The authors effectively connect the CMMD objective to Hilbert space embeddings and kernel methods, providing a solid foundation for the proposed approach.  The inclusion of ablation studies and comparisons with existing methods adds further credibility to the findings. However, the novelty feels incremental. While the extension to the conditional setting is valuable, the core mechanism—MMD matching—is well-established.  A deeper exploration of the limitations of CMMD, especially concerning high-dimensional conditional spaces or complex dependencies, would be beneficial. While the paper acknowledges limitations, more detailed analysis in this regard would strengthen the contribution. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Sound theoretical foundation and clear explanation.
*   Broad experimental validation demonstrating competitive performance.
*   Logical extension of existing MMD-based generative models.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Incremental novelty; the core MMD approach is not new.
*   Limited discussion of limitations, particularly regarding scalability to high-dimensional conditional spaces.
*   The presentation leans heavily on existing work, and the specific advantages of CGMMN over alternatives (e.g., conditional GANs or VAEs) are not fully highlighted.

**Overall:** This is a well-written and technically sound paper that presents a reasonable extension of existing work. While the novelty is moderate, the solid theoretical foundation, thorough experiments, and clear presentation make it a worthwhile contribution. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limitations and provide a more compelling argument for the benefits of CGMMN over alternative conditional generative models.
