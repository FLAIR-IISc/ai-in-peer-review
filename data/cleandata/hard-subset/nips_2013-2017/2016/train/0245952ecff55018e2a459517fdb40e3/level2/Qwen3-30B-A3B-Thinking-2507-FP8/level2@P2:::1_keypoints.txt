- Introduces CGMMN: extends GMMN to conditional distributions using a Conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Uses kernel mean embedding and MMD framework while retaining training via backpropagation.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on three domains: predictive modeling (MNIST/SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST/Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge (Boston housing).  
- Theoretical contribution: extends MMD to conditional distributions with Theorem 3 giving a sufficient condition for conditional distribution equality.  
- Generative process is well-motivated and consistent with prior deep generative model designs.  
- Experiments cover multiple tasks and include comparisons to relevant baselines, including CMMVA and various deep architectures.  
- Strength: clear theoretical development of CMMD and demonstrated versatility across application domains.  
- Strength: interesting application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Weakness: experimental details missing for reproducibility (kernel choices, regularization, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Weakness: superficial comparison with Conditional GANs and no discussion of key GAN-related advantages or failure modes (e.g., stability, mode collapse).  
- Weakness: weak theoretical justification for preferring CMMD over other conditional moment-matching approaches; claim of filling a “research void” is not substantiated.  
- Major concern: lack of meaningful, direct comparison with the most relevant prior work—Conditional GANs.  
- Bayesian dark knowledge results lack comparisons to alternative distillation methods and statistical significance testing.  
- Significance is reduced because the paper does not clearly demonstrate practical advantages over established conditional generative methods, especially Conditional GANs.  
- Recommended improvements: more thorough comparisons with Conditional GANs, visual sample comparisons, and analysis of training dynamics.  
- Writing is generally clear and organized, but some RKHS/kernel sections need more intuitive explanation and figure captions need more detail.  
- Overall: theoretically sound and promising empirically, but insufficient experimental rigor and comparisons limit the paper’s impact.
