- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a conditional deep generative model trained by minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD) between conditional mean embedding operators.  
- CMMD objective matches conditional distributions by minimizing the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between estimated conditional mean embedding operators from data and model.  
- Architecture: deep network taking conditioning variables and noise to output conditional samples; trained with backpropagation using mini-batch CMMD estimates.  
- Empirical evaluation covers predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces, and code-space via autoencoder), and Bayesian distillation (matching PBP teacher).  
- Strength: conceptually clean, principled extension of MMD to conditionals with a stable, non-adversarial training procedure.  
- Strength: practical mini-batch CMMD training recipe and observation that kernels on x need not be differentiable for optimization.  
- Strength: demonstrated applicability across prediction, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation tasks.  
- Strength: theoretically sound derivation grounded in RKHS/conditional mean embedding theory with clear sufficiency conditions.  
- Strength: compatible with different architectures (MLP, CNN) and learned code spaces to address high-dimensional outputs.  
- Weakness: conditional generation evaluation is largely qualitative with no quantitative metrics (e.g., Parzen, classifier scores) or comparisons to cGAN/cVAE.  
- Weakness: predictive performance lags state-of-the-art discriminative models and lacks evaluation of uncertainty quality (NLL, Brier score).  
- Weakness: limited ablation/sensitivity analysis on kernel choices, bandwidths, and regularization; selection and robustness unclear.  
- Weakness: computational costs under-characterized — CMMD has O(B^3) per minibatch due to matrix inversions, but runtime/memory not reported.  
- Weakness: Bayesian distillation shown on a single small dataset with only RMSE; calibration and NLL are not assessed.  
- Quality: technically sound and derivations are correct; claims are consistent, but empirical trade-offs need more characterization.  
- Clarity: overall well-written with helpful explanation of CMMD–MMD relation, but experiment details (kernels, bandwidths, discrete y handling, λ, batch size) need clarification.  
- Originality: novel and natural extension of GMMN to conditionals using operator matching, filling a gap between cGAN/cVAE and MMD-based models.  
- Significance: moderate — offers a stable alternative for conditional generators but requires stronger quantitative comparisons to increase impact.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled CMMD objective, simple/stable training, broad application scope, and competitive DGM predictive performance.  
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient quantitative generative/un________certainty evaluation, missing cGAN/cVAE comparisons, and inadequate kernel/computational analysis.  
- Essential suggestions: add quantitative conditional generation metrics and cGAN/cVAE comparisons; report uncertainty metrics (NLL, Brier, calibration); provide kernel/λ ablations and bandwidth selection procedures.  
- Optional suggestions: profile training time/memory and scale-up strategies (low-rank, random features, Nyström); test additional conditional tasks with quantitative metrics; analyze learned representation (autoencoder) effects systematically.  
- Overall: solid, well-motivated, and technically sound contribution that needs stronger quantitative experiments and analyses to be fully convincing.
