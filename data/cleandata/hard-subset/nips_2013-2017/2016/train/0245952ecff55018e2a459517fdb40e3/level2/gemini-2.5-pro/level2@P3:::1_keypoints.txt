- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to model p(y|x).  
- CMMD measures distance via the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional kernel mean embedding operators.  
- Generator is a deep network mapping x and noise h to y, trained by backprop to minimize CMMD, avoiding adversarial min-max training.  
- Evaluated on prediction (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian knowledge distillation; uses an autoencoder to improve sample quality.  

Strengths:
- Principled and significant: logical extension of moment-matching to conditional models, grounded in kernel embedding theory.  
- Clear, well-organized presentation with helpful exposition of MMD→CMMD connection.  
- Versatile experiments demonstrating utility across prediction, generation, and Bayesian distillation.  
- Stable training objective that sidesteps GAN-style instability.  

Weaknesses / Suggestions:
- Computational cost: CMMD requires kernel matrix inversion (O(B^3)), raising scalability concerns that aren’t discussed.  
- Missing direct quantitative comparisons to contemporary conditional DGMs (e.g., cGANs, CVAEs) for generation tasks.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choice, bandwidth, regularization λ) and selection procedure are not addressed.  
- Base generative samples are somewhat noisy; could explore better architectures (deconvolutions) or kernels to improve quality.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel, well-founded extension for conditional density estimation.  
- CMMD yields a principled, more stable alternative to adversarial training.  
- Supported by diverse, convincing experiments.  
- Clearly written and a solid contribution to deep generative models.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Unaddressed computational scalability of the CMMD objective.  
- Lack of direct quantitative comparison with contemporaneous conditional generative models.  

Overall:
- Strengths outweigh weaknesses; CGMMN is a valuable contribution, with suggested improvements to address scalability, comparisons, and hyperparameter discussion.
