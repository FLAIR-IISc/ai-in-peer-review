This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to conditional distributions via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective defined as a Hilbert–Schmidt distance between conditional mean embedding operators. The model samples auxiliary noise and concatenates it with condition x, passing through a DNN to generate y; training minimizes CMMD using backprop. Experiments span predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST digits, Yale Faces), and “Bayesian dark knowledge” distillation (Boston Housing), showing competitive classification accuracy, plausible conditional samples, and parity with a Bayesian teacher’s RMSE. The work builds on kernel mean embeddings (Gretton et al.; Song et al.), GMMN (Li et al.; Dziugaite et al.) and relates to cGAN (Mirza & Osindero) and CVAE (Sohn et al.).

Quality: The objective is well-motivated and mathematically consistent under standard universality and smoothness assumptions; the empirical CMMD estimator is implementable with minibatches. Experimental support is mixed but generally positive: CGMMN-CNN is competitive with max-margin DGMs on MNIST/SVHN; generation is demonstrated with nearest-neighbor checks; distillation preserves RMSE. However, statistical significance, calibration, and robustness analyses are missing; scalability of CMMD (cubic in batch) is not empirically characterized; sensitivity to kernel choices, λ, and minibatch approximations is underexplored.

Clarity: The paper is mostly clear, with helpful preliminaries and an algorithm summary. Reproducibility suffers from missing details (kernel types/bandwidth selection, λ, perturbation magnitude for distillation, exact architectures/hyperparameters), and there is no public code.

Originality: Using an operator-norm CMMD to train conditional deep generators is a natural but nontrivial extension of MMD/GMMN and conditional embeddings. Novelty is incremental relative to prior conditional GAN/CVAE work, but the non-adversarial formulation and Bayesian distillation application are valuable.

Significance: The approach is practically useful when adversarial training is unstable, offering a simple, backprop-only conditional generator. Demonstrations are on modest datasets; broader impact would be clearer with stronger baselines and larger-scale studies.

Pros:
- Conceptually clean, stable objective; solid connection to conditional embeddings
- Simple architecture; broad applicability (prediction, generation, distillation)
- Competitive results; interesting application to Bayesian dark knowledge

Cons:
- Limited novelty relative to cGAN/CVAE; missing head-to-head comparisons
- Scalability and kernel sensitivity not analyzed; incomplete reproducibility details
- Generation evaluation largely qualitative; no calibration or likelihood metrics

Suggestions: Compare against cGAN/CVAE/MDNs; report ECE/NLL and runtime/memory; ablate kernels, λ, batch size, and noise dimension; consider RFF/Nyström for scalability; explore learned/adaptive kernels and conditioning mechanisms; release code and full hyperparameters. Overall, a sound and useful contribution; with stronger empirical rigor, it would be a solid accept.
