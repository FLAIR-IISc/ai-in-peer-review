- Presents a novel approach (Conditional GMMN) for learning conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Extends generative moment-matching networks to conditional settings with a deep generative architecture trained by SGD/backprop.  
- CMMD measures the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between kernel mean embeddings of empirical conditional distribution and the model.  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Strength: Proposes a novel CMMD-based method for conditional distribution learning.  
- Strength: Clear theoretical explanation and derivation of the approach.  
- Strength: Empirical results show practical utility across multiple tasks.  
- Weakness: Relies on smoothness assumptions (CY|X ∈ F ⊗ G) for CMMD to be well-defined without practical guidance to ensure them.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of computational complexity for large-scale applications.  
- Weakness: Does not compare against other state-of-the-art conditional distribution learning methods.  
- Overall: Well-written and technically sound but needs clarification on assumptions and complexity analysis.
