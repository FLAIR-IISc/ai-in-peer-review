**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a new framework called Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) for learning conditional distributions. The framework is based on the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) criterion, which is used to measure the difference between the kernel mean embeddings of two distributions. The authors extend the MMD criterion to conditional distributions, called Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), and use it as the training objective for CGMMN. The network architecture consists of a deep neural network that takes both the given conditional variables and an extra set of random variables as inputs, and then passes through a nonlinear transformation to produce the samples of the target variables. The authors evaluate CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.
