

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to handle conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors present a theoretically grounded framework for learning conditional distributions through back-propagation, with applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

The paper makes several strong contributions. The theoretical development of CMMD is sound and builds appropriately on prior work in kernel embedding and GMMN. The experimental evaluation across diverse tasks (MNIST/SVHN classification, MNIST/Yale face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation) demonstrates competitive performance against relevant baselines. The architecture is simple and well-motivated, with the conditional generative process being particularly elegant. The authors correctly acknowledge related work, including conditional GANs and conditional VAEs, and position their contribution as filling a gap in the moment-matching network literature.

The main strengths include a clear theoretical foundation, practical implementation details, and comprehensive experimental validation across multiple domains. The paper effectively demonstrates how CGMMN can be applied to both predictive modeling (where it achieves results comparable to state-of-the-art DGMs like CMMVA) and generation tasks (producing class-conditioned samples that resemble target classes without simply copying training data).

However, there are notable limitations. The performance on SVHN (Table 2) shows CGMMN-CNN trailing behind discriminative models like DSN, which the paper acknowledges but doesn't thoroughly explain. The paper also lacks statistical significance testing for the reported results, which would strengthen the experimental claims. While the authors mention limitations, they could have more deeply discussed potential failure modes of the CMMD criterion, particularly for complex conditional distributions.

The paper is well-written and technically sound, making it a valuable contribution to the generative modeling literature. The work is sufficiently novel (extending GMMN to conditional distributions) and significant (providing a simple, back-propagation-friendly approach for conditional generation). The theoretical development is solid, and the experiments provide adequate support for the claims, though additional statistical validation would strengthen the empirical results.

Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a meaningful contribution to the field. With minor improvements to the experimental validation and deeper discussion of limitations, it would be an excellent fit for NIPS. I recommend acceptance.
