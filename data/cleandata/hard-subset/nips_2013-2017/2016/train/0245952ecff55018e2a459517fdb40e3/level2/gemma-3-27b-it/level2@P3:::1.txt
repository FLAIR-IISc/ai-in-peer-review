This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) as an extension of GMMNs to learn conditional distributions. The core idea is to adapt the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) criterion to a conditional setting (CMMD), enabling the training of deep generative models for tasks like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The approach leverages back-propagation for efficient gradient calculation.

The paper is well-structured and clearly presents the motivation for CGMMNs. The connection to existing work in generative modeling (GANs, GMMNs) and the use of MMD as a foundation are effectively highlighted. The mathematical formulation of CMMD is presented rigorously, building upon the concepts of kernel embeddings and Hilbert spaces. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is a particularly interesting and novel use case. The experimental evaluation is extensive, covering a range of tasks and datasets, demonstrating competitive performance compared to existing methods.

A strength of this work lies in the simplicity of the proposed method, maintaining the relatively straightforward training process of GMMNs while extending its capabilities to conditional generation. The derivation of the CMMD criterion appears sound, and the connection to existing kernel methods is well-established. The extensive experimental section adds credibility to the claims. The inclusion of results on both MNIST and SVHN for predictive modeling and the demonstration of dark knowledge distillation are commendable.

However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the computational complexity of the CMMD calculation, especially in high-dimensional spaces. While the paper mentions a mini-batch version of the algorithm, a more detailed analysis of the scaling behavior with respect to the size of the datasets and the dimensionality of the feature space would be valuable. A comparison to other conditional generative models like Conditional GANs (CGANs) or Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) beyond simply mentioning them in the introduction would strengthen the results. A quantitative analysis of the quality of generated samples (e.g., using Inception Score or Fréchet Inception Distance for image generation) would be beneficial, especially in the context of the experiments on MNIST and Yale Face dataset. The choice of hyperparameters, such as the kernel bandwidth (λ), and its impact on the performance of the CGMMN could be discussed in more detail. While the paper claims the CMMD objective is well-defined under certain assumptions, a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential issues with those assumptions, and how the empirical estimator addresses those, would be beneficial.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of GMMNs to conditional distributions.
*   Sound theoretical foundation based on kernel methods and MMD.
*   Comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating competitive performance.
*   Interesting application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
*   Relatively simple training procedure via back-propagation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of detailed computational complexity analysis.
*   Limited comparison to other conditional generative models.
*   Absence of quantitative evaluation metrics for sample quality.
*   Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.
*   Assumptions for CMMD validity could be explained more clearly.

**Overall:**

This is a solid contribution to the field of generative modeling. The paper introduces a novel and theoretically sound approach to conditional generation, and the experimental results demonstrate its effectiveness. While some minor improvements could be made, the strengths of the work outweigh the weaknesses. This reviewer recommends acceptance with minor revisions to address the points raised above, specifically a discussion of computational complexity, a more comprehensive comparison to existing conditional generative models and a quantitative analysis of sample quality.
