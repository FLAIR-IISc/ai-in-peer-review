- Main contribution: Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to learn p(y|x) via kernel mean embeddings and deep nets mapping (x,h) → y.

- Strength — Originality & significance: Elegant and principled extension of MMD to conditional distributions, filling a gap among conditional deep generative models.

- Strength — Clarity & organization: Well-written, clear motivation and derivation; Remark 1 gives an intuitive weighted-MMD interpretation of CMMD.

- Strength — Broad applicability: Demonstrates versatility across prediction (MNIST, SVHN), label-conditioned image generation, and Bayesian model compression.

- Strength — Training stability: Direct discrepancy minimization may yield more stable training than adversarial min-max methods (e.g., conditional GANs).

- Weakness — Computational complexity: CMMD requires forming/inverting B×B kernel Gram matrices (O(B^3)), raising scalability concerns for large mini-batches; no discussion of approximations (Nyström, RFF) or practical limits.

- Weakness — Lack of quantitative generative evaluation: Contextual generation results are qualitative only; no quantitative comparisons (e.g., likelihoods or scores) versus conditional GANs/VAEs.

- Weakness — Hyperparameter selection unclear: Missing details on kernel choices and bandwidths for x and y and sensitivity analyses, hindering reproducibility.

- Weakness — Two-stage generation with autoencoder: Using an autoencoder code space makes generation two-stage; paper should discuss why base CGMMN yields noisy samples and whether CMMD or architecture choices could enable end-to-end high-quality sampling.

- Overall assessment: Strong, original, and well-executed work with theoretical grounding and broad experiments, tempered by practical concerns about computational cost and evaluation detail.
