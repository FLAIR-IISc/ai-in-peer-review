The paper "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks" presents a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors extend the capability of generative moment-matching networks (GMMN) to address various application problems, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The main idea of the paper is to learn a conditional distribution given some input variables based on the CMMD criterion, which measures the Hilbert-Schmidt norm between the kernel mean embedding of an empirical conditional distribution and that of the generative model. The authors propose a deep generative architecture and a learning algorithm that uses stochastic gradient descent with the gradient calculated by back-propagation.
