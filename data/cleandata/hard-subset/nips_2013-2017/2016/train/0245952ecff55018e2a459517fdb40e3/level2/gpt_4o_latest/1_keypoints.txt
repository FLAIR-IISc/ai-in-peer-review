- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional distributions using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.
- CMMD is theoretically grounded with clear connections to kernel mean embeddings and Hilbert space theory.
- Derivation of CMMD and implementation details are rigorous and reproducible.
- Evaluated on diverse tasks (predictive modeling, generative tasks, Bayesian dark knowledge) showing competitive performance.
- Paper is well-organized and clear, with useful visualizations and quantitative comparisons.
- Training is simple and practical, relying on backpropagation, making CGMMN accessible to practitioners.
- Lacks direct comparisons with strong conditional baselines like CVAEs and cGANs.
- Generated samples show visual artifacts and noise; reliance on autoencoders to improve quality is insufficiently explored.
- Scalability concerns due to cubic cost of kernel Gram matrix computations; mini-batch solution not convincingly demonstrated on large-scale data.
- Limited discussion of limitations such as sensitivity to kernel choice and challenges in high-dimensional conditional spaces.
- Suggest adding comparisons with CVAEs and cGANs for a more comprehensive evaluation.
- Suggest exploring architectural or regularization methods to improve generative quality without autoencoders.
- Suggest providing detailed computational analysis of the mini-batch CMMD algorithm on large datasets.
- Recommend explicitly discussing limitations and future directions (e.g., kernel selection, scalability improvements).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address comparative analysis and scalability concerns.
