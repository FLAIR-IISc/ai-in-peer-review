- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to model conditional distributions.  
- Provides a theoretically grounded CMMD framework building on kernel embedding and GMMN.  
- Learns conditional distributions via back-propagation, enabling practical training.  
- Applies CGMMN to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Experimental evaluation across MNIST/SVHN classification, MNIST/Yale generation, and knowledge distillation shows competitive performance versus baselines.  
- Architecture is simple, well-motivated, and features an elegant conditional generative process.  
- Correctly positions contribution relative to conditional GANs and VAEs, filling a gap in moment-matching networks.  
- Strengths: clear theory, practical implementation details, and comprehensive multi-domain experiments.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN effectiveness for both predictive tasks (comparable to state-of-the-art DGMs) and class-conditioned generation without memorization.  
- Limitation: CGMMN-CNN underperforms on SVHN compared to discriminative models like DSN, with insufficient analysis.  
- Experimental reporting lacks statistical significance testing to bolster empirical claims.  
- Would benefit from deeper discussion of CMMD failure modes for complex conditional distributions; overall recommendation: accept with minor experimental and discussion improvements.
