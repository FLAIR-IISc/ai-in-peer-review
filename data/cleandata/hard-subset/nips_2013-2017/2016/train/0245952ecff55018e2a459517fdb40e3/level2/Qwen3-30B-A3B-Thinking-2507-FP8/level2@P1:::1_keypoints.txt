- Introduces CGMMN, an extension of GMMN for conditional distributions using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) grounded in kernel mean embeddings.  
- The CMMD criterion is rigorously derived with clear theoretical justification for conditional settings.  
- Evaluations are comprehensive across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical results are competitive across datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, Boston housing), with strong performance in Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, and provides thorough mathematical formulations and illustrative figures.  
- Effectively situates the method relative to related work (conditional GANs, conditional VAEs).  
- Practical advantage: simpler training via backpropagation without min–max optimization required by cGANs.  
- Limited experimental comparison with conditional GANs; lacks direct metrics on sample quality, stability, and efficiency.  
- Claims about CMMD assumptions possibly failing are not supported by empirical sensitivity analysis.  
- Hyperparameter discussion (notably regularization λ) is sparse and lacks sensitivity/selection guidance.  
- Ablation on hidden variable dimensionality is insufficient and not systematically explored for performance or training time.  
- Suggestion to include quantitative image-quality comparisons (e.g., FID) and direct cGAN baselines.  
- Suggestion to analyze computational complexity, especially matrix inversion costs and mini-batch scalability.  
- Suggestion to provide thorough hyperparameter sensitivity (λ) and compare CGMMN with/without autoencoder quantitatively.  
- Overall verdict: significant, well-founded contribution to conditional generative modeling with practical advantages; recommend acceptance despite minor empirical and analysis gaps.
