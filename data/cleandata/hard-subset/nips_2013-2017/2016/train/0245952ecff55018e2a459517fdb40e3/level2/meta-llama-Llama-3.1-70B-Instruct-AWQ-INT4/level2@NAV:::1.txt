This paper presents a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using a generative moment-matching network (CGMMN). The authors extend the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion to conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) and propose a deep generative architecture that can learn conditional distributions by minimizing the CMMD objective. The paper provides a thorough theoretical foundation, including a clear explanation of the CMMD criterion and its relationship to MMD.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The experimental results show that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art methods in these tasks. The paper also provides a detailed analysis of the results, including visualizations of generated samples and comparisons with other methods.

Strengths of the paper:

* The paper presents a novel and well-motivated approach to learning conditional distributions.
* The theoretical foundation is thorough and well-explained.
* The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on various tasks.
* The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the CMMD criterion and its relationship to MMD.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The paper assumes that the reader is familiar with the concept of MMD and its application to generative models. A brief review of MMD and its relevance to the paper's contributions would be helpful.
* Some of the experimental results, such as the comparison with other methods, could be presented in a more detailed and systematic way.
* The paper could benefit from a more in-depth analysis of the limitations and potential applications of CGMMN.

Originality and significance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using a generative moment-matching network.
* The CMMD criterion is a significant contribution to the field of generative models and has the potential to be widely adopted.
