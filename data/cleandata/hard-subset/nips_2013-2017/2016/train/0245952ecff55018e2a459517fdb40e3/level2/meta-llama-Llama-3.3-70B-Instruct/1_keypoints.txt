- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to learn conditional distributions.
- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to tasks like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.
- Main claims: CGMMN can learn flexible conditional distributions; CMMD effectively measures differences between conditional distributions.
- Claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments demonstrating competitive performance.
- Paper is well-written with clear background, organized technical sections, and consistent notation.
- Strength: CMMD is a novel and effective metric for comparing conditional distributions.
- Strength: CGMMN framework is flexible and broadly applicable across tasks.
- Strength: Experimental results are thorough and competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
- Weakness: Relies on assumption that conditional mean embedding operator CY|X ∈ F ⊗ G, which may not hold in practice (authors provide an approximation).
- Weakness: Computational cost of kernel Gram matrix grows cubically with sample size, limiting scalability.
- Overall assessment: Significant contribution to deep generative models and conditional distribution learning; recommendation to accept.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, empirical performance, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical assumption concerns and scalability limitations.
