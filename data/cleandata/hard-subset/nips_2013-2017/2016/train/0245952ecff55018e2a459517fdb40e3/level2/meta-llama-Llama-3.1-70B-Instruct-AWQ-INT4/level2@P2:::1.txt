This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a deep generative model that learns a conditional distribution given some input variables. The model is trained using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, which is an extension of the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion used in Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed model and its training objective. The use of CMMD as a training objective is a significant contribution, as it allows the model to learn a conditional distribution that is robust to changes in the input variables. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the model's performance on various tasks, including a comparison with state-of-the-art models.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a unified framework for various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The authors demonstrate that CGMMN can be used for a wide range of applications, from image classification to image generation.

The experimental results are also impressive, with CGMMN achieving competitive performance on various tasks. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the model's performance, including a comparison with state-of-the-art models. The results demonstrate that CGMMN is a robust and effective model for learning conditional distributions.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of a clear explanation of the relationship between CGMMN and other deep generative models, such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs). While the authors provide a brief discussion of the differences between CGMMN and GMMN, a more detailed analysis of the relationship between CGMMN and other deep generative models would be helpful.

Another potential weakness is the limited analysis of the model's limitations. While the authors provide a detailed analysis of the model's performance, they do not provide a clear explanation of the model's limitations, such as its ability to handle high-dimensional data or its robustness to outliers.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed model and its training objective. The experimental results are also impressive, with CGMMN achieving competitive performance on various tasks. However, a more detailed analysis of the relationship between CGMMN and other deep generative models, as well as a clear explanation of the model's limitations, would be helpful.
