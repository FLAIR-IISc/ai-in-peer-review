- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to learn p(y|x).
- CMMD measures distance between conditional distributions by comparing conditional embedding operators in an RKHS, extending MMD theory.
- Generative model: deep network takes conditioning x and noise h and outputs y, trained by SGD to minimize CMMD.
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings and MMD literature, offering a non-adversarial alternative to cGANs and cVAEs with potentially more stable training.
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks: classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Strength: novel, theoretically well-grounded CMMD objective with clear motivation and derivation.
- Strength: well-written, organized presentation with intuitive connection between MMD and CMMD.
- Weakness: CMMD requires inversion of N×N kernel matrices (O(N^3)), raising scalability and minibatch-size trade-off concerns.
- Weakness: no direct experimental comparison to conditional GANs—missing quantitative/qualitative and stability comparisons.
- Weakness: missing experimental details for reproducibility (choice of kernel functions, bandwidths, and kernel definition over output y).
- Weakness: generated MNIST samples are noisier and less sharp than GANs; two-stage autoencoder fix adds complexity and reliance on another model.
- Weakness: unclear handling of discrete conditioning (per-class estimation vs. shared-statistics motivation) — needs clarification.
- Overall: strong, original contribution with high significance; recommended for acceptance despite practical and experimental gaps.
