- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional MMD (CMMD) to train conditional generators by matching conditional mean embeddings in RKHS.  
- Represents conditional distributions via conditional mean operators CY|X and trains y = f(x,h;w) to minimize ∥ĈdY|X − ĈsY|X∥^2 (Hilbert–Schmidt norm).  
- Derives a practical CMMD estimator using Gram matrices (Eq. 2) enabling minibatch stochastic training with backpropagation.  
- Evaluates CGMMN on prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston housing), showing competitive results.  
- Builds on prior work: extends GMMN (joint MMD) and leverages conditional mean embeddings (Song et al., Grunewalder et al.), contrasting with cGANs and cVAEs by avoiding adversarial games and variational bounds.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a sufficient condition (Theorem 3) under which equality of conditional mean operators implies equality of conditional distributions.  
- CMMD estimator is clearly stated and computable; regularization λ and assumptions are discussed.  
- Algorithmically straightforward: minibatch training and backprop through generator are simple; K̃−1 depends only on X so X-kernels need not be differentiable.  
- Empirically competitive for classification among generative approaches; qualitative conditional generation and successful distillation without RMSE degradation.  
- Paper is generally clear with useful links between MMD and CMMD, but some technical details are under-specified.  
- Missing clarity/details on kernel choices, bandwidth selection for L/K, computational complexity/memory, and sampling protocol for x in CMMD estimation.  
- Originality: novel conditional extension of GMMN combining conditional mean embeddings with backprop-based generators.  
- Significance: provides a simple, stable, non-adversarial objective broadly applicable to prediction, generation, and distillation.  
- Strengths: conceptually clean RKHS-grounded objective, simple training, broad applicability, competitive performance, and insightful link between CMMD and MMD.  
- Major empirical weakness: lacks direct baseline comparisons to conditional VAEs and conditional GANs on generation tasks.  
- Quantitative generation metrics are missing (e.g., NLL, Parzen estimates, modern image metrics); evaluation is primarily qualitative.  
- Scalability concern: CMMD requires inverting K̃ per batch (O(b^3)), with limited discussion and no exploration of kernel approximations or blockwise strategies.  
- Sensitivity concern: paper does not report kernel choice/selection procedures or sensitivity analyses for kernels and λ.  
- Predictive use could be improved: currently uses single-sample argmax; should evaluate averaging, NLL, and calibration metrics.  
- Theoretical assumptions (existence of CY|X, smoothness) are standard but not empirically validated; finite-sample properties of the operator estimator are not analyzed.  
- Concrete suggestions: add comparisons to cGAN/cVAE, report runtime/memory/batch sizes and K̃−1 handling, explore kernel approximations and batch-size ablations.  
- Additional suggested analyses: detail kernel choices and bandwidth selection, sensitivity to λ, evaluate multiple-sample prediction and calibration, clarify x-sampling protocol for CMMD, and expand discrete-X treatment.  
- Recommend adding more distillation baselines and tests on additional datasets to assess generality.  
- Pros for acceptance: novel, well-motivated, easy-to-implement objective with broad applicability and a compelling distillation use case.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing strong conditional generative baselines, limited quantitative generation evaluation, and modest predictive gains over discriminative methods.  
- Overall assessment: solid, well-motivated extension of GMMN to conditional modeling; reviewer leans positive but requests stronger empirical baselines and deeper analysis of computational and kernel/hyperparameter aspects.
