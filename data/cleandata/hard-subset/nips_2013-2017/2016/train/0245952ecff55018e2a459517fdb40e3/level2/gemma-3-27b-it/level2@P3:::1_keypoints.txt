- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions via a CMMD criterion.  
- Adapts Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) to a conditional setting using kernel embeddings and Hilbert-space theory.  
- Uses back-propagation to compute gradients for efficient training of deep conditional generative models.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly motivates the CGMMN approach.  
- Mathematical formulation of CMMD is rigorous and connects coherently to existing kernel methods.  
- Novel and interesting application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Experimental evaluation is extensive and covers tasks/datasets like predictive modeling, MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance and supports claims with broad experiments.  
- Method maintains GMMN’s relative training simplicity while enabling conditional generation.  
- CMMD derivation appears sound and theoretically grounded.  
- Lacks detailed computational complexity analysis and scaling behavior for high-dimensional data despite a mini-batch mention.  
- Limited empirical comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., CGANs, VAEs).  
- Missing quantitative image-sample quality metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID) in experiments.  
- Insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, notably kernel bandwidth selection.  
- Assumptions underlying CMMD validity need clearer explanation and discussion of potential limitations and empirical estimator behavior.  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing complexity analysis, broader comparisons, and quantitative sample-quality evaluation.
