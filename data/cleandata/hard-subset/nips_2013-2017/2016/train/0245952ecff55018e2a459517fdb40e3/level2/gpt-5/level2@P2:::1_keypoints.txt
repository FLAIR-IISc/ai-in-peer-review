- Introduces CGMMN: deep conditional generator trained by minimizing CMMD between empirical conditional distributions and model.  
- CMMD is the squared HS-norm between conditional mean embedding operators, optimized via back-propagation using minibatch CMMD.  
- Model conditions on x and noise h concatenated, passed through a deep network to sample y ~ pθ(y|x).  
- Experiments: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston Housing).  
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings and MMD-based generative models; analogous to cGAN/CVAE but uses a single-sided, stable objective.  
- Main novelty: leveraging conditional mean embedding operator as a learning target for deep conditional generators.  
- Missing empirical comparisons to strong conditional baselines (cGAN, CVAE) on generation tasks.  
- Conceptually clean extension of GMMNs to conditional modeling with a principled CMMD objective.  
- Practical minibatch training; inverses of Gram matrices w.r.t. x are independent of θ, simplifying gradients.  
- Versatile applications demonstrated across prediction, generation, and distillation.  
- Produces competitive classification for a generative method and visually plausible conditional samples on MNIST/Yale.  
- Distillation experiment compelling: matches PBP RMSE on Boston with a compact student.  
- Kernel choices and hyperparameters (kernels on X and Y, bandwidths, λ) are underspecified and sensitivity is unclear.  
- Computational/memory complexity concern: per-minibatch matrix inversions are O(m^3); no wall-clock or scaling comparisons to GAN/CVAE.  
- Lacks finite-sample analysis and discussion of estimator bias/variance, effect of λ, and minibatch estimation error on stability.  
- Classification setup unclear: sampling-based argmax, no analysis of stochasticity effects, calibration, or discriminative baselines.  
- Distillation evaluation limited to one small dataset with no uncertainty metrics (NLL, calibration) reported.  
- Methodologically sound and derivations consistent, but several empirical choices lack detail and experimental evaluation is incomplete.  
- Paper is generally clear with helpful background, but contains notation typos and needs explicit training/kernel details and gradient expressions.  
- Using CMMD to train deep conditional generators appears novel and is a meaningful, non-adversarial alternative to cGANs.  
- Potentially significant as a stable conditional generative objective, but impact is moderate pending competitive validation at scale.  
- Suggestion: add quantitative conditional generation metrics (FID/IS, retrieval, precision/recall) and cGAN/CVAE comparisons.  
- Suggestion: specify kernels, bandwidth selection, λ, conduct sensitivity analyses, and consider learned/multiple kernels.  
- Suggestion: report computational costs and discuss ways to reduce O(m^3) (low-rank, random features, conjugate gradients).  
- Suggestion: analyze minibatch-size effects and consider unbiased/linear-time CMMD estimators.  
- Suggestion: include cross-entropy discriminative baseline, calibration (ECE/NLL), and analyze effect of multiple y samples for classification.  
- Suggestion: expand distillation experiments to more datasets and uncertainty metrics; assess multimodal posterior capture.  
- Minor fixes: correct typos (e.g., “MINIST”, “convoluational”), clarify R10+ and x perturbation, add nearest-neighbor/diversity analyses.  
- Recommendation: leaning borderline accept; strengthen empirical comparisons, kernel specification, and scalability analyses to improve acceptability.  
- Pros: simple, principled, stable objective; broad applicability; competitive non-adversarial results.  
- Cons: missing strong baselines/quantitative metrics, limited scalability/sensitivity analysis, under-specified kernel/hyperparameter choices.
