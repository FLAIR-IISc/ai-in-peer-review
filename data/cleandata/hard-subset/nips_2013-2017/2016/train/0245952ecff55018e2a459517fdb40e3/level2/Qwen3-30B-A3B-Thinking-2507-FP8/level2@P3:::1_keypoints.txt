- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) for modeling conditional distributions.
- CMMD measures differences between kernel mean embeddings of empirical conditional distributions and the generative model.
- Framework builds on kernel mean embedding techniques and trains efficiently via backpropagation, offering a simpler alternative to conditional GANs.
- Theoretical contribution is well-motivated with clear definitions and theorems extending MMD to conditional distributions.
- Properly contextualizes related work (GMMN, conditional GANs, CVAEs, conditional SPNs) and fills a gap in moment-matching conditional modeling.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- CGMMN-CNN achieves competitive MNIST and SVHN results without data augmentation or ensembling.
- Contextual generation shows diverse class-conditional samples and evidence they are not training copies.
- Bayesian dark knowledge application effectively distills Bayesian models (Boston housing) with minimal performance loss.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient algorithmic and experimental details for reproducibility.
- Minor weakness: computational complexity of CMMD (cubic kernel matrix inversion) is not thoroughly analyzed despite a mini-batch workaround.
- Could better address trade-offs between computational efficiency and kernel approximation quality.
- Generative samples can be noisy; authors suggest integrating autoencoders to improve visual quality.
- Significance and novelty are strong: a principled, simple extension of GMMN to conditional settings with practical advantages; recommendation to accept.
