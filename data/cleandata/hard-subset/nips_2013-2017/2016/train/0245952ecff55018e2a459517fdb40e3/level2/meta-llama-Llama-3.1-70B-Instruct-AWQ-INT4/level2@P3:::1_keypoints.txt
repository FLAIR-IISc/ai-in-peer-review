- Summary: Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), using Conditional MMD (CMMD) to train deep nets that generate conditional samples by matching kernel mean embeddings.

- Strength — Flexibility: CGMMN is applicable to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

- Strength — Simplicity: Training is straightforward and performed via backpropagation.

- Strength — Competitive Performance: Empirically competitive on evaluated predictive and contextual generation tasks.

- Strength — Theoretical Foundations: Provides solid theory, extending MMD to conditional distributions.

- Weakness — Assumptions: Requires the conditional mean embedding operator to be Hilbert–Schmidt, which may not hold in practice.

- Weakness — Computational Cost: Kernel Gram matrix computation scales poorly (cubic in sample size), causing high cost.

- Weakness — Limited Evaluation: Experiments cover only a limited set of tasks and datasets.

- Originality — Extension of MMD: Novel extension of MMD to conditional distributions (CMMD).

- Originality — CGMMN Framework: New hybrid approach combining deep nets with kernel methods to learn conditional distributions.

- Significance — Improved Discrimination: Can enhance discriminative ability of deep generative models by incorporating conditional information.

- Significance — Relaxed Assumptions: Potentially relaxes unnecessary assumptions about conditional variables, improving performance.

- Significance — Bayesian Dark Knowledge: Offers a new method for distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

- Clarity — Organization: Paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

- Clarity — Explanations: Technical concepts and the CGMMN framework are clearly explained.

- Clarity — References: Adequate citation of related work and theoretical foundations.

- Quality — Technical Soundness: Theoretical development is sound and well-supported.

- Quality — Experimental Evaluation: Provides thorough experimental evaluation across multiple tasks/datasets (though limited in variety).

- Quality — Completeness: Presents a complete, self-contained framework for learning conditional distributions.

- Pro Acceptance: Novel contribution with solid theory, practical training, and competitive empirical results.

- Con Acceptance: Limited empirical scope, strong operator assumptions, and high computational cost of kernel operations.

- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance, noting novelty and soundness but suggesting broader evaluation would strengthen the paper.
