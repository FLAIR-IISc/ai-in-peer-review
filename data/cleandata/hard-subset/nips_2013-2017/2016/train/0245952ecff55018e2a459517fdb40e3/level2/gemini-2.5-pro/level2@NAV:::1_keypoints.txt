- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions p(y|x).  
- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a novel training objective based on kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- CMMD measures the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between true and model conditional embedding operators.  
- Trains the model by minimizing the CMMD loss via back-propagation, extending GMMN to conditional settings.  
- Offers an alternative to Conditional GANs and VAEs aiming for simpler, more stable training than adversarial min–max.  
- The CMMD objective is theoretically well-founded and elegant for conditional density estimation.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-structured, and provides sufficient background.  
- Demonstrates versatility across tasks: predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.  
- Experimental results are solid and competitive on benchmarks like MNIST and SVHN.  
- Does not consistently outperform top methods (falls behind discriminative models like DSN in prediction).  
- Generated images (without autoencoder) can be noisy and lack direct quantitative/qualitative comparison to strong baselines like Conditional GANs.  
- CMMD has high computational cost—scales cubically with mini-batch size due to kernel matrix inversion—and needs a deeper discussion on scalability and mini-batch approximations.  
- Overall assessment: novel, significant, and high-quality contribution whose originality outweighs limitations in performance and scalability.
