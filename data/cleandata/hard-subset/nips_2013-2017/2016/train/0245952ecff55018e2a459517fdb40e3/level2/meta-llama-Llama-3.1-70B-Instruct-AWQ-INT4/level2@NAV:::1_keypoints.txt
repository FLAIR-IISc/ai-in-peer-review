- Paper introduces CGMMN, a deep generative architecture for learning conditional distributions by minimizing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) objective.
- Authors extend MMD to CMMD and provide a thorough theoretical foundation and clear explanation of the CMMD criterion and its relation to MMD.
- Experimental evaluation covers predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge, showing CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
- Results include visualizations and comparisons with other methods, demonstrating empirical effectiveness across tasks.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach to learning conditional distributions.
- Strength: rigorous and well-explained theoretical development of CMMD.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental results and clear presentation of CMMD.
- Weakness: paper assumes readers are familiar with MMD; a brief review would help accessibility.
- Weakness: comparisons with other methods could be more detailed and systematic.
- Weakness: lacking deeper analysis of limitations and potential applications of CGMMN.
- Originality/significance: CMMD is a significant contribution with potential for wide adoption.
- Writing/presentation: paper is well-written and easy to follow but would benefit from more systematic comparisons.
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestions to add a brief MMD review, more detailed comparisons, and a more in-depth limitations/applications analysis.
