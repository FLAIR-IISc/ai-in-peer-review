- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional MMD (CMMD) via kernel conditional mean embeddings to train conditional generators.
- Embeds conditional distributions as operators CY|X in a tensor RKHS and measures discrepancy by the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical operators.
- Learns a generator y = f(x,h;w) by minimizing CMMD between data and generated conditionals; implements CNN/MLP variants and minibatch training.
- Builds on prior work: GMMN, kernel mean embeddings, and classical empirical conditional operator estimator Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤.
- Replaces GAN-style min–max with a single CMMD objective, inheriting GMMN’s training stability advantages.
- Positions relative to CVAE/cGAN and conditional SPNs but lacks direct empirical comparisons to cGAN/CVAE on generation tasks.
- Strength: clear, principled extension of MMD to conditional distributions via operator embeddings.
- Strength: simple, stable training with backprop on a single objective and kernel inverses independent of generator parameters.
- Strength: versatile applications demonstrated for classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.
- Insight: clarifies relation between MMD and CMMD as uniform vs x-weighted discrepancies and provides practical generator recipes (noise concatenation/corruption).
- Weakness: novelty is incremental since CMMD follows from standard conditional mean embedding theory; prior conditional-MMD work should be cited.
- Weakness: limited theoretical guarantees—no generalization analysis linking empirical CMMD minimization to convergence of pθ(y|x) or bias analysis from λ/finite samples.
- Weakness: scalability concerns—batchwise inversion of K+λI is O(B^3); paper underreports empirical cost, memory, and caching strategies.
- Weakness: sensitivity to kernel choices and bandwidths not addressed; no ablations on kernel selection or bandwidth tuning.
- Weakness: empirical scope limited—classification gains unclear vs cross-entropy, generation lacks quantitative comparisons to cGAN/CVAE, and distillation experiments are small-scale.
- Quality: technically sound derivations but omits statistical properties of the conditional estimator and some experimental/implementation details.
- Clarity: paper is mostly well-written but should state when Kd=Ks, report computational complexity/timings, specify kernels/bandwidth selection, and clarify MMD estimator choice and theorem assumptions.
- Originality: moderate — natural step from MMD to CMMD with application to deep conditional generators; distillation application is interesting but incremental.
- Significance: moderate — offers a stable alternative to adversarial training, but limited impact without stronger empirical baselines or scalability/theoretical advances.
- Key questions for authors: how are kernel bandwidths chosen and how sensitive are results; do you reuse x to reuse K inverses; training time vs cGAN/CVAE; mode collapse diagnostics; quantitative comparisons to cGAN/CVAE.
- Pros: simple stable objective, solid kernel-embedding grounding, versatile applications with competitive/pleasing results.
- Cons: limited novelty, missing critical empirical comparisons and ablations, unclear scalability and kernel-selection procedures, no consistency/generalization analysis.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept with score 6/10; confidence 0.7.
