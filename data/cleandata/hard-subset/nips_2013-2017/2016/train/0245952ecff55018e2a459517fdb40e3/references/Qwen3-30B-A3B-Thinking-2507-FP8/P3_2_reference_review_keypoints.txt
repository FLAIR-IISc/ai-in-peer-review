- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- Solid theoretical foundation: CMMD derived from kernel embedding and supported by three theorems.
- Maintains GMMN training simplicity (backprop-based) while handling conditional generation.
- Demonstrates versatility across predictive modeling, conditional image generation, and Bayesian distillation tasks.
- Properly situates work relative to GMMN, conditional GANs, and related methods.

- Major weakness: no direct experimental comparison to state-of-the-art conditional generative models (cGANs, CVAEs).
- Theoretical assumptions (CY|X ∈ F⊗G, Hilbert–Schmidt condition) are stated but not empirically justified for chosen kernels/datasets.
- Experimental details are insufficient for reproducibility (kernel choices, λ, auxiliary variable dimensionality, teacher training details).
- Claims of competitive performance are overstated given results that do not significantly surpass existing methods (e.g., MNIST, SVHN error rates).
- Limited discussion of CMMD/CGMMN limitations (handling multimodality, computational cost of kernel matrices, scaling with dimensionality).

- Practical implementation details unclear: role of inverse Gram matrices in gradient computation not explained.
- Choice of uniform prior for latent variables and fixed latent dimensionalities lack justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Insufficient experimental comparison tables and visual examples versus cGAN/CVAE baselines.
- Appendix/minibatch implementation details referenced but not provided, harming reproducibility.

- Overall: theoretically sound but experimentally weak; results do not convincingly demonstrate superiority or clear advantages.
- Clarity: well-written and organized, but experimental section lacks necessary implementation detail.
- Originality/significance: moderate—natural extension of existing kernel embedding ideas but not clearly differentiated from dominant conditional generative models.

- Recommendation: reject due to inadequate experimental comparison and insufficient evidence of advantage over existing methods.

- Suggested improvements: add direct comparisons with cGANs/CVAEs (quantitative and qualitative), provide full implementation/hyperparameter details, analyze sensitivity to kernels/λ and latent dimensionality, discuss limitations (multimodality, scalability), and include statistical significance testing.
