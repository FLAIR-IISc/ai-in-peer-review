- Introduces Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) using conditional mean embeddings in RKHS and a conditional MMD (CMMD) training objective to match P(Y|X).
- Trains a conditional generator y = f(x,h;w) by minimizing the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical conditional embedding operators from real and generated pairs.
- CMMD can be computed from Gram matrices, allowing gradient-based training through the Y-kernel while X-kernel need not be differentiable.
- Provides a sufficient condition (Theorem 3) linking equality of conditional embedding operators to equality of conditional distributions.
- Demonstrates applications: classification (MNIST/SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and distillation of a Bayesian regressor (Boston Housing).
- Builds on GMMN and kernel conditional embeddings; relates to cGANs and CVAEs but uses a simpler, non-adversarial objective.
- Novelty: explicit use of conditional mean embeddings as a CMMD criterion in deep generative modeling.
- Strength: natural, well-motivated extension from MMD to CMMD with insightful weighted-MMD perspective.
- Strength: single-objective training is simpler and typically more stable than adversarial training.
- Strength: flexible across prediction, conditional generation, and teacher-student distillation tasks.
- Strength: empirical breadth suggests generality across several tasks.
- Concern: Theorem 3’s assumptions are unclear/missing (need characteristic kernel on Y, measure-theoretic “a.e.” vs pointwise conditions).
- Concern: Regularization λ in empirical operator introduces bias; impact on consistency and optimization is not discussed.
- Concern: Computational cost is high due to O(b^3) inversion per batch; scalability, runtime, and memory not reported.
- Concern: Conditional generation experiments lack comparisons to cGAN/CVAE and quantitative metrics (e.g., FID/IS, NLL).
- Concern: Classification-as-generation setup is unconventional; accuracy lags discriminative baselines and lacks calibration/uncertainty analysis.
- Concern: Practical details (kernels on X/Y, bandwidths, noise-dimension effects) and sensitivity analyses are missing.
- Concern: Distillation experiment is small-scale and lacks comparison to standard dark-knowledge objectives and uncertainty evaluation.
- Overall quality: method is sound but theoretical statements and empirical evaluation need strengthening.
- Clarity: well written with clear derivation, but contains typos and omits practical experimental details; some figures low-res.
- Originality: moderate — novel combination though built from established components.
- Significance: potentially useful for non-adversarial conditional generation but impact limited without stronger empirical evidence.
- Suggestions: clarify Theorem 3 conditions and references, report kernel/λ choices and sensitivity, provide runtime/scalability analysis, and add quantitative comparisons to cGAN/CVAE.
- Pros: principled CMMD extension, simple/stable training, elegant operator view.
- Cons: theoretical caveats, estimator bias unaddressed, scalability and empirical baselines insufficient.
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on tightening theory and adding stronger empirical comparisons; confidence: high.
