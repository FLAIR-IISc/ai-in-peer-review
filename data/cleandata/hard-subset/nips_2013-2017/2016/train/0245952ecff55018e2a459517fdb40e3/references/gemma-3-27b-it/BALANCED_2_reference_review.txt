This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the capabilities of GMMNs to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to leverage a deep network to transform simple distributions, conditioned on input variables, to generate target samples, and to learn the network parameters by minimizing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) objective. The paper addresses a relevant problem—conditional generation—and builds upon existing work in generative modeling, specifically GMMNs and GANs. The motivation for CGMMNs, stemming from predictive modeling, contextual generation, and building large networks, is clearly articulated.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Motivation and Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the need for conditional generative models and provides compelling use cases.
* **Solid Theoretical Foundation:** The paper builds upon established concepts like kernel embeddings, MMD, and extends them to the conditional setting with CMMD. The theorems and derivations appear sound and provide a good theoretical underpinning for the proposed approach.
* **Well-Defined Objective Function:**  The CMMD objective is a reasonable choice for learning conditional distributions, avoiding the instability issues associated with GANs and offering a smooth optimization landscape.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The experimental evaluation covers a diverse set of tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge) on different datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face), demonstrating the versatility of CGMMN.
* **Competitive Performance:** The results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods on the tested tasks, indicating the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
* **Detailed Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and provides a detailed explanation of the method and experimental setup, including implementation details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty:** While the extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting is valuable, the core concept is relatively straightforward. The novelty lies in the CMMD formulation rather than a fundamentally new generative modeling approach.
* **Computational Complexity:** The CMMD objective involves computing kernel matrices, which can be computationally expensive, especially for large datasets. The paper mentions a mini-batch version, but a more detailed discussion of scalability concerns would be beneficial.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The performance of kernel methods is often sensitive to the choice of kernel and its parameters (e.g., bandwidth). The paper does not explicitly discuss the sensitivity of CGMMN to these hyperparameters or provide guidelines for their selection.
* **Lack of Comparison to VAEs:** While GANs are mentioned, a comparison to Conditional Variational Autoencoders (CVAEs) would be valuable. CVAEs are another popular approach for conditional generation and often provide a strong baseline.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:** The paper would benefit from more ablation studies to assess the contribution of different components of the CGMMN architecture and training procedure. For instance, analyzing the impact of the hidden layer size or the choice of kernel function.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper presents a technically sound extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting, addressing a relevant problem in generative modeling.
* The CMMD objective is well-motivated and provides a stable training procedure.
* The comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrates the effectiveness and versatility of CGMMN on a variety of tasks and datasets.
* The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the method and experimental results.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The novelty of the proposed approach is somewhat limited, as it primarily extends existing techniques.
* The computational complexity of the CMMD objective could be a barrier to scaling up to larger datasets.
* The lack of comparison to CVAEs and more thorough ablation studies weakens the assessment of the method's strengths and weaknesses.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of generative modeling. The extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting is valuable, and the CMMD objective provides a stable and effective training procedure. While the novelty is not groundbreaking, the comprehensive experimental evaluation and clear presentation make this a worthwhile read. 

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above, specifically:

1.  **Discuss scalability concerns** and potentially explore techniques for reducing the computational cost of the CMMD objective.
2.  **Include a comparison to Conditional Variational Autoencoders (CVAEs)** in the experimental evaluation.
3.  **Perform more ablation studies** to assess the sensitivity of CGMMN to different hyperparameters and architectural choices.
4. **Elaborate on the kernel choice**: Discuss the implications of different kernel choices (e.g., RBF, polynomial) on the performance and computational cost of the CMMD objective.
