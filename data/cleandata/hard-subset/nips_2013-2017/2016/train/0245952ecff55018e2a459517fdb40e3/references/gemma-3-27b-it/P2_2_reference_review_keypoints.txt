- Proposes CGMMN: extension of Generative Moment Matching Networks to model conditional distributions p(y|x) using a Conditional MMD objective.  
- Builds on kernel embeddings and MMD to avoid adversarial training complexities.  
- Well-written paper with clear mathematical derivations and helpful figures.  
- Provides theoretical justification (theorems) for the approach.  
- Introduces a mini-batch algorithm to make the CMMD objective computationally practical.  
- Empirically evaluated on diverse tasks (predictive modeling on MNIST/SVHN, contextual generation, Bayesian dark knowledge).  
- Shows competitive performance relative to state-of-the-art given the method's simplicity.  
- Technical contribution is incremental—a natural adaptation of MMD rather than a novel theory.  
- Network architectures (MLP, CNN) are standard and lack architectural novelty.  
- Lacks ablation studies and sensitivity analysis (e.g., kernel choice, regularization λ).  
- Limited discussion and analysis of the specific challenges in learning conditional distributions.  
- Dependence on pretrained PBP in the Bayesian dark knowledge experiment makes CGMMN's contribution there less clear.  
- Quality: technically sound and experimentally supported.  
- Clarity: exceptionally clear and easy to follow.  
- Originality: moderate—logical extension of existing work.  
- Significance: moderate—useful and simple addition to generative modeling tools but not a major breakthrough.  
- Pros for acceptance: clear motivation, solid theory, competitive results, practical usefulness for conditional generation.  
- Cons against acceptance: incremental novelty, missing ablation studies, limited architectural innovation.  
- Final recommendation: Weak Accept.
