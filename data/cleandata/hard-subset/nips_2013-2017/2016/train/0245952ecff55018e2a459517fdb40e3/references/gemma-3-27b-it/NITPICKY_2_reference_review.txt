## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the existing Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting. The core idea involves leveraging a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to train a deep generative model that maps a combination of random variables and conditional inputs to target samples. The authors demonstrate the approach on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, showing competitive results on several datasets.

While the paper addresses a relevant problem – extending generative modeling to the conditional domain with a relatively simple training objective – the execution and presentation exhibit a surprising number of flaws, ultimately hindering a positive assessment. My main concern is a pervasive lack of rigor and attention to detail, particularly concerning the mathematical formulations and experimental validation.

**Strengths:**

*   **Conceptually sound:** The extension of GMMN to the conditional setting is a logical progression and addresses a common need in generative modeling.
*   **Simplicity:** The CMMD objective and the overall generative process are relatively straightforward, potentially offering a practical alternative to GANs and VAEs.
*   **Wide applicability:** The paper demonstrates the applicability of CGMMN to multiple tasks (prediction, generation, distillation).

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Mathematical Imprecision & Notations:** The notation is inconsistent and sometimes poorly defined. For example, the definition of Φd and Υd are stated *after* they are used in equation (2). There are multiple instances of missing subscripts and unclear variable definitions, hindering a precise understanding of the CMMD formulation. Theorem 3's assumptions regarding F being a universal RKHS feel tacked on and not adequately justified in the context of the described network architecture. The statement “Asymptotically, L̂2MMD is an unbiased estimator” is a well-known result but should be cited.
*   **Kernel Choice:** The choice of kernel is not justified. The paper mentions RKHS properties but doesn’t discuss the practical implications of kernel selection or how it influences the results. Simply stating that a "smooth kernel" is used is insufficient. The sensitivity of the results to different kernels is not explored.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The role of the regularization parameter λ in the CMMD objective (L̂2CMMD) is not adequately addressed. A sensitivity analysis, or at least a justification for the chosen value, is missing.
*   **Experimental Details:** The experimental sections are frequently vague. For instance, the MLP architecture in MNIST is described, but the choice of activation functions and optimization details are not consistently presented across experiments. The statement about the number of parameters being "comparable" lacks quantitative support.  The description of the Bayesian dark knowledge experiment lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility. How precisely were the samples generated for the teacher network? What perturbations were applied?
*   **Comparison to Baselines:** The comparisons to baseline methods are not always fair. For example, claiming competitiveness without acknowledging the advantages of DSN (deeper supervision) feels disingenuous.
* **Mini-batch Implementation Details**: The claim of using a mini-batch version in Algorithm 1 needs more details, how the gradient is computed with the mini-batch and the entire dataset, how is the computation handled?
*   **Figure Quality**: The image quality in Figs. 2, 4 and 5 is low and does not showcase the generated samples effectively. 

**Specific Points:**

*   In Section 2.3, the statement “The result is summarized as follows.” is followed by Theorem 2, which is already presented.
*   Equation (2) is extremely complex and difficult to parse without careful attention. Rewriting it with clearer intermediate steps would significantly improve readability.
* The discussion of the connection between MMD and CMMD in Section 3.1, while insightful, is somewhat awkwardly presented.
*   The claim in Section 4.3 that the distilled model "does not harm" performance is a weak statement. A more quantitative assessment of the performance degradation (or lack thereof) is necessary.



**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   The core idea is novel and addresses a practical need.
*   The paper demonstrates the applicability of the approach to multiple tasks.
*   The CMMD criterion provides a theoretically grounded objective for conditional generative modeling.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   The numerous flaws in mathematical presentation and experimental validation undermine the credibility of the results.
*   The lack of rigor and attention to detail raises concerns about the robustness of the approach.
*   The paper fails to adequately address the limitations of the method or explore the sensitivity to key hyperparameters.
* The lack of details and clarity of the experiments make it difficult to reproduce.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potentially interesting contribution, the numerous flaws in this paper outweigh its strengths. The lack of mathematical precision, the vague experimental details, and the lack of thorough analysis create significant doubts about the validity and generalizability of the results. I believe the paper requires substantial revisions to address these issues before it would be suitable for publication at NIPS. 

**Recommendation:** **Reject**.  I would encourage the authors to significantly revise the paper, paying close attention to the points raised in this review. A more rigorous presentation, a more thorough experimental evaluation, and a more nuanced discussion of the limitations would be essential for a successful resubmission.
