- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.
- Leverages kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings to avoid strong distributional assumptions.

- Addresses the important problem of conditional generative modeling and extends moment-matching approaches to the conditional setting.
- Builds on solid theoretical foundations (MMD and RKHS embeddings) with correct derivations and clear presentation.
- Provides a practical training algorithm using backpropagation suitable for real-world use.
- Empirically validated across tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, Bayesian distillation) with competitive results on MNIST, SVHN, etc.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with clear motivation and consistent notation.

- Core idea has limited novelty since MMD-based generative modeling is existing work; main novelty is the CMMD formulation.
- Results may be sensitive to kernel and regularization hyperparameters, with insufficient guidance on selection or sensitivity analysis.
- CMMD estimation can be computationally expensive and scalability tradeoffs are not fully explored.
- Lacks comprehensive comparison to GANs and VAEs in terms of training stability and sample quality.
- Missing ablation studies to quantify the impact of components like kernel choice and latent dimensionality.

- Quality rated 8/10: technically sound but missing sensitivity and comparative analysis.
- Clarity rated 9/10: well-written and clearly explained.
- Originality rated 7/10: useful extension but not highly novel.
- Significance rated 8/10: addresses a relevant problem and shows promising, competitive results.

- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, theoretically sound, practical algorithm, and strong empirical results with clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited core novelty, potential computational cost, hyperparameter sensitivity, and insufficient comparisons to other generative models.

- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions, addressing hyperparameter sensitivity and broader comparisons.
