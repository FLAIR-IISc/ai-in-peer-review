

# Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extends the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to handle conditional distributions. The core contribution is the development of Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), a criterion for training conditional generative models that leverages kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions. The authors demonstrate CGMMN on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and distilling Bayesian models (Boston housing).

**Strengths:**

1. **Novelty**: The conditional extension of GMMN represents a significant conceptual contribution. While conditional generative models exist (e.g., conditional GANs [21]), this work specifically addresses the conditional moment-matching approach, filling an important gap in the literature. The CMMD formulation is well-motivated and theoretically grounded, building on kernel embedding techniques for conditional distributions [29].

2. **Broad Applicability**: The paper demonstrates the approach across three distinct application domains (predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation), showing the framework's versatility. This multi-task validation is impressive and strengthens the claim of general applicability.

3. **Conceptual Clarity**: The theoretical development of CMMD is well-structured and logically presented. The paper effectively connects kernel embedding theory to the conditional generative modeling problem, making the approach accessible to readers familiar with GMMN.

4. **Practical Implementation**: The CGMMN architecture is straightforward to implement using standard deep learning frameworks, and the training algorithm (Algorithm 1) is simple and efficient. The paper demonstrates this with concrete implementations using both MLP and CNN architectures.

**Weaknesses:**

1. **Limited Comparison with Conditional GANs**: While the paper references conditional GANs [21], it doesn't directly compare CGMMN with them on the same tasks. Conditional GANs have been highly successful for conditional generation, so the paper would benefit from showing how CGMMN compares to this state-of-the-art approach in terms of both quality and training stability.

2. **Questionable Significance**: The paper claims CGMMN "largely extends the capability of GMMN," but doesn't convincingly argue why moment-matching networks are superior to GANs for conditional generation. The experimental results show competitive performance but don't clearly demonstrate the advantages of the moment-matching approach over discriminative approaches like conditional GANs.

3. **Theoretical Justification**: While the CMMD objective is well-defined, the paper could strengthen its theoretical foundation by providing more intuition about why the conditional embedding approach is preferable to other conditional generative modeling techniques.

**Assessment:**

This paper presents a novel extension of generative moment-matching networks to conditional distributions, which is a significant conceptual contribution to the field. The authors have demonstrated the approach across multiple application domains with competitive results, showing the framework's versatility.

While the paper has some minor weaknesses in comparison to conditional GANs and theoretical justification, these do not undermine the core novelty of the conditional extension. The work represents a meaningful advancement of the moment-matching approach to generative modeling, addressing an important gap in the literature.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novelty and bold conceptual contributions over methodological perfection, I find this paper to be a strong candidate for acceptance. The conditional extension of GMMN is sufficiently novel and the multi-task validation demonstrates its practical utility. The paper would be significantly strengthened by a more direct comparison with conditional GANs, but this is a minor issue given the overall novelty of the approach.

**Recommendation: Accept**
