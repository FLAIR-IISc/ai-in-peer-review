## Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a conceptually sound extension of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting (CGMMN). The authors address a relevant problem – flexible conditional distribution learning – and propose a theoretically grounded approach using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD). The experimental evaluation is broad, covering predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation, demonstrating the versatility of the method. However, while the idea is promising, the paper lacks depth in several key areas, particularly regarding the practical advantages over existing methods and a more rigorous ablation study. I recommend **weak acceptance** contingent on addressing the concerns outlined below.

**1. Summary & Related Work (Quality/Originality):**

The paper effectively introduces the motivation for conditional generative models and positions CGMMN within the landscape of DGMs, GANs, and previous moment-matching approaches. The authors correctly identify the limitations of joint distribution modeling for tasks requiring conditional generation and highlight the benefits of a direct p(y|x) approach. The connection to related work (GANs, GMMNs, and conditional GANs) is well-established. The theoretical foundation rooted in kernel methods and MMD is clearly explained.  The framing of the problem and the proposed solution are logically consistent. 

**2. Strengths:**

*   **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The use of CMMD as a training objective, based on kernel mean embeddings, provides a solid theoretical basis for the approach. The theorems presented are clearly stated and provide a rationale for the method.
*   **Generality & Flexibility:** CGMMN's ability to handle various conditional generation tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, knowledge distillation) is a major strength, demonstrating its broad applicability.
*   **Straightforward Training:**  The authors emphasize the simplicity of the training process due to the direct optimization via back-propagation, which is a significant advantage over the often unstable training dynamics of GANs.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The experimental section covers a diverse set of tasks and datasets, providing a reasonable assessment of the method's performance.

**3. Weaknesses (and suggestions for improvement):**

*   **Limited Novelty:** While extending GMMN to the conditional setting is a natural step, the core idea doesn’t present a radical departure from existing techniques. The authors don’t fully articulate *why* CGMMN would be preferred over other established methods like conditional GANs or conditional VAEs, beyond its potentially more stable training. A more in-depth comparative analysis is needed.
*   **Lack of Ablation Study:** The experiments lack a thorough ablation study to understand the contribution of different components (e.g., the choice of kernel, the dimensionality of the hidden variables, network architecture). For instance, how sensitive is the performance to the regularization parameter λ in the CMMD objective?
*   **Limited Discussion of Kernel Choice:** The paper mentions the importance of using a universal kernel, but doesn't provide guidance on kernel selection for different tasks.  The impact of the kernel choice on performance is not investigated. A discussion on the computational cost and practical considerations of different kernels would be valuable.
*   **Comparative Performance:** While CGMMN achieves competitive results, it rarely *outperforms* state-of-the-art methods significantly. The results on the MNIST and SVHN datasets are comparable but not demonstrably superior. A more compelling demonstration of the method's advantages is needed.
*   **Mini-batch Implementation Details:** The reference to an appendix for details on the mini-batch implementation (Algorithm 1) is insufficient. Key details like how the samples are drawn and the impact on computational efficiency need to be described in the main body of the paper.
* **Clarity on Assumptions:** The authors acknowledge the assumptions required for the CMMD objective to be well-defined, but don’t fully discuss how these assumptions might be violated in practice and how the method behaves under such violations.



**4.  Arguments For & Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a theoretically sound and well-motivated approach to conditional generative modeling.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the versatility of CGMMN across different tasks.
*   The training procedure is relatively simple and stable.

**Against Acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the approach is incremental, and the paper doesn't sufficiently justify why CGMMN would be preferred over existing methods.
*   The lack of an ablation study limits the understanding of the method's behavior and sensitivity to different parameters.
*   The comparative performance is not consistently superior to state-of-the-art alternatives.

**5.  Recommendations:**

I recommend **weak acceptance** contingent on the following revisions:

1.  **Enhanced Comparative Analysis:** Provide a more detailed comparison with other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs, conditional VAEs), highlighting the specific advantages and disadvantages of CGMMN.
2.  **Ablation Study:** Conduct a thorough ablation study to assess the impact of key hyperparameters (e.g., kernel choice, regularization parameter, hidden variable dimensionality) on performance.
3.  **Kernel Discussion:** Expand the discussion on kernel selection, providing guidance and potentially analyzing the performance of different kernels on the evaluated tasks.
4.  **Mini-batch details:** Provide full details on the mini-batch implementation within the main text.
5.  **Discussion of Limitations:** Acknowledge and discuss the limitations of the approach, including potential violations of the underlying assumptions and their impact on performance.




I believe addressing these points will significantly strengthen the paper and make it a more valuable contribution to the NIPS community.
