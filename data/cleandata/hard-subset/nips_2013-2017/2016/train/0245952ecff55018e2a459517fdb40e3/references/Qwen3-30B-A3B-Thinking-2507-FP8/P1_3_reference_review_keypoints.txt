- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion and evaluates across several tasks.

- Theorem provides a sound theoretical foundation establishing sufficient condition for conditional distribution matching.

- Experiments are comprehensive across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation with appropriate baselines.

- Implementation details (architectures, hyperparameters, training) are sufficiently provided for reproducibility.

- Authors acknowledge limitations and compare to state-of-the-art methods.

- Lacks thorough comparison with conditional VAEs and other conditional generative models beyond GANs.

- Theoretical analysis of the CMMD estimator is limited; no convergence rates or sample complexity provided.

- SVHN results lag behind state-of-the-art (CGMMN-CNN 3.13% vs DSN 1.92%) without sufficient analysis or empirical improvement using more sophisticated architectures.

- Computational complexity of CMMD relative to alternatives (e.g., conditional GANs) is not discussed.

- Paper is well-organized with clear sections and consistent notation.

- Figures effectively illustrate architecture and generated samples.

- Some CMMD technical details are dense and lack intuition or examples.

- Connection between theoretical CMMD objective and the stochastic gradient implementation (Algorithm 1) is unclear.

- Important details are deferred to appendices, hindering readability for readers without appendix access.

- Extending GMMN to conditional settings is a natural and useful contribution.

- CMMD is a novel adaptation of MMD to the conditional setting using conditional embedding operators.

- Demonstrated versatility across diverse applications (predictive modeling, generation, distillation).

- Core idea is somewhat incremental given prior conditional GANs and VAEs, and distinctions are not sharply articulated.

- Paper doesn’t clearly explain how CMMD differs from or improves upon adversarial conditional distribution matching.

- Related work discussion is insufficiently deep on differences from previous conditional generative models.

- Addresses a practical need for conditional generative models and offers a simpler training procedure than GANs.

- Experimental results are competitive across tasks, indicating practical utility.

- Performance improvements over state-of-the-art are modest and not convincing, especially on SVHN.

- No evidence CGMMN handles more complex conditionals or high-dimensional conditional dependencies better than existing methods.

- Bayesian dark knowledge experiment is limited to a single small regression task (Boston housing), limiting generalizability.

- Overall recommendation leans toward rejection due to incremental novelty and insufficient evidence of clear advantages.

- Suggestion: provide detailed comparisons with conditional GANs and VAEs on performance and training dynamics.

- Suggestion: supply theoretical analysis for CMMD convergence rates or sample complexity.

- Suggestion: run additional experiments on more challenging datasets to show clear advantages.

- Suggestion: clarify how theoretical CMMD maps to the practical SGD implementation in Algorithm 1.

- Suggestion: discuss computational complexity, training time, and memory requirements compared to alternatives.

- For acceptance: clear extension of GMMN to conditional distributions with sound theory and simple training; comprehensive experiments and competitive performance.

- Against acceptance: limited novelty vs. conditional GANs/VAEs, modest performance gains (notably on SVHN), limited theoretical analysis, and insufficient evidence of computational advantages.
