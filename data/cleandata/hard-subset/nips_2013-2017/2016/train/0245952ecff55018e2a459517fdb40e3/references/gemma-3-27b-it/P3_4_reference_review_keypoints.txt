- Overall: Conceptually sound extension of GMMNs to conditional setting (CGMMN); recommends weak acceptance pending revisions.
- Motivation & framing: Well-motivated need for direct conditional modeling and clear positioning among DGMs, GANs, and GMMNs.
- Theoretical basis: Strong theoretical foundation using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) and kernel mean embeddings.
- Generality: CGMMN is flexible and applicable to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation.
- Training simplicity: Training is straightforward via backpropagation and potentially more stable than GANs.
- Evaluation breadth: Experiments cover diverse tasks and datasets, showing method versatility.
- Limited novelty: Extension to conditional setting is natural but incremental; not a radical departure from prior work.
- Comparative justification: Paper does not convincingly argue why CGMMN should be preferred over conditional GANs or VAEs.
- Lack of ablation: Missing thorough ablation studies on kernel choice, hidden dimension, architecture, and regularization sensitivity.
- Kernel discussion: Insufficient guidance and empirical analysis on kernel selection and its computational implications.
- Comparative performance: Results are competitive but rarely significantly better than state-of-the-art baselines.
- Mini-batch implementation: Key implementation details (sample drawing, efficiency) are relegated to appendix and need inclusion in main text.
- Assumptions clarity: Paper acknowledges CMMD assumptions but lacks discussion of practical violations and robustness.
- For acceptance argument: Theoretically solid, versatile, and simpler training warrant consideration.
- Against acceptance argument: Incremental novelty, missing ablations, and lackluster performance gains argue against acceptance.
- Required revisions: Add detailed comparative analysis with conditional GANs/VAEs, thorough ablation studies, expanded kernel guidance, full mini-batch details in main text, and explicit limitations discussion.
