## Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to train a deep network that maps a simple distribution (e.g., uniform) and given conditional variables to a target sample, using a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective function. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, achieving competitive performance on various datasets.  The work builds on recent advances in generative modeling, particularly GMMNs and GANs, offering a potentially simpler training procedure than the adversarial approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Extending GMMNs to the conditional setting is a valuable contribution.  While conditional GANs exist, a moment-matching approach for conditional generation is relatively unexplored. The CMMD criterion is a well-motivated extension of MMD.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a clear mathematical foundation for CMMD, building upon established concepts like Hilbert space embeddings and kernel methods. The theorems presented regarding the equivalence of conditional distribution matching via operator equality are solid.
* **Simplicity & Training Stability:** The CMMD objective appears simpler and potentially more stable than the min-max game of GANs. The authors highlight the ease of training via backpropagation, which is a significant practical advantage.
* **Versatility:** Demonstrated application across diverse tasks (prediction, generation, knowledge distillation) illustrates the flexibility of the CGMMN framework.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results on MNIST, SVHN, and the Boston Housing dataset demonstrate competitive performance compared to existing methods, including state-of-the-art approaches like CMMVA and DSN. The inclusion of generative examples and analysis (nearest neighbors) enhances the empirical evaluation.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Ablation Studies:** While the results are promising, the paper lacks detailed ablation studies. For example, exploring the impact of the hidden layer size, different kernel choices, or the dimensionality of the auxiliary noise variable 'h' would strengthen the analysis.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:**  The optimal hyperparameters (e.g., regularization parameter λ) are not thoroughly discussed. It’s unclear how sensitive the performance is to these choices and what strategies were used for tuning.
* **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** In some experiments (e.g., SVHN), the comparison could be improved by including more recent and powerful generative models beyond CMMVA.
* **Complexity of CMMD Computation:** While theoretically elegant, the CMMD objective involves inverting kernel matrices, which can be computationally expensive for large datasets. While the mini-batch version is mentioned, a more detailed discussion of the computational tradeoffs and potential scalability issues is needed.
* **Clarity in some sections:** The description of the conditional embedding operator and its estimation in Section 2.3 could be more intuitive. The transition between theory and implementation is sometimes abrupt.
* **Lack of Qualitative assessment of generated images:** While nearest neighbors are used, a more comprehensive qualitative evaluation of the generated images (e.g., using Inception Score or FID) would be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a well-written and technically sound paper that presents a valuable extension to the GMMN framework. The introduction of CGMMN and the CMMD objective offer a promising alternative to GANs for conditional generative modeling. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach across a range of tasks. While some weaknesses exist, they are not critical and could be addressed with further experimentation and analysis.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel extension of GMMN to conditional distributions.
* Strong theoretical foundation and clear mathematical derivation of CMMD.
* Competitive performance across multiple tasks.
* Simpler training procedure compared to GANs.
* Potential for wider applicability in conditional generative modeling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lack of detailed ablation studies.
* Limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity.
* Potential computational challenges with CMMD for large datasets.
* Could benefit from stronger baseline comparisons in some experiments.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept** with minor revisions. 

I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS 2016. The authors should address the weaknesses outlined above by:

1.  Conducting more comprehensive ablation studies.
2.  Discussing hyperparameter tuning strategies and sensitivity analysis.
3.  Providing a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity of CMMD and potential scalability solutions.
4. Adding stronger baselines for comparison in certain experiments (e.g., more recent generative models).
5.  Clarifying the explanation of the conditional embedding operator.
6. Include a more rigorous qualitative assessment of generated images.
