## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the existing Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to learn a generative model that, given input variables, produces samples from the conditional distribution.  The authors propose a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective for training, leveraging kernel methods to avoid distributional assumptions. They demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on tasks including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an important problem:** Conditional generative modeling is a crucial area with broad applications. Extending existing moment-matching approaches to the conditional setting is a logical and valuable contribution.
* **Solid theoretical foundation:** The paper builds upon well-established concepts like Hilbert space embeddings and MMD, providing a sound theoretical basis for the proposed CMMD criterion. The theorems and derivations appear correct and are presented clearly.
* **Practical Algorithm:** The authors provide a concrete algorithm for training CGMMN with efficient backpropagation, which is a key strength for real-world applicability.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The paper presents extensive experiments across various tasks demonstrating competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods. The results on MNIST, SVHN, and the Bayesian distillation experiment are encouraging.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and positions the work within the existing literature.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Core Idea:** While the application of MMD to *conditional* generation is a valuable extension, the core idea of using MMD for generative modeling isn't entirely new (as acknowledged in the introduction). The primary contribution lies in the specific CMMD formulation and its application.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Kernel methods are known to be sensitive to kernel parameter selection and the regularization parameter (lambda). The paper does not adequately address the sensitivity of the results to these choices, nor provide clear guidance on how to select them.
* **Computational Cost:** Estimating the CMMD objective can be computationally expensive, especially for large datasets. While the mini-batch algorithm is mentioned, a more detailed discussion of the computational tradeoffs and potential scalability issues would be beneficial.
* **Comparison to GANs/VAEs:** The paper briefly mentions GANs and VAEs but lacks a comprehensive comparison. It would be valuable to discuss the advantages and disadvantages of CGMMN relative to these popular generative modeling techniques (e.g., training stability, sample quality).
* **Lack of Ablation Studies:** The paper would benefit from ablation studies to demonstrate the importance of different components of the CGMMN architecture, such as the choice of kernel or the dimensionality of the latent space.




**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (8/10):** The paper is technically sound, with a solid theoretical grounding and well-supported claims through experiments. However, the lack of sensitivity analysis and more in-depth comparison to competing methods slightly detracts from the overall quality.
* **Clarity (9/10):**  The paper is well-written and clearly explains the core concepts and methodology.  The equations are well-defined, and the notation is consistent.
* **Originality (7/10):** The extension of MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting is a useful contribution. However, the underlying ideas aren’t completely novel, and the work largely builds upon existing concepts.
* **Significance (8/10):** The paper addresses a relevant problem and offers a promising approach to conditional generative modeling.  The experimental results demonstrate that CGMMN is competitive, suggesting that it has the potential to be used by other researchers and practitioners.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*  Addresses an important and practical problem in generative modeling.
*  Presents a theoretically sound and well-motivated approach.
*  Demonstrates competitive performance across a variety of tasks.
*  Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  The core idea isn't groundbreaking, and the paper primarily extends existing work.
*  The computational cost of the CMMD objective could be a limiting factor.
*  The sensitivity to hyperparameters and the lack of comparison to GANs/VAEs are weaknesses.




**Overall Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling by extending MMD-based approaches to the conditional setting. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above—particularly providing more detail on hyperparameter sensitivity and comparison to other generative models—would further strengthen the paper.  I believe the potential impact of this work justifies its inclusion in NIPS.
