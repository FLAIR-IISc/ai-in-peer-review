- Proposes Conditional GMMN (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions using a Conditional MMD (CMMD) training objective.  
- Theoretical development correctly extends kernel embedding framework and provides a well-justified CMMD objective.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation with appropriate baselines.  
- CGMMN-CNN achieves competitive MNIST/SVHN classification without data augmentation; generative samples show reasonable quality but noticeable noise.  
- Paper lacks analysis of training stability relative to conditional GANs and does not discuss mode collapse.  
- Suggested improvements (e.g., using autoencoders) are shown qualitatively but not quantified.  
- Does not address scalability to high-dimensional conditional variables or very large datasets.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized with consistent notation and effective figures; reproducibility mostly adequate but some hyperparameters missing.  
- Implementation details are under-specified (e.g., “proper regularization” in mini-batch algorithm not defined—weight decay, early stopping unclear).  
- Core contribution is significant but conceptually a straightforward extension from unconditional GMMN.  
- Paper insufficiently cites related work on conditional kernel embeddings (e.g., Song et al. 2013) and possible conditional MMD methods from 2015–2016.  
- CMMD is a novel application of kernel embedding techniques to conditional distributions.  
- Significance is strong because CGMMN provides a simple alternative to conditional GANs, avoiding min-max optimization.  
- More compelling evidence is needed to demonstrate clear advantages over existing methods in specific scenarios (e.g., when DSN outperforms CGMMN).  
- Final recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to add comparative analysis with conditional GANs, quantify improvements (e.g., autoencoder denoising), clarify implementation/regularization, and improve related-work citations.
