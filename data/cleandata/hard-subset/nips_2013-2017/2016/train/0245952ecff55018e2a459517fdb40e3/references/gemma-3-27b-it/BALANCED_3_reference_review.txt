This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the original GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to leverage the simplicity of MMD-based training for generative models in scenarios requiring conditional generation, such as predictive modeling, contextual generation, and building larger generative networks. The authors propose a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective and demonstrate its effectiveness through various experiments.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a relevant problem in generative modeling – extending the capabilities of MMD-based methods to conditional settings. The connection to previous work on GANs and GMMNs is clearly established, and the advantages of the proposed approach – simpler training compared to GANs – are highlighted. The theoretical foundation, building on kernel embeddings and MMD, is sound and appropriately referenced. The experimental evaluation is thorough, covering diverse tasks and datasets, demonstrating the competitiveness of CGMMN against existing methods. The inclusion of results on distilling Bayesian models is particularly interesting and showcases a practical application of the technique.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** Extending GMMN to the conditional setting is a valuable contribution. While conditional GANs exist, offering a MMD-based alternative with potentially simpler training is significant.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper builds upon a strong theoretical foundation in kernel methods and MMD, providing a well-justified approach. The derivation of CMMD is clear and concise.
* **Empirical Validation:** The extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN across a range of tasks and datasets.  The results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
* **Practical Application:** The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is insightful and demonstrates the practical utility of the framework.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to understand the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Computational Cost:** While simpler in principle than GAN training, the computation of the CMMD objective, particularly the kernel matrix inversions, can be computationally expensive, especially for large datasets. The mini-batch approach is mentioned, but a more detailed discussion of scalability considerations would be beneficial.
* **Kernel Choice:** The performance of MMD-based methods is sensitive to the choice of kernel. The paper doesn't delve deeply into the impact of different kernel functions on the results. A more comprehensive analysis of kernel selection would strengthen the work.  The regularization parameter λ also impacts performance and a discussion of how to tune it would be useful.
* **Limited Comparison to CVAE:**  While comparisons to GANs and other generative models are present, there’s limited comparison to Conditional Variational Autoencoders (CVAEs).  CVAEs are a strong baseline for conditional generation and a direct comparison would clarify the advantages of CGMMN.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper lacks a detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning. Reporting the range of hyperparameters explored and their impact on performance would enhance reproducibility and provide practical guidance to readers.
* **Visual Clarity of Generated Samples:** While the paper presents generated samples, the visual quality in some cases (e.g., MNIST) isn't dramatically superior to other methods. Higher resolution images or more detailed qualitative analysis would be helpful.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to conditional generative modeling.
* The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating the competitiveness of CGMMN across various tasks.
* The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is a valuable contribution.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presents the proposed method.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost of the CMMD objective could be a limiting factor for large-scale applications.
* The paper lacks a detailed analysis of kernel choice and hyperparameter sensitivity.
* The comparison to CVAEs is limited.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN framework offers a promising alternative to GANs for conditional generation, with the potential for simpler training and a solid theoretical foundation. While there are some weaknesses related to computational cost and hyperparameter tuning, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**  

I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Provide a more detailed discussion of the computational cost and scalability of the CMMD objective.
2.  Conduct a more thorough analysis of kernel choice and its impact on performance. Include a discussion of how the regularization parameter λ is tuned.
3.  Include a direct comparison to Conditional Variational Autoencoders (CVAEs).
4.  Provide more detailed information on hyperparameter tuning and report a range of explored values.
5.  If possible, include higher resolution generated images or a more detailed qualitative analysis of the generated samples.
