**Summary**
This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework to model conditional distributions `p(y|x)`. The core contribution is the development of a novel training objective, Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), which measures the discrepancy between two conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). CMMD is defined as the Hilbert-Schmidt norm between the conditional mean embedding operators of the true and generated data distributions. The proposed CGMMN model generates samples by passing the conditioning variable `x` along with a random noise vector `h` through a deep neural network. The authors demonstrate the utility of their framework on a diverse set of tasks: predictive modeling (classification on MNIST and SVHN), contextual generation (images from labels on MNIST and Yale Faces), and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

**Strengths**
1.  **Originality and Significance:** The paper presents a logical and significant extension of moment-matching networks to the conditional setting. This broadens the applicability of this class of models, which avoids the often unstable min-max optimization of GANs, to a wider range of important machine learning problems. The proposed CMMD objective is a principled and theoretically grounded method for this extension.
2.  **Broad Applicability:** The authors do an excellent job of demonstrating the versatility of their model across three distinct and relevant application areas. The use of CGMMN for "Bayesian dark knowledge" is a particularly novel and interesting application.
3.  **Simple Training:** Like its predecessor GMMN, the CGMMN framework retains the advantage of a simple, single objective function that can be optimized directly with back-propagation, sidestepping the difficulties associated with training adversarial networks.

**Weaknesses**
Despite the promising core idea, the paper suffers from a significant number of flaws in its technical presentation, clarity, and experimental reporting. These issues, though some are minor individually, collectively undermine the quality and reproducibility of the work.

1.  **Lack of Clarity and Reproducibility:** Several crucial details are either missing or explained ambiguously, making it difficult for an expert to reproduce the results.
    *   **Kernel Choice:** The CMMD objective is critically dependent on the choice of kernel functions for both the input `x` and output `y` spaces. The paper fails to specify which kernels (e.g., Gaussian RBF, polynomial) and what hyperparameter settings (e.g., bandwidth `sigma`) were used in any of the experiments. This is a major omission.
    *   **Predictive Task Setup (Sec 4.1.1):** The setup for the classification experiments is unclear. The paper states the generated sample is a vector `y ∈ R10+`, where "each yi denotes the confidence". It is not explained how the ground-truth `y` vectors are constructed for the training data. Are they one-hot vectors? If so, the model is being trained to regress to the corners of the simplex, which should be stated. The notation `R10+` is also non-standard.
    *   **Bayesian Distillation Setup (Sec 4.3):** The experiment on the Boston housing dataset mentions that the teacher model is given inputs generated by "adding noise into training data". The type and magnitude of this noise are not specified, which is a key parameter for this experiment.
    *   **Discrete Conditional Variables (Sec 4.2.1):** For the image generation task, the paper states that since the conditional variable `y` (label) is discrete, `CY X` and `C−1XX` are estimated "directly". This is an important implementation detail that is relegated to the appendix. A brief explanation in the main text would significantly improve clarity.

2.  **Technical and Notational Imprecision:** The paper contains several instances of imprecise language and notation.
    *   **Equation (1):** The properties of the conditional embedding operator are poorly formatted. The text reads ". µY |x = CY |Xφ(x); 2. EY |x[g(Y )|x] = ...". This should be formatted as a numbered list. Furthermore, the expectation `EY |x[g(Y )|x]` is notationally redundant; `EY [g(Y )|x]` is standard.
    *   **Remark 1:** The rewriting of the MMD objective as `Tr(Ld · 1) + ...` is non-standard. The conventional empirical MMD estimator is a sum of kernel evaluations. This novel formulation is presented without derivation or citation and is potentially confusing or incorrect.
    *   **Generative Process (Sec 3.2):** An alternative generative process `x̃ = x + h` is suggested. This is only well-defined if `x` and `h` have compatible dimensions and semantics, which is not generally true (e.g., `x` is a high-dimensional image, `h` is a low-dimensional noise vector). This suggestion is confusing without further qualification.
    *   **Computational Cost:** The CMMD objective involves inverting `N x N` matrices, where `N` is the mini-batch size, leading to a computational cost of `O(N^3)`. This is a potential limitation compared to GANs and is not discussed.

3.  **Formatting and Minor Errors:** The paper lacks the polish expected of a top-tier publication.
    *   **Citations:** There are clear errors and inconsistencies. Reference [2] ("Learning to generate chairs...") does not seem appropriate for the cited task of image-to-text/text-to-image generation. The reference list formatting is highly inconsistent (e.g., some entries have years, others do not; some are arXiv pre-prints, others full journal citations, with no uniform style).
    *   **Typos:** There are several typos, such as "data augumentation" (Sec 4.1.1) and "Through there is a gap" (Sec 4.1.2).
    *   **Submission Formatting:** The manuscript includes a NIPS footer and a "Corresponding author" mark, which should be removed for anonymous review.

**Recommendation**

While the central idea of extending GMMNs to the conditional setting with a CMMD loss is valuable, the current manuscript is marred by a large number of minor-to-moderate flaws. The lack of crucial experimental details severely hinders reproducibility, and the numerous instances of notational and presentational sloppiness detract from the paper's technical quality. The cumulative effect of these issues leads me to believe the paper is not yet ready for publication at NIPS. I would be willing to reconsider my assessment if these issues, particularly those related to clarity and reproducibility, are thoroughly addressed in a revision.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Presents a novel, theoretically-grounded objective (CMMD) for conditional generative modeling.
*   Extends an important class of generative models (GMMNs) and avoids the training instability of GANs.
*   Demonstrates impressive versatility across generation, prediction, and a novel model distillation task.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
*   Critical experimental details (kernel choices, target variable construction, noise models) are missing, making the work impossible to reproduce.
*   The paper suffers from numerous notational imprecisions, unclear explanations, and formatting errors that reduce its overall quality.
*   Potential limitations, such as the computational cost of the CMMD objective, are not discussed.
