This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of previous Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting. The core idea is to leverage the simplicity of MMD-based generative modeling while enabling generation conditioned on input variables. The authors propose Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as the objective function and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

This work builds upon the recent resurgence of moment-matching approaches for generative modeling, particularly GMMN [19], which offered a simpler alternative to GANs. The extension to conditional generation is a natural and valuable direction, addressing a key limitation of the original GMMN. The problem setting of conditional generation, covering predictive modeling, contextual generation, and building blocks for larger models, is well-motivated and clearly articulated, referencing relevant work like conditional GANs [21] and conditional VAEs [28]. The theoretical foundation relies on kernel embeddings and MMD, drawing upon established literature [7, 29].

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear and Concise Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The motivation, methodology, and experiments are presented in a logical manner.
*   **Sound Technical Foundation:** The use of CMMD as an objective function, based on kernel embeddings, is theoretically sound and aligns with the existing literature on MMD and its applications.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The authors evaluate CGMMN on a diverse set of tasks, demonstrating its versatility and applicability to various scenarios.  The inclusion of predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation provides a strong empirical assessment.
*   **Mini-batch Implementation:** The discussion and implementation of a mini-batch version of the algorithm address the computational challenges associated with kernel methods, making the approach more practical.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty in Core Idea:** While the extension of GMMN to the conditional setting is valuable, the core idea isn't groundbreaking. Conditional generation is a well-explored area, and the approach is largely a direct adaptation of existing techniques.
*   **Lack of Comparison to Strong Baselines:** In several experiments, the comparison to state-of-the-art methods is not always comprehensive. While some strong baselines are included, more recent and highly competitive models are missing (e.g., Transformers for contextual generation). Specifically, in the generative experiments, comparing with VAEs with similar capacity would be insightful.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper does not adequately address the sensitivity of the model to hyperparameters, particularly the kernel parameters and regularization strength (lambda). A more thorough investigation of hyperparameter choices would strengthen the results.
*   **Limited Ablation Studies:** Ablation studies exploring the impact of different components of the CGMMN architecture (e.g., the dimensionality of the hidden variables, the choice of activation functions) are lacking.
*   **Theoretical Assumptions:** The discussion of the theoretical assumptions underlying CMMD (smoothness, Hilbert-Schmidt properties) could be more thorough. The authors acknowledge potential violations of these assumptions but do not fully explore their impact on the empirical results.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and well-motivated extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
*   The experimental results demonstrate that CGMMN is competitive with existing methods on a diverse set of tasks.
*   The mini-batch implementation addresses a key practical challenge of kernel methods.
*   The paper is clearly written and easy to follow.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The core idea lacks significant novelty.
*   The comparison to state-of-the-art baselines is not always comprehensive.
*   The paper lacks a thorough analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and ablation studies.
*   The theoretical discussion could be more in-depth.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to the field of generative modeling. While the core idea isn’t revolutionary, the extension of GMMN to the conditional setting is a valuable addition to the toolkit. The comprehensive evaluation and clear presentation are strong points. However, the lack of thorough comparison to strong baselines, limited ablation studies, and incomplete discussion of theoretical assumptions raise some concerns.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **weak accept**.  The paper is of sufficient quality to be included in NIPS, but addressing the weaknesses mentioned above would significantly strengthen the contribution. I would encourage the authors to perform more comprehensive comparisons, conduct ablation studies, and provide a more in-depth discussion of the theoretical aspects.
