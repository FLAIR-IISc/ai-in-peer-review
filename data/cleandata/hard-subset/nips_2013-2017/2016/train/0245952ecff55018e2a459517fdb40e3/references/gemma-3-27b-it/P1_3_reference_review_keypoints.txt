- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to learn conditional generative models.
- CGMMN maps simple noise plus conditioning variables through a deep network to generate target samples.
- CMMD is a well-motivated extension of MMD for conditional distributions using Hilbert space embeddings and kernel methods.
- Theoretical derivations show operator-equality conditions for matching conditional distributions and provide a clear mathematical foundation.
- Training via CMMD is simpler and potentially more stable than adversarial GAN training, allowing straightforward backpropagation.
- The method is versatile, demonstrated on prediction, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation tasks.
- Empirical results on MNIST, SVHN, and Boston Housing show competitive performance against existing methods and include qualitative nearest-neighbor analyses.
- Novelty: conditional extension of GMMNs fills a gap compared to conditional GANs and is relatively unexplored.
- Weakness: lacks detailed ablation studies (hidden sizes, kernel choices, noise-dimensions) to understand design choices.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and tuning strategies, e.g., regularization λ.
- Weakness: comparisons miss some stronger, more recent generative-model baselines (notably for SVHN).
- Weakness: CMMD requires kernel-matrix inversions, raising computational and scalability concerns not fully addressed beyond mini-batching.
- Weakness: some explanations (conditional embedding operator estimation) are abrupt and could be clearer/ more intuitive.
- Weakness: qualitative evaluation of generated images is limited; metrics like Inception Score or FID are not reported.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, promising alternative to GANs; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel conditional GMMN, solid theory, competitive results, simpler/stable training, broad applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing ablations, unclear hyperparameter sensitivity, computational scalability concerns, weaker baseline comparisons.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions and requested improvements.
- Requested revisions: add comprehensive ablations, analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, detail CMMD computational complexity/scalability, include stronger baselines, clarify conditional embedding operator, and provide more rigorous qualitative image assessment.
