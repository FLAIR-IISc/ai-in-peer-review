

# Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to model conditional distributions. The authors introduce Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a training objective for conditional generative models, building upon the kernel embedding of conditional distributions as described in [29]. The paper addresses three key application areas: predictive modeling (classification), contextual generation (image generation conditioned on labels), and distilling Bayesian models (Bayesian dark knowledge).

The paper relates CGMMN to previous work by positioning it as a natural extension of GMMN [19] for conditional distributions, while noting that conditional GANs [21] and conditional VAEs [28] have been explored previously. The authors correctly identify that conditional distributions are crucial for many applications including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and building large generative models.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound extension of MMD to conditional distributions. The theoretical development of CMMD (Theorem 3 and Equation 2) is well-motivated and builds logically on prior work [7, 29]. The authors provide a clear derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator and an efficient training algorithm (Algorithm 1). The experimental evaluation covers multiple tasks with appropriate baselines (e.g., CMMVA [18] for classification, various generative models for image generation). The paper acknowledges limitations, such as the smoothness assumptions required for the CMMD objective. However, the paper lacks a thorough analysis of computational complexity compared to alternatives like conditional GANs, and does not provide convergence guarantees for the CGMMN training procedure.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized with clear sections that progress logically from theoretical foundations to applications. The mathematical derivations are presented with sufficient detail, and Figures 1-5 effectively illustrate the model architecture and results. The writing is generally clear and accessible to NIPS readers. The authors provide sufficient implementation details (e.g., network architectures, hyperparameters) for reproducibility. One minor weakness is the limited discussion of kernel selection, which is critical for MMD-based methods.

**Originality:** The paper makes a clear contribution by extending GMMN to conditional distributions through the CMMD criterion. While conditional GANs [21] and conditional VAEs [28] have been explored, the authors correctly position their work as filling a research void in moment-matching networks for conditional distributions. The CMMD objective (Equation 2) represents a novel adaptation of MMD to conditional settings. The paper adequately references related work, including conditional GANs [21] and conditional VAEs [28], and clearly distinguishes CGMMN's approach.

**Significance:** The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance across multiple tasks: classification (MNIST and SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST and Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The paper shows that CGMMN can achieve comparable results to state-of-the-art methods like CMMVA [18] in classification tasks, generate high-quality images conditioned on labels, and successfully distill Bayesian models without significant performance degradation. The simplicity of the training procedure (via backpropagation) and flexibility (compatible with various network architectures) enhance the practical significance of the work.

**Strengths:**
- Clear theoretical extension from MMD to CMMD
- Comprehensive experimental validation across diverse tasks
- Simple and efficient training procedure via backpropagation
- Demonstrated applicability to important problems (predictive modeling, generation, knowledge distillation)
- Well-organized presentation with clear mathematical derivations

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited comparison with conditional GANs (the most relevant prior work for conditional generation)
- Insufficient discussion of kernel selection and its impact on performance
- Lack of ablation studies to isolate the contribution of the conditional component
- No detailed analysis of computational complexity compared to alternatives
- Theoretical analysis lacks convergence guarantees for the training procedure

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- The paper presents a clear, well-motivated extension of GMMN to conditional distributions
- The CMMD criterion is theoretically sound and well-justified
- The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance across multiple domains
- The method is simple to implement and train, making it accessible to practitioners
- The approach fills a research gap in moment-matching networks for conditional distributions

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
- The paper does not adequately compare with the most relevant prior work (conditional GANs)
- The lack of ablation studies and kernel analysis weakens the contribution
- The theoretical foundation lacks convergence guarantees for the training procedure
- The computational complexity analysis is insufficient for a NIPS paper

Overall, this paper makes a solid contribution to the field of generative models by extending moment-matching networks to conditional distributions. The theoretical development is sound, the experimental results are promising, and the approach has clear practical value. While the paper has some limitations in comparison with the most relevant prior work and theoretical analysis, the contribution is significant and well-executed. The paper meets the quality, clarity, originality, and significance criteria for NIPS.
