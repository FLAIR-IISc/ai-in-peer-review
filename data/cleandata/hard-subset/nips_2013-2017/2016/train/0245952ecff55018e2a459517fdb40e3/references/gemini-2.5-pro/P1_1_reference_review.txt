### **Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"**

#### **Summary of the Paper**

This paper introduces the Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Network (CGMMN), a deep generative model for learning conditional distributions `p(y|x)`. This work extends the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN), which models joint distributions, to the conditional setting. The core contributions are twofold:

1.  **A new objective function, Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD):** The authors propose a principled metric to measure the discrepancy between two conditional distributions. This metric is defined as the Hilbert-Schmidt norm between their respective conditional kernel mean embedding operators. This is a natural extension of the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) used in the original GMMN, building directly upon the theoretical framework of conditional kernel embeddings developed by Song et al. (2009).

2.  **A deep generative model trained with CMMD:** The paper proposes a generator network that takes the conditioning variable `x` and a random noise vector `h` as input to produce a sample `y`. The network's parameters are learned by minimizing the CMMD between the distribution of generated samples and the true data distribution.

The paper successfully situates this work in the context of other deep generative models like Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and their conditional variants, positioning CGMMN as an alternative that avoids the complexities of min-max adversarial training. The authors demonstrate the model's versatility through a comprehensive set of experiments, including predictive modeling on MNIST/SVHN, contextual image generation on MNIST and Yale Faces, and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

#### **Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:**
*   **Strengths:** The paper is technically sound. The proposed CMMD objective is well-founded in the theory of reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces and provides a solid foundation for the model. The experimental evaluation is strong and diverse, covering three distinct problem domains. The results are compelling, showing that CGMMN is competitive with other state-of-the-art deep generative models like CMMVA on prediction tasks and is capable of producing high-quality conditional samples. The application to "Bayesian dark knowledge" is a particularly interesting demonstration of the model's flexibility.
*   **Weaknesses:** A potential weakness lies in the scalability of the CMMD objective. The computation in Equation (2) requires inverting kernel matrices of size `(batch_size x batch_size)`, which has a time complexity of `O(batch_size³)`. While the authors use a mini-batch approach, this could become a significant bottleneck for large batch sizes, potentially limiting its application compared to models like Conditional GANs. A discussion of this limitation and comparison to linear-time MMD estimators would strengthen the paper.

**Clarity:**
*   **Strengths:** The paper is very well-written and organized. The introduction provides excellent motivation for conditional modeling. The background section on MMD and conditional kernel embeddings is clear and essential for understanding the main contribution. The overall generative process and training algorithm are explained effectively.
*   **Weaknesses:** The derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator in Equation (2) from the Hilbert-Schmidt norm definition is not fully expanded. While likely straightforward for experts in kernel methods, including a more detailed derivation in the appendix would improve the paper's clarity and reproducibility for a broader audience. Remark 1 draws an interesting parallel between MMD and CMMD objectives, but this insight could be slightly more formalized to make the connection clearer.

**Originality:**
*   **Strengths:** The main contribution—the CMMD objective for training a deep generative model—is novel and significant. While the architecture of the generator is standard for conditional models, the formulation of a moment-matching loss for the conditional setting is a new and valuable contribution. It successfully fills a gap by extending the GMMN framework, providing a non-adversarial alternative to Conditional GANs. This work clearly distinguishes itself from prior work by being the first to combine conditional kernel embeddings with a deep generative network for this purpose.
*   **Weaknesses:** As mentioned, the network architecture itself is not novel. The originality is almost entirely concentrated in the loss function.

**Significance:**
*   **Strengths:** This work is highly significant. It provides the community with a new, principled, and stable method for training conditional deep generative models. Given the training challenges often associated with GANs, this alternative is very welcome. The demonstrated success across prediction, generation, and model distillation tasks suggests that CGMMN could become a widely used tool for both researchers and practitioners. By extending moment-matching networks to the conditional case, the paper greatly expands the applicability of this entire class of models.
*   **Weaknesses:** For purely predictive tasks, the results show that CGMMN, like other generative models, is outperformed by purely discriminative deep networks (e.g., DSN). This is an expected trade-off but limits its appeal for applications where only state-of-the-art predictive accuracy is required.

#### **Arguments for and against acceptance**

**Pros:**
*   Introduces a novel, theoretically sound, and well-motivated objective function (CMMD) for training conditional generative models.
*   Provides a stable, non-adversarial training framework, which is a significant advantage over the often-unstable training of Conditional GANs.
*   The model's effectiveness and versatility are demonstrated convincingly through a wide range of experiments on diverse tasks.
*   The paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and properly contextualized within existing literature.

**Cons:**
*   The computational complexity of the CMMD objective may pose scalability challenges for very large batch sizes.
*   Predictive performance, while competitive for a generative model, does not surpass state-of-the-art discriminative approaches.

#### **Conclusion and Recommendation**

This is a high-quality paper presenting a novel and significant contribution to the field of deep generative models. The development of the Conditional MMD objective is a logical and powerful extension of existing work, leading to a flexible and stable framework for conditional density estimation. The thorough experimental validation underscores the model's practical utility. The weaknesses identified are primarily limitations in scope or scalability rather than fundamental flaws in the methodology. Overall, this paper represents a solid advancement and is a clear candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
