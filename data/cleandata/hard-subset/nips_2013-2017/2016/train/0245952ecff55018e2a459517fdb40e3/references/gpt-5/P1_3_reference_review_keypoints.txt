- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN): deep conditional generator y = f(x,h;w) trained by minimizing conditional MMD (CMMD) via kernel conditional mean embeddings.
- CMMD objective is the squared Hilbert–Schmidt distance between empirical conditional mean operators estimated from real and generated samples, with gradients backpropagated through output Gram matrices.
- Practical training notes: kernels on x need not be differentiable; minibatch algorithm used to avoid O(n^3) scaling.
- Empirical applications: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), class- and identity-conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston housing).
- Related work: extends GMMN using conditional embeddings; compared to cGAN/CVAE as a non-adversarial, non-likelihood alternative.
- Strength: conceptually clean extension of GMMN to conditional setting using established conditional mean embeddings.
- Strength: relatively simple training procedure with a practical observation that K̃^{-1} on X is constant w.r.t. generator parameters.
- Strength: demonstrated broad applicability across supervised prediction, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation with MLP and CNN backbones.
- Strength: theoretical grounding linking CMMD operator equality to equality of conditional distributions in universal RKHSs.
- Strength: practical additions like minibatch training, noise-injection strategies, and combining with autoencoders to improve samples.
- Weakness: limited quantitative evaluation of generative quality—no standard metrics or comparisons to cGAN/CVAE.
- Weakness: predictive results are competitive but not state-of-the-art and unclear advantages over strong discriminative baselines.
- Weakness: scalability and statistical bias of minibatch CMMD are not analyzed; alternatives like random features or Nyström are not explored.
- Weakness: kernel choices, bandwidth selection, and centering are under-specified despite their importance in high-dimensional outputs.
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees lack analysis of consistency/sample complexity for parametric generators and effects of regularized operators.
- Weakness: Bayesian distillation experiment is limited (small dataset) and lacks uncertainty calibration or clear test-time sampling protocol.
- Quality: derivation follows standard kernel embedding literature and is technically sound, but important implementation details and empirical analysis are missing.
- Clarity: generally well organized but contains typos, minor inconsistencies, and lacks explicit experimental hyperparameters for reproducibility.
- Originality: incremental but meaningful novelty by combining conditional mean embeddings with deep generators and practical CMMD training.
- Significance: offers a non-adversarial conditional modeling alternative that could be impactful if it scales and matches cGAN/CVAE performance.
- Questions for authors: which kernels/bandwidths/centering were used, sensitivity to kernel/λ, computational/memory costs and tricks for B^3 inversions, test-time sampling protocol and calibration, and how x’s are chosen for conditional generation.
- Suggestions: add quantitative generative evaluations and cGAN/CVAE comparisons, ablations on kernels and batch sizes, consider random Fourier features for scaling, report stds and hyperparams, explore richer conditioning and latent dimensionality, and expand Bayesian distillation experiments.
- Pros for acceptance: principled, simple conditional extension of GMMN with broad applicability and a stable training objective that may interest the community.
- Cons for acceptance: limited empirical evaluation, missing kernel/implementation details, shallow scalability analysis, and incremental novelty.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept—clean and potentially useful idea but needs stronger empirical validation and more implementation detail.
