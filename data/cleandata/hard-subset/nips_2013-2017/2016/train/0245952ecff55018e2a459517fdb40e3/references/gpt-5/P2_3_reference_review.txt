Summary and relation to prior work
The paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions p(y|x). The key contribution is the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), which measures the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators CY|X in RKHS, and a practical stochastic gradient training procedure for a conditional deep generator f(x, h; w) driven by noise h. The authors derive a closed-form CMMD estimator in terms of kernel Gram matrices (with Tikhonov-regularized inverses on the x-kernel), and show how to train via backpropagation using generated samples. They evaluate on: (1) predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN) by learning y|x; (2) conditional generation (MNIST digits given labels; Yale Faces given identity), including an AE+CGMMN variant; and (3) distilling Bayesian predictive distributions (PBP on Boston housing).

This builds on GMMN (Li et al., ICML’15) using MMD for unconditional matching, and on kernel embeddings of conditional distributions (Song et al., ICML’09; Grunewalder et al., ICML’12). It is a counterpart to conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and conditional VAEs (Sohn et al., NIPS’15), offering a simpler, non-adversarial loss. The AE+GMMN idea mirrors prior GMMN+AE work. The Bayesian distillation task relates to Korattikara et al. (NIPS’15).

Quality
- Theory: The CMMD objective is well-motivated via conditional mean embeddings. Theorem 3 (equality of operators implies equality of conditionals) reflects known properties under universality and smoothness assumptions; stating it as a sufficient condition is appropriate. The empirical operator estimator Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤ is standard; using the Hilbert–Schmidt norm yields a tractable trace expression. The discussion acknowledges the required assumptions and regularization.
- Optimization: The gradient flows through kernels on y only (since K̃−1 on x is parameter-independent), enabling flexible kernels on x. The mini-batch CMMD reduces cubic costs; however, the statistical properties (bias/variance of the mini-batch estimator) are not analyzed. Practical choices (kernel types, bandwidth selection, λ) are not fully specified in the main text.
- Empirics: The experiments are diverse and consistent with claims: CGMMN-CNN is competitive with MMVA/CMMVA on MNIST/SVHN (though below strong discriminative baselines), conditional samples look reasonable, and distillation preserves RMSE on Boston housing. However, several weaknesses reduce the evidential strength:
  - For conditional generation, there is no quantitative comparison to conditional GAN or conditional VAE; evaluation is mostly qualitative with nearest-neighbor checks. No likelihood proxies or modern image metrics (understandable for the time, but some quantitative measure would help).
  - For classification, using CMMD to match one-hot conditional distributions is an unusual choice versus cross-entropy; ablations comparing to a purely discriminative network with the same architecture would better contextualize performance.
  - Sensitivity to kernel choice/bandwidth and to λ is not explored; reproducibility details (kernels on x and y, bandwidth selection rule, regularization) are missing from the main text.
  - Computational costs of CMMD (per batch) and scalability to large-condition settings are not reported.

Clarity
The paper is generally clear and well-organized: background on MMD and conditional embeddings, derivation of CMMD, architecture and algorithm, then applications. The trace-form expression for CMMD is useful. Some notational choices (switching s/d superscripts for “source/data” vs “generated”) are mildly confusing. Important implementation details (kernel choices, bandwidths, λ, data perturbation scheme for distillation) are deferred or absent. There are minor typos and reference formatting issues.

Originality
The core idea—training a conditional deep generator by matching conditional mean embeddings via a CMMD loss—appears novel relative to GMMN and prior conditional embedding literature. It is a natural but nontrivial extension that yields a new practical objective. The AE+CGMMN variant is closely related to prior GMMN+AE. The Bayesian distillation use case is a nice, relatively new angle at the time.

Significance
The work provides a stable, simple alternative to adversarial training for conditional generation and a principled way to match conditionals without likelihoods. It broadens the applicability of MMD-based generative training to supervised/conditional settings and to distillation. While the empirical advances over strong baselines are modest, the method is likely to be of interest to researchers who prefer moment-matching to adversarial losses and to those using kernel embeddings. The lack of stronger quantitative comparisons on generation and limited analysis of design choices temper the immediate impact.

Strengths
- Conceptually clean extension of GMMN to conditionals with a principled CMMD objective.
- Simple training via backprop; no adversarial min–max.
- Flexible choice of kernels on x, and differentiable kernels on y.
- Diverse applications (classification, conditional generation, Bayesian distillation).
- Competitive predictive performance with prior DGM baselines; plausible conditional samples.

Weaknesses
- Limited quantitative evaluation for generation; no direct comparison to cGAN/cVAE.
- Missing details on kernel choices, bandwidth tuning, and sensitivity; no analysis of mini-batch CMMD estimator properties.
- Computational complexity discussion is brief; scalability to large batches/continuous, high-dim x unclear.
- For classification, benefits over standard cross-entropy training are not clearly demonstrated.

Questions/requests for authors
- Specify the kernels used on x and y, bandwidth selection (e.g., median heuristic?), and λ values. Provide sensitivity plots if possible.
- Report CMMD computation time per batch and how batch size affects stability/performance.
- Include quantitative generation metrics (e.g., class-conditional Parzen, classification accuracy of a pretrained classifier on generated digits) and comparisons to cGAN/cVAE.
- For classification, compare to an identical architecture trained with cross-entropy to isolate the contribution of CMMD.

Recommendation: Weak accept
Rationale: The paper introduces a principled and practically usable CMMD objective for training conditional deep generators, with solid theoretical grounding and a breadth of applications. While the empirical section could be stronger, the contribution is clear and likely to be useful. Strengthening comparisons and adding missing implementation details would further improve the paper.
