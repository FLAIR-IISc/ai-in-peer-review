- Overall: Proposes Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) using CMMD for conditional generation; promising experiments but incremental novelty; recommend accept with minor revisions.  
- Motivation: Addresses conditional generation limitations of joint modeling and situates work within GANs/VAEs and kernel/MMD literature.  
- Methodology: Uses CMMD as a principled training objective for conditional generative modeling.  
- Versatility: Framework applies to diverse tasks and multiple types of conditional variables (labels, inputs).  
- Performance: Experimental results are competitive with GANs and other complex models on benchmark tasks.  
- Theoretical grounding: Leverages kernel methods and provides mathematical justification (e.g., Theorem 3).  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with clear and consistent notation.  
- Novelty concern: Extension from GMMN to conditional setting is incremental compared to conditional GANs and VAEs; need clearer differentiation and advantages.  
- Architecture justification: Insufficient theoretical/empirical rationale for chosen MLP/CNN architectures and low-dimensional hidden spaces.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lacks analysis on regularization (λ) and other hyperparameter selection and their effects.  
- CMMD analysis: Limited discussion of CMMD’s limitations, failure modes, and comparison to other conditional discrepancy measures.  
- Ablation studies: More ablations needed to quantify contributions of components (e.g., uniform prior on hidden variables).  
- Equation (2): Explanation of kernel matrices (Kd, Ks, Ld, Ls, Ksd, Lds) is dense and needs clearer, intuitive descriptions.  
- Bayesian dark knowledge: Section 4.3 is brief; needs more detail on why CGMMN suits distillation from Bayesian models.  
- Generated samples and evaluation: Figures show varying sample quality; authors should provide more samples and quantitative metrics (e.g., FID).  
- Mini-batch algorithm: Alg.1 is useful but should include discussion of computational costs of estimating CMMD in mini-batches.  
- Pros for acceptance: Strong theoretical basis, versatility, competitive results, and clear presentation.  
- Cons against acceptance: Incremental novelty, weak architectural justification, and lack of detailed CMMD and hyperparameter analyses.  
- Required revisions: Clarify novelty vs. CGANs/CVAEs, justify architectures and hyperparameters, analyze CMMD properties/limitations, and add comparative results against CGANs.
