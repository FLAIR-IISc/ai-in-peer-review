## Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a conceptually sound extension of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting (CGMMN). The approach leverages the simplicity of MMD-based learning and applies it to a wider range of tasks. While the technical contribution is incremental, the paper is well-written, clearly explains the method, and demonstrates competitive performance on several benchmarks. I believe it meets the criteria for acceptance, leaning towards a **weak accept**.

**1. Summary & Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper addresses the need for conditional generative models, which are often more practical than joint models in tasks like prediction, contextual generation, and building complex probabilistic systems. It builds upon the recent work on GMMNs [19, 3] which offered a simpler alternative to GANs by using MMD for learning. The authors propose CGMMN, which extends this framework to model conditional distributions p(y|x) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  The paper correctly positions itself within the landscape of generative modeling, referencing GANs [5], VAEs [14], and other related work such as conditional GANs [21] and sum-product networks [24]. 

**2. Strengths:**

*   **Conceptual Simplicity & Soundness:** The core idea of extending GMMNs to the conditional setting using CMMD is intuitive and logically sound.  The formulation builds directly on established theory (kernel embeddings, MMD) and avoids the complexities of adversarial training.
*   **Clear Explanation:** The paper is very well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical derivations are clear, and the explanation of CMMD is well-motivated.  The figures (especially Fig. 1) are helpful.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The authors evaluate CGMMN on a diverse set of tasks: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The results are competitive with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly considering the relative simplicity of the approach.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The inclusion of theorems and clear explanation of the underlying principles (Hilbert space embedding, MMD) strengthens the credibility of the approach.
*   **Mini-batch Algorithm:** Addressing the computational cost of the CMMD objective with a mini-batch algorithm (Algorithm 1) demonstrates awareness of practical considerations.

**3. Weaknesses:**

*   **Incremental Contribution:** While the extension to the conditional setting is a natural one, the technical innovation is somewhat incremental. The CMMD objective is a relatively straightforward adaptation of MMD, and the overall architecture is standard.  The paper doesn't present a fundamentally new theoretical insight.
*   **Limited Novelty in Architectures:** The network architectures used (MLP, CNN) are fairly standard and lack significant novelty. While the performance is competitive, it isn’t demonstrably *better* than existing approaches using more sophisticated architectures.
*   **Lack of Ablation Studies:** The paper lacks detailed ablation studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of specific components of the CGMMN architecture or the CMMD objective.  For example, how sensitive is performance to the choice of kernel or the regularization parameter λ?
*   **Limited Analysis of Conditional Distributions:** While the paper *models* conditional distributions, there's limited discussion about the properties or challenges inherent in learning these distributions specifically.
*   **Dependence on Existing Methods:** The Bayesian dark knowledge experiment relies heavily on the performance of the pre-trained PBP model, and the contribution of CGMMN in that scenario is less clear.



**4. Addressing the Review Criteria:**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. Claims are supported by experiments, although further analysis (ablation studies) would strengthen the argument. It’s a complete piece of work for a conference paper.
*   **Clarity:** Excellent. The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to understand.
*   **Originality:** Moderate. The core idea is a logical extension of existing work, but the implementation and empirical validation are solid.
*   **Significance:** Moderate.  CGMMN provides a valuable addition to the toolbox of generative modeling techniques. Its simplicity and competitive performance make it a potentially useful approach for practitioners, but it doesn't represent a major breakthrough.

**5. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

*   Clear and well-motivated approach.
*   Competitive experimental results on diverse tasks.
*   Solid theoretical foundation.
*   Addresses a practical need for conditional generative models.

**Con:**

*   Incremental technical contribution.
*   Lack of ablation studies.
*   Limited novelty in architecture.




**Final Recommendation:** **Weak Accept**.  

I recommend accepting this paper because it presents a well-executed and theoretically sound extension of GMMNs. While the technical innovation is modest, the clarity of the presentation and the competitive empirical results justify its inclusion in NIPS. However, I encourage the authors to consider incorporating more thorough ablation studies and a more in-depth analysis of the conditional distributions learned by the model in future work.
