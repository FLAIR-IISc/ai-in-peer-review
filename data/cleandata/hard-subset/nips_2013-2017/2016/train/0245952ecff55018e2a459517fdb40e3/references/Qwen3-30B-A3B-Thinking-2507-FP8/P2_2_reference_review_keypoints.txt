- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective and a conditional generator taking both conditions and noise.
- Theoretical foundation is sound with clear derivation of CMMD and its relation to conditional distributions (Theorems 1–3).
- Practical implementation of CMMD is described, but lacks detailed implementation specifics (kernels, regularization, computational complexity).
- Experiments cover predictive modeling, conditional image generation, and distilling Bayesian models, showing competitive performance across tasks.
- Comparison baselines are generally appropriate, but omission of Conditional GANs (the primary competitor) is a significant gap.
- Experimental scope is limited (Bayesian dark knowledge only on one regression task) and lacks training curves, convergence/stability analysis, and ablation studies.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written with consistent notation and a motivating introduction.
- Some technical sections (kernel embedding of conditional distributions) need more intuition and a diagram of the CGMMN architecture would help.
- Novelty is incremental: natural extension of GMMN to conditional settings with a sound CMMD contribution but not strongly differentiated from Conditional GANs/VAEs.
- Paper does not clearly articulate advantages over existing conditional generative models (stability, efficiency, sample quality).
- Significance is moderate: useful problem and competitive results, but not shown to advance state-of-the-art convincingly.
- Strengths: solid theoretical basis, clear presentation, competitive empirical results, and practical applications.
- Weaknesses: missing comparison to Conditional GANs, insufficient implementation/hyperparameter details, limited experiments, and no analysis of training dynamics.
- Recommendation: Weak accept (score 6) contingent on addressing comparisons, implementation details, clearer advantages, and expanded experiments.
