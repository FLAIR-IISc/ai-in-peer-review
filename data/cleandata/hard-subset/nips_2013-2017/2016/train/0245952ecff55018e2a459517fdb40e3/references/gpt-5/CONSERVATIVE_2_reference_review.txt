Summary
The paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of GMMN to conditional distributions. The model draws a noise vector h from a simple prior, concatenates it with the conditioning variable x, and maps through a deep network to produce y. Training minimizes a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) objective: the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical conditional embedding operators estimated from data and from the model, using kernel mean embeddings and the operator estimator Φ(K+λI)^{-1}Υ^T. The paper provides a sufficiency theorem that equality of conditional embedding operators implies equality of the conditional distributions. Empirically, CGMMN is evaluated on (i) classification on MNIST and SVHN, (ii) conditional generation on MNIST and Yale Faces, including a variant that trains in an autoencoder code space, and (iii) distilling “Bayesian dark knowledge” on Boston Housing by matching a Bayesian teacher’s predictive distribution.

Relation to prior work
The work builds on GMMN (Li et al., ICML’15) and kernel mean embeddings/MMD (Gretton et al.). The conditional embedding operator and its empirical estimator have been extensively developed in the kernel literature (Song et al., 2009; Grunewälder et al., 2012). The proposed CMMD objective is a natural extension aligning conditional operators rather than marginal means, and is related to conditional embedding regression. The application to deep conditional generators parallels conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero) and conditional VAEs (Sohn et al.), and the “GMMN+AE” variant mirrors GMMN+AE. Distillation relates to Korattikara et al. (Bayesian dark knowledge) and Hernandez-Lobato & Adams (PBP).

Strengths
- Conceptually clean: CMMD is a straightforward and principled extension of MMD to conditional settings via conditional mean embeddings.
- Simple training: minibatch-based backprop with an explicit, non-adversarial objective.
- Generality: applies to prediction, conditional generation, and teacher–student distillation; supports various deep backbones (MLP, CNN).
- Empirical results are reasonable: competitive MNIST/SVHN classification for a generative framework; visually plausible conditional samples; Boston Housing RMSE on par with PBP.

Weaknesses
- Novelty is incremental: the conditional operator and its empirical estimator are established; the main novelty is using the operator norm as a loss for deep conditional generators. Comparisons to CVAE and cGAN are missing, despite being the most relevant baselines.
- Limited empirical rigor:
  - Classification: no comparison to standard cross-entropy training on the same architectures; no calibration/NLL; unclear kernel choice on the output space y and its impact; turning a real-valued y into an argmax label is ad hoc.
  - Generation: only qualitative visuals; no quantitative metrics (e.g., Parzen log-likelihood as used in GMMN, Inception-type scores, conditional accuracy).
  - Distillation: only a single small dataset (Boston); no negative log predictive density or uncertainty calibration; no speed/latency numbers to demonstrate practical benefit of the distilled student.
- Scalability and computation: CMMD entails inverting K+λI per batch; complexity and memory overhead are not analyzed; sensitivity to batch size, kernel bandwidth, and λ is not studied.
- Theory is modest: Theorem 3 gives a sufficiency statement under strong assumptions; there is no consistency/generalization analysis of minimizing empirical CMMD to recover p(y|x) with finite samples and regularization.
- Important details are underspecified: kernel choices for X and Y, bandwidth selection, regularization, stability tricks, and how x is perturbed when sampling for CMMD are not clearly described. For discrete labels as X (in generation), the special-case estimator is mentioned but not detailed.

Quality
The method is technically sound at a high level, but the empirical evidence is not strong enough to demonstrate clear advantages over established conditional generative methods. Ablation studies and sensitivity analyses are missing. The evaluation metrics for generation and uncertainty are insufficient, and key baselines are absent.

Clarity
The paper is generally readable and well-structured. However, notational overload between s/d datasets and occasional inconsistencies could be improved. More implementation details (kernels, hyperparameters, bandwidth selection, computational cost) are needed for reproducibility.

Originality
Moderate. The core building blocks (conditional embedding, operator estimator, MMD training of generators) are known. Framing CMMD as a training loss for deep conditional generators is a reasonable but incremental step. The combination with AE and Bayesian distillation follows existing lines.

Significance
Currently limited. The method is simple and may be of practical interest, but the lack of strong empirical evidence against cGAN/CVAE or discriminative baselines, and the absence of quantitative sample quality/uncertainty metrics, weakens the impact.

Questions and requests for the authors
1. Baselines: Please compare against CVAE and cGAN on conditional generation and against cross-entropy-trained CNNs on classification, using identical backbones.
2. Kernels: Specify kernels for X and Y, bandwidth selection (median heuristic or tuned on validation), and report sensitivity analyses and performance variability w.r.t. λ and batch size.
3. Complexity: Provide per-epoch training time, memory footprint, and scaling behavior with batch size for CMMD. How do you stabilize K+λI inversions?
4. Metrics: Add quantitative generation metrics and conditional consistency checks (e.g., a pretrained classifier’s accuracy on generated samples). For distillation, report NLPD/calibration and inference-time speedup.
5. Theory: Can you provide any guarantee relating empirical CMMD minimization to excess risk in conditional density estimation, or at least a consistency result under standard assumptions?
6. Output space: For classification, what kernel is used on y? Is y constrained to the simplex? Why is CMMD preferable to cross-entropy in this setting?

Pros
- Clear, principled objective; avoids adversarial training instability.
- Flexible conditional generator applicable across tasks.
- Competitive results for a generative approach; simple implementation.

Cons
- Missing key baselines and metrics; limited and small-scale experiments.
- Incremental novelty relative to conditional embedding literature and existing conditional deep generators.
- Computational and hyperparameter sensitivity not addressed.
- Theoretical guarantees are minimal.

Recommendation
Reject. While the idea is neat and potentially useful, the contribution over prior work is incremental and the experimental validation falls short of NeurIPS standards, particularly given the lack of comparisons to CVAE/cGAN, limited quantitative evaluation, and absence of analysis on scalability and sensitivity. Strengthening the empirical section with rigorous baselines, metrics, and ablations, and adding clearer implementation details and some consistency analysis, could make a future version much more compelling.

Confidence
High.
