- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional MMD (CMMD) to match conditional kernel mean embeddings via a deep generator y = f(x,h;w).  
- CMMD objective is the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical conditional embedding operators, computed from Gram matrices on X and Y.  
- Training uses backprop with minibatches by drawing model samples conditioned on inputs.  
- Builds on prior work: GMMN/GMMN+AE, conditional mean embeddings (Song et al.), and conditional generative models (cGAN, CVAE).  
- Technical novelty: applies conditional mean embeddings to deep conditional generation yielding a simple, non-adversarial objective.  
- Theoretical grounding: CMMD formulation and empirical gradients are consistent with conditional mean embedding theory; Theorem 3 gives a correct sufficient condition under standard assumptions.  
- Regularization via (K + λI)^{-1} is acknowledged and used in practice.  
- Practical concern: per-minibatch inversion of Gram matrices is O(b^3), raising scalability issues for large batches.  
- Paper mentions a minibatch approximation but provides limited analysis of its bias or stability.  
- Kernel choices and bandwidth selection (on X and Y) are crucial but under-specified and not sensitivity-analyzed.  
- Gaussian kernels on high-dimensional Y (images) can be brittle; autoencoder codes help but quantitative evaluation is lacking.  
- Prediction experiments are competitive with some deep generative baselines but lag behind discriminative CNNs.  
- Conditional generation results are qualitative only, lacking comparisons to cGAN/CVAE and quantitative metrics.  
- Distillation experiment is small-scale (Boston Housing) and reports only RMSE without calibration, likelihood, or runtime analysis.  
- Claims of broad applicability are supported, but claims of superiority are not; authors position CGMMN as competitive rather than SOTA.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with concise reviews of MMD and conditional embeddings and clear CMMD derivation.  
- Missing details: kernel types/bandwidths, preprocessing, training specifics (noise dim, concatenation/corruption, Y kernel choices).  
- Would benefit from discussion of computational approximations (Nyström, random features, multi-kernel MMD) for reproducibility.  
- Originality is moderate: meaningful incremental extension combining known tools; CMMD for deep conditional generation appears novel but theory is mostly adaptations.  
- Significance is moderate: offers a stable alternative to adversarial training but empirical gains and scalability remain unproven.  
- Strengths: conceptually clean extension, simple non-adversarial training, solid kernel-theory grounding, and plausible conditional samples.  
- Weaknesses: missing strong baselines/metrics, limited kernel/bandwidth analysis, scalability concerns unaddressed, sparse distillation eval, and modest theoretical novelty.  
- Key questions for authors: specifics and sensitivity of kernel/bandwidth choices; quantitative generation metrics vs cGAN/CVAE; minibatch scaling and approximation methods; classification encoding/calibration; distillation NLL/calibration and speed gains.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled objective, broad task applicability, and relevance to kernel embeddings and generative modeling communities.  
- Cons for acceptance: incomplete empirical evaluation, lack of kernel and scalability discussion, and incremental theoretical contribution.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject — idea is appealing and sound but needs stronger baselines, metrics, and scalability analysis to justify acceptance.
