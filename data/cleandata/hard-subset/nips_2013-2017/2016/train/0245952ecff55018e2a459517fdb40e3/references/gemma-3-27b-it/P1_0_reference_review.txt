## Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of the recently proposed Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional generation setting. The core idea is to learn a conditional distribution p(y|x) by training a deep network to map a sample from a simple distribution, concatenated with the conditioning variable 'x', to a sample 'y'.  The training objective is a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), which measures the difference between the kernel mean embeddings of the empirical conditional distribution and the model's generative distribution. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks, achieving competitive results on various datasets.

**Relation to Prior Work:**

The paper builds upon the growing body of work in deep generative modeling, particularly GMMN, GANs, and VAEs. It correctly positions itself within this landscape, acknowledging the challenges of GAN training and the strengths of MMD-based approaches like GMMN.  The work acknowledges and builds upon previous efforts in conditional GANs and VAEs, highlighting the benefit of its simpler training objective compared to GANs, and its flexibility in handling conditional variables. The connection to kernel methods and MMD is also well established, citing relevant work by Gretton et al. 

**Strengths:**

* **Conceptual Simplicity & Elegance:** The CGMMN framework is conceptually straightforward and elegant. Extending GMMN to the conditional setting with CMMD is a logical and effective approach.
* **Training Stability:**  The MMD-based objective is generally easier to optimize than the min-max game in GANs, leading to more stable training.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a clear theoretical justification for CMMD, grounding it in kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings. Theorems 3 and 2 are helpful in establishing the validity of the approach.
* **Versatility:**  The framework is demonstrated to be versatile, applicable to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.
* **Competitive Performance:**  The experimental results show that CGMMN achieves competitive performance on a diverse range of tasks, often comparable to state-of-the-art methods.
* **Clear Explanation:** The paper is generally well-written and provides a clear explanation of the method and experimental setup. The use of a diagram (Fig. 1) is helpful.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of MMD-based methods (including CGMMN) can be sensitive to the choice of kernel and its parameters (e.g., bandwidth). The paper does not thoroughly discuss the sensitivity of the results to these hyperparameters. While lambda is mentioned for regularization, a broader discussion would be helpful.
* **Computational Cost:** Computing the kernel matrices, particularly for larger datasets, can be computationally expensive. While the paper mentions a mini-batch approach (Algorithm 1), a more detailed discussion of the computational complexity and potential scalability issues would be beneficial.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:** The ablation studies are relatively limited. For example, in the MNIST experiments, the impact of different network architectures (e.g., varying the number of hidden layers or units) on performance is not thoroughly investigated.
* **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** While the comparisons are adequate, including results against more recent and powerful generative models (e.g., more advanced GAN variants, normalizing flows) might further strengthen the evaluation.
* **Lack of Qualitative Examples (Yale Face):** The qualitative results presented for the Yale Face dataset are lacking. Showing generated samples would be very informative.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and conceptually sound extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
* The CMMD objective is theoretically well-motivated and provides a stable training signal.
* The experimental results demonstrate the versatility and competitive performance of CGMMN.
* The paper is clearly written and well-organized.
* The work addresses an important problem in deep generative modeling and has the potential to be influential.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The computational cost of CMMD may limit its scalability to very large datasets.
* The paper lacks a thorough investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity and the impact of architectural choices.
* Comparisons to stronger baseline generative models are missing.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this is a solid contribution to the field of deep generative modeling.  The CGMMN framework offers a compelling alternative to GANs and VAEs for conditional generation, with the benefit of a simpler and more stable training process.  While there are some weaknesses related to computational cost and hyperparameter sensitivity, these do not outweigh the strengths of the work. 

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.**  I recommend accepting the paper, contingent on the authors addressing the following points:

1.  **Discuss Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the results to the choice of kernel and its parameters.
2. **Computational Analysis:**  Provide a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of CMMD and discuss potential strategies for improving scalability.
3. **Extended Ablation Studies:**  Conduct more comprehensive ablation studies to investigate the impact of different network architectures and hyperparameters.
4. **Qualitative Results:** Include visual examples of generated samples for the Yale Face dataset.
5. **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** If feasible, add a comparison to more recent and powerful generative models.
