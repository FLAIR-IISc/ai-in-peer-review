

# Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to conditional distributions. The authors propose Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a training objective for conditional generative models, which allows the model to learn flexible conditional distributions when some input variables are given. CGMMN follows a simple generative process: draw a sample from a simple distribution, pass it along with conditional variables through a deep network to generate target samples, and optimize parameters using CMMD.

The paper builds on the GMMN framework (Li et al., 2015) and extends it to conditional settings, which is a natural progression. The authors position their work as filling a "research void" in moment-matching networks, but this claim is somewhat overstated given existing work on conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and conditional VAEs (So et al., 2015).

**Strengths:**
- The theoretical foundation is sound, with clear development of CMMD and its relationship to MMD.
- The paper demonstrates the approach across diverse applications: predictive modeling (MNIST/SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST/Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- The implementation is straightforward, leveraging backpropagation without complex optimization schemes.
- The empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods on the tested tasks.

**Weaknesses:**
- The novelty is limited. Conditional generative models have been extensively explored in prior work (conditional GANs, conditional VAEs), and the paper doesn't convincingly demonstrate how CGMMN offers a fundamentally different or superior approach.
- The comparison with state-of-the-art methods is insufficient. The paper shows competitive results but doesn't demonstrate clear advantages over conditional GANs or VAEs, which are the dominant approaches for conditional generation.
- The paper's claim that "CGMMN largely extends the capability of GMMN" is not strongly supported. The extension to conditional settings is logical but not particularly innovative.
- The experimental section lacks depth in analyzing why CGMMN performs as it does compared to alternatives, and the theoretical justification for why CMMD might outperform other conditional criteria is underdeveloped.

**Originality (3/5):** The paper presents a reasonable extension of GMMN to conditional settings, but this is not a significant new direction in the field. The core idea of using MMD for generative modeling is already established, and the conditional extension follows naturally from existing work. While CMMD is a specific technical contribution, it doesn't represent a bold new approach that would significantly shift the field's direction.

**Significance (3/5):** The paper demonstrates the approach on multiple tasks, but the significance is limited because the improvements appear incremental. For predictive modeling, CGMMN-CNN achieves competitive results with state-of-the-art methods, but doesn't show clear advantages over conditional GANs or VAEs. For generation tasks, the results are reasonable but not groundbreaking. The Bayesian dark knowledge application is interesting but not sufficiently explored to demonstrate significant impact.

**Quality (4/5):** The paper is technically sound, with clear theoretical development and thorough experimental evaluation. The methodology is well-justified, and the implementation details are provided. However, the paper could have better addressed the limitations of their approach and provided more rigorous comparisons with the most relevant baselines.

**Clarity (4/5):** The paper is well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the theoretical concepts. The experimental setup is described thoroughly, making it reproducible for experts.

**Overall Assessment:**
This paper presents a reasonable extension of GMMN to conditional settings using CMMD. While the approach is technically sound and demonstrates competitive performance across multiple tasks, the novelty is limited compared to existing work on conditional generative models. The paper doesn't offer a compelling argument for why CMMD is fundamentally better than other conditional generative approaches, and the results don't demonstrate clear superiority over state-of-the-art methods.

As a reviewer who prioritizes novel ideas over methodological perfection, I find this paper's contribution to be incremental rather than bold. The extension to conditional distributions is logical but not sufficiently innovative to justify acceptance at NIPS, especially when compared to other recent work on conditional generative models that has already established strong baselines.

**Recommendation:** Reject

The paper would be strengthened by either: (1) demonstrating clear advantages over existing conditional generative approaches that go beyond the tested baselines, or (2) presenting a more fundamental theoretical insight that fundamentally changes how we think about conditional generative modeling. As it stands, the paper makes a reasonable but incremental contribution to a well-trodden area.
