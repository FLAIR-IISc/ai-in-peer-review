

# Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to conditional distributions. The authors develop Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a training objective for conditional generative models, enabling the model to learn flexible conditional distributions p(y|x) through a simple generative process (passing conditional inputs x and random noise h through a deep network to generate y). The paper demonstrates CGMMN across three application domains: predictive modeling (classification on MNIST and SVHN), contextual image generation (conditioned on labels), and distilling Bayesian models for efficient prediction. This work extends Li et al.'s GMMN (ICML 2015) and relates to conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and kernel embedding of conditional distributions (Song et al., ICML 2013).

## Strengths
1. **Technical Soundness**: The paper provides a solid theoretical foundation for CMMD and its relationship to kernel embedding of conditional distributions. The authors carefully justify the mathematical derivations and address the smoothness assumptions required for the CMMD objective to be well-defined.

2. **Clarity and Organization**: The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The mathematical formalism is presented logically, and the experimental setup is thoroughly described. The figures (particularly Fig. 2-5) effectively illustrate the generative capabilities of the model.

3. **Practical Utility**: The simplicity of the training process (using backpropagation with CMMD) is a significant advantage over more complex conditional generative models. The paper demonstrates that CGMMN can achieve competitive performance across multiple tasks without requiring complex training procedures.

4. **Broad Applicability**: The framework is successfully applied to diverse tasks (classification, image generation, Bayesian distillation), demonstrating the versatility of the approach. The Bayesian dark knowledge application is particularly novel and well-motivated.

## Weaknesses
1. **Limited Theoretical Analysis**: While the paper provides a good theoretical foundation, it lacks deeper analysis of convergence properties or generalization bounds for the CGMMN framework. It would be valuable to understand how the CMMD objective relates to other conditional generative objectives (e.g., conditional GANs) in terms of statistical guarantees.

2. **Inadequate Comparison with State-of-the-Art**: The paper compares CGMMN to several baselines but doesn't provide a thorough comparison with recent state-of-the-art conditional generative models. For example, the results on image generation don't compare to conditional GANs (which have been highly successful in this domain), making it difficult to assess the relative advantages of CGMMN.

3. **Computational Efficiency Concerns**: The paper acknowledges that CMMD involves matrix inverses that scale cubically with sample size, but doesn't provide concrete analysis of how this affects training time compared to alternatives like conditional GANs. The mini-batch approach (Algorithm 1) is mentioned but not thoroughly evaluated.

4. **Reproducibility Concerns**: The paper lacks sufficient detail about hyperparameters and training procedures. For instance, the authors mention "we did not observe much difference with a higher dimension [of hidden space]" but don't specify the exact dimensions used in experiments.

5. **Limited Exploration of Conditional Complexity**: The paper focuses on simple conditional scenarios (e.g., class labels) but doesn't explore more complex conditional generation tasks (e.g., text-to-image generation), which would better demonstrate the framework's full potential.

## Overall Assessment

CGMMN presents a valuable extension of GMMN to conditional distributions with a clean theoretical foundation and demonstrated utility across multiple applications. The simplicity of the training procedure is a significant advantage, and the results on classification and Bayesian distillation are particularly compelling. However, the paper would benefit from more thorough comparison with state-of-the-art conditional generative models, deeper theoretical analysis, and more detailed experimental protocols.

The work makes a meaningful contribution to the field of generative modeling by providing a simple yet effective framework for conditional generation. While the approach may not outperform the best conditional GANs in image generation tasks (as suggested by the SVHN results), it offers a compelling alternative with simpler training dynamics.

## Recommendation

**Recommendation: Weak Accept**

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
- Solid theoretical foundation for CMMD
- Demonstrated effectiveness across multiple application domains
- Simpler training process compared to many alternatives
- Novel application to Bayesian dark knowledge

**Arguments for Rejection:**
- Limited comparison with state-of-the-art conditional generative models (particularly conditional GANs)
- Insufficient theoretical analysis of convergence properties
- Lack of detailed experimental protocols for reproducibility
- Limited exploration of complex conditional generation scenarios

**Suggestions for Improvement:**
1. Add a detailed comparison with conditional GANs on the image generation tasks to better contextualize the performance.
2. Provide a more thorough theoretical analysis of the convergence properties of the CMMD objective.
3. Include more details about hyperparameters and training procedures to improve reproducibility.
4. Explore more complex conditional generation scenarios (e.g., text-to-image) to better demonstrate the framework's potential.
5. Discuss the computational efficiency of CGMMN relative to other methods in more detail.

This paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of conditional generative modeling and would be significantly strengthened by addressing the above points.
