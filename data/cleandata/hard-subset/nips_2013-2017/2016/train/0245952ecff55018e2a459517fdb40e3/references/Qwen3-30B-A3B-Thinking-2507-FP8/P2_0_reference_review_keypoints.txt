- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- CMMD trains conditional generative models by matching kernel mean embeddings of empirical and model conditional distributions.
- Demonstrated on three tasks: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual image generation (conditioned on labels), and Bayesian model distillation (dark knowledge).
- Theoretical foundation is solid with clear derivations linking CMMD to kernel embeddings and correct mathematical treatment.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed, showing competitive performance and strong CGMMN-CNN classification results.
- Comparisons include relevant baselines (GANs, VAEs, discriminative nets) and experiments are reproducible with detailed architectures and hyperparameters.
- Visual results (figures) effectively showcase the model’s generative capabilities.
- Novelty: first extension of GMMN to conditional settings via CMMD, offering a simpler objective than adversarial methods.
- Paper properly positions and cites related work (cGANs, cVAEs, prior moment-matching) and differentiates the approach.
- Demonstrates practical utility across diverse applications, including knowledge distillation from Bayesian models.
- Lacks thorough direct comparison to state-of-the-art conditional GANs and the latest conditional VAEs.
- Does not provide detailed analysis of computational complexity or scalability of CMMD compared to other conditional generative methods.
- Limited discussion on handling high-dimensional conditional variables.
- Theoretical guarantees for CMMD depend on specific smoothness assumptions and are somewhat limited.
- No in-depth comparison to other conditional moment-matching approaches.
- Overall recommendation: accept — meaningful, well-executed extension with solid theory and competitive results despite some comparative and scalability limitations.
