- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that learn p(y|x) by mapping (x,h)→y and minimizing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- Empirically evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling Bayesian models with competitive results on MNIST and SVHN.
- Positions CGMMN as a natural extension of GMMN, acknowledging related work in conditional GANs and VAEs and citing relevant MMD literature.
- Strength: solid theoretical foundation with correct CMMD derivation and clear links to kernel methods.
- Strength: simple to implement and train via backpropagation, presented as easier than GAN training.
- Strength: flexible framework applicable to multiple conditional generation tasks.
- Strength: competitive predictive performance and promising sample generation on benchmark datasets.
- Strength: paper is mostly clearly written with accessible explanations of Hilbert embeddings and MMD.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—main contribution is extending GMMN to the conditional case via CMMD.
- Weakness: empirical evaluation is limited—lacks ablation studies, sensitivity analysis (hidden layers, kernels), and comparisons to stronger modern baselines (conditional VAEs, flows).
- Weakness: CMMD estimation is computationally expensive due to kernel matrix operations; scalability and approximations are not thoroughly analyzed.
- Weakness: practical theoretical assumptions (smoothness) are stated but not justified or discussed in depth.
- Weakness: generation results are basic and missing modern quantitative metrics (Inception Score, FID); visuals are not competitive with advanced GANs.
- For acceptance: technically sound extension, novel CMMD criterion, and versatile competitive results support publication.
- Against acceptance: perceived incremental novelty, shallow empirical analysis, and computational concerns argue against strong acceptance.
- Overall assessment: reviewer recommends a weak accept pending improvements.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—expand empirical comparisons, add ablations, discuss CMMD computational cost and approximations, detail theoretical assumptions, and include stronger generative evaluation metrics.
