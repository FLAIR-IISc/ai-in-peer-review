- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions via Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) and kernel embedding theory.

- Positions work within generative-model literature as a non-adversarial conditional model alternative to cGANs and conditional VAEs.

Strengths:
- Clearly motivated: fills gap between unconditional GMMN and practical need for conditional modeling.
- Theoretically grounded: CMMD development is tied to kernel-embedding literature and supported by Theorems 1–3.
- Simple training: can be trained with standard backpropagation, avoiding GAN min–max optimization.
- Diverse evaluation: experiments span predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.
- Reproducibility: provides sufficient implementation details (architectures, hyperparameters, training).

Weaknesses:
- Key theoretical claim (Theorem 3) is insufficiently justified; lacks proof and clear conditions, and reference [29] is under-explained.
- Missing empirical comparison to conditional GANs (cGANs), the most relevant state-of-the-art baseline for conditional image generation.
- Computational concerns: CMMD requires kernel matrices/inverses (O(N^3)), and mini-batch trade-offs and complexity comparisons are not analyzed.
- Limited empirical advantage: results on MNIST/SVHN are competitive but not clearly superior to baselines, especially absent cGAN comparison.
- No ablation studies to assess impact of noise dimensionality, kernel choice, or architecture.
- Insufficient discussion of limitations, including performance on higher-dimensional data and kernel sensitivity.

Suggested improvements:
- Provide a full proof or detailed conditions for Theorem 3 and better integrate reference [29].
- Add direct empirical comparisons with cGANs (and similar conditional GAN variants) on the image tasks.
- Analyze computational trade-offs of CMMD, detail mini-batch approximations, and compare training time/complexity with cGANs/VAEs.
- Extend experiments to harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet) and include SOTA baselines.
- Include ablation studies for noise dimension, kernel selection, and network depth.
- Add a dedicated limitations section discussing failure cases and applicability boundaries.

Overall assessment:
- A reasonable, theoretically motivated extension of GMMN to conditional settings with simple training, but weakened by missing cGAN comparisons and incomplete theoretical justification.

Recommendation:
- Weak Reject

Pro (summary):
- Clear motivation, solid CMMD grounding, simple backprop training, varied experiments, and good presentation.

Con (summary):
- Theorem 3 lacks justification, no meaningful cGAN comparison, unaddressed computational complexity, modest empirical gains, and missing ablations/limitations.
