- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to model conditional distributions using Conditional MMD (CMMD).  
- Defines CMMD as a Hilbert–Schmidt norm between kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Proposes a conditional generator that takes both conditional variables and noise to produce target samples.  
- Evaluates CGMMN on classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation (Bayesian dark knowledge).  
- Builds on prior work (GMMN, kernel embeddings) and positions itself relative to conditional GANs and VAEs while avoiding adversarial training.  
- Solid theoretical derivation and clear connection of CMMD to the MMD framework.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability with competitive empirical results across multiple domains.  
- Training is simple and stable compared to GANs, using backpropagation without min–max optimization.  
- Experiments are comprehensive: multiple datasets, baselines, ablations, and checks against trivial solutions.  
- Provides clear algorithmic details and practical implementation considerations.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison with conditional GANs for conditional generation tasks.  
- CMMD involves matrix inversions with cubic scaling; scalability to large datasets is not thoroughly addressed.  
- Relies on smoothness assumptions for CMMD without empirical validation that they hold for used datasets/architectures.  
- Does not compare CGMMN to conditional VAEs for generation tasks.  
- Missing ablation studies on kernel choice, noise dimensionality, and network architecture.  
- Technically sound overall, with well-supported claims but incomplete analysis of computational complexity.  
- Well-written and organized, with sufficient detail for reproduction though some details are in the appendix.  
- Novel contribution: conditional extension of moment-matching networks using kernel embeddings.  
- Significant: offers a simple, competitive alternative to conditional GANs and useful applications like Bayesian distillation.  
- Recommendation: Accept.  
- Suggested improvements: add cGAN comparisons, analyze and mitigate computational complexity, conduct ablation studies, expand VAE comparisons, and empirically verify smoothness assumptions.
