- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) trained by minimizing Conditional MMD (CMMD) using conditional mean embeddings.  
- Uses Gram-matrix minibatch estimators and backprop through a smooth kernel on Y with generator y = f(x,h; w).

Strengths
- CMMD is a natural, principled extension of MMD to conditional distributions with a tractable minibatch implementation.  
- Framework is architecture- and kernel-agnostic; X kernels need not be differentiable.  
- Demonstrates applicability across prediction, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation tasks.  
- Solid connection to RKHS literature and conditional mean-embedding operators.

Empirical rigor and baselines (weaknesses)
- Predictive experiments lack in-house cross-entropy baselines using identical architectures, so advantage over discriminative training is unclear.  
- Conditional generation lacks quantitative metrics (e.g., FID, Parzen estimates) and comparisons to cGANs/cVAEs.  
- Distillation evaluated on a single small dataset with only RMSE; lacks NLL/calibration metrics and broader datasets.

Missing or underspecified details
- Kernel choices on X and Y, bandwidth selection, and λ tuning are not specified, hindering reproducibility.  
- Computational cost (O(m^3) inversion per minibatch) and runtimes, batch sizes, or scalable approximations are not reported.  
- Details on kernel gradients through generated y and any stability issues are not clarified.

Theory issues
- Theorem linking CY|X equality to PY|x equality would benefit from a proof sketch/citation and discussion of support assumptions.  
- The estimator formulation omits discussion of centering and bias introduced by regularization.

Positioning
- Needs clearer positioning relative to other conditional kernel methods and conditional two-sample/independence tests.

Quality
- Technically sound and well-posed, but main concerns are empirical—insufficient controlled baselines and quantitative evaluations.

Clarity
- Generally readable with helpful remarks, but contains typographical errors, incomplete references, and missing implementation details.

Originality
- Incremental but meaningful: extends GMMN to conditional setting via conditional mean embeddings, not previously presented in this exact form.

Significance
- Moderately significant; useful alternative to adversarial methods, but current empirical evidence is insufficient to demonstrate state-of-the-art benefits.

Pros for acceptance
- Simple, principled conditional objective extending MMD.  
- Broadly applicable framework avoiding adversarial minimax issues.  
- Theoretical grounding via conditional RKHS embeddings.

Cons for acceptance
- Empirical evaluation lacks strong, controlled baselines and quantitative generation/distillation metrics.  
- Missing critical implementation details (kernels, hyperparameters).  
- Limited discussion of scalability and ablation studies.

Concrete suggestions
- Add cross-entropy baselines with identical architectures for classification tasks.  
- Report kernel choices, bandwidth/λ selection, and ablation studies on them.  
- Include quantitative generation metrics and direct comparisons to cGAN/cVAE.  
- Expand distillation experiments to multiple datasets and report NLL/calibration.  
- Discuss computational cost and scalable approximations (Nyström, random features, Cholesky).

Overall recommendation
- Borderline weak accept if empirical section is substantially strengthened; otherwise weak reject due to insufficient experimental support.
