This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions. The core idea – leveraging the simplicity of moment matching with a deep generative model – is appealing, and the development of Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) is a natural and logical step. The paper tackles a relevant problem: many applications require modeling conditional distributions directly, and existing methods often struggle with this. The authors demonstrate the CGMMN's applicability across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, showcasing versatility.

The strength of this work lies in its conceptual simplicity and potential for broader application. Moving beyond joint distributions to focus on conditional generation is a valuable contribution, and the CMMD objective provides a theoretically sound and practically viable training mechanism. The authors successfully demonstrate that the method is competitive across a range of tasks, indicating a solid implementation and practical effectiveness. The connection to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is particularly interesting and demonstrates a nuanced understanding of the method's capabilities. The paper is reasonably well-written and the organization is logical.

However, the paper feels somewhat short on detailed analysis and thorough validation. While results are reported across several tasks, the experimental setup lacks depth. For example, the hyperparameter tuning process isn’t discussed. The ablation studies are absent, making it difficult to assess the impact of individual components or design choices. The comparison to existing methods, while present, isn’t always comprehensive. In the MNIST predictive task, while competitive, the improvements over existing state-of-the-art aren't dramatic, and the advantage isn't clearly articulated. More sophisticated comparison with methods specifically designed for conditional generation (like CVAE or conditional GANs) would strengthen the evaluation. The discussion on the assumptions needed for the CMMD objective (smoothness, Hilbert-Schmidt properties) feels brief, and a more rigorous analysis of potential limitations would be beneficial. While the authors acknowledge the assumptions, a more detailed exploration of their practical impact and potential remedies would increase confidence in the method's robustness. The scaling behavior of the CMMD calculation (mentioning mini-batch approach) is good, but a discussion of computational costs relative to competing methods would be insightful.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting with CMMD is a genuine contribution, filling a gap in the existing literature.
*   **Potential Impact:** The versatility of CGMMNs, demonstrated across different tasks, suggests significant potential for wider adoption and further research.
*   **Sound Methodology:** The theoretical foundation based on MMD and kernel embeddings is solid.
*   **Demonstrated Competitiveness:** The empirical results, while not groundbreaking, show that the method performs competitively with existing approaches.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Depth of Analysis:** The experimental evaluation lacks thoroughness and detailed analysis.
*   **Insufficient Ablation Studies:** The absence of ablation studies hinders understanding of component contributions.
*   **Brief Discussion of Assumptions:** The theoretical assumptions underpinning CMMD deserve more detailed consideration.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the shortcomings in experimental rigor and analysis depth, I lean towards accepting this paper. The core idea is genuinely novel and the potential impact is significant. The paper presents a conceptually clean and theoretically sound approach to conditional generative modeling. I believe the novelty and potential outweigh the current limitations, and these limitations could be addressed in future work. I recommend acceptance with the caveat that the authors should prioritize more detailed analysis and experimentation in follow-up studies. I would encourage them to conduct more thorough ablation studies, investigate the method's performance on a wider range of datasets, and provide a more rigorous analysis of the theoretical assumptions and their practical implications.
