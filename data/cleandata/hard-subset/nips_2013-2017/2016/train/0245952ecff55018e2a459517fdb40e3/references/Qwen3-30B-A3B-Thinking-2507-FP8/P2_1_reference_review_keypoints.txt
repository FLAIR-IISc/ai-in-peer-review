- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to train conditional generative models.  
- Builds on kernel embedding techniques and extends GMMN to handle conditional distributions by inputting conditional variables plus noise into a generator.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and distilling Bayesian models (Boston housing); shows competitive performance.  
- Theoretical foundation for CMMD is rigorous with clear conditions for the objective to be well-defined (Theorem 3).  
- Implementation is simple and efficient, leveraging backpropagation and avoiding GAN-style min-max training difficulties.  
- Comprehensive experiments across diverse tasks with appropriate baselines and clear figures.  
- Well-written and well-organized paper with consistent notation and reproducible detail.  
- Addresses a practical need by extending moment-matching methods to conditional modeling.  
- Limited novel theoretical contribution beyond applying existing kernel-embedding/CMMD ideas to conditional settings.  
- Performance improvements are incremental; CGMMN does not clearly outperform state-of-the-art (e.g., SVHN error higher than DSN).  
- Insufficient discussion and solutions for continuous conditional variables and limited treatment of kernel method computational costs.  
- Superficial differentiation from related work, especially conditional GANs; lacks clear argument why CMMD is preferable in practice.  
- Missing ablation studies to quantify the contribution of key components (conditional embedding, kernel choices, noise dimensionality).  
- Overall quality: technically sound and thorough experiments, but limited theoretical depth and critical analysis of limitations.  
- Clarity: very clear writing and adequate detail for reproduction, with minor appendix gaps.  
- Originality: a useful extension of GMMN to conditionals but only moderate novelty relative to existing conditional generative models.  
- Significance: addresses an important gap but results are not dramatically better than alternatives; Bayesian distillation application underdeveloped.  
- Recommendation: weak accept (accept with minor revisions).  
- Suggested improvements: discuss continuous conditionals, deepen comparison with conditional GANs, add ablation studies, analyze computational scaling, and clarify advantages over other conditional generative methods.  
- Final assessment: solid, practical extension of GMMN to conditional cases that would be stronger with more theoretical analysis, clearer limitations, and stronger comparisons.
