- Overall: Reasonable extension of GMMNs to conditional setting (CGMMN) using CMMD; clear writing and diverse experiments; recommendation = weak accept with minor revisions.  
- Core contribution: Formulates Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) to train a network that maps noise and condition x to samples from p(y|x).  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on GMMN/MMD and kernel embedding literature; positioned against conditional GANs and VAEs as a potentially more stable moment-matching alternative.  
- Strength — clarity: Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and frames the conditional generative problem clearly.  
- Strength — methodology: CMMD is a logical, theoretically grounded extension of MMD for conditional generation.  
- Strength — experiments: Evaluation across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation shows versatility.  
- Strength — performance: Results on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face are competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Strength — theory: Solid theoretical foundation via kernel methods and Hilbert-space embeddings.  
- Weakness — novelty: Extension is incremental and not clearly differentiated from conditional GANs/VAEs beyond being moment-matching.  
- Weakness — empirical gains: Improvements over strong baselines are often marginal and lack deeper explanation.  
- Weakness — missing analyses: No hyperparameter sensitivity study or ablation experiments (e.g., kernel choice, λ, auxiliary noise).  
- Weakness — scalability: Computational cost of kernel matrix computations is acknowledged but lacks detailed scalability/training-time comparison to GANs/VAEs.  
- Weakness — clarity: Mini-batch algorithm (Algorithm 1) and how CMMD is approximated in mini-batches need clearer explanation.  
- Pro acceptance: Technically sound, well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and experimentally viable.  
- Con acceptance: Incremental novelty, limited empirical advantages, and insufficient analysis of hyperparameters and scalability.  
- Questions for authors: Provide detailed comparison to conditional GANs/VAEs (trade-offs), complexity/scalability analysis, sensitivity to kernel/λ, ablation on auxiliary noise, and clearer mini-batch CMMD approximation details.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept pending minor revisions addressing clarity, limitations, and additional empirical analyses.
