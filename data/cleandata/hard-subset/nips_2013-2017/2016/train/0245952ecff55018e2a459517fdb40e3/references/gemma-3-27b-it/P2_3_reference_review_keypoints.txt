- Summary: Proposes CGMMN, extending GMMNs to learn p(y|x) by mapping noise+condition to y and optimizing with a novel conditional MMD (CMMD); evaluated on prediction, contextual generation, and distillation.

- Strength: Clear, well-motivated extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.

- Strength: Introduces a novel, theoretically grounded CMMD criterion for training conditional generative models.

- Strength: Strong empirical validation across diverse tasks (MNIST, SVHN, contextual generation, Bayesian dark knowledge) with competitive results.

- Strength: Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful preliminaries on MMD and kernel embeddings.

- Strength: Addresses practical implementation issues like mini-batch training and conditional embedding estimation.

- Weakness: Overall architecture is fairly standard (similar to GAN/VAE mappings); main novelty is CMMD itself.

- Weakness: Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel bandwidth, regularization λ).

- Weakness: Comparisons omit more recent and stronger baselines in some experiments (e.g., MNIST prediction).

- Weakness: CMMD derivation depends on smoothness assumptions for the conditional embedding operator without discussion of practical violations.

- Weakness: Generated sample visualizations are adequate but could be improved (more samples, interpolation, better presentation).

- Quality: Technically sound with correct derivations and generally well-supported claims, though novelty beyond CMMD is limited.

- Clarity: Clear exposition and easy-to-follow mathematical and procedural explanations.

- Originality: CMMD is a significant original contribution; the network architecture is less novel.

- Significance: CGMMN and CMMD are potentially useful for conditional generative tasks, with promising distillation results.

- For acceptance: Novel CMMD, diverse strong empirical results, clear presentation, and relevance to unsupervised learning.

- Against acceptance: Limited overall novelty, missing hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and lack of comparisons to recent baselines.

- Overall assessment: Strong accept with minor revisions; recommend addressing comparisons to recent baselines and hyperparameter analysis.
