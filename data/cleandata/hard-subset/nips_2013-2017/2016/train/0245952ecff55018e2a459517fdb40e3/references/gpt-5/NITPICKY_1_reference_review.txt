Summary
This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a conditional deep generative model trained by minimizing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) between the empirical conditional distribution P(Y|X) and the model distribution. The key idea is to represent conditional distributions via RKHS conditional mean embeddings CY|X, and to train a neural generator f(x, h; w) (with x the conditioning input and h exogenous noise) by minimizing the Hilbert–Schmidt norm ||ĈY|X(data) − ĈY|X(model)||^2. A closed-form expression for the empirical CMMD objective in terms of Gram matrices is derived, enabling backpropagation (since matrices involving X are independent of w). Experiments include: (i) prediction on MNIST and SVHN, (ii) conditional generation on MNIST and Yale Faces, and (iii) distillation of Bayesian models (PBP) on Boston Housing.

Relation to prior work
- Extends GMMN (Li et al., ICML’15; Dziugaite et al., UAI’15) from unconditional to conditional settings using kernel conditional mean embeddings (Song et al., ICML’09; Grünewälder et al., ICML’12).
- Alternative to CGAN (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and CVAE (Sohn et al., NIPS’15) for learning conditional generators; to MMVA (Li et al., NIPS’15) for discriminative performance using generative models.
- Distillation connects to Bayesian dark knowledge (Korattikara et al., NIPS’15).

Strengths
- Conceptually clean extension of MMD-based training to conditional distributions via operator embeddings; the objective avoids adversarial min-max instability and keeps backprop training simple.
- The CMMD objective has a clear (sufficient) criterion: if CY|X operators coincide, then PY|X coincide pointwise (under stated assumptions).
- Practical insight that K̃−1 depends only on X, simplifying gradients and kernel choices on X.
- The conditional generation results are reasonable; classification on SVHN/MNIST roughly matches prior MMD/Max-margin DGMs.
- Distillation experiment suggests feasibility of using CGMMN as a student model.

Weaknesses and concerns
Technical/theoretical
- Theorem 3: As stated, CY|X = CZ|X implies PY|x = PZ|x for all x. This requires stronger conditions than listed (e.g., well-definedness of CY|X, universality on X, injectivity of the feature map, equality holding for all x in the support, and technical conditions on CXX invertibility or regularization). Please clarify assumptions and provide a rigorous proof or cite an exact theorem.
- Estimation: ĈY|X = Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤ is standard, but the paper provides no consistency or sample complexity result for the CMMD objective when both operators are estimated from finite samples and when the model samples reuse X from the data. The minibatch CMMD (with per-batch inverses) introduces additional bias/variance; no analysis or ablation is provided.
- MMD estimator: The paper claims “Asymptotically, L̂^2_MMD is an unbiased estimator,” while using the biased V-statistic form (with diagonals). The unbiased U-statistic form is well-known; please correct and be precise about which estimator you use for both MMD and CMMD.
- Kernel choices and regularization: No details on kernels for X and Y, bandwidth selection, multi-kernel mixtures (as used in GMMN), or λ selection. Since the objective value is sensitive to these, this is a critical omission for reproducibility and fairness.
- Computational cost: Each minibatch requires inverting K+λI (O(b^3)). With b=300–500, this can dominate training. No wall-clock, memory, or scaling discussion; no tricks (e.g., low-rank, conjugate gradients, Nystrom) are explored.
- Using the same X for data and model (or perturbed) makes the operator inverses independent of w, but may encourage overfitting to the training X distribution; generalization implications are not discussed.

Methodology/empirics
- Classification setup: The output y is a “confidence vector” without clear normalization (sigmoid is mentioned in CNN; softmax vs simplex constraint unclear). Using CMMD to match a delta at the one-hot label per x is unusual; a standard cross-entropy baseline with the same architecture is missing. That baseline is essential to contextualize the predictive value of CMMD training.
- Baselines for conditional generation: No comparison to CGAN or CVAE, which are the most directly relevant conditional generative baselines. Visuals only; no quantitative metrics (e.g., conditional Parzen, FID/KID on class-conditional samples), no human eval.
- Distillation: Only one small dataset (Boston Housing), with a narrow performance gap. No details on input perturbation distribution for data generation from the teacher. No runtime or storage comparison to the teacher, which is central to distillation’s motivation.
- Reproducibility: Many crucial hyperparameters are unspecified (kernels, bandwidths, λ, noise dimension choice sensitivity, learning rates/schedules, early stopping), and appendices with details are not included here.

Presentation/clarity
- Numerous typos and formatting issues: “MINIST”, “Varitional”, “adverisarial”, “convoluational”, “Through there is a gap…”, broken algorithm formatting, dangling NIPS header line, hyphenation (“net-work”), inconsistent acronym usage (VA/VAE), inconsistent capitalization (Adam/AdaM), and multiple incomplete/malformed references (missing years, venues, author names misspelled). Figures/tables are referenced but not shown; some tables lack baselines and proper captions.
- Notation: s/d datasets and cross-gram matrices are easy to confuse; define unambiguously. Explain clearly when X is shared between datasets versus resampled/perturbed.

Pros
- Elegant, principled conditional extension of MMD-based training using kernel conditional mean embeddings.
- Simple backprop training without adversarial games; practically attractive.
- Demonstrates versatility across prediction, generation, and distillation.

Cons
- Missing critical baselines (CGAN, CVAE; same-architecture cross-entropy classifier).
- Insufficient experimental rigor and quantitative evaluation for generation.
- Theoretical assumptions and estimator properties not fully justified; incorrect statement about MMD estimator.
- Reproducibility gaps and substantial presentation/format issues.
- Computational scalability not addressed.

Questions for authors
1) Precisely specify kernels and bandwidth selection for X and Y in all experiments; provide sensitivity analyses and λ selection method. 2) Provide a proof (or citation) for Theorem 3 with full assumptions. 3) Compare against CGAN and CVAE on conditional generation (quantitative metrics), and against a matched-architecture cross-entropy classifier. 4) Report training time/memory and batch inverse cost; consider scalable approximations. 5) Clarify the output layer and constraints on y for classification. 6) Detail teacher-sample generation and input perturbation for distillation.

Recommendation: Reject (promising idea but too many methodological, empirical, and presentation shortcomings in current form)

Confidence: 3/5
