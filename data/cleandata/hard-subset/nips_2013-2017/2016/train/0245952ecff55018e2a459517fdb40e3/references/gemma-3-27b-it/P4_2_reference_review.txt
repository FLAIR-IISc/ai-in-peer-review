This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the existing Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to learn a generative model that, given input variables, produces samples from the conditional distribution p(y|x). This is achieved by defining a simple generative process involving a deep network and employing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion for training. The authors evaluate CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, demonstrating competitive performance.

The paper builds upon recent advances in generative modeling, particularly GMMN [19] and GANs [5]. GMMN offered a simpler training objective compared to GANs, utilizing MMD for distribution matching. This work extends this approach to the conditional setting, addressing a common need in many applications. The use of MMD and its conditional extension aligns with a broader trend of non-parametric density estimation and distribution matching techniques in deep learning. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is a novel and interesting use case.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of CMMD appears mathematically sound, building on established concepts like Hilbert space embeddings and MMD. The use of back-propagation for training is a practical advantage. The theoretical justification provided through Theorems 1, 2 and 3 strengthens the methodological basis.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation for conditional generative modeling is clearly explained. The concepts of Hilbert space embedding and MMD are reviewed adequately. The notation is consistent and helps in understanding the equations.
* **Originality:** Extending GMMN to the conditional setting is a reasonable and valuable contribution. While conditional GANs exist [21], providing a moment-matching alternative offers a different perspective and potentially advantages in training stability. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is a novel aspect.
* **Significance:** The ability to model conditional distributions is crucial for a wide range of applications. The competitive performance demonstrated on diverse tasks suggests practical relevance. The simplicity of the training procedure (back-propagation) makes CGMMN accessible and potentially scalable.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Ablation Studies:** While the authors demonstrate competitive performance, the paper lacks extensive ablation studies to understand the contribution of different components of CGMMN (e.g., the choice of kernel, the network architecture).  How sensitive is the performance to the regularization parameter λ?
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The impact of hyperparameters (learning rate, network architecture, etc.) is not thoroughly discussed. A more detailed analysis of hyperparameter tuning would increase confidence in the results.
* **Comparison to Strong Baselines:** While several baselines are included, some comparisons seem incomplete. For example, the comparison to DSN is only on MNIST. A more comprehensive comparison across all tasks would strengthen the evaluation. The choice of baselines could be justified more thoroughly.
* **Practical Considerations:** The computational cost of CMMD, especially with large datasets, is not explicitly addressed. While mini-batch optimization is mentioned, a more detailed discussion of scalability and memory requirements would be beneficial.
* **Missing Related Work:** The paper mentions relevant work, but a few potentially relevant papers could be included. For example, recent advances in normalizing flows for conditional density estimation [e.g., Papamakarios et al., 2017] or other conditional generative models based on diffusion models.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a technically sound and original extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
* The CMMD criterion is well-motivated and theoretically justified.
* The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on a diverse set of tasks.
* The simplicity of the training procedure is a significant advantage.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of extensive ablation studies and hyperparameter analysis weakens the empirical validation.
* The comparison to strong baselines is not always comprehensive.
* The computational cost and scalability of CMMD are not thoroughly discussed.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling. The extension of GMMN to the conditional setting is a logical and well-executed step, and the CMMD criterion provides a principled approach to training. While some weaknesses exist, the strengths of the paper outweigh them.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above, particularly regarding ablation studies, hyperparameter analysis, and a more thorough comparison to relevant baselines.

**Rating:** Accept with minor revisions.
