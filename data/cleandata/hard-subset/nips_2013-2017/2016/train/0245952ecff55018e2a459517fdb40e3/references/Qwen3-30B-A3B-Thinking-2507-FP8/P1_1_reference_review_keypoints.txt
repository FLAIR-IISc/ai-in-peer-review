- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional MMD (CMMD) to model conditional distributions.  
- CGMMN generates samples by feeding conditional variables and noise through a deep network trained with CMMD.  
- Evaluated across three tasks: classification (predictive modeling), label-conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Builds on kernel embedding/MMD theory and extends it to the conditional setting with a clear derivation (Theorem 3).  
- Practical implementation details are thorough, with reproducible architectures, hyperparameters, and simple backpropagation training.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and include appropriate baselines and checks against memorization.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid and the paper is technically sound and complete.  
- Writing and organization are clear, though the CMMD derivation could use more intuitive explanation for non-kernel experts.  
- Novelty is incremental: natural extension of GMMN to conditional problems and not strongly differentiated from existing conditional generative models.  
- Results are competitive but do not outperform state-of-the-art (e.g., DSN on MNIST and SVHN).  
- Computational complexity concerns: CMMD requires matrix inversions that may not scale well to very large datasets.  
- Limited discussion of limitations: scalability with many conditional classes, high-dimensional conditionals, and effect of noise dimensionality are not fully addressed.  
- Lacks comparisons to more recent conditional generative approaches (e.g., conditional diffusion models, advanced GAN variants).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: better contrast with conditional GANs, analyze where CGMMN outperforms baselines, discuss computational cost and scalability, expand limitations section, and update related work to include recent models.
