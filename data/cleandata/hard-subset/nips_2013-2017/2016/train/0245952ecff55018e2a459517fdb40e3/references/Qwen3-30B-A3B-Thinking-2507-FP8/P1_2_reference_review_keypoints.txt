- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMN to model conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- CGMMN architecture conditions on x and noise h to generate targets y for conditional generation tasks.  
- Theoretical results link CMMD to equality of conditional distributions, providing a solid kernel-embedding justification.  
- Experiments cover predictive modeling, class-conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation across several datasets.  
- Empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., strong performance on SVHN) and show diverse, non-copying conditional samples.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear derivations and accessible exposition of kernel mean embeddings and conditional distributions.  
- Experimental setup and network details are well documented, with useful figures illustrating the architecture.  
- Major technical concern: CMMD computation is cubic in sample size; scalability and trade-offs of mini-batching are not thoroughly explored.  
- Choice of kernel and its impact on performance is insufficiently discussed or evaluated.  
- Lack of direct experimental comparison with conditional GANs (cGANs), despite mentioning them as related work.  
- Implementation detail for CMMD regularization (λ) and its effects is under-discussed.  
- Originality: meaningful and novel extension of moment-matching to conditional settings, building on GMMN and kernel conditional embedding literature.  
- Significance: versatile approach for conditional tasks, but practical utility is tempered by computational cost and unclear advantages over cGANs.  
- Strengths: clear theoretical extension, comprehensive experiments, practical algorithm description, demonstrated conditional learning, and relevant baseline comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: inadequate cGAN comparison, limited scalability discussion, little kernel-choice analysis, restricted distillation experiments, and unclear practical advantages beyond avoiding adversarial training.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions, contingent on addressing key comparative and implementation concerns.  
- Requested revisions: add direct cGAN comparisons, expand discussion/analysis of CMMD computational complexity, study kernel choices and their impact, and broaden knowledge-distillation experiments.  
- Specific reviewer suggestions reiterate the need for deeper complexity analysis, kernel exploration, cGAN baselines, and more challenging distillation tasks.
