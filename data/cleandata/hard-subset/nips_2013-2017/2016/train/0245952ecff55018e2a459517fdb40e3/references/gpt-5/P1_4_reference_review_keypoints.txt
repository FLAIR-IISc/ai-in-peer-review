- Proposal: Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to match conditional distributions via kernel mean embeddings.  
- Generator: Uses a deep network y = f(x, h | w) that takes conditioning variable x and noise h for flexible conditional sampling.  
- CMMD objective: Measures Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical conditional mean embedding operators with a closed-form Gram-matrix expression enabling backprop.  
- Training: Inverse Gram matrices on x, (K + λI)−1, are independent of network parameters, simplifying differentiation and minibatch training.  
- Applications: Demonstrated for predictive modeling p(y|x), class-conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian predictive distillation (Boston housing).  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on MMD/kernel embeddings, GMMN, and conditional DGMs (cGAN, CVAE); CMMD is a natural extension of MMD to conditionals.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides a sufficient-identifiability statement (Theorem 3) under universal kernels and regularity assumptions.  
- Strength — clarity: Conceptually clear extension of GMMN/MMD to conditional distributions with an implementable CMMD loss.  
- Strength — practicality: Minibatch algorithm is simple, uses standard optimizers (Adam), and benefits from parameter-independent inverse Gram matrices.  
- Strength — versatility: Shows three distinct use cases with competitive performance, including close SVHN results to a strong DGM baseline.  
- Strength — insights: Useful remark connecting CMMD to reweighted MMD and AE+CGMMN trick for smoother samples.  
- Strength — stability: Avoids adversarial training instability present in cGANs.  
- Weakness — theoretical: Lacks analysis of empirical CMMD estimator (bias/variance) and conditions under which empirical minimization implies true conditional convergence.  
- Weakness — computational: Inverting (K + λI) per minibatch is O(m^3), with no runtime/memory reporting or discussion of scalable kernel approximations.  
- Weakness — experimental generation: Limited to MNIST/Yale Faces qualitatively, missing quantitative metrics and comparisons to cGAN/CVAE and a joint-GMMN baseline.  
- Weakness — kernel/hyperparams: Missing details on kernel choices, bandwidth selection, λ values, and sensitivity analyses despite K's central role.  
- Weakness — training protocol clarity: Need clarification on whether x is reused in minibatches, extent of x-perturbations, and implications for generalization.  
- Weakness — predictive evaluation: Predictive results lag discriminative methods; lacks proper scoring rules, calibration, and uncertainty evaluation.  
- Weakness — Bayesian distillation: Minimal experiment (Boston housing) with RMSE only and no predictive log-likelihoods, calibration, or interval coverage.  
- Quality: Method is technically sound but would benefit from empirical ablations, kernel/complexity analysis, and broader generative comparisons.  
- Clarity: Generally clear and organized; paper contains typos and needs full specification of kernels, λ, architectures, and hyperparameters for reproducibility.  
- Originality: Natural but novel combination of conditional moment-matching with kernel conditional operators in deep nets.  
- Significance: Important direction—stable conditional generation without adversarial training—if strengthened experimentally and made scalable.  
- Questions for authors: Which kernels/bandwidths and λ were used; computational costs and scalability; comparison to joint GMMN; x-sharing/perturbation details; fuller Bayesian metrics.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled conditional extension of GMMN, clear implementable objective, versatile demonstrations, avoids adversarial instability.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited generative evaluation and baselines, missing kernel/hyperparameter and cost details, no consistency guarantees, minimal Bayesian evidence.  
- Overall verdict: Solid, well-motivated contribution leaning toward acceptance if experimental gaps and clarity issues are addressed; otherwise borderline.
