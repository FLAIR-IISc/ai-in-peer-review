## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to learn a conditional distribution p(y|x) by mapping a simple distribution, combined with the conditioning variable x, through a deep neural network. The method utilizes a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective for training, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks.

While the paper addresses an important problem and builds upon the recent advances in moment-matching methods, a multitude of issues, ranging from minor notational inconsistencies to concerns about experimental details and clarity, significantly detract from the overall quality of the work.  The presentation lacks the rigor expected of a NIPS submission.

**Strengths:**

*   **Relevant Problem:** Conditional generative modeling is a crucial area, and extending existing methods to this domain is valuable.
*   **Sound Core Idea:** The concept of leveraging a simple distribution and a deep network to model the conditional distribution is reasonable and aligns with successful approaches in GANs and GMMNs.
*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The CMMD objective is grounded in sound theoretical principles relating to kernel embeddings and Hilbert spaces.
*   **Empirical Validation:**  The authors evaluate CGMMN on a range of tasks, demonstrating its potential applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity & Notation:** The paper suffers from several clarity issues. For example, the notation used for datasets (DsXY vs. DdXY) is inconsistent and needlessly complex. The use of “x̃” to denote the concatenation of x and h is confusing as it's not consistently used throughout.
*   **Lack of Detail in Experimental Setup:** The experimental sections lack crucial details. For instance, the MLP architectures are described generally, but specific layer sizes and activation functions within each layer aren’t always provided, hindering reproducibility.  The hyperparameter tuning process is not discussed. The regularization parameter λ used in CMMD is never specified or justified.
*   **Minor Mathematical Errors & Imprecision:**  Equation (2) contains some notational ambiguities that make it difficult to follow.  The justification for assuming EY |X [g(Y )|X] ∈ F and EZ|X [g(Z)|X] ∈ F in Theorem 3 seems weak and should be more thoroughly discussed. The statement about the kernel not needing to be differentiable is misleading—the backpropagation *requires* a differentiable kernel to calculate gradients.
*   **Limited Novelty:** While extending GMMN to the conditional setting is a contribution, the core idea feels incremental. The paper doesn’t sufficiently highlight how CGMMN differs from or outperforms established conditional generative models like conditional GANs or variational autoencoders.
*   **Insufficient Ablation Studies:** The authors compare CGMMN with several baselines, but lack any ablation studies. For example, what is the impact of the dimensionality of the hidden variable 'h'? Does the choice of kernel function matter?
*    **Overstated Claims:** The statement that the CMMD objective avoids issues with finite sample sizes is overly optimistic. While it's true that separate tests don't share statistics, CMMD still relies on accurate estimation of kernel mean embeddings from finite samples.
*   **Formatting Issues:** Numerous minor formatting issues are present throughout the paper, including inconsistent spacing and capitalization.

**Specific Points & Questions:**

*   **Equation (2):**  What is the precise meaning of `Tr` applied to matrices in this context? Is it a standard trace operation? The indices are unclear.
*   **Appendix References:** Several references to appendices (e.g., Appendix A.2.2) are made without providing sufficient information in the main text about what the appendix contains.
*   **Choice of Kernel:** The paper doesn’t justify the choice of kernel function used in the experiments.
*   **Regularization:** The role of the regularization parameter λ is unclear. How was it chosen? What impact does it have on performance?
*   **Bayesian Dark Knowledge:** The experimental setup for Bayesian dark knowledge is insufficiently detailed. What specific PBP model was used? How were the samples generated for the teacher network?

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Against:**

*   Numerous minor errors and inconsistencies in notation and presentation.
*   Lack of detailed experimental setup and hyperparameter discussion.
*   Limited novelty compared to existing conditional generative models.
*   Insufficient ablation studies.
*   Overstated claims about the benefits of CMMD.

**For:**

*   Addresses a relevant and important problem.
*   The core idea is technically sound.
*   Demonstrates competitive performance on several tasks.
*   The theoretical foundations are well-established.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the potential of the CGMMN framework, the numerous flaws in the presentation and experimental details raise significant concerns. The paper, as currently written, lacks the polish and rigor expected of a NIPS publication. I recommend **rejection**.  The authors should address the issues outlined above, provide more detailed experimental results, and clearly articulate the novel contributions of their work before resubmitting. A substantial revision is required to bring this paper up to NIPS standards.
