

# Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

## Summary
This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extends the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to handle conditional distributions. The authors propose Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a criterion for training conditional generative models, allowing the model to learn flexible conditional distributions p(y|x) by matching the kernel mean embeddings of the empirical conditional distribution and the model's conditional distribution. The paper demonstrates the approach on three tasks: predictive modeling (classification on MNIST and SVHN), contextual generation (image generation conditioned on class labels), and distilling Bayesian models (Bayesian dark knowledge). The work builds on previous GMMN research [19] and addresses a gap in the literature for conditional moment-matching networks.

## Quality Assessment
The paper is technically sound with a solid theoretical foundation. The authors provide clear derivations of CMMD and its connection to the kernel embedding of conditional distributions. The theoretical framework is well-supported by the experimental results across multiple tasks. The mathematical derivations appear correct, and the paper properly references the necessary background on kernel mean embeddings and MMD.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive and well-designed. The authors compare CGMMN to relevant baselines (including GAN-based approaches, VAEs, and discriminative networks) across three distinct tasks. The results demonstrate competitive performance in all evaluated settings, with CGMMN-CNN achieving strong results on classification tasks and generating high-quality samples for contextual generation.

However, the paper has some limitations in its evaluation. The comparison to conditional generative models is somewhat limited, particularly lacking a thorough comparison to the state-of-the-art conditional GANs (cGANs) [21] and conditional VAEs (cVAEs) [28]. While the authors mention cGANs as related work, they don't directly compare their approach to the most recent conditional generative models. Additionally, the paper doesn't provide a detailed analysis of computational complexity or scalability of CMMD compared to other conditional generative approaches.

## Clarity Assessment
The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly states the problem and motivation, the preliminary section provides necessary background on kernel embeddings and MMD, and the main contributions are presented logically. The mathematical derivations are clear and well-motivated. The experimental section is well-structured, with detailed descriptions of architectures, hyperparameters, and results.

The paper provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results, including model architectures, training procedures, and hyperparameters. The figures (particularly Figures 2-5) effectively illustrate the generative capabilities of the model. The writing is precise and avoids unnecessary jargon, making the paper accessible to a broad audience in machine learning.

## Originality Assessment
The paper makes a meaningful contribution by extending the GMMN framework to conditional distributions. While conditional generative models have been explored in prior work (e.g., cGANs [21], cVAEs [28]), the specific approach of using CMMD for conditional generation is novel. The authors clearly differentiate their approach from previous work, emphasizing the simplicity of the training objective compared to GANs and the direct connection to the moment-matching framework.

The paper properly cites and discusses related work, including conditional GANs, conditional VAEs, and previous moment-matching approaches. The authors clearly articulate how their work differs from these approaches and why their specific formulation (CMMD) is advantageous for conditional generation. The extension of GMMN to conditional settings is a significant step that fills an important gap in the literature.

## Significance Assessment
The work addresses an important problem in generative modeling: learning conditional distributions. The ability to learn flexible conditional distributions has broad applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation. The paper demonstrates that CGMMN can achieve competitive performance across these diverse tasks without the complexity of adversarial training.

The significance of this work is demonstrated through the comprehensive evaluation across multiple tasks. The results on classification (competitive with state-of-the-art DGMs for prediction) and generation (producing high-quality samples conditioned on labels) show the practical utility of the approach. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge further demonstrates the versatility of the framework.

However, the paper could have strengthened the significance argument by providing more concrete evidence of how CGMMN advances the state of the art. While the results are competitive, the paper doesn't clearly establish a substantial improvement over existing methods that would justify a major advancement in the field.

## Strengths
- Clear and well-structured presentation of a meaningful extension to GMMN
- Solid theoretical foundation with proper mathematical derivations
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across three distinct tasks
- Competitive results on classification, generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation
- Well-written with excellent organization and clarity

## Weaknesses
- Limited comparison to the most recent conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs, conditional VAEs)
- Lack of detailed analysis of computational complexity and scalability
- Insufficient discussion of handling high-dimensional conditional variables
- Theoretical guarantees for CMMD are limited to specific smoothness assumptions
- No thorough analysis of how CMMD compares to other conditional moment-matching approaches

## Recommendation
I recommend acceptance of this paper. The work presents a clear and meaningful extension to the GMMN framework with solid theoretical foundations and competitive empirical results. The paper is well-written, well-organized, and makes a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling. While there are some limitations in the comparison to state-of-the-art conditional generative models, the core contribution of extending moment-matching networks to conditional settings is significant and well-executed.

The paper fills an important gap in the literature and provides a simple, effective approach for learning conditional distributions. The experimental results across multiple tasks demonstrate the practical utility of the framework. While the comparison to the most recent conditional generative models could have been more thorough, the paper still makes a substantial contribution to the field that is well-suited for NIPS.

The paper meets all four criteria (Quality, Clarity, Originality, Significance) at a high level, with the main area for improvement being a more comprehensive comparison to the most recent conditional generative models. Given the quality of the theoretical contribution and the solid experimental results, I believe this paper is a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS.
