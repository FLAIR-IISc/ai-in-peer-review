- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective using kernel embeddings.
- Samples generated by passing conditional variables and noise through a deep network, avoiding GAN-style adversarial training.
- Demonstrated on classification (MNIST/SVHN), conditional image generation, and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation.
- Theoretical foundation is sound, building correctly on kernel embedding of conditional distributions and MMD.
- Training is practically simple, using straightforward backpropagation rather than min-max GAN optimization.
- Evaluation is broad, comparing against relevant baselines across multiple application domains.
- Paper is clearly presented with an accessible algorithm description.
- Lacks convergence guarantees or statistical analysis; impact of kernel choice and regularization on convergence/generalization is not addressed.
- Insufficient comparison and discussion relative to conditional GANs; advantages/disadvantages versus GANs are not explored.
- Generated images show artifacts and lower quality than conditional GANs/VAEs; reliance on an autoencoder hybrid undermines claimed simplicity.
- Bayesian distillation results are marginal (negligible RMSE difference) and not convincingly significant.
- Fails to discuss key limitations such as cubic complexity of kernel matrix inversion, kernel/λ selection, and scaling to high-dimensional conditionals.
- Main contribution (CMMD) is somewhat incremental given prior conditional GAN/VAE work and does not clearly outperform existing methods.
- Overall assessment: technically valid and comprehensively evaluated but of limited significance—recommended Weak Accept / borderline.
- Suggested improvements: more thorough comparisons with conditional GANs/VAEs, detailed analysis of kernel/regularization effects, explicit discussion of scalability/limitations, more qualitative analysis of generated samples, and theoretical convergence/statistical guarantees.
