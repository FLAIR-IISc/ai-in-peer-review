- Proposes CGMMN: extends GMMN to conditional distributions using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective from kernel mean embeddings.  
- Training remains simple and backprop-friendly, preserving GMMN's practical advantages.  
- The CMMD objective is rigorously derived and the mathematical presentation is clear.  
- Experiments are broad, covering classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation with results on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, and Boston housing.  
- Fails to compare meaningfully to key prior conditional generative models (Conditional GANs, Conditional VAEs), weakening competitiveness claims.  
- Lacks analysis of practical concerns: effect of kernel choice, scaling with conditional variable dimensionality, and validity of conditional mean embedding assumptions.  
- Empirical gains are modest or absent; some results (e.g., SVHN, generated images) do not convincingly outperform established methods and require post-processing.  
- Missing ablation studies to attribute gains specifically to CMMD versus network architecture.  
- Originality/significance limited: logical extension but not clearly superior to existing conditional generative approaches.  
- Recommendation: reject—needs stronger comparisons, theoretical justification, and more substantial empirical advantages for NIPS.
