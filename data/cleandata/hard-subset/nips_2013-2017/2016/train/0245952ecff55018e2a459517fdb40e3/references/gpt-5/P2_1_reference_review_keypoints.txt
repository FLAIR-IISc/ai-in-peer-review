- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that learn p(y|x) via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- CMMD is defined as the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators estimated from data and a neural generator.  
- Generator is a deep network taking [x, h] (context + noise) and trained by backprop through the CMMD loss.  
- Evaluated on supervised prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST digits, Yale faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston housing).  
- Builds on three strands: MMD-based generative models, kernel conditional embeddings/operators, and conditional deep generative models (cGAN, cVAE).  
- Theoretical claim that matching conditional embeddings implies matching conditionals is standard in the conditional embedding literature.  
- CMMD objective and estimator are technically sound and gradients are straightforward; avoids adversarial min–max instability.  
- Empirically works across architectures (MLP/CNN) and tasks; classification is competitive with some DGMs and conditional samples are reasonable.  
- Combines with autoencoders to improve perceptual quality, consistent with prior GMMN findings.  
- Bayesian distillation experiment shows equal RMSE to teacher on Boston housing but is limited in scope.  
- Missing key baselines: no comparisons to conditional GANs or conditional VAEs, which are natural competitors.  
- No quantitative generative metrics provided (e.g., log-likelihood proxies, Parzen, classifier-based scores); only qualitative checks.  
- Limited analysis of kernel choices, bandwidth selection, regularization λ, and sensitivity to these hyperparameters.  
- Scalability concerns: CMMD requires inverting K+λI per minibatch; computational cost, stability, and batch-size trade-offs are not reported.  
- Minibatch CMMD estimator bias/variance properties are not analyzed.  
- Implementation details under-specified: exact kernels, λ values, number of generated samples per x, use of multi-kernel MMD, and label/kernel choices for y.  
- Paper is generally well organized and figures/algorithms are clear, but computational complexity/memory costs are not analyzed.  
- Originality: novel application of conditional embedding matching to train deep generators, but theoretical foundation and extension from MMD are incremental.  
- Significance: conceptually a simple, stable alternative to adversarial training, but empirical gains are modest and missing comparisons weaken impact.  
- Pros: principled objective, no adversarial training, simple backprop-based learning, works across tasks and architectures, reasonable qualitative samples.  
- Cons: lacks cGAN/cVAE baselines and quantitative generation metrics, limited kernel and scalability analysis, limited theoretical novelty, minimal distillation evaluation.  
- Suggested improvements: add strong conditional baselines and quantitative metrics, provide kernel/hyperparameter sensitivity and ablations, analyze scalability and minibatch properties, expand distillation experiments.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject—sound and elegant but empirical evaluation is insufficient to establish significance; stronger experiments needed.  
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
