- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) to match conditional distributions via conditional mean embedding operators.
- Generator is a deterministic deep net y = f(x,h;w) taking x and noise h, trained by backpropagating a CMMD loss computed with kernel Gram matrices on mini-batches.
- Theoretical sufficiency (Theorem 3): equality of conditional embedding operators implies equality of conditional distributions under standard universality/regularity assumptions — aligns with prior conditional mean embedding work.
- CMMD is a natural, principled extension of MMD to conditional settings and the estimator via regularized empirical operators is appropriate.
- Missing statistical analysis: no consistency or finite-sample bounds for CMMD, no treatment of bias from regularization λ, and no kernel/bandwidth selection guidance.
- Algorithmic scalability concerns: per-mini-batch inversion K̃^{-1} is O(b^3) time and O(b^2) memory (b up to 500), with no reported runtimes or kernel-approximation solutions.
- Observation that K̃−1 is independent of generator parameters implies non-differentiable kernels on x are possible, but when x in data and generated sets match CMMD reduces to a weighted label-Gram discrepancy — this reduction is noted but not analyzed.
- Empirics: classification competitive with DGMs but behind strong discriminative baselines on SVHN; generative results are qualitative with no quantitative comparisons to CGAN/CVAE or unconditional GMMN.
- Use of an autoencoder for MNIST generation suggests pixel-space kernels are ill-suited; this issue is acknowledged but not systematically analyzed or quantified.
- Bayesian distillation experiment is promising but limited to a single small dataset and lacks comparison to established student baselines.
- Exposition: RKHS embeddings, MMD, and conditional embeddings are generally clear and CMMD Gram-matrix expression is helpful.
- Missing implementation details: kernel choices and bandwidths for x and y, λ value and role, number of h samples per x, noise-dimension sensitivity, minibatch-size stability, and whether K is recomputed or cached.
- Lacks explicit relation and formal comparison between CMMD and joint MMD with product kernels; remark exists but a formal treatment would help.
- Originality: novel and natural extension of GMMN to conditional setting, offering a stable non-adversarial training objective.
- Should better acknowledge connections to conditional mean embedding regression and operator-valued kernel regression to situate the contribution.
- Significance: useful architecture-agnostic conditional training objective without adversarial games, but empirical advantages are modest and scalability limits practical impact.
- Requests for clarification include: specific kernels/bandwidths used and sensitivity to λ; whether x is identical in data and generated minibatches and a derivation of the resulting reduction; number of h samples per x; computational cost comparisons to CGAN/CVAE; quantitative generative metrics and comparisons; and exploration of kernel approximations or reuse of K̃−1.
- Pros: principled CMMD grounded in RKHS embeddings, simple stable training via backprop, broad applicability demonstrated across tasks, and competitive classification performance with DGMs.
- Cons: limited theoretical analysis (no consistency/concentration), scalability issues from O(b^3) inversions, missing implementation details and ablations, lack of quantitative generative evaluation, and modest gains over standard classifiers.
- Recommendation: borderline accept — idea is sound and timely but paper needs more implementation details, sensitivity analyses, quantitative generative evaluation, clarification of the CMMD reduction when x is shared, and mitigation of computational costs.
