- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) to model conditional distributions via moment-matching.  
- Builds on GMMN and contrasts with conditional GANs and VAEs, avoiding GAN-style min-max training.  
- Theoretical soundness: provides well-motivated theorems (1–3) linking CMMD to MMD and conditional embedding operators.  
- Comprehensive experiments on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face showing competitive predictive performance and diverse sample generation.  
- Practical implementation details (mini-batch Algorithm 1, use of autoencoders) improve reproducibility and sample quality.  
- Novel application: successfully applies CGMMN to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation with negligible loss in predictive performance.  
- Weakness: lacks direct empirical comparison to conditional GANs in generation tasks, weakening claims of competitiveness.  
- Weakness: computational complexity concerns (kernel Gram matrix scaling) are noted but not analyzed or benchmarked for large datasets.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choice, regularization λ, hidden dimension).  
- Weakness: limited guidance/theoretical justification on selecting appropriate kernels for different data/conditionals.  
- Quality assessment: technically solid with strong theoretical foundations and careful experiments, but missing cGAN comparisons.  
- Clarity assessment: well-written and organized with clear math, though preliminaries may challenge non-experts.  
- Originality assessment: natural but valuable extension of GMMN to conditional settings; CMMD and dark-knowledge application are novel.  
- Significance assessment: addresses an important gap by providing a stable, theoretically grounded conditional generative method likely useful to practitioners.  
- Recommendation: accept to NIPS — meaningful contribution despite some evaluation gaps.  
- Suggested improvements: add conditional GAN comparisons, analyze computational scalability, study hyperparameter sensitivity, and provide practical kernel-selection guidance.
