- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to model P(y|x).
- Extends GMMN to conditional generation, targeting predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation.
- Builds on MMD and kernel embeddings with supportive theorems and a solid theoretical foundation.
- CMMD mathematical formulation and backprop-based implementation are well explained, including kernel matrix and mini-batch handling.
- Empirical validation on MNIST/SVHN, image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation shows competitive performance.
- Clear motivation, well-written, and logically organized presentation.
- Fills a gap by adapting the GMMN framework to conditional settings.
- Novelty is moderate—primarily an adaptation of MMD to conditional cases rather than a fundamentally new generative approach.
- Performance likely sensitive to kernel hyperparameters and regularization (λ); robustness not thoroughly explored.
- Paper lacks ablation studies (e.g., MLP vs. CNN, role of auxiliary variable h).
- Comparisons omit more recent strong baselines (e.g., conditional GANs, conditional VAEs).
- Dependence on kernel choice is significant, but discussion of kernel selection is limited.
- Kernel matrix computation can be computationally expensive; detailed scaling analysis is missing.
- Quality score: 7/10; Clarity: 8/10; Originality: 6/10; Significance: 7/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound extension, competitive results, contributes to moment-matching models, readable presentation.
- Arguments for rejection: moderate novelty, insufficient hyperparameter/ablation analysis, limited baseline comparisons, computational concerns.
- Overall recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Suggested revisions: add hyperparameter sensitivity and kernel choice discussion, perform ablation studies, include comparisons to state-of-the-art conditional generative models, and provide computational scaling analysis.
