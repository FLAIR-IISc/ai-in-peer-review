

# Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

## Summary
This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions. The authors introduce conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) as a training objective and present a conditional generative architecture that takes both conditional variables and random noise as inputs. The paper evaluates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks.

## Strengths
The paper provides a clear theoretical foundation for CMMD and demonstrates the applicability of the method across multiple domains. The experimental results on MNIST and SVHN for predictive modeling are promising, and the contextual generation results on MNIST and Yale Face datasets are visually compelling. The extension of GMMN to conditional settings is a logical step that addresses a gap in the literature.

## Weaknesses

### Technical and Methodological Issues
1. **Inconsistent Matrix Notation**: The paper presents the empirical CMMD objective as:
   L̂2CMMD = Tr(KdK̃⁻¹d LdK̃⁻¹d) + Tr(KsK̃⁻¹s LsK̃⁻¹s) - 2 · Tr(KsdK̃⁻¹d LdsK̃⁻¹s)
   However, the dimensions of these matrices do not align for the proposed operations. For example, Kd is Υd^T Υd (N×N), Ld is Φd^T Φd (N×N), and K̃⁻¹d is also N×N. The expression KdK̃⁻¹d LdK̃⁻¹d would require Kd to be N×M, but it's actually N×N, making the matrix multiplication undefined.

2. **Theoretical Justification Gap**: The paper claims "the empirical estimator Φ(K + λI)⁻¹Υ⊤ is always an element in the tensor product space which gives as a well-justified approximation (i.e., the Hilbert-Schmidt norm exists) for practical use [29]." This is a significant claim that requires more justification. Why is the empirical estimator a valid approximation for practical use? The paper provides no theoretical justification for this.

3. **Algorithm Ambiguity**: Algorithm 1 states "For each x ∈ B, generate a y" but doesn't specify whether the noise h is sampled independently for each x in the batch or if the same h is used for all x. This critical detail for reproducibility is missing.

### Presentation and Clarity Issues
1. **Inconsistent Terminology**: The paper inconsistently uses "CGMMN" (with capitalization) versus "CGMMN" throughout the text.

2. **Citation Errors**: Multiple citations are incomplete (e.g., "Deep generative image models using a laplacian pyramid of adversarial networks, E. Denton, S. Chintala, A. Szlam, R. Fergus, NIPS, ,"). There's also a typo in "Generative adverisarial nets" (should be "adversarial").

3. **MNIST Data Split**: The paper states "The whole dataset is divided into 3 parts with 50,000 training examples, 10,000 validation examples and 10,000 testing examples," but this is inconsistent with the standard MNIST split (60k/10k). The authors don't justify this unusual split.

4. **Missing Comparison**: The paper claims to "largely extend the capability of GMMN" but doesn't clearly articulate what specific limitations of GMMN are overcome by CGMMN. The connection between GMMN and CGMMN needs more explicit explanation.

### Originality Concerns
The paper fails to adequately distinguish its contribution from existing conditional GAN work. The Mirza and Osindero (2014) conditional GAN work [21] already addresses conditional generation, and the paper doesn't clearly explain how CGMMN differs from this approach. The claim that "this work fills the research void to significantly improve the applicability of moment-matching networks" is unsubstantiated without a thorough comparison with existing conditional GAN methods.

## Overall Assessment

The paper presents an interesting extension of GMMN to conditional settings with promising experimental results. However, the technical inconsistencies in the matrix operations, the lack of theoretical justification for key claims, and the failure to adequately distinguish the contribution from existing work significantly undermine the paper's technical credibility.

The paper also contains multiple minor but persistent formatting and citation issues that suggest a lack of careful proofreading. While the paper's core idea has potential, the methodological flaws and lack of sufficient theoretical justification for key claims make it difficult to evaluate the proposed method's true merits.

The paper would benefit from a thorough revision to correct the mathematical inconsistencies, provide clearer theoretical justification, and better articulate the novelty compared to existing conditional generative models. Without these revisions, the paper's contribution cannot be properly assessed.

## Recommendation
**Reject**

The paper contains multiple technical errors in the mathematical formulation, lacks sufficient theoretical justification for key claims, and fails to adequately distinguish its contribution from existing work. While the experimental results are promising, the methodological flaws prevent a proper evaluation of the proposed approach. The paper does not meet the technical standards expected at NIPS.
