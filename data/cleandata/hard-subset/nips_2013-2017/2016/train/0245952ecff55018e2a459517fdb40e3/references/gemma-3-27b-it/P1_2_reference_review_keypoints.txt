- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional generation using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) training objective.  
- CMMD enables sampling conditioned on inputs and is grounded in kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation across datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face).  
- Positions work relative to GANs, GMMNs, conditional GANs/VAEs, and kernel MMD literature.  
- Theoretical foundation: clear theorems on Hilbert space embedding and conditional distributions supporting the method.  
- Training likely more stable and simpler than GANs due to absence of adversarial min-max optimization.  
- CGMMN is a flexible, general framework applicable to multiple conditional generation tasks.  
- Empirical results are competitive and experiments are well-presented and analyzed.  
- Paper is generally well-written with adequate technical explanations for reproduction.  
- Novelty is limited: conditional kernel embedding ideas have prior work (e.g., Song et al., 2013); contribution is mainly implementation within GMMN.  
- Performance appears sensitive to hyperparameters (e.g., CMMD regularization λ) with insufficient guidance or ablation studies.  
- Lacks detailed implementation specifics (learning rates, batch sizes, architectures); code release would improve reproducibility.  
- Comparisons omit some stronger, recent generative baselines (e.g., advanced GAN variants).  
- Computational complexity discussion is incomplete—matrix inverses addressed but overall cost vs. GANs/VAEs not analyzed.  
- Evaluation of generated samples is limited; lacks quantitative metrics like Inception Score or FID.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound extension, competitive empirical performance, and a stable MMD-based alternative to GANs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, missing implementation details/code, and incomplete comparisons to state-of-the-art.  
- Overall assessment: above-average contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions and encouragement to release code.  
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
