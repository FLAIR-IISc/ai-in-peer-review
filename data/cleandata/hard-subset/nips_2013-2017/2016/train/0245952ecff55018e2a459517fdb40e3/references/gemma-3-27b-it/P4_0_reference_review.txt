This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to learn a generative model p(y|x) by minimizing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) between the empirical conditional distribution and the model’s generated distribution. The paper clearly articulates the motivation for conditional generative models, highlighting applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and building larger probabilistic models. The proposed CMMD criterion and the overall approach build upon existing work in MMD and kernel methods for distribution comparison.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Problem Definition and Motivation:** The paper effectively presents the need for conditional generative models and justifies the proposed approach. The identified application areas are relevant and well-explained.
*   **Solid Theoretical Foundation:** The derivation of the CMMD criterion is mathematically sound, building upon established concepts like Hilbert space embeddings and MMD. The connection to existing work (GMMN, MMD) is clearly established.
*   **Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation:** The authors evaluate CGMMN on diverse tasks—predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation—demonstrating the versatility of the approach. Results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.
*   **Practical Considerations:** The paper addresses practical aspects of implementation, such as mini-batch optimization and handling inverse kernel matrices, offering valuable insights for potential users.
*   **Thorough Related Work:** The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the related literature, citing relevant work on generative models (GANs, VAEs), MMD, and kernel methods.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Novelty:** While CGMMN extends GMMN to the conditional setting, the core idea of applying MMD for conditional distribution matching isn't entirely novel. Similar concepts appear in prior work on conditional kernel embeddings (e.g., Song et al., 2013). The paper could more explicitly contrast its contributions with these prior approaches.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of kernel methods, including CGMMN, can be sensitive to the choice of kernel and bandwidth parameters (λ in the paper). The paper lacks a detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on the results.
*   **Computational Cost:** The computational complexity of computing and inverting kernel matrices (even with mini-batch approximations) can be significant, particularly for large datasets. This limitation isn't thoroughly addressed in the paper.
*   **Clarity of Figure 1:** While conceptually useful, Figure 1 lacks detail regarding the architecture of the DNN (number of layers, types of activation functions, etc.), making it difficult to fully understand the model implementation.
*   **Limited Ablation Studies:** The paper presents results with both MLP and CNN architectures, but doesn't conduct extensive ablation studies to assess the contribution of different components or design choices within the CGMMN framework.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and well-motivated extension of GMMN to conditional generative modeling.
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness and versatility of the proposed approach across a range of tasks.
*   The paper is clearly written and provides sufficient details for reproducibility.
*   The work addresses a relevant and important problem in generative modeling.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The novelty of the approach is incremental, building upon existing work in kernel methods and conditional embeddings.
*   The paper doesn't sufficiently address the computational challenges associated with kernel-based methods.
*   A more thorough analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and model design choices would strengthen the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid paper that presents a practical and effective approach to conditional generative modeling. While the novelty is incremental, the technical soundness, comprehensive experimental evaluation, and clear presentation make it a valuable contribution to the field. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above, specifically a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter tuning, computational complexity, and related work.

**Rating:** Accept (with minor revisions)
