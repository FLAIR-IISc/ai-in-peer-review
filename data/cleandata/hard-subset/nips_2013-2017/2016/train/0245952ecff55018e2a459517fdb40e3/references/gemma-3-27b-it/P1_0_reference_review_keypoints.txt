- Summary: Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) loss to learn p(y|x) by mapping noise and x to y.
- Relation to prior work: Extends GMMN to conditional setting, positioned against GANs/VAEs and grounded in kernel MMD literature.
- Strength — Conceptual simplicity: CGMMN is an elegant, straightforward extension of GMMN to conditional generation.
- Strength — Training stability: MMD-based objective avoids GAN min-max instability and yields more stable training.
- Strength — Theoretical foundation: CMMD is justified via kernel mean embeddings and supporting theorems.
- Strength — Versatility: Applicable to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.
- Strength — Competitive performance: Experimental results are often comparable to state-of-the-art across tasks.
- Strength — Clear exposition: Paper is well-written with helpful figures and clear experimental setup.
- Weakness — Hyperparameter sensitivity: Performance may depend strongly on kernel choice and bandwidth, not thoroughly analyzed.
- Weakness — Computational cost: Kernel matrix computation is expensive for large datasets; scalability discussion is limited.
- Weakness — Limited ablation studies: Insufficient exploration of network architectures and hyperparameter effects (e.g., MNIST architectures).
- Weakness — Comparison gaps: Missing comparisons to more recent/stronger generative models (advanced GANs, normalizing flows).
- Weakness — Lack of qualitative Yale Face samples: Qualitative results for Yale Face dataset are scarce.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, well-motivated CMMD extension with stable training, versatile experiments, clear writing, and potential impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: Scalability concerns, insufficient hyperparameter/architecture analysis, and missing stronger baselines.
- Overall assessment: Solid contribution offering a compelling, simpler alternative to GANs/VAEs despite computational and sensitivity concerns.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
- Requested revision 1: Add a detailed discussion and analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choices and parameters).
- Requested revision 2: Provide a computational complexity analysis of CMMD and discuss scalability strategies.
- Requested revision 3: Expand ablation studies to evaluate architectural and hyperparameter impacts.
- Requested revision 4: Include qualitative generated samples for the Yale Face dataset.
- Requested revision 5: If feasible, add comparisons to more recent and powerful generative baselines.
