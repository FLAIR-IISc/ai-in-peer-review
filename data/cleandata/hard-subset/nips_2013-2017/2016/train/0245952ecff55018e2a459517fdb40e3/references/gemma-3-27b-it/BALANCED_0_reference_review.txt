This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the capabilities of GMMNs to address conditional distribution learning. The core idea is to leverage a deep neural network to map a simple distribution, combined with conditional variables, to generate target samples, optimized via a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks.

The paper builds upon recent advancements in generative modeling, particularly GMMNs [19, 3] and GANs [5], offering an alternative approach with a simpler optimization objective compared to GANs. The contribution lies in adapting the MMD-based framework to the conditional setting, providing a theoretically sound and practically implementable method for learning conditional generative models.  The related work section appropriately cites relevant papers in generative modeling and kernel methods.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting is a valuable contribution. CMMD provides a principled way to measure the difference between conditional distributions, building on the theoretical foundation of kernel methods.
* **Theoretical Soundness:** The paper provides a clear mathematical formulation of CMMD, grounded in Hilbert space embedding theory, and demonstrates its connection to the equivalence of distributions. Theorems 1, 2, and 3 provide a solid theoretical justification for the approach.
* **Practicality:** The proposed method leverages back-propagation for efficient training, making it amenable to large-scale datasets and complex network architectures.
* **Empirical Validation:** The authors demonstrate the versatility of CGMMN through a diverse set of experiments, including predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation. Competitive results are achieved across these tasks.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and organized, making the key ideas and technical details accessible to readers familiar with generative modeling and kernel methods.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** While the paper mentions regularization (λ), a more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choice, network architecture, learning rate, etc.) would strengthen the empirical analysis. How do these parameters affect performance and stability?
* **Limited Comparison to Strong Baselines:** In some experiments (e.g., MNIST predictive modeling), the comparison to state-of-the-art methods isn't completely exhaustive. While CMMN is compared to several established techniques, including more recent works (DSN) would bolster the claims.
* **Computational Cost:**  The paper acknowledges the cubic complexity of kernel matrix computation and proposes a mini-batch approach.  A more detailed analysis of the computational cost compared to other methods (e.g., GANs or VAEs) would be useful, particularly for large datasets. Specifically, how does the mini-batch approach affect the accuracy of the CMMD estimation?
* **Assumptions of CMMD:** The paper briefly mentions the assumptions required for the CMMD objective to be well-defined. A more detailed discussion of these assumptions and their potential impact on practical applications would be beneficial.  Are there scenarios where the CMMD objective may be unreliable?
* **Visual quality:** While the visual results in the generating task are adequate, there's room for improvement, and comparison against more recent state-of-the-art generative models (e.g., StyleGAN) could provide additional context.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.
* The proposed CMMD criterion offers a principled approach to learning conditional generative models.
* The empirical results demonstrate the versatility and competitiveness of CGMMN across a range of tasks.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key ideas and technical details.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper lacks a detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and computational cost.
* The comparison to state-of-the-art methods is not always exhaustive.
* The discussion of the assumptions underlying the CMMD objective could be more thorough.
* The visual quality of the generated samples, while adequate, is not state-of-the-art.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN framework is novel, theoretically well-founded, and empirically validated. While some areas could be strengthened (hyperparameter analysis, computational cost, comparison to stronger baselines), the overall quality of the work warrants acceptance.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above. Specifically, I suggest adding a section discussing the computational complexity in more detail, performing a more thorough hyperparameter sensitivity analysis, and including a more comprehensive comparison to recent state-of-the-art generative models.
