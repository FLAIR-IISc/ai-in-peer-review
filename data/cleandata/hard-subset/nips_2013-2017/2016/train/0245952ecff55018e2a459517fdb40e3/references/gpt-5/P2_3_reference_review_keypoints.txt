- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to model p(y|x) with a conditional deep generator f(x,h;w).
- Main contribution is Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), measuring the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators, with a closed-form kernel Gram-matrix estimator and Tikhonov regularization.
- Training via stochastic gradients/backprop through generated samples; gradients flow through y-kernels only, allowing flexible kernels on x.
- Builds on prior GMMN and kernel conditional embedding work; positioned as a non-adversarial counterpart to conditional GANs and conditional VAEs.
- Theoretical grounding is sound: Theorem 3 (operator equality implies conditional equality) is appropriate under universality/smoothness; empirical operator estimator is standard.
- Using the Hilbert–Schmidt norm yields a tractable trace-form CMMD; regularization and required assumptions are acknowledged.
- Mini-batch CMMD reduces cubic costs, but the statistical bias/variance properties of the mini-batch estimator are not analyzed.
- Practical kernel choices, bandwidth selection rules, and regularization λ are not fully specified or justified in the main text.
- Experiments cover predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces, AE+CGMMN), and Bayesian distillation (PBP on Boston housing).
- Empirically CGMMN-CNN is competitive with prior DGM baselines and produces reasonable conditional samples; distillation preserves RMSE.
- Major empirical weakness: no quantitative comparison to conditional GANs or cVAEs for generation and mostly qualitative evaluation without modern image metrics.
- Classification via CMMD on one-hot labels is an unusual choice versus cross-entropy; no ablation comparing to the same architecture trained discriminatively.
- Sensitivity analyses to kernel choice, bandwidth, and λ are missing, harming reproducibility.
- Computational cost per batch and scalability to large/continuous high-dim condition spaces are not reported.
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized; trace-form derivation is useful, but some notation is confusing and several implementation details are deferred.
- Originality: a novel and natural extension of GMMN to conditionals; AE+CGMMN parallels prior GMMN+AE; Bayesian distillation application is a nice addition.
- Significance: offers a stable, simple alternative to adversarial training for conditional generation and broadens MMD-based training, though empirical impact is modest.
- Strengths: principled CMMD objective, simple backprop training (no adversarial min–max), flexible kernels, and diverse applications with competitive results.
- Weaknesses: limited quantitative generative evaluation, missing kernel/tuning sensitivity and mini-batch analysis, brief complexity discussion, unclear advantage over cross-entropy for classification.
- Requests for authors: specify kernels on x and y, bandwidth selection method, and λ values; provide sensitivity plots.
- Requests for authors: report CMMD computation time per batch and effect of batch size on stability/performance.
- Requests for authors: include quantitative generation metrics (e.g., Parzen, classifier accuracy) and comparisons to cGAN/cVAE.
- Requests for authors: compare classification results to an identical architecture trained with cross-entropy to isolate CMMD’s effect.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — principled, useful contribution with solid theory but empirical validation and implementation details need strengthening.
