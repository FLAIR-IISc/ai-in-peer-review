

# Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to handle conditional distributions. The authors introduce Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as the training objective and demonstrate the approach on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation tasks.

## Strengths
The paper presents a clear extension of GMMN to conditional settings, which is a valuable contribution to the generative modeling literature. The CMMD objective is well-motivated and theoretically grounded in Hilbert space embeddings. The experimental results are comprehensive across multiple tasks, and the implementation details are sufficiently detailed for reproducibility. The authors successfully demonstrate that CGMMN achieves competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods in various applications.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical inconsistencies**: The paper contains several notational and theoretical inconsistencies that undermine its technical credibility. Theorem 3 states "CY|X = CZ|X" without defining Z, which should refer to the conditional distribution PZ|X. The paper also inconsistently uses "MMD[K, PX, PY]" and "MMD[K, p, q]" without clarifying the notation. The definition of φ(x) = k(x, ·) in Section 2.1 is non-standard; typically, φ(x) is the feature map and k(x, x') = ⟨φ(x), φ(x')⟩.

2. **Technical inaccuracies**: The claim that "the inverse matrices K̃⁻¹ₛ and K̃⁻¹d in the CMMD objective are independent of the model parameters" is misleading. While K̃ = K + λI doesn't depend on model parameters w, K = ΥᵀΥ does depend on the input data, which is fixed during training but not independent of the model. This subtle point matters for understanding the optimization landscape.

3. **Formatting and typographical errors**: The paper contains multiple typos ("Varitional Auto-encoder" should be "Variational Auto-encoder"), inconsistent capitalization ("Maximum Mean Discrepancy" vs. "maximum mean discrepancy"), and incorrect references (e.g., "[5]" cited for nearest neighbor distance when [5] refers to the GAN paper). The reference to "recent work [1]" in the introduction is inconsistent with the citation style (should be [21] based on the references).

4. **Experimental details**: The paper claims "we did not observe much difference with a higher dimension (e.g., 20 or 50)" but provides no evidence for this assertion. The comparison to CVAE (Conditional Variational Autoencoders) is notably absent, which is a standard approach for conditional generation that should have been included for context.

5. **Methodological ambiguities**: The paper doesn't clearly explain why a uniform prior is chosen for the hidden variables in Section 3.2, especially when they acknowledge alternative approaches like adding Gaussian noise to conditional variables. The claim that CGMMN "largely extends the capability of GMMN" lacks concrete examples of tasks GMMN cannot handle.

6. **Statistical significance**: The paper reports performance numbers without statistical significance tests, making it difficult to assess whether observed differences are meaningful. For example, the statement that "CGMMN-CNN is competitive with various state-of-the-art competitors" lacks statistical evidence.

## Minor but significant issues

The paper's title and abstract claim CGMMN "extends the capability of GMMN," but the connection to previous conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs [21], CVAE [28]) is underdeveloped. The authors cite conditional GANs but don't explicitly compare to them or explain how CGMMN differs in a meaningful way beyond the objective function.

In Section 4.1.1, Table 1 reports "CGMMN" for the MLP case but "CGMMN-CNN" for the CNN case, creating inconsistent naming throughout the paper. The paper also claims "CGMMN is compatible with various network architectures" but doesn't demonstrate this by showing results with different architectures beyond MLP and CNN.

## Recommendation

While the paper presents a technically sound extension of moment-matching networks to conditional settings, the numerous minor but significant errors and inconsistencies in notation, references, and experimental details undermine the paper's credibility. The theoretical foundations are largely correct but presented with insufficient precision, and the experimental section lacks sufficient detail to fully evaluate the claims. The paper would benefit from a thorough proofreading to address the numerous formatting and notational inconsistencies, and it should more explicitly compare to relevant work on conditional generative models.

I recommend rejection due to the cumulative effect of these minor but important issues, which collectively weaken the paper's scientific contribution. While the core idea is interesting, the presentation's technical imprecision and inconsistent execution prevent it from meeting NIPS's high standards for a rigorous scientific contribution.
