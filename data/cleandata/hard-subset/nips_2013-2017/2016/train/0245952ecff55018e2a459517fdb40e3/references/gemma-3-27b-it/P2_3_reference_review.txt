## Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

**Summary:**

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting. The core idea is to learn a conditional distribution p(y|x) by training a deep network to map a simple distribution (e.g., uniform) along with the conditioning variable x to samples of y, and optimizing this mapping using a novel conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Extension of GMMNs:** The paper provides a logical and well-motivated extension of existing GMMN work to handle conditional distributions, a crucial capability for many applications.
* **Novel CMMD Criterion:**  The proposed CMMD criterion is a significant contribution, providing a theoretically sound and practical method for training conditional generative models based on moment matching.  The derivation and explanation of CMMD are reasonably clear.
* **Strong Empirical Validation:** The authors demonstrate the versatility of CGMMN across a diverse set of tasks – predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  Competitive results are reported against established baselines.
* **Well-Written & Organized:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with clear explanations of the key concepts and mathematical formulations. The inclusion of a detailed preliminary section is helpful for readers unfamiliar with MMD and kernel embeddings.
* **Practical Considerations:** The authors address practical considerations like mini-batch training and the estimation of the conditional embedding operator, demonstrating awareness of implementation challenges.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty Beyond CMMD:** While the CMMD criterion is a strong contribution, the overall architecture (deep network mapping a simple distribution) is relatively standard in the generative modeling literature (e.g., similar to approaches used in GANs and VAEs).  The novelty primarily lies in the specific moment-matching approach and the CMMD formulation.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't extensively discuss the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameters (e.g., kernel bandwidth, regularization parameter λ).  A more thorough analysis of these parameters would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Comparison to Stronger Baselines:** In some experiments (e.g., MNIST prediction), the comparison is primarily against models from 2015-2016.  More recent and state-of-the-art generative and discriminative models should be included for a more comprehensive evaluation.
* **Theoretical Assumptions:** The CMMD derivation relies on assumptions about the smoothness of the conditional embedding operator. While the authors acknowledge this, a discussion of the practical implications of violating these assumptions would be valuable.
* **Visualizations could be Improved:** The generated samples (Fig. 2 and Fig. 5) are of reasonable quality but could be visually more compelling.  Generating more samples and perhaps using a different visualization method (e.g., latent space interpolation) could provide a better understanding of the model's generative capabilities.



**Quality:**

The paper is technically sound and the claims are generally well-supported by experimental results. The mathematical derivations appear correct and the empirical evaluation is reasonably thorough. However, the novelty of the overall approach is somewhat limited, and a more detailed analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity and comparison to more recent baselines would strengthen the claims.

**Clarity:**

The paper is well-written and organized, making it relatively easy to follow the key ideas. The authors provide clear explanations of the mathematical concepts and the training procedure.

**Originality:**

The core contribution of the paper is the CMMD criterion, which is a novel and interesting approach to conditional generative modeling. The overall architecture is less novel, but the application of MMD to the conditional setting is a significant contribution.

**Significance:**

CGMMN has the potential to be a useful tool for a variety of applications requiring conditional generative modeling. The CMMD criterion could be adopted by other researchers working in this area.  The results on Bayesian dark knowledge distillation are particularly promising.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and well-motivated CMMD criterion for conditional generative modeling.
* Strong empirical results across diverse tasks.
* Clear and well-organized presentation.
* Addresses a significant problem in unsupervised learning.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited novelty beyond the CMMD criterion.
* Hyperparameter sensitivity not thoroughly explored.
* Comparison to more recent and stronger baselines would be beneficial.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept** with minor revisions. The CMMD criterion is a significant contribution to the field of generative modeling, and the empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN.  Addressing the weaknesses outlined above (particularly the comparison to more recent baselines and the analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity) would further strengthen the paper. The paper is of high quality, clearly written, and has the potential to be influential in the field.
