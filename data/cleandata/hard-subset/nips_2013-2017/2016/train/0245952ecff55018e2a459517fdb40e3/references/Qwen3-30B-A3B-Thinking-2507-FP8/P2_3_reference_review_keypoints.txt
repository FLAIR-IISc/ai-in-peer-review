- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions via Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).  
- Provides theoretical foundation: shows equality of conditional embedding operators implies equality of conditional distributions (Theorem 3) using kernel embedding framework (Theorem 2).  
- CGMMN avoids adversarial training instability and is straightforward to implement with standard backpropagation and mini-batch CMMD estimation.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability with experiments on predictive modeling, conditional image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art (e.g., CGMMN-CNN achieves 3.13% error on SVHN, comparable to CMMVA).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a helpful preliminaries section and logical presentation of experiments.  
- Limited empirical comparison with conditional GANs, leaving sample quality, stability, and efficiency vs. cGANs underexplored.  
- Insufficient discussion of method limitations, including scaling of CMMD with conditional variable dimensionality and handling of complex conditional dependencies.  
- Theoretical gaps remain: lacks convergence/bias guarantees or detailed statistical analysis of the CMMD estimator.  
- No ablation studies to isolate the impact of components (e.g., conditional embedding operator, kernel choices) on performance.  
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practically useful contribution that extends moment-matching to conditional settings and merits acceptance.
