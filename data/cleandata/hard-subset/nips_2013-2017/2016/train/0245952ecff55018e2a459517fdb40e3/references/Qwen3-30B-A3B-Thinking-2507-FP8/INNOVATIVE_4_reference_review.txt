

**Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"**

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a significant extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to handle conditional distributions. The authors develop Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a training objective for conditional generative models, enabling the learning of flexible conditional distributions without the min-max optimization required by conditional GANs. The paper makes a compelling case for the importance of conditional modeling in predictive tasks, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.

The theoretical foundation is strong, with clear derivations of CMMD and its relationship to kernel embedding of conditional distributions. The authors properly contextualize their work against related conditional generative models like conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and conditional VAEs (So et al., 2015), demonstrating how CGMMN offers a simpler training objective while maintaining generative flexibility.

The experimental section demonstrates CGMMN's versatility across three distinct applications:
1. **Predictive modeling**: CGMMN-CNN achieves competitive results on MNIST and SVHN datasets, matching or exceeding state-of-the-art DGMs like CMMVA while using a simpler training objective.
2. **Contextual generation**: The model successfully generates diverse samples conditioned on labels for MNIST and Yale Face datasets, with qualitative results showing meaningful generation rather than data copying.
3. **Bayesian dark knowledge**: CGMMN effectively distills predictive distributions from Bayesian models (PBP) without degrading prediction performance on the Boston housing dataset.

The paper's strongest contribution is its bold extension of GMMN to conditional settings—a meaningful gap in the literature that opens up numerous practical applications. While GMMN was previously limited to unconditional generation, CGMMN enables moment-matching networks to address a broad class of conditional modeling problems that were previously dominated by GANs and VAEs. This is particularly valuable as conditional modeling is crucial for many real-world applications, and CGMMN provides a simpler training process than conditional GANs.

The authors effectively demonstrate CGMMN's flexibility by applying it to diverse tasks with comparable performance to specialized methods. The simplicity of the approach (no min-max optimization, straightforward backpropagation training) is a significant practical advantage over conditional GANs.

Minor weaknesses include:
- The theoretical guarantees could be strengthened (e.g., convergence analysis for CMMD optimization)
- Some experimental details are sparse (hyperparameters, training times)
- Limited comparison with all conditional GAN variants

However, these are minor concerns given the paper's significant contribution. The conditional extension of GMMN represents a meaningful advancement that addresses an important practical gap. The authors have clearly demonstrated the applicability of their approach across multiple domains, and the method is simple enough to be widely adopted.

This paper makes a bold contribution by expanding the utility of moment-matching networks to conditional distributions—a substantial step beyond the original GMMN framework. While the method builds on prior work, the conditional extension represents a significant new direction that opens up many new application possibilities. The results across diverse tasks show that CGMMN is not just a theoretical extension but a practical solution that competes with specialized methods.

I believe the novelty and practical significance of this work outweigh the minor methodological limitations. The paper addresses an important gap in the literature and provides a simple, effective solution that can be applied to a wide range of conditional modeling problems. This aligns with NIPS's emphasis on significant new directions in machine learning.

**Recommendation**: Accept

**Pro Arguments**:
- Significant extension of GMMN to conditional distributions, addressing a meaningful gap in the literature
- Novel CMMD metric with solid theoretical foundation
- Demonstrated applicability across multiple domains (predictive modeling, generation, Bayesian distillation)
- Simple training process compared to conditional GANs
- Competitive results with state-of-the-art methods

**Con Arguments**:
- Somewhat incremental in methodology (extending GMMN rather than introducing a completely new framework)
- Limited theoretical guarantees beyond the basic CMMD formulation
- Some experimental details could be more complete

The paper presents a bold and valuable extension to the generative modeling literature that should be of significant interest to the NIPS community. The conditional extension of GMMN represents a meaningful step forward in making moment-matching networks more broadly applicable.
