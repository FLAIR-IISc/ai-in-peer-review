- Paper introduces Conditional GMMN (CGMMN), learning p(y|x) via a deep network trained with Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- Clear and logical extension of GMMN to conditional generation; CMMD is a natural adaptation of MMD.
- Solid theoretical foundation via kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings, supported by theorems.
- Demonstrates broad applicability across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation.
- Empirical results on MNIST, SVHN, and Bayesian dark knowledge are competitive with existing methods.
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes a helpful algorithm description.
- Main novelty is limited—core idea (MMD for generative modeling) is inherited from GMMN and the extension feels incremental.
- Performance appears sensitive to kernel choice and regularization (λ); ablation on hyperparameters and choice of input noise distribution is lacking.
- Missing comparisons to stronger conditional generative baselines like conditional GANs and CVAEs.
- CMMD estimation can be computationally expensive (kernel matrix inversions); scalability and complexity are not fully addressed.
- Limited quantitative evaluation of generated samples (e.g., no Inception Score or FID reported).
- Quality: technically sound but needs deeper analysis of CMMD’s strengths/weaknesses; Clarity: generally clear; Originality and significance: moderate.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled MMD-based conditional modeling, competitive results, clear presentation, useful GMMN extension.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient hyperparameter and scalability analysis, and lack of comparisons to stronger baselines.
- Overall assessment: borderline accept/reject, leaning toward reject pending major revisions (add cGAN comparisons, hyperparameter/scalability analysis).
