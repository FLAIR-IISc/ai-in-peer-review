- Overall assessment: solid extension of GMMNs to conditional setting (CGMMN) with well-motivated conditional MMD (CMMD) and recommendation to accept with minor revisions.  
- Core contribution: propose CGMMN that trains a DNN generator using a novel CMMD objective to model conditional distributions.  
- Theoretical foundation: derivation of CMMD is clear and supported by Theorem 3, grounding the approach in MMD theory.  
- Practical significance: addresses important limitation of GMMNs by enabling conditional generation for predictive and contextual tasks.  
- Simplicity: training objective is straightforward and avoids instability of adversarial (min-max) training.  
- Generality & flexibility: framework applies to multiple conditional tasks, demonstrated across prediction, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical validation: comprehensive experiments on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face with ablations and comparisons to strong baselines.  
- Presentation: paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful algorithmic details (Algorithm 1).  
- Limited novelty: extension to conditional MMD is natural and not a fundamental breakthrough relative to conditional GANs/VAEs.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance likely depends on kernel choice and regularization λ, with insufficient guidance or sensitivity analysis.  
- Computational cost: kernel matrix computation/inversion can be expensive and scalability concerns are not fully addressed (e.g., Nyström not explored).  
- Image quality concerns: generated images appear blurry/noisy; visual fidelity comparisons to state-of-the-art conditional GANs/VAEs are lacking.  
- Hilbert–Schmidt assumptions: theoretical assumptions are discussed briefly but practical implications and workarounds need more detail.  
- Latent prior choice: using a uniform prior for hidden variables h seems arbitrary; effect of other priors (e.g., Gaussian) is not explored.  
- Experimental protocol issues: unclear use of validation set and early stopping in MNIST experiments.  
- Diversity/quality metrics missing: lack of quantitative metrics like Inception Score or FID to objectively assess generative quality and diversity.  
- Reproducibility detail missing: Bayesian dark knowledge experiment (PBP) lacks sufficient architectural/hyperparameter details.  
- Recommendation for authors: clarify novelty, provide kernel/regularization guidance, discuss scalability and mitigation strategies, add quantitative image metrics, and supply more PBP experiment details.
