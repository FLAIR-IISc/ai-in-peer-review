

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to learn conditional distributions using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD). The authors build upon the kernel mean embedding framework established in previous NIPS work (Gretton et al., JMLR 2012; Song et al., ICML 2009) and extend the GMMN approach (Li et al., ICML 2015) to handle conditional distributions. The key innovation is developing CMMD as a criterion to measure the difference between conditional distributions, enabling training via backpropagation without the min-max optimization challenges of GANs.

The paper demonstrates CGMMN's effectiveness across three application domains: predictive modeling (on MNIST and SVHN datasets), contextual generation (with conditional labels), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The experimental results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, particularly showing strong performance on classification tasks where CGMMN-CNN achieves 3.13% error on SVHN (comparable to CMMVA's 3.09%).

Strengths include clear theoretical motivation, well-executed experiments across diverse tasks, and a simple training procedure that avoids the instability of GAN training. The paper also provides useful connections to prior work on kernel embeddings and conditional distributions. The methodology is technically sound with appropriate theoretical grounding.

Weaknesses include limited comparison to recent conditional GAN variants (e.g., conditional WGAN-GP), minimal ablation studies on kernel choices or hidden dimensions, and insufficient discussion of computational complexity relative to competing methods. While the Bayesian dark knowledge application is interesting, the regression results on Boston housing are somewhat preliminary.

Quality: Good technical foundation with well-supported claims (4/5). Clarity: Well-organized but some technical details could be more precise (4/5). Originality: Extends GMMN to conditional settings, which is valuable but not revolutionary (3.5/5). Significance: Demonstrates utility across multiple applications, though the advance over conditional GANs isn't fully established (3.5/5).

Pro: Strong experimental validation, clear methodology, good connection to prior kernel embedding work. Con: Limited theoretical novelty, insufficient comparison to recent conditional generative models, minimal discussion of computational trade-offs.

Overall, this is a solid contribution that extends the GMMN framework to conditional settings with practical benefits. While not a major theoretical leap, the paper provides a useful alternative to conditional GANs with simpler training dynamics. I recommend acceptance.
