This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to learn conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The approach leverages back-propagation for efficient training. The authors evaluate CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, demonstrating competitive performance.

The paper builds upon recent advancements in deep generative modeling, notably GMMNs [19, 3] and GANs [5], offering an alternative approach that avoids the challenges associated with the min-max optimization in GANs. The extension to conditional distributions addresses a practical limitation of standard GMMNs, opening up applications in predictive modeling and contextual generation, areas where conditional models are often preferred [23, 16]. The use of MMD, rooted in kernel methods [7], provides a theoretically grounded framework for comparing distributions without strong parametric assumptions. The Bayesian dark knowledge application, drawing from [15], further demonstrates the versatility of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Problem Definition:** The paper clearly articulates the problem of learning conditional distributions within the generative modeling framework and motivates the need for CGMMNs.
*   **Theoretical Foundation:** The derivation of CMMD builds logically upon existing MMD theory [7, 29] and provides a solid theoretical basis for the proposed method. The theorems presented are well-defined and contribute to the understanding of the approach.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method appears technically sound, leveraging back-propagation for efficient training, a crucial aspect for deep learning models. The algorithm presented (Algorithm 1) is reasonably clear.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The evaluation across diverse tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge) demonstrates the practical utility of CGMMNs. Competitive results are reported against established baselines.
*   **Connections to Existing Work:** The paper effectively situates its contributions within the broader landscape of generative modeling, acknowledging and building upon related work in GANs, GMMNs, and Bayesian methods.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity of CMMD Objective:** The derivation and explanation of the CMMD objective (Equation 2) could benefit from further clarity. While the mathematical formulation is presented, a more intuitive explanation of the terms involved, particularly regarding the kernel gram matrices, would improve understanding. The discussion regarding the assumptions needed for the objective to be well-defined could be expanded upon.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper lacks a detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity. The choice of kernel, regularization parameter (λ), and network architecture significantly impacts performance, and a more systematic exploration of these parameters would strengthen the empirical analysis.
*   **Limited Ablation Studies:**  The experiments don't include comprehensive ablation studies to isolate the contribution of different components of the CGMMN framework. For instance, analyzing the impact of different network architectures (MLP vs. CNN) in more detail or varying the dimensionality of the hidden variables would provide valuable insights.
*   **Experimental Detail:** While results are presented for multiple datasets, details on the precise training procedures (optimization algorithms, learning rates, and epoch numbers) could be more thoroughly documented to aid reproducibility.
*   **Comparison to Conditional GANs/VAEs:** A more direct comparison to recent conditional GANs [21] and conditional VAEs [28] would offer a more nuanced assessment of CGMMNs' strengths and weaknesses. The paper mentions these related works but doesn't provide a head-to-head performance comparison.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically grounded extension of GMMNs to conditional distributions.
*   The empirical results demonstrate competitive performance on a range of tasks, suggesting the practical utility of the proposed method.
*   The use of back-propagation enables efficient training of the model.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly articulates its contributions.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The explanation of the CMMD objective could be more intuitive and accessible.
*   The lack of a detailed hyperparameter analysis and ablation studies limits the robustness of the empirical validation.
*   A more thorough comparison to recent conditional GANs and VAEs is needed to establish a clear understanding of the method's relative strengths and weaknesses.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling by extending GMMNs to handle conditional distributions. The theoretical foundation is solid, and the empirical results are promising. While some aspects of the paper could be improved with more detailed analysis and comparison, the overall quality and novelty of the work justify acceptance. I recommend accepting the paper with minor revisions to address the clarity and experimental detail concerns. Specifically, the authors should elaborate on the CMMD objective, provide a more thorough discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, and consider a more direct comparison with recent conditional GANs and VAEs.
