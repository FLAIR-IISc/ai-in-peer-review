- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).  
- Extends kernel mean embedding and GMMN frameworks to conditional settings, enabling training via backpropagation without min–max GAN optimization.  
- CMMD is developed as the criterion to measure differences between conditional distributions.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Experimental results are competitive with state-of-the-art; CGMMN-CNN achieves 3.13% error on SVHN (comparable to CMMVA’s 3.09%).  
- Strengths: clear theoretical motivation, sound methodology, diverse and well-executed experiments, and simple/stable training.  
- Provides useful connections to prior work on kernel embeddings and conditional distributions.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to recent conditional GAN variants (e.g., conditional WGAN-GP).  
- Weakness: minimal ablation studies on kernel choices and hidden dimensions.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity relative to competing methods.  
- Bayesian dark knowledge application is interesting, but regression results (Boston housing) are somewhat preliminary.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 4/5, Clarity 4/5, Originality 3.5/5, Significance 3.5/5.  
- Overall: solid, practically useful extension of GMMN to conditional settings; recommend acceptance.
