- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions.  
- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a new objective comparing kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Trains a deep generative model using CMMD that inputs conditional variables plus noise.  
- Provides a clear introduction to the problem and limitations of existing approaches.  
- Offers a thorough related-work review, including GMMN and GANs.  
- Approach is well-motivated with a clear explanation of CMMD and CGMMN training.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly describes the method.  
- Experiments are thorough, covering predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Experimental results are well-presented and demonstrate effectiveness across tasks.  
- Strength: Novel CMMD objective with clear motivation and explanation.  
- Strength: Comprehensive, well-presented experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: High clarity and organization of the paper.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detailed analysis of CMMD and its relation to GMMN/GANs.  
- Weakness: Lacks insight into hyperparameter choices and tuning.  
- Weakness: Needs a more detailed discussion of limitations and future work.  
- Overall: Well-written, clear, and experimentally strong contribution.  
- Scores: Originality 8/10, Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- For acceptance: Novel CMMD, thorough evaluation, and clear writing.  
- Against acceptance: Requires deeper CMMD analysis, hyperparameter details, and discussion of limitations.
