- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Network (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to learn conditional distributions.
- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) by conditioning on input variables plus extra random noise passed through a deep network to produce target samples.
- Targets applications including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge (student network distillation).
- Theoretical analysis supports the method and claims; the paper is technically sound.
- Experimental results demonstrate effectiveness across multiple tasks.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides enough detail for expert reproduction.
- Original contribution: novel CMMD-based framework and a new approach for building student networks in Bayesian dark knowledge.
- Significant: advances state of the art and offers practical ideas likely useful to researchers and practitioners.
- Pro: clear explanation, strong experimental validation, and addresses a difficult problem better than previous work.
- Con: needs more comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.
- Con: implementation details are sparse and could be expanded.
- Con: discussion of limitations and directions for future work is limited.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
