- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that learn p(y|x) by minimizing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- Defines CMMD as the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators in tensor RKHSs, estimated via regularized kernel ridge regression.
- Generator concatenates x with noise h and maps through a DNN; parameters learned by backprop through a trace-form CMMD objective computed from Gram matrices.
- Evaluated on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (class-conditional MNIST, Yale Faces, with/without autoencoder), and Bayesian distillation (replicating PBP on Boston).
- Builds on GMMN and kernel conditional mean embedding literature, and parallels conditional GAN/CVAE but uses a single non-adversarial kernel-based loss.
- Theoretical: CMMD criterion is well-motivated (matching conditional mean embeddings implies matching conditionals under assumptions) and estimator is standard with regularization.
- Practical estimator and trace-form objective are implementable with minibatches; assumptions and regularization needs are acknowledged.
- Algorithmically straightforward minibatch training; kernel on x need not be differentiable since inverses are parameter-independent.
- Experiments: predictive accuracy competitive with deep generative baselines but below strong discriminative models; conditional samples visually plausible and improved by autoencoder; distillation matches teacher.
- Main empirical shortcomings: no likelihood/FID/diversity metrics for generation, limited ablations (kernels, λ, batch size, noise dim), no runtime/scalability analysis, and missing head-to-head cGAN/CVAE comparisons.
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well-organized, with concise reviews of kernel embeddings and CMMD, but omits important implementation details in the appendix.
- Missing details include exact kernel choices and bandwidth selection, computational complexity and wall-clock runtimes, memory footprint, batch-size stability, and how outputs y are kernelized for classification.
- Originality: novel application of conditional mean embedding discrepancy as a training objective for deep conditional generators; architecture is standard but loss and mini-batched implementation are original.
- Significance: broadly applicable and appealing as a stable, non-adversarial objective, but empirical evidence does not yet show clear advantages over cGAN/CVAE and scalability is uncertain.
- Strengths: conceptually simple and well-motivated objective, avoids GAN instability, flexible conditioning kernels, competitive results, successful Bayesian distillation, and clear derivations.
- Weaknesses: limited quantitative generation evaluation, computational scalability concerns (O(b^3) per-batch inverses, quadratic memory), sparse ablations, under-specified output-kernel choices, and no finite-sample generalization bounds.
- Suggested improvements: add quantitative generation metrics (FID/Inception/diversity), compare to cGAN/CVAE on same tasks, provide scalability approximations (RFF/Nyström, preconditioning), ablate kernels and hyperparameters, and report calibration, runtime, and reproducibility details.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, elegant conditional objective that is a simpler, stable alternative to adversarial training with competitive results across tasks.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete empirical evaluation and baselines, unresolved scalability and computational trade-offs, and insufficient ablation/detailing.
- Recommendation: Weak accept — interesting and technically sound contribution, but empirical and scalability analyses should be strengthened.
