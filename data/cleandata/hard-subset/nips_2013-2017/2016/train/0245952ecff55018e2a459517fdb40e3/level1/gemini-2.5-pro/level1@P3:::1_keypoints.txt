- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that learn p(y|x) by minimizing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- Extends GMMN to conditional setting using kernel conditional mean embeddings and trains a deep net that maps (x, h) -> y with backprop on CMMD.  
- Novelty/significance: principled and theoretically grounded extension of moment-matching to conditional modeling.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, with clear motivation, background, and model/training description.  
- Technical quality: CMMD formulation and empirical estimator are sound and integrated into SGD-based deep learning.  
- Experiments: comprehensive and diverse—predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Results: competitive predictive performance and plausible conditional generation; effective for model compression ("Bayesian dark knowledge").  
- Computational complexity: CMMD requires computing/inverting Gram matrices per minibatch (O(B^3)), which may limit scalability.  
- Missing comparisons: lacks direct experimental/qualitative comparisons to key baselines (cGAN, CVAE) to contextualize performance and stability.  
- Sample quality: generated images can be noisy/sharpness-limited; using autoencoder latent space improves results, suggesting limitations modeling pixels directly.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: CMMD relies on λ and kernel choice/bandwidth; selection and sensitivity analysis are underreported.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, principled, non-adversarial objective; clear exposition; strong and varied experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unaddressed scalability concerns and absence of direct comparisons to most relevant conditional generative baselines.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
