- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), a principled extension of MMD to measure dissimilarity between conditional distributions via kernel mean embedding operators.
- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that generate y given x by passing x and noise h through a deep network trained to minimize empirical CMMD.
- CMMD objective is differentiable and enables end-to-end training with SGD, providing a non-adversarial alternative to cGANs.
- Demonstrates versatility across tasks: classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image synthesis (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian model distillation.
- Strength: original, theoretically grounded contribution filling a gap for moment-matching in conditional generation.
- Strength: technically sound formulation and clear connection between MMD and CMMD with appropriate use of RKHS theory.
- Strength: potential for more stable training than adversarial methods and broad empirical applicability.
- Strength: paper is clearly written with helpful background and architecture/training descriptions.
- Weakness: CMMD requires inversion of kernel Gram matrices, yielding O(B^3) complexity in mini-batch size and raising scalability concerns.
- Weakness: no discussion of scalable kernel approximations (e.g., random features, Nyström) to mitigate cubic cost.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on kernel choices and hyperparameter selection for x and y, hurting reproducibility and sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness: directly generated samples are noisier than contemporary GAN results and rely on a pre-trained autoencoder for high-quality outputs; discrete-label estimator variant is relegated to the appendix.
