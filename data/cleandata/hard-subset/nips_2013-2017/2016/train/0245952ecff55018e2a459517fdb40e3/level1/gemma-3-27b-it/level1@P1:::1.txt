This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) as an extension of GMMNs to handle conditional distributions. The core idea is to leverage the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion for learning, enabling applications like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The authors demonstrate competitive performance across these tasks.

This work builds upon the growing body of research utilizing MMD for generative modeling, notably GMMNs [19, 3] and GANs [5]. While GANs have achieved remarkable results, their training instability is a well-known issue. GMMNs offer a more stable alternative by framing the generative modeling problem as a moment matching task. Extending this to the conditional setting with CGMMNs is a logical and valuable step, addressing a common need in many applications.  Prior work on conditional GANs [21] provides a comparison point, with CGMMNs aiming for a simpler and potentially more stable training process. The use of MMD for conditional distributions is also related to work on kernel embedding of conditional distributions [29], although the authors focus on practical implementation via CMMD.  The Bayesian dark knowledge distillation aspect connects to recent work in model compression and knowledge transfer [15].

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The theoretical foundation based on kernel methods and MMD appears solid. The derivation of CMMD and its connection to the Hilbert-Schmidt norm provide a principled approach. The use of backpropagation for gradient estimation is practical and efficient.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and outlines the contributions. The mathematical notation is mostly clear, and the explanation of CMMD is reasonably accessible.
* **Originality:** Extending GMMNs to the conditional setting is a novel contribution. The CMMD criterion provides a new way to approach conditional generative modeling. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge is also interesting and practical.
* **Significance:**  The ability to learn conditional distributions is fundamental to many machine learning tasks. CGMMNs offer a potentially more stable and simpler alternative to existing methods like conditional GANs. The demonstrated performance across diverse tasks suggests practical utility.  The application to Bayesian dark knowledge is particularly promising for model compression and efficient inference.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the derivation of CMMD is presented, a more detailed analysis of its properties (e.g., convergence guarantees, sensitivity to kernel choice) would strengthen the paper. The assumption about smoothness and Hilbert-Schmidt properties is acknowledged but lacks deeper investigation.
* **Experimental Details:**  The experimental section, while demonstrating competitive results, lacks some important details.  For example, the kernel used for MMD/CMMD is not explicitly stated. A more thorough ablation study varying kernel parameters and network architectures would provide better insights. More details on the hyperparameter tuning process are also needed.
* **Comparison to Baselines:** While the authors compare to several baselines, the comparison isn’t consistently thorough. In some cases (e.g., predictive performance), the baselines are not state-of-the-art methods. More recent and competitive methods should be included for a more robust evaluation.
* **Scalability:**  The computational cost of calculating the CMMD, especially with large datasets, is a potential concern. Although the authors mention mini-batch implementation, a more detailed discussion of scalability issues and potential optimizations would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.
* The CMMD criterion offers a potentially more stable and simpler alternative to existing methods like conditional GANs.
* The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance across a range of tasks.
* The application to Bayesian dark knowledge is a significant and practical contribution.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis could be more thorough.
* The experimental details are somewhat lacking, hindering reproducibility and deeper insights.
* The comparison to baselines could be more comprehensive.
* Scalability concerns are not fully addressed.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of generative modeling. The idea of extending GMMNs to the conditional setting with CMMD is novel and promising. While the paper has some weaknesses, particularly regarding theoretical analysis and experimental detail, the strengths outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised above. Specifically, the authors should provide more details on the kernel choice, hyperparameter tuning, and scalability considerations. A more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art baselines would also strengthen the paper.
