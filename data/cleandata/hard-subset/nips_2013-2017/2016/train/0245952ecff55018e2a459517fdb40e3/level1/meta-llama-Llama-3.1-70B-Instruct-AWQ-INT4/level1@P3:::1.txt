**Summary of the Paper**

The paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a deep generative model that learns a conditional distribution given some input variables. The model is based on the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, which measures the difference between the kernel mean embedding of an empirical conditional distribution and that of the generative model. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon the work on Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) [Li et al., 2015], which uses Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) to learn a joint distribution of variables. The authors extend GMMN to learn a conditional distribution by introducing CMMD, which is a natural extension of MMD to the conditional setting. The paper also relates to other deep generative models, such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) [Goodfellow et al., 2014] and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) [Kingma and Welling, 2014].

**Strengths**

1. **Simple and efficient training process**: The paper proposes a simple and efficient training process for CGMMN using stochastic gradient descent with back-propagation.
2. **Flexibility**: The model can be used for various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.
3. **Competitive performance**: The authors demonstrate that CGMMN achieves competitive performance on various tasks, including image classification and generation.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited theoretical analysis**: The paper lacks a thorough theoretical analysis of the CMMD criterion and its relationship to other metrics, such as MMD.
2. **Assumptions on the kernel**: The paper assumes that the kernel is smooth and satisfies certain properties, which may not always be the case in practice.
3. **Limited comparison to other models**: The paper only compares CGMMN to a few other models, such as VAEs and GANs, and does not provide a comprehensive comparison to other deep generative models.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a new deep generative model, CGMMN, which is based on the CMMD criterion. The model is a natural extension of GMMN to the conditional setting and provides a new perspective on learning conditional distributions.

**Significance**

The paper has significant implications for various applications, including image classification, generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The proposed model provides a new tool for learning conditional distributions, which is a fundamental problem in machine learning and statistics.

**Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance**

Pro:

* The paper proposes a new deep generative model that achieves competitive performance on various tasks.
* The model is simple and efficient to train, making it a practical tool for many applications.

Con:
