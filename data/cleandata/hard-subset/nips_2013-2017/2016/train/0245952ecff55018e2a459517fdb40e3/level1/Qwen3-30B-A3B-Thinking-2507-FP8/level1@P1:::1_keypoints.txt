- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Targets three applications: predictive modeling (p(y|x)), contextual generation (conditioned sample generation), and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation (student approximating Bayesian predictions).  
- Builds on prior work (GMMN, conditional GANs, kernel embedding techniques) and contrasts with conditional VAEs and cGANs by emphasizing simpler training.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid: Theorem 3 provides a rigorous justification for using CMMD to match conditional distributions.  
- Training is simpler than GANs — uses backpropagation without min-max optimization dynamics.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, and Bayesian distillation tasks.  
- Ablation studies and comparisons to relevant baselines (CMMVA, DSN, CNN, etc.) show competitive performance.  
- Paper is very well written and organized, with clear mathematical formulations, proofs, and reproducible experimental details.  
- Figures effectively illustrate model capabilities and results.  
- Original contribution: novel extension of moment-matching networks to conditional settings, filling a gap in literature.  
- Bayesian dark-knowledge application is novel and well-executed.  
- Method addresses practically important problems and demonstrates broad relevance across applications.  
- Lacks discussion of limitations, e.g., handling multimodal conditional distributions and validity of smooth conditional embedding assumptions.  
- Comparison with conditional GANs is limited, especially on generation tasks where GANs typically excel.  
- Practical implementation detail missing: insufficient explanation of how CMMD is adapted to mini-batch training (Algorithm 1).  
- Could better distinguish contributions from concurrent conditional GAN/conditional VAE work, including training dynamics and guarantees.  
- Unclear whether CGMMN advances state-of-the-art beyond existing conditional methods; Bayesian distillation advantages need more justification.  
- Pro: novel conditional extension, rigorous theory, comprehensive experiments, simple training, and practical relevance.  
- Con: limited discussion of limitations, incomplete GAN comparisons, unclear mini-batch CMMD adaptation, and less clear SOTA advancement.  
- Recommendation: accept — significant, timely contribution with solid theory and validation despite some gaps to address.
