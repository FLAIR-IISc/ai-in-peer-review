This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to conditional distributions using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The core idea is sound: leveraging moment matching in a Hilbert space to learn conditional generative models offers a potentially attractive alternative to GANs and VAEs, particularly given the simpler training objective compared to GANs. The paper positions itself well within the existing literature on deep generative models, citing relevant works like GANs, GMMNs, and VAEs, and clearly articulating the motivation for focusing on conditional generation.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** While building on GMMNs, extending them to the conditional setting with CMMD is a clear contribution. The theoretical justification based on kernel embeddings and the associated theorems (3) provide a strong foundation.
* **Technical Soundness:** The derivation of the CMMD objective (Eq. 2) appears mathematically sound, and the authors acknowledge and discuss the assumptions underlying its validity. The proposed algorithm (Alg. 1) seems practical and addresses the computational challenges of MMD estimation.
* **Empirical Evaluation:** The paper demonstrates the applicability of CGMMN across three diverse tasks – predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation – on standard datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Boston Housing). Competitive performance is reported relative to existing methods, suggesting the approach is viable.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and logically structured. The introduction clearly outlines the motivation and contributions. The preliminary section provides necessary background on Hilbert space embeddings and MMD.
* **Completeness:** The paper addresses key aspects of the approach, including the generative model architecture, the learning objective, and the optimization algorithm. It also acknowledges potential limitations and discusses related work.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Ablation Studies:** The experiments primarily focus on demonstrating overall performance. A more thorough analysis of the impact of different architectural choices (e.g., MLP vs. CNN, hidden layer sizes) and hyperparameters (e.g., kernel bandwidth, regularization parameter λ) would strengthen the conclusions.
* **Comparison Baselines:** While several baselines are included, some comparisons feel incomplete. For instance, the predictive modeling results on MNIST don't include comparisons with more recent and potentially stronger models. The choice of baselines for each task could be better justified.
* **Computational Complexity:**  The paper acknowledges the cubic complexity of computing kernel gram matrices, but doesn’t fully address scalability concerns. While mini-batch optimization is mentioned, a more detailed discussion of its effectiveness in high-dimensional settings would be valuable.
* **Theoretical Assumptions:** The paper correctly points out the assumptions required for the CMMD objective to be well-defined, but doesn't provide much guidance on how to assess or mitigate violations of these assumptions in practice.
* **Visual Quality (Generation):** While the MNIST generation results (Fig. 2 & 4) are reasonable, the visual quality isn't as sharp or diverse as those produced by state-of-the-art GANs or VAEs.  This suggests that the CGMMN might struggle with capturing the full complexity of the data distribution.  More rigorous quantitative evaluation of generative quality (e.g., Inception Score, FID) would be beneficial.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to conditional generative modeling.
* The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance across diverse tasks.
* The paper is well-written and clearly articulates the contributions.
* The work addresses an important challenge in deep learning – learning flexible conditional distributions.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of detailed ablation studies and a more comprehensive comparison with state-of-the-art models weakens the empirical validation.
* The computational complexity of the approach and the assumptions underlying the CMMD objective raise practical concerns.
* The generated samples lack the visual quality of some other generative models.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a solid contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting with CMMD is a valuable addition to the toolkit. While there are some weaknesses, particularly regarding experimental analysis and scalability, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should:

1.  Include a more thorough ablation study, investigating the impact of different architectural choices and hyperparameters.
2.  Compare with stronger and more recent baselines where appropriate.
3.  Discuss scalability concerns in more detail and potentially explore techniques for reducing the computational cost.
4.  Consider including quantitative metrics for evaluating generative quality.
5.  Expand the discussion on assessing and mitigating violations of the assumptions required for the CMMD objective.
