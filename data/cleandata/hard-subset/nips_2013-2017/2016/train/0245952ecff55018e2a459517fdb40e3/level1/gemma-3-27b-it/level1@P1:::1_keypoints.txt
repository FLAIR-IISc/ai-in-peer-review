- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional MMD (CMMD) to learn conditional distributions.
- Targets applications: predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, showing competitive performance.
- Builds on MMD/GMMN and GAN literature, aiming for a simpler, more stable conditional generative method and relating to kernel embedding of conditional distributions.
- Strength — Technical soundness: principled CMMD derivation based on kernel methods and Hilbert–Schmidt norm with practical backprop gradient estimation.
- Strength — Clarity: well-written, clear motivation, organized presentation, and mostly clear mathematical notation.
- Strength — Originality: novel extension of GMMNs to conditional setting and interesting application to Bayesian dark knowledge.
- Strength — Significance: addresses fundamental conditional learning tasks and offers a potentially more stable alternative to conditional GANs with practical utility.
- Weakness — Limited theoretical analysis: lacks deeper study of CMMD properties, convergence guarantees, and sensitivity to kernel choice.
- Weakness — Experimental details lacking: kernel choice not specified, missing ablation studies, and insufficient hyperparameter tuning details.
- Weakness — Baseline comparisons incomplete: some evaluations omit recent or state-of-the-art baselines.
- Weakness — Scalability concerns: CMMD computation may be costly on large datasets; mini-batch implementation and optimization strategies need more discussion.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, technically sound method with competitive experiments and useful application to model compression.
- Argument against acceptance: theoretical gaps, reproducibility concerns from incomplete experimental detail, incomplete baselines, and unaddressed scalability.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to detail kernel choice, hyperparameter tuning, scalability, and stronger baseline comparisons.
