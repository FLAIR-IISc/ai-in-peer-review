

# Peer Review: Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to conditional distributions using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors build upon GMMN (Li et al., ICML 2015), which uses MMD to learn generative models without the unstable training dynamics of GANs. CGMMN addresses three key applications: predictive modeling (where it learns p(y|x) instead of joint p(x,y)), contextual generation (generating samples conditioned on specific inputs like class labels), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation (learning a small student network to approximate Bayesian model predictions).

The paper makes a clear and meaningful extension of GMMN to conditional settings. It relates well to previous work, particularly GMMN (Li et al., 2015), conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014), and kernel embedding techniques (Gretton et al., JMLR 2012). The authors effectively distinguish their approach from conditional VAEs (So et al., NIPS 2015) and conditional GANs by emphasizing the simplicity of training via backpropagation without min-max optimization.

**Strengths:**

*Quality:* The theoretical foundation is sound, with Theorem 3 providing a rigorous justification for using CMMD to ensure conditional distribution matching. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian knowledge distillation. The ablation studies and comparison with relevant baselines (including CMMVA, DSN, CNN, etc.) demonstrate the approach's competitiveness.

*Clarity:* The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The mathematical formulations are clearly presented with appropriate theorems and proofs. The experimental section provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, including network architectures, hyperparameters, and training procedures. Figures 1-5 effectively illustrate the model's capabilities.

*Originality:* The extension of moment-matching networks to conditional distributions represents a novel contribution that fills an important gap in the literature. While conditional GANs and VAEs exist, CGMMN offers a simpler training procedure without the min-max optimization challenges of GANs. The Bayesian dark knowledge application is particularly novel and well-executed.

*Significance:* The method addresses important practical problems in machine learning, particularly in scenarios where conditional modeling is more natural than joint modeling. The applications to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation demonstrate broad relevance. The competitive results across multiple tasks suggest the approach will be valuable to practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**

*Quality:* The paper lacks a thorough discussion of the method's limitations. For instance, it doesn't address cases where the conditional distribution is multimodal or where the assumption of smooth conditional embedding operators (needed for CMMD) might fail. The comparison with conditional GANs is limited to predictive modeling, and it would be valuable to see how CGMMN performs in generation tasks where GANs typically excel.

*Clarity:* While the paper is generally clear, the connection between the theoretical CMMD formulation and the practical implementation (Algorithm 1) could be strengthened. The paper mentions using a mini-batch version but doesn't fully explain how the CMMD objective is adapted to mini-batches, which is crucial for practical implementation.

*Originality:* The paper could better distinguish its contribution from the concurrent work on conditional GANs (e.g., Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and conditional VAEs (So et al., 2015). While the authors acknowledge these works, a more detailed comparison of the training dynamics, theoretical guarantees, and practical performance would strengthen the originality claim.

*Significance:* The paper demonstrates competitive performance but doesn't clearly establish how CGMMN advances the state-of-the-art beyond what's already achievable with conditional GANs or VAEs. The Bayesian dark knowledge application is promising but needs more discussion on why CGMMN is particularly well-suited for this task compared to other distillation approaches.

**Pro/Con Acceptance Arguments:**

*Pro:*
- Novel extension of moment-matching networks to conditional distributions
- Theoretical foundation with rigorous justification
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple challenging tasks
- Simple training procedure without min-max optimization
- Practical applications with competitive results

*Con:*
- Limited discussion of method limitations
- Comparison with conditional GANs is incomplete
- Insufficient detail on mini-batch implementation
- Less clear demonstration of advancement over existing conditional methods

**Recommendation:**

I recommend acceptance. The paper makes a significant contribution by extending moment-matching networks to conditional distributions, addressing an important gap in the literature. The method is theoretically sound, well-validated across multiple applications, and demonstrates competitive performance. While the paper could benefit from addressing some of the limitations noted above, these do not undermine the core contribution. The work aligns well with NIPS' focus on high-quality, scientifically rigorous machine learning research. The extension to conditional distributions is particularly timely given the growing interest in conditional generative models. The paper would be valuable for both theoretical and applied machine learning researchers.
