- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions via a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to handle a variety of conditional tasks.  
- CMMD is presented as a simple, efficient, and well-motivated criterion for learning conditional distributions.  
- CGMMN is applicable to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and technically well-organized.  
- Technical sections provide a thorough explanation of the CMMD criterion and CGMMN architecture.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough across tasks and shows competitive performance versus state-of-the-art methods.  
- Assumes substantial background in deep learning and kernel methods, which may hinder non-expert readers.  
- Lacks sufficient intuition and explanation for the choice of CMMD and architectural decisions.  
- Some experimental results (e.g., image generation) need more detailed analysis and evaluation.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to provide more intuition/explanation and improve experimental analysis.  
- Rating: 8/10.
