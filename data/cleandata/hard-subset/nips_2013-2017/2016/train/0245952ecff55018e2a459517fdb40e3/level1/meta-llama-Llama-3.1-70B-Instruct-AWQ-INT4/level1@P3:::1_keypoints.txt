- Proposes CGMMN, a deep generative model that learns conditional distributions using the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Measures discrepancy via kernel mean embeddings between empirical conditional distributions and the generative model.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual generation, Bayesian dark knowledge, and image tasks.  
- Extends GMMN by moving from MMD (joint) to CMMD (conditional); relates to GANs and VAEs.  
- Strength: simple and efficient training with stochastic gradient descent and backpropagation.  
- Strength: flexible applicability across tasks (prediction, conditional generation, distillation).  
- Strength: shows competitive empirical performance on image classification and generation.  
- Weakness: lacks thorough theoretical analysis of CMMD and its relation to other metrics.  
- Weakness: assumes smooth kernels with specific properties that may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparisons to the broader family of deep generative models.  
- Originality: natural and novel extension of GMMN to the conditional setting, offering a new perspective.  
- Significance: potentially important for applications and for advancing methods to learn conditional distributions.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding deeper theoretical analysis of CMMD and broader comparative experiments.
