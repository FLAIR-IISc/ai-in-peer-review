- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) to model conditional distributions.
- Introduces a deep generative architecture that combines input variables with auxiliary stochastic variables for conditional sample generation.
- Evaluates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks.
- Demonstrates competitive performance against strong baselines across diverse datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face).
- Novel extension of GMMN to conditional settings, offering a simpler training alternative to adversarial methods.
- Solid theoretical grounding with clear derivations linking CMMD to kernel mean embeddings and MMD, and a practical empirical estimator.
- Broad applicability shown by experiments across multiple tasks and datasets.
- Paper is well-written and well-organized, with effective figures and tables.
- Lacks direct comparisons with Conditional GANs and Conditional VAEs in the generative experiments.
- Scalability concerns due to kernel methods' cubic complexity; mini-batch variant proposed but not thoroughly evaluated on very large datasets.
- Generated samples show diversity but exhibit visual artifacts and noise; combining with autoencoders partially mitigates this.
- Missing detailed ablation studies to quantify effects of kernel choice, network architecture, and auxiliary variables.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address scalability issues and include additional comparisons with Conditional GANs/VAEs.
