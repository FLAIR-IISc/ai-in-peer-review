- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Enables tasks like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid, based on kernel mean embeddings and Hilbert space operators.  
- Novel contribution filling the gap of conditional modeling for GMMN, aligning with conditional GANs/VAEs.  
- CMMD formulation and gradient computation via backprop enable efficient training.  
- Empirical results are competitive across tasks (predictive modeling on MNIST/SVHN, generative on MNIST/Yale Face, knowledge distillation).  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Lacks detailed ablation studies (e.g., impact of architectures and kernel choices).  
- Insufficient discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly the regularization λ.  
- Comparisons omit some strong recent baselines (e.g., normalizing flows), limiting assessment of advantages.  
- Scalability concerns due to kernel matrix computations; mini-batch approaches mentioned but not fully analyzed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem, is theoretically sound and novel, and shows encouraging empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing comprehensive ablations, limited hyperparameter analysis, and restricted baseline comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, with encouragement to address ablation studies and hyperparameter/scalability analyses in a revision.
