- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to model conditional distributions.
- Core idea is sound: moment matching in RKHS offers a simpler alternative to GANs/VAEs for conditional generation.
- Positions work well within literature, citing GANs, GMMNs, and VAEs and motivates conditional generation clearly.
- Novelty: clear contribution extending GMMNs to conditional setting with theoretical justification via kernel embeddings and Theorem 3.
- Technical soundness: CMMD derivation (Eq. 2) and practical algorithm (Alg. 1) are mathematically justified and address MMD estimation challenges.
- Empirical evaluation: demonstrates applicability across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge on MNIST, SVHN, and Boston Housing with competitive results.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, logically structured, and provides necessary background on Hilbert space embeddings and MMD.
- Completeness: covers model architecture, learning objective, optimization algorithm, related work, and acknowledges limitations.
- Weakness: limited ablation studies on architecture and hyperparameter impacts (e.g., MLP vs CNN, kernel bandwidth, λ).
- Weakness: comparison baselines are incomplete; some tasks lack recent or stronger model comparisons and justifications for baseline choices.
- Weakness: computational complexity concerns—kernel Gram matrix computation is cubic and scalability discussion is insufficient despite mention of mini-batching.
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions for CMMD are stated but guidance on assessing or mitigating assumption violations in practice is lacking.
- Weakness: visual quality of generated samples is weaker than state-of-the-art GANs/VAEs; lacks rigorous quantitative metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID).
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound approach; competitive experimental results; well-written and addresses an important problem.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ablation and stronger baselines; scalability and practical concerns from CMMD assumptions; lower visual sample quality.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution; strengths outweigh weaknesses; recommends acceptance with minor revisions.
- Requested revisions: add thorough ablation studies on architectures/hyperparameters.
- Requested revisions: compare against stronger and recent baselines where appropriate.
- Requested revisions: expand discussion and solutions for scalability and reduce computational cost.
- Requested revisions: include quantitative metrics for generative quality.
- Requested revisions: provide guidance on assessing and mitigating violations of CMMD assumptions.
