- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to model conditional distributions.  
- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) using kernel mean embeddings to learn conditional distributions.  
- Builds on prior kernel embedding work (Song et al., 2009; Grunewalder et al., 2012) with a logically derived extension from MMD to CMMD.  
- Avoids adversarial training instability of GANs by using the simpler MMD-based criterion.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation with competitive results.  
- Experiments are well-structured with appropriate baselines and thorough evaluations across datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, Boston housing).  
- Bayesian dark knowledge application is novel and practically useful for model compression.  
- Paper is technically clear and well-written with clear mathematical formulations and logical flow.  
- Lacks comprehensive empirical comparison with conditional GANs on the same benchmarks, limiting assessment of relative advantages.  
- Insufficient theoretical analysis of CMMD learning—missing convergence properties and generalization bounds.  
- Discussion of limitations is underdeveloped, notably the cause and impact of noisy generated samples and the role of the autoencoder fix.  
- Unclear and insufficiently demonstrated advantages over other conditional generative methods (conditional VAEs, conditional GANs); novelty appears incremental.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically grounded extension, practical utility across domains, stable alternative to GANs, and novel Bayesian application.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate comparisons to dominant methods, incomplete theoretical treatment, unclear advantages, and insufficiently addressed limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound with well-executed experiments; recommend acceptance with minor revisions contingent on stronger comparative and theoretical analysis.
