- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions p(y|x), extending GMMN to the conditional setting.  
- Core technical contribution: Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective using Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators.  
- Generative model: deep neural network taking conditioning variable x and noise h to produce y, trained by minimizing CMMD via SGD and backprop.  
- Positions CGMMN as an alternative to cGANs and CVAEs, claiming simpler/stable training than cGANs and a non-variational alternative to CVAEs.  
- Empirical evaluation covers predictive tasks (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST digits by label, Yale faces by identity), and Bayesian model distillation ("Bayesian dark knowledge").  
- Originality/significance: principled extension of MMD to conditional modeling, increasing applicability to structured prediction and contextual generation.  
- Theoretical soundness: CMMD grounded in established kernel mean embedding theory and well-motivated derivations.  
- Experiments are broad and convincing, demonstrating competitiveness in discriminative tasks and a novel distillation application.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, organized, and provides intuitive links between MMD and CMMD.  
- Scalability concern: CMMD requires computing/inverting kernel Gram matrices (O(B^3) time, O(B^2) memory), which limits usable mini-batch sizes and is not fully addressed.  
- Sample quality: generated images are noisier/less sharp than contemporary GANs; using an autoencoder improves quality but adds extra training steps.  
- Lack of qualitative side-by-side comparisons with cGANs on generation tasks limits assessment of visual quality.  
- Missing implementation details: kernel choices, bandwidth selection, and regularization λ tuning are not described, hindering reproducibility.  
- Pro-acceptance reasons: novel principled method, simpler/stabler objective than adversarial training, strong theoretical grounding, and extensive experiments.  
- Con-acceptance reasons: unresolved scalability, image quality lagging GANs without extra components, and omitted hyperparameter selection details.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, timely contribution that advances MMD-based generative modeling; reviewer recommends acceptance.
