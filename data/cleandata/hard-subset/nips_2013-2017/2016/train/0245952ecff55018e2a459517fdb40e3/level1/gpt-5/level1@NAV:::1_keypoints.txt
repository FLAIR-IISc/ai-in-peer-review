- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that train a conditional generator y = f(x,h;w) by minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD) — the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators of data and model.  
- Leverages conditional kernel mean embeddings and computes a mini-batch CMMD objective via Gram matrices, optimized with backpropagation.  
- Demonstrates empirical results on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, reporting competitive performance.  
- Relates to prior work: extends GMMN, compares conceptually to GANs/cGANs and conditional VAEs, offering a non-adversarial conditional training objective.  
- Theoretical contribution: CMMD formulation is technically sound and Theorem 3 provides a clear sufficiency result under standard conditional embedding assumptions.  
- Practical estimator/training: mini-batch CMMD and SGD/backprop pipeline are reasonable, but finite-sample behavior and the effects of regularization and kernel choice are not deeply analyzed.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well-organized with useful MMD/embedding background; minor typos and missing implementation details remain.  
- Missing implementation specifics: kernel choices/bandwidth selection and computational/memory costs of inverting K̃ per batch are not detailed.  
- Originality: operator-level matching for conditional generators is novel relative to prior MMD-based deep generative models.  
- Significance: presents a promising, simpler alternative to cGAN/CVAE training dynamics, but tested mainly on standard tasks; broader/higher-dimensional conditional generation would increase impact.  
- Pros: conceptually clean CMMD objective that avoids adversarial training; bridges kernel conditional embeddings and deep generators; versatile applications; straightforward SGD/backprop pipeline.  
- Cons: limited quantitative evaluation of uncertainty (NLL, calibration) and multimodality; sensitivity to kernel/bandwidth/λ not explored; scalability concern due to O(B^3) kernel inverse per batch; lacks direct comparisons to CVAE/cGAN on conditional generation.  
- Suggestions: add ablations on kernels and regularization, report calibration/NLL and distillation speedups, compare to CVAE/cGAN, consider random Fourier features to reduce cubic costs, and discuss bandwidth-selection stability.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
