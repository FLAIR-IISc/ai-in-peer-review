This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN training with a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The core idea is to learn a flexible conditional generator y = f(x, h; w), trained by minimizing the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators of the empirical data and the model. The method leverages conditional kernel mean embeddings (Song et al., 2009; Grunewalder et al., 2012) and computes a mini-batch CMMD objective via Gram matrices, optimized by backpropagation. Empirically, the paper evaluates predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and “Bayesian dark knowledge” distillation (Korattikara et al., 2015), showing competitive results. The work relates to GMMN (Dziugaite et al., 2015), GANs and conditional GANs (Goodfellow et al., 2014; Mirza & Osindero, 2014), and conditional VAEs (Sohn et al., 2015), positioning CMMD as a simpler, non-adversarial conditional training objective.

Quality: The CMMD formulation is technically sound and well-motivated; Theorem 3 gives a clear sufficiency result under standard conditional embedding assumptions. The empirical estimator and mini-batch training are reasonable, though finite-sample properties and the impact of regularization (λ) and kernel choices are not deeply analyzed. Experiments are solid but lack quantitative uncertainty metrics and broader comparisons on conditional generation.

Clarity: Generally clear and well-organized, with helpful background on MMD and conditional embeddings. Minor typos and some missing implementation details (kernel choices/bandwidth selection, computational/memory costs of K̃−1 per batch) could be improved.

Originality: The use of operator-level matching to train conditional generators is novel relative to prior MMD-based DGMs, and distinct from adversarial or variational conditional training.

Significance: Promising alternative to cGAN/CVAE with simpler training dynamics. The demonstrated tasks are standard; broader, higher-dimensional conditional generation or structured prediction would strengthen impact.

Pros:
- Conceptually clean CMMD objective; avoids adversarial min–max.
- Bridges kernel conditional embeddings and deep generators.
- Versatile applications (prediction, generation, distillation) with competitive results.
- Straightforward SGD/backprop pipeline.

Cons:
- Limited quantitative evaluation of uncertainty (e.g., NLL, calibration) and multimodality.
- Scalability concerns: O(B^3) kernel inverses per batch; no RFF/NTK/deep-kernel alternatives.
- Sensitivity to kernel/bandwidth/λ not explored; few ablations.
- Comparisons to CVAE/cGAN on conditional generation are missing.

Suggestions: Add ablations on kernels and λ; report calibration/NLL and speedup in distillation; compare to CVAE/cGAN on contextual generation; consider random Fourier features to reduce cubic costs; discuss bandwidth selection stability.

Recommendation: Weak accept.
