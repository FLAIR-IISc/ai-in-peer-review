This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a framework for learning conditional distributions with deep generative models. The core technical contribution is a novel training objective called Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), which measures the distance between conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space. This work is a logical and important extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN), which leveraged MMD for joint distributions, and provides a compelling non-adversarial alternative to Conditional GANs (cGANs). The authors demonstrate the model's effectiveness on a wide range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

**Quality & Clarity:** The paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written. The theoretical motivation for CMMD is well-grounded in the literature on kernel mean embeddings, and its derivation from first principles is easy to follow. The experimental evaluation is thorough, providing strong empirical support for the proposed method's versatility. The claims are well-supported by the results presented.

**Originality & Significance:** The formulation of a CMMD objective for training deep conditional generative models is a novel and significant contribution. It addresses the important problem of conditional modeling while avoiding the unstable min-max dynamics often associated with GAN training. This provides the community with a valuable and more stable alternative. The application to "Bayesian dark knowledge" is particularly interesting, showcasing a creative use case for the framework that could inspire future work in model compression and distillation. The work significantly enhances the applicability of moment-matching networks, filling an important gap in the literature.

**Weaknesses:** One weakness is that the quality of the generated images appears slightly less sharp than contemporary GAN-based approaches, as evidenced by the need for an autoencoder to produce smoother samples. A second point for improvement is the discussion of computational complexity; the CMMD objective requires kernel matrix inversion, which scales cubically with the mini-batch size. While tractable for the batch sizes used, a more explicit analysis of this computational trade-off would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   Introduces a novel and theoretically elegant CMMD objective for conditional generation.
*   Offers a stable, non-adversarial training alternative to cGANs.
*   Demonstrates impressive versatility across diverse and challenging tasks.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**
*   Generated sample quality lags slightly behind state-of-the-art adversarial methods.
*   Potential scalability concerns due to kernel matrix inversion in the loss function.

In summary, this is a strong and well-executed paper presenting a novel and useful contribution to the field of generative modeling. Its strengths clearly outweigh its weaknesses.
