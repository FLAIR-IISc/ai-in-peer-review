- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Extends the MMD criterion from GMMN to the conditional setting, a non-trivial methodological contribution.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Provides clear, well-organized exposition with thorough background and related work.  
- Learning algorithm and CMMD criterion are presented clearly and concisely.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and ablation studies.  
- Experimental results are strong and competitive across multiple tasks.  
- Strength: novel and effective deep generative model for conditional distributions.  
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation supporting claims.  
- Weakness: assumes substantial background in deep learning/generative models, limiting accessibility.  
- Weakness: lacks deeper intuition for why CMMD effectively learns conditional distributions.  
- Weakness: would benefit from more visualizations of generated samples, especially for contextual generation.  
- Significance: applicable to many real-world problems (image generation, NLP, decision-making under uncertainty).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong empirical results, and thorough evaluation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility concerns, missing intuition for CMMD, and insufficient sample visualizations.
