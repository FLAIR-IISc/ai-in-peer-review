- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to learn conditional distributions.  
- CGMMN conditions on inputs and noise, uses a deep network to generate samples, and is trained via backpropagation.  
- CMMD generalizes MMD by measuring Hilbert–Schmidt norm between kernel mean embeddings of empirical and model conditional distributions.  
- Empirical evaluation covers predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Results are competitive: CGMMN-CNN achieves 3.13% on SVHN (comparable to CMMVA) and minimal RMSE degradation in Bayesian distillation.  
- Theoretical extension of MMD to conditional distributions is well-motivated with clear definitions and supporting theorems.  
- Experiments are comprehensive with appropriate baselines and ablation studies (e.g., MLP vs CNN); implementation details and a mini-batch algorithm are provided for reproducibility.  
- The Bayesian dark knowledge application is a novel and practical demonstration of CGMMN’s utility.  
- Comparison with conditional GANs is limited and would strengthen the evaluation.  
- The theoretical justification could be deeper, especially on convergence properties and validity conditions for CMMD.  
- The connection to prior work on conditional Hilbert space embeddings (Song et al., 2009) is under-emphasized.  
- Computational complexity analysis of CMMD versus other methods is missing, leaving trade-offs unclear.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and reproducible; overall the contribution is novel, significant, and merits acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
