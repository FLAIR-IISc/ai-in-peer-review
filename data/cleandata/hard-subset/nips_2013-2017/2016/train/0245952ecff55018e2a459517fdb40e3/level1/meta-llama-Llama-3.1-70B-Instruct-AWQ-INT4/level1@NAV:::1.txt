This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a deep generative model that learns a conditional distribution given some input variables. The model is based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, which is an extension of the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion used in Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN). The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on a wide range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The authors provide a thorough introduction to the background and related work, including a review of GMMN and MMD. The presentation of the CGMMN model is clear and concise, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of the CMMD criterion and the learning algorithm.

The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating competitive performance of CGMMN on various tasks. The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the model, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and ablation studies.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel and effective deep generative model that can learn conditional distributions.
* The authors provide a thorough evaluation of the model, including comparisons with state-of-the-art methods and ablation studies.
* The experimental results are impressive, demonstrating competitive performance of CGMMN on various tasks.
* The paper is well-written and clearly organized.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of background knowledge in deep learning and generative models, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors could provide more intuition on why the CMMD criterion is effective in learning conditional distributions.
* The paper could benefit from more visualizations of the generated samples, especially in the contextual generation task.
