- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) for learning conditional distributions with deep generative models.  
- Core technical contribution is Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), a novel objective measuring distance between conditional distributions in an RKHS.  
- Logical and important extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) from joint to conditional distributions.  
- Provides a compelling, stable, non-adversarial alternative to Conditional GANs (cGANs).  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across tasks: predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and distillation from Bayesian models.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-organized, and clearly written with solid theoretical motivation from kernel mean embeddings.  
- Derivation of CMMD is clear and grounded in first principles; experimental evaluation is thorough and supports claims.  
- Novelty and significance: CMMD for deep conditional generative models fills an important gap and avoids GAN min-max instability.  
- Creative application to “Bayesian dark knowledge” highlights usefulness for model compression and distillation.  
- Weakness: generated images are slightly less sharp than state-of-the-art adversarial methods, requiring an autoencoder for smoother samples.  
- Weakness: computational complexity concerns—CMMD requires kernel matrix inversion that scales cubically with mini-batch size.  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically elegant objective; stable non-adversarial training; versatile empirical performance.  
- Argument against acceptance: sample quality lags adversarial methods and potential scalability issues from kernel inversion.  
- Overall assessment: strong, well-executed paper whose strengths outweigh its weaknesses.
