- Summary: Proposes CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion; simple generator trained by backprop; evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation with competitive results.
- Quality: Technically sound with Theorem 3 linking CMMD to conditional distribution matching; clear algorithm and implementation; lacks deeper convergence/finite-sample analysis of the CMMD estimator.
- Clarity: Exceptionally well-written and organized with concise background, effective figures, and a helpful appendix.
- Originality: Novel CMMD extension addressing conditional generation without adversarial min-max training; distinguishes itself from conditional GANs/VAEs.
- Significance: Practical, flexible framework applicable to multiple tasks; simpler training than GANs and competitive empirical performance broaden applicability of moment-matching approaches.
- Strengths: Strong theoretical foundation, comprehensive experiments across domains, straightforward backprop-based training, demonstrated utility in diverse applications, good baseline comparisons.
- Weaknesses: Limited comparison with conditional GANs, inadequate discussion of computational cost (matrix inverses), claims of competitiveness without clear superiority, theoretical justification for Hilbert–Schmidt norm could be stronger.
- Recommendation: Accept — strengths and practical contributions outweigh the noted weaknesses.
- Arguments for acceptance: Clear GMMN-to-CGMMN extension with novel CMMD, thorough evaluations, practical training procedure, and real-world applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: Insufficient GAN comparisons, some theoretical gaps in CMMD analysis, and overstated performance claims.
