### Review of "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks"

#### Summary and Relation to Previous Work

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a framework for learning deep conditional generative models of the form `p(y|x)`. This work is a direct and significant extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) [19, 3], which learn joint distributions by minimizing the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) between the model distribution and the data distribution.

The core technical contribution is the development of a novel training objective called Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD). Drawing on the theory of kernel embeddings for conditional distributions [29], CMMD measures the discrepancy between two conditional distributions by computing the Hilbert-Schmidt norm of the difference between their respective conditional mean embedding operators. This provides a principled, non-parametric criterion for matching the conditional distribution produced by a generator network to the true data distribution.

The proposed generative model is a deep neural network that takes the conditioning variable `x` and a random noise vector `h` as input to produce a sample `y`. The network's parameters are learned via stochastic gradient descent by minimizing the CMMD objective, which is differentiable and can be optimized using standard back-propagation.

This work is well-situated within the landscape of deep generative models. Conceptually, the extension from GMMN to CGMMN is analogous to the extension from Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) [5] to Conditional GANs [21]. However, CGMMN offers a fundamentally different training paradigm. Instead of the unstable min-max game of adversarial training, it proposes a single, stable objective function, which is a key advantage. The paper demonstrates the versatility of this framework on a wide range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and a novel application in distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

#### Evaluation Against NIPS Criteria

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality.

*   **Strengths:** The theoretical foundation is sound, building directly on established work in kernel mean embeddings [29, 7]. The derivation of the CMMD as a metric for conditional distributions is principled and well-justified by Theorem 3. The claims are well-supported by a comprehensive set of experiments that cover three distinct and relevant application domains (prediction, generation, and model distillation). This empirical breadth convincingly demonstrates the model's flexibility. The results on classification tasks (MNIST, SVHN) are competitive with other state-of-the-art deep generative models from the period, such as max-margin DGMs [18]. The authors are also honest about potential weaknesses, for example, acknowledging the noisy appearance of generated images and suggesting a practical remedy by combining CGMMN with an autoencoder.

*   **Weaknesses:** The main technical weakness lies in the computational complexity of the CMMD objective. The formulation in Eq. (2) requires the inversion of kernel Gram matrices (`K + λI`), which scales cubically with the mini-batch size (`O(B³)`). While the use of mini-batches makes this tractable, it remains a significant computational bottleneck compared to the objectives of VAEs or GANs, which typically scale linearly with batch size. This scalability issue could limit the model's application to problems requiring very large batches. Furthermore, while the generated images for MNIST and Yale Faces are recognizable, their visual quality (e.g., sharpness in Fig. 2) appears to be lower than what was being achieved by contemporary GAN-based models.

**Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized.

*   **Strengths:** The authors provide a concise but effective preliminary section that introduces the necessary concepts of MMD and kernel embeddings of conditional distributions, making the paper accessible to readers familiar with kernel methods. The motivation for conditional modeling is well-articulated in the introduction. The generative process and training algorithm are described clearly. Remark 1 provides a particularly insightful connection, explaining CMMD as an instance-reweighted version of MMD, which enhances the reader's intuition.

*   **Weaknesses:** The jump to the final CMMD objective in Equation (2) is rather abrupt. While space constraints in conference papers are understood, a few more intermediate steps or a clearer reference to the appendix for the full derivation would have improved readability for non-experts in this specific area of kernel methods.

**Originality:** The work presents a novel and valuable contribution.

*   **Strengths:** The central idea of using a CMMD objective based on conditional mean operators to train a deep generative model is highly original. While it builds upon existing theory [29] and models (GMMN), the synthesis of these ideas into a practical learning framework for conditional DGMs is a novel contribution. The application of this framework to distill knowledge from Bayesian models ("Bayesian dark knowledge") is also an interesting and original idea that showcases the model's utility beyond standard generative tasks.

*   **Weaknesses:** The high-level conceptual leap from an unconditional to a conditional model by incorporating conditioning variables is not new in itself; as noted, it parallels the development of conditional GANs [21] and conditional VAEs [28]. However, the specific technical machinery developed here (CMMD) is entirely distinct and constitutes the core novelty.

**Significance:** The paper's contributions are significant and likely to be influential.

*   **Strengths:** The most significant contribution is providing a stable, non-adversarial training framework for deep conditional generative models. The training instability of GANs was a major challenge for researchers and practitioners at the time, and this paper presents a compelling and viable alternative with a single, well-behaved objective function. The demonstrated success across diverse tasks—from discriminative prediction to creative generation and model compression—highlights its broad applicability. This work successfully fills a research gap by developing the moment-matching approach to generative modeling into a more powerful and flexible tool, likely inspiring further research into kernel-based training objectives for deep learning.

*   **Weaknesses:** The practical impact might be somewhat tempered by the aforementioned issues of computational scaling and, potentially, sample quality compared to the rapidly advancing state of the art in GANs.

#### Arguments Pro and Con Acceptance

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
1.  **Novelty and Soundness:** Introduces CMMD, a novel, theoretically sound, and well-motivated objective for training conditional generative models.
2.  **Training Stability:** Offers a crucial alternative to the often-unstable min-max optimization of conditional GANs, which is a significant practical contribution.
3.  **Versatility:** The model's effectiveness is demonstrated across a diverse and compelling set of experiments, including prediction, generation, and a novel model distillation task.
4.  **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and positions the work well within the existing literature.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**
1.  **Computational Scalability:** The `O(B³)` complexity of the CMMD objective is a notable limitation for scaling to very large batch sizes.
2.  **Sample Quality:** The visual quality of the generated samples, while good, may not be on par with the best contemporary adversarial models, potentially limiting its impact in the image synthesis community.

#### Conclusion

This is a strong paper that makes a significant contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN framework and its associated CMMD loss function are novel, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated on a wide range of tasks. Its main advantage—a stable, single-objective training procedure—addresses a key weakness of the dominant adversarial approaches. Despite concerns about computational scalability and sample quality, the strengths of this work in terms of originality, technical quality, and potential impact are compelling. I strongly recommend acceptance.
