- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions using a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.  
- Trains CGMMN efficiently with back-propagation and evaluates on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Provides a solid theoretical foundation by deriving CMMD from MMD/kernel methods and presenting well-defined theorems.  
- Technically sound algorithmic design (Algorithm 1) that integrates with deep learning training.  
- Empirical results are competitive across diverse tasks and compared against established baselines.  
- Clearly situates contributions relative to GMMNs, GANs, and Bayesian literature.  
- CMMD objective derivation and intuitive explanation, especially around kernel Gram matrices and assumptions, lack clarity.  
- Insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choice, regularization λ, network architecture).  
- Limited ablation studies to isolate contributions of components like architecture choices and latent dimensionality.  
- Experimental details (optimizers, learning rates, epochs) are under-specified, hindering reproducibility.  
- Missing direct, head-to-head comparisons to recent conditional GANs and conditional VAEs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded extension with practical utility and efficient training; well-written presentation.  
- Arguments for rejection: need clearer CMMD explanation, more thorough hyperparameter/ablation studies, and stronger comparisons to conditional GAN/VAE baselines.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify CMMD, expand hyperparameter/ablation analysis, and include more direct comparisons to conditional GANs/VAEs.
