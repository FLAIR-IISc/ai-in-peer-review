- Proposes Conditional GMMNs (CGMMN) trained with a conditional MMD (CMMD) that matches conditional embedding operators directly.
- Solid technical contribution: defines CMMD as the Hilbert–Schmidt distance between estimated conditional embedding operators and shows computation via Gram matrices and backprop.
- Practical insight: K matrices do not depend on model parameters, allowing flexible kernels on x.
- Empirical results are promising on MNIST/SVHN classification, conditional generation (MNIST/Yale), and Bayesian dark knowledge (Boston).
- Novel framing: operator matching addresses per-condition data sparsity better than per-condition matching, especially for continuous conditions.
- Missing baseline: no comparison to a naive per-condition GMMN with shared parameters, which could be competitive for low-cardinality discrete labels.
- Missing comparisons to natural alternatives: CGAN and CVAE baselines are not included.
- Computational concerns: CMMD requires O(b^3) matrix inversions per minibatch; batch-size sensitivity, numerical stability, and approximations are not discussed.
- Suggest discussing numerical stability and approximations (Cholesky, λ selection, Nyström/RFF, preconditioned CG, running-average estimators).
- Clarity issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow; notation needs tightening and cross-covariance/centering assumptions should be explicit with a worked example of Eq. (2).
- Minor technical inaccuracies: incorrect statement about MMD estimator being unbiased (V-statistic is biased; U-statistic is unbiased) and a deviation from [29] in the conditional embedding operator definition.
- Significance: approach is general and applicable to conditional synthesis and Bayesian model compression; adding NLL/ECE uncertainty calibration metrics would increase impact.
- Pros: clean CMMD formulation, simple SGD training, and diverse encouraging experiments.
- Cons: missing naive baseline and CGAN/CVAE comparisons, insufficient treatment of computational/variance trade-offs, and exposition gaps.
- Recommendation summary: weak accept contingent on adding the naive baseline, clarifying derivations, and addressing computational issues.
