- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to learn p(y|x).  
- Extends GMMN framework to conditional modeling, enabling backprop-based training via kernel mean embeddings.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Technical soundness: CMMD derivation is logically consistent and grounded in kernel embedding/MMD theory.  
- Clarity: Paper is very well-written, clearly organized, and includes a helpful appendix for reproducibility.  
- Originality: Novel derivation of conditional estimator, practical mini-batch implementation, and application to Bayesian distillation.  
- Significance: Efficient conditional distribution learning has broad impact; promising for distilling Bayesian models.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion on sensitivity to kernel choice and its effect on performance.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail on hyperparameter tuning, particularly the CMMD regularization λ.  
- Weakness: Comparisons to state-of-the-art are sometimes limited; more baselines and ablation studies are needed.  
- Weakness: Lack of explicit analysis of computational cost and scalability of CMMD for large datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, well-motivated, clearly explained, strong experimental support, and significant applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate analysis of kernel/hyperparameter sensitivity and limited comparisons/compute discussion.  
- Overall assessment: Strong paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to address kernel selection, hyperparameter sensitivity, and broader comparisons.
