The paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) metric. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN in various tasks, including classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.

The main idea of CGMMN is to treat all conditionals as a family and match the conditional embedding operator directly, avoiding per-condition data sparsity. This approach is particularly useful when dealing with continuous conditioning variables, where the naive approach of training a Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) per conditional distribution sharing network parameters suffers from data sparsity.

The authors provide a clean formulation of Conditional MMD, which can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD. The K matrices in Eq. (2) are independent of model parameters, allowing standard backpropagation to be used for optimization. However, it is worth noting that the computation of Conditional MMD may require large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch noise, which can be computationally expensive.

The experimental results for classification and Bayesian model distillation are interesting and promising. The authors demonstrate that CGMMN can achieve competitive performance in various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.

However, there are some minor issues with the presentation. The explanation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow, and the reviewer had to consult referenced papers for clarity. Additionally, there is a minor error in the statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased. Finally, there is a minor inconsistency in the definition of the conditional embedding operator, which differs from reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition is needed.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective approach to learning conditional distributions using kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD metric. The experimental results are promising, and the approach has the potential to be widely applicable in various tasks.
