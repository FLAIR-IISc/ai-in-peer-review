This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a conditional deep generative model trained by minimizing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective derived from kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions. The model draws noise h from a simple prior, concatenates it with conditioning variables x, and maps through a DNN to produce y ~ pθ(y|x). The CMMD loss compares the conditional mean embedding operator of the empirical data with that of the model via Gram matrices and a regularized inverse on the x-kernel. Empirical studies cover (i) supervised prediction (MNIST, SVHN), (ii) contextual generation (MNIST digits conditioned on labels; Yale Faces conditioned on identity; also a variant with a frozen autoencoder code space), and (iii) Bayesian “dark knowledge” distillation (learning a student that mimics a teacher’s predictive distribution on Boston Housing).

Relation to prior work: CGMMN extends GMMN (Li et al., ICML’15) by moving from MMD on joint distributions to CMMD on conditional mean embeddings (Song et al., ICML’09; Grunewalder et al., ICML’12). Compared to GAN/cGAN, it replaces the min–max game with a direct discrepancy minimization; compared to VAEs/CVAEs, it avoids likelihood bounds and amortized inference in favor of moment matching. The paper positions CGMMN as a simple, backprop-friendly conditional generator with wide applicability.

Quality:
- Theory: The CMMD objective is well-motivated from conditional embeddings. Theorems 1–2 correctly cite prior results; however, Theorem 3 (equality of conditional operators implies equality of conditional distributions) is stated without a proof or citation and needs justification under the stated regularity/universality assumptions. The mapping from Eq. (2) to a “weighted MMD” interpretation is interesting but currently opaque due to notation (see Clarity).
- Algorithm: The mini-batch training loop is straightforward; importantly, K̃−1 depends only on x, so gradients do not require differentiability of kx. The cubic cost in batch size remains a practical concern; mini-batching mitigates it, but more discussion on stability/variance of the CMMD estimator and computational cost would help. The sensitivity to λ and kernel choices is not analyzed.
- Empirics: Classification is competitive with DGM baselines (MMVA/CMMVA), though below strong discriminative models on SVHN. Generative samples are reasonable and improved via code-space modeling. Distillation on Boston shows parity with the teacher. Results are solid but limited to relatively simple datasets and a single distillation setup.

Clarity:
- Notation/overloading: The symbol C is overloaded (conditional embedding operator, cross-covariance, and constants C1–C3 in the “weighted MMD” remark). Please rename the constants or use a different symbol for the matrices in Remark 1 to avoid confusion.
- Equation (2) and Remark 1: The “dot” notation in Tr(L · C) must be clarified as standard matrix multiplication, not Hadamard product. If the weighted-MMD view relies on elementwise weights, specify the exact operations and shapes of C1, C2, C3, and provide a brief derivation.
- Theorem 3: Provide a proof sketch or a citation to a result on injectivity of conditional mean embeddings under universal kernels (e.g., related to Fukumizu et al. or Song et al.), and state the assumptions precisely (measurability, boundedness, characteristics of kx, ky).
- Experimental details: Specify the kernels kx and ky used in all experiments (RBF? bandwidth selection? for y being labels vs images vs codes), the regularization λ, and sensitivity analyses. For the autoencoder variant, report architecture and reconstruction performance. Minor typos remain; an editing pass would help.

Originality:
- The core novelty is using conditional mean embedding operators as a training objective for conditional deep generators. While conditional embeddings are established, and GMMN exists for unconditional settings, this paper fills a gap by turning conditional embedding comparisons into a practical, differentiable objective with a simple sampling scheme. Relative to cGAN/CVAE, the objective/learning dynamics are distinct and may offer stability benefits.

Significance:
- The approach is likely most impactful for conditional generative modeling and knowledge distillation scenarios where stable, likelihood-free training is attractive. The present empirical scope demonstrates feasibility but stops short of establishing state-of-the-art on challenging benchmarks. Broader adoption will benefit from stronger results on datasets like CIFAR-10/LSUN and more comprehensive distillation studies across teachers and tasks, as well as analyses of calibration and uncertainty.

Pros for acceptance:
- Clear, principled extension of MMD-based training to conditional settings via CMMD.
- Simple training with backprop; avoids adversarial min–max issues.
- Demonstrates versatility: prediction, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation.
- Empirical results are competitive with DGM baselines and qualitatively reasonable.

Cons for acceptance:
- Theorem 3 lacks proof/citation; some theoretical claims need tightening.
- Notation and Eq. (2) presentation are confusing; the weighted-MMD connection is underspecified.
- Empirics are limited to relatively easy datasets; no results on more challenging modern benchmarks.
- Scalability and sensitivity to kernel/λ are not systematically studied.
- Distillation is shown on a single small dataset/teacher; generality uncertain.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide a proof or authoritative citation for Theorem 3, and precisely state the conditions under which CMMD=0 implies equality of conditionals.
- Clarify Eq. (2), define all Gram matrices and operations unambiguously, and clean up symbol overloading.
- Report kernel choices, bandwidth selection strategies (e.g., median heuristic), λ values, and ablations on these hyperparameters and on latent dimensionality/noise injection schemes.
- Discuss computational complexity per mini-batch; consider Nyström or random feature approximations for scaling, and analyze estimator variance vs. batch size.
- Strengthen empirical evaluation with: (i) conditional generation on CIFAR-10/LSUN (class-conditional), (ii) comparisons to cGAN/CVAE under matched architectures, (iii) additional distillation experiments (e.g., BNNs on UCI, classification teachers), and (iv) uncertainty calibration metrics (NLL/Brier) for supervised tasks.
- Release code to aid reproducibility.

Overall: A solid and conceptually clean contribution that extends MMD-based deep generative modeling to conditional settings. With improved clarity and stronger empirical validation, this work could have meaningful impact, especially for conditional generation and non-adversarial training paradigms.
