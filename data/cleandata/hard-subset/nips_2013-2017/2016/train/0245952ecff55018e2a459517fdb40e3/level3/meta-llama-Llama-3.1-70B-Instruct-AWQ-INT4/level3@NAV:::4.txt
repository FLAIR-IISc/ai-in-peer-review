This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a novel framework for learning conditional distributions using a kernel-embedding of conditional moments. The authors extend Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to conditional generation, enabling the model to learn a flexible conditional distribution given some input variables.

The paper is technically sound and well-presented, with clear explanations of the proposed method and its theoretical foundations. The authors provide a thorough derivation of the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, which is used to train the CGMMN model. The empirical estimator derivation is non-obvious and useful, making a significant contribution to the field.

The paper demonstrates competitive performance on a wide range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation. The results are impressive, especially in the generative performance on MNIST and Yale Face datasets. The authors also show that the distilled model does not harm the prediction performance in the Bayesian dark knowledge experiment.

The framework is easy to apply, and the algorithm is well-presented in a clear and concise manner. The paper is well-written, and the appendix contains helpful methodological and experimental details.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and technically sound framework for learning conditional distributions.
* The authors provide a thorough derivation of the CMMD criterion and its empirical estimator.
* The paper demonstrates competitive performance on a wide range of tasks.
* The framework is easy to apply, and the algorithm is well-presented.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential drawbacks of the proposed method.
* Some of the experiments could be more thoroughly evaluated, such as the Bayesian dark knowledge experiment.
