- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), training deep conditional generators by minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD) via RKHS conditional mean embeddings.
- Generator produces y = f(x,h;w) and CMMD matches conditional mean operators of data and model using Gram matrices over x and y.
- Extends prior GMMN work to conditional distributions and connects to conditional mean embedding literature.
- Positioned as a simpler, stable alternative to conditional GANs and complementary to CVAEs, with applications in prediction, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation.
- Empirical evaluation includes predictive tasks (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and distillation of a Bayesian NN on Boston housing.
- Strength: Conceptual novelty — principled operator-based extension of MMD to conditional settings.
- Strength: Simplicity and stability — closed-form CMMD objective allows standard backprop without adversarial min–max.
- Strength: Demonstrates breadth of applications and provides practical mini-batch training details.
- Weakness: Predictive performance lags state-of-the-art generative and discriminative baselines on MNIST/SVHN.
- Weakness: Conditional generation results are qualitative-only and lack quantitative comparisons to cGANs/CVAEs.
- Weakness: Ambiguity in operator estimation and centering (whether covariance operators and Gram matrices are centered) undermines reproducibility and theoretical claims.
- Weakness: Sensitivity to kernel choices, bandwidth selection, and regularizer λ not studied; per-batch inversions scale cubically and scalability issues not addressed.
- Weakness: Presentation has typos and missing implementation details about conditioning protocol and batching.
- Technical soundness: CMMD objective and gradients are sound under standard assumptions but centering/operator existence issues need clarification.
- Empirical support: Shows the method works but does not establish clear advantages over cGAN/CVAE; Bayesian distillation result is small-scale but promising.
- Clarity: Generally readable but operator estimation and gradient details need precise specification; proofreading needed.
- Originality: Natural yet novel extension of MMD to conditional deep generators with an operator-based formulation.
- Significance: Potentially useful stable alternative to adversarial training, but current experiments limit impact; stronger comparisons would help.
- Specific requests: Clarify centering of ĈYX and ĈXX and Gram matrices, explain gradient flow when K̃−1 depends only on x, and detail kernel choices and λ selection.
- Specific requests: Provide complexity/ wall-clock comparisons to cGAN/CVAE and discuss scalable approximations (Nyström, random features).
- Specific requests: Add quantitative generation metrics (classifier consistency, conditional likelihood proxies, human studies) and matched-baseline comparisons.
- Typos: Correct obvious errors (e.g., “Frobeniu norm”, “an universal”) and fix grammar/spacing.
- Pros for acceptance: Principled conditional MMD extension, stable training, and interesting Bayesian distillation application.
- Cons for acceptance: Weak predictive/generative comparisons, centering ambiguity, and missing kernel/λ ablations.
- Post-rebuttal: Authors clarified centering and gradients, addressing main methodological concern; score raised but stronger empirical work still encouraged.
- Recommendation: Borderline reject initially, upgraded after rebuttal; paper is technically interesting but needs clearer/operator details and stronger empirical comparisons.
