- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Learns p(y|x) by sampling a simple noise distribution and passing it with x through a deep network; evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Builds on GMMN and GAN literature, offering an alternative to GANs’ min-max optimization with a moment-matching objective.  
- Well-motivated focus on conditional distributions with clear applications and connection to larger generative models.  
- Solid theoretical foundation using MMD and Hilbert space embeddings to handle distributions nonparametrically.  
- Novel extension that broadens the applicability of moment-matching approaches to conditional settings.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance, notably on MNIST and SVHN predictive tasks.  
- Paper is generally well-written and well-structured with logical flow.  
- Mathematical exposition (CMMD derivation, notation) is sometimes hard to follow and needs clearer, stepwise explanation.  
- Experiments are limited to relatively small datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face); scaling to larger datasets is not demonstrated.  
- Potentially high computational cost from kernel computations and matrix inverses; lacks runtime/efficiency comparisons to GANs or DSN.  
- Reproducibility concern: code is not publicly available.  
- Model descriptions (MLP, CNN architectures) lack sufficient detail (layers, kernels, activations) for replication.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, competitive results, important problem, well-organized.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear math, limited experimental scale, missing runtime comparison, no public code, insufficient model detail.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and promising contribution but needs improvements in clarity, scalability evidence, reproducibility, and implementation details.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing code release, clearer mathematical exposition, runtime comparison, and detailed model specifications.
