The paper "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks" proposes a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using kernel embeddings and a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) metric. The authors extend the idea of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting, allowing for the modeling of complex conditional distributions.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of Conditional GMMNs (CGMMN), which can be used for a variety of tasks such as classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several benchmark datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face.

One of the key advantages of the proposed approach is its ability to treat all conditionals as a family and match the conditional embedding operator directly, avoiding per-condition data sparsity. This is particularly useful when dealing with continuous conditioning variables, where the naive approach of training a GMMN per conditional distribution can suffer from data sparsity.

The authors also provide a clean formulation of the conditional MMD metric, which can be computed similarly to the unconditional MMD. The K matrices in Eq. (2) are independent of the model parameters, allowing for standard backpropagation.

However, the reviewer raises a concern about the potential need for large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch noise, which can make computation expensive. The authors should consider addressing this concern in future work.

The experimental results for classification and Bayesian model distillation are interesting and promising. The authors demonstrate competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods, including max-margin DGMs and discriminative deep networks.

The presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is somewhat hard to follow, and the reviewer had to consult referenced papers for clarity. The authors may want to consider revising these sections to make them more accessible to readers.

Additionally, there are a few minor errors and inconsistencies in the paper. For example, the statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect, and the definition of the conditional embedding operator differs from reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition is needed.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and promising approach to learning conditional distributions using kernel embeddings and CMMD. While there are some minor errors and inconsistencies, the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several benchmark datasets, and the reviewer believes that the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and promising approach to learning conditional distributions using kernel embeddings and CMMD.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several benchmark datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face.
* The proposed approach has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of deep learning and generative models.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is somewhat hard to follow, and the reviewer had to consult referenced papers for clarity.
* There are a few minor errors and inconsistencies in the paper, including the statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased and the definition of the conditional embedding operator.
* The authors do not provide a clear solution to the potential need for large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch noise, which can make computation expensive.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the concerns raised by the reviewer.
