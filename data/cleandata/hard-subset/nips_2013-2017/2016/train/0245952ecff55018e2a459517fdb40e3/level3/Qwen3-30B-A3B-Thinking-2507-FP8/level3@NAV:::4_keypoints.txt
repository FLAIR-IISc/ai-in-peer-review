- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional generation via kernel-embedding of conditional moments.  
- Derives a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion that can be optimized with back-propagation like GMMN.  
- Presents a careful, non-trivial derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation with competitive results.  
- Framework is simple to implement (Algorithm 1) and applicable for practitioners using deep generative models.  
- Theoretical derivation is clear and the experimental evaluation is thorough with strong baselines.  
- Authors appropriately acknowledge limitations (e.g., need for regularization).  
- Paper is well-written, logically structured, and supported by a helpful appendix.  
- Originality is moderate: a natural extension of GMMN but with a meaningful technical contribution.  
- Significance is strong: addresses a key gap in conditional moment-matching with high practical relevance.  
- Main limitation: comparisons to more recent conditional generative models (e.g., conditional VAEs) could be expanded.  
- Overall: technically sound, practically useful, and a strong candidate for acceptance.
