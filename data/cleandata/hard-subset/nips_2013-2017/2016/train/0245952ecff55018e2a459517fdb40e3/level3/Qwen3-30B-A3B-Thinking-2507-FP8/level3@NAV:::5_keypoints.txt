- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions via RKHS embeddings.  
- Defines a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) training criterion that preserves backprop-based optimization.  
- Evaluates CGMMN on predictive tasks (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation.  
- Strong theoretical motivation and meaningful extension of kernel-embedding/generative moment-matching ideas.  
- Critical technical flaw: empirical conditional operator omits essential centering terms involving μ_X and μ_Y, which are assumed but not justified.  
- Omission raises fundamental questions about gradient computation and whether experiments properly handled centering.  
- Empirical results are disappointing: CGMMN has higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Generative evaluation is incomplete—lacks comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), so generation advantages are unclear.  
- Paper contains typographical errors (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").  
- Suggested revisions: provide rigorous justification for centering terms, clarify gradient handling, expand experimental comparisons, and correct typos.  
- Overall assessment: novel and well-motivated but technical shortcomings and weak experiments limit the paper’s contribution and suitability for NIPS.
