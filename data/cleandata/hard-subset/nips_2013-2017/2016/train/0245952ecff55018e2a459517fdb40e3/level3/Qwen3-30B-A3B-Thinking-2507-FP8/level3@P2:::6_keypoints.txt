- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to model conditional distributions via kernel mean embeddings and backpropagation.  
- Evaluated on three tasks: predictive classification, contextual generation, and distilling Bayesian models (Bayesian dark knowledge).  
- Originality: Extends GMMN to conditional modeling, filling a gap and distinguishing from unconditional GMMN and conditional GANs.  
- Theoretically sound CMMD formulation grounded in kernel embedding theory.  
- Significance: Competitive empirical results (CGMMN-CNN) on MNIST/SVHN without augmentation and diverse conditional generation on MNIST/Yale Face.  
- Shows practical utility for distilling Bayesian predictive distributions without performance loss (Boston housing).  
- Quality: Rigorous technical development, well-motivated backprop training, and efficient gradient computation since K matrices are parameter-independent.  
- Clarity concern: Presentation of conditional embedding operators is hard to follow and relies on external references.  
- Technical inaccuracy: Incorrect claim that MMD is an unbiased estimator for finite samples; it is only asymptotically unbiased.  
- Missing baseline: No comparison to training a separate GMMN per condition (shared parameters), which could be a strong discrete-conditioning baseline.  
- Computational concern: Cubic scaling from matrix inversion (K̃⁻¹) for large minibatches is noted but not adequately addressed.  
- Recommendation: Strong submission; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: clarify conditional embedding–CMMD connection, correct MMD unbiasedness claim, add per-condition GMMN baseline comparison, and expand discussion of computational trade-offs.
