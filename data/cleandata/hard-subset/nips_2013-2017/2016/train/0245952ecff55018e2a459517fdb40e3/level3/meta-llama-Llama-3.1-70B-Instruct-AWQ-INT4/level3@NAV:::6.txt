The paper "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks" proposes a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) metric. The authors introduce Conditional GMMNs (CGMMN), which extend the capability of GMMNs to address a wide range of application problems while keeping the training process simple.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, making it easy to follow and understand. The paper adequately informs the reader about the proposed method, its advantages, and its limitations.

The originality of the paper lies in its proposal of a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using CMMD. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be applied to various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of Bayesian models. The paper also provides a thorough comparison with existing methods, highlighting the advantages of the proposed approach.

The significance of the paper is evident in its potential to improve the state-of-the-art in various applications, including image generation, multimodal learning, and missing data imputation. The authors demonstrate that their approach can be used to distill knowledge from Bayesian models, which is a challenging problem in machine learning.

However, there are some minor errors and inconsistencies in the paper. For example, the statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect. Additionally, the definition of the conditional embedding operator differs from the reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition is needed.

In conclusion, the paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is novel and significant. The experimental results are promising, and the paper demonstrates the potential of CGMMN to improve the state-of-the-art in various applications.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using CMMD.
* The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.
* The paper provides a thorough comparison with existing methods, highlighting the advantages of the proposed approach.
* The paper has the potential to improve the state-of-the-art in various applications.

Arguments against acceptance:

* There are some minor errors and inconsistencies in the paper.
* The presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow, and the reviewer had to consult referenced papers for clarity.
* The paper requires large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch noise, but large minibatches make computation expensive.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the errors and inconsistencies mentioned above.
