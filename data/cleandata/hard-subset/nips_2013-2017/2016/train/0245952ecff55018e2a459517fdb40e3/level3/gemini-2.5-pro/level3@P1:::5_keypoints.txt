- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to learn p(y|x).  
- Generator is a deep network taking x and noise h, trained by SGD/backprop.  
- Theoretical contribution: CMMD defined as Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators, building on prior kernel embedding work.  
- Empirical evaluation covers predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Major technical concern: the empirical CMMD estimator appears uncentered (omits μ_X and μ_Y terms), conflicting with the centered cross-covariance definition and raising correctness/gradient issues.  
- The derivation/justification for the estimator Φ(K+λI)⁻¹Υᵀ is unclear and must be clarified (intentional simplification vs. error).  
- Predictive results are competitive but do not outperform the state-of-the-art CMMVA.  
- Generative evaluation is purely qualitative; no quantitative comparisons to strong baselines like Conditional GANs.  
- Paper would be stronger with quantitative generative metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID) and direct comparisons to conditional generative models.  
- Clarity is generally good and motivation is compelling, but the CMMD derivation lacks transparency and there are minor typos/grammar errors.  
- Originality is high: extending GMMNs to conditional setting via conditional mean embeddings is novel and elegant.  
- Significance is potentially high if technical ambiguity is resolved and empirical performance is made competitive.  
- Recommendation: resolve the CMMD centering issue, justify the estimator, and strengthen/quantify empirical comparisons before publication.
