- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-free and gradient-based) for non-convex loss minimization in Supervised PageRank models.  
- Formulates the task as a constrained optimization problem minimizing discrepancy between ranking scores and labels.  
- Provides thorough theoretical analysis including convergence rate guarantees and complexity bounds.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains methods and theory.  
- Experimental results show the proposed methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based method on a web page ranking task.  
- Strength: Methods are novel and potentially applicable beyond Supervised PageRank.  
- Strength: Theoretical convergence and complexity guarantees are provided.  
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating practical effectiveness.  
- Weakness: Assumes loss is convex in a small vicinity of the optimum, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Performance is sensitive to hyperparameter choices (e.g., Lipschitz constant, accuracy).  
- Weakness: Lacks more detailed comparisons with other optimization methods like SGD and its variants.  
- Overall: Significant contribution with practical potential, but further work needed to understand limitations and broader applicability.  
- Pro-accept: Novel methods with theoretical guarantees, strong experimental evidence, and clear presentation.  
- Con-accept: Questionable convexity assumption, hyperparameter sensitivity, and insufficient comparisons to other optimizers.
