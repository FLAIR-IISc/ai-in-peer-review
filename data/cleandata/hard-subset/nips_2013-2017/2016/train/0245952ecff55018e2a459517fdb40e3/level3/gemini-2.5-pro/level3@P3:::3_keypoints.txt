- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to model P(Y|X) via conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- CMMD enables training conditional generators by backpropagating a moment-matching loss, avoiding adversarial min-max instability.  
- Demonstrates versatility across tasks: supervised classification, conditional image generation (digits, faces), and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical results on MNIST and Yale Faces show reasonable generative quality; competitive predictive performance on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Novel application: distilling Bayesian predictive distributions ("Bayesian dark knowledge") into a single feed-forward network.  
- Clarity issues: Remark 1 and Equation (2) use non-standard notation (e.g., Tr(Ld · C1)) and unclear dot-product meaning.  
- Overloaded notation: symbol C represents multiple different operators/matrices, reducing readability.  
- Missing theoretical justification: Theorem 3 (conditional embedding implies equality of conditional distributions) is presented without proof or citation.  
- Limited experimental scope: generative evaluation confined to simple datasets (MNIST, Yale Faces) and needs testing on harder datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Bayesian distillation experiment is narrow (only Boston housing and one teacher model PBP), leaving generality unproven.  
- Overall assessment: strong, principled extension of GMMNs with solid proof-of-concept experiments but needs clearer notation, theoretical proof, and broader experiments.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify/standardize notation, provide proof or citation for Theorem 3, and expand empirical evaluation for scalability and generality.
