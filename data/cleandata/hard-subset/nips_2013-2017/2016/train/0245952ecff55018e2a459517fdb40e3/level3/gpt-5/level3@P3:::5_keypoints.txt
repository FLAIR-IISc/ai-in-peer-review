- Proposes Conditional GMMNs (CGMMNs) that extend GMMNs to conditional generation by matching conditional mean embeddings P(Y|X) in an RKHS via a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.  
- Learns generator y = f(x,h;w) conditioned on x and noise h to match the empirical conditional mean embedding operator ĈY|X.  
- CMMD is defined as the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional embedding operators, building on Song et al. (2009) and related work.  
- Evaluated on classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST digits, Yale Face B), and Bayesian distillation (Boston Housing).  
- CMMD objective is optimized by backpropagation with minibatches.  
- Technical correctness: CMMD derivation is grounded in RKHS theory but the paper omits explicit centering terms for the empirical conditional operator.  
- Centering omission matters: without centering the estimator matches cross-moments not cross-covariances, affecting bias, consistency, and gradients through CMMD.  
- Authors should state whether they center features/Gram matrices (HKH, HLH), how centering is implemented per minibatch, and whether inverses/centering are treated as constants in backprop.  
- If not centering, the paper must justify theoretically (e.g., equivalence to KRR with bias) and discuss impact on consistency of ĈY|X.  
- Practical details missing: critical kernel choices and hyperparameters (kernels for X and Y, bandwidth, λ), selection methods, normalization, and handling of discrete labels.  
- Need to specify how C−1XX is computed/regularized and report sensitivity analyses for kernel/hyperparameter choices.  
- Computational aspects: inverting K̃ per batch raises numerical stability and complexity concerns; suggest discussing λ selection and scalable alternatives (Nyström, random Fourier features).  
- Experimental results: CGMMN is competitive but not superior to other conditional deep generative models; predictive accuracy trails CMMVA and strong discriminative baselines.  
- Conditional generation results are visually plausible but lack quantitative evaluation and comparisons to conditional GANs and VAEs.  
- Authors should include quantitative metrics (classifier accuracy on generated samples, diversity measures, likelihood proxies) and side-by-side CGAN/CVAE comparisons.  
- Bayesian distillation result is promising (CGMMN matches PBP’s RMSE) but needs more datasets, calibration metrics (NLL/CRPS), and inference-time/throughput reporting.  
- Clarity issues: paper needs explicit empirical formulas for ĈXY, ĈXX, ĈY|X with centering, kernels/hyperparameters per experiment, and details on stochastic gradient computation under CMMD.  
- Typos and minor notation inconsistencies need fixing (e.g., “Frobeniu norm”, “an universal”, “convoluational”, superscript notation).  
- Originality: combining conditional mean embeddings with deep generators and CMMD is novel and well-motivated, offering a principled extension of GMMN to conditional modeling.  
- Significance: CGMMN could be a stable alternative to adversarial/variational methods if robustness, evaluations, and scalability issues are addressed.  
- Pros: conceptually clean operator-based extension, simple backprop training without adversarial min-max, broad applicability, promising Bayesian distillation result.  
- Cons: ambiguity around centering and its effects, predictive accuracy behind state-of-the-art, generative evaluation lacks quantitative baselines, limited ablations on kernels/λ/batch size, and scalability not demonstrated.  
- Key questions requested: clarify centering and gradients; provide kernels and bandwidth selection; compare CMMD to joint-MMD baselines; add CGAN/CVAE comparisons with quantitative metrics; report calibration and inference speed for distillation.  
- Recommendation: borderline reject pending rebuttal addressing centering/gradient clarifications and stronger empirical comparisons; potential to raise score if authors adequately address issues.
