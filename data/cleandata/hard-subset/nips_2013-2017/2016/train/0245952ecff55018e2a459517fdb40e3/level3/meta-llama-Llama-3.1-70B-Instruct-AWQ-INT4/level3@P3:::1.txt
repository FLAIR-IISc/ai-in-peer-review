This paper proposes a neural network method based on conditional maximum mean discrepancy (cMMD) for learning conditional distributions. The authors extend the capability of generative moment-matching networks (GMMN) to address a wide range of application problems, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' ideas. The mathematical exposition is generally clear, although it may be hard to follow at times for readers without a strong background in kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings. The authors provide a good overview of the related work, including GMMN, GAN, and other deep generative models.

The proposed method, conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN), is an interesting and relevant extension in deep generative modeling. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The results show that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art methods in these tasks.

The experimental tasks are somewhat small, but they are comparable to recent work in the area. However, it would be beneficial to see more extensive experiments on larger datasets to further demonstrate the scalability of CGMMN.

One concern is that the training process involves computationally expensive steps, such as computing the inverse of the kernel gram matrix. It would be helpful to provide a runtime comparison with other methods, such as DSN, to give a better understanding of the computational efficiency of CGMMN.

Another concern is the reproducibility of the results. The authors should provide reference code to strengthen the paper and make it easier for others to reproduce the results.

Finally, the model description lacks sufficient detail for practitioners to exactly reproduce the evaluated models. The authors should provide more details on the architecture, hyperparameters, and training procedures used in the experiments.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective method for learning conditional distributions. While there are some concerns regarding the computational efficiency and reproducibility of the results, the paper is well-written and provides a good contribution to the field of deep generative modeling.
