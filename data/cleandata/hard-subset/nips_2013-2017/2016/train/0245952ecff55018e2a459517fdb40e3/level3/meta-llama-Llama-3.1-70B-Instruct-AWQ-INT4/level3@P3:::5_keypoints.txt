- Proposes CGMMN: a novel, well-motivated method using conditional MMD (CMMD) to learn conditional distributions via RKHS embeddings.  
- Provides a clear theoretical overview linking CMMD to MMD.  
- Describes the CGMMN architecture for conditional distribution learning in detail.  
- Experiments on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face show competitive generative quality and predictive performance.  
- CGMMN has higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on evaluated datasets (disappointing empirically).  
- Major concern: empirical conditional operator \hat{C}_{Y|X} omits terms involving μ_X and μ_Y, relying on unjustified RKHS centering.  
- Unclear how centering was handled in gradient computation and experiments; authors must clarify the centering procedure.  
- Paper contains multiple typos/grammatical errors (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal") that detract from quality.  
- Technically sound overall; theoretical claims are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Originality: extends GMMN to conditional settings, but needs clearer differentiation from prior work.  
- Significance: tackles an important problem and shows promising results, but empirical advantage is limited.  
- Acceptance arguments: novelty, theoretical clarity, and empirical demonstrations.  
- Rejection arguments: problematic operator definition/centering omission, insufficient experimental explanation, typos, and inferior predictive results.
