- Novelty: Extends GMMNs to conditional modeling via Conditional MMD (CMMD), enabling direct learning of P(Y|X).
- Significance: CMMD based on matching conditional embedding operators is a principled, well-motivated objective for conditional generation.
- Technical soundness: Kernel-embedding formulation and operator-norm matching are statistically powerful and permit end-to-end optimization.
- Empirical strength: Strong, diverse results — competitive classification, good conditional image generation, and effective Bayesian model distillation.
- Clarity issue: Theoretical sections (cross-covariance operators, Hilbert–Schmidt norms) are dense and lack intuitive exposition.
- Computational cost: CMMD requires kernel matrix inversion (cubic in batch size), forcing a difficult trade-off between batch size and training stability.
- Missing baseline: No empirical comparison to a simple GMMN that treats conditioning as an input, which would help quantify practical benefits.
- Technical error: Incorrect claim that the empirical squared MMD estimator is unbiased; the unbiased estimator is a different U-statistic.
- Notational inconsistency: Definition of the conditional embedding operator appears to differ from the cited formulation and needs clarification.
- Overall assessment: Strong, original, and empirically validated paper with addressable presentation and computational concerns; recommend acceptance.
