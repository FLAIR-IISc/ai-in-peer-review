- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions via conditional MMD (CMMD).  
- Avoids adversarial training, retaining simple gradient-based optimization for conditional generative modeling.  
- Leverages kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions (Theorem 2) to define CMMD, optimized by backpropagation.  
- Evaluated across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation tasks.  
- Shows competitive empirical performance (e.g., CGMMN-CNN 3.13% on SVHN, close to CMMVA’s 3.09%).  
- Demonstrates a novel and effective Bayesian distillation application, distilling complex Bayesian models without performance loss.  
- Theoretical foundations (Theorems 1–3) are sound and well-developed.  
- Core idea and contributions are clearly motivated, though some mathematical sections are dense.  
- Reproducibility is weak: missing hyperparameters, exact CNN architectures, kernel choices, regularization, and distillation implementation details; no reference code.  
- Computational cost concerns: requires expensive kernel matrix inversions with cubic scaling, but no runtime comparisons or quantified cost analysis.  
- Scope limited to relatively small-scale datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale faces), though authors justify choices via comparable baselines.  
- Recommendation: strong NIPS contribution that merits acceptance subject to minor revisions addressing reproducibility and computational analysis.  
- Pro: novel conditional MMD framework, competitive results, compelling Bayesian distillation, solid theory.  
- Con: insufficient implementation details, missing runtime comparisons, and limited dataset scale.
