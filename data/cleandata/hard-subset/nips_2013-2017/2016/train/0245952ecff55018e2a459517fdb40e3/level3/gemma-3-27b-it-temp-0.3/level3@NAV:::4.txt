## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the existing Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to conditional generation. The core idea is to leverage a kernel embedding of conditional moments to define a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, enabling learning via standard backpropagation. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation, achieving competitive results on various datasets.

The work builds upon the recent success of MMD-based generative models like GMMN, addressing a significant limitation – their inability to model conditional distributions directly. The extension to the conditional setting, while conceptually natural, isn’t trivial. The derivation of the CMMD estimator and its compatibility with backpropagation are key contributions. The paper is well-structured and clearly written, with a helpful appendix providing detailed methodological and experimental information. The inclusion of diverse experimental tasks strengthens the evaluation and demonstrates the versatility of the proposed framework.  The authors also appropriately acknowledge and compare against relevant baselines, including GANs, VAEs, and other MMD-based approaches.

A strength of this work lies in its practical applicability. Algorithm 1 provides a clear roadmap for implementation, making it accessible to practitioners working with deep generative models or knowledge distillation. The authors also highlight the flexibility of the framework, allowing for different network architectures (MLP, CNN) and integration with autoencoders. 

However, the paper could benefit from a more in-depth discussion of the theoretical assumptions underlying the CMMD estimator, particularly regarding the smoothness requirements for the conditional mean embedding operator. While acknowledged, a more detailed analysis of these assumptions and their potential impact on performance would be valuable. Additionally, the ablation studies could be expanded to better understand the contribution of different components of the CGMMN framework. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and non-obvious extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
*   Technically sound and well-presented, with a clear derivation of the CMMD estimator.
*   Competitive performance across a diverse range of tasks.
*   Practical applicability and ease of implementation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of the theoretical assumptions underlying the CMMD estimator.
*   Ablation studies could be more comprehensive.



**Overall:** I recommend acceptance. This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling, offering a practical and effective framework for conditional generation. The strengths of the work outweigh the minor weaknesses, and it is likely to be of interest to a broad audience within the NIPS community.
