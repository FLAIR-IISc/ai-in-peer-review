- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD metric.  
- Clearly outperforms naive baseline that trains a GMMN per condition, which suffers from data sparsity (esp. with continuous conditioning).  
- Provides a clean Conditional MMD formulation that can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices are independent of model parameters enabling standard backprop.  
- CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch noise, but large minibatches make computation (notably matrix inverses) expensive.  
- Experimental results on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation are promising and competitive across tasks.  
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow; reviewer had to consult referenced papers for clarity.  
- Contains minor errors/inconsistencies, e.g., incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased.  
- Definition of the conditional embedding operator differs from reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator is needed.  
- Paper is overall well-written, technically sound, and provides reproducible results for expert readers.  
- Presents a novel combination of techniques and clearly differentiates from prior work (originality).  
- Results are significant and likely usable by practitioners/researchers, advancing the state of the art.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, promising experimental performance, significant contribution.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear presentation in parts, minor technical errors, and potentially high computational cost.  
- Reviewer score: 8/10, confidence 8/10.
