This paper extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting by introducing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion based on kernel embeddings of conditional distributions. The core idea is to learn a generator y = f(x, h; w) that maps inputs x and noise h through a deep network, and to measure fit via the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between the conditional mean operators CY|X of the data and the model. The derivation yields a practical empirical estimator expressed with Gram matrices over inputs and outputs, enabling optimization by back-propagation with essentially the same machinery as unconditional GMMN. Experiments cover predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (class-conditional MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian prediction distillation (PBP on Boston housing), showing competitive results. The work builds on MMD and kernel conditional mean embeddings (e.g., Gretton et al.; Song et al.) and relates to conditional DGMs such as conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero) and conditional VAEs (Sohn et al.).

Quality: The technical development is sound, with a clear CMMD objective, practical minibatch training (Algorithm 1), and sensible architectural choices. While I did not verify every equation, the estimator is well-motivated and aligns with prior theory on conditional mean operators. Empirical results are solid and diverse, though largely competitive rather than state-of-the-art.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, well organized, and the appendix is helpful with methodological and experimental details. Algorithm 1 makes the method easy to reproduce.

Originality: The conditional extension is natural, but the specific empirical CMMD estimator and its trace-form expression are non-trivial and practically useful. The positioning relative to conditional mean embedding literature is appropriate.

Significance: CGMMN offers a simple, broadly applicable framework for conditional generation and distillation that practitioners can readily adopt.

Weaknesses and suggestions:
- Scalability: CMMD requires inverting (K + λI), with cubic cost in minibatch size. Discuss/experiment with Nyström or random features to scale and analyze minibatch-induced bias/variance.
- Kernel choices: Provide guidance/sensitivity for kernels on X and Y and the regularizer λ; ablations would strengthen the paper.
- Evaluation: Generative results are primarily qualitative; including quantitative metrics (e.g., FID/Inception score for images or proper scoring rules like NLL/CRPS where applicable) and diversity assessments would help. Comparisons to cGAN/CVAE baselines on the same tasks would clarify tradeoffs.
- Theory: Beyond sufficiency, a brief discussion of consistency/rates for the empirical CMMD estimator (or assumptions needed) would be valuable.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Clear, technically sound extension with a useful estimator
- Simple, general, and easy-to-implement framework
- Competitive results across varied tasks
- Relevance to practitioners (distillation, conditional generation)

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited quantitative generative evaluation and baseline comparisons
- Scalability and kernel-sensitivity not thoroughly analyzed
- Distillation experiment is small-scale

Overall, I lean toward acceptance given the methodological contribution and practicality.
