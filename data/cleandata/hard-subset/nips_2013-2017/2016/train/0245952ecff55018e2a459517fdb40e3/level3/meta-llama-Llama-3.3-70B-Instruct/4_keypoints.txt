- Proposes two novel optimization methods (gradient-free and gradient-based) for non-convex supervised PageRank learning.
- Shows technical soundness and competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation.
- Novel empirical estimator and non-obvious conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework.
- Framework is practical and easy to apply, exemplified by Algorithm 1.
- Well-written paper with a useful appendix detailing methods and experiments.
- Thorough analysis of the trade-off between lower-level accuracy and overall computational complexity.
- Provides theoretical guarantees on convergence rate and complexity bounds.
- Methods outperform the state-of-the-art in experiments.
- Gradient-based algorithm: convergence guarantees without requiring convexity and fewer input parameters.
- Gradient-free algorithm: avoids computing derivatives for each element of large matrices.
- Potential weakness: assumes Φ is a small convex vicinity around a local minimum, which may not hold in practice.
- Paper lacks sufficient discussion on robustness to general non-convexity.
- Originality: combines random gradient-free and gradient methods with the inexact oracle concept.
- Well-structured and potentially impactful for web-page ranking and related applications.
- Needs more experimental evidence to demonstrate scalability and applicability to other problems.
