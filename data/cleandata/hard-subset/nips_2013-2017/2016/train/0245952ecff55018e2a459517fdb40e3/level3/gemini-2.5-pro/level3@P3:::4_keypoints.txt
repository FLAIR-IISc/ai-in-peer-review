- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions p(y|x).
- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a novel training criterion measuring distance between conditional distributions via kernel mean embedding operators.
- Derives a practical empirical estimator for CMMD that enables learning with standard SGD and backprop, like GMMN.
- Theoretical development is well-grounded in kernel methods and plausibly derived from prior work on conditional embeddings.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, showing competitive performance on classification (MNIST, SVHN), contextual image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and well-organized, with helpful intuition (Remark 1) and an easy-to-follow Algorithm 1.
- Appendix provides methodological and experimental details that aid reproducibility.
- The extension from GMMN to conditional setting is non-trivial and original; empirical CMMD estimator is a key novelty.
- Work is significant: expands applicability of GMMNs to supervised and conditional tasks and offers a stable alternative to GAN-style training.
- Creative application to distilling Bayesian "dark knowledge" demonstrates flexibility and practical impact.
- Minor caveat: reviewer did not exhaustively check all equations, though derivations appear plausible.
