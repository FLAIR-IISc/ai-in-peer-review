- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to learn conditional distributions.  
- Extends GMMN to the conditional setting, addressing a non-trivial gap in generative modeling literature.  
- Empirical evaluation covers predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge, showing competitive results across diverse tasks.  
- CGMMN can be trained with standard backpropagation and does not require complex training procedures.  
- Connection to Bayesian dark knowledge provides a compelling, application-focused use case.  
- Clarity issues: paper opens with heavy formal math and lacks an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective and practical challenges.  
- Equation (2) is presented without sufficient derivation, assumptions, or explanation of its relation to conditional embedding operators.  
- Insufficient algorithmic details: the path from Equation (2) to an implemented backprop-based algorithm is unclear and supplemental material is hard to follow.  
- Comparison to conditional GANs is weak; novelty and advantages over existing conditional generative models are not clearly articulated.  
- Computational efficiency claims lack concrete complexity analysis or runtime comparisons with alternatives.  
- Pro arguments: conditional extension is valuable, empirical results demonstrate utility, and the method is conceptually simple and implementable in standard frameworks.  
- Con arguments: poor clarity and exposition hinder evaluation of novelty/correctness, mathematical presentation lacks intuition, and relation to related conditional models is inadequately established.  
- Overall assessment: interesting and promising contribution but undermined by insufficient clarity and missing practical/derivational details; recommend rejection unless major clarifications (intuition, Eq.2 derivation, algorithm mapping, and comparisons) are provided.
