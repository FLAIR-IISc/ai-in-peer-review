- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions and introduces the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) loss.  
- CGMMN conditions on given variables and random noise; CMMD measures difference between kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions and is used as the training objective.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, background, and a thorough related-work review (GMMN, GAN, VAE).  
- Method is technically sound and accompanied by theoretical justification for the CMMD loss.  
- Experiments demonstrate effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation, with results comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Notation problems: the symbol C is used ambiguously for both conditional embedding and covariance matrix.  
- The mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form and the dot notation are not clearly explained and need clarification.  
- Theorem 3 is presented without reference or proof and requires additional justification.  
- Empirical analysis is limited—missing details on hyperparameter tuning and sensitivity analysis.  
- Results would be more impactful with experiments on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Summary scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension of GMMN, thorough literature review, and solid experimental support.  
- Arguments against acceptance: confusing notation, unclear derivation steps, and insufficient empirical depth on hyperparameters and harder datasets.
