- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions.
- Introduces a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective based on kernel mean embeddings to measure divergence between conditionals.
- Derives an empirical CMMD estimator that enables end-to-end training via back-propagation, avoiding adversarial min–max optimization.
- Retains GMMN training stability while providing a non-adversarial alternative for conditional generation.
- Demonstrates versatility and effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and supported by Algorithm 1 and a detailed appendix for reproducibility.
- The CMMD estimator derivations appear correct and the motivation is strong.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, showing competitive performance against strong baselines.
- Main practical weakness is cubic scaling from kernel-matrix inversion in CMMD; mini-batch approximation trade-offs need more discussion.
- Contribution may be seen as incremental (adapting existing framework), but the empirical estimator’s technical elegance and practical utility justify the work.
