- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) and kernel mean embedding.  
- Trains deep networks to generate samples conditioned on inputs; evaluated on prediction, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Rigorous theoretical foundation with formalization of conditional kernel mean embedding and Theorems 1–3.  
- Empirical evaluation uses standard benchmarks (MNIST, SVHN, Yale) and compares to relevant baselines (CMMVA, DSN).  
- Computational cost concern: CMMD requires O(n^3) kernel operations and runtime/compute not adequately addressed.  
- No runtime comparisons to discriminative baselines (e.g., DSN), limiting practical viability assessment.  
- Generative results are visually plausible but noisier than state-of-the-art GANs.  
- Lacks ablation studies on kernel choices and conditional variable encodings, weakening analysis of design decisions.  
- Bayesian dark knowledge application is interesting but tested on only a single regression dataset.  
- Paper is well-organized and mathematically precise, though Sections 2–3 are dense.  
- Reproducibility issues: missing hyperparameters (CNN learning rates, kernels), unclear sampling strategies, incomplete autoencoder integration details; Algorithm 1 omits regularization and minibatch handling is insufficient in appendix.  
- Originality: significant and novel extension of GMMN to conditional settings, offering a non-adversarial alternative to conditional GANs; could better contextualize vs. recent conditional moment-matching work.  
- Significance: theoretically grounded alternative for conditional generation with competitive predictive performance, but limited by small-scale benchmarks and potential scalability hurdles.  
- Pros: novel theoretical contribution, competitive prediction results, interesting distillation use case, flexible conditional sampling.  
- Cons: reproducibility gaps, unbenchmarked computational overhead, subpar generative quality vs. GANs, limited scalability experiments.  
- Recommendation: weak accept (3/5); requests code, full hyperparameters, and runtime comparisons for camera-ready.
