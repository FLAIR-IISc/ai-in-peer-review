This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs), a principled extension of the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework for modeling conditional distributions. Drawing on prior work in kernel mean embeddings, the authors propose a novel training objective based on Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), which measures the divergence between conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space. This work follows a natural trajectory seen in other deep generative models, such as the extension of GANs to Conditional GANs, by broadening the applicability of the moment-matching approach. The key technical contribution is the derivation of an empirical CMMD estimator that enables end-to-end training via standard back-propagation, thus retaining the training stability of GMMNs while avoiding the difficult min-max optimization of adversarial methods. The authors compellingly demonstrate the versatility and effectiveness of CGMMN on a diverse set of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and the distillation of Bayesian models.

**Strengths:**
*   **Quality and Clarity:** The paper is technically sound and very clearly written. The motivation is strong, and the development of the CMMD objective and its empirical estimator is methodical and well-explained. While I have not verified every equation in detail, the derivations appear correct. The inclusion of Algorithm 1 and a detailed appendix greatly enhances the paper's clarity and reproducibility.
*   **Originality and Significance:** While extending GMMNs to the conditional setting is an intuitive next step, the paper’s formulation is elegant. The derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator is a non-obvious and valuable contribution that makes the framework practical. The proposed method is significant as it provides a stable, non-adversarial alternative for conditional generation. The framework is easy to apply and its demonstrated success in knowledge distillation from Bayesian models is a particularly novel and impactful application that should be of high interest to practitioners.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental evaluation is thorough, covering three distinct and important problem domains. The results show that CGMMN achieves competitive performance against strong baselines, validating its efficacy as a flexible and powerful conditional generative model.

**Weaknesses:**
*   A potential weakness is the computational cost associated with inverting kernel matrices in the CMMD objective, which scales cubically with the mini-batch size. While the authors propose a practical mini-batch algorithm, a more detailed discussion of the trade-offs of this approximation would be beneficial.
*   The novelty could be viewed as somewhat incremental, as it adapts an existing framework to a conditional setting. However, the technical elegance and practical utility of the proposed CMMD objective sufficiently justify the contribution.
