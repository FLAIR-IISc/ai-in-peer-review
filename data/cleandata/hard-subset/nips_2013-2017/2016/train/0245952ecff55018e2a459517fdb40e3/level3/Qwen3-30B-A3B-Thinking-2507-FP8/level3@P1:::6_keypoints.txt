- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions.  
- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) to measure differences between kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- CMMD enables training conditional generative models via standard backpropagation because kernel matrices are independent of model parameters.  
- Core contribution: matches conditional embedding operator directly, avoiding per-condition model training and data sparsity issues.  
- Mathematical formulation is clean and computable using kernel matrices.  
- Evaluated on classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical results are strong and competitive with state-of-the-art discriminative models; conditional samples are meaningful.  
- Demonstrates effective distillation of Bayesian predictive distributions without performance loss.  
- Presentation weakness: conditional embedding operator sections (2.3, 3.1) are hard to follow without referenced papers.  
- Minor correctness issue: statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect—it's asymptotically unbiased.  
- Definition of the conditional embedding operator differs from prior work [29], causing potential confusion about cross-covariance operators.  
- Practical concern: CMMD requires kernel matrix inverses, scaling cubically with sample size and posing minibatch tradeoffs not adequately addressed.  
- Experimental omission: no comparison to naive baseline of training separate GMMNs per condition.  
- Overall: theoretically sound, original extension of MMD to conditional settings, significant and practically promising despite clarity and scalability concerns.  
- Recommendation: accept; strengths outweigh minor presentation and experimental omissions.
