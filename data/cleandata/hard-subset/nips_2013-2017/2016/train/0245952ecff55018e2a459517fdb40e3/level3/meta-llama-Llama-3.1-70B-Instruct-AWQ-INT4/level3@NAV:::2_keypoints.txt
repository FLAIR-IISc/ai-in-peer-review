- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) as an extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.  
- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as a new training objective to measure difference between empirical and generated conditional distributions.  
- Evaluates CGMMN comprehensively on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge tasks.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical analysis and experimental results supporting claims.  
- Presentation is clear and well-organized, making the method easy to follow for experts.  
- Original contribution: non-trivial extension of GMMN using kernel mean embedding and MMD for conditional modeling.  
- Significance: shows potential across multiple applications and competitiveness with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Limitation: heavy formal mathematics at the start may be challenging for non-experts.  
- Limitation: lacks an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective.  
- Limitation: does not provide a clear step-by-step mapping from the optimized formula to a practical implementation.  
- Suggested revisions: add intuitive explanation, clarify algorithmic steps, and include more visualizations/illustrations.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions.
