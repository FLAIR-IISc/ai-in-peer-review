- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions via RKHS embedding.  
- Novel, well-motivated framework providing a flexible representation of conditional distributions.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear explanation; RKHS embedding of conditionals is a key contribution.  
- Thorough analysis including connection to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) and applicability to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Experimental results are promising on several real-world datasets.  
- Missing clear explanation of how centering in the RKHS was handled, a crucial methodological detail.  
- Lacks comparison to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs).  
- Technically sound but contains typos and minor errors that require proofreading.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: clarify RKHS centering, include comparisons to methods like conditional GANs, and fix typos/minor errors.
