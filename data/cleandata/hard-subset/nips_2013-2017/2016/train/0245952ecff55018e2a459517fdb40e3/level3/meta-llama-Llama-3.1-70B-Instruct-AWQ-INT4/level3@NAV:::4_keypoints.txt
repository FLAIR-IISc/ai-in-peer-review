- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions via kernel-embedded conditional moments.  
- Extends GMMN to conditional generation, enabling flexible conditional modeling given inputs.  
- Technically sound, well-presented paper with clear method explanations and theoretical foundations.  
- Provides a thorough and non-obvious derivation of the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion and its empirical estimator.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance across tasks: predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation.  
- Shows strong generative results on MNIST and Yale Face datasets.  
- Bayesian dark knowledge experiment indicates distillation does not harm prediction performance.  
- Framework and algorithm are easy to apply and clearly described.  
- Paper is well-written and appendix contains helpful methodological and experimental details.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and potential drawbacks of the method.  
- Some experiments (notably the Bayesian dark knowledge one) could be evaluated more thoroughly.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant contribution to deep generative models with competitive results.
