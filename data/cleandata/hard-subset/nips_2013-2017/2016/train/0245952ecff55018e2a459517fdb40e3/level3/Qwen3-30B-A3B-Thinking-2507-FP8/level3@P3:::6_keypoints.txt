- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting, introducing Conditional GMMNs (CGMMNs) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.
- Main technical contribution: directly matches the conditional embedding operator to learn conditional distributions, avoiding training separate models per condition and data sparsity.
- CMMD formulation is computable similarly to unconditional MMD with kernel matrices independent of model parameters, enabling backpropagation.
- Clear, theoretically sound extension that addresses a real limitation of the naive approach.
- Strong empirical results across classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian model distillation (Boston housing).
- CGMMN architecture and training are straightforward to implement with standard neural network components.
- Demonstrates significant, practical applications and potential for broad adoption.
- Minor technical errors: incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased and a differing definition of the conditional embedding operator relative to prior work.
- Presentation issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and would benefit from more self-contained explanations.
- Practical concern: CMMD may require large minibatches, which are computationally expensive due to matrix inverses; paper lacks discussion of remedies used.
- Missing fair comparison to the naive baseline (one GMMN per condition), which may be competitive for low-cardinality discrete conditions.
- Overall quality: technically sound and experimentally well-supported; weaknesses are mainly fixable presentation and minor technical issues.
- Originality: novel and clean solution to conditional generative modeling by matching conditional embedding operators.
- Significance: addresses an important limitation, is broadly applicable, and warrants acceptance after revision to fix clarity and comparison gaps.
