- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using conditional MMD (CMMD) and kernel mean embeddings to learn conditional distributions.
- Extends GMMN to model conditional probabilities, enabling controlled generation without adversarial or variational methods.
- Demonstrates applicability to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation with competitive results on several datasets.
- Builds on prior MMD/GMMN work and relates to conditional GANs, VAEs, and sum-product networks.
- Technical soundness: CMMD derivation is logically consistent and amenable to backprop-based training.
- Clarity: paper is generally well-written and organized with a straightforward explanation of CMMD vs MMD.
- Originality: extending MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting is a novel contribution.
- Significance: learning conditional distributions broadens generative modeling applications; distillation use-case is notably interesting.
- Notation clarity issues: transition from Equation (2) to weighted MMD is unclear and dot notation ambiguity needs fixing.
- Symbol overloading: reuse of 'C' for multiple meanings is confusing and should be resolved.
- Theorem 3 lacks a proof or citation; providing one would strengthen theoretical foundations.
- Empirical scope is limited to simpler datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face); results on harder datasets (CIFAR-10, ImageNet, LSUN) would increase impact.
- Supervised learning application feels less compelling compared to the generative contributions.
- Bayesian distillation experiment is intriguing but limited to one dataset/model and needs broader validation.
- Overall recommendation: valuable and novel work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to clarify notation, supply Theorem 3 proof/reference, and expand empirical evaluation.
