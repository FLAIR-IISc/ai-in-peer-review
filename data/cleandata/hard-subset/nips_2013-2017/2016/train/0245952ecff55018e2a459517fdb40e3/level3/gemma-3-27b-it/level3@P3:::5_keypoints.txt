- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to extend GMMNs to conditional distributions p(y|x).
- Learns conditionals by embedding distributions in RKHS and minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Method builds on prior kernel-embedding work (Song et al., 2009) and GMMNs (Li et al., 2016).
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge transfer across several datasets.
- Novel and well-motivated extension with a theoretically grounded RKHS framework.
- Highlights potential advantages over GANs due to a simpler moment-matching optimization objective.
- Critical flaw: omission of centering terms in the empirical conditional operator \hat{C}_{Y|X} with no justification provided.
- Centering omission casts serious doubt on gradient correctness and experimental validity.
- Empirical performance is disappointing: CGMMN underperforms CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN for predictive tasks.
- Generative evaluation is limited and lacks comparisons to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs).
- Several minor typos and grammatical errors reduce polish.
- Quality: technical soundness is questionable because of the missing centering; experimental claims are not fully supported.
- Clarity: generally well-written and organized, but the unexplained centering omission detracts from clarity.
- Originality: an original conditional extension but heavily builds on existing work.
- Significance: potential is limited by weak empirical validation and unresolved implementation concerns.
- Overall: initial serious reservations due to centering and results, but after authors’ clarification the reviewer now views it as a worthwhile contribution and adjusted their score.
