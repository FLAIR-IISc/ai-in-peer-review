- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMN) trained with a conditional MMD (CMMD) loss to match P(y|x) via conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Model samples noise h, conditions on x, passes [x,h] through a neural net, and matches conditional distributions using the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical conditional embedding operators.  
- Extends GMMN/MMD from marginal matching to conditional matching and connects to prior kernel work and conditional generative models (cGANs, CVAEs).  
- Evaluated on supervised prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST digits, Yale faces), and Bayesian distillation (PBP teacher on Boston housing).  
- Revision addressed earlier concerns about latent dimensionality, scalability (mini-batch CMMD), and task utility.  
- The CMMD objective is natural and grounded in operator-valued conditional embeddings; Theorems 1–2 correctly cite prior MMD and embedding results.  
- Theorem 3 (CY|X = CZ|X ⇒ PY|X = PZ|X with universal kernels) lacks citation or formal proof and should reference Fukumizu et al. or Song et al. work.  
- Equation (2) provides a computable CMMD via Gram matrices, but the mapping to a “weighted MMD” requires explicit expansion and definitions of weight matrices.  
- Notation issues: the dot operator and all-ones matrix usage are unclear and the symbol C is heavily overloaded, causing confusion.  
- Mini-batch CMMD retains O(b^3) cost from K̃−1, acceptable for b≈200–500 but limiting scalability; suggest Nyström, random features, block solves, or preconditioned CG and reporting timing.  
- Good point that K̃−1 not depending on model parameters allows non-differentiable kernels on x, but gradients require smooth kernels on y—kernels and bandwidths should be stated.  
- Experimental results are solid but modest; competitive with other DGMs but not SOTA discriminative CNNs for classification.  
- Generative samples are reasonable and comparable to GMMN; AE-code space generation improves perceptual quality.  
- Bayesian distillation on Boston housing shows no degradation versus PBP but is limited as a single dataset/model case.  
- Paper is generally well organized and readable but needs more derivation steps, inline symbol definitions, and clarity on training x selection.  
- The CGMMN idea is an original, nontrivial extension of GMMN leveraging kernel operator theory and offers a simpler alternative to adversarial/variational objectives.  
- Main impact in conditional generative modeling and settings benefiting from kernels over complex outputs; supervised application is less compelling.  
- Pros: principled extension of GMMN, stable objective, decent empirical breadth, mini-batch training, and ability to use non-differentiable x-kernels.  
- Cons: missing proof/citation for Theorem 3, unclear Eq.(2) derivation and overloaded notation, scalability limits from cubic kernel costs, and limited experimental scope.  
- Suggested improvements: add reference/proof for Theorem 3, expand Eq.(2) derivation, fix notation, report kernel choices and sensitivity, discuss/implement scalability methods, add harder generation and more distillation experiments, and include qualitative/quantitative generation analyses.  
- Overall: technically sound and well-motivated extension with competitive results; recommend acceptance if theoretical citation/proof and CMMD/notation clarifications are added, otherwise a strong borderline paper.
