This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a method for learning deep generative models of conditional distributions p(y|x). The approach extends the recent work on Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN), which learn a joint distribution by minimizing the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) between model and data distributions. The core contribution of this work is the development of a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective. This objective measures the discrepancy between two conditional distributions by computing the Hilbert-Schmidt norm of the difference between their conditional mean embedding operators in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS), building upon the theoretical framework of Song et al. (2009). The model is trained via stochastic gradient descent with back-propagation. The authors demonstrate the utility of CGMMN on a variety of tasks, including classification, conditional image generation, and knowledge distillation from Bayesian models.

**Strengths**

*   **Originality and Motivation:** The paper addresses a well-motivated and important problem. Extending generative moment-matching networks to the conditional setting is a natural and logical next step, broadening their applicability to a wide range of supervised and contextual tasks. This work provides an alternative to other conditional deep generative models like conditional GANs [21] and conditional VAEs [28], with the potential advantage of a simpler, non-adversarial training objective.
*   **Conceptual Simplicity:** The proposed framework is elegant. It combines a simple generative process (transforming random noise and a conditioning variable through a deep network) with a well-defined moment-matching objective (CMMD).

**Weaknesses**

*   **Technical Soundness:** My main concern is with the technical formulation of the empirical CMMD objective. The paper defines the cross-covariance operator C_XY as `E[φ(X)⊗φ(Y)] - μ_X⊗μ_Y`, which correctly includes centering terms. However, the empirical estimator for the conditional operator is given as `Ĉ_{Y|X} = Φ(K+λI)⁻¹Υᵀ`. This estimator seems to correspond to an uncentered cross-covariance operator, effectively using `E[φ(X)⊗φ(Y)]` without subtracting the empirical estimates of `μ_X⊗μ_Y`. This omission of centering is not justified in the text. This raises critical questions:
    1.  Is the presented formulation of `L̂²_CMMD` in Eq. (2) technically correct without centering?
    2.  If centering was performed in the experiments, how was it implemented, particularly in a mini-batch setting?
    3.  If it was not performed, what is the justification and how does this impact the validity of the objective as a discrepancy measure between conditional distributions?
    This lack of clarity on a core technical detail significantly undermines the confidence in the paper's methodology and results.

*   **Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental results are not sufficiently strong to demonstrate a clear advance in the state of the art.
    *   **Predictive Performance:** In the classification experiments on both MNIST and SVHN (Tables 1 and 2), the proposed CGMMN-CNN is outperformed by CMMVA [18], which is a state-of-the-art max-margin deep generative model. While the results are reasonable, they do not establish CGMMN as a competitive alternative for predictive tasks compared to existing DGMs.
    *   **Generative Performance:** The evaluation of generative quality is purely qualitative. While the generated samples in Figures 2, 4, and 5 are interesting, the paper provides no quantitative comparisons to other conditional generative models. It is crucial to benchmark against strong, relevant baselines like conditional GANs [21] or conditional VAEs [28] to properly assess the model's generative capabilities. Without such comparisons, the significance of the generative results is difficult to ascertain.

*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, but it contains several distracting typos and grammatical errors (e.g., in Section 3.1, "Frobeniu norm"; in Theorem 1, "an universal RKHS"; in Sec. 4.2.1, "which is sufficiently large."). A thorough proofreading would improve the manuscript's quality.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**
*   The paper proposes a novel and well-motivated method for conditional density estimation, extending the promising line of research on moment-matching networks.
*   The CMMD objective offers a potentially more stable training alternative to the adversarial objective of conditional GANs.

**Arguments for Rejection:**
*   There is a significant potential technical flaw in the formulation of the CMMD objective related to missing centering terms, which is not adequately addressed or justified.
*   The experimental results are weak. The model underperforms key baselines in predictive tasks and lacks any comparative evaluation in generative tasks, making it hard to judge its practical significance.

In summary, while the idea of a conditional GMMN is appealing and significant, the current manuscript is weakened by a lack of clarity on a core technical detail and an unconvincing empirical evaluation. For the paper to be acceptable, the authors would need to clarify the issue regarding the centering of the conditional operator and provide a stronger, more comprehensive experimental comparison against relevant state-of-the-art methods.
