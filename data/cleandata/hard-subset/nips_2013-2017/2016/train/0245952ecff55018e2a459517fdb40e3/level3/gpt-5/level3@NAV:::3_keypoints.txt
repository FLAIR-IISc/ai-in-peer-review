- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN/MMD to conditional settings with a new CMMD loss and conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Model concatenates conditioning variables with noise and is trained by backprop; evaluated on supervised classification, contextual image generation, and Bayesian distillation.  
- Connects conditional kernel mean operators to deep generative modeling and complements adversarial/variational conditional models.  
- Revision addresses earlier concerns about low-dimensional latents, scalability, and utility versus alternatives.  
- Technical grounding is solid, with CMMD derived from conditional embeddings and efficient minibatch training.  
- Major theoretical gap: Theorem 3 lacks proof or citation—needs a proof sketch (e.g., via characteristic kernels and operator equality).  
- Equation (2) derivation is opaque; clarify the “·” notation (matrix vs. Hadamard) and show algebraic steps mapping to weighted MMD.  
- Notation issues: symbol C is overloaded (conditional operators vs. other quantities), causing confusion.  
- Clarity could be improved by detailing minibatch CMMD complexity (cost of K̃−1, memory) and practical kernel choices.  
- Originality: conditional extension is natural but nontrivial, differing from cGAN/CVAE by avoiding adversarial/variational losses.  
- Significance: Empirical results are decent and versatile, strongest for pure conditional generation.  
- Limitations: Bayesian distillation demo is limited to one dataset/model, restricting generality claims.  
- Experimental breadth is limited—no results on more challenging datasets like CIFAR-10 or LSUN.  
- Pros: clean, stable conditional MMD extension; versatile applications; addresses prior reviewer concerns.  
- Cons: missing theoretical proof/reference, unclear Eq. (2) and overloaded notation, limited experiments.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on fixing theory/notation issues and strengthening experiments.
