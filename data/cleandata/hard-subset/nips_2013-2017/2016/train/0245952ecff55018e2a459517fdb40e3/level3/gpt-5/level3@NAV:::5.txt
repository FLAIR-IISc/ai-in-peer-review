This paper extends GMMNs to the conditional setting via conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD), constructing a conditional mean operator in RKHS to match P(Y|X) between data and a generator. Building on Song et al. (2009) and the MMD literature (Gretton et al.), the model concatenates context x with noise h and trains a deep network by minimizing the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical conditional operators estimated from real and generated pairs. The authors evaluate CGMMN on prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and “Bayesian dark knowledge,” and position the work relative to GANs (Mirza & Osindero) and DGMs such as MMVA/CMMVA and CVAE.

Quality: The CMMD objective is well-motivated, and the architecture is simple to train with back-propagation. However, the definition and use of the empirical conditional operator omit the centering terms involving μX and μY. The paper assumes “centering in RKHS” but does not justify whether Gram matrices are centered (e.g., K ← HKH, L ← HLH, cross-centering across datasets) or how this interacts with mini-batch CMMD, regularization λ, and gradients. Omitting centering may bias the operator and changes the gradient paths through L, Ksd, and Lds. Please clarify: (i) whether and how centering is performed in practice for both single- and cross-dataset terms; (ii) whether centering matrices depend on parameters; (iii) how mini-batch CMMD approximates full-batch centering and its variance; (iv) stability of K̃−1 with λ selection.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear but contains typos and grammatical errors (e.g., “Frobeniu norm,” “an universal,” “which the,” “adverisarial”). Derivations would benefit from an explicit centered-operator formula and gradient sketch.

Originality: The conditional extension of GMMN via operator matching is novel and conceptually clean, distinct from conditional GANs and CVAEs.

Significance: Current empirical evidence is mixed. Predictive performance trails state-of-the-art CMMVA on both MNIST and SVHN. Generative results are qualitative and lack comparisons to conditional GANs or CVAEs, or quantitative metrics (e.g., FID, precision/recall). The dark knowledge experiment is small-scale.

Pros:
- Novel, principled CMMD objective; simple training and sampling.
- Broad applicability to conditional modeling and knowledge distillation.

Cons:
- Ambiguity around centering and gradients; potential estimator bias.
- Weaker predictive accuracy than strong baselines; limited generative evaluation.
- Presentation issues and minor errors.

Update: The authors have addressed the centering/gradient concerns and clarified experimental handling; with added comparisons/metrics, I would raise my scores. For the camera-ready, please include the centering details, ablations on mini-batch size/λ, and comparisons to cGAN/CVAE with quantitative metrics.
