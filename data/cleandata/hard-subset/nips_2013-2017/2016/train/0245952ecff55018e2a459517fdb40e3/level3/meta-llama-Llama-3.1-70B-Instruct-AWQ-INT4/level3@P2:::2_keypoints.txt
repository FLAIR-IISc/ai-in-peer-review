- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Develops a deep generative architecture and SGD/backprop algorithm to optimize the CMMD objective.  
- Builds on prior GMMN work and relates to conditional GANs and VAEs; extension to conditional setting is non-trivial.  
- Strength: novel and significant extension of GMMN to conditional modeling with broad applicability.  
- Strength: thorough mathematical derivation of the CMMD criterion and learning algorithm.  
- Strength: empirical results on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge show competitive performance.  
- Weakness: heavy mathematical formalism makes the paper hard to follow for non-experts.  
- Weakness: lacks intuitive explanation of the conditional objective and underlying principles.  
- Weakness: insufficient clarity on actual computations performed by the algorithm and its computational efficiency.  
- Weakness: derivation of Equation (2) is unexplained in terms of assumptions and practical implications.  
- Weakness: the path from Equation (2) to the implemented algorithm is not sufficiently detailed.  
- Quality: technically sound with claims supported by theory and experiments, but needs clearer exposition.  
- Clarity: well-organized but dense writing; would benefit from more intuition, examples, and computational details.  
- Originality: a meaningful original contribution as an extension of GMMN, though building on existing generative model work.  
- Significance: potential for broad application and promising empirical results, but would benefit from expanded discussion of applications and limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to add intuitive explanations, clearer computation/efficiency details, and more discussion of applications/limitations.
