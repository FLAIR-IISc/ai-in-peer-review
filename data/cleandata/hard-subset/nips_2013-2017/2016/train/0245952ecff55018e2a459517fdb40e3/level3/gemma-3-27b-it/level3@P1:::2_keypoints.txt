- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn p(y|x) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Grounded in kernel embeddings and Hilbert space theory, extending MMD to the conditional setting.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation, showing competitive performance.  
- Builds on prior GMMN and GAN work, aiming to retain moment-matching stability vs. GAN instability.  
- Strength: tackles an important problem by extending moment matching to conditional models.  
- Strength: provides a solid theoretical foundation connecting CMMD to existing MMD theory.  
- Strength: demonstrates competitive empirical results across diverse tasks.  
- Strength: training is enabled by backpropagation for efficient learning.  
- Strength: introduction clearly motivates conditional generative modeling and limitations of prior methods.  
- Weakness: lacks intuitive explanation of the CMMD objective and the leap from unconditional MMD to conditional CMMD.  
- Weakness: fails to discuss computational complexity and practical cost of estimating/optimizing CMMD on large datasets.  
- Weakness: Equation (2) is presented without derivation, assumptions, or clear connection to kernel Gram matrices.  
- Weakness: insufficient mapping from Equation (2) to the implemented algorithm; supplemental material is hard to follow, harming reproducibility.  
- Weakness: early sections are overly formal and dense, reducing accessibility.  
- Overall: solid theoretical and empirical contribution but hindered by poor intuition and missing computational/implementation details.  
- For acceptance: novel, technically sound extension with competitive results on relevant problems.  
- Against acceptance: core intuition and practical computation/derivations (esp. Eq.2) are inadequately explained.  
- Requested revisions: provide an intuitive, step-by-step derivation of Equation (2).  
- Requested revisions: give a clear mapping from Equation (2) to the implemented algorithm, including Gram matrix computation, optimization details, approximations, and mini-batching.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Weak Accept; addressing the requested clarifications could justify stronger acceptance.
