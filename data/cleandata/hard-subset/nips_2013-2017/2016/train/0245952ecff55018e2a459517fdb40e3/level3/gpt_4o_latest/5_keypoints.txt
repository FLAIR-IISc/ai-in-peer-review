- Novel extension: Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions via CMMD.  
- Theoretical novelty: Uses RKHS embeddings and provides rigorous derivations linking CMMD to MMD.  
- Broad applicability: Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical results: Competitive performance and qualitative samples on datasets like MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face.  
- Clarity issue: Assumes centered data in RKHS (μX, μY = 0) without adequate justification, affecting gradient computation clarity.  
- Missing term concern: Definition of empirical conditional operator Ć_{Y|X} omitted terms related to μX and μY that need clarification.  
- Performance gap: Predictive accuracy lags behind state-of-the-art models such as CMMVA in some tasks.  
- Lack of comparisons: No benchmarking against other conditional generative models (e.g., Conditional GANs, CVAEs), limiting assessment of generative quality.  
- Minor errors: Manuscript contains typographical mistakes (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal").  
- Rebuttal response: Authors addressed RKHS assumption and missing-term concerns in the rebuttal, improving clarity.  
- Remaining limitation: Despite rebuttal, insufficient comparisons with alternative conditional generative methods persist.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions, given novelty and theoretical strength but with room for improved empirical benchmarking.  
- Final score: 7/10.  
- Future work suggestion: Improve predictive performance and provide comprehensive comparisons to other conditional generative approaches.
