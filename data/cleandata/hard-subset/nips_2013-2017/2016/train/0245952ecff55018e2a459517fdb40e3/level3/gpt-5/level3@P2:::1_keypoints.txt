- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) trained by minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD) using conditional mean embeddings.
- Extends prior MMD-based generative models to model conditional distributions by concatenating conditioning x with noise h and mapping to y via a deep network.
- CMMD objective measures the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between estimated conditional embedding operators and is optimized via backpropagation.
- Positions CGMMN relative to conditional GANs and VAEs, offering a non-adversarial, non-variational training criterion.
- Evaluated on classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation; results competitive with prior MMD models and close to some DGMs on classification.
- Technical soundness: CMMD derivation is well-motivated and follows standard operator estimators with Tikhonov regularization.
- Observation that the inverse Gram block for x does not depend on model parameters simplifies gradient computation.
- Theoretical guarantees require smoothness (CY|X ∈ F ⊗ G); paper notes this may not strictly hold and uses regularization.
- Paper should state explicit kernel choices and bandwidth/regularization selection strategies for x and y.
- Experiments: fair comparisons but on small datasets; conditional samples are qualitative and AE+CGMMN improves samples as expected.
- Bayesian distillation shows no RMSE degradation vs PBP but lacks uncertainty metrics and runtime comparisons.
- Efficiency concern: per-minibatch Gram matrix inverses imply O(b^3) cost; runtime/throughput comparisons to baselines are missing.
- Clarity issues: several derivations (e.g., Eq.2) are terse; need dimension annotations and explicit kernel definitions.
- Algorithm details missing: Adam settings, learning rates, epochs, early stopping, and kernel hyperparameter selection method.
- Reproducibility: network architectures (filters, strides, normalizations, activations), noise dimensionality, and exact concatenation scheme are underspecified.
- Originality: conceptually natural but nontrivial; CMMD formulation for deep conditional generation appears novel.
- Significance: broadens MMD-based DGMs to conditional tasks and offers stability/simplicity benefits, but does not present new SOTA results.
- Pros: principled extension of MMD to conditional models, simple training/sampling, broad applicability, and competitive classification performance.
- Cons: computational cost uncharacterized, limited experimental scope and quantitative generative evaluation, missing reproducibility details, and limited ablation studies.
- Key questions: exact kernels/bandwidths used; bandwidth/λ selection; per-iteration and total training time and minibatch sizes; reuse/caching of inverses; teacher sample counts and input perturbation in distillation; predictive log-likelihood/calibration and speedups; quantitative generation metrics vs cGAN/CVAE.
- Suggestions: release code and precise implementation details, provide runtime/memory profiling and scalable solvers (low-rank/RFF), include ablations (kernel/λ/minibatch/noise/x–h), expand quantitative generation and uncertainty evaluations, and clarify theoretical assumptions with a proof sketch.
- Recommendation: Weak accept.
- Reviewer confidence: Medium.
