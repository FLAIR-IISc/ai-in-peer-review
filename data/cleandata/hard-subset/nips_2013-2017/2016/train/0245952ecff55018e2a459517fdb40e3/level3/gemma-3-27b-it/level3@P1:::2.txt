This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) as an extension of previous Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting. The core idea is to learn a conditional distribution p(y|x) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors address the challenge of extending MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional case, and evaluate CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, demonstrating competitive performance.

The work builds on a growing body of research leveraging kernel methods for generative modeling, notably the earlier GMMN work [19] and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) [5]. While GANs have achieved impressive results, they suffer from training instability. GMMN offered a more stable alternative via a moment matching approach. This paper extends that stability-focused approach to conditional distributions, an important step for broader applicability. The authors correctly identify the increasing relevance of conditional models in tasks like predictive modeling [23, 16] and contextual generation [21, 32, 31]. Related work includes conditional GANs [21] and conditional variational autoencoders [28].

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Extending moment matching to the conditional setting is a logical and valuable step, unlocking a wider range of applications.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid theoretical basis for CMMD, grounding the method in kernel embeddings and Hilbert spaces.  The connection to existing MMD theory is clearly established.
* **Competitive Performance:**  Experimental results across diverse tasks demonstrate that CGMMN achieves competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods. The experiments showcase the versatility of the approach.
* **Backpropagation Enabled Learning:** The method leverages the convenience of backpropagation for efficient training, a significant advantage.
* **Clear Motivation:** The introduction effectively articulates the motivation for conditional generative modeling and the limitations of existing approaches.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Lack of Intuition:** While the theoretical treatment is rigorous, the paper lacks an intuitive explanation of the CMMD objective.  The leap from the unconditional MMD to the conditional CMMD is not adequately motivated conceptually. This makes understanding the core contribution difficult for readers less familiar with the technical details.
* **Computational Clarity:** The paper glosses over the practical computational details.  The computational complexity of estimating and optimizing CMMD, particularly with large datasets, is not discussed.
* **Equation (2) Explanation:** Equation (2), the optimized CMMD objective, is presented without sufficient explanation. The derivation is absent, as are discussions of the assumptions underlying the formula and its practical implications. It is unclear how this equation is connected to the kernel gram matrices.
* **Algorithm Transparency:**  The step-by-step mapping from Equation (2) to the actual implemented algorithm is insufficiently detailed.  The supplemental material is reportedly difficult to follow, exacerbating this issue. This hinders reproducibility and understanding.
* **Overly Formal Initial Sections:** The initial sections are very dense with mathematical notation.  A more gradual introduction of the concepts would improve accessibility.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a solid contribution that extends existing moment matching approaches to the important problem of conditional generative modeling. The theoretical foundation is strong, and the experimental results are promising. However, the paper suffers from a lack of intuitive explanation and insufficient detail regarding the computational aspects of the method. The complexity of the mathematics overwhelms the underlying ideas, hindering understanding and potentially limiting the paper’s impact.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*  Presents a novel and technically sound extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
*  Demonstrates competitive performance on a range of tasks.
*  Addresses a relevant and important problem in generative modeling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*  Lacks intuitive explanations, making the core contribution difficult to grasp.
*  Insufficiently details the practical computation and implementation of the algorithm.
*  The derivation of key equations (specifically Equation 2) is missing.




**Specific Requests for Revision:**

1. **Intuitive Derivation of Equation (2):** Please provide an intuitive, step-by-step derivation of Equation (2), explaining the reasoning behind each term and the connection to the conditional MMD objective.  Focus on building an understanding of *why* this equation is the appropriate objective.
2. **Algorithm Mapping:**  Provide a clear, step-by-step mapping from Equation (2) to the practical algorithm implemented in the experiments. Detail exactly how the kernel gram matrices are computed, how the optimization is performed, and what approximations or simplifications are made. Clarify how mini-batching is used to estimate the CMMD.



I rate this paper as a **Weak Accept**. Addressing the requested revisions, particularly clarifying the intuition and computational details, would significantly improve the paper’s clarity and impact, justifying a stronger acceptance recommendation.
