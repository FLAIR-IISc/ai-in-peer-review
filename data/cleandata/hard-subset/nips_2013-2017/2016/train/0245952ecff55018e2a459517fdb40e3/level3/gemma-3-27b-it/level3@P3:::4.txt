This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to conditional generation using a kernel embedding of conditional moments. The approach leverages the existing optimization machinery of GMMN, offering a conceptually straightforward and practically efficient method for learning conditional distributions. The authors demonstrate competitive performance across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation, supported by a clear and well-structured presentation.

The work builds upon recent advancements in deep generative modeling, notably Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and GMMNs. While GANs have shown impressive results, their training can be unstable due to the min-max optimization. GMMNs offer a more stable alternative based on maximum mean discrepancy (MMD). This paper logically extends GMMNs to the conditional setting, addressing a crucial limitation of the original framework. Related work in conditional generative modeling includes conditional GANs and variational autoencoders, providing alternative approaches to tackling similar problems. However, the presented CGMMN offers a distinct advantage through its streamlined optimization process inherited from the GMMN framework.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The paper demonstrates a strong understanding of kernel methods and their application to generative modeling. The derivation of the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion is well-explained and appears theoretically sound. While a complete verification of all equations was not possible within the scope of this review, the presented logic is compelling and consistent with established principles.
* **Clarity and Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The concepts are clearly articulated, and the mathematical notation is used effectively. The inclusion of Algorithm 1 provides a practical guide for implementing the proposed method. The appendix further enhances understanding by providing detailed methodological and experimental information.
* **Originality:** Extending GMMNs to the conditional setting is a natural progression, but the derivation of the empirical estimator for CMMD is not trivial and represents a valuable contribution. The technique of leveraging kernel embeddings for conditional distributions, coupled with the efficient backpropagation-based optimization, sets this work apart.
* **Significance:** The paper addresses a significant problem in generative modeling – learning conditional distributions – with a practical and efficient solution. The demonstrated competitive performance on diverse tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, Bayesian distillation) suggests the potential for widespread adoption. The ease of application, as highlighted by the authors, makes it particularly relevant for practitioners in the field.
* **Empirical Evaluation:** The thorough experimental evaluation across various datasets and tasks convincingly demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approach. The results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, highlighting the practical value of CGMMN.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn’t extensively discuss the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter settings (e.g., kernel bandwidth, regularization strength). A more detailed analysis of hyperparameter tuning and its impact on performance would strengthen the empirical evaluation.
* **Computational Cost:** While the paper highlights the efficiency of backpropagation, the computational cost of kernel calculations, particularly for large datasets, isn’t thoroughly addressed. Exploring techniques for reducing the computational burden (e.g., using approximations or optimized kernel implementations) could further enhance the practicality of the method.
* **Limited Comparison to Strongest Baselines:** While comparisons are made to relevant baselines, some of the strongest contemporary generative models (e.g., recent advancements in GAN architectures) are not directly compared. Adding these comparisons, where feasible, would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the CGMMN's performance.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.
* The approach is well-motivated, clearly explained, and supported by strong experimental results.
* The method is practical and potentially impactful for a wide range of applications.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and easy to follow.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity and computational cost is limited.
* The comparison to some of the most recent and powerful generative models is missing.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The authors have successfully extended the GMMN framework to the conditional setting, providing a practical and efficient method for learning conditional distributions. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and supported by compelling experimental results. Despite minor weaknesses regarding hyperparameter analysis and comparison to the very latest models, the overall quality of the work warrants acceptance to NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the noted weaknesses. Specifically, the authors should expand the discussion on hyperparameter tuning and computational cost, and consider including comparisons to more recent generative models if feasible.
