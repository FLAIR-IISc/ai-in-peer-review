- Paper extends GMMN to conditionals by introducing a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective and training deep conditional generators via backpropagation.  
- Main idea: compare conditional mean embeddings CY|X of data and model using Hilbert–Schmidt distance and optimize generator f(x,h;w).  
- Positions work relative to prior kernel embedding literature (Gretton et al.; Song et al.) and deep generative models (GANs, CVAEs, VAEs).  
- Experiments: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, with broadly competitive results.  

Strengths:
- Conceptually clean and significant extension of MMD to conditional models that avoids min–max optimization.  
- Broad applicability with a simple sampling/training loop compatible with CNN/MLP architectures.  
- Empirical results competitive with strong baselines; Bayesian distillation use-case is interesting.  

Weaknesses / clarity concerns:
- Presentation begins with heavy formalism and lacks an accessible intuition for the conditional objective compared to standard MMD.  
- Equation (2), central to the method, is under-explained: no derivation, assumptions, or practical implications are given.  
- The path from Eq. (2) to the implemented algorithm is insufficiently detailed and the supplement is hard to follow.  
- Computational details are opaque: kernel choices on X and Y, choice of λ, batchwise Gram matrix construction, and complexity/memory costs are not specified.  
- No discussion of approximations or scalability techniques (e.g., low-rank approximations or random features) for large batches/datasets.  

Requested clarifications (high priority):
- Provide an intuitive derivation of Eq. (2) starting from CY|X ≈ Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤, expand ∥Ĉd−Ĉs∥2F⊗G, and show how it yields the trace form.  
- Use the finite-X case to link CMMD to a weighted MMD where weights depend on X similarities and include a toy example illustrating non-uniform weights from different conditionals.  
- Give a clear, step-by-step mapping from Eq. (2) to the practical algorithm: how mini-batches are sampled and how Υ, Φ, Ks, Kd, Ls, Ld, cross-Grams, and K̃−1 are computed.  
- State which quantities depend on the generator parameters w and confirm that gradients flow only through the Φ of generated Y.  
- Specify kernel choices, differentiability requirements for kernels, per-batch computational complexity, memory usage, and any stabilization tricks (bandwidth selection, λ, centering).  

Pros for acceptance:
- Novel and timely conditional extension of MMD training.  
- Simple, general framework with competitive empirical performance.  
- Bridges kernel embedding theory and practical deep conditional generation.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Insufficiently clear objective intuition and inadequate algorithmic exposition.  
- Limited analysis of computational efficiency and scalability.  
- Derivation and assumptions around Eq. (2) are under-documented and need stronger justification.  

- With the requested clarifications and stronger exposition of derivations and computational details, the paper could become a solid contribution.
