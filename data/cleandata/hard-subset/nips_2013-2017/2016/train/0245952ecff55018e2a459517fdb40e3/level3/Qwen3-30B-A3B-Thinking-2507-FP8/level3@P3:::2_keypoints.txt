- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to model conditional distributions using a new Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- Builds on kernel embedding of conditional distributions and extends MMD to the conditional case.
- Addresses a practical limitation of GMMN by learning p(y|x) for predictive and contextual generation.
- Theoretical foundation is sound and the extension is conceptually meaningful.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Shows competitive performance and strong conditional generation results, including class-specific digit generation.
- Novel application to Bayesian dark knowledge demonstrates practical utility.
- Presentation is unclear and overly mathematical, lacking sufficient intuitive explanations.
- Fails to clearly articulate the core difficulty of matching different conditional output distributions across inputs.
- Equation (2), the central objective, is presented without derivation, assumptions, or justification.
- The mapping from the theoretical objective to the implemented SGD algorithm is insufficiently detailed.
- Computational efficiency concerns (cubic kernel Gram matrix cost) are mentioned but not properly addressed for mini-batch use.
- Lacks an intuitive explanation for why CMMD succeeds, unlike the clearer intuition for non-conditional GMMN.
- Insufficient differentiation from related work (conditional GANs, conditional VAEs) and unclear comparative advantages.
- Recommendation: reject due to poor exposition; authors should provide intuitive derivation of Eq.(2), clear algorithmic mapping, efficiency analysis, and clearer contrasts to related methods before resubmission.
