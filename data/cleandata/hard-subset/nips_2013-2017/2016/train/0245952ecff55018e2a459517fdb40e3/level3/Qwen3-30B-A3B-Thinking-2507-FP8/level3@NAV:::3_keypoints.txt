- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions via conditional kernel mean embeddings.
- Proposes a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) training criterion for conditional generative modeling.
- Targets applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.
- Builds on established kernel embedding techniques and GMMN; main novelty is adapting MMD to conditional settings.
- Technical approach is sound with clear theoretical foundations and straightforward implementation.
- Empirical results are competitive on MNIST and SVHN for classification tasks.
- Authors addressed prior concerns about low-dimensional latent spaces, scalability, and utility comparisons.
- Bayesian distillation application is novel and interesting but evaluated on only a single dataset.
- Mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear; dot notation ambiguity (matrix vs. Hadamard) needs clarification.
- Symbol C is overloaded (conditional embedding vs. other uses), causing notation confusion.
- Theorem 3 lacks proper reference or proof, undermining an important theoretical claim.
- Empirical validation is limited to digit/face datasets; lack of experiments on challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) limits generality.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and contains sufficient detail for reproducibility.
- Originality is moderate—an extension of existing work rather than a fundamentally new approach.
- Overall contribution is modest and technically competent; recommendation is rejection pending theoretical clarifications and additional experiments.
