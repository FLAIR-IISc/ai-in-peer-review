- Proposes CGMMN: a neural method using conditional MMD to learn conditional distributions, extending GMMN.  
- Paper is well-organized and generally clearly written; math exposition can be hard to follow without kernel/Hilbert-space background.  
- Provides a good related-work overview (GMMN, GANs, other deep generative models).  
- CGMMN is a novel and relevant extension in deep generative modeling.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Experimental results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Experiments are relatively small-scale; larger datasets are needed to show scalability.  
- Training requires computationally expensive steps (e.g., kernel Gram matrix inverse); runtime/efficiency comparisons (e.g., to DSN) are missing.  
- Reproducibility is a concern—reference code should be provided.  
- Model description lacks sufficient implementation detail (architectures, hyperparameters, training procedures) for exact reproduction.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical and experimental support for its claims.  
- Overall significant contribution, but computational efficiency and reproducibility issues need to be addressed.
