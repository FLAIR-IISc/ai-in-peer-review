- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions.  
- Introduces the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion with derivation and theoretical analysis.  
- Proves CMMD is a sufficient condition for equality of two conditional distributions.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear overview of related work and motivations for the conditional extension.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Results show CGMMN is competitive with VA, MMVA, and CGAN, with good-quality generated samples.  
- Heavy reliance on formal mathematics makes the paper harder for non-experts to follow.  
- Lacks an intuitive explanation of CMMD and its relationship to MMD.  
- Fails to clearly describe algorithmic computations and assess computational efficiency.  
- Insufficient discussion of hyperparameter choices (e.g., regularization λ, minibatch size).  
- No thorough analysis of robustness to noise or outliers in data.  
- Originality is strong: a novel and significant extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.  
- Potentially significant impact for predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, solid theoretical support, and strong experimental validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: accessibility due to math, missing computational details, and limited hyperparameter/robustness analysis.  
- Reviewer rating: 8/10; Confidence: 8/10.
