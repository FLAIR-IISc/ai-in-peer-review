- Proposes a novel Supervised PageRank learning approach that incorporates node and edge features.  
- Introduces two optimization methods: a gradient-free random optimization with inexact oracle and a gradient-based adaptive projected method with inexact first-order oracle.  
- Methods are designed to handle non-convex loss functions and inexact oracles.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear explanations.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical guarantees: convergence rates and complexity bounds for both methods.  
- Experiments on a web page ranking task show the proposed methods outperform a state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline.  
- Strength: novel, effective optimization methods for Supervised PageRank.  
- Strength: thorough, rigorous analysis of convergence and complexity.  
- Strength: empirical validation on a real-world dataset.  
- Weakness: insufficient detailed comparisons with other existing methods.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation limited to a single dataset, limiting claims of robustness.  
- Weakness: some notation and terminology may be hard to follow for non-experts in optimization/graph theory.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address comparisons, broader experiments, and clarity.  
- Rating: 8/10.
