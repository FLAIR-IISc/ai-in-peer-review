- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Core idea: use kernel embeddings and MMD for conditional generative modeling as a natural extension of prior GMMN work.  
- Demonstrates utility across tasks: predictive modeling (classification), contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive on MNIST and Yale Face datasets; Bayesian distillation shown on a single dataset.  
- Revisions addressed prior reviewer concerns about latent space dimensionality, scalability, and model utility.  
- The mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear and needs explicit explanation of the weighting mechanism and implications.  
- Overloading the symbol 'C' (conditional embedding and other variables) causes significant confusion and should be fixed.  
- Theorem 3 is presented without citation or proof, which is a critical theoretical oversight.  
- Results are comparable to GMMN but not substantially better, so benefits of the conditional extension need further justification.  
- Supervised learning (classification) results are acceptable but not a strong showcase of the model’s capabilities.  
- Bayesian distillation result is interesting but requires evaluation on broader datasets and models to establish generality.  
- Wider adoption will require stronger results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to conditional distributions, solid kernel/MMD grounding, diverse empirical evaluation, and addressed prior concerns.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear derivation of Eq.(2), symbol overloading, missing proof/reference for Theorem 3, incremental improvement over GMMN, and limited Bayesian distillation evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions—clarify Eq.(2), fix symbol overloading, and provide proof or reference for Theorem 3.
