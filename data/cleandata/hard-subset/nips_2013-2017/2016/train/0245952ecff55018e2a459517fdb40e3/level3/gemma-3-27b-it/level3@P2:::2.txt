This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the framework of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) to learn conditional distributions. The core idea is to employ a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion for training, enabling applications like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The authors demonstrate competitive performance across these tasks.

The paper builds upon a strong foundation in deep generative models and kernel methods. GMMNs, themselves a recent advancement offering a simpler training objective than GANs, are extended to the conditional setting – a significant step, as simply conditioning a GMMN is not straightforward. Related work in conditional GANs (Mirza et al., 2014) and conditional variational autoencoders (Sohn et al., 2015) are appropriately cited, positioning CGMMN within the broader landscape of generative modeling. The use of MMD, drawing from Gretton et al. (2012), is a well-established technique for non-parametric density estimation and distribution comparison.

**Strengths:**

* **Novel Extension:** Extending MMD-based generative models to the conditional setting is a valuable contribution. Many applications fundamentally require modeling conditional distributions, and this work provides a principled approach.
* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a solid theoretical basis for CMMD, leveraging kernel embeddings and Hilbert space properties. Theorems 1-3 clearly establish the mathematical grounding.
* **Empirical Validation:** The comprehensive evaluation across diverse tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, and dark knowledge distillation) demonstrates the versatility and competitive performance of CGMMN. Results on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face datasets are encouraging.
* **Clear Presentation of Related Work:** The authors demonstrate a good understanding of the related literature and appropriately position their work within the field.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Lack of Intuition:** While mathematically sound, the initial presentation of CMMD feels dense and lacks an intuitive explanation. The leap from the unconditional MMD to the conditional CMMD objective is not sufficiently motivated for readers unfamiliar with the intricacies of kernel methods.
* **Computational Clarity:** The computational details, particularly surrounding Equation (2), are insufficient. The paper doesn’t clearly delineate the practical implications of the equation – how it’s implemented efficiently, the computational cost, and the regularization parameter’s influence.
* **Derivation and Algorithm Mapping:** The derivation of Equation (2) is presented as a conclusion, rather than a step-by-step derivation, making it hard to grasp the underlying assumptions and simplifications. Crucially, the paper lacks a clear mapping from Equation (2) to the actual algorithm implemented, particularly concerning mini-batch estimation and the handling of inverse matrices. The reference to the supplementary material doesn’t fully address this issue, as it remains highly technical and lacks a high-level explanation.
* **Assumptions and Limitations:** While the authors mention some assumptions (e.g., smoothness for the CMMD objective), a more thorough discussion of the limitations of the approach would strengthen the paper. For example, the sensitivity to kernel choice and the potential for high computational cost with large datasets are not adequately addressed.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a technically sound and potentially valuable contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The extension of MMD to the conditional setting is a significant step, and the empirical results are promising. However, the lack of intuitive explanation, insufficient computational clarity, and missing link between the theoretical formulation and the practical algorithm hinder the paper's overall impact. The paper fulfills the quality criterion by being technically sound and presenting a complete piece of work, but clarity could be significantly improved. Originality is strong as it addresses a novel extension of GMMNs. Significance is good; conditional generative models are important, and a competitive approach is valuable.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and theoretically grounded extension of GMMNs.
* Competitive empirical performance across diverse tasks.
* Clear presentation of related work and strong mathematical foundation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Lack of intuitive explanation for the conditional objective (CMMD).
* Insufficient computational details and a missing link between the equation and the algorithm.
* Limited discussion of the assumptions, limitations, and potential drawbacks of the approach.



**Specific Requests for Revision:**

1. **Intuitive Derivation of Equation (2):** Provide a more accessible, step-by-step derivation of Equation (2), explaining the key assumptions and simplifications made. Focus on building intuition rather than purely mathematical rigor.
2. **Algorithm Mapping:** Include a detailed, step-by-step explanation of how Equation (2) is translated into the practical algorithm used in the experiments, particularly regarding mini-batch estimation, kernel computations, and the handling of matrix inversions. Explain how the algorithm handles potential computational bottlenecks.
