- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- The approach is grounded in kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions and relates to prior conditional kernel embedding work.  
- Novelty: first MMD-based method for conditional generative modeling and an extension to Bayesian distillation.  
- Theoretical foundation is generally sound, but Theorem 3 is presented without reference or proof.  
- Notation issues reduce clarity: ambiguous dot notation (matrix vs. Hadamard product) and overloaded symbol C.  
- The connection between the CMMD objective and standard MMD is insufficiently explained for non-experts.  
- Empirical results show competitive performance on predictive tasks (MNIST, SVHN) and generative tasks (MNIST, Yale Face).  
- Bayesian distillation application is interesting and novel but evaluated on only a single regression dataset.  
- Scalability concerns are partly addressed via mini-batch implementation; low-dimensional latent space concern was clarified by authors.  
- Lack of evaluation on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10) limits claims of broad applicability.  
- No clear performance advantage over existing conditional generative models (conditional GANs/VAEs) in presented experiments.  
- Supervised learning application appears weaker than generative modeling strengths.  
- Strengths: clear theoretical extension, versatility across tasks, competitive empirical results, and responsiveness to prior reviewer concerns.  
- Weaknesses: ambiguous notation, missing proof/reference for Theorem 3, limited and narrow empirical evaluation, and no demonstrated superiority over alternatives.  
- Recommendation: Accept, conditional on clarifying notation and providing the missing proof/reference; authors should expand evaluations on harder datasets in future work.
