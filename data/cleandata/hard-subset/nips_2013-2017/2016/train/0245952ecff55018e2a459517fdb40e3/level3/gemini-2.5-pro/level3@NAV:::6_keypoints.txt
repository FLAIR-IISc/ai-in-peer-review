- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs) and a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective extending GMMNs to conditional modeling.  
- CMMD uses kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and matches the conditional embedding operator directly.  
- Treating all conditional distributions as one family avoids data sparsity issues, especially with continuous conditioning variables.  
- CMMD has a clean formulation and key matrices independent of model parameters, making it amenable to backpropagation.  
- Empirical results on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation are promising and show the method’s versatility.  
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are difficult to follow and not self-contained, harming accessibility and reproducibility.  
- Missing experimental comparison to a naive baseline (e.g., a GMMN trained per condition with shared parameters), which would quantify the method’s advantage.  
- Potential computational trade-off: CMMD may need large minibatches to reduce estimator variance while matrix inversions incur high cost; sensitivity analysis is required.  
- Technical errors: the paper’s claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect for the used formulation.  
- Clarify apparent inconsistency in the conditional embedding operator definition relative to reference [29].  
- Overall assessment: core contribution is solid, principled, and potentially impactful despite clarity and experimental shortcomings.
