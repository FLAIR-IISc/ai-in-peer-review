This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the Generative Moment Matching Network (GMMN) framework to learn conditional distributions. The core idea leverages conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) as a training criterion, utilizing kernel mean embeddings to avoid assumptions about the underlying distributions. The authors demonstrate the applicability of CGMMN across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, showing competitive performance on various datasets.

The work builds upon the success of MMD in deep generative modeling, as pioneered by Gretton et al. (2012) and further developed by Dziugaite et al. (2015) with GMMNs.  It addresses a critical limitation of GMMN – its focus on joint distributions – by introducing a method for effectively modeling conditional probabilities. This extension aligns with the broader trend in generative modeling towards more controlled generation, as seen in Conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and Variational Autoencoders (Kingma & Welling, 2013).  The authors also connect their work to related areas like sum-product networks (Gens & Domingos, 2008) and demonstrate a practical application in knowledge distillation, building on the concept introduced by Hinton et al. (2015).

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper presents a theoretically sound approach, building upon established concepts from kernel methods and generative modeling. The derivation of the CMMD criterion appears logically consistent, and the use of back-propagation for training is a practical advantage.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The introduction clearly motivates the problem and outlines the contributions. The explanation of CMMD and its relation to MMD is relatively straightforward.
*   **Originality:** Extending MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting is a novel contribution. The proposed CMMD criterion provides a principled way to learn conditional distributions without relying on adversarial training or variational inference.
*   **Significance:** The work offers a valuable addition to the toolkit for generative modeling. The ability to learn conditional distributions opens up possibilities for a wider range of applications, including controlled image generation, contextual prediction, and knowledge distillation. The distillation application is particularly interesting.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Clarity (Mathematical Notation):** The transition from Equation (2) to the described weighted MMD form is unclear and requires further elaboration. The meaning of the dot notation (matrix multiplication versus Hadamard product) needs explicit clarification to avoid ambiguity.
*   **Clarity (Symbol Overloading):** The reuse of the symbol 'C' to denote both the conditional embedding operator *and* other quantities is confusing and should be avoided to improve readability.
*   **Technical Soundness (Missing Proof/Citation):** Theorem 3, stating the equivalence between conditional embedding operators and conditional distributions, lacks a proper reference or proof.  Providing either would significantly strengthen the theoretical foundation of the work.
*   **Significance (Limited Empirical Scope):** While the empirical results are promising, the evaluation is limited to relatively simple datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face). Demonstrating performance on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, ImageNet, LSUN) would significantly increase the impact of the paper.
*   **Significance (Supervised Learning Application):** Using CGMMN for supervised learning (predictive modeling) feels somewhat less compelling than its generative capabilities. While it demonstrates versatility, this application doesn't fully leverage the strengths of the generative approach.
*   **Significance (Bayesian Distillation Generalizability):** The Bayesian distillation experiment is intriguing but limited to a single dataset and Bayesian model. Demonstrating wider applicability would strengthen this contribution.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach for learning conditional generative models.
*   The CMMD criterion provides a principled alternative to existing methods like GANs and VAEs.
*   The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on a range of tasks.
*   The work addresses a gap in the existing literature by extending MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The clarity of the mathematical notation and symbolism could be improved.
*   The theoretical justification for Theorem 3 needs further attention.
*   The empirical evaluation is limited in scope and could benefit from more challenging datasets.
*   The application to supervised learning feels less central to the core contribution of the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN framework offers a promising alternative to existing methods, with a clear theoretical foundation and encouraging empirical results. Addressing the minor weaknesses related to clarity and empirical scope would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions. Specifically, the authors should clarify the mathematical notation, provide a reference or proof for Theorem 3, and consider including results on more challenging datasets.
