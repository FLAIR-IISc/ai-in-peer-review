- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.
- Matches moments between true and generated conditional distributions via CMMD.
- Evaluated on classification, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation tasks.
- Builds on GMMNs and GANs, arguing GMMNs offer more stable training than GANs.
- Technically sound: solid kernel/MMD foundation and a clean CMMD formulation.
- Treating all conditionals as a family avoids data sparsity compared to per-condition GMMNs.
- Experimental results are promising and competitive on MNIST, SVHN, and distillation.
- Backpropagation-friendly formulation; K matrices independent of model parameters simplify gradients.
- Clarity issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and rely too much on reference [29] without sufficient in-paper derivation.
- Computational cost concern: CMMD may require large minibatches and expensive matrix inversions; trade-offs not fully explored.
- Minor technical error: paper incorrectly states the MMD estimator is unbiased; it is asymptotically consistent but not unbiased.
- Inconsistency with reference [29]: conditional embedding operator definition differs and needs clarification/justification.
- Limited ablation studies: missing analysis of hidden variable dimensionality, kernel choice, and other architecture components.
- Originality: novel extension of MMD to conditional generation and practical application to Bayesian model distillation.
- Significance: potential impact on image generation, machine translation, time series, and model distillation.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, competitive experiments, theoretically grounded CMMD, and practical training.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems, computational burden, minor errors, and insufficient ablations.
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address clarity, technical corrections, and computational mitigation.
