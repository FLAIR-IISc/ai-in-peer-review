This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a framework for learning conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors extend the capability of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to address a wide range of application problems, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The paper provides a thorough mathematical formulation of the CMMD criterion, which measures the difference between the kernel mean embeddings of two conditional distributions. The authors also present a deep generative architecture and a learning algorithm for CGMMN, which can be trained using stochastic gradient descent with back-propagation.

The experimental results demonstrate that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art models in various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The authors evaluate CGMMN on several datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face, and show that it can achieve comparable performance to other models while keeping the training process simple.
