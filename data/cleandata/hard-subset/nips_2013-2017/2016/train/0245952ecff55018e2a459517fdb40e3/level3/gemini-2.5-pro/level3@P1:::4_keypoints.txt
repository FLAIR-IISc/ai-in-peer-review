- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN): a conditional generative model mapping conditioning variable x and noise h to output y trained by minimizing CMMD.
- Proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD): an MMD extension to conditional distributions using kernel mean embedding operators in an RKHS.
- Derives an empirical, differentiable CMMD estimator computable from samples, enabling end-to-end training with SGD and backprop.
- Builds on GMMN and conditional kernel embedding theory (Song et al. 2009), analogous to Conditional GANs/VAEs as the conditional counterpart.
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian model distillation ("Bayesian dark knowledge").
- Strength: strong theoretical foundation and a principled, non-adversarial objective for conditional generative modeling.
- Strength: practical and simple training algorithm (Algorithm 1) using kernel Gram matrices and backprop, offering stable training compared to adversarial methods.
- Strength: broad applicability with convincing empirical evaluation and an interesting distillation application.
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation and helpful appendix that aids understanding and reproducibility.
- Weakness: computational scalability — CMMD requires inverting batch-sized kernel matrices (O(B^3)), which can bottleneck large-batch training; needs discussion of approximations (e.g., random features).
- Weakness: generative sample quality is noisier and less sharp than contemporary GANs; base CGMMN may need architectural/kernel improvements and lacks direct qualitative GAN comparisons.
- Overall assessment: high-quality, clear, original, and significant contribution that extends MMD-based models to conditional settings and is well-suited for the conference despite scalability and sample-quality caveats.
