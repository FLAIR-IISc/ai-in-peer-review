- Introduces CGMMN: extends GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embeddings of conditional moments.  
- Leverages GMMN’s MMD-based optimization for a conceptually simple and practically efficient conditional generator.  
- Shows competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured; includes Algorithm 1 and a detailed appendix.  
- Theoretical contribution: derivation of conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) is well-explained and appears sound.  
- Places work in context of GANs/GMMNs/VAEs; CGMMN offers a more stable alternative to GANs by avoiding min–max optimization.  
- Originality: non-trivial empirical estimator for CMMD and use of kernel embeddings for conditional distributions.  
- Significance: addresses the important problem of learning conditional distributions with practical applicability.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough across datasets and tasks, demonstrating competitive results.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., kernel bandwidth, regularization).  
- Weakness: computational cost of kernel calculations for large datasets is not thoroughly discussed.  
- Weakness: limited direct comparison to some of the strongest recent generative-model baselines (advanced GANs).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—paper is strong, technically sound, and practically useful.  
- Requested fixes: expand discussion on hyperparameter tuning and computational cost, and add comparisons to more recent generative models where feasible.
