- Proposal: Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using conditional MMD (CMMD) to learn p(y|x) via kernel embeddings and deep generators that concatenate x with noise.  
- Training: Minimizes Hilbert–Schmidt norm between estimated conditional mean operators with an end-to-end, trace-based, backprop-compatible loss.  
- Motivation/prior work: Builds on MMD/GMMN, kernel mean embeddings and conditional embeddings (Song et al., Grunewalder et al.); positioned against cGAN and CVAE as a non-adversarial alternative.  
- Applications shown: Classification (labels as conditional outputs), conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation (PBP → student on Boston Housing).  

Strengths
- Conceptual novelty: Principled extension of MMD/GMMN to conditional setting via operator-valued embeddings.  
- Optimization: Avoids adversarial min–max instability; uses a simple differentiable objective.  
- Empirical versatility: Demonstrations across classification, conditional generation, and distillation; shows combination with autoencoders improves samples.  
- Practicality: Provides minibatch training recipe and simple sampling strategy.

Weaknesses
- Theoretical gaps: Theorem 3 lacks proof/citation and required assumptions are not clarified.  
- Notation/derivations unclear: Mapping from operator norms to weighted MMD form (Eq.2) and ambiguous symbols/dot notation need clarification.  
- Limited experiments: Conditional generation only on digits/faces, no comparisons to strong baselines (cGAN/CVAE) or challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Missing quantitative metrics: No Inception score, FID, conditional likelihood proxies, or broader evaluation beyond visuals.  
- Supervised performance: CGMMN underperforms strong discriminative baselines; value in supervised setting is limited.  
- Distillation evaluation limited: Only one small regression dataset and one teacher; lacks calibration and NLL metrics.

Quality / Requests for revision
- Proofs/citations: Provide proof or citation for Theorem 3 and clarify measurability/universality conditions.  
- Eq. (2) derivation: Spell out conversion from operator norm to Gram-matrix trace form and define all matrix operations and shapes.  
- Minibatch estimator properties: Discuss bias/variance of CMMD minibatch estimator and whether stochastic gradients are unbiased.  
- Complexity/scalability: Address O(b^3) K̃−1 inversion per minibatch; report batch sizes, runtimes, memory, and mitigation strategies (Nyström, RFF, block solves).  
- Kernel choices: Specify kernels and bandwidth selection for x and y, and sensitivity analyses.  
- Distillation metrics: Report predictive NLL and calibration for teacher/student evaluations.

Clarity improvements
- Notation: Avoid overloading C; use CY|X for operators, Kx/Ky for Gram matrices, and explicit symbols for weighting matrices.  
- Matrix definitions: Define all matrices in equations with dimensions and replace ambiguous “·” with explicit multiplication or ∘ for Hadamard.  
- Minor fixes: Correct typos (e.g., “MINIST”, “adverisarial”).  
- Gradient summary: Explicitly summarize gradients w.r.t. y and network parameters and indicate which terms depend on parameters vs. treated constant.

Originality
- Novel use of conditional mean embeddings to derive a tractable, minibatch-implementable training loss for deep conditional generators; original relative to GMMN/CVAE/cGAN.

Significance
- Moderate: Offers a simple, stable alternative for conditional generation with potential practical appeal, but broader impact requires stronger benchmarks on complex tasks and comparisons.

Pros for acceptance
- Clean conceptual extension of GMMN to conditional settings.  
- Stable, easily implementable objective; versatile application demos.

Cons against acceptance
- Empirical breadth and rigor are limited; lacks comparison to strong baselines and quantitative metrics.  
- Theoretical statements and derivations need clarification or proof.  
- Modest classification and distillation evaluations do not strongly demonstrate advantage.

Questions for authors
- Provide proof or reference for Theorem 3 and clarify necessary assumptions.  
- Which kernels and bandwidths were used per task, and how sensitive are results to these choices?  
- Is the minibatch CMMD gradient unbiased, and what are convergence properties?  
- Report runtime/memory vs. batch size and compare training cost to cGAN/CVAE.  
- For distillation, can you add NLL and calibration metrics and test a second teacher/dataset (e.g., SGHMC BNN)?

Suggested experiments and improvements
- Add experiments on CIFAR-10 or LSUN with conditional labels/attributes and compare to cGAN/CVAE with quantitative metrics.  
- Explore kernel approximations (Nyström, RFF) to scale batch sizes.  
- Ablations: noise dimensionality, conditioning strategy (concatenate vs. additive), and kernel choices.  
- Release code to aid reproducibility.

Overall evaluation
- Recommendation: Weak accept — principled and useful conditional extension of GMMN with promising preliminary results, but needs theoretical clarifications and stronger empirical comparisons.
