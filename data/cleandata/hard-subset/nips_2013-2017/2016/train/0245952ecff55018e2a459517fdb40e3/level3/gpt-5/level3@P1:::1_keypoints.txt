- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMN to learn conditional distributions via a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) objective.
- Key idea: embed conditional distributions using the conditional mean operator CY|X in an RKHS and minimize the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical and generator-induced operators.
- Generator f(x,h;w) conditions on x and noise h and is trained end-to-end by backprop through a differentiable kernel on outputs.
- CMMD admits a closed-form trace/Gram-matrix expression, enabling minibatch SGD training.
- Experiments: discriminative prediction (MNIST/SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian predictive distillation (“Bayesian dark knowledge”).
- Empirical performance is competitive with prior MMD-based generators and some conditional methods, but lags behind strong discriminative CNNs.
- The CMMD objective is well-motivated theoretically under universality assumptions and derivation to Gram-matrix trace form is sound.
- Important experimental and implementation details are missing or buried in the appendix (kernels, bandwidths, λ, noise dimension, sampling details, stability tricks).
- Computational cost is a concern due to O(B^3) per-minibatch inversions of (K+λI); no runtime or memory comparisons are provided.
- Paper clarity is generally good but CMMD derivation is dense; key assumptions and mini-batch estimator steps should be moved into the main text.
- Reproducibility would benefit from explicit hyperparameter lists (kernels, λ, noise distribution/dim, learning rates) and an algorithm box for minibatch CMMD.
- Originality: natural but nontrivial extension from joint to conditional MMD via operator matching; novel empirical application to deep conditional generative models (circa NIPS 2016).
- Using CMMD for Bayesian dark knowledge is an interesting alternative to KL-based distillation methods.
- Significance: conditional modeling with a stable, single-objective alternative to adversarial training is valuable, but empirical scope is limited to small/medium benchmarks.
- Strengths: principled, single-objective training; flexible architectures; closed-form minibatch objective; competitive results on standard tasks.
- Weaknesses: scalability (cubic batch cost), sensitivity to kernel/hyperparameter choices, limited baselines (no cVAE/cGAN comparisons), and insufficient quantitative sample evaluation.
- Reproducibility concerns: missing architectural/training details and no released code.
- Theoretical caveats: operator equality is sufficient but may not hold in practice (non-characteristic kernels on x); robustness discussion is lacking.
- Suggested additions: report wall-clock times and ablations on batch size/λ, sensitivity analyses for kernels/h, compare to cVAE/cGAN, and add sample-quality metrics and calibration/NLL for distillation.
- Recommend releasing reference code and full hyperparameter/architecture specs to aid reproducibility.
- Arguments for acceptance: conceptually clean generalization of MMD to conditionals, stable objective, and broad applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: unresolved scalability/runtime issues, limited empirical scope and baselines, and reproducibility gaps.
- Overall: solid and relevant contribution leaning toward acceptance if authors address computational cost, add missing baselines/details, and improve reproducibility.
