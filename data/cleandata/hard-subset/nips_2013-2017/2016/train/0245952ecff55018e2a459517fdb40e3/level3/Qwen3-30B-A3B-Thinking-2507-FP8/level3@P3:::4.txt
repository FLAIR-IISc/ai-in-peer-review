

# Review: Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a natural extension of the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to conditional generation. While GMMN (Li et al., ICML 2015) learns unconditional distributions via maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) with kernel mean embeddings, CGMMN extends this to conditional distributions using conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD). The authors derive a CMMD objective that measures the difference between kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions, and show that it can be optimized using the same back-propagation machinery as the unconditional GMMN. This work addresses an important gap in the literature, as previous moment-matching approaches were limited to unconditional generation, while conditional generation is crucial for many applications including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.

The paper is technically sound and well-justified. The authors provide solid theoretical foundations, including Theorem 3 which establishes that equality of the conditional embedding operators implies equality of conditional distributions. The derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator (Equation 2) is particularly noteworthy, as it's non-obvious but crucial for practical implementation. The authors demonstrate competitive performance on three distinct tasks: predictive modeling (MNIST and SVHN classification), contextual generation (MNIST and Yale Face images conditioned on class labels), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation (on Boston housing regression). The experimental design is comprehensive, with careful attention to architectural details and hyperparameters, and the results consistently show that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art methods in each domain.

The paper is exceptionally well-written and clear. The mathematical derivations are presented logically, with helpful explanations of the connections between MMD and CMMD (Remark 1). Algorithm 1 provides a concise, practical implementation guide for practitioners. The appendix contains valuable supplementary details on experimental setup, architectural choices, and additional results, which greatly enhances the reproducibility of the work. The writing style is accessible to readers familiar with deep learning and kernel methods, making the technical content approachable.

Regarding novelty, while extending GMMN to conditional generation might seem natural, the authors successfully demonstrate that the derivation of a suitable empirical estimator for CMMD is non-trivial and makes a meaningful contribution. The paper clearly differentiates itself from related work on conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and conditional VAEs (So et al., 2015) by leveraging the moment-matching approach rather than adversarial training or variational inference. The authors also thoughtfully reference relevant prior work, including conditional kernel embeddings (Song et al., 2009) and conditional GANs.

The significance of this work is strong. The CGMMN framework is straightforward to implement (as evidenced by Algorithm 1), and the authors successfully demonstrate its applicability across multiple domains. The Bayesian dark knowledge application is particularly interesting, showing that CGMMN can effectively distill knowledge from Bayesian models while maintaining predictive performance (Table 3). This could be valuable for practitioners seeking efficient, probabilistic prediction models.

The paper has no significant weaknesses that would prevent acceptance. The technical content is sound, the experiments are thorough and well-designed, and the writing is clear. The only minor limitation is that the authors don't explicitly compare to conditional versions of other moment-matching approaches (though this is likely because CGMMN is the first such approach).

Arguments for acceptance:
- Novel extension of GMMN to conditional generation with non-trivial derivation of empirical estimator
- Demonstrated competitive performance across multiple challenging tasks
- Well-written, clear presentation with helpful algorithm and appendix
- Practical, easy-to-implement framework with clear relevance to practitioners
- Addresses a gap in the literature for moment-matching approaches

Arguments against acceptance:
- None substantial; the paper makes a clear, meaningful contribution to the field

This paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep generative models by extending moment-matching networks to conditional distributions in a theoretically sound and practically useful way. The technical quality is high, the experimental evaluation is comprehensive, and the writing is excellent. The framework is easy to apply and demonstrates strong performance across multiple domains, making it relevant to both researchers and practitioners. I recommend acceptance at NIPS 2016.
