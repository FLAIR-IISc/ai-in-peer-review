## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of GMMNs to model conditional distributions using a conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The core idea is to avoid per-condition data sparsity by treating all conditionals as a family and directly matching the conditional embedding operator, a clever approach to leverage data more efficiently. The work is motivated by the need for conditional generative models in tasks like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation, and demonstrates promising results across these applications.

The proposed method builds upon established concepts like kernel embeddings and MMD, extending them to the conditional setting. The formulation of CMMD is clean and mathematically sound, and the authors correctly point out the computational advantages of having kernel matrices independent of model parameters, enabling standard backpropagation. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering classification, conditional generation, and a particularly interesting application in Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The results demonstrate competitive performance, suggesting the approach is viable and potentially advantageous.

However, the paper isn’t without weaknesses. The authors acknowledge the potential for large minibatch requirements due to CMMD, leading to expensive matrix inversions – this is a significant practical concern that warrants further discussion and potentially exploration of approximation techniques. While the advantage over a naive per-condition GMMN is clear, a comparison against this baseline, especially in low-cardinality discrete condition scenarios (like MNIST), would strengthen the empirical validation. 

Clarity is a concern in sections 2.3 and 3.1. The explanations of the conditional embedding operator and its relation to cross-covariance require consulting the referenced papers for full comprehension, hindering readability. Additionally, the statement regarding the MMD estimator being unbiased is incorrect, and there’s a minor inconsistency in the definition of the conditional embedding operator compared to reference [29].

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.
*   Clear theoretical formulation of CMMD.
*   Promising experimental results across diverse tasks.
*   Addresses a relevant problem with a potentially efficient solution.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Potential computational bottleneck with large minibatches.
*   Lack of comparison to a naive per-condition GMMN baseline.
*   Clarity issues in key sections.
*   Minor technical inaccuracies.



**Overall:** Despite the identified weaknesses, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling. The core idea is sound, the theoretical foundation is solid, and the experimental results are encouraging. Addressing the clarity issues and providing a more thorough discussion of the computational challenges would significantly strengthen the paper. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
