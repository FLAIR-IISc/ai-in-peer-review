- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Extends GMMN to learn conditional distributions, important for many real-world tasks.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written.  
- Main contributions: introduction of CMMD and a deep generative architecture for conditional distribution learning.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Experimental results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Strength: clear problem introduction and discussion of limitations of existing approaches.  
- Strength: CMMD is well-motivated and theoretically sound.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation across multiple tasks.  
- Weakness: mathematical exposition can be difficult for readers lacking kernel/Hilbert space background.  
- Weakness: experimental tasks are relatively small; scalability to larger problems is unclear.  
- Weakness: training involves computationally expensive steps; no runtime comparison (e.g., to DSN).  
- Weakness: lacks reference code, hindering reproducibility.  
- Weakness: model description needs more detail for exact replication by practitioners.  
- Overall: significant contribution with strengths outweighing weaknesses.  
- Pro-acceptance: novel approach addressing a significant problem, strong experimental results, clear writing.  
- Con-acceptance: accessibility concerns due to math, limited-scale experiments, and costly training.  
- Rating/recommendation: 8/10 — accept with minor revisions.
