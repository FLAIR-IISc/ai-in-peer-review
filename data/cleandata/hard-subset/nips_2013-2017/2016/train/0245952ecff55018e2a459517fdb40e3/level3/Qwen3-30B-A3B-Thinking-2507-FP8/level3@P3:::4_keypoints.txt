- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional generation via Conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Derives a CMMD objective that compares kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions; the empirical estimator derivation is non-trivial and key for practice.
- Strong theoretical foundations: Theorem 3 shows equality of conditional embedding operators implies equality of conditional distributions.
- Optimization can be done with backprop like GMMN; Algorithm 1 offers a concise practical implementation.
- Empirical results show competitive performance on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian dark knowledge (Boston housing).
- Experimental design is comprehensive with careful architecture/hyperparameter choices and a reproducible appendix.
- Writing is exceptionally clear; explains MMD–CMMD connections (Remark 1) and presents derivations logically.
- Novel contribution: meaningful extension of GMMN to conditional settings, distinguished from conditional GANs and VAEs and connected to conditional kernel embeddings.
- High practical significance: straightforward to implement and useful for tasks including Bayesian model distillation.
- No substantial weaknesses; minor omission is lack of explicit comparison to other conditional moment-matching approaches.
- Strong recommendation for acceptance (NIPS 2016).
