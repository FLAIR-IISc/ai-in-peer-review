This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extends the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to learn conditional distributions. The authors introduce a new loss function called Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) that measures the difference between the kernel mean embeddings of two conditional distributions. The CMMD loss is used to train a deep generative model that takes both the given conditional variables and an extra set of random variables as inputs.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method. The introduction provides a good background on the importance of conditional modeling and the limitations of existing methods. The authors also provide a thorough review of related work, including GMMN, Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN), and Variational Autoencoders (VAE).

The proposed method is technically sound, and the authors provide a theoretical justification for the CMMD loss function. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation. The results are comparable to state-of-the-art methods, and the authors provide a detailed analysis of the results.

However, there are some minor issues with the paper. The notation is sometimes confusing, and the authors use the symbol C to represent both the conditional embedding and the covariance matrix. The mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is not entirely clear, and the authors should provide more explanation on the dot notation. Additionally, Theorem 3 lacks a reference or proof, and the authors should provide more justification for this theorem.

The empirical results are decent, but the authors could provide more analysis on the performance of the proposed method. For example, they could provide more details on the hyperparameter tuning and the sensitivity of the results to the choice of hyperparameters. Additionally, the authors could provide more results on more challenging datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
