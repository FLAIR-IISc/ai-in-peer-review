This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a novel framework for learning conditional distributions. The authors extend the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to match conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS). The proposed approach is well-motivated and novel, as it provides a flexible way to represent conditional distributions.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method. The use of RKHS to embed conditional distributions is a key contribution of the paper. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its connection to Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) and its application to various tasks such as predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The experimental results are promising, and the authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on several real-world datasets. However, there are some concerns regarding the experimental setup and the comparison to other methods. Specifically, the authors do not provide a clear explanation of how they handled centering in the RKHS, which is a crucial step in the proposed method. Additionally, the comparison to other conditional generative methods, such as conditional GANs, is missing.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method. However, there are some typos and minor errors throughout the paper. The authors should proofread the paper carefully to eliminate these errors.

Regarding originality, the paper proposes a novel framework for learning conditional distributions, which is a significant contribution to the field. The use of RKHS to embed conditional distributions is a new idea, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method.

In terms of significance, the paper has the potential to impact the field of machine learning and artificial intelligence. The proposed method can be applied to various tasks, such as predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge, which are important problems in the field.
