- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) for learning conditional distributions.
- Proposes a novel training objective based on Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (cMMD), extending MMD to the conditional setting.
- Frames learning as minimizing kernel mean embedding discrepancy, avoiding adversarial min–max optimization.
- Demonstrates CGMMN on tasks including discriminative classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.
- Original and significant contribution, filling a gap by extending moment-matching models to conditional problems.
- The cMMD objective is an interesting theoretical development.
- Empirical results are competitive against strong baselines on standard (though small) benchmarks.
- Mathematical exposition of cMMD can be difficult to follow, harming clarity.
- Reproducibility is weak due to missing architectural details and hyperparameter settings; reference code and appendices are recommended.
- Training appears computationally expensive (kernel matrix inversions); a complexity and runtime comparison is needed.
- Evaluation lacks demonstrations of scalability to larger, more complex datasets.
