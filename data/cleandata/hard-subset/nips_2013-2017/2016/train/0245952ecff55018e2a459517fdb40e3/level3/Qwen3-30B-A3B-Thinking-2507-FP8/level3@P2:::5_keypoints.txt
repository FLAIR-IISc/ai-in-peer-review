- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion to learn conditional distributions via kernel embeddings.  
- CGMMN uses a deep network to transform noise and conditional variables into samples, contrasting with conditional GANs/VAEs by using MMD instead of adversarial training.  
- Theoretical grounding is solid but has a critical omission: the empirical conditional operator definition omits essential centering terms (estimators of μ_X and μ_Y).  
- The omission of centering raises questions about gradient computation and the validity of the training procedure unless clarified.  
- Experimental results are weak: CGMMN underperforms state-of-the-art baselines (e.g., worse than CMMVA on MNIST/SVHN).  
- Generative performance lacks convincing comparisons to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), undermining claims of competitiveness.  
- Presentation issues: several typos and mathematically dense/unclear exposition of the CMMD objective.  
- Experimental setup lacks sufficient detail for reproducibility, especially regarding how centering was handled.  
- Originality: novel and meaningful extension of GMMN to conditional distributions with appropriate relation to prior work.  
- Significance: potential applications are notable (predictive modeling, contextual generation, distillation) but not empirically demonstrated.  
- Strengths summarized: novel idea, clear theoretical motivation, simple generative process, broad applicability.  
- Weaknesses summarized: missing centering in theory, inferior empirical performance, no key baseline comparisons, typos and unclear math, unsupported performance claims.  
- For acceptance: novelty and potential impact argue positively if issues are addressed.  
- Against acceptance: technical omission and poor empirical results currently undermine validity and claims.  
- Recommendation: reject as-is; recommend revision to fix centering issue and provide stronger empirical comparisons before reconsideration.
