- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMN to learn conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (cMMD) criterion.  
- Develops theoretical foundation for cMMD using kernel/Hilbert space embeddings and links it to standard MMD.  
- Implements CGMMN with a practical back-propagation training procedure, avoiding GAN-style min-max optimization.  
- Demonstrates the method on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge (Boston housing).  
- Shows competitive empirical performance, with particularly strong contextual generation conditioned on labels.  
- Highlights conceptual simplicity and elegance compared to conditional GANs while retaining deep learning benefits.  
- Notes mathematical exposition is dense and can be challenging due to kernel embedding theory.  
- Experimental scope is limited to small benchmark datasets, leaving scalability to complex tasks unproven.  
- Raises computational concerns (matrix inverses) and lacks runtime/scalability comparisons with strong baselines like DSN.  
- Reproducibility is insufficient: missing implementation details for conditional embedding operator, hyperparameters, and reference code.  
- Overall assessment: meaningful, theoretically solid contribution; recommends acceptance contingent on addressing reproducibility (provide code and implementation details).
