## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper extends Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting, introducing Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN). The core idea is to leverage the appealing properties of MMD-based generative models – simpler training compared to GANs – to learn conditional distributions. This is a valuable extension, as many applications require modeling *p(y|x)* rather than the joint distribution *p(x,y)*. The authors demonstrate the method’s applicability in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, reporting competitive performance on several datasets.

The paper builds upon prior work in deep generative modeling, particularly MMD-based approaches like GMMN and GANs. It correctly identifies the challenges of extending MMD to the conditional case, where distributions vary across different conditional inputs. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge is a particularly interesting use case, showcasing a practical benefit beyond standard generative tasks.

However, the paper suffers from a significant lack of intuitive explanation and clarity, particularly surrounding the mathematical formulations. While the authors present a theoretically-grounded approach, the jump from the stated objectives to the practical implementation feels large and insufficiently detailed. The introduction of Equation (2), the optimized CMMD objective, is particularly problematic. It’s presented without sufficient derivation, explanation of assumptions, or discussion of its practical implications. The connection to the actual algorithm implemented is murky; the supplemental material is mentioned, but is reportedly hard to follow, which is a serious issue. 

The authors successfully identify the core difficulty of matching distributions over mini-batches for varying conditional inputs, but fail to provide a clear intuition for *how* the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) tackles this challenge. The non-conditional GMMN has a relatively straightforward explanation; the conditional case needs an equally accessible presentation. 

**Specifically, I request the following clarifications:**

1.  **An intuitive derivation of Equation (2):**  What steps are taken to arrive at this formula from the initial CMMD objective? What approximations or assumptions are made?
2.  **A clear, step-by-step mapping from Equation (2) to the implemented algorithm:**  How are the terms in Equation (2) actually computed in practice, given the mini-batch setup?  What are the computational complexities of each step?

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Extends a promising generative modeling technique (GMMN) to a practically relevant conditional setting.
*   Demonstrates competitive performance across diverse tasks.
*   Addresses a non-trivial problem in matching distributions across different conditionals.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity and intuitive explanation of the core mathematical concepts, particularly Equation (2).
*   Insufficient detail regarding the practical implementation of the algorithm.
*   The connection between theory and practice is insufficiently clear.



**Overall:**  While the research has potential, the current presentation significantly hinders understanding and reproducibility. I lean towards **rejection** with encouragement to revise and address the requested clarifications.  A more accessible presentation, particularly around the derivation of Equation (2) and the algorithm mapping, is crucial for the paper to be considered a strong contribution.
