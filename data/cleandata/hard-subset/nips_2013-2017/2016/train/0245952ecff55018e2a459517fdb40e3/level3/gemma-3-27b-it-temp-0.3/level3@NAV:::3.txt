## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to learn conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors demonstrate the approach on predictive modeling (classification), contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation, showcasing its versatility. The work builds upon recent advances in generative modeling using MMD and addresses the limitations of GMMN by enabling conditional generation.

The core contribution lies in formulating the CMMD objective and demonstrating its feasibility for training via backpropagation. The authors appropriately position their work within the landscape of generative models, referencing GANs, GMMNs, and variational autoencoders. The experimental evaluation is reasonably comprehensive, covering diverse tasks and datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face). The results are competitive with existing methods, particularly GMMN, and the Bayesian distillation application is a novel and interesting use case. The authors have clearly addressed concerns from previous reviews regarding low-dimensional latent spaces, scalability, and utility, providing a more thorough empirical evaluation.

However, several points require attention. The mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form lacks sufficient clarity. A more detailed explanation of the derivation would be beneficial. The overloading of the symbol ‘C’ (representing both conditional embedding and elsewhere) is confusing and should be rectified. Theorem 3 lacks a citation or proof, which weakens the theoretical foundation of the work. While the empirical results are decent, the Bayesian distillation experiment is limited to a single dataset/model, hindering a conclusive assessment of its generalizability. 

The strength of this approach likely lies in pure generative modeling, and while the supervised learning application demonstrates versatility, it isn’t the most compelling use case. The datasets used are adequate for demonstrating the core idea, but wider adoption will necessitate evaluation on more challenging benchmarks like CIFAR-10 or LSUN. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of GMMN to conditional distributions.
*   Sound theoretical foundation (with the caveat regarding Theorem 3).
*   Competitive empirical performance on multiple tasks.
*   Addresses previous reviewer concerns.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity in the derivation of Equation (2).
*   Symbol overloading (C).
*   Missing proof/citation for Theorem 3.
*   Limited scope of the Bayesian distillation experiment.



**Overall:** This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling. While some clarifications and additions are needed, the work is technically sound, well-motivated, and demonstrates promising results. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
