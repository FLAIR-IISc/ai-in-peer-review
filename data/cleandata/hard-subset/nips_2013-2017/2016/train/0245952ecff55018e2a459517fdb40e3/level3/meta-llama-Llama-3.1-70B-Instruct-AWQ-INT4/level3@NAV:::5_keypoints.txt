- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via RKHS embeddings.  
- Introduces Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as the training objective comparing kernel mean embeddings of empirical conditionals and the generative model.  
- Paper is well-motivated, novel, and clearly written with a good balance of math and intuition.  
- Experimental predictive performance is disappointing: higher error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Demonstrates competitive generative performance (image generation) and success in Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Major technical concern: empirical conditional operator definition omits μ_X and μ_Y terms and assumes RKHS centering without justification, raising questions about gradient computation and implementation.  
- Authors should clarify centering in RKHS, how gradients were computed, and provide implementation details.  
- Minor issues: typos (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal") and a few awkward phrasings.  
- Lacks comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs).  
- Summary ratings: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, clear exposition, competitive generative results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weaker predictive results, unclear technical details, minor editorial and comparison gaps.
