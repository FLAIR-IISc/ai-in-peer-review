- Proposes Conditional GMMNs (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional distributions via a new Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.  
- CMMD measures discrepancy between conditional distributions using the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between kernel mean embedding operators.  
- CGMMN architecture: deep network taking conditional variable x and noise h to generate y, trained by minimizing CMMD with backprop.  
- Demonstrated across tasks: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (labeled MNIST, Yale faces), and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Theoretical motivation is sound and grounded in kernel conditional embedding literature.  
- Experimental results are diverse and competitive, especially for prediction tasks (on par with max-margin DGMs/CMMVA).  
- Mathematical exposition (Section 3.1) is dense; the derivation from operator norm to empirical estimator (Eq.2) needs clearer, more intuitive explanation.  
- Reproducibility is weak: missing key hyperparameters (kernels, bandwidths, λ), learning rates, and full architectural details; source code should be released.  
- Computational cost concern: per-mini-batch kernel matrix inversions scale cubically with batch size; scalability and runtimes are not discussed.  
- Originality: novel and well-motivated combination of GMMNs with kernel conditional embedding, offering a non-adversarial conditional generative alternative.  
- Significance: meaningful contribution to conditional density estimation with potential broad impact and interesting applications like knowledge distillation.  
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, significance, versatility across tasks, and relevance to the field.  
- Cons for acceptance: scalability/computational issues, reproducibility gaps, and experiments limited to relatively small datasets.  
- Recommendation: accept, contingent on addressing computational complexity and reproducibility (ideally by releasing code).
