- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.
- Leverages kernel mean embeddings to avoid explicit distributional assumptions, a core strength carried over from GMMN.
- Extends moment-matching to the conditional setting (CMMD), a natural and valuable theoretical contribution.
- Training via backpropagation simplifies implementation and enables scaling to larger datasets.
- Positions CGMMN relative to other deep generative models (GANs, VAEs), clarifying connections and distinctions.
- Demonstrates applicability across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Empirical evaluation on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face shows the method’s versatility, though not dominant performance.
- Bayesian dark knowledge distillation application is interesting and suggests efficient knowledge transfer from Bayesian models.
- Derivation and explanation of the CMMD objective (notably the step from Eq. (2) to weighted MMD) are unclear and need more detail.
- Dot notation is ambiguous (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product) and requires clarification.
- Symbol ‘C’ is overloaded, representing multiple entities (conditional embedding operator and others), causing confusion.
- Theorem 3’s claim about operator equality implying conditional distribution equality lacks citation or proof and needs justification.
- Empirical results are competitive but do not substantially outperform state-of-the-art methods.
- Comparisons across models use different architectures, making direct performance comparisons difficult.
- Generation experiments lack standard quantitative metrics (e.g., Inception Score, FID) for rigorous evaluation.
- Bayesian distillation experiment is limited to one dataset and a single teacher (PBP), limiting conclusions about generality.
- Originality: builds on established kernel/MMD methods but contributes a novel conditional generative modeling approach (CMMD).
- Significance: offers a potentially simpler, more stable alternative to conditional GANs with attractive theoretical and training properties.
- Key revision requests: clarify CMMD derivation, disambiguate notation, provide a citation/proof for Theorem 3, add quantitative generation metrics, and broaden Bayesian distillation experiments.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution worthy of acceptance pending revisions to address clarity, theoretical justification, and quantitative evaluation.
