- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings and RKHS theory (Song et al., 2009), providing a sound theoretical foundation.
- Trains conditional models via backpropagation, maintaining the simplicity of GMMNs.
- Applies CGMMN to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Main technical concern: empirical conditional operator definition omits centering terms (estimators of μ_X and μ_Y), which is not adequately justified.
- Omission of centering raises questions about gradient correctness and potential biases in implementation and experiments.
- Experimental predictive performance on MNIST and SVHN is worse than state-of-the-art (e.g., CMMVA), so CGMMN is competitive but not superior.
- Generative evaluation is weak: lacks comparisons to conditional GANs and conditional VAEs, and nearest-neighbor checks are an inadequate robustness metric.
- Authors later responded explaining minimal practical impact of omitted centering and provided additional experimental comparisons.
- After rebuttal and added comparisons (including conditional GANs), reviewer revised judgement to weak accept.
- Strengths: novel conditional extension, solid theoretical grounding, broad applicability, and simplicity of training.
- Weaknesses: missing centering clarification, underwhelming empirical results, weak generative baselines, and minor typographical errors.
- Overall: valuable core contribution but initially flawed by clarity and empirical gaps; adequately addressed major concerns after author response, leading to Weak Accept.
