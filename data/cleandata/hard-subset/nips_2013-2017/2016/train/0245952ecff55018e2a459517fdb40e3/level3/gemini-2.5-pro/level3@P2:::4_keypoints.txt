- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions p(y|x).
- Main technical contribution: a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective comparing conditional mean embedding operators in RKHS.
- Extends GMMN/MMD framework to conditional setting, enabling training with standard backpropagation.
- Theoretical foundation builds on kernel mean embeddings and conditional embedding literature (e.g., Song et al., 2009).
- Demonstrated empirically on prediction (MNIST, SVHN), contextual image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian model distillation.
- Provides a moment-matching alternative to Conditional GANs and Conditional VAEs, avoiding adversarial min-max training.
- Derivation of CMMD and its empirical estimator appears sound and logically grounded in prior work.
- Comprehensive experiments and checks (e.g., verifying model doesn't copy training data) support the claims.
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and includes helpful background and appendix details.
- Model architecture, training algorithm, and implementation are simple and reproducible.
- Novelty lies in the practical, kernel-based empirical estimator for conditional operator discrepancy and its application to deep conditional generative modeling.
- Distilling knowledge from Bayesian models into a CGMMN student network is a novel and interesting application.
- Significant because it broadens moment-matching approaches to many conditional modeling problems and offers potentially more stable training than adversarial methods.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong theoretical contribution, practical/versatile framework, clear alternative to GANs, and high clarity/completeness.
- Argument against acceptance: generated image quality may lag state-of-the-art GANs, though authors acknowledge this and propose combining CGMMN with an autoencoder as mitigation.
