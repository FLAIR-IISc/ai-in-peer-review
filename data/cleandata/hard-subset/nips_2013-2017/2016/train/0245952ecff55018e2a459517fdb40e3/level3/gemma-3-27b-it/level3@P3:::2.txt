This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to conditional distributions. The core idea is to learn a conditional distribution p(y|x) by minimizing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. While the extension from unconditional to conditional generation is a natural and valuable direction, the paper suffers from a significant lack of clarity regarding the derivation and implementation details of the proposed CMMD objective and associated algorithm.

The paper builds upon recent advances in deep generative modeling, particularly GMMNs [19] and GANs [5], positioning itself as an alternative with a simpler optimization objective than GANs. The motivation for focusing on conditional distributions is well-articulated, highlighting applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and building larger generative models. This contextualization within the broader field of generative modeling is a strength. The extension to conditional distributions is also a relevant contribution, addressing a crucial limitation of the original GMMN approach. 

However, the paper’s strengths are overshadowed by critical weaknesses in clarity and implementation details. The authors begin with a heavy mathematical formulation without adequately motivating it with intuitive explanations. This is a significant flaw, as the conditional objective, and particularly Equation (2), remains opaque. The derivation of this equation is entirely missing – there's no explanation of the assumptions made or the mathematical steps involved in arriving at this form. The connection to the underlying kernel methods and the Hilbert space embedding is not adequately illuminated for readers less familiar with these concepts.

More critically, the paper fails to clearly articulate *how* the proposed algorithm is actually implemented. The jump from Equation (2) to the practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) is substantial and lacks sufficient detail. The supplemental material is described as difficult to follow, exacerbating this issue. A step-by-step explanation of the computational process, including specific choices made in approximating the integrals and handling the kernel matrices, is essential for reproducibility and understanding. The mini-batch optimization mentioned is welcome, but its impact on the CMMD estimation and resulting bias isn't discussed.

The non-conditional GMMN framework has a relatively intuitive explanation (sampling from a simple distribution and transforming it with a deep network). The conditional case demands an equally accessible intuition, but this is entirely missing. The authors don’t adequately explain *why* the proposed conditional approach should work or how it differs in practice from simply training separate GMMNs for each conditional input.  

Specifically, two concrete clarifications are needed: 1) an intuitive derivation of Equation (2), explaining the mathematical reasoning behind it and its connection to the conditional kernel mean embedding; and 2) a clear, step-by-step mapping from Equation (2) to the practical algorithm, detailing how the kernel matrices are computed and how the optimization is performed in practice.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses an important problem: extending GMMNs to the conditional setting.
*   Presents a novel objective function (CMMD) for conditional generation.
*   Demonstrates competitive performance across various tasks.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity: The mathematical formulation is presented without sufficient intuition or explanation.
*   Implementation details are insufficient: The connection between Equation (2) and the algorithm is unclear.
*   Missing derivation: The derivation of Equation (2) is absent, making it difficult to assess its validity and understand its implications.
*   Reproducibility concerns: Insufficient detail hinders reproducibility.

**Quality:** The paper’s technical soundness is questionable due to the lack of clarity in derivation and implementation. The experimental results suggest potential, but the lack of details makes it difficult to assess the robustness of the findings. It feels incomplete.

**Clarity:** The paper is poorly written and organized. The heavy mathematical notation without sufficient explanation makes it difficult to follow.

**Originality:** The extension to conditional distributions is novel, but the lack of clarity regarding the method diminishes its impact.

**Significance:** The potential significance of the work is hampered by the current lack of clarity. If the method could be implemented and understood more easily, it could be a valuable contribution to the field.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **rejection**. While the problem addressed is relevant and the experimental results are promising, the significant lack of clarity and missing implementation details make it impossible to assess the true value of the work. The authors need to provide a more intuitive explanation of the CMMD objective, a detailed derivation of Equation (2), and a clear step-by-step description of the algorithm. Addressing these issues would significantly improve the paper’s quality and potential impact.
