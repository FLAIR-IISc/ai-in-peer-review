This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to model conditional distributions. The authors build on the work of Song et al. (2009) and embed conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The paper is well-motivated, and the approach is novel.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background and related work, including deep generative models, maximum mean discrepancy (MMD), and kernel embedding of conditional distributions. They also provide a thorough explanation of the proposed CGMMN model, including the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, the deep generative architecture, and the learning algorithm.

The experimental results are presented on several real datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face. The authors evaluate the performance of CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge tasks. While the results are not always competitive with state-of-the-art methods, they demonstrate the potential of CGMMN in various applications.

However, there are some concerns and limitations with the paper. The definition of the empirical conditional operator \hat{C}_{Y|X} omits the terms involving estimators of μ_X and μ_Y, and centering in RKHS is assumed but not justified. This raises questions about gradient computation and how experiments handled centering. The authors should clarify this point to ensure the correctness and reproducibility of their results.

Additionally, the experimental results are somewhat disappointing, with CGMMN having higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on both MNIST and SVHN datasets. The generative performance is also unclear, as no comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs) are provided. The authors should consider including more comprehensive experiments to demonstrate the strengths and weaknesses of CGMMN.

Finally, there are some minor issues with the paper, including typos (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal") and formatting issues.
