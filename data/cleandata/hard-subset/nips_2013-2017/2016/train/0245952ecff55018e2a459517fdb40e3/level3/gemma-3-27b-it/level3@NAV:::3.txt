## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors demonstrate the utility of CGMMN across various tasks, including predictive modeling (classification), contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation. The core idea – leveraging kernel embeddings and MMD for conditional generative modeling – is a natural and logical extension of prior work in the field. This builds directly on the success of GMMN and offers a potentially simpler alternative to GAN-based conditional generation methods.

The paper demonstrates a solid understanding of related work, appropriately citing key contributions in generative modeling, MMD, and conditional distributions. The revisions appear to address concerns raised by previous reviewers regarding low-dimensional latent space, scalability, and the model’s utility. The empirical evaluation is comprehensive, showcasing the model’s performance on MNIST and Yale Face datasets. The application to Bayesian distillation, while limited to a single dataset, presents an interesting use case. 

However, several points require attention. The mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form lacks clarity. The authors need to explicitly explain how the weighting is achieved and its implications. The overloading of the symbol 'C', representing both the conditional embedding and other variables, is a significant source of confusion and should be remedied. Furthermore, Theorem 3 lacks a citation or proof, a critical oversight for a theoretical claim. While the results are decent and comparable to GMMN, they aren’t significantly better, suggesting the benefit of the conditional extension needs further investigation. 

The model’s performance in supervised learning (classification) is not a strong demonstration of its capabilities, though acceptable. The Bayesian distillation result, while interesting, requires evaluation on a broader range of datasets and models to establish its generalizability. The authors acknowledge that wider adoption necessitates stronger results on more challenging datasets like CIFAR-10 and LSUN, which is a valid point.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of GMMN to conditional distributions.
*   Clear theoretical grounding in kernel methods and MMD.
*   Comprehensive empirical evaluation across diverse tasks.
*   Addresses concerns from previous reviews.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of clarity in the derivation of Equation (2).
*   Symbol overloading creates confusion.
*   Theorem 3 lacks proof/reference.
*   Incremental improvement over GMMN.
*   Limited evaluation of Bayesian distillation.



**Overall:** I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. Addressing the clarity issues regarding Equation (2), the symbol overloading, and providing a proof or reference for Theorem 3 are crucial. While the results are not groundbreaking, the paper presents a valid contribution to the field, particularly in the context of moment-matching approaches to generative modeling.
