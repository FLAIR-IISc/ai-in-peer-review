- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions using RKHS embeddings and CMMD.  
- Well-motivated and novel extension of prior work.  
- Provides clear, concise background on deep generative models, MMD, and kernel conditional embeddings.  
- Thorough explanation of CGMMN model, CMMD criterion, deep architecture, and learning algorithm.  
- Experiments on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face covering predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Results show potential but are not consistently competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- CGMMN yields higher predictive error than CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Generative performance is unclear due to absence of comparisons with other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs).  
- Definition of empirical conditional operator \hat{C}_{Y|X} omits terms involving estimators of μ_X and μ_Y and assumes RKHS centering without justification.  
- Omission raises questions about gradient computation and how centering was handled in experiments, affecting correctness and reproducibility.  
- Paper needs more comprehensive experiments and comparisons to demonstrate strengths and weaknesses.  
- Minor issues: typos and formatting errors (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal").  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-explained method with potential applications.  
- Arguments against acceptance: weak experimental performance, omitted operator terms, and insufficient empirical validation.
