- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional generation.
- Proposes the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) to measure differences between kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.
- CMMD objective is optimized with stochastic gradient descent for efficient CGMMN training.
- Provides a thorough theoretical derivation of CMMD and its relation to MMD.
- Discusses connections and key differences between CMMD and standard MMD.
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with a helpful appendix for extra details.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and shows competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation.
- Compares CGMMN to several strong baselines (VAEs, MMVA, DSN) and reports convincing results.
- Demonstrates CGMMN’s flexibility and applicability across tasks and its compatibility with models like autoencoders.
- Presents a novel kernel-embedding approach to conditional generation with promising impact on deep generative models.
- For acceptance: novel/effective approach, strong theory, comprehensive experiments, and clear presentation.
- Against acceptance: assumes substantial background knowledge, lacks exhaustive evaluation on more tasks/datasets, and omits detailed comparisons to alternatives like conditional GANs.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
