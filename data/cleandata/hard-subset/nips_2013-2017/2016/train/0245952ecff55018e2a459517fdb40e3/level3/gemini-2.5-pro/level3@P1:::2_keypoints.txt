- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions p(y|x) via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- Core technical contribution: development of CMMD by comparing conditional kernel mean embedding operators in a tensor product Hilbert space.  
- Simple generative process: deep neural network takes conditioning x and noise h to produce samples, trained end-to-end with backprop.  
- Demonstrated versatility across tasks: classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Empirical results are strong and competitive on MNIST and SVHN, produce plausible conditional images, and successfully distill Bayesian models.  
- Strength: original and significant extension of moment-matching to conditional density estimation, broadening applicability of non-adversarial deep conditional generators.  
- Strength: broad applicability shown by experiments on three distinct problem domains.  
- Strength: competitive empirical performance indicating practical effectiveness of CMMD.  
- Main weakness: lack of intuition for CMMD — paper presents heavy formalism without an accessible explanation linking it to standard MMD intuition.  
- Main weakness: opaque derivation of the practical objective (Equation (2)); missing explanation linking Hilbert–Schmidt norm of operator differences to the trace/Gram-matrix form.  
- Main weakness: unclear how Equation (2)’s terms are constructed from mini-batches of real and generated data, impeding understanding of actual computations.  
- Main weakness: missing computational details regarding matrix inversions (e.g., (Kd+λI)⁻¹), their O(N^3) cost, and how this is handled in practice.  
- Requested clarifications: provide an intuitive derivation from E_x[MMD^2(p(y|x),q(y|x))] to Equation (2).  
- Requested clarifications: give explicit mini-batch algorithm/pseudocode — how generated samples are paired with real x, exact construction of Gram matrices (Kd, Ks, Ld, Ls, Ksd, Lds), and how gradients backpropagate through trace/inversion operations.  
- Overall: promising and impactful idea but currently hindered by insufficient clarity and missing practical/reproducibility details.
