- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) to learn conditional distributions.  
- CGMMN generates samples by feeding noise and conditional variables through a deep network and matching kernel mean embeddings to empirical conditionals.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on classification, contextual generation (digits/faces), and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation with competitive results.  
- Theoretical foundation is solid with Theorems 1–2 supported by prior work; overall derivation of CMMD is generally correct.  
- Notation issues: ambiguous dot operator (matrix vs. Hadamard) and overloaded symbol "C" hinder reproducibility.  
- Theorem 3 is stated without reference or proof, needing citation or justification.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough for the chosen tasks but limited in scope—only simple datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face) and a single dataset (Boston housing) for Bayesian distillation.  
- Mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is confusing and the notation should be clarified.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; connection between conditional generative process and CMMD is well explained.  
- CGMMN is a meaningful and novel extension of GMMN to conditional settings, distinct from conditional GANs and VAEs.  
- Using CMMD offers a simpler, theoretically grounded alternative to adversarial training for conditional generation.  
- Results show CGMMN is competitive and potentially more practitioner-friendly, but broader applicability is unclear due to limited dataset difficulty and comparisons.  
- Arguments for acceptance: principled extension, solid theory, versatile experiments, and simpler training than GANs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: technical clarity issues in Eq.(2), limited challenging-dataset evaluation (e.g., CIFAR-10), and narrow Bayesian distillation testing.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify Equation (2), resolve notation overload, and strengthen empirical claims.
