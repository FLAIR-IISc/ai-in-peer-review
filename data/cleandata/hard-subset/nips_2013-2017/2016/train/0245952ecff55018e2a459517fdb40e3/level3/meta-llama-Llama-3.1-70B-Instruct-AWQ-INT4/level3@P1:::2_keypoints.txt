- Proposes CGMMN: extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Provides a rigorous mathematical formulation of the CMMD measuring differences between kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Introduces a deep generative architecture and a training algorithm for CGMMN using SGD and back-propagation.  
- Evaluates on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face showing competitive performance in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Demonstrates applicability of CGMMN to Bayesian dark knowledge as an interesting application.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical contribution in formulating CMMD.  
- Strength: practical deep architecture with simple training procedure.  
- Weakness: paper is heavily mathematical and lacks intuitive explanations of the conditional objective and algorithm.  
- Weakness: does not clearly state the actual computations performed or discuss computational efficiency.  
- Weakness: derivation of Equation (2) and the path to the implemented algorithm are insufficiently detailed.  
- Originality: novel extension of GMMN to conditional distributions using CMMD.  
- Significance: competitive empirical results suggest broad applicability and impact.  
- Quality: well-written and organized but needs more intuition and implementation detail.  
- Clarity: overly mathematical; requires clearer, more accessible explanations and computational specifics.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel framework, rigorous formulation, and strong experiments.  
- Arguments for rejection: lack of intuition, missing computational/algorithmic details, and incomplete derivations.
