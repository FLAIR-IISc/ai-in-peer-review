The paper "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks" proposes a novel approach to deep generative modeling using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors extend the capability of generative moment-matching networks (GMMN) to learn conditional distributions, which is a crucial aspect in many real-world applications. The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments.

The key contributions of the paper are the introduction of the CMMD criterion and the development of a deep generative architecture that can learn conditional distributions using this criterion. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on a range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The results show that their method is competitive with state-of-the-art approaches in these areas.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of conditional generative modeling and the limitations of existing approaches.
* The proposed CMMD criterion is well-motivated and theoretically sound, providing a novel solution to the problem of learning conditional distributions.
* The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on a range of tasks.

However, there are also some weaknesses and areas for improvement:

* The mathematical exposition can be hard to follow at times, particularly for readers without a strong background in kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings.
* The experimental tasks are somewhat small compared to recent work in the area, and it would be beneficial to see the authors' approach applied to more challenging problems.
* The training process involves computationally expensive steps, and it would be useful to provide a runtime comparison with other methods, such as DSN.
* To strengthen the paper, it would be beneficial to provide reference code for the proposed approach, allowing other researchers to reproduce the results.
* The model description could be improved to provide more detail for practitioners to exactly reproduce the evaluated models.

Overall, the paper is a significant contribution to the field of deep generative modeling and provides a novel solution to the problem of learning conditional distributions. While there are some areas for improvement, the strengths of the paper outweigh its weaknesses, and it is a valuable addition to the literature.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to deep generative modeling that addresses a significant problem in the field.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on a range of tasks.
* The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The mathematical exposition can be hard to follow at times, which may limit the paper's accessibility to some readers.
* The experimental tasks are somewhat small compared to recent work in the area, which may raise questions about the scalability of the proposed approach.
