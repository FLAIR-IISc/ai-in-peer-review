- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMNs to model P(Y|X) using conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD).
- Defines CMMD as the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional embedding operators and derives a practical empirical estimator using Gram matrices and regularization.
- Trains a conditional deep generator y = f(x,h|w) by back-propagating the CMMD loss with a mini-batch algorithm to mitigate cubic kernel costs.
- Positions work at the intersection of kernel distribution methods and deep generative models, related to GMMN, cGAN, and CVAE.
- Demonstrates applications: class-conditional prediction on MNIST/SVHN, contextual generation on MNIST/Yale (including autoencoder code-space), and Bayesian neural network distillation on Boston housing.
- CMMD objective is well motivated by conditional kernel embedding theory and has a well-defined finite-sample estimator under standard assumptions.
- Stochastic gradient optimization is straightforward; kernel inverses depend only on X so X-kernel need not be differentiable.
- Empirical results are competitive and the distillation experiment preserves predictive accuracy, but the experimental scope is modest.
- Comparisons to strong conditional generative baselines (cGAN, CVAE) are limited and image evaluation is primarily qualitative.
- Kernel Gram matrices introduce O(B^3) per-batch costs for inverses, potentially limiting scalability for large batches.
- Discussion on kernel choice, bandwidth selection, and sensitivity analyses is limited and would strengthen the work.
- Paper is generally clear and well organized, with a strong derivation and helpful appendix.
- Suggested clarifications: per-mini-batch time/memory complexity, stable inverse tricks and λ selection, kernel/hyperparameter strategies, and ablations for x/h combination methods.
- The extension from MMD/GMMN to CMMD/CGMMN is natural but non-trivial; operator-based CMMD and empirical estimator are useful contributions.
- CGMMN’s simplicity (no adversarial min–max or variational bound) and generality make it attractive and broadly useful for conditional synthesis and distillation.
- Pros: principled derivation, simple stable training, versatile applications, competitive results, and good writing with detailed appendix.
- Cons: limited experimental breadth and baselines, mostly qualitative generative evaluation, computational overhead from kernel inverses, few ablations, and sparse practical guidance on when CMMD helps most.
- Reviewer suggestions: add comparisons to cGAN/CVAE, provide runtime/memory benchmarks, ablate kernel types/bandwidth/λ, and evaluate uncertainty calibration (ECE/NLL) for predictions and distillation.
- Recommendation: leaning accept, contingent on stronger empirical comparisons and analysis.
