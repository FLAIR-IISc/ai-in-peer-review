**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting. The authors aim to learn a conditional distribution given some input variables using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The CMMD measures the difference between the kernel mean embedding of an empirical conditional distribution and that of the generative model. The authors develop a deep generative architecture and a learning algorithm to optimize the CMMD objective using stochastic gradient descent with back-propagation.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on GMMN, which uses maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) to learn deep generative models. The authors extend this work to the conditional setting, which is a non-trivial extension. The paper also relates to other work on conditional generative models, such as conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and conditional Variational Autoencoders (VAEs).
