- Contribution: Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective to learn p(y|x) by matching conditional mean embedding operators in RKHS.  
- Method: Extends GMMN to conditional setting, trains deep generator conditioning on x and noise via SGD/backprop.  
- Demonstrated tasks: Classification, conditional image generation, and knowledge distillation from Bayesian models.  
- Strength: Well-motivated and original extension of moment-matching networks to conditional problems, broadening applicability.  
- Strength: Conceptually simple and elegant combination of a simple conditional generator with a CMMD objective (non-adversarial).  
- Major weakness — technical soundness: The empirical estimator Ĉ_{Y|X}=Φ(K+λI)⁻¹Υᵀ appears uncentered despite the paper defining centered cross-covariance, and centering omission is unjustified.  
- Critical unanswered questions: Is Eq.(2)’s L̂²_CMMD correct without centering? If centering was used in experiments, how was it implemented (especially in mini-batches)? If not, what is the justification and impact on CMMD as a discrepancy?  
- Experimental weakness — predictive: CGMMN-CNN is outperformed by CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN, so it does not clearly advance predictive SOTA.  
- Experimental weakness — generative: Generative evaluation is only qualitative with no quantitative comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., conditional GANs or VAEs), limiting assessment of generative quality.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally readable but contains distracting typos and grammatical errors that need proofreading.  
- Acceptance argument: Novel, well-motivated method offering a potentially more stable, non-adversarial alternative for conditional generation.  
- Rejection argument: Potentially serious technical flaw regarding missing centering and weak empirical comparisons undermine confidence and practical significance.  
- Summary: Idea is appealing but manuscript is weakened by unclear/possibly incorrect CMMD formulation and insufficient experimental validation; authors must clarify centering and strengthen experiments.
