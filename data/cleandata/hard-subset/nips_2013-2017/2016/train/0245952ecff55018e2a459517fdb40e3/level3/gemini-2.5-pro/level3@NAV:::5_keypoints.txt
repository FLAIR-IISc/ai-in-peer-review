- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions p(y|x).
- Core contribution: Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) measuring distance between conditional distributions via Hilbert–Schmidt norm of conditional mean embedding operators in an RKHS.
- Builds on prior theoretical work (Song et al. 2009) and generative moment-matching frameworks.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated, and elegant extension providing a non-adversarial method for training conditional deep generative models with applications in contextual generation and prediction.
- Major technical concern: empirical conditional operator Ĉ_Y|X appears to omit centering terms (μ_X, μ_Y), assuming centered RKHS data without justification and calling CMMD estimator and gradient correctness into question.
- Empirical results not compelling: higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN, weakening the "competitive performance" claim.
- Generative evaluation insufficient: lacks comparisons to standard conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), making practical utility unclear.
- Presentation issues: several typographical errors that detract from clarity (e.g., "Frobeniu norm," "an universal").
- Update: authors’ rebuttal satisfactorily addressed the centering issue with implementation details and theoretical justification, leading the reviewer to raise scores and recommend acceptance.
