This paper extends GMMNs to the conditional setting by introducing Conditional GMMNs (CGMMN) trained with a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective that matches the conditional embedding operator directly. The idea relates to prior work on GMMNs and conditional mean embeddings (Song et al., 2009; Fukumizu et al.), and offers an alternative to conditional GANs and CVAEs with a simpler, non-adversarial loss. The method is evaluated on classification, conditional image generation, and distilling Bayesian predictors.

Quality: The core technical contribution—defining CMMD as the Hilbert–Schmidt distance between estimated conditional embedding operators and showing it can be computed with Gram matrices and standard backprop—is sound and well-motivated. The observation that K matrices are independent of model parameters is practical and allows flexible kernels on x. Results on MNIST/SVHN classification, conditional generation on MNIST/Yale, and Bayesian dark knowledge (Boston) are promising. However, the paper does not compare against the most natural baseline: a “naive” per-conditional GMMN with shared parameters. While such a baseline should suffer with continuous x due to data sparsity, it may be competitive for low-cardinality discrete labels (e.g., MNIST), and should be included. A further concern is computational: CMMD requires matrix inverses per minibatch; large batches reduce stochastic noise but make O(b^3) inversions expensive. Discussion of batch-size sensitivity, numerical stability (e.g., Cholesky, λ selection), and possible approximations (Nyström/RFF, preconditioned CG, running-average estimators) would strengthen the paper.

Clarity: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow; several derivations require consulting [29]. Please tighten notation, explicitly define the cross-covariance operator and centering assumptions, and give a worked example of Eq. (2). Minor error: the statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect as written; the commonly used V-statistic is biased but consistent, while the U-statistic is unbiased. Minor inconsistency: the conditional embedding operator definition deviates from [29]; adjust or clarify the implied definition of CXY.

Originality: Framing conditional deep generation via operator matching rather than per-condition matching is novel and addresses the data-sparsity issue for continuous conditions.

Significance: The approach is general and could be adopted for conditional synthesis and Bayesian model compression. Including comparisons to CGAN/CVAE and reporting uncertainty calibration (e.g., NLL/ECE) for distillation would increase impact.

Pros:
- Clean CMMD formulation; simple SGD training with backprop.
- Addresses per-condition data sparsity by matching operators.
- Diverse and encouraging experiments.

Cons:
- Missing naive baseline and comparisons to CGAN/CVAE.
- Computational/variance trade-offs of CMMD not fully addressed.
- Exposition gaps; minor technical inaccuracies.

Overall: Weak accept, contingent on adding the naive baseline, clarifying derivations, and discussing computational aspects.
