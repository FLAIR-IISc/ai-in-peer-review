- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs) that learn P(Y|X) by minimizing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Leverages kernel embeddings of conditional distributions as a theoretically sound, tractable alternative to methods like GANs.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN effectiveness across classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation tasks.  
- Elegant extension of MMD to the conditional setting with a clearly presented CMMD formulation.  
- Highlights advantage over naive per-condition GMMN approaches, especially for continuous conditioning and data sparsity.  
- Training via standard backpropagation is a practical benefit of the CMMD objective.  
- Experimental results, notably in Bayesian model distillation, are promising and suggest utility for knowledge transfer/model compression.  
- Missing direct comparison to per-condition baseline (e.g., low-cardinality MNIST) weakens the empirical evaluation.  
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and need clearer exposition or restructuring.  
- Incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased—should be corrected to “consistent.”  
- Inconsistency in the conditional embedding operator definition versus cited work [29]; cross-covariance operator definition needs clarification.  
- Potential computational bottleneck from large minibatches and matrix inversions for CMMD is noted but not practically addressed or analyzed.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable contribution; accept with minor revisions addressing clarity, baseline comparisons, minor errors, and computational trade-offs.
