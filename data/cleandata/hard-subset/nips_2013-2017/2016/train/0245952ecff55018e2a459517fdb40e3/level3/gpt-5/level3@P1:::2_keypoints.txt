- Proposes Conditional GMMN (CGMMN): a neural generator y = f(x,h;w) trained with a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective to model p(y|x).
- CMMD measures the Hilbert–Schmidt distance between conditional mean embedding operators CY|X of data and model and yields a Gram-matrix-based training loss (Eq.2).
- Training allows backpropagation through the output kernel, enabling likelihood-free conditional generation and prediction.
- CMMD derivation and assumptions behind Eq. (2) are under-explained (operator-to-trace transition, role of regularizer λ, when CY|X ∈ F ⊗ G).
- Practical implications of mini-batch approximations (bias, variance, stability) are not discussed.
- Empirical results are competitive across tasks (prediction, conditional generation, Bayesian distillation) but lack key ablations and stronger generative baselines.
- Missing sensitivity studies: kernel choice/bandwidth, λ, batch size, noise dimension, and runtime/complexity analysis.
- Implementation details mapping Eq. (2) to per-batch computations (which Gram matrices, where gradients flow, inversion strategy) are unclear, harming reproducibility.
- Presentation is formal-heavy and lacks an accessible intuition (CMMD ≈ MMD on outputs with input-dependent kernel-regression weights).
- Eq. (2) is stated without full derivation or unpacking of assumptions (e.g., K̃−1 dependence and differentiation).
- Supplement is hard for non-specialists; practical algorithmic steps (Gram formation, kernel choices, inversion/regularization, gradient paths) should be explicit.
- Conceptually natural but non-trivial extension of GMMN using conditional mean embeddings; mini-batch/operator implementation is interesting.
- Paper should sharpen empirical and conceptual positioning vs CGAN/CVAE (when CMMD provides advantages).
- Scalability concerns: per-batch complexity O(b^3) for K solves and O(b^2) memory, limiting impact without mitigation strategies.
- Pros: clear modular idea, kernel-theoretic grounding, simple backprop (no adversarial min–max), versatile applications, and competitive performance.
- Cons: weak derivation/intuition for Eq. (2), missing algorithmic mapping to SGD, no complexity/runtime analysis, limited ablations/comparisons, glossed practical estimator choices.
- Requested clarification 1: provide an intuitive stepwise derivation of Eq. (2) from ĈY|X = Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤ and the Hilbert–Schmidt norm expansion.
- Requested clarification 2: give a per-mini-batch algorithm mapping (sampling, Gram matrix construction, Cholesky/solves for K̃−1, where gradients flow, and complexity estimates).
- Suggested fixes: add derivation and pseudocode/figure in main text, discuss λ's role, provide complexity/runtime and sensitivity studies, compare to CVAE/CGAN on conditional generation, and discuss scalability remedies (Nyström, random features, shared x).
