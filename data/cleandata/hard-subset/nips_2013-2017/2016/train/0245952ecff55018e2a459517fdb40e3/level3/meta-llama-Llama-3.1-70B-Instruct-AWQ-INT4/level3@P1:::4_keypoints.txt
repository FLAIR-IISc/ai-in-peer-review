- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional generation via kernel embedding and the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD).
- Derives an empirical estimator for CMMD that can be optimized with back-propagation, enabling practical training.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and technically sound based on the reviewer’s reading.
- Framework is straightforward to apply (Algorithm 1) and relevant to practitioners in deep generative modeling and distillation.
- Empirical results show competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation.
- Experiments are thorough and well-documented, with a helpful appendix for methodology and details.
- Related work coverage is comprehensive, including links to GMMN, GANs, VAEs, and conditional generative methods.
- Novelty: a significant and non-obvious conditional extension of GMMN.
- Strength: empirical estimator derivation is particularly useful for implementation and training.
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of CGMMN’s properties.
- Weakness: lacks empirical comparisons with other conditional generative models.
- Weakness: no discussion of potential limitations of the approach.
- Acceptance arguments: novel extension, technical soundness, practical estimator, easy application, and strong empirical results.
- Rejection arguments: needs deeper theoretical analysis, comparative baselines, and discussion of limitations.
- Recommendation: accept, but advise the authors to address theoretical analysis, comparisons, and limitations in future work.
