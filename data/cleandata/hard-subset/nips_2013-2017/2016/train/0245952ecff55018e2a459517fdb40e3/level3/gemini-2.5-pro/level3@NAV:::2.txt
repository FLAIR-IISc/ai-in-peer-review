This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework to model conditional distributions. Building on prior work in kernel mean embeddings and Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD), the authors propose a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective to train a deep generative model. This work is positioned alongside other conditional generative models like conditional GANs and VAEs, offering a potentially simpler training objective by avoiding the min-max game of adversarial training.

**Strengths:**
The primary strength of this work is its novel extension of moment-matching networks to the conditional setting. This is a significant and non-trivial contribution, as it addresses the core difficulty of matching distributions over mini-batches of outputs, each corresponding to a different conditional input. The authors demonstrate the model's versatility across a wide range of tasks—including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation—which speaks to the potential significance and broad applicability of the proposed method.

**Weaknesses:**
Despite the promising contribution, the paper suffers from a significant lack of clarity that severely impacts its reproducibility and overall quality. The presentation begins with dense formalism, failing to provide an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective. The core objective in Equation (2) is introduced without a clear derivation, a discussion of its underlying assumptions, or its practical implications. This leaves the reader to guess how the complex terms involving matrix inverses and traces are derived and what they represent conceptually.

Furthermore, the path from the theoretical objective to the implemented algorithm is insufficiently detailed. The paper does not clearly state the exact computations performed during training, particularly within a mini-batch setting, nor does it discuss their computational efficiency. While the unconditional GMMN admits a simple, intuitive explanation, this crucial accessibility is missing for the conditional case, making the work difficult to penetrate. The supplemental material was not sufficient to resolve these ambiguities.

**Requested Clarifications:**
For this paper to be considered for publication, the clarity must be substantially improved. I request two concrete clarifications from the authors:
1.  A clear, intuitive derivation of the CMMD objective in Equation (2).
2.  A detailed, step-by-step mapping from Equation (2) to the practical mini-batch algorithm, ideally with pseudo-code and a discussion of computational complexity.

Without these additions, the paper is difficult to fully evaluate, understand, or build upon.
