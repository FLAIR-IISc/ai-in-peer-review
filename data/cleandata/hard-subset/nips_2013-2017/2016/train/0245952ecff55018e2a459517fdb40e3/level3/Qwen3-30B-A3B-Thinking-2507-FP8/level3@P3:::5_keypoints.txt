- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions.  
- Uses RKHS conditional embedding and proposes Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) as the training objective.  
- Generates samples by feeding conditional variables and noise through a deep network trained with SGD.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound and properly relates to Song et al. (2009) and prior GMMN work.  
- Method is technically novel and training via backprop is simpler than adversarial approaches like GANs.  
- Main implementation concern: empirical conditional operator omits centering terms (estimators of μ_X, μ_Y), questioning gradient correctness and experimental validity.  
- Authors must clarify how centering was handled in implementation to validate results.  
- Predictive performance is disappointing—CGMMN underperforms CMMVA on MNIST/SVHN, contradicting the “competitive performance” claim.  
- Generative evaluation is incomplete, lacking quantitative comparisons to conditional GANs/VAEs and missing evidence for advantage in Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Paper contains multiple typos and grammatical errors that hurt readability and professionalism.  
- Overall: theoretically interesting and original, but limited significance due to implementation ambiguity and weak empirical results.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on clarifying implementation (centering), improving experimental comparisons, and documenting reproducibility.
