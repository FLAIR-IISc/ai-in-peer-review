- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions via a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.  
- Positions method alongside conditional GANs/VAEs, claiming simpler training by avoiding adversarial min-max.  
- Main strength: novel, non-trivial extension of moment-matching to the conditional setting, addressing matching across minibatches with different inputs.  
- Demonstrated versatility across tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, knowledge distillation), indicating broad applicability.  
- Major weakness: significant lack of clarity that harms reproducibility and overall quality.  
- Presentation begins with dense formalism and lacks an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective.  
- Equation (2) is presented without clear derivation, stated assumptions, or discussion of practical implications.  
- Complex terms (matrix inverses, traces) are not explained conceptually, leaving their meaning unclear.  
- Insufficient detail on how the theoretical objective is implemented in practice, especially in a minibatch setting.  
- No discussion of computational efficiency or the exact computations performed during training.  
- Supplemental material does not resolve the ambiguities.  
- Requested clarification 1: provide a clear, intuitive derivation of the CMMD objective (Eq. 2).  
- Requested clarification 2: provide a detailed step-by-step mapping from Eq. 2 to the minibatch algorithm, with pseudocode and computational-complexity analysis.  
- Conclusion: without these clarifications the paper is difficult to evaluate, understand, or build upon.
