- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions p(y|x).
- Core technical contribution is the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion enabling training by back-propagation.
- Addresses practical needs: predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Positions work relative to GANs and GMMNs, arguing for simpler, more stable moment-matching alternative to adversarial training.
- Empirical results show competitive performance across MNIST, SVHN, contextual image generation, and knowledge distillation tasks.
- Derivation of CMMD and empirical estimator appears technically sound and sufficiently detailed for replication.
- Provides a practical stochastic gradient algorithm (Algorithm 1) and comprehensive appendix for reproducibility.
- Writing is clear and paper is well-organized, accessible to readers unfamiliar with MMD/GMMNs.
- Limited ablation studies; more analysis on sensitivity to kernel choices and network architectures is needed.
- Theoretical discussion of CMMD assumptions (smoothness, Hilbert–Schmidt norm) is underdeveloped.
- Scalability concerns due to cubic kernel computations are only briefly addressed; mini-batching partially mitigates this.
- Limited experimental exploration of combining CGMMN with autoencoders despite mentioning potential benefits.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to conditional setting, technical soundness, strong empirical results, clear presentation, and practical impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient ablations, incomplete theoretical discussion, scalability worries, and limited AE synergy experiments.
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, minor improvements needed, recommended for acceptance to NIPS.
