- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using conditional MMD (cMMD) to learn conditional distributions.  
- Extends GMMN to conditional generative modeling with a thorough mathematical formulation of the cMMD criterion.  
- Provides rigorous mathematical exposition, though parts are challenging to follow.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across predictive, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge tasks.  
- Results are promising and competitive with state-of-the-art methods on benchmark tasks.  
- Novelty: use of cMMD as a training objective and analysis of connections to MMD and related methods.  
- Quality: technically sound with rigorous derivations.  
- Clarity: overall reasonable but model description and some math are hard to follow.  
- Reproducibility concerns: insufficient implementation/model details and no reference code provided.  
- Evaluation scope: tasks are somewhat small; needs testing on more challenging datasets.  
- Significance: has potential to improve deep generative modeling but requires broader evaluation to fully assess impact.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, strong theory, comprehensive experiments, and competitive results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity and reproducibility issues, incomplete model details, and limited experimental scale.
