- Introduces CGMMN, an extension of GMMN that uses conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) to learn conditional distributions.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks.  
- Shows competitive performance with state-of-the-art models (e.g., DSN, CMMVA) and produces high-quality samples.  
- Training is straightforward via backpropagation with a CMMD objective, avoiding GAN-style adversarial training.  
- Mathematical exposition of CMMD and kernel embedding is dense and could use more intuitive explanation or visual aids.  
- Experimental evaluation is limited to small-scale benchmarks (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face) and lacks large-scale, real-world tests.  
- Training involves expensive kernel Gram matrix computations, but no detailed runtime comparison to alternatives is provided.  
- No reference code and insufficient detail on kernel selection and hyperparameter tuning hinder reproducibility.  
- Cubic complexity of kernel computations raises scalability concerns; the mini-batch proposal is not demonstrated on large datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound method filling a gap in conditional generative modeling, with versatile and competitive results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope, unresolved computational/scalability issues, and lack of reproducibility resources.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions if authors clarify the math, include training time comparisons, and release reference code.
