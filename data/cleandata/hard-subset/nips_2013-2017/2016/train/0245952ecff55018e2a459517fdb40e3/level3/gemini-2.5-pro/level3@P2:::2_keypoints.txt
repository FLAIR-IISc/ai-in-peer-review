- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a novel Conditional MMD (CMMD) that compares Hilbert-space embedding operators for p(y|x).  
- CGMMN is trained end-to-end via backpropagation and SGD.  
- Experiments show competitive results on predictive classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Strength: grounded in sound theory of kernel mean embeddings and extends GMMN to the conditional setting.  
- Strength: demonstrates practical viability across diverse tasks with competitive empirical performance.  
- Weakness: incomplete implementation details and computational aspects hinder reproducibility.  
- Weakness: presentation lacks intuition—CMMD is overly formal and its re-weighting by K̃⁻¹ is not explained.  
- Weakness: algorithmic description is opaque; Equation (2) is given without clear derivation from conditional embedding operators.  
- Weakness: no clear explanation of how gradients of the mini-batch CMMD objective are computed w.r.t. network parameters.  
- Computational concern: Equation (2) requires inverting batch-size kernel matrices (O(n^3)); no discussion of this bottleneck or mitigation strategies despite large batch sizes.  
- Originality: novel and non-trivial extension of moment-matching to conditional models; first principled CMMD operator formulation.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem—stable, non-adversarial conditional generative modeling with broad applicability.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novelty/significance, strong empirical results, and avoidance of adversarial training instabilities.  
- Anti-acceptance arguments: poor clarity of core contribution and unclear computational scalability/practicality.  
- Requested clarifications: provide an intuitive derivation of Equation (2) explaining the role of K̃⁻¹ as conditional weighting.  
- Requested clarifications: give a step-by-step derivation of mini-batch CMMD gradients w.r.t. generator parameters and discuss computational complexity.
