## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending the existing Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) framework to the conditional setting. The core idea is to learn a conditional distribution P(Y|X) by minimizing a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. This approach leverages kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and offers a theoretically sound and computationally tractable alternative to methods like GANs. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMNs across a diverse set of tasks including classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models, showcasing the method’s versatility.

The strength of this work lies in its elegant extension of MMD to the conditional setting. The CMMD formulation is clearly presented, and the authors correctly highlight its advantages over naive per-condition GMMN approaches, particularly concerning data sparsity issues with continuous conditioning variables. The ability to utilize standard backpropagation for training due to the properties of the CMMD objective is a significant benefit. The experimental results, especially in Bayesian model distillation, are promising and suggest potential applications in knowledge transfer and model compression. 

However, several weaknesses need addressing. While the advantage over a per-condition baseline is clear, a direct comparison against it, even for low-cardinality discrete conditions like MNIST, would strengthen the evaluation. The authors acknowledge this but don’t provide those results. Section 2.3 and 3.1 are notably difficult to follow; a more detailed explanation or restructuring is needed to improve clarity for readers unfamiliar with the referenced papers.  A minor but important correction is required: the statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect – it’s consistent. Furthermore, there's an inconsistency in the definition of the conditional embedding operator compared to the cited work [29], requiring a clarification of the cross-covariance operator definition. The potential computational bottleneck of large minibatches needed for CMMD, due to the matrix inversions, is appropriately identified as a concern, but the paper doesn’t offer practical solutions or analyze this trade-off in detail. 

**Arguments for acceptance:** Novel extension of MMD to conditional distributions, theoretically sound, promising experimental results in diverse tasks, computationally efficient training via backpropagation.
**Arguments against acceptance:** Clarity issues in key sections, missing baseline comparison, minor errors, potential computational challenges not thoroughly addressed.

**Overall:**  This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling.  Addressing the identified weaknesses, particularly regarding clarity and a more comprehensive experimental evaluation, would significantly strengthen the manuscript and justify acceptance to a competitive conference like NIPS. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions.
