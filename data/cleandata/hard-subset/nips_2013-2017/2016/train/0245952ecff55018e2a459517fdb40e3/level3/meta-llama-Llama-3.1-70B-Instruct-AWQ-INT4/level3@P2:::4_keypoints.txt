- Proposes CGMMN, an extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks for conditional generation tasks.
- Derives a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to train CGMMN using the same machinery as GMMN.
- Training performed with stochastic gradient descent and back-propagation; provides a simple Algorithm 1.
- Novel contribution is the empirical estimator of CMMD, which is non-obvious and practically useful.
- Empirical results on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation show competitiveness with state-of-the-art.
- Paper is technically sound with clear, well-organized derivations of CMMD and CGMMN architecture.
- Well-written manuscript and a helpful appendix with methodological and experimental details.
- Framework is easy to apply and relevant to practitioners using deep generative models or distillation.
- Weakness: lacks exhaustive theoretical analysis of CGMMN’s properties.
- Weakness: does not discuss limitations of the CMMD criterion and CGMMN architecture.
- Some equations and derivations may be difficult for non-experts to follow.
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective method, strong empirical competitiveness, clear writing and appendix.
- Arguments for rejection: missing theoretical analysis and absence of discussion on limitations.
