- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions using Conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Leverages kernel mean embedding techniques (Song et al., 2009) to embed conditional distributions and uses backpropagation for training.
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks.
- Builds on solid kernel-methods foundation and offers a potentially simpler alternative to adversarial GAN training.
- Extension to conditional distributions is natural and broadens moment-matching networks’ applicability.
- Properly cites related work in GANs and moment-matching literature.
- Strength: clear motivation and novel application of MMD to conditional generative modeling.
- Strength: strong theoretical grounding linking to kernel embeddings of conditional distributions.
- Strength: practical implementation with a range of experiments demonstrating versatility.
- Major weakness: empirical conditional operator estimator (\hat{C}_{Y|X}) appears to omit centering terms, raising theoretical and stability concerns.
- Experimental weakness: higher error rates on MNIST and SVHN compared to state-of-the-art (e.g., CMMVA).
- Generative performance hard to assess due to lack of comparisons with conditional GANs and limited qualitative results.
- Writing contains minor typos that reduce polish and professionalism.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, well-motivated, grounded in literature, and demonstrated across diverse applications.
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of centering terms, subpar predictive performance, insufficient comparative evaluation with other conditional generative models, and minor writing issues.
- Overall initial assessment: interesting idea but insufficient theoretical justification and experimental evidence; recommended rejection pending major revisions.
- Post-rebuttal update: authors justified omitting centering terms, added a conditional GAN comparison on MNIST, and fixed typos; reviewer now recommends acceptance with minor revisions (more quantitative GAN comparison).
