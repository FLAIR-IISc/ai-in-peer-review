- Contribution: Extends GMMNs to the conditional setting via conditional MMD (CMMD) by constructing a conditional mean operator in RKHS to match P(Y|X) between data and a generator.

- Method: Concatenates context x with noise h and trains a deep network by minimizing the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical conditional operators from real and generated pairs.

- Related work: Builds on Song et al. (2009) and MMD/Gretton et al., and is positioned relative to conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero), MMVA/CMMVA, and CVAE.

- Main quality concern: The empirical conditional operator definition omits centering terms (μX, μY); paper assumes “centering in RKHS” but gives no practical justification.

- Centering questions to clarify: (i) whether/how Gram matrices are centered (K ← HKH, L ← HLH, cross-centering across datasets) for single- and cross-dataset terms; (ii) whether centering matrices depend on model parameters; (iii) how mini-batch CMMD approximates full-batch centering and its variance; (iv) stability of (K̃)−1 with λ selection.

- Clarity issues: Generally clear but contains typos/grammar errors and would benefit from an explicit centered-operator formula and a sketch of gradients.

- Originality: The conditional extension via operator matching is novel, conceptually clean, and distinct from cGANs and CVAEs.

- Significance/empirics: Mixed — predictive performance lags state-of-the-art CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN; generative results are qualitative with no comparisons to conditional GANs/CVAEs or quantitative metrics (e.g., FID, precision/recall).

- Dark knowledge experiment: Small-scale and limited in scope.

- Pros: Novel principled CMMD objective; simple to train with backprop; broadly applicable to conditional modeling and knowledge distillation.

- Cons: Ambiguity around centering and gradient computation (potential estimator bias); weaker predictive accuracy than strong baselines; limited generative evaluation; presentation issues and minor errors.

- Recommendation for camera-ready: Include centering details, mini-batch size/λ ablations, and quantitative comparisons to cGAN/CVAE (with metrics); note authors have since addressed centering/gradient concerns.
