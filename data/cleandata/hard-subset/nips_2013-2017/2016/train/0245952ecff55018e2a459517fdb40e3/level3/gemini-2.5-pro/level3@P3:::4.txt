### **Summary**

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a framework for learning conditional distributions `p(y|x)`. This work extends the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework, which was limited to modeling joint distributions, by developing a novel training criterion called Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD). The CMMD measures the distance between two conditional distributions by computing the Hilbert-Schmidt norm between their kernel mean embedding operators. A key contribution is the derivation of a practical empirical estimator for the CMMD objective, which allows the network parameters to be learned efficiently using standard stochastic gradient descent and backpropagation, mirroring the simplicity of the original GMMN. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness and versatility of CGMMN across a range of applications, including competitive performance on predictive modeling (classification on MNIST and SVHN), contextual image generation, and a novel application in distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

### **Strengths and Weaknesses**

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a complete piece of work. The theoretical development is well-grounded in the literature on kernel methods, logically extending the principles of Maximum Mean Discrepancy to the conditional setting by leveraging prior work on the kernel embedding of conditional distributions. While the equations have not been exhaustively checked by this reviewer, the derivations appear plausible and are well-explained. The experimental evaluation is thorough and provides strong support for the paper's claims. The authors evaluate their model on a diverse set of tasks, comparing it against relevant and state-of-the-art baselines. The results demonstrate that CGMMN is a competitive approach in several domains, validating its utility.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and well-organized. The authors do an excellent job of motivating the need for conditional GMMNs and provide a concise but sufficient background on the necessary concepts from kernel methods. The proposed CGMMN architecture and the CMMD objective are explained with clarity. The inclusion of Remark 1, which provides an intuitive link between the MMD and CMMD objectives (highlighting CMMD as a non-uniform re-weighting of MMD), is particularly helpful for understanding. Algorithm 1 provides a clear and simple recipe for implementation. The paper is self-contained, and the helpful appendix provides additional methodological and experimental details that would aid in reproducibility.

**Originality:** The core contribution is the extension of moment-matching networks to the conditional setting. While the idea of making a generative model conditional is a natural research direction (as seen with Conditional GANs and Conditional VAEs), the specific technical derivation for this framework is non-trivial and novel. The key innovation lies in formulating the CMMD and, critically, deriving its empirical estimator (Equation 2). This derivation, which enables optimization using the same machinery as the unconditional GMMN, is a non-obvious and valuable contribution that distinguishes the paper from being a minor, incremental extension. The application to distilling Bayesian models is also a novel use case for this type of generative framework.

**Significance:** This work is highly significant because it greatly expands the applicability of GMMNs, making them relevant for a much wider range of tasks beyond unconditional generation. The ability to model `p(y|x)` opens the door to applications in supervised learning, structured prediction, and any task involving contextual generation. The training stability of moment-matching methods compared to the min-max optimization of GANs makes CGMMN an attractive and practical alternative. The framework is presented in a way that is easy to apply (Algorithm 1), increasing its potential for adoption by practitioners. The demonstration of distilling "Bayesian dark knowledge" is a creative and compelling example of the model's flexibility and is likely to inspire further work on model compression and knowledge transfer. This paper provides a valuable tool for researchers and practitioners working with deep generative models.
