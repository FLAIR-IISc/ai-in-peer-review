This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD metric. The authors evaluate CGMMN on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models. The paper is well-motivated, and the proposed approach has a clear advantage over the naive baseline, which trains a GMMN per conditional distribution sharing network parameters and suffers from data sparsity, especially with continuous conditioning.

The authors provide a clean formulation of Conditional MMD, which can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD. The K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, allowing standard backpropagation. However, the authors note that CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch noise, but large minibatches make computation (notably matrix inverses) expensive.

The experimental results for classification and Bayesian model distillation are interesting and promising. The authors demonstrate competitive performance in all tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. However, the presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow, and the reviewer had to consult referenced papers for clarity.

There are a few minor errors and inconsistencies in the paper. The statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect. Additionally, the conditional embedding operator definition differs from reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition is needed.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of their proposed approach. The experimental results are promising, and the paper makes a significant contribution to the field of deep generative models.
