- Proposal: Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn p(y|x) via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.
- Core idea: Represent conditionals as RKHS operators CY|X and minimize Hilbert–Schmidt norm between empirical operators from data and from a generator y = f(x,h;w).
- Training: CMMD yields a computable trace-form objective using Gram matrices with Tikhonov regularization and admits simple back-propagation and SGD (Algorithm 1).
- Advantage vs GANs: Provides a non-adversarial, likelihood-free conditional training criterion that avoids min–max instability of GANs.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on kernel mean embeddings and GMMN, relates to cGANs/cVAEs as alternative conditional DGMs, and connects to Bayesian distillation literature.
- Experiments: Evaluated on prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST digits, Yale Faces, code-space sampling), and Bayesian distillation (student for PBP).
- Empirical results: Competitive for prediction with credible conditional samples, though not state-of-the-art across tasks.
- Methodological quality: Sound, well-motivated derivation with acknowledged assumptions and practical regularized estimators; learning algorithm is clear.
- Clarity: Paper is well-written, organized, with concise background, explicit CMMD derivation, algorithmic details, and helpful figures/appendix.
- Originality: Natural but non-trivial extension of unconditional MMD to operator-based conditional CMMD; novel combination of conditional embeddings with GMMN and backprop.
- Significance: Practical alternative to adversarial training; simple algorithm compatible with various networks and kernels, broadening conditional generative modeling tools.
- Strength: Clean, principled extension of GMMN to conditionals and useful estimator derivation.
- Strength: Simple, stable back-propagation training that avoids adversarial optimization.
- Strength: Broad applicability demonstrated across prediction, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation.
- Strength: Compatibility with CNN/MLP backbones and autoencoder code-space modeling.
- Strength: Clear exposition and thorough supplementary material.
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—main step is importing conditional embeddings into GMMN framework.
- Weakness: Computational costs from Gram-matrix inverses; minibatching helps but statistical impact of minibatch CMMD is unanalyzed.
- Weakness: Kernel/bandwidth choices for x and y not systematically studied; sensitivity guidelines missing.
- Weakness: Limited baselines for conditional generation (no direct cGAN or cVAE comparisons) and mostly qualitative evaluation.
- Weakness: Classification uses single sampled y with argmax; lacks comparisons to discriminative losses and calibration analysis.
- Weakness: Bayesian distillation shown only on Boston Housing, lacking broader benchmarks or direct comparison to original dark-knowledge methods.
- Suggestions: Add quantitative generation metrics and cGAN/cVAE baselines; analyze kernel sensitivity and bandwidth selection.
- Suggestions: Provide computational profiling and consider Nyström/Random Features for scalability.
- Suggestions: Explore multiple test-time samples, report calibration (ECE/NLL), expand Bayesian distillation experiments, and release code.
- Decision: Reviewer recommends weak accept—principled, useful contribution with broader impact pending stronger baselines and analyses; confidence: medium.
