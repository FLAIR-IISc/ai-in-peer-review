- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Leverages kernel mean embeddings to model conditionals, offering a theoretically motivated approach (Theorems 1 & 2).  
- Potentially simpler and more stable training than GANs, using backpropagation for practical optimization.  
- Empirical results show viability across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation, but evaluation is not extensive.  
- Revised manuscript addresses previous concerns about latent dimensionality and scalability.  
- Major theoretical weakness: Theorem 3 lacks a proof or citation.  
- Clarity issues: mapping from Equation (2) to weighted MMD is unclear and needs more explanation.  
- Notational confusion: dot notation (matrix vs. Hadamard) is ambiguous and symbol ‘C’ is overloaded.  
- Requires a more intuitive connection between CMMD and standard MMD formulations.  
- Mini-batch algorithm (Alg. 1) and its relation to the full CMMD objective need clearer explanation.  
- Originality: novel combination of GMMN, MMD, and kernel embeddings to address conditional generative modeling.  
- Offers a valuable alternative to conditional GANs, potentially avoiding GAN training instability.  
- Application to Bayesian distillation is a useful demonstration but limited in scope and datasets.  
- Significance: competitive but not state-of-the-art results; broader impact depends on stronger evaluations on datasets like CIFAR-10 or LSUN.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound extension, addresses prior concerns, multi-task demonstration, potential training stability, flexible framework.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing proof/citation for Theorem 3, unclear Equation (2) and notation, empirical gains modest, limited Bayesian distillation evaluation, needs harder-dataset validation.  
- Overall: solid contribution with minor clarity/theoretical fixes needed; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
