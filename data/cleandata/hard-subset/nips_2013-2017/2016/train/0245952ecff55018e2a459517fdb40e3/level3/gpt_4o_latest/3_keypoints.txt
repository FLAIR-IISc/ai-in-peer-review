- Proposal: Introduces Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) using a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) based on conditional kernel mean embeddings for conditional generative modeling and applications like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation.

- Novelty: CMMD extends GMMN to conditional settings and fills a gap in moment-matching generative models.

- Technical soundness: CMMD derivation and connections to MMD are well-motivated and grounded in kernel mean embedding theory; generative process is clearly explained.

- Empirical validation: Shows competitive performance on MNIST and SVHN classification, digit/face generation, and an application to Bayesian model distillation.

- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written, organized, and includes helpful generative visualizations.

- Scalability concern: Experiments are limited to low-complexity datasets (e.g., MNIST, Yale Faces), leaving performance on harder datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) unexplored.

- Theoretical gap: Theorem 3, central to claims, lacks a formal proof or reference, undermining theoretical rigor.

- Notation/clarity issues: Mapping in Equation (2) to weighted CMMD is unclear; distinction between matrix multiplication and Hadamard product is ambiguous; symbol "C" is used for multiple meanings.

- Limited dataset diversity: Bayesian distillation evaluated only on Boston Housing, raising questions about generalization to other or larger domains.

- Supervised applicability: Utility in supervised tasks is debatable since discriminative models often outperform generative ones for pure prediction.

- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions pending proof for Theorem 3, clearer notation, and expanded empirical evaluation on more challenging/diverse datasets.

- Pros summary: Novel conditional extension of GMMN, competitive predictive/generative results, interesting Bayesian distillation application.

- Cons summary: Limited scalability and dataset diversity, missing key theorem proof, and notation/clarity problems.
