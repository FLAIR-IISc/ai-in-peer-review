- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation and prediction using kernel embeddings and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.
- CMMD is theoretically well-formulated and computationally similar to unconditional MMD, with backpropagation straightforward because kernel matrices are parameter-independent.
- CGMMNs address data sparsity in continuous conditional settings where naive GMMN extensions fail.
- Experiments show competitive performance across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation, demonstrating versatility.
- Novelty: extends GMMNs to conditional settings using kernel embeddings and CMMD, filling a gap in the literature.
- Technical soundness: CMMD objective is rigorously defined and advantages over naive approaches are clearly argued.
- Experimental validation: diverse tasks (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, Bayesian distillation) with competitive results, especially in predictive modeling.
- Practical implementation: training via SGD and backprop is straightforward; mini-batch algorithm helps mitigate large kernel matrix costs.
- Clarity issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and need more explanation or visual aids for the conditional embedding operator and CMMD.
- Missing baselines: lacks direct empirical comparisons with naive conditional baselines, weakening claims of superiority.
- Scalability concerns: CMMD may require large minibatches and expensive matrix inversions, which are acknowledged but not fully resolved.
- Minor inaccuracies: incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased and inconsistency in the conditional embedding operator definition vs. reference [29].
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a significant gap, is theoretically sound and implementable, and shows promising empirical results likely to inspire further research.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear key sections hindering reproducibility, missing naive-baseline comparisons, and unresolved scalability issues.
- Recommendation: overall a strong contribution; accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, correct inaccuracies, and add empirical comparisons.
