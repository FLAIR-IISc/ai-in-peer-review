This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a novel framework for learning conditional distributions. The authors extend the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to conditional generation by leveraging a kernel embedding of conditional moments. The proposed approach enables the optimization of the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion using the same machinery as the unconditional GMMN.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their work. The technical soundness of the paper is evident, with a thorough derivation of the CMMD criterion and its empirical estimator. Although the reviewer did not exhaustively check the equations, the presentation appears to be correct.

The novelty of the paper lies in the conditional extension of GMMN, which is a natural but non-obvious step. The empirical estimator derivation is particularly useful, as it enables the optimization of the CMMD criterion using back-propagation. The framework is easy to apply, as demonstrated by Algorithm 1, and is likely to be relevant to practitioners using deep generative models or distillation.

The authors demonstrate the competitive performance of CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, with a helpful appendix containing methodological and experimental details.

In terms of related work, the authors provide a comprehensive overview of the existing literature on deep generative models, including GMMN, Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs). The authors also discuss the connections between their work and other approaches, such as conditional GANs and conditional VAEs.

Overall, the paper is well-structured, and the authors provide a clear and concise presentation of their work. The technical soundness of the paper is evident, and the novelty of the approach is significant. The experimental results are thorough and well-presented, demonstrating the competitive performance of CGMMN on various tasks.

Strengths:

* Novel extension of GMMN to conditional generation
* Clear and concise presentation of the work
* Technical soundness of the paper is evident
* Empirical estimator derivation is particularly useful
* Framework is easy to apply
* Competitive performance on various tasks

Weaknesses:

* Limited analysis of the theoretical properties of CGMMN
* No comparison with other conditional generative models
* No discussion of the potential limitations of the approach

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and significant extension of GMMN to conditional generation.
* The technical soundness of the paper is evident, and the empirical estimator derivation is particularly useful.
* The framework is easy to apply, and the experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on various tasks.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may benefit from a more thorough analysis of the theoretical properties of CGMMN.
* A comparison with other conditional generative models would strengthen the paper.
* A discussion of the potential limitations of the approach would provide a more balanced view.

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting the paper, as it presents a novel and significant extension of GMMN to conditional generation. The technical soundness of the paper is evident, and the empirical estimator derivation is particularly useful. The framework is easy to apply, and the experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on various tasks. However, I suggest that the authors consider addressing the weaknesses mentioned above in future work.
