

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to the conditional setting by developing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors present a generative model that conditions on input variables and learns the conditional distribution through CMMD optimization. The paper evaluates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge tasks, reporting competitive results.

The paper's main strength lies in its empirical validation across diverse applications, demonstrating the versatility of the conditional framework. The experiments on MNIST, SVHN, and face generation provide reasonable evidence of the model's capabilities, and the Bayesian dark knowledge application is particularly novel.

However, the paper suffers from significant clarity issues that undermine its technical contribution. The core difficulty of matching distributions over large mini-batches of outputs to many different conditional inputs is not adequately explained. The authors begin with heavy formal math (Section 3.1) without providing an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective, making it difficult for readers to grasp the fundamental concept. Equation (2), the key optimization objective, is presented without derivation, assumptions, or practical implications. The transition from this mathematical formulation to the practical algorithm is insufficiently detailed, and the supplemental material is hard to follow. This is particularly problematic since the non-conditional version admits a simple, intuitive explanation, and the conditional case requires equally accessible presentation.

The paper also fails to clearly state the actual computations performed by the algorithm and their computational efficiency, which is critical for practical adoption. The authors do not address how the computational complexity scales with batch size or conditional dimensionality.

While the work addresses a meaningful extension to GMMN, the lack of clarity in explaining the conditional objective and algorithm implementation significantly weakens the contribution. The paper would benefit greatly from an intuitive derivation of Equation (2) and a clear step-by-step mapping from the mathematical formulation to the practical implementation. Without these clarifications, the paper's technical contribution remains obscured, limiting its potential impact.
