- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Extends GMMN to conditional modeling, addressing tasks like prediction, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation.  
- Core idea: use kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and match the conditional embedding operator via CMMD for training with SGD/backprop.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance across diverse experiments (predictive modeling on MNIST/SVHN, contextual generation, Bayesian dark knowledge).  
- Motivation: avoids per-condition data sparsity by modeling the family of conditional distributions jointly rather than training separate GMMNs.  
- Strength: clear mathematical formulation and compatibility with standard deep learning training procedures.  
- Strength: CMMD objective can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD and kernel matrices are independent of model parameters.  
- Weakness: theoretical sections (2.3, 3.1) are dense and hard to follow; reader must consult prior work to fully understand.  
- Minor inaccuracy: claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect — it is consistent but not unbiased.  
- Minor inconsistency: definition of the conditional embedding operator appears to differ from reference [29] and needs clarification.  
- Computational concern: CMMD may require large minibatches, increasing costs (e.g., matrix inversions) and stochastic noise issues.  
- Recommendation: discuss mitigation strategies for computational cost (low-rank approximations, alternative kernels, detailed minibatch trade-offs).  
- Suggested experiment: compare to the naive baseline of training separate GMMNs per condition on low-cardinality discrete datasets (e.g., MNIST).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, addresses data sparsity, competitive empirical results, and solid mathematical grounding.  
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity issues in theory, minor theoretical inaccuracies/inconsistencies, computational scalability concerns, and missing baseline comparison.  
- Overall: recommend acceptance conditional on clarifying theoretical sections, correcting inaccuracies, and addressing/acknowledging computational and baseline comparison issues.
