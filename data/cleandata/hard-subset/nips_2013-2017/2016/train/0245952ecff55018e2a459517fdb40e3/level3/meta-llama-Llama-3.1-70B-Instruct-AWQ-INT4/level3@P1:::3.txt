This paper proposes a novel framework for conditional generative modeling, called Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN). The authors extend the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to handle conditional variables by introducing a new loss function based on conditional kernel mean embeddings. The proposed approach is evaluated on various tasks, including classification, digit and face generation, and Bayesian distillation.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach and its relationship to previous work. The use of conditional kernel mean embeddings is a key contribution of the paper, and the authors provide a thorough discussion of the theoretical foundations of this approach.

The empirical results presented in the paper are decent and comparable to the original GMMN in generative tasks. However, the results on supervised learning tasks are not as strong, and the authors acknowledge that this is not the primary application of the proposed approach. The Bayesian distillation application is interesting, but the evaluation is limited to a single dataset and model, which makes it difficult to assess the generality of the approach.

One of the main strengths of the paper is its ability to provide a flexible framework for conditional generative modeling. The authors demonstrate the versatility of the approach by applying it to various tasks, and the results show that the proposed method is competitive with state-of-the-art approaches.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear, and the notation could be improved to avoid confusion. Additionally, Theorem 3 lacks a reference or provided proof, which makes it difficult to assess the validity of the claim.

Overall, the paper provides a significant contribution to the field of generative modeling, and the proposed approach has the potential to be widely adopted. However, further evaluation on more challenging datasets and tasks is necessary to fully assess the strengths and limitations of the approach.
