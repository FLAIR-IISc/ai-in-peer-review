- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMN to model conditional distributions p(y|x).
- Proposes a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective measuring discrepancy via the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional kernel mean embedding operators.
- Model maps (x, noise h) → y using a deep neural network trained by SGD and backpropagation.
- Fills an important gap by providing a theoretically grounded conditional counterpart to GMMN, analogous to conditional GANs/VAEs.
- Avoids adversarial min-max training, offering a single loss function that may improve training stability.
- Demonstrates broad applicability with experiments on predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Shows competitive classification/generation results on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Faces.
- Strength: novel, elegant, and theoretically grounded CMMD formulation increasing versatility of moment-matching methods.
- Weakness: dense mathematical exposition (Section 3.1) that lacks intuitive explanation or illustrations for non-experts.
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details (network architectures, initialization, optimizer settings, kernel choices, λ) and no provided source code, harming reproducibility.
- Weakness: computational cost—CMMD requires Gram matrix inversion per minibatch (O(B^3)), potentially much slower than VAEs/GANs; runtime comparisons missing.
- Weakness: experiments limited to small-scale datasets; lacks evaluation on larger/challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, CelebA, LSUN) and samples appear noisier than contemporary GANs.
- Key question: practical choices and sensitivity of kernels and regularization parameter λ.
- Key question: clarification of Algorithm 1’s batching procedure—whether CMMD computation effectively uses batch size 2|B|.
- Key question: does reliance on an autoencoder for better samples imply CGMMN struggles with raw pixel modeling and is better suited to latent-space modeling?
- Arguments for acceptance: introduces a novel, non-adversarial conditional objective with demonstrated versatility and solid empirical results.
- Arguments against acceptance: potential scalability/computational bottleneck, limited experimental scale, and reproducibility concerns.
