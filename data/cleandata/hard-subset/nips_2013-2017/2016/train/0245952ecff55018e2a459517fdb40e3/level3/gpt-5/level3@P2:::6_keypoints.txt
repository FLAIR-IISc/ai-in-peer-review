- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), training conditional generators by minimizing conditional MMD (CMMD) via kernel conditional mean embeddings.  
- Defines CMMD as the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional embedding operators and instantiates generator y = f(x,h|w) with noise h and condition x.  
- Observation that the x-kernel inverse K̃−1 depends only on x and λ (not on network weights) simplifies backpropagation.  
- Empirical evaluation on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston Housing) with competitive but not state-of-the-art results.  
- Extends prior GMMN work from unconditional to conditional generation, paralleling cGAN/CVAE transitions and leveraging conditional mean embedding literature.  
- Soundness: CMMD objective follows standard conditional mean embedding theory and the K̃−1 independence claim is correct.  
- Theory: Theorem showing operator equality implies conditional distribution equality is standard under universal kernels; discussion of necessity/characteristic kernels would help.  
- Practical estimator use (regularized Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤) is appropriate but relies on regularization.  
- Algorithmic complexity: mini-batch CMMD requires O(B^3) inversion per batch, raising scalability and variance concerns.  
- Empirics: interesting classification and distillation experiments; combining with autoencoders improves generative quality.  
- Missing stronger quantitative comparisons to conditional baselines (cGAN, CVAE) and richer generative/uncertainty metrics.  
- Clarity: exposition of conditional embeddings and CMMD is terse; operator domains/codomains and adjoint conventions need clearer statements.  
- Minor technical error: statement that L̂2MMD is asymptotically unbiased is misleading—V-statistic MMD2 is biased; U-statistic is unbiased.  
- Notational inconsistency around operator mapping directions and transposes relative to prior work could cause confusion.  
- Originality: novel, natural, and nontrivial kernel-based extension to conditional deep generators distinct from adversarial/variational methods.  
- Significance: fills a gap for non-adversarial conditional generation, offering stability and simplicity; distillation application is promising.  
- Strengths: principled CMMD objective, simple backprop training, versatile applications, qualitative conditional control and nearest-neighbor checks.  
- Weaknesses: computational scalability and estimator variance, lack of naive baselines (per-class GMMN) and local baselines, limited quantitative evaluation, and clarity issues.  
- Suggested improvements: add per-class and local baselines, report sensitivity to batch size/λ/kernel bandwidths, and explore scalable approximations (Nyström, RFF, precompute K̃−1).  
- Suggested metrics: include classification NLL/Brier, predictive NLL for distillation, conditional inception/accuracy or class-conditional MMD for generation.  
- Clarify Section 2.3 with explicit operator domains/adjoints and align notation with cited work; correct the MMD estimator statement.  
- Recommend a toy multi-modal conditional regression to demonstrate stochastic/multi-modal outputs beyond label-conditioned images.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled extension, simple/stable objective, solid diverse-task results, interesting distillation use case.  
- Cons against acceptance: underexplored computational/variance trade-offs, missing key baselines and quantitative evaluations, and clarity/technical inaccuracies.  
- Overall: promising and principled contribution that would be stronger with improved clarity, baselines, quantitative evaluation, and scalability analysis.  
- Questions for authors: which kernels/bandwidths/λ were used and sensitivity; effective batch sizes and compute/memory costs for K̃−1; can K̃−1 be precomputed or approximated (Nyström/inducing); does CGMMN capture multi-modal p(y|x)?
