- Proposes Conditional GMMNs (CGMMN) using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) to learn complex conditional distributions.
- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks to conditional settings, enabling conditional generation, classification, and Bayesian model distillation.
- Key advantage: treats all conditionals as a family and matches the conditional embedding operator directly, mitigating per-condition data sparsity.
- Particularly useful for continuous conditioning variables where training separate GMMNs per condition suffers from sparsity.
- Provides a clear formulation of conditional MMD that can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD.
- Notes that K matrices in Eq. (2) are independent of model parameters, enabling standard backpropagation.
- Empirical results on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face show competitive performance for classification and Bayesian model distillation.
- Reviewer concern: method may require large minibatches to reduce stochastic minibatch noise, increasing computational cost.
- Presentation issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and rely on referenced papers for clarity.
- Minor errors/inconsistencies: incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased; conditional embedding operator definition differs from prior work ([29]).
- Overall assessment: novel and promising approach with strong results; recommendation to accept with minor revisions.
- Rating: 8/10.
