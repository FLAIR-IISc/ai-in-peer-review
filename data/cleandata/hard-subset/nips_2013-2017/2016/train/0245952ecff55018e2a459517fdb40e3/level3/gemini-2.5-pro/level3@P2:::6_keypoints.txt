- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to learn p(y|x) by matching conditional kernel-mean embedding operators.  
- CGMMN is a deep network taking x and noise to generate y, trained by backprop on the CMMD loss.  
- Significant and natural extension of GMMNs to conditional modeling, offering an alternative to conditional GANs/VAEs without min-max optimization.  
- The CMMD objective has a clean RKHS operator formulation and benefits from kernel-trick computation; kernel matrices for x are parameter-independent, easing gradient computation.  
- Strong experimental validation across classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation, with competitive results on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Conceptual advantage: matching the full conditional embedding operator handles continuous/high-cardinality x and avoids per-x data sparsity of naive approaches.  
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is dense and needs clearer, more intuitive explanation of operator matching for accessibility.  
- Missing baseline: no comparison to training separate GMMNs per class (or shared-parameter variant) for low-cardinality discrete conditional generation (e.g., MNIST).  
- Computational concern: CMMD requires kernel-matrix inversion (O(batch^3)), creating a trade-off between minibatch size, estimator variance, and computational cost that needs discussion/guidance.  
- Minor technical inaccuracies: the presented empirical squared MMD estimator is claimed unbiased but is biased, and the conditional embedding operator definition appears inconsistent with cited [29] and should be clarified.
