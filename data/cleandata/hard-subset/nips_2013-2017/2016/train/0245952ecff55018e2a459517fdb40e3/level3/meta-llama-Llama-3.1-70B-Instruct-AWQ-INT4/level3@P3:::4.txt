This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a novel extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to conditional generation tasks. The authors introduce a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, which measures the difference between the kernel mean embeddings of two conditional distributions. The CMMD objective is optimized using stochastic gradient descent, allowing for efficient training of the CGMMN model.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical foundation for their approach, including a detailed derivation of the CMMD objective and its relationship to the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion used in GMMN. They also discuss the connections between CMMD and MMD, highlighting the key differences between the two objectives.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the technical details of the CGMMN model. The authors also provide a helpful appendix containing additional methodological and experimental details.

The experimental evaluation of CGMMN is comprehensive and convincing, demonstrating competitive performance on a range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation. The authors compare their model to several state-of-the-art baselines, including Variational Auto-encoders (VA), max-margin DGMs (MMVA), and Deeply-supervised nets (DSN).

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to demonstrate the flexibility and applicability of the CGMMN model to different tasks and domains. The authors show that CGMMN can be used for both predictive modeling and generative tasks, and that it can be combined with other models, such as autoencoders, to improve its performance.

The paper also makes a significant contribution to the field of deep generative models, providing a new and effective approach to conditional generation tasks. The authors' use of kernel mean embeddings and CMMD objective provides a novel perspective on the problem of conditional generation, and their results demonstrate the potential of this approach.

Overall, I believe that this paper is a strong contribution to the field of deep generative models, and that it has the potential to make a significant impact on the development of new models and applications.
