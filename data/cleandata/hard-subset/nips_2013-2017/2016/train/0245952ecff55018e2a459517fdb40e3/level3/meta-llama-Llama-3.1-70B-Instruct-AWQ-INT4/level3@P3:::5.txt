The paper "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks" presents a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors extend the generative moment-matching networks (GMMN) framework to match conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). This approach is well-motivated, as it allows for the modeling of complex conditional distributions without making unnecessary assumptions about the underlying data.

The paper provides a clear and concise overview of the theoretical background, including the definition of CMMD and its relationship to the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion. The authors also provide a detailed description of the conditional generative moment-matching network (CGMMN) architecture, which is designed to learn a conditional distribution given some input variables.

The experimental results presented in the paper demonstrate the effectiveness of the CGMMN approach on several real-world datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face. The authors show that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art methods in terms of predictive performance and generative quality. However, the results are somewhat disappointing, as CGMMN has higher predictive error than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets.
