- Proposes two novel optimization methods for Supervised PageRank: a gradient-free randomization-based approach and a gradient-based adaptive projected gradient method.
- Extends existing bilevel optimization frameworks to handle inexact oracles, addressing a crucial practical challenge.
- Provides clear problem formulation and well-structured presentation of methods and theoretical analysis.
- Delivers theoretical guarantees for convergence rates and complexity bounds for both proposed methods.
- Empirical results show the proposed methods outperform the state-of-the-art gradient-based baseline.
- Analyzes the trade-off between lower-level algorithm accuracy and the overall two-level computational complexity.
- Strength: Novel methods that explicitly handle inexact oracles, a significant contribution to optimization for learning PageRank models.
- Strength: Rigorous theoretical analysis supporting convergence and complexity claims.
- Strength: Strong experimental validation demonstrating practical effectiveness.
- Weakness: Assumes local convexity of the loss near the optimum, which may not hold in general nonconvex settings.
- Weakness: Performance is sensitive to hyperparameter choices (e.g., Lipschitz constant, accuracy levels).
- Weakness: Lacks a more detailed empirical comparison with other existing methods, especially those not relying on inexact oracles.
- Authors addressed reviewer concerns in the rebuttal and revised the manuscript to improve clarity.
- Overall recommendation: Accept — the paper is a significant, well-presented contribution despite some limitations.
