- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) loss for learning P(Y|X).
- Positions CMMD/CGMMN as a principled, significant extension of GMMN and an alternative to conditional VAEs/GANs.
- CMMD objective is well-motivated via conditional kernel mean embeddings.
- Experimental evaluation shows versatility across tasks (classification, conditional generation, Bayesian model distillation).
- Generative results on digits and faces are competent and validate the core idea.
- Authors claim to have addressed prior scalability and utility concerns.
- Clarity issues: the link between Equation (2) and the weighted MMD interpretation in Remark 1 is opaque.
- Notation problems: dot notation meaning (matrix vs. Hadamard product) is unspecified and symbol ‘C’ is overloaded.
- Major theoretical gap: Theorem 3 (matching conditional embedding operators implies matching conditional distributions) is presented without proof or citation.
- Experiments lack rigor and generality: Bayesian distillation tested on only one small dataset.
- Empirical impact limited by absence of results on stronger, standard benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
- Overall: novel and promising idea but needs clarity fixes and theoretical justification before publication.
