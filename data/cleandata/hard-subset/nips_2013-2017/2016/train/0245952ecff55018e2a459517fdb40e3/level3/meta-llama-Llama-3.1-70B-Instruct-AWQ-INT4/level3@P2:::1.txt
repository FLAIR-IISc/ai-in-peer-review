This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a neural network method that extends the capability of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to learn a flexible conditional distribution given some input variables. The authors introduce a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion as the training objective, which measures the difference between the kernel mean embedding of an empirical conditional distribution and that of the generative model.

The main idea of CGMMN is to learn a conditional distribution by minimizing the CMMD objective, which is defined as the Hilbert-Schmidt norm between the kernel mean embedding of the conditional distributions. The authors provide a theoretical foundation for CMMD and show that it is a sufficient condition to guarantee that two conditional distributions are the same.

The paper evaluates CGMMN on a wide range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The results demonstrate that CGMMN can obtain competitive performance in all these tasks. Specifically, the authors show that CGMMN can achieve state-of-the-art performance on the MNIST and SVHN datasets for predictive modeling, and generate high-quality images on the MNIST and Yale Face datasets for contextual generation.

The strengths of the paper include its novel extension of GMMN to conditional distributions, its theoretical foundation, and its competitive performance on various tasks. The paper is also well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its applications.
