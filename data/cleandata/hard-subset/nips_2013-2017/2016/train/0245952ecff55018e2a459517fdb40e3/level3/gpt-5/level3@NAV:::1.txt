This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a conditional deep generative model trained by minimizing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) between the model’s conditional distribution and the empirical conditional distribution represented via RKHS conditional embeddings. The generator maps [x, h] → y with h drawn from a simple prior, and the CMMD objective is computed from kernel Gram matrices; gradients are obtained by back-propagation. Experiments span discriminative prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST digits, Yale faces), and “Bayesian dark knowledge” distillation from a Bayesian neural network (PBP). The work builds naturally on MMD/GMMN and kernel conditional mean embeddings, and relates to CGANs and CVAEs while avoiding adversarial min–max optimization.

Quality: The CMMD objective is well-motivated: equality of conditional embedding operators implies equality of conditionals, and the empirical operator estimator is standard. However, training requires matrix inverses per batch and cubic costs in batch size; the paper should report runtime and memory vs DSN/CMMVA/CGAN/CVAE, as well as wall-clock to reach a fixed error. Empirical results are competitive on small benchmarks but do not clearly surpass strong discriminative baselines; additional quantitative generative metrics and uncertainty calibration (for prediction/distillation) would strengthen claims. Ablations on kernel choices/bandwidths, λ, minibatch size, and perturbation magnitude in distillation are missing.

Clarity: The paper is generally clear, but the mathematical exposition can be hard to follow. More intuition about CMMD vs MMD, a complexity section, and explicit training pseudo-code would help. Crucially, the model description lacks enough detail for reproduction: specify kernel types for x and y, bandwidth selection, λ, all architecture/layer sizes, optimizer settings, and sampling procedures. Reference code should be provided.

Originality: Extending GMMN to the conditional setting with a principled CMMD loss is a meaningful and timely contribution that connects deep generative modeling with conditional mean embeddings. The work is distinct from CGAN/CVAE in objective and optimization.

Significance: Moderate. The approach is simple and broadly applicable (including distillation), but demonstrated only on relatively small tasks; scalability evidence is limited.

Pros:
- Conceptually clean conditional extension of GMMN with sound operator-based objective.
- Versatile formulation applicable to prediction, generation, and distillation.
- Competitive results across tasks.

Cons:
- Computational overhead not characterized; scalability unclear.
- Experiments limited in scale; some baselines stronger.
- Reproducibility concerns: insufficient details and no code.

Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on adding runtime comparisons and releasing code with full training details.
