

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions via conditional kernel mean embeddings. The authors propose a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion for training, enabling applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation. The work builds upon established kernel embedding techniques and GMMN, with the key innovation being the adaptation of MMD to conditional settings.

The paper demonstrates several strengths: the approach is technically sound with clear theoretical foundations, the implementation is straightforward, and the empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art methods on MNIST and SVHN for classification tasks. The authors have addressed previous reviewer concerns regarding low-dimensional latent spaces, scalability, and utility comparison. The Bayesian distillation application is novel and interesting, though limited to a single dataset.

However, several significant issues require clarification. The mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear, particularly regarding the dot notation's meaning (matrix vs. Hadamard product). The symbol C is overloaded for both conditional embedding and other purposes, causing confusion. Crucially, Theorem 3 lacks a proper reference or proof, undermining the theoretical foundation. While the empirical results are adequate for digit and face generation tasks, the lack of evaluation on more challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) limits the claimed generality.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, with sufficient detail for reproducibility. The originality is moderate—it extends existing work rather than introducing a fundamentally novel approach. The significance is somewhat constrained by the limited scope of empirical validation, though the conditional modeling framework could benefit generative modeling research.

Overall, this is a technically competent paper that makes a modest contribution to conditional generative modeling. While the core idea is sound, the theoretical gaps and limited empirical validation prevent it from being a strong contribution to NIPS. I recommend rejection pending clarification of theoretical issues and additional experiments on more challenging datasets. The work would benefit from addressing the clarity issues with Equation (2), resolving the symbol overload, and providing a proper proof for Theorem 3.
