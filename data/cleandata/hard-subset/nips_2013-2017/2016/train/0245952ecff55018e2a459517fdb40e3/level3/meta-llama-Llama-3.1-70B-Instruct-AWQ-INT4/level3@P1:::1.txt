This paper proposes a novel approach to deep generative modeling using conditional maximum mean discrepancy (cMMD). The authors introduce Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend the capability of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) to learn conditional distributions. The paper provides a thorough mathematical formulation of the cMMD criterion and demonstrates its effectiveness on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the limitations of existing methods and how their approach addresses these limitations. The mathematical exposition is rigorous, but it can be challenging to follow at times. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering both discriminative and generative performance on standard benchmark tasks. The results are promising, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art methods.

However, there are some concerns regarding the paper's clarity and reproducibility. The model description could be more detailed, providing sufficient information for practitioners to exactly reproduce the evaluated models. Additionally, the authors should provide reference code to strengthen the paper and facilitate reproducibility. Furthermore, the experimental tasks are somewhat small, and it would be beneficial to evaluate the method on more challenging tasks.

In terms of originality, the paper presents an interesting and relevant extension in deep generative modeling. The use of cMMD as a training objective is novel and has the potential to improve the performance of deep generative models. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the connections between cMMD and other related methods, such as MMD.
