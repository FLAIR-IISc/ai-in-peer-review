

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend the GMMN framework to conditional generation through a kernel-embedding of conditional moments. The authors derive a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion that enables optimization using the same back-propagation machinery as unconditional GMMN. The method is evaluated across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation tasks, demonstrating competitive performance.

The paper makes a technically sound contribution that builds upon the GMMN work of Li et al. (ICML 2015) while addressing a natural extension to conditional distributions. The derivation of the CMMD objective is non-trivial yet well-executed, and the empirical estimator is carefully developed. The framework is elegantly simple to implement (Algorithm 1) and applicable to practitioners using deep generative models.

Quality is strong: The theoretical derivation is clear, the experimental evaluation covers multiple relevant tasks with thorough comparisons to strong baselines, and the authors appropriately acknowledge limitations (e.g., the need for regularization terms). The paper is well-written with a helpful appendix containing methodological and experimental details.

Clarity is excellent: The paper is logically structured, with clear explanations of the conditional embedding framework and the CMMD objective. The experimental setup and results are presented comprehensively.

Originality is moderate: While the conditional extension is a natural progression from GMMN, the non-obvious derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator provides a meaningful technical contribution. The paper adequately contextualizes this work against conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) and other conditional generative approaches.

Significance is strong: The framework addresses a critical gap in moment-matching networks for conditional generation, which is essential for predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation. The ease of implementation and competitive performance across diverse tasks suggest high practical relevance.

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling by extending GMMN to conditional settings while maintaining simplicity. The technical soundness, clear presentation, and practical utility make this a strong candidate for acceptance. The main limitation is that the comparison to more recent conditional generative models (e.g., conditional VAEs) could be more extensive, though the paper's focus on moment-matching rather than variational approaches is justified. Overall, this is a well-executed, significant contribution with clear practical value.
