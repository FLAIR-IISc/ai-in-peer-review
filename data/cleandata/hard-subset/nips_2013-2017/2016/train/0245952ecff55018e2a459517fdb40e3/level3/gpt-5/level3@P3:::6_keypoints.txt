- Proposal: CGMMN extends GMMN to conditional distributions using kernel conditional mean embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective that matches conditional embedding operators CY|X across data and model.

- Implementation: CMMD admits a compact trace form computed from Gram matrices; inverses (K + λI)−1 depend only on conditioning variables, enabling standard SGD/backprop through a conditional generator f(x,h;w).

- Relation to prior work: Builds on GMMN and kernel conditional mean embeddings; complements cGANs/CVAEs by offering a stable, non-adversarial kernel-based formulation.

- Empirical evaluation: Tested on classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation (student CGMMN mimicking teacher predictive distributions).

Strengths
- Conceptual clarity: CMMD is a natural conditional analogue of MMD with a clean operator-norm to trace-form derivation and useful implementation insight about K-matrices.

- Addresses per-condition sparsity: Operator matching couples all conditionals, avoiding wasteful per-x training and handling continuous x.

- Broad applicability: Competitive classification, convincing conditional generation (including autoencoder code space), and promising Bayesian distillation (no accuracy loss on Boston Housing).

- Simple training: No adversarial min–max game; compatible with standard deep architectures.

Weaknesses and concerns
- Computational cost: CMMD requires inverting minibatch Gram matrices (O(batch^3)), likely needing large minibatches; paper omits sensitivity analysis and resource reporting; scalable approximations (Nyström, RFF+ridge, CG, Woodbury) are unaddressed.

- Missing baselines: No direct comparison to naive per-condition GMMN with shared parameters on low-cardinality discrete x, nor to cGAN/CVAE for conditional generation.

- Clarity issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow; operator domains, adjoints, and Theorem 3 assumptions need explicit statements and intuition.

- Minor technical/notation errors: Incorrect wording about MMD estimator being “unbiased” asymptotically; conditional embedding operator notation differs from reference [29] and needs alignment and precise operator definitions.

Quality
- Technically sound with a correct, differentiable CMMD objective, but empirical claims weakened by lack of computational analysis and incomplete baselines.

Clarity
- Moderately clear overall; theoretical sections should be more self-contained and include derivation sketches and an appendix on computational approximations; fix estimator and notation issues.

Originality
- Novel and nontrivial extension of GMMN to conditional modeling via conditional mean embeddings.

Significance
- Potentially significant as a stable, simple alternative for conditional generative modeling and Bayesian distillation if scalability and baseline comparisons are addressed.

Suggestions for improvement
- Add naive per-condition GMMN baseline (shared params) and cGAN/CVAE comparisons; report training time/memory and minibatch sensitivity; evaluate scalable CMMD variants (Nyström, random features + ridge); clarify operator notation and derivations; include a continuous-conditioning benchmark.

Pros for acceptance
- Principled extension of MMD to conditional models; simple backprop-compatible training without adversarial instability; diverse and promising empirical results including Bayesian distillation.

Cons for acceptance
- Missing key baselines and limited analysis of computational scalability; notational inconsistencies and clarity problems in theoretical sections.

Overall
- Solid, timely contribution; lean toward acceptance if authors address clarity, minor technical fixes, and strengthen experimental/scalability evaluations, otherwise borderline but promising.
