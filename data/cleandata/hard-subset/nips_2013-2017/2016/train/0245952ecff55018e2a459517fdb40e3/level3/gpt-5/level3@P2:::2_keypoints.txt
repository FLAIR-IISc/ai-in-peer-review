- Paper extends GMMN to conditional setting by training y = f(x,h;w) via conditional MMD (CMMD) between Pdata(Y|X) and Pmodel(Y|X).
- CMMD objective measures HS norm between conditional mean embedding operators and is computed using kernel Gram matrices enabling backprop through deep generators.
- Experiments: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston Housing).
- Builds on MMD and kernel mean embeddings and generalizes GMMN; positioned as an adversary-free alternative to cGANs and CVAEs.
- Strength: principled, kernel-based conditional objective with a sufficiency condition linking operator matching to conditional matching.
- Strength: simple sampling/training loop compatible with CNN/MLP and stable optimization without adversarial training.
- Strength: demonstrates breadth of use cases—classification, conditional sampling, and distillation—with competitive empirical results.
- Weakness: presentation of CMMD and Eq.(2) is formal and lacks intuition, derivation steps, and a clear path to the implemented mini-batch algorithm.
- Weakness: computational costs not quantified—per-batch Gram construction and O(m^3) inversion, numerical stability, and regularization issues are unaddressed.
- Weakness: experimental evaluation lacks quantitative metrics for conditional generation, ablation studies (batch size, λ, kernels), and direct comparisons to cGAN/CVAE baselines.
- Weakness: methodological details (kernel/bandwidth selection, λ choice, batch matching, perturbation use) are underspecified in the main text.
- Quality: core objective is technically sound but practical estimator, gradient correctness, and efficiency remain ambiguously documented.
- Clarity: overall writing is fluent but needs more intuition for the conditional objective, a clearer derivation of Eq.(2), and explicit algorithm mapping.
- Originality: moderate—applies known conditional mean embeddings to produce a practical deep conditional generator, distinct from adversarial/variational methods.
- Significance: moderate—broad applicability and competitive results, but limited by missing baselines and quantitative evaluation.
- Requested clarification: provide an intuitive, stepwise derivation of Eq.(2) from CMMD and empirical conditional operators with Tikhonov regularization.
- Requested clarification: detailed mapping from Eq.(2) to the minibatch algorithm—data/model sampling, kernel choices, Gram computations, regularization/inversion, loss evaluation, and gradient flow.
- Requested clarification: quantify complexity and stability, recommend approximations (Nyström, random features) for larger batches, and specify λ selection and numerical methods (Cholesky).
- Requested clarification: specify kernel choices and bandwidth heuristics, whether x batches are shared, and details of any input perturbations used.
- Pros for acceptance: principled general conditional objective, simple and stable training, broad applicability, competitive predictive results, and useful distillation application.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient intuition and algorithmic specification, lack of computational analysis, and limited experimental comparisons for conditional generation.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept pending additions of intuitive derivation, algorithmic mapping, computational analysis, and kernel/hyperparameter clarifications.
