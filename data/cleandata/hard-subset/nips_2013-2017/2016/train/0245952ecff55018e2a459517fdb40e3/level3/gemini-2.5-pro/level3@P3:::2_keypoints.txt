- Contribution: Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective to learn p(y|x) via kernel mean embeddings in RKHS.  
- Model/Training: Trains a neural generator taking x and noise h to produce y, optimizing CMMD with mini-batch SGD and backprop.  
- Evaluation: Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and Bayesian model distillation with competitive results.  

Strengths:
- Important problem: extending moment-matching networks to conditional generation is a meaningful and useful advance.  
- Principled approach: CMMD is a non-trivial, theoretically motivated extension of MMD using conditional mean operators.  
- Broad validation: Experiments span multiple datasets/tasks, supporting the method’s versatility.

Weaknesses:
- Overall clarity: Paper is hard to follow and impedes understanding and reproducibility of the main technical ideas.  
- Lack of intuition: Fails to provide an accessible, intuitive explanation of CMMD analogous to unconditional MMD.  
- Opaque derivation: Equation (2), the empirical CMMD estimator, is presented without a clear derivation from the Hilbert–Schmidt norm.  
- Insufficient algorithmic detail: Missing precise mini-batch computations, efficient computation of kernel matrices/inverses, complexity analysis, and exact backpropagation path for gradients.

Requested clarifications:
- Provide a step-by-step, intuitive derivation of Equation (2) from the squared Hilbert–Schmidt norm, explaining each term (especially (K+λI)⁻¹).  
- Give a detailed mapping from Equation (2) to the algorithm: matrix dimensions for batch size B, explicit ∂L̂_CMMD/∂w, and discussion of computational complexity and handling of O(B^3) matrix inversions.

Summary & recommendation:
- Promising and novel idea with strong experiments, but severe clarity and reproducibility issues; currently borderline with a lean toward rejection, contingent on substantial revisions addressing the above points.
