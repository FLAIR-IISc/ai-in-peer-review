This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a method for learning conditional distributions within a deep generative modeling framework. The work is a natural and significant extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN), which use Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) to match joint distributions. The core contribution is a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) loss, derived from conditional kernel mean embeddings, which allows the model to learn P(Y|X). This positions the work as an alternative to other conditional deep generative models, such as conditional VAEs and GANs, filling an important gap in the moment-matching literature.

The paper has several notable strengths. The proposed CMMD objective is a principled and well-motivated extension of MMD to the conditional setting. The experimental evaluation demonstrates the model's versatility across multiple tasks, including classification, conditional generation, and an interesting application to Bayesian model distillation. The results on generating digits and faces are competent, showing performance comparable to the original GMMN and successfully validating the core idea. The authors also appear to have addressed previous concerns regarding the model's scalability and utility.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses that diminish its impact. The clarity needs significant improvement in key areas. First, the connection between the CMMD objective in Equation (2) and the weighted MMD interpretation in Remark 1 is opaque. The dot notation's meaning (e.g., matrix vs. Hadamard product) is not specified, making the intuition difficult to follow. Second, the overloading of the symbol 'C' is confusing. Most critically, Theorem 3, which justifies that matching conditional embedding operators implies matching conditional distributions, is presented without a proof or a citation. This is a major theoretical gap that undermines the work's technical soundness.

While the experiments are sufficient to show proof-of-concept, they lack the rigor to demonstrate a significant advance. The Bayesian distillation is tested on only one small dataset, leaving its generality unclear. For the primary generative task, wider adoption of this method would require stronger empirical results on more challenging, standard benchmarks like CIFAR-10 or LSUN.

In summary, while the core idea is novel and promising, the paper is currently hampered by significant clarity issues and a critical theoretical omission. These issues should be addressed before publication.
