- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD metric to learn conditional distributions.  
- Treats all conditionals as a family and matches the conditional embedding operator directly to avoid per-condition data sparsity.  
- Particularly useful for continuous conditioning variables where per-conditional GMMNs suffer from data sparsity.  
- Provides a clean formulation of Conditional MMD that can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD.  
- K matrices in the Conditional MMD are independent of model parameters, allowing standard backpropagation for optimization.  
- Conditional MMD may require large minibatches to reduce stochastic minibatch noise, increasing computational cost.  
- Experimental results are promising: competitive performance in classification, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Paper is technically sound and original, with potential for significant impact in tasks involving conditional distributions.  
- Presentation has clarity issues—Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and required consulting references.  
- Minor mistakes: incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased and an inconsistency in the conditional embedding operator definition versus reference [29].  
- Overall recommendation: novel and effective approach with strong experiments, tempered by minor presentation and computational concerns.
