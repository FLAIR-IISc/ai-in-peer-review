## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper extends Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting, introducing Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN). The core idea is to leverage the MMD criterion for learning conditional distributions, offering an alternative to GANs and variational methods. The authors demonstrate the applicability of CGMMN across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, reporting competitive performance on various datasets. While the extension of MMD to the conditional setting is a valuable contribution, the paper suffers from significant clarity and explanation issues, hindering a full assessment of its novelty and impact.

The initial presentation is heavily mathematical, immediately diving into formal definitions without sufficient intuitive motivation for the conditional objective (CMMD). While the authors acknowledge the simplicity of the original GMMN objective, they fail to provide an equally accessible explanation for the conditional case. The derivation of Equation (2), the core CMMD objective, is presented without sufficient context, assumptions, or practical implications. It’s unclear *why* this specific formulation arises and what its limitations might be. Furthermore, the paper lacks a clear, step-by-step explanation of how Equation (2) translates into the implemented algorithm. The supplemental material is mentioned, but is reportedly difficult to follow, exacerbating this issue. This makes it challenging to understand the actual computations performed and assess their computational efficiency, a crucial aspect for practical adoption.

Despite these weaknesses, the extension of moment matching to the conditional setting is a worthwhile endeavor. The experimental results, while not overwhelmingly superior to existing methods, demonstrate the viability of the approach. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is particularly interesting. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
*   Demonstrated applicability across multiple tasks.
*   Competitive performance compared to existing methods.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Lack of intuitive explanation for the conditional objective (CMMD).
*   Insufficiently detailed derivation and justification of Equation (2).
*   Unclear mapping from the theoretical formulation to the implemented algorithm.
*   Limited discussion of computational efficiency.



**I request the authors to address the following points:**

1.  **Provide an intuitive derivation of Equation (2).** Explain the reasoning behind each step, the underlying assumptions, and the practical implications of the resulting formulation. A non-technical explanation would be highly beneficial.
2.  **Offer a clear, step-by-step mapping from Equation (2) to the practical algorithm.** Detail how the equation is discretized, how the kernel matrices are computed, and how the gradient is calculated and applied during training. A pseudocode snippet would be helpful.



Overall, while the core idea is promising, the lack of clarity and detailed explanation significantly detracts from the paper’s quality. Addressing the requested clarifications would substantially improve the paper and allow for a more informed assessment of its contribution. I currently lean towards **reject** with encouragement to revise and resubmit.
