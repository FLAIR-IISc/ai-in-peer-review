- Proposal: CGMMN extends GMMN to model p(y|x) by matching conditional mean embeddings in RKHS via a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.
- Method: CMMD is defined as the squared Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean operators, with a trace-form empirical loss using Gram matrices; a deep generator takes x and noise h and is trained by backprop.
- Prior work: Builds on kernel conditional embeddings (Song et al., Grunewalder et al.) and generalizes GMMN; contrasted with GANs, VAEs, and other deep generative models.
- Applications: Demonstrated on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (label-conditioned MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation (“Bayesian dark knowledge”).
- Strength — conceptual: CMMD offers a clean, kernel-based conditional matching objective that avoids adversarial min–max and variational bounds.
- Strength — practical training: Mini-batch CMMD with per-batch (K+λI)−1; gradients only flow through outputs y via Y-kernel so X-kernels need not be differentiable.
- Strength — versatility: Single framework applied to classification, conditional generation, and knowledge distillation.
- Strength — novelty: The trace-form CMMD derivation is useful and appears novel in deep generative modeling.
- Weakness — centering: Paper omits discussion of centering the conditional operator estimator, raising concerns about bias, relation to ridge regression, and gradient stability.
- Weakness — empirical competitiveness: CGMMN-CNN lags behind SOTA discriminative models and is slightly worse than a strong generative baseline (CMMVA).
- Weakness — generative evaluation: Only qualitative samples shown; lacks quantitative metrics and direct comparisons to conditional GANs/VAEs.
- Weakness — scalability: Per-batch cost scales cubically due to (K+λI)−1; no discussion of batch sizes, runtimes, or scalable approximations (Nyström, RFF).
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: No analysis of kernel/bandwidth selection or sensitivity to λ.
- Weakness — limited distillation results: Only one real dataset reported, with no runtime, calibration, or uncertainty-quality metrics.
- Weakness — clarity/typos: Several typographical errors and notational overload that need cleanup.
- Quality assessment: CMMD is mathematically motivated and optimization is sound, but estimator centering/consistency should be explicitly discussed.
- Clarity assessment: Generally well-organized but needs explicit clarification on centering, kernel choices and hyperparameters, λ selection, and complexity analysis.
- Originality: Novel integration of conditional mean embeddings with deep conditional generators trained by CMMD, extending GMMN to conditionals.
- Significance: Potentially impactful as a stable, non-adversarial alternative, but current empirical evidence and baselines limit impact.
- Suggested improvements: State whether Gram matrices were centered, add quantitative generative metrics and baselines, provide kernel/λ sensitivity and scalability analyses, expand distillation experiments, and fix typos.
- Pros for acceptance: Principled extension of GMMN, broad applicability including distillation, and avoidance of adversarial training instabilities.
- Cons against acceptance: Not SOTA predictive performance, missing quantitative generative comparisons, and lacking clarity on centering, scalability, and hyperparameter sensitivity.
- Post-rebuttal update: Authors clarified minibatch Gram centering and committed to ablations and quantitative results, largely addressing soundness and clarity concerns.
