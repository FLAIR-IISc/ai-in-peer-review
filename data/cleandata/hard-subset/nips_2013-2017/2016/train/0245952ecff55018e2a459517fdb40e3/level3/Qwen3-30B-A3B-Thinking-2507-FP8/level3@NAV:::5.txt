

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend GMMNs to model conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in an RKHS, building on Song et al. (2009). The approach defines a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion for training, allowing the model to learn conditional distributions while maintaining the simplicity of back-propagation-based optimization. The authors evaluate CGMMN on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

The paper demonstrates strong theoretical motivation, extending previous work on kernel embeddings and generative moment matching. The core idea of using CMMD to match conditional distributions represents a meaningful extension of GMMNs, addressing a gap in the literature for conditional generative models. However, significant issues undermine the paper's technical quality. The empirical conditional operator $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ omits critical centering terms involving estimators of $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$, which are assumed but not justified. This omission raises fundamental questions about gradient computation and how experiments handled centering, potentially invalidating the theoretical foundation. The authors should clarify this in the revision.

The experimental results are disappointing: CGMMN shows higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on both MNIST and SVHN datasets. The generative performance evaluation is incomplete, as the paper lacks comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), making it impossible to assess whether CGMMN offers any advantages in generation tasks. Additionally, the paper contains several typos ("Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").

While the approach is novel and well-motivated, the technical shortcomings and underwhelming empirical results significantly limit the paper's contribution. The authors have addressed these concerns in their revision, but the initial submission's quality falls short of NIPS standards. The paper would benefit from a thorough theoretical justification of the centering terms, more comprehensive experimental comparisons, and correction of the typographical errors.

Pro: Novel extension of GMMNs to conditional distributions, well-motivated, clear generative process.
Con: Theoretical gap in centering terms, disappointing empirical results, incomplete comparisons, typos.
