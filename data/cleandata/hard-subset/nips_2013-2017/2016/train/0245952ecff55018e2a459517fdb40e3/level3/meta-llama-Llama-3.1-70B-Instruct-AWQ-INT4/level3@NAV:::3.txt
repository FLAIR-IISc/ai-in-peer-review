This paper proposes a novel approach to learning conditional generative models, dubbed Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN). The authors extend the concept of Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) to conditional distributions, introducing Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD). They demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

**Quality**: The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its connections to previous work. The experimental evaluation is thorough, covering multiple datasets and tasks.

**Clarity**: The paper is well-written, and the organization is logical. The authors provide sufficient background information on the relevant concepts, making the paper accessible to non-experts. However, some notations and equations could be clarified, such as the dot notation in Equation (2).

**Originality**: The paper presents a novel approach to learning conditional generative models, which is a significant contribution to the field. The extension of MMD to conditional distributions is a non-trivial contribution, and the authors demonstrate its effectiveness on various tasks.

**Significance**: The results are important, as they demonstrate the potential of CGMMN for various applications. The authors show that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art methods on multiple tasks, including predictive modeling and generative tasks. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is particularly interesting, as it provides a new approach to distilling knowledge from complex Bayesian models.

**Minor issues**:

* The symbol C is overloaded, which can be confusing.
* Theorem 3 lacks a reference or provided proof.
* The authors could provide more details on the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method.

**Arguments for acceptance**:

* The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of generative models.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed method on various tasks.
* The paper is well-written and accessible to non-experts.

**Arguments against acceptance**:

* The paper could benefit from more clarification on some notations and equations.
* The authors could provide more details on the computational cost and scalability of the proposed method.
