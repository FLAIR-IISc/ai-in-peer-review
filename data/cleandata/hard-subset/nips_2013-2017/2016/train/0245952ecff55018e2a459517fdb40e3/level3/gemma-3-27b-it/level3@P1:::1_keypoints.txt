- Introduces CGMMN, extending MMD to learn conditional distributions p(y|x).
- Trains conditional MMD objective with backprop, extending GMMN to conditional generation.
- Positioned as a non-adversarial alternative to GANs and complementary to conditional GANs/CVAEs.
- Theoretical grounding provided via kernel methods and RKHS; conditional MMD derivation is well-motivated.
- Empirical results on MNIST, SVHN, and Boston Housing show competitive performance across tasks.
- Demonstrated use for predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear explanations, figures, and tables.
- Training computationally expensive due to kernel computations and matrix inversions; scalability unclear.
- No publicly available code, which hinders reproducibility.
- Missing detailed network architecture and hyperparameter specifications, complicating exact replication.
- Some mathematical exposition (kernel embeddings) is dense and hard to follow for non-experts.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically validated, and fills a gap in conditional MMD modeling.
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of code, computational cost concerns, and insufficient model detail.
- Overall recommendation: accept, with strong encouragement to release code and add architecture/hyperparameter details.
