- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional MMD (CMMD) to learn conditional distributions by matching the conditional embedding operator.  
- Method avoids training separate GMMNs per condition, addressing data sparsity for continuous conditioning.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation tasks.  
- Theoretical foundation of CMMD is well-motivated and builds on kernel embedding techniques.  
- Conditional MMD formulation is mathematically clean and can be computed efficiently via kernel matrices.  
- Experimental results on MNIST, SVHN, and Bayesian distillation are competitive with strong baselines.  
- Demonstrated advantage over naive per-condition training, especially for continuous conditioning.  
- Presentation of key concepts (Sections 2.3 and 3.1) is hard to follow and relies on external references.  
- Incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased; it is asymptotically unbiased.  
- Inconsistency between the paper’s definition of the conditional embedding operator and reference [29] needs clarification.  
- CMMD may require large minibatches for stability, which raises significant computational cost due to matrix inversions.  
- Technical quality is solid, but theoretical exposition could be strengthened.  
- Originality is moderate—extends existing MMD-based generative models rather than introducing fundamentally new concepts.  
- Significance is demonstrated across applications, but computational demands may limit practical adoption.  
- Recommendation: fix presentation and theoretical errors and provide a detailed discussion of computational trade-offs.
