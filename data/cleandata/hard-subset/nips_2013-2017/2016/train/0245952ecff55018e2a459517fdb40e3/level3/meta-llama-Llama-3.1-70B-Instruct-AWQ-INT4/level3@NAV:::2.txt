This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting. The authors propose a new training objective, Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), to measure the difference between the empirical and generated conditional distributions. The paper presents a comprehensive evaluation of CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, making it easy to follow and understand. The paper adequately informs the reader about the proposed method, its strengths, and its weaknesses.

The originality of the paper lies in its extension of GMMN to the conditional setting, which is non-trivial compared to the single-example conditioning. The authors provide a novel combination of familiar techniques, including kernel mean embedding and maximum mean discrepancy, to address the challenges of conditional distribution modeling.

The significance of the paper is evident in its potential applications to various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The authors demonstrate the competitiveness of CGMMN with state-of-the-art methods in these tasks, making it a valuable contribution to the field.

However, the paper has some limitations. The authors begin with heavy formal math, which may be challenging for non-experts to follow. The paper lacks an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective, making it difficult for readers to understand the underlying principles. Additionally, the authors do not provide a clear step-by-step mapping from the optimized formula to the practical algorithm, which may make it challenging for readers to implement the method.

Overall, I would recommend this paper for acceptance, with some minor revisions to address the limitations mentioned above. Specifically, I would suggest that the authors:

1. Provide an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective to make it more accessible to non-experts.
2. Clarify the step-by-step mapping from the optimized formula to the practical algorithm.
3. Consider adding more visualizations or illustrations to help readers understand the underlying principles and the proposed method.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
* The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of CGMMN on various tasks.
* The paper demonstrates the competitiveness of CGMMN with state-of-the-art methods.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper lacks an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective.
* The authors do not provide a clear step-by-step mapping from the optimized formula to the practical algorithm.
* The paper may be challenging for non-experts to follow due to the heavy formal math.
