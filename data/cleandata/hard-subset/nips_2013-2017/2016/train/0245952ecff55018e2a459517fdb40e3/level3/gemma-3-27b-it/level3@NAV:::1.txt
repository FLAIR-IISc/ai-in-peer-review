## Review of Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks

This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of existing Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting. The core idea is to leverage a conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to learn a conditional distribution given input variables. The authors demonstrate the method’s applicability to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, evaluating it on several small benchmark datasets.

The work builds upon a recent surge in deep generative modeling, notably following the success of GANs and the introduction of GMMNs as a more stable alternative. Extending MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting is a natural and relevant progression, particularly given the widespread use of conditional distributions in tasks like prediction and controllable generation. The approach aligns with the broader trend of utilizing kernel methods within deep learning frameworks to avoid restrictive distributional assumptions.

**Strengths:** The paper tackles an interesting problem with a principled approach. Extending the GMMN framework to the conditional case opens up new possibilities for applications. The mathematical formulation of CMMD is sound, and the application to Bayesian dark knowledge is a particularly creative use case. The experimental results, while on small datasets, demonstrate competitive performance, suggesting the method's potential. The paper is reasonably clear in its overall presentation of ideas.

**Weaknesses:** The mathematical exposition, particularly surrounding the CMMD derivation and tensor operations, can be challenging to follow. The experimental evaluation, while adequate, is limited to relatively small datasets. A significant concern is the lack of a runtime comparison with existing methods, such as DSN, given that training involves computationally expensive kernel calculations. Reproducibility is also a major issue. The model description lacks sufficient detail to allow for exact replication of the reported results. The authors mention implementation details, such as layer sizes and activation functions, but crucial aspects like the kernel used, regularization parameters, and optimization schedule are not fully specified.

**Arguments for Acceptance:** The extension of MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting is a novel and potentially impactful contribution. The theoretical foundation is solid, and the experimental results are promising.

**Arguments against Acceptance:** The lack of a runtime analysis and the reproducibility concerns significantly weaken the paper. The limited scope of the experimental evaluation also raises questions about the generalizability of the results.

**Overall:** I recommend **rejection** in its current form. While the core idea is valuable and the paper demonstrates promise, the reproducibility issues and the lack of a detailed performance analysis (including runtime) are significant drawbacks. Addressing these concerns, specifically by providing reference code and a thorough runtime comparison, would substantially strengthen the paper and warrant reconsideration. The authors should also clarify the mathematical derivations to improve clarity and accessibility.
