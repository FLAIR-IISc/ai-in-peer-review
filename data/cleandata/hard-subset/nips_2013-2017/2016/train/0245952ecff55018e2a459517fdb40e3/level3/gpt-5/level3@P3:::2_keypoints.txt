- Paper extends GMMN to conditional setting by replacing MMD with Conditional MMD (CMMD) matching conditional mean embeddings CY|X via empirical operator Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤.
- Derives a minibatch loss (Eq. 2) expressed in Gram matrices and trains conditional generator y = f(x,h;w) by backpropagating through the Y-kernel.
- Offers a non-adversarial alternative to conditional GANs and VAEs, building on conditional kernel mean embedding literature.
- Key conceptual contribution: principled extension of MMD to match conditional distributions and avoid adversarial min-max training.
- Sound use of RKHS tools: CMMD reduces to Gram-matrix traces and allows non-differentiable X-kernels since gradients flow only through the Y-kernel.
- Practical training: simple stochastic gradient procedure that reuses minibatch x’s for stability.
- Broad applicability: demonstrates prediction, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation with competitive results.
- Main clarity issue: presentation starts with heavy formalism and lacks an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective.
- Eq. (2) is presented without derivation, assumptions, or discussion of implications like weighting structure and conditions when K_s = K_d.
- Algorithmic mapping from Eq. (2) to implementation is underspecified: gradients, computational cost, and kernel recommendations are not clearly given.
- Computational efficiency concern: cubic dependence on minibatch size due to (K+λI)−1 is un-analyzed; no scalable approximations or solver strategies discussed.
- Empirical scope limited: little sensitivity analysis for λ, kernel bandwidths, or minibatch size; minimal comparisons to cGAN/CVAE for conditional generation.
- Quality: CMMD objective is theoretically grounded and training recipe is sound, but core derivation and algorithmic details need to be explicit for reproducibility.
- Clarity recommendations: add intuitive CMMD narrative, step-by-step derivation of Eq. (2), explicit gradients and complexity, and clear notation for data vs synthetic sets.
- Originality: moderate — conditional mean embeddings are established, novelty lies in integrating them as a loss for deep conditional generators.
- Significance: moderate — useful non-adversarial baseline with appealing distillation application; impact would grow with clearer exposition and scaling analysis.
- Intuition: CMMD acts like MMD with x-dependent weights via kernel ridge regression α(x) = (K+λI)−1k_x, causing nearby x’s to contribute more to μ̂Y|x.
- Derivation sketch: CMMD = ||A−B||_HS^2 with A,B = ΦK̃^{-1}Υ⊤ leads to trace form in Eq. (2) using HS inner-product identities and Gram matrices; requires universal kernels and λ>0.
- Minibatch recipe: reuse same x for data and synthetic to share K and need one inverse per batch; build K, L_d, L_s, L_ds and compute CMMD via traces.
- Kernels: choose kX (e.g., RBF) for inputs and differentiable kY (RBF/IMQ) for outputs; median heuristic and Tikhonov λ recommended.
- Gradients: compute S = K̃^{-1} K K̃^{-1} and backpropagate ∂LCMMD^2/∂L_s = S, ∂/∂L_ds = −2S, then chain-rule through kY to y′ and f.
- Complexity per batch: O(b^2) Gram builds, O(b^3) Cholesky solve, O(b^2 d_y) gradient formation, memory O(b^2); suggest Nyström/RFF or Cholesky reuse for scaling.
- Practical tips: normalize features, anneal σ, tune λ (1e−3–1e−1), use Adam, clip gradients; K̃^{-1} independent of w so need not be backpropagated.
- Requests to authors: provide intuitive derivation of Eq. (2), explicit algorithmic mapping (minibatch, kernels, gradients, complexity), and clear notation for data vs synthetic.
- Additional suggestions: improve notation consistency, discuss reuse vs perturb x across sets, provide ablations on λ/bandwidth/batch size, and stronger cGAN/CVAE comparisons.
- Pros for acceptance: principled non-adversarial objective, broad applicability, elegant use of conditional embeddings, and simple training loop.
- Cons for acceptance: insufficient explanation of Eq. (2) and algorithmic details, limited scalability analysis, and weaker empirical comparisons to other conditional DGMs.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept contingent on clearer derivation and precise algorithmic/practical details to enable reproduction and assess efficiency.
