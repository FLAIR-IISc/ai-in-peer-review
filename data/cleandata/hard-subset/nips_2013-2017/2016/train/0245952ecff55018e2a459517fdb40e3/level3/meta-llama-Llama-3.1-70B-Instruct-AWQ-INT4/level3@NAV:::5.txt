This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a novel extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) that learns conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS). The authors propose a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion as the training objective, which measures the difference between the kernel mean embedding of an empirical conditional distribution and that of the generative model.

The paper is well-motivated, and the approach is novel and interesting. The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background and related work, making it easy to understand the context and significance of their contribution. The technical sections are also well-written, with a good balance of mathematical derivations and intuitive explanations.

The experimental results are somewhat disappointing, as CGMMN has higher predictive error than state-of-the-art Conditional Maximum Margin Variational Autoencoder (CMMVA) on both MNIST and SVHN datasets. However, the authors do demonstrate competitive performance in generative tasks, such as image generation and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

One of the main concerns is the definition of the empirical conditional operator, which omits the terms involving estimators of μ_X and μ_Y. The authors assume centering in RKHS but do not justify it, which raises questions about gradient computation and how experiments handled centering. The authors should clarify this point and provide more details on the implementation.

Additionally, there are some minor issues with the paper, such as typos (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the") and a lack of comparison to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs).

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the approach is interesting and novel. However, the experimental results are not as strong as expected, and some technical details need to be clarified.

**Quality:** 7/10 (technically sound, but some minor issues with the definition of the empirical conditional operator and lack of comparison to other methods)

**Clarity:** 8/10 (well-written, but some minor typos and lack of clarity on implementation details)

**Originality:** 9/10 (novel extension of GMMN to conditional distributions)

**Significance:** 8/10 (competitive performance in generative tasks, but not as strong as expected in predictive tasks)

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Novel and interesting approach to learning conditional distributions
* Well-motivated and clearly written introduction to the background and related work
* Competitive performance in generative tasks

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Disappointing predictive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods
* Lack of clarity on implementation details and definition of the empirical conditional operator
* Minor issues with typos and lack of comparison to other conditional generative methods
