- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs) by extending GMMNs to conditional modeling via a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.  
- CMMD is derived from kernel embeddings of conditional distributions, learning a conditional operator that maps condition embeddings to output embeddings.  
- The objective minimizes the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between estimated conditional embedding operators for data and model.  
- Correctly treats P(Y|x) as a single object to learn, avoiding separate models per condition and handling continuous/high-cardinality conditioning.  
- Novel and compelling application to distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.  
- CMMD loss has a clean kernel-matrix form; crucially, (K+λI)⁻¹ depends only on conditioning variables, simplifying gradients and backpropagation through outputs.  
- Experiments show competitive performance on classification, conditional image generation, and model distillation, validating the approach.  
- Presentation is unclear: sections on kernel embedding and CMMD (2.3, 3.1) are dense and need more intuition and links from operator theory to Eq. 2.  
- Practical concern: CMMD relies on minibatch kernel estimates; small batches give noisy gradients, large batches make O(N³) matrix inverses expensive—sensitivity and computational cost need discussion.  
- Experimental comparison is incomplete: should compare to the naive baseline of separate GMMNs per class (e.g., MNIST) to demonstrate empirical superiority.  
- Technical minor issue: paper claims an unbiased MMD estimator but uses the V-statistic; the unbiased U-statistic should be mentioned/corrected.  
- Minor inconsistency in the conditional embedding operator definition (µY|x = C_Y|X φ(x) vs. µY|x = C_Y|X k(x,·)) should be clarified.
