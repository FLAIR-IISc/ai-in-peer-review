- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Offers an alternative to GANs that avoids unstable min-max training dynamics.  
- Strong theoretical foundation grounded in kernel methods, Hilbert space embeddings, and MMD.  
- Demonstrates versatility across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation (MNIST, SVHN, generative tasks) reporting competitive performance versus baselines.  
- Paper is generally well-written and logically organized.  
- Mathematical exposition is dense and may be inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with kernel/Hilbert-space theory.  
- Insufficient analysis of computational cost and scalability, especially kernel-matrix inversion and minibatch method effectiveness.  
- Lack of publicly available code hinders reproducibility.  
- Experimental model descriptions lack precise architecture, activation, and parameter-count details for replication.  
- Limited ablation studies on kernel choices, architectures, and regularization weaken empirical analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, competitive results, and clear contributions.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reproducibility issues, unclear scalability, and potentially inaccessible math.  
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution; recommendation to accept conditional on releasing code and minor clarifications (math accessibility, runtime/architecture details).
