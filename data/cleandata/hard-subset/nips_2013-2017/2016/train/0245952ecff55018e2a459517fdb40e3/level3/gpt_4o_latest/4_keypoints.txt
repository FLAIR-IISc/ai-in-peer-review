- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN for conditional generation using kernel moment embeddings.
- Proposes a novel conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion and an empirical estimator to train CGMMN.
- Technical derivation of CMMD is sound and consistent with prior kernel and deep generative model work.
- The CMMD empirical estimator is a significant novel contribution enabling moment-matching for conditional tasks.
- Demonstrates competitive predictive performance on MNIST and SVHN, comparable to state-of-the-art DGMs.
- Shows high-quality, diverse contextual generation on MNIST and Yale Face datasets.
- Effectively distills Bayesian models without degrading predictive performance, demonstrated on the Boston housing dataset.
- Framework is simple, easy to implement (Algorithm 1), and compatible with common architectures like MLPs and CNNs.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with useful appendices for derivations and experimental details.
- Lacks deeper theoretical analysis of CMMD properties (e.g., robustness to noise, high-dimensional behavior).
- Empirical comparisons are limited—missing baselines such as conditional GANs and conditional VAEs.
- Scalability concerns due to cubic complexity of kernel Gram matrix computations; mini-batching helps but trade-offs are not fully addressed.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel conditional extension, practical CMMD criterion, thorough experiments, technical rigor, and relevance to NeurIPS.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete empirical comparisons with key baselines and unresolved scalability issues.
- Recommendation: accept—minor limitations do not outweigh the contribution, novelty, and empirical validation.
