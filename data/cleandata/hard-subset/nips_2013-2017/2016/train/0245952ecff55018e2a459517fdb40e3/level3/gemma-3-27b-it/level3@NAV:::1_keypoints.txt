- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion to learn conditional distributions.
- Builds on GMMN and extends MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting, a relevant and natural progression.
- The CMMD mathematical formulation is sound and the approach aligns with using kernel methods in deep learning.
- Creative application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation demonstrates an interesting use case.
- Experimental results on small benchmarks are promising and show competitive performance.
- Mathematical exposition (CMMD derivation and tensor operations) is difficult to follow and needs clarification.
- Experiments are limited to small datasets, raising concerns about generalizability to larger or more complex data.
- No runtime comparison with existing methods (e.g., DSN), despite potentially expensive kernel computations.
- Reproducibility is weak: crucial implementation details (kernel choice, regularization, optimization schedule) are missing.
- Paper is reasonably clear overall but lacks sufficient model/implementation detail for exact replication.
- Recommendation: rejection in current form due to reproducibility and performance-analysis shortcomings, but promising if addressed.
- Suggested fixes: provide reference code, thorough runtime comparisons, and clearer mathematical derivations.
