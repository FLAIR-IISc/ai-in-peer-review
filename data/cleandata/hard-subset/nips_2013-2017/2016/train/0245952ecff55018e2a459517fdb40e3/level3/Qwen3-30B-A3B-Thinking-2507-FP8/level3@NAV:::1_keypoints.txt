- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using conditional MMD (cMMD) to learn conditional distributions.  
- Proposes a simple generative process: sample from a simple distribution, pass through a deep network with conditional inputs, and optimize cMMD via backprop.  
- Evaluated on three tasks: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation.  
- Fills an important gap by extending MMD-based generative methods to conditional settings.  
- Builds on GMMN and conditional kernel methods and offers a simpler training procedure compared to conditional GANs.  
- Strength: clear theoretical foundation for the cMMD criterion.  
- Strength: demonstrates competitive performance across diverse tasks.  
- Strength: practically applicable to Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Strength: effectively connects to relevant prior work in the introduction and related work.  
- Weakness: mathematical exposition is sometimes difficult to follow, especially in the conditional embedding sections.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to small benchmark datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face).  
- Weakness: computational cost and runtime comparisons (e.g., vs DSN) are not provided.  
- Major concern: reproducibility—model description lacks sufficient implementation detail and no reference code is provided.  
- Overall: technically sound and original but limited significance due to modest gains and small-scale experiments; requires substantial revisions and more comprehensive comparisons before acceptance.
