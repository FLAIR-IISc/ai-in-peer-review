- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional modeling via conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Introduces a new loss based on conditional kernel mean embeddings with thorough theoretical discussion.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly explains relation to prior work.  
- Empirical results are decent and comparable to original GMMN on generative tasks (digits, faces).  
- Supervised learning results are weaker and not the primary focus of the method.  
- Bayesian distillation application is interesting but evaluated on only one dataset/model, limiting generality.  
- Main strength: flexible, versatile framework for conditional generative modeling demonstrated across tasks.  
- Limitation: mapping from Eq. (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear and notation can be confusing.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a reference or provided proof, making the claim hard to assess.  
- Needs further evaluation on more challenging datasets and tasks to fully assess strengths and limitations.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel conditional framework, flexible applicability, competitive empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing proof/reference for Theorem 3, unclear Eq.(2) mapping/notation, limited Bayesian distillation evaluation.
