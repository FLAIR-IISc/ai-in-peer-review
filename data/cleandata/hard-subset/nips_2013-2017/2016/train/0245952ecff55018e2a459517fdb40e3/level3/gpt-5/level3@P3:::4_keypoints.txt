- Extends GMMN to conditional generation by replacing MMD with a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) based on conditional mean embeddings.
- Proposes a neural conditional generator y = f(x,h;w) trained by minimizing an empirical CMMD that can be written using Gram matrices and enables backprop.
- Key practical trick: matrix inverses in the CMMD estimator depend on input X but not on model parameters w, enabling efficient training.
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation with competitive empirical results.
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings and GMMN literature and complements conditional deep generative models like cGANs and CVAEs.
- Strength: principled, conceptually clear conditional extension of MMD-based deep generative modeling.
- Strength: elegant, practical CMMD estimator with a trace–Gram formulation and mini-batch training (Algorithm 1).
- Strength: broad, relevant applications and straightforward implementation appealing to practitioners.
- Strength: well-written paper with helpful appendix and actionable algorithmic details.
- Weakness: computational scalability—CMMD requires per-minibatch matrix inversions with cubic cost in batch size; lacks runtime/complexity analysis.
- Weakness: limited theoretical guarantees—finite-sample consistency and effects of regularization λ are not analyzed in-depth.
- Weakness: generative evaluation is mainly qualitative; lacks quantitative comparisons to strong conditional baselines (cGAN, CVAE) on standard metrics.
- Weakness: predictive experiments are not state-of-the-art and lack ablations (kernels, bandwidths, noise dim, perturbed vs. exact x).
- Weakness: Bayesian distillation shown on a small dataset without uncertainty calibration metrics (NLL, predictive intervals).
- Suggestion: report computational cost/memory vs. batch size and justify λ and kernel choices; provide sensitivity analyses.
- Suggestion: compare to cGAN/CVAE on benchmark conditional generation metrics and include calibration metrics for Bayesian distillation.
- Suggestion: analyze effect of perturbed vs. exact inputs and consider scalable approximations (random features, Nyström).
- Minor: add proof sketches for CY|X ∈ F⊗G assumptions, clarify notation (s/d superscripts, N=M), and fix missing reference details.
- Arguments for acceptance: principled, practical conditional extension of GMMN with simple training and broad applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited quantitative evaluation, scalability concerns, and theoretical gaps in finite-sample analysis.
- Overall assessment: weak accept with high reviewer confidence.
