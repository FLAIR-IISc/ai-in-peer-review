- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) objective to learn conditional distributions.  
- Extends GMMNs to the conditional setting, addressing a non-trivial and valuable gap for conditional generative modeling.  
- Positions CGMMN well within related work (conditional GANs, conditional VAEs) and draws on established MMD literature.  
- Provides solid theoretical foundation for CMMD with formal results (Theorems 1–3) based on kernel embeddings.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Shows promising results on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face datasets, indicating versatility.  
- Presentation of related work is clear and situates contributions appropriately.  
- Initial presentation of CMMD is dense and lacks intuitive motivation for readers unfamiliar with kernel methods.  
- Computational details around Equation (2) are insufficient: implementation, cost, and regularization impact are unclear.  
- Derivation of Equation (2) is presented as a conclusion without stepwise development or clear assumptions.  
- Missing clear mapping from Equation (2) to the implemented algorithm, especially for mini-batch estimation and handling matrix inverses.  
- Insufficient discussion of assumptions and limitations, including sensitivity to kernel choice and scalability to large datasets.  
- Overall: technically sound, original, and significant, but clarity and practical explanation need improvement to increase impact.  
- Requested revisions: provide an intuitive, step-by-step derivation of Equation (2) and a detailed algorithm mapping covering mini-batches, kernel computations, matrix inversions, and computational bottlenecks.
