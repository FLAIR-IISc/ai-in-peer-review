- Summary: Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that train a generator by minimizing Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) via conditional mean embeddings in RKHS to model p(y|x).  
- Strength — Novelty: Extends GMMNs to conditional setting with a solid theoretical basis linking to Song et al. (2009).  
- Strength — Simple training: CMMD is a single, stable loss (no minimax adversarial optimization).  
- Strength — Versatility: Applied to prediction, contextual image generation, and model distillation, showing broad applicability.  
- Weakness — Centering ambiguity: Paper inconsistently treats centering of cross-covariance/Gram matrices; theoretical justification and implementation details are unclear and affect reproducibility and gradients.  
- Weakness — Predictive performance: CGMMN-CNN underperforms compared to CMMVA baselines on MNIST and SVHN, weakening claims of competitive performance.  
- Weakness — Generative evaluation: Lacks quantitative or comparative metrics (e.g., FID/Inception) and comparisons to strong baselines like conditional GANs/VAEs.  
- Weakness — Presentation issues: Contains typos and grammatical errors that reduce polish.  
- Detailed request: Authors must clarify whether Gram matrices were centered in implementation, justify any deviation from established theory, and improve experimental comparisons and analysis of underperformance.  
- Overall assessment: Promising, theoretically grounded contribution that avoids adversarial instability, but requires major clarification and stronger experimental validation.  
- Update: Authors clarified the centering issue satisfactorily and committed to manuscript revisions; reviewer will raise scores accordingly.
