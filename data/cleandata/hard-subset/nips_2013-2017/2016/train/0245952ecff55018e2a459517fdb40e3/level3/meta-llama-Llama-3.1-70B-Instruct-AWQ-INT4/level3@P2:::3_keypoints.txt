- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) to learn conditional distributions.  
- CMMD extends MMD by measuring differences between kernel mean embeddings of empirical conditional distributions and the generative model.  
- Theorem 3 claims equality of conditional distributions when their conditional embeddings are equal (PY|x = PZ|x for every fixed x).  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on generative tasks (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face) and shows competitive performance with state-of-the-art models.  
- Applies CGMMN to supervised learning and Bayesian distillation (Bayesian dark knowledge) to show versatility.  
- Theoretical foundation and explanation of CMMD/MMD relationship are thorough and well-presented.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear background and related-work exposition.  
- Authors responded to prior reviewer concerns and clarified parts of the revision.  
- Mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear and needs clarification (dot notation ambiguous: matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product).  
- Symbol C is overloaded (used for conditional embedding and elsewhere), causing confusion and inconsistent notation.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a reference or an explicit proof and should include one.  
- Bayesian distillation evaluation is limited to one dataset/model, so its generality is uncertain.  
- CGMMN may not be the strongest approach for supervised tasks; results are not significantly better than current state-of-the-art.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, original extension of MMD to conditionals, significant potential impact, but needs notational fixes, a proof/reference for Theorem 3, and broader empirical validation.
