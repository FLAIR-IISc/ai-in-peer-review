- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model flexible conditional distributions.  
- Introduces conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) as the training objective comparing kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Provides theoretical foundation showing CMMD is a sufficient condition for equality of conditional distributions.  
- Trains CGMMN by minimizing the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Evaluates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge tasks.  
- Reports competitive performance and state-of-the-art results on MNIST and SVHN for predictive modeling.  
- Demonstrates high-quality contextual image generation on MNIST and Yale Face datasets.  
- Strengths: novel extension of GMMN to conditional settings, solid theoretical grounding, clear writing.  
- Weakness: mathematical exposition is sometimes hard to follow and some proofs are omitted or deferred to the appendix.  
- Weakness: experiments are limited to relatively small-scale tasks and need evaluation on larger, more challenging datasets.  
- Weakness: training involves computationally expensive steps; no runtime comparison provided (e.g., against DSN).  
- Originality: CMMD and conditional extension of GMMN are novel contributions to deep generative modeling.  
- Significance: method shows potential across multiple applications and could be practically adopted given its competitive performance.  
- Clarity/Quality: generally well-written and high-quality, but mathematical and experimental detail could be improved.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions — request clearer mathematical exposition, larger-scale evaluations, and runtime comparisons.
