- Proposes a loss for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending GMMN/MMD via conditional kernel mean embeddings and demonstrates on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian distillation.  
- Previous reviewer concerns (low-dimensional latent space, scalability, utility vs. other models, minor clarifications) appear addressed in the revised text.  
- Mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear; the dot notation’s meaning (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product) must be clarified.  
- The symbol C is overloaded (used for conditional embedding and elsewhere), which is confusing and unfortunate.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 cite prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference or provided proof and needs one.  
- Empirical results are decent and comparable to the original GMMN in generative tasks.  
- Using the model for supervised learning is not the strongest application but acceptable to show versatility.  
- The Bayesian distillation application is interesting but only evaluated on one dataset/model, so generality is unclear.  
- The approach likely gains most traction in pure generative modeling.  
- Datasets used (digits and faces) suffice to show the idea works.  
- Wider adoption will require stronger results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
