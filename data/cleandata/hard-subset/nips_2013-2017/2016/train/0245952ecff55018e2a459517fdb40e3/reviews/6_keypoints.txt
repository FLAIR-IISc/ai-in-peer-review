- Paper proposes Conditional GMMNs (CGMMN) using kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD metric.  
- CGMMN evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.  
- Naive baseline: train a GMMN per conditional distribution sharing network parameters; suffers from data sparsity, especially with continuous conditioning.  
- Proposed method treats all conditionals as a family and matches the conditional embedding operator directly, avoiding per-condition data sparsity.  
- Advantage of the proposed approach is clear, though naive approach may perform reasonably in low-cardinality discrete conditions (e.g., MNIST); comparison to this baseline is suggested.  
- Conditional MMD has a clean formulation and can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters allowing standard backprop.  
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch noise, but large minibatches make computation (notably matrix inverses) expensive.  
- Experimental results for classification and Bayesian model distillation are interesting and promising.  
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow; reviewer had to consult referenced papers for clarity.  
- Minor error: statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect.  
- Minor inconsistency: the conditional embedding operator definition differs from reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition is needed.
