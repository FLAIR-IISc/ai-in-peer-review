- Paper extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting, which is non-trivial compared to trivial single-example conditioning.
- Core difficulty: matching distributions over large mini-batches of outputs to many different conditional inputs (distributions differ across conditionals).
- Authors begin with heavy formal math and lack an intuitive explanation of the conditional objective.
- The paper fails to clearly state the actual computations performed by the algorithm and their computational efficiency.
- Equation (2) (the optimized formula) is not explained in terms of derivation, assumptions, or practical implications.
- The path from Equation (2) to the implemented algorithm is insufficiently detailed; supplemental material is hard to follow.
- The non-conditional version admits a simple, intuitive explanation; the conditional case needs an equally accessible intuition and presentation.
- Reviewer requests two concrete clarifications: an intuitive derivation of Equation (2), and a clear step-by-step mapping from Equation (2) to the practical algorithm.
