- Introduces CGMMNs extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD metric.
- Core innovation: align model with the conditional embedding operator instead of matching individual conditional distributions.
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation tasks.
- Naive extension (a GMMN per conditional distribution with shared parameters) suffers from data sparsity, especially for continuous conditioning.
- Treating all conditional distributions as a unified family addresses sparsity by directly matching the conditional embedding operator.
- Naive approach could still perform reasonably in discrete-conditioning cases (e.g., MNIST with 10 classes); authors should compare against this baseline.
- Conditional MMD objective is elegantly formulated and computable similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, so backpropagation is straightforward.
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to reduce stochasticity, which raises computational cost and expensive matrix inversions for large batches.
- Experimental results (classification and Bayesian distillation) are interesting and promising.
- Presentation clarity needs improvement in Sections 2.3 and 3.1; understanding required consulting referenced papers.
- Minor issues: (1) Line 77 incorrectly claims the MMD estimator is unbiased. (2) Line 90's conditional embedding operator definition is inconsistent with reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition is needed.
