This paper extends the “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” (GMMN) framework to conditional generation. The authors derive a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion and corresponding kernel embedding for conditional distributions, enabling optimization via back-propagation, similar to the original GMMN approach. The proposed Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) are evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, demonstrating competitive performance. 

The paper is technically sound, presenting a logical extension of existing work. While the core idea of a conditional extension to GMMN is natural, the derivation of the CMMD criterion and the associated empirical estimator are non-trivial and well-presented. The paper demonstrates originality in this derivation and its practical implementation. The proposed framework appears readily applicable, as evidenced by Algorithm 1, and is likely to be a useful tool for researchers interested in deep generative models and knowledge distillation.

The paper is clearly written and well-organized, facilitating comprehension. The inclusion of an appendix provides valuable details regarding the methodology and experimental setup. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel extension to a recently proposed generative modeling framework.
*   The proposed method achieves competitive performance on a diverse set of tasks.
*   The paper is well-written, clearly explaining the technical details and providing sufficient experimental validation.
*   The approach is practical, with a straightforward implementation (Algorithm 1) and potential for broad applicability.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The experimental evaluation, while demonstrating competitiveness, does not showcase a significant performance advantage over existing state-of-the-art methods.
*   The theoretical assumptions underlying the CMMD criterion (specifically regarding the Hilbert-Schmidt properties) may not always hold in practice, although the authors acknowledge this limitation.
