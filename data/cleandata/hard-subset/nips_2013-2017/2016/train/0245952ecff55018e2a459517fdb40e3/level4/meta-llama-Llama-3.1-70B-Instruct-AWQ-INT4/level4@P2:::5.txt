The authors propose a new approach to learning conditional distributions by extending the generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to match conditional distributions instead of joint distributions. The key idea is to embed the conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), a technique proposed by Song et al. in 2009. This approach, called conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN), allows for a flexible and simple way to learn conditional distributions.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the background and related work, including the concept of GMMNs, maximum mean discrepancy (MMD), and kernel mean embedding of conditional distributions. The proposed CGMMN algorithm is well-motivated and easy to follow, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion and the deep generative architecture.

The experimental results are presented on several real datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face, demonstrating the effectiveness of CGMMN in various tasks such as predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The authors also provide a comparison with state-of-the-art algorithms, including CMMVA and max-margin DGMs, showing that CGMMN is competitive in terms of predictive performance.

However, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed. In the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90), the term related to $\muX$ and $\muY$ (more precisely, their estimators) is missing. It is also unusual to assume that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zeroes, i.e., the data is already centered in the RKHS. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is at the heart of CGMMN, it is desirable that the authors provide clarifications for this missing term, as well as how to deal with it in gradient computation and real experiments.

Additionally, there are some typos in the paper, such as "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).

Overall, the proposed approach is new and neat, and the experimental results are promising. With some minor revisions to address the above issues, this paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of deep generative models.
