- Paper proposes a non-trivial extension of moment-matching networks to the conditional setting, unlike trivial single-example conditioning.

- Training requires matching distributions of many generated samples per minibatch to many input conditionals, and these distributions differ, complicating updates.

- Main weakness is exposition: the manuscript uses heavy formalism instead of building intuition for the conditional extension.

- The paper fails to clearly state what computations the algorithm actually performs and how efficient those computations are.

- The final objective (Eq. (2)) is not explained: missing required assumptions, practical computation details, and computational cost analysis.

- Because Eq. (2) is opaque, readers cannot see the concrete operations carried out by the algorithm.

- The conditional version lacks the clear, intuitive explanation given for the non-conditional case in the original paper.

- The paper should show an intuitive derivation of Eq. (2) and provide exact, step-by-step derivations from Eq. (2) to the practical training algorithm (the supplement is insufficient/hard to follow).
