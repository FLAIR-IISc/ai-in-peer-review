

The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by modeling conditional distributions instead of joint distributions. The core idea involves embedding conditional distributions into a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), leveraging a technique introduced by Song et al. (2009). The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is evaluated empirically on several real-world datasets. However, a critical issue arises in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), where the terms related to $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (and their estimators) are omitted. It is also unusual to assume $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero, implying the data is already centered in the RKHS. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to CGMMN, the authors must clarify the missing terms and explain their handling in gradient computation and practical implementations. While the approach is novel and elegant, the experimental results are underwhelming. For predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art method CMMVA on both datasets. Regarding generative performance, the absence of comparisons with conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs) leaves it unclear whether CGMMN produces superior samples.
