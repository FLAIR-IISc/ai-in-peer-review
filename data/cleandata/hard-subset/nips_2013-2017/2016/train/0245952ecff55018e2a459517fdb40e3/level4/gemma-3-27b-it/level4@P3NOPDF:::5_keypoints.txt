- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to match conditional distributions.  
- Core idea uses RKHS embeddings of conditional distributions, building on Song et al. (2009).  
- Empirical evaluation performed on several real-world datasets.  
- Method is novel and conceptually clean.  
- Major concern: definition of estimator ĤC_{Y|X} appears to omit a crucial term involving estimators of μ_X and μ_Y.  
- Questionable assumption that μ_X and μ_Y are zero (requires pre-centering in RKHS) needs justification and discussion of practical implications for gradients/implementation.  
- Experiments are underwhelming: predictive performance is consistently worse than state-of-the-art CMMVA.  
- Generative evaluation lacks comparisons to established conditional generative models (e.g., cGANs), making sample-quality assessment difficult.  
- Several minor typographical errors noted (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").  
- Update: authors’ rebuttal addressed the missing-term and zero-mean concerns and added comparative experiments; reviewer found clarifications and experiments convincing and will revise scores.
