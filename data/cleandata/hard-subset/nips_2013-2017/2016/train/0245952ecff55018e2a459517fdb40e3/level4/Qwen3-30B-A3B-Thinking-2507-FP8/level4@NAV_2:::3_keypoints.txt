- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to model conditional distributions.  
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings for conditional distributions (Song et al., ICML 2010) and GMMN (Li et al., ICML 2015).  
- Maintains simple back-propagation training consistent with deep learning practice.  
- Demonstrates versatility across predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (handwritten digits, facial images), and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Successfully distills predictive distributions from Bayesian models without observable performance degradation.  
- Strong theoretical development with clear theorems establishing the CMMD framework.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a referenced proof source.  
- Experimental design uses appropriate baselines, including DSN, CMMVA, VAE, and GAN variants.  
- CMMD objective (Eq. 2) is theoretically sound, but the connection to the weighted MMD in Remark 1 needs clarification on the dot notation (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product).  
- Strength: well-motivated theoretical extension of GMMN to conditional settings.  
- Strength: generative process is simple and easy to implement.  
- Strength: showed multi-domain applicability and a novel Bayesian knowledge distillation application.  
- Weakness: experiments on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Faces are competent but not sufficiently challenging for broad impact; harder datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) are needed.  
- Weakness: Bayesian dark knowledge evaluated only on the small Boston housing dataset, limiting assessment of general utility.  
- Weakness: ambiguous notation (using C for both conditional embedding operator and regularization matrix), especially in Remark 1.  
- Quality: theoretical rigor is high; experimental evaluation is complete for presented tasks but scope is narrow.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and explanations are clear; the Eq. (2) to weighted MMD connection needs clearer notation.  
- Originality: meaningful and distinct extension from conditional GANs and conditional VAEs.  
- Significance: framework is promising but significance limited by narrow dataset evaluation; Bayesian distillation is promising but requires broader testing.  
- Pro: strong theory, clear extension, versatile applications, and a well-executed Bayesian distillation experiment.  
- Con: limited experimental scope on challenging datasets, ambiguous notation, and limited assessment of Bayesian distillation beyond one dataset.
