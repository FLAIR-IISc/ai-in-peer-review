This paper builds on a deep generative modeling approach called "Generative Moment-Matching Network" and creates an extension that supports conditional generation. (This is done by creating a kernel embedding of moments that define the conditional distribution, which can be optimized using the same method as the unconditional version.) The proposed model demonstrates sufficiently competitive performance in predictive modeling, relevant generation, and extracting predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears to be technically sound and the proposals are well-presented (however, I did not examine all the equations in detail). In terms of novelty, while the idea of creating a conditional extension of the Generative Moment Matching Framework is quite natural, the process and emergence of the empirical assumptions were not clear (at least to me). Therefore, in this case, the paper seems to have novelty and utility. The framework itself would be quite easy to implement, as shown in Algorithm 1. Consequently, this paper will likely be useful and relevant for individuals interested in using deep models for generative purposes or extracting more extensive statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, and the provided appendix contains more complete information about the methods used and the details of the experiments.
