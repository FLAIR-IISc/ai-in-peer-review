- Paper extends moment-matching networks to a conditional setting, which is non-trivial compared to single-example conditional models.  
- Main technical challenge: algorithm must match distributions over large batches of samples to large batches of inputs, and these distributions differ unless identical conditioning is used.  
- Using identical conditioning per update would be computationally inefficient.  
- Primary weakness: premature focus on formal mathematics without providing intuitive motivation for derived formulas.  
- Authors fail to clarify the actual computational steps performed by the algorithm.  
- Unclear whether and how the method is computationally efficient and what assumptions enable its implementation.  
- Contrast: the non-conditional original paper was explained intuitively; the conditional case lacks an equally intuitive explanation.  
- Reviewers request an intuitive derivation of Equation (2).  
- Reviewers request clear practical implementation steps translating Equation (2) into the algorithm (supplement contains details but is hard to follow).
