This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard, small-scale benchmark tasks to assess its discriminative and generative capabilities. Overall, this is an interesting contribution to the subfield of deep learning research focused on generative models. The paper is reasonably well-written, although, like many papers in this area, the mathematical derivations can be difficult to follow at times.

The tasks used in the experiments are somewhat small in scale, but this is not unreasonable when compared to other recent work in this field. As the training algorithm involves computationally expensive steps, I would ask the authors to comment on the model's training time in the experiments section, especially in comparison to existing approaches like DSN.

The paper would be much stronger if the authors made reference code available for the experiments. Based on the description in the model section, it may be difficult for a practitioner to reproduce the exact models evaluated.
