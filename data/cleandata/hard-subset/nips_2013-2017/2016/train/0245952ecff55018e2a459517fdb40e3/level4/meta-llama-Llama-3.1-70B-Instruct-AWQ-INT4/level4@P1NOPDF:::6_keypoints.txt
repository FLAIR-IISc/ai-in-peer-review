- Proposes conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMNs) using kernel embedding of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD metric for conditional generation and prediction.  
- Evaluates CGMMN on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.  
- Naive baseline: train a GMMN per conditional distribution (sharing network parameters) suffers from data sparsity, especially with continuous conditioning.  
- Proposed approach treats all conditionals as a family and matches the conditional embedding operator directly, avoiding per-condition data sparsity.  
- Notes that the naive baseline can still perform reasonably when conditioning takes few discrete values (e.g., MNIST); recommends comparing to this baseline.  
- Conditional MMD objective has a clean formulation and can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD (Remark 1).  
- Backpropagation is straightforward because the K matrices in the objective are independent of model parameters.  
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to control stochastic error, but large minibatches make the objective (especially matrix inverses) computationally expensive.  
- Overall assessment: CMMD is a promising way to model conditional distributions and the experimental results are interesting.  
- Presentation issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and require reading referenced papers to understand details.  
- Minor issues: (1) claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect; (2) the conditional embedding operator used is inconsistent with reference [29], likely due to a different cross-covariance operator definition.
