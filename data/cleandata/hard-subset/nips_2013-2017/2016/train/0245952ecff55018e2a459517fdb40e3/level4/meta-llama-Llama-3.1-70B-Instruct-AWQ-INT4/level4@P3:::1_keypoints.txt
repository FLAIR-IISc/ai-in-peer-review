- Proposes a neural network method based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion for generative modeling.
- Evaluated for both discriminative and generative capabilities on standard benchmarks.
- Paper is reasonably clear with good background, though some mathematical exposition is hard to follow.
- Experiments use somewhat small tasks but include MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face datasets.
- Method is competitive with state-of-the-art on the reported benchmarks.
- Concern about computational cost: training is potentially expensive and run-time comparisons (e.g., versus DSN) are missing.
- Lack of reference code hampers reproducibility and practical adoption.
- Paper is technically sound with theory and experiments supporting the claims.
- Review recommends acceptance pending revisions to report computational cost and release code.
- Pros: novel extension, effective on benchmarks, well-written and explains concepts clearly.
- Cons: unclear training cost, no reference code, and experiments on relatively small tasks.
