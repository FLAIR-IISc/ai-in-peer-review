- Proposal: Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using conditional MMD (CMMD) to learn conditional generators via kernel mean embeddings and operator norm minimization.
- Training detail: Back-propagation is straightforward because Gram matrices over conditioning variables do not depend on network parameters.
- Relation to prior work: Builds on GMMNs and kernel conditional embeddings; complementary to conditional GANs and VAEs and improves over naive per-condition GMMNs by jointly matching conditionals.
- Strength: Clear motivation to model p(y|x) directly for conditional and cross-modal generation tasks.
- Strength: Sound theoretical formulation—CMMD naturally extends MMD to conditionals using RKHS operators, with a practical Gram-matrix estimator.
- Strength: Implementation simplicity—no need for differentiable kernels on x since K matrices are parameter-independent.
- Strength: Empirical scope—demonstrated on classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation with competitive results.
- Weakness: Missing strong baseline—no comparison to naive per-condition GMMN or per-class MMD training to quantify CMMD’s empirical gains.
- Weakness: Computational cost and stability—CMMD requires minibatch Gram matrix inverses (O(b^3)), raising runtime, memory, variance, and minibatch-sensitivity concerns.
- Weakness: Clarity issues—Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and rely on cited work; operator and cross-covariance definitions need to be self-contained and consistent.
- Minor issue 1: Incorrect/unqualified claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased; need to specify estimator (U-statistic vs V-statistic) and bias/variance.
- Minor issue 2: Conditional embedding operator definition appears inconsistent with citation [29]; notation and assumptions must be reconciled or restated precisely.
- Quality: CMMD objective is well-motivated, but the paper lacks thorough discussion of estimator variance, computational complexity, and numerical stability for large-scale problems.
- Clarity recommendation: Provide explicit definitions, assumptions, a brief derivation of Eq.2, clarify regularization λ’s role, and state when the operator norm is well-defined.
- Originality: Natural but non-trivial extension of MMD to conditional deep generators; distinct from conditional GANs and VAEs.
- Significance: Conditional modeling is useful and a back-propagation-friendly objective is valuable, but impact is limited until baseline and computational trade-offs are addressed.
- Suggestion: Add naive discrete-condition baseline (e.g., per-class GMMN or per-class MMD with shared network).
- Suggestion: Analyze sensitivity to minibatch size and λ; report wall-clock time and memory usage.
- Suggestion: Consider scalable approximations for Gram matrix inversion (Nyström, random features, cached/block inverses).
- Suggestion: Clarify theoretical exposition, reconcile operator definitions with prior work, and correct MMD estimator bias statements.
- Pros: Conceptually clean extension, simple stable training without adversarial instability, and competitive empirical performance including a useful Bayesian distillation application.
- Cons: Lacks naive baseline, potentially high computational cost, and some theoretical/expositional claims need correction.
- Overall: CGMMN is a neat, potentially useful contribution but requires added baselines and clearer computational and theoretical treatment to be significantly strengthened.
