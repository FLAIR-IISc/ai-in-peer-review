

This paper introduces a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending generative moment matching networks (GMMNs) by replacing their unconditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD)-based loss with a conditional variant. The approach utilizes conditional kernel mean embeddings to construct the loss, demonstrating efficacy across diverse generative tasks including classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and knowledge distillation in Bayesian models. This is my second review of the paper. The previous reviewers' minor concerns—regarding the choice of low-dimensional latent space, scalability to higher dimensions, comparative utility relative to existing models, and other clarifications—appear to have been directly addressed in the revised manuscript. However, Remark 1 remains unclear: the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD formulation is not evident. Specifically, the dot notation used requires clarification—is it matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product? I recommend elaborating this step to make the derivation transparent. Additionally, the choice of $C$ is problematic as it conflicts with the notation for the conditional embedding. Theorems 1 and 2 cite relevant prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference; a citation to a proof or the proof itself should be provided. The empirical results are adequate. While prior reviews noted that supervised learning applications are less compelling, the authors' justification for including them—to showcase model versatility—is acceptable. The knowledge distillation in Bayesian inference is intriguing but limited to a single dataset and model, making its broader applicability unclear. This method would likely gain the most traction in pure generative modeling, where the experiments (using digits and faces) are comparable to the original GMMN benchmarks. To encourage wider adoption, compelling results on more challenging datasets such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN would be necessary.
