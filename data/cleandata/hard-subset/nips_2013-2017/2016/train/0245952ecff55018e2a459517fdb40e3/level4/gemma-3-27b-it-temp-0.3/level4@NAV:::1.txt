This paper introduces a novel neural network method grounded in a conditional maximum mean discrepancy objective. The proposed approach is assessed on established benchmark tasks to demonstrate its performance in both discriminative and generative contexts. In general, this represents a worthwhile contribution to the field of deep learning, specifically within the development of generative models. The writing is generally lucid, though, consistent with the literature, the mathematical details present some complexity. The experimental tasks are relatively limited in scale, but comparable to those found in recent related work. Given the computational demands of the training algorithm, including a comparison of training time against existing methods such as DSN would be beneficial. The paper would be significantly enhanced by the public release of implementation code, as the level of detail in the model description may hinder precise replication of the reported results.
