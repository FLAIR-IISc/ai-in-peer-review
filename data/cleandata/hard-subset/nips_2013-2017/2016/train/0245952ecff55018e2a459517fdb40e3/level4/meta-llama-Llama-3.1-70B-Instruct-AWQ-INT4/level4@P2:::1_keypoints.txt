- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion.  
- Evaluated on standard discriminative and generative benchmarks, showing competitive performance.  
- Paper is reasonably clear overall, though some math is hard to follow.  
- Thorough explanation of CGMMN framework, conditional MMD, architecture, and learning algorithm.  
- Experiments are well-presented with clear tasks, datasets, and evaluation metrics.  
- Experimental tasks are somewhat small but comparable to recent work.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Computational cost and training time are not thoroughly discussed; needs runtime comparisons (e.g., vs DSN).  
- Reference code is not provided, limiting reproducibility.  
- Lacks detailed comparison with existing generative models like GANs and VAEs to clarify strengths/weaknesses.  
- Overall solid and interesting contribution that would be stronger with computational details and code.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel approach, broad evaluation, thorough explanations; Rejection arguments: missing cost analysis, no code, insufficient comparison.
