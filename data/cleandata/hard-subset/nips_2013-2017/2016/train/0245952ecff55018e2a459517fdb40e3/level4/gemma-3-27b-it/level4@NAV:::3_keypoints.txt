- Introduces a novel loss for directed generative models with conditionals, extending GMMN’s MMD loss via conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on classification, handwritten digit/face generation, and Bayesian model knowledge distillation.  
- Authors have adequately addressed prior concerns about low-dimensional latent space choice, scalability, and utility versus existing methods.  
- Clarification needed in Remark 1: ambiguous dot notation in Eq.(2) — specify whether it denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product.  
- Notation confusion: the symbol 'C' is used for both the conditional embedding and another parameter and should be disambiguated.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 properly cite prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a citation or provided proof — include a reference or the proof.  
- Empirical results are encouraging and support the model’s versatility, addressing past doubts about supervised-task applicability.  
- Bayesian inference distillation is interesting but currently evaluated on only one dataset/model, limiting claims about generalizability.  
- Anticipated strongest impact is in pure generative modeling, with performance comparable to the original GMM paper.  
- To promote wider adoption, additional experiments on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) would be beneficial.
