- Introduces CGMMN: extends GMMN to conditional distributions using kernel conditional mean embeddings and a CMMD objective.
- Generator conditions on x and noise h, matches conditional embeddings CY|X of data and model via CMMD.
- Training uses backprop with mini-batch Gram matrix estimates; only the y Gram matrix L depends on model parameters.
- Evaluated on classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST digits, Yale faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston Housing).
- Shows competitive classification performance and plausible conditional samples, improved when using an autoencoder code space.
- Core novelty: training deep conditional generators by matching conditional mean embeddings, bridging kernel conditional embeddings and deep generative models.
- Remark 1: CMMD reduces to MMD with non-uniform weights determined by x, linking to standard MMD training.
- Formulation is sound: CMMD measures the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional embedding operators.
- Experimental results support claims but lack important baselines and quantitative generation metrics.
- Missing a “naïve” conditional baseline (e.g., per-class GMMNs or shared network with per-class MMD) to assess benefits when x is discrete.
- No comparisons to cVAE and cGAN on conditional generation tasks.
- No quantitative metrics or human evaluation for image generation quality.
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are unclear: cross-covariance and conditional operator definitions need clearer assumptions and notation aligned with prior work [29].
- Eq. (2) needs a short derivation or clearer pointer to the appendix.
- Critical implementation details missing: kernels for x and y, bandwidth selection, regularization λ, mini-batch sizes, Gram inverse caching, and per-iteration computational cost.
- CMMD may require large mini-batches; matrix inversions per batch are cubic in batch size and can be costly.
- Limited analysis of CMMD estimator bias/variance and sensitivity to batch size and kernel choices.
- Strengths: elegant MMD-to-conditional extension, simple end-to-end backprop training, broad applicability, competitive empirical examples.
- Weaknesses: scalability concerns, limited baselines and quantitative evaluation, and notational/theoretical inconsistencies.
- Suggested experiments: add naïve conditional baseline, compare to cVAE/cGAN, report hyperparameters, and analyze sensitivity to batch size and kernels.
- Suggest reporting runtime/complexity and mitigation strategies (caching K̃−1, low-rank approximations, random Fourier features, conjugate gradients).
- Clarify whether multiple y per x are sampled in forming L and whether that reduces variance.
- Improve derivations/notation for the conditional operator and reconcile with [29], explicitly stating assumptions.
- Minor issues: correct the claim about MMD estimator unbiasedness (V-statistic vs U-statistic), reconcile operator definition with [29], and fix typos/grammar/formatting.
- Pros for acceptance: principled and useful extension, simple training, broad applicability, and a compelling distillation application.
- Cons for acceptance: clarity and notation issues, limited baselines/quantitative evaluation, and potential computational burden with limited scalability analysis.
- Overall: solid contribution extending GMMN to conditional modeling, but needs clearer exposition, stronger baselines, and analyses of computational/estimator trade-offs.
