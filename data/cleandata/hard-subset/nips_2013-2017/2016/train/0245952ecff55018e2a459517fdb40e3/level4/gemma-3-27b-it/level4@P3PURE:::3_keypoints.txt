- Paper proposes a conditional MMD loss for directed generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Demonstrates method on classification, handwritten digits, face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Authors addressed prior minor concerns about low-dimensional latent space, scalability, and model utility.  
- Mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD in Remark 1 is unclear; dot-product notation (matrix vs. Hadamard) must be clarified.  
- Reuse of symbol “C” for both the conditional embedding and another parameter is confusing and should be changed.  
- Theorem 1 and 2 cite relevant work, but Theorem 3 lacks a citation or a provided proof (should be added to the appendix).  
- Empirical results are encouraging and comparable to the original GMM paper.  
- Using supervised tasks is justified as a versatility demonstration and is acceptable.  
- Application to distilled Bayesian inference is interesting but evaluated on only one dataset/model, limiting claims of generalizability.  
- Method will likely be most impactful within pure generative modeling.  
- Current datasets (digits, faces) establish feasibility, but stronger evidence on harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) is needed for wider adoption.
