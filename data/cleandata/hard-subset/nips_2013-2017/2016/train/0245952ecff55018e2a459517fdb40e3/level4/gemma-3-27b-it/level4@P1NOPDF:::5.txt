The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by focusing on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The key idea is to embed these conditional distributions within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, leveraging a technique originally proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is evaluated empirically on several real datasets. 

In the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), a term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (specifically, their estimators) appears to be missing. It’s also an unusual assumption to set $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ to zero, implying that the data is already centered in the RKHS. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to the CGMMN algorithm, the authors should clarify this omission and explain how it is handled during gradient computation and in their experiments.

The proposed approach is novel and well-presented, but the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. Regarding predictive performance, CGMMN exhibits higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA algorithm on both datasets. Concerning generative performance, the lack of comparison with other conditional generative methods—such as conditional generative adversarial networks (cGANs)—makes it difficult to assess whether CGMMN generates superior samples.

The paper also contains some minor typographical errors, including “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).
