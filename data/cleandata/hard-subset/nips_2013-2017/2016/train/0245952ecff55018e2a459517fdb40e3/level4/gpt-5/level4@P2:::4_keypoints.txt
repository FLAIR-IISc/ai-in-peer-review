- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to learn p(y|x) via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.
- Introduces CMMD as the squared Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional mean embedding operators, with an empirical estimator using Gram matrices and Tikhonov regularization.
- Uses a conditional generator neural network that inputs x and noise h to produce y, trained by backpropagation to minimize CMMD.
- Positions the work between kernel mean embeddings/MMD literature and deep generative models, offering a kernel-based, likelihood-free alternative to CGAN/CVAE without adversarial training.
- Empirical CMMD estimator is differentiable and amenable to minibatch SGD through Gram-matrix-based losses and input-gram inverses.
- Architectures tested include MLP and CNN backbones; sampling is performed by drawing h conditioned on x.
- Tasks evaluated: predictive classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (class-conditional MNIST, Yale Faces, AE+CGMMN in code space), and Bayesian distillation (PBP→CGMMN on Boston Housing).
- Classification uses argmax over generated y; distilled student matches teacher RMSE in the Bayesian distillation task.
- Strength: Conceptually clear and technically sound conditional analogue of MMD using operator-valued embeddings.
- Strength: Simpler and more stable training compared to adversarial or variational methods, relying on standard backprop/minibatches.
- Strength: General framework compatible with diverse architectures and tasks (classification, generation, distillation).
- Strength: Empirically competitive classification results on MNIST/SVHN without augmentation/ensembling; diverse and label-consistent generations.
- Strength: Practical and easy-to-implement algorithm; straightforward sampling and teacher-distillation applicability.
- Weakness: Computational cost and scalability concerns due to O(n^3) Gram-matrix inversions per batch; need discussion of memory/time trade-offs and batch-size sensitivity.
- Weakness: Sensitivity to kernel choices, bandwidths, and regularization λ; lacks principled hyperparameter-selection strategy and kernel ablations.
- Weakness: Theoretical assumption that conditional operator lies in tensor-product RKHS needs clarification on approximation robustness and λ’s bias/variance effects.
- Weakness: Generative evaluation is mainly qualitative; lacks quantitative metrics (e.g., FID/Inception) and direct comparisons to CGAN/CVAE on identical setups.
- Weakness: Limited ablations on noise dimensionality, ways of injecting x/noise, and number of generated samples per x.
- Weakness: Need clarity on classification training details—whether CMMD alone is used, calibration of outputs, and benefits of sample averaging or temperature scaling at test time.
- Questions for authors: How are kernel hyperparameters chosen, and do different input/output kernels materially affect performance?
- Questions for authors: What is the per-minibatch computational/time/memory complexity vs batch size and feature dimensions, compared to cross-entropy training?
- Questions for authors: Does averaging multiple y samples per x improve accuracy or calibration for classification?
- Questions for authors: Can CGMMN handle structured outputs (e.g., sequences) via appropriate kernels, and what practical limits exist?
- Evaluation: Method appears high quality, clear, original as a nontrivial conditional extension of GMMN, and significant for broadening MMD-based conditional modeling.
- Arguments for acceptance: Principled and useful extension of MMD to conditional tasks with simple, stable training and competitive results.
- Arguments against acceptance: Computational overhead of Gram inverses and limited, non-comprehensive generative evaluations.
- Overall: Clear, technically sound, and practically useful contribution that extends MMD-based deep generative models to conditional settings, subject to scalability and evaluation concerns.
