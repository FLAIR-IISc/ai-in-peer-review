- Paper extends moment matching networks (MMNs) to the conditional setting, which is non-trivial because generated-sample distributions must be matched across many, varying conditioning inputs rather than a single shared distribution.
- Main weakness is expository: the paper starts with heavy formalism instead of building intuition for the final objective and practical algorithm.
- Eq. (2) (the optimized objective) is not explained: missing assumptions, derivation steps, and mapping of terms to concrete computations.
- Computational efficiency is unclear: per-update and overall cost, and practical scalability, are not sufficiently discussed.
- The transition from the objective to an implementable training algorithm is under-specified in the main text; supplemental material is hard to follow.
- By contrast, non-conditional MMN was explained intuitively in prior work; the conditional case needs an equally clear, intuitive presentation.
- Reviewer requests an intuitive derivation/motivation for eq. (2) starting from the goal of matching conditional distributions across a mini-batch.
- Reviewer requests a step-by-step account of practical training: statistics computed per mini-batch, how samples and conditionals are paired/grouped, estimator assumptions, and per-update computational cost.
- Clarify how the algorithm resolves the “many samples vs many conditionals” mismatch without using the same conditional for all samples, specifying what is matched to what and any weighting/aggregation.
- Summarize essential supplemental derivations and provide a small concrete example illustrating the required computations.
- Overall: idea is interesting and potentially valuable, but the paper must prioritize intuition, explicitly state computations and assumptions behind eq. (2), and make the efficiency–accuracy trade-offs clear for reproducibility.
