- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.
- Demonstrates both discriminative and generative capabilities on several standard benchmark tasks.
- Overall assessment: solid, interesting, and a valuable extension from joint to conditional moment-matching models.
- Strength: novel and significant—addresses a clear limitation of prior moment-matching networks and broadens applicability.
- Strength: paper is reasonably well-written with clear motivation and structure.
- Weakness: reproducibility—experimental and model details (hyperparameters, regularization) are insufficient; releasing code is recommended.
- Weakness: computational cost—training requires expensive operations (e.g., kernel matrix inversion) that scale poorly; lacks discussion of training time and complexity versus alternatives (DSN, Conditional GANs).
- Weakness: experimental scale—evaluations are on standard but relatively small datasets; stronger claims would need demonstration on higher-dimensional/complex data.
- Weakness: mathematical presentation—some derivations are hard to follow; more intuition alongside formal CMMD definitions would improve accessibility.
