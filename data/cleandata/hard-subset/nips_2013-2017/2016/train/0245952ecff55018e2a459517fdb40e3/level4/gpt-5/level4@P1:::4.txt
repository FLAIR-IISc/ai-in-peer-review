This paper extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional setting, enabling conditional generation. The authors do this by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments that define the conditional distribution and designing an empirical estimator that can be optimized with the same machinery as in the unconditional case. The proposed model is evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model, and achieves reasonably competitive performance across these tasks.

Quality: The paper appears technically sound, and the proposed estimator and training procedure are clearly motivated and presented. I did not verify every equation in full detail, but the derivations seem consistent with the kernel mean embedding literature and the prior GMMN framework. The experimental evaluation spans several applications and supports the claims of effectiveness, showing competitive results without excessive complexity.

Clarity: The manuscript is well written, clear, and easy to follow. The main ideas are explained succinctly, and the connection to the unconditional GMMN is made explicit. Algorithm 1 illustrates the practicality of the approach, and the appendix provides useful details on both methodology and experimental setup.

Originality: While a conditional extension of moment-matching networks is a natural direction, the specific mechanism and the derivation of the empirical estimator are not obvious. In that sense, the paper offers both novelty and practical value. The approach fits neatly into the existing GMMN framework while broadening its scope to conditional modeling.

Significance: The resulting framework is straightforward to implement and train via backpropagation, making it accessible to practitioners. Given its competitive performance on predictive modeling, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation, the method will likely be useful to researchers and practitioners working with deep generative models or seeking to distill complex statistical procedures into efficient predictors.

Pros:
- Clear and well-presented extension of GMMN to conditional generation.
- Empirical estimator and training algorithm are simple and practical.
- Competitive results across diverse tasks.
- Appendices provide helpful methodological and experimental details.

Cons:
- I did not verify all equations in full detail.

Overall, this is a solid contribution that meaningfully extends GMMN to conditional modeling with a clean, well-motivated estimator and practical training procedure. The work is likely to be of interest to the NIPS community, particularly those using deep generative models or interested in model distillation.
