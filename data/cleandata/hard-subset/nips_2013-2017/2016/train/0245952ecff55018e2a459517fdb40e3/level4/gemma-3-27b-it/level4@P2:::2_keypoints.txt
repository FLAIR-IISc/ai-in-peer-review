- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.  
- Extends GMMN from joint to conditional modeling, enabling tasks like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Trains CGMMN via stochastic gradient descent and back-propagation, leveraging kernel mean embeddings and MMD theory.  
- Empirically shows competitive performance across diverse conditional generation tasks.  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important problem: conditional generative modeling with broad applications.  
- Solid theoretical foundation based on kernel mean embeddings and MMD.  
- Demonstrates competitive experimental results versus state-of-the-art methods.  
- Clearly situates CGMMN within prior work on GANs and VAEs.  

Weaknesses:
- Lacks intuitive explanation for the CMMD objective; derivation feels abstract.  
- Insufficient discussion of computational complexity and efficiency, with key implementation details relegated to supplementary material.  
- Does not clearly state assumptions required for CMMD to be well-defined and effective.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Provides a novel, theoretically grounded extension of GMMN to conditional settings.  
- Shows practical effectiveness through experiments.  
- Addresses a significant problem with a potentially valuable approach.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Poor intuitive exposition and computational clarity hinder understanding and reproducibility.  
- Missing discussion of limitations and assumptions questions generality and robustness.  

Overall assessment:
- Solid contribution but requires clearer intuition, detailed computational discussion, and explicit assumptions; recommend weak accept pending these clarifications.
