- Introduces CGMMN: extension of GMMN to learn conditional distributions via moment matching in RKHS (CMMD).
- Motivates focusing on conditional distributions for predictive modeling and contextual generation versus joint approaches.
- Uses conditional maximum mean discrepancy as a natural extension of MMD; explains efficient gradient computation via backprop.
- Strong theoretical grounding in kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings with a clear CMMD derivation.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering predictive tasks, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Initial concern about the conditional embedding operator centering (μ_X, μ_Y) and its impact on gradients was raised and addressed in rebuttal.
- Empirical results are promising but not consistently state-of-the-art; slight gains on MNIST and SVHN over some baselines.
- Lacks direct comparisons to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), limiting assessment of advantages.
- Minor typographical errors were present (e.g., “Frobeniu norm”) but noted as corrected after rebuttal.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical soundness, broad applicability, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: inconsistent SOTA performance, insufficient comparative baselines, and initial need for clarification (now resolved).
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, well-motivated contribution despite not uniformly superior empirical results.
