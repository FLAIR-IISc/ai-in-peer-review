This paper presents conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMN), an extension of GMMNs to conditional generation and prediction applications. The key contribution is the application of kernel embedding of conditional distributions and a corresponding conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) metric for measuring the discrepancy between conditional distributions. The proposed CGMMN is evaluated on tasks including classification, conditional generation, and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models. A significant advantage of CGMMN over naively extending GMMNs to the conditional setting – estimating a separate GMMN for each conditional distribution – is its ability to address data sparsity, particularly when the conditioning variable is continuous. The proposed method treats all conditional distributions as a family and matches the model to the conditional embedding operator, rather than individual distributions. While the advantage appears clear, a comparison to such a naive approach as a baseline would be informative, especially for tasks like conditional generation on MNIST where the conditioning variable takes a limited number of values. The formulation of the CMMD objective is well-presented and efficiently computable, similar to the unconditional MMD, as highlighted in Remark 1. The independence of the K matrices in equation 2 from model parameters allows for straightforward backpropagation. A potential concern is the computational cost and minibatch size requirements of the CMMD objective; the stochasticity of small minibatches may dominate the error, while large minibatches can be computationally expensive due to matrix inversions. Overall, CMMD offers a promising approach to modeling conditional distributions. The experimental results on classification and Bayesian model distillation are encouraging. The presentation in sections 2.3 and 3.1 could be improved for clarity; readers may need to consult the referenced papers for a deeper understanding. There are a couple of minor issues: (1) line 77 states the MMD estimator is unbiased, which is inaccurate; (2) line 90 presents a conditional embedding operator inconsistent with the definition in reference [29], potentially requiring a modified cross-covariance operator.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses an important problem – extending generative moment matching to the conditional setting – and proposes a theoretically sound solution using kernel methods and conditional embeddings.
*   The CMMD objective is well-motivated and efficiently computable.
*   The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on a variety of tasks.
*   The work builds upon and extends existing literature in generative modeling and kernel methods.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The presentation in some sections (2.3, 3.1) is dense and could benefit from more detailed explanations.
*   A comparison to a naive baseline (separate GMMNs for each conditional distribution) is missing.
*   Minor technical inaccuracies (unbiased MMD estimator, inconsistent conditional embedding) exist.
*   The potential computational challenges of the CMMD objective with large datasets are not fully addressed.
