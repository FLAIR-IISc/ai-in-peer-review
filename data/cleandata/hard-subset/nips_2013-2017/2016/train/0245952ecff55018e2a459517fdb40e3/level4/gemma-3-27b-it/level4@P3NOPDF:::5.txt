This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) that focuses on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea leverages the embedding of these conditional distributions within a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS), building upon the work of Song et al. (2009). The authors evaluate CGMMN empirically on several real-world datasets.

The paper presents a novel and conceptually clean approach to conditional generative modeling. However, a significant concern arises in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), where a crucial term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (specifically, their estimators) appears to be omitted. Furthermore, the assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero – effectively requiring the data to be pre-centered in the RKHS – is unusual and warrants justification. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to the functionality of CGMMN, clarification regarding this missing term, and a discussion of its implications for gradient computation and practical implementation in the experiments, are essential.

The experimental results, while demonstrating the feasibility of the approach, are somewhat underwhelming. Regarding predictive performance, CGMMN consistently yields higher error rates compared to the current state-of-the-art, CMMVA, on the tested datasets.  Concerning generative performance, the lack of comparison to other established conditional generative models – such as conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs) – makes it difficult to assess the relative quality of the samples generated by CGMMN. A comparative analysis with cGANs, or other relevant techniques, would significantly strengthen the paper's contribution and better position the proposed method within the field.

The paper also contains several minor typographical errors, including "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238). Addressing these would improve the overall polish of the manuscript.

**Update after Author Rebuttal:**

The authors have thoroughly addressed all of my initial concerns regarding the missing term in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$, the assumption of zero means, and the lack of comparison to alternative generative models. The clarifications and supplementary experiments provided in the rebuttal convincingly demonstrate the validity of their approach and offer valuable insights into its performance characteristics.
