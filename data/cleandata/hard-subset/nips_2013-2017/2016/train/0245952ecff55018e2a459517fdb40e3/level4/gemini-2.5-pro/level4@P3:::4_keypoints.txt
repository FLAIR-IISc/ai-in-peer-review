- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) for learning conditional distributions p(y|x) using a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- CMMD measures discrepancy between conditional distributions via Hilbert–Schmidt norm of difference between conditional kernel mean embedding operators.  
- Provides a practical empirical CMMD estimator (Eq. 2) that can be optimized with mini-batch SGD and backpropagation.  
- Theoretical motivation is well-grounded in kernel methods and the derivation appears correct.  
- Evaluated across diverse tasks: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and distilling Bayesian models ("Bayesian dark knowledge").  
- Empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art deep generative methods and demonstrate versatility.  
- Paper quality is high: technically sound, well-executed experiments, and reproducible presentation (Algorithm 1 and appendix).  
- Exceptionally clear writing with helpful background on kernel embeddings and logical structure from theory to experiments.  
- Original contribution: CMMD objective for conditional generative modeling and novel application to knowledge distillation.  
- Significant impact: broadens applicability of moment-matching networks to supervised/semi-supervised problems with a simple backprop-compatible loss.  
- Strength: novel, principled CMMD objective generalizing MMD for conditional settings.  
- Strength: broad applicability demonstrated on multiple important ML tasks.  
- Strength: strong empirical performance supporting practical utility.  
- Strength: conceptual simplicity and ease of integration into standard deep learning pipelines.  
- Weakness: CMMD requires kernel matrix inversion, giving cubic complexity in batch size — needs discussion on practical scaling vs. GANs/VAEs.  
- Weakness: generated images are somewhat noisy; autoencoder combination improves quality, indicating CGMMN may struggle with fine image details.  
- Weakness: sensitivity to kernel choice and regularization λ not analyzed; hyperparameter robustness should be discussed.  
- Conclusion: overall excellent, principled, and practical contribution with minor limitations; strong recommendation for acceptance.
