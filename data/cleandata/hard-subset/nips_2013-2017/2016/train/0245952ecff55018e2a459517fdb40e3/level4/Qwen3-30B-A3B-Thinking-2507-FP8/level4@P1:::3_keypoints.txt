- Proposes CGMMN: extension of GMMN to learn conditional distributions using conditional MMD and conditional kernel mean embeddings, trainable by backpropagation.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Authors addressed prior minor reviewer concerns about latent dimensionality, scalability, and justification of approach in the revision.  
- Major technical concern: unclear connection in Remark 1 / Equation (2) between CMMD and a weighted MMD formulation; the mapping needs clarification.  
- Ambiguity in Equation (2): dot notation is unclear (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product) and should be specified.  
- Notational conflict: symbol "C" is used for both the conditional embedding operator and the regularization matrix, causing confusion.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a citation or proof; needs a reference or concise proof that equal conditional embedding operators imply identical conditional distributions.  
- Experiments show competitive classification performance (MNIST, SVHN), especially with CNNs, and visually convincing conditional image samples on MNIST and Yale Face.  
- Authors provide nearest-neighbor checks to argue generated samples are not simple copies of training data.  
- Bayesian distillation demonstration is interesting but limited: single regression task (Boston) with one Bayesian model (PBP), so scope is too narrow to generalize.  
- Claim that CGMMN’s main value is in pure generative modeling is reasonable, but results are only comparable to original GMMN on simple datasets.  
- Recommendation to strengthen impact by including results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and original as a natural extension of GMMN, moderate significance due to limited experimental scope and some unresolved theoretical/notation issues.
