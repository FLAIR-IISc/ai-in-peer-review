- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model p(y|x) using a deep net that maps (x, noise) to y and is trained via a new Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- CMMD measures dissimilarity between conditional distributions using the Hilbert–Schmidt norm of differences between kernel mean embedding operators in an RKHS.  
- Training minimizes a single CMMD loss by backpropagation, avoiding adversarial min–max optimization and its instabilities.  
- Demonstrates versatility on prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (digits, faces), and model compression (“Bayesian dark knowledge”).  
- CMMD is a principled, novel extension of MMD to the conditional setting, providing a solid theoretical foundation.  
- Moment-matching objective yields stable training dynamics and avoids common GAN issues like mode collapse.  
- Empirical results are competitive with state-of-the-art conditional generative models and show realistic, diverse conditioned samples.  
- CMMD requires inversion of kernel Gram matrices, causing O(B^3) per-batch cost and potential scalability issues compared to CVAEs/CGANs.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing kernel choices/parameters, regularization λ, and other implementation details; code release would help.  
- Mathematical derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator is dense; a more detailed appendix derivation would improve clarity.  
- Experiments are on relatively small datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale); larger/higher-resolution benchmarks (CIFAR-10, CelebA, LSUN) would better demonstrate scalability.  
- Overall: solid, well-written contribution with a novel, stable, and versatile framework, but practical scalability and reproducibility need addressing.
