- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions by extending GMMNs.  
- Leverages RKHS conditional distribution embeddings and introduces a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to compare conditional distributions.  
- Provides clear theoretical exposition of RKHS, kernel mean embedding, and MMD foundations.  
- Describes the CGMMN architecture and the learning algorithm for optimizing the CMMD objective.  
- Empirically evaluated on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face, showing competitiveness in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Strength: novel and elegant solution for modeling conditional distributions with GMMNs.  
- Strength: theoretical foundations are well-explained and easy to follow.  
- Strength: thorough experimental evaluation demonstrating competitive results.  
- Weakness: lacks broader comparisons with state-of-the-art methods for generative performance.  
- Weakness: contains typos and minor errors that should be corrected.  
- Originality/significance: an original, potentially impactful extension of deep generative models to conditional settings.  
- Quality/clarity: well-written and concise with solid theoretical and experimental presentation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel contribution, competitive experiments, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments for rejection: need more comparative experiments and fixes for minor errors.  
- Reviewer rating and recommendation: 8/10; accept with minor revisions.
