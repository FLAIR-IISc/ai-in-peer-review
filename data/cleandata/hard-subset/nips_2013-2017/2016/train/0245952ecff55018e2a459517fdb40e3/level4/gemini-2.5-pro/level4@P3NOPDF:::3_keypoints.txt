- Proposes a conditional MMD loss as a principled extension of GMMNs using conditional kernel mean embeddings for directed generative models with conditional variables.
- Demonstrates the method on supervised classification, generating handwritten digits and faces, and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.
- Authors have addressed prior minor reviewer concerns (latent-space dimensionality, scalability, utility comparisons), improving the manuscript.
- Remark 1’s derivation connecting Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear; provide a more detailed mapping and clarify the ambiguous "dot notation" (matrix vs. Hadamard).
- Theorem 3 is presented without proof or citation; include a proof in the appendix or cite a source.
- Supervised learning experiments show versatility but are unlikely to motivate adoption over specialized classifiers.
- Bayesian knowledge distillation evaluation is novel but limited to a single dataset/model; needs broader experiments to assess generality.
- Generative modeling results are comparable to original GMMN on low-dimensional datasets; require testing on higher-dimensional benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) to demonstrate practical advantages.
- Minor notation issue: using `C` for the weighting matrix conflicts with its use for the conditional embedding operator—change the symbol to avoid confusion.
- Overall: theoretically well-motivated and promising contribution; addressing theoretical clarity and expanding empirical benchmarks would substantially strengthen the paper.
