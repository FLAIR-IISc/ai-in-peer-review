The authors extend the generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by considering matching conditional distributions instead of joint distributions. The key idea is to embed the conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, a technique proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm CGMMN is evaluated empirically on several real datasets.

The authors provide a thorough overview of the related work, including GMMNs, maximum mean discrepancy (MMD), and kernel embedding of conditional distributions. They also present a clear explanation of the proposed approach, including the definition of conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) and the architecture of CGMMN.

However, there are some minor issues that need to be addressed. In the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90), the term related to $\muX$ and $\muY$ (more precisely, their estimators) is missing. It is also unusual to assume that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zeroes, i.e., the data is already centered in the RKHS. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is at the heart of CGMMN, it is desirable that the authors provide clarifications for this missing term, as well as how to deal with it in gradient computation and real experiments.

The experimental results are somewhat disappointing. In terms of predictive performance, on both datasets, CGMMN gives larger error rates than the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMVA. In terms of generative performance, since no comparison between CGMMN and other conditional generative methods (e.g., the conditional generative adversarial nets) is provided, it is unclear whether the proposed algorithm could generate better samples.

The paper contains some typos, e.g., "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).

Arguments for acceptance:

* The proposed approach is new and neat.
* The authors provide a thorough overview of the related work.
* The experimental results are somewhat competitive with the state-of-the-art algorithms.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The experimental results are somewhat disappointing.
* There are some minor issues that need to be addressed.
* The paper contains some typos.

Rating: 7
