- Proposes a novel loss for directed conditional generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings, extending GMMN/MMD.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on tasks: classification, handwritten digits, faces, and distilled Bayesian inference.  
- Previous minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space, scaling, and model utility) have been adequately addressed in the revision.  
- Mathematical clarity lacking: unclear mapping of Equation (2) to the weighted MMD in Remark 1.  
- Ambiguity in dot notation—needs clarification whether it denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product.  
- Poor choice of the variable name "C" since it conflicts with the conditional embedding definition.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 are properly cited to prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks reference or proof.  
- Recommend providing a reference or including a proof for Theorem 3 to maintain rigor.  
- Empirical results are decent, though supervised learning is not the strongest application for the model; authors justify it as demonstrating versatility.  
- Distilled Bayesian inference application is intriguing but evaluated on only one dataset and one model, limiting conclusions.  
- Likely most useful for pure generative modeling; experiments comparable to original GMM paper and datasets (digits/faces) are sufficient to show viability.  
- To convince broader adoption, stronger results on challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) are necessary.
