- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.  
- Core idea: match the model to the conditional embedding operator of the data rather than estimating separate conditional distributions.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation with generally compelling experimental results.  
- Naive baseline: training separate (parameter-shared) GMMNs per condition suffers from data sparsity, especially with continuous conditioning.  
- However, the naive approach can work reasonably well when the number of conditioning values is small (e.g., MNIST with 10 labels); a comparison to this baseline is missing and would be valuable.  
- CMMD formulation is elegant and, per Remark 1, can be computed with similar ease to unconditional MMD.  
- K matrices in the CMMD objective are independent of model parameters, enabling standard backpropagation.  
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to reduce stochasticity effects, which is computationally costly due to matrix inversions in objective evaluation.  
- Presentation of sections 2.3 and 3.1 is somewhat difficult to follow and requires reading cited papers for full comprehension.  
- Minor issue 1: Claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect.  
- Minor issue 2: Definition of the conditional embedding operator (line 90) is inconsistent with reference [29]; achieving the paper’s operator form appears to require a different cross-covariance operator definition.
