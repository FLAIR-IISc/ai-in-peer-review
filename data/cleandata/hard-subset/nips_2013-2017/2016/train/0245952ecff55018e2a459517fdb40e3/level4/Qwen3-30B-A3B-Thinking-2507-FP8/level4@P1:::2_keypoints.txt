- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to model conditional distributions.
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge (model distillation) tasks.
- Significant contribution: non-trivial extension of GMMN to conditional settings rather than simply concatenating conditions to inputs.
- Key technical challenge: matching distributions across mini-batches with different conditional variables, not identical conditions per batch.
- Mathematical foundation is sound but the paper over-relies on formal notation without offering intuitive explanation of the CMMD objective.
- Major weakness: equation (2) (CMMD) lacks intuitive derivation and clear practical computational steps from formula to implementation.
- Supplement contains some implementation details but is hard to follow without clearer exposition in the main text.
- Paper claims inverse matrices in CMMD are independent of model parameters, implying non-differentiable kernels may be usable, but does not explain computational or implementation implications.
- For a method touted as "simple" to train via backpropagation, the actual computational steps and efficiency concerns are not clearly explained.
- Empirical results: competitive predictive performance on MNIST and SVHN, and reasonable conditional generation on MNIST and Yale Faces.
- Bayesian dark knowledge experiment is compelling—CGMMN can distill Bayesian models without degrading predictive performance.
- Strengths summarized: novel conditional extension of moment-matching networks, strong empirical results, and applicability to Bayesian model distillation.
- Weaknesses summarized: poor intuition for CMMD, insufficient implementation detail, over-reliance on heavy mathematics, and limited comparison to other conditional generative models (cGANs, cVAEs).
- Recommendation: add an intuitive explanation of CMMD, step-by-step computational implementation, and clearer comparisons to other conditional generative models to improve accessibility and impact.
