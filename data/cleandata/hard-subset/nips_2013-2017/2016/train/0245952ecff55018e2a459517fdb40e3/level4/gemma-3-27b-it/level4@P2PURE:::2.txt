The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional setting. While extending to single-example updates in other models is straightforward (simply feeding the condition into the network), this presents a non-trivial challenge here. This is because the goal is to match distributions derived from a mini-batch of samples to a potentially large number of inputs, and these distributions are no longer identical unless the same condition is used for every update – an inefficient approach. A primary weakness of the paper is its reliance on complex mathematical formalism without first establishing the underlying intuition. The authors fail to clearly articulate the computations performed by the algorithm and their efficiency. Specifically, the derivation and justification of the optimized formula (eq (2)) are lacking; the paper does not explain the assumptions required for its validity or how it translates into a practical algorithm. The original, non-conditional version of the algorithm benefits from an intuitive explanation (e.g., via reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces and a readily understandable minimization target). A similar intuitive explanation would significantly improve the presentation of the conditional case. To aid understanding, it would be helpful for the authors to address the following questions: How does one intuitively arrive at formula (2)? And, what are the concrete steps bridging formula (2) to the implemented algorithm, as details provided in the supplement are difficult to follow?
