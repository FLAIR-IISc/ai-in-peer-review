This paper extends the deep generative modeling approach of “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” (GMMN) to the conditional generation setting. The authors achieve this by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, enabling optimization using the same computational machinery as the original, unconditional GMMN framework. The proposed conditional GMMN (CGMMN) is demonstrated through competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model.

The paper is technically sound and well-presented. While a complete verification of all equations was not performed, the derivations appear logically consistent and clearly explained. The novelty of this work lies in effectively extending the GMMN framework to conditional generation. While the *idea* of conditioning GMMNs is natural, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious – at least, they were not to me – suggesting a worthwhile contribution. The combination of theoretical development and empirical validation supports the claim of novelty and usefulness. 

The derived framework appears readily applicable, as illustrated by the clear presentation of Algorithm 1. This accessibility will likely make the CGMMN model valuable to researchers interested in deep generative modeling or in distilling the outputs of more complex statistical processes. The writing is clear, concise, and easy to follow, contributing significantly to the paper’s overall quality. Furthermore, the appendix provides valuable supplementary information regarding methodological details and experimental setup, enhancing reproducibility and understanding.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Extension of Existing Work:** The paper builds directly and logically upon a previous NIPS publication, strengthening its connection to the existing research landscape.
*   **Novel Derivation:** The derivation of the conditional moment matching estimator is non-trivial and contributes to the advancement of the field.
*   **Practical Applicability:** The proposed framework is presented in a way that suggests straightforward implementation and application to a variety of problems.
*   **Comprehensive Evaluation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the CGMMN in multiple tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and model distillation.
*   **Excellent Presentation:** The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to understand. The inclusion of a detailed appendix further improves its clarity and reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**

*   While the experiments demonstrate competitive performance, a more thorough comparison to state-of-the-art conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs, VAEs) on standardized datasets would further strengthen the empirical evaluation. The justification for the specific baselines chosen could be expanded.
*   The paper would benefit from a discussion of the limitations of the approach, such as potential sensitivity to kernel choice or computational cost for high-dimensional data.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The technical soundness, clear presentation, and demonstrated usefulness of the proposed method make it a worthy addition to NIPS. I recommend acceptance, contingent on minor revisions addressing the points raised above regarding comparison to state-of-the-art methods and discussion of limitations.



**Confidence:** 8/10
