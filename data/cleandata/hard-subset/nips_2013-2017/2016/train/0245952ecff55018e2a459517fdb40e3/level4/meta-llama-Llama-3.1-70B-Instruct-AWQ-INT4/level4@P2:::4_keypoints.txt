- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via kernel moment embeddings.  
- Derives an empirical estimator for the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD), a non-obvious and significant technical contribution.  
- Uses the same optimization machinery as unconditional GMMN, enabling straightforward training of the conditional model.  
- Demonstrates competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian prediction distillation tasks.  
- Evaluated on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, and Boston housing datasets with results comparable to VAEs, MMVA, and DSN.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-presented, and easy to follow with clear explanations.  
- Appendix provides useful methodological and experimental details that aid reproducibility.  
- Novelty is moderate—conditional extension is natural—but the estimator derivation and implementation are non-trivial and valuable.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations and potential application scenarios.  
- Weakness: some equations and derivations could be clarified or expanded for greater clarity.  
- Overall recommendation: makes a significant contribution to deep generative models and is worthy of acceptance.
