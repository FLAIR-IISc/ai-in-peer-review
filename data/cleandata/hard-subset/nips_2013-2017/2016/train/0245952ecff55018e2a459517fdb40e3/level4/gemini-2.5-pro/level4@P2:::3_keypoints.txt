- Introduces CGMMN and CMMD: a conditional extension of GMMNs using kernel mean embeddings to measure distances between conditional distributions in RKHS.  
- Model design: deep neural network conditioned on inputs plus noise, trained by minimizing CMMD via SGD/backprop.  
- Strength — originality: natural and significant extension of GMMNs, offering an alternative to conditional GANs and VAEs.  
- Strength — simplicity: single CMMD objective yields simpler, potentially more stable training than adversarial methods.  
- Strength — versatility: demonstrated on supervised classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Weakness — Remark 1: connection to weighted MMD is under-explained and notation (e.g., reuse of C) is confusing; needs clearer derivation and notation.  
- Weakness — Theorem 3: stated without proof or citation; must include a proof or reference to substantiate the claim.  
- Weakness — Bayesian distillation experiments: limited to one dataset (Boston) and one teacher model (PBP); needs more datasets and teacher types.  
- Weakness — generative experiments: evaluated only on MNIST and Yale faces; should include harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) to assess competitiveness.  
- Summary: solid, well-written contribution with minor theoretical clarity issues and insufficient experimental breadth; overall positive recommendation.
