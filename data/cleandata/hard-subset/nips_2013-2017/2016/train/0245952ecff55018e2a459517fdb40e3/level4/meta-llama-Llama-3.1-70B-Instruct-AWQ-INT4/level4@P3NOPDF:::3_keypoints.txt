- Proposes a novel loss for directed conditional generative models extending GMMNs via conditional kernel mean embeddings, demonstrated on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Authors have addressed prior minor concerns, clarifying use of low-dimensional latent space, scalability, and model utility, though a few minor issues remain.  
- Mapping in Remark 1 is unclear: Equation (2)’s dot notation is ambiguous (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product) and should be clarified; reuse of the variable "C" is unfortunate.  
- Theorem 3 is presented without a reference or proof; include a citation or provide the proof for consistency.  
- Empirical results are decent and authors acknowledge supervised learning limitations; distilled Bayesian inference is interesting but only validated on one dataset/model, so generality is unclear.  
- Experiments match the original GMM paper’s scope (digits and faces) and show feasibility, but stronger results on harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) are needed for broader adoption.  
- Overall positive: substantial progress on initial concerns and an interesting approach that could contribute meaningfully after minor revisions.
