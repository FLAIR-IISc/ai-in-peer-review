- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) metric to model conditional distributions via kernel embeddings.  
- CMMD measures discrepancy between conditional distributions directly, avoiding per-condition density estimation.  
- Builds on GMMN and kernel-embedding/MMD literature, offering a simpler alternative to conditional GANs by avoiding min–max optimization.  
- Demonstrates utility on three tasks: classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Technically sound and well-motivated; addresses data sparsity of training separate GMMNs for each condition.  
- Experimental results are competitive across multiple applications and show practical utility.  
- Method is simple to train via standard backpropagation, making it appealing for practitioners.  
- Original contribution: unified moment-matching framework for conditional modeling distinct from conditional GANs/VAEs.  
- Broad applicability, including an effective “Bayesian dark knowledge” distillation use case.  
- Core idea is clearly presented, but Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are mathematically dense and need clearer exposition.  
- Incorrect claim: MMD estimator described as “unbiased” when it is asymptotically unbiased but not unbiased.  
- Conditional embedding operator definition appears inconsistent with Song et al. (2009) and requires clarification.  
- Lacks comparison to the naive baseline of training separate GMMNs per condition (e.g., per MNIST class).  
- Computational complexity (matrix inverses in CMMD) and practical mini-batch trade-offs are not sufficiently discussed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix mathematical inaccuracies, clarify connections to prior work, add naive-baseline comparison, and discuss computational trade-offs.
