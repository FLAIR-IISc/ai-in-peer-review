- Proposes a novel conditional loss for directed generative models by extending unconditional MMD using conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Demonstrates the method on tasks: classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and Bayesian model knowledge distillation.  
- Previous reviewer concerns (motivation for low-dimensional latent space and scalability, practical advantages over alternatives, and clarifications) have been comprehensively addressed in the revision.  
- Minor ambiguity in Remark 1: the dot-product notation is unclear (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product) and reusing ‘C’ for multiple meanings is confusing.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a citation or included proof; authors should provide a reference or add the proof.  
- Empirical results are encouraging and comparable to original GMM results on digits and faces.  
- Using the model for supervised learning is acceptable here as a demonstration of versatility.  
- Bayesian knowledge distillation application is interesting but evaluated on only one dataset/model, so its generalizability is uncertain.  
- Recommend additional experiments on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) to show broader applicability and encourage adoption.
