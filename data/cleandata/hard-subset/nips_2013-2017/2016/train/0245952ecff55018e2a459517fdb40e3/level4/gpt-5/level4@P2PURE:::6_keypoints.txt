- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.
- Key idea: match the conditional embedding operator for the whole family of conditionals instead of fitting separate GMMNs per condition to avoid data sparsity.
- Suggestion: include a naive baseline that fits separate GMMNs (with shared parameters) per condition, since this may perform well in low-cardinality settings like MNIST.
- Strength: CMMD has a clean formulation and can be computed almost exactly like unconditional MMD; K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, allowing standard backpropagation.
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid minibatch stochasticity dominating the error, but large batches are expensive due to costly matrix inversions.
- Empirical takeaway: CMMD is a compelling approach with interesting results on classification and Bayesian model distillation.
- Readability issue: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are difficult to follow and required consulting cited papers to understand.
- Minor issue 1: Claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect.
- Minor issue 2: Definition of the conditional embedding operator (line 90) is inconsistent with reference [29] and appears to imply a different cross-covariance operator.
