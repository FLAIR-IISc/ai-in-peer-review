This paper extends the “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” (GMMN) framework to conditional generation. It introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) by deriving a kernel embedding for the moments defining the conditional distribution, enabling optimization using similar techniques as the original GMMN. The proposed model demonstrates competitive performance across several tasks – predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. 

The paper is technically sound, with clear presentation of the proposed method. While a complete verification of all equations wasn't performed, the approach appears well-reasoned and logically developed. The extension to conditional generation from GMMN is a natural progression, but the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious, suggesting novelty. The framework’s potential usefulness is supported by its straightforward application, exemplified by Algorithm 1, and its relevance to researchers interested in deep generative modeling and knowledge distillation.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, facilitating comprehension. The inclusion of an appendix provides valuable supplementary details regarding the methodology and experimental setup. 

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The approach builds upon a solid foundation (GMMN) and presents a coherent extension.
*   **Novelty:** The derivation of the conditional extension and empirical estimator appears original.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
*   **Usefulness:** The CGMMN framework offers a practical approach to conditional generation with potential applications in various domains.
*   **Experimental Validation:** Demonstrates competitive performance across diverse tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Depth of Equation Verification:** While the approach seems logically sound, a complete verification of all equations wasn't performed.
*   **Incremental Advance:** Although novel, the extension is a natural progression from existing work, making it an incremental contribution.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel extension to a recent generative modeling framework.
*   The approach is clearly explained and supported by experimental results.
*   The method has potential for broader impact within the generative modeling and knowledge distillation communities.
*   The paper adheres to NIPS standards of quality and clarity.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The contribution, while valuable, might be considered incremental.
*   The lack of full equation verification introduces a minor level of uncertainty.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN framework extends the capabilities of GMMNs and offers a practical approach to conditional generation. While the contribution might be considered incremental, the technical soundness, clarity, and experimental validation make this a strong submission suitable for acceptance.
