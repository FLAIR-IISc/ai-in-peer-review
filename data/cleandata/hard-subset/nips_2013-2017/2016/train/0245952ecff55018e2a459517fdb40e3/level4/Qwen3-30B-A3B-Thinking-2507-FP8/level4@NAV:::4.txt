

This paper extends the deep generative modeling framework "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" to enable conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, such that these moments can be optimized using the same machinery as the unconditional variant. The proposed model demonstrates competitive performance across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper is technically sound and the proposals are well presented (though I did not verify all equations in full detail). Regarding novelty, while the conditional extension to the generative moment-matching framework is conceptually natural, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were non-trivial. Consequently, the paper exhibits meaningful novelty and utility. The derived framework is straightforward to implement, as evidenced by Algorithm 1, suggesting significant practical relevance for researchers employing deep generative models or seeking to distill complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with the appendix providing valuable supplementary methodological and experimental details.
