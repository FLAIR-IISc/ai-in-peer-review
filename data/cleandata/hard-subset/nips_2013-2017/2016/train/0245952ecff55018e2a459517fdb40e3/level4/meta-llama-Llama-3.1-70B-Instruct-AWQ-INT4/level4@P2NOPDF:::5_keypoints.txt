- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that match conditional distributions via RKHS conditional embedding (Song et al., 2009).
- Empirical evaluation performed on several real-world datasets.
- Missing term in the estimator hat{C}_{Y|X} (related to empirical means mu_X and mu_Y) is apparent in the manuscript.
- Assuming mu_X and mu_Y are zero (data centered in RKHS) is unusual and needs justification.
- Clarification required on how the missing term affects gradient computation and experimental implementation.
- Experimental results are underwhelming: CGMMN shows higher prediction error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets.
- No comparison to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), so sample quality claims are unclear.
- Manuscript contains several typos ("Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").
- Update: authors addressed the reviewer's concerns, prompting the reviewer to revise scores upward.
