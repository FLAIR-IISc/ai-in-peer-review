- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMNs to model conditional distributions using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Optimizes CMMD via backpropagation to learn conditional generative models.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation with competitive results.  
- Motivated by limitations of GMMN (joint-only) and relevance of conditional models to many applications.  
- Builds on moment-matching alternatives to GANs and leverages kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings.  
- Strength: addresses an important and practical problem of conditional generative modeling.  
- Strength: provides a rigorous theoretical foundation for the CMMD criterion.  
- Strength: empirical validation on diverse tasks (MNIST, SVHN, generation, distillation) showing competitive performance.  
- Strength: retains GMMN advantage of efficient backprop-based training, avoiding GAN min-max issues.  
- Weakness: paper lacks intuitive exposition of CMMD and derivation steps (e.g., Eq. 2), hindering accessibility.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity and practical implementation details (mini-batch specifics, assumptions).  
- Weakness: limited ablation studies on kernel choice, regularization λ, and hidden-variable dimensionality.  
- Weakness: experimental comparisons need more detail and statistical significance analysis.  
- Argument for acceptance: technically sound, relevant contribution with promising empirical results and practical efficiency.  
- Argument against acceptance: presentation favors mathematical rigor over intuition and omits key practical/empirical analyses affecting robustness.  
- Overall: solid contribution that would be stronger with clearer intuition, more implementation details, and additional ablations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add intuitive explanation of CMMD, elaborate computational considerations, and include some ablation studies.
