

The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core approach embeds conditional distributions into a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), leveraging a technique introduced by Song et al. (2009). The proposed method, CGMMN, is empirically evaluated on multiple real-world datasets. However, the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90) omits the term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (specifically their estimators). Furthermore, assuming $\mu_X = \mu_Y = 0$ (i.e., data centered in RKHS) is unconventional. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to CGMMN, the authors must clarify this missing term and its handling in gradient computation and practical implementations. While the approach is novel and elegant, the experimental results are underwhelming. For predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets. For generative performance, the absence of comparisons with conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs) leaves it unclear whether CGMMN produces superior samples.
