- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions, extending GMMN.  
- Addresses a gap: prior moment-matching approaches modeled only joint distributions while many applications need conditional modeling.  
- Builds on kernel embedding/MMD literature and introduces Conditional MMD (CMMD) as the training criterion.  
- Enables applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation.  
- Strong theoretical foundation for CMMD with sound mathematical justification.  
- Provides a clear generative process supporting efficient sampling and backpropagation-based optimization.  
- Implementation details for the conditional case are well-justified and feasible with standard deep learning tools.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation across datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face) showing competitive performance.  
- Demonstrates competitive predictive performance and diverse conditional sample generation.  
- Bayesian dark-knowledge application is compelling, showing efficient distillation without large performance loss.  
- Clarity is hindered by early introduction of complex formalism and insufficient intuitive explanations.  
- Derivation and computational implementation of the CMMD objective (equation 2) lack intuitive explanation.  
- Transition from the theoretical formulation to the practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) is not clearly explained.  
- Computational efficiency and scalability of the CMMD objective are not adequately discussed.  
- Originality is strong, and the approach is well-differentiated from conditional GANs (avoids their optimization challenges).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve intuition and clarity around equation (2) and implementation details.
