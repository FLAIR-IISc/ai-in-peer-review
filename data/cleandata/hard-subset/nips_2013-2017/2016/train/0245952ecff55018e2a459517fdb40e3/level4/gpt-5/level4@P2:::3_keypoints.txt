- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMN to model conditional distributions p(y|x) using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) objective.
- Generator architecture concatenates input x with noise h and maps to y via a deep network trained by back-propagation to minimize CMMD.
- Evaluated on supervised prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST digits conditioned on labels, Yale Faces), and a Bayesian distillation proof-of-concept (Boston Housing); results competitive with prior DGMs.
- Builds on kernel conditional mean embeddings and replaces unconditional MMD loss with a CMMD loss, yielding a single non-adversarial objective.
- Compared to GANs and conditional VAEs, CMMD offers simpler, stable optimization and avoids adversarial training, while being distinct from variational bounds.
- Theoretical use of CMMD is principled, but Theorem 3 asserting operator equality implies distribution equality lacks citation or proof and needs stated assumptions.
- Discussion of empirical conditional operator existence and regularization is appropriate but could be tightened.
- Minibatch training algorithm is clear; important observation that K̃−1 on x does not depend on network parameters simplifies computation.
- Empirical results show competitive classification and controllable conditional generation, but distillation experiment is limited to one dataset and model.
- Paper is generally well written and addresses many prior-review concerns (latent dimensionality, scalability, utility).
- Remark 1 (weighted-MMD interpretation of Eq. 2) is unclear: requires explicit derivation, unambiguous definition of the “dot” operator, and explicit forms for weighting matrices C1, C2, C3.
- Notation overload: avoid reusing symbol C for both conditional operators and weighting matrices.
- Theorem 3 needs a citation or appendix proof and explicit regularity assumptions (e.g., universality of kernels, invertibility/regularization conditions).
- Computational costs: cubic cost in minibatch size for forming K̃−1 needs discussion and possible mitigations (low-rank approximations, Nyström, block updates).
- Kernel and regularization sensitivity should be reported (choices of kernels/bandwidths on x and y, sensitivity to λ, effect of mismatched kernels).
- Ablation studies requested: latent dimensionality h and different ways to combine x and h (concatenation vs. additive noise).
- Generation evaluation should include quantitative metrics (e.g., Parzen-window likelihoods) and more challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) to demonstrate scalability.
- Distillation evaluation should be expanded beyond Boston/PBP to other Bayesian teachers and datasets to assess generality.
- Pros: principled extension of GMMN to conditional modeling, simple stable objective, compatible with MLP/CNN, versatile across tasks, addresses earlier review concerns.
- Cons: Theorem 3 lacks proof/citation, ambiguous/overloaded notation, limited generative experiments focused on digits/faces, narrow distillation evaluation, incomplete analysis of computational and kernel-selection trade-offs, and supervised tasks may not showcase strengths.
- Overall: Solid, useful contribution demonstrating feasibility of CMMD-trained conditional deep generators; requires clearer derivations/proofs and stronger experiments for broader impact.
