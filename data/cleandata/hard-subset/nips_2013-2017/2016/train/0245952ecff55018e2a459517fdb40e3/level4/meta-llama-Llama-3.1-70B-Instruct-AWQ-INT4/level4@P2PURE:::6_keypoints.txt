- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation and prediction using kernel embeddings and conditional MMD.
- Core method uses kernel embedding of conditional distributions and a conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) metric to match conditional distributions.
- Evaluations cover classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models, with noteworthy experimental results.
- Naive baseline of training separate GMMNs per conditional distribution (sharing network) suffers from data sparsity and should be compared as a baseline.
- Proposed approach models all conditional distributions as a family by matching the conditional embedding operator rather than individual conditionals.
- Suggestion to compare against the naive baseline in settings with limited conditioning values (e.g., MNIST).
- CMMD objective formulation is clean and its computation is almost identical to unconditional MMD; K matrices are independent of model parameters enabling backpropagation.
- Concern that CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid high stochasticity, and large minibatches make computation expensive due to matrix inverses.
- Presentation of technical material in sections 2.3 and 3.1 is unclear and relies on external references to follow.
- Minor issues: the paper’s claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect.
- Minor issue: the conditional embedding operator definition is inconsistent with reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator would be required.
