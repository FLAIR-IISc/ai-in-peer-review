- Proposes CGMMN: an extension of GMMNs that matches conditional (not joint) distributions via RKHS conditional embeddings (Song et al., 2009).
- Central algorithm uses the conditional covariance/operator embedding to learn conditional generative models.
- Key methodological concern: empirical conditional covariance operator definition appears to omit mean-embedding terms (μ_X, μ_Y), implying an implicit RKHS pre-centering assumption that must be clarified and analyzed for gradient computation and experiments.
- Empirical validation weak on predictive tasks: CGMMN shows higher error rates than state-of-the-art CMMVA on tested datasets.
- Generative evaluation incomplete: no comparisons to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), making sample quality hard to assess.
- Manuscript needs proofreading to fix typos (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").
- After the authors’ rebuttal, the reviewer’s concerns were fully addressed and scores were raised.
