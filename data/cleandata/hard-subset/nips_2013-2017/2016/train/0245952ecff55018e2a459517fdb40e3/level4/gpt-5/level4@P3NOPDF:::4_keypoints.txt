- Extends GMMNs to the conditional setting via a kernel embedding of conditional moments, allowing use of the same optimization machinery as the unconditional model.  
- Demonstrates the conditional GMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling Bayesian predictors, showing competitive performance.  
- Methodology is technically sound; derivations are plausible and align with prior kernel embedding and moment-matching work.  
- The empirical estimator derivation and integration into the training objective provide novelty and practical value despite the natural conceptual transition.  
- Algorithm 1 concisely summarizes training and makes the framework straightforward to apply in practice.  
- Experimental section is balanced and indicates the method is a viable alternative for conditional generation and model distillation.  
- Writing and presentation are clear; the appendix offers useful technical details that aid reproducibility.  
- Significance: kernel-based moment-matching for conditional generative modeling is appealing because it avoids adversarial training and is implementable.  
- Minor suggestions: include discussion/measurements of computational complexity, guidance on kernel choice and hyperparameter sensitivity, and clarification of potential failure modes.  
- Overall judgment: well-motivated, useful, and nontrivial extension of GMMNs to conditional generation; recommend acceptance.
