- Proposes CGMMN: a conditional generative model using a conditional MMD (CMMD) loss based on conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Extends GMMNs to conditional settings and demonstrates applicability across diverse generative tasks.  
- Experimental results are solid and comparable to original GMMN on digits and faces, showing promise for pure generative modeling.  
- Addresses prior reviewer concerns about latent space dimensionality, model utility, and clarifications.  
- Remark 1’s mapping to weighted MMD is unclear; need to clarify whether dot denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product.  
- Reuse of symbol ‘C’ for both conditional embedding and within Equation (2) is potentially confusing and should be disambiguated.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a citation or proof; authors should provide a reference or include the proof.  
- Bayesian dark knowledge application is interesting but evaluated on only a single dataset/model, limiting assessment of broader applicability.  
- Wider adoption requires stronger empirical validation on more challenging benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Paper is technically sound with a well-defined objective and clear methodology; claims are supported by experiments.  
- Writing and organization are generally good, with mostly clear mathematical notation aside from the noted ambiguities.  
- Originality: novel application of MMD to conditional generative modeling, logically extending GMMNs and conditional kernel embeddings.  
- Significance: offers a potentially valuable, simpler alternative to GANs/VAEs, but impact depends on validation on harder datasets.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, clear presentation, competitive performance, responsive to prior reviews.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experiments on simple datasets, insufficient evaluation of Bayesian dark knowledge, clarity issues in Remark 1, missing reference for Theorem 3.
