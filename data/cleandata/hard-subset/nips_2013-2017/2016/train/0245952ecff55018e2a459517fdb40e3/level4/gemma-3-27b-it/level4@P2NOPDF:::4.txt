This paper extends the deep generative modeling approach of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) to the conditional generation setting. The authors achieve this by developing a kernel embedding of the moments that define the conditional distribution, enabling optimization using the same techniques as the original, unconditional GMMN framework. The proposed conditional GMMN demonstrates competitive performance across a range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation from a Bayesian model. 

The paper appears technically sound, though a complete verification of all equations was not possible within the scope of this review. The extension to conditional generation feels natural given the existing GMMN framework; however, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious, indicating a non-trivial contribution. This suggests the work possesses both novelty and practical utility. 

The derived framework is straightforward to apply, as clearly illustrated in Algorithm 1, and should be valuable to researchers and practitioners interested in deep generative modeling or the simplification of more complex statistical procedures like Bayesian inference. The paper is well-written and easy to follow, and the appendix provides comprehensive details regarding the methodology and experimental setup, enhancing its reproducibility and clarity. Overall, this work presents a useful and potentially impactful extension to existing generative modeling techniques.
