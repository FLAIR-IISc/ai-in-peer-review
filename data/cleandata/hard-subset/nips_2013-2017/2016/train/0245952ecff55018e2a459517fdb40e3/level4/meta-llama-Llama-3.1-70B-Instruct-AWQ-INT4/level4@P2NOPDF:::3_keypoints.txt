- Proposes a novel loss for directed conditional generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings, extending unconditional MMD-based loss.
- Demonstrates method on classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.
- Authors addressed prior minor concerns about low-dimensional choices, scalability to higher-dimensional latent spaces, and model utility.
- Request clarification on Remark 1: ambiguous dot notation in mapping from Eq.(2) to weighted MMD—matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product.
- Notation conflict: variable C is confusing because it’s already used for the conditional embedding; recommend a distinct symbol.
- Theorems 1 and 2 are referenced from other papers, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference or provided proof—add one for consistency.
- Empirical results are satisfactory and demonstrate model versatility, though supervised learning may not be the most effective application.
- Distilled Bayesian inference experiment is interesting but shown on only one dataset and one model, limiting generality.
- Method is most promising for pure generative modeling rather than supervised tasks.
- Experiments are comparable to the original GMM paper and use adequate digit and face datasets to show feasibility.
- Recommend stronger, more challenging benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) to encourage broader adoption.
