The authors propose an extension to the generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by shifting the focus from matching joint distributions to matching conditional distributions. This key idea leverages the technique of embedding conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), which was first introduced by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) algorithm is empirically evaluated on several real-world datasets. However, upon examining the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90), it appears that the term related to the estimators of $\muX$ and $\muY$ is omitted. Furthermore, assuming that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero, implying that the data is already centered in the RKHS, is unconventional. Given the central role of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ in CGMMN, clarification on this missing term, as well as its treatment in gradient computation and real-world experiments, is necessary. While the proposed approach is novel and elegant, the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. Specifically, in terms of predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art Conditional Moment-Matching Variational Autoencoder (CMMVA) on both datasets. Moreover, since a comparison between CGMMN and other conditional generative methods, such as conditional generative adversarial networks, is not provided, it is unclear whether the proposed algorithm can generate superior samples. Additionally, there are several typos throughout the paper, including "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
