- Extends moment matching networks (MMNs) to the conditional setting, which is non-trivial because mini-batches contain many different conditionals.  
- Main concern: presentation is overly formal and lacks intuitive explanation of the method.  
- Equation (2) is introduced without explaining the intuition behind the objective.  
- The paper does not show how Equation (2) reduces to concrete mini-batch operations.  
- It fails to state which estimators are used and under what assumptions they are valid.  
- Computational and memory costs per iteration are not provided.  
- Unlike the unconditional MMN, the conditional case needs an accessible, intuition-first treatment rather than heavy RKHS machinery.  
- Important algorithmic details are relegated to the supplement and should be summarized in the main text.  
- The paper should explain from first principles what mismatch Equation (2) minimizes and why it is the appropriate conditional analogue.  
- Needs explicit steps from Equation (2) to implementation: how conditionals are organized in a batch, how sample pairs are formed, which kernels/features are used, and how gradients are computed.  
- Must state assumptions required for practical estimators (e.g., unbiasedness of mini-batch estimates, independence across conditionals, kernel properties).  
- Clarify why simply feeding the conditional to the network is insufficient in mini-batch updates and how the proposed estimator resolves the distribution mismatch.  
- Recommend adding a small worked example (e.g., a batch with two distinct conditionals) and clear statements on efficiency and assumptions in the main paper.
