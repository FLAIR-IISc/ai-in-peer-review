- Paper presents a non-trivial extension of moment matching networks to the conditional setting.
- Main technical challenge: matching mini-batches of generated samples to corresponding target distributions conditioned on different inputs.
- Because targets in a mini-batch differ, the usual distribution-matching objective becomes more complex.
- A naive solution of using the same conditional input per mini-batch is computationally inefficient.
- Primary weakness: poor presentation—paper jumps into RKHS formalism and equation (2) without building intuition.
- Unlike the original unconditional paper, the conditional objective lacks a clear, intuitive explanation.
- The manuscript fails to clearly map the theoretical objective (eq. 2) to a practical algorithm.
- Missing details on concrete computational steps, efficiency considerations, and implementation assumptions.
- Some implementation information is relegated to the supplement but is hard to follow.
- Requested improvements: provide an intuitive derivation/explanation of eq. (2) and a precise, step-by-step translation into a practical algorithm.
