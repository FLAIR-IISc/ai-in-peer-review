- Contribution: Proposes conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMNs) using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) to model conditional distributions for generation and prediction.

- Key methodological idea: Match the model to the conditional embedding operator directly (treating all conditionals as a family) instead of fitting a separate GMMN per conditional to avoid data sparsity.

- Empirical suggestion: A naive baseline training separate GMMNs per condition (with shared parameters) could perform reasonably in some settings (e.g., MNIST) and should be included.

- Implementation note: The CMMD objective is formulated so its kernel matrices are independent of model parameters, allowing backpropagation similarly to unconditional MMD.

- Computational concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid stochasticity dominating the error, and large minibatches make costly matrix inverses that could be prohibitive — this needs discussion and empirical characterization.

- Evaluation: Experiments on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation are interesting and indicate broad usefulness.

- Presentation issue: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and the paper should be made more self-contained to aid readability.

- Minor correctness issues: Claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect; and the paper’s definition of the conditional embedding operator seems inconsistent with reference [29].
