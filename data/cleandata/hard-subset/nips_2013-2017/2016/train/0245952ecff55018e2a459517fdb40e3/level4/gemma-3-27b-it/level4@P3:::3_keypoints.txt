- Proposes conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) using a conditional MMD (CMMD) to model conditional distributions.  
- Leverages kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions to provide a principled input-dependent generative framework.  
- Extends GMMNs to the conditional setting, addressing their inability to handle input-dependent outputs.  
- CMMD offers an alternative to GAN-style min-max optimization and enables simpler, stable training via backpropagation.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear derivation grounded in kernel methods and RKHS concepts.  
- Demonstrates versatility across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Experiments on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face show competitive results with existing methods.  
- Writing and presentation are clear, connections to prior work are well established, and previous reviewer concerns were addressed.  
- Equation (2) and the remark relating CMMD to a weighted MMD are ambiguous—dot notation needs clarification (matrix vs. Hadamard product).  
- Theorem 3, a key condition for conditional distribution equivalence, lacks citation or proof and needs theoretical justification.  
- Bayesian distillation experiment is promising but evaluated on only a single dataset/model, limiting claims of generality.  
- Experimental scope is limited to relatively simple datasets; results on more complex datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) are needed to demonstrate scalability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension; CMMD is a viable training alternative; competitive empirical performance; authors responsive to feedback.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing proof/citation for Theorem 3, limited evaluation on complex datasets, and insufficient breadth in Bayesian distillation experiments.  
- Overall assessment: valuable and practical contribution to conditional deep generative modeling; recommend accept with minor revisions addressing Eq.2 clarity, Theorem 3, and discussion/limitations of evaluations.
