- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.
- Core idea: learn an embedding operator mapping conditioning variables to target distributions, avoiding per-conditional distribution estimation and data sparsity.
- Works across tasks (classification, conditional generation, Bayesian model distillation) with encouraging experimental results.
- Naive baseline (separate GMMNs per conditional) can work when conditioning is low-cardinality (e.g., discrete MNIST); a direct comparison is recommended.
- Derivation of CMMD is elegant and parallels unconditional MMD; kernel matrices are parameter-independent, allowing backpropagation.
- Major computational concern: CMMD likely needs large minibatches and costly matrix inversions for stable gradients, which the paper does not address.
- Paper should discuss mitigation strategies for computational bottlenecks (approximate inverses, alternative optimizers).
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 lack clarity and require more self-contained exposition and notation explanation.
- Minor factual issues: claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect; the estimator is generally biased.
- Minor technical inconsistency: the conditional embedding operator definition differs from reference [29] and needs clarification.
