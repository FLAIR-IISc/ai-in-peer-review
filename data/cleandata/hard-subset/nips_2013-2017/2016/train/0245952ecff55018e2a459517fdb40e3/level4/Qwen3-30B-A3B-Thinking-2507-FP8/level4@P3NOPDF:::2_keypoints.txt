- Paper extends moment matching networks (MMN) to a non-trivial conditional setting requiring batch-wise distribution matching under varying conditions.  
- Core contribution is meaningful but the paper’s presentation severely undermines clarity and impact.  
- Main weakness: the paper lacks intuitive motivation and launches directly into formal mathematics.  
- The conditional formulation (Eq. 2) is presented without explanation of why that specific form addresses the problem.  
- Key unanswered intuition: how to reason toward Eq. (2) from the goal of matching distributions across varying conditions.  
- Key unanswered algorithmic question: the concrete computational steps from Eq. (2) to a practical implementation are unclear.  
- Omission of computational assumptions (kernel choices, scalability approximations) makes feasibility unclear.  
- The paper fails to quantify computational complexity relative to standard MMN or other alternatives.  
- It does not explain how the conditional term is integrated without degenerating into inefficient per-example updates.  
- The claim that "distributions are not the same anymore" is not elaborated—missing which distributions differ and why the objective is necessary/sufficient.  
- Recommended restructuring: start with a clear intuitive problem statement and derive Eq. (2) as a natural solution.  
- Authors should map algorithmic steps explicitly to Eq. (2) and highlight computational trade-offs (e.g., complexity per batch).  
- As written, the work risks being seen as a technical exercise rather than a principled methodological advance.  
- Recommendation: Major revision to clarify intuition, answer the two key questions in the main text, and provide a step-by-step computational breakdown.
