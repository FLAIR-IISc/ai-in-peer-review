- Paper extends moment matching networks (MMNs) to the conditional setting, a non-trivial and potentially valuable problem.  
- Presentation obscures core ideas by starting with heavy formal math instead of building intuition.  
- The key challenge is matching distributions of mini-batches conditioned on different inputs—this differs fundamentally from the original MMN.  
- The paper fails to explain how distribution matching is achieved in practice and what the computational costs are.  
- Equation (2) is presented without sufficient derivation, intuition, or explanation of underlying principles.  
- It is unclear what concrete computations are performed to minimize the objective in equation (2).  
- Missing description of necessary assumptions for optimization success and conditions under which the method may fail.  
- Computational complexity and scaling (batch size, input/output dimensionality) are not analyzed or compared to alternatives.  
- Supplementary material briefly touches issues but remains difficult to follow and insufficiently clarifying.  
- Authors should provide an intuitive derivation of the objective and explain why its form was chosen.  
- A detailed algorithmic walkthrough bridging equation (2) to the implemented method is needed (core computations, step-by-step).  
- A clear discussion of assumptions, limitations, and practical implications would greatly improve clarity and impact.
