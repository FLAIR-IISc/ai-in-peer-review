This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard benchmark tasks for discriminative and generative capabilities. Overall, this is an interesting extension for the sub-area of deep learning research focused on building generative models.

The paper text is reasonably clear, although the mathematical derivations can be somewhat challenging to follow at times. The authors have done a good job in explaining the key concepts and ideas, making the paper accessible to readers with a basic understanding of deep learning and generative models.

The tasks used in the experiments are somewhat small but not entirely unreasonable when compared to other recent work in this area. The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their approach on several benchmark datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face.

One potential area for improvement is in providing more details about the computational efficiency of the proposed approach. As the algorithm for training the model involves some computationally expensive steps, it would be helpful to include some comments in the experiment section on how long the model takes to train compared to existing approaches like DSN.

Additionally, making the reference code available for the experiments would significantly strengthen the paper. This would allow practitioners to easily reproduce the exact models evaluated in the paper and explore the proposed approach in more detail.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors have demonstrated a good understanding of the related work in the area and have provided a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

Overall, I would recommend this paper for publication, pending some minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above. The paper has the potential to make a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning and generative models, and the authors have demonstrated a good understanding of the key concepts and ideas.
