- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs) that extend GMMNs to model conditional distributions using a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.
- CMMD is derived from differences between kernel mean embeddings of conditional operators, allowing training of p(y|x) via standard back-propagation.
- Demonstrates versatility with experiments on predictive modeling, contextual image generation, and distillation from a Bayesian teacher to a simpler student network.
- Original and significant contribution: non-trivial extension of moment-matching to the conditional setting with a novel empirical CMMD estimator.
- Technically sound and empirically validated across diverse tasks (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Faces, regression distillation), showing competitive performance for a generative model.
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization, with useful background and a detailed appendix aiding reproducibility.
- Limitation: predictive performance does not beat state-of-the-art discriminative models (e.g., Deeply-Supervised Nets) on MNIST/SVHN, which warrants brief discussion.
- Limitation: derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator (Equation 2) is dense and would benefit from more intuition or a step-by-step appendix walkthrough.
