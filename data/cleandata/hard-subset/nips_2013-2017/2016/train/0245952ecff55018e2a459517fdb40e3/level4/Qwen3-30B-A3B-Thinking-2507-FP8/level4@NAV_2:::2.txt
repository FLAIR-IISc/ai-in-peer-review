

The authors propose Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to handle conditional distributions. This work addresses a significant gap in the literature, as previous moment-matching approaches like GMMN (Li et al., 2015) only model joint distributions, while many applications require conditional modeling. The paper builds upon the kernel embedding techniques used in MMD (Gretton et al., 2012) and extends them to conditional distributions through the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. This is a meaningful extension that enables applications in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, as demonstrated in the experiments.

The paper demonstrates strong technical quality, with a sound theoretical foundation for CMMD and thorough experimental evaluation across multiple datasets. The authors provide a clear generative process that allows for efficient sampling and backpropagation-based optimization. The implementation details for the conditional case are well-justified, and the experimental results show competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods in predictive modeling (MNIST and SVHN) and generative tasks (MNIST and Yale Face datasets).

The clarity of the paper is somewhat hindered by the early introduction of complex mathematical formalism without sufficient intuitive explanation. While the theoretical development is rigorous, the paper could better connect the mathematical formulation to practical implementation. Specifically, the derivation of the CMMD objective (equation 2) and its computational implementation could benefit from more intuitive explanation. The transition from the theoretical formulation to the practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) is not sufficiently explained, making it difficult for readers to understand the actual computations performed during training. A more intuitive explanation of how equation (2) leads to the practical optimization procedure would greatly enhance the paper's accessibility.

The originality of this work is strong, as it successfully extends moment-matching networks to the conditional setting, addressing a clear need in the field. While conditional GANs (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) exist, they face optimization challenges that CGMMN avoids by using the simpler CMMD criterion. The paper adequately distinguishes its approach from related work, particularly in the introduction and discussion.

The significance of the work is demonstrated through compelling applications that show CGMMN's versatility. The results on predictive modeling are competitive with state-of-the-art discriminative models, and the contextual generation results show the model's ability to produce diverse samples conditioned on specific inputs. The Bayesian dark knowledge application is particularly interesting, showing how CGMMN can efficiently distill knowledge from Bayesian models without significant performance degradation.

Strengths:
- Novel extension of moment-matching networks to conditional settings
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple domains
- Theoretical foundation is sound with proper mathematical justification
- Practical implementation is feasible with standard deep learning techniques
- Demonstrates competitive performance on standard benchmarks

Weaknesses:
- Insufficient intuitive explanation of the CMMD formulation (equation 2)
- Limited connection between theoretical formulation and practical implementation
- The paper starts with complex mathematical formalism without providing intuition
- Computational efficiency of the CMMD objective is not adequately discussed
- The transition from theory to algorithm (Algorithm 1) could be clearer

The paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling by extending moment-matching networks to conditional distributions. With improved explanation of the CMMD objective and its practical implementation, this work would be significantly stronger. The current work is technically sound and demonstrates clear applicability, but would benefit from greater clarity in explaining the core algorithmic innovation. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the clarity issues, particularly regarding the intuition behind equation (2) and its implementation.
