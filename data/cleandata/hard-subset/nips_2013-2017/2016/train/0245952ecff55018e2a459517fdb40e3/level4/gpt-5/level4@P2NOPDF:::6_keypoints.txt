- Introduces conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.  
- Core idea: match the model to the conditional embedding operator (family of conditional distributions) rather than matching each condition separately to overcome data sparsity.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models with promising results.  
- Note that a simple shared-parameter GMMN per-condition baseline is a natural comparator; for small discrete condition sets (e.g., MNIST labels) it may perform well and should be included.  
- CMMD formulation is sound and computable similarly to unconditional MMD; Gram matrices in Eq. (2) are independent of model parameters enabling backprop.  
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches because small batches induce high gradient noise; large batches increase computation and may be costly.  
- Computational cost worry: matrix inverses and Gram-matrix operations can be prohibitively expensive—need discussion of complexity, batch-size sensitivity, and practical tricks (regularization, approximations).  
- Presentation/readability issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and rely on prior work; more self-contained explanations and intuition are needed.  
- Minor correction 1: Claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is inaccurate—clarify which estimator (U-statistic vs. biased V-statistic) is used.  
- Minor correction 2: Definition of the conditional embedding operator appears inconsistent with Reference [29]; reconcile notation and assumptions or justify the differing definition.  
- Overall: principled and appealing extension with solid objective and interesting applications, but would be strengthened by adding the baseline, clarifying computational/implementation details, and improving exposition.
