This paper introduces conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions, extending the previous work on generative moment matching networks (GMMN). The core idea is to adapt the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion to a conditional setting, using a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective. The authors demonstrate the utility of CGMMN across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, reporting competitive performance on various datasets.

This work builds upon the recent successes of moment matching approaches to generative modeling, particularly GMMN [19], which offered a simpler alternative to GANs. While extending GMMN to the conditional setting may appear straightforward—simply feeding the conditional variables into the network—the authors correctly identify the non-trivial challenge of matching distributions over a large number of inputs, necessitating the development of CMMD. The paper relates to broader research in conditional generative models, including conditional GANs [21] and conditional variational autoencoders [28], offering a distinct approach based on moment matching.

The strengths of this paper lie in its theoretically sound extension of MMD to the conditional case and its empirical validation on a diverse set of tasks. The proposed CMMD criterion provides a well-defined objective for learning conditional distributions, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN in various scenarios. The inclusion of Bayesian dark knowledge distillation as an application is particularly interesting, showcasing the practical utility of the framework. The authors clearly articulate the mathematical foundations of their approach, leveraging concepts from kernel methods and reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces.

However, the presentation of the methodology could be improved. The paper initially focuses on the mathematical formalism of CMMD, potentially obscuring the intuitive motivation behind the objective function. A clearer explanation of how CMMD relates to the original MMD and how the conditional setting necessitates the more complex formulation would be beneficial. Specifically, the derivation and computational aspects of the CMMD objective (Eq. 2) are not sufficiently detailed. The authors state the computational complexity but lack detail on its practical implications or optimizations. Further clarification on the assumptions underlying the CMMD objective and their impact on the algorithm's performance would also strengthen the work. While some details are provided in the supplementary material, integrating these crucial aspects into the main text would improve readability and accessibility.  The connection between the theoretical formulation and the practical algorithm implementation isn’t immediately obvious. Addressing questions like “How does one intuitively arrive at Eq. (2)?” and “What are the concrete steps from Eq. (2) to the actual implementation?” would be extremely valuable.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and technically sound extension of moment matching to conditional distributions.
*   Competitive performance across a range of tasks demonstrating practical utility.
*   Clear mathematical formulation grounded in kernel methods.
*   Addresses an important problem in generative modeling—learning conditional distributions.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The initial presentation of the CMMD criterion is overly mathematical and lacks intuitive explanation.
*   Insufficient detail regarding the computational efficiency and practical implementation of the CMMD objective.
*   Limited discussion of the assumptions underlying the CMMD objective and their impact on performance.
