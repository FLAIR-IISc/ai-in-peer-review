Review- The authors propose a conditional extension of moment matching networks. Unlike models that update on a single example—where the extension is trivial (feed the conditioning into the network)—this setting is non-trivial. The challenge is that we must match distributions over many samples (a mini-batch) to many inputs, yet these distributions are no longer identical (unless the same conditional is reused for an update, which is inefficient). The main issue with the paper is that it begins with heavy formalism instead of building intuition for the objective, and it does not make clear what computations the algorithm actually performs or how efficient they are.

The authors extend moment matching networks to the conditional setting. Unlike single-example update models, where the extension is straightforward (inject the conditioning), this case is non-trivial because we must align distributions of many samples to many inputs, but those distributions differ (unless the same conditional is reused, which is inefficient). The central problem is that the paper does not explain the details of the final objective being optimized (eq. (2)), its computational efficiency, or the assumptions that make it workable, so the reader cannot see what computations are actually carried out. Likewise, the intuition is missing; the exposition jumps straight into formal mathematics. The non-conditional version can be explained (as in the original paper) without heavy machinery (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert space)—the formula being minimized is intuitive. It would be helpful to provide an equally clear intuitive explanation for the conditional case. It would be good to address the following:
- How one would intuitively derive formula (2).
- The concrete steps from formula (2) to the practical algorithm.
