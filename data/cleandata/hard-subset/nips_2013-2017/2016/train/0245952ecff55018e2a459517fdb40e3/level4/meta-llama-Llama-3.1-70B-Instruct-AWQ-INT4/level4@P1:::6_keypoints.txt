- Paper introduces Conditional GMMNs (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD metric.
- CGMMN is evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.
- Proposed method matches the family of conditional distributions via a conditional embedding operator rather than fitting separate GMMNs per conditioning value.
- Naive baseline (separate GMMN per conditional distribution) suffers from data sparsity, especially with continuous conditioning, but could perform reasonably when conditioning takes few discrete values (e.g., MNIST).
- Reviewer recommends including the naive per-condition GMMN baseline for comparison.
- Conditional MMD objective is nicely formulated and computable similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices are parameter-independent and backpropagation is straightforward.
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid stochastic minibatch error, making computation (especially matrix inverses) expensive.
- Presentation in sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow and relies heavily on referenced papers.
- Minor errors: incorrect claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased; conditional embedding operator form inconsistent with reference [29] without altering the cross-covariance definition.
- Strengths summarized: a principled new way to model conditional distributions, interesting experimental results, and a computable conditional MMD objective.
- Weaknesses summarized: clarity issues in exposition, potential computational/minibatch costs, and lack of comprehensive comparison to naive approaches.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel method, competitive experiments, and generally well-written paper.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems, computational concerns, and missing baseline comparisons.
- Overall verdict: good contribution that requires minor revisions to address clarity, minor errors, and baseline/computational concerns.
