- Paper proposes Conditional GMMN (CGMMN): model p(y|x) by passing x and noise h through a network and training by minimizing Conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings for conditionals and on MMD-based GMMN training; evaluated on prediction, conditional generation, and model distillation with competitive results.
- Strength: natural, principled extension of GMMN to conditional distributions, broadening applicability.
- Strength: training is simpler and more stable than adversarial min–max methods, using straightforward backprop and sampling.
- Strength: CMMD objective is principled (operator norm between conditional mean embeddings) and connects to prior kernel literature.
- Strength: empirical results are competitive across tasks and architectures and compatible with standard optimizers.
- Main weakness: paper lacks intuitive explanation of CMMD and derivation/intuition for Eq. (2).
- Main weakness: under-specified transition from Eq. (2) to a practical, efficient mini-batch algorithm; supplement insufficiently clear.
- Main weakness: insufficient discussion of computational complexity and efficiency (Gram matrix formation/inversion scale poorly with batch size).
- Main weakness: practical implications of assumptions for CMMD (smoothness, regularization, Hilbert–Schmidt conditions) are not discussed.
- Main weakness: unclear how often and at what cost K̃−1 for x is computed and whether it can be cached efficiently.
- Main weakness: Algorithm 1 omits critical implementation details (kernel choices, normalization, bandwidth selection, number of h samples per x, x perturbation).
- Request: provide an intuitive derivation of Eq. (2), e.g., show CMMD as a weighted MMD over outputs related to kernel ridge regression.
- Request: explicitly explain steps to implement the mini-batch algorithm, how to construct K and L, stable computation of K̃−1 (Cholesky), and gradient flow.
- Request: include a brief complexity analysis: per-iteration time/memory vs. batch size, output dim, kernel costs, and wall-clock training times.
- Request: detail kernel choices and hyperparameter selection strategies for x and y (e.g., Gaussian kernels, bandwidth heuristics); discuss non-differentiable conditioning kernels.
- Request: clarify how conditioning inputs x for data and generated sets are chosen per iteration (same x vs. perturbed) and implications for bias/variance.
- Suggestion: add a short, self-contained section prioritizing operational clarity and algorithmic blueprint over heavy formalism.
- Quality assessment: method is technically sound and grounded in kernel embedding theory but under-specifies computational steps hindering reproducibility.
- Clarity assessment: organization should prioritize intuition and practical algorithmic detail before formal operator notation; supplement is hard to navigate.
- Originality: combination of deep conditional generative modeling with conditional mean embeddings and operator-norm training appears novel.
- Significance: if clarified and made practical, CMMD-trained CGMMNs could be a useful stable alternative to adversarial/variational methods.
- Pros summary: principled extension, simple non-adversarial training, broad applicability with competitive results.
- Cons summary: poor intuitive explanation and practical algorithmic detail, limited discussion of complexity and hyperparameters, supplement insufficient.
- Actionable questions: request intuitive derivation of Eq. (2), exact mini-batch implementation and per-iteration complexity, kernel/bandwidth choices, sensitivity to λ/batch size/h dimension, and wall-clock/memory stats.
