This paper proposes conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN), a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables. The method extends generative moment matching networks by utilizing a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, leveraging recent work on conditional kernel mean embeddings. The approach is demonstrated across diverse generative tasks, including predictive modeling, handwritten digit and face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation. This is my second review of this paper; previous minor concerns regarding the dimensionality of the latent space, model utility compared to alternatives, and clarification points have been addressed. Regarding Remark 1, the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is currently unclear. Further detail clarifying whether the dot notation represents matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product would be beneficial. The use of ‘C’ to define both the conditional embedding and within Equation (2) is potentially confusing. Theorems 1 and 2 cite relevant papers, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference; a citation to a proof or the inclusion of a proof itself is necessary.

The empirical results are solid. While previous reviews questioned the suitability of applying this model to supervised learning, the authors rightly emphasize demonstrating the model’s versatility, which is a reasonable justification. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge is interesting, though its evaluation is currently limited to a single dataset and model, making it difficult to assess broader applicability. The method’s potential is likely greatest in purely generative modeling, where the experiments demonstrate performance comparable to the original GMMN paper. The datasets – digits and faces – are adequate for initial validation.

Wider adoption will require compelling results on more challenging benchmarks like CIFAR-10 or LSUN.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound, with a well-defined objective function and a clear explanation of the methodology. Claims are supported by experimental results, and the authors acknowledge limitations. It presents a complete piece of work, though extending the experiments to more complex datasets would strengthen the contribution.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is mostly clear, but as noted above, the mapping in Remark 1 could benefit from further elaboration. The paper adequately informs the reader, providing sufficient detail for an expert to understand the approach.

**Originality:** The work presents a novel application of MMD to conditional generative modeling. It’s a logical extension of previous work on GMMNs and conditional kernel embeddings. The combination of these techniques and the resulting CMMD criterion are original contributions. Related work is appropriately referenced.

**Significance:** The results are important, particularly for the field of generative modeling. CGMMN provides a potentially valuable alternative to GANs and VAEs, offering a simpler training process. While the current results are promising, further validation on more challenging datasets is necessary to fully establish its significance and potential for broader impact. The Bayesian dark knowledge application is also noteworthy and suggests potential for practical use.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Technically sound and novel extension of existing methods.
*   Clear and well-organized presentation.
*   Competitive performance on several generative tasks.
*   Addresses previous reviewer comments.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Experiments are limited to relatively simple datasets.
*   Bayesian dark knowledge application is not extensively evaluated.
*   Clarity could be improved in Remark 1 regarding the mapping to weighted MMD.
*   Theorem 3 lacks a reference.
