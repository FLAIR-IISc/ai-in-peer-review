This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard, small-scale benchmark tasks to assess its discriminative and generative capabilities.

Overall, this is an interesting contribution to the subfield of deep generative models. The paper is reasonably clear, although, like many papers in this area, the mathematical derivations can be difficult to follow at times. The tasks used in the experiments are somewhat small-scale but are comparable to those in other recent work.

Given that the training algorithm involves computationally expensive steps, the experiments section would benefit from a discussion of the model's training time compared to existing approaches, such as DSN. The paper would also be much stronger if reference code were made available, as the current model description may lack sufficient detail for a practitioner to reproduce the exact models evaluated.
