- Paper proposes a conditional MMD loss for directed generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Method extends GMMNs by replacing unconditional MMD with a conditional variant.  
- Evaluated on classification, handwritten digits, faces, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Previous minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space, scalability, comparative utility, clarifications) appear addressed in the revision.  
- Equation (2) mapping to weighted MMD is unclear; dot notation (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard) must be explicitly defined.  
- Choice of symbol C is unfortunate due to collision with the conditional embedding notation.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 relate to prior work; Theorem 3 lacks a citation or included proof and needs one.  
- Empirical results are decent and comparable to the original GMM paper for digits and faces.  
- Supervised learning application is not the most compelling, but the paper reasonably aims to showcase versatility.  
- Bayesian distillation example is interesting but demonstrated on only one dataset/model, limiting evidence of broader usefulness.  
- Likely most traction will be in pure generative modeling; adoption would be strengthened by results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
