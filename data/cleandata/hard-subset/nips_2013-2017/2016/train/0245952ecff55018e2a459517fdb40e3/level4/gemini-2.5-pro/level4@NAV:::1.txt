This paper introduces a neural network approach founded on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The proposed method is evaluated for its discriminative and generative performance on standard, small-scale benchmark tasks.

Overall, this work represents an interesting contribution to the subfield of deep learning focused on generative models. The paper is written with reasonable clarity; however, akin to many papers in this area, the mathematical derivations can be difficult to follow at times. The experimental tasks are somewhat small in scale, yet this is not entirely unreasonable when compared with other recent literature in this domain.

As the training algorithm seems to involve computationally expensive steps, I would request that the authors comment on the model's training duration in the experiments section, especially in comparison to existing approaches like DSN. The paper's contribution would be substantially stronger if reference code for the experiments were made available. Based on the model section, a practitioner might find it challenging to reproduce the exact models that were evaluated.
