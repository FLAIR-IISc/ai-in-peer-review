- Paper extends moment matching networks to a conditional setting, which is non-trivial because updates must match minibatch-derived distributions to many different conditions.
- Matching distributions fails unless the same condition is used for every update, which would be inefficient.
- Main weakness: heavy reliance on complex mathematical formalism without first providing intuition for the conditional case.
- The authors do not clearly describe the computations performed by the algorithm or analyze its efficiency.
- Derivation and justification of the optimized formula (eq (2)) are insufficiently explained; required assumptions and validity are not stated.
- Unlike the original non-conditional method (which had RKHS-based intuition and a clear minimization target), the conditional method lacks an analogous intuitive explanation.
- Reviewer asks for an intuitive derivation of formula (2).
- Reviewer asks for concrete, step-by-step explanation of how formula (2) is turned into the implemented algorithm, since supplement details are hard to follow.
