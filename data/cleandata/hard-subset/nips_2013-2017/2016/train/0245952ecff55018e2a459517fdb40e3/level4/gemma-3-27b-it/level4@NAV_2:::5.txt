The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting, proposing conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions. The core idea is to leverage kernel mean embeddings to match conditional distributions, building upon the work of Song et al. (2009). The proposed CGMMN is evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks. A potential oversight exists in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), where terms related to $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (and their estimators) appear to be missing. Furthermore, the assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero – effectively centering the data in the reproducing kernel Hilbert space – is unusual and warrants clarification, along with a discussion of its implications for gradient computation and experimental results. The approach represents a novel and well-motivated extension to existing generative modeling techniques. However, the empirical results are somewhat underwhelming. On both MNIST and SVHN datasets, CGMMN achieves higher error rates compared to the state-of-the-art CMMVA (Li et al., 2016) for predictive tasks. In generative tasks, the absence of a direct comparison to other conditional generative models – such as conditional generative adversarial networks (Mirza & Osindero, 2014) – makes it difficult to assess the relative performance of CGMMN. The paper contains minor typographical errors, including “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238). 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of GMMNs to conditional distributions.
*   Solid theoretical foundation based on kernel mean embeddings.
*   Demonstrated applicability across diverse tasks (prediction, generation, distillation).

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Empirical performance is not state-of-the-art on predictive tasks.
*   Lack of comparison to other conditional generative models.
*   Potential issue in the formulation of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ and the assumption of zero-centered data.
*   Minor typographical errors.
