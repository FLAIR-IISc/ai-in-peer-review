This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion, presenting an interesting extension to the sub-area of deep learning research focused on building generative models. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard benchmark tasks for discriminative and generative capabilities.

The paper text is reasonably clear, although, like most papers in this area, the mathematical derivations can be challenging to follow at times. The experimental tasks used are somewhat small but not entirely unreasonable when compared against other recent work in this area.

One potential area for improvement is the inclusion of more detailed information on computational efficiency in the experiment section. Specifically, it would be beneficial to provide comments on how long the model takes to train compared to existing approaches, such as DSN. This would help to contextualize the proposed method's performance and provide a more comprehensive understanding of its strengths and limitations.

Furthermore, making reference code available for the experiments would significantly strengthen the paper. As it stands, a practitioner may encounter difficulties in reproducing the exact models evaluated in the paper, particularly in the model section. Providing code would not only facilitate replication but also enable other researchers to build upon and extend the proposed method more easily.

Overall, the paper presents a novel approach to building generative models using neural networks. While some minor improvements can be made to enhance clarity and reproducibility, the work contributes to the ongoing efforts in deep learning research and has the potential to inspire further developments in this area.
