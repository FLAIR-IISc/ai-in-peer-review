The authors have successfully extended moment matching networks to their conditional counterpart. Notably, this extension is non-trivial, particularly when compared to models that can be updated with a single example, where incorporating conditionals is relatively straightforward. The challenge arises when attempting to match distributions of a large number of samples (mini-batch) to a large number of inputs, as these distributions are no longer identical unless the same conditional is applied for a given update, which is inefficient.

The primary concern with this paper is its lack of intuitive explanation for the underlying formula and the computational efficiency of the proposed algorithm. The authors delve into complex mathematical formulations without providing a clear understanding of the actual computations involved and their implications on efficiency. Specifically, the formula presented in equation (2) lacks a detailed explanation, including the assumptions made to optimize it and the resulting computational process.

In contrast, the non-conditional version of the algorithm can be explained intuitively, as demonstrated in the original paper, without resorting to advanced mathematical concepts such as reproducing kernel Hilbert space. The minimized formula in this case is straightforward and easy to comprehend.

To enhance the clarity and accessibility of the paper, it would be beneficial for the authors to provide an intuitive explanation for the conditional case, akin to the non-conditional version. Addressing the following questions would also be helpful:

1. How can one intuitively derive formula (2)?
2. What are the steps involved in transitioning from formula (2) to the practical algorithm? While some of this information is included in the supplement, it is not easily comprehensible and warrants further elaboration.
