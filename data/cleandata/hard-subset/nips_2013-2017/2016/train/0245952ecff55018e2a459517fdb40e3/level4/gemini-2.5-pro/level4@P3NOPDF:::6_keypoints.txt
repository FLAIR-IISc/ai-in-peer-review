- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embeddings and Conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Principled and novel: matches conditional embedding operator to share statistical strength across conditioning contexts, avoiding per-condition models.
- Elegant, practical objective: CMMD has an empirical estimate analogous to unconditional MMD and kernel matrices independent of model parameters, enabling standard backprop.
- Compelling applications demonstrated, notably classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.
- Missing baseline: no empirical comparison to the naive approach of training a separate GMMN per condition (e.g., MNIST 10-class), which could be a reasonable competitor.
- Scalability/computation concerns: CMMD may require large minibatches and expensive matrix inversions; need analysis of performance vs minibatch size or approximation strategies.
- Presentation clarity: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and should include more self-contained explanations of kernel conditional embeddings and the CMMD derivation.
- Minor correction: Statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is inaccurate—U-statistic is unbiased, the commonly used V-statistic is biased.
- Minor correction: The paper’s definition of the conditional embedding operator appears inconsistent with reference [29]; clarify or correct the cross-covariance/operator formulation.
- Overall: Strong, valuable contribution with elegant CMMD and promising experiments; primary improvements needed are stronger baseline comparisons and clearer theoretical exposition.
