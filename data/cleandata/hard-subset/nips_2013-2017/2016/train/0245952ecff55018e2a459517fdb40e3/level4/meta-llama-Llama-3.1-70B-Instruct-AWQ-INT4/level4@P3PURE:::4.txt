This paper builds upon the deep generative modeling approach of "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" and derives an extension that enables conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing them to be optimized using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears to be technically sound, and the proposals are well presented, although I did not verify all the equations in full detail.

In terms of novelty, while the idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework is quite natural, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not obvious, at least to me. Consequently, the paper seems to possess novelty and usefulness. The derived framework itself should be easily applicable, as demonstrated in Algorithm 1, making this paper likely to be useful and relevant to individuals interested in using deep models for generative purposes or to distill more elaborate statistical processes.

The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, with the provided appendix offering more comprehensive information regarding the details of the methods used and the experiments. Overall, the paper's technical contributions, clarity, and potential impact make it a valuable addition to the field of deep generative modeling.
