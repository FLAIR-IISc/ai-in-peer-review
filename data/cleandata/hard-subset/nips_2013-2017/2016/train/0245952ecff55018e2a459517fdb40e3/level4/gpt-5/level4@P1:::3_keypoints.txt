- Introduces CGMMN: a conditional extension of GMMN trained by minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD) with a generator conditioning on x and noise h via backprop/SGD.  
- Builds on GMMN and conditional kernel mean embeddings, replacing adversarial min–max training with a single smooth objective; positioned relative to GANs and variational methods.  
- Theorem 3 (equality of conditional mean operators ⇒ equality of conditional distributions) is stated without citation or proof and needs a reference or included proof.  
- Remark 1’s claim that CMMD is a “weighted MMD” is under-explained; the derivation is terse and unclear.  
- Clarify whether the “·” in expressions like Tr(Ld · C1) denotes matrix multiplication or elementwise (Hadamard) product.  
- Provide an explicit derivation showing how CMMD reduces to an MMD with non-uniform weights induced by K̃−1.  
- Notation collision: using “C” for both conditional embedding operators and weighting matrices is confusing; rename weighting matrices (e.g., W1,W2,W3).  
- Practical note: K̃−1 on conditioning variables is independent of model parameters, so x-kernels need not be differentiable.  
- Mini-batch approximation is reasonable given the cubic computational cost in batch size.  
- Predictive modeling: MNIST/SVHN results are decent and competitive with generative baselines but fall short of strong discriminative baselines (e.g., DSN).  
- Conditional generation: class-conditional digits and faces are solid; autoencoder+CGMMN improves visual quality and nearest-neighbor checks suggest no memorization.  
- Bayesian distillation: distillation on Boston Housing matches PBP performance but is limited to one dataset/teacher, reducing generality of the claim.  
- Originality: a natural, well-motivated synthesis of known components into a practical end-to-end conditional generator that appears novel and useful.  
- Significance: method is simple, general, and compatible with deep architectures, but lacks results on harder generative benchmarks (CIFAR-10, LSUN) to increase impact.  
- Presentation: paper is generally clear and organized; addressing Theorem 3, Remark 1, and notation issues would improve rigor and readability.  
- Pros: principled CMMD extension of GMMN, end-to-end backprop without adversarial training, broad applicability, competitive samples/results on digits/faces.  
- Cons: missing proof/citation for Theorem 3, unclear weighted-MMD explanation and overloaded notation, limited Bayesian distillation experiments, no challenging dataset results.
