This paper proposes a neural network method grounded in a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion. The approach is evaluated on standard, small-scale benchmarks to assess both discriminative and generative performance. Overall, it is an interesting contribution to the strand of deep learning focused on generative modeling.

The paper is generally clear and readable, though, as is common in this area, some of the mathematical derivations are difficult to follow in places.

The experimental tasks are relatively small, but this choice is not out of line with recent work and provides a reasonable basis for comparison. That said, the training procedure appears computationally intensive. It would greatly help to include a discussion of training time and resource usage, and to compare these costs against existing methods such as DSN. Please specify hardware, wall-clock time, and the number of training epochs to contextualize the reported results.

Reproducibility is a concern. The work would be much stronger with reference code for the models and experiments. As written, a practitioner may struggle to precisely replicate the reported models based on the description in the model section. Providing implementation details—such as exact architectures, hyperparameters, optimization settings, and any data preprocessing—would make the contribution more actionable and easier to verify.
