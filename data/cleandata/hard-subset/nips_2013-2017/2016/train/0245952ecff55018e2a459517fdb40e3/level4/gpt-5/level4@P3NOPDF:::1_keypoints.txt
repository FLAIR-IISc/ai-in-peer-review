- Proposes a neural network generative model trained with a conditional MMD criterion and evaluated on standard small benchmarks.  
- Interesting and timely core idea: integrating conditional MMD into deep generative modeling.  
- Paper is generally clear and well written despite some dense derivations.  
- Experimental tasks align with common prior work but are modest in scale.  
- Datasets and tasks are relatively small, limiting claims about scalability and real-world applicability.  
- Training appears computationally intensive, but the paper omits runtime, resource usage, and efficiency comparisons to baselines like DSN.  
- Reproducibility is weak: insufficient model/training details and no released reference implementation.  
- Requests runtime/resource details: total and per-epoch training time, hardware specifications, and comparisons to baselines under matched conditions.  
- Requests MMD specifics: kernel choices, hyperparameter selection/adaptation, biased vs. unbiased estimators, batch size, and handling of O(n^2) cost.  
- Asks how conditioning is implemented in the MMD term and sensitivity to that design.  
- Asks about training stability and sensitivity to hyperparameters, failure modes, and instability issues.  
- Requests evaluation details: exact metrics, protocols, number of runs, and reporting of standard deviations.  
- Suggests adding runtime analysis and comparisons to DSN and another baseline under matched hardware and capacity.  
- Recommends strengthening reproducibility by releasing code, models, full architecture and optimization details, preprocessing steps, and random seeds.  
- Advises clarifying mathematical presentation with pseudocode, defined symbols, and moved derivations to an appendix.  
- Recommends ablations and sensitivity analyses: unconditional vs. conditional MMD, kernel/bandwidth variations, alternative conditioning schemes, and batch-size effects.  
- Suggests expanding empirical scope to larger/higher-resolution datasets or, if not possible, discussing expected scaling behavior.  
- Urges reporting means and standard deviations over multiple seeds and, where applicable, statistical significance tests.  
- Overall: solid and relevant contribution with promising results but limited empirical scope, missing computational reporting, and reproducibility gaps that should be addressed to strengthen impact.
