The authors present an extension to generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by focusing on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. Their approach leverages reproducing kernel Hilbert space embedding of these conditional distributions, building upon the work of Song et al. (2009). The proposed Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) is evaluated on multiple real-world datasets. A critical detail within the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90) appears to be incomplete, specifically the omission of a term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (and their corresponding estimators). The assumption of zero means for $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$, effectively requiring data to be pre-centered within the RKHS, is also atypical. Given the central role of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ in CGMMN, clarification regarding this missing term – and its impact on gradient calculation and experimental results – would be beneficial. While the approach is novel and well-presented, the empirical results are somewhat underwhelming. CGMMN demonstrates higher error rates than the current state-of-the-art, CMMVA, on both tested datasets. Furthermore, a lack of comparison to other conditional generative models (such as conditional generative adversarial networks) prevents a clear assessment of CGMMN’s generative capabilities. The manuscript includes several typographical errors, including “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).
