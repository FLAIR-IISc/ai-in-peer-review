The authors extend moment matching networks to their conditional version, a non-trivial task. Unlike models that can be updated with a single example, where incorporating conditionals is straightforward (e.g., by feeding the conditional into the network), the extension here is more complex. This complexity arises from the need to match distributions of a large number of samples (a mini-batch) to a large number of inputs, where these distributions are no longer identical (unless the same conditional is used for a given update, which is inefficient).

The main issue with the paper is that it delves into advanced mathematical formulations without first providing an intuitive understanding of the underlying concepts. Specifically, the authors do not clearly explain the computations performed during the algorithm, their efficiency, or the assumptions made to derive the optimized formula (Equation 2). In contrast to the non-conditional version of the algorithm, which can be explained intuitively (as demonstrated in the original paper) without resorting to sophisticated mathematical frameworks (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert space), the formula being minimized in the conditional case lacks a clear, intuitive explanation.

To improve the paper, it would be beneficial to provide an intuitive explanation for the conditional case, akin to that of the non-conditional version. The authors should consider addressing the following questions:

1. How can one intuitively derive the formula presented in Equation 2?
2. What are the steps involved in translating Equation 2 into a practical algorithm?

While some of this information is included in the supplement, it is difficult to follow and could be more effectively integrated into the main body of the paper to enhance clarity and readability.
