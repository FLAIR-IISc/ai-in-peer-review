- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Network (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions via RKHS conditional embeddings (Song et al.).

- Core contribution: adapt moment-matching objective from joint to conditional distributions using conditional embedding operators.

- Novelty: applying moment matching to conditional generation is an elegant, potentially stable non-adversarial alternative.

- Major technical concern: empirical conditional embedding operator definition seems to omit mean embedding terms (μ_X, μ_Y), raising correctness and reproducibility questions.

- Clarification requested on whether RKHS pre-centering is assumed and how mean terms are handled in gradients and experiments.

- Experimental weakness: predictive performance is worse (higher error) than the CMMVA baseline on evaluated datasets.

- Generative evaluation incomplete: lacks comparisons to prominent conditional generative models (cGANs, cVAEs), making sample-quality assessment difficult.

- Minor issues/typos: "Frobeniu norm" → "Frobenius norm"; "an universal" → "a universal"; grammatical error "which the".

- After rebuttal, authors satisfactorily addressed concerns about the operator formulation and experiments.

- Reviewer raised scores and judges the paper a valuable contribution to generative-model literature.
