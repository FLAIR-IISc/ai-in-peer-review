- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation via kernel embedding and conditional MMD.  
- Core idea: match the model to the conditional embedding operator instead of individual conditional distributions.  
- This approach avoids data sparsity inherent in fitting separate GMMNs per condition.  
- A naive baseline (single NN sharing parameters across conditions) could still work for small discrete condition sets (e.g., MNIST); such a comparison is missing.  
- Conditional MMD objective is clearly formulated and computed similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices are independent of model parameters, allowing standard backprop.  
- Concern: CMMD may need large minibatches to reduce stochasticity, which increases computational cost, especially due to matrix inverses.  
- Experimental results show effectiveness on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Presentation of sections 2.3 and 3.1 is unclear and needs better exposition.  
- Minor issue: the paper’s claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect.  
- Minor issue: the conditional embedding operator definition contradicts the definition in reference [29].  
- Recommendations: investigate minibatch size vs. computational cost trade-offs and include comparisons with the naive baseline in varied scenarios.
