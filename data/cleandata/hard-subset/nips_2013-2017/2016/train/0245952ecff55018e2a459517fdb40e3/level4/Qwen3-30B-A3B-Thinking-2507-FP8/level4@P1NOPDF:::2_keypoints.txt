- Paper extends moment matching networks (MMNs) to the conditional setting; this is non-trivial because matching distributions across minibatches with varying conditionals is challenging and inefficient if conditionals are identical per update.  
- Main weakness: presentation lacks intuitive explanation and computational clarity, jumping straight to formal math.  
- The authors fail to explain the intuition behind Equation (2), the paper’s key optimization objective.  
- The paper does not clarify the practical computational steps that implement Equation (2) (supplementary details are hard to follow).  
- The authors do not justify the algorithm’s efficiency or the assumptions that enable it.  
- By contrast, the non-conditional MMN was explained intuitively; the conditional version needs a comparable accessible explanation.  
- Reviewers request explicit guidance on how to intuitively derive Equation (2) and which concrete steps translate it into the algorithm.  
- As written, readers cannot discern the actual computations or assess feasibility, making the method seem ad hoc.  
- The technical contribution is promising but undermined by insufficient exposition of why the formula works and how it operates; a concise intuitive justification would strengthen the paper.
