This paper presents an extension of moment matching networks (MMNs) to a conditional setting. While extending MMNs to handle conditional updates is a non-trivial problem – unlike single-example updates where simply feeding the condition into the network suffices – the paper’s presentation obscures the core ideas and practical implications of this extension. The central issue lies in the authors’ choice to begin with a highly formal mathematical treatment without first establishing clear intuition for the approach.

The challenge the authors address is compelling: efficiently matching distributions of mini-batches to different input conditions. This differs fundamentally from the original MMN framework because distributions corresponding to different inputs are generally distinct, making a straightforward application of the original method impractical. However, the paper fails to adequately explain *how* this distribution matching is achieved in practice, and what the computational costs are.

Specifically, the optimization target in equation (2) is presented without sufficient explanation of its derivation or underlying principles. A crucial weakness is the lack of clarity regarding the actual computations performed to minimize this objective. What assumptions are necessary for this optimization to succeed? What is the computational complexity of the algorithm resulting from minimizing equation (2)? The supplementary material touches upon these points, but the explanation remains difficult to follow. 

The original, non-conditional MMN framework possesses an intuitive explanation – the minimization can be understood without relying on abstract mathematical constructs like reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces. The authors successfully conveyed this intuition in their prior work. A similar, accessible explanation is desperately needed for the conditional extension.  

To significantly improve this work, the authors should address the following key questions:

* **Intuitive Derivation:** How does one intuitively arrive at the formulation presented in equation (2)? What problem is it trying to solve, and why is this particular form chosen? 
* **Algorithm from Formula:** What are the concrete steps bridging the theoretical objective function (equation (2)) and the practical algorithm used for conditional moment matching? A detailed walkthrough of the implementation, focusing on the core computations, would be invaluable.
* **Computational Efficiency:** What is the computational cost of this conditional MMN, and how does it scale with batch size and the dimensionality of the input and output spaces? How does it compare to alternative approaches for conditional density estimation or generative modeling?
* **Assumptions & Limitations:** What are the underlying assumptions of this method? Under what conditions might it fail or perform poorly? A discussion of the limitations of the approach will strengthen the paper.



Currently, the paper feels unnecessarily dense and mathematically focused, hindering understanding of the practical contributions. While the problem addressed is interesting and the extension to the conditional setting represents a potential advance, the lack of intuition and practical details makes it difficult to assess the significance of this work.  Addressing the points outlined above would greatly improve the clarity and impact of the paper.
