

This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a natural extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) [19] that enables conditional distribution modeling. The authors derive a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to train conditional generative models, allowing the framework to handle tasks requiring conditional generation such as predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation from Bayesian models. CGMMN generates samples by conditioning on input variables through a deep network that processes both the input and random noise, with training performed via stochastic gradient descent using back-propagation.

The technical contribution is well-motivated and builds logically on prior work. The authors effectively contextualize their extension within the GMMN framework while addressing key limitations of unconditional generative models for conditional tasks. The derivation of CMMD is technically sound, with careful attention to the mathematical details of kernel embedding for conditional distributions. The paper provides a clear explanation of how to estimate conditional kernel mean embeddings and the corresponding CMMD objective, making the approach accessible to researchers in the field.

The novelty lies in the specific mechanism for deriving CMMD rather than the general concept of conditional generative models. While conditional extensions to generative models have been explored (e.g., conditional GANs [21], conditional VAEs [28]), the authors make a compelling case that their CMMD-based approach offers a simpler optimization procedure that avoids the min-max challenges of GANs while retaining the benefits of deep learning. The derivation of the empirical estimator, which the authors note was non-obvious, represents a significant technical contribution that distinguishes this work from prior approaches.

The paper demonstrates strong empirical results across multiple applications. For predictive modeling, CGMMN achieves competitive performance on MNIST and SVHN datasets, matching or exceeding state-of-the-art methods without data augmentation or ensemble techniques. For contextual generation, the model successfully produces class-specific digits on MNIST and diverse facial images on Yale Face, with visual examples confirming the generated samples are not mere copies of training data. The Bayesian dark knowledge experiment demonstrates that CGMMN can effectively distill predictive distributions from Bayesian models without degrading performance on the Boston housing regression task.

The writing is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with careful explanations of complex concepts and effective use of figures to illustrate key points. The appendix provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, including implementation specifics and additional experimental results. The paper's clarity makes it accessible to a broad audience while maintaining technical rigor.

In terms of significance, CGMMN addresses a practical gap in the literature: the lack of simple, effective methods for conditional generative modeling. While conditional GANs and VAEs exist, they often require complex training procedures. CGMMN offers a straightforward alternative that retains the stability and ease of optimization characteristic of MMD-based methods. The demonstrated applications across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation showcase the framework's versatility and potential to influence future research.

The main limitation is the relatively limited comparison with the most recent conditional generative approaches, particularly the refined conditional GAN variants that have emerged since the original GAN paper. However, the authors do provide reasonable comparisons with state-of-the-art methods on the specific tasks they consider.

Overall, this is a strong contribution to the field that makes a clear technical advancement while offering practical utility. The paper is technically sound, well-written, and addresses an important gap in conditional generative modeling. The work extends the GMMN framework in a theoretically grounded and practically useful manner, with potential to impact both research and applications in generative modeling.

Strengths: Clear technical contribution to conditional generative modeling; elegant derivation of CMMD; strong empirical results across multiple tasks; excellent writing and organization; practical implementation with clear algorithm description.

Weaknesses: Limited comparison with the most recent conditional generative approaches; some theoretical assumptions about conditional embedding operators could be more thoroughly justified.
