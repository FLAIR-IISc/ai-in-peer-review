This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard small benchmark tasks for discriminative and generative capabilities. Overall, this is an interesting extension for the sub-area of deep learning research focused on building generative models.

The paper text is reasonably clear, although, like most papers in this area, the math can be a bit difficult to follow at times. The tasks used in experiments are somewhat small but not entirely unreasonable when compared against other recent work in this area.

As the algorithm for training the model involves some computationally expensive steps, I would ask for some comments in the experiment section on how long the model takes to train compared with existing approaches like DSN. This would provide a better understanding of the practicality of the proposed approach.

Additionally, the paper would be much stronger if reference code were made available for the experiments. From the model section, a practitioner may have difficulty reproducing the exact models evaluated, and having the code available would facilitate easier replication and extension of the work.

In terms of the quality of the paper, the authors have made a good effort to provide a clear and concise description of the proposed approach. However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. For example, the connection between the proposed approach and existing work on generative models could be more clearly explained. Additionally, the experimental results could be more thoroughly discussed, including a more detailed analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.

Overall, I would say that the paper is well-written and the proposed approach is interesting and relevant to the field. However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved to make it more suitable for publication.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a new approach to building generative models based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion.
* The approach is evaluated on standard benchmark tasks for discriminative and generative capabilities.
* The paper provides a clear and concise description of the proposed approach.

Weaknesses:

* The math can be a bit difficult to follow at times.
* The tasks used in experiments are somewhat small.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.
* The connection between the proposed approach and existing work on generative models could be more clearly explained.

Acceptance: I would recommend accepting the paper, but with some revisions to address the areas mentioned above.
