- Paper extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting, which is non-trivial because matching mini-batch sample distributions to varying inputs breaks the identical-distribution assumption unless the same condition is reused (inefficient).
- Single-example updates are trivial in many models but are challenging here due to the need to match distributions across differing conditions.
- Main weakness is presentation: the paper starts with complex formalism without first giving intuition for the core formula.
- The paper fails to clearly delineate the algorithmic computations and to assess computational efficiency.
- Lacks a detailed explanation of the optimization target in equation (2), the assumptions that justify it, and how it maps to practical computation.
- An accessible, RKHS-style intuitive explanation for the conditional case (analogous to the non-conditional original paper) is missing and would help readers.
- The manuscript does not clearly show the specific steps needed to transform equation (2) into a practical algorithm; supplement details are present but hard to follow.
- Authors should explicitly answer: How does one intuitively derive equation (2)? What concrete steps produce the practical algorithm from that formula?
