This paper extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting. This extension is non-trivial. In many generative models, conditioning is a straightforward matter of feeding the conditional information as an additional input. In this work, however, the core mechanism involves matching the distributions of entire mini-batches. In the conditional setting, each generated sample in a mini-batch may be conditioned on a different input, meaning the model must simultaneously match a set of distinct target distributions. Using the same conditional for the entire batch to simplify this problem would be highly inefficient.

The primary weakness of the paper is its presentation, which prioritizes mathematical formalism over clear intuition. The authors immediately introduce advanced concepts (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces) to derive their objective function (Eq. 2) without first providing a conceptual motivation for its form. This is a significant departure from the original moment matching network paper, where the objective was well-motivated and intuitive. The paper would be much stronger if it included a similar intuitive explanation for the conditional case.

A second major issue is the lack of clarity regarding the practical implementation and computational cost. The paper does not adequately explain the concrete steps required to translate the objective in Eq. (2) into a practical algorithm. Key details are missing, such as the specific computations performed, the assumptions required to make it tractable, and an analysis of the algorithm's efficiency. While some of this information is in the supplement, it is difficult to follow and should be summarized more clearly in the main text.

To improve the paper, the authors should address the following questions:
- How can the objective function in Eq. (2) be derived or explained intuitively?
- What are the precise computational steps that lead from the objective function to the final, practical algorithm? A clear, step-by-step description is needed.
