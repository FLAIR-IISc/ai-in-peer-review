The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by focusing on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea involves embedding these conditional distributions within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, leveraging a technique originally proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is empirically evaluated on several real datasets. However, in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), a term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (specifically, their estimators) appears to be missing. Furthermore, the assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero – implying the data is already centered in the RKHS – is unconventional. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to the CGMMN algorithm, the authors should clarify this omission and detail how it is handled during gradient computation and in the reported experiments. The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated, but the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. Regarding predictive performance, CGMMN exhibits higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA algorithm on both datasets. Concerning generative performance, the lack of comparison to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional generative adversarial networks) makes it difficult to assess whether CGMMN generates superior samples. The paper also contains several typographical errors, including “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).
