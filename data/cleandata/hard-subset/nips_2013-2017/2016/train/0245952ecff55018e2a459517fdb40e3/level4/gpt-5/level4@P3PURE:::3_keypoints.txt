- Proposes a conditional-loss extension of GMMN using conditional kernel mean embeddings and MMD, evaluated on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Reviewer’s previous minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space choice, scalability, practical utility, clarifications) appear to have been addressed.  
- Remark 1: mapping from Eq.(2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear; dot notation is ambiguous (matrix vs. elementwise) and needs explicit detail.  
- Notation: using symbol C is unfortunate since C already denotes the conditional embedding; change to avoid confusion.  
- Theorems 1–2 relate to prior work, but Theorem 3 has no reference or proof—provide a citation or include a proof.  
- Empirical results are decent; supervised learning examples are acceptable as illustrations of versatility though not the most compelling application.  
- Bayesian inference distillation is interesting but demonstrated on only one dataset and one model, leaving broader utility unclear.  
- Approach is most likely to gain traction in pure generative modeling; experiments comparable to original GMMN but broader adoption will require strong results on harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
