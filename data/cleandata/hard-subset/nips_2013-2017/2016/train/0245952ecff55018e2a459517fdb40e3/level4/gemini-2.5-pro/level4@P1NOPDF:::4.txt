This paper extends the deep generative modeling approach, "Generative Moment-Matching Networks," to allow for conditional generation. The authors achieve this by deriving a kernel embedding for the moments of the conditional distribution, which allows them to be optimized using the same framework as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model.

The paper appears technically sound and its proposals are well-presented, though I did not check all the equations in full detail. In terms of novelty, while the idea of extending the generative moment-matching framework to the conditional setting is a natural one, the specific mechanism and the derivation of the empirical estimator are non-trivial. The paper therefore makes a novel and useful contribution.

The derived framework should be straightforward to apply, as shown in Algorithm 1. Consequently, this paper will likely be useful to researchers interested in deep generative models or in methods for distilling more elaborate statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, and the appendix helpfully provides further details regarding the methods and experiments.
