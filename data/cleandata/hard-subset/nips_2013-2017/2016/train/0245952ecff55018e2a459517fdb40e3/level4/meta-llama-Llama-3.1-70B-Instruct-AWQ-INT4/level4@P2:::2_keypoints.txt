- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to extend GMMN for learning conditional distributions via a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD).
- Provides a theoretical foundation for CMMD and shows its connection to standard MMD.
- Develops a practical deep generative architecture trained with stochastic gradient descent and backpropagation to minimize CMMD.
- Empirically evaluates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge, showing competitive performance with state-of-the-art.
- Strength: novel and significant extension of GMMN to conditional modeling.
- Strength: well-established theoretical grounding of the CMMD criterion.
- Weakness: heavy use of mathematical derivations at the start reduces intuitive accessibility.
- Weakness: lacks a clear explanation of the actual computations performed and their computational efficiency.
- Weakness: insufficient intuitive explanation of the conditional case compared to the non-conditional version.
- Suggestion: provide a clear, intuitive explanation of CMMD and its relation to MMD.
- Suggestion: explain the algorithm’s computations in detail and analyze efficiency.
- Suggestion: add a concise section summarizing key contributions and implications.
- Argument for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically effective, and likely to inspire further research.
- Argument against acceptance: readability issues and missing computational/experimental detail reduce clarity and impact.
