- Paper introduces a conditional MMD-based loss for directed generative models via conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Method extends GMMNs to handle conditional variables and is evaluated on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Authors addressed previous minor concerns (latent space choice, scalability, utility) and clarified the manuscript.  
- Core contribution is well-motivated and technically sound: conditional kernel mean embeddings are an elegant extension.  
- Experimental validation is diverse and demonstrates the framework's versatility.  
- Application to distilled Bayesian inference is novel and interesting.  
- Derivations need clarity: Remark 1’s mapping to weighted MMD is not obvious and should be expanded.  
- Notation ambiguity: the dot operator’s meaning (matrix multiply vs Hadamard, etc.) must be explicitly defined.  
- Symbol conflict: ‘C’ is used for two different meanings and should be renamed in one place.  
- Theoretical gap: Theorem 3 is presented without proof or citation and needs either a proof in the appendix or a proper reference.  
- Experimental scope: supervised learning example is weak but acceptable for demonstrating versatility.  
- Distilled Bayesian inference experiment is limited (single dataset/model), so generality is unclear.  
- Recommendation to strengthen empirical claims by evaluating on larger, standard generative benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
