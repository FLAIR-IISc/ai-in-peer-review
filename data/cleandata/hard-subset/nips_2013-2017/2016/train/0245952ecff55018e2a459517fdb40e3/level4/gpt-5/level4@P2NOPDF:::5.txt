This paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by matching conditional rather than joint distributions. The main idea is to use conditional mean embeddings in an RKHS (Song et al., 2009). The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is evaluated on several real datasets.

A key technical concern is the definition of the estimator for the conditional operator ĈY|X (line 90). The centering terms involving the mean elements μX and μY (or their empirical estimates) appear to be omitted. It is also uncommon to assume μX = μY = 0, i.e., that the data are already centered in the RKHS. Because ĈY|X is central to CGMMN, please clarify:
- Whether you explicitly center features in the RKHS, include empirical estimates of μX and μY in the estimator, or make another approximation.
- How this choice affects the derivation and stability of gradients during training.
- How it is handled in the implementation used for the reported experiments.

The approach is novel and technically neat, but the empirical results are underwhelming. For predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets. For generative performance, there is no comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), making it difficult to assess whether CGMMN produces competitive samples.

The manuscript also contains several typos and minor issues that should be corrected, including:
- “Frobeniu norm” (line 48) → “Frobenius norm”
- “an universal” (line 65) → “a universal”
- “which the” (line 238) → likely missing a word or requires rephrasing
