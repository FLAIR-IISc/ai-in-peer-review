- Summary: Proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) using kernel conditional embeddings and a CMMD objective to model conditional distributions for prediction and conditional generation.

- Strength — Conceptual clarity: Framing conditional generation via conditional distribution embeddings is elegant and mitigates per-condition data sparsity.

- Strength — Objective function: The CMMD objective is cleanly formulated and computed analogously to unconditional MMD, with kernel matrices independent of network parameters.

- Strength — Practical relevance: Demonstrates versatility by applying CGMMN to classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.

- Strength — Implementation detail: Emphasizes that K matrices in Eq. (2) are fixed w.r.t. model parameters, simplifying backpropagation.

- Weakness — Missing baseline: No comparison to a simple “naive” conditional baseline (separate/shared GMMN per condition), which can be competitive for discrete, small-condition sets like MNIST.

- Weakness — Minibatch variance & cost: CMMD estimator sensitive to minibatch size; small batches increase variance, large batches make kernel computations and linear solves expensive.

- Weakness — Request: Discuss variance–batch trade-off and stabilization techniques (moving averages, control variates, regularization).

- Weakness — Request: Report practical per-iteration complexity and wall-clock times for different batch sizes.

- Weakness — Numerical strategies: Suggest using Cholesky solves, iterative solvers with warm starts, low-rank/Nyström approximations, random Fourier features, block/linear-time MMD, or caching Gram matrices for discrete conditions.

- Weakness — Clarity/self-containment: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 need a concise derivation of the conditional embedding operator, explicit cross-covariance definitions, operator domains/codomains, and role of regularization.

- Weakness — Assumptions & hyperparameters: Request summary of assumptions (e.g., characteristic kernels) and how kernel hyperparameters/bandwidths are chosen and their sensitivity.

- Weakness — Notation for Eq. (2): Ask for clear notation for all matrices, their sizes, and minibatch construction.

- Weakness — Experimental depth: Empirics should include the naive conditional baseline, sensitivity to regularization and batch size, kernel choice ablations, and runtime/memory comparisons versus other baselines.

- Specific — MMD estimator wording: Clarify which MMD estimator is used and correct claim that the estimator is “unbiased.”

- Specific — Conditional embedding consistency: Reconcile the definition of the conditional embedding operator with reference [29] or explicitly state deviations and implications.

- Specific — Remark 1 clarification: Explicitly state which matrices are independent of model parameters and how gradients propagate through sampled outputs.

- Specific — Notation & regularization: Distinguish population operators from empirical (regularized) estimates and state the regularization (e.g., ridge λ) and its selection.

- Questions — High-dim continuous conditioning: How does CGMMN perform when conditioning space is high-dimensional and sparsely covered?

- Questions — Batch size sensitivity: What batch sizes yielded best models and how sensitive are results to smaller batches?

- Questions — Reusing kernel matrices: Do you reuse kernel matrices for discrete conditions and what speedup is achieved?

- Questions — Complexity & training time: Provide per-iteration complexity, memory usage, and training time comparisons with alternative conditional generative models.

- Questions — Distillation comparisons: How does CGMMN compare to conditional VAEs or cGANs for Bayesian distillation, and what is the calibration quality of distilled models?

- Overall evaluation: Solid, timely contribution with an elegant CMMD objective and compelling applications; main gaps are in empirical baselines, computational cost reporting, and clearer exposition of conditional embedding machinery.
