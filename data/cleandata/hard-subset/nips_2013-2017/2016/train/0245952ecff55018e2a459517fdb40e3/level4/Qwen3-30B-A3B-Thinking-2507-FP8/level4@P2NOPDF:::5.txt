

Review:  
The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by shifting from matching joint to conditional distributions, embedding the latter in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS) following Song et al. (2009). The proposed CGMMN algorithm is empirically evaluated on real datasets. However, a critical issue arises in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), where terms involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (and their estimators) are omitted. This omission is problematic, as assuming $\mu_X = \mu_Y = 0$ (i.e., data centered in RKHS) is unconventional and lacks justification. Since $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is foundational to CGMMN, the authors must clarify: (1) the rationale for omitting these terms, (2) how their absence impacts gradient computation, and (3) practical strategies for handling this in experiments. Without this, the method’s theoretical grounding remains incomplete.  

The approach itself is conceptually novel and well-motivated. However, the experimental results are underwhelming. For predictive performance, CGMMN achieves higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets. For generative performance, the paper fails to compare CGMMN against conditional generative baselines (e.g., conditional GANs), making it impossible to assess whether the method produces superior samples. This omission significantly undermines the contribution’s practical relevance.  

Additional minor issues include typographical errors: "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238). These should be corrected for professionalism.  

-----Update after Author Rebuttal-----  
The authors have comprehensively addressed all technical concerns, including the missing terms in $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$, the zero-mean assumption, and experimental comparisons. Their clarifications are sufficient to resolve the methodological gaps. I therefore adjust my evaluation accordingly.
