- Proposes a conditional loss for directed generative models by extending GMMN with MMD and conditional kernel mean embeddings; demonstrated on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Second review; prior minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space, scalability, model utility, clarifications) appear addressed in the revision.  
- Remark 1 unclear: mapping from Eq. (2) to weighted MMD is not evident; dot notation is ambiguous (matrix multiply vs. Hadamard) and needs a clearer explanation.  
- Symbol choice: using ‘C’ is confusing because it already denotes the conditional embedding.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 are cited appropriately, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference or proof and needs one.  
- Empirical results are solid; using supervised tasks is acceptable as a demonstration of model versatility.  
- Bayesian distillation application is interesting but evaluated on only one dataset/model, limiting conclusions about generality.  
- Likely most impactful within pure generative modeling; experiments match results from the original GMM paper and digits/faces are sufficient to show feasibility.  
- Recommendation: include stronger results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10 or LSUN) to encourage wider adoption.
