This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to model conditional distributions. The core of this work is the development of a novel training objective, Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD), which is derived from the kernel mean embedding of conditional distributions. This allows the model to be trained efficiently using the same back-propagation machinery as the unconditional version. The authors demonstrate the proposed model's effectiveness with competitive performance on a diverse set of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling knowledge from a Bayesian model.

The paper is technically sound and the proposed methods are well-presented. In terms of originality, while extending the GMMN framework to the conditional setting is a natural conceptual step, the technical derivation of the CMMD criterion and its empirical estimator is non-trivial and represents a significant contribution. The resulting framework is simple to apply, as shown in Algorithm 1, which enhances its practical utility. Consequently, this work will likely be useful and relevant to researchers interested in deep generative modeling and model distillation. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, and the appendix provides helpful details regarding the methods and experiments.
