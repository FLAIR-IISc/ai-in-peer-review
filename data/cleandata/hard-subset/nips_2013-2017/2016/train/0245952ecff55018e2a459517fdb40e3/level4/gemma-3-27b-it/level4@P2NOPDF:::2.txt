This paper introduces a conditional extension of moment matching networks (MMN). While extending MMN to handle conditional updates presents a significant challenge – unlike single-example updates where conditioning is straightforward – the authors’ approach feels unnecessarily complex and lacks sufficient clarity in its presentation. The core difficulty lies in matching distributions derived from mini-batches of data to varying conditional inputs, a problem more nuanced than the original MMN framework.

The primary weakness of this work is its reliance on formal mathematical presentation *before* establishing a clear intuitive understanding of the method. The paper dives directly into complex notation without adequately motivating the underlying principles or detailing the computational steps involved. Specifically, the optimized objective function (eq. 2) is presented without sufficient explanation of *why* this formulation is chosen, the assumptions it relies on, or a practical breakdown of how it’s efficiently computed. 

The original, non-conditional MMN algorithm benefits from a relatively intuitive explanation – even without delving into advanced mathematical concepts like Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces – because the minimized formula is easily grasped. A comparable intuitive explanation is critically missing for the conditional extension. The authors should prioritize accessibility and clarity.

To improve the paper, the authors should address the following questions: 

*   **Motivation:** What is the intuitive reasoning behind formulating the conditional MMN objective as shown in equation (2)? What problem does this specific formulation solve that alternatives wouldn't?
*   **Algorithm Derivation:** What are the concrete steps required to translate equation (2) into a practical, implementable algorithm? While some details are provided in the supplement, these are difficult to follow and should be integrated into the main text with greater clarity.
*   **Computational Efficiency:**  A detailed discussion of the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed method is needed. How does the cost of conditioning affect performance, especially with a large number of inputs?
*   **Assumptions:** What assumptions are being made to ensure the successful application of this method? Explicitly stating these assumptions will help readers understand the limitations of the approach.

In summary, the concept of extending moment matching networks to conditional settings is promising. However, the current presentation hinders understanding and evaluation. A stronger emphasis on intuition, a detailed explanation of the algorithmic steps, and a discussion of computational efficiency are necessary to make this work more accessible and impactful.
