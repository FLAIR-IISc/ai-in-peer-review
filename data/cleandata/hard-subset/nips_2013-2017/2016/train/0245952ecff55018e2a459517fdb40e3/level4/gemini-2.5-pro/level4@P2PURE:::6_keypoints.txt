- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction using kernel embeddings and Conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Core idea: match model to the conditional embedding operator for a family of conditional distributions rather than fitting a separate GMMN per condition.
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation tasks.
- Addresses data sparsity of naive per-condition GMMNs, especially problematic for few or continuous-condition examples.
- Missing empirical comparison to the naive baseline (e.g., conditional MNIST) that could serve as an important reference.
- Strength: CMMD objective is elegant and computable with complexity similar to unconditional MMD; kernel matrices are parameter-independent, enabling standard backprop.
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to control estimation noise, but large minibatches make matrix inversions computationally expensive.
- Presentation issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are unclear and force readers to consult cited papers to understand the methodology.
- Minor issue 1: paper incorrectly claims the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77).
- Minor issue 2: definition of the conditional embedding operator (line 90) seems inconsistent with reference [29] and may require a different cross-covariance operator definition.
