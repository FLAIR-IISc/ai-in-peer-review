- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) learning p(y|x) via a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective using conditional mean embeddings.  
- Model samples noise h, concatenates with x, maps (x,h) through a deep network to produce y and trains by minimizing Hilbert–Schmidt distance between conditional embedding operators.  
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings and MMD literature; positioned as a non-adversarial alternative to GANs and a likelihood-free alternative to CVAEs.  
- Theoretically defines CMMD as squared Hilbert–Schmidt norm and shows operator equality implies equality of conditionals under standard assumptions.  
- Derives a computable empirical estimator using Gram matrices and relates CMMD to standard MMD in a special case.  
- Training uses minibatch SGD; the batchwise inverse (K̃−1) depends only on x and λ, simplifying differentiation.  
- Minibatch formulation mitigates cubic costs in batch size, but per-batch complexity remains a concern.  
- Experiments span classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale faces), and Bayesian distillation (Boston), showing competitive, reasonable qualitative results.  
- Nearest-neighbor analysis suggests the model does not simply memorize training examples; using autoencoder code space improves sample quality.  
- Missing sensitivity analysis and detail on kernel choices, bandwidth selection, λ, and batch size effects.  
- Computational costs and memory/scaling details (e.g., O(B^3) inverses, use of Cholesky or low-rank approximations) are not quantified.  
- Generative evaluation lacks quantitative metrics (e.g., Parzen estimates) and head-to-head comparisons with cGAN/cVAE.  
- Distillation experiments are narrow (single dataset, RMSE only) with no calibration, NLL, or runtime comparisons.  
- Clarity is generally good, but the paper should specify kernel choices/selection strategy, computational/numerical-stability details, and how predictions are produced (single sample vs. averaging).  
- Paper contains minor typos and grammatical errors to fix.  
- Originality: a natural but nontrivial and novel extension of GMMN to conditional learning via operator-based discrepancy.  
- Significance: offers a simple, stable, broadly applicable non-adversarial method for conditional generation and distillation, though not universally state-of-the-art.  
- Strengths: principled CMMD derivation, simple end-to-end training (no adversary), broad applicability, and competitive empirical/qualitative results.  
- Weaknesses: limited discussion on kernel tuning and sensitivity, unquantified computational scaling, missing quantitative generative comparisons, and narrow distillation evaluation.  
- Suggestions: include kernel/bandwidth selection and sensitivity studies, report per-epoch cost and peak memory, consider Cholesky/low-rank inverses, evaluate sampling/averaging and calibration, add quantitative generative metrics and expanded distillation experiments.  
- Recommendation: solid, well-motivated, and generally well-executed paper with a leaning toward acceptance.
