This paper introduces conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction tasks. The core idea is to use kernel embeddings of conditional distributions together with a conditional MMD to quantify discrepancies between conditional distributions. The method is evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models. A straightforward extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting would fit a separate GMMN for each conditional distribution, tying parameters by sharing a common neural network. The issue with this approach is that each conditional slice has very few samples; when the conditioning variable is continuous, a “condition” may have only a single observation, leading to severe data sparsity. The proposed method instead treats all conditionals as a family and directly matches the model to the conditional embedding operator, rather than matching each conditional distribution separately. While the advantages of this approach are evident, there are cases where the naive baseline may still perform reasonably—for example, conditional generation with 10 discrete classes as in MNIST—so it would be useful to include this baseline for comparison. It is appealing that the conditional MMD objective has a clean formulation and is almost as straightforward to compute as the unconditional MMD, as noted in Remark 1. All K matrices in equation 2 are independent of the model parameters, allowing backpropagation to proceed as before. One concern is that the CMMD objective may require large minibatches; otherwise, minibatch stochasticity could dominate the error. Conversely, large minibatches make the objective expensive, particularly because the necessary matrix inverses may be prohibitively costly. Overall, CMMD offers a compelling way to model conditional distributions. The experimental results on classification and Bayesian model distillation are also interesting. The presentations in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 were not easy to follow; I needed to consult the cited papers to fully understand the details. Minor issues: (1) line 77 claims the MMD estimator is unbiased, which is incorrect. (2) line 90’s conditional embedding operator is not consistent with reference [29]; to obtain the form used here, it seems a different definition of the cross-covariance operator is required.
