The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional setting. While extending existing models to conditional versions can sometimes be trivial (simply feeding the conditional input into the network), this is non-trivial here. This is because the objective requires matching distributions over a large number of samples (a mini-batch) to a large number of conditional inputs, and these distributions are not equivalent unless the same conditional input is used for each update – an inefficient approach. A key weakness of the paper is its initial focus on complex mathematical formalism rather than providing clear intuition behind the core ideas. The authors do not sufficiently highlight the computations involved in the algorithm and its efficiency. 

Specifically, the paper lacks a detailed explanation of the optimized formula (Eq. 2), including its computational efficiency and underlying assumptions. A clear description of how the formula translates into a practical algorithm is missing, making it difficult for the reader to grasp the actual computations performed. While the non-conditional version of the algorithm has an intuitive explanation (as presented in the original GMMN paper), the conditional case lacks a similarly accessible explanation. It would be beneficial to address the following questions:

*   How was the formula (2) intuitively derived?
*   What are the steps connecting formula (2) to the implemented algorithm? (While some details are in the supplement, they are difficult to follow.)

The paper introduces conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) to learn flexible conditional distributions. CGMMN extends the capabilities of GMMN to a variety of applications, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation. The authors define a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion and demonstrate that the resulting objective can be efficiently optimized via back-propagation. Experiments on several datasets demonstrate competitive performance across these tasks.

**Strengths:**

*   The paper addresses an important problem – extending moment matching networks to the conditional setting.
*   The proposed CMMD criterion appears to be a reasonable approach for learning conditional distributions.
*   The experimental results demonstrate that CGMMN achieves competitive performance on a variety of tasks.
*   The work builds upon existing literature in generative modeling, specifically GMMNs and MMD, and positions itself well within the field.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The paper’s initial presentation is heavily mathematical, lacking sufficient intuitive explanation of the proposed method. This makes it difficult to understand the core ideas and the practical implications of the formulation.
*   The authors do not adequately discuss the computational complexity and scalability of the proposed approach. Details regarding the implementation and optimization of the CMMD objective are lacking.
*   The connection between the theoretical formulation (Eq. 2) and the practical algorithm is not clearly articulated. The supplement provides some details, but these are difficult to follow.
*   While the paper acknowledges the assumptions required for the CMMD objective, a more thorough discussion of their implications and potential limitations would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The work presents a novel extension of a recent and promising generative modeling technique (GMMN).
*   The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on several benchmark tasks.
*   The paper is technically sound and makes a clear contribution to the field of generative modeling.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of intuitive explanation and detailed computational analysis hinders understanding of the method's practical implications.
*   The paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the assumptions and limitations of the proposed approach.
*   The presentation is dense and may be inaccessible to readers not already familiar with the underlying mathematical concepts.

**Overall:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution to the field of generative modeling. However, the presentation could be improved by providing a more intuitive explanation of the core ideas and a more detailed analysis of the computational aspects. Addressing these weaknesses would significantly enhance the clarity and impact of the work. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to improve clarity and address the concerns outlined above.
