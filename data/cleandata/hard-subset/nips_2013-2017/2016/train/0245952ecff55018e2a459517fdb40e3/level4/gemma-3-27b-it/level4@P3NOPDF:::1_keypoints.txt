- Proposes a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion for generative modeling.
- Evaluated on standard benchmark tasks to assess discriminative and generative performance.
- Represents an interesting extension in deep generative modeling and is generally well-written.
- Mathematical formulation is challenging to follow and would benefit from clearer, more gradual notation and intuitive explanations.
- Experiments use relatively small datasets, leaving scalability and generalization to larger datasets unclear.
- Training algorithm is computationally intensive; the paper lacks quantitative discussion of training time and comparisons (e.g., to DSN).
- Absence of publicly available code significantly hinders reproducibility and independent implementation.
- Strength: Novel application of conditional MMD within a neural network framework.
- Strength: Core ideas are conveyed clearly and the motivation is sound.
- Strength: Use of established benchmarks enables fair comparison with related work.
- Weakness: Insufficient quantitative analysis of computational cost and scalability.
- Weakness: Model details are inadequate for exact reproduction without authors' configurations.
- Question: Provide detailed analysis of computational complexity and scaling with dataset size and model parameters.
- Question: Report hardware (CPU/GPU/RAM) and typical training times per dataset; include direct comparisons to DSN or similar models.
- Question: Do the authors plan to release the experimental code to improve reproducibility?
- Question: How sensitive is performance to hyperparameters (e.g., MMD kernel bandwidth) and was a systematic search performed?
- Question: Clarify limitations—are there data types or generative tasks where the method is expected to perform poorly?
- Overall recommendation: Promising work but not recommendable for acceptance until code is released and a detailed training-time/scalability analysis is provided; reviewer would reconsider if addressed.
