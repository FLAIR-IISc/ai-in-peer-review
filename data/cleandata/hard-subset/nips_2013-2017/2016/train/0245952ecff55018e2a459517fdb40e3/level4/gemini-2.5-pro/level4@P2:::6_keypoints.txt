- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to model p(y|x).
- Core innovation: Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) that compares conditional distributions via kernel mean embedding operators in RKHS.
- CMMD has a closed-form expression in kernel matrices, enabling optimization by backpropagation.
- Treats conditional distributions as a unified family by matching the operator mapping x to the output mean embedding.
- Demonstrates versatility with tasks: classification, contextual generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.
- Strong empirical results on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Faces, and Bayesian-model distillation.
- Theoretical exposition (Sections 2.3 and 3.1) is dense and lacks intuitive explanation, hindering accessibility.
- CMMD requires inverting kernel matrices (cubic in minibatch size), creating a trade-off between computational cost and estimator stability.
- No empirical analysis of minibatch-size trade-offs; potential training instability from noisy small minibatches is not addressed.
- Missing comparison to simple baselines (e.g., separate GMMNs per discrete class or shared-layer variants) for conditional generation tasks.
- Quality: technically sound overall but practical computational concerns and minor technical inaccuracies exist.
- Clarity: generally well-written, but core theoretical sections need clearer intuition and exposition.
- Originality: highly original—novel CMMD formulation and conditional generative modeling via moment matching.
- Significance: broadens applicability of MMD-based generative models and offers an alternative to GANs/VAEs for conditional tasks.
- Arguments for acceptance: proposes principled CMMD criterion, extends MMD-based models, and shows strong empirical performance across tasks.
- Arguments for rejection: unclear theory sections, unaddressed computational/stability trade-offs, and lack of simple baseline comparisons.
- Minor correction 1: The paper claims the MMD V-statistic estimator is unbiased, but the presented V-statistic is biased; an unbiased estimator uses a different formulation.
- Minor correction 2: The definition of the conditional embedding operator and cross-covariance differs from cited work [29]; clarify the specific operator definitions used.
