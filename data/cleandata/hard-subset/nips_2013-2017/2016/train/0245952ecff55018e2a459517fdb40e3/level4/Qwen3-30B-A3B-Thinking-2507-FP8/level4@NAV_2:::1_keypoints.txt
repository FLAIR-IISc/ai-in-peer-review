- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMN to conditional generation using Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- Builds on Li et al. (ICML 2015) GMMN work to enable generating samples conditioned on input variables while keeping GMMN's simpler training paradigm.
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, Boston housing), showing competitive results and good conditional sample quality.
- Theoretical foundation is well-motivated and grounded in Hilbert space embeddings; extension to conditional distributions is sound.
- Experimental evaluation is diverse, showcasing versatility across multiple tasks and datasets.
- Paper is well-organized and mathematically detailed for the target audience.
- Fails to discuss computational complexity and training time of the CMMD objective, lacking comparisons to methods like DSN.
- Insufficient reproducibility details: missing exact architectures, layer sizes, activations, and hyperparameters for key experiments.
- Needs clearer articulation of differences and unique advantages relative to conditional GANs and conditional VAEs.
- Some mathematical sections (conditional embedding operator, CMMD objective) would benefit from more intuitive explanations.
- Novel contribution: conditional extension of GMMN is original and distinct from adversarial or variational approaches.
- Significance is promising but limited by evaluation on relatively small benchmarks; larger-scale experiments would strengthen practical impact.
- Recommendation: add training time comparisons, more computational analysis, and release reference code to improve reproducibility and impact.
