- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions.  
- Extends GMMN (Li et al. ICML 2015) by developing a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion for conditional generation.  
- Derivation requires careful formulation of kernel embeddings for conditional distributions and empirical estimation.  
- Offers a simpler training procedure than conditional GANs by avoiding min-max optimization.  
- Theoretical contributions are sound: defines conditional kernel embeddings and proves CMMD is a valid divergence.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and competitive across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation.  
- Tasks/datasets include MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, and Boston housing, with appropriate baselines and reproducible details.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized; figures and appendix effectively support the paper.  
- Originality is moderate: a natural but non-trivial extension of GMMN that fills a gap in moment-matching literature.  
- Significance is high due to practical utility, simplicity (Algorithm 1), and broad applicability in research and industry.  
- Strengths for acceptance: novel extension, solid theory, comprehensive experiments, clear writing, simple implementation.  
- Weaknesses against acceptance: somewhat incremental extension and some theoretical details (e.g., smoothness assumptions) need clarification.  
- Overall recommendation: technically sound, practically useful, and worthy of acceptance despite limited novelty.
