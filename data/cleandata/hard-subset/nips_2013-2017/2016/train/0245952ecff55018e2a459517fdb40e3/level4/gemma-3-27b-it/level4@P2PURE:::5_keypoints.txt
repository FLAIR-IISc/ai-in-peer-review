- Paper extends GMMNs to match conditional distributions via RKHS embeddings, building on Song et al. (2009).  
- Proposes a conditional GMMN (CGMMN) and evaluates it empirically on several real datasets.  
- Definition of empirical conditional operator \hat{C}_{Y|X} (Line 90) appears to omit terms involving \mu_X and \mu_Y (their estimators).  
- The authors’ assumption that \mu_X and \mu_Y are zero (i.e., data centered in RKHS) is unusual and needs justification.  
- Because \hat{C}_{Y|X} is central to CGMMN, the paper should clarify the omission and explain how to include these terms in gradient computation and experiments.  
- The approach is novel and well-conceived, but experimental results are underwhelming.  
- Predictive performance: CGMMN has consistently higher error rates than state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets.  
- Generative performance: paper lacks comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), making sample quality hard to assess.  
- Paper contains several typos (e.g., “Frobeniu norm” Line 48, “an universal” Line 65, “which the” Line 238).  
- Update after rebuttal: authors have successfully addressed all concerns; reviewer will revise scores accordingly.
