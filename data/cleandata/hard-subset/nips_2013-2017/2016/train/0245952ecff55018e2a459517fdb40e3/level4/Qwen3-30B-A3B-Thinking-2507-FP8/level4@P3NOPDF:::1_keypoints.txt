- Proposes a novel neural architecture for conditional generative modeling based on the CMMD criterion with sound theoretical motivation.  
- Clear exposition overall, but Section 3’s mathematical derivations are dense and may be inaccessible to readers unfamiliar with kernel methods.  
- Experimental evaluation uses small-scale benchmarks (MNIST, CIFAR-10) and needs justification and comparison with recent SOTA generative models (e.g., DCGAN, VAEs).  
- No analysis of computational efficiency; kernel operations likely expensive and authors must report training times (per epoch and total) versus baselines.  
- Reproducibility is poor: no reference code and insufficient model details (layer configs, kernel choices) impede replication.  
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation (e.g., MMD vs CMMD) and an overstated abstract claim of “superior generative quality” without quantitative baseline comparisons.  
- Theoretical guarantees section (3.2) is underdeveloped; should analyze how CMMD addresses mode collapse and sample diversity.  
- Recommended improvements: add training-time comparisons, release public code, and provide stronger experimental justification for benchmark scale; recommendation: accept pending these revisions.
