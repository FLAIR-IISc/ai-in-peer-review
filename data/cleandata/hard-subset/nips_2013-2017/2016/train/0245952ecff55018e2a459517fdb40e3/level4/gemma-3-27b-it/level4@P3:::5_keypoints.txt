- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions using kernel mean embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.  
- Training is straightforward via backpropagation, avoiding adversarial min-max optimization.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation with competitive performance.  
- Builds on prior work: MMD/GMMN, GANs and conditional GANs, and kernel embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Novelty: extends moment-matching to the conditional setting as an alternative to GANs.  
- Theoretical foundation: grounded in kernel mean embeddings and MMD theory.  
- Simplicity: training procedure is simpler than adversarial approaches.  
- Versatility: demonstrated applicability across diverse tasks.  
- Presentation: paper is generally well-organized and accessible.  
- Experimental weakness: results are competitive but do not consistently outperform state-of-the-art (e.g., higher MNIST predictive error than CMMVA).  
- Limited empirical comparison: missing direct baselines against other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs).  
- Implementation concern: original omission in the estimated conditional operator formulation (terms involving μX and μY) raised issues, though authors addressed it in rebuttal.  
- Minor typographical errors present in the submission.  
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, novel, theoretically motivated, simpler alternative to GANs, and responsive authors.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inconsistent empirical advantage, insufficient comparisons, and initial implementation oversight.  
- Overall assessment and recommendation: solid contribution deserving acceptance.
