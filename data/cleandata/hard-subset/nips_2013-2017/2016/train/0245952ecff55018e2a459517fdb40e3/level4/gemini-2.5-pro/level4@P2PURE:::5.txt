This paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting by matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core of the approach is to embed these conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, a technique introduced by Song et al. (2009). The resulting algorithm, Conditional GMMN (CGMMN), is evaluated empirically on several real-world datasets.

A key concern lies in the definition of the empirical conditional covariance operator, $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90). The formulation appears to be missing terms related to the mean embeddings $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (or, more precisely, their empirical estimators). This omission implies an unusual assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero, meaning the data is pre-centered in the RKHS. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to the CGMMN algorithm, the authors should clarify this formulation, addressing the missing terms and explaining how they are handled during gradient computation and in the experiments.

While the proposed approach is novel and elegant, the experimental results are not fully convincing. In the predictive performance evaluation, CGMMN achieves higher error rates than the state-of-the-art method, CMMVA, on both datasets presented. Regarding generative performance, the quality of the generated samples is difficult to assess, as the paper lacks a comparison against other conditional generative models, such as Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks. This makes it unclear if the proposed algorithm generates superior samples.

The manuscript also contains minor typos, for instance: "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
