

The authors extend moment matching networks (MMNs) to a conditional setting. This extension is non-trivial, unlike in models that update on single examples (where conditioning simply involves feeding the condition into the network). Here, the challenge arises because the algorithm must match distributions across a mini-batch of samples to a corresponding set of inputs—yet these distributions differ when conditionals vary across the batch (using identical conditionals per update would be inefficient).  

The primary weakness of the paper lies in its lack of intuitive explanation and computational clarity. Instead of first establishing intuition for the conditional extension, the authors immediately present formal mathematics, obscuring the core idea. Crucially, they fail to:  
(1) Explain the intuition behind Equation (2) (the key optimization objective),  
(2) Clarify the practical computational steps derived from this equation, and  
(3) Justify the efficiency and assumptions enabling the algorithm.  

For context, the non-conditional MMN (as in the original paper) is explained intuitively without heavy mathematical machinery (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces), making its optimization objective transparent. The conditional version should similarly benefit from an accessible explanation. To bridge this gap, the authors should explicitly address:  
- *How* one might intuitively derive Equation (2), and  
- *What specific steps* translate Equation (2) into the practical algorithm (some details appear in the supplement but remain difficult to follow).  

Without these, readers cannot discern the actual computational operations or assess the method’s feasibility. The current presentation risks making the approach seem ad hoc rather than grounded in clear reasoning. The paper’s technical contribution is promising but undermined by insufficient exposition of *why* the formula works and *how* it operates in practice. A concise, intuitive justification—comparable to the non-conditional case—would significantly strengthen the paper’s impact.
