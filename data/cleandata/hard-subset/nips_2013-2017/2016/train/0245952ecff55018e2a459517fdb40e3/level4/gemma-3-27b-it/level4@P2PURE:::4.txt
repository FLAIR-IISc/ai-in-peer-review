This paper extends the deep generative modeling approach of “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” to enable conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing it to be optimized using the same techniques as the original, unconditional model. The proposed model demonstrates competitive performance in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears technically sound and is well-presented, although a complete verification of all equations was not performed. While extending generative moment matching to the conditional case is a natural progression, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious—at least, not to this reviewer—suggesting both novelty and usefulness. The resulting framework is straightforward to apply, as illustrated in Algorithm 1, and will likely be valuable to researchers interested in deep generative models or distilling more complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, and the appendix provides helpful supplementary details regarding the methodology and experimental setup.
