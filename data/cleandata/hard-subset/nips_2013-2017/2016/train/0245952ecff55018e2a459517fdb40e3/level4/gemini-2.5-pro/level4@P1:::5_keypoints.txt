- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- CMMD is based on Hilbert space embeddings of conditional distributions (Song et al., 2009) and CGMMN is trained with backpropagation.  
- The approach is novel and elegant but requires clarifications and stronger empirical evidence.  
- Major concern: the empirical conditional embedding operator Ĉ_{Y|X} appears to omit RKHS mean terms (μ_X, μ_Y), implying an unrealistic centering assumption that must be clarified.  
- Predictive performance is weak: CGMMN yields higher error rates than state-of-the-art CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN, so it is not yet competitive for discriminative tasks.  
- Generative evaluation is incomplete: no comparisons to other conditional generative models (e.g., Conditional GANs), leaving sample quality/diversity unclear.  
- Minor issues: a few typos (“Frobeniu norm” → Frobenius norm, “an universal” → a universal, and a grammatical error at line 238).  
- Update: authors addressed the concerns in rebuttal, and the reviewer plans to raise their scores.
