- Introduces conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction.  
- Central idea: use kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and the conditional MMD (CMMD) as the discrepancy measure.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models.  
- Naive baseline (train separate GMMN per conditional with shared NN parameters) suffers from data sparsity, motivating the proposed global conditional-embedding matching.  
- However, for small discrete condition sets (e.g., MNIST labels) the naive baseline might perform reasonably—include it to strengthen experiments.  
- CMMD objective is clean and practically convenient: K matrices in Eq.(2) don’t depend on model parameters, enabling standard backprop.  
- Practical concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid noisy gradients, and large batches + matrix inverses are computationally expensive.  
- Presentation/readability issue: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and should be made more self-contained and clearer.  
- Minor technical issues: the claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (Line 77) is incorrect; the definition of the conditional embedding operator (Line 90) is inconsistent with reference [29] and needs reconciliation.
