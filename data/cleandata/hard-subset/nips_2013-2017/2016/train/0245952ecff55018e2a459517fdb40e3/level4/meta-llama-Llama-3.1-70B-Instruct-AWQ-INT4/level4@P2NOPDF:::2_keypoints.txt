- Paper proposes a non-trivial extension of moment matching networks to the conditional setting.
- Core technical challenge: matching distributions from large mini-batches to many distinct conditionals, which are not identical across samples.
- Single-example update approaches don’t generalize; using the same conditional for an update is inefficient.
- Primary concern: poor presentation — heavy math without intuitive explanation.
- Authors fail to explain the actual computations performed by the algorithm and their computational efficiency.
- Equation (2) is not adequately motivated or unpacked; its assumptions and computational implications are unclear.
- The non-conditional version admits a simple, intuitive explanation, but the conditional case lacks a comparable intuition.
- Key missing items the paper should address: an intuitive derivation of eq. (2) and the concrete steps from eq. (2) to the practical algorithm.
- Supplementary material contains some details but is hard to follow; the paper needs clear, step-by-step explanations and intuition.
