This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework to model conditional distributions. Building on prior work that uses the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) for learning joint distributions, this paper proposes a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The CMMD objective is derived from the difference between kernel mean embeddings of conditional operators, enabling the training of a deep generative model for p(y|x) via standard back-propagation. The authors demonstrate the model's effectiveness on a variety of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling knowledge from a more complex Bayesian model into a simpler student network.

**Strengths:**
*   **Originality and Significance:** The primary contribution is a well-motivated and non-trivial extension of the moment-matching framework to the conditional setting. While the idea of a conditional GMMN is a natural progression, the technical derivation of the CMMD objective and its empirical estimator is a novel contribution. The resulting framework is significant because it addresses a wide range of important problems while retaining a simple and efficient training procedure, making it likely to be adopted and built upon by the community.
*   **Quality:** The paper appears technically sound. The proposed method is empirically validated across a diverse set of experiments, demonstrating competitive performance on classification (MNIST, SVHN), image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and a regression task for model distillation. These results effectively support the authors' claims about the model's versatility.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow. The authors provide the necessary background on kernel embeddings before clearly motivating and deriving their proposed CMMD objective. The inclusion of a detailed appendix with algorithmic and experimental details strengthens the paper and aids reproducibility.

**Weaknesses:**
*   The predictive performance, while competitive for a generative model, does not set a new state-of-the-art when compared to purely discriminative deep networks on benchmarks like MNIST and SVHN. For instance, it is outperformed by Deeply-Supervised Nets (DSN). This is a reasonable trade-off for a generative approach, but a brief discussion of this limitation would be beneficial.
*   The derivation of the empirical CMMD estimator (Equation 2) is quite dense. While the derivation appears correct, the paper could be improved by providing more intuition or a step-by-step walk-through in the appendix to clarify how the Hilbert-Schmidt norm of the operator difference translates into the final expression involving Gram matrices.
