This paper extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting. The key idea is to derive a kernel embedding of the moments that define the conditional distribution, enabling optimization with essentially the same machinery as in the unconditional case.

The model is evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model, where it delivers reasonably competitive performance. The paper appears technically sound and the proposals are well presented, although I did not verify all equations in full detail.

In terms of novelty, while a conditional extension of the GMMN framework is a natural direction, the specific mechanism and the derivation of the empirical estimator were not obvious (at least to me). In this respect, the paper offers both novelty and practical usefulness.

The framework seems easy to apply in practice—Algorithm 1 is clear—and the work is likely to be relevant for researchers interested in deep generative models or in distilling more elaborate statistical procedures.

The paper is well written, clear, and easy to read. The appendix is helpful and provides more complete details for both the methods and the experiments.
