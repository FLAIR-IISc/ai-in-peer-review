- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to match conditional distributions via RKHS conditional embeddings (Song et al., 2009).
- Empirically evaluated on several real-world datasets.
- Definition of \hat{C}_{Y|X} appears to omit terms related to estimators of μ_X and μ_Y.
- Assuming μ_X and μ_Y = 0 (data centered in RKHS) is unconventional and requires justification.
- Clarification needed on how the missing term affects gradient computation and experimental results.
- Experimental performance is underwhelming: CGMMN has higher error rates than CMMVA on both datasets.
- No comparison to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), so sample quality superiority is unclear.
- Paper contains several typos (“Frobeniu norm”, “an universal”, “which the”).
- After the author rebuttal, the reviewer states all concerns were addressed and will revise scores.
