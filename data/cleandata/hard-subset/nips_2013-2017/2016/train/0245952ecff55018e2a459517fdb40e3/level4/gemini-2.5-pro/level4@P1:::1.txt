This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a novel deep generative model based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The work extends the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework to model conditional distributions, thereby broadening its applicability to tasks like predictive modeling and contextual generation. The authors evaluate the proposed technique on several standard benchmark tasks, demonstrating both its discriminative and generative capabilities.

Overall, this is a solid and interesting contribution to the sub-field of deep learning focused on building deep generative models. The extension from joint to conditional distribution modeling using a moment-matching criterion is a logical and valuable step.

**Strengths:**
*   **Novelty and Significance:** The paper addresses a clear limitation of previous moment-matching networks by introducing a principled way to handle conditional generation. This significantly expands the scope and utility of this class of models.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is reasonably well-written. The motivation for conditional modeling is clearly articulated, and the overall structure is easy to follow.

**Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement:**
*   **Reproducibility:** The paper would be much stronger if the experimental details were more complete. As written, the model section may lack sufficient detail for a practitioner to reproduce the exact models and results. For instance, specifics on hyperparameter selection and regularization would be beneficial. Providing source code for the experiments would be the best way to address this and would be a valuable asset to the research community.
*   **Computational Cost:** The training algorithm involves computationally expensive steps, such as kernel matrix inversion, which scales poorly with batch size. The paper would benefit from a discussion of the model's training time and computational complexity, especially in comparison to existing approaches like DSN and Conditional GANs. Adding this information to the experimental section would give readers a clearer picture of the practical trade-offs involved.
*   **Experimental Scale:** The tasks used in the experiments are standard but relatively small-scale. While not entirely unreasonable when compared against other recent work in this area, the claims of the paper would be more compelling if the model were demonstrated on more complex or higher-dimensional datasets.
*   **Mathematical Presentation:** While the core ideas are clear, some of the mathematical details can be difficult to follow at times. A bit more intuition alongside the formal definitions, particularly for the empirical CMMD objective, could help improve the paper's accessibility.
