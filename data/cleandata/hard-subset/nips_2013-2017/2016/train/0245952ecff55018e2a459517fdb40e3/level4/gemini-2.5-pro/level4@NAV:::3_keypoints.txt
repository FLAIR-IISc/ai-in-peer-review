- Proposes a novel conditional-extension of GMMN using conditional kernel mean embeddings to form a new loss for directed generative models with conditional variables.  
- Experimental evaluation includes classification, digit and face generation, and Bayesian model knowledge distillation.  
- Authors have addressed previous minor reviewer concerns about low-dimensional latent space, scalability, comparative utility, and clarifications.  
- The derivation connecting Equation (2) to a weighted MMD is unclear and needs more detailed steps.  
- The dot notation in that derivation is ambiguous (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product) and must be clarified.  
- The symbol 'C' is a poor notational choice due to conflict with the conditional embedding definition and should be changed.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 are well-supported by references, but Theorem 3 lacks a proof or citation and requires one.  
- Empirical evaluation is solid and justifies including supervised experiments to show versatility.  
- The Bayesian distillation application is interesting but evaluated on only one dataset and model, limiting claims about generalizability.  
- Primary contribution likely in pure generative modeling; digit and face results match original GMMN and serve as a proof-of-concept.  
- For broader adoption, the method needs compelling results on harder benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
