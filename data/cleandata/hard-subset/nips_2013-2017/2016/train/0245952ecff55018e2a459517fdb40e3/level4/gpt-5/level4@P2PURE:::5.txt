The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by matching conditional rather than joint distributions. The core idea is to embed conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), following the approach of Song et al. (2009). The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is evaluated on several real datasets.

In the definition of \hat{C}_{Y|X} (Line 90), the terms involving μ_X and μ_Y (specifically, their empirical estimates) are omitted. It is also atypical to assume μ_X = μ_Y = 0, i.e., that the data are already centered in the RKHS. Because \hat{C}_{Y|X} is central to CGMMN, the paper should clarify this missing term and explain how it is handled in both gradient computation and real experiments.

The approach is novel and elegant, but the empirical results are somewhat disappointing. For predictive performance, on both datasets CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art method CMMVA. For generative performance, there is no comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), leaving it unclear whether CGMMN produces better samples.

The paper also contains several typos, e.g., “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).
