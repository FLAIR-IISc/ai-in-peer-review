This paper presents an extension of the deep generative modeling approach known as "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" to enable conditional generation. The authors achieve this by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments that define the conditional distribution, allowing them to be optimized using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates competitive performance in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model.

The paper appears to be technically sound, with well-presented proposals. Although a thorough check of all equations was not conducted, the overall framework is convincing. In terms of novelty, while the idea of extending the generative moment matching framework to conditional settings is natural, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious. This suggests that the paper contributes novel and useful ideas to the field.

The derived framework is likely to be useful and relevant to researchers interested in using deep models for generative purposes or distilling complex statistical processes. The framework's applicability is demonstrated in Algorithm 1, indicating that it can be easily applied in various contexts. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, making it accessible to a broad audience. The appendix provides additional information on the methods and experiments, which is a valuable resource for readers seeking more details.

Overall, the paper's technical soundness, novelty, and usefulness make it a valuable contribution to the field of deep generative modeling.
