- Extends GMMN to conditional generation by deriving a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) and kernel embedding for conditional distributions.  
- CMMD criterion and its empirical estimator are non-trivial, well-derived, and amenable to back-propagation.  
- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) with a practical Algorithm 1 for implementation.  
- Empirical evaluation on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation shows competitive performance.  
- Technically sound and a logical, original extension of existing generative modeling work.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes a helpful appendix with methodological and experimental details.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical, competitive results, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: experimental gains are not clearly superior to state-of-the-art.  
- Theoretical limitation: CMMD’s Hilbert–Schmidt related assumptions may not always hold in practice (acknowledged by authors).
