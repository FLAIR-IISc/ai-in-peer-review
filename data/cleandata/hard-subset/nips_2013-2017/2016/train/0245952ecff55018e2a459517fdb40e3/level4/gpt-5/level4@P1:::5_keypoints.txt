- Extends GMMN to conditional setting by embedding conditional distributions in RKHS and defining a conditional MMD (CMMD); trains CGMMN with SGD/backprop.
- Technical correctness concern: empirical estimator C_hat(Y|X) appears to omit mu_X and mu_Y (centering), requiring clarification on centering, regularization, gradient effects, and reproducibility.
- Otherwise methodological development is sound; CMMD is a neat and natural extension of MMD to conditionals.
- Originality: novel use of conditional mean embeddings to train deep conditional generators, contrasting with adversarial conditional GANs.
- Predictive performance: CGMMN has higher error rates than the strong baseline CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN.
- Generative modeling: produces plausible conditional samples but lacks quantitative or comparative evaluation against conditional GANs or other methods.
- Dark knowledge distillation: interesting application, but evaluation is limited; Boston Housing shows no degradation versus teacher, broader evidence needed.
- Clarity/presentation: generally readable but contains typos and must explicitly address RKHS centering and its impact on empirical CMMD and gradients.
- Pros: conceptually clean extension, simple backprop training avoiding min–max optimization, and broad applicability across tasks.
- Cons: weaker predictive performance than a strong baseline, no direct comparisons for sample quality/diversity, and ambiguity in conditional embedding implementation.
- Update: authors addressed all concerns in rebuttal; reviewer will change scores.
