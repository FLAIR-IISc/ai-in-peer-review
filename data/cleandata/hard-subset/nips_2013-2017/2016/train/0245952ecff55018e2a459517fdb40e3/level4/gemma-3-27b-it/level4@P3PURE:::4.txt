This paper extends the deep generative modeling approach of “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” to enable conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing it to be optimized using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears technically sound and the presentation is clear; however, a complete verification of all equations was not performed. While extending generative moment matching to a conditional setting is a natural progression, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious, suggesting both novelty and usefulness. The derived framework is straightforward to apply, as illustrated in Algorithm 1, and this work is likely to be valuable and relevant to researchers interested in deep generative models or distilling more complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, and the appendix effectively provides detailed information regarding the methods and experiments.
