- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) by deriving a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective via kernel embedding of conditional moments.  
- CMMD estimator enables training conditional generative models using the same SGD/backprop machinery as unconditional GMMNs.  
- Theoretical development is rigorous: preliminaries on RKHS embeddings, derivation of CMMD, and Theorem 3 linking embeddings to distribution equality.  
- Network architecture is straightforward and easily implementable; training summarized in Algorithm 1.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical results are competitive on MNIST and SVHN for prediction and produce realistic samples on MNIST and Yale Face.  
- Demonstrates effective distillation of Bayesian models into smaller CGMMN student networks.  
- Novelty: natural but nontrivial and useful extension of GMMN to the conditional setting; estimator derivation not obvious.  
- Writing and presentation are clear, well-organized, and supplemented by a helpful appendix for reproducibility.  
- Strengths: technical soundness, clarity, novelty, significance across multiple application areas, and experimental completeness.  
- Weaknesses: limited analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel bandwidth, regularization), unclear computational cost relative to other conditional models.  
- Weaknesses: lacks more direct empirical comparisons with other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional VAEs, conditional GANs).  
- Reviewer recommendation: accept — minor weaknesses do not outweigh the paper’s technical contributions and promising results.
