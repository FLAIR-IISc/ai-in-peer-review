

This paper introduces conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMNs), extending generative moment matching networks (GMMNs) to conditional generation and prediction tasks. The core innovation lies in kernel embedding of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD metric for quantifying discrepancies between conditional distributions. The proposed CGMMN is evaluated across classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation. The naive extension of GMMNs to conditional settings—training a separate GMMN per conditional distribution with shared neural network parameters—suffers from data sparsity, particularly when conditioning variables are continuous (leading to single-example conditional distributions). The authors' approach instead models the entire family of conditional distributions, matching the model directly to the conditional embedding operator rather than individual distributions. While the proposed method offers clear advantages, the naive approach may still perform reasonably well in scenarios with discrete conditioning (e.g., 10 classes in MNIST conditional generation), warranting comparison as a baseline. The conditional MMD objective is well-formulated (as noted in Remark 1), computable nearly identically to unconditional MMD, with all K matrices in Equation 2 independent of model parameters, enabling standard backpropagation. A key concern is the potential need for large minibatches to mitigate stochasticity-induced errors, which increases computational cost—particularly for expensive matrix inverses. Overall, the CMMD framework provides a valuable approach for conditional distribution modeling, with compelling results on classification and Bayesian model distillation. However, sections 2.3 and 3.1 are difficult to follow without consulting referenced papers. Minor issues include: (1) Line 77 incorrectly claims the MMD estimator is unbiased; (2) Line 90 uses a conditional embedding operator inconsistent with reference [29], requiring a different cross-covariance operator definition for the paper's formulation.
