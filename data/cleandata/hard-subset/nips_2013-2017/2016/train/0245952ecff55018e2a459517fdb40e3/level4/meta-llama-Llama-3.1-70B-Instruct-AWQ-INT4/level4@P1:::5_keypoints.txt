- Contribution: Proposes CGMMN, extending GMMNs to match conditional distributions by embedding them in an RKHS using CMMD.  
- Context and motivation: Provides thorough introduction and positions CGMMN relative to GMMNs and GANs, explaining their limitations.  
- Technical clarity: CGMMN algorithm and use of conditional MMD as the training objective are well explained.  
- Empirical evaluation: Experiments on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face for both predictive and generative tasks show potential.  
- Performance caveat: Results are promising but not consistently competitive with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Major technical concern: Definition of the estimated conditional operator \hat{C}_{Y|X} omits centering terms involving μ_X and μ_Y.  
- Unjustified assumption: Assuming μ_X and μ_Y are zero in the RKHS (data already centered) is unusual and needs justification.  
- Required clarification: Authors should explain how the missing centering terms affect gradient computation and experimental implementation.  
- Comparative experiments: More comparisons with other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs) are needed.  
- Minor issues: Several typos present ("Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").  
- Overall assessment: A neat, significant idea with clear exposition but needs clarifications and stronger empirical comparisons to be fully convincing.  
- Acceptance pros/cons: Pros — novel conditional generative approach and clear technical presentation; Cons — weaker empirical performance, unresolved estimator/centering issues, and limited comparisons.
