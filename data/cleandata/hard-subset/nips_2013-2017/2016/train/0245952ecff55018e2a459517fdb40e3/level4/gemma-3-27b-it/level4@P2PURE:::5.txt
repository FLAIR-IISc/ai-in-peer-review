The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by focusing on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. Their key idea is to embed these conditional distributions within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, building on the technique introduced by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed conditional GMMN (CGMMN) is evaluated empirically using several real datasets. However, the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90) appears to omit a term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (specifically, their estimators). Furthermore, the assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero – implying the data is already centered in the RKHS – is unusual. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to CGMMN, the authors should clarify this omission and detail how to account for it during gradient computation and in their experiments. The approach presented is novel and well-conceived, but the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. Regarding predictive performance, CGMMN consistently yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA algorithm on both datasets. Concerning generative performance, the lack of comparison with other conditional generative methods, such as conditional generative adversarial networks, makes it difficult to assess the quality of samples generated by the proposed algorithm.
