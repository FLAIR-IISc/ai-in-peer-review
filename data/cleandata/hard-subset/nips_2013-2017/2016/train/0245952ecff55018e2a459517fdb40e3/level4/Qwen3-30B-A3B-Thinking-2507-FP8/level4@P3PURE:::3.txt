

This paper introduces a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending generative moment matching networks (GMMNs) by incorporating conditional kernel mean embeddings to define a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) loss. The approach is evaluated across multiple generative tasks, including classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and knowledge distillation in Bayesian models. This is my second review of the manuscript; the minor issues raised in the prior review—concerning the choice of low-dimensional latent space, scalability to higher dimensions, comparative utility against existing methods, and miscellaneous clarifications—appear to have been adequately addressed in the revised text.  

Regarding Remark 1, the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form requires further clarification. Specifically, the dot notation used in the equation is ambiguous: is it matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product? A more detailed derivation would improve the exposition. Additionally, the notation choice for \( \mathcal{C} \) is problematic, as it conflicts with the existing definition of \( \mathcal{C} \) for conditional embeddings.  

Theorem 3 lacks a reference to a supporting paper or an explicit proof. A citation to a relevant proof or a brief derivation should be provided.  

The empirical results are sufficient for the tasks presented. While the supervised learning application was previously critiqued as suboptimal, the authors’ justification for its inclusion—highlighting model versatility—is acceptable. The distilled Bayesian inference application is novel but limited to a single dataset and model; its broader applicability remains unclear. This approach would likely gain the most traction in pure generative modeling, where the experiments (digits and faces) are comparable to those in the original GMMN paper. To establish wider adoption, compelling results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10 or LSUN) would be necessary.
