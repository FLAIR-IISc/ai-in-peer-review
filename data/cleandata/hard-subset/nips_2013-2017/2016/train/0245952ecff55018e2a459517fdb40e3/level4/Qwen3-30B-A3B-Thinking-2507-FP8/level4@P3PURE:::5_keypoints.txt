- Paper extends GMMNs to model conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in an RKHS, building on Song et al. (2009).  
- Proposed CGMMN algorithm is evaluated on several real-world datasets.  
- Critical flaw: definition of \hat{C}_{Y|X} (Line 90) omits terms related to μ_X and μ_Y (and their estimators), effectively assuming zero-centered RKHS features without justification.  
- Authors must clarify the missing terms and how they affect gradient computation and practical implementation of CGMMN.  
- Novel and elegant approach, but experimental results are underwhelming.  
- Predictive performance: CGMMN has higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets.  
- Generative performance: no comparisons to conditional generative baselines (e.g., conditional GANs), so sample quality advantage is unclear.  
- Minor typographical errors: “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).  
- Update: after the author rebuttal, the reviewer states all concerns were addressed and will adjust the score.
