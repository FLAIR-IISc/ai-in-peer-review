

The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional framework. Unlike in models that permit single-example updates where the extension is trivial (e.g., simply feeding conditionals into the network), this conditional adaptation is non-trivial. The core challenge arises because the method requires matching distributions across large batches of samples to corresponding inputs, yet these distributions are no longer identical when conditionals vary across the batch (using identical conditionals for all samples in a batch would be inefficient).  

The paper's main deficiency lies in its presentation: it begins with formal mathematical machinery without first establishing intuitive motivation for the proposed approach. Specifically, it fails to clarify:  
1. The intuitive rationale behind the optimization objective (equation (2)),  
2. The concrete computational steps executed by the algorithm,  
3. The efficiency of these computations, and  
4. The assumptions enabling the approach.  

This contrasts sharply with the non-conditional version (presented in the original paper), where the optimization objective is explained intuitively without heavy mathematical formalism (e.g., avoiding reliance on reproducing kernel Hilbert space). For the conditional case, the authors should provide an equally accessible explanation. To address this gap, they should explicitly answer:  
- How one might intuitively derive equation (2), and  
- The precise algorithmic steps translating equation (2) into practice.  

While some details appear in the supplement, the explanation remains fragmented and difficult to follow. A clear, step-by-step derivation of the practical algorithm from the theoretical formulation is essential for reproducibility and reader comprehension.
