This paper proposes a novel loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables, building upon the concept of generative moment matching networks. The authors extend the unconditional loss function based on the maximum mean discrepancy criterion by leveraging work on conditional kernel mean embeddings. The proposed approach is demonstrated on various generative tasks, including classification, generating handwritten digits and faces, and distilling knowledge in Bayesian models.

I have reviewed this paper previously, and the authors have addressed the minor concerns raised in the initial review. Specifically, they have clarified the use of a low-dimensional space and its potential scalability to higher-dimensional latent spaces, as well as the utility of their model compared to others. However, I still have some minor concerns that require attention.

Regarding Remark 1, I find it unclear how to map Equation (2) onto the weighted MMD form. The dot notation used in this context is ambiguous, and it would be beneficial to specify whether it represents matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product. I would appreciate a more detailed explanation to make the mapping more apparent. Furthermore, the choice of the variable "C" is somewhat unfortunate, as it is already used to define the conditional embedding.

The authors reference Theorems 1 and 2 from other papers, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference. To maintain consistency and transparency, I recommend providing a reference to a paper containing a proof or including the proof itself.

The empirical results presented in the paper are decent, and the authors have acknowledged the limitations of using this model for supervised learning. The application to distilled Bayesian inference is intriguing, but its utility is only demonstrated on a single dataset with one model. Consequently, it is unclear whether this approach will be effective in other cases. I believe that this method would be most impactful in the context of pure generative modeling.

The experiments conducted in this paper are comparable to those in the original GMM paper, and the datasets used (involving digits and faces) are sufficient to demonstrate the feasibility of the proposed idea. However, to encourage widespread adoption of this method, more compelling results on challenging datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN, would be necessary.

Overall, the authors have made significant progress in addressing the initial concerns, and the paper presents an interesting approach to directed generative models. With some minor revisions to address the remaining concerns, this paper has the potential to contribute meaningfully to the field.
