- Proposes CMMD, a conditional extension of GMMN using conditional kernel mean embeddings and an MMD-based loss for directed generative models with conditioning variables.
- Method derives a CMMD criterion and provides an efficient implementation via backpropagation.
- Paper demonstrates results on predictive modeling, generating digits and faces, and distilling Bayesian models.
- Empirical results are solid and show the method’s competitiveness across diverse tasks, sufficient as a proof of concept.
- Authors have addressed prior reviewers’ minor concerns about latent dimensionality, model utility, and clarifying details.
- Equation (2) to weighted MMD connection is unclear; dot-product notation needs clarification (matrix vs. Hadamard) and variable ‘C’ is overloaded.
- Theorem 3 lacks citation or a provided proof and needs theoretical justification.
- Bayesian-distillation application is intriguing but evaluated on only a single dataset/model, limiting assessment of generalizability.
- The method is most impactful for pure generative modeling; broader adoption requires results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
- Arguments for acceptance: logical extension of GMMN, significant technical contribution (CMMD derivation and implementation), strong experiments, and addressed previous concerns.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing theoretical justification for Theorem 3, unclear CMMD–weighted MMD connection, limited Bayesian distillation scope, and need for evaluation on harder datasets.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify theoretical points and expand experimental evaluation.
