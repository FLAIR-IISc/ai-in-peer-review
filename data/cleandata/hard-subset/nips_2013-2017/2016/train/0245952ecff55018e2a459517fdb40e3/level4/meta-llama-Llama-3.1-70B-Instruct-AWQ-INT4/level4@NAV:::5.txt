The authors propose an extension to generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by shifting focus from matching joint distributions to conditional distributions. This approach leverages the embedding of conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, a method initially proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The empirical evaluation of the proposed CGMMN algorithm is conducted on multiple real-world datasets. However, the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90) appears to be incomplete, as it lacks the term associated with $\muX$ and $\muY$ (or their estimators). Furthermore, assuming $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero, implying that the data is centered in the RKHS, is unconventional. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is a crucial component of CGMMN, the authors should provide clarification on the missing term and its treatment in gradient computation and real-world experiments. Although the proposed approach is innovative and elegant, the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. In terms of predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA algorithm on both datasets. Moreover, the absence of a comparison between CGMMN and other conditional generative methods (such as conditional generative adversarial networks) makes it unclear whether the proposed algorithm can generate superior samples.
