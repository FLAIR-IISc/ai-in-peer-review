- Proposes CGMMNs: extends GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embedding of conditional distributions and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.  
- Treats all conditional distributions as a family via the conditional embedding operator, avoiding per-condition estimators and data sparsity.  
- Naive baseline (separate GMMN per condition with shared network) could perform well when conditioning has few discrete values (e.g., MNIST); paper lacks this comparison.  
- CMMD objective is nicely formulated and computable similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, so backpropagation remains straightforward.  
- CMMD may demand large minibatches to reduce stochastic error, but large batches make matrix inverses expensive and potentially prohibitive.  
- Experiments on classification, conditional generation, and distilling Bayesian models are interesting and show promise.  
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow; reviewer needed to consult cited papers for clarity.  
- Minor issues: (1) statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect; (2) the conditional embedding operator definition used is inconsistent with reference [29] and seems to require a different cross-covariance operator.
