- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to conditional generation by deriving a kernel embedding for conditional moments and an empirical estimator (CGMMN).  
- Optimizable using techniques similar to GMMN and presented practically via Algorithm 1.  
- Shows competitive empirical performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Technically sound and logically developed; derivation appears novel though not all equations were fully verified.  
- Well-written and clearly organized with a helpful appendix and experimental details.  
- Strengths: solid foundation, originality in conditional derivation, clarity, practical usefulness, and experimental validation.  
- Weaknesses: incomplete verification of some equations and the contribution may be viewed as an incremental advance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension with clear exposition, experiments, and potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: perceived incremental novelty and minor uncertainty from unverified equations.  
- Overall assessment: a worthwhile, practical contribution to deep generative modeling suitable for acceptance despite limited incremental nature.
