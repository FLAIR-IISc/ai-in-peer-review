- Introduces conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction via kernel conditional embeddings and CMMD.
- Core idea: match the model to a conditional embedding operator (family of conditionals) instead of estimating individual conditional GMMNs to avoid data sparsity.
- Uses a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) metric built on kernel embeddings; objective is elegantly formulated and backprop-friendly since kernel matrices are parameter-independent.
- Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, showing competitive results across tasks.
- Demonstrates promising Bayesian model distillation experiments that support the method’s utility.
- Lacks comparison to the simpler baseline of individual conditional GMMNs (e.g., for discrete labels like MNIST), which would strengthen empirical claims.
- Potential computational concern: CMMD may require large mini-batches and costly matrix inversions, risking high computation/memory cost.
- Suggests need for discussion of mitigation strategies (approximations or efficient linear algebra) for scalability.
- Clarity issues: sections 2.3 and 3.1 are dense and hard to follow, requiring external references for full understanding.
- Minor technical inaccuracies: the paper claims the MMD estimator is unbiased (it is consistent but not unbiased).
- Another technical issue: the conditional embedding operator definition appears inconsistent with the cited reference [29] and needs reconciliation or explicit derivation.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel application of MMD/kernel methods to conditional generative modeling, addresses data sparsity, competitive experiments, and elegant CMMD formulation.
- Arguments against acceptance: clarity problems, minor technical errors, missing naive baseline comparison, and possible computational challenges.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve exposition (sections 2.3/3.1), correct technical inaccuracies, reconcile the conditional operator definition, and add baseline comparisons.
