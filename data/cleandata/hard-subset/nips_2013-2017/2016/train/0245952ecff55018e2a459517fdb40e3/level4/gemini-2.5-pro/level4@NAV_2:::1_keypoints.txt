Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective derived from kernel embeddings.  
Generates samples by feeding a conditioning variable and noise through a deep network trained by minimizing CMMD with SGD/backprop.  
Evaluated on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
Originality/significance: novel and well-motivated extension of MMD-based generative models to the conditional setting, offering an alternative to conditional GANs/VAEs.  
Technical quality: CMMD is theoretically well-grounded and its connection to standard MMD is clearly articulated.  
Experimental breadth: diverse tasks, including a novel application to “Bayesian dark knowledge,” demonstrate model versatility.  
Performance: competitive predictive accuracy comparable to CMMVA and convincing qualitative class-conditional samples.  
Reproducibility concern: key hyperparameters (kernel types, bandwidths, regularization λ) and implementation details are not specified and code is not released.  
Computational cost concern: CMMD requires Gram-matrix computation/inversion per mini-batch (cubic scaling), but no runtime or complexity analysis is provided.  
Scale concern: experiments use relatively small/standard datasets; evaluation on larger, higher-dimensional datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, CelebA) is needed to demonstrate scalability.  
Clarity concern: Section 3.1 is technically dense and would benefit from a more intuitive explanation of the CMMD objective and the role of K̃⁻¹ re-weighting.
