- Proposes a novel loss for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending generative moment matching networks.  
- Derives the loss using conditional kernel mean embeddings and an MMD-based criterion.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on tasks: classification, generating handwritten digits and faces, and distilling Bayesian models.  
- Authors addressed prior-round minor concerns (low-dimensional space, scalability, utility, clarifications).  
- Remark 1: mapping from Eq. (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear and needs clarification.  
- Ambiguous dot notation in Remark 1—must specify whether it denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product.  
- Notation conflict: constant C reuses symbol already used for conditional embedding; recommend a distinct symbol.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a proof or reference and must include one to preserve rigor.  
- Empirical results are decent but supervised application is not the method’s strongest use—shows versatility instead.  
- Distilled Bayesian inference application is interesting but only evaluated on one dataset/model, so generality is unclear.  
- Method is likely most effective for pure generative modeling rather than supervised tasks.  
- Experiments are comparable to original GMM work; datasets (digits, faces) demonstrate feasibility but stronger results on challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) are needed for broader adoption.
