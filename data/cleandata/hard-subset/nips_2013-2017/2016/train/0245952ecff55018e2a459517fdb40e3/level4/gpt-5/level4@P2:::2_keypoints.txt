- Paper proposes Conditional GMMN (CGMMN), training a generator p(y|x) with noise h via a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective in RKHS.
- Generator passes conditioning x and noise h through a deep network; learning matches conditional distributions of generated and real data.
- Positions CGMMN relative to GMMN/MMD literature and as a counterpart to conditional GANs/VAEs; CMMD addresses principled comparison of conditionals.
- Strength: Extending MMD to conditionals is meaningful and non-trivial—matching distributions across varying x is an important problem.
- Strength: Training is compatible with standard backprop and conceptually simple once the objective is specified.
- Strength: Broad applications (predictive modeling, conditional generation, Bayesian distillation) and competitive empirical performance.
- Weakness: Exposition starts with heavy RKHS formalism and Eq. (2) with little intuition; CMMD isn’t made interpretable like vanilla MMD.
- Weakness: Lacks clear, practical description of minibatch computation—what Gram matrices to build, which inverses to compute, reuse strategies, and complexity scaling.
- Weakness: Assumptions for conditional embedding operator (existence, regularization) are stated but not unpacked or made practical.
- Weakness: Does not clearly address the practical challenge of matching different conditionals in minibatches—statistical efficiency and variance issues are not explained.
- Request: Provide a high-level derivation/intuition of Eq. (2) from MMD (e.g., CMMD as a weighted MMD where weights reflect x-similarity).
- Request: Give concise step-by-step minibatch update instructions (which matrices K,L,Ksd,Lds to form, adding λI, inverting, trace computation, backprop paths, per-iteration complexity).
- Request: Clarify efficiency details—reuse of (K+λI)−1 within/across iterations, effects of batch size, kernel choices, and λ on runtime/stability.
- Request: State conditions on kernels/data that ensure well-behaved conditional embedding estimates and sensitivity to λ and conditioning kernel.
- Request: Explain sampling scheme for x in continuous X—whether to reuse same x’s, sample nearby x’s, and empirical trade-offs.
- Suggestion: Rework Section 3 to start from MMD intuition, motivate non-uniform weighting, then derive CMMD and present operator view afterwards.
- Suggestion: Include compact pseudocode listing matrices, inverses, gradient paths, and per-minibatch complexity/memory analysis.
- Suggestion: Add ablations/discussion on kernel choices for x and y, batch size effects, and impact of reusing vs. resampling x’s; move key supplement derivations/algorithm into main paper.
