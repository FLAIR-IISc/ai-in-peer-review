- Extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting, which is nontrivial because you must match distributions for many samples against many, differing conditionings.  
- Unlike single-example updates, conditioning here cannot be handled simply by feeding conditioning variables into the network.  
- Reusing the same conditioning variables per update would be inefficient, highlighting a practical challenge.  
- Main critique: the paper emphasizes heavy formalism up front instead of first providing intuition for the objective.  
- The derivation and implications of Eq. (2) are not unpacked—readers can’t see the necessary assumptions or what computations are actually performed.  
- Computational cost and efficiency of the proposed method are unclear.  
- The transition from Eq. (2) to the practical training procedure is insufficiently explained; the supplement is hard to follow.  
- The paper should offer an accessible, RKHS-free intuition for the conditional case analogous to the non-conditional presentation.
