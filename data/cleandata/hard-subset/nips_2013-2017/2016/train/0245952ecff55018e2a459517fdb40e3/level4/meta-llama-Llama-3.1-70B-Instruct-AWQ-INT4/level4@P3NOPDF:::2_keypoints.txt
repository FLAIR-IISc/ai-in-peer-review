- The paper tackles a non-trivial extension of moment matching networks to the conditional setting, which requires matching distributions across many inputs (mini-batch) rather than a single identical distribution.  
- The main technical challenge is matching multiple, non-identical distributions per update, causing inefficiency unless the same conditional is used repeatedly.  
- The primary criticism is the lack of an intuitive explanation for the proposed formula and the actual computations performed by the algorithm.  
- The authors present complex mathematics before establishing basic intuition, making the method hard to follow.  
- Readers are left unclear about what equation (2) actually optimizes, its computational steps, efficiency, and underlying assumptions.  
- The non-conditional version is cited as an example of clear, intuition-driven exposition that the conditional case lacks.  
- Reviewers ask for an intuitive derivation of formula (2) and a step-by-step translation of that formula into a practical algorithm.  
- Some details are in the supplement but are hard to follow and should be integrated into the main text for clarity.
