- Introduces CGMMNs: extends GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction using kernel embedding and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.
- Core innovation: matching the model to the conditional embedding operator rather than individual conditional distributions.
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation with promising results.
- Naive baseline (separate GMMN per condition with shared NN) suffers severe data sparsity for continuous conditions; CGMMN addresses this by unifying conditional distributions.
- However, the naive baseline performs acceptably for discrete conditioning (e.g., MNIST); an explicit comparison against it is needed.
- CMMD formulation is elegant and computationally efficient—kernel matrices are parameter-independent, enabling backprop like unconditional MMD.
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to control stochastic error, and large minibatches are costly due to expensive matrix inversions.
- Practical impact of computational constraints (minibatch size vs. matrix inversion cost) should be clarified and discussed.
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 lacks clarity and forces readers to consult referenced papers.
- Specific error: Line 77 incorrectly claims the MMD estimator is unbiased; this holds only under specific finite-sample/kernel conditions.
- Specific inconsistency: Conditional embedding operator definition (line 90) conflicts with reference [29] and needs a distinct cross-covariance operator definition to justify the derivation.
- Technical contributions are sound; fixing minor technical inconsistencies and clarifying computational trade-offs would significantly strengthen the paper.
