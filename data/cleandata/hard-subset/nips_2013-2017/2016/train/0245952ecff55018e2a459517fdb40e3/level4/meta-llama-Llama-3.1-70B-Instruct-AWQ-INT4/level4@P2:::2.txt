This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend the capability of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to learn conditional distributions. The authors propose a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion to measure the difference between two conditional distributions and develop a deep generative architecture to learn the conditional distribution. The learning process is performed using stochastic gradient descent with the gradient calculated by back-propagation.

The main idea of CGMMN is to learn a conditional distribution given some input variables based on the CMMD criterion. The authors provide a theoretical foundation for CMMD and show its connection to the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion. They also propose a practical algorithm to estimate the CMMD objective and optimize it using gradient descent.

The authors evaluate CGMMN on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The experimental results demonstrate that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art models in these tasks.

Strengths:

* The authors propose a novel extension of GMMN to learn conditional distributions, which is a significant contribution to the field of deep generative models.
* The theoretical foundation of CMMD is well-established, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the concept.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN in various tasks.

Weaknesses:

* The paper starts with fancy math, which may make it difficult for some readers to understand the intuition behind the formula.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the actual computations performed during the algorithm and their efficiency.
* The paper could benefit from a more intuitive explanation of the conditional case, similar to the non-conditional version of the algorithm.

Suggestions for improvement:

* Provide a clear and intuitive explanation of the CMMD criterion and its connection to MMD.
* Explain the actual computations performed during the algorithm and their efficiency.
* Consider adding a section that summarizes the key contributions and implications of the paper.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of deep generative models.
* The theoretical foundation of CMMD is well-established, and the experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN.
* The paper has the potential to inspire new research directions in the field of deep generative models.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper may be difficult to understand for some readers due to the lack of intuitive explanation.
* The authors do not provide a clear explanation of the actual computations performed during the algorithm and their efficiency.
* The paper could benefit from more detailed analysis and discussion of the experimental results.
