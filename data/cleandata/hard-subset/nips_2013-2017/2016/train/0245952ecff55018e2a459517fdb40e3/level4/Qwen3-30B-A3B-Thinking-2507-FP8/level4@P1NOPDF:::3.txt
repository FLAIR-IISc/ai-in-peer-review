

This paper proposes a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending generative moment matching networks (GMMN) by incorporating conditional kernel mean embeddings. The approach uses the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion to define a conditional loss, demonstrated across generative tasks including classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  

This is my second review of the paper. The authors have addressed all prior minor concerns: the rationale for low-dimensional latent spaces, scalability to higher dimensions, comparative utility against existing methods, and other clarifying points. However, two technical points require clarification. First, the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form in Remark 1 is unclear. Specifically, the "dot notation" used is ambiguous—does it denote matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product? A more detailed derivation would significantly improve clarity. Second, the choice of symbol $C$ is problematic, as it conflicts with the notation for the conditional embedding (defined earlier in Section 3). To avoid confusion, the authors should adopt a different symbol for the weighting coefficient.  

Additionally, Theorem 3 lacks a citation or proof. While Theorems 1 and 2 reference prior work, Theorem 3 requires either a citation to a source containing the proof or a brief derivation in the appendix.  

The empirical results are satisfactory. The authors’ justification for applying the model to supervised classification (previously questioned) as a demonstration of versatility is reasonable. The Bayesian distillation application is novel but limited to a single dataset and model, making it difficult to assess broader utility. This suggests the method’s strongest potential lies in pure generative modeling, where the experiments (digits and faces) are sufficiently compelling to validate the core idea. The results align with those in the original GMMN paper, but for wider adoption, the authors should demonstrate performance on more challenging benchmarks like CIFAR-10 or LSUN. Without such evidence, the method’s practical impact remains uncertain.  

Overall, the technical contributions are sound, and the revisions address prior feedback. Clarifying the notation in Remark 1, resolving the $C$ conflict, and providing the missing reference for Theorem 3 would strengthen the paper significantly. The current experiments support the method’s viability for basic generative tasks but fall short of establishing its broader utility.
