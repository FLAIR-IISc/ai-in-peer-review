- Novel contribution: extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting, which is non-trivial due to matching mini-batch sample distributions to many different conditionals.
- Main critique: paper opens with heavy formalism instead of building intuition for the reader.
- Key technical gap: eq (2) (the final objective) is not well explained—its derivation, assumptions, and practical implications are unclear.
- Computational concern: the paper does not detail the actual computations performed during updates nor analyze the efficiency of the algorithm.
- Reproducibility/usability issue: mapping from eq (2) to a practical algorithm is insufficiently explained; supplemental material is hard to follow.
- Requested clarification: provide an intuitive derivation of eq (2) and a step-by-step description of how to implement the algorithm in practice.
- Strength: provides a solid mathematical foundation for the proposed conditional moment matching method.
- Strength: empirical results show effectiveness across tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, Bayesian dark knowledge) and competitive performance with SOTA.
- Weakness: lacks discussion of assumptions, limitations, and when the method may fail or be impractical.
- Clarity: well-written mathematically but potentially inaccessible to non-experts without more intuition and algorithmic detail.
- Quality: thorough evaluation and comparisons bolster the paper’s empirical credibility.
- Originality/significance: offers a novel perspective on matching distributions across large sample sets and inputs, addressing a meaningful extension of prior work.
