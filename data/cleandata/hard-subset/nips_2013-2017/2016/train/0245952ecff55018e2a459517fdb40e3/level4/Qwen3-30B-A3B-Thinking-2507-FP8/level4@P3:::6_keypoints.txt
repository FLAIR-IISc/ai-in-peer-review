- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion to learn p(y|x) by matching conditional embedding operators.  
- Addresses data sparsity of training separate GMMNs per condition by treating conditional distributions as a family and matching operators directly.  
- Builds on GMMN and kernel conditional embedding literature; focuses on conditional p(y|x) which is more practical for prediction and contextual generation.  
- Theoretical result (Theorem 3) shows matching conditional embedding operators suffices to match conditional distributions, extending MMD to conditional settings.  
- Originality: provides a moment-matching alternative to conditional GANs that is theoretically grounded and simpler computationally.  
- Experimental quality: comprehensive evaluation on classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Empirical performance: CGMMN-CNN achieves competitive SVHN error (3.13% vs 3.09% state-of-the-art CMMVA); distillation retains prediction performance (RMSE comparable).  
- Significance: applicable to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and efficient Bayesian model distillation; relevant to the community.  
- Clarity issue: presentation of conditional embedding and CMMD is dense and hard to follow without cited references.  
- Needs clearer comparison to naive approach (training separate GMMNs), with a simple example especially for discrete conditions.  
- Technical inaccuracy: incorrectly claims MMD estimator is unbiased for finite samples; it is only asymptotically unbiased.  
- Formal inconsistency: definition of the conditional embedding operator differs from Grünewälder et al. (2012) and requires clarification and justification.  
- Computational concern: CMMD requires matrix inverses that scale cubically; paper lacks detail on practical approximations and minibatch inverse computation.  
- Stochasticity/minibatch issue: unclear how large minibatches must be to mitigate variance and how inverses are approximated in large-scale settings.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address clarity, technical corrections, operator definition consistency, and computational details.  
- Specific suggested revisions: clarify CMMD vs naive GMMNs, correct MMD unbiased claim, reconcile/justify conditional operator definition, describe matrix-inverse approximations, and discuss computational complexity relative to other conditional generative models.
