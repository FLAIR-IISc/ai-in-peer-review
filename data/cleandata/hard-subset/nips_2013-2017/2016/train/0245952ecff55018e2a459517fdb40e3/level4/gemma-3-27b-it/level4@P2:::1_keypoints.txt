- Introduces CGMMN using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion — a novel extension of GMMNs to conditional generation.  
- Strong theoretical grounding in kernel methods and maximum mean discrepancy (MMD).  
- Demonstrates both discriminative and generative capabilities on several standard benchmark tasks.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, though mathematical details require careful attention.  
- Experimental evaluation uses reasonable, typical-size datasets but lacks larger-scale tests.  
- Shows competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Computational cost is insufficiently analyzed — matrix inversions in CMMD could be expensive and need training-time comparisons (e.g., with DSN).  
- Reproducibility is a concern because code is not publicly released.  
- Limited experiment scope — should evaluate on larger/more complex datasets to demonstrate scalability and generalization.  
- Overall recommendation: accept for novelty and theoretical/empirical promise, contingent on authors releasing code to address reproducibility.
