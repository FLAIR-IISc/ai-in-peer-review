The authors extend moment matching networks to their conditional version, a non-trivial task that requires matching distributions of a large number of samples (mini-batch) to a large number of inputs. Unlike models that can update on a single example, where the extension is straightforward (feeding the conditional into the network), this paper tackles a more complex problem. The challenge arises from the need to match multiple distributions, which are no longer identical, unless the same conditional is used for a given update, resulting in inefficiency.

The primary issue with the paper is its failure to provide an intuitive explanation behind the proposed formula and the actual computations performed during the algorithm. The authors delve into complex mathematics without first establishing a clear understanding of the underlying concepts. This makes it difficult for readers to grasp the essence of the formula being optimized (eq. (2)), its efficiency, and the assumptions made to ensure its functionality. As a result, the reader is left without a clear understanding of the actual computations involved.

In contrast, the non-conditional version of the algorithm can be explained without resorting to advanced mathematical concepts (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert space), as demonstrated in the original paper. The formula being minimized is intuitive and easy to comprehend. It would be beneficial for the authors to provide an equally clear and intuitive explanation for the conditional case.

To improve the paper, the authors should address the following questions:

1. How would one intuitively derive formula (2)?
2. What are the steps involved in translating formula (2) into a practical algorithm?

While some of this information is provided in the supplement, it is difficult to follow and could be better integrated into the main text to enhance the overall clarity and coherence of the paper.
