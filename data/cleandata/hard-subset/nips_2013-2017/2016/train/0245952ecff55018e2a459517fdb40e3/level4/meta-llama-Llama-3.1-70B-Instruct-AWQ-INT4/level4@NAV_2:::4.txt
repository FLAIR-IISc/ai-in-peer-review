This paper builds upon the deep generative modeling approach "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" and derives an extension that allows for conditional generation. The proposed model, Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), is demonstrated with reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of the predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears to be technically sound and the proposals are well presented.

The main idea of the paper is to extend the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion to a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, which allows for learning a conditional distribution given some input variables. The CMMD criterion is derived using the kernel embedding of conditional distributions, and it is shown to be a sufficient condition to guarantee that two conditional distributions are the same.

The paper is well-organized and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the background and the proposed method, and they demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through a variety of experiments. The experimental results show that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art methods in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

The strengths of the paper include its technical soundness, clarity of presentation, and the novelty of the proposed approach. The authors have done a good job of explaining the background and the proposed method, and they have demonstrated the effectiveness of their approach through a variety of experiments.

The weaknesses of the paper include the limited scope of the experiments and the lack of comparison with other deep generative models. While the authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their approach in several tasks, it would be useful to see more experiments and comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods.

In terms of novelty, while the idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework is quite natural, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator was not obvious. The derived framework itself should be quite easy to apply, as shown in Algorithm 1, and this paper will likely be useful and of relevance to people who are interested in using deep models for generative purposes or to distill more elaborate statistical processes.
