- Introduces Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) with a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to model conditional distributions.  
- CMMD leverages kernel embeddings of conditional distributions to measure discrepancy between model and true conditional outputs.  
- Method addresses data sparsity from naive per-conditioning-value GMMNs, especially for continuous conditioning variables.  
- Principled, theoretically grounded extension that matches conditional embedding operators to share statistical strength across conditions.  
- CMMD is an intuitive extension of MMD; conditioning-variable kernel matrices are independent of model parameters, enabling backprop-based optimization.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, covering classification, contextual image generation, and distilling Bayesian models, demonstrating versatility.  
- Lacks direct empirical baselines for discrete-conditioning tasks (e.g., training multiple GMMNs with shared parameters) to validate advantages.  
- Potential scalability and computational concerns: CMMD estimation may need large minibatches and involves costly matrix inversions that scale poorly.  
- Presentation clarity needs improvement in Sections 2.3 and 3.1; conditional mean embeddings and CMMD are introduced too concisely.  
- Two minor technical inaccuracies: (1) claim that MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect for the common V-statistic; (2) form of conditional embedding operator (line 90) may not match cited reference and needs clarification.
