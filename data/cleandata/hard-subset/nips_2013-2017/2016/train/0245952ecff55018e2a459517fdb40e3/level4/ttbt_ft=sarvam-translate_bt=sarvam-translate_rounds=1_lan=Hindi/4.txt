This research paper is based on the deep generative modeling approach "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" and achieves an extension that allows conditional generation. (This is done by defining the kernel embedding of moments in such a way that the conditional distribution can be adapted using the same machinery as the unconditional version.) The proposed model has been demonstrated with reasonably competitive performance in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of predictions from Bayesian models. The paper appears technically sound, and the proposals are well-presented (although, I did not fully verify all equations). In terms of novelty, while the idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework is quite natural, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not clear (at least to me). And therefore, in this case, the paper appears to be novel and useful. The framework obtained should be quite easy to implement, as shown in Algorithm 1, resulting in the paper being likely to be useful and relevant for those interested in using deep models for generative purposes or distilling more detailed statistical processes. The paper was well-written, clear, and easy to read, and the provided appendix provides more complete information about the details of the methods and experiments used, which is useful.
