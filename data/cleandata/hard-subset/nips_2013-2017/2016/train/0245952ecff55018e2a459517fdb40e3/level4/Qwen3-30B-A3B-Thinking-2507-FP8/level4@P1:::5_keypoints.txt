- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to conditional distributions via conditional MMD (CMMD).  
- Learns generative model y = f(x, h | θ) with h ~ Uniform and trains by minimizing empirical CMMD using kernel mean embeddings.  
- Conceptually clean and well-motivated extension with solid RKHS theoretical grounding and practical training algorithm.  
- CMMD adapts MMD to conditional settings without explicit density modeling, enabling backprop-based training.  
- Straightforward generative architecture combining conditioning input x and latent noise h; experiments span predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Novel application: successfully distills Bayesian predictive distributions (Bayesian dark knowledge) without performance loss.  
- Critical technical gap: definition of empirical conditional operator ĤC_{Y|X} omits mean terms (μ_X, μ_Y) and assumes zero-centered RKHS, needing justification and impacting gradients/validity.  
- Limited experimental comparison: no comparisons to conditional generative baselines like cGAN or conditional VAE and no quantitative sample quality metrics (e.g., FID).  
- Understated limitations: predictive errors lag state-of-the-art (e.g., SVHN), generative models use simple MLPs versus stronger architectures, and no ablation studies (e.g., latent dimension).  
- Minor typos and clarity issues throughout the paper (examples provided).  
- Overall: technically sound and promising but the ĤC_{Y|X} omission and weak empirical comparisons undermine competitiveness.  
- Recommendation: reject pending major revisions to clarify ĤC_{Y|X} and add fair comparisons; would support acceptance if authors promptly resolve these concerns.
