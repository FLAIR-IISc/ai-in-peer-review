- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) to model conditional distributions via deep networks that map conditional variables + noise to samples.
- Builds on kernel embedding of conditional distributions (Song et al. 2009) and GMMN (Li et al. 2015), and acknowledges related conditional generative work (e.g., conditional GANs).
- Strength — Technical soundness: solid theoretical foundation and efficient gradient computation for CMMD with clear connection to kernel embedding techniques.
- Strength — Practical implementation: well-described generative process and practical mini-batch training (Algorithm 1) enabling efficient sampling and optimization.
- Strength — Diverse applications: evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge, showing framework versatility.
- Weakness — Theoretical gap: conditional embedding operator (Eq. 3) omits centering term (μ_X⊗μ_Y), undermining CMMD’s theoretical basis and gradient justification.
- Weakness — Empirical underperformance: CGMMN-CNN has higher error rates than state-of-the-art (e.g., SVHN 3.13% vs. 1.92%), with insufficient analysis of why.
- Weakness — Comparative evaluation lacking: insufficient comparisons with other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs) in generation tasks.
- Weakness — Clarity issues: several grammatical errors and typos reduce readability and professionalism.
- Suggestion — Fix theoretical formulation: include full cross-covariance (subtract μ_X⊗μ_Y) to properly define CMMD and validate gradient derivations.
- Suggestion — Expand experiments: compare against conditional generative baselines (conditional GANs, etc.) on the same datasets.
- Suggestion — Analyze underperformance: explain why CGMMN lags state-of-the-art in predictive tasks and assess whether architectural changes could help.
- Suggestion — Proofread manuscript to remove grammatical errors and typos.
- NIPS criteria — Quality: technically sound but weakened by the missing term in the conditional embedding operator; empirical evaluation is broad.
- NIPS criteria — Clarity: generally well-structured but marred by typos and a theoretical formulation issue.
- NIPS criteria — Originality: meaningful extension of GMMN to conditional settings, though incremental relative to existing conditional generative work.
- NIPS criteria — Significance: promising versatility, but empirical results do not convincingly beat state-of-the-art, limiting impact.
- Overall recommendation: Solid contribution recommended for acceptance after minor revisions to fix the theoretical gap and strengthen experimental comparisons; reviewer recommends acceptance after rebuttal addressed concerns.
