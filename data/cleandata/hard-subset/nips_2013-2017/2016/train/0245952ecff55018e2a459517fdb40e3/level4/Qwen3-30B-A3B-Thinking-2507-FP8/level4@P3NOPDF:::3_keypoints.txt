- Introduces a conditional loss for directed generative models via conditional kernel mean embeddings, extending GMMNs.
- Shows efficacy across tasks (digit/face generation, classification, Bayesian distillation), addressing limitations of unconditional GMMNs.
- Authors have addressed prior minor concerns (low-dimensional latent justification, scalability, comparative utility) in the revised manuscript.
- Technical issue 1: mapping from Eq.(2) to weighted MMD is ambiguous—dot notation in E_z[φ(x,z) · ψ(z)] must be specified (matrix vs Hadamard) and derived.
- Technical issue 2: constant C in Eq.(2) conflicts with earlier use for conditional embedding—rename (e.g., c) to avoid notational ambiguity.
- Theorem 3 lacks attribution or proof—provide a proof sketch or cite a foundational source (e.g., Song et al., 2018).
- Empirical results are strong for generative modeling; digit and face experiments match or exceed original GMMN benchmarks.
- Supervised classification use is justified as a demonstration of model versatility.
- Bayesian knowledge distillation experiment is too narrow (single dataset/architecture); needs broader evaluations (e.g., CIFAR-10, varied architectures).
- Scalability/generalization concern: focus on low-dimensional latents is reasonable for current tasks but unclear for high-dimensional natural images; discuss computational constraints and adaptations.
- Overall: solid contribution with recommendation to accept pending minor revisions addressing the technical clarifications and expanded distillation experiments.
