This paper builds on the deep generative modeling approach of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) and presents a conditional extension. The extension is obtained by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments that define the conditional distribution, enabling optimization with the same machinery used for the unconditional version. The proposed model achieves reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears technically sound and is clearly presented, though I did not verify every equation in full detail.

Regarding novelty, while a conditional extension to the moment-matching framework is a natural direction, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not obvious (at least to me). In this sense, the paper offers both novelty and practical value. The framework itself seems straightforward to apply—as illustrated in Algorithm 1—and should be useful to researchers interested in deep generative models or in distilling more complex statistical procedures. The paper is well written and easy to follow, and the appendix provides helpful, detailed information about the methods and experiments.
