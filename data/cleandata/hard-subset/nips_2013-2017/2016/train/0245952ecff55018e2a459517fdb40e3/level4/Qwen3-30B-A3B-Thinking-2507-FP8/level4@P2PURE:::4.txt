

This paper extends the deep generative modeling framework "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" to enable conditional generation. The extension is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing optimization via the same machinery as the unconditional variant. The proposed model demonstrates competitive performance across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from Bayesian models. The paper is technically sound and the proposals are well presented (though the reviewer did not verify all equations in full detail). Regarding novelty, while the conditional extension to the generative moment-matching framework is a natural progression, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not immediately apparent. Consequently, the paper exhibits meaningful novelty and usefulness. The derived framework is straightforward to implement, as evidenced in Algorithm 1, suggesting the work will be valuable to researchers employing deep models for generative tasks or distilling complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with the appendix providing useful supplementary details on methodological implementation and experimental setup.
