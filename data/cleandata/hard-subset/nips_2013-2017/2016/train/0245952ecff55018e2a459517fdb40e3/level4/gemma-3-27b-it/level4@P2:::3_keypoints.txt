- Proposes conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) that extend GMMN to model conditional distributions using conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD).  
- Builds on kernel mean embeddings to define a CMMD-based loss for directed generative models.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN across tasks: predictive modeling, contextual generation (digits and faces), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Authors resubmitted and addressed prior reviewer concerns about latent space dimensionality and model utility.  
- Well-motivated extension offering a simpler alternative to methods like GANs while leveraging deep learning.  
- Experimental validation is broad and shows solid results on MNIST and Yale Face datasets.  
- Application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is interesting and suggests practical utility beyond generation.  
- Clarification needed: mapping in Remark 1 from Equation (2) to a “weighted MMD form” is unclear.  
- Notation ambiguity: unclear whether dot denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product; explicit statement needed.  
- Notation conflict: reuse of ‘C’ for the conditional embedding operator and other contexts creates potential confusion.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a citation or provided proof; a reference or included proof is required for rigor.  
- Limited evaluation for distillation: only one dataset and model configuration were used, weakening generality claims.  
- Dataset scope is limited; evaluating on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) would better demonstrate scalability.  
- Strengths summarized: novelty, theoretical foundation, versatility across tasks, improved clarity from prior round, and competitive performance.  
- Weaknesses summarized: unclear Remark 1, missing citation for Theorem 3, limited distillation evaluation, limited dataset scope, and notation confusion.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, theoretically sound extension with competitive and versatile experimental results; prior concerns addressed.  
- Rejection arguments: mathematical clarity issues and missing citation reduce rigor; limited empirical scope and lack of complex-dataset evaluation limit broader impact.  
- Overall assessment: borderline accept/reject; recommend fixing mathematical/notation clarity, adding Theorem 3 reference or proof, and expanding empirical evaluation before final acceptance.
