This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion, which is an interesting extension for the sub-area of deep learning research focused on building generative models. The proposed technique, Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), is evaluated on standard benchmark tasks for discriminative and generative capabilities, demonstrating competitive performance.

The paper text is reasonably clear, although the math can be a bit difficult to follow at times. The authors provide a thorough explanation of the CGMMN framework, including the conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion, the deep generative architecture, and the learning algorithm. The experimental results are also well-presented, with a clear description of the tasks, datasets, and evaluation metrics used.

The tasks used in the experiments are somewhat small but not entirely unreasonable when compared to other recent work in this area. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation tasks, which is a good starting point for evaluating the proposed technique.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved. Firstly, the computational cost of training the model is not thoroughly discussed. As the algorithm involves some computationally expensive steps, it would be helpful to provide some comments in the experiment section on how long the model takes to train compared to existing approaches like Deeply-supervised nets (DSN). This would give readers a better understanding of the practicality of the proposed technique.

Secondly, the paper would be much stronger if reference code were made available for the experiments. This would allow practitioners to easily reproduce the exact models evaluated in the paper and explore the proposed technique further.

Lastly, while the authors provide a good overview of the related work, it would be helpful to have a more detailed comparison with existing generative models, such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs). This would help readers understand the strengths and weaknesses of CGMMN compared to other approaches.

Overall, this is a solid paper that presents an interesting extension to the field of deep generative models. With some additional details on computational cost and code availability, it has the potential to be even stronger.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel approach to building generative models based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion.
* The proposed technique is evaluated on a range of tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
* The authors provide a thorough explanation of the CGMMN framework and the experimental results.

Weaknesses:

* The computational cost of training the model is not thoroughly discussed.
* Reference code is not made available for the experiments.
* A more detailed comparison with existing generative models would be helpful.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and interesting approach to building generative models.
* The proposed technique is evaluated on a range of tasks, demonstrating its effectiveness.
* The authors provide a thorough explanation of the CGMMN framework and the experimental results.

Arguments for rejection:

* The computational cost of training the model is not thoroughly discussed.
* Reference code is not made available for the experiments.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with existing generative models.
