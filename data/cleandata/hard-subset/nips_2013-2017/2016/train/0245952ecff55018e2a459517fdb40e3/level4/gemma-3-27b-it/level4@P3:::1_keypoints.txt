- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional distributions via a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion.  
- Method enables predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clear, though mathematical notation is occasionally dense.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, based on kernel mean embeddings and Hilbert space properties; CMMD derivation is logical.  
- Novelty: valuable extension of MMD-based generative modeling to the conditional setting, alternative to conditional GANs/VAEs.  
- Demonstrates versatility through multiple application scenarios.  
- Experimental results on MNIST, SVHN, and Boston housing are competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Presentation and organization are clear, making core ideas accessible.  
- Lacks detailed discussion of computational complexity, particularly CMMD estimation and kernel matrix inversion.  
- No reported training times or computational comparison to baselines.  
- Experimental scope is limited; more datasets and broader model comparisons are needed.  
- Reproducibility is hindered by absence of publicly available code.  
- No analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., kernel bandwidth, regularization λ).  
- Insufficient discussion of connections to other conditional density estimation methods (normalizing flows, autoregressive models).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, promising empirical results, addresses important problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing computational analysis, reproducibility concerns, limited experiments, and lack of hyperparameter analysis.  
- Overall: solid contribution with limited impact unless computational details, code release, and broader evaluation are added.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Requested revisions: include computational complexity/training times, release code, expand experiments/comparisons, and analyze hyperparameter sensitivity.
