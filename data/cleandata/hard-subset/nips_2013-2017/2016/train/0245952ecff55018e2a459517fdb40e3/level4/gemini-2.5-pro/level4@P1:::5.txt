This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) designed to model conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea is to define a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective based on the Hilbert space embedding of conditional distributions, a technique originally proposed by Song et al. (2009). The proposed CGMMN is trained using back-propagation and evaluated empirically on several tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

The proposed approach is novel and elegant. However, there are several points that require clarification and the experimental results are not fully convincing.

**Weaknesses and Questions for the Authors:**

1.  **Clarity on the CMMD Estimator:** My main concern relates to the definition of the empirical conditional embedding operator, `Ĉ_{Y|X}`. According to the theory of kernel embeddings, the cross-covariance operator `C_{XY}` is defined as `E[φ(X)⊗φ(Y)] - μ_X ⊗ μ_Y`. The formulation in the paper appears to omit the mean terms `μ_X` and `μ_Y` (or their empirical estimators). This omission implies an assumption that the data is centered in the RKHS (i.e., `μ_X = 0` and `μ_Y = 0`), which is a strong assumption that is unlikely to hold in practice. Since `Ĉ_{Y|X}` is central to the proposed CMMD objective, it is crucial for the authors to clarify this point. How are these mean terms handled in the gradient computation and in the experiments?

2.  **Predictive Performance:** The experimental results for prediction are somewhat disappointing. On both the MNIST and SVHN datasets, CGMMN reports higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA model. This suggests that, for discriminative tasks, the proposed method is not yet competitive with leading deep generative models.

3.  **Generative Performance:** For the generative tasks, the evaluation is limited because no comparisons are provided against other conditional generative methods (e.g., Conditional Generative Adversarial Nets). Without a direct qualitative or quantitative comparison, it is unclear whether the proposed algorithm generates better or more diverse samples than existing approaches.

**Minor Issues:**

*   Line 65: "an universal" should be "a universal."
