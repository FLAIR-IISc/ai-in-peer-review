This paper proposes the Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Network (CGMMN), an extension of the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework. The key contribution is to adapt the moment-matching objective from joint distributions to conditional distributions. To achieve this, the authors leverage the technique of embedding conditional distributions into a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS), as proposed by Song et al. (2009), to define a distance metric between the model's conditional distribution and the true data's conditional distribution. The proposed algorithm is evaluated empirically on several real-world datasets.

The core idea of applying moment matching to conditional generation is novel and elegant, offering a potentially stable, non-adversarial alternative for this important task. However, the initial submission raised some questions regarding technical clarity and experimental evaluation.

**Weaknesses and Questions for the Authors:**

1.  **Clarity on the Conditional Operator Formulation:** My primary concern revolves around the definition of the empirical conditional embedding operator, $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$, presented in Line 90. Specifically, the definition appears to omit the terms related to the mean embeddings, $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (or their empirical estimators). It is unconventional to assume that the data is pre-centered in the RKHS, which would imply that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero. Since this operator is central to the proposed CGMMN objective, its precise formulation is critical for the correctness and reproducibility of the method. Could the authors please clarify the justification for omitting these terms? If they are not omitted in practice, how are they incorporated into the gradient computations and the experiments?

2.  **Underwhelming Experimental Results:** While the proposed approach is theoretically interesting, the experimental results presented are somewhat disappointing.
    *   **Predictive Performance:** In the predictive tasks on both datasets, the proposed CGMMN exhibits higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA baseline. This suggests that, at least for these tasks, the new method is not yet competitive with existing approaches.
    *   **Generative Performance:** The evaluation of the model's generative capabilities is incomplete. The paper lacks a comparison against other prominent conditional generative models, such as conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs) or conditional Variational Autoencoders (cVAEs). Without these baselines, it is difficult to assess the quality of the samples generated by CGMMN or to understand its relative strengths and weaknesses compared to other established techniques.

**Minor Issues:**

The paper contains a few typos that should be corrected:
*   Line 48: "Frobeniu norm" should be "Frobenius norm".
*   Line 65: "an universal" should be "a universal".
*   Line 238: "which the" is grammatically incorrect.

**Update After Author Rebuttal**

I thank the authors for their detailed and insightful rebuttal. They have satisfactorily addressed all of the concerns raised in my initial review. In particular, their clarification regarding the formulation of the conditional embedding operator and its practical implementation has resolved my primary concern about the method's correctness. The additional context provided for the experimental results was also helpful. Given these clarifications, I have re-evaluated the paper and have raised my scores. I believe the work is a valuable contribution to the literature on generative models.
