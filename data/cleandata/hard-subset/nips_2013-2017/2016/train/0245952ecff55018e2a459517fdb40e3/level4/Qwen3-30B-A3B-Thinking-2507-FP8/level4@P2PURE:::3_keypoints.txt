- Introduces a conditional MMD-based loss for directed generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings, extending GMMNs.
- Demonstrates efficacy on classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.
- Second review notes previous minor concerns (latent-space dimensionality, scalability, comparative utility, clarifications) have been addressed.
- Remark 1 is unclear: the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD formulation is not evident.
- Ambiguity in the dot notation—unclear if it denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product—needs clarification.
- Recommend elaborating the derivation step to make the mapping transparent.
- Choice of symbol C conflicts with notation for the conditional embedding and is problematic.
- Theorems 1 and 2 properly cite prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference or provided proof.
- Empirical results are adequate overall.
- Supervised learning applications are less compelling but acceptable as a demonstration of model versatility.
- Knowledge distillation result is interesting but limited to one dataset and model, so broader applicability is unclear.
- The method is most promising for pure generative modeling; digit/face experiments match original GMMN benchmarks.
- Wider adoption would require stronger results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
