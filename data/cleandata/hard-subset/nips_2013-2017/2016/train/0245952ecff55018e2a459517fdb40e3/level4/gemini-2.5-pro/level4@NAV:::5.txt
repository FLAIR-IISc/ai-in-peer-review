This work proposes an extension to generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) that shifts the focus from matching joint distributions to matching conditional distributions. The central idea is to leverage the embedding of conditional distributions into a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, a method introduced by Song et al. (2009). The resulting algorithm, CGMMN, is empirically validated on several real-world datasets.

A key concern lies in the formulation of the empirical conditional covariance operator, $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90). Specifically, the definition appears to omit the terms corresponding to the mean embeddings, $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (or their empirical estimates). This formulation implies an assumption that the data is pre-centered in the RKHS, which is not standard practice. Given that this operator is foundational to the proposed CGMMN algorithm, it is crucial for the authors to clarify this omission and discuss its practical implications for gradient computation and the experimental setup.

While the proposed methodology is elegant and novel, its empirical validation is not fully convincing. Regarding predictive tasks, the reported error rates for CGMMN are higher than those of the state-of-the-art CMMVA on the tested datasets. For the generative aspect, the evaluation is incomplete. The absence of a comparative analysis against other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional generative adversarial nets) makes it difficult to assess the quality of the samples generated by CGMMN relative to existing methods.

The manuscript would also benefit from proofreading to correct minor typographical errors, such as "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
