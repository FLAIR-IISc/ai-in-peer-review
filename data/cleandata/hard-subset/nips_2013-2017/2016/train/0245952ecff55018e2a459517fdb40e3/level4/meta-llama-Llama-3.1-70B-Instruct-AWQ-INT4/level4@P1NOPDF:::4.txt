This paper builds upon the deep generative modeling approach "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" and derives an extension that enables conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing them to be optimized using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears to be technically sound, and the proposals are well presented. While I did not verify all the equations in full detail, the overall technical quality of the paper is convincing. In terms of novelty, extending the generative moment matching framework to conditional generation is a natural idea, but the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious. At least to this reviewer, the paper's contribution is not trivial, and it seems to offer both novelty and usefulness. The derived framework is straightforward to apply, as illustrated in Algorithm 1, making it likely to be useful and relevant to researchers interested in using deep models for generative purposes or to distill more elaborate statistical processes. The paper is well written, clear, and easy to read, and the provided appendix usefully supplements the main text with more detailed information about the methods and experiments.
