The paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting by matching conditional rather than joint distributions. The key idea is to represent conditional distributions via conditional mean embeddings in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), following Song et al. (2009). The resulting algorithm, CGMMN, is evaluated on several real datasets.

A major technical clarification is needed: in the definition of the empirical conditional operator C_{Y|X} (Line 90), the terms involving the mean embeddings μ_X and μ_Y (and their estimators) are missing. It is also unusual to assume μ_X = 0 and μ_Y = 0, i.e., that the data are already centered in the RKHS. Because the empirical C_{Y|X} is central to CGMMN, please clarify this omission and explain how these terms are handled both in the gradient computations and in the experiments.

While the proposed approach is new and elegant, the empirical results are somewhat underwhelming. For predictive performance, on both datasets CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA. For generative performance, there is no comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), making it unclear whether CGMMN produces higher-quality samples.

Minor issues: several typos, e.g., “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).
