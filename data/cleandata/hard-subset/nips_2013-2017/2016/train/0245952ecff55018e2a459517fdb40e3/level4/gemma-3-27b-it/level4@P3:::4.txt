This paper extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to the conditional generation setting, introducing Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN). The authors derive a kernel embedding for the conditional distribution, enabling optimization using a similar backpropagation-based approach as the original GMMN. They demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, achieving competitive performance in all tasks. 

The paper builds on recent advances in deep generative modeling, notably GMMN [19] and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) [5]. Unlike GANs, which rely on a challenging min-max optimization, GMMN utilizes a moment-matching criterion (Maximum Mean Discrepancy - MMD [7]) for training. This work extends this approach to the conditional setting, addressing a common need in many applications where modeling p(y|x) is more direct and efficient than p(x,y) [23, 16]. Recent work has explored conditional GANs [21], and sum-product networks [4], but this paper presents a distinct approach based on extending the MMD framework.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The paper appears technically sound, with a clear derivation of the Conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion and a practical algorithm (Algorithm 1) for training. The use of kernel methods allows for flexible distribution modeling without strong parametric assumptions.
* **Novelty:** Extending GMMN to the conditional setting is a natural but non-trivial step. The derivation of the CMMD estimator, and its efficient implementation via backpropagation, represent a valuable contribution.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly organized. The explanations of the underlying concepts (Hilbert space embedding, MMD) are concise and accessible. The inclusion of an appendix with additional details is helpful.
* **Empirical Validation:** The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN on a diverse set of tasks, demonstrating its applicability to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and knowledge distillation. The competitive performance against established baselines strengthens the findings.
* **Practicality:** The proposed method is presented as readily implementable, leveraging standard deep learning tools and techniques, as illustrated by Algorithm 1.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Theoretical Assumptions:** The paper acknowledges the assumptions required for the CMMD objective to be well-defined (smoothness, Hilbert-Schmidt properties [8]), but a more thorough discussion of the practical implications of these assumptions would be beneficial. Specifically, how robust is the method to violations of these assumptions in real-world datasets?
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The impact of hyperparameters (e.g., kernel bandwidth λ) on performance is not extensively explored. A sensitivity analysis could provide insights into the robustness of the method and guide practitioners in choosing appropriate parameters.
* **Limited Comparison:** While the results are competitive, a comparison to more recent and advanced conditional generative models (e.g., those based on normalizing flows) would provide a more comprehensive assessment of the proposed method’s strengths and weaknesses.
* **Experiment Depth:** While the experiments cover a range of tasks, the depth of analysis within each task could be increased. For example, in the Bayesian dark knowledge distillation experiment, a more detailed comparison of the distilled model's uncertainty estimates to those of the original Bayesian model would be valuable.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of GMMN to the conditional setting.
* The proposed CMMD criterion and training algorithm are well-motivated and efficiently implemented.
* The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on a variety of tasks.
* The paper is clearly written and easy to understand, making it accessible to a broad audience.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

* The theoretical assumptions underlying the method could be more thoroughly discussed.
* The sensitivity of the method to hyperparameter choices is not fully explored.
* The comparison to state-of-the-art conditional generative models could be more comprehensive.

**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN framework offers a promising approach to conditional generation, building upon the strengths of GMMN while addressing a practical need in many applications. While some minor weaknesses exist, the strengths of the paper outweigh them. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised above, specifically regarding a more thorough discussion of the theoretical assumptions and a more comprehensive comparison to recent advances in conditional generative modeling.
