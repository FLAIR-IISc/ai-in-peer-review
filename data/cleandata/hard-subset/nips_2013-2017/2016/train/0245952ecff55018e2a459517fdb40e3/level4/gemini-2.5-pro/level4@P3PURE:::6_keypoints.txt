- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) using kernel embeddings and conditional MMD (CMMD) for conditional generation and prediction.  
- Motivates CGMMN by pointing out the data-sparsity failure of a naive per-condition GMMN approach, especially with continuous conditioning variables.  
- Treats all conditional distributions as a unified family by matching the conditional embedding operator, addressing data sparsity elegantly.  
- CMMD objective has a clean formulation and can be computed almost as easily as unconditional MMD.  
- Kernel matrices in CMMD are independent of model parameters, allowing straightforward backpropagation similar to the unconditional case.  
- Empirical evaluation on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation shows promising results.  
- Missing baseline: no direct empirical comparison to the naive per-condition GMMN, which could be competitive for small discrete condition sets (e.g., MNIST classes).  
- Computational scalability concern: reliable stochastic CMMD estimates may require large minibatches, increasing cost (especially matrix inversions) and sensitivity to batch size.  
- Presentation clarity: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and need more self-contained explanation.  
- Technical correction 1: statement that the common empirical MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect and should be clarified.  
- Technical correction 2: the paper’s definition of the conditional embedding operator appears inconsistent with reference [29]; clarify the cross-covariance operator assumptions.
