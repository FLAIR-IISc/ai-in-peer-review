- Proposes a novel loss for directed conditional generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings and an MMD-based criterion.  
- Demonstrates the method on classification, digit and face generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Authors addressed earlier minor concerns about low-dimensional embedding choice, scalability to higher-dimensional latents, and model utility.  
- Clarifying issue: unclear mapping of Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form in Remark 1.  
- Ambiguity in dot notation—need to specify whether it denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product.  
- Recommend clearer mapping details to improve paper readability.  
- Notation concern: using C as a constant clashes with its use in defining the conditional embedding—suggest a different symbol.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 are properly referenced; Theorem 3 lacks a proof or citation and needs attribution or a detailed proof.  
- Empirical results are decent and previous supervised-learning concerns have been addressed.  
- Distilled Bayesian inference application is intriguing but limited to one dataset and one model, raising generalizability questions.  
- Method seems better suited to pure generative modeling; experiments are comparable to the original GMMN paper.  
- Suggest stronger, more compelling results on challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) to encourage broader adoption.
