- Paper extends GMMNs to match conditional distributions using RKHS embeddings (building on Song et al. 2009) and proposes the CGMMN algorithm.
- CGMMN is empirically evaluated on several real datasets.
- Definition of the conditional operator estimator \hat{C}_{Y|X} appears to omit terms involving the RKHS means \mu_X and \mu_Y.
- The authors assume \mu_X and \mu_Y are zero (data centered in RKHS), which is unconventional and needs justification.
- Because \hat{C}_{Y|X} is central to CGMMN, the paper should clarify the omission and explain how this is handled in gradient computation and experiments.
- The approach is novel and well-motivated.
- Experimental results are underwhelming: CGMMN has higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets.
- Generative evaluation lacks comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), making sample quality assessment inconclusive.
- The manuscript contains several typographical errors (e.g., “Frobeniu norm”, “an universal”, “which the”).
- After the author rebuttal, the reviewer reports all concerns were addressed and will revise scores accordingly.
