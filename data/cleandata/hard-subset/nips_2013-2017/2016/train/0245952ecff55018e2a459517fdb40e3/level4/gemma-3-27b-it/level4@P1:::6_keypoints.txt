- Proposes CGMMNs: an extension of GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.  
- Evaluates CGMMN on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models with promising results.  
- Addresses data sparsity of naive per-condition GMMNs by matching a conditional embedding operator across the family of conditionals.  
- Notes that naive per-condition GMMNs may still work well when the conditioning variable has few discrete values (e.g., MNIST), so comparison to that baseline is missing.  
- CMMD objective is well-defined, computable similarly to unconditional MMD, and kernel matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, enabling backpropagation.  
- Raises concern that CMMD may need large mini-batches to reduce stochastic error, making computation (especially matrix inversions) expensive.  
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and require consulting cited papers for clarity.  
- Minor technical inaccuracies: the paper’s claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect.  
- Minor technical inconsistency: the conditional embedding operator formulation (line 90) appears inconsistent with reference [29] and may require a different cross-covariance operator definition.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension to conditional generative modeling, solid kernel-based theoretical foundation, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: specific clarity issues in key sections, the noted technical inaccuracies, missing naive-baseline comparisons, and scalability/computational cost concerns.
