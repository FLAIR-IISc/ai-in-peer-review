This paper extends the deep generative modeling framework “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” by introducing a conditional variant. (This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments characterizing the conditional distribution, enabling optimization with the same machinery as in the unconditional case.) The model is evaluated with reasonably competitive results on prediction tasks, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model. The work appears technically sound and the ideas are clearly presented. (However, I did not verify every equation in full detail.) Regarding novelty, although a conditional extension to the generative moment-matching framework is a natural direction, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not obvious (at least to me). Thus, the paper offers both novelty and practical value. The resulting framework seems straightforward to apply, as illustrated in Algorithm 1, and should be useful and relevant to researchers interested in deep generative modeling or in distilling more complex statistical procedures. The paper is well written, clear, and easy to follow, and the appendix usefully supplies more comprehensive details on the methods and experiments.
