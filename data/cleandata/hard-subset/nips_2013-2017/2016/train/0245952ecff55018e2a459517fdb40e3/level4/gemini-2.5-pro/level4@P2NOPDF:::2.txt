This paper extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting. The authors correctly identify that this extension is non-trivial. In many models where updates are performed on single examples, conditioning is as simple as feeding the conditional variable into the network. However, in the moment matching framework, the objective involves matching distributions over entire minibatches. When the conditioning variables differ across the minibatch, the target and generated distributions are no longer simple distributions but mixtures, which complicates the matching process.

The primary weakness of the paper lies in its exposition. The presentation jumps directly into a formal mathematical framework without first providing the reader with an intuition for the proposed objective function (Equation 2). The original moment matching network paper succeeded in part because it offered a clear, intuitive justification for its objective—minimizing the distance between the means of samples in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS). This made the method accessible even without a deep understanding of the underlying mathematical machinery. This paper would be significantly strengthened by a similar intuitive explanation for the conditional case. The reader is left wondering why Equation (2) is the natural or correct way to formulate the conditional moment matching problem.

A second, related issue is the lack of clarity regarding the computational aspects of the algorithm. The paper does not adequately explain the concrete steps required to implement and optimize the objective in Equation (2). Important details are either omitted or relegated to a supplementary material that is difficult to follow. For the reader to properly evaluate the proposed method, the main text should clearly describe the actual computations performed during a training step. This includes any assumptions or approximations made to render the objective tractable and an analysis of the algorithm's computational efficiency.

To improve the paper, I would strongly recommend that the authors revise the manuscript to address the following questions:

1.  How can the objective function in Equation (2) be derived or justified from an intuitive standpoint? Instead of starting with the formal RKHS definition, it would be beneficial to first explain the high-level goal (matching conditional distributions for varied inputs in a minibatch) and then build up to Equation (2) as a sensible solution.

2.  What are the precise steps to get from the theoretical objective in Equation (2) to a practical, trainable algorithm? A clear, step-by-step description of the computations in a single training update would greatly improve the paper's clarity and reproducibility. This should connect the mathematical theory to the final implementation.
