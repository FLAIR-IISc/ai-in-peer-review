The authors propose an extension of generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by focusing on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. This approach leverages the technique of embedding conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, as introduced by Song et al. in 2009. The empirical evaluation of the proposed algorithm, Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), is conducted on several real-world datasets.

However, upon reviewing the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), it appears that the term related to $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (or more specifically, their estimators) is missing. Furthermore, it is unusual to assume that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero, implying that the data is already centered in the RKHS. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is a crucial component of CGMMN, it is essential for the authors to provide clarification on this missing term, as well as how it is handled in gradient computation and real-world experiments.

While the proposed approach is novel and well-structured, the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. In terms of predictive performance, CGMMN yields larger error rates compared to the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMVA on both datasets. Moreover, since no comparison is made between CGMMN and other conditional generative methods (such as conditional generative adversarial networks), it is unclear whether the proposed algorithm can generate superior samples. Additionally, the paper contains several typos, including "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).

Update after author rebuttal: The authors have addressed all concerns raised in this review. As a result, I will revise my scores to reflect the improved clarity and thoroughness of the manuscript.
