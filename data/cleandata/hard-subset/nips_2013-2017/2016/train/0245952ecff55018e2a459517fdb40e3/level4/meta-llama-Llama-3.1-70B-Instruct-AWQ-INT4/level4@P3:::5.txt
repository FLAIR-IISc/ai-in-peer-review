The authors propose Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) to model conditional distributions. This is achieved by leveraging the technique of embedding conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), which was introduced by Song et al. in 2009. The key contribution of the paper is the development of a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, which measures the difference between the kernel mean embeddings of two conditional distributions.

The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the theoretical foundations of their approach, including the concepts of RKHS, kernel mean embedding, and maximum mean discrepancy (MMD). They also present a detailed description of the CGMMN architecture and the learning algorithm used to optimize the CMMD objective.

The experimental evaluation of CGMMN is conducted on several real-world datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face. The results demonstrate the competitiveness of CGMMN in various tasks, such as predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

Strengths of the paper:

1. The authors provide a novel and elegant solution to the problem of modeling conditional distributions using GMMNs.
2. The theoretical foundations of the approach are well-explained, making it easy to follow and understand.
3. The experimental evaluation is thorough and demonstrates the competitiveness of CGMMN in various tasks.

Weaknesses of the paper:

1. The experimental results could be improved by providing more comparisons with other state-of-the-art methods, especially in terms of generative performance.
2. There are some typos and minor errors in the paper, which should be corrected in the final version.

Originality and significance:

The paper presents a novel and significant contribution to the field of deep generative models. The idea of extending GMMNs to model conditional distributions is original and has the potential to impact various applications in machine learning and computer vision.
