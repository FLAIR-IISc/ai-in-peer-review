This paper presents a conditional extension to the "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" framework. This is accomplished by deriving a kernel embedding for the moments that define the conditional distribution, enabling optimization with the same machinery as the unconditional version. The authors demonstrate the model's effectiveness on tasks including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of a Bayesian model's predictions, where it achieves competitive performance.

The paper's technical contributions appear solid, and the proposed methods are articulated clearly, although a line-by-line verification of all equations was not conducted. Regarding novelty, while extending the generative moment-matching framework to the conditional setting is an intuitive direction, the specific mechanism and the derivation of the empirical estimator are non-trivial. Consequently, the paper possesses both novelty and practical value.

The derived framework is straightforward to apply, as illustrated in Algorithm 1. This suggests the work will be highly relevant and useful for researchers and practitioners interested in using deep models for generative tasks or for distilling more elaborate statistical processes. The manuscript is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, and the appendix usefully provides comprehensive details regarding the methodology and experimental setup.
