- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to conditional generation via a kernel embedding of conditional moments and an empirical estimator (CGMMN).
- Enables optimization using the same machinery as unconditional GMMN and is summarized in Algorithm 1.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of Bayesian model predictions.
- Technically sound and clearly presented; derivations appear logically consistent and well-explained.
- Novel contribution: non-trivial derivation of the conditional moment-matching estimator—conditioning is natural but the estimator is not obvious.
- Readily applicable and accessible to researchers, with a detailed appendix improving reproducibility.
- Strength: clear extension of prior work and good connection to the research landscape.
- Strength: practical applicability and comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple tasks.
- Strength: excellent writing, organization, and supplementary material.
- Weakness: empirical comparisons lack thorough benchmarking against state-of-the-art conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs, VAEs); baseline justification could be expanded.
- Weakness: missing discussion of limitations such as sensitivity to kernel choice and computational costs in high-dimensional settings.
- Overall assessment: strong paper, recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address empirical comparisons and limitations.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
