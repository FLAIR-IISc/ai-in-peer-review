- Proposes CGMMN: a neural conditional generative model optimized with a conditional maximum mean discrepancy objective.
- Evaluated on standard small benchmarks for both discriminative and generative tasks, showing promising performance.
- Central idea is conceptually clear and aligns with current deep generative modeling research.
- Paper is reasonably well written; main ideas are understandable despite some challenging math.
- Experiments are limited to relatively small datasets, reducing confidence in scalability.
- Training appears to involve computationally expensive steps, but runtime and resource usage are not reported.
- Reproducibility is a concern due to sparse implementation details (architectures, hyperparameters, training schedules).
- Reviewer requests explicit training time comparisons to baselines (e.g., DSN) under comparable hardware/settings.
- Reviewer requests detailed architectural and training hyperparameters to facilitate replication.
- Reviewer asks whether the authors will release reference code for the experiments.
- Quality: technically sound and empirically supported on small tasks, but practicality is unclear without cost analysis.
- Clarity: generally organized and clear, but more implementation detail (and code) needed for reproducibility.
- Originality: interesting/meaningful extension of moment-matching to conditional generative models.
- Significance: promising for conditional generative modeling, but would be stronger with scalability, efficiency reporting, and released code.
- Pros: conceptually appealing, demonstrates discriminative and generative capabilities, clear presentation of main ideas.
- Cons: limited empirical scale, missing training-time/resource comparisons, reproducibility issues.
- Recommendation: Borderline — accept if authors report training resources/times, provide implementation details, and release code.
