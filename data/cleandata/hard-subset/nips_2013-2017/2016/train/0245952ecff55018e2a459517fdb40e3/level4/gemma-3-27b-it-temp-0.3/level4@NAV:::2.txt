The authors present a conditional extension of moment matching networks, a task that is considerably more complex than simply conditioning standard single-example update models. While extending to a conditional setting is straightforward in cases updating based on individual samples, this work addresses the non-trivial challenge of matching distributions across a mini-batch of samples and inputs, where these distributions diverge unless inefficiently conditioned identically. 

A key weakness of the paper lies in its presentation. The authors prioritize formal mathematical derivation over intuitive explanation. Specifically, the core optimization target (eq. (2)) lacks sufficient explanation regarding the underlying computations, efficiency, and necessary assumptions. This makes it difficult to understand *how* the algorithm functions in practice. The original moment matching approach benefitted from an intuitive explanation, avoiding complex mathematical formalism; a similar approach would greatly benefit the clarity of this conditional extension. 

To improve the paper, the authors should address the following: Provide an intuitive rationale for the formulation of equation (2). Detail the practical steps involved in translating equation (2) into a concrete algorithm, clarifying the computational process. While some details are present in the supplementary material, they are difficult to follow.
