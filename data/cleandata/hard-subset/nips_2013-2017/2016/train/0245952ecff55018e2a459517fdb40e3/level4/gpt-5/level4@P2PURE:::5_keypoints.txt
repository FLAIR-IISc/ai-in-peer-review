- Paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to model conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in an RKHS (following Song et al., 2009) and evaluates the proposed CGMMN on real datasets.
- Key notation/estimator issue: the empirical conditional operator \hat{C}_{Y|X} omits terms involving empirical means μ_X and μ_Y, which must be clarified.
- The manuscript atypically assumes μ_X = μ_Y = 0 (data centered in RKHS); the authors should explain/justify this and how centering is handled in gradient computation and experiments.
- Novel and elegant approach, but empirical results are disappointing: predictive performance of CGMMN has higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA.
- Generative evaluation is incomplete: no comparisons to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), so sample quality relative to alternatives is unclear.
- Manuscript contains several typos (e.g., “Frobeniu norm,” “an universal,” “which the”) that should be corrected.
- After the author rebuttal, the reviewer states all concerns were addressed and will revise their scores accordingly.
