The authors propose an extension to the generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by focusing on matching conditional distributions instead of joint distributions. This approach is based on embedding the conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), a technique introduced by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm, Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), is evaluated empirically on several real-world datasets.

However, upon reviewing the manuscript, I noticed an omission in the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90). Specifically, the term related to $\muX$ and $\muY$ (or more precisely, their estimators) is missing. Furthermore, it is unusual to assume that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero, implying that the data is already centered in the RKHS. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is a crucial component of CGMMN, I believe it is essential for the authors to provide clarification on this missing term, as well as how to handle it in gradient computation and real-world experiments.

The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated, but the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. In terms of predictive performance, CGMMN yields larger error rates than the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMVA on both datasets. Moreover, the generative performance of CGMMN is unclear, as no comparison with other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional generative adversarial networks) is provided. Therefore, it is uncertain whether the proposed algorithm can generate better samples.

Additionally, I noticed a few typos in the manuscript, including "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
