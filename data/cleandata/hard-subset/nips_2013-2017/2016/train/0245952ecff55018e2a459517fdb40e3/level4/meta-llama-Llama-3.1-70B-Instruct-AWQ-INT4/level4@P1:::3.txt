This paper proposes a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables, which is an extension of generative moment matching networks. The method leverages the work on conditional kernel mean embeddings to produce the loss function. The results are demonstrated on a variety of generative tasks, including classification, generating handwritten digits and faces, and distilling knowledge in Bayesian models.

The paper provides a good overview of the related work and clearly explains the proposed approach. The authors address the issues raised in the previous review, such as the choice of low-dimensional space and the comparison with other models. However, there are a few minor issues that need to be clarified.

Firstly, in Remark 1, the authors mention that the objective function of CMMD can be expressed as a weighted MMD form. However, the notation used is unclear, and it would be helpful to provide more details on how to map from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form. Specifically, it would be useful to clarify whether the dot notation represents matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product.

Secondly, the choice of notation for the conditional embedding operator is a bit unfortunate, as it uses the same symbol "C" as the conditional embedding operator. It would be better to use a different symbol to avoid confusion.

Thirdly, Theorem 3 is not referenced to any paper, and it would be helpful to provide a reference to a paper containing a proof or provide the proof itself.

The empirical results are decent, and the authors provide a good explanation of the results. However, as pointed out in the previous review, using this model for supervised learning may not be the best application, and the authors acknowledge this. The distilled Bayesian inference application is interesting, but it would be useful to see more results on different datasets and models to demonstrate its effectiveness.

In terms of the quality of the paper, it is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed approach and the related work. The paper is well-organized, and the figures and tables are helpful in understanding the results.

Overall, I think the paper is a good contribution to the field of generative models, and it provides a useful extension to the generative moment matching networks. However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed, and it would be useful to see more results on different datasets and models to demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.
