- Proposes a novel loss for directed conditional generative models based on MMD and conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Authors addressed prior minor concerns about low-dimensional choices, scalability to higher-dimensional latent spaces, and model utility versus alternatives.  
- Clarification needed on Remark 1: mapping from Eq. (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear and notation is ambiguous (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product).  
- The symbol "C" is potentially confusing because it’s also used to denote the conditional embedding—needs disambiguation.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 are cited from other works, but Theorem 3 lacks citation or proof and should be provided.  
- Empirical results are satisfactory, though supervised learning may not be the strongest application of this method.  
- Application to distilled Bayesian inference is intriguing but limited—only demonstrated on a single dataset and model.  
- Method appears most promising for pure generative modeling; experiments on digits and faces adequately demonstrate feasibility.  
- For wider adoption, the approach needs compelling results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
