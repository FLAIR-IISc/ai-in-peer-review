- Main contribution: extends GMMNs to conditional generation via a kernel embedding of conditional moments that integrates with original GMMN training.  
- Technical novelty: non-trivial derivation of conditional moment embeddings enabling an empirically estimable objective without ad hoc modifications.  
- Empirical performance: competitive or near–state-of-the-art results on predictive modeling (MNIST, CIFAR-10), contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Robustness and ablations: ablation studies validate design choices (kernel selection, moment order) and support practical utility.  
- Clarity and reproducibility: well-structured paper with clear exposition, Algorithm 1, and a detailed appendix including hyperparameters and experimental configs.  
- Theoretical soundness: derivations follow established kernel/moment-matching principles; supplementary material provides sufficient depth though not every equation was independently checked.  
- Novelty assessment: incremental but meaningful—avoids auxiliary networks/discriminator training by directly matching conditional moments using conditional kernel embeddings.  
- Practical relevance: simple, implementable framework applicable to tasks like image captioning, time-series forecasting, and model distillation.  
- Minor improvements suggested: expand discussion of computational scaling with conditional dimensionality, compare to recent conditional GAN variants (e.g., InfoGAN), and briefly state theoretical convergence guarantees.  
- Overall recommendation: technically solid, empirically validated, readable, and recommended for acceptance.
