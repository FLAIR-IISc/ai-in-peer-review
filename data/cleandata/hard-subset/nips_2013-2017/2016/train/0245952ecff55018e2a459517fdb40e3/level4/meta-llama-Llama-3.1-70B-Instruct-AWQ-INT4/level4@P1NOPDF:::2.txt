The authors extend moment matching networks to their conditional version, a non-trivial task. Unlike models that can be updated with a single example, where incorporating conditionals is straightforward, the extension proposed here is more complex. This complexity arises from the need to match distributions of a large number of samples (a mini-batch) to a large number of inputs, with these distributions no longer being the same unless the same conditional is used for a given update, which is inefficient.

The primary issue with the paper is its failure to provide an intuitive explanation behind the formula and its computational efficiency. Instead of starting with the mathematical formulation, it would be beneficial to first explain the intuition and then delve into the formal mathematics. The authors should highlight the actual computations performed during the algorithm and their efficiency. For instance, the non-conditional version of the algorithm can be explained without resorting to advanced mathematical concepts (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert space), and the formula being minimized is intuitive. A similar intuitive explanation for the conditional case would greatly enhance the paper's clarity.

To improve the paper, the authors should address the following questions: How can one intuitively derive formula (2)? What are the steps from formula (2) to the practical algorithm? While some of this information is provided in the supplement, it is difficult to follow and would benefit from a clearer explanation in the main text.
