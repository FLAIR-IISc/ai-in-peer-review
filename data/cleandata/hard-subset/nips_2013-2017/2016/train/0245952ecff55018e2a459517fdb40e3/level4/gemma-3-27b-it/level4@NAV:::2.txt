The authors present a conditional extension to moment matching networks, a challenging task given that extending single-example update models is straightforward, but matching distributions across a mini-batch of samples to varying inputs is not. The core difficulty lies in the differing distributions necessitating updates – using a uniform conditional input would negate this benefit and be computationally inefficient. 

However, the paper’s presentation is a significant weakness. It begins with complex mathematical formalism without first establishing the underlying intuition. Critically, the authors fail to clearly articulate the computations performed by the algorithm and their associated efficiency. Specifically, the optimized objective function (eq. (2)) lacks sufficient explanation regarding its derivation, underlying assumptions, and practical implementation. 

The original, non-conditional version of this algorithm benefits from an intuitive explanation (e.g., framed without recourse to reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces). A similar intuitive understanding is missing for the conditional extension. To improve clarity, the authors should address: (1) What is the intuitive motivation behind formulating equation (2)? and (2) What are the concrete steps linking equation (2) to the actual algorithm used in practice? While some details exist in the supplementary material, they are difficult to follow.
