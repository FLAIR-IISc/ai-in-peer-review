- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD).
- Maintains the simplicity of MMD-based training while enabling conditional generation, avoiding GAN-style min-max training.
- Provides a solid theoretical foundation: develops conditional kernel mean embeddings and proves that matching conditional embedding operators implies identical conditional distributions (Theorem 3).
- Simple generative architecture: network takes conditional inputs plus noise and is trained with SGD/backprop, compatible with deep learning frameworks.
- Empirical evaluation spans predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation (Boston housing).
- Results are competitive with state-of-the-art; CGMMN-CNN achieves strong SVHN performance (3.13% error vs CMMVA 3.09%).
- Clarity is generally good with clear math and organization, but Sections 2.2–2.3 are technical and could be more accessible to readers unfamiliar with kernel embeddings.
- Practical connection between theory and implementation needs clearer explanation, especially how CMMD is computed in practice.
- Original and significant: offers a non-adversarial alternative to conditional GANs and appropriately situates itself relative to prior work (GMMN, MMD, cGANs).
- Demonstrates useful and diverse applications, with Bayesian dark knowledge distillation highlighted as a novel use-case.
- Lacks detailed computational comparisons and explicit training-time benchmarks versus strong baselines like DSN to substantiate claims of simplicity.
- Experiments use relatively small architectures compared to recent SOTA; evaluation with larger models would strengthen impact claims.
- Main weakness: limited discussion and quantification of computational complexity (Gram matrix cubic growth) and unclear scalability to large datasets.
- Overall assessment: well-executed, theoretically solid, and meaningful extension of GMMN with minor practical/scale-related concerns.
