This paper extends the "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" (GMMN) deep generative modeling framework to enable conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding for the moments that define the conditional distribution, allowing them to be optimized using the same machinery as the original unconditional model.

The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on several tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling the predictions of a Bayesian model. The paper appears technically sound and the proposed methods are well-presented, although I have not verified all equations in full detail.

Regarding novelty, while the idea of a conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework is a natural one, the specific mechanism and the derivation of the empirical estimator are not obvious. The paper, therefore, demonstrates both novelty and utility. The derived framework is straightforward to apply, as illustrated in Algorithm 1. Consequently, this work will likely be useful and relevant to researchers interested in deep generative models or in methods for distilling complex statistical processes.

The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read. The appendix provides useful and comprehensive details regarding the methodology and experiments.
