- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to conditional generation and prediction.  
- Uses kernel embedding and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective to model conditional distributions.  
- CMMD objective is well-formulated and computationally similar to unconditional MMD.  
- Authors present a stochastic gradient descent algorithm to optimize the CMMD objective.  
- Experiments show competitive performance on classification (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian model distillation.  
- CGMMN generates diverse and realistic conditional samples and can effectively distill Bayesian predictive distributions.  
- Presentation of technical material in sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow and relies on referenced papers.  
- No comparison provided with a naive extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.  
- CMMD computation requires large minibatches, which can be computationally expensive.  
- The CMMD objective and use of kernel embedding are original contributions.  
- Proposed approach is significant for improving deep generative models in conditional tasks.  
- Paper is generally well-organized and experiments are clearly described.  
- Overall technically sound with theoretical and experimental support, but clarity and some baseline comparisons should be improved.
