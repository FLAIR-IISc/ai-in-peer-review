- Paper proposes a conditional extension of moment matching networks but the extension is substantially more complex than the single-example update case.
- Complexity arises because the algorithm must match distributions across many samples/mini-batches to many different conditionals, which differ unless inefficiently repeated per conditional.
- The manuscript starts with formal derivations instead of providing an intuitive explanation of the core formula and algorithm.
- The authors fail to clearly describe the computations performed, their computational cost, and the assumptions required for the method to work.
- Equation (2), which is central, is not intuitively derived or connected transparently to the practical algorithm.
- Much of the operational detail is buried in the supplementary material and is hard to follow.
- By contrast, the non-conditional original algorithm was explained intuitively and accessibly, and the conditional version needs similar treatment.
- Recommended fixes: provide an intuitive derivation of equation (2) and explicit, concrete steps translating it into the implementable algorithm to improve accessibility and impact.
