- Introduces CGMMNs and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) metric to learn conditional distributions by kernel mean embeddings, trained via backprop; evaluated on classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian distillation.  
- Novel and principled extension of MMD to the conditional setting with solid kernel-embedding foundations.  
- Training is more stable than GANs since CGMMN minimizes a single CMMD objective amenable to SGD.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability and strong empirical results (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Faces, Bayesian dark knowledge).  
- Provides clear intuition (Remark 1) relating CMMD to MMD via non-uniform weighting of Gram matrices.  
- Theoretical sections (kernel embedding and CMMD derivation) are dense and need more intuition and a self-contained presentation.  
- Computational cost/scalability concern: per-minibatch inversion of N×N kernel matrices is O(N^3); paper should discuss implications and approximation remedies (e.g., RFF, Nyström).  
- Missing baseline: training separate GMMNs per class (or shared-parameter variants) for MNIST conditional generation is not compared.  
- Minor clarification: statement that empirical squared MMD estimator is asymptotically unbiased conflicts with known V-statistic bias—specify estimator.  
- Minor clarification: empirical conditional embedding operator formulation differs from referenced definition—please justify or correct.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, well-executed and useful contribution with practical effectiveness, but would benefit from clearer theory exposition and scalability discussion.
