

The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to handle conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core methodology employs kernel mean embedding of conditional distributions, a technique originally proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) learn conditional distributions given input variables through a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, with training performed via stochastic gradient descent using back-propagation. The approach is empirically evaluated across multiple tasks including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

The paper presents a technically sound extension of GMMNs to conditional settings, with a well-structured methodology that builds upon established kernel methods. However, several critical concerns require attention. First, the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90) omits a crucial term related to $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (and their estimators), which is essential for the conditional embedding operator. Second, the assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero (implying data is centered in the RKHS) is unusual and inadequately justified. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is fundamental to CGMMN, these omissions necessitate clarification regarding both theoretical formulation and practical implementation, particularly for gradient computation. Third, the experimental results are disappointing: CGMMN achieves higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both MNIST and SVHN datasets for predictive modeling tasks. Fourth, for generative performance, the paper fails to compare CGMMN with established conditional generative methods such as conditional GANs, making it impossible to assess whether the proposed approach generates superior samples. Finally, the paper contains multiple typographical errors including "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
