- Introduces CGMMN: extends GMMN to conditional distributions via conditional MMD (CMMD) and conditional kernel mean embeddings for tasks like prediction, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation.  
- CMMD is formulated as the Hilbert–Schmidt norm between conditional embedding operators; kernel matrices are parameter-independent, enabling backprop optimization.  
- Treating conditional distributions as a family via CMMD mitigates data sparsity from per-condition GMMN training and connects to MMD through non-uniform kernel weighting (Remark 1).  
- Experiments show competitive classification (MNIST, SVHN), coherent conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and effective Bayesian model distillation (Boston housing).  
- Strengths: simple, broadly applicable, computationally feasible objective and comprehensive empirical evaluation.  
- Weakness: Exposition in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is unclear; needs concise high-level summaries and clearer operator definitions, and to resolve inconsistencies with reference [29].  
- Weakness: Incorrect statement that the MMD estimator is “unbiased”; it is asymptotically unbiased but biased for finite samples.  
- Weakness: Lacks comparison to the naive baseline of training separate GMMNs per discrete condition (e.g., per-label MNIST), which should be evaluated.  
- Weakness: Scalability concerns from required matrix inverses ((K+λI)^{-1}) for CMMD; paper should quantify minibatch vs. cost trade-offs and discuss approximations (e.g., Nyström).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, correct bias claim, and add baseline/computational discussion.
