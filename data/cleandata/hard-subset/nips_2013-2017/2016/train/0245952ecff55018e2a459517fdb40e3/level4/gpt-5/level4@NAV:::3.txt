This paper introduces a loss function for directed generative models with conditioning variables. The method extends generative moment matching networks by replacing the original unconditional maximum mean discrepancy loss with a conditional version derived from conditional kernel mean embeddings. The approach is evaluated across several generative tasks, including classification, synthesizing handwritten digits and faces, and distilling knowledge in Bayesian models.

This is my second review of this submission. The prior concerns were minor: the rationale for using a low-dimensional latent space and whether the method scales to higher-dimensional latents, the comparative utility of this model relative to alternatives, and various clarifications. These points now appear to be addressed explicitly in the text.

Regarding Remark 1, the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is not clear. What does the dot notation denote here—is it standard matrix multiplication or the Hadamard (elementwise) product? Please expand this derivation so the correspondence is unambiguous. Also, the choice of the symbol C is unfortunate given that C is already used for the conditional embedding.

Theorems 1 and 2 cite prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference. Please provide a citation to a source containing a proof, or include a proof in the paper.

The empirical results are solid. While earlier reviews noted that supervised learning may not be the most compelling application, the authors’ rationale—showcasing the model’s versatility—seems reasonable. The distilled Bayesian inference example is interesting but demonstrated on a single dataset with a single model, leaving its broader applicability uncertain. This approach likely has the most promise in purely generative settings. The experiments in that direction are on par with the original GMMN work. Using digits and faces suffices to show the idea functions as intended; however, broader adoption would likely require strong results on more challenging datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
