

The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional variant. Unlike models permitting single-example updates where the extension is straightforward (merely conditioning the network input), this adaptation is non-trivial. The challenge arises because the method requires matching the distributions of a large number of samples (mini-batch) to a corresponding set of conditional inputs, yet these distributions are inherently distinct across different conditions (using identical conditions for all updates during a single step would be computationally inefficient). The primary weakness of the paper lies in its overreliance on formal mathematical notation without first establishing intuitive motivation for the derived formula. Specifically, the authors fail to clarify: (1) the practical computational steps executed by the algorithm, (2) its efficiency characteristics, and (3) the underlying assumptions enabling its operation. This lack of clarity is particularly notable when contrasted with the non-conditional version, which is explained intuitively in the original work without recourse to advanced mathematical frameworks like reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces. The formula in Equation (2), central to the conditional approach, lacks an accessible intuitive derivation. The authors should address: How is Equation (2) intuitively motivated? What are the concrete steps translating Equation (2) into the practical algorithm? While some details appear in the supplement, they remain difficult to follow. A clear, intuitive explanation for the conditional case—comparable to the non-conditional version—would significantly strengthen the paper.
