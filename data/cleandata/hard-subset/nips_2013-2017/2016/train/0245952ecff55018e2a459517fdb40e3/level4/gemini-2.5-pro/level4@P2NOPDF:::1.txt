This paper introduces a neural network model based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion. The authors evaluate the proposed technique's discriminative and generative capabilities on standard, small-scale benchmark tasks.

Overall, this work is an interesting contribution to the subfield of deep generative models. The paper is generally well-written, although, like many publications in this area, the mathematical derivations can be dense and challenging to follow at times. The experiments are conducted on tasks that are somewhat small in scale; however, the chosen benchmarks are reasonable and in line with other recent work in this domain.

A few points could strengthen the paper:

1.  **Computational Cost:** The training algorithm appears to involve computationally expensive steps. To better assess the model's practicality, the experimental section should include an analysis of its training time. A direct comparison of training duration against existing approaches, such as DSN, would be particularly valuable.

2.  **Reproducibility:** The paper would be significantly stronger if the authors made the source code for their experiments available. Based on the details provided in the model section, a practitioner might have difficulty precisely reproducing the evaluated models and verifying the results.
