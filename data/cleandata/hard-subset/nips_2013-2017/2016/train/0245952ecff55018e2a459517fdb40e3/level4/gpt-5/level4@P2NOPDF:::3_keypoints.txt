- Introduces a conditional MMD loss for directed generative models via conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Evaluated on supervised classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian posterior distillation.  
- Previously raised minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space, scalability, comparative utility, clarifications) appear addressed.  
- Remark 1: mapping from Eq. (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear; the “·” notation is ambiguous (matrix vs. Hadamard) and should be made explicit.  
- Avoid overloading the symbol C since it was previously used for the conditional embedding.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 properly cite prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a citation or proof and needs one.  
- Empirical results are solid; supervised learning is less compelling but demonstrates versatility.  
- Bayesian posterior distillation experiment is intriguing but limited to a single dataset/model, so broader usefulness is unclear.  
- Image generation results are broadly comparable to original GMMN on digits and faces, demonstrating feasibility.  
- For wider adoption, stronger results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) are needed.  
- Overall: interesting, well-motivated contribution that would benefit from clearer weighted-MMD exposition, unambiguous notation, a reference/proof for Theorem 3, and more ambitious empirical validation.
