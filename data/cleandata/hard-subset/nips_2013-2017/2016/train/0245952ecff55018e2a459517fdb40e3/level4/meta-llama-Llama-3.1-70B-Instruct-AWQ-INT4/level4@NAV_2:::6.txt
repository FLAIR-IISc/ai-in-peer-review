This paper presents conditional generative moment matching networks, an extension of GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction applications. The key to the proposed method is the kernel embedding of conditional distributions and conditional MMD metric for measuring discrepancy of conditional distributions. The proposed CGMMN was tested on classification, conditional generation, and distilling Bayesian models.

The authors provide a thorough review of the background, including maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) and kernel embedding of conditional distributions. They also clearly explain the proposed conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion and the deep generative architecture of CGMMN. The learning algorithm, which uses stochastic gradient descent to optimize the CMMD objective, is well-described.

The experiments demonstrate the competitive performance of CGMMN in various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge. The results on MNIST and SVHN datasets show that CGMMN is comparable to state-of-the-art competitors in classification tasks. The generating results on MNIST and Yale Face datasets demonstrate the ability of CGMMN to model complex conditional distributions. The experiment on distilling Bayesian models shows that CGMMN can effectively learn a conditional distribution model for efficient prediction.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its applications. The presentation is generally easy to follow, although some sections, such as 2.3 and 3.1, may require additional effort to understand the technical details.

The strengths of the paper include the clear explanation of the proposed method, the thorough review of the background, and the competitive performance of CGMMN in various tasks. The weaknesses include the potential requirement of big minibatches, which may dominate the error and make the computation of the objective expensive.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel extension of GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction applications.
* The proposed CMMD criterion and deep generative architecture of CGMMN are well-described and clearly explained.
* The experiments demonstrate the competitive performance of CGMMN in various tasks.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its applications.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The potential requirement of big minibatches may dominate the error and make the computation of the objective expensive.
* Some sections, such as 2.3 and 3.1, may require additional effort to understand the technical details.
* The paper may benefit from additional experiments, such as comparing CGMMN to a naive approach that estimates a GMMN for each conditional distribution.

Overall, I think the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning and generative models. The proposed CMMD criterion and deep generative architecture of CGMMN provide a nice way to model conditional distributions, and the experiments demonstrate the competitive performance of CGMMN in various tasks.
