- Proposes CGMMN, extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via a new Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).
- Derives CMMD using kernel embedding of conditional distributions with careful mathematical detail.
- Provides an empirical CMMD estimator whose derivation is non-obvious and a notable technical contribution.
- Generates conditional samples with a deep network that processes inputs plus noise, trained by SGD/backprop.
- Claims simpler, more stable optimization than GANs by avoiding min-max training while retaining deep-model benefits.
- Shows competitive predictive performance on MNIST and SVHN without data augmentation or ensembles.
- Demonstrates successful contextual generation (class-conditional MNIST digits, diverse Yale Face images) without memorization.
- Applies CGMMN to Bayesian dark knowledge, effectively distilling predictive distributions on Boston housing regression.
- Paper is very well written and organized, with useful figures and a reproducible appendix.
- Significance: fills a practical gap for simple, effective conditional generative modeling and is broadly applicable.
- Limitation: comparisons with more recent refined conditional GAN variants are limited.
- Weakness: some theoretical assumptions about conditional embedding operators could be more thoroughly justified.
