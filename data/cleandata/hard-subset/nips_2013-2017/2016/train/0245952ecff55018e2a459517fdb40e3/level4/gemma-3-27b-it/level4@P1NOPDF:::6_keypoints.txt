- Introduces CGMMNs: an extension of GMMNs for conditional generation/prediction using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD metric.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Naive conditional GMMN (separate GMMN per conditional) suffers from data sparsity, especially with continuous conditioning.  
- CGMMN’s advantage: matches the conditional embedding operator as a family, avoiding per-condition estimation.  
- Naive baseline can work well when conditioning has few discrete values (e.g., MNIST digits); a direct comparison to this baseline is recommended.  
- Conditional MMD objective is formulated conveniently and can be computed nearly as efficiently as unconditional MMD.  
- K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, enabling standard backpropagation.  
- Computational concern: accurate conditional MMD may need large minibatches, raising cost (notably matrix inversions).  
- Experimental results for classification and Bayesian distillation are interesting and promising.  
- Presentation issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and require consulting cited papers.  
- Minor technical issues: (1) claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect; (2) the conditional embedding operator usage (line 90) does not match the definition in [29] and seems to require a different cross-covariance definition.
