

The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional framework. Unlike models that support single-example updates where the conditional extension is trivial (e.g., simply feeding the condition into the network), this extension is non-trivial. The challenge arises because the algorithm must match distributions over large batches of samples to large batches of inputs, and these distributions are no longer identical (unless identical conditioning is applied per update, which is computationally inefficient). The primary weakness of the paper is its premature focus on formal mathematics without providing intuitive motivation for the derived formula. Specifically, the authors fail to clarify the actual computational steps performed by the algorithm, its computational efficiency, or the assumptions enabling its implementation. In contrast, the non-conditional version (as presented in the original paper) is explained intuitively without advanced mathematical machinery (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces), with its objective function being straightforward to interpret. An equally intuitive explanation for the conditional case is essential. The authors should address the following:  
- How to intuitively derive Equation (2).  
- The practical implementation steps translating Equation (2) into the algorithm (some details appear in the supplement but are difficult to follow).
