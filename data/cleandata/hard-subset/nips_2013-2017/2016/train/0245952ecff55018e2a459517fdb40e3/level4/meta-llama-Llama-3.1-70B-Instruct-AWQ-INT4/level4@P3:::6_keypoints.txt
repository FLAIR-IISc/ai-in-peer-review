- Paper proposes conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMN) using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) for conditional generation and prediction.  
- Key idea: match the conditional embedding operator for the whole family of conditionals instead of fitting separate GMMNs per conditional.  
- This avoids data-sparsity problems of the naive approach, especially with continuous conditioning variables.  
- CMMD objective has a clean formulation, computed similarly to unconditional MMD, and K matrices in Eq.2 are parameter-independent (facilitates backprop).  
- Main concern: CMMD may need large minibatches; small minibatch stochasticity can dominate error.  
- Large minibatches make objective computation expensive, notably matrix inverses.  
- Experiments (classification and Bayesian model distillation) are interesting and competitive with state-of-the-art.  
- Missing experimental baseline: direct comparison to the naive per-conditional GMMN (e.g., finite-conditioning cases like MNIST).  
- Presentation is unclear in sections 2.3 and 3.1 and needs clearer exposition/visualizations.  
- Minor errors: claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect; conditional embedding operator definition differs from reference [29].  
- Originality: novel extension of GMMNs to conditional settings and introduction of CMMD.  
- Significance: approach has potential to be a valuable contribution to deep generative models.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on addressing the minor errors and improving presentation and experimental evaluation.
