- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.
- Main contribution: theoretically grounded framework for conditional generative modeling that preserves GMMN's simple training while embedding conditional distributions in RKHS.
- Demonstrated across tasks: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, showing competitive empirical performance.
- Strength — Originality: non-trivial and distinct extension of GMMN to conditional settings, avoiding GAN min-max training.
- Strength — Significance: versatile applications and a compelling Bayesian distillation use-case for model compression.
- Strength — Quality: sound theoretical treatment of conditional kernel embeddings and comprehensive experiments.
- Weakness — Clarity: heavy formalism up front with insufficient intuitive motivation for the CMMD objective and key equations.
- Weakness — Algorithmic detail: insufficient explanation of how CMMD (eqn.2) maps to the practical algorithm, kernel computation scaling, and matrix inverses during training.
- Weakness — Comparison to prior work: inadequate contrast with other conditional moment-matching methods and conditional mean embedding literature.
- Suggestion: provide an intuitive preface to CMMD explaining why conditioning requires different comparison and how CMMD arises.
- Suggestion: explicitly outline computational steps for eqn.(2), kernel matrix construction, inverse handling, and complexity with batch size.
- Suggestion: add a brief comparison to conditional VAEs to clarify advantages over variational inference approaches.
- Pro acceptance: fills an important gap with a theoretically sound approach, strong empirical results, and preserves GMMN simplicity.
- Pro acceptance: offers a new practical perspective on conditional distribution learning useful for practitioners.
- Con acceptance: major contribution is obscured by poor exposition and missing algorithmic clarity in the main paper.
- Con acceptance: weak theory-to-practice connection and potentially hard-to-reproduce implementation limit accessibility.
- Overall assessment: novel and empirically strong but hampered by presentation and implementation clarity; borderline recommend pending substantial exposition and algorithmic detail improvements.
