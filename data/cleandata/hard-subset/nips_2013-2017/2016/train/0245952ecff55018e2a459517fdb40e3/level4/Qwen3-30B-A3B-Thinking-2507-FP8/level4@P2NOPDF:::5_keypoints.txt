- Paper extends generative moment-matching networks to conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in an RKHS (following Song et al. 2009).  
- Critical technical issue: estimator \hat{C}_{Y|X} omits terms involving μ_X and μ_Y, implying an unjustified zero-mean assumption in RKHS.  
- Reviewer asks for clarification on (1) rationale for omitting those terms, (2) how their absence affects gradient computation, and (3) practical handling in experiments.  
- Approach is conceptually novel and well-motivated.  
- Experimental results are underwhelming: CGMMN shows higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA.  
- Generative evaluation is incomplete—no comparisons to conditional generative baselines (e.g., conditional GANs), so sample quality is not properly assessed.  
- Minor typographical errors noted ("Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the") that should be corrected.  
- Update: after rebuttal, authors addressed the technical concerns (including the missing terms and zero-mean issue) and the reviewer adjusted the evaluation positively.
