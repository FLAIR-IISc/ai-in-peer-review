Summary
The paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting by matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea is to embed conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), following the conditional mean embedding framework of Song et al. (2009). The proposed conditional GMMN (CGMMN) is evaluated empirically on several real datasets.

Major comments
- Definition of the conditional operator: In the definition of ˆC_{Y|X} (Line 90), the term involving the mean embeddings μ_X and μ_Y (more precisely, their estimators) is missing. It is also unusual to assume μ_X and μ_Y are zero, i.e., that the data are already centered in the RKHS. Since ˆC_{Y|X} is central to CGMMN, the paper should clarify this missing term and explain how it is handled in gradient computation and in the actual experiments.
- Empirical results: While the approach is new and neat, the experimental results are disappointing. In predictive tasks, on both datasets, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA. For generative performance, there is no comparison to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional generative adversarial networks), leaving it unclear whether CGMMN generates superior samples.
