- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMN to model conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) training criterion.
- Leverages kernel mean embedding techniques for conditional distributions, building on Song et al. (2009).
- Provides a well-developed theoretical foundation and rigorous derivation of CMMD and the conditional embedding operator.
- Notes that conditional distributions are important for predictive modeling, contextual generation, and hierarchical generative models.
- Training via back-propagation avoids the min–max optimization challenges of GANs, offering a simpler learning procedure.
- Equation (1) in the paper omits the cross-covariance/centering term (μ_X, μ_Y), a critical theoretical omission for C_{Y|X}=C_{YX}C_{XX}^{-1}.
- Authors use the empirical estimator Φ(K+λI)^{-1}Υ^T in practice, but the paper should clarify how this addresses the missing theoretical terms for gradients.
- Paper clarity and structure are generally good, with progressive theoretical exposition.
- Experimental design is appropriate and well-documented with details on architectures and hyperparameters.
- Evaluations cover predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Face), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation (Boston housing).
- Experimental results are mixed: CGMMN is competitive but falls short of state-of-the-art methods like CMMVA and DSN on MNIST and SVHN.
- Contextual generation samples are reasonable but lack direct comparisons to conditional GANs and other contemporary conditional generative models.
- Bayesian dark knowledge experiment is promising but limited to a single regression task, reducing generality of claims.
- Significance: addresses an important gap by extending moment-matching to conditional settings, but practical impact is limited without stronger comparative evidence.
- Originality is moderate: a novel conditional GMMN extension but builds on established kernel embeddings and parallels conditional extensions of GANs/VAEs.
- Strength: strong theoretical contribution and formalization of conditional moment matching.
- Strength: identifies practical importance of conditional distributions and covers diverse application tasks.
- Weakness: critical theoretical omission in conditional embedding derivation undermines rigor.
- Weakness: lacks convincing superiority over state-of-the-art conditional generative models.
- Weakness: insufficient direct comparison with conditional GANs and related methods.
- Weakness: some grammatical errors and overstated claims about performance competitiveness.
- Overall: technically sound and a valuable incremental contribution, but requires fixes to the theoretical derivation, more comprehensive comparisons, and toned claims before acceptance.
