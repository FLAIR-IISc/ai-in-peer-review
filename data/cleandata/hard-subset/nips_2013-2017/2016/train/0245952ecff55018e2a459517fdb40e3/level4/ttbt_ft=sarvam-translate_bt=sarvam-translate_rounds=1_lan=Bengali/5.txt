The authors have extended the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN), considering conditional distributions instead of joint distribution. The core idea is to embed conditional distributions in a regeneration kernel Hilbert space, a technique proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm CGMMN has been experimentally evaluated on several real datasets. In the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (line 90), the term related to $\muX$ and $\muY$ (more precisely, their assumptions) is absent. It is also unusual that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero, meaning the data is already concentrated in the RKS. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is at the center of the CGMMN, it is desirable for the authors to provide an explanation for this missing term, as well as how to deal with it in gradient computation and real-world experiments. The proposed method is new and neat, but the experimental results are somehow disappointing. In terms of predictive performance, in both datasets, the CGMMN gives a larger error rate than the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMA. In terms of generative performance, since no comparison has been provided between CGMMN and other conditional generative methods (e.g., Conditional Generative Adversarial Net), it is unclear whether the proposed algorithm can produce better samples.
