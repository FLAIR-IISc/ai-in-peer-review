- Extends GMMNs to conditional setting by minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD) between empirical and model conditional mean embedding operators.  
- Builds on prior kernel mean embedding and MMD-based deep generator work; training uses Gram matrices and backprop without adversarial min–max.  
- Experiments include predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional generation (MNIST digits, Yale Face), and Bayesian distillation (Boston Housing).  
- Strength: Conceptually clean conditional extension leveraging conditional mean embeddings.  
- Strength: Simple training pipeline; gradients flow only through the generator and Gram-matrix inverses don’t depend on model parameters.  
- Strength: Demonstrates broad applicability across prediction, generation, and distillation tasks.  
- Strength: Practical implementation tips (minibatch CMMD, CNN/MLP backbones, autoencoder code-space) aid adoption.  
- Major weakness: Empirical estimator for the conditional operator ĈY|X omits centering terms—centering should be clarified/implemented.  
- Requests clarification on whether and how feature/Gram matrix centering, shrinkage, and their effect on gradients and bias/variance are handled.  
- Empirical weakness: Predictive performance lags state-of-the-art DGM baseline (CMMVA) on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Empirical weakness: No direct comparisons to conditional GANs or cVAEs, and lacks quantitative generative metrics (FID, Inception score) or human evaluation.  
- Scalability concern: CMMD requires inverting minibatch Gram matrices (O(n^3)); need discussion of runtime/memory tradeoffs, batch-size/λ sensitivity, kernel choices, and recomputation strategy.  
- Clarity: Generally clear exposition but needs consistent notation, symbol definitions, and implementation details for centering, kernels, and complexity.  
- Originality: Moderate—natural combination of established conditional embedding theory with deep generators; main contribution is the CMMD training recipe and applications.  
- Significance: Potentially useful as a non-adversarial conditional generative approach, but impact limited by weaker empirical comparisons.  
- Pros for acceptance: Principled objective, simple/stable training, broadly applicable alternative to adversarial/variational methods.  
- Cons against acceptance: Missing/unclear centering in ĈY|X, predictive results behind strong baselines, no conditional GAN/cVAE comparisons or quantitative sample metrics, limited scalability analysis.  
- Suggestions: Implement/clarify centering in ĈY|X, add conditional GAN/cVAE comparisons with standard metrics and qualitative side-by-sides, and report training time, memory, and sensitivity analyses for batch size, λ, and kernels.  
- Minor issues/typos to fix: “Frobeniu norm” → “Frobenius norm”; “an universal” → “a universal”; grammar at Line 238; consistent capitalization (e.g., ReLU) and spelling checks.  
- Update: After rebuttal, authors addressed concerns about ĈY|X and improved experimental comparisons; reviewer will update scores accordingly.
