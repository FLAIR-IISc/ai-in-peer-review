- Paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to conditional distributions using conditional MMD and kernel mean embeddings (Song et al. 2009).
- Method trains conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) via a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion using SGD and backprop.
- Empirical evaluation spans predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation.
- Methodologically sound and builds on established kernel techniques.
- Key theoretical issue: definition of \hat{C}_{Y|X} omits necessary terms involving μ_X and μ_Y (and their estimators) for the conditional embedding operator.
- Unjustified and unusual assumption that μ_X and μ_Y are zero (i.e., RKHS centering) requires clarification.
- These omissions impact both the theoretical formulation and practical gradient computations for CGMMN.
- Experimental shortcoming: CGMMN yields higher error rates than state-of-the-art CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN for predictive tasks.
- Missing comparisons to established conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), preventing assessment of generative sample quality.
- Paper contains multiple typographical errors (examples: "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").
- Update: after the author rebuttal, the reviewer states all concerns have been addressed and will adjust their evaluation.
