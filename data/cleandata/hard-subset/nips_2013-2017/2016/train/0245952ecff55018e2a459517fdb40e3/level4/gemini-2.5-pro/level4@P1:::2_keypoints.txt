- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions p(y|x), extending GMMN.  
- Core technical contribution: Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) measuring discrepancy between conditional distributions via the Hilbert–Schmidt norm of kernel mean embedding operators.  
- Generator architecture: deep neural network taking x and noise h, trained by backprop to minimize CMMD.  
- Evaluated on predictive tasks (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian model distillation with competitive results.  
- Strength: Addresses a non-trivial extension of moment-matching methods to conditional generation, broadening GMMN applicability.  
- Strength: CMMD is a principled RKHS-based objective for comparing conditional distributions.  
- Strength: Comprehensive experimental evaluation demonstrating versatility across tasks.  
- Weakness: Paper lacks intuitive motivation for CMMD, presenting heavy formalism without an accessible explanation of what the objective computes.  
- Weakness: Insufficient practical details on computing CMMD and its gradient for minibatch training, leaving implementation and complexity unclear.  
- Requested improvement: Provide intuitive derivation/interpretation linking standard MMD to CMMD and explain the role of weighting matrices (K̃⁻¹).  
- Requested improvement: Add step-by-step computation or pseudocode for a single minibatch update and discuss computational complexity w.r.t. batch size.  
- Overall: Novel and potentially significant contribution, but impact limited by presentation; clarifying intuition and implementation would substantially strengthen the paper.
