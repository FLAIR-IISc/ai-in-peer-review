- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional generation by deriving a conditional MMD (CMMD) via kernel embeddings.  
- Provides an algorithm enabling backpropagation-based training of CGMMN similar to original GMMN.  
- Demonstrates CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation with competitive results.  
- Builds on GMMN and GAN literature, offering a moment-matching alternative to GANs’ min-max optimization.  
- Uses kernel methods to flexibly model conditional distributions without strong parametric assumptions.  
- Derivation of CMMD and its efficient implementation represent a novel and non-trivial contribution.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and supplemented with a helpful appendix.  
- Empirical validation spans diverse tasks, strengthening claims of applicability.  
- Method is practical and implementable with standard deep learning tools (Algorithm 1).  
- Theoretical assumptions (smoothness, Hilbert–Schmidt properties) are acknowledged but need deeper discussion regarding real-world robustness.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., kernel bandwidth) is not thoroughly analyzed; a sensitivity study is missing.  
- Comparisons omit some recent conditional generative models (e.g., normalizing flows), limiting benchmarking.  
- Experimental depth within tasks could be increased, e.g., more detailed uncertainty comparisons in knowledge distillation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound extension, well-motivated CMMD criterion, efficient implementation, and competitive empirical results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient discussion of theoretical assumptions, limited hyperparameter analysis, and incomplete comparisons to state-of-the-art conditional models.  
- Overall recommendation: paper is a valuable contribution and should be accepted with minor revisions to address theoretical discussion and broader comparisons.
