

The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core approach embeds conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), leveraging a technique from Song et al. (2009). The proposed conditional GMMN (CGMMN) is empirically evaluated on multiple real datasets. However, the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90) omits the term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (specifically their estimators). It is also unusual to assume $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero, implying the data is already centered in the RKHS. Since $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to CGMMN, the authors must clarify the missing term and explain its handling in gradient computation and practical experiments. While the approach is novel and well-motivated, the experimental results are disappointing. For predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets. For generative performance, the absence of comparisons with other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs) makes it unclear whether CGMMN generates superior samples.
