- Proposes a conditional MMD loss by extending GMMNs with conditional kernel mean embeddings for directed generative models with conditional variables.  
- Validated across tasks: classification, digit and face generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Authors addressed prior minor concerns about low-dimensional latent space, scalability to higher dimensions, comparative utility, and technical clarifications.  
- Mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear: the dot notation needs specification (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard product).  
- Choice of constant C is problematic because it conflicts with notation used for conditional embedding and needs clarification.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 appropriately cite external work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference or explicit proof and requires citation or proof.  
- Empirical results are sufficient for the presented tasks and comparable to original GMMN experiments.  
- Supervised learning application is justified as demonstration of model versatility.  
- Distilled Bayesian inference example is promising but currently limited to a single dataset and model, raising questions about generality.  
- Method appears most promising for pure generative modeling.  
- Wider adoption will require stronger results on more challenging benchmarks such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
