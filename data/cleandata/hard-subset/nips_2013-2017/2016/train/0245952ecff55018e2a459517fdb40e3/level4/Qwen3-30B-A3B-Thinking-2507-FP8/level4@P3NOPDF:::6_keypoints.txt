- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) for conditional generation and prediction.  
- Core innovation: match the model to the conditional embedding operator across a family of conditionals, avoiding per-condition GMMNs and severe data sparsity.  
- Important use-case: avoids the extreme data sparsity problem when conditioning on continuous variables (e.g., single example per condition).  
- Suggests the authors should compare to the naive baseline (separate GMMN per discrete condition) in discrete settings (e.g., MNIST with 10 labels).  
- CMMD formulation is a major strength: kernel matrices in the loss are parameter-independent, enabling straightforward backpropagation and easy integration into GMMN frameworks.  
- Practical concern: CMMD likely needs large minibatches to reduce sampling variance, creating a trade-off with computational cost (e.g., expensive kernel-matrix inversions).  
- Authors should discuss scalability/mitigation strategies for minibatch variance (e.g., variance reduction techniques).  
- Experimental results (classification and Bayesian model distillation) are promising but Sections 2.3 and 3.1 lack clear, self-contained explanations.  
- Exposition needs clearer, intuitive explanation or diagrams of the conditional embedding operator to avoid forcing readers to consult external references.  
- Technical error 1: Claim that “the MMD estimator is unbiased” is incorrect—MMD estimators are consistent but not unbiased for finite samples.  
- Technical error 2: Definition of the conditional embedding operator (Line 90) conflicts with [29]; the cross-covariance operator formulation must be reconciled or corrected.  
- Overall assessment: CGMMNs are a principled, computationally feasible contribution; accept with minor revisions to fix technical errors, clarify presentation, and add the naive baseline comparison.
