This paper presents conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN), extending generative moment matching networks (GMMNs) to handle conditional generation and prediction tasks. The core idea is to leverage kernel embedding of conditional distributions and a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) metric to measure the discrepancy between distributions. The authors demonstrate CGMMN’s effectiveness across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.

The paper addresses a significant challenge: efficiently modeling conditional distributions, a task often hampered by data sparsity when considering each conditional distribution individually. The proposed approach smartly bypasses this by directly matching the model with the conditional embedding operator, treating all conditional distributions as a family. This is a clear advantage over naively estimating a GMMN for each conditional setting, particularly when the conditioning variable domain is continuous. The formulation of the CMMD objective is elegant and allows for relatively straightforward computation, inheriting the backpropagation benefits of GMMNs. The observation that the kernel matrices in Eq. (2) are independent of model parameters is a key enabler for efficient training. 

The strengths of this work lie in its theoretical soundness, clear connection to existing work (MMD, GMMNs, kernel methods), and demonstration of practical applicability. The experiments are diverse and showcase the model's competitive performance. The authors successfully apply CGMMN to tasks beyond standard classification, including Bayesian knowledge distillation, highlighting the versatility of the approach.

However, there are some areas for improvement. The explanations in sections 2.3 and 3.1, particularly regarding the mathematical foundations, are dense and could benefit from increased clarity. A reader unfamiliar with kernel methods and embeddings might struggle to follow the derivations. While the paper mentions the potential computational cost of the CMMD objective (especially matrix inversions), a more thorough discussion of mitigation strategies (e.g., approximations, mini-batch optimization techniques beyond those briefly alluded to) would be valuable. While the authors mention the benefit of the independence of the K matrices, the practicality of large mini-batches with expensive matrix inverses is not fully addressed. 

It would also be beneficial to include a baseline comparison with the aforementioned “naive” approach – training separate GMMNs for each conditional distribution. While the authors correctly anticipate that the proposed method will outperform in many cases, empirically demonstrating this, particularly on simpler datasets like MNIST where conditioning variables take discrete values, would strengthen the argument. Additionally, the statement on line 77 that the MMD estimator is unbiased is inaccurate and should be corrected. The conditional embedding operator definition on line 90 appears inconsistent with the formulation in the cited reference [29]; clarification is needed.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel application of kernel methods and MMD to conditional generative modeling.
*   Addresses a practical challenge in conditional modeling (data sparsity).
*   Competitive performance across diverse tasks.
*   Clear theoretical grounding and connection to existing literature.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Sections 2.3 and 3.1 could benefit from improved clarity and pedagogical explanations.
*   Limited discussion of computational challenges and mitigation strategies.
*   Missing baseline comparison with a naive GMMN-per-condition approach.
*   Minor inaccuracies in the text (unbiased MMD estimator claim, conditional embedding operator definition).



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite minor issues with clarity and a lack of certain baseline comparisons, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN offers a principled and effective approach to conditional generation, and the experimental results are promising. I believe the paper has the potential to influence future research in this area and would recommend **acceptance** contingent upon the authors addressing the identified points of clarification and potential experimentation.
