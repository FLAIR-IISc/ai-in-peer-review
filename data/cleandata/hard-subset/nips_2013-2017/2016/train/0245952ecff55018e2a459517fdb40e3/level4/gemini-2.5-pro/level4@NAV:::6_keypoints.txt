- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) using kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric for conditional generation and prediction.
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation tasks.
- Naive approach of fitting a separate GMMN per condition suffers severe data sparsity, especially for continuous conditioning variables.
- Proposed method avoids sparsity by treating conditional distributions as a unified family and matching the conditional embedding operator directly.
- Suggests including a baseline comparison to the naive/shared-parameter approach (e.g., conditional MNIST with 10 discrete conditions).
- CMMD objective has an elegant formulation and can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices in Eq.2 are parameter-independent, enabling standard backprop.
- Concern that CMMD may require large minibatches to reduce stochastic estimation error, creating a computational trade-off due to expensive matrix inversions.
- Overall assessment: CMMD is well-founded and effective, and experiments are compelling.
- Presentation of Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is difficult to follow and requires consulting referenced papers.
- Minor issues: (1) Claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect. (2) The conditional embedding operator formulation differs from reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition is needed.
