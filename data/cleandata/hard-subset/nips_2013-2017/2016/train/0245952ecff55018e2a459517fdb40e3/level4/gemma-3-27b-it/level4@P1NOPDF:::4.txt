This paper extends the deep generative modeling approach of “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” to enable conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing it to be optimized using the same techniques as the original, unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears technically sound and well-presented, though I haven’t verified every equation in complete detail. 

Regarding novelty, while extending the generative moment matching framework to a conditional setting feels natural, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not immediately obvious – at least, not to me. Consequently, the paper presents both novelty and practical utility. The derived framework appears readily applicable, as illustrated in Algorithm 1, and will likely be valuable to researchers interested in using deep models for generative tasks or for distilling more complex statistical processes. 

The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, and the appendix usefully supplements the main text with detailed methodological information and experimental details.
