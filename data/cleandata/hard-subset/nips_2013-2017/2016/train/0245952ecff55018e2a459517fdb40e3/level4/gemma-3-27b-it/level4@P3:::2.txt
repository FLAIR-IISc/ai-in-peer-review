This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending the capabilities of previous Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMN) to model conditional distributions. The core idea is to leverage a conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, optimized via back-propagation, to learn these distributions. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN across a range of tasks—predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation—achieving competitive results.

This work builds upon the growing area of deep generative models, particularly recent advancements utilizing moment matching techniques as alternatives to the often-challenging training dynamics of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). GMMNs [19] offered a simplified training objective based on MMD [7], and this paper logically extends this approach to the conditional setting, addressing a limitation of the original GMMN which focused solely on joint distributions. The motivation for focusing on conditional distributions is well-articulated, highlighting their relevance to predictive modeling, contextual generation (citing relevant work in GANs [21] and cross-modal generation [2, 31]), and building complex generative architectures [1]. 

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses an Important Problem:** Extending moment matching to the conditional setting is a natural and valuable step. Conditional generative models are fundamental to many applications.
* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a rigorous mathematical treatment of the CMMD criterion, grounding the approach in kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings.
* **Empirical Validation:** The authors demonstrate the performance of CGMMN on diverse tasks, including image prediction (MNIST, SVHN), generation, and knowledge distillation, showing competitive results compared to existing methods.
* **Backpropagation Efficiency:** The method maintains the key advantage of GMMNs: efficient training via backpropagation, avoiding the min-max optimization issues of GANs.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Lack of Intuition:** The paper dives into the mathematical formalism of CMMD very quickly, potentially hindering understanding for readers not deeply familiar with kernel methods. The intuition behind the derivation of equation (2) and its connection to the practical algorithm is not adequately explained. A more accessible explanation, analogous to the intuitive description of the non-conditional MMD objective in the original GMMN paper [19], would be beneficial.
* **Computational Details:** The computational complexity and practical considerations of implementing and optimizing the CMMD objective (Eq. 2) are not sufficiently discussed. Clarifying the assumptions required for its effectiveness and the specifics of the mini-batch implementation (mentioned in Algorithm 1) would be valuable. The paper would benefit from a detailed breakdown of the computational steps involved.
* **Limited Ablation Studies:** While results are presented on several tasks, the paper lacks targeted ablation studies exploring the impact of key design choices, such as the choice of kernel, the regularization parameter λ, or the dimensionality of the hidden variables.
* **Presentation of Results:** The experimental section could be improved by presenting more detailed comparisons with baselines, including statistical significance tests to demonstrate the robustness of the observed improvements.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a relevant and important problem in generative modeling.
* The technical approach is sound and builds upon established theoretical foundations.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN across multiple tasks.
* The method offers a potentially efficient alternative to GANs for conditional generation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The paper's presentation prioritizes mathematical rigor over intuitive explanation, potentially limiting accessibility.
* The lack of detailed discussion regarding the computational aspects of CMMD and the limited ablation studies raise concerns about the practical applicability and robustness of the method.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a solid contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The extension of moment matching to the conditional setting is a logical and valuable advancement. However, the paper would benefit from improved clarity in its explanation of the CMMD criterion and a more detailed discussion of its practical implementation. While the results are promising, further experimentation and analysis would strengthen the claims made.

**Recommendation:**

I recommend **accepting** this paper, but with a request for minor revisions to address the concerns regarding clarity and computational details. Specifically, the authors should consider adding a section dedicated to providing an intuitive explanation of the CMMD objective and elaborating on the practical considerations involved in its implementation. Including some ablation studies would also be highly beneficial.
