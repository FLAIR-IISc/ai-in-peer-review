This paper proposes an extension to the deep generative modeling approach "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" to allow for conditional generation. The authors derive a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, enabling optimization using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model, Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), is demonstrated with reasonably competitive performance on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model.

The paper is technically sound, and the proposals are well-presented. The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the theoretical background, including the Hilbert space embedding of conditional distributions and the derivation of the empirical estimator. The mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator, in particular, are not immediately obvious and demonstrate a good understanding of the underlying concepts.

In terms of novelty, while the idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework is natural, the authors' approach is unique and provides a valuable contribution to the field. The derived framework is easy to apply, as shown in Algorithm 1, making this paper likely to be useful and relevant to researchers interested in using deep models for generative purposes or to distill more elaborate statistical processes.

The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read. The authors provide sufficient details about the methods used and the experiments, and the appendix usefully supplements the main text with additional information. The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on various tasks.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel extension to the generative moment matching framework, allowing for conditional generation.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the theoretical background and the derivation of the empirical estimator.
* The proposed model is demonstrated with reasonably competitive performance on various tasks.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read.

Weaknesses:

* The paper may benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods, particularly in the context of conditional generation.
* Some of the experimental results, such as the generated samples, may be difficult to evaluate without additional context or visualizations.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and valuable extension to the generative moment matching framework.
* The authors provide a clear and thorough explanation of the theoretical background and the derivation of the empirical estimator.
* The proposed model is demonstrated with reasonably competitive performance on various tasks.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The paper may not provide sufficient novelty or contribution to the field, as the idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework is natural.
* Some of the experimental results may be difficult to evaluate without additional context or visualizations.

Overall, I recommend accepting this paper, as it provides a valuable contribution to the field of deep generative modeling and demonstrates a good understanding of the underlying concepts.
