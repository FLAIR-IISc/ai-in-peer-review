- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to model conditional distributions p(y|x) using a new Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- CMMD compares kernel mean embedding operators in RKHS to measure distance between true and learned conditional distributions.  
- The generative model conditions on x and noise h through a deep network to generate y.  
- Empirical evaluation is strong and diverse: predictive tasks (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Novel and significant: extending moment-matching networks to conditional settings is non-trivial and broadens applicability.  
- Well-grounded theoretically by building on kernel embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Main weakness: exposition lacks intuition; jumps into RKHS formalism without motivating CMMD or explaining Eq.(2) at a high level.  
- Underdeveloped insight: Remark 1 (CMMD as weighted Gram matrices) is not clearly explained or illustrated.  
- Lacks practical implementation details and a clear derivation from the abstract CMMD norm to the Gram-matrix form in Eq.(2).  
- Does not discuss computational cost (matrix inversions, O(N^3) behavior), mini-batch trade-offs, or how these were handled in experiments.  
- Missing guidance on the regularization parameter λ (role, stability, how it was chosen in experiments).  
- Recommendation: revise to add intuition, step-by-step derivation, computational complexity analysis, and practical hyperparameter guidance to strengthen impact.
