- Paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) that match conditional distributions via RKHS embeddings (extension of GMMNs).
- Definition of empirical conditional operator ĤC_{Y|X} is missing the terms involving μ_X and μ_Y (their estimators); assumes zero mean in RKHS, which is unusual.
- Authors must clarify the missing term and explain how it affects gradient computation and practical experiments.
- Approach is novel and well-motivated but experimental results are weak: CGMMN has higher predictive error than state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets.
- Generative quality is unclear: no comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), so sample quality advantage is unproven.
- Manuscript contains typos (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the") that should be corrected.
- Update: after the authors' rebuttal, the reviewer states all concerns were addressed and will revise scores accordingly.
