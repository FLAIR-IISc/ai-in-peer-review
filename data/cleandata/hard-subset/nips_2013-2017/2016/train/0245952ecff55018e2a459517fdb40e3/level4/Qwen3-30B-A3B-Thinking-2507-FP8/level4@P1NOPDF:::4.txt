

This paper extends the deep generative modeling framework "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" to enable conditional generation. The authors derive a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, enabling optimization via the same machinery as the unconditional variant. The proposed model achieves competitive results on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model. The technical approach appears sound, and the presentation is clear and well-structured (though I did not verify every equation in full detail). Regarding novelty, while the conditional extension of the generative moment-matching framework is conceptually natural, the derivation of the empirical estimator—particularly its mechanism—is less straightforward. This non-obvious technical contribution lends the paper meaningful novelty. The framework is straightforward to implement, as demonstrated in Algorithm 1, suggesting it will be immediately useful to practitioners seeking to apply deep generative models or distill complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with the appendix providing valuable supplementary details on methodology and experimental protocols. Overall, the work makes a solid contribution to the field.
