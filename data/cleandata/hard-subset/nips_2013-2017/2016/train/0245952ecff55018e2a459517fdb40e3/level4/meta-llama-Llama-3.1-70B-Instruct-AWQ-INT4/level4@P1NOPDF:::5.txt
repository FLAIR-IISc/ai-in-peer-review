The authors extend the generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by considering the matching of conditional distributions instead of joint distributions. The key idea is to embed the conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, a technique proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is evaluated empirically on several real datasets.

In the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90), the term related to $\muX$ and $\muY$ (more precisely, their estimators) appears to be missing. Furthermore, it is unusual to assume that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero, implying that the data is already centered in the RKHS. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is a crucial component of CGMMN, it would be desirable for the authors to provide clarification on this missing term, as well as how to address it in gradient computation and real experiments.

Although the proposed approach is novel and elegant, the experimental results are somewhat disappointing. In terms of predictive performance, CGMMN yields larger error rates than the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMVA on both datasets. Moreover, in terms of generative performance, the lack of comparison between CGMMN and other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional generative adversarial nets) makes it unclear whether the proposed algorithm can generate better samples.

Additionally, there are some minor errors in the paper, such as "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
