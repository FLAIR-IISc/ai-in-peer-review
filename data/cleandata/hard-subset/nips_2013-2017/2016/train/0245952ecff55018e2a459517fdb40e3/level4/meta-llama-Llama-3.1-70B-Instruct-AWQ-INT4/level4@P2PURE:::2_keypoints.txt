- Paper proposes a conditional extension of moment matching networks, which is non-trivial compared to single-example-updatable conditional models and can be inefficient due to matching distributions across mini-batches and diverse inputs.

- Main criticism: the manuscript focuses on heavy mathematical derivations without providing an intuitive explanation of the underlying formulas or the actual algorithmic computations and their efficiency.

- Equation (2) is not sufficiently explained: the paper omits details on what is being optimized, the computational steps, and the assumptions that make the formula valid and practical.

- The non-conditional algorithm has a clear intuitive explanation in prior work, but the conditional case lacks a comparable intuitive derivation and walkthrough.

- The reviewer requests explicit answers to: how to intuitively derive equation (2), and what are the concrete steps to move from equation (2) to the practical algorithm.

- Some clarifying material exists in the supplement but is hard to follow; a clearer, more accessible explanation in the main text is needed to improve clarity and impact.

- The paper relies on advanced concepts (e.g., RKHS) without motivating or simplifying them for understanding the conditional extension.
