This paper presents a conditional extension to the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) framework. The core technical contribution is the derivation of a kernel embedding for the moments of the conditional distribution, which cleverly allows the conditional model to be trained using the same optimization machinery as the original unconditional version.

The authors demonstrate the model's effectiveness on several tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model, achieving competitive performance. The paper appears technically sound and the proposed methods are presented clearly. Although I have not verified every equation in detail, the overall derivation seems correct.

Regarding novelty, while extending a generative framework to the conditional setting is a natural direction, the specific mechanism proposed here—particularly the derivation of the empirical estimator for the conditional moments—is non-trivial and insightful. This constitutes a valuable contribution to the literature on moment-matching generative models.

The resulting framework is straightforward to implement, as illustrated in Algorithm 1. This practicality suggests the paper will be useful for researchers and practitioners working on deep generative modeling or knowledge distillation from more complex statistical processes.

Overall, the paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow. The appendix provides comprehensive details about the methods and experimental setup, which strengthens the paper's reproducibility.
