- Paper proposes a conditional MMD loss for directed generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings, extending GMMNs to the conditional setting.
- Authors addressed prior reviewer concerns about latent dimensionality, scalability, practical utility, and clarified empirical results in the revision.
- Remark 1: The derivation mapping Eq. (2) to the weighted MMD is unclear; the “dot” notation must be disambiguated with a step-by-step algebraic expansion.
- Notation: The symbol C is overloaded (used for both a constant and the conditional embedding operator) and should be renamed to avoid confusion.
- Theorems 1 and 2 are cited appropriately, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference or proof and requires either citation or an appendix proof.
- Empirically, results are decent and support feasibility; supervised learning use is acceptable as a demonstration of versatility.
- Bayesian distillation application is interesting but shown on only one dataset/model, limiting claims of generality; more varied experiments are needed.
- Generative modeling experiments on digits and faces match prior GMMN work, but more challenging benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) and comparisons to strong baselines are recommended.
- Scalability concerns: authors should report experiments varying latent dimensionality, training time, memory, and stability to address earlier scalability questions.
- An ablation on kernel choices and bandwidth selection in the conditional setting would be informative.
- Strength: Conceptually clean extension of MMD-based training to conditional generative models via conditional mean embeddings.
- Strength: Proposed loss is simple to implement and applicable across tasks (supervised prediction, conditional generation, knowledge distillation).
- Strength: Paper is generally well written and most prior concerns appear to have been addressed.
- Limitations: Key derivation is under-explained and notation is overloaded, hurting readability.
- Limitations: Theoretical coverage is incomplete without a citation or proof for Theorem 3.
- Limitations: Empirical evaluation lacks challenging benchmarks and broader validation for the Bayesian distillation claim.
- Suggestions: Provide detailed derivation for Remark 1, rename overloaded symbols, include a notation table, and supply a proof or reference for Theorem 3.
- Suggestions: Augment experiments with at least one challenging generative benchmark, sensitivity analyses for latent dimensionality and kernel hyperparameters, and additional datasets/models or diagnostics for Bayesian distillation.
- Overall: Thoughtful and timely extension with promise, requiring clearer derivations and stronger empirical validation to make a solid contribution.
