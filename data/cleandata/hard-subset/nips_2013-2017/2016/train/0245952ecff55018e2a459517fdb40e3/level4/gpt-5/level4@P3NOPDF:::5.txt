Summary
This paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting by matching conditional distributions via conditional mean embeddings in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), following the framework of Song et al. (2009). The resulting method, CGMMN, is evaluated on several real datasets.

Strengths
- Conceptually neat extension of MMD-based learning to conditional generation without adversarial training.
- Builds on a solid kernel-embedding foundation and presents a principled objective.
- Empirical evaluations on multiple datasets demonstrate feasibility.

Main concerns and questions
1) Empirical conditional operator definition. The estimator of the conditional operator (line 90) appears to omit the centering terms involving the mean embeddings μX and μY (more precisely, their empirical estimates). Assuming zero mean in RKHS is unusual unless features are explicitly centered. Since this operator is central to CGMMN, please:
- Provide the exact estimator you implement, including centering. If you rely on the identity C_Y|X = C_YX (C_XX + λI)^{-1}, specify how C_YX and C_XX are empirically computed (e.g., with centering matrices) and how regularization is applied.
- Clarify whether centering is performed per mini-batch or with running estimates, and discuss any bias introduced by mini-batch centering.
- Explain how gradients are computed through the operator, including numerical stabilization (e.g., linear solves vs. matrix inverses, Cholesky factorization, damping).
- State explicitly any assumptions made (e.g., μX = 0 and μY = 0) and justify them, or revise the text to include the missing terms.

2) Predictive performance. On both datasets, CGMMN has higher error rates than the reported state-of-the-art method CMMVA. Please analyze this gap:
- Are there implementation or hyperparameter differences that disadvantage CGMMN?
- Can you provide ablations on kernel choice, bandwidth selection, regularization strength, and sample size?
- Include training time and stability comparisons.

3) Generative performance and baselines. The paper does not compare CGMMN to established conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs or other conditional MMD/VAEs). Without such baselines it is hard to assess sample quality.
- Please add comparisons (both qualitative samples and quantitative metrics such as FID, Inception Score, or task-appropriate alternatives).
- If such comparisons are impractical, give a clear rationale and at least provide strong qualitative evidence and standard metrics on public benchmarks.

4) Clarity and reproducibility.
- Provide enough detail to reproduce results: kernel types, bandwidth selection procedure, regularization constants, optimizer settings, early stopping, and architecture details.
- Discuss sensitivity to the choice of conditioning features and any preprocessing steps.
- Clarify how you handle invertibility of C_XX (regularization, rank issues) and its effect on performance.

Minor issues and typos
- “Frobeniu norm” (line 48) → “Frobenius norm”
- “an universal” (line 65) → “a universal”
- “which the” (line 238) → likely “which” or “that the”
- Ensure consistent notation for operators, matrices, and mean embeddings; define all symbols on first use.
- Cite Song et al. (2009) precisely where the conditional embedding is introduced.

Overall assessment
The paper proposes a novel and elegant conditional extension of GMMNs grounded in RKHS conditional mean embeddings. However, the current presentation omits crucial details about the empirical conditional operator and its treatment in optimization, and the experiments do not yet demonstrate advantages over strong baselines in either predictive accuracy or generative quality. Addressing the missing terms in the operator estimator, clarifying implementation and gradient computation, and strengthening empirical comparisons would substantially improve the paper’s clarity and impact.

Update after author rebuttal
The authors have addressed all of my concerns, including clarifying the empirical conditional operator and its implementation, and responding to the experimental issues. I will adjust my scores accordingly.
