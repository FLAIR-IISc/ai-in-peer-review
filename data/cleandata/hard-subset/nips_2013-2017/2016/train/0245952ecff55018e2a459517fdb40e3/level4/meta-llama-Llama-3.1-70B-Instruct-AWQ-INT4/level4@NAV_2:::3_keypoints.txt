- Proposes a loss for directed conditional generative models: conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) extending generative moment matching networks.
- Derives the CMMD loss using conditional kernel mean embeddings and shows it compares empirical conditional and model conditional kernel mean embeddings.
- Provides theoretical justification that CMMD is a sufficient condition for equality of two conditional distributions.
- Demonstrates competitive experimental results on classification, MNIST digit and face generation, and distilling Bayesian models.
- Compares performance against other methods including variational autoencoders and max-margin deep generative models.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the method and methodology.
- Strength: novel extension enabling conditional variables in generative moment matching networks.
- Strength: solid theoretical framing and empirical evaluation across multiple tasks.
- Weakness: Remark 1 notation is unclear; mapping from Eq.(2) to the weighted MMD form needs more detail.
- Weakness: unfortunate/ambiguous choice of notation for the conditional embedding operator.
- Weakness: Theorem 3 has no reference or provided proof; a citation or proof is required.
- Overall assessment: technically sound and potentially useful for generative modeling, but minor clarity and completeness issues must be addressed.
