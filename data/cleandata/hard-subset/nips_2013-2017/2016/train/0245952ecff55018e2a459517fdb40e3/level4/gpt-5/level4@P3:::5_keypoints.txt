- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks to the conditional setting via conditional kernel mean embeddings and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective; generator y = f(x,h;w) trained by backprop through Gram-matrix expressions with regularized inverses.  
- Evaluated on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual conditional generation (MNIST digits, Yale Faces), and Bayesian distillation of predictive distributions.  
- Builds on prior MMD deep-generator work and conditional mean embeddings; complements conditional GANs/VAEs by offering a single-objective, non-adversarial training criterion.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides a sufficient condition (Theorem 3) for equality of conditional distributions when conditional embedding operators match.  
- Practicality: training reduces to kernel Gram computations and backprop; Gram inverses on x are parameter-independent, simplifying gradients.  
- Generality: architecture-agnostic (MLP/CNN), handles discrete and continuous conditioning, applies to both generation and prediction tasks.  
- Application strength: demonstrates Bayesian distillation where the CGMMN efficiently approximates teacher predictive distributions.  
- Estimator clarity issue: conditional embedding estimator form (Φ(K+λI)−1Υ⊤) and the role of centering/mean terms required clarification (addressed in rebuttal).  
- Lacks quantitative, side-by-side comparisons to conditional GANs and conditional VAEs; nearest-neighbor visuals are insufficient.  
- Predictive performance shortfall: underperforms strong discriminative baselines and max-margin DGM on MNIST/SVHN; causes (kernel choice, batch size, architecture) need analysis.  
- Scalability concern: cubic dependence on minibatch size for Gram inverses limits practicality; suggests low-rank, random-feature, or blockwise approximations and ablation studies.  
- Robustness question: needs ablations on kernel choices for x/y, λ-regularization, and noise dimensionality to clarify sensitivity.  
- Presentation issues: minor typos and some derivations need clearer pointers to appendices and assumptions.  
- Quality assessment: methodology is technically sound and RKHS-grounded; CMMD derived cleanly; empirical results competent though not state-of-the-art.  
- Clarity assessment: paper generally well-organized; rebuttal clarified estimator/centering and added useful gradient and complexity notes.  
- Originality: a natural but nontrivial and (to the reviewer’s knowledge) novel extension—first deep conditional generator trained with CMMD distinct from adversarial/variational approaches.  
- Significance: broadens moment-matching generators to conditional tasks and offers a stable alternative to adversarial training; impact depends on stronger empirical comparisons and scalability fixes.  
- Pros for acceptance: principled CMMD objective with theory, simple/stable training, and diverse applications including effective Bayesian distillation.  
- Cons for acceptance: predictive performance lags baselines, missing quantitative cGAN/cVAE comparisons, and computational cost of kernel matrices may limit scalability.  
- Rebuttal update: authors clarified the conditional operator estimator and centering effects, will fix typos, and provided additional practical discussion; reviewer’s concerns were addressed.
