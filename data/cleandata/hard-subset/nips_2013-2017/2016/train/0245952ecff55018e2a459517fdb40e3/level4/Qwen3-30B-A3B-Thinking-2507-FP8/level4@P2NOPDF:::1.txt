

This paper introduces a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion, offering a meaningful contribution to deep learning research on generative models. The method is evaluated on standard small-scale benchmark tasks to assess both discriminative and generative capabilities. While the paper is generally well-written, the mathematical exposition can be challenging to follow at times, a common hurdle in this field. The selected tasks are modest in scale but remain reasonable when contextualized against recent literature.  

A key concern is the computational cost of the training algorithm, which involves intensive steps. The authors should include a comparative analysis of training time relative to established methods like DSN to provide clarity on practical feasibility. Additionally, releasing reference code would substantially strengthen reproducibility, as the model description in Section 3 may not suffice for precise replication by practitioners.  

The work represents a promising extension to generative modeling, though minor improvements would elevate its impact. The core idea is sound, and the empirical results align with the stated objectives. However, the lack of training time benchmarks and code availability limits the paper’s utility for the broader community. Addressing these points would make the contribution more robust and accessible. Overall, this is a solid, incremental advance worthy of consideration for publication.
