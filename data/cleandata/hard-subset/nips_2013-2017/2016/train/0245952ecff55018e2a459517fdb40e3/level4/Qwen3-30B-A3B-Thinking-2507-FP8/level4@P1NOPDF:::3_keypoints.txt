- Proposes a loss for directed conditional generative models by extending GMMN with conditional kernel mean embeddings and an MMD-based conditional loss.  
- Demonstrates the method on classification, digit and face generation, and a Bayesian model distillation task.  
- Authors have addressed earlier minor concerns about latent dimensionality, scalability, and comparative utility.  
- Ambiguity in Remark 1: the mapping from Eq.(2) to the weighted MMD uses a “dot” notation that could mean matrix multiplication or Hadamard product—needs a detailed derivation.  
- Notation conflict: the symbol C is reused for the weighting coefficient but was already defined as the conditional embedding in Section 3; recommend renaming.  
- Theorem 3 lacks citation or proof and requires either a reference or a short derivation in the appendix.  
- Empirical results are satisfactory and justify using supervised classification to demonstrate versatility.  
- Bayesian distillation application is novel but evaluated only on a single dataset and model, limiting assessment of generality.  
- Strongest promise appears to be in pure generative modeling, where digit and face experiments compellingly validate the core idea.  
- Results are comparable to the original GMMN, but broader impact is uncertain without evaluations on harder benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Overall, technical contributions are sound; clarifying Remark 1, fixing the C notation, and adding Theorem 3’s reference/proof would significantly strengthen the paper.
