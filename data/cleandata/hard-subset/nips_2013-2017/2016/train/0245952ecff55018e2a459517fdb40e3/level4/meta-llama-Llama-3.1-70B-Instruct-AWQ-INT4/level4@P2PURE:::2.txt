The authors propose a conditional extension of moment matching networks, a non-trivial task, particularly when compared to models that can be updated with a single example by simply feeding in the conditional information. The challenge arises from the need to match distributions of a large number of samples (mini-batch) to a large number of inputs, where these distributions are no longer identical unless the same conditional information is used for a given update, which is inefficient.

The primary issue with the paper is that it delves into complex mathematical derivations without providing an intuitive understanding of the underlying formula and the actual computations performed during the algorithm, as well as their efficiency. Specifically, the authors do not explain the details behind the formula being optimized in equation (2), its efficiency, and the assumptions made to make it work, leaving the reader without a clear understanding of the actual computation performed.

In contrast, the non-conditional version of the algorithm can be explained intuitively, as demonstrated in the original paper, without relying on advanced mathematical concepts such as reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces. The formula being minimized in the non-conditional case is straightforward to understand. A similar intuitive explanation for the conditional case would be beneficial. To improve the paper, the authors should consider addressing the following questions:

- How can one intuitively derive formula (2)?
- What are the steps involved in transitioning from formula (2) to the practical algorithm?

While some of this information is provided in the supplement, it is difficult to follow, and a clearer explanation would enhance the overall clarity and impact of the paper.
