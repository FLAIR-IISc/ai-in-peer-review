- Summary: Extends GMMNs to conditional generation using RKHS conditional mean embeddings (CGMMN) and evaluates on real datasets.

- Strength: Conceptually neat MMD-based conditional generation without adversarial training.

- Strength: Principled objective grounded in solid kernel-embedding theory.

- Strength: Empirical evaluations on multiple datasets show feasibility.

- Main concern 1 (empirical conditional operator): The paper omits centering terms in the empirical conditional operator estimator and must provide the exact implemented estimator, centering strategy, regularization, and gradient/backprop details.

- Main concern 1 (implementation details): Clarify whether centering is per mini-batch or running, discuss bias from mini-batch centering, and state any assumptions like μX=0 or μY=0.

- Main concern 1 (numerical stability): Explain how gradients are computed through (C_XX+λI)^{-1} (e.g., linear solves, Cholesky, damping) and measures for numerical stabilization.

- Main concern 2 (predictive performance): CGMMN has higher error than CMMVA; analyze gaps due to implementation/hyperparameter differences, and provide ablations on kernel, bandwidth, regularization, and sample size plus training time/stability.

- Main concern 3 (generative baselines): Lack of comparisons to conditional GANs, VAEs, or other conditional-generative methods; add quantitative metrics (FID, Inception Score) and qualitative samples or justify omission.

- Main concern 4 (clarity & reproducibility): Provide full reproducibility details—kernel types, bandwidth selection, regularization, optimizer, early stopping, architectures, preprocessing, conditioning feature sensitivity, and handling of C_XX invertibility/rank issues.

- Minor issues and typos: Fix spelling/grammar (e.g., “Frobeniu”→“Frobenius”, “an universal”→“a universal”), ensure consistent notation, and properly cite Song et al. (2009).

- Overall assessment: Novel and elegant conditional GMMN grounded in RKHS, but missing crucial empirical/operator and experimental details; addressing these would improve clarity and impact.
