- Proposes CGMMN: extension of GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel conditional embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and distilling Bayesian models.
- Key advantage: models the family of conditional distributions jointly via a conditional embedding operator, mitigating data sparsity especially for continuous conditioners.
- Lacks comparison to a naive baseline (separate GMMN per conditional distribution), which is important e.g. for discrete labels like MNIST.
- CMMD objective is well-formulated and efficiently computable, analogous to unconditional MMD.
- Independence of K matrices from model parameters simplifies backpropagation.
- Potential scalability concern: CMMD may need large minibatches for stability and involves costly matrix inversions.
- Experimental results (classification and Bayesian distillation) are encouraging and competitive.
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is dense and could be clearer; readers may need referenced papers for full understanding.
- Minor technical error: statement that the MMD estimator is unbiased is inaccurate.
- Minor technical inconsistency: presented conditional embedding operator differs from reference [29] and may require a modified cross-covariance operator.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important problem with theoretically sound kernel-based solution, efficient objective, good experiments, and extension of prior work.
- Arguments against acceptance: presentation clarity issues, missing naive baseline comparison, minor technical inaccuracies, and unaddressed computational challenges for large datasets.
