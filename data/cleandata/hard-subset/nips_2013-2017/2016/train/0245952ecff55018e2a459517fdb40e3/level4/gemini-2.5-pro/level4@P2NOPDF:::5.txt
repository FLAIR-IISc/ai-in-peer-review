This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of GMMNs designed to model conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea is to leverage a technique from Song et al. (2009) that embeds conditional distributions into a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS), allowing for the definition of a distance metric between them. The proposed algorithm is evaluated empirically on several real-world datasets.

While the approach is novel and elegant, there are several points that require clarification and the experimental results could be strengthened.

**Major Concerns:**

1.  **Definition of the Conditional Covariance Operator:** My primary technical concern is with the definition of the empirical conditional covariance operator, $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$, presented in Line 90. The formula appears to omit terms corresponding to the mean embeddings $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (or their empirical estimators). This formulation implies an assumption that the data is centered in the RKHS (i.e., that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero), which is a strong condition not typically met by real-world data. Since this operator is central to the CGMMN objective function, this point warrants clarification. It would be beneficial if the authors could explain the reasoning for this omission and clarify how this is handled in the gradient computations and the practical implementation for the experiments.

2.  **Experimental Performance:** The empirical results are not fully convincing.
    *   **Predictive Performance:** In the predictive tasks, the proposed CGMMN method is outperformed by the state-of-the-art baseline, CMMVA, reporting higher error rates on both datasets. This raises questions about the practical utility of the method for prediction compared to existing approaches.
    *   **Generative Performance:** The evaluation of the model's generative capabilities is limited. The paper does not include a comparison against other contemporary conditional generative models, such as Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs). Without such baselines, it is difficult to assess the quality of the samples generated by CGMMN relative to the current state of the art.

**Minor Comments:**

The paper contains a few typos that should be corrected for the final version:
*   Line 48: "Frobeniu norm" should be "Frobenius norm".
*   Line 65: "an universal" should be "a universal".
*   Line 238: The phrase "which the" seems to be a grammatical error.

-----Update after the author rebuttal-----
The authors have provided a thorough rebuttal that satisfactorily addresses all the concerns raised in my initial review. Their clarifications on the conditional covariance operator and the context provided for the experimental results were helpful. In light of their response, I have updated my scores accordingly.
