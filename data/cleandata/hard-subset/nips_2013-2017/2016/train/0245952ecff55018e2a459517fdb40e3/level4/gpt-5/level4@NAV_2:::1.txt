This paper presents a neural network approach grounded in a conditional maximum mean discrepancy objective. The method is evaluated on standard, relatively small benchmark tasks to assess both discriminative and generative performance. Overall, this is a noteworthy extension within the deep learning subfield focused on generative models. The writing is reasonably clear, though—as is common in this area—the mathematical exposition can be somewhat hard to follow in places. The experimental tasks are on the small side, but not unreasonable relative to recent comparable work. Given that the training algorithm includes computationally intensive steps, I would like to see explicit commentary in the experiments section on wall-clock training time and efficiency compared to existing approaches such as DSN. The paper would be much stronger with reference code for the experiments. As currently described, the model section may not provide enough detail for practitioners to faithfully reproduce the exact models evaluated.
