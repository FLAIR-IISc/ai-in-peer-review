1. Proposes a loss for directed conditional generative models by extending GMMNs with conditional kernel mean embeddings and a conditional MMD criterion.  
2. Demonstrated on classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and Bayesian model knowledge distillation.  
3. This is the second review; previous minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space, scaling, utility) appear to have been directly addressed.  
4. Remark 1: derivation from Equation (2) to weighted MMD is unclear and needs a more detailed explanation.  
5. Ambiguity in dot notation—unclear whether it denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product; clarify usage.  
6. Notation conflict: using 'C' for a constant is unfortunate since 'C' already denotes the conditional embedding operator.  
7. Theorem 3 is presented without citation or proof; a reference or appendix proof is required.  
8. Empirical results are solid and serve as a convincing proof of concept.  
9. Including a supervised learning task is reasonable to show versatility, though not the most compelling application.  
10. The distilled Bayesian inference experiment is interesting but evaluated on only one dataset/model, limiting assessment of general usefulness.  
11. Method shows promise for pure generative modeling and matches original GMMN performance on standard digit and face datasets.  
12. For wider adoption, the approach needs compelling results on higher-dimensional datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
