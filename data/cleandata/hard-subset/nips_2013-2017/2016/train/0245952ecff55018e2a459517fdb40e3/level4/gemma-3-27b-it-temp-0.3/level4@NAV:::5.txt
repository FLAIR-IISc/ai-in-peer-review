The authors present an extension to generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by focusing on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. Their core innovation lies in embedding these conditional distributions within a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, building upon the framework introduced by Song et al. (2009). The proposed Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) is then evaluated through empirical studies using several real-world datasets. However, the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90) appears to omit a crucial term involving $\muX$ and $\muY$ (specifically, their estimates). Furthermore, the assumption that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero – implying the data is already centered in the RKHS – is atypical and warrants further explanation. Given the central role of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ within CGMMN, clarification regarding this missing term, and its implications for gradient computation and practical implementation, would be beneficial. While the approach is novel and well-presented, the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. CGMMN demonstrates higher error rates than the current state-of-the-art, CMMVA, on both tested datasets. Regarding generative capabilities, the lack of comparison to other conditional generative models, such as conditional generative adversarial networks, makes it difficult to assess the quality of the generated samples. The manuscript also contains minor typographical errors, including "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
