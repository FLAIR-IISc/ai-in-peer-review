- Proposes CGMMN, extending GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embedding and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.  
- Provides thorough background review of MMD and kernel embedding of conditional distributions.  
- Clearly explains the CMMD criterion and the deep generative architecture of CGMMN.  
- Learning algorithm (SGD optimization of CMMD) is well-described.  
- Experiments cover classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- MNIST and SVHN classification results are comparable to state-of-the-art.  
- Generative results on MNIST and Yale Face show capacity to model complex conditional distributions.  
- Distillation experiment demonstrates CGMMN can learn conditional distributions for efficient prediction.  
- Paper is well-written and generally easy to follow, though sections 2.3 and 3.1 are technically dense.  
- Strengths: clear exposition, thorough background, and competitive empirical performance.  
- Main weakness: may require large minibatches, making CMMD computation expensive and error-prone.  
- Other weaknesses: some technical clarity issues and lack of comparisons to naive per-condition GMMN baselines.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel extension, clear method, solid experiments, good writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: computational/minibatch concerns, technical clarity, limited additional baselines.
