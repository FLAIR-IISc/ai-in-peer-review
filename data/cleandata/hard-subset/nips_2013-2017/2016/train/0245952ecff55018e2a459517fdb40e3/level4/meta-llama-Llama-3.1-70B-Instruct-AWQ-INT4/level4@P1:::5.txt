The authors extend the generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by considering matching conditional distributions instead of joint distributions. The key idea is to embed the conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, a technique proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm CGMMN is evaluated empirically on several real datasets.

The authors provide a thorough introduction to the problem of learning conditional distributions and the existing approaches, including GMMNs and generative adversarial networks (GANs). They clearly explain the limitations of these methods and how their proposed approach, CGMMN, addresses these limitations. The technical details of the CGMMN algorithm are well-explained, including the use of conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) as the training objective.

The experimental results are presented on several datasets, including MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face. The authors evaluate the performance of CGMMN on both predictive and generative tasks. While the results are not always competitive with the state-of-the-art algorithms, they demonstrate the potential of CGMMN in various applications.

However, there are some concerns that need to be addressed. In the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90), the term related to $\muX$ and $\muY$ (more precisely, their estimators) is missing. It is also unusual to assume that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zeroes, i.e., the data is already centered in the RKHS. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is at the heart of CGMMN, it is desirable that the authors provide clarifications for this missing term, as well as how to deal with it in gradient computation and real experiments.

Additionally, the experimental results could be improved by providing more comparisons with other conditional generative methods, such as conditional GANs. This would help to better understand the strengths and weaknesses of CGMMN.

There are also some minor issues, such as typos ("Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238)).

Overall, the paper presents a new and neat approach to learning conditional distributions, but some clarifications and improvements are needed to make it more convincing.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a new approach to learning conditional distributions, which is a significant contribution to the field.
* The technical details of the CGMMN algorithm are well-explained, and the experimental results demonstrate its potential in various applications.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental results are not always competitive with the state-of-the-art algorithms.
* There are some concerns about the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ and the assumption that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zeroes.
* More comparisons with other conditional generative methods are needed to better understand the strengths and weaknesses of CGMMN.
