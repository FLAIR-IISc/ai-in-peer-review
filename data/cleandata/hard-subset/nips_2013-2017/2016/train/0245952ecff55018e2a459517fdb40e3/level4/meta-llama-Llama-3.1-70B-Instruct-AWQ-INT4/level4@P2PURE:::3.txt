This paper proposes a novel loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending the generative moment matching networks approach. The method leverages conditional kernel mean embeddings to construct the loss function, which is based on the maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on various generative tasks, including classification, generating handwritten digits and faces, and distilling knowledge in Bayesian models. 

Having reviewed this paper for the second time, I noticed that the authors have addressed the minor concerns raised in the previous review, including the choice of a low-dimensional space, scalability to higher-dimensional latent spaces, and the utility of the model compared to others. However, I still have some clarifying questions. Specifically, I found it unclear how to map Equation (2) onto the weighted MMD form, as described in Remark 1. The dot notation used in this context is ambiguous, and it would be helpful to specify whether it represents matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product. Providing more details on this mapping would improve the readability of the paper.

Additionally, the choice of notation for the constant C seems unfortunate, given its use in defining the conditional embedding. The authors should consider using a more distinct notation to avoid potential confusion. Furthermore, while Theorems 1 and 2 are properly referenced, Theorem 3 lacks a reference to a proof or the proof itself. It is essential to provide a clear attribution or a detailed proof for this theorem.

The empirical results presented in the paper are decent, and the authors have adequately addressed previous concerns regarding the application of their model to supervised learning. The demonstration of their approach's versatility is reasonable, and the application to distilled Bayesian inference is intriguing. However, the limited scope of this experiment, using only one dataset and one model, raises questions about its generalizability. I believe this approach is more suitable for pure generative modeling, and the experiments in this context are comparable to those in the original GMM paper. The use of digit and face datasets is sufficient to demonstrate the feasibility of the idea. Nevertheless, to encourage broader adoption, the authors should provide compelling results on more challenging datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
