- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMNs) with a novel Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) loss extending GMMNs to conditional distributions.  
- CMMD is derived from conditional kernel mean embedding theory and provides a principled nonparametric discrepancy for conditionals.  
- Demonstrates versatility across tasks: classification, contextual image generation (digits and faces), and distillation of Bayesian models.  
- Authors addressed prior minor concerns (choice of low-dimensional latent space, scalability, and utility) from an earlier review cycle.  
- Strength — originality and soundness: a theoretically grounded, logical extension of GMMNs to conditional modeling.  
- Strength — versatility: a single framework successfully applied to diverse applications, showing wide potential.  
- Strength — clarity: paper is generally well-written with clear links to MMD and kernel mean embedding literature.  
- Weakness — Remark 1 unclear: authors should clarify notation (e.g., Tr(Ld · 1) and whether · is standard or Hadamard multiplication) and detail the derivation and weighting matrices (C1,C2,C3) to avoid confusion with C.  
- Weakness — missing proof/citation for Theorem 3: must provide a proof or cite prior work showing equality of conditional embedding operators implies equality of conditional distributions.  
- Weakness — limited experiments for Bayesian distillation: evaluated only on Boston Housing with one teacher (PBP); needs more datasets or different Bayesian models to show general utility.  
- Weakness — limited generative benchmarking: MNIST and Yale Faces suffice as proof of concept, but stronger results on challenging benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) are needed to demonstrate broader impact.  
- Final assessment: solid, elegant contribution with sound theory and convincing proof-of-concept experiments; clarifications and stronger, broader empirical validation would significantly increase impact.
