This paper builds upon the deep generative modeling approach of “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” and presents an extension for conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing optimization using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears technically sound, and the proposals are well presented. (While I did not verify all equations in full detail.) In terms of novelty, extending the generative moment matching framework to a conditional setting is a natural step, but the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not immediately obvious (at least to me). Thus, the paper demonstrates both novelty and usefulness. The derived framework should be readily applicable, as shown in Algorithm 1, and will likely be relevant to researchers interested in deep generative models or distilling complex statistical processes. The paper is well written, clear, and easy to read, with a useful appendix providing further details on the methods and experiments. 

The central idea is to learn a conditional distribution *p(y|x)* given input variables *x*. Previous work has shown success in deep generative modeling using maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) to characterize joint distributions. This paper extends this by introducing conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN), trained using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion. The learning process leverages stochastic gradient descent with gradients calculated via back-propagation. The authors evaluate CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge transfer – distilling knowledge from a Bayesian model into a smaller CGMMN student network. Their results demonstrate competitive performance across all tasks.

The introduction effectively contextualizes the work within the broader landscape of deep generative models, referencing GANs [5] and previous moment matching networks [19, 3]. It clearly motivates the need for conditional generative models in scenarios like predictive modeling, contextual generation, and building larger probabilistic models.  The presentation of the three application areas is concise and persuasive.

The paper rigorously develops the theoretical foundation for CGMMN. The preliminary section provides a clear review of Hilbert space embedding and MMD, establishing the necessary background for understanding the proposed CMMD. The derivation of the CMMD objective (Section 3.1) is well-explained, and Theorem 3 provides a crucial link between the embedding of conditional distributions and the equality of the distributions themselves. The network architecture (Section 3.2) is straightforward and utilizes standard deep learning techniques, making it easily implementable.

The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering a variety of tasks and datasets. The results on MNIST and SVHN for predictive modeling are competitive with state-of-the-art methods. The qualitative results on MNIST and the Yale Face dataset demonstrate the ability of CGMMN to generate realistic samples. The application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation is particularly interesting and showcases the potential of CGMMN to compress complex Bayesian models.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper is built on a solid theoretical foundation, with clear derivations and justifications.
*   **Clarity:** The writing is clear, concise, and well-organized. The paper is easy to follow, even for readers unfamiliar with the specific details of MMD or generative modeling.
*   **Novelty:**  While building upon existing work, the conditional extension of GMMN and the derivation of CMMD represent a novel contribution.
*   **Significance:** The framework has the potential to be useful in a variety of applications, including predictive modeling, data generation, and knowledge distillation.  The experiments demonstrate competitive performance on diverse tasks.
*   **Completeness:** The inclusion of an appendix with additional details enhances the reproducibility and understanding of the work.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Analysis of Hyperparameters:** The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameter settings (e.g., kernel bandwidth, regularization strength).
*   **Computational Cost:** While Algorithm 1 addresses mini-batch training, a more detailed discussion of the computational cost of CMMD compared to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs) would be valuable.
*   **Comparison with Other Conditional Generative Models:** A more direct comparison with recent conditional generative models, such as conditional VAEs [28], could further highlight the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed approach.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel extension to existing generative modeling techniques.
*   The experimental results are promising and demonstrate competitive performance on a variety of tasks.
*   The framework is clearly explained and potentially useful to a wide range of researchers.
*   The paper is well-written and easy to understand.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   The analysis of hyperparameters and computational cost could be more thorough.
*   A more direct comparison with other conditional generative models would strengthen the paper.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend accepting this paper. The technical contributions are significant, the presentation is excellent, and the experimental results are compelling. The minor weaknesses identified above do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. This paper represents a valuable addition to the literature on deep generative modeling and is likely to be of interest to a broad audience within the NIPS community.
