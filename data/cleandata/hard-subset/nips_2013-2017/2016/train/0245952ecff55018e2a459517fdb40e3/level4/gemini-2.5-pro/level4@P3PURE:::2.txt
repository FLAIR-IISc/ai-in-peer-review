This paper extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting. This extension is non-trivial. Unlike in models where conditioning can be achieved by simply feeding the conditional variable as an additional network input, the moment matching framework poses a unique challenge. The core difficulty lies in matching a mini-batch of generated samples to a corresponding set of target distributions, where each target is conditioned on a different input. Consequently, the target distributions within a mini-batch are no longer identical, which complicates the distribution matching objective. A naive approach of using the same conditional input for an entire mini-batch would be computationally inefficient.

The primary weakness of the paper is its presentation. It immediately delves into formal mathematics (e.g., Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Spaces) to introduce its main objective, equation (2), without first building an intuition for the formula. This is in stark contrast to the original, non-conditional moment matching paper, where the objective function is intuitive. The paper would be significantly strengthened by providing an equally clear and intuitive explanation for the conditional case.

Furthermore, the paper does not clearly delineate the path from the theoretical objective in equation (2) to a practical algorithm. Key details are missing regarding the concrete computational steps, their efficiency, and the assumptions required for implementation. This makes it difficult for the reader to understand what computations are actually performed. While some of this information is in the supplement, it is hard to follow.

To improve the paper, I would like the authors to address the following questions:
- How can the objective function in equation (2) be derived or explained from an intuitive standpoint?
- What are the precise steps required to translate equation (2) into the practical algorithm? A clear breakdown of the computations would be beneficial.
