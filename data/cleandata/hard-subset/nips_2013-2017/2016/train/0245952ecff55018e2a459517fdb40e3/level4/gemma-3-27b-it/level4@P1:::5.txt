The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to model conditional distributions instead of joint distributions. The key idea is to embed these conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, building upon the technique introduced by Song et al. (2009). The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is empirically evaluated on several real datasets. There appears to be a missing term in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90) related to $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (more precisely, their estimators). Furthermore, the assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero – implying the data is already centered in the RKHS – is unusual. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to CGMMN, clarification regarding this missing term, as well as its handling during gradient computation and in the experiments, would be beneficial.

The proposed approach is novel and well-motivated, but the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. Regarding predictive performance, CGMMN exhibits higher error rates than the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMVA on both datasets. For generative performance, the lack of comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional generative adversarial networks) makes it difficult to assess whether CGMMN generates superior samples. The paper contains minor typographical errors, such as "Frobenius norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.
*   Sound theoretical foundation based on kernel methods and Hilbert space embedding.
*   Demonstrated applicability to diverse tasks (predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge).

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Experimental results do not consistently outperform strong baselines.
*   Lack of comparison to other conditional generative models.
*   Potential issue with the formulation of the conditional embedding operator $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$.
*   Minor typographical errors.
