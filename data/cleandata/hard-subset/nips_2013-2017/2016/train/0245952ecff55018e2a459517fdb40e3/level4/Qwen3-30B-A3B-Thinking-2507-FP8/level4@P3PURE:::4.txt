

This paper extends the deep generative modeling framework "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" to enable conditional generation. The authors derive a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, enabling optimization via the same machinery as the unconditional variant. The proposed approach demonstrates competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model. The technical content is sound, and the proposals are clearly presented; the reviewer verified the core arguments but did not scrutinize all equations in full detail. While the conditional extension idea is conceptually straightforward, the derivation and mechanism for the empirical estimator are non-trivial, lending the paper meaningful novelty. The framework is straightforward to implement, as evidenced by Algorithm 1, suggesting broad utility for researchers employing deep generative models or seeking to distill complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and accessible, with the supplementary appendix providing valuable methodological and experimental details.
