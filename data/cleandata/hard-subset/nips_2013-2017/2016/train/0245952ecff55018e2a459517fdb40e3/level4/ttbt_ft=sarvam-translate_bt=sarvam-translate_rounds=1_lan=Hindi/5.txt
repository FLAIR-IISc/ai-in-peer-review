The authors extend the Generative Moment-Matching Network (GMMN) by considering a matching conditional distribution instead of a joint distribution. The main idea is to embed a conditional distribution in a regenerative kernel Hilbert space, a technique proposed by Song et al. in 2009. The proposed algorithm CGMMN has been empirically evaluated on several real datasets. In the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (line 90), the term related to $\muX$ and $\muY$ (more precisely, their estimates) is missing. It is also unusual to assume that $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero, i.e., the data is already centered in the RKHs. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is at the center of the CGMMN, it is desirable that the authors provide an explanation for this missing term, as well as how to deal with it in gradient computation and real experiments. The proposed approach is new and clean, but the experimental results are somehow disappointing. In terms of predictive performance, on both datasets, the CGMMN gives a larger error rate than the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMA. In terms of generative performance, since no comparison has been provided between CGMMN and other conditional generative methods (e.g., Conditional Generative Adversary Net), it is not clear whether the proposed algorithm can generate better samples.
