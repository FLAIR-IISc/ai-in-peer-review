The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional setting. While extending existing methods to handle single-example updates is often trivial – simply feeding the conditional information into the network – this proves non-trivial here. This is because the task requires matching distributions across a large number of samples (a mini-batch) to a large number of inputs, and these distributions are no longer identical unless the same conditional input is used for every update, which would be highly inefficient. 

The primary weakness of the paper is its presentation. The authors begin with complex mathematical formalism instead of first providing the intuition behind their approach. They fail to clearly delineate the actual computations performed by the algorithm and to discuss their computational efficiency. The extension to the conditional case lacks the clear, intuitive explanation present in the original, non-conditional work. 

Specifically, the paper does not adequately explain the details of the optimized formula (eq (2)). Key questions remain unanswered: how efficient is the optimization, what assumptions are necessary for it to function, and what are the concrete computational steps involved? While some details are relegated to the supplement, they are difficult to follow. 

The original, non-conditional version of the algorithm is explained effectively without resorting to advanced mathematical concepts like reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces; the minimized formula is readily intuitive. A similar intuitive explanation would significantly benefit the presentation of the conditional case. To improve clarity, the authors should address the following:

* How would one intuitively arrive at formula (2)?
* What are the specific steps connecting formula (2) to the practical algorithm implementation?
