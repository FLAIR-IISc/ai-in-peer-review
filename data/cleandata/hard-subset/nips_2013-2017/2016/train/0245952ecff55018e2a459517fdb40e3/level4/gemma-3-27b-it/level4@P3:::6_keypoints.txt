- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) extending GMMNs to conditional generation using kernel embeddings and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.  
- Core idea: match the model to the conditional embedding operator, treating all conditional distributions as a family to avoid data sparsity from per-condition estimation.  
- CMMD objective is elegant, compatible with backpropagation, and benefits from kernel matrices that are independent of model parameters.  
- Theoretical grounding is strong with clear connections to MMD, GMMNs, and kernel methods.  
- Demonstrates practical effectiveness across predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Experiments are diverse and show competitive performance; method is versatile beyond standard classification tasks.  
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are dense and need clearer, more pedagogical explanations for readers unfamiliar with kernel methods.  
- Computational cost concern: CMMD requires matrix inversions and potential expensive operations; mitigation strategies (approximations, scalable minibatch techniques) need more thorough discussion.  
- Practicality of large minibatches with expensive matrix inverses is not fully addressed despite K-matrix independence.  
- Missing baseline comparison: no empirical comparison to the “naive” approach of training separate GMMNs per condition (e.g., on discrete-conditioned datasets like MNIST).  
- Minor factual errors: claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect and should be fixed.  
- Inconsistency in the conditional embedding operator definition (line 90) relative to cited reference [29]; needs clarification.  
- Recommendation: overall positive—recommend acceptance contingent on addressing clarity issues, computational discussion, baseline experiments, and the noted corrections.
