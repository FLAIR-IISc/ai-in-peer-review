This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a deep generative model that extends the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to conditional distributions. The authors derive a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing for optimization using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model is demonstrated with competitive performance on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model.

The paper appears to be technically sound, and the proposals are well-presented. The derivation of the empirical estimator for the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion is a notable contribution, as it is not immediately obvious. The authors provide a clear and well-written explanation of the proposed framework, making it easy to follow and understand.

The CGMMN framework is evaluated on several tasks, including predictive modeling on the MNIST and SVHN datasets, contextual generation on the MNIST and Yale Face datasets, and distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model on the Boston housing dataset. The results show that CGMMN is competitive with state-of-the-art models, including Variational Auto-encoders (VA), Max-margin Deep Generative Models (MMVA), and Deeply-supervised Nets (DSN).

The paper is well-organized, and the appendix provides useful additional information regarding the details of the methods used and the experiments. The writing is clear, and the authors provide sufficient background information to make the paper accessible to readers who may not be familiar with the GMMN framework or kernel mean embeddings.

In terms of novelty, while the idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment matching framework is natural, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not obvious. The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of deep generative models, and the proposed framework is likely to be useful and relevant to researchers and practitioners interested in using deep models for generative purposes or to distill more elaborate statistical processes.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel extension to the GMMN framework, allowing for conditional distributions.
* The derivation of the empirical estimator for the CMMD criterion is a significant contribution.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
* The results demonstrate competitive performance on various tasks.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations and potential applications of the proposed framework.
* Some of the equations and derivations could be further clarified or expanded upon.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel and significant extension to the GMMN framework.
* The derivation of the empirical estimator for the CMMD criterion is a notable contribution.
* The results demonstrate competitive performance on various tasks.
* The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
