- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective for learning conditional distributions.  
- CGMMN generates samples by passing conditional variables and noise through a neural network trained to minimize CMMD via SGD/backprop.  
- The work is a natural and significant extension of GMMN to the conditional setting, broadening applicability to prediction, structured output, and contextual generation.  
- CMMD is a principled, theoretically grounded extension of MMD, leveraging conditional mean embeddings and offering simpler, more stable training than adversarial GAN objectives.  
- Experiments show versatility across predictive modeling, conditional image generation, and distilling Bayesian models ("Bayesian dark knowledge").  
- Clarification requested about the empirical estimator for the conditional embedding operator Ĉ_Y|X and whether RKHS centering or omission of mean terms affects gradients.  
- Predictive performance on MNIST and SVHN is promising but slightly below the state-of-the-art CMMVA in the authors' comparisons.  
- Conditional image generation results are qualitatively interesting but lack direct comparison to key baselines such as Conditional GANs, limiting assessment of generative quality.  
- Minor issues: a few typos (e.g., "an universal RKHS") and small presentation fixes needed.  
- Pro-accept: novel, theoretically sound extension with simpler/stable training and broad applicability.  
- Con-accept: minor theoretical clarity issue and experimental results not consistently state-of-the-art or compared to critical baselines.  
- After rebuttal, authors resolved technical ambiguity and contextualized experiments; reviewer raised score and now strongly recommends acceptance.
