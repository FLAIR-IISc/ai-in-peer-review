This paper introduces a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables. The method is a natural extension of generative moment matching networks (GMMNs), which utilize an unconditional loss function based on the maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion. The authors leverage the theory of conditional kernel mean embeddings to formulate the conditional loss. The proposed approach is demonstrated on a variety of tasks, including classification, generation of handwritten digits and faces, and knowledge distillation in Bayesian models.

Having reviewed a previous version of this manuscript, I can confirm that the authors have addressed the minor issues raised during the last cycle. These included questions about the use of a low-dimensional latent space, the model's scalability to higher-dimensional latent spaces, its utility compared to other models, and other miscellaneous points requiring clarification. The current text appears to answer these points directly.

However, a few areas still require improvement:

1.  In Remark 1, the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear. The dot notation used is ambiguous; it should be specified whether it represents matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product. Providing more detail in this derivation would make the connection much more obvious.

2.  The choice of the letter 'C' for the constant in the weighted MMD is potentially confusing, as 'C' is also used to denote the conditional embedding. Using a different symbol would prevent ambiguity.

3.  While Theorems 1 and 2 are appropriately cited, Theorem 3 lacks a reference. The authors must either provide a citation to a paper containing the proof or include a proof in the appendix.

The empirical results are decent. While the application to supervised learning was previously noted as perhaps not being the model's strongest use case, I accept the authors' justification that it serves to highlight the framework's versatility. The application to distilled Bayesian inference is interesting; however, as it is demonstrated on only a single dataset and model, it is difficult to assess its general utility.

In my view, the method shows the most promise in the domain of pure generative modeling. The experiments on digit and face generation yield results comparable to those of the original GMMN paper, which is sufficient to demonstrate that the proposed conditional extension works. For the method to see wider adoption by the community, however, it would need to show compelling results on more challenging datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
