This paper extends the “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” framework to the conditional setting. The authors derive a conditional kernel embedding of moments so that the conditional distribution can be trained with essentially the same optimization tools as in the unconditional case. The method is evaluated with solid, competitive results on predictive modeling, conditional sample generation, and distilling the predictions of a Bayesian model.

The work appears technically sound and the presentation is clear. (I did not verify every equation in detail.) Although moving from an unconditional to a conditional version is a natural step, the specific construction and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious, and here the paper provides both novelty and practical value. The resulting framework seems straightforward to implement—Algorithm 1 makes the procedure explicit—and should be useful to practitioners building deep conditional generative models or distilling complex probabilistic models. The writing is lucid and well organized, and the appendix supplies helpful details for both the methodology and experiments.
