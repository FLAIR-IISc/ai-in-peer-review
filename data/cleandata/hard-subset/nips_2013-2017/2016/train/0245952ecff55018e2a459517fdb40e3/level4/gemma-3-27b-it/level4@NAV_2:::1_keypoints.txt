- Introduces conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN) using a conditional MMD (CMMD) criterion, extending GMMN to conditional modeling.  
- Evaluated on standard benchmarks to test both discriminative and generative capabilities.  
- Presentation is reasonably clear, though mathematical details can be challenging to follow.  
- Experimental tasks are relatively small-scale but comparable to prior recent work.  
- Training involves computationally expensive steps; the paper lacks runtime comparisons (e.g., vs DSN).  
- Public release of reference code is recommended to improve reproducibility.  
- Provides a thorough background on Hilbert space embeddings, MMD, and kernel conditional embeddings.  
- CMMD criterion is well-motivated and leverages kernel methods to avoid strong distributional assumptions.  
- Authors clearly motivate conditional modeling with practical applications (predictive modeling, contextual generation, large networks).  
- Strength: theoretically sound method and clear presentation of the CMMD criterion.  
- Strength: empirical validation shows competitive performance across diverse tasks.  
- Strength: demonstrates flexibility by using both MLP and CNN architectures.  
- Experimental results: competitive on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- Weakness: assumptions required for CMMD to be well-defined are not fully discussed or empirically examined.  
- Weakness: limited scope of experiments; lacks evaluation on larger, more challenging datasets.  
- Weakness: comparisons omit more recent/advanced generative models (e.g., modern VAEs).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational cost and scalability, especially for kernel computations.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable and novel contribution deserving acceptance if concerns about experimental scope, computational cost, and code availability are addressed.
