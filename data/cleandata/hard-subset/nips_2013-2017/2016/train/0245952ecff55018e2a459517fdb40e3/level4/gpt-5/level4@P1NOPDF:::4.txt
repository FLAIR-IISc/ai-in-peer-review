This paper builds on Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) by deriving a conditional extension for deep generative modeling. The key contribution is a kernel embedding of the moments that characterize the conditional distribution, formulated so the model can be trained with essentially the same machinery used in the unconditional case.

Empirically, the method is demonstrated with reasonably competitive performance across three settings: predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears technically sound and is well presented, though I did not verify every equation in full detail.

Regarding novelty, while extending the GMMN framework to conditional generation is a natural direction, the specific mechanism and the derivation of the empirical estimator were not obvious to me. In this respect, the paper provides genuine novelty and practical value.

The proposed framework looks straightforward to apply in practice—Algorithm 1 makes the procedure clear—and should be useful to researchers and practitioners interested in deep generative models or in distilling more elaborate statistical processes into tractable models.

Overall, the paper is clearly written and easy to follow. The appendix is helpful, offering fuller methodological details and experimental specifications.
