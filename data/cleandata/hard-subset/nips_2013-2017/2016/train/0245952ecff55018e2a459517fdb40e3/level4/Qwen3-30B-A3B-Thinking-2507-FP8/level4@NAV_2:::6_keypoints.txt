- Introduces CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional generation/prediction via conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) using kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Empirical evaluation on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation shows competitive performance.  
- Addresses naive conditional modeling (separate GMMN per condition) which suffers severe data sparsity; CGMMN models the family of conditionals via a conditional embedding operator instead.  
- Reviewer requests a baseline comparison to the naive per-condition GMMN, especially for discrete conditions (e.g., 10-class MNIST) where naive may be viable.  
- CMMD objective is well-formulated and computationally efficient because kernel matrices are independent of model parameters, enabling straightforward backpropagation and echoing unconditional MMD advantages.  
- Potential limitation: CMMD may need large minibatches to reduce stochasticity and can be computationally expensive due to matrix inverses on large datasets.  
- Experimental results for classification and Bayesian distillation are promising and demonstrate practical utility.  
- Presentation clarity issues: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 require clearer explanations; reliance on referenced papers to follow technical details.  
- Two technical issues to fix: (1) the paper’s claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect, and (2) the conditional embedding operator definition (line 90) is inconsistent with reference [29] and needs clarification regarding the cross-covariance operator.  
- Overall assessment: CGMMN is a valuable, theoretically sound, and practically implementable extension of moment-matching networks for conditional modeling.
