- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional generation via kernel embedding of conditional moments.
- Derives a kernel-based empirical estimator for conditional distributions, enabling optimization with the same machinery as unconditional GMMN.
- Demonstrates competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation from Bayesian models.
- Technically sound with a clear, thorough explanation of Hilbert space embeddings and estimator derivation.
- The empirical estimator derivation is non-obvious and well-executed, indicating strong understanding.
- The conditional extension is a natural idea but implemented in a unique, easy-to-apply way (Algorithm 1).
- Paper is well-written, clear, and supplemented by a useful appendix with methodological and experimental details.
- Strength: novel conditional extension to the generative moment-matching framework.
- Strength: clear theoretical background and rigorous estimator derivation.
- Strength: demonstrated competitive experimental performance across tasks.
- Strength: readability and completeness of details.
- Weakness: insufficient comparison with other state-of-the-art conditional generation methods.
- Weakness: some experimental results (e.g., generated samples) are hard to evaluate without additional context or visualizations.
- For acceptance: provides a valuable, novel extension with clear theory and competitive results.
- Against acceptance: limited perceived novelty (natural extension) and some unclear experimental evaluation.
- Overall recommendation: accept.
