

The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea embeds conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), leveraging techniques from Song et al. (2009). The proposed conditional GMMN (CGMMN) is evaluated empirically on real datasets. However, a critical issue arises in the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90), where the term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (and their estimators) is omitted. It is also unusual to assume $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero, i.e., that the data is already centered in the RKHS. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to CGMMN, the authors must clarify: (1) the rationale for omitting these terms, (2) how this omission affects gradient computation, and (3) how it is handled in practical implementations. Without these clarifications, the theoretical foundation of the method remains incomplete.

While the approach is novel and well-motivated, the experimental results are underwhelming. For predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets. Regarding generative performance, the paper lacks comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), leaving it unclear whether CGMMN produces superior samples. The absence of such benchmarks undermines the claimed advantages of the method.

The paper contains several typographical errors: "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238). These should be corrected for professionalism.
