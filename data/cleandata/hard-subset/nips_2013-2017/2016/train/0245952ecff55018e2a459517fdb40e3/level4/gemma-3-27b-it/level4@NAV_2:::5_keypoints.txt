- Proposal: Introduces conditional GMMNs (CGMMN) to learn conditional distributions by leveraging kernel mean embeddings (building on Song et al., 2009).
- Technical concern: Definition of \hat{C}_{Y|X} may omit terms involving μ_X and μ_Y; the assumption that μ_X and μ_Y are zero (centering in RKHS) needs clarification and discussion of gradient/experimental implications.
- Empirical scope: Evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark-knowledge distillation.
- Empirical performance: Results are underwhelming — CGMMN yields higher error rates on MNIST and SVHN than CMMVA (Li et al., 2016).
- Missing baselines: No direct comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), making generative performance hard to assess.
- Positives: Novel, well-motivated extension with a solid theoretical/kernel-embedding foundation and demonstrated applicability across tasks.
- Negatives: Not state-of-the-art empirically, potential formulation/assumption bug, and minor typographical errors.
