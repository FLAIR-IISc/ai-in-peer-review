- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation and prediction.  
- Core innovation: kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) metric.  
- Empirical evaluation on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Naive extension (separate GMMNs per condition) suffers severe data sparsity for continuous/high-cardinality conditions.  
- CGMMN remedy: model all conditionals as a unified family and match the conditional embedding operator, avoiding per-condition estimation.  
- However, the naive baseline may suffice for discrete low-cardinality conditions (e.g., MNIST); a direct baseline comparison is missing.  
- Strength: CMMD formulation keeps all K matrices independent of model parameters, enabling straightforward backpropagation and tractability.  
- Major concern: CMMD’s minibatch requirements — small minibatches cause noisy estimates, large minibatches are computationally costly (matrix inversions).  
- Experimental results for classification and Bayesian distillation are promising, showing practical utility.  
- Presentation issues: sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and rely heavily on external papers; a clearer, self-contained exposition is needed.  
- Two technical errors to fix: (1) claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect for finite samples; (2) the paper’s conditional embedding operator conflicts with [29] and requires an explicit, justified cross-covariance operator definition.  
- Overall: principled and valuable contribution with evident potential, but needs naive-baseline comparisons, resolution of computational trade-offs, and fixes to presentation and technical inconsistencies.
