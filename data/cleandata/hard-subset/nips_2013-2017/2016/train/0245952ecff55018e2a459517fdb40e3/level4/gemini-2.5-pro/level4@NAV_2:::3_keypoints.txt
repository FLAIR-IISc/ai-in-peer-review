- Novelty: Proposes CGMMN, extending GMMN to conditional settings via a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) grounded in kernel mean embedding theory.  
- Versatility: Demonstrated on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written and addresses prior review concerns about model design.  
- Theoretical gap: Theorem 3, central to CMMD, is stated without proof or citation and must be proven or attributed.  
- Exposition issue: Remark 1’s intuition for CMMD is hard to follow; notation (dot product vs. Hadamard, use of ‘C’) is ambiguous and needs clarification.  
- Limited generative evaluation: Results on MNIST and Yale Faces are convincing but not more challenging than prior GMMN work; lacks benchmarks like CIFAR-10 or LSUN.  
- Limited distillation evaluation: Bayesian distillation shown on only one dataset and one teacher model, limiting assessment of general utility.  
- Pros for acceptance: Novel, theoretically motivated method; demonstrated versatility; fills gap by extending GMMN to conditional modeling.  
- Cons for acceptance: Insufficiently challenging empirical benchmarks and a missing proof/citation for a key theoretical result.  
- Overall: Solid, addressable contribution—conditional MMD loss is a valuable addition and the Bayesian distillation application is promising.
