- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions.  
- Develops a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion and shows matching conditional embedding operators implies matching conditional distributions.  
- Proposes a neural generative architecture taking conditional inputs and noise, trainable via backpropagation.  
- Demonstrates competitive empirical results on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, Boston Housing).  
- Strength: well-motivated extension to conditional distributions with a clean architecture and diverse experimental evaluation.  
- Strength: effectively addresses predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge use cases.  
- Weakness: presentation is overly technical—Section 3.1 introduces Hilbert-space embeddings without intuitive motivation for CMMD.  
- Weakness: lack of clear connection between theoretical CMMD objective and practical implementation/gradient computation.  
- Weakness: algorithm (Algorithm 1) omits details on computing conditional embeddings and how kernel matrices relate to the network.  
- Critical weakness: computational efficiency concerns—CMMD requires matrix inverses scaling cubically with sample size; mini-batch solution is not analyzed.  
- Missing analysis: no quantification of mini-batch computational savings or discussion of how it affects theoretical guarantees.  
- Requested improvements: add intuitive motivation for CMMD, map CMMD to gradient updates, discuss scalability/efficiency, and clarify theory-to-implementation assumptions.  
- Key questions for authors: why match the conditional embedding operator rather than conditional distributions directly; how to explain CMMD to non-kernel readers; practical steps from CMMD to gradients in Algorithm 1.  
- Overall: technically sound and promising contribution, but current excessive formalism and lack of practical detail obscure its impact; clarity and integration of supplementary material needed.
