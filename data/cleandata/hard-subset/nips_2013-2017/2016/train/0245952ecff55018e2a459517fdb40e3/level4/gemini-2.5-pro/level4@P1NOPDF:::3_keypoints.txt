- Paper proposes a conditional loss for directed generative models using conditional kernel mean embeddings, extending GMMs/MMD.  
- Authors demonstrated effectiveness on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Previous minor concerns have been addressed: justification for low-dimensional latent space, scaling to higher dimensions, model utility, and clarifications.  
- Clarity needed on mapping in Remark 1 from Eq.(2) to the weighted MMD form; a detailed derivation is requested.  
- Ambiguity in the dot notation in the weighted MMD expression—specify whether it denotes matrix multiplication or Hadamard product.  
- Notation conflict: using `C` in the weighted MMD is confusing since `C` already denotes the conditional embedding operator.  
- Theorem 3 is presented without citation or proof; authors must provide a reference or include a proof (main text or appendix).  
- Empirical results are solid and justify including a supervised application to show versatility, though that use case is not the most compelling.  
- Distilled Bayesian inference application is interesting but demonstrated on only one dataset/model, limiting assessment of general utility.  
- Main strength appears to be in pure generative modeling; digit and face experiments match original GMM results and provide a viable proof of concept.  
- To encourage wider adoption and demonstrate competitiveness, the method must be scaled to harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
