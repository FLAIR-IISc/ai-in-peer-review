This paper proposes an extension of Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) to conditional generation and prediction tasks, termed Conditional GMMNs (CGMMNs). The core of this method lies in the kernel embedding of conditional distributions and the Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) metric, which measures the discrepancy between conditional distributions. The authors evaluate the proposed CGMMN on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models. 

A straightforward approach to extending GMMNs to conditional settings would involve estimating a separate GMMN for each conditional distribution, with shared parameters through a common neural network. However, this approach suffers from data sparsity issues, as each conditional distribution often has limited examples, particularly when dealing with continuous conditioning variables. In contrast, the proposed CGMMN treats all conditional distributions as a family and directly matches the model with the conditional embedding operator, rather than individual distributions. 

While the advantages of this approach are apparent, the naive method may still perform reasonably well in certain scenarios, such as conditional generation with discrete conditioning variables (e.g., MNIST). A comparison with this baseline approach would be a valuable addition. The formulation of the conditional MMD objective is elegant and computationally efficient, as noted in Remark 1. The K matrices in equation 2 are independent of model parameters, allowing for backpropagation similar to the unconditional case. 

However, one concern with the proposed approach is that the CMMD objective may require large minibatches to mitigate stochasticity, which can lead to computationally expensive matrix inverses. Nevertheless, the CMMD provides a promising framework for modeling conditional distributions. The experimental results on classification and Bayesian model distillation are also noteworthy. 

Some minor issues were encountered: the presentation in sections 2.3 and 3.1 could be improved for better clarity. Additionally, there are two errors: (1) the claim of an unbiased MMD estimator on line 77 is incorrect, and (2) the conditional embedding operator on line 90 is inconsistent with the definition in reference [29], potentially requiring a revised definition of the cross-covariance operator.
