- Summary: Introduces Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) training a generator y = f(x,h;w) by minimizing a conditional MMD (CMMD) between empirical and model conditional distributions using kernel Gram matrices.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on kernel mean embeddings/conditional operators (Song et al.) and GMMN (Li et al.); complements cGANs, CVAEs, and discriminative DGMs.

- Strength: Principled extension: replacing MMD with CMMD aligns with kernel embedding theory.

- Strength: Practical training: single-sided, differentiable objective compatible with backprop/SGD (no adversary).

- Strength: Versatile framework applicable to prediction, contextual generation, and Bayesian distillation.

- Strength: Empirical results show competitive classification, plausible class-conditional samples, and promising Bayesian distillation without RMSE degradation.

- Weakness: Lack of intuition for CMMD and Eq. (2); needs a reader-friendly explanation and link to MMD.

- Weakness: Missing clear algorithmic path from Eq. (2) to minibatch computations, including construction of K and L and gradient flow.

- Weakness: Computational complexity concerns: per-minibatch O(b^3) inversion of K+λI—needs discussion of batch sizes, wall-clock costs, and remedies (Nyström, RFF, CG).

- Weakness: Unclear practical implications of theoretical assumptions (universality, existence of operators); need discussion of estimator bias/variance and effects of λ and batch size.

- Weakness: Insufficient detail on kernel choices and hyperparameters (which kernels on X and Y, bandwidth selection, sensitivity analyses).

- Weakness: Empirical generation evaluation is mainly qualitative; lacking quantitative baselines and metrics (cGAN, CVAE, Parzen, classifier accuracy, inception-like scores).

- Weakness: Bayesian distillation reporting is incomplete—need model size, test-time speedup, and calibration metrics (NLL, predictive log-likelihood, ECE) and comparison to dark-knowledge objective.

- Weakness: Baselines may be unfair/incomplete; report NLL/Brier and note data augmentation/multi-model practices.

- Weakness: Missing ablations: noise dimension, x-h combination methods, λ and batch size effects, and kernel variations.

- Quality: Method is technically sound and optimization via backprop is straightforward, but reproducibility suffers from missing algorithmic and hyperparameter details.

- Clarity: Manuscript needs a motivating non-technical derivation of CMMD, an explicit minibatch algorithm box (shapes/costs/gradient dependencies), and consolidated kernel/regularization details.

- Originality: The CMMD-based training for deep conditional models is a novel, nontrivial extension of GMMN and distinct from cGAN/CVAE.

- Significance: Provides a stable, adversary-free objective for conditional generation with potential broad adoption, but needs stronger comparisons and scalability demos.

- Pros (summary): Principled, simple training without min–max, demonstrated across tasks, competitive/class-conditional results.

- Cons (summary): Lacks intuition, algorithmic/complexity details, kernel/hyperparameter specification, quantitative generative evaluation, and full distillation reporting.

- Key requests: Provide intuitive derivation of Eq. (2), per-minibatch computations and gradients with costs, full kernel/hyperparameter specs and sensitivity, and quantitative generation and calibration results.

- Minor comments: Clarify whether multiple y per x are used, suggest feature-space kernels for X to improve conditioning, and fix typos/consistency (e.g., MNIST). 

- Recommendation: Weak accept — sound and useful idea but needs improved clarity, algorithmic detail, complexity analysis, and empirical strengthening.
