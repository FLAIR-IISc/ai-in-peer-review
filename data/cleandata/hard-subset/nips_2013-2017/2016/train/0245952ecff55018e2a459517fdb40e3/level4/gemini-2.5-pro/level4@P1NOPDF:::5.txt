This paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting, matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The key idea is to embed these conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), leveraging a technique introduced by Song et al. (2009). The proposed algorithm, Conditional GMMN (CGMMN), is evaluated empirically on several real-world datasets.

My primary technical concern relates to the definition of the empirical conditional mean embedding operator, $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$, in Line 90. The definition appears to be missing a term related to the mean embeddings $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (or, more precisely, their empirical estimators). It is unusual to implicitly assume that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero, which implies that the data is already centered in the RKHS. Given that $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ is central to the CGMMN algorithm, the authors should clarify this point and explain how this missing term is handled during gradient computation and in the experiments.

While the proposed approach is novel and elegant, the experimental results are disappointing. In terms of predictive performance, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art algorithm CMMVA on both datasets. Regarding generative performance, the paper lacks a comparison with other conditional generative methods, such as conditional generative adversarial networks. Without this comparison, it is difficult to assess whether the proposed algorithm generates higher-quality samples than existing approaches.

Finally, the paper contains some minor typos, for example: "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238).
