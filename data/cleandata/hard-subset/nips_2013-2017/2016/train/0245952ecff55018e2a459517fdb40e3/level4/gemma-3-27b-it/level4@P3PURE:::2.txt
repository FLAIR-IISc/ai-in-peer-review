The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional setting. While extending to a single-example update is trivial for many models (simply feeding the condition into the network), this is non-trivial here. This is because the method requires matching distributions of a mini-batch of samples to a set of inputs, and these distributions are no longer identical under differing conditions – unless the same condition is reused for each update, which would be computationally inefficient. 

The primary weakness of this paper is its presentation. The authors begin with complex mathematical formalism without first providing the intuition behind the core formula. They also fail to clearly delineate the computations performed by the algorithm and assess their efficiency. Specifically, the paper lacks a detailed explanation of the optimization target in equation (2), including the underlying assumptions that enable its function and how it translates to practical computation. The non-conditional version of this algorithm, and its intuitive basis (e.g., using a reproducing kernel Hilbert space), is readily explained in the original paper; a similarly accessible explanation for the conditional case would be highly beneficial. 

To improve clarity, the authors should address the following questions: How does one intuitively arrive at formula (2)? What are the specific steps required to transform formula (2) into a practical algorithm? While some details are provided in the supplement, they are difficult to follow.
