This paper presents Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), a deep generative model for learning conditional distributions p(y|x). The work is a direct and significant extension of the previously proposed Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN), which learn joint distributions. The core technical contribution is the development of a novel objective function, Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD). This criterion measures the discrepancy between two conditional distributions by computing the Hilbert-Schmidt norm between their respective kernel mean embedding operators. As with the unconditional MMD, this objective can be optimized efficiently using stochastic gradient descent and backpropagation. The authors demonstrate the versatility of their framework on a diverse set of applications, including classification (predictive modeling), class-conditional image synthesis (contextual generation), and model compression (distilling knowledge from a Bayesian model).

### **Strengths**

*   **Novelty and Technical Contribution:** While extending an unconditional generative model to a conditional one is a natural research direction, the proposed solution is non-trivial and elegant. The formulation of the CMMD criterion based on the Hilbert-Schmidt norm of conditional embedding operators is a key contribution that provides a theoretically-grounded way to apply moment-matching principles to conditional generation. This work successfully fills a gap, providing a moment-matching counterpart to other conditional deep generative models like Conditional GANs and Conditional VAEs.

*   **Simplicity and Practicality:** A significant strength of the proposed framework is its simple and stable training procedure. Unlike Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), which often require carefully balancing a difficult min-max game, CGMMN training involves minimizing a single, well-defined objective function. The learning algorithm (Algorithm 1) is straightforward to implement and leverages standard backpropagation, making the method highly accessible.

*   **Comprehensive Experimental Validation:** The paper provides a strong empirical evaluation across three distinct and relevant tasks. The experiments on predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation demonstrate the model's flexibility and wide applicability. The results are shown to be competitive with other state-of-the-art deep generative models, effectively validating the proposed approach.

*   **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow. The authors provide a concise yet sufficient background on kernel embeddings before clearly motivating and deriving the CMMD objective. The main ideas are communicated effectively, and the inclusion of a detailed appendix is very helpful for reproducibility and understanding implementation details.

### **Weaknesses**

*   **Comparison with Discriminative Models:** In the predictive modeling experiments (Section 4.1), CGMMN's performance is competitive with other deep generative models (e.g., CMMVA) but lags behind purely discriminative methods like Deeply-Supervised Nets (DSN). While this is somewhat expected, as generative models are not solely optimized for discriminative performance, a brief discussion acknowledging this trade-off between generative capability and raw predictive power would strengthen the paper.

*   **Computational Considerations:** The CMMD objective function (Equation 2) involves the inversion of kernel matrices, an operation that scales cubically with the mini-batch size. Although the use of mini-batches makes the training tractable, this can still be a computational bottleneck compared to the forward/backward passes in GANs or VAEs, especially if larger batch sizes are needed for stable gradient estimates. A discussion of the computational complexity and its practical implications would be a valuable addition.

*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The performance of MMD-based methods often depends on the choice of kernel and its associated hyperparameters (e.g., kernel bandwidth). The paper does not include an analysis of how sensitive the model's performance is to these choices. While space is limited, a brief discussion or an experiment in the appendix showing this sensitivity would improve the paper's completeness.

### **Summary of Evaluation**

*   **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The derivation of the CMMD objective appears correct, and the claims are well-supported by a comprehensive set of experiments. The work is presented as a complete and polished contribution.

*   **Clarity:** The paper is written with excellent clarity. The organization is logical, the notation is consistent, and the core ideas are explained intuitively.

*   **Originality:** The paper makes a novel contribution by extending moment-matching networks to the conditional setting. The formulation of the CMMD objective is original and provides a new tool for the deep generative modeling community. The work is well-situated within the existing literature and clearly distinguishes itself from prior contributions.

*   **Significance:** The results are important and likely to be impactful. By providing a stable and easy-to-train alternative to Conditional GANs, this work is likely to be used by other researchers and practitioners. The demonstrated application to Bayesian dark knowledge is particularly interesting and opens up a promising new use case for this class of models.

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a novel, well-motivated, and practically useful extension to moment-matching networks. The weaknesses are minor and do not detract from the significance of the core contribution.
