This paper proposes conditional generative moment-matching networks (CGMMN), extending generative moment matching networks (GMMN) to model conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The approach leverages recent work on kernel mean embeddings of conditional distributions, offering a principled framework for learning generative models with input-dependent outputs. The authors demonstrate the versatility of CGMMN across several tasks – predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation – with competitive results.

This work builds upon prior research in deep generative modeling, notably the success of GANs [Goodfellow et al., 2014] and the more stable training offered by GMMNs [Li et al., 2015]. The CMMD criterion presented provides an alternative to the min-max optimization of GANs and offers a potentially simpler training process. The extension to conditional distributions addresses a crucial limitation of GMMNs, enabling applications like predictive modeling and controlled generation. 

**Strengths:**

* **Technically Sound:** The paper presents a clear derivation of the CMMD criterion, building upon established concepts from kernel methods and reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces. The use of backpropagation for training is a significant advantage, making the method practical for deep networks.
* **Versatility:**  Demonstrating the model's applicability to predictive modeling, generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation showcases its flexibility.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and provides sufficient detail to understand the proposed method. The connection to prior work is well established. The authors have addressed concerns from previous reviews regarding the latent space dimension and the model's utility beyond supervised learning.
* **Competitive Results:** The experimental results are competitive with existing methods on the tested datasets (MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Clarity – Equation (2) Mapping:** The connection between Equation (2) (the CMMD objective) and the weighted MMD form mentioned in Remark 1 requires further clarification. The use of dot notation is ambiguous and could benefit from explicitly stating whether it represents matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product.
* **Theoretical Justification – Theorem 3:** Theorem 3 states a crucial condition for conditional distribution equivalence, but lacks a citation or proof. Providing a reference or a concise proof would strengthen the theoretical foundation of the work.
* **Limited Scope of Bayesian Distillation:** While the Bayesian dark knowledge distillation experiment is promising, its evaluation is limited to a single dataset and model.  The generalizability of this approach requires further investigation.
* **Limited Evaluation on Complex Datasets:**  The current experiments primarily focus on relatively simple datasets (digits and faces). Demonstrating performance on more challenging datasets like CIFAR-10 or LSUN would significantly strengthen the paper's impact and highlight its scalability.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and technically sound extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.
* The CMMD criterion offers a viable alternative to existing methods for training generative models.
* The experimental results demonstrate competitive performance on several tasks.
* The authors have addressed previous reviewer concerns, indicating a responsiveness to feedback.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The lack of a reference or proof for Theorem 3 weakens the theoretical underpinning.
* The limited evaluation on more complex datasets restricts the assessment of the method’s scalability and generalizability.
* The Bayesian distillation experiment lacks sufficient breadth to establish its broader utility.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of deep generative modeling. The proposed CGMMN framework offers a principled and practical approach to conditional generation. While some minor clarifications and further validation on more complex datasets would strengthen the paper, I believe the contributions are significant enough to warrant acceptance.

**Recommendation:**  **Accept** (with minor revisions addressing the points raised regarding Equation 2 and Theorem 3). The authors should also consider adding a discussion of the limitations of the current evaluation and outlining future research directions, potentially including experiments on more challenging datasets.
