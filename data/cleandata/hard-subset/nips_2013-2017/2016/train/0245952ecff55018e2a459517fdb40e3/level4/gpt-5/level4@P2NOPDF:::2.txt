The paper extends moment matching networks (MMNs) to the conditional setting. Unlike models that update on single examples—where conditioning is trivially handled by feeding the condition as input—this extension is nontrivial. In the conditional case, training must match distributions of many generated samples to many distinct conditioning inputs within a minibatch. These distributions differ across conditions; using the same condition per update would be inefficient and undermines batching.

My main concern is exposition. The paper launches into formalism without first providing intuition or a clear picture of the computations and their efficiency. In particular, the objective that is ultimately optimized (Eq. 2) is insufficiently motivated: the paper does not explain the assumptions that make it tractable, what quantities are actually estimated in a minibatch, or the computational and memory costs of each step. The non-conditional MMN was explained in prior work with minimal RKHS machinery and an intuitive objective; a similarly accessible explanation for the conditional case would substantially improve readability.

Please consider:
- Provide an intuitive derivation of Eq. (2): what conditional moments are being matched, and why this form captures the learning goal.
- Map Eq. (2) to a practical algorithm step by step: how minibatch statistics are formed when each example has a different condition; how many samples per condition are needed; how gradients are computed and backpropagated; and any variance-reduction or approximation used.
- State explicitly the assumptions required (e.g., properties of the kernels or features, independence assumptions, unbiasedness/consistency of the estimators), and discuss their implications.
- Detail computational complexity and memory: per-batch costs (e.g., pairwise terms), whether linear-time approximations, subsampling, or low-rank methods are used, and the trade-offs involved.
- Summarize these steps in the main text (e.g., brief pseudo-code or an algorithm box) rather than relying on a supplement that is hard to follow.

Overall, the technical direction is promising and the conditional extension is meaningful, but the paper needs a clearer, more intuitive presentation of the objective and the concrete training procedure to be fully convincing.
