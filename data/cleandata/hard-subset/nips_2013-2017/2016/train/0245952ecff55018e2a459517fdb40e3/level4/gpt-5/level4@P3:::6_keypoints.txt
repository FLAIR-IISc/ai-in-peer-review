- Proposal: Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model P(Y|X) by minimizing conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) between data and model conditional embeddings.
- Method: Estimates conditional mean embedding operator CY|X from paired (x,y) data using kernel methods and trains a deep generator by backpropagating a CMMD trace-form loss.
- Relation to prior work: Connects kernel mean embeddings, MMD-trained deep generative models (GMMN), and conditional generative frameworks (conditional GANs/VAEs); main novelty is replacing unconditional MMD with CMMD for single-model conditional training.
- Strength — principled objective: CMMD yields a simple, differentiable loss closely mirroring MMD and amenable to backpropagation.
- Strength — theoretical grounding: Uses operator equality in a universal RKHS as a condition for conditional distribution equality (Theorem 3).
- Strength — versatility and results: Same training recipe applies to classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian distillation; competitive classification and effective distillation shown.
- Strength — practicality: Flexible generator architectures (MLP/CNN) and straightforward training loop.
- Weakness — baselines/ablations missing: Lacks comparison to naïve per-class GMMNs, conditional GANs/VAEs for generation, and original “dark knowledge” distillation baseline.
- Weakness — scalability and minibatch sensitivity: CMMD requires inverting Kx+λI per minibatch (O(B^3)) and O(B^2) Gram computations; no quantified cost, memory, or batch-size sensitivity analysis.
- Weakness — kernel/hyperparameter details missing: Needs explicit kernel choices/bandwidth selection for X and Y, clarity on characteristic kernels, and handling of discrete labels.
- Weakness — clarity: Sections on conditional embeddings (2.3, 3.1) are hard to follow; request for self-contained derivation, algorithm box, and intuitive explanation.
- Weakness — quantitative generation evaluation: Paper relies on visual samples; should include Parzen/FID/retrieval/nearest-neighbor metrics.
- Technical — MMD estimator claim: The paper incorrectly labels the V-statistic MMD estimator as unbiased; should use U-statistic or correct to “consistent.”
- Technical — conditional operator estimator/centering: The relation CY|X = CYX C−1XX and empirical estimator omit/obscure centering and regularization details; needs reconciliation or justification for uncentered variant.
- Technical — notation and minor errors: Overloaded symbols (Φ, Υ, K, L) and typos (e.g., “MINIST”, “ReLu”); recommend a notation table and consistency fixes.
- Suggestions: Add naïve conditional-GMMN and conditional GAN/VAE baselines, provide computational complexity and batch-size sensitivity experiments, specify kernel selection procedures, clarify estimator/centering details, and add quantitative generation metrics.
- Overall assessment: CGMMN is a novel, theoretically motivated, and practical alternative to adversarial conditional generators, but would benefit from stronger baselines, scalability analysis, clearer derivations, and more quantitative evaluation.
