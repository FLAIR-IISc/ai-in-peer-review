- Extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) to a conditional setting via kernel embedding of conditional moments.  
- Uses an empirical estimator for the conditional embedding that can be optimized with the same machinery as the unconditional model.  
- Shows competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation from Bayesian models.  
- Framework and derivations appear technically sound and well-presented (author checked overall correctness).  
- Novelty is moderate: a natural progression but the empirical estimator derivation is non-obvious and useful.  
- Algorithm 1 demonstrates the framework is relatively easy to apply in practice.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and the appendix provides valuable methodological and experimental details.  
- Likely to be useful and relevant for researchers using deep generative models or distilling statistical processes.  
- Weakness: conditional extension may be seen as incremental by some readers.  
- Weakness: lacks a more detailed comparison with state-of-the-art methods.  
- Overall rating 8/10 with recommendation to accept subject to minor revisions addressing the weaknesses.
