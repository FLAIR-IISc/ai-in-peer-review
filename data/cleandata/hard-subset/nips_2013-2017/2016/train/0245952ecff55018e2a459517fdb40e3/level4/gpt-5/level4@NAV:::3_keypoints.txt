- Paper proposes a loss for directed conditional generative models by replacing unconditional MMD with a conditional MMD derived from conditional kernel mean embeddings; evaluated on classification, digit/face synthesis, and Bayesian model distillation.

- Prior minor concerns (low-dimensional latent rationale, scalability to higher-dimensional latents, comparative utility, clarifications) appear to be addressed in the revision.

- Remark 1: The mapping from Eq.(2) to the weighted MMD form is unclear—dot notation must be disambiguated (matrix vs. Hadamard product) and the derivation expanded; avoid reusing symbol C.

- Theorems 1 and 2 properly cite prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference; provide a citation or include a proof.

- Empirical results are solid; supervised learning is a reasonable demonstration of versatility though not the most compelling application.

- Bayesian distillation result is interesting but demonstrated on a single dataset/model, so its generality is uncertain; approach likely most promising for purely generative tasks.

- Generative experiments (digits and faces) are on par with original GMMN work, but broader adoption would require stronger results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
