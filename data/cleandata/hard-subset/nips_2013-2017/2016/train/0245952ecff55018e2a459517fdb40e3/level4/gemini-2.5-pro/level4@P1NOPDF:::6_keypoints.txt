- Introduces CGMMNs, extending GMMNs for conditional generation/prediction using kernel embeddings and conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Key idea: match the model’s conditional embedding operator to the data’s operator rather than estimating separate GMMNs per condition.
- This unified approach sidesteps data sparsity issues inherent in per-condition models, especially with continuous conditioning variables.
- For discrete/low-cardinality conditions (e.g., MNIST), the naive per-condition baseline might be reasonable; paper should compare against it.
- CMMD is formulated so computation is almost as easy as unconditional MMD; kernel matrices are parameter-independent, enabling standard backprop.
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches—small batches yield noisy gradient estimates, while large batches are computationally expensive due to matrix inverses.
- Experiments show compelling results on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.
- Presentation could be improved: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and require consulting cited papers.
- Technical correction: claim that the standard MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect—the empirical estimator is biased.
- Technical inconsistency: the paper’s definition of the conditional embedding operator appears inconsistent with reference [29], implying a different cross-covariance operator definition.
