This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion, presenting an interesting extension to the sub-area of deep learning research focused on building generative models. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard small benchmark tasks for discriminative and generative capabilities. 

The paper's text is reasonably clear, although the mathematical derivations can be challenging to follow at times, which is a common issue in papers within this area. The benchmark tasks used in the experiments are somewhat small, but they are not entirely unreasonable when compared to other recent work in this area.

However, there are a few areas that could be improved to strengthen the paper. Firstly, as the algorithm for training the model involves some computationally expensive steps, it would be beneficial to include comments in the experiment section on the training time compared to existing approaches like DSN. This would provide valuable insight into the efficiency of the proposed method.

Furthermore, making the reference code available for the experiments would significantly enhance the paper. Currently, a practitioner may encounter difficulties reproducing the exact models evaluated in the paper, as described in the model section. Providing the code would facilitate replication and extension of the work, thereby increasing its impact and usefulness to the research community.
