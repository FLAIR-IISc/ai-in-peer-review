- Proposes a novel loss for directed conditional generative models by extending generative moment matching networks with conditional kernel mean embeddings (conditional MMD).

- Evaluated on classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and Bayesian model knowledge distillation.

- Reviewer (second-time) notes authors addressed prior minor concerns about low-dimensional embedding choice, scalability to higher-dimensional latents, and comparative utility.

- Clarification requested on mapping from Eq.(2) to weighted MMD: the dot notation is ambiguous (matrix multiplication vs. Hadamard) and should be made explicit.

- Notation critique: using C for a variable is unfortunate given its role in defining the conditional embedding.

- Theorems 1 and 2 are properly cited, but Theorem 3 lacks a proof or citation—author should provide a reference or include the proof.

- Empirical results are satisfactory; inclusion of supervised tasks is reasonably justified as showing model versatility.

- Application to distilled Bayesian inference is intriguing but evaluation is limited to one dataset/model, leaving general usefulness uncertain.

- Most promising application is pure generative modeling; current experiments match original GMMN results on digits/faces, but broader adoption requires strong results on harder datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).
