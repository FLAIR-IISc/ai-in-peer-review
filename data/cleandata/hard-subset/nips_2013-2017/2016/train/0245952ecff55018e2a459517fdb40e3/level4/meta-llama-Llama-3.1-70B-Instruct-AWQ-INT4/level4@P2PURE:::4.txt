This paper builds upon the deep generative modeling approach of "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" and derives an extension that enables conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, allowing them to be optimized using the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears to be technically sound, and the proposals are well-presented; however, I did not verify all the equations in full detail.

In terms of novelty, while the idea of extending the generative moment matching framework to conditional settings is natural, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator were not immediately obvious. Consequently, the paper exhibits novelty and usefulness in this regard. The derived framework is straightforward to apply, as illustrated in Algorithm 1, making it likely to be useful and relevant to researchers interested in using deep models for generative purposes or distilling more complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read, with the provided appendix offering useful supplementary information regarding the methodological details and experimental setup.
