The authors extend generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) by aligning conditional rather than joint distributions. The main idea is to represent conditional distributions via embeddings in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space, following the technique of Song et al. (2009). The proposed algorithm, CGMMN, is evaluated empirically on several real datasets.

In the definition of $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ (Line 90), the component involving $\muX$ and $\muY$ (specifically, their empirical estimates) is omitted. It is also nonstandard to assume $\muX$ and $\muY$ are zero, i.e., that the data are already centered in the RKHS. Since $\hat{C}{Y|X}$ is central to CGMMN, the authors should clarify this missing term and explain how it is handled in gradient computation and in practical experiments.

The approach is novel and elegant, but the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. For predictive performance, on both datasets, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art method CMMVA. For generative performance, without comparisons to other conditional generative techniques (e.g., conditional generative adversarial networks), it is unclear whether the proposed model produces better samples.

The paper also contains several typos, e.g., “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).
