This paper extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting, enabling conditional sample generation. The key idea is to construct a kernel embedding of the conditional distribution’s moments so that the resulting objective can be optimized with the same machinery as in the unconditional GMMN.

The proposed conditional model is evaluated on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model, showing reasonably competitive performance across these tasks. The methodology appears technically sound and is clearly presented, though I did not verify every equation in full detail.

In terms of novelty, while a conditional extension of the GMMN framework is a natural direction, the specific mechanism—particularly the construction of the kernel embedding and the derivation of the empirical estimator—was not obvious to me. In that sense, the work offers both conceptual novelty and practical value.

The resulting framework seems straightforward to apply in practice, as summarized in Algorithm 1. Consequently, the paper should be of interest to researchers and practitioners working on deep generative models, as well as those seeking to distill complex statistical procedures into faster predictive models.

The paper is well written, clear, and easy to follow. The appendix is informative and provides useful additional details about the methods and experimental setup. Overall, this is a solid contribution that extends a known generative approach in a meaningful and applicable way.
