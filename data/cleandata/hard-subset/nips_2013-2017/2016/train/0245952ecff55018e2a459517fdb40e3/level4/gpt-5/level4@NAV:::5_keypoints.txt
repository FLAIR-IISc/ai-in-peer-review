- CGMMN extends GMMNs by aligning conditional (not joint) distributions using RKHS conditional embeddings (Song et al., 2009).

- The empirical estimator \hat{C}_{Y|X} in the paper omits terms involving empirical means μ_X and μ_Y; this omission and the implicit RKHS-centering assumption are nonstandard.

- The authors must clarify how the missing mean terms are handled and how they affect gradient computation and experiments.

- The approach is novel and elegant.

- Experimental results are underwhelming: CGMMN has higher predictive error than CMMVA on both datasets.

- Generative evaluation is incomplete—no comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs), so sample quality gains are unclear.

- Paper contains several typos (e.g., “Frobeniu norm”, “an universal”, “which the”).

- After the rebuttal, the reviewer reports that all concerns were addressed and will adjust scores.
