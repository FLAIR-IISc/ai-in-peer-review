- Introduces a novel loss for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending GMMNs with an unconditional MMD-based loss.  
- Leverages conditional kernel mean embeddings to derive the loss and evaluates on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Previous minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space rationale, scalability to high dimensions, comparative utility, clarifications) have been adequately addressed.  
- Remark 1: mapping from Eq.(2) to weighted MMD is unclear; dot notation is ambiguous (matrix vs. Hadamard).  
- Symbol choice: using "C" is problematic since it conflicts with the conditional embedding notation.  
- Theorems 1 and 2 properly reference prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a citation.  
- Authors should provide a reference or include the proof for Theorem 3.  
- Empirical results are satisfactory and comparable to the original GMM paper.  
- Using supervised learning as an application is less compelling but acceptable to demonstrate versatility.  
- Bayesian distillation application is interesting but only demonstrated on one dataset/model, so broader applicability is uncertain.  
- The approach appears most promising for pure generative modeling.  
- Current datasets (digits, faces) are sufficient as proof of concept; stronger results on challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) are needed for wider adoption.
