- Proposes Conditional GMMNs (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction via kernel embedding and Conditional MMD (CMMD).
- Core idea: match the model to the conditional embedding operator rather than individual conditional distributions.
- Addresses data sparsity of naive per-condition GMMNs, especially with continuous conditioning variables.
- Naive baseline (separate GMMNs with shared parameters) may still work well for discrete conditionings (e.g., MNIST); a direct comparison is missing.
- CMMD formulation is elegant and, per Remark 1, computationally efficient because K matrices are parameter-independent enabling standard backprop.
- CMMD may require large minibatches to reduce stochasticity, leading to expensive matrix inverses and higher computation.
- Experimental results on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation are promising and noteworthy.
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 could be clarified for better readability.
- Two errors identified: (1) claim of an unbiased MMD estimator is incorrect, and (2) the conditional embedding operator definition is inconsistent with reference [29], possibly needing a revised cross-covariance operator.
