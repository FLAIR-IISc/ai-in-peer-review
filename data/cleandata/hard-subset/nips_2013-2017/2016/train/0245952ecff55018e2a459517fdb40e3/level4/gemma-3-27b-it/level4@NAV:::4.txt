This work extends the “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” framework to enable conditional generation. This is achieved through a kernel embedding of the moments characterizing the conditional distribution, allowing for optimization via the existing GMMN methodology. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed model with strong results in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation. The paper is technically rigorous and clearly presented – though a full verification of all equations was not undertaken. While a conditional extension of the generative moment matching approach is a logical progression, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are non-trivial and represent a novel contribution. This framework appears readily applicable, as illustrated by Algorithm 1, and should be of interest and value to researchers employing deep generative models or focused on distilling complex statistical models. The manuscript is well-written, accessible, and benefits from a comprehensive appendix detailing methodological specifics and experimental setup.
