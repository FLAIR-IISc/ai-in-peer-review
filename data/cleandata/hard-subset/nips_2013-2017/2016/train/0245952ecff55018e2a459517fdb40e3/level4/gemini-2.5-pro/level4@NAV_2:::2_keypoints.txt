- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to model conditional distributions via a Conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.
- CMMD measures discrepancy between conditional embedding operators in an RKHS to match p(y|x) instead of the joint distribution.
- Extension from unconditional to conditional setting is non-trivial and addresses the minibatch challenge where each x has a different target p(y|x).
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and strong across diverse tasks: predictive modeling, conditional image generation, and distilling Bayesian models.
- The method demonstrates practical utility and competitive performance, showing potential significance.
- Main weakness: lack of intuitive motivation for the core CMMD objective (Equation (2)); presentation jumps into heavy math without a conceptual bridge.
- Paper fails to clearly explain how Equation (2) naturally arises from the goal of matching conditional distributions.
- Practical implementation details are insufficient: the computational steps linking Equation (2) to the final algorithm are unclear.
- Missing clarity on algorithmic efficiency, scalability, mini-batch applicability, and assumptions required for tractability.
- Relevant implementation details are buried in the supplement and are hard to follow, hurting reproducibility.
- Strengths summarized: originality/significance (novel conditional extension), quality (strong empirical results), and well-motivated problem.
- Weaknesses summarized: poor clarity of CMMD motivation and insufficient description of computational/practical aspects; recommend revising to add intuition and explicit algorithmic steps.
