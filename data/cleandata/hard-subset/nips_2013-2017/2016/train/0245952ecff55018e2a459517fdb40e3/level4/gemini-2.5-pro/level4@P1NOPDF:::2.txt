This paper extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting. This extension is non-trivial. In many generative models, conditioning is a simple matter of feeding the conditional variable as an additional input to the network. Here, however, the core mechanism involves matching the distribution of a mini-batch of generated samples to a target distribution. In the conditional case, each sample in the mini-batch is conditioned on a different input, meaning the model must simultaneously match a set of different target distributions. The alternative—using the same conditional variable for the entire mini-batch—is computationally inefficient.

The primary weakness of the paper lies in its presentation. The authors immediately delve into complex mathematical formalism, such as reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces, to derive their objective function (Eq. 2) without first providing a clear, high-level intuition for their approach. This is particularly unfortunate because the original moment matching network paper provided a very intuitive explanation for its objective function, largely without resorting to such abstract mathematics. The current paper would be significantly strengthened by a similarly intuitive derivation for the conditional case.

Beyond the lack of intuition, the paper also fails to clearly detail the practical implementation of the proposed algorithm. It is difficult for the reader to understand the concrete computational steps that follow from the objective in Eq. 2, the computational efficiency of these steps, or the key assumptions required to make the method tractable. While some of these details are present in the supplementary material, they are difficult to follow.

To improve the paper, I would ask the authors to address the following points in the main text:
1.  Could you provide an intuitive, step-by-step motivation for the objective function in Eq. (2)?
2.  What is the precise sequence of computations required to implement the algorithm, starting from Eq. (2)? A clear description of the practical algorithm, its complexity, and its underlying assumptions is needed.
