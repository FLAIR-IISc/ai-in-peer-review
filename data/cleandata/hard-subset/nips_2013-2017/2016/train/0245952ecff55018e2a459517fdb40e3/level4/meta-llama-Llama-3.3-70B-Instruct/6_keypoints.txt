- Proposes conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMN) using kernel embeddings and conditional MMD for conditional generation and prediction.
- Demonstrates CGMMN effectiveness on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation tasks.
- Naive baseline of training separate GMMNs per conditional distribution suffers from data sparsity, especially with continuous conditioning variables.
- Proposed approach treats all conditional distributions as a unified family and matches the conditional embedding operator instead of individual distributions.
- For discrete conditioning (e.g., MNIST classes), the naive baseline can perform reasonably, so a comparison with this baseline would be informative.
- CMMD objective is simple and computationally efficient; K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, enabling straightforward backpropagation.
- Concern: CMMD requires large minibatches to reduce stochasticity-induced errors, increasing computational cost due to matrix inverses.
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 need clearer exposition and currently require consulting external sources for full understanding.
- Minor inaccuracies: the MMD estimator is incorrectly described as unbiased, and the paper’s conditional embedding operator definition diverges from reference [29], suggesting a different cross-covariance operator is needed.
