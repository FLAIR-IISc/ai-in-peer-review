This paper builds upon the deep generative modeling approach "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" and derives a conditional extension, allowing for the generation of data based on specific conditions. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments defining the conditional distribution, which can be optimized using the same machinery as the unconditional version.

The proposed model demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and the distillation of predictions from a Bayesian model. The paper appears to be technically sound, and the proposals are well-presented. Although I did not verify all the equations in full detail, the overall framework and derivations seem to be correct.

In terms of novelty, while the idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment-matching framework is a natural progression, the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator are not immediately obvious. Therefore, the paper seems to possess both novelty and usefulness. The derived framework itself is relatively easy to apply, as demonstrated in Algorithm 1. Consequently, this paper will likely be useful and relevant to researchers interested in using deep models for generative purposes or to distill more elaborate statistical processes.

The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to read. The provided appendix is particularly useful, as it offers more complete information regarding the details of the methods used and the experiments. Overall, the authors have done an excellent job of presenting their work, making it accessible to a broad audience.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel conditional extension to the generative moment-matching framework.
* The derived framework is relatively easy to apply, as demonstrated in Algorithm 1.
* The paper demonstrates reasonably competitive performance on various tasks.
* The writing is clear, and the appendix provides useful additional information.

Weaknesses:

* The idea of making a conditional extension to the generative moment-matching framework is a natural progression, and some readers may find it less innovative than others.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other state-of-the-art methods.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept, with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
