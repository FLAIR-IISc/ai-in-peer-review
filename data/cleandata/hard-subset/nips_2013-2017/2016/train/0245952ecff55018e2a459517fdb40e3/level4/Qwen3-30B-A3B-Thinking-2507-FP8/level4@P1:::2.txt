

This paper proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), which extend the Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMN) framework to model conditional distributions. The authors introduce a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion for learning conditional distributions, and evaluate CGMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge tasks.

The paper makes an important contribution by extending the GMMN framework to handle conditional distributions, which is non-trivial compared to simply feeding conditional variables into the network. The key challenge lies in matching distributions across mini-batches where the conditional variables differ, rather than using the same condition for all samples in a batch (which would be inefficient). While the mathematical foundation is sound, the paper's presentation suffers from a lack of intuitive explanation for the CMMD objective, which is critical for readers to understand the approach.

The main weakness of the paper is its over-reliance on formal mathematical notation without providing sufficient intuition behind the CMMD formula (equation 2). The non-conditional GMMN paper effectively explains the objective without heavy mathematical machinery, but the conditional extension lacks this clarity. The authors should have addressed these key questions: How does one intuitively arrive at equation (2)? What are the practical computational steps from this formula to the implementation? The supplement contains some details, but they're difficult to follow without clear explanations.

The paper states that "the inverse matrices K̃⁻¹ₛ and K̃⁻¹_d in the CMMD objective are independent of the model parameters," which suggests the method can use non-differentiable kernels for conditional variables. However, the paper doesn't explain how this impacts computational efficiency or practical implementation. For a method that claims to be "simple" to train via backpropagation, the review would benefit from a clearer explanation of the actual computational steps involved.

The evaluation section demonstrates competitive performance on MNIST and SVHN datasets for predictive modeling, and shows reasonable generation capabilities on MNIST and Yale Face datasets. The Bayesian dark knowledge experiment is particularly interesting, showing that CGMMN can effectively distill knowledge from Bayesian models without degrading predictive performance. However, the paper could better highlight how CGMMN compares to other conditional generative models like conditional GANs or conditional VAEs.

The paper's strengths include:
- A novel extension of moment-matching networks to conditional distributions
- Strong empirical results across multiple application domains
- Demonstrated applicability to Bayesian model distillation

The weaknesses include:
- Lack of intuitive explanation for the CMMD objective (equation 2)
- Insufficient detail about the practical computational implementation
- Over-reliance on formal mathematics without sufficient motivation
- Limited comparison with other conditional generative models in the main text

The paper makes a technically sound contribution that extends the GMMN framework to conditional settings, which is valuable for applications requiring conditional generation or prediction. However, the presentation would be significantly improved with clearer explanations of the intuition behind the CMMD objective and the practical implementation details. The current presentation may make it difficult for readers to grasp the key insights of the method without delving deeply into the mathematical details.

The paper is well-motivated and addresses a meaningful extension to the GMMN framework. The authors successfully demonstrate the utility of CGMMN across multiple tasks, showing that it can compete with state-of-the-art methods in predictive modeling and generate meaningful samples in conditional generation tasks. The Bayesian dark knowledge experiment is particularly compelling as it demonstrates a novel application of the method.

For a NIPS paper, the technical contribution is significant but the presentation could be clearer. The authors should consider adding a more intuitive explanation of the CMMD objective before presenting the formal mathematics, and provide a clearer step-by-step explanation of how the objective translates to practical implementation. This would make the paper more accessible to the broader NIPS audience and strengthen the contribution.

In summary, the paper presents a valuable extension to moment-matching networks that enables conditional distribution modeling. While the technical approach is sound, the paper would benefit from improved exposition that better explains the intuition behind the method and clarifies the practical implementation details. With these improvements, the paper would make a stronger contribution to the field.
