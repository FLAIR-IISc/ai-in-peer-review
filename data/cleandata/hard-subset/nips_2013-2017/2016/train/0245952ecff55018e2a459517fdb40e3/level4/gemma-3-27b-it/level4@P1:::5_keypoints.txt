- Extends GMMNs to model conditional distributions via RKHS embeddings (builds on Song et al. 2009).  
- Introduces CGMMN algorithm and reports empirical results on several real datasets.  
- Definition of empirical conditional operator Ĥ_{Y|X} appears to miss terms involving the empirical means μ_X and μ_Y.  
- Assumption that μ_X and μ_Y are zero (i.e., data centered in RKHS) is unusual and needs justification.  
- Since Ĥ_{Y|X} is central, authors should clarify how the missing term is handled in gradient computations and experiments.  
- Approach is novel and well-motivated.  
- Theoretical foundation is sound, based on kernel methods and Hilbert space embeddings.  
- Demonstrates applicability to diverse tasks: predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Experimental predictive performance is underwhelming; CGMMN shows higher error rates than CMMVA.  
- Generative evaluation is incomplete—no comparison to other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs).  
- Potential formulation issue with the conditional embedding operator could undermine results.  
- Paper contains minor typographical errors (e.g., "Frobenius norm", "an universal", "which the").
