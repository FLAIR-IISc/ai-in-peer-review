- Extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting, which is non-trivial due to matching distributions across many conditional inputs and mini-batches.
- Key technical contribution: defines a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion for learning conditional distributions.
- Demonstrates that the CMMD objective can be optimized via back-propagation and applies to predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.
- Experiments show competitive performance across several benchmark tasks.
- Strength: addresses an important problem and builds on promising prior work (GMMN/MMD).
- Strength: proposed CMMD appears reasonable and empirically effective.
- Weakness: initial presentation is overly mathematical and lacks clear intuition for core ideas.
- Weakness: computational complexity, scalability, and implementation/optimization details of CMMD are insufficiently discussed.
- Major clarity gap: Eq. (2) lacks an intuitive derivation, explanation of assumptions, and a clear mapping to the implemented algorithm.
- Supplement contains some implementation details but is difficult to follow; the practical computation steps are not clearly articulated.
- Need explicit answers: how Eq. (2) was derived intuitively and the step-by-step connection from Eq. (2) to code/algorithm.
- Paper should more thoroughly discuss assumptions, their implications, and potential limitations of CMMD.
- Acceptance arguments: novel extension, solid empirical results, technically sound contribution to generative modeling.
- Rejection concerns: presentation density, lack of intuitive explanation and detailed computational analysis hinder accessibility and practical understanding.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to improve clarity, provide intuition, and add detailed computational/implementation analysis.
