- Paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) using kernel embeddings and a Conditional MMD (CMMD) to match conditional distributions.
- Core contribution: training to match the conditional embedding operator instead of individual conditional distributions to avoid data sparsity.
- Theoretical advantage over naive per-condition GMMNs is clear, especially for continuous or sparse conditioning variables.
- Experimental evaluation covers classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.
- Reviewer requests empirical comparison against the simpler naive baseline (e.g., MNIST discrete labels) to validate practical superiority.
- Strength: CMMD objective is elegantly formulated and computable almost as easily as unconditional MMD, with kernel matrices independent of model parameters enabling standard backpropagation.
- Experiments on classification and Bayesian distillation are compelling and demonstrate framework versatility.
- Concern: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 lack clarity and should be more self-contained to avoid reliance on cited papers.
- Concern: CMMD’s stochastic estimation may require large minibatches for low variance, raising computational and scalability issues (e.g., costly matrix inversions).
- Minor technical error: Claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect for the common V-statistic; only the U-statistic is unbiased.
- Minor technical issue: Definition of the conditional embedding operator seems inconsistent with reference [29]; authors should verify and correct definition or citation.
- Overall: novel, principled method with a valuable CMMD contribution but needs stronger empirical comparisons, clearer exposition, and discussion of computational trade-offs.
