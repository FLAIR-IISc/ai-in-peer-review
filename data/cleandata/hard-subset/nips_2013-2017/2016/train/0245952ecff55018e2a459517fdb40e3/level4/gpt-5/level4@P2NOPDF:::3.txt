This paper introduces a loss function for directed generative models with conditioning variables. The method extends generative moment matching networks by moving from an unconditional MMD-based loss to a conditional formulation derived from conditional kernel mean embeddings. The approach is evaluated on several tasks—supervised classification, generation of handwritten digits and faces, and distilling posteriors in Bayesian models.

This is my second review of the paper. Previously raised concerns were relatively minor: the use of a low-dimensional latent space and whether the method scales to higher-dimensional latents, the comparative utility of the approach, and several clarifications. Based on the current manuscript, these points appear to be addressed.

However, I still have some technical and notational concerns. In Remark 1, the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form is not clear. In particular, the “·” notation is ambiguous: is this standard matrix multiplication or a Hadamard (elementwise) product? Please make this mapping explicit and add enough detail to render the equivalence transparent. Also, the choice of the symbol C is unfortunate given its prior use for the conditional embedding; please avoid overloading notation. Regarding the theoretical results, Theorems 1 and 2 appropriately cite prior work, but Theorem 3 has no reference. A citation to a source containing a proof is needed, or a complete proof should be included.

The empirical results are solid. While supervised learning may not be the most compelling application for this model, the authors’ motivation—to highlight versatility—is reasonable. The distilled Bayesian inference experiment is intriguing, but the evidence is limited to a single dataset and model, leaving its broader usefulness unclear. The method seems most promising for pure generative modeling. The image generation results are broadly comparable to those in the original GMMN work on digits and faces, which is sufficient to demonstrate feasibility. For wider adoption, stronger results on more challenging datasets (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) would be important. Overall, the contribution is interesting and well-motivated, but it would benefit from clearer exposition of the weighted MMD connection, unambiguous notation, a reference or proof for Theorem 3, and more ambitious empirical validation.
