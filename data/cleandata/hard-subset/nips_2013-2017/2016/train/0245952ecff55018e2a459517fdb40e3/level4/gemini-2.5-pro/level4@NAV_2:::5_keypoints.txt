- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMNs to conditional distributions using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective.  
- Builds on kernel embeddings for conditional distributions (Song et al., 2009) and is evaluated on prediction, conditional generation, and knowledge distillation tasks.  
- Novel and elegant principled MMD-based extension to deep conditional generative models.  
- Demonstrates versatility across diverse applications, suggesting CGMMN as a general-purpose conditional density estimator.  
- Key technical concern: empirical conditional embedding operator Ĉ_Y|X appears to omit empirical mean terms (μ_X, μ_Y), implying an unstated RKHS centering and requiring clarification on gradient computation and experiments.  
- Predictive performance is not consistently state-of-the-art—CGMMN is outperformed by CMMVA on MNIST and SVHN.  
- Generative evaluation is incomplete: lacks quantitative or comparative evaluation against prominent conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs).  
- Presentation issues: several minor typographical errors and proofreading problems.  
- For acceptance: offers a novel, theoretically grounded method and a meaningful extension of GMMNs to the conditional setting.  
- Against acceptance: unclear central technical detail, no clear empirical advantage over state-of-the-art, and insufficient benchmarking of generative performance.
