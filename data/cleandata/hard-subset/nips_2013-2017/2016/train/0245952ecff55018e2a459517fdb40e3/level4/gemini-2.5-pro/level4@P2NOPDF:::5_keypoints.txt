- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) to model conditional distributions using RKHS embeddings of conditional distributions from Song et al. (2009).
- Core idea: embed conditional distributions into an RKHS to define a distance metric for training CGMMN.
- Empirical evaluation is performed on several real-world datasets.
- Major technical concern: empirical conditional covariance operator definition appears to omit mean embedding terms, implying an unrealistic centered-data assumption.
- Clarification needed on how omitted mean terms affect the objective, gradient computations, and practical implementation.
- Predictive performance concern: CGMMN is outperformed by baseline CMMVA, showing higher error rates on both datasets.
- Generative evaluation concern: lacks comparisons to contemporary conditional generative models (e.g., cGANs), making sample quality hard to assess.
- Minor issues: typos—“Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and grammatical error “which the” (Line 238).
- Update: authors’ rebuttal satisfactorily addressed initial concerns, and the reviewer updated their scores accordingly.
