This paper introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMN), an extension of previous Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) that focuses on matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea leverages the technique presented by Song et al. (2009) to embed these conditional distributions within a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS). The authors demonstrate the efficacy of CGMMN through empirical evaluation on several real-world datasets.

However, a potential issue exists within the definition of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ (Line 90). The term involving $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ – specifically, their estimators – appears to be omitted. Furthermore, the assumption that $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ are zero, implying the data is pre-centered in the RKHS, is an unusual one. Given the central role of $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$ in the CGMMN framework, clarification regarding this missing term, along with a discussion of its impact on gradient computation and experimental results, would significantly strengthen the paper.

The proposed approach is novel and conceptually sound. Nevertheless, the experimental results are somewhat underwhelming. Regarding predictive performance, CGMMN consistently exhibits higher error rates compared to the current state-of-the-art method, CMMVA, across both tested datasets. Concerning generative performance, the lack of comparison with other established conditional generative models – such as conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (cGANs) – makes it difficult to assess whether CGMMN generates superior samples.

The manuscript also contains minor typographical errors, including "Frobeniu norm" (Line 48), "an universal" (Line 65), and "which the" (Line 238). Addressing these would improve the overall polish of the paper.
