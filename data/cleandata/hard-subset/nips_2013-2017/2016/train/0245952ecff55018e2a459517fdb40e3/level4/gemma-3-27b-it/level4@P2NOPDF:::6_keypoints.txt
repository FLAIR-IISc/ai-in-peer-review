- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional generation and prediction tasks.  
- Core innovation: use kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD metric to match model and data conditionals.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation with encouraging results.  
- CGMMNs avoid data sparsity from per-conditional estimators by treating all conditionals as a family and matching conditional embedding operators.  
- The naive per-conditional-distribution baseline (sharing network parameters) can perform surprisingly well in some settings (e.g., discrete conditioning on MNIST) and should be included.  
- Conditional MMD objective is elegant and nearly as efficient as unconditional MMD; K matrices are parameter-independent, simplifying backprop.  
- Practical drawbacks: high computational cost, large minibatch requirements, and expensive matrix inversions that can hamper stochastic optimization.  
- Presentation in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is unclear and requires consulting cited references, reducing accessibility.  
- Technical inaccuracies: claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased is incorrect, and the conditional embedding operator definition does not match reference [29].  
- Overall assessment: conceptually sound and potentially impactful; addressing clarity, baseline comparisons, and technical corrections would substantially improve the paper.
