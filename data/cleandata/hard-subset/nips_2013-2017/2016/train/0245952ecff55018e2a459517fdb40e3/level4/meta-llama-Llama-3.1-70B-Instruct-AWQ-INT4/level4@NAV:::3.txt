This paper introduces a novel loss function for directed generative models that incorporate conditional variables, building upon the concept of generative moment matching networks. By leveraging conditional kernel mean embeddings, the authors extend the traditional unconditional loss function based on the maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The proposed approach is evaluated on various generative tasks, including classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and knowledge distillation in Bayesian models. As a second-time reviewer of this paper, I note that the authors have addressed the minor concerns raised in the previous review, including the choice of low-dimensional space and the model's scalability to higher-dimensional latent spaces, as well as its utility compared to other models.

Regarding Remark 1, I would appreciate further clarification on the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form. Specifically, the dot notation used in this context is unclear, and it would be helpful to explicitly state whether it represents matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product. Additionally, the choice of notation for the variable C is somewhat unfortunate, given its use in defining the conditional embedding.

The authors' reliance on Theorems 1 and 2 from other papers is properly cited; however, Theorem 3 lacks a reference to a proof or the proof itself. I suggest providing a citation to a relevant paper or including the proof in the manuscript.

The empirical results presented in the paper are satisfactory. Although the authors' decision to apply the model to supervised learning tasks has been questioned in previous reviews, they have adequately justified its inclusion as a demonstration of the model's versatility. The application of the model to distilled Bayesian inference is intriguing, but its usefulness in other contexts is uncertain due to the limited evaluation on a single dataset and model. I believe this approach holds the most promise in the realm of pure generative modeling. The experiments conducted in this context are comparable to those in the original GMM paper, and the use of digit and face datasets is sufficient to demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed method. Nevertheless, to gain widespread adoption, the authors would need to provide compelling results on more challenging datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
