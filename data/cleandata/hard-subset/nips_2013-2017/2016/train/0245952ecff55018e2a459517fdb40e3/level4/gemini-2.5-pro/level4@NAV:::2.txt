This paper extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting. The authors correctly identify that this is a non-trivial extension. Unlike models that process single examples, where conditioning can be achieved by simply augmenting the network input, moment matching operates on mini-batches. The core challenge arises from needing to match a distribution of generated samples to a distribution of target outputs, where each target is conditioned on a different input within the mini-batch. Using a single conditioning variable for the entire batch would be computationally inefficient and defeat the purpose of the model.

The primary weakness of the manuscript is its presentation, which prioritizes mathematical formalism over intuition and practical clarity. The paper immediately introduces advanced mathematical concepts (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces) to derive the objective in Equation (2) without first building an intuitive understanding for the reader. The original paper on non-conditional moment matching successfully presented its core objective in an intuitive manner; a similar, accessible explanation is needed for this more complex conditional case.

Furthermore, the manuscript does not sufficiently detail the practical implementation derived from Equation (2). Key aspects such as the specific computations performed in each step, the algorithm's computational efficiency, and the underlying assumptions required for its practical application are not clearly articulated. This makes it difficult for the reader to grasp the tangible algorithm and its associated costs. While some implementation details are present in the supplement, they are not easily followed.

To strengthen the paper, I strongly suggest the authors revise the presentation to answer the following questions:
- How can the objective in Equation (2) be motivated from an intuitive standpoint, before introducing the formal mathematical machinery?
- What are the explicit computational steps that bridge the theoretical formulation in Equation (2) and a practical, implementable algorithm? A clear, step-by-step description in the main paper would greatly improve the reader's understanding of the proposed method's feasibility and complexity.
