This work extends the “Generative Moment-Matching Networks” framework to enable conditional generation. This is achieved through a kernel embedding of the moments characterizing the conditional distribution, allowing for optimization using the existing methodology developed for the unconditional case. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach with promising results on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian model distillation. The paper is technically rigorous and clearly presented; while a complete verification of all equations was not performed, the approach appears sound. The extension to conditional generation feels natural, however, the specific mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator represent a non-trivial contribution. Consequently, the paper demonstrates both novelty and practical utility. The resulting framework, as exemplified in Algorithm 1, appears readily applicable to researchers employing deep generative models or seeking to distill complex statistical models. The manuscript is well-written, accessible, and benefits from a comprehensive appendix detailing methodological specifics and experimental setup.
