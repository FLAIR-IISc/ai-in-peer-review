- Summary: Proposes Conditional GMMN (CGMMN) using a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective that matches kernel mean embedding operators to model p(y|x), trained by SGD and demonstrated on prediction, conditional generation, and model distillation.

- Strength — Novelty: Extends moment-matching networks to the conditional setting via a principled CMMD criterion, addressing matching a family of conditional distributions per minibatch.

- Strength — Broad applicability: Shows effectiveness on prediction (MNIST, SVHN), conditional image generation (MNIST, Yale Faces), and distilling Bayesian models.

- Strength — Empirical results: Thorough experiments with competitive performance, notably strong results on SVHN.

- Weakness — Clarity: The CMMD objective lacks intuitive motivation; paper dives into Hilbert space operators without a clear bridge from unconditional MMD intuition.

- Weakness — Motivation structure: The crucial insight that CMMD is a non-uniformly weighted MMD (weights depend on x) is presented late and should be central to the exposition.

- Weakness — Computational details: Paper omits discussion of computational cost and scalability, notably the O(B^3) cost of inverting kernel matrices in Equation (2).

- Weakness — Minibatch ambiguity: It is unclear how the practical minibatch algorithm maps to the full-batch formula and whether DsXY/DdXY are simply minibatch samples.

- Weakness — Regularization role: The choice and role of the regularization parameter λ in matrix inversions are not discussed.

- Question 1: Provide an intuitive, step-by-step motivation for CMMD (e.g., start from weighted MMD based on x-similarity and derive Equation (2)).

- Question 2: Detail the computational steps and per-iteration complexity of Algorithm 1 and compare to methods like conditional GANs.

- Question 3: Clarify whether the need for an autoencoder implies CMMD struggles with high-dimensional pixel outputs and discuss general applicability to high-dimensional y.

- Question 4: Explain kernel choices for x and y in experiments and analyze sensitivity of performance to those kernel selections.

- Conclusion: The paper makes a significant, well-supported contribution but requires substantial revision for clearer intuition, stronger exposition linking theory to practice, and fuller computational details.
