This paper develops a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy criterion. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard, small-scale benchmark tasks to assess its discriminative and generative capabilities. Overall, this is an interesting extension to the sub-field of deep learning research focused on building generative models.

The paper is reasonably clear, although, like many publications in this area, the mathematical details can be difficult to follow at times. The tasks used in the experiments are somewhat small but not unreasonable when compared against other recent work.

As the training algorithm involves computationally expensive steps, the experiments section would benefit from a discussion of the model's training time compared to existing approaches like DSN. The paper would be much stronger if reference code were made available for the experiments. Furthermore, the description in the model section may lack sufficient detail for a practitioner to reproduce the exact models evaluated.
