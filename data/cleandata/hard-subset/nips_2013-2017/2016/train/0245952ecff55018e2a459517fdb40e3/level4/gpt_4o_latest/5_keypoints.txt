- Paper extends GMMNs to match conditional rather than joint distributions via RKHS embeddings (following Song et al., 2009).
- Proposed algorithm CGMMN is empirically evaluated on multiple real-world datasets.
- Definition of \hat{C}_{Y|X} (Line 90) appears to omit the terms involving estimators of μ_X and μ_Y.
- Assumption that μ_X and μ_Y are zero (i.e., data pre-centered in RKHS) is unconventional and requires justification.
- Authors must clarify the missing μ_X/μ_Y term and explain how it affects gradient computation and practical experiments.
- Experimental predictive performance is underwhelming: CGMMN shows higher error rates than state-of-the-art CMMVA on both datasets.
- Generative performance comparisons are incomplete—no comparisons to other conditional generative methods (e.g., conditional GANs).
- Paper contains several typographical errors (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the").
- Update: After rebuttal, reviewer states authors have adequately addressed concerns and will revise scores.
