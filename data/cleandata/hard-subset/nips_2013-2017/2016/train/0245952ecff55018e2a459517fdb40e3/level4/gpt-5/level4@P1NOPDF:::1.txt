This paper proposes a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion. The method is evaluated on standard small-scale benchmark tasks to assess both discriminative and generative capabilities. Overall, it is an interesting extension within the sub-area of deep learning focused on generative modeling. The writing is reasonably clear, though—as with many papers in this area—the mathematical derivations can be difficult to follow at times.

The experimental tasks are relatively small, but this is not unreasonable compared with other recent work in the field. However, because the training algorithm involves some computationally expensive steps, the paper should include comments in the experiments section on training time and resource usage, and compare them with existing approaches such as DSN. This would help readers gauge the practical costs and scalability of the method.

The paper would be much stronger if reference code were made available. As presented, the model description may not provide enough detail for practitioners to reproduce the exact architectures and settings used in the experiments, which could hinder replication.
