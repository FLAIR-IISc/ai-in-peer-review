- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) with a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective based on kernel conditional embeddings.  
- CMMD measures discrepancy between conditional distributions by matching the conditional embedding operator instead of individual p(y|x).  
- Addresses data-sparsity issues of the naive approach (separate GMMN per condition), especially when x is continuous.  
- Originality/significance: principled, novel extension of moment-matching to conditional settings that broadens GMMN applicability.  
- Technical quality: elegant CMMD formulation; kernel matrices on conditioning variables are independent of model parameters, simplifying gradient computation.  
- Methodological soundness: well-motivated shift from matching individual conditionals to matching a single conditional embedding operator.  
- Empirical evaluation: demonstrates versatility across tasks (classification, conditional generation, Bayesian model distillation) with competitive results and an interesting distillation use-case.  
- Clarity concern: theoretical sections (conditional mean embedding and operators) are dense and need more intuition for non-experts.  
- Computational concern: trade-off between minibatch size for stable gradients and cubic cost of kernel-matrix inverse; authors should discuss practical strategies used.  
- Missing baseline: no empirical comparison to the “naive” baseline (separate/shared-parameter GMMNs per class), which would be feasible for discrete-conditioning tasks like MNIST.  
- Minor technical inaccuracies: incorrect claim that the presented MMD estimator is unbiased (V-statistic is biased); the empirical estimator for the conditional embedding operator needs clarification relative to cited definitions.  
- Overall judgment: strong, well-motivated contribution worthy of acceptance, but could be improved in clarity, computational discussion, and baseline comparisons.
