- Proposes a conditional MMD-style loss for directed generative models by extending GMMNs via conditional kernel mean embeddings.  
- Evaluated on classification, digit/face generation, and Bayesian knowledge distillation.  
- Previous minor concerns (low-dimensional latent space, scalability, practical utility, clarifications) appear addressed.  
- Clarity concern: Remark 1—mapping of Equation (2) to weighted MMD is unclear; “dot” notation ambiguous (matrix vs. Hadamard); needs explicit definitions and a short derivation.  
- Notation issue: symbol C is reused for two different quantities (conditional embedding operator and another C); one should be renamed to avoid confusion.  
- Theorem 3 lacks a citation or proof; authors should provide a reference or include a full proof in the appendix.  
- Empirical results are solid and justify supervised-use demonstration as a versatility example.  
- Bayesian distillation is interesting but shown on only one dataset/model, limiting assessment of general applicability; more diverse Bayesian experiments needed.  
- Greatest potential impact is in pure generative modeling; current experiments match original GMMN on simple datasets (digits, faces).  
- To convince practitioners and demonstrate scalability/stability, results on harder benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN) and higher-dimensional latent spaces are recommended.  
- Overall: well-motivated extension with reasonable validation; fixing notational/derivational ambiguities and adding challenging benchmarks would significantly strengthen the paper.
