

This paper introduces a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables, extending generative moment matching networks (GMMNs) by incorporating conditional kernel mean embeddings to formulate a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) loss. The approach is validated across diverse generative tasks, including classification, handwritten digit and face generation, and knowledge distillation in Bayesian models. As this is the second review cycle, the authors have addressed prior minor concerns regarding the use of low-dimensional latent space, scalability to higher dimensions, comparative utility against existing methods, and clarifications on technical details.  

Regarding Remark 1, the mapping from Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form remains unclear. Specifically, the dot notation requires specification: is it matrix multiplication or the Hadamard product? Additional detail is needed to clarify this derivation. Furthermore, the choice of constant $C$ is problematic as it conflicts with the notation for conditional embedding. Theorems 1 and 2 cite external work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference; a citation to a proof or an explicit proof must be provided.  

The empirical results are sufficient for the presented tasks. While the supervised learning application was previously questioned, the authors’ justification for demonstrating model versatility is acceptable. The distilled Bayesian inference example is promising but limited to one dataset and model, raising questions about broader applicability. The method shows strongest potential in pure generative modeling, with experiments comparable to the original GMMN work. The digit and face datasets adequately validate the core idea, though wider adoption would require compelling results on more challenging benchmarks like CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
