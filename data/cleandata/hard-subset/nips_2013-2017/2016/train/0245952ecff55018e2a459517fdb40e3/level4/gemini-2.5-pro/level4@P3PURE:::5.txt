This paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting by matching conditional distributions rather than joint distributions. The core idea is to embed the conditional distributions in a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), a technique previously introduced by Song et al. (2009). The proposed algorithm, Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Network (CGMMN), is evaluated empirically on several real-world datasets.

A primary concern lies with the definition of the empirical conditional embedding operator, $\hat{C}_{Y|X}$, in Line 90. The formulation appears to be missing terms related to the mean embeddings $\mu_X$ and $\mu_Y$ (or their empirical estimates). This implies an unusual assumption that the data is pre-centered in the RKHS (i.e., $\mu_X=0$ and $\mu_Y=0$). Given that this operator is central to the proposed CGMMN framework, the authors should clarify why these terms are omitted and discuss the implications for both the gradient computation and the practical implementation in their experiments.

While the proposed approach is novel and elegant, the experimental results could be strengthened.
1.  **Predictive Performance:** In the predictive tasks, CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art method, CMMVA, on both datasets presented.
2.  **Generative Performance:** The paper lacks a comparison with other conditional generative models, such as conditional generative adversarial networks (cGANs). Without these relevant baselines, it is difficult to assess the quality of the samples generated by CGMMN and understand its advantages over existing methods.

The paper also contains a few minor typos that should be corrected:
*   Line 48: "Frobeniu norm" should be "Frobenius norm".
*   Line 65: "an universal" should be "a universal".
*   Line 238: The phrase "which the" appears to be a grammatical error.
