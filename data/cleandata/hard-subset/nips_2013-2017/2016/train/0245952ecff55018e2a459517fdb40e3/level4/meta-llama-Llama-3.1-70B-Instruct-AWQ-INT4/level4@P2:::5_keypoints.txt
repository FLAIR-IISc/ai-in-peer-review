- Proposes CGMMN: extends GMMNs to learn conditional distributions by embedding conditionals in an RKHS (building on Song et al. 2009).  
- Approach is flexible and simple for learning conditional distributions.  
- Clear and concise background and related work coverage (GMMNs, MMD, kernel mean embeddings).  
- CGMMN algorithm is well-motivated, easy to follow, with detailed CMMD criterion and deep generative architecture.  
- Experiments on MNIST, SVHN, and Yale Face demonstrate effectiveness in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.  
- Competitive predictive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods (CMMVA, max-margin DGMs).  
- Definition of ĤC_{Y|X} is missing a term involving μ_X and μ_Y (their estimators); this is a core omission.  
- Unusual/unsupported assumption that μ_X and μ_Y are zero (i.e., data centered in RKHS); needs justification.  
- Require clarification on how the missing term affects gradient computation and empirical experiments.  
- Experimental evaluation is promising but not comprehensive; more comparisons with other conditional generative methods are needed.  
- Minor typos present (e.g., "Frobeniu norm", "an universal", "which the") that should be fixed.  
- Overall assessment: novel and neat approach with promising results; recommend minor revisions — rating 8/10.
