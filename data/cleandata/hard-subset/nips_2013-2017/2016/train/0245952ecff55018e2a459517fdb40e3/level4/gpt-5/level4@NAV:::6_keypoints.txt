- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs), extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction.  
- Core idea: use kernel embeddings of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD to measure discrepancy between conditional distributions.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Naive baseline: fit a separate GMMN per conditional (sharing NN parameters) suffers from data sparsity when conditioning variables are continuous.  
- Proposed approach treats all conditionals as a family and matches the model to the conditional embedding operator instead of each conditional separately.  
- Note: the naive baseline may still work reasonably in low-cardinality discrete settings (e.g., 10-class MNIST); such a baseline should be included for comparison.  
- Conditional MMD objective has a clean formulation and is almost as easy to compute as unconditional MMD; all K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters, allowing backpropagation.  
- Concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to avoid stochasticity dominating error, but large minibatches make the objective expensive and matrix inverses costly.  
- Overall assessment: CMMD is a compelling way to model conditional distributions with interesting experimental results.  
- Presentation critique: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and required consulting cited papers for clarity.  
- Minor issues: (1) claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect; (2) the conditional embedding operator form (line 90) is inconsistent with reference [29] without a different cross-covariance operator definition.
