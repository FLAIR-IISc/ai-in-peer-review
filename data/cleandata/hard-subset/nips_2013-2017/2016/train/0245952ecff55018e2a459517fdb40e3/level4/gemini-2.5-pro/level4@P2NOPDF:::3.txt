### **Summary**

This paper introduces a novel loss function for training directed generative models with conditional variables. The proposed method extends Generative Moment Matching Networks (GMMNs) by incorporating a conditional loss function based on the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) criterion. This is achieved by leveraging the theory of conditional kernel mean embeddings. The authors demonstrate the model's versatility across several tasks, including supervised classification, generative modeling of handwritten digits and faces, and knowledge distillation for Bayesian models.

The authors have diligently addressed the minor concerns raised in the previous review cycle, such as the justification for using a low-dimensional latent space, the model's scalability, and its utility compared to other approaches. The current manuscript is clearer as a result.

### **Strengths**

The paper's core contribution—extending the MMD-based loss of GMMNs to the conditional setting—is well-motivated and technically sound. The use of conditional kernel mean embeddings is an elegant way to achieve this. The experimental validation covers a diverse set of tasks, which effectively showcases the framework's versatility. The application to distilled Bayesian inference is particularly novel and interesting.

### **Areas for Improvement**

While the paper is strong, several points could be clarified and improved to strengthen the submission.

1.  **Clarity of Derivations and Notation:**
    *   The derivation in **Remark 1**, which connects Equation (2) to the weighted MMD form, could be clarified. The mapping is not immediately obvious. The authors should explicitly define the dot notation used—it is unclear whether it represents matrix multiplication, the Hadamard product, or another operation. Providing a more detailed derivation here would significantly improve the reader's understanding.
    *   A minor but important point on notation: the symbol **'C'** is used both as a constant in the weighted MMD form and as part of the definition of the conditional embedding operator. To avoid confusion, I recommend using a different symbol for one of these.

2.  **Theoretical Justification:**
    *   While Theorems 1 and 2 are appropriately cited, **Theorem 3** is presented without a reference or a proof. For a key theoretical result, it is essential to either include a proof in the appendix or cite the original work where the proof can be found.

3.  **Experimental Scope:**
    *   The supervised learning application was previously noted as a potentially weak use case, but the authors' justification of demonstrating versatility is reasonable.
    *   The **distilled Bayesian inference** experiment is intriguing, but its demonstration on a single dataset with a single model makes it difficult to assess its general utility. It is unclear how well this approach would transfer to other models or datasets.
    *   The framework's greatest potential appears to be in **pure generative modeling**. The current experiments on digits and faces are sufficient to validate the core idea and are comparable to the original GMMN paper. However, to make a compelling case for broader adoption, the paper would be significantly strengthened by demonstrating performance on more complex and widely-recognized benchmark datasets, such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
