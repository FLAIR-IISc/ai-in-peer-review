The paper extends generative moment-matching networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting by matching conditional distributions via RKHS conditional mean embeddings (as in Song et al., 2009). The proposed CGMMN is trained with stochastic gradient descent using back-propagation, and is evaluated on several real-world datasets.

A key technical concern: in the definition of the empirical conditional embedding operator hat C_{Y|X} (Line 90), the components involving mu_X and mu_Y (specifically, their empirical estimates) are omitted. It is atypical to assume mu_X and mu_Y are zero, i.e., that the data are already centered in the RKHS. Since hat C_{Y|X} is central to CGMMN, the paper should clarify the treatment of these terms and explain how they are handled in gradient computation and in the actual experiments.

While the approach is novel and elegant, the empirical results are somewhat underwhelming. For prediction, on both datasets CGMMN yields higher error rates than the state-of-the-art CMMVA. For generation, the paper does not compare CGMMN against other conditional generative models (e.g., conditional GANs), leaving it unclear whether CGMMN produces superior samples.

The manuscript has some typos, e.g., “Frobeniu norm” (Line 48), “an universal” (Line 65), and “which the” (Line 238).
