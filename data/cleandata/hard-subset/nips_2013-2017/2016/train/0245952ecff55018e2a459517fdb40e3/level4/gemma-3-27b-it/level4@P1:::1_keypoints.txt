- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to conditional distributions using a CMMD criterion.  
- Uses a deep network that inputs conditioning variables and auxiliary noise to generate conditional samples.  
- Evaluated on MNIST, SVHN, and a regression task, showing competitive discriminative and generative performance.  
- Demonstrates application to Bayesian dark knowledge distillation, showing ability to learn from complex teacher networks.  
- Principled, theoretically motivated alternative to GANs by leveraging MMD/CMMD and avoiding min–max training.  
- Paper is reasonably clear and well organized, though some mathematical details are hard to follow.  
- Experimental tasks are relatively small-scale but comparable to other recent work.  
- Training includes computationally expensive steps; reviewer requests reporting of training time comparisons (e.g., vs. DSN).  
- Lacks detailed discussion of smoothness/theoretical assumptions required for CMMD and their practical implications.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity and effects of different network architectures are not thoroughly investigated.  
- No publicly available code, which hinders reproducibility and practitioner adoption.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel conditional extension, principled MMD-based approach, competitive results, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical discussion, missing computational cost and hyperparameter studies, absence of code, and relatively small-scale experiments.
