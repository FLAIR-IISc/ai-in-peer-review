- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN), extending GMMN to learn conditional distributions via Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD).  
- Targets three applications: predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation.  
- CGMMN avoids the min-max optimization of conditional GANs, offering a simpler training objective.  
- Provides rigorous theoretical foundations linking CMMD to kernel embeddings of conditional distributions.  
- Presents a clear generative process and straightforward training algorithm implementable in standard deep learning frameworks.  
- Experimental evaluation on MNIST, SVHN, Yale Face, and Boston housing shows competitive performance across tasks, especially in contextual generation.  
- Strength — Quality: sound theoretical derivations and comprehensive comparisons to strong baselines (e.g., CMMVA, DSN).  
- Strength — Originality: fills a gap by extending moment-matching methods to conditional settings and contrasts clearly with prior work.  
- Strength — Significance: applicable to multiple important domains and achieves competitive or strong empirical results.  
- Weakness — Clarity: some dense math (Section 3.1) and unclear practical connection of Theorem 3 need more intuition and examples.  
- Weakness — Computational considerations: lacks empirical training time and complexity comparisons, and kernel operations may scale poorly without demonstrated mitigation.  
- Weakness — Reproducibility: would be strengthened by releasing reference code and more implementation details.  
- Pro/Con summary: theoretically solid and practically promising, but needs more computational analysis and code to aid adoption.  
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions — valuable contribution suitable for NIPS, contingent on addressing complexity analysis, reproducibility, and clarity issues.
