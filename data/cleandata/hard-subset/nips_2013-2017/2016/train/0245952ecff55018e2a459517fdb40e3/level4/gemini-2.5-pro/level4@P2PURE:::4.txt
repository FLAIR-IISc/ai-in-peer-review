This paper presents a conditional extension to the "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" framework. The authors achieve this by deriving a kernel embedding for the moments of the conditional distribution, enabling optimization with the same machinery used in the unconditional setting. The proposed model is demonstrated with reasonably competitive performance on tasks such as predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model.

The paper appears technically sound and the proposals are well-presented, although I have not verified every equation in full detail. Regarding novelty, while extending the generative moment-matching framework to the conditional setting is a natural step, the specific mechanism and the derivation of the empirical estimator are non-trivial. The work therefore makes a novel and useful contribution.

As shown in Algorithm 1, the derived framework seems straightforward to apply. Consequently, this paper will likely be relevant to researchers using deep generative models or those interested in distilling complex statistical processes. The paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow, and the appendix provides helpful, comprehensive details about the methods and experiments.
