This paper presents a neural network approach based on a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) criterion. The proposed technique is evaluated on standard, small-scale benchmark tasks to assess both its discriminative and generative capabilities.

Overall, this is an interesting contribution to the subfield of deep learning research focused on generative models. The paper is reasonably clear, although, like many papers in this area, the mathematical derivations can be difficult to follow at times. The experiments are conducted on datasets that are somewhat small in scale, but this is not entirely unreasonable when compared against other recent work in this domain.

However, the paper could be strengthened in a few key areas:

1.  **Computational Cost:** The algorithm for training the model appears to involve computationally expensive steps. To better assess the practicality of the proposed method, it would be beneficial to include comments in the experiments section on how long the model takes to train. A direct comparison of training times against existing approaches, such as DSN, would be particularly valuable.

2.  **Reproducibility:** The paper would be much stronger if reference code were made available for the experiments. Based on the model section alone, a practitioner may have difficulty reproducing the exact models and results. Providing the code would resolve potential ambiguities in the architectural details and greatly benefit the research community by ensuring the work is easily reproducible and extendable.
