This paper extends Generative Moment-Matching Networks (GMMNs) to the conditional setting by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments that define conditional distributions, enabling optimization with the same machinery used for the unconditional model. The authors demonstrate the resulting conditional GMMN on predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distilling predictions from a Bayesian model, showing competitive performance across these tasks.

Overall, the paper appears technically sound and the methodology is clearly presented. While I did not verify every equation in detail, the derivations are plausible and align with prior work on kernel embeddings and moment matching. The transition from unconditional to conditional generation is natural in concept, but the specific mechanism—particularly the derivation of the empirical estimator and its integration into the training objective—was not obvious to me beforehand. In this sense, the work has both novelty and practical value.

The framework is straightforward to apply in practice. Algorithm 1 concisely summarizes the training procedure, lowering the barrier for practitioners to implement the method. The experimental section provides a balanced view of the model’s capabilities, and the results suggest the method is a viable alternative for conditional generation tasks and for distilling more complex statistical procedures, such as Bayesian predictors, into fast feed-forward models.

Clarity and presentation are strong. The writing is clear and easy to follow, and the paper remains focused on the key ideas without unnecessary digressions. The appendix adds useful technical detail about the methods and experimental setup, which helps with reproducibility and understanding of design choices.

In terms of significance, conditional generative modeling is an important problem, and a kernel-based moment-matching approach that avoids adversarial training is appealing. This work provides a conceptually clean and implementable method with empirical evidence of competitiveness. I anticipate it will be of interest to researchers and practitioners working with deep generative models, conditional synthesis, and model distillation.

Minor comments and suggestions:
- It would help to briefly discuss computational complexity (both in theory and measured empirically), especially with respect to batch size and kernel computations in the conditional setting.
- A short discussion of kernel choice and sensitivity to hyperparameters would guide practitioners.
- If space permits, clarifying any failure modes or cases where the conditional MMD objective may underperform would be valuable.

In summary, this is a well-written and well-motivated paper that contributes a useful and nontrivial extension of GMMNs to conditional generation. The method is easy to apply, empirically competitive, and likely to be of practical relevance. I recommend acceptance.
