- Paper extends moment-matching/MMD training to conditional generative modeling, which is non-trivial because varying conditioning variables break shared target distributions across a batch.
- Strength: Addresses a meaningful and challenging gap in conditional MMD training.
- Strength: Proposed framework appears general and potentially applicable across conditional tasks.
- Strength: Leveraging moment matching in conditional spaces is promising as a stable alternative to likelihood or adversarial methods.
- Weakness: The paper launches into formal RKHS derivations without intuitive explanation of the final objective (Eq. 2), making it hard to follow for non-experts.
- Weakness: The practical algorithm implied by Eq. (2) is not clearly described; translation to per-batch computations and approximations is unclear.
- Weakness: Efficiency and computational complexity are insufficiently discussed; pairwise kernel costs may be quadratic in batch size.
- Weakness: Assumptions required for tractability and low bias (characteristic kernels, independence, regularization, repeated conditioning) are not clearly stated.
- Weakness: Supplemental material contains algorithmic details but is hard to follow and does not bridge theory to implemented training steps.
- Clarification requested: Provide a step-by-step, non-RKHS intuition/derivation of Eq. (2) starting from matching p(y|x) and qθ(y|x).
- Clarification requested: Explain why unconditional MMD works for fixed x and what complications arise when x varies across a mini-batch.
- Clarification requested: Show how the proposed objective aggregates conditional discrepancies across varying x.
- Clarification requested: Precisely outline per-step computations given a batch {(xi, yi)} and generated samples {ŷij}: matrices/vectors formed and kernels used.
- Clarification requested: State whether the objective is an unbiased or biased estimator of population conditional MMD and how variance is controlled.
- Clarification requested: Provide computational and memory complexity in terms of batch size B, samples per x m, and feature/random-feature dimension.
- Clarification requested: State whether there are O(B^2) pairwise terms over x and/or y and what approximations or batching strategies mitigate this.
- Clarification requested: Do you require multiple outputs per x (m>1) to estimate conditionals well, and what m is needed in practice?
- Clarification requested: Explain how matching is enforced “per x” vs accidentally matching across unrelated conditionings within a batch.
- Clarification requested: Do you reuse identical x values or rely on an x-kernel to transfer information across nearby x, and how is x-kernel bandwidth chosen and analyzed?
- Clarification requested: Specify kernel properties required (e.g., characteristic kernels) for the discrepancy to be a valid metric between conditional distributions.
- Clarification requested: Describe any regularization (e.g., on kernel matrices) essential for numerical stability.
- Clarification requested: Under what conditions does optimizing Eq. (2) lead to consistent estimation or convergence of qθ(y|x) to p(y|x)?
- Implementation detail request: Provide pseudocode for a single update including sampling strategy, kernel feature computation, objective assembly, and backpropagation.
- Implementation detail request: Describe how gradients flow through the conditional objective to model parameters θ.
- Implementation detail request: State whether random Fourier features or low-rank approximations are used, and report their empirical impact on cost and accuracy.
- Exposition suggestion: Add an intuition-first section before the formal RKHS treatment and a compact derivation from conditional expectations to Eq. (2).
- Exposition suggestion: Include a small didactic 1D example illustrating batch computations, x-kernel coupling, and gradient calculation.
- Exposition suggestion: Report timing and memory per iteration versus batch size and m, and compare to a “same-conditional-per-batch” baseline.
- Exposition suggestion: Clarify hyperparameter sensitivity (kernel choices and bandwidths for x and y, number of samples per x).
- Minor comment: Ensure consistent notation around Eq. (2), especially indices over conditioning variables and generated samples.
- Minor comment: Summarize key algorithmic steps from the supplement in the main text so the appendix is not required to understand the core method.
- Minor comment: Clearly position the method relative to conditional mean embeddings and prior conditional MMD objectives, highlighting the novel contribution.
- Overall assessment: The idea is important and promising but the presentation obscures key ideas and practicalities; clearer intuition, explicit algorithmic steps, and efficiency/assumption discussion are required for impact.
