- Extends moment matching networks to the conditional setting, a non-trivial extension because moment matching operates on mini-batches rather than single examples.
- Core challenge: matching a distribution of generated samples to a distribution of targets when each target is conditioned on a different input in the mini-batch.
- Cannot use a single conditioning variable for the whole batch without losing computational advantage and model purpose.
- Main weakness: presentation favors mathematical formalism (e.g., RKHS) over intuition and practical clarity.
- Paper introduces advanced concepts early to derive Equation (2) without first providing an accessible, intuitive explanation.
- Practical implementation from Equation (2) is under-specified: missing explicit computations, step-by-step algorithm, efficiency analysis, and required assumptions.
- Some implementation details are relegated to the supplement but are not easy to follow.
- Strong recommendations: add an intuitive motivation for Equation (2) and a clear, step-by-step bridge from theory to a practical, implementable algorithm (including complexity/feasibility).
