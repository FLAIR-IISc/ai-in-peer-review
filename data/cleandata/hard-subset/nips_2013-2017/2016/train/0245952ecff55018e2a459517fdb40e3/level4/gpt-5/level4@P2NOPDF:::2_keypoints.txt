- Paper extends MMNs to the conditional setting, which is nontrivial because training must match many generated samples to many distinct conditions within a minibatch.
- Main concern: poor exposition—paper dives into formalism without giving intuition or a clear picture of computations and efficiency.
- Eq. (2) is insufficiently motivated: the paper fails to explain the assumptions that make it tractable or why it captures the learning goal.
- Missing explanation of what quantities are estimated in a minibatch and how conditional distributions differing across conditions are handled.
- Lack of a clear, low-math intuitive derivation analogous to prior non-conditional MMN explanations.
- Need a step-by-step mapping from Eq. (2) to a practical algorithm: how minibatch statistics are formed when each example has a different condition.
- Clarify how many samples per condition are needed, how gradients are computed/backpropagated, and any variance-reduction or approximation techniques used.
- Explicitly state required assumptions (kernel/feature properties, independence, unbiasedness/consistency) and discuss their implications.
- Detail computational complexity and memory costs per batch (e.g., pairwise terms), and discuss linear-time approximations, subsampling, low-rank methods, and trade-offs.
- Recommend summarizing the procedure in the main text with brief pseudocode or an algorithm box rather than relying on a hard-to-follow supplement.
- Overall: technical direction is promising and the conditional extension is meaningful, but clarity and a concrete training procedure are needed to be convincing.
