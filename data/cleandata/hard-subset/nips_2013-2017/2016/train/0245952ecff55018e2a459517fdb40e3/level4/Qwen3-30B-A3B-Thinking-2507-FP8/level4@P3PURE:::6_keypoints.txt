- Proposes conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional tasks via kernel embedding of conditional distributions and a conditional MMD (CMMD) metric.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Naive extension (train one GMMN per condition with shared parameters) suffers severe data sparsity, especially for continuous conditioning where a single example per condition breaks estimation.  
- CGMMN addresses sparsity by modeling the family of conditional distributions directly and matching the conditional embedding operator, avoiding per-condition estimation.  
- The naive baseline may still work for discrete-conditioning cases (e.g., 10 classes on MNIST); an explicit comparison would strengthen the paper.  
- CMMD objective is well-formulated and computationally efficient in that kernel matrices in Eq.2 do not depend on model parameters, allowing standard backpropagation.  
- Major concern: stochastic approximation error may require very large minibatches to control, increasing computational cost substantially due to expensive matrix inversions.  
- Experimental results on classification and Bayesian model distillation are compelling and support the method.  
- Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow without consulting cited papers, hurting readability.  
- Two technical corrections: (1) Line 77 incorrectly states the MMD estimator is unbiased; (2) Line 90 uses a conditional embedding operator inconsistent with reference [29], requiring a different cross-covariance operator definition.
