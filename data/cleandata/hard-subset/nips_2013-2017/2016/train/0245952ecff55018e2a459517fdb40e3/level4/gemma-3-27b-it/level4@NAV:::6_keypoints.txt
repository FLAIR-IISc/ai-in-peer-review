- Introduces CGMMNs that use kernel embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) to model conditional distributions for generation and prediction.
- Core innovation: match the model to a conditional embedding operator instead of fitting each conditional distribution separately.
- Avoids data sparsity issues of per-condition GMMNs, especially with continuous conditioning variables where each condition has few samples.
- Notes that per-condition GMMN with shared parameters is a simple baseline that can work for small discrete condition sets (e.g., MNIST); including this baseline would strengthen evaluation.
- CMMD formulation is well-presented and efficiently computable; K matrices are independent of model parameters, enabling backpropagation.
- Optimization may require large minibatches to reduce stochasticity, causing computational challenges (notably matrix inversion costs).
- Experimental results for classification and Bayesian model distillation are compelling.
- Explanations in Sections 2.3 and 3.1 need clearer exposition; required consulting original papers to understand concepts.
- Minor technical issues: incorrect claim of an unbiased MMD estimator (line 77), and the conditional embedding operator definition (line 90) deviates from reference [29], requiring a revised cross-covariance operator.
