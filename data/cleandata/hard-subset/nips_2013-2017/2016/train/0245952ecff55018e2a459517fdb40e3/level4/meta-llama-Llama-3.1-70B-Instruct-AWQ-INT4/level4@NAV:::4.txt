This paper presents an extension of the deep generative modeling approach known as "Generative Moment-Matching Networks" to accommodate conditional generation. This is achieved by deriving a kernel embedding of the moments that define the conditional distribution, enabling optimization via the same machinery as the unconditional version. The proposed model demonstrates competitive performance in predictive modeling, contextual generation, and distillation of Bayesian model predictions. The paper's technical soundness and clear presentation of the proposals are notable strengths. Although a detailed examination of all equations was not conducted, the paper appears to be well-founded. The novelty of the paper lies in the mechanism and derivation of the empirical estimator for the conditional extension of the generative moment matching framework, which is not immediately apparent. Consequently, the paper offers a useful and novel contribution. The framework's applicability, as illustrated in Algorithm 1, suggests that it will be easily adoptable by researchers interested in leveraging deep models for generative purposes or distilling complex statistical processes. The paper's clarity, readability, and comprehensive appendix, which provides detailed information on the methods and experiments, further enhance its value.
