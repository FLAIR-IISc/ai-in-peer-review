The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional version. Unlike in models that can do updates on single examples, where the extension is trivial (feeding the conditional into the network), here it is non-trivial. That's because we need to match distributions of large numbers of samples (mini-batches) to large numbers of inputs, but these distributions are not the same anymore (unless the same conditional would be used for a given update, which is inefficient).

The main problem with the paper is that they start with fancy math instead of giving the intuition behind this formula and do not highlight what the actual computations performed during this algorithm are and their efficiency. The authors should explain the details behind the formula that is being optimized in the end (eq (2)), how efficient it is, what assumptions are made to make it work, so that the reader sees what the actual computation performed is.

Equally, they do not explain the intuition behind it but instead start with formal math. The non-conditional version of the algorithm can be explained (and is in the original paper) without fancy math (e.g., reproducing kernel Hilbert space) - the formula that is minimized is intuitive. It would be good to come up with an equally good intuitive explanation for the conditional case.

It would be good to answer the following questions: 
- How would you intuitively come up with formula (2)?
- What are the steps from formula (2) to the practical algorithm? Some of it is in the supplement but hard to follow.

Strengths:
- The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional version, which is a non-trivial task.
- They provide a formal math explanation of the conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion.
- They evaluate their model on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge.

Weaknesses:
- The authors do not provide an intuitive explanation of the CMMD criterion and the actual computations performed during the algorithm.
- They do not highlight the efficiency of the algorithm and the assumptions made to make it work.
- The supplement is hard to follow, and some steps from formula (2) to the practical algorithm are not clear.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide an intuitive explanation of the CMMD criterion and the actual computations performed during the algorithm.
- Highlight the efficiency of the algorithm and the assumptions made to make it work.
- Clarify the steps from formula (2) to the practical algorithm in the main text or supplement.

Originality:
- The authors extend moment matching networks to a conditional version, which is a new contribution.
- They provide a formal math explanation of the CMMD criterion, which is a new contribution.

Significance:
- The authors evaluate their model on various tasks, including predictive modeling, contextual generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge, which demonstrates the significance of their contribution.
- Their model can be used in various applications, such as image generation, multimodal learning, and missing data imputation.

Quality:
- The authors provide a formal math explanation of the CMMD criterion, which is technically sound.
- They evaluate their model on various tasks, which demonstrates the quality of their contribution.
- However, the lack of an intuitive explanation and the efficiency of the algorithm are major drawbacks.

Clarity:
- The authors' writing style is formal and math-heavy, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
- They do not provide an intuitive explanation of the CMMD criterion and the actual computations performed during the algorithm.
- The supplement is hard to follow, and some steps from formula (2) to the practical algorithm are not clear.

Recommendation:
- Accept with minor revisions. The authors should provide an intuitive explanation of the CMMD criterion and the actual computations performed during the algorithm. They should also highlight the efficiency of the algorithm and the assumptions made to make it work.
