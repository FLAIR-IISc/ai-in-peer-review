- Introduces a neural-network method based on conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) that meaningfully extends generative modeling.  
- Evaluated on standard small-scale benchmarks to test both discriminative and generative performance.  
- Paper is generally well-written, but the mathematical exposition is sometimes hard to follow.  
- Chosen tasks are modest in scale but appropriate given recent literature.  
- Major concern: the training algorithm is computationally intensive.  
- Authors should provide comparative training-time analysis against methods like DSN to clarify practical feasibility.  
- Releasing reference code is recommended, as Section 3’s model description may not suffice for replication.  
- Empirical results support the core idea and objectives, making the contribution promising.  
- Lack of training-time benchmarks and code availability limits the paper’s utility and accessibility.  
- Overall assessment: a solid, incremental advance that would benefit from minor improvements and is worthy of consideration for publication.
