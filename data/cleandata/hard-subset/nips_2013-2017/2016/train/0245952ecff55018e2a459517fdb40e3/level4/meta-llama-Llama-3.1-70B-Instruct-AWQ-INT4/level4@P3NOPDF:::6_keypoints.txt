- Proposes Conditional Generative Moment Matching Networks (CGMMNs) extending GMMNs to conditional generation/prediction using kernel embedding and Conditional MMD (CMMD).  
- Core contribution: formulation of CMMD for measuring discrepancy between conditional distributions via conditional embedding operators.  
- Evaluated on classification, conditional generation, and distillation of Bayesian models with noteworthy experimental results.  
- Naive baseline (separate GMMN per conditional distribution sharing network) suffers from data sparsity, especially for continuous conditioning; could still work for small discrete condition sets (e.g., MNIST).  
- Suggestion: include comparison with the naive baseline for completeness.  
- CMMD objective is a useful contribution and can be computed similarly to unconditional MMD; K matrices in Eq.2 are independent of model parameters enabling backprop.  
- Practical concern: CMMD may require large minibatches to reduce minibatch stochasticity, increasing computational cost (notably matrix inverses).  
- Presentation of Sections 2.3 and 3.1 is hard to follow and needs clearer exposition or more self-contained explanation.  
- Minor correctness issues: claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (line 77) is incorrect.  
- Minor technical inconsistency: the conditional embedding operator definition (line 90) differs from reference [29] and may require a different cross-covariance operator definition.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
