- Introduces CGMMN: extends GMMN to model conditional distributions using CMMD and kernel mean embeddings.  
- Theoretical contributions: rigorous CMMD formulation (Theorem 3) and connection to conditional kernel mean embeddings (Theorem 2).  
- Training retains GMMN simplicity: uses backpropagation, avoiding GAN-style min-max optimization.  
- Demonstrated applications: predictive modeling (MNIST, SVHN), contextual generation (digits, faces), and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation (Boston housing).  
- Strength: sound theoretical foundation with clear links to prior kernel embedding and MMD work.  
- Strength: novel and meaningful extension to conditional generative modeling, addressing a literature gap.  
- Strength: versatile applications, with Bayesian knowledge distillation promising for accelerating Bayesian inference.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized writing and appropriate mathematical formalization.  
- Weakness: Remark 1 and Eq.(2) lack clarity—ambiguous "dot" operator and confusing use of "C" conflicting with conditional embedding notation.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is limited to simple datasets and lacks tests on challenging benchmarks (e.g., CIFAR-10, LSUN).  
- Weakness: insufficient comparison to state-of-the-art conditional models (conditional GANs, conditional VAEs) despite claiming competitive performance.  
- Weakness: explanation for why a low-dimensional latent space suffices for predictive tasks remains thin.  
- Quality: technically sound but would benefit from broader, more challenging experiments.  
- Clarity: generally clear, but notation in Remark 1 needs fixing to elucidate MMD–CMMD relationship.  
- Originality: meaningful and novel extension of moment-matching methods to conditional distributions.  
- Significance: promising applications, but impact would increase with stronger empirical evidence on complex tasks.  
- Revision request 1: clarify notation and mathematical relationships in Remark 1 and how Eq.(2) relates to weighted MMD.  
- Revision request 2: provide comprehensive comparisons with conditional GANs and conditional VAEs.  
- Revision request 3: include experiments on more challenging datasets (CIFAR-10, LSUN) to assess scalability.  
- Revision request 4: expand discussion comparing CGMMN to other conditional generative approaches and better justify latent-dimension choices.  
- Overall: valuable extension of GMMN to conditional settings with promising Bayesian application, but needs stronger empirical validation and clearer notation to be a stronger contribution.
