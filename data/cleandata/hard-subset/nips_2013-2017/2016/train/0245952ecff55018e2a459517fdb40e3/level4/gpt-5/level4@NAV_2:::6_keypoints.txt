- Introduces CGMMN: extends GMMNs to conditional prediction/generation using RKHS conditional embeddings and a conditional MMD (CMMD) objective.
- Core idea: match the conditional mean embedding operator for the whole set of conditionals instead of training separate GMMNs per conditioning value, avoiding data inefficiency when slices have few samples.
- Naïve baseline (one GMMN per conditioning value with shared parameters) could still work in discrete, low-cardinality settings (e.g., MNIST); including this comparison would be informative.
- CMMD has a clean form closely matching unconditional MMD (Remark 1); kernel K matrices depend only on conditioning variables and are independent of model parameters, so gradients can be backpropagated normally.
- Practical concern: CMMD may need large minibatches to reduce stochastic noise, making the objective costly and matrix inversions a potential computational bottleneck.
- Empirical evaluation on classification, conditional image generation, and Bayesian dark knowledge distillation; classification and distillation results are interesting.
- Exposition problems: Sections 2.3 and 3.1 are hard to follow and require consulting cited references to fully understand.
- Minor technical issues: (1) Claim that the MMD estimator is unbiased (Line 77) is incorrect as stated. (2) Expression for the conditional embedding operator (Line 90) appears inconsistent with [29] and seems to require a different cross-covariance operator definition.
- Overall verdict: CMMD is a compelling and useful approach for modeling conditional distributions.
