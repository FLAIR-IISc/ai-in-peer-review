This paper introduces a loss function for directed generative models with conditional variables. The method extends generative moment matching networks by replacing the unconditional MMD loss with a conditional variant derived from conditional kernel mean embeddings. The approach is evaluated on multiple generative tasks, including classification, generation of handwritten digits and faces, and distilling knowledge in Bayesian models.

This is my second time reviewing the paper. The previous concerns were minor: the use of a low-dimensional latent space and whether the approach scales to higher-dimensional latent spaces, the comparative utility of this model versus alternatives, and several clarifications. These appear to have been addressed in the revision.

Regarding Remark 1, I do not see how Equation (2) maps to the weighted MMD form. What exactly does the dot notation denote—matrix multiplication or the Hadamard (elementwise) product? Please expand this part so the mapping is explicit. Also, the choice of the symbol C is unfortunate given that C is already used for the conditional embedding.

Theorems 1 and 2 are tied to prior work, but Theorem 3 lacks a reference. Please cite a source containing a proof or include a proof in the paper.

The empirical results are decent. While prior reviewers noted that supervised learning may not be the most compelling application, the authors’ stated aim was to illustrate the model’s versatility, which seems reasonable. The distilled Bayesian inference application is interesting, but it is demonstrated on only one dataset with one model, so its broader usefulness is unclear. I expect this approach to gain the most traction in pure generative modeling. In that setting, the experiments are comparable to those in the original GMM paper. The digits and faces datasets are sufficient to show the idea works, but wider adoption would likely require compelling results on more challenging datasets such as CIFAR-10 or LSUN.
