This paper presents conditional generative moment matching networks (CGMMN), an extension of generative moment matching networks (GMMNs) to conditional generation and prediction applications. The authors propose a novel approach to model conditional distributions using kernel embedding and conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) metric. The paper is well-organized, and the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of CGMMN in various tasks, including classification, conditional generation, and distilling Bayesian models.

The main idea of CGMMN is to learn a conditional distribution given some input variables based on a CMMD criterion. The authors extend the capability of GMMN to address a wide range of application problems while keeping the training process simple. The CMMD objective is well-formulated and can be easily computed, similar to the unconditional maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) objective. The authors also provide a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for optimizing the CMMD objective.

The experiments demonstrate the competitive performance of CGMMN in various tasks. The results on classification tasks, such as MNIST and SVHN, show that CGMMN is comparable to state-of-the-art competitors. The conditional generation results on MNIST and Yale Face datasets demonstrate the ability of CGMMN to generate diverse and realistic samples. The distillation of Bayesian models experiment shows that CGMMN can effectively learn a conditional distribution model for efficient prediction.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to model conditional distributions using kernel embedding and CMMD metric.
* The CMMD objective is well-formulated and can be easily computed.
* The experiments demonstrate the competitive performance of CGMMN in various tasks.
* The paper provides a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for optimizing the CMMD objective.

Weaknesses:

* The presentation in sections 2.3 and 3.1 is not easy to follow, and the reader needs to dive into the referenced papers to understand the details.
* The paper does not provide a comparison with a naive approach of extending GMMNs to conditional setting, which would be interesting to see.
* The computation of the CMMD objective requires big minibatches, which can be computationally expensive.

Originality:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to model conditional distributions using kernel embedding and CMMD metric.
* The CMMD objective is a new contribution, and the authors provide a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for optimizing it.

Significance:

* The paper demonstrates the effectiveness of CGMMN in various tasks, including classification, conditional generation, and distilling Bayesian models.
* The proposed approach has the potential to improve the performance of deep generative models in conditional generation and prediction tasks.

Clarity:

* The paper is well-organized, and the experiments are clearly described.
* However, the presentation in sections 2.3 and 3.1 is not easy to follow, and the reader needs to dive into the referenced papers to understand the details.

Quality:

* The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.
* The authors provide a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for optimizing the CMMD objective, which is a significant contribution.

Overall, I think the paper provides a nice way to model conditional distributions using kernel embedding and CMMD metric. The experiments demonstrate the competitive performance of CGMMN in various tasks, and the proposed approach has the potential to improve the performance of deep generative models in conditional generation and prediction tasks. However, the presentation in sections 2.3 and 3.1 needs to be improved, and the paper should provide a comparison with a naive approach of extending GMMNs to conditional setting.
