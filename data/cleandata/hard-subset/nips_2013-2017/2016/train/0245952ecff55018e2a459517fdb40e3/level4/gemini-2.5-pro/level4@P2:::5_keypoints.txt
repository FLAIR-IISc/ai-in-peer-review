- Introduces Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks (CGMMN) and a Conditional Maximum Mean Discrepancy (CMMD) objective for learning p(y|x).  
- CMMD measures dissimilarity between conditional distributions via the Hilbert–Schmidt norm of conditional kernel mean embedding operators.  
- CGMMN is a deep network that generates y from x and noise h, trained by minimizing the CMMD loss with backpropagation.  
- Strength: Novel, theoretically grounded extension of moment-matching to conditional modeling, building on kernel embedding theory.  
- Strength: Training is simple and stable, avoiding adversarial minimax optimization required by GANs.  
- Strength: Broad applicability demonstrated on predictive tasks, conditional image generation, and Bayesian model distillation.  
- Weakness: Unclear treatment of mean-centering in the empirical conditional embedding operator; omission of μ_X ⊗ μ_Y needs clarification.  
- Weakness: Predictive performance is weaker than strong baselines (e.g., CMMVA) on MNIST and SVHN, raising practical concerns for discriminative tasks.  
- Weakness: Generative evaluation lacks quantitative and comparative baselines (e.g., CGANs), making sample quality/diversity hard to assess.  
- Weakness: Paper contains minor typographical errors that should be fixed.  
- After rebuttal: Authors clarified the CMMD implementation and experimental choices; reviewer’s concerns were addressed and the paper is recommended for acceptance.
