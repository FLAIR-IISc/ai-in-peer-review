The paper "Conditional Generative Moment-Matching Networks" proposes a novel approach to learning conditional distributions using a conditional maximum mean discrepancy (CMMD) criterion. The authors extend the generative moment-matching networks (GMMN) framework to accommodate conditional variables, leveraging the concept of conditional kernel mean embeddings. The proposed method is evaluated on various tasks, including classification, image generation, and distilling knowledge from Bayesian models.

The paper provides a clear and concise introduction to the background and motivation of the work. The authors effectively summarize the main ideas and relate them to previous work in the field, including GMMN and conditional kernel mean embeddings. The technical presentation is generally sound, with a clear explanation of the CMMD criterion and the proposed CGMMN architecture.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach on various tasks. The classification results on MNIST and SVHN datasets are competitive with state-of-the-art methods, and the image generation results on MNIST and Yale Face datasets are visually appealing. The distillation of knowledge from Bayesian models is an interesting application, and the results on the Boston housing dataset are encouraging.

However, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed. The notation in Remark 1 is unclear, and the authors should provide more detail on the mapping between Equation (2) and the weighted MMD form. The choice of notation for the conditional embedding operator is also somewhat unfortunate, as it conflicts with the notation used for the CMMD criterion. Additionally, Theorem 3 is not properly referenced, and the authors should provide a proof or a reference to a paper containing a proof.

Overall, the paper presents a novel and effective approach to learning conditional distributions using a CMMD criterion. The experimental results are decent, and the proposed method has the potential to be useful in various applications, including pure generative modeling. However, the authors should address the minor issues mentioned above to improve the clarity and rigor of the presentation.

Strengths:

* Novel approach to learning conditional distributions using a CMMD criterion
* Effective extension of the GMMN framework to accommodate conditional variables
* Competitive results on classification and image generation tasks
* Interesting application to distilling knowledge from Bayesian models

Weaknesses:

* Minor notation issues (Remark 1 and Theorem 3)
* Limited evaluation of the proposed method on more challenging datasets
* Unclear whether the approach will be widely adopted by the research community
