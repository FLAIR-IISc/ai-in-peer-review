The authors propose an extension of moment matching networks to their conditional version. Unlike models that can be updated with a single example, where incorporating conditionals is straightforward, this extension is non-trivial. The challenge arises from matching distributions of a large number of samples (mini-batch) to a large number of inputs, where these distributions are no longer identical, unless the same conditional is used for a given update, which is inefficient.

The primary concern with the paper is its presentation. The authors delve into complex mathematical formulations without providing an intuitive understanding of the underlying concepts. Specifically, they fail to elucidate the actual computations performed during the algorithm and their efficiency. Furthermore, the formula being optimized (eq. (2)) is not thoroughly explained, including its efficiency, underlying assumptions, and the resulting computational implications.

In contrast, the non-conditional version of the algorithm can be explained intuitively, without relying on advanced mathematical concepts such as reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces. The minimized formula in the original paper is straightforward to understand. A similar intuitive explanation for the conditional case would be beneficial. To improve the paper, the authors should address the following questions:

- How can one intuitively derive formula (2)?
- What are the steps from formula (2) to the practical algorithm?

Some of these details are provided in the supplement, but they are difficult to follow. Providing clear, step-by-step explanations and intuition behind the formula would significantly enhance the paper's clarity and readability.
