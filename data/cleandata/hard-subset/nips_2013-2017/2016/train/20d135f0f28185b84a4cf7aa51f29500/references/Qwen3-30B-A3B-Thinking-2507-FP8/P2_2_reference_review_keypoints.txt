- Proposes sMCL: a winner-take-gradient training method that directly minimizes oracle loss to produce diverse, specialized ensemble members without costly retraining.  
- Key insight: update only the lowest-error predictor per example so ensembles naturally specialize.  
- Empirical evaluation on CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO shows consistent improvements over independent ensembles, MCL, and related baselines.  
- Experimental metrics include oracle accuracy, IoU, and CIDEr-D, with qualitative visualizations showing emergent specializations.  
- Strong technical execution and comprehensive experiments; algorithm is simple and practical for deep networks.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why sMCL yields specialization or guarantees convergence.  
- Claim of being "parameter-free" is misleading because ensemble size M is a required hyperparameter and its effect isn't explored.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, organized, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Original contribution: makes Multiple Choice Learning practical for deep nets via stochastic block gradient descent, avoiding sequential retraining.  
- Significant practical impact: simple, general, efficient method producing 6–10% relative gains in oracle metrics and interpretable ensemble behaviors.  
- Weaknesses: no analysis of computational cost versus standard ensembles and limited discussion of failure cases/limitations.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept.
