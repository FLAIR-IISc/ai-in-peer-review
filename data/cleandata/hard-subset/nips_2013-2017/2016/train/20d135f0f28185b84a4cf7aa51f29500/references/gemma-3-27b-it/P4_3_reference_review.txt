This paper proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a method for training diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss. The core idea is to modify stochastic gradient descent to backpropagate gradients only to the ensemble member with the lowest loss for each example in a batch, effectively implementing a “winner-take-gradient” scheme. The authors demonstrate the applicability of sMCL to image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning tasks, showcasing improved performance compared to standard ensembles and other diversity-inducing methods like MCL and the approach by Dey et al. The paper is well-motivated, framing the problem within the broader context of multiple choice learning and highlighting the limitations of existing ensemble training techniques.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed sMCL algorithm is a clever and efficient adaptation of stochastic gradient descent. The derivation of the gradient update rule is clear and logically sound. The method addresses a crucial limitation of previous approaches like MCL which were computationally expensive due to repeated retraining.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and experimental results. The notation is consistent, and the algorithms (1 & 2) are presented in a readable format. The visualizations in Figures 4, 5, 6, and 7 effectively illustrate the emergent specialization of ensemble members.
*   **Originality:** While building upon the concept of Multiple Choice Learning, this work significantly advances the field by providing a practical and scalable algorithm (sMCL) for training diverse deep ensembles. The application of this approach to different network architectures and tasks demonstrates its versatility.
*   **Significance:** The problem addressed – generating diverse and reliable predictions – is highly relevant to real-world applications where uncertainty is inherent. sMCL offers a promising approach to improve the robustness and interpretability of deep learning models. The demonstrated performance gains across multiple tasks suggest potential for broader adoption. The analysis of ensemble member specialization provides valuable insights into the behavior of these models.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Comparison to Modern Ensemble Methods:** The comparison to baselines is somewhat limited. While the included baselines are relevant (Indp., MCL, Dey et al.), recent advances in ensemble diversity techniques, such as those leveraging adversarial training or knowledge distillation specifically for diversity, are not considered. A more comprehensive comparison would strengthen the claims of superiority.
*   **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper does not extensively explore the sensitivity of sMCL to hyperparameters, such as learning rate and batch size. A brief analysis of these parameters and their influence on performance would be beneficial.
*   **Computational Cost Discussion:** Although sMCL is presented as more efficient than MCL, a more detailed discussion of the computational cost (e.g., training time, memory usage) in relation to standard ensembles would be valuable. The exact speedup factor (5x compared to MCL) could be more rigorously quantified, perhaps with a table comparing training times.
*   **Captions/Figure References:** There's a minor inconsistency in caption references within the paper.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound algorithm for training diverse deep ensembles.
*   The experimental results demonstrate significant improvements over existing baselines across multiple tasks.
*   The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides insightful analysis of the learned ensemble members.
*   The proposed method addresses a practically important problem with real-world relevance.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The comparison to other ensemble diversity techniques is somewhat limited.
*   The hyperparameter sensitivity analysis is not comprehensive.
*   A more detailed computational cost analysis would be beneficial.


**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of ensemble learning. The proposed sMCL algorithm is a significant improvement over previous approaches, offering a practical and scalable solution for training diverse deep ensembles. Despite minor weaknesses, the strengths of the paper outweigh the drawbacks. I recommend acceptance.
