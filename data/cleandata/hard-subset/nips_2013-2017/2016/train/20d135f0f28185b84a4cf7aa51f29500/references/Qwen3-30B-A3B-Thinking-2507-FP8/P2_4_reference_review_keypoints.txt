- Introduces sMCL: a stochastic Multiple Choice Learning method that optimizes oracle loss by backpropagating gradients only to the lowest-loss ensemble member ("winner-take-gradient").  
- Addresses MCL's practical limitation by avoiding costly retraining; sMCL is parameter-free and applicable to any SGD-trained model.  
- Provides a clear theoretical foundation (eq.2) showing sMCL's gradient update matches the ideal case when the winner is identified.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements (6–10% in oracle metrics) across CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO.  
- Reports sMCL is approximately 5× faster than prior MCL approaches.  
- Shows automatic, interpretable specialization among ensemble members (class-wise expertise and diverse predictions).  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and uses appropriate baselines (classical ensembles, MCL, Dey et al.).  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and compellingly motivated with clear formalization and effective figures.  
- Original contribution: concurrent stochastic training that avoids iterative retraining and yields emergent specialization without manual intervention.  
- Broad applicability across architectures (CNN, FCN, CNN+RNN) and tasks, making it practically useful for real-world applications needing diverse hypotheses.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of scenarios where diversity is undesirable and lack of analysis for large ensemble sizes (M > 6).  
- Weakness: Insufficient detailed computational complexity/time-per-epoch breakdown to fully justify the 5× speedup claim.  
- Weakness: Lacks deeper theoretical justification of convergence properties and formal relation to the original MCL objective.  
- Overall: Strong contribution with compelling empirical validation; minor gaps in limitations discussion and theory.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
