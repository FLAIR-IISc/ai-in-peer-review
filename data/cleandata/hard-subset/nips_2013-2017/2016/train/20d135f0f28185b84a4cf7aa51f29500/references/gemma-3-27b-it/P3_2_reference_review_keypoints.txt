- Overall assessment: sMCL is a compelling, technically sound, and practical method for training diverse deep ensembles; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Contribution: Proposes sMCL, a scalable SGD-based algorithm that directly minimizes oracle loss for diverse hypothesis generation.  
- Key technique: Uses a “winner-take-gradient” approach to enable efficient ensemble training without costly retraining.  
- Empirical strength: Comprehensive experiments across image classification, segmentation, and captioning showing consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Specialization finding: Ensemble members automatically develop meaningful specializations (class- or segment-specific expertise).  
- Presentation: Well-written, clear notation, and concise algorithmic descriptions.  
- Hyperparameter concern: Claim of “parameter-free” is misleading—batch size, learning rate, and ensemble size can affect performance and need sensitivity analysis.  
- Beam search baseline: Comparison to beam search in captioning is uneven since beam search is an inference technique for a single model, not an ensemble; computational-cost comparison is needed.  
- Theoretical gap: Lacks deeper theoretical analysis of convergence or formal justification for avoiding poor local optima.  
- Metric limitation: Reliance on oracle loss may not reflect real downstream selection processes; evaluation on an actual downstream task is desirable.  
- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a novel, well-motivated, and empirically validated solution.  
- Against acceptance: Needs more thorough hyperparameter analysis, a nuanced beam-search comparison, and stronger theoretical grounding.  
- Revision suggestions: Add hyperparameter sensitivity discussion, clarify/compare beam search computationally, include theoretical remarks or limitations, and report at least one downstream-task evaluation.
