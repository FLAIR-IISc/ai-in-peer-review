- Introduces sMCL, a novel training algorithm using a "winner-take-gradient" SGD scheme to directly minimize oracle loss for diverse deep ensembles.  
- sMCL enables concurrent training of ensemble members and avoids costly iterative retraining required by prior MCL methods.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning benchmarks.  
- Positions sMCL against traditional diversity methods and builds on Multiple Choice Learning, improving practicality for deep networks.  
- Clarifies relation to boosting-like sequential methods (Dey et al.), highlighting sMCL’s advantage of parallel training.  
- Novelty and significance: directly addressing oracle loss and overcoming MCL’s computational limits is a substantial contribution.  
- Empirical results: consistent superiority over baselines (MCL, Dey et al.) in oracle accuracy with convincing qualitative examples.  
- Interpretability: ensemble members naturally specialize (class-specific segmentation, diverse caption patterns) with effective visualizations.  
- Practicality: simple, parameter-free, minimal code changes for existing SGD pipelines and broadly adaptable.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with concise, accessible mathematical formulation.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed hyperparameter sensitivity analysis to assess robustness across settings.  
- Weakness: needs a more rigorous computational cost comparison (wall-clock, memory) versus MCL and baselines.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of convergence properties for the winner-take-gradient approach.  
- Weakness: more targeted ablation studies (e.g., ensemble size, loss choice) would clarify drivers of performance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, strong empirical evidence, interpretability, practicality, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient hyperparameter analysis, limited theoretical justification, and missing ablations.  
- Overall: strong, significant contribution with compelling results; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
