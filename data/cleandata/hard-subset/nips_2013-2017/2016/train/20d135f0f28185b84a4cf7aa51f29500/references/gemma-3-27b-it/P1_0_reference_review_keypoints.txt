- sMCL: train deep ensembles by applying gradients only to the ensemble member with lowest loss per example (stochastic block SGD) to directly minimize oracle loss.
- Practical advance over prior MCL: enables concurrent training of ensemble members without iterative retraining.
- Strong empirical results: outperforms classical ensembles, MCL, and baselines across classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Learned ensembles show interpretable specialization (label-space clustering and qualitative specialization in segmentation/captioning).
- Clear derivation of the gradient update rule grounding the method theoretically.
- Method is parameter-free and loss-agnostic, making it broadly applicable across architectures.
- Paper is well-written and organized, with a clear algorithmic description (Algorithm 2).
- Builds on Guzman-Rivera et al. (MCL) and is positioned as the first scalable MCL formulation for deep networks.
- Limited comparison to more recent diversity-promoting ensemble techniques (e.g., adversarial methods, distillation, dropout/perturbation ensembles).
- Missing systematic analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (batch size, learning rate, momentum).
- Scalability/memory concerns: concurrent training of large deep ensembles can be resource-intensive; captioning experiments hit memory limits.
- Theoretical analysis is limited—lacks a formal explanation of why sMCL induces specialization.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance based on novelty, strong results, interpretability, and clarity.
- Main criticisms: limited comparisons, lack of hyperparameter study, and scalability caveats.
- Reviewer confidence: 8/10.
