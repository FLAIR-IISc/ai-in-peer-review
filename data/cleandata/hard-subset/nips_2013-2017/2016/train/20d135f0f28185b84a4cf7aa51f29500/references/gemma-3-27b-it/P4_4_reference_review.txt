The paper introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a method for training diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss. The core idea is to modify stochastic gradient descent to only backpropagate the gradient to the ensemble member that achieves the lowest loss on each example within a batch. This approach avoids the computationally expensive retraining steps required by previous Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) methods. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of sMCL across various tasks – image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning – showcasing its ability to induce specialization among ensemble members and improve oracle performance.

This work builds upon the MCL paradigm [8, 9], addressing its key limitation – the high computational cost of iterative retraining. It differentiates itself from related work on ensemble diversity [27, 21, 19, 26, 2, 12] by directly optimizing for oracle loss rather than relying on techniques like resampling or joint loss functions. Furthermore, it contrasts with methods for generating multiple solutions from a single model [3, 15, 16, 23, 24] and sequential training approaches [5, 29] by providing a parallel and efficient SGD-based algorithm applicable to modern deep architectures.  The authors appropriately cite relevant prior work and position their contribution within the existing literature.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed sMCL algorithm is logically sound and well-motivated. The derivation of the gradient update rule is clear and concise.  The method leverages the inherent properties of SGD to efficiently implement oracle loss minimization.
* **Novelty:** The adaptation of MCL to deep networks via a stochastic gradient descent approach is a significant contribution. The algorithm overcomes the practical limitations of previous MCL implementations, making it feasible for large-scale deep learning models.
* **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key concepts and the proposed algorithm. The notation is consistent and the explanations are easy to follow. The use of algorithms 1 and 2 effectively illustrates the process.
* **Significance:**  The results demonstrate that sMCL consistently outperforms existing baselines across different tasks and network architectures.  The ability to induce specialization within ensembles and improve oracle performance has practical implications for applications requiring diverse and reliable predictions. The method is broadly applicable and parameter-free.
* **Experimental Validation:** The authors conduct extensive experiments with strong baselines. The qualitative results, particularly in semantic segmentation and image captioning, provide valuable insights into the behavior of sMCL ensembles. The detailed analysis of ensemble member specialization is compelling.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the intuition behind sMCL is clear, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of its convergence properties and the conditions under which it is guaranteed to outperform standard ensemble learning would be valuable.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** Although the paper claims sMCL is parameter-free, the standard hyperparameters for the underlying SGD optimization (learning rate, batch size, momentum, weight decay) still require tuning. A discussion of the sensitivity of sMCL performance to these hyperparameters would be helpful.
* **Comparison to Beam Search:** The comparison to beam search in image captioning is somewhat limited. While sMCL outperforms beam search with a small beam width, a more thorough comparison with larger beam widths and different decoding strategies might be beneficial.
* **Scalability Discussion:** While the method is presented as scalable, the experiments are limited to relatively small ensemble sizes (up to 6). A discussion of the expected performance and computational cost as the ensemble size increases would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant problem in ensemble learning – efficiently optimizing for oracle loss.
* The proposed sMCL algorithm is a novel and effective solution that overcomes the limitations of previous methods.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the broad applicability of sMCL.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the key concepts.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The lack of rigorous theoretical analysis is a minor weakness.
* The limited discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity could be improved.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of ensemble learning. The proposed sMCL algorithm is novel, technically sound, and demonstrably effective. While the paper could benefit from a more rigorous theoretical analysis and a more detailed discussion of hyperparameter sensitivity, the strengths of the work outweigh its weaknesses. I recommend accepting this paper.
