- Proposes sMCL, a novel stochastic algorithm that directly minimizes oracle loss for diverse deep ensembles.  
- Replaces costly MCL retraining with concurrent SGD, enabling scalable training for deep networks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning) and architectures.  
- Shows emergent specialization among ensemble members and provides visual examples supporting diversity.  
- Positions work as a practical extension of Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL, addressing its computational bottleneck.  
- Differentiates from single-model multi-solution methods by focusing on training diverse ensembles.  
- References and situates itself well within ensemble learning literature.  
- Novelty: sMCL is a significant practical advance over prior MCL approaches.  
- Efficiency: Reports ~5x speedup over prior MCL, making it suitable for large datasets and models.  
- Generality: Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic, applicable to many tasks.  
- Empirical validation: Strong improvements over baselines (independent ensembles, MCL, Dey et al.) are convincing.  
- Interpretability: Analysis of specialization is insightful and supports claims of meaningful diversity.  
- Presentation: Paper is clear, well-organized, and mathematically accessible.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Lacks thorough analysis of robustness to learning rate, batch size, and other hyperparameters.  
- Baselines: Missing comparisons to some stronger or more recent baselines (e.g., advanced decorrelation methods, diverse beam search variants).  
- Mechanism of specialization: Demonstrates specialization but provides limited explanation of why it occurs; deeper feature-analysis is suggested.  
- Memory footprint: Concurrent ensemble training still demands significant memory; mitigation strategies are not discussed.  
- Recommendation: Strong paper worth acceptance with minor revisions; suggestions include hyperparameter discussion, additional baselines, and expanded analysis of specialization.
