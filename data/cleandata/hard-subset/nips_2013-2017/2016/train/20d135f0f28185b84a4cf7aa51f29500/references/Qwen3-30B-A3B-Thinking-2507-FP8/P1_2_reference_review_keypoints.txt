- Summary: Proposes sMCL, a stochastic "winner-take-gradient" method to train diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss without costly retraining.

- Strength 1 (Motivation): Strong real-world motivation for multiple-prediction systems in ambiguous tasks, connecting to prior structured-prediction work.

- Strength 2 (Technical): Simple, general SGD-compatible method that avoids previous MCL retraining and is motivated by derivative analysis.

- Strength 3 (Experiments): Validated across classification, segmentation, and captioning, showing consistent oracle improvements (6–10%) and ~5x speedup over MCL.

- Strength 4 (Analysis): Demonstrates automatic, interpretable specialization among ensemble members (e.g., class- or segment-specific experts).

- Strength 5 (Practicality): Easy to implement, parameter-free, and agnostic to architecture and loss function.

- Weakness 1 (Theory): Lacks deeper theoretical analysis, convergence guarantees, and relation between relaxation parameter k and specialization.

- Weakness 2 (Related Work): Insufficient comparison to more recent diverse-ensemble methods (e.g., entropy-regularized ensembles).

- Weakness 3 (Ensemble size): Does not analyze how to choose ensemble size M or marginal returns beyond M=6.

- Weakness 4 (Evaluation): Relies on oracle metrics without discussing limitations or how oracle gains translate to real downstream performance.

- Weakness 5 (Implementation): Missing implementation details for ties, scaling to very large ensembles, and other practical challenges.

- Overall Assessment: Strong, well-written contribution with practical impact and insightful findings on specialization.

- Recommendation: Accept.
