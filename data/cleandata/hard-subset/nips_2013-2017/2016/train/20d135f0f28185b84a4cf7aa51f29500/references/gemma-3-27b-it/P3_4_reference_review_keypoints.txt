- Overall assessment: sMCL is a compelling, well-motivated method and the reviewer recommends acceptance.  
- Core idea: sMCL directly minimizes oracle loss using a "winner-take-gradient" SGD procedure that enables concurrent training of ensemble members.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and overcomes its scalability limits; clearly differentiated from resampling/boosting and related methods.  
- Practicality & scalability: Scales to modern deep networks and is far more efficient than previous MCL approaches.  
- Empirical strengths: Strong, consistent results across image classification, semantic segmentation, and captioning, outperforming classical ensembles and baselines.  
- Emergent specialization: Ensemble members develop specialized expertise, supported by visualizations and analyses.  
- Theoretical soundness: Stochastic gradient update derivation is clear and well-connected to optimization literature.  
- Clarity & presentation: Paper is well-written, organized, and concise.  
- Parameter-free & agnostic: Method is parameter-free and applicable to any SGD-trained model.  
- Weakness — ablation: Limited ablation studies (e.g., effect of ensemble size M and diminishing returns).  
- Weakness — tie-breaking: Tie-breaking for lowest-error predictor is arbitrary and its impact is not explored.  
- Weakness — oracle cost: Oracle evaluation during training/testing remains computationally expensive for complex tasks.  
- Weakness — hyperparameter sensitivity: Claim of parameter-free advantage would benefit from sensitivity analysis of baseline hyperparameters.  
- For acceptance: Significant methodological advance, addresses MCL scalability, broad applicability, and strong empirical validation.  
- Against acceptance: Concerns limited ablations, tie-breaking strategy, and persistent oracle evaluation cost.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
