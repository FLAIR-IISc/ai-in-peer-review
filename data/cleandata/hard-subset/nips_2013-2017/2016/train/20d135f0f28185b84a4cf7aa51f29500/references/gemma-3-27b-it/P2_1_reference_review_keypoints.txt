- Introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a training algorithm for deep ensembles that minimizes oracle loss.
- Core idea: “winner-take-gradient” during SGD, assigning each example to the ensemble member with lowest loss and backpropagating only through that member.
- sMCL avoids costly iterative retraining required by prior MCL methods, making it practical for deep networks.
- Demonstrated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with consistent improvements over strong baselines.
- Emergent, interpretable specialization of ensemble members observed in experiments.
- Algorithm derivation and relation to oracle loss minimization are clear and technically sound.
- Paper is generally well-written with clear notation and straightforward algorithm presentation.
- Addresses an important, realistic problem (diverse/reliable predictions) with practical significance.
- Lacks comparison to more recent diversity-inducing techniques (e.g., adversarial or information-maximization methods).
- Does not fully analyze hyperparameter sensitivity (batch size, learning rate, ensemble size) and its effect on performance.
- Qualitative analysis is limited; more systematic diversity metrics (e.g., for captioning) would strengthen the evaluation.
- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and effective contribution with minor weaknesses that don’t undermine the work.
- Recommendation: accept (Strong Accept, 8.5/10) and address comparison and sensitivity questions in rebuttal.
