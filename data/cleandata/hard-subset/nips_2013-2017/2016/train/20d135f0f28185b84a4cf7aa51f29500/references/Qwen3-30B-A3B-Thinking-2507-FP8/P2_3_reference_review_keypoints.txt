- Proposes sMCL, a practical "winner-take-gradient" SGD algorithm that trains deep ensembles to optimize oracle loss by backpropagating only through the ensemble member with lowest error per example.  
- Formalizes Multiple Choice Learning for deep networks and provides an easy-to-implement Algorithm 2.  
- Demonstrates consistent oracle metric improvements (6–10%) across CIFAR-10 classification, Pascal VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning.  
- Shows sMCL is ~5× faster than prior MCL retraining-based methods and requires no extra hyperparameters or architecture changes.  
- Provides thorough experiments, ablations (e.g., varying k), and qualitative analysis showing automatic emergence of ensemble specialization.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and presents informative figures and experimental detail.  
- Method is original: simple, practical extension of MCL that avoids costly retraining and is the first practical algorithm for diverse deep ensembles in this setting.  
- Significant practical relevance for tasks needing multiple plausible outputs (autonomy, assistive systems) due to improved oracle performance and interpretability.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis explaining why the winner-take-gradient strategy converges or alters the optimization landscape.  
- Comparison misses some recent diversity-focused ensemble methods (e.g., ICML 2020, NeurIPS 2021 papers).  
- Scalability not fully explored: experiments use small ensembles (M=1–6), so behavior for larger ensembles is unclear.  
- Overall recommendation: Accept — strong empirical contribution, clarity, and practical impact despite moderate theoretical and comparison gaps.
