- Presents sMCL: a stochastic adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning enabling concurrent training of diverse deep ensembles.  
- Addresses MCL scalability by removing the costly retraining bottleneck and enabling efficient SGD-based training.  
- Empirically validated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, consistently outperforming strong baselines.  
- Derivation of the stochastic gradient update is clear and the algorithm is simple to implement.  
- sMCL induces natural specialization in ensemble members, improving interpretability (e.g., class-wise and segmentation analyses).  
- Positions the work well relative to prior methods, highlighting advantages over resampling, joint-loss, and sequential/boosting approaches.  
- Comparison to beam search in captioning shows beam search can yield higher oracle performance, but trade-offs between ensembles and beam search need deeper analysis.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and supported by informative figures and tables.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and potential failure modes, such as sensitivity to hyperparameters or cases where specialization may not occur.  
- Claims of being parameter-free are misleading since learning rates, batch sizes, etc., still require tuning; hyperparameter selection is underexplained.  
- Theoretical analysis is limited; convergence properties and the impact of stochasticity on oracle loss minimization are not thoroughly explored.  
- Comparison to beam search in captioning experiments feels incomplete and would benefit from a fuller discussion of trade-offs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and technically sound algorithm, strong empirical results, interpretability, clear presentation, and high potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient limitations discussion, limited theoretical backing, and incomplete hyperparameter details.  
- Overall score 8.5/10 with a recommendation of Strongly Accept.
