- Proposes sMCL: train diverse deep ensembles by optimizing oracle loss with a winner-take-gradient rule.
- Extends MCL by using stochastic SGD to train all ensemble members concurrently, avoiding costly retraining.
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO, showing consistent oracle improvements and interpretable specialization.
- Theoretical grounding links sMCL to constrained MCL while eliminating its computational burden.
- Experimental design is comprehensive with appropriate baselines (classical ensembles, MCL, Dey et al.).
- Analysis reveals task-specific specialization (label-space clustering, class/shape specialization, n-gram specialization).
- Paper is clear and well-organized; algorithms are reproducible; minor suggestion to clarify "oracle loss" for some readers.
- Novelty lies in the simple yet powerful winner-take-gradient optimization enabling application to deep networks.
- Significant practical impact: parameter-free, architecture-agnostic, ~5x faster than MCL, 6–10% relative oracle gains.
- Strengths: strong motivation, practical algorithm, broad evaluation, insightful analysis, easy integration.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of when diversity is unnecessary, incomplete cost-vs-performance analysis, unclear scalability for M>6, limited explanation of why winner-take-gradient improves coverage.
- Recommendation: strong accept — high quality, original, significant contribution with minor issues.
