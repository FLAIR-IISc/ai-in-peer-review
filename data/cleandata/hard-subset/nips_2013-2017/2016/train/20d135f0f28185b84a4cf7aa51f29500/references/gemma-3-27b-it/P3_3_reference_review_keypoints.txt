- Introduces sMCL: an efficient training algorithm that optimizes oracle loss via a "winner-take-gradient" rule to specialize ensemble members.  
- Demonstrates sMCL improves performance and training efficiency across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Bridges theoretical MCL formulations and practical deep learning application.  
- Correctly positions itself relative to prior MCL work (Guzman-Rivera) and sequential methods (Dey et al.), highlighting their limitations for deep nets.  
- Argues sMCL induces specialization as a byproduct of optimizing oracle loss rather than explicitly enforcing diversity.  
- Novel and significant contribution offering a practical, scalable solution for minimizing oracle loss in deep ensembles.  
- Theoretical derivation of the stochastic update and the "winner-take-gradient" approach is clear and convincing.  
- Comprehensive empirical validation with appropriate oracle metrics and insightful ablation studies.  
- Strong interpretability analysis showing emergent ensemble specialization, supported by effective visualizations.  
- Clear, well-written presentation and concise algorithm description.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., batch size, learning rate schedule).  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to more recent diversity-inducing techniques (e.g., adversarial diversity methods).  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of computational cost and scalability for large ensembles.  
- Weakness: assumes oracle loss improvements translate to downstream application gains without demonstrating in realistic pipelines.  
- For acceptance: novel, theoretically sound, empirically strong, and interpretable contribution addressing an important problem.  
- Against acceptance: needs deeper hyperparameter analysis and broader comparisons to recent methods.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper; reviewer recommends Strong Accept.
