- Paper introduces sMCL, an SGD-based adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning that trains diverse deep ensembles by optimizing oracle loss without costly retraining.  
- Positions contribution relative to ensemble diversity, multiple-solution generation, and MCL prior work.

Strengths:
- Technical: presents a clear, elegant "winner-take-gradient" method enabling concurrent training of ensemble members.  
- Applicability: demonstrated across classification, segmentation, and captioning with consistent improvements.  
- Analysis: provides interpretable insights into ensemble member specialization (class-wise and segment-wise).  
- Practicality: simple to implement and reportedly ~5× faster than prior MCL approaches.

Weaknesses:
- Theory: lacks convergence analysis or formal guarantees linking stochastic updates to the MCL objective.  
- MCL comparison: limited by insufficient meta-iterations due to compute constraints, weakening claims about superiority to MCL.  
- Oracle evaluation: heavy reliance on oracle metrics without discussing how this maps to real-world scenarios lacking an oracle.  
- Parameter sensitivity: claims "parameter-free" but introduces k (number penalized) and does not thoroughly study its effects.  
- Statistical rigor: experiments do not report statistical significance tests for improvements.

Overall assessment:
- Strong, practical contribution that meaningfully advances ensemble training for deep networks despite theoretical and experimental gaps.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
