- Introduces sMCL, a winner-take-gradient SGD method for training diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss.  
- sMCL assigns each training example to the best-performing ensemble member and backpropagates only through that member.  
- Positions sMCL relative to prior ensemble and MCL work, arguing it overcomes scalability limitations of alternating retraining and boosting methods.  
- Novelty: reframes MCL as a stochastic gradient problem enabling concurrent training of ensemble members.  
- Theoretical soundness: clear derivation of the gradient update rule that approximates oracle loss minimization.  
- Empirical results: comprehensive experiments across classification, segmentation, and captioning showing consistent outperformance of baselines.  
- Emergent specialization: visualizations show ensemble members develop class-wise expertise, supporting increased diversity.  
- Practicality: simple implementation (single new layer) and parameter-free design make sMCL accessible.  
- Writing and organization are clear, making the method and results easy to follow.  
- Limited baseline comparison: comparison to Dey et al. is insufficiently analyzed; more detail on why it saturates is needed.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: paper lacks analysis of sMCL’s sensitivity to batch size, learning rate, and other training hyperparameters.  
- Computational cost: more explicit discussion and quantification of training overhead versus standard ensembles is missing.  
- Downstream impact: focuses on oracle metrics but does not demonstrate benefits on realistic downstream or human-in-the-loop tasks.  
- Tie-breaking: arbitrary handling of ties is noted but its effect on results and sensitivity is not explored.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, novel, and well-validated contribution with minor weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
