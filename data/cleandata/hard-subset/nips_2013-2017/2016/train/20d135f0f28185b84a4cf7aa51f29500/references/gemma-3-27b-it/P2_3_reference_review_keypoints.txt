- Proposes sMCL, a scalable "winner-take-gradient" training algorithm for deep ensembles that minimizes oracle loss and induces specialization.  
- Positions sMCL within Multiple Choice Learning, addressing computational limits of prior MCL (block coordinate descent) for deep networks.  
- Demonstrates sMCL on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning using CNN, FCN, and CNN+RNN architectures.  
- Empirically outperforms classical ensembles, Dey et al.'s method, and (where comparable) original MCL, with ~5x speedup over MCL.  
- Shows interpretable specialization emerges in ensemble members, supported by qualitative examples and class-wise clustering visualizations.  
- Claims model-agnostic and loss-function-agnostic applicability, enhancing potential impact.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and technically well presented.  
- Limited comparisons to strongest or task-specific baselines (e.g., advanced captioning ensemble methods beyond single-model beam search).  
- Lacks systematic analysis of hyperparameter sensitivity (learning rates, batch sizes, etc.).  
- Theoretical justification is limited; convergence and relation to other optimization techniques need deeper analysis.  
- Reliance on oracle metrics alone may obscure real-world downstream benefits; lacks demonstration in realistic application scenarios.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses practical MCL bottleneck, strong empirical results, insights into specialization, general and implementable method, clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete baseline comparisons, insufficient hyperparameter analysis, and weaker theoretical rigor.  
- Overall assessment: significant and high-quality contribution likely to impact ensemble learning; recommend acceptance (Strong Accept, 7/10).
