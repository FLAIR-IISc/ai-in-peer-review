- Proposes sMCL: a stochastic block SGD method to scale Multiple Choice Learning to deep neural networks.  
- sMCL is applicable to any SGD-trained model and agnostic to the task loss.  
- Demonstrated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Empirical results show sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, including MCL.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the method and experiments.  
- The method is technically sound and thoroughly evaluated.  
- Algorithmic novelty may be incremental relative to Guzman-Rivera et al. [8].  
- Reviewer is convinced the incremental change is useful and leans toward acceptance.  
- Suggests comparing sMCL to MCL when both are trained with mini-batches (not full-dataset MCL).  
- Requests more quantitative analysis of the ensemble "diversity" effect (beyond qualitative examples).  
- Recommends adding additional quantitative results/comparisons to strengthen claims of advantage.  
- Claims the paper has potential significance due to broad applicability and practical utility.
