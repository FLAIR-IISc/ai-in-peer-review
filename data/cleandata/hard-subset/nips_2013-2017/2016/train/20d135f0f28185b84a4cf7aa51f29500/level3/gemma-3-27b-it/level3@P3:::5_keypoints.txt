- Paper proposes sMCL, a novel, computationally efficient method to train ensembles that directly minimize oracle loss for diverse multi-output predictions.  
- sMCL enables concurrent training of ensemble members via stochastic block gradient descent, making MCL practical for deep networks.  
- Empirical results show sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and baselines on image classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Analysis reveals interpretable specialization among ensemble members (e.g., class-wise clustering).  
- The algorithm is simple, well-motivated, technically sound, and clearly presented.  

- Authors acknowledge but insufficiently investigate sensitivity to initialization (random vs. pre-trained).  
- Baseline comparability is unclear—paper does not state whether baselines used the same initialization as sMCL.  
- Limited quantitative analysis of ensemble diversity; qualitative examples are insufficient without diversity metrics.  
- Related-work discussion could better compare trade-offs with multi-head/single-model approaches like Lee et al. (2015).  

- For acceptance: addresses an important problem, introduces a novel and practical method, shows strong empirical gains, and offers interpretability insights.  
- Against acceptance: robustness concerns from unexamined initialization effects, unclear baseline setup, and incomplete diversity evaluation.  

- Overall: strong contribution recommending acceptance with minor revisions to report initialization details, ensure fair baseline comparisons, and add quantitative diversity analyses.
