- Proposes sMCL, a winner-take-gradient ensemble training method that updates only the lowest-loss member per example to train diverse deep ensembles.  
- Builds on MCL but overcomes prior computational limitations by enabling concurrent SGD training of ensemble members.  
- Positions work within ensemble learning literature, referencing resampling, diversity regularization, and ensemble-of-expert approaches.  
- Strength: Simple and elegant algorithmic idea (“winner-take-gradient”).  
- Strength: Strong empirical improvements across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Strength: Scalable to deep architectures compared to prior MCL methods.  
- Strength: Parameter-free method reducing hyperparameter tuning burden.  
- Strength: Observed emergent specialization among ensemble members, improving interpretability.  
- Weakness: Testing phase description is vague and may rely on ground-truth selection of the best member—this must be clarified.  
- Weakness: Terms like “independent ensembles” and “regular ensembles” are undefined, hindering result interpretation.  
- Weakness: Claim of member specialization is not convincingly justified given per-example reassignment during training.  
- Weakness: Misses discussion of related diversity-regularization work (e.g., Chen et al.) that should be acknowledged and contrasted.  
- Weakness: Limited ablation study; sensitivity to hyperparameters and architectures is not thoroughly explored.  
- For acceptance: Addresses an important problem with a simple, efficient method and strong empirical evidence.  
- Against acceptance: Experimental validity, terminology clarity, specialization claims, and literature omissions are significant concerns.  
- Overall: Novel and promising contribution but requires clarifications (especially testing procedure and terminology) — recommendation: Weak Accept contingent on revisions.
