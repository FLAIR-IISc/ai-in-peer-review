- Proposes sMCL, a novel, simple-to-implement method for training diverse deep ensembles that is architecture- and loss-agnostic.
- Provides thorough analysis showing ensemble member specialization and expertise emerge automatically under sMCL.
- Demonstrates sMCL’s effectiveness across tasks (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning) and various network architectures.
- Includes comprehensive comparisons to baselines (classical ensembles, MCL, Dey et al.).
- Fails to analyze the importance of initialization; unclear whether pre-trained weights are necessary versus random init.
- Experimental baseline comparisons are unclear about whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL.
- Evaluation relies solely on oracle accuracy, which may not capture the full benefits of the approach.
- Omits comparison with related relevant work (e.g., Lee et al. 2015 "Why M Heads are Better than One").
