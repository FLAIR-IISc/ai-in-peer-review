This paper proposes a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), which minimizes the oracle loss directly. The authors formalize the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm in the context of deep networks and introduce a stochastic gradient descent-based algorithm to train ensemble members concurrently.

The main idea of sMCL is to interleave the assignment step with batch updates in stochastic gradient descent, allowing the ensemble members to specialize on subsets of the data distribution. This approach is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free.

The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL for training diverse deep ensembles on a wide range of problem domains and network architectures, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning. The experimental results show that sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles, Dey et al. [5], and MCL (while providing a 5x speedup over MCL).

The analysis of the training and output behaviors of the resulting ensembles demonstrates how ensemble member specialization and expertise emerge automatically when trained using sMCL. The authors also provide qualitative results showing the diversity in the segmentations predicted by different members of the ensemble.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach and results. The experiments are thorough, and the comparison with baselines is fair.

Weaknesses:

* The experimental comparison with baselines is not entirely clear, as it is not specified whether the baselines use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL.
* The evaluation of the method is limited to oracle accuracy, and the authors do not provide an analysis of ensemble diversity.
