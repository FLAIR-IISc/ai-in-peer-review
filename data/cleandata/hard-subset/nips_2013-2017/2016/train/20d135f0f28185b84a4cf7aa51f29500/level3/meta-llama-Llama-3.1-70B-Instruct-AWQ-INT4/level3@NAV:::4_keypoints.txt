- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel SGD-based algorithm that minimizes oracle loss to train diverse deep ensembles.  
- sMCL allows ensemble members to specialize on subsets of the data distribution.  
- Method is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear problem statement and solution explanation.  
- Related work section is comprehensive and properly references prior contributions.  
- Technical section is too short and lacks sufficient mathematical derivations and algorithmic detail.  
- Authors should provide more mathematical explanations to clarify the method.  
- Experiments span classification, segmentation, and captioning and show sMCL's effectiveness.  
- Experimental baselines are weak; stronger baselines are needed for fair comparison.  
- Should evaluate on more challenging datasets (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) to demonstrate robustness.  
- Paper has high significance and potential to advance the state of the art.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to expand technical details and strengthen experiments.
