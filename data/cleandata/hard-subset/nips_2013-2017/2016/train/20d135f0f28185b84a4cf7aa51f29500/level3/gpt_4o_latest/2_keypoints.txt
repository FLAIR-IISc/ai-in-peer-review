- Proposes sMCL: a method that trains DNN ensembles by directing backprop updates to the best network per instance to minimize oracle loss.  
- Extends Multiple Choice Learning to deep models with an SGD-based algorithm that trains all ensemble members concurrently.  
- Avoids expensive retraining/sequential training; is architecture-agnostic and parameter-free.  
- Introduces a novel "winner-take-gradient" strategy to enforce specialization among ensemble members.  
- Produces diverse, interpretable outputs that align well with top-k evaluation metrics.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains in oracle metrics across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Shows computational efficiency compared to classical ensembles, MCL, and other baselines.  
- Qualitative results highlight class clustering, diverse segmentations, and varied captions.  
- Weakness: lacks discussion/guidance on selecting or exploiting ensemble predictions in practical downstream applications.  
- Weakness: pseudo-code provided as a raster image, reducing clarity and reproducibility.  
- Strengths summarized: novel and efficient approach, strong multi-domain results, clear exposition, and interpretable specialization.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to address deployment guidance and improve pseudo-code presentation.
