- Proposes a novel "review network" extension to encoder-decoder that performs multiple review steps with attention to produce thought vectors.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on image captioning and source code captioning tasks, achieving state-of-the-art on MSCOCO.  
- Paper is well-organized with a clear explanation of architecture and components.  
- Strength: Architecture novelty and thorough experiments across two tasks.  
- Strength: Impressive MSCOCO results and detailed performance analysis with attention and thought-vector visualizations.  
- Weakness: Testing phase description is unclear, including model selection for evaluation.  
- Weakness: Claim that ensemble members specialize to different output classes is insufficiently justified and mechanism is unclear.  
- Weakness: Lacks discussion of related work, notably diversity regularization techniques in neural networks.  
- Quality: Technically sound with experimental claims supported by results.  
- Suggestion: Paper would benefit from a detailed ablation study to quantify each component's contribution.  
- Clarity: Clearly written and provides sufficient information for reproduction.  
- Originality: High—review network is a novel extension of encoder-decoder models.  
- Significance: High—achieves SOTA on a challenging benchmark and has potential cross-task applicability.  
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing testing clarity and justification for ensemble specialization.
