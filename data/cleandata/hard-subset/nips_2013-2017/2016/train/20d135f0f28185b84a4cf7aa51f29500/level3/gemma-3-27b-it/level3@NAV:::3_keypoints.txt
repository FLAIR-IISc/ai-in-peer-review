- Proposes sMCL: a "winner-takes-the-gradient" adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning for SGD to train diverse deep ensembles.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Addresses an important scalability problem in ensemble training and is simple/easy to implement.  
- Qualitative analysis shows interpretable specializations within ensemble members.  
- Method implicitly encourages diversity via oracle loss but does not include an explicit diversity regularizer.  
- Contribution is incremental relative to Guzman et al. (2016); novelty is mainly adaptation/scalability rather than a fundamentally new concept.  
- Raises concern about baseline MCL convergence: need details on how baseline learners were ensured to converge to avoid biased comparisons.  
- Recommends toning down emphasis on “diversity” in title/text since diversity is not explicitly enforced.  
- Minor issues: reword first sentence of abstract for clarity.  
- Minor issues: typo on line 108 (“f” should be “g”) and superfluous period on line 115.  
- Arguments for acceptance: important problem, simple scalable solution, strong diverse-task results.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, lack of explicit diversity enforcement, and missing baseline convergence details.
