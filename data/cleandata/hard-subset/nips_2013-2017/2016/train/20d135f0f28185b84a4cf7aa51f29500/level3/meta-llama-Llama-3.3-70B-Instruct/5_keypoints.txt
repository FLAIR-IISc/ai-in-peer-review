- Proposes a novel "review network" extension to encoder-decoder models that performs multiple review steps with attention on encoder hidden states to produce thought vectors.  
- The review network is generic and can be integrated into existing encoder-decoder architectures.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on image captioning and source code captioning tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explaining motivation, architecture, and training procedure.  
- Provides thorough empirical analysis across two tasks and compares to several baselines, including attentive encoder-decoders.  
- Results show consistent improvement over baselines, indicating more compact and effective input representations.  
- Experimental presentation, organization, and related-work discussion are clear and accessible.  
- Lacks deeper analysis on the role of initialization and its impact on results.  
- Comparison settings need clearer description, specifically whether baselines used the same pre-trained networks.  
- Evaluation should include ensemble member diversity (not only oracle accuracy) for a more complete analysis.  
- Missing comparison to related approaches on ensemble diversity (e.g., Stefan Lee et al.) to contextualize strengths/weaknesses.  
- Overall: novel, effective architecture with promising applications; addressing the analysis and comparison gaps would strengthen the paper.
