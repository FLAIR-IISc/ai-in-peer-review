- Proposes sMCL: backpropagate each training example only to the ensemble member with lowest loss to optimize oracle loss.  
- Key contribution: adapts Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL to stochastic gradient descent, making it practical for large deep networks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with significant oracle-performance gains.  
- Produces qualitatively more diverse outputs and emergent specialization among ensemble members.  
- Algorithm is simple, general (loss- and architecture-agnostic), parameter-free, and trains ensemble members concurrently.  
- Empirical evaluation is comprehensive, using standard architectures and strong baselines.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-motivated, and transparently related to prior work.  
- Main weakness: incremental novelty—idea follows directly from prior MCL; novelty is primarily the stochastic/implementation adaptation.  
- Suggested stronger baseline: compare to a mini-batch/online variant of original MCL to isolate benefits of tight interleaving vs. batching.  
- Suggested more quantitative diversity metrics: distinct predicted classes per image (classification), average pairwise IoU (segmentation), and pairwise BLEU/CIDEr or n-gram diversity (captioning).  
- Overall assessment: well-executed, practically significant adaptation with strong results; recommendation to accept.
