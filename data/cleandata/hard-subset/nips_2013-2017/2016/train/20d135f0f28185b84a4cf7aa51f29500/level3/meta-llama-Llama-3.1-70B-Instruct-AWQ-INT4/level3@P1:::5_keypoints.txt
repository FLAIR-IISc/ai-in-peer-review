- Proposes sMCL (Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning), minimizing oracle loss via a stochastic block gradient descent for producing multiple outputs.
- Evaluated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, and is faster than the original MCL (MCL is ~5x slower).
- Analysis shows ensemble members automatically specialize and develop expertise under sMCL training.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of the method and applications.
- Experiments are thorough, well-designed, and results are clearly presented.
- Strength: novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles for multi-output interaction.
- Strength: empirical validation across three tasks with detailed behavioral analysis.
- Weakness: lacks analysis of the effect of initialization (random vs. pre-trained) on sMCL performance.
- Weakness: unclear whether baseline comparisons used the same pre-trained initializations as sMCL.
- Weakness: evaluation relies only on oracle accuracy and omits ensemble diversity metrics.
- Weakness: does not compare against related work like "Why M Heads are Better than One" (Lee et al., 2015).
- Quality: technically sound and clearly explained but needs more clarity on initialization and baseline parity.
- Originality: introduces a stochastic MCL variant that meaningfully extends the MCL paradigm.
- Significance: potentially impactful for applications requiring multiple candidate outputs and improved oracle performance.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong multi-task results, and clear analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient initialization/baseline analysis and narrow evaluation metrics.
