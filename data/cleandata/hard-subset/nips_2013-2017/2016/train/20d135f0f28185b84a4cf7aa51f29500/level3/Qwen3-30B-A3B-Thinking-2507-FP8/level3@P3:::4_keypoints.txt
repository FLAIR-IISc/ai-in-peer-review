- Proposes sMCL: a "winner-take-gradient" SGD modification to train diverse deep ensembles by updating only the lowest-error ensemble member per example.  
- Positions sMCL as a practical extension of Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL for deep networks, claiming first practical method to directly minimize oracle loss for deep ensembles.  
- Demonstrates sMCL on classification (CIFAR-10), segmentation (Pascal VOC subset), and captioning (MSCOCO), reporting improved oracle performance.  
- Strength: Strong, practical motivation for producing multiple diverse predictions in perception systems.  
- Strength: Method is conceptually simple and easy to implement.  
- Strength: Broad applicability shown across multiple tasks and architectures.  
- Strength: Insightful analysis of specialization patterns (e.g., class-wise clustering).  
- Weakness (originality): Contribution seen as incremental; sMCL is framed as a minor adaptation of prior MCL work and not clearly distinct from existing ensemble diversity methods.  
- Weakness (quality): Technical section too short and lacks mathematical detail and justification; computational complexity claims (avoiding retraining) are not analyzed.  
- Weakness (experiments): Baselines are weak (independent ensembles), improvements are marginal (6–10% relative), and evaluation scope is limited.  
- Weakness (clarity): Writing is poor, and distinctions between sMCL and related methods (multi-task training, ensemble approaches) are inadequately explained.  
- Weakness (significance): Segmentation evaluated on nonstandard split (VOC 2011 subset), lacks comparison to stronger recent baselines, and claims about interpretable ambiguity are under-supported.  
- Recommendation 1: Clarify novelty and distinctions from prior ensemble diversity and multi-prediction work.  
- Recommendation 2: Expand the technical section with rigorous mathematical details and justification.  
- Recommendation 3: Evaluate on standard benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and compare to stronger segmentation baselines.  
- Recommendation 4: Provide deeper experimental analysis to show practical significance of gains.  
- Pro acceptance arguments: Relevant motivation, simple and implementable method, demonstrated across multiple domains.  
- Con acceptance arguments: Incremental novelty, insufficient technical depth, weak experimental evaluation, and unclear distinction from existing approaches.  
- Overall recommendation: Reject in current form; encourage substantial revisions to address novelty, technical rigor, and experimental strength.
