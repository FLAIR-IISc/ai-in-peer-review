- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based method to train diverse deep ensembles minimizing oracle loss.  
- Generalizes Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for joint learning of deep network ensembles.  
- Method is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Evaluated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, including a slower MCL procedure.  
- Strengths: well-motivated, clear exposition, easy implementation, and comprehensive evaluation across tasks.  
- Weakness: paper motivates “diversity” heavily but does not include an explicit diversity enforcement term.  
- Weakness: contribution appears incremental relative to Guzman et al.’s relaxation.  
- Minor issues: typos (line 108), extra period (line 115), and suggested rewording of abstract’s first sentence and tone down the title’s emphasis on diversity.  
- Related work: comprehensive review provided, but needs clearer differentiation and articulation of key advantages over existing methods.  
- Writing quality: generally clear but contains passive voice and some awkward phrasing.  
- Originality/significance: novel and potentially impactful approach addressing an important deep learning problem.  
- Reviewer recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
