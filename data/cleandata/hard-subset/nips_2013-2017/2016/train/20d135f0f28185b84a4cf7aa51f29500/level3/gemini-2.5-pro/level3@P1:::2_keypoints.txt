1. Introduces sMCL, a training algorithm for deep ensembles that minimizes oracle loss via a "winner-take-gradient" rule.  
2. sMCL backpropagates gradients only through the ensemble member with lowest loss per example, enabling concurrent training.  
3. Method is a stochastic, efficient approximation of prior MCL block coordinate descent, suitable for deep networks.  
4. sMCL encourages specialization among ensemble members, producing diverse, high-quality predictions.  
5. Validated across CIFAR-10, PASCAL VOC, and MSCOCO, outperforming independently trained ensemble baselines.  
6. Technically sound, clearly written, well-organized, and supported by thorough experiments and analyses.  
7. Qualitative analysis shows interpretable emergent expertise (class, segmentation style, caption n-grams).  
8. Minor presentation issue: low-resolution pseudocode in Figure 2 should be replaced with typeset/vector graphics.  
9. Contribution is original in formulating an efficient, single-backward-pass MCL for deep learning and is parameter-free and architecture-agnostic.  
10. Significant practical potential for problems with ambiguous labels where multiple plausible hypotheses are useful.  
11. Major weakness: paper omits any practical inference strategy for selecting/using ensemble outputs without an oracle.  
12. This omission limits practical significance, as oracle gains are not translated into real-world utility.  
13. Arguments for acceptance: novel/elegant algorithm, strong empirical results, and insightful emergent behavior analysis.  
14. Argument for rejection: failure to address inference makes the work feel incomplete and less applicable.  
15. Conclusion: strong training contribution and empirical validation, but needs explicit discussion or solutions for inference; recommend acceptance with this caveat.
