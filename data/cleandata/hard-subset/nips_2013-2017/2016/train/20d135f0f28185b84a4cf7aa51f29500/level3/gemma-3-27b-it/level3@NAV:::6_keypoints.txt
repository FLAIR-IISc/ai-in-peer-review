- Proposes sMCL: update only the ensemble member with the lowest loss per example — a simple, parameter-free, architecture-agnostic SGD extension.  
- Makes MCL computationally tractable for deep networks, avoiding costly retraining/sequential schemes from prior work.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results on classification, segmentation, and captioning; outperforms classical ensembles and baselines with competitive oracle performance.  
- Practical advantages: simple, effective, computationally efficient, and easy to integrate into existing frameworks.  
- Testing procedure is unclear and likely uses ground truth to pick the best member at test time, which could inflate reported performance and needs transparency.  
- Terminology issues: “independent ensembles (Indp.)” and “regular ensembles” are used without definition, causing confusion.  
- Claim that ensemble members “specialize” to different classes is overstated — observed label-space clustering may be an emergent optimization artifact rather than an enforced property.  
- Suggestion: include an explicit diversity-promoting term in the loss to better encourage and stabilize member specialization.  
- Paper omits relevant related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) that offers theoretical perspectives.  
- Recommendation: weak accept, contingent on clarifying testing methodology, defining ambiguous terms, and discussing diversity-regularization literature.
