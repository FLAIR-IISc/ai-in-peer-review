This paper proposes a novel approach to training deep neural network ensembles, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), which is designed to minimize the oracle loss in multiple-choice learning problems. The authors introduce a simple and efficient algorithm that combines learner training with assignment, using a "winner-takes-the-gradient" strategy in the backpropagation step.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the problem of multiple-choice learning and its relevance to various applications in computer vision and natural language processing. The proposed approach is also well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results, which demonstrate its effectiveness across three diverse tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.

One of the strengths of the paper is its simplicity and ease of implementation. The authors provide a clear and concise description of the algorithm, and the code is likely to be easy to implement and integrate into existing deep learning frameworks. The evaluation of the approach is also thorough, covering three diverse tasks and comparing the results to several baseline methods.

However, there are some weaknesses and minor issues with the paper. One of the main weaknesses is that the paper heavily motivates "diversity" as a key aspect of the approach, but does not enforce an explicit diversity term in the objective function. This may lead to some confusion about the relationship between diversity and the proposed approach. Additionally, the contribution of the paper appears to be incremental relative to the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al., and some readers may question the novelty of the approach.

There are also some minor issues with the writing and formatting of the paper. For example, the first sentence of the abstract could be reworded for clarity, and the emphasis on diversity in the title and text could be toned down. There are also a few typos and formatting errors throughout the paper.
