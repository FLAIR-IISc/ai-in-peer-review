- Proposes sMCL: train diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss using a "winner-take-gradient" strategy.  
- Claims to extend Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to deep networks and avoid costly MCL retraining.  
- Core idea: backpropagate gradients only to the ensemble member with lowest loss for each example, enabling concurrent training.  
- Problem is well-motivated and method is technically sound.  
- Contribution seen as incremental—mainly a neural-network adaptation of existing MCL with minor SGD-compatible changes.  
- Technical exposition (Section 3) is too brief and fails to clearly differentiate sMCL from multi-task learning or other ensemble methods.  
- Unclear distinction from existing techniques for producing diverse predictions.  
- Experimental evaluation is weak: modest gains (6–10%) and inadequate comparisons.  
- Baselines are limited (independent ensembles and original MCL); stronger diversity-focused methods are omitted.  
- Lacks validation on standard vision benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) necessary for proper assessment.  
- Qualitative "interpretable expertise" analysis is interesting but does not compensate for weak quantitative validation.  
- Strengths: clear problem statement, simple implementation, and broad applicability (classification, segmentation, captioning).  
- Overall judgment: insufficient novelty/significance and poor validation; recommend stronger baselines and more rigorous experiments before reconsideration (not recommended for acceptance).
