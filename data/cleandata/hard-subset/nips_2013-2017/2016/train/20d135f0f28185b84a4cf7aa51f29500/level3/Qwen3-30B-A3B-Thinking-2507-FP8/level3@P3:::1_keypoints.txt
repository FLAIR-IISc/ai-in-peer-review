- Introduces sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based variant of Multiple Choice Learning that trains ensembles to produce diverse multiple outputs concurrently.
- Replaces MCL’s expensive alternating retraining with a simple SGD modification, enabling scalability to deep networks.
- Demonstrates consistent improvements (≈6–10% in oracle metrics) over independent ensembles and Dey et al.’s sequential boosting across CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO.
- Achieves large practical speedups (≈5× faster than MCL) making diverse deep ensembles feasible for modern architectures.
- Clear motivation and exposition; Algorithm 2 is a simple, well-explained change to standard training.
- Experiments are comprehensive, covering multiple tasks, architectures, and qualitative analyses that show ensemble member specialization.
- Technically sound with appropriate baselines and thorough validation.
- Method is simple, parameter-free, and agnostic to architecture and loss, increasing practical appeal.
- Main originality concern: algorithmic change is incremental over Guzman-Rivera et al. (MCL), though practically significant.
- Suggested improvements: compare to MCL using minibatch/meta-iteration variants and analyze diversity with metrics beyond oracle accuracy (e.g., inter-ensemble correlation, coverage).
- Pro-acceptance: first practical method for diverse deep ensembles; easy to implement; proven across domains; well-written and motivated.
- Con-acceptance: incremental novelty relative to prior MCL; limited diversity analysis beyond oracle performance.
- Overall recommendation: accept — practical impact and strong validation outweigh incremental novelty.
