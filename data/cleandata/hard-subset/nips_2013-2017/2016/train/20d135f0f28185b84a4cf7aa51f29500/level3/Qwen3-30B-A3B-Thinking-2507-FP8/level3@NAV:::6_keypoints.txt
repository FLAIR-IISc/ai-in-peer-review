- Proposes sMCL, a simple algorithm for diverse deep ensembles that updates only the ensemble member with the lowest loss per example.  
- Core mechanism claimed to produce automatic specialization of ensemble members across classes/tasks.  
- Method is parameter-free and architecture-agnostic.  
- Strong empirical results across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Outperforms baselines (independent ensembles, MCL, Dey et al.) in oracle performance.  
- Main strengths: simplicity, easy implementation, and significant improvements in oracle metrics.  
- Motivation for producing diverse outputs to handle ambiguity is well-justified with practical examples.  
- Major concern: testing procedure unclear—paper appears to use ground truth to pick the best member at inference, which is invalid in practice.  
- Baseline "independent ensembles" (Indp.) is used without proper explanation, causing confusion.  
- Claim of "specialization" is not convincingly supported; per-example reassignment need not lead to class-specific specialization.  
- Missing related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), weakening theoretical positioning.  
- Overall significance moderate due to lack of theoretical grounding and incomplete explanations; recommends weak acceptance if authors address testing ambiguity, clarify terminology, and incorporate related literature.
