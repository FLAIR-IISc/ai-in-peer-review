- Proposes sMCL, a novel SGD-based method to train diverse deep ensembles that minimize oracle loss by backpropagating each example only to its lowest-error predictor.  
- Generalizes Multiple Choice Learning to jointly train deep network ensembles; method is simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Interleaves assignment with minibatch SGD updates, making sMCL applicable to any SGD-trained learner and modern deep networks.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability and strong performance on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning tasks.  
- Empirical results show sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and other baselines, including the slower MCL procedure.  
- Provides informative analysis showing automatic emergence of ensemble member specialization and expertise.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, with clear notation and informative figures.  
- Technically sound, well-motivated, and experiments are thorough and convincing.  
- Importance of initialization (random vs. pre-trained) is insufficiently explored and should be investigated.  
- Unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL, making comparisons potentially unfair.  
- Evaluation relies only on oracle accuracy; an analysis of ensemble diversity is missing.  
- Should include comparison to related work such as "Why M Heads are Better than One" (Lee et al., 2015) to position sMCL relative to prior methods.  
- Overall contribution is novel and significant with potential for wide adoption, but needs additional experiments and clearer baseline comparisons to strengthen the paper.
