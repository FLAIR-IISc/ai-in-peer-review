- Paper proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL): a winner-take-gradient SGD variant where only the lowest-loss ensemble member backpropagates per example.  
- sMCL interleaves assignment and learning on-the-fly, avoiding expensive alternating retraining of classical MCL.  
- Method is parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and derived from the MCL objective.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR10 classification, Pascal VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning with consistent oracle (top-k) improvements.  
- sMCL outperforms independent ensembles, submodular/boosting-style diversity methods, and a block-coordinate MCL baseline.  
- sMCL is about 5× faster than the block-coordinate MCL baseline while training all members concurrently.  
- Qualitative analyses show emergent, interpretable specializations across ensemble members (class specialists, segmentation biases, caption style differences).  
- Main novelty is practical SGD-compatible instantiation of MCL for deep networks; conceptually incremental but practically significant.  
- Paper situates itself relative to ensembling, diverse-output methods (M-best, DPPs), and prior MCL work.  
- Experiments are broad and technically sound, with clear metrics (top-k accuracy/IoU/CIDEr-D) and helpful ablations.  
- Concerns about fairness of the MCL baseline due to potentially limited meta-iterations and convergence criteria.  
- Sensitivity analyses are limited: initialization, batch size, learning rate, tie-breaking, and risk of “dead” experts need study.  
- Trade-offs on standard (non-oracle) metrics are underreported; impact on top-1 or overall quality is unclear.  
- Diversity is emergent rather than explicitly enforced; paper emphasizes diversity without an explicit term.  
- Captioning comparisons conflate training and inference differences (beam search) and need clearer context.  
- Requests for practical details: tie-breaking policy, gradient scaling when only one model receives signal, and starvation prevention heuristics.  
- Suggested additions: sensitivity analyses (seeds, batch size, tie-breaking), non-oracle metrics, standard diversity measures (Self-BLEU/distinct-n), and human preference evaluations.  
- Suggest discussing lightweight stabilization mechanisms (warm starts, entropy regularization, exploration updates) to avoid expert starvation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical, general method with strong empirical gains, speedups, and interpretable specializations likely to be adopted.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental conceptual advance, potentially unfair baseline comparisons, and limited robustness/sensitivity analysis.  
- Minor editorial fixes: reword abstract first sentence, avoid overstating “diverse,” correct notation/typos (line 108, 115), and clarify Algorithm 2 tie-breaking and per-batch overhead.  
- Overall recommendation: positive leaning contingent on clarifying MCL baseline comparison and adding limited sensitivity/robustness analyses.
