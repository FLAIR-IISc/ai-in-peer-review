- Proposes sMCL, a novel method to train diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss.  
- Formalizes Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep networks and introduces an SGD-based concurrent training algorithm.  
- sMCL interleaves assignment with batch updates, enabling ensemble members to specialize on data subsets.  
- Method is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Demonstrated across domains (classification, segmentation, captioning) and architectures, showing broad applicability.  
- Empirical results show sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles, Dey et al., and MCL, with a 5x speedup over MCL.  
- Analysis shows automatic emergence of ensemble member specialization and expertise during training.  
- Qualitative results illustrate diverse segmentations from different ensemble members.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides clear notation and algorithm explanation.  
- Experiments are thorough and baseline comparisons are generally fair.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of the importance and effect of initialization.  
- Weakness: unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL, complicating comparisons.  
- Weakness: evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy and omits quantitative analysis of ensemble diversity.  
- Recommendation: include analysis of initialization effects on sMCL performance.  
- Recommendation: clarify and standardize baseline initialization in experiments.  
- Recommendation: evaluate ensemble diversity with additional metrics beyond oracle accuracy.  
- Arguments for acceptance mirror strengths: novelty, simplicity, strong empirical results, and insight into specialization.  
- Arguments against acceptance mirror weaknesses: missing initialization analysis, unclear baseline setups, and limited diversity evaluation.
