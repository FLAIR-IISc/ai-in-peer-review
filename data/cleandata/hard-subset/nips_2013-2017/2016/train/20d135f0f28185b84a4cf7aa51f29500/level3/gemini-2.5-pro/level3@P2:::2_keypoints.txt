- Proposes sMCL: a simple SGD-based "winner-take-gradient" method to train ensembles that directly optimize oracle loss for diverse, high-quality predictions.  
- sMCL is a stochastic, parallel adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning that avoids costly iterative retraining.  
- During each minibatch, only the ensemble member with minimum loss for an example receives the gradient, encouraging specialization.  
- Demonstrated across CIFAR-10 classification, PASCAL VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning with significant oracle performance gains over baselines.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, spanning multiple architectures (CNNs, FCNs, CNN+RNNs) and tasks.  
- Analysis shows interpretable, task-dependent specialization (e.g., class-based splits, n-gram diversity).  
- Algorithm is technically sound, simple to implement, and well-motivated.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, with effective figures and tables; however Algorithm 2 pseudocode is a low-resolution image.  
- Original contribution: first practical stochastic formulation of MCL for deep ensembles with a novel winner-take-gradient rule.  
- Method is general, parameter-free, architecture-agnostic, and addresses an important problem of producing diverse hypotheses.  
- Major weakness: entire training and evaluation rely on an oracle; no method or discussion for selecting or combining outputs at inference time.  
- Lack of inference-time strategy (re-ranker, selector, uncertainty-based choice, or human-in-loop use) leaves a critical gap for practical deployment.  
- Recommendation: add discussion or experiments on inference strategies and typeset pseudocode properly to improve completeness and presentation.
