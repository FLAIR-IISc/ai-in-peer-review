- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic multiple choice learning method using a "winner-take-gradient" rule to train diverse deep ensembles concurrently.  
- sMCL adapts Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL to SGD, avoiding costly iterative retraining and making MCL scalable to deep networks.  
- For each example, only the ensemble member with lowest loss receives gradient updates during backpropagation.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10 (classification), Pascal VOC (segmentation), and MSCOCO (captioning).  
- Empirically outperforms classical ensembles, MCL (with reported 5× speedup), and Dey et al.’s sequential approach.  
- Automatically induces interpretable specializations among ensemble members and captures task ambiguity.  
- Experimental design is strong with thorough comparisons across architectures and tasks.  
- Theoretical analysis is limited; paper needs deeper exploration of how diversity is induced.  
- Novelty is incremental relative to [8]; main contribution is practical SGD-based adaptation for deep learning.  
- Clarity is excellent — well-organized with clear method and results presentation.  
- High practical significance for applications requiring diverse outputs or downstream oracle selection.  
- Recommendation: accept, as practical utility outweighs modest algorithmic novelty.  
- Suggested improvements: compare MCL with batch-based training to contextualize speedup, and add quantitative diversity metrics beyond oracle accuracy.
