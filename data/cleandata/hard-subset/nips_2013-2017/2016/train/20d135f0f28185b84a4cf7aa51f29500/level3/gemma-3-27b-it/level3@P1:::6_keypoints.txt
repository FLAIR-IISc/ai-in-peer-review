- Paper introduces sMCL: a simple "winner-take-gradient" SGD algorithm that trains diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss.
- Method enables concurrent, parameter-free, architecture-agnostic training of ensemble members without iterative retraining.
- Strong empirical results across classification, segmentation, and captioning, outperforming baselines and speeding up prior MCL methods.
- Observed emergent specialization of ensemble members, with visualizations suggesting meaningful expertise per model.
- Algorithm is general and readily applicable to many deep learning architectures and loss functions.
- Evaluation procedure during inference is unclear, particularly reliance on an "oracle" to pick the best prediction.
- Concern that using ground truth to select the best member at test time may inflate reported performance and not reflect realistic applications.
- Terms like "independent ensembles" and "regular ensembles" are not well defined, reducing experimental clarity.
- The claim that per-example winner-take-gradient updates alone cause long-term specialization is not fully justified; assignment dynamics need analysis.
- Related work on diversity regularization (e.g., information bottleneck or adversarial decorrelation methods) is missing and should be discussed.
- Suggest investigating explicit diversity terms in the loss (e.g., penalizing output correlation) to potentially improve specialization and performance.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a practical problem, proposes a novel/simple effective algorithm, shows consistent strong results, and is likely adoptable.
- Arguments for rejection: unclear evaluation, weak justification for specialization, and omission of relevant diversity-regularization literature.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept conditional on clarifying evaluation/testing, defining terminology, and expanding related work on diversity methods.
