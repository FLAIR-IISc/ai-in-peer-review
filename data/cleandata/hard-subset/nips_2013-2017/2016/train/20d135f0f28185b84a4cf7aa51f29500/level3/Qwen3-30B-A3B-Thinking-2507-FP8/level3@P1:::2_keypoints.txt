- Introduces sMCL: a novel "winner-take-gradient" method for training diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss.  
- Trains ensemble members concurrently with SGD — only the lowest-loss member for each example receives gradients.  
- Method is architecture-agnostic, loss-agnostic, and parameter-free, inducing automatic specialization across models.  
- Empirical gains on CIFAR-10: 6–10% higher oracle accuracy than baselines, with clear class-specialization.  
- Semantic segmentation (Pascal VOC): ~7% relative improvement and qualitatively diverse, disambiguating segmentations.  
- Image captioning (MSCOCO): significantly higher oracle CIDEr-D, more diverse/interpretable captions, avoids mode-seeking.  
- Computationally efficient: ~5× faster than prior MCL due to concurrent training.  
- Strength — originality: first SGD-based MCL for deep networks, removing costly retraining/sequential updates.  
- Strength — quality: rigorous multi-domain experiments and interpretable evidence of specialization.  
- Strength — clarity: well-structured paper with pseudocode and ablation studies.  
- Major weakness: fails to address how to exploit ensembles at inference without an oracle — limits practical deployment.  
- Minor weakness: Figure 2 pseudocode embedded as a raster image, harming reproducibility/accessibility.  
- Overall: strong technical and empirical contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to discuss inference strategies.
