- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based algorithm to train deep ensembles that minimize oracle loss and produce diverse outputs.  
- Reformulates Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep networks using a winner-takes-the-gradient backpropagation layer to encourage specialization.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains in oracle performance and diversity across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- sMCL is simple to implement and applicable to any deep network architecture.  
- Method is task-loss-agnostic and broadly usable across different loss functions and problems.  
- Provides thorough analysis showing ensemble members specialize on different parts of the data distribution.  
- Lacks a clear theoretical or intuitive explanation for why sMCL outperforms original MCL.  
- Experimental comparisons omit other state-of-the-art alternatives beyond classical ensembles and original MCL.  
- Does not analyze the computational complexity or training cost of the proposed approach in detail.  
- Fails to discuss potential limitations, such as needing many ensemble members for strong performance.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and generally easy to follow, though some technical details could be clearer.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
