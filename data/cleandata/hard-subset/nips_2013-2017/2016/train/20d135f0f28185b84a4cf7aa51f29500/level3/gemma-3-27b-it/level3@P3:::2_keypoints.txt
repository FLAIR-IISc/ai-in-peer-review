- Proposes sMCL: a training method for deep ensembles that uses an oracle to pick the best prediction and backpropagates only through the chosen member.  
- Encourages ensemble member specialization to cover diverse outputs, motivated by perception systems needing multiple hypotheses.  
- Improves scalability and training efficiency over prior MCL methods by enabling concurrent SGD and avoiding costly retraining.  
- Strong empirical performance: improved oracle metrics across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Effectively positions work relative to prior MCL and ensemble literature, contrasting specialization with decorrelation approaches.  
- The stochastic gradient update derivation is clear and the method is technically sound and novel.  
- Paper is generally well-written and organized, with useful illustrative figures.  
- Experiments are thorough, include relevant baselines, ablations, and analyses of member specialization.  
- Original contribution: applying SGD to directly minimize oracle loss and scaling MCL to deep networks.  
- Major weakness: no discussion of how to use the trained ensemble at inference without access to the oracle, limiting practical applicability.  
- Authors offer no preliminary or problem-specific strategies for exploiting ensemble diversity in real-world settings.  
- Presentation flaw: Fig. 2 pseudocode is a raster image, making it hard to read and harming reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound, strong experiments, clear motivation and writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: omission of inference/exploitation strategies and poor-quality pseudocode reduce practical impact.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on adding discussion/experiments about inference without an oracle and replacing Fig. 2 with vector/text pseudocode.
