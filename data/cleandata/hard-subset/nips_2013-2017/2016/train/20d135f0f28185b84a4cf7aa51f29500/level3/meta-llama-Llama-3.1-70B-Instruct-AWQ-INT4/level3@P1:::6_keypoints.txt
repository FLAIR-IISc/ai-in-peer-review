- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based algorithm that minimizes oracle loss to train diverse deep ensembles.  
- sMCL updates only the ensemble member with the lowest loss for each training example.  
- Algorithm is simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Demonstrates efficacy across tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent formulation tailored to oracle loss minimization.  
- Shows automatic emergence of interpretable specializations among ensemble members.  
- Strong empirical results and significant improvements over baselines.  
- Broad applicability to varied problem domains and network architectures.  
- Testing procedure is unclear; reviewer suspects ground-truth may be used to pick the best member.  
- Terms "independent ensembles" and "regular ensembles (Indp.)" are not defined.  
- Claim of member "specialization" is unconvincing because member assignment is per-example and can reassign during training.  
- Paper omits discussion of related diversity-regularization literature in neural networks.  
- Suggested fixes: clarify testing protocol, define ensemble terminology, provide stronger evidence of specialization, and discuss diversity regularization work.  
- Originality: simple but novel idea; effectiveness demonstrated empirically across domains.  
- Significance: potential to improve ensemble performance and generate multiple diverse outputs (useful for tasks like captioning).  
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing clarity and related-work concerns.
