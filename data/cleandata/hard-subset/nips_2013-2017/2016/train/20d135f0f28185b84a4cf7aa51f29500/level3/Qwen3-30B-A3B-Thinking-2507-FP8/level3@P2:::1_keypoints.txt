- Introduces sMCL, which trains diverse deep ensembles by updating only the lowest-error member per example, enabling concurrent training for modern deep nets.  
- Empirically improves oracle performance across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Method is technically sound with theoretical justification, proper baselines/metrics, and convincing ablation studies showing interpretable specialization.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with simple pseudocode and illustrative figures for quantitative and qualitative results.  
- Practically significant: yields consistent 6–10% gains and produces meaningful ensemble specialization for ambiguous tasks.  
- Originality is moderate: extends Guzman-Rivera et al.’s MCL, but adapting it to SGD is a meaningful practical contribution.  
- Weakness: algorithmic novelty is incremental and needs clearer differentiation from other diversity-inducing methods.  
- Weakness: limited quantitative analysis of how diversity is achieved (e.g., mutual information, coverage metrics).  
- Weakness: comparison to batch MCL is incomplete; a direct batch-based MCL baseline is missing for fairness.  
- For acceptance: simple, practical, broadly applicable method with strong empirical results and insights into ensemble specialization.  
- Against acceptance: incremental novelty and insufficient diversity/related-work analysis weaken the novelty claims.  
- Recommendation: accept — practical impact and strong execution outweigh minor novelty and analysis shortcomings.
