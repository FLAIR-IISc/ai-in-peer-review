- Introduces sMCL: trains ensembles by optimizing oracle loss via winner-take-all gradient updates to the lowest-loss member.
- Adapts Guzman-Rivera et al.’s Multiple Choice Learning to SGD, making it practical for large-scale deep networks.
- Core idea is simple, parameter-free, elegant, and directly applicable during concurrent training.
- Strong empirical gains demonstrated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Paper is exceptionally clear, well-motivated, and experimentally well-documented.
- Main originality concern: novelty is incremental since winner-take-all oracle optimization follows prior MCL work.
- Unclear whether sMCL’s superiority stems from SGD formulation or concurrent training; comparison to MCL is not fully isolating.
- A better baseline would be an MCL variant operating on mini-batches to disentangle effects of batching vs. algorithmic change.
- Suggestion: include quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., inter-model disagreement) and correlate them with oracle performance.
- Overall verdict: modest algorithmic novelty but substantial practical impact; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
