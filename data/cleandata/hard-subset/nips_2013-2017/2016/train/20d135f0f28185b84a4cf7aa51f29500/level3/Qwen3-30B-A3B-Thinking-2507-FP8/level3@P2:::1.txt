

# Review for NIPS 2016

## Summary
This paper introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles that produces multiple hypotheses rather than single predictions. The authors frame this as an extension of the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm proposed by Guzman-Rivera et al. (NIPS 2015), but address the key limitation of the original approach: its infeasibility for training deep networks due to the need for repeated retraining of models. The sMCL method uses a simple stochastic gradient descent strategy where, for each example in a batch, only the lowest-error ensemble member receives gradient updates. This enables concurrent training of all ensemble members, making the approach practical for modern deep architectures. The paper demonstrates the efficacy of sMCL across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning tasks, showing consistent improvements in oracle performance compared to baselines.

## Strengths

**Quality and Technical Soundness (Excellent):** The paper presents a well-structured technical contribution with thorough experimental validation across multiple domains. The authors provide clear theoretical justification for their approach and demonstrate its practical effectiveness through comprehensive experiments. The method is technically sound, with appropriate baselines and metrics (oracle accuracy, IoU, CIDEr-D) that appropriately evaluate the multiple-output nature of the approach. The ablation studies (e.g., Figure 4c) convincingly show how the method automatically induces interpretable specialization in ensemble members.

**Clarity (Excellent):** The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The problem statement, motivation, and technical approach are presented with clarity, making the contribution accessible to readers. The pseudocode (Algorithm 2) is simple and directly explains the method. The results are presented in a clear, organized manner with appropriate figures that illustrate both quantitative improvements and qualitative insights about the diversity of outputs.

**Significance (Strong):** The problem of generating multiple diverse hypotheses is highly relevant to practical perception systems that interact with downstream oracles. The paper demonstrates that sMCL produces meaningful improvements across multiple challenging tasks, with consistent gains of 6-10% over baselines. The ability to automatically induce interpretable specializations in ensemble members (e.g., class-specific specialists in classification, shape-specific specialists in segmentation) provides valuable insights for understanding how diverse ensembles can capture task ambiguity.

**Originality (Moderate to Strong):** While the core idea builds on Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], the adaptation to stochastic gradient descent represents a significant practical contribution that enables the method to be applied to modern deep networks. The authors convincingly argue that this small but crucial change makes the approach feasible for real-world applications, addressing a major limitation of prior work.

## Weaknesses

**Originality (Moderate):** The algorithmic contribution is incremental in the sense that it directly builds on the MCL framework. A more thorough discussion of how this differs from other approaches to diversity in ensembles (e.g., Kirillov et al. [15], Dey et al. [5]) would strengthen the novelty claim. The paper would benefit from a clearer articulation of why the stochastic approach is fundamentally different from the batch-based MCL method.

**Diversity Analysis (Moderate):** While the paper shows that sMCL produces diverse outputs, there's limited analysis of *how* the diversity is achieved and whether it's superior to other diversity-inducing methods. More quantitative analysis of the diversity properties (e.g., mutual information between ensemble members, coverage metrics) would strengthen the claims.

**Comparison with Batch MCL (Moderate):** The paper compares sMCL with MCL using 5 meta-iterations, but doesn't provide a fair comparison where MCL would use batch processing rather than the entire dataset. A more direct comparison with a batch-based MCL implementation would better demonstrate the efficiency gains.

## Arguments for and Against Acceptance

**For Acceptance:**
- The method is simple, practical, and enables the use of Multiple Choice Learning with modern deep networks
- Demonstrates consistent, significant improvements across multiple domains and tasks
- Provides valuable insights about how ensemble specialization emerges naturally
- Well-written with clear experiments and analysis
- Addresses an important practical problem in perception systems

**Against Acceptance:**
- The core algorithmic contribution is incremental, building directly on prior work
- Limited analysis of the diversity properties beyond qualitative examples
- Some claims about novelty could be better supported with more comprehensive comparison to related work

## Recommendation
I recommend acceptance of this paper. While the algorithmic novelty is incremental, the practical impact is substantial—making Multiple Choice Learning feasible for modern deep architectures. The paper's strength lies in its clear demonstration of the method's effectiveness across diverse tasks, its simplicity of implementation, and the valuable insights it provides about how diversity emerges in ensembles. The authors have addressed a significant practical limitation of prior work and provided a method that is immediately applicable to many deep learning applications.

The minor weaknesses in the novelty discussion and diversity analysis are outweighed by the paper's strong technical execution, clear writing, and significant practical contributions. The paper would benefit from a more thorough comparison with batch-based MCL implementations (as suggested in the key points), but this is a minor point given the overall quality of the work.

This work represents a valuable contribution to the field of ensemble learning for deep networks, with clear potential for impact in practical applications where diverse output generation is beneficial. The paper is well-suited for NIPS and addresses a problem of genuine significance to the community.
