- Proposes sMCL: train ensembles by updating only the ensemble member with lowest loss per example to encourage specialization.  
- Strong empirical results across image classification, segmentation, and captioning, outperforming independent training, MCL, and boosting baselines.  
- Well-written paper with thorough experiments and compelling visualizations of emergent member expertise.  
- Builds on MCL but removes iterative retraining cost by enabling concurrent SGD training, yielding ~5x speedup in experiments.  
- Correctly situates work in ensemble learning and connections to multi-solution generation literature.  
- Technical soundness: clear derivation of the gradient update rule (Eq. 2) and solid motivation.  
- Empirical validation: extensive experiments and consistent improvements over strong baselines.  
- Clarity: straightforward method description and detailed experimental setup.  
- Originality: novel adaptation of MCL to deep networks via stochastic SGD and observation of ensemble specialization.  
- Significance: enables efficient diverse ensembles useful for ambiguous tasks and improves interpretability.  
- Major weakness: no practical inference strategy when an oracle is unavailable; selection/re-ranking of outputs is not addressed.  
- Minor weakness: Figure 2 provided as a raster image, hurting readability.  
- Limited discussion of sensitivity to underlying training hyperparameters despite claiming sMCL is parameter-free.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, effective algorithm; compelling empirical results; clear writing; practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: lack of inference strategy limits usability; minor figure issue.  
- Overall assessment: strong, impactful paper recommended as Strong Accept with encouragement to address inference use in future work.
