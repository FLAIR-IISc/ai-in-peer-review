- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based method for Multiple Choice Learning that trains diverse deep ensembles via a “winner-take-gradient” update.  
- Core idea: minimize oracle loss that selects the best ensemble prediction, encouraging diversity and handling ambiguous/multi-modal outputs.  
- Demonstrates efficacy across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, outperforming classical ensembles and recent methods.  
- Algorithm is simple, broadly applicable to various architectures and loss functions, and computationally practical for deep networks.  
- Paper is clearly written with a straightforward mathematical formulation and accessible presentation.  
- Provides insightful qualitative analysis (e.g., label-space clustering and emergent expertise among ensemble members).  
- Main novelty is incremental: adapts existing MCL (Guzman-Rivera et al.) to deep learning rather than introducing a fundamentally new optimization.  
- Comparison to original MCL is limited; a batched/mini-batch implementation of MCL would make for a fairer runtime/accuracy comparison.  
- Diversity analysis is ad hoc; the paper would benefit from systematic, quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., average pairwise distance in feature/prediction space).  
- Practical scaling claims need more detail: discuss behavior with larger ensemble sizes and increased dataset complexity.  
- Acceptance arguments: addresses an important problem, makes MCL practical for deep nets, strong empirical results, and excellent presentation.  
- Rejection concerns: incremental algorithmic novelty and insufficient quantitative diversity and batched-MCL comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to include batched MCL comparison, more quantitative diversity analysis, and expanded discussion of scaling.
