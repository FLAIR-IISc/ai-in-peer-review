- Proposes sMCL: a method to train ensembles of deep nets to produce multiple diverse outputs for oracle selection scenarios.  
- Technical contribution is incremental: appears to be an SGD adaptation of prior Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) with minor tweaks for deep nets.  
- Core algorithmic description is underdeveloped; Section 3 is surprisingly short and lacks implementation details.  
- Writing is convoluted and the paper is difficult to follow; clarity and organization need substantial improvement.  
- Fails to clearly distinguish sMCL from standard multi-task learning or other diversity-promoting ensemble methods.  
- Claim that sMCL avoids costly retraining of [8] is not well-explained beyond stating it interleaves assignment with batch updates.  
- Experiments cover classification, segmentation, and captioning, showing broad applicability.  
- Baselines are weak (independent random initializations), and MCL [8] appears under-implemented (only 5 meta-iterations).  
- Reported improvements are generally small, raising questions about practical significance.  
- Lacks evaluation on stronger, standard benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and comparisons to recent diversity methods.  
- Quality: technical soundness is questionable due to lacking detail and rigorous comparison.  
- Originality: low, largely building on existing MCL with minimal modification.  
- Significance: limited by modest gains and insufficient evidence of advantage over existing techniques.  
- Recommendation: reject; authors should expand technical details, use stronger baselines and benchmarks, and better articulate novelty.
