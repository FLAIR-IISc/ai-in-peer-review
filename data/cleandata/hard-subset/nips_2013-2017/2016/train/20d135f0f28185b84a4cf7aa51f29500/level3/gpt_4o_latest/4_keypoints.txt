- Proposes sMCL: an SGD-based, architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free method to train diverse ensembles minimizing oracle loss, claimed simpler and more efficient than MCL.  
- Motivated by practical need for diverse predictions to handle ambiguity; oracle loss minimization is well-argued.  
- Computationally efficient by avoiding costly MCL retraining, making it practical for deep ensembles.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning) and architectures (CNNs, FCNs, CNN+RNNs).  
- Observes emergent specialization: ensemble members automatically develop task-specific expertise without explicit design.  
- Novelty is incremental: sMCL is a straightforward SGD adaptation of prior MCL work rather than a fundamentally new idea.  
- Paper clarity is poor: terse technical exposition with missing details, hindering reproducibility.  
- Insufficient differentiation from other ensemble/diversity methods beyond computational efficiency.  
- Experimental baselines are weak; lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art methods (e.g., DeepLab, Transformer captioners).  
- Reported performance gains (~6–10%) are modest and may not demonstrate significant advancement.  
- Validation is limited (e.g., PASCAL VOC experiments); stronger datasets and baselines are needed.  
- For acceptance: practical relevance, efficiency, generality, and insightful specialization analysis support publication.  
- Against acceptance: incremental contribution, poor writing, weak empirical results, and inadequate validation argue for rejection.  
- Recommendation: reject in current form; encourage major revisions to improve writing, add technical detail, and strengthen experiments before resubmission.
