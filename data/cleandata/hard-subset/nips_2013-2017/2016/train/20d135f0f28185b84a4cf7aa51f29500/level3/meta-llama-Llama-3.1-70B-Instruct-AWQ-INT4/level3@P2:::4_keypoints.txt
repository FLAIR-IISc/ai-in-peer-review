- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based method to train deep ensembles that produce multiple outputs and is agnostic to task loss.  
- Claims broad applicability across architectures and tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning).  
- Demonstrates that ensemble member specialization and expertise emerge automatically under sMCL.  
- Strength: clear motivation for multiple-choice learning and thorough empirical evaluation across several tasks and datasets.  
- Strength: technically sound implementation with detailed analysis of training and output behaviors.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—reads like a neural-network adaptation of prior multiple-choice/ensemble work.  
- Weakness: technical section is too short and lacks theoretical depth.  
- Weakness: unclear distinction between the proposed framework and common alternative/multi-task training approaches.  
- Weakness: experiments use weak baselines and report only small improvements over existing methods.  
- Weakness: poor writing style and clarity issues make parts of the paper difficult to follow.  
- Originality: introduces a novel training formulation for multi-output ensembles but does not substantially advance the state of the art.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential cross-domain impact, but impact is limited by small gains and insufficient comparisons.  
- Recommendation: evaluate on stronger benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and baselines, add theoretical analysis, clarify differences from multi-task training, and improve writing and technical detail.
