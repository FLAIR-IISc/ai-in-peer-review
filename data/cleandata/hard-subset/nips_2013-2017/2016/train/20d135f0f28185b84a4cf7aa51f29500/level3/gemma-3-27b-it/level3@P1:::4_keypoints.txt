- Introduces sMCL: adapts Multiple Choice Learning to deep networks using stochastic gradient descent to train diverse ensembles minimizing oracle loss.  
- Claims: parameter-free, architecture-agnostic, computationally efficient, and useful when a downstream oracle selects from multiple hypotheses.  
- Experiments: evaluated on CIFAR10 classification, Pascal VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning, showing improvements over standard ensembles.  
- Strength: frames diversity generation as minimizing oracle loss, a practical approach for human-in-the-loop or verification settings.  
- Strength: logical and useful extension of MCL to deep networks that addresses scalability issues of prior MCL retraining methods.  
- Weakness: limited originality—main idea is an incremental adaptation of existing MCL work rather than a major technical novelty.  
- Weakness: related work discussion is superficial and fails to clearly differentiate sMCL from alternative diversity-promoting methods.  
- Weakness: technical presentation (Section 3) is too concise; lacks rigorous derivation and clear explanation of the SGD formulation.  
- Weakness: unclear how sMCL fundamentally differs from multi-task learning or ensemble regularization approaches that promote diversity.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines are weak (only standard ensembles); lacks comparison to stronger or state-of-the-art diversity techniques.  
- Weakness: reported gains are modest and may not justify added method complexity.  
- Writing quality is subpar, with convoluted phrasing and clarity issues.  
- Recommendation: reject; authors should strengthen experiments with stronger baselines/datasets (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012), provide detailed technical analysis, and improve clarity of presentation.
