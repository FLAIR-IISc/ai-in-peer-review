- Proposes sMCL: a simple “winner-takes-the-gradient” method assigning each training example to the ensemble member with lowest loss during SGD.  
- Adapts MCL to modern deep networks, avoiding impractical alternating block coordinate descent from prior work.  
- Demonstrates strong performance across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with varied architectures.  
- Method is simple, parameter-free, easy to implement, and broadly applicable.  
- Empirically outperforms baseline ensembles and matches/surpasses prior MCL approaches while being faster.  
- Shows emergent specialization: ensemble members automatically focus on different classes/segments/captions.  
- Does not explicitly enforce diversity in the loss; diversity is emergent and not guaranteed.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental—primarily an SGD adaptation of existing MCL ideas.  
- Unclear how convergence was determined for baseline MCL; needs clarification on convergence criteria and potential early stopping effects.  
- Minor issues: reword verbose first sentence of abstract, tone down “diversity” emphasis, fix typo (line 108: “f”→“g”), remove extra “.” on line 115.  
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles an important problem, simple and effective method, comprehensive evaluation, insightful analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution and lack of explicit diversity objective.  
- Overall: solid, practical paper recommended for acceptance with minor revisions and clarification on baseline convergence.
