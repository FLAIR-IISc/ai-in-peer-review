- Proposes sMCL, training ensembles by optimizing oracle loss where only the ensemble member with lowest loss receives the gradient ("winner-take-gradient").  
- Frames sMCL as a stochastic-SGD adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL), enabling concurrent training of ensemble members.  
- Demonstrates sMCL on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, outperforming baselines including independent ensembles.  
- Empirical results show lower oracle error and faster training (claimed ~5x speedup over original MCL).  
- Provides qualitative evidence of emergent specialization (class-based, segmentation-style, n-gram specialists).  
- Experiments are comprehensive and technically sound, supporting the paper’s claims.  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear motivation, algorithm explanation, and informative figures/tables.  
- Main originality concern: core objective and idea come from prior MCL work; the contribution is a practical SGD adaptation rather than a new theoretical advance.  
- Significance is high because sMCL makes MCL practical for modern deep networks; method is general, parameter-free, and easy to implement.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical impact, strong empirical validation across tasks, clarity/simplicity, and insightful analysis of specialization.  
- Argument against acceptance: perceived incremental novelty—more an engineering/practical insight than fundamental research.  
- Suggested improvement: include a fairer mini-batch or epoch-based MCL baseline to isolate benefits of concurrent updates.  
- Suggested improvement: add quantitative diversity measures (e.g., average IoU for segmentation, semantic-distance metrics for captions) beyond qualitative examples.  
- Overall conclusion: strong, well-presented paper with significant practical contribution; recommendation to accept despite incremental novelty.
