- Proposes sMCL: an SGD-based adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning to train diverse deep ensembles without costly retraining.
- Demonstrates sMCL on classification, segmentation, and captioning, reporting improvements over standard ensembles and some baselines.
- Motivation for producing diverse outputs for downstream oracle evaluation is clear and relevant.
- Technical contribution is incremental—a straightforward application of MCL to neural nets using SGD rather than a major algorithmic advance.
- Paper lacks analysis of why sMCL works or what deep-network properties enable its success beyond avoiding retraining.
- Writing quality is poor: verbose, unclear, and Section 3 is unexpectedly short for the technical content.
- Does not clearly differentiate sMCL from multi-task learning or diversity-promoting regularization methods.
- Experimental evaluation relies on relatively weak baselines and reports only small improvements.
- Experimental scope is limited: omits PASCAL VOC 2012 and comparisons to strong segmentation baselines such as DeepLab or Mask R-CNN.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a relevant problem and offers a practical improvement to MCL by avoiding retraining.
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, weak baselines, poor writing, and limited experimental validation.
- Recommendation: Reject; authors should strengthen experiments, clarify technical contribution, and improve writing before resubmission.
