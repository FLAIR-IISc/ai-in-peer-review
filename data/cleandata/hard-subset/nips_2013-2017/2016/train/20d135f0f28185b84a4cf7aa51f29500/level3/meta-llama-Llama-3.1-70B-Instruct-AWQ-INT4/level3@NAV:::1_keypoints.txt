- Proposes sMCL, an SGD-based method for training diverse deep ensembles that minimizes oracle loss.
- Demonstrates effectiveness on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Reports outperforming existing baselines across tasks.
- Technically sound with theoretical analysis and supporting experimental results.
- Clear, well-organized writing with reproducible notation and algorithms.
- Novel contribution: applying SGD to the MCL paradigm to train diverse ensembles.
- Significant impact: shows sMCL is effective, general, and useful across tasks.
- Method is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.
- Concern: algorithmic novelty may be incremental relative to prior MCL work.
- Concern: lacks detailed analysis of how the method induces or quantifies "diversity."
- Suggestion: compare sMCL to the original MCL when training with mini-batches rather than whole-dataset updates.
- Suggestion: provide deeper analysis of the diversity effect and its relation to ensemble output quality.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution and should be accepted with minor revisions.
