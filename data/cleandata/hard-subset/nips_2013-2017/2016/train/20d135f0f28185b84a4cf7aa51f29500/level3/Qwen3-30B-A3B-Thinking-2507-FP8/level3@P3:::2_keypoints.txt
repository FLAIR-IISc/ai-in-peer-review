- Introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a concurrent SGD-based method that trains diverse deep ensembles by updating only the lowest-loss network per instance.  
- Extends Multiple Choice Learning to deep networks, avoiding costly sequential retraining required by prior MCL approaches.  
- Key insight: per-instance oracle-based updates cause ensemble members to naturally specialize and cover different input subspaces.  
- Algorithm is simple, parameter-free, easy to implement, and compatible with any SGD-trained deep architecture.  
- Strong empirical validation across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with multiple architectures.  
- Reports consistent oracle-performance gains (6–10% relative) and ~5× speedup over MCL.  
- Well-written, clear problem formulation, thorough experiments, and useful qualitative analysis of specialization.  
- Presentation flaw: Figure 2 pseudocode provided as a raster image, hindering reproducibility.  
- Original contribution: first practical concurrent training adaptation of MCL for deep nets; specialization emerges without explicit diversity constraints.  
- Significant potential for systems needing multiple hypotheses; broadly applicable and practitioner-friendly.  
- Major weakness: no practical strategy presented for inference without access to the oracle used during training.  
- Lacks discussion on approximating the oracle or selecting outputs at test time in common real-world scenarios.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix Figure 2 and add a brief discussion of possible inference/selection strategies for deployment.
