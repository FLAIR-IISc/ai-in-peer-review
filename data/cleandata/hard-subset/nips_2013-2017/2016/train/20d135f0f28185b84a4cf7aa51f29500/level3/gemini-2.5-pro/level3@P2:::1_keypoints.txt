- Proposes sMCL: a SGD-based "winner-take-gradient" algorithm to train ensembles that minimize oracle loss by backpropagating only through the lowest-loss member per example.  
- Builds on Guzman-Rivera et al.'s Multiple Choice Learning (MCL), adapting the oracle-loss objective to a practical stochastic concurrent training setting.  
- Main contribution is making MCL feasible for deep networks by replacing block coordinate descent with a simple stochastic training rule.  
- Evaluated on classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning using modern architectures (CNN, FCN, CNN+RNN).  
- Empirical results show substantial improvements in oracle metrics over independent ensembles and baselines across tasks.  
- Method induces interpretable, task-specific specialization among ensemble members without explicit supervision.  
- Paper is technically sound, experiments are comprehensive, and claims are well-supported.  
- Presentation is exceptionally clear, with effective motivation, explanation, and informative figures.  
- Novelty is incremental (translating known objective to SGD), but the change is impactful and enables large-scale application.  
- Significance is high due to simplicity, generality, parameter-free nature, and likely adoption in ambiguous-task settings.  
- Argument for acceptance: practical impact—makes MCL usable for deep learning.  
- Argument for acceptance: strong empirical results across diverse tasks and architectures.  
- Argument for acceptance: simplicity and generality—applies to any SGD-trained model without model/loss changes.  
- Argument for acceptance: clarity—well-written and accessible.  
- Argument against acceptance: primary contribution may be seen as an incremental algorithmic change over prior work.  
- Suggestion: include a mini-batch hard-EM / MCL-style baseline to isolate benefits of concurrent training vs. the winner-take-gradient rule.  
- Suggestion: quantify diversity explicitly (e.g., inter-member IoU for segmentation, intra-ensemble caption dissimilarity metrics) in addition to oracle performance.
