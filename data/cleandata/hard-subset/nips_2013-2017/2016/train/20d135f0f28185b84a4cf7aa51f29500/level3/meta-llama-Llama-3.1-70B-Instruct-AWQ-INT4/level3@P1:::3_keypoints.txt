- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach to train deep ensemble members to minimize oracle loss in multiple-choice learning.  
- Introduces a simple "winner-takes-the-gradient" algorithm that combines learner training with assignment during backpropagation.  
- Paper is well-motivated with a clear introduction to multiple-choice learning and relevant applications in vision and NLP.  
- Provides theoretical analysis and strong experimental results across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Method is simple, efficient, easy to implement, and likely integrable into existing deep learning frameworks.  
- Evaluation is thorough, covering diverse tasks and comparisons to several baseline methods.  
- Main weakness: emphasizes diversity but does not include an explicit diversity term in the objective, causing potential confusion.  
- Contribution may be viewed as incremental relative to the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.  
- Minor issues with writing and formatting (e.g., abstract wording, typos, emphasis on diversity).  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Originality 7/10, Clarity 9/10, Impact 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel effective approach, simplicity, thorough evaluation, and clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution vs. prior work, lack of explicit diversity objective, and minor writing issues.
