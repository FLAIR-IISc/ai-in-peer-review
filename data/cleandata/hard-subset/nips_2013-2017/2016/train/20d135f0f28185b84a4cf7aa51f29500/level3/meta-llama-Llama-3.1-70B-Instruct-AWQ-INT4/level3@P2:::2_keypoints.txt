- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL): use an oracle to pick the best ensemble member per instance and backpropagate only on that network.  
- Training concurrently with stochastic gradient descent is a key contribution enabling scalable ensemble learning.  
- sMCL encourages member specialization and diversity, producing ensembles that cover varied outputs.  
- Evaluated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with strong empirical results.  
- Uses oracle loss as an evaluation metric, enabling fair comparison to other methods.  
- Paper is well-written; experiments are thorough and clearly presented.  
- Demonstrates interpretable specialization (e.g., label-space clustering, segmentation specialists).  
- Clearly distinguishes sMCL from prior ensemble/multiple-choice learning work.  
- Theoretical analysis and experiments support the technical claims; the paper is technically sound.  
- Original and significant contribution that reframes ensemble/multiple-choice learning for deep networks.  
- Highly relevant to the NIPS community and likely broadly applicable.  
- Major limitation: no discussion or strategy for selecting/exploiting ensemble members at inference without an oracle.  
- Practical usability is limited because exploitation strategies are problem-specific and not explored.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 2 embedded as raster image makes the pseudocode hard to read.  
- Recommendation: accept, but authors should address inference-time exploitation and fix presentation issues.
