This paper introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles specifically designed for scenarios where multiple hypotheses are desired, such as interaction with users or downstream processing. The core idea is to frame the task as Multiple Choice Learning (MCL), minimizing a loss function with respect to an oracle that selects the best output from an ensemble. The authors address a significant limitation of previous MCL work – the computational expense of retraining models – by proposing a stochastic block gradient descent optimization tailored for deep networks, enabling concurrent training of all ensemble members. The method is presented as simple to implement, parameter-free, and applicable to various architectures and loss functions.  Experiments on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning demonstrate the efficacy of sMCL, consistently outperforming classical ensembles and other baselines.

The paper builds upon a growing body of work in ensemble learning, particularly methods aimed at promoting diversity among ensemble members (e.g., [27, 21]). However, it distinguishes itself by framing diversity as a direct consequence of minimizing oracle loss, rather than imposing diversity constraints. It also directly addresses the limitations of previous MCL approaches [8, 9] by providing a practical algorithm for deep networks that avoids costly retraining. The work is related to recent methods for generating multiple solutions [3, 15, 16, 23, 24], but differs in its generality and efficiency, handling structured outputs without relying on probabilistic models.  The authors correctly position their contribution as the first practical algorithm for training diverse deep ensembles via oracle loss minimization.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novel Formulation:** The paper presents a compelling formulation of the multiple-output learning problem as MCL, particularly relevant for real-world applications where a single prediction is insufficient.
*   **Practical Algorithm:** sMCL offers a significant contribution by adapting the MCL paradigm to deep networks with a computationally efficient stochastic gradient descent approach. The ease of implementation and parameter-free nature are strong advantages.
*   **Strong Empirical Results:**  The experimental results across three diverse tasks (image classification, segmentation, and captioning) consistently demonstrate the superiority of sMCL over baselines. The consistent outperformance across different architectures and tasks indicates a robust method.
*   **Interpretable Expertise:** The observed emergence of specialization among ensemble members, visualized through oracle assignments, is a valuable insight and suggests the method learns meaningful representations.
*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and experimental setup.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Initialization Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't address the potential influence of initialization on the learned ensemble specialization.  Are the observed specializations consistent across different random initializations? The impact of pre-training versus random initialization is also not thoroughly explored.
*   **Baseline Comparison:**  A critical clarification is needed regarding the initialization of baseline models. Were the baseline ensembles initialized with the same pre-trained weights as the sMCL ensemble? If not, this introduces a confounding factor, as pre-training can significantly influence performance.  A fair comparison necessitates controlling for initialization.
*   **Limited Diversity Analysis:** While the paper highlights specialization, a more quantitative analysis of ensemble diversity would be valuable. Beyond the oracle assignment visualization, metrics like pairwise distance between outputs or entropy of the generated predictions could provide a more comprehensive assessment of diversity.
*   **Missing Related Work:**  The authors should consider citing "Why M Heads are Better than One" (Lee et al., 2015), which explores multi-head attention mechanisms as a means of capturing different aspects of input data. This work offers a conceptually related approach to diversity generation and could provide a useful point of comparison.
*   **Oracle-Only Evaluation:** The reliance solely on oracle metrics provides a limited view of the system's overall performance. It doesn’t reflect the performance of a downstream process choosing *from* the ensemble's outputs – the very scenario the method is designed for.  Analyzing the performance of simple selection mechanisms (e.g., averaging, majority voting) would strengthen the evaluation.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound approach to training diverse deep ensembles.
*   The proposed sMCL algorithm is practical, efficient, and easy to implement.
*   The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate significant improvements over existing methods.
*   The work addresses a relevant problem with real-world applications and offers a valuable contribution to the field.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The lack of thorough analysis regarding initialization sensitivity raises concerns about the robustness of the results.
*   The unclear baseline comparison compromises the validity of the empirical evaluation.
*   The limited diversity analysis prevents a complete understanding of the method’s behavior.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field of ensemble learning. The proposed sMCL algorithm is a valuable addition to the toolkit for training diverse deep networks. While there are some weaknesses regarding initialization sensitivity and baseline comparison, these concerns can be addressed with further experimentation. I believe the paper, with minor revisions addressing these points, is suitable for acceptance to NIPS. I recommend acceptance contingent on the authors clarifying the baseline initialization procedure and providing a more detailed analysis of ensemble diversity.
