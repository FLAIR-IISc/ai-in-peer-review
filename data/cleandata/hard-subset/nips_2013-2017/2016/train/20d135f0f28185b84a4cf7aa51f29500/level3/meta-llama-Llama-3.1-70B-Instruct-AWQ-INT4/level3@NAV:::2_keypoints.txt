- Proposes sMCL, an SGD-based method that trains ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss and encouraging member specialization.  
- sMCL trains ensemble members concurrently and is compatible with any SGD-trained model and any task-dependent loss.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks and architectures: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Empirical results show sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, including the slower MCL.  
- sMCL induces interpretable emergent expertise, producing label-space clustering where predictors specialize in certain classes.  
- Particularly useful for tasks needing multiple plausible hypotheses rather than a single prediction.  
- Major omission: no discussion or method for selecting an optimal ensemble member at inference without an oracle.  
- Practical usability is reduced by lack of guidance on deploying the ensemble in non-oracle settings.  
- Presentation issues: figures (e.g., Figure 2) are low-quality raster images making text hard to read.  
- Paper is technically sound with theoretical support and careful experimental evaluation.  
- Writing and organization are generally good, though some sections (notably related work) could be clearer.  
- High originality and significance: novel approach with potential broad impact on deep learning ensembles.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; paper is a good candidate for acceptance.
