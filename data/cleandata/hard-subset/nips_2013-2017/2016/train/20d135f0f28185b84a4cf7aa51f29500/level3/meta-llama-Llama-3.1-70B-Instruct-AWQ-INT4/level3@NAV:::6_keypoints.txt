- Proposes sMCL, a simple, parameter-free ensemble training algorithm that minimizes oracle loss via concurrent SGD.
- sMCL is architecture- and loss-agnostic and easy to implement.
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across image classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Provides thorough qualitative and quantitative analyses of ensemble behavior and outputs.
- Shows emergent ensemble member specialization and expertise when trained with sMCL.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- Experimental claims are significant and potentially impactful for deep learning ensembles.
- Testing phase is unclear, particularly the use of ground truth to select the best member.
- Terms like “independent ensembles” and “regular ensembles” are not well-defined or related to sMCL.
- Evidence for induced specialization is not fully convincing and needs more rigorous analysis.
- Omits discussion of related work on diversity regularization in neural networks.
- Recommendation: weak accept; scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.
