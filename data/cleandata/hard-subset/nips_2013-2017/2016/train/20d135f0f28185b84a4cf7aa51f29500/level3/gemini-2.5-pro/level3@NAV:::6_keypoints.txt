- Proposes sMCL: a stochastic, online “winner-take-gradient” adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning for training deep ensembles.  
- For each example, only the ensemble member with lowest loss receives a gradient update.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical oracle performance gains on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Strengths: simple, parameter-free, easily integrated with SGD, significant gains over baselines, and more computationally efficient than iterative MCL.  
- Major clarity concern: oracle evaluation description is ambiguous and may imply using ground-truth to choose members at test time—requires immediate clarification.  
- Baseline “independent ensembles (Indp.)” is not well defined, hindering comparison.  
- Claim that sMCL induces stable member “specialization” is unconvincing given per-example reassignment during training.  
- Suggestion: consider incorporating explicit specialization mechanisms into the loss to encourage class-level expertise.  
- Missing related work: omits key literature on diversity regularization in ensembles (e.g., Chen et al., 2019).  
- Overall: promising and effective idea but clarity issues and unsubstantiated claims limit confidence.  
- Recommendation: Weak Accept, contingent on clarifying test-phase oracle and addressing raised concerns.
