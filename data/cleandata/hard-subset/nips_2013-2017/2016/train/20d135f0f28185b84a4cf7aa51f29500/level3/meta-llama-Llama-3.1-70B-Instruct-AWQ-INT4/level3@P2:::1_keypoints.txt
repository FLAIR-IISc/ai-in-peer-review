- Proposes sMCL: an SGD-based method to minimize oracle loss in Multiple Choice Learning to produce diverse ensemble predictions scalable to deep networks.  
- Extends Guzman-Rivera et al. [8] by using SGD, allowing parallel training to convergence instead of iterative MCL schedules.  
- Main technical contribution is a stochastic block gradient descent formulation for minimizing oracle loss applicable to any SGD-trained model.  
- Empirical results on image classification, semantic segmentation, and captioning show sMCL outperforms baselines and classical ensembles with high oracle performance.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and provides thorough analysis and comparisons.  
- Concern that algorithmic novelty may be incremental relative to [8], though the change is practically useful.  
- Request to compare sMCL against MCL using mini-batches (rather than full-dataset MCL) for a fairer comparison.  
- Request for more quantitative analysis of ensemble diversity (e.g., pairwise distances, output entropy) beyond qualitative examples.  
- Reviewer recommendation: favors acceptance; rating 8/10, confidence 9/10.
