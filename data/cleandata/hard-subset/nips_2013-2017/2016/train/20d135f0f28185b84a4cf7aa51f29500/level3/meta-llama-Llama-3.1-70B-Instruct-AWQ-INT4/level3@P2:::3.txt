This paper proposes a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), which is a stochastic gradient descent-based method for minimizing the loss with respect to an oracle. The authors generalize the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm to jointly learn ensembles of deep networks that minimize the oracle loss directly. The proposed method is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The method is evaluated on three diverse tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning. The experimental results show that sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and other strong baselines, including the 5x slower MCL procedure.

The strengths of the paper include addressing an important MCL-for-deep-learning problem relevant to the community, the simplicity and ease of implementation of the method, and the comprehensive evaluation across three diverse tasks. The authors also provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its advantages.

However, there are some weaknesses and minor issues with the paper. One weakness is that the paper heavily motivates "diversity" but does not enforce an explicit diversity term, contrary to expectations. Another weakness is that the contribution appears incremental relative to the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al. Additionally, there are some minor issues, such as a typo on line 108, an extra "." in the middle of the sentence on line 115, and a suggestion to reword the first sentence of the abstract and tone down the emphasis on diversity in the title and text.

In terms of related work, the authors provide a comprehensive review of existing methods for training ensembles and generating multiple solutions. However, it would be helpful to clarify how the proposed method differs from existing methods and what are the key advantages of the proposed approach.

Regarding the quality of the paper, the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its advantages. The experimental results are well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the results. However, there are some minor issues with the writing style, such as the use of passive voice and some awkwardly phrased sentences.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles, which is a significant contribution to the field. The method is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the field of deep learning, and the proposed method has the potential to make a significant impact on the field. The authors provide a comprehensive evaluation of the method, and the results show that sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and other strong baselines.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method and its advantages. The experimental results are well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough analysis of the results. However, there are some minor issues with the writing style, and the contribution appears incremental relative to the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.
