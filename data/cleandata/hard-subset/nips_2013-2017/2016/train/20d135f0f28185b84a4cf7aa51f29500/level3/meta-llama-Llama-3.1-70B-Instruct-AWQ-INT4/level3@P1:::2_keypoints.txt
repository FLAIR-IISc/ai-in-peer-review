- Proposes sMCL: train ensembles by using an oracle to select the best member per example and backprop only on that network, encouraging specialization and diverse outputs.
- Thorough experimental evaluation across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning showing sMCL outperforms classical ensembles, MCL, and Dey et al.
- sMCL produces interpretable expertise: label-space clustering where predictors become specialists for certain classes.
- Task-specific specializations emerge (e.g., segmentation: shape/frequency-based specialists; captioning: diverse sentences capturing ambiguity).
- Major omission: no strategy given to exploit or select ensemble members at test time without the oracle.
- Lack of explored, problem-agnostic exploitation strategies limits practical usability and real-world applicability.
- Minor presentation issue: Figure 2 pseudocode is embedded as a raster image and hard to read; should be provided as text/vector.
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and presents strong, convincing empirical results.
- Could benefit from a more detailed discussion of limitations and directions for future work.
- Originality and significance: novel approach with potential for broad impact in training diverse deep ensembles.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, thorough experiments, clear presentation and strong results.
- Arguments against acceptance: missing inference/exploitation method and the Figure 2 presentation flaw.
