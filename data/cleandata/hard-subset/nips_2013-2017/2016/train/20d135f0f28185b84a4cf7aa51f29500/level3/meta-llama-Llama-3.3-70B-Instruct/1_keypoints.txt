- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensembles to produce multiple likely hypotheses.  
- Builds on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and adapts it to deep neural networks.  
- Key contribution is enabling SGD-based training, making the method more efficient and practical.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear, concise introduction and thorough related work review.  
- Technical contribution is sound and the sMCL algorithm is well-explained.  
- Experiments show broad applicability across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, including the 5x slower MCL.  
- Paper’s clarity and organization are major strengths.  
- Novelty may be viewed as incremental since it builds on existing MCL work.  
- Lacks comparison of sMCL using batches versus the entire dataset—recommended for further evaluation.  
- Needs more quantitative analysis of the algorithm’s “diversity” effects beyond qualitative examples.  
- Overall promising work with solid results; additional analyses could strengthen the contribution.
