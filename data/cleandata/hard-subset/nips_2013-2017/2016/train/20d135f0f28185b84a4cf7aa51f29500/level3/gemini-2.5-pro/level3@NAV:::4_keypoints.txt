- Proposes sMCL: training ensembles by minimizing oracle loss where only the best-performing member per example receives gradients.  
- Extends prior Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to modern deep networks by adapting optimization for SGD and concurrent training.  
- Demonstrated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning tasks.  
- Strength: well-motivated goal of generating multiple plausible hypotheses for downstream selection.  
- Strength: method is simple, intuitive, broadly applicable across architectures and losses, and adds no extra parameters.  
- Weakness: originality is incremental—largely an adaptation of prior MCL with minor SGD tweaks.  
- Weakness: conceptual novelty is limited.  
- Weakness: paper clarity is poor; technical description (Section 3) is very brief and hampers reproducibility.  
- Weakness: fails to adequately distinguish sMCL from other ensemble techniques like alternative or multi-task training.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is unconvincing—improvements are marginal and baselines appear weak.  
- Weakness: needs stronger, contemporary baselines and evaluation on established benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) for compelling validation.  
- Overall: interesting idea but lacks technical depth and strong empirical validation for acceptance.
