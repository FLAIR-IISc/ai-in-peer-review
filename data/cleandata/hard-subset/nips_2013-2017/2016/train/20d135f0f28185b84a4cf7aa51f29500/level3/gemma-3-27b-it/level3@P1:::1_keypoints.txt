- Proposes sMCL, an SGD-compatible adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning to train diverse deep ensembles efficiently.  
- Demonstrates sMCL improvements across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with emergent specialization of ensemble members.  
- Addresses scalability limitations of prior MCL approaches that required costly retraining.  
- Paper is well-written, clear, and situates the work effectively within ensemble and multiple-output prediction literature.  
- Technical approach is sound and justified; experiments show consistent gains over strong baselines.  
- Adaptation to SGD is a practical and valuable contribution, enabling applicability to modern deep architectures.  
- Results are significant due to broad applicability across modalities and interpretability of learned specialization.  
- Novelty is somewhat incremental since the core idea (oracle-loss minimization) stems from prior MCL work.  
- Comparison to original batch-based MCL is limited; a direct batch-update baseline would better quantify benefits.  
- Diversity analysis is mainly qualitative; more quantitative diversity metrics beyond oracle performance are needed.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions given practical impact despite incremental novelty.  
- Suggested improvements: add direct comparison to batch-based MCL, include quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise prediction distance, entropy), and discuss computational cost (training time/memory).
