- Introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a method training diverse deep ensembles using an oracle to select the best network per instance during backpropagation.
- Core idea: encourage ensemble member specialization so the ensemble covers a wider range of plausible outputs for downstream perception systems.
- Positions sMCL clearly within ensemble learning and MCL literature, differentiating it from computationally costly prior retraining/sequential methods.
- Strength: conceptually simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and computationally efficient.
- Strength: strong empirical results across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, outperforming baselines, MCL, and Dey et al.
- Strength: analysis shows emergent specialization among ensemble members, with effective visualizations in classification experiments.
- Presentation is generally clear and experiments are thorough and convincing.
- Major weakness: no discussion or solution for how to use the trained ensemble at inference time without access to the oracle.
- Practical usability is limited by the omission of exploitation/inference strategies and no exploration of problem-specific deployment approaches.
- Minor presentation issue: Fig. 2 pseudocode is embedded as a raster image, harming readability and copy-pasting.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-motivated approach with strong empirical validation and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: critical omission of practical inference strategies, limited usability, and a minor figure/presentation flaw.
- Overall recommendation: reject, with encouragement to address the inference-without-oracle problem to make the work practically impactful.
