- Introduces sMCL, a method for training ensembles to minimize oracle loss using a stochastic block gradient descent and a "winner-take-gradient" strategy compatible with end-to-end backprop.
- Extends Multiple Choice Learning to deep networks without retraining overhead.
- Demonstrates superior oracle accuracy versus classical ensembles, MCL, and other baselines across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Reports a notable 5× speedup over traditional MCL.
- Method is architecture- and task-agnostic, showing broad applicability.
- sMCL induces interpretable specialization among ensemble members, aiding ambiguity capture.
- Parameter-free and straightforward to implement, making it practitioner-friendly.
- Does not analyze sensitivity to initialization or compare random vs. pre-trained initialization effects.
- Baseline comparison details are unclear—uncertainty whether baselines used the same pre-trained networks as sMCL.
- Insufficient analysis of ensemble diversity beyond oracle accuracy.
- Omits comparison to relevant prior work "Why M Heads are Better than One" (Lee et al., 2015).
- Suggestion: include experiments on initialization sensitivity.
- Suggestion: clarify baseline pretraining/fairness in comparisons.
- Suggestion: provide deeper ensemble diversity analysis (e.g., pairwise disagreement, diversity indices).
- Suggestion: compare and position sMCL relative to Lee et al. (2015).
- Recommendation: Accept, contingent on addressing initialization, baseline clarity, diversity analysis, and additional comparisons in revision.
