- Proposes sMCL: a stochastic SGD-based method to train diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss without expensive retraining.  
- Avoids MCL’s retraining bottleneck by backpropagating gradients only to the “winning” predictor per example, enabling concurrent training.  
- Method is parameter-free, architecture-agnostic, and requires minimal implementation change (single sMCL layer).  
- Demonstrated across image classification (CIFAR-10), semantic segmentation (Pascal VOC), and image captioning (MSCOCO).  
- Shows consistent gains over classical ensembles, prior MCL, and Dey et al. (ICCV 2015), up to ~10% higher oracle accuracy.  
- Achieves substantial speedups (reported ~5× faster than MCL) making MCL practical for deep networks.  
- Experiments are comprehensive with effective visualizations and ablation studies (e.g., varying k) supporting algorithm behavior.  
- The oracle-loss framing is well-motivated for real-world ambiguous-output applications (e.g., autonomous driving, assistive tech).  
- Novelty is incremental: builds directly on Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], with stochastic implementation argued as a practical necessity.  
- Paper should better justify why stochastic approach is fundamentally more scalable (not only faster) and applicability to non-deep models.  
- Lacks rigorous comparison to a batch-based MCL variant (e.g., mini-batch MCL) to strengthen efficiency claims.  
- Missing quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise dissimilarity, entropy) to support claims of producing diverse/interpretable outputs.  
- Technical quality is high, results are statistically significant, and the work has strong practical significance for practitioners.  
- Recommendation: accept — practical, well-executed contribution that makes MCL usable for modern deep learning; future work should quantify diversity and clarify theoretical distinctions.
