- Introduces sMCL: trains diverse deep ensembles by selecting one optimal network per instance (via an oracle) and backpropagating only to that network.  
- Generalizes Multiple Choice Learning to deep networks and avoids costly retraining from prior MCL work.  
- Shows strong empirical gains (≈6–10%) over baselines on CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO.  
- sMCL induces interpretable member specialization (e.g., class-specific experts, semantic-role specialization) without manual design.  
- Method is technically sound, parameter-free, architecture-agnostic, and trains ensembles efficiently and concurrently.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough, using oracle-based metrics (top-k accuracy, oracle IoU) and compelling qualitative analyses.  
- Addresses a relevant problem: producing multiple plausible outputs for ambiguous real-world perception tasks.  
- Major omission: no practical inference strategy—paper assumes an oracle at test time and does not provide deployment methods (clustering, ranking, etc.).  
- Lack of guidance on problem-specific exploitation strategies makes the work less usable for practitioners.  
- Reproducibility/clarity issue: Figure 2 pseudocode is embedded as a raster image.  
- Overall: strong technical/significant results but the unaddressed inference gap is a critical flaw; recommendation: Weak Reject.
