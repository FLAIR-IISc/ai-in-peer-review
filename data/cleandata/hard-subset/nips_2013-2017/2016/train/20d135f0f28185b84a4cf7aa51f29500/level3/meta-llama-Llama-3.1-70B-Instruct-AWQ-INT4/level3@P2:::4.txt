The paper "Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning for Training Diverse Deep Ensembles" proposes a novel approach to training deep neural networks that produce multiple outputs, rather than a single prediction. The authors frame this problem as a learning task over an ensemble of deep networks, and introduce a stochastic gradient descent-based approach to minimize the loss with respect to an oracle. The paper relates to previous work at NIPS and elsewhere in the areas of ensemble learning, multiple choice learning, and structured output prediction.

The authors' main contribution is the formulation of a stochastic block gradient descent optimization approach, which they call Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL). This approach is applicable to any model trained with stochastic gradient descent and is agnostic to the form of the task-dependent loss. The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL for training diverse deep ensembles on a wide range of problem domains and network architectures, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.

The strengths of the paper include its clear motivation for the problem of multiple choice learning, and its thorough experimental evaluation on several tasks and datasets. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the training and output behaviors of the resulting ensembles, demonstrating how ensemble member specialization and expertise emerge automatically when trained using sMCL.

However, the paper has several weaknesses. Firstly, the novelty of the approach is incremental, and the method reads like a neural-network adaptation of prior work with minor adjustments. The technical section is also too short, and the distinction between the proposed framework and widely used alternative/multi-task training is not clarified. Furthermore, the experiments cover classification, segmentation, and captioning, but use weak baselines and show only small improvements.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-supported argument for their approach. However, the paper could benefit from more detailed theoretical analysis and a more thorough comparison to existing methods. The clarity of the paper is also affected by its poor writing style, which makes it difficult to follow in some sections.

In terms of originality, while the paper builds on existing work, it does introduce a novel approach to training deep neural networks that produce multiple outputs. However, the contribution is incremental, and the paper does not significantly advance the state of the art in a demonstrable way.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in machine learning, and the results have the potential to impact a wide range of applications, including computer vision, natural language processing, and robotics. However, the impact of the paper is limited by the small improvements over existing methods, and the lack of a thorough comparison to stronger baselines.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper addresses an important problem in machine learning, and the results have the potential to impact a wide range of applications.
* The authors provide a clear and well-supported argument for their approach.
* The paper is technically sound, and the experiments are thorough and well-executed.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The novelty of the approach is incremental, and the method reads like a neural-network adaptation of prior work with minor adjustments.
* The paper is poorly written, and the technical section is too short.
* The experiments use weak baselines and show only small improvements.
* The distinction between the proposed framework and widely used alternative/multi-task training is not clarified.

Recommendation:

* Evaluate the approach on PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against stronger baselines to validate effectiveness.
* Provide a more detailed theoretical analysis and a more thorough comparison to existing methods.
* Clarify the distinction between the proposed framework and widely used alternative/multi-task training.
* Improve the writing style and provide more details in the technical section.
