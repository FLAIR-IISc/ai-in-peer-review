**Paper Review: Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning for Training Diverse Deep Ensembles**

**Summary**

This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent (SGD) based approach for training diverse deep ensembles, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL). The method builds upon the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm, which aims to minimize the oracle loss by producing multiple outputs. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of sMCL on various tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, and show that it outperforms existing baselines.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful in evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work.

**Clarity**

The paper is clearly written, and the authors provide enough information for the expert reader to reproduce their results. The notation is well-defined, and the algorithms are presented in a clear and concise manner.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles, which is a significant contribution to the field. The authors build upon existing work on MCL, but their use of SGD to minimize the oracle loss is a new and innovative approach.

**Significance**

The results presented in the paper are significant, as they demonstrate the effectiveness of sMCL on various tasks. The authors show that their method outperforms existing baselines, and that it can be used to train diverse deep ensembles that produce high-quality outputs.

**Arguments For Acceptance**

* The paper proposes a novel and innovative approach to training diverse deep ensembles.
* The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their method.
* The results presented in the paper are significant, and demonstrate the effectiveness of sMCL on various tasks.
* The method is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free.

**Arguments Against Acceptance**

* The algorithmic novelty of the paper may be only incremental compared to existing work on MCL.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the algorithm's "diversity" effect.

**Suggestions for Improvement**

* The authors should compare their method with the MCL algorithm when using batches (rather than the entire dataset).
* The authors should provide more analysis of the algorithm's "diversity" effect, and how it relates to the quality of the outputs produced by the ensemble.

Overall, I believe that the paper is a strong contribution to the field, and that it should be accepted for publication. The authors have proposed a novel and innovative approach to training diverse deep ensembles, and have demonstrated its effectiveness on various tasks. With some minor revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, the paper has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.
