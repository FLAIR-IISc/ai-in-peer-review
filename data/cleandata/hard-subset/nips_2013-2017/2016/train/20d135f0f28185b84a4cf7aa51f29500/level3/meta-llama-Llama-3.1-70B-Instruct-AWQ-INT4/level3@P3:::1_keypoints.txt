- Proposes an SGD-based algorithm to train ensembles for Multiple Choice Learning (MCL), extending Guzman-Rivera et al. [8] to deep neural networks.  
- Main contribution: using SGD enables scaling and practical training of MCL for deep networks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and well-organized.  
- Thorough analysis and application to image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Experiments show the method outperforms baselines and yields high oracle performance.  
- Algorithmic novelty is somewhat incremental relative to [8], though the SGD adaptation is a useful practical improvement.  
- Lacks sufficient quantitative analysis of the diversity of produced solutions; current results are mainly qualitative.  
- Would benefit from comparisons with other ensemble methods (e.g., bagging, boosting).  
- Needs more discussion of limitations, hyperparameter/experimental details, and evaluation with different task-dependent losses.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10; Recommendation: Accept.
