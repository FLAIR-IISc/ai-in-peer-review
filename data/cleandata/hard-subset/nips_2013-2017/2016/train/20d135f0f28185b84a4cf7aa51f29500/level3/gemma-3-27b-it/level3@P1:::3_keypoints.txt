- Paper introduces sMCL, a stochastic gradient-based Multiple Choice Learning method that uses a "winner-takes-gradient" backprop rule to train diverse deep ensembles for producing multiple hypotheses.  
- Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free, and framed to minimize oracle loss efficiently.  
- Works across tasks (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning) and outperforms baselines in extensive experiments.  
- Demonstrates interpretable emergent specialization ("expertise") among ensemble members.  
- Ties to prior ensemble and MCL literature, positioning sMCL as a practical, more efficient alternative to Guzman-Rivera et al.  
- Simple to implement and integrates cleanly with standard SGD, increasing practical accessibility.  
- Strong empirical evaluation with comprehensive comparisons to classical ensembles, prior MCL, and related methods.  
- Main weakness: diversity is not explicitly enforced—diversity emerges indirectly via oracle loss minimization, contrary to the paper’s strong emphasis on diversity.  
- Contribution viewed as relatively incremental; core algorithmic advance is a relaxed optimization/SGD adaptation.  
- Lacks thorough theoretical analysis linking the relaxation to the proposed SGD approach.  
- Minor issues: unclear first sentence of abstract, overstated emphasis on diversity, typographical errors (lines 108 and 115).  
- Experimental question: unclear how baseline MCL convergence was ensured and whether early stopping hurt baseline performance.  
- Overall recommendation: solid, practical paper worthy of acceptance with minor revisions to address stylistic and theoretical concerns.
