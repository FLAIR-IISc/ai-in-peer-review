- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic gradient descent–based method for training diverse deep ensembles to minimize oracle loss.  
- Frames ensemble training as stochastic block gradient descent with a "winner-take-gradient" update for concurrent member training.  
- Method is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and technical exposition.  
- Provides thorough related work review and situates the contribution relative to prior work.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning) with strong empirical gains.  
- Experimental results show sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and a slower MCL baseline.  
- Builds on existing methods with only minor adjustments, limiting perceived originality.  
- Technical section lacks clarity on how the framework differs from alternative training methods.  
- Experiments lack strong baselines and comprehensive comparative analysis.  
- Paper needs more detailed analysis of training dynamics and the ensembles' output behavior.  
- Technically sound, claims supported by theory and experiments, and reproducible for expert readers.  
- Recommendation: accept with revisions to include stronger baselines and deeper analysis of behaviors.
