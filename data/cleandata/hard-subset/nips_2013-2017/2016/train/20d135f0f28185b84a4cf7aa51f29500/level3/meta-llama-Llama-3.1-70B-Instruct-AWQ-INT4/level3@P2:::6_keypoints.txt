- Proposes sMCL, a novel, SGD-based algorithm for minimizing oracle loss in multiple choice learning.
- sMCL is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.
- Evaluated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with lower oracle error than existing methods.
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly explained, and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Related work coverage is comprehensive overall but omits literature on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.).
- Testing phase is unclear; reviewer suspects ground-truth is used to select the best ensemble member.
- Terms "independent ensembles" and "regular ensembles" (Indp.) are used but not defined, causing confusion.
- Claim that ensemble members "specialize" to different classes is not convincingly justified; reassignments are per-example.
- Reviewer suggests exploring explicit specialization in the loss function as a possible improvement.
- Empirical results are strong and demonstrate the method's effectiveness across tasks.
- Paper is technically sound, honest about limitations, and generally reproducible for expert readers.
- Writing and organization are clear, though minor clarifications are needed (e.g., undefined terms and testing details).
- The approach is original and significant, with potential for wide applicability.
- Reviewer recommends weak acceptance pending minor revisions to address the noted issues.
