- Proposes sMCL: a simple winner-take-gradient method that stochastically optimizes oracle loss for diverse deep ensembles and avoids costly MCL retraining.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10, PASCAL VOC, and MSCOCO, showing substantial oracle-performance gains over baselines (e.g., ~7% relative IoU improvement).  

Strengths:
- Simplicity and practicality: easy to implement, parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, trains ensemble members concurrently.  
- Strong empirical performance: consistent, significant improvements in oracle metrics across classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Emergent specialization: qualitative evidence that members specialize on semantic classes or varied captions without explicit supervision.  

Weaknesses:
- Evaluation clarity: oracle metrics may be misinterpreted as deployable performance; paper doesn't clearly state oracle is only for measuring hypothesis-set quality.  
- Undefined baselines: "independent/regular ensembles" training procedure is not precisely described, harming reproducibility.  
- Weak justification for specialization: no analysis of why per-example winner selection yields stable, class-level specialization or assignment stability over training.  
- Missing related work: fails to discuss explicit diversity regularization literature (e.g., Negative Correlation Learning) and contrast implicit vs. explicit diversity methods.  

Suggestions / Requests:
- Explicitly state oracle evaluation intent and discuss real-world use cases (human-in-loop, rerankers).  
- Precisely define the independent-ensemble baseline (training protocol, initializations).  
- Provide analysis of assignment stability (how winners for a class change during training) to support specialization claims.  
- Consider and/or compare explicit diversity terms as a complementary baseline and add related-work discussion on diversity regularization.  

Overall recommendation:
- Weak Accept: novel, practical, and empirically strong, but needs clearer evaluation, baseline definition, specialization justification, and related-work context.
