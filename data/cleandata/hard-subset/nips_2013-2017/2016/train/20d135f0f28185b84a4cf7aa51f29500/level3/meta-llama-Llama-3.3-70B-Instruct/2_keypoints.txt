- Proposes sMCL, a novel, simple, parameter-free method for training ensembles that minimizes oracle loss and encourages specialization.  
- Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic and easy to implement.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Shows that ensemble members automatically specialize and develop expertise under sMCL.  
- Provides thorough analysis of training dynamics and output behaviors of ensembles.  
- Empirical results outperform classical ensembles and strong baselines, achieving state-of-the-art on several tasks.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly presents the sMCL idea and experiments.  
- Lacks a detailed discussion of limitations and potential drawbacks of sMCL.  
- Needs more insight into interpretability and practical use of specialized ensemble members.  
- Would benefit from additional visualizations and illustrative examples of sMCL’s effects.  
- Does not explore applications beyond the evaluated domains, e.g., NLP or reinforcement learning.  
- Overall, promising and effective approach with minor improvements needed; strong case for acceptance.
