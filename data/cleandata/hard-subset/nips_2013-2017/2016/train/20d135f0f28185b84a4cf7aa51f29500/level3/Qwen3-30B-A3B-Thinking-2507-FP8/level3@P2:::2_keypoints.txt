- Proposes sMCL: SGD-based training for deep ensembles that backpropagates only to the lowest-error (winner) model per example.
- Extends Multiple Choice Learning to deep networks, avoiding costly reassign/retrain cycles by enabling concurrent training.
- Formalizes training ensembles directly to minimize oracle loss, targeting systems where an oracle selects the best hypothesis.
- Evaluated on CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO, showing consistent 6–10% oracle-performance improvements over strong baselines.
- Compares favorably to independent ensembles, classical MCL, and Dey et al.'s sequential boosting approach.
- Demonstrates task-dependent, interpretable specializations (label clustering for classification, class/shape for segmentation, n-gram for captioning).
- Method is parameter-free, architecture-agnostic, and computationally efficient (reported ~5× faster than MCL).
- Provides a clear theoretical derivation for the winner-take-gradient update rule.
- Experiments and analysis are thorough and technically sound.
- Well-written and organized paper overall.
- Presentation flaw: Figure 2 pseudocode is embedded as a raster image, making it hard to read and reference.
- Originality is strong: first practical, concurrent deep training algorithm that directly minimizes oracle loss and scales MCL to deep models.
- Significance is substantial for applications needing diverse outputs and handling ambiguity (e.g., autonomous driving, assistive captioning).
- Major weakness: does not address how to exploit the trained ensemble at inference time without an oracle or with imperfect/no oracle.
