- Introduces sMCL, a training algorithm that directly minimizes oracle loss to produce diverse ensemble hypotheses.  
- Extends prior Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to deep networks via stochastic block gradient descent.  
- Key technical idea: update only the "winning" network (lowest loss) per example in a batch.  
- Efficient and compatible with standard backprop, avoiding costly iterative retraining of earlier MCL methods.  
- Motivation: multiple plausible outputs are valuable for human-in-the-loop or verification systems.  
- Method is simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and easy to implement.  
- Empirical results: oracle experiments on classification, segmentation, and captioning show clear gains over independent ensembles and baselines.  
- Experimental ambiguity: unclear whether baselines shared the same pre-trained initialization used for sMCL in segmentation.  
- Evaluation limitation: relies solely on oracle accuracy and does not provide quantitative diversity metrics.  
- Missing comparison to related work such as Lee et al. (2015) "Why M Heads are Better than One."  
- Overall: novel and practical contribution, but experimental evaluation should be clarified and strengthened.
