- Introduces sMCL: a winner-take-gradient training scheme where only the ensemble member with lowest loss for an example receives updates.
- sMCL is parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and simple to implement.
- Adapts original MCL to stochastic gradient descent, making it computationally feasible for deep networks.
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning versus baselines including MCL.
- Experiments are comprehensive and include useful qualitative examples for segmentation and captioning.
- Well-motivated problem framing and appropriate positioning relative to ensemble and multiple-choice learning literature.
- Evaluation methodology is unclear: reviewer questions whether oracle test results use ground truth to pick the best member.
- Terminology issues: terms like “Indp. ensembles” are introduced without definition and need clarification.
- Claim of ensemble member “specialization” is not fully convincing and may be an artifact of per-example assignment.
- Suggests exploring explicit specialization or diversity-promoting losses rather than relying solely on winner-take-gradient.
- Related work is incomplete; paper should discuss diversity-regularization methods (e.g., decorrelation approaches) and broader literature.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, practical, simple, and empirically compelling across tasks.
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear evaluation, insufficient justification of specialization, incomplete related work, and minor clarity issues.
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on clarifying evaluation, defining terms, and better covering diversity-related literature.
