Proposes sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based method for training diverse deep ensembles to minimize loss with respect to an oracle.  
Frames multiple-output prediction as learning an ensemble, motivated by practical systems needing multiple plausible hypotheses.  
Positions sMCL as a generalization of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and reviews related ensemble/multi-solution work thoroughly.  
Technical contribution: stochastic block gradient descent applicable to any SGD-trained model and agnostic to task-dependent loss.  
Demonstrates sMCL across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, showing ensemble member specialization emerges automatically.  
Experiments report sMCL significantly outperforming classical ensembles and other baselines, and being faster than standard MCL.  
Method appears technically sound and claims are supported by experimental results.  
Limitation: method resembles a neural-network adaptation of prior work [8] with only minor adjustments, raising originality concerns.  
Technical section (Sec. 3) is too brief and lacks detailed explanation of the optimization and its advantages.  
Missing analysis of optimization convergence properties and deeper theoretical justification.  
Experiments use relatively weak baselines and improvements are modest; stronger baselines and harder datasets (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) are recommended.  
Results presentation could be more concise and easier to interpret; some sections would benefit from clearer exposition.  
Paper could give a more critical discussion of limitations and potential application impact.  
Acceptance pros: novel approach, broad applicability, empirical improvements.  
Acceptance cons: limited novelty vs prior work, insufficient technical detail, weak experimental baselines and analysis.
