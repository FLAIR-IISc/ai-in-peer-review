- Proposes sMCL, an SGD-based algorithm adapting Guzman-Rivera et al.’s multiple-choice learning to train diverse deep-network ensembles by minimizing oracle loss.  
- sMCL enables concurrent training of ensemble members and avoids expensive retraining required by the original MCL.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, method, and experimental presentation.  
- Addresses a relevant problem: producing diverse outputs when a single prediction is insufficient (e.g., autonomy, HCI).  
- Demonstrates practicality and scalability across architectures (CNNs, FCNs, CNN+RNNs) and tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning).  
- Empirical results show consistent improvements over standard ensembles and match original MCL while providing substantial speedups.  
- Observes emergent specialization among ensemble members, offering insight into sMCL’s mechanisms.  
- Algorithmic novelty is incremental—the core oracle-loss idea is from [8], with SGD adaptation primarily an engineering advance.  
- Lacks deeper theoretical discussion on implications of using SGD vs. alternating block coordinate descent and whether stochasticity adds benefits or biases.  
- Missing a direct comparison to a batch-based implementation of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8] to isolate SGD advantages.  
- Diversity analysis is limited and mostly qualitative; needs quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., KL divergence or output dissimilarity).  
- Should explore trade-offs between oracle performance and diversity more thoroughly.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practicality, strong empirical validation, clear presentation, and relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, missing batch-based baseline, and insufficient quantitative diversity analysis.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, contingent on adding a batch-MCL comparison and more quantitative diversity analysis.
