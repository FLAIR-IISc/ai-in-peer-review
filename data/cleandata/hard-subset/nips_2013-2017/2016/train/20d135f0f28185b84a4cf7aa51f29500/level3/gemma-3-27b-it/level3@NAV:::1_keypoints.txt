- Proposes sMCL: a SGD "winner-take-gradient" method to train diverse deep ensembles within the MCL framework.  
- Builds on Guzman-Rivera et al. by removing costly retraining, enabling scaling to deep networks.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and connects to ensemble and multiple-output literature.  
- Experimental evaluation shows consistent improvements on classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Analysis reveals emergent specialization and interpretable expertise among ensemble members.  
- Practical contribution is significant: makes MCL applicable and accessible for real-world deep learning.  
- Algorithmic novelty is incremental relative to prior MCL work.  
- Lacks thorough comparisons with other diversity-inducing techniques in mini-batch settings.  
- Quantification of diversity is limited; needs explicit metrics for output dissimilarity beyond qualitative examples.  
- Overall recommendation: accept, subject to addressing the diversity-quantification and comparative-analysis suggestions.
