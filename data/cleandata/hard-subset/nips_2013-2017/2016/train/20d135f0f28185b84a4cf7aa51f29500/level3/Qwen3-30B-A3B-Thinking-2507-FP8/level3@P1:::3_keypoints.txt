- Proposes sMCL: train deep ensembles by assigning the winning (lowest-error) predictor to take the gradient during backprop.
- Targets the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) setting to produce multiple hypotheses for ambiguous inputs.
- Method is simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free, and trains ensemble members concurrently.
- Properly connects to prior MCL work, noting prior block-coordinate approaches are infeasible for modern deep nets.
- Main technical novelty: an SGD-based algorithm that interweaves assignment and training during the backward pass.
- Strong empirical gains on CIFAR10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO, outperforming independent ensembles, MCL baseline, and boosting with 6–10% oracle improvements.
- Demonstrates natural ensemble specialization (class/shape/ngram specialists) without explicit diversity regularization.
- Strengths: relevant problem, technically sound, easy to implement, and evaluated across diverse domains.
- Key weakness: emphasis on “diversity” is misleading because diversity is not explicitly enforced and arises as a byproduct of oracle loss minimization.
- Contribution seen as incremental: core MCL objective is from prior work; novelty is practical adaptation to deep learning.
- Minor issues: abstract first sentence awkward, title should tone down diversity claim, and small typographical errors (e.g., line 108, 115).
- Experimental clarity concern: unclear whether baseline MCL ensembles were fully converged or limited by meta-iterations.
- Evaluation summary: Quality strong but limited by implicit diversity; Clarity good but title mismatch; Originality incremental; Significance high.
- Recommendation: Accept with revisions to clarify the diversity claims and fix the noted minor issues.
- Overall: Useful, practical contribution for applying MCL to deep learning—valuable for practitioners though not a breakthrough.
