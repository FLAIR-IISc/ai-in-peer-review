- Proposes sMCL: a winner-take-gradient training strategy using an oracle per instance to encourage specialization and diverse ensemble outputs.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides a clear motivation for handling ambiguity.
- Thorough related work review covering ensemble learning and multiple-choice learning.
- Technical contribution: stochastic block gradient descent formulation to minimize oracle loss in deep ensembles.
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks and architectures (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning).
- Experimental results: sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, and is faster than MCL.
- Analysis shows ensemble member specialization and expertise emerge automatically under sMCL.
- Major omission: no discussion or guidance on using the trained ensemble at inference without the oracle (how to select or combine members).
- Minor presentation issue: pseudocode in Figure 2 is a raster image rather than editable text/vector graphic.
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.
- Original and significant contribution with potential impact across computer vision, NLP, and robotics.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address the inference/usage omission.
- Rating: 8/10; Confidence: 9/10.
