- Introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) for training diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss.  
- Uses stochastic block gradient descent integrated into standard backprop for end-to-end joint ensemble training.  
- Avoids computationally expensive retraining required by prior MCL methods.  
- Demonstrates empirical improvements on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic, simple, and parameter-free.  
- Qualitative results suggest emergence of interpretable expertise among ensemble members.  
- Major weakness: impact of initialization is not adequately analyzed; unclear if baselines used the same pre-trained initialization.  
- Evaluation relies solely on oracle accuracy; lacks quantitative ensemble diversity metrics (e.g., output distance, prediction entropy).  
- Missing comparison to related work on ensemble diversity, notably Lee et al. (2015) "Why M Heads are Better than One."  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and effective approach, clear theoretical/practical integration, strong empirical results, and practical relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of initialization effects, missing diversity quantification, and lack of comparison to closely related methods.  
- Reviewer recommendation: lean toward acceptance with minor revisions addressing initialization, diversity metrics, and related-work comparison.
