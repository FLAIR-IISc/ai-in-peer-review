- Proposes sMCL: a novel SGD-based, architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free algorithm to minimize oracle loss in Multiple Choice Learning.  
- Trains ensembles whose oracle-picked member minimizes error, aimed at handling ambiguous data where multiple solutions are useful.  
- Paper is well-written and clearly organized with a thorough review of related work and detailed method analysis.  
- Experiments show sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines (including a 5x slower MCL) across tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning).  
- Qualitative results demonstrate the diversity of solutions produced by sMCL.  
- Strength: simple, efficient, broadly applicable solution for training diverse deep ensembles.  
- Limitation: testing phase is unclear—use of ground truth to select best member is not explained practically.  
- Limitation: terms "independent ensembles" and "regular ensembles" are not well-defined or contrasted with sMCL.  
- Limitation: claim of member specialization lacks clear justification or empirical/mechanistic explanation.  
- Suggestion: specialization could be explicitly incorporated into the loss but is not explored.  
- Limitation: paper omits discussion of diversity-regularization literature in neural networks, a closely related area.  
- Evaluation scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10; overall recommendation: Weak acceptance.  
- For acceptance: novelty, broad applicability, and demonstrated diversity; against: unclear testing, weak specialization justification, missing related work.
