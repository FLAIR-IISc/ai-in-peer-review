- Introduces sMCL: backpropagate gradients only to the oracle-selected “winning” ensemble member per example, adapting Multiple Choice Learning to SGD and avoiding costly retraining.  
- Method is simple, elegant, parameter-free, and broadly applicable.  
- Strong empirical validation across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with consistent oracle-performance improvements over baselines.  
- Qualitative analysis convincingly shows induced specialization and diversity among ensemble members.  
- Paper is generally well-written and clearly presented.  
- Major omission: training relies on an oracle but the paper does not provide any strategy for selecting or combining outputs at inference time, harming practical significance.  
- Authors suggest downstream human/verification use but do not explore concrete exploitation or deployment strategies.  
- Minor presentation issue: pseudocode in Figure 2 is a low-resolution raster image, reducing clarity.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and original, but incomplete due to the lack of an inference/exploitation mechanism.
