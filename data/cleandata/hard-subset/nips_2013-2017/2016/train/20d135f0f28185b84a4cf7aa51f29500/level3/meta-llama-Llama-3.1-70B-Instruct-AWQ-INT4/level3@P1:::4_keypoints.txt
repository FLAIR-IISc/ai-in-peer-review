- Proposes sMCL: an SGD-based algorithm adapting Multiple Choice Learning to train diverse deep ensembles that minimize oracle loss.  
- Idea is intriguing but novelty is incremental over existing MCL work.  
- Paper is well-motivated and clearly explains the need for multiple hypotheses in real-world applications.  
- Authors demonstrate sMCL on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Technical description is brief and lacks implementation details of the algorithm.  
- Distinction between sMCL and existing alternative/multi-task training methods is unclear.  
- Experiments use weak baselines and show only modest performance gains.  
- Paper is technically sound but hampered by poor writing and reduced clarity.  
- Originality limited, though adapting MCL to deep networks is a notable contribution.  
- Significance: potential to improve oracle performance of deep ensembles across tasks.  
- Recommendation: evaluate on harder datasets (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and compare to stronger baselines for fuller validation.
