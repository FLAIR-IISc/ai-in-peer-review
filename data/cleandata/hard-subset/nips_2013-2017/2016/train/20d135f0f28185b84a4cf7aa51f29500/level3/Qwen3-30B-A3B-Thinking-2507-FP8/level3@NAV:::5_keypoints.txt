- Proposes sMCL: a stochastic block SGD that backpropagates only to the ensemble member with lowest loss per example to train deep ensembles efficiently.  
- Avoids costly retraining of prior MCL methods, enabling end-to-end ensemble training.  
- Applies sMCL across diverse architectures (CNNs for classification, FCNs for segmentation, CNN+RNN for captioning).  
- Reports substantial oracle accuracy improvements (6–10% gains) over baselines.  
- sMCL is parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and technically sound.  
- Method naturally induces interpretable specialization among ensemble members.  
- Excellently described and straightforward to implement.  
- Main experimental concern: unclear whether baselines used the same initialization as sMCL, possibly biasing results.  
- Evaluation focuses solely on oracle accuracy and lacks analysis of ensemble diversity, a core claim.  
- Insufficient related-work comparison, notably omitting Lee et al. (2015) "Why M Heads are Better than One."  
- Overall: strong practical and technical contribution, but evaluation and related-work coverage need strengthening to fully support superiority claims.
