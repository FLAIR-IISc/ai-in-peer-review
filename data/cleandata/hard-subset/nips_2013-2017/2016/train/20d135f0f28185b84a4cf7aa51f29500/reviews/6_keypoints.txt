- Proposes an ensemble training algorithm where only one learner is active per training example (the member with lowest loss w.r.t. true label is updated); simple idea with strong empirical results — reviewer recommends weak acceptance.  
- Testing phase is unclear; reviewer suspects authors use ground truth to select the best member (line 159, p.5).  
- Terms/baselines "independent ensembles" / "regular ensembles" (Indp.) appear in text/figures but are not explained (line 181, p.5).  
- Claim of member "specialization" to different output classes is not convincingly justified since training reassignments occur on a per-example basis.  
- Reviewer asks whether specialization could be explicitly incorporated into the loss function.  
- Related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) is not discussed; this literature is closely related and more rigorous but under-studied in deep settings.
