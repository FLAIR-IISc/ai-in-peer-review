- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize oracle loss to generate multiple outputs for user interaction.  
- Oracle experiments on three tasks demonstrate the proposed method's effectiveness.  
- Integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization for oracle loss.  
- Optimization is easy to implement within backprop and enables end-to-end training.  
- Concern: importance of initialization — unknown effect of random vs. pre-trained initialization.  
- Experimental comparison unclear — did baselines use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL?  
- Evaluation uses oracle accuracy only; lacks analysis of ensemble diversity.  
- Suggests comparison with related work: "Why M Heads are Better than One" (Lee et al., 2015).
