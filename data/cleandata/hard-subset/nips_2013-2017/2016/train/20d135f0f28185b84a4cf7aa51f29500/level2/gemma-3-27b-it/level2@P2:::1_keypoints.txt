- Proposes sMCL: a stochastic "winner-take-gradient" method that assigns each example to the ensemble member with lowest loss and backpropagates only through that member.
- Demonstrates sMCL improves performance and ensemble diversity on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Clearly connects to Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and positions sMCL as a practical, concurrent-training alternative to costly retraining approaches.
- Well-written paper with consistent notation and clear presentation of technical details.
- Novel formulation treating MCL as stochastic block gradient descent enabling efficient concurrent training.
- Practical to integrate into existing SGD-based pipelines and modern deep architectures.
- Strong empirical results showing consistent improvements over baselines, including traditional MCL.
- Provides interpretability via analysis of ensemble member specialization (e.g., class-wise clustering).
- Limited comparison to other diversity-inducing techniques (e.g., dropout, adversarial training, diversity-specific data augmentation).
- Claims of being parameter-free are undermined by baseline hyperparameters and lack of sensitivity analysis for sMCL and baselines.
- Heavy reliance on oracle loss as the primary metric; lacks discussion of applicability when oracles are imperfect or unavailable.
- Qualitative analysis helpful but would benefit from deeper, quantitative examination of member disagreement and relation to input uncertainty.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical algorithm with strong empirical evidence and valuable interpretability insights.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient comparisons to other diversity methods, missing hyperparameter sensitivity discussion, and reliance on oracle loss.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to include broader diversity comparisons and a brief hyperparameter sensitivity discussion.
