- Introduces sMCL, a novel SGD-based method for training diverse deep ensembles that directly minimizes oracle loss.  
- Extends Multiple Choice Learning by enabling concurrent training of ensemble members, avoiding costly retraining.  
- Core mechanism: "winner-take-gradient"—only the lowest-error predictor receives backpropagated gradients per example.  
- Validated across CIFAR10 classification, Pascal VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning, showing consistent gains.  
- sMCL outperforms independent ensembles and baselines (MCL, Dey et al.) in oracle metrics (6–10% relative improvement).  
- Achieves about 5× speedup over MCL in training.  
- Qualitative results show ensemble members develop interpretable specializations (class- or pattern-specific expertise).  
- Method is simple, architecture-agnostic, parameter-free, and broadly applicable to perception tasks requiring multiple hypotheses.  
- Paper is well-written, technically sound, and well-situated within ensemble/diversity literature.  
- Limitations: unclear scaling to very large ensembles (e.g., M=100) and handling of extremely high-dimensional outputs.  
- Claim of loss-function agnosticism needs more concrete examples beyond tested losses.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with practical utility; reviewer recommends acceptance.
