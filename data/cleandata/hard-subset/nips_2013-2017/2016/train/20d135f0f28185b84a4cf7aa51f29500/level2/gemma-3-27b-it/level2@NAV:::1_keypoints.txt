- Introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a method for training diverse deep ensembles.
- Uses a "winner-take-gradient" SGD rule: backpropagate only through the ensemble member with the best prediction per example.
- Aims to minimize oracle loss to produce multiple plausible hypotheses for oracle/human-in-the-loop evaluation.
- Demonstrated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with CNN, FCN, and CNN+RNN models.
- Empirical results show sMCL consistently outperforms standard ensembles, original MCL, and a boosting-like baseline.
- Method is conceptually simple, parameter-free, avoids costly MCL retraining, and applies to any SGD-trained model.
- Qualitative analysis reveals interpretable specialization among ensemble members, capturing task ambiguity.
- Experiments are comprehensive across diverse architectures and tasks, supporting practical applicability.
- Weakness: limited comparison to other diversity-promoting techniques beyond those tested.
- Weakness: lacks quantitative analysis of computational cost relative to standard training.
- Weakness: presentation issues—Figure 4 (classification) could clarify label-space clustering visualization.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the above weaknesses.
