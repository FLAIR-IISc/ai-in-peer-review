- Proposal: Introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) to train ensembles that produce multiple diverse, accurate predictions.  
- Motivation: Argues many real-world tasks benefit from multiple plausible hypotheses rather than a single prediction.  
- Main claim: sMCL yields lower oracle error than existing methods across diverse tasks and architectures.  
- Technical contribution: Presents a novel SGD-based method to minimize oracle loss for ensembles.  
- Experiments: Extensive evaluations on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning demonstrate broad applicability and effectiveness.  
- Presentation: Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and comprehensive related work.  
- Strengths: Novel, technically sound approach with well-designed, thoroughly evaluated experiments.  
- Analysis: Includes qualitative examples and thorough analysis of diverse solutions produced by sMCL.  
- Weakness: Does not provide a detailed analysis of computational cost compared to existing methods.  
- Weakness: Limited insight into interpretability of specializations that emerge among ensemble members.  
- Recommendation: Reviewer recommends acceptance.
