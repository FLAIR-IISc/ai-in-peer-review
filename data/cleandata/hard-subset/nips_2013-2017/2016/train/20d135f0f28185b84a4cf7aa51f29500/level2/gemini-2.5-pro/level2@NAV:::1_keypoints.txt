- Introduces sMCL, a novel, efficient method to train ensembles that produce diverse plausible outputs by optimizing oracle loss.  
- Uses a "winner-take-gradient" rule where only the ensemble member with lowest error for a sample gets updated within standard SGD.  
- Makes Multiple Choice Learning practical for deep nets by replacing costly iterative retraining with concurrent training, yielding significant speed-up.  
- Originality and significance: simple, elegant, and computationally feasible solution for generating diverse predictions, relevant to real-world applications.  
- Technical quality: strong, with comprehensive experiments on classification, segmentation, and captioning showing sMCL outperforms independent ensembles and prior MCL in oracle performance.  
- Demonstrates "emergent expertise": ensemble members specialize meaningfully, supported by qualitative and quantitative analysis.  
- Clarity: well-written and easy-to-follow motivation and method description.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of computational/memory trade-offs from training M models in parallel versus a single model.  
- Weakness: incomplete comparison to beam search in image captioning; single-model beam search achieves higher oracle CIDEr, and applying beam search to each sMCL member could clarify benefits.  
- Recommendation: strengths substantially outweigh minor weaknesses; a solid, impactful contribution deserving acceptance.
