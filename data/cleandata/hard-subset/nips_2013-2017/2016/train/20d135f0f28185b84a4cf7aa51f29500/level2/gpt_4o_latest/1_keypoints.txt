- Introduces sMCL, a parameter-free, architecture-agnostic method for training diverse ensembles to minimize oracle loss.  
- Motivates multiple hypotheses for ambiguous perception tasks where a single prediction is insufficient.  
- sMCL integrates with SGD and avoids costly retraining, achieving ~5x speedup over traditional MCL.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks and architectures (image classification, segmentation, captioning; CNNs, FCNs, CNN+RNN).  
- Shows consistent empirical improvements in oracle metrics and interpretable specialization among ensemble members.  
- Method is simple, practical, has no extra hyperparameters, and fits existing pipelines.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes clear figures and qualitative examples.  
- Lacks deeper theoretical analysis of why sMCL outperforms baselines, including convergence or generalization guarantees.  
- Scalability evaluation is limited to small ensembles (up to six members); larger-ensemble behavior is unexplored.  
- Missing ablation studies to isolate effects of design choices like the “winner-take-gradient” strategy.  
- Focuses on oracle metrics assuming a perfect downstream oracle and does not evaluate real-world downstream utility (e.g., reranking or human-in-the-loop).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical analysis and downstream/scalability evaluations.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical, broadly applicable, and empirically strong.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical insight and insufficient evaluation on larger ensembles/downstream tasks.
