- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based algorithm to train deep ensembles that minimize oracle loss.  
- sMCL is simple, parameter-free, and agnostic to architecture and loss function.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations and thorough related-work analysis.  
- Demonstrates sMCL on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Experimental results show sMCL outperforms baselines and yields high oracle performance.  
- Analysis shows ensemble member specialization and expertise emerge automatically with sMCL.  
- Strength: novel algorithm directly minimizing oracle loss.  
- Strength: simple implementation and broad applicability across architectures/tasks.  
- Strength: extensive experiments and detailed analysis of training/output behaviors.  
- Weakness: Assumes oracle loss is the primary evaluation metric, which may not always be appropriate.  
- Weakness: Lacks thorough analysis of computational complexity for large-scale use.  
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of robustness to hyperparameter tuning and noise.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address complexity and robustness concerns.  
- Overall rating: 8/10.
