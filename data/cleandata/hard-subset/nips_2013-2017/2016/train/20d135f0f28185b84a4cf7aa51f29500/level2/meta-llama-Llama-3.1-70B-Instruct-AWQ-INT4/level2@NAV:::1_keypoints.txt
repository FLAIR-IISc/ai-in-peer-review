- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a SGD-based algorithm that minimizes oracle loss for diverse deep ensembles.
- sMCL is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free, and time-efficient.
- Generalizes Multiple Choice Learning to jointly train ensembles of deep networks.
- Introduces a training algorithm that avoids costly retraining and suits modern deep architectures.
- Demonstrates broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL across multiple problem domains and network architectures.
- Empirical results show sMCL outperforms classical ensembles, Dey et al.'s method, and the slower MCL procedure.
- Provides qualitative analysis showing automatic emergence of ensemble member specialization and expertise.
- Strength: novel, well-motivated approach addressing shortcomings of existing diverse-ensemble training methods.
- Strength: thorough experimental analysis with both quantitative and qualitative evaluations.
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparisons to other ensemble techniques using different loss functions or regularization.
- Weakness: limited insight into interpretability of emergent specializations, especially for structured prediction.
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of sMCL limitations, including potential overfitting or mode collapse.
- Recommendation: paper makes a significant contribution and is recommended for acceptance despite areas for improvement.
