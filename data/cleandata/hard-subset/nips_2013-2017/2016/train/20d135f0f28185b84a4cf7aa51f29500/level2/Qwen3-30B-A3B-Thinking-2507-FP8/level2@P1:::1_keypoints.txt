- Proposes sMCL: an SGD-based ensemble training method that backpropagates only to the ensemble member with lowest loss for each example to minimize oracle loss.  
- Replaces expensive MCL retraining with concurrent training, yielding up to 5x speedup over MCL and up to ~10% relative oracle accuracy gains.  
- Strong empirical validation on CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO, outperforming baselines (classical ensembles, MCL, Dey et al.).  
- Method is simple, parameter-free, and agnostic to architecture and loss, making it practical for deep networks.  
- Concurrent training of ensemble members is a significant practical advance enabling MCL for modern architectures.  
- Emergent interpretable specialization: networks automatically become class/shape experts without manual design.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation, concise technical formulation, and thorough experiments.  
- Lacks theoretical analysis of convergence or guarantees for the winner-takes-gradient strategy; a brief discussion linking to stochastic optimization would help.  
- Related work misses some recent diversity-inducing ensemble methods (e.g., Kirillov et al. 2015, Ahmed et al. 2016); add brief comparisons to clarify the niche.  
- No ablation on the gradient assignment strategy—unclear if relaxing “only winner” to top-k would affect diversity or oracle accuracy; suggest experimenting with k.  
- Needs clearer comparison of absolute computational cost to standard ensembles and emphasis that concurrent sMCL training is roughly equivalent to training one model.  
- Significant and original contribution: makes MCL practical for deep learning, demonstrates novel emergent specialization, and merits acceptance.
