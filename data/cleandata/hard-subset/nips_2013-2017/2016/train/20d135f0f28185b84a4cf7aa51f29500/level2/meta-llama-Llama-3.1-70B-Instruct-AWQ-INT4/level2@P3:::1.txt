**Summary of Main Ideas and Relation to Previous Work**

This paper proposes a novel approach called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) for training diverse deep ensembles. The main idea is to formulate Multiple Choice Learning as a stochastic block gradient descent optimization problem, which allows for concurrent training of ensemble members. This approach is applicable to any model trained with stochastic gradient descent and is agnostic to the form of the task-dependent loss function. The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL for training diverse deep ensembles on various tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.

The paper relates to previous work on ensemble learning, Multiple Choice Learning, and generating multiple solutions. The authors acknowledge the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8] on Multiple Choice Learning and propose an alternative approach that overcomes the limitations of the original method. They also compare their approach to other baselines, including classical ensembles, MCL, and Dey et al. [5].
