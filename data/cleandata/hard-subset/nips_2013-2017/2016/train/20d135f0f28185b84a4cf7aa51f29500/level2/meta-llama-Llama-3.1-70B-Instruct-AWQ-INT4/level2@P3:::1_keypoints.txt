- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic block gradient descent formulation of Multiple Choice Learning for concurrently training diverse deep ensembles.  
- Applicable to any SGD-trained model and agnostic to task-dependent loss functions.  
- Demonstrated efficacy on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Positions work relative to ensemble learning, MCL (Guzman-Rivera et al.), and other baselines including Dey et al.  
- Novel approach that extends and overcomes limitations of prior MCL methods.  
- Enables efficient concurrent training of ensemble members, reducing training time.  
- Produces interpretable specializations among ensemble members.  
- Experiments are well-designed and paper is clearly written.  
- Limited depth of analysis and comparisons with other baselines.  
- No theoretical guarantees provided for sMCL’s performance.  
- Experimentation is somewhat limited in tasks and architectures, questioning generalizability.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 9/10, Significance 9/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address analysis, theory, and broader experiments.
