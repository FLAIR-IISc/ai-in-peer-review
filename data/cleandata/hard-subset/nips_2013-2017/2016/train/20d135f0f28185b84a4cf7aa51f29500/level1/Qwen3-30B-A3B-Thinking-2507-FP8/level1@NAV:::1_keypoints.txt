- Introduces sMCL, a novel SGD-based method for training diverse deep ensembles that produce multiple high-quality predictions.  
- Extends Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to deep networks using a "winner-take-gradient" rule where only the lowest-error predictor is updated per example.  
- Avoids costly retraining of prior MCL approaches and directly optimizes oracle loss (minimum loss over ensemble members).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains (6–10% improvements) on CIFAR10, PASCAL VOC, and MSCOCO, and is ~5× faster than prior MCL.  
- Shows sMCL naturally induces interpretable specialization among ensemble members (class-specific classifiers, object-specific segmenters, distinct captioning styles).  
- Method is technically sound, broadly applicable, and validated across diverse vision and NLP tasks.  
- Strength: the winner-take-gradient approach is simple, effective, and yields clear ensemble specialization without manual design.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison of computational cost against standard parallel ensemble training.  
- Weakness: limited theoretical analysis of why specialization emerges from the optimization dynamics.  
- Quality: high—rigorous experiments, strong baselines, and honest limitation discussion.  
- Clarity: excellent—well-organized writing and effective figures.  
- Originality: significant—first practical method to train deep ensembles by direct oracle loss minimization.  
- Significance: high—practical implications for systems needing multiple plausible predictions.  
- Recommendation: Accept.
