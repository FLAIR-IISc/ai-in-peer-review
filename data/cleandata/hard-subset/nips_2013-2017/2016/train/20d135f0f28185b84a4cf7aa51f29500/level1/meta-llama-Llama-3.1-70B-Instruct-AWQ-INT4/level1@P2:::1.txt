This paper introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles. The authors pose the task of producing multiple outputs as a learning problem over an ensemble of deep networks, with the goal of minimizing the loss with respect to an oracle. The proposed method is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and understand their contributions. The introduction provides a thorough motivation for the work, highlighting the limitations of traditional supervised learning and the benefits of producing multiple outputs. The authors also provide a clear and concise overview of the related work, distinguishing their approach from existing methods.

The technical contribution of the paper is the formulation of a stochastic block gradient descent optimization approach, which is well-suited for minimizing the oracle loss in ensembles of deep networks. The authors provide a detailed description of the algorithm and its implementation, making it easy to reproduce their results.

The experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL for training diverse deep ensembles. The authors evaluate their method on a wide range of tasks and deep architectures, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning. The results show that sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and other strong baselines, including the 5x slower MCL procedure.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to provide interpretable expertise among ensemble members. The authors demonstrate that sMCL can induce label-space clustering, segmentation specialists, and n-gram specialists, depending on the task. This is a significant contribution, as it shows that the proposed method can not only improve performance but also provide insights into the behavior of the ensemble.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The authors are careful and honest about evaluating both the strengths and weaknesses of their work. The paper is a complete piece of work, and the authors provide sufficient information for the expert reader to reproduce their results.

In terms of originality, the paper presents a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles. While the idea of multiple choice learning is not new, the authors' formulation of a stochastic block gradient descent optimization approach is a significant contribution. The paper also provides a unique perspective on the problem of producing multiple outputs, highlighting the benefits of minimizing the oracle loss.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the field of machine learning. The ability to produce multiple outputs is crucial in many applications, and the proposed method provides a significant improvement over existing approaches. The paper also has the potential to impact other areas of research, such as structured prediction and ensemble learning.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles.
* The proposed method is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free.
* The experimental evaluation is thorough and convincing, demonstrating the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL.
* The paper provides interpretable expertise among ensemble members, which is a significant contribution.
* The paper addresses an important problem in the field of machine learning and has the potential to impact other areas of research.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The paper may benefit from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.
* The authors could provide more insights into the behavior of the ensemble, such as the impact of the number of ensemble members on performance.
* The paper may benefit from a more detailed comparison with other existing methods, such as Bayesian neural networks or deep deterministic policy gradients.

Overall, I strongly recommend accepting this paper. The authors present a significant contribution to the field of machine learning, and their work has the potential to impact other areas of research. The paper is well-written, technically sound, and provides a thorough and convincing experimental evaluation.
