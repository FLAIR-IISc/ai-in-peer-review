- Tackles Multiple Choice Learning (MCL): training ensembles to produce diverse, high-quality hypotheses for ambiguous perception tasks.  
- Main contribution: Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel, practical algorithm for deep networks.  
- sMCL uses a "winner-take-gradient" rule: for each example, only the model with lowest loss receives the gradient.  
- Trains all ensemble members concurrently in one pass, avoiding costly iterative retraining of prior MCL methods.  
- Frames sMCL as a stochastic block SGD optimization of the MCL objective.  
- Demonstrates generality and effectiveness across CIFAR-10 classification, PASCAL VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning.  
- Empirical results show improved oracle performance and emergent, interpretable model specialization.  
- Algorithm is simple, parameter-free, and easy to integrate into standard training pipelines.  
- Paper is technically sound with comprehensive experiments and clear presentation.  
- Qualitative visualizations effectively illustrate the diversity benefits over standard ensembles.  
- Limited theoretical analysis: convergence and optimization dynamics of the hard-assignment procedure are not deeply studied.  
- Applicability constrained: requires a downstream oracle to select the best prediction, which may not always be available or cheap to query.  
- Strong case for acceptance: novel, practical method with strong empirical gains, high impact potential, and excellent clarity.  
- Counterpoints against acceptance: relatively brief theoretical grounding and dependence on the specific oracle-loss problem setting.
