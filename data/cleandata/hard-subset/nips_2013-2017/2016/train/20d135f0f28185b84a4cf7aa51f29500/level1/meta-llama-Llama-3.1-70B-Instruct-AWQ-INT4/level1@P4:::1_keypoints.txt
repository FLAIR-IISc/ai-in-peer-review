- Proposes sMCL, a novel SGD-based method to train diverse deep ensembles minimizing oracle loss.  
- Generalizes Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to jointly learn deep network ensembles.  
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization (sMCL) that trains ensemble members concurrently.  
- Method is applicable to any SGD-trained model, agnostic to task-dependent loss, parameter-free, and time-efficient.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Empirical results show sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, including a 5x slower MCL.  
- sMCL naturally induces specialization among ensemble members along task-specific dimensions.  
- Paper is technically sound with supportive theoretical analysis and thorough experimental evaluation.  
- Writing, notation, and mathematical derivations are clear and easy to follow.  
- The approach is original and represents a significant contribution to training diverse ensembles.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem with potential for wide adoption across tasks.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed computational complexity comparison to existing methods.  
- Weakness: experimental evaluation could include more baselines or comparisons to additional state-of-the-art methods.  
- Weakness: relies on oracle loss as the performance measure, which may not always reflect practical objectives.
