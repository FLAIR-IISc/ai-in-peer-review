This paper proposes a novel approach to training deep neural network ensembles, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), which aims to minimize the oracle loss directly. The authors generalize the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm to jointly learn ensembles of deep networks and present a stochastic gradient descent (SGD) based algorithm to train ensemble members concurrently. This approach is applicable to any model trained with stochastic gradient descent, is agnostic to the form of the task-dependent loss, and is parameter-free.

The main idea of sMCL is to interleave the assignment step with batch updates in stochastic gradient descent, where the gradient of the loss for each example is backpropagated only to the lowest error predictor on that example. This approach is simple to implement and can be used with existing ensemble architectures.

The authors evaluate sMCL on various tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, and demonstrate its broad applicability and efficacy. They show that sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and other strong baselines, including the 5x slower MCL procedure. The analysis also shows that sMCL automatically generates specializations among ensemble members along different task-specific dimensions.
