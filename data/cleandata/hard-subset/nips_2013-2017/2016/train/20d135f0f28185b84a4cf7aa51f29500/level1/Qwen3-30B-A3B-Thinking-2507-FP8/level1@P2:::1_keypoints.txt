- Introduces sMCL, an SGD-based method that optimizes oracle loss for diverse deep ensembles and avoids costly retraining required by prior MCL.  
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over strong baselines (classical ensembles, MCL, submodular) on CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO.  
- Technically sound formulation with clear derivation (Eq.1) and an efficient Algorithm 2 that minimizes oracle loss.  
- Comprehensive experimental design covering multiple architectures, tasks, and metrics (oracle accuracy, IoU, CIDEr-D).  
- Provides a 5x speedup over previous MCL and reports 6–10% relative performance gains across domains.  
- Method is simple, parameter-free, and architecture-agnostic, making it practical for adoption.  
- Novel finding: interpretable ensemble specializations emerge automatically (e.g., class-wise specialization).  
- Writing and organization are excellent, with accessible notation and effective visualizations (e.g., Figure 4c).  
- Insufficient theoretical analysis of sMCL’s convergence properties compared to standard SGD.  
- Missing implementation details, notably tie-breaking for determining the “winning” predictor during training.  
- Underexplored relationship between the number of ensemble members and oracle performance.  
- Does not discuss potential limitations in extremely large-scale applications.  
- Overall: significant, original, and practically relevant contribution to ensemble learning; recommended for acceptance.
