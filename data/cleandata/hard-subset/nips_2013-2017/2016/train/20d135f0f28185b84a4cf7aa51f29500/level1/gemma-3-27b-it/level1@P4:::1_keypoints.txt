- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic multiple-choice learning method that minimizes oracle loss via a "winner-take-gradient" update during SGD.  
- Claims sMCL is parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and more efficient than prior MCL approaches.  
- Formulates training as stochastic block gradient descent to enable concurrent training of ensemble members.  
- Demonstrates empirical effectiveness on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Reports a roughly 5x speedup over iterative MCL retraining for deep networks.  
- Shows sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and baselines (including MCL and Dey et al.) across tasks.  
- Observes emergent specialization among ensemble members, improving interpretability.  
- Builds on prior MCL and ensemble-diversity literature, positioning sMCL as a practical extension to deep models.  
- Novelty: provides an efficient, conceptually simple solution to training diverse deep ensembles for oracle loss.  
- Generality: experiments support applicability across architectures and loss types.  
- Efficiency: concurrent training materially reduces training time compared to prior MCL.  
- Weak baseline comparisons: lacks evaluation against more recent/advanced ensemble diversity techniques (e.g., dropout variants, adversarial diversity).  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: claim of being parameter-free overlooks tuning of standard training hyperparameters (learning rate, batch size, etc.).  
- Limited ablations: needs more analysis (e.g., effect of ensemble size M) to isolate component contributions.  
- Theoretical gap: lacks formal theoretical justification or analysis of optimization behavior.  
- Presentation issue: some figures (e.g., Fig. 4b) are hard to read and need clearer labeling/font sizes.  
- Acceptance arguments: novel, effective, practical solution with strong empirical results and clear exposition.  
- Rejection concerns: limited theory, incomplete baseline comparisons, and unaddressed hyperparameter sensitivity.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper with important contributions; recommended for acceptance despite noted limitations.
