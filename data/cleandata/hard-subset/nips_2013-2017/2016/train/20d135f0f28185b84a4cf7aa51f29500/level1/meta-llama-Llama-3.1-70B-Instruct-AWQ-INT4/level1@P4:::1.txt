The paper "Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning for Training Diverse Deep Ensembles" proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent-based approach to train diverse deep ensembles that minimize oracle loss. The authors generalize the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm to jointly learn ensembles of deep networks and introduce a stochastic block gradient descent optimization approach, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL). The method is applicable to any model trained with stochastic gradient descent, is agnostic to the form of the task-dependent loss, is parameter-free, and is time-efficient, training all ensemble members concurrently.

The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL for training diverse deep ensembles on a wide range of problem domains and network architectures, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning. The experimental results show that sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and other strong baselines, including the 5x slower MCL procedure. The analysis also shows that exactly the same algorithm (sMCL) automatically generates specializations among ensemble members along different task-specific dimensions.
