- Introduces sMCL, an SGD-based method for training ensembles to minimize oracle loss by producing multiple plausible outputs.  
- Frames multiple-output generation as a learning task where ensemble members produce diverse solutions to reduce oracle error.  
- sMCL is parameter-free, computationally efficient, and compatible with any architecture or loss function.  
- Uses a "winner-take-gradient" strategy that integrates with SGD to avoid costly retraining or sequential training.  
- Demonstrates sMCL across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with consistent oracle performance and diversity gains.  
- Comprehensive experiments compare sMCL to classical ensembles, MCL, and other baselines, showing significant performance improvements.  
- Shows interpretable specialization among ensemble members (e.g., label-space clustering, diverse segmentations).  
- Technical novelty: sMCL substantially improves over existing MCL approaches.  
- Broad applicability: validated on diverse tasks and architectures.  
- Efficiency: reports ~5x speedup over MCL while maintaining or improving performance.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear methodology and results.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of failure cases (e.g., imbalanced data or extreme ambiguity).  
- Weakness: comparison with beam search for captioning lacks detailed trade-off analysis.  
- Weakness: scalability to larger models/datasets (e.g., ImageNet) is not demonstrated.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical, scalable solution with thorough experiments and easy implementation.  
- Cons against acceptance: insufficient exploration of limitations and scalability.  
- Recommendation: accept — strong technical contribution and evidence, with suggested future work to address weaknesses.
