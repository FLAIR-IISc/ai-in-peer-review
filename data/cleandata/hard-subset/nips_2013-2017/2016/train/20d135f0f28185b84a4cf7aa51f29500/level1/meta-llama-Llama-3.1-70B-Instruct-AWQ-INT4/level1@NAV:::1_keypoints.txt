- Proposes sMCL, an SGD-based, parameter-free algorithm that directly minimizes oracle loss to train diverse deep ensembles.  
- Method is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness across tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Builds on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and avoids costly retraining and sequential training.  
- Outperforms classical ensembles and the method of Dey et al. in experiments.  
- Paper is technically sound with supporting theoretical analysis and thorough experiments.  
- Writing is clear and provides enough detail for expert readers to reproduce results.  
- Novel contribution: directly optimizing oracle loss for multiple-output tasks and applicable to modern deep networks.  
- Significant potential to advance state of the art in ensemble methods and multi-output tasks.  
- Weaknesses: needs more detailed theoretical analysis and deeper insight into how ensemble members specialize.
