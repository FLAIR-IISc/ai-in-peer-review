

# Peer Review

## Summary
This paper introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach for training diverse deep ensembles that directly minimizes the oracle loss (the minimum loss across multiple predictions). The authors build upon the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework introduced by Guzman-Rivera et al. [8, 9], but overcome a critical limitation of previous work: the need for costly retraining of models in an alternating fashion. sMCL is formulated as a stochastic gradient descent approach where, for each example, only the model with the lowest loss on that example receives gradient updates during backpropagation. This enables concurrent training of ensemble members without the need for iterative retraining. The paper demonstrates sMCL's effectiveness across three distinct deep learning tasks: image classification (CIFAR-10), semantic segmentation (Pascal VOC), and image captioning (MSCOCO), showing consistent improvements over existing baselines (including classical ensembles, MCL, and Dey et al. [5]).

## Strengths

**Quality**: The paper presents a technically sound approach with clear theoretical grounding. The authors provide a rigorous formulation of the problem and a well-motivated solution. The experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering multiple architectures, tasks, and dataset sizes. The paper carefully addresses both strengths and weaknesses (e.g., noting that sMCL's specialization is less evident in segmentation than in classification). The results consistently demonstrate significant improvements (6-10% relative improvement in oracle metrics) over baselines, with the sMCL ensembles achieving lower oracle loss and faster convergence than MCL (5x faster).

**Clarity**: The paper is exceptionally well-written and organized. The problem statement, motivation, and technical contributions are clearly articulated. The algorithm description (Algorithm 2) is concise and easy to understand. The figures effectively illustrate key concepts, particularly the emergence of interpretable specializations among ensemble members (Figures 4c, 5b, 7). The paper provides sufficient detail for reproducibility, including specific training parameters for each task.

**Originality**: The work makes a clear and significant contribution to the literature. While Guzman-Rivera et al. [8, 9] introduced MCL, their approach required costly retraining, making it impractical for deep networks. The sMCL approach is novel in its use of stochastic gradient descent to overcome this limitation, and the authors properly contextualize their work against previous approaches. The paper demonstrates that sMCL is the first practical algorithm for training diverse deep ensembles, which the authors correctly position as a significant gap in the literature.

**Significance**: The problem addressed has clear practical importance, as many real-world perception systems benefit from multiple plausible hypotheses rather than single predictions. The paper convincingly demonstrates that sMCL provides a simple, general solution to this problem that can be applied across multiple domains (classification, segmentation, captioning) with significant improvements in oracle performance. The method is straightforward to implement (requiring only a minor modification to existing training code) and is parameter-free, making it highly accessible to practitioners.

## Weaknesses

**Quality**: While the paper demonstrates strong empirical results, the theoretical justification for why sMCL leads to better specialization than other methods could be strengthened. The authors note that specialization emerges "automatically" but provide limited analysis of why this occurs. Additionally, the paper doesn't thoroughly explore the impact of different loss functions on the specialization patterns, though this is noted as a limitation.

**Clarity**: The paper could be clearer about the practical limitations of the approach. For example, it would be helpful to know how the method scales with very large ensemble sizes (beyond M=6 as tested) and whether there are diminishing returns. The discussion of the k-parameter in Figure 4 (which controls the number of predictors penalized per example) is somewhat brief.

**Originality**: While the paper properly cites relevant work, it could more explicitly contrast sMCL with other recent approaches to ensemble diversity. For instance, the paper mentions "generalist" network performance statistics [12, 2], but doesn't clearly explain how sMCL differs from these approaches.

**Significance**: The paper demonstrates strong empirical results but could better articulate the broader impact of the work. While the paper mentions applications like autonomous vehicles and visually-impaired assistance, it could provide more specific examples of how sMCL would improve real-world systems beyond just better oracle metrics.

## Pro and Con Acceptance Arguments

**Pro:**
- Novel approach that solves a significant practical problem (training diverse deep ensembles) with a simple, efficient algorithm
- Comprehensive experimental validation across multiple domains and architectures
- Clear demonstration of how the method leads to interpretable specializations among ensemble members
- Simple implementation requiring only minor modifications to existing training pipelines
- Significant performance gains (6-10% relative improvement) over strong baselines

**Con:**
- Limited theoretical analysis of why the approach leads to specialization
- Limited exploration of the method's behavior with very large ensemble sizes
- Some questions remain about how the approach generalizes to other structured prediction problems beyond those tested

## Overall Assessment
This paper presents a technically sound, well-motivated, and practically significant contribution to the field of ensemble learning. The sMCL approach is a clear advancement over previous Multiple Choice Learning methods, providing a simple, efficient, and effective solution to training diverse deep ensembles. The paper is well-written, clearly organized, and thoroughly evaluated. The method's simplicity, broad applicability, and strong empirical results make it a valuable contribution to the field. While the theoretical justification could be strengthened, the practical benefits and clear demonstration of the approach's advantages make this a strong candidate for acceptance at NIPS. The paper addresses an important problem with a novel solution that has clear potential for real-world impact.
