- Proposes sMCL, a novel method to minimize oracle loss for deep ensembles by generalizing Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and training members jointly.  
- sMCL interleaves assignment with SGD: backpropagates each example only to the lowest-error predictor; parameter-free and SGD-agnostic.  
- Simple to implement and compatible with existing ensemble architectures.  
- Applicable to any model trained with SGD and agnostic to task-dependent loss form.  
- Evaluated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Demonstrates significant improvements over classical ensembles and other baselines, including faster and better than 5x slower MCL.  
- Automatically induces specialization among ensemble members along task-specific dimensions.  
- Paper is well-written with clear notation, figures, and thorough, convincing experiments.  
- Technically sound; claims supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Original and significant contribution with broad implications for deep learning ensembles.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, clarity, strong experimental support, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: needs more experiments on more challenging datasets, more analysis of computational complexity and scalability, and clearer discussion of limitations.  
- Suggestions: provide computational complexity/scalability analysis, discuss limitations and future work, and add experiments on harder tasks/datasets.
