- Proposes sMCL, a method that trains diverse deep ensembles by assigning gradients to the lowest-loss member per example, minimizing oracle loss via SGD.  
- Builds on Multiple Choice Learning but removes expensive retraining, making diversity training feasible for large networks.  
- Gradient derivation and algorithm are technically sound and integrate with standard SGD training pipelines.  
- Parameter-free approach and practical efficiency are notable contributions.  
- Demonstrates consistent empirical improvements over independent ensembles and original MCL across classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Shows interpretable specialization of ensemble members, indicating emergent expertise.  
- Paper is well-written and organized, with helpful illustrative figures.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical analysis of convergence and formal guarantees for sMCL.  
- Comparisons could include more recent diversity-inducing deep learning methods beyond independent ensembles.  
- Does not thoroughly analyze hyperparameter sensitivity, particularly the effect of ensemble size M.  
- Qualitative analysis of emergent expertise is insightful but would benefit from more systematic visualization and evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong, practically relevant contribution with solid results; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing theory and hyperparameter analysis.
