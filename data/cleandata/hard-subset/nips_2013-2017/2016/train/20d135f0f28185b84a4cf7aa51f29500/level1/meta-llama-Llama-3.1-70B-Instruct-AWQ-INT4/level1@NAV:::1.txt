**Summary of the Main Ideas**

This paper proposes a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL). The authors introduce a stochastic gradient descent-based algorithm that minimizes the oracle loss directly, which is a more suitable objective for tasks that require multiple outputs. The proposed method is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free. The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL on various tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm, which was introduced by Guzman-Rivera et al. [8]. However, the proposed sMCL algorithm overcomes the limitations of MCL by avoiding costly retraining and sequential training. The authors also compare their approach to other existing methods, such as classical ensembles and the method of Dey et al. [5], and show that sMCL outperforms these baselines.
