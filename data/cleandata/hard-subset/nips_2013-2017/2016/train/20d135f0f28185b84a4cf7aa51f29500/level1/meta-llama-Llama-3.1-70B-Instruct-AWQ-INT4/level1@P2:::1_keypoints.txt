- Introduces sMCL, a novel, simple, parameter-free method for training diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss.  
- Technical core: stochastic block gradient descent formulation for optimizing oracle loss in ensembles.  
- Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic and easy to implement and reproduce.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides thorough motivation and related-work discussion.  
- Experiments are thorough and convincing across tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning) and architectures.  
- sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, including a 5x slower MCL.  
- sMCL produces interpretable specialization among ensemble members (label clusters, segmentation specialists, n-gram specialists).  
- Claims are technically sound and supported by theoretical analysis and empirical results.  
- Originality: extends multiple choice learning with a novel stochastic optimization perspective.  
- Significance: addresses an important problem (producing multiple outputs) with potential impact on structured prediction and ensemble learning.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novelty, simplicity, strong empirical results, interpretability, broad applicability.  
- Con-acceptance concerns: lacks detailed computational complexity analysis, needs more insight on effect of ensemble size, and more comparisons to alternatives (e.g., Bayesian NNs, DDPG).  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept.
