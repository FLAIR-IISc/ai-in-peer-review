## Review of "Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning for Training Diverse Deep Ensembles"

This paper introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss using stochastic gradient descent. The core idea is to assign gradients only to the ensemble member with the lowest loss on each example during training, effectively encouraging specialization. This builds upon previous work on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) [Guzman-Rivera et al., NIPS 2016], but crucially avoids the computationally expensive retraining steps required by earlier methods. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of sMCL across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning tasks, showing improvements over both standard ensembles and the original MCL approach. 

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed sMCL algorithm is well-defined and leverages the readily available infrastructure of SGD-based training. The derivation of the gradient update rule is clear and logically sound.
* **Originality:** While building on the MCL framework, sMCL offers a significant practical advancement by making ensemble diversity training feasible for large deep networks. The parameter-free nature of the approach is a notable advantage.
* **Significance:** The ability to train diverse ensembles with minimal overhead is valuable. The experiments demonstrate consistent improvements over baselines across varied tasks, suggesting broad applicability. The interpretable specialization of ensemble members is a particularly compelling result.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a clear explanation of the problem, proposed method, and experimental setup. The inclusion of illustrative figures (e.g., Fig. 1, Fig. 4) enhances understanding.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Theoretical Analysis:** While the gradient derivation is sound, a more rigorous theoretical analysis of the convergence properties of sMCL would be beneficial. 
* **Comparison to Modern Diversity Techniques:** The comparison to baselines, while comprehensive, could be strengthened by including more recent diversity-inducing techniques specifically designed for deep learning, beyond simple independent ensembles. 
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't explicitly discuss the sensitivity of the results to hyperparameters such as ensemble size (M). A more thorough analysis of how performance changes with varying M would be useful.
* **Qualitative Analysis Depth:** While the qualitative results are insightful, a more systematic analysis of the emergent expertise within the ensembles (e.g., using visualization techniques) could further strengthen the claims.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a practically relevant problem - training diverse ensembles efficiently.
* Offers a novel and effective solution with strong empirical results.
* Demonstrates clear advantages over existing approaches (MCL and independent ensembles).
* The method is broadly applicable and easy to implement.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited theoretical analysis.
* Could benefit from a more thorough investigation of hyperparameter sensitivity.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of ensemble learning. The proposed sMCL algorithm addresses a significant practical challenge and demonstrates promising results. While some areas could be further strengthened, the benefits outweigh the drawbacks. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions addressing the points mentioned above.
