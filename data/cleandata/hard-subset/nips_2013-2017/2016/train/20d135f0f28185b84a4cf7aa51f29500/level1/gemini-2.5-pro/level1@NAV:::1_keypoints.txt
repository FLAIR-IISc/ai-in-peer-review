- Introduces sMCL, a training algorithm for deep ensembles that minimizes an "oracle loss" by penalizing only the best hypothesis in a set.  
- Adapts Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to modern deep learning by integrating it into stochastic gradient descent.  
- Uses a simple "winner-take-gradient" rule: for each mini-batch example, backpropagate gradients only to the ensemble member with lowest loss.  
- Encourages specialization and diversity among ensemble members, producing diverse high-quality predictions.  
- Empirically validated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, outperforming independently trained ensembles and original MCL.  
- Method is simple, parameter-free, broadly applicable, and much faster/practical than prior MCL approaches.  
- Demonstrates that interpretable specializations naturally emerge among ensemble members.  
- Original contribution lies in making oracle-loss minimization feasible for deep nets via concurrent SGD (stochastic block gradient descent).  
- Paper is exceptionally well-written, clearly motivated, and effectively presented with strong figures/tables.  
- Main weakness: utility relies on a downstream "oracle" (e.g., human) to select the correct hypothesis; the selection problem is not addressed.  
- Overall assessment: an excellent, significant, and practically useful contribution likely to be widely adopted.
