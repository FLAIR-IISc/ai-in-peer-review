- Introduces sMCL, a method for training diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss.  
- Core idea: during SGD, backpropagate gradients only to the ensemble member with lowest loss on each example (winner-take-all).  
- Contrasts with prior methods (Guzman-Rivera, Dey et al.) that require iterative/sequential retraining and don’t scale well to deep networks.  
- Provides clear theoretical justification: derives gradient update rule and shows equivalence to a constrained optimization problem.  
- Strong empirical results demonstrating improvements on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Thorough comparisons against appropriate baselines (independent ensembles, MCL, Dey et al.) showing sMCL’s advantages.  
- Ablation studies and qualitative visualizations reveal emergent specialization/expertise within ensemble members.  
- Paper is generally well-written with clear technical details and reproducible experimental setup.  
- Related work section is somewhat verbose and could be more focused on the most relevant contributions.  
- Originality: problem is known but sMCL’s practical, scalable SGD formulation and broad application are novel.  
- Significance: practical, general method applicable to any SGD-trained model/loss with potential real-world impact and improved interpretability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty & practicality, strong empirical evidence, clear technical contribution, interpretability, and generality.  
- Arguments against acceptance: related work needs tightening and limited discussion of failure cases or scenarios where sMCL may underperform.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance (Strong Accept).
