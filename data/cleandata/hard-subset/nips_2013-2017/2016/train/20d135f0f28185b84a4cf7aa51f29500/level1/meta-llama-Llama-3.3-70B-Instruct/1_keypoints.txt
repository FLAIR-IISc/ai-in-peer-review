- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) to train ensembles that produce multiple diverse solutions by minimizing oracle loss.  
- Formulates ensemble training as a stochastic block gradient descent optimization problem.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear introduction and thorough related work overview.  
- sMCL is a simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free SGD-based approach.  
- Authors analyze training and output behaviors, showing automatic member specialization and expertise.  
- Demonstrates strong experimental results across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and stronger baselines, including a slower MCL variant.  
- Strength: novel and effective approach for multiple choice learning in deep networks.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of ensemble behavior and specialization.  
- Strength: impressive empirical validation and broad applicability.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed comparison to other methods for generating multiple solutions (e.g., probabilistic structured-output models).  
- Weakness: limited analysis of computational complexity and scalability for large-scale applications.  
- Recommendation: Accept — significant technical contribution with practical applicability.
