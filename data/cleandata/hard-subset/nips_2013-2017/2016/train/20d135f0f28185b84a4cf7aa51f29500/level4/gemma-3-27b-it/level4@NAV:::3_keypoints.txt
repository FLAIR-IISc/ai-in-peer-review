- Paper tackles multiple choice learning (MCL) for deep networks using a “winner-takes-the-gradient” layer to jointly optimize learners and assignments.  
- Evaluated on image classification, segmentation, and captioning, showing strong empirical performance.  
- Strengths: addresses an important MCL problem for the community, proposes an elegant/simple method, and provides comprehensive experiments across tasks.  
- Major concern: paper emphasizes “diversity” (even in the title) but does not explicitly enforce a diversity term in the model.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental, building on existing relaxation techniques (e.g., Guzman et al.).  
- Minor edits: rewrite the abstract opening for clarity; temper the focus on diversity; fix typo on line 108 (“f”→“g”); remove extra period on line 115.  
- Question for authors: How was convergence ensured for each ensemble network before truncating training—could early termination have hurt ensemble performance?
