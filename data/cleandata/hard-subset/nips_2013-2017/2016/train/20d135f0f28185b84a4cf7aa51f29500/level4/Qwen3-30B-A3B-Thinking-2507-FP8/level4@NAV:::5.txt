

The authors introduce a method for generating multiple outputs by jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize the oracle loss, offering utility in user-interaction scenarios. The approach demonstrates effectiveness across three oracle-based tasks. This work integrates deep networks into the MCL framework and proposes a stochastic block gradient descent optimization technique to minimize the MCL oracle loss. The optimization is readily implementable within standard back-propagation and enables end-to-end training of the entire model. Key concerns include: (1) How critical is the initialization strategy? Specifically, what performance degradation occurs when models are initialized randomly rather than using a pre-trained network? (2) The paper lacks clarity on comparison settings: Do baseline methods employ the same pre-trained network as sMCL for initialization? (3) The evaluation relies solely on oracle accuracy; the diversity of ensemble members requires deeper analysis. Additionally, the authors should compare against the closely related work: Stefan Lee, Senthil Purushwalkam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, *Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks*, CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015).
