- Paper proposes jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize an oracle loss by integrating deep nets into Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and using a stochastic block gradient descent procedure for end-to-end backprop.  
- Request experiments on sensitivity to initialization: report results with random (non-pretrained) initializations, convergence behavior, and variance across seeds; explain if and why pretraining is essential for data partitioning.  
- Ask for details to ensure fair comparisons: clarify whether baselines use the same pretrained initialization and match learning rates, augmentation, training duration, and model capacity.  
- Request evaluation beyond oracle accuracy: include diversity metrics (pairwise disagreement, error correlation, coverage), quantify specialization and its relation to oracle gains, and add qualitative complementary-prediction examples.  
- Demand discussion and (if possible) empirical comparison with related work on diverse ensembles, especially Lee et al. (2015), covering architecture choices (independent vs multi-head), loss formulations, and training protocols.  
- Overall assessment: idea and end-to-end optimization are strong, but addressing initialization sensitivity, fair baselines, diversity analyses, and related-work comparisons would substantially strengthen the paper.
