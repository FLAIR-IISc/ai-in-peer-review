- Proposes a novel method to generate diverse outputs by jointly training an ensemble of deep networks using oracle-loss minimization, useful for interactive systems.  
- Demonstrates method effectiveness via oracle experiments on three distinct tasks.  
- Integrates deep learning with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent method to minimize MCL oracle loss.  
- Optimization is compatible with standard back-propagation, enabling end-to-end trainability.  
- Method shows sensitivity to initialization; impact of using random vs. pre-trained weights needs investigation.  
- Experimental setup and baseline comparisons lack sufficient detail (unclear if baselines share the same pre-trained initialization).  
- Reports oracle accuracy but omits analysis of diversity between ensemble members—diversity metrics and investigation required.  
- Needs comparison to closely related work, especially “Why M Heads are Better than One” (Lee et al., 2015).
