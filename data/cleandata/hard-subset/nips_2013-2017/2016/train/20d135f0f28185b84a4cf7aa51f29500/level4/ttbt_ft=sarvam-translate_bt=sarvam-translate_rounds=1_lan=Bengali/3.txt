This paper proposes a simple solution to the problem of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL), where students are deep learning networks. Current solutions to MCL do not directly extend to deep learning networks. This paper shows that a simple layer of winner-takes-the-gradient in back propagation allows students' training to be combined with an assignment problem in an ensemble setting. Experimental results show that this method performs well in various problems: such as image classification, image segmentation, and captioning.

Strengths: - In general, the MCL problem for deep learning is of considerable importance to the NIPS community. - I liked the simplicity of the proposed method. - The experiments show the results of 3 different tasks.

Weaknesses: - My biggest concern with this paper is that it encourages "diversity" extensively (even though the word "diversity" is in the title), but the model does not explicitly apply diversity. I was very excited to see how the authors were able to bring the word "diversity" into their model and was disappointed when I found out that there was no diversity. - The proposed solution is a step forward considering the relaxations proposed by Guzman et. al.

Small suggestions: - The first sentence of the abstract needs to be rewritten. - Diversity should be reduced. - Line 108, the first "f" should be replaced with "g" "we fixed the form of .." - An extra "." in the middle of a sentence at line 115.

One question: For baseline MCL with deep learning, how did the authors ensure that each network converged to a reasonable result? Cutting students too early can significantly affect the effectiveness of the ensemble.
