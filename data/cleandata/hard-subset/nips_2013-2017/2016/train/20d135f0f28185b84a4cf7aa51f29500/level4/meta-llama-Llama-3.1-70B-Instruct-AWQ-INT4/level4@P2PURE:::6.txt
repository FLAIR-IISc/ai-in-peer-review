The paper proposes an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners, distinct from previous deep and classical ensembles in that only one learner is active for each training example. During training, the member with the lowest loss with respect to the true label is identified and updated with the gradient. This simple yet effective idea yields surprisingly good experimental results, addressing some deficiencies of existing methods, which is why I recommend weak acceptance.

However, there are a few aspects that require clarification. Firstly, the testing phase is not clearly explained, and it appears that the authors use the ground truth to find the best member, as mentioned in line 159 on page 5. Secondly, the paper refers to 'independent ensembles' and 'regular ensembles' (denoted as 'Indp.' in figures and line 181 on page 5), but the explanation for this baseline is missing. 

Furthermore, the authors emphasize the 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes, but the reassignment during training occurs on a per-example basis, which does not fully justify this argument. It would be beneficial to incorporate specialization into the loss function to strengthen this claim. Additionally, the authors overlook the related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), a more rigorous technique that warrants investigation in the deep learning setting.
