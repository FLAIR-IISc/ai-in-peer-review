This paper introduces a stochastic gradient descent method integrated with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). The authors apply this framework to a variety of tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning, asserting its advantages.

1.  The novelty of the proposed sMCL method appears to be incremental. While the motivation for using MCL is well-explained, the concept itself is not a novel contribution of this work. The proposed method reads as a neural network adaptation of the work in [8], incorporating some minor modifications.

2.  The manuscript's presentation could be improved. The core technical description in Section 3 is notably brief, covering less than a full page. Furthermore, the paper does not adequately distinguish the proposed framework from common multi-task optimization strategies like alternating training, which is a widely used approach when handling multiple objectives.

3.  The authors present experiments across a range of computer vision applications, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the empirical evaluation is not entirely convincing. The experiments lack comparisons against strong, state-of-the-art baselines, and the reported performance improvements are marginal. To better validate the efficacy of the proposed framework, the authors should conduct experiments on a standard, challenging benchmark such as PASCAL VOC 2012 and include comparisons against more competitive methods.
