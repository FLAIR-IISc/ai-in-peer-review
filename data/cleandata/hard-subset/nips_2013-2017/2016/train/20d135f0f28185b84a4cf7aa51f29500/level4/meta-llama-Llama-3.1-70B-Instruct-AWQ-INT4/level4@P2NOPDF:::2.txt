The paper proposes a novel approach for training ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) using an oracle that selects the optimal network for each training instance. By applying backpropagation only to the chosen network, the method enables specialization among networks, resulting in ensembles that effectively cover a wide range of possible outputs. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their approach across various domains, achieving impressive performance.

The paper presents an intriguing idea for training DNN ensembles with an oracle-guided backpropagation update, which promotes specialization in classification tasks. The overall presentation is clear, and the experiments are well-conducted across multiple domains. This is a strong work that merits publication.

However, I believe the paper would be outstanding if it addressed the exploitation of the trained DNN ensembles. Currently, the oracle is used during training, but the utilization of the resulting ensemble is not thoroughly explored beyond its potential to provide a broad pool of possible results and its coherence with the top-k metric proposed for certain problems (e.g., ImageNet). This omission is a crucial aspect for making the proposed approach fully practical. Nevertheless, the contribution is significant, and the missing component is relatively problem-specific.

One minor suggestion is to improve the presentation of Figure 2, which is currently a raster image embedded in the paper. Consider either including the pseudo-code directly within the paper or embedding the figure as a vector image to enhance readability.
