- Introduces sMCL: train ensembles by updating only the lowest-loss member per example ("winner-take-gradient") to optimize oracle loss.
- sMCL is simple, parameter-free, and architecture- and loss-agnostic, requiring minimal changes to standard pipelines.
- Trains all ensemble members concurrently, offering substantial speedups over iterative MCL retraining approaches.
- Shows strong empirical gains across classification, segmentation, and captioning, improving oracle performance and output diversity.
- Analysis indicates ensemble members learn interpretable specializations (e.g., classes or caption styles).
- Evaluation relies on an oracle selector; practical selection/reranking mechanisms for real-world use are not discussed.
- Baselines (e.g., "Indp. ensembles") need clearer, explicit definitions for reproducibility.
- The mechanism causing stable, semantically meaningful specialization from per-example winner updates needs further explanation or justification.
- Paper omits comparison to explicit diversity-regularization methods and should discuss related work on diversity-promoting losses.
- Recommendation: overall positive—simple, effective, and practical method with strong results; reviewer recommends acceptance.
