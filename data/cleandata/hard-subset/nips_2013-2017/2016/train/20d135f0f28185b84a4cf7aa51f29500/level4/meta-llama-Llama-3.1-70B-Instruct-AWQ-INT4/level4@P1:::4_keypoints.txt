- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic gradient descent method extending Multiple Choice Learning to deep neural networks.  
- sMCL interleaves assignment with batch updates to enable concurrent training of ensemble members, avoiding repeated retraining.  
- Claims sMCL is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Demonstrates applicability and efficacy on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning benchmarks.  
- Provides clear explanation of the framework and its relation to prior work.  
- Presents thorough analysis of results, showing ensemble member specialization and interpretable expertise.  
- Technical section is relatively short and may lack sufficient detail for some readers.  
- Experiments could be strengthened with more robust baselines and evaluations on more challenging datasets (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012).  
- Novelty is limited—main contribution is extending existing MCL to deep networks rather than a fundamentally new idea.  
- Overall assessment: solid contribution with potential after revisions.  
- Reviewer scores: Quality 7/10, Clarity 8/10, Originality 6/10, Significance 7/10.
