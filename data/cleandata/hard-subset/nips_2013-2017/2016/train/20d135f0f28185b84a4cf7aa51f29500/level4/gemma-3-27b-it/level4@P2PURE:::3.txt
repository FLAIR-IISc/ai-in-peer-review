This paper introduces a straightforward approach to multiple choice learning (MCL) with deep neural networks, a setting where existing MCL methods do not readily apply. The authors demonstrate that incorporating a winner-takes-the-gradient layer into backpropagation effectively integrates learner training with the assignment problem, enabling an ensemble learning framework. The method’s effectiveness is validated through experiments on image classification, image segmentation, and captioning tasks. 

Strengths: The problem of MCL for deep learning is significant and relevant to the NIPS community. The proposed method is notable for its simplicity. The experimental evaluation spans three diverse tasks.

Weaknesses: A primary concern is the emphasis on "diversity"—reflected even in the title—without explicit enforcement of diversity within the model. The motivation led me to expect a diversity term in the model, and its absence is disappointing. Furthermore, the solution represents an incremental advance over the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al. 

Minor suggestions: The opening sentence of the abstract would benefit from rewriting. The language regarding diversity should be moderated. On line 108, “f” should be corrected to “g” in “we fixed the form of…”. Line 115 contains an extraneous period mid-sentence.

Question: Regarding the baseline MCL implementation with deep learning, how was convergence ensured for each network? Prematurely halting learner training could negatively impact ensemble performance.
