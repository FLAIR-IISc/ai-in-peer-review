- Paper introduces sMCL, a practical SGD-friendly adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning that backpropagates each example only to the ensemble member with lowest error ("winner-take-gradient").

- sMCL enables concurrent training of deep-network ensembles, avoiding costly alternating retraining of original MCL.

- Demonstrated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, showing improved oracle performance and qualitatively diverse, interpretable outputs.

- Practical significance: makes MCL feasible for large deep networks and reports substantial speedups (e.g., ~5x over 5-iteration MCL).

- Generality and simplicity: architecture-, loss-, and parameter-agnostic method that produces task-specific specializations automatically.

- Strong empirical validation: outperforms independent ensembles, related baselines, and original MCL across tasks, with compelling qualitative examples.

- Well-written and clearly motivated paper with good connection to prior work.

- Limited novelty: core objective is identical to Guzman-Rivera et al. [8]; main contribution is adapting it to SGD rather than a fundamentally new algorithm.

- Diversity analysis is weak: reliance on oracle metrics; more direct quantitative diversity measures (e.g., pairwise dissimilarity, JS-divergence, 1–IoU) would strengthen claims.

- Missing comparison: no evaluation against a mini-batch/epoch-updated variant of original MCL to disentangle benefits of concurrent training versus more frequent assignment updates.

- Recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance due to significant practical impact despite incremental algorithmic novelty.

- Suggestions: explicitly acknowledge incremental novelty, add quantitative diversity metrics, and—if feasible—compare to a mini-batch version of original MCL.
