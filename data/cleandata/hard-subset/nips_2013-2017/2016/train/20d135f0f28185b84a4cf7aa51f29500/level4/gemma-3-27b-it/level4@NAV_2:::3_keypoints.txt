- Proposes a simple "winner-take-gradient" backprop strategy to scale multiple choice learning (MCL) to deep neural networks, enabling joint training and ensemble assignment.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Addresses a relevant problem for the community and is broadly applicable to models trained with SGD.
- Method is conceptually simple and easy to implement.
- Experimental evaluation covers a wide range of tasks and architectures.
- Paper overemphasizes "diversity" in title and text despite not explicitly enforcing a diversity objective.
- The approach relies on implicit specialization via oracle loss minimization rather than a direct diversity mechanism.
- Novelty is incremental, primarily a relaxation/extension of prior work by Guzman et al.
- Minor editorial fixes: rephrase first sentence of abstract; tone down "diversity" claims; replace first "f" with "g" on line 108; remove extraneous period on line 115.
- Question: How was convergence of individual networks in the baseline MCL implementation with deep nets ensured to avoid premature halting harming ensemble performance?
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a practical problem, offers a simple effective algorithm that reduces computational cost of prior MCL methods, and shows strong results across tasks.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty over prior work and lack of explicit diversity enforcement may restrict exploration of solution space.
