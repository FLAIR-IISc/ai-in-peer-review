- Proposes oracle-guided ensemble training: for each training example, an oracle selects the optimal member and only that member receives backpropagated gradients.  
- Method encourages ensemble member specialization so the group collectively covers diverse outputs.  
- Experiments across multiple domains show consistently strong results and the idea is clearly presented.  
- Paper lacks a developed strategy for exploiting the trained ensemble at inference time; training depends on an oracle not available at test time.  
- Notes alignment with top-k metrics (e.g., ImageNet) but leaves inference-time usage problem-specific and undeveloped.  
- Despite the inference gap, the core contribution is valuable and the paper merits publication.  
- Minor presentation issue: Figure 2’s pseudocode is embedded as raster images and should be typeset or provided as vector graphics for readability.
