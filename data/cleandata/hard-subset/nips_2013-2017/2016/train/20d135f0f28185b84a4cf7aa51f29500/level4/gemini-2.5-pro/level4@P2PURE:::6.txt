This paper proposes an ensemble training algorithm for deep neural networks. Its key novelty is that for each training example, only a single member of the ensemble—the one with the lowest loss with respect to the ground truth label—is selected and updated. This contrasts with traditional methods where all members are typically trained. The proposed method is conceptually simple, yet it yields surprisingly strong experimental results and addresses some deficiencies of existing approaches. For these reasons, I recommend weak acceptance.

However, several points require clarification and improvement:

1.  The inference procedure at test time is not clearly explained. It is unclear how the ensemble makes a prediction on a new example without access to the ground truth label. The description on page 5, line 159, seems to imply that the best member is selected using the ground truth, which would be invalid during testing. The authors must clarify this critical aspect of their method.

2.  Throughout the figures and text (e.g., page 5, line 181), the paper refers to baselines called 'independent ensembles' or 'regular ensembles' (denoted as 'Indp.'). However, the paper does not provide a definition or reference for this baseline, making it difficult for the reader to properly interpret the comparative results.

3.  The authors repeatedly claim that the method encourages 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes. This argument is not sufficiently justified, as the member selection mechanism operates on a per-example basis, not a per-class basis. It is not obvious how this per-example assignment leads to stable, class-specific experts. A more direct approach might be to incorporate a specialization term into the loss function.

4.  Related to the point above, the paper fails to discuss the highly relevant work on diversity regularization in neural network ensembles (e.g., Chen et al.). These techniques offer a more rigorous approach to encouraging diversity. While they have yet to be thoroughly studied in modern deep learning settings, a discussion and comparison would significantly strengthen the paper's contribution.
