- Proposes sMCL, a method to jointly train deep-network ensembles to minimize oracle loss and generate multiple outputs; generalizes MCL, avoids costly retraining, and is simple/agnostic/parameter-free.
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization that integrates with backprop for end-to-end training.
- Experiments on three tasks show sMCL outperforms baselines on oracle accuracy and induces automatic specialization of ensemble members.
- Strength: novel and effective approach for training diverse deep ensembles.
- Strength: method is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.
- Strength: provides thorough analysis of training dynamics and output behaviors of ensembles.
- Weakness: unclear importance of initialization—no analysis of random vs. pre-trained initialization effects.
- Weakness: insufficient experimental details—unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL.
- Weakness: evaluation relies on oracle accuracy with limited quantitative analysis of ensemble diversity and member specialization.
- Weakness: missing comparison to related work ("Why M Heads are Better than One," Lee et al. 2015).
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing the above clarifications and additional analyses.
