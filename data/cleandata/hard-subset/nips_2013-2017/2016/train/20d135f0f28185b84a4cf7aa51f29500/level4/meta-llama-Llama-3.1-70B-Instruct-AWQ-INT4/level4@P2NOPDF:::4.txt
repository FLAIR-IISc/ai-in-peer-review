The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim that the proposed framework offers advantages in multiple tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, this paper demonstrates that the stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) method is somewhat incremental in novelty. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it cannot be considered a contribution of this work, as it is essentially a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments.

The paper's writing quality is also a concern. The technical section (Section 3) is brief, spanning only one page, and fails to clearly articulate the differences between the proposed framework and alternative training methods, which are widely adopted when training multiple tasks together.

The authors conduct experiments on various computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. Nevertheless, the experiments lack strong baselines, and the improvement in performance is marginal. To convincingly demonstrate the efficiency of their framework, the authors should perform experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012, comparing their results to more robust baselines. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their method's effectiveness.
