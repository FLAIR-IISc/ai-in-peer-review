- Introduces a novel SGD algorithm for training ensemble models, extending Guzman-Rivera et al. [8].  
- Ensemble loss defined as the minimum loss across individual classifiers, which promotes diversity.  
- Main contribution: adapting the min-loss ensemble framework to SGD for efficient deep neural network training.  
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making technical content accessible.  
- Primary concern: apparent incremental novelty because change is largely replacing batch training with SGD.  
- Authors convincingly argue the SGD shift substantially improves practicality for large-scale deep learning.  
- Recommendation to evaluate the original MCL algorithm under mini-batch SGD to isolate the effect of SGD vs. batch processing.  
- Recommendation to provide quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise disagreement, entropy) to empirically demonstrate increased diversity.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, clarity and practical utility mitigate novelty concerns.  
- Final verdict: recommend acceptance pending the suggested clarifications and additional experiments.
