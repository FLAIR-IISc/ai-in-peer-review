- Proposed method: joint ensemble of deep networks within the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework, minimizing ensemble oracle loss via a stochastic block gradient descent that is end-to-end trainable with standard backprop; shown effective on three tasks using the oracle metric.

- Concern 1 — Importance of initialization: paper lacks ablation on random vs. pre-trained initialization to assess robustness and dependence on pre-training.

- Concern 2 — Clarity of experimental comparisons: unclear whether baselines were initialized with the same pre-trained network as sMCL; this must be clarified for fair evaluation.

- Concern 3 — Evaluation metrics and diversity: evaluation relies solely on oracle accuracy and omits quantitative analysis of output diversity, a key motivation for ensemble methods.

- Concern 4 — Missing related work: authors overlooked a closely related prior paper (Lee et al., 2015, "Why M Heads are Better than One") and should discuss/compare to it.
