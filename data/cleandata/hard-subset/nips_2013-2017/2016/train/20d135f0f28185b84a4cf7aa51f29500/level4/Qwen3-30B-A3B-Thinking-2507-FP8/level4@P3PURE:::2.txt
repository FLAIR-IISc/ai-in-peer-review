

Review: The paper introduces a novel method for training ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or other gradient-based models by leveraging an oracle that identifies the optimal ensemble member for each training instance. During training, backpropagation is applied exclusively to the network selected by the oracle based on the true label. This approach encourages specialization within the ensemble, enabling comprehensive coverage of the target output space. The authors report strong empirical results across multiple domains, demonstrating the method's effectiveness in classification tasks. The core idea of using an oracle to guide backpropagation toward the highest-performing network per instance is well-motivated and effectively enforces specialization. The presentation is clear, and the experimental evaluation is thorough, covering diverse datasets and tasks. Overall, this represents a strong contribution that deserves publication. However, the paper would be significantly strengthened if it addressed the practical utilization of the trained ensemble. While the training procedure is well-defined, the paper does not develop how the resulting ensemble is deployed in practice beyond noting it provides a diverse set of predictions and noting consistency with top-k metrics (e.g., ImageNet). This omission is critical for real-world applicability, as the value of the ensemble hinges on its post-training use. The current contribution is substantial, but this missing component represents a key gap for full practical adoption. Additionally, Figure 2 presents pseudo-code as raster images embedded in the paper; it would be preferable to include the pseudo-code directly in the text or as a vector graphic to ensure readability.
