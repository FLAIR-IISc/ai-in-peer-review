- Paper tackles training deep ensembles for Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and proposes sMCL, a scalable SGD-based "winner-take-gradient" algorithm that backpropagates loss only to the best-performing ensemble member per example.  
- sMCL interleaves data assignment and model updates, enabling concurrent training of ensemble members and demonstrated superior oracle performance on classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Strength: Addresses an important problem for ambiguous tasks and downstream/human-in-loop systems.  
- Strength: Method is simple, elegant, parameter-free, easy to implement, and agnostic to loss functions and architectures.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation is extensive and convincing across three diverse domains with modern architectures.  
- Weakness: Framing emphasizes “diversity” despite no explicit diversity term in the objective; diversity arises only as an emergent property of oracle loss minimization.  
- Weakness: Contribution may be seen as incremental—a stochastic/online adaptation of prior hard-EM MCL rather than a new theoretical formulation.  
- Minor: Abstract’s opening is too general; consider stating the core problem (intractability of training deep ensembles for oracle loss) up front.  
- Minor: Suggest toning down diversity claims and frame the work as an efficient algorithm for minimizing oracle loss that yields specialization/diversity.  
- Minor/question: Need clarification on how the baseline MCL deep implementation ensured per-learner convergence within meta-iterations, since premature stopping could bias comparisons in favor of sMCL.
