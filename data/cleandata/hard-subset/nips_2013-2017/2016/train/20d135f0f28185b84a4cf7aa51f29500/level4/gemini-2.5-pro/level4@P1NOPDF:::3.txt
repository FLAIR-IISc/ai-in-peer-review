This paper addresses the problem of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) in the context of deep neural networks. As conventional MCL solutions are not readily applicable to deep learning, the authors propose a simple and effective method. Their approach introduces a "winner-takes-the-gradient" mechanism during backpropagation, which allows for the joint training of an ensemble of learners and the simultaneous solution of the assignment problem. The method's effectiveness is demonstrated through a series of experiments on diverse tasks, including image classification, image segmentation, and image captioning.

**Strengths:**

*   The problem of adapting Multiple Choice Learning for deep networks is timely and of significant interest to the NIPS community.
*   The proposed method is appealing in its simplicity and elegance.
*   The experimental evaluation is thorough, demonstrating strong performance across three distinct and challenging tasks.

**Weaknesses:**

*   My primary concern is the significant disconnect between the paper's motivation and the proposed model. The paper, including its title, heavily emphasizes the concept of "diversity" among the learners. This framing leads the reader to expect an explicit mechanism or regularization term designed to promote diversity. However, the model does not contain such a component; diversity is hoped to emerge as a by-product of the learning process rather than being explicitly enforced. Given the strong emphasis in the text, the absence of a diversity-promoting term in the final model is surprising.
*   The proposed solution can be viewed as an incremental improvement over the relaxation technique proposed by Guzman-Rivera et al.

**Minor Suggestions:**

*   The first sentence of the abstract should be revised for clarity.
*   Given the point raised above, the claims regarding "diversity" should be toned down throughout the manuscript to more accurately reflect what the model actually does.
*   Line 108: There appears to be a typo in "we fixed the form of f...". Based on the context, the first "f" should be "g".
*   Line 115: There is an extra period in the middle of the sentence.

**Question for Authors:**

*   Regarding the baseline implementation of MCL with deep networks, could you clarify how you ensured that each individual network had converged to a reasonable solution? Terminating the training of individual learners prematurely could significantly impact the final ensemble performance, so the protocol for this baseline is important for a fair comparison.
