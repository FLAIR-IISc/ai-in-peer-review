- Summary: Proposes sMCL, a simple SGD-based "winner-take-gradient" training for ensembles that optimizes oracle loss to produce diverse high-quality predictions.

- Strength: Addresses a practical problem of producing multiple plausible hypotheses for ambiguous perception tasks by optimizing oracle loss.

- Strength: sMCL is novel, simple, parameter-free, and integrates easily into existing deep learning pipelines.

- Strength: Demonstrated strong, consistent oracle performance improvements across classification, segmentation, and captioning tasks and architectures.

- Weakness: Experimental clarity on initialization is lacking—unclear how crucial pre-trained initialization is versus training from random weights.

- Weakness: Fairness concern—need to state whether baseline methods used the same pre-trained initialization or identical starting conditions.

- Weakness: No quantitative diversity analysis—evaluation relies solely on oracle metrics without reporting pairwise diversity measures (e.g., Jaccard, BLEU/CIDEr distances).

- Weakness: Omission of a key related work (Lee et al., "Why M Heads are Better than One"); paper should compare and position sMCL relative to that method.

- Overall: Solid, well-written contribution making MCL practical for deep networks; primary issues (initialization clarity, diversity metrics, related work) should be addressed but do not negate acceptance.
