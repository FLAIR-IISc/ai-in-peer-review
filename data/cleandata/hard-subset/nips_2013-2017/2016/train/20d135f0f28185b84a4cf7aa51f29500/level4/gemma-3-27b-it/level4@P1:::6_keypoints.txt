- Introduces sMCL, a “winner-take-gradient” ensemble training method that updates only the lowest-loss member per example.  
- Contrasts with traditional ensembles that update all members; enables concurrent training for deep nets using standard SGD.  
- Well-motivated connection to Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and addresses MCL’s retraining/computational issues.  
- Strong, comprehensive experimental results across image classification, segmentation, and captioning showing consistent improvements.  
- Analysis shows member specialization qualitatively, providing insight into learned representations.  
- Testing/inference procedure is under-specified—seems to select best member using ground truth; needs clear, practical explanation.  
- The terms “independent” and “regular” ensembles (Indp.) are not precisely defined; baseline training details should be clarified.  
- Claim of true specialization is not fully justified given per-example reassignment; stronger evidence or mechanisms to encourage specialization are needed.  
- Missing discussion of diversity-regularization literature (e.g., Chen et al., 2018) and its relevance/limitations for deep ensembles.  
- Recommendation: weak accept — novel, practical, and effective but needs clarification and additional comparisons.
