- Introduces sMCL: a stochastic "winner-take-gradient" training algorithm that backpropagates only through the ensemble member with lowest loss per example, adapting MCL to SGD.  
- Makes MCL practical for deep networks, achieving about a 5x speedup over a limited MCL baseline.  
- Algorithm is simple, parameter-free, and architecture-/loss-agnostic, demonstrated on CNNs, FCNs, and CNN+RNNs.  
- Strong empirical results: outperforms independent ensembles and baselines on CIFAR-10, PASCAL VOC, and MSCOCO in oracle performance.  
- Observed emergent, interpretable specialization among ensemble members (e.g., class or caption structure specialization).  
- Paper is well-written, clearly motivated, and situates itself with prior work.  
- Novelty concern: adaptation from block coordinate descent to stochastic updates may be viewed as incremental, though practically significant.  
- Lacks quantitative diversity metrics for classification and segmentation (beyond n-gram counts for captioning).  
- Missing comparison to a more direct stochastic MCL baseline (e.g., updating data-to-model assignments per epoch/mini-batch).  
- Recommendation: overall positive—favorable for acceptance due to practical impact and strong empirical validation.
