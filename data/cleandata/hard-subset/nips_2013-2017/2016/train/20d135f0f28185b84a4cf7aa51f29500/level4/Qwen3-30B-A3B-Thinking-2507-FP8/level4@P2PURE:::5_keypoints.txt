- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize oracle loss to generate multiple outputs for user-interaction scenarios.  
- Oracle-based experiments on three tasks show the method is effective.  
- Integrates deep networks into the MCL framework and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent method to minimize oracle loss.  
- Optimization is compatible with back-propagation and enables end-to-end training of the model.  
- Major concern about initialization: paper does not address whether random initialization (vs. pre-trained) affects results.  
- Baseline comparison is unclear—paper doesn’t state if baselines use the same pre-trained network as the proposed SMCL initialization.  
- Evaluation relies only on oracle-based accuracy; analysis of ensemble member diversity is missing.  
- Should compare against closely related prior work (Lee et al., "Why M Heads are Better than One," 2015).
