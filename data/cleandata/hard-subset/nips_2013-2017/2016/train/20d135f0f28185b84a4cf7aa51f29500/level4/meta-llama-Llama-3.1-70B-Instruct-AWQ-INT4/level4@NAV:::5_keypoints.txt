- Proposes a novel method for generating multiple outputs via joint learning of an ensemble using oracle loss to enable user interaction.  
- Validated effectiveness on three tasks through experiments.  
- Integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent to minimize MCL oracle loss.  
- Optimization can be integrated into back-propagation for end-to-end training.  
- Concern about the importance of initialization: how critical is using a pre-trained network versus random initialization?  
- Missing details on comparison settings: do baseline methods use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL?  
- Evaluation relies mainly on oracle accuracy; diversity of ensemble members is not sufficiently analyzed.  
- Suggests comparison to related work: "Why M Heads are Better than One" (Lee et al., 2015).
