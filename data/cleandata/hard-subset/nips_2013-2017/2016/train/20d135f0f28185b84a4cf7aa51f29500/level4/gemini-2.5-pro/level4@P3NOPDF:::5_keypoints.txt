- Adapts Multiple Choice Learning to deep networks, proposing joint training that directly minimizes ensemble oracle loss to produce diverse outputs.  
- Proposes stochastic block gradient descent that integrates with backprop for end-to-end ensemble training.  
- Empirical results show improved oracle accuracy on three distinct tasks compared to baselines.  
- Novel and practical contribution for generating diverse predictions useful in user-interactive settings.  
- Optimization method is elegant, practical, and likely easy for others to implement.  
- Experimental results clearly demonstrate empirical gains.  
- Role and sensitivity of initialization is unclear; needs ablation comparing pre-trained vs. random initialization.  
- Baseline experimental details are insufficient—unclear if baselines used the same initialization, affecting fairness of comparisons.  
- Evaluation relies only on oracle accuracy and lacks direct diversity measures (e.g., disagreement rate, prediction distances, visualizations).  
- Omits discussion and empirical comparison to closely related work (Lee et al., 2015) that addresses similar goals.  
- Overall promising but weakened by unclear experimental setup, limited diversity evaluation, and missing related-work comparison.
