

This paper introduces a novel stochastic gradient descent approach for training ensemble models. The authors extend the framework of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], wherein the ensemble loss is defined as the loss of the single classifier producing the best output—thereby fostering classifier diversity. Their key contribution lies in adapting this approach via stochastic gradient descent, enabling its application to deep neural networks. The manuscript is well-motivated and clearly presented. My primary concern pertains to the novelty: whether the proposed algorithm constitutes merely an incremental refinement over [8]. While the authors persuasively argue that their minor modification significantly enhances practical utility, I remain cautious about the conceptual advancement. I recommend acceptance pending the authors addressing two points: (1) a comparative analysis of the MCL algorithm using mini-batches (instead of full datasets), and (2) a deeper investigation into the empirical impact of the claimed "diversity" effect.
