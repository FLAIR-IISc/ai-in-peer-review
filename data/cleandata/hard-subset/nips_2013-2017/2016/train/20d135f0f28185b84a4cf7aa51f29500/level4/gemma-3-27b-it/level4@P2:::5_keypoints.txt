- Addresses generating multiple plausible hypotheses; frames as Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and proposes Stochastic MCL (sMCL) to train ensembles minimizing oracle loss.  
- sMCL is parameter-free, architecture-agnostic, efficiently trainable, and integrates into standard backprop for end-to-end ensemble training.  
- Empirical results show sMCL outperforms existing methods on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Produces diverse, interpretable solutions that capture task ambiguity, supported by qualitative results.  
- Novel contribution: adapts MCL to deep networks without expensive retraining steps required by prior methods.  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Initialization impact is not thoroughly investigated; need experiments with random initialization versus pre-trained models.  
- Clarify whether baseline methods used the same pre-trained initialization to ensure fair comparisons.  
- Lacks quantitative evaluation of ensemble diversity; should include inter-model dissimilarity metrics beyond oracle accuracy.  
- Should analyze how ensemble size affects both performance and diversity.  
- Missing direct comparison and discussion of closely related work (Lee et al., 2015).  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel adaptation of MCL, elegant and efficient algorithm, strong empirical support, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited initialization analysis, no quantitative diversity metrics, missing related-work comparison, ambiguous experimental setup.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper—recommend accept contingent on addressing initialization effects, diversity quantification, and comparison to related work.
