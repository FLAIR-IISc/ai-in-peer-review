The authors propose a method for generating multiple outputs by jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize the oracle loss, which is particularly useful in interactive settings with users. The oracle experiments conducted on three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. This paper successfully integrates deep networks into the Multi-Class Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method to minimize the oracle loss of MCL. The proposed optimization method can be easily implemented within the back-propagation learning framework, allowing the whole model to be trained in an end-to-end fashion.

However, several concerns arise regarding the proposed method. Firstly, the importance of initialization is not thoroughly discussed. It is unclear how the method would perform if the models were initialized with random values instead of a pre-trained network. Furthermore, the authors do not provide sufficient details about the experimental settings, particularly the initialization methods used for the baseline approaches. Specifically, it is unclear whether the same pre-trained network was used to initialize the baseline methods as was used for the stochastic Multi-Class Learning (sMCL) approach.

The authors use oracle accuracy as the primary metric for evaluation, but do not provide a comprehensive analysis of the diversity of the ensemble members. A more in-depth examination of this aspect would be beneficial in understanding the strengths and limitations of the proposed method. Additionally, the authors may want to consider comparing their work with a similar idea presented in the paper "Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks" by Stefan Lee et al. (2015), as this could provide valuable insights and context for their research.
