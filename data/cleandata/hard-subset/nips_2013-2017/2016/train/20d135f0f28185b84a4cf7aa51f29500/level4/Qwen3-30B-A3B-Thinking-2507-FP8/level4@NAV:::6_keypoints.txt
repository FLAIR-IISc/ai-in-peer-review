- Introduces a novel ensemble training algorithm where only one member is active per training example and the member with lowest loss is updated.  
- Simple idea yields strong experimental results; reviewer recommends weak acceptance.  
- Testing procedure is unclear and seems to use ground-truth labels to pick the best member, which is not justified.  
- Baseline “independent/regular ensembles” (Indp.) is not defined or motivated.  
- Claim that members “specialize” to different classes is insufficiently supported given per-example reassignment; consider formalizing specialization in the loss.  
- Related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) is missing and underexplored.
