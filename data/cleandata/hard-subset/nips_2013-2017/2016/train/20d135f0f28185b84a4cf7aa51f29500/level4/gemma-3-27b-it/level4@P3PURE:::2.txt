The paper proposes a novel method for training an ensemble of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), or any gradient-based approach, by leveraging an oracle that identifies the optimal network within the ensemble for each training instance. Backpropagation is then applied solely to this selected network. This approach encourages specialization among the networks, leading to an ensemble capable of covering a wide range of possible outputs. The authors report strong performance across diverse domains. Overall, this is a well-written paper presenting an interesting idea – training an ensemble of DNNs with an oracle guiding backpropagation updates to the network producing the best output (as determined by the true label). The method appears to effectively enforce specialization, particularly in classification tasks. The presentation is excellent, and the experiments are comprehensive, spanning several domains. Globally, this is a strong contribution deserving publication. 

I would have considered this paper outstanding had it also addressed the exploitation of the resulting DNN ensemble. Currently, the focus is on oracle-guided training, but the practical application of the trained ensemble is not fully developed beyond noting its potential for providing a diverse set of results and its coherence with top-k metrics (e.g., for ImageNet). This is a significant omission for ensuring the approach’s usability in real-world scenarios. Nevertheless, the contribution remains substantial in its current form, with the missing component being relatively problem-specific. 

Finally, Fig. 2 presents pseudo-code as raster images embedded within the paper. It would be preferable to include the pseudo-code directly in the paper text, or at least embed the figure as a vectorial image for clarity and scalability.
