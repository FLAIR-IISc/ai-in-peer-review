- Proposes stochastic gradient descent (sMCL) algorithm for training ensemble models, extending Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL.
- Key contribution: enables application of MCL-style training to deep neural networks via SGD.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains MCL and its stochastic extension.
- Trains ensembles to minimize oracle loss (best single-member loss), producing diverse specialists.
- Diverse ensembles are useful for tasks with multiple reasonable interpretations.
- Demonstrates efficacy on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Strength: thorough evaluation and broad applicability across tasks.
- Strength: in-depth analysis of training dynamics and emergence of interpretable expertise among ensemble members.
- Weakness: algorithmic contribution may be incremental relative to prior work.
- Suggestion: compare MCL performance when using batches instead of full-dataset assignments.
- Suggestion: provide a deeper analysis of ensemble diversity and its effect on performance.
- Overall recommendation: strengths outweigh weaknesses; reviewer favors acceptance.
