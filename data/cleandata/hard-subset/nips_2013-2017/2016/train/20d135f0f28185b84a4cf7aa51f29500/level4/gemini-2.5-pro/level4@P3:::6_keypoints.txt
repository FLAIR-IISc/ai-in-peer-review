- Proposes sMCL: a simple "winner-take-gradient" SGD algorithm to train diverse deep ensembles by backpropagating only the lowest-loss member per example.  
- sMCL is a stochastic, parallelizable adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning that trains all ensemble members concurrently without full retraining.  
- Demonstrated across CIFAR-10 classification, PASCAL VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning using CNN, FCN, and CNN+RNN architectures.  
- Empirical results show sMCL consistently outperforms independent ensembles and other baselines on oracle performance metrics.  
- Method is parameter-free, easy to implement, and significantly more efficient/practical than prior MCL approaches.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation and compelling qualitative examples of diverse outputs.  
- Key claim: sMCL induces emergent specialization where ensemble members become experts on different data subsets or outputs.  
- Evaluation uses oracle metrics (best of M predictions vs. ground truth), which should be clarified as an analysis tool dependent on a downstream oracle or user.  
- The dynamics behind emergent specialization are not deeply analyzed; more explanation is needed on why per-instance competition yields stable specialization.  
- Related work discussion is incomplete—paper should compare to explicit diversity-promoting regularization and prior diversity methods.  
- The definition of the "Indp. ensembles" baseline (independent losses + different initializations) is buried and should be stated earlier.  
- Arguments for acceptance: simple, novel, practical, parameter-free method with strong empirical gains across tasks and architectures.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient analysis of specialization, incomplete related-work coverage, and minor clarity issues in evaluation and baseline description.  
- Overall recommendation: strengths substantially outweigh weaknesses; reviewer recommends acceptance.
