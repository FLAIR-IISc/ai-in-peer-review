1. Introduces an SGD method using multiple choice learning (sMCL) and claims benefits across classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
2. Novelty appears incremental—largely a neural-network implementation of the approach in [8] with minor modifications.  
3. Motivation is understandable but not a significant standalone contribution.  
4. Presentation is unclear and lacks detail; Section 3 is too brief and hinders comprehension.  
5. Does not clearly differentiate sMCL from existing multi-task training strategies or explain advantages over them.  
6. Experimental evaluation is weak: baselines are not strong and reported improvements are modest.  
7. Needs more rigorous comparisons to state-of-the-art, e.g., on standard benchmarks like PASCAL VOC 2012, for a fair assessment.
