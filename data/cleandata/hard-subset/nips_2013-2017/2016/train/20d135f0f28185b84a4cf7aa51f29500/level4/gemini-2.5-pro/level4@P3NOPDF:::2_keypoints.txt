- Proposes a novel training method where an oracle selects the best ensemble member per example and only that network receives the backprop update.  
- Encourages specialization and diversity among ensemble members, producing a pool of experts covering different data subsets/aspects.  
- Method is intuitive and original, particularly effective in classification settings.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation and motivation.  
- Strong empirical validation across multiple domains showing consistent performance gains.  
- Major weakness: no concrete strategy for inference—oracle requires ground truth and is unavailable at test time.  
- The brief mention of compatibility with top-k evaluation (e.g., ImageNet) is underdeveloped and insufficient.  
- Would be stronger if authors discussed or evaluated inference mechanisms (e.g., averaging, voting, selection heuristics) or framed them as future work.  
- Minor presentation issue: the pseudo-code (Figure 2) is a raster image and should be typeset or vectorized for clarity.  
- Overall assessment: solid, novel contribution with compelling results; recommend acceptance despite the inference-time gap.
