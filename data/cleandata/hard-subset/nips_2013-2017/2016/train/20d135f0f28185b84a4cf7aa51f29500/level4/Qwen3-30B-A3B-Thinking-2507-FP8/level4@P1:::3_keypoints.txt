- Proposes sMCL: an SGD method that backpropagates gradients only to the lowest-error predictor per example to minimize oracle loss for deep ensembles.  
- Avoids costly retraining of prior MCL methods, achieving ~5× speedup.  
- Parameter-free and architecture-agnostic, applicable to CNNs, FCNs, and CNN+RNNs.  
- Demonstrates state-of-the-art oracle performance across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Empirical gains of roughly 6–10% oracle accuracy over baselines (Fig. 5a, Table 1).  
- Shows emergent specialization among ensemble members without explicit diversity terms (supported by Figs. 4c, 5b, 7, 6).  
- Clear derivation (Eq. 2) and rigorous experimental validation; well-organized and readable exposition.  
- Novel contribution: first practical method to extend MCL to deep networks with a simple concurrent training scheme.  
- Significance: relevant to real-world perception systems that benefit from multiple hypotheses (autonomy, assistive tech).  
- Weakness: paper overstates "diversity" as a design goal—diversity is an emergent byproduct of oracle-loss minimization, not an explicit mechanism.  
- Weakness: comparison to prior MCL lacks important retraining/convergence details (e.g., number of meta-iterations, convergence criteria), which may affect fairness.  
- Minor clarity/grammar issues in the abstract and wording; recommend tempering "diversity" phrasing and rewording awkward sentences.  
- Recommendation: Accept — method is novel, effective, and broadly applicable; clarity and comparison details can be fixed in revision.
