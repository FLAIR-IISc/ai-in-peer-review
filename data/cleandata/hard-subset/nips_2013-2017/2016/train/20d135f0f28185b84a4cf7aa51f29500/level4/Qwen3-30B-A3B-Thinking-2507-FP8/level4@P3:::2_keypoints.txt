- Introduces sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based method that trains deep ensembles by backpropagating only to the lowest-loss "winning" model per example to produce diverse high-quality predictions.  
- Frames the problem as minimizing oracle loss (minimum loss over ensemble members) and extends Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL to practical deep learning.  
- sMCL avoids costly retraining of prior MCL methods and is applicable to any SGD-trained architecture; it's simple and parameter-free.  
- Technically sound with a rigorous formulation and well-motivated stochastic optimization approach.  
- Strong empirical results: 5–10% absolute gains across CIFAR-10 classification, Pascal VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning.  
- Demonstrates emergence of interpretable specialist behaviors among ensemble members.  
- Exceptionally clear writing and organization; figures effectively illustrate behaviors (algorithm pseudocode should be vector text for reproducibility).  
- Original contribution by making MCL practical for modern deep networks, differentiating from traditional ensemble/diversity and multiple-solution methods.  
- Significant practical relevance: addresses need for multiple hypotheses in perception systems and is easily adoptable.  
- Weakness: evaluation focuses on oracle metric and lacks exploration of real-world deployment scenarios or comparisons using multiple outputs in downstream tasks.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of trade-offs between ensemble size, performance, and concrete computational costs (wall-clock time, memory).  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, merits acceptance despite minor evaluation gaps.
