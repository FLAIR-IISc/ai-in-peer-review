This paper proposes an SGD-based training procedure for ensemble models built on the objective of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], where the ensemble loss is the loss of the best-performing single classifier for each example, which encourages diversity among the classifiers. The main contribution is to show that this objective can be optimized effectively via stochastic gradient descent, enabling application to deep neural networks. The paper is interesting and clearly written.

My primary concern is that the algorithmic novelty may be only incremental relative to [8]. However, the authors have convinced me that their small modification makes the approach much more practical and thus substantially more useful, so I am in favor of acceptance.

An additional comparison I would like to see is an evaluation of the MCL algorithm under mini-batch training (rather than using the entire dataset). I would also like to see more analysis of the resulting “diversity” effect.
