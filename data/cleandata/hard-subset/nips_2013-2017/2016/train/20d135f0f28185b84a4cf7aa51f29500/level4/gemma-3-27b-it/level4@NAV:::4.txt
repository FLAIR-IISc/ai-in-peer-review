The authors propose a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They assert the benefits of this framework across diverse tasks including classification, segmentation, and captioning. 

1. While the paper demonstrates the sMCL method, the novelty appears incremental. The motivation for employing MCL is readily understandable, however, this foundational concept doesn’t represent a core contribution of this work. The proposed approach largely mirrors a neural network implementation of [8] with some modifications.

2. The paper’s presentation could be improved. Critically, the technical details outlining the method (Section 3) are condensed to a single page. A clearer explanation of how the proposed framework differentiates itself from established multi-task training methodologies, which are already widely utilized, is needed.

3. The experimental evaluation spans several computer vision tasks – classification, image segmentation, and captioning – but lacks robust baseline comparisons. The observed performance gains are modest. To convincingly demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed framework, experimentation on the PASCAL VOC 2012 dataset, coupled with comparisons to stronger baselines, is highly recommended.
