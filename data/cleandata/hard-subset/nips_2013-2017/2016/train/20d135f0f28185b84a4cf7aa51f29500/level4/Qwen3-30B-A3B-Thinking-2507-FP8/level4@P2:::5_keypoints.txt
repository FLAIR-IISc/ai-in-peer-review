- Introduces sMCL: a stochastic SGD method for training deep ensembles that directly minimizes oracle loss by backpropagating only through the lowest-loss ensemble member per example.  
- sMCL enables concurrent training of all ensemble members without architectural or loss-function changes, making it efficient and scalable to modern deep nets.  
- Validated on CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO, sMCL consistently outperforms baselines (classical ensembles and MCL) by ~6–10% in oracle metrics.  
- sMCL is approximately 5× faster than prior MCL due to avoiding costly iterative retraining.  
- Method automatically induces interpretable specialization among ensemble members (e.g., class-focused classifiers, diverse segmentations/captions).  
- Technical contribution is sound: sMCL is rigorously derived from the oracle loss and well-motivated for real-world ambiguous prediction tasks.  
- Originality is high: first practical adaptation of MCL to deep networks via stochastic optimization, distinct from resampling, boosting, or explicit diversity losses.  
- Clarity is high: paper is well-written, with precise formulation, clear algorithm description, and informative visualizations.  
- Weakness: baseline initialization protocols are unclear—need to specify whether comparisons used identical pre-training and seeds.  
- Weakness: lack of quantitative diversity measures (e.g., pairwise KL or mutual information) to statistically support claims of diversity.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion and experimental comparison to Lee et al. (2015) and other negative-correlation/diversity-loss methods; should clarify distinctions and comparative performance.  
- Overall assessment: strong, high-quality contribution with minor, addressable revisions; recommendation to accept.
