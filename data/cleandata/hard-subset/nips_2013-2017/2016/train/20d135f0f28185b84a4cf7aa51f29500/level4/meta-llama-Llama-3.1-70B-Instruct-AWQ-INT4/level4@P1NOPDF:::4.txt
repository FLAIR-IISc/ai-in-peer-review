The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim that the proposed framework has advantages in multiple tasks such as classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, this paper demonstrates that the stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) method is somewhat incremental in novelty. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it cannot be considered a contribution of this work. Essentially, this work is a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments.

The paper could be improved in terms of writing quality. Notably, the technical section (Section 3) is only one page long, which raises questions about the clarity and thoroughness of the explanation. Specifically, the authors could more clearly elucidate the differences between the proposed framework and alternative training methods that have been widely adopted when training multiple tasks together.

The authors conduct experiments on various computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the experiments lack strong baselines, and the improvement in performance is relatively small. To convincingly demonstrate the efficiency of their framework, the authors should perform experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 to compare with more robust baselines.
