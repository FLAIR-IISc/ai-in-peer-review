- Proposes a novel ensemble training algorithm that activates and trains only the single member with lowest loss per example.
- Recommendation: weak accept based on simplicity and empirical effectiveness.
- Testing procedure is ambiguous—unclear how the active member is selected at inference and whether ground-truth labels are (improperly) used.
- Baseline definitions are missing—“independent ensembles” vs “regular ensembles” are not precisely defined for fair comparison.
- Claim that per-example reassignment yields sustained specialization is insufficiently justified by the presented evidence.
- Suggest measuring statistical significance of specialization and/or explicitly encouraging diversity (e.g., a loss term penalizing output overlap).
- Related work on diversity regularization (e.g., Chen et al.) is omitted and should be discussed and compared.
- Strength: the selective-activation scheme is novel and simple to implement.
- Strength: strong empirical results reported on tested datasets.
- Weakness: ambiguous testing procedure undermines reproducibility.
- Weakness: undefined baselines and missing related work weaken the paper’s contextualization and contribution.
