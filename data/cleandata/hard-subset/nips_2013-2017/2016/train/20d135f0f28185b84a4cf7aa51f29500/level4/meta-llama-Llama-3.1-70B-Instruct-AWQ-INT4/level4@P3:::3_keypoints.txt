- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based method enabling training of diverse deep ensembles by interleaving assignment with batch updates.  
- Claims sMCL overcomes costly retraining and sequential training limitations of existing methods.  
- Problem is well-motivated and important for the community.  
- Method is simple, easy to implement, and accessible.  
- Experimental results are comprehensive, covering image classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Paper provides clear explanation and thorough analysis, highlighting oracle performance gains and interpretability.  
- Emphasis on "diversity" is questionable because diversity is not explicitly enforced by the model.  
- Unclear whether ensemble member specialization arises from sMCL or experimental artifacts.  
- Contribution is somewhat incremental, building on Guzman et al.'s relaxation, requiring clearer discussion of novelty/significance.  
- Minor edits suggested: rephrase first abstract sentence, tone down diversity claims, and fix typographical errors (e.g., line 108 "f" → "g").  
- Unanswered question: how baseline MCL ensemble networks were ensured to converge and whether early cutting of learners affected performance.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and strong candidate for publication with scores — Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, simplicity, and strong experimental validation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unjustified emphasis on diversity, incremental novelty, and minor presentation issues.
