- Paper proposes sMCL: train ensembles by backpropagating each example only to the member with lowest loss ("winner-take-gradient") to directly optimize oracle loss.
- Strength — significance: Tackles practical need for multiple plausible hypotheses in ambiguous perception tasks; oracle loss is a sensible objective when a downstream chooser exists.
- Strength — simplicity/general: sMCL is elegant, architecture- and loss-agnostic, introduces no new hyperparameters, and is easy to implement.
- Strength — experiments: Validated across classification, segmentation, and captioning with diverse architectures; shows emergent specialization and improved oracle performance.
- Weakness — originality: Mainly an SGD-based adaptation of prior MCL [8]; conceptual novelty over [8] is limited.
- Weakness — technical clarity: Section 3 is brief; needs more discussion and comparisons to related optimization schemes (e.g., alternating training, hard-EM).
- Weakness — experimental rigor: Improvements over independent ensembles are sometimes modest and lack deeper analysis of when sMCL helps most.
- Weakness — baselines/datasets: Semantic segmentation evaluated on a PASCAL VOC 2011 subset instead of standard VOC2012 and lacks stronger contemporary diversity/ensemble baselines.
- Weakness — captioning comparison: Comparison to beam search is not directly comparable (inference vs. training); should test beam search on sMCL ensemble members to assess complementarity.
