The paper presents an ensemble training algorithm for deep learning models. Its novelty lies in activating only one learner within the ensemble for each training example, a departure from traditional deep and classical ensemble methods. During training, the member network with the lowest loss with respect to the true label is identified and updated using gradient descent. Despite its simplicity, the approach yields surprisingly strong experimental results and addresses limitations of existing techniques. Therefore, I recommend weak acceptance. (Continuing from the summary)

1. The testing phase lacks a clear explanation. I suspect the method utilizes the ground truth label to select the best-performing member, as suggested in line 159 of page 5.

2. The paper refers to ‘independent ensembles’ and ‘regular ensembles’ (denoted as ‘Indp.’ in the figures and line 181 on page 5). However, the definition of this baseline is not provided. 

3. The authors frequently highlight the `specialization` of ensemble members towards different output classes. However, the reassignment of members during training is performed on a per-example basis. I believe this argument for specialization is insufficiently justified. Would it be possible to incorporate a specialization term directly into the loss function?

4. Related to the above point, the authors do not discuss relevant work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.). While this technique offers a more rigorous approach, its application within deep learning remains an open area for investigation.
