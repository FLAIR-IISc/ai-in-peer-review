- Novelty & simplicity: proposes a "winner-takes-all" training rule that updates only the ensemble member with lowest loss per example to encourage diversity.  
- Strong empirical performance: reported results show the method outperforms several baselines across tested datasets.  
- Computational efficiency: training uses a single forward/backward pass per example to one member, reducing computational cost versus independent training.  
- Unclear inference/testing procedure: paper does not explain how the best member is selected at test time without ground-truth labels; if labels were used, reported results would be invalid.  
- Undefined baseline ("Indp."): the paper fails to clearly define how the independent/regular ensemble baseline was trained (separate training, bagging, etc.).  
- Weak justification of "specialization": per-example assignments may not yield stable class-level specialization; authors should show class-assignment distributions or add objectives to enforce specialization.  
- Missing related work: lacks discussion/comparison to explicit diversity-regularization methods (e.g., correlation or information-theoretic approaches such as Chen et al.).  
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on resolving methodological clarifications, substantiating specialization claims, and expanding related-work discussion.
