- Novelty is incremental: sMCL largely adapts the existing MCL framework to neural networks with only minor adjustments rather than offering a substantial new contribution.
- Paper clarity and technical exposition are poor: core technical details are compressed into one page and the method is not clearly distinguished from standard multi-task or alternating training strategies.
- Evaluation is unconvincing: experiments lack strong baselines, show marginal gains, and should include standard benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and comparisons to established methods.
