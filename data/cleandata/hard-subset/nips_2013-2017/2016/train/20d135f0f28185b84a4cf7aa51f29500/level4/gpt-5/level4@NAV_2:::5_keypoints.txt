- Introduces a method to jointly train an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss, extending Multiple Choice Learning to deep architectures with a stochastic block gradient descent strategy.  
- Approach is simple to implement within standard backprop, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and supports end-to-end training.  
- Empirical results on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning show strong oracle performance.  
- Concern: Sensitivity to initialization—what happens when models are trained from scratch versus from pretrained weights?  
- Concern: Comparison setup needs more detail—were all baselines initialized identically (e.g., same pretrained model) to ensure fairness with sMCL?  
- Concern: Evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy; additional metrics and analysis of diversity among ensemble members are needed.  
- Concern: Missing comparison to closely related prior work (Stefan Lee et al., “Why M Heads are Better than One,” 2015) and other relevant baselines.
