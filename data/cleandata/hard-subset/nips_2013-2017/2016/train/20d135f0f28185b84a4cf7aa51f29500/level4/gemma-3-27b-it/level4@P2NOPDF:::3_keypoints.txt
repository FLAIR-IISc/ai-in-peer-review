- Problem: Tackles multiple-choice learning (MCL) for deep neural networks where prior MCL methods do not readily generalize.  
- Method: Proposes a simple “winner-takes-the-gradient” layer during backprop to couple training of ensemble choices with assignment.  
- Evaluation: Validated on image classification, segmentation, and captioning, showing promising results across tasks.  
- Strengths: Relevant to the community, addresses an important problem, elegant/simple approach, and comprehensive experimental validation.  
- Main concern: Paper emphasizes “diversity” (including in the title) but offers no explicit mechanism to enforce it.  
- Missing evidence: Does not demonstrate or quantify that the method actually produces diverse ensemble solutions.  
- Novelty/related work: Contribution seems incremental versus prior work (e.g., Guzman et al.); needs clearer discussion of extensions/improvements.  
- Writing/focus: Abstract’s opening sentence is awkward; the paper should reduce its emphasis on “diversity” to match the method’s properties.  
- Technical errors: On line 108 the first “f” should be “g”; extraneous period in the middle of a sentence on line 115.  
- Experimental detail requested: Clarify how baseline MCL ensured convergence of individual networks and justify the chosen training schedule to avoid premature stopping.
