The paper introduces a novel ensemble training algorithm for deep learning models. Its key innovation lies in activating only a single member of the ensemble for each training instance, updating it based on its loss relative to the true label. This is a conceptually simple approach that yields strong empirical results and addresses limitations present in existing ensemble methodologies, leading to a recommendation for weak acceptance. 1. The description of the testing procedure lacks clarity. It appears the authors utilize ground truth labels to identify the best-performing member, as suggested in line 159 of page 5. 

2. The manuscript refers to “independent ensembles” and “regular ensembles” (denoted as ‘Indp.’ in figures and line 181 on page 5) without providing a clear definition or justification for this baseline comparison.

3. The authors emphasize the specialization of ensemble members towards specific output classes. However, given that member reassignment occurs on a per-example basis during training, the extent of this specialization is not convincingly demonstrated. Exploring the incorporation of specialization directly into the loss function may strengthen this claim.

4. Connected to the above point, the paper does not discuss relevant work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.). While more complex, this technique offers a more formalized approach to promoting diversity, and its potential within deep learning settings remains largely unexplored.
