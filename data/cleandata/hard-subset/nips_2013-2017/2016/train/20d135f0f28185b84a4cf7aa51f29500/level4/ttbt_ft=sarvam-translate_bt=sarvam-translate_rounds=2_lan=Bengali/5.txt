The authors have proposed a method for generating multiple outputs. In this method, an ensemble of deep networks jointly learns to reduce Oracle loss, which is useful for interacting with users. Three Oracle-related experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. This paper combines MCL examples with deep networks and proposes a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method for reducing Oracle loss in MCL. The proposed optimization method can be easily applied within a back-propagation learning framework, and the entire model can be trained end-to-end. My concern is how important initialization is? What if the models start with random values instead of pre-trained networks? The authors do not explain much about the comparison setting. Do the authors use the same pre-trained network as sMCL for initialization? The authors use Oracle accuracy as a metric for evaluation. What about the diversity of ensemble members? I hope to see more analysis on this matter. Also, there is a work with a similar concept that the authors have proposed for comparison. Stefan Li, Senthil Purusawalia, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, Y.M. Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks. CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015)
