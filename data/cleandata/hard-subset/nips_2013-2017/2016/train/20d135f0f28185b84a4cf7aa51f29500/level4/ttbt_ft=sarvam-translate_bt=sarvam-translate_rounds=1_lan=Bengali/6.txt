The paper provides an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners. It is unique because, unlike previous deep and classical ensembles, only one learner in the ensemble is active for each training example. During training, the member with the least loss relative to the true label is identified and updated with the gradient. Overall, it is a simple concept that yields surprisingly good experimental results and corrects some of the shortcomings of existing methods, so I recommend it for weak acceptability. (Redirected from summary) 1. The testing phase is not clearly explained; I suspect they use ground truth and find the best member described on line 159 of page 5. 2. In the figures and text, they refer to 'independent ensemble' and 'regular ensemble' (identified as 'Indp.' in figures and line 181 of page 5). I did not find any explanation for this baseline. 3. The authors repeatedly point to the 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes. However, the re-assigning in training is done on a per-example basis. I think this argument is not fair enough. Can you include specialization in the loss function? 4. Related to the above, the authors did not discuss the very related work of diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et.al.). This technique is more rigorous but has not yet been studied in deep learning settings.
