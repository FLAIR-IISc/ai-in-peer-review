- Problem relevance: Efficiently training diverse ensembles for multi-hypothesis deep learning is important and timely.  
- Simplicity: sMCL is a simple, parameter-free “winner-takes-the-gradient” SGD modification that’s easy to implement.  
- Broad applicability: Method is demonstrated across CNNs, FCNs, and CNN+RNNs for classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Strong empirical results: sMCL consistently outperforms classical ensembles and a baseline MCL implementation.  

- Limited diversity enforcement: The method does not explicitly optimize diversity; diversity emerges indirectly via oracle loss, so emphasis on “diversity” is misleading.  
- Incremental advance: Builds directly on Guzman-Rivera et al., mainly improving scalability rather than introducing a fundamentally new idea.  
- Baseline convergence concerns: Unclear how convergence was ensured for baseline MCL networks; premature stopping could bias comparisons.  

- Suggestion — abstract: Make the opening sentence more concise and impactful.  
- Suggestion — tone down diversity claims: Reduce emphasis on “diversity” to reflect that oracle loss drives variation.  
- Suggestion — fix typos: Correct identified typographical errors (e.g., line 108, line 115).  

- Question for authors: What criteria determined convergence for each baseline MCL network, and how was premature convergence mitigated?  

- Arguments for acceptance: Addresses a practical problem; method is simple, effective, and broadly applicable; compelling empirical improvements; well-written.  
- Arguments against acceptance: No explicit diversity objective, incremental contribution, and insufficient baseline implementation details.  

- Overall assessment: Valuable contribution with minor weaknesses; recommend acceptance pending minor revisions to clarify diversity claims and baseline details.
