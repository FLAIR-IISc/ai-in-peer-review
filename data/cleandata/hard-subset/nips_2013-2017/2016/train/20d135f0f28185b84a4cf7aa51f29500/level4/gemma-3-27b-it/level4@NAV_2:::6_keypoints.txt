- Proposes sMCL: frames ensemble learning as Multiple Choice Learning and trains with a stochastic SGD method.  
- Key update rule: for each training example, only the ensemble member with the lowest loss is updated.  
- Method is computationally efficient compared to prior MCL approaches.  
- Agnostic to network architecture and loss function and requires no additional hyperparameters.  
- Strong empirical improvements shown across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Outperforms baseline ensembles and related methods (MCL, Dey et al.).  
- Emergent behavior: ensemble members specialize, yielding diverse solutions that match task ambiguity.  
- Paper is well-motivated, conceptually simple, technically sound, and clearly written.  
- Qualitative examples effectively illustrate benefits of diversity and specialization.  
- Testing/inference procedure is not fully specified (how final predictions are generated).  
- Terms “independent ensembles” and “regular ensembles” (’Indp.’) are not clearly defined.  
- The causal link between per-example reassignment and true specialization is not fully established; explicit diversity regularization could be explored.  
- Missing discussion and comparison to related diversity-regularization work (e.g., Chen et al.).  
- Reviewer recommendation: weak accept, contingent on addressing the clarity and related-work concerns.
