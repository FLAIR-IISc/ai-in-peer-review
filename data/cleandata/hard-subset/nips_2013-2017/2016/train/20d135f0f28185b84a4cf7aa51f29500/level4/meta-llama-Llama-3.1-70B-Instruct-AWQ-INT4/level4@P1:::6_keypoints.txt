- Proposes sMCL, a simple, parameter-free SGD-based method for training diverse deep ensembles, agnostic to architecture and loss.  
- Demonstrates sMCL across tasks and architectures (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning).  
- Paper is well-motivated, intuitive, easy to implement, and experimentally effective.  
- Authors provide analysis of strengths/weaknesses and comparisons to existing baselines.  
- Testing procedure is unclear; it appears they use ground-truth to pick the best ensemble member but this must be explicitly stated.  
- Baselines labeled as "independent ensembles" and "regular ensembles (Indp.)" are not clearly explained and need brief definitions.  
- Claim that ensemble members specialize to different output classes is unconvincing given per-example reassignment; specialization should be formalized, e.g., in the loss.  
- Paper omits discussion of related work on diversity regularization and the trade-offs between those methods and sMCL.  
- Technically sound with theoretical support and experimental validation of claims.  
- Original contribution: updating only the lowest-loss ensemble member during training is novel compared to existing ensemble methods.  
- Significant potential impact due to simplicity, practicality, and broad applicability.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, strong experimental results, clear writing.  
- Arguments against acceptance: unclear testing protocol, poorly described baselines, weak justification for specialization, and missing diversity-regularization discussion.
