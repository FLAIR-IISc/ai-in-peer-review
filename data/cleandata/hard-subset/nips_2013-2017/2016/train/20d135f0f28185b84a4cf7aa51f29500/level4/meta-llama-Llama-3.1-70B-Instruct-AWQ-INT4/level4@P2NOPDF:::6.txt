The paper proposes an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners, distinct from previous deep and classical ensembles in that only one learner of the ensemble is active for each training example. During training, the member with the lowest loss with respect to the true label is identified and updated with the gradient. This simple yet effective idea yields surprisingly good experimental results and addresses some deficiencies of existing methods, leading me to recommend weak acceptance.

However, several aspects of the paper require clarification. Firstly, the testing phase is not clearly explained, and it appears that the authors use the ground truth to find the best member, as mentioned in line 159 on page 5. A more detailed explanation of the testing procedure would be beneficial.

Additionally, the authors refer to 'independent ensembles' and 'regular ensembles' in the figures and text (denoted as 'Indp.' in figures and line 181 on page 5), but fail to provide a clear explanation for this baseline. A brief description of these terms and their significance in the context of the proposed algorithm would be helpful.

The authors emphasize the 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes, but this argument is not sufficiently justified. Since the reassignment of examples to ensemble members occurs on a per-example basis during training, it is unclear how specialization emerges. One potential approach to address this issue would be to incorporate specialization into the loss function.

Furthermore, the authors do not discuss the related work on diversity regularization in neural networks, such as Chen et al. This technique is more rigorous and has been studied in other contexts, but its application to deep learning remains unexplored. A discussion of this related work would strengthen the paper and provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed algorithm's contributions.
