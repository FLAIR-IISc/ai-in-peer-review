The paper proposes a novel method for training an ensemble of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) by utilizing an oracle to guide the backpropagation update to the network with the best output, according to the true label. This approach enables the networks to specialize themselves, resulting in ensembles that effectively cover most possible outputs of interest. The authors demonstrate the efficacy of their method across various domains, achieving impressive performance.

The paper presents an interesting idea for training an ensemble of DNNs with an oracle, which enforces specialization in a classification context. The overall presentation is clear and well-organized, and the experiments are comprehensive, covering multiple domains. This is a strong work that deserves publication.

However, to make this paper truly outstanding, the authors should address the issue of exploiting the ensemble of DNNs trained with the proposed approach. Currently, the training is done with the oracle, but the use of the resulting ensemble is not developed further, beyond stating that it can provide a broad pool of possible results and that the approach is coherent with the top-k metric proposed for some problems (e.g., ImageNet). This is an essential missing part for making the proposed approach fully usable in practice.

Notwithstanding this limitation, the contribution is significant in its current form, and the missing part is relatively problem-specific. One minor suggestion is to replace the pseudo-code presented in Figure 2 with actual code within the paper or embed the pseudo-code figure as a vectorial image.

Strengths:
- Novel approach for training ensemble of DNNs using an oracle
- Effective in enforcing specialization among networks
- Comprehensive experiments across multiple domains
- Clear and well-organized presentation

Weaknesses:
- Lacks discussion on exploiting the trained ensemble
- Minor issue with pseudo-code presentation

Acceptance:
- Yes, with minor revisions
