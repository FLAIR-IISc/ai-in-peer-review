- Summary: Proposes a simple “winner-take-gradient” ensemble training where only the lowest-loss member receives the gradient for each example; shows strong empirical performance but needs clearer evaluation and specialization support.

- Strength: Conceptual simplicity — the per-example single-member update is elegant and easy to implement.

- Strength: Empirical performance — minimal change yields surprisingly strong results, suggesting practical value.

- Strength: Potential for specialization — the method plausibly encourages members to focus on different input/output regions.

- Issue: Test-time procedure unclear — paper ambiguously implies using ground-truth to pick members; must state exactly how predictions are combined and whether any label leakage occurred.

- Issue: Baselines undefined — must specify how “independent” and “regular” ensembles are trained, how predictions are combined, and whether compute/parameter budgets and tuning are matched.

- Issue: Specialization claims under-supported — need quantitative evidence such as per-member class distributions, confusion matrices, per-class gains, and diversity metrics.

- Suggestion: Encourage specialization via objective modifications — recommend diversity regularization, orthogonality or cosine penalties on final-layer weights, class-aware priors, load-balancing terms, or annealed soft selection.

- Issue: Related work incomplete — must discuss mixture-of-experts, hard/soft gating, and prior diversity-promoting ensemble methods and compare theoretically/empirically.

- Suggestion: Ablations needed — vary ensemble size K, hard vs soft selection, label-noise and class-imbalance sensitivity to understand behavior.

- Issue: Training dynamics/stability — investigate and report whether one member dominates early and what safeguards (exploration, smoothing, LR schedules) are used.

- Issue: Compute reporting missing — forward-pass cost may still require evaluating all members; report wall-clock, FLOPs, and compare to standard ensembles with matched budgets.

- Issue: Parameter sharing unclear — clarify whether members share parameters/features or are independent and how initialization diversity is controlled.

- Reproducibility: Provide pseudocode, full hyperparameters, data preprocessing, seeds, splits, early stopping, and code availability plans.

- Overall assessment: Neat, minimal-change method with potential impact; recommend weak accept conditional on clarifying evaluation, baselines, and providing stronger quantitative support for specialization and diversity.
