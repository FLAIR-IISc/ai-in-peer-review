

The paper introduces an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners, characterized by the unique property that only one ensemble member is active per training example, differing from prior deep and classical ensemble approaches. During training, the member yielding the lowest loss with respect to the true label is selected and updated via gradient descent. This simple yet effective approach achieves strong experimental results and addresses limitations of existing methods, warranting weak acceptance.  

1. The testing phase lacks clarity: the paper appears to use ground truth during inference to select the best-performing member (line 159, p. 5), but this procedure is not explicitly described.  
2. The baseline "independent ensembles" (denoted "Indp." in figures and line 181, p. 5) is referenced repeatedly without definition or justification. The relationship between this baseline and "regular ensembles" requires clarification.  
3. The authors emphasize "specialization" of ensemble members toward distinct output classes. However, the reassignment mechanism operates at the example level, not class level, making this claim insufficiently supported. Could specialization be explicitly incorporated into the loss function to strengthen this argument?  
4. The work overlooks related research on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), a more rigorous technique that has not been thoroughly explored in deep learning contexts. This omission weakens the contribution's novelty.
