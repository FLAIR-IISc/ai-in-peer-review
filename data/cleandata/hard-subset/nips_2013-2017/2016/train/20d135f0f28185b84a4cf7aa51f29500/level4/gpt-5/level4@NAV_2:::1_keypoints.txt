- Introduces an SGD procedure for training ensembles.
- Builds on Guzman-Rivera et al.; uses oracle loss (error of best-performing member per example) to encourage diversity.
- Main contribution: a winner-take-gradient SGD scheme that makes this approach practical for deep neural networks.
- Paper is clear and engaging.
- Main concern: algorithmic novelty may be incremental relative to [8].
- Authors convincingly argue the modification substantially improves usability; reviewer inclined to accept.
- Suggested additional experiment: compare against MCL run with mini-batches rather than full-dataset alternation.
- Requests deeper analysis of the induced “diversity” effects.
