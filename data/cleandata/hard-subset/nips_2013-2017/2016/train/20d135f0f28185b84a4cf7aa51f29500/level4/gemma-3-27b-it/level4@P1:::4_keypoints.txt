- Novelty is incremental: mainly a deep-network implementation of prior MCL [8] with efficiency tweaks rather than a fundamentally new method.
- sMCL motivation is clear but not presented as a core novel contribution.
- Writing needs improvement for clarity and conciseness, especially in technical exposition.
- Section 3 is concise but lacks detailed comparison to alternative multi-task training strategies.
- The paper does not adequately explain how sMCL differs from or improves on established multi-task learning methods.
- Experiments cover classification, segmentation, and captioning but use weak baselines and show only modest gains.
- Stronger evaluation is needed (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and comparisons to competitive state-of-the-art methods.
- Positive: addresses a relevant problem of generating diverse predictions where single outputs are insufficient.
- Positive: sMCL is computationally efficient, avoiding costly retraining required by earlier MCL methods.
- Positive: experiments show consistent improvements across tasks and architectures despite baseline weaknesses.
- Positive: analysis indicates ensembles learn interpretable specializations, offering insight into model behavior.
- Negative: core technical contribution (adapting MCL to SGD/deep nets) feels incremental and not fully distinguished from multi-task learning.
- Negative: insufficient experimental baselines make it hard to judge the practical significance of results.
