- Proposes sMCL: train ensembles by using an oracle to backpropagate only through the network with the best prediction, encouraging specialization.  
- Demonstrates strong performance gains across tasks (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning).  
- Novel idea: oracle-guided gradient updates for ensemble training.  
- Method directly optimizes oracle loss to produce diverse, specialized ensemble members.  
- Broad applicability: architecture- and loss-agnostic approach.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, covering diverse architectures and datasets.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation and theoretical grounding.  
- Recommendation: strong contribution that merits acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Weakness: limited discussion on how to utilize the trained ensemble diversity during inference and downstream tasks.  
- Suggestion: add more exploration of inference strategies and practical applications of ensemble outputs (e.g., using top-k predictions).  
- Minor presentation issue: pseudocode in Figure 2 is rasterized, reducing readability and scalability.  
- Suggestion: embed pseudocode in text or provide as a vector graphic to improve clarity.
