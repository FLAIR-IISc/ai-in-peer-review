- Proposes an SGD training procedure for ensemble models using the min-over-members (MCL) loss to make MCL practical for deep networks.
- Paper is clearly written and the idea is intuitive.

Strengths:
- Practical contribution: recasting MCL for SGD makes the approach scalable and applicable to modern deep architectures.
- Clarity and simplicity: algorithmic description is easy to follow and straightforward to implement.
- Potential impact: enables diverse, specialized ensembles for deep models.

Weaknesses:
- Incremental novelty: core objective and diversity mechanism are inherited from Guzman‑Rivera et al. [8]; novelty is mainly an optimization/implementation advance.
- Limited analysis of diversity: lacks deep empirical and quantitative investigation of how and why diversity emerges and its relation to performance.
- Missing baseline: no comparison to the original MCL implemented with mini-batches or a straightforward stochastic approximation.

Requests for clarification / suggested experiments:
- Subgradients: clarify how subgradients for the non‑smooth min-over-members loss are chosen (ties) and whether stochastic updates are unbiased/consistent.
- Assignment granularity: specify whether example-to-member assignments are per mini-batch or use memory across batches/epochs and sensitivity to batch size and ensemble size.
- Stability and optimization: report convergence behavior, assignment variance over training, and any stabilization techniques (soft-min, entropy regularization, delayed specialization).
- Diversity metrics: provide quantitative measures (pairwise disagreement, error correlation, KL divergence, negative correlation) and relate them to accuracy.
- Ablations: test random vs min-loss assignment; independent training vs min-loss coupling; soft-min (log-sum-exp) vs hard min; various batch sizes and learning-rate schedules.
- Comparisons: compare to other diversity/specialization methods (mixture-of-experts with gating, negative correlation learning, bagging/boosting variants, snapshot ensembles).
- Computational cost: report wall-clock time, memory, and FLOPs relative to training K independent models and to [8], and quantify any savings from avoiding global assignments.
- Test-time strategy: clarify inference rule (argmax across members, learned gating, averaging) and discuss any train/inference mismatch and its impact.

Overall evaluation:
- SGD formulation substantially improves practicality and scalability for applying MCL to deep networks.
- Well written and clean idea; recommend acceptance contingent on stronger empirical diversity analysis, minibatch MCL comparison, and additional ablations.
