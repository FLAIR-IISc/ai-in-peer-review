The authors propose a stochastic gradient descent method for Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) and claim advantages across classification, segmentation, and captioning. However:

1) The novelty of sMCL appears incremental. While the motivation for MCL is clear, it is not unique to this work and thus cannot be considered its main contribution. The method is essentially a neural network instantiation of [8] with some minor adjustments.

2) The paper is not well written. The core technical section (Section 3) spans only a single page. It is unclear how the proposed framework differs from alternative training strategies that are widely used when multiple tasks are trained jointly.

3) Although the authors evaluate on several computer vision tasks (classification, image segmentation, and captioning), the experiments lack strong baselines and the performance gains are small. To more convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the framework, the authors should include experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against stronger baselines.
