The authors propose a method for generating multiple outputs by jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize the oracle loss, which is particularly useful in interactive settings. Experiments on three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. This paper integrates deep networks into the Multi-Class Label (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method to minimize the oracle loss of MCL. Notably, the proposed optimization method can be easily implemented within the back-propagation learning framework, allowing the entire model to be trained end-to-end.

However, I have concerns regarding the importance of initialization in the proposed method. Specifically, how would the performance be affected if the models were initialized with random values instead of a pre-trained network? The authors do not provide sufficient explanation about the experimental settings, particularly the initialization procedure used for comparison with baseline methods. It is unclear whether the same pre-trained network was used for initialization in both the proposed method and the baseline methods.

Furthermore, the authors use oracle accuracy as the primary evaluation metric, but I expect to see a more in-depth analysis of the diversity among ensemble members. Additionally, there is a prior work with a similar idea that the authors should consider comparing their approach to: Stefan Lee, Senthil Purushwalkam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, "Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks" (CoRR abs/1511.06314, 2015).
