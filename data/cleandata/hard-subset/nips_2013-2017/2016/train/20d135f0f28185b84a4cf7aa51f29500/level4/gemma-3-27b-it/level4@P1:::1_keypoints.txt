- Proposes sMCL, an SGD-based algorithm to train diverse ensembles of deep networks building on the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework.  
- Main contribution: adapts MCL to SGD, making the approach practical for modern deep neural networks by avoiding costly retraining.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and technical description of sMCL.  
- Experimental validation is extensive, showing improvements across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Method is general: applicable to any model trained with SGD and agnostic to network architecture and loss function.  
- Algorithm is parameter-free and easy to implement.  
- Provides insightful analysis showing emergent specialization/expertise among ensemble members.  
- Initial concern: algorithmic novelty is incremental relative to prior MCL work.  
- Authors convincingly argue that the practical impact (scalability to deep models) outweighs the incremental novelty.  
- Suggested improvement: compare to original MCL trained with mini-batches to better isolate benefits of SGD adaptation.  
- Suggested improvement: quantify diversity of ensemble outputs with metrics beyond oracle performance.  
- Recommendation: accept — technically sound, practically valuable, and supported by compelling experiments.
