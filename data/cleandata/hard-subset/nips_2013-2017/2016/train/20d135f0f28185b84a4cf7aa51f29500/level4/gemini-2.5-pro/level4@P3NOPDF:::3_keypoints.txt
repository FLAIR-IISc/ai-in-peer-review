- Problem relevance: Adapting Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep neural networks is timely and important for ensemble training in deep learning.  
- Method simplicity: The "winner-takes-the-gradient" backpropagation scheme is simple, intuitive, and integrates learner selection into end-to-end training.  
- Empirical validation: Strong experiments show effectiveness across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, supporting generality.  
- Motivation-method disconnect: Paper emphasizes "diversity" but offers no explicit loss/constraint to enforce diversity, so framing may be misleading.  
- Incremental contribution: Method may be a straightforward application of Guzman-Rivera et al.'s relaxation for latent variable models to deep ensembles; novelty should be clarified.  
- Question for authors: Clarify baseline MCL training protocol—how were individual learners trained and how was convergence determined before assignment?  
- Minor suggestion: Revise the abstract's opening sentence for clarity and impact.  
- Minor suggestion: Tone down emphasis on "diversity" in title/introduction to match technical content.
