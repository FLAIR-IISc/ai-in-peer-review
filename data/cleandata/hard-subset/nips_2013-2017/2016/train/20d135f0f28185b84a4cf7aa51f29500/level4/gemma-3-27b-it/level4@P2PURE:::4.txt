The authors propose a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim advantages for this framework across diverse tasks including classification, segmentation, and captioning. 1. The presented sMCL method exhibits only incremental novelty. While the motivation for MCL is clear, it does not represent a substantial contribution of this work, which essentially implements a neural network version of [8] with some modifications. 2. The paper suffers from a lack of clarity in its presentation. The technical details of the method (Section 3) are compressed into a single page. Furthermore, the paper fails to clearly differentiate the proposed framework from alternative multi-task training approaches that are already well-established. 3. The authors evaluate their method on a range of computer vision tasks—classification, image segmentation, and captioning—however, the experimental evaluation lacks strong baselines and demonstrates only marginal performance improvements. To effectively demonstrate the efficacy of the framework, the authors should include experiments on the PASCAL VOC 2012 dataset and compare against more competitive baselines.
