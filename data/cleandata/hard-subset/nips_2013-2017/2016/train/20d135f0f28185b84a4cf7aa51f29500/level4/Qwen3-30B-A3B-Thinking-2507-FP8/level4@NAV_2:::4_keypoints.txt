- Introduces sMCL: training diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss with a stochastic SGD approach, applied to classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Technically sound but incremental; primarily an adaptation of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8] to deep nets with minimal theoretical novelty.
- Core contribution is extending MCL with stochastic optimization rather than proposing a substantially new methodology.
- Section 3 is too brief and lacks sufficient explanation of how sMCL differs from standard ensemble training.
- The paper does not adequately contrast sMCL with existing techniques for jointly training multiple models.
- Poor organization and vague writing reduce clarity and make technical contributions hard to assess.
- Authors fail to clearly articulate distinctions between sMCL and parallel training of multiple networks on the same data.
- Originality is limited: the approach mainly applies existing MCL to deep networks and does not sufficiently differentiate from prior work [8] or other ensemble methods.
- Reported performance gains are marginal (6–10% relative) and may not justify the claimed significance.
- Experiments lack strong, recent baselines (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012 for segmentation), weakening support for the claim that sMCL "significantly outperforms" baselines.
