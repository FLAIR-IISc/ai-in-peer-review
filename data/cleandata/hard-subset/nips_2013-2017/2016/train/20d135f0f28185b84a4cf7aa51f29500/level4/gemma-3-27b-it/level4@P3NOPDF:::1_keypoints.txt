- Introduces an SGD-based algorithm for training ensembles that minimizes the loss of the best ensemble output, extending Guzman-Rivera et al. [8].
- Main technical contribution: adapts the best-output ensemble loss to efficient SGD, enabling application to deep neural networks.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of the algorithm and theoretical basis.
- Motivation is convincing: original method impractical for large datasets and deep models, motivating an SGD adaptation.
- Experimental results show competitive or superior performance versus existing ensemble methods on tested datasets.
- Primary concern: novelty is incremental relative to Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], since the core idea (maximizing the minimum output) remains unchanged.
- Authors convincingly argue the practical impact of enabling scalability to large, complex models, supporting acceptance.
- Suggested comparison: evaluate against the original MCL algorithm using mini-batch SGD to more directly assess benefits of the SGD adaptation.
- Suggested analysis: quantify classifier diversity (e.g., disagreement or feature coverage) and correlate diversity metrics with ensemble performance.
- Suggested investigation: analyze sensitivity to hyperparameters controlling diversity.
