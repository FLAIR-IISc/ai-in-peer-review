- Introduces a simple "winner-takes-the-gradient" layer to extend multiple choice learning (MCL) to deep networks, enabling joint training and assignment resolution.  
- Validated across image classification, segmentation, and captioning with strong empirical performance.  
- Strength: addresses a relevant MCL gap for the NIPS community.  
- Strength: method simplicity is a clear advantage.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments on three distinct tasks.  
- Weakness: paper heavily emphasizes "diversity" but does not explicitly enforce or model it, contradicting title/motivation.  
- Weakness: incremental advance over Guzmán et al.’s MCL relaxation.  
- Minor: rewrite the abstract's first sentence for clarity.  
- Minor: reduce emphasis on "diversity" throughout the manuscript.
