- Paper proposes training ensembles by activating/updating only the single member with lowest loss per example; shows strong empirical results and reviewer recommends weak acceptance.

- Testing procedure is unclear; reviewer suspects the method may use ground-truth labels to select the best member (possible test-time data leakage).

- The baseline called “independent ensembles” / “regular ensembles” (Indp.) is not defined in the paper and must be clarified.

- The claim that ensemble members “specialize” to different classes is weakly justified because member reassignment is done per-example; consider adding an explicit specialization term to the loss.

- The paper omits discussion of prior work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), which is relevant and should be addressed.
