The paper provides an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners. It is unique because, unlike previous deep and classic ensembles, only one learner is active for each training instance in the ensemble. During training, the member with the least loss compared to the actual label is identified and updated with the gradient. Overall, it is a simple concept that yields surprisingly good experimental results and corrects some of the shortcomings of existing methods, so I recommend it for weak acceptability. In the figure and text, they refer to 'independent ensemble' and 'regular ensemble' (identified as 'Indp.' in figure and line 181 on page 5). I did not find any explanation for this baseline. 3. The authors repeatedly point to the 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes. However, the re-assigning in training is done on a per-instance basis. I think this argument is not fair enough. Can you include specialization in the loss function? 4. Related to the above, the authors did not discuss the very related task of diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et.al.). This technique is more rigorous but has not yet been studied in deep learning settings.
