This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models. The authors are based on the work of Guzman-Rivera et al [8], where the disadvantage of ensemble models is the loss of the best output of a single classifier in the ensemble, resulting in a diverse set of classifiers. Their contribution is to suggest using a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training, which enables the use of this algorithm for deep neural networks. The paper is interesting and clearly written. My biggest concern is whether the paper's algorithm novelty is only incremental compared to [8]. Since the authors assured me that the small change they added makes this algorithm even more useful, I am in favor of acceptance. I believe an interesting comparison would be to examine the MCL algorithm when using batches (instead of the entire dataset). I would like to see more analysis of the "diversity" effect.
