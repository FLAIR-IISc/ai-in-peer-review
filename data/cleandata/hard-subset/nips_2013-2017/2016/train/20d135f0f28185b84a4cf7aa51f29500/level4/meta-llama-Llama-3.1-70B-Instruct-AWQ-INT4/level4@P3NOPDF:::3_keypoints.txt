- Proposes a simple MCL solution for deep networks using a winner-takes-the-gradient layer to combine training and assignment.  
- Current MCL methods don’t extend to deep networks, making this contribution valuable.  
- Experiments show strong, versatile performance across image classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Strengths: problem is important/relevant, method is simple and easy to implement, and evaluation covers three tasks.  
- Major weakness: paper heavily motivates "diversity" but does not explicitly enforce a diversity term, which is misleading.  
- Incremental contribution: approach is a relatively small step beyond the relaxation by Guzman et al.  
- Minor suggestions: rewrite the abstract’s first sentence, tone down emphasis on “diversity,” and fix typos (line 108 'f'→'g'; extra period in line 115).  
- Open question/concern: unclear how baseline MCL networks were ensured to converge and whether early cutting of learners harmed ensemble performance.
