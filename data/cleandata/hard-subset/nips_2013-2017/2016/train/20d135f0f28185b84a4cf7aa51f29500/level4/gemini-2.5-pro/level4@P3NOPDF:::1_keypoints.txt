- Introduces an SGD-based adaptation of Minimum Classification Loss (MCL) for training ensembles, enabling scalability to deep networks and large datasets.  
- Paper is clear and well-written, with contributions articulated relative to prior work.  
- Practically significant: converting MCL to SGD makes the method applicable to modern deep learning architectures.  
- Initial concern about incremental novelty over Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], but the authors argue the SGD adaptation is non-trivial and practically important.  
- Experimental analysis is promising but could be strengthened for more conclusive evidence.  
- Recommend comparing to a naive mini-batch implementation of the original MCL to isolate benefits of the stochastic formulation.  
- Recommend more direct, quantitative diversity analyses (e.g., disagreement rate, Q-statistic) and visualizations showing ensemble member specialization.  
- Overall positive recommendation for acceptance, with encouragement to add the suggested experiments and analyses.
