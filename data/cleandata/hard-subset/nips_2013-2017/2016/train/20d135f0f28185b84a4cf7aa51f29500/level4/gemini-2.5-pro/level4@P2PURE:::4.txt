This paper proposes a stochastic gradient descent method based on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and applies it to several tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, the work has several weaknesses:

1.  The novelty of the proposed sMCL method appears to be incremental. While the motivation for MCL is clear, it is not a contribution of this work. The proposed method is largely a neural network adaptation of the framework presented in [8], with some minor adjustments.

2.  The paper is not written clearly. The core technical details in Section 3 are allocated only one page, which is insufficient for a thorough explanation. Furthermore, the paper fails to adequately differentiate the proposed framework from standard multi-task learning strategies like alternating training.

3.  While the authors conduct experiments on multiple computer vision tasks, the evaluation is not convincing. The experiments lack strong baselines, and the reported performance gains are marginal. To convincingly demonstrate the framework's effectiveness, the authors should perform experiments on a standard benchmark like PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against stronger, more established methods.
