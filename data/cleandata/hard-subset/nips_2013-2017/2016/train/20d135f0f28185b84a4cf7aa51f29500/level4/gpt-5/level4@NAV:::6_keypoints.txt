- Proposes a simple ensemble training method where only one ensemble member is active per training example (the member with smallest loss), yielding strong empirical results.  
- Novelty: single-member activation per example contrasts with prior deep and classical ensemble methods.  
- Reviewer recommends weak acceptance based on empirical performance and mitigation of some shortcomings.  
- Test-time procedure is unclear; reviewer suspects the authors may be using ground-truth labels to select the best member (possible data leakage).  
- Baseline labeled “independent ensembles” / “regular ensembles (Indp.)” is referenced in figures/text but not described.  
- Claims of ensemble-member “specialization” toward different classes are not sufficiently substantiated, since training reassignments occur per example.  
- Suggests exploring whether specialization can be enforced via the loss and notes missing discussion of related diversity-regularization work (e.g., Chen et al.).
