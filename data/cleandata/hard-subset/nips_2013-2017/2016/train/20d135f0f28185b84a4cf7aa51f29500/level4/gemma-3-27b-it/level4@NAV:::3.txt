This paper addresses the multiple choice learning (MCL) problem, specifically within the context of deep network learners – a setting where existing MCL solutions do not readily apply. The authors present a straightforward approach leveraging a “winner-takes-the-gradient” layer during backpropagation to jointly optimize learner training and the ensemble assignment problem. The method is evaluated across diverse tasks including image classification, segmentation, and captioning, demonstrating strong performance. 

The work’s strengths lie in the significance of the MCL problem for deep learning within the NIPS community, the elegance and simplicity of the proposed method, and the comprehensive experimental validation across three distinct tasks. 

However, a key concern is the paper's emphasis on "diversity" – reflected even in the title – without explicitly enforcing diversity within the model itself. The lack of a concrete diversity term, despite its strong motivational role, is a notable limitation. Furthermore, while effective, the proposed solution represents an incremental advancement building upon existing relaxation techniques, such as those presented by Guzman et al.

Minor points for improvement include rewriting the opening sentence of the abstract for clarity, moderating the focus on diversity throughout the paper, correcting a typographical error on line 108 ("f" should be "g"), and removing an extraneous period on line 115. 

Finally, I have a question regarding the deep learning baseline for MCL: How was convergence ensured for each network within the ensemble before truncating the learning process? Early termination could potentially impact overall ensemble performance.
