The authors propose a method for generating multiple outputs by jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize the oracle loss, which is useful in interactive settings. Oracle experiments on three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The paper integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent procedure to minimize the MCL oracle loss. The optimization can be implemented easily within the standard back-propagation framework, allowing end-to-end training.

My main concerns are:
- How important is initialization? What happens if the models are initialized randomly rather than from a pre-trained network?
- The comparison setup is under-specified. Did the authors use the same pre-trained network to initialize the baselines as they did for sMCL?
- The evaluation relies on oracle accuracy. What about the diversity of the ensemble members? I would like to see more analysis on this point.

In addition, the authors should compare against closely related work with a very similar idea: Stefan Lee, Senthil Purushwalkam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks. CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015).
