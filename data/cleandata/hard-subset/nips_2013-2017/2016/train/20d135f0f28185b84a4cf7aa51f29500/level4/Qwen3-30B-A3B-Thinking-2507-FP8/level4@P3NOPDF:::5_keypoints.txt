- Core contribution: sMCL integrates deep networks into the MCL framework and proposes a stochastic block gradient descent to minimize oracle loss, enabling end-to-end training with standard backprop.  
- Empirical result: Experiments on three tasks show consistent improvements in oracle accuracy over baselines.  
- Concern 1: The method relies on pre-trained initialization but the paper omits experiments with random initialization and sensitivity analysis.  
- Concern 2: Baseline initialization is unclear — it’s not stated whether baselines used the same pre-trained starts, risking unfair comparisons.  
- Concern 3: Evaluation focuses only on oracle accuracy and omits ensemble diversity metrics and their correlation with performance.  
- Missing related work: The paper overlooks and must compare to Lee et al. (2015) "Why M Heads Are Better than One," which addresses similar multi-head/diverse-ensemble training.  
- Strength: Practical, efficiently implementable end-to-end training and solid empirical oracle gains.  
- Overall recommendation: Minor revision pending experiments/clarifications.  
- Requested revisions: (1) add random-initialization sensitivity experiments, (2) clarify/ensure fair baseline initialization, (3) include diversity metrics and analysis, (4) compare and contrast with Lee et al. (2015).
