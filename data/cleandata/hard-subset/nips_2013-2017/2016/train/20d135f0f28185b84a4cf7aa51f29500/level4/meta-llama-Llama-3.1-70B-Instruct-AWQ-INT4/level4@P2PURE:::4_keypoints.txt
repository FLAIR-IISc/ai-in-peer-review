- Claims: paper proposes stochastic MCL (sMCL) and asserts advantages for classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Novelty concern: sMCL appears to be an incremental extension of existing ideas rather than a significant contribution.
- Originality issue: method seems like a neural-network version of prior work [8] with minor adjustments.
- Presentation problem: writing is unengaging and Section 3 (technical description) is overly brief (one page).
- Missing comparison: paper does not clearly explain how the framework differs from common alternative multi-task training methods.
- Experimental weakness: experiments on classification, segmentation, and captioning use weak baselines and show only small improvements.
- Suggested fix: authors should evaluate on stronger benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and compare to more robust baselines.
