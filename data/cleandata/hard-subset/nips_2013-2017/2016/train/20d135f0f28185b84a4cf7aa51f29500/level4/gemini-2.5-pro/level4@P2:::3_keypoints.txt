- Paper tackles Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for ensembles, aiming to minimize oracle loss for ambiguous tasks.  
- Proposes sMCL: compute forward passes for all members but backpropagate loss only through the member with lowest error ("winner-take-gradient").  
- sMCL enables concurrent training of ensemble members in one SGD run, avoiding costly iterative retraining.  
- Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic, simple to implement, and requires no extra hyperparameters.  
- Strong empirical results: consistent oracle performance gains on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Significance: makes MCL practical for modern deep networks and for tasks where multiple plausible outputs are valuable.  
- Main weakness: framing emphasizes "diversity" despite no explicit diversity term; diversity is emergent, not an objective.  
- Conceptual novelty is limited: sMCL is a stochastic/online adaptation of prior block coordinate descent MCL methods (Guzman-Rivera et al.).  
- Reviewer requests clarification on implementation details of the Guzman-Rivera MCL baseline, specifically convergence criteria used across meta-iterations.  
- Minor suggestions: tone down diversity-centric claims in title/abstract, simplify the abstract's first sentence, and fix typos (line 108 mapping typo, extra period at line 115).
