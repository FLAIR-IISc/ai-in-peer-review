Summary:
The paper proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize the oracle loss, enabling the model to generate multiple outputs that can be useful in interactive settings. The authors integrate deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduce a stochastic block gradient descent optimization procedure to minimize the MCL oracle loss. The approach is straightforward to implement within standard back-propagation and supports end-to-end training. Oracle-based experiments on three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the method.

Strengths:
- Clear integration of deep networks with the MCL framework.
- A simple stochastic block gradient descent optimization that fits naturally into back-propagation.
- End-to-end training with empirical evidence across three tasks showing strong oracle performance.

Concerns and questions:
- Initialization: How critical is initialization to the final performance? What happens if models are initialized randomly rather than from a pre-trained network?
- Comparative setup: The experimental setup for comparisons is not sufficiently described. Do the baseline methods use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL? Please clarify to ensure the comparisons are fair.
- Evaluation metric: Results are reported primarily in terms of oracle accuracy. What about the diversity among ensemble members? More analysis of diversity is needed to understand whether improved oracle accuracy stems from genuinely diverse hypotheses or from other factors.

Related work:
- There is closely related work that should be compared against or discussed: Stefan Lee, Senthil Purushwalkam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, “Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks,” CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015). The paper appears to pursue a very similar idea; a direct comparison and discussion would strengthen the contribution.

Suggestions for improvement:
- Provide an ablation on initialization (pre-trained vs. random), and analyze its impact on convergence and final oracle performance.
- Clarify the comparative experimental setup, especially the initialization protocol for all methods, to ensure fairness.
- Include explicit diversity analyses of the ensemble members alongside oracle accuracy.
