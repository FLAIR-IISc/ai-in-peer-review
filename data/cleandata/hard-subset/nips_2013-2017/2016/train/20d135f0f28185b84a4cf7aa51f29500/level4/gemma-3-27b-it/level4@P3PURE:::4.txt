The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim advantages for the proposed framework across multiple tasks including classification, segmentation, and captioning. 

1. This paper presents the sMCL method, which represents an incremental addition to existing work. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it cannot be considered a novel contribution of this work. The presented approach is essentially a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments.

2. The paper is not well-written, and the technical description (Section 3) is surprisingly concise, occupying only one page. The distinction between the proposed framework and alternative multi-task training strategies – which are widely adopted – remains unclear. 

3. The authors conduct experiments on a variety of computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, these experiments lack strong baselines, and the observed performance improvements are marginal. To convincingly demonstrate the efficacy of the framework, the authors should perform experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against more competitive baselines.
