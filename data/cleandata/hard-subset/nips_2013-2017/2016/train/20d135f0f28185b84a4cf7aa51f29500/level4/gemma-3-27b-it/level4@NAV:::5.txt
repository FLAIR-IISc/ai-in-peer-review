The authors present a novel approach for generating diverse outputs by simultaneously training an ensemble of deep networks, guided by the minimization of oracle loss – a technique particularly beneficial in interactive systems. The method’s efficacy is demonstrated through oracle experiments across three distinct tasks. This work effectively bridges deep learning with the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm, introducing a stochastic block gradient descent optimization strategy specifically designed to minimize MCL’s oracle loss. A key strength lies in the optimization method’s compatibility with standard back-propagation and the resulting end-to-end trainability of the entire model. 

However, the sensitivity of the method to initialization remains a concern. The impact of initializing models with random weights, rather than pre-trained networks, deserves further investigation. Additionally, the paper lacks sufficient detail regarding the experimental setup and comparison baselines. Specifically, it is unclear whether baseline methods utilize the same pre-trained network as sMCL for initialization. While oracle accuracy is reported, a crucial aspect missing is an analysis of the diversity *between* the ensemble members. Further investigation into ensemble diversity is needed. 

Finally, the authors should consider and compare their approach to related work, particularly “Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks” (Lee et al., CoRR 2015), which presents a highly similar concept.
