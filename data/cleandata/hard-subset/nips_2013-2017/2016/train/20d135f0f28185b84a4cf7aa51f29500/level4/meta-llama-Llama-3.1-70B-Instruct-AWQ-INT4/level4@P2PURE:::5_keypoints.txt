- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs, aimed at interactive settings.
- Experiments on three tasks demonstrate the proposed approach’s effectiveness.
- Integrates deep networks into the Multi-Class Label (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent method to minimize MCL oracle loss.
- Optimization method is easily implemented within backprop, enabling end-to-end training.
- Major concern about initialization: unclear how performance would change with random initialization vs pre-trained models.
- Experimental settings lack detail, specifically whether the same pre-trained initialization was used for the proposed method and baselines.
- Evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy but lacks a thorough analysis of diversity among ensemble members.
- Missing comparison to relevant prior work: Lee et al., "Why M Heads are Better than One" (2015), which the authors should consider.
