This paper formulates a stochastic gradient descent variant for Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) and claims advantages across classification, segmentation, and captioning. My main concerns are:

1) Novelty and contribution: The proposed sMCL method appears incremental in novelty. While the motivation for MCL is clear and well known, it cannot be counted as a key contribution of this work. The method reads largely as a neural network instantiation of [8] with minor adjustments, rather than a substantive conceptual advance.

2) Clarity and technical depth: The paper is not well written, and the technical core (Section 3) is only about one page, which is insufficient to support the claims. It is unclear how the proposed framework differs from standard multi-task training practices (e.g., alternative/alternating training or other commonly used strategies when multiple tasks are trained jointly). The paper should explicitly articulate the algorithmic differences, assumptions, and expected benefits relative to these widely adopted baselines, and provide sufficient derivations and implementation details.

3) Empirical evaluation: Although the authors present experiments on several computer vision tasks (classification, image segmentation, and captioning), the baselines are not strong and the observed gains are modest. To convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the framework, the authors should compare against stronger, commonly accepted baselines. In particular, an evaluation on PASCAL VOC 2012 for segmentation, with competitive baselines, would significantly strengthen the empirical case.
