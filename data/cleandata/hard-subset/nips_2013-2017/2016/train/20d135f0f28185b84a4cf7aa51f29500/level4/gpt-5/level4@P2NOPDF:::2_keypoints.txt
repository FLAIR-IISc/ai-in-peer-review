- Introduces oracle-guided training where only the best ensemble member per instance is updated to encourage specialization.  
- Core idea is simple, well-motivated, and effective for enforcing specialization in classification.  
- Presentation is clear and empirical results across multiple domains are strong, often beating baselines.  
- Method is practical to implement and broadly applicable to gradient-based learners beyond DNNs.  
- Main gap: no concrete strategies for inference-time use of the trained ensemble without oracle access.  
- Needs evaluation/discussion of how to select a single model at test time (e.g., learned gating, confidence-based routing, meta-classifiers).  
- Should address how to combine outputs when top-1 accuracy is required (ensembling, voting, calibration).  
- Should analyze trade-offs between top-1 and top-k performance and how specialization affects each.  
- Requires assessment of robustness and calibration of specialized members under distribution shift.  
- Minor presentation issue: Figure 2 pseudo-code is rasterized and should be rendered as text or a vector graphic.  
- Overall: strong paper with solid validation and recommended acceptance; would be outstanding with principled inference-time strategies.
