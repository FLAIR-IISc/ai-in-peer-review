- Introduces sMCL: training ensembles by backpropagating gradients only to the lowest-loss member per example ("winner-take-gradient") to directly optimize oracle loss.  
- sMCL integrates selection into SGD, avoiding costly alternating retraining used in prior MCL methods.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on image classification (CIFAR-10), semantic segmentation (PASCAL VOC), and image captioning (MSCOCO) using appropriate deep architectures.  
- Novel, simple, and practical method that encourages automatic specialization and diverse high-quality predictions across ensemble members.  
- Technically sound with extensive experiments showing consistent, significant improvements over baselines and prior work (including independently trained ensembles and Dey et al.).  
- Analysis and visualizations (e.g., emergent class-specialization) provide compelling evidence for the method’s intended behavior.  
- Well-written, clearly motivated, and the algorithm is presented simply and accessibly.  
- Broad applicability: architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free, and easy to integrate into existing pipelines.  
- Main weakness: limited discussion or solutions for inference-time selection when an oracle is not available (re-ranking/combining outputs).  
- Minor issue: pseudo-code in Figure 2 is rasterized; should be typeset or embedded as vector for clarity.  
- Overall conclusion: strong contribution, suited for NIPS, likely to inspire follow-up work; reviewer recommends acceptance.
