

The paper introduces a novel training paradigm for ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or other gradient-based models, leveraging an instance-specific oracle to identify the optimal ensemble member for each training sample. Backpropagation is applied exclusively to this selected network, enabling effective specialization across the ensemble and ensuring comprehensive coverage of the target output space. Results demonstrate strong performance across diverse domains, with clear presentation and thorough experimental validation. The method presents a significant contribution to ensemble learning, particularly in classification contexts where specialization is critical. While the core training approach is well-motivated and robust, the paper would be substantially strengthened by addressing practical ensemble deployment—specifically, how the trained ensemble is utilized beyond theoretical alignment with top-k metrics (e.g., ImageNet). Currently, the focus remains solely on training efficacy, leaving deployment considerations unexplored. The contribution remains valuable in its current form, though this gap limits immediate practical applicability. Additionally, Figure 2 (pseudocode) is provided as a raster image; embedding the pseudocode directly in the text or using a vector format would improve reproducibility. Overall, this is a strong work deserving publication, with minor revisions to address deployment and figure formatting.
