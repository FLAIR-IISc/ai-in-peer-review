- Paper proposes a simple solution for multiple-choice learning (MCL) with deep networks by adding a winner‑takes‑the‑gradient layer to backpropagation to jointly optimize learners and assignments.  
- Authors demonstrate good empirical performance across image classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Strength: MCL for deep learning is a relevant problem for the community.  
- Strength: The proposed method is admirably simple.  
- Strength: Experiments cover three different tasks, showing breadth.  
- Weakness: Title and text emphasize “diversity,” but the method does not explicitly enforce a diversity term.  
- Weakness: Contribution appears incremental relative to the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.  
- Minor: Rewrite the first sentence of the abstract.  
- Minor: Tone down the emphasis on diversity.  
- Minor: Line 108 typo — first “f” should be “g.”  
- Minor: Line 115 contains an extra period in the middle of a sentence.  
- Question: How did you ensure each network in the deep‑learning MCL baseline converged to a reasonable solution, since prematurely stopping some learners could affect ensemble performance?
