1) Incremental novelty — sMCL appears to be a neural-network adaptation of prior Multiple Choice Learning ([8]) with only minor adjustments and limited original contribution.

2) Clarity and technical depth — paper is poorly written, the core method is described too briefly, and it fails to distinguish the framework from standard alternating training strategies.

3) Weak experimental evaluation — experiments use weak baselines and show marginal gains; needs rigorous comparisons to stronger state-of-the-art methods and standard benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012 for segmentation).
