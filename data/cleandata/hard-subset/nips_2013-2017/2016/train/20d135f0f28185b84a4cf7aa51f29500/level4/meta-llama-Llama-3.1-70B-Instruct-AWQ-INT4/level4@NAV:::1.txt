This paper introduces a novel stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models, expanding on the work of Guzman-Rivera et al [8]. The authors' approach leverages the loss of the ensemble model as the loss of the best output from a single classifier within the ensemble, resulting in a diverse set of classifiers. Their key contribution lies in proposing the use of stochastic gradient descent for training, thereby enabling the application of this algorithm to deep neural networks. The paper is well-organized and clearly articulated. My primary concern revolves around the algorithmic novelty of the paper, questioning whether it merely builds incrementally upon the work of [8]. However, the authors successfully demonstrate that their modification significantly enhances the algorithm's utility, leading me to support its acceptance. A potential direction for future exploration would be to investigate the performance of the MCL algorithm when utilizing batches rather than the entire dataset. Furthermore, a more in-depth analysis of the "diversity" effect would provide valuable insights.
