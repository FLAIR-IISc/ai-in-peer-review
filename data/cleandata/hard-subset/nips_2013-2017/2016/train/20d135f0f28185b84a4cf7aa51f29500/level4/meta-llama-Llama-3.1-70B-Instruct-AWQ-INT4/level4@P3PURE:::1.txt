This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models, building upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al [8]. The authors leverage the concept of using the loss of the best output of a single classifier in the ensemble as the loss of the ensemble model, yielding a diverse set of classifiers. Their primary contribution lies in the suggestion to employ a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training, thereby enabling the application of this algorithm to deep neural networks. The paper is well-written and engaging.

My major concern is whether the algorithmic novelty presented in this paper is merely incremental to that of [8]. However, the authors successfully argue that the small modification they introduce significantly enhances the algorithm's utility, which inclines me towards acceptance. An intriguing comparison would be to investigate the performance of the MCL algorithm when using batches instead of the entire dataset. Furthermore, I would appreciate a more in-depth analysis regarding the "diversity" effect, as it would provide a deeper understanding of the algorithm's behavior.
