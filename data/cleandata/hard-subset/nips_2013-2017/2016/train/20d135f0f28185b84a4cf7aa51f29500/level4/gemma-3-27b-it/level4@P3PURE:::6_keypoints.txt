- Proposed method: train ensembles by updating only the member with lowest loss per training example; simple, effective, and yields strong empirical results — reviewer recommends weak acceptance.

- Testing procedure is unclear; it appears the ground-truth label may be used to select the best member (line 159), so a detailed description of test-time selection is needed.

- The terms “independent ensembles” / “regular ensembles” (Indp.) are used without clear definitions or justification; baselines and comparison purpose must be clarified.

- The claim that ensemble members specialize to different classes is not convincingly justified because member assignment is per-example; consider adding a specialization term to the loss to encourage/measure specialization.

- The paper omits discussion of diversity-regularization literature (e.g., Chen et al.), which provides more rigorous methods to promote ensemble diversity and should be considered.
