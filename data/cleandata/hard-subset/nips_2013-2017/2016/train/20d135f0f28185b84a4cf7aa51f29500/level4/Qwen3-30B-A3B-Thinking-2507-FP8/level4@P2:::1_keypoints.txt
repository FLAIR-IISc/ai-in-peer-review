- Introduces sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based method for training diverse deep ensembles that minimizes oracle loss directly.  
- Builds on Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL but addresses MCL’s impractical requirement of repeated full retraining for deep networks.  
- sMCL forwards gradients only to the lowest-error predictor per example during backprop, enabling concurrent training of ensemble members.  
- Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic and applicable to any model trained with SGD.  
- Strong empirical gains across CIFAR-10 classification, Pascal VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning (6–10% relative oracle improvements).  
- sMCL is about 5× faster than classic MCL in experiments.  
- Demonstrates automatic, interpretable specialization of ensemble members (class- or style-specific experts).  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and includes accessible algorithm descriptions and illustrative figures.  
- Experimental design is robust, with comprehensive ablations and comparisons to relevant baselines (classical ensembles, MCL, boosting-like methods).  
- Technical claims are well-supported; method is simple, generalizable, and parameter-free.  
- Originality is incremental: adapts MCL practically to deep learning rather than introducing new theory.  
- Significant practical impact for systems needing multiple hypotheses (e.g., human-in-the-loop, verification pipelines).  
- Weakness: need stronger emphasis and conceptual differentiation from Guzman-Rivera et al. [8].  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis/metrics quantifying diversity beyond oracle performance.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to an MCL variant using batched updates rather than full retraining.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggested revisions to deepen diversity analysis and strengthen comparisons to batch-based MCL.
