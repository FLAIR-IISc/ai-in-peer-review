This paper proposes an ensemble training algorithm for deep learning models, distinct from existing approaches in that it activates only one learner per training example. The algorithm identifies the learner with the lowest loss relative to the true label and updates it using the gradient during training. Despite its simplicity, this approach yields surprisingly good experimental results, addressing some limitations of previous methods, and thus warrants a weak acceptance recommendation.

However, several aspects require clarification: 

1. The testing phase lacks clear explanation, suggesting the use of ground truth to identify the best member, as mentioned in line 159 on page 5.

2. The paper refers to 'independent ensembles' and 'regular ensembles' (denoted as 'Indp.' in figures and line 181 on page 5), but the explanation for this baseline is not provided.

3. The authors emphasize the 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes, but since reassignment occurs on a per-example basis during training, this argument seems unjustified. A potential improvement could be incorporating specialization into the loss function.

4. The authors have overlooked discussing the related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), which, although more rigorous, remains unexplored in the deep learning context.
