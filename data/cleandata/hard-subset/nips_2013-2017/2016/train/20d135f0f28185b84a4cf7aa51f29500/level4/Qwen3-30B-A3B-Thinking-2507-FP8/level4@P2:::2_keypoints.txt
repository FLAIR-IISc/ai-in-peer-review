- Introduces sMCL: trains diverse deep ensembles by backpropagating only to the ensemble member with lowest error per example, encouraging specialization.  
- sMCL operates within standard SGD, removing the iterative retraining requirement of prior MCL methods and enabling efficient training of deep networks.  
- Demonstrated effectiveness across image classification (CIFAR-10), semantic segmentation (Pascal VOC), and image captioning (MSCOCO).  
- Empirical gains: consistent oracle performance improvements over baselines (6–10%) and ~5x faster than prior MCL.  
- Strong theoretical grounding in stochastic block gradient descent and technically sound methodology.  
- Comprehensive experimental design and thorough analysis of ensemble specialization and interpretability.  
- Clear, well-written presentation with effective figures illustrating specialization.  
- Originality: first practical method enabling efficient diverse ensemble training for deep architectures; architecture- and loss-agnostic.  
- Significance: addresses practical need for multiple hypotheses in perception systems with potential applications in autonomy and assistive tech.  
- Primary weakness: limited discussion of how to use ensembles at inference time or implement the oracle in real-world systems.  
- Minor issue: Algorithm 2 presented as a raster image, hindering copyability and reproducibility.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, practical approach with strong empirical validation and clear contribution to ensemble learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: inadequate treatment of practical deployment/inference considerations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix algorithm presentation and add a brief section on practical deployment/use cases.
