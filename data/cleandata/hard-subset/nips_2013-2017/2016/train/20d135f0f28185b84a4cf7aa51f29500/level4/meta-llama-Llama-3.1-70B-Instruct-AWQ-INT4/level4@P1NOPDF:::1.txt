This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models, building upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al [8]. The ensemble model's loss is defined as the loss of the best output of a single classifier in the ensemble, yielding a diverse set of classifiers. The authors' primary contribution is the suggestion to use a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training, which enables the application of this algorithm to deep neural networks. The paper is well-written and interesting to read.

My main concern is whether the algorithmic novelty presented in this paper is merely incremental to that of [8]. However, the authors have convincingly demonstrated that the small changes they introduce significantly enhance the algorithm's usefulness, which inclines me to support its acceptance. An intriguing comparison would be to investigate the performance of the MCL algorithm when using batches rather than the entire dataset. I would appreciate more in-depth analysis regarding the "diversity" effect, as this aspect is crucial to understanding the algorithm's behavior and advantages.
