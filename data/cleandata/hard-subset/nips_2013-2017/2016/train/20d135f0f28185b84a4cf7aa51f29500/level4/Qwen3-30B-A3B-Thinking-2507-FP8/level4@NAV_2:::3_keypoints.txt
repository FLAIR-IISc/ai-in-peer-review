- Addresses Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep ensembles to produce multiple diverse hypotheses rather than a single prediction.  
- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL): SGD-based concurrent training that backpropagates only to the lowest-loss predictor per example.  
- sMCL avoids costly retraining from prior MCL methods and is agnostic to architecture and loss function.  
- Evaluated on CIFAR10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO, showing consistent oracle performance gains (6–10%) over baselines.  
- Demonstrates automatic, interpretable specialization among ensemble members without explicit diversity constraints.  
- Strength: tackles a significant practical problem and offers a simple, broadly applicable method.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation across three domains with clear oracle improvements.  
- Strength: insightful analysis of emergent class-specific specialization in ensembles.  
- Weakness: paper overemphasizes “diversity” despite diversity arising implicitly, not via explicit mechanisms.  
- Weakness: incremental contribution over prior MCL work (mainly removes retraining) with limited theoretical novelty.  
- Weakness: minor clarity/typo issues in abstract and specific lines (e.g., line 108, line 115).  
- Recommendation: weak acceptance/borderline—valuable engineering contribution but not sufficiently transformative; revise framing and clarify baseline MCL implementation details.
