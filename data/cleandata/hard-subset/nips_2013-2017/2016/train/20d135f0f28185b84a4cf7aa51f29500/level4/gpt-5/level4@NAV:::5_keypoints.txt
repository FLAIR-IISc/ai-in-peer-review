- Method: jointly train an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss and produce multiple outputs.
- Application: beneficial for interactive settings where multiple candidate outputs are useful.
- Empirical result: oracle-based experiments on three tasks demonstrate effectiveness.
- Contribution: brings deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces stochastic block gradient descent (sMCL) to minimize MCL oracle loss.
- Implementation: sMCL is straightforward to implement within standard back-propagation and supports end-to-end training.
- Concern: sensitivity to initialization—how does performance change with random vs. pretrained initialization?
- Experimental detail missing: unclear whether baseline methods used the same pretrained initialization as sMCL.
- Evaluation gap: focus is on oracle accuracy without sufficient analysis of diversity among ensemble members.
- Request: more analysis on ensemble diversity is needed.
- Missing comparison: should compare to closely related work (Lee et al., 2015, "Why M Heads are Better than One").
