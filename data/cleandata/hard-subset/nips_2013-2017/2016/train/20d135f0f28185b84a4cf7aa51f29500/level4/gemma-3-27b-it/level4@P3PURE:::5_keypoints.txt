- Proposes joint learning of an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss, useful for interactive settings.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness via oracle experiments on three tasks.  
- Integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm.  
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent (sMCL) method to minimize MCL oracle loss.  
- sMCL is easy to implement within standard back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training.  
- Major concern: method appears sensitive to initialization; authors do not test random initialization vs. pre-trained networks.  
- Experimental comparisons lack detail—unclear if baselines use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL.  
- Evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy but does not quantify or analyze ensemble member diversity.  
- Suggests comparing to related work on training diverse ensembles (e.g., Lee et al., “Why M Heads are Better than One,” 2015).
