The paper introduces a method for training an ensemble of deep neural networks (and more generally, any gradient-based models) using an oracle that, for each training instance, identifies the ensemble member that is optimal for that instance. Backpropagation is then applied only to the selected network. This encourages the ensemble members to specialize, yielding a collection that collectively covers most outputs of interest. The authors report results across multiple domains, showing strong overall performance.

I find this to be a strong paper. It clearly presents an interesting idea: training an ensemble with an oracle that directs gradient updates to the member whose output is best with respect to the true label. In a classification setting, the approach appears to enforce specialization effectively. The presentation is solid, and the experiments span several domains. Overall, this is a strong submission that merits publication.

I would consider the paper outstanding if it also addressed how to exploit the resulting ensemble at inference time. As it stands, training relies on the oracle, but the paper does not develop how to use the trained ensemble beyond noting that it provides a broad pool of candidate outputs and aligns with top-k metrics used in some settings (e.g., ImageNet). This omission is important for making the approach fully practical. That said, the core contribution is already valuable, and the missing component is likely problem-specific.

A minor presentation issue: Figure 2 shows pseudo-code as a rasterized image. Please typeset the pseudo-code directly in the paper or, at a minimum, include it as a vector graphic.
