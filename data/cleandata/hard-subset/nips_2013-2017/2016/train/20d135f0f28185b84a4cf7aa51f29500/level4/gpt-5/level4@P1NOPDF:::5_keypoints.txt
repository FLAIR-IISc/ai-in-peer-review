- Proposal: Jointly train an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss using an MCL framework with a stochastic block gradient descent optimizer.  
- Implementation: Method is straightforward to implement with standard backprop and supports end-to-end training.  
- Empirical evidence: Oracle-based experiments on three tasks show encouraging effectiveness.  
- Sensitivity to initialization: Reviewer asks for quantification of how critical initialization is, especially random vs. pre-trained starts.  
- Fairness of comparisons: Baseline initialization, fine-tuning schedules, and augmentations must be matched and clearly reported to avoid confounded comparisons.  
- Evaluation beyond oracle accuracy: Request analyses of ensemble diversity (pairwise disagreement, error overlap, trade-offs with per-model accuracy) to explain oracle gains.  
- Related work missing: Must discuss and compare to prior MCL/diversity work, notably Lee et al. (2015), both conceptually and empirically.  
- Overall: Simple/practical approach but empirical rigor requires addressing initialization sensitivity, fair baselines, and diversity analysis.
