- Introduces sMCL: a winner-take-gradient method that minimizes oracle loss to train diverse deep ensembles.  
- sMCL generalizes Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL to deep networks by using SGD, avoiding costly alternating retraining.  
- Method is simple, parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and easy to implement (single-line change).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains across classification, segmentation, and captioning tasks.  
- Achieves ~5× faster training than prior MCL and 6–10% relative oracle performance improvements over baselines.  
- sMCL induces emergent, interpretable specialization of ensemble members without manual design.  
- Clarify testing protocol: unclear how the oracle selects the lowest-error prediction at inference (simulated using ground truth or practical selection?).  
- Baseline ambiguity: "independent ensembles" poorly defined—need to specify training procedure and relation to bagging/standard ensembles.  
- Specialization claim needs justification: training assigns examples to lowest-loss members (example-level), not explicitly per-class specialization.  
- Omission of related work: ignores recent diversity-regularization methods for deep nets (e.g., Chen et al., ICML 2015) and should discuss tradeoffs.  
- Overall assessment: significant, practical contribution with broad applicability and solid empirical validation.  
- Pro: simple, efficient, yields interpretable specialization and substantial oracle accuracy gains across tasks.  
- Con: ambiguous testing protocol, insufficient theoretical/empirical support for specialization claim, and missing discussion of diversity regularization literature.  
- Recommendation: weak accept (7/10) contingent on clarifying testing, baseline definitions, specialization evidence, and adding related-work discussion; consider exploring combination with diversity regularization.
