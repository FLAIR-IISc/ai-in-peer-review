This paper proposes a stochastic gradient descent method for Multiple Choice Learning (MCL), which is an extension of the original MCL framework to deep neural networks. The authors claim that their approach, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free. They demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL for training diverse deep ensembles on various computer vision tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.

The main idea of sMCL is to interleave the assignment step with batch updates in stochastic gradient descent, allowing for concurrent training of ensemble members. This approach is in contrast to the original MCL framework, which requires retraining models multiple times. The authors provide a clear explanation of the technical details of sMCL and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on several benchmark datasets.

One of the strengths of this paper is its ability to provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed framework and its relation to existing work in the field. The authors also provide a thorough analysis of the results, including an examination of the specialization of ensemble members and the emergence of interpretable expertise.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. Firstly, the technical section of the paper is relatively short, and some readers may find it difficult to fully understand the proposed framework without additional details. Secondly, the experiments could be strengthened by including more robust baselines and evaluating the performance of sMCL on more challenging datasets, such as PASCAL VOC 2012.

In terms of novelty, while the authors build upon existing work in the field, their contribution is primarily in extending the MCL framework to deep neural networks. The motivation behind MCL is clear, but it is not a novel contribution of this work.

Overall, this paper provides a solid contribution to the field of deep learning and ensemble methods. With some revisions to address the areas mentioned above, it has the potential to be a strong paper.

Arguments For Acceptance:
