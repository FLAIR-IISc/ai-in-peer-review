The paper proposes a novel method for training an ensemble of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or other gradient-based approaches by utilizing an oracle that identifies the optimal network for each training instance. The oracle selectively applies backpropagation to the chosen network, allowing the ensemble members to specialize in specific themes and cover a wide range of possible outputs of interest. The authors report impressive results across various domains. Overall, this is a strong paper that effectively presents an intriguing idea for training DNN ensembles with an oracle-guided backpropagation approach. This method appears to enforce specialization effectively in classification contexts. The presentation is clear, and the experiments are conducted across multiple domains. However, to elevate this paper to outstanding status, I would have liked to see a more comprehensive discussion on exploiting the trained DNN ensembles. While the proposed approach leverages an oracle during training, it does not elaborate on utilizing the resulting ensemble beyond mentioning its potential to provide a broad pool of possible results and its coherence with the top-k metric used in certain problems, such as ImageNet. Addressing this aspect is crucial for making the proposed approach fully practical. Nevertheless, the contribution is significant in its current form, with the missing component being relatively problem-specific. Furthermore, Figure 2 presents pseudo-code as a raster image embedded in the paper. It would be beneficial to include the pseudo-code directly in the paper or, at the very least, embed it as a vector image for improved readability.
