The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim that their proposed framework offers advantages in multiple tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning.

However, this paper exhibits incremental novelty, and the sMCL method builds upon existing ideas. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it cannot be considered a novel contribution of this work. Instead, this work can be viewed as a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments.

Furthermore, the paper requires improvement in its writing and technical presentation. The technical section (Section 3) is only one page long, which is insufficient to provide a comprehensive explanation of the proposed framework. A more detailed comparison with alternative training methods, which are widely adopted when training multiple tasks together, is necessary to demonstrate the unique aspects of the proposed framework.

The authors conduct experiments on various computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the experiments lack strong baselines, and the performance improvements are relatively small. To convincingly demonstrate the efficiency of their framework, the authors should perform experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare their results with stronger baselines. This would provide a more comprehensive evaluation of their method and help establish its advantages over existing approaches.
