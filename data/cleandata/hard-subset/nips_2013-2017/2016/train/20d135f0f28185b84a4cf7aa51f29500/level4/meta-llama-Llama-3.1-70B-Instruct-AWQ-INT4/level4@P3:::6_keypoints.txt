- Proposes a novel ensemble training algorithm where only the lowest-loss member is active and updated per training example.  
- Training-by-updating-a-single-member yields surprisingly strong experimental performance and addresses deficiencies of existing ensemble methods.  
- Paper gives a clear, concise introduction to Multiple Choice Learning and a thorough related-work review.  
- Testing phase is unclear—paper seems to use ground truth to select the best ensemble member at test time and needs clarification.  
- Baselines labeled “independent ensembles” / “regular ensembles (Indp.)” are not well explained or referenced.  
- Claim that ensemble members “specialize” to different output classes is weakly justified given per-example reassignment during training.  
- It’s unclear whether observed specialization is an algorithmic effect or an experimental artifact; consider incorporating specialization into the loss to test this.  
- The paper omits discussion of related diversity-regularization work in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), which is highly relevant.  
- Experimental results are impressive and demonstrate broad applicability and efficacy.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with revisions to clarify testing, baselines, specialization justification, and related diversity-regularization work.
