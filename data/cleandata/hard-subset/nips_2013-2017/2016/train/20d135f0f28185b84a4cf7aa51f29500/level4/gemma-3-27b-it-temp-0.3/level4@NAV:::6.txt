The paper introduces a novel ensemble training algorithm for deep learning models. Its key innovation lies in activating only a single member of the ensemble for each training example, updating it based on its loss relative to the true label. This approach, while conceptually straightforward, yields strong empirical results and addresses limitations present in existing ensemble methods, leading me to recommend acceptance with minor revisions. (Contd. from summary) 1. The description of the testing procedure lacks clarity. It appears the ground truth is utilized to identify the optimal member, as suggested in line 159 of page 5. 2. The terms 'independent ensembles' and 'regular ensembles' (denoted as 'Indp.' in figures and line 181 on page 5) are used without sufficient definition. The baseline represented by these terms requires further explanation. 3. The authors emphasize the `specialization` of ensemble members to distinct output classes. However, given the per-example reassignment strategy during training, the justification for this specialization claim feels incomplete. Exploring the incorporation of specialization directly into the loss function could strengthen this argument. 4. The discussion would benefit from acknowledging related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.). This technique offers a more formalized approach to promoting diversity, and its potential within the deep learning context remains an open area for investigation.
