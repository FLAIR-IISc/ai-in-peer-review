- Proposes sMCL, a novel method for training diverse deep ensembles guided by an oracle.
- sMCL is simple to implement, agnostic to architecture and loss, and parameter-free.
- Interleaves assignment steps with batch SGD so ensemble members can be trained concurrently.
- Method overcomes shortcomings of existing approaches for diverse ensemble training.
- Experiments cover image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning across architectures.
- sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and other strong baselines.
- Automatically induces specialization among ensemble members, producing interpretable emergent expertise.
- Paper is well-written, well-motivated, and provides a clear explanation of underlying principles.
- Experimental comparisons are thorough and results are convincing.
- Lacks detailed discussion on how to exploit/use the resulting ensemble in practice (e.g., top-k deployment).
- Pseudo-code (Figure 2) is rasterized and hard to read; should be presented as text or vector graphics.
- Reviewer scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
- Overall: strong, publishable paper if ensemble exploitation details and pseudo-code presentation are addressed.
