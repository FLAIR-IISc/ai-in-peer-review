- Proposes an ensemble training method where only one member is active per training example (selecting and updating the member with lowest loss); simple, effective, and warrants weak acceptance.  
- Testing/inference procedure is unclear—paper seems to use ground-truth to select the best member at test time, but this is not explicitly described.  
- The "independent ensembles" baseline is referenced without definition or justification, and its relation to "regular ensembles" is unclear.  
- Claim of members specializing to distinct classes is unsupported because reassignment is done per example, not per class—consider incorporating explicit specialization in the loss.  
- Paper omits related work on diversity regularization in neural nets (e.g., Chen et al.), weakening the novelty and positioning of the contribution.
