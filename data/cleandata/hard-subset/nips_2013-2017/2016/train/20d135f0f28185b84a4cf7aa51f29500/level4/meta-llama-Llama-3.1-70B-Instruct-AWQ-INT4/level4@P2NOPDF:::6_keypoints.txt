- Paper proposes an ensemble training method where only one learner (the lowest-loss member) is updated per example; simple, effective, and yields strong experimental results — reviewer recommends weak acceptance.  
- Testing procedure is unclear and appears to use ground-truth to select the best member (line 159); needs a detailed explanation.  
- Terms/baselines "independent ensembles" / "regular ensembles" (Indp.) are used but not defined; provide brief descriptions and their relevance.  
- Claim that ensemble members "specialize" to different classes is not justified given per-example reassignment during training; mechanism for specialization is unclear.  
- Suggestion: incorporate specialization explicitly into the loss function to encourage/measure specialization.  
- Related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) is not discussed; add comparison and discussion to situate the contribution.
