- Proposes a novel training method for DNN ensembles that uses an oracle to pick the best network per instance and backpropagates only to that network.  
- Oracle-guided updates enable networks to specialize, producing ensembles that cover a wide range of outputs.  
- The idea is particularly effective for classification and promotes specialization in networks.  
- Presentation is clear and experiments across multiple domains show impressive performance.  
- Paper is strong and merits publication (rating: 8/10).  
- Major missing piece: the paper trains with an oracle but does not sufficiently develop how to exploit the resulting ensemble at inference/use time.  
- The lack of exploration on leveraging ensembles (e.g., top-k usage) is important for practicality, though it may be problem-specific.  
- Minor suggestion: improve Figure 2 by embedding pseudo-code as vector graphics or including the pseudo-code directly for readability.
