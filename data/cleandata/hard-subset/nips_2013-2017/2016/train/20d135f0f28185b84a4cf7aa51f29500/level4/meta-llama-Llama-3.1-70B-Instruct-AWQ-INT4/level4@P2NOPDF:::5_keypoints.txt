- Proposes jointly learned ensemble of deep networks that minimize oracle loss to generate multiple outputs for interactive settings.  
- Oracle experiments on three tasks show the proposed method is effective.  
- Integrates deep networks into the Multi-Class Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces stochastic block gradient descent to minimize MCL oracle loss.  
- Optimization is simple to implement within back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training.  
- Fails to discuss the importance of initialization and how performance would change with random vs. pre-trained initialization.  
- Experimental details are insufficient—unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL.  
- Evaluation relies on oracle accuracy only and lacks a thorough analysis of ensemble member diversity.  
- Recommends comparing against related work (Stefan Lee et al., "Why M Heads are Better than One", 2015) for additional context.
