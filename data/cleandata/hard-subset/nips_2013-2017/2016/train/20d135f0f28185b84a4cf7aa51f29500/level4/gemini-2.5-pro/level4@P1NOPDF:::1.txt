This paper introduces a stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm for training ensemble models. The approach builds on the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], which defines the ensemble loss as the loss of the best-performing individual classifier in the ensemble—a strategy designed to yield a diverse set of models. The key contribution of this paper is the adaptation of this training objective to an SGD framework, which enables its application to large-scale models such as deep neural networks.

Overall, the paper is interesting and clearly written. My primary reservation is that the algorithmic novelty could be viewed as incremental over the work of [8]. However, the authors successfully argue that this seemingly minor change significantly expands the method's applicability and practical utility, particularly for deep neural networks. Given this impact, I am in favor of acceptance.

To further strengthen the paper, I would suggest two additions. First, an interesting comparison would be to evaluate the original MCL algorithm from [8] when trained with mini-batches rather than the entire dataset. Second, the paper would benefit from a more in-depth analysis and discussion of the "diversity" effect that this training strategy is purported to encourage.
