- Proposes sMCL: jointly trains an ensemble to minimize oracle loss and produce multiple diverse outputs.  
- Method integrates deep networks into MCL and uses stochastic block gradient descent.  
- Compatible with standard backpropagation, enabling end-to-end training without architectural changes.  
- Experiments on three tasks report improved oracle accuracy over baselines.  
- Initialization is underexplored: paper uses pre-trained networks but lacks sensitivity analysis (random vs pre-trained) and fails to state whether baselines used the same initialization.  
- Lacks analysis of ensemble diversity; needs quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise disagreement, feature coverage, entropy) to verify varied outputs.  
- Omits comparison to closely related work (Lee et al., 2015); should explicitly compare to that baseline to establish novelty.  
- Recommendations: clarify initialization protocols and ablations, include diversity measurements, and compare against Lee et al. to strengthen the paper.
