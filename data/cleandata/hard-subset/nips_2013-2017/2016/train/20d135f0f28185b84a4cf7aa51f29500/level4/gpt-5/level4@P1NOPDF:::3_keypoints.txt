- Addresses bringing multiple-choice learning (MCL) to deep networks, an important and relevant problem for NeurIPS.
- Proposes a simple winner-takes-the-gradient backpropagation mechanism that couples learner training with assignment in ensembles.
- Experiments on image classification, segmentation, and captioning show strong, broad empirical performance.
- Major concern: paper emphasizes “diversity” but provides no explicit diversity term or constraint, relying on an implicit scheme that does not guarantee diverse outputs.
- Contribution appears incremental relative to the relaxation by Guzman et al.; needs clearer articulation of technical novelty and why it matters empirically/theoretically.
- Minor suggestion: rewrite the first sentence of the abstract for clarity and impact.
- Minor suggestion: tone down claims about diversity or add explicit methods/analysis demonstrating induced diversity.
- Typos: Line 108 — the first “f” should be “g”; Line 115 — extra period in the middle of a sentence.
- Question/request: clarify the training protocol for the deep-learning MCL baseline (epochs, early stopping, learning-rate schedules) and measures taken to ensure each network converged to a reasonable solution before ensemble evaluation.
