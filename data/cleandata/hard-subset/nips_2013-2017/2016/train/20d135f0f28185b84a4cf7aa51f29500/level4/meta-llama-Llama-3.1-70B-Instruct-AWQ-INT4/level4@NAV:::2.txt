The paper introduces a novel approach for training ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or other gradient-based methods by leveraging an oracle that identifies the optimal ensemble member for each training instance. This oracle selectively applies backpropagation to the chosen network, allowing the ensemble to specialize in various themes and effectively cover a broad range of outputs of interest. The authors demonstrate impressive performance across multiple domains. 

Overall, this paper presents a compelling concept for training DNN ensembles with oracle-guided backpropagation, which successfully promotes specialization in classification contexts. The presentation is clear, and the experiments are conducted across several domains. This is a strong contribution that merits publication.

However, to elevate this paper to outstanding, it would be beneficial to address the exploitation of DNN ensembles trained with the proposed approach. While the oracle is utilized during training, the application of the resulting ensemble is not thoroughly explored beyond its potential to provide a diverse set of results and its consistency with the top-k metric used in certain problems, such as ImageNet. This omission is a crucial aspect that hinders the practical usability of the proposed approach.

Notwithstanding this limitation, the current contribution is valuable, and the missing component is relatively problem-specific. A minor suggestion is to replace the raster image of pseudo-code in Fig. 2 with either directly typeset code or a vector image to enhance readability.
