- Proposes jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating diverse outputs, suited to interactive applications.  
- Shows effectiveness via oracle experiments on three tasks.  
- Integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework and introduces stochastic block gradient descent (sMCL) to minimize the MCL oracle loss.  
- sMCL is easy to implement within standard back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training.  
- Does not investigate sensitivity to initialization (random vs. pre-trained), a potential weakness.  
- Experimental comparisons lack detail—unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL.  
- Diversity of ensemble members is not analyzed despite reporting oracle accuracy; additional diversity metrics/analysis are needed.  
- Should compare against related work with similar premises, e.g., Lee et al. (2015) “Why M Heads are Better than One.”
