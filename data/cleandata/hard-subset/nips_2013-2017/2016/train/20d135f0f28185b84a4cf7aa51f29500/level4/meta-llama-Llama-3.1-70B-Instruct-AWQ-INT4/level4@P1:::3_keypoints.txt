- Proposes a simple "winner-takes-the-gradient" modification to backprop to solve Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) with deep networks.  
- Addresses an important problem for the community and provides an intuitive, easy-to-implement method.  
- Experimental results are thorough and show effectiveness across image classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Strength: tackles a complex problem with a straightforward and well-explained approach.  
- Weakness: paper emphasizes "diversity" but the method does not explicitly enforce diversity, creating a misleading claim.  
- Contribution is useful but incremental relative to the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.; more detailed comparison needed.  
- Clarity is high overall, though there are minor textual errors (e.g., stray punctuation) and one abstract sentence should be rephrased.  
- Methodological concern: unclear how baseline MCL networks were ensured to converge and whether early cutoff affected ensemble performance.  
- Suggest toning down claims about diversity to avoid confusion.  
- Suggest adding a clearer explanation and analysis of differences from existing approaches, especially Guzman et al.  
- Recommend clarifying the convergence/training procedure for baseline MCL with deep learning.  
- Overall evaluation: technically sound, significant potential impact, likely acceptable after revisions (Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10).
