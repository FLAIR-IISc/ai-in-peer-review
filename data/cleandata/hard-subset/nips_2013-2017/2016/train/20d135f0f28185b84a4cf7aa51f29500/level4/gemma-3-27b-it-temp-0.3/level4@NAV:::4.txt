The authors present a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They assert the benefits of this framework across diverse tasks including classification, segmentation, and captioning. 

1. While the paper introduces the sMCL method, the novelty appears incremental. The motivation for employing MCL is readily understandable, however, it doesn’t represent a substantial contribution in and of itself. The presented work largely resembles a neural network implementation of [8] with some modifications.

2. The paper’s presentation could be improved. The technical details of the method (Section 3) are condensed to a single page. A clearer articulation of the differences between the proposed framework and established multi-task training approaches – which are already widely utilized – is needed.

3. The experimental evaluation covers a range of computer vision tasks (classification, segmentation, and captioning). However, the experiments lack robust baseline comparisons, and the observed performance gains are modest. To convincingly demonstrate the efficacy of the framework, experiments on a standard benchmark like PASCAL VOC 2012, alongside stronger baselines, are recommended.
