- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize oracle loss to generate multiple outputs, useful for interactive settings.
- Integrates deep networks into the Monte Carlo Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent method to minimize oracle loss.
- The proposed optimization can be implemented within standard back-propagation and allows end-to-end training.
- Experiments on three tasks demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.
- Major concern: the paper does not clarify how crucial initialization is—whether pre-trained weights are required or random initialization suffices.
- Concern about fairness: baseline comparison settings are unclear—did baselines use the same pre-trained initialization as the proposed method?
- Evaluation relies on oracle accuracy but lacks analysis of ensemble diversity; diversity metrics are needed to assess varied output generation.
- Suggestion to compare with related prior work: “Why M Heads are Better than One” (Lee et al., 2015), which has a very similar idea.
