- Paper proposes using a “winner-takes-the-gradient” layer during backprop to extend multiple choice learning (MCL) to deep networks, combining learner training with assignment.  
- Empirical results show strong performance on image classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Strength: MCL for deep learning is an important problem for the community.  
- Strength: The proposed method is notably simple.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation covers three distinct tasks.  
- Weakness: Emphasis on “diversity” (including the title) is misleading because the model does not explicitly enforce diversity; diversity appears emergent.  
- Weakness: The method is an incremental improvement over the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.  
- Minor: Revise the first sentence of the abstract for clarity.  
- Minor: Reduce the paper’s emphasis on “diversity” throughout.  
- Minor: On line 108, the first instance of “f” should be “g.”  
- Minor: Line 115 contains an extraneous period mid-sentence.  
- Question: How did the authors ensure each network in the baseline MCL deep-learning implementation converged to a reasonable solution, avoiding premature termination that could harm ensemble performance?
