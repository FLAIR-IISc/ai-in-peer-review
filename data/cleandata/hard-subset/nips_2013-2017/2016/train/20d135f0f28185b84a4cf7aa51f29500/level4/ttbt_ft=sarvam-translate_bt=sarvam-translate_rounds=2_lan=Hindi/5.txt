The authors propose a method that allows a group of deep networks to learn collaboratively and generate multiple outputs, reducing Oracle loss, which is useful when interacting with users. Oracle experiments conducted on three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. This paper integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and proposes a Stochastic Block Gradient Descent optimization method to reduce Oracle loss in MCL. The proposed optimization method can be easily implemented within a back-propagation learning framework, and the entire model can be trained end-to-end. My concern is how important initialization is? What if the model is initialized with random values instead of a pre-trained network? The authors do not provide more information about the setting for comparison. Do the authors use a pre-trained network similar to SMCL as a baseline method for initialization? The authors use Oracle accuracy as a metric for evaluation. What about the diversity of ensemble members? I hope to see more analysis on this. Furthermore, there is a very similar idea work that the authors have suggested for comparison. Stephen Lee, Senthil Purushvalakam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, Why Am Heads Are Better Than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks. CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015)
