The authors propose a method for generating multiple outputs by jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize the oracle loss, a capability particularly valuable in interactive settings. The effectiveness of this approach is demonstrated through oracle experiments on three tasks. This paper integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method designed to minimize the MCL oracle loss. A key benefit of the proposed optimization method is its straightforward implementation within the back-propagation learning framework, allowing for end-to-end training of the entire model. 

A primary concern is the sensitivity of the method to initialization. The authors do not explore the impact of initializing models with random values instead of utilizing a pre-trained network. Furthermore, the experimental setup for comparison lacks sufficient detail. Specifically, it is unclear whether baseline methods employ the same pre-trained network used for initializing sMCL. While the authors evaluate performance using oracle accuracy, the diversity of the ensemble members remains unaddressed. Additional analysis quantifying and qualifying this diversity would significantly strengthen the work. 

Finally, the authors should consider and compare their method to related work with a similar premise, such as “Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks” by Stefan Lee et al. (CoRR abs/1511.06314, 2015).
