

This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm for training ensemble models, extending the approach of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8]. The authors build upon their work, where the ensemble loss is defined as the loss of the best-performing single classifier within the ensemble—this formulation inherently promotes classifier diversity. The key contribution is adapting this framework to use SGD, enabling its application to deep neural networks. The paper is well-written and presents an interesting approach. My primary concern is whether the algorithmic novelty is sufficiently substantial beyond the incremental modification in [8]. However, the authors convincingly demonstrate that their relatively minor modification significantly enhances practical utility, particularly for deep learning. I therefore recommend acceptance. To strengthen the work further, I suggest two specific additions: (1) a comparison of the MCL algorithm under mini-batch training (as opposed to full-batch), and (2) more rigorous analysis of the diversity effect induced by the proposed method.
