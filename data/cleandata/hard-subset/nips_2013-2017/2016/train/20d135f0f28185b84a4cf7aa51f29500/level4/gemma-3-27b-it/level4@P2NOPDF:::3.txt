This paper addresses the challenging problem of multiple choice (MCL) learning in the context of deep neural networks, a setting where existing MCL solutions do not readily generalize. The authors propose a novel and surprisingly simple approach – a “winner-takes-the-gradient” layer during backpropagation – that effectively couples the training of individual deep networks (the "choices") with the assignment problem inherent in an ensemble learning framework. The method is evaluated on a diverse set of tasks, including image classification, image segmentation, and image captioning, demonstrating promising performance across these domains.

The paper’s strengths lie in its relevance to the NIPS community, the inherent importance of MCL for deep learning, and the elegance of the proposed solution. The simplicity of the method is particularly appealing, and the comprehensive experimental validation on three distinct tasks strengthens the results. 

However, a significant concern centers around the emphasis placed on “diversity” – prominently featured even in the paper’s title – without a corresponding explicit mechanism to *enforce* diversity within the learned ensemble. The motivation led me to anticipate a model incorporating a diversity-promoting term, and its absence is somewhat disappointing. While the method implicitly *may* lead to diverse solutions, the paper does not demonstrate or quantify this effect, creating a disconnect between the stated motivation and the technical approach.

Furthermore, the contribution feels incremental given prior work, specifically the relaxation presented by Guzman *et al.*, and could benefit from a more thorough discussion of how it extends or improves upon these existing approaches.

Several minor points should be addressed. The opening sentence of the abstract is awkwardly phrased and could be improved for clarity. The focus on “diversity” should be reduced throughout the paper to better reflect the method's actual properties. Additionally, there are two minor technical errors: on line 108, the first instance of "f" should be "g" in “we fixed the form of…”, and there is an extraneous period in the middle of a sentence on line 115.

Finally, I have a question regarding the baseline MCL implementation with deep learning: How did the authors ensure the convergence of each individual network before terminating training? Prematurely halting the training of the constituent learners could negatively impact the overall ensemble performance, and a justification for the chosen training schedule would be valuable.
