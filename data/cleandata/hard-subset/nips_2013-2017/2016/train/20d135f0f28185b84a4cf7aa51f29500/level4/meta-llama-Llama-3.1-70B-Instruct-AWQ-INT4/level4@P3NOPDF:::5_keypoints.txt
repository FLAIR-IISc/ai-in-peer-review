- Proposes learning an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs, integrated into the MCL paradigm.  
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization that is end-to-end and implementable within back-propagation.  
- Oracle experiments on three tasks demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Concern: Importance of initialization not analyzed—authors use pre-trained networks but do not report performance with random initialization.  
- Suggestion: Provide comparisons across different initialization settings to assess robustness.  
- Concern: Unclear whether baseline methods used the same pre-trained initialization, risking unfair comparisons—needs clarification.  
- Concern: Evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy only and lacks analysis of ensemble diversity; diversity metrics/analysis are needed.  
- Suggestion: Compare and discuss relation to prior work, e.g., “Why M Heads are Better than One” (Stefan Lee et al., 2015).  
- Overall: Method is promising but would be strengthened by addressing initialization, baseline setup, diversity evaluation, and related-work comparison.
