The paper "Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning for Training Diverse Deep Ensembles" proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent-based approach to minimize the loss with respect to an oracle. The authors build upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8] and extend it to deep neural networks. The main idea is to train an ensemble of deep networks that produce multiple outputs, and the oracle loss is minimized by selecting the best output from the ensemble.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem, the proposed approach, and the experimental results. The idea of using stochastic gradient descent to train the ensemble is interesting and has the potential to be widely applicable.

The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach on several tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning. The results show that their approach outperforms existing methods, including classical ensembles and the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) algorithm [8]. The authors also provide an analysis of the diversity of the ensemble members and show that their approach can produce interpretable specializations among the members.

The strengths of the paper include:

* The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the problem and the proposed approach.
* The experimental results are thorough and demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach on several tasks.
* The authors provide an analysis of the diversity of the ensemble members, which is an important aspect of the proposed approach.

The weaknesses of the paper include:

* The algorithm novelty of the paper may be considered incremental to that of [8]. However, the authors convincingly argue that their approach is more practical and widely applicable.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the "diversity" effect and how it relates to the performance of the ensemble.
