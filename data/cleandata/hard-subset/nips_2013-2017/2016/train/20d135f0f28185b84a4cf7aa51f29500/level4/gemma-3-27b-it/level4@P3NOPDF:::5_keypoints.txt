- Proposes joint training of an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for interactive applications.  
- Integrates deep networks into the Mixture of Conditional Likelihoods (MCL) framework and introduces stochastic block gradient descent for end-to-end backprop.  
- Experiments on three tasks show improved performance over existing approaches.  
- Strength: elegant combination of MCL and deep learning that yields diverse, accurate ensembles and simplifies training via end-to-end learning.  
- Concern: method relies on pre-trained ensemble members; sensitivity to random initialization is unknown and should be explored.  
- Concern: experimental setup lacks detail—unclear whether baselines used the same pre-training, making comparisons potentially unfair.  
- Concern: evaluation focuses only on oracle accuracy and omits ensemble diversity/robustness metrics (e.g., average pairwise disagreement, prediction variance).  
- Concern: related work coverage is incomplete—needs comparison to Lee et al. (2015) “Why M Heads are Better than One” and discussion of methodological differences.  
- Recommendation: address initialization sensitivity, clarify experimental details, include diversity analyses, and compare thoroughly to related work before acceptance.
