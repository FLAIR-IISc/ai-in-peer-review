- Addresses training diverse deep ensembles to produce multiple plausible hypotheses rather than a single prediction.  
- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based adaptation making Multiple Choice Learning practical for deep networks.  
- Well-motivated with clear connections to CV and NLP tasks where ambiguity necessitates multiple solutions.  
- Builds appropriately on prior ensemble and multiple-solution literature.  
- Technically sound derivation and clear presentation of the algorithm.  
- Extensive empirical validation across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning demonstrating consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Provides interpretability analysis showing ensemble member specialization (networks focus on different classes/aspects).  
- Practical: parameter-free and applicable to any SGD-trained model, easy to implement.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—core oracle-loss idea follows prior MCL work, with main advance being SGD adaptation.  
- Weakness: lacks comparison to a batched implementation of original MCL to more definitively quantify sMCL benefits.  
- Weakness: diversity analysis is limited—needs more quantitative metrics of output dissimilarity beyond specialization examples.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to beam search in captioning experiments; wider beam-width evaluations would strengthen claims.  
- Overall: strong paper that meaningfully improves practicality of MCL and merits acceptance, with minor revisions recommended (batched MCL comparison and deeper diversity analysis).
