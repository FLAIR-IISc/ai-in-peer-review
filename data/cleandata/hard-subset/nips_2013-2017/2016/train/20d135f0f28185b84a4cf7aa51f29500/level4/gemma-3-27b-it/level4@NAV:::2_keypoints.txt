- Introduces a novel training method: an oracle selects the best network per instance and backpropagates only to that network, encouraging specialization.  
- Method is applicable to DNNs and other gradient-based models.  
- Empirical results show strong performance across diverse domains and datasets.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-presented, and supported by comprehensive experiments; overall a strong contribution deserving publication.  
- Main weakness: lacks a deeper exploration of how to practically utilize the trained ensemble beyond noting broad coverage and top-k compatibility.  
- This usability limitation is relatively problem-specific and does not materially diminish the contribution.  
- Minor presentation issue: Fig. 2 contains rasterized pseudo-code—replace with inline pseudo-code or a vector graphic for clarity.
