- Introduces sMCL, a method to train deep ensembles by optimizing oracle loss so the best ensemble member per example is trained.  
- Key technical contribution: stochastic "winner-take-gradient" block SGD where only the current best network for an example receives the gradient.  
- Strength: Addresses an important problem of producing multiple diverse hypotheses, adapting MCL to deep networks in a practical way.  
- Strength: Method is simple, parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, easy to implement, and trains all models concurrently.  
- Strength: Empirical evaluation across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning shows consistent improvements over baselines.  
- Strength: Paper is well-written with clear motivation, precise algorithm description, and insightful qualitative analyses showing emergent specialization.  
- Weakness: Initialization importance not fully explored; segmentation experiments use pre-trained ensembles and results from random init are missing.  
- Weakness: Unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization, raising fairness concerns in comparisons.  
- Weakness: Heavy reliance on oracle metrics without quantitative diversity measures (e.g., disagreement or variance) to support the diversity claim.  
- Weakness: Omits citation/comparison to closely related work (Lee et al., 2015) on training diverse deep ensembles.  
- Acceptance argument: Simple, novel, general, and computationally efficient solution to training diverse ensembles with convincing experiments.  
- Rejection argument: Experimental clarity/initialization issues, lack of quantitative diversity metrics, and missing key related work undermine the paper.
