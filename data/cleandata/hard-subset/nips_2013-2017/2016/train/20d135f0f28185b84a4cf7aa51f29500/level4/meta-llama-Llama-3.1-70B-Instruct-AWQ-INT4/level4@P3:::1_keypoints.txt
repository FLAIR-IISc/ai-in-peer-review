- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic SGD algorithm for training diverse deep ensembles that minimizes oracle loss; builds on Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], is simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Paper is well-written with clear motivation and a thorough related-work analysis.  
- Experimental results show broad applicability and that sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines.  
- Provides clear explanation of the algorithm and detailed analysis showing interpretable specializations among ensemble members.  
- Concern about novelty: unclear if sMCL is significantly new compared to MCL in [8]; requests detailed comparison when using batches.  
- Requests more quantitative evaluation of ensemble "diversity" beyond the provided qualitative results.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel algorithm, strong experimental evidence, and clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: potential incremental novelty relative to [8] and insufficient diversity analysis.  
- Reviewer recommendation: favorable to acceptance, contingent on addressing the novelty and diversity-analysis concerns.  
- Review scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
