- sMCL is incremental in novelty and not a major new contribution.  
- MCL motivation is clear but cannot be considered a contribution of this work.  
- The method is essentially a neural-network version of prior work [8] with minor adjustments.  
- Writing quality needs improvement; technical section (Section 3) is too short and unclear.  
- The paper fails to clearly explain differences from widely adopted alternative multi-task training methods.  
- Experiments cover classification, segmentation, and captioning but use weak baselines and show only small improvements.  
- To be convincing, the authors should evaluate on PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against stronger baselines.
