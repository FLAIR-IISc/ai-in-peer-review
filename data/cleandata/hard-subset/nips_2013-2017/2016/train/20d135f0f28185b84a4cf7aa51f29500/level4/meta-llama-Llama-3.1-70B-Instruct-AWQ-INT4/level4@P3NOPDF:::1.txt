This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models, building upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al [8]. The authors' key insight is to leverage the loss of the ensemble model as the loss of the best output of a single classifier in the ensemble, yielding a diverse set of classifiers. Their primary contribution lies in the application of a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training, thereby enabling the use of this algorithm for deep neural networks.

The paper is well-written and presents an interesting approach. However, my main concern is whether the algorithmic novelty presented in this paper is merely incremental to that of [8]. After careful consideration, I am convinced that the authors' modifications make the algorithm significantly more useful, which inclines me to support its acceptance.

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest an interesting comparison: evaluating the MCL algorithm when batches are used, rather than the entire dataset. This could provide valuable insights into the algorithm's performance under different conditions. Additionally, I would like to see more in-depth analysis regarding the "diversity" effect, as this is a crucial aspect of the proposed approach. Overall, the paper has the potential to make a meaningful contribution to the field, and with some additional experimentation and analysis, it could be even more impactful.
