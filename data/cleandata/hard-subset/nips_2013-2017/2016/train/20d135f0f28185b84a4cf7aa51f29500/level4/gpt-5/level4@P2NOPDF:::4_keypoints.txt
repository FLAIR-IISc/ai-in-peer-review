1) Novelty concerns: sMCL is incremental and largely a neural-network instantiation of prior MCL ([8]) rather than a substantive conceptual advance.

2) Clarity/technical depth: Paper is poorly written with an underdeveloped Section 3; it fails to clearly differentiate from standard multi-task/alternating training and lacks derivations and implementation details.

3) Empirical evaluation: Experiments use weak baselines and show modest gains; stronger baselines and additional tests (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012 segmentation) are needed to validate effectiveness.
