- Proposes sMCL: a stochastic gradient-based extension of Multiple Choice Learning for training diverse deep ensembles that minimize oracle loss.  
- Introduces a "winner-take-gradient" rule where only the lowest-error predictor's gradient is backpropagated for each example.  
- Method is architecture-agnostic, parameter-free, and enables concurrent training of ensemble members (no costly retraining).  
- Demonstrates strong empirical gains (6–10% absolute improvement in oracle metrics) across CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO.  
- sMCL is ~5× faster than previous MCL approaches and induces interpretable specialization among ensemble members.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and provides sufficient technical detail and theoretical justification (Equation 2).  
- Experiments are thorough across diverse tasks, and the oracle metric is an appropriate evaluation for multiple-output systems.  
- Related work coverage is incomplete: fails to cite/compare with Lee et al. (2015) "Why M Heads Are Better than One," which uses a similar winner-take-all gradient idea.  
- Major concern: sensitivity to initialization—paper uses pre-trained networks but does not test random initialization or report robustness.  
- Major concern: lack of quantitative analysis of ensemble diversity (e.g., n-gram overlap, entropy) beyond oracle metrics.  
- Unclear whether baselines (MCL, standard ensembles) used identical initialization, potentially confounding comparisons.  
- Overall recommendation: Major Revision — address initialization sensitivity, quantify diversity, and compare explicitly with Lee et al. (2015).
