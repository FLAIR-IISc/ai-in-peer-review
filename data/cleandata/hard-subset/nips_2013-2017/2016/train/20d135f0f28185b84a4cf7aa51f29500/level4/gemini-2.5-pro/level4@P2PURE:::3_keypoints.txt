- Paper proposes a "winner-takes-the-gradient" backprop layer to enable end-to-end Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep networks, evaluated on classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Strength: Tackles a timely and relevant problem of adapting MCL to deep models for the NIPS community.  
- Strength: The method is appealingly simple and easy to implement.  
- Strength: Experimental evaluation is comprehensive across three challenging, diverse tasks.  
- Weakness: Title and motivation stress "diversity" but the model contains no explicit diversity objective—diversity is emergent, not enforced.  
- Weakness: Contribution seems incremental compared to prior relaxation-based approaches (e.g., Guzman et al.).  
- Minor: Rewrite the unclear first sentence of the abstract.  
- Minor: Tone down claims about "diversity" to match the method's actual mechanism.  
- Minor corrections: on line 108 the first 'f' should likely be 'g'; remove the extraneous period on line 115.  
- Question: Please clarify the convergence criteria used for baseline MCL individual networks—how was it ensured each network had satisfactorily converged to avoid biased ensemble comparisons?
