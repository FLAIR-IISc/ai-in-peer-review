- Paper proposes an SGD-based Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) method and claims benefits for classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Limited novelty: sMCL is incremental and largely an NN instantiation of prior MCL work (notably [8]).
- Unclear technical contribution: Section 3 is too brief and lacks detail on how the framework differs from common alternative training.
- Insufficient technical depth: Missing precise algorithmic details, assumptions, and theoretical/empirical justification for design choices.
- Weak experimental evaluation: Results show small gains and lack strong baselines.
- Missing standard benchmarks: No evaluation on PASCAL VOC 2012 or comparisons to state-of-the-art baselines.
- Suggested fixes: Rigorously differentiate from prior work and alternative training, expand technical exposition, and add stronger experimental comparisons.
- Overall: Contribution appears incremental, presentation is too brief to judge novelty/rigor, and empirical gains are modest—substantial clarification and stronger experiments required.
