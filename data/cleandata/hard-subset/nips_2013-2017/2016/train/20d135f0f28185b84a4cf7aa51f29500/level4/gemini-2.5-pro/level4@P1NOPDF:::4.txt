This paper formulates a stochastic gradient descent method based on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and claims that the proposed framework offers advantages for a variety of tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning. While the idea is interesting, the paper has several weaknesses in its current form.

1.  **Incremental Novelty:** The novelty of the proposed stochastic MCL (sMCL) method appears to be limited. While the motivation for MCL is well-explained, the core concept of MCL itself is not a novel contribution of this paper. The work can be seen as an adaptation of the method in [8] for neural networks, with some modifications. The contribution is therefore more of an application than a fundamental advance.

2.  **Clarity and Technical Depth:** The paper's presentation could be significantly improved. The core technical description in Section 3 is notably brief, spanning only a single page. This section lacks the necessary detail for the reader to fully grasp the method. Furthermore, the paper fails to clarify the key differences between the proposed framework and standard alternating training strategies, which are commonly used for multi-task learning. This comparison is crucial for understanding the unique contributions of the proposed method over existing techniques.

3.  **Insufficient Experimental Validation:** The authors evaluate their method on several computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the experimental evaluation is not convincing. The chosen baselines are not particularly strong, and the reported performance improvements are marginal. To provide a more compelling demonstration of the framework's effectiveness, the evaluation should be extended. A thorough comparison on a standard, challenging benchmark such as PASCAL VOC 2012 against stronger, state-of-the-art baselines would be necessary to substantiate the claims made in the paper.
