- Proposes a simple "winner-takes-the-gradient" layer to adapt multiple-choice learning (MCL) to deep neural networks, unifying learner training and data assignment during backpropagation.
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Addresses an important, timely problem of adapting MCL for deep models relevant to the NeurIPS community.
- Method is simple and elegant in design.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough and shows positive results on three distinct tasks.
- Major concern: paper heavily emphasizes "diversity" but the method contains no explicit diversity-promoting mechanism, creating a motivation–method mismatch.
- Contribution appears incremental compared to the relaxation technique of Guzman et al.
- Suggest revising the first sentence of the abstract for clarity.
- Suggest toning down emphasis on "diversity" to match the method's actual contribution.
- Typo: Line 108 — first "f" should be "g".
- Typo: Line 115 — extraneous period in the middle of a sentence.
- Question: What procedure ensured each deep-learning MCL baseline network had fully converged, since premature termination could unfairly favor the proposed method?
