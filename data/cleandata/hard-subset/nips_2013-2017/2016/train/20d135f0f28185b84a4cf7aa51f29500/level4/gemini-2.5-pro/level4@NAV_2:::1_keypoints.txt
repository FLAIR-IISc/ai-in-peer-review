- Proposes sMCL: an SGD-based "winner-take-gradient" training procedure that directly optimizes oracle loss to train diverse deep ensembles concurrently.  
- Replaces MCL's block coordinate descent with a stochastic approach, making oracle-loss training feasible for modern deep networks and yielding ~5x speedup.  
- Empirically outperforms independently trained ensembles and baselines across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Encourages specialization of ensemble members, producing qualitatively diverse high-quality predictions.  
- Method is simple, parameter-free, agnostic to architecture and loss, and easy to implement.  
- Paper is clearly written with insightful analysis of emergent specialization.  
- Main weakness: conceptual novelty is incremental since the objective is borrowed from prior MCL work; primary contribution is the optimization strategy.  
- Suggests adding quantitative diversity metrics, e.g., comparing average individual-member performance to classical ensembles.  
- Recommends a more direct comparison to MCL (e.g., a "mini-batch MCL" baseline) to isolate benefits of per-batch stochastic updates.  
- Overall judgment: practical, effective, and broadly applicable method with strong empirical results — practical impact justifies acceptance despite incremental novelty.
