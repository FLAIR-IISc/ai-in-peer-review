- Tackles Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep networks to produce diverse, high-quality ensemble predictions where at least one matches the ground truth.  
- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) that integrates data assignment and model updates into SGD using a "winner-takes-the-gradient" rule.  
- For each minibatch example, only the ensemble member with lowest loss receives the gradient, enabling concurrent end-to-end training.  
- Demonstrates sMCL across classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, showing improved oracle performance over baselines.  
- Strength: Addresses a practically important problem (multiple plausible hypotheses) and adapts MCL for deep learning.  
- Strength: Method is simple, intuitive, parameter-free, easy to implement, and integrates with standard SGD.  
- Strength: Strong empirical validation across diverse tasks and architectures, indicating broad applicability and robustness.  
- Weakness: Framing emphasizes explicit diversity but the method minimizes oracle loss—diversity is emergent rather than explicitly enforced.  
- Weakness: Contribution is largely a practical/engineering adaptation of prior block coordinate descent MCL work, not a fundamental reformulation.  
- Request: Provide detailed protocol for the baseline MCL ensembles, especially how "convergence" was defined and enforced during meta-iterations.  
- Suggestion: Tighten and clarify the abstract for improved clarity and impact.  
- Suggestion: Tone down claims about explicit diversity in title/framing and clarify that diversity emerges from optimization rather than being an objective.
