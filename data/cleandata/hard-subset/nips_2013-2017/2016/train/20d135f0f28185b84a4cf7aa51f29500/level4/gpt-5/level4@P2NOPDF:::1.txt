This paper presents a stochastic gradient descent (SGD) training procedure for ensemble models built on the framework of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], in which the ensemble loss is defined as the loss of the best-performing classifier on each example, thereby encouraging diversity among ensemble members. The key contribution is to replace the original training scheme with an SGD-based algorithm, making the approach practical for training deep neural networks.

The paper is clearly written and addresses an interesting and relevant problem. My primary concern is novelty: the algorithmic change relative to [8] appears incremental. However, the authors provide convincing evidence that this modification significantly improves usability and scalability, particularly for deep models. On balance, I am inclined to recommend acceptance.

I suggest adding the following to strengthen the paper:
- A comparison to MCL when trained with mini-batches rather than using the entire dataset at once.
- A more thorough analysis of the diversity effect, including quantitative diversity metrics and ablations that clarify how the method induces diversity and how that diversity correlates with ensemble accuracy.
