- Proposal: SGD-based adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to train ensembles by minimizing the loss of the best-performing classifier to encourage diversity.  
- Main contribution: removes MCL’s retraining requirement, making it applicable to deep neural networks via stochastic gradient descent.  
- Writing: paper is well-written and clearly explains contributions.  
- Novelty concern: algorithmic novelty is incremental relative to Guzman-Rivera et al. [8].  
- Practical impact: convincingly enables MCL for modern deep learning where repeated retraining is infeasible; reviewer leans toward acceptance.  
- Suggested experiment: compare against the original MCL implemented with batch updates (not full-dataset retraining) to isolate the effect of stochasticity.  
- Suggested analysis: quantify ensemble diversity using metrics beyond oracle performance to directly assess solution dissimilarity.  
- Empirical support: shows consistent performance gains across tasks and architectures (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning).  
- Insight: observed emergent specialization among ensemble members is an informative finding.  
- Arguments against: primarily incremental novelty and insufficient diversity analysis.
