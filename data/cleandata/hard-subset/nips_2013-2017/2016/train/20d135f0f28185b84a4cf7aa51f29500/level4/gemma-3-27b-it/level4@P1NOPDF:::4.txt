The authors propose a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim advantages for this framework across multiple tasks including classification, segmentation, and captioning.

1. This paper presents the sMCL method, but its novelty is incremental. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it doesn’t represent a significant contribution of this work. The approach appears to be a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments.

2. The paper’s writing could be improved. The technical details of the method (Section 3) are presented in just one page. Crucially, the paper doesn't clearly articulate the difference between the proposed framework and alternative multi-task training approaches, which are already widely used. A more detailed comparison is needed to understand the benefits of sMCL.

3. The authors evaluate their method on several computer vision tasks – classification, image segmentation, and captioning – but the experiments lack strong baselines, and the reported performance improvements are modest. To better demonstrate the efficacy of the framework, the authors should conduct experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against more competitive baselines.
