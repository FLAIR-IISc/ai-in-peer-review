The authors present a method for generating diverse outputs by jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize the oracle loss, a setup particularly relevant for interactive applications. The effectiveness of this approach is demonstrated through oracle experiments on three tasks. This work integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method to minimize the MCL oracle loss. A key advantage of this optimization method is its ease of implementation within a standard back-propagation framework, enabling end-to-end training of the entire model. 

However, I have some concerns regarding the sensitivity of the method to initialization. The paper does not explore the impact of initializing the models with random values rather than using a pre-trained network. Furthermore, the experimental setup for comparison is not sufficiently detailed. Specifically, it is unclear whether baseline methods utilize the same pre-trained network as sMCL for initialization. While the authors evaluate performance using oracle accuracy, the diversity of the ensemble members is not analyzed, and I believe further investigation in this area is warranted. Finally, the authors should consider comparing their approach to related work with a similar premise, such as Lee et al. (2015), “Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks” (CoRR abs/1511.06314).
