- Proposes sMCL: a stochastic gradient descent-based adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for diverse deep ensembles.  
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization technique claimed to be simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Clear motivation for MCL and its potential benefits across applications.  
- Novelty is incremental—builds on Guzman-Rivera et al. rather than introducing a wholly new concept.  
- Main technical contribution is adapting MCL to deep networks via stochastic SGD.  
- Section 3 (technical development) is concise and lacks detailed exposition.  
- Paper should compare more thoroughly with alternative multi-task/multi-model training methods to clarify differences.  
- Experimental evaluation is broad (classification, segmentation, captioning) demonstrating applicability.  
- Experiments use relatively weak baselines and show only modest performance gains.  
- Recommends testing on more challenging datasets (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and stronger baselines to validate effectiveness.  
- Paper is technically sound and claims are supported by analysis and experiments.  
- Well-organized and generally clear, but some sections need more detail and explanation.  
- Overall assessment: worthy of acceptance with revisions to expand technical detail and strengthen experimental baselines.
