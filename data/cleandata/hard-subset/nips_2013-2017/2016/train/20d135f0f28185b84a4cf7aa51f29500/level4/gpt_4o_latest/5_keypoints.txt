- Proposes jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs in user-interactive scenarios.  
- Experiments on three tasks using the oracle demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces stochastic block gradient descent to minimize the oracle loss.  
- Optimization is straightforward to implement within backprop, enabling end-to-end training of the whole model.  
- Concern: significance of initialization—unclear performance when models are randomly initialized versus pre-trained.  
- Concern: comparison setup is insufficiently detailed—do baselines (e.g., sMCL) use the same pre-trained initialization?  
- Concern: evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy but lacks analysis of diversity among ensemble members.  
- Suggestion: compare against closely related prior work (Lee et al., 2015, "Why M Heads are Better than One").
