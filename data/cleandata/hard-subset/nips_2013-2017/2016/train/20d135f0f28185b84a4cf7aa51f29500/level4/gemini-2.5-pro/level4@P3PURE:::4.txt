This paper proposes a stochastic gradient descent method based on Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) and claims its effectiveness across several tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, the paper has several weaknesses in its current form.

1.  **Incremental Novelty:** The novelty of the proposed sMCL method appears incremental. While the motivation for Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) is well-explained, the concept itself is not a contribution of this work. The proposed method seems to be a neural network-based adaptation of the framework presented in [8], with only minor adjustments. The contribution is therefore limited.

2.  **Clarity and Technical Depth:** The paper is not well-written and lacks sufficient technical detail. The core technical description of the proposed method in Section 3 is overly brief, spanning only one page. Furthermore, the authors fail to adequately differentiate their framework from standard alternating training strategies, which are commonly used in multi-task learning settings.

3.  **Weak Experimental Evaluation:** The experimental evaluation is not convincing. Although the authors conduct experiments on several computer vision tasks, the evaluation suffers from a lack of strong baselines, and the reported performance improvements are marginal. To convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework, a more rigorous comparison against stronger, state-of-the-art methods on standard benchmarks is required. For example, for the image segmentation task, experiments on the PASCAL VOC 2012 dataset would be necessary to provide a compelling comparison with established baselines.
