- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based approach for training diverse deep ensembles.  
- Builds on Guzman-Rivera et al.'s MCL, adapting it to deep networks.  
- Main technical contribution: stochastic block gradient descent formulation that minimizes oracle loss for ensembles.  
- Clear motivation: single predictions are insufficient in many supervised learning settings.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability across tasks and architectures (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning).  
- Paper is technically well-structured and explanations are easy to follow.  
- Shows that ensemble member specialization and expertise emerge automatically under sMCL.  
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, comparing to several strong baselines across multiple tasks.  
- sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and a 5× slower MCL procedure.  
- Provides qualitative results demonstrating diversity and interpretability, though more qualitative examples would help.  
- Limitations: lacks deeper theoretical insight into the optimization process and foundations of the framework.  
- Needs more detailed analysis of experimental results, especially quantifying sMCL’s performance gains.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add theoretical discussion, deeper result analysis, and additional qualitative examples.
