- Proposes training an ensemble of deep networks by directly minimizing oracle loss to produce diverse, high-quality outputs for user-interactive applications.
- Integrates Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) with deep networks and introduces stochastic block gradient descent (sMCL) for optimization.
- sMCL is practical and integrates straightforwardly into standard back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training.
- Experiments on three tasks show improved oracle accuracy over baselines.
- Strength: tackles the important problem of generating multiple diverse outputs from a single model.
- Strength: combining MCL with deep networks is a sound and interesting contribution.
- Strength: sMCL is well-motivated, practical, and compatible with existing deep learning pipelines.
- Weakness: role of initialization is not fully explored—performance may depend heavily on pre-trained networks and needs an ablation (e.g., random initialization).
- Weakness: experimental details for baselines are unclear—must clarify whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization for a fair comparison.
- Weakness: evaluation is limited to oracle accuracy and lacks quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., disagreement or distance between ensemble predictions).
- Weakness: missing discussion/comparison with closely related work (Lee et al., 2015); should explain similarities/differences and ideally provide empirical comparison.
