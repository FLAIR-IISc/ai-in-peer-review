

1. The paper introduces a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating Multiple Choice Learning (MCL), claiming advantages across classification, segmentation, and captioning tasks. However, the novelty of the proposed MCL method is incremental. While the motivation for MCL is clear, it does not represent a substantive contribution; this work constitutes a neural network adaptation of [8] with minor adjustments.  
2. The paper is poorly written. The technical exposition (Section 3) is limited to one page, insufficiently differentiating the proposed framework from established multi-task training approaches commonly employed when handling multiple tasks simultaneously.  
3. Experiments are conducted across multiple computer vision tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning), but the evaluation lacks sufficiently strong baselines and demonstrates only marginal performance improvements. To validate the framework's efficacy, experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 should be included for comparison against more robust baselines.
