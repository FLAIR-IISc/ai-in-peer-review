This paper introduces a novel training methodology for ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (or other gradient-based models). The core idea is to utilize an oracle for each training instance to identify the optimal ensemble member. Backpropagation is then applied exclusively to this single, selected network. A key advantage of this approach is that it encourages the individual networks to specialize, leading to an ensemble that achieves broad coverage of the potential output space. The method is evaluated on a variety of domains, demonstrating strong overall performance.

This is a high-quality paper that clearly presents an interesting concept for training ensembles via an oracle-guided update rule. The mechanism effectively enforces specialization among the models in a classification context. The manuscript is well-written, and the experiments are comprehensive, spanning several different domains. Overall, this is a strong contribution that merits publication.

The primary limitation preventing this work from being considered outstanding is the unaddressed issue of how to exploit the resulting ensemble at inference time. The training relies on an oracle, but the paper does not develop a strategy for using the ensemble when this oracle is unavailable, beyond noting that it provides a diverse pool of results and is compatible with top-k metrics (e.g., for ImageNet). This is a significant missing component for the practical application of the proposed method. However, the contribution in its current form is still valuable, and the challenge of inference-time exploitation can be problem-specific.

On a minor note, the pseudo-code in Figure 2 is presented as a raster image. For improved clarity and quality, the authors should consider typesetting the pseudo-code directly within the paper or embedding it as a vector graphic.
