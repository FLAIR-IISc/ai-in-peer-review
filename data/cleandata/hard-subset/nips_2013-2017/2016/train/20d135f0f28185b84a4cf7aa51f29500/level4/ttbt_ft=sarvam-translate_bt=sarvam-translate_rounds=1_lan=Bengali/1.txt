This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent algorithm for ensemble model training. The authors built it based on the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], where the loss of the ensemble model is the loss of the best output of a classifier within the ensemble. This results in the creation of different classifiers. Their contribution is to suggest the use of a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training, which enables the use of this algorithm for deep neural networks. The paper is interesting and clearly written. My biggest concern is whether this algorithm is new compared to [8]. Since the authors assured me that the small change they added has made this algorithm more useful, I am in favor of adopting it. I think an interesting comparison would be to test the MCL algorithm when using batches (instead of the entire dataset). I would like to see more analysis on the "diversity" effect.
