- Proposes a novel ensemble training method where only one member is active per training example (the member with lowest loss) and is updated, yielding strong empirical results and a weak-accept recommendation.  
- Testing procedure is unclear and may improperly use ground-truth to select the best ensemble member—needs clarification.  
- The terms "independent ensembles" / "regular ensembles" (Indp.) are not defined, leaving the baseline ambiguous.  
- The claim that ensemble members specialize to different classes is unsupported given per-example reassignment; adding a specialization term to the loss could strengthen this.  
- Related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) is omitted and should be discussed.
