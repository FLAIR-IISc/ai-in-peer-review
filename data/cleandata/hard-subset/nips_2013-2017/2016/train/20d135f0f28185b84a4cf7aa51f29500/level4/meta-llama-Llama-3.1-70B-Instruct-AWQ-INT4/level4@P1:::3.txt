This paper proposes a simple yet effective solution to the multiple choice learning problem where the learners are deep networks. Current solutions to Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) do not directly extend to deep networks, and this paper shows that a simple modification to the backpropagation algorithm, which the authors term "winner-takes-the-gradient," allows them to combine the training of the learners with the assignment problem in an ensemble setting.

The problem of MCL for deep learning is of significant importance to the NIPS community, and the authors' proposed method is simple and intuitive. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of this method across a wide range of problems, including image classification, image segmentation, and captioning.

One of the strengths of this paper is its ability to tackle a complex problem with a straightforward solution. The authors' approach is easy to understand and implement, making it a valuable contribution to the field. Additionally, the experimental results are thorough and well-presented, providing a clear understanding of the method's performance.

However, one concern with this paper is the emphasis on "diversity" in the title and throughout the text. While the authors do present some analysis on the specialization of ensemble members, the model itself does not explicitly enforce diversity. This discrepancy between the paper's claims and the actual method may be misleading.

Furthermore, the proposed solution can be seen as an incremental step considering the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al. While the authors do provide some analysis on the differences between their method and existing approaches, a more detailed comparison would be beneficial.

Minor suggestions include rephrasing the first sentence of the abstract to better reflect the paper's content. Additionally, the emphasis on diversity should be toned down to avoid confusion. There are also some minor errors in the text, such as an extra period in the middle of a sentence in line 115.

One question remains regarding the baseline MCL with deep learning: how did the authors ensure that each of the networks converged to a reasonable result? Cutting the learners early on might significantly affect the ensemble performance, and a clearer explanation of this process would be helpful.

Overall, this paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of deep learning, and with some revisions to address the concerns mentioned above, it has the potential to be a strong addition to the NIPS conference.
