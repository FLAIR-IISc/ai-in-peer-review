The authors present a novel approach for generating diverse outputs through the joint training of a deep network ensemble, optimized to directly minimize oracle loss – a particularly valuable characteristic for interactive systems. The method effectively integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization strategy for minimizing MCL’s oracle loss. A key strength is the end-to-end trainability and ease of implementation within standard backpropagation frameworks. 

However, the sensitivity of the method to initialization warrants further investigation. Specifically, the impact of random initialization, as opposed to utilizing pre-trained networks, is unclear. The experimental setup and comparison to existing methods also require clarification. It would be beneficial to know if baseline methods employ the same pre-trained network used for sMCL initialization. While oracle accuracy is a useful metric, the diversity of the ensemble members themselves deserves more attention and analysis. 

Finally, the authors should consider and compare their work to related research, such as Lee et al. (2015) – “Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks” – which explores a similar concept of ensemble diversity.
