- Proposes a novel oracle-guided training method for ensembles where, per example, the oracle selects the optimal member and only that model is updated.  
- Method encourages specialization within the ensemble, producing diverse models that collectively cover varied outputs.  
- Applicable to any gradient-based model and particularly effective in classification settings.  
- Paper is well-written and presents the idea clearly and compellingly.  
- Experimental validation is thorough and shows strong performance across multiple domains.  
- Major weakness: no concrete strategy for inference-time exploitation of the trained ensemble when ground truth is unavailable.  
- Claim that the ensemble aligns with top-k evaluations (e.g., ImageNet) is insufficient as a general deployment solution.  
- Minor presentation issue: Figure 2’s pseudo-code is low-resolution; should be typeset or provided as a vector graphic.  
- Overall assessment: strong and valuable contribution to ensemble training, deserving publication despite the open inference question.
