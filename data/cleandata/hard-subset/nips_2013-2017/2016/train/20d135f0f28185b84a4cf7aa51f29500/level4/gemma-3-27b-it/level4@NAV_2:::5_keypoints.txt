- Problem: Generating multiple diverse outputs so an oracle can pick the best prediction.
- Contribution: Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) to directly minimize oracle loss for deep ensembles.
- Method: Integrates Multiple Choice Learning with deep nets using a stochastic block gradient descent for end-to-end SGD training.
- Empirical scope: Evaluated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with consistent improvements over baselines.
- Results: Strong quantitative gains and qualitative evidence of ensemble member specialization.
- Technical quality: The approach is theoretically motivated, clearly explained, and well-implemented.
- Generality: Method is loss-function agnostic and applicable to any model trained with SGD.
- Practicality: Computationally efficient and easy to integrate into existing frameworks.
- Weakness — initialization: Limited analysis of sensitivity to initialization and reliance on pre-trained networks not fully explored.
- Weakness — baselines: Unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL, affecting fairness.
- Weakness — diversity metrics: Evaluation lacks comprehensive, quantitative measures of ensemble output dissimilarity.
- Acceptance arguments: Novel, technically sound, empirically validated, practical, and significant for real-world tasks.
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient analysis of initialization effects and incomplete evaluation of ensemble diversity.
- Related work: Needs explicit comparison and discussion with Lee et al. (2015) on training diverse deep ensembles.
