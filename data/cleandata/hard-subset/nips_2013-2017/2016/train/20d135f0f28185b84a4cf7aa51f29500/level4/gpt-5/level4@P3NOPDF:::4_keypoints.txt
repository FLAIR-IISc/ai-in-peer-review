- Summary: Proposes sMCL, a stochastic variant of Multiple Choice Learning to train networks that produce M diverse predictions with the best-of-M improving performance across tasks.

- Strength: Clear and timely motivation for multiple-choice predictions and diversity in outputs.

- Strength: Simple, easy-to-implement training scheme with practical appeal.

- Strength: Empirical evaluation covers multiple computer-vision tasks, suggesting potential generality.

- Weakness: Limited novelty—appears to be an NN instantiation of prior MCL with minor optimization tweaks and unclear fundamental differences.

- Weakness: Insufficient technical detail—Section 3 is too short; objective, optimization, stability, and theoretical grounding are under-specified.

- Weakness: Missing strong baselines—no comparisons to multi-head averaged-loss training, alternating training, mixture-of-experts with gating, or standard ensembles.

- Weakness: Incomplete evaluation—lacks comparisons on widely used benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012 for segmentation) and competitive baselines.

- Weakness: No ablations or analyses—missing studies on M, assignment strategy (hard vs soft), diversity metrics, parameter sharing, initialization sensitivity, computational overhead, and convergence.

- Weakness: Writing and organization issues—paper is hard to follow in places, with grammatical problems and insufficient technical exposition.

- Request: Explicitly compare and formalize how sMCL differs from [8] and relate to mixture-of-experts, ensembles, and diversity-promoting methods.

- Request: Precisely define loss and assignment rules (per sample/mini-batch backprop, tie handling), hard vs soft assignments, and measures to avoid head collapse.

- Request: Describe regularization for diversity/load balancing, provide pseudocode, and give computational/memory complexity relative to independent models and shared-trunk multi-head baselines.

- Request: Add strong baselines: multi-head averaged loss, round-robin training, independent ensembles with equal budget, and MoE with gating.

- Request: Clarify evaluation protocol—oracle (best-of-M) vs practical selector at test time—and report non-oracle performance with learned/heuristic selectors.

- Request: Provide ablations varying M, per-head capacity, parameter sharing, seeds, report diversity metrics and statistical significance/confidence intervals.

- Request: Discuss practical aspects—convergence properties, hyperparameter sensitivity, runtime overhead, and failure modes like head collapse.

- Request: Improve Section 3 with full derivations, clearer notation, limitations subsection, and a stronger related-work discussion.

- Overall assessment: Motivation is sound and idea is appealing but contribution seems incremental and exposition/experiments are insufficient for acceptance.

- Recommendation: Reject (weak reject); major revisions needed to clarify novelty and strengthen experiments.

- Confidence: Medium.
