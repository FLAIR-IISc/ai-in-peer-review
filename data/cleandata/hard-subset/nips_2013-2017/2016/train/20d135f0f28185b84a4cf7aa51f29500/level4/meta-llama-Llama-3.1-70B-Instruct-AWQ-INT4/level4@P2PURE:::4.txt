This paper presents a stochastic gradient descent method with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). The authors claim that their proposed framework offers advantages in various tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, I have several concerns regarding the novelty and presentation of this work.

Firstly, the stochastic MCL (sMCL) method appears to be an incremental extension of existing ideas. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it cannot be considered a significant contribution of this work. In essence, this paper presents a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments, which raises questions about its originality.

Secondly, the writing style of the paper is not engaging, and the technical section (Section 3) is surprisingly brief, spanning only one page. A crucial aspect that is not adequately addressed is how the proposed framework differs from alternative training methods that are widely adopted when multiple tasks are trained together.

Thirdly, the authors conduct experiments on various computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, these experiments lack strong baselines, and the performance improvements are relatively small. To convincingly demonstrate the efficiency of their framework, the authors should consider performing experiments on the PASCAL VOC 2012 dataset to compare with more robust baselines.
