- Addresses multiple-choice learning (MCL) for deep neural networks, noting existing MCL methods don't directly extend to deep learning.  
- Proposes a winner-takes-all gradient mechanism integrated into backprop to train learners and resolve assignments simultaneously.  
- Reports strong empirical performance on image classification, image segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Strength: problem is significant and relevant to the NIPS community.  
- Strength: proposed method is notably simple.  
- Strength: experiments validate performance across three distinct tasks.  
- Weakness: title and emphasis stress "diversity" despite no explicit diversity-enforcing mechanism in the model.  
- Weakness: contribution appears only a minor incremental step beyond Guzman et al.'s relaxation framework.  
- Minor suggestion: rewrite the first sentence of the abstract for clarity.  
- Minor suggestion: reduce emphasis on "diversity" throughout the paper.
