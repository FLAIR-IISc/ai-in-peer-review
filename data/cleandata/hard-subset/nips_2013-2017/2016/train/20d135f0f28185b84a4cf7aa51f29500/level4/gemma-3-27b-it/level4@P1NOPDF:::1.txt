This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm for training ensemble models. The authors build upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], which focuses on minimizing the loss of the best output from a single classifier within an ensemble – a strategy designed to yield a diverse set of classifiers. The key contribution of this work is demonstrating how to effectively apply SGD to this loss function, enabling its use with deep neural networks.

The paper is interesting and clearly written. My primary concern revolves around the degree of novelty; it feels incremental compared to the work in [8]. However, the authors persuasively argue that their addition significantly enhances the algorithm's practical utility, and therefore I am inclined to accept the paper. 

I believe a compelling addition would be a comparison of their algorithm’s performance to the MCL algorithm when using mini-batches (rather than the entire dataset). Furthermore, I would appreciate more in-depth analysis regarding the observed “diversity” effect – quantifying and understanding how the algorithm promotes diversity among the ensemble members would strengthen the paper.
