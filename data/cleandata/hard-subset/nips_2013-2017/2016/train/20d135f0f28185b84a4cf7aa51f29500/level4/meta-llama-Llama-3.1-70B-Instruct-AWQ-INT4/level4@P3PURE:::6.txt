This paper proposes an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners, distinct from previous approaches in that only one learner of the ensemble is active for each training example. During training, the member with the lowest loss with respect to the true label is identified and updated with the gradient. Although the idea is simple, it yields surprisingly good experimental results and addresses some limitations of existing methods, which is why I recommend it for weak acceptance.

However, there are several aspects of the paper that require clarification or improvement. Firstly, the testing phase is not clearly explained, and it appears that the authors use the ground truth to find the best member, as mentioned in line 159 on page 5. Secondly, the paper refers to 'independent ensembles' and 'regular ensembles' (denoted as 'Indp.' in figures and line 181 on page 5), but does not provide a clear explanation for this baseline.

Furthermore, the authors repeatedly emphasize the 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes. However, the reassignment of examples to learners during training is done on a per-example basis, which undermines this argument. To strengthen this claim, the authors could consider incorporating specialization into the loss function. Additionally, the paper does not discuss the related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), which is a more rigorous approach that has yet to be explored in the deep learning setting.
