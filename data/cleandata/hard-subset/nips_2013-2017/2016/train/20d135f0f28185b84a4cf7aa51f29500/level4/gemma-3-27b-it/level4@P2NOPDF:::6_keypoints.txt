- Proposes a novel ensemble training method that activates and trains only the single ensemble member with lowest loss per example.  
- Empirically strong performance leads the reviewer to recommend weak acceptance.  
- Testing procedure is unclear; it seems to use ground-truth labels to pick the best member and needs an explicit description.  
- Definitions for baseline comparisons (“independent ensembles” / “regular ensembles”) are missing and must be clearly specified.  
- The claim that members “specialize” to different classes is weakly supported because per-example reassignment does not inherently encourage specialization.  
- Suggests exploring explicit specialization via a regularization term or loss modification to encourage distinct representations across members.  
- Paper omits discussion of diversity-regularization related work (e.g., Chen et al.); a comparison and contextualization are needed.  
- Addressing these clarifications and comparisons would significantly improve clarity and impact.
