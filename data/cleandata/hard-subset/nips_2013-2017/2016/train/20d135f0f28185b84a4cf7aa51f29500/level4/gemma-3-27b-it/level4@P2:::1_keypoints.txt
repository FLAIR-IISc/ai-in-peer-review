- Introduces sMCL, an SGD-based adaptation of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for training diverse deep ensembles that minimizes the loss of the best ensemble member.  
- Addresses practicality by enabling efficient, scalable training of deep ensembles with SGD.  
- Method is general and agnostic to network architecture and loss function.  
- Strong empirical results showing consistent improvements across image classification, segmentation, and captioning with varied architectures.  
- Provides interpretable analysis: ensemble member specialization and compelling visualizations showing captured ambiguity.  
- Well-written and clearly explains motivation and technical details.  
- Core contribution is incremental, building directly on Guzman-Rivera et al. [8] with SGD as the primary modification.  
- Would benefit from a deeper theoretical discussion of differences between sMCL and prior MCL.  
- Lacks a direct comparison to MCL run with mini-batches (batched MCL) to isolate the impact of the SGD adaptation.  
- Needs more quantitative diversity analysis (e.g., n-gram diversity or semantic-similarity metrics) for generated outputs.  
- Arguments for acceptance: solves a practical limitation, strong and consistent empirical evidence, broadly applicable, and offers insightful ensemble analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: algorithmic novelty is modest and the missing batched-MCL comparison limits understanding of the key contribution.  
- Overall assessment: Despite incremental novelty, the practical gains and empirical strength justify acceptance.
