- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic SGD-based method to train diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss for multiple-hypothesis outputs.  
- Paper clearly explains the problem, approach, and relation to prior work.  
- Writing and organization are good, with sufficient background and references.  
- Technical content is sound and supported by theoretical analysis and experiments.  
- Demonstrates broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL across tasks and architectures.  
- Strength: method is simple, general, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and parameter-free.  
- Strength: analysis shows emergent ensemble member specialization and expertise during training.  
- Weakness: testing phase description is unclear—use of ground truth to pick best member is not explicitly stated.  
- Weakness: unclear definitions/explanations of baseline terms "independent ensembles" and "regular ensembles."  
- Weakness: claim of class-wise specialization is unconvincing because training reassignment is per-example; suggests incorporating specialization into the loss.  
- Weakness: related work on diversity regularization in neural networks is not discussed.  
- Originality/significance: sMCL is a notable contribution, though the winner-take-gradient idea is not entirely novel; overall recommendation: weak accept.
