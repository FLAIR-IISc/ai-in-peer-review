- Proposes an ensemble training method that activates and updates only the lowest-loss member per training example, aiming for efficiency and specialization.
- Shows strong empirical performance across benchmarks, particularly under constrained computation.
- Major concern: Inference procedure is unclear and appears to rely on ground-truth labels; authors must clarify how members are selected at test time without labels (e.g., confidence-based selection).
- Major concern: The "independent ensembles" (Indp.) baseline is undefined; the paper must state how this baseline is constructed and combined.
- Major concern: The claim that members specialize by class is insufficiently justified—per-example lowest-loss assignment does not inherently produce class specialization without theoretical or empirical evidence.
- Major concern: The paper omits related work on diversity regularization (e.g., Chen et al. 2015) and should discuss how the proposed method relates to or differs from diversity-focused approaches.
- Strengths: Elegant, computationally efficient idea with convincing empirical gains.
- Weaknesses: Key gaps in test-time description, baseline definition, specialization justification, and literature context prevent full assessment.
- Recommendation: Weak accept contingent on addressing the four major issues (testing ambiguity, baseline definition, specialization evidence, and related work).
