The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim that the proposed framework offers advantages in multiple tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning.

However, this paper demonstrates that the stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) method is somewhat incremental in novelty. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it cannot be considered a significant contribution of this work. Essentially, this work is a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments.

Furthermore, the paper is not well-written. The technical section (Section 3) is only one page long, which raises concerns about the thoroughness of the explanation. A crucial aspect that is not clearly addressed is the difference between the proposed framework and alternative training methods, which are widely adopted when training multiple tasks together.

The authors conduct experiments on various computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, these experiments lack strong baselines, and the improvement in performance is relatively small. To convincingly demonstrate the efficiency of their framework, the authors should perform experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 to compare with more robust baselines.
