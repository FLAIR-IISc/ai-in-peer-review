- Novel, well-explained method: oracle-guided selective backpropagation to train ensemble members.  
- Encourages effective specialization: members become experts on different data subsets, improving diversity.  
- Strong empirical support: comprehensive evaluation across multiple domains showing good performance.  
- Clear, high-quality presentation and writing.  
- Major omission: no effective strategy provided for inference-time model selection or aggregation without the oracle.  
- Underdeveloped discussion on how to combine specialists’ outputs; simple averaging may be suboptimal.  
- Suggest exploring gating networks or meta-learners to predict which specialist to trust at test time.  
- Need for general principles or baseline inference approaches to make the method practically applicable.  
- Minor issue: low-resolution raster pseudo-code (Figure 2); should be typeset or provided as a vector graphic.  
- Overall recommendation: strong paper, recommended for acceptance; address inference-time usage to strengthen impact.
