The paper proposes a novel method for training ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), or more generally, any gradient-based approach. The method utilizes an oracle – which identifies the optimal network within the ensemble for each training instance – and applies backpropagation only to that selected network. This approach encourages specialization among the networks, resulting in an ensemble that effectively covers a wide range of possible outputs. The authors report strong performance across a variety of domains, and I believe this is a good paper. It clearly presents an interesting idea: training a DNN ensemble with an oracle to guide backpropagation updates to the network producing the best output (according to the ground truth label). The results suggest this method effectively enforces specialization, particularly in a classification setting. 

The overall presentation is excellent, and the experiments are conducted on several datasets. Globally, this is a strong contribution that deserves publication. I would have considered it outstanding had the paper also addressed the practical exploitation of the resulting DNN ensemble. Currently, the training process leverages the oracle, but the use of the trained ensemble itself is not further developed, beyond stating that it provides a diverse pool of potential results and is consistent with top-k metrics used in problems like ImageNet. This is a significant omission that hinders the full usability of the proposed approach in practice. However, the core contribution remains valuable even in its current state, as addressing the ensemble utilization is relatively problem-specific.

Finally, Fig. 2 presents pseudo-code as raster images embedded within the paper. It would be preferable to include the pseudo-code directly in the paper text, or at the very least, embed the figure as a vector graphic for improved clarity and scalability.
