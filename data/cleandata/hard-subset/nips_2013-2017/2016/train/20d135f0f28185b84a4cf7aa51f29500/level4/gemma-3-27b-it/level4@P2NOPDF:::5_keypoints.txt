- Proposes a novel method to generate diverse ensemble outputs by directly minimizing oracle loss for interactive systems.  
- Integrates deep learning with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent for the non-convex objective.  
- Method is compatible with standard backprop, allowing end-to-end training.  
- Experiments on three tasks demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Concern: method may be sensitive to initialization; authors use pre-trained networks but do not evaluate random initialization.  
- Need clarification whether baseline methods used the same pre-trained initialization for fair comparison.  
- Evaluation focuses only on oracle accuracy, which does not fully capture ensemble benefits.  
- Recommend reporting ensemble diversity metrics (e.g., average pairwise feature distance or prediction variance).  
- Related work coverage is incomplete; must compare and contrast with Lee et al. (2015) "Why M Heads are Better than One."  
- Overall promising, but addressing initialization, diversity analysis, and related-work comparison would strengthen the paper.
