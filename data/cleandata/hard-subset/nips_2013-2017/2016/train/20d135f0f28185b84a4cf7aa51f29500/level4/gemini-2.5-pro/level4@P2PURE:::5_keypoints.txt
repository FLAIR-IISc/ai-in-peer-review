- Proposal: integrates deep networks into the Minimum Conditional Loss (MCL) paradigm and uses stochastic block gradient descent to jointly train an ensemble that directly minimizes oracle loss.  
- Strength: optimization integrates seamlessly with standard back-propagation, allowing end-to-end training.  
- Empirical result: experiments on three tasks demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Weakness: importance of network initialization is not addressed; unclear how random vs. pre-trained initialization affects performance.  
- Weakness: baseline comparisons lack experimental detail—unclear whether baselines used the same pre-trained initialization, raising fairness concerns.  
- Weakness: evaluation relies on oracle accuracy but omits analysis of diversity among ensemble members.  
- Suggestion: include diversity analysis of ensemble outputs to better characterize benefits.  
- Suggestion: acknowledge and compare to related work on diverse ensembles, e.g., Lee et al. (2015) “Why M Heads are Better than One.”
