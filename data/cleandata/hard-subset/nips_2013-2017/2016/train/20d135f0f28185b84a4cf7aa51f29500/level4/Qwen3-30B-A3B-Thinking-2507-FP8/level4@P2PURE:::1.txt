

This paper introduces a novel stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models. The authors extend the framework of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], in which the ensemble loss is defined as the loss corresponding to the best-performing single classifier within the ensemble—resulting in diverse classifiers. Their key contribution is adapting this approach via stochastic gradient descent, enabling application to deep neural networks. The paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and presents an interesting method. My primary concern is whether the algorithmic novelty is merely incremental compared to [8]. However, the authors have convincingly demonstrated that their relatively minor modification significantly enhances practicality, leading me to support acceptance. I recommend including a comparison of the MCL algorithm under mini-batch training (as opposed to full-batch training) to better assess scalability. Additionally, a more thorough quantitative analysis of the diversity effect would strengthen the paper's claims.
