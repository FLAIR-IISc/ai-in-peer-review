- Novel contribution: Proposes a simple, elegant ensemble training algorithm that updates only the single ensemble member with lowest loss per example and shows strong empirical performance.
- Testing-phase clarity: Inference procedure is unclear and wording suggests (invalid) use of ground-truth to select the best member at test time; authors must clarify how predictions are made on unseen data.
- Undefined baseline: The "independent/regular ensembles (Indp.)" baseline is not defined in the text and needs a clear description for fair comparison.
- Specialization claim weak: The paper's claim that members specialize by class is unconvincing given per-example assignment; consider explicitly encouraging class-based specialization in the loss.
- Missing related work: Omits relevant literature on diversity regularization in ensembles (e.g., Chen et al.) and should discuss or compare to these methods.
- Overall verdict: Simple and effective idea with valuable contribution; reviewer leans weak accept contingent on addressing the above issues.
