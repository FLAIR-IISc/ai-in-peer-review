- Proposes sMCL, a simple, parameter-free "winner-take-gradient" SGD method to directly minimize oracle loss for ensembles of deep nets.  
- Makes MCL practical for deep networks by enabling concurrent, end-to-end training rather than block coordinate descent.  
- Addresses a significant, practical problem: producing diverse multiple outputs for ambiguous tasks where a downstream agent selects the correct hypothesis.  
- Algorithm is architecture- and loss-agnostic and is demonstrated on classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Empirically outperforms baselines (independent ensembles, original MCL, Dey et al.), and is ~5× faster than original MCL, with compelling qualitative specialization.  
- Major omission: fails to discuss or compare with closely related contemporaneous work by Lee et al. (2015) that uses a similar winner-take-all specialization.  
- Requests clarification on experimental setup and initialization, especially whether sMCL requires pre-training and whether baselines used the same initialization.  
- Suggests adding quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise disagreement) to complement oracle-performance results.  
- Overall judgment: well-written, effective, and broadly applicable contribution; recommended for acceptance subject to addressing the related-work, initialization, and diversity-evaluation concerns.
