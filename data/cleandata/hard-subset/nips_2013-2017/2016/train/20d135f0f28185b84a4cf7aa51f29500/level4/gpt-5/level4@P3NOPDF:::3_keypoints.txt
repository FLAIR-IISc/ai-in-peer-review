- Proposes a “winner-takes-the-gradient” (WTG) rule: for each example, only the ensemble member that currently predicts best receives the gradient update, enabling end-to-end MCL with SGD.  
- Strength: Addresses an important MCL problem with broad relevance to ensemble and multi-output learning.  
- Strength: Method is conceptually simple, easy to implement, and integrates into standard deep learning pipelines.  
- Strength: Evaluated across three domains (classification, segmentation, captioning), suggesting broad applicability.  
- Weakness: Claims of promoting “diversity” are misleading because no explicit diversity objective or constraint is used; diversity emerges only implicitly.  
- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—mainly an engineering adaptation of prior MCL/hard-assignment ideas to deep nets without deeper theoretical insight.  
- Weakness: Analysis is shallow; lacks measures showing when WTG yields useful specialization versus mode collapse.  
- Suggestion: Tone down “diversity” claims or provide explicit diversity metrics (pairwise disagreement, KL divergence, assignment entropy/coverage).  
- Suggestion: Include ablations comparing hard WTG to soft/temperature-controlled assignments to justify hard updates.  
- Suggestion: Compare against stronger ensemble and MoE baselines (independent inits, bagging, snapshot ensembles, learned/fixed gating, loss top-k).  
- Suggestion: Report and equalize training budgets and stopping criteria to ensure fair baseline comparisons.  
- Suggestion: Provide per-network training curves and assignment histograms over epochs to demonstrate stable specialization and rule out single-model dominance.  
- Suggestion: Discuss failure modes and mitigation strategies (warm-up, balanced sampling, periodic reassignment).  
- Suggestion: Relate WTG to hard EM / mixture-of-experts literature and discuss implications for convergence and bias–variance trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: Add a small synthetic experiment illustrating specialization across data modes.  
- Minor edits: Rewrite the abstract for clarity; reduce repeated use of “diversity”; fix typos (Line 108: “f”→“g”, Line 115 extra period).  
- Minor edits: Move concise description of loss and assignment rule into the main text and ensure consistent, unambiguous notation.  
- Questions for authors: Clarify baseline training details—per-learner stopping criteria, training budget, early stopping policies, and measures used to prevent under-training of some learners.  
- Overall: Practical and easy-to-implement contribution with promising results, but needs reframing around diversity, stronger baselines, and deeper analysis of specialization and stability.
