- Proposes sMCL: a novel stochastic multiple choice learning method to train diverse deep ensembles by minimizing the oracle loss.  
- Oracle loss is valuable for user-facing systems because it lets models output multiple likely hypotheses rather than a single prediction.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness on three tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Main technical contribution: a stochastic block SGD optimization to minimize oracle loss, generalizable to any SGD-trained model.  
- Empirical analysis shows automatic emergence of ensemble member specialization and expertise under sMCL.  
- Strengths: clear, concise writing; thorough evaluation; interpretable results.  
- Compares in detail to classical ensembles, MCL, and the method of Dey et al.  
- Lacks analysis of model initialization: unclear how crucial pre-training is versus random initialization.  
- Lacks detailed quantitative evaluation of ensemble output diversity beyond specialization observations.  
- Omits comparison to a closely related method by Stefan Lee et al. ("Why M Heads are Better than One").  
- NIPS scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.  
- Recommendation: Accept, with suggested revisions to address initialization, diversity analysis, and missing comparison.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel approach, thorough evaluation, clear exposition.  
- Arguments against acceptance: missing analyses on initialization, diversity, and comparison to Lee et al.
