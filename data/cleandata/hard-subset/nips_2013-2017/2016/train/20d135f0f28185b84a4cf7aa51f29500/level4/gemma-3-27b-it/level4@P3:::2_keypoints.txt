- Proposes sMCL, a novel method that trains ensembles by routing gradients to the network producing the best prediction via an oracle.  
- Encourages specialization and diversity among ensemble members, improving oracle performance and interpretability.  
- Implements training as stochastic block gradient descent, yielding a parameter-free, computationally efficient approach.  
- Architecture- and loss-agnostic, demonstrated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Strong empirical results showing consistent outperformance of baseline ensembles and emergence of meaningful specialization.  
- Well-motivated, clearly written, and technically well-explained.  
- Strength: Novel approach that directly optimizes oracle performance and promotes diversity.  
- Strength: Computationally efficient and practical for large deep networks.  
- Strength: Broad applicability across architectures and tasks.  
- Strength: Clear qualitative examples illustrating interpretable specialization.  
- Weakness: Limited discussion of how to utilize ensemble outputs in downstream applications (beyond selecting the best).  
- Weakness: Poor figure quality (pseudo-code embedded as a raster image) harms readability.  
- Weakness: Related work could better connect to diversity-promoting methods like VAEs and GANs.  
- Acceptance arguments: Significant problem addressed, convincing experiments, solid theory, and strong fit for the venue.  
- Rejection arguments: Insufficient exploration of practical ensemble usage and minor presentation issues (fixable).  
- Overall: Strong contribution recommended for acceptance with minor revisions to improve figure quality and expand discussion of ensemble utilization.
