The paper proposes a novel method for training an ensemble of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or any other gradient-based approach, leveraging an oracle that knows which ensemble member is optimal for each training instance. By applying backpropagation only to the network with the selected output, this approach enables the networks to specialize in specific themes, resulting in ensembles that cover a broad range of possible outputs of interest. The authors report impressive results across various domains. Overall, I think this is a strong paper that presents an interesting idea for training DNN ensembles with an oracle-guided backpropagation update to the network with the best output, according to the true label. This approach effectively enforces specialization in a classification context. The presentation is clear, and the experiments are comprehensive, spanning multiple domains.

Globally, this is a solid work that deserves publication. However, I would have considered this paper outstanding if it had also addressed the issue of exploiting ensembles of DNNs trained with the proposed approach. Currently, the training process utilizes the oracle, but the use of the resulting ensemble is not further developed beyond stating that it can provide a broad pool of possible results and that the approach is coherent with the top-k metric proposed for certain problems, such as ImageNet. This is an important missing component for making the proposed approach fully usable in practice. Nevertheless, the contribution is significant in its current form, with the missing part being relatively problem-specific.

One minor suggestion is to improve the presentation of Fig. 2, which is currently a raster image embedded in the paper. It would be more readable and professional to typeset the pseudo-code directly within the paper or, at the very least, embed the pseudo-code figure as a vector image.
