- sMCL method: winner-take-gradient training where, per instance, only the ensemble member with lowest loss is backpropagated to, directly optimizing oracle loss and encouraging specialization.

- Method is simple, intuitive, and elegant, allowing concurrent training of all ensemble members rather than sequential/iterative procedures.

- Demonstrated versatility across tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning) and architectures (CNNs, FCNs, CNN+RNNs).

- Strong empirical performance: consistently and significantly outperforms independent ensembles and boosting-like baselines.

- Analysis convincingly shows induced specialization among ensemble members, supporting the core claims.

- Experiments are thorough and comprehensive, demonstrating broad applicability.

- Presentation is clear and well-written, with precise method description and effective result presentation.

- Main weakness: no detailed guidance or automated strategies for inference-time selection/combination of outputs when a ground-truth oracle is unavailable.

- Minor presentation issue: pseudo-code figure is low-resolution and should be typeset or provided as a vector graphic.

- Overall conclusion: a significant, practical, and novel contribution to training diverse deep ensembles; recommendation for acceptance.
