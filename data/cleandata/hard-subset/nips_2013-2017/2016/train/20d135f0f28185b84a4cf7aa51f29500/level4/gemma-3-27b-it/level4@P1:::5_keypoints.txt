- Proposes sMCL: jointly learns an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs.  
- Applies method to image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with positive empirical results.  
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization that integrates easily into backprop for end-to-end training.  
- Claims computational efficiency and practical implementability within existing frameworks.  
- Does not investigate sensitivity to initialization (pre-trained vs random weights), which could affect performance.  
- Experimental details about baseline initialization are missing—unclear if baselines used the same pre-trained networks.  
- Lacks quantitative analysis of ensemble diversity; no metrics (e.g., cosine similarity, n-gram overlap) reported.  
- Fails to adequately compare to related work, notably Lee et al. (2015) on training diverse ensembles.  
- Strength: addresses practical problem of generating diverse plausible hypotheses for downstream processes.  
- Strength: sMCL is novel and theoretically well-motivated.  
- Strength: method is computationally efficient and easy to implement.  
- Strength: experiments show effectiveness across tasks and architectures.  
- Weakness: initialization sensitivity not explored.  
- Weakness: insufficient baseline setup details for fair comparison.  
- Weakness: insufficient quantitative diversity analysis of ensemble members.  
- Weakness: omission of relevant related work discussion (e.g., Lee et al. 2015).  
- Argument for acceptance: novel, sound approach with promising experimental results and practicality.  
- Argument against acceptance: missing analyses on initialization, diversity, and related-work comparisons weaken conclusions.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to add experimental details, initialization sensitivity study, diversity metrics, and related-work discussion.
