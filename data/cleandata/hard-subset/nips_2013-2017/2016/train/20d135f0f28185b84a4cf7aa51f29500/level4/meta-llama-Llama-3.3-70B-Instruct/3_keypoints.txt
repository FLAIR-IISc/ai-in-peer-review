- Proposes a simple winner-takes-the-gradient layer to integrate learner training with assignment for multiple-choice learning in deep networks.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness across image classification, segmentation, and captioning tasks.  
- Relevant to the NIPS community due to the importance of multiple-choice learning in deep learning.  
- Methodology is commendably simple.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, covering three distinct tasks.  
- Major weakness: paper emphasizes "diversity" but does not explicitly enforce it in the model, making the title/motivation misleading.  
- Incremental contribution, especially given the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.  
- Suggestion: rewrite the abstract's first sentence.  
- Suggestion: temper claims about diversity to match the model's actual capabilities.  
- Typos: incorrect 'f' vs 'g' on line 108 and an extra period on line 115.  
- Open question: how did the authors ensure each baseline MCL network converged and avoid premature termination affecting ensemble performance?
