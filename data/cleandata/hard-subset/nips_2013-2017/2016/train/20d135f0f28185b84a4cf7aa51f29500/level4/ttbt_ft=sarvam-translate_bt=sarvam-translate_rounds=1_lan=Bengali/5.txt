The authors have proposed a method for generating multiple outputs. In this method, a ensemble of deep networks jointly learns to minimize the Oracle loss, which is useful for interacting with users. Three Oracle-related tests demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. This paper combines deep networks with MCL instances and proposes a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method for minimizing the Oracle loss of MCL. The proposed optimization method can be easily applied within a back-propagation learning framework, and the entire model can be trained end-to-end. My concern is how important initialization is? What if the models are initialized with random values instead of pre-trained networks? The authors do not explain much about the setting for comparison. Do the authors use the same pre-trained network as sMCL for initialization? The authors use Oracle accuracy as a metric for evaluation. What about the diversity of ensemble members? I hope to see more analysis on this topic. Also, there is a work with a similar concept that the authors have proposed for comparison. Stefan Li, Senthil Purushwalayam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, Why M Heads Are Better Than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks. CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015)
