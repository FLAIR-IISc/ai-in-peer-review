- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs.  
- Integrates Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) with deep networks via a novel stochastic block gradient descent.  
- Optimization method integrates with back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training.  
- Demonstrates effectiveness through oracle-based experiments on three tasks.  
- Requires ablation on initialization to assess sensitivity to random vs. pre-trained starting weights.  
- Needs clarification whether baseline methods used the same pre-trained initialization for fair comparison.  
- Evaluation is limited to oracle accuracy and should include analysis of ensemble diversity.  
- Omits discussion and comparison with closely related work by Lee et al. (2015) on training diverse ensembles.
