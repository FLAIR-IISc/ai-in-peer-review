This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models, building upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al [8]. The authors' key insight is to leverage the loss of the best output of a single classifier in the ensemble, resulting in a diverse set of classifiers. Their primary contribution lies in adapting this approach for use with stochastic gradient descent, thereby enabling its application to deep neural networks. The paper is well-written and engaging.

However, my main concern is whether the algorithmic novelty presented in this paper is merely an incremental improvement upon the work of [8]. After careful consideration, I am convinced that the authors' modification significantly enhances the usefulness of the algorithm, which inclines me towards acceptance. To further strengthen the paper, I suggest an interesting comparison: evaluating the MCL algorithm with batch processing, rather than using the entire dataset. Additionally, I would appreciate more in-depth analysis on the "diversity" effect achieved by the proposed algorithm.
