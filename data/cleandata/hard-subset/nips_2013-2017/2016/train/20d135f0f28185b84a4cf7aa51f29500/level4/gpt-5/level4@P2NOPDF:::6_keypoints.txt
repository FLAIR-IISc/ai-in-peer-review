- Paper proposes a winner-takes-update ensemble training rule: update only the ensemble member with lowest loss per training example.  
- Method is simple, computationally efficient per example, and shows strong empirical performance.  
- Conceptually minimal overhead compared to training independent models.  
- Test-time procedure is unclear — clarify how predictions are made without ground-truth, the selection/aggregation rule used, and computational cost; ensure reported results use that procedure.  
- Baselines need precise definitions: how they are trained (data splits, seeds, optimization), whether parameters/training budgets/model counts are matched, and what inference rule (averaging, majority vote, etc.) is used.  
- Claims of class-wise specialization are weakly supported; provide quantitative analyses (per-member class distributions, confusion matrices, entropy/calibration) or incorporate specialization/diversity into the objective.  
- Related work on diversity regularization (e.g., Chen et al.) is under-discussed; compare conceptually and empirically, and discuss whether per-example selection implicitly promotes diversity.  
- Addressing the above clarifications would substantially improve clarity and impact.  
- Overall: neat, practical idea with promising results; reviewer recommends weak accept.
