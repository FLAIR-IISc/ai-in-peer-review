This paper introduces a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating multiple choice learning (MCL). The authors assert its benefits across diverse tasks including classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, the novelty of the proposed sMCL method appears incremental. While the motivation for employing MCL is readily understandable, it doesn’t represent a significant contribution in itself; the work largely presents a neural network implementation of the approach described in [8], with some modifications.

The paper’s presentation suffers from a lack of clarity and detail. Critically, the technical description of the method (Section 3) is concise, occupying only one page. This brevity hinders understanding. Furthermore, the paper fails to adequately differentiate the proposed framework from established multi-task training strategies that are already widely utilized. A clearer explanation of the advantages of sMCL *over* these alternatives is necessary.

The authors evaluate sMCL on a variety of computer vision tasks – classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the experimental evaluation is weak. The baselines chosen are not sufficiently strong, and the reported performance improvements are modest. To convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of this framework, the authors should include a more rigorous comparison to state-of-the-art methods, specifically on a standard benchmark like PASCAL VOC 2012. This would allow for a fair assessment of its performance relative to established techniques.
