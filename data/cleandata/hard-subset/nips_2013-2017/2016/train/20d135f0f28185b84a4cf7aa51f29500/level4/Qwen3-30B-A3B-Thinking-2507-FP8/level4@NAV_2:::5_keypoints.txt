- Introduces sMCL: a novel, simple, architecture-agnostic, parameter-free method to train deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss.  
- Effectively extends Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to deep networks with a stochastic block gradient descent enabling end-to-end training.  
- Empirical results on classification, segmentation, and captioning show sMCL outperforms baselines on oracle metrics and is ~5x faster than MCL.  
- Concern: Impact of initialization is not analyzed—unclear sensitivity to random vs. pre-trained weight initialization.  
- Concern: Baseline initialization protocols are not specified; differences could bias comparative results.  
- Concern: Paper lacks quantitative and qualitative analysis of ensemble diversity to support claims of producing diverse, interpretable solutions.  
- Concern: Missing comparison to related work by Lee et al. (2015) on training diverse deep ensembles, which is necessary to establish novelty.  
- Overall: Promising contribution, but addressing initialization, diversity analysis, and related-work comparison would strengthen the paper.
