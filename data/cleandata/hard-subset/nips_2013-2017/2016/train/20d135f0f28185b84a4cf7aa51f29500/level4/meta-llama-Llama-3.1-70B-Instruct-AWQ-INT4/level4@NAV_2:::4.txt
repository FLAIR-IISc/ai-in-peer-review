This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent based approach to minimize the loss with respect to an oracle, introducing Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) for training diverse deep ensembles. The authors' main contribution lies in the formulation of a stochastic block gradient descent optimization approach well-suited to minimizing the oracle loss in ensembles of deep networks. The proposed method is applicable to any model trained with stochastic gradient descent, is agnostic to the form of the task-dependent loss, is parameter-free, and is time efficient, training all ensemble members concurrently.

The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL for training diverse deep ensembles on a wide range of problem domains and network architectures, including Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) ensembles for image classification, Fully Convolutional Network (FCN) ensembles for semantic segmentation, and combined CNN and Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) ensembles for image captioning. The method outperforms existing baselines and produces sets of outputs with high oracle performance.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear formulation of the problem and the proposed solution. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the technical part of the work (Section 3) is quite brief and could be expanded to provide more details on the proposed framework.

Clarity: The paper is well-organized, but the writing could be improved. The authors could provide more explanations and examples to help readers understand the proposed method and its advantages. The paper could also benefit from more detailed comparisons with alternative training methods.

Originality: While the authors build upon the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm, the proposed stochastic gradient descent based approach is novel and provides a significant improvement over existing methods. However, the authors could provide more discussion on the differences between their approach and alternative training methods.

Significance: The results presented in the paper demonstrate the potential of the proposed method for training diverse deep ensembles. The authors show that sMCL can be applied to a wide range of problem domains and network architectures, and that it outperforms existing baselines. However, the improvement in performance is not always significant, and the authors could provide more analysis on the benefits and limitations of their approach.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent based approach for training diverse deep ensembles.
* The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of the proposed method on a wide range of problem domains and network architectures.
* The method outperforms existing baselines and produces sets of outputs with high oracle performance.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The technical part of the work (Section 3) is quite brief and could be expanded to provide more details on the proposed framework.
* The paper could benefit from more detailed comparisons with alternative training methods.
* The improvement in performance is not always significant, and the authors could provide more analysis on the benefits and limitations of their approach.
