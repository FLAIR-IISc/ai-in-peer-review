- Proposes an SGD-based training procedure for ensembles built on Guzman-Rivera et al.'s framework, where ensemble loss is the per-example loss of the best-performing classifier to encourage diversity.
- Key contribution: replacing the original training scheme with SGD to make the approach practical and scalable for deep neural networks.
- Paper is clearly written and tackles a relevant problem.
- Primary concern: the algorithmic change relative to Guzman-Rivera et al. appears incremental.
- Empirical results convincingly demonstrate improved usability and scalability, leading the reviewer to lean toward acceptance.
- Suggestion: compare to MCL when trained with mini-batches rather than using the entire dataset at once.
- Suggestion: include a more thorough analysis of diversity with quantitative metrics and ablations to show how diversity is induced and how it correlates with ensemble accuracy.
