- Novelty: Contribution is incremental—sMCL reads as a neural-network instantiation of prior work ([8]) with minor adjustments, not a fundamentally new approach.
- Clarity and technical depth: Paper is poorly written and technically thin; Section 3 is too short and omits key algorithmic details, training dynamics, and justification.
- Comparison to existing training schemes: It’s unclear how sMCL differs from or improves upon standard alternating/joint training; concrete algorithmic, computational, or empirical advantages are not demonstrated.
- Experiments and baselines: Empirical evaluation is underpowered with small gains and weak baselines; needs stronger SOTA comparisons and benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012 for segmentation).
- Overall assessment: Potentially useful idea hampered by incremental novelty, insufficient exposition, and weak experiments; stronger technical clarity and baselines required.
