- Proposal: sMCL — a stochastic “winner-take-gradient” method to minimize oracle loss by training ensemble members concurrently with standard SGD.  
- Practical benefit: Makes MCL practical for deep nets, avoiding repeated retraining and enabling concurrent training.  
- Empirical evaluation: Tested on CIFAR-10 classification, PASCAL VOC segmentation, and MSCOCO captioning with consistent oracle improvements and ~5x speedup over MCL; shows interpretable specialization.  
- Technical soundness: Winner-take-gradient is a correct stochastic subgradient update for the block objective (ties handled arbitrarily).  
- Empirical strength: Typical improvements of 6–10% over classical ensembles and analyzed specialization and top-k variants.  
- Baselines/comparisons: Includes independent ensembles, MCL (5 meta-iterations), and Dey et al.; lacks an online/mini-batch MCL and combined beam-search+sMCL captioning baseline.  
- Reproducibility concerns: Key training details provided but need documentation of tie-breaking, batch-size effects on specialization, and mechanisms to prevent early member starvation.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written with compelling figures; requests explicit pseudocode for tie handling and k-winner, discussion of shared-layer costs, and note on assignment stability.  
- Originality: Incremental relative to MCL theoretically, but novel and useful as a practical, parameter-free SGD reformulation for deep models; reliable emergent specialization is noteworthy.  
- Significance: Simple, general, loss-agnostic, and fast method likely to be adopted for systems needing multiple hypotheses and reranking.  
- Suggested additional experiments: add mini-batch/online MCL baseline; report diversity metrics (pairwise disagreement, assignment entropy, n-gram coverage); explore soft assignments/temperature and k-winner weighting.  
- Suggested analyses: study sensitivity to initialization, batch size, and ensemble size M; investigate member starvation and mitigation (e.g., exploration updates).  
- Captioning-specific suggestions: compare sMCL+beam search under equal inference budgets and report oracle BLEU/METEOR and diversity-aware metrics.  
- Pros: Simple, general SGD algorithm; consistent oracle gains across tasks; substantial speedup; clear qualitative specialization; parameter-free and easy to integrate.  
- Cons: Limited theoretical novelty; MCL baseline not fully exhaustive; limited stability/initialization/diversity analyses; one captioning setting where single-model beam search matches/surpasses oracle.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept.
