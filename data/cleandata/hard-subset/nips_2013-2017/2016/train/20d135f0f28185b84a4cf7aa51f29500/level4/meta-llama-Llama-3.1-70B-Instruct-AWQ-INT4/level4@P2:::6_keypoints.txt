- Proposes sMCL: a novel ensemble training algorithm where only the single ensemble member with lowest loss for each training example is updated.  
- Simple approach yields surprisingly strong empirical performance and addresses deficiencies of prior ensemble methods.  
- Paper provides a thorough analysis and demonstrates broad applicability of sMCL to diverse deep ensembles.  
- sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and strong baselines, including the 5× slower MCL procedure.  
- Analysis shows automatic emergence of ensemble member specialization and expertise under sMCL training.  
- Testing phase is ambiguously described; reviewer suspects ground-truth is used to choose the best member—needs clear explanation.  
- Baselines labeled "independent ensembles"/"regular ensembles" (Indp.) are not clearly defined and require clarification.  
- The specialization claim is not fully justified because training reassignment is per-example; authors should discuss or test incorporating specialization into the loss.  
- Related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) is not discussed; relation to sMCL should be addressed.  
- Overall judgment: promising and effective method; recommend weak acceptance pending clarifications and additional discussion.
