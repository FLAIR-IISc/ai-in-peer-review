- Proposes a novel oracle-guided training method for ensembles that backprops only the network that produces the best output per instance.
- Method encourages specialization among ensemble members and covers a wide range of outputs.
- Reports strong empirical performance across multiple domains and datasets, especially effective in classification.
- Presentation is excellent and experiments are thorough; reviewer recommends publication.
- Paper would be outstanding if it addressed practical inference/use of the trained ensemble beyond noting top-k applicability.
- Omission of concrete strategies for exploiting the ensemble at test time is a significant practical limitation, though problem-specific.
- Fig. 2 uses rasterized pseudo-code; recommend embedding pseudo-code as text or a vector graphic for clarity.
