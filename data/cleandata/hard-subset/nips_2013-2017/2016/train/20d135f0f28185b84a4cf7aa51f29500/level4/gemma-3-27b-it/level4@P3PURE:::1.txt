This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm for training ensemble models. The authors build upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], which minimizes the loss of the best output from a single classifier within the ensemble, encouraging diversity among the classifiers. The key contribution of this work is demonstrating the applicability of SGD to this training scheme, thereby enabling its use with deep neural networks. The paper is interesting and clearly written. My primary concern relates to the incremental nature of the algorithmic novelty compared to that of [8]. However, the authors have convinced me that this seemingly small change significantly enhances the practicality and usefulness of the algorithm, leading me to recommend acceptance. I believe a worthwhile comparison would be to evaluate the proposed MCL algorithm using mini-batches, rather than the entire dataset, to assess its performance in a more common training scenario. Furthermore, I would appreciate more detailed analysis regarding the observed “diversity” effect and its impact on overall performance.
