This paper proposes a novel method for training ensembles of deep neural networks (DNNs) using a stochastic multiple choice learning (sMCL) approach. The key idea is to utilize an oracle that guides the backpropagation update to the network with the best output for each training instance. This approach allows the networks to specialize themselves, resulting in ensembles that cover a wide range of possible outputs.

The paper is well-written, and the idea of using an oracle to guide the training of an ensemble of DNNs is intriguing. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their method, including the formulation of the problem, the optimization approach, and the experimental setup. The results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of sMCL in various domains, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to enforce specialization in the ensemble members. The authors provide a detailed analysis of the training and output behaviors of the resulting ensembles, showing that the ensemble members automatically specialize in different aspects of the task. This is particularly evident in the image classification experiments, where the ensemble members become specialists for certain classes.

The paper also highlights the advantages of sMCL over existing methods, including its ability to handle structured output labels and its efficiency in training deep networks. The authors demonstrate that sMCL is 5x faster than the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) approach, which is a significant improvement.

However, there are a few areas where the paper could be improved. One of the limitations of the paper is that it does not address the issue of exploiting the ensemble of DNNs trained with the proposed approach. While the authors mention that the approach is coherent with the top-k metric proposed for some problems, they do not provide a clear explanation of how to use the resulting ensemble in practice. This is an important missing part, as it would make the proposed approach more usable in real-world applications.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work. While the authors provide a brief overview of existing methods for training ensembles, they do not provide a comprehensive analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of these methods. This would help to better situate the proposed approach within the existing literature.

Finally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the evaluation metrics used. While the authors provide a clear explanation of the tasks and the architectures used, they do not provide a detailed explanation of the hyperparameters and the training protocols used. This would help to ensure that the results are reproducible and that the proposed approach is robust to different experimental settings.

Overall, this is a strong paper that presents a novel and effective approach for training ensembles of DNNs. While there are a few areas where the paper could be improved, the contribution is significant, and the paper deserves to be published.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel and effective approach for training ensembles of DNNs.
* The approach is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the optimization approach.
* The results are impressive, demonstrating the effectiveness of sMCL in various domains.
* The paper highlights the advantages of sMCL over existing methods, including its ability to handle structured output labels and its efficiency in training deep networks.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper does not address the issue of exploiting the ensemble of DNNs trained with the proposed approach.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the related work and a more comprehensive analysis of the strengths and weaknesses of existing methods.
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the experimental setup and the evaluation metrics used.
