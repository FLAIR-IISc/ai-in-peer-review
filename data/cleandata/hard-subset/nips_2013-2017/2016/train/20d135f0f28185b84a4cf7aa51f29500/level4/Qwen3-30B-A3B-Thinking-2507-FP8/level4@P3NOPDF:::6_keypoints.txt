- Proposes a novel ensemble training method updating only the ensemble member with lowest loss per example, yielding strong empirical results and practical value.  
- Testing procedure is ambiguous and seems to rely on ground-truth for member selection—must explicitly state and justify the actual inference method.  
- The "independent ensembles" baseline is undefined; the paper must describe how it is constructed and how it differs from standard independent training.  
- The claim that members develop class-level "specialization" is unsupported: per-example reassignment suggests instance-level effects and lacks class-wise evidence or targeted loss terms.  
- Key prior work on diversity regularization (e.g., Chen et al., ICML 2016) is omitted or mischaracterized; the method should be positioned relative to explicit diversity approaches.  
- Overall: idea is simple, novel, and empirically promising—recommendation is weak accept contingent on major clarifications.  
- Required revisions: explicitly define test-time procedure; detail the independent-ensemble baseline; provide empirical or methodological support for specialization (or add class-specific loss); incorporate and discuss diversity-regularization literature.
