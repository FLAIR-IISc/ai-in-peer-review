- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic block gradient descent method that generalizes Multiple Choice Learning to jointly train diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss.  
- Well-motivated problem and clear explanation of method and optimization.  
- sMCL is simple to implement and integrates with backprop for end-to-end training.  
- Experiments on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning show sMCL outperforms classical ensembles and MCL on oracle accuracy.  
- Theoretical analysis and experimental results support the technical soundness of the method.  
- Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with consistent notation and readable figures.  
- Method is original and addresses an important problem of producing multiple predictions to capture data ambiguity.  
- Concern: No detailed analysis of initialization (random vs. pretrained) or whether baselines use the same initialization.  
- Concern: Evaluation relies mainly on oracle accuracy without measuring ensemble diversity or inter-model correlation.  
- Suggestion: Include additional diversity metrics and analysis to better characterize ensemble behavior.  
- Suggestion: Compare thoroughly to related work by Stefan Lee et al. (2015) and other similar methods.  
- Recommendation: Accept conditional on addressing initialization, diversity analysis, and comparisons with related work.
