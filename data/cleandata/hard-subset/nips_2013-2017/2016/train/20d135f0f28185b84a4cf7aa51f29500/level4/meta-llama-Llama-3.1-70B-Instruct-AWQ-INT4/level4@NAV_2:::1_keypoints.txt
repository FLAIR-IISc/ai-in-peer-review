- Proposes an SGD-based extension of Multiple Choice Learning (Guzman-Rivera et al.) to train diverse deep ensembles that minimize oracle loss.  
- Trains ensembles of deep networks producing multiple outputs, selecting the best output per sample to minimize oracle loss.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of problem, method, and experiments.  
- Using stochastic gradient descent for ensemble training is interesting and potentially widely applicable.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Method outperforms classical ensembles and the original MCL algorithm in reported experiments.  
- Provides analysis of ensemble diversity and shows interpretable specializations among members.  
- Strength: clear presentation of method and thorough experimental validation.  
- Strength: inclusion of diversity analysis as an important aspect of the approach.  
- Weakness: algorithmic novelty may be viewed as incremental relative to [8].  
- Weakness: lacks deeper analysis of how diversity quantitatively relates to ensemble performance.  
- Recommendation: reviewer favors acceptance but requests more detailed analysis of the diversity effect.
