- Proposes a novel training method for DNN ensembles using an oracle to pick the best member per training instance.  
- Applies backprop only to the oracle-chosen network, encouraging specialization among ensemble members.  
- Specialization enables broader coverage of the output space, particularly effective in classification without explicit regularization.  
- Core idea is conceptually elegant and implemented effectively.  
- Empirical results across diverse domains are strong and consistently outperform baselines.  
- Experiments are comprehensive, presentation is clear, and analysis supports claims about predictive coverage.  
- Major limitation: no discussion of post-training ensemble utilization or concrete deployment strategies (output combination, uncertainty estimation, downstream integration).  
- This omission reduces practical impact; addressing it would substantially strengthen the work.  
- Figure 2 is a raster image—pseudo-code should be provided as vectorized text for reproducibility and readability.  
- Overall contribution is meaningful and validated; recommended for acceptance with encouragement to address deployment issues in future work.
