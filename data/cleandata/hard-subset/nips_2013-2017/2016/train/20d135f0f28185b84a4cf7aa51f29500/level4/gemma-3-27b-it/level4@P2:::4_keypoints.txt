- Introduces sMCL: an SGD-based ensemble training method for Multiple Choice Learning that minimizes loss w.r.t. an oracle selecting the best ensemble output.  
- Demonstrates sMCL on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning with improved oracle performance over baselines.  
- Motivation is solid: multiple hypotheses are useful in real-world perception systems with downstream verification or human interaction.  
- Novelty is incremental: adapts prior MCL (Guzman-Rivera et al.) to deep nets; not fundamentally new.  
- Framing sMCL as a wholly new approach may be overstated given prior MCL formulation.  
- Clarity can be improved: Section 3 is concise but needs clearer differentiation from other multi-task/gradient-conflict methods.  
- Authors should better justify advantages of the “winner-take-gradient” rule compared to existing gradient conflict solutions.  
- Notation is adequate but could be streamlined for readability.  
- Experimental scope is broad, but reported performance gains are modest.  
- Lacks comparisons to stronger baselines (e.g., SOTA on Pascal VOC 2012 for segmentation) to establish competitiveness.  
- Missing analysis of computational cost relative to other ensemble training methods despite claimed efficiency.  
- Needs more detailed ablation studies (ensemble size, hyperparameters) and quantitative diversity metrics (entropy, dissimilarity scores).  
- Qualitative results show diversity and interpretability, but quantitative diversity analysis would strengthen claims.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing baseline comparisons, clarifying technical novelty, and adding ablations and computational-cost discussion.
