This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models. The authors build upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], in which the ensemble loss is defined as the loss of the single best-performing classifier to encourage diversity. The primary contribution of this work is adapting this method to use stochastic gradient descent, thereby making the approach scalable to deep neural networks.

The paper is interesting and clearly written. My main concern is that the algorithmic novelty might be considered incremental over [8]. However, the authors make a convincing case that their modification significantly increases the utility and applicability of the original method. For this reason, I am in favor of acceptance.

To further strengthen the paper, an interesting comparison would be to evaluate the original MCL algorithm from [8] when trained on mini-batches rather than the entire dataset. I would also like to see a more in-depth analysis of the "diversity" effect among the ensemble classifiers.
