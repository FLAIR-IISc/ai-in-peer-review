- Proposes "winner-takes-the-gradient" layer to route gradients to the winning network per sample for Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) with deep nets.  
- Method is simple, lightweight, and integrates assignment and learner training without extra computational overhead.  
- Empirical validation on image classification, segmentation, and captioning shows consistent gains over baseline MCL and standard ensembles.  
- Strength: simplicity avoids complex retraining or auxiliary objectives and is broadly applicable.  
- Strength: thorough experiments and strong baseline comparisons, showing benefit when learner diversity matters.  
- Major weakness: claims of promoting "diversity" are unsupported—method does not explicitly enforce or justify diversity.  
- Risk that ensemble may default to the strongest single network, contradicting the diversity rationale.  
- Incremental contribution: technical refinement of Guzman et al.’s relaxed MCL for deep networks rather than a conceptual advance.  
- Clarity issues: awkward abstract phrasing, overemphasis on "diversity," and specific typos (lines 108 and 115).  
- Baseline validation concern: unclear whether individual networks in MCL baselines were allowed to fully converge, possibly biasing comparisons.  
- Question for authors: how were individual network convergences verified in the baseline MCL to ensure reliable learners before ensemble formation?  
- Suggested fixes: remove or substantiate "diversity" claims, clarify relation to prior work, and report baseline convergence metrics.  
- Overall: technically sound and useful but misaligned motivation; recommendation: Weak Accept with major revisions.
