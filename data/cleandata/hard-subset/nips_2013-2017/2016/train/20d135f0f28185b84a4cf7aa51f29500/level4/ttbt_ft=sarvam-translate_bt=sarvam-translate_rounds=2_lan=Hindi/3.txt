This paper presents a simple solution to the problem of multi-choice learning where learners are deep networks. Current solutions for MCL do not directly extend to deep networks. This paper demonstrates that a simple layer of winner-take-the-gradient in back propagation allows them to combine the training of learners in an ensemble setting with an assignment problem. Experimental results show that this method performs well in a wide range of problems: image classification, image segmentation, and captioning. Strengths: - In general, the problem of MCL for deep learning is of considerable importance to the NIPS community. - I like the simplicity of the proposed method. - Experiment 3 shows results on 3 different tasks. Weaknesses: - My biggest concern with this paper is that it largely induces "diversity" (even though the word "diversity" is in the title) but the model clearly does not apply diversity. I was very excited to see how the authors achieved the word "diversity" in their model and was disappointed when I found out there was no diversity. - The proposed solution is a gradual step considering the discount proposed by Guzman et al. Minor suggestions: - The first sentence of the abstract needs to be rewritten. - Diversity should be reduced. - We corrected the form of line 108, the first "f" should be "g". - An extra "." in the middle of a sentence in line 115. One question: For baseline MCL with deep learning, how did the authors ensure that each network converged to reasonable results? Cutting learners early can significantly affect ensemble performance.
