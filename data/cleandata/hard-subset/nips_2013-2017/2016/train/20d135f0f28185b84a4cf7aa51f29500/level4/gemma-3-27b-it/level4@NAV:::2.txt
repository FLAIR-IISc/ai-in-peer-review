This paper introduces a novel method for training ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) – and more generally, gradient-based models – leveraging an oracle to identify the optimal network within the ensemble for each training instance and applying backpropagation solely to that network. This approach promotes specialization among network members, leading to ensembles with broad and effective coverage of the output space. The reported results across diverse domains demonstrate strong performance. 

The core idea – training an ensemble of DNNs guided by an oracle that directs backpropagation to the best-performing network based on the ground truth – is well-presented and appears to successfully enforce specialization, particularly in classification tasks. The paper is clearly written and supported by a comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple datasets. Overall, this is a strong contribution deserving publication. 

However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by a deeper exploration of *utilizing* the trained ensemble. While the training process is thoroughly described, the practical application of the resulting ensemble is not fully developed beyond noting its potential for broad coverage and compatibility with top-k metrics.  Addressing this aspect would enhance the approach’s usability. This limitation, however, is relatively problem-specific and doesn’t diminish the current contribution.
