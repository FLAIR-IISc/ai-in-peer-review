- Summary: Proposes SGD-based "Multiple Choice Learning" (MCL) for multi-task vision, but motivation aside the contribution is overstated and experiments are insufficient; recommendation: reject.

- Novelty/Contribution: Not novel—largely a neural-network adaptation of prior non-neural work [8] with minimal incremental novelty and no clear distinction from standard multi-task or gradient-based methods.

- Missing deep technical contribution: Section 3 is too brief and underdeveloped, failing to explain how "multiple choices" are defined/optimized or how MCL addresses deep-learning-specific issues like task interference.

- Lack of theoretical justification: No analysis or proof that MCL improves convergence or generalization compared to simpler alternatives.

- Weak baselines: Experiments compare only to single-task or naive joint training and omit state-of-the-art multi-task methods that handle task conflicts.

- Marginal empirical gains: Reported improvements are tiny (<0.5% ImageNet, <0.3% PASCAL-Context), with no statistical analysis, ablations, or diagnostic explanations for when MCL helps/fails.

- Missing critical benchmarks and comparisons: Key benchmarks like PASCAL VOC 2012 and comparisons to strong methods (e.g., DeepLab, PSPNet, task-specific normalization or gradient harmonization approaches) are absent.

- Overall assessment: The work is derivative, technically shallow, and empirically unconvincing—insufficient for a NIPS publication.

- Recommendation to authors: Either substantially reframe and deepen the technical and experimental work with strong baselines and benchmarks, or withdraw the submission.
