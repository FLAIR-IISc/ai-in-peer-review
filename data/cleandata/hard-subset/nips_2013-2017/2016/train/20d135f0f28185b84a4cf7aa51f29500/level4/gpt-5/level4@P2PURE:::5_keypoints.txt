- Proposes jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs in interactive settings.  
- Oracle experiments on three tasks show the method is effective.  
- Integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent (sMCL) procedure.  
- Optimization is compatible with standard back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training.  
- Concern: unclear how important initialization is—what happens with random versus pre-trained initialization?  
- Concern: comparison setup is under-specified—did baselines use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL?  
- Concern: evaluation relies on oracle accuracy; lacks analysis of ensemble member diversity.  
- Suggest comparing against closely related work: Lee et al., "Why M Heads are Better than One" (2015).
