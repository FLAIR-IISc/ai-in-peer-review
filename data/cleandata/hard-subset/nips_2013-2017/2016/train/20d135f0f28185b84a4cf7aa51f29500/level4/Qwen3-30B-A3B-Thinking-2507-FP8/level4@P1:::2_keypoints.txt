- Proposes sMCL, a practical algorithm for training diverse deep ensembles that directly minimizes oracle loss.  
- Formalizes Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep networks to produce multiple hypotheses per input.  
- Introduces a simple "winner-take-gradient" rule: backpropagate only through the ensemble member with lowest loss per example.  
- Method is architecture- and loss-agnostic, easy to implement, and avoids costly retraining of prior MCL approaches.  
- Adapts MCL to stochastic gradient descent, enabling concurrent training and significant speedups (~5x) over previous methods.  
- Demonstrates consistent performance gains across image classification (CIFAR-10), semantic segmentation (Pascal VOC), and image captioning (MSCOCO).  
- Compares favorably to baselines including independent ensembles, original MCL, and sequential boosting (Dey et al.).  
- Emergent specialization observed: ensemble members naturally cluster on classes or semantic concepts without explicit constraints.  
- Well-written paper with clear notation and strong motivation, connecting naturally to top-k evaluation metrics.  
- Lacks discussion on practical deployment: how to implement the oracle and select the best output in real-world systems.  
- Minor presentation issue: Figure 2 contains rasterized pseudo-code that is hard to read and reproduce.  
- Overall recommendation: significant, novel contribution; accept with minor revisions to address deployment discussion and the figure.
