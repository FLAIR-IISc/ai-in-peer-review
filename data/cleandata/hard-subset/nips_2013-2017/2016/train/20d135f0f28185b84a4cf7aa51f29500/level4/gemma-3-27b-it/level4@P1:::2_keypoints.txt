- Proposes a novel training method where an oracle selects the best ensemble member per instance and backpropagation is applied only to that member.  
- Method encourages specialization among ensemble members, producing diverse coverage of possible outputs.  
- Evaluated across multiple domains (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning) and shows strong performance vs. baselines.  
- Clear presentation with a solid theoretical foundation connecting sMCL to existing Multiple Choice Learning (MCL).  
- Experiments demonstrate emergent specialization and more informative, diverse predictions from the ensemble.  
- Main weakness: limited discussion of how to exploit or deploy the trained ensembles in practice (inference strategies, downstream applications).  
- Paper would be stronger if it developed practical usage/exploitation of the ensemble beyond top-k metric alignment.  
- Minor issue: Figure 2 contains rasterized pseudo-code; should be embedded as vector or included directly for readability.  
- Overall judgment: substantial, novel contribution deserving publication.
