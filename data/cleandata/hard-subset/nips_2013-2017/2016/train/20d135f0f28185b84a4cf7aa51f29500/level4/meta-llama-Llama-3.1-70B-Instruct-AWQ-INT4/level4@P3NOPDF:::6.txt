This paper proposes an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners, distinct from previous deep and classical ensembles in that only one learner of the ensemble is active for each training example. During training, the member with the lowest loss with respect to the true label is identified and updated with the gradient. This simple idea yields surprisingly good experimental results and addresses some deficiencies of existing methods, leading me to recommend weak acceptance.

However, there are several aspects of the paper that require clarification. Firstly, the testing phase is not clearly explained. Based on the description in line 159 on page 5, I suspect that the authors use the ground truth to find the best member. A more detailed explanation of the testing phase would be beneficial.

Furthermore, the authors refer to 'independent ensembles' and 'regular ensembles' (denoted as 'Indp.' in figures and line 181 on page 5) without providing a clear explanation for this baseline. It would be helpful to include a brief description of these terms to facilitate understanding.

Additionally, the authors emphasize the 'specialization' of ensemble members towards different output classes. However, the reassignment in training is on a per-example basis, which raises questions about the validity of this argument. It would be interesting to explore incorporating specialization into the loss function to further justify this claim.

Moreover, the authors do not discuss the related work of diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), which is a more rigorous technique that has yet to be studied in the deep setting. A discussion of this related work would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed algorithm and its potential applications.

Overall, while the paper presents a promising approach to ensemble training, addressing these points would strengthen the manuscript and provide a clearer understanding of the proposed method.
