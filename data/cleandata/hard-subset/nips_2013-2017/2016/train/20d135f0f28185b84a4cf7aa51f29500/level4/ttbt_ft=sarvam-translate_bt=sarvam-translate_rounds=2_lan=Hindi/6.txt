This paper provides an ensemble training algorithm for deep learners. It is unique in that, unlike previous deep and classical ensembles, only one learner is active in the ensemble for each training example. During training, the member with the lowest loss relative to the actual label is identified and updated with the gradient. Overall, it is a simple idea that yields surprisingly good practical results and addresses some of the shortcomings of existing methods, so I recommend it for weak acceptance. (Continuing from the summary) 1. The testing phase is not clearly explained; I suspect they use ground truth and find the best member described in line 159 on page 5. 2. In the figures and text, they refer to 'independent ensemble' and 'regular ensemble' (in the figures 'Indp.' and line 181 on page 5). I did not find any explanation for this baseline. 3. The authors repeatedly point to the 'expertise' of ensemble members towards different output classes. However, the reassignment in training is per-example. I think this argument is not sufficiently valid. Can you incorporate expertise into the loss function? 4. Related to the above, the authors did not discuss the very related task of diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.). This technique is more rigorous but has not yet been studied in a deep setting.
