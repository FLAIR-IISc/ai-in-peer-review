- Introduces a novel ensemble training method that uses an oracle to pick the best network per training instance and backpropagates only to that network.
- Encourages specialization among ensemble members, producing diverse and complementary outputs.
- Applicable to DNNs and any gradient-based models, with strong empirical results across multiple datasets and domains.
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and experimentally supported; contribution merits publication.
- Lacks analysis of how to use the trained ensemble at inference (ensemble combination strategies and inference-time behavior).
- Needs further exploration of alignment with top-k metrics and practical real-world applicability of the ensemble outputs.
- Minor presentation issue: pseudo-code in Figure 2 is embedded as raster images; should be included as text or vector graphics for readability.
