

The paper introduces a novel method for training ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or other gradient-based models by leveraging an oracle that identifies the optimal ensemble member for each training instance. During training, backpropagation is applied exclusively to the selected network. This approach encourages specialization among ensemble members, resulting in comprehensive coverage of the target output space. The method demonstrates strong performance across diverse domains, with well-executed experiments and clear presentation. The core idea—using an oracle to guide backpropagation toward the network producing the best output (according to the true label)—is compelling and effectively enforces specialization in classification tasks. While the contribution is significant and the paper is well-written, a critical gap remains: the paper trains ensembles using the oracle but does not develop the practical use of the resulting ensemble beyond noting it provides a broad pool of outputs and aligns with top-k metrics (e.g., for ImageNet). This omission limits the approach's immediate applicability, as a concrete strategy for ensemble deployment is essential for real-world utility. Nevertheless, the proposed training mechanism itself is a valuable contribution, and the missing ensemble utilization aspect is largely problem-specific. Additionally, Figure 2 presents pseudo-code as raster images embedded in the text. To enhance readability and reproducibility, the pseudo-code should be included as editable text or a vector graphic. Overall, this is a strong paper deserving publication, with the caveat that addressing ensemble utilization would elevate it to outstanding status.
