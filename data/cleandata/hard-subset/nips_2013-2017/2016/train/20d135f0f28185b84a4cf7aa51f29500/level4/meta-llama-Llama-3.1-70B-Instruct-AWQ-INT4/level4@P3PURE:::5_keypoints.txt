- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs in interactive settings.  
- Integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent method that fits into standard backprop and end-to-end training.  
- Oracle experiments on three tasks show the method is effective.  
- Major concern: importance of initialization is not analyzed — need experiments with random initialization vs. pre-trained networks.  
- Experimental comparison unclear: unspecified whether baselines and sMCL used the same pre-trained initialization.  
- Evaluation overly reliant on oracle accuracy; lacks analysis of ensemble diversity (e.g., pairwise distance or diversity metrics).  
- Suggestion: compare against Stefan Lee et al. (2015) "Why M Heads are Better than One" to contextualize results and contributions.
