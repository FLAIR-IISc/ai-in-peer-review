- Proposes joint training of an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs, aimed at interactive applications.
- Validated effectiveness via oracle experiments on three different tasks.
- Main contributions: integrating deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework and introducing a stochastic block gradient descent algorithm to minimize MCL oracle loss.
- Optimization is compatible with standard back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training of the whole model.
- Missing analysis of sensitivity to initialization — need results when components are randomly initialized versus pretrained.
- Experimental comparisons lack detail — unclear whether baseline methods use the same pretrained initialization as sMCL for a fair comparison.
- Evaluation relies solely on oracle accuracy; should include analysis/metrics of output diversity among ensemble members.
- Omits highly relevant prior work (Lee et al., "Why M Heads are Better than One," 2015); authors should discuss and compare to that paper.
