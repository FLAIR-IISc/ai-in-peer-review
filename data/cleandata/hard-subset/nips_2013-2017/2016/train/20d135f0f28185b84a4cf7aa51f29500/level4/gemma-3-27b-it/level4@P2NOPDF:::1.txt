This paper introduces a novel stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm for training ensemble models. The work extends the approach of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], which focuses on minimizing the loss of the *best* prediction from an ensemble – a strategy designed to promote classifier diversity. The key contribution of this paper is demonstrating how to efficiently apply SGD to this loss function, thereby enabling its use with deep neural networks, a setting not readily addressed by the original method. 

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm. My primary concern lies in assessing the degree of novelty relative to [8]. While the core idea builds directly upon existing work, the authors persuasively argue that the introduction of SGD significantly expands the practical applicability of the method. Consequently, I lean towards acceptance. 

To further strengthen the paper, I suggest a comparative analysis of the proposed algorithm with the MCL (Marginalized Composite Likelihood) approach when applied using mini-batch training, rather than the full dataset. This would provide valuable insights into the efficiency gains achieved by their SGD implementation. Additionally, a more in-depth investigation into the “diversity” effect – specifically, quantifying and analyzing the characteristics of the learned ensemble members – would be beneficial. Exploring metrics beyond overall performance to directly measure the diversity encouraged by this approach would be particularly insightful.
