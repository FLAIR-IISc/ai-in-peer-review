- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel SGD-based method to minimize oracle loss for diverse deep ensembles.  
- Main technical contribution: a stochastic block gradient descent formulation tailored to oracle loss minimization in deep network ensembles.  
- Method is applicable to any SGD-trained model, agnostic to task loss, parameter-free, and time-efficient by training ensemble members concurrently.  
- Demonstrates sMCL on CNNs for classification, FCNs for segmentation, and CNN+RNN for image captioning.  
- Empirically outperforms existing baselines and yields sets of outputs with high oracle performance.  
- Paper is technically sound with clear problem formulation and supporting theoretical and experimental evidence.  
- Section 3 (technical development) is brief and should be expanded with more details.  
- Writing is organized but could use clearer explanations, examples, and more detailed comparisons to aid reader understanding.  
- Builds on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) but presents a novel stochastic SGD approach; needs more discussion of differences from alternative methods.  
- Results show broad applicability and potential significance across domains and architectures.  
- Performance improvements are not always large; more analysis of benefits and limitations is needed.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, broad applicability, and superior oracle performance compared to baselines.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient technical detail in Section 3, lack of comprehensive comparisons, and inconsistent significance of performance gains.
