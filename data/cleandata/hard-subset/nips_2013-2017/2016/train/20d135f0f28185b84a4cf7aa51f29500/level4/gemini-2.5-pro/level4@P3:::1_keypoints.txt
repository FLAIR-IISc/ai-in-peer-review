- Paper tackles generating diverse high-quality predictions via Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) and proposes Stochastic MCL (sMCL) to minimize oracle loss with SGD.  
- sMCL uses a "winner-take-gradient" rule: only the ensemble member with lowest loss for each example receives the backpropagated gradient.  
- sMCL enables concurrent training of deep ensemble members in a single pass, avoiding MCL’s costly per-iteration convergence requirement.  
- Evaluated on classification (CIFAR-10), segmentation (PASCAL VOC), and captioning (MSCOCO), showing consistent oracle-performance improvements.  
- sMCL outperforms independently trained ensembles, original MCL, and a boosting-inspired baseline, while being ~5× faster than MCL.  
- Qualitative analyses show interpretable specialization among ensemble members (diverse segmentation masks and captions).  
- Strength: high practical significance—makes MCL feasible for modern deep networks.  
- Strength: method is simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and requires no new hyperparameters.  
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation across diverse domains with strong and consistent gains.  
- Strength: clear writing, precise method description, and effective qualitative visualizations.  
- Weakness: algorithmic novelty is incremental—sMCL is a straightforward stochastic adaptation of prior MCL optimization.  
- Suggestion: add quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., average pairwise IoU for segmentation, semantic dissimilarity for captions).  
- Suggestion: include a more direct baseline/ablation, e.g., a mini-batch variant of original MCL to isolate effects of concurrent vs. alternating training.  
- Conclusion: well-executed, practical contribution with strong empirical evidence; novelty concern is outweighed by impact, recommendation to accept.
