Paper introduces a novel single-active-member-per-example ensemble training method that yields strong empirical results; reviewer recommends weak acceptance pending clarifications.

Testing protocol ambiguity: unclear how the ensemble is used at inference—text suggests selecting the member via ground-truth, which is invalid unless clarified as training-only.

Undefined baseline terminology: the term "independent ensembles" (Indp.) is not defined, making comparisons to "regular ensembles" ambiguous.

Overstated specialization claim: per-example loss-based member selection does not imply class-level specialization without supporting analyses (e.g., class-wise losses) or revising the claim; exploring specialization-aware losses is suggested.

Omission of related work: fails to discuss diversity-regularization methods (e.g., Chen et al.), which are directly relevant and should be compared or contrasted.
