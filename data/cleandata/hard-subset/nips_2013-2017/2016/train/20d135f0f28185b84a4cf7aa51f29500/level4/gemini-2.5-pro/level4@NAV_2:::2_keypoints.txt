- Proposes sMCL: train ensembles by backpropagating only through the member with lowest error ("winner-take-gradient") to directly optimize oracle loss and encourage specialization.  
- Generalizes Multiple Choice Learning to deep networks trained with SGD, avoiding costly iterative retraining.  
- Parameter-free, stochastic, parallel algorithm suitable for large-scale deep learning.  
- Demonstrates significant oracle performance improvements over independent ensembles and baselines on image classification, semantic segmentation, and captioning.  
- Qualitative results show emergent class-wise specialization and diverse outputs (Fig. 4c, 6, 7).  
- Strong technical contribution with comprehensive experiments and sound claims.  
- Paper is clear, well-written, and places the method in context with prior work.  
- Main weakness: no method proposed for selecting or re-ranking hypotheses at inference without a ground-truth oracle, limiting practical deployment.  
- Minor issue: pseudo-code in Fig. 2 is low-resolution; should use vector/algorithm formatting for readability.
