- Proposes a novel training method for ensembles that uses an oracle to select the optimal member per training instance.  
- During training, backpropagation is applied only to the oracle-selected network, encouraging member specialization.  
- Specialization leads to broad coverage of the target output space and improved ensemble behavior.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance across diverse domains with well-executed experiments and clear presentation.  
- Core idea is compelling: oracle-guided backpropagation effectively enforces specialization in classification tasks.  
- Major gap: paper trains with an oracle but does not provide a concrete strategy for deploying or using the resulting ensemble in practice.  
- Lack of ensemble utilization strategy limits immediate applicability; a practical deployment approach is essential for real-world utility.  
- The missing deployment aspect is largely problem-specific, somewhat mitigating the critique.  
- Presentation issue: Figure 2 pseudo-code is embedded as raster images — should be provided as editable text or vector graphics for readability and reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: strong paper deserving publication; addressing ensemble utilization would elevate it to outstanding.
