1. Novelty is incremental — sMCL seems like a neural-network adaptation of prior MCL work ([8]) with limited original contribution.  
2. Paper is poorly written and under-detailed (Section 3); fails to clearly distinguish sMCL from existing multi-task/multi-model training approaches and needs a more thorough comparison.  
3. Experiments cover classification, segmentation, and captioning but use weak baselines and show modest gains; stronger baselines (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and more competitive comparisons are needed.
