- Paper proposes sMCL (stochastic gradient descent integrated with Multiple Choice Learning) applied to various vision tasks.  
- Contribution is incremental—sMCL appears to be a direct adaptation of prior MCL work, lacking novelty.  
- Technical description is too brief and unclear (Section 3 limited to one page), lacking implementation/theoretical depth.  
- Fails to clearly differentiate sMCL from multi-task or other joint-training strategies.  
- Experimental validation is weak: baselines are not strong/state-of-the-art.  
- Reported performance gains are marginal and possibly not statistically significant.  
- Reviewer recommends rejection unless the authors substantially improve technical clarity and provide rigorous comparisons against strong, relevant baselines (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012 segmentation).
