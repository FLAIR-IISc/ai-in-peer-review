- Problem: generating multiple diverse hypotheses from deep models framed as Multiple Choice Learning (MCL).  
- Proposal: sMCL, an SGD-based method to minimize oracle loss and train ensemble members concurrently without costly retraining.  
- Main contribution: a practical, parameter-free optimization strategy applicable across architectures and loss functions.  
- Empirical results: lower oracle error on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning compared to baselines.  
- Interpretability: ensembles exhibit emergent specialization (e.g., class-wise clustering, diverse segmentations).  
- Novelty: extends prior MCL work to deep networks, addressing scalability challenges of large ensembles.  
- Advantage over prior methods: avoids iterative retraining/sequential training and intrinsically encourages diversity.  
- Strengths: technically sound, simple, broadly applicable, strong quantitative and qualitative results, generally clear writing.  
- Weakness: limited analysis of initialization effects (random vs. pre-trained) and insufficient detail on baseline initializations.  
- Weakness: lacks explicit quantification of ensemble diversity (e.g., DPP, MMD).  
- Weakness: missing comparison/discussion of related work by Lee et al. (2015).  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of computational cost and strategies for scaling to large ensembles.  
- Verdict: strong paper; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing initialization, diversity metrics, and related work.
