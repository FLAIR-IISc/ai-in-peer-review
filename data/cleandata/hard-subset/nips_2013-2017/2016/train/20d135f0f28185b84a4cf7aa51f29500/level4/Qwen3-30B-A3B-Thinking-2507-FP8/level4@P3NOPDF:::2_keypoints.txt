- Proposes oracle-guided training that routes each training instance’s backpropagation to the single most competent ensemble member to induce specialization.  
- Dynamic routing enforces complementary capabilities across members, broadening coverage of the output space.  
- Shows consistent empirical performance gains across domains (image classification, NLP) over baseline ensembles and single models.  
- Presentation is clear, technical contributions are well-motivated, and experiments include ablations and relevant baselines.  
- Elegant way to avoid redundancy and foster specialization without explicit regularization or complex training protocols; broadly applicable.  
- Method naturally aligns with top-k evaluation scenarios and appears to increase ensemble diversity.  
- Major weakness: paper fails to discuss post-training deployment and how to practically use the trained ensemble.  
- Open practical questions: recommended prediction strategy (voting/top-k/averaging), inference speed/memory/robustness comparisons, and real-world utility when only some predictions are actionable.  
- Lacks empirical validation of top-k accuracy improvements and analysis of ensemble size versus performance trade-offs.  
- Minor issue: Figure 2 pseudocode is rasterized and should be provided in vector/LaTeX form for readability.  
- Minor issue: claims of “strong specialization” are unsubstantiated by quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise diversity, feature coverage).  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—address ensemble utilization/deployment details and fix figure formatting to strengthen practical impact.
