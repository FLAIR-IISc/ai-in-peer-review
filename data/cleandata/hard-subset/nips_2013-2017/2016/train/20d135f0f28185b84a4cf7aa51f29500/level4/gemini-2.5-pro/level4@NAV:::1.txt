This paper introduces a new algorithm for training ensemble models based on stochastic gradient descent. The work extends the approach of Guzman-Rivera et al [8], which defines the ensemble loss as the loss of the best-performing individual classifier to promote diversity. The core contribution here is the integration of a stochastic gradient descent optimization, which enables the method's application to deep neural networks.

The paper is engaging and well-written. My primary concern was that the algorithmic novelty might be considered an incremental step beyond [8]. However, the authors effectively argued that this modification substantially increases the algorithm's utility, leading me to recommend acceptance.

For future work, I suggest an empirical comparison with the MCL algorithm when it is trained using batches rather than the entire dataset. The paper would also be strengthened by a more in-depth analysis of the "diversity" effect that the method produces.
