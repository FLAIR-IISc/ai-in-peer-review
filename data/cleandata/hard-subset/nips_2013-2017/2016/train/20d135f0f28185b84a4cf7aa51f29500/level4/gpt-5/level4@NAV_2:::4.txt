The authors propose a stochastic gradient descent approach to Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) for training diverse deep ensembles, and claim advantages across classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.

1. Novelty: The contribution appears incremental. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it is not specific to this work. The method largely reads as a neural-network instantiation of [8] with minor adjustments.

2. Clarity/technical depth: The paper is not well written; the core technical section (Section 3) occupies only about one page. It remains unclear how the proposed framework meaningfully differs from alternating training schemes that are widely used when jointly training multiple tasks.

3. Experiments/baselines: Although experiments span several computer vision tasks (classification, segmentation, and captioning), the baselines are not particularly strong and the performance gains are modest. To convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the framework, the authors should include results on PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against stronger baselines.
