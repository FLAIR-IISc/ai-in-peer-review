- Proposes sMCL, a novel method that trains diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss using a stochastic block gradient descent.  
- Integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm for ensemble diversity.  
- sMCL is simple to implement, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and easily integrated into existing frameworks.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical performance on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and faster than the 5x slower traditional MCL procedure.  
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the method and its advantages.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of model initialization (random vs. pre-trained) and its effect on results.  
- Unclear whether baseline comparisons use the same initialization or training settings.  
- Evaluation focuses primarily on oracle accuracy, with insufficient analysis of ensemble member diversity or specialization.  
- Suggests comparing sMCL to related work (e.g., Stefan Lee et al., 2015) for a more comprehensive evaluation.  
- Strengths: novel approach, broad applicability, thorough multi-task evaluation, and implementation simplicity.  
- Weaknesses: missing initialization details, unclear baseline settings, over-reliance on oracle accuracy, and limited diversity analysis.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, well-evaluated, and addresses an important problem in deep learning.  
- Arguments against acceptance: methodological and evaluation details need clarification and additional diversity/comparative analysis.  
- Overall: promising and well-presented work that requires addressing the noted experimental and comparative concerns.
