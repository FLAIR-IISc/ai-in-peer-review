- Summary: Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks to minimize the MCL oracle loss using a stochastic block SGD procedure, enabling end-to-end training and showing strong oracle performance on three tasks.

- Strength: Clearly integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework.

- Strength: Introduces a simple stochastic block gradient descent optimization that fits naturally into back-propagation.

- Strength: Provides end-to-end training with empirical evidence across three tasks demonstrating strong oracle accuracy.

- Concern: Initialization sensitivity—how critical is pre-trained vs. random initialization to final performance?

- Concern: Comparative setup unclear—do baseline methods use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL, and are comparisons fair?

- Concern: Evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy without sufficient analysis of ensemble diversity to explain the gains.

- Related work: Missing comparison/discussion of closely related prior work (Lee et al., “Why M Heads are Better than One,” 2015).

- Suggestion: Provide an ablation study on initialization (pre-trained vs. random) and its impact on convergence and oracle performance.

- Suggestion: Clarify experimental protocols, especially initialization, to ensure fair comparisons.

- Suggestion: Include explicit diversity analyses of ensemble members alongside oracle accuracy.
