- sMCL offers only incremental novelty; MCL motivation is clear but not a new contribution—essentially a neural-network version of [8] with minor adjustments.
- Paper claims advantages across tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning) but those claims are not convincingly supported.
- Technical exposition is weak: Section 3 is too brief and fails to clearly differentiate the proposed framework from common multi-task training alternatives.
- Experimental evaluation lacks strong baselines and shows only marginal performance gains.
- Recommend more comprehensive experiments (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) and comparisons to robust baselines to substantiate effectiveness.
