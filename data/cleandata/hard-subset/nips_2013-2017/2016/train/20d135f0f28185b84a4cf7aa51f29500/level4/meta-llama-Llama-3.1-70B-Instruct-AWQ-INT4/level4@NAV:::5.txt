The authors introduce a novel approach to generating multiple outputs through joint learning of an ensemble of deep networks, leveraging the oracle loss to facilitate user interaction. Experimental results from three tasks validate the proposed method's effectiveness. This paper successfully integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and presents a stochastic block gradient descent optimization technique to minimize MCL's oracle loss. Notably, this optimization method can be seamlessly incorporated into the back-propagation learning framework, enabling end-to-end training of the entire model.

However, I have some concerns regarding the initialization process. How crucial is the initialization method, and what impact would random initialization have on the model's performance, as opposed to utilizing a pre-trained network? The authors do not provide sufficient details about the comparison settings. Specifically, do the baseline methods employ the same pre-trained network as sMCL for initialization?

The authors rely on oracle accuracy as the primary evaluation metric. I would appreciate more in-depth analysis on the diversity of ensemble members, as this aspect is not thoroughly explored. Furthermore, the authors may benefit from comparing their work to a similar study: Stefan Lee, Senthil Purushwalkam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks (CoRR abs/1511.06314, 2015).
