- Proposal: Introduces sMCL, a simple backprop-compatible procedure to train ensembles that directly minimize oracle loss and produce multiple plausible outputs.  
- Empirical claim: Reports consistent oracle-metric improvements across three tasks.  
- Strength: Clear, well-motivated objective to optimize oracle loss for multi-hypothesis prediction.  
- Strength: Practical, conceptually simple training procedure that integrates with standard deep learning toolchains.  
- Strength: End-to-end formulation without special inference machinery.  
- Weakness: Initialization sensitivity unclear—paper hints at pretraining; ablations from random starts and multiple seeds are needed.  
- Weakness: Baseline fairness uncertain—need to confirm matching initializations, capacities, augmentations, LR schedules, and early stopping.  
- Weakness: Missing diversity analysis—oracle accuracy alone may hide redundancy among ensemble members.  
- Diversity metrics needed: pairwise disagreement/error correlation, pairwise KL divergence, specialization/mutual information, mode coverage, and diversity–accuracy trade-offs.  
- Evaluation shortcoming: For interactive use-cases, report top-K accuracy, expected user regret, or success rate under limited user queries rather than only oracle metrics.  
- Related work gap: Must compare and contrast with prior multi-head/diverse-ensemble work (notably Lee et al. 2015) regarding shared trunk vs separate networks, loss, stability, and cost, and include empirical head-to-head comparisons.  
- Optimization details missing: Clarify assignment granularity (per-sample vs per-mini-batch), update frequency, tie-breaking, any smoothing/temperature, convergence behavior, and computational overhead.  
- Hyperparameter sensitivity: Need analysis of performance vs number of members M, batch size, learning rate, regularization, and point of diminishing returns.  
- Suggested experiments: thorough initialization ablations, multiple seeds with variance, and matched setups across all methods including training time and parameter counts.  
- Suggested analyses: quantitative and qualitative diversity visualizations, specialization examples, diversity–oracle curves, and calibration of individual members.  
- Suggested clarifications: define oracle loss formally, provide exact training algorithm/pseudocode, assignment schedule, regularizers, and reproducibility details (code if possible).  
- Overall assessment: Promising and practically appealing approach, but key questions on initialization, fair comparisons, and actual learned diversity leave the paper borderline positive pending revisions.
