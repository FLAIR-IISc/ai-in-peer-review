- Proposes a novel SGD algorithm for training ensemble models, extending Guzman-Rivera et al. [8].  
- Leverages ensemble loss defined as the minimum loss across classifiers to encourage diversity.  
- Main technical contribution: adapting the ensemble loss to SGD/mini-batches for efficient deep network training (practical advance over prior batch methods).  
- Paper is well-motivated, clearly written, and shows promising empirical results across multiple datasets.  
- Primary concern: limited theoretical novelty relative to [8]; contribution is primarily practical/engineering.  
- Authors argue SGD plus minor mini-batch modifications substantially broaden applicability to large-scale deep learning.  
- Experiments demonstrate scalability and performance gains, supporting the incremental contribution.  
- Recommend direct comparison with MCL (batch-based) and MCL extended to mini-batches to clarify advantage and computational efficiency.  
- Recommend rigorous quantification of “diversity” (e.g., disagreement error, prediction entropy, pairwise similarity) and ablation studies vs. non-diverse baselines.  
- Suggest adding convergence speed comparisons (SGD vs. full-batch) and hyperparameter sensitivity analysis (batch size, learning rate).  
- Overall: strong, practical contribution with empirical rigor; accept with suggested revisions.
