This paper proposes a simple solution to the multiple choice learning (MCL) problem when the learners are deep networks. Current solutions to MCL do not directly extend to deep networks, making this work a valuable contribution. The authors demonstrate that a simple layer of winner-takes-the-gradient in backpropagation allows them to combine the training of the learners with the assignment problem in an ensemble setting.

The experimental results show that this method performs well across a wide range of problems, including image classification, image segmentation, and captioning. These results suggest that the proposed approach is effective and versatile.

The paper has several strengths. Firstly, the problem of MCL for deep learning is of significant importance to the NIPS community, making this work relevant and timely. Secondly, the proposed method is simple and easy to implement, which is a desirable property for any machine learning algorithm. Finally, the experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach on three different tasks, providing a thorough evaluation of the method.

However, the paper also has some weaknesses. My biggest concern is that the paper extensively motivates the importance of "diversity" (even including the word in the title), but the model does not explicitly enforce diversity. I was initially excited to see how the authors incorporated a diversity term into their model, but was disappointed to find that it was not included. This discrepancy between the motivation and the actual method may be misleading to some readers.

Additionally, the proposed solution can be seen as an incremental step considering the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al. While the approach is still valuable, it may not be as groundbreaking as initially suggested.

In terms of minor suggestions, I recommend rewriting the first sentence of the abstract to better capture the essence of the paper. Furthermore, the emphasis on "diversity" should be toned down, as it is not a key component of the proposed method. There are also a few minor errors in the text, including a typo in line 108 ("f" should be "g" in "we fixed the form of") and an extra period in the middle of a sentence in line 115.

One question I have is how the authors ensured that each of the networks in the baseline MCL with deep learning converged to reasonable results. Cutting the learners early on might significantly affect the ensemble performance, and it is unclear whether the authors took any specific steps to mitigate this issue.
