- Introduces sMCL: trains diverse deep ensembles by minimizing oracle loss, updating only the lowest-loss ensemble member per example to enable specialization.
- Generalizes Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) to deep networks with a stochastic SGD-compatible algorithm requiring no architectural changes.
- Demonstrates broad applicability across classification, segmentation, and captioning with consistent 6–10% relative gains in oracle metrics.
- Method is simple, efficient (claimed ~5x faster than prior MCL), and avoids costly retraining or sequential model training.
- Experimental analysis is thorough and shows ensemble members specialize to different classes or semantic regions.
- Argues practical benefits for perception systems where multiple hypotheses help handle ambiguity.
- Testing/inference procedure is underspecified: unclear how the oracle selection is implemented without ground-truth at deployment.
- Baseline “independent ensembles (Indp.)” is not clearly defined in the methodology and needs explicit description.
- Claim of emergent specialization lacks stronger theoretical justification or analysis of why it arises from the training dynamics.
- Paper omits discussion of related diversity-regularization methods (e.g., Chen et al., 2016) and how they relate or could be incorporated.
- Overall quality and reproducibility are high, but clarity could be improved in sections on testing and baselines.
- Originality is moderate-to-high: novel stochastic adaptation of MCL to deep nets that distinguishes itself from prior work.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing clarifications about testing, baseline definition, and theoretical grounding for specialization.
