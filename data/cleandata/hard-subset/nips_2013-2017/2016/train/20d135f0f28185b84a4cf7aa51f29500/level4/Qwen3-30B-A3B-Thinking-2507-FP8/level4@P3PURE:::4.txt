

The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method based on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim advantages of their proposed framework across multiple tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, the following concerns require attention:  

1. The paper's novelty claim is weak. While the motivation for MCL is clear, it does not constitute a contribution. The proposed method is largely an NN-based adaptation of [8] with minor adjustments, offering no significant conceptual advancement.  

2. The paper suffers from poor technical exposition. Section 3 (the core technical contribution) spans only one page, providing insufficient detail. Crucially, the paper fails to articulate how the proposed framework differs from standard alternative training approaches, which are widely adopted for multi-task learning.  

3. The experimental evaluation lacks rigor. While the authors evaluate on diverse computer vision tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning), the baselines are not sufficiently strong, and the reported performance gains are marginal. To establish the efficacy of the framework, experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 with stronger baselines are essential.
