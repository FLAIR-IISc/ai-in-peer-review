The authors present a method to produce multiple outputs by jointly training an ensemble of deep networks to minimize the oracle loss, which is beneficial for interactive settings. Oracle-based experiments on three tasks demonstrate the method’s effectiveness. The paper brings deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization to minimize the MCL oracle loss. The optimization is straightforward to implement within standard back-propagation, and the entire model can be trained end-to-end. My concern is the importance of initialization: how sensitive is the approach to it? What happens if the models are initialized randomly instead of from a pretrained network? The comparison setup is not described in sufficient detail. Did the authors use the same pretrained initialization for the baseline methods as for sMCL? The evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy; what about the diversity among ensemble members? I would like to see more analysis on this aspect. In addition, there is a closely related work the authors should compare against: Stefan Lee, Senthil Purushwalkam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks. CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015).
