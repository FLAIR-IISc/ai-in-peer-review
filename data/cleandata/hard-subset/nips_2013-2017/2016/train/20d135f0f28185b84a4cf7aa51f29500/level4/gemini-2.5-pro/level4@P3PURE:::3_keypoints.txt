- Addresses Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) for deep networks and proposes a "winner-takes-the-gradient" layer to jointly train learners and assign examples via backprop, validated on classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Strength: The MCL-for-deep-learning problem is relevant and of interest to the community.
- Strength: The proposed method is simple and appealing.
- Strength: Experiments are thorough and demonstrate effectiveness across three challenging tasks.
- Weakness: Major disconnect between the paper’s emphasis on “diversity” and the lack of any explicit mechanism to enforce or encourage diversity among learners.
- Weakness: The contribution appears incremental relative to the relaxation method of Guzman-Rivera et al.
- Minor: The first sentence of the abstract is unclear and should be rewritten.
- Minor: Claims about “diversity” should be toned down given the absence of an explicit diversity mechanism.
- Minor: Line 108 likely has a typo—first 'f' should be 'g'.
- Minor: Line 115 contains a superfluous period mid-sentence.
- Question: Clarify how baseline MCL learners were trained to ensure each individual network converged to a reasonable solution before ensemble evaluation (to avoid unfairly handicapping the baseline).
