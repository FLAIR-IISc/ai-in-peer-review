1. Paper proposes an ensemble training method where only one learner is active per example, selecting and updating the member with lowest loss; straightforward and yields strong results — weak accept.  
2. Testing procedure is insufficiently described; it appears ground truth may be used to pick the best member (line 159), requiring clarification.  
3. The "independent ensembles"/"regular ensembles" baseline (Indp.) is not clearly defined, making comparisons hard to interpret.  
4. The claim that ensemble members specialize to different output classes is weakly justified given per-example reassignment; suggest adding explicit specialization in the loss.  
5. Related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) is not discussed and should be included to contextualize and formalize diversity claims.
