This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent-based approach to train diverse deep ensembles that minimize the oracle loss, which is useful when interacting with users or additional components capable of evaluating the quality of predicted solutions. The authors generalize the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm to jointly learn ensembles of deep networks and introduce a stochastic block gradient descent optimization approach, called Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), to minimize the oracle loss directly.

The paper is well-motivated, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the problem and the proposed solution. The experiments on three tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. The results show that sMCL outperforms existing baselines, including classical ensembles and MCL, in terms of oracle accuracy.

The paper is well-organized, and the authors provide a good overview of the related work in the field. The proposed optimization method is simple to implement and can be easily integrated within the back-propagation learning framework, allowing for end-to-end training of the whole model.

However, there are a few concerns and suggestions for improvement. Firstly, the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the initialization setting. It is unclear whether the models are initialized with random values or a pre-trained network, and how this affects the performance of the proposed method. Additionally, it is unclear whether the same pre-trained network is used for initialization in the baseline methods.

Secondly, the authors use oracle accuracy as the primary metric for evaluation, but do not provide a thorough analysis of the diversity of ensemble members. It would be beneficial to include additional metrics, such as the diversity of predictions or the correlation between ensemble members, to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the proposed method's performance.

Lastly, the authors are suggested to compare their method with a similar work by Stefan Lee et al. (2015), which also proposes a method for training a diverse ensemble of deep networks.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. The proposed method is novel and has the potential to make a significant impact in the field. However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the initialization setting and the diversity of ensemble members.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and the experimental results. The notation is consistent, and the figures are well-organized and easy to understand.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel optimization method that generalizes the MCL paradigm to jointly learn ensembles of deep networks. The proposed method is distinct from existing methods and has the potential to make a significant impact in the field.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the field of deep learning, which is the need for methods that can provide multiple predictions and capture ambiguity in the data. The proposed method has the potential to be widely applicable and could have a significant impact on the field.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel optimization method that generalizes the MCL paradigm to jointly learn ensembles of deep networks.
* The proposed method is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

Arguments for rejection:
