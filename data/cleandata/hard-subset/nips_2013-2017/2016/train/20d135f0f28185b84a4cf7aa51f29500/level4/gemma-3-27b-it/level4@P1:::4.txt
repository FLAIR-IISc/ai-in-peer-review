The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method within a Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework. They claim advantages for this approach across multiple tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning.

1.  The novelty of this work is incremental. While the motivation behind MCL is clear, it’s not a core contribution of this paper. This work primarily presents a deep neural network implementation of the approach introduced in [8], with some adjustments for efficient training.

2.  The paper’s writing could be improved for clarity. While the technical core of the work (Section 3) is concise, it lacks a detailed comparison to alternative training strategies commonly used when training for multiple tasks simultaneously. Specifically, it doesn’t adequately explain how sMCL differs from, and improves upon, established multi-task learning techniques.

3.  The authors conduct experiments on a range of computer vision tasks – classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the experiments lack sufficiently strong baselines, and the reported performance improvements are modest. To convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the framework, the authors should perform experiments on a standard benchmark like PASCAL VOC 2012 and compare against more competitive state-of-the-art methods. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant problem: generating diverse predictions for scenarios where a single prediction is insufficient.
*   The proposed sMCL method offers a computationally efficient approach to training diverse ensembles, avoiding the costly retraining required by previous MCL methods like [8].
*   The experiments, despite lacking strong baselines, demonstrate consistent improvements across different tasks and network architectures.
*   The analysis suggests that the ensembles learn interpretable specializations, providing insights into the model’s decision-making process.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The core technical contribution – adapting MCL to deep networks via stochastic gradient descent – feels incremental, and the paper doesn't fully articulate its distinct advantages over existing multi-task learning methods.
*   The experimental evaluation lacks strong baselines, making it difficult to assess the true significance of the reported improvements.
*   The writing quality could be improved for clarity and conciseness, especially regarding the technical details and the comparison with related work.
