- Introduces sMCL, training ensembles to minimize oracle loss by using only the best ensemble member's loss per input.  
- Core algorithm: stochastic, concurrent SGD with "winner-take-gradient" — backpropagate gradients only to the lowest-loss model for each example.  
- Makes MCL practical for deep networks by avoiding iterative retraining and enabling simultaneous training of ensemble members.  
- Method is simple, parameter-free, architecture- and loss-agnostic, and computationally efficient.  
- Empirically validated on classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, showing improved oracle performance over baselines.  
- Demonstrates emergence of interpretable specializations among ensemble members (e.g., class-based experts, varied caption styles).  
- Well-written paper with clear presentation and useful qualitative analysis.  
- Originality is limited since the oracle-loss objective stems from prior MCL work; novelty lies mainly in the stochastic/concurrent optimization.  
- Technical exposition (Section 3) is concise but could be expanded, especially to compare sMCL with alternating minimization and selective-backpropagation schemes.  
- Experimental benchmarks could be stronger; segmentation should include standard PASCAL VOC 2012 evaluation for better comparison to state-of-the-art.  
- Some gains over independently trained ensembles are modest, raising questions about practical benefit versus implementation overhead.
