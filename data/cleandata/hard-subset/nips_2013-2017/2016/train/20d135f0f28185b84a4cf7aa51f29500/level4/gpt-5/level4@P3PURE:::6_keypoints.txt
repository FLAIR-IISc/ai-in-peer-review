- Proposal: train ensembles by updating only the single member with lowest loss per example (hard per-example assignment), yielding simple, novel training and strong empirical results.  
- Strength: Clear, simple training rule with intuitive appeal.  
- Strength: Strong experimental performance relative to standard ensemble baselines.  
- Strength: Potential to encourage members to focus on different parts of the data.  
- Concern 1: Test-time procedure is unclear and may leak ground-truth labels; authors must clarify exact inference (no label use) and re-report results if needed.  
- Concern 2: Baseline definitions (“independent” vs “regular” ensembles) are underspecified—need details on architecture, data splits, initialization, augmentations, hyperparameters, and aggregation rules.  
- Concern 3: Claims of member “specialization” are not well supported by per-class evidence; should provide class-level routing/stats or modify the loss to explicitly encourage stable specialization.  
- Concern 4: Missing discussion of related work on diversity regularization (e.g., Chen et al.); authors should position their method relative to explicit diversity penalties and discuss complementarity or subsumption.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept, contingent on clarifying test-time inference and baseline definitions, and better support/contextualization of specialization and diversity-related prior work.
