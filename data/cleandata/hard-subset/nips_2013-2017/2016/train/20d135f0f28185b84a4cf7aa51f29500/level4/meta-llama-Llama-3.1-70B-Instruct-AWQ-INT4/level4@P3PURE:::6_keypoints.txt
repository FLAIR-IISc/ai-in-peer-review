- Proposes ensemble training where, for each training example, only the member with the lowest loss is active and receives the gradient update.  
- The idea is simple but yields surprisingly strong experimental results; reviewer recommends weak acceptance.  
- Testing protocol is unclear and may use ground-truth to select the best member (line 159, p.5); requires clarification.  
- The baseline labeled "independent ensembles" / "regular ensembles" ("Indp." in figures) is not clearly defined or explained.  
- The paper's repeated claim that members "specialize" to different classes is undermined by per-example reassignment of learners during training.  
- Reviewer suggests incorporating specialization into the loss function to substantiate specialization claims.  
- Related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.) is missing and should be discussed.
