- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based method that directly minimizes oracle loss for MCL with deep networks.
- sMCL is simple to implement, agnostic to architecture and loss, and parameter-free.
- Paper is well-motivated; MCL for deep learning is an important problem for the community.
- sMCL is clearly explained and evaluated across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Experimental results show sMCL outperforms baselines and yields high oracle performance.
- Major strength: methodological simplicity and practical applicability.
- Major weakness: despite motivating diversity, the model does not explicitly enforce or explain how diversity is achieved.
- Concern that the contribution may be incremental relative to Guzman et al.'s relaxation.
- Insufficient detail on how baseline MCL networks were trained to ensure all learners converged reasonably (risk of early cutting harming ensembles).
- Minor suggestions: rewrite first sentence of abstract, tone down emphasis on diversity, and fix small textual errors (e.g., line 108 typo).
