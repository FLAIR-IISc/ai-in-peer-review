- Proposes a novel ensemble method that trains deep networks to minimize oracle loss for generating multiple outputs, targeting interactive user scenarios.
- Experimental results on three tasks (oracle experiments) support the method's effectiveness.
- Extends the MCL paradigm by incorporating deep networks and introducing a stochastic block gradient descent (sMCL) optimization.
- sMCL can be integrated into back-propagation, enabling end-to-end training of the model.
- Raises question about the impact of initialization: how would random initialization compare to using a pre-trained network?
- Comparison setup is unclear: do baseline methods use the same pre-trained network as sMCL initialization?
- Evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy but omits analysis of diversity among ensemble members.
- Prior related work (Stefan Lee et al., 2015) presents a similar concept and should be compared against.
