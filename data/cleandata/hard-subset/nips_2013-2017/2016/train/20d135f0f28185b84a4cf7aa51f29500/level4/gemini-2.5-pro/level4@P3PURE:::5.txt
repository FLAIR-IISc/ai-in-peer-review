This paper proposes a method to generate multiple outputs by jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks designed to minimize the oracle loss, a useful objective for interactive applications. The core contribution is the integration of deep networks with the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm, optimized via a novel stochastic block gradient descent method. A key advantage of this optimization technique is that it integrates seamlessly with back-propagation, enabling the entire model to be trained end-to-end. The effectiveness of the proposed method is demonstrated through oracle-based experiments on three distinct tasks.

While the approach is promising, there are several points that require clarification and further investigation:

1.  **Initialization:** The paper does not sufficiently explore the importance of network initialization. An ablation study is needed to show performance when the models are initialized with random values, as opposed to using a pre-trained network. This would clarify the method's sensitivity to the quality of its starting point.

2.  **Experimental Comparison:** The experimental settings for the baseline comparisons lack detail. For a fair and convincing comparison, it is crucial to clarify whether the baseline methods also utilized the same pre-trained network for initialization as the proposed sMCL method.

3.  **Evaluation Metrics:** The evaluation is currently limited to oracle accuracy. Since a primary motivation for this work is generating multiple distinct outputs, the paper would be significantly strengthened by an analysis of the diversity among the ensemble members.

4.  **Missing Related Work:** The authors should discuss and compare their method with the highly relevant work of Lee et al. (2015), which explores a very similar idea of training a diverse ensemble of deep networks.

    *   Lee, S., Purushwalkam, S., Cogswell, M., Crandall, D. J., & Batra, D. (2015). Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1511.06314*.
