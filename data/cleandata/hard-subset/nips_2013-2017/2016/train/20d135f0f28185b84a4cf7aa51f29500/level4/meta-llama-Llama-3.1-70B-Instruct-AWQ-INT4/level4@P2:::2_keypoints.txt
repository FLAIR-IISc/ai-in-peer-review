- Proposes Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), an SGD-based method for training DNN ensembles where an oracle selects the best network per instance and only that network is backpropagated.  
- Oracle-driven training causes ensemble members to specialize, yielding diverse outputs that cover a wide range of possibilities.  
- Paper is well-written and presents the core idea clearly.  
- Experiments span image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning, demonstrating broad applicability.  
- Results show significant performance improvements over baseline methods.  
- Detailed analysis reveals emergence of interpretable specializations among ensemble members.  
- Strong comparison to related work, highlighting advantages over existing ensemble methods.  
- Major weakness: paper does not sufficiently address how to exploit or deploy the diverse ensemble outputs in practice (e.g., leveraging top-k predictions).  
- Presentation issue: pseudo-code is embedded as raster images rather than text or vector figures, reducing readability and professionalism.  
- Overall assessment: strong contribution with potential for outstanding impact if exploitation/use-case details are expanded.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address ensemble exploitation and improve pseudo-code presentation.  
- Rating: 8/10.
