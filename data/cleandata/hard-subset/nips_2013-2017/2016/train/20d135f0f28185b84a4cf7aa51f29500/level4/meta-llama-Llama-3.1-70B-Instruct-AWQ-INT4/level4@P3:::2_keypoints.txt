- Proposes sMCL: an oracle-guided backprop update that assigns each training instance to the ensemble member with the best output, enabling specialization.  
- Paper is well-written with clear problem formulation, optimization, and experimental setup.  
- Demonstrates strong empirical results across image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Ensemble members automatically specialize (e.g., class specialists in classification experiments).  
- sMCL handles structured outputs and is more efficient—reported ~5× faster than MCL.  
- Does not explain how to exploit or deploy the trained ensemble in practice (e.g., selection/combination of outputs).  
- Related work section is brief and lacks comprehensive comparative analysis of existing ensemble methods.  
- Experimental details (hyperparameters, training protocols, evaluation metrics) are under-specified, hurting reproducibility.  
- Contribution is significant and method is effective overall.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions; rating 8/10.
