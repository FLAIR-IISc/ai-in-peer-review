The authors propose Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a stochastic gradient descent-based approach to train diverse deep ensembles. The paper presents a novel formulation of Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) in the context of deep networks, introducing a stochastic block gradient descent optimization technique. The authors claim that their method is simple to implement, agnostic to both architecture and loss function, and parameter-free.

The paper provides a clear motivation for MCL, highlighting its potential benefits in various applications. However, the novelty of the proposed framework is incremental, building upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8]. The technical contribution of this work is the adaptation of MCL to deep networks using stochastic gradient descent.

The technical section of the paper (Section 3) is concise, spanning only one page. However, it would be beneficial to provide a more detailed comparison with alternative training methods, which are widely adopted when training multiple tasks together. The authors should clarify how their proposed framework differs from these approaches.

The experimental evaluation of the proposed method is extensive, covering various computer vision tasks such as classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the experiments lack strong baselines, and the performance improvements are relatively small. To demonstrate the effectiveness of their framework, the authors should consider performing experiments on more challenging datasets, such as PASCAL VOC 2012, and comparing their results with stronger baselines.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the authors provide a clear and well-supported argument for their proposed method. The claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results.

Regarding clarity, the paper is well-organized, and the authors provide sufficient information for the reader to understand the proposed method. However, some sections, such as the technical part, could benefit from more detail and explanation.

The originality of the paper lies in its adaptation of MCL to deep networks using stochastic gradient descent. While the idea of MCL is not new, the authors' formulation and application to deep networks are novel.

The significance of the paper is evident in its potential to improve the performance of deep ensembles in various applications. The authors demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of their proposed method, and the results show promise for further research and development.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel formulation of MCL in the context of deep networks.
* The authors provide a clear and well-supported argument for their proposed method.
* The experimental evaluation is extensive, covering various computer vision tasks.
* The results show promise for further research and development.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The novelty of the proposed framework is incremental, building upon existing work.
* The technical section of the paper is concise and could benefit from more detail.
* The experiments lack strong baselines, and the performance improvements are relatively small.

Recommendation:

* Accept the paper, but encourage the authors to address the concerns mentioned above, particularly regarding the technical section and experimental evaluation.
* Suggest that the authors provide more detail and explanation in the technical section to clarify the differences between their proposed framework and alternative training methods.
* Encourage the authors to perform experiments on more challenging datasets, such as PASCAL VOC 2012, to demonstrate the effectiveness of their framework.
