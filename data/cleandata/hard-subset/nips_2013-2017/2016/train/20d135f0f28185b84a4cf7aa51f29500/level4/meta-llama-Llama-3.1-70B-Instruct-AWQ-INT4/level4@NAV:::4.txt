This paper proposes a stochastic gradient descent method incorporating Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). The authors assert that their framework offers advantages in various tasks, including classification, segmentation, and captioning. However, several concerns need to be addressed:

1. The novelty of the sMCL method appears to be incremental. Although the motivation behind MCL is clear, it cannot be considered a significant contribution of this work. Essentially, this research presents a neural network adaptation of [8] with some modifications.

2. The writing quality of the paper requires improvement. Notably, the technical aspects of this work (Section 3) are condensed into a single page. A crucial comparison that needs to be elaborated upon is the difference between the proposed framework and alternative training methods, which are widely used when multiple tasks are trained simultaneously.

3. The authors conducted experiments on multiple computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. Nevertheless, the experiments lack robust baselines, and the performance improvement is marginal. To convincingly demonstrate the efficiency of their framework, the authors should conduct experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012, allowing for a comparison with more robust baselines.
