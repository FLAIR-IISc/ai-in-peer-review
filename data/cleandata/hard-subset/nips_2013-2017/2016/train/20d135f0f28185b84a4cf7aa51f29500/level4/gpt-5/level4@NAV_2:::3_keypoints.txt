- Paper proposes a simple "winner-take-the-gradient" backprop mechanism to extend multiple-choice learning (MCL) to deep networks and shows strong empirical performance on image classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Strength: Tackles a timely, relevant problem (MCL for deep learning) for the NIPS community.
- Strength: The proposed method is appealingly simple.
- Strength: Demonstrates results across three distinct tasks, supporting broad applicability.
- Weakness: Title and motivation emphasize "diversity" but the method does not include an explicit diversity-promoting objective.
- Weakness: Contribution seems incremental compared to prior relaxation by Guzman-Rivera et al.
- Minor: Revise the first sentence of the abstract for clarity.
- Minor: Tone down the emphasis on "diversity" in text and title.
- Minor: Line 108 typo — first “f” should be “g” in “we fix the form of …”.
- Minor: Line 115 has an extra “.” mid-sentence.
- Question: How was the deep-learning MCL baseline trained to ensure each network reached reasonable convergence, since early stopping could harm ensemble performance?
