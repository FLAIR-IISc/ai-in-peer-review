- Proposes a winner-takes-the-gradient backpropagation mechanism to extend multiple-choice learning (MCL) to deep networks, with strong empirical results on classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Importance: MCL for deep learning is a relevant and timely problem for the community.
- Simplicity: the proposed method is appealingly simple.
- Experimental coverage: evaluated across three distinct tasks.
- Major weakness: paper emphasizes “diversity” heavily (even in title) but provides no explicit diversity term or constraint.
- Incrementality: contribution appears incremental relative to the relaxation method of Guzman et al.
- Minor edits: rewrite the first abstract sentence; tone down claims about diversity; fix Line 108 (“f” → “g”) and remove extra period on Line 115.
- Open question: how did the authors ensure each network converged to a reasonable solution and avoid prematurely cutting learners?
- Overall: timely and simple approach with multi-task results, but framing overstates diversity and novelty.
