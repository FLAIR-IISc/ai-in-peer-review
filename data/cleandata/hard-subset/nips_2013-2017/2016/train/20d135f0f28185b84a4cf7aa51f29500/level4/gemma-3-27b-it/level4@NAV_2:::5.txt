The authors address the problem of generating multiple diverse outputs from deep learning models, motivated by applications where an oracle (e.g., a human or downstream process) can select the best prediction from a set of candidates. They propose Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach to train ensembles of deep networks by directly minimizing the oracle loss using a stochastic gradient descent-based optimization. This work effectively integrates the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm with deep networks, and the proposed stochastic block gradient descent method offers a practical and efficient solution for minimizing oracle loss, enabling end-to-end training. The method is demonstrated on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning tasks, consistently achieving superior performance compared to existing baselines. 

The paper is technically sound and presents a clear and well-organized approach. The authors carefully explain the theoretical motivation behind sMCL and detail the algorithm's implementation. The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across various tasks and network architectures. The inclusion of qualitative results further strengthens the analysis, providing insights into the emergent specialization of ensemble members. The method’s generality – its applicability to any model trained with stochastic gradient descent and its loss function agnosticism – is a significant strength.

A potential weakness lies in the limited discussion of the impact of initialization. While the experiments demonstrate strong performance starting from pre-trained networks, it would be beneficial to investigate the method’s sensitivity to initialization strategies, such as random initialization. Clarifying whether baseline methods utilize the same pre-trained networks for initialization as sMCL is also important for a fair comparison. Furthermore, while oracle accuracy is a valuable metric, a more comprehensive evaluation of ensemble diversity would be desirable. Metrics quantifying the dissimilarity between outputs from different ensemble members would provide a more complete understanding of the method’s performance.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   **Novelty:** The paper presents a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method is theoretically grounded and well-implemented.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate strong performance gains across diverse tasks and architectures.
*   **Practicality:** sMCL is computationally efficient and can be easily integrated into existing deep learning frameworks.
*   **Significance:** The ability to generate diverse and high-quality predictions is crucial for many real-world applications.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   **Limited Analysis of Initialization:** The impact of initialization strategies is not thoroughly investigated.
*   **Incomplete Diversity Evaluation:** The evaluation lacks a comprehensive assessment of ensemble diversity.

**Related Work Consideration:** The authors should address the relationship to and differences from the work of Lee et al. (2015) – "Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks" – which presents a similar idea and could be a relevant comparison point.
