

The authors propose a method for generating multiple outputs by jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize the oracle loss, which is useful in user-interaction scenarios. The oracle experiments across three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. This paper integrates deep networks into the MCL paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method to minimize the oracle loss of MCL. The proposed optimization method integrates seamlessly into the backpropagation learning framework, enabling end-to-end training of the entire model.  

A critical concern is the sensitivity to initialization. How important is the choice of initialization, particularly when models are initialized with random weights rather than a pre-trained network? The authors do not clarify whether baseline methods used the same pre-trained network as sMCL for initialization. Additionally, the paper evaluates solely using oracle accuracy. It lacks analysis of the diversity among ensemble members, which is essential for ensemble effectiveness. The authors should provide a more thorough examination of diversity metrics.  

Furthermore, the work by Lee et al. ("Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks," CoRR abs/1511.06314, 2015) presents a very similar approach and should be compared against.
