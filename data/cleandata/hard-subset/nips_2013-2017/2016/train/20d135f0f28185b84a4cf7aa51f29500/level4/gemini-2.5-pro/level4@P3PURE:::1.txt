This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent (SGD) algorithm for training ensemble models. The approach builds upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], which defines the ensemble loss as the loss of the single best-performing classifier on a given example—a formulation intended to yield a diverse set of classifiers. The key contribution of this work is the adaptation of this training objective to an SGD framework, which is a significant extension that enables the method's application to deep neural networks.

The paper is interesting and clearly written. My primary concern was that the algorithmic novelty might be only incremental compared to [8]. However, the authors make a convincing case that this seemingly small change makes the approach substantially more useful and scalable. On this basis, I am in favor of acceptance.

I would, however, like to see two points addressed to strengthen the paper further:
1.  An interesting comparison would be to evaluate the MCL algorithm using mini-batches rather than the entire dataset.
2.  The paper would benefit from a more thorough analysis of the "diversity" effect among the ensemble members.
