- Proposal: Ensemble training algorithm that activates only one learner per training example—selects the learner with lowest loss and updates it—simple method with surprisingly strong experimental results; reviewer recommends weak acceptance.

- Testing unclear: The testing phase seems to rely on ground-truth to choose the best member (line 159), which needs clarification.

- Baseline missing explanation: The paper refers to "independent ensembles"/"regular ensembles" (Indp.) in figures and text (line 181) but does not define this baseline.

- Specialization claim questionable: Arguing members specialize to different output classes is unjustified given per-example reassignment; suggest incorporating specialization into the loss.

- Related work omitted: Paper overlooks diversity-regularization literature in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.), which should be discussed for context.
