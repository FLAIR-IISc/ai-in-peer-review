- Introduces an SGD algorithm adapting Guzman-Rivera et al.’s ensemble loss (best-member loss) for deep neural networks, enabling scalable ensemble training.  
- Significant practical advance: replaces computationally prohibitive full-batch optimization with SGD for large-scale deep learning.  
- Well-structured and clearly written; experiments on standard benchmarks show competitive or superior performance to state-of-the-art ensembles.  
- Preserves diversity-promoting properties of the original loss while reducing training time and maintaining accuracy.  
- Novelty caveat: core loss formulation is unchanged from [8], but the SGD adaptation is non-trivial and meaningfully broadens applicability.  
- Recommendation: strong accept with minor revisions.  
- Required experiment: compare against MCL [8] using mini-batch training to isolate the effect of the SGD adaptation versus the ensemble mechanism.  
- Required analysis: provide quantitative diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise disagreement, entropy) and correlate diversity with test accuracy to validate diversity claims.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and practically useful; addressing suggested comparisons and diversity analysis will strengthen rigor and contribution.
