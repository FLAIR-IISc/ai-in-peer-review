Proposes a simple winner-takes-the-gradient backprop layer to solve multiple choice learning (MCL) with deep networks.
Addresses a gap where existing MCL methods do not directly extend to deep networks, making the contribution valuable.
Method effectively combines learner training with the assignment problem in an ensemble setting.
Demonstrates strong empirical performance across image classification, segmentation, and captioning.
Strengths: relevant to the community, simple method, and experiments on three diverse tasks.
Major weakness: heavily emphasizes “diversity” in title/motivation but does not explicitly enforce diversity in the model.
Contribution is somewhat incremental, building on the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.
Minor suggestion: rephrase the first sentence of the abstract for clarity and tone down emphasis on diversity.
Typographical fixes needed: change first "f" to "g" in line 108 and remove the extra period in line 115.
Open question: unclear how authors ensured each baseline MCL network converged to reasonable results and whether premature cutoff affected ensemble performance.
