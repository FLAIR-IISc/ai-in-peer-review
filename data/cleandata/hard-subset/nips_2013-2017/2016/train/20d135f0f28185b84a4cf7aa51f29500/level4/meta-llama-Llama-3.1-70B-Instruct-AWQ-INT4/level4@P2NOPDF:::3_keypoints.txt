- Proposes a simple winner-takes-the-gradient layer to solve multiple choice learning (MCL) for deep networks, combining learner training with assignment.
- Demonstrates effective performance across image classification, image segmentation, and captioning tasks.
- Problem is timely and highly relevant to the NIPS community.
- Methodic simplicity is a significant advantage.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive, covering three diverse tasks.
- Major concern: paper emphasizes "diversity" but does not explicitly enforce it, which is potentially misleading.
- Contribution appears incremental, building on the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al.
- Minor edits suggested: rephrase first abstract sentence, tone down "diversity" emphasis, fix typos (use 'g' not 'f' in line 108; remove extra period in line 115).
- Clarification needed on how baseline MCL networks were ensured to converge—early cutting of learners may have impacted ensemble performance.
