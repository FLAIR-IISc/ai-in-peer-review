- Proposes an ensemble training algorithm where only one learner (the lowest-loss member) is active per training example; simple, effective, and merits weak acceptance.  
- Testing phase is unclear and appears to use ground truth to select the best member (line 159); provide a detailed, explicit testing procedure.  
- Baseline terminology is confusing: "independent ensembles"/"regular ensembles" ('Indp.' in figures, line 181) lacks definition and should be clearly described.  
- Claim of member "specialization" is unconvincing because reassignments happen per example; either provide stronger evidence or incorporate specialization into the loss.  
- Related work omission: no discussion of diversity-regularization methods (e.g., Chen et al.); include and contrast this literature with the proposed approach.  
- Experimental results are surprisingly strong and address some deficiencies of prior methods, supporting the recommendation for acceptance.
