- Proposes a simple “winner‑takes‑the‑gradient” backprop layer to solve multiple-choice learning (MCL) with deep neural networks.  
- Shows strong empirical performance on image classification, image segmentation, and captioning tasks.  
- Strength: tackles an important MCL-for-deep-learning problem relevant to the NIPS community.  
- Strength: method’s simplicity is appealing.  
- Strength: experiments span three distinct tasks, demonstrating breadth.  
- Weakness: heavy emphasis on “diversity” in motivation and title is misleading because the model does not explicitly enforce diversity.  
- Weakness: contribution is incremental relative to the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al., limiting scope.  
- Minor suggestion: rephrase the first sentence of the abstract for clarity.  
- Minor suggestion: tone down the discussion of “diversity” to better match the model’s capabilities.  
- Minor suggestion: fix typo on line 108 (first “f” should be “g”).  
- Minor suggestion: remove the extraneous period mid‑sentence on line 115.  
- Question for authors: how was convergence of each network in the baseline MCL experiments ensured, to avoid premature termination affecting ensemble performance?
