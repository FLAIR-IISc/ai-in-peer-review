This paper introduces a stochastic gradient descent procedure for training ensembles. Building on Guzman-Rivera et al. [8], the ensemble’s objective is the oracle loss—the error of the best-performing member on each example—which promotes diversity among predictors. The contribution is to use an SGD-based, winner-take-gradient training scheme that makes the approach practical for deep neural networks. The paper is clear and engaging. My main concern is that the algorithmic novelty may be incremental relative to [8]. However, the authors convincingly argue that this seemingly small modification substantially improves usability, so I am inclined to accept. A valuable additional experiment would compare against MCL run with mini-batches (rather than full-dataset alternation). I would also like to see deeper analysis of the induced “diversity” effects.
