

The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method based on Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim advantages of the proposed framework across multiple tasks including classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
1. The paper asserts that MCL is incrementally novel, but the motivation behind MCL is clear and cannot be considered a contribution of this work. This approach is essentially an neural network adaptation of [8] with minor adjustments.  
2. The paper is poorly written. The technical details (Section 3) occupy only one page. Crucially, the paper fails to clarify how the proposed framework differs from standard alternative training methods, which are widely adopted for joint multi-task training.  
3. Experiments are conducted across multiple computer vision tasks (classification, segmentation, captioning), but they lack strong baselines and demonstrate only marginal performance improvements. To validate the framework's efficacy, the authors should conduct experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 against stronger baselines.
