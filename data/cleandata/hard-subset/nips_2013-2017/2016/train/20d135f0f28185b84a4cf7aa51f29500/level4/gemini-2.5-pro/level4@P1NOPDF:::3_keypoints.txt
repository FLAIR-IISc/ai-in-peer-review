- Proposes a "winner-takes-the-gradient" method for MCL in deep nets, jointly training an ensemble and assignments; evaluated on image classification, segmentation, and captioning.
- Strength: Problem is timely and relevant to the NIPS community.
- Strength: Method is simple and elegant.
- Strength: Thorough experimental evaluation showing strong performance across three tasks.
- Weakness: Emphasizes "diversity" heavily but provides no explicit diversity-promoting mechanism; diversity is only expected to emerge implicitly.
- Weakness: Solution appears to be an incremental improvement over Guzman-Rivera et al.'s relaxation technique.
- Minor: First sentence of the abstract should be revised for clarity.
- Minor: Claims about "diversity" should be toned down to match the actual model.
- Minor: Typo on Line 108 ("f" should be "g") and an extra period on Line 115.
- Question for authors: Please clarify the baseline MCL training protocol and how you ensured each individual network converged to a reasonable solution for a fair comparison.
