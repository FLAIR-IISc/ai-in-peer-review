- Introduces sMCL, a novel SGD-based method for training diverse deep ensembles to minimize oracle loss.  
- Well-motivated and relevant to the NIPS community.  
- Method is simple and easy to implement.  
- Strong experimental results across image classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Generalizes to different architectures and loss functions.  
- Analysis shows emergence of interpretable expertise among ensemble members.  
- Claims of promoting "diversity" are overstated because diversity is not explicitly enforced.  
- Builds incrementally on prior relaxations (e.g., Guzman et al.); relation to other ensemble methods should be discussed.  
- Question about how baseline MCL networks were prevented from being cut early and whether they converged to reasonable solutions.  
- Minor fixes suggested: rephrase first sentence of abstract, tone down diversity emphasis, correct typos (e.g., line 108 "f" → "g").  
- Scores well on quality, clarity, originality, and potential significance.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel/simple approach, strong empirical validation, thorough behavioral analysis.  
- Arguments against acceptance: misleading emphasis on diversity and perceived incremental contribution.
