The paper introduces a novel method for training an ensemble of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), and more generally any gradient-based approach, by leveraging an oracle that identifies the optimal network within the ensemble for each training instance. Backpropagation is then applied solely to this selected network. This approach encourages network specialization, leading to ensembles that effectively cover a wide range of possible outputs. The authors demonstrate strong performance across diverse domains. Overall, this is a well-written paper presenting an interesting idea: training a DNN ensemble with an oracle guiding backpropagation updates to the network producing the best output, as determined by the true label. The results suggest this effectively promotes specialization, particularly in classification tasks. The presentation is clear and the experiments are comprehensive, spanning multiple domains. I consider this a strong contribution deserving publication. 

However, I believe the paper would be outstanding if it also addressed the practical exploitation of the trained ensemble. Currently, the work focuses on oracle-guided training, but lacks development regarding how to best *use* the resulting ensemble beyond noting its potential for providing a diverse set of predictions and its compatibility with top-k metrics like those used for ImageNet. This is a significant omission that limits the immediate usability of the proposed approach, although the missing component is relatively problem-specific. Finally, the pseudo-code presented in Fig. 2 consists of raster images embedded in the paper. I recommend including the pseudo-code directly within the paper text or, at a minimum, embedding the figure as a vector graphic for improved clarity and scalability.
