- Core idea: sMCL trains diverse ensembles by updating only the ensemble member with the lowest loss per example, creating a stochastic winner-take-all training dynamic.  
- Contribution: Parameter-free, efficient alternative to prior MCL methods that avoids costly retraining.  
- Applicability: Algorithm is architecture- and loss-agnostic, usable across classification, segmentation, and captioning.  
- Empirical results: Consistently outperforms strong baselines across multiple tasks and datasets.  
- Specialization: Ensemble members empirically specialize, providing interpretable, multimodal predictions for ambiguous tasks.  
- Writing: Paper is clearly written and explains concepts and technical details well.  
- Weakness — testing: Testing-phase procedure is not clearly described; selection of best member appears to use ground truth and needs explicit clarification.  
- Weakness — baselines: The term “independent/regular ensembles” is undefined; baseline training details (hyperparameters, initializations) are unclear.  
- Weakness — specialization justification: Link between per-example reassignment and emergence of specialization is underexplained; could benefit from explicit loss-based encouragement of specialization.  
- Weakness — related work: Missing discussion of diversity-regularization literature that would contextualize and compare methods.  
- For acceptance: Novel, efficient method with strong empirical gains and practical implementability.  
- Against acceptance: Lacks clarity on testing protocol and baseline definitions, weak theoretical justification for specialization, and omits some relevant prior work.  
- Overall recommendation: Valuable contribution with minor clarifications needed; recommend weak acceptance.
