The authors formulate a stochastic gradient descent method with Multiple Choice Learning (MCL). They claim advantages of the proposed framework across multiple tasks such as classification, segmentation, and captioning.

1. The novelty of the sMCL method appears incremental. The motivation behind MCL is clear but cannot be counted as this work’s contribution. This paper is essentially a neural network version of [8] with some adjustments.

2. The paper is not well written. The technical content (Sec. 3) spans only one page. What is the difference between the proposed framework and alternative training strategies, which are widely adopted when multiple tasks are trained jointly?

3. The authors conduct experiments on several computer vision tasks, including classification, image segmentation, and captioning. However, the experiments lack strong baselines, and the observed improvements are small. To better demonstrate the framework’s effectiveness, the authors should run experiments on PASCAL VOC 2012 to compare against stronger baselines.
