- Presents sMCL, a practical SGD-based algorithm for Multiple Choice Learning that does forward passes for all ensemble members but backpropagates only through the lowest-loss "winner" per example.  
- Claims sMCL yields diverse, specialized ensemble members and improves oracle loss across CIFAR-10, Pascal VOC, and MSCOCO, with ~5x speedup over prior MCL.  
- Strength: tackles a relevant problem—producing multiple high-quality hypotheses—for real-world perception systems.  
- Strength: sMCL is simple, architecture- and loss-agnostic, parameter-free, and easy to integrate.  
- Strength: strong empirical validation across three domains, with comparisons to independent ensembles, original MCL, and a boosting-style baseline.  
- Weakness: framing overemphasizes "diversity" despite no explicit diversity objective—diversity is emergent from oracle-loss minimization.  
- Weakness: contribution is largely an incremental, engineering/optimization advance (stochastic/online adaptation of Guzman-Rivera et al.'s BCD) rather than a fundamental reformulation.  
- Comment: abstract opening is verbose and could be tightened for impact.  
- Comment: paper should shift emphasis from promoting diversity to efficient optimization of the oracle loss.  
- Comment: notation typo—likely 'f' should be 'g' at Line 108—and an extraneous period at Line 115.  
- Question: how was convergence ensured for each network in the original MCL baseline’s block coordinate descent meta-iterations, given deep models’ long training times and sensitivity to premature stopping?
