The paper proposes an ensemble training procedure for deep models. Its novelty is that only a single ensemble member is active for each training example, unlike prior deep and classical ensemble methods. During training, the member with the smallest loss with respect to the true label is identified and its parameters are updated via the gradient. The idea is simple yet delivers surprisingly strong empirical results and mitigates some shortcomings of existing approaches; I recommend weak acceptance.

(Contd. from summary)
1. The test-time procedure is not clearly described; I suspect they use the ground truth to select the best member, as suggested at line 159 on page 5.
2. The figures and text refer to “independent ensembles” and “regular ensembles” (denoted “Indp.” in the figures and at line 181 on page 5). I could not find a description of this baseline.
3. The authors repeatedly claim “specialization” of ensemble members toward different output classes. However, the reassignment during training is per example. This claim is not sufficiently substantiated. Can specialization be incorporated directly into the loss?
4. Relatedly, the authors do not discuss closely related work on diversity regularization in neural networks (e.g., Chen et al.). This approach is more rigorous, though it remains underexplored in the deep setting.
