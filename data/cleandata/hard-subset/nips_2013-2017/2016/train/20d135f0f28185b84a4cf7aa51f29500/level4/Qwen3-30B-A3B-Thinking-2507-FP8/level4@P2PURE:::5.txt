

The authors propose a method for generating multiple outputs by jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks that minimize the oracle loss, which is beneficial for user-interaction scenarios. The oracle-based experiments across three tasks demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. This work integrates deep networks into the MCL framework and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization method to minimize the oracle loss for MCL. The proposed optimization can be seamlessly implemented within the back-propagation learning framework, enabling end-to-end training of the entire model. A key concern is the criticality of the initialization procedure: what if models are initialized randomly rather than using a pre-trained network? The paper does not sufficiently clarify the baseline comparison setup—specifically, whether the baseline methods employ the same pre-trained network used for SMCL initialization. Additionally, the evaluation relies solely on oracle-based accuracy; the diversity of ensemble members requires further analysis. The authors should also compare with the closely related work: Stefan Lee, Senthil Purushwalkam, Michael Cogswell, David J. Crandall, Dhruv Batra, "Why M Heads are Better than One: Training a Diverse Ensemble of Deep Networks," CoRR abs/1511.06314 (2015).
