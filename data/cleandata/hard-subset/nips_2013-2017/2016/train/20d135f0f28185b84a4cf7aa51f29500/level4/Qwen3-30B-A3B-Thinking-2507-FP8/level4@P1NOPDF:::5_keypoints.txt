- Proposes joint training of an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss, useful for interactive multi-output settings.  
- Integrates deep networks into the MCL framework and introduces a stochastic block-coordinate descent optimization for oracle loss.  
- Method can be implemented with standard backprop and enables end-to-end training.  
- Oracle experiments across three tasks show the method is effective.  
- Lacks analysis of initialization sensitivity—unclear how critical pre-trained initialization is versus random initialization and whether baselines used the same initialization.  
- Reliance solely on oracle accuracy; no analysis of ensemble diversity (e.g., pairwise disagreement, coverage) to show complementary predictions rather than duplicates.  
- Should include metrics/analysis that quantify the accuracy–diversity tradeoff.  
- Missing comparison to closely related work: Stefan Lee et al., "Why M Heads Are Better Than One" (2015), which addresses joint training for diverse ensembles.  
- Overall: well-motivated and technically sound, but missing initialization, diversity analyses, and key prior-work comparison weaken the contribution.
