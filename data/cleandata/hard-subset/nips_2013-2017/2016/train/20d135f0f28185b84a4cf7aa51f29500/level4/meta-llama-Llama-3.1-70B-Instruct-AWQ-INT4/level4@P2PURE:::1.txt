This paper proposes a novel stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models, building upon the work of Guzman-Rivera et al [8]. The authors adopt the loss function of the ensemble model as the loss of the best output of a single classifier in the ensemble, yielding a diverse set of classifiers. Their key contribution lies in suggesting the use of a stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training, thereby enabling the application of this algorithm to deep neural networks. The paper is well-written and engaging.

However, my primary concern is whether the algorithmic novelty presented in this paper is merely an incremental extension of the work in [8]. Despite this initial reservation, the authors successfully demonstrate that the small modification they introduce significantly enhances the algorithm's usefulness, which inclines me towards acceptance. An intriguing direction for comparison would be to investigate the performance of the MCL algorithm when using batches instead of the entire dataset. Furthermore, I would appreciate a more in-depth analysis of the "diversity" effect, providing a clearer understanding of its implications.
