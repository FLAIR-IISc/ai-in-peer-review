

This paper introduces a straightforward approach to multiple choice learning (MCL) with deep network learners, addressing a gap where existing MCL solutions fail to extend directly to deep learning. The authors demonstrate that integrating a simple "winner-takes-the-gradient" layer within backpropagation enables the simultaneous training of learners and resolution of the assignment problem within an ensemble framework. Experimental validation across diverse tasks—image classification, image segmentation, and captioning—demonstrates strong performance.  
Strengths:  
- The MCL problem for deep learning holds significant relevance for the NIPS community.  
- The proposed method’s simplicity is a notable advantage.  
- Experiments comprehensively evaluate the approach on three distinct tasks.  
Weaknesses:  
- The paper heavily emphasizes "diversity" (even in the title) yet fails to enforce it explicitly in the model. The absence of a diversity mechanism is a major drawback, as the title and motivation suggest its incorporation.  
- The solution represents a modest incremental advance over Guzmán et al.’s relaxation of the MCL problem.  
Minor suggestions:  
- Rewrite the first sentence of the abstract for clarity.  
- Reduce emphasis on "diversity" throughout the manuscript.  
- Correct "f" to "g" in line 108 ("we fixed the form of...").  
- Remove the extraneous period in line 115.  
One question: For the baseline MCL with deep learning, how did the authors ensure each network achieved reasonable convergence before ensemble integration? Early termination of learners could substantially degrade ensemble performance.
