- Incremental novelty: MCL concept is not new and the paper largely implements prior work in a neural-network form with unclear added value.  
- Insufficient justification for why a neural-network implementation is necessary or why applying MCL to multi-task learning is a substantive contribution.  
- Poor clarity and technical detail (Section 3 too brief); the MCL implementation is not thoroughly explained.  
- Fails to clearly differentiate the approach from established multi-task methods or show which mechanism gives an advantage; needs conceptual comparisons and ablations.  
- Experimental evaluation is unconvincing: reported gains are small and not shown to be statistically significant.  
- Lacks comparisons to strong, task-specific and multi-task baselines (e.g., shared-representation + task heads, gradient surgery; attention/RL for captioning).  
- Should evaluate on standard benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012 for segmentation) and include error analysis/failure cases.  
- Overall: idea has intuitive appeal but lacks significant novelty, clear presentation, and rigorous experimental validation; major revisions needed.
