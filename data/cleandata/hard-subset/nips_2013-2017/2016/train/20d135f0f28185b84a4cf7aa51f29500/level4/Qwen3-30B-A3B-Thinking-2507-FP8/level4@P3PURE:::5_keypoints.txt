- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks to minimize oracle loss for multi-output/user-interaction scenarios.  
- Oracle experiments on three tasks show the proposed approach is effective.  
- Integrates deep networks into the Mixture of Classifiers/Learners (MCL) paradigm.  
- Introduces a stochastic block gradient descent method to minimize MCL oracle loss.  
- Optimization integrates with backprop, allowing end-to-end training.  
- Major concern: sensitivity to initialization—importance of random vs. pre-trained weights is not clarified.  
- Unclear whether baseline methods used the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL.  
- Evaluation limited to oracle accuracy; lacks analysis of ensemble diversity and relevant diversity metrics.  
- Missing comparison to closely related prior work (Lee et al., 2015) which should be included.
