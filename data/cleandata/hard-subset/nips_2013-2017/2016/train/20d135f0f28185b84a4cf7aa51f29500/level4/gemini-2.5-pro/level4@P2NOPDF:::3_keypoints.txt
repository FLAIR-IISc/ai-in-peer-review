- Tackles Multiple Choice Learning for deep networks via a "winner-takes-the-gradient" backprop modification enabling joint end-to-end training and data-to-learner assignment.
- Demonstrates strong performance on image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.
- Strength: addresses a relevant and timely problem for the community.
- Strength: method is simple, elegant, and integrates cleanly into standard backprop without major overhead.
- Strength: thorough empirical validation across three distinct tasks showing general applicability.
- Weakness: mismatch between motivation and method—paper emphasizes promoting diversity but contains no explicit diversity regularizer; diversity appears emergent, so framing is misleading.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental relative to prior work (Guzman-Rivera et al.); better framed as an adaptation of existing relaxation for end-to-end deep training.

- Minor: tone down emphasis on "diversity" (including the title) to better reflect the actual contribution.
