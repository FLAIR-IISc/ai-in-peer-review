This paper proposes a new stochastic gradient descent algorithm for training ensemble models, building upon the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) framework introduced by Guzman-Rivera et al. [8]. The core idea is to minimize the loss of the best-performing classifier within the ensemble, promoting diversity in the generated solutions. The authors’ primary contribution lies in adapting this approach for deep neural networks by utilizing a stochastic gradient descent (SGD) based optimization, overcoming limitations of the original method’s retraining requirement. The paper is well-written and clearly articulates its contributions. 

My primary concern initially revolved around the incremental nature of the algorithmic novelty compared to [8]. However, the authors convincingly demonstrate the practical significance of their SGD-based approach, enabling application to modern deep learning architectures where repeated retraining is infeasible. Therefore, I lean towards acceptance. 

A potentially insightful comparison would be to evaluate the original MCL algorithm with batch updates, rather than processing the entire dataset at once, to assess the impact of the stochasticity introduced by the authors. Furthermore, while the paper demonstrates improved performance, I would appreciate more in-depth analysis quantifying the “diversity” achieved by the ensembles. Specifically, exploring metrics beyond oracle performance to directly assess the dissimilarity between generated solutions could strengthen the paper’s claims.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The proposed method addresses a practical limitation of prior work [8], making MCL applicable to deep learning.
*   The experiments demonstrate consistent performance gains across diverse tasks and architectures (image classification, semantic segmentation, image captioning).
*   The paper is clearly written and well-motivated.
*   The emergent specialization observed in ensemble members provides valuable insights.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The algorithmic novelty, while practical, is arguably incremental.
*   The analysis of diversity could be strengthened with dedicated metrics.
