1. Testing procedure is unclear and may use ground-truth labels to select the best ensemble member, risking data leakage.  
2. The terms “independent ensembles”/“regular ensembles” (’Indp.’) are undefined and the baseline is unjustified.  
3. Specialization claims are unconvincing because per-example reassignment weakens evidence; consider adding specialization to the loss.  
4. The paper omits discussion of diversity-regularization literature (e.g., Chen et al.), which offers formal methods to promote ensemble diversity.
