This paper introduces Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL), a novel approach to training ensembles of deep neural networks. The core idea is to leverage an oracle – a mechanism capable of identifying the best prediction from an ensemble for a given instance – to guide the training process. Specifically, sMCL applies backpropagation only to the network within the ensemble that produces the optimal output according to the oracle, encouraging specialization among ensemble members. The authors demonstrate strong performance gains across diverse tasks, including image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning. 

The paper clearly presents an interesting and well-motivated concept for training ensembles. By directly optimizing for oracle loss, sMCL effectively encourages specialization, leading to ensembles that collectively cover a wider range of possible outputs. The experimental evaluation is thorough, showcasing the method's effectiveness across various architectures and datasets. The presentation is generally excellent, and the experiments are well-designed. Overall, this is a strong contribution that merits publication.

However, the paper would be further strengthened by a more in-depth discussion of how to effectively utilize the trained ensembles in practical applications. While the authors note the potential for providing a diverse set of predictions and its connection to metrics like top-k accuracy, a more detailed exploration of inference strategies and downstream applications would enhance the work's impact. Currently, the focus is primarily on *training* the ensembles; exploring the benefits of the resulting diversity during *inference* is a crucial next step.

A minor point concerns the presentation of the algorithm's pseudocode in Figure 2. The use of raster images for the pseudocode reduces readability and scalability. Embedding the pseudocode directly into the text or providing it as a vector graphic would improve the paper's overall presentation.

**Strengths:**

*   **Novelty:** The approach of using an oracle to guide gradient updates during ensemble training is a novel and insightful idea.
*   **Effectiveness:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate the superiority of sMCL over existing ensemble training methods.
*   **Generality:** The method is agnostic to both network architecture and loss function, making it widely applicable.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
*   **Thoroughness:** The experimental evaluation covers a diverse range of tasks and datasets.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Limited Discussion of Ensemble Utilization:** The paper lacks a detailed exploration of how to best leverage the diversity of the trained ensembles during inference.
*   **Pseudocode Presentation:** The use of raster images for the algorithm’s pseudocode hinders readability.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a technically sound and novel method for training diverse deep ensembles.
*   The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach.
*   The method is broadly applicable and addresses a relevant problem in machine learning.
*   The paper is well-written and clearly presents the core ideas.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of a detailed discussion regarding the practical utilization of the trained ensembles limits the paper's overall impact.
*   The presentation of the pseudocode could be improved for better readability.



**Overall Assessment:**

Despite the minor weaknesses, the strengths of this paper significantly outweigh its limitations. The proposed method represents a valuable contribution to the field of ensemble learning, and the experimental results are compelling. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the concerns regarding ensemble utilization and pseudocode presentation.
