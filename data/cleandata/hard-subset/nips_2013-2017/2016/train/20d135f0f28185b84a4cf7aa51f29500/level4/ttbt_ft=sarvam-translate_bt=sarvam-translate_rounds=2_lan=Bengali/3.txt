This paper proposes a simple solution to the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) problem, where students are deep learning networks. Current MCL solutions do not directly extend to deep learning networks. This paper demonstrates that a simple layer of winner-gradient-receiver in back propagation allows students' training to be combined with an assignment problem in an ensemble setting. Experimental results show that this method works well in various problems: such as image classification, image segmentation, and captioning.

Strengths: - In general, the MCL problem for deep learning is important enough for the NIPS community. - I liked the simplicity of the proposed method. - The experiments show the results of 3 different tasks.

Weaknesses: - My biggest concern with this paper is that it encourages "diversity" extensively (although the word "diversity" is in the title), but the model does not explicitly apply diversity. I was very excited about how the authors were able to bring the word "diversity" into their model, and I was disappointed when I found out that there was no diversity. - The proposed solution is a step considering the relaxations proposed by Guzman et. al.

Small suggestions: - The first sentence of the abstract needs to be rewritten. - Diversity should be reduced. - Line 108, the first "f" should be replaced with "g" "we . fixed the form of." - An extra "."" in the middle of a sentence at line 115.

One question: For baseline MCL with deep learning, how did the authors ensure that each network converged to a reasonable result? It can significantly affect the performance of the ensemble cut for students very quickly.
