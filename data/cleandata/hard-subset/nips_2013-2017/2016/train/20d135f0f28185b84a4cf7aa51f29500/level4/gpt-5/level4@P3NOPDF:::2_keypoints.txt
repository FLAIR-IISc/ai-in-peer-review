- Proposes training ensembles by routing each training example’s gradient only to the member with the best output (oracle-based hard assignment) to induce specialization.  
- Method is model-agnostic, easy to implement on top of standard backprop.  
- Hard assignment promotes diversity without explicit diversity regularizers.  
- Empirical results are strong and consistent across multiple domains.  
- Paper is well written and clearly communicates the core idea.  
- Primary weakness: test-time exploitation is under-specified—no clear strategy when the oracle is unavailable.  
- Suggest evaluating test-time selection methods (gating network or feature-based selector) versus uniform selection.  
- Suggest evaluating combination strategies (averaging, logit fusion, stacking) and reporting calibration effects.  
- Recommend reporting top-1 and top-k metrics and exploring distillation into a single model for deployment constraints.  
- Ask for analysis of specialization and load balancing: report assignment distributions, check for monopolization, and describe tie-breaking/exploration mechanisms.  
- Request examples of complementary error profiles among members to demonstrate useful specialization.  
- Clarify the oracle’s criterion for “best output” (loss definition, tie-breaking, extension to regression/structured outputs).  
- Compare empirically and conceptually to hard-gated Mixture-of-Experts, Boosting, independent ensembles, and snapshot/cyclic LR ensembles under matched compute.  
- Call for ablations on ensemble size, compute/memory/time costs, and robustness to class imbalance and noisy labels.  
- Ask for practical heuristics (ensemble size, learning rates, initialization) and discussion of failure modes.  
- Minor presentation fixes: typeset pseudo-code (Figure 2) and include full reproducibility details (hyperparameters, schedules, seeds, code release).  
- Overall: elegant, broadly applicable contribution with compelling results; addressing inference strategies and analyses would elevate the paper; recommend acceptance.
