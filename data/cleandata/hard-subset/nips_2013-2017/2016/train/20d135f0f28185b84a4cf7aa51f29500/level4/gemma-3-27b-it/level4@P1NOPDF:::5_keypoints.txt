- Proposes jointly learning an ensemble of deep networks to generate multiple outputs by minimizing oracle loss, useful for user-interactive settings.
- Integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent method to minimize the MCL oracle loss.
- Optimization approach is easy to implement within standard back-propagation, allowing end-to-end training of the whole model.
- Experiments on three tasks demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.
- Primary concern: sensitivity to initialization — unclear how important pre-training is and how performance would change with random initialization.
- Missing experimental details: do baseline methods use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL for fair comparison?
- Evaluation focuses on oracle accuracy but lacks quantitative analysis of ensemble member diversity.
- Suggests comparing against related work: Lee et al., “Why M Heads are Better than One” (2015).
