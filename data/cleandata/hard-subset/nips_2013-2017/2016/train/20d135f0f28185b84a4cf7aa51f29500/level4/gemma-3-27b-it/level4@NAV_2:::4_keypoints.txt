- Proposal of sMCL: a stochastic gradient descent approach for Multiple Choice Learning to train deep network ensembles.  
- Core idea: minimize oracle loss by encouraging ensemble members to specialize and produce diverse, plausible hypotheses.  
- Demonstrated across tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Novelty is incremental: adapts existing MCL paradigm to deep networks rather than introducing a fundamentally new idea.  
- Main technical contribution: stochastic block gradient descent that avoids costly retraining required by prior MCL methods.  
- Section 3 is brief; the technical adaptation lacks depth and detailed derivation.  
- Needs clearer comparison and discussion of differences with alternative multi-task or concurrent training/gradient-based methods.  
- Experiments are comprehensive in scope but show only modest performance gains.  
- Baseline comparisons are not exhaustive; stronger, more recent baselines and competitive benchmarks (e.g., PASCAL VOC 2012) should be included.  
- Ablation studies are missing and would help isolate contributions of sMCL components.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a practical problem, offers computational efficiency, shows consistent improvements over independent ensembles, and produces interpretable diverse outputs.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, concise technical explanation, modest empirical gains, and writing could be clearer and more concise.
