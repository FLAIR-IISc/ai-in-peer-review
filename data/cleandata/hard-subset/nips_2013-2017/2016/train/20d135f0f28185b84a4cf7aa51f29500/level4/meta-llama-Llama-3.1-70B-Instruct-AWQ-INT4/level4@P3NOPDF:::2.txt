The paper proposes a novel approach for training ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or other gradient-based methods. This approach leverages an oracle that identifies the optimal network for each training instance and applies backpropagation only to the selected network. The key advantage of this method is that it enables networks to specialize in specific tasks, resulting in ensembles that effectively cover a wide range of possible outputs.

The authors present an interesting idea for training DNN ensembles using an oracle to guide backpropagation updates to the network with the best output, as determined by the true label. This approach appears to promote specialization effectively in classification contexts. The overall presentation is clear, and the experiments are conducted across multiple domains, yielding impressive performance results.

This is a strong paper that deserves publication. The authors' contribution is significant, and their approach has the potential to improve the performance of DNN ensembles. However, to consider this paper outstanding, it would be beneficial to address the exploitation of ensembles trained with the proposed approach. Currently, the authors focus on training with the oracle but do not fully develop the use of the resulting ensemble. While they mention that the approach can provide a broad pool of possible results and is coherent with the top-k metric proposed for certain problems (e.g., ImageNet), this aspect is not explored in depth.

This missing component is crucial for making the proposed approach fully practical. Nevertheless, the contribution is substantial, and the missing part is relatively problem-specific. Therefore, the paper remains a valuable contribution to the field.

One minor suggestion for improvement is related to the presentation of Figure 2, which is a raster image embedded in the paper. It would be more readable and convenient to present the pseudo-code directly within the paper or, at the very least, embed the pseudo-code figure as a vectorial image.

Rating: 8/10
