This paper proposes a simple solution to the multiple choice learning (MCL) problem when the learners are deep networks. Existing solutions to MCL do not readily extend to deep learning architectures. This paper demonstrates that a straightforward “winner-takes-all” gradient assignment during backpropagation enables effective joint training of the learners and resolution of the assignment problem within an ensemble setting. Experimental results show the method performs well across a wide range of problems – image classification, image segmentation, and captioning.

**Strengths:**

*   The problem of MCL for deep learning is a relevant and important topic for the NIPS community.
*   The proposed method is conceptually simple and easy to implement.
*   The experimental evaluation spans three diverse tasks, demonstrating broad applicability.

**Weaknesses:**

*   A primary concern is the emphasis on “diversity” – reflected even in the title – while the model itself does not explicitly enforce diversity. The motivation surrounding diversity is strong, but the approach implicitly relies on oracle loss to induce specialization, rather than directly promoting it.
*   The proposed solution represents an incremental improvement over the relaxation proposed by Guzman et al. While valuable, the novelty in this adaptation for deep networks could be more clearly articulated.

**Minor Suggestions:**

*   The first sentence of the abstract could be rephrased for clarity.
*   The emphasis on "diversity" should be toned down throughout the paper to more accurately reflect the method’s mechanism (oracle loss-driven specialization).
*   Line 108: The first “f” should be “g” in “we fixed the form of…”.
*   Line 115: Remove the extraneous period in the middle of the sentence.

**One Question:**

For the baseline MCL implementation with deep learning, how did the authors ensure that each network had converged to a reasonable solution before proceeding to the assignment step? Prematurely terminating training of the individual learners could significantly affect the overall ensemble performance.

**Arguments Pro Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a practical problem – producing multiple hypotheses for systems interacting with oracles.
*   Offers a simple and effective solution applicable to a wide range of deep learning models and loss functions.
*   Demonstrates clear performance improvements over strong baselines across multiple tasks.
*   The approach is parameter-free and computationally efficient.

**Arguments Con Acceptance:**

*   The lack of explicit diversity enforcement, despite the strong emphasis on it, may limit the method’s ability to explore the full space of possible solutions.
*   The incremental nature of the contribution compared to prior work (Guzman et al.) requires careful consideration of its overall novelty.
