The paper proposes a novel method for training an ensemble of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) or other gradient-based approaches. This method utilizes an oracle that identifies the optimal network for each data instance during training, applying backpropagation only to the selected network. A key advantage of this approach is that it allows the networks to specialize in specific themes, resulting in ensembles that effectively cover most possible outputs of interest. The authors report impressive performance across various domains.

Overall, I find this to be a strong paper that presents an intriguing idea for training DNN ensembles with an oracle-guided backpropagation update. This approach appears to promote specialization effectively in classification contexts. The presentation is well-organized, and the experiments are conducted across multiple domains. The work deserves publication.

However, I would have considered this paper outstanding if it had also addressed the issue of exploiting the trained DNN ensembles. While the oracle is used during training, the utilization of the resulting ensemble is not further developed beyond stating that it can provide a broad pool of possible results and is coherent with the top-k metric proposed for certain problems, such as ImageNet. This omission is a crucial aspect for making the proposed approach fully practical. Nevertheless, the contribution is significant in its current form, with the missing component being relatively problem-specific.

One minor suggestion for improvement is related to Figure 2, which presents pseudo-code as a raster image embedded in the paper. It would be beneficial to include the pseudo-code directly within the paper or, at the very least, embed the figure as a vector image to enhance readability.
