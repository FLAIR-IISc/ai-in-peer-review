- Proposes sMCL, a stochastic gradient descent modification of Multiple Choice Learning tailored for deep neural networks.  
- sMCL trains diverse deep ensembles by directly minimizing oracle loss without costly retraining.  
- Algorithm is simple, parameter-free, and agnostic to architecture and loss function.  
- Well-written paper with clear motivation, methodology, and experimental presentation.  
- Thorough analysis of sMCL’s strengths and weaknesses is provided.  
- Empirical results show sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles, strong baselines, and original MCL.  
- Demonstrated applicability across tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning.  
- Main concern: algorithmic novelty may be incremental relative to Guzman-Rivera et al. [8].  
- Suggested additional experiments: compare MCL using mini-batches rather than full-dataset training.  
- Suggested additional analysis: deeper investigation of the diversity effect induced by sMCL.  
- Reviewer recommendation: in favor of acceptance.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 7/10, Significance 8/10.
