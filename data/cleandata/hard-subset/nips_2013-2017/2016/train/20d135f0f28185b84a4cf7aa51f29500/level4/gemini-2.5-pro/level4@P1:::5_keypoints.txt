- Proposes sMCL to train ensembles that minimize oracle loss so at least one of M outputs is correct, useful for human-in-the-loop or selector downstream.  
- Implements sMCL as stochastic block gradient descent with a simple "winner-take-gradient" rule (backprop only through lowest-error member per example).  
- Evaluated on classification (CIFAR-10), segmentation (Pascal VOC), and captioning (MSCOCO) with consistent oracle performance gains.  
- Significance/originality: adapts MCL for deep nets, avoiding expensive iterative retraining of original MCL.  
- Generality/simplicity: architecture- and loss-agnostic, easy to implement with minor backprop changes.  
- Strong empirical results: consistent improvements over standard ensembles, MCL, and boosting-like baselines; ~5x speedup vs MCL.  
- Analysis: provides qualitative evidence of ensemble member specialization varying by task.  
- Question on initialization: unclear how critical pre-trained ensemble initialization is, especially for segmentation vs random init.  
- Baseline fairness: need clarity whether baselines used identical pre-trained initialization to ensure fair comparison.  
- Quantitative diversity missing: recommend explicit diversity metrics (e.g., pairwise output distance, n-gram dissimilarity).  
- Missing related work discussion: should compare/contrast with Lee et al. (2015) on training diverse deep ensembles.  
- Overall: well-written, simple, and effective approach with minor/addressable weaknesses.
