This paper introduces a novel training methodology for ensembles of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) – and, more broadly, gradient-based models. The core idea is to leverage an oracle, identifying the optimal network within the ensemble for each training instance, and applying backpropagation solely to that selected network. This approach encourages specialization among ensemble members, leading to improved coverage of the output space. The reported results across diverse domains demonstrate strong performance. 

The paper clearly articulates an interesting and potentially impactful concept: oracle-guided backpropagation for training DNN ensembles, effectively directing updates to the network producing the correct output. This mechanism demonstrably promotes specialization, particularly in classification tasks. The presentation is excellent, and the experimental evaluation is comprehensive, spanning multiple datasets. 

Overall, this is a strong contribution deserving publication. However, the paper would be significantly strengthened by exploring the practical implications of *using* the trained ensemble. While the training process is well-defined, the paper lacks discussion on how to effectively leverage the resulting specialized networks beyond acknowledging their potential for broad coverage and compatibility with metrics like top-k accuracy. Addressing this aspect would enhance the approach’s usability. This missing component is, however, relatively problem-dependent and doesn’t negate the current contribution. 

A minor point: the pseudo-code in Fig. 2 is presented as a raster image embedded within the paper. Consider presenting the pseudo-code directly in the text or, at a minimum, embedding it as a vector graphic for improved clarity and scalability.
