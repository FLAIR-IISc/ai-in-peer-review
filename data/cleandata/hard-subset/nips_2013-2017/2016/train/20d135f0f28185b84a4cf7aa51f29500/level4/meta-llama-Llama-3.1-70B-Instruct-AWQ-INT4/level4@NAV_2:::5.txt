The authors propose a novel approach to training diverse deep ensembles by minimizing the oracle loss directly, which is particularly useful in practical perception systems that require interactions with users or additional components. The proposed Stochastic Multiple Choice Learning (sMCL) method integrates deep networks into the Multiple Choice Learning (MCL) paradigm and introduces a stochastic block gradient descent optimization approach to minimize the oracle loss.

The authors provide a thorough overview of the MCL paradigm and its limitations, highlighting the need for a more efficient and effective method for training diverse deep ensembles. They demonstrate the broad applicability and efficacy of sMCL on three tasks: image classification, semantic segmentation, and image captioning. The results show that sMCL significantly outperforms classical ensembles and other strong baselines, including the 5x slower MCL procedure.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its advantages. However, there are a few concerns and suggestions for improvement. Firstly, the authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the initialization settings for the models. It is unclear whether the models are initialized with random values or a pre-trained network, and how this affects the performance of the proposed method. Additionally, the authors do not explain the setting for comparison with baseline methods, including whether the same pre-trained network is used for initialization.

Furthermore, the authors primarily use oracle accuracy as the evaluation metric, but it would be beneficial to see more analysis on the diversity of ensemble members. For example, how do the ensemble members specialize in different tasks, and what is the impact of this specialization on the overall performance?

Lastly, the authors may want to consider comparing their method with other similar approaches, such as the work by Stefan Lee et al. (2015) on training a diverse ensemble of deep networks. This comparison would provide a more comprehensive understanding of the strengths and limitations of the proposed method.
