- Proposes joint training of an ensemble of deep networks to minimize the oracle loss for generating multiple outputs, aimed at user-interaction scenarios.
- Shows effectiveness across three oracle-based tasks and integrates deep networks into the MCL framework.
- Introduces stochastic block gradient descent to minimize the MCL oracle loss; optimization is implementable with standard back-propagation and supports end-to-end training.
- Concern: how critical is initialization—what is the performance degradation if models are randomly initialized instead of using a pre-trained network?
- Concern: unclear comparison settings—do baseline methods use the same pre-trained initialization as sMCL?
- Concern: evaluation depends solely on oracle accuracy; analysis of ensemble member diversity is needed.
- Suggests comparing against related work: Lee et al., "Why M Heads are Better than One" (2015).
