This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a widely used real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm, to reduce its computational complexity. The authors introduce the Cholesky-CMA-ES, which replaces the traditional matrix square root computation with a triangular Cholesky factorization, achieving optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the CMA-ES algorithm and its limitations. They also provide a thorough theoretical justification for their proposed modification, including a proof that the error introduced by the approximation decreases over time as the algorithm converges.

The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating a significant speedup of the Cholesky-CMA-ES compared to the standard CMA-ES, especially for high-dimensional problems. The authors also show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES, indicating that the approximation does not compromise the algorithm's performance.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the CMA-ES algorithm and its limitations.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical justification for their proposed modification, including a proof that the error introduced by the approximation decreases over time.
* The experimental results are convincing, demonstrating a significant speedup of the Cholesky-CMA-ES compared to the standard CMA-ES.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a certain level of familiarity with the CMA-ES algorithm and its applications, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison with other existing methods for reducing the computational complexity of the CMA-ES, such as the delay strategy or the non-triangular Cholesky factor approach.
* The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, such as its sensitivity to the choice of parameters or its performance on non-convex problems.
