- Paper introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, replacing the covariance matrix square root with its Cholesky factor to reduce computation and memory.
- Method yields a numerically stable quadratic-time update (O(µd²)) and lowers memory requirements compared to original CMA-ES.
- Authors claim no loss in optimization performance while significantly reducing runtime and memory usage.
- The paper provides theoretical justification including convergence analysis and error bounds.
- Empirical validation on standard benchmark functions shows comparable optimization performance and faster runtimes versus state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants.
- Practical utility emphasized for high-dimensional problems, with efficient computation of eigenvalues and Cholesky inverse.
- Paper is well-structured and includes pseudocode, enhancing clarity and reproducibility.
- Weakness: experiments are limited to synthetic benchmarks and lack real-world application tests (e.g., RL, hyperparameter tuning).
- Weakness: limited empirical analysis of the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor and its impact on convergence.
- Weakness: method is constrained to cases where full covariance matrices are feasible; no exploration of hybrid or low-rank extensions for extreme dimensionality.
- Pro: addresses a critical CMA-ES bottleneck, improving scalability and efficiency.
- Pro: strong theoretical and empirical support and novel contribution to derivative-free optimization.
- Con: real-world performance remains untested due to benchmark-only experiments.
- Con: paper does not investigate extensions for situations where maintaining a full covariance matrix is infeasible.
- Recommendation: accept, with encouragement to expand experimental evaluation and explore hybrid approaches for larger-scale problems.
