## Review of CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) aimed at reducing both runtime and memory complexity during covariance matrix updates. The core idea is to maintain and update the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix itself, circumventing the costly eigenvalue decomposition typically required. The authors argue this is theoretically justified and doesn’t alter the algorithm’s convergence properties, empirically demonstrating speedups, especially in higher dimensions.

The paper clearly identifies the bottleneck in CMA-ES – covariance matrix updates – and proposes a well-motivated solution. The connection to existing work on Cholesky updates is properly acknowledged, and the authors distinguish their contribution by addressing the core requirement of the standard CMA-ES: the matrix square root. The theoretical justification, leveraging the convergence of the Cholesky factor, is a significant strength. The experimental results on a range of benchmark functions, including those designed to challenge CMA-ES, show consistent speedups in wall-clock time while maintaining comparable performance in terms of function evaluations. The inclusion of rotation invariance testing and analysis of the coordinate system changes are also valuable.

However, the paper would benefit from a more detailed discussion of the potential impact of the approximation introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor. While Lemma 1 provides a theoretical basis for convergence, a more in-depth analysis of the error introduced and its effect on performance for specific problem types would be helpful. Furthermore, the discussion on the impact of exceeding the L2 cache in the experiments feels somewhat cursory. A more detailed investigation of memory access patterns and scalability would strengthen the conclusions. The experimental setup, while comprehensive, could be enhanced by comparing against more recent and advanced optimization algorithms beyond the standard CMA-ES variants. 

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant practical bottleneck in CMA-ES.
*   Provides a theoretically sound and well-motivated approach.
*   Demonstrates consistent speedups in experiments.
*   Clear writing and well-organized presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The impact of the approximation (Cholesky factor vs. matrix square root) could be further explored.
*   Scalability discussion beyond L2 cache limitations is limited.
*   Comparison against a broader range of state-of-the-art algorithms would be beneficial.

**Overall:**

This is a strong paper presenting a valuable contribution to the CMA-ES community. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a practical and theoretically justifiable improvement to a widely used algorithm. While some areas could benefit from further investigation, the strengths of the paper outweigh the weaknesses. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the points raised above.
