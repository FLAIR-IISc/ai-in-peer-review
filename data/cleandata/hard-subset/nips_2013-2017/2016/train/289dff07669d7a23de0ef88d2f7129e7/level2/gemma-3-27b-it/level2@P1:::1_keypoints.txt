- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update and store covariance via its Cholesky factor to improve computational efficiency.
- Uses quadratic-time Cholesky updates and adapts step-size by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor.
- Provides theoretical justification, notably convergence of the rotation matrix (Lemma 1).
- Empirical results show improved runtime, particularly in higher dimensions.
- Well-motivated and positioned relative to prior work (model complexity restriction, direct Cholesky updates, delayed updates).
- Strength: theoretically sound approach with promising computational gains.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation across benchmarks and comparisons with CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES.
- Strength: clear algorithm description and complexity analysis; implementation available in the Shark library.
- Weakness: approximation error from replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor needs more empirical quantification.
- Weakness: limited analysis of solution quality and robustness beyond function-evaluation parity.
- Weakness: experiments are primarily standard benchmarks; lacks challenging real-world applications (e.g., RL, hyperparameter tuning).
- Weakness: brief discussion on cache size impact (d=256) and whether it is a fundamental limitation or implementation artifact.
- Weakness: scalability to extremely high-dimensional problems is not fully addressed despite quadratic complexity.
- Arguments for acceptance: tackles a significant practical CMA-ES bottleneck, with theoretical and empirical support and available code.
- Arguments against acceptance: incomplete error analysis, limited real-world evaluation, and constrained scalability discussion.
- Overall assessment: significant, high-quality contribution; recommendation — Accept.
