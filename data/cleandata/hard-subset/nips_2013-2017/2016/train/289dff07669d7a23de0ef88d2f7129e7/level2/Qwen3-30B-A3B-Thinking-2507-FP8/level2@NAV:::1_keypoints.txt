- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce computation.
- Claims optimal asymptotic time O(µd²) and storage complexity while not increasing required objective evaluations.
- Theoretically justifies replacing C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹ in CSA, arguing it does not deteriorate performance.
- Lemma 1 shows the rotation matrix ETₜ₋₁Eₜ converges to identity, so the introduced error vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Experiments on benchmark functions (d=4–256) show equal function-evaluation counts to CMA-ES but substantial runtime speedups (up to 20× at d=64), increasing with dimensionality.
- Strengths: solid theoretical foundation, well-designed experiments, practical relevance, and proper positioning within prior work.
- Minor limitation: limited analysis of effects on convergence rate and handling of ill-conditioned covariance matrices.
- Overall: valuable, theoretically sound and empirically validated approach for scaling CMA-ES; recommend acceptance.
