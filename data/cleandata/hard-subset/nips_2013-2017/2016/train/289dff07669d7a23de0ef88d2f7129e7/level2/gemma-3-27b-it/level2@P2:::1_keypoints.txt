- Proposes updating and storing the CMA-ES covariance via its Cholesky factor to reduce time and memory, especially in high dimensions.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based work but extends it to full CMA-ES with theoretical justification showing minimal performance degradation.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly explains motivation, related work, method, and experiments.
- Theoretical analysis (including Lemma 1) convincingly argues why the Cholesky approximation preserves algorithm behavior and rotation invariance.
- Experimental evaluation compares Cholesky-CMA-ES to standard and modified CMA-ES variants across diverse benchmarks and dimensions.
- Demonstrates substantial speedups and reduced memory footprint in higher dimensions, improving CMA-ES scalability for ML applications.
- Noted technical soundness: solid mathematical justification and discussion of polar decomposition and step-size adaptation.
- Originality: meaningful contribution by extending Cholesky approaches to standard CMA-ES with novel analysis of step-size impact.
- Comprehensive evaluation against multiple baselines supports empirical claims.
- Reproducibility concern: lacks specific computational/implementation details (CPU, memory, LAPACK version) and deeper memory usage analysis.
- Lemma 1 caveat: assumption of convergence needs further elaboration and discussion of scenarios where approximation may fail.
- Comparison gap: should include other efficient CMA-ES variants (e.g., low-rank or subspace-based approaches) to better position the work.
- Limited analysis of parameter sensitivity (e.g., cσ, dσ) — only default parameters were used without exploration of robustness.
- Arguments for acceptance: technically sound, significant speedup without accuracy loss, convincing theory, thorough experiments, broad applicability.
- Arguments against acceptance: reproducibility details missing, Lemma 1 limitations under-discussed, and comparisons to other complexity-reduction methods incomplete.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution recommending acceptance, with revisions to address experimental details, Lemma 1 limitations, and broader comparisons.
