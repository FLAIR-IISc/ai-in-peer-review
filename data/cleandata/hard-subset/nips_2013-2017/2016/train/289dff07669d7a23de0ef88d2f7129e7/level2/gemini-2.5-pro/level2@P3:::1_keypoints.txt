- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain and update triangular Cholesky factor A instead of forming covariance C to reduce complexity.
- Reduces time complexity to O(µd^2) per iteration and storage to O(d^2), achieving asymptotically optimal costs for full covariance.
- Addresses CSA path update challenge by approximating C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, avoiding explicit matrix square-root inversion.
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the approximation error is a slowly changing rotation that converges to identity, so behavior is preserved.
- Positions method relative to prior work, emphasizing stability and uniqueness of triangular Cholesky factor versus non-triangular approaches.
- Empirical evaluation on standard benchmarks shows large wall-clock speedups with no increase in function evaluations, especially in high dimensions.
- Paper quality is high: clear motivation, rigorous theory, well-designed experiments, and reproducible algorithmic details (Algorithm 1 & 2).
- Writing and organization are strong, with useful background, clear method description, and interpretable results.
- Original contribution: first rigorous, effective adaptation of Cholesky-factor updates to standard CMA-ES with analysis of CSA approximation.
- High significance: makes CMA-ES practical for higher-dimensional problems and likely to be widely adopted as a drop-in replacement.
- Essential correction needed: incorrect claim that diagonal of triangular Cholesky are square roots of eigenvalues — this holds only for diagonal matrices; eigenvalues are not obtained "for free."
- Minor clarification suggested: discuss implications of the covariance-shape convergence assumption or summarize empirical behavior of rotation matrices E_t in the main text.
