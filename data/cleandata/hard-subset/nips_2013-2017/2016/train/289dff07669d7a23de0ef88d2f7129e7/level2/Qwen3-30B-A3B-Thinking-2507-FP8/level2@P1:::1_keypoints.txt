- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor in step-size adaptation to reduce cost.
- Claims optimal time complexity O(µ d²) for covariance updates while keeping comparable optimization performance to standard CMA-ES.
- Lemma 1 provides strong theoretical justification: error from using Cholesky factor vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Experiments show same number of function evaluations as CMA-ES but large runtime improvements (up to 20× at d=64), with speedup growing with dimensionality.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and includes precise implementation details (algorithm available in the Shark library).
- Experimental setup is comprehensive: multiple benchmarks, varying dimensionality, rotation-invariance tests, and comparisons to relevant baselines (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES).
- Strength: more rigorous theoretical justification for the Cholesky modification than prior attempts.
- Strength: clear demonstration of asymptotic time-complexity improvement.
- Strength: practical implementation showing real-world performance gains.
- Strength: careful and thorough comparison with existing approaches.
- Improvement: provide more detailed analysis of optimization trajectories to understand effects on convergence path, especially for non-spherical functions.
- Improvement: supply empirical evidence on how often the Cholesky-induced error remains small during optimization before convergence, given CMA-ES randomness.
- Improvement: discuss practical impact of cache/memory effects at very large dimensions (e.g., d=256) and implications for memory-constrained applications.
- Improvement: expand comparison with Loshchilov (2015) low-dimensional approximation methods commonly used when full covariance updates are infeasible.
- Significance: substantial — makes CMA-ES more scalable for ML tasks (hyperparameter tuning, RL) without sacrificing performance.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address convergence-analysis evidence, memory limitations, and integration with other CMA-ES variants.
