

This paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a modified version of the covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) that replaces the standard matrix square root computation with a triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity. The main claim is that this modification achieves optimal asymptotic time complexity (O(µd²)) and storage complexity without significantly increasing the number of objective function evaluations required for convergence.

The paper makes a strong theoretical contribution by justifying why replacing C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹ (where A is the Cholesky factor) in the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism does not deteriorate performance. The authors prove that the introduced error vanishes as the algorithm converges, supported by Lemma 1 showing that the rotation matrix ETₜ₋₁Eₜ converges to identity under reasonable assumptions. The experimental evaluation across multiple benchmark functions and dimensions (4-256) demonstrates that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES but achieves significant runtime improvements (up to 20x faster for d=64), with the speedup increasing with dimensionality.

Strengths include a clear theoretical foundation, well-designed experiments showing consistent performance across different problem types, and practical relevance to scaling CMA-ES for larger problems. The paper properly contextualizes its contribution within the existing literature, acknowledging prior work on Cholesky-based updates for specialized CMA-ES variants.

A minor limitation is that the paper doesn't thoroughly explore the impact of the modification on the convergence rate, though the empirical results suggest it's comparable. The authors might consider discussing how their approach handles cases where the covariance matrix becomes ill-conditioned during optimization.

This work makes a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization by addressing a practical bottleneck in one of the most widely used algorithms. The proposed method is both theoretically sound and empirically validated, offering a practical solution for scaling CMA-ES to higher-dimensional problems without compromising optimization quality. The paper is well-written, clear, and makes a significant contribution to the state of the art in evolutionary optimization. I recommend acceptance.
