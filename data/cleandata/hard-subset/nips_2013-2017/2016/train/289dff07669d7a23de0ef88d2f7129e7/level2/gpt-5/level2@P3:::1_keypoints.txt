- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain/update triangular Cholesky factor A to avoid costly eigendecompositions while preserving evaluation-wise search behavior.  
- Approximates CSA by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, enabling O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) memory updates.  
- Lemma 1 argues rotation between A^{-1} and C^{-1/2} vanishes as normalized covariance converges, motivating the CSA approximation.  
- Empirically, Cholesky-CMA-ES matches reference CMA-ES in function evaluations but yields large wall-clock speedups, increasing with dimension (e.g., ~20× at d=64).  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-update work (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) and contrasts with other complexity-reduction approaches (sep/LM/sample reuse).  
- Targets regime where full covariance adaptation is necessary (non-separable, ill-conditioned problems).  
- Strength: asymptotically optimal time and storage for full-covariance CMA-ES with practical wall-clock improvements.  
- Strength: careful rank-one Cholesky assembly implements standard rank-1 and rank-µ updates without explicit decompositions.  
- Strength: triangular inversion enables efficient CSA computation using A^{-1}.  
- Strength: Lemma 1 provides a plausible theoretical rationale for the CSA approximation near stationarity.  
- Strength: thorough experiments across d=4–256 with 100 trials, including comparisons to non-triangular Cholesky and delayed updates.  
- Strength: clear algorithms and reproducibility (Shark, LAPACK, default CMA-ES parameters).  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees limited — central assumption of normalized C_t convergence not proven, and no quantitative error bounds for CSA approximation.  
- Weakness: departure from affine/rotation invariance of CSA not fully quantified; more direct empirical invariance evidence needed in main text.  
- Weakness: experimental reporting gaps — excluded non-convergent Rosenbrock runs, no success rates reported, and conditional vs unconditional time-to-target not shown.  
- Weakness: robustness to noise and constrained problems not evaluated; CSA approximation behavior under noise unknown.  
- Weakness: missing comparisons to widely used fast baselines (sep-CMA, LM-CMA) to contextualize practical trade-offs.  
- Weakness: memory usage and cache effects are noted but not quantified; only CPU runtimes reported.  
- Technical inaccuracy: claim that Cholesky diagonal entries are square roots of eigenvalues is false — they are square roots of Cholesky pivots, not eigenvalues.  
- Minor issues: unusual use of ω(d^2) for decomposition cost (should state O(d^3)) and formatting artifacts in references.  
- Suggestion: correct the Cholesky/eigenvalue claim and clarify what the diagonal represents.  
- Suggestion: empirically quantify CSA approximation error (||p_σ^{C^{-1/2}}|| vs ||p_σ^{A^{-1}}||) and σ trajectory sensitivity.  
- Suggestion: report success rates, include previously excluded Rosenbrock trials, and run noise experiments for robustness.  
- Suggestion: add brief comparisons to sep-CMA and LM-CMA where full covariance is still feasible, and measure memory/cache sensitivity.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong practical speedups with unchanged evaluation efficiency, simple elegant modification, and plausible theoretical justification.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited formal guarantees for CSA approximation, one incorrect technical claim, and incomplete experimental reporting/comparisons.  
- Overall: solid, practically impactful contribution that needs minor corrections and expanded empirical analysis of the CSA approximation before publication.
