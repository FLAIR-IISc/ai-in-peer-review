- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that stores/updates only triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T), achieving O(µ d^2) time and optimal memory per iteration by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA, with argument that induced rotation error vanishes as covariance stabilizes.  
- Provides pseudocode and a rank-one Cholesky update; empirically matches baseline CMA-ES in function evaluations up to d=256 while giving substantial wall-clock speedups and outperforming Suttorp et al. non-triangular method.  
- Builds on prior CMA-ES acceleration work (diagonal/LM variants, direct Cholesky updates, rank-one efficiency) while retaining full covariance adaptation and practical invariances at lower cost.  
- Quality: clear algorithmic derivation and a lemma on rotation stability; comprehensive empirical comparisons (100 trials); theoretical argument is heuristic without finite-sample error bounds; implementation details aid reproducibility.  
- Clarity: well organized with explicit algorithms and rationale; helpful explanation of triangular-factor uniqueness; minor issues include malformed references and missing confidence intervals in figures/tables.  
- Originality: novel extension of fast Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES via a principled CSA modification and a new lemma on rotation convergence.  
- Significance: practically useful when covariance updates dominate runtime (e.g., policy search, hyperparameter tuning); reports up to 20× speedups at d=64 with no loss in evaluation efficiency.  
- Limitations/suggestions: add statistical tests/confidence bands, compare to LM-CMA and sep-CMA, include ablations on CSA approximation during non-stationary/noisy/constraint settings, discuss affine invariance formally, and release code/scripts.  
- Arguments for acceptance: strong practical impact, solid algorithmic contribution, clear presentation, and empirical validation across dimensions and rotations.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical guarantees for the CSA approximation, missing comparisons to strong baselines and noisy settings, and minor reporting/formatting gaps.
