- Addresses CMA-ES’s O(d^3) bottleneck caused by eigen-decomposition and CSA’s need for C^(-1/2).
- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates Cholesky factor A with rank-1 updates, reducing update cost to O(µ d^2).
- Approximates C^(-1/2) by A^(-1) and justifies this with Lemma 1 relying on the rotational component stabilizing as covariance shape converges.
- Theoretical justification argues error from ignoring the rotation diminishes due to CSA’s exponentially weighted averaging.
- Empirical results show identical function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES but large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20x at d=64).
- Significance: makes full-covariance CMA-ES computationally feasible at much higher dimensions and highly applicable in ML tasks.
- Originality/technical quality: novel, principled solution to the CSA adaptation issue that previous Cholesky approaches struggled with.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally well-written with a clear problem statement, method, and experiments.
- Weakness: incorrect claim that the diagonal of A yields eigenvalues—eigenvalues still require O(d^3) computation.
- Weakness: Lemma 1’s proof depends on convergence of the normalized covariance; authors should expand discussion/cite convergence literature.
- Weakness: proof of Lemma 1 is concise; add a reference for the continuity of the RQ-decomposition to strengthen rigor.
- Overall: excellent, practically significant contribution with minor correctable issues; strong recommendation for acceptance.
