This paper presents Cholesky-CMA-ES, a computationally efficient variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES). The core contribution is a method for directly updating the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix, rather than re-computing the decomposition from the full matrix in each step. This reduces the per-iteration time complexity for the update from O(d^3) to an asymptotically optimal O(d^2) and lowers the storage complexity. To achieve this, the authors modify the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism, replacing the inverse matrix square root with the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor. This work builds upon previous efforts to reduce CMA-ES complexity but is novel in successfully applying the Cholesky update to the standard CMA-ES with a convincing theoretical justification for the required approximation.

The paper’s strengths are significant.
*   **Quality & Significance:** The work is technically sound and addresses a critical bottleneck in a widely used optimization algorithm. The speed-up is not just theoretical; the empirical results show a dramatic reduction in wall-clock time on benchmark problems (e.g., 20x faster for d=64) without degrading optimization performance in terms of function evaluations. This makes CMA-ES more practical for high-dimensional problems common in machine learning, a significant contribution to the field. The claims are well-supported by both a theoretical argument (Lemma 1) and a thorough experimental comparison.
*   **Originality & Clarity:** The paper is clearly written and well-organized. It lucidly explains the problem, the proposed solution, and its theoretical underpinnings. The novelty lies in providing a valid approximation for the CSA step that allows the use of a fast and stable triangular Cholesky update, a problem that had hindered previous attempts.

The paper has one minor weakness that can be easily addressed.
*   **Technical Accuracy:** In the "Properties of the update rule" section, the authors incorrectly state that the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the factorized matrix. This is only true if the matrix is diagonal. This claim should be corrected in the final version.

Overall, this is an excellent paper presenting a valuable and practical improvement to a cornerstone algorithm. The benefits are clearly demonstrated and well-justified, and the work will likely be of immediate use to researchers and practitioners. I strongly recommend acceptance.
