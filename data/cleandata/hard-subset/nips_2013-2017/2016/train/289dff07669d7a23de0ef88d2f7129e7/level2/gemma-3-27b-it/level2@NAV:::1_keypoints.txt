- Proposes updating the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix to reduce runtime and memory complexity.  
- Avoids costly eigenvalue decomposition while preserving the required matrix square root for CMA-ES.  
- Provides theoretical justification showing convergence of the Cholesky factor and that algorithm convergence properties are maintained.  
- Properly connects to and differentiates from prior work on Cholesky updates.  
- Demonstrates consistent wall-clock speedups in experiments, especially in higher dimensions, without degrading function-evaluation performance.  
- Includes rotation invariance testing and analysis of coordinate system changes.  
- Clearly identifies covariance updates as the main computational bottleneck in CMA-ES.  
- Writing and presentation are clear and well-organized.  
- Lacks detailed analysis of the approximation error introduced by using the Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root and its impact on specific problem types.  
- Discussion of memory effects (e.g., exceeding L2 cache) and memory access patterns is cursory and needs deeper investigation.  
- Experimental comparisons are comprehensive for CMA-ES variants but should include more recent or advanced optimization algorithms.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions addressing approximation impact, scalability/memory analysis, and broader algorithmic comparisons.
