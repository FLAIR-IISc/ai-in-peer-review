- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates a triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to avoid recomputing matrix square roots, replacing C^{-1/2}(Δm/σ) with A^{-1}(Δm/σ) for step-size adaptation.
- Complexity: Enables quadratic-time O(µ d^2) rank-one updates on A, avoiding cubic-time decompositions and reducing memory by storing only a triangular factor.
- Theoretical justification: Argues RQ-decomposition C^{1/2}=A E has stabilizing rotations E_t, so omitting E_t has vanishing effect on the CSA norm as covariance shape converges.
- Empirical results: Matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluations while achieving large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) on benchmarks up to d=256.
- Strength — practical impact: Substantial time and memory savings while preserving solution quality, useful for ML and black-box optimization.
- Strength — algorithmic contribution: Provides numerically stable rank-one updates for triangular Cholesky factor including rank-µ updates and efficient inversion.
- Strength — theory & clarity: Offers a clean convergence-based argument and clear algorithmic presentation with helpful algorithms.
- Strength — experiments: Comprehensive evaluation on rotated/unrotated problems and multiple baselines with careful timing using Shark/LAPACK.
- Weakness — incorrect eigenvalue claim: The paper wrongly states triangular Cholesky diagonals are square roots of eigenvalues; needs correction and clarification of what is obtained for free.
- Weakness — theory scope: Lemma relies on convergence of shape-normalized covariance; lacks bounds on CSA error vs. recent rotation drift and should summarize rotation diagnostics in main text.
- Weakness — invariance concerns: Dropping rotation E_t breaks strict affine invariance; recommend reporting success rates across random rotations and step-size dynamic statistics.
- Weakness — experimental reporting: Excluding non-convergent Rosenbrock runs biases results; report success rates and conditional/unconditional time-to-target and clarify evaluation time assumptions.
- Weakness — numerical stability: Need discussion of stability of rank-one updates, roundoff accumulation, references to stable Cholesky update routines, and any periodic re-factorization/safeguards.
- Weakness — reproducibility: Provide Shark integration link/commit, exact parameters, and hardware/BLAS details for timing.
- Quality: Algorithmic development is sound and empirical results support speedups, aside from the eigenvalue error and limited convergence argument.
- Clarity: Generally clear but suggest moving assumptions upfront and bringing rotation drift and step-size diagnostics into the main text.
- Originality: Extends Cholesky rank-one updates to full CMA-ES with rank-µ and a principled CSA modification distinct from prior work.
- Significance: High practical significance for moderate-to-large dimensional full-covariance CMA-ES users; likely adoption in libraries.
- Questions for authors: Provide bounds/empirical stats on CSA norm error from omitting E_t, refactorization frequency, sensitivity to µ, and success rates on Rosenbrock/rotated multimodal functions.
- Pros for acceptance: Strong practical contribution, simple drop-in change, reasonable theory and thorough empirical support.
- Cons for acceptance: Needs correction of eigenvalue claim, tighter analysis or diagnostics for CSA approximation, and fuller experimental reporting (including failures).
