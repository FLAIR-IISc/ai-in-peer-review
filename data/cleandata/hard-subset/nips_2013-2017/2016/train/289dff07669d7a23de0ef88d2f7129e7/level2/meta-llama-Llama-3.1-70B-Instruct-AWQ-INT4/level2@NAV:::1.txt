This paper presents a novel variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity. The main claim of the paper is that the proposed algorithm reduces the runtime complexity of the CMA-ES with no significant change in the number of objective function evaluations, making it more efficient for large-scale optimization problems.

The support for this claim is provided through a combination of theoretical analysis and empirical experiments. The authors demonstrate that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a better time complexity of O(µd2) for the covariance update and optimal memory complexity, compared to the standard CMA-ES. They also show that the algorithm is numerically stable and provides the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix without additional costs.

The experiments conducted on various benchmark functions demonstrate a significant increase in optimization speed, with the Cholesky-CMA-ES requiring much less wall-clock time compared to the standard CMA-ES, while maintaining the same number of objective function evaluations. The results also show that the speed-up increases with the search space dimensionality.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical foundation. The experiments are well-designed, and the results are presented in a clear and easy-to-understand manner.
