- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, a CMA-ES variant achieving optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity.
- Main claim: reduces runtime complexity of CMA-ES without increasing objective evaluations, enabling large-scale efficiency.
- Theoretical analysis shows O(µ d^2) time for covariance update and optimal memory use versus standard CMA-ES.
- Algorithm is numerically stable and yields covariance eigenvalues without extra cost.
- Empirical benchmarks show significantly lower wall-clock time while keeping the same number of evaluations.
- Speed-up increases with problem dimensionality according to experimental results.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the algorithm and theoretical foundation.
- Strengths: novel and efficient algorithm, strong theoretical and empirical support, clear presentation.
- Weaknesses: assumes familiarity with CMA-ES which may hinder non-experts; experiments limited to specific benchmark functions.
- Originality: novel use of triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the standard square root in CMA-ES.
- Significance: potential impact for large-scale optimization and machine-learning applications.
- Overall recommendation: accept (supported by theory and experiments).
