**Summary of the Main Ideas**

The paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a powerful real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm. The standard CMA-ES requires computing the square root of the covariance matrix, which dominates the time and space complexity. The authors introduce a numerically stable quadratic-time covariance matrix update scheme using triangular Cholesky factors, which reduces the runtime complexity and memory requirements. They also provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm, called Cholesky-CMA-ES.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The paper builds upon previous work on CMA-ES and its variants, including the use of Cholesky factorization for updating the covariance matrix. However, the authors' approach differs from previous attempts in that it provides a theoretical justification for the convergence of the algorithm and achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity.

**Strengths**

1. **Improved efficiency**: The Cholesky-CMA-ES reduces the runtime complexity and memory requirements of the standard CMA-ES, making it more efficient for large-scale optimization problems.
2. **Theoretical justification**: The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the algorithm, which is a significant contribution to the field.
3. **Empirical evaluation**: The paper includes an empirical evaluation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES on various benchmark functions, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.

**Weaknesses**

1. **Limited applicability**: The Cholesky-CMA-ES is designed for non-separable ill-conditioned problems, which may limit its applicability to other types of optimization problems.
2. **Assumptions**: The authors assume that the covariance matrix converges to a certain shape, which may not always be the case in practice.
3. **Comparison to other algorithms**: The paper only compares the Cholesky-CMA-ES to other CMA-ES variants and not to other optimization algorithms, which may limit the understanding of its performance in a broader context.

**Originality**

The paper presents a novel approach to updating the covariance matrix in CMA-ES using triangular Cholesky factors. The authors' theoretical justification for the convergence of the algorithm and their empirical evaluation of its performance are significant contributions to the field.

**Significance**

The Cholesky-CMA-ES has the potential to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of CMA-ES in large-scale optimization problems, particularly in machine learning applications. The paper's contributions to the theoretical understanding of the algorithm and its empirical evaluation make it a significant contribution to the field.
