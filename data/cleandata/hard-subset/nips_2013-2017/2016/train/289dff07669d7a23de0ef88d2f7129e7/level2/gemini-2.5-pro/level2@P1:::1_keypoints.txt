- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to reduce covariance-update cost to O(µ d^2) and replace C^{-1/2} in CSA with A^{-1}, justified by a lemma showing approximation error vanishes as covariance converges.

- High technical quality: algorithm clearly described and motivated; theoretical argument plausible though contingent on a convergence assumption.

- Thorough experimental validation: matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially at large dimensions.

- Well-written and organized paper: concise background, clear explanation of the CSA modification, helpful pseudo-code, and informative figures.

- Original contribution: first to successfully integrate Cholesky updates into the standard CMA-ES by addressing the CSA path’s reliance on the matrix square root via a novel approximation and RQ-decomposition justification.

- Significant impact: makes CMA-ES practical for higher-dimensional and cheap-evaluation settings and is likely to be widely adopted.

- Argument for acceptance — practical impact: provides large, practical speedups for a widely used optimization algorithm.

- Argument for acceptance — soundness: method supported by plausible theory and strong empirical evidence across benchmarks.

- Argument for acceptance — clarity and completeness: the paper is clear, well-presented, and implements a complete solution from motivation to validation.

- Argument for acceptance — novelty: solves a long-standing challenge of applying Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES.

- Argument against acceptance — theory caveat: relies on convergence of the normalized covariance matrix; behavior when this fails is not discussed.

- Argument against acceptance — minor technical error: claim that Cholesky diagonal gives covariance eigenvalues appears incorrect and needs correction.
