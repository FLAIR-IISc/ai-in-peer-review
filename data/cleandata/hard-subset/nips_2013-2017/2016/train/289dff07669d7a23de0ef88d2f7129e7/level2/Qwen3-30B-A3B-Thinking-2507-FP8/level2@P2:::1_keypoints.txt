- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time/space while maintaining performance.
- Complexity claim: Authors claim O(µ d²) time for covariance updates versus O(d³) for standard CMA-ES.
- Theoretical strength: Lemma 1 argues the approximation error (using A⁻¹ₜ instead of C⁻¹/²ₜ) vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Prior work: Properly contrasts with earlier Cholesky-based CMA-ES attempts and provides theoretical guarantees those lacked.
- Empirical speedup: Shows same number of function evaluations as CMA-ES but up to ~20× runtime reduction (d=64), with speedup growing with dimensionality.
- Presentation: Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful pseudocode.
- Experimental design: Uses standard benchmark functions across multiple dimensions and measures both function evaluations and wall-clock time.
- Theoretical gap: Lemma 1 assumes Ct converges, but CMA-ES is randomized and Ct may not converge; no analysis of error during early, rapidly changing phases.
- Unsupported claim: The statement that the approximation error is “small” needs stronger empirical or theoretical evidence.
- Ambiguous optimality: “Optimal” complexity is not defined; unclear if O(µ d²) is information-theoretically or practically optimal for full covariance adaptation.
- Numerical stability: Claim of “numerically stable computation” is unsubstantiated despite Cholesky’s known issues on ill-conditioned matrices.
- Limited comparisons: Experiments omit important baselines like LM-CMA and recent low-rank CMA-ES variants referenced but not evaluated.
- Presentation minor issues: Confusing figure references, vague supplementary material, and insufficient detail on how rotation matrix Et was measured.
- Overall assessment: Solid, practically significant contribution with moderate originality; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to strengthen theory, stability evidence, and empirical comparisons.
