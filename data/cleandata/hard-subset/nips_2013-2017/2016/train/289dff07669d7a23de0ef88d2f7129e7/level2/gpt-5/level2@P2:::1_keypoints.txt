- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains lower-triangular A with C = A A^T to reduce per-iteration cost to O(µ d^2) versus cubic for standard CMA-ES.  
- Replaces CSA’s C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, justifying the approximation via a smooth-rotation (polar/RQ) argument that error vanishes asymptotically.  
- Empirically matches reference CMA-ES in function-evaluation efficiency while substantially reducing wall-clock time and outperforming a prior non-triangular Cholesky update.  
- Extends rank-one Cholesky updates to full rank-µ CMA-ES updates with near-optimal complexity and memory for full-covariance schemes.  
- Addresses a real bottleneck when objective evaluations are cheap and dimensionality is moderate-to-large.  
- Clean, numerically stable algorithmic design using efficient rank-one updates and practical O(d^2) computation of A^{-1}.  
- Thoughtful treatment of CSA mismatch; provides a plausible asymptotic rationale and supporting experiments.  
- Strong empirical evidence across benchmarks and rotations; good software engineering (LAPACK, Shark integration).  
- Theoretical justification is limited: Lemma 1 is continuity-based and provides no finite-time bounds on CSA approximation or σ dynamics during transients.  
- Incorrect technical claim: diagonal of Cholesky factor are not generally square roots of covariance eigenvalues; eigenvalues relate to singular values of A, not diag(A).  
- Experimental bias: Rosenbrock results exclude non-convergent runs, risking inflated efficiency estimates; success rates/time-to-target should be reported.  
- Missing comparisons: omits limited-memory and other scalable baselines (e.g., LM-CMA) in wall-clock evaluations for broader context.  
- Clarity issues: some important details relegated to supplement (e.g., rotation change metric), rankOneUpdate stability/β constraints not emphasized, and minor typos remain.  
- Overall technically sound for updates and complexity claims; CSA argument plausible but not rigorous; experiments support main claims.  
- Originality: novel and useful extension of Cholesky-update ideas to full rank-µ CMA-ES with practical performance gains.  
- High practical significance for ML/RL users when evaluations are cheap; likely adoptable in libraries though theoretical impact is modest.  
- Suggestions: correct eigenvalue claim, quantify CSA approximation error (even worst-case bounds), report success rates/include failed runs (ECDFs/performance profiles), compare with LM-CMA and similar baselines, discuss numerical stability/β constraints and regularization, and provide code/commit hashes for reproducibility.  
- Pros for acceptance: significant speedups without loss in evaluations, simple well-motivated method, convincing empirical results.  
- Cons for acceptance: qualitative/asymptotic CSA justification only, misstatement about eigenvalues, and biased reporting on at least one benchmark.  
- Recommendation: Leaning accept, contingent on correcting the eigenvalue claim and clarifying experimental reporting.
