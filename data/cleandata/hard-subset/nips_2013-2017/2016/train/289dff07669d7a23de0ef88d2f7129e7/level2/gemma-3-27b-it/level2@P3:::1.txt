This paper introduces a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) aimed at reducing computational complexity during covariance matrix updates. The core idea is to maintain and update the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix itself, leveraging existing efficient update methods for Cholesky factors. A theoretical justification is provided to argue that this modification does not significantly alter the algorithm’s performance, and empirical results demonstrate significant speed-ups, particularly in higher dimensions. The work builds upon previous attempts to use Cholesky factorization within CMA-ES variations, specifically addressing the limitations of applying this approach to the standard CMA-ES.

The strengths of the paper are substantial. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES addresses a key bottleneck in the standard CMA-ES – the computational cost of covariance matrix manipulation. The theoretical analysis, particularly Lemma 1 regarding the convergence of the rotation matrix, provides a solid foundation for the proposed modification and convincingly argues why the algorithm’s fundamental behavior remains intact. The empirical results convincingly demonstrate the significant runtime improvements achievable with the Cholesky-CMA-ES, especially as dimensionality increases.  The comparison with existing CMA-ES variants (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES) is thorough and highlights the advantages of the proposed approach.  The inclusion of experiments specifically testing rotation invariance is a thoughtful addition. The writing is generally clear and well-organized, with sufficient detail provided to understand the proposed method and its justification.  Linking the work to broader concepts like information geometry further strengthens the paper.

However, there are areas where the paper could be improved. While the paper demonstrates that the number of function evaluations remains comparable, a more in-depth analysis of the *quality* of the solutions found could be valuable.  Specifically, is there a slight trade-off in solution accuracy for the speed gain? A more detailed discussion of the sensitivity of the algorithm to numerical precision issues, which can be problematic with Cholesky decompositions, would be beneficial. The discussion of the L2 cache limitations at d=256 is brief; providing more detail about the performance degradation observed and potential mitigation strategies would add value. While Lemma 1 provides theoretical justification, a more detailed discussion of the conditions under which the error introduced by the approximation is small would be helpful. The reference to Akimoto et al. (2012) is a bit vague; specifying which aspect of their work is relevant would improve clarity.  Finally, the experimental setup, while reasonably thorough, could benefit from a more rigorous statistical analysis to assess the significance of the observed performance differences.

Arguments for acceptance:
*   The paper addresses a significant bottleneck in a widely-used optimization algorithm.
*   The proposed method offers a substantial improvement in runtime, particularly in high dimensions.
*   The theoretical analysis provides a convincing justification for the modification.
*   The empirical results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the approach.

Arguments against acceptance:
*   The paper lacks a detailed analysis of the potential trade-off between runtime and solution quality.
*   The discussion of numerical stability and potential limitations is somewhat limited.
*   The statistical analysis of the experimental results could be more rigorous.



Overall, this is a strong paper with a clear contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES represents a valuable improvement to the standard CMA-ES, and the thorough analysis and empirical validation make a compelling case for its adoption. Addressing the minor weaknesses outlined above would further strengthen the paper.  This reviewer recommends acceptance.
