## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES): the computational and memory cost of updating and storing the covariance matrix. The authors propose a Cholesky-CMA-ES variant leveraging a quadratic-time covariance matrix update scheme based on maintaining triangular Cholesky factors, coupled with a modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism. The core idea – replacing the matrix square root with its Cholesky factor – is well-motivated and builds upon prior work attempting similar optimizations (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015). 

The paper clearly outlines the problem and its significance, particularly for high-dimensional optimization problems common in machine learning applications like reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning. The theoretical justification for the modification, arguing that it doesn’t fundamentally alter the algorithm’s convergence behavior, is a key strength. The authors correctly identify the potential issue with coordinate system rotations introduced by the Cholesky decomposition and provide a compelling argument, supported by Lemma 1, that this error diminishes with convergence.

The experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing the Cholesky-CMA-ES against the standard CMA-ES with different update frequencies and a related approach (Suttorp-CMA-ES) across a range of benchmark functions and dimensions. The results convincingly demonstrate that the proposed method achieves significant runtime improvements, especially in higher dimensions, without sacrificing the number of function evaluations. 

However, the paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations. While the authors acknowledge the quadratic scaling with dimensionality, a more nuanced analysis of scenarios where this becomes a bottleneck would be valuable.  Furthermore, the impact of numerical stability with extremely ill-conditioned problems isn’t explicitly addressed.  The connection to existing work on low-rank covariance matrix approximations (e.g., Loshchilov, 2014, 2015) could be further explored, positioning the Cholesky-CMA-ES as a complementary approach.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Addresses a significant practical limitation of CMA-ES.
*   Provides a theoretically sound and well-motivated approach.
*   Demonstrates substantial runtime improvements through comprehensive experiments.
*   Clear and well-written presentation.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   Limited discussion of limitations, particularly concerning numerical stability and scaling to very high dimensions.
*   Could benefit from a more detailed comparison to related work on low-rank approximations.



**Overall:** This is a strong paper presenting a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a practical and efficient improvement to a widely used algorithm. I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the limitations mentioned above.
