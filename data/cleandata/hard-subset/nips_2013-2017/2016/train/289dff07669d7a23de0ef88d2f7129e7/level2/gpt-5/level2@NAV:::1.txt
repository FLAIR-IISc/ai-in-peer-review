Summary and relation to prior work:
The paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of CMA-ES that updates and stores only the triangular Cholesky factor A of the covariance C = A A^T, yielding O(µ d^2) time and optimal memory complexity per iteration. The key change is to modify CSA by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, justified by an analysis showing that the induced rotation error vanishes as the covariance stabilizes; pseudocode and a rank-one Cholesky update are provided. Empirically, on standard rotation-invariant benchmarks up to d = 256, the method matches the baseline CMA-ES in function evaluations while achieving substantial wall-clock speedups, and outperforms a non-triangular Cholesky update method (Suttorp et al., 2009). This builds on prior acceleration strategies for CMA-ES (diagonal/limited-memory variants: Ros & Hansen, Loshchilov; direct Cholesky updates: Suttorp et al.; rank-one efficiency: Krause & Igel) and addresses their shortcomings by retaining full covariance adaptation and practical invariances while reducing computational cost.

Quality:
The claims are supported by a clear algorithmic derivation, a simple but pertinent lemma about the stability of the rotation term in CSA, and comprehensive empirical comparisons across multiple functions and dimensions with 100 trials. The theoretical argument is heuristic regarding CSA equivalence (no finite-sample error bounds), but consistent with the observed evaluation counts. Implementation details (Shark, LAPACK, defaults) aid reproducibility.

Clarity:
The paper is well organized, with concise background, explicit algorithms, and rationale for each design choice. The explanation of why triangular factors avoid the non-uniqueness issues is helpful. Minor issues: some references appear malformed; figures/tables should include confidence intervals.

Originality:
Extending fast Cholesky updates to the standard CMA-ES with a principled CSA modification and showing optimal asymptotic update/storage complexity is novel relative to Suttorp et al. and limited-memory/diagonal variants. The lemma about rotation convergence provides new insight.

Significance:
The approach is practically useful whenever covariance updates dominate runtime (common in ML policy search and hyperparameter tuning). Speedups of up to 20x at d=64 are compelling, with no loss in evaluation efficiency.

Limitations and suggestions:
- Provide statistical tests or confidence bands on evaluation counts and runtimes.
- Compare against LM-CMA and sep-CMA to position the trade-off vs low-memory methods at larger d.
- Include ablations quantifying the CSA approximation error during non-stationary phases and on noisy/constraint benchmarks.
- Discuss affine invariance formally and any impact of breaking exact CSA normalization.
- Release code and scripts for full reproducibility.

Arguments for acceptance:
- Strong practical impact with significant speedups and full-covariance fidelity
- Solid algorithmic contribution and clear presentation
- Empirical validation across dimensions and rotations

Arguments against acceptance:
- Limited theoretical guarantees for CSA approximation
- Missing comparisons to other strong baselines (LM-CMA, sep-CMA) and noisy settings
- Minor reference formatting and statistical reporting gaps
