- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: a numerically stable, quadratic-time covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors that reduces runtime and memory compared to standard CMA-ES and includes a convergence proof.  
- Builds on prior CMA-ES and Cholesky update work but claims novel theoretical convergence justification and optimal asymptotic storage/runtime.  
- Strength: Improves efficiency for large-scale problems by reducing time and memory complexity.  
- Strength: Provides theoretical justification for algorithm convergence.  
- Strength: Includes empirical benchmarks showing effectiveness and efficiency.  
- Weakness: Targeted mainly at non-separable ill-conditioned problems, limiting broader applicability.  
- Weakness: Relies on assumptions that the covariance matrix converges to a specific shape, which may not hold in practice.  
- Weakness: Comparisons are limited to CMA-ES variants and omit other optimizer classes, limiting broader performance context.  
- Originality: Introduces a novel Cholesky-based covariance update with theoretical and empirical contributions.  
- Significance: Potential to make CMA-ES more practical for large-scale optimization and relevant ML applications.  
- Pro-accept: Novel, efficient update method with convergence proof and empirical support.  
- Con-accept: Questionable assumptions about covariance convergence, limited applicability, and narrow baselines.  
- Revision 1: Clarify and justify the assumptions about covariance convergence and their practical implications.  
- Revision 2: Add comparisons to other optimization algorithms (e.g., quasi-Newton, SGD) for broader context.  
- Revision 3: Discuss limitations explicitly and provide guidance on problem settings where Cholesky-CMA-ES is appropriate.
