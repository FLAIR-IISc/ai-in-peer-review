- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance to reduce computational cost.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) arguing the modification does not significantly change algorithm behavior.  
- Empirical results show substantial runtime speed-ups, especially in high dimensions.  
- Thorough comparisons against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES demonstrate advantages.  
- Includes experiments testing rotation invariance.  
- Connects the method to broader concepts like information geometry; writing is clear and organized.  
- Strength: addresses a major computational bottleneck in standard CMA-ES covariance updates.  
- Strength: convincing theoretical foundation (rotation convergence) and practical speed gains.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed analysis of potential trade-offs in solution quality despite similar function-evaluation counts.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of numerical stability and precision issues inherent to Cholesky decompositions.  
- Weakness: brief and underdeveloped discussion of L2 cache limitations at d=256 and mitigation strategies.  
- Weakness: Lemma 1 needs more discussion on the conditions under which approximation error remains small.  
- Weakness: citation to Akimoto et al. (2012) is vague about which results are relevant.  
- Weakness: experimental results would benefit from more rigorous statistical analysis.  
- Overall: strong contribution to derivative-free optimization; reviewer recommends acceptance subject to addressing minor weaknesses.
