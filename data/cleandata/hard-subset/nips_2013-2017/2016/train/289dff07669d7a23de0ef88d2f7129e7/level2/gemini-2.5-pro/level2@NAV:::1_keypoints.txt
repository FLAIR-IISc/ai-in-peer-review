- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the triangular Cholesky factor directly, reducing per-iteration update time from O(d^3) to O(d^2) and lowering storage.
- Modifies cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) by using the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the inverse matrix square root.
- Novel contribution: successfully applies a fast, stable Cholesky update to standard CMA-ES with a theoretical justification (Lemma 1) for the approximation used.
- Empirical results show dramatic wall-clock speed-ups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) with no loss in optimization performance measured by function evaluations.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and explains the problem, solution, and theoretical basis lucidly.
- Minor technical error: incorrectly claims Cholesky diagonal elements are square roots of eigenvalues (only true for diagonal matrices) — needs correction.
- Overall assessment: valuable, practical improvement to CMA-ES with strong evidence; strongly recommended for acceptance.
