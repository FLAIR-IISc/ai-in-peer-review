- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain/update lower-triangular A with C = A A^T to avoid full eigendecomposition.  
- Rewrites rank-1 plus rank-µ covariance update as sequential rank-1 updates to A, yielding O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) storage.  
- Replaces C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA to avoid expensive matrix-square-root operations.  
- Lemma 1 argues the rotational discrepancy from using A^{-1} vanishes asymptotically when normalized covariance converges.  
- Empirically matches CMA-ES in function evaluations while being substantially faster in wall-clock time, outperforming delayed-update and a non-triangular Cholesky method.  
- Extends prior Cholesky updates from restricted CMA-ES variants and generalizes Krause & Igel (2015) to rank-µ full CMA-ES.  
- Theoretical derivation of O(µ d^2) rank-1 triangular updates is sound and aligned with prior fast Cholesky work.  
- Key approximation (C^{-1/2} → A^{-1}) is justified asymptotically but lacks non-asymptotic error bounds and guarantees in non-convergent regimes.  
- Incorrect technical claim: diagonal of Cholesky factor does not generally give eigenvalues of C; only log-determinant and triangular solves are “free.”  
- Benchmarks (various standard functions, random rotations, up to d=256) are appropriate and show growing wall-time benefits with dimension.  
- Excluding non-convergent Rosenbrock trials biases results; success rates and failures should be reported and included in summaries.  
- Recommend reporting statistical variability (medians/CIs) and more precise hardware/cache details due to observed cache effects at d=256.  
- Missing comparison to limited-memory CMA-ES (LM-CMA) to contextualize trade-offs at higher dimensions.  
- Implementation details (Shark and LAPACK) and complexity claims are clearly stated; open-source details implied.  
- Suggest clarifying uniqueness/continuity conditions for the RQ/QR decomposition used in Lemma 1 (e.g., positive diagonal convention).  
- Clarify whether replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} further weakens affine-invariance and relate this to the empirical rotation study with quantitative summaries in the main text.  
- Discuss numerical stability of repeated rank-1 Cholesky updates (need for periodic re-factorization, jitter) and state safeguards used in code.  
- Originality: novel integration of fast triangular Cholesky updates with full rank-µ CMA-ES and ASA modification with asymptotic rotational-error justification.  
- Significance: practical speedups for full covariance adaptation make the approach valuable for derivative-free ML optimization at moderate-to-high dimensions.  
- Pros: clear algorithmic contribution, asymptotically optimal complexity, practical speedups without loss in evaluation efficiency, theoretical rationale, clean integration.  
- Cons: incorrect eigenvalue claim, asymptotic-only theoretical justification, biased empirical reporting on Rosenbrock, missing LM-CMA comparison, unclear numerical-stability safeguards.  
- Questions for authors: report Rosenbrock success rates including failures; state if/when fresh Cholesky refactorization is done; provide quantitative norms for rotational change in main text; compare to LM-CMA at higher dimensions.  
- Overall assessment: technically solid and practically meaningful improvement to CMA-ES pending corrections to the eigenvalue claim and clearer empirical and stability reporting.
