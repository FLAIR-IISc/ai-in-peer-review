- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update covariance via Cholesky factor to avoid explicit matrix square root and reduce cost.  
- Claims preservation of algorithm behavior and convergence, with theoretical justification (including Lemma 1 on rotation matrices).  
- Empirical results show substantial speedups, especially in high dimensions.  
- Argues optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity for full covariance adaptation.  
- Well-motivated problem and clear differentiation from prior work.  
- Clear presentation and logical organization of method and theory.  
- Comprehensive experiments comparing to several baselines across benchmarks and dimensions.  
- Strength: significant practical runtime improvement for large-scale problems.  
- Strength: theoretical justification addressing key concerns about the modification.  
- Strength: supports claim of optimal complexity and provides solid empirical validation.  
- Weakness: theoretical justification is abstract and could use a more intuitive explanation.  
- Weakness: impact of rotation/approximation error is acknowledged but not thoroughly analyzed experimentally.  
- Weakness: limited exploration of parameter sensitivity; mostly default CMA-ES parameters used.  
- Weakness: brief acknowledgment of L2 cache effects without deeper analysis of memory/cache influences.  
- Weakness: LM-CMA is included but not discussed in sufficient comparative detail.  
- For acceptance: novel, practical, theoretically grounded, and empirically effective; enables larger-scale CMA-ES.  
- Against acceptance: accessibility of theory, incomplete analysis of approximation error, and limited sensitivity/comparison studies.  
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to provide more intuitive theory explanation and analyze rotation error.
