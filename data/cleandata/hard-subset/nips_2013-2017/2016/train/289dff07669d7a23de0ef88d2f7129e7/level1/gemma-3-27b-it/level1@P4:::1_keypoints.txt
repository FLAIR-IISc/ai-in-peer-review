- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update Cholesky factor instead of covariance matrix square root, yielding a numerically stable, quadratic-time update.
- Motivated by high computational and memory cost of covariance updates in standard CMA-ES, especially in high dimensions.
- Lemma 1 provides theoretical justification by showing convergence of the Cholesky factor, assuming Ct converges.
- Empirical evaluation is extensive: compares against standard CMA-ES and variants across benchmark functions and dimensions.
- Shows similar performance in function evaluations while significantly reducing runtime and memory usage.
- Technical soundness: leverages established numerical linear algebra and presents convincing analysis (with noted assumptions).
- Clarity: well-written with helpful Algorithm 1 and 2 for implementation details.
- Originality: novel application of Cholesky updates within standard CMA-ES despite related prior works.
- Significance: runtime and memory improvements expand CMA-ES applicability to larger-scale problems (e.g., hyperparameter tuning, RL).
- Empirical validation is compelling, with runtime graphs and comprehensive comparisons.
- Theoretical caveat: relies on Ct convergence, which is not guaranteed in non-stationary or complex landscapes.
- Comparison to Suttorp-CMA-ES is brief and would benefit from a more detailed implementation/trade-off analysis.
- Cache/memory hierarchy effects are noted (e.g., at d=256) but not thoroughly investigated at very high dimensions.
- Limited discussion on parameter sensitivity; mostly uses default CMA-ES settings without exploring tuning.
- Overall recommendation: accept—strong practical impact and empirical evidence outweigh theoretical caveats.
