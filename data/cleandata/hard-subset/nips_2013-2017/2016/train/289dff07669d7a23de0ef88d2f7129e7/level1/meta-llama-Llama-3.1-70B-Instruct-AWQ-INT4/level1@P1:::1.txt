**Summary**

This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm by using a triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the matrix square root. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical experiments on various benchmark functions.

**Relation to Previous Work**

The CMA-ES algorithm is a well-established method for real-valued derivative-free optimization, and several variants have been proposed to improve its efficiency. The authors build upon previous work by Suttorp et al. [2009] and Krause and Igel [2015], who proposed updating the Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix instead of the covariance matrix itself. However, the original CMA-ES relies on the matrix square root, which cannot be replaced one-to-one by a Cholesky factor. The authors' contribution is to extend the update to the standard CMA-ES, leading to a runtime of O(µd^2), which is asymptotically optimal.
