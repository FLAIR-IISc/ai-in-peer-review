## Review of CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) aimed at improving its computational efficiency, specifically focusing on the covariance matrix update and storage. The core idea is to utilize the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix directly during adaptation, rather than repeatedly computing the square root or performing eigenvalue decomposition. The authors argue this leads to quadratic time complexity for the update, optimal storage, and doesn’t significantly alter the algorithm’s convergence behavior. 

The paper effectively situates the work within the context of existing CMA-ES improvements, such as delayed updates and restricted model complexity. It clearly explains the motivation – the computational bottleneck of covariance matrix operations – and provides a strong theoretical justification for the proposed modification, citing the convergence properties maintained through a careful analysis of coordinate system rotations.  The authors appropriately reference relevant work, including Suttorp et al.’s Cholesky-based approaches and highlight the differences and advantages of their method.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method appears theoretically well-grounded, with a convincing argument for maintaining CMA-ES's core properties despite the modification. The detailed explanation of the Cholesky update and the discussion of coordinate system rotations are particularly strong.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The explanation of the algorithm and the rationale behind it are clear. Algorithm 1 and 2 provide a concise representation of the method.
* **Originality:** While building upon existing work on Cholesky factorizations, the paper presents a novel approach to integrating this with the *standard* CMA-ES, offering a theoretically justified alternative to previous attempts.
* **Significance:** The potential speedup, especially in high-dimensional problems, is significant. Reducing the time complexity of the covariance update is a crucial improvement for applying CMA-ES to increasingly complex problems.  The empirical results demonstrate a substantial runtime reduction.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Experimental Scope:** The experiments are relatively limited in scope, focusing mainly on benchmark functions. While these demonstrate the speedup, it would be beneficial to see performance on more real-world applications, particularly those commonly used with CMA-ES (e.g., reinforcement learning, hyperparameter tuning).
* **Cache Effects:** The authors acknowledge the impact of cache size on performance at high dimensionality. A more detailed analysis of memory access patterns and potential optimizations would be valuable.
* **Assumptions:** The argument for convergence relies on the assumption of slowly changing covariance matrices. A more robust analysis of scenarios where this assumption might not hold could strengthen the paper.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant bottleneck in CMA-ES.
* Provides a theoretically sound and well-justified modification.
* Demonstrates a substantial speedup in empirical evaluation.
* Clear presentation and strong connection to existing literature.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* Limited scope of experimental validation.
* Some assumptions regarding convergence could benefit from further investigation.




**Overall:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a compelling improvement to a widely used algorithm. While the experimental validation could be expanded, the theoretical justification and demonstrated speedup are convincing.  I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the points raised above.
