This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, termed Cholesky-CMA-ES, which utilizes a Cholesky decomposition instead of directly computing the square root of the covariance matrix for updates. The motivation stems from the computational cost associated with covariance matrix updates, particularly in high-dimensional spaces, which dominates the runtime of standard CMA-ES. The authors present a numerically stable, quadratic-time update scheme and argue that the modification preserves the core behavior of the algorithm, maintaining similar performance in terms of function evaluations while significantly reducing runtime and memory usage.

The paper is well-structured, beginning with a clear introduction outlining the problem and the proposed solution. It builds upon the existing literature on CMA-ES and related optimization techniques, citing relevant works such as Hansen & Ostermeier (2001), Beyer (2007), and Suttorp et al. (2009). The theoretical justification for the proposed approach is presented with Lemma 1, which demonstrates the convergence of the Cholesky factor. The experimental section is comprehensive, comparing Cholesky-CMA-ES against standard CMA-ES and other variants on a range of benchmark functions, varying the dimensionality to highlight the benefits of the proposed method.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed Cholesky-based update is mathematically sound and leverages established numerical linear algebra techniques. The analysis regarding the convergence of the algorithm is presented convincingly, although relying on assumptions about the convergence of Ct.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow, with clear explanations of the concepts and algorithms involved. Algorithm 1 and 2 are particularly helpful for understanding the implementation details.
*   **Originality:** The idea of utilizing a Cholesky decomposition for covariance matrix updates in the standard CMA-ES framework, accompanied by a theoretical justification to preserve algorithm performance, is novel.  While previous works (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015) explored Cholesky-based updates, this paper tackles the challenges associated with the original CMA-ES's reliance on the matrix square root.
*   **Significance:**  The runtime improvement, especially as dimensionality increases, is significant. This addresses a major bottleneck in applying CMA-ES to larger-scale problems, expanding its applicability to domains like hyperparameter optimization and reinforcement learning.  The reduction in memory footprint is also valuable.
* **Empirical Validation**: The extensive experiments across multiple benchmark functions and dimensionality levels convincingly demonstrate the benefits of the Cholesky-CMA-ES. The included runtime graphs and comparison data are compelling.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Theoretical Caveats:**  The theoretical justification relies on Lemma 1, which assumes the convergence of Ct. While reasonable, this assumption is not universally guaranteed for CMA-ES, especially in non-stationary or highly complex optimization landscapes.
* **Comparison to Suttorp-CMA-ES**: Although the paper mentions and compares against the Suttorp-CMA-ES variant, the description of its implementation feels somewhat brief. A more detailed comparison, perhaps outlining the differences in the update procedure and their respective trade-offs, would strengthen the paper.
*   **Cache Effects:** The authors acknowledge the impact of cache size at d=256, but a more thorough investigation of the algorithm's performance at even higher dimensions, considering memory hierarchy effects, would be beneficial.
* **Limited Discussion on Parameter Sensitivity**: The paper largely uses the default CMA-ES parameters. A brief discussion on the sensitivity of the Cholesky-CMA-ES to parameter tuning, and whether different parameter settings might be optimal, would be valuable.




**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and technically sound approach to a significant performance bottleneck in CMA-ES.
*   The theoretical justification, while not without assumptions, is compelling and provides insights into the behavior of the algorithm.
*   The experimental results demonstrate a significant improvement in runtime without sacrificing performance in terms of function evaluations.
*   The potential impact on applications of CMA-ES in high-dimensional spaces is substantial.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical foundation relies on the convergence of Ct, which is not guaranteed in all scenarios.
* The comparison to existing Cholesky based CMA-ES variants could be strengthened with a more detailed analysis.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses an important practical issue in the application of CMA-ES. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a significant improvement in runtime, particularly for high-dimensional problems, without compromising performance. While the theoretical justification has some caveats, the extensive experimental validation supports the claims made by the authors.  I recommend **acceptance** of this paper.
