- Proposes modifying CMA-ES to update the Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix square root to reduce computational cost.
- Provides a theoretical justification (lemma on Cholesky factor convergence) arguing the approximation shouldn’t significantly affect performance.
- Introduces a well-defined "Cholesky-CMA-ES" algorithm with a proposed step-size adaptation modification.
- Empirical results show substantial runtime speedups, especially in higher dimensions, without increasing the number of function evaluations.
- Experimental setup is comprehensive, comparing against relevant CMA-ES variants (delayed updates, Suttorp’s method) across benchmarks and dimensions.
- Paper correctly identifies the quadratic complexity bottleneck of standard CMA-ES and offers a plausible solution.
- Authors acknowledge and attempt to mitigate potential issues such as loss of invariance from the approximation.
- Clarity is generally good with helpful pseudocode, but some mathematical notation and derivations are dense and could use more step-by-step explanation.
- Related work is cited but could be more concise and include deeper discussion of some references.
- Originality is moderate: Cholesky use is known, but this work extends it to standard CMA-ES with theoretical justification and practical performance gains.
- Significance is high: reducing runtime for CMA-ES in high dimensions is valuable and likely to be adopted by practitioners.
- Limitations: insufficient discussion of cache and performance effects at very high dimensions.
- Limitation: limited exploration of parameter sensitivity and how different settings affect Cholesky-CMA-ES versus standard CMA-ES.
- Overall recommendation: accept — technically sound, well-executed, convincing empirical and theoretical contributions with minor areas for improvement.
