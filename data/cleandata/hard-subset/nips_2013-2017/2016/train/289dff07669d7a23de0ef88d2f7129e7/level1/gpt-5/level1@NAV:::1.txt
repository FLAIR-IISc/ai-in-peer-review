This paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of CMA-ES that maintains and updates the triangular Cholesky factor A of the covariance C instead of C (or C1/2) itself, yielding an O(µ d^2) per-iteration update with optimal storage. To preserve cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) without explicitly computing C−1/2, the authors replace it with A−1 and analyze the induced rotational discrepancy via an RQ decomposition argument: under mild convergence of the normalized covariance, successive rotations Et−1T Et tend to identity, making the CSA error small and vanishing in the limit. Empirically, on standard noiseless benchmarks (Sphere, Cigar/Discus/Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) across d up to 256 and random rotations, Cholesky-CMA-ES matches the function-evaluation efficiency of a strong CMA-ES reference and achieves substantial wall-clock speedups, increasing with d. The work builds on and unifies prior ideas: delayed decompositions in standard CMA-ES (Hansen), restricted models (Ros & Hansen; Loshchilov), and Cholesky-based updates in special CMA-ES variants ((1+1), MO-CMA-ES; Suttorp et al.; Krause & Igel), while retaining the behavior of the standard algorithm.

Quality: The algorithmic derivation and complexity claims are largely sound, and the CSA approximation is justified with a clear, if asymptotic, argument. Experiments are careful and include competitive baselines. However, the claim that “the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the factorized matrix” is incorrect in general; diag(A) relates to leading principal minors, not eigenvalues. One can obtain det(C) for free (via ∏ diag(A)^2) and inexpensive bounds on eigenvalues, but not exact eigenvalues without an eigendecomposition or SVD.

Clarity: The paper is generally well written and organized; pseudocode is helpful. Some references are malformed; tightening the exposition around Lemma 1’s assumptions and the numerical stability of rankOneUpdate (range of β) would help.

Originality: A meaningful extension that makes the Cholesky update compatible with standard CMA-ES and CSA. The CSA modification and its justification are novel and practically important.

Significance: High. Matching evaluation counts while cutting runtime and memory for full-covariance CMA-ES is valuable for black-box optimization in ML.

Pros for acceptance:
- Strong practical impact with clear speedups and optimal storage.
- Maintains evaluation efficiency and near-rotation invariance in practice.
- Simple, implementable modification; code availability noted.

Cons against acceptance:
- Incorrect claim regarding eigenvalues from Cholesky; needs correction.
- No quantitative bound on CSA bias; experiments are limited to noiseless, unconstrained settings.
- Limited discussion of very high-d and cache/implementation effects.
