This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) focusing on reducing computational complexity during covariance matrix updates and storage. The core idea is to utilize the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix square root, combined with a carefully considered adjustment to the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism. The authors convincingly argue and demonstrate that this "Cholesky-CMA-ES" achieves quadratic time complexity for updates while maintaining the algorithmic performance in terms of function evaluations, and reduces memory footprint. This is a significant contribution given the computational bottleneck posed by covariance matrix handling in standard CMA-ES, particularly for high-dimensional problems.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written. The introduction effectively motivates the problem and outlines the proposed solution. The related work section is comprehensive, accurately positioning the work within the existing landscape of CMA-ES variants and highlighting the limitations of previous approaches (e.g., delayed updates, restricted covariance models). The theoretical justification, building on Lemma 1 regarding the convergence of the Cholesky factor, is particularly strong and provides confidence in the proposed modification’s efficacy. The experimental setup is robust, utilizing standard benchmark functions and a thorough comparison against existing CMA-ES implementations. The results consistently demonstrate the significant speedup offered by the Cholesky-CMA-ES, especially as dimensionality increases, without sacrificing performance.

**Strengths:**

* **Significant practical improvement:** The proposed method demonstrably reduces runtime complexity, offering substantial speedups, particularly in high dimensions, without compromising performance.
* **Strong theoretical foundation:** The justification for the modification to the CSA mechanism, based on the convergence properties of the Cholesky factor, is well-reasoned and provides confidence in the algorithm’s behavior.
* **Clear and concise writing:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, making the complex concepts accessible and easy to understand.
* **Comprehensive experimental evaluation:** The experimental setup is thorough, covering a variety of benchmark functions and comparing against state-of-the-art implementations.
* **Optimal complexity:** The algorithm achieves asymptotically optimal time and storage complexity.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited exploration of parameter sensitivity:** While the experiments demonstrate overall performance gains, a deeper investigation into the sensitivity of the Cholesky-CMA-ES to its parameters (e.g., the CSA parameters) compared to the standard CMA-ES might be beneficial.
* **Cache effects:** The authors acknowledge cache effects at d=256 but further investigation into the practical limits of scalability given memory access patterns could strengthen the analysis.
* **Lemma 1's assumptions:** The convergence assumption of Lemma 1 isn’t always met in practice; acknowledging the potential impact of deviations from this assumption, especially during early stages of optimization, would be valuable.
* **Lack of analysis on ill-conditioned problems:** While Diffpowers is included, a more focused analysis on highly ill-conditioned problems, and how the Cholesky factorization specifically handles them, could be insightful.
* **Potential for numerical instability:** Though mitigated by the Cholesky factorization, the presented method could be prone to numerical instability for some problem formulations; a discussion of this potential issue and any implemented safeguards would add robustness to the work.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a critical bottleneck in CMA-ES, providing a significant practical improvement in runtime complexity.
* The theoretical justification is strong and supports the claim that the modification doesn’t negatively impact performance.
* The experimental results are compelling and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method across a range of benchmark functions.
* The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains complex concepts.
* The contribution is substantial and likely to be widely adopted by researchers and practitioners using CMA-ES.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* While the theoretical analysis is sound, the assumptions underlying Lemma 1 might not always hold in practice, potentially affecting performance in some cases.
* The lack of deeper exploration of parameter sensitivity could leave some questions unanswered for users implementing the algorithm.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a significant contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a substantial improvement in runtime complexity without sacrificing performance, and the theoretical justification is well-supported. The weaknesses are relatively minor and do not detract from the overall quality of the work. I strongly recommend acceptance to NIPS 2016. The method is likely to become a standard component of CMA-ES implementations due to its clear advantages.
