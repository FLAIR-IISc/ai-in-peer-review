- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce computation.  
- Maintains algorithm performance while achieving optimal time and storage complexity for covariance updates.  
- Novel theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing Cholesky substitution introduces a vanishing error as the algorithm converges.  
- Extends prior Cholesky-update work to the standard CMA-ES with rigorous proof, unlike earlier limited/ungrounded attempts.  
- Experiments (d = 4–256) show same function-evaluation efficiency as CMA-ES with up to 20× speedup (increasing with dimension).  
- Time complexity reduced from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2) for covariance updates; storage optimized and implementation numerically stable.  
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and logically organized with clear pseudocode and experimental setup.  
- Significance: practical for high-dimensional optimization and valuable alternative to low-rank approximations that hurt non-separable problems.  
- Limitation: still scales quadratically, so computational challenges remain for extremely high dimensions (d > 1000).  
- Limitation: theoretical analysis assumes Ct converges to a shape, but CMA-ES randomness means convergence isn’t guaranteed—discussion could be deeper.  
- Limitation: would benefit from more detailed comparisons with other large-scale CMA-ES methods (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, comprehensive experiments, numerically stable implementation, and clear identification of limitations.  
- Recommendation: high-quality, significant contribution; paper should be accepted and Cholesky-CMA-ES adopted where covariance updates are costly.
