- Proposes rank-µ triangular Cholesky CMA-ES updating A with C = A A^T to avoid per-iteration eigendecompositions, achieving O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) memory.
- Replaces CSA’s C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, arguing the resulting rotation vanishes as covariance shape stabilizes, formalized by a lemma on RQ decomposition continuity after det-normalization.
- Extends prior Cholesky and decomposition-update work to a practical rank-µ update for standard CMA-ES with theoretical rationale for the CSA approximation.
- Empirically matches reference CMA-ES in function-evaluation counts up to d=256 while producing substantial wall-clock speedups and preserving rotation invariance.
- Strength: Clear, concrete engineering that enables full covariance adaptation without per-iteration eigendecompositions.
- Strength: Thoughtful treatment of the CSA approximation with intuitive argument and supporting empirical evidence.
- Strength: Strong empirical results showing preserved sample efficiency and significant runtime gains; numerical/storage aspects well discussed.
- Weakness: Incorrect technical claim that diagonals of a triangular Cholesky factor are square roots of eigenvalues—true only for diagonal C, not in general.
- Weakness: Theoretical support for the CSA approximation is qualitative and assumes Ct convergence; lacks quantitative bounds and analysis for nonstationary or noisy settings.
- Weakness: Missing important baselines, notably active CMA-ES (negative updates) and limited-memory hybrids (e.g., LM-CMA), which would contextualize trade-offs.
- Weakness: Experimental reporting issues—filtering out unsuccessful Rosenbrock runs biases results; baseline decomposition-delay setting appears misstated and may unfairly penalize the reference.
- Weakness: Implementation concern—explicitly computing A^{-1} is unnecessary and potentially less stable; triangular solves should be used instead.
- Quality: Core rank-µ Cholesky updates are technically sound and experiments convincing for noiseless benchmarks, but CSA justification needs stronger support.
- Clarity: Exposition is generally clear with helpful algorithm boxes, but must fix the Cholesky/eigenvalue error, clarify baseline settings, and clean reference formatting.
- Originality: Novel integration of triangular Cholesky rank-µ updates with CSA via A^{-1}; incremental but practically meaningful.
- Significance: Valuable practical contribution likely to be adopted where eigendecompositions dominate runtime, especially in medium-to-high dimensions.
- Suggestions: Correct the eigenvalue claim; add quantitative/ablative analysis of CSA error; report success rates including failures; fix baseline delay; consider active CMA-ES downdates and SPD guarantees; avoid explicit A^{-1}; compare to LM-CMA.
- Arguments for acceptance: Practical, widely useful speed/memory improvement with clear algorithmic idea and strong empirical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: Weak theoretical guarantees for CSA, missing baselines/incomplete reporting, and a fundamental technical misstatement.
- Overall: Solid, practically important paper with compelling experiments but requires correction of the Cholesky/eigenvalue claim and stronger theoretical/experimental support; reviewer leans toward acceptance after revision.
