- Proposes using Cholesky factorization directly in CMA-ES to avoid repeated matrix square roots/eigendecompositions, reducing covariance update cost.
- Claims quadratic-time covariance updates with optimal storage while preserving convergence behavior.
- Well-motivated by the computational bottleneck of covariance matrix operations in CMA-ES.
- Theoretical justification is strong, including analysis of coordinate system rotations and maintained convergence properties.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based work but offers a novel, theoretically justified integration with standard CMA-ES.
- Paper is clear and well-organized; Algorithm 1 and 2 concisely present the method.
- Empirical results show substantial runtime reduction, especially in high-dimensional problems.
- Experimental evaluation is limited to benchmark functions and lacks real-world application tests (e.g., RL, hyperparameter tuning).
- Performance is sensitive to cache effects at high dimensionality; more analysis of memory access and optimizations is needed.
- Convergence argument depends on the assumption of slowly changing covariance matrices; scenarios violating this need further analysis.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key bottleneck, theoretically sound modification, demonstrated speedup, and strong literature connection.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited experimental scope and untested convergence assumptions.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to expand experiments and analyze assumptions.
