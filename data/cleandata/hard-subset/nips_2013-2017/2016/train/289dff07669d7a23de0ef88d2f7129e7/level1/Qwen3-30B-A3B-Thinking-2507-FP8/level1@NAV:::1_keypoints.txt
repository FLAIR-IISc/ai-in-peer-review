- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with Cholesky factor to achieve optimal time and storage complexity.  
- Novelty: builds on prior specialized Cholesky updates but provides the first theoretical justification for standard CMA-ES.  
- Key theoretical insight: error from using Cholesky factor vanishes as the algorithm converges (Lemma 1).  
- Technical quality: clear theory and rigorous derivations supporting the method.  
- Empirical validation: thorough experiments on benchmark functions (Sphere, Cigar, Rosenbrock, etc.) across dimensions 4–256.  
- Performance: matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluation counts while substantially reducing wall-clock time (up to 20× faster at d=64).  
- Scalability: speedups increase with dimensionality; covariance updates achieve O(µ d²) time vs O(d³) for standard CMA-ES.  
- Memory: maintains same memory complexity as standard CMA-ES.  
- Practical benefits: provides eigenvalues of the covariance for free and enables numerically stable matrix inversion.  
- Weakness: limited testing on real-world machine-learning problems beyond standard benchmarks.  
- Weakness: could better address numerical stability concerns of the Cholesky approach relative to matrix square root.  
- Weakness: the claim that behavior (required evaluations) is unchanged needs stronger convergence analysis.  
- Significance: important for scaling CMA-ES to large problems and addresses a key practical bottleneck.  
- Overall assessment: high-quality contribution with strong theory and experiments; strong candidate for acceptance.
