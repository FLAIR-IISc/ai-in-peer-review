**Summary of Main Ideas**

The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, which is a widely used derivative-free optimization method in machine learning. The authors aim to reduce the time and space complexity of the algorithm by using a triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the square root of the covariance matrix. This modification is based on maintaining triangular Cholesky factors and requires a change in the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on various benchmark functions.

**Related Work**

The CMA-ES algorithm has been extensively studied and modified in the past to improve its performance and efficiency. Previous approaches have focused on reducing the time complexity of the algorithm by updating the covariance matrix less frequently or by using approximations. However, these approaches often compromise on the accuracy of the optimization process. The authors' approach is distinct in that it uses a triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce the time and space complexity of the algorithm while maintaining its accuracy.
