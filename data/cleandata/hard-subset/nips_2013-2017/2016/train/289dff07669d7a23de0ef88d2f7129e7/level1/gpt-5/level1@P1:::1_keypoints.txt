- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain triangular Cholesky factor A and use A−1 in CSA to avoid costly eigen-decomposition.
- Core idea: perform efficient rank-one updates on A to get O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) storage for full-covariance CMA-ES.
- Theoretical justification: argue C−1/2 ≈ A−1 via RQ decomposition C1/2 = A E and smooth, converging rotations E that minimally affect CSA.
- Builds on prior work (delayed updates, restricted/limited-memory covariances, factorization updates) but insists on triangular A for bijection C↔A.
- Strength: clear algorithmic contribution with numerically stable triangular rank-one updates and minimal memory.
- Strength: thoughtful analysis of rotation convergence and exponential forgetting supporting the CSA approximation.
- Strength: empirical results on standard noiseless benchmarks up to d=256 show matching evaluation counts and large wall-clock speedups (~20× at d=64).
- Strength: good discussion of invariance properties and practical benefits (A−1 in O(d^2), no stale matrices).
- Weakness: incorrect claim that Cholesky diagonal elements are square roots of eigenvalues; diagonals are Cholesky pivots, not eigenvalues.
- Weakness: theoretical CSA justification depends on assumptions (normalized covariance convergence) that are plausible but unproven for general CMA-ES.
- Weakness: experimental scope limited to noiseless benchmarks; missing tests on noisy, constrained, box-bounded, or multimodal problems where CSA may behave differently.
- Weakness: baselines may be unfair—reference CMA-ES decomposes every iteration; need tuned decomposition-delay baselines and comparisons to LM-CMA variants.
- Weakness: reproducibility details (hardware, BLAS threading, compiler flags) are sparse, complicating wall-clock comparisons.
- Quality: rank-µ Cholesky update is technically sound; complexity claims (O(µ d^2) time, O(d^2) storage) are accurate, but eigenvalue claim must be fixed.
- Clarity: generally clear and well-organized; minor clarity issues (RQ vs QR, expand Algorithm 2 derivation, reference formatting).
- Originality: incremental but meaningful novelty in combining rank-µ Cholesky updates with CSA modification and convergence-based justification.
- Significance: practical impact is high—substantial runtime reductions without extra evaluations make full-covariance CMA-ES more usable.
- Suggestions: correct eigenvalue claim, add noisy/constrained experiments, test sensitivity to µ and c1/cµ, include tuned delay and LM-CMA baselines, and report system/BLAS details.
- Minor comments: fix “adaption” → “adaptation,” clarify big-Omega and cubic costs, and clean up reference metadata/formatting.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong practical speedups, solid engineering, and relevance to ML optimization tooling.
- Arguments against acceptance: theoretical guarantees for CSA are suggestive not formal, experiments and baselines need broadening, and contains a factual error to fix.
- Overall: well-executed, impactful engineering improvement that would be strong with the noted correction and modest experimental/extensions.
