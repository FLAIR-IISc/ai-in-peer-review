- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: use Cholesky factorization and adjusted CSA to reduce covariance update/storage costs.  
- Achieves quadratic (O(d^2)) time updates and reduced memory while preserving function-evaluation performance.  
- Convincingly addresses the covariance matrix bottleneck in standard CMA-ES, especially for high-dimensional problems.  
- Strong theoretical justification centered on Lemma 1 about Cholesky factor convergence.  
- Well-structured, clearly written paper that makes complex concepts accessible.  
- Comprehensive related-work section situating the method among CMA-ES variants.  
- Robust experimental setup using standard benchmarks and thorough comparisons to state-of-the-art implementations.  
- Results show consistent significant speedups with increasing dimensionality without performance loss.  
- Algorithm attains asymptotically optimal time and storage complexity.  
- Limited exploration of parameter sensitivity (e.g., CSA parameters) compared to standard CMA-ES.  
- Acknowledged cache effects at d=256; needs deeper investigation of memory access patterns and practical scalability.  
- Lemma 1 relies on assumptions that may not hold in practice, especially in early optimization stages.  
- Insufficient focused analysis on highly ill-conditioned problems and how Cholesky handles them.  
- Potential numerical instability concerns; more discussion and implemented safeguards would strengthen the work.  
- Overall: strong, significant contribution likely to be widely adopted; reviewer strongly recommends acceptance despite minor weaknesses.
