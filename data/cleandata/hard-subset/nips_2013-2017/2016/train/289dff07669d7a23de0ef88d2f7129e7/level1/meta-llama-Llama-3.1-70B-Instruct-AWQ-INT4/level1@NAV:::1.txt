This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, a powerful real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm. The modification, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, replaces the traditional matrix square root update with a triangular Cholesky factorization, resulting in a more efficient algorithm with optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity.

The authors provide a thorough analysis of the original CMA-ES algorithm and its limitations, particularly with regards to the computation of the matrix square root, which dominates the time and space complexity. They then introduce the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm, which updates the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the covariance matrix itself. This approach reduces the runtime complexity to O(µd2), where µ is the effective sample size and d is the dimensionality of the problem.

The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical experiments on various benchmark functions. The results show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm achieves a significant increase in optimization speed compared to the standard CMA-ES, while maintaining the same number of objective function evaluations.

Strengths of the paper:

* The authors provide a thorough analysis of the original CMA-ES algorithm and its limitations, which helps to understand the motivation behind the proposed modification.
* The Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm is well-motivated and theoretically justified, with a clear explanation of the benefits and trade-offs of the new approach.
* The empirical experiments are well-designed and provide a comprehensive evaluation of the new algorithm, including comparisons with other CMA-ES variants.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The paper assumes a high level of familiarity with the CMA-ES algorithm and its variants, which may make it difficult for non-experts to follow.
* Some of the mathematical derivations and proofs are not provided in the paper, which may limit the understanding of the algorithm's properties and behavior.

Originality and significance:

* The Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm is a novel modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, which addresses a significant limitation of the original algorithm.
* The paper provides a thorough analysis of the algorithm's properties and behavior, including its convergence and computational complexity.
