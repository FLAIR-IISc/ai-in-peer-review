This paper presents a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm aimed at reducing its computational cost, particularly the covariance matrix update. The core idea is to work directly with the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix square root, leveraging existing efficient updates for Cholesky factors. The authors provide a theoretical justification for why this approximation should not significantly impact performance and demonstrate empirical improvements in runtime, especially in higher dimensions, without sacrificing the number of function evaluations.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and presents a clear and logical progression of ideas. The theoretical justification, while relying on a lemma about convergence of the Cholesky factor, is a significant contribution. The proposed "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is a well-defined algorithm. The experimental setup appears comprehensive, including comparisons against several CMA-ES variants (delayed updates, Suttorp’s method) across a range of benchmark functions and dimensions. The results convincingly show a speedup in runtime without a corresponding increase in function evaluations. However, the discussion of the limitations regarding cache size at very high dimensions could be expanded. The analysis correctly identifies the quadratic complexity bottleneck of the standard CMA-ES and offers a plausible solution. The paper isn't merely a position paper; it's a complete, well-executed work. The authors acknowledge potential issues (e.g., loss of invariance) and attempt to mitigate them with their analysis and results.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clear, and the algorithm is presented in a straightforward manner. The use of pseudocode (Algorithms 1 & 2) is helpful. However, the mathematical notation can be dense at times, and some readers might benefit from more detailed explanations of the theoretical derivations. The connections between equations and the algorithm's steps are generally clear, but a more step-by-step explanation of the derivation of the Cholesky update rule would be beneficial. The related work section is relevant, but could be more concise. Some references are cited without substantial discussion.

**Originality:** The idea of using the Cholesky factorization within CMA-ES isn’t entirely new (Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel are acknowledged). However, this paper’s contribution lies in extending this approach to the *standard* CMA-ES, providing a theoretical justification for its validity, and demonstrating substantial practical performance improvements.  The authors carefully address the issues that prevented previous attempts from being directly applicable to the original algorithm. The core novelty is the argument and empirical support that replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor, *with* the proposed step-size adaptation modification, doesn’t fundamentally alter the algorithm's behavior regarding function evaluation requirements.  This distinguishes it from prior work.

**Significance:** The results are significant. CMA-ES is a powerful algorithm used in various fields, including machine learning (hyperparameter optimization, reinforcement learning). Reducing its runtime, especially for high-dimensional problems, is a valuable contribution. The algorithm’s potential impact is substantial; practitioners and researchers are likely to adopt it if the performance gains hold up in their specific applications. The work advances the state-of-the-art by providing a more efficient implementation of CMA-ES.  The fact that the number of function evaluations is preserved is important, as this metric often dominates the cost of optimization in expensive black-box settings.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   **Significant Performance Improvement:** Demonstrates a substantial reduction in runtime without increasing the number of function evaluations.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** Provides a reasonable theoretical argument for why the approximation should work.
*   **Well-Defined Algorithm:** Presents a clear and implementable algorithm.
*   **Comprehensive Experiments:** Includes a thorough empirical evaluation against relevant baselines.
*   **Addresses a Practical Bottleneck:** Tackles a key computational cost in CMA-ES, making it more applicable to larger problems.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   **Mathematical Density:** Some theoretical sections could be more accessible.
*   **Cache Limitations:** Discussion of performance impact at very high dimensions is limited; further analysis could be helpful.
*   **Limited Exploration of Parameter Sensitivity**: The impact of different parameter settings on the performance of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, compared to the standard CMA-ES, could be explored further.



**Overall Assessment:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper. It addresses a relevant problem in a technically sound and well-supported manner. The proposed algorithm offers a significant practical improvement over the standard CMA-ES, and the authors have provided a convincing justification for its validity. The paper meets the NIPS criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance. While there are minor areas for improvement, the overall contribution is substantial and likely to be of interest to the NIPS community.
