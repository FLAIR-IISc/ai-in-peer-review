- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains triangular Cholesky factor A of covariance C, giving O(µ d^2) per-iteration complexity and optimal storage.  
- Replaces C−1/2 in CSA with A−1 and analyzes induced rotational discrepancy via RQ decomposition, arguing CSA error vanishes asymptotically under mild normalized-covariance convergence.  
- Empirical results on standard noiseless benchmarks (d up to 256, random rotations) show matching function-evaluation efficiency to strong CMA-ES and substantial wall-clock speedups that grow with d.  
- Builds on and unifies prior ideas (delayed decompositions, restricted models, prior Cholesky updates) while retaining standard CMA-ES behavior.  
- Algorithmic derivation and complexity claims are largely sound; CSA approximation has a clear, if asymptotic, justification.  
- Experiments are careful and include competitive baselines, and code availability is noted.  
- Incorrect technical claim: diagonal of triangular Cholesky factor are not generally square roots of eigenvalues; diag(A) relates to leading principal minors, not eigenvalues.  
- One can obtain det(C) via ∏ diag(A)^2 and inexpensive eigenvalue bounds, but exact eigenvalues require eigendecomposition/SVD.  
- Clarity is generally good with helpful pseudocode, but some references are malformed.  
- Exposition should tighten around Lemma 1’s assumptions and the numerical stability/range of the rankOneUpdate (β).  
- Originality: meaningful and novel extension making Cholesky updates compatible with standard CMA-ES and CSA.  
- Significance: high — matches evaluations while reducing runtime and memory for full-covariance CMA-ES.  
- Pros: strong practical impact, near-rotation invariance, simple implementable modification, code provided.  
- Cons: wrong eigenvalue claim needs correction; no quantitative bound on CSA bias; experiments limited to noiseless, unconstrained settings.  
- Additional concern: limited discussion of very high-dimensional behavior and cache/implementation effects.
