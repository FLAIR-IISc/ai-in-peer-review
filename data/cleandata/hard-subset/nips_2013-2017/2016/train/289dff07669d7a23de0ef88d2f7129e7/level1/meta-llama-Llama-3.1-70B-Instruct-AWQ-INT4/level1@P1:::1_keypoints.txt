- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce runtime complexity.
- Provides theoretical convergence justification and empirical validation on benchmark functions.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-update work (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) and extends updates to standard CMA-ES.
- Achieves asymptotically optimal runtime O(µd^2).
- Analysis is technically sound with convincing theoretical and empirical support.
- Detailed comparison with other CMA-ES variants, including delayed covariance decomposition.
- Paper is well-written with consistent notation and clear mathematical derivations.
- Novel contribution that significantly reduces cost of covariance updates.
- Significant impact for derivative-free optimization and ML applications (policy search, hyperparameter tuning).
- Pros for acceptance: novelty, thorough analysis, and strong experiments.
- Cons against acceptance: assumes familiarity with CMA-ES and lacks some implementation/complexity detail.
- Recommendation: accept.
