- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, a novel CMA-ES variant using triangular Cholesky factors to reduce runtime and memory while preserving optimization performance.  
- Replaces full covariance update with a numerically stable quadratic-time Cholesky update.  
- Theoretical justification with proofs and lemmas supporting convergence and stability.  
- Shows approximation in step-size adaptation has negligible impact.  
- Empirical validation on benchmarks up to 256 dimensions demonstrates substantial runtime speed-ups with similar function-evaluation counts to standard CMA-ES.  
- Particularly valuable for large-scale optimization where covariance storage/update is a bottleneck.  
- Well-organized, clearly written, and reproducible with detailed algorithms and open-source references.  
- Limited empirical comparisons: lacks evaluation against non–CMA-ES state-of-the-art derivative-free optimizers.  
- Missing quantitative metrics/visualizations to track the Cholesky approximation error during optimization.  
- Still scales quadratically with dimension; may not be feasible for extremely high-dimensional problems and lacks explored hybrid/extensible solutions.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a known CMA-ES bottleneck, is theoretically and empirically sound, and has practical applicability.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited broader comparisons and the quadratic scalability limitation.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with minor limitations — recommend acceptance.
