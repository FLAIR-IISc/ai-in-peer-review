- Proposal: Replace the CMA-ES matrix square root with a Cholesky factorization to reduce covariance update cost from O(d^3) to O(d^2).
- Algorithm: Introduce Cholesky-CMA-ES using a triangular Cholesky factor in the CSA mechanism instead of the matrix square root.
- Novelty: Builds on prior Cholesky attempts but is the first to provide a theoretical justification for standard CMA-ES.
- Theoretical result: Prove a lemma showing the error from using the Cholesky factor vanishes asymptotically as the algorithm converges.
- Empirical performance: Matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while achieving substantial runtime speedups (up to ~20x for d=64).
- Experimental setup: Comprehensive evaluation on standard benchmark functions across multiple dimensions, measuring both evaluations and wall-clock time.
- Complexity/limitations: Still scales quadratically with dimensionality and becomes less effective when problem dimension exceeds cache size.
- Clarity: Paper is well-organized, clearly written, with clear pseudocode that matches the theory.
- Minor clarity issue: Explanation of maintaining the Cholesky factor during full covariance updates could be clearer.
- Theoretical scope: Analysis focuses on asymptotic behavior and does not quantify transient error magnitudes during optimization.
- Numerical concerns: The impact of numerical precision in Cholesky factorization on final results is not explored.
- Comparative evaluation: Compared against several prior variants (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES) showing better time complexity for equal performance.
- Missing comparisons: Limited discussion/comparison with other low-complexity variants (e.g., Loshchilov, 2015) beyond a brief mention.
- Strength: Provides rigorous theoretical justification for Cholesky use without degrading performance.
- Strength: Demonstrates clear empirical runtime improvements without sacrificing optimization quality.
- Strength: Clear presentation and implementation details with comprehensive experiments.
- Significance: Addresses a known CMA-ES bottleneck, enabling more efficient use in higher-dimensional applications (e.g., policy search, hyperparameter tuning).
- Recommendation: Strong accept — technically sound, novel in its theoretical justification, practically significant; acknowledged as somewhat incremental but valuable.
