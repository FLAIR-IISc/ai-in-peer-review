- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replacing covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time and space complexity.  
- Well-written, clearly explains original CMA-ES, the modification, and theoretical basis.  
- Provides a lemma/theoretical justification establishing convergence of the new approach.  
- Empirical results show significant optimization speed improvements over original CMA-ES on benchmarks.  
- Paper is well-organized, with clear language and helpful figures/tables.  
- Assumes high familiarity with CMA-ES, which may limit accessibility for non-expert readers.  
- Lacks detailed empirical comparison with other optimization algorithms to contextualize strengths/weaknesses.  
- Does not sufficiently discuss potential limitations, notably scalability to high-dimensional problems.  
- Novel modification to a widely used algorithm, making a meaningful contribution to optimization literature.  
- Potentially significant impact by improving efficiency of widely used derivative-free optimizers.  
- Quality of experiments and presentation is strong and supports the claims.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, theoretical justification, and empirical effectiveness.  
- Arguments for rejection: limited accessibility, missing broader comparisons, and absent discussion of scalability/limitations.  
- Recommendation: accept, with suggestions to add broader comparisons and discussion of potential limitations (e.g., high-dimensional scalability) in future work.
