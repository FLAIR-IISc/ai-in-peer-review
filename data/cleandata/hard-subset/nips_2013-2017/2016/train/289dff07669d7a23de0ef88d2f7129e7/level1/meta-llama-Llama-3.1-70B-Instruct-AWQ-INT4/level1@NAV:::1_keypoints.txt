- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root update with triangular Cholesky factor update.
- Reduces dominant time/space costs, achieving optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity.
- Runtime complexity lowered to O(µ d^2) (µ = effective sample size, d = dimension).
- Provides theoretical justification for convergence of the new algorithm.
- Empirical benchmarks show significant speedup over CMA-ES while keeping the same number of function evaluations.
- Thorough analysis of original CMA-ES and clear motivation for the modification.
- Cholesky-CMA-ES is well-motivated with explained benefits and trade-offs.
- Experiments are comprehensive and include comparisons with other CMA-ES variants.
- Assumes high familiarity with CMA-ES, limiting accessibility to non-experts.
- Some mathematical derivations and proofs are omitted, limiting understanding of properties.
- Novel and significant contribution addressing a key limitation of CMA-ES.
- Potential to impact derivative-free optimization due to improved efficiency.
- Arguments for acceptance: novelty, empirical effectiveness, and thorough property/complexity analysis.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited accessibility and missing formal derivations/proofs.
