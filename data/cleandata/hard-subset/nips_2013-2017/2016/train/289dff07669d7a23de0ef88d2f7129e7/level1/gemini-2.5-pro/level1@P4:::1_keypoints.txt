- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain/update lower-triangular Cholesky factor A_t instead of forming C_t, avoiding explicit d×d covariance matrix.
- Uses rank-one updates to A_t, yielding O(µ d^2) per iteration, asymptotically optimal for full covariance maintenance.
- Main technical hurdle: CSA requires C_t^{-1/2}; replacing it with A_t^{-1} is nontrivial due to a rotating orthogonal factor E_t.
- Theoretical contribution (Lemma 1): if the normalized covariance shape converges, the sequence of rotations E_t converges and the approximation error vanishes under exponential CSA weighting.
- Empirical results: nearly identical function-evaluation counts to standard CMA-ES but significantly faster wall-clock time, especially at larger d.
- Technical quality is high: leverages established numerical Cholesky updates and provides both theoretical justification and strong empirical evidence.
- Clarity: paper is exceptionally clear, with concise background, logical presentation of the solution, and helpful pseudo-code.
- Originality: adapts Cholesky updates successfully to the standard CMA-ES and novely analyzes the CSA approximation error.
- Significance: removes the O(d^3) bottleneck, making full-covariance CMA-ES practical at higher dimensions and likely to become a new standard.
- Relation to prior work: differs from delayed updates, restricted-matrix models, and earlier Cholesky attempts by addressing the CSA issue rigorously.
- Main weakness: theoretical argument depends on the assumption that the normalized covariance shape converges, which may fail on highly multimodal objectives.
- Overall: strengths far outweigh the minor theoretical assumption caveat; proposed method is practical, well-justified, and impactful.
