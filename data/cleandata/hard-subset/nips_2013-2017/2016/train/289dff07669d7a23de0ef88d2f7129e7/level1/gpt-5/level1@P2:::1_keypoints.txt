- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to enable O(µ d^2) updates and O(d^2) memory while preserving sampling x = m + σ A y.
- Key technical challenge: CSA uses C^{-1/2}; authors replace it with A^{-1}, introducing orthogonal rotations E_t from an RQ decomposition and arguing these rotations stabilize asymptotically.
- Theoretical support: Lemma showing E_{t-1}^T E_t → I if normalized covariance converges, implying asymptotic preservation of the pσ norm under the A^{-1} substitution.
- Algorithmic contribution: Efficient rank-µ (rank-one to rank-µ) Cholesky updates (Algorithm 2) with O(d^2) access to A^{-1} and "free" eigenvalue information from A.
- Relation to prior work: Generalizes prior Cholesky updates for specialized CMA-ES variants and extends Krause & Igel (2015) to full (µ/µ_w, λ)-CMA-ES; improves over nontriangular factorization work and differs from low-complexity approximations by keeping full covariance.
- Strength — computational: Principled, numerically stable O(µ d^2) update with clear speed advantage over periodic eigendecomposition.
- Strength — theoretical: Convincing asymptotic rationale for replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, supported by smoothness and bijectivity of triangular factorization.
- Strength — empirical: Matches CMA-ES in evaluations on benchmarks up to d=256 while substantially reducing wall-clock time (e.g., >20× at d=64); comparisons include relevant baselines.
- Strength — practical: Provides A^{-1} and eigenvalues cheaply for monitoring and variance-floor enforcement; code integrated with LAPACK and Shark indicates readiness.
- Weakness — theoretical: Main lemma is conditional/asymptotic and lacks finite-time bounds; CMA-ES stochasticity and nonstationarity may break convergence assumptions.
- Weakness — invariance: Modified CSA breaks exact rotation invariance; empirical impact seems small but not explicitly quantified for rapidly rotating problems.
- Weakness — empirical scope: Limited evaluation under noise, constraints, and real ML tasks where CSA robustness is critical.
- Weakness — numerical details: Algorithm 2’s stability claims need comparison to standard cholupdate/downdate, discussion of conditioning safeguards, and analysis of error accumulation.
- NIPS quality assessment: Algorithmic development and experiments are solid; theoretical justification is high-level but lacks nonasymptotic guarantees.
- NIPS clarity assessment: Paper is clear with helpful pseudocode, but some notational/notation choices and minor presentation issues should be clarified.
- NIPS originality assessment: Novel extension of triangular Cholesky updates to full rank-µ CMA-ES and justification for A^{-1} in CSA.
- NIPS significance assessment: High — substantial practical speedups with preserved evaluation efficiency could make this a new default for full-covariance CMA-ES.
- Pros for acceptance: Strong speedups, minimal behavioral change, solid theory+experiments, practical utility (eigenvalues/A^{-1}).
- Cons for acceptance: No finite-time CSA error bounds, limited noisy/real-world evaluations, and missing numerical-analysis details for Cholesky updates.
- Suggested improvements: Provide finite-time bound or error estimate for pσ discrepancy, add noisy and at least one ML application experiment, and compare Algorithm 2 to standard cholupdate routines with conditioning safeguards.
- Recommendation: Accept — clear, useful, and well-substantiated contribution with high practical impact.
