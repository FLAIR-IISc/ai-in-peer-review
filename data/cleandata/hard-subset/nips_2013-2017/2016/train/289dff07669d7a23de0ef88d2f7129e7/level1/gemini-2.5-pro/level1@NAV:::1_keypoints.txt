- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, updating a triangular Cholesky factor to achieve O(µ d²) time and O(d²) space for covariance updates.
- Core idea: maintain and directly update the Cholesky factor to avoid O(d³) eigen-decomposition in standard CMA-ES.
- Main technical challenge: CSA requires the inverse matrix square root; authors approximate this with the inverse triangular Cholesky factor.
- Lemma 1 provides theoretical justification that the CSA approximation error vanishes as the covariance shape converges.
- Builds on prior complexity-reduction efforts (delayed updates, non-triangular factors) but offers a novel, theoretically-grounded approach for standard CMA-ES.
- Algorithm is technically sound, clearly presented, and numerically stable.
- Strong empirical validation demonstrating dramatic wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) without increasing function evaluations.
- Contribution is likely to be highly significant and adopted by practitioners, enabling CMA-ES in much higher dimensions.
- Minor weakness: theoretical guarantees rely on a strong convergence assumption for the normalized covariance matrix.
- Authors argue practical sufficiency because covariance changes slow in later optimization stages, supported by empirical results.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a critical bottleneck, elegant and efficient solution, solid theory and empirical evidence, high practical impact.
- Argument against acceptance: theoretical guarantees may not hold formally in all randomized scenarios.
