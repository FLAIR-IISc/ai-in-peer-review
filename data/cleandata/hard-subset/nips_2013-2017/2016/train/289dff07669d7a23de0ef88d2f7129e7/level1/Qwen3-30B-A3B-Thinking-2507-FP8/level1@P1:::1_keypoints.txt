- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce time/space complexity.
- Achieves O(µ d^2) per-iteration time (µ typically O(log d)) vs. standard O(d^3), enabling large-d scaling.
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the approximation error vanishes on convergence.
- Empirical evaluation across benchmark functions and dimensions (4–256) shows same function-evaluation performance with substantial runtime speedups (up to 20× for d=64).
- Builds on prior CMA-ES complexity-reduction work but is novel in theoretically justifying Cholesky use for standard CMA-ES.
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and reproducible (clear algorithms and experiments).
- Significant practical significance: removes a bottleneck, making CMA-ES applicable to larger ML problems (hyperparameter tuning, policy search).
- Main weakness: does not adequately address numerical stability, especially for ill-conditioned problems.
- Main weakness: incomplete comparison with some recent complexity-reduction approaches (e.g., Loshchilov 2015, Sun et al. 2013).
- Main weakness: limited concrete discussion/examples of practical impact for specific ML applications.
- Pro-acceptance points: rigorous theory, clear empirical speedups without performance loss, comprehensive experiments, and high relevance to ML.
- Con-acceptance points: numerical stability concerns, incomplete related-work comparisons, and limited practical impact discussion.
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution with minor weaknesses; recommendation for acceptance.
