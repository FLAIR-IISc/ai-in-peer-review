This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, a powerful real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm widely used in machine learning. The authors aim to reduce the time and space complexity of the algorithm by using a triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the square root of the covariance matrix.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the original CMA-ES algorithm, the proposed modification, and the theoretical justification for the new approach. The authors also provide empirical results comparing the original CMA-ES with the new Cholesky-CMA-ES on various benchmark functions.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a clear and concise explanation of the original CMA-ES algorithm and the proposed modification.
* The authors provide a theoretical justification for the new approach, including a lemma that establishes the convergence of the algorithm.
* The empirical results demonstrate a significant increase in optimization speed for the Cholesky-CMA-ES compared to the original CMA-ES.
* The paper is well-organized and easy to follow.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes a high level of familiarity with the CMA-ES algorithm and its applications in machine learning. Readers without this background may find the paper difficult to follow.
* The authors do not provide a detailed comparison of the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other optimization algorithms, which would be useful for understanding its strengths and limitations.
* The paper does not discuss the potential limitations of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, such as its scalability to high-dimensional problems.
