## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper addresses a critical bottleneck in the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES): the computational and memory cost of updating and storing the covariance matrix. The authors propose a Cholesky-CMA-ES variant that leverages a quadratic-time covariance matrix update scheme based on maintaining Cholesky factors, significantly reducing both runtime and memory requirements, particularly in high dimensions. The core idea – replacing the matrix square root with its Cholesky factor in the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism – is well-motivated and theoretically justified, with the authors acknowledging and addressing potential issues related to coordinate system rotations.

The paper is well-structured and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and experimental setup. It effectively connects to prior work, referencing key papers in the CMA-ES literature (Hansen & Ostermeier, Beyer, etc.) and related approaches like those by Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel. The theoretical justification, while concise, provides a convincing argument for why the proposed modification shouldn’t drastically alter the algorithm’s convergence properties. The experimental results are compelling, demonstrating significant speedups, especially as dimensionality increases, while maintaining comparable performance in terms of function evaluations. 

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a significant practical limitation:** The computational cost of covariance matrix updates is a major bottleneck for CMA-ES, especially in high-dimensional problems.
* **Strong theoretical justification:** The authors provide a reasonable argument for why the Cholesky factorization approach doesn't negatively impact convergence.
* **Clear and well-written:** The paper is easy to follow and understand, even for those not intimately familiar with CMA-ES.
* **Empirically validated:** The experimental results clearly demonstrate the benefits of the proposed approach.
* **Optimal Complexity:** The algorithm achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited discussion of potential drawbacks:** While the authors acknowledge the approximation introduced by replacing the matrix square root, a more in-depth discussion of scenarios where this approximation might be problematic would be beneficial.
* **Cache effects:** The authors mention cache effects in the experiments, but a more detailed analysis of how cache size impacts performance would be valuable.
* **Comparison to other low-rank CMA variants:** While Suttorp et al. is compared, a broader comparison to other recent low-rank or subspace-based CMA-ES variants could strengthen the paper.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a novel and effective solution to a significant practical problem in CMA-ES.
* The theoretical justification is sound and the experimental results are convincing.
* The proposed algorithm has the potential to significantly broaden the applicability of CMA-ES to higher-dimensional problems.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The theoretical analysis could be more rigorous.
* The discussion of potential drawbacks is somewhat limited.



**Overall:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES variant is a significant improvement over the standard algorithm, and the paper is well-written and thoroughly researched. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses mentioned above.
