

# Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

The paper proposes a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm that replaces the matrix square root operation with a Cholesky factorization, reducing the time complexity of covariance matrix updates from O(d^3) to O(d^2) while maintaining the same optimization performance. The authors introduce the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm, which uses a triangular Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root in the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism. This work builds on previous attempts to use Cholesky factorization (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause and Igel, 2015) but provides a theoretical justification for why this modification does not deteriorate the algorithm's performance. The authors prove that the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor vanishes as the algorithm converges to the optimum, making this approach applicable to the standard CMA-ES.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and well-supported by both theoretical analysis and empirical results. The authors provide a rigorous lemma establishing that the error from using the Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root vanishes asymptotically as the algorithm converges. The empirical evaluation demonstrates that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES but achieves significant runtime improvements, particularly for higher-dimensional problems (up to 20x faster for d=64). The experimental setup is comprehensive, using standard benchmark functions across multiple dimensions and evaluating both function evaluations and wall-clock time. The paper properly acknowledges limitations, noting that the approach still scales quadratically with dimensionality and becomes less effective when the problem dimensionality exceeds the cache size.

**Clarity:** The paper is well-organized and clearly written. The background section effectively explains the CMA-ES algorithm and the motivation for the proposed modification. The theoretical justification is presented with sufficient mathematical detail. The pseudocode for the proposed algorithm (Algorithm 1) is clear and matches the theoretical explanation. The experimental section adequately describes the benchmark functions, experimental setup, and evaluation metrics. One minor weakness is that the explanation of how the Cholesky factorization is maintained during the covariance matrix update could be slightly clearer, particularly regarding the relationship between the Cholesky factor and the covariance matrix in the full update process.

**Originality:** The paper makes a significant contribution by providing a theoretically justified approach to using Cholesky factorization in the standard CMA-ES, addressing a gap in previous work. While Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015) proposed using Cholesky factorization for specific variants of CMA-ES, they did not provide a theoretical justification for why this would work for the standard CMA-ES. The authors explicitly state that their approach is the first to provide this justification, which is a novel contribution to the field. The paper demonstrates this novelty by comparing against previous approaches (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES) and showing that their method achieves the same performance with better time complexity.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a significant bottleneck in the CMA-ES algorithm that has been recognized in previous work (Hansen and Ostermeier, 2001; Hansen, 2015). For high-dimensional optimization problems, the O(d^3) complexity of the covariance matrix update can dominate the runtime, limiting the applicability of CMA-ES to large-scale problems. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm directly addresses this limitation, enabling more efficient use of CMA-ES for larger problems where maintaining the full covariance matrix is still computationally feasible. The empirical results demonstrate clear practical advantages, with runtime improvements that increase with dimensionality. This is particularly significant for applications like direct policy search in reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning in machine learning, where CMA-ES is commonly applied.

**Strengths:**
1. Theoretical justification for why the Cholesky factorization approach does not degrade performance
2. Demonstrated empirical improvements in runtime without sacrificing optimization quality
3. Clear presentation of the algorithm and its implementation details
4. Comprehensive experimental evaluation across multiple benchmark functions and dimensions
5. Practical implications for real-world applications of CMA-ES

**Weaknesses:**
1. The paper could provide more detail on how the Cholesky factor is maintained during the full covariance matrix update process
2. The theoretical analysis focuses on asymptotic behavior but doesn't quantify the error magnitude during the optimization process
3. The paper doesn't explore the impact of numerical precision in the Cholesky factorization on the final optimization results
4. The comparison with other low-complexity variants (e.g., Loshchilov, 2015) is limited to a brief mention in the conclusion

**Recommendation:**
The paper makes a strong contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization by providing a theoretically justified and practically efficient modification to the CMA-ES algorithm. The authors successfully address a significant computational bottleneck in the standard algorithm while maintaining its optimization performance. The paper is technically sound, clearly written, and demonstrates significant practical impact. The theoretical justification is particularly important as it distinguishes this work from previous attempts to use Cholesky factorization in CMA-ES variants.

Pro acceptance: The paper provides a theoretically sound and practically significant improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm, with clear empirical evidence of the benefits. The approach is well-motivated, properly evaluated, and addresses a real bottleneck in the algorithm.

Con rejection: The paper's contribution is incremental in the sense that it builds on previous work using Cholesky factorization, but the theoretical justification and application to the standard CMA-ES makes it a significant advancement. The empirical results are convincing and the runtime improvements are substantial.

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of optimization algorithms and is well-suited for publication at NIPS. The technical soundness, clarity, novelty, and significance meet the highest standards of the conference.
