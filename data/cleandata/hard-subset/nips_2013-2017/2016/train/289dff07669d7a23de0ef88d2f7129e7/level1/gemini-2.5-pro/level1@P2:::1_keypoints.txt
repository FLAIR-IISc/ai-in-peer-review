- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T) to reduce covariance update cost from O(d^3) to O(d^2).  
- Replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA, introducing a rotational error term but enabling efficient updates.  
- Provides Lemma 1 showing the approximation error vanishes as the normalized covariance converges.  
- Empirical results show virtually identical function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES.  
- Demonstrates large wall-clock speed-ups, growing with dimension (e.g., ~20× faster at d=64).  
- Algorithm is clearly described and appears technically sound.  
- Theoretical justification is insightful but relies on an unproven convergence assumption for the full algorithm.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against reference implementations on standard benchmarks.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear and reproducible, with precise algorithm descriptions and clean figures.  
- Original in successfully integrating Cholesky updates into the modern standard CMA-ES where prior work did not.  
- Significance is high: removes a major scalability barrier for CMA-ES and is likely to become a new standard implementation.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: elegant O(d^2) solution, solid theory + empirical support, practical impact.  
- Main argument against acceptance: reliance on a convergence assumption makes the theoretical claim heuristic rather than a full proof.  
- Overall conclusion: excellent paper with substantial practical and scientific contribution; strong recommendation for acceptance.
