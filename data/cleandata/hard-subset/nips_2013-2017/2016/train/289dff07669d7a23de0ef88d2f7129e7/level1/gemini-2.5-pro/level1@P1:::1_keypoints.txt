- Paper reduces CMA-ES bottleneck by updating the Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T) instead of C, achieving O(µd^2) time and O(d^2) storage.
- Replaces C^(-1/2) with A^(-1) in CSA and justifies it via Lemma 1: rotational error shrinks as covariance shape converges.
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while providing large wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension.

Strengths:
- Significant practical impact: substantial speedups (e.g., ~20x at d=64) making CMA-ES practical for medium-to-large problems.
- Technical novelty: an elegant theoretical justification for using A^(-1) in CSA that addresses a long-standing challenge.
- Clarity and organization: well-written, clearly motivated, with explicit algorithms.
- Thorough experiments: appropriate baselines and convincing empirical evidence, with effective visualizations of asymptotic gains.

Weaknesses:
- Theoretical justification is heuristic: Lemma 1 assumes normalized covariance convergence and offers no formal error bound for the stochastic setting.
- Novelty clarity: Cholesky rank-one update is standard; the paper should emphasize that the novelty is integrating it into standard CMA-ES CSA.
- Experimental scope: lacks a real-world machine-learning experiment (e.g., hyperparameter tuning or policy search) to demonstrate practical applicability.

Overall:
- Strong, practical contribution with minor caveats; recommendation: Accept.
