## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization by introducing the Cholesky-CMA-ES, a modification to the standard CMA-ES algorithm. The core idea – leveraging triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square root computation in covariance matrix updates – is well-motivated and addresses a significant computational bottleneck, particularly in high-dimensional problems. The authors correctly identify that the matrix update often dominates runtime, and their approach demonstrably reduces this complexity while maintaining the number of objective function evaluations. This work builds upon previous efforts to accelerate CMA-ES (e.g., Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015) by providing a theoretically grounded approach applicable to the full CMA-ES framework, rather than simplified variants.

The paper’s strengths lie in its clear presentation of the problem, the elegant solution using Cholesky factorization, and the empirical validation demonstrating significant speedups, especially as dimensionality increases. The theoretical justification, while present, could benefit from further elaboration. The authors acknowledge a potential error introduced by replacing the matrix inverse with the inverse of the Cholesky factor and provide a Lemma attempting to show its diminishing impact with convergence. However, a more detailed explanation of *why* this approximation is numerically more stable, beyond the convergence argument, would strengthen the paper. Specifically, discussing the conditioning of the Cholesky factor versus the direct matrix inverse would be beneficial.

The experimental results are convincing, showing that the Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains comparable performance (in terms of function evaluations) to the standard CMA-ES while achieving substantial reductions in wall-clock time. The comparison to other acceleration techniques (CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES) further highlights the effectiveness of the proposed method. 

A promising direction for future work, as suggested by the authors, is combining this approach with low-dimensional approximation techniques (Loshchilov, 2015) to tackle even higher-dimensional problems, such as those encountered in neural network training. 

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Significant reduction in runtime complexity with no increase in function evaluations.
*   Valuable implementation of Cholesky factorization for CMA-ES.
*   Clear presentation and strong empirical validation.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Theoretical justification for numerical stability could be more detailed.



**Overall:** I recommend acceptance of this paper. It presents a well-motivated, theoretically sound, and empirically validated improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm. The clarity of the presentation and the potential impact of the work make it a valuable addition to the NIPS conference.
