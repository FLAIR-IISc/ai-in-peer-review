- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor, reducing covariance update cost from O(d³) to O(μd²) per iteration.  
- Key insight: omit estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t in cumulative step-size adaptation and use the Cholesky factor directly.  
- Theoretical claim: under restrictive convergence assumptions (E_t → I), the Cholesky approximation is asymptotically equivalent to full CMA-ES.  
- Provides a novel theoretical argument (Lemma 1) justifying the approximation under certain conditions.  
- Empirical results show significant runtime speedups across dimensions with no loss in optimization performance on standard benchmarks.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and extends it to standard CMA-ES with theoretical support.  
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written; explains the Cholesky–Gaussian sampling connection effectively.  
- Quality: empirical validation and runtime comparisons are strong, but formal theoretical guarantees are limited and need stronger justification.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of prior methods with a novel focus on optimal storage and computational complexity.  
- Significance: substantial for high-dimensional problems with cheap function evaluations, but limited practical impact for real-world tasks with expensive objective evaluations.  
- Suggested experiments (track E_t evolution, test on real-time robotics) would strengthen claims about practical significance.  
- Recommendation: technically sound and valuable for a specific niche; recommend acceptance with caveats about practical impact and theoretical assumptions.
