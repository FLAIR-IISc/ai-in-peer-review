- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the triangular Cholesky factor directly, reducing per-iteration complexity to O(µd²) and lowering memory use.  
- Addresses CMA-ES bottleneck from O(d³) covariance decomposition, enabling scalability to higher dimensions.  
- Main technical challenge: CSA requires C^(-1/2); authors replace it with A^(-1) and justify the substitution theoretically.  
- Lemma 1 and analysis show the error from using A^(-1) vanishes as the covariance shape converges.  
- Empirical results on standard benchmarks show massive wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) with no significant increase in function evaluations.  
- Builds on prior efficiency efforts (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) but provides a more complete, well-justified solution for standard CMA-ES.  
- Secondary benefit: diagonal of Cholesky factor yields eigenvalues for free, useful for monitoring conditioning.  
- Strength — Significance: makes CMA-ES practical for ML tasks (RL, hyperparameter tuning) where iteration overhead matters.  
- Strength — Quality: sound technical contribution, plausible theoretical justification, and thorough empirical evaluation.  
- Strength — Clarity/Originality: well-written, clearly motivated, and introduces a novel, justified CSA modification enabling efficient rank‑one updates.  
- Weakness — Clarification 1: need explicit statement whether all compared implementations used the same language and linear algebra libraries to ensure fair wall-clock comparisons.  
- Weakness — Clarification 2: request intuition for why performance gap on ill-conditioned Cigar/Discus at d=256 between Cholesky-CMA-ES and CMA-ES/d is small despite delayed updates.  
- Overall: excellent, practical contribution with strong evidence and clear recommendation for acceptance.
