- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates and stores the triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) and replaces C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA.  
- Theoretical justification: RQ/polar-decomposition argument and Lemma 1 show rotation error from approximation diminishes as distribution stabilizes.  
- Complexity: Implements covariance update as rank-one updates to A with O(µ d^2) time and optimal O(d^2/2) memory.  
- Practical benefit: Diagonal of A yields covariance eigenvalues “for free” and enables efficient A^{-1} updates.  
- Empirical results: Matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while giving substantial wall-clock speedups, increasing with dimension (e.g., ~20x at d=64).  
- Benchmarks: Tested on rotated suites up to d=256 with careful ablations against delayed-decomposition CMA-ES and a non-triangular Cholesky variant.  
- Positioning: Builds on delayed decompositions and restricted models, distinct from prior Cholesky updates that didn’t extend to standard CMA-ES with CSA.  
- Novelty: First to justify and make Cholesky updating work with standard CMA-ES’s CSA in a practically compelling way.  
- Technical soundness: Rank-one derivations and complexity analysis are clear; Lemma 1 is a reasonable (though not full) stochastic convergence proof.  
- Evaluation honesty: Authors acknowledge cache effects at d=256 and include CMA-ES/d baseline.  
- Clarity: Paper is clearly written with helpful algorithms, concise theory, and well-explained motivation and prior work relation.  
- Significance: Offers measurable practical advantages without losing evaluation efficiency; relevant for ML tasks using CMA-ES.  
- Strength: Reduces update cost and memory with stable triangular rank-one updates.  
- Strength: Provides eigenvalues and efficient inverses at no extra cost.  
- Strength: Empirically preserves evaluations and improves runtime on high dimensions.  
- Weakness: Need implementation parity details (language, codebase, compiler/BLAS, CPU/cache, threading, vectorization, memory layout) to validate wall-clock claims.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 (d=256 Cigar/Discus) shows smaller runtime gap; request per-iteration time breakdown to explain causes (iteration count, memory/cache, optimized routines).  
- Weakness: CSA approximation could have transient effects during sharp conditioning changes; request sensitivity/ablation (occasional exact CSA or tracking E_t).  
- Weakness: Numerical stability concerns—ask about conditioning of A, rounding error accumulation, and need for re-triangularization or jitter.  
- Pros (accept): Strong practical contribution, plausible theoretical motivation, optimal complexity, high relevance to ML.  
- Cons (reject arguments): CSA justification lacks formal stochastic guarantees; runtime claims need more controlled implementation parity and time breakdown.  
- Suggestions: Add implementation details, per-iteration time breakdown, diagnostics for rotational term E_t E_{t-1}^T, and release code/scripts for reproducibility.  
- Overall: High-quality, well-executed work with clear practical impact; recommend acceptance after addressing minor clarifications.
