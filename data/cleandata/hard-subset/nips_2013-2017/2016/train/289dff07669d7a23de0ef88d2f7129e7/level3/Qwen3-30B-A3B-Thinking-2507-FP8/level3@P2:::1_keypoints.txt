- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root in step-size adaptation with triangular Cholesky factor update to reduce cost.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal O(μ d²) time and O(d²) space for covariance updates while preserving optimization performance.  
- Theoretical novelty: proves (Lemma 1) that replacing C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹ (Cholesky factor) does not degrade performance as error vanishes on convergence.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) but provides the first rigorous justification for standard CMA-ES.  
- Empirical results: identical function-evaluation counts to standard CMA-ES with delayed decomposition.  
- Empirical speedups: significant wall-clock improvements (up to 20× at d=64) with speedup increasing with dimensionality.  
- Experimental design is thorough: 100 trials per setting, rotation invariance tests, and comparisons to relevant baselines.  
- Runtime analysis correctly identifies the O(d³) bottleneck in standard CMA-ES and shows how Cholesky-CMA-ES resolves it.  
- Paper is exceptionally clear: concise background, well-motivated method, precise Algorithm 1, accessible theory, and statistically rigorous results.  
- Practical utility: includes implementation details (storing Cholesky factors) and provides eigenvalues "for free" as diagnostics.  
- Acknowledges limitation that quadratic scaling remains prohibitive for very large d where low-rank approximations may be needed.  
- Weakness: unclear whether wall-clock timing comparisons used identical implementations/libraries and numerical optimizations across variants.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 anomaly at d=256 (Cigar/Discus) attributed to L2 cache effects only in supplementary material; should be clarified in main text.  
- Overall recommendation: strongly recommend acceptance due to novel theoretical contribution, comprehensive empirical validation, and practical impact.
