- Proposal: replace O(d³) eigendecomposition in CMA-ES with direct triangular Cholesky factor updates costing O(µ d²).
- Key technical challenge: CSA requires C⁻¹/², authors approximate it with A⁻¹ and argue the approximation error decreases as the covariance stabilizes (Lemma 1).
- Empirical finding: Cholesky-CMA-ES matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations but yields substantial wall-clock speedups, especially in high dimensions.
- Strength: compelling and practical wall-clock speedup results demonstrating clear runtime benefits.
- Weakness: no formal proof that the proposed sequence of rank-one Cholesky updates is equivalent to the standard rank-µ covariance update for general (µ, λ)-CMA-ES.
- Weakness: central claim about the CSA-path approximation lacks direct empirical validation tracking rotational error over optimization runs (e.g., Frobenius norm of E_{t-1}ᵀE_t − I).
- Clarity: generally well-written with a clearly presented Algorithm 1.
- Weakness: time-complexity discussion is imprecise; should use Θ-notation and clarify per-iteration Θ(µ d²) vs amortized strategies of reference implementations.
- Minor issues: typos/inconsistencies — Algorithm 2 `wk` likely `αk`; Table 1 uses `n` vs `d`; inconsistent function notation f(xi,t) vs F.
- Originality: limited—an incremental extension of Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015); main novelty is CSA-path approximation handling.
- Significance: potentially high if technical weaknesses are resolved—could become a standard, enabling CMA-ES for larger problems.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important computational bottleneck, clear empirical speedups, well-motivated method.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, missing equivalence proof for covariance update, lack of empirical support for CSA approximation, and imprecise complexity analysis.
- Conclusion: promising and practically valuable but not ready for publication without formal derivations and additional empirical validation; recommend adding proofs and error-tracking experiments.
