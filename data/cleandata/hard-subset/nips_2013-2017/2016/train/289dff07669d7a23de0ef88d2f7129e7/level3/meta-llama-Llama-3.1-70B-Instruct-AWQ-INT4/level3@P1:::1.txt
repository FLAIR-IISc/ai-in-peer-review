This paper proposes a novel variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a powerful real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm. The new approach, dubbed Cholesky-CMA-ES, updates the covariance matrix via triangular Cholesky factors, significantly reducing the update complexity and memory requirements. This is particularly important for large-scale optimization problems, where the standard CMA-ES can become computationally expensive.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, including a proof that the error introduced by approximating the step-size adaptation is small and decreases over time as the algorithm converges to the optimum. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through extensive empirical evaluations on various benchmark functions, including the Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, and Diffpowers functions.

The results show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves the same number of function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES, but with significantly reduced wall-clock time, particularly in high-dimensional problems. This makes the Cholesky-CMA-ES a promising candidate for medium to large-scale CMA-ES applications.

The paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated, with a clear presentation of the theoretical and empirical results. The authors also provide a detailed discussion of the related work and the relevance of their approach to machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.

One minor request is to clarify the implementation details to ensure that the CPU/time comparisons are comparable. Specifically, it would be helpful to know whether the same programming language and implementation were used for both the standard CMA-ES and the Cholesky-CMA-ES.

Another minor request is to explain why Figure 2 does not show more pronounced differences between the Cholesky-CMA-ES and the standard CMA-ES for the Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256. It would be helpful to understand why the speedup is less significant for these functions at high dimensions.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization and machine learning, and the Cholesky-CMA-ES is likely to become a new default for medium to large-scale CMA-ES applications.
