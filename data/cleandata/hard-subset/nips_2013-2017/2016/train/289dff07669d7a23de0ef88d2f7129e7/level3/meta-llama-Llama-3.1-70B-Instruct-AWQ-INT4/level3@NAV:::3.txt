This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm with no significant change in the number of objective function evaluations. The authors achieve this by using a triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the square root of the covariance matrix in the standard CMA-ES.

The main idea of the paper is to replace the matrix square root by the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor, which can be computed in quadratic time. The authors provide a theoretical justification for why this modification does not impair the performance of the algorithm. They also present experimental results comparing the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants, demonstrating a significant increase in optimization speed.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical justification. The experimental results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough comparison of the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants.

However, the contribution of the paper is limited, as the extension to rank-μ is a straightforward generalization of the work by Krause et al. (2015). The novelty of the paper is not convincing, and the authors could have done more to differentiate their work from existing research.

Additionally, the theoretical support for covariance convergence could be strengthened by referencing more recent work, such as Beyer (2014). The authors also mention that the introduction of the new random rotation matrix Et could introduce some error, but they do not provide a thorough analysis of this error.

Minor issues include a typo in the denominator of Eq. (1), where σ should be replaced by \tilde{σ}. There is also an extra "by" in line 94, which should be removed.
