- Main contribution: introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, replacing the full covariance with a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time complexity to O(μd^2) from O(d^3).  
- Key idea: maintain and update the Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix for more efficient representation in high dimensions.  
- Theoretical claim: provides convergence justification showing approximation error decreases as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical results: demonstrates significant speed-up while maintaining comparable optimization performance to original CMA-ES across benchmark functions and dimensions.  
- Scalability: shows applicability to a range of benchmark functions with varying dimensionality.  
- Writing and presentation: paper is well-written, clear, and well-organized with easy-to-understand empirical results.  
- Strength: novel modification that reduces computational complexity of CMA-ES.  
- Strength: solid theoretical justification for the proposed approximation.  
- Strength: empirical evidence supports speed-up without loss of performance.  
- Limitation: assumes cheap objective evaluations and high dimensionality, which may not hold in many real-world cases.  
- Limitation: lacks thorough analysis of limitations such as parameter sensitivity and performance on non-separable problems.  
- Limitation: would benefit from additional experiments and broader comparisons with other optimization methods.  
- Suggestion: test Cholesky-CMA-ES on real-world high-dimensional problems.  
- Suggestion: analyze sensitivity to parameters and behavior on non-separable problems.  
- Suggestion: compare against other methods (e.g., quasi-Newton, gradient-based) to further validate effectiveness.  
- Scores: Quality 8/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 8/10.
