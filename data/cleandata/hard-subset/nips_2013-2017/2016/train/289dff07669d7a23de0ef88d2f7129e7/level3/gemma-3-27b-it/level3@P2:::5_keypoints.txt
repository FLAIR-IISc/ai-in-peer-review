- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the covariance matrix to reduce update cost.
- Complexity: Reduces computational complexity to O(μ d²) versus standard CMA-ES’s O(d³) (or delayed-update strategies).
- Theoretical justification: Omits estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t, arguing E_t → I under convergence assumptions, making the method asymptotically equivalent to CMA-ES.
- Empirical results: Standard benchmarks show comparable optimization performance with substantial wall-clock speed-ups.
- Context/prior work: Builds on delayed updates, restricted models, and prior Cholesky-update methods while addressing their limitations.
- Strength — technical soundness: Method is grounded in numerical linear algebra and clearly described; appears well-engineered.
- Strength — computational efficiency: Offers significant scalability improvements for high-dimensional problems.
- Strength — empirical validation: Demonstrates speed-ups without increased function evaluations and includes comparisons to other variants.
- Strength — presentation: Paper is well-written and provides algorithms that facilitate implementation and reproduction.
- Weakness — restrictive assumptions: Reliance on convergence of E_t to identity may not hold universally and needs deeper analysis.
- Weakness — limited real-world evaluation: Lacks demonstration on a real-world task (e.g., robotics policy search) to show practical impact.
- Weakness — incomplete baseline comparison: Does not report wall-clock runtimes for standard CMA-ES without delay strategies.
- Weakness — experimental scope: Benchmarks may be too narrow; tests on continuously varied spectra and broader problems are needed.
- Arguments for acceptance: Novel, technically sound, substantial efficiency gains, empirically validated, and clearly presented.
- Arguments against acceptance: Theoretical assumptions are restrictive and practical/experimental validation is limited.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to analyze E_t’s evolution and include a real-world application or broader experiments.
