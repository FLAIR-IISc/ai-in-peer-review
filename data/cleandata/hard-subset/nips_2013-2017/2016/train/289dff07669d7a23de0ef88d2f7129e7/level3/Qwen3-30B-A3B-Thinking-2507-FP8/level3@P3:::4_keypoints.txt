- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce computation and storage.  
- Main theoretical contribution: proves Cholesky-based update does not degrade performance, filling a gap in prior work.  
- Lemma 1: error from replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} vanishes as algorithm converges due to exponential weighting in CSA.  
- Reduces covariance-update complexity from O(d^3) to O(d^2) and achieves asymptotically optimal O(µ d^2) runtime with minimal memory.  
- Empirical results match standard CMA-ES in objective evaluations while greatly reducing wall-clock time; speedups grow with dimension.  
- Strong experimental validation across benchmarks and dimensions (d = 4…256); e.g., 20× faster at d = 64 versus reference.  
- Implementation publicly available in the Shark library.  
- Strength: clear theoretical justification improving over prior Cholesky attempts.  
- Strength: runtime and memory efficiency with practical implementation and thorough comparisons.  
- Strength: additional benefits of Cholesky—free eigenvalues and numerically stable inverse computation.  
- Weakness: insufficient detailed analysis/proofs about the claimed numerical stability and error bounds.  
- Weakness: connection to low-dimensional approximation methods (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) is noted but not explored or demonstrated.  
- Weakness: need clearer explanation of error decay for non-convergent or unstable trajectories.  
- Quality/clarity/originality: technically sound, well-written, novel theoretical justification extending Cholesky use to standard CMA-ES.  
- Significance: addresses major computational bottleneck in CMA-ES with practical, scalable speedups—recommended for adoption where covariance can be stored.  
- Recommendation: overall high-quality paper; minor revisions on numerical-stability analysis suggested; reviewer recommends acceptance.
