- Proposes updating CMA-ES covariance via triangular Cholesky factors, yielding quadratic time and reduced memory vs eigen-decomposition.  
- Extends and theoretically justifies prior Cholesky-based approaches, adapting them to the full CMA-ES framework.  
- Provides clear mathematical grounding (Lemma 1) and addresses potential coordinate rotation issues.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while delivering substantial wall-clock speedups, especially at higher dimensions.  
- Demonstrates practical relevance for large-scale ML, RL, and hyperparameter optimization tasks.  
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and cites relevant prior work including information geometry.  
- Minor ambiguity: unclear whether all implementations (CMA-ES-Ref, Suttorp-CMA-ES, Cholesky-CMA-ES) used identical programming language/compiler/optimizations for fair timing comparisons.  
- Empirical caveat: speedups are less pronounced for the Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256 and need further explanation.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant complexity reduction without performance loss, strong theory, clear practical benefits, and broad relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: minor timing-implementation ambiguity and limited analysis of muted speedups on some functions.  
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution with substantial impact potential; recommended for acceptance.
