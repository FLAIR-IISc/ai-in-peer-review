- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains the lower-triangular Cholesky factor and implements rank-1 and rank-μ updates via sequential rank-1 compositions.

- Replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA and argues (under a covariance-convergence assumption) that induced random rotations vanish asymptotically.

- Experiments show similar evaluation efficiency to reference CMA-ES but substantial wall-clock speedups, especially in higher dimensions.

- Builds on Suttorp et al. (2009) and extends Krause & Igel (2015) from rank-1 to general rank-μ updates.

- Cholesky update derivation is sound and implementation details are clear and reproducible.

- CSA modification is motivated by an RQ/polar-decomposition argument and supported by Lemma 1.

- The covariance-convergence assumption is strong; the paper lacks formal bounds on CSA bias or variance inflation from replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}.

- Needs clearer conditions or formal guarantees under which the rotation error becomes negligible.

- Experiments are appropriate but would benefit from broader stress tests (noisy functions, more ill-conditioned non-separable problems, and higher dimensionalities).

- Writing and organization are good; the rank-μ generalization and algorithmic-complexity benefits are clearly presented.

- The rank-μ extension is a natural/generalization and thus of limited novelty; the CSA change is more novel but remains partly heuristic without stronger theory.

- Related work mostly covered, but should add discussion of Beyer (ECJ 2014) and other convergence analyses to contextualize Lemma 1.

- Significance: practically valuable speedups for full-covariance CMA-ES users; contribution is incremental but useful.

- Suggestions: provide theoretical support or explicit error bounds (e.g., leveraging Beyer 2014), compare to LM-CMA and other efficient variants, and add ablations isolating rank-μ vs rank-1 effects.

- Pros: strong engineering impact (time/memory), clear exposition, reproducible details, and empirical speedups without loss of evaluation efficiency.

- Cons: limited novelty relative to Krause & Igel (2015), CSA lacks rigorous guarantees, and experimental breadth is limited.

- Minor issues: denominator in Eq. (1) should be tilde-σ; typo on line 94—remove extra “by.”
