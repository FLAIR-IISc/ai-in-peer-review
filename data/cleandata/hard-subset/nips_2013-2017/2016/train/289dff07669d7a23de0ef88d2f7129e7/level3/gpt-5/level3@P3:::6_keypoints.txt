- Proposal: maintain/update a triangular Cholesky factor A and replace C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} to get quadratic-time full-covariance CMA-ES with substantial wall-clock speedups.
- Technical change: rewrite rank-1 and rank-μ covariance updates as sequential quadratic-time rank-one updates on A and adjust CSA accordingly.
- Empirical claim: similar evaluation counts to reference CMA-ES with increasing wall-clock speedups at higher dimensions.
- Relation to prior work: Cholesky-based updates date to Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015); paper applies known ideas to multi-λ CMA-ES.
- Novelty assessment: incremental—mainly looping known rankOneUpdate for rank-μ and substituting A^{-1} in CSA.
- Missing comparisons: no direct algorithmic or empirical baselines vs Suttorp (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015).
- Strength: practical, simple, robust implementation with clear algorithmic description and plausible CSA approximation argument.
- Engineering benefits: reduced memory, quadratic-time inversion of A, and a clean rank-one update primitive.
- Naming issue: reusing “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and creates priority/confusion problems.
- Experimental incompleteness: figures should include Suttorp’s and Krause’s variants for both eval counts and wall-clock timings.
- Plotting concern: median/aggregation method and handling of censored runs unclear; Cigar plateau difference may be an artifact.
- Overstated claims: assertions of “optimality” and superiority ignore cases where diagonal/low-rank variants are sufficient and misrepresent the magnitude of gains.
- Numerical-stability claim: assertion that Suttorp’s method is unstable is unsupported and contradicts Suttorp et al.’s reported stability.
- Scalability limitation: complexity remains O(μ d^2), so method does not enable truly large-scale CMA-ES like LM-CMA.
- Minor factual corrections needed: eigenvalue decompositions are periodic in practice, and Cholesky diagonal elements are not eigenvalues in general.
- Quality verdict: technically sound with a reasonable approximation argument, but lacks crucial empirical/algorithmic comparisons and contains overstated claims.
- Clarity verdict: well-written overall but needs clearer positioning relative to Suttorp/Krause, explicit statement of the core algorithmic change, and clearer plotting/stability explanations.
- Originality verdict: incremental contribution—practical application of known rank-one Cholesky updates; naming overpromises novelty.
- Significance verdict: useful engineering contribution for practitioners, but significance would increase with thorough comparisons and clearer quantification.
- Requested fix: resolve naming conflict (suggestion: Triangular-Cholesky CMA-ES) to avoid priority confusion.
- Requested fix: add Suttorp and Krause variants as baselines (eval counts and runtimes); move extended plots to supplement if needed.
- Requested fix: explicitly state that the main algorithmic change is looping rankOneUpdate over rank-μ terms and using A^{-1} in CSA, or provide deeper justification/analysis.
- Requested fix: provide evidence for numerical-stability claims (conditioning tests, failure cases) or soften the statements.
- Requested fix: clarify plotting methodology (median definition, censored runs) and address the Cigar plateau discrepancy.
- Pros summary: simple, implementable change giving strong speedups while preserving full-covariance modeling and evaluation efficiency.
- Cons summary: naming conflict, insufficient engagement with closely related work, incremental novelty, some factual inaccuracies and overstated claims.
- Recommendation: borderline reject in current form; potentially acceptable after addressing naming, missing comparisons, stability evidence, and overstated claims.
