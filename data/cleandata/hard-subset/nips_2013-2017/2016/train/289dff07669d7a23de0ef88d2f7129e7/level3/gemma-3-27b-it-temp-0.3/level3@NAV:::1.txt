## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper presents a compelling modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) focusing on a more efficient covariance matrix update scheme using triangular Cholesky factors. The core idea – replacing the matrix square root with its Cholesky factor in the step-size adaptation – is well-motivated by the desire to reduce both computational complexity and memory requirements, particularly in high-dimensional optimization problems. This builds upon previous work attempting Cholesky-based updates (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015) by providing a theoretical justification for maintaining performance and extending the approach to the standard CMA-ES framework. 

The authors clearly articulate the problem – the computational bottleneck of covariance matrix updates in standard CMA-ES – and position their work within the existing landscape of optimization algorithms. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a quadratic time complexity for the update, which is asymptotically optimal, and offers the added benefit of providing eigenvalues of the covariance matrix without extra cost. The empirical evaluation is thorough, demonstrating that the new algorithm maintains comparable function evaluation counts to the standard CMA-ES while achieving substantial wall-clock speedups, especially as dimensionality increases. This is a significant practical advantage. The paper is exceptionally well-written and clearly explains the technical details. Its relevance extends beyond optimization, impacting areas like machine learning, reinforcement learning, and information geometry.

However, a couple of minor points could be clarified. The authors should provide more detail regarding the implementation environment (programming language, libraries used, hardware specifications) to ensure a fair comparison of CPU/time measurements. While the results are convincing, knowing these details would strengthen the analysis. Additionally, the lack of pronounced differences in Figure 2 for the Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256 is somewhat puzzling, as these functions are known to be sensitive to covariance adaptation. A brief discussion explaining this observation would be beneficial.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Significant reduction in wall-clock time, particularly for large-dimensional problems.
*   Maintains comparable performance (function evaluation counts) to standard CMA-ES.
*   Theoretically sound and well-motivated approach.
*   Clear and comprehensive writing.
*   Strong empirical validation.
*   Likely to become a default choice for medium/large-scale CMA-ES applications.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

*   Minor clarification needed regarding implementation details for CPU/time comparisons.
*   A brief explanation of the limited differences observed in Figure 2 would be helpful.

**Overall:** This is a high-quality paper with a significant contribution to the field. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES is a practical and theoretically sound improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm. I strongly recommend acceptance.
