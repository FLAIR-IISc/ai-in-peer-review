- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces matrix square root with Cholesky factorization, claiming covariance updates cost drops from O(d^3) to O(μd^2).  
- Claims maintained algorithmic performance with much lower computational overhead for high-dimensional problems.  
- Builds on prior work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and asserts theoretical justification for no performance degradation.  
- Strengths: clear writing, reasonable methodology, detailed algorithm, storage advantage, and empirical speedups up to 20× for d=64; offers insights on rotation-matrix error convergence.  
- Originality is limited — approach is nearly identical to Krause & Igel (2015) and fails to sufficiently distinguish itself from that prior work.  
- Missing critical equivalence proof that replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} preserves CSA behavior; this proof is essential even if available elsewhere.  
- Complexity claim is misleading — using O(μd^2) is imprecise; authors should use Θ-notation and clearly explain why this outperforms delayed-update strategies.  
- Claim of a “small path error that decreases” contradicts Hansen and lacks temporal validation (only final performance is shown, not error evolution).  
- Contains several notation errors (e.g., wk→α_k, n→d, 'F'→'f') that undermine credibility.  
- Overall: promising computational benefits but fails NIPS standards due to insufficient originality, missing theoretical justification, and unclear complexity claims; requires substantial revision.
