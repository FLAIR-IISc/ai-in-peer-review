- Proposes updating a triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance to avoid repeated cubic-cost decompositions.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-update CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and approximates CSA to operate on the triangular factor.  
- Strength: addresses CMA-ES covariance update bottleneck and shows significant wall-clock speedup over a reference implementation.  
- Strength: triangular factor representation is more memory-efficient than storing the full covariance.  
- Major flaw: fails to compare algorithmically or empirically to the most relevant baselines (Suttorp 2009 and Krause 2015).  
- Novelty appears limited—main change is extending a rank-one update to rank-µ by iterating it in a loop, which seems incremental.  
- Claim that Suttorp’s method is “prone to numerical instabilities” is unsubstantiated and contradicts the original paper.  
- Naming the method “Cholesky-CMA-ES” is misleading/conflicting with prior work and must be resolved.  
- Claims of “optimality” are overstated; complexity remains quadratic and does not address scalability for large dimensions.  
- Method is not optimal for separable functions where a diagonal covariance would suffice.  
- Experiments are incomplete without comparisons to Suttorp and Krause variants.  
- Reported plateau on the Cigar function may be an artifact of plotting median results and needs clarification.  
- Overall assessment: limited novelty, missing critical comparisons, misleading claims and naming—major revision required before acceptance.
