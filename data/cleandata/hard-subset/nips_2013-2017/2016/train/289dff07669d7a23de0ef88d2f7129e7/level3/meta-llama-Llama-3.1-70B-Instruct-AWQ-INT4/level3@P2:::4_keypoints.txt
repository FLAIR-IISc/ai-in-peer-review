- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to eliminate the square root and reduce matrix-inverse computation.  
- Provides theoretical justification that using the Cholesky factor does not degrade CMA-ES performance.  
- Experimental results show significant wall-clock speedups, especially in high dimensions, while keeping objective evaluation counts essentially unchanged.  
- Thorough background on CMA-ES and clear presentation of the proposed modification.  
- Strength: substantial runtime complexity reduction for derivative-free optimization.  
- Strength: rigorous and well-explained theoretical justification.  
- Strength: comprehensive experimental evaluation on benchmark functions.  
- Weakness: unclear explanation of the claimed "numerically more stable" behavior and how it is achieved.  
- Weakness: insufficient discussion of limitations and the impact of quadratic scaling in very high-dimensional problems.  
- Originality: novel algorithmic modification and original theoretical analysis.  
- Significance: important implications for high-dimensional derivative-free optimization and potential wide adoption.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and experimental results are clearly presented.  
- Quality: high-quality work with solid theory and experiments.  
- Recommendation: suitable for publication in a top-tier venue, pending clarification of weaknesses.  
- Suggestions: clarify numerical stability claims, discuss high-dimensional limitations in more detail, and consider combining with low-dimensional approximation methods (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).
