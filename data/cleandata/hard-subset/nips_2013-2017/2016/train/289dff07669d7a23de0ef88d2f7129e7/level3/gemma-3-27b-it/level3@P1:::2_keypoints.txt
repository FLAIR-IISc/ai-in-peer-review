- Proposes replacing CMA-ES covariance eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factor updates to reduce runtime and memory.
- Maintains and updates a triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix.
- Modifies the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) to accommodate the Cholesky-based update and provides theoretical justification for convergence.
- Claims empirical parity with standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while offering substantial runtime speedups.
- Argues reduced complexity to O(μd^2), improving over the O(d^3) cost of full covariance updates.
- Targets a real practical bottleneck in high-dimensional derivative-free optimization.
- Strength: potential for significant speedup in high dimensions.
- Strength: clear, logical presentation and helpful explanation of Cholesky decomposition.
- Strength: empirical validation on benchmark functions showing comparable performance and faster runtime.
- Weakness: limited originality—similar ideas exist (Krause & Igel 2015; Suttorp et al. 2009) and comparison is insufficient.
- Weakness: lacks a formal proof or quantifiable bound demonstrating equivalence between the original and Cholesky updates.
- Weakness: ambiguous complexity claim—needs clearer Θ-notation analysis and practical comparisons (e.g., delayed updates).
- Weakness: unvalidated assertion that approximation error is small and decreases over time; no experiments tracking this path error.
- Weakness: minor technical errors and inconsistent notation reduce polish (e.g., variable/typo issues).
- For acceptance: potential speedup, clear exposition, and supporting empirical results.
- Against acceptance: limited novelty, missing formal proofs, insufficient error analysis, and minor technical flaws.
- Overall assessment: potentially useful contribution but rejects due to limited originality and lack of rigorous theoretical/empirical validation; recommends revision and resubmission after addressing issues.
