- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T) via rank-one updates to avoid full covariance decompositions, and replaces C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky/factor-update ideas (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and aims to extend them to rank-μ CMA-ES and justify the CSA change.  

Strengths:
- Clear, practical, and easy-to-implement algorithm with available code.  
- Good motivation: avoids cubic-time eigen/sqrt ops important when evaluations are cheap and d large.  
- Empirical results show similar function-evaluation counts to CMA-ES reference and substantial wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension.  
- Triangular factor gives useful byproducts (diagonal eigenvalues, cheap triangular solves/inversion).

Weaknesses:
- Limited originality: appears as a straightforward generalization of known Cholesky-update work; novelty not crisply delineated.  
- Missing formal proof that sequential rank-one updates on A reproduce the rank-μ covariance update and preserve SPD; correctness/stability not shown.  
- Complexity claims underspecified: O(μ d^2) stated without Θ-notation, constants, or amortized comparison to delayed-decomposition CMA-ES.  
- CSA approximation insufficiently validated; time-resolved diagnostics are missing despite known sensitivity of CSA normalization.  
- Empirical scope limited: lacks comparisons to more scalable methods (e.g., LM-CMA) at higher dimensions.

Quality (technical/theory/experiments):
- Overall idea reasonable and empirical results convincing for tested regimes, but two technical gaps remain: equivalence/stability proof for Algorithm 2 and quantitative CSA-path-error analysis.  
- Lemma 1 relies on strong asymptotic assumptions; finite-time bounds or measurements of rotation drift/path-length bias are needed.  
- Experiments solid for rotated benchmarks and speed, but should add time-series diagnostics, discrepancy measurements between A^{-1} and C^{-1/2}, small-d exact-CSA ablations, and amortized cost comparisons.

Clarity and presentation:
- Well written and easy to follow, but needs a proof sketch of equivalence for rank-μ Cholesky updates and conditions for SPD preservation.  
- Requires a clear complexity table using Θ-notation and explicit per-iteration costs.  
- Should explicitly discuss invariance differences introduced by the CSA modification.  
- Minor typos: Algorithm 2 “wk” → α_k; Table 1 “n” → d; Line 185 “F” → “f”.

Originality:
- Moderate: main novelty is extending Cholesky rank-one updates to rank-μ CMA-ES and proposing a practical CSA approximation, but contribution is incremental without sharp contrast to prior work.

Significance:
- Potentially high practical impact if reliable, but currently incremental and lacks validation to establish robustness across problems.

Requested revisions / essential experiments:
- Provide a formal proof or reference-adapted derivation that Algorithm 2 yields the Cholesky factor for Eq. (2) and discuss stability/SPD preservation.  
- Add a thorough complexity analysis with Θ-notation, amortized comparisons across d and λ, and report constant factors/BLAS usage.  
- Empirically quantify CSA path error over time: plot ||p_σ||/E{χ}, discrepancies between exact and approximate normalized steps, and rotation drift ||E_t E_{t-1}^T − I||; include small-d runs with exact CSA via polar decomposition.  
- Optionally compare against LM-CMA on larger d to identify the method’s practical sweet spot.

Pros (summary):
- Practical, integrable algorithm with clear wall-clock speedups and memory efficiency; useful implementation byproducts.

Cons (summary):
- Incremental novelty, missing formal equivalence/stability proof, incomplete complexity analysis, and insufficient diagnostics validating CSA approximation.

Overall recommendation:
- Reject in current form; promising and practically relevant but requires formal equivalence/stability proofs, rigorous CSA validation, and clearer complexity/amortized analyses.
