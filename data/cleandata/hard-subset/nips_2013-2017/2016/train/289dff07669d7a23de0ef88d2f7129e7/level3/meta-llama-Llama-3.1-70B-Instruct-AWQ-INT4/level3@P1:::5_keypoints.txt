- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix.  
- Reduces complexity of covariance update/storage from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2), avoiding the matrix square root.  
- Main idea: directly update Cholesky factor in quadratic time to eliminate dominant cubic operation.  
- Provides theoretical justification showing approximation error vanishes under certain assumptions.  
- Empirical results show substantial speed-up while maintaining optimization performance compared to delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Strength: solid contribution for speeding up CMA-ES, useful when evaluations are cheap and dimensionality is high.  
- Strength: thorough analysis of algorithmic complexity and convergence properties.  
- Limitation: theoretical assumptions are restrictive and formal guarantees are limited.  
- Limitation: limited practical impact because objective evaluation time often dominates and typical problem dimensions are modest.  
- Suggestion: track evolution of random orthogonal matrix E_t by running full CMA-ES and approximation simultaneously.  
- Suggestion: report actual wall-clock runtimes and include performance of full CMA-ES without delay strategies.  
- Suggestion: run quadratics with continuously varied spectra to study solution quality and E_t error versus spectrum.  
- Suggestion: include a real-world experiment where speed matters (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients).  
- Overall: valuable and original contribution but needs additional experiments and relaxed theory to strengthen practical relevance.
