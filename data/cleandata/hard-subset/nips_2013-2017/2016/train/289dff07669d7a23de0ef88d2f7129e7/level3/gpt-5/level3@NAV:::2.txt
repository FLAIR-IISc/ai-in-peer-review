This paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of CMA-ES that maintains a triangular Cholesky factor and replaces the eigen-decomposition and C−1/2 in CSA by A−1, aiming to reduce per-iteration time and memory while preserving performance. The paper relates to a long line of work on efficient covariance updates (Suttorp et al., 2009; Ros & Hansen, 2008; Loshchilov, 2014/2015) and, most directly, to Krause & Igel (FOGA’15), which derived quadratic-time rank-one Cholesky updates. The authors argue that their modification to CSA is benign because the rotation error introduced by using A−1 instead of C−1/2 decays as the algorithm stabilizes. Empirically, they report similar evaluation counts and substantial wall-clock speedups.

Quality: The idea is reasonable and the exposition is mostly clear. However, several key claims are insufficiently supported. Most critically, the paper lacks a formal equivalence proof showing that the proposed rank-one updates to A implement the covariance update of Eq. (2) (or a precise statement of where they differ). The CSA “path error is small and decreases” is only argued heuristically via Lemma 1 and validated by final-performance equality; it requires time-resolved experimental evidence (e.g., trajectories of ∥pσ∥, EtETt−1, or an empirical bound on rotation drift), particularly given prior skepticism (e.g., Hansen) about invariance under CSA. The complexity discussion is also unclear: O(μ d^2) per iteration matches the amortized complexity of CMA-ES with delayed decomposition; the paper should use Θ-notation and explain where the constant-factor gains arise (e.g., avoiding cubic eigen-decomposition, cache locality, avoiding recomputation of inverses), and ensure a fair baseline with standard delay settings.

Clarity: Writing is generally clear, but there are several notation/typo issues: wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk; n in Table 1 should be d; “F” should be “f” on line 185. The relationship to “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies” should be positioned more precisely.

Originality: Limited. The core technical component (Cholesky rank-one updates) appears to be a straightforward extension of Krause & Igel (2015) to the standard CMA-ES, with the main novelty being the CSA substitution and its heuristic justification.

Significance: Potentially useful in practice if the equivalence and speed claims are rigorously established; the approach could become a standard engineering choice.

Pros:
- Clear motivation; practical speedups are plausible.
- Clean and memory-efficient implementation idea.

Cons:
- Limited novelty relative to prior work.
- Missing equivalence proof to Eq. (2).
- Insufficient validation of CSA path-error dynamics.
- Complexity advantage not convincingly justified.

Recommendation: Not acceptable for NIPS in its current form. I encourage the authors to add the equivalence proof, provide time-resolved CSA analyses, clarify complexity with Θ-notation and fair baselines, and fix notation/typos.
