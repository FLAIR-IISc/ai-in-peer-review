- Proposes replacing CMA-ES covariance eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factorization to reduce computational cost and memory.
- Claims runtime complexity O(µ d^2) and optimal memory complexity for the modified algorithm.
- Paper is well-written with clear explanation of CMA-ES and the proposed modification.
- Mathematical derivations are sound and a theoretical convergence justification is provided.
- Experiments show significant speedup versus standard CMA-ES while keeping the same number of objective evaluations.
- Originality is limited because Cholesky-based approaches for CMA-ES have been explored previously.
- Main contribution is extending Cholesky update to standard CMA-ES and providing a convergence argument, rather than a novel problem or insight.
- Lacks a rigorous equivalence proof that replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor yields only small, vanishing error.
- Complexity claim using O(µ d^2) is unclear; reviewer recommends using Θ-notation for precision.
- Notation, formatting, and typographical errors need improvement to avoid reader confusion.
- Overall significance is practical (implementation speed) but not fundamental; current form is not acceptable for NIPS.
- Recommendation: reject pending addressing originality concerns, providing a rigorous equivalence proof, clarifying complexity, and fixing notation/formatting.
