This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, which is a widely used derivative-free optimization algorithm. The main contribution of this work is the use of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square root in the standard CMA-ES, reducing the computation for matrix inverse. The authors provide a theoretical justification for why this approach does not degrade the algorithm's performance and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed method. The introduction provides a thorough background on the CMA-ES algorithm and its applications, as well as the motivation for the proposed modification. The authors also provide a detailed description of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and its properties, including its time and memory complexity.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it achieves a significant speedup over the standard CMA-ES while maintaining the same number of objective function evaluations. The authors also provide a comparison with other CMA-ES variants, including the Suttorp-CMA-ES, which uses a non-triangular Cholesky factorization.

The paper's strengths include its clear and concise writing style, its thorough background and motivation, and its well-designed experiments. The authors also provide a detailed analysis of the proposed method's properties and its advantages over other approaches.

One potential weakness of the paper is that it could benefit from a clearer explanation of why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable." While the authors provide some discussion on this topic, it is not entirely clear how the triangular Cholesky factorization improves the numerical stability of the algorithm.

Another potential area for improvement is the exploration of combinations with low-dimensional approximation methods, such as the one proposed by Loshchilov (2015). The authors mention this as a potential direction for future work, but it would be interesting to see some preliminary results or discussion on how the Cholesky-CMA-ES could be combined with these methods.

Overall, this paper makes a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization and provides a well-designed and well-written presentation of the proposed method. The authors demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and provide a thorough analysis of its properties and advantages.
