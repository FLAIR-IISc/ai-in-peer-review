- Proposes using triangular Cholesky factors to reduce CMA-ES time/space complexity.
- Achieves claimed asymptotic time complexity O(µ d^2) for covariance updates and optimal memory complexity.
- Approach requires modifying the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism.
- Paper gives a clear introduction, is well-organized, and includes theoretical justification and empirical results.
- Main contribution labeled "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) naming and needs resolution.
- Omits discussion and empirical comparison with Suttorp (2009) Cholesky-CMA-ES.
- Omits algorithmic comparison with Krause et al. (2015).
- Novelty is questioned: essentially looping rankOneUpdate for all individuals (a single "for" loop).
- Experiments are incomplete—missing comparisons to relevant prior variants.
- Plots raise concerns (e.g., unexplained Cigar function plateau) and need clarification on median plotting.
- The "optimality" claim is overstated; not optimal for Sphere/separable functions and only ~2× cheaper than Suttorp’s method.
- Assertion that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable contradicts the original paper and is questionable.
- Does not fundamentally address large-scale CMA-ES since complexity remains quadratic despite savings.
- Recommendations: resolve naming conflict, add comprehensive discussion and empirical comparisons with prior work.
- Recommendations: clarify algorithmic contributions, provide more experimental details, fix plotting issues, and temper optimality claims.
