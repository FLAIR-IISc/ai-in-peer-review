This paper proposes a variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which uses a triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce the runtime complexity of the algorithm. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and demonstrate its performance through experiments on various benchmark functions.

The main contribution of the paper is the development of a quadratic-time covariance matrix update scheme with minimal memory requirements. The authors show that this approach can significantly reduce the runtime complexity of the CMA-ES, making it more efficient for large-scale optimization problems.

However, there are some concerns with the paper. Firstly, the naming of the variant as "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with the established name by Suttorp et al. (2009). This needs to be resolved to avoid confusion in the literature.

Secondly, the paper omits a discussion and empirical comparison with Suttorp et al. (2009) and algorithmic comparison with Krause et al. (2015). This is a significant oversight, as these papers are directly related to the proposed method. The authors should include a thorough comparison with these methods to demonstrate the advantages of their approach.

Thirdly, the incremental algorithmic contribution of the paper appears to be simply looping the rankOneUpdate for all individuals (one "for" loop). The authors should clarify the significance of this contribution and provide a more detailed explanation of the algorithm.

Fourthly, the experiments in the paper are incomplete. The authors should empirically compare their method to Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause's variant and update the figures accordingly.

Lastly, the paper makes an overstated "optimality" claim. The method is not optimal for Sphere or separable functions (where covariance/diagonal suffice) and is only approximately 2 times cheaper than Suttorp's method. The authors should be more accurate in their claims and provide a more nuanced discussion of the advantages and limitations of their approach.

In terms of quality, the paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, the paper could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the related work and a more detailed explanation of the algorithm.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, and the notation is clear. However, some of the sections could be better organized, and the authors could provide more intuitive explanations of the technical concepts.

In terms of originality, the paper proposes a novel approach to reducing the runtime complexity of the CMA-ES. However, the contribution is incremental, and the authors should provide a more detailed explanation of the significance of their approach.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses an important problem in the field of optimization. The CMA-ES is a widely used algorithm, and reducing its runtime complexity can have significant implications for large-scale optimization problems. However, the authors should provide a more nuanced discussion of the advantages and limitations of their approach and its potential impact on the field.

Overall, the paper is a good contribution to the field, but it could benefit from a more thorough discussion of the related work, a more detailed explanation of the algorithm, and a more nuanced discussion of the advantages and limitations of the approach.
