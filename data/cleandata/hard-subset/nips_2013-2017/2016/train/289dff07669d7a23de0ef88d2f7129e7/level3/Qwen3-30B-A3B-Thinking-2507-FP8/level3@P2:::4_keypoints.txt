- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factor, reducing covariance update complexity from O(d^3) to O(d^2).
- Provides Lemma 1 theoretical justification that the error from using Cholesky vanishes on convergence, so performance is maintained.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-CMA-ES attempts but crucially proves the algorithm’s behavior remains unchanged in standard CMA-ES.
- Empirical evaluation on benchmark functions (d=4–256) shows identical function-evaluation counts but much faster wall-clock times (≈20× at d=64).
- Algorithm and theory are clearly presented (Algorithm 1) with logically structured arguments.
- Adequately cites relevant prior work (Hansen & Ostermeier, Ros & Hansen, Suttorp et al.).
- Practical benefits: eigenvalues obtained for free and triangular Cholesky enables numerically stable O(d^2) matrix inversion.
- Demonstrates optimal storage complexity, useful for memory-constrained applications.
- Main strength: novel theoretical justification that Cholesky-based update maintains CMA-ES performance.
- Main weakness: claims of improved numerical stability are insufficiently supported—lacks concrete evidence or detailed analysis.
- Limited discussion about combining this approach with low-dimensional approximation methods for extremely high-dimensional problems.
- High significance for ML (hyperparameter tuning, RL) by removing a major computational bottleneck.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify and provide evidence for the numerical stability claim.
