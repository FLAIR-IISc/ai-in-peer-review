This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a widely used derivative-free optimization algorithm. The main contribution of the paper is the use of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square root in the standard CMA-ES, reducing the computation required for the matrix inverse. This modification, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, provides a significant reduction in runtime complexity while keeping the number of objective function evaluations essentially unchanged.

The paper provides a thorough background on the CMA-ES algorithm and its variants, highlighting the challenges associated with computing the matrix square root and its inverse. The authors then present their proposed modification, which replaces the matrix square root with the triangular Cholesky factor. They provide a theoretical justification for why this approach does not degrade the algorithmic performance, based on the properties of the Cholesky factorization and the behavior of the CMA-ES algorithm.

The paper also presents experimental results comparing the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants on a range of benchmark functions. The results show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves significant speedups over the standard CMA-ES, especially for high-dimensional problems. The authors also demonstrate that the Cholesky-CMA-ES retains the same evaluation count as the standard CMA-ES, but lowers the wall-clock time, with savings growing as dimensionality increases.

The paper is well-written and provides a clear explanation of the proposed modification and its theoretical justification. The experimental results are also well-presented and provide a thorough evaluation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a significant contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization by proposing a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm that reduces its runtime complexity.
* The theoretical justification for the proposed modification is thorough and well-explained.
* The experimental results are comprehensive and provide a thorough evaluation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES.

Weaknesses:

* The paper could benefit from a clearer explanation of why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable." While the authors provide some discussion on this topic, it is not entirely clear what they mean by "numerically more stable" and how the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves this.
* The paper could also benefit from a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, particularly in high-dimensional problems. While the authors mention that the Cholesky-CMA-ES scales quadratically with the problem dimensionality, they do not provide a thorough discussion of how this affects its performance.
