- Proposal: Modify CMA-ES by updating the covariance via its Cholesky factor to avoid explicit matrix square roots, aiming for lower memory and faster computation in high dimensions.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015) by extending Cholesky-based updates to standard CMA-ES.  
- Quality: Technically sound with reasonable theoretical convergence arguments, but empirical validation is incomplete and some claims are overstated.  
- Theoretical claims: Lemma and convergence discussion are relevant and mostly well-explained, though the “optimal” asymptotic complexity claim is exaggerated.  
- Numerical stability: The paper’s claim that Suttorp et al. is numerically unstable is unsupported and contradicts the original work.  
- Clarity: Well-written and organized; algorithm is clearly presented, but the link between eq. (6) and the approximation and the effect of coordinate rotations need clearer explanation.  
- Originality: Incremental contribution—systematic rank-one Cholesky updates and approximation analysis are useful, but core innovation (looping rankOneUpdate) is limited.  
- Significance: Moderate; could be practically useful if empirical speedups hold, but modest (~2x) gains and retained quadratic complexity limit impact on scalability.  
- Strengths: Clear presentation, sound theoretical justification, demonstrated speedups at higher dimensions, attention to numerical stability, and a useful correction on eigenvalue decomposition frequency.  
- Weaknesses — naming: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” is already used in prior work and the paper should adopt a different name.  
- Weaknesses — prior work comparison: Fails to empirically and adequately compare against Suttorp (2009) and Krause (2015), a major omission.  
- Weaknesses — incremental novelty: Main algorithmic change is essentially adding a for-loop over rankOneUpdate, which feels insufficient for a full paper.  
- Weaknesses — experiments: Experiments are incomplete and must include direct comparisons to prior Cholesky variants; plots need updating.  
- Weaknesses — plotting/analysis: The Cigar function plateau and median-plotting effects require clarification.  
- Weaknesses — overstated claims: Optimality and claimed speed/complexity benefits are overstated, especially for separable/Sphere cases.  
- Weaknesses — unsupported stability claim: Must provide evidence for any numerical instability claims or retract them.  
- Weaknesses — scalability: Quadratic complexity remains, so fundamental CMA-ES scalability issues are not solved.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form due to missing comparisons, incremental contribution, and overstated/questionable claims.  
- Required revisions: Resolve naming conflict; run comprehensive experiments including Suttorp and Krause variants; clarify that the main algorithmic change is the for-loop or strengthen its justification; substantiate or retract numerical stability claims; and moderate optimality claims.
