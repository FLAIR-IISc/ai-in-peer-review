- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor in CSA to reduce covariance update/storage from O(d^3) to O(d^2).  
- Claims identical optimization performance to standard CMA-ES while substantially reducing wall-clock time.  
- Extends prior Cholesky-based work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) to standard CMA-ES with rank-1 and rank-μ updates.  
- Theoretical justification (Lemma 1) argues replacing square root with Cholesky factor does not significantly alter behavior when covariance converges.  
- Empirical results show same number of function evaluations and large speedups (≈20x at d=64) as dimensionality increases.  
- Implementation details are clear and related work/limitations are acknowledged.  
- Main novelty is limited—extension of prior rank-1 results to rank-μ is a straightforward generalization.  
- Paper does not sufficiently distinguish its contribution from earlier Cholesky-based CMA-ES variants.  
- Theoretical claim relies on a covariance convergence assumption not fully established for standard CMA-ES; should cite Beyer (2014).  
- Minor technical errors/typos (σ should be \tilde{σ} in Eq.1; "which can by be motivated") reduce credibility.  
- Quality: solid experiments and practical gains, but theoretical grounding needs strengthening.  
- Significance: useful for intermediate-dimensional problems where full covariance is feasible but costly; less relevant for very high dimensions using low-rank approximations.  
- Recommendation: accept—practical improvement with convincing empirical results, conditional on fixing minor errors and better relating to prior work and theory.
