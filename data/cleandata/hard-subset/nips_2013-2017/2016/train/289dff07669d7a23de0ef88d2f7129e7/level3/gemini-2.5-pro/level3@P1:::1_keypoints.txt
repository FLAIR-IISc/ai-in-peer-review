- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain and update triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance update complexity from O(d^3) to O(μd^2) and halve memory.
- Replaces CSA's C^{-1/2} with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1} and provides Lemma 1 showing the approximation error vanishes as covariance converges.
- Extends prior Cholesky-update ideas to the full modern CMA-ES (including rank-μ) and resolves the known CSA obstacle.
- Theoretical derivation is sound and the approximation for CSA is convincingly justified.
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: convergence in function-evals matches standard CMA-ES while wall-clock time is substantially reduced (e.g., ~20× at d=64).
- Paper is exceptionally clear, with helpful pseudocode and well-presented experimental figures.
- High practical significance: a likely drop-in replacement for medium-to-large dimensional CMA-ES use cases.
- Minor request: confirm baseline CMA-ES-Ref used similarly optimized LAPACK routines to ensure fair wall-clock comparisons.
- Minor request: clarify why Cigar/Discus at d=256 show smaller speedups and how L2 cache effects differentially impact those benchmarks.
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — technically solid, practically important, likely widely adoptable.
