- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES using a triangular Cholesky factor for covariance updates to reduce time/space to O(µd²) versus the standard matrix square root.  
- Extends Krause & Igel (2015)’s Cholesky approach from (1+1)-CMA-ES to full CMA-ES with both rank-1 and rank-µ updates.  
- Argues CSA behavior is preserved when using the Cholesky factor and proves Lemma 1 that the introduced error vanishes as covariance converges.  
- Relies on a convergence assumption supported by Beyer (2014), but the paper could connect that theoretical foundation more explicitly.  
- Experiments on benchmark functions show similar function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES with much lower wall-clock time (up to ~20× faster at d=64).  
- Strengths: clear technical contribution, well-justified theory, thorough experiments, and practical implementation details (Algorithm 2).  
- Practical advantages include optimal storage complexity and free access to covariance eigenvalues.  
- Main weakness: limited novelty because the extension from prior work is straightforward and not strongly distinguished from Krause & Igel (2015) or Suttorp et al. (2009).  
- Theoretical justification for the convergence assumption (Lemma 1) should be strengthened with more explicit literature ties.  
- Minor technical errors: σ in denominator of Eq.(1) should be \tilde{σ}; typo “which can by be motivated” should be “which can be motivated.”  
- Paper quality: well-written, well-structured, and provides sufficient detail for reproducibility.  
- Experimental design: appropriate and comprehensive across multiple dimensions and problem types, clearly demonstrating computational benefits.  
- Significance: useful for practitioners facing high-dimensional covariance-update bottlenecks, but limited since it does not reduce function-evaluation counts.  
- Overall: technically sound and practically valuable improvement with marginal novelty; would benefit from stronger theoretical grounding and clearer delimitation from prior work.
