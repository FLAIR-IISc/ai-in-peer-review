- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: replaces covariance square root with a triangular Cholesky factor to reduce computation and memory.  
- Enables quadratic-time covariance updates and removes explicit eigenvalue computations.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the approximation preserves performance and provides convergence/error guarantees.  
- Empirical benchmarks demonstrate maintained optimization quality with large wall-clock speedups, e.g., up to 20x at dimension 64.  
- Particularly suitable for medium- to large-scale problems (e.g., neural network direct policy search) due to reduced overhead.  
- Significant contribution addressing a key CMA-ES bottleneck and improving scalability.  
- Strong theoretical rigor and clear exposition of methodology and results.  
- Comprehensive empirical validation on standard benchmark functions showing runtime gains.  
- Practical relevance from reduced memory footprint and computational complexity.  
- Unclear whether CPU/environment settings were directly comparable across CMA-ES variants, which could affect runtime claims.  
- Unexplained discrepancies in high-dimensional performance (Figure 2) need clarification.  
- Benchmarks are standard but lack additional real-world optimization tasks to strengthen validation.  
- Pro-acceptance: significant, theoretically sound, empirically validated advancement with clear practical impact.  
- Con-acceptance: minor issues (CPU comparability, high-dim discrepancies) and limited benchmark scope that should be addressed.  
- Recommendation: accept pending authors’ clarification of minor issues.
