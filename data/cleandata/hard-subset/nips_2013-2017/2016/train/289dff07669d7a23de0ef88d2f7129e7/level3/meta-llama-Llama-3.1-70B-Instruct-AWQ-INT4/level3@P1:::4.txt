This paper presents a novel approach to improving the efficiency of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a widely used optimization algorithm in machine learning. The main contribution of this work is the use of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the need for computing the square root of the covariance matrix, which is a major computational bottleneck in the standard CMA-ES algorithm.

The authors provide a clear and concise introduction to the CMA-ES algorithm and its applications, as well as a detailed explanation of the proposed modification. They also offer a theoretical justification for why the Cholesky-based approach does not degrade the algorithm's performance, which is a crucial aspect of the paper.

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, showing that it reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm while keeping the number of objective function evaluations essentially unchanged. The paper also presents a valuable implementation of Cholesky factorization for CMA-ES, which can be useful for practitioners.

One potential area for improvement is in providing a clearer theoretical explanation of why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable." While the authors mention that the Cholesky factorization is more stable, they do not provide a detailed analysis of the numerical properties of the algorithm.

Another suggestion for future work is to explore the combination of the proposed method with low-dimensional approximation techniques, such as those proposed by Loshchilov (2015). This could potentially allow the algorithm to handle high-dimensional problems more efficiently, such as neural network training.

Overall, the paper is well-written and presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization. The authors have demonstrated the effectiveness of their approach through thorough experimentation, and the paper is a valuable addition to the literature on CMA-ES.
