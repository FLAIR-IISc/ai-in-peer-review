- Proposes "Cholesky-CMA-ES" that updates the Cholesky factor directly to reduce covariance update time and memory.
- Builds on prior work by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015), avoiding explicit matrix square roots.
- Claims quadratic-time complexity with minimal memory and no loss in objective-evaluation performance.
- Paper is well-motivated and addresses a known CMA-ES bottleneck in high dimensions.
- Theoretical justification (Lemma 1) about coordinate system rotations is a useful contribution.
- Experiments show significant wall-clock speedups, especially at higher dimensionalities, on standard benchmark functions.
- Naming "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with established nomenclature from Suttorp et al. (2009) and must be resolved.
- Lacks direct empirical comparison with Suttorp (2009) and Krause et al. (2015); a head-to-head comparison is essential.
- Algorithmic contribution appears incremental (essentially looping rankOneUpdate) and should be explicitly acknowledged or better justified.
- Claim of "optimality" is overstated; not optimal when diagonal or low-rank approximations suffice.
- Reported ~2x speedup over Suttorp’s method needs further contextualization and validation.
- Asserting Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable contradicts the original paper and requires strong evidence.
- Scalability remains quadratic; the method mitigates but does not solve large-scale CMA-ES issues where full covariance is infeasible.
- Cigar function plots require clarification because plateau differences may be plotting artifacts.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a key bottleneck, provides theoretical justification, and demonstrates practical speedup.
- Arguments against acceptance: misleading naming, missing crucial comparisons, incremental contribution, and overstated claims.
- Recommendation: Major revision—must fix naming, add comparative experiments, and clarify the novelty and claims.
