- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the Cholesky factor directly to avoid explicit matrix square roots, reducing computation and memory.  
- The authors provide theoretical justification claiming the modification does not alter fundamental algorithm behavior.  
- Empirical results show significant runtime reduction on standard benchmarks, especially at higher dimensionalities.  
- Analysis linking coordinate system rotation to acceptable approximation error is a useful contribution.  
- The chosen name “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and risks confusion; renaming is recommended.  
- The paper lacks direct empirical comparisons to prior Cholesky-based methods (Suttorp 2009; Krause & Igel 2015), limiting evaluation.  
- The algorithmic change appears incremental (iterating a rank-one update in a loop) and needs clearer justification of novelty.  
- Experimental presentation should be improved (expand/move figures to supplementary) and clarify median-visualization issues that may hide behaviors like the Cigar-function plateau.  
- The claim of “optimal” complexity is overstated — not optimal when simpler covariance structures suffice and only ~2x speedup over Suttorp’s method is shown.  
- The statement that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable contradicts the original paper and requires supporting evidence or a nuanced discussion.  
- While runtime and memory are reduced, the method still has quadratic complexity and does not solve scalability for extremely high-dimensional problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical runtime improvement, clear theoretical support, and benchmark performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: misleading naming, missing direct comparisons, incremental contribution, overstated claims, and potential inaccuracies about prior methods’ stability.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept conditional on renaming the method, adding thorough comparisons to Suttorp/Krause, and clarifying the novelty and claims.
