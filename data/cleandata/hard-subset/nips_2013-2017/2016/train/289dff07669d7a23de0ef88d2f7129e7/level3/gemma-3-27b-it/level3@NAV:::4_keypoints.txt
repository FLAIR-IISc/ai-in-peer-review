- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: use triangular Cholesky factorization to avoid costly matrix square roots in covariance updates.  
- Claims maintained algorithmic performance of standard CMA-ES while reducing runtime, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Provides theoretical argument that smoothness of the Cholesky factor causes minimal behavioral impact.  
- Builds on prior cost-reduction work (delayed updates, low-rank approximations) and extends Cholesky approaches to full standard CMA-ES.  
- Highlights advantages: reduced computational complexity, lower memory footprint, and preserved optimization performance.  
- Empirical validation shows comparable function evaluations and substantial runtime speedups that increase with dimensionality (Figures 1 & 2).  
- Implementation is a valuable contribution to the CMA-ES toolkit.  
- Theoretical discussion of “numerical stability” via Cholesky smoothness is promising but underdeveloped and needs more detail, especially for ill-conditioned problems.  
- Method still exhibits quadratic scaling with dimensionality, limiting applicability to extremely high-dimensional tasks.  
- Recommends exploring combinations with low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) for very high-dimensional problems as future work.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant runtime gains without performance loss, solid theoretical grounding, and strong experiments.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient detail on numerical stability and limited scalability to very large dimensions.
