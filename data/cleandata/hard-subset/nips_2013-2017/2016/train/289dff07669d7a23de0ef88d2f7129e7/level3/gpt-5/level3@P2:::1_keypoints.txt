- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to achieve O(µ d^2) per-iteration updates with minimal memory while preserving function-evaluation efficiency.  
- Replaces CSA's C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} and justifies this by an RQ-decomposition argument showing rotation errors vanish as covariance shape stabilizes.  
- Extends prior work (diagonal/LM variants, delayed decompositions, prior Cholesky updates) to the standard full rank-µ CMA-ES with a theoretical rationale.  
- Empirically matches reference CMA-ES in evaluation counts up to d = 256 and yields substantial wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d = 64), with larger benefits at higher d.  
- Technical soundness: rank-1/µ Cholesky updates align with literature and the CSA approximation is plausible via Lemma 1, but only asymptotic, not finite-time, guarantees are given.  
- Suggestion: add a finite-time bound on CSA error (e.g., in terms of rotation drift ||E_t E_{t-1}^T − I|| and CSA horizon).  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough: multiple functions (incl. rotated), dimensions, 100 trials, and both function-evaluation and wall-clock metrics with reasonable baselines.  
- Reproducibility: pseudo-code and implementation provided, but clarify whether baselines used identical code paths, compiler flags, BLAS/LAPACK backends, and threading for fair runtime comparisons.  
- Clarity: paper is well written and organized; motivation and CSA implications are explained clearly.  
- Correction needed: the claim that diag(A) are square roots of eigenvalues is incorrect—clarify what quantities are used to monitor conditioning and enforce variance floors.  
- Fig. 2: reduced speedup at d = 256 for Cigar/Discus is attributed to L2 cache effects—request quantification (memory-bandwidth profiling) and explanation for why this is more pronounced for those functions.  
- Originality: while Cholesky-factor approaches exist, applying them to full rank-µ CMA-ES with a CSA-compatible theoretical argument and practical O(µ d^2) update is a novel, substantive advance.  
- Significance: impactful for practitioners needing full covariance adaptation—same optimization performance with much lower runtime/memory; relevant to ML applications (RL, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Robustness question: how does the CSA approximation behave during transient phases with rapidly changing covariance (e.g., early Rosenbrock) and under noise—request phase-wise breakdown and noise experiments.  
- Numerical stability question: do you refactorize from scratch to control round-off error, how often, and what diagonal-jitter strategy ensures SPD?  
- Runtime methodology question: provide hardware details, compiler flags, BLAS vendor/threads, and confirm identical sampling/evaluation pipelines and memory layouts; note if Suttorp-style factorization has different memory traffic.  
- Baselines suggestion: discuss when LM-CMA (Loshchilov) is preferable for very large d to help practitioner choices.  
- Pros: principled algorithmic change with strong practical upside, theoretical justification for CSA approximation, comprehensive experiments, and accessible implementation details.  
- Cons: inaccurate eigenvalue claim, lack of finite-time CSA error bound, insufficiently documented runtime-comparison methodology, and limited discussion of noise/transient robustness.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practically important contribution recommended for acceptance pending minor clarifications and corrections.
