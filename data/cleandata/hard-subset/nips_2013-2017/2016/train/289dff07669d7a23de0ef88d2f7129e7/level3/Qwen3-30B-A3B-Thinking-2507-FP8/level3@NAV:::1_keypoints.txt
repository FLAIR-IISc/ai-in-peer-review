- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square-root with triangular Cholesky factor updates to remove computational bottleneck.
- Theoretically justifies replacing C⁻¹/²ₜ with A⁻¹ₜ in CSA, with Lemma 1 showing the error vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Achieves optimal O(μ d²) time complexity for covariance updates without degrading algorithmic performance.
- Empirically does not increase function evaluations compared to standard CMA-ES across benchmarks.
- Delivers substantial wall-clock speedups, e.g., ~20× faster at d=64 and evaluated up to d=256.
- Provides covariance eigenvalues “for free,” a useful practical benefit.
- Builds on prior work (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) but offers a novel, theoretically grounded solution.
- Paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated on standard benchmark functions and multiple dimensions.
- Strong originality and relevance to ML, reinforcement learning, and information geometry communities.
- Minor concern: clarify CPU implementation details to ensure fair runtime comparisons (same language/implementation).
- Minor concern: explain limited performance differences on Cigar/Discus at d=256, given sensitivity to covariance adaptation.
- Overall recommendation: strong contribution, should be accepted and considered a default for medium/large-scale applications.
