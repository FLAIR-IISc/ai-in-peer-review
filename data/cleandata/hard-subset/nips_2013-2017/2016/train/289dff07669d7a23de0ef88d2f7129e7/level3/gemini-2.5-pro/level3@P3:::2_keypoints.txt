- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing O(d³) eigen-decomposition with O(µ d²) direct updates of the Cholesky factor.
- Replaces C⁻¹ᐟ² with A⁻¹ in CSA, claiming introduced rotational error is negligible and vanishes on convergence.
- Empirically achieves significant wall-clock speedups over standard CMA-ES without increasing function evaluations.
- Situates work within scalability efforts for CMA-ES; direct matrix-factor updates have prior art (Suttorp et al. 2009).
- Technique appears closely related to Krause & Igel (2015), extending rank-one updates to rank-µ, raising novelty concerns.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and has a strong practical motivation.
- Implementation is sound and experiments are compelling, suggesting practical impact if theoretical issues are resolved.
- Lacks a proof that sequential rank-one updates of A are equivalent to the Cholesky factor of the updated covariance C_{t+1}.
- Complexity claim O(µ d²) is misleading without Θ-notation and clarification versus amortized O(d²) delayed-update strategies.
- The claim that the CSA path error is negligible is unvalidated; Lemma 1 assumes shape convergence and needs empirical verification (e.g., error-over-time plots).
- Contains minor notation and typo errors (Algorithm 2 wk→α_k; Table 1 header n→d; line 185 F→f).
- Recommendation: reject due to limited originality and insufficient rigorous validation.
- Suggested fixes: clarify novelty relative to Krause & Igel, provide formal equivalence proof, empirically validate CSA approximation error, and refine complexity analysis.
