- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance eigen-decomposition with Cholesky to reduce computational cost.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and easy to follow.
- Authors provide a theoretical convergence justification for the new algorithm.
- Use of Cholesky is reasonable but not novel—similar ideas exist in prior work.
- Major missing element: no rigorous equivalence proof between the proposed algorithm and original CMA-ES (formula 2).
- Complexity claim is unclear: they state O(μ d^2) which matches CMA-ES-Ref; should use Θ-notation and justify actual speedup.
- Claim that path error is small and decreases over time lacks experimental validation over time (only final-performance comparisons shown).
- Minor notation and typo issues (e.g., wk should be α_k, n in Table 1 should be d, 'F' should be 'f').
- Overall recommendation: not acceptable in current form due to limited originality, missing equivalence proof, unclear complexity argument, and unvalidated path-error claim.
- Arguments for acceptance: potential computational savings, clear explanation, and reasonable theoretical motivation for using Cholesky.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, lack of equivalence proof, unclear/unsupported complexity claims, insufficient experimental validation, and minor editorial errors.
