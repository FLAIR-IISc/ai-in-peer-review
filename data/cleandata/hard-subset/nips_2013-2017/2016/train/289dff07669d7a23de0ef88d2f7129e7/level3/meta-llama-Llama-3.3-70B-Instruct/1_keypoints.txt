- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: updates covariance via triangular Cholesky factors to reduce time and memory for large-scale optimization.  
- Provides theoretical justification for convergence of the new algorithm.  
- Empirical results show same number of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES but much lower wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Claims asymptotically optimal time complexity and optimal memory complexity for the method.  
- Paper is well-written with clear background, motivation, and detailed algorithm description.  
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation and thorough discussion of related work and advantages over other methods.  
- Minor weakness: implementation details are under-specified (how Cholesky factors and their inverses are updated/computed).  
- Minor weakness: some experimental results (e.g., sudden wall-clock time increase for CMA-ES-Ref in Fig. 2) need clearer explanation.  
- Could add more discussion on potential limitations and challenges of the proposed algorithm.  
- Overall assessment: significant contribution likely to become a default method for medium/large-scale applications.
