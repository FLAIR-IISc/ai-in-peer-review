- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces O(d^3) covariance/square-root ops with O(μ d^2) by maintaining a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A and using A^{-1} in CSA.
- Main algorithmic change: CSA uses A^{-1} instead of C^{-1/2}, avoiding computation/tracking of the rotation E_t and costly decompositions.
- Asymptotic justification: under convergence of normalized covariance shape, the rotation E_t → I and the approximation becomes equivalent to standard CMA-ES.
- Builds on prior decomposition-updating work (e.g., Suttorp et al. 2009, Krause & Igel 2015) and complements delayed/diagonal/LM complexity reductions while keeping full covariance.
- Empirical results: substantial wall-clock speedups with similar numbers of function evaluations, scaling sensibly with dimension.
- Strength — clear algorithmic contribution: O(μ d^2)-time, O(d^2)-space CMA-ES with CSA designed to preserve behavior without cubic operations.
- Strength — solid engineering: numerically stable triangular updates, O(d^2) inversion of A, and eigenvalue info available from diag(A).
- Strength — theoretical insight: plausible argument that omitted rotations vanish under covariance-shape convergence.
- Strength — strong empirical evidence across synthetic benchmarks showing consistent speedups without loss in optimization performance.
- Strength — practical utility for problems with cheap evaluations and moderate-to-large dimensionality (e.g., controller tuning, ML inner loops).
- Weakness — theory relies on restrictive convergence/continuity assumptions and lacks finite-time bounds for CSA error from omitting E_t.
- Weakness — limited practical impact when objective evaluations dominate cost or when typical problem dimensionality is modest.
- Weakness — baselines incomplete: missing a full “no-delay” CMA-ES baseline and finer wall-clock accounting for decomposition vs sampling/selection.
- Weakness — CSA modification breaks precise normalization; invariance claims are only “in practice” and need diagnostics of rotation error.
- Weakness — limited real-world validation; experiments confined to synthetic benchmarks without demonstrations where speedup changes feasibility.
- Quality — method is technically sound and well-specified; asymptotic equivalence plausible but finite-time guarantees are lacking.
- Clarity — paper is well written; explanation of C^{1/2}=A E and rationale for A^{-1} substitution is clear, with explicit algorithmic details.
- Clarity suggestion — explicitly report norm of p_sigma error from omitting E_t and clarify the exact delay policy used in reference baseline.
- Originality — novel extension of Cholesky rank-μ updates to full CMA-ES with a principled CSA workaround and complexity improvement without delaying updates.
- Significance — meaningful advance for users where internal linear algebra is a bottleneck (cheap evals, medium-to-large d), but broader impact limited by typical use cases.
- Suggested experiment — track E_t explicitly (e.g., ‖E_t − I‖_F, principal angles) by running both algorithms with shared randomness to quantify CSA error.
- Suggested experiment — provide end-to-end wall-clock breakdown (sampling, ranking, cov update, decomposition) including a no-delay CMA-ES baseline.
- Suggested experiment — test quadratics with varied spectra to study solution quality, condition numbers, and E_t error across spectral distributions.
- Suggested experiment — demonstrate a real-world use case where faster internal updates enable tighter time budgets (e.g., real-time robotics or inner-loop hyperparameter tuning).
- Suggested experiment — robustness checks on noisy/non-stationary objectives and very high dimensions to assess numerical stability and conditioning safeguards.
- Pros for acceptance — well-motivated algorithmic improvement with clear complexity gains, careful implementation, and strong empirical speedups.
- Cons for acceptance — theoretical justification is asymptotic without finite-time guarantees, limited real-world validation, and incomplete baseline/diagnostic coverage.
- Overall — solid, practical contribution that improves scalability of full-covariance CMA-ES; leans toward acceptance if diagnostics of E_t, fuller baselines, and a real-world demo are added.
