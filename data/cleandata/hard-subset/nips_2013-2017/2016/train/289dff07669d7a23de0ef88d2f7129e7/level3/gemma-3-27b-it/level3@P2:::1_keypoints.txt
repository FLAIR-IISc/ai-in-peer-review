- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: update covariance via Cholesky factor to improve computational efficiency in high dimensions.  
- Core idea: replace covariance updates/decompositions with Cholesky-based updates, yielding quadratic time and reduced memory.  
- Modifies CSA by using the inverse Cholesky factor instead of the inverse square root of the covariance.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing the approximation error vanishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal time complexity O(µ d²) and optimal storage complexity.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based attempts but adapts them rigorously to the standard CMA-ES.  
- Empirical results: substantial speedups in high dimensions across diverse benchmark functions.  
- Additional benefit: can obtain covariance eigenvalues without extra computation.  
- Strength: technically sound proofs and strong argument about coordinate rotation effects.  
- Strength: original and significant advance likely to impact large-scale CMA-ES usage.  
- Strength: clear, well-organized presentation with thorough comparisons to other CMA-ES variants.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation detail (language, compiler optimizations, library use) for fair CPU/time comparisons.  
- Weakness: Figure 2 lacks discussion on why Cigar and Discus show smaller differences at d=256.  
- Weakness: brief mention of polar decomposition cost but no discussion of alternative orthogonal-matrix update methods.  
- For acceptance: strong empirical and theoretical contributions, practical bottleneck addressed, high impact potential.  
- Against acceptance: minor omissions on implementation specifics and limited explanation of particular experimental anomalies.  
- Overall: high-quality paper with substantial improvements and strong recommendation to accept.
