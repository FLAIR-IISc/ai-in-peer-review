Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that uses triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the matrix square root to improve computational efficiency in high dimensions.

Modifies the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism and justifies it under an assumption of covariance convergence (Lemma 1).

Empirical results show comparable function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES but substantially reduced runtime, especially as dimensionality increases.

Extends prior work (Krause & Igel 2015) by applying Cholesky updates to the full CMA-ES framework including rank-1 and rank-µ updates.

Strengths include improved scalability, lower runtime complexity, optimal asymptotic storage, numerically stable inverse computation, and free access to eigenvalues.

Theoretical justification adds credibility but crucially relies on the covariance convergence assumption.

Experimental validation supports the runtime and performance claims.

Novelty is somewhat incremental — the extension from prior Cholesky updates appears a fairly direct generalization.

The paper relies on covariance convergence with limited supporting analysis beyond citing Beyer (2014).

Minor technical errors: σ in Eq.(1) likely should be \tilde{σ}; a grammatical mistake on line 94.

Arguments for acceptance: addresses practical CMA-ES limitations, provides theory-backed modification, demonstrates empirical runtime gains, and delivers desirable algorithm properties.

Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution, reliance on an under-justified assumption, and minor technical/grammatical issues.

Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix typos and strengthen discussion of the covariance convergence assumption and method limitations.
