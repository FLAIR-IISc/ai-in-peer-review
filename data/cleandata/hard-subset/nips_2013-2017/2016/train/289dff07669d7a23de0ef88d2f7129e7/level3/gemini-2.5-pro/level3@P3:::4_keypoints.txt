- Addresses CMA-ES's O(d^3) covariance decomposition bottleneck by proposing Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a triangular Cholesky factor, reducing per-iteration complexity to O(µ d^2).
- Replaces the CSA requirement of C^{-1/2} with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1}, and Lemma 1 argues the approximation error vanishes as covariance converges.
- Builds on prior complexity-reduction work but provides a more direct and efficient implementation for standard CMA-ES with a rationale for performance equivalence.
- Experiments show significant wall-clock time reduction without degrading convergence in function evaluations, with advantages increasing with dimensionality.
- The contribution is significant and practical, likely to be adopted as a default implementation for moderately high-dimensional problems.
- Technically sound: rank-one Cholesky updates are appropriate and the theoretical argument distinguishes this work from earlier attempts.
- Empirical validation is thorough, comparing against reference and delayed-update variants and confirming claimed speedups.
- Paper is clear, well-organized, and easy to follow.
- Requests clarification on the claim of improved numerical stability—whether it stems from avoiding explicit C formation or from better conditioning of triangular inversion.
- Questions scalability to very high dimensions (d >> 1000) and suggests discussing combinations with low-rank or limited-memory approximations.
- Overall assessment: excellent, original, significant contribution with only minor clarifications needed; suitable for NIPS.
