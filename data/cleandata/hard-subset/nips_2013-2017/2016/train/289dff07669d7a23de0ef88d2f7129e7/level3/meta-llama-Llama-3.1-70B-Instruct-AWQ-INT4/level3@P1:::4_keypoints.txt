- Main contribution: use triangular Cholesky factorization to remove the need for computing the covariance matrix square root in CMA-ES.  
- Computational benefit: method reduces runtime complexity while keeping objective function evaluations essentially unchanged.  
- Theoretical claim: authors provide justification that the Cholesky-based approach does not degrade CMA-ES performance.  
- Implementation: paper supplies a practical Cholesky factorization implementation for CMA-ES useful to practitioners.  
- Experiments: thorough experimental results demonstrating effectiveness and runtime improvements.  
- Clarity: well-written, clear introduction to CMA-ES and concise explanation of the proposed modification.  
- Originality: novel application of triangular Cholesky factorization to avoid covariance square-root computation.  
- Significance: approach has potential to improve CMA-ES efficiency across many applications.  
- Weakness: lacks a detailed theoretical/numerical analysis explaining why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable."  
- Weakness: does not explore combining the method with low-dimensional approximation techniques for high-dimensional problems.  
- Suggested future work: investigate integration with low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to better handle high-dimensional tasks like neural network training.  
- Acceptance argument: novel, effective, well-evaluated contribution with practical implementation and clear exposition.  
- Rejection argument: insufficient theoretical analysis of numerical stability and limited exploration of scalability extensions.
