- Proposes updating the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES to reduce runtime and memory complexity.  
- Introduces a specific adjustment to the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) to accommodate the Cholesky update.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) based on convergence of the Cholesky factor.  
- Empirical results show a significant runtime speedup without major performance loss.  
- Extends rank-1 Cholesky-update ideas from Krause and Igel (2015) to the full, standard CMA-ES.  
- Motivation is clear: covariance updates are a computational bottleneck in high dimensions.  
- Paper is well-written and easy to follow.  
- Core contribution is incremental—more an engineering/generalization than a novel algorithmic breakthrough.  
- Theoretical support is weak, relying on a covariance convergence assumption without stronger references (e.g., Beyer 2014).  
- Experimental evaluation is limited and could explore more parameter settings and problem types.  
- Practical improvement may benefit practitioners, but novelty may not meet top-conference standards.  
- Recommendation: reject for lack of substantial novelty; better suited to a specialized evolutionary computation venue.  
- Minor issues: σ in Eq. (1) and footnote 2 should be \tilde{σ}; remove extra "by" in line 94.
