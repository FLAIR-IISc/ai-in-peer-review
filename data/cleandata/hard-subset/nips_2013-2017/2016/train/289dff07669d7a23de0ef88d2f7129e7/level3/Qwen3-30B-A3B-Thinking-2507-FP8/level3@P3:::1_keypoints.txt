- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with triangular Cholesky updates, reducing time and space complexity for covariance adaptation from O(d³) to O(d²).
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing the error from replacing C⁻¹/²ₜ with A⁻¹ₜ vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Extends prior low-complexity CMA-ES efforts by delivering the first theoretically justified modification for the standard CMA-ES with CSA.
- Thorough empirical evaluation across multiple benchmark functions (Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) and dimensions (4–256).
- Empirical results show identical function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES while achieving substantial wall-clock speedups (≈20× at d=64, increasing with dimension).
- Algorithm yields covariance eigenvalues for free via the Cholesky diagonal, useful for monitoring.
- Paper is well-written, with clear pseudocode and straightforward implementation guidance.
- Demonstrates immediate practical significance for ML tasks (RL policy search, hyperparameter tuning) and recommends replacing standard CMA-ES for medium/large-scale problems.
- Review rates the work highly on quality (solid theory, comprehensive experiments), clarity, originality, and significance.
- Minor concern: CPU comparison implementation details (language, compiler flags, hardware) need clarification.
- Minor concern: Cigar and Discus show modest differences at d=256; a brief explanation (cache effects) should be expanded.
- Argument for acceptance: significant, practical improvement with strong theoretical and empirical support.
- Argument against acceptance: speedups are less pronounced for small dimensions, though this aligns with the paper’s medium/large-scale focus.
