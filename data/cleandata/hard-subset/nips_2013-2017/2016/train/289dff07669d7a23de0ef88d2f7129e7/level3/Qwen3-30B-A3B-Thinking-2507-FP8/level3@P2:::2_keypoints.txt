- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce computational cost.
- Claims O(μd²) covariance update time while maintaining performance comparable to standard CMA-ES.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and implementation is available in the Shark library, aiding reproducibility.
- Empirical results show promising wall-clock speedups (up to ~20× for d=64) without increasing function evaluations.
- Correctly identifies the need to reduce memory and time costs for high-dimensional optimization.
- Limited originality: core idea closely extends Krause & Igel (2015) and the paper fails to sufficiently distinguish its contribution.
- Lacks theoretical justification: no proof that the modification is equivalent to standard CMA-ES (critical claim).
- Unclear complexity claims: authors state O(μd²) but do not use Θ-notation or compare rigorously to delayed-square-root CMA-ES variants.
- Unvalidated path-error claim: Lemma 1’s claim that the A⁻¹ vs C⁻¹/² error decreases over time is not supported by experiments.
- Experimental validation focuses on final performance and wall-clock time, not on the claimed algorithmic complexity or intermediate error behavior.
- Notational inconsistencies (e.g., wk vs α_k, n vs d, F vs f) reduce clarity and need correction.
- Overall quality: numerically plausible but undermined by missing proofs and limited experimental evidence for key claims.
- Originality/significance: useful practical speedups but insufficient novelty and theoretical support for NIPS acceptance.
- Acceptance arguments: clear presentation, reproducible implementation, and promising empirical speedups.
- Rejection arguments: insufficient novelty, missing equivalence proof, unclear complexity analysis, unvalidated theoretical claims, and notational errors.
