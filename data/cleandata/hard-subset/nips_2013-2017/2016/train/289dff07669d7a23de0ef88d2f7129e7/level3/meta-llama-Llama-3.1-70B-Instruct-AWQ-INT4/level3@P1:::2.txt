This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES. The main idea is to replace the eigen-decomposition of the covariance matrix with a Cholesky decomposition, which can be computed more efficiently. The authors claim that this modification does not change the behavior of the CMA-ES in terms of required objective function evaluations, but reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm.

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the CMA-ES algorithm and the proposed modification. The use of Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition is a reasonable approach to reduce the computational complexity of the algorithm. The authors also provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm.

However, the originality of the paper is limited. The idea of using Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition is not new and has been proposed before in the context of evolution strategies. The authors should have provided a more detailed comparison with existing methods and highlighted the novelty of their approach.

One of the major concerns is the lack of an equivalence proof between the proposed algorithm and the original CMA-ES formula (2). The authors should provide a rigorous proof to show that the two algorithms are equivalent, even if it has been proven previously.

The complexity claim made by the authors is also unclear. They claim that the runtime complexity of the algorithm is O(μ d^2), but this is the same as the CMA-ES-Ref algorithm. The authors should use Θ-notation and explain in detail why their method is actually faster.

Another concern is the claim that the path error is small and decreases over time. This claim requires experimental validation over time, not just a final-performance comparison. The authors should provide more experimental results to support this claim.

There are also some minor issues with notation and typos. For example, wk in Algorithm 2 should be α_k, and n in Table 1 should be d. Additionally, 'F' should be 'f' in line 185.

Overall, while the paper is well-written and the idea is reasonable, it is not acceptable for NIPS in its current form due to the limited originality, lack of equivalence proof, unclear complexity claim, and unvalidated path-error claim.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm that can potentially reduce the computational complexity.
* The authors provide a clear explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical justification.
* The use of Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition is a reasonable approach to reduce the computational complexity of the algorithm.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The originality of the paper is limited, and the idea of using Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition is not new.
* The lack of an equivalence proof between the proposed algorithm and the original CMA-ES formula (2) is a major concern.
* The complexity claim made by the authors is unclear, and they should use Θ-notation and explain in detail why their method is actually faster.
* The claim that the path error is small and decreases over time requires experimental validation over time, not just a final-performance comparison.
