

This paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy that replaces the matrix square root operation with a triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the need for square root computations. The authors demonstrate that this modification achieves optimal asymptotic runtime and storage complexity (O(μd²)) while maintaining the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES. The work builds upon previous attempts to optimize CMA-ES (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause and Igel, 2015) but provides a theoretically justified approach that avoids the numerical instability issues of prior methods.

The paper's main strengths include a clear theoretical justification for why the Cholesky-based approach does not degrade algorithmic performance (supported by Lemma 1), thorough empirical validation across multiple benchmark functions and dimensions, and a well-structured algorithmic description (Algorithm 1). The experimental results convincingly demonstrate significant runtime improvements (up to 20x faster at d=64) with no loss in optimization performance, and the speed-up grows with dimensionality, making it particularly valuable for high-dimensional problems.

However, the paper would benefit from a more detailed theoretical explanation of why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable" as requested by the reviewer. The current justification relies on asymptotic convergence arguments, but a more concrete numerical analysis of stability properties would strengthen the contribution. Additionally, while the authors suggest combining their method with low-dimensional approximations (Loshchilov, 2015), this remains a superficial suggestion without concrete analysis or implementation.

The paper excels in quality, with sound theoretical foundation and comprehensive empirical evaluation. It is clearly written and makes a significant contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The originality is strong as the authors provide a theoretically grounded implementation of Cholesky factorization for standard CMA-ES, distinct from prior work. The significance is high, as the method directly addresses a computational bottleneck in a widely used optimization algorithm with practical benefits for machine learning applications.

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the numerical stability explanation. The paper represents a valuable technical contribution that advances the state of the art in CMA-ES implementation.
