- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance update cost from O(d³) to O(μd²).  
- Main theoretical insight: replacing the matrix square root in CSA with the Cholesky factor introduces a rotation error E_t that is shown to converge to identity under covariance convergence (Lemma 1).  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluation performance while delivering substantial runtime speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64; up to 20× faster reported).  
- Compares against two baselines (CMA-ES-Ref delayed updates and Suttorp-CMA-ES non-triangular Cholesky) with thorough benchmark evaluation across d=4–256 and 100 trials.  
- Technical contribution is sound and O(μd²) update complexity is asymptotically optimal for full covariance adaptation.  
- Implementation details and algorithms are clearly presented and reproducible (Algorithms 1–2).  
- Theoretical justification relies on restrictive convergence assumptions (C_t → C̄ with det C̄ = 1), limiting generality for non-convergent or rapidly changing landscapes.  
- No formal error bounds provided for the E_t approximation, weakening the theoretical claim for practical nonideal runs.  
- Missing experiments: E_t tracking to quantify rotation error, full CMA-ES runtime baseline without delayed updates, and a real-world application demonstrating tangible speedup.  
- Clarity is generally good, but the role of the orthogonal matrix E_t in CSA needs a more intuitive explanation for non-expert readers.  
- Runtime plots use logarithmic scales that highlight scaling but may obscure absolute time differences for small dimensions.  
- Originality is incremental: builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work but fills a key gap by theoretically addressing the E_t rotation, making it applicable to standard CMA-ES.  
- Significance is high for high-dimensional optimization where covariance updates dominate (d > ~64) but limited for typical ML scenarios where objective evaluation costs dominate (d < ~50).  
- Strengths: addresses a clear computational bottleneck, provides principled justification, retains optimization performance, and achieves optimal storage complexity with easy eigenvalue extraction.  
- Weaknesses: restrictive theoretical assumptions, potentially overstated broader impact, missing key validation experiments, and the O(μd²) factor (μ = O(log d)) may matter for moderate dimensions.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on adding suggested experiments (particularly E_t tracking and real-world validation).
