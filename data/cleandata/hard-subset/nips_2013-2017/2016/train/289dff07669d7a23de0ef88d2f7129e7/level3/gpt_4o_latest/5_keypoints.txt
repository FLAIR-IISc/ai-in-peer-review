- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor, reducing covariance update complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2), suited for high-dimensional optimization.  
- The authors provide theoretical justification that omitting the random orthogonal matrix E_t introduces negligible error asymptotically and analyze convergence.  
- Empirical benchmarks show substantial speed-ups with comparable optimization performance to state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants.  

Strengths:
- Significant runtime and memory reduction while preserving CMA-ES performance, valuable for high-dimensional problems.  
- Rigorous theoretical analysis supporting convergence and the diminishing impact of E_t.  
- Comprehensive empirical validation across multiple functions and dimensionalities demonstrating runtime advantages.  
- Practical implications for scenarios with fast objective evaluations (e.g., policy search in RL).  

Weaknesses:
- Benefits are most relevant in niche scenarios where internal algorithm cost dominates objective evaluation time.  
- Missing empirical analyses such as tracking E_t evolution and runtime comparisons without delay strategies.  
- No real-world experiments (e.g., robotics or hyperparameter tuning), limiting practical applicability.  
- Theoretical sections are dense and may be hard to follow for readers unfamiliar with CMA-ES or Cholesky methods.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Tackles a recognized CMA-ES bottleneck with strong theoretical and empirical support.  
- Advances the state-of-the-art in derivative-free optimization for high dimensions.  
- Novel and meaningful improvement over prior work.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Practical impact may be limited in common scenarios where objective evaluations dominate.  
- Absence of real-world experiments reduces practitioner relevance.  
- Some theoretical and empirical aspects need further clarification and expansion.  

Recommendation:
- Recommend acceptance, encouraging the authors to address empirical gaps and add real-world experiments in future work.
