- Proposes modifying CMA-ES by using a triangular Cholesky factorization to remove the matrix square root and reduce inverse computation.
- Provides theoretical justification that the Cholesky-based approach does not degrade algorithm performance.
- Describes the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm in detail, including time and memory complexity.
- Experimental results show significant runtime speedup while keeping the same number of objective evaluations as standard CMA-ES.
- Compares performance with other CMA-ES variants, including the non-triangular Suttorp-CMA-ES.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and easy to follow.
- Introduction gives thorough background and strong motivation for the modification.
- Detailed analysis of method properties and advantages over other approaches.
- Strengths: clear presentation, solid experiments, and technically sound contribution.
- Weakness: explanation of why the method is "numerically more stable" is not sufficiently clear.
- Weakness: lacks exploration or preliminary results on combining with low-dimensional approximation methods (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution to derivative-free optimization with high quality, clarity, originality (somewhat incremental), and significance; scores: Quality 9/10, Clarity 9/10, Originality 8/10, Significance 9/10.
