- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix.  
- Proves convergence under certain assumptions and analyzes algorithm properties.  
- Main contribution: numerically stable quadratic-time covariance update with minimal memory.  
- Contribution significance reduced by similarity to Krause et al. (2015) using rank-1 Cholesky updates.  
- Technical quality is good overall but contains errors (incorrect notation in Eq. (1), typo in line 94).  
- Suggests that a more rigorous theoretical convergence analysis (especially for covariance) would strengthen Lemma 1.  
- Experiments show same function-evaluation efficiency as standard CMA-ES but much faster wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Pros: novel/efficient algorithm, strong empirical results, thorough property/convergence analysis.  
- Cons: limited novelty relative to prior work, technical errors needing correction, and convergence proof could be improved.  
- Overall: well-written and valuable contribution to derivative-free optimization, but significance and technical quality need refinement.
