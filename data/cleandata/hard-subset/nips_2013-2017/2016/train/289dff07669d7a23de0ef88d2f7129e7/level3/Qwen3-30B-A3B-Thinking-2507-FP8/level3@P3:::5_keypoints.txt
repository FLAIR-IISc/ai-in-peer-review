- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain/update lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of full covariance to reduce complexity.
- Reduces computational complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) per iteration (μ typically O(log d)).
- Proves asymptotic equivalence to standard CMA-ES by showing orthogonal matrix E_t converges to identity under certain assumptions (Lemma 1).
- Empirical results: same number of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES with substantially reduced runtime (e.g., ~20x faster at d=64).
- Compares against CMA-ES-Ref (delayed matrix sqrt) and CMA-ES/d (delayed updates) baselines.
- Strength: solid technical contribution that meaningfully reduces covariance update cost for high-dimensional problems.
- Strength: provides theoretical justification stronger than prior heuristic Cholesky approaches.
- Strength: clear presentation with helpful pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and thorough benchmark experiments.
- Strength: practical utility for cheap-objective, very high-dimensional problems (e.g., some neural-net scenarios).
- Weakness: theoretical convergence relies on strong, potentially unrealistic assumptions about covariance convergence.
- Weakness: limited practical impact because most real-world tasks have expensive objectives and moderate dimensions (d < 100).
- Weakness: empirical evaluation incomplete—missing wall-clock runtimes for standard CMA-ES-Ref and suggested diagnostics (E_t evolution, varied quadratic spectra, robotics experiments).
- Weakness: claim of “optimal asymptotic storage and runtime” is overstated, only true when full covariance updates are required.
- Quality: technically sound with experiments supporting claims, but theoretical justification limited by restrictive assumptions.
- Clarity: well-written and organized, reproducible via clear pseudocode.
- Originality: novel, theoretically justified Cholesky-based extension distinguishing it from prior work.
- Significance: important for niche high-dimensional settings where covariance updates dominate, but limited relevance for common ML use cases.
- Recommendation: accept conditional on acknowledging context-dependent utility; paper would benefit from expanded empirical analysis.
