- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES variant using triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce CMA-ES runtime complexity.  
- Main contribution: quadratic-time covariance update with minimal memory, improving efficiency for large-scale optimization.  
- Provides theoretical convergence justification and empirical results on benchmark functions.  
- Naming conflict: calling the variant "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and should be resolved.  
- Missing related-work discussion and empirical comparison with Suttorp et al. (2009) and algorithmic comparison with Krause et al. (2015).  
- Algorithmic contribution appears incremental—essentially looping rankOneUpdate for all individuals—and needs clearer justification.  
- Experiments are incomplete; must empirically compare to Suttorp’s and Krause’s variants and update figures accordingly.  
- Overstated optimality claim: not optimal for Sphere/separable functions and only ~2× cheaper than Suttorp’s method; claims should be tempered.  
- Paper is technically sound with supported claims, but related work discussion and algorithm explanation need expansion.  
- Clarity: well-written with clear notation, though some sections need better organization and more intuitive explanations.  
- Originality: proposes a novel approach but is an incremental improvement; significance depends on clearer positioning and limitations.  
- Recommendations: resolve naming conflict, add thorough comparisons, clarify algorithmic significance, complete experiments, and provide a nuanced discussion of advantages/limitations.
