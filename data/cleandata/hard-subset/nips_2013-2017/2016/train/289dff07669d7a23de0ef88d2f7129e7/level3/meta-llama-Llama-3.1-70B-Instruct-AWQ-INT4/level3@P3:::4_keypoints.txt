- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factorization.
- Reduces covariance update time complexity to O(µ d^2) and achieves optimal memory complexity.
- Provides theoretical justification that the modification preserves algorithm performance.
- Empirical benchmarks show reduced computational cost while maintaining optimization performance.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and gives thorough CMA-ES background.
- Balances theoretical and empirical contributions; experiments are well-designed and supported by figures/tables.
- Suggests future work combining Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-dimensional approximations for high-dimensional problems.
- Technically sound and free of major errors; presentation is clear and concise.
- Lacks a clear theoretical analysis explaining the claimed numerical stability improvement.
- Needs more implementation details and comparisons with other optimization methods.
- Original and innovative application of Cholesky factorization to CMA-ES, despite Cholesky’s prior use elsewhere.
- Potentially significant impact for large-scale optimization and covariance-adaptive algorithms.
- Reviewer recommends acceptance.
