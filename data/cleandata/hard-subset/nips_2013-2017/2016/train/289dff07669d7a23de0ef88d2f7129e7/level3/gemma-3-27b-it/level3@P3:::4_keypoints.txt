- Core contribution: Replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor in CSA to reduce covariance update cost without degrading performance.  
- Theoretical grounding: Lemma 1 supports convergence of the modified CSA and quadratic runtime complexity is established.  
- Practical impact: Significant runtime reduction and preserved evaluation count, especially beneficial in high dimensions.  
- Comparison to prior work: Builds on and overcomes limitations of previous Cholesky-based and delayed-update approaches (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel, Hansen & Ostermeier).  
- Information-geometry link: Properly connects covariance update rule to information-geometric foundations (Akimoto et al.).  
- Clarity and presentation: Well-written, logically organized, with helpful Algorithm 1 and 2 explanations.  
- Originality: Novel extension of Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES with solid theoretical backing.  
- Experimental validation: Empirical speed-ups shown (Figure 2) support claims; code available via Shark for reproducibility.  
- Significance: Valuable for high-dimensional optimization tasks, including potential neural network training applications.  
- Numerical stability concern: Paper insufficiently explains why the Cholesky-based computation is numerically more stable in practice.  
- Limited benchmarking: Comparisons focus on CMA-ES variants; lacks broader baselines (e.g., BFGS or other derivative-free methods).  
- Cache/memory effects: Acknowledges cache impact at d=256 but lacks detailed analysis of memory access patterns and optimizations.  
- Acceptance arguments: Addresses a practical bottleneck, strong theory, reproducible code, and notable speed-ups justify acceptance.  
- Rejection arguments: Need for deeper numerical-stability explanation and broader algorithmic comparisons could warrant revision.  
- Future directions: Suggests combining with low-dimensional approximation methods for very high-dimensional problems.
