- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: replaces expensive matrix square root with direct triangular Cholesky factor updates, achieving O(µd^2) time.
- Provides a novel theoretical justification that modifying CSA does not worsen function-evaluation performance as covariance converges.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-update work (e.g., Suttorp et al., 2009) but offers new analysis and a complete implementation for standard CMA-ES.
- Empirical results show substantial wall-clock speed-ups that grow with problem dimensionality, making CMA-ES more practical for moderate dimensions.
- Central claim—reduced runtime without increasing function evaluations—is supported by Lemma 1 and thorough benchmark experiments.
- Paper is technically sound, clearly written, and presents an original, well-motivated contribution.
- Claim of being "numerically more stable" is underexplained and lacks sufficient theoretical or empirical evidence.
- Quadratic complexity still limits applicability to very high-dimensional problems; discussion of future directions (e.g., hybrids with low-rank/LM-CMA) is needed.
- Overall assessment: strong, empirically validated improvement to CMA-ES with a recommendation for acceptance.
