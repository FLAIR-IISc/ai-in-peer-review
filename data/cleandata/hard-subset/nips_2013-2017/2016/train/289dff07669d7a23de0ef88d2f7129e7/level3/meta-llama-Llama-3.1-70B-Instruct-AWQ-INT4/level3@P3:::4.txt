The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" presents a novel modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, a widely used derivative-free optimization method in machine learning. The authors propose replacing the square root of the covariance matrix with its triangular Cholesky factorization, which reduces the computational complexity of the algorithm while maintaining its performance.

The main contribution of this paper is the introduction of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm, which achieves a better time complexity of O(µd^2) for the covariance update and optimal memory complexity. The authors provide a theoretical justification for why this modification does not impair the performance of the algorithm, and they demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical experiments on various benchmark functions.
