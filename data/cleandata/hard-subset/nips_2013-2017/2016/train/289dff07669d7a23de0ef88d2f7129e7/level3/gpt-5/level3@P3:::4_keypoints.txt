- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates triangular Cholesky factor A of C = A A^T and replaces C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA to avoid eigendecompositions.  
- Enables rank-1/rank-µ updates directly on A in O(µ d^2) time with optimal storage (triangular A only).  
- Theoretical argument shows the error from replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} vanishes asymptotically when covariance shape stabilizes.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time; speedup increases with dimension (e.g., ~20× at d=64).  
- Builds on prior work: delayed covariance updates, restricted/limited-memory models, and prior direct factor update methods; novelty is principled integration of triangular Cholesky updates into rank-µ CMA-ES.  
- Clear computational win: avoids costly matrix square roots/eigendecompositions and enables fast triangular solves/inversions when needed.  
- Strong theoretical rationale via RQ/polar decomposition showing rotation convergence and exponential forgetting in CSA.  
- Well-engineered: provides rank-one update algorithm on A, careful implementation discussion, and code release in Shark.  
- Numerical stability claim needs stronger theoretical grounding and citations (e.g., Higham) about Cholesky rank-one updates/downdates and triangular solves.  
- Must quantify accumulated rounding error and risk of losing SPD: track symmetry/PSD violations, condition number growth, and A A^T vs C drift; report how often re-factorization is needed.  
- Clarify that triangular solves should be used in CSA rather than forming A^{-1} explicitly for numerical stability.  
- Correct or clarify the incorrect/misleading statement that diag(A) gives eigenvalues of C; singular values of A equal sqrt(eigenvalues) but diag(A) generally does not.  
- Theory scope limited: provide finite-time bounds on CSA norm error as a function of rotation drift and algorithm parameters, and discuss non-stationary/noisy regimes.  
- Benchmarking scope limited: suggest COCO/BBOB suites with budgets, restarts, noise, larger d beyond 256, and ablation over µ, λ settings.  
- High-dimensional extensions missing: discuss integration with LM-CMA/limited-memory or hybrid strategies and applicability to very large d (≥1000) or neural policy search.  
- Quality assessment: technically sound, correct complexity analysis, convincing experiments, but stability diagnostics and eigenvalue claim need tightening.  
- Clarity: generally well written; suggest making stability claims precise, separating proven vs heuristic claims, and providing pseudocode emphasizing triangular solves.  
- Significance: high — a drop-in variant preserving evaluation efficiency while reducing runtime/memory is likely to be widely adopted.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical impact, simple elegant modification, optimal asymptotic complexity, solid experiments, and theoretical rationale.  
- Cons for acceptance: need clearer stability justification and diagnostics, incorrect eigenvalue statement, limited benchmarks, and no exploration with limited-memory variants.  
- Questions for authors to address: finite-time bounds on CSA norm error; frequency/need for periodic re-Cholesky; stability metrics across long runs; and how to combine with LM-CMA for d ≥ 1000.  
- Overall: valuable, implementation-driven contribution with convincing empirical benefits; recommend acceptance if stability analysis and clarified claims are strengthened.
