- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing covariance eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factorization, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μd^2).  
- Paper gives a clear, well-written description of CMA-ES and its limitations.  
- Thorough analysis and theoretical justification claiming no performance impairment from the modification.  
- Experiments show significant wall-clock speedups, especially in high dimensions.  
- No formal proof of equivalence between original CMA-ES and Cholesky-CMA-ES is provided.  
- Claim that path error is small and decreases over time is not experimentally validated, relying only on theory.  
- Contribution appears to be a trivial extension of prior work (Krause and Igel), with insufficient evidence of novelty/significance.  
- Minor clarity issues: notation/typos (e.g., "wk" → "α_k" in Algorithm 2, "n" → "d" in Table 1).  
- Recommendation: paper is not acceptable in current form; needs revisions to prove equivalence, validate path error experimentally, and better justify novelty.
