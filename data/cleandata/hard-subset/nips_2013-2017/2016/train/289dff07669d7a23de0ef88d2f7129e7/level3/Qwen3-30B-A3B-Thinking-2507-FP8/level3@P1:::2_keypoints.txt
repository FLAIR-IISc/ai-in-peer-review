- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce time/space complexity.
- Claims same optimization performance as standard CMA-ES while reducing per-iteration runtime from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2).
- Builds on Krause & Igel (2015) which used a similar Cholesky update for a restricted CMA-ES, extending it to the standard algorithm.
- Provides Lemma 1 arguing the error from replacing the square root with the Cholesky factor diminishes as the algorithm converges.
- Empirical results show comparable function-evaluation performance and much faster wall-clock time (up to 20× speedup at d=64).
- Writing is clear and organization makes technical content accessible.
- Method addresses a real computational bottleneck and could be useful in practice.
- Originality is limited: core idea closely mirrors Krause & Igel (2015) and the extension to standard CMA-ES appears straightforward.
- Complexity claim is misleading: CMA-ES-Ref’s delayed-square-root makes its average per-iteration cost O(d^2), so Cholesky-CMA-ES’s O(µ d^2) (with µ ≈ O(log d)) is asymptotically comparable and requires Θ-notation clarification.
- The key claim that the norm error vanishes via exponential weighting is unvalidated; no experiment tracks this error over time despite its importance for step-size adaptation.
- Paper contains technical errors/inconsistencies in notation (Algorithm 2 'wk' vs 'α_k'), table labeling (n vs d), and objective function naming (F vs f), which can impede reproducibility.
- Significance is limited for NIPS: incremental extension lacking novel theoretical insight; better suited for an evolutionary-computation venue.
- Recommendation: reject for NIPS unless authors clearly demonstrate novelty, validate error-propagation claims experimentally, fix technical errors, or substantially reframe the contribution.
