- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES using a triangular Cholesky factor and A−1 instead of eigen-decomposition and C−1/2 to reduce time and memory while aiming to preserve performance.  
- Positions work within prior efficient covariance-update literature, most directly extending Krause & Igel (FOGA’15) rank-one Cholesky updates.  
- Claims rotation error from using A−1 decays as the algorithm stabilizes and thus CSA substitution is benign.  
- Empirical results show similar evaluation counts and significant wall-clock speedups.  
- Lacks a formal equivalence proof that the proposed rank-one updates to A implement the covariance update of Eq. (2) or a precise statement of differences.  
- CSA “path error is small and decreases” is only heuristically argued (Lemma 1) and validated by final performance; time-resolved empirical evidence is missing.  
- Complexity discussion is unclear; should use Θ-notation, compare to amortized O(μ d^2) with delayed decomposition, and explain constant-factor gains (e.g., avoiding cubic eigen-decomposition, cache locality).  
- Needs fair baseline comparisons with standard delay settings to justify claimed speed advantage.  
- Writing is generally clear but contains notation/typo errors (wk→αk, n→d in Table 1, “F”→“f” line 185) and needs clearer positioning relative to prior Rank-one update work.  
- Originality is limited: core technical component is a straightforward extension of Krause & Igel with main novelty being the CSA substitution and heuristic justification.  
- Potentially useful in practice if equivalence and speed claims are rigorously established; could become a standard engineering choice.  
- Pros: clear motivation, plausible practical speedups, clean and memory-efficient implementation idea.  
- Cons: limited novelty, missing equivalence proof, insufficient validation of CSA path-error dynamics, and unconvincing complexity advantage justification.  
- Recommendation: Not acceptable in current form; request addition of equivalence proof, time-resolved CSA analyses, clarified complexity with Θ-notation and fair baselines, and fixes to notation/typos.
