- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, an efficient CMA-ES variant that updates the covariance via its Cholesky factor to reduce complexity.
- Reduces per-iteration time from O(d^3) to O(µd^2) and uses optimal O(d^2) memory.
- Introduces a stable adaptation of the cumulative step-size mechanism tailored to the Cholesky update.
- Provides a theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the approximation does not degrade optimization performance.
- Empirical evaluation shows substantial wall-clock speedups over the reference implementation, especially for d ≥ 64, without increasing function evaluations.
- Additional practical benefit: covariance matrix eigenvalues become available at no extra cost.
- Builds on prior efficiency efforts but contributes a novel, stable modification making Cholesky-based updates feasible for standard CMA-ES.
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and positions the contribution within existing literature.
- Weakness: implementation details for runtime comparisons need clarification (ensuring same language and numerical libraries like LAPACK).
- Weakness: results in Figure 2 need more explanation, specifically why L2 cache effects at d=256 make Cigar and Discus performance differences less pronounced.
