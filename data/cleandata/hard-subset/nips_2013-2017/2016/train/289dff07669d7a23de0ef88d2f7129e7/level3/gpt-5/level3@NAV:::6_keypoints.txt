- Proposal: maintain triangular Cholesky factor A of covariance, apply rank-one updates to A, and approximate CSA by using A^{-1} instead of C^{-1/2}, yielding O(µ d^2) time and optimal storage with reported runtime speedups.  
- Relation to prior work: extends Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015) to standard CMA-ES with µ>1 and modifies CSA.  
- Strength: clear motivation, practical engineering value, readable derivation, and plausible heuristic justification (Lemma 1) for the CSA approximation.  
- Strength: promising wall-clock speedups and implementation details useful to practitioners.  
- Naming issue: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009); adopt a distinct name and acknowledge lineage.  
- Missing baselines: must include empirical and algorithmic comparisons to Suttorp (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015) (can put full plots in supplement).  
- Incremental contribution concern: novelty seems limited to looping rankOneUpdate over µ individuals plus CSA replacement—state this explicitly or justify greater novelty.  
- Overstated optimality: claim of “optimal” is exaggerated—method is still quadratic and only ~2× cheaper than Suttorp’s scheme; discuss diagonal/low-rank (LM-CMA) alternatives and temper scalability claims.  
- Numerical stability claim unsupported: manuscript asserts Suttorp is unstable contrary to Suttorp’s claims—provide stress tests/conditioning plots or retract the claim.  
- Experiments/ablations missing: add Suttorp’s and Krause’s variants as baselines and ablation isolating CSA approximation (e.g., occasional polar decomposition) and report effects on evaluation counts.  
- Plotting/protocol clarity: clarify aggregation/plotting protocol (median vs mean) since this may explain artifacts like the Cigar plateau.  
- Correction/clarity: eigendecompositions are typically done periodically, not every update; make the key algorithmic changes (the for-loop and CSA modification) explicit in exposition.  
- Pros summary: practical speedups and clear, implementable update rules.  
- Cons summary: naming conflict, incomplete related-work positioning and baselines, overstated claims on optimality and stability.  
- Significance/recommendation: moderate if reframed as an engineering improvement; reviewer leans toward rejection unless requested fixes are addressed.
