- Paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factors to reduce time and memory while matching CMA-ES performance.
- Strength: well-written and organized with clear explanation and effective presentation of empirical speedups.
- Strength: significant practical impact—reduces runtime and memory, enabling CMA-ES to scale to high-dimensional problems.
- Strength: thorough experiments comparing to state-of-the-art variants, showing same optimization performance and substantial speedups.
- Weakness: originality is limited—incremental extension of rank-one updates and prior Cholesky-based work.
- Weakness: lacks a formal proof of equivalence to formula (2) and does not rigorously validate claims about error in the path.
- Weakness: insufficient detailed analysis of computational trade-offs versus other approaches (e.g., CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES).
- Weakness: experiments focus on final metrics and do not validate intermediate claims such as step-size adaptation path errors.
- Weakness: minor notational inconsistencies and typographical errors reduce polish (e.g., wk vs. alphak, n vs. d, 'F' vs. 'f').
- Recommendation: not ready for acceptance—needs theoretical proofs, deeper complexity discussion, additional validation experiments, and notation fixes.
- For acceptance: promising experimental speedups, practical relevance for large-scale optimization, and clear writing.
- Against acceptance: limited novelty, missing theoretical/experimental validations, and presentation issues.
- Overall: method shows potential but requires substantial revisions before being acceptable.
