- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that reduces CMA-ES complexity from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2) by maintaining and updating the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky/rank-1 update work (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015) and generalizes to rank-µ updates.  
- Main challenge is CSA’s need for C^{-1/2}; authors approximate it with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1}.  
- Lemma 1 theoretically justifies the A^{-1} approximation by arguing rotational error vanishes if the covariance shape converges.  
- Comprehensive empirical benchmarks show substantial wall-clock speed-up with no observable loss in function-evaluation performance.  
- Practical significance: addresses a key bottleneck, making CMA-ES more usable in high-dimensional or fast-evaluation settings (e.g., RL policy search).  
- Clear methodology and presentation; the algorithm and the approximation rationale are well explained.  
- Strong experimental design and comparisons against relevant baselines across dimensions and problems.  
- Limited originality: approach is incremental since Cholesky updates and rank-µ generalization are straightforward extensions of prior work.  
- Theoretical reliance on an unproven assumption: convergence of the normalized covariance in a single stochastic run is not fully justified.  
- High overall quality and clarity: technically sound, well-organized, and presented as a usable implementation.  
- Minor issues: a simplification in update notation ((mt+1−mt)/σt vs. division by temporary σ̃t) may confuse; small typo ("which can by be motivated").
