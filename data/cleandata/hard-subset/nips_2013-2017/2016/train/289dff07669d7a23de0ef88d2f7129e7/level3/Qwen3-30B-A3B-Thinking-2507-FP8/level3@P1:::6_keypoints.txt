- Proposes "Cholesky-CMA-ES": replace covariance matrix square root with inverse Cholesky factor to reduce time/space complexity.  
- Claims optimal asymptotic complexity and comparable objective-evaluation performance; theoretical justification (Lemma 1) for Cholesky approximation.  
- Builds on CMA-ES and prior Cholesky-based updates (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015); identifies O(d^3) bottleneck and targets O(µ d^2) via rank-one Cholesky updates.  
- Strengths: sound theoretical lemma, clear algorithm description, experiments generally show similar performance with reduced runtime, storage benefits, and free eigenvalues.  
- Naming is misleading/conflicts with Suttorp (2009); must clarify relationship or choose a distinct name.  
- Major omission: no experimental comparison with Suttorp (2009) or Krause (2015), only compared to delayed CMA-ES and CMA-ES/d.  
- Contribution seems incremental—main change is applying a rankOneUpdate in a loop; novelty not clearly motivated.  
- "Optimal" complexity claim is overstated: only relevant for non-separable ill-conditioned problems; separable functions need only diagonal updates.  
- Numerical stability claim is questionable; authors contradict Suttorp’s claim of numerical stability without proper analysis or citations.  
- Experimental issues: unexplained plateau on the Cigar function, unclear median computation, possible sampling artifacts.  
- Reported speedup (e.g., "20× faster" at d=64) appears exaggerated given comparable theoretical complexity to Suttorp’s method.  
- Claim that the method "scales up CMA-ES to large problems" is misleading—complexity remains quadratic, so it doesn’t solve fundamental high-dimensional scalability.  
- Significance limited for the community: technically sound but marginal, incremental, and lacking key comparisons, thus limited practical impact.  
- Recommended fixes: resolve naming/context, include direct comparisons with Suttorp/Krause, clarify optimality claims, analyze numerical stability, and temper scalability claims.  
- Overall verdict: technically correct modification but incremental and overstated; paper would be stronger after the suggested revisions.
