

# Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper proposes a variant of the CMA-ES algorithm that uses triangular Cholesky factors for covariance matrix adaptation instead of the matrix square root, aiming to reduce computational complexity while maintaining performance. The authors extend the rank-1 Cholesky update from Krause and Igel (2015) to include rank-μ updates, providing a theoretically justified modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism. Under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges, they show that replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} (where A is the Cholesky factor) does not significantly change algorithm behavior. The paper demonstrates through experiments that the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves similar performance to standard CMA-ES with significantly reduced runtime complexity.

**Quality:** The paper presents a technically sound approach with reasonable theoretical justification (Lemma 1) and solid experimental validation. The theoretical argument that the error introduced by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} vanishes as the covariance converges is plausible, though the paper would benefit from stronger theoretical grounding. Specifically, citing Beyer's work on covariance convergence (ECJ 2014) would strengthen Lemma 1's credibility. The experimental results are well-designed, showing consistent performance across multiple benchmark functions and dimensions, with the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieving significant speedups (up to 20x for d=64) without increasing the number of function evaluations. However, the paper does not sufficiently address how the algorithm performs on very large-scale problems where the quadratic complexity might become limiting.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-organized and clearly written. The algorithm descriptions are precise, and the experimental setup is thoroughly explained. However, there are minor technical errors: the denominator in Eq. (1) should be \tilde{σ} rather than σ (same error in footnote 2 on p.3), and there's a typo in line 94 ("which can by be motivated" → "which can be motivated"). These are easily fixable but detract from the paper's professionalism.

**Originality:** The core idea of using Cholesky factors for covariance adaptation is not novel (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause and Igel 2015). The extension to rank-μ updates is a straightforward generalization rather than a significant theoretical advance. The paper fails to adequately distinguish this work from previous attempts to use Cholesky factors in CMA-ES, particularly in the discussion of related work. The novelty claim is weakened by the fact that the main contribution is a technical extension rather than a conceptual breakthrough.

**Significance:** The paper addresses a genuine practical problem in CMA-ES implementation (computational complexity of covariance matrix updates), which is important for real-world applications. The demonstrated speed improvements are substantial and meaningful, especially for higher-dimensional problems. However, the significance is limited by the fact that the algorithm is essentially a practical optimization of an existing method rather than a fundamental advancement. The paper's claim that this "can and should replace the standard CMA-ES" may be overstated, as the speedup is only significant for problems where covariance updates are a bottleneck, and the quadratic complexity still limits scalability for very large problems.

**Arguments for acceptance:** The paper presents a useful practical improvement to a widely used algorithm with strong empirical validation. The theoretical justification for why the approximation doesn't significantly affect performance is reasonable and well-supported by experiments. The implementation details and experimental results are thorough and convincing.

**Arguments against acceptance:** The novelty is limited, as the key extension from rank-1 to rank-μ is straightforward. The theoretical justification would be stronger with better grounding in existing covariance convergence literature. The paper doesn't sufficiently differentiate this work from previous attempts to use Cholesky factors in CMA-ES.

In conclusion, this is a solid technical contribution that improves the efficiency of an important optimization algorithm with minimal loss in performance. While the novelty is somewhat limited, the practical significance and thorough experimental validation make it a worthwhile contribution to the field. The paper would be significantly improved by strengthening the theoretical justification and more clearly articulating how this work differs from prior attempts to use Cholesky factors in CMA-ES. With minor revisions addressing the theoretical gaps and technical errors, this paper would be a strong candidate for acceptance.
