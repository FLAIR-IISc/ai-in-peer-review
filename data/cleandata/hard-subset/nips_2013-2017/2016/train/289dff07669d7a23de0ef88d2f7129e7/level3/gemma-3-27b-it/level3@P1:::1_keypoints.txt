- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update the Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance to reduce compute and memory.
- Theoretically justifies replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor, quantifying introduced error (Lemma 1).
- Achieves optimal asymptotic storage/runtime and significant wall-clock speedups at higher dimensionalities with similar function-evaluation performance.
- Builds on prior Cholesky approaches but provides the first theoretical grounding within the standard CMA-ES framework.
- Paper is well-written and organized, facilitating understanding and reproduction by knowledgeable readers.
- Technical analysis is sound and convergence properties are convincingly argued.
- Original contribution with likely high impact for large-scale derivative-free optimization and ML applications.
- Produces covariance eigenvalues as a useful byproduct.
- Experimental results lack detailed implementation info (language, compiler, libraries) needed to ensure fair runtime comparisons.
- Figure 2 shows smaller benefits on Cigar and Discus at d=256; this observation needs explanation.
- Scalability discussion is limited—practical limits (cache effects, behavior beyond d=256) and mitigation strategies are not fully addressed.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a major CMA-ES bottleneck, strong theoretical and empirical support, clear presentation, significant potential impact.
- Arguments for rejection: minor reproducibility concerns from missing implementation details and unexplained benchmark behavior.
- Overall assessment: high-quality work with strong recommendation for acceptance, pending minor clarifications.
