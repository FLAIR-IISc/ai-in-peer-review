- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces the covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factors, reducing covariance-update cost to O(μ d^2) while keeping the same number of objective evaluations.  
- Authors provide theoretical arguments that the modification preserves algorithm performance and ensures numerical stability.  
- Empirical results show substantial wall-clock time reductions, especially in high-dimensional search spaces.  
- Theoretical rigor: detailed analysis arguing Cholesky-CMA-ES retains standard CMA-ES performance with lower computational overhead.  
- Empirical validation: thorough comparisons against multiple CMA-ES variants on benchmark functions demonstrating clear speed-ups as dimensionality increases.  
- Practical significance: reduced runtime and memory make the method relevant for large-scale optimization tasks (e.g., RL, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Numerical stability: triangular Cholesky factors enable efficient eigenvalue and inverse computations useful for monitoring and control.  
- Weakness — stability explanation: claim of greater numerical stability is not fully elaborated; needs deeper theoretical or empirical support.  
- Weakness — extreme scalability: method remains O(d^2) and may be prohibitive for very high-dimensional problems; combining with low-rank/approximate approaches should be discussed.  
- Weakness — applicability: experiments focus on benchmarks; lacking real-world application evaluations (e.g., reinforcement learning, hyperparameter optimization).  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to better justify numerical stability and discuss strategies for scaling to extremely high dimensions.  
- Pro-acceptance arguments: novel, theoretically justified, and empirically demonstrates significant runtime improvements addressing a key CMA-ES bottleneck.  
- Against-acceptance arguments: insufficient discussion of numerical stability, unaddressed extreme-dimension scalability, and lack of real-world benchmarks.
