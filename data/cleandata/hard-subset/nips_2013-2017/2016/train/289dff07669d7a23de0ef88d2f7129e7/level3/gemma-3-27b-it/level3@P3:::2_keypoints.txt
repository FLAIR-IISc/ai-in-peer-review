- Proposal: replace costly eigen-decomposition in CMA-ES covariance updates with Cholesky decomposition to speed up high-dimensional problems.  
- Strength: clear writing, good overview of CMA-ES, and well-motivated avoidance of frequent eigen-decompositions.  
- Contribution concern: limited originality and is a straightforward extension of Krause & Igel (2015) and related prior work; needs stronger differentiation.  
- Missing theoretical proof: no explicit equivalence proof showing the Cholesky-based update matches the original CMA-ES update, undermining performance claims.  
- Complexity claim issue: stating O(μd^2) is inadequate—must use Θ-notation, analyze constant factors, and clarify how μ affects practical runtime.  
- Path error claim unsupported: assertion of small/decreasing path error vs. Hansen lacks temporal experimental validation across generations.  
- Experimental validation: final-performance comparisons alone are insufficient; need experiments tracking error and behavior over time.  
- Practical speedup claim: authors must explain why their implementation is faster despite having the same asymptotic complexity as CMA-ES-Ref.  
- Notation and presentation errors: algorithm and table notation mistakes (wk vs α_k, n vs d, 'F' vs 'f') indicate lack of meticulousness.  
- Arguments for acceptance: potential for significant speedups in high dimensions, clear presentation, and well-motivated problem.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, missing equivalence proof, unclear complexity justification, unvalidated path-error claim, and minor errors.  
- Recommendation: reject as submitted; require rigorous theoretical analysis and more comprehensive experimental validation before resubmission.
