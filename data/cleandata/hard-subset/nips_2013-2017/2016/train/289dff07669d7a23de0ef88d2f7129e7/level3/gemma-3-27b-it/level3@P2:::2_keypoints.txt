- Proposal: Replace the CMA-ES covariance matrix square root with its Cholesky factor for step-size adaptation to reduce computation and memory.  
- Writing/motivation: Paper is clearly written and addresses a well-motivated bottleneck in CMA-ES.  
- Positioning: Properly situates work within prior literature (Hansen & Ostermeier, Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel, etc.).  
- Theoretical support: Provides a lemma on Cholesky convergence but lacks a formal proof that the Cholesky update is equivalent to the original covariance-based update for general CMA-ES.  
- Equivalence concern: Equivalence proof is critical—current claim of unchanged performance is unsubstantiated without it (prior proof exists only for µ=λ=1).  
- Complexity claim: States O(µ d²) complexity, which matches standard CMA-ES with delayed updates; needs Θ-notation and a detailed cost breakdown to show where any speedup arises.  
- "Asymptotically optimal" label: Requires stronger justification.  
- Empirical strengths: Experiments show comparable function-evaluation performance and noticeable wall-clock speedups at higher dimensions.  
- Empirical weaknesses: Insufficient evidence for the claimed small/decreasing path error from the ŝ_t approximation; final performance parity alone is inadequate.  
- Needed experiments: Should track E_t over time or directly measure approximation impact on step-size adaptation and convergence behavior.  
- Minor issues: Notational inconsistencies and typos (e.g., w_k vs α_k, 'F' vs 'f', 'n' vs 'd' in Table 1).  
- Novelty concern: Approach appears to be a relatively straightforward extension of existing Cholesky-based methods, possibly lacking substantial novelty.  
- Recommendation: Reject—encourage revision to provide formal equivalence proof, clarify complexity analysis, and add rigorous experimental validation of the approximation.
