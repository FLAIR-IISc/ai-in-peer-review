- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factorization to remove square root computations.
- Achieves optimal asymptotic runtime and storage complexity O(μd²) while preserving the same number of objective evaluations as standard CMA-ES.
- Builds on prior optimization attempts but provides a theoretically justified approach that avoids previous numerical-instability issues.
- Clear theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing the Cholesky-based approach does not degrade algorithmic performance.
- Thorough empirical validation across multiple benchmark functions and dimensions with a well-structured algorithm description (Algorithm 1).
- Experimental results show significant runtime improvements (up to 20× faster at d=64) and speed-ups that grow with dimensionality.
- Claim of improved numerical stability relies on asymptotic convergence arguments and needs a more concrete numerical stability analysis.
- Suggestion to combine with low-dimensional approximations (Loshchilov, 2015) is superficial and lacks analysis or implementation.
- High overall quality: sound theory, comprehensive experiments, clear writing, and a significant contribution to derivative-free optimization.
- Strong originality: provides a theoretically grounded Cholesky implementation distinct from prior work.
- High significance: directly addresses a computational bottleneck in CMA-ES with practical benefits for machine learning applications.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to strengthen the numerical stability explanation.
