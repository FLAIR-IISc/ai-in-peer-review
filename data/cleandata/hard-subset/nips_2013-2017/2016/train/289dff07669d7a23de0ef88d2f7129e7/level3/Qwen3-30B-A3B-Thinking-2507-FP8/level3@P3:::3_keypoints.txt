- Proposal: replace covariance square root with triangular Cholesky factors in CMA-ES, extend rank-1 Cholesky update to rank-μ, and modify CSA, showing A^{-1} approximates C^{-1/2} under covariance convergence.
- Theoretical claim: Lemma 1 argues the error from replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} vanishes as covariance converges, but needs stronger theoretical grounding.
- Suggestion: cite Beyer (ECJ 2014) on covariance convergence to strengthen Lemma 1’s credibility.
- Empirical results: Cholesky-CMA-ES matches standard CMA-ES performance across benchmarks and dims while achieving large runtime speedups (up to ~20× at d=64) without extra function evaluations.
- Quality assessment: technically sound approach with solid experiments, though theoretical justification could be deeper.
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with precise algorithm descriptions; contains minor technical errors (Eq.(1) denominator should be \tilde{σ}; typo “which can by be motivated”).
- Originality: core idea is not novel (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015); rank-μ extension is a straightforward generalization rather than a major conceptual advance.
- Significance: practically important speed improvements for problems where covariance updates are a bottleneck, but limited by remaining quadratic complexity for very large-scale problems.
- Arguments for acceptance: useful practical improvement, thorough implementation and experiments, and reasonable empirical/theoretical support.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient differentiation from prior Cholesky-CMA-ES work, and the theoretical argument needs stronger literature support.
- Recommendation: accept after minor revisions to strengthen theoretical grounding, better distinguish from prior work, and fix technical errors.
