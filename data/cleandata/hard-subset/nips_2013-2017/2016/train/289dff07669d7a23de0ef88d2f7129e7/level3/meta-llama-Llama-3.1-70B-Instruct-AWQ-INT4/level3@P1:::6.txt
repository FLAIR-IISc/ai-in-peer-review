The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, which is a popular derivative-free optimization method. The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of a triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the traditional square root of the covariance matrix, leading to a more efficient update scheme.

The CMA-ES algorithm is a Monte Carlo method that samples from a multivariate normal distribution and adapts the mean and covariance matrix based on the ranking of the sampled points. The update of the covariance matrix dominates the time and space complexity of the algorithm. The proposed modification, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, replaces the inverse of the square root of the covariance matrix with the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor. This change leads to a more efficient update scheme with a time complexity of O(µd^2), where µ is the number of samples and d is the dimensionality of the problem.

The paper provides a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and presents empirical results comparing the original CMA-ES with the new Cholesky-CMA-ES on various benchmark functions. The results show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of function evaluations as the original CMA-ES but is significantly faster in terms of wall-clock time, especially for high-dimensional problems.

However, there are some concerns with the paper. The naming of the variant "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with the established name by Suttorp et al. (2009), and the paper omits discussion and empirical comparison with Suttorp's method and algorithmic comparison with Krause et al. (2015). The main novelty of the paper appears to be simply looping the rankOneUpdate for all individuals, which may not be a significant contribution. Additionally, the experiments are incomplete, and the paper should empirically compare to Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause's variant and update the figures accordingly.

The paper also makes some claims that need clarification. The claim of "optimality" is overstated, as the method is not optimal for all problems, such as separable functions where covariance or diagonal suffices. The paper also asserts that Suttorp's method is numerically unstable, despite the original paper claiming the opposite. Furthermore, the complexity of the algorithm remains quadratic, so the approach does not fundamentally solve large-scale CMA-ES issues, although it saves time and memory for full covariance.

In conclusion, the paper proposes a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm that leads to a more efficient update scheme. However, there are some concerns with the paper, including the naming conflict, missing prior-work comparison, and incomplete experiments. The paper should clarify its contribution, run additional experiments, and address the concerns mentioned above.
