- Proposes a Cholesky-based CMA-ES that updates a triangular Cholesky factor A via O(μd^2) sequences of rank-1 updates, generalizing Krause & Igel (2015) to rank-μ CMA-ES.
- Modifies CSA by replacing C−1/2 with A−1 and analyzes the missing rotation using RQ/polar factorization, arguing rotation error vanishes under covariance shape-convergence.
- Empirical results show nearly identical evaluation counts to reference CMA-ES and substantial wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension, supporting preserved optimization performance with lower runtime.
- Cholesky rank-1 update formulation is technically sound and correctly adapted to sequence rank-1 updates for both rank-1 and rank-μ terms.
- Lemma on asymptotic rotation cancellation is plausible and correct under the stated shape-convergence assumption.
- Central theoretical justification depends on covariance shape-convergence, which is a common but nontrivial assumption needing stronger theoretical backing or citations.
- Recommends citing Beyer (2014) or related work to substantiate shape-convergence and clarify conditions where it holds.
- Experiments are comprehensive (rotated benchmarks, up to d=256, 100 runs) but would benefit from additional diagnostics and settings.
- Missing analyses: report per-iteration rotation magnitudes (EtETt−1) in main text and measure ‖pσ‖ discrepancy between A−1 and C−1/2, especially early in runs or on rapidly changing functions.
- Suggests testing robustness under noise and adding broader benchmarks (BBOB-style, ill-conditioned/nonconvex) and limited-memory baselines (e.g., LM-CMA).
- Clarity: paper is well-organized with clear algorithms; recommend making the covariance convergence assumption explicit earlier and contrasting triangular vs non-triangular factorization sooner.
- Implementation details need clarification: numerical safeguards for rank-1 updates, conditioning checks, when A−1 is formed vs solved via triangular solves.
- Originality is modest: straightforward extension of prior rank-1 Cholesky work, with some theoretical perspective but limited scope.
- Significance is practical: valuable drop-in speed/memory improvements for full-covariance CMA-ES in moderate-to-high dimensions, likely useful for adoption.
- Pros for acceptance: practical speedup with preserved evaluation performance, optimal O(μd^2) time/memory, and plausible theoretical rationale plus rotation-invariance tests.
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, reliance on shape-convergence assumption, and incomplete quantification of early-iteration behavior and noise robustness.
- Specific actionable suggestions: cite Beyer (2014), include rotation and ‖pσ‖ diagnostic traces, add more diverse/noisy benchmarks and LM baselines, and move key diagnostics from supplement to main text.
- Minor fixes: correct denominator in Eq.(1) to \tilde{σ}, fix typo “which can by be motivated,” and summarize memory savings and practical constants for O(μd^2).
- Overall assessment: solid, well-implemented engineering improvement with practical impact and assumption-dependent theory; leans toward acceptance if theoretical discussion and diagnostics are strengthened.
