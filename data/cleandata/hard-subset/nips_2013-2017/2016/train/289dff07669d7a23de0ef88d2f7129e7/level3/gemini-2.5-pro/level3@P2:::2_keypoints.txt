- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates Cholesky factor A to reduce covariance update cost from O(d^3) to Θ(µd^2).  
- Replaces required C^{-1/2} in CSA with A^{-1}, arguing (Lemma 1) the approximation error is a vanishing rotation as covariance converges.  
- Empirical results show substantial wall-clock speedups with no degradation in function-evaluation performance on benchmarks.  
- Missing formal proof that sequential rank-1/µ updates to A yield A_{t+1}A_{t+1}^T = C_{t+1}, essential for correctness claims.  
- Central theoretical claim (C^{-1/2} ≈ A^{-1}) is not empirically validated over runs, especially during early iterations or on non-converging problems.  
- Complexity claims need clearer Θ/Θ-notation and a detailed comparison since reference CMA-ES with delayed updates has amortized Θ(d^2).  
- Several minor typos and notation inconsistencies (wk vs α_k, n vs d, f(x1,t) vs f(x_{1,t})) hurt polish.  
- Originality concern: core idea closely extends Krause & Igel (2015) rank-one Cholesky update, making the conceptual novelty limited.  
- Practical significance is high: a faster, memory-efficient CMA-ES could enable applications in higher dimensions and become a new standard.  
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses an important practical bottleneck, clear presentation, easy implementation, and strong runtime improvements.  
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient experimental/theoretical validation of the CSA approximation, and missing technical details.  
- Conclusion: promising and practically valuable but not acceptable in current form; needs formal equivalence proof and direct empirical validation of the CSA path error.
