- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates the triangular Cholesky factor directly (instead of the matrix square root) to reduce computation and memory for CMA-ES, extending Krause & Igel (2015) to full CMA-ES.  
- Theoretical basis: Provides justification via Lemma 1 linking rotation-matrix convergence to approximated step-size adaptation, but depends on an assumption of covariance convergence.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates comparable optimization performance with reduced runtime, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Strength — Practical improvement: Meaningful runtime reduction without loss of function-evaluation performance, useful for large-scale problems.  
- Strength — Theoretical support: Presents a reasonable theoretical argument (though assumption-dependent).  
- Strength — Presentation: Well-written with clear algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1 & 2).  
- Strength — Experiments: Thorough benchmarking across functions and dimensionalities.  
- Weakness — Limited novelty: Extension of prior Cholesky-update work is incremental rather than a major conceptual advance.  
- Weakness — Reliance on assumption: Convergence-based assumption is insufficiently supported; should cite Beyer (2014) for convergence analysis.  
- Weakness — Minor technical errors: Noted mistakes in Eq. (1) (σ vs. \tilde{σ}) and a typographical error on line 94.  
- Weakness — Comparisons: Superficial treatment of alternative Cholesky-based methods (e.g., Suttorp et al. 2009); trade-offs not deeply discussed.  
- Overall quality: Technically sound and well-evaluated but moderate significance due to limited novelty and assumption dependence.  
- Recommendation: Lean accept if authors fix theoretical support/citations, correct minor errors, and expand discussion of alternative methods.
