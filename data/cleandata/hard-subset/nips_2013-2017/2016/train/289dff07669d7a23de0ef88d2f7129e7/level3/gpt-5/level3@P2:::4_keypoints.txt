- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) and uses A^{-1} in CSA to avoid cubic decompositions.  
- Complexity: Achieves O(µ d^2) per-iteration time and optimal O(d^2) storage while preserving positive definiteness.  
- Theoretical justification: Argues CSA approximation is valid via a rotation convergence lemma—rotation between sqrt and triangular factor converges to identity, making CSA error vanish.  
- Empirical performance: Matches standard CMA-ES on function-evaluation counts and yields large wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension (e.g., ≈20× at d=64).  
- Prior work relation: Extends earlier Cholesky/factorization update attempts to integrate with standard CSA-based CMA-ES and provides a clearer theoretical rationale.  
- Motivation strength: Clearly motivated by costly eigen/sqrt decompositions in full CMA-ES for moderate-to-large d.  
- Algorithmic design strength: Rank-one Cholesky updates and triangular form enable fast inversion and simple bookkeeping.  
- Theoretical strength: Provides a principled argument why triangularity gives a bijective, smooth mapping from C to A and why rotations matter.  
- Empirical strength: Demonstrates near-identical evaluation counts on smooth, ill-conditioned, and rotated benchmarks with substantial runtime improvements.  
- Practical strength: Implementation-ready and integrated into an open-source library.  
- Numerical stability concern: “Numerically stable” is asserted but not analyzed—needs definition (SPD preservation, round-off bounds, conditioning) and diagnostics (re-factorization frequency, symmetry/positivity drift, condition numbers).  
- CSA-approximation concern: Lacks quantitative transient error bounds; rotation lemma depends on shape convergence and may not cover rapid changes—empirical ablation vs exact C^{-1/2}-CSA is recommended.  
- Missing plots/data suggestion: Move key supplement plots (e.g., ||p_σ||/E{χ} trajectories, ||E_t E_{t-1}^T − I|| over time) into the main text for clarity.  
- Incorrect claim: The statement that Cholesky diagonal gives “eigenvalues for free” is generally false—diag(A) ≠ sqrt(eigenvalues(C)); clarify or correct.  
- Experimental scope limitation: Lacks tests on noisy, constrained, and rugged multimodal problems where C fluctuates; sensitivity to noise and affine invariance should be reported.  
- Complexity claims need substantiation: Provide absolute memory usage and per-iteration flop counts to support stated complexity benefits.  
- Practical guidance missing: Advise on periodic re-factorization/refresh strategies, block- or cache-aware implementations (noting cache effects at d=256), and guidance on µ and baseline fairness.  
- Overall quality: Technically sound and well-implemented; CSA approximation plausible but would benefit from more quantitative analysis and numerical evidence.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written and organized, but fix the eigenvalue statement and expand the numerical-stability discussion; clean up reference formatting.  
- Originality: Moderately novel and impactful—nontrivial integration of Cholesky updates into CSA-based CMA-ES.  
- Significance: High practical significance for moderate-to-large d; complementary to limited-memory/low-rank methods and promising for hybrids.  
- Pros for acceptance: Large runtime reductions without extra evaluations, theoretically motivated change to CSA, asymptotically optimal time/storage, and practical implementation.  
- Cons for acceptance: Limited theoretical quantification of CSA error and numerical stability, misleading eigenvalue claim, and narrower experimental scope.  
- Questions for authors: Please report ||p_σ||/E{χ} distributions for both CSA variants, frequency of needed re-factorizations, performance under noise and constraints, and benchmarks combining with low-rank/LM-CMA-ES.
