- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains a triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) and replaces C^{-1/2} in CSA with A^{-1} to avoid cubic square-root/eigendecompositions.  
- Theoretical claim: The rotation between A and the matrix square root converges when normalized shape of C converges, so the CSA norm discrepancy decays (exponential forgetting).  
- Covariance update: Implements weighted rank-1 and rank-µ updates as sequential Cholesky updates, achieving O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) memory per iteration.  
- Empirics: Matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluations and yields large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64), with improvements growing with dimension up to d=256.  

Strengths
- Principled technical contribution: triangular Cholesky-factor update for full CMA-ES that preserves sampling correctness.  
- Theoretical insight: justification for using A^{-1} in CSA via RQ decomposition and exponential forgetting.  
- Engineering value: removes periodic cubic decompositions, simplifies A^{-1} access, integrates with BLAS/LAPACK, and is open-source.  
- Empirical validation: consistent evaluation counts and substantial runtime reductions across rotated and standard benchmarks.  

Weaknesses / Questions
- Numerical stability: unclear whether A^{-1} is formed explicitly or applied via triangular solves; quantitative stability/conditioning evidence is needed.  
- Spectrum claim: statement that Cholesky diagonals give eigenvalues is incorrect in general; clarify which spectral quantities are cheaply obtained.  
- Theory scope: lemma is asymptotic; need finite-time bounds on CSA norm error and discussion for non-stationary/noisy objectives.  
- Invariance: approximation may further affect rotation invariance; more diagnostics (e.g., eigenvector angle changes vs progress) should be summarized.  
- Experimental scope: limited to synthetic tests—add at least one practical task (RL or hyperparameter tuning), runtime breakdowns (sampling, ranking, updates), and µ sensitivity.  
- High-dimensional regimes: still O(d^2); discuss or evaluate combinations with low-rank/limited-memory approaches for very large d.  

Quality & Clarity
- Development is sound and consistent with prior rank-one Cholesky work; empirical methodology is solid.  
- Paper is generally clear but should clarify numerical stability, spectrum claims, A^{-1} usage, and include a complexity comparison table.  

Originality & Significance
- Novel extension of triangular Cholesky updates to weighted full CMA-ES and a new theoretical angle on the CSA replacement.  
- Significant practical impact for cases where evaluation cost is not dominant; likely adoption in libraries.  

Suggestions
- Provide quantitative stability analysis and recommend triangular solves over explicit inversion.  
- Correct or justify the eigenvalue claim and add finite-time error bounds or empirical CSA norm-error plots.  
- Include a real-world application experiment and detailed runtime component breakdowns; discuss hybridization with limited-memory models.  

Recommendation
- Lean accept: valuable, implementable improvement with solid theory and convincing experiments; address clarity and stability concerns to strengthen the paper.
