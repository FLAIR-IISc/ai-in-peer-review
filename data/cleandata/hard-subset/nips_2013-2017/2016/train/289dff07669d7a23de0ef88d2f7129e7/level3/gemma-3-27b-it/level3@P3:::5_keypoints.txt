- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain/update Cholesky factor to reduce runtime from O(d³) to O(μd²).
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work but provides a theoretical justification under E_t → I.
- Well-motivated: targets CMA-ES’s computational bottleneck with links to information geometry and CSA analysis.
- Strength: significant contribution to speeding up a widely used optimizer.
- Strength: provides theoretical argument for asymptotic equivalence to full CMA-ES under specific conditions.
- Strength: empirical results show substantial speed-up in high dimensions without losing optimization performance on benchmarks.
- Strength: clear and well-organized presentation.
- Weakness: theoretical guarantee relies on strong assumption that E_t converges to identity, which may not hold in practice.
- Weakness: practical impact may be limited because function evaluation time often dominates and typical problem sizes are moderate.
- Weakness: incomplete baseline—missing wall-clock runtimes and full CMA-ES (no-delay) comparison.
- Weakness: experiments lack real-world case studies where speed is critical.
- Suggestion: track and report evolution of E_t (e.g., norms) by running full CMA-ES alongside Cholesky-CMA-ES.
- Suggestion: study performance under continuously varied spectra to assess behavior with different covariance structures.
- Suggestion: include a real-world experiment where optimization speed matters to demonstrate practical relevance.
- Overall: valuable and well-executed contribution but theoretical limitations and limited practical evaluation warrant caution.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions—add missing baseline results and discuss real-world limitations.
