This paper proposes a variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) that updates the covariance matrix via triangular Cholesky factors, reducing the update complexity and memory requirements. The approach effectively adapts the underlying metric and provides covariance eigenvalues without additional cost. The empirical results show that the proposed method, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, does not worsen the function-evaluation counts compared to the standard CMA-ES. Moreover, it demonstrates substantial wall-clock speedups, particularly in large-dimensional problems.

The paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated, making it a significant contribution to the field. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed method, its theoretical justification, and its advantages over existing approaches. The experimental results are comprehensive and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method in various benchmark functions.

The strengths of the paper include its originality, clarity, and significance. The proposed method addresses a crucial issue in the CMA-ES algorithm, which is the high computational cost of updating the covariance matrix. The authors provide a thorough analysis of the proposed method, including its theoretical justification and empirical evaluation. The paper is well-organized, and the writing is clear and concise.

One minor request is to clarify the implementation details to ensure that the CPU/time comparisons are comparable. Specifically, it would be helpful to know whether the same language and implementation were used for both the standard CMA-ES and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES.

In terms of significance, the paper addresses a crucial issue in the CMA-ES algorithm, which is widely used in machine learning and other fields. The proposed method has the potential to significantly improve the performance of the CMA-ES algorithm, particularly in large-dimensional problems.
