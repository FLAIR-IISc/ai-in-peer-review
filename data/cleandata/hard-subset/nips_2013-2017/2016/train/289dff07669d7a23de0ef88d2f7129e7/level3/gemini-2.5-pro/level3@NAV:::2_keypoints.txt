- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces O(d^3) eigen-decomposition with quadratic-time updates of the covariance matrix's Cholesky factor to improve scalability, building on prior acceleration efforts.  
- Strength: Clearly written and well-motivated; leveraging Cholesky updates addresses a known CMA-ES bottleneck and empirical results show promising speedups.  
- Weakness — Originality: Appears to be a trivial extension of Krause and Igel (2015) and does not convincingly articulate novel contributions.  
- Weakness — Technical soundness: Lacks a formal proof that the update is equivalent to the standard covariance update and provides insufficient validation of the claimed small/decreasing path error.  
- Weakness — Experimental validation: Comparison of final performance is insufficient; authors should track the path-error term during optimization to substantiate claims.  
- Weakness — Clarity and analysis: Complexity claims are imprecise (use Θ-notation), and the manuscript contains typos/notation errors (e.g., wk→α_k, n→d, F→f).  
- Recommendation: Not acceptable in current form due to limited originality and missing essential proofs and experimental validation.
