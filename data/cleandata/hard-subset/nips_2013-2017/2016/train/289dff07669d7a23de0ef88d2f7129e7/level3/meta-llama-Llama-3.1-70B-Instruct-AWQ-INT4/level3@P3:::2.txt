This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a widely used derivative-free optimization algorithm, to reduce its computational complexity. The authors replace the eigen-decomposition of the covariance matrix with a Cholesky factorization, which leads to a runtime complexity of O(µd^2) and optimal memory complexity.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear explanation of the CMA-ES algorithm and the proposed modification. The mathematical derivations are sound, and the authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm. The experimental results demonstrate a significant increase in optimization speed compared to the standard CMA-ES, while maintaining the same number of objective function evaluations.

However, the paper's originality is limited, as the idea of using Cholesky factorization in CMA-ES has been previously explored. The authors acknowledge this and provide a comparison with existing approaches, but the contribution of the paper lies mainly in the extension of the Cholesky update to the standard CMA-ES and the provision of a theoretical justification for its convergence.

A major concern is the lack of an equivalence proof between the proposed algorithm and the original CMA-ES. The authors claim that the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor is small and decreases over time, but this claim is not rigorously proven. Furthermore, the complexity claim of O(µd^2) is unclear, and the authors should use Θ-notation to provide a more accurate description of the algorithm's complexity.

Additionally, the paper's notation and formatting could be improved. There are several typos and inconsistencies in the notation, which may cause confusion for readers.

In terms of significance, the paper's contribution is mainly practical, as it provides a faster and more efficient implementation of the CMA-ES algorithm. However, the paper does not address any fundamentally new problems or provide new insights into the optimization process.

Overall, while the paper is well-written and provides a useful contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization, its limitations in originality, lack of equivalence proof, and unclear complexity claim make it not acceptable for NIPS in its current form.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper provides a clear and well-written explanation of the CMA-ES algorithm and the proposed modification.
* The experimental results demonstrate a significant increase in optimization speed compared to the standard CMA-ES.
* The paper provides a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm.

* The lack of an equivalence proof between the proposed algorithm and the original CMA-ES is a major concern.
* The complexity claim of O(µd^2) is unclear, and the authors should use Θ-notation to provide a more accurate description of the algorithm's complexity.
* The paper's notation and formatting could be improved.

Recommendation: The paper is not acceptable for NIPS in its current form. The authors should address the concerns mentioned above, provide a rigorous equivalence proof, and improve the notation and formatting before resubmitting the paper.
