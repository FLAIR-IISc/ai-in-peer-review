This paper proposes "Cholesky-CMA-ES," a modification of the standard Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy designed to improve computational efficiency. The central idea is to replace the expensive O(d^3) eigen-decomposition step with a direct, quadratic-time update of the covariance matrix's triangular Cholesky factor. This is intended to reduce the per-iteration time and storage complexity, making CMA-ES more scalable. This work builds upon previous efforts to accelerate evolution strategies, such as those by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015), by applying a similar Cholesky update strategy to the standard CMA-ES framework.

**Strengths:**
The paper is clearly written and the proposed method is well-motivated. The core idea of leveraging Cholesky updates to avoid full matrix decomposition is sound and addresses a known bottleneck in CMA-ES. The empirical results demonstrate promising speedups over reference implementations, suggesting that if the method's theoretical underpinnings are solidified, it could offer a significant practical benefit and potentially become a new standard for practitioners.

**Weaknesses:**
Despite its potential, the paper suffers from several major issues that prevent its acceptance in its current form.
*   **Originality:** The contribution appears to be a trivial extension of Krause and Igel's (2015) "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The paper does not sufficiently articulate its novelty in light of this closely related work.
*   **Technical Soundness:** The paper is missing crucial validation. A formal proof is required to show that the proposed update mechanism is mathematically equivalent to the standard covariance update in formula (2). Furthermore, the authors make a strong claim that the path error introduced by their approximation is small and decreases over time, which seems to contradict findings by Hansen. This claim is not substantiated with direct evidence; a simple comparison of final performance is insufficient. The authors should provide experimental validation that tracks this error term over the course of the optimization.
*   **Clarity:** The complexity analysis is imprecise. Claiming an O(μ d^2) runtime is not informative, as this is the same as the reference CMA-ES implementation. The authors should use Θ-notation and provide a more detailed analysis to explain precisely why their approach is faster. The manuscript also contains several typos and notational errors (e.g., wk should be α_k in Alg. 2; n should be d in Table 1; 'F' should be 'f' on line 185).

**Recommendation:**
Overall, while the direction is promising, the paper is not acceptable for NIPS in its current state due to concerns about limited originality and a lack of essential proofs and experimental validation for its core claims.
