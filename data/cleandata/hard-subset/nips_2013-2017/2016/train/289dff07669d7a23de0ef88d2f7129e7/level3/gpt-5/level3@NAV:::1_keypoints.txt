- Proposal: CMA-ES variant that maintains a triangular Cholesky factor, achieving optimal O(µ d^2) update complexity and minimal storage while preserving function-evaluation behavior.
- Key idea: replace CSA normalization C^{-1/2} with inverse Cholesky A^{-1}; rotational mismatch vanishes as covariance stabilizes (proved via RQ-factor lemma).
- Relation to prior work: extends delayed decompositions, diagonal/limited-memory CMA, and earlier Cholesky updates (Suttorp; Krause & Igel) to standard CMA-ES with theoretical justification.
- Relevance: Applicable to ML tasks (RL policy search, hyperparameter tuning) and connects to information-geometry–motivated updates.
- Derivation quality: Algorithmic derivation and numerical considerations are sound; rank-one updates for the triangular factor are clearly specified.
- CSA approximation: The argument for replacing CSA with A^{-1} is reasonable and empirically supported.
- Experiments: Rotated benchmarks up to d=256 show matched evaluation counts to reference CMA-ES and large wall-clock speedups, aligning with claimed complexity.
- Runtime concern: Details for fair runtime comparison are underspecified (same language/compiler, BLAS, threading, cache effects).
- Plot anomaly: Some plots (Cigar/Discus at d=256) do not show expected widening and need explanation (cache misses, delayed decompositions, or step-size behavior).
- Clarity/reproducibility: Paper is well organized with helpful algorithm boxes; reproducibility strong but needs clearer parameter defaults, seeding, BLAS/LAPACK usage, and code availability.
- Originality: Novel and nontrivial integration using triangular Cholesky updates to obtain optimal storage/time and “eigenvalues for free.”
- Significance: Substantial empirical and asymptotic gains in medium/large dimensions; a compelling default when full covariance adaptation is feasible.
- Pros: Optimal update/storage complexity, no degradation in function evaluations, free/stable eigenvalues, thorough evaluation, ML relevance.
- Cons: Need stronger clarity on runtime comparability and explanation for d=256 anomalies.
- Recommendation: Strong accept, pending clarification of implementation details and discussion of the d=256 anomalies.
