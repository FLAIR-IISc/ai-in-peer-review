- Proposes updating a triangular Cholesky factor of the CMA-ES covariance matrix to reduce per-iteration cost from O(d^3) to O(µd^2).
- Replaces C^{-1/2} in CSA with the inverse triangular factor A^{-1} and argues the approximation error vanishes as the covariance shape converges.
- Empirical results show significant wall-clock speedups with no increase in function evaluations on benchmark functions.
- Strength: addresses a major runtime bottleneck in CMA-ES, offering a practically useful speedup for full covariance adaptation.
- Strength: achieves O(µd^2) time and O(d^2) space complexity, which is asymptotically optimal for full-rank covariance updates.
- Strength: provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) supporting the CSA approximation.
- Weakness: novelty is limited—core triangular rank-one update is not new and the main contribution appears to be extending it to the rank-µ case via iteration.
- Weakness: inadequate engagement with prior work—insufficient discussion and comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015).
- Weakness: experimental evaluation is incomplete; lacks direct, detailed empirical comparison with Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel implementations.
- Weakness: some claims are overstated—“optimality” is only relative to full-rank methods and the claim of unchanged CMA-ES behavior is too strong given the approximation.
- Weakness: clarity issues (e.g., odd flattening in Figure 1, oversimplification about eigen decomposition frequency) that need clarification.
- Suggestion: rename the algorithm to avoid confusion with Suttorp et al. (2009) (e.g., “Triangular Cholesky CMA-ES”).
- Suggestion: strengthen related work and explicitly frame the contribution as extending triangular updates to the rank-µ case and justify triangular vs non-triangular factors.
- Suggestion: expand experiments to implement and compare against Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015), and provide runtime/convergence trade-offs.
- Suggestion: moderate and qualify claims about optimality and numerical stability, and provide evidence if asserting instability of other methods.
- Overall: practically useful and well-motivated but insufficiently novel and experimentally compared to prior work, preventing acceptance in current form; addressing naming, positioning, and experiments would substantially improve the paper.
