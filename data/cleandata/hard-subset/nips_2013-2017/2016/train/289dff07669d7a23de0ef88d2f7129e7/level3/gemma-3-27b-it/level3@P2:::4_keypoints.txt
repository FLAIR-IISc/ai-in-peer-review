- Proposes replacing the costly matrix square root in CMA-ES with an update of the triangular Cholesky factor to reduce runtime.  
- Motivation is strong: matrix square root dominates runtime in high-dimensional settings.  
- Authors argue the change preserves algorithmic behavior, supported by theoretical justification (Lemma 1) about coordinate rotations.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based attempts (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) and extends applicability to standard CMA-ES.  
- Paper is well-structured with clear background on Cholesky decomposition and its link to matrix square roots.  
- Derivation of the Cholesky update rule appears rigorous and technically sound.  
- The approach is original in combining Cholesky updates with standard CMA-ES and providing theoretical correctness.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough across benchmark functions and dimensions, showing runtime improvements without extra function evaluations.  
- Significant runtime gains, especially at higher dimensions, with optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity.  
- Potential impact is high for large-scale optimization tasks in ML (policy search, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Claim of “numerical stability” is underdeveloped; more explanation and analysis of mitigated numerical issues and possible downsides are needed.  
- Scaling to very high dimensions needs deeper discussion; authors note d=256 degradation due to L2 cache and should consider low-dimensional approximations.  
- Rotation invariance is theoretically addressed but empirically insufficiently explored; more experiments under varied rotations would strengthen the claim.  
- Overall: strong, practical, and well-executed contribution with minor gaps in numerical stability and extreme-scale discussion; reviewer recommends strong accept.
