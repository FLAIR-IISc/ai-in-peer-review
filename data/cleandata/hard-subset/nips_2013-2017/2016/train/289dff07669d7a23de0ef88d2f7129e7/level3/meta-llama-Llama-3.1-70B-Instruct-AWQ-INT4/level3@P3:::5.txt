The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, which is a widely used derivative-free optimization method. The main contribution of the paper is the introduction of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, which approximates the original CMA-ES with a reduced time complexity of O(μd^2) compared to the original O(d^3) complexity.

The key idea behind the Cholesky-CMA-ES is to maintain and update a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix. This approach is motivated by the fact that the Cholesky factorization is a more efficient way to represent the covariance matrix, especially in high-dimensional spaces. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, showing that the error introduced by the approximation decreases over time as the algorithm converges to the optimum.

The paper presents empirical results comparing the Cholesky-CMA-ES to the original CMA-ES and two other state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants. The results show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a significant speed-up in optimization time while maintaining the same level of optimization performance as the original CMA-ES. The authors also demonstrate the scalability of the Cholesky-CMA-ES by applying it to a range of benchmark functions with varying dimensions.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and its theoretical justification. The empirical results are also well-presented and provide a thorough evaluation of the proposed method.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel modification to the CMA-ES algorithm that reduces its time complexity.
* The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the Cholesky-CMA-ES.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES in achieving a significant speed-up in optimization time while maintaining the same level of optimization performance as the original CMA-ES.

Weaknesses:

* The paper assumes that the objective function evaluations are cheap and the dimensionality is high, which may not always be the case in real-world applications.
* The authors do not provide a thorough analysis of the limitations of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, such as its sensitivity to the choice of parameters and its performance on non-separable problems.
* The paper could benefit from additional experiments and comparisons with other optimization methods to further demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES.

Suggestions for future work:

* Investigate the performance of the Cholesky-CMA-ES on real-world problems with high-dimensional search spaces.
* Analyze the sensitivity of the Cholesky-CMA-ES to the choice of parameters and its performance on non-separable problems.
* Compare the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other optimization methods, such as quasi-Newton methods and gradient-based methods, to further demonstrate its effectiveness.
