- Proposes replacing the covariance square-root with a triangular Cholesky factor for CMA-ES, yielding a more efficient update scheme.  
- Claims time complexity of updates becomes O(µ d^2) (µ = number of samples, d = dimension) and reduces storage.  
- Provides theoretical justification for convergence of the modified algorithm.  
- Empirical results show similar numbers of function evaluations but significantly faster wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Naming the variant "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009).  
- Omits discussion and empirical comparison with Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES (2009).  
- Omits algorithmic and empirical comparison with Krause et al. (2015).  
- Main novelty appears to be looping the rankOneUpdate across individuals, which may be incremental.  
- Experiments are incomplete and should include comparisons to Suttorp and Krause variants and updated figures.  
- The claim of "optimality" is overstated and not valid for all problem classes (e.g., separable functions).  
- Asserts Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable, contradicting Suttorp’s original claims and needing evidence.  
- Complexity remains quadratic in d, so the approach does not fundamentally resolve large-scale CMA-ES limitations.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly organized, and explains the modification and theory clearly.  
- Empirical evidence supports faster runtimes but lacks breadth of baseline comparisons.  
- Recommendation: clarify contributions, temper optimality/stability claims, and run additional experiments comparing prior work.
