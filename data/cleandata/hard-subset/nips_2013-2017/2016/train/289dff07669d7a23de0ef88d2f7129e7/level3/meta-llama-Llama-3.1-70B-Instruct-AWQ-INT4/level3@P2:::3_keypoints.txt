- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains triangular Cholesky factors instead of the full covariance matrix to reduce time and space complexity.  
- Uses rank-1 updates on the Cholesky factor enabling quadratic-time updates.  
- Modifies cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) by replacing the inverse sqrt covariance with the inverse triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Provides theoretical analysis showing the modification does not significantly change algorithm behavior assuming covariance convergence.  
- Empirical experiments on benchmark functions demonstrate effectiveness, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- Clear presentation and well-written exposition with a sound technical approach.  
- Contribution is limited because the rank-μ extension is a straightforward generalization of prior work.  
- Lacks rigorous theoretical support for the covariance convergence assumption specific to their setting.  
- Minor clarity issues: inconsistent notation in Equation (1) and a small typo.
