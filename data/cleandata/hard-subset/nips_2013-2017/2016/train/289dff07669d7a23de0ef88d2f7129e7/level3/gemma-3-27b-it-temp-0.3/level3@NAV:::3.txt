## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper proposes a modification to the standard Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) by utilizing a Cholesky factorization for covariance matrix updates, aiming to reduce both runtime and memory complexity. The authors argue that replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor, coupled with a modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA), doesn’t significantly alter the algorithm’s behavior. They provide a theoretical justification based on the convergence of the Cholesky factor and demonstrate empirical performance gains, particularly in higher dimensions.

The work builds upon previous efforts to improve CMA-ES computational efficiency, specifically referencing Krause and Igel (2015) and Suttorp et al. (2009). It addresses a well-known bottleneck in CMA-ES – the cost of covariance matrix maintenance – and offers a potentially valuable optimization for large-scale problems. The core idea of leveraging the Cholesky decomposition is sound, and the authors correctly identify the potential for quadratic time complexity. 

However, the paper’s contribution feels incremental. The extension to rank-μ updates is presented as a straightforward generalization of existing work, diminishing the perceived novelty. While the theoretical analysis regarding the convergence of the Cholesky factor is a positive step, it relies on the assumption of covariance convergence, which could be strengthened by referencing more established results in the field (e.g., Beyer, ECJ 2014). The empirical results are encouraging, showing speedups, but a more detailed comparison against other efficient CMA-ES variants (beyond those presented) would be beneficial.

**Strengths:**

*   Addresses a practical limitation of CMA-ES.
*   Presents a theoretically motivated approach.
*   Demonstrates empirical performance improvements, particularly with increasing dimensionality.
*   Clear presentation of the algorithm and its theoretical underpinnings.

**Weaknesses:**

*   Limited novelty – the rank-μ extension feels like a natural progression.
*   Theoretical justification could be bolstered by referencing stronger convergence results.
*   Comparison to a wider range of efficient CMA-ES variants would be valuable.

**Minor Issues:**

*   σ in the denominator of Eq. (1) should be \tilde{σ} (same error in footnote 2 on p.3).
*   Typo: delete extra "by" in line 94 ("which can by be motivated ..." → remove "by").

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

*   **Against:** The paper’s contribution is relatively modest, and the theoretical analysis could be more robust.
*   **For:** The work presents a practical optimization for a widely used algorithm, with clear theoretical justification and empirical validation.

**Overall:** I recommend **weak accept** with the caveat that the authors address the minor issues and potentially expand on the theoretical justification and comparative analysis. The work is a solid engineering contribution, but falls short of being a groundbreaking advance.
