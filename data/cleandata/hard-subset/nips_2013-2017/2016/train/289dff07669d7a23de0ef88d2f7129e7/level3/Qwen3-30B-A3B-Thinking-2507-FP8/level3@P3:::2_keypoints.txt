- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factors, claiming O(μd²) time and reduced memory while preserving performance.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based work (Krause & Igel 2015) and appears to be a straightforward extension rather than a major conceptual advance.
- Fails to provide a proof of equivalence to standard CMA-ES (formula 2), leaving the core correctness claim unverified.
- Complexity claim O(μd²) is unclear and not rigorously compared (use Θ-notation and compare to CMA-ES-Ref).
- Path-error claim ("small and decreases") is unvalidated over time; only final-performance comparisons are shown.
- Experimental results show reduced wall-clock time in high dimensions but similar function-evaluation performance, possibly due to cache effects.
- Theoretical justification for non-deterioration of performance is weak and depends on unproven convergence assumptions.
- Notation and typographical errors (e.g., wk→α_k, n→d, F→f) indicate sloppiness in the manuscript.
- Claims of superiority over existing variants like CMA-ES-Ref and Suttorp-CMA-ES are not convincingly supported.
- Strengths: numerically stable Cholesky approach, lower memory requirements, and promising wall-clock speedups for large problems.
- Weaknesses: missing equivalence proof, unclear/insufficient complexity analysis, inadequate distinction from prior work, and unvalidated empirical/theoretical claims.
- Recommendation: reject in current form; require substantial revisions including equivalence proof, rigorous complexity analysis, time-series validation of path error, and clearer novelty positioning.
