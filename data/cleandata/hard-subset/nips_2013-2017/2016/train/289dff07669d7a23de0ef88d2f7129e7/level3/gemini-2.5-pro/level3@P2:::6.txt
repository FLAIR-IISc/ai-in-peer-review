This paper proposes an efficient update scheme for the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a powerful derivative-free optimization algorithm. The main idea is to avoid the standard O(d^3) complexity of decomposing the covariance matrix in each iteration. Instead, the authors maintain and directly update a triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix, achieving a quadratic time complexity of O(µd^2). This requires a modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism, where the inverse of the matrix square root (C^{-1/2}) is replaced by the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor (A^{-1}). The authors provide a theoretical justification that this approximation is sound, arguing that as the shape of the covariance matrix converges, the error introduced by this substitution diminishes. Empirical results on several benchmark functions demonstrate that the proposed method achieves a significant speedup in wall-clock time compared to the standard CMA-ES, without negatively impacting the number of function evaluations required for convergence.

This work relates to a line of research aimed at reducing the computational complexity of CMA-ES. Previous work, such as Suttorp et al. (2009), also proposed updating a Cholesky factor directly, while Krause and Igel (2015) demonstrated a quadratic-time update for a triangular factor in the context of a simpler (1+1)-CMA-ES. The current paper extends this idea to the standard (µ, λ)-CMA-ES.

**Strengths**

*   **Significant Practical Improvement:** The paper addresses a well-known bottleneck in CMA-ES. The proposed "Cholesky-CMA-ES" provides a substantial, empirically verified speedup for problems where the covariance matrix update dominates runtime, without degrading optimization performance in terms of function evaluations. This is a valuable contribution for practitioners.
*   **Optimal Complexity:** The method achieves an O(µd^2) time and O(d^2) space complexity for updating a full covariance matrix, which is asymptotically optimal. Using a triangular factor is also memory-efficient.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a sound argument (Lemma 1) for why their approximation in the CSA update is reasonable. The reasoning that the error term vanishes as the covariance matrix shape converges is compelling and adds technical depth.

**Weaknesses**

*   **Originality and Incremental Contribution:** The paper's novelty appears limited and is not clearly articulated in relation to prior work. The core `rankOneUpdate` (Algorithm 2) for a triangular Cholesky factor is not new. The main algorithmic contribution seems to be extending the update from the rank-1 case (Krause and Igel, 2015) to the rank-µ case by applying it iteratively in a loop. The paper needs to better clarify what is novel beyond this extension.
*   **Insufficient Comparison with Prior Art:** The paper critically fails to adequately discuss and compare against the most relevant prior work.
    1.  **Suttorp et al. (2009):** This work already introduced an algorithm named "Cholesky CMA-ES" that updates a (non-triangular) Cholesky factor. The current paper adopts the same name, which is confusing and misleading. Furthermore, it makes an unsubstantiated claim that Suttorp's method is "prone to numerical instabilities" without providing evidence, while the original paper claimed the opposite. A thorough discussion of the pros and cons of a triangular vs. non-triangular factorization is missing.
    2.  **Krause and Igel (2015):** This paper is cited but its contribution is understated. Krause and Igel already proposed the quadratic-time update of the triangular Cholesky factor for a (1+1)-CMA-ES. The current work should be framed more explicitly as an extension of their method to the full (µ, λ)-CMA-ES.
*   **Incomplete Experimental Evaluation:** The experiments are insufficient to support the paper's claims of superiority. A direct and detailed empirical comparison with the methods of Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015) is necessary but absent. While a "Suttorp-CMA-ES" is included in the plots, its performance is not discussed in detail, making the comparison superficial.
*   **Overstated Claims:**
    1.  The claim of "optimal" complexity is context-dependent. For separable or spherical functions, methods that only adapt a diagonal covariance matrix or no matrix at all have linear O(d) complexity and would be far more efficient. The optimality is only with respect to methods that adapt a full-rank covariance matrix.
    2.  The claim that the algorithm "does not change the behavior of the CMA-ES" is strong. While empirically supported here, it is still an approximation, and its behavior in more complex scenarios is unknown.
*   **Clarity:** In Figure 1 (Cigar), the performance curve for CMA-ES/d flattens out, which is unusual. This might be an artifact of how the median is computed or plotted, but it requires clarification. Additionally, the statement that standard CMA-ES computes an eigenvalue decomposition at every iteration is an oversimplification; most implementations use a delay mechanism, as the authors correctly implement in their reference version.

**Detailed Comments and Suggestions for Improvement**

1.  **Resolve Naming Conflict:** The algorithm must be renamed to avoid confusion with Suttorp et al. (2009). "Triangular Cholesky CMA-ES" or a similar name would be more accurate and respectful of prior work.
2.  **Strengthen Related Work and Contribution:** The introduction and related work sections should be revised to clearly position this work relative to Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015). The authors should explicitly state that their main contribution is the extension of the triangular update to the rank-µ case and justify why the triangular approach is preferable to Suttorp's non-triangular one (e.g., uniqueness of the factor, direct access to eigenvalues).
3.  **Expand Experiments:** The experimental section must be extended to include a proper comparison with the Suttorp et al. (2009) algorithm. The authors should implement it as described in the original paper and provide a detailed analysis of the runtime and convergence trade-offs.
4.  **Moderate Claims:** Claims regarding "optimality" and "numerical stability" should be toned down and properly qualified. Any claim about the instability of other methods must be supported by evidence.

In summary, the paper presents a practically useful and well-motivated speedup for the CMA-ES. However, its significant overlap with existing work is not properly addressed, the novelty seems incremental, and the experimental comparison is incomplete. These issues of originality and scholarly context are serious and prevent a recommendation for acceptance in its current form. If the authors were to address the naming, properly situate their contribution, and provide a more comprehensive experimental comparison, the paper would be much stronger.
