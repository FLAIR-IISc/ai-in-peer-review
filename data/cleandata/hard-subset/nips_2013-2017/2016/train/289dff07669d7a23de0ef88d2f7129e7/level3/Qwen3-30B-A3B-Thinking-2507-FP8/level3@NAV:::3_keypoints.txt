- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce computational complexity.  
- Extends Krause and Igel (2015) rank-1 update approach to standard CMA-ES with rank-μ updates.  
- Shows modification to CSA does not significantly change behavior under covariance convergence assumptions.  
- Provides theoretical justification for the claim (Lemma 1).  
- Empirical results show comparable function evaluations with improved runtime complexity.  
- Clear exposition of algorithmic modifications.  
- Thorough experimental validation across multiple benchmark functions.  
- Computational complexity analysis is technically sound, demonstrating quadratic-time updates and optimal storage.  
- Effectively addresses practical covariance update costs in high-dimensional optimization.  
- Novelty is limited; extension to rank-μ is a straightforward generalization of prior work.  
- Paper does not sufficiently distinguish itself from prior literature in the area.  
- Lemma 1’s justification would be strengthened by citing Beyer (ECJ 2014) for covariance convergence.  
- Minor issues: Eq. (1) should use \tilde{σ} instead of σ, and typo on line 94 ("which can by be motivated").  
- Significance is constrained—more an implementation detail than a substantial advancement with limited evidence for broad adoption.  
- Overall technically sound and well-presented but lacks the originality expected for NIPS acceptance.
