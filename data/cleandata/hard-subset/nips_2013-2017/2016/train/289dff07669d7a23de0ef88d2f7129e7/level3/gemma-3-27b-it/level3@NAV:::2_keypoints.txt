- Proposes Cholesky-based CMA-ES update to accelerate covariance updates and reduce storage complexity.
- Paper is clearly written with logical presentation and detailed algorithms (Alg. 1 and 2).
- Motivation correctly identifies standard CMA-ES covariance update as a computational bottleneck.
- Approach closely resembles prior work (Krause & Igel), so originality is questionable.
- Authors downplay prior art and do not convincingly differentiate their contribution.
- Key claimed equivalence to standard CMA-ES lacks a rigorous proof; lemma about rotation convergence is insufficient.
- Avoiding polar decomposition to preserve rotation invariance introduces error that is not formally addressed.
- Complexity claim of O(µd^2) is stated but not properly justified; authors should use Θ-notation and explain practical speed advantages.
- Experimental results show wall-clock speedups but lack micro-benchmarks and detailed timing analysis to substantiate complexity claims.
- Claim that path error is small and decreasing (contrary to Hansen) is unvalidated over time; only final performance is shown.
- Minor notation and typographical errors: ‘wk’ → ‘α_k’ in Alg.2, ‘n’ → ‘d’ in Table 1, ‘F’ → ‘f’ on line 185.
- Pros: clear writing, potentially useful optimization, demonstrated wall-clock speedups on benchmarks.
- Cons: limited originality, missing equivalence proof, unclear complexity justification, unvalidated error claims, and minor errors.
- Overall recommendation: Reject; paper needs substantial revision to address the major theoretical and experimental shortcomings.
