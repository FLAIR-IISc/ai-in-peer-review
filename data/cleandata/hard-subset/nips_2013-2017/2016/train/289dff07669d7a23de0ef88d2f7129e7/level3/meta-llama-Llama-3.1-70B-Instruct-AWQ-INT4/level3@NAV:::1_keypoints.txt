- Proposes a novel Cholesky-based CMA-ES that updates covariance via triangular factors to reduce update complexity and memory for high-dimensional problems.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the modification preserves CMA-ES behavior in terms of required objective function evaluations.  
- Empirical results show substantial wall-clock speedup versus standard CMA-ES while keeping the same number of function evaluations.  
- Paper is well-written with clear explanations of concepts and the proposed method.  
- Experiments are thorough and include comparisons with other CMA-ES variants.  
- Strength: approach is theoretically sound and well-justified.  
- Strength: demonstrates significant practical wall-clock speedup for large-scale optimization.  
- Strength: provides thorough empirical evaluation and variant comparisons.  
- Weakness: insufficient analysis of parameter choices and sensitivity of the algorithm to these parameters.  
- Weakness: lacks a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed method.  
- Originality: presents a novel covariance update approach that is a meaningful contribution to optimization.  
- Significance: has potential impact for large-scale optimization applications, including machine learning and reinforcement learning.  
- Recommendation: overall positive — paper is a significant contribution and should be accepted.  
- Pro-accept: novel, theoretically sound approach; empirical wall-clock improvements; thorough evaluation and comparisons.  
- Con-accept: needs deeper computational-complexity analysis and parameter sensitivity insights.  
- Minor request: clarify implementation details to ensure fair CPU/time comparisons (same language/implementation?).  
- Minor request: explain why Figure 2 shows no pronounced differences for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
