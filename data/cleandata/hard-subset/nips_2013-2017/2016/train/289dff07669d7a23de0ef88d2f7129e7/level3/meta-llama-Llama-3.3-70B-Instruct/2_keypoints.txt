- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace eigen-decomposition with Cholesky to reduce time and storage complexity.  
- Claims improved asymptotic time O(µ d^2) for covariance update and optimal memory complexity while keeping same objective-evaluation performance.  
- Provides clear writing and theoretical justification for convergence of the modified algorithm.  
- Experiments show large speedups (up to ~20×) over standard CMA-ES in high-dimensional problems.  
- Originality is limited—appears to be a trivial/very similar extension of prior work.  
- Lacks a proof of algorithm equivalence to formula (2) and missing detailed discussion of computational complexity.  
- Claim that path error decreases over time contradicts Hansen’s claim and needs additional empirical validation beyond final-performance comparison.  
- Contains notation errors (e.g., wk→αk, n→d, F→f around line 185) that must be fixed.  
- Paper would benefit from a deeper discussion of implications and potential applications of the method.  
- Meets conference criteria for clarity, organization, and reproducibility for expert readers.  
- Acceptance pros: improved complexity, strong empirical speedups, clear exposition.  
- Acceptance cons: limited novelty, missing proofs/details, contradictory theoretical claim, and notation mistakes.
