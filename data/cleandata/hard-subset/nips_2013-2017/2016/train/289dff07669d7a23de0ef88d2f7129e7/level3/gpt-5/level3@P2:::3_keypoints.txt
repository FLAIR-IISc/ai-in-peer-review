- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) via O(d^2) rank-one updates, giving O(μ d^2) time and ~O(d^2/2) storage per iteration.  
- Replaces CSA’s C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, introducing a time-varying rotation Et and proving (Lemma 1) that if normalized shape matrices converge then rotation error vanishes.  
- Extends prior work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) by generalizing triangular rank-one updates to full rank-μ CMA-ES and adapting CSA.  
- Empirically matches function-evaluation efficiency of standard CMA-ES while achieving large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) on rotated noiseless benchmarks up to d=256.  
- Technical soundness: rank-μ triangular update derivation is correct and CSA modification justified conditionally; related analyses suggest shape stabilization supports the assumption.  
- Empirical evaluation: uses standard rotated benchmarks and reports both evaluations and runtime, but lacks noisy and ML-relevant tasks (e.g., policy search).  
- Numerical considerations: claims good numerical stability and efficient triangular inversion but should discuss safeguards near degeneracy (jitter, min variance).  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear with useful pseudocode and careful CSA discussion; a complexity table would aid non-experts.  
- Originality: incremental — main algorithmic idea follows prior work, novelty lies in generalization and the CSA approximation with Lemma 1.  
- Significance: practically important for ML/DFO by reducing runtime and storage while preserving evaluation efficiency; unlikely to change theory.  
- Pros for acceptance: practical, fast, storage-efficient variant; sound implementation; CSA approx justified and empirically validated; careful experiments and code integration.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited novelty; theoretical justification depends on unproven shape convergence; evaluations restricted to noiseless synthetic tasks; possible unfavorable baseline settings.  
- Suggested improvements: link Lemma 1 assumptions to existing CMA-ES convergence results or show diagnostics; quantify CSA approximation error during runs; add noisy/ML experiments; report per-iteration time breakdown and memory; clarify numerical safeguards.  
- Minor comments: notation typo for \tilde{σ} in Eq.(1) and footnote; small typo on line 94; explicitly define E{χ} (expected norm).  
- Overall recommendation: borderline reject (leaning reject) due to limited novelty, though practically valuable and could be strong with stronger theory and broader experiments.
