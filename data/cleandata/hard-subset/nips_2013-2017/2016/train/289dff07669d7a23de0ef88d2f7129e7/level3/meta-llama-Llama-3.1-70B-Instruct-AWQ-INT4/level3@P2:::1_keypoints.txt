- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a CMA-ES variant that updates covariance via triangular Cholesky factors.  
- Reduces update time complexity from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2) and lowers storage by storing only triangular Cholesky factors.  
- Targets large-scale, derivative-free optimization by addressing original CMA-ES computational bottleneck.  
- Provides a thorough theoretical analysis including a convergence proof.  
- Empirical results show similar number of function evaluations to original CMA-ES but substantially reduced wall-clock time.  
- Includes detailed comparisons with other CMA-ES variants to contextualize results.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and thoroughly evaluated.  
- Quality/Clarity/Originality/Significance scores: 9/10, 9/10, 8/10, 9/10.  
- Minor requests: clarify implementation details (language/platform) for CPU/time comparisons.  
- Minor request: explain why Figure 2 lacks pronounced differences for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and practical variant, solid theoretical backing, strong empirical speed-up.  
- Arguments against acceptance: focus on a specific CMA-ES variant may limit broad appeal; benchmarks are limited.  
- Overall recommendation: accept.
