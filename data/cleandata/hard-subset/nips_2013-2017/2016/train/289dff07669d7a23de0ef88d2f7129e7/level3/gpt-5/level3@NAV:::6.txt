Summary and relation to prior work: The paper proposes a CMA-ES variant that maintains a triangular Cholesky factor A of the covariance (C = A A^T), applies rank-one updates directly to A, and approximates CSA by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}. This yields O(µ d^2) time and optimal storage for full-covariance CMA-ES, with empirical results suggesting similar evaluation counts and reduced wall-clock time versus a reference implementation with periodic eigendecompositions. The idea builds on Suttorp et al. (2009), who introduced Cholesky-based updates for (1+1)-CMA-ES and MO-CMA-ES, and on Krause & Igel (2015), who provided an efficient rank-one update; the present paper extends these to standard CMA-ES with µ > 1 and modifies CSA.

Strengths: clear motivation; practical engineering value for full-covariance CMA-ES; readable derivation; plausible heuristic justification (Lemma 1) for the CSA approximation; promising speedups; implementation details likely useful to practitioners.

Weaknesses and requested changes:
- Naming: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009); please adopt a distinct name and acknowledge the lineage.
- Missing baselines: The paper omits empirical and algorithmic comparisons with Suttorp (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015). These should be included (full plots can go to the supplement).
- Incremental contribution: Algorithmically, the novelty appears to be looping rankOneUpdate over all µ selected individuals plus the CSA replacement. Please state this explicitly or justify that the contribution is more than this.
- Quality: The “optimal” claim is overstated. For separable or near-spherical functions, diagonal/low-rank variants suffice; the proposed method is still quadratic and only about 2× cheaper than Suttorp’s non-triangular scheme. The scalability story remains unchanged; please temper claims and discuss LM-CMA/diagonal alternatives.
- Numerical stability: The manuscript asserts Suttorp is numerically unstable, while Suttorp et al. claim the opposite. Provide evidence (stress tests, conditioning plots) or retract.
- Experiments: Add Suttorp’s and Krause’s variants as baselines; provide ablations isolating the CSA approximation (e.g., occasional polar decomposition) and report effects on evaluation counts. Clarify the plotting protocol; the Cigar plateau difference may stem from median aggregation details.
- Clarity/accuracy: Correct that eigendecompositions are typically performed periodically, not every update in practice. Improve the exposition to make the main change (the “for” loop in Algorithm 1 and CSA modification) explicit.

Pros:
- Practical speedups for full-covariance CMA-ES
- Clear, implementable update rules

Cons:
- Naming conflict and missing related-work positioning
- Incomplete baselines and ablations
- Overstated claims about optimality and stability

Significance: Moderate if positioned as a solid engineering improvement; currently limited by incomplete comparisons. I would lean toward rejection unless the above fixes are addressed.
