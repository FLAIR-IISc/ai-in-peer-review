- Proposes replacing covariance matrix square root with a Cholesky factor in CMA-ES, claiming optimal asymptotic storage/runtime with similar performance.
- Provides theoretical justification that replacing C^(-1/2) with A^(-1) (C = AA^T) has limited performance impact (Lemma 1).
- Empirical results show speed improvements for larger dimensions compared to a reference CMA-ES implementation.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based work by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015) but conflicts with Suttorp’s established naming ("Cholesky-CMA-ES").
- Main algorithmic change appears incremental: looping the rankOneUpdate for all individuals (Algorithm 1 "for" loop).
- Fails to compare empirically against Suttorp's and Krause's Cholesky-based variants, omitting directly relevant baselines.
- Claims of "optimal" O(µd^2) complexity are overstated, as Suttorp’s approach achieves similar asymptotic complexity.
- Reported speedup over prior Cholesky methods is modest (~2×), not a dramatic improvement.
- Incorrectly claims superiority for non-separable ill-conditioned problems while ignoring that simpler diagonal approaches suffice for separable functions.
- Makes an unsupported claim that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable, contradicting Suttorp et al.’s own assertions.
- Experimental presentation is incomplete/misleading: missing baselines, potential plotting artifacts (median/plateau on Cigar), and incorrect statements about eigenvalue decomposition frequency.
- Strengths: clear algorithm description, reasonable theoretical justification for the approximation, and observed empirical speedups in high dimensions.
- Quality: technical claims not fully supported due to factual errors and incomplete empirical evaluation.
- Clarity: algorithm and theory are well explained but results presentation is misleading without proper comparisons.
- Originality: contribution is incremental rather than novel, insufficiently distinguished from prior work.
- Significance: modest practical speedup and no fundamental scalability breakthrough; significance is overstated.
- Recommendation: reject in current form; paper needs renaming, inclusion of Suttorp/Krause comparisons, clearer framing of the contribution, and correction of factual errors.
- Suggested revisions: resolve naming conflict, add empirical comparisons to prior Cholesky variants, acknowledge the "for" loop as main contribution, and correct claims about numerical stability and eigen-decomposition.
