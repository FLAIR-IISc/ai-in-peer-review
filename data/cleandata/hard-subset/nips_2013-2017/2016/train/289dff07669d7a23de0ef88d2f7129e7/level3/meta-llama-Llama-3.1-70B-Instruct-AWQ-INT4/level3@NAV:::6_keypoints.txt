- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES using a triangular Cholesky factor to update the covariance, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μd^2) with theoretical convergence and benchmarks.  
- Technically sound: theoretical analysis and experiments support claims, but has naming conflict with Suttorp et al. (2009) and omits comparisons with relevant prior work.  
- Well-written and easy to follow, though some mathematical derivations need clearer presentation.  
- Novelty: triangular Cholesky factorization applied to covariance updates, but main idea may be just looping rank-one updates, which is potentially incremental.  
- Shows significant speedups over standard CMA-ES, but the “optimality” claim is overstated and not true for all functions (e.g., separable functions).  
- Scalability claim is questionable: complexity remains quadratic in d, so scalability benefits are limited.  
- Recommendation: resolve the naming conflict with Suttorp et al. (2009).  
- Recommendation: run experiments including Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause et al. (2015) variants for empirical comparison.  
- Recommendation: better clarify and justify the contribution beyond a simple loop of rank-one updates.  
- Recommendation: correct or tone down the “optimality” claim and provide a nuanced discussion of strengths and weaknesses.  
- Recommendation: address scalability concerns and provide a more detailed complexity analysis.  
- For acceptance: novel covariance update approach, demonstrated speed improvements, and clear exposition.  
- Against acceptance: unresolved naming conflict, missing related-work comparisons, limited novelty, and overstated claims on optimality and scalability.
