- Paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a triangular Cholesky factor A via rank-one updates instead of eigendecomposition, with a CSA modification using A−1 for step-size control.  
- Claimed benefits: O(μ d^2) update time, reduced memory, and substantial wall-clock speedups while matching CMA-ES in function evaluations.  
- Prior work overlap: Cholesky-update idea is long-known (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015); novelty over FOGA’15 and others is not clearly delineated.  
- Strength: clear writing, good motivation for avoiding repeated eigendecompositions.  
- Strength: practical relevance—triangular factor updates can yield real engineering speedups and better memory locality.  
- Strength: empirical results show similar evaluations to CMA-ES and much lower wall time as d grows.  
- Strength: triangular factor choice gives numerical stability, cheap inversion, and eigenvalue access via the diagonal.  
- Weakness: missing formal proof that the sequence of rank-one updates to A is equivalent to the canonical rank-1 + rank-μ covariance update (Eq. (2)).  
- Weakness: complexity claims are vague—O(μ d^2) is insufficient; detailed Θ-notation operation counts and comparisons to amortized eigendecomposition baselines are required.  
- Weakness: need to explain why the method is faster in practice when asymptotic costs appear comparable (constant factors, cache behavior, avoidance of O(d^3) spikes).  
- Weakness: “asymptotically optimal” claim needs justification or a formal lower bound (e.g., Ω(μ d^2)).  
- Weakness: central theoretical justification for CSA modification (path-error small and decreasing) is unvalidated empirically and needs time-series metrics.  
- Required experiments: report ‖pσ,t‖/E{χ} vs standard CSA, rotation drift ‖Et ETt−1 − I‖, and correlations across functions (including Rosenbrock, noisy settings).  
- Baselines: clarify and broaden baselines (CMA-ES with eigendecomposition every Θ(d) iterations, amortized implementations), provide per-iteration cost breakdowns, and justify delay schedules used.  
- Reproducibility: supply code, seeds, hardware, compiler flags, and exact parameter settings for wall-clock comparisons.  
- Specific suggestions: provide a formal equivalence theorem, numerical stability bounds, and a bound relating ŝt − st to rotation drift and exponential averaging.  
- Experiments should report time-series metrics for path error, rotation drift, and conditioning, and include noisy/constrained problems.  
- Pros summary: clear exposition, promising speedups, and sound engineering approach.  
- Cons summary: incremental novelty, missing equivalence proof, insufficient complexity analysis, and unvalidated central claim about diminishing path error.  
- Minor issues: notation/typos (Algorithm 2 wk→αk; Table 1 use d not n; Line 185 ‘F’→‘f’), inconsistent symbols (μeff, weights, yi vs xi − m normalized), and prefer Θ(·) over O(·).  
- Overall recommendation: not ready for acceptance—strengthen theoretical equivalence, complexity justification, novelty clarification, and add requested time-series validation for resubmission.
