- Proposes maintaining and updating a triangular Cholesky factor (A) of the covariance matrix to avoid O(d^3) eigen-decomposition, reducing update cost to O(μd^2).
- Replaces C^{-1/2} in CSA with A^{-1}, with Lemma 1 arguing the approximation error diminishes as covariance converges.
- Empirically shows similar function-evaluation convergence to standard CMA-ES but with significant wall-clock speedups.
- Strength: tackles an important scalability problem and offers a valid, practically faster Cholesky-based update.
- Strength: provides a plausible theoretical justification (Lemma 1) for the CSA approximation.
- Weakness: Claims of being "asymptotically optimal" in time and memory are misleading given remaining O(μd^2) complexity and superior O(d) options for special cases.
- Weakness: Assertion that non-triangular factorization methods are "prone to numerical instabilities" is unsubstantiated and contradicts prior claims in Suttorp et al. (2009).
- Weakness: Fails to adequately compare or empirically evaluate against closely related prior work (notably Suttorp et al. 2009 and Krause & Igel 2015).
- Originality concern: Updating a Cholesky factor is not new and the name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009), causing potential confusion.
- Clarity: Paper is generally well-written, but the introduction misstates how frequently expensive eigen-decompositions are performed in practice.
- Clarity: Convergence plots (e.g., Figure 1) may be misleading; plotting methodology and possible failed runs need clarification.
- Significance: Could be important for ML applications if superiority over existing efficient CMA-ES variants is demonstrated.
- Against acceptance: Major shortcomings in engagement with prior work, misleading naming, incomplete experiments, and overstated claims.
- For acceptance: Presents a sensible idea with notable practical speedups and plausible theory.
- Recommended fix: Rename the algorithm to avoid conflict with Suttorp et al. (2009).
- Recommended fix: Include implementations and empirical comparisons to Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and discuss Krause et al. (2015).
- Recommended fix: Clearly state and justify the core algorithmic novelty (e.g., the rank-μ update decomposition).
- Recommended fix: Tone down or contextualize "optimality" claims and either justify or remove the numerical instability claim.
- Recommended fix: Clarify plotting methodology, include full convergence plots, and correct statements about eigen-decomposition frequency.
