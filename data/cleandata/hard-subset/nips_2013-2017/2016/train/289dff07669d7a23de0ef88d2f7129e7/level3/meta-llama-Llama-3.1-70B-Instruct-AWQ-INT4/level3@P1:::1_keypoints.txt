- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates covariance via triangular Cholesky factors, reducing update complexity and memory for large-scale problems.  
- Provides theoretical analysis proving the step-size adaptation approximation error is small and decreases as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirically evaluated on benchmarks: Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, and Diffpowers.  
- Achieves the same number of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES but with significantly reduced wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Presents Cholesky-CMA-ES as a promising default for medium-to-large-scale CMA-ES applications.  
- Paper is well-written with clear presentation of theoretical and empirical results and thorough related-work discussion.  
- Strength: novel and efficient covariance update yielding lower complexity and memory use.  
- Strength: thorough theoretical proof uncommon in optimization literature.  
- Strength: strong empirical evidence of runtime improvements without loss in evaluation efficiency.  
- Weakness: unclear implementation details—need confirmation that CPU/time comparisons use comparable implementations/languages.  
- Weakness: insufficient explanation for why speedup is less significant on Cigar and Discus at dimension 256 (Figure 2).  
- Originality: distinct approach from previous CMA-ES variants with substantial theoretical backing.  
- Significance: potential wide impact across machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Overall quality: high-quality contribution with thorough analysis and evaluation.
