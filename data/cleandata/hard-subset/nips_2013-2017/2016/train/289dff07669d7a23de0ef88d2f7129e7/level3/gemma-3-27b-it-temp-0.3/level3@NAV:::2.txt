## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) by replacing the standard eigenvalue decomposition with a Cholesky factorization for updating the covariance matrix. The core idea is to achieve a faster runtime, specifically O(μd^2), and reduced memory footprint, particularly relevant for high-dimensional optimization problems. The authors argue that this approach maintains the performance characteristics of the original CMA-ES, validated through empirical results.

The proposed method builds upon previous work exploring Cholesky updates within CMA-ES variations (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015). However, the authors aim to extend this to the full CMA-ES, addressing the challenge of maintaining the algorithm’s theoretical properties. The paper clearly outlines the theoretical justification for the modification, focusing on the impact of replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor in the step-size adaptation. 

The strengths of the paper lie in its clear writing and the reasonable method proposed. The potential for speedups by leveraging Cholesky decomposition is promising and could indeed become a standard practice if the claims hold. The experiments demonstrate a significant reduction in runtime, especially as dimensionality increases, supporting the authors' claims.

However, the paper suffers from several weaknesses. The originality is limited; the approach feels like a relatively straightforward extension of existing work, specifically resembling “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies.” Crucially, the authors *must* provide a proof demonstrating the equivalence of their algorithm to formula (2) in the original CMA-ES paper (Hansen & Ostermeier, 2001). Even if this equivalence has been proven elsewhere, it needs to be explicitly stated and referenced. 

The claim of O(μd^2) complexity is unclear. Using Θ-notation is essential, and a detailed explanation of why this method is *actually* faster than CMA-ES-Ref is required. The authors also claim a small and decreasing path error, contrary to findings by Hansen. This claim requires rigorous experimental validation *over time*, not just a comparison of final performance. 

Finally, there are minor notation and typo errors (wk should be α_k, n should be d, and 'F' should be 'f' on line 185).

**Overall Recommendation:** This paper is **not acceptable** for NIPS in its current form. The missing equivalence proof, unclear complexity analysis, and unvalidated path-error claim are significant concerns. Addressing these issues, along with correcting the minor errors, would substantially improve the paper's quality and potential for acceptance.
