The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" proposes a new variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, called Cholesky-CMA-ES. The main contribution of this work is the development of a numerically stable quadratic-time covariance matrix update scheme with minimal memory requirements based on maintaining triangular Cholesky factors. This approach reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm from O(d^3) to O(μd^2), where μ is the number of samples used to update the covariance matrix.

The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical results on standard benchmark functions. The results show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES but is significantly faster in terms of wall-clock time, especially for high-dimensional problems.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical foundation. The empirical results are also well-presented, and the authors provide a thorough comparison with other state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants.

Strengths of the paper:

* The proposed algorithm has a significant impact on the runtime complexity of the CMA-ES, making it more suitable for high-dimensional problems.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm.
* The empirical results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm and its superiority over other state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants.

Weaknesses of the paper:

* The authors assume that the objective function evaluation time is negligible compared to the time complexity of the algorithm. However, in many real-world applications, the objective function evaluation time can be significant, and the proposed algorithm may not provide a substantial speed-up.
* The authors do not provide a detailed analysis of the memory requirements of the proposed algorithm, which can be an important consideration for large-scale optimization problems.
* The authors do not explore the application of the proposed algorithm to real-world problems, which can provide more insight into its practical effectiveness.

Suggestions for future work:

* Investigate the application of the proposed algorithm to real-world problems, such as training neural networks or optimizing complex systems.
* Analyze the memory requirements of the proposed algorithm and compare them with other state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants.
* Explore the possibility of combining the proposed algorithm with other optimization techniques, such as gradient-based methods or surrogate-based methods, to further improve its performance.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of optimization and provides a promising new direction for improving the efficiency of the CMA-ES algorithm. However, further work is needed to fully explore its potential and limitations.
