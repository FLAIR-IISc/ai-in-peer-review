- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains the triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance update cost from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2).
- Builds on Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015), extending the rank-1 Cholesky update to the full rank-μ CMA-ES.
- Main technical challenge is adapting CSA, which needs C^{-1/2}; authors approximate this by A^{-1} (inverse Cholesky) and prove (Lemma 1) the approximation error diminishes as covariance converges.
- Primary strength is practical significance: removes a well-known O(d^3) bottleneck with tangible speed-ups.
- Experiments convincingly show wall-clock speed improvements over references without degrading function-evaluation performance.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides a complete algorithm ready for practitioners.
- Novelty is limited: extension from rank-1 to rank-μ is a relatively straightforward generalization of prior work.
- Theoretical justification relies on a strong assumption of covariance matrix convergence, which is questionable for stochastic algorithms and should be acknowledged.
- Recommend citing supporting theoretical results (e.g., convergence of E[C_t], Beyer ECJ 2014) to bolster the convergence assumption.
- Minor issues: σ in Eq.(1) and footnote 2 should be \tilde{σ}; typo in line 94 ("which can by be motivated...").
- Overall: valuable, practical contribution likely to become a standard implementation for medium-to-large scale problems despite modest theoretical novelty.
