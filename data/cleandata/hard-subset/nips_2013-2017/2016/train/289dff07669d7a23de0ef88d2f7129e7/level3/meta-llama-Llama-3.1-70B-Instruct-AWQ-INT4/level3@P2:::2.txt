This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, replacing the eigenvalue decomposition with a Cholesky factorization to update the covariance matrix. The authors claim that this modification, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, achieves a better time complexity of O(μd^2) and optimal memory complexity.

The paper is well-written, and the method is reasonably clear. The idea of replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factorization is promising and could potentially lead to significant speedups. However, the originality of the paper is limited, as it appears to be a trivial extension of previous work, specifically "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies" by Krause and Igel.

A major concern is that the authors do not provide a proof of equivalence between their algorithm and the original CMA-ES formula (2). This is essential, even if a similar proof has been presented previously. Without this proof, it is unclear whether the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm is indeed equivalent to the original CMA-ES.

Furthermore, the authors' claim of achieving a better time complexity of O(μd^2) is unclear. They should use Θ-notation to provide a more precise analysis of the time complexity. Additionally, they should explain in detail why their method is actually faster than the original CMA-ES-Ref. A simple statement that the Cholesky-CMA-ES is faster is not sufficient.

Another concern is the claim that the path error is small and decreases over time. This claim requires experimental validation, not just a final-performance comparison. The authors should provide empirical evidence to support this claim, as it is contrary to previous findings by Hansen.

There are also some minor issues with notation and typos. For example, "wk" in Algorithm 2 should be "α_k", "n" in Table 1 should be "d", and "'F'" should be "'f'" on line 185.

In conclusion, while the idea of replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factorization is promising, the paper is not acceptable for NIPS in its current form. The authors need to address the concerns mentioned above, including providing a proof of equivalence, clarifying the time complexity claim, and validating the path error claim.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The idea of replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factorization is promising and could potentially lead to significant speedups.
* The paper is well-written, and the method is reasonably clear.

Arguments con acceptance:

* Limited originality, as the paper appears to be a trivial extension of previous work.
* Lack of proof of equivalence between the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and the original CMA-ES formula (2).
* Unclear time complexity claim, which requires more precise analysis and explanation.
* Unvalidated path error claim, which requires experimental evidence.
* Minor issues with notation and typos.

Recommendation: Reject, but encourage the authors to revise and resubmit.
