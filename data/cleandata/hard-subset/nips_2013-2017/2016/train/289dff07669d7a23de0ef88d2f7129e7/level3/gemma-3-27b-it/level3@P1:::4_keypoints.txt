- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update and store the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix to replace matrix square roots in CMA-ES.  
- Achieves quadratic-time covariance updates, reducing computational cost especially in high dimensions versus eigen/sqrt-based updates.  
- Provides theoretical justification that the modification does not change required objective function evaluations.  
- Empirical results show significant wall-clock speedups at higher dimensions while maintaining comparable function-evaluation performance.  
- Positions work within prior CMA-ES research and distinguishes from earlier Cholesky attempts by applying to standard CMA-ES.  
- Clearly identifies the covariance-update bottleneck and offers an elegant, practical solution.  
- Theoretical treatment of modified step-size adaptation helps allay performance concerns.  
- Experiments are convincing and the paper is well-written with sufficient reproducibility details.  
- Claim of improved "numerical stability" is asserted but not thoroughly substantiated or detailed.  
- Limited analysis of convergence behavior beyond achieving target function values.  
- Experimental suite could be broader (e.g., constrained problems and more diverse problem types).  
- Scalability discussion is limited; practical issues (cache limits at d=256) are noted but not deeply explored.  
- Suggests potential combination with low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to address extreme-high-dimension limits.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, efficient modification; strong theory; convincing empirical speedups; addresses key bottleneck; clear presentation.  
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient substantiation of numerical-stability claims; limited scalability discussion; need for more extensive experimental evaluation.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — contribution is significant and practical despite minor shortcomings.
