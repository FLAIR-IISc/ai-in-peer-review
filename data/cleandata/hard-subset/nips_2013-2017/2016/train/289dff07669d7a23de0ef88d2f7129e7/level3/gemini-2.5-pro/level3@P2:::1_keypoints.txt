- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance, reducing per-iteration time to O(µ d^2) and memory to O(d^2).  
- Replaces the CSA step's need for the inverse matrix square root with the inverse triangular Cholesky factor and argues the approximation error vanishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical results across benchmarks show significant wall-clock speedups in higher dimensions without increasing function evaluations to convergence.  
- Positions work within prior CMA-ES scaling efforts, identifying limitations of delayed updates and diagonal restrictions.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-factor ideas but provides the first practical CSA adaptation that preserves standard CMA-ES performance.  
- Technical contribution is sound; Lemma 1 convincingly justifies the CSA approximation.  
- Experimental methodology is rigorous, with comparisons to standard and state-of-the-art baselines across functions and dimensions.  
- Paper is clear and well-written; background, motivation, and technical challenges are well explained.  
- Inclusion of pseudocode (Algorithms 1 and 2) and clear figures aids reproducibility and understanding.  
- Originality lies in the novel CSA update for triangular Cholesky factorization and its theoretical justification.  
- Significance is high: makes full-rank CMA-ES practical in much higher dimensions and is likely to be widely adopted.  
- Strong arguments for acceptance: practical speedup, theoretical and empirical support, novelty, clarity, and high potential impact.  
- No substantive arguments against acceptance; reviewer lists only minor suggestions for improvement.  
- Suggested improvements: explicitly confirm all methods used the same implementation language and optimized linear algebra libraries for fair wall-clock comparisons.  
- Suggested improvements: briefly elaborate on observed plateau in performance between d=128 and d=256 (likely L2 cache effects) in the results section.
