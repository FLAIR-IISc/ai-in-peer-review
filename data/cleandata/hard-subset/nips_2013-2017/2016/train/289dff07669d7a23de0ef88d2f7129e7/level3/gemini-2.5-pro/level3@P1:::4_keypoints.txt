- Problem: CMA-ES has an O(d^3) bottleneck from decomposing the d×d covariance matrix to compute C^{-1/2} for CSA.  
- Contribution: Proposes an O(d^2) algorithm by maintaining and updating a triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T), avoiding full decomposition.  
- Key technical change: Replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in the CSA update to enable Cholesky updates.  
- Theoretical justification: Lemma 1 argues rotational difference between C^{1/2} and A vanishes as covariance shape converges, and CSA’s exponential averaging makes the approximation error decay.  
- Empirical results: Matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluation convergence while significantly reducing wall-clock time, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Novelty: Builds on prior Cholesky/rank-one ideas but is novel in successfully adapting them to standard CMA-ES with a principled CSA approximation.  
- Quality: Derivations are correct, experiments well-designed, and comparisons use appropriate baselines.  
- Clarity/reproducibility: Paper is well-written, organized, and provides enough detail for reproduction.  
- Significance: Makes full-covariance CMA-ES feasible for moderately high dimensions (e.g., d>100), likely useful to many practitioners.  
- Limitation: Still O(d^2), so not suitable for very high-dimensional problems (e.g., large neural networks) without further approximations.  
- Suggested future work: Combine Cholesky updates with limited-memory or low-rank approximations to improve scalability further.  
- Weakness: Claim of improved numerical stability is asserted but not theoretically or empirically substantiated; needs discussion or citation.  
- Theoretical caveat: The justification relies on the (reasonable but not guaranteed) assumption of covariance-shape convergence.  
- Acceptance arguments: Solves a long-standing bottleneck, is practical and empirically effective, and offers a novel theoretical insight enabling adoption.  
- Rejection/improvement arguments: Unsubstantiated numerical-stability claim and reliance on convergence assumption are the main weaknesses.
