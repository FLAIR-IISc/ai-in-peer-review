- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that reduces per-iteration complexity from O(d³) to O(μd²) by maintaining and updating a lower-triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Main algorithmic deviation: omit estimation of orthogonal matrix E_t in CSA and argue E_t → I under convergence, making the approximation asymptotically equivalent to CMA-ES.  
- Theoretical contribution: provides an argument for vanishing approximation error under restrictive convergence assumptions.  
- Empirical result: demonstrates substantial wall-clock runtime reduction with no measurable loss in optimization performance (function evaluations) on standard benchmarks.  
- Practical significance: makes full-covariance CMA-ES viable when function evaluations are cheap and dimensionality is large.  
- Weakness: theoretical guarantees depend on strong assumptions about covariance-shape convergence that are not validated empirically.  
- Suggestion: empirically track the omitted E_t (e.g., ||E_t − I||_F) by running full CMA-ES to verify the convergence claim.  
- Weakness: experimental baselines are incomplete; need wall-clock times for standard CMA-ES without delay strategies to clarify speed-up.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on problems with continuously varied spectral distributions (e.g., Stich & Müller 2012) to test robustness under different conditioning.  
- Weakness: practical impact is under-motivated because many real-world tasks are dominated by evaluation cost.  
- Suggestion: include at least one real-world experiment (e.g., real-time policy search in robotics) where the internal speed-up is critical.
