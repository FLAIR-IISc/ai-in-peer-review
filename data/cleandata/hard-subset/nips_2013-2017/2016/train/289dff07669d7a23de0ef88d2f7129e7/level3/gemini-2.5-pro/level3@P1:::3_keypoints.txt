- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor A to avoid storing/decomposing full covariance C.  
- Replaces the O(d³) covariance decomposition with O(µ d²) time and O(d²) storage for updates.  
- Extends Krause & Igel (2015) rank-1 Cholesky update to the full rank-µ CMA-ES update via a sequence of rank-1 updates.  
- Replaces the CSA-required C⁻¹/² by A⁻¹ and provides Lemma 1 arguing this approximation is benign as the rotation between factors converges to identity.  
- Shows empirical results where Cholesky-CMA-ES requires similar function evaluations but yields substantial wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension.  
- Notes numerical benefits of Cholesky factor: stability, efficient inversion, and easy access to diagonal for eigenvalue/conditioning monitoring.  
- Strength: addresses a critical bottleneck, making CMA-ES practical for higher-dimensional, cheap-evaluation problems (e.g., some ML settings).  
- Strength: achieves asymptotically optimal update complexity and storage while remaining numerically stable.  
- Strength: provides a novel theoretical argument (Lemma 1) explaining why the CSA approximation does not harm performance in practice.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — extending rank-1 to rank-µ is a relatively straightforward generalization of prior work.  
- Weakness: theoretical argument relies on the assumption that normalized covariance C̄t converges, but the paper does not sufficiently justify or cite supporting convergence results.  
- Recommendation: cite convergence analyses (e.g., Beyer 2014) to strengthen confidence in Lemma 1’s assumption.  
- Minor issues: unclear notation for σt in Equation (1)/footnote (clarify which step-size is used), and a typo ("which can by be motivated" → "which can be motivated").
