- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) instead of C, avoiding eigendecompositions.  
- Complexity: Achieves O(μ d^2) per-iteration updates (μ = O(log d) typically) vs. ω(d^2)–O(d^3) for recomputing C^{1/2}.  
- CSA change: Uses A^{-1} to normalize steps instead of C^{-1/2}, omitting the orthogonal factor Et.  
- Theoretical claim: If the normalized covariance stabilizes, Et^T E_{t-1} → I and the CSA approximation error vanishes asymptotically.  
- Empirical results: On synthetic (rotated and unrotated) benchmarks up to d=256, shows large wall-clock speedups with no increase in function evaluations.  
- Baseline comparisons: Evaluated against reference CMA-ES with delayed decompositions and a non-triangular Cholesky-update variant.  
- Relation to prior work: Positioned among amortized/deferred decompositions, restricted models (diagonal/low-rank/LM), and factor-update methods (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel).  
- Strength: Clear algorithmic idea and solid engineering enabling optimal storage and quadratic-time rank-one triangular updates.  
- Strength: Theoretical rationale backed by a convergence lemma and exponentially decaying weights argument.  
- Strength: Comprehensive synthetic evaluation reporting both evaluations and wall-clock times.  
- Strength: Practical advantages include numerical stability, easy A^{-1} application in O(d^2), and tangible runtime gains.  
- Weakness: Theory depends on strong assumptions (normalized covariance convergence, smooth factor evolution) and offers no finite-time bounds.  
- Weakness: Potentially weakens affine/rotational invariance further than standard CMA-ES; lacks theoretical characterization.  
- Weakness: Empirical scope limited to synthetic functions; no noisy, constrained, or real-world benchmarks where runtime matters.  
- Weakness: Missing comparisons to important large-scale baselines (e.g., LM-CMA / LM-MA-ES) that trade model complexity for speed.  
- Technical error: Incorrect claim that Cholesky diagonals are square roots of eigenvalues—false unless C is diagonal.  
- Quality: Factor-update derivation and Algorithm 2 are technically sound and stable, but finite-time and non-convergent analyses are missing.  
- Empirical critique: Benchmarks support gains, but the paper should quantify CSA approximation error (angle/norm) across iterations.  
- Clarity: Generally readable; RQ/polar decomposition exposition is helpful.  
- Clarity issues: Must correct Cholesky/eigenvalue statement, fix references, state per-iteration costs including A^{-1} handling, and clarify preserved/ lost invariances.  
- Originality: Incremental but novel in extending triangular Cholesky updates to multi-parent CMA-ES with a justified CSA approximation.  
- Significance: Valuable for regimes with cheap evaluations and moderate-to-large d; limited-memory methods still needed for very large d or expensive evaluations.  
- Suggested experiments: Track ∥Et^T E_{t-1} − I∥ and CSA discrepancy, compare to LM-CMA, add at least one real-world/noisy benchmark, and study quadratics with controlled spectra.  
- Suggested analysis: Discuss invariance explicitly, attempt partial guarantees for small rotations, and clarify how eigenvalues would be obtained.  
- Pros for acceptance: Simple, implementable method with clear speedups, practical relevance, and theoretical motivation.  
- Cons against acceptance: Asymptotic-only guarantees, missing key baseline comparisons and real-world tests, and the noted technical misstatement.  
- Overall assessment: Strong engineering contribution with plausible theory and convincing synthetic results; requires the suggested corrections and additional experiments to be fully compelling.
