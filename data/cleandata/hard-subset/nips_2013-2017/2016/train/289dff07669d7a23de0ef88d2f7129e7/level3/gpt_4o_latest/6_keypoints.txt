- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: a quadratic-time covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors to reduce memory and computation while preserving CMA-ES behavior.  
- Claims "optimal covariance update and storage complexity" and reports significant empirical runtime improvements over standard CMA-ES.  
- Naming is misleading because a similar Cholesky-based approach was introduced by Suttorp et al. (2009), which is not cited.  
- Main algorithmic novelty is minor—an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015) by applying a rankOneUpdate loop to all individuals.  
- Evaluation omits comparisons (algorithmic and empirical) with Suttorp’s and Krause’s Cholesky-CMA-ES variants, weakening claims of superiority.  
- Claim of optimality is misleading: not optimal for separable problems or when a full covariance matrix is unnecessary.  
- Assertion that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable is made without sufficient evidence.  
- Empirical results show practical benefits and significant runtime gains, and the method is reportedly twice as efficient as Suttorp’s approach.  
- Despite runtime benefits, complexity remains quadratic, so the method does not address large-scale optimization.  
- Paper is well-written and organized but contribution clarity and attribution need improvement.  
- Recommendation: not ready for acceptance; authors should fix naming/citation issues, add comparisons to related methods, and better articulate novelty.  
- Pros summarized: substantial runtime improvements and practical benefits for full covariance adaptation.  
- Cons summarized: limited novelty, missing related-work comparisons, and misleading claims about optimality and stability.
