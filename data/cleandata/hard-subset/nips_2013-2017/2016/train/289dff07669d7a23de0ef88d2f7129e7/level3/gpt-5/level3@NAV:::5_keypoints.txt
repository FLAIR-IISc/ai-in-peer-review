- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor and updates via a sequence of rank-one Cholesky updates, achieving O(µ d^2) time and optimal storage vs cubic cost.  
- Replaces C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in the CSA (omitting the per-iteration random orthogonal rotation E_t), arguing asymptotic equivalence since E_t → I under convergence assumptions.  
- Empirically shows substantial wall-clock speed-ups on standard rotated synthetic benchmarks with no degradation in optimization vs two delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Novel integration of a triangular factor with standard CMA-ES and a theoretically motivated omission of E_t, distinct from prior Cholesky and acceleration works.  
- Theoretical analysis is technically sound but depends on strong convergence-of-shape assumptions and offers limited non-asymptotic control on CSA error.  
- Implementation appears careful and experiments are solid but the empirical study could be more comprehensive.  
- Paper is generally clear and organized, but Lemma 1 assumptions should be stated more prominently and implications for non-convergent/slowly rotating regimes clarified.  
- Reproducibility needs improvement: report exact hardware, BLAS/LAPACK settings, and memory footprints.  
- Significance: speedups matter when evaluations are cheap or d is large; limited practical impact when evaluation time dominates.  
- Pros: principled algorithm with asymptotically optimal update/storage, strong runtime gains without loss in evaluations, and good relation to prior acceleration strategies.  
- Cons: restrictive theory with limited finite-sample guarantees, modest impact in evaluation-dominated settings, and missing comparisons/diagnostics.  
- Suggested diagnostics: track evolution of E_t (e.g., ‖E_t − I‖, ‖E_tE_{t−1}^T − I‖) by running full CMA-ES and Cholesky-CMA-ES in parallel.  
- Suggested baselines: report wall-clock times for full CMA-ES without delay and include LM-CMA as a baseline.  
- Suggested benchmarks: evaluate on quadratics with continuously varied spectra to relate solution quality and E_t error to spectral distributions.  
- Suggested real-world test: include at least one real-time or cheap-evaluation task (e.g., robotics policy gradients) where speedup matters.  
- Suggested ablations: analyze the CSA modification, numerical stability, and report memory usage.
