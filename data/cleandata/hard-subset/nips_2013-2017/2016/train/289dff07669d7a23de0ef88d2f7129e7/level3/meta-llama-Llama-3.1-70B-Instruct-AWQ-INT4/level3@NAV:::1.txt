This paper proposes a novel variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a powerful real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm, which updates the covariance matrix via triangular Cholesky factors. This approach significantly reduces the update complexity and memory requirements, making it particularly suitable for large-dimensional problems.

The authors provide a thorough theoretical justification for their approach, demonstrating that the modification does not change the behavior of the CMA-ES in terms of required objective function evaluations. They also present empirical results, showing that the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a substantial wall-clock speedup compared to the standard CMA-ES, while maintaining the same number of function evaluations.

The paper is well-written, and the authors have made a significant effort to clearly explain the underlying concepts and the proposed approach. The experiments are thoroughly evaluated, and the results are presented in a clear and concise manner.

The strengths of this paper include:

* The proposed approach is theoretically sound and well-justified.
* The empirical results demonstrate a significant speedup in wall-clock time, making the algorithm more practical for large-scale optimization problems.
* The authors have provided a thorough evaluation of the algorithm, including a comparison with other CMA-ES variants.

The weaknesses of this paper include:

* The authors could have provided more insight into the choice of parameters and the sensitivity of the algorithm to these parameters.
* The paper could have benefited from a more detailed analysis of the computational complexity of the proposed approach.
