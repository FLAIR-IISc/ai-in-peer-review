This paper proposes a new variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which replaces the eigen-decomposition of the covariance matrix with a Cholesky factorization. This modification leads to a significant reduction in computational complexity, from O(d^3) to O(μd^2), where μ is the effective sample size and d is the dimensionality of the search space.

The authors provide a clear and well-written description of the CMA-ES algorithm and its limitations. They also present a thorough analysis of the proposed modification, including a theoretical justification for why it does not impair the performance of the algorithm. The experimental results demonstrate a significant increase in optimization speed, with the Cholesky-CMA-ES requiring much less wall-clock time than the standard CMA-ES, especially for high-dimensional problems.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. The authors do not provide a proof of equivalence between the original CMA-ES and the Cholesky-CMA-ES, which is essential to establish the validity of the proposed modification. Additionally, the claim that the path error is small and decreases over time is not experimentally validated, and the authors rely on a theoretical argument to support this claim.

Furthermore, the paper appears to be a trivial extension of previous work, specifically "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies" by Krause and Igel. The authors do not provide sufficient evidence to demonstrate the novelty and significance of their contribution.

In terms of clarity, the paper is well-written, but there are some minor issues with notation and typos. For example, the notation "wk" in Algorithm 2 should be "α_k", and the variable "n" in Table 1 should be "d".

Overall, while the paper presents an interesting modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, it falls short in terms of originality and rigor. The authors need to address the limitations mentioned above and provide a more thorough analysis of their contribution to establish its significance.

Recommendation: The paper is not acceptable in its current form. The authors need to revise the paper to address the limitations mentioned above, including providing a proof of equivalence, experimentally validating the path error claim, and demonstrating the novelty and significance of their contribution.
