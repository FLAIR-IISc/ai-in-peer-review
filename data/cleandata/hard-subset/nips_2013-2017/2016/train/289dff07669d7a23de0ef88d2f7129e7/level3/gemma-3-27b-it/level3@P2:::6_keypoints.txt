- Proposes updating and storing CMA-ES covariance via Cholesky factor to reduce runtime and memory costs.  
- Rewrites CMA-ES update rules to operate on the triangular Cholesky factor and adjusts step-size adaptation.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) arguing convergence and preservation of performance characteristics.  
- Demonstrates practical runtime improvements, especially in higher dimensions, and includes clear algorithms.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes helpful Algorithm 1 and 2.  
- Name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with prior work (Suttorp et al., 2009) and causes confusion.  
- Fails to include direct empirical comparison with Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015), weakening evaluation.  
- Algorithmic novelty appears incremental (looped rankOneUpdate for individuals) and needs stronger justification beyond implementation detail.  
- Experimental scope is limited and should compare against existing Cholesky-based CMA-ES variants.  
- Claim of "optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity" is overstated since complexity remains quadratic and not optimal for diagonal/low-rank cases.  
- Asserts numerical instability of Suttorp et al.’s method without sufficient evidence or justification.  
- Incorrectly states eigenvalue decomposition occurs every iteration; standard implementations use delayed updates.  
- For acceptance: addresses a relevant problem, offers sound theory, shows runtime gains, and integrates code into a popular library.  
- Against acceptance: confusing naming, lack of comprehensive comparisons, incremental contribution, and overstated/unsupported claims.  
- Overall assessment: worthwhile but incremental improvement with sound analysis; impact reduced by evaluation gaps and naming issues.  
- Recommendation: Reject with request for major revisions—rename method, add comprehensive empirical comparisons, justify novelty, clarify optimality and stability claims.
