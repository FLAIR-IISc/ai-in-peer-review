The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" presents a novel approach to improving the efficiency of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a widely used derivative-free optimization algorithm. The authors propose a modification to the standard CMA-ES, which they term Cholesky-CMA-ES, that reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm while maintaining its performance.

The main idea behind the Cholesky-CMA-ES is to update the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix itself. This approach has been previously explored in the literature, but the authors extend it to the standard CMA-ES, which requires both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the modified algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical experiments.

The paper's contribution is significant, as it addresses a major limitation of the standard CMA-ES, namely its high computational complexity. The Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a better, asymptotically optimal time complexity of O(μd^2) for the covariance update and optimal memory complexity. The authors also demonstrate that the modified algorithm requires the same amount of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES but requires much less wall-clock time, making it a more efficient option for large-scale optimization problems.

However, the novelty of the paper's contribution is not entirely convincing, as the extension to rank-μ updates is a straightforward generalization of previous work by Krause et al. (2015). Additionally, the paper could benefit from stronger theoretical support for the covariance convergence assumption, which is crucial to the modified cumulative step-size adaptation. Providing more rigorous theoretical analysis or referencing existing work, such as Beyer (2014), could strengthen confidence in Lemma 1.

Minor issues with the paper include a typo in Equation (1), where σ should be replaced with \tilde{σ}, and an extra "by" in line 94, which should be removed.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization, and its experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES. However, the authors could further strengthen their contribution by addressing the limitations mentioned above.

**Arguments For Acceptance:**

1. The paper presents a novel approach to improving the efficiency of the CMA-ES, a widely used derivative-free optimization algorithm.
2. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the modified algorithm and demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical experiments.
3. The Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a better, asymptotically optimal time complexity of O(μd^2) for the covariance update and optimal memory complexity.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

2. The paper could benefit from stronger theoretical support for the covariance convergence assumption.
3. Minor issues with the paper, such as typos and extra words, detract from its overall quality.
