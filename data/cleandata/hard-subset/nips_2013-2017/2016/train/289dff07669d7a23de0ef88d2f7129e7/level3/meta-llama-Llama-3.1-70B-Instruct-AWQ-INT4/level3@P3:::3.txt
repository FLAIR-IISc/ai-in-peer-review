The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" presents a modification of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES. The main contribution of this work is the use of the triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the square root in the standard CMA-ES, which reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm from O(d^3) to O(μd^2), where μ is the number of samples used to update the covariance matrix.

The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and show that the error introduced by replacing the square root with the Cholesky factorization is small and decreases over time as the algorithm converges to the optimum. They also present experimental results comparing the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants, demonstrating a significant increase in optimization speed while maintaining the same number of objective function evaluations.

The paper is well-written and clearly organized, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed modifications to the CMA-ES algorithm. The use of mathematical notation and equations is clear and concise, and the authors provide a detailed explanation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and its properties.

One of the strengths of the paper is its ability to balance theoretical and empirical contributions. The authors provide a solid theoretical foundation for the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm, including a proof of convergence and an analysis of the error introduced by the Cholesky factorization. At the same time, they present experimental results that demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed algorithm.

However, there are some limitations to the paper. One of the main concerns is the novelty of the contribution. The authors acknowledge that the idea of using the Cholesky factorization in CMA-ES is not new, and that similar approaches have been proposed in the past. While the authors provide a more detailed analysis of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and its properties, it is not clear how their work differs significantly from previous contributions.

Another limitation of the paper is the lack of a more detailed comparison with other optimization algorithms. While the authors compare the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants, it would be useful to see a comparison with other popular optimization algorithms, such as gradient-based methods or other evolutionary algorithms.

In terms of the conference reviewing guidelines, the paper scores well on quality, clarity, and originality. The authors provide a clear and well-organized presentation of their work, and the paper is well-written and free of major errors. However, the novelty of the contribution is a concern, and the authors could benefit from a more detailed comparison with other optimization algorithms.
