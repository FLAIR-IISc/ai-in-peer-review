- Addresses the O(d^3) covariance decomposition bottleneck in CMA-ES and its impact on high-dimensional problems.  
- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains the triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T) to avoid full decomposition.  
- Reduces update complexity to O(µ d^2) and achieves optimal storage complexity for full covariance updates.  
- Main technical challenge: CSA requires C^{-1/2}; authors replace it with A^{-1} and provide theoretical justification (Lemma 1).  
- Lemma 1 argument: error from using A^{-1} vanishes as the covariance matrix shape converges.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky and delayed-update work but is novel in making this approach work for standard CMA-ES.  
- Extensive experiments on benchmark functions show large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) without harming function-evaluation performance.  
- Comparisons are fair and reference implementations are appropriately used.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, and includes clear algorithms for reproducibility.  
- Claim of improved numerical stability for A and A^{-1} updates is asserted but not empirically or theoretically substantiated.  
- Theoretical justification depends on the covariance shape converging, which may not always hold in practice.  
- Highly significant contribution: lowers the barrier to applying CMA-ES in higher dimensions and is a practical drop-in replacement.  
- Acceptance arguments: solves a major bottleneck, provides optimal complexity, strong theory and experiments, and clear presentation.  
- Rejection concerns: insufficient evidence on numerical stability and reliance on convergence assumption; suggest discussing combination with low-rank/limited-memory approximations for very high dimensions.
