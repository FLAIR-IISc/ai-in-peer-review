- Paper extends Cholesky CMA-ES to support both rank-1 and rank-μ updates on the triangular Cholesky factor to improve runtime and memory efficiency.  
- Proposes a numerically stable quadratic-time update scheme reducing covariance update complexity to O(μ d^2) while maintaining the same number of function evaluations.  
- Provides theoretical justification, including analysis of error from replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor and Lemma 1 building on prior work.  
- Empirical results show significant wall-clock runtime improvements over existing CMA-ES variants, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- Strength: strong theoretical rigor supporting convergence and stability of the modified algorithm.  
- Strength: practical impact via substantial speed-ups relevant to large-scale ML tasks (e.g., neural network training, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across benchmarks and dimensions demonstrating scalability and efficiency.  
- Weakness: novelty is incremental—main idea builds on Krause et al. (2015) and prior Cholesky-based approaches.  
- Weakness: minor typographical and notational errors (e.g., Eq. (1), footnote 2, extraneous "by") needing correction.  
- Weakness: empirical scope could be expanded to compare against more recent low-rank or diagonal approximation methods (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).  
- Pro argument: addresses a practical CMA-ES bottleneck with solid theory and convincing empirical speed-ups.  
- Con argument: limited novelty and insufficient contextualization relative to recent scalable CMA-ES advances.  
- Recommendation: accept conditional on fixing presentation issues and better situating the work among recent scalable CMA-ES variants.
