- Problem: Standard CMA-ES has an O(d^3) bottleneck from eigendecomposition / matrix square root of the d×d covariance matrix.  
- Proposal: Introduce Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to avoid repeated decompositions.  
- Complexity: Reduces covariance update cost to O(µ d^2), which is asymptotically optimal.  
- Core approximation: Replace C^{-1/2} in CSA with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1}.  
- Theoretical justification: Argues orthogonal matrix E_t relating decompositions changes slowly and converges, so the approximation error vanishes (Lemma 1), but relies on a strong convergence assumption.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Krause & Igel (2015) and prior speed-up attempts (e.g., delayed updates, non-triangular factorizations) while offering a more stable, theoretically motivated full-CMA-ES variant.  
- Experimental results: Thorough benchmarks show significant wall-clock speed-up with no measurable loss in function-evaluation performance against state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants.  
- Numerical stability and storage: Method is numerically stable and has optimal storage requirements.  
- Clarity: Paper is exceptionally well-written, with clear background, logical explanation, helpful pseudocode, and effective figures.  
- Originality: Novel contribution is successfully adapting Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES by handling the CSA step via the A^{-1} approximation.  
- Significance: Makes CMA-ES practical for higher-dimensional problems when function evaluations are cheap, broadening applicability.  
- Practical limitation: Impact is limited where objective evaluations dominate runtime (e.g., many machine-learning tasks).  
- Suggested experiment 1: Empirically track E_t (e.g., ||E_t^T E_{t-1} - I||_F) over iterations to verify convergence assumption.  
- Suggested experiment 2: Include a baseline with full O(d^3) decomposition every iteration to provide a true runtime upper bound.  
- Suggested experiment 3: Test on covariance-adaptation-challenging problems (e.g., quadratics with varied spectra) to probe robustness of omitting E_t.  
- Suggested experiment 4: Add a simple real-world task (e.g., RL policy search) to demonstrate practical impact in a claimed application domain.  
- Acceptance pros: Addresses major bottleneck, yields dramatic speed-up, is algorithmically sound and stable, and is clearly presented.  
- Acceptance cons: Theoretical argument depends on strong assumptions and practical gains are niche when function-eval cost is high.  
- Recommendation: Overall strong contribution with a recommendation for acceptance; should become the new standard for relevant problem classes.
