- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace costly matrix square root in CSA with triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Reduces covariance update time/space complexity from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2) with µ = O(log d).  
- Cholesky factorization yields covariance eigenvalues at no extra cost for conditioning monitoring.  
- Theoretical guarantee (Lemma 1): error from replacing C^{-1/2}_t with A^{-1}_t vanishes as algorithm converges.  
- Empirical evaluation: 100 trials on 8 benchmark functions (rotated, non-separable, ill-conditioned) for d=4..256.  
- Comparisons against state-of-the-art variants (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES).  
- Results show no degradation in function-evaluations and substantial wall-clock speedups (≈20× at d=64, larger gaps for higher d).  
- Implementation in Shark using LAPACK ensures high efficiency; clarify whether all variants used identical codebases.  
- Paper is very clear: precise algorithms, logical theory development, transparent experimental design, and clear link between Cholesky and eigenvalue monitoring.  
- Minor clarity suggestion: explain why Fig.2 shows smaller speedups for Cigar/Discus at d=256 (likely L2 cache effects).  
- Originality: extends Cholesky-based updates to full CMA-ES with CSA modification and theoretical guarantees, beyond prior restricted-case work.  
- Significance: enables scalable, practical CMA-ES for ML tasks (RL, hyperparameter tuning) by addressing O(d^3) bottleneck.  
- Strengths: rigorous theory, thorough experiments, no-performance loss, significant speedups, useful eigenvalue diagnostics.  
- Weaknesses: minor implementation-detail clarification and brief note about cache-related behavior in high-dim plots.  
- Conclusion: technically sound, impactful, and well-written; recommended for acceptance as a top-tier contribution.
