- Proposal: maintain and update triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) and adapt step-size by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA.  
- Covariance update: decompose rank-1 + rank-µ update into a sequence of rank-1 updates applied to A in O(µ d^2) time per iteration with O(d^2) storage.  
- Theoretical issue: using A^{-1} introduces a rotation E (from RQ decomposition); Lemma 1 argues rotations stabilize so CSA approximation error vanishes under covariance-shape convergence.  
- Experiments: benchmarks (Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) over d=4…256 with random rotations.  
- Empirical results: Cholesky-CMA-ES matches reference CMA-ES in function evaluations to target (1e-14) but achieves large wall-clock speedups (≈20× at d=64).  
- Invariance: key invariance properties are preserved empirically with only small deviations.  
- Relation to prior work: builds on CMA-ES, structured/limited-memory covariance models, and prior Cholesky/rank-1 updates (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel); contribution is extending factor updates to rank-µ and adapting CSA.  
- Quality: algorithmic development is sound, with careful, numerically stable Cholesky rank-1 implementation and credible complexity claims.  
- Theoretical support: justification for small CSA error is heuristic—relies on convergence of normalized covariance shape and continuity of RQ decomposition, not a rigorous proof for full stochastic CMA-ES.  
- Reproducibility concerns: missing runtime environment details (hardware, BLAS/LAPACK, compiler flags) and excluding non-convergent Rosenbrock runs could bias results; failure rates should be reported.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and well organized; CSA modification and rotation E are presented early; minor notation and typographical issues remain.  
- Originality: incremental relative to prior Cholesky/rank-1 work, but nontrivial and practically useful integration for standard CMA-ES.  
- Significance: strong practical impact for moderate-to-high dimensions where wall-clock matters; provides O(d^2) eigenvalue/inverse computations useful for diagnostics.  
- Pros for acceptance: large practical speedups without loss of evaluations, preserved invariances, stable and optimal-factor updates, code available, comprehensive rotated benchmarks.  
- Cons for acceptance: limited novelty, heuristic theoretical support, omitted noisy/constrained benchmarks and LM-CMA comparisons, and some questionable reporting choices.  
- Suggested improvements: strengthen theory via known convergence results or finite-time bounds; add ablations measuring ‖E_t^T E_{t-1}−I‖ and σ effects; include periodic RQ recomputation variant; add noisy/constrained tests, failure rates, LM-CMA/time-budget comparisons, and detailed runtime/memory reporting.  
- Minor fixes: correct notation in Eq.(1) (σ vs. tilde σ), fix typo on line 94, and clean up citation formatting.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept — well executed with clear practical benefits despite incremental novelty and heuristic theory.
