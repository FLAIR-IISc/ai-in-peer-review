- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: a numerically stable, triangular Cholesky-factor-based covariance update reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).
- Maintains minimal memory and provides a quadratic-time covariance update scheme.
- Provides theoretical justification for convergence of the new algorithm.
- Empirical results on benchmarks show same number of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES but significantly faster wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.
- Paper is well-written with clear algorithm description and sound theoretical foundation.
- Thorough empirical comparison against state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants demonstrating effectiveness.
- Strength: Major runtime improvement making CMA-ES more suitable for high-dimensional problems.
- Strength: Solid theoretical convergence analysis.
- Strength: Strong empirical performance and runtime advantage.
- Weakness: Assumes objective evaluation time is negligible, which may not hold in many real-world applications.
- Weakness: Lacks detailed analysis of actual memory requirements for large-scale problems.
- Weakness: Does not evaluate the method on real-world tasks to demonstrate practical effectiveness.
- Suggestion: Test the algorithm on real-world problems (e.g., neural network training, complex systems).
- Suggestion: Provide a detailed memory-requirements analysis and comparisons to other CMA-ES variants.
- Suggestion: Explore combining the approach with gradient-based or surrogate-based optimization methods.
- Overall: Significant and promising contribution to CMA-ES efficiency, but further work is needed to assess practical limitations and memory behavior.
