- Targets CMA-ES bottleneck: avoid full covariance matrix decomposition by maintaining triangular Cholesky factor A and applying rank-one updates to A.  
- Algorithmic change: replace C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA; lemma shows successive rotation error vanishes as shape converges.  
- Complexity: O(µ d^2) per iteration (µ typically O(log d)); storage O(d^2) by keeping triangular factor.  
- Empirical claim: matches evaluation efficiency of standard CMA-ES while giving large wall-clock speedups and lower memory up to d=256.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-factor CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al., 2009) and rank-one updates in (1+1)-CMA-ES (Krause & Igel, 2015).  
- Strength — practical impact: substantial runtime and memory savings for full-covariance CMA-ES without loss of evaluations.  
- Strength — implementation: clear O(d^2) triangular rank-one update and A inversion.  
- Strength — theory: persuasive rotation-vanishing argument justifying the CSA approximation.  
- Strength — experiments: benchmarks show matched evaluations and runtime gains increasing with d; rotation invariance probed.  
- Weakness — naming conflict: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” already used; recommend distinct name and early clarification.  
- Weakness — incomplete prior-work positioning: need explicit algorithmic comparison to Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel, and explain what prevents similar CSA modification there.  
- Weakness — experimental baselines thin: require parameter parity, per-iteration cost breakdowns, inclusion of Krause & Igel’s method, sep-CMA-ES and LM-CMA-ES on separable problems.  
- Weakness — “optimality” overstated: asymptotically optimal among full-covariance methods but not globally optimal for separable/easy problems; temper claims and specify regime.  
- Weakness — numerical stability claim against Suttorp’s approach is unsubstantiated; request empirical evidence (failure rates, condition numbers, sensitivity).  
- Weakness — scalability: still O(d^2); should be framed as preferred full-covariance variant up to feasible d and defer larger scales to low-rank/LM approaches.  
- Weakness — plotting/statistics: clarify median/mean, success criteria, handling of timeouts, provide confidence intervals and evaluation-based progress.  
- Minor — clarify that many implementations perform periodic decomposition; mention delayed decomposition baseline earlier.  
- Quality: technically sound; CSA approximation is main novel analysis but would benefit from quantitative error bounds.  
- Clarity: generally well written; fix naming and sharpen related-work and explicit statement of main algorithmic changes.  
- Originality: incremental on covariance updates, more novel in CSA replacement and its justification.  
- Significance: high practical significance for medium-to-large d full-covariance use; limited for truly large-scale problems.  
- Pros for acceptance: meaningful speed/memory improvements, simple to adopt, plausible theoretical support for CSA change.  
- Cons against acceptance: naming/positioning issues, overstated claims, unproven stability criticisms, missing baselines/ablations.  
- Questions for authors: report quantitative rotation drift Et and correlation with CSA error/performance?  
- Questions for authors: how often is A recomputed/reorthogonalized to mitigate numerical drift?  
- Questions for authors: under what conditions does the Suttorp baseline become unstable in your implementation?  
- Recommendation: borderline accept conditional on resolving naming, tempering claims, and adding requested comparisons and analyses.
