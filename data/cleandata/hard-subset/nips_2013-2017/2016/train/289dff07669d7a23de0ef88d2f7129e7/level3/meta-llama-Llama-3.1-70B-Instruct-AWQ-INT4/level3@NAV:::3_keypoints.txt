- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: uses triangular Cholesky factor instead of covariance matrix square root to reduce runtime complexity.
- Replaces matrix square root with inverse of triangular Cholesky factor computable in quadratic time, with theoretical justification.
- Experimental results show significant increase in optimization speed compared to other CMA-ES variants.
- Paper is well-written, clearly explained, and experiments are well-presented with thorough comparisons.
- Contribution is limited: rank-μ extension is a straightforward generalization of Krause et al. (2015), reducing novelty.
- Theoretical support for covariance convergence is insufficient and should reference more recent work (e.g., Beyer 2014).
- Introduction of the new random rotation matrix Et may introduce error, but the paper lacks a thorough error analysis.
- Minor issues: typo in denominator of Eq. (1) (σ should be \tilde{σ}) and an extra "by" in line 94.
- Meets conference quality criteria (technically sound, clear, well-organized) but has limited significance and impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: technical soundness, clear presentation, and strong empirical speedup.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, weakened theoretical convergence support, and missing error analysis for Et.
