**Paper Review: CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity**

**Summary**

The paper proposes a modified version of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which uses a triangular Cholesky factorization to update the covariance matrix. This approach reduces the time complexity of the algorithm from O(d^3) to O(μd^2), where μ is the number of updates and d is the dimensionality of the problem. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and demonstrate its performance on various benchmark functions.

**Quality**

The paper is technically sound, and the claims are well-supported by theoretical analysis and experimental results. However, there are some issues with the naming of the algorithm, as it conflicts with the established name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" by Suttorp et al. (2009). Additionally, the paper omits discussion and empirical comparison with Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmic comparison with Krause et al. (2015).

**Clarity**

The paper is well-written and easy to follow. The authors provide a clear explanation of the CMA-ES algorithm and the modifications they propose. However, some of the mathematical derivations could be improved for better clarity.

**Originality**

The paper proposes a novel approach to updating the covariance matrix in CMA-ES using a triangular Cholesky factorization. However, the main novelty appears to be simply looping the rankOneUpdate for all individuals, which may not be a significant contribution.

**Significance**

The results are important, as they demonstrate a significant increase in optimization speed for the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm compared to the standard CMA-ES. However, the paper overstates the "optimality" claim, as the algorithm is not optimal for all functions (e.g., separable functions). Additionally, the paper's scalability claim is questionable, as the complexity remains quadratic.

**Recommendations**

1. Resolve the naming conflict with Suttorp et al. (2009).
2. Run experiments including Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause et al.'s (2015) variants.
3. Clarify the contribution of the paper and justify why the proposed approach is more than just a simple loop.
4. Correct the overstated "optimality" claim and provide a more nuanced discussion of the algorithm's strengths and weaknesses.
5. Address the scalability concern and provide a more detailed analysis of the algorithm's complexity.

**Arguments For and Against Acceptance**

For acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel approach to updating the covariance matrix in CMA-ES.
* The results demonstrate a significant increase in optimization speed.
* The paper is well-written and easy to follow.

Against acceptance:

* The naming conflict with Suttorp et al. (2009) needs to be resolved.
* The paper omits discussion and empirical comparison with relevant work.
* The main novelty may not be significant.
* The paper overstates the "optimality" claim and scalability.
