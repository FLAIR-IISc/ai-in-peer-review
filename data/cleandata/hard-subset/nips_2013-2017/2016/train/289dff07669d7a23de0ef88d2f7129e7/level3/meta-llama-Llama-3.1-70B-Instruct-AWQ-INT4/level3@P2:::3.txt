This paper presents an extension to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, a widely used derivative-free optimization method. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm aims to reduce the time and space complexity of the original CMA-ES by maintaining triangular Cholesky factors instead of the covariance matrix itself. This approach is not entirely new, as similar methods have been proposed in the past, but the authors provide a theoretical justification for their modification and demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical experiments.

The main idea behind the Cholesky-CMA-ES is to update the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix itself. This is achieved by applying a sequence of rank-1 updates to the Cholesky factor, which can be done in quadratic time. The authors also modify the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism to work with the Cholesky factor, which requires replacing the inverse of the square root of the covariance matrix with the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor.

The authors provide a theoretical analysis of their modification, showing that it does not significantly change the behavior of the algorithm under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges to a certain shape. They also demonstrate the effectiveness of their approach through experiments on various benchmark functions, comparing the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants.

The strengths of the paper include its clear presentation of the proposed algorithm and its theoretical analysis. The experimental results also demonstrate the potential benefits of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, particularly in high-dimensional optimization problems. However, the contribution of the paper is somewhat limited, as the extension to rank-μ updates is a straightforward generalization of previous work.

One potential weakness of the paper is the lack of theoretical support for the covariance convergence assumption. While the authors cite previous work on the convergence of the CMA-ES, they do not provide a rigorous analysis of the convergence of the covariance matrix in their specific setting. This could be an area for future research.

In terms of clarity, the paper is generally well-written, but there are a few minor issues. For example, the notation in Equation (1) is inconsistent, and there is a minor typo in the text.

Overall, the paper presents a useful extension to the CMA-ES algorithm, and the experimental results demonstrate its potential benefits. However, the contribution is somewhat limited, and the theoretical analysis could be strengthened with more rigorous support for the covariance convergence assumption.
