- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain and update triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T) to avoid O(d^3) covariance decomposition.
- Reduces per-iteration matrix update complexity to O(µ d^2), claimed asymptotically optimal.
- Main technical challenge: CSA in standard CMA-ES requires C^{-1/2}, not A^{-1}; authors replace C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}.
- Provide theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the rotation error from using A^{-1} vanishes as covariance shape converges.
- Extends prior Cholesky-update work to full rank-µ case via a series of rank-1 updates.
- Empirical results: matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.
- Strength — Significance: addresses a major practical bottleneck, enabling scalable CMA-ES for cheap-to-evaluate objectives.
- Strength — Quality: algorithm description and complexity analysis are sound; experiments are thorough and supportive.
- Strength — Clarity: paper is well-written with helpful pseudocode and figures.
- Weakness — Originality: approach is incremental relative to Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015); rank-µ extension is straightforward.
- Weakness — Theory: Lemma 1 relies on unproven assumption that covariance shape converges; conditions for this convergence are not discussed.
- Argument for acceptance: significant practical improvement and ready-to-adopt implementation for practitioners.
- Argument for acceptance: complete contribution with algorithm, justification, and extensive validation.
- Argument against acceptance: limited novelty over recent rank-1 Cholesky update work.
- Argument against acceptance: theoretical reliance on an unproven convergence assumption weakens the approximation claim.
- Conclusion: overall strong engineering contribution with high practical impact; recommended for acceptance despite limited conceptual novelty.
- Minor comments: clarify σ_t notation in Eq. (1)/footnote and fix typo "which can by be motivated" → "which can be motivated."
