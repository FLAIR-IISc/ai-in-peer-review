

# Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper proposes a variant of the CMA-ES algorithm that replaces the eigen-decomposition used for covariance matrix updates with a Cholesky decomposition, claiming O(μd²) time complexity instead of the standard O(d³). The authors present a new algorithm called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which maintains triangular Cholesky factors instead of the full covariance matrix, and argue this approach reduces both time and space complexity while preserving the optimization performance of the original CMA-ES.

The paper builds on existing work in CMA-ES variants that aim to reduce computational complexity. Notably, it extends the work by Krause and Igel (2015) "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies," which already proposed a similar Cholesky-based approach for the special case of (1+1)-CMA-ES. The authors acknowledge this prior work but claim their extension to the standard CMA-ES is novel. However, the core idea appears to be a straightforward extension of prior methods rather than a significant conceptual advance.

**Quality:** The paper has several technical weaknesses that undermine its quality. First, the authors fail to provide a proof showing their algorithm is equivalent to the original CMA-ES (formula 2), which is essential even if the equivalence was established in previous work. Without this proof, it's impossible to validate their claim that the algorithm's behavior remains unchanged. Second, their complexity claim is unclear—they state the complexity is O(μd²) but do not explain how this is actually faster than CMA-ES-Ref (which delays the matrix square root computation). They should use Θ-notation and provide a detailed complexity analysis comparing their approach to the standard implementation. Third, their claim that "path error is small and decreases" (Section 3) lacks experimental validation over time—only final-performance comparisons are provided, contradicting Hansen's findings that the path length is a critical parameter in CMA-ES.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written, with clear algorithm descriptions. However, there are several notation errors that undermine clarity: "wk" in Algorithm 2 should be "α_k," "n" in Table 1 should be "d," and "F" should be "f" (line 185). These are easily fixable but indicate sloppiness in the final manuscript.

**Originality:** The paper's main contribution appears to be a trivial extension of Krause and Igel (2015), which already proposed a similar Cholesky-based approach for evolution strategies. The authors fail to adequately distinguish their work from this prior contribution, making it difficult to assess the novelty. The core idea—using Cholesky factors instead of the matrix square root for covariance updates—is not new to the field. While the authors claim to provide "theoretical justification for why it does not deteriorate the algorithm's performance," this justification is weak and relies on unproven assumptions about convergence.

**Significance:** The claimed speedup of O(μd²) is potentially significant for high-dimensional optimization problems, but the paper fails to convincingly demonstrate that the method actually provides this advantage in practice. The experimental results show similar function evaluations but reduced wall-clock time, which could be due to better cache utilization rather than the theoretical complexity improvement. The authors do not adequately address why their approach would be superior to existing methods like CMA-ES-Ref (which delays the matrix square root computation) or the Suttorp-CMA-ES variant they tested.

**Strengths:** The paper presents a reasonable method that could potentially improve computational efficiency for CMA-ES. The use of Cholesky factorization for covariance updates is numerically stable and has minimal memory requirements, which is valuable for large-scale optimization. The empirical results do show reduced wall-clock time for higher dimensions, which is promising.

**Weaknesses:** The paper suffers from multiple critical issues: lack of equivalence proof, unclear complexity claims, unvalidated theoretical assumptions, and insufficient differentiation from prior work. The authors' claim that their method "can and should replace the standard CMA-ES" is overstated without sufficient validation.

**Recommendation:** The paper is not acceptable for NIPS in its current form. The main contribution appears to be a trivial extension of existing work with insufficient novelty. The authors must address the missing equivalence proof, clarify their complexity claims, validate their path-error claim with time-series experiments, and properly distinguish their work from Krause and Igel (2015). Without these substantial revisions, the paper does not meet NIPS' standards for technical soundness, originality, and significance.

**Summary of Arguments:**

*Pro acceptance:*
- Clear writing and reasonable method for covariance update
- Empirical results show reduced wall-clock time for high-dimensional problems
- The approach could be valuable for large-scale optimization

*Con acceptance:*
- Limited originality (trivial extension of Krause & Igel 2015)
- Missing equivalence proof between their algorithm and CMA-ES
- Unclear complexity claim (O(μd²) vs CMA-ES-Ref)
- Unvalidated path-error claim
- Notation errors and typos
- Overstated significance claims

The paper has potential but requires substantial revisions to establish its novelty, clarify its technical contributions, and provide sufficient validation before it could be considered for publication at NIPS.
