- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update the triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix to improve efficiency.  
- Extends prior Cholesky-based methods to handle both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for standard CMA-ES.  
- Provides theoretical justification for convergence of the modified algorithm and its cumulative step-size adaptation.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal time complexity O(μ d^2) for covariance updates and optimal memory complexity.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in objective evaluations while requiring much less wall-clock time.  
- Novelty is limited: rank-μ extension is a straightforward generalization of Krause et al. (2015).  
- Requires stronger theoretical support for the covariance convergence assumption; Lemma 1 needs more rigorous analysis or citations (e.g., Beyer 2014).  
- Minor issues: typo in Equation (1) (σ → \tilde{σ}) and an extra "by" in line 94.  
- Overall contribution is valuable for large-scale derivative-free optimization.  
- Experimental results convincingly demonstrate effectiveness.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to address theoretical and minor editing issues.  
- Reviewer rating: 7/10.
