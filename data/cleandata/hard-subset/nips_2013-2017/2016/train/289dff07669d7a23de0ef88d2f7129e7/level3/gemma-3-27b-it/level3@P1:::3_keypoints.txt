- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update/store Cholesky factor instead of full covariance to reduce computation and memory.
- Operates directly on the triangular Cholesky factor, avoiding explicit matrix square root.
- Introduces a theoretically justified modification to cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA).
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES performance while delivering significant speedups, especially in high dimensions.
- Builds on prior work (notably Krause & Igel 2015) by extending Cholesky updates to the full CMA-ES.
- Clear presentation and theoretical justification that the CSA modification does not change algorithm behavior.
- Practical implementation available in the Shark library, easing adoption by researchers.
- Addresses a major practical bottleneck (O(d^2) covariance updates), improving scalability to larger problems.
- Novelty is limited—primarily a straightforward generalization of previous results.
- Relies on the covariance convergence assumption without sufficiently discussing conditions or consequences if violated.
- Experimental evaluation is strong but should include comparisons to more recent high-dimensional optimization methods.
- Contains minor presentation issues (typos and notation inconsistencies) that need fixing.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty, discuss the covariance assumption, and broaden experimental comparisons.
