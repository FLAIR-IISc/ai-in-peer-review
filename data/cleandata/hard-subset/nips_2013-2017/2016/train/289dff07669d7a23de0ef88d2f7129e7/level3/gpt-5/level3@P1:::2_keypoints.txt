- Proposes a Cholesky-based CMA-ES variant that updates a triangular factor A by rank-one updates and substitutes A^{-1} for C^{-1/2} in CSA to reduce memory/time.  
- Claims O(µ d^2) time and optimal memory for full covariance adaptation while keeping objective-evaluation counts unchanged.  
- Uses a continuity argument (RQ decomposition) to argue the step-size path error decays as the covariance shape converges.  
- Empirically matches reference CMA-ES in evaluation count and yields large wall-clock speedups on rotated and ill-conditioned benchmarks.  
- Builds on prior work replacing full EVD/square-root recomputation (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and extends it to µ > 1 with a CSA approximation.  
- Contrasts with model-restricted variants (diagonal / limited-memory) that trade performance for efficiency.  
- Strength: targets a known CMA-ES bottleneck and demonstrates practical wall-clock gains.  
- Strength: implementation-friendly triangular factor reduces memory and enables efficient triangular solves.  
- Strength: asymptotic justification for A^{-1} substitution is plausible and consistent with empirical results.  
- Strength: open-source integration and fair baselines (delayed decomposition, Suttorp-style factor).  
- Weakness: Missing formal proof that the sequence of rank-one updates on A exactly factorizes the intended Ct+1 for general µ and scaling—needs detailed derivation or citation.  
- Weakness: CSA approximation theory is weak for a randomized, non-stationary algorithm and does not quantify pσ or σ errors over time.  
- Requested: empirically compare exact CSA (C^{-1/2}) vs proposed CSA (A^{-1}) over iterations (pσ norms, step-size trajectories, deviations).  
- Requested: report ∥Et Eᵀt−1 − I∥ and angles between sk and ŝk to test rotation-drift assumptions, and include slowly rotating/difficult cases and varying λ, µ.  
- Weakness: Complexity claims need precision—use Θ-notation and give per-iteration amortized breakdowns including sampling/sorting and delayed EVD costs.  
- Requested: clarify when the method is asymptotically faster than CMA-ES with delayed decomposition, discuss constant factors, µ≈Θ(log d), and cache effects.  
- Weakness: Incorrect claim that diagonal of Cholesky factor equals square roots of eigenvalues; must correct and remove conclusions relying on that.  
- Weakness: Numerical stability of rank-one Cholesky up-/downdates not addressed—need conditions for SPD preservation, citations (cholupdate literature), and diagnostics (SPD checks, condition numbers) on ill-conditioned problems.  
- Weakness: Experimental scope limited—recommend COCO/BBOB, higher dimensions (d>256), ablations over λ and µ, noisy functions, and fairness details (BLAS/LAPACK, threading, seeds, CPU specs).  
- Clarity issues: notation/typos (αk/wk mismatch, n→d, F→f), possible transcription errors in Algorithm 2; needs concise derivation mapping Eq.(2) to rank-one updates.  
- Quality judgment: strong empirical wall-clock results but needs rigorous equivalence proof, corrected eigenvalue claim, and deeper CSA validation.  
- Originality: incremental—main technical ingredient known; novelty is integration into standard CMA-ES for µ>1 plus CSA approximation.  
- Significance: potentially high for practitioners if correctness and stability are established.  
- Pros for acceptance: addresses important bottleneck, simple and applicable implementation, promising empirical speedups.  
- Cons for acceptance: missing rigorous covariance-update proof, insufficient CSA validation, incorrect eigenvalue claim, ambiguous complexity comparison.  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form; encourage resubmission after equivalence proof, corrected claims, stronger CSA validation, and precise complexity/experimental analysis.
