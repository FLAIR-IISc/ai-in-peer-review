- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance-update cost from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2).  
- Approximates the normalization term C^(-1/2) in CSA with the inverse Cholesky factor A^(-1) and provides a theoretical argument for asymptotic validity.  
- Empirical results show identical optimization performance in function evaluations and large wall-clock speed-ups versus delayed-update CMA-ES baselines.  
- Extends prior Cholesky-update work to the full (µ,λ)-CMA-ES with a novel CSA modification.  
- Strength: substantial practical speed-up making CMA-ES viable for higher-dimensional problems with cheap evaluations (d > ~50).  
- Strength: provides a principled theoretical justification (Lemma 1) rather than a mere heuristic for the A^(-1) approximation.  
- Strength: strong, well-designed experiments and runtime plots that match the theoretical complexity claims.  
- Weakness: limited impact in many ML settings where function-evaluation cost dominates, so internal algorithmic speed-ups may be negligible.  
- Weakness: theoretical justification relies on the strong assumption that the normalized covariance converges, lacking formal guarantees or transient-phase error bounds.  
- Weakness: experiments could be deeper to better characterize approximation behavior across problem types and conditions.  
- Suggestion: empirically validate the core assumption by tracking ||E_t - I|| or similar during full CMA-ES runs.  
- Suggestion: include vanilla full CMA-ES (no delay) runtimes to quantify the complete speed-up range.  
- Suggestion: test on quadratic problems with varied eigenvalue spectra to study dependence on problem conditioning.  
- Suggestion: add a real-world use case (e.g., fast-evaluation RL/robotics controller search) where O(d^2) matters in wall-clock time.  
- Quality: technically sound algorithm and experiments, but limited theoretical scope.  
- Clarity: paper is clear, well-organized, and effectively presented.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of Cholesky updates to standard (µ,λ)-CMA-ES with a non-trivial CSA modification.  
- Significance: important for the evolutionary computation community and high-dimensional cheap-evaluation problems; limited broader ML impact due to typical evaluation costs.
