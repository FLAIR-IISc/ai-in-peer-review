- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution and can be seen as learning a metric.  
- The paper proposes a new covariance matrix update based on triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The proposed update substantially reduces computational complexity and memory requirements compared to prior updates.  
- A notable benefit is that covariance eigenvalues become available without additional decomposition.  
- The empirical evaluation is careful and convincing.  
- The new update preserves performance in terms of function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Experiments indicate the method scales more favorably with dimension without sacrificing solution quality.  
- The contribution is significant and likely attractive as a default for medium- to large-scale applications due to lower memory, cost, and free eigenvalues.  
- The paper is clearly written, methodologically sound, and thoroughly evaluated; appropriate for NIPS.
