This paper studies the Cholesky CMA-ES and extends the update of the triangular Cholesky factor to include both rank-1 and rank-μ updates, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. It proves, under the assumption that the covariance converges, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly change the algorithm’s behavior. The paper also presents experiments on algorithm performance.

1. Novelty and significance. While the paper considers the Cholesky CMA-ES and extends the triangular Cholesky factor update to combine rank-1 and rank-μ updates, I do not find the contribution significant in light of Krause et al. (2015), which already employed a rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The present work appears to be a straightforward generalization of that procedure to a rank-μ update realized as a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2. Theoretical assumptions. The convergence of the covariance C_t in CMA-ES has been established by theoretical analysis (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although that analysis is not based on the exact CMA-ES formulae. This result may increase confidence in Lemma 1.

3. Other issues.
3.1. In Equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σ_t. The same issue appears in footnote 2 on page 3.
3.2. Line 94: “which can by be motivated by principles from ...” — the word “by” should be deleted.
