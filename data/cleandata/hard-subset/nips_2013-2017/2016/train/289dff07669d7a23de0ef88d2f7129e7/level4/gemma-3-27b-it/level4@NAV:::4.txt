This paper introduces a valuable improvement to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) by employing triangular Cholesky factorization. This technique eliminates the need for square root calculations present in the standard CMA-ES, resulting in computational savings when inverting matrices. The authors provide a theoretical rationale demonstrating that this modification does not negatively impact the algorithm’s performance. The proposed method demonstrably reduces runtime complexity without a substantial increase in the number of objective function evaluations. 

However, further theoretical elaboration on the mechanism achieving ‘numerically more stable’ computation would be beneficial. While the Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains comparable objective function evaluation counts to the standard CMA-ES, its advantage in reduced wall-clock time becomes more pronounced as the dimensionality of the search space increases. Given this, the authors might consider investigating a combination of their framework with low-dimensional approximation techniques, such as those proposed by Loschilov [2015]. This could be particularly relevant for practical, high-dimensional applications commonly encountered in fields like neural network training and direct policy search, where problem dimensionality is a crucial factor.
