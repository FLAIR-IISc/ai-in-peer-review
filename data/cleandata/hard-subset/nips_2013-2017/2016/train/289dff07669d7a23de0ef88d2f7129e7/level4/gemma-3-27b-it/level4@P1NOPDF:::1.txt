CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic/randomized algorithm for (derivative-free) optimization. It combines different learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can equivalently be viewed as adaptation of an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new variant for updating the covariance matrix, based on triangular Cholesky factors, that significantly reduces the computational complexity and memory requirements compared to previous update schemes. The new method is carefully evaluated numerically, demonstrating that the update does not degrade the performance of standard CMA-ES in terms of function evaluations needed to reach a given target, while substantially reducing wall-clock time, particularly in high dimensions. 

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of optimization. I anticipate that this variant of CMA-ES could become the default implementation for medium- to large-scale problems, given its clear advantages over the current standard method: namely, reduced memory usage, lower computational complexity, and the readily available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix. The paper is well-written and the approach is thoroughly evaluated. It is particularly appropriate to see this work presented at NIPS, considering CMA-ES’s strong connections to machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in areas like reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
