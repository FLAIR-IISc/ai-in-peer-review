- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to approximate CMA-ES, reducing cost from O(d^3) to O(mu d^2).
- Main approximation stems from omitting estimation of a random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.
- Authors provide an asymptotic argument (under restrictive assumptions) that E_t converges to identity, making schemes asymptotically equivalent.
- Empirical results on standard benchmarks show comparable optimization performance and substantial computational speed-up.
- Comparisons include two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that defer full covariance updates.
- Paper constitutes a solid contribution to accelerating CMA-ES for high-dimensional, fast-evaluation settings.
- Practical limitation: in many applications objective evaluations dominate runtime and typical dimensions are small, so speed gains may be limited to niche scenarios with millisecond evaluations and large d.
- Theoretical guarantees are hard for CMA-ES-style schemes, so empirical validation is key.
- Recommendation: track and report the evolution of E_t along optimization trajectories (e.g., norms) by running full CMA-ES in parallel for comparison.
- Recommendation: include actual wall-clock runtimes and results for vanilla CMA-ES without delay strategies.
- Recommendation: for quadratic objectives, run a simulation varying spectral distributions to compare solution quality and E_t error as a function of spectrum.
- Recommendation: add a real-world experiment (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) where speed matters and parameter dimensionality is moderate.
