- Summary: Proposes a Cholesky-CMA-ES variant claiming optimal covariance update/storage while preserving behavior; main change appears to loop a rank-one Cholesky update over selected individuals, with similar empirical performance and better runtime.

- Strength: Clear focus on reducing time and memory for full-covariance CMA-ES while aiming to retain algorithmic behavior.

- Strength: Practical value if a full covariance matrix must be maintained; approach is directionally appropriate under that constraint.

- Strength: Experiments use relevant benchmarks and indicate runtime savings.

- Major concern 1 (naming/prior work): The name “Cholesky-CMA-ES” and related prior work (Suttorp et al. 2009) are not properly acknowledged, which is misleading.

- Major concern 2 (incremental contribution): The core change may be just looping rankOneUpdate over multiple individuals (a for-loop), making the contribution incremental unless justified.

- Major concern 3 (missing comparisons): Empirical study omits Suttorp et al. (2009) and does not sufficiently dissect differences from Krause et al. (2015), preventing assessment of true advantage.

- Major concern 4 (optimality claims): Claiming “optimal covariance update and storage complexity” is too broad and should be qualified to the full-covariance CMA-ES setting.

- Major concern 5 (numerical stability): The paper asserts Suttorp’s method is unstable contrary to the original work without presenting quantitative evidence; omission from key figures is misleading.

- Major concern 6 (large-scale relevance): The method remains O(n^2) in time and memory and does not address truly large-scale regimes requiring O(n) or O(n log n) methods.

- Empirical concern 1 (Cigar plots): Plateaus at different objective values for CMA-ES-Ref and proposed method on the Cigar function suggest aggregation or run-selection artifacts; clarify aggregation, show CIs, or per-run diagnostics.

- Empirical concern 2 (speed claims vs Suttorp): The reported 2× advantage over Suttorp’s variant must be demonstrated with matched implementations, reporting wall-clock and operation counts.

- Suggestion (naming): Resolve naming by acknowledging Suttorp et al. (2009) or adopting a distinct, descriptive name (e.g., “Rank-μ Cholesky-CMA-ES”).

- Suggestion (empirical baselines): Include Suttorp (2009) and relevant Krause (2015) variants in main figures, or move full comparisons to the supplement if cluttered.

- Suggestion (algorithmic comparison): Provide side-by-side analysis vs Krause et al. (2015) showing per-evaluation/per-generation complexity, memory, and stability safeguards.

- Suggestion (clarify novelty): Explicitly state if the novelty is applying rankOneUpdate in a loop (rank-μ-like) and justify practical impact (conditioning, robustness, equivalence).

- Suggestion (qualify optimality): Reframe optimality claims as “near-optimal among full-covariance CMA-ES variants” and discuss when diagonal/limited-memory methods are preferable.

- Suggestion (stability evidence): Substantiate numerical-stability claims about prior work with quantitative metrics (restarts, eigenvalue drift, corrective jitter) or soften the claims.

- Suggestion (plotting/cigar): Clarify plotting methodology for the Cigar function and correct/annotate figures if needed.

- Suggestion (computational reporting): Report per-iteration and per-function-evaluation times, memory footprints, and scaling with n and λ.

- Minor comment 1 (eigendecomposition frequency): Correct the statement that eigendecomposition is performed at every update—standard implementations perform it periodically.

- Minor comment 2 (terminology): Ensure consistent terminology for selection and update strategies (“plus” vs “comma,” rank-one vs rank-μ).

- Minor comment 3 (reproducibility): Provide sufficient implementation details (update frequencies, regularization, step-size control, numerical safeguards).

- Overall assessment: Relevant practical improvement but incremental, with naming conflicts and missing baselines; recommend revision with clearer prior-work positioning, scoped claims, and comprehensive comparisons.
