- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES reducing per-iteration complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) (μ typically O(log d)).
- Maintains and updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix and its square root.
- Main methodological difference: does not estimate the random orthogonal matrix E_t at every step.
- Under the assumption that covariance matrices converge to a target, authors argue E_t → identity, making the scheme asymptotically equivalent to original CMA-ES.
- Empirical results on synthetic benchmarks show runtime speed-up and no performance degradation versus two delayed-update CMA-ES variants.
- Paper is a solid, practically motivated contribution that substantially reduces overhead while preserving solution quality on reported tests.
- Practical impact may be limited because wall-clock time in many applications is dominated by expensive black-box evaluations, not the optimizer.
- The method primarily targets the niche where evaluations are very fast and dimensionality is high.
- Reviewer requests tracking the evolution of E_t along trajectories to quantify its magnitude and temporal fluctuations.
- Reviewer asks to report actual runtimes and the baseline CMA-ES without delay strategies to quantify absolute speed-ups.
- Suggests controlled quadratic experiments varying the spectrum to compare solution quality and E_t error as a function of spectral distribution.
- Recommends including a real-world experiment (e.g., policy-gradient optimization in real-time robotics) where speed matters.
- Overall: convincing initial evidence but would be strengthened by the additional experiments to clarify when and how benefits manifest.
