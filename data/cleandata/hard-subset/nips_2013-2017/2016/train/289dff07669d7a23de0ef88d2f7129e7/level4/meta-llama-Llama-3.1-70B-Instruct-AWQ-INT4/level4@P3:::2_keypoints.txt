- Proposes modifying CMA-ES by using Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition to reduce time and storage.
- Paper is well-written and the method appears reasonable.
- Experiments show promising speedups, suggesting potential to become a new standard CMA-ES variant.
- Core idea: replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor; theoretical justification provided.
- Originality is limited—method is an extension of prior work ("A More Efficient Rank-one..."); authors should clarify novel contributions.
- Missing proof of equivalence to the original CMA-ES update rule (formula (2)); must be included for completeness.
- Complexity claim O(μd^2) equals CMA-ES-Ref; authors need a detailed discussion (use Θ notation) explaining why their algorithm is actually faster.
- Claim that path error is small and decreases over time contradicts Hansen; requires experimental validation rather than only final-performance comparison.
- Theoretical analysis of path error is not deterministic; more experiments or proofs are needed to support the claim.
- Minor notation errors: wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk; n in Table 1 should be d; 'F' should be 'f' in line 185.
- In current form, paper is not ready for NIPS due to missing essential content and unclear contributions.
- With revisions addressing proofs, complexity discussion, and path-error validation, the paper could make a significant contribution.
- Recommendation: revise to include missing proofs, detailed complexity justification, and experiments validating path-error claims.
- Review rating: 5/10 (marginally above acceptance); reviewer confidence: 8/10.
