- Proposal: Introduces "Cholesky-CMA-ES" replacing matrix square root in CSA with inverse triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time/space complexity.  
- Claim: Maintains performance while achieving optimal asymptotic storage/runtime for full covariance updates, validated on benchmarks.  
- Relation to prior work: Extends prior Cholesky-based efforts (Krause & Igel 2015; Suttorp et al. 2009) by adapting Cholesky update to standard CMA-ES.  
- Naming conflict: Using "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and the authors fail to acknowledge that prior work.  
- Modest novelty: Main algorithmic contribution is generalizing a rank-1 update to a loop over µ individuals, a minor extension.  
- Theoretical strength: Lemma 1 soundly shows introduced CSA error vanishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical strength: Demonstrates up to 20× runtime improvements for d=64 without increased function evaluations.  
- Complexity strength: Achieves optimal storage O(d²) (vs O(d³) for explicit decomposition) and provides eigenvalues at no extra cost.  
- Originality weakness: Misrepresents novelty due to naming conflict and insufficient contrast with prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES variants.  
- Significance weakness: Claim of "optimal" complexity is overstated—only applies to non-separable problems requiring full covariance; runtime is still quadratic in d.  
- Quality weakness: Omits empirical comparison to Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES (and Krause), possibly misleading readers; Figure 1(b) shows inconsistent plateau behavior.  
- Clarity weakness: Fails to clearly distinguish from Suttorp’s variant and lacks evidence for claimed numerical instability; incorrectly states eigenvalue decompositions are typically done every update.  
- Recommendation: Rename the method to avoid confusion with prior work.  
- Recommendation: Add empirical comparisons to Suttorp and Krause methods and update figures (or include in supplement).  
- Recommendation: Qualify the "optimal" claim to apply only to full covariance updates in non-separable settings.  
- Recommendation: Explicitly state that the key algorithmic extension is the loop over µ individuals in the CSA step.  
- Overall: Practical improvement for full-covariance CMA-ES but incremental and misrepresents prior work; acceptable after revisions.
