This work’s main contribution is the application of triangular Cholesky factorization to the standard Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES). This technique eliminates the square root operation typically required for matrix inversion, resulting in computational savings, particularly when dealing with high-dimensional problems. The authors provide a theoretical justification demonstrating that this factorization does not degrade the algorithm’s performance. The proposed method reduces runtime complexity without significantly altering the number of objective function evaluations. Consequently, this paper represents a valuable implementation of Cholesky factorization within the CMA-ES framework. 

A potential area for further exploration is a more detailed theoretical explanation of why this factorization leads to a ‘numerically more stable’ computation. While the Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES, its reduction in wall-clock time increases with the dimensionality of the search space. Given this, the authors might consider integrating a low-dimensional approximation technique, such as that proposed by Loschilov [2015], into their framework. In many practical applications, such as direct policy search and neural network training, the high dimensionality of the problem is a significant concern that warrants attention.
