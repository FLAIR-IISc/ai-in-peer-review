- Proposal: Maintain and update a triangular Cholesky factorization inside CMA-ES to eliminate explicit matrix square roots and costly matrix inverses.  
- Theoretical claim: Authors argue the modification does not degrade CMA-ES performance.  
- Empirical finding: Method reduces wall-clock runtime while keeping the number of objective function evaluations essentially unchanged.  
- Strength — computational benefit: Avoiding explicit square roots/inverses lowers per-iteration runtime, with gains increasing in problem dimension.  
- Strength — theoretical support: Paper provides justification that the proposed updates preserve algorithmic performance.  
- Strength — empirical behavior: Evaluation counts remain similar to standard CMA-ES while runtime decreases, an attractive practical trade-off.  
- Clarification needed — numerical stability: Request for detailed theoretical explanation or formal bounds on why Cholesky-based updates are numerically more stable and how error propagates versus standard CMA-ES.  
- Clarification needed — runtime breakdown: Ask for an explicit breakdown of where computational savings occur in the update steps and clearer presentation of evaluation count vs. wall-clock time trade-offs.  
- Suggestion: Consider integrating or discussing compatibility with low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to improve applicability to high-dimensional problems.  
- Overall assessment: Practical, well-motivated improvement to CMA-ES runtime that preserves evaluation efficiency, but would be strengthened by stability theory and exploration of low-rank approximations.
