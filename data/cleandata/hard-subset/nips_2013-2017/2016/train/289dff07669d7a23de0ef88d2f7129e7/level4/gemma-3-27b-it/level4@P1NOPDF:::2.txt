This paper adopts Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of CMA-ES. Overall, the paper is clearly written, the proposed method appears reasonable, and the experimental results – particularly the speedup rates – are quite promising. I believe this approach has the potential to become a new standard implementation of CMA-ES. However, while the work is valuable, its originality is limited. It feels like a straightforward extension of “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,” and the methods are, to a large extent, identical. I would appreciate clarification on this point; please correct me if my assessment is inaccurate. 

Furthermore, some important details are surprisingly absent. First, the paper should demonstrate the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2). I understand space is limited and this equivalence has been previously established, but reiterating it is crucial for completeness. Second, the paper claims a computational complexity of O(μd<sup>2</sup>), which is identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A detailed discussion – perhaps using Θ-notation instead of O-notation – is needed to explain *why* the proposed algorithm performs faster in practice. Third, the authors claim that the error in the path is small and decreases over time, which contradicts Hansen’s findings. Given that the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to specifically validate this claim. Comparing only final performance is insufficient; a more detailed analysis tracking error evolution would be beneficial.

Finally, some minor comments: 1) Please carefully review the notation throughout the paper. For example, *w<sub>k</sub>* in Algorithm 2 should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, and *n* in Table 1 should be *d*. 2) Line 185 contains a typographical error: ‘F’ should be ‘f’. 

In its current form, I do not believe this paper meets the standards for acceptance to NIPS.
