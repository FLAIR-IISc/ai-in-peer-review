This paper proposes a Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) that extends the update for triangular Cholesky factors using both rank-1 and rank-µ updates. The authors prove, under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly change the algorithm's behavior. The paper also presents experiments on algorithm performance.

While the paper is well-written and the idea is sound, I am not convinced that its contribution is significant, considering the work of Krause et al. (2015), which uses rank-1 updates to triangular Cholesky factors. The current paper presents a straightforward generalization of this procedure to rank-µ updates in a sequence of rank-1 updates.

It would be beneficial to discuss the theoretical foundation of the CMA-ES in more detail, particularly in relation to the convergence of the covariance matrix Ct. Beyer (2014) provides a convergence analysis of evolutionary algorithms based on information geometry, which may improve the confidence in Lemma 1.

There are a few minor issues with the paper. In equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σt. The same problem occurs in the footnote on page 3. Additionally, in line 94, the word "by" should be deleted from the sentence "which can by be motivated by principles from...".

Overall, the paper is well-organized and easy to follow, but its contribution to the field could be more clearly highlighted.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a new CMA-ES variant that extends the update for triangular Cholesky factors using both rank-1 and rank-µ updates.
* The authors provide a theoretical justification for the modified cumulative step-size adaptation.
* The paper presents experiments on algorithm performance.

Weaknesses:

* The contribution of the paper may not be significant, considering the work of Krause et al. (2015).
* The theoretical foundation of the CMA-ES could be discussed in more detail.
* There are minor issues with the paper, such as typos and incorrect notation.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a new CMA-ES variant that extends the update for triangular Cholesky factors.
* The authors provide a theoretical justification for the modified cumulative step-size adaptation.
* The paper presents experiments on algorithm performance.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The contribution of the paper may not be significant, considering the work of Krause et al. (2015).
* The paper does not provide a clear discussion of the theoretical foundation of the CMA-ES.
* There are minor issues with the paper, such as typos and incorrect notation.

Overall, I would recommend accepting the paper, but with minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above.
