CMA-ES is a leading stochastic optimization algorithm, particularly effective for derivative-free problems. It adapts the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution – equivalently, an underlying metric – through various learning mechanisms. This paper introduces a novel covariance matrix update based on triangular Cholesky factors. This approach offers a significant reduction in both computational complexity and memory requirements compared to traditional updates, without compromising performance. The method is thoroughly evaluated numerically, demonstrating that it maintains the function evaluation efficiency of standard CMA-ES while substantially decreasing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions. I consider this a highly impactful contribution to the optimization field. I anticipate this variant of CMA-ES becoming a default choice for medium- to large-scale problems due to its clear advantages: lower memory footprint, reduced computational cost, and the readily available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix. The paper is well-written and the evaluation is comprehensive. It is fitting to see this work presented at NIPS, given CMA-ES’s strong ties to machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in areas like reinforcement learning and supervised learning. A minor point: please clarify how implementation comparability was ensured regarding CPU and programming language.
