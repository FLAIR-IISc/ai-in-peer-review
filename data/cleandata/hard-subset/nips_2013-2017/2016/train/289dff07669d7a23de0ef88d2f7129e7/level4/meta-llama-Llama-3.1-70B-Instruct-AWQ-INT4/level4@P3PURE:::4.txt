This paper's main contribution is the utilization of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square root in standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing the computational cost associated with matrix inversion. The author provides a theoretically justified approach that demonstrates the proposed method does not compromise the algorithm's performance. Notably, the method achieves a lower runtime complexity without a significant increase in the number of objective function evaluations. This work represents a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES.

A further theoretical explanation would be beneficial to understand why the 'numerically more stable' computation is achieved through this approach. The results suggest that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires a comparable number of objective function evaluations to standard CMA-ES, while reducing wall-clock time, with this advantage increasing with the dimensionality of the search space. Considering the high dimensionality of many practical problems, such as training neural networks in direct policy search, the author may want to explore combining the low-dimensional approximation proposed by Loshchilov (2015) with the existing framework to address this concern.
