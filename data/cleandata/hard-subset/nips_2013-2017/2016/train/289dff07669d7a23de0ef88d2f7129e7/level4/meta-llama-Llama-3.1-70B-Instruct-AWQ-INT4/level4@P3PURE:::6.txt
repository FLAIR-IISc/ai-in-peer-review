The paper proposes a version of CMA-ES with "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity", which the authors claim does not alter the algorithm's behavior but reduces its computational cost. The proposed method, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, is misleadingly named, as this term was first introduced by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009 for a similar algorithm, which is well-established in the field. The authors fail to acknowledge this, instead presenting their variant as a novel contribution.

The proposed method is an incremental modification to the work of Krause et al. (2015). The only algorithmic contribution is the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function, which is already present in Krause et al.'s work, but is now called in a loop for all individuals. Empirically, a difference is observed on the Cigar function in Figure b), where the plateau has distinct objective function values for CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES. This discrepancy may be due to the authors' decision to plot median results.

To provide a comprehensive evaluation, the paper should empirically compare the proposed approach to Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically compare it to Krause's Cholesky-CMA-ES. The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is somewhat misleading, as it is not optimal for functions like the Sphere, where the covariance matrix is not required, or for separable functions, where only the diagonal is necessary. Moreover, the proposed approach is only twice as efficient as Suttorp's Cholesky CMA-ES, which is omitted from Figure 3. This omission may mislead the reader about the actual contribution of the paper. Furthermore, the original paper by Suttorp et al. suggests that their method is not prone to numerical instabilities, contrary to the claims made in this paper.

I do not believe that the proposed approach significantly impacts CMA-ES for large-scale optimization, as the complexity remains quadratic. However, I acknowledge that the proposed modification might be the most efficient way to save time and memory when considering the full covariance matrix.

Overall, I find the work to be too incremental, with the algorithmic contribution being essentially a single "for" loop. To strengthen the paper, I suggest the following modifications: a) resolve the naming issue; b) perform experiments with variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. and update all three figures; if the figures become unreadable, consider showing only the best-performing variant in the main text and providing the full figures in the Supplementary Material; c) better explain the main algorithmic contribution, which is the "for" loop in Algorithm 1, or provide a justification for why this is not the case.

Minor notes: The statement "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is inaccurate, as this is not typically done in proper implementations; instead, it is performed periodically.
