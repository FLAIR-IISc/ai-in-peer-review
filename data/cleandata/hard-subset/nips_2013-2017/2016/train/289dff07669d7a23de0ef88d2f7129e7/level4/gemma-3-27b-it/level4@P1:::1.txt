CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic/randomized algorithm for (derivative-free) optimization. It combines different learning mechanisms for adapting the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can be equivalently viewed as adaptation of an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new variant for updating the covariance matrix based on triangular Cholesky factors. This approach offers a significant reduction in computational complexity and memory requirements compared to previous updates, making it particularly advantageous for large-scale problems. The new method is carefully evaluated numerically, demonstrating that it maintains the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations required to reach a given target, while substantially reducing the wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions. 

I believe this paper represents a very significant contribution to the field of optimization. I anticipate that this variant of CMA-ES will become a default implementation for medium to large-scale problems due to its clear advantages: reduced memory usage, lower computational complexity, and readily available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix. The paper is well-written and the approach is thoroughly evaluated. It is particularly fitting for NIPS given the strong connections between CMA-ES and areas such as machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.
