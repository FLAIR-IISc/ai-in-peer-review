This paper considers a Cholesky covariance matrix optimization development strategy for the triangular Cholesky factor (rank-1 update in sequence) with both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. This paper proves, under the convergence notion of covariance, that the modified cumulative step-size optimization algorithm does not make significant changes in practice. This paper presents experiments on the algorithm's performance.

1. This paper considers the Cholesky CMA-ES. It extends the update for the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. However, I do not think its contribution is significant, given the work of Krauz et al. 2015, which uses rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. This paper presents a straightforward generalization of this process for rank-μ updates in a series of rank-1 updates.

2. Generally, the convergence of covariance $Ct$ in CMA-ES is proven by theoretical analysis (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although it is not based on the exact formulas of CMA-ES. This may increase the confidence of Lemma 1.
