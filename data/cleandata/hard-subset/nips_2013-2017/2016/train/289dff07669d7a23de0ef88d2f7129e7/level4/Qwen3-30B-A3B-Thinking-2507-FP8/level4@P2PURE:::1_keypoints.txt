- CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic derivative-free optimization algorithm.  
- The paper introduces a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The new update reduces computational complexity and memory requirements versus prior methods.  
- The proposed variant is rigorously and numerically evaluated.  
- Results show it preserves standard CMA-ES performance in function-evaluations to target.  
- Results show it substantially reduces wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The contribution is significant and likely to become the default for medium-to-large-scale problems.  
- Advantages include reduced memory, lower computational complexity, and automatic acquisition of covariance eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and relevant to NIPS given CMA-ES ties to ML, information geometry, RL, and supervised learning.
