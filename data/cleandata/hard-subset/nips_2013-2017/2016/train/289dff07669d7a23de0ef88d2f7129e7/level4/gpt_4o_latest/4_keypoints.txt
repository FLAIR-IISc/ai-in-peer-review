- Primary contribution: uses triangular Cholesky factorization to remove the square-root operation in standard CMA-ES, reducing computational cost of matrix inversion.  
- Presents a theoretically justified approach that claims no loss in algorithmic performance.  
- Reduces runtime complexity while not significantly affecting the number of objective function evaluations.  
- Demonstrates a valuable application of Cholesky factorization within the CMA-ES framework.  
- Requests a more detailed theoretical explanation of the claim that the method yields "numerically more stable" computations.  
- Observes that Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains the same evaluation count but reduces wall-clock time, with larger gains in higher-dimensional problems.  
- Suggests exploring integration of the low-dimensional approximation (Loshchilov, 2015) to better handle very high-dimensional tasks such as neural-network direct policy search.
