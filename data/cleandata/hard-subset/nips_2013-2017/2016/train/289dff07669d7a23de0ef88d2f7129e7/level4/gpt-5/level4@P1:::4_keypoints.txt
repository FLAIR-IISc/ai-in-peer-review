- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that stores and updates only lower-triangular A with C = A A^T and replaces C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA to avoid expensive decompositions.  
- Argues the CSA approximation error is small and vanishes as the normalized covariance shape converges, supported by Lemma 1 and exponential averaging.  
- Extends prior triangular/Cholesky update work to full rank-µ CMA-ES and provides a convergence-based theoretical justification.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal storage (triangular factor only) and O(µ d^2) per-iteration covariance updates with cheap A^{-1} computation and direct access to eigenvalues via A’s diagonal.  
- Empirical results show essentially identical function-evaluation counts to reference CMA-ES and large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64), matching complexity analysis.  
- Good practical discussion of when full covariance is needed versus diagonal/low-rank methods and observed cache/L2 effects at large d.  
- Claim of numerical stability for triangular updates and inversion is stated but lacks explicit bounds or detailed error accumulation analysis.  
- Need for empirical comparison of rounding/conditioning behavior versus eigen/sqrt-based updates and tracking of error growth over long runs.  
- Theoretical CSA justification hinges on convergence of normalized covariance shape, which may not hold in stochastic, multimodal, or noisy settings.  
- Requests to characterize error in ||p_σ|| when C drifts and to bound impact on step-size adaptation when E[t]E[t-1]^{-1} deviates from I.  
- Suggests an ablation isolating the CSA approximation (compare A^{-1} vs explicit C^{-1/2} norms) to quantify approximation effects.  
- Invariance concerns: need clearer empirical evaluation of how replacing C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} affects affine/rotation invariance, especially at higher d.  
- Experimental scope limited to noiseless, unconstrained benchmarks; calls for tests under noise, constraints, and RL-like scenarios to show robustness.  
- Recommends expanded comparisons with low-rank/limited-memory CMA-ES at higher d to identify practical crossover points.  
- Requests full reproducibility details (hardware, compiler, BLAS, seeds, stopping criteria) and possibly sensitivity studies (blocking/packed storage).  
- Paper is generally clear but should explicitly state where it departs from standard CMA-ES (only CSA change) and summarize invariance consequences.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of triangular Cholesky updates to full rank-µ CMA-ES with CSA modification and convergence-based justification.  
- Significance: substantial practical speed-ups without extra function evaluations, relevant for medium-to-large d where full covariance is useful.  
- Suggestions: provide formal/empirical stability analysis, quantify CSA approximation error across dimensions, explore hybrids with low-rank methods, and report runtime dependence on µ and delay strategies.  
- Arguments for acceptance: practical impact, simple elegant modification, solid motivation, and careful empirical study including rotated cases.  
- Arguments against acceptance: CSA guarantees are asymptotic and may not hold broadly; experiments omit noisy/realistic settings and empirical quantification of approximation error is limited.
