- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update a triangular Cholesky factor via rank-one updates in O(µ d^2) time, reducing storage and runtime for full-covariance adaptation.
- Replaces inverse matrix square root in CSA with inverse triangular factor and argues the induced rotational error vanishes as covariance shape stabilizes.
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations up to d=256 while substantially reducing wall-clock time, with speedups increasing with dimension.
- Compares against delayed-decomposition CMA-ES variants and a non-triangular Cholesky update baseline.
- Builds on prior factor-update and rank-one Cholesky update work (e.g., Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) but extends to standard CMA-ES with a principled CSA modification.
- Novelty lies in applying factor-based updates to standard CMA-ES with a theoretically motivated CSA adjustment and a numerically stable implementation.
- Technical derivation and rank-one update algorithm are clear and appropriate.
- CSA modification is well-motivated, but the analysis depends on covariance-shape stability and lacks finite-time or nonstationary robustness guarantees.
- Experiments are well chosen (rotations, multiple dimensions) and support runtime claims, but timing-parity and implementation detail reporting are insufficient.
- Incorrect claim: diagonal of triangular Cholesky factor is not generally the square roots of covariance eigenvalues; this statement should be corrected.
- Clarity improvements requested: specify timing/implementation details (language, BLAS/LAPACK, threading, CPU/NUMA, codebase parity).
- Ask for explanation of reduced speedup on Cigar/Discus at d=256 (possible cache, bandwidth, µ scaling, or function-specific reasons).
- Request reporting of how µ scales with d and whether µ influences observed speedups.
- Recommend per-iteration runtime breakdown (sampling, ranking, covariance/factor updates) to attribute speed differences.
- Clarify rotation application (one fixed random rotation per run) and supply seeds/configs for reproducibility; consider releasing code/scripts.
- Significance: important for medium-to-large dimensions where full-covariance CMA-ES is practical; clarifies limits for very high d where quadratic cost still dominates.
- Pros: principled, practical algorithm with asymptotically optimal storage, runtime gains, theoretical justification for CSA change, and numerical stability for L and L^{-1}.
- Cons: analysis is asymptotic and stability-assuming, runtime comparisons need stricter parity, eigenvalue claim is wrong, limited discussion of cache/memory effects at very large d.
- Minor suggestions: document implementation parity, explain diminished speedup cases, add ablation isolating CSA modification effects, compare to LM-CMA, and detail numerical stability safeguards.
- Overall recommendation: strong, practical, and well-evaluated contribution; accept after minor clarifications and correction about eigenvalues.
