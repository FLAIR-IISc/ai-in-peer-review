- Proposes replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky decomposition to reduce time and storage complexity of CMA-ES.
- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES maintaining triangular Cholesky factors, achieving optimal asymptotic storage and runtime for full covariance adaptation.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based updates (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) but extends to standard CMA-ES with theoretical justification.
- Paper is clearly written and well organized.
- Theoretical justification for convergence appears sound.
- Empirical results show significant speedups (up to 20× for d=64) without extra function evaluations.
- Thorough empirical comparisons against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES.
- Practical advantages include numerically stable Cholesky inversion in quadratic time and free eigenvalue computation.
- Originality is limited and method seems a straightforward extension of Krause & Igel (2015).
- Fails to adequately distinguish itself from prior work, problematic given NIPS emphasis on novelty.
- Theoretical claim that path error decreases over time is not sufficiently validated experimentally; need to show error evolution, not just final performance.
- Complexity claim O(μd²) matches CMA-ES-Ref; paper lacks detailed Θ analysis explaining practical speedup.
- Omits explicit proof of equivalence to standard CMA-ES update formula (2), which is essential for validity.
- Contains notation errors (e.g., wk should be αk, n in Table 1 should be d) and a typographical error ('F' → 'f').
- Pro-accept: technically sound, promising practical results, could become standard for large-scale CMA-ES.
- Con-accept: insufficient novelty demonstration, theoretical claims need stronger empirical support, and inadequate differentiation from prior work.
- Recommendation: not ready for NIPS as-is; requires stronger originality argument, detailed Θ runtime analysis, and experiments validating error convergence to be competitive for acceptance.
