This paper explores the use of Cholesky decomposition as an alternative to eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of the CMA-ES algorithm. Overall, the paper is well-written, and the proposed method appears reasonable. The experimental results, particularly the speedup rates, are quite promising, and it is possible that the proposed method could become a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future.

Although the paper has the potential to be meaningful, its originality is limited. Upon reviewing the content, it seems that this work is a relatively straightforward extension of the previous work, "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The methods presented in this paper are essentially identical to those in the previous work. I would appreciate it if the authors could clarify or correct this assessment.

Furthermore, some crucial information is noticeably absent from the paper. Firstly, it would be beneficial to demonstrate whether the proposed algorithm is equivalent to formula (2). Although the proof may have been established in the previous work, and space is limited, this is an essential aspect that should be addressed in this paper.

Secondly, the authors claim that the computational complexity of their algorithm is O(\mu d^2), which is identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A detailed discussion is needed to explain why the proposed algorithm is faster, potentially using the '\Theta' notation instead of the 'O' notation.

Thirdly, the authors assert that the error in the path is small and decreases over time, contradicting Hansen's claim. Given that the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to validate this claim. Comparing only the final performance is insufficient.

Additionally, I have a few minor comments. The authors should carefully review their notation, as there are some inconsistencies. For example, 'wk' in Algorithm 2 should be '\alphak', and 'n' in Table 1 should be 'd'. Furthermore, 'F' should be 'f' in line 185.

In its current form, I do not believe that this paper is suitable for NIPS.
