- Summary: Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces C^{-1/2} with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1}, justified by a lemma showing vanishing rotational error as covariance shape converges, reducing complexity to O(µd²) and matching standard CMA-ES in evaluations while lowering wall-clock time.

- Strength 1 — Practical impact: Turns the O(d³) CMA-ES bottleneck into an O(µd²) update, enabling full-rank CMA-ES to scale to higher dimensions and practical tasks like direct policy search.

- Strength 2 — Theoretical novelty: Provides a novel, elegant justification (Lemma 1) for substituting C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}, addressing a key challenge in prior Cholesky approaches.

- Strength 3 — Empirical validation: Well-designed experiments compare against standard and alternative speed-ups, showing similar convergence in evaluations but substantial wall-clock speedups as d grows.

- Strength 4 — Clarity: The paper is well-written, clearly situating prior work, detailing the method and theory, and presenting results accessibly.

- Weakness 1 — Numerical stability claim: The claim of improved numerical stability is asserted but not substantiated; the paper should provide theoretical arguments or empirical evidence (e.g., condition number tracking).

- Weakness 2 — High-dimensional limits: More discussion is needed on integrating the approach with low-rank or other approximations for very large d (thousands–millions), and how it complements existing scalable CMA-ES variants.

- Overall assessment: Strong, original, and practically significant contribution with solid theory and experiments; minor omissions on stability and large-scale extensions do not detract from its suitability for publication.
