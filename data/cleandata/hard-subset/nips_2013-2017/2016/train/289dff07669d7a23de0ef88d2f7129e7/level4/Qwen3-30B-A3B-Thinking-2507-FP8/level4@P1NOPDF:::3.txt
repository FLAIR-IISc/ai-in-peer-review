

This paper investigates Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) with both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, implemented through a sequence of rank-1 updates. Under the convergence assumption of the covariance matrix, the authors prove that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper presents empirical results on algorithm performance.  

1. The core contribution—extending the Cholesky CMA-ES update to incorporate rank-μ updates via sequential rank-1 updates—lacks sufficient novelty. Krause et al. (2015) already established the rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor, and this work merely generalizes their approach to rank-μ. This represents a straightforward extension rather than a substantial advancement.  

2. The theoretical foundation for covariance convergence in CMA-ES (Beyer, 2014, Evolutionary Computation) relies on asymptotic analysis rather than exact CMA-ES dynamics. Strengthening Lemma 1 with a proof grounded in the precise algorithmic formulation would significantly bolster the paper's theoretical claims.  

3. Several minor issues require correction:  
   - In Equation (1), the denominator should use σₜ (not σ), and this error also appears in Footnote 2 (p. 3).  
   - In Line 94, "which can by be motivated" contains a typo; "by" should be removed ("which can be motivated").  

The empirical evaluation is presented but lacks comparative depth against established CMA-ES variants. While the technical arguments are sound, the incremental nature of the contribution and the minor errors detract from the paper's impact.
