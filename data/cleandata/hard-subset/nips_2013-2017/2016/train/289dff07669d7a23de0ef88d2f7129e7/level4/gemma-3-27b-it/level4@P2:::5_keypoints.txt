- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance-update complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).
- Theoretical claim: under convergence assumptions, the Cholesky approximation converges to the original CMA-ES because an orthogonal matrix E_t approaches identity.
- Empirical results on benchmarks show comparable optimization performance with substantial runtime speed-ups, especially as dimensionality increases.
- Clear problem statement identifying covariance updates as the computational bottleneck in standard CMA-ES.
- Methodological novelty: moves beyond delayed updates by directly approximating covariance adaptation via Cholesky factors.
- Provides theoretical justification for the approximation, though it depends on potentially strong convergence assumptions.
- Paper is well-written and logically organized, making the contributions easy to follow.
- Practical relevance may be limited because objective-function evaluations often dominate total runtime in many applications.
- Performance gains are most pronounced in high-dimensional settings, which may be less common in practice.
- Empirical analysis is limited; additional experiments would strengthen the paper’s claims.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically grounded approach addressing a known bottleneck, with demonstrated speed-ups and clear writing.
- Arguments for rejection: limited practical impact, reliance on strong assumptions, and insufficient empirical validation.
- Suggestion: visualize or quantify the evolution of E_t and compare it between full CMA-ES and Cholesky-CMA-ES to support theoretical claims.
- Suggestion: include runtime comparison against standard CMA-ES without delayed-update strategies for completeness.
- Suggestion: run spectral sensitivity studies (e.g., quadratics with varied spectra) to assess how problem conditioning affects E_t and performance.
- Suggestion: demonstrate the method on a real-world, time-critical application (e.g., robotics policy gradients) to show practical benefits.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution to black-box optimization; recommend acceptance with minor revisions addressing the suggested analyses.
