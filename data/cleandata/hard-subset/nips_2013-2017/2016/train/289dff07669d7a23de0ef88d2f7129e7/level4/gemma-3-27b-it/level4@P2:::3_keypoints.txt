- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that applies rank-1 and rank-µ updates to the triangular Cholesky factor, with theoretical justification that CSA behavior is largely unchanged assuming covariance convergence, and empirical validation.

- Context: Builds on prior Cholesky factor updates (Krause & Igel, 2015) and relies on known covariance convergence results (e.g., Beyer, 2014) for Lemma 1.

- Strength — Theoretical justification: Provides a theoretical argument that Cholesky approximation does not significantly degrade CMA-ES performance.

- Strength — Computational efficiency: Achieves reduced runtime complexity (O(µ d²)) and lower memory use, beneficial for high-dimensional problems.

- Strength — Clear presentation: Paper is well-written and presents algorithms (Alg. 1 & 2) concisely and clearly.

- Strength — Empirical validation: Experiments show comparable function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES with notable wall-clock speedups, especially in higher dimensions.

- Weakness — Incremental contribution: Extension from rank-1 to rank-µ is somewhat straightforward and may be seen as incremental over prior work.

- Weakness — Limited experiments: Evaluation is narrow; needs broader comparisons to other state-of-the-art optimizers and more diverse benchmark functions (constraints, multi-modality, etc.).

- Weakness — Minor errors: A few typos (σ in denominator should be σt in Eq.1 and footnote; wording error "which can by be motivated…").

- Weakness — Dependence on convergence assumption: Theoretical results hinge on covariance convergence; paper should better acknowledge effects if this assumption fails.

- Arguments for acceptance: Offers useful theoretical backing for a practical efficiency improvement to CMA-ES, demonstrates runtime speedups without loss of optimization quality, and is clearly presented.

- Arguments against acceptance: Contribution feels incremental, experimental scope is limited, and there are minor typographical issues.

- Overall assessment: Solid, well-executed contribution that improves CMA-ES efficiency with theoretical support; recommend acceptance with minor revisions (fix typos and expand experiments).
