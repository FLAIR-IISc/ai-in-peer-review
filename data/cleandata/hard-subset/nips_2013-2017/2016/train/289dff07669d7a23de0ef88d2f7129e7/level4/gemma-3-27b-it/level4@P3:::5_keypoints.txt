- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).
- Approximation introduces an orthogonal matrix E_t difference; authors argue E_t converges to identity under certain assumptions.
- Empirical results on standard benchmarks show substantial speedup with maintained optimization performance versus state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants with delay strategies.

- Significant computational speedup, especially beneficial in high-dimensional settings.
- Theoretical justification links the approximation to the behavior of E_t, adding credibility.
- Comprehensive experiments using standard benchmarks and relevant comparisons.
- Clear, well-organized presentation that makes ideas accessible.
- High practical relevance by addressing the covariance-update bottleneck in CMA-ES.

- Theoretical justification depends on convergence assumptions that may not hold in all cases.
- Speedup benefits may be limited when objective evaluation time dominates (oracle domination), particularly in low dimensions.
- Experiments are primarily synthetic; lack of real-world application validation.
- Missing empirical evidence showing the trajectory/behavior of E_t during optimization.

- Addresses an important computational bottleneck in a widely used algorithm.
- Demonstrates improved performance on benchmarks with a plausible theoretical basis.
- Clear writing and thorough experimental evaluation support the contribution.
- Practical relevance and scalability improvements justify publication.

- Practical impact may be limited if objective evaluations dominate runtime.
- Theoretical assumptions underlying convergence may not be generally valid.
- Absence of real-world demonstration reduces confidence in practical utility.

- Add empirical visualizations/quantitative analysis of E_t evolution (e.g., norms vs identity).
- Include direct comparisons to standard CMA-ES without delay strategies.
- Conduct spectral analysis on quadratic functions with varying eigenvalue distributions.

- Overall: strong, valuable contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to bolster empirical validation and analysis.
