- Naming conflict: The term "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is already used by Suttorp et al. (2009); the paper fails to acknowledge this prior work and creates confusion by reusing the name.  
- Marginal algorithmic contribution: The proposed change is essentially replacing a single rankOneUpdate call with a loop over individuals—a minor implementation tweak rather than a substantive algorithmic advance.  
- Lack of justification/context: Authors do not explain why the loop is necessary, why Krause et al. didn’t use it, or how it meaningfully differs from prior methods.  
- Incomplete empirical comparisons: Experiments omit Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES (and Krause’s method), undermining claims of novelty and speedup.  
- Inconsistent empirical results: Figure 2b shows unexplained plateaus on the Cigar function; aggregation/median calculation or sampling variance may explain discrepancies.  
- Misleading "optimal" claim: The title/abstract claim of "optimal covariance update and storage complexity" is overstated—storage is only reduced by a constant factor (O(n²) → O(n²/2)), not asymptotically.  
- Irrelevance on separable problems: On separable functions (e.g., Sphere), covariance updates are unnecessary and proposed changes offer no benefit.  
- Questionable speedup vs. prior work: Claimed 2× speedup over Suttorp is dubious; Suttorp addresses numerical stability and similar storage complexity.  
- Focus on storage over compute: CMA-ES’s main bottleneck is computational cost, not memory, so the paper’s storage-centric contribution is of limited practical impact.  
- Numerical stability misrepresentation: The paper incorrectly implies Suttorp’s method has stability issues; Suttorp explicitly discusses and addresses stability.  
- Minor technical correction needed: Eigenvalue decomposition is not performed every update in practice—it’s done periodically (e.g., every 10–20 iterations).  
- Recommendations: Acknowledge and differentiate from Suttorp (2009); include comparisons to Suttorp and Krause in experiments; clarify and justify the loop in Algorithm 1; remove/soften "optimal" language; fix minor technical inaccuracies.  
- Overall assessment: Highly incremental work with insufficient novelty, misleading claims, and missing comparisons; not suitable for publication without substantial revision.
