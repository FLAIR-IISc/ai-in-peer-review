- The paper renames a variant as "Cholesky-CMA-ES" without acknowledging Suttorp et al. (2009), making the name misleading.  
- The main algorithmic change is minimal: replacing a single rankOneUpdate (Krause et al. 2015) with a loop over individuals.  
- Empirical claims are questionable—e.g., differing plateau values in Fig.1(b) may be a plotting/median artifact.  
- The paper omits empirical comparison to Suttorp et al.'s Cholesky-CMA-ES and fails to clarify the relation to Krause et al.; both comparisons (and updated figures) are needed.  
- The claim of "optimal covariance update and storage complexity" is misleading since separable problems need only a diagonal approximation, making full covariance unnecessary.  
- The authors wrongly assert Suttorp's method is numerically unstable, contradicting the original paper; omitting Suttorp from Fig.3 misrepresents the contribution.  
- The approach does not advance large-scale CMA-ES because complexity remains quadratic; it's a practical optimization for full-covariance scenarios, not a fundamental scaling improvement.  
- The work is overly incremental; authors should (a) correct naming, (b) run comparisons against both Suttorp and Krause variants, and (c) explicitly state or justify that the loop in Algorithm 1 is the core contribution.  
- Minor factual error: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically, not at every update step.
