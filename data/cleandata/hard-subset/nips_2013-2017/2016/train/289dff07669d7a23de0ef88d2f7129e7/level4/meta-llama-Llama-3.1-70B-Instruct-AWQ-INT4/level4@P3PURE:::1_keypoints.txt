- Introduces a new CMA-ES variant that updates covariance via triangular Cholesky factors.  
- Method reduces computational complexity and memory requirements compared to previous approaches.  
- Approach adapts the underlying metric by combining different learning mechanisms for the multivariate normal sampler.  
- Thorough numerical evaluation shows comparable performance to standard CMA-ES in function evaluations.  
- New update significantly reduces wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- Obtains covariance matrix eigenvalues “for free” as an added benefit.  
- Paper is well-written and links CMA-ES to machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Minor clarification requested: ensure implementations used in evaluation are comparable in CPU performance and state which implementation language was used.  
- Minor clarification requested: explain why the dimensionality trend does not hold for Cigar and Discus functions at 256 dimensions.  
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution deserving NIPS publication and strong candidate for default method in medium- to large-scale optimization.
