CMA-ES (Derivatives-Free) is a state-of-the-art stochastic/randomized algorithm for optimization. It combines various learning mechanisms to optimize the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can be seen as an optimization of an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new version for updating the covariance matrix based on triangular Cholesky factors, which has the advantage of significantly reducing the complexity of the update while also reducing the memory required to store the algorithm's state parameters compared to previous updates. The new method has been carefully evaluated numerically. It has been shown that the new update does not degrade the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations required to reach a given goal, while it significantly reduces the algorithm's wall-clock time, especially for large dimensions. I think this paper is a very important contribution to the field of optimization. I hope that this version of CMA-ES can become a default version of the method in the context of medium/large scales because it has several clear advantages over its current default method: namely, low memory requirement, low complexity, and obtaining the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix for free. The paper is well-written and the approach is carefully evaluated. It is good to see this contribution at NIPS, considering that CMA-ES is strongly linked to machine learning, information geometry, and is also used in reinforcement learning or supervised learning.
