This paper proposes a Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) that extends the update for triangular Cholesky factors using both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the modified cumulative step-size adaptation, showing that it does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior under the assumption of converging covariance. The paper also presents experiments demonstrating the algorithm's performance.

While the idea of using Cholesky CMA-ES is not new, the extension to rank-μ updates is a notable contribution. However, it is essential to acknowledge the work of Krause et al. (2015), which used rank-1 updates to triangular Cholesky factors. The current paper can be seen as a straightforward generalization of this procedure to rank-μ updates in a sequence of rank-1 updates.

The convergence analysis of the covariance Ct in CMA-ES, as proofed by Beyer (ECJ, 2014), provides a theoretical foundation for the algorithm. Although this analysis is not based on the exact formulae of CMA-ES, it can still improve the confidence in Lemma 1.

There are a few minor issues with the paper. In equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σt. The same problem occurs in the footnote on page 3. Additionally, in line 94, the word "by" should be deleted from the phrase "which can by be motivated by principles from..."

The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky CMA-ES in reducing the runtime complexity of the algorithm while maintaining a similar number of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES. The comparison with other CMA-ES variants, such as CMA-ES-Ref and Suttorp-CMA-ES, provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed algorithm.

Overall, the paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization, and the proposed Cholesky CMA-ES has the potential to improve the efficiency of CMA-ES in high-dimensional problems.

Arguments For Acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel extension to the CMA-ES algorithm, which can improve its efficiency in high-dimensional problems.
* The theoretical justification for the convergence of the modified cumulative step-size adaptation is sound and well-supported.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm in reducing runtime complexity while maintaining a similar number of objective function evaluations.

Arguments Against Acceptance:

* The contribution of the paper may be seen as a straightforward generalization of existing work, rather than a significant breakthrough.
* There are minor issues with the paper, such as typos and notation errors, which need to be addressed.
* The comparison with other CMA-ES variants could be more comprehensive, including a broader range of benchmark functions and problem sizes.

Recommendation:

I recommend accepting the paper, but with minor revisions to address the issues mentioned above. The paper presents a valuable contribution to the field, and with some polishing, it can become a strong addition to the conference proceedings.
