1. Misleading name: "Cholesky-CMA-ES" was introduced by Suttorp et al. (2009) and the paper fails to acknowledge this prior work.  
2. Incremental modification: The method is a minor change to Krause et al. (2015); main algorithmic novelty is repeatedly calling rankOneUpdate in a loop.  
3. Questionable empirical evidence: Differences shown (e.g., Cigar function plateau) may arise from median-plotting choices and need more thorough evaluation.  
4. Missing baselines: No comparison to Suttorp (2009) or Krause (2015) variants, leaving the actual contribution unclear.  
5. Misleading optimality claim: "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is not universally optimal (e.g., Sphere or separable functions).  
6. Efficiency/instability claim disputed: The method is only ~2× more efficient than Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES, and claims of numerical instability contradict the original paper; omission in Fig. 3 is concerning.  
7. Limited impact on complexity: The modification does not change the quadratic complexity of CMA-ES for large-scale problems, though it may help when using the full covariance.  
8. Overall assessment: Work is too incremental—main contribution appears to be a single for-loop—and needs stronger justification.  
9. Recommendations: Acknowledge prior work/rename, include experiments with Suttorp and Krause variants and update figures, and clearly explain or strengthen the algorithmic contribution.  
10. Minor correction: The claim that eigenvalue decomposition is performed at each update is inaccurate—proper implementations do it periodically.
