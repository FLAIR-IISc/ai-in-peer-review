- Summary: Paper proposes updating the Cholesky factor directly to reduce CMA-ES covariance update complexity from O(n³) to O(n²).
- Strength: Practical engineering solution that effectively reduces computational overhead for full covariance updates in CMA-ES.
- Major concern — Naming and prior work omission: Calling the method "Cholesky-CMA-ES" ignores and misattributes prior work by Suttorp et al. (2009).
- Major concern — Incremental contribution: Core novelty appears to be a minor implementation change (applying rankOneUpdate per individual) relative to Krause et al. (2015) and needs stronger justification.
- Major concern — Missing baselines: Experimental evaluation omits the original Suttorp et al. Cholesky-CMA-ES and other relevant variants, biasing performance claims.
- Major concern — Unexplained empirical discrepancy: Reported differences (e.g., plateau value on the Cigar function) contradict the authors’ claim of unchanged algorithmic behavior and need investigation.
- Major concern — Overstated complexity/optimality claims: Claiming "optimal" update/storage complexity is misleading since cheaper alternatives exist for simpler problem classes (e.g., diagonal updates).
- Major concern — Overstated scalability claims: O(n²) complexity remains prohibitive for truly large-scale optimization, so the paper’s impact on scalability is limited.
- Major concern — Modest performance gain: Improvement over Suttorp et al. appears to be roughly a factor of two—meaningful but not transformative.
- Suggestion: Fix naming and properly cite Suttorp et al. (2009); choose a distinct, unambiguous name.
- Suggestion: Expand experiments to include Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. variants, present key comparisons in the main text, and full results in supplementary material.
- Suggestion: Investigate and explain the Cigar-function discrepancy and the possibly misleading plotting/median artifacts.
- Suggestion: Clearly articulate and justify the algorithmic novelty (why the per-individual loop is a significant conceptual advance).
- Minor comment: Correct the inaccurate statement that eigenvalue decompositions are performed every update—modern implementations amortize that cost by doing decompositions periodically (e.g., every n/10 generations).
