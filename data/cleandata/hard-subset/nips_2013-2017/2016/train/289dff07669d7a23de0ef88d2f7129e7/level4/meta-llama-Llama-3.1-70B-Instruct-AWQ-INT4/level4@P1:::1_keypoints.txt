- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (an underlying metric).
- The paper introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, updating the covariance via triangular Cholesky factors instead of the usual matrix square root.
- Cholesky-CMA-ES reduces runtime complexity and memory footprint, making it more suitable for large-scale optimization.
- The method also provides the covariance eigenvalues without extra cost.
- The paper gives a clear theoretical background and a detailed description of the algorithm, properties, and advantages.
- Empirical results demonstrate Cholesky-CMA-ES is efficient and effective on benchmark functions compared to other CMA-ES variants.
- The manuscript is well-structured, clearly written, and accessible to a broad ML audience.
- Minor issue: insufficient detail on implementation/CPU comparability across experiments (e.g., language, LAPACK usage, other factors).
- Minor issue: unexplained anomaly in Fig. 2 where Cigar and Discus at 256D do not show the expected wall-clock time differences.
- Overall: significant and valuable contribution to optimization with strong relevance to ML, information geometry, and RL, supporting acceptance.
