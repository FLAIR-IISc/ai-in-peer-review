The covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) is a leading derivative-free optimization algorithm, effectively adapting the parameters of a multivariate normal distribution through sophisticated learning mechanisms. This paper presents a novel variant, the Cholesky-CMA-ES, which utilizes a triangular Cholesky factorization for covariance matrix updates. This approach offers significant advantages over traditional methods, notably reduced computational complexity and memory requirements. The authors provide a thorough numerical evaluation demonstrating that the Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains comparable performance in terms of function evaluations while substantially decreasing wall-clock time, particularly in high-dimensional problems. 

This is a significant contribution to the optimization field. The proposed method addresses a key bottleneck in CMA-ES – the computational cost of covariance matrix updates – offering a practical improvement that could make the algorithm more accessible for medium to large-scale problems. The benefits of reduced memory footprint, lower complexity, and the readily available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix are compelling. The paper’s relevance to NIPS is well-established, given CMA-ES’s strong ties to machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in areas like reinforcement learning and supervised learning.

The paper is clearly written and well-organized. The motivation is well-articulated, and the theoretical justification for the approximation is sound. The experimental setup is comprehensive, and the results are presented effectively. The connection to previous work, including alternative covariance update strategies like those of Suttorp et al. [2009] and Krause & Igel [2015], is appropriately acknowledged.

**Strengths:**

*   **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method is mathematically grounded, with a clear justification for its approximation and its impact on algorithm behavior.
*   **Computational Efficiency:** The Cholesky factorization approach demonstrably reduces computational complexity, particularly for high-dimensional problems.
*   **Practical Impact:** The reduced memory requirements and faster runtime make CMA-ES more feasible for a wider range of applications.
*   **Thorough Evaluation:** The extensive experimental results convincingly demonstrate the performance benefits of the proposed method across various benchmark functions.
*   **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is well-written, logically structured, and easy to understand.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Implementation Details:** While the authors mention using the Shark library, a more detailed discussion of implementation specifics—such as the programming language used and compilation flags—would enhance reproducibility and facilitate comparisons with other implementations. A clarification regarding CPU specifications for different experiments would also be beneficial.
*   **Dimensionality Scaling for Specific Functions:** The statement that higher dimensionality leads to more pronounced performance differences (referencing Figure 2) doesn’t fully hold for the Cigar and Discus functions at d = 256. An explanation for this observation would strengthen the analysis.
*   **Convergence Guarantee:** While Lemma 1 provides a theoretical basis, a more formal discussion of the convergence properties of the Cholesky-CMA-ES compared to the standard CMA-ES would be valuable.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a novel and theoretically sound improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm.
*   The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the practical benefits of the proposed method, particularly in terms of runtime.
*   The paper addresses a significant bottleneck in CMA-ES, making it more applicable to a broader range of problems.
*   The work is well-written, clearly explained, and relevant to the NIPS audience.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The lack of detailed implementation specifics could hinder reproducibility.
*   The observed discrepancy in dimensionality scaling for certain benchmark functions requires further investigation.
*   A more rigorous analysis of convergence properties would strengthen the theoretical foundation.

**Overall Assessment:**

Despite minor weaknesses, the Cholesky-CMA-ES represents a significant advancement in the field of derivative-free optimization. The clear benefits in terms of computational efficiency and memory usage, coupled with the sound theoretical justification and thorough experimental evaluation, make this a valuable contribution. I strongly recommend acceptance.
