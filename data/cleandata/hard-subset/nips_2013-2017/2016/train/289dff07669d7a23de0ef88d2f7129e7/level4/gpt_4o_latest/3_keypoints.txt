- Studies Cholesky CMA-ES with rank-1 and rank-μ updates applied to the triangular Cholesky factor (realized as a sequence of rank-1 updates).  
- Shows that under covariance convergence assumption, the modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) has no significant impact on algorithm behavior.  
- Provides experimental results evaluating algorithm performance.  
- Contribution is limited: Krause et al. (2015) already use rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor; this work mainly generalizes that to rank-μ via sequential rank-1 updates.  
- Prior theoretical analyses of covariance convergence (e.g., Beyer 2014) give additional support to Lemma 1 despite different formulaic assumptions.  
- Minor issues to correct: equation (1) and footnote 2 should use sigma_t instead of sigma; fix typo "which can by be motivated…" (remove "by").
