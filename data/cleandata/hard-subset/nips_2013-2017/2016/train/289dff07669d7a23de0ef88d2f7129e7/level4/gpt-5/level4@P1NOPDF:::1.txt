CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It combines multiple learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can equivalently be viewed as adapting an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new covariance-matrix update based on triangular Cholesky factors. Compared to previous updates, it significantly reduces both the computational complexity of the update and the memory required to store the algorithm’s state.

The numerical evaluation is careful and convincing. The results show that the new update preserves the performance of standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations needed to reach a prescribed target, while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions. I view this as a significant contribution to optimization. I expect this variant of CMA-ES could become the default for medium- and large-scale problems, given its clear advantages over the current default method: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and obtaining the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix essentially for free. The paper is well written, and the empirical analysis is thorough. It is also a welcome contribution to NIPS, given CMA-ES’s strong connections to machine learning and information geometry, and its use in reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
