- Significance: Addresses CMA-ES's O(d^3) bottleneck by reducing covariance updates to O(µd^2), extending applicability to higher dimensions where evaluations are cheap.  
- Quality: Method is technically sound—uses valid rank-one Cholesky updates and provides Lemma 1 to justify the CSA approximation.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written; Algorithm 1 and experiments clearly present the method and support the wall-clock speedup claim.  
- Practical value: Empirical speedup is substantial (e.g., >20× at d=64) without increasing function-evaluation counts.  

- Prior-work naming issue: The name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" was used by Suttorp et al. (2009) and is not acknowledged.  
- Incrementality vs prior work: The algorithm appears an incremental extension of Krause & Igel (2015) from (1+1) to rank-µ updates; novelty is not clearly delineated.  
- Missing baselines: Experiments omit direct comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) and lack detailed comparison to Krause & Igel (2015); the claim that Suttorp's method is unstable is unsubstantiated.  
- Overstated claims: Title/abstract claim of "optimal" complexity and "large-scale" applicability are misleading—optimality holds only among full-covariance methods and O(µd^2) still limits very high dimensions.  
- Result discrepancy: On the Cigar function (Fig.1b) Cholesky-CMA-ES reaches a worse plateau than reference CMA-ES, contradicting the claim of identical behavior and requiring explanation.  

- Suggestion: Acknowledge Suttorp et al. (2009) or rename/position the algorithm to avoid confusion.  
- Suggestion: Explicitly state contributions beyond Krause & Igel (2015), e.g., extension to rank-µ and the theoretical justification via Lemma 1.  
- Suggestion: Include Suttorp et al. (2009) in experiments and provide evidence regarding numerical stability claims.  
- Suggestion: Explain the Cigar function performance discrepancy.  
- Suggestion: Moderate language about "optimality" and "large-scale" to specify scope (e.g., optimal for full covariance updates).  

- Summary: A practically valuable, well-justified speedup to CMA-ES, but significance is obscured by omitted prior work, incomplete comparisons, and some overstated claims—addressing these would materially strengthen the paper.
