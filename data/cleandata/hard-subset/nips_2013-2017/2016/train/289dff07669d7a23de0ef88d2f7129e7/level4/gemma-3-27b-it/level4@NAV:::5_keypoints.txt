- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a new covariance update using Cholesky decomposition to avoid full covariance and its square root.  
- Reduces computational complexity from O(d^3) (original CMA-ES) to O(μ d^2), with d dimension and μ sample size.  
- Key algorithmic change: maintain and update Cholesky factors instead of the full covariance matrix.  
- Omits estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et each iteration, a major departure from standard CMA-ES.  
- Provides an asymptotic argument that Et converges to the identity under certain covariance convergence assumptions, justifying the approximation.  
- Empirically demonstrates speedup and preserved optimization performance on benchmark functions versus two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants with delayed covariance updates.  
- Notes practical limitation: in many real problems, objective evaluation time dominates, so CMA-ES runtime improvements may be negligible.  
- Notes applicability limitation: biggest gains occur for high-dimensional problems with extremely fast (millisecond) evaluations, whereas CMA-ES is often used for tens of variables.  
- Recommends visualizing Et evolution by running full CMA-ES and Cholesky-CMA-ES in parallel to better understand Et behavior.  
- Recommends including runtime and performance comparisons to standard CMA-ES without any delay strategies for a fuller baseline.  
- Suggests simulations on quadratic functions varying spectral distributions to study solution quality and Et error (e.g., following Stich & Müller, PPSN 2012).  
- Suggests validating practical impact on a real-world, time-critical application (e.g., policy gradients in real-time robotics) where speed matters and parameter space is moderate.
