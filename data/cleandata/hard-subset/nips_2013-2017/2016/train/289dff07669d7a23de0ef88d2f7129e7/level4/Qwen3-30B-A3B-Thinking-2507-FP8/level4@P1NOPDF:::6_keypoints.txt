- The name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is problematic because Suttorp et al. (2009) already proposed that variant and the paper fails to acknowledge it.  
- The proposed method is essentially an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015), not a novel algorithm.  
- The sole algorithmic change is replacing a single rankOneUpdate with a loop over all individuals, but the paper does not clearly explain the loop’s significance or non-triviality.  
- Figure 3b shows differing plateaus on the Cigar function (likely due to median plotting), but the paper does not investigate or explain this discrepancy.  
- The authors omit empirical comparisons to Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause et al.’s method, misrepresenting the contribution and complexity claims.  
- For separable problems (e.g., Sphere), a full covariance matrix is unnecessary and the proposed method offers no advantage over diagonal updates.  
- The paper incorrectly claims Suttorp’s variant is numerically unstable; Suttorp addressed stability and the paper fails to acknowledge that.  
- The complexity claim of "optimal" is overstated: the method still requires quadratic time for full-rank covariance matrices.  
- The contribution is incremental and modest; algorithmic novelty is minimal (just a loop), weakening the paper’s impact.  
- The authors should rename and cite Suttorp (2009), include comparisons to Suttorp and Krause in experiments, and explicitly state/justify that the loop over individuals is the core contribution.  
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is not performed every update in standard implementations but periodically; the paper’s claim is inaccurate.  
- Overall, lack of contextualization, misleading terminology, and omission of key prior work undermine the paper’s novelty and impact; revisions are essential.
