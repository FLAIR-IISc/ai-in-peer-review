- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root inverse with inverse of triangular Cholesky factor and modify CSA to avoid costly matrix square-root computations.
- Provides theoretical justification that the Cholesky substitution preserves algorithm performance and asymptotic optimality.
- Experimental results show significant runtime reduction in higher dimensions while keeping the number of objective function evaluations largely unchanged.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES attempts and addresses their inability to directly replace the matrix square root required by standard CMA-ES.
- Places work in context of derivative-free optimization literature and ML applications like RL and hyperparameter tuning.
- Strength: substantial computational efficiency improvement, especially for high-dimensional problems.
- Strength: clear theoretical analysis and modification to CSA that increases confidence in algorithm behavior.
- Strength: well-written paper with clear technical exposition and comprehensive benchmark comparisons to CMA-ES variants.
- Weakness: insufficient detail on how Cholesky factorization improves numerical stability compared to eigenvalue decomposition.
- Weakness: limited discussion and no empirical analysis of the error introduced by replacing st with ŝt.
- Weakness: brief treatment of integration with low-dimensional approximation techniques; unclear if Cholesky can be applied to reduced subspaces.
- Weakness: experiments are robust on benchmarks but lack evaluation on complex real-world ML applications to demonstrate practical utility.
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a core computational bottleneck in CMA-ES with solid theory and empirical runtime gains.
- Argument against acceptance: benefits mainly reduce runtime (not evaluations), stability and low-rank integration issues need more exploration, and real-world validation is limited.
- Overall recommendation: strong, practical, and theoretically sound contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor improvements suggested.
