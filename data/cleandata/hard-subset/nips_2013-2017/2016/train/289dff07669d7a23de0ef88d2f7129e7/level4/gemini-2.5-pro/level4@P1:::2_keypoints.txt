- Addresses CMA-ES scalability by replacing O(d^3) eigen-decomposition with O(d^2) Cholesky factor updates.  
- Empirical results show substantial wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20x at d=64) without more function evaluations.  
- Potential to become standard CMA-ES implementation for higher-dimensional problems if theory holds.  
- Main originality concern: largely appears as an incremental application of Krause & Igel (2015) rank-one Cholesky updates to CMA-ES.  
- Paper lacks a clear statement of novel contributions beyond prior rank-one Cholesky update work.  
- Missing derivation: need explicit proof that rank-one updates to A equal the covariance matrix update in Equation (2).  
- Complexity analysis is incomplete—claimed O(µd^2) needs clearer Θ notation and comparison to amortized costs of reference CMA-ES.  
- Core approximation (replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA) is insufficiently justified theoretically; reliance on informal slow-change arguments is weak.  
- Insufficient empirical validation of the CSA approximation; recommend experiments comparing CSA path vectors p_σ between original and proposed methods.  
- Minor issues: notation and typos (e.g., wk vs. αk in Algorithm 2, inconsistent use of n vs. d in Table 1, 'F' vs. 'f' on line 185).  
- Conclusion: promising empirical work but originality, missing derivations, complexity clarity, and stronger validation are required before publication.
