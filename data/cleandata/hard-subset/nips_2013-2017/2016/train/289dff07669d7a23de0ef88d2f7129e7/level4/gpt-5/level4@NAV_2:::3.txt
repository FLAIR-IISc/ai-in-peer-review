This paper studies a Cholesky-based variant of CMA-ES that maintains a triangular Cholesky factor and incorporates both rank-1 and rank-µ contributions by expressing the latter as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors argue, under an assumption about the convergence of the covariance, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA)—which replaces C^{-1/2} with the inverse triangular factor—does not materially alter the algorithm’s behavior. Empirical results comparing this method to standard CMA-ES variants are presented.

1. The paper focuses on a Cholesky CMA-ES and extends the triangular Cholesky-factor update to include both rank-1 and rank-µ components. However, the contribution seems incremental in view of Krause and Igel (2015), which already provided a triangular Cholesky rank-1 update; the present work mainly generalizes that approach to rank-µ by composing a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2. In general, convergence properties of the covariance Ct in CMA-ES have been analyzed theoretically (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), albeit not for the exact CMA-ES formulas. This lends additional credibility to Lemma 1.
