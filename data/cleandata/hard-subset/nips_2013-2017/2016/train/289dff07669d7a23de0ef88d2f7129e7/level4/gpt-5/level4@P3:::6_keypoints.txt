- Paper proposes maintaining a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A and using A−1 in CSA to reduce runtime/memory for full-covariance CMA-ES.
- Authors provide a quadratic-time rank-one update for A achieving O(µ d^2) per-iteration and O(d^2) storage, with inexpensive eigenvalue and A−1 access.
- Empirical results on benchmarks (rotated versions, up to d=256) show similar evaluation counts to reference CMA-ES but substantial wall-clock speedups (>20× at moderate d).
- Builds on prior cost-reduction work (periodic decomposition, separable/diagonal structures) and factor-based updates; closely related to Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015).
- Practical contribution: useful speed/memory improvements when function evaluations are cheaper than matrix updates.
- Sound engineering: triangular factor gives optimal storage, free diagonal eigenvalues, stable O(d^2) inverse and update routine.
- Theoretical support: plausible argument that using A−1 in CSA induces vanishing error as covariance stabilizes, though not a full proof.
- Empirical evaluation is broad and includes rotation-invariance checks and scaling studies.
- Naming/attribution issue: should clearly acknowledge Suttorp et al. (2009) and distinguish triangular vs non-triangular Cholesky variants.
- Incremental novelty: main algorithmic changes extend known triangular-factor updates to µ>1 and approximate CSA, which is conceptually modest.
- Overgeneralized “optimal” claims: O(µ d^2) and O(d^2) optimality holds only for full-covariance adaptation, not separable/diagonal cases.
- Baselines and fairness concerns: inconsistent inclusion of Suttorp-CMA-ES and delayed-decomposition CMA-ES in plots; comparisons to Krause & Igel (2015) not explicit.
- Numerical stability claims are contradictory with prior reports and need empirical evidence (conditioning, breakdown rates, reconstruction accuracy).
- CSA approximation analysis relies on convergence assumptions that may not hold; finite-time error quantification and ablation studies are needed.
- Experimental details lacking: clarify median computation, include dispersion (IQR/error bars), report success rates and explain Cigar plateau differences.
- Clarity and references: fix misleading statements about decomposition frequency, correct reference formatting, and add pointers/derivation for Algorithm 2.
- Evaluation summary: technically sound core and correct complexity claims for full covariance, but heuristic theoretical justification and uneven baselines weaken the contribution.
- Originality and significance: limited originality but moderate practical significance for full-covariance CMA-ES; does not change asymptotic barriers for large-scale settings.
- Recommended revisions: resolve naming, include comprehensive baselines, clarify novelty and CSA role, temper “optimal” messaging, provide stability evidence, and fix clarity/experimental reporting.
- Overall recommendation: weak reject in current form due to incremental novelty and missing/ambiguous baselines; could become a solid practical contribution after requested revisions.
