This paper presents a significant contribution to the field of evolutionary computation by leveraging the triangular Cholesky factorization technique to eliminate the square root in the standard Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES). This modification has the potential to reduce computational costs associated with matrix inversion, thereby enhancing the efficiency of the algorithm. The author provides a theoretically grounded approach, demonstrating that this adaptation does not compromise the algorithm's performance.

The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES method achieves a reduction in runtime complexity without incurring a substantial increase in the number of objective function evaluations. This is a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES, offering a promising avenue for improving the algorithm's scalability.

However, it would be beneficial for the author to provide a more in-depth theoretical explanation for why the proposed method is considered "numerically more stable." While the paper demonstrates that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES, it is evident that the wall-clock time is reduced, with this advantage becoming more pronounced as the dimensionality of the search space increases.

A potential direction for future research could be the integration of the low-dimensional approximation technique proposed by Loshchilov (2015) into the existing framework. In many practical applications, such as training neural networks in direct policy search, the high dimensionality of the problems is a significant concern that cannot be neglected. Exploring the combination of these approaches may yield further improvements in the efficiency and scalability of CMA-ES.
