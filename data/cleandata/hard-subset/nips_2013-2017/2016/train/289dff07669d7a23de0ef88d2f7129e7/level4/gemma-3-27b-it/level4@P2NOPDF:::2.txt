This paper presents an interesting application of Cholesky decomposition as an alternative to eigen-decomposition within the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, aiming to reduce computational time and storage requirements. The paper is generally well-written and the proposed method appears sound; the reported experimental results, particularly the observed speedup, are encouraging and suggest potential for this approach to become a standard variant of CMA-ES. 

However, despite the potential value of this work, I have concerns regarding its originality and completeness. The core methodology feels highly similar to the approach outlined in “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,” and appears to be a relatively incremental extension. While I may be mistaken, the fundamental techniques appear nearly identical. A clearer differentiation from this prior work would significantly strengthen the submission.

Furthermore, several key elements are missing or insufficiently addressed. First, establishing the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2) – even with acknowledgment of its prior proof – is crucial for clarity and completeness within this paper. The limited space is understandable, but demonstrating this connection is essential for a rigorous presentation. 

Second, the claim of O(μd²) computational complexity, mirroring that of CMA-ES-Ref, requires further justification. While the big-O notation matches, the paper asserts faster performance. A more detailed analysis, potentially utilizing Θ-notation to specify tighter bounds on the computational cost, is necessary to explain the observed speedup. This analysis should focus on *why* the Cholesky decomposition leads to practical improvements despite the comparable asymptotic complexity.

Third, the paper posits a decreasing error in path length, which directly contradicts established findings by Hansen. Given the non-deterministic nature of the theoretical analysis, this claim demands empirical validation beyond simply comparing final performance. Experiments tracking the error evolution over time are needed to support this assertion; a single point comparison is insufficient.

Finally, several minor points require attention. The notation should be carefully reviewed for consistency; specifically, *w<sub>k</sub>* in Algorithm 2 should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, and *n* in Table 1 should be *d*. Additionally, “F” on line 185 should be corrected to “f”. 

In its current form, I do not believe this paper meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS. Addressing the concerns regarding originality, completeness of analysis, and providing supporting empirical evidence would significantly improve the manuscript.
