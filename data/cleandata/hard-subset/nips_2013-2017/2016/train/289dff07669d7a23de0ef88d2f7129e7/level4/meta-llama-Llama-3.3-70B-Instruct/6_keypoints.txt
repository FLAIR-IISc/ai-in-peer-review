- Proposes a modified CMA-ES called "Cholesky-CMA-ES" claiming optimal covariance update and storage without changing algorithmic behavior, reducing computational cost.
- Naming is problematic because T. Suttorp et al. (2009) already used "Cholesky-CMA-ES" and the paper does not acknowledge this precedent.
- Method builds on Krause et al. (2015); the main algorithmic change is iteratively applying rankOneUpdate for all individuals, a technique present in Krause et al.'s work.
- Empirical discrepancy on the Cigar function likely arises from plotting median results rather than reflecting an algorithmic difference.
- Paper lacks direct comparisons with Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause's variant, which are necessary to validate claims.
- Claiming "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading because optimality depends on problem context (e.g., Sphere or separable functions).
- The proposed approach is only about twice as efficient as Suttorp's method, and the paper's claim that Suttorp's approach is numerically unstable contradicts Suttorp's original findings.
- Omission of Suttorp's method from Figure 3 can mislead readers about the paper's actual contribution.
- Although it reduces time and memory for full covariance matrices, the approach still suffers from quadratic complexity, limiting impact on large-scale optimization.
- The contribution is largely incremental — essentially adding a single "for" loop — rather than a substantial algorithmic advance.
- Recommended fixes: resolve naming issue, run experiments including Suttorp and Krause variants and update figures, and clarify the actual algorithmic contribution.
- Minor corrections needed (e.g., correct frequency of eigenvalue decomposition in proper implementations).
