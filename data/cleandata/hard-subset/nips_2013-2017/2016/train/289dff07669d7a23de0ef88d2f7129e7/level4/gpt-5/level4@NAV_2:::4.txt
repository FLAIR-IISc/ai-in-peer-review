The central contribution is to employ a triangular Cholesky factorization to remove the explicit matrix square root in standard CMA-ES, thereby cutting the computational cost of covariance handling and facilitating cheaper matrix inversions. The authors provide a theoretical rationale suggesting this substitution does not degrade the algorithm’s performance, and empirically the method lowers runtime without materially changing the number of objective function evaluations. Overall, this is a useful application of Cholesky-based updates within CMA-ES. Could the authors expand on the theoretical basis for the claimed “greater numerical stability”? The results indicate that Cholesky-CMA-ES matches the standard CMA-ES in evaluation count while reducing wall-clock time, with gains growing in higher dimensions. Given that many practical problems (e.g., neural network policy search) are high dimensional, would it be possible to integrate the low-dimensional/limited-memory approximation of Loshchilov (2015) within this framework?
