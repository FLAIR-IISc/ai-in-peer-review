Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing the covariance matrix square root with a triangular Cholesky factor to reduce runtime complexity while preserving performance.

Provides theoretical justification and empirical evidence showing reduced wall-clock time with the same number of objective evaluations as standard CMA-ES.

Paper is well-written with clear explanation of background, algorithm, and properties.

Triangular Cholesky factorization significantly reduces computational cost of matrix inversion compared to standard CMA-ES.

Balanced contribution: solid theoretical foundation supported by well-designed empirical experiments and clear results.

Originality: novel application of triangular Cholesky factorization within CMA-ES.

High significance: method has potential impact across evolutionary computation, machine learning, optimization, and engineering design.

Weakness: lacks a clear, detailed explanation of why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable."

Weakness: does not address scalability to high-dimensional problems common in practical applications (e.g., policy search, neural network training).

Suggested extension: consider combining with low-dimensional approximations (Loshchilov, 2015) to handle high-dimensional settings.

Recommendation: accept the paper but require the authors to address the identified weaknesses.

Requested revisions: add a detailed explanation of numerical stability claims and discuss or evaluate high-dimensional extensions/approaches.
