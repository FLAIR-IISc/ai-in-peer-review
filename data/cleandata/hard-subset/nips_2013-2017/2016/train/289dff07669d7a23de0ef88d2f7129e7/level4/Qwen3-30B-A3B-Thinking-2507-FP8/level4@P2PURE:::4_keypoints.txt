- Main contribution: apply triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square root in standard CMA-ES, reducing computational cost of matrix inversion.  
- Theoretical justification: authors argue the approach does not degrade algorithmic performance.  
- Runtime vs evaluations: technique reduces runtime complexity while keeping the number of objective function evaluations essentially unchanged.  
- Practical impact: yields lower wall-clock time with speedup that scales with search-space dimensionality.  
- Request for clarity: authors should further explain theoretically why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable."  
- Suggested extension: consider integrating the low-dimensional approximation method from Loshchilov (2015) into this framework.  
- Motivation for extension: combining approaches could address high-dimensional challenges in applications like direct policy search for neural-network training.
