This paper considers Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy with both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, applied sequentially as rank-1 updates. The paper proves, under convergence assumptions on the covariance matrix, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm’s behavior. Experimental results demonstrating algorithm performance are also presented.

1. This paper extends the Cholesky CMA-ES by incorporating both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. However, I find the contribution to be incremental, particularly considering the work of Krause et al. (2015), which already utilized rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. This paper presents a straightforward generalization of that procedure to rank-\mu updates, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. 

2. The convergence of the covariance matrix $C_t$ in CMA-ES is generally proven through theoretical analysis (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), albeit not always based on the precise formulae of the algorithm itself. Referencing this broader body of work may strengthen the justification for Lemma 1.
