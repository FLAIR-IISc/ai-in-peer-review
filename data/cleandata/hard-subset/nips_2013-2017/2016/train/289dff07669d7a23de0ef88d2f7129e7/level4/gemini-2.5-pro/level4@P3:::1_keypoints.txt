- Addresses CMA-ES bottleneck by maintaining/updating a triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance update cost from O(d^3) to asymptotically optimal O(µ d^2).  
- Replaces expensive C^{-1/2} in CSA with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1}, enabling efficient normalization.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1): the orthogonal matrices relating sqrt(C) and Cholesky factor converge, so rotational error from the substitution vanishes.  
- Retains original CMA-ES optimization performance (similar function-evaluation counts) while achieving large wall-clock speedups, especially as dimension grows.  
- Empirical validation is thorough and shows scaling benefits aligned with complexity analysis across benchmark functions.  
- Novel and technically sound integration of Cholesky updates into standard CMA-ES and CSA; first work to justify this substitution formally.  
- Significant practical impact: makes CMA-ES feasible for medium-to-large problems and relevant applications (policy search, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Algorithm is numerically stable; Cholesky diagonal gives free insight into covariance conditioning/eigenvalues.  
- Paper is very well-written, clear, and well-organized with good connection to prior work.  
- Minor empirical note: speedup tapering at d=256 for Cigar/Discus likely due to L2 cache effects—suggest calling this out in the figure caption.  
- Minor reproducibility suggestion: explicitly confirm all compared variants were implemented/benchmarked within the same framework (Shark/LAPACK) to avoid implementation confounds.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — significant, novel, theoretically justified, and empirically validated contribution.
