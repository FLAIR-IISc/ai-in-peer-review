- Proposes replacing the matrix square root in CSA with the inverse triangular Cholesky factor, calling it "Cholesky-CMA-ES."
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) but fails to adequately acknowledge them.
- Lemma 1 theoretically justifies that the error from replacing the square root with the Cholesky factor vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Experiments show large wall-clock speedups (up to 20x at d=64) while keeping comparable function-evaluation counts.
- Method conveniently provides eigenvalues of the covariance matrix without extra cost.
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation of the algorithmic modification.
- Strength: theoretically sound argument supporting preserved performance.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation across benchmarks and dimensions.
- Strength: practical implementation details that aid reproducibility.
- Major weakness: naming the method "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is misleading and ignores Suttorp et al.'s prior use of the term.
- Major weakness: algorithmic novelty is minimal—essentially a simple replacement/loop and an extension of Krause & Igel (2015).
- Major weakness: claim of "optimal" storage/runtime complexity is misleading—only applies when full covariance is needed and ignores separable cases where diagonal updates are optimal.
- Experimental omission: fails to compare against Suttorp-CMA-ES despite criticizing it, creating a misleading experimental picture.
- Experimental concern: unexplained plateau discrepancy in the Cigar function results and potential issues from median-based plotting.
- Limited significance: incremental advance that does not resolve CMA-ES's fundamental O(d^2) scalability limits for large-scale problems.
- Clarity is good overall, but poor contextualization with prior literature reduces the paper's impact.
- Recommendation: reject unless authors substantially revise to acknowledge prior work, fix naming, add fair comparisons, and clarify the scope of "optimal" complexity claims.
