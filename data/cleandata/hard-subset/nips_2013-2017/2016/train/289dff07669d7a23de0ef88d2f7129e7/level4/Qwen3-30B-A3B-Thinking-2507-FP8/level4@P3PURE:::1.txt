

CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It adapts the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which equivalently corresponds to adapting an underlying metric. This paper proposes a novel variant for updating the covariance matrix using triangular Cholesky factors. This approach reduces both the computational complexity and memory requirements for storing the algorithm's state parameters compared to prior updates. The method is rigorously evaluated numerically, demonstrating that it maintains the standard CMA-ES performance in terms of function evaluations required to reach a target, while significantly reducing wall-clock time—particularly for high-dimensional problems. I believe this represents a highly significant contribution to optimization. The proposed variant is well-positioned to become the default choice for medium- to large-scale optimization due to its clear advantages: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and the inherent provision of eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no additional cost. The paper is well-written, and the evaluation is thorough. The relevance to NIPS is notable, given CMA-ES's strong connections to machine learning, information geometry, and its established use in reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
