- Refines CMA-ES by using triangular Cholesky factorization to remove square-root operations during matrix inversion.
- Core contribution: reduces computational overhead without degrading algorithmic performance, supported by theory and experiments.
- Demonstrates identical objective function evaluation counts to standard CMA-ES while significantly lowering wall-clock runtime.
- Runtime improvements scale with dimensionality, making the method valuable for high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural network training).
- Theoretical justification that convergence properties and solution quality are preserved is a strong point.
- Implementation avoids square-root computations by operating on the lower-triangular Cholesky factor, reducing the constant factor in O(d^3) updates.
- Claim of improved numerical stability is unsubstantiated—needs theoretical explanation and empirical evidence (e.g., condition number or error analyses).
- Lacks discussion on combining with dimensionality-reduction techniques (e.g., Loshchilov 2015), which could further improve scalability in practice.
- Overall assessment: well-motivated and technically rigorous; suitable for publication after minor revisions addressing stability proof and integration with reduction methods.
