- Proposal: maintain and update a triangular Cholesky factor with repeated rank-one updates, using A−1 in CSA to avoid explicit eigendecompositions, yielding similar evaluations but lower time/memory.

- Relation to prior work: builds on Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015) by extending triangular rank-one updates to rank-µ and modifying CSA.

- Theoretical justification: use RQ/polar decomposition to argue rotation matrices stabilize so replacing C−1/2 by A−1 yields vanishing error in the cumulative path norm.

- Theory caveat: this justification assumes convergence or slow change of C_t, which is not guaranteed for general CMA-ES behavior.

- Empirical rotation diagnostics: reviewer requests moving rotation measurements from supplement to main text and quantifying ‖E_t E_{t−1}^T − I‖ over problems and time.

- Numerical stability claim: paper’s statements contradict Suttorp et al. (2009); authors should provide quantitative evidence (failure rates, condition numbers) if claiming instability.

- Experiments: thorough benchmarks up to d=256 showing clear wall-clock and memory gains, but an apparent discrepancy on the Cigar function medians needs verification.

- Minor correction: statement that eigenvalue decompositions occur every update is inaccurate; practical implementations perform them periodically — correct this in the paper.

- Naming/clarity: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” is already used; rename or clearly qualify the variant and explicitly position relative to prior Cholesky-based methods.

- Algorithmic novelty: core change is applying known triangular rank-one update µ times plus CSA via A−1; authors should explicitly state this and justify its significance.

- Originality: approach is an incremental extension of prior triangular-update work; theoretical CSA argument is helpful but overall novelty is modest.

- Significance: offers substantial practical speedups for full-covariance problems (e.g., ~20× at d=64) but does not change asymptotic O(µ d^2) complexity; “optimality” claims should be qualified.

- Empirical comparisons: role of Suttorp-CMA-ES baseline is unclear — include it across figures or in supplement and clarify algorithmic differences and expected behavior.

- Pros: clear practical speed/memory improvements, easy to implement, provides efficient eigenvalues and inversion, and supplies a useful theoretical CSA justification.

- Cons: limited algorithmic novelty, naming conflicts, unsupported stability/optimality claims, reliance on assumptions that may not hold, and insufficient main-text empirical diagnostics.

- Recommended revisions: rename the method; add full empirical comparisons to Suttorp and Krause & Igel; state the core contribution clearly; provide quantitative stability data; bring rotation diagnostics into main text; fix the eigen-decomposition claim.

- Overall assessment: solid engineering contribution with practical benefits but incremental; reviewer leans toward rejection now but would support acceptance after the requested clarifications and additional evidence.
