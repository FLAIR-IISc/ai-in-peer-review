This paper considers the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) with both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, applied sequentially with rank-1 updates. The authors prove that, under the assumption of convergence of the covariance, the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper also presents experimental results on algorithm performance.

While the paper explores an interesting extension of the CMA-ES, I question the significance of its contribution. Specifically, Krause et al. (2015) have already employed rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, and the current paper's generalization to rank-μ updates, albeit in a sequence of rank-1 updates, seems straightforward. The contribution, therefore, may not be substantial enough to warrant publication.

Regarding the theoretical analysis, the convergence of the covariance Ct in CMA-ES has been established by Beyer (ECJ, 2014), albeit not based on the exact formulae of CMA-ES. This existing result could be used to bolster the confidence in Lemma 1.

There are also a few technical issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, in equation (1), the denominator should feature σt instead of σ. The same error is repeated in footnote 2 on page 3.
