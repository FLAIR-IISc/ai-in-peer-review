- CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization, adapting a multivariate normal sampling distribution (i.e., an underlying metric).  
- The paper proposes a new covariance-matrix update based on triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The new update significantly reduces computational complexity and memory required to store the algorithm’s state.  
- Numerical evaluation is careful and convincing.  
- The new update preserves standard CMA-ES performance in terms of function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- This is a significant contribution and could become the default for medium- and large-scale problems due to reduced memory, lower complexity, and cheaply obtained eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well written, the empirical analysis is thorough, and the work is relevant to NIPS given links to ML, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Minor comment: clarify how implementations were made comparable in CPU—are they written in the same language, and which one?  
- Minor comment: explain why the claim “the higher the dimensionality, the more pronounced the differences” does not hold for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
