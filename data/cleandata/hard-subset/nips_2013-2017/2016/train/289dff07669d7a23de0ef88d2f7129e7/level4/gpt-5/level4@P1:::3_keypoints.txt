- Proposes Cholesky-based CMA-ES that maintains lower-triangular factor A (C = A A^T) and updates A via sequenced rank-1 updates to implement rank-1 + rank-µ adaptation.  
- Achieves O(µ d^2) time per iteration and optimal memory by storing only the triangular factor.  
- Replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA and justifies this by showing the rotation error vanishes as covariance shape converges.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, with speedups increasing with dimension.  
- Compares to reference CMA-ES (with delayed decomposition), a d-step delay variant, and a non-triangular Cholesky update, reporting appropriate benchmarks and cache-effect observations.  
- Builds on Krause & Igel (2015) and prior covariance-simplification and delay strategies; extends rank-1 triangular update to rank-µ via sequencing.  
- Strength: practical efficiency — asymptotically optimal time/memory for full-covariance adaptation.  
- Strength: empirical performance — consistent evaluation counts with large runtime reductions.  
- Strength: numerical advantages — stable O(d^2) inversion and readily available eigenvalue (diagonal) information.  
- Strength: preserves core CMA-ES behavior; CSA approximation is theoretically motivated and empirically validated.  
- Weakness: limited novelty — extension from rank-1 to rank-µ via sequential updates is incremental.  
- Weakness: theoretical justification depends on convergence assumptions and lacks non-asymptotic guarantees for full CMA-ES.  
- Weakness: minor typographical/presentation errors (e.g., σ_t vs σ in eqn and footnote; stray "by").  
- Quality: algorithmic derivation is sound; CSA approximation argued reasonably with references to convergence literature.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written and organized; algorithms and motivations are clearly presented.  
- Originality: useful engineering contribution but conceptually incremental over prior triangular-update work.  
- Significance: materially reduces runtime for practitioners using full-covariance CMA-ES at moderate-to-high dimensions; less impact for very large d where full covariance is impractical.  
- Pros for acceptance: large runtime savings, asymptotic optimality, stable/simple implementation, solid empirical validation.  
- Cons against acceptance: incremental novelty, assumption-dependent CSA theory without rigorous guarantees, minor typos to fix.  
- Overall: solid practical engineering improvement with convincing empirical support and reasonable theory under assumptions; novelty modest, recommend fixing typos and clarifying theoretical scope.
