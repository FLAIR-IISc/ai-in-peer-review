- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that reduces covariance adaptation complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) (μ typically O(log d)).
- Maintains and updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix and its square root.
- Main algorithmic change is omitting estimation of a random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.
- Provides an asymptotic argument (under restrictive convergence assumptions) that E_t → I, making the schemes equivalent in the limit.
- Empirical validation: runtime and convergence plots on benchmark functions against two state-of-the-art delayed-update CMA-ES variants show substantial runtime reduction without degrading performance on synthetic tests.
- Important limitation: speedups matter only when objective evaluations are extremely cheap and dimension is high; in many applications function-evaluation time dominates.
- Recommended additional experiments: track evolution of E_t during runs (e.g., norms) by running full CMA-ES in parallel.
- Recommended additional experiments: include standard (no-delay) CMA-ES in runtime and performance comparisons for completeness.
- Recommended additional experiments: extend quadratic experiments to a spectral variation study (à la Stich & Müller 2012) to assess solution quality and E_t error dependence on spectral distribution.
- Suggests demonstrating practical impact on a real-world task (e.g., real-time robotics RL with moderate parameter sizes) to validate applicability.
- Overall assessment: solid contribution to accelerating CMA-ES.
