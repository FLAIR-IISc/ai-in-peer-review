- Summary: Proposes a triangular Cholesky-factor CMA-ES with an inverse-Cholesky CSA approximation to match evaluation behavior while reducing per-iteration time and memory; shows empirical speedups and argues asymptotic CSA accuracy as covariance stabilizes.

- Relation to prior work: Builds on Suttorp et al. (2009) Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause & Igel (2015) triangular rank-one update, extending the latter from µ=1 to rank-µ and adapting CSA to avoid C−1/2.

- Strength: Clear motivation that covariance decomposition dominates runtime/memory and a practical drop-in replacement achieving large wall-clock speedups with O(µd^2) time and O(d^2) storage.

- Strength: Theoretical justification via rotation-convergence argument and practical advantages from triangular factor (cheap eigenvalues and inverse).

- Strength: Broad experiments including rotation-invariance tests showing consistent speedups, especially in higher dimensions.

- Weakness: Naming/credit ambiguity—calling it “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009); author should rename or clearly distinguish.

- Weakness: Novelty is incremental—main algorithmic change is looping rank-one updates and swapping A−1 for C−1/2 in CSA; authors should explicitly scope and justify nontriviality.

- Weakness: Missing or de-emphasized baseline—Suttorp et al. (2009) should be included prominently in figures and stability claims need evidence.

- Weakness: Overstated “optimality” claim—optimality applies only to full-covariance methods and should be qualified against diagonal/LM approaches.

- Weakness: CSA approximation relies on covariance stabilization; authors should quantify approximation error and ablate effects on step-size dynamics, especially for slowly evolving problems.

- Weakness: Plot interpretation issues (e.g., Cigar plateau) need clarification and additional statistics (quartiles or mean±std) to avoid misinterpretation.

- Minor clarity: Correct statement that eigen decompositions are typically done periodically, not every update.

- Assessment — Quality: Technically sound with solid empirical speedups, but missing baselines and unqualified claims reduce confidence.

- Assessment — Clarity: Generally well organized but needs improved attribution, precise novelty scope, CSA clarification, and plotting fixes.

- Assessment — Originality: Incremental extension (practical value high, conceptual novelty modest).

- Assessment — Significance: Useful for full-covariance settings; does not change quadratic scaling so limited impact for very large-scale problems.

- Pros: Faster wall-clock times with similar evaluations; optimal storage for full-covariance; simple implementable update with good numerical properties.

- Cons: Attribution conflict, incremental novelty, inconsistent baseline presentation, and overbroad optimality claims.

- Requested revision 1: Resolve naming/credit explicitly (rename or distinguish from Suttorp et al.).

- Requested revision 2: Include head-to-head empirical comparisons with Suttorp et al. (2009) and discuss Krause & Igel (2015); update figures accordingly.

- Requested revision 3: Clarify algorithmic novelty beyond looping rank-one updates and provide CSA ablations including rotation metrics and step-size dynamics.

- Requested revision 4: Qualify the optimality claim and discuss when diagonal/LM variants are preferable.

- Requested revision 5: Fix minor clarity issues, e.g., note that eigen decompositions are performed periodically.

- Overall recommendation: Promising and practically useful but currently reads as an incremental extension with gaps in crediting and evaluation; accept after requested comparisons, clearer positioning, and CSA ablations.
