The manuscript introduces a novel update scheme, Cholesky-CMA-ES, that approximates the original covariance matrix adaptation with reduced complexity. This is achieved by maintaining and updating a lower-triangular Cholesky matrix instead of a full covariance matrix, resulting in a time complexity of O(\mu d^2) compared to O(d^3) in the original CMA-ES. The key difference between the Cholesky and the original CMA-ES lies in the absence of estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et in every step. Under certain restrictive assumptions, the authors demonstrate that the sequence of Et converges to an identity matrix, rendering the two schemes equivalent. The manuscript validates the Cholesky-CMA-ES using standard runtime and convergence plots on selected objective functions and compares it to two state-of-the-art CMA-ES alternatives that delay the full covariance matrix update. This contribution significantly accelerates the celebrated CMA-ES algorithm, which is well-suited for non-convex black-box optimization with limited knowledge of the objective function's topology and inaccessible or unhelpful first or higher-order information. The proposed method achieves considerable speedup without compromising optimization performance on synthetic test cases. However, it is essential to note that in practice, the overall optimization process is often dominated by the runtime of the black-box oracle, making the actual runtime of the CMA-ES negligible in many scenarios. The dimensionality of problems typically addressed by CMA-ES is also relatively low, making the runtime of CMA-ES less critical. Nevertheless, the proposed method targets scenarios with high dimensionality and fast function evaluations, which may be less common in practice. As theoretical guarantees for CMA-ES are challenging to obtain, further empirical experiments and plots can strengthen the presentation of the results. It would be instructive to investigate the evolution of Et on selected optimization trajectories, compare the full CMA-ES with the proposed approximation, and analyze the actual fluctuations of Et over time. Additionally, including actual runtimes and performance of CMA-ES without delay strategies would enhance the completeness of the study. A simulation study on quadratic functions with varying spectral distributions could provide valuable insights into the solution quality and error in E_t. Finally, a real-world experiment, such as policy gradients in real-time robotics applications, would complement the simulation results and demonstrate the practical relevance of the proposed method.
