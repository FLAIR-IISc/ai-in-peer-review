This paper considers the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) with both rank-1 and rank-µ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, in a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors prove, under the convergence assumption of the covariance, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly change the algorithm's behavior. The paper presents experiments on algorithm performances.

The main idea of the paper is to extend the update for the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-µ updates. While the authors' contribution is a generalization of the procedure to rank-µ updates in a sequence of rank-1 updates, it is worth noting that Krause et al. (2015) previously used rank-1 updates to triangular Cholesky factors. Therefore, the significance of this paper's contribution may be limited.

The authors' theoretical analysis is generally sound, but it may be improved by referencing the convergence proof of the covariance Ct in CMA-ES by Beyer (ECJ, 2014), which, although not based on the exact formulae of CMA-ES, provides a solid foundation for Lemma 1.

There are several minor issues with the paper. In equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σt. The same problem occurs in the footnote on page 3. Additionally, in line 94, the word "by" should be deleted from the sentence "which can by be motivated by principles from...".

In terms of the paper's quality, it is technically sound and well-written. The authors provide a clear and concise explanation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and its theoretical justification. The experimental results are also well-presented and demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Arguments pro acceptance:

* The paper presents a novel extension of the CMA-ES algorithm using triangular Cholesky factors.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of the algorithm's behavior.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm.

Arguments con acceptance:

* The significance of the paper's contribution may be limited due to the previous work by Krause et al. (2015).
* There are minor issues with the paper, such as the incorrect notation in equation (1) and the unnecessary word "by" in line 94.

Overall, I recommend acceptance of the paper, but the authors should address the minor issues mentioned above to improve the paper's quality.
