- Primary contribution: use of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square-root in standard CMA-ES, reducing matrix inversion cost.
- The method is theoretically justified and claimed not to degrade algorithmic performance.
- Runtime complexity is decreased while the number of objective function evaluations remains largely unchanged.
- The paper supplies a useful implementation of the Cholesky factorization approach for CMA-ES.
- Reviewer requests a more detailed explanation of why the proposed computation is "numerically more stable."
- Empirical results show equivalent evaluation counts to CMA-ES but lower wall-clock time, with gains growing at higher dimensionalities.
- Suggestion to explore combining this approach with Loshchilov (2015) low-dimensional approximations to better handle very high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural network direct policy search).
