- Misleading naming: calling the method “Cholesky-CMA-ES” ignores and conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and is not acknowledged.
- Incremental contribution: the main algorithmic change appears to be applying rankOneUpdate in a loop for all individuals, a minor modification of Krause et al. (2015).
- Missing baselines: the paper fails to directly compare to Krause et al.’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and omits Suttorp et al.’s method from key figures.
- Contradictory claim on numerical stability: the paper asserts Suttorp’s method is unstable, contradicting Suttorp (2009) and requiring explanation.
- Overstated optimality: claiming “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is misleading—full covariance isn’t necessary for simple or separable problems.
- Behavioral differences: the claim that algorithm behavior is unchanged is contradicted by empirical differences (e.g., different plateau levels on the Cigar function).
- Modest practical gain: reported speedup (~2×) over Suttorp’s method is limited, and excluding that baseline may overstate impact.
- Limited impact on scalability: complexity remains O(n^2), so the work doesn’t fundamentally change CMA-ES scalability, though it may be the most efficient full-covariance update currently.
- Recommended fixes: resolve naming and citation issues, include comprehensive comparisons to Suttorp and Krause in experiments, and explicitly state/justify the true algorithmic contribution (the “for” loop if that is it).
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically in efficient implementations, not every iteration as claimed.
