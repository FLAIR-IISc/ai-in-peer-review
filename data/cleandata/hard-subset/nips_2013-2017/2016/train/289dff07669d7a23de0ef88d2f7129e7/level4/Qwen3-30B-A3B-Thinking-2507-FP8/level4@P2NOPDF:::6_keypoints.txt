- Misleading name: "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is already established by Suttorp et al. (2009); the paper fails to acknowledge this prior work.  
- Prior-work omission: Authors incorrectly present their method as novel and erroneously claim Suttorp’s variant is numerically unstable, contradicting Suttorp’s stability analysis.  
- Algorithmic novelty is minor: The core change is a trivial loop over individuals replacing a single rank-one update, a small modification of Krause et al. (2015).  
- Insufficient justification: The loop modification is not shown to be a non-trivial or significantly advantageous innovation compared to existing implementations.  
- Empirical issues: Figure 2(b) shows differing plateau values likely due to median plotting, but the paper does not address or explain this discrepancy.  
- Missing baselines: The evaluation omits comparisons with Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause’s variant, misrepresenting the contribution (e.g., Figure 3).  
- Overstated complexity claim: Claiming "optimal covariance update and storage complexity" is inaccurate—diagonal/separable approximations suffice for separable problems and the method retains quadratic complexity.  
- Limited practical gain: The method yields only ~2× speedup over Suttorp’s approach while keeping O(n^2) cost, so it does not advance large-scale optimization.  
- Required clarifications: Authors must explicitly acknowledge Suttorp (2009), reproduce empirical comparisons including Suttorp and Krause, and state/justify that the loop is the primary change.  
- Minor correction needed: Eigenvalue decomposition is not performed every update in standard CMA-ES; it is typically done periodically and this statement should be corrected.  
- Overall assessment: Technically sound but overly incremental; naming confusion, omitted baselines, and unsupported complexity claims undermine the paper’s novelty and significance for NIPS.
