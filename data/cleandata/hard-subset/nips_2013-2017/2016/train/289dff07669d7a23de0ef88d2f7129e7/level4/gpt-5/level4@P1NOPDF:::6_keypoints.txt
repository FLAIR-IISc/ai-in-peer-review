- The method is misleadingly named "Cholesky-CMA-ES" without acknowledging the prior Cholesky-CMA-ES of Suttorp et al. (2009).

- The paper's algorithmic contribution is incremental, mainly differing from Krause et al. (2015) by looping rankOneUpdate over all individuals.

- The rankOneUpdate itself is already present in Krause et al., so novelty is limited.

- Empirical plots (e.g., Cigar function) show unexplained discrepancies (plateau differences) that may be due to plotting medians.

- The paper omits direct empirical comparison to Suttorp et al.'s Cholesky-CMA-ES and an explicit algorithmic comparison to Krause et al.; these should be added.

- The claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is overstated—Sphere and separable problems don't require full covariance, and the benefit is limited.

- The claimed runtime/memory improvement is modest (about 2×) compared to the omitted Suttorp variant.

- The paper asserts Suttorp’s approach is numerically unstable, but that contradicts the original report and is unsupported here.

- Omitting Suttorp et al. from Figure 3 risks misleading readers about novelty and practical benefit.

- The method still has quadratic complexity, so it does not materially change large-scale CMA-ES performance; at best it’s a practical improvement if keeping full covariance.

- The main algorithmic change is essentially a single "for" loop in Algorithm 1; the authors should either acknowledge this or justify otherwise.

- Minor note: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically in proper implementations, not at every update as claimed.
