- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time and storage while preserving algorithm behavior.  
- Method uses Cholesky factorization instead of full covariance matrix.  
- Builds upon Krause & Igel (2015) which introduced similar Cholesky updates.  
- Main contribution is extending the Cholesky update approach to the standard CMA-ES.  
- Theoretical justification: error from replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factor is small and decreases as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical results show significant speedup versus other CMA-ES variants, especially in high dimensions.  
- Claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading and not universally optimal (e.g., separable problems or when covariance is unnecessary).  
- Paper fails to adequately acknowledge Suttorp et al. (2009) and should clarify differences and compare methods.  
- The algorithmic role and contribution of the "for" loop in Algorithm 1 are not clearly explained.  
- Minor inaccuracy: it is not typical to perform an eigenvalue decomposition at every update step.  
- Strength: reduces time and storage complexity of CMA-ES.  
- Strength: provides theoretical analysis and empirical comparisons.  
- Weaknesses: inadequate citation/comparison to Suttorp et al., unclear explanation of the "for" loop, and overstated optimality claim.  
- Recommendations: clarify differences with Suttorp et al., explain the "for" loop's algorithmic contribution, and revise the optimality claim.  
- Overall: promising contribution but requires revisions to address prior work, clarify algorithmic novelty, and temper claims.
