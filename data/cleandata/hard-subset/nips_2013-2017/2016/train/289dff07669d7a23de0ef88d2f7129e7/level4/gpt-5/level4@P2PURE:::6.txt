The paper introduces a CMA-ES variant advertised as having “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity,” claiming identical behavior to standard CMA-ES but lower computational cost. The method is named Cholesky-CMA-ES, which is misleading: this name was first used by T. Suttorp et al. (2009) for a similar and well-known algorithm. The authors do not acknowledge this and instead write “Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES.”

Algorithmically, the contribution is incremental relative to Krause et al. (2015). The only substantive change is to call rankOneUpdate (already present in Krause et al.) not once, as in Plus strategies, but in a loop over all individuals. Empirically, on the Cigar function in Figure b), the plateau appears at different objective values for CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES; this might be due to how the medians are plotted.

The paper should compare the proposed approach to Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES empirically and to Krause et al.’s approach algorithmically. The claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is also somewhat misleading: on the Sphere function, no covariance matrix is needed, and on separable functions, a diagonal covariance suffices. Moreover, the proposed approach is only about twice as cheap as the (omitted) Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp. While this paper claims that Suttorp’s method is prone to numerical instabilities, the original work suggests the opposite. Again, omitting Suttorp’s variant from Figure 3 can mislead readers about the actual contribution.

I do not believe this approach materially advances large-scale optimization with CMA-ES, as the complexity remains quadratic. That said, if one insists on maintaining a full covariance matrix, this modification may be close to the best available option for saving time and memory.

Overall, the contribution feels too incremental (algorithmically, it amounts to adding a single “for” loop). I recommend the following:
a) Resolve the naming conflict.
b) Run experiments including the variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al., and update all three figures. If the plots become cluttered, show only the best-performing variant in the main text and place the full set of figures in the Supplementary Material.
c) Explain more clearly that the main algorithmic change is the added “for” loop in Algorithm 1, or justify why this interpretation is incorrect.

Minor note: The statement “Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step” is atypical; in proper implementations, the decomposition is performed periodically, not at every update.
