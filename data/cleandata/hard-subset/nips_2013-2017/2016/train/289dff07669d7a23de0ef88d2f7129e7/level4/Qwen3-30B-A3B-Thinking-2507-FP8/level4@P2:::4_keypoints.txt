- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce compute and memory costs.  
- Addresses CMA-ES bottleneck of covariance matrix update/storage with a triangular factorization.  
- Key theoretical insight: Cholesky factor can substitute the matrix square root in CSA without harming performance (Lemma 1).  
- Achieves optimal asymptotic runtime O(µ d²) (µ ~ O(log d)) versus standard O(d²), keeping identical function-evaluation needs.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky attempts but provides the first theoretical justification for standard CMA-ES.  
- Empirical evaluation on benchmarks shows comparable optimization quality and up to 20× wall-clock speedup at d=64, increasing with dimension.  
- Theoretical analysis shows introduced error vanishes during convergence as Cholesky rotation smooths.  
- Paper is technically sound with robust experimental design across functions and dimensions (d=4..256).  
- Exceptionally clear writing, concise CMA-ES background, and reproducible algorithmic details and visualizations.  
- Significant practical impact for high-dimensional optimization in machine learning (hyperparameter tuning, RL, etc.).  
- Strengths: solid theory, comprehensive experiments, reduced complexity without quality loss, practical relevance, storage and numerical-stability analysis.  
- Weaknesses: lacks detailed explanation of numerical-stability benefits and does not explore integration with low-dimensional approximations for very high-dimensional problems.  
- Recommendation: strong accept — theoretically grounded, empirically validated, and practically valuable.
