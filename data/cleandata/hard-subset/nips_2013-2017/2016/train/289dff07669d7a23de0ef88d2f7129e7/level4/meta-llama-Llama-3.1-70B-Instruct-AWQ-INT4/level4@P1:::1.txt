The CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic/randomized algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It combines different learning mechanisms for adapting the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution that can be equivalently seen as adaptation of an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new variant for the update of the covariance matrix based on triangular Cholesky factors that has the advantage compared to previous updates to significantly reduce the complexity of the update as well as the memory needed to store the state parameters of the algorithm.

The authors provide a thorough introduction to the CMA-ES algorithm, its applications in machine learning, and its limitations, particularly with respect to the update and storage of the covariance matrix. They then present their new method, Cholesky-CMA-ES, which uses a triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the square root in the standard CMA-ES. This approach leads to a reduction in runtime complexity and memory footprint, making it more efficient for large-scale optimization problems.

The paper is well-structured and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the theoretical background and the proposed method. The authors provide a detailed description of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm, including its properties and advantages over previous approaches. They also present empirical results comparing the Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants, demonstrating its efficiency and effectiveness in various benchmark functions.

The strengths of the paper include its clear and concise writing style, the thorough introduction to the CMA-ES algorithm, and the detailed presentation of the proposed method. The authors also provide a comprehensive evaluation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, including its theoretical justification and empirical performance.

One minor point that could be improved is the explanation of how the different implementations used in the experiments are comparable in terms of CPU. The authors mention that they used the LAPACK library for high efficiency, but it would be helpful to provide more details on the implementation language and any other relevant factors that ensure the comparability of the results.

Additionally, when commenting on Figure 2, the authors mention that the higher the dimensionality, the more pronounced the differences in wall-clock time between the Cholesky-CMA-ES and other CMA-ES variants. However, this does not seem to be the case for Cigar and Discus in dimension 256. It would be helpful to provide an explanation for this anomaly.

Overall, I think that this paper is a significant contribution in the domain of optimization. The Cholesky-CMA-ES variant has several clear advantages over the current default method, including reduced memory needed, reduced complexity, and getting the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix for free. The paper is well-written, and the approach is carefully evaluated. It is nice to see this contribution at NIPS, given the strong connection between CMA-ES and machine learning, information geometry, and reinforcement learning or supervised learning.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a new and efficient variant of the CMA-ES algorithm, which is a significant contribution in the domain of optimization.
* The authors provide a thorough introduction to the CMA-ES algorithm and its limitations, making the paper accessible to a wide range of readers.
