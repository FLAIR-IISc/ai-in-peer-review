This paper proposes a modified version of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, which aims to reduce the time and storage complexity of the original algorithm. The authors claim that their approach does not alter the behavior of the algorithm but makes it more computationally efficient.

The proposed method is based on maintaining a triangular Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix itself. This approach is not entirely new, as it builds upon the work of Krause and Igel (2015), who introduced a similar method for updating the Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix. The main contribution of this paper is the extension of this approach to the standard CMA-ES algorithm.

The authors provide a theoretical justification for their approach, showing that the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor is small and decreases over time as the algorithm converges. They also present empirical results comparing the performance of their approach with other CMA-ES variants, including the original CMA-ES with delayed covariance matrix decomposition.

The results show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a significant speedup compared to the other algorithms, especially for high-dimensional problems. However, the authors' claim about "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is somewhat misleading, as it is not optimal for all types of problems, such as separable functions or problems where the covariance matrix is not necessary.

One of the main concerns with this paper is that it does not adequately acknowledge the work of Suttorp et al. (2009), who introduced a similar algorithm also referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES. The authors should clarify the differences between their approach and the one proposed by Suttorp et al. and provide a more detailed comparison of the two methods.

Additionally, the paper could benefit from a more detailed explanation of the algorithmic contribution of the "for" loop in Algorithm 1, which is the main difference between the proposed approach and the one by Krause and Igel (2015).
