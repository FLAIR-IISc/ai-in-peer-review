- Misleading name: "Cholesky-CMA-ES" duplicates Suttorp et al. (2009) and the authors fail to acknowledge or justify using this established name.  
- Missing baselines: No empirical comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) or Krause et al. (2015), omitting crucial related-work baselines.  
- Unjustified critique: The authors’ claim that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable contradicts the original paper and requires justification.  
- Overstated speedup: The reported ~2× speedup over Suttorp is presented without including Suttorp’s method in experiments, overstating gains.  
- Limited novelty: The main algorithmic change appears to be applying rankOneUpdate in a loop over individuals—an incremental modification similar to prior work.  
- Need to clarify contribution: Authors must either show substantive additional contributions beyond the loop or acknowledge the incremental nature.  
- Overstated optimality: Claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading—it only holds if a full covariance is required and ignores separable/sphere cases.  
- Empirical discrepancy: Performance on the Cigar function differs between methods (plateau mismatch), implying plotting artifacts or a real behavioral change that must be investigated.  
- Limited large-scale impact: While time/memory are reduced, complexity remains quadratic in dimension, so the method doesn’t fundamentally enable large-scale CMA-ES.  
- Required revisions: Resolve the naming conflict, include Suttorp and Krause variants in experiments, and explicitly clarify the core algorithmic contribution.  
- Minor correction: The paper’s statement about eigenvalue decomposition per update is incorrect—decomposition is typically done periodically, not every generation.
