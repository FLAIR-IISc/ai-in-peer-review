- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution and underlying metric.  
- The paper proposes a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based update substantially reduces update complexity and memory required for state parameters.  
- Numerical experiments are provided to evaluate the new method.  
- The new update maintains standard CMA-ES performance in terms of function evaluations to reach targets.  
- It significantly lowers wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional settings.  
- The method provides covariance eigenvalues at no extra cost.  
- The paper is well-written, carefully evaluated, and highly relevant to machine learning and related fields.  
- The reviewer expects this variant to become the default for medium to large-scale optimization.  
- Minor suggestion: clarify how implementations were made comparable in CPU performance (same language/which one).  
- Minor suggestion: explain why Figure 2’s claim about dimensionality differences does not hold for Cigar and Discus at 256 dimensions.
