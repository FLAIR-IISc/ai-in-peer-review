This paper proposes a modification to the covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) by utilizing a triangular Cholesky factorization instead of the traditional square root of the covariance matrix. The authors provide a theoretical justification for this modification, showing that it does not significantly impact the algorithm's behavior under the assumption of converging covariance. They also present experimental results demonstrating the improved performance of their approach, which they term Cholesky-CMA-ES.

The main idea of the paper is to update the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix using both rank-1 and rank-µ updates. While this is a natural extension of the work by Krause and Igel (2015), which used rank-1 updates for triangular Cholesky factors, the contribution of this paper may be considered incremental. However, the authors provide a thorough analysis and experimental evaluation of their approach, which is valuable for the research community.

One potential improvement to the paper is to further discuss the convergence properties of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES. While the authors mention that the convergence of Ct has been proven by theoretical analysis (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), they do not elaborate on this point. Providing more context and insights into the convergence properties of Ct could strengthen the confidence in Lemma 1 and the overall analysis.

There are a few minor issues with the paper. In equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σt. The same error occurs in the footnote on page 3. Additionally, in line 94, the word "by" should be deleted from the sentence "which can by be motivated by principles from...".

Overall, the paper presents a well-motivated and thoroughly evaluated modification to the CMA-ES algorithm. While the contribution may be considered incremental, the authors provide a valuable analysis and experimental evaluation of their approach.

Strengths:

* The paper provides a thorough analysis and experimental evaluation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm.
* The authors provide a theoretical justification for their modification to the CMA-ES algorithm.
* The experimental results demonstrate the improved performance of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm.

Weaknesses:

* The contribution of the paper may be considered incremental, as it builds upon previous work by Krause and Igel (2015).
* The paper could benefit from a more detailed discussion of the convergence properties of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES.
* There are a few minor errors in the paper, such as the incorrect use of σ in equation (1) and the unnecessary word "by" in line 94.
