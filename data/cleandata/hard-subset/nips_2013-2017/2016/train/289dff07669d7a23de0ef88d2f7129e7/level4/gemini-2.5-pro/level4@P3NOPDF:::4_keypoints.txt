- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update covariance via its triangular Cholesky factor to avoid the costly matrix square root.
- Lowers covariance update complexity from O(N^3) to O(N^2), with empirical wall-clock speedups that grow with dimensionality.
- Maintains search performance: theoretical support and experiments show comparable numbers of function evaluations to standard CMA-ES.
- Delivers practical value by making CMA-ES more feasible for higher-dimensional problems where per-iteration cost is limiting.
- Claims improved numerical stability from Cholesky-based updates but lacks a detailed theoretical explanation specific to CMA-ES.
- Scalability concern: O(N^2) time and memory remain prohibitive for very large N (e.g., direct policy search for neural nets), and evaluation count scaling is still an issue.
- Suggests exploring combinations with high-dimensional approximation methods (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to extend applicability.
- Overall assessment: a solid, useful algorithmic improvement that would be strengthened by addressing numerical stability proofs and extreme-scale scalability.
