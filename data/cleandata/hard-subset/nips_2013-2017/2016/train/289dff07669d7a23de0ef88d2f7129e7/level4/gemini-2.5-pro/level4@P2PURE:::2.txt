This paper proposes a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, replacing the standard eigendecomposition with a Cholesky decomposition to reduce computational and memory costs. The paper is clearly written, the proposed method is methodologically sound, and the experimental results, particularly the speedup rates, are quite promising. This approach has the potential to become a new standard implementation of CMA-ES.

Despite its potential impact, the paper's primary weakness is its limited originality. The proposed method appears to be a straightforward extension of the prior work, "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies," and the core technical approaches seem nearly identical. The authors should clarify the novel contributions of their work in relation to this specific prior art.

Furthermore, the paper is missing several critical components that are necessary for a complete and convincing contribution:

1.  **Equivalence to Standard CMA-ES:** The authors should demonstrate that their algorithm is equivalent to the standard formulation in Equation (2). While this may have been proven in previous work and space is limited, this proof is essential for the paper's self-containment and rigor.

2.  **Detailed Complexity Analysis:** The paper states the computational complexity is O(μd²), which is asymptotically identical to the reference CMA-ES implementation. To justify the reported speedups, a more detailed complexity analysis is required. The authors should elaborate on the practical performance gains, perhaps by providing a tighter analysis (e.g., using Θ-notation) that better reflects the lower constant factors involved in their method compared to the reference.

3.  **Empirical Validation of Path Error:** The authors claim that the error in the evolution path is small and decreases over time, a claim that appears to contradict findings by Hansen. As the paper's theoretical analysis is not deterministic, this claim requires direct empirical validation. The paper should include experiments that specifically track this error throughout the optimization process; comparing only the final performance is insufficient to substantiate this key claim.

Minor comments:
*   Notational consistency should be checked. For example, `wk` in Algorithm 2 should be `αk`, and `n` in Table 1 should be `d`.
*   There appears to be a typo in line 185, where 'F' should be 'f'.

Given the significant concerns regarding originality and the omission of essential analysis and validation, I believe the paper, in its current form, does not meet the standards for NIPS.
