- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update lower-triangular Cholesky factor to approximate full covariance update, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).  
- Avoids maintaining the full covariance matrix and its square root by operating on Cholesky factors.  
- Key approximation: omits estimating the random orthogonal matrix E_t at each iteration.  
- Provides an asymptotic argument (under restrictive convergence assumptions) that E_t converges to the identity, justifying the approximation.  
- Empirical results (runtime and convergence plots) on benchmark functions show substantial speedup without degrading optimization performance.  
- Compares favorably to two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that use delay strategies for covariance updates.  
- Major contribution: significant computational savings for non-convex black-box optimization, especially when function evaluations are very fast and dimensionality is high.  
- Caveat: in many practical problems, objective evaluation time dominates and typical dimensions are modest, limiting practical impact.  
- Suggestion: visualize evolution of E_t (e.g., its norm over time) to give insight into the approximation’s behavior.  
- Suggestion: run full CMA-ES alongside Cholesky-CMA-ES to empirically track E_t fluctuations and approximation error.  
- Suggestion: include standard CMA-ES without delay as a baseline and perform spectrum-varying quadratic simulations (per Stich & Mueller) to study solution quality and E_t error.  
- Suggestion: add a real-world experiment (e.g., policy-gradient robotics) to demonstrate practical utility in time-critical, moderately sized parameter spaces.
