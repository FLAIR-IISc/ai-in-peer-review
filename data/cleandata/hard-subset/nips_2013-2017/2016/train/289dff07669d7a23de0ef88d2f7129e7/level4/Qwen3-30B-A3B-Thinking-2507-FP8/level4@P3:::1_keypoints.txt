- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace costly matrix square root in CMA-ES with numerically stable Cholesky factorization.
- Reduces covariance update complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) (μ typically O(log d)), preserving function-evaluation counts to reach solution quality.
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the approximation error vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Empirical evaluation on standard benchmarks (dimensions 4–256, 100 trials) shows similar solution quality with substantial runtime gains, especially in high dimensions.
- Clearly relates to and distinguishes from prior work (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel, etc.) by providing the missing theoretical justification.
- Paper quality is high: sound theory, comprehensive experiments, and well-explained implementation (including Algorithm 2).
- Clarity and exposition are excellent with accessible notation and reproducibility-focused experimental details.
- Original contribution: first theoretically justified Cholesky factor modification applicable to standard CMA-ES without restricting problem/algorithm variants.
- Significant practical impact: lower memory, faster runtime, and extra covariance eigenvalue info for ML practitioners (hyperparameter tuning, policy search).
- Positions Cholesky-CMA-ES as an optimal middle ground versus low-rank approximations that can degrade performance.
- Strengths summarized: theoretical soundness, comprehensive validation, reduced complexity/memory, practical implementation details, strong practical benefits.
- Weakness: Figure 2 inconsistency for Cigar and Discus at d=256 needs explanation (cache effects hypothesized).
- Weakness: Requires more detail confirming implementation consistency across methods (language, libraries, delay strategies) for fair comparison.
- Weakness: Does not fully explore the impact of the Cholesky approximation on affine invariance and CSA update properties.
- Overall recommendation: meets NIPS criteria (quality, clarity, originality, significance) and is recommended for acceptance.
