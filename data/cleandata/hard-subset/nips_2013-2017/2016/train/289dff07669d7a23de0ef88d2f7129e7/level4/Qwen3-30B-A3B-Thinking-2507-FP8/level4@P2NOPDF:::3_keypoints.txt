- Paper proposes a Cholesky CMA-ES variant that extends triangular Cholesky factor updates to include both rank-1 and rank-μ updates via sequential rank-1 operations.  
- Authors claim their modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially change behavior under covariance convergence, supported by empirical results.  
- Contribution is incremental: Krause et al. (2015) already introduced rank-1 updates for triangular Cholesky in CMA-ES, and this work is a straightforward extension to rank-μ without major theoretical or algorithmic novelty.  
- Theoretical foundation for covariance convergence (Beyer, ECJ 2014) is established and should be explicitly cited to strengthen Lemma 1.  
- Minor but critical errors: Equation (1) denominator should use σ_t (not σ), same typo in Footnote 2 (Page 3).  
- Grammatical error on Line 94: "which can by be motivated" → "which can be motivated."  
- Experimental section is shallow: lacks comparisons to state-of-the-art methods and does not analyze why rank-μ via sequential rank-1 improves over rank-1.  
- Practical relevance is unclear without evidence of improved efficiency or robustness.  
- Overall assessment: method is correctly formulated but offers insufficient novelty and superficial empirical validation, plus avoidable technical errors, making it unsuitable for NIPS.
