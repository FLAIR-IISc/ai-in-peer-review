- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AAT) via rank-1 updates to realize rank-1 and rank-µ covariance updates.  
- Achieves O(µ d^2) per-iteration time and O(d^2) storage for full-covariance adaptation, with an efficient way to compute A−1.  
- Replaces C−1/2 with A−1 in CSA, arguing via continuity and Lemma 1 that the induced error vanishes as covariance shape stabilizes.  
- Empirically matches evaluation counts of reference CMA-ES while substantially reducing wall-clock time, with speedups increasing with dimension.  
- Builds on prior work (Krause & Igel 2015; Suttorp et al. 2009) and other cost-reduction strategies for CMA-ES.  
- Strength: Clear, practical algorithm integrating triangular Cholesky updates with standard rank-µ CMA-ES and an efficient rank-one update routine.  
- Strength: Theoretical justification (Lemma 1) suggesting CSA approximation error diminishes asymptotically.  
- Strength: Solid empirical benchmarks and attention to numerical implementation (Shark, LAPACK).  
- Weakness: Incremental novelty relative to prior Cholesky factor approaches; main additions are rank-µ extension and CSA tweak.  
- Weakness: Theoretical support for CSA is qualitative and assumes convergence-of-shape; no finite-time error bounds or proof of affine invariance.  
- Weakness: Limited experiments — only noiseless problems, modest dimensions (≤256), excluded failed Rosenbrock runs, and no ablation isolating CSA effect.  
- Weakness: Contains a technical inaccuracy claiming Cholesky diagonals equal sqrt eigenvalues, which is false.  
- Quality: Algorithmic approach is sound and well-motivated; CSA plausibility is reasonable but contingent on assumptions; stronger validation needed.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally clear with useful background but has minor typos, notational issues, and needs explicit Lemma 1 conditions and complexity scope.  
- Originality: Moderate — useful engineering integration rather than a fundamentally new algorithmic concept.  
- Significance: Potentially high practical impact for medium-to-high dimensional full-covariance CMA-ES; modest conceptual novelty.  
- Key suggestion: Correct the Cholesky/eigenvalue claim and clarify what quantities are being monitored.  
- Key suggestion: Strengthen theory by stating Lemma 1 assumptions clearly and, if possible, provide finite-time bounds on CSA error.  
- Key suggestion: Expand experiments — include CSA ablations, report medians/IQRs and success rates (don’t exclude failures), add noisy/constrained/real-world tasks, larger d (512/1024), memory and timing breakdowns.  
- Key suggestion: Discuss LM-CMA and other limited-memory approaches to clarify when full covariance is worth quadratic cost.  
- Pros: Practical, implementable speedup; negligible long-run CSA impact; careful numerical considerations.  
- Cons: Incremental novelty, asymptotic/assumption-dependent theory, limited experimental breadth, and at least one technical error.  
- Recommendation: Borderline reject unless authors correct the technical error, broaden and strengthen empirical validation (including ablations), and clarify theoretical assumptions/bounds.
