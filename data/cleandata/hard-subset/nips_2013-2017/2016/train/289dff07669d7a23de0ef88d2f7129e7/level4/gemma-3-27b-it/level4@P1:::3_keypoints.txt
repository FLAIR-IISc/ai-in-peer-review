- Paper extends Cholesky CMA-ES to include rank-µ updates implemented as sequences of rank-1 updates to the triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Authors prove that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly change behavior, assuming covariance convergence.  
- Experimental results show significant runtime speedups over standard CMA-ES, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Work builds directly on Krause & Igel (2015) by generalizing their rank-1 update to rank-µ, making the contribution feel incremental.  
- Theoretical justification relies on the assumption of covariance convergence; prior convergence analyses (e.g., Beyer 2014) could strengthen Lemma 1.  
- Minor typographical/formula errors: σ in denominator of Eq.(1) should be σ_t, and a wording typo in line 94.  
- Argument for acceptance: provides a theoretically justified extension to Cholesky-based CMA-ES implementations.  
- Argument for acceptance: demonstrates practical runtime improvements, aiding scalability to larger problems.  
- Argument for acceptance: empirical results are convincing and show practical benefit.  
- Argument against acceptance: contribution is incremental relative to prior work.  
- Argument against acceptance: covariance convergence assumption is not fully established for all CMA-ES scenarios.  
- Argument against acceptance: paper contains minor typographical errors that need correction.  
- Overall assessment: solid engineering optimization with practical benefits; leans toward acceptance pending fixes and stronger theoretical justification.
