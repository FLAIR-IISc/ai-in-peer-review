The main contribution of this work is the use of the triangular Cholesky factorization technique, which can eliminate square roots in standard CMA-ES and save computation costs when computing matrix inverses. The author presents a method with a theoretical argument that does not degrade the performance of the algorithm. The proposed method reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm without significantly changing the number of function evaluations. This paper is a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES. Can the author theoretically explain further why 'numerically more stable' calculations can be achieved? It seems that the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES, and it reduces wall-clock time and increases search space dimensionality. Can the author consider integrating low-dimensional approximations [Loshchilov, 2015] with the existing framework, since the high dimensionality of the problem, such as neural network training in direct policy search, should be a negligible concern?
