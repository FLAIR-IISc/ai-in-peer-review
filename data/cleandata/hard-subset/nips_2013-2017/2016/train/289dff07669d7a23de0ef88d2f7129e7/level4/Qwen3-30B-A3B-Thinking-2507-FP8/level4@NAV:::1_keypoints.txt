- CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic/derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (i.e., an underlying metric).  
- The paper proposes a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-factor update substantially reduces computational complexity and memory for storing state.  
- Numerical evaluation shows equivalent performance in function evaluations to standard CMA-ES but much lower wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The method is a significant contribution with strong potential to become the default CMA-ES for medium/large-scale problems.  
- Key advantages: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and free availability of eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written and the methodology is thoroughly validated.  
- The work is timely for NIPS given CMA-ES relevance to machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Minor concern: clarify implementation comparability (same language, CPU, compiler/flags) to ensure fair performance comparisons.  
- Minor concern: explain anomaly in Figure 2 where differences are not more pronounced for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
