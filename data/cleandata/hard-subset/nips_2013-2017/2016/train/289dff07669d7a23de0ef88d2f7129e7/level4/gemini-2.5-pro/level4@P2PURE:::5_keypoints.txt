- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, reducing covariance update complexity from O(d³) to O(μd²) with μ typically O(log d).  
- Core idea: maintain and update the lower-triangular Cholesky factor directly instead of the full covariance matrix.  
- Key distinction: avoids estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t each step.  
- Provides an asymptotic argument that under restrictive convergence assumptions E_t → I, making the two schemes equivalent in the limit.  
- Empirical validation via runtime and convergence plots on standard benchmarks showing considerable speed-up without apparent performance degradation.  
- Compares the method to two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that also reduce complexity by different delay mechanisms.  
- Strength: solid contribution accelerating a leading algorithm for non-convex black-box optimization.  
- Practical concern: in many real-world problems objective evaluations dominate cost, so update speed-up may be negligible for typical problem sizes (tens of variables).  
- Applicability limited to scenarios with very fast evaluations and higher dimensions; practical prevalence is debatable.  
- Suggestion to strengthen empirical case by visualizing E_t evolution (e.g., matrix norm) during runs to show deviation from identity.  
- Recommend direct comparison against standard CMA-ES (no complexity reduction) to establish a clear baseline.  
- Recommend a systematic study on quadratics by varying spectral properties (e.g., condition number) to analyze dependence on problem structure.  
- Recommend adding a real-world experiment (e.g., policy-gradient robotics) where evaluation speed and moderate parameter dimensionality make the approach relevant.
