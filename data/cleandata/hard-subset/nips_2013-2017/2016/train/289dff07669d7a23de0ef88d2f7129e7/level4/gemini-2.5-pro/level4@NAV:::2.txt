This paper presents a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, replacing the standard eigen-decomposition with a Cholesky decomposition to improve its time and memory efficiency. On the whole, the manuscript is clearly written, the proposed methodology appears methodologically sound, and the experimental results—particularly the reported speedup rates—are highly encouraging. This work demonstrates considerable potential to become a new standard implementation of CMA-ES.

However, despite these strengths, there is a significant concern regarding the paper's originality. The contribution appears to be a trivial extension of the previously published work, "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies," as the core methods are nearly identical. I welcome the authors to correct this assessment if it is inaccurate.

Furthermore, several critical components are absent from the manuscript:

1.  The paper fails to demonstrate that the proposed algorithm is mathematically equivalent to the formulation in equation (2). While acknowledging that this may have been proven in prior work and that space is limited, this proof remains an essential element for the paper's completeness.

2.  The authors claim a computational complexity of O(μd²), which is identical to the reference CMA-ES implementation. This high-level analysis does not explain the observed performance gains. A more detailed discussion is required—perhaps using tighter asymptotic bounds like Θ-notation—to precisely articulate why the proposed algorithm is faster in practice.

3.  The claim that the error in the evolution path is small and decreases over time contradicts previous findings by Hansen. As the paper's theoretical analysis is not deterministic, this assertion requires direct empirical validation. A comparison of final performance is insufficient; dedicated experiments tracking this error over the optimization run must be conducted and presented.

A few minor points for correction:
1.  The notation should be carefully checked for consistency. For example, `wk` in Algorithm 2 should be `αk`, and `n` in Table 1 should be `d`.
2.  On line 185, 'F' should be lowercase 'f'.

Given these concerns about novelty and the omission of crucial analytical and experimental details, I conclude that the paper is not ready for publication at this conference in its current form.
