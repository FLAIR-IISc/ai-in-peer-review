- Proposes updating the CMA-ES covariance via Cholesky factor to reduce time and memory in high dimensions.
- Core idea of directly updating the Cholesky factor is sound and addresses a known CMA-ES bottleneck.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains motivation and algorithmic changes.
- Experiments show significant runtime speedups without increasing function evaluations to converge.
- Theoretical justification (notably Lemma 1) argues the Cholesky approximation does not materially harm convergence.
- Practical improvement is especially notable for larger dimensionalities.
- Empirical validation consistently supports the claimed speedups over standard CMA-ES.
- Originality is limited because the method heavily builds on Krause & Igel (2015) and Suttorp et al. (2009).
- Complexity analysis (O(µd^2)) is correct but lacks discussion of constant factors explaining observed speedups.
- Needs further empirical error analysis—e.g., behavior of rotation matrix E_t and explicit quantification of approximation error.
- Minor notation inconsistencies (wk, 'F' vs 'f', 'n' vs 'd') should be corrected.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant practical speedup, reasonable theoretical basis, and clear presentation.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited novelty, insufficient complexity detail, and incomplete error validation.
- Overall: valuable but incremental contribution; strengthen relation to prior work, expand complexity and error analyses.
- Recommendation: Reject with encouragement to revise and resubmit after addressing the weaknesses.
