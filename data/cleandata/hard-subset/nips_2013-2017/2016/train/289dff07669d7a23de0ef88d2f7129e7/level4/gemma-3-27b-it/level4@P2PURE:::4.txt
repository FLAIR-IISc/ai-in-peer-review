This work’s primary contribution is the application of triangular Cholesky factorization to the standard CMA-ES algorithm, eliminating the square root computation and thereby reducing the cost of matrix inversion. The authors provide a theoretical justification demonstrating that this modification does not negatively impact algorithm performance. The proposed method lowers the runtime complexity without a substantial increase in the number of objective function evaluations. This paper represents a valuable application of Cholesky factorization within the CMA-ES framework. Could the authors elaborate on the theoretical reasons behind the observed improved numerical stability? While the Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES, the reduction in wall-clock time appears to grow with the dimensionality of the search space. Given this, the authors might consider integrating a low-dimensional approximation technique, such as that presented in [Loshchilov, 2015], with their framework. The high dimensionality of many practical problems – for example, neural network training in direct policy search – necessitates addressing this concern.
