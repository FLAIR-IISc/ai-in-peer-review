This paper adopts the Cholesky decomposition instead of the eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of CMA-ES. Generally speaking, this paper is clearly written. The proposed method seems reasonable, and the experimental results (especially the speedup rate) are quite promising. I believe the proposed method could be a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future. Although this paper may be meaningful, the originality is limited because I think this work is a trivial extension of a previous work: a more efficient rank-one covariance matrix update for evolution strategies. The methods are essentially identical. Please correct me if I am wrong. Moreover, some very important points are strangely missing. 1) Your algorithm should show whether it is equivalent to formula (2). I understand that space is limited and this has been proven in previous work, but it is still essential for this paper. 2) You claim that the computational complexity is O(\mu d^2), which is exactly like CMA-ES-Ref. Please discuss in detail (perhaps using '\Theta' notation instead of 'O') why your algorithm is faster. 3) You claim that the path error is small and decreases over time, which contradicts Hansen's claim. Since your theoretical analysis is not conclusive, you should conduct tests to verify your claim. It is not enough to simply compare the final performance. Some minor comments: 1) Please check your notations carefully. For example, wk in algorithm 2 should be \alphak, and n in table 1 should be d. 2) 'F' in line 185 should be 'f'. For example, I think this paper is not worthy of NIPS in its current form.
