- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor directly, avoiding full covariance and square-root computations.
- Reduces complexity from O(d^3) (original CMA-ES) to O(μ d^2), with μ typically O(log d).
- Key approximation: omits estimation of a random orthogonal matrix E_t at each iteration.
- Provides an asymptotic argument that under convergence of covariance sequences, E_t → I and the methods become equivalent.
- Empirically validated on benchmark functions, showing substantial speed-up and no loss of optimization performance on synthetic tests.
- Compared against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.
- Practical caveat: in many applications objective evaluations dominate runtime, and typical CMA-ES problems have only a few tens of dimensions, limiting practical impact.
- Target use case appears to be fast-evaluation and higher-dimensional problems, where CMA-ES runtime matters.
- Suggested experiment: visualize evolution/norms of E_t by running full CMA-ES while computing the Cholesky approximation in parallel.
- Suggested inclusion of baseline results for standard CMA-ES runtimes/performance without delay strategies.
- Suggested systematic study on quadratic functions by varying spectral distributions (e.g., Stich & Müller 2012) to assess solution quality and E_t error.
- Suggested real-world experiment (e.g., policy-gradient in real-time robotics) to demonstrate practical benefit in time-critical, moderate-dimensional settings.
