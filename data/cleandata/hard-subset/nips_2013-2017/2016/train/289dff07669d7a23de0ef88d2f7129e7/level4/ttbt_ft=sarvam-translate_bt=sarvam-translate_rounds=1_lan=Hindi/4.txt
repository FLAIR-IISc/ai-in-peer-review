The main contribution of this work is the use of the triangular Cholesky factorization technique, which can eliminate square roots in standard CMA-ES, thereby saving computation cost when calculating the inverse of a matrix. The author presents an approach that comes with theoretical justification to show that it does not degrade the performance of the algorithm. The proposed method reduces the algorithm's runtime complexity without any significant change in the number of objective function evaluations. This paper is a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES. Can the author theoretically explain why a 'numerically more stable' calculation can be achieved? It seems that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same objective function evaluation as standard CMA-ES, and it reduces wall-clock time, and this search space grows with dimensionality. Can the author consider combining a low-dimensional approximation [Loshchilov, 2015] with the existing framework because, in most practical cases, such as training neural networks in direct policy search, the high dimensionality of the problems should be a non-negligible concern?
