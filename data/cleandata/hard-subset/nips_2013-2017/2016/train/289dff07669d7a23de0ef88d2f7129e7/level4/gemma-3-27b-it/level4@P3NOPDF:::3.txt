## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission: [Paper Title - Please Insert]

This paper investigates Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) with a novel update scheme for the triangular Cholesky factor, employing both rank-1 and rank-$\mu$ updates in a sequential manner. The authors demonstrate, under convergence assumptions for the covariance matrix, that their modified cumulative step-size adaptation maintains the algorithm's core behavior. The paper supports these claims with experimental results illustrating algorithm performance.

While the work presents a logical extension of existing CMA-ES techniques, its overall contribution appears incremental. The paper builds upon the rank-1 update to the triangular Cholesky factor introduced by Krause et al. (2015), generalizing this approach to a rank-$\mu$ update implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. However, given the existing literature, the novelty of this generalization feels limited. It’s unclear whether the increased complexity of the rank-$\mu$ approach consistently yields substantial improvements over the simpler rank-1 method. A more thorough comparative analysis demonstrating a clear benefit would strengthen the paper's argument.

The theoretical analysis presented, particularly Lemma 1, aims to justify the modification to the step-size adaptation. While the authors acknowledge that rigorous convergence proofs for CMA-ES are often based on approximations rather than the exact formulae (citing Beyer, ECJ 2014), this point could be elaborated upon to bolster confidence in the lemma’s validity. A deeper discussion of the implications of these approximations, and how they relate to the assumptions made in the current work, would be valuable.  

Several minor issues detract from the paper’s polish. First, in Equation (1), the $\sigma$ in the denominator should be $\sigma_t$. The same typographical error appears in Footnote 2 on page 3. Second, in line 94, the phrase “which can by be motivated by principles from…” contains a superfluous “by” and should read “which can be motivated by principles from…”. Addressing these details would improve clarity and demonstrate attention to detail.

**Strengths:**

*   The paper addresses a relevant topic within the CMA-ES research area.
*   The analysis provides some theoretical justification for the proposed modification.
*   Experimental results are presented to support the claims.

**Weaknesses:**

*   The contribution feels incremental given related work (Krause et al., 2015).
*   The justification for the added complexity of the rank-$\mu$ update is not fully convincing.
*   The connection to existing convergence proofs could be more thoroughly discussed.
*   Minor typographical errors and grammatical inconsistencies are present.



**Questions for the Authors:**

*   Can you provide a more compelling justification for using a rank-$\mu$ update over the simpler rank-1 update proposed by Krause et al.? Specifically, under what conditions would the rank-$\mu$ approach be demonstrably superior?
*   How sensitive are the results to the choice of $\mu$? Is there a principled way to determine an appropriate value for $\mu$ based on the problem characteristics?
*   Could you elaborate on the implications of the approximations used in existing CMA-ES convergence proofs (e.g., Beyer, 2014) and how these relate to the assumptions made in your analysis?
*   What is the computational cost overhead of the rank-$\mu$ update compared to the rank-1 update?



**Overall Recommendation:**

I currently rate this paper as a **weak accept**. While the work is technically sound and presents a logical extension of existing methods, the limited novelty and lack of a strong justification for the added complexity prevent me from recommending a strong acceptance. Addressing the weaknesses outlined above, particularly providing a more compelling argument for the rank-$\mu$ update and clarifying the theoretical foundation, could significantly improve the paper.  I would be open to reconsidering with major revisions.
