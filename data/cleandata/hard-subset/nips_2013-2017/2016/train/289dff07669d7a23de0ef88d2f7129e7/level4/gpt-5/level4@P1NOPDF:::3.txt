This paper studies the Cholesky CMA-ES and extends the update of the triangular Cholesky factor to include both rank-1 and rank-mu updates, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. It proves, under the assumption that the covariance converges, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly change the algorithm’s behavior. The paper also presents experiments evaluating the algorithm’s performance.

1. Contribution and novelty. While the paper addresses the Cholesky CMA-ES and extends the triangular Cholesky factor update to combine rank-1 and rank-mu updates, I do not find the contribution substantial relative to prior work. In particular, Krause et al. (2015) already proposed a rank-1 update to the triangular Cholesky factor. The present work appears to be a straightforward generalization of that procedure to rank-mu updates by applying a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2. Theoretical support for assumptions. The convergence of the covariance C_t in CMA-ES has been proved through theoretical analysis (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although that analysis does not use the exact CMA-ES update equations. This result may increase confidence in the convergence assumption underlying Lemma 1.
