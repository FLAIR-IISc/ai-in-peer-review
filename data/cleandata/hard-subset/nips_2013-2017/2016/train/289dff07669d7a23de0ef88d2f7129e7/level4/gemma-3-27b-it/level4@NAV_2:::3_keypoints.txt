- Paper extends Cholesky-CMA-ES to support rank-µ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor and replaces the matrix square root in CSA with the triangular Cholesky factor.
- Authors show theoretically (via Lemma 1) that this modification does not significantly change algorithm behavior under convergence assumptions.
- Empirical results show runtime and memory speedups and performance gains, especially in higher dimensions.
- Work builds on Krause and Igel (2015) by generalizing rank-1 updates to rank-µ as a sequence of rank-1 updates; novelty is incremental.
- Theoretical justification is valuable but could better connect to existing covariance convergence proofs (e.g., Beyer 2014).
- Strength: clear presentation of the algorithm and its theoretical approximation.
- Strength: demonstrated computational efficiency gains (reduced runtime and memory) without more function evaluations.
- Weakness: minor typographical and grammatical errors (e.g., σ/σt, footnote, line 94).
- Weakness: analysis focuses on asymptotic complexity; lacks detailed discussion of constant factors and practical implications of approximating st with ŝt.
- Weakness: need empirical analysis of how quickly the rotation matrix Et converges and its impact during early iterations.
- Weakness: experimental comparisons could be broader, including more recent CMA-ES variants.
- Argument for acceptance: theoretically justified, practically useful improvement addressing a CMA-ES bottleneck.
- Argument against acceptance: incremental novelty and theoretical assumptions not fully justified for the modified CSA.
- Overall recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical connections, additional empirical comparisons, and typographical fixes.
