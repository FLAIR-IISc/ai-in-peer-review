Summary
The paper proposes replacing the eigen/square-root decomposition in CMA-ES with a triangular Cholesky factor that is updated directly. Sampling uses x = A y + m with y ∼ N(0, I), and the covariance update (rank-1 plus rank-µ) is implemented as a sequence of rank-one updates to the Cholesky factor A, yielding O(µ d^2) time per iteration and minimal memory. To avoid needing C−1/2 in the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA), the authors replace it with A−1, argue that the induced rotation error diminishes as the algorithm converges, and empirically observe similar numbers of function evaluations while achieving substantial wall-clock speedups. This builds on prior work that updated non-triangular factors [Suttorp et al., 2009] and on rank-one Cholesky updates for special CMA-ES variants [Krause & Igel, 2015], and extends them to standard CMA-ES with rank-µ updates and a modified CSA.

Strengths
- Addresses a highly relevant bottleneck in CMA-ES; the speedups reported are impressive.
- Clear high-level presentation; the algorithm is simple to implement and reduces memory.
- The triangular factor gives eigenvalues “for free” and allows a numerically stable A−1 in O(d^2).
- Empirical results suggest no loss in evaluation efficiency and substantial runtime gains, particularly in higher dimensions.

Weaknesses and requested clarifications
Originality. While the paper is meaningful and potentially impactful, the novelty seems limited. The method appears to be a straightforward extension of “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies” (Krause & Igel, 2015) to rank-µ with a triangular Cholesky factor, plus a CSA modification. Please clarify precisely what is technically new beyond:
- Extending from µ = 1 to general rank-µ updates, and
- The specific CSA approximation and its analysis.
If I am misunderstanding the relation to Krause & Igel (2015), please correct me and state the specific differences.

Quality and completeness.
1) Equivalence to the covariance update (Eq. 2). It is essential to show (or cite a complete proof) that the sequence of rank-one updates to A indeed yields the same C_{t+1} as Eq. (2) (rank-1 plus rank-µ) when reconstructed as C = A A^T. Even if space is limited, a concise theorem with a pointer to a full derivation (or a self-contained derivation in the supplement) is needed.

2) Complexity discussion. You claim O(µ d^2) complexity, which is the same order as the amortized O(d^2) of CMA-ES with delayed decompositions. Please:
- Use Θ-notation and provide an amortized analysis for the reference implementation (including the cost and frequency of eigen/square-root computations) to make a fair comparison.
- Explain where your gains come from in practice (constant factors, eliminating cubic EVDs, better cache locality, avoiding uneven compute due to delays, fewer auxiliary matrices, etc.).
- Discuss memory complexity quantitatively (e.g., triangular factor vs storing C, B, D) and its impact on cache effects for large d.

3) CSA approximation and error analysis. You claim the path error is small and decreases over time owing to the convergence of rotations E_t, but the analysis is non-deterministic and relies on smooth changes in C_t. Hansen (2015) emphasizes the role of C−1/2 in CSA and notes a lack of invariance guarantees even with the standard update. To substantiate your claim, please add experiments that directly assess the CSA proxy, not only final performance:
- Track ||p_σ||/E{χ} over time for both algorithms and compare distributions/trajectories.
- Compare the step-size trajectories σ_t side by side.
- Quantify rotation drift via ∥E_t E_{t−1}^T − I∥ and relate it to deviations in p_σ.
- Report these diagnostics during both transient and near-converged phases, including difficult functions (e.g., Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) where C_t may change rapidly early on.
Some of this seems to be in the supplement (rotation measurements), but the main text should include key plots to validate the CSA claim.

Additional comments on clarity and correctness.
- Notation and typos: in Algorithm 2, w_k should be α_k; in Table 1, n should be d; “F” should be “f” in line 185. Please proofread carefully for consistency (e.g., QR vs RQ vs polar decomposition).
- Numerical stability: discuss whether periodic reconditioning of A is needed under repeated rank-one updates and how this compares to reorthogonalization in the reference implementation.
- Invariance: while full affine invariance is not guaranteed for CSA, please clarify what invariances are retained or lost under the proposed approximation, and back this with the rotation experiments.
- “Optimal” complexity: if you claim asymptotic optimality for full covariance adaptation, formalize the lower bound (e.g., the need to process µ rank-one terms implies Θ(µ d^2) flops under standard arithmetic), or cite appropriate results.

Significance
If the above issues are addressed, the approach could become a practical default for full CMA-ES, offering strong runtime savings without sacrificing evaluation efficiency. The significance hinges on clearly demonstrated equivalence to Eq. (2), a rigorous and empirical validation of the CSA approximation, and a thorough complexity discussion.

Pros for acceptance
- Tackles an important bottleneck with substantial empirical speedups.
- Simple, memory-efficient implementation with practical benefits (eigenvalues, A−1).
- Broad applicability to non-separable, ill-conditioned problems where full C is needed.

Cons for acceptance
- Novelty over prior Cholesky-update work appears limited and should be clarified.
- Missing proof/derivation of equivalence to the standard covariance update.
- CSA approximation not convincingly validated beyond final performance metrics.
- Complexity comparison needs a precise amortized Θ-analysis and constant-factor discussion.
- Notational errors and minor clarity issues.

Recommendation
Promising but not yet ready. I lean toward rejection in its current form. With a clear proof of equivalence to Eq. (2), rigorous CSA validation (theory plus diagnostics), and a precise complexity analysis, this could be a strong paper and a useful new default for CMA-ES.
