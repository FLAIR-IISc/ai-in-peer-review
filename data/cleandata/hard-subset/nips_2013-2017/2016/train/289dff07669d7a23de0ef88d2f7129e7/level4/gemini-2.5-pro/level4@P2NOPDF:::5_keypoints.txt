- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update Cholesky factor directly to approximate covariance adaptation, reducing complexity from O(d³) to O(μd²).  
- Key approximation: forgoes estimating a per-iteration random orthogonal matrix E_t and assumes E_t → I asymptotically under restrictive conditions.  
- Theoretical justification provided but relies on strong, potentially unrealistic convergence assumptions for the covariance sequence.  
- Empirical results show significant runtime speed-up and comparable optimization performance on synthetic benchmarks versus two state-of-the-art low-cost CMA-ES variants.  
- Major practical limitation: internal CMA-ES cost is often negligible compared to expensive black-box function evaluations, so speedups matter mainly for high-dimension, very-fast-evaluation scenarios.  
- Strength: meaningful contribution to algorithmic efficiency for scenarios with fast evaluations and large dimensions.  
- Suggested experiment 1: track and plot the evolution of E_t (or its deviation from identity) by running full CMA-ES in parallel to validate the core approximation.  
- Suggested experiment 2: include the standard (full) CMA-ES as a baseline to clarify trade-offs against the original algorithm.  
- Suggested experiment 3: run controlled studies on quadratic functions while varying Hessian condition number to assess performance and approximation accuracy vs. conditioning.  
- Suggested experiment 4: demonstrate utility on a real-world, speed-critical task (e.g., policy optimization in RL or robotics) to show practical impact.
