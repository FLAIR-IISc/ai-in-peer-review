- Proposes a CMA-ES variant called “Cholesky-CMA-ES” that reduces covariance update and storage complexity.  
- Naming is misleading: Suttorp et al. (2009) already introduced “Cholesky-CMA-ES” and is not cited.  
- Method builds on Krause et al. (2015); main algorithmic change is repeated application of rankOneUpdate to all individuals (a single “for” loop).  
- Empirical results show a discrepancy on the Cigar function plateau between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES, possibly due to median-plotting methodology.  
- Paper should include empirical comparison to Suttorp et al.’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and a detailed algorithmic comparison to Krause et al.  
- Claiming “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is misleading: not optimal for all problems (e.g., Sphere or separable functions) and only a constant (≈2×) cost reduction over Suttorp et al.  
- The paper’s claim that Suttorp et al.’s method is numerically unstable contradicts the original publication’s position.  
- Omitting Suttorp et al. from Figure 3 is problematic and obscures the paper’s true contribution.  
- The approach likely doesn’t change CMA-ES’s large-scale optimization limits (still quadratic complexity) but may be the most effective way to save time/memory when using the full covariance.  
- The work is incremental—core contribution is essentially a single for-loop—and revisions should: resolve naming, add comparisons to Suttorp/Krause variants (update figures or move full results to supplement), and explicitly state or justify the main algorithmic contribution.  
- Minor: The statement that eigenvalue decomposition is performed in each update is inaccurate; in practice it is done periodically, not every iteration.
