This paper's primary contribution is the utilization of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the need for square root operations in the standard CMA-ES algorithm, thereby reducing computational costs associated with matrix inversion. The author presents a theoretically justified approach that maintains the algorithm's performance while decreasing its runtime complexity, without significantly impacting the number of objective function evaluations. This work provides a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES.

A more detailed theoretical explanation for the 'numerically more stable' computation achieved by the proposed method would be beneficial. The results indicate that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES, while reducing wall-clock time, particularly with increasing search space dimensionality. The author may consider combining the low-dimensional approximation proposed by Loshchilov (2015) with the existing framework, as high-dimensionality is a significant concern in many practical applications, such as training neural networks in direct policy search.
