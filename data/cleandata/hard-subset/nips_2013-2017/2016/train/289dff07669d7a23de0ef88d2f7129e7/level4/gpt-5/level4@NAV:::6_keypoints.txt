- Problematic naming: calling the method “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and is not acknowledged.
- Incremental contribution: algorithmic change is minor—essentially adding a “for” loop to invoke rankOneUpdate over all individuals.
- Methodological overlap: proposal is an incremental extension of Krause et al. (2015) rather than a novel approach.
- Missing comparisons: paper should empirically compare to Suttorp et al.’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically to Krause et al.
- Empirical discrepancy: inconsistent plateau levels on the Cigar function between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES suggest plotting/median issues.
- Misleading optimality claim: “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is overstated—does not apply to Sphere or separable problems and ignores diagonal parameterizations.
- Limited speedup: proposed method appears only about twice as cheap as Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES, not a dramatic improvement.
- Numerical stability concerns: authors suggest Suttorp’s method is unstable, contradicting the original paper; omitting that method from Figure 3 risks biasing readers.
- Scalability unchanged: complexity remains quadratic, so no meaningful improvement for large-scale CMA-ES, though it may be the best option if full covariance is retained.
- Overall assessment: contribution is overly incremental (mostly adding a single loop).
- Requested revisions: clarify naming conflict; include experiments with Suttorp and Krause variants and update figures; explicitly state or justify that the main change is the “for” loop.
- Minor factual correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically in well-designed implementations, not at every update step.
