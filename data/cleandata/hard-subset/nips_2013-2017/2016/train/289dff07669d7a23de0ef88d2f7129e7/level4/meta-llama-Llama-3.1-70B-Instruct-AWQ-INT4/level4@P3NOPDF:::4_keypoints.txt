- Introduces triangular Cholesky factorization to remove the square root in CMA-ES, reducing matrix inversion cost.  
- Provides a theoretically grounded modification that does not degrade algorithmic performance.  
- Cholesky-CMA-ES lowers runtime complexity while keeping objective function evaluations nearly unchanged.  
- Demonstrates noticeable wall-clock time reductions, increasingly significant with higher problem dimensionality.  
- Claims improved numerical stability but lacks a deeper theoretical explanation for this assertion.  
- Suggests integrating Loshchilov (2015) low-dimensional approximation to address high-dimensional problems.  
- Notes applicability to practical high-dimensional tasks (e.g., direct policy search for neural networks) and potential for further scalability gains.
