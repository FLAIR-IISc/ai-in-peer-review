- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES using triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce covariance update and storage cost, with theoretical justification that performance is preserved.
- Main contribution: achieves asymptotically optimal time complexity O(µ d^2) for covariance updates and optimal memory complexity.
- Enables numerically stable quadratic-time computation of the inverse Cholesky factor and provides covariance eigenvalues at no extra cost.
- Paper is well-structured and clearly written, making the method and arguments easy to follow.
- Experiments: matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations but significantly reduces wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.
- Weakness: lacks a detailed analysis of why the Cholesky approach is numerically more stable than standard CMA-ES.
- Weakness: does not explore combining Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-dimensional approximation techniques for very high-dimensional problems.
- Suggested future work: integrate low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to improve scalability for tasks like neural network policy search.
- Overall assessment: valuable contribution with potential wide adoption; recommendation to accept with minor revisions (rating 8/10).
