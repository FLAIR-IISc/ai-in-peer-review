This paper proposes an extension to the Cholesky Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) by incorporating both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. The rank-μ update is implemented as a sequence of individual rank-1 updates. The authors provide a theoretical analysis showing that, under the assumption of covariance matrix convergence, their proposed modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper's claims are supported by experimental results evaluating the algorithm's performance.

**Major Comments**

1.  **Limited Novelty:** The primary concern with this work lies in its incremental nature. The core idea of applying a rank-1 update directly to the triangular Cholesky factor has been previously explored by Krause et al. (2015). The proposed extension to a rank-μ update, by decomposing it into a sequence of rank-1 updates, appears to be a straightforward generalization of this existing procedure. The contribution would be more significant if the authors could better articulate the unique challenges or non-trivial insights gained from this extension.

2.  **Theoretical Justification:** The theoretical analysis, particularly Lemma 1, is based on the assumption that the covariance matrix C_t converges. While this is a common assumption, the authors could strengthen their argument by acknowledging and discussing existing theoretical work on the convergence of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014). Although these prior analyses may not apply to the exact CMA-ES formulation, referencing them would provide valuable context and bolster the justification for the convergence assumption made in this paper.

**Minor Comments and Corrections**

The paper contains a few minor errors that should be corrected:

*   In Equation (1) and in footnote 2 on page 3, the step-size in the denominator should be indexed by time, i.e., `\sigma_t` instead of `\sigma`.
*   In line 94, the phrase "which can by be motivated by principles from..." contains a superfluous "by". It should be corrected to "which can be motivated by...".
