1. Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce time and space complexity.  
2. Shows Cholesky modification preserves optimization performance in terms of function evaluations.  
3. Demonstrates significant wall-clock speedups, increasing with dimension (up to ~20x at d=64).  
4. Provides a theoretically justified adaptation that preserves standard CMA-ES behavior and achieves optimal complexity.  
5. Lemma 1: error from replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factor vanishes as the algorithm converges.  
6. Empirical evaluation is comprehensive: multiple CMA-ES variants, benchmark functions, dimensions 4–256.  
7. Notes numerical-stability benefits: Cholesky gives eigenvalues cheaply and enables efficient inversion.  
8. Paper is clear and reproducible: well-structured explanations, good pseudocode (Algorithm 1 & 2).  
9. Clarifies relationship between Cholesky factor and matrix square root with clear theoretical justification.  
10. Original contribution: novel theoretical justification distinguishing it from prior Cholesky-based work.  
11. High practical significance for high-dimensional optimization tasks (hyperparameter tuning, RL policy search).  
12. Minor weakness: lacks exploration of combining Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-rank/approximate methods for very large d.  
13. Unclear threshold for "too large" dimensionality; cache effects observed at d=256 raise scalability questions for d~1000+.  
14. Needs more detailed theoretical/empirical evidence supporting claims of improved numerical stability.  
15. Would benefit from discussion/comparison with diagonal CMA-ES on problems suited to diagonal approximations.  
16. Overall assessment: strong, well-justified contribution; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address discussed limitations.
