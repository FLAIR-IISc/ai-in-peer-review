This paper explores the application of the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy, incorporating both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates for the triangular Cholesky factor in a sequential rank-1 update framework. The authors demonstrate, under the assumption of covariance convergence, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm's behavior. Experimental results are presented to evaluate the algorithm's performance.

1. The paper focuses on the Cholesky CMA-ES, extending the update mechanism for the triangular Cholesky factor by utilizing both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates. However, the significance of this contribution is questionable, as it builds upon the work of Krause et al. (2015), which employed rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. The current paper presents a straightforward extension of this approach to rank-\mu updates in a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2. The convergence of the covariance matrix Ct in CMA-ES has been theoretically established (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), albeit not based on the exact CMA-ES formulae. This existing proof may enhance the confidence in Lemma 1.
