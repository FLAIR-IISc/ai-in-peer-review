- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce complexity from O(d^3) to O(μd^2).
- Key idea: avoid estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t each step by maintaining Cholesky factor directly.
- Theoretical claim: under restrictive convergence assumptions, E_t converges to identity, making Cholesky-CMA-ES asymptotically equivalent to original CMA-ES.
- Empirical validation: runtime and convergence plots on standard benchmarks show substantial speed-up with no performance deterioration on synthetic tests.
- Compared against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay covariance updates.
- Major contribution: meaningful computational speed-up for CMA-ES in high-dimensional, fast-evaluation settings.
- Practical limitation: in many applications objective evaluation dominates cost, and typical problem dimensions are only a few tens, limiting practical impact.
- Target use-case narrow: benefits most when function evaluations are millisecond-scale and dimension is high.
- Suggested experiment 1: track evolution of E_t on trajectories (e.g., norms) by running full CMA-ES and comparing approximations.
- Suggested experiment 2: include actual runtimes and performance of standard (no-delay) CMA-ES for completeness.
- Suggested experiment 3: study quadratics with continuously varying spectra to relate solution quality and E_t error to spectral distribution.
- Suggested experiment 4: add a real-world, time-critical application (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) to demonstrate practical relevance.
- Weakness: theoretical assumptions may be too restrictive and applicability in practice is unclear.
- Weakness: paper needs more empirical breadth and real-world demonstrations to strengthen claims.
