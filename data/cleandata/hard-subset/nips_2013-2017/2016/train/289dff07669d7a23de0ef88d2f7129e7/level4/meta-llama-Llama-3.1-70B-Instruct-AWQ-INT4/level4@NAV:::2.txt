This paper explores an alternative approach to the CMA-ES by utilizing Cholesky decomposition in place of eigen-decomposition, thereby reducing time and storage complexity. Overall, the manuscript is well-written, and the proposed methodology appears sound. The experimental results, particularly the observed speedup rates, are encouraging and suggest that this approach may potentially become a standard variant of CMA-ES in the future. However, the originality of this work is somewhat limited, as it appears to be a relatively straightforward extension of previous research, specifically 'A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,' with which the methods bear a strong resemblance.

Several crucial aspects of the paper are lacking. Firstly, it is essential to demonstrate the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2), even if this has been previously established, due to the importance of this detail in the context of this manuscript. Secondly, the computational complexity of the algorithm is stated to be O(\mu d^2), identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A more in-depth discussion, potentially employing the '\Theta' notation, is necessary to elucidate why the proposed algorithm achieves superior performance. Furthermore, the claim that the error in the path is small and decreases over time contradicts Hansen's assertion. Given the non-deterministic nature of the theoretical analysis, experimental validation of this claim is required, with a comparison of final performance alone being insufficient.

Additionally, several minor issues were noted. The notation should be carefully reviewed, with specific examples including the substitution of 'wk' with '\alphak' in Algorithm 2 and the correction of 'n' to 'd' in Table 1. Moreover, 'F' should be replaced with 'f' in line 185. In its current form, this paper does not meet the standards for acceptance at NIPS.
