- Misleading naming and missing citation: calling the method "Cholesky-CMA-ES" ignores and fails to acknowledge prior Cholesky-CMA-ES work by Suttorp et al. (2009).  
- Incremental contribution: the algorithm appears to be a minor modification of Krause et al. (2015), primarily applying rankOneUpdate in a loop; authors must clarify novelty or reframe contribution.  
- Incomplete empirical/algorithmic comparisons: the paper omits comparisons to Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause et al.'s variant, which are essential.  
- Unsupported claim about numerical instability: the paper asserts Suttorp's algorithm is "prone to numerical instabilities" but provides no empirical evidence and contradicts the original publication.  
- Unverified efficiency claim: the statement that the proposed approach is "only two times cheaper" than Suttorp's method needs empirical verification and discussion.  
- Overstated optimality: claiming "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is too strong—many problems (e.g., Sphere, separable functions) require far less than a full covariance.  
- Limited large-scale impact: complexity remains O(n^2), so the work does not solve the core scaling challenge for very large-scale optimization despite constant-factor improvements.  
- Empirical discrepancy on Cigar: differing performance plateaus between CMA-ES-Ref and the proposed method suggest their behavior is not identical and requires investigation.  
- Revision required—naming/context: rename or clearly contextualize the algorithm relative to Suttorp et al. and other prior work.  
- Revision required—experiments: include Suttorp and Krause variants in experiments and update all figures; move full plots to supplement if needed.  
- Revision required—clarify novelty: explicitly explain whether the central contribution is the for-loop application of rankOneUpdate or something more substantial.  
- Minor correction: the claim that eigenvalue decomposition is typically performed at every update is incorrect—proper implementations do this only periodically.
