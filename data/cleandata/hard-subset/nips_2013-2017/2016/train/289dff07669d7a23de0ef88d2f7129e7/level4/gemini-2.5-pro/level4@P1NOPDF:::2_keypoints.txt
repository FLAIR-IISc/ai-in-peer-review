- Proposes replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky decomposition to reduce time and storage in CMA-ES; clearly written, sound method, promising speedups and potential to become a standard.

- Primary concern: limited originality—method appears nearly identical to prior work ("A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies"); clarification requested.

- Missing equivalence proof: paper must show the proposed algorithm is mathematically equivalent to the standard formulation (Equation (2)) for self-containment and correctness.

- Insufficient complexity analysis: claiming O(μd²) like reference is not enough—need tighter (Θ) analysis or discussion of constant factors explaining observed practical speedup.

- Unsupported claim about path error: assertion that approximation error is small and decreases contradicts prior findings and requires direct empirical tracking of the evolution-path error over optimization.

- Minor issues: notation inconsistencies (e.g., wk likely should be αk; n in Table 1 should be d) and a typo (line 185: 'F' should be 'f').

- Recommendation: cannot recommend acceptance in current form due to limited novelty and omission of key analytical and experimental details.
