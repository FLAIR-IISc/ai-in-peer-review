Summary
The paper studies a Cholesky-based CMA-ES variant that maintains and updates the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix. It extends prior work that used a rank-1 update by adding a rank-μ update, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The paper also analyzes a modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) and argues that, under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges, the modification does not materially change the algorithm’s behavior. Experimental results on benchmark problems are reported.

Strengths
- The topic is relevant: Cholesky-based updates are attractive for reducing computational cost in CMA-ES.
- The extension to include rank-μ information within the Cholesky framework is natural and practically useful.
- The theoretical argument about the modified CSA, while conditional, clarifies that the change should not adversely affect behavior under the stated assumption.
- The presentation appears generally clear and implementable.

Weaknesses and novelty
- The contribution is incremental relative to Krause et al. (2015), which already introduced rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. The proposed rank-μ update is essentially a straightforward generalization realized as a sequence of rank-1 updates. As presented, the work offers limited conceptual novelty beyond this extension.
- The theoretical result hinges on a strong assumption (covariance convergence) and does not deliver new convergence guarantees for CMA-ES. While this may be acceptable for a practical algorithmic paper, it limits the theoretical significance.
- The empirical evaluation, as summarized, does not convincingly demonstrate substantial performance gains or new insights beyond what could be expected from incorporating rank-μ information. Without clear evidence of improved scalability, robustness, or runtime efficiency relative to prior Cholesky CMA-ES methods, the impact remains modest.

Comments on theory
- The reliance on the convergence of the covariance matrix to justify the invariance of behavior under modified CSA is plausible but assumes a property that is not established for the exact algorithmic variant studied. Existing theoretical analyses (e.g., Beyer, ECJ 2014) provide convergence results for related settings but not the exact CMA-ES with the proposed modifications. Citing these results can increase confidence in Lemma 1, but the mismatch should be acknowledged.
- It would strengthen the paper to articulate precisely how the assumption aligns with or departs from known results, and to delimit the conditions under which the lemma is expected to hold in practice.

Comments on experiments
- Please detail the benchmark set, evaluation protocol, and computational budget, and compare against (at minimum) Krause et al. (2015) and a standard CMA-ES baseline with full covariance updates.
- Include ablations isolating: (i) rank-1 versus rank-μ within the Cholesky framework, and (ii) standard CSA versus the modified CSA, to quantify their respective contributions.
- Report both solution quality and computational cost (per-iteration time, memory, and wall-clock to target) to justify the practicality of the proposed updates.
- Provide statistical significance where appropriate and discuss variability across runs.

Clarity and presentation
- The paper is generally readable, but the relationship to prior Cholesky-CMA-ES work should be discussed more explicitly. Please clarify what is fundamentally new (algorithmically or analytically) beyond extending to rank-μ via repeated rank-1 updates.
- Include a concise complexity analysis and a discussion of numerical stability (e.g., accumulation of round-off errors across successive rank-1 updates, reorthogonalization needs, and safeguards such as jitter or periodic recomputation).
- A high-level algorithmic pseudocode block would help reproducibility.

Minor issues and typos
- Equation (1): The denominator should be σ_t rather than σ. The same issue appears in footnote 2 on page 3.
- Line 94: “which can by be motivated by principles from ...” — remove “by”.
- Please proofread for other minor inconsistencies.

Overall assessment
This submission addresses a practical variant of CMA-ES with an intuitively useful extension from rank-1 to rank-μ updates in the Cholesky factor, and it offers an analysis suggesting that a modified CSA does not substantially alter behavior under a convergence assumption. However, the novelty beyond prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work appears limited, and the theoretical contribution is conditional rather than establishing new guarantees. The empirical section needs to more clearly demonstrate advantages over existing methods, especially Krause et al. (2015), in terms of both performance and efficiency.

I encourage the authors to (i) better delineate their unique contributions relative to prior art, (ii) strengthen the empirical evidence with targeted ablations and runtime analyses, and (iii) refine the presentation and correctness of technical details. With these improvements, the paper could provide a more compelling case for the practical value of the proposed extension.
