- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of full covariance to reduce complexity.
- Reduces time complexity to O(μ d^2) versus O(d^3) in original CMA-ES.
- Key algorithmic difference is avoiding per-step estimation of a random orthogonal matrix E_t.
- Under restrictive assumptions, authors show E_t converges to identity, making the approximation equivalent to full CMA-ES.
- Empirical validation uses runtime and convergence plots on selected objective functions.
- Compares Cholesky-CMA-ES to two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.
- Reports considerable speedup with no loss of optimization performance on synthetic test cases.
- Notes practical limitation: black-box oracle runtime often dominates, so CMA-ES internal runtime is usually negligible.
- Points out typical CMA-ES applications are low-dimensional, so runtime improvements may be less impactful.
- Argues proposed method mainly targets high-dimensional, fast-evaluation scenarios that are relatively uncommon.
- Recommends more empirical evidence to strengthen claims, given theoretical guarantees are hard to obtain.
- Suggests tracking evolution and fluctuations of E_t along trajectories and directly comparing full CMA-ES vs approximation.
- Requests inclusion of actual runtimes (including CMA-ES without delay), simulation studies on quadratics with varied spectra, and a real-world experiment (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients).
