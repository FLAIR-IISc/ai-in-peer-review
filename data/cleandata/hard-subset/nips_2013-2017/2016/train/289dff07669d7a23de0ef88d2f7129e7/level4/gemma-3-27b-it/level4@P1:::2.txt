This paper adopts the Cholesky decomposition instead of the eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of the CMA-ES. Generally, the paper is clearly written and well-organized. The proposed method appears reasonable and the experimental results, particularly the reported speedup rates, are promising. This approach has the potential to become a new standard implementation of CMA-ES. While the paper presents a valuable contribution, the originality is somewhat limited as it builds directly upon prior work, specifically “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies” [Krause and Igel, 2015]. The core update mechanism shares significant similarities. It would be helpful if the authors could explicitly address the novelty beyond this prior work. Furthermore, several key aspects are missing or could be strengthened.

First, the authors should demonstrate the equivalence of their algorithm to the standard update rule in equation (2). While space is limited and the connection to previous work exists, explicitly showing this equivalence is crucial for clarity and completeness. Second, the paper claims a computational complexity of O(µd<sup>2</sup>), which is the same asymptotic complexity as the reference CMA-ES implementation. A more detailed discussion, potentially using Θ-notation to highlight the constant factors and practical differences, is needed to explain *why* the proposed algorithm achieves faster runtime performance. Third, the authors assert that the error introduced by replacing *s<sub>t</sub>* with *ŝ<sub>t</sub>* is small and decreases over time, seemingly contrasting with Hansen’s claim [Hansen, 2015]. Given that the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, further experimentation is required to validate this claim. Comparing only final performance is insufficient. Analyzing the error trajectory over iterations, or demonstrating convergence rates, would be beneficial. 

Several minor comments also warrant attention: 1) Please carefully review the notation throughout the paper. For example, *w<sub>k</sub>* in Algorithm 2 should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, and *n* in Table 1 should be *d*. 2) There is a typographical error on line 185, where ‘F’ should be ‘f’.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Addresses a practical bottleneck in CMA-ES (covariance matrix update and storage).
*   Presents a theoretically grounded approach with a potential for improved efficiency.
*   Demonstrates significant speedups in empirical evaluations, especially as dimensionality increases.
*   Clear and generally well-written explanation of the method.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   Limited originality, building heavily on previous work without clearly articulating the distinct contributions.
*   Insufficient explanation of the practical performance gains given the same asymptotic complexity.
*   Lack of rigorous validation of the error introduced by the approximation in the step-size adaptation.
*   Minor notational inconsistencies and typographical errors.

Overall, while this paper presents a potentially useful improvement to the CMA-ES algorithm, the concerns regarding originality, detailed analysis, and validation prevent me from recommending acceptance in its current form. Further clarification, analysis, and experimentation are needed to strengthen the contribution and address the identified weaknesses.
