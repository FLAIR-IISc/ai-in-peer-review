

CMA-ES represents the state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It adapts the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution through various learning mechanisms, which can equivalently be viewed as metric adaptation. This paper introduces a novel covariance matrix update strategy based on triangular Cholesky factors. Compared to prior methods, this approach significantly reduces both computational complexity and memory requirements for storing the algorithm's state parameters. The authors provide thorough numerical evaluation, demonstrating that the new update maintains the standard CMA-ES performance in terms of function evaluations required to reach a target, while substantially reducing wall-clock time—particularly for high-dimensional problems.  

This work constitutes a significant contribution to optimization. The proposed variant is well-positioned to become the default CMA-ES implementation for medium-to-large-scale problems, offering clear advantages over the current standard: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and the immediate availability of the covariance matrix's eigenvalues at no additional cost. The paper is well-written and the methodology is rigorously validated. Given CMA-ES's strong connections to machine learning, information geometry, and its established use in reinforcement learning and supervised learning, this contribution aligns well with NIPS' scope.
