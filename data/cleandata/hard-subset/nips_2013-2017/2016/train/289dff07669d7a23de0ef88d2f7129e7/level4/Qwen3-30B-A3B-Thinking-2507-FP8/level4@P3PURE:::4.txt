

The primary contribution of this work lies in employing triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate square roots in standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing computational cost for matrix inversion. The authors present a theoretically justified approach demonstrating that this modification does not degrade algorithmic performance. The proposed method achieves reduced runtime complexity without a significant increase in the number of objective function evaluations, representing a valuable implementation of Cholesky factorization for CMA-ES. The authors should clarify the theoretical basis for the claimed numerical stability improvement. While the Cholesky-CMA-ES variant requires the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES and reduces wall-clock time—particularly as search space dimensionality increases—the authors are encouraged to consider integrating low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loshchilov, 2015) into their framework. This integration would address a critical practical concern, as high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural network training in direct policy search) remain prevalent in real-world applications.
