- Primary contribution: uses triangular Cholesky factorization to remove square roots in CMA-ES, reducing computational cost of matrix inversion.
- Theoretical justification: modification is shown not to degrade algorithmic performance.
- Empirical/complexity claim: achieves reduced runtime complexity without a significant increase in objective function evaluations—valuable Cholesky implementation for CMA-ES.
- Request for clarification: authors should justify the theoretical basis for the claimed numerical stability improvement.
- Performance detail: Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective evaluations as standard CMA-ES while reducing wall-clock time, especially as dimensionality increases.
- Recommendation: consider integrating low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to better handle very high-dimensional problems.
- Practical concern: addressing scalability to high-dimensional tasks (e.g., neural network training in direct policy search) is critical for real-world applicability.
