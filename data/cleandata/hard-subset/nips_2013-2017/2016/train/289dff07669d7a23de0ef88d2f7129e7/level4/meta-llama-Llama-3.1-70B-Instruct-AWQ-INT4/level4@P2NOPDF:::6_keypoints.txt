- Title "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is misleading and fails to acknowledge prior use by T. Suttorp et al. (2009).
- Authors do not cite or compare to Suttorp et al.'s similar Cholesky-CMA-ES, presenting the variant as novel.
- Method is an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015); main change is repeated rank-one updates.
- The repeated rank-one update (loop over individuals) is essentially already present in Krause et al.'s work.
- Empirical results show discrepancies on the Cigar function (plateau differences), possibly due to plotting medians.
- Authors should empirically compare to Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically to Krause’s variant.
- Claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading—doesn't apply to Sphere or separable functions.
- Proposed method is only about twice as efficient as Suttorp’s approach, which is omitted from Figure 3 and could mislead readers.
- Contrary to the paper’s claims, Suttorp et al. report their method is not prone to numerical instabilities.
- Even with improvements, complexity remains quadratic, so impact on large-scale optimization is limited.
- Overall contribution is incremental—the primary algorithmic change is adding a single for-loop.
- Recommendations: fix naming/citation issues, run comparisons with Suttorp and Krause variants, and clarify or justify the main algorithmic contribution.
- Minor: statement that eigenvalue decomposition is performed at every update is inaccurate—proper implementations do it periodically.
