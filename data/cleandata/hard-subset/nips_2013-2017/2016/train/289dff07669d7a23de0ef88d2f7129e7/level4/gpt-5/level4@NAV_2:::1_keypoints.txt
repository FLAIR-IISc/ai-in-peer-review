- Paper introduces a new CMA-ES covariance-update using triangular Cholesky factors that reduces update cost and memory footprint.
- Method can be viewed as adapting an underlying metric and yields covariance eigenvalues at no extra cost.
- Empirical evaluation shows the new update matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluation efficiency.
- New method delivers substantial wall-clock time reductions, especially in higher dimensions.
- Contribution is highly significant and likely to become the default for medium-to-large-scale problems.
- Clear advantages: reduced memory, reduced update complexity, and free access to covariance eigenvalues.
- Paper is well written, evaluation is careful, and the topic fits NIPS given ties to ML, information geometry, and RL/supervised learning.
- Minor concern: clarify how implementations were made comparable across compute environments (language, platform, CPU).
- Minor concern: explain why Figure 2’s “more pronounced differences with higher dimensionality” does not hold for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
