This paper introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, a computationally efficient variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES). The standard CMA-ES algorithm's performance can be bottlenecked by the need to compute the square root of the d x d covariance matrix, an O(d^3) operation. The core contribution of this work is to replace this expensive step with a direct, quadratic-time update on the matrix's triangular Cholesky factor. This is achieved by modifying the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism, which normally relies on the inverse of the matrix square root. The authors provide a theoretical justification showing that this approximation does not deteriorate the algorithm's performance, as the rotational error introduced by using the Cholesky factor diminishes as the algorithm converges. The proposed method significantly reduces the per-iteration time and space complexity, making the full-covariance CMA-ES more practical for higher-dimensional problems.

**Strengths:**

*   **Sound Theoretical Justification:** A key strength of the paper is the theoretical argument provided to justify the approximation in the step-size adaptation. Lemma 1 and the subsequent discussion convincingly argue that as the shape of the covariance matrix converges, the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root's RQ-decomposition with a simple triangular Cholesky factor becomes negligible. This provides a solid foundation for the proposed modification.
*   **Significant Performance Improvement:** The empirical results are compelling. The experiments clearly demonstrate that Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a dramatic reduction in wall-clock time compared to the standard CMA-ES implementation, especially as the problem dimension increases. Critically, this speed-up is achieved without sacrificing performance in terms of the number of function evaluations required to find a solution.
*   **Practical Significance:** The proposed algorithm represents a valuable and practical contribution to the field. It addresses a well-known computational bottleneck in one of the most powerful derivative-free optimization methods. By providing an efficient, drop-in replacement that maintains the performance characteristics of the original algorithm, this work extends the applicability of CMA-ES to more challenging problems in machine learning, such as direct policy search in reinforcement learning or hyperparameter tuning.

**Weaknesses and Questions for the Authors:**

This is a well-executed and valuable piece of work. The following points are primarily requests for clarification and suggestions for discussion.

1.  **Clarification on Numerical Stability:** The paper states that the proposed method is "numerically stable" and avoids potential instabilities associated with other approaches (e.g., separate updates for a matrix and its inverse). Could the authors please elaborate further on this point? A brief theoretical or intuitive explanation of why operating on a single triangular Cholesky factor provides enhanced numerical stability would strengthen this claim.
2.  **Discussion on High-Dimensional Scaling:** The authors correctly identify that for extremely large-scale problems, limited-memory approximations become necessary. The experiments confirm that Cholesky-CMA-ES still scales quadratically with dimensionality. Could the authors comment on the potential for combining their efficient Cholesky update mechanism with low-dimensional approximation methods, such as LM-CMA [Loshchilov, 2015]? It would be valuable to understand if the principles of updating a Cholesky factor could be extended to benefit these limited-memory variants, potentially offering a path to further improve their efficiency.

In summary, this paper presents a well-motivated, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated improvement to the standard CMA-ES algorithm. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES is a significant contribution that should become the new standard for applications where the full covariance matrix update is the computational bottleneck.
