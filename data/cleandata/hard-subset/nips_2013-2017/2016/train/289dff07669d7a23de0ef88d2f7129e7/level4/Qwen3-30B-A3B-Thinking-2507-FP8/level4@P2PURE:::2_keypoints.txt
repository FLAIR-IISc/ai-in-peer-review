- Paper uses Cholesky instead of eigendecomposition to reduce CMA-ES time/storage; clearly written with promising speedup results.  
- Approach has potential to become a new standard for CMA-ES.  
- Originality is limited—appears to be a trivial extension of "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies" and methods may be fundamentally identical.  
- Must explicitly demonstrate equivalence to Equation (2) within the paper.  
- The computational complexity claim O(μd²) (same as CMA-ES-Ref) needs detailed discussion using Θ notation to clarify why the algorithm is actually faster.  
- Claim that path error is small and decreases contradicts Hansen; because the theoretical analysis is non-deterministic, experimental validation of path error is required beyond final performance.  
- Minor notation corrections: wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk; n in Table 1 should be d.  
- Minor typo: change 'F' to 'f' in line 185.  
- Given these unresolved issues, the paper does not meet NIPS standards in its current form.
