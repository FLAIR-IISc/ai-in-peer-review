- Name conflict: The proposed "Cholesky-CMA-ES" shares its name with Suttorp et al. (2009); the paper should acknowledge and distinguish from that prior work.
- Incremental novelty: The method is mainly an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015), essentially repeating rankOneUpdate for all individuals; the significance of this change must be better explained.
- Incomplete experiments: Empirical comparisons omit Suttorp (2009) and Krause (2015) variants; additional experiments and updated figures (with supplement if needed) are required.
- Misleading optimality claim: Claiming optimal covariance update/storage complexity is overstated — simpler approaches suffice on separable problems or Sphere, so limitations must be discussed.
- Unconvincing superiority claim: The assertion that this is the best way to save time/memory for full covariance is not convincing; provide a balanced comparison, especially addressing Suttorp’s numerical stability claims.
- Overall contribution unclear: The work appears too incremental and its impact/significance is not well justified; address naming, additional experiments, and clarify the modification’s importance.
- Quality assessment: Technically sound but contributions and comparisons are unclear (Quality 6/10).
- Clarity assessment: Generally well-written but some sections need clearer explanation of significance (Clarity 7/10).
- Originality assessment: Limited originality due to incremental nature (Originality 5/10).
- Significance assessment: Some interesting ideas but unclear impact and incomplete comparisons (Significance 6/10).

- Against acceptance: Contributions are unclear, comparisons are incomplete, and complexity/optimality claims are misleading.
