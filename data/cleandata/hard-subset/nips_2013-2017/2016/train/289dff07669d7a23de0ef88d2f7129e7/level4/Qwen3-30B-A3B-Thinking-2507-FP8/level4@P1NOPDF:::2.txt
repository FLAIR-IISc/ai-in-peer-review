

This paper proposes using Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition to reduce time and storage complexity in CMA-ES. The writing is clear and well-structured. The method appears sound, and the experimental results—particularly the speedup metrics—are compelling. The approach could establish a new standard for CMA-ES implementations. However, the work’s originality is limited, as it constitutes a straightforward extension of prior research: "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The core methodology is nearly identical to this earlier work; please clarify if I have misunderstood.  

Furthermore, several critical elements are omitted. First, the paper should explicitly establish equivalence to Equation (2), as this is essential for reproducibility despite prior proofs. Second, the claim that computational complexity is O(μd²)—matching CMA-ES-Ref—requires deeper justification. Using Θ notation would clarify whether the algorithm’s constant factors are genuinely smaller, not just asymptotically equivalent. Third, the assertion that path errors decrease over time contradicts Hansen’s established analysis. Without deterministic theoretical support, experimental validation beyond final performance is necessary to substantiate this claim.  

Minor issues also require attention:  
1. Notation inconsistencies: Algorithm 2 uses `wk` instead of `αk`, and Table 1 uses `n` where `d` (dimension) is standard.  
2. Typographical error: 'F' should be 'f' in line 185.  

While the speedup results are promising, the paper fails to sufficiently differentiate itself from existing work or address these gaps. The lack of rigorous complexity analysis and unresolved theoretical contradictions undermine its contribution. For NIPS, the paper must demonstrate a meaningful advancement beyond incremental improvements. As currently written, it does not meet the conference’s standards for novelty and technical depth.
