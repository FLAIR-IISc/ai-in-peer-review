- Employs triangular Cholesky factorization to remove explicit matrix square root in CMA-ES, reducing covariance-handling costs and enabling cheaper matrix inversions.  
- Provides a theoretical rationale claiming this substitution does not degrade algorithm performance.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in number of objective evaluations while reducing wall-clock time.  
- Runtime gains grow with problem dimensionality, making the approach attractive for high-dimensional tasks.  
- Reviewer notes the work is a useful application of Cholesky-based updates within CMA-ES.  
- Requests expansion of the theoretical basis for the claimed “greater numerical stability.”  
- Suggests exploring integration of Loshchilov (2015) low-dimensional/limited-memory approximation for high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural network policy search).
