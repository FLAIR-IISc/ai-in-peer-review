- Proposes replacing eigenvalue decomposition in CMA-ES with Cholesky decomposition, reducing time and storage complexity.  
- Claims runtime of O(µd^2) for the adapted Cholesky-CMA-ES and provides theoretical convergence justification.  
- Experimental results show promising speedups, suggesting the method could become a new standard.  
- Originality is limited: method is an extension of prior work (Krause and Igel, 2015).  
- Missing proof/demonstration of equivalence to the original CMA-ES update rule (equation 2) for completeness.  
- Claims of being faster than CMA-ES-Ref are not fully justified despite identical O(µd^2) complexity; needs Θ-style analysis.  
- Claim that path error is small and decreases contradicts Hansen; theoretical analysis is non-deterministic and requires experimental validation.  
- Experiments should compare more than final performance to validate path-error and other dynamic properties.  
- Minor notation/formatting errors need correction (e.g., wk → αk in Algorithm 2, n → d in Table 1, F → f in line 185).  
- In current form, paper is not suitable for NIPS due to omissions and limited novelty, but could be significant after revisions.  
- Strength: efficient modification that reduces complexity and demonstrates empirical speedups.  
- Weakness: limited novelty and missing essential analyses/validations that undermine the paper’s claims.  
- Recommendation: include equivalence proof, provide detailed complexity analysis, run experiments validating path-error claims, and fix notation/formatting.
