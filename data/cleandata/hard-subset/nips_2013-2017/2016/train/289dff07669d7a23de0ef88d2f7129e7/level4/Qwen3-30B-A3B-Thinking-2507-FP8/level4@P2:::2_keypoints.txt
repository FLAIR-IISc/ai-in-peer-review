- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that uses Cholesky factor updates to avoid matrix square roots, aiming to reduce time and space complexity.  
- Claims maintain performance of standard CMA-ES while achieving optimal O(μd²) runtime and storage, validated on benchmark functions.  
- Clear, well-organized paper with thorough background and contextualization in prior literature.  
- Empirical results show large wall-clock speedups (up to 20× at d=64) and increasing gains with dimensionality.  
- Storage complexity and free eigenvalue extraction from Cholesky factors are practical advantages for large-scale problems.  
- Core method appears to be a direct extension of Krause & Igel (2015), so originality is limited.  
- Theoretical justification for applying Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES (especially CSA modification) is underdeveloped and not clearly differentiated from prior work.  
- Claim that the replacement error “decreases over time” lacks deterministic proof and relies mainly on empirical evidence.  
- Paper does not address Hansen’s point that CSA’s affine invariance may be violated by the modification.  
- Equivalence to Eq. (2) is asserted but not proven or algebraically derived.  
- Complexity claims (O(μd²)) need clarification: compare Θ-notation, constant factors, cache effects, and why practical speedup arises versus delayed-decomposition baselines.  
- Validation should include iteration-wise error metrics (e.g., ||Eₜ⁻¹Eₜ − I||) to support the “error decreases over time” claim.  
- Suggested revisions: explicitly contrast with Krause & Igel (2015), prove equivalence to key equations, report constants/Θ comparisons, and add error-tracking experiments.  
- Minor fixes: correct notation errors (e.g., wₖ→αₖ, n→d, F→f).  
- Recommendation: Reject in current form due to insufficient originality and theoretical depth, but potentially publishable after major revisions addressing theoretical justification, comparisons, and validation.
