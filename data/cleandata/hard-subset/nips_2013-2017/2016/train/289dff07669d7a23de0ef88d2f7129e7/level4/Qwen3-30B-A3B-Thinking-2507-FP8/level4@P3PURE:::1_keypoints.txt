- Paper introduces a novel CMA-ES variant that updates the covariance via triangular Cholesky factors.  
- Cholesky-based update reduces computational complexity and memory requirements vs prior covariance updates.  
- Numerical evaluation shows the variant preserves standard CMA-ES performance in function-evaluations to target.  
- The method significantly reduces wall-clock runtime, especially on high-dimensional problems.  
- The approach yields eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no extra cost.  
- The contribution is judged highly significant and suitable as the default for medium-to-large-scale optimization.  
- Paper is well-written and the experimental evaluation is thorough.  
- Relevance to NIPS is strong given CMA-ES’s ties to ML, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Minor concern: clarify how implementation comparability was ensured (identical language, compiler, hardware, etc.).  
- Minor concern: explain anomaly in Figure 2 where Cigar and Discus at dimension 256 do not show the expected trend.
