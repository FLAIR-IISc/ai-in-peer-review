- Main contribution: apply triangular Cholesky factorization to CMA-ES, eliminating the matrix square root and reducing computational cost in sampling and covariance updates.
- Theoretical justification: authors show the modification does not degrade algorithm performance.
- Runtime vs evaluations: method reduces runtime complexity without significantly changing the required number of objective function evaluations.
- Empirical result: similar objective-evaluation counts to standard CMA-ES but lower wall-clock time, with advantages growing with problem dimensionality.
- Overall assessment: a valuable and practical contribution to improving CMA-ES efficiency.
- Request 1: provide a more detailed theoretical explanation to substantiate the claim of being "numerically more stable."
- Request 2: address scalability to very high-dimensional problems, e.g., by combining with low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).
