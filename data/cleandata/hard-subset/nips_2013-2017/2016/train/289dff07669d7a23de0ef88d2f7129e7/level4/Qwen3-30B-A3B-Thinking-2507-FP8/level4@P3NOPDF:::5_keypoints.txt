- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, reducing covariance update cost from O(d³) to O(μd²) where μ ≈ O(log d).  
- Key innovation: maintain lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix and its square root.  
- Theoretical claim: under asymptotic assumptions the omitted orthogonal correction Eₜ converges to identity, making the methods equivalent in the limit.  
- Empirical results: comparable convergence to standard CMA-ES and significant runtime speedups on synthetic benchmarks.  
- Outperforms two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that use delayed full covariance updates.  
- Practical caveat: in typical applications (d ≤ 50) objective evaluation dominates runtime, so the speedup mainly benefits high-d, very fast-evaluation regimes.  
- Recommendation 1: plot ||Eₜ − I|| over optimization trajectories (compare full CMA-ES and Cholesky) to validate the asymptotic assumption.  
- Recommendation 2: include runtime and performance of standard (non-delayed) CMA-ES as a baseline to contextualize speedups.  
- Recommendation 3: expand quadratic tests to continuous eigenvalue spectra and relate ||Eₜ − I|| and solution quality to condition numbers.  
- Recommendation 4: add a real-world case study (e.g., fast-eval robotics RL) to demonstrate practical impact beyond synthetic problems.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound, well-written, and a valuable contribution; acceptance recommended pending the suggested empirical enhancements.
