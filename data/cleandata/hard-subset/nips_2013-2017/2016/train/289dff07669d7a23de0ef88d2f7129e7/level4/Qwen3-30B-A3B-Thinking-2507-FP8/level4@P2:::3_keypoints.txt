- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing the covariance matrix square root with a triangular Cholesky factor for adaptation.  
- Reduces time complexity from O(d³) to O(µ d²) and achieves optimal storage complexity.  
- Extends Krause & Igel (2015) from rank-1 updates to rank-µ updates via a sequence of rank-1 operations.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly change behavior under covariance convergence.  
- Empirical results on benchmarks show equivalent evaluation counts to standard CMA-ES but substantial wall-clock speedups, up to ~20× at d=64.  
- Well-motivated and addresses an important practical implementation bottleneck in CMA-ES.  
- Clear connection to prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) while applying it to standard CMA-ES.  
- Strong technical quality: sound theory and comprehensive experimental design across functions and dimensions.  
- Practical importance: significant runtime savings for large-scale problems where covariance updates dominate.  
- Contribution is incremental rather than a fundamental theoretical advance.  
- Lemma 1’s convergence assumptions lack a stronger theoretical foundation; connection to Beyer (2014) could help.  
- Paper is generally clear and well-organized, with a precise algorithm description and documented experiments.  
- Contains minor grammatical/notation errors to fix (e.g., denominator in Eq.(1) should be σ_t; typo at line 94).  
- Originality: novel extension to rank-µ but insufficiently distinguished from prior work, weakening novelty claims.  
- Significance: practically meaningful for cases where full covariance is still maintained, but limited outside that setting.  
- Acceptance pros: practical efficient implementation, theoretically justified extension, and strong empirical validation.  
- Acceptance cons: incremental novelty, limited theoretical convergence justification, and insufficient differentiation from Krause & Igel (2015).
