- Proposes replacing CMA-ES eigendecomposition with Cholesky to reduce computational/storage cost.  
- Paper is well-written, methodologically sound, and shows promising empirical speedups.  
- Proposed method could become a widely adopted CMA-ES variant given performance gains.  
- Major weakness: limited novelty—appears to closely extend prior work ("A More Efficient Rank-one...") with functionally identical updates.  
- Authors should clarify key differences and novel theoretical/algorithmic contributions versus prior work.  
- Missing formal proof or clear reference showing equivalence to standard CMA-ES covariance update (formula 2).  
- Complexity analysis is insufficient: O(μd²) hides constant factors; need Θ-notation or operation counts to justify observed speedup.  
- Claim that evolution path error is small/diminishing is unsubstantiated and may contradict prior analyses; requires empirical tracking of the error over generations.  
- Minor issues: notational inconsistencies (Algorithm 2: wk→αk; Table 1: n→d) and a typo (line 185: 'F'→'f').  
- Overall recommendation: promising but insufficient novelty and missing critical theoretical/empirical justifications—cannot recommend acceptance.
