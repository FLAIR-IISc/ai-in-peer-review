- Paper extends Cholesky CMA-ES to include rank-μ updates by decomposing them into sequential rank-1 updates (core claimed contribution).  
- Under the covariance-matrix convergence assumption, authors prove the modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) does not materially change algorithm behavior.  
- Main contribution lacks novelty: Krause et al. (2015) already derived rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor; generalizing to rank-μ is a straightforward extension.  
- Theoretical support relies on asymptotic covariance convergence results (Beyer, 2014); Lemma 1 should be strengthened with a proof using the exact algorithmic dynamics.  
- Empirical evaluation is provided but lacks sufficient comparative depth against established CMA-ES variants.  
- Several minor errors need correction: Equation (1) denominator should be σₜ (not σ) — same mistake in Footnote 2 — and Line 94 has a typo ("which can by be motivated" → remove "by").  
- Overall: technically sound but incremental contribution and minor errors reduce the paper’s impact.
