- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: a novel update scheme using a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to approximate standard CMA-ES.  
- Reduces per-iteration complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2), with μ typically O(log d).  
- Maintains and updates the Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix and its square root.  
- Key methodological difference: omits estimating the random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.  
- Provides an asymptotic argument that under the restrictive assumption of covariance convergence, E_t → I and the schemes become equivalent.  
- Empirically validated with runtime and convergence plots on benchmarks, comparing favorably to two state-of-the-art delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Paper is a solid contribution that significantly reduces computational cost without degrading performance on synthetic tests.  
- Major practical limitation: optimization runtime is usually dominated by expensive black-box evaluations, so CMA-ES overhead is often negligible unless evaluations are very fast.  
- CMA-ES is commonly used in low-dimensional problems (tens of dims), where the proposed speedup is less relevant.  
- The targeted high-dimensional, fast-evaluation scenario is plausible but niche, potentially limiting broader impact.  
- Recommended experiment 1: track E_t evolution (spectral/Frobenius norms) by running full CMA-ES alongside the Cholesky approximation.  
- Recommended experiment 2: include baseline runtime data for standard CMA-ES (no delay strategies) to contextualize speedup claims.  
- Recommended experiment 3: perform a spectral sensitivity study on quadratics varying condition number, reporting solution quality and E_t error.  
- Suggests adding a real-world case study (e.g., real-time robotics policy optimization) to demonstrate practical utility beyond synthetic benchmarks.
