This paper introduces a valuable improvement to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) by employing triangular Cholesky factorization. This technique eliminates the square root operation present in the standard CMA-ES, resulting in computational savings, particularly during matrix inversion. The authors provide a theoretical rationale demonstrating that this modification does not compromise the algorithm’s performance.  Experimentally, the proposed method reduces runtime complexity without substantially increasing the number of objective function evaluations. This represents a significant and practical application of Cholesky factorization within the CMA-ES framework. 

To further strengthen the work, the authors could elaborate on the theoretical basis for the observed numerical stability achieved with this approach. While the Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains comparable objective function evaluation counts to the standard CMA-ES, its runtime benefits become more pronounced with increasing search space dimensionality. Given this, the authors might consider investigating a combination of their framework with low-dimensional approximation techniques, such as those proposed by Loschilov [2015]. Such integration could address the challenges posed by high-dimensional problems frequently encountered in applications like neural network training and direct policy search.
