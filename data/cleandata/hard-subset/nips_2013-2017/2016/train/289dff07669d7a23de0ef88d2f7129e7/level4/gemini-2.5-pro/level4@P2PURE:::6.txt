The paper presents a variant of CMA-ES intended to have "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity." The authors claim their modification reduces the computational cost of the algorithm without altering its behavior.

The primary concerns with the manuscript are related to novelty, naming, and the completeness of the evaluation.

1.  **Naming and Relation to Prior Work:** The proposed method is named "Cholesky-CMA-ES," which is misleading as this name was first introduced by T. Suttorp et al. (2009) for a similar and well-known algorithm. The authors fail to acknowledge or discuss this naming conflict, instead presenting their variant as "Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES." This omission is a significant issue.

2.  **Incremental Contribution:** The proposed method appears to be an incremental modification of the work by Krause et al. (2015). Specifically, the sole algorithmic contribution seems to be applying the `rankOneUpdate` operation (also present in Krause et al.'s work) within a loop for all individuals, rather than once as in standard Plus strategies.

3.  **Incomplete Comparisons:** The paper should provide a direct algorithmic comparison to the Cholesky-CMA-ES by Krause et al. and a thorough empirical comparison to the Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. The omission of Suttorp's method from Figure 3 is particularly problematic, as it is a critical baseline. The paper claims Suttorp's method is prone to numerical instabilities, which contradicts the claims made in the original 2009 paper; this discrepancy should be addressed.

4.  **Questionable Claims:**
    *   The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is an overstatement. The method is not optimal for problem classes where a full covariance matrix is unnecessary, such as on the Sphere function (no matrix needed) or on separable functions (a diagonal matrix is sufficient).
    *   The assertion that the algorithm's behavior is unchanged is contradicted by the empirical results. In Figure 1(b), the observed plateau on the Cigar function occurs at different objective function values for CMA-ES-Ref and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES. This difference requires explanation.
    *   The performance improvement over omitted baselines may be modest. The proposed approach is only about two times more efficient than Suttorp's method, and excluding this baseline from the figures may overstate the practical impact of the contribution.

5.  **Impact:** The work does not fundamentally alter the scalability of CMA-ES for large-scale optimization, as the complexity remains quadratic in the problem dimension. However, it is acknowledged that if the goal is to maintain and update a full covariance matrix, the proposed modification might indeed be the most efficient approach currently available.

Overall, the contribution appears to be too incremental, hinging on a single "for" loop. To strengthen the paper, the following modifications are suggested:

a)  Resolve the naming issue and properly contextualize the work with respect to Suttorp et al. (2009).
b)  Perform comprehensive experiments including the variants from Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. in all figures. If the figures become unreadable, present the most critical comparisons in the main text and move the complete set to supplementary material.
c)  Clarify the main algorithmic contribution. If it is the "for" loop in Algorithm 1, this should be explicitly stated and justified. If the contribution is more profound, this needs to be explained more effectively.

Minor note: The statement, "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step," is inaccurate. In most efficient implementations, this decomposition is performed periodically, not in every iteration.
