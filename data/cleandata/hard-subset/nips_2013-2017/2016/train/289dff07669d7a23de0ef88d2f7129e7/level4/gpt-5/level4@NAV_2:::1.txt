CMA-ES is a leading stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It combines multiple learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can equivalently be viewed as adapting an underlying metric. This paper introduces a new covariance-update variant based on triangular Cholesky factors that, compared to prior schemes, substantially lowers both the computational cost of the update and the memory required to maintain the algorithm’s state. The method is thoroughly evaluated empirically. Results show that the new update matches the standard CMA-ES in the number of function evaluations needed to reach a target value, while delivering a marked reduction in wall-clock time, particularly in higher dimensions. I consider this a highly significant contribution to optimization. I anticipate this CMA-ES variant could become the default for medium- to large-scale problems because it offers several clear advantages over the current default: reduced memory footprint, reduced update complexity, and obtaining the covariance eigenvalues at no additional cost. The paper is well written and the evaluation is careful. It is also a strong fit for NIPS given CMA-ES’s close connections to machine learning, information geometry, and its use in reinforcement and supervised learning.
