This paper considers Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) with both rank-1 and rank-$\mu$ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, applied sequentially as a series of rank-1 updates. The authors prove, under convergence assumptions for the covariance matrix, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm’s behavior. The paper also presents experimental results demonstrating algorithm performance.

1. This paper extends the Cholesky CMA-ES by incorporating both rank-1 and rank-$\mu$ updates to the triangular Cholesky factor. However, I do not believe the contribution is substantial, given the prior work of Krause et al. (2015), which already employs rank-1 updates to the triangular Cholesky factor. This paper presents a straightforward generalization of that procedure to rank-$\mu$ updates, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. 

2. Generally, the convergence of the covariance matrix $C_t$ in CMA-ES is supported by theoretical analysis (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although this analysis isn't based on the exact formulae used in CMA-ES. Referencing this work could strengthen the justification for Lemma 1.
