- Main contribution: replace the standard matrix square root in CMA-ES with a Cholesky factorization.  
- This change reduces the computational cost of matrix inversion and cuts wall-clock time, with larger gains at higher dimensionalities.  
- The authors provide theoretical justification that the modification does not degrade performance and requires a comparable number of objective evaluations to standard CMA-ES.  
- Reviewer views the work as a valuable practical improvement to CMA-ES implementations.  
- Question 1: Please provide a more detailed theoretical explanation for the claim of "numerically more stable" computation.  
- Question 2: Have the authors considered combining their method with low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to further improve scalability for high-dimensional problems like neural-network policy search?
