- CMA-ES is a leading derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate Gaussian and effectively learns a problem metric.  
- This paper proposes a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based update substantially reduces update computational cost and overall memory footprint.  
- Benchmarks show comparable convergence in function evaluations to standard CMA-ES while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The method could become the standard for medium-to-large-scale CMA-ES due to lower complexity, smaller memory needs, and free eigenvalue access.  
- The manuscript is well-written, rigorously validated, and highly relevant to the NeurIPS community and ML applications.  
- Minor request: clarify implementation details for runtime comparisons (e.g., were all algorithms implemented in the same language?).  
- Minor request: explain why the dimensionality trend in Figure 2 breaks for the Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256.
