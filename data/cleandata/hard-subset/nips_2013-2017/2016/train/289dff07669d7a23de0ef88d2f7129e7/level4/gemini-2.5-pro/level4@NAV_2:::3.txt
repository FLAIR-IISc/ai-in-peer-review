This paper presents a variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), termed Cholesky-CMA-ES, which aims to reduce the computational complexity of the standard algorithm. The core idea is to maintain and directly update a triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix, rather than storing the full matrix and re-decomposing it in each iteration. This approach reduces the time complexity for the covariance matrix update from cubic to quadratic, O(μd²), and also reduces the storage requirements to O(d²). The main technical challenge addressed is the modification of the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism, which normally requires the inverse of the matrix square root. The authors propose replacing this with the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor and provide a theoretical justification (Lemma 1) arguing that as the covariance matrix's shape converges, the error introduced by this approximation vanishes. The paper's contribution is validated through empirical comparisons on several benchmark functions, showing that the proposed method significantly reduces wall-clock time without increasing the number of function evaluations compared to the standard CMA-ES.

**Strengths:**

*   **Significance and Practical Impact:** The paper addresses a well-known computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, a widely used and powerful optimization algorithm. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a significant practical speed-up, especially for high-dimensional problems where the covariance matrix update dominates runtime. This makes the work highly relevant to practitioners using CMA-ES for tasks like policy search or hyperparameter tuning.
*   **Technical Quality:** The proposed algorithm is technically sound. The use of rank-one updates for the Cholesky factor is well-established, and its application here is implemented correctly. The empirical evaluation is thorough, comparing against appropriate baselines (including a delayed-update strategy) across multiple benchmark functions and dimensions, clearly demonstrating the claimed performance benefits.
*   **Clarity:** The paper is well-written and organized. It provides the necessary background on CMA-ES, clearly motivates the problem, and explains the proposed modification and its theoretical justification in a comprehensible manner. Algorithm 1 and 2 provide a clear specification of the method.

**Weaknesses and Areas for Improvement:**

*   **Originality:** The primary contribution is the extension of a direct triangular Cholesky factor update to incorporate the rank-μ update of the standard CMA-ES. This builds directly upon prior work, such as Krause and Igel (2015), which demonstrated a similar update for the rank-1 case. Since the rank-μ update is implemented as a sequence of μ rank-1 updates, the novelty of this step could be considered incremental. The paper would be stronger if it more clearly articulated the specific technical challenges involved in this generalization that were not present in the simpler rank-1 setting.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The theoretical argument in Lemma 1 relies on the assumption that the normalized covariance matrix C̄t converges. While this is a reasonable and intuitive assumption for many problems, the authors could strengthen this claim by connecting it more formally to existing convergence analysis of CMA-ES. For instance, citing work like Beyer (2014), which analyzes the convergence of the expected covariance matrix in simplified settings, would provide stronger support for the assumption and thus increase confidence in the theoretical result.
