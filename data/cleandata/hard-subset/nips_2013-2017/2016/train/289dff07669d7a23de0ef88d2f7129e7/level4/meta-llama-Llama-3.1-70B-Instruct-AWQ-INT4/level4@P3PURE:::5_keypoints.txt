- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update scheme that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance/square-root.
- Reduces complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) (μ typically O(log d)).
- Key difference from original CMA-ES: omits estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et at each step.
- Provides asymptotic argument that Et → I under restrictive convergence assumptions, making the schemes equivalent.
- Empirical validation via standard runtime and convergence plots on selected benchmark functions.
- Compared against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.
- Shows substantial speed-up without losing optimization performance on synthetic tests.
- Practical limitation: for many real problems the black-box evaluation dominates runtime and CMA-ES cost is negligible.
- Targeted high-dimension/fast-evaluation scenario may be uncommon in typical CMA-ES applications.
- Recommends visualizing the evolution of Et (e.g., norms) and comparing approximated Et to full CMA-ES trajectories.
- Suggests reporting actual runtimes including non-delayed CMA-ES baseline for completeness.
- Advises additional simulations on quadratic functions with continuously varied spectra (e.g., Stich & Müller, PPSN 2012) to study solution quality and Et error.
- Recommends adding a real-world experiment (e.g., real-time robotics/policy gradients) where speed matters and parameter spaces are moderate.
