## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission: [Paper Title]

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, replacing the standard eigen-decomposition with a Cholesky decomposition to reduce computational time and storage requirements. The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the proposed method, which appears reasonable given the stated goal. The experimental results, particularly the reported speedup rates, are encouraging and suggest the potential for this approach to become a practical alternative to standard CMA-ES.

However, despite the potential practical benefits, I have concerns regarding the originality and completeness of this work that currently prevent me from recommending acceptance to NIPS. The core idea of utilizing a Cholesky decomposition for covariance matrix updates feels remarkably similar to the work presented in “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies.” While I may be mistaken, the presented methods appear largely identical, and the contribution feels incremental rather than significantly novel. The authors should clearly delineate the differences and advantages of their approach relative to this prior work.

Furthermore, several crucial details are missing or inadequately addressed. Firstly, while space constraints are understandable, demonstrating the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2) from the original CMA-ES framework is essential for establishing its correctness and providing readers with complete confidence in the method. A brief derivation, or at least a clear reference demonstrating this equivalence, should be included. 

Secondly, the paper claims a computational complexity of O(μd<sup>2</sup>), which is the same as CMA-ES-Ref. This assertion requires significant clarification. Simply stating the same asymptotic complexity while claiming a speedup is insufficient. A detailed discussion, potentially utilizing Θ-notation to more accurately represent the constants hidden within the O-notation, is necessary to explain *why* the proposed method demonstrably performs faster in practice. Analyzing the constant factors, or providing a more fine-grained complexity analysis of each step, would be valuable. 

Finally, the paper suggests the error in the path accumulates less rapidly than predicted by Hansen’s analysis. Given that the theoretical analysis presented is not deterministic, this claim necessitates empirical validation beyond simply comparing final performance. Experiments specifically designed to track the error evolution over time are crucial to support this assertion. Reporting path length error curves, or similar metrics, would provide convincing evidence. 

Several minor points also require attention. There are inconsistencies in notation that should be corrected. Specifically, *w<sub>k</sub>* in Algorithm 2 should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, and *n* in Table 1 should be replaced with *d*. Additionally, a typographical error exists on line 185, where ‘F’ should be ‘f’.

In summary, while the proposed method holds promise for improving the efficiency of CMA-ES, the limited originality and missing details currently disqualify this submission for NIPS. Addressing the concerns regarding the relationship to prior work, providing a thorough complexity analysis, and validating the claims about path error accumulation are essential for strengthening the paper and demonstrating its suitability for publication. As it stands, I recommend rejection.
