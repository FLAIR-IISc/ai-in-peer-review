- Proposes a Cholesky CMA-ES variant that implements rank-μ updates via sequential rank-1 updates to the triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Claims the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially change algorithm behavior under the convergence assumption for the covariance matrix.  
- Main novelty concern: the rank-μ extension appears to be a straightforward generalization of Krause et al. (2015) rather than a significant theoretical or methodological advance.  
- The paper does not convincingly demonstrate meaningful performance gains or address limitations unmet by prior CMA-ES variants.  
- Theoretical weakness: Lemma 1 relies on covariance convergence without rigorous justification using the exact CMA-ES update rules; should reference or adapt Beyer (2014) to strengthen the argument.  
- Experimental evaluation is insufficient: limited baseline comparisons and no clear quantification of how rank-μ affects convergence speed or solution quality versus rank-1 or standard CMA-ES.  
- Minor but important technical errors: Equation (1) and footnote use σ instead of σ_t, and a redundant word ("by") appears in line 94.  
- Overall assessment: contribution is incremental and under-supported theoretically and empirically, so not suitable for NIPS; better for a specialized optimization venue.  
- Revision suggestions: either justify why the rank-μ extension is non-trivial and superior, or reframe the paper as a technical implementation note.
