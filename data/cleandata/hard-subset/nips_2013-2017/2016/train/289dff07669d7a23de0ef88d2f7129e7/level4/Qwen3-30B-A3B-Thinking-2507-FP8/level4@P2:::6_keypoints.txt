- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES variant that maintains triangular Cholesky factors to reduce runtime and memory while claiming same evaluation behavior as standard CMA-ES.  
- The method builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) but fails to acknowledge or distinguish itself from that prior art.  
- Naming the method "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is problematic because Suttorp et al. (2009) introduced that term for a similar approach.  
- Main technical novelty is extending a rank-one Cholesky update loop to all μ individuals, but this is a limited incremental change over existing mechanisms.  
- Claims of "optimal" complexity are misleading: the approach achieves O(μd²), optimal only for maintaining a full covariance, not universally (e.g., separable problems can be O(d)).  
- Paper lacks nuanced discussion of when the proposed approach is beneficial versus diagonal or low-rank alternatives.  
- Experimental comparison is incomplete and omits direct comparison to Suttorp-CMA-ES and Krause’s method, undermining claims of superiority and stability.  
- Excluding Suttorp-CMA-ES from key figures (e.g., Fig. 3) is especially misleading.  
- Contains a technical inaccuracy: standard CMA-ES does not perform eigenvalue decomposition every update but periodically (e.g., every ~110d(c1+cm) iterations).  
- Contribution is incremental and does not resolve CMA-ES scalability limits for very high dimensions (quadratic complexity remains).  
- Strengths: sound theoretical justification, clear algorithm description, empirical runtime reduction without extra function evaluations, Shark implementation, and error analysis of the Cholesky approximation.  
- Reviewer recommends major revisions: properly acknowledge Suttorp et al., expand experiments to include prior Cholesky methods, temper "optimal" complexity claims, correct the matrix square-root frequency statement, and clarify why the μ-loop extension is non-trivial.
