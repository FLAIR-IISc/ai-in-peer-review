- CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (an underlying metric).  
- The paper introduces a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The proposed method substantially reduces computational complexity of the update.  
- The method significantly lowers memory required to store algorithm state parameters.  
- Numerical evaluation shows comparable performance in function evaluations while greatly reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The variant could become the default for medium- to large-scale problems and automatically computes covariance eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and highly relevant to ML, information geometry, RL, and supervised learning.  
- Minor: clarify implementation comparability for CPU performance (programming language, etc.).  
- Minor: explain why Figure 2’s claim about dimensionality effects doesn’t hold for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
