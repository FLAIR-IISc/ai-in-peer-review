- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES using triangular Cholesky factor updates for rank-1 and rank-μ covariance adaptation.
- Theoretically justifies replacing the matrix square root with the triangular Cholesky factor in CSA, showing introduced error vanishes under covariance convergence.
- Generalizes prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) to the standard rank-μ CMA-ES via sequential rank-1 updates.
- Achieves optimal asymptotic time complexity O(µd²) for covariance updates and optimal storage, improving scalability for high-dimensional problems.
- Experimental results show equal numbers of objective evaluations to standard CMA-ES but substantially reduced wall-clock time, especially in higher dimensions.
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides detailed algorithms and sufficient experimental validation.
- Main contribution is a straightforward extension of prior work, limiting the novelty of the paper.
- Lemma 1’s theoretical claim relies on covariance convergence, but the paper does not adequately address how this assumption affects practical performance.
- Could better integrate established CMA-ES convergence analyses (e.g., Beyer 2014) to strengthen the theoretical foundation.
- Contains minor technical errors to fix: σ_t missing in Equation (1) and a footnote, and a grammatical error (“can by be” → “can be”).
- Pro-acceptance reasons: practical computational improvement, sound theoretical justification, and clear experimental evidence.
- Con-acceptance reasons: limited novelty relative to existing Cholesky methods, incomplete theoretical connection to convergence results, and required technical corrections.
- Overall: technically sound and reproducible, but incremental in originality and moderate in significance given prior work.
