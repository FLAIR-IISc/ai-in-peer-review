- The name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is misleading and previously used by T. Suttorp et al. (2009); the paper fails to acknowledge this prior work.  
- The algorithmic contribution is incremental relative to Krause et al. (2015); the main change is calling rankOneUpdate in a loop over individuals.  
- Empirical plots show discrepancies (e.g., different plateau levels on the Cigar function) that may result from how medians are computed/visualized.  
- The paper should include empirical comparisons to Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmic comparisons to Krause et al.’s method.  
- The “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” claim is misleading because many problems (Sphere, separable functions) do not require a full covariance matrix.  
- The proposed method is only about twice as cheap as Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES, so gains are limited.  
- The paper’s claim that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable contradicts the original work and is not substantiated here.  
- Omitting Suttorp’s variant from key figures (e.g., Fig. 3) can mislead readers about the novelty and performance gains.  
- The complexity remains quadratic, so the approach does not materially advance large-scale CMA-ES optimization.  
- If one requires a full covariance matrix, the modification may be among the best available time/memory savings, but gains are modest.  
- Overall contribution is too incremental (essentially adding a single "for" loop); authors should clarify or justify this characterization.  
- Recommendations: resolve the naming conflict; run/add comparisons to Suttorp and Krause variants and update figures; explicitly explain the main change as the added loop or justify otherwise.  
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically in implementations, not at every update.
