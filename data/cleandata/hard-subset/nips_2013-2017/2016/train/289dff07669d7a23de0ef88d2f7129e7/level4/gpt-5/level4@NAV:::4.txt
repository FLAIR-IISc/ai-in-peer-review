The primary contribution of this work is the use of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the matrix square root in standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing the computational cost associated with computing matrix inverses. The author presents an approach with theoretical justification showing that it does not degrade the algorithm’s performance. The proposed method lowers the runtime complexity of the algorithm without significantly changing the number of objective function evaluations. This paper offers a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES. Could the author further clarify, from a theoretical perspective, why the computation is “numerically more stable”? It appears that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES, while decreasing wall-clock time, with the benefit growing with search-space dimensionality. Could the author consider integrating the low-dimensional approximation [Loshchilov, 2015] into the current framework, given that in many practical settings, such as training neural networks in direct policy search, high dimensionality is a critical concern?
