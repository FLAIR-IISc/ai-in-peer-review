### **Summary**

This paper proposes an extension to the Cholesky Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES). Specifically, it introduces a method for updating the triangular Cholesky factor that incorporates both the rank-1 and rank-µ updates, where the latter is implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors provide a theoretical analysis, proving that under the assumption of covariance matrix convergence, a modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) rule does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper concludes with experimental results to evaluate the performance of the proposed algorithm.

### **Major Comments**

1.  **Limited Novelty and Contribution:** My primary concern is the incremental nature of the contribution. The core idea is to extend the update mechanism for the triangular Cholesky factor to include the rank-µ update. However, this appears to be a direct generalization of the rank-1 update for the Cholesky factor proposed by Krause et al. (2015). The implementation as a sequence of rank-1 updates feels like a straightforward extension of that existing work. The paper would be significantly strengthened if the authors could better articulate the technical challenges that were overcome and explain why this generalization is non-trivial. What non-obvious insights or difficulties arise when moving from a single rank-1 update to the sequential rank-µ update framework?

2.  **Foundation of Theoretical Analysis:** The theoretical analysis presented in Lemma 1 is contingent on the assumption that the covariance matrix C_t converges. While this is a reasonable assumption in practice, the argument could be made more robust by contextualizing it within existing literature. For example, the work of Beyer (ECJ, 2014) provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES, even if not for the exact formulation used here. Citing and briefly discussing such prior work would provide stronger justification for the assumption and increase the reader's confidence in the theoretical claims.

### **Minor Comments and Typos**

The paper is generally well-written, but I have identified a few minor issues that should be addressed for clarity and correctness:

*   **Equation (1):** The step-size σ in the denominator should be written as σ_t to maintain consistency with the time-dependent notation used elsewhere.
*   **Footnote 2 (Page 3):** A similar correction is needed here; σ should be σ_t.
*   **Line 94:** The sentence reads, "which can by be motivated by principles from...". The word "by" is redundant and should be deleted.
