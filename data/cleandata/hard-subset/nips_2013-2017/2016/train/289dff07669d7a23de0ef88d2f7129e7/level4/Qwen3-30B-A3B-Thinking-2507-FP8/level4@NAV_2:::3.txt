

This paper proposes a Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm that employs triangular Cholesky factor updates for both rank-1 and rank-μ covariance matrix adaptation. The authors provide theoretical justification for their approach, demonstrating that replacing the matrix square root with the triangular Cholesky factor in the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior under the convergence assumption of the covariance matrix. The paper includes comprehensive experimental results comparing the proposed method with standard CMA-ES variants on various benchmark functions.

The work extends previous research on Cholesky-based CMA-ES methods, particularly building upon Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015), who focused on rank-1 updates for specialized CMA-ES variants. The authors generalize this approach to the standard CMA-ES with rank-μ updates, which is achieved through a sequence of rank-1 updates to the triangular Cholesky factor.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a theoretically sound justification for the Cholesky-based CSA modification, demonstrating that the introduced error vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- The proposed algorithm achieves optimal asymptotic time complexity O(µd²) for covariance updates and optimal storage complexity, representing a significant practical improvement for high-dimensional optimization problems.
- The experimental results clearly demonstrate that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES while substantially reducing wall-clock time, particularly for higher dimensions.
- The paper is well-structured, clearly written, and provides detailed algorithms with sufficient experimental validation.

Weaknesses:
- The main contribution constitutes a straightforward extension of Krause and Igel (2015), who already established the feasibility of Cholesky-based updates for the (1+1)-CMA-ES. The extension to rank-μ updates is not particularly novel, as it can be achieved by sequentially applying the rank-1 update procedure.
- The theoretical foundation in Lemma 1 relies on the convergence of the covariance matrix Ct, but the paper does not adequately address how this convergence assumption impacts practical performance. While Beyer (2014) provides theoretical convergence analysis for CMA-ES, the paper could better integrate this to strengthen its theoretical foundation.
- The paper contains several minor technical errors that require correction: Equation (1) should have σ_t in the denominator (not σ), the same error appears in the footnote on page 3, and line 94 contains a grammatical error ("can by be" should be "can be").

Pro arguments for acceptance:
- The algorithm offers a practical improvement to CMA-ES with optimal time and space complexity, which is valuable for high-dimensional optimization problems.
- The theoretical justification is sound and provides a solid basis for the approach.
- The experimental results clearly demonstrate the computational benefits of the proposed method.

Con arguments against acceptance:
- The contribution lacks sufficient novelty compared to existing Cholesky-based CMA-ES methods.
- The theoretical foundation could be strengthened by more explicitly connecting to established convergence analysis of CMA-ES.
- The technical errors identified require correction before the paper can be considered for publication.

Quality: The paper is technically sound with well-supported claims through theoretical analysis and experiments, though the novelty of the approach is limited.

Clarity: The paper is clearly written, well-organized, and provides sufficient detail for experts to reproduce the results.

Originality: The main contribution is a straightforward generalization of prior work, which limits its novelty relative to existing literature.

Significance: The algorithm provides practical computational benefits for high-dimensional optimization, but the significance is somewhat diminished by the incremental nature of the contribution.

While the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm offers a useful practical improvement to the standard CMA-ES, the novelty of the approach is limited as it represents a straightforward extension of prior work. The paper would benefit from a more thorough discussion of how this approach differs from and improves upon existing Cholesky-based CMA-ES methods, particularly Krause and Igel (2015). The minor technical errors should also be corrected before publication.
