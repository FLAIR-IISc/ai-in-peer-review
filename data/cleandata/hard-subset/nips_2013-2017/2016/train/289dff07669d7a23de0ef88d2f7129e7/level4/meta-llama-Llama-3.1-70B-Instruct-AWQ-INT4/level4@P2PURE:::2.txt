This paper proposes an alternative to the eigen-decomposition in CMA-ES by employing the Cholesky decomposition, thereby reducing time and storage complexity. The paper is generally well-written, and the proposed method appears reasonable. The experimental results, particularly the speedup rates, are promising, and the method may potentially become a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future.

However, the originality of this work is limited, as it seems to be a straightforward extension of the previous work "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The methods presented are essentially identical, and I would appreciate clarification if this assessment is incorrect.

Furthermore, some crucial contents are noticeably absent from the paper. Firstly, it is essential to demonstrate the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2), despite the limited space and the fact that it has been proven in previous work. Secondly, the authors claim that the computational complexity is O(\mu d^2), which is identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A detailed discussion is necessary to explain why the proposed algorithm is faster, possibly using the '\Theta' notation instead of 'O'. Thirdly, the authors assert that the error in the path is small and decreases over time, contradicting Hansen's claim. Since the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to validate this claim, and comparing only the final performance is insufficient.

Additionally, some minor corrections are needed: the notation 'wk' in Algorithm 2 should be '\alphak', and 'n' in Table 1 should be 'd'. Moreover, 'F' should be 'f' in line 185. In its current form, I do not think this paper meets the qualifications for NIPS.
