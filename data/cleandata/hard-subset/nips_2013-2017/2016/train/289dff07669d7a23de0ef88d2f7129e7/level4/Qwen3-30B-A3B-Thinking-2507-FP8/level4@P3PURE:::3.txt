

This paper investigates the Cholesky CMA-ES, extending the update for the triangular Cholesky factor to incorporate both rank-1 and rank-μ updates through sequential rank-1 updates. Under the convergence assumption of the covariance matrix, the authors prove that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper includes empirical performance evaluations.  

1.  The paper considers the Cholesky CMA-ES and extends the triangular Cholesky factor update to include rank-μ updates via sequential rank-1 updates. However, the contribution appears limited, as Krause et al. (2015) already established the rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. This work presents a straightforward generalization of that procedure to rank-μ updates.  
2.  The convergence of the covariance matrix $C_t$ in CMA-ES is theoretically established in Beyer (ECJ, 2014), though the analysis does not rely on the exact update equations of CMA-ES. Addressing this gap could strengthen the confidence in Lemma 1.
