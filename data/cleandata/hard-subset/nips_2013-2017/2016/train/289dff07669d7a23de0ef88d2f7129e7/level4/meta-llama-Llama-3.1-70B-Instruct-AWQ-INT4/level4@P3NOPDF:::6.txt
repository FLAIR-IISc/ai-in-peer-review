The paper proposes a modified version of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, which claims to achieve "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity". However, this name is misleading, as it was first introduced by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009 for a similar algorithm. The authors fail to acknowledge this existing work, instead presenting their variant as a novel contribution.

The proposed method is an incremental modification to the work of Krause et al. (2015). Upon closer inspection, the only algorithmic contribution appears to be the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function, which is already present in the work of Krause et al. This minor modification is achieved by calling the function in a loop for all individuals, rather than just once as in Plus strategies.

Empirical results are presented in Figure b), which shows a difference in the observed plateau for the Cigar function between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES. However, this discrepancy may be attributed to the way the authors plot median results. A more comprehensive evaluation is necessary to validate the proposed approach.

Notably, the paper lacks a comparison to the original Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp (2009) and the variant by Krause et al. (2015). Such a comparison would provide valuable insights into the actual contribution of the proposed method. Furthermore, the claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is somewhat misleading, as it is not optimal for all scenarios, such as the Sphere function, where the covariance matrix is not required, or separable functions, where a diagonal matrix suffices.

Moreover, the proposed approach is only twice as efficient as the omitted Cholesky CMA-ES by Suttorp, which is claimed to be prone to numerical instabilities, contrary to the original paper's findings. The omission of this comparison in Figure 3 may mislead the reader regarding the actual contribution of the paper.

In the context of large-scale optimization, the proposed modification does not significantly impact the complexity of CMA-ES, which remains quadratic. However, it may be a useful optimization when considering the full covariance matrix.

Overall, while the work is not without merit, it appears to be too incremental, with the main algorithmic contribution being a single "for" loop. To strengthen the paper, the authors should consider the following modifications:

1. Resolve the naming issue by acknowledging the existing work of Suttorp et al. and distinguishing their contribution.
2. Perform experiments with variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. and update all figures to provide a comprehensive comparison. If the figures become unreadable, consider presenting only the best-performing variant in the main text and including the full figures in the supplementary material.
3. Provide a clearer explanation of the main algorithmic contribution and its significance, or address the criticism that it is a minor modification.

Minor notes:

* The statement "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is inaccurate, as this is typically done periodically in proper implementations.
