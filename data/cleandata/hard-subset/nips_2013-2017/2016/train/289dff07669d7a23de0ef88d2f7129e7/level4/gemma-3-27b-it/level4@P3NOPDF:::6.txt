## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission: Cholesky-CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity

This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) termed “Cholesky-CMA-ES” with the goal of reducing computational cost and memory usage during covariance matrix updates. While the authors demonstrate some empirical gains, particularly on the Cigar function, the paper suffers from issues in naming, presentation, and justification of its contribution.

The core algorithmic contribution – iterating the rank-one update procedure for all individuals – builds directly upon the work of Krause et al. (2015) and is, in essence, a looped application of a previously established technique. The authors position this as an improvement over the “Plus” strategy, but the fundamental operation remains the same.  The claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is misleading. While the proposed method offers improvements over some existing approaches, the notion of “optimality” is questionable; for problems where the covariance structure is unimportant (e.g., Sphere functions, or separable functions where a diagonal covariance matrix suffices), the full covariance update is unnecessary overhead.  Furthermore, the claimed two-fold speedup compared to Suttorp et al. (2009) is marginal, especially given the existing literature.

A significant concern is the reuse of the name “Cholesky-CMA-ES”. This name was previously introduced by Suttorp et al. (2009) to describe a related, albeit different, algorithm. The authors acknowledge this prior work only in passing (“Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES”), without adequately addressing the potential for confusion or outlining the distinct characteristics of their approach.  The omission of the original Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. (2009) from the comparative analysis (particularly in Figure 3) further exacerbates this issue and misrepresents the novelty of the contribution. The authors also claim the Suttorp version is prone to numerical instabilities, which contradicts the original publication.

Empirically, the results presented are not entirely convincing. The difference observed in Figure 1(b) on the Cigar function – a differing plateau value between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES – raises concerns about the plotting of median results or a potential subtle implementation detail affecting the observed behavior. 

Regarding scalability, the paper does not convincingly demonstrate that this approach addresses the quadratic complexity inherent in full covariance matrix updates.  While the authors admit the improvement is most relevant when a full covariance matrix is explicitly maintained, the algorithmic complexity remains a barrier for very high-dimensional problems.  I acknowledge that the proposed modification may represent a practical improvement for situations where the full covariance matrix is already being used.

Overall, the work feels incremental. The algorithmic contribution boils down to a single “for” loop.  To strengthen this paper, I recommend the following revisions:

a) **Resolve the naming conflict:**  The authors should choose a distinct name for their algorithm to avoid confusion with the existing Cholesky-CMA-ES.

b) **Comprehensive Comparison:**  The paper *must* include a thorough empirical comparison with both Cholesky-CMA-ES (Suttorp et al., 2009) and the method proposed by Krause et al. (2015).  All three figures (1, 3, and potentially others) should be updated to reflect these comparisons. If including all lines in the figures makes them unreadable, prioritize clarity by presenting only the best-performing variant in the main text, and providing the full comparison in Supplementary Material.

c) **Clarify Algorithmic Contribution:** The authors need to more clearly articulate the significance of the repeated rank-one update.  Either explicitly state why this looping is non-trivial or provide a justification as to why it might be perceived as a simple implementation detail and not a substantive algorithmic contribution. Algorithm 1 should be highlighted as the core modification.

Minor comments: The statement “Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step” is inaccurate. In well-optimized CMA-ES implementations, eigenvalue decomposition is performed periodically, not at every iteration.
