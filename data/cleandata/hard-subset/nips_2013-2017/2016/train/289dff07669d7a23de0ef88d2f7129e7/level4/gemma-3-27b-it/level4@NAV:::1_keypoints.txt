- CMA-ES is state-of-the-art in stochastic, derivative-free optimization, adapting a multivariate normal sampling distribution (the metric) via adaptive learning mechanisms.  
- The paper introduces a novel covariance matrix update using triangular Cholesky factors, reducing computational complexity and memory needs versus existing methods.  
- Numerical evaluation shows the new update preserves CMA-ES performance in function evaluation counts while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The method is a significant advancement with potential to become the default for medium- to large-scale problems.  
- Key advantages: lower memory consumption, reduced computational cost, and direct availability of covariance matrix eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written and empirically thorough.  
- The work is appropriate for NIPS given connections to machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement and supervised learning.  
- Minor request: clarify measures taken to ensure comparability across implementations/CPU characteristics (e.g., implementation language).  
- Minor request: explain why Figure 2’s claim about larger differences at higher dimensionality does not hold for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
