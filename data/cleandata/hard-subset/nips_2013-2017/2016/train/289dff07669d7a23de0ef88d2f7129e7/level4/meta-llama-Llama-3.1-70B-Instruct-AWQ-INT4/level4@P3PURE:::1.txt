This paper presents a significant contribution to the domain of optimization by introducing a new variant of the CMA-ES algorithm, a state-of-the-art stochastic optimization method. The proposed variant updates the covariance matrix using triangular Cholesky factors, which offers several advantages over previous methods, including reduced computational complexity and memory requirements. This new approach can be seen as an adaptation of the underlying metric, achieved through the combination of different learning mechanisms for the multivariate normal sampling distribution.

The authors provide a thorough numerical evaluation of the new method, demonstrating its effectiveness in maintaining the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations required to reach a given target. Moreover, the new update significantly reduces the wall-clock time of the algorithm, particularly in high-dimensional spaces. This improvement makes the proposed variant an attractive choice for medium- to large-scale optimization problems.

The paper is well-written, and the approach is carefully evaluated. The connection between CMA-ES and machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in reinforcement learning and supervised learning make this contribution a valuable addition to the NIPS conference.

However, there are a few minor points that require clarification. Firstly, it would be beneficial to explain how the different implementations used in the evaluation were ensured to be comparable in terms of CPU performance. Specifically, it would be helpful to know whether the same implementation language was used and, if so, which one.

Additionally, in the discussion of Figure 2, the authors state that "the higher the dimensionality, the more pronounced the differences." However, this trend does not appear to hold for the Cigar and Discus functions in 256 dimensions. A brief explanation for this discrepancy would be appreciated.

Overall, this paper presents a significant improvement to the CMA-ES algorithm, and its publication in NIPS is well-deserved. The proposed variant has the potential to become the default method for medium- to large-scale optimization problems due to its clear advantages, including reduced memory requirements, computational complexity, and the added benefit of obtaining the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix for free.
