- Main contribution: apply triangular Cholesky factorization to CMA-ES to avoid the matrix square-root operation.  
- Eliminates square-root in matrix inversion, yielding computational savings, especially in high dimensions.  
- Authors provide theoretical justification that the factorization does not degrade algorithmic performance.  
- Method reduces runtime complexity while keeping the number of objective function evaluations essentially unchanged.  
- Demonstrates increasing wall-clock time reduction as search dimensionality grows.  
- Paper is a valuable practical implementation of Cholesky factorization within the CMA-ES framework.  
- Suggestion: provide a more detailed theoretical explanation for the claimed “numerically more stable” computation.  
- Suggestion: consider integrating low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loschilov 2015) for very high-dimensional applications like direct policy search and neural network training.
