- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (equivalently, an underlying metric).  
- The paper proposes a new covariance-matrix update based on triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The new update significantly reduces computational complexity compared to previous updates.  
- The new update also substantially reduces memory required to store the algorithm’s state.  
- The method is evaluated through comprehensive numerical experiments.  
- Results show no degradation in the number of function evaluations needed to reach targets versus standard CMA-ES.  
- Results show substantial reductions in wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- The contribution is significant and likely to become the default for medium- to large-scale problems.  
- Key advantages: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and free access to covariance eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well written and the approach is carefully evaluated.  
- The work is well suited to NIPS due to connections with machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.
