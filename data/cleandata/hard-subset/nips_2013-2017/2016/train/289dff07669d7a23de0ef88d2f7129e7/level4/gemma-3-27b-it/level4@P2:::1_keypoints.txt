- Paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: updates covariance via triangular Cholesky factors, reducing compute and memory.  
- Method preserves standard CMA-ES performance (similar function evaluations) while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in higher dimensions.  
- The approach is theoretically justified; the Cholesky-factor approximation does not significantly degrade performance.  
- Extensive experiments convincingly demonstrate runtime speedups as dimensionality increases.  
- Practical benefits include lower memory footprint, reduced computational cost, and easy access to covariance eigenvalues.  
- Paper is well-written, clearly presented, and highly relevant to NIPS applications (RL, hyperparameter optimization, info-geometry).  
- Implementation details are under-discussed; comparability across implementations (language, compiler, optimizations) should be addressed.  
- Some benchmark results contradict the claim of increasing improvements with dimensionality (e.g., Cigar and Discus at d=256) and need explanation.  
- Quadratic scaling in dimensionality remains a fundamental limitation for very high-dimensional problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: significant practical improvement, sound theory, compelling empirical evidence, and addresses a key CMA-ES bottleneck.  
- Arguments against acceptance: improvements may be incremental for certain problem types and quadratic scaling persists.  
- Overall recommendation: strong accept — technically sound, well-evaluated, and likely to become a standard variant for medium-to-large-scale problems.
