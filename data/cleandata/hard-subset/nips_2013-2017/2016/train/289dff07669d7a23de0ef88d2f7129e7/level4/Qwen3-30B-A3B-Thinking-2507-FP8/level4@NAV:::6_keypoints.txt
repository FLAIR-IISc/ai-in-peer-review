- Introduces a CMA-ES variant called "Cholesky-CMA-ES" claiming optimal covariance update and storage complexity.
- Name conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009), who already used "Cholesky-CMA-ES" for a different, well-known algorithm; authors fail to acknowledge this.
- Method is a minor incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015), not a substantial new algorithmic advance.
- The sole algorithmic novelty is replacing one rankOneUpdate call with a loop over individuals in Algorithm 1.
- Empirical discrepancy: Figure b) shows different plateau values for Cigar likely due to median plotting, requiring clarification.
- Paper should include empirical comparisons against Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmic comparisons against Krause’s variant.
- Claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading—does not hold for separable problems where diagonal covariance suffices (e.g., Sphere).
- The proposed method is only about twice as efficient as Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES, contrary to the paper’s implication and Suttorp’s claims of numerical stability.
- Omitting Suttorp’s variant from Figure 3 misrepresents the contribution and completeness of evaluation.
- Approach does not advance large-scale CMA-ES meaningfully because computational complexity remains quadratic in dimension.
- While there may be practical efficiency gains when full covariance is required, the algorithmic contribution is minimal.
- Overall assessment: the work is overly incremental and needs stronger justification or broader empirical validation.
- Recommendations: (a) resolve the naming/citation conflict; (b) add empirical comparisons to Suttorp and Krause variants and revise all figures (full results in supplement if needed); (c) explicitly state that the core contribution is the loop in Algorithm 1 or justify its non-triviality.
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically performed periodically, not at every update step, contrary to the paper’s statement.
