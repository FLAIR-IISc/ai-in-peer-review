CMA-ES represents the current state-of-the-art in stochastic, derivative-free optimization, achieving this through adaptive learning mechanisms that adjust the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution – effectively adapting the underlying metric. This paper introduces a novel covariance matrix update strategy, utilizing triangular Cholesky factors, which demonstrably reduces computational complexity and memory requirements compared to existing methods. The proposed approach is rigorously evaluated numerically, demonstrating that this new update maintains the performance of standard CMA-ES, measured by function evaluation count, while substantially decreasing wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional problems. 

This work constitutes a significant advancement in the field of optimization. The presented variant of CMA-ES has the potential to become the preferred default for medium- to large-scale problems due to its clear advantages: lower memory consumption, reduced computational cost, and the readily available covariance matrix eigenvalues. The paper is well-written and the evaluation is thorough. Its inclusion in NIPS is particularly appropriate given the strong ties between CMA-ES and areas like machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in reinforcement and supervised learning.
