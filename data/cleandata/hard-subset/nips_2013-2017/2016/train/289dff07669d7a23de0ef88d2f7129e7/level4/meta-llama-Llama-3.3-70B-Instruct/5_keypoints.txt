- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of full covariance to reduce complexity to O(μ d^2) from O(d^3) (μ ~ O(log d)).  
- Core idea: avoid estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et by maintaining the Cholesky factor directly.  
- Theoretical claim: under restrictive convergence assumptions, Et asymptotically tends to identity, making the new scheme equivalent to original CMA-ES.  
- Empirical validation: runtime and convergence plots on selected objectives show the method is valid and efficient.  
- Comparison: evaluated against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.  
- Main contribution: substantial acceleration of CMA-ES without degrading optimization performance on synthetic test cases.  
- Practical limitation: in many real applications the black-box evaluation dominates runtime, so CMA-ES speedups may have little overall impact.  
- Practical limitation: typical problem dimensions are often only a few tens, reducing the scenarios where CMA-ES runtime is critical.  
- Recommendation: visualize evolution of Et (e.g., norms) by running full CMA-ES and the approximation in parallel to assess Et fluctuations.  
- Recommendation: report actual wall-clock runtimes and performance of CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.  
- Recommendation: run simulation study on quadratic functions with varied spectral distributions (cf. Stich & Müller PPSN 2012) to assess solution quality and Et error versus spectra.  
- Recommendation: include a real-world speed-critical experiment (e.g., policy gradients in real-time robotics) to demonstrate practical applicability.
