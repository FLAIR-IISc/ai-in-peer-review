This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a widely used derivative-free optimization algorithm, by utilizing the triangular Cholesky factorization technique to reduce the computational cost of updating and storing the covariance matrix. The authors provide a theoretical justification for their approach, demonstrating that it does not compromise the algorithm's performance.

The main contribution of this work is the development of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm, which achieves a better, asymptotically optimal time complexity of O(µd2) for the covariance update and optimal memory complexity. The authors also show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES allows for numerically stable computation of the inverse of the Cholesky factor in quadratic time and provides the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix without additional costs.

The paper is well-structured and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' arguments and understand the proposed method. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, showing that it requires the same amount of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES but significantly less wall-clock time, especially for high-dimensional problems.

One potential area for improvement is the explanation of why the Cholesky-CMA-ES is "numerically more stable" than the standard CMA-ES. While the authors provide some intuition, a more detailed analysis of the numerical properties of the Cholesky factorization technique would be helpful.

Another potential direction for future work is the combination of the Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-dimensional approximation techniques, such as those proposed by Loshchilov (2015). As the authors note, high-dimensionality is a significant concern in many practical applications, such as training neural networks in direct policy search. Exploring ways to adapt the Cholesky-CMA-ES to these settings could lead to further improvements in efficiency and scalability.

Overall, this paper is a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization, and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm has the potential to become a widely used tool in the machine learning community.

Arguments for acceptance:

* The paper proposes a novel modification to the CMA-ES algorithm that reduces computational cost and improves efficiency.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical analysis of their approach, demonstrating its validity and potential benefits.
* The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, showing significant improvements in wall-clock time for high-dimensional problems.

Arguments against acceptance:

* The paper could benefit from a more detailed analysis of the numerical properties of the Cholesky factorization technique.
* The authors do not explore the potential combination of the Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-dimensional approximation techniques, which could be an important direction for future work.

Rating: 8/10

Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions.
