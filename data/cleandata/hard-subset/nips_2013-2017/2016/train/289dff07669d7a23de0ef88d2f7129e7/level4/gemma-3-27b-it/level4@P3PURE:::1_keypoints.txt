- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (metric adaptation).  
- The paper proposes a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based update significantly reduces computational complexity.  
- The method also substantially lowers memory requirements for storing state parameters.  
- Numerical evaluation is careful and thorough across benchmarks.  
- The new update preserves standard CMA-ES performance in terms of function evaluations to reach targets.  
- It substantially reduces wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- The approach provides readily available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix.  
- The reviewer views this as a significant contribution likely to become a default for medium/large-scale problems.  
- The paper is well-written and appropriate for NIPS given links to ML, information geometry, RL, and supervised learning.  
- Minor requests: clarify implementation/CPU comparability (language, processor details) and explain why Figure 2’s trend fails for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
