- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T) via a sequence of rank-1 updates to realize rank-μ covariance updates with O(μ d^2) time and optimal storage.
- Replaces expensive C_t^{-1/2} in CSA with A_t^{-1} and justifies the approximation by a lemma about vanishing rotation error if normalized covariance matrices converge.
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations on rotated benchmarks up to d=256 while significantly reducing wall-clock time.
- Compares favorably to CMA-ES variants with delayed decompositions and to a non-triangular Cholesky approach in runtime performance.
- Related work: Suttorp et al. (2009) update a non-triangular Cholesky factor (more parameters, less stable); Krause & Igel (2015) gave triangular rank-one updates for other CMA-ES variants.
- Technical soundness: rank-μ via sequential rank-1 Cholesky updates is correct and useful, and the CSA lemma is mathematically sound under its assumptions.
- Theoretical caveat: the CSA justification relies on convergence of normalized covariance sequences, which is not proven for full CMA-ES and lacks explicit error bounds.
- Numerical stability claims need more evidence (conditioning, eigenvalue drift, failure modes on ill-conditioned problems).
- Empirical evaluation is strong on wall-clock gains but would be strengthened by ablations isolating the A_t^{-1} vs C_t^{-1/2} effect on CSA.
- Missing baseline comparisons to large-scale alternatives (e.g., LM-CMA, sep-CMA-ES) to better position where full covariance O(μ d^2) is preferable.
- Clarity: paper is generally well organized with clear algorithms and helpful discussion of rotations, but contains typographical and phrasing issues and some ambiguous complexity statements.
- Originality: incremental but meaningful—extends triangular rank-one updates to full rank-μ CMA-ES and provides a theoretical rationale for the CSA modification.
- Significance: high practical impact—reduces matrix-update cost while preserving sample efficiency, likely useful in ML where function evaluations are cheap.
- Strengths: simple, code-ready algorithm; optimal storage; O(μ d^2) updates; sensible CSA approximation; strong empirical speed-ups without extra evaluations.
- Weaknesses: theoretical guarantees are assumption-based and lack bounds; potential loss of affine invariance is only informally addressed; limited analysis of CSA approximation and rotation drift.
- Suggestions: add empirical ablation on CSA approximation, quantify rotation changes E_t in main text, clarify complexity wording, report numerical stability diagnostics, and compare to LM-CMA/sep-CMA-ES.
- Pros for acceptance: practical, impactful speed-up; clean integration into CMA-ES; theoretical rationale (under assumptions).
- Cons for acceptance: incremental over prior Cholesky-update work; assumptions-dependent theory without bounds; empirical analysis could better isolate CSA effects.
- Minor issues: multiple typos and formatting artifacts (Eq. (1) σ_t, phrasing fixes, complexity wording, reference formatting, truncated header) need correction.
- Overall recommendation: borderline accept — valuable and practical contribution but would benefit from stronger empirical and theoretical analysis around the CSA approximation.
