- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor A of C = A A^T and replaces C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA, yielding quadratic-time, minimal-storage updates.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on Cholesky-based CMA-ES updates (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and generalizes triangular rank-1 updates to rank-µ standard CMA-ES.  
- Strength — motivation: Addresses dominant cost of covariance decompositions in moderate-to-large d with a numerically stable, lower-cost approach.  
- Strength — technical: Extends triangular Cholesky rank-1 updates to rank-µ and provides a Lemma arguing the CSA error from using A^{-1} vanishes as covariance stabilizes.  
- Strength — practical: Eigenvalues available from diag(A), A^{-1} computable in O(d^2), and storage optimal (triangular factor only).  
- Strength — empirical: Matches reference CMA-ES in function-evaluations and achieves substantial wall-clock speedups growing with dimension.  
- Weakness — novelty clarity: Needs a crisper statement of what is genuinely new vs Krause & Igel (2015) and Suttorp et al. (2009) (explicitly list the three claimed novelties).  
- Weakness — missing derivation: Must provide a self-contained proof (or appendix) that Algorithm 2 exactly implements the rank-µ covariance update (Eq. 2) up to floating-point error.  
- Weakness — complexity claims: Use Θ-notation, supply per-iteration cost breakdown (sampling, ranking, covariance update, decomposition) for all compared algorithms, and discuss delayed-eigendecomposition amortization and constant factors.  
- Weakness — profiling: Provide runtime profiling to substantiate that covariance update dominates runtime in experiments and explain cache/locality effects.  
- Weakness — CSA/invariance: Replacing C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} breaks exact affine invariance; the theoretical argument is suggestive but non-deterministic and requires stronger empirical diagnostics.  
- CSA diagnostics requested: Offline compare p_σ computed with C^{-1/2} vs A^{-1} (norm ratio, angle) over time, report ∥E_t^T E_{t-1} − I∥ and correlate with p_σ discrepancy, show transient-phase behavior on rotated vs unrotated functions, and test slowly converging/highly conditioned/noisy cases.  
- Weakness — numerical stability: Provide bounds or empirical evidence under high condition numbers, discuss safeguards (variance floors, reconditioning), and document potential failure modes.  
- Weakness — experimental scope: Test larger d (address L2/cache effects), provide wall-clock vs evaluation-count trade-offs for delayed-update baselines across delay schedules, and include ablations for µ and λ to confirm O(µ d^2) scaling.  
- Reproducibility: Provide code link or commit hash, list parameter settings and seeds (integration into Shark needs clear provenance).  
- Quality assessment: Algorithmic idea is sound and practically meaningful; theory for CSA modification is suggestive but not conclusive and needs targeted diagnostics.  
- Clarity: Paper is generally well written but fix notation/typos (Algorithm 2 wk→αk, Table 1 n→d, “F”→“f”), clean references, and state assumptions/limitations in Lemma 1 explicitly.  
- Originality: Contribution appears incremental but practically useful; must clarify delta relative to prior Cholesky work to address novelty concerns.  
- Significance: If validated, the variant is a strong practical default for full-covariance CMA-ES in moderate-to-large dimensions due to speedups without extra evaluations.  
- Acceptance pros: Clear practical improvement, preserves evaluation performance, and backed by plausible theory plus broad experiments.  
- Acceptance cons: Limited novelty relative to prior work, insufficient empirical validation of the CSA modification, and missing equivalence proof for the covariance update.  
- Overall recommendation: Lean toward rejection in current form, but paper could be strong if it adds explicit novelty claims, equivalence proof, precise complexity/profiling, and targeted CSA diagnostics.
