- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce costly sqrt computations and runtime complexity.  
- Theoretical justification argues the approximation preserves algorithm performance.  
- Empirical results show significant wall-clock speedups without increasing objective evaluations, especially in high dimensions.  
- Extends prior Cholesky-based and accelerated CMA-ES work to the full standard CMA-ES.  
- Achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity for full covariance adaptation.  
- Strong theoretical analysis addressing performance impact of the approximation.  
- Practical implementation provided in the Shark library, aiding reproducibility and adoption.  
- Writing is clear and well-organized, effectively explaining technical details.  
- Claim of improved numerical stability needs clearer explanation and supporting detail.  
- The approximation used in step-size adaptation requires more rationale and elaboration.  
- Quadratic scaling remains a limitation for extremely high-dimensional problems.  
- Lacks discussion of combining the approach with low-dimensional approximations for very large-scale tasks.  
- Additional analysis of scenarios where Cholesky-CMA-ES might underperform alternatives (e.g., Suttorp-CMA-ES) would strengthen the evaluation.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practically and theoretically valuable contribution; recommended for acceptance with minor revisions.
