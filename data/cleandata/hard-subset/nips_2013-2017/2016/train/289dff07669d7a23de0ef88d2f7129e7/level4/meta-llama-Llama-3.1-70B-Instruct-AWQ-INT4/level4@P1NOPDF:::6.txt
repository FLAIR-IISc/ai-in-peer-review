The paper proposes a modified version of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) with "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity". The authors claim that this modification does not alter the algorithm's behavior but reduces its computational cost. They refer to their method as Cholesky-CMA-ES, which is misleading since this name was first introduced by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009 for a similar algorithm, and has since become well-known. The authors fail to acknowledge this, instead presenting their variant as "Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES".

The proposed method is an incremental extension of the work by Krause et al. (2015). The only algorithmic contribution is the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function, which is also present in Krause et al.'s work, but is now called in a loop for all individuals. Empirical results show a difference in the Cigar function in Figure b), where the observed plateau has different objective function values for CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES. This discrepancy might be due to the authors' decision to plot median results. To provide a more comprehensive evaluation, the paper should compare the proposed approach to both Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES (empirically) and Krause's Cholesky-CMA-ES (algorithmically).

The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is somewhat misleading. This optimality does not hold for the Sphere function, where the covariance matrix is not necessary, or for separable functions, where only the diagonal elements are relevant. Moreover, the proposed approach is only twice as efficient as Suttorp's Cholesky CMA-ES, which is omitted from the comparison. The original paper by Suttorp et al. actually suggested that their method is more numerically stable, contrary to the claims made in this paper. The omission of Suttorp's Cholesky CMA-ES in Figure 3 may mislead the reader about the actual contribution of the paper.

I do not believe that the proposed approach significantly impacts the applicability of CMA-ES to large-scale optimization, as the complexity remains quadratic. However, I acknowledge that the proposed modification might be the most effective way to reduce computational time and memory when considering the full covariance matrix.

Overall, I think that the work is too incremental, with the only algorithmic contribution being the addition of a "for" loop. To strengthen the paper, I suggest the following modifications: a) Resolve the naming issue; b) Perform experiments with variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. and update all three figures. If the figures become too cluttered, consider showing only the best-performing variant in the main text and include the full figures in the Supplementary Material; c) Provide a clearer explanation of the main algorithmic contribution, which is the "for" loop in Algorithm 1, or justify why this interpretation is incorrect.

Minor notes: The statement "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is inaccurate. In practice, eigenvalue decomposition is typically performed periodically, not at each update step.
