- CMA-ES is a leading stochastic, derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (an underlying metric) via learning mechanisms.  
- The paper introduces a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based update significantly reduces computational complexity and memory usage compared to traditional updates.  
- Performance (in terms of function evaluations) is preserved despite the reduced complexity.  
- Numerical evaluation shows substantially lower wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- The contribution is judged highly impactful and likely to become a default for medium- to large-scale problems.  
- Clear practical advantages: lower memory footprint, reduced computational cost, and readily available covariance eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written and evaluated comprehensively.  
- The work is appropriate for NIPS given CMA-ES’s relevance to machine learning, information geometry, and applications like RL and supervised learning.
