- Proposal: extend Cholesky CMA-ES by adding rank-1 and rank-μ updates to the triangular Cholesky factor, implementing rank-μ as a sequence of rank-1 updates.  
- Theory: under the assumption that the covariance matrix C_t converges, the modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) does not substantially alter algorithm behavior.  
- Experiments: empirical results presented support the paper’s performance claims.  
- Limited novelty: contribution is incremental—rank-1 update to Cholesky was explored by Krause et al. (2015), and rank-μ as sequential rank-1 updates seems a straightforward generalization.  
- Need clearer contribution: authors should better articulate unique challenges or non-trivial insights arising from the rank-μ extension.  
- Theoretical assumption concern: analysis depends on C_t convergence; authors should cite and discuss prior work on covariance convergence (e.g., Beyer, ECJ 2014) to justify/ contextualize this assumption.  
- Minor corrections: Equation (1) and footnote 2 should use time-indexed step-size σ_t (not σ).  
- Typo: line 94 “which can by be motivated” → “which can be motivated.”
