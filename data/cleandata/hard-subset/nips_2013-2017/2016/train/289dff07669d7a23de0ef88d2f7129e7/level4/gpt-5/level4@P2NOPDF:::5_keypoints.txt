- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that reduces complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) by maintaining a lower-triangular Cholesky factor and avoiding estimating an orthogonal matrix E_t.
- Argument under restrictive assumptions: if covariance matrices converge to a target, E_t → I and the method is asymptotically equivalent to standard CMA-ES.
- Empirical results on standard test functions show substantial speedups with no measurable loss in optimization quality compared to two delayed-update CMA-ES variants.
- Overall contribution is a meaningful, well-engineered acceleration of CMA-ES with competitive performance on reported synthetic benchmarks.
- Practical relevance is limited because many real-world problems are dominated by costly objective evaluations; optimizer overhead matters mainly in high-dimensional, very-fast-evaluation regimes.
- Recommends tracking the evolution of E_t (e.g., plot norms of E_t − I) during runs to directly assess the omitted rotation component.
- Requests wall-clock timing breakdowns (sampling, updates, evaluations) and inclusion of vanilla CMA-ES timings to clarify sources of speedups.
- Suggests systematic quadratic experiments varying eigenvalue spectra to evaluate solution quality and E_t error versus spectral conditioning.
- Proposes adding a real-world experiment (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradient) where fast evaluations and moderate dimensionality make optimizer speed important.
- Concludes that clarifying when E_t stays close to identity, expanding empirical comparisons, testing across controlled spectra, and demonstrating real-application benefits would strengthen the paper.
