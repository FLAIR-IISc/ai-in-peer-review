- Proposes replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky factors to achieve a numerically stable quadratic-time covariance update with minimal memory and a modified CSA using the inverse triangular factor.
- Paper is well-written and clearly explains the method; experimental speedup results are promising and suggest potential adoption.
- Originality is limited because the method extends prior work (notably "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies"); differences need clarification.
- Authors must explicitly clarify differences and contributions relative to the previous study.
- The paper should prove (or clearly state) equivalence of their algorithm to the standard CMA-ES update formula (2), even if previously shown elsewhere.
- The claimed computational complexity O(μd^2) needs a more detailed discussion (use Θ-notation) to explain why the method is actually faster than CMA-ES-Ref.
- The claim that path error is small and decreases over time contradicts Hansen; because the analysis is non-deterministic, experiments validating this claim are required beyond final-performance comparisons.
- Minor notation errors need correction (e.g., change "wk" to "αk" in Algorithm 2, "n" to "d" in Table 1, and "F" to "f" in line 185).
- In its current form the paper lacks clarity and necessary discussions and is not ready for NIPS acceptance.
- With proper revisions addressing equivalence, complexity analysis, and empirical validation of path errors, the paper could make a significant contribution.
- Strength: proposes a numerically stable, minimal-memory quadratic-time covariance update.
- Strength: experiments show promising speedups suggesting practical value.
- Strength: presentation and exposition are clear and well-written.
- Weakness: limited originality as an extension of prior work.
- Weakness: missing clear discussion/proof of equivalence to standard CMA-ES and detailed complexity justification.
- Weakness: lacks experimental validation that the path error is small and decreases over time.
- For acceptance: potential significant contribution to derivative-free optimization if revised.
- Against acceptance: current lack of clarity, limited originality, and insufficient experimental/theoretical validation.
