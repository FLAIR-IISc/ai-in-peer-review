- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the covariance via a lower-triangular Cholesky factor, reducing per-iteration complexity from O(d^3) to O(µd^2).
- Approximates the CSA-required inverse matrix square root using the inverse of the Cholesky factor.
- Provides a theoretical argument (Lemma 1) that rotational error from the approximation vanishes under convergence of the covariance shape.
- Empirical results on standard benchmarks show substantial wall-clock speed-ups without loss of sample efficiency.
- Strength: Addresses a major computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, improving scalability for high-dimensional problems with cheap evaluations.
- Strength: Method is original and well-justified, adapting Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES and handling CSA cleverly.
- Strength: Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and presents the algorithm precisely.
- Strength: Experiments convincingly demonstrate wall-clock improvements and outperform delayed-update baselines.
- Weakness: Practical impact is limited to problems where function evaluations are cheap relative to matrix updates; not emphasized enough.
- Suggestion: More explicit delineation of target problem classes where the speed-up is most relevant (e.g., large d, cheap evaluations).
- Weakness: Theoretical assumption about convergence of the rotation matrix Et is not empirically validated.
- Suggestion: Empirically track and plot the rotational approximation error over optimization runs to support the theory.
- Weakness: Experimental analysis could be expanded—compare against full O(d^3) CMA-ES updates and vary Hessian eigenvalue spectra.
- Suggestion: Include studies on quadratic functions with controlled conditioning to assess behavior under ill-conditioning/separability.
- Weakness: No real-world experiments; all results are on synthetic benchmarks despite claimed applications like policy search.
- Suggestion: Add a small real-world experiment (e.g., hyperparameter tuning or simple policy search) to demonstrate practical utility.
- Argument for acceptance: Clear, significant, and well-executed contribution likely to become standard for computation-bound CMA-ES use-cases.
- Argument against acceptance: Limited practical scope and incomplete empirical validation of the approximation and real-world benefits.
