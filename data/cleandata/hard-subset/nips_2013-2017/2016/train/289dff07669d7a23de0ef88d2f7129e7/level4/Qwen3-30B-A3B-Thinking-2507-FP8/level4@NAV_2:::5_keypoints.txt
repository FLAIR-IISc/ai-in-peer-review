- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: replaces full covariance update with a numerically stable O(μ d²) triangular Cholesky-factor update, removing the O(d³) matrix-square-root bottleneck.  
- Theoretical justification: shows omission of the orthogonal matrix Eₜ in step-size adaptation becomes negligible when the covariance converges (Lemma 1 → Eₜ → I under plausible conditions).  
- Empirical validation: optimization performance (function evaluations) matches standard CMA-ES on benchmarks (Sphere, Cigar, Rosenbrock, etc.) while achieving large runtime speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64).  
- Complexity/storage benefit: provides optimal asymptotic time O(μ d²) and O(d²) storage for standard CMA-ES, distinct from prior Cholesky or diagonal approximations.  
- Strength — Quality: algorithm is numerically stable and theoretically grounded; empirical speedups align with complexity analysis.  
- Strength — Clarity: algorithm description and theoretical arguments are well-structured and the distinction from prior work is clearly articulated.  
- Strength — Originality: novel application of Cholesky factorization to the standard CMA-ES with theoretical support for the step-size change.  
- Strength — Significance: enables CMA-ES scaling to higher dimensions where covariance updates dominate runtime, relevant for some DFO/RL/hyperparameter settings.  
- Weakness — Practical relevance: speedups mainly matter for fast objective evaluations and high dimensionality (d > ~50), which may be uncommon in typical CMA-ES use cases.  
- Weakness — Empirical gaps: missing direct visualization of Eₜ evolution (e.g., ||Eₜ − I|| over iterations).  
- Weakness — Empirical gaps: lacks runtime comparisons against CMA-ES without delay strategies to contextualize speedups.  
- Weakness — Empirical gaps: no real-world end-to-end application (e.g., neural network training) demonstrating practical benefit.  
- Weakness — Theoretical scope: Lemma 1 assumes covariance convergence; paper does not address cases where covariance does not converge and potential performance effects.  
- Overall pro: novel, theoretically grounded, empirically validated speedup addressing a key CMA-ES bottleneck.  
- Overall con: limited broad practical impact as presented and missing experiments to fully validate theoretical assumptions and real-world utility; authors should add Eₜ plots, no-delay baselines, and a real-world high-d experiment.
