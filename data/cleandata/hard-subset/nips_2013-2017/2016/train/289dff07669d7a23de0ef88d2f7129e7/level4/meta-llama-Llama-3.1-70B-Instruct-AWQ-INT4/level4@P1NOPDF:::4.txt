This work's primary contribution is the utilization of triangular Cholesky factorization, which eliminates the need for the square root in standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing computational costs associated with matrix inversion. The author provides a theoretically justified approach, demonstrating that this modification does not compromise the algorithm's performance. The proposed method achieves a reduction in runtime complexity without significantly altering the number of objective function evaluations. This paper presents a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES.

A more detailed theoretical explanation would be beneficial in understanding why the proposed computation is 'numerically more stable'. It appears that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires a similar number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES, while reducing wall-clock time, with this advantage increasing with the dimensionality of the search space. The author may want to consider integrating the low-dimensional approximation proposed by Loshchilov (2015) into the existing framework, as high-dimensionality is a significant concern in many practical applications, such as training neural networks in direct policy search.
