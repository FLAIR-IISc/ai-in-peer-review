This paper presents a Cholesky Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) that incorporates both rank-1 and rank-$\mu$ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, where the rank-$\mu$ update is implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. Assuming the convergence of the covariance matrix, the authors prove that their modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper concludes with experiments evaluating the algorithm's performance.

1.  The primary contribution is the extension of the update for the triangular Cholesky factor to include a rank-$\mu$ update in addition to the rank-1 update. However, the significance of this contribution appears limited when considering the work of Krause et al. (2015), which previously introduced the rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The approach presented here seems to be a straightforward generalization of that procedure.

2.  The analysis in Lemma 1 relies on the assumption that the covariance matrix $C_t$ converges. It is worth noting that the convergence of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES has been previously established through theoretical analysis (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), even if not for the exact CMA-ES formulation used here. Citing such work could strengthen the justification for this assumption.
