- Proposal: replace eigen-decomposition with Cholesky decomposition in CMA-ES to reduce time and storage complexity.  
- Positive: writing is clear and experimental results show significant speedups.  
- Potential: method could become a standard CMA-ES variant if substantiated.  
- Originality issue: method is nearly identical to Hansen et al. (2014) rank-one covariance update and appears a trivial adaptation.  
- Lack of novel contribution: no new theoretical insight, convergence guarantees, or clear performance improvements beyond prior work.  
- Theoretical gap 1: no proof or derivation showing equivalence to Equation (2), undermining reproducibility.  
- Theoretical gap 2: computational complexity claims (O(μd²)) lack rigorous justification—need Θ-notation, constant-factor analysis (flop counts, cache effects), and implementation details.  
- Theoretical gap 3: unsupported claim that path error is small and decreases—requires deterministic theory or trajectory-based empirical comparisons to standard CMA-ES.  
- Minor technical errors: notation inconsistencies (Algorithm 2: wk→α_k; Table 1: n→d; Line 185: F→f) indicating poor proofreading.  
- Conclusion: despite promising experiments, paper lacks novelty, theoretical rigor, and completeness; recommend rejection unless substantially revised or retracted.
