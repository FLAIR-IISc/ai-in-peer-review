

This paper proposes replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky decomposition in CMA-ES to reduce time and storage complexity. The writing is clear, the method is well-motivated, and the experimental results—including significant empirical speedups—are promising. The approach holds potential to become a standard variant of CMA-ES. However, the work's originality is limited, as the core update mechanism closely aligns with the prior publication "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies," rendering it a straightforward extension rather than a novel contribution. The authors should clarify whether this work constitutes a meaningful advancement over the cited prior art.

Three critical gaps undermine the paper's technical rigor:  
1. **Equivalence proof**: The paper claims equivalence to Equation (2) but omits a derivation or reference to the prior work's proof. While space constraints are acknowledged, this is essential for validating the proposed method's correctness within the current context.  
2. **Complexity justification**: The claim that computational complexity is *O*(μd²), matching CMA-ES-Ref, requires deeper analysis. The authors should explicitly compare constant factors using *Θ* notation to justify the observed speedups, rather than merely asserting asymptotic equivalence.  
3. **Error analysis validation**: The assertion that "path error decreases over time" directly contradicts Hansen's theoretical findings. Given the non-deterministic nature of the analysis, experiments must demonstrate this behavior beyond final performance metrics (e.g., tracking error evolution across iterations).  

Minor issues also require attention:  
- Notation inconsistencies: Algorithm 2 uses *w<sub>k</sub>* instead of *α<sub>k</sub>*; Table 1 uses *n* instead of *d*.  
- Typographical errors: "F" should be "f" in line 185.  

The paper’s current form does not meet NIPS’s standards for originality or technical completeness. While the empirical results are compelling, the lack of rigorous justification for the core claims and the insufficient differentiation from prior work prevent it from being considered for publication. Substantial revisions addressing the three critical gaps and minor errors are necessary before reconsideration.
