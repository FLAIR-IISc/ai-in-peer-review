The paper presents a CMA-ES variant claiming “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity.” The authors argue that the modification preserves the algorithm’s behavior while reducing computational cost. Labeling the method “Cholesky-CMA-ES” is problematic because this name was introduced by T. Suttorp et al. (2009) for a closely related algorithm and is widely known. The manuscript does not acknowledge this and instead states “Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES.”

Methodologically, the proposal is an incremental extension of Krause et al. (2015). Concretely, the only algorithmic change is to invoke rankOneUpdate (already used in Krause et al.) not once as in Plus strategies, but in a loop over all individuals.

Empirically, there is a discrepancy on the Cigar function in Figure b): the plateau levels differ between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES. This may stem from how median results are plotted.

The paper should compare the proposed method against the Cholesky-CMA-ES of Suttorp et al. (empirically) and the Cholesky-CMA-ES of Krause et al. (algorithmically).

The claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is somewhat misleading. It is not optimal on the Sphere function where the covariance is unnecessary, and on separable problems a diagonal parameterization suffices. The proposed approach appears only about twice as cheap as the (omitted) Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp. This work suggests that the latter suffers from numerical instabilities, whereas the original paper reported the opposite. Its omission from Figure 3 risks giving readers a distorted view of the contribution.

I do not see a meaningful change for large-scale CMA-ES since the complexity remains quadratic. That said, the modification may be the best available option to reduce time and memory when a full covariance matrix is retained.

Overall, the contribution feels overly incremental (algorithmically, it amounts to adding a single “for” loop).

I recommend the following revisions:
a) Address the naming conflict clearly.
b) Include experiments with the variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al., and update all three figures. If the plots become cluttered, present only the top performers in the main text and move the full set to the Supplement.
c) Clarify that the primary algorithmic change is the “for” loop in Algorithm 1, or justify why this characterization is inaccurate.

Minor note: The manuscript states that an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is typically performed at each update step. This is not standard practice; in well-designed implementations it is done periodically.
