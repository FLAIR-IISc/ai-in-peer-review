- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (equivalently, an underlying metric).  
- The paper proposes updating the covariance matrix via its triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-factor update substantially reduces update computational complexity and memory for stored state.  
- Numerical experiments show the new update does not worsen the number of function evaluations to reach targets vs. standard CMA-ES.  
- The method yields substantial wall-clock time reductions, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- The variant could become the default for medium/large-scale CMA-ES due to lower memory, lower complexity, and free covariance eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written, carefully evaluated, and relevant to NIPS given links to ML, information geometry, reinforcement and supervised learning.  
- Minor comment: clarify how CPU time comparability between implementations was ensured (were implementations in the same language; which one?).
