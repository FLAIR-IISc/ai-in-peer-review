This paper proposes to improve the time and storage complexity of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) by replacing the standard eigendecomposition with a Cholesky decomposition.

Overall, the paper is well-written and easy to follow. The proposed approach is methodologically sound, and the empirical results are promising, particularly with respect to the reported speedup rates. The method shows potential to become a new standard implementation of CMA-ES.

Despite its merits, the paper's primary weakness is its limited originality. The proposed technique appears to be a direct extension of the prior work, "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The core methods seem largely identical, raising significant concerns about the novelty of the contribution. I would welcome clarification from the authors if my understanding of the relationship to this previous work is incorrect.

Furthermore, the paper omits several critical details that are necessary for a complete and convincing presentation:

1.  **Equivalence to the Original Formulation:** The authors should explicitly demonstrate that their proposed algorithm is equivalent to the standard CMA-ES update rule (formula 2). While this may have been proven in prior work and space is a consideration, its inclusion is essential for the paper's self-containment and rigor.

2.  **Detailed Complexity Analysis:** The paper states the computational complexity is O(μd²), which is asymptotically the same as the reference CMA-ES implementation. To justify the claims of superior speed, a more detailed analysis is required. The authors should elaborate on why their method is faster in practice, perhaps by providing a more precise analysis of the constant factors involved (e.g., using Θ-notation) or by detailing the computational cost of the specific operations in both methods.

3.  **Validation of Theoretical Claims:** The authors claim that the error in the evolution path is small and decreases over time. This appears to contradict claims made by Hansen in previous work. Given that the paper's theoretical analysis is not deterministic, this assertion requires empirical validation. The current experiments, which only compare final performance, are insufficient. Specific experiments designed to track and measure this error over the course of the optimization process should be conducted to substantiate this claim.

**Minor Comments:**
*   Please review the mathematical notation for consistency. For instance, in Algorithm 2, `wk` appears to be used where `αk` is intended. Similarly, in Table 1, `n` should likely be `d`.
*   There is a typo on line 185, where 'F' should be 'f'.

In its current form, due to the significant concerns regarding originality and the omission of essential analytical and experimental details, I do not believe the paper meets the standards for publication at NIPS.
