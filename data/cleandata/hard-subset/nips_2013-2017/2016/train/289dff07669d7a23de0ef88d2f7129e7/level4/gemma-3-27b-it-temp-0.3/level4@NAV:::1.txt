CMA-ES represents the current state-of-the-art in stochastic, derivative-free optimization, effectively adapting parameters through learning mechanisms applied to a multivariate normal sampling distribution – equivalently, adapting an underlying metric. This paper introduces a novel covariance matrix update strategy utilizing triangular Cholesky factors. This approach demonstrably reduces both the computational complexity and memory requirements for storing the algorithm’s state parameters, offering a significant advantage over existing update methods.

The proposed method is rigorously evaluated numerically, demonstrating that it maintains the performance of standard CMA-ES in terms of function evaluation count for achieving a target accuracy, while substantially decreasing wall-clock time, particularly in high-dimensional problems. This work constitutes a highly valuable contribution to the field of optimization. 

I anticipate that this variant of CMA-ES will become a preferred implementation for medium- to large-scale problems due to its clear benefits: lower memory footprint, reduced computational cost, and the readily available covariance matrix eigenvalues. The paper is well-written and the experimental evaluation is thorough. Its presentation at NIPS is particularly fitting given CMA-ES’s strong connections to machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in areas like reinforcement learning and supervised learning.

Minor points for consideration: Please clarify the measures taken to ensure comparability of the different implementations used in the experiments (e.g., programming language, specific compiler/interpreter). Additionally, the statement in the text regarding Figure 2 – “the higher the dimensionality, the more pronounced the differences” – does not appear to hold for the Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256. An explanation for this observation would be beneficial.
