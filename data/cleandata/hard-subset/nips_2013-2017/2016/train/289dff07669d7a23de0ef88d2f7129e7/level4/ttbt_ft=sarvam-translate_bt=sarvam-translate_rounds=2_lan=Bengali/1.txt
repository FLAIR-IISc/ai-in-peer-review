CMA-ES is an advanced stochastic/randomized algorithm for (derivative-free) optimization. It combines various learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sample distribution, which can also be seen as an adaptation of the underlying metric. This paper proposes a new version for updating the covariance matrix based on triangular Cholesky factors, which provides significant advantages compared to previous updates in terms of the update complexity required to preserve the algorithm's state parameters and memory reduction. The new method has been carefully evaluated numerically. It has been shown that the new update does not reduce the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations required to reach a specific goal, but it significantly reduces the algorithm's wall-clock time, especially for large dimensions. I believe this paper is a very important contribution to the optimization domain. I hope this version of CMA-ES can become a default version of the method in the medium/large size range, as it has several clear advantages over the current default method: such as required memory reduction, complexity reduction, and freely available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix. The paper is well-written and the method has been carefully evaluated. It is good to see that this contribution has appeared in NIPS, as CMA-ES is also used in machine learning, information geometry, and reinforcement learning or supervised learning.
