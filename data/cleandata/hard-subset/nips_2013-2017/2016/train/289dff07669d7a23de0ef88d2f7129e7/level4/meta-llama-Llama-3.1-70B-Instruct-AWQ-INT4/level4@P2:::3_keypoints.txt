- Proposes a Cholesky CMA-ES that extends triangular Cholesky factor updates to include rank-1 and rank-μ updates.  
- Provides theoretical justification that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation converges and does not significantly alter algorithm behavior under covariance convergence.  
- Experiments show reduced runtime complexity while maintaining a similar number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES.  
- Extension to rank-μ is a notable contribution but is a straightforward generalization of Krause et al. (2015), who used rank-1 updates.  
- Beyer (ECJ 2014) convergence analysis of covariance Ct supports Lemma 1 despite not using exact CMA-ES formulae.  
- Minor issues: σ in the denominator of Eq.(1) and a footnote should be σt; delete an extra "by" in line 94.  
- Comparisons include CMA-ES-Ref and Suttorp-CMA-ES but could be broader across benchmarks and problem sizes.  
- Arguments for acceptance: useful for high-dimensional problems, sound theoretical support, and effective empirical performance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental novelty, minor typos/notation errors, and limited experimental breadth.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to fix errors and strengthen empirical comparisons.
