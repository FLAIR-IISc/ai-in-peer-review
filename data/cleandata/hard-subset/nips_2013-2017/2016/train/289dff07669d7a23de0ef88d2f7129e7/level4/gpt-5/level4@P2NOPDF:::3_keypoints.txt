- Main algorithmic contribution is incremental: generalizes Krause et al. (2015) rank-1 Cholesky update to rank-μ via sequential rank-1 updates.  
- Theoretical result: modified CSA is behavior-preserving under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges.  
- Limitation: theory relies on the covariance-convergence assumption rather than proving convergence under the algorithm’s exact dynamics.  
- Suggestion: explicitly connect and cite prior theoretical work (e.g., Beyer 2014) to strengthen Lemma 1 and clarify applicability.  
- Experiments: empirical comparisons reported but lack sufficient detail on design, statistics, and effect sizes to judge strength.  
- Recommendation: ensure fair baselines (including Krause et al., 2015) and include ablation comparing rank-μ vs pure rank-1 updates.  
- Writing: generally understandable but needs clearer positioning relative to prior work and clearer statement of novelty beyond sequential composition.  
- Minor issues/typos: Equation (1) and Footnote 2 use σ instead of time-indexed σ_t; Line 94 contains “by” error; overall proofreading for indexing/typography.  
- Overall assessment: practical variant with modest theoretical reassurance and empirical results, but limited novelty over prior work and needs clearer theory, stronger experiments, and tighter writing.
