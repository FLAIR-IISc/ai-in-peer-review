- Problematic naming: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” was used by Suttorp et al. (2009) and the authors fail to acknowledge this prior work.  
- Novelty is limited: method incrementally builds on Krause et al. (2015) by applying a rank-one update per individual rather than once per generation.  
- Missing comparisons: no empirical or algorithmic comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) Cholesky-CMA-ES or to Krause et al. (2015).  
- Empirical discrepancy: Figure b) (Cigar function) shows differing plateau values between reference CMA-ES and proposed method, possibly due to reporting medians.  
- Overstated optimality claim: “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is misleading—diagonal or separable problems need not store full covariance.  
- Limited improvement: method offers only a two-fold cost reduction versus Suttorp et al., and the paper’s claim of numerical instability of Suttorp et al. contradicts the original publication.  
- Omission in figures: Suttorp et al.’s method is omitted from Figure 3, obscuring true contribution.  
- Scalability: does not change CMA-ES’s quadratic complexity for large-scale problems, though may be practically useful when full covariance is required.  
- Incremental algorithmic contribution: main change is essentially adding a “for” loop, which should be justified or reframed.  
- Requested revisions: (a) resolve naming conflict; (b) add experiments/comparisons to Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. and update figures (or move full results to Supplement); (c) clarify or justify the significance of the “for” loop in Algorithm 1.  
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically performed periodically in proper implementations, not at every update step.
