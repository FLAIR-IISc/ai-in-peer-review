This paper proposes a novel variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a state-of-the-art stochastic optimization algorithm, by utilizing triangular Cholesky factors to update the covariance matrix. This approach significantly reduces the complexity and memory requirements of the algorithm, making it particularly suitable for medium to large-scale optimization problems. The authors provide a thorough theoretical justification for their method and demonstrate its efficacy through extensive numerical experiments.

The CMA-ES is a powerful derivative-free optimization algorithm widely used in machine learning, information geometry, and reinforcement learning. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES variant offers several advantages over the standard CMA-ES, including reduced memory requirements, lower computational complexity, and the ability to obtain the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix without additional cost. These advantages make the Cholesky-CMA-ES an attractive alternative for large-scale optimization problems.

The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach. The numerical experiments are carefully designed and executed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES in reducing the wall-clock time of the algorithm while maintaining its performance in terms of the number of function evaluations.

One minor point that could be addressed is a more detailed explanation of how the different implementations used in the experiments are comparable in terms of CPU performance. Specifically, it would be helpful to know whether the same implementation language was used for all variants and whether any optimizations were applied.

Additionally, the authors note that the differences in runtime between the Cholesky-CMA-ES and other variants become more pronounced with increasing dimensionality. However, this trend does not seem to hold for the Cigar and Discus functions in 256 dimensions. A brief explanation for this discrepancy would be helpful in providing a more complete understanding of the results.

Overall, this paper makes a significant contribution to the field of optimization, and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES variant has the potential to become a default method for medium to large-scale optimization problems. Its connection to machine learning, information geometry, and reinforcement learning makes it a valuable contribution to the NIPS community.

Strengths:

* The paper proposes a novel and efficient variant of the CMA-ES algorithm.
* The authors provide a thorough theoretical justification for their approach.
* The numerical experiments are carefully designed and executed, demonstrating the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES.
* The paper is well-written, and the authors provide a clear and concise explanation of their approach.

Weaknesses:
