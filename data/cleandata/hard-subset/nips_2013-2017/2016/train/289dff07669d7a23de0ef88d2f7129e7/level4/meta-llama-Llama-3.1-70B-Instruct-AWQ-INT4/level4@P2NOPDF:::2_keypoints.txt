- Proposes using Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition for CMA-ES to reduce time and storage complexity; paper is well-written and reports promising speedup results.  
- Experimental speedups are encouraging and suggest the method could become a new standard variant of CMA-ES.  
- Originality is limited: method appears a straightforward extension of "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies"; authors should clarify this connection.  
- Need to demonstrate (or reference a proof of) equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2); a brief explanation or citation would suffice.  
- Complexity claim O(μ d^2) matches CMA-ES-Ref; authors must use tighter notation (e.g., Θ) and a more detailed argument to explain why their algorithm is actually faster.  
- Claim that path error is small and decreases (contradicting Hansen) requires experimental validation over time, not just final-performance comparison.  
- Minor issues: notation inconsistencies (wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk; n in Table 1 should be d) and typos (e.g., 'F' on line 185 should be 'f').  
- Overall verdict: paper is not acceptable for NIPS in its current form; addressing originality, theoretical clarifications, complexity explanation, and additional experiments is required.
