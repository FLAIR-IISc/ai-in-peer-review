- Proposes a novel CMA-ES variant using triangular Cholesky factors to update the covariance, reducing time and space complexity.  
- Provides theoretical justification and a thorough empirical evaluation of the proposed method.  
- Matches standard CMA-ES in number of function evaluations while significantly reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Detailed comparisons with other CMA-ES variants (including delayed decomposition) show substantial speedups for large-scale problems.  
- Reduces memory requirements and computational complexity; yields covariance eigenvalues without extra cost for conditioning monitoring.  
- Method is relevant to the NIPS/ML community and could become a default for medium-to-large-scale optimization.  
- Paper is well-written, well-organized, and clearly explains the method, theory, and results.  
- Strengths: novel, efficient update scheme; thorough analysis; empirical speedups in high dimensions.  
- Weakness: lacks detailed explanation of how different implementations were made CPU-comparable in experiments.  
- Weakness: unexplained discrepancy where wall-clock advantages do not hold for Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel efficient method, solid theory, and convincing empirical speedups.  
- Arguments against acceptance: need clearer experimental comparability and explanation for the 256-dimension anomalies.
