The paper introduces a CMA-ES variant aimed at reducing the time and memory costs of covariance updates while preserving empirical behavior. The method—called Cholesky-CMA-ES—maintains triangular Cholesky factors and claims to leave the number of function evaluations essentially unchanged while lowering per-iteration computation and storage. However, the chosen name is problematic: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” was already used by Suttorp et al. (2009) for a related approach and is well known in the community. The manuscript does not acknowledge this and instead presents the term as if newly introduced (“our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES”). Algorithmically, the contribution appears to be a small extension of Krause and Igel (2015): the only substantive change is applying rankOneUpdate (already present in Krause and Igel) in a loop across all contributing individuals rather than once as in plus strategies. Empirically, on the Cigar function (Figure b)), the plateau levels differ between CMA-ES-Ref and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES; this might be a plotting artifact related to how medians are computed or displayed. The paper should include a direct empirical comparison to the Cholesky-CMA-ES of Suttorp et al. (2009) and a clear algorithmic comparison to Krause and Igel (2015). The headline claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is also somewhat misleading: on the Sphere function the covariance is unnecessary, and on separable problems one can restrict to a diagonal; in those regimes the proposed full-matrix update is not optimal. Moreover, the proposed approach seems only about twice as fast as the omitted Cholesky CMA-ES of Suttorp et al.; while this paper suggests that Suttorp’s variant is numerically unstable, the original work reported the opposite. Omitting that baseline from Figure 3 risks overstating the novelty and practical impact. I do not expect this modification to materially change the large-scale landscape for CMA-ES because the complexity remains quadratic; nonetheless, within the full-covariance setting, this may indeed be the best available option to cut runtime and memory. Overall, the work feels too incremental (algorithmically it reduces to adding a “for” loop). I recommend the following revisions: a) address the naming conflict; b) include experiments with the variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause and Igel and update all three figures—if the figures become cluttered, present only the best-performing variant in the main text and move full comparisons to the Supplementary Material; c) clarify that the primary algorithmic change is the additional loop in Algorithm 1, or explain why this characterization is inaccurate. Minor note: “Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step” is not typical—correct implementations do this periodically, not every iteration.
