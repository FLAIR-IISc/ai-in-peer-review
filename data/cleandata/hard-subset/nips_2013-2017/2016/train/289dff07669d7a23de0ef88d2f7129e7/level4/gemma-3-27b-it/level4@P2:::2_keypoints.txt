- Proposes using the Cholesky factor to update the covariance in CMA-ES to reduce time and storage complexity.
- Core idea is clearly articulated and logically motivated as an alternative to the matrix square root.
- Demonstrates good understanding of CMA-ES and prior optimizations, citing Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015).
- Experimental results show significant wall-clock speedups, especially in high-dimensional problems.
- Provides a theoretical argument (Lemma 1) supporting asymptotic optimality and small approximation error.
- Method is a logical extension of prior work; the novelty claim should be tempered and the relation to Krause & Igel (2015) discussed further.
- Complexity claim of O(µd^2) matches reference CMA-ES but lacks detailed explanation of constant factors; Θ-notation and practical implications needed.
- Missing empirical analysis of the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor; need to track algorithmic path (e.g., rotation matrix Et).
- Potential contradiction with Hansen’s claims is noted but not explored or supported with evidence.
- Several minor typographical and notational errors reduce polish (e.g., w_k vs α_k, n vs d in Table 1, F vs f).
- Equation (2) is referenced as proven elsewhere, which may hinder readers unfamiliar with prior work from verifying the approach.
- Arguments for acceptance: well-motivated improvement, significant practical speedup, and plausible theoretical justification.
- Arguments for rejection: limited originality, insufficient complexity and error analysis, and minor presentation errors.
- Overall assessment: worthwhile but borderline; needs stronger complexity analysis, error validation, and clearer relation to prior work.
- Recommendation: Weak reject in current form, with encouragement to resubmit after addressing weaknesses.
