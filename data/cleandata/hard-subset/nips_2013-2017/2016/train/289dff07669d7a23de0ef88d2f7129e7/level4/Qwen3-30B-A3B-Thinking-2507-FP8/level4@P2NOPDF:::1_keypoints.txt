- Proposes a novel CMA-ES covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors that cuts computational complexity and memory.  
- New update preserves optimization performance (function evaluations) while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Empirical results show same optimization effectiveness as standard CMA-ES with lower per-iteration cost, suitable for medium-to-large scale problems.  
- Key advantages: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and implicit eigenvalue computation (no explicit extraction).  
- Well-motivated, rigorously evaluated, and highly relevant to machine learning applications (reinforcement learning, supervised learning, information geometry).  
- Could become the default practical implementation of CMA-ES due to tangible benefits without quality loss.  
- Paper is well-written with clear exposition, precise technical presentation, and thorough experiments up to 256 dimensions.  
- Minor concern: clarify implementation consistency across compared CMA-ES variants (language, compiler, hardware, optimization flags).  
- Minor concern: explain why Figure 2’s claimed trend (greater differences at higher dimensionality) does not hold for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
