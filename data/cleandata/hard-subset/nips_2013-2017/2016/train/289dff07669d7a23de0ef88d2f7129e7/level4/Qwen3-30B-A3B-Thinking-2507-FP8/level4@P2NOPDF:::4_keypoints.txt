- Introduces triangular Cholesky factorization for CMA-ES to avoid explicit square-root computations.  
- Theoretical analysis shows reduced computational overhead without degrading algorithmic performance.  
- Empirically maintains identical objective-function evaluation counts while lowering wall-clock runtime.  
- Runtime savings scale favorably with problem dimensionality, benefiting high-dimensional applications.  
- Claim of "numerically more stable" needs stronger theoretical support (e.g., error propagation bounds, behavior in ill-conditioned cases).  
- Suggest presenting absolute runtime comparisons across dimensions (e.g., 10D vs. 100D) to contextualize savings.  
- Recommend exploring integration with dimensionality-reduction techniques (e.g., Loshchilov et al. 2015) for very high-dimensional problems.  
- Ask authors to discuss potential limitations, such as the overhead of maintaining the triangular factor during adaptive updates.  
- Review of related work should better position the method against recent CMA-ES variants (low-rank updates) and compare costs with other decompositions (e.g., QR).  
- Minor grammatical edits suggested for clarity and wording (several phrasing improvements provided).  
- Overall: technically sound, practically impactful; address stability justification and high-dimensional applicability to strengthen the paper.
