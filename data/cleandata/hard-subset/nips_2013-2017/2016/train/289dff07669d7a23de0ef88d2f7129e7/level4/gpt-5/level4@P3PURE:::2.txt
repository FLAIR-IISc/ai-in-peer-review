This paper replaces the eigen-decomposition with a Cholesky decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of CMA-ES. Overall, the paper is clearly written, the proposed approach appears reasonable, and the empirical speedups are promising. I could imagine this line of work becoming a new standard variant of CMA-ES in the future.

That said, I find the originality limited. The contribution appears to be a trivial extension of “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies.” As far as I can tell, the methods are essentially identical; please correct me if I am wrong.

More importantly, several essential elements are missing:

1) Equivalence to formula (2): You should explicitly show that your algorithm is equivalent to formula (2). I understand space is limited and that prior work may contain the proof, but demonstrating this equivalence is still essential for this paper.

2) Complexity vs. CMA-ES-Ref: You state the computational complexity is O(μ d^2), which is exactly the same as CMA-ES-Ref. Please discuss in detail why your algorithm is faster in practice. Using Θ-notation rather than O-notation would help clarify constant factors and tighter bounds. If the gains come from improved constants, cache behavior, or reduced memory traffic due to Cholesky updates, that should be carefully analyzed and justified.

3) Path error claim vs. Hansen: You claim the error in the path is small and decreases over time, which runs contrary to Hansen’s claim. Since your theoretical analysis is not deterministic, you should provide experimental validation specifically for this claim. Comparing only final optimization performance is not sufficient; please include direct measurements of the path error over time and across problem instances.

Minor comments:
- Notation needs careful checking. For example, wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk, and n in Table 1 should be d.
- “F” should be “f” on line 185.

Conclusion: While the empirical results are promising and the exposition is clear, the paper in its current form lacks the necessary originality and missing validations/derivations. I do not believe it is ready for NIPS at this time.
