- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update the lower-triangular Cholesky factor directly instead of the full covariance and its matrix square root.  
- Claims computational complexity reduction from O(d³) to O(μ d²), with μ typically O(log d).  
- Method omits estimation of the random orthogonal matrix Et used in standard CMA-ES.  
- Provides theoretical justification under restrictive assumptions (covariance sequence converges to a target; Et → I asymptotically).  
- Empirical results (runtime and convergence plots) on benchmark functions show substantial speed-up and no apparent loss in optimization performance versus two delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Notes contribution is valuable for accelerating CMA-ES but questions practical applicability since objective evaluations usually dominate total time and CMA-ES is often used in low dimensions.  
- Points out the target scenario (high-dimensional problems with very fast evaluations) is narrow and of questionable prevalence.  
- Recommends visualizing the omitted Et during runs by running full CMA-ES and computing the approximation in parallel to measure Et deviations from identity.  
- Suggests including baseline results for standard CMA-ES (no delayed updates) showing performance and actual runtimes.  
- Recommends deeper analysis on quadratic objectives by systematically varying eigenvalue spectra (e.g., Stich & Müller methodology) to study solution quality and Et approximation error.  
- Encourages adding a real-world application (e.g., policy-gradient optimization in real-time robotics) to demonstrate practical impact.
