This paper presents an interesting modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, utilizing Cholesky decomposition in place of eigen-decomposition to address computational time and storage concerns. The writing is clear, the proposed approach appears sound, and the reported experimental results – particularly the observed speedup – are encouraging. This work has the potential to establish a new, widely-used variant of CMA-ES. 

However, the novelty of this contribution feels incremental. The core methodology bears a strong resemblance to the work presented in “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,” and appears to be a direct extension of that approach. I would appreciate clarification on this point. 

Furthermore, several key elements are absent or insufficiently detailed. First, demonstrating the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2) is crucial, even acknowledging the space constraints and prior proof in related work. Second, while the paper states a computational complexity of O(μd²), identical to CMA-ES-Ref, a more in-depth discussion – perhaps employing Θ notation – is needed to explain the observed performance gains. Finally, the claim of diminishing path error contradicts established findings by Hansen, and requires experimental validation beyond a comparison of final performance metrics. A time-series analysis would be beneficial.

Minor points requiring attention include: inconsistencies in notation (specifically, *w<sub>k</sub>* in Algorithm 2 should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, and *n* in Table 1 should be *d*), and a typographical error ('F' should be 'f' on line 185).

Considering these issues, I do not believe this paper currently meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS.
