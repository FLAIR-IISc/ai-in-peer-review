This paper adopts Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of CMA-ES. Generally, the paper is clearly written. The proposed method seems reasonable, and the experimental results (especially the speed rates) are quite promising. I think the proposed method could become a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future. Although this paper may be worthwhile, originality is limited because I think this work is a trivial extension of previous work: a more efficient rank-one covariance matrix update for development strategies. The methods are essentially the same. Please correct me if I am wrong. Also, some very important material is strangely missing. 1) You should show whether your algorithm is equivalent to formula (2). I understand that space is limited and this has been proven in previous work, but it is still necessary for this paper. 2) You claim that the computational complexity is O(\mu d^2), which is similar to the CMA-ES-ref. Please discuss in detail (perhaps using '\Theta' notation instead of 'O') why your algorithm is faster. 3) You claim that the error in the path is small and decreases with time, which contradicts Hansen's claim. Since your theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be used to validate your claim. It is not enough to simply compare the final performance. Some minor comments: 1) Please check your notation carefully. For example, wk in algorithm 2 should be \alphak and n in table 1 should be d. 2) 'F' should be 'f' in line 185. Thus, I think this paper is not worthy of NIPS in its current form.
