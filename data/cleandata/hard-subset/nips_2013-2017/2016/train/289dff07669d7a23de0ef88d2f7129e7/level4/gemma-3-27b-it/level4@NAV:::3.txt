This paper investigates the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES), specifically extending the update procedure for the triangular Cholesky factor to incorporate both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors demonstrate, under established convergence assumptions for the covariance matrix, that the proposed modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm’s core behavior, and support these findings with experimental results. 

However, I find the contribution of this work to be incremental. While the paper presents a natural generalization of the rank-1 update to the triangular Cholesky factor – as previously explored by Krause et al. (2015) – to a rank-\mu update scheme, its novelty appears limited in light of existing literature. 

Regarding the theoretical foundations, the convergence analysis of the covariance matrix, *C<sub>t</sub>*, in CMA-ES is generally established through theoretical analysis (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), though not always directly based on the exact formulae of the algorithm. Referencing such work may strengthen the justification for Lemma 1.
