

The paper proposes a variant of CMA-ES termed "Cholesky-CMA-ES" with claimed "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity." The authors assert this modification preserves algorithmic behavior while reducing computational cost. However, the name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is misleading, as it was first introduced by Suttorp et al. (2009) for a distinct algorithm and is well-established in the literature. The authors fail to acknowledge this, instead introducing their variant as "Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES."  

Algorithmically, the contribution is minimal: it modifies Krause et al. (2015) by replacing a single rankOneUpdate call (present in Krause et al.) with a loop over all individuals. The empirical claim of improvement is questionable. Figure 1(b) shows differing plateau objective values for CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES on the Cigar function, which may stem from how median results are plotted. Crucially, the paper omits empirical comparison to Suttorp et al.'s Cholesky-CMA-ES and fails to clarify the relationship to Krause et al.'s work. The authors should compare against both variants and update all figures accordingly; if space constraints arise, they may present the best-performing variant in the main text and include full results in supplementary material.  

The claim of "optimal covariance update and storage complexity" is misleading. For separable functions (e.g., Sphere), the covariance matrix is unnecessary, and a diagonal approximation suffices—rendering the full covariance update suboptimal. While the proposed method is approximately two times cheaper than Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES, the paper incorrectly asserts Suttorp's variant is numerically unstable (the original paper claims the opposite). Omitting Suttorp's method in Figure 3 misrepresents the paper's contribution. Furthermore, the approach does not advance large-scale CMA-ES, as complexity remains quadratic. The modification is merely a practical optimization for full covariance scenarios, not a fundamental improvement.  

The work is overly incremental: the sole algorithmic change is the loop in Algorithm 1. I recommend:  
(a) Correct the naming to avoid confusion with Suttorp et al. (2009);  
(b) Conduct experiments against both Suttorp and Krause variants, updating all figures (with full results in supplementary material if needed);  
(c) Explicitly clarify that the core contribution is the loop in Algorithm 1, or justify why this is not merely a trivial adjustment.  

Minor note: The statement "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is inaccurate; proper implementations perform this periodically, not in every step.
