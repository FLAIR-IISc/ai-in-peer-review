- Summary: Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to replace full covariance eigendecomposition, reducing per-iteration cost from O(d^3) to O(mu d^2) and claiming asymptotic equivalence when the covariance converges.

- Strength: Clear, practical algorithmic idea that avoids repeated eigendecompositions.

- Strength: Significant computational savings observed with no loss of solution quality on tested benchmarks.

- Strength: Provides a plausible asymptotic argument explaining when the approximation matches CMA-ES.

- Strength: Empirical comparisons to reasonable baselines that also aim to reduce covariance-update costs.

- Limitation: Practical impact may be limited when function evaluations dominate runtime (i.e., optimizer overhead is negligible).

- Limitation: Theoretical argument depends on strong convergence assumptions, leaving transient/non-asymptotic behavior unclear.

- Limitation: Empirical suite is narrow and lacks controlled studies probing spectrum, dimension, and noise sensitivity.

- Limitation: Paper omits results for vanilla CMA-ES in the same runtime/performance framework, making absolute gains hard to quantify.

- Limitation: Numerical stability of repeated Cholesky updates/downdates over long runs is not fully addressed.

- Request 1: Track Et during optimization and report deviations (e.g., norms, principal angles, spectral radii) over time across functions and dimensions.

- Request 2: Include unmodified CMA-ES baseline with wall-clock times, iterations to target, per-iteration runtime breakdown, and memory usage.

- Request 3: Use a controlled quadratic testbed with varied spectra (condition numbers, eigenvalue decay, clusters) to study convergence and Et deviation.

- Request 4: Add a real-world or near-real-time application with low evaluation cost (e.g., robotics policy search) to demonstrate practical relevance.

- Request 5: Detail Cholesky update formulas and safeguards (jitter, refactorization), report refactorization frequency and its cost/impact.

- Request 6: Perform sensitivity analyses over mu and CMA-ES hyperparameters (c1, cmu, step-size) with ablations to show robustness and regimes of benefit.

- Request 7: Clarify scope of the asymptotic argument, discuss when Et can deviate in finite regimes, and provide diagnostics to detect approximation degradation.

- Presentation: State complexities and constants precisely, compare to other CMA-ES simplifications (limited-memory/diagonal), include concise pseudocode, and release reference code.

- Overall assessment: Compelling and well-motivated simplification with strong speed-ups in relevant regimes, but would be much stronger with broader empirical characterization and numerical-stability analysis.
