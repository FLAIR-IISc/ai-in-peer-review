- Paper proposes a novel covariance matrix update for CMA-ES using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- Key advantage: substantial reduction in computational complexity and memory vs. traditional CMA-ES.  
- Evaluation is rigorous and based on numerical experiments.  
- Results show comparable function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES while significantly reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Contribution is significant and likely to become a default for medium-to-large-scale problems due to lower memory, reduced complexity, and readily available covariance eigenvalues.  
- Paper is well-written, evaluation thorough, and appropriate for NIPS given ties to ML, information geometry, and RL/supervised learning.  
- Minor suggestion: clarify how implementation comparability was ensured (hardware/software, language, compiler versions, equivalent CPU environments).  
- Minor suggestion: explain apparent inconsistency in Figure 2 for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256 regarding the claim that higher dimensionality increases differences.
