- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates a triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix.  
- Reduces covariance update time complexity to O(μ d²) and storage to O(d²).  
- Replaces CSA’s requirement for the inverse matrix square root with the inverse triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Provides Lemma 1 arguing the approximation error vanishes as the covariance shape converges.  
- Empirical results on benchmarks show significant wall-clock speedups without increasing function evaluations.  
- Strength: Addresses a major computational bottleneck in CMA-ES with clear practical impact for high-dimensional problems.  
- Strength: Technically sound implementation using rank-one Cholesky updates and thorough empirical comparisons (including delayed-update baseline).  
- Strength: Well-written, well-organized paper with clear algorithms (Algorithm 1 and 2).  
- Weakness: Incremental novelty—extends prior rank-1 Cholesky updates (e.g., Krause & Igel 2015) to rank-μ via sequential rank-1 updates.  
- Weakness: Theoretical claim in Lemma 1 depends on assumed convergence of normalized covariance C̄t and would benefit from stronger links to CMA-ES convergence analysis (e.g., cite Beyer 2014).  
- Minor: Equation (1) and the related footnote denominator should be σt, not σ; typo "which can by be motivated" → "which can be motivated".
