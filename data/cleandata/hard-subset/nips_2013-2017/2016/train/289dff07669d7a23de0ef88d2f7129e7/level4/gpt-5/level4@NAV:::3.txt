This paper studies a Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy that applies both rank-1 and rank-μ updates to the triangular Cholesky factor, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. Under the assumption that the covariance converges, the authors show that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm’s behavior. The paper also reports experimental results on algorithm performance.

1. The work focuses on Cholesky CMA-ES and extends the triangular Cholesky-factor update to include both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. However, relative to Krause et al. (2015), which employs rank-1 updates to the triangular Cholesky factor, the contribution seems limited: the method is essentially a straightforward generalization to rank-μ via a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2. In general, convergence of the covariance C_t in CMA-ES has been established theoretically (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), though not from the exact CMA-ES equations. This may bolster confidence in Lemma 1.
