- Paper claims a novel "Cholesky-CMA-ES" but fails to cite Suttorp et al. (2009), which introduced the same name/approach—naming is misleading.  
- Authors present their variant as novel despite established prior work; citation/acknowledgement of Suttorp (2009) is missing.  
- Algorithmic contribution is incremental: essentially applying Krause et al.’s rankOneUpdate iteratively to all individuals (a single added "for" loop).  
- The presented method is not substantially different from Krause et al. (2015) algorithmically and closely follows existing techniques.  
- Empirical discrepancy: Figure 3(b) shows a different plateau on the Cigar function between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES; this may be due to plotting medians and needs investigation.  
- paper should empirically compare to Suttorp (2009) Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically to Krause (2015); include these comparisons in figures.  
- Claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading—it's not optimal for Sphere or separable functions (where covariance or diagonal suffices).  
- The proposed approach is only about twice as efficient as Suttorp et al. (2009)’s Cholesky-CMA-ES, not a fundamentally optimal method.  
- Authors’ claim that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable contradicts Suttorp et al.’s original claims and must be substantiated.  
- Omitting comparisons to the prior Cholesky variants risks misleading readers about the true novelty and contribution.  
- Complexity remains quadratic, so the method does not fundamentally change CMA-ES performance for large-scale optimization, though it may offer a good time-memory trade-off when full covariance is required.  
- Overall assessment: work is too incremental (essentially one added loop); authors should either justify why this is a significant contribution or admit it may not be substantial.  
- Required revisions suggested: (a) resolve naming/conflict; (b) run comparisons to Suttorp (2009) and Krause (2015) and update figures (or move full results to supplement); (c) explain/defend the significance of the "for" loop in Algorithm 1.  
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition in well-implemented CMA-ES is done periodically, not at every iteration—statement in paper is inaccurate.
