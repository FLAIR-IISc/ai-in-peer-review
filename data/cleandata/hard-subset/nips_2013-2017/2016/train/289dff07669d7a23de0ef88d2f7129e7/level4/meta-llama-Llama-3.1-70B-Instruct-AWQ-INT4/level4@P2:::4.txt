This paper presents a modification of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, a powerful real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm widely used in machine learning. The main contribution of this work is the utilization of triangular Cholesky factorization to replace the square root in the standard CMA-ES, which reduces the computational cost of calculating the inverse of matrices. The authors provide a theoretical justification for this approach, demonstrating that it does not deteriorate the algorithm's performance.

The proposed method, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, achieves a better asymptotic time complexity of O(µd2) for the covariance update and optimal memory complexity. The authors also show that the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same amount of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES but significantly less wall-clock time, with the speed-up increasing with the search space dimensionality.

The paper is well-organized and clearly written, making it easy to follow the authors' reasoning and methodology. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach, showing a significant increase in optimization speed compared to the state-of-the-art CMA-ES with delayed covariance matrix decomposition.

One potential area for improvement is the theoretical explanation for the "numerically more stable" computation achieved by the Cholesky-CMA-ES. While the authors provide some insights into this aspect, a more detailed analysis would strengthen the paper's contribution.

Another possible extension of this work is to combine the low-dimensional approximation [Loshchilov, 2015] with the existing framework. In many practical cases, such as training neural networks in direct policy search, the high dimensionality of the problems is a significant concern. Exploring ways to integrate the Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-dimensional approximations could further enhance its applicability and efficiency.

Strengths:

* The paper presents a novel modification of the CMA-ES algorithm, utilizing triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce computational costs.
* The authors provide a theoretical justification for their approach, demonstrating its effectiveness and efficiency.
* The experimental results show a significant increase in optimization speed compared to the state-of-the-art CMA-ES.

Weaknesses:

* The theoretical explanation for the "numerically more stable" computation achieved by the Cholesky-CMA-ES could be more detailed and thorough.
* The paper could benefit from exploring ways to integrate the Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-dimensional approximations to enhance its applicability and efficiency.
