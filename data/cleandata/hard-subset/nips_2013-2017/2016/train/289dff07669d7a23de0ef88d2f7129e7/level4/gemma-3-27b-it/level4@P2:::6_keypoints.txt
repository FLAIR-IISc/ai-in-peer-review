- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update/store triangular Cholesky factor instead of full covariance to reduce time and memory for CMA-ES.  
- Replaces matrix square root in CSA with inverse of triangular Cholesky factor to maintain performance.  
- Claims maintained algorithmic performance with improved efficiency, especially in high dimensions.  

Strengths:
- Potential for substantial runtime and memory savings in high-dimensional problems.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) arguing no negative impact on convergence and rotations.  
- Clear presentation and concise algorithm summary (Algorithm 1).  
- Empirical results show comparable function-evaluation performance to CMA-ES with significant runtime speedup.  

Weaknesses:
- The name “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with prior use by Suttorp et al. (2009) and may be misleading.  
- Contribution appears incremental—main idea extends existing rank-one Cholesky updates to all individuals.  
- Lacks direct empirical comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) and detailed algorithmic comparison to Krause & Igel (2015).  
- Claim of “optimal covariance update and storage complexity” is overstated and conditional on needing a full covariance matrix.  
- Inconsistency in results/presentation (e.g., unexplained plateau difference in Figure 1(b)) and possible masking by median plots.  
- Minor inaccuracy: eigenvalue decomposition is not typically performed at every update in standard implementations.  

Arguments for acceptance:
- Practical, impactful modification addressing a known CMA-ES bottleneck.  
- Theoretical analysis supports the approach’s effectiveness.  
- Empirical evidence shows significant runtime improvements without losing performance.  
- Paper is well written and tackles a relevant optimization problem.  

Arguments against acceptance:
- Algorithmic novelty is limited and may be viewed as incremental.  
- Naming may cause confusion with existing literature.  
- Incomplete comparison to closely related prior work.  
- Optimality claims are potentially misleading.  

Overall assessment:
- Worthwhile but incremental improvement; recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address naming, broader comparisons, and clarify optimality and experimental presentation.
