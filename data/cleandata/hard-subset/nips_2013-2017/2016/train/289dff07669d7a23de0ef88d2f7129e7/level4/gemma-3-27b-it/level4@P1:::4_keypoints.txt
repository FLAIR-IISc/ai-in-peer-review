- Problem addressed: CMA-ES covariance matrix update and storage dominate time and space complexity.  
- Proposed solution: maintain the triangular Cholesky factor for numerically stable, quadratic-time covariance updates with reduced memory.  
- Required algorithm change: modify CSA by replacing the inverse square root of the covariance matrix with the inverse Cholesky factor.  
- Theoretical claim: Lemma on convergence of coordinate system rotations justifies that the modification does not alter convergence behavior.  
- Empirical results: experiments show consistent function-evaluation counts but significant wall-clock speedups, especially in higher dimensions (Figures 1–3).  
- Comparisons: thorough benchmarks against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES demonstrate the method’s effectiveness.  
- Literature positioning: situates work relative to delayed updates, model-complexity restrictions, and prior Cholesky-based attempts, noting their limitations for standard CMA-ES.  
- Strength — technical soundness: method is well-motivated, theoretically justified, and empirically validated.  
- Strength — practical impact: substantial runtime/memory improvements make CMA-ES more applicable to large-scale optimization.  
- Strength — presentation: clear writing and explanation of method and benefits.  
- Weakness — numerical stability: claim of being “numerically more stable” needs a more explicit explanation in the CMA-ES context.  
- Weakness — high-dimensional scaling: discussion of limitations and integration with low-rank or approximation techniques for very high dimensions is brief and could be expanded.  
- Acceptance recommendation: overall a strong, practical contribution; reviewer recommends acceptance.
