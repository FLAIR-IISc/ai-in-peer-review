This study's primary contribution lies in the application of triangular Cholesky factorization, which eliminates the need for the square root operation in traditional CMA-ES, thereby reducing computational costs associated with matrix inversion. The author presents a theoretically grounded approach, demonstrating that this modification does not compromise the algorithm's performance. The proposed method achieves a lower runtime complexity without significantly impacting the number of objective function evaluations. This paper provides a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES. 

A more in-depth theoretical explanation for the 'numerically more stable' computation achieved by this approach would be beneficial. It appears that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires a comparable number of objective function evaluations to standard CMA-ES, while reducing wall-clock time, with this advantage increasing as the search space dimensionality grows. The author may consider integrating the low-dimensional approximation proposed by Loshchilov (2015) into the existing framework, as high-dimensionality is a critical concern in many practical applications, such as training neural networks in direct policy search.
