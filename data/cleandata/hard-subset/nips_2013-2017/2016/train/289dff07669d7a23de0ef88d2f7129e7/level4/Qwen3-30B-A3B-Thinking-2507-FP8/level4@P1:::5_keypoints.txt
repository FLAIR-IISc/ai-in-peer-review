- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square-root with lower-triangular Cholesky factorization, reducing per-iteration complexity from O(d³) to O(μ d²) (μ typically O(log d)).  
- Maintains and updates the Cholesky factor directly, avoiding explicit covariance storage and square-root computations.  
- Argues the approximation Cₜ⁻¹/² ≈ Aₜ⁻¹ does not degrade performance, with theoretical justification via Lemma 1 showing the orthogonal correction Eₜ → I as covariance stabilizes.  
- Empirical results on standard benchmarks show equivalent function-evaluation counts to CMA-ES-Ref but much faster wall-clock times (up to 20× speedup at d=64).  
- Practical advantages include optimal storage complexity, free eigenvalue extraction for conditioning monitoring, and numerically stable inversion.  
- Strength — Theoretical rigor: Lemma 1 fills a gap in prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work by justifying performance equivalence.  
- Strength — Practical impact: Achieves asymptotically optimal O(μ d²) complexity, addressing a key CMA-ES scalability bottleneck.  
- Strength — Clear presentation: Well-structured pseudocode, complexity analysis, and comparisons to strong baselines (CMA-ES-Ref, Suttorp-CMA-ES).  
- Weakness/consideration — Practical relevance: Speedups matter mainly when function evaluations are cheap and d is large (e.g., d > 64); many CMA-ES applications have smaller d or expensive evaluations.  
- Suggestion: Better contextualize which real-world scenarios benefit most (e.g., large-scale problems with fast evaluations).  
- Weakness — Empirical depth: Eₜ evolution results are relegated to the supplement and should be integrated into the main text for stronger evidence.  
- Experimental recommendations: include ∥Eₜ − I∥ plots for key trajectories (e.g., Rosenbrock d=128), extend quadratic tests varying spectral condition number, and compare against non-delayed standard CMA-ES to isolate Cholesky effects.  
- Overall assessment: Technically solid, novel, and impactful contribution that meaningfully improves CMA-ES scalability and meets conference standards.  
- Recommendation: Accept; suggested additions are constructive refinements rather than critical flaws.
