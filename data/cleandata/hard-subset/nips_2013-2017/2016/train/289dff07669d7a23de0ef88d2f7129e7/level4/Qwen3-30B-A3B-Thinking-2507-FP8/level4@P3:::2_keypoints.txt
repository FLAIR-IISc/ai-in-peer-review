- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factors to reduce time and storage.  
- Claims O(µd²) time complexity for covariance updates vs O(d³) for standard CMA-ES.  
- Theoretical claim: error from using Cholesky instead of matrix square root diminishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical claim: up to 20× speedup for d=64 with no increase in required function evaluations.  
- Builds on Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015); extends Cholesky approach to standard CMA-ES with added theoretical justification.  
- Strength: strong practical significance for large-scale optimization and clear integration/implementation details.  
- Strength: convincing experimental speedups across multiple benchmark functions and increasing gains with dimension.  
- Strength: provides a theoretical argument addressing concerns about the introduced approximation error.  
- Weakness: limited originality—method closely follows prior work and does not clearly distinguish its novelty.  
- Weakness: complexity analysis is insufficiently precise—needs Θ(µd²) and discussion of constant factors.  
- Weakness: lacks experimental validation of the key theoretical claim about the error trajectory (no plots of Et or path error over iterations).  
- Weakness: minor but important notational inconsistencies (Algorithm 2: "wk" vs "αk"; Table 1: "n" vs "d").  
- Overall assessment: practical and promising but insufficient novelty and insufficient theoretical/experimental validation—recommend rejection in current form.  
- Revision suggestions: explicitly differentiate from prior work, provide precise complexity (Θ and constants), and include experiments showing error diminishing over time.
