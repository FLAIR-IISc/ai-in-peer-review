The Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) stands as a leading probabilistic algorithm for derivative-free optimization. The method operates by adapting the parameters of a multivariate Gaussian sampling distribution, a process that can also be interpreted as learning an underlying problem metric. This work introduces a novel formulation for the covariance matrix update, leveraging its triangular Cholesky factors. Compared to existing approaches, this method offers the distinct advantages of substantially decreasing the computational cost of the update step and reducing the algorithm's overall memory footprint.

The proposed method is thoroughly benchmarked. The results demonstrate that this new update scheme achieves a comparable convergence rate (in terms of function evaluations) to the standard CMA-ES, while markedly reducing the algorithm's wall-clock time, with the speed-up being most pronounced in high-dimensional settings. In my view, this paper represents a substantial and valuable contribution to the field of optimization. I anticipate that this variant has the potential to become the standard implementation of CMA-ES for medium- to large-scale problems, given its clear benefits over the conventional approach, including a smaller memory requirement, lower computational complexity, and the added advantage of providing the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no extra cost.

The manuscript is well-written and the methodology is rigorously validated. This contribution is particularly relevant for the NeurIPS community, considering the strong connections of CMA-ES to machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in areas such as reinforcement and supervised learning.
