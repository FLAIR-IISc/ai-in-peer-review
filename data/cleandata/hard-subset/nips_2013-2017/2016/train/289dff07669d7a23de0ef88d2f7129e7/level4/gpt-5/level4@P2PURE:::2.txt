This paper replaces the eigen-decomposition with a Cholesky decomposition to reduce the time and memory costs of CMA-ES. Overall, the paper is clearly written. The method appears reasonable, and the experimental results—especially the reported speedups—are promising. I can imagine this approach becoming a new standard variant of CMA-ES. That said, the originality seems limited: this work appears to be a trivial extension of the prior paper “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies.” The methods look essentially the same; please correct me if I am mistaken.

In addition, several important elements are missing:
1) You should demonstrate that your algorithm is equivalent to Equation (2). I realize space is limited and this was proven in prior work, but establishing this equivalence is still essential here.
2) You state the computational complexity is O(mu d^2), which is the same as CMA-ES-Ref. Please provide a detailed discussion—ideally using Theta notation rather than big-O—clarifying why your algorithm is actually faster.
3) You claim the error in the path is small and decreases over time, which contradicts Hansen’s claim. Since your theoretical argument is not deterministic, you should include experiments specifically designed to validate this claim. Comparing only final performance is not sufficient.

Minor comments:
1) Please check your notation carefully. For example, wk in Algorithm 2 should be alpha_k, and n in Table 1 should be d.
2) “F” should be “f” on line 185.

In its current form, I do not believe this paper meets the standard for NIPS.
