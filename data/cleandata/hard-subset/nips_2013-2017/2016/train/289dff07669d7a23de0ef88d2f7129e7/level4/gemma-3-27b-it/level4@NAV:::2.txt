This paper presents an interesting modification to CMA-ES, leveraging Cholesky decomposition in place of eigen-decomposition to address computational time and memory usage. The writing is clear, the proposed approach appears sound, and the experimental results, particularly the reported speedup, are encouraging. This work has the potential to establish a new, widely adopted variant of CMA-ES. 

However, the novelty of this contribution feels somewhat incremental. The core methodology closely resembles that of “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,” and appears to be a direct application of that work’s principles. I would appreciate clarification on this point. 

Furthermore, several crucial elements appear to be missing or insufficiently addressed. First, demonstrating the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2) – even with acknowledgement of prior proofs – is vital for completeness. Second, while the paper states a computational complexity of O(μd<sup>2</sup>), mirroring that of CMA-ES-Ref, a more detailed justification for the observed speedup is necessary. Utilizing Θ-notation might provide a more precise comparison. Finally, the claim of a diminishing path error, contrasting with established findings by Hansen, requires robust experimental validation beyond final performance metrics; an analysis of the error’s evolution over time is needed, given the non-deterministic nature of the theoretical analysis.

Several minor points require attention: Algorithm 2 uses ‘wk’ where ‘αk’ is likely intended, and ‘n’ in Table 1 should be ‘d’. Additionally, a typographical error exists on line 185, where ‘F’ should be ‘f’.

Based on these concerns, I do not believe this paper, in its current form, meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS.
