The primary contribution of this work is the application of triangular Cholesky factorization to the CMA-ES algorithm. This technique circumvents the matrix square root operation required by the standard method, thereby decreasing the computational cost of matrix inversions. The authors supplement their approach with a theoretical justification demonstrating that the algorithm's performance is not compromised. The proposed method successfully reduces runtime complexity while maintaining a comparable number of objective function evaluations. This paper presents a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization technique for CMA-ES.

For further clarification, could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical explanation as to how the method achieves greater numerical stability?

Additionally, the results indicate that the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires a similar number of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES, while reducing wall-clock time, particularly as search space dimensionality grows. In this context, have the authors considered integrating their framework with low-dimensional approximation methods, such as [Loshchilov, 2015]? Such a combination could be highly relevant for practical, high-dimensional problems, like training neural networks in direct policy search, where dimensionality is a significant concern.
