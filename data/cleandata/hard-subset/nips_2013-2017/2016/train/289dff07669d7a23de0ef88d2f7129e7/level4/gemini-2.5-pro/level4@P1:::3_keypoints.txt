- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the triangular Cholesky factor directly, reducing matrix-update complexity from O(d³) to O(µd²) and lowering memory use.  
- Replaces the inverse matrix-square-root in CSA with the inverse Cholesky factor and argues this preserves algorithm behavior as covariance converges.  
- Theoretical Lemma 1 claims the error from this approximation diminishes as the covariance matrix converges.  
- Empirical results show substantial wall-clock speedups over standard CMA-ES across benchmarks and dimensions, without increasing function-evaluations to solution.  
- Strength: Addresses a practical bottleneck, making CMA-ES more viable for higher-dimensional problems when evaluations are cheap.  
- Strength: Provides principled theoretical justification (not just heuristic) for the CSA modification.  
- Strength: Thorough experiments convincingly support runtime gains and maintained optimization performance.  
- Weakness: Perceived as incremental—generalizes Krause & Igel (2015) rank-1 Cholesky update to rank-µ via sequential rank-1 updates; paper should better articulate technical challenges and novelty.  
- Weakness: Lemma 1 relies on the covariance normalization convergence assumption; this needs stronger theoretical support or citations (e.g., Beyer 2014).  
- Minor: Notation inconsistency—σ in Equation (1) and footnote 1 should be σt to match iteration-indexed variables.
