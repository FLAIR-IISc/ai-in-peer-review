The paper introduces a modification to CMA-ES, termed “Cholesky-CMA-ES,” aiming to reduce computational cost without altering the algorithm’s fundamental behavior. However, the naming choice is problematic, as “Cholesky-CMA-ES” was previously used by Suttorp et al. (2009) for a related method, a connection the authors do not acknowledge. The presented work builds incrementally upon Krause et al. (2015), with the primary algorithmic contribution being the iterative application of the `rankOneUpdate` function to all individuals – a function already present in Krause et al.’s work. 

Empirical results reveal a discrepancy in the plateau observed for the Cigar function (Figure b) between the reference CMA-ES and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES, potentially stemming from the presentation of median results. A crucial comparison is missing: the paper should include a thorough empirical evaluation against Suttorp et al.’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and a detailed algorithmic comparison to Krause et al.’s approach. 

The claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is overstated. While beneficial in scenarios requiring the full covariance matrix, it’s not universally optimal; for instance, the Sphere function and separable problems may only necessitate diagonal matrix representations. Furthermore, the proposed method offers only a two-fold cost reduction compared to Suttorp et al.’s Cholesky CMA-ES, which the authors claim is numerically unstable – a claim that contradicts the original publication. The omission of Suttorp et al.’s method from Figure 3 further obscures the true contribution of this work.

Despite the quadratic complexity remaining unchanged, potentially limiting impact on large-scale optimization, the modification may represent a practical improvement for scenarios demanding full covariance matrix maintenance. Overall, the algorithmic contribution appears limited – essentially an additional “for” loop. 

I recommend the following revisions: a) Address the naming conflict; b) Include comparative experiments with both Suttorp et al.’s and Krause et al.’s variants, updating Figures 1-3 accordingly (or moving comprehensive figures to Supplementary Material if readability suffers); c) Clearly articulate the significance of the “for” loop in Algorithm 1, or justify why it shouldn’t be considered the core algorithmic contribution. Finally, the statement “Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step” is inaccurate; in standard implementations, eigenvalue decomposition is performed periodically, not in every iteration.
