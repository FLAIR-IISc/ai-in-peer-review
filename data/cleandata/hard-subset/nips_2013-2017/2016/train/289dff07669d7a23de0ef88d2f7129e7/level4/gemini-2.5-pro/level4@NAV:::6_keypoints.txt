- The name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and the manuscript fails to acknowledge that prior work.  
- The method appears to be an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015), mainly by applying rankOneUpdate inside a loop over individuals.  
- The core algorithmic novelty seems to reduce to adding a single "for" loop in Algorithm 1; the authors should explicitly clarify this.  
- Figure b) shows a discrepancy on the Cigar function (different plateau objective values) that may be a plotting artifact but requires explanation.  
- The paper lacks direct empirical comparisons to both the Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. variants, weakening the experimental evaluation.  
- The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is overstated and does not hold for cases like Sphere or separable functions where full covariance is unnecessary.  
- Reported efficiency gains are modest (roughly 2× over Suttorp et al.); omitting that baseline risks misrepresenting the contribution.  
- The authors’ justification for omitting Suttorp et al. (numerical instability) contradicts the original 2009 claims and is not substantiated here.  
- The algorithm remains quadratic in complexity, so the impact on large-scale optimization and scalability is limited.  
- Overall contribution is overly incremental rather than a substantial advancement.  
- Recommended revisions: resolve the naming conflict; include comprehensive experiments comparing to Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. (with figures or supplementary material); and clearly state or better justify the core algorithmic contribution.  
- Minor note: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically in standard implementations, not at every iteration as stated.
