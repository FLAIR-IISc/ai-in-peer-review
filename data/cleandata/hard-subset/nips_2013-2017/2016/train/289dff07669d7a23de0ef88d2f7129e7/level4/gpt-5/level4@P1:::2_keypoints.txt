- Proposes replacing eigen/square-root decomposition in CMA-ES with a directly updated triangular Cholesky factor A, sampling x = A y + m and applying rank-one updates to A for rank-µ covariance updates.  
- Claims O(µ d^2) time per iteration, minimal memory, avoids C^{-1/2} in CSA by using A^{-1}, and reports similar evaluation counts with large wall-clock speedups.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky and rank-one update work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and extends to standard rank-µ CMA-ES with a modified CSA.  

Strengths:
- Addresses an important CMA-ES bottleneck and reports impressive runtime speedups.  
- High-level presentation is clear; algorithm is simple to implement and reduces memory usage.  
- Triangular factor yields eigenvalues cheaply and a numerically stable A^{-1} in O(d^2).  
- Empirics indicate no loss in evaluation efficiency and strong runtime gains in high dimensions.  

Weaknesses / requested clarifications:
- Originality: novelty unclear beyond extending µ=1 to general µ and proposing a CSA approximation; authors should explicitly state technical differences from Krause & Igel (2015).  
- Equivalence proof: must show (or cite) that sequential rank-one updates to A reconstruct the same C_{t+1} as Eq. (2), with a concise theorem or supplement derivation.  
- Complexity: replace O-claims with Θ-notation, provide amortized analysis of the reference CMA-ES (including EVD/sqrt costs and frequency), and explain practical gains (constant factors, cache, removing cubic EVDs, etc.).  
- Memory: quantify memory complexity (triangular A vs storing C, B, D) and discuss cache effects for large d.  
- CSA approximation: current analysis is non-deterministic; add diagnostics and experiments directly assessing CSA proxy (||p_σ||/E{χ}, σ_t trajectories, rotation drift ∥E_t E_{t−1}^T − I∥) across transient and near-converged phases and on hard functions.  
- Present key rotation/CSA validation plots in main text, not only supplement.  

Additional clarity/correctness points:
- Notation/typos: fix Algorithm 2 (w_k → α_k), Table 1 (n → d), “F” → “f”, and inconsistent decomposition names (QR/RQ/polar).  
- Numerical stability: discuss need for periodic reconditioning of A under repeated rank-one updates vs reorthogonalization in reference implementation.  
- Invariance: clarify which invariances are retained or lost with the CSA approximation and support claims with rotation experiments.  
- “Optimal” complexity claim: formalize lower bound (e.g., processing µ rank-one terms implies Θ(µ d^2)) or cite supporting results.  

Significance:
- If equivalence, rigorous CSA validation, and precise complexity analysis are provided, approach could become a practical default for full CMA-ES with substantial runtime benefits.  

Pros for acceptance:
- Tackles key bottleneck, simple and memory-efficient, provides eigenvalues and A^{-1}, applicable to ill-conditioned/non-separable problems.  

Cons for acceptance:
- Limited novelty vs prior Cholesky-update work, missing equivalence proof, insufficient CSA validation, incomplete complexity amortized analysis, and notational/clarity errors.  

Recommendation:
- Promising but not ready; reviewer leans toward rejection until proofs, CSA diagnostics/theory, and precise complexity analysis are added.
