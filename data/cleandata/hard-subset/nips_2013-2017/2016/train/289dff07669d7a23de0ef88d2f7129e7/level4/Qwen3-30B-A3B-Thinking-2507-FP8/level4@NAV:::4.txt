

The primary contribution of this work lies in employing triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate square roots in the standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing computational cost during matrix inversion. The authors provide a theoretically sound justification demonstrating that this approach does not compromise algorithmic performance. The proposed method reduces runtime complexity without increasing the number of objective function evaluations. This constitutes a valuable adaptation of Cholesky factorization for CMA-ES. The authors should further clarify theoretically how the "numerically more stable" computation is achieved. Additionally, it is noted that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES while reducing wall-clock time—this time reduction becomes more pronounced with increasing search space dimensionality. Could the authors explore integrating the low-dimensional approximation technique [Loshchilov, 2015] into their framework, particularly given that high-dimensional problems remain a critical challenge in practical applications such as direct policy search for neural network training?
