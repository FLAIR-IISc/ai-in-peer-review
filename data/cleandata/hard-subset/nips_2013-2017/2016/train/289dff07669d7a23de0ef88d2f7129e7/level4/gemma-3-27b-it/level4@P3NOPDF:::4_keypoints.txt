- Core contribution: use triangular Cholesky factorization in CMA-ES to eliminate square-root computations when inverting the covariance matrix, reducing computational cost.
- Theoretical justification: authors show equivalence between the original and proposed methods.
- Integration: demonstrates how Cholesky factorization can be incorporated into standard CMA-ES without harming optimization behavior.
- Empirical results: achieves comparable objective function evaluations to standard CMA-ES while reducing runtime, with larger gains in higher dimensions.
- Practical significance: reduces the common matrix-inversion bottleneck in high-dimensional optimization.
- Presentation: paper is clearly written; theoretical analysis and empirical validation are convincing.
- Numerical stability claim: authors state improved stability but do not fully explain why this occurs.
- Request for clarification: recommend elaborating on numerical properties (e.g., condition numbers or eigenvalue distributions) to justify stability claims.
- Scalability suggestion: discuss combining Cholesky-CMA-ES with dimensionality reduction/low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loschilov 2015) for very high-dimensional problems.
- Overall assessment: strong, practical, and theoretically sound work; recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address numerical stability and scalability discussion.
