- Proposes a novel derivative-free variant of CMA-ES that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution via multiple learning mechanisms.  
- Introduces an innovative covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors, lowering computational complexity and memory use.  
- Empirical results show similar function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Provides free access to covariance eigenvalues without extra cost.  
- Claims high significance for medium-to-large-scale applications and potential to become the default choice.  
- Paper is well-written and methodology is carefully assessed.  
- Strong relevance to machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Minor request: clarify how CPU comparability across implementations is ensured and state the implementation language.  
- Minor issue: Figure 2’s claim that differences increase with dimensionality is unclear for the Cigar and Discus functions at 256D and needs explanation.
