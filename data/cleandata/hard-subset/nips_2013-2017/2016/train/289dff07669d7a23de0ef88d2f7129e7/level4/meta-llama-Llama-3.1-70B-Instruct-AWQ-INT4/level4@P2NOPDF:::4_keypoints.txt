- Uses triangular Cholesky factorization to remove square-root operations in CMA-ES, reducing matrix inversion costs.
- Theoretically justified method that preserves algorithm performance while lowering runtime complexity.
- Implementation of Cholesky-based CMA-ES is provided and valuable.
- Requests a more detailed theoretical explanation for the claimed "numerically more stable" computation.
- Empirical results show equal objective-function evaluations to standard CMA-ES but reduced wall-clock time, especially in higher dimensions.
- Suggests combining this approach with Loshchilov (2015) low-dimensional approximation to better address high-dimensional problems (e.g., neural network policy search).
