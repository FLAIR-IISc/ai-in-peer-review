- Proposes updating the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix to reduce time and space complexity of CMA-ES.  
- Claims preservation of CMA-ES theoretical convergence while offering significant runtime improvements, especially in high dimensions.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and situates the work within existing CMA-ES literature.  
- Theoretical justification provided via Lemma 1 on convergence of the Cholesky factor.  
- Empirical results show large speedups in runtime with comparable objective-function evaluation performance.  
- Practical impact: readily implementable improvement useful for large-scale ML tasks (reinforcement learning, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Thorough analysis of computational complexity and memory usage, with experimental validation on standard benchmarks.  
- Authors acknowledge limitations such as quadratic scaling in dimension and added complexity in extremely high dimensions.  
- Originality is incremental—builds directly on prior work (Krause & Igel 2015; Suttorp et al. 2009) without fundamental changes to CMA-ES.  
- Needs clearer comparison and contrast with Krause & Igel [2015] and Suttorp et al. [2009], highlighting unique advantages and theory.  
- Should clarify relationship to the rank-one update in Krause & Igel and demonstrate equivalence to formula (2).  
- Runtime improvement needs more rigorous theoretical analysis (Θ-notation) beyond empirical evidence.  
- The claimed small error in step-size adaptation approximation requires stronger experimental support.  
- Investigate and report behavior of the rotation matrix Et over iterations and compare convergence paths between Cholesky-CMA-ES and standard CMA-ES.  
- Overall: solid, practically relevant contribution but would be strengthened by addressing originality, comparisons to prior work, and additional experimental/theoretical validation.
