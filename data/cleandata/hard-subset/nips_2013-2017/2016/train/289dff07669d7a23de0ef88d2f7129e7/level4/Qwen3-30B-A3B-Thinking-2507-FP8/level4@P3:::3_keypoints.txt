- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce time/space complexity.
- Achieves optimal asymptotic complexity: O(μ d²) for covariance updates and O(d²) storage.
- Extends prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES methods (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) to full rank-μ updates in standard CMA-ES.
- Lemma 1: shows the error from using the Cholesky factor vanishes as the algorithm converges, addressing approximation concerns.
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in objective evaluations across benchmarks (Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) and dimensions d=4…256.
- Reports significant runtime improvements (up to 20× faster for d=64), with speedup growing with dimensionality.
- Provides clear implementation details and releases code to support reproducibility.
- Strength: technically sound contribution with rigorous theoretical analysis directly addressing core concerns.
- Strength: comprehensive empirical evaluation across multiple variants, functions, and dimensions.
- Weakness: novelty is incremental — a natural generalization of Krause & Igel (2015) rather than a major conceptual advance.
- Weakness: theoretical justification for covariance convergence could be strengthened by connecting to existing results (e.g., Beyer 2014).
- Weakness: contains minor technical errors to fix (Equation (1) and footnote 2 should use σ_t in denominator; grammatical error “can by be” → “can be”).
- Weakness: discussion of practical limitations is incomplete — lacks comparison to other high-dimensional methods (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) and cache effects not fully explored.
- Clarity: generally well-written and precise, though some theoretical details could be presented more accessibly.
- Overall assessment: solid technical and practical contribution with strong empirical support but limited novelty; recommendation: Weak Accept.
