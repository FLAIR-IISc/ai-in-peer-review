- Presents Cholesky-CMA-ES: updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to approximate CMA-ES, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).
- Core idea: maintain/update Cholesky factor instead of full covariance and its square root.
- Main difference from original CMA-ES: avoids estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et at each step.
- Provides an asymptotic argument (under restrictive convergence assumptions) that Et → I, making the two schemes equivalent.
- Demonstrates validity and efficiency with runtime and convergence plots on standard benchmarks.
- Compares favorably to two state-of-the-art delayed-covariance-update CMA-ES variants, achieving substantial speed-ups without loss of optimization performance on synthetic tests.
- Notes practical caveat: objective-function (oracle) runtime often dominates, so CMA-ES runtime is usually negligible and speed-ups matter mainly when evaluations are very fast and dimension is large.
- Observes that typical CMA-ES applications have dimensions of only a few tens, limiting practical impact of the speed-up.
- Recommends additional empirical evidence by visualizing the evolution of Et along optimization trajectories (e.g., in different norms) and comparing full CMA-ES and the approximation simultaneously.
- Suggests including raw runtimes and performance of CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.
- Proposes a simulation study on quadratic functions with continuously varied spectral distributions (e.g., Stich & Müller PPSN 2012) to assess solution quality and Et error versus spectrum.
- Recommends a real-world experiment (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) where speed matters and parameter dimensionality is moderate.
