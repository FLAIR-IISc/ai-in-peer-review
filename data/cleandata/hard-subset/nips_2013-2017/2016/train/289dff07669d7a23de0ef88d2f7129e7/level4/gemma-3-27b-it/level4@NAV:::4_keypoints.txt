- Introduces triangular Cholesky factorization into CMA-ES.  
- Removes the need for square-root calculations present in standard CMA-ES.  
- Lowers computational cost for matrix inversion, reducing runtime complexity.  
- Provides theoretical rationale that the modification does not harm algorithm performance.  
- Demonstrably reduces wall-clock time without substantially increasing objective-function evaluations.  
- Claim of “numerically more stable” computation requires further theoretical elaboration.  
- Wall-clock time benefits become more pronounced as search-space dimensionality increases.  
- Suggests investigating combination with low-dimensional approximation methods (e.g., Loschilov 2015) for high-dimensional applications like neural-net training and direct policy search.
