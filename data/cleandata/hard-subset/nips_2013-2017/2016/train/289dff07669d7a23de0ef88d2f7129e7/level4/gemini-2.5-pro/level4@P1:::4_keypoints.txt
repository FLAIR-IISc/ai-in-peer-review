- Replaces the O(d^3) covariance square-root in CMA-ES with an O(d^2) update on the triangular Cholesky factor (Cholesky-CMA-ES).  
- Modifies cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) to operate with the Cholesky factor instead of the inverse matrix square root.  
- Theoretical justification (Lemma 1): rotational error from the approximation diminishes as the covariance converges.  
- Empirical results show large wall-clock time reductions, especially at high dimensions, without increasing function evaluations.  
- Cuts per-iteration time and space complexity, making full-covariance CMA-ES practical for larger problems.  
- Practical significance for applications like direct policy search in RL and hyperparameter tuning.  
- Strength: sound theoretical foundation supporting the approximation.  
- Strength: compelling empirical validation of efficiency and retained optimization performance.  
- Request for clarification: explain why updating a single triangular Cholesky factor yields better numerical stability (theoretical or intuitive rationale).  
- Question: discuss potential to combine the Cholesky update approach with limited-memory methods (e.g., LM-CMA) for very large-scale problems.  
- Overall: a well-motivated, theoretically grounded, empirically validated improvement likely to become the standard when covariance updates are the bottleneck.
