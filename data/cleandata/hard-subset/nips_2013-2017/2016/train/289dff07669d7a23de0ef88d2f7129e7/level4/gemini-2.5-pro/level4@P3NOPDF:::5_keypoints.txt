- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor directly to reduce per-iteration complexity from O(d^3) to O(μd^2).

- Omits estimating an orthogonal matrix each step and provides an asymptotic argument that this matrix converges to identity under restrictive assumptions.

- Validates speed-up and comparable optimization performance on synthetic benchmark functions against two accelerated CMA-ES variants.

- Strength: well-motivated, elegant technical idea addressing computational bottlenecks in CMA-ES.

- Strength: achieves considerable runtime speed-up on presented tests without apparent loss in optimization performance.

- Weakness: practical relevance is unclear because objective evaluation often dominates optimization time except for very fast evaluations and high dimensions.

- Suggestion: empirically validate the core assumption by measuring the evolution of E_t (e.g., Frobenius norm of E_t − I) alongside standard CMA-ES.

- Suggestion: include the standard (non-accelerated) CMA-ES as a baseline for runtime and performance comparisons.

- Suggestion: run systematic experiments on quadratic functions with varying conditioning to analyze dependence on problem structure.

- Suggestion: demonstrate real-world applicability with a use case where fast evaluations and moderate-to-high dimensionality make internal CMA-ES runtime a bottleneck (e.g., real-time policy optimization in robotics).

- Overall: promising and technically sound contribution, but needs stronger empirical validation and justification of practical impact.
