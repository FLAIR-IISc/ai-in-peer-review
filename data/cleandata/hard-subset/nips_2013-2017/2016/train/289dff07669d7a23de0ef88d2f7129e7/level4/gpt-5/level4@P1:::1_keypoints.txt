- Paper proposes replacing CMA-ES’s full covariance update with updates on a triangular Cholesky factor and using its inverse in CSA.  
- This change yields a numerically stable, quadratic-time update with minimal memory footprint.  
- The method matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially at higher dimensions.  
- The approach is theoretically motivated via RQ/orthogonal decompositions, arguing the CSA modification introduces only a small, vanishing error.  
- Achieves O(µ d^2) time per iteration and optimal storage; eigenvalues can be read from the triangular factor diagonal.  
- Efficient quadratic-time inversion of the triangular factor is a practical benefit.  
- Empirical evaluation is thorough with fair baselines: reference CMA-ES with delayed decompositions, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES.  
- Experiments on rotated benchmarks show equal evaluation efficiency and growing runtime gains with dimension.  
- Paper is clearly written, well organized, and presents algorithmic details and error behavior convincingly.  
- Originality: makes triangular-factor updates compatible with standard CMA-ES via a principled CSA modification; analysis of induced rotation is novel.  
- Weakness: CSA approximation lacks non-asymptotic (formal) bounds on evaluation overhead; support is empirical and asymptotic.  
- Weakness/question: Rosenbrock trials that failed to converge were excluded—frequency of exclusion and impact on comparisons should be clarified.  
- Experimental details (hardware, language, compiler, BLAS/LAPACK, threading) need clarification to strengthen runtime claims.  
- Minor suggestion: clarify Figure 2’s dimensionality effects (e.g., cache-size impacts) and quantify if cache effects explain anomalies at d=256.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical contribution likely to become a default choice for medium-to-large problems; reviewer recommends acceptance.
