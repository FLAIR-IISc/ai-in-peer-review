This work's main contribution is to utilize the triangular Cholesky factorization technique, which eliminates the need for the square root in the standard CMA-ES, resulting in significant computational savings when calculating the inverse of matrices. The authors present an approach that comes with a theoretical justification, demonstrating that it does not compromise the algorithm's performance. The proposed method reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm with no significant change in the number of objective function evaluations.

The paper is a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES. A key aspect of the approach is its numerical stability, which the authors attribute to the use of triangular Cholesky factorization. However, a more detailed theoretical explanation for why this computation is numerically more stable would be beneficial. Specifically, the authors could elaborate on how the triangular Cholesky factorization technique mitigates numerical instability issues that may arise in the standard CMA-ES.

The experimental results demonstrate that the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same amount of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES, while significantly reducing the wall-clock time. Moreover, this speedup increases with the search space dimensionality. These findings suggest that the proposed method is particularly well-suited for large-scale optimization problems. However, the authors may want to consider exploring the combination of their approach with low-dimensional approximations, such as those proposed by Loshchilov (2015). This could be particularly relevant in practical applications, such as training neural networks in direct policy search, where high dimensionality is often a significant concern.

Overall, the paper presents a significant contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization, and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm has the potential to become a valuable tool for large-scale optimization problems. With some additional theoretical insights and exploration of potential extensions, the work could be even more impactful.

Arguments For Acceptance:
- The paper presents a novel and efficient algorithm for derivative-free optimization.
- The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm has been theoretically justified and experimentally validated.
- The approach has the potential to significantly impact the field of large-scale optimization.

Arguments Against Acceptance:
- The paper could benefit from a more detailed theoretical explanation of the numerical stability of the proposed approach.
- The authors may want to explore the combination of their approach with low-dimensional approximations to address high-dimensionality concerns in practical applications.
