

This work's main contribution is the application of triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the square root operation in the standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing computational cost during matrix inversion. The authors propose a method with theoretical justification demonstrating that this approach does not degrade algorithmic performance. The proposed technique reduces runtime complexity without significantly increasing the number of objective function evaluations. This represents a valuable application of Cholesky factorization to CMA-ES. The authors should further explain theoretically why the "numerically more stable" computation is achieved. It is noted that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES, yet reduces wall-clock time, with the speedup scaling with search space dimensionality. Could the authors consider integrating the low-dimensional approximation technique from [Loshchilov, 2015] into their framework? Given that high dimensionality is a significant concern in practical applications such as direct policy search for neural network training, this combination may be beneficial.
