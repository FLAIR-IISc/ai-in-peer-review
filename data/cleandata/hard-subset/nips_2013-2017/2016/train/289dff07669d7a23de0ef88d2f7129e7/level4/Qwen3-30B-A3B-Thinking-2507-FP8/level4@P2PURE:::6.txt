

The paper proposes a CMA-ES variant termed "Cholesky-CMA-ES" with claims of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity." The authors assert that their modification preserves algorithmic behavior while reducing computational cost. However, the name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is misleading, as it was first introduced by Suttorp et al. (2009) for a related algorithm and is well-established in the literature. The authors fail to acknowledge this prior work, instead introducing their variant as "Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES."  

Algorithmically, the proposed method is an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015). The sole contribution is replacing a single call to `rankOneUpdate` (already present in Krause et al.) with a loop over all individuals, differing only from Plus strategies in this aspect. Empirically, Figure 2b shows a discrepancy on the Cigar function: the observed plateau exhibits different objective function values for CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES. This discrepancy likely stems from how the authors plot median results. The paper must empirically compare against Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically against Krause's variant.  

The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading. It is not optimal for the Sphere function (where the covariance matrix is unnecessary) or separable functions (where a diagonal covariance suffices). The proposed approach offers only a two-fold computational reduction over Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES, which the paper incorrectly labels as prone to numerical instabilities—contradicting Suttorp et al.'s original assertion. Omitting Suttorp's method in Figure 3 misleads readers about the paper's actual contribution. The claim that the method advances large-scale optimization is inaccurate, as the complexity remains quadratic. While the modification may be the best practical approach for saving time/memory when using the full covariance matrix, the work is algorithmically incremental (reducing to a single "for" loop).  

I recommend the following revisions:  
a) Resolve the naming conflict by acknowledging Suttorp et al. (2009).  
b) Conduct experiments comparing against Suttorp's and Krause's variants, and update all three figures (if figures become cluttered, present only the best-performing variant in the main text and include full comparisons in Supplementary Material).  
c) Clarify that the core algorithmic contribution is the loop in Algorithm 1, or justify why this perspective is incorrect.  

Minor note: "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is inaccurate; this is atypical, as proper implementations perform it periodically.
