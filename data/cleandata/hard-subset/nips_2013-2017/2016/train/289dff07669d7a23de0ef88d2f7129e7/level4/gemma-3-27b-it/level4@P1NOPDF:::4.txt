This work’s main contribution is the application of triangular Cholesky factorization to the standard CMA-ES algorithm, eliminating the square root operations and thereby reducing computational cost when inverting matrices. The authors present a well-justified approach demonstrating that this modification does not negatively impact the algorithm’s performance. The proposed method lowers the runtime complexity without a significant increase in the number of objective function evaluations. This paper represents a valuable implementation of Cholesky factorization within the CMA-ES framework. 

Could the authors further elaborate on the theoretical reasons why this approach results in a ‘numerically more stable’ computation? While the Cholesky-CMA-ES requires a comparable number of objective function evaluations to the standard CMA-ES, it achieves reductions in wall-clock time, and these reductions appear to grow with the dimensionality of the search space. Given this, the authors might consider integrating a low-dimensional approximation technique, such as that proposed by Loschilov [2015], into their framework. In many practical applications – particularly those involving high-dimensional problems like training neural networks via direct policy search – dimensionality remains a critical concern that should not be overlooked.
