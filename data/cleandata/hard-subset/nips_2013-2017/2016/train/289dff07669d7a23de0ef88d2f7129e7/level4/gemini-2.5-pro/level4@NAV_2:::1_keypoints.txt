- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update and store triangular Cholesky factor to overcome CMA-ES covariance bottleneck.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal time complexity O(µ d^2) and optimal storage complexity.  
- Replaces expensive inverse matrix square root in CSA with inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Theoretical analysis shows the CSA approximation’s rotational error vanishes during convergence.  
- Preserves the full covariance matrix, maintaining performance on non-separable and ill-conditioned problems.  
- Practical impact: significantly reduces wall-clock time and memory, enabling CMA-ES for medium-to-large problems.  
- Additional benefit: obtains covariance eigenvalues “for free” for monitoring.  
- Technical quality: rigorous theory and thorough empirical comparisons demonstrating increasing speed-ups with dimensionality.  
- Clarity and originality: well-written; novelty in integrating Cholesky updates with CSA within standard CMA-ES.  
- Minor concern: ensure wall-clock time comparisons use comparable implementations/libraries to avoid implementation bias.  
- Minor concern: explain why performance gaps for Cigar and Discus shrink at d=256 (e.g., hardware cache effects).  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical, and original contribution likely to become the new standard implementation.
