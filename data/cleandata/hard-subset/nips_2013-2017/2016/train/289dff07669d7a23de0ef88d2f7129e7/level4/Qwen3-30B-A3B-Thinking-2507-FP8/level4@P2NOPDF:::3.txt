

This paper proposes a Cholesky CMA-ES variant that extends the triangular Cholesky factor update to incorporate both rank-1 and rank-μ updates via sequential rank-1 operations. The authors claim that under the convergence assumption of the covariance matrix, their modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm's behavior, and they support this with empirical results.  

While the technical approach is sound, the contribution appears incremental. Krause et al. (2015) already introduced rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor in CMA-ES, and this work merely generalizes their procedure to rank-μ updates through sequential rank-1 steps. This is a straightforward extension without novel theoretical insights or significant algorithmic innovation, diminishing the paper's novelty.  

The theoretical foundation for the covariance convergence in CMA-ES (Beyer, ECJ 2014) is well-established, though it relies on approximations rather than exact CMA-ES dynamics. Explicitly referencing this prior work would strengthen the confidence in Lemma 1, which analyzes the step-size adaptation.  

Several minor but critical errors require correction:  
1. In Equation (1), the denominator should use $\sigma_t$ (not $\sigma$), a typo also present in Footnote 2 (Page 3).  
2. Line 94 contains a grammatical error: "which can by be motivated" should read "which can be motivated."  

The experimental section lacks depth; it does not compare against state-of-the-art methods or provide sufficient analysis of why rank-μ updates (via sequential rank-1) improve performance over the established rank-1 approach. Without compelling evidence of superior efficiency or robustness, the practical relevance of the proposed extension remains unclear.  

Overall, the paper addresses a technical refinement of existing CMA-ES variants but fails to demonstrate meaningful advancement. The theoretical claims lack novelty, the empirical validation is superficial, and the manuscript contains avoidable technical errors. While the method is correctly formulated, the contribution is insufficient for publication in NIPS given the conference’s high standards for novelty and impact.
