- Misnaming and missing attribution: calling the method "Cholesky-CMA-ES" ignores Suttorp et al. (2009) and fails to position relative to Krause et al. (2015).  
- Incremental contribution concern: the main change (placing rankOneUpdate inside a loop) appears minor and needs stronger justification.  
- Overstated optimality claim: claiming "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading and only meaningful within the narrow class of full-covariance algorithms.  
- Unsupported numerical-instability claim: asserting Suttorp et al. (2009) is numerically unstable is not substantiated and contradicts the original paper.  
- Missing baseline in experiments: Suttorp et al.'s variant is omitted from comparisons despite being an essential and reasonably faster baseline.  
- Empirical inconsistency: observed behavioral differences (e.g., Cigar function plateau) contradict the claim that the variant does not change CMA-ES behavior and need explanation.  
- Limited large-scale impact: the method does not change CMA-ES's quadratic time/memory scaling, so it is not a breakthrough for large-scale optimization.  
- Revision request — naming/differences: explicitly acknowledge prior Cholesky-CMA-ES and clearly explain differences to Suttorp and Krause.  
- Revision request — experiments: rerun and include direct comparisons to Suttorp and Krause variants, with complete results in the supplement if figures get crowded.  
- Revision request — clarify novelty: better explain why the algorithmic change is non-trivial or correct the interpretation if it is incremental.  
- Minor correction: the statement that eigen decomposition is performed every update is inaccurate; modern implementations do it periodically.  
- Recommendation: as submitted the paper is too incremental, poorly contextualized, and should be rejected unless substantially revised.
