This paper proposes a variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) that is claimed to have "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity." The authors suggest that their modification reduces the computational cost of the algorithm without changing its search behavior.

While the goal is worthwhile, the paper has several significant issues in its current form.

**Major Concerns:**

1.  **Naming and Acknowledgment of Prior Work:** The proposed method is named Cholesky-CMA-ES. This name is misleading, as it was first introduced for a similar and well-known algorithm by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009. The authors fail to acknowledge this prior use, instead introducing their method as "Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES." This naming issue must be resolved, and the work must be properly contextualized with respect to existing literature.

2.  **Incremental Contribution:** The proposed method is an incremental modification of the work by Krause et al. (2015). The primary algorithmic contribution appears to be applying the `rankOneUpdate` function (which is also present in Krause et al.'s work) within a `for` loop over all individuals, rather than once as in standard Plus strategies. The paper should either clarify why this perception is incorrect or explicitly frame the work as this specific, targeted improvement.

3.  **Incomplete and Potentially Misleading Comparisons:**
    *   The paper lacks essential comparisons. It should be compared empirically to the Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. and algorithmically to the variant by Krause et al.
    *   The omission of Suttorp's algorithm is particularly concerning. The paper claims it is "prone to numerical instabilities," which contradicts the claims in the original publication. By omitting it from the empirical results in Figure 3, the reader may be misled about the true performance benefits of the proposed method.
    *   The claim about the proposed approach being "only two times cheaper than the omitted Cholesky CMA-ES by Suttorp" should be empirically verified and discussed.

4.  **Overstated Claims of Optimality:** The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is too strong. This optimality does not hold in general. For example, on the Sphere function, a covariance matrix is not needed at all, and on separable functions, only a diagonal matrix is required, leading to far lower complexity than the proposed method.

5.  **Limited Impact on Large-Scale Optimization:** The paper does not fundamentally alter the utility of CMA-ES for large-scale optimization, as the complexity remains quadratic in the problem dimension. While the constant-factor improvements are valuable, the core scaling challenge is not addressed. However, I acknowledge that for problems where a full covariance matrix is indeed necessary, the proposed modification may be the most effective way to reduce time and memory costs.

6.  **Empirical Discrepancy:** In Figure b), the performance on the Cigar function shows a plateau at different objective function values for CMA-ES-Ref and the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES. This suggests their behavior is not identical, contrary to the paper's claims. This discrepancy should be investigated and explained; it might be an artifact of how the median results are plotted.

**Summary and Required Revisions:**

Overall, the work appears to be a highly incremental improvement whose contribution is not clearly delineated from prior art. For the paper to be considered for publication, I recommend the following substantial revisions:

a)  **Resolve the naming issue.** The algorithm should be renamed to avoid confusion with the work of Suttorp et al., and the paper must properly discuss the relationship to this and other prior works.
b)  **Perform a comprehensive experimental evaluation.** The experiments must include comparisons with the variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. All three figures should be updated. If the plots become unreadable, the main text could show the best-performing variant, with the full figures included in the Supplementary Material.
c)  **Clarify the algorithmic contribution.** The authors should better explain whether the main contribution is the `for` loop in Algorithm 1 or provide a more detailed explanation of the method's novelty.

**Minor Note:**
The statement, "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step," is atypical. In all proper implementations, this computationally expensive operation is performed only periodically.
