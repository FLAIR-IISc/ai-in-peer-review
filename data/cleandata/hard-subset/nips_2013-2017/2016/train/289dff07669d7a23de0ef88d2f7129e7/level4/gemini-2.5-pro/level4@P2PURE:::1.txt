The Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) is a state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It operates by adapting the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, a process that is equivalent to adapting an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new variant for updating the covariance matrix based on its triangular Cholesky factors. Compared to previous update methods, this approach significantly reduces both the computational complexity of the update step and the memory required to store the algorithm's state parameters.

The new method is rigorously evaluated through numerical experiments. The results demonstrate that the proposed update does not degrade the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations needed to reach a given target. However, it substantially reduces the algorithm's wall-clock time, an improvement that is particularly pronounced in high-dimensional settings.

This paper is a very significant contribution to the field of optimization. I expect this new variant could become the default implementation of CMA-ES for medium- and large-scale problems due to its clear advantages over the current standard: reduced memory requirements, lower computational complexity, and providing the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no additional cost.

The paper is well-written, and the approach is carefully evaluated. This contribution is a welcome addition to NIPS, given that CMA-ES has strong connections to machine learning, information geometry, and is applied in areas such as reinforcement and supervised learning.
