- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of full covariance, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) with μ typically O(log d).  
- Core distinction: removes estimation of a random orthogonal matrix Et at each step.  
- Provides an asymptotic argument (under restrictive convergence assumptions) that Et converges to identity, making the schemes equivalent.  
- Empirical validation: shows general speed-up and comparable optimization performance on selected synthetic benchmarks.  
- Compares against two state-of-the-art delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Contribution: solid acceleration of CMA-ES without degrading performance on tested cases.  
- Practical caveat: in many real problems the objective evaluation dominates runtime, and problem dimensionality is often only a few tens, so the target scenario may be uncommon.  
- Suggests visualizing the evolution of Et (e.g., in different norms) and comparing Et from full CMA-ES and the approximation on the same runs.  
- Recommends reporting actual runtimes and baseline CMA-ES performance without delay strategies for completeness.  
- Advises additional experiments on quadratic functions with continuously varied spectra (e.g., Stich & Müller 2012) and a real-world test (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) to strengthen empirical evidence.
