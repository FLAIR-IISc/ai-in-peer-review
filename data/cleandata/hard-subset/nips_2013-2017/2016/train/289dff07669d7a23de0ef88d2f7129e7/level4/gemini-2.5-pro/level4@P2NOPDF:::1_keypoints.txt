- Introduces a new CMA-ES variant that updates the covariance via its triangular Cholesky factors.  
- Core benefit: substantially reduced computational complexity and memory footprint compared to standard CMA-ES.  
- More scalable and practical for higher-dimensional problems.  
- Empirical results: no deterioration in convergence (function evaluations) and significant wall-clock time reduction, increasingly pronounced with dimension.  
- Provides eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no extra computational cost.  
- Strong contribution: likely to become default for medium- and large-scale applications.  
- Paper is well-written, rigorously evaluated, and highly relevant to NIPS (ties to ML, information geometry, RL, hyperparameter optimization).
