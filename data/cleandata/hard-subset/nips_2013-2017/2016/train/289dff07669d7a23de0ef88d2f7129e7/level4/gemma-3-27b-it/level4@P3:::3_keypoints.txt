- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the covariance via its Cholesky factor to reduce runtime and memory, especially in high dimensions.  
- Core idea: Leverages quadratic-time updates of the Cholesky factor to avoid costly matrix square-root computations.  
- Theoretical support: Provides Lemma 1 and arguments why the Cholesky-based step-size adaptation should preserve convergence behavior.  
- Empirical results: Demonstrates improved wall-clock runtime without increasing function-evaluation counts, particularly in higher dimensions.  
- Complexity: Achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime for full covariance adaptation.  
- Presentation: Paper is generally well-structured with clear technical exposition and connection to prior work.  
- Relation to prior work: Builds on direct-Cholesky-update literature but extension to rank-μ updates feels incremental relative to Krause & Igel (2015).  
- Convergence caveat: Theoretical justification depends on a covariance-convergence assumption that may not hold universally.  
- Experimental limitations: Experiments focus on runtime and eval counts; deeper analyses (e.g., rotation matrices Et) are missing.  
- Technical issues: Minor typos and equation inconsistencies need proofreading and correction.  
- For acceptance: Addresses a practical bottleneck, provides useful theory, shows clear runtime gains, and includes code implementation.  
- Against acceptance: Limited novelty, reliance on a strong assumption, and minor technical errors reduce the paper’s strength.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify novelty, acknowledge convergence limitations, fix errors, and expand experiments.
