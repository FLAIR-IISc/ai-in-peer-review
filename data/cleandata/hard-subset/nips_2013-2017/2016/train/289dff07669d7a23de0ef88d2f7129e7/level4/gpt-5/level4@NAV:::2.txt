This paper uses Cholesky factorization instead of eigendecomposition to reduce the computational and memory complexity of CMA-ES. Overall, the paper is well written. The proposed approach appears reasonable, and the experimental results (especially the speedup rates) are quite promising. I could imagine this becoming a new standard variant of CMA-ES in the future.

That said, while the work may be meaningful, its originality appears limited because it seems to be a straightforward extension of the prior work: A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies. The methods look essentially identical. Please correct me if I am mistaken.

In addition, several important elements are conspicuously missing. 1) You should demonstrate whether your algorithm is equivalent to formula (2). I understand space is limited and that this was proven previously, but it remains essential for this paper. 2) You claim the computational complexity is O(μ d^2), which is exactly the same as CMA-ES-Ref. Please discuss in detail (perhaps using Θ instead of O) why your algorithm is faster. 3) You claim the error in the path is small and decreases over time, which contradicts Hansen’s assertion. Since your theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to validate your claim. Comparing only the final performance is not sufficient.

Some smaller comments: 1) Please check your notation carefully. For example, wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk, and n in Table 1 should be d. 2) “F” should be “f” on line 185.

As such, I do not think this paper is ready for NIPS in its current form.
