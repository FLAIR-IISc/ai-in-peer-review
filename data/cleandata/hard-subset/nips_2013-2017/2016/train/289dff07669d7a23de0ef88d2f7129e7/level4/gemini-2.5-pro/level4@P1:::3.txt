This paper proposes the Cholesky-CMA-ES, a computationally efficient variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy. The key contribution is an update mechanism that operates directly on the triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix, which reduces the time complexity for the matrix update from O(d³) to an asymptotically optimal O(µd²), and also reduces the memory footprint. To enable this efficient update, the authors modify the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism by replacing the inverse of the matrix square root with the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor. The paper provides a theoretical argument that this modification does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior, particularly as the covariance matrix converges. The authors present empirical results on various benchmark functions, showing that the Cholesky-CMA-ES significantly reduces runtime compared to the standard implementation without increasing the number of function evaluations needed to find a solution.

**Strengths**

*   **Practical Significance:** The paper addresses a well-known computational bottleneck in the standard CMA-ES. The proposed method offers a substantial and practical speedup, making the algorithm more viable for higher-dimensional problems, especially in scenarios where function evaluations are relatively inexpensive compared to the matrix operations.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a sound theoretical justification for the approximation made in the CSA mechanism. Lemma 1 argues convincingly that the error introduced by using the Cholesky factor's inverse instead of the matrix square root's inverse should diminish as the covariance matrix converges. This provides a principled basis for the modification, setting it apart from purely heuristic changes.
*   **Solid Empirical Validation:** The experimental evaluation is thorough and effectively supports the paper's claims. The comparison with the reference CMA-ES implementation and other variants clearly demonstrates that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a significant reduction in wall-clock time across a range of benchmark functions and dimensions, without degrading optimization performance in terms of function evaluations.

**Weaknesses**

*   **Originality and Contribution:** The primary weakness of this work lies in its perceived incremental novelty. The proposed method appears to be a direct generalization of the work by Krause and Igel (2015), which introduced a rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor in a simpler ES variant. This paper extends this concept to the standard CMA-ES by handling the rank-µ update as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The manuscript would be stronger if it more clearly articulated the technical challenges of this generalization and highlighted why it is a non-trivial extension of the prior art.
*   **Support for Theoretical Assumption:** The theoretical argument in Lemma 1 is predicated on the assumption that the normalized covariance matrix converges. While this is a plausible assumption, the justification could be significantly strengthened. The authors could cite existing theoretical analyses on the convergence of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014) to provide a more rigorous foundation for the premise of their lemma.

**Minor Comments and Corrections**

*   In Equation (1) and in footnote 1 on page 3, the step-size σ in the denominator should be denoted as σt to be consistent with the notation used for other variables in the same iteration.
