- Proposes replacing eigendecomposition with Cholesky decomposition to reduce time and storage complexity of CMA-ES.
- Paper is well-written, methodologically sound, shows promising empirical speedups, and could become a new standard implementation.
- Major concern: limited originality—appears to be a direct extension of "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies"; clarification requested.
- Missing explicit proof of equivalence to the standard CMA-ES update (formula 2); needs inclusion for self-containment and rigor.
- Complexity analysis is insufficient: claiming O(μd²) but must justify practical speedup by analyzing constant factors/Θ-notation and specific operation costs.
- Theoretical claim that evolution path error is small and decreases over time lacks validation and may contradict prior work (Hansen); needs experiments tracking this error during optimization.
- Minor issues: inconsistent notation (wk vs αk, Table 1: n should be d) and a typo ('F' should be 'f' on line 185).
- Recommendation: as currently presented, originality and missing analytical/experimental details prevent acceptance for NIPS.
