- Proposal: Use Cholesky-factor-based rank-one covariance update in CMA-ES to reduce time and storage versus eigendecomposition.
- Strength: Well-written, methodologically sound, and shows impressive empirical speedups with potential practical impact.
- Major concern — originality: Method appears substantially similar to Ros & Hansen (GECCO 2008); authors must clearly differentiate their contribution.
- Major concern — missing proof: No proof (or citation) showing mathematical equivalence of the proposed update to the standard CMA-ES update; should be included (appendix acceptable).
- Major concern — complexity analysis: Claiming O(d²) is misleading; need precise constant-aware (Θ) complexity breakdown to explain observed speedups.
- Major concern — path error claim: Assertion that evolution-path error is small/decreasing is unvalidated and may contradict Hansen; requires experiments tracking error accumulation over time.
- Minor: Notation inconsistencies (e.g., Algorithm 2 wk vs αk; Table 1 uses n while paper uses d).
- Minor: Typo — function F should likely be lowercase f (line 185).
- Recommendation: Not ready for NIPS in current form; reviewer would reconsider after major revisions addressing the above points.
