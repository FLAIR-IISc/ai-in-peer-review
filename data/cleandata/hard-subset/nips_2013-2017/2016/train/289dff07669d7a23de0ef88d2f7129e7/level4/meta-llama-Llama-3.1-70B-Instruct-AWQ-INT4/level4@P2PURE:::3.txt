This paper explores the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) with both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, applied sequentially as a series of rank-1 updates. The authors prove that, under the assumption of covariance convergence, the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. Experimental results on algorithm performance are also presented.

The paper's primary contribution is the extension of the update for the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. However, the significance of this contribution is questionable, given the work of Krause et al. (2015), which employed a rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The current paper's generalization to rank-μ update in a sequence of rank-1 updates appears to be a straightforward extension of this existing procedure.

The convergence of the covariance matrix Ct in CMA-ES has been theoretically analyzed (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), albeit not based on the exact CMA-ES formulae. This existing result may enhance the confidence in Lemma 1.

Several issues were identified in the paper. Firstly, in Equation (1), the denominator should contain σt instead of σ. The same error is present in Footnote 2 on page 3. Secondly, in line 94, the phrase "which can by be motivated by principles from..." should be corrected by removing the word "by".

Overall, while the paper presents some interesting results, its contribution to the field is limited, and the errors and minor issues need to be addressed to improve the overall quality of the paper.
