- Proposes a Cholesky CMA-ES variant extending triangular Cholesky factor updates to include both rank-1 and rank-µ updates.
- Provides a theoretical result showing the modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) does not significantly change behavior, assuming covariance matrix convergence.
- Presents experimental evaluation of algorithm performance.
- Paper is well-written and the idea is sound.
- Contribution may be limited given Krause et al. (2015) already applies rank-1 updates to triangular Cholesky factors.
- The rank-µ extension appears to be a straightforward generalization implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates.
- Recommends deeper discussion of CMA-ES theoretical foundations and covariance convergence (Ct), citing Beyer (2014) as useful background to strengthen Lemma 1.
- Minor errors: σ in the denominator of equation (1) and a footnote should be σt.
- Minor wording issue: delete the extra "by" in line 94 ("which can by be motivated...").
- Overall organization and clarity are strong, but the paper should better highlight its novel contribution.
- Strengths summarized: new CMA-ES variant, theoretical justification for modified CSA, and experiments.
- Weaknesses summarized: limited novelty vs prior work, insufficient theoretical discussion, and small typos/notation errors.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to address theoretical discussion and minor mistakes.
