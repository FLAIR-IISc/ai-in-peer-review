CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic/randomized method for derivative-free optimization. It integrates multiple learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can equivalently be interpreted as adapting an underlying metric. This paper introduces a new covariance-matrix update based on triangular Cholesky factors, which, compared to previous updates, markedly reduces both the computational complexity of the update and the memory required to store the algorithm’s state. The proposed approach is carefully assessed through numerical experiments. The results show that, in terms of the number of function evaluations needed to reach a target, the standard CMA-ES performance is preserved, while wall-clock time is substantially reduced, especially in high-dimensional settings. I view this as a highly significant contribution to optimization. I expect this CMA-ES variant could become the default for medium/large-scale problems, given its clear advantages over the current default: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and obtaining the covariance eigenvalues essentially for free. The paper is well written and the methodology is rigorously evaluated. It is welcome to see this contribution at NIPS, given CMA-ES’s strong connections to machine learning and information geometry, as well as its use in reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
