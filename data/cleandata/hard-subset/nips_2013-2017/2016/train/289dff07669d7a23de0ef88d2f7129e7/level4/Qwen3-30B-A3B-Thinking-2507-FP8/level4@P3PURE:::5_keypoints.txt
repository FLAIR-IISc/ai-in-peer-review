- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance to approximate CMA-ES.  
- Reduces per-iteration complexity from O(d³) to O(μ d²), with μ typically O(log d).  
- Key methodological change: omits estimating a random orthogonal matrix Εₜ at each step.  
- Under the restrictive assumption that covariance matrices converge to a target, authors argue Εₜ → I, implying asymptotic equivalence.  
- Empirical results (runtime and convergence plots) show validity and speedup on benchmark functions versus two delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Paper is a solid contribution given CMA-ES’s role for non-convex black-box optimization.  
- Major practical limitation: objective-function evaluation usually dominates runtime, so CMA-ES speedups are often irrelevant for typical problem sizes (tens of variables).  
- The scenario where CMA-ES runtime matters—very fast evaluations and high dimensions—may be uncommon in practice.  
- Theoretical guarantees are difficult; the asymptotic claim depends on restrictive assumptions and needs empirical corroboration.  
- Suggested experiment 1: plot evolution of Εₜ (operator/Frobenius norm) along runs, comparing full CMA-ES and the Cholesky approximation.  
- Suggested experiment 2: include runtime comparisons with standard (no-delay) CMA-ES to contextualize speedups.  
- Suggested experiment 3: conduct spectral sensitivity analysis on quadratics (vary spectrum) reporting solution quality and Εₜ error.  
- Suggested experiment 4: validate on a real-world, speed-critical task (e.g., policy-gradient robotics) with moderate parameter dimensionality.
