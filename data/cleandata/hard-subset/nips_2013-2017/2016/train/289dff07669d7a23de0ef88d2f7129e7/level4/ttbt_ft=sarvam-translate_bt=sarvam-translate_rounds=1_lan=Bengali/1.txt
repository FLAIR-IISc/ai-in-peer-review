CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic/randomized algorithm for (derivative-free) optimization. It combines various learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can also be seen as an adaptation of an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new version for updating the covariance matrix based on triangular Cholesky factors, which offers significant benefits compared to previous updates in terms of reduced update complexity and memory required to store the algorithm's state parameters. The new method has been carefully evaluated numerically. It has been shown that the new update does not reduce the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations required to reach a specific goal, but it significantly reduces the algorithm's wall-clock time, especially for large dimensions. I believe this paper is a very important contribution to the optimization domain. I hope that this version of CMA-ES can become a default version of the method in medium/large-sized cases, as it has several clear advantages over the current default method: such as reduced required memory, reduced complexity, and the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix are freely available. The paper is well-written and the method has been carefully evaluated. It is good to see that this contribution has come to NIPS, as CMA-ES is also used in machine learning, information geometry, and reinforcement learning or supervised learning.
