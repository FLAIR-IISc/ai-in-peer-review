This paper presents a Cholesky decomposition-based approach to updating the covariance matrix in the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), aiming to reduce computational time and storage complexity. The core idea – leveraging the Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root – is clearly articulated and logically motivated. The paper demonstrates a good understanding of the CMA-ES algorithm and its existing optimizations. Experimental results suggest a significant speedup, particularly in higher-dimensional problems, making this a potentially valuable contribution to the field.

The work builds upon previous efforts to improve CMA-ES efficiency, notably Suttorp et al. [2009] and Krause and Igel [2015]. While the proposed method is a logical extension, its justification and empirical validation are strong enough to warrant consideration. The authors correctly identify the asymptotic optimality of the proposed update and provide a theoretical argument, based on Lemma 1, for why the approximation introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor should not significantly degrade performance.

**Strengths:**

*   **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-written and easy to follow, with a clear explanation of the motivation and approach.
*   **Potential for Impact:** The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a substantial speedup, particularly for high-dimensional problems, potentially making CMA-ES more accessible for larger-scale applications.
*   **Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a theoretical argument for the correctness of their approach, grounding it in the convergence properties of the algorithm.
*   **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results demonstrate a clear performance benefit of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, particularly in terms of wall-clock time.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Originality:** The connection to Krause and Igel [2015] warrants further discussion. While not a direct replication, the method is a natural extension of their work and could benefit from a more thorough acknowledgment of this relationship. The claim of "novelty" could be tempered.
*   **Complexity Analysis:** The statement that the complexity is O(µd<sup>2</sup>), the same as the reference CMA-ES, is not adequately explained. A more nuanced discussion of the constant factors and the practical implications of the update is needed. Using Θ notation could clarify the performance gain.
*   **Error Analysis:** While Lemma 1 provides a theoretical basis for the approximation, experimental validation of the error introduced by the Cholesky factor replacement is crucial. Simply showing comparable final performance isn’t sufficient; analyzing the path taken by the algorithm (e.g., tracking the rotation matrix *E<sub>t</sub>*) would strengthen the argument. The paper references a possible contradiction with Hansen’s claims, which requires further exploration and evidence.
*   **Minor Issues:** Several minor typographical errors and notational inconsistencies were observed (e.g., *w<sub>k</sub>* should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, *n* in Table 1 should be *d*, and “F” should be “f” in line 185). While minor, these detract from the paper's overall polish.
*   **Missing Verification:** While mentioning equation (2) is essential for establishing the connection to standard CMA-ES, the paper states it has been proven in previous work, which might make it challenging for readers unfamiliar with Krause and Igel [2015] to verify the approach.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   The paper presents a well-motivated and clearly explained improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm.
*   The experimental results demonstrate a significant and practically relevant speedup.
*   The theoretical justification, while needing further validation, provides a plausible explanation for the observed performance.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

*   The originality of the approach is somewhat limited, as it builds directly upon prior work.
*   The analysis of the computational complexity and the error introduced by the approximation are not sufficiently detailed.
*   The paper contains minor errors and inconsistencies.



**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a worthwhile contribution that has the potential to improve the efficiency of CMA-ES. However, addressing the weaknesses outlined above – particularly providing a more detailed complexity analysis, validating the error introduced by the approximation, and clarifying the relationship to prior work – is crucial. As it stands, the paper is borderline.

**Recommendation:** Weak Reject. I recommend rejection in its current form but encourage the authors to address the weaknesses and resubmit. A strengthened analysis and validation would significantly increase the paper’s impact and merit.
