- Introduces a novel Cholesky-factor-based covariance update for CMA-ES that reduces computational complexity and memory usage.  
- Core contribution: an efficient covariance update mechanism that circumvents expensive previous methods.  
- Maintains standard CMA-ES performance in function-evaluation counts while greatly reducing wall-clock time, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Provides covariance matrix eigenvalues as a byproduct of the Cholesky decomposition.  
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, carefully conducted, and well presented.  
- Paper is well-written and represents a substantial, practically relevant advancement for medium-to-large scale problems.  
- Contribution is highly relevant to the NIPS community (hyperparameter optimization, information geometry, RL, supervised learning).  
- Likely to become a preferred CMA-ES implementation due to reduced memory and computation.
