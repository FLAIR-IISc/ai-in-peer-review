- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update Cholesky factor directly (triangular factor) and modify CSA to reduce covariance update cost and memory.  
- Core claim: direct Cholesky updates yield quadratic time complexity while preserving algorithmic performance.  
- Builds on extensive CMA-ES literature, extending Cholesky update ideas from Krause & Igel (2015) to standard CMA-ES.  
- Strong theoretical justification (Lemma 1) arguing CSA replacement by Cholesky factor does not change convergence behavior.  
- Empirical results show comparable function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES with significant runtime improvements in high dimensions.  
- Runtime complexity and memory footprint analyses are clearly presented and supportive of efficiency claims.  
- Naming “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with prior use by Suttorp et al. (2009) and needs clarification to avoid ambiguity.  
- Algorithmic novelty is incremental (essentially applying rankOneUpdate in a loop) and requires stronger justification of significance.  
- Paper acknowledges Krause & Igel (2015) but lacks a direct algorithmic and empirical comparison to that work.  
- Empirical comparisons (e.g., Figure 2) show small differences; broader/clearer comparisons to Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel would strengthen claims.  
- Claim of “optimal” complexity is overstated because simpler covariance structures (e.g., diagonal) can be more appropriate in some scenarios.  
- Unexplained discrepancy in the plateau region of the Cigar function (Figure 1b) requires further investigation and discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: theoretically sound, empirically validated, practical runtime/memory gains, and clear presentation addressing a relevant CMA-ES bottleneck.  
- Arguments against acceptance: incremental contribution, potentially misleading naming, incomplete empirical comparisons with existing Cholesky variants, and an overstated optimality claim.  
- Overall recommendation: solid contribution with convincing theory and experiments; accept with minor revisions to naming and comparative evaluation.
