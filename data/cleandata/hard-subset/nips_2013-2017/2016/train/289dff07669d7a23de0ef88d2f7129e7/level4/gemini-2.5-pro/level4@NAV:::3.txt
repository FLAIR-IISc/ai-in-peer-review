This work investigates the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES), incorporating both rank-1 and rank-µ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors prove that, under the assumption of covariance matrix convergence, their proposed modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm's behavior. The theoretical analysis is supported by experiments evaluating the algorithm's performance.

1.  The paper's focus is on extending the update mechanism for the triangular Cholesky factor in CMA-ES to handle both rank-1 and rank-µ information. However, the significance of this contribution appears limited in the context of prior work, specifically Krause et al. (2015), which already introduced a rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The method presented here seems to be a direct generalization of that procedure, implementing the rank-µ update as a series of sequential rank-1 updates.

2.  The assumption regarding the convergence of the covariance matrix C_t is central to Lemma 1. Confidence in this lemma could be strengthened by referencing existing theoretical work that has established the convergence of C_t in CMA-ES, such as the analysis by Beyer (ECJ, 2014), even if those proofs do not use the exact formulation presented in this paper.
