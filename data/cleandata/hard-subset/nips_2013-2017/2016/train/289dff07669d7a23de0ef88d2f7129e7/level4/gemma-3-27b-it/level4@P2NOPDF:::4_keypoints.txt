- Introduces triangular Cholesky factorization in CMA-ES to remove the square-root in the inverse calculation, lowering computational cost with increasing dimensionality.  
- Provides theoretical justification that the factorization does not degrade algorithmic performance.  
- Experimental results show Cholesky-CMA-ES retains comparable numbers of objective evaluations to standard CMA-ES while reducing runtime.  
- Clearly articulates a significant runtime complexity reduction.  
- Claim of improved numerical stability is asserted but needs stronger theoretical explanation of why it is more stable.  
- Runtime advantage increases with problem dimensionality, making it relevant for modern high-dimensional applications.  
- Suggests exploring combinations with low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loschilov 2015) to boost scalability for tasks like direct policy search and neural network training.  
- Overall the technique is sound and practically beneficial; strengthening the numerical-stability theory and discussing dimensionality-reduction synergies would improve the paper.
