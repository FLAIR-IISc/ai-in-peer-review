The CMA-ES algorithm is a state-of-the-art stochastic optimization method that employs multiple learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, effectively adapting an underlying metric. This paper introduces a novel update rule for the covariance matrix based on triangular Cholesky factors, which offers significant advantages over previous updates, including reduced computational complexity and memory requirements for storing state parameters. The authors conduct a thorough numerical evaluation of the new method, demonstrating that it maintains the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of function evaluations while substantially reducing the algorithm's wall-clock time, particularly in high-dimensional settings. This contribution is highly significant in the field of optimization, and I anticipate that this variant of CMA-ES will become the default choice for medium to large-scale problems due to its clear advantages, including reduced memory requirements, lower complexity, and the ability to obtain eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no additional cost. The paper is well-written, and the approach is carefully evaluated. It is also noteworthy that the CMA-ES algorithm is closely related to machine learning, information geometry, and reinforcement learning, making this contribution a valuable addition to the NIPS conference. 

Minor comments: To ensure the comparability of the different implementations, it would be helpful to provide details on the implementation language and any other relevant factors that may impact CPU performance. Additionally, in the discussion of Figure 2, the authors note that "the higher the dimensionality, the more pronounced the differences," but this trend does not appear to hold for the Cigar and Discus functions in 256 dimensions. Providing an explanation for this anomaly would strengthen the presentation.
