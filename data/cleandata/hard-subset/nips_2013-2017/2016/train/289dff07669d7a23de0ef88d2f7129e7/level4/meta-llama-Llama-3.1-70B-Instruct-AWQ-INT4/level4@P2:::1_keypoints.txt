- Proposes a novel CMA-ES variant (Cholesky-CMA-ES) using triangular Cholesky factors to update the covariance matrix, reducing complexity and memory.
- Provides a thorough theoretical justification for the Cholesky-based update.
- Demonstrates efficacy through extensive numerical experiments showing reduced wall-clock time while maintaining function-evaluation performance.
- Enables obtaining covariance eigenvalues without additional computational cost.
- Particularly suitable for medium to large-scale optimization; advantages increase with dimensionality.
- Paper is well-written with a clear and concise explanation of the approach.
- Request for clearer implementation comparability: specify whether the same language and optimizations were used across variants and CPU performance details.
- Notes a discrepancy where runtime advantages do not hold for Cigar and Discus functions at 256 dimensions and asks for a brief explanation.
- Strengths: novel efficient variant, solid theory, careful experiments, clear writing.
- No objections to acceptance noted; argues the method could become a default for medium/large-scale problems and is relevant to ML, information geometry, and RL.
