This paper investigates the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) with both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, applied sequentially as a series of rank-1 updates. The authors prove, under the assumption of convergence of the covariance, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. Experimental results on algorithm performance are also presented.

1. This paper focuses on the Cholesky CMA-ES, extending the update for the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. However, I do not believe this contribution is substantial, considering the work of Krause et al. (2015), which utilizes rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. This paper presents a straightforward generalization of this procedure to rank-μ updates in a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2. Generally, the convergence of the covariance Ct in CMA-ES has been theoretically analyzed (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although not based on the exact formulae of CMA-ES. This may enhance the confidence in Lemma 1.

3. There are several issues with the paper. 
3.1. In equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σt. The same problem occurs in footnote 2 on page 3.
3.2. In line 94, the phrase 'which can be motivated by principles from ...' contains an unnecessary 'by', which should be deleted.
