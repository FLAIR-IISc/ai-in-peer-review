

This paper proposes a variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) that uses a triangular Cholesky factorization for covariance matrix updates, which the authors term "Cholesky-CMA-ES." The authors present a theoretically justified modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism, replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor. They argue this modification maintains algorithm performance while reducing computational complexity to O(μd²) for covariance updates compared to the standard O(d³) complexity. The paper demonstrates empirical results showing Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves similar optimization performance to standard CMA-ES but with significantly improved runtime, particularly for higher-dimensional problems.

The work builds on prior research in CMA-ES variants, particularly the approaches by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015), which explored using Cholesky factors for covariance updates in specific CMA-ES variants. The key contribution is generalizing these approaches to the standard CMA-ES with both rank-1 and rank-μ updates, providing theoretical justification for why the modification does not significantly affect algorithm behavior.

Strengths:
- The paper provides a clear theoretical justification for the Cholesky factor replacement through Lemma 1, demonstrating that the error introduced by this approximation diminishes as the algorithm converges.
- The experimental design is thorough, with comparisons across multiple benchmark functions and dimensions, showing that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires similar numbers of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES but with substantially reduced wall-clock time, especially for higher-dimensional problems.
- The implementation offers practical advantages including optimal storage complexity, free access to covariance matrix eigenvalues, and numerically stable computation of the inverse Cholesky factor.

Weaknesses:
- The main contribution appears to be a straightforward extension of Krause and Igel's (2015) work, which only considered the (1+1)-CMA-ES with rank-1 updates. The generalization to standard CMA-ES with rank-μ updates is not particularly novel, as it essentially applies the same technique to a more general algorithm through a sequence of rank-1 updates.
- The theoretical justification relies on the convergence of the covariance matrix Ct, but this convergence is not rigorously established for the standard CMA-ES (Beyer, 2014 proves convergence for simplified versions but not for the exact algorithm). This weakens the foundation of Lemma 1, which assumes Ct converges to a certain shape.
- There are several technical errors requiring correction: 
  * In Equation (1), the denominator should be σ_t (not σ), and this error appears again in footnote 2 on page 3.
  * In line 94, the phrase "which can by be motivated" contains a typo ("by" should be removed).

Overall, while the Cholesky-CMA-ES implementation is technically sound and offers clear computational advantages, the novelty of the approach is limited. The paper would be strengthened by more clearly differentiating its contribution from prior work and providing a more rigorous theoretical foundation for Lemma 1. The technical errors should be corrected before publication.

Pro and Con acceptance:
- Pro: The paper presents a practical implementation with demonstrable computational advantages, well-designed experiments, and clear theoretical justification for the key modification.
- Con: The main contribution is a straightforward extension of prior work, and the theoretical foundation for the key lemma is not sufficiently strong.

The paper addresses a practical concern in CMA-ES implementation (computational complexity of covariance updates) and offers a solution with clear benefits. However, given the limited novelty compared to existing work, I would lean toward a borderline acceptance, with the understanding that the paper needs to be revised to better highlight its contribution and correct the technical errors.
