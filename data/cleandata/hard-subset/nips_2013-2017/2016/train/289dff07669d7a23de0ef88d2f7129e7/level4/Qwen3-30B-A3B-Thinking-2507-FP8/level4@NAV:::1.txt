

CMA-ES represents the state-of-the-art stochastic/derivative-free optimization algorithm. It integrates multiple learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which is equivalently interpreted as adapting an underlying metric. This paper introduces a novel covariance matrix update mechanism leveraging triangular Cholesky factors. Compared to prior updates, this approach significantly reduces computational complexity and memory requirements for storing the algorithm's state parameters. The proposed method undergoes rigorous numerical evaluation, demonstrating that it maintains the standard CMA-ES performance in terms of function evaluations required to achieve a target, while substantially decreasing wall-clock time—especially for high-dimensional problems. This work constitutes a highly significant contribution to optimization. The new variant has strong potential to become the default CMA-ES implementation for medium/large-scale applications due to its clear advantages: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and the inherent provision of eigenvalues at no additional cost. The paper is well-written, and the methodology is thoroughly validated. The contribution is particularly timely for NIPS, given CMA-ES's strong ties to machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
