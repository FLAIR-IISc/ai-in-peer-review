- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor and adapt CSA, reducing covariance update complexity to O(μd²) from O(d³).  
- Provides Lemma 1 as theoretical justification that the error from the Cholesky approximation vanishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical results show comparable optimization performance to standard CMA-ES with substantially reduced wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and generalizes the approach to standard CMA-ES with rank-1 and rank-μ updates.  
- Strength: clear theoretical argument for the modification and demonstration that approximation error decreases during convergence.  
- Strength: thorough experiments across multiple benchmarks and dimensions showing similar function evaluations but faster runtime.  
- Strength: practical implementation benefits—optimal storage, easy access to covariance eigenvalues, and numerically stable inverse Cholesky computation.  
- Weakness: novelty is limited—essentially a straightforward extension of Krause & Igel (2015) from (1+1)-CMA-ES to standard CMA-ES.  
- Weakness: Lemma 1 assumes convergence of the covariance matrix Ct, but rigorous convergence for standard CMA-ES is not established, weakening the theoretical basis.  
- Technical errors to fix: Equation (1) denominator should be σ_t (not σ), same error in footnote 2, and typo in line 94 ("which can by be motivated").  
- Recommendation: borderline acceptance—practical and well-evaluated contribution but needs clearer differentiation from prior work, stronger theoretical support for Lemma 1, and corrections of technical errors.
