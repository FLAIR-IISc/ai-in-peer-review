The paper "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" proposes a novel variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a state-of-the-art stochastic optimization algorithm. The new approach, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, updates the covariance matrix using triangular Cholesky factors, significantly reducing the computational complexity and memory requirements of the algorithm.

The CMA-ES is a widely used optimization technique in machine learning, particularly in direct policy search and hyperparameter tuning. The standard CMA-ES algorithm has a high computational complexity due to the update of the covariance matrix, which dominates the runtime in each iteration. The authors address this issue by introducing a numerically stable quadratic-time covariance matrix update scheme based on maintaining triangular Cholesky factors.

The Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm is carefully evaluated numerically, and the results show that it does not deteriorate the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations to reach a given target. However, it significantly reduces the wall-clock time of the algorithm, particularly for large dimensions. The authors also demonstrate that the new method is invariant under affine linear transformations and provides the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix without additional costs.

The paper is well-written, and the approach is thoroughly evaluated. The authors provide a clear explanation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm and its advantages over the standard CMA-ES. The experimental results are convincing, and the comparison with other CMA-ES variants is helpful in understanding the benefits of the new approach.

The paper is a significant contribution to the field of optimization, and the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm has the potential to become a default variant of the CMA-ES method for medium to large-scale optimization problems. The reduction in memory requirements and computational complexity makes it an attractive choice for applications where these resources are limited.

One minor point that could be improved is the explanation of how the different implementations used in the experiments are comparable in terms of CPU. It would be helpful to know if the same implementation language was used and if any optimizations were applied to ensure a fair comparison.

Additionally, the authors mention that the higher the dimensionality, the more pronounced the differences in wall-clock time between the Cholesky-CMA-ES and other CMA-ES variants. However, this does not seem to be the case for the Cigar and Discus functions in dimension 256. It would be good to provide an explanation for this discrepancy.

Overall, the paper is well-written, and the Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm is a significant contribution to the field of optimization. The experimental results are convincing, and the comparison with other CMA-ES variants is helpful in understanding the benefits of the new approach.
