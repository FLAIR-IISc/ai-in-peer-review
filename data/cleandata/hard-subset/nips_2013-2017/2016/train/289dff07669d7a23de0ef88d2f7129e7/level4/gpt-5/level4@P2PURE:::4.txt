The main contribution of this work is the use of a triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate the matrix square root in standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing the computational cost when computing matrix inverses. The paper provides a theoretical justification indicating that this modification does not deteriorate the algorithm’s performance. The proposed method reduces the algorithm’s runtime complexity without a significant change in the number of objective function evaluations. Overall, this is a valuable Cholesky-based implementation for CMA-ES.

Could the author further elaborate, from a theoretical standpoint, why the computation is “numerically more stable”? It appears that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES while reducing wall-clock time, with the advantage increasing with the dimensionality of the search space. Finally, would the author consider integrating the low-dimensional approximation of Loshchilov (2015) into the current framework? In many practical scenarios—such as training neural networks in direct policy search—the high dimensionality of the problems is a critical concern that should not be overlooked.
