- Proposes a novel Cholesky-CMA-ES update that reduces complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) by approximating covariance adaptation.  
- Core idea: maintain and update a lower-triangular Cholesky matrix instead of the full covariance and its square root.  
- Main algorithmic difference: omission of estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.  
- Theoretical claim: under restrictive convergence assumptions, E_t asymptotically converges to the identity, making the approximation equivalent to the original.  
- Empirical results: runtime and convergence plots on benchmarks show substantial speedups and no performance degradation versus two delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Contribution: significant acceleration of CMA-ES, relevant for non-convex black-box optimization where derivative information is unavailable.  
- Limitation: in many real applications the objective evaluation time dominates and problem dimensionality is often small, so runtime gains may be less impactful; method targets fast-evaluation, higher-dimensional scenarios.  
- Suggested experiment: monitor the evolution of E_t (e.g., in various norms) along optimization trajectories to empirically assess its behavior.  
- Suggested experiment: run full CMA-ES and the approximation simultaneously to compare fluctuations of E_t over time.  
- Suggested comparison: include runtime and performance of standard CMA-ES without any delayed-update strategies.  
- Suggested simulation: test on quadratic functions with continuously varied spectra (per Stich & Müller) to study solution quality and E_t error vs spectral distribution.  
- Suggested real-world test: add an application experiment (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) to demonstrate practical benefits.
