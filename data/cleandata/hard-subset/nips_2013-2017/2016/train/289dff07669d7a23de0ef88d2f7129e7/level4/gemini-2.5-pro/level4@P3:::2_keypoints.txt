- Paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update Cholesky factor A directly to reduce CMA-ES per-iteration time/space from O(d³) to O(d²).
- Core technical idea: approximate C⁻¹/² needed by CSA with A⁻¹ and justify theoretically (Lemma 1) as error shrinking when covariance stabilizes.
- Empirically shows large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) without degrading function-evaluation performance on benchmarks.
- Strength: Substantial practical impact—faster, more memory-efficient CMA-ES likely useful for ML tasks like hyperparameter tuning and policy search.
- Strength: Sound and elegant approach with clear motivation and logical presentation linking approximation to convergence properties.
- Strength: Paper is well-written, organized, and easy to follow.
- Weakness: Insufficient validation of the key approximation—Lemma 1 assumes normalized covariance convergence, which may not hold during runs; no direct empirical measures of approximation error.
- Weakness: Limited technical novelty—builds on prior Cholesky-update work (e.g., Krause & Igel 2015) and should better delineate new contributions.
- Weakness: Insufficient precision in analysis and derivations—complexity claims need clearer Θ-notation comparison and the decomposition of the rank-µ update into rank-one updates is omitted.
- Analysis subpoint: Asymptotic O(µd²) matches delayed-update implementations; reported speedup stems from avoiding O(d³) operations and better constants, which needs clearer explanation.
- Suggested fix: Provide direct empirical validation of CSA approximation by comparing true pσ,t+1 (using C⁻¹/²) and approximated pσ,t+1 (using A⁻¹) over iterations (e.g., norms or angles).
- Suggested fix: Clarify complexity analysis to explain practical speed advantages despite similar asymptotic complexity.
- Suggested fix: Include a brief derivation or explanation of decomposing the rank-µ update into rank-one updates used in Algorithm 1.
- Suggested fix: Correct minor notation errors (e.g., αk in Algorithm 2, n vs. d in Table 1).
- Overall recommendation: Practical contribution is strong but scientific rigor is lacking; lean toward rejection unless authors add direct empirical validation and tighten analysis, after which reviewer would reconsider.
