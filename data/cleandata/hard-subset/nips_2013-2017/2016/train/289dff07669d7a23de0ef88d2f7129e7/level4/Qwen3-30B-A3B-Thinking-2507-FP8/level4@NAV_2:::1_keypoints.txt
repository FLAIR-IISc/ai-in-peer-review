- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: replaces matrix square root in CSA with inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the rotational error from this change vanishes asymptotically as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluation counts while reducing runtime complexity from O(d³) to O(μd²) and lowering memory requirements.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-update work but uniquely extends it to full CMA-ES and supplies a convergence argument missing from earlier variants.  
- Connects to information-geometric optimization and ML applications of CMA-ES, making it relevant to NIPS topics.  
- Quality: rigorous theoretical analysis and strong empirical validation across 5 benchmark functions with 100 trials each.  
- Quality: fair comparisons to CMA-ES-Ref (delayed decomposition) and CMA-ES/d demonstrate runtime benefits.  
- Clarity: exposition and algorithms are precise and reproducible (detailed experimental setup d=4–256, 100 trials).  
- Clarity: eigenvalue extraction from the Cholesky factor presented as a useful free byproduct.  
- Originality: distinct from diagonal approximations and delayed updates, uniquely justifies CSA modification for full CMA-ES.  
- Significance: substantial practical speedups (up to ~20× at d=64) and reduced memory make it impactful for high-dimensional ML tasks and easy to integrate (Shark library).  
- Minor concern: reduced runtime advantage for Cigar/Discus at d=256 likely due to L2 cache effects—authors should clarify this in revision.  
- Overall recommendation: well-written, technically sound, compelling case for adopting Cholesky-CMA-ES; acceptance recommended.
