- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).
- Main algorithmic change: omission of estimating the random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.
- Provides an asymptotic argument (under specific convergence assumptions) that E_t converges to the identity, implying equivalence of the two schemes.
- Empirical validation via runtime and convergence plots on benchmark functions.
- Comparison against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that use delayed full covariance updates.
- Reports substantial speed-ups without loss of optimization performance on tested synthetic problems.
- Practical limitation: total optimization time is often dominated by expensive black-box function evaluations, making internal CMA-ES runtime less critical in many applications.
- Applicability caveat: typical CMA-ES use-cases have dimensions of only a few tens, so the high-dimensional, fast-evaluation scenario targeted here may be uncommon.
- Reviewer suggests visualizing the evolution of E_t along optimization trajectories (e.g., via norms).
- Suggests running full CMA-ES and the approximation simultaneously to directly compare temporal fluctuations of E_t.
- Recommends including baseline results for standard CMA-ES without delay strategies for a more complete comparison.
- Proposes a simulation study varying quadratic-form spectra (as in Stich & Müller 2012) to analyze solution quality and E_t error versus spectral distribution.
- Recommends adding a real-world experiment (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) where speed is critical and parameter dimensionality is moderate.
