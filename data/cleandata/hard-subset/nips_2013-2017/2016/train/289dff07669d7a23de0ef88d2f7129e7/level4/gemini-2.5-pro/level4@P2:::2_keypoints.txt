- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace O(d³) eigen-decomposition with quadratic-time updates of the triangular Cholesky factor to reduce runtime.
- Core approximation: replace CSA’s inverse matrix square root C⁻¹/² with the inverse Cholesky factor A⁻¹ and argue error vanishes as covariance shape converges.
- Empirical claim: significant wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) with no increase in function evaluations on benchmarks.
- Strength — practical significance: addresses a major bottleneck enabling CMA-ES use in higher-dimensional, ML-relevant problems.
- Strength — efficiency: achieves O(μd²) time and O(d²) memory, improving over standard O(d³) cost.
- Strength — clarity: paper is generally well-written and motivates the solution and theory clearly.
- Weakness — novelty: perceived as an incremental extension of Krause & Igel (2015); authors should clarify technical challenges in extending rank-one to rank-μ updates.
- Weakness — insufficient validation of core approximation: lacks direct empirical comparison of approximated vs. true CSA path and transient-phase behavior.
- Suggested experiments: compare norms of approximated vs. true evolution paths and plot ||Eᵀ_{t-1}E_t - I||_F over runs to show rotation convergence.
- Weakness — complexity analysis: needs a more detailed Θ/operation-count comparison versus amortized CMA-ES implementations to justify practical speedups.
- Weakness — completeness/reproducibility: include the decomposition of the full covariance update into the sequence of rank-one updates used in Algorithm 1.
- Minor issues: typo/ambiguity in Algorithm 2 (γ usage) and notational inconsistencies (d vs. n in Table 1).
- Recommendation: not ready for acceptance—promising and potentially impactful, but requires stronger novelty justification and direct empirical/theoretical validation of the CSA approximation.
