- Main contribution: apply triangular Cholesky factorization to CMA-ES to eliminate square-root operations and reduce matrix inversion cost.  
- Authors demonstrate the modification is well-justified and does not harm algorithm performance.  
- Proposed method lowers runtime complexity without significantly increasing objective function evaluations.  
- Paper provides a valuable implementation of Cholesky factorization within the CMA-ES framework.  
- Reviewer requests further theoretical explanation for why the approach is “numerically more stable.”  
- Empirical results show comparable evaluations but reduced wall-clock time, with reductions increasing as dimensionality grows.  
- Reviewer suggests integrating low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loschilov 2015) to improve scalability.  
- Notes that high dimensionality remains critical in practical applications (e.g., training neural networks via direct policy search).
