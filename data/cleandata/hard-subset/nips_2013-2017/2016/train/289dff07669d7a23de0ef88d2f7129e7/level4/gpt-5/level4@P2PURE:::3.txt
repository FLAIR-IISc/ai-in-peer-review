This paper studies the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (Cholesky CMA-ES) and updates the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-μ terms, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. Under the assumption that the covariance converges, the paper proves that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not materially change the algorithm’s behavior. It also reports experimental results on algorithm performance.

1. Contribution: While the paper extends the triangular Cholesky factor update to include both rank-1 and rank-μ terms, I do not find the contribution substantial given Krause et al. (2015), which already applies a rank-1 update to the triangular Cholesky factor. The present work appears to be a straightforward generalization to rank-μ via a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2. Convergence: The convergence of the covariance C_t in CMA-ES has been established theoretically (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), even though that analysis does not use the exact CMA-ES recursions. This result increases confidence in Lemma 1.

3. Other issues:
   3.1. In Equation (1), the denominator should be sigma_t rather than sigma. The same issue appears in footnote 2 on page 3.
   3.2. Line 94: in the phrase “which can by be motivated by principles from ...,” the word “by” should be removed.
