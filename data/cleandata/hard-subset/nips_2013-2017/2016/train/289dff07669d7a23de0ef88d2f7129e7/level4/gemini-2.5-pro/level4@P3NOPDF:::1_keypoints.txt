- Core contribution: introduces a CMA-ES variant updating covariance via Cholesky factors, reducing update time from O(N^3) to O(N^2) and lowering memory needs.  
- Empirical result: achieves these efficiency gains without degrading convergence in function-evaluation terms.  
- Major significance: important practical advance for derivative-free optimization, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Practical benefits: substantial computational complexity reduction, lower memory overhead, and direct access to covariance eigenvalues for analysis/termination.  
- Experimental validation: rigorous and convincing empirical study showing preserved convergence and dramatically reduced wall-clock time, with gains increasing with dimensionality.  
- Presentation quality: paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and methodologically thorough.  
- Relevance: highly relevant to the NIPS community due to ties to ML applications (RL, hyperparameter tuning) and information geometry.  
- Recommendation: strong endorsement for acceptance—likely to have lasting impact and wide adoption.  
- Minor concern 1: clarify whether all CMA-ES variants were implemented in the same language/libraries to ensure wall-clock speedups stem from algorithmic, not implementation, differences.  
- Minor concern 2: explain why performance gaps narrow for Cigar and Discus functions when moving from dimension 64 to 256, as noted in Figure 2.
