This paper studies the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (Cholesky CMA-ES) and extends the update of the triangular Cholesky factor to include both rank-1 and rank-μ terms, implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. Under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges, the authors prove that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) does not materially change the algorithm’s behavior. The paper also reports empirical results on optimization benchmarks.

Assessment of contribution:
- While the topic is relevant, the main algorithmic contribution appears incremental. Krause et al. (2015) already proposed a rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The present work largely generalizes that approach to rank-μ by composing a sequence of rank-1 updates, which is a natural and straightforward extension rather than a substantial conceptual advance.
- The theoretical result shows that the modified CSA is essentially behavior-preserving under covariance convergence. This is useful but limited in scope, as it relies on an assumption rather than establishing convergence under the exact algorithmic dynamics.

Theoretical context:
- The assumption on covariance convergence aligns with prior theoretical analyses of CMA-ES (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), even though those results are not derived from the exact CMA-ES update equations. Citing and discussing this connection more explicitly would strengthen the credibility of Lemma 1 and clarify the conditions under which the result is expected to hold.

Experiments:
- The paper provides empirical comparisons, but the review cannot fully assess their strength without more details on experimental design, statistical rigor, and effect sizes. Ensuring fair baselines (including Krause et al., 2015) and ablating the impact of the rank-μ extension versus pure rank-1 updates would better substantiate the claimed benefits.

Writing and presentation:
- The manuscript is generally understandable, though some passages would benefit from clearer positioning relative to prior work, especially in articulating what is novel beyond the sequential composition of rank-1 updates.

Minor issues and typos:
- Equation (1): The denominator should use σ_t (time-indexed), not σ. The same issue appears in Footnote 2 on page 3.
- Line 94: “which can by be motivated by principles from …” — remove “by.”
- Please proofread for similar indexing and typographical consistency throughout.

Overall:
- The paper addresses a practical variant of CMA-ES and offers a modest theoretical reassurance about CSA under a convergence assumption, along with empirical results. However, the novelty over Krause et al. (2015) is limited, as the core idea is a direct rank-μ generalization via sequential rank-1 updates. Clarifying theoretical assumptions, strengthening the experimental evidence, and tightening the writing would improve the paper.
