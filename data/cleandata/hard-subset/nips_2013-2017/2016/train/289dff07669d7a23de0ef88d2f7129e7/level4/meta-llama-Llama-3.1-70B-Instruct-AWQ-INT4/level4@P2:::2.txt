This paper adopts the Cholesky decomposition instead of the eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of the CMA-ES. The authors propose a numerically stable quadratic-time covariance matrix update scheme with minimal memory requirements based on maintaining triangular Cholesky factors. This approach requires a modification of the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) mechanism in the CMA-ES, where the inverse of the square root of the covariance matrix is replaced by the inverse of the triangular Cholesky factor.

The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method. The experimental results, especially the speedup rates, are promising and suggest that the proposed method may become a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future. However, the originality of the paper is limited, as the method is an extension of previous work, specifically "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies". The authors should clarify the differences and contributions of their work compared to the previous study.

Several important contents are missing or not clearly discussed in the paper. Firstly, the authors should show that their algorithm is equivalent to the standard CMA-ES update formula (2), even if it has been proven in previous work. Secondly, the authors claim that the computational complexity of their algorithm is O(μd^2), which is the same as CMA-ES-Ref. However, they should provide a detailed discussion on why their algorithm is faster, possibly using the Θ notation. Lastly, the authors claim that the error in the path is small and decreases over time, contrary to Hansen's claim. Since the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to validate this claim, and comparing only the final performance is not sufficient.

Some minor comments include checking the notations carefully, such as correcting "wk" to "αk" in Algorithm 2 and "n" to "d" in Table 1. Additionally, "F" should be corrected to "f" in line 185.

In its current form, the paper is not qualified for NIPS due to the lack of clarity and discussion on the mentioned points. However, with proper revisions and additions, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.
