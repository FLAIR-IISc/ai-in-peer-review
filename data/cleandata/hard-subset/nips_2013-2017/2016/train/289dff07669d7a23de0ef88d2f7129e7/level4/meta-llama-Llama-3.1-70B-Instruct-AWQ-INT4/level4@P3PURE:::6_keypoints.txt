- Paper renames an existing method as "Cholesky-CMA-ES" without citing Suttorp et al. (2009), which originally introduced that term and algorithm.  
- The contribution is incremental: essentially calling an existing rankOneUpdate in a loop for all individuals (single "for" loop).  
- Empirical discrepancy on the Cigar function (different plateau values) may stem from plotting medians rather than a true algorithmic difference.  
- Authors should empirically compare to Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically compare to Krause et al.'s variant; current comparisons are incomplete.  
- The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading — not optimal for Sphere or separable functions where full covariance is unnecessary.  
- The proposed method is only about twice as efficient as Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES, but Suttorp’s method is omitted from key figures, which may mislead readers.  
- The paper incorrectly asserts numerical instability of prior work; Suttorp et al. report their method is not prone to such instabilities.  
- The complexity remains quadratic, so the approach does not substantially change CMA-ES scaling for large-scale optimization.  
- Acknowledgement that the modification might still be the most efficient way to save time and memory when maintaining the full covariance matrix.  
- To strengthen the paper: (a) resolve the naming/citation issue, (b) run experiments including Suttorp and Krause variants and update figures (or move full plots to supplement), (c) clearly explain or justify the central "for" loop in Algorithm 1.  
- Overall assessment: work is too incremental; the main algorithmic novelty is minimal.  
- Minor factual correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically performed periodically in implementations, not at every update step as claimed.
