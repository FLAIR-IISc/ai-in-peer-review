- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to approximate covariance adaptation, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) with μ typically O(log d).  
- Key technical change: avoids estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t each step; under the assumption of covariance convergence, shows asymptotic E_t → I, making the methods equivalent.  
- Empirical evaluation: runtime and convergence plots on synthetic benchmarks comparing to two delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Main contribution: a solid, practical speed-up of CMA-ES without observed loss in optimization performance on the tested benchmarks.  
- Practical caveat: in many real applications objective evaluations dominate runtime and problem dimensions are moderate, so the overhead reduction matters mainly when evaluations are very cheap and dimensions are large.  
- Suggested experiment: track evolution of E_t along runs (e.g., via matrix norms) by running standard CMA-ES and computing the Cholesky approximation in parallel.  
- Suggested baseline: report actual wall-clock runtimes and include standard (non-delayed) CMA-ES in comparisons.  
- Suggested robustness test: on quadratics, vary the spectrum continuously and compare solution quality and E_t error versus spectral distribution.  
- Suggested real-world test: add an application where speed matters (e.g., policy-gradient methods in real-time robotics) to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: well-motivated and relevant speed-up with convincing initial evidence; additional experiments would clarify when the approximation is tight and broaden comparative validation.
