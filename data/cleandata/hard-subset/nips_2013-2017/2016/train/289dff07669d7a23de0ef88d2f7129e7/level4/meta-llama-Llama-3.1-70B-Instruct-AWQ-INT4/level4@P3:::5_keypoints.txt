- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: update covariance via a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance or its square root.  
- Reduces covariance update complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) (μ typically O(log d)) and achieves optimal memory complexity.  
- Provides theoretical justification that the Cholesky modification does not degrade algorithm performance.  
- Empirical results: matches standard CMA-ES in number of objective evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time.  
- Speed-up increases with problem dimensionality, making the approach attractive for larger-scale problems.  
- Demonstrated comparisons include state-of-the-art CMA-ES with delayed covariance decomposition.  
- Notes that in many applications the black-box objective runtime dominates, so CMA-ES runtime savings may be negligible.  
- Points out typical CMA-ES use-cases have modest dimensionality (tens of variables), limiting immediate applicability.  
- Recommends additional experiments: track evolution of E_t on selected trajectories and in different norms to show empirical fluctuations.  
- Requests runtime and performance comparisons including CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.  
- Suggests a simulation study on quadratic functions with continuously varied spectra (e.g., Stich & Müller-style) to assess solution quality and E_t error versus spectral distribution.  
- Proposes adding a real-world experiment (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) where runtime speed matters and dimensions are moderate.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and efficient update, solid theoretical backing, extensive experiments, and clear speed gains without performance loss.  
- Arguments against acceptance: possibly too specialized for the conference audience, limited practical scenarios where it matters, and paper needs additional experiments/plots.
