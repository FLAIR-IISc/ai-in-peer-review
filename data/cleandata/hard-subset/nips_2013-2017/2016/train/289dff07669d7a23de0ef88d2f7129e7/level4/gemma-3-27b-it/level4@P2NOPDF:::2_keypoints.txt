- Positive: Novel application of Cholesky decomposition within CMA-ES to reduce computation and storage; paper is well-written and method appears sound.
- Positive: Reported experimental results show encouraging speedup, suggesting potential to become a standard CMA-ES variant.
- Major concern: Originality is unclear — approach appears highly similar to “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies”; clearer differentiation needed.
- Methodological completeness: Must explicitly establish equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2) in the paper for clarity and rigor.
- Complexity claim: The O(μd²) computational-cost claim needs tighter justification (use Θ-notation) to explain the observed practical speedup despite matching asymptotic complexity.
- Empirical validation: The claim of decreasing error in path length contradicts Hansen’s results and requires experiments tracking error evolution over time, not just final comparisons.
- Minor corrections: Fix notation inconsistencies — w_k in Algorithm 2 should be α_k; n in Table 1 should be d; “F” on line 185 should be “f”.
- Recommendation: In current form the paper is not suitable for acceptance; addressing originality, analytical completeness, and additional empirical evidence would substantially improve it.
