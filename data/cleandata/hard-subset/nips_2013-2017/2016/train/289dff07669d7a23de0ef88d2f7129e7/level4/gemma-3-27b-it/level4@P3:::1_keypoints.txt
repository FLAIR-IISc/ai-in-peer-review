- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: uses triangular Cholesky factorization to update covariance in CMA-ES.  
- Reduces computational complexity and memory requirements compared to standard CMA-ES.  
- Maintains comparable performance in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Provides theoretical justification for the approximation (Lemma 1) and sound mathematical grounding.  
- Thorough numerical evaluation across benchmark functions demonstrates practical benefits.  
- Readily available eigenvalues from the Cholesky factor are an additional advantage.  
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and motivates the work effectively.  
- Relevant to NIPS due to links with machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Strength: significant practical impact by addressing a key computational bottleneck in CMA-ES.  
- Strength: reduced memory footprint and faster runtime make the algorithm more usable for medium to large-scale problems.  
- Strength: experimental setup is comprehensive and results are convincingly presented.  
- Weakness: insufficient implementation details (language, compilation flags, CPU specs) hinder reproducibility.  
- Weakness: dimensionality-scaling claim not fully supported for Cigar and Discus at d=256; needs explanation.  
- Weakness: convergence properties relative to standard CMA-ES need a more formal, rigorous discussion.  
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, theoretically sound improvement with convincing empirical runtime benefits and broad relevance.  
- Arguments against acceptance: reproducibility concerns, unexplained scaling anomaly, and limited convergence analysis.  
- Overall assessment: despite minor weaknesses, the work is a significant advancement and merits acceptance.
