- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES, an update scheme approximating CMA-ES by directly updating a lower-triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Reduces update complexity from O(d³) to O(µ d²), with µ typically O(log d).  
- Core idea: maintain and update Cholesky factor instead of computing full covariance and its square root.  
- Main approximation: omit estimating the random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.  
- Theoretical claim: if covariance matrices converge, E_t converges to identity, making the method asymptotically equivalent to original CMA-ES.  
- Empirical validation: benchmark experiments show speed-up with no observed degradation on synthetic test functions.  
- Comparison: evaluated against state-of-the-art delay-based covariance update variants using runtime and convergence plots.  
- Strength: addresses scalability of CMA-ES and provides significant internal speed-up.  
- Strength: empirical results indicate no loss in optimization performance on tested benchmarks.  
- Strength: provides a theoretical rationale for the approximation under convergence assumptions.  
- Weakness: in many real applications objective evaluation dominates runtime, limiting practical impact.  
- Weakness: typical CMA-ES use cases have low-to-moderate dimension, reducing scenarios where this is most beneficial.  
- Suggestion: visualize E_t evolution and its distance from identity to empirically test the core assumption.  
- Suggestion: include the original full-complexity CMA-ES (no delays) as a baseline for completeness.  
- Suggestion: perform systematic analysis on quadratic functions varying Hessian spectra to study conditioning effects.  
- Suggestion: add a real-world case study (e.g., direct policy search) to demonstrate practical value in high-dimensional, cheap-evaluation settings.
