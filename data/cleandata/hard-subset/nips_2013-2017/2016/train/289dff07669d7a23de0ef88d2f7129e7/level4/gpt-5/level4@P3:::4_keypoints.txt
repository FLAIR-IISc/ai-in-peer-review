- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES maintains the lower-triangular Cholesky factor A and replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA to avoid eigen-/sqrt-decompositions, achieving O(µ d^2) per-iteration time and O(d^2) storage.  
- Novelty: extends triangular-factor updates to full CMA-ES with CSA and rank-µ updates plus a convergence-based justification for the CSA modification.  
- Theoretical claim: using A^{-1} introduces orthogonal rotations E_t whose contribution vanishes asymptotically because CSA exponentially downweights past rotations.  
- Empirical result: matches reference CMA-ES in function-evaluation counts and yields substantial wall-clock speedups (increasing with d) on benchmarks up to d=256.  
- Implementation: method implemented in Shark with LAPACK support.  
- Technical soundness: rank-one triangular update derivations and O(µ d^2) complexity are sound; lemma is plausible but relies on convergence-in-expectation assumptions.  
- Numerical stability: triangular Cholesky parameterization is generally stable and preserves SPD, but the paper lacks quantitative analysis of condition numbers, round-off accumulation, and failure modes.  
- Empirical evaluation quality: benchmark choices and rotations are appropriate, but statistical reporting (variability/confidence) and broader tests (noisy, constrained) are missing.  
- Clarity: paper is well organized with useful pseudocode, but has minor typos, reference formatting issues, and informal use of “ω(d^2)”.  
- Incorrect statement: claiming Cholesky diagonal entries are square roots of eigenvalues is wrong; eigenvalues are squares of singular values, not diagonal entries.  
- Conditioning monitoring: authors should explain how they monitor conditioning without computing eigenvalues (e.g., periodic eigendecompositions, Gershgorin bounds, or triangular-factor estimates).  
- Originality assessment: contribution is meaningful despite prior Cholesky-CMA-ES variants; asymptotically optimal time/storage within full-covariance CMA-ES is valuable.  
- Significance: high practical impact for low-cost evaluations and moderate-to-large d; not sufficient alone for very high-dimensional problems where limited-memory methods are needed.  
- Pros: simple drop-in replacement for CMA-ES, clear algorithmic contribution, strong runtime reductions without extra evaluations, and practical availability of A^{-1} via triangular solves.  
- Cons: numerical stability claims are under-analyzed, theory depends on convergence assumptions (early-phase behavior unexamined), experiments lack breadth, and there are minor inaccuracies.  
- Request 1: provide a bound on CSA discrepancy when replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} (e.g., in terms of ||E_t E_{t-1}^T − I||) and empirical plots over time on hard functions.  
- Request 2: elaborate on numerical stability—analyze round-off accumulation in rank-one updates, describe safeguards to maintain SPD (jitter), and compare to periodic eigendecompositions.  
- Request 3: report results on noisy benchmarks and constrained problems to assess robustness when C does not settle quickly.  
- Request 4: evaluate or discuss hybridization with low-rank/limited-memory approaches (e.g., LM-CMA) for very high-dimensional settings.  
- Request 5: clarify practical computational cost and frequency of forming/applying A^{-1} (triangular solves vs. explicit inversion) within iterations.  
- Recommendation: solid, practically important contribution with a leaning toward acceptance pending minor corrections and expanded discussion on numerical stability and CSA discrepancy.
