- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that reduces covariance update cost from O(d³) to O(μd²) by using a lower-triangular Cholesky factor and removing the need to estimate a random orthogonal matrix Eₜ.  
- Argues asymptotic equivalence to full CMA-ES under the restrictive assumption that the covariance sequence approaches a fixed target and Eₜ → I.  
- Empirically compares against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants (delayed full updates) on benchmarks, showing substantial speedups with no performance loss on synthetic tests.  
- Addresses a real algorithmic bottleneck in CMA-ES (covariance updates) for derivative-free nonconvex optimization.  
- Practical impact is limited: black-box function evaluations typically dominate runtime, so speedups matter mainly when evaluations are extremely fast and dimensionality is high—scenarios that are uncommon.  
- Paper should more clearly contextualize the target application domain where the acceleration is meaningful.  
- Recommend tracking Eₜ evolution (e.g., Frobenius/spectral norms) by running full CMA-ES and Cholesky-CMA-ES in parallel to empirically validate the convergence claim.  
- Recommend including runtime comparisons with standard (no-delay) CMA-ES to quantify realistic speedups.  
- Recommend a spectral sensitivity analysis using quadratic tests with varying condition numbers to show robustness to ill-conditioning and plot Eₜ error vs spectrum.  
- Suggest adding a real-world case study (e.g., real-time robotics policy gradients) to demonstrate practical relevance for low-latency, moderate-dimension problems.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound and empirically convincing, but current presentation understates the narrow practical niche; the suggested experiments would strengthen adoption claims.
