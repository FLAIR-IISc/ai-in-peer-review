This paper proposes a modified Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) that incorporates both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, applied sequentially as a series of rank-1 updates. The authors prove, under the assumption of convergent covariance, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. Experimental results are presented to demonstrate the performance of the algorithm.

The paper explores an extension of the Cholesky CMA-ES by updating the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-μ updates. However, considering the work of Krause et al. (2015), which applied rank-1 updates to the triangular Cholesky factor, the contribution of this paper may not be substantial. The paper presents a straightforward generalization of this procedure to rank-μ updates, applied in a sequence of rank-1 updates.

The convergence of the covariance matrix Ct in CMA-ES has been theoretically analyzed (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), albeit not based on the exact formulae of CMA-ES. This analysis may enhance the confidence in Lemma 1. Nevertheless, there are several issues that need to be addressed. Firstly, in Equation (1), the denominator should be σt instead of σ. A similar error occurs in Footnote 2 on page 3. Secondly, in line 94, the phrase "which can by be motivated by principles from" contains a typographical error; the word "by" should be deleted.

Overall, while the paper presents an extension of the Cholesky CMA-ES, its contribution may be limited by the existing work of Krause et al. (2015). Additionally, the paper requires corrections to address the errors mentioned above.
