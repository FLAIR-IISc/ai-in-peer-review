

This paper introduces a significant improvement to CMA-ES, the state-of-the-art derivative-free optimization algorithm. The authors propose a novel covariance matrix update mechanism based on triangular Cholesky factors, which substantially reduces both computational complexity and memory requirements compared to existing methods. Crucially, the new update maintains the algorithm’s performance—measured by the number of function evaluations needed to reach target accuracy—while significantly accelerating wall-clock time, especially for high-dimensional problems.  

The contribution is well-motivated and rigorously evaluated. The numerical experiments demonstrate that the proposed variant achieves the same optimization performance as standard CMA-ES but with lower per-iteration cost, making it highly suitable for medium- to large-scale applications. Key advantages include reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and the implicit computation of eigenvalues (avoiding explicit extraction). Given CMA-ES’s strong relevance to machine learning—particularly in reinforcement learning, supervised learning, and information geometry—this work is exceptionally timely for NIPS. The authors have clearly established that their variant could become the default implementation in practice, offering tangible benefits without compromising solution quality.  

The paper is well-written, with a clear exposition of the method and thorough experimental validation across benchmark functions and dimensions (up to 256). The technical presentation is precise, and the results are convincingly presented.  

Minor concerns:  
1. **Implementation consistency**: The evaluation compares different CMA-ES implementations. To ensure fair comparison, please clarify the CPU environment (e.g., identical language, compiler, hardware, and optimization flags) across all tested variants.  
2. **Figure 2 interpretation**: The caption states, “the higher the dimensionality, the more pronounced the differences,” but this trend is not observed for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256. A brief explanation for this anomaly would strengthen the analysis.  

Overall, this is a strong contribution with clear practical impact. The proposed variant addresses critical scalability limitations of CMA-ES while preserving its core strengths. The work merits acceptance at NIPS for its technical soundness, relevance to machine learning, and potential to influence future optimization practice.
