- Summary: Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A and replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA to reduce per-iteration cost to O(µ d^2) while matching evaluation counts of standard CMA-ES.
- Key technical idea: avoid cubic decompositions by updating rank-one Cholesky factors and omitting the orthogonal factor E_t, justified by assumed stabilization of rotations (E_{t-1}^T E_t → I).
- Empirical claim: matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluation counts and yields large wall-clock speed-ups that grow with dimension.
- Relation to prior work: builds on Cholesky updates from special CMA-ES variants and connects to information-geometric and low-complexity CMA literature.
- Strength: Clear, simple algorithmic idea achieving optimal storage and reduced asymptotic time complexity.
- Strength: Thoughtful, plausible asymptotic modification of CSA with reasoning why the approximation should be harmless near convergence.
- Strength: Strong empirical runtime improvements and identical evaluation efficiency on benchmarks; easy to integrate into existing code.
- Strength: Good positioning and comparison to delayed-update and other Cholesky-based strategies.
- Weakness: Theoretical justification is heuristic and relies on restrictive convergence assumptions that may not hold in stochastic or non-convergent settings.
- Weakness: Main benefit is runtime, not reducing function-evaluation counts, so limited impact when objective evaluations dominate or d is small.
- Weakness: Experiments are largely synthetic and lack a real-world case where CMA-ES overhead is critical.
- Weakness: Missing explicit baseline runtime comparisons for standard CMA-ES with forced per-iteration decomposition versus built-in delay strategies.
- Quality: Method is technically sound and numerically stable; asymptotic CSA argument is plausible but not formal.
- Quality: Empirical evaluation is thorough on standard benchmarks and reproducible (LAPACK, Shark integration).
- Clarity: Paper is well written; the role of A^{-1} vs omitted E_t and complexity consequences are clearly explained.
- Originality: Novel extension of Cholesky-based updates to standard CMA-ES with a principled CSA approximation, despite related prior ideas.
- Significance: High for settings with cheap evaluations and moderate-to-large d; limited immediate impact where evaluations dominate.
- Suggested experiments: Track E_t or E_{t-1}^T E_t over runs to quantify CSA approximation error; run paired seeds for direct comparison.
- Suggested experiments: Report standard CMA-ES runtimes with forced per-iteration decomposition and with default delays to isolate costs.
- Suggested experiments: Systematic study on quadratic functions with varied spectra to assess evaluation counts and CSA error vs conditioning.
- Suggested addition: Include at least one real-world task (e.g., fast simulators or small-model hyperparameter tuning) to demonstrate practical gains.
- Pros for acceptance: Elegant, simple modification with clear complexity benefits, strong empirical speed-ups, solid links to prior work, implementation-ready.
- Cons against acceptance: Relies on restrictive theoretical assumptions, limited practical relevance in many ML settings, and lacks real-world showcase.
- Overall assessment: Solid, practically relevant contribution that speeds full-covariance CMA-ES without harming evaluation efficiency; recommended for acceptance with suggested additional analyses.
