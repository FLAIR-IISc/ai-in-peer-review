

This paper employs Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition to reduce time and storage complexity in CMA-ES. The paper is well-written, with a reasonable method and promising experimental results (particularly speedup rates), suggesting potential to establish a new standard for CMA-ES. However, the originality is limited, as this work appears to be a trivial extension of prior research ("A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies"), with methods largely identical. Please clarify if this assessment is incorrect. Additionally, several critical elements are missing:  
1) The equivalence to Equation (2) must be explicitly demonstrated, despite prior proofs and space constraints, as it is essential for this paper.  
2) The claimed complexity of O(μd²) matches CMA-ES-Ref; a detailed discussion using Θ notation is required to justify the speed advantage.  
3) The assertion that path error is small and decreases over time contradicts Hansen’s findings; experimental validation beyond final performance metrics is necessary.  
Minor issues:  
- Algorithm 2: wk should be αk; Table 1: n should be d.  
- Line 185: 'F' should be 'f'.  
Given these deficiencies, the paper is not suitable for NIPS in its current form.
