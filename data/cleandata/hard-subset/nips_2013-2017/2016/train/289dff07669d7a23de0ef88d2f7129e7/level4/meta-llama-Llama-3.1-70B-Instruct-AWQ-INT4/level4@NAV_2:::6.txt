The paper proposes a version of CMA-ES with optimal covariance update and storage complexity. The authors suggest that the proposed modification does not change the behavior of the algorithm but makes it cheaper to run. The proposed method is called Cholesky-CMA-ES, although this name was first introduced by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009 for a similar algorithm. The authors should acknowledge this and avoid potential confusion.

The proposed method is an incremental modification to the work of Krause et al. in 2015. The only algorithmic contribution is the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function in a loop for all individuals. Empirically, there are some differences in the results, such as the plateau in Figure 1(b) with different objective function values for CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES.

The paper should compare the proposed approach to Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. empirically and Cholesky-CMA-ES by Krause et al. algorithmically. The claim about optimal covariance update and storage complexity is somewhat misleading, as it is not optimal for all cases, such as the Sphere function where the covariance matrix is not needed, or separable functions where only the diagonal is required. The proposed approach is only twice as efficient as the omitted Cholesky CMA-ES by Suttorp et al., which is claimed to be prone to numerical instabilities, although the original paper suggested the opposite.

The omission of Cholesky CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. in Figure 3 can be misleading regarding the actual contribution of the paper. While the proposed approach may not change the complexity of CMA-ES for large-scale optimization, it might be the best modification to save time and memory when the full covariance matrix is considered.

Overall, the work is somewhat incremental, and the main algorithmic contribution is the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function in a loop. The authors should either resolve the naming issue, perform experiments with variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al., and update the figures, or better explain the significance of the proposed modification.

Minor notes: The statement "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is not accurate, as in proper implementations, it is done periodically.
