The authors propose an adaptation of the CMA-ES algorithm, dubbed Cholesky-CMA-ES, which supposedly reduces the computational cost without altering the algorithm's behavior. However, the name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" has been previously used by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009 for a similar approach, and the authors fail to acknowledge this. The proposed method builds upon the work of Krause et al. (2015), with the primary contribution being the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function for all individuals, which is a minor incremental modification. The empirical results show a difference in the Cigar function, but this might be attributed to the plotting of median results. A comparison with Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause's variant is necessary to validate the proposed approach. The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is somewhat misleading, as it is not optimal for functions like the Sphere function, where the covariance matrix is unnecessary. The proposed approach is only twice as efficient as Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES, which is also claimed to be prone to numerical instabilities, contrary to the original paper. The omission of Suttorp's method in Figure 3 may mislead the reader about the paper's contribution. While the proposed modification might be useful for saving time and memory when considering the full covariance matrix, it does not significantly impact large-scale optimization due to its quadratic complexity. Overall, the work seems too incremental, and the primary contribution is a simple loop. To improve the paper, the authors should: a) address the naming issue, b) conduct experiments with Suttorp's and Krause's variants and update the figures, and c) provide a clearer explanation of the main algorithmic contribution. Additionally, the statement regarding eigenvalue decomposition should be corrected, as it is typically performed periodically, not in each update step.
