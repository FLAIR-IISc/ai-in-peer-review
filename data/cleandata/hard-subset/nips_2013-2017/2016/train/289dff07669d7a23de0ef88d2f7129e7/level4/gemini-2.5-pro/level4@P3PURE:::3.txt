This paper proposes an extension to the Cholesky Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) that incorporates both rank-1 and rank-µ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. The rank-µ update is implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors provide a theoretical analysis showing that, under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges, their modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper concludes with experimental results evaluating the algorithm's performance.

**Major Comments:**

1.  The primary concern is the significance of the paper's contribution. The proposed method extends the work of Krause et al. (2015), which introduced the rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The main novelty of this work appears to be the generalization to a rank-µ update by implementing it as a sequence of rank-1 updates. This comes across as a straightforward extension, and the paper would be stronger if it better articulated the technical challenges and advantages of this specific formulation.

2.  The theoretical analysis in Lemma 1 relies on the assumption that the covariance matrix C_t converges. The authors could strengthen this claim by referencing existing theoretical work that supports it. For instance, Beyer (ECJ, 2014) provides a theoretical analysis of the convergence of C_t in CMA-ES, even if not for the exact formulation used here. Discussing such related work would increase confidence in the paper's theoretical foundation.

**Minor Issues:**

There are a few minor typographical errors that should be corrected:

*   In Equation (1), the denominator should contain `\sigma_t` instead of `\sigma`. The same error appears in footnote 2 on page 3.
*   In line 94, the phrase "which can by be motivated by principles from..." contains a superfluous "by".
