- Primary contribution: apply triangular Cholesky factorization to CMA-ES to replace the costly matrix square root in covariance updates.  
- Theoretical justification provided showing the modification does not degrade algorithm performance.  
- Empirical benefit: reduces wall-clock time per iteration with larger savings in higher dimensions while keeping similar numbers of objective evaluations.  
- Overall assessment: a valuable, practical improvement to CMA-ES.  
- Clarification requested: provide a more detailed theoretical explanation for the claim that the method is "numerically more stable."  
- Suggestion/request: consider combining the approach with low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) for very high-dimensional problems like neural-network policy search.
