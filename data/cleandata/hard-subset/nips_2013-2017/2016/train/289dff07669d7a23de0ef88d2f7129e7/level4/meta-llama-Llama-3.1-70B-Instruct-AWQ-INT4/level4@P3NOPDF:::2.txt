This paper proposes an alternative to the eigen-decomposition in the CMA-ES algorithm by utilizing the Cholesky decomposition, which reduces time and storage complexity. The manuscript is generally well-written, and the proposed method appears reasonable. The experimental results, particularly the speedup rates, are promising and suggest that the proposed method may become a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future.

However, the originality of this work is limited, as it seems to be a trivial extension of the previous work "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The methods presented in this paper are essentially identical to those in the previous work. I would appreciate clarification on this point if I am incorrect.

Furthermore, several crucial aspects are missing from the manuscript. Firstly, it is essential to demonstrate the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2), even if the proof has been established in previous work. Due to space constraints, a brief explanation or reference to the original proof would be sufficient.

Secondly, the authors claim that the computational complexity of their algorithm is O(\mu d^2), which is identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A detailed discussion, possibly using \Theta notation, is necessary to explain why the proposed algorithm is faster. This is particularly important, as the claim of improved performance is a key contribution of the paper.

Thirdly, the authors assert that the error in the path is small and decreases over time, contradicting Hansen's claim. Since the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to validate this claim. Comparing only the final performance is insufficient; intermediate results or additional analyses are necessary to support this assertion.

Additionally, I have a few minor comments. The authors should carefully review their notation, as there are several inconsistencies. For example, 'wk' in Algorithm 2 should be '\alphak', and 'n' in Table 1 should be 'd'. Furthermore, 'F' in line 185 should be 'f'.

In its current form, I do not believe that this paper is suitable for publication at NIPS. Addressing the above concerns and providing additional clarification and supporting evidence would be necessary to strengthen the manuscript.
