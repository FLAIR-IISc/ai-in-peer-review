This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES. The authors claim that their modification does not change the behavior of the algorithm but reduces its computational cost. However, the name Cholesky-CMA-ES is misleading, as it was first introduced by Suttorp et al. in 2009 for a similar algorithm.

The proposed method is an incremental modification to the work of Krause et al. (2015). The only algorithmic contribution is the application of the rankOneUpdate function in a loop for all individuals. While the authors provide empirical results comparing their approach to the standard CMA-ES, they fail to compare it to the Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause et al. (2015), which is essential to evaluate the novelty and impact of their work.

The claim about "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is also misleading. The proposed approach is not optimal for all cases, such as when dealing with separable functions or the Sphere function, where the covariance matrix is not necessary. Moreover, the comparison to the omitted Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. (2009) suggests that the proposed approach is only twice cheaper, and the original paper by Suttorp et al. (2009) claimed that their approach is not prone to numerical instabilities, contrary to the statement in this paper.

The paper's contribution to large-scale optimization is also limited, as the complexity remains quadratic. However, the proposed modification might be the best approach to save time and memory when considering the full covariance matrix.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should:

* Resolve the naming issue by acknowledging the previous work by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause et al. (2015).
* Perform experiments comparing their approach to the variants by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause et al. (2015) and update the figures accordingly.
* Better explain the main algorithmic contribution, which is the application of the rankOneUpdate function in a loop for all individuals, and clarify why this is not a trivial extension of previous work.

Minor notes:

* The statement "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is incorrect, as it is atypical and done periodically in proper implementations.

Quality: The paper is technically sound, but the lack of comparison to previous work and the misleading naming and claims reduce its overall quality.

Clarity: The paper is well-written, but the omission of essential comparisons and the misleading statements reduce its clarity.

Originality: The paper's originality is limited, as it builds upon previous work without providing significant new insights or contributions.

Significance: The paper's significance is also limited, as it does not provide a substantial improvement over previous work, and its contribution to large-scale optimization is restricted.
