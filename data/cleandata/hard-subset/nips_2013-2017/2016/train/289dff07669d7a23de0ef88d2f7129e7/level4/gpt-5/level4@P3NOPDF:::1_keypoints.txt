- Proposes a triangular Cholesky-factor covariance update for CMA-ES that reduces computational cost and memory while preserving core behavior.
- Empirical results show matching function-evaluation efficiency to standard CMA-ES with substantial wall-clock speedups, especially in high dimensions.
- Technical strength: elegant, well-motivated factorization that maintains positive definiteness and adaptive covariance behavior.
- Practical impact: lower runtime and memory for medium-to-large problems and easier access to covariance spectral information for diagnostics.
- Empirical thoroughness: careful, convincing experiments demonstrating preserved optimization efficiency and runtime gains.
- Community relevance: improvement targets a key CMA-ES bottleneck and could become a default for larger-scale derivative-free optimization.
- Requests clarification on timing fairness: specify implementation languages, compiler flags, and whether optimized BLAS/LAPACK/MKL/OpenBLAS were used.
- Requests clarification on threading and hardware parity: state single- vs multi-threading and confirm identical CPU/cache/memory configurations.
- Requests disclosure of RNG, seeding, and numeric precision differences (single vs double) that might affect timing.
- Asks for explanation of high-dimensional anomalies (e.g., Cigar and Discus at d=256), such as axis-aligned structure, step-size dynamics, or cache effects reducing Cholesky advantage.
- Suggests adding robustness/numerical-stability details: frequency of re-factorization, regularization, and behavior under extreme condition numbers and long runs.
- Significance: reduces memory, per-iteration cost, and provides spectral information, making it practically impactful for medium/large problems.
- Presentation: paper is clear, well-organized, and well-motivated with a relevant experimental section.
- Recommendation: strong accept, pending minor clarifications on implementation/timing and explanations for observed anomalies.
