The paper proposes a version of CMA-ES with optimal covariance update and storage complexity. The authors suggest that the proposed modification does not change the behavior of the algorithm but makes it cheaper to run. However, the proposed method, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, shares its name with a similar algorithm introduced by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009, which is well known. The authors should acknowledge this and clarify the distinction between their variant and the existing one.

The proposed method is an incremental modification to the work of Krause et al. (2015). The main algorithmic contribution appears to be the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function in a loop for all individuals. However, the authors should provide a clearer explanation of the significance of this modification and why it is not simply a minor extension of existing work.

Empirically, the paper presents a comparison of the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES with other CMA-ES variants. However, the comparison is incomplete, as it does not include the Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause et al. (2015). The authors should perform additional experiments to include these variants and update the figures accordingly. If the resulting figures become too cluttered, the authors could consider showing only the best-performing variant in the main text and providing the full figures in the supplementary material.

The paper's claim about achieving optimal covariance update and storage complexity is somewhat misleading. While the proposed approach may be more efficient than the standard CMA-ES, it is not optimal in all cases. For example, on separable functions, a diagonal-only approach may be sufficient, and on the Sphere function, the covariance matrix is not needed at all. The authors should provide a more nuanced discussion of the complexity of their approach and its limitations.

Furthermore, the authors' statement that their approach is the best way to save time and memory when considering the full covariance matrix is not entirely convincing. The omitted Cholesky CMA-ES by Suttorp et al. (2009) is claimed to be prone to numerical instabilities, but the original paper suggests the opposite. The authors should provide a more balanced comparison of their approach with existing methods.

Overall, while the paper presents some interesting ideas, the work appears to be too incremental, and the contributions are not entirely clear. The authors should address the naming issue, provide additional experiments, and clarify the significance of their modification to the CMA-ES algorithm.
