- Introduces a Cholesky-based CMA-ES variant that maintains triangular Cholesky factors to reduce per-iteration time and memory while keeping function-evaluation behavior similar.
- Problematic naming: “Cholesky-CMA-ES” is already used by Suttorp et al. (2009) and is not acknowledged by the authors.
- Algorithmic contribution appears minor—a small extension of Krause and Igel (2015) by applying rankOneUpdate in a loop across individuals rather than once.
- Empirical concern: differing plateau levels on the Cigar function may be a plotting/median-computation artifact.
- Paper should include direct empirical comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) and a clear algorithmic comparison to Krause and Igel (2015).
- The claim “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is misleading because full-matrix updates are not optimal for Sphere or separable problems where covariance is unnecessary or diagonal suffices.
- The proposed method seems only ~2× faster than Suttorp’s omitted variant; the paper’s suggestion that Suttorp’s method is unstable contradicts the original report—omitting that baseline may overstate impact.
- Complexity remains quadratic, so the method is unlikely to change large-scale CMA-ES applicability, though it may be the best option within the full-covariance setting.
- Overall the work is incremental—algorithmically reducible to adding a “for” loop.
- Requested revisions: (a) resolve the naming conflict, (b) include experiments with Suttorp and Krause & Igel variants and update figures (moving full comparisons to the supplement if needed), (c) clarify that the primary algorithmic change is the additional loop or justify otherwise.
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance is typically done periodically, not every iteration.
