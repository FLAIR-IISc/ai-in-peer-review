This paper adopts Cholesky decomposition in place of eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of the CMA-ES algorithm. Overall, the paper is clearly written, the proposed method appears reasonable, and the experimental results – particularly the reported speedup rates – are promising. This work has the potential to become a new standard implementation of CMA-ES. However, while meaningful, the originality of this contribution is limited. The approach feels like a straightforward extension of the work presented in “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,” as the methods are fundamentally similar. I would appreciate clarification on this point. 

Furthermore, some crucial elements are surprisingly absent. First, the authors should demonstrate the equivalence of their algorithm to formula (2). While space is limited and this equivalence has been previously established, reiterating it is essential for the completeness of this paper. Second, the paper claims a computational complexity of O(μd<sup>2</sup>), which is identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A detailed discussion – potentially using Θ notation – is necessary to explain the observed speed improvements. Finally, the authors claim a small and decreasing error in the path length, a claim that contradicts Hansen’s findings. Given the non-deterministic nature of the theoretical analysis, experiments specifically designed to validate this claim are required. Comparing only final performance is insufficient; a more granular analysis of the error’s behavior over time is needed. 

Minor comments include: please carefully review the notation throughout the paper. Specifically, *w<sub>k</sub>* in Algorithm 2 should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, and *n* in Table 1 should be *d*. Also, 'F' should be 'f' on line 185. 

In its current form, I do not believe this paper meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS.
