- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates the triangular Cholesky factor A (C = AA^T) to avoid O(d^3) matrix square-root computations, achieving O(µ d^2) time.

- CSA modification: Replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in cumulative step-size adaptation and theoretically argues (Lemma 1) the approximation error vanishes as covariance shape converges.

- Empirical results: Demonstrates similar function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially at higher dimensions, with clear per-trial plots.

- Complexity: Achieves asymptotically optimal O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) space for full-covariance methods, improving over the standard O(d^3) implementation.

- Strength — practical impact: Addresses a major computational bottleneck in CMA-ES relevant when function evaluations are cheap.

- Strength — novelty and justification: Provides a principled, theoretically supported approximation enabling direct Cholesky updates where prior Cholesky methods were incompatible with standard CSA.

- Strength — empirical validation: Experiments are thorough and well-designed across benchmarks and dimensionalities, supporting the claimed speedups without loss of optimization quality.

- Strength — optimality: The algorithm attains the best possible asymptotic complexity for full-covariance approaches.

- Weakness — numerical stability explanation: The paper asserts numerical stability but lacks a brief theoretical or qualitative explanation for why direct triangular Cholesky updates are more stable than alternatives.

- Weakness — scope/applicability: Should discuss limits for very high-dimensional problems and whether the Cholesky update framework can be combined with low-rank or limited-memory approaches (e.g., adapting rank-one updates).

- Evaluation — quality: High; technically sound with solid theoretical and experimental support.

- Evaluation — clarity: High; well-written, organized, and clearly presented algorithms and results.

- Evaluation — originality: High; the A^{-1} approximation in CSA is a novel distinguishing insight.

- Evaluation — significance: High; makes CMA-ES substantially more practical for medium-to-high dimensions and a strong candidate for becoming the standard implementation.

- Recommendation: Accept — strong, valuable, and well-executed contribution.
