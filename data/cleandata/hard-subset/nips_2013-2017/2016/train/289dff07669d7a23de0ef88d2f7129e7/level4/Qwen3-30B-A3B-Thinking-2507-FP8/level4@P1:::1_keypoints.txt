- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor for covariance adaptation.  
- Targets the O(d²) per-iteration bottleneck from matrix square root computation in standard CMA-ES.  
- Provides theoretical justification that using the Cholesky factor preserves asymptotic optimization behavior.  
- Achieves optimal asymptotic time complexity O(µ d²) for covariance updates (µ typically O(log d)) while keeping storage complexity unchanged.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations but substantially reduces wall-clock time (≈20× faster at d=64), with gains increasing with dimension.  
- Secondary benefits: diagonal of Cholesky yields eigenvalues for free; Cholesky can be inverted in quadratic time to avoid numerical instability; only triangular factor needs storage.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES on multiple benchmark functions and dimensions with 100 trials each.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, balances theory and experiments, and is relevant to machine learning applications.  
- Minor concern 1: need to clarify comparability of implementations (programming language, compiler optimizations, hardware).  
- Minor concern 2: explain anomaly where dimensionality gains do not hold for Cigar and Discus at d=256.  
- Overall assessment: strong, practical contribution with sound theory and experiments; recommendation to accept.
