- Naming conflict: calling the method "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is problematic because T. Suttorp et al. (2009) already introduced that name for a similar, widely recognized algorithm.  
- Literature omission: the paper fails to properly address or compare to Suttorp et al.'s Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause et al.'s related work.  
- Algorithmic novelty limited: the only real change is invoking rankOneUpdate in a loop for all individuals, which closely builds on Krause et al. (2015).  
- Empirical inconsistency: Figure b) (Cigar function) shows differing plateau values between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES, possibly due to median-plotting quirks.  
- Missing comparisons: the paper should include empirical results for Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmic comparison to Krause's variant.  
- Misleading “optimal” claim: calling the method “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is inaccurate (e.g., Sphere and separable functions need less than full covariance).  
- Efficiency overstatement: the method is only about twice as efficient as Suttorp’s omitted variant, not a dramatic improvement.  
- Questionable critique of prior work: paper labels Suttorp’s method as numerically unstable despite original claims to the contrary.  
- Impact overstated for large-scale problems: complexity remains quadratic, so the contribution does not substantially change large-scale CMA-ES applicability.  
- Contribution is incremental: primary change is essentially adding a single for-loop, making the advance minor.  
- Revision requests: (a) address the naming conflict, (b) run and plot experiments including Suttorp and Krause variants (best in main, full in supplement if cluttered), (c) explicitly state that the core contribution is the for-loop or justify otherwise.  
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is not typically performed every update in proper implementations; it is done periodically.
