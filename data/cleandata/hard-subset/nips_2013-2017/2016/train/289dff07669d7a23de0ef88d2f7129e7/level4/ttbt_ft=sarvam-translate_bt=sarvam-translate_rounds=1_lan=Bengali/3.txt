This paper considers the Cholesky-Kovalevsky adaptation evolution technique for the triangular Cholesky factor with both Rank-1 and Rank-μ updates (in the order of Rank-1 updates). The paper proves, under the assumption of the convergence of the covariance, that the modified order-accumulated step-size adaptation algorithm does not significantly change its behavior. This paper presents an examination of the algorithm's performance.

1. This paper considers the Cholesky CMA-ES. It extends the update for the triangular Cholesky factor using both Rank-1 and Rank-μ updates. However, considering Krause et al.'s 2015 work, I do not think its contribution is significant. Krause et al. used Rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. This paper presents a simplification of this method for Rank-μ updates in the order of Rank-1 updates.

2. In general, the convergence of the covariance $Ct$ in CMA-ES is proven through theoretical analysis (Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although it is not based on the exact formula of CMA-ES. This may increase the confidence of Lemma 1.
