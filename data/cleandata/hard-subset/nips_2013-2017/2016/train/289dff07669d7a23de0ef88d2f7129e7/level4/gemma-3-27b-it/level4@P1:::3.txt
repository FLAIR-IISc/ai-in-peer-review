This paper considers a Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy, extending the covariance update to utilize both rank-1 and rank-µ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor. The authors prove, under the assumption of covariance convergence, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm’s behavior. The paper further presents experimental results demonstrating the algorithm's performance.

The paper builds on existing work in Cholesky decomposition within CMA-ES. While it extends the approach of Krause and Igel [2015], which utilized a rank-1 update, to include a rank-µ update implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates, the contribution feels incremental. The core idea is a straightforward generalization of previous work. 

The convergence of the covariance matrix *C<sub>t</sub>* in CMA-ES has been analyzed theoretically (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), though not necessarily based on the exact formulae of the standard CMA-ES. Referencing such analyses could strengthen the justification for Lemma 1.

Several minor issues should be addressed:

1.  In equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σ<sub>t</sub>. The same error occurs in the footnote on page 3.
2.  In line 94, “which can by be motivated…” should read “which can be motivated…”.

**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   The paper provides a theoretically justified extension to existing Cholesky-based CMA-ES implementations.
*   The experimental results demonstrate a significant speedup in runtime compared to standard CMA-ES, particularly in higher dimensions.
*   The work offers a practical improvement for scaling CMA-ES to larger problems where covariance matrix manipulation is computationally expensive.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The core contribution is incremental, building directly on prior work (Krause & Igel, 2015).
*   The theoretical justification relies on an assumption of covariance convergence, which, while plausible, isn’t fully established for all CMA-ES scenarios.
*   The paper contains minor typographical errors (as noted above).

**Overall Assessment:**

The paper presents a valuable optimization to the CMA-ES algorithm, focusing on runtime efficiency through a well-justified use of the Cholesky decomposition. While the contribution isn't groundbreaking, it is a solid engineering improvement with potential practical benefits. The empirical results are convincing. Addressing the minor issues would further strengthen the manuscript. I lean towards acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the raised points.
