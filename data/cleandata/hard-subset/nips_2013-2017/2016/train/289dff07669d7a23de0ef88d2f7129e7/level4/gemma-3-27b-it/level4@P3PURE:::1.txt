CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for (derivative-free) optimization. It combines various learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can be equivalently interpreted as adaptation of an underlying metric. This paper proposes a novel variant for updating the covariance matrix, based on triangular Cholesky factors, that significantly reduces both the computational complexity and the memory requirements for storing the algorithm’s state parameters, compared to previous update schemes. The new method is carefully and thoroughly evaluated numerically. Results demonstrate that this update does not degrade the performance of standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations needed to reach a given target, while substantially reducing the algorithm’s wall-clock time, particularly in high dimensions. 

I believe this paper represents a significant contribution to the field of optimization. I anticipate that this variant of CMA-ES will become a default implementation, especially for medium- and large-scale problems, given its clear advantages over the current standard method: reduced memory usage, lower computational complexity, and the readily available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix. The paper is well-written and the approach is meticulously evaluated. It is particularly appropriate to see this work presented at NIPS, given the strong connections between CMA-ES and machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in areas like reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
