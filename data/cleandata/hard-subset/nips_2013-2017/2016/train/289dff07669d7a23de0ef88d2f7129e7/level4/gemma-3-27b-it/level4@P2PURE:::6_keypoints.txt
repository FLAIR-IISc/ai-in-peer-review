- Naming conflict: calling the method “Cholesky-CMA-ES” is problematic because Suttorp et al. (2009) used the same name and the authors fail to acknowledge that prior work.  
- Incremental contribution: the algorithm mainly builds on Krause et al. (2015); the key change is applying rankOneUpdate iteratively to all individuals rather than once as in Plus strategies.  
- Empirical inconsistency: Cigar function results show a plateau discrepancy between reference CMA-ES and the proposed method, possibly due to plotting medians.  
- Missing empirical comparisons: the paper omits experimental comparison to Suttorp et al.’s Cholesky-CMA-ES implementation.  
- Missing algorithmic comparisons: there is no detailed algorithmic comparison to Krause et al.’s approach.  
- Overstated optimality claim: “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is misleading—many problems (e.g., Sphere, separable functions) don’t need a full covariance or can use diagonal approximations.  
- Limited improvement magnitude: the method gives at best a two-fold cost reduction relative to Suttorp et al.’s variant, not a fundamentally better complexity.  
- Numerical-stability claim disputed: the authors claim Suttorp et al.’s method is numerically unstable, which contradicts the original paper and is unsubstantiated here.  
- Omission in figures: excluding Suttorp et al.’s method from Figure 3 obscures the true contribution and comparative performance.  
- Scalability limitation: quadratic complexity remains, limiting benefits for large-scale optimization despite being a practical improvement when full covariance is required.  
- Incremental nature: algorithmic novelty appears minimal—essentially adding a single “for” loop—raising questions about significance.  
- Requested revisions: (a) resolve the naming conflict; (b) include experimental comparisons to Suttorp and Krause (or add full figures in supplement); (c) clarify the significance of the “for” loop or justify why it’s substantial.  
- Incorrect technical statement: the claim that eigenvalue decomposition is performed in each update is inaccurate—proper implementations usually perform it periodically.
