CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic/randomized algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It combines several learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, which can equivalently be viewed as adapting an underlying metric. This paper proposes a new covariance-matrix update based on triangular Cholesky factors that, compared with previous updates, significantly reduces both the computational complexity and the memory required to store the algorithm’s state. The method is carefully evaluated through comprehensive numerical experiments. The results show that the new update does not degrade the performance of standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations needed to reach a target, while it substantially reduces wall-clock time, particularly in high dimensions.

Overall, this is a significant contribution to optimization. I expect this variant could become the default for medium- to large-scale problems, given its clear advantages over the current default method: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and free access to the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix. The paper is well written and the approach is carefully evaluated. This contribution is well suited to NIPS, as CMA-ES is closely connected to machine learning and information geometry and is used in reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
