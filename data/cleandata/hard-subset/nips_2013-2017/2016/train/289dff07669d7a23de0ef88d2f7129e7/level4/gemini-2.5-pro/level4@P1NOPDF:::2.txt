This paper proposes using a Cholesky decomposition in place of the standard eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of the CMA-ES algorithm. Overall, the paper is clearly written, the proposed method is sound, and the experimental results—particularly the reported speedup rates—are quite promising. This approach has the potential to become a new standard for implementing CMA-ES.

However, despite these merits, I have significant concerns about the paper's contribution in its current form.

My primary concern is the limited originality of the work. The proposed method appears to be a straightforward extension of the prior work, "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The core methods presented in this manuscript seem nearly identical to those in the previous paper. I would welcome clarification from the authors if my understanding on this point is incorrect.

Furthermore, several crucial elements are notably absent from the analysis and discussion:

1.  **Equivalence Proof:** The paper must demonstrate that the proposed algorithm is mathematically equivalent to the standard formulation in Equation (2). While this may have been proven in prior work and omitted for brevity, its inclusion is essential for the paper to be self-contained and for readers to verify the method's correctness.

2.  **Complexity Analysis:** The authors state that the computational complexity is O(μd²), which is the same as the reference CMA-ES implementation. A more detailed analysis is required to formally explain why the proposed algorithm is significantly faster in practice. The paper would be greatly strengthened by a tighter analysis (e.g., using Θ-notation) or a discussion of the constant factors that account for the observed speedup.

3.  **Validation of Claims on Path Error:** The authors claim that the approximation error in the evolution path is small and decreases over time. This claim appears to contradict previous findings by Hansen. Since the theoretical analysis provided is not deterministic, this assertion requires direct empirical validation. The current experiments, which only compare final performance metrics, are insufficient. The authors should conduct and present experiments that specifically measure and track this error over the course of the optimization to substantiate their claim.

Finally, here are some minor comments:

*   Please review the notation for consistency. For instance, `wk` in Algorithm 2 should likely be `αk`, and `n` in Table 1 should be `d`.
*   There appears to be a typo in line 185, where 'F' should be 'f'.

In summary, while the proposed method shows promise, the concerns regarding limited novelty and the omission of key analytical and experimental details are significant. Due to these issues, I cannot recommend acceptance of this paper for NIPS in its current form.
