- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art randomized derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (metric adaptation).  
- The paper proposes a new covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based update substantially reduces computational complexity and memory usage compared to previous updates.  
- The approach is particularly advantageous for medium-to-large-scale optimization problems.  
- Numerical evaluation shows the new method preserves the standard CMA-ES performance in function-evaluation count.  
- Experiments demonstrate substantial wall-clock time reductions, especially in high dimensions.  
- The contribution is significant and likely to become a default implementation for larger problems due to reduced memory, lower complexity, and available eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and appropriate for NIPS given connections to ML, information geometry, reinforcement and supervised learning.  
- Minor requests: clarify experimental implementation comparability (programming language, compiler optimizations).  
- Minor request: explain why Figure 2’s claim about increasing dimensionality does not hold for the Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256.
