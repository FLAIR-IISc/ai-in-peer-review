- Proposal: Replace covariance matrix square root with a triangular Cholesky factor in CMA-ES to reduce time and storage while keeping algorithm behavior and objective evaluations unchanged.  
- Complexity claim: Achieves optimal asymptotic complexity O(μd²) time and O(d²) space for covariance updates, removing O(d³) per-iteration matrix square root.  
- Theoretical contribution: Provides justification (Lemma 1) that the step-size adaptation error from the approximation diminishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical results: Thorough experiments (d=4..256, multiple benchmarks) show identical convergence to CMA-ES-Ref and substantial speedups (up to 20× at d=64).  
- Practical benefits: Enables free eigenvalue extraction and numerically stable inversion; significant runtime gains in high dimensions due to cache effects.  
- Strength: Paper is exceptionally clear, well-structured, and fills a theoretical gap left by prior rank-1 Cholesky work.  
- Weakness — originality: Overstates novelty; core update is a direct extension of Krause & Igel (2015); real novelty is the step-size error justification which should be emphasized.  
- Weakness — complexity nuance: Complexity claim needs refinement—CMA-ES-Ref has average O(d²/τ) but per-update O(d²); authors should use Θ(μd²) and clarify speedup source (eliminating repeated O(d³) square root).  
- Weakness — error analysis validation: Lack of empirical validation of error dynamics; should include plots of error norm ‖pσ,t+1 − E[pσ,t+1]‖ over iterations and ETₜ₋₁Eₜ convergence to identity.  
- Minor issues: Notation errors (wk→αₖ, n→d), typo (F→f), and need to state Krause & Igel was limited to μ=λ=1 while this generalizes to μ=λ/2.  
- Recommendation: Technically sound and useful but insufficiently novel and missing critical validation; borderline acceptance pending fixes—decision: Weak Reject.
