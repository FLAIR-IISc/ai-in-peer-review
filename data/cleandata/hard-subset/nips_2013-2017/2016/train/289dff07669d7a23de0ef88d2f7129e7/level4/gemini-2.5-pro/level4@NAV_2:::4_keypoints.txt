- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that achieves O(d^2) time and space for covariance updates by updating a triangular Cholesky factor directly.
- Identifies the O(d^3) cost of standard CMA-ES (matrix square root) as a major bottleneck and notes prior work traded performance for efficiency.
- Notes previous Cholesky-update approaches exist but integrating them with standard CMA-ES is hard due to CSA’s use of C^{-1/2}.
- Key technical idea: replace C^{-1/2} with the inverse triangular Cholesky factor A^{-1} and justify this approximation.
- Provides Lemma 1 showing the approximation error is expected to be small and decreases as the algorithm converges.
- Empirically shows no degradation in function-evaluation performance while drastically reducing wall-clock time (e.g., >20× speed-up for d=64).
- Significance: addresses a well-known bottleneck, making CMA-ES practical for higher-dimensional, cheap-evaluation settings like direct policy search.
- Quality: technically sound with solid theoretical support and thorough empirical comparisons to state-of-the-art implementations.
- Originality: novel integration of Cholesky updates into standard CMA-ES via a justified CSA approximation.
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and includes clear algorithms and experimental details.
- Weakness: claim of "numerical stability" for the Cholesky rank-one update is asserted but not substantiated or referenced.
- Weakness/Suggestion: should discuss potential combinations with low-rank or limited-memory CMA-ES variants for extremely high-dimensional problems (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).
