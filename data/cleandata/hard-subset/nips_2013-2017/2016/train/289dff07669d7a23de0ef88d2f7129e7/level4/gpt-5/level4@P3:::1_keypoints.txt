- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates triangular Cholesky factor A instead of C and replaces C−1/2 with A−1 in CSA to reduce computation.  
- Extends prior Cholesky-based and rank-one update work to multi-recombinant rank-µ CMA-ES, decomposing full update into quadratic-time rank-one updates.  
- Achieves time complexity O(µ d^2) and memory O(d^2), matching optimal full-covariance adaptation with typical µ = O(log d).  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in higher dimensions.  
- Theoretical motivation: orthogonal factor between C1/2 and A varies smoothly as C stabilizes, so CSA error decays over time.  
- Strong implementation and reproducibility efforts (LAPACK use, Shark integration, provided details).  
- Compares favorably to delayed decomposition, diagonal/limited-memory models, and non-triangular factorizations with numerical stability.  
- Weakness: No quantitative bound on deviation introduced by replacing C−1/2 with A−1, especially during rapid C changes or noisy/transient phases.  
- Weakness: Possible further erosion of affine invariance; rotation-effect experiments are only in the supplement and should be summarized in the main text.  
- Weakness: Rosenbrock results excluded non-convergent trials—success rates and conditioned/unconditioned metrics should be reported to avoid bias.  
- Weakness: Timing comparison fairness unclear—need to confirm same language, compiler, BLAS/LAPACK backend, hardware, and default parameters across implementations.  
- Observation: Figure 2’s claimed increasing speedup with dimension is unclear for Cigar and Discus at d=256; likely affected by L2 cache and memory bandwidth, should be explicitly attributed.  
- Technical error: Incorrect claim that diagonal elements of triangular Cholesky are square roots of eigenvalues of C; this must be corrected.  
- Suggestion: Add a noisy or real-world experiment to test CSA approximation robustness in practical settings (e.g., RL, tuning).  
- Quality: Algorithm is technically sound, numerically stable, and well-engineered; suggest adding conditioning checks and variance floors.  
- Clarity: Paper is well-written but needs citation fixes, correction of the Cholesky/eigenvalue statement, and clearer implementation comparability details.  
- Originality: Novel extension of Cholesky updates to standard µ > 1 CMA-ES with a justified CSA approximation.  
- Significance: High practical impact by reducing computation without increasing function evaluations, making it a strong candidate for medium-to-large-dimension use.  
- Suggested improvements: Report medians/IQRs and success rates, include noisy-function test, explain d=256 cache effects, correct eigenvalue claim, and detail computational environment.  
- Acceptance arguments: Practical speedups, solid theoretical intuition, optimal asymptotic costs, good empirical evidence, and open-source availability.  
- Rejection arguments: Lack of quantitative CSA bounds and noise analysis, some biased experimental reporting, missing timing-detail transparency, and a minor technical misstatement.
