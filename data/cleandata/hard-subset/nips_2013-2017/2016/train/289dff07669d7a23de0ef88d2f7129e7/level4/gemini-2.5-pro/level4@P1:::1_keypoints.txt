- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: a CMA-ES variant that updates the covariance via triangular Cholesky factors.  
- Main benefit: significantly reduced computational complexity of the covariance update (asymptotically optimal).  
- Memory requirement lowered for storing algorithm state.  
- Provides eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no extra cost, useful for monitoring.  
- Empirical evaluation shows no loss in function-evaluation efficiency vs. standard CMA-ES.  
- Substantial wall-clock time reduction, increasingly pronounced with higher dimensionality.  
- Paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and highly relevant to ML applications (RL, hyperparameter tuning, info geometry).  
- Reviewer expects Cholesky-CMA-ES could become the default for medium-to-large scale problems.  
- Minor clarification 1: request comment on comparability of software implementations (languages/LAPACK/etc.) used in timing comparisons.  
- Minor clarification 2: ask for explanation of why performance gap narrows for Cigar/Discus at d=256 (possible L2 cache effects).
