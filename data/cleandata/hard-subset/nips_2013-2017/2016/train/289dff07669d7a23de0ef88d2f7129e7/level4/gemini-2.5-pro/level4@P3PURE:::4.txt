This work's primary contribution is the application of triangular Cholesky factorization to the CMA-ES algorithm. This approach replaces the computationally expensive matrix square root operation in the standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing the computational cost of updating the covariance matrix. The authors provide a theoretical justification demonstrating that this modification does not degrade the algorithm's performance.

The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES reduces wall-clock time per iteration, with savings that increase with the dimensionality of the search space, while requiring a comparable number of objective function evaluations to the standard CMA-ES. This makes the paper a valuable and practical improvement for the CMA-ES algorithm.

I have a few points for clarification and discussion:

1.  The paper states that the proposed computation is "numerically more stable." Could the authors provide a more detailed theoretical explanation to support this claim?

2.  For many practical problems, such as training neural networks via direct policy search, high dimensionality remains a significant concern. Have the authors considered combining their framework with low-dimensional approximation techniques, such as those proposed in [Loshchilov, 2015], to address these high-dimensional scenarios?
