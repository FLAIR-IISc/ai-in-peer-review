

This paper employs Cholesky decomposition rather than eigen-decomposition to reduce the time and storage complexity of the CMA-ES. The paper is clearly written, and the proposed method appears reasonable with promising experimental results, particularly regarding speedup rates. I believe this approach has potential to become a new standard for CMA-ES. However, the originality is limited, as this work constitutes a trivial extension of prior research: "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The methods are fundamentally identical; please correct me if this assessment is inaccurate. Furthermore, several critical elements are notably absent:  
1) The equivalence to Equation (2) must be explicitly demonstrated. While space constraints exist and this equivalence was established in prior work, it remains essential for this paper.  
2) The claim of computational complexity O(μd²), identical to CMA-ES-Ref, requires detailed discussion—specifically, using Θ notation instead of O—to clarify why the algorithm is faster.  
3) The assertion that path error is small and decreases over time contradicts Hansen's findings. Since the theoretical analysis is non-deterministic, experimental validation is necessary; final performance comparisons alone are insufficient.  

Minor corrections:  
1) Verify notations (e.g., wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk; n in Table 1 should be d).  
2) Correct 'F' to 'f' in line 185.  
Given these unresolved issues, the paper does not meet NIPS standards in its current form.
