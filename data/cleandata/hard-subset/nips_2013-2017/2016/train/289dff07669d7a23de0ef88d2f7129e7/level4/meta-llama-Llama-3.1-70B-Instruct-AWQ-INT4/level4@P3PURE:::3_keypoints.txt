- Paper studies CMA-ES with triangular Cholesky factor updates (rank-1 and rank-μ applied as sequential rank-1 updates) and provides a proof that, assuming covariance convergence, a modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially change behavior.  
- Experimental results on algorithm performance are presented.  
- Contribution significance is questionable because Krause et al. (2015) already used rank-1 updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, and extending to rank-μ via sequential rank-1 updates appears straightforward.  
- Theoretical confidence could be strengthened by citing Beyer (ECJ, 2014), who established convergence of Ct (though not for the exact CMA-ES formulae), to support Lemma 1.  
- Technical error: in equation (1) the denominator should be σt (not σ); the same mistake appears in footnote 2 on page 3.  
- Minor grammatical error: delete the word "by" in "which can by be motivated by..." on line 94.
