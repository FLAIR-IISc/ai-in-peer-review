- Proposes replacing CMA-ES eigendecomposition with a triangular Cholesky factor update to reduce per-iteration cost.  
- The change targets the O(N^3) eigendecomposition bottleneck, improving scalability in high dimensions.  
- Authors provide theoretical justification that the Cholesky reformulation preserves original algorithm performance.  
- Empirical results show reduced wall-clock time and maintained convergence in function evaluations, with benefits growing with dimensionality.  
- Strength: elegant, theoretically sound adaptation that operates directly on the Cholesky factor.  
- Suggestion: provide more detailed theoretical or empirical evidence supporting the claim of improved numerical stability in this specific CMA-ES context.  
- Question: confirm that Cholesky-CMA-ES matches standard CMA-ES in objective evaluations while reducing runtime.  
- Suggestion: discuss combining the Cholesky approach with limited-memory or low-rank covariance approximations for very large-scale problems and potential integration challenges.
