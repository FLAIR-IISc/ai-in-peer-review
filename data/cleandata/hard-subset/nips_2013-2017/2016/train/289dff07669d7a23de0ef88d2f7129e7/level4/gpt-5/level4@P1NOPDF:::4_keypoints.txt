- Main contribution: uses triangular Cholesky factorization in CMA-ES to avoid explicit matrix square roots and reduce matrix inversion cost.  
- Theoretical claim: modification is justified and does not degrade optimization performance.  
- Empirical result: reduces runtime complexity while keeping the number of objective function evaluations largely unchanged; benefits increase with dimensionality.  
- Numerical stability concern: request clearer theoretical explanation—specify assumptions (covariance conditioning, update magnitudes) and precise stability metrics (round-off bounds, sensitivity to perturbations).  
- Objective evaluations vs wall-clock: ask to confirm interpretation that evaluations are similar while wall-clock is reduced, and to quantify how wall-clock savings scale with dimension and population size.  
- High-dimensional scalability: suggest exploring hybridization with low-dimensional/limited-memory approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to improve practicality for very high-dimensional problems.  
- Overall recommendation: valuable and practical contribution, but needs more rigorous stability analysis and discussion of integration with low-memory approximations to broaden applicability.
