- Addresses CMA-ES bottleneck by replacing matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce runtime and memory in high dimensions.  
- Modifies cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) to accommodate Cholesky factorization.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the modification does not harm convergence.  
- Empirically demonstrates significant runtime speedups while keeping the same number of function evaluations, especially as dimensionality grows.  
- Identifies and achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity for the method.  
- Clear and well-structured presentation explaining modifications and rationale.  
- Thorough experimental evaluation on multiple benchmark functions across dimensions 4–256.  
- Claims improved numerical stability but lacks detailed explanation of why Cholesky gives this benefit.  
- Acknowledges but does not deeply address quadratic scaling limits for very high-dimensional problems.  
- Connection to prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work is noted but could be made more explicit, especially versus Krause and Igel (2015).  
- Experimental setup details (e.g., rotation matrix generation and exact parameters) are insufficient for full reproducibility.  
- Argument for acceptance: addresses a known bottleneck, has strong theory and clear empirical gains, and is well-written.  
- Argument against acceptance: improvement is runtime-focused rather than reducing function evaluations, with limited discussion on stability and scaling limits.  
- Overall assessment: solid, practical contribution deserving acceptance (Recommendation: Accept).
