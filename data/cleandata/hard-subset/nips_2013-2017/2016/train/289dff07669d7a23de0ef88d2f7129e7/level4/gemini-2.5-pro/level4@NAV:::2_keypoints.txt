- Proposes replacing eigen-decomposition with Cholesky in CMA-ES to reduce time and memory; manuscript is clear and reports encouraging speedup results with potential to become a new standard.

- Significant originality concern: contribution appears to be a trivial extension of "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies" with nearly identical core methods.

- Omits a proof that the proposed algorithm is mathematically equivalent to the formulation in equation (2), which is essential for completeness.

- The stated computational complexity O(μd²) matches reference CMA-ES and does not explain observed speedups; a tighter/asymptotically precise analysis (e.g., Θ-notation) is needed.

- The claim that evolution-path error is small and decreases over time contradicts prior findings (Hansen); this non-deterministic theoretical claim requires direct empirical validation by tracking the error throughout optimization.

- Experimental comparison of final performance alone is insufficient; dedicated experiments documenting error dynamics and other diagnostics are required.

- Minor corrections needed: notation inconsistencies (wk should be αk in Algorithm 2; n in Table 1 should be d) and typographical fix (line 185: 'F' → 'f').

- Overall recommendation: not ready for publication due to questions about novelty and omission of critical analytical and experimental details.
