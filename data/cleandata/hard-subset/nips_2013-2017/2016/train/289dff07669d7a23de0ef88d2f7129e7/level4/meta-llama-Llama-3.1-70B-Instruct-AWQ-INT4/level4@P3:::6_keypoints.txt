- Proposal of a Cholesky-CMA-ES modification claiming unchanged behavior but reduced computational cost.  
- Naming is misleading because a similar Cholesky-CMA-ES was introduced by Suttorp et al. (2009).  
- Method is an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015), not a fundamentally new algorithm.  
- Main algorithmic contribution is merely applying rankOneUpdate in a loop over individuals.  
- Authors fail to compare against prior Cholesky-CMA-ES variants (Suttorp 2009, Krause 2015), crucial for novelty assessment.  
- Claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading and not universally true (e.g., separable or Sphere functions).  
- Comparison to omitted prior work suggests the approach is only about twice cheaper, contradicting some claims about numerical instability.  
- Contribution to large-scale optimization is limited since complexity remains quadratic, though it may offer practical time/memory savings.  
- Required fixes: acknowledge prior work (Suttorp 2009, Krause 2015) and resolve the naming issue.  
- Required fixes: run experiments comparing to the omitted prior variants and update figures accordingly.  
- Required fixes: better explain and justify the significance of applying rankOneUpdate in a loop and why it is non-trivial.  
- Minor note: statement that eigenvalue decomposition is "typically" done every update is incorrect—proper implementations do it periodically.  
- Overall assessment: technically sound but reduced quality, clarity, originality, and significance due to missing comparisons and misleading claims.
