- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that approximates covariance adaptation with complexity O(μ d^2) vs. original O(d^3).
- Core idea: maintain and update a lower-triangular Cholesky matrix instead of the full covariance matrix or its square root.
- Key difference: omits estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et at each step.
- Theoretical claim: under convergence of covariance sequence, Et asymptotically converges to identity, making the schemes equivalent.
- Empirical results: runtime and convergence plots on benchmark functions show utility and speed-up.
- Comparisons made to two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.
- Achieves significant speed-ups without degrading optimization performance on synthetic tests.
- Limitation: in practice objective-function evaluation often dominates runtime, so CMA-ES runtime speed-ups may be irrelevant.
- Practical niche: targeted scenario (high dimension, cheap evaluations) is uncommon since typical problems have few tens of variables.
- Suggested experiment: track evolution of Et along optimization trajectories (different norms) and compare to full CMA-ES.
- Suggested inclusion: runtime and performance comparisons with the original (non-delayed) CMA-ES.
- Suggested simulation: quadratic functions with continuously varied spectra to study solution quality and Et error (e.g., Stich & Müller PPSN 2012).
- Suggested real-world test: apply to a speed-critical task like policy-gradient optimization in real-time robotics to show practical relevance.
