The paper proposes a CMA-ES variant with “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity,” claiming unchanged algorithmic behavior at lower computational cost. The method is named Cholesky-CMA-ES, which is misleading: this name was established by Suttorp et al. (2009) for a similar and well-known algorithm. The authors do not acknowledge this and instead introduce their method as “Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES.”

Algorithmically, the contribution appears incremental relative to Krause et al. (2015). In particular, the only substantive change is to call rankOneUpdate—not once as in Plus strategies, but in a loop over all individuals. The rankOneUpdate itself is already present in Krause et al.

Empirically, Figure b) on the Cigar function shows differing plateau values for CMA-ES-Ref versus Cholesky-CMA-ES. This discrepancy may be due to plotting medians. The paper should include a direct empirical comparison with the Cholesky-CMA-ES of Suttorp et al. and an explicit algorithmic comparison with the variant of Krause et al. The claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is also overstated: on the Sphere function the covariance matrix is not needed at all, and for separable problems a diagonal approximation suffices. Moreover, the proposed method is only about twice as cheap as the (omitted) Cholesky-CMA-ES of Suttorp et al. The paper further asserts that the Suttorp approach is numerically unstable, whereas the original paper reported the opposite. Its omission from Figure 3 risks misleading readers about the actual novelty and benefit of the proposed approach.

I do not believe the method changes the large-scale optimization picture for CMA-ES, since the complexity remains quadratic. That said, if one insists on maintaining a full covariance matrix, this modification may well be the best available option to reduce time and memory.

Overall, the work feels too incremental (algorithmically, it is essentially a single “for” loop). I recommend the following revisions:
a) Resolve the naming conflict with prior Cholesky-CMA-ES work by Suttorp et al.
b) Add experiments including the variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al., and update all three figures accordingly. If the figures become crowded, show only the best-performing variants in the main text and include full results in the Supplementary Material.
c) Clarify that the main algorithmic change is the “for” loop in Algorithm 1, or argue convincingly why this assessment is incorrect.

Minor note: “Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step.” This is atypical; in proper implementations, the decomposition is performed periodically.
