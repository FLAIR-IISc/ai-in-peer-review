- Main contribution: replaces the matrix square root in CMA-ES with triangular Cholesky factorization to avoid costly matrix inverses.  
- Theoretical justification: author shows the Cholesky-based approach does not degrade algorithmic performance.  
- Complexity benefit: method lowers runtime complexity (wall-clock time) without significantly changing computational budget in terms of operations per generation.  
- Evaluation cost: Cholesky-CMA-ES appears to require the same number of objective function evaluations as standard CMA-ES.  
- Scalability: runtime/benefit grows with search-space dimensionality, making the speedup more pronounced in high dimensions.  
- Implementation: the paper provides a valuable practical implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES.  
- Request for clarification: author should theoretically justify the claim that the Cholesky computation is “numerically more stable.”  
- Extension suggestion: consider integrating low-dimensional approximation techniques (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) to better handle very high-dimensional problems like neural-network policy search.
