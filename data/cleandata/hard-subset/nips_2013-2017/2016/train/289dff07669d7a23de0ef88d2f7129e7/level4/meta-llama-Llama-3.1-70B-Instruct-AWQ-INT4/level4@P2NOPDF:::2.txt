This paper explores an alternative approach to the CMA-ES algorithm by utilizing Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition, thereby reducing time and storage complexity. The paper is generally well-written, and the proposed method appears reasonable. The experimental results, particularly the speedup rates, are promising, suggesting that the proposed method may potentially become a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future.

However, the originality of this work is somewhat limited, as it seems to be a straightforward extension of the previous work "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The methods presented in this paper are largely identical to those in the previous work, and it would be beneficial for the authors to clarify this connection.

Furthermore, some crucial aspects of the paper are missing or require further elaboration. Firstly, it would be essential to demonstrate the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2), even if this has been proven in the previous work. Given the space constraints, a brief explanation or reference to the relevant section in the previous work would suffice.

Secondly, the authors claim that the computational complexity of their algorithm is O(\mu d^2), which is identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A more detailed discussion, possibly using the \Theta notation, is necessary to explain why the proposed algorithm is faster. This would provide a clearer understanding of the underlying reasons for the improved performance.

Thirdly, the authors claim that the error in the path is small and decreases over time, contradicting Hansen's claim. Since the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experimental validation of this claim is necessary. Comparing only the final performance is insufficient; the authors should provide additional experimental results to support their assertion.

Some minor comments include: the authors should carefully review their notation, as there are some inconsistencies (e.g., wk in Algorithm 2 should be \alphak, and n in Table 1 should be d). Additionally, 'F' in line 185 should be 'f'.

In its current form, this paper does not meet the qualification standards for NIPS. Addressing the above concerns and providing further clarification and experimental validation would be necessary to strengthen the paper.
