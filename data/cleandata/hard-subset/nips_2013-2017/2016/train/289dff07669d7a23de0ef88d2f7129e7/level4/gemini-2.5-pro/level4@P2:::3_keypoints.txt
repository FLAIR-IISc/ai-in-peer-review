- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains the triangular Cholesky factor to avoid explicit covariance decomposition, reducing update time to O(µd^2) and memory to O(d^2).  
- Replaces the CSA term C^{-1/2} with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1} and provides Lemma 1 as theoretical justification.  
- Argues the A^{-1} approximation error is negligible as covariance shape converges, due to exponential weighting in the CSA path.  
- Empirical results show identical function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES with substantial wall-clock speedups (e.g., >20× at d=64).  
- Significant practical impact: makes full-covariance CMA-ES feasible in higher dimensions relevant to ML applications.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-update work and integrates efficient updates into standard CMA-ES in a principled, numerically stable way.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal storage complexity and provides clear implementation details (pseudocode).  
- Clarity and presentation are strong; paper is well-written and experiments are convincing.  
- Originality is somewhat limited because similar Cholesky-update ideas exist (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015); the main novelty is generalizing to rank-µ updates and modifying CSA.  
- Theoretical justification relies on the assumption that the normalized covariance converges, which is not strictly guaranteed for stochastic CMA-ES.  
- Recommends citing Beyer (2014) earlier to support the convergence assumption and strengthen the theoretical basis.  
- Notes minor typos: denominator in Eq.(1) should be σt, and remove the extra "by" in the rank-update sentence.  
- Overall conclusion: well-executed, practically valuable, and a clear candidate for acceptance despite being an incremental advance.
