- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: a novel lower-triangular Cholesky update approximating CMA-ES covariance adaptation.  
- Reduces time complexity from O(d^3) to O(μd^2), targeting large-scale optimization.  
- Provides theoretical justification showing orthogonal matrices Et converge to identity under assumptions.  
- Empirical results on benchmark functions show speed-up with no loss of optimization performance.  
- Compares against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.  
- Method is most beneficial when problem dimensionality is high and function evaluations are fast.  
- Notes that in many real cases the black-box oracle runtime dominates, limiting practical gains.  
- Suggests adding plots of Et evolution (e.g., in different norms) comparing full CMA-ES and approximation.  
- Recommends reporting actual runtimes including vanilla CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.  
- Suggests simulation study on quadratic functions with varying spectral distributions to assess Et error.  
- Proposes a real-world experiment (e.g., policy gradients in real-time robotics) to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Manuscript is well-written with clear explanation and solid experimental design.  
- Minor writing improvements suggested: expand related-work discussion and clarify result implications.  
- Overall recommendation: accept; contribution is valuable, novel, and empirically validated.  
- Counterpoints against acceptance: practical benefits may be scenario-limited and presentation has minor gaps.
