- Proposes a Cholesky-factor-based covariance update for CMA-ES that cuts computational complexity and memory footprint.  
- Empirical results show comparable function-evaluation performance to CMA-ES while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Method yields covariance eigenvalues essentially for free, making it attractive for medium- to large-scale problems.  
- Paper is well written, empirically thorough, and appropriate for NIPS given CMA-ES’s ML and information-geometry relevance.  
- Reviewer expects this variant to become a default choice for larger-scale optimization due to reduced memory and update cost.
