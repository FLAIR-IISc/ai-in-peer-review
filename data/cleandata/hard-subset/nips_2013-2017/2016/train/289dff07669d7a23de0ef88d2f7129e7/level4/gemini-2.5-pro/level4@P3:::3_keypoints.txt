- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates a triangular Cholesky factor to avoid full covariance eigen-decomposition.  
- Reduces time complexity of covariance updates to O(µ d²) and storage to O(d²), yielding asymptotically optimal costs.  
- Core technical challenge: standard CSA requires C⁻¹/², which cannot be trivially replaced by Cholesky inverse A⁻¹ without rotational artifacts.  
- Main contribution: propose replacing C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹ and give Lemma 1 to justify the approximation as rotation errors vanish with covariance shape convergence.  
- Empirical results: significant wall-clock speedups over standard and other efficient CMA-ES variants, with no loss in function-evaluation convergence.  
- Builds on prior scalability work; most closely extends Krause & Igel (2015) from rank-1 to rank-µ updates via sequential rank-1 updates.  
- Strength — practical impact: provides a drop-in, faster, memory-efficient alternative that extends applicability of full-covariance CMA-ES to higher dimensions.  
- Strength — technical quality: sequential rank-1 updates are elegant, numerically stable, and yield practical benefits like easy eigenvalue access and efficient inverse computation.  
- Strength — theoretical justification: Lemma 1 offers an intuitive rationale for the CSA approximation under a covariance-shape convergence assumption.  
- Strength — empirical validation: thorough benchmarks against multiple baselines convincingly demonstrate runtime benefits without compromising optimization performance.  
- Weakness — originality: contribution is incremental given Krause & Igel (2015); main novelty is analysis of CSA approximation and empirical demonstration for full CMA-ES.  
- Suggestion — strengthen Lemma 1 by citing theoretical convergence results (e.g., Beyer 2014) to support the covariance-shape convergence assumption.  
- Minor corrections: fix σₜ in Equation (1) and footnote, and remove the extraneous "by" in the phrase "which can by be motivated by...".
