The paper proposes replacing the eigenvalue-based square-root computation with a Cholesky factorization to reduce the time and memory costs in CMA-ES. The manuscript is generally clear, the approach appears sensible, and the empirical speedups are compelling; this variant could plausibly become the standard CMA-ES when full covariance adaptation is needed. That said, the novelty seems limited: the contribution looks like a straightforward extension of “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,” and the techniques appear essentially the same—please correct me if this impression is mistaken. In addition, several crucial elements are missing.

Major points:
1) Equivalence to Eq. (2): Please demonstrate that your update is equivalent to the standard covariance update (2). Even if prior work contains a proof, a self-contained derivation or explicit, precise reference is essential here.
2) Complexity vs. speed: You state the update complexity is O(µ d^2), which is the same asymptotic order as CMA-ES-Ref. Please explain in detail—ideally using Θ-notation and discussing dominant constants and operation counts—why your implementation achieves the observed speedups in practice.
3) Path error claim: You claim the error introduced in the evolution path is small and decreases over time, which conflicts with Hansen’s stated concerns. Since your theoretical argument is not deterministic, experiments should directly measure and report this error over iterations. Comparing only final performance is insufficient to substantiate this claim.

Minor comments:
1) Please check notation carefully. For example, wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk, and n in Table 1 should be d.
2) “F” should be “f” on line 185.

Given these issues, I do not think the paper is ready for NIPS in its current form.
