This paper investigates the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES), specifically extending the update procedure for the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates, implemented sequentially as rank-1 updates. The authors demonstrate, under established convergence assumptions for the covariance matrix, that the proposed modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm’s core behavior. Experimental results evaluating algorithm performance are also presented. 

1. While this work builds upon the Cholesky CMA-ES framework and generalizes the rank-1 update to the triangular Cholesky factor – as previously explored by Krause et al. (2015) – the contribution feels incremental. The paper essentially provides a direct generalization to a rank-\mu update within a sequence of rank-1 updates, and its novelty appears limited in this context.

2. The convergence analysis of the covariance matrix, *Ct*, within CMA-ES typically relies on theoretical foundations (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although these analyses are not always directly based on the precise formulae of the CMA-ES algorithm. Strengthening the justification for Lemma 1 by referencing these broader theoretical results could enhance confidence in its validity.

3. Several minor issues require attention:
    3.1. In equation (1), the denominator contains σ, which should be σ<sub>t</sub>. This inconsistency also appears in footnote 2 on page 3.
    3.2. On line 94, the phrase “which can by be motivated by principles from…” contains a redundant “by” and should be revised to “which can be motivated by principles from…”.
