The Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) is a state-of-the-art algorithm for stochastic, derivative-free optimization. It combines different learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, a process that can be equivalently seen as adapting an underlying metric. This paper proposes a novel variant for updating the covariance matrix based on its triangular Cholesky factors. This approach offers significant advantages over previous methods by reducing both the computational complexity of the update and the memory required to store the algorithm's state parameters.

The new method is rigorously evaluated through numerical experiments. The results demonstrate that the proposed update does not degrade the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of the number of function evaluations required to reach a given target value. Crucially, it significantly reduces the algorithm's wall-clock time, particularly in high-dimensional settings.

This paper represents a very significant contribution to the field of optimization. I expect this variant of CMA-ES could become the default choice for medium- to large-scale problems, given its clear advantages over the current standard method: namely, reduced memory requirements, lower computational complexity, and the ability to provide the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no additional cost. The paper is well-written, and the proposed approach is carefully evaluated. This contribution is a welcome addition to the NIPS proceedings, as CMA-ES has strong connections to machine learning, information geometry, and applications in reinforcement and supervised learning.
