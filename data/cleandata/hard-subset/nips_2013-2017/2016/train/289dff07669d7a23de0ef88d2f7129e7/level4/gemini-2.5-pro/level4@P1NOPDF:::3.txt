This paper presents a Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) that incorporates both rank-1 and rank-µ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, with the rank-µ update implemented as a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors prove, under the assumption that the covariance matrix converges, that their proposed modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper also includes experiments to evaluate the algorithm's performance.

1.  The primary contribution of this work is the extension of the Cholesky CMA-ES to handle both rank-1 and rank-µ updates. However, the significance of this contribution appears limited, especially when considering the work of Krause et al. (2015), which already introduced a rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The method presented here seems to be a straightforward generalization of that procedure, implementing the rank-µ update as a sequence of rank-1 updates.

2.  Regarding the theoretical analysis, Lemma 1 relies on the assumption that the covariance matrix C_t converges. It is worth noting that the convergence of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES has been previously established in the literature (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), even if not for the exact formulation used in this paper. Citing this related work could strengthen the justification for the convergence assumption made in Lemma 1 and increase the reader's confidence in the result.

3.  There are a few minor issues that should be addressed:
    *   In Equation (1), the σ in the denominator should be σ_t. The same error appears in footnote 2 on page 3.
    *   In line 94, the phrase "which can by be motivated by principles from..." contains an extraneous word "by", which should be deleted.
