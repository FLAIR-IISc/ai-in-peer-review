This paper investigates Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES), specifically focusing on updating the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-$\mu$ updates applied sequentially as rank-1 updates. The authors demonstrate, under the assumption of covariance convergence, that the proposed modification to the cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm’s overall behavior. The paper also includes experimental results evaluating the performance of the algorithm.

My primary concern is the incremental nature of the contribution. While the paper extends the existing work on updating the triangular Cholesky factor – notably the rank-1 update proposed by Krause et al. (2015) – to a rank-$\mu$ update within a sequence of rank-1 updates, I find the advance relatively modest. The proposed method represents a straightforward generalization of Krause et al.’s procedure, and its novelty feels limited in that context.

Regarding theoretical grounding, the convergence of the covariance matrix $C_t$ in CMA-ES has been addressed by theoretical analyses, such as Beyer (ECJ, 2014), although these analyses do not always rely directly on the precise formulae of the standard CMA-ES algorithm. Acknowledging and potentially building upon this existing body of work could strengthen the justification for Lemma 1 and increase confidence in its validity.

Finally, there are a few minor issues that should be addressed. First, in Equation (1), the $\sigma$ in the denominator should be $\sigma_t$. This same error appears in Footnote 2 on page 3. Second, in line 94, the phrase “which can by be motivated by principles from…” contains an unnecessary “by” and should be revised to “which can be motivated by principles from…”.
