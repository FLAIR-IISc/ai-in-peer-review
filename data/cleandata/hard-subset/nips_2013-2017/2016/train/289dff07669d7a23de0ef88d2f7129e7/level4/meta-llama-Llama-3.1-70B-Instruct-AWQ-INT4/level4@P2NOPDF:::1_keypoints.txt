- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (interpretable as metric adaptation).  
- The paper proposes a novel covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based update reduces computational complexity and memory for storing state parameters.  
- The method allows obtaining covariance matrix eigenvalues at no additional cost.  
- Numerical experiments show no degradation in number of function evaluations to reach targets.  
- The new variant substantially reduces wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- The reviewer views this as a significant contribution likely to become the default for medium-to-large-scale applications; relevant to ML, information geometry, RL, and supervised learning.  
- The paper is well-written and carefully evaluated.  
- Minor clarification requested: confirm implementations were comparable in CPU usage (e.g., same implementation language and relevant details).  
- Minor clarification requested: explain why the dimensionality trend in Figure 2 does not hold for the Cigar and Discus functions at 256 dimensions.
