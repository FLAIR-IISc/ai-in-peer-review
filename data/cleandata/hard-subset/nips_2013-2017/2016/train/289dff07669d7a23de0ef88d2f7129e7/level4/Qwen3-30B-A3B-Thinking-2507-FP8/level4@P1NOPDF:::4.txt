

This paper makes a valuable contribution by introducing a triangular Cholesky factorization approach for CMA-ES that eliminates the need for square roots during matrix inverse computations. This modification reduces computational cost without compromising the algorithm's performance, as rigorously justified in the theoretical analysis. The method achieves lower runtime complexity while maintaining the same number of objective function evaluations, resulting in significant wall-clock time reductions—particularly as search space dimensionality increases.  

The authors successfully demonstrate that the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES preserves the algorithm's efficacy while improving efficiency. However, the claim of "numerically more stable" computation warrants deeper theoretical explanation. Specifically, it would be helpful to clarify the mathematical mechanisms ensuring stability, as this is a critical aspect of numerical methods.  

Additionally, the paper notes that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires identical objective function evaluations to standard CMA-ES but reduces wall-clock time. This efficiency gain grows with problem dimensionality, making it highly relevant for high-dimensional applications. Given that practical scenarios like neural network training in direct policy search often involve high-dimensional spaces (where dimensionality is a major concern), the authors should consider integrating their framework with low-dimensional approximation techniques, such as those proposed by Loshchilov (2015). Such a combination could further enhance scalability for real-world optimization tasks without sacrificing the core efficiency gains demonstrated here.
