

This paper investigates a variant of the Cholesky CMA-ES that employs both rank-1 and rank-μ updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, implemented sequentially via rank-1 updates. The authors claim that, under the convergence assumption of the covariance matrix, their modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm's behavior. While the paper provides empirical results on algorithm performance, the contribution and technical merits warrant careful evaluation.

The primary concern lies in the novelty of the proposed method. The paper extends the rank-1 update strategy for the Cholesky factor (introduced by Krause et al., 2015) to incorporate rank-μ updates through sequential rank-1 steps. However, this appears to be a straightforward generalization rather than a significant advancement. Krause et al. already established the rank-1 update framework for the Cholesky factor, and the extension to rank-μ—while technically valid—does not introduce novel theoretical insights or substantial empirical improvements over existing methods. The authors do not convincingly demonstrate that this modification yields meaningful performance gains or addresses limitations unmet by prior work. Consequently, the contribution seems incremental, especially given the substantial body of literature on CMA-ES variants.

Regarding the theoretical analysis, Lemma 1 states that the modified step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior under covariance convergence. However, the proof relies on the convergence of the covariance matrix $C_t$, which, while widely accepted in practice, lacks rigorous justification in the context of the exact CMA-ES update rules. Beyer (ECJ, 2014) provides convergence analysis for CMA-ES but not based on the precise update formulae used here. Strengthening Lemma 1 by explicitly referencing or adapting Beyer’s framework would significantly bolster the theoretical foundation. Without this, the lemma’s validity remains partially dependent on assumptions not fully substantiated in the paper.

Several minor but important technical issues require correction:  
- Equation (1) contains a typographical error: the denominator should use $\sigma_t$ (not $\sigma$). This same error appears in footnote 2 on page 3.  
- Line 94 states: "which can by be motivated by principles..." The redundant word "by" should be removed.  

These errors, while trivial, undermine the paper’s professionalism and require correction before publication.  

The experimental section evaluates the algorithm’s performance on standard benchmarks. However, the results do not sufficiently justify the proposed extension. The comparison with baseline methods is limited, and the paper fails to quantify how the rank-μ update specifically impacts convergence speed or solution quality relative to the rank-1 approach (Krause et al.) or standard CMA-ES. Without clear evidence of superiority or unique advantages, the empirical claims lack persuasive force.  

In summary, while the paper presents a technically coherent extension of Cholesky CMA-ES, its contribution is limited. The rank-μ update is a natural generalization of Krause et al.’s rank-1 method, not a novel advancement. The theoretical argument for the step-size adaptation would benefit from stronger grounding in existing convergence literature, and the empirical evaluation does not convincingly demonstrate added value. The minor typographical errors further detract from the work’s rigor.  

Given these points, the paper does not meet the bar for acceptance at NIPS. The incremental nature of the contribution, combined with the underdeveloped theoretical and empirical support, suggests it would be better suited for a more specialized venue focused on optimization algorithms. The authors should consider revising the manuscript to either: (i) provide a compelling justification for why the rank-μ extension is non-trivial and superior to existing approaches, or (ii) clarify the scope of their contribution as a technical note on implementation variants rather than a novel method.
