- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, replacing covariance matrix square root with a triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time and space complexity.  
- Provides a theoretically grounded analysis showing replacing C_t^{-1/2} with A_t^{-1} introduces negligible error in the CSA mechanism.  
- Lemma 1 proves the orthogonal component of the Cholesky decomposition converges to identity, so the error vanishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal O(µ d^2) time for covariance updates (vs O(d^3) standard) and optimal O(d^2) storage.  
- Offers free eigenvalue computation of the covariance matrix, useful for monitoring problem conditioning.  
- Empirical results show equal numbers of function evaluations to standard CMA-ES with significant speedups (up to 20× at d=64), increasing with dimension.  
- Experiments are comprehensive and well-designed, using standard benchmarks across multiple dimensions and testing rotation invariance.  
- Paper is well-structured, clearly written, and includes thorough theoretical justification and convincing figures.  
- Builds on prior efficiency work (delayed updates, prior Cholesky attempts) but provides novel, rigorous analysis applicable to standard CMA-ES.  
- Practical benefits include numerical stability and applicability to medium-to-large scale optimization in ML (policy search, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Primary weakness: need clarification on implementation details—whether all algorithms were implemented in the same language/environment and used identical numerical libraries (e.g., LAPACK).  
- Notes an unexplained deviation in Figure 2: speedup does not increase for Cigar and Discus at d=256, requiring explanation or discussion of limitations/implementation effects.  
- Concludes the method can replace standard CMA-ES in many settings without sacrificing solution quality while offering major computational advantages.  
- Recommendation: strong accept—meets NIPS criteria for quality, clarity, originality, and significance.
