The CMA-ES algorithm is a state-of-the-art stochastic optimization technique that leverages multiple learning mechanisms to adapt the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution. This distribution can also be interpreted as an adaptation of an underlying metric. This paper introduces a novel variant of the CMA-ES algorithm, utilizing triangular Cholesky factors to update the covariance matrix. This approach offers a significant reduction in complexity and required memory compared to existing methods. The authors conduct a thorough numerical evaluation of the proposed method, demonstrating that it maintains the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of function evaluations while substantially reducing the algorithm's wall-clock time, particularly in high-dimensional spaces. In my opinion, this paper represents a substantial contribution to the field of optimization. The proposed variant of CMA-ES is poised to become a default method for medium to large-scale applications due to its advantages, including reduced memory requirements, lower complexity, and the ability to obtain eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no additional cost. The paper is well-written, and the approach is carefully evaluated. It is fitting to see this contribution at NIPS, given the strong connections between CMA-ES, machine learning, information geometry, and its applications in reinforcement learning and supervised learning. Minor suggestions include providing details on the comparability of different implementations in terms of CPU, such as the implementation language used. Additionally, the authors may want to address the apparent inconsistency in Figure 2, where the differences in dimension 256 for Cigar and Discus do not seem to follow the trend of increasing differences with higher dimensionality.
