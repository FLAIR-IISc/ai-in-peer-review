- Authors reuse the name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" but fail to cite T. Suttorp et al. (2009), who used the same name and a similar approach.  
- Method builds on Krause et al. (2015); main novelty is repeatedly applying rankOneUpdate for all individuals — a minor incremental change.  
- Primary algorithmic contribution effectively amounts to a simple loop around an existing update.  
- Empirical differences (e.g., on the Cigar function) may be an artifact of plotting medians rather than a robust performance gain.  
- Paper omits direct comparisons to Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause’s variant; such comparisons are necessary to validate claims.  
- Claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading — not optimal for problems like Sphere where covariance is unnecessary.  
- Proposed approach is only about twice as efficient as Suttorp’s method, not a dramatic improvement.  
- Authors assert Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable, but that claim contradicts the original paper and needs justification.  
- Omitting Suttorp’s method from Figure 3 risks misleading readers about the paper’s novelty and performance.  
- Quadratic complexity remains, so the method does not meaningfully change feasibility for large-scale optimization.  
- Overall assessment: work is overly incremental and lacks substantial algorithmic advancement.  
- Required fixes: (a) resolve the naming/citation issue, (b) run experiments including Suttorp’s and Krause’s variants and update figures, (c) clarify and formalize the main algorithmic contribution.  
- Technical correction needed: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically, not at every update step — correct this statement.
