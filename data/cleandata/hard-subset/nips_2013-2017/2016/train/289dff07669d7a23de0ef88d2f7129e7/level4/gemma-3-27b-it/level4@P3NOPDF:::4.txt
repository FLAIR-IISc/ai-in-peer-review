## Review of NIPS 2016 Submission – [Paper ID Redacted]

This paper presents a valuable improvement to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm by leveraging the triangular Cholesky factorization to eliminate square root computations when inverting the covariance matrix. This factorization results in a reduction in computational cost, particularly noticeable during matrix inversion, without compromising the algorithm’s performance. The authors provide a theoretical justification supporting the equivalence of the original and proposed methods.

The core contribution lies in demonstrating how this factorization can be integrated into the standard CMA-ES framework while maintaining its optimization capabilities. Experimental results show that the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a comparable number of objective function evaluations to the standard CMA-ES, but with a reduction in runtime, especially as the dimensionality of the search space increases. This is a significant practical benefit, as matrix inversion frequently constitutes a computational bottleneck in higher-dimensional problems. 

The paper is clearly written and the motivation is well-explained. The theoretical analysis appears sound and the empirical validation provides convincing evidence of the method’s effectiveness. The reduction in runtime without a corresponding increase in function evaluations is a noteworthy achievement.

However, a few points would benefit from further clarification. While the authors state that the Cholesky factorization leads to a “numerically more stable” computation, the underlying reason for this improved stability isn't fully elucidated. Expanding on *why* this factorization achieves greater numerical stability would strengthen the paper. A more detailed explanation of the numerical properties – perhaps relating to condition numbers or eigenvalue distributions – would be appreciated.

Furthermore, the benefit of reduced wall-clock time is most pronounced in high-dimensional search spaces. Given that many real-world applications of CMA-ES, such as direct policy search in reinforcement learning and hyperparameter optimization for neural networks, are characterized by extremely high dimensionality, it is worth considering how this approach might be combined with existing dimensionality reduction techniques. Specifically, the authors should discuss the potential synergy between Cholesky-CMA-ES and low-dimensional approximations, such as those proposed by Loschilov [2015]. Integrating such techniques could address the inherent scalability challenges posed by high-dimensional optimization problems, expanding the applicability of the proposed method.

**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper presenting a practical and theoretically sound improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a genuine performance gain in terms of runtime without sacrificing optimization effectiveness. Addressing the points raised above regarding numerical stability and scalability would further enhance the paper's contribution. 

**Recommendation:** Accept with minor revisions. The authors should expand on the numerical stability argument and discuss potential combinations with dimensionality reduction techniques.
