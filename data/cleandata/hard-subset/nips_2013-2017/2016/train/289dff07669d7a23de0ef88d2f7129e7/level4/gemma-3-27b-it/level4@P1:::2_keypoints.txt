- Proposes using Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition to reduce CMA-ES time and storage complexity.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and presents promising empirical speedups, especially at high dimensionality.
- Approach could become a new standard implementation of CMA-ES given its practical efficiency gains.
- Originality is limited because the method builds directly on Krause & Igel (2015) and shares core update similarities.
- Authors should explicitly state novelty and distinct contributions beyond the prior rank-one update work.
- Must demonstrate equivalence of their algorithm to the standard update rule (equation (2)) for clarity and completeness.
- Claimed computational complexity O(µ d^2) matches reference CMA-ES; need Θ-notation and constant-factor discussion to explain observed runtime improvements.
- The claim that error from replacing s_t with ŝ_t is small and decreasing conflicts with Hansen (2015); more rigorous validation is required.
- Require additional experiments analyzing error trajectories or convergence rates over iterations, not just final performance.
- Minor notation issues: w_k in Algorithm 2 should be α_k, and n in Table 1 should be d.
- Typographical error: ‘F’ should be ‘f’ on line 185.
- Arguments for acceptance: addresses a practical bottleneck, is theoretically grounded, shows significant speedups, and is clearly explained.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited originality, insufficient explanation of practical gains, lack of rigorous validation of approximation error, and minor notational/typo issues.
- Overall recommendation: promising but not ready for acceptance—needs clearer novelty statement, tighter analysis, and more experiments to validate claims.
