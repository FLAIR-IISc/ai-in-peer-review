The paper proposes a variant of CMA-ES with a focus on “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity.” The authors claim that their modification doesn’t alter the algorithm’s behavior but reduces its computational cost. The proposed method, termed “Cholesky-CMA-ES,” is a misleading name, as it was first introduced by T. Suttorp et al. (2009) for a similar algorithm and is well-established in the literature. The authors fail to acknowledge this prior work, instead presenting their variant as “Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES.” The work builds incrementally upon Krause et al. (2015); specifically, the algorithmic contribution is applying the `rankOneUpdate` function (also present in Krause et al.’s work) iteratively to all individuals, rather than just once as in Plus strategies.

Empirically, Figure 3(b) reveals a difference in the plateau observed for the Cigar function between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES, with differing objective function values. This discrepancy may stem from the authors’ method of plotting median results. The paper should include a comparison to both Suttorp’s (2009) Cholesky-CMA-ES, empirically, and Krause’s (2015) Cholesky-CMA-ES, algorithmically. 

The claim of “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is somewhat misleading. It isn't optimal when considering functions like Sphere, where the covariance matrix is unnecessary, or separable functions where a diagonal matrix suffices. The proposed approach is only twice as efficient as the omitted Cholesky CMA-ES from Suttorp et al. (2009). While the authors claim the latter is prone to numerical instabilities, the original paper suggested the opposite. Furthermore, omitting this comparison in Figure 3 risks misleading the reader about the true contribution of this work. 

I don’t believe this approach fundamentally changes CMA-ES’s performance in large-scale optimization, as the complexity remains quadratic. However, I concede that the proposed modification may represent the best possible trade-off between computational time and memory usage when a full covariance matrix is required.

Overall, the work feels too incremental – algorithmically, it essentially adds a single “for” loop. I suggest the following revisions: a) Resolve the naming conflict; b) Conduct experiments comparing the proposed method to the variants of Cholesky-CMA-ES from Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause et al. (2015), and update all three figures accordingly. If this results in unreadable figures, present only the best-performing variant in the main text and include the full figures in the Supplementary Material; c) Either clearly articulate why the “for” loop in Algorithm 1 constitutes a significant algorithmic contribution, or explain why it might *not* be considered a substantial change.

Minor comment: The statement “Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step” is inaccurate. In well-implemented CMA-ES algorithms, eigenvalue decomposition is typically performed periodically, not in every iteration.
