This paper proposes an efficient modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a powerful real-valued derivative-free optimization algorithm. The authors replace the eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix with a Cholesky decomposition, significantly reducing the time and storage complexity. The paper is well-written, and the proposed method appears reasonable. The experimental results, particularly the speedup rates, are promising and suggest that the proposed method could become a new standard version of CMA-ES.

The paper's main contribution is the adaptation of the Cholesky-CMA-ES to the standard CMA-ES, which leads to a runtime of O(µd^2). The authors provide a theoretical justification for the convergence of the new algorithm and demonstrate its efficiency through experiments. However, the originality of the paper is limited, as the method is an extension of previous work (Krause and Igel, 2015).

Some essential contents are missing or not fully addressed. Firstly, the authors should demonstrate the equivalence of their algorithm to the original CMA-ES update rule (equation 2). Although the proof may have been established in previous work, it is crucial to include it in this paper for completeness. Secondly, the authors claim that their algorithm is faster than CMA-ES-Ref, despite having the same computational complexity of O(µd^2). A more detailed analysis, potentially using the Θ notation, is necessary to explain why the proposed algorithm outperforms CMA-ES-Ref. Lastly, the authors' claim that the error in the path is small and decreases over time contradicts Hansen's assertion. While the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to validate this claim, and a more in-depth comparison than just the final performance is required.

Additionally, there are some minor issues with notation and formatting. For example, 'wk' in Algorithm 2 should be 'αk', and 'n' in Table 1 should be 'd'. Furthermore, 'F' should be 'f' in line 185.

In its current form, the paper is not suitable for NIPS due to the aforementioned limitations and omissions. However, with revisions to address these concerns, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field.

* The paper proposes an efficient modification to CMA-ES, reducing time and storage complexity.
* The experimental results demonstrate a significant speedup, suggesting the proposed method could become a new standard version of CMA-ES.

Weaknesses:

* Limited originality, as the method is an extension of previous work.
* Essential contents are missing or not fully addressed, such as the equivalence to the original CMA-ES update rule and a detailed analysis of the computational complexity.
* Theoretical analysis is not deterministic, and experiments should be conducted to validate the claim about the error in the path.
