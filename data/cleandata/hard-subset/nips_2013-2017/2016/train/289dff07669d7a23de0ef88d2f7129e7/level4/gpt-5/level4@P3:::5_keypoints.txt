- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A and replaces C^{-1/2} in CSA with A^{-1}, omitting a time-varying rotation E as the main approximation.  
- Theoretical justification: under mild convergence of the normalized covariance, the missing rotations align asymptotically (E_{t-1}^T E_t → I), so CSA error vanishes.  
- Algorithmic contribution: extends rank-one Cholesky updates to rank-1 + rank-μ, yielding O(μ d^2) time and optimal storage while avoiding cubic decompositions.  
- Empirical results: on standard benchmarks and random rotations, matches reference CMA-ES in function evaluations and gives large wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension.  
- Technical soundness: algebraic decomposition and stable quadratic-time Cholesky updates are well motivated; lemma supports asymptotic CSA approximation.  
- Experimental support: experiments span multiple problem classes, include rotation tests, and report both evaluations and CPU time; implementation uses Shark + LAPACK.  
- Main limitation: theoretical guarantees are asymptotic and do not address transient behavior or noisy objectives; CSA approximation dynamics need more empirical analysis.  
- Clarity: paper is generally clear and organized; CSA exposition is helpful, but some diagnostics belong in the main text and there are minor typos/citation issues.  
- Originality: incremental but meaningful—applies Cholesky-update ideas to full rank-μ CMA-ES with a practical CSA approximation.  
- Significance: valuable when evaluations are cheap and d is moderate-to-large (e.g., policy search, algorithm configuration); less relevant when evaluations dominate or d is extreme.  
- Pros: O(μ d^2) update with optimal storage; asymptotic CSA justification; empirical parity in evaluations with large runtime gains; provides eigenvalues and efficient inversion; rotation-robust and publicly implemented.  
- Cons: CSA guarantees only asymptotic; robustness under noise not analyzed; practical gains depend on evaluation cost; missing comparisons to limited-memory hybrids at very high d; some analyses kept in supplement.  
- Suggested improvements: quantify CSA approximation error/rotation drift over time; run paired CSA trajectory comparisons; provide per-iteration CPU/memory breakdown and break-even analysis; add fixed-time-budget curves; include LM-CMA/diagonal baselines and noise/constraint robustness tests; add a real-world fast-eval case study; move key diagnostics to main text and fix reproducibility/formatting issues.  
- Overall: a solid, practical contribution that speeds full-covariance CMA-ES without losing performance and would benefit from additional empirical diagnostics and broader baseline comparisons.
