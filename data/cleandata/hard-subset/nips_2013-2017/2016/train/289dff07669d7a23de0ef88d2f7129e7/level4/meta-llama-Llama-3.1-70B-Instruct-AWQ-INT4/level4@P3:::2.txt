This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) by utilizing the Cholesky decomposition instead of the eigen-decomposition, aiming to reduce the time and storage complexity of the algorithm. The paper is well-written, and the proposed method appears reasonable. The experimental results, particularly the speedup rates, are promising and suggest that the proposed method may become a new standard version of CMA-ES in the future.

The main idea of the paper is to replace the matrix square root with the triangular Cholesky factor in the CMA-ES, which is a common approach in optimization algorithms. The authors provide a theoretical justification for this modification and demonstrate its effectiveness through experiments on various benchmark functions.

However, the originality of the paper is limited, as the method is an extension of previous work, specifically "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies." The authors should clarify the differences and contributions of their work compared to the previous study.

There are some essential contents missing in the paper. Firstly, the authors should prove that their algorithm is equivalent to the original CMA-ES update rule (formula (2)). Although the proof may have been established in previous work, it is crucial to include it in this paper for completeness.

Secondly, the authors claim that the computational complexity of their algorithm is O(μd^2), which is the same as the CMA-ES-Ref. However, they should provide a detailed discussion on why their algorithm is faster, possibly using the Θ notation instead of the O notation.

Thirdly, the authors claim that the error in the path is small and decreases over time, which contradicts Hansen's claim. Since the theoretical analysis is not deterministic, experiments should be conducted to validate this claim. Comparing only the final performance is not sufficient.

Some minor comments include:

* The authors should check their notations carefully. For example, wk in Algorithm 2 should be αk, and n in Table 1 should be d.
* 'F' should be 'f' in line 185.

In its current form, the paper is not qualified for NIPS due to the lack of essential contents and clarity on the contributions of the work. However, with revisions addressing these issues, the paper has the potential to make a significant contribution to the field of optimization algorithms.
