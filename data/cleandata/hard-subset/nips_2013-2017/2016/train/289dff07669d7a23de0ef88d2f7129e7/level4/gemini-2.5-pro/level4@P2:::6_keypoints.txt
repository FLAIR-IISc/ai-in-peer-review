- Proposes "Cholesky-CMA-ES" that updates the Cholesky factor in O(µ d^2) to avoid O(d^3) EVD in CMA-ES.  
- Approximates CSA by replacing the inverse matrix square root with the inverse triangular Cholesky factor; Lemma 1 claims the approximation error vanishes on convergence.  
- Empirically shows substantial wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension while maintaining similar numbers of function evaluations.  

- Strength: Tackles a practical bottleneck, making CMA-ES more applicable to higher-dimensional problems where full covariance is needed.  
- Strength: Theoretical argument for the CSA approximation is sound and gives intuition via convergence of rotation matrices.  
- Strength: Experiments (Figures 1–2) convincingly demonstrate runtime improvements without harming convergence behavior in tested benchmarks.  

- Weakness: Incremental novelty — mainly extends Krause & Igel (2015)'s rankOneUpdate to the rank-µ setting; novelty not clearly articulated.  
- Weakness: Naming clash with Suttorp et al. (2009), who already used "Cholesky-CMA-ES"; the paper fails to acknowledge or distinguish prior work.  
- Weakness: Lacks direct experimental comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009); claims about that method’s numerical instability are unsupported.  
- Weakness: Title/claims are overstated — "optimal" complexity is only under full-rank assumptions and O(d^2) still limits very large-scale use; wording should be qualified.  
- Weakness: Unexplained empirical discrepancy on the Cigar function where medians differ between methods; requires investigation.  

- Recommendation: Explicitly differentiate contributions from Krause & Igel (2015).  
- Recommendation: Acknowledge or rename to resolve the Suttorp et al. (2009) conflict and clarify differences.  
- Recommendation: Include empirical comparisons with Suttorp et al. (2009) and provide evidence for any stability claims.  
- Recommendation: Temper "optimal" and "large-scale" claims and correct minor inaccuracies (e.g., EVD is not typically done every iteration in optimized implementations).
