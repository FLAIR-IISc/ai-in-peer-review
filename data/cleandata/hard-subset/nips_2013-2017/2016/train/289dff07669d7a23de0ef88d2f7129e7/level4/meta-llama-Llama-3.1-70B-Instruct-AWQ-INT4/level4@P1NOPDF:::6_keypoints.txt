- Naming is misleading: calling the method "Cholesky-CMA-ES" ignores prior use by Suttorp et al. (2009) and fails to acknowledge that work.
- Omission of Suttorp et al.'s Cholesky-CMA-ES from citation and experiments is unacceptable.
- The method is an incremental extension of Krause et al. (2015); the core novelty is repeatedly calling rankOneUpdate in a loop.
- The paper does not convincingly justify novelty—adding a for-loop is presented as the main algorithmic contribution.
- Empirical results show a discrepancy on the Cigar function between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES, possibly due to plotting medians, making results unclear.
- The paper should compare empirically to Suttorp's variant and algorithmically to Krause's variant.
- The "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" claim is misleading: it doesn't hold for Sphere or separable functions and only yields ~2x improvement over Suttorp's method.
- Excluding Suttorp's method from Figure 3 risks misleading readers about the paper's contribution; Suttorp et al. actually claim improved numerical stability, contrary to this paper's claims.
- The approach does not change asymptotic complexity (still quadratic), so it likely does not substantially advance large-scale applicability, though it may give practical speed/memory gains.
- Recommendations: fix naming/citation issues; run experiments including Suttorp/Krause variants and update figures (use supplement if cluttered); clearly explain or justify the for-loop as the main contribution.
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically performed periodically in practice, not at every update step.
