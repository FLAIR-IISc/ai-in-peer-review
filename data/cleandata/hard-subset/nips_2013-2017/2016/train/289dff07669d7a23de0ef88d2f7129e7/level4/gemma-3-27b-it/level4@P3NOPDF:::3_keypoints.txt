- Proposes a Cholesky CMA-ES variant with a sequential rank-1 scheme implementing a rank-µ update to the triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Claims modified cumulative step-size adaptation preserves algorithm behavior under covariance convergence assumptions.  
- Provides experimental results to support algorithmic claims.  
- Contribution appears incremental, mainly generalizing Krause et al. (2015) rank-1 update to rank-µ.  
- Novelty is limited and it's unclear whether rank-µ consistently outperforms the simpler rank-1 method.  
- Lacks thorough comparative analysis to demonstrate clear benefits of the rank-µ approach.  
- Theoretical justification (Lemma 1) relies on approximations; needs deeper discussion relating to existing CMA-ES convergence proofs.  
- Authors should elaborate on implications of approximations and how assumptions affect validity.  
- Typographical errors: σ in Eq.(1) and Footnote 2 should be σ_t; grammatical error “which can by be motivated…” needs fixing.  
- Strengths: addresses a relevant CMA-ES topic, offers some theoretical justification, and includes experiments.  
- Weaknesses: incremental contribution, unconvincing justification for added complexity, incomplete connection to convergence literature, minor presentation issues.  
- Questions for authors: justify rank-µ over rank-1 and conditions where it is superior; sensitivity and principled choice of µ; implications of approximations in convergence proofs; computational cost overhead of rank-µ.  
- Overall recommendation: weak accept; technically sound but needs stronger justification and theoretical clarification, open to reconsideration after major revisions.
