This paper proposes a modification to the Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy (CMA-ES) by incorporating both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates for the triangular Cholesky factor in a sequence of rank-1 updates. The authors demonstrate, under the assumption of convergence of the covariance, that the modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not significantly alter the algorithm's behavior. The paper also presents experimental results on the algorithm's performance.

While the paper explores an interesting extension of the CMA-ES, I am not convinced that its contribution is substantial, particularly in light of the work by Krause et al. (2015), which employed a rank-1 update for the triangular Cholesky factor. The current paper presents a straightforward generalization of this procedure to rank-\mu updates in a sequence of rank-1 updates. However, this extension may not be significant enough to warrant a standalone publication.

Furthermore, the convergence of the covariance matrix $C_t$ in CMA-ES has been theoretically analyzed by Beyer (ECJ, 2014), albeit not based on the exact formulae of CMA-ES. This existing work may enhance the confidence in Lemma 1 presented in the paper.

In addition to these concerns, there are a few minor issues that need to be addressed:

1. In equation (1), the denominator should be $\sigma_t$ instead of $\sigma$. A similar error occurs in footnote 2 on page 3.
2. In line 94, the phrase "which can by be motivated by principles from ..." should be corrected by removing the word "by".

Overall, while the paper presents a coherent idea, its contribution and impact are limited by the existing work in the field. The authors should carefully consider these concerns and revise the paper to address them.
