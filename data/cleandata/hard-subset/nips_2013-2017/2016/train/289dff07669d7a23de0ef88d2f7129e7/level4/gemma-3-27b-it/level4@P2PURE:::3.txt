This paper investigates Cholesky covariance matrix adaptation evolution strategy, employing both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates for the triangular Cholesky factor, applied sequentially as rank-1 updates. The authors prove, under the assumption of covariance convergence, that their modified cumulative step-size adaptation does not substantially alter the algorithm’s behavior. The paper also includes experimental results demonstrating algorithm performance. 1. The paper extends the Cholesky CMA-ES by generalizing the update procedure for the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-\mu updates. However, I find the contribution limited, given prior work by Krause et al. (2015), which already applied rank-1 updates to the triangular Cholesky factor. This work presents a direct generalization of that procedure to rank-\mu updates within a sequence of rank-1 updates. 2. Convergence of the covariance matrix $C_t$ in CMA-ES is typically established through theoretical analysis (e.g., Beyer, ECJ, 2014), although this analysis often doesn’t rely on the precise formulae of CMA-ES.
