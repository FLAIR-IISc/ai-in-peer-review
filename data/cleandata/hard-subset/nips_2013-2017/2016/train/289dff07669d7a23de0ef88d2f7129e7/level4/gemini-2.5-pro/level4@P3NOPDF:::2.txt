This paper proposes an efficient update mechanism for the covariance matrix in the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES). By leveraging a Cholesky decomposition instead of the standard but more costly eigendecomposition, the authors aim to reduce the time and storage complexity of the algorithm, particularly for the rank-one update.

The paper is well-written and the proposed method appears to be methodologically sound. The primary strength of this work lies in its impressive empirical results, which demonstrate a significant speedup over standard CMA-ES implementations. Given these performance gains, the proposed variant has the potential to become a new standard for CMA-ES in practical applications.

Despite these strengths, the paper has several major weaknesses that, in my opinion, make it unsuitable for publication at NIPS in its current form.

**Major Concerns:**

1.  **Limited Originality:** My primary concern is the paper's novelty in light of prior work, specifically "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies" (Ros & Hansen, GECCO 2008). The core method presented here—using Cholesky factors to perform a more efficient rank-one update of the covariance matrix—appears to be substantially similar, if not identical, to the approach described in that paper. The current submission needs to clearly and explicitly differentiate its contributions from this existing work. As it stands, the paper reads as an implementation or validation of a previously published idea rather than a novel algorithmic contribution. If I have misunderstood the key differences, I urge the authors to clarify them.

2.  **Omission of Fundamental Proof:** The paper claims its update rule is an efficient implementation of the standard CMA-ES covariance matrix update (formula 2), but it does not provide or cite a proof of their mathematical equivalence. While space is limited and this may have been established in previous literature, this equivalence is fundamental to the paper's central claim. For the paper to be self-contained and for its claims to be rigorously verified, this proof should be included, at the very least in an appendix.

3.  **Insufficient Complexity Analysis:** The authors state that the computational complexity of their algorithm is O(d²), which is the same asymptotic complexity as the reference CMA-ES implementation they compare against. This is misleading, as the significant speedups reported in the experiments clearly stem from differences in the constant factors hidden by the big-O notation. A more precise analysis is needed to theoretically ground the observed performance gains. The authors should provide a detailed breakdown of the computational costs, perhaps using Θ-notation, to clearly show why their sequence of operations is substantially faster than the standard eigendecomposition-based approach.

4.  **Unvalidated Claim Regarding Path Error:** The authors claim that the error in the evolution path is small and decreases over time. This assertion appears to contradict previous analyses by Hansen, who suggested that such errors can accumulate. Since the paper's theoretical analysis of this error is not deterministic, the claim requires strong empirical validation. The current experiments, which only compare final optimization performance, are insufficient to support this claim. The authors should conduct and present results from experiments specifically designed to measure and track the accumulation of this error throughout the optimization process.

**Minor Comments:**

1.  **Notation Inconsistencies:** Please review the notations for consistency. For instance:
    *   In Algorithm 2, `wk` should likely be `αk`.
    *   In Table 1, the problem dimension is denoted by `n`, whereas it is referred to as `d` throughout the rest of the paper.
2.  **Typo:** In line 185, the function `F` should likely be lowercase `f` to maintain consistency with the problem definition.

**Conclusion:**

In summary, while the paper addresses an important practical problem and presents promising empirical results, it is undermined by significant concerns regarding its originality, the omission of a key mathematical proof, an imprecise complexity analysis, and an unsubstantiated claim about error accumulation. These issues prevent me from recommending acceptance at this time. However, the work shows potential, and I would be willing to reconsider my evaluation if the authors can thoroughly address these points in a major revision.
