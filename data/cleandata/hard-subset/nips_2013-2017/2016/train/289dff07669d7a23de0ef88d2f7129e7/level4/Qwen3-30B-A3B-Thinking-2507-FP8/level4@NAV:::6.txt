

The paper introduces a CMA-ES variant termed "Cholesky-CMA-ES" with claimed "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity." The authors assert this modification preserves algorithmic behavior while reducing computational cost. However, the name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" was previously established by Suttorp et al. (2009) for a distinct algorithm and is well-recognized in the field. The authors fail to acknowledge this, instead presenting their variant as "Our CMA-ES variant, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES." The method constitutes a minor incremental adjustment to Krause et al. (2015), with the sole algorithmic novelty being the replacement of a single `rankOneUpdate` call (already present in Krause et al.) with a loop over all individuals. Empirically, Figure b) shows differing plateau objective values for Cigar on CMA-ES-Ref versus Cholesky-CMA-ES, likely due to median plotting; the paper should empirically compare against Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically against Krause’s work. The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is misleading: it is not optimal for separable functions (e.g., Sphere, where covariance is unnecessary or diagonal suffices), and the proposed method is only approximately twice as efficient as Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES—contradicting the original Suttorp paper’s assertion of numerical stability. Omitting Suttorp’s variant from Figure 3 misrepresents the contribution. The approach does not advance large-scale CMA-ES, as complexity remains quadratic. While the modification may offer practical efficiency gains when full covariance is required, the algorithmic contribution is minimal (reducing to a single loop in Algorithm 1). The work is overly incremental. I recommend: a) resolving the naming conflict; b) adding empirical comparisons against Suttorp and Krause variants and revising all three figures (with full results in Supplementary Material if needed); c) clarifying that the core contribution is the loop in Algorithm 1 or justifying why this is non-trivial. Minor note: "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition... is performed in each update step" is inaccurate; it is standard practice to perform this periodically, not in every step.
