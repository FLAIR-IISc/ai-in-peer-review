This work's primary contribution is the utilization of the triangular Cholesky factorization technique, which eliminates the need for the square root operation in the standard CMA-ES, thereby reducing the computational cost associated with matrix inversion. The author presents an approach with theoretical justification, demonstrating that this modification does not compromise the algorithm's performance. The proposed method decreases the runtime complexity of the algorithm without significantly altering the number of objective function evaluations. This paper provides a valuable implementation of the Cholesky factorization method for CMA-ES.

To further strengthen the theoretical foundation, it would be beneficial for the author to provide a more detailed explanation of why the proposed computation is 'numerically more stable'. The results suggest that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires the same number of objective function evaluations as the standard CMA-ES, while reducing wall-clock time, with this advantage increasing as the dimensionality of the search space grows. Considering the high dimensionality often encountered in practical applications, such as training neural networks in direct policy search, the author may want to explore combining the low-dimensional approximation proposed by Loshchilov (2015) with the existing framework, as this could be a valuable extension for addressing high-dimensional problems.
