- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution, interpretable as adapting an underlying metric.  
- The paper proposes a novel CMA-ES variant that updates the covariance via triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based approach significantly reduces computational complexity and memory requirements compared to existing methods.  
- Numerical evaluation shows the new method preserves standard CMA-ES performance in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The method is a substantial contribution and is likely to become a default for medium-to-large-scale applications due to lower memory/complexity and free eigenvalue access.  
- The paper is well-written, carefully evaluated, and appropriate for NIPS given ties to ML, information geometry, RL, and supervised learning.  
- Minor suggestion: report implementation/comparison details (CPU, implementation language) to ensure fair runtime comparisons.
