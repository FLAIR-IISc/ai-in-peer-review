- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, replacing matrix square root in CSA with inverse triangular Cholesky factor.  
- Theoretically justifies that replacing C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹ yields vanishing error as algorithm converges (Lemma 1).  
- Reduces time complexity to O(µd²) and space to O(d²) for full covariance adaptation.  
- Empirically validated on 12 benchmark functions up to d=256, showing identical function evaluations to standard CMA-ES.  
- Runtime experiments show up to ~20× speedup at d=64 versus CMA-ES-Ref, with speedup increasing with dimension.  
- Novelty: first theoretically grounded Cholesky-based CSA for standard full-covariance CMA-ES, distinct from prior limited or non-guaranteed approaches.  
- Significance: directly addresses scalability bottleneck in high-dimensional derivative-free optimization (e.g., RL, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Clarity: well-organized presentation with clear algorithms and strong connection between theory and experiments.  
- Weakness: Insufficient detail justifying claimed numerical stability of inverse Cholesky vs. matrix square root (no condition-number or decomposition comparison).  
- Weakness: Superficial treatment of integration with low-rank/low-dimensional approximation methods and no quantified crossover point.  
- Pro/Con summary: Rigorous, empirically backed speedup with optimal complexity for full covariance; minor gaps in numerical-stability explanation and hybridization guidance.  
- Recommendation: Accept — strong contribution with easily addressable minor weaknesses.
