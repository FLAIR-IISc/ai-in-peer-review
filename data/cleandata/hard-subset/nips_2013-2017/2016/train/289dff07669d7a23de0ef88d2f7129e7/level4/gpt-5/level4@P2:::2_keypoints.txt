- Paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain/update triangular Cholesky factor A of covariance C directly with quadratic-time rank-1 and rank-µ updates instead of recomputing C and its eigendecomposition.  
- Replace CSA’s C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} and argue (via RQ/polar decomposition) that rotation error is small and vanishes as covariance stabilizes.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while giving large wall-clock speedups, lower memory, and easy access to eigenvalues.  
- Builds on prior work updating decompositions (Suttorp et al. 2009) and rank-one Cholesky updates (Krause & Igel 2015); claimed novelty is extending to full rank-µ CMA-ES and justifying CSA change.  
- Strength: large, consistent runtime speedups that grow with dimension without degrading optimization performance.  
- Strength: numerically stable, quadratic-time updates, optimal storage, and efficient triangular inversion.  
- Strength: broad empirical evaluation with rotated benchmarks and higher-dimensional tests.  
- Strength: generally clear and well-organized manuscript.  
- Weakness: novelty not clearly distinguished from Krause & Igel (2015) and Suttorp et al. (2009); need explicit statement of new technical contributions.  
- Weakness: lacks concise derivation/theorem proving Algorithm 2 yields the same Ct+1 as the standard covariance update (eq. 2); correctness depends on this equivalence.  
- Weakness: complexity claims (O(µ d^2)) need precise Θ operation counts, comparison to CMA-ES-Ref with delayed eigendecompositions, and empirical flop-normalized scaling.  
- Weakness: CSA approximation/theoretical error analysis is non-deterministic; missing targeted experiments comparing pσ, σ trajectories, rotation drift metrics, and ablations isolating A^{-1} vs C^{-1/2}.  
- Weakness: rotation/CSA invariance metrics should be reported in the main text (not only supplementary) and deviations discussed.  
- Weakness: claim of asymptotic optimality O(µ d^2) needs a supporting lower-bound argument.  
- Weakness: experimental implementation details unclear (BLAS/LAPACK use, cache effects), µ as function of d, memory comparisons, and larger-d experiments (d > 256) need discussion.  
- Minor: notation/typos (Algorithm 2 wk→αk, Table 1 d vs n, Line 185 “F”→“f”), inconsistent bolding/indices/normalization; fix references and terminology (Cholesky factor vs matrix square root, RQ vs polar).  
- For acceptance: practical impact, numerical stability, and empirical speedups make the work valuable to CMA-ES users.  
- Against acceptance: perceived incremental novelty, missing equivalence proof, insufficient CSA/invariance validation, and unclear complexity claims warrant rejection pending revision.  
- Overall: promising and practically useful but presently not at conference bar—needs clearer novelty, formal equivalence proof, tightened complexity analysis, and targeted experiments validating CSA and invariance.
