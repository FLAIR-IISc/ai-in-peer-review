- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance updates from O(d^3) eigendecomposition to O(µ d^2) and O(d^2) storage.  
- Key technical change: adapt CSA by approximating C^{-1/2} with the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1}; theoretical argument assumes normalized covariance convergence and empirical results show wall-clock speedups without extra evaluations.  
- Significance: tackles a major CMA-ES bottleneck, making the method more practical for higher-dimensional problems and cheap-evaluation settings, likely to see wide adoption.  
- Quality: method appears technically sound and experiments convincingly demonstrate substantial runtime reductions across benchmarks.  
- Clarity: paper is well-written, well-organized, and easy to follow.  
- Originality concern: needs stronger contextualization relative to Krause & Igel (2015) and explicit explanation of the nontrivial challenges in extending rank-one Cholesky updates to rank-µ (µ,λ)-CMA-ES.  
- CSA approximation concern: Lemma 1 relies on a strong convergence assumption for a stochastic algorithm; the paper lacks direct empirical validation that the approximation does not alter search behavior.  
- Complexity discussion weakness: asymptotic O(d^3)→O(µ d^2) is clear, but the paper should analyze leading constants (Cholesky update vs full eigendecomposition) to explain observed practical speedups.  
- Request 1 (detail): Include or cite a derivation showing that the sequence of rank-one updates on the Cholesky factor is equivalent to the full rank-µ covariance update.  
- Request 2 (experiment): Add experiments measuring the discrepancy between approximate and true CSA paths (e.g., cosine distance between pσ computed with A^{-1} vs C^{-1/2}) during runs.  
- Request 3 (edits): Fix minor notational inconsistencies (Algorithm 2 variable typo wk vs αk, table uses n vs d, objective denoted F vs f).  
- Overall conclusion: Valuable, practically impactful work with impressive empirical gains, but would be stronger after clarifying novelty over prior work and providing direct empirical support for the central CSA approximation.
