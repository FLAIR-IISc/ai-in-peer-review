

This paper proposes using Cholesky decomposition instead of eigen-decomposition to reduce time and storage complexity in CMA-ES. The writing is clear, the method appears well-motivated, and the experimental results—particularly the speedup rates—are promising. The approach could become a new standard for CMA-ES. However, the originality is limited, as the method constitutes a trivial extension of prior work ("A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies"), with essentially identical technical content; please correct me if this assessment is inaccurate.  

Three critical omissions undermine the paper's technical contribution:  
1. The equivalence to Equation (2) must be explicitly demonstrated. While the prior work provides proof, this is essential for the current paper's validity.  
2. The claim of \(O(\mu d^2)\) computational complexity (identical to CMA-ES-Ref) requires detailed justification. Specifically, use \(\Theta\) notation to clarify asymptotic speedup and compare constant factors.  
3. The assertion that "path error is small and decreases over time" directly contradicts Hansen's findings. Since the theoretical analysis is non-deterministic, experiments must validate this claim beyond final performance metrics (e.g., track error evolution during optimization).  

Minor issues:  
- Notation errors: \(w_k\) in Algorithm 2 must be \(\alpha_k\); \(n\) in Table 1 should be \(d\).  
- Typo: 'F' in line 185 should be 'f'.  

Given these significant gaps in technical rigor and novelty, the paper is not suitable for NIPS in its current form.
