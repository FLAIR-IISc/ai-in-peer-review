The paper presents a modified version of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) with "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity". The authors claim that their proposed modification, referred to as Cholesky-CMA-ES, does not alter the algorithm's behavior but reduces its computational cost. However, the name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is misleading, as it was first introduced by T. Suttorp et al. in 2009 for a similar algorithm, which is well-known in the field. The authors fail to acknowledge this existing work and instead present their variant as a novel contribution.

The proposed method is an incremental extension of the work by Krause et al. (2015). The only algorithmic contribution is the repeated application of the rankOneUpdate function, which is already present in Krause et al.'s work, but now called in a loop for all individuals. Empirical results show a difference in the Cigar function in Figure b), where the observed plateau has different objective function values for CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES. This discrepancy might be due to the authors' plotting of median results.

To strengthen the paper, the authors should empirically compare their proposed approach to the Cholesky-CMA-ES by Suttorp and algorithmically compare it to the Cholesky-CMA-ES by Krause. The claim of "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is somewhat misleading, as it is not optimal for functions like the Sphere function, where the covariance matrix is not necessary, or separable functions, where only the diagonal is required. Furthermore, the proposed approach is only twice as efficient as the omitted Cholesky CMA-ES by Suttorp, which is claimed to be prone to numerical instabilities, contrary to the original paper's findings. The omission of Suttorp's method in Figure 3 may mislead the reader about the actual contribution of the paper.

I question whether the proposed approach significantly impacts CMA-ES for large-scale optimization, as the complexity remains quadratic. However, I acknowledge that the proposed modification might be the most efficient way to save time and memory when considering the full covariance matrix.

Overall, I find the work to be too incremental, with the only algorithmic contribution being a single "for" loop. To improve the paper, I suggest the following modifications: a) resolve the naming issue; b) perform experiments with variants of Suttorp et al. and Krause et al. and update all three figures; if the figures become unreadable, show only the best-performing variant in the main text and include the full figures in the Supplementary Material; c) better explain the main algorithmic contribution or clarify why it is incorrect to view it as a simple "for" loop. Minor notes: the statement "Typically, an eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix is performed in each update step" is inaccurate, as it is typically done periodically in proper implementations.
