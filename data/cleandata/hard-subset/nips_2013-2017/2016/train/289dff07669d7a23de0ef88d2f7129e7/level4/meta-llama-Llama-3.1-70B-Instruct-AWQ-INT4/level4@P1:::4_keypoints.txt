- Main contribution: use triangular Cholesky factorization to remove the square root in CMA-ES, cutting computation for matrix inverse with theoretical justification and no performance loss.
- Algorithmic benefit: reduces runtime complexity while keeping the number of objective function evaluations unchanged.
- Empirical result: Cholesky-CMA-ES matches standard CMA-ES in evaluations but significantly reduces wall-clock time, with larger speedups in higher dimensions.
- Applicability: especially suited for large-scale optimization problems.
- Numerical stability: authors claim improved numerical stability due to triangular Cholesky, but a deeper theoretical explanation is needed.
- Suggested extension: explore combining the approach with low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) for very high-dimensional practical tasks like neural network policy search.
- Overall assessment: significant and valuable contribution to derivative-free optimization with potential for strong impact.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel and efficient algorithm, theoretical justification, and experimental validation with potential large-scale impact.
- Arguments against acceptance: insufficient theoretical detail on numerical stability and limited exploration of extensions for high-dimensional practical applications.
