- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains and updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor to avoid full covariance decomposition.  
- Core technical idea: approximate C^{-1/2} in the CSA update by the inverse Cholesky factor A^{-1}, backed by Lemma 1 showing rotation matrices converge so approximation error vanishes.  
- Achieves asymptotically optimal O(μ d^2) update time and O(d^2) memory, versus O(d^3) for standard CMA-ES.  
- Empirical results show large wall-clock time reductions in higher dimensions with no loss in function-evaluation efficiency.  
- Significant impact: addresses a major bottleneck in a widely used derivative-free optimizer, making CMA-ES practical for medium-to-large d.  
- Technical quality: sound theoretical justification (RQ-decomposition argument) — not an ad-hoc heuristic.  
- Presentation: paper is clear, well-organized, and distinguishes itself from prior Cholesky-based attempts.  
- Experiments: thorough comparisons on diverse benchmarks, reporting both evaluation counts and wall-clock times.  
- Practical advantages: cheap access to covariance eigenvalues and improved numerical stability from updating a single triangular factor.  
- Weakness: wall-clock comparisons need implementation details (language, compilers, BLAS/LAPACK, optimization flags) to ensure fairness.  
- Weakness: high-dimensional behavior (d=256) needs discussion—possible cache/memory-access differences causing reduced speedup versus d=128.  
- Overall verdict: excellent, high-impact contribution with minor, easily addressable clarifications.
