- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor directly to reduce covariance adaptation complexity from O(d³) to O(μd²).

- Method omits estimation of the orthogonal matrix E_t used in standard CMA-ES.

- Provides an asymptotic argument (under restrictive convergence assumptions) that E_t → I, implying equivalence of the schemes in the limit.

- Empirically demonstrates runtime speed-up and comparable convergence on standard benchmark functions versus two CMA-ES variants with update delays.

- Claims a solid contribution by achieving substantial speed-up without apparent performance degradation on synthetic benchmarks.

- Raises practical concern that objective-function evaluation often dominates runtime, so update speed-ups matter mainly for very fast evaluations and higher-dimensional problems.

- Suggests the paper should better motivate the practical relevance of the targeted high-speed, high-dimension scenario.

- Recommends tracking the evolution/deviation of E_t from identity (e.g., using a matrix norm) to provide empirical evidence for the core assumption.

- Requests a direct empirical comparison with the original (no-delay) CMA-ES as a baseline for runtime and performance.

- Calls for a more systematic study on quadratic functions, examining performance and E_t approximation error as a function of Hessian conditioning/spectrum.

- Proposes adding a real-world experiment (e.g., policy optimization in real-time robotics) where fast evaluations make update speed critical.
