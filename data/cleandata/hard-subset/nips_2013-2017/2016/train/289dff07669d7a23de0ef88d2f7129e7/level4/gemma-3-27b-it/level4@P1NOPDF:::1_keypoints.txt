- CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (i.e., an underlying metric).  
- The paper proposes a new covariance update using triangular Cholesky factors that significantly reduces computational complexity and memory usage relative to prior schemes.  
- Numerical evaluation shows the new update preserves performance in function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The reviewer views this as a significant contribution that could become the default for medium- to large-scale problems due to lower memory, reduced complexity, and readily available covariance eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and appropriate for NIPS given CMA-ES’s links to machine learning, information geometry, RL, and supervised learning.  
- Minor comment: clarify how implementations were made comparable in CPU resources (implementation language, compiler versions, etc.).  
- Minor comment: explain why Figure 2’s claim about increased differences with dimensionality does not hold for the Cigar and Discus functions at 256 dimensions.
