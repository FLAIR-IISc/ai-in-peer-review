- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance, reducing covariance update complexity to O(µd²).  
- CSA approximation: replaces C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹, introducing an orthogonal error E_t that is argued to converge to identity under covariance-shape convergence.  
- Empirical claim: shows substantial wall-clock speed-up over delayed-update CMA-ES variants with no significant increase in function evaluations on standard benchmarks.  

Strengths:
- Significance & originality: addresses a key CMA-ES bottleneck and attains asymptotically minimal update cost.  
- Technical quality: non-trivial derivation and sound theoretical rationale for the approximation.  
- Clarity: well-written, clearly motivated algorithm and accessible theoretical argument.  
- Empirical validation: convincing benchmark results demonstrating wall-clock gains without loss of optimization performance.  

Weaknesses / areas for improvement:
- Practical relevance: speed-up mainly matters for cheap objective evaluations and high dimensions (e.g., d > 50); target scenario should be better motivated.  
- Missing direct empirical check: the critical assumption that E_t → I is not measured or visualized in experiments.  
- Benchmarking scope: needs more systematic tests (e.g., varying Hessian condition numbers/spectra) to probe robustness.  
- Real-world demonstration: lacks a high-dimensional, fast-eval real application (e.g., direct policy search in RL or robotics) to show practical impact.  
- Baseline completeness: should include standard (non-delayed) CMA-ES runtimes for lower dimensions to bound achievable speed-up.  

Summary:
- Pro: novel, well-motivated, technically solid method achieving optimal asymptotic complexity with strong synthetic benchmark results.  
- Con/for revision: limited practical regime, unverified core approximation empirically, and evaluation could be more comprehensive and realistic.
