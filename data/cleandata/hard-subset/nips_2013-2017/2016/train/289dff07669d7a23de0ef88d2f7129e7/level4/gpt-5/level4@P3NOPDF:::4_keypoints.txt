- Summary: Proposes a Cholesky-based CMA-ES that updates a triangular factor of the covariance to avoid eigen-decompositions/inversions while preserving search behavior and efficiency.  
- Strength: Clear computational benefit—avoids costly eigen-decompositions and repeated inversions, reducing wall-clock time especially in higher dimensions.  
- Strength: Theoretical grounding—authors argue the search distribution and convergence properties are preserved, implying no significant increase in function evaluations.  
- Strength: Practical relevance—an implementation improvement likely useful where wall-clock time matters.  
- Weakness: Numerical stability claim is underdeveloped—needs formal detail and evidence.  
- Weakness (stability specifics): Clarify which numerical errors (e.g., accumulated asymmetry, eigen-decomposition sensitivity, loss of PD) are mitigated by triangular updates.  
- Weakness (stability specifics): State conditions under which Cholesky updates maintain positive definiteness without frequent re-factorization or ad-hoc repairs, and provide worst-case error/drift bounds.  
- Weakness (stability specifics): Analyze how conditioning of C evolves versus standard CMA-ES and whether the Cholesky update introduces or reduces bias in covariance adaptation.  
- Weakness: Complexity characterization is informal—provide a crisp per-generation amortized cost comparison (e.g., O(n^3) every k gens vs O(n^2) per gen).  
- Weakness: High-dimensional regimes need discussion/experiments—consider combining with low-rank or limited-memory variants for n in the thousands.  
- Weakness: Empirical scope is limited—add benchmarks on ill-conditioned/non-separable problems, very large dimensions (n ≥ 1000), and sensitivity analyses for step-size and covariance learning rates.  
- Weakness: Implementation details lacking—report any safeguards (jitter, periodic re-factorization, symmetry enforcement), memory footprint vs standard CMA-ES, and parallel-sampling implications.  
- Weakness: Numerical precision requirements (single vs double) and their impact on stability/speed are not addressed.  
- Clarity: Provide explicit derivation of triangular update rules and state invariants guaranteeing positive definiteness.  
- Clarity: Include a consolidated complexity table comparing standard CMA-ES and the Cholesky variant with amortization discussion.  
- Clarity: Expand related work to situate the method among low-rank, diagonal, and limited-memory CMA-ES variants and discuss complementarity.  
- Suggestions: Strengthen theoretical section with formal lemmas/results on Cholesky update/downdate stability.  
- Suggestions: Evaluate or discuss integration with low-rank/limited-memory approximations for very high dimensions.  
- Suggestions: Provide more benchmarks (ill-conditioned, very high-dim) and ablations on stability safeguards.  
- Suggestions: Release reference code and scripts to facilitate adoption and reproducibility.  
- Overall assessment: Solid, practical contribution that significantly reduces per-iteration overhead without apparent loss in effectiveness, but needs stronger treatment of numerical stability, complexity analysis, and high-dimensional extensions to maximize impact.
