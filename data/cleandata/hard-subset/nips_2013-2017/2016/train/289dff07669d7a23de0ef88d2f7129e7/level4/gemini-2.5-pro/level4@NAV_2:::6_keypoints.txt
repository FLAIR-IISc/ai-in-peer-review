- Proposes updating the triangular Cholesky factor directly (Cholesky-CMA-ES) to avoid full covariance decomposition, with a CSA approximation replacing the matrix-square-root inverse by the Cholesky inverse.  
- Theoretical argument claims the CSA substitution does not change long-run algorithm behavior.  
- Empirical results show significant wall-clock speed-ups, especially at higher dimensions, without increasing function evaluations to reach targets.  
- Practical impact: addresses a common runtime bottleneck when objective evaluations are cheap and dimensionality is moderate.  
- Efficiency: achieves asymptotic O(µ d²) time and O(d²) storage for full covariance adaptation.  
- Positioning/naming issue: "Cholesky-CMA-ES" was used by Suttorp et al. (2009); authors must clarify differences or rename.  
- Incremental novelty: main algorithmic change is extending Krause & Igel’s rank-one update to rank-µ via a loop; the precise novelty needs clearer articulation.  
- Missing baseline: fails to compare empirically against Suttorp et al. (2009), yet claims that method is numerically unstable without evidence.  
- Overstated optimality: claiming "optimal covariance update and storage complexity" is too broad—optimality only holds if a full d×d covariance must be kept.  
- Overstated scalability: suggesting this enables "large-scale optimization" is misleading since O(d²) remains prohibitive for true large-scale problems.  
- Clarity issue: Figure 1(b) (Cigar) needs explanation for differing plateau objective values between algorithms—may be a median-plot artifact.  
- Minor technical inaccuracy: statement that eigen-decomposition is typically performed every update is incorrect—modern implementations perform it periodically to save computation.  
- Verdict: useful, efficient implementation but contribution is more incremental than claimed; authors should resolve naming, better situate novelty relative to prior work, and include direct empirical comparison with Suttorp et al. (2009).
