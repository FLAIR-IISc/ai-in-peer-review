- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that uses the Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root to reduce covariance update runtime and memory.
- Theoretical justification: Provides a lemma and argument that replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor should not degrade algorithmic performance.
- Practical improvement: Empirical results show comparable function-evaluation performance to standard CMA-ES with significant wall-clock speedups, especially in higher dimensions.
- Complexity claim: Authors assert optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity for covariance updates.
- Clarity: Paper is well-structured with clear algorithm descriptions (Algorithm 1 and 2).
- Relevance: Addresses a major computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, useful for large-scale optimization.
- Naming issue: Using "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is potentially misleading because Suttorp et al. (2009) used the same name for a related method; prior work should be acknowledged and a new name considered.
- Incremental contribution: Core algorithmic change (iterating rank-one updates) is seen as relatively incremental and needs clearer positioning versus prior work.
- Related-work comparison: Lacks direct empirical comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) and insufficiently contrasts with Krause & Igel (2015); claims of instability for Suttorp et al. are unsubstantiated.
- Overstated optimality: Claim of “optimal” covariance update complexity is too strong and should be moderated to reflect context dependence.
- Empirical anomaly: Plateaus/differences observed in Figure 1b (Figure 3) between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES need further investigation and explanation.
- Arguments for acceptance: The method is theoretically sound, practically effective, and yields substantial runtime improvements in high dimensions.
- Arguments against acceptance: Contribution is incremental, naming is confusing, and comparative evaluation is incomplete.
- Recommended revisions: Rename algorithm or acknowledge prior naming, add empirical comparison to Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel, clarify the for-loop’s contribution, and tone down claims of optimality.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions contingent on addressing naming, comparisons, explanation of incremental parts, and moderating optimality claims.
