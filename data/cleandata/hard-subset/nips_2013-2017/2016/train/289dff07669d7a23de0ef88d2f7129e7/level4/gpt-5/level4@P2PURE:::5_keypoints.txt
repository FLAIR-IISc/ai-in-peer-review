- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance update cost from O(d^3) to O(mu d^2) (mu ≈ O(log d)).
- Main difference from standard CMA-ES: does not estimate the random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.
- Under restrictive assumptions (covariance sequence converges), provides asymptotic argument that E_t → I, making the two schemes equivalent.
- Empirically demonstrates validity and speed-up with runtime and convergence plots on selected test functions.
- Compares performance against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that defer full covariance updates.
- Finds substantial computational reduction without degrading optimization performance on synthetic benchmarks.
- Notes caveat that in practice total runtime is often dominated by the objective function and CMA-ES overhead is negligible, especially for low-dimensional problems.
- Recommends additional experiments: track E_t evolution by running full CMA-ES in parallel, report runtimes/performance of vanilla CMA-ES, run spectrum-varying quadratic simulations to study E_t error, and add a real-world task (e.g., real-time robotics policy-gradient).
