- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: an update scheme approximating CMA-ES covariance adaptation with complexity O(μ d^2) vs O(d^3).  
- Core idea: maintain and update a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix and its square root.  
- Key approximation: omit estimating the random orthogonal matrix E_t at each step.  
- Theoretical claim: under restrictive assumptions (covariance matrices converge to a target), E_t asymptotically converges to the identity, making the schemes equivalent.  
- Empirical evaluation: runtime and convergence plots on benchmarks show validity and speed-up versus two state-of-the-art delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Contribution: substantial acceleration of CMA-ES without loss of optimization performance on synthetic tests.  
- Practical caveat: in many real-world uses the black-box evaluation dominates runtime and problem dimensionality is often only a few tens, so the speed-up may have limited impact.  
- Suggested experiment 1: track the evolution/norms of E_t by running full CMA-ES and the Cholesky approximation in parallel to observe actual E_t fluctuations.  
- Suggested experiment 2: report actual runtimes and performance for CMA-ES without any delay strategies for completeness.  
- Suggested experiment 3: run simulations on quadratic functions with continuously varied spectra (e.g., Stich & Müller 2012) to study solution quality and E_t error versus spectral distribution.  
- Suggested experiment 4: include a real-world test (e.g., policy-gradient tasks in real-time robotics) where evaluation speed and modest parameter dimensions make algorithm runtime critical.
