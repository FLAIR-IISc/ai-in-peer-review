CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for (derivative-free) optimization. It combines different learning mechanisms for adapting the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution, equivalently viewed as adaptation of an underlying metric. This paper proposes a novel covariance matrix update variant based on triangular Cholesky factors, offering significant reductions in computational complexity and memory requirements compared to previous approaches. The method is carefully and thoroughly evaluated numerically, demonstrating that it maintains the performance of the standard CMA-ES in terms of function evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time, particularly in high dimensions. This is a very significant contribution to the field of optimization. I anticipate that this CMA-ES variant will become a default choice for medium- to large-scale problems due to its clear advantages: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and the readily available eigenvalues of the covariance matrix. The paper is well-written and the approach is rigorously evaluated. Its relevance to NIPS is strong, given CMA-ES’s connections to machine learning, information geometry, and applications in reinforcement learning and supervised learning.
