- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a novel CMA-ES variant that updates covariance via triangular Cholesky factors.
- Achieves reduced computational complexity and memory requirements compared to standard CMA-ES.
- Introduces a numerically stable quadratic-time covariance update scheme based on Cholesky factors.
- Maintains optimization performance (function evaluations to target) comparable to standard CMA-ES.
- Significantly reduces wall-clock time, especially in high-dimensional problems.
- Method is affine invariant and provides covariance eigenvalues without extra cost.
- Paper is well-written with a clear explanation of the algorithm and its advantages.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough and comparisons with other CMA-ES variants are convincing.
- Potential to become the default CMA-ES variant for medium- to large-scale optimization tasks.
- Minor concern: unclear whether different experimental implementations are CPU-comparable (language/optimizations).
- Minor concern: unexplained wall-clock time discrepancy for Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256.
- Overall recommendation: significant contribution with convincing results, pending clarification of the two minor issues.
