- Misleading name: "Cholesky-CMA-ES" was introduced by Suttorp et al. (2009) and the paper fails to acknowledge this prior work.  
- Algorithmic novelty is limited: the method is an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015), essentially replacing a single rankOneUpdate call with a loop over individuals.  
- The proposed change differs mainly from Plus strategies only by iterating over individuals, not a fundamentally new algorithmic idea.  
- Empirical plotting issue: Figure 2b shows a plateau discrepancy on the Cigar function likely caused by how median results are plotted.  
- Missing comparisons: the paper must empirically compare against Suttorp's Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically against Krause's variant.  
- Misleading optimality claim: "Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity" is incorrect for Sphere and separable functions where full covariance is unnecessary.  
- Overstated efficiency: the approach yields at best a two-fold computational reduction over Suttorp's method, not a fundamentally better complexity.  
- Incorrect claim about numerical instability: labeling Suttorp's method as prone to numerical instabilities contradicts Suttorp et al.'s original claims.  
- Omission in figures: excluding Suttorp's method from Figure 3 misleads readers about the true contribution and performance.  
- Large-scale optimization claim is inaccurate: complexity remains quadratic, so the method does not advance large-scale optimization asymptotically.  
- Core contribution needs clarification: authors should explicitly state the core algorithmic change is the loop in Algorithm 1 or justify why this is not the main contribution.  
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is not typically performed every update step; proper implementations perform it periodically.
