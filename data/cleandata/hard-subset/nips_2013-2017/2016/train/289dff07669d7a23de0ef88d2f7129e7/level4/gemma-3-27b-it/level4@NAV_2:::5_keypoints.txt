- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce covariance update complexity from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2) (µ typically O(log d)).  
- Core idea: avoid explicitly estimating orthogonal matrix Et; under assumptions Et → I, Cholesky-CMA-ES is asymptotically equivalent to original CMA-ES.  
- Empirical validation: runtime and convergence plots on benchmarks show speed-up and comparable optimization performance versus delayed-update CMA-ES variants.  
- Significant contribution: accelerates a leading black-box optimizer, especially beneficial for high-dimensional problems with cheap function evaluations.  
- Shows the method maintains optimization quality on synthetic test cases while reducing computational cost.  
- Limitation: when objective evaluation cost dominates (common in lower-dimensional or expensive simulations), the algorithmic speed-up has limited practical impact.  
- Best-case scenario: fast function evaluations and high dimensionality, a setting that may be less frequent in real applications.  
- Suggestion: visualize Et evolution (e.g., norms) during runs and compare to full CMA-ES to empirically support Et → I claim.  
- Suggestion: include performance results for the full (no-delay) CMA-ES to provide a fuller baseline comparison.  
- Suggestion: run quadratic-form simulations varying spectral distributions (à la Stich & Müller) to relate Et error and solution quality to spectra.  
- Suggestion: evaluate on a real-world, speed-sensitive application (e.g., robotics policy gradients) to demonstrate practical benefits.  
- Pros for acceptance: technically sound, original Cholesky update approach, potentially significant speed-up, and clear exposition.  
- Cons against acceptance: limited practical relevance in many settings, insufficient empirical breadth, and reliance on theoretical assumptions that may not always hold.
