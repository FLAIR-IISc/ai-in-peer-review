- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time and space complexity.  
- Theoretical contribution: proves replacing the square root in CSA preserves performance because the replacement error vanishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Key lemma: Lemma 1 shows the orthogonal matrix in the RQ-decomposition converges to the identity, supporting the theoretical claim.  
- Empirical results: matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations but achieves large wall-clock speedups (up to ~20× at d=64), with speedup increasing with dimension.  
- Implementation: detailed Algorithm 1 and efficient implementation in Shark using LAPACK.  
- Complexity: achieves optimal time and space complexity for covariance updates, addressing a major high-dimensional bottleneck.  
- Clarity: well-structured paper with precise algorithm descriptions and well-documented experiments.  
- Numerical stability: claim of being "numerically more stable" is asserted but not sufficiently explained; needs stronger discussion.  
- Originality: first theoretically justified Cholesky-based approach for the standard CMA-ES that maintains performance guarantees.  
- Significance: substantial practical impact for applications (e.g., direct policy search, hyperparameter tuning) where covariance updates dominate runtime.  
- Limitations: still scales quadratically with dimension, so applicability to extremely large d is limited without dimensionality reduction.  
- Integration gaps: paper does not fully address how the approach combines with other recent CMA-ES improvements.  
- Suggested revisions: expand the numerical stability discussion and add a brief treatment of combining Cholesky-CMA-ES with dimensionality-reduction methods for very high dimensions.
