

This paper proposes a Cholesky-CMA-ES variant that replaces the covariance matrix square root with a triangular Cholesky factor to reduce time and storage complexity in the standard CMA-ES. The approach maintains the algorithm's behavior while achieving optimal asymptotic complexity (O(μd²) time and O(d²) space for covariance updates) without increasing objective function evaluations. The authors provide theoretical justification for the error introduced in the step-size adaptation mechanism, showing it diminishes as the algorithm converges, and support this with empirical results demonstrating significant speedups (up to 20× for d=64) while matching the standard CMA-ES's convergence rate.

**Strengths:**  
The paper is exceptionally clear and well-structured. The theoretical justification for the Cholesky approximation (Lemma 1 and its implications) is a notable strength, addressing a critical gap in prior work. The experimental design is thorough, testing multiple benchmark functions across dimensions (d=4 to 256), including rotation-invariant cases and challenging non-separable problems. The results convincingly show that Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves identical optimization performance to the reference CMA-ES (CMA-ES-Ref) while substantially reducing runtime—particularly impactful for high-dimensional problems where cache effects become significant. The practical benefits (e.g., free eigenvalue extraction, numerically stable inversion) are clearly articulated.

**Weaknesses:**  
1. **Originality and Novelty:** While the paper builds on prior work (e.g., Krause & Igel, 2015), it overstates the novelty by implying this is a "new standard." The core update mechanism is a direct extension of their rank-1 Cholesky approach to the full CMA-ES, but the *theoretical justification for the step-size adaptation error* (Section 3) is the genuine contribution. This should be emphasized more prominently to distinguish it from the "trivial extension" critique. The paper must explicitly clarify how this justification differs from and improves upon prior methods.

2. **Complexity Analysis:** The claim that Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves O(μd²) complexity requires refinement. CMA-ES-Ref (with delayed updates) has an *average* complexity of O(d²/τ) where τ = Ω(d), but its *per-update* complexity remains O(d²). The Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves a *consistently* O(μd²) per-iteration complexity without delays, which is asymptotically optimal when adaptation is required. Using Θ(μd²) notation would strengthen this point. The paper should clarify that the speedup comes from eliminating the O(d³) matrix square root computation *in every iteration*, not just comparing asymptotic orders.

3. **Error Analysis Validation:** The claim that "the error decreases over time" (Section 3) is not empirically validated beyond final performance. Hansen’s CSA mechanism relies on the matrix square root for theoretical invariance, and the authors’ approximation introduces a rotation-dependent error (Equation 6). To substantiate this claim, the authors should include: (i) a plot of the error norm ‖pσ,t+1 − E[pσ,t+1]‖ over iterations for a representative run, and (ii) a comparison of the rotation matrix ETₜ₋₁Eₜ (as mentioned in supplementary material) to show convergence to identity. Final performance alone cannot confirm the error dynamics.

**Minor Issues:**  
- Notation errors: "wk" in Algorithm 2 should be "αₖ"; "n" in Table 1 should be "d".  
- Typo: "F" (line 185) should be "f".  
- The reference to "A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update" (Krause & Igel, 2015) is accurate but should explicitly state that their method was limited to μ=λ=1, whereas this work generalizes to the full CMA-ES (μ=λ/2).  

**Recommendation:**  
The paper presents a technically sound and practically valuable improvement to CMA-ES with strong empirical validation. However, it does not fully meet NIPS’s standards for originality due to insufficient emphasis on the *theoretical novelty* of the step-size adaptation justification (Section 3), and the complexity argument requires greater precision. The missing experimental validation of the error dynamics is a critical gap. If the authors address these points (particularly the error analysis and complexity nuance), the paper would be a strong contribution. As-is, it is borderline for acceptance but falls short of NIPS’s bar for significance and novelty. **Recommendation: Weak Reject.**
