- Paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update covariance via a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to reduce complexity from O(d^3) to O(µ d^2).  
- Core idea: maintain and update Cholesky matrix instead of full covariance and its square root, avoiding explicit orthogonal matrix E_t.  
- Theoretical claim: under strong convergence assumptions the sequence E_t converges to identity, yielding asymptotic equivalence to original CMA-ES.  
- Empirical validation: runtime and convergence plots on benchmarks show speed-up and comparable optimization performance to standard CMA-ES variants.  
- Comparison: evaluated against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants with delayed covariance updates.  
- Strength: significant computational speedup, especially in higher dimensions and when µ = O(log d).  
- Strength: provides theoretical justification (with assumptions) that approximation does not harm performance.  
- Strength: strong empirical results on synthetic benchmark functions.  
- Practical note: most real-world CMA-ES runtime is often dominated by expensive function evaluations, limiting practical impact of algorithmic speedup.  
- Practical note: typical applications have modest dimensionality (tens of variables), so speedup is most relevant when evaluations are cheap and dimension is large.  
- Suggested experiment: visualize evolution of E_t across trajectories (e.g., norms) by running full CMA-ES and approximation in parallel.  
- Suggested experiment: include runtime and performance comparisons to standard CMA-ES without delay strategies.  
- Suggested experiment: simulation study on quadratic functions with varying spectral distributions to measure solution quality and E_t error (cf. Stich & Müller PPSN 2012).  
- Suggested experiment: add a real-world test (e.g., policy gradients in real-time robotics) where speed matters and parameter dimensionality is moderate.  
- Weakness: theoretical results rely on strong assumptions about covariance convergence.  
- Weakness: limited real-world validation; experiments are primarily synthetic benchmarks.  
- Weakness: speedup may be negligible in settings where oracle runtime dominates optimization time.
