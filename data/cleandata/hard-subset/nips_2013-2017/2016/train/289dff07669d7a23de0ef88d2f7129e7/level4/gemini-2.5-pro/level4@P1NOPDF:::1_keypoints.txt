- CMA-ES is a state-of-the-art derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution, equivalent to adapting an underlying metric.  
- The paper proposes a new covariance matrix update using triangular Cholesky factors.  
- The Cholesky-based update reduces computational complexity of the update step.  
- The method also lowers memory required to store algorithm state parameters.  
- The approach yields eigenvalues of the covariance matrix at no additional cost.  
- Numerical experiments show no degradation in performance (function evaluations to target) versus standard CMA-ES.  
- Experiments demonstrate significant wall-clock time reductions, especially in high-dimensional problems.  
- The variant is a significant contribution and could become the default for medium- to large-scale problems.  
- The paper is well-written, thoroughly evaluated, and relevant to ML and information geometry applications.  
- Minor point: clarify whether CPU time comparisons were fair and implementations used the same language.  
- Minor point: explain why the dimensionality trend is not observed for Cigar and Discus functions at dimension 256.
