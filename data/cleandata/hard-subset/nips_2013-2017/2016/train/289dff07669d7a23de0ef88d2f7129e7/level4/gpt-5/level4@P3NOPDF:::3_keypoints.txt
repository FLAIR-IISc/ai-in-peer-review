- Studies a Cholesky-based CMA-ES variant that extends rank-1 updates to rank-μ via a sequence of rank-1 updates and analyzes a modified CSA under a covariance-convergence assumption, with benchmark experiments reported.
- Topic relevance: Cholesky-based updates are attractive for lowering CMA-ES computational cost.
- Extension value: Incorporating rank-μ information within the Cholesky framework is a natural and practically useful idea.
- Theoretical claim: The modified CSA is argued not to materially change behavior assuming the covariance matrix converges.
- Presentation: The paper is generally clear and appears implementable.
- Novelty concern: Contribution is incremental relative to Krause et al. (2015); rank-μ via repeated rank-1 updates offers limited conceptual novelty.
- Theoretical limitation: Results rely on a strong covariance-convergence assumption and do not provide new convergence guarantees for CMA-ES.
- Empirical weakness: Experiments do not convincingly demonstrate substantial performance, scalability, robustness, or runtime gains over prior Cholesky CMA-ES methods.
- Theory note: The convergence assumption is plausible but not established for the exact variant studied; citing related results (e.g., Beyer 2014) helps but the mismatch must be acknowledged.
- Theory suggestion: Precisely state how the assumption aligns with or departs from known results and delimit conditions for the lemma’s validity in practice.
- Experimental request: Provide detailed benchmark set, evaluation protocol, computational budget, and comparisons at minimum to Krause et al. (2015) and full-covariance CMA-ES.
- Ablation request: Include ablations isolating (i) rank-1 vs rank-μ in the Cholesky framework and (ii) standard CSA vs modified CSA.
- Metrics request: Report solution quality and computational cost (per-iteration time, memory, wall-clock to target) and include statistical significance and run variability.
- Clarity request: Explicitly discuss relationship to prior Cholesky-CMA-ES work and clarify what is fundamentally new beyond repeated rank-1 updates.
- Complexity & stability: Include a concise complexity analysis and discuss numerical stability issues (round-off accumulation, reorthogonalization, jitter, periodic recomputation).
- Reproducibility: Add a high-level algorithmic pseudocode block.
- Minor issues: Fix Equation (1) denominator to σ_t (and footnote), remove extraneous “by” on line 94, and proofread for other minor inconsistencies.
- Overall assessment: Practical and intuitively useful extension but limited novelty and conditional theory; empirical evidence needs strengthening to demonstrate clear advantages.
- Recommendations: Better delineate unique contributions, strengthen empirical ablations and runtime analyses, and refine presentation and technical correctness.
