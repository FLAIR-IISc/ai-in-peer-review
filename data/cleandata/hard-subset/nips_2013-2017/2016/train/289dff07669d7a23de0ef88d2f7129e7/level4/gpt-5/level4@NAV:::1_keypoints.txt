- CMA-ES is the state-of-the-art stochastic derivative-free optimizer that adapts a multivariate normal sampling distribution (i.e., an underlying metric).  
- The paper proposes a new covariance-matrix update using triangular Cholesky factors that reduces update time and memory storage.  
- The proposed Cholesky-based approach is carefully evaluated via numerical experiments.  
- Results show preserved performance in function-evaluation count while substantially reducing wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- The contribution is highly significant and could become the default for medium/large-scale problems.  
- Key advantages: reduced memory footprint, lower computational complexity, and near-free access to covariance eigenvalues.  
- The paper is well written and the methodology is rigorously evaluated.  
- The work is appropriate for NIPS given CMA-ES’s relevance to machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.  
- Minor methodological query: clarify how implementations were made comparable for CPU performance (same programming language, which one, etc.).  
- Minor empirical query: explain why Figure 2’s claim about stronger differences with higher dimensionality does not hold for Cigar and Discus at dimension 256.
