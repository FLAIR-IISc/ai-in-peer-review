

CMA-ES represents the state-of-the-art stochastic algorithm for derivative-free optimization. It adapts the parameters of a multivariate normal sampling distribution through various learning mechanisms, equivalent to metric adaptation. This paper introduces a novel covariance matrix update using triangular Cholesky factors, reducing both computational complexity and memory requirements compared to prior methods. The proposed variant is rigorously evaluated numerically. Results demonstrate that the new update maintains the standard CMA-ES performance in terms of function evaluations required to reach a target, while substantially reducing wall-clock time—particularly in high-dimensional settings. This paper presents a significant contribution to optimization. The proposed variant is likely to become the default for medium-to-large-scale problems due to its clear advantages: reduced memory usage, lower computational complexity, and automatic acquisition of the covariance matrix's eigenvalues. The paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated. Its relevance to NIPS is notable, given CMA-ES's strong ties to machine learning, information geometry, reinforcement learning, and supervised learning.
