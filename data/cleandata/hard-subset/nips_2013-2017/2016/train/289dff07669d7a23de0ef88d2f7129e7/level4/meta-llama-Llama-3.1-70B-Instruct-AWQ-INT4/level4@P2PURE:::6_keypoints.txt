- Claim of a novel "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is misleading because Suttorp et al. (2009) introduced a similar Cholesky-based CMA-ES and is not acknowledged.  
- The method is an incremental extension of Krause et al. (2015); the only algorithmic novelty is repeatedly calling rankOneUpdate in a loop (a simple for-loop).  
- Empirical results show a discrepancy on the Cigar function between CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES that may be due to plotting medians.  
- The paper should empirically compare to Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and algorithmically to Krause’s variant; omission of Suttorp’s method (e.g., in Fig. 3) is misleading.  
- The “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” claim is misleading: not optimal for Sphere or separable functions, and only ~2× more efficient than Suttorp’s method.  
- The authors’ claim that Suttorp’s method is numerically unstable contradicts the original paper’s findings and should be substantiated.  
- Impact on large-scale optimization is questionable since asymptotic complexity remains quadratic, though the method may be a practical improvement for full covariance use.  
- Overall contribution is too incremental; paper needs (a) naming fix, (b) experiments with Suttorp and Krause variants and updated figures, and (c) clearer explanation or justification why the change is more than a simple for-loop.  
- Minor correction: eigenvalue decomposition is typically done periodically in implementations, not at every update.
