- Paper proposes a CMA-ES variant that updates the covariance via triangular Cholesky factors.  
- New update reduces computational complexity and memory requirements.  
- Eigenvalues of the covariance matrix can be obtained at no extra cost.  
- Numerical experiments show no loss in function-evaluation performance compared to standard CMA-ES.  
- New method substantially reduces wall-clock time, especially in high dimensions.  
- Contribution is significant for medium to large-scale optimization and may become the default.  
- Paper is well-written and thoroughly evaluated.  
- Work is relevant to machine learning, information geometry, and reinforcement learning.  
- Reviewer requests clarification that experimental implementations were CPU-comparable (same language, optimizations).  
- Reviewer asks for explanation why Fig. 2’s dimensionality trend fails for Cigar and Discus at 256D.
