- The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES mainly applies repeated rank-one updates for all individuals, directly building on Krause et al. (2015) and thus feels incremental.  
- The “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” claim is misleading: full covariance updates are unnecessary (and not optimal) for problems where covariance structure is irrelevant.  
- The reported two-fold speedup over Suttorp et al. (2009) is marginal and not clearly significant given prior work.  
- Reusing the name “Cholesky-CMA-ES” conflicts with Suttorp et al. (2009) and risks confusion; the paper treats this prior name only superficially.  
- Suttorp et al. (2009) is omitted from empirical comparisons (e.g., Fig. 3), misrepresenting novelty and comparative performance.  
- The authors’ claim that the Suttorp version is numerically unstable contradicts the original publication and is inadequately justified.  
- Empirical evidence is unconvincing: the Cigar-function result (Fig. 1b) shows a puzzling plateau difference that may reflect plotting/median issues or subtle implementation bugs.  
- The approach does not address the inherent quadratic complexity of full covariance updates; scalability to very high dimensions remains unconvincing.  
- The main algorithmic change reduces to a single “for” loop; the paper must better justify why this is a substantive contribution rather than an implementation detail.  
- Revision request a): rename the algorithm to avoid conflict with prior “Cholesky-CMA-ES.”  
- Revision request b): include thorough empirical comparisons with Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause et al. (2015), updating figures and/or moving full comparisons to supplementary material.  
- Revision request c): clearly articulate and justify the significance of the repeated rank-one update and highlight Algorithm 1 as the core modification.  
- Minor comment: the statement that eigenvalue decomposition is performed at every update is inaccurate—well-optimized CMA-ES implementations perform it periodically.
