- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: a novel update scheme approximating CMA-ES by updating a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix.  
- Reduces complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2), with μ typically O(log d).  
- Main methodological difference is estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et at each step.  
- Under restrictive convergence assumptions, authors argue Et converges to identity, making the schemes asymptotically equivalent.  
- Empirical validation uses standard runtime and convergence plots on selected synthetic objective functions.  
- Compares performance to two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.  
- Claims a significant speed-up without degrading optimization performance on tested synthetic cases.  
- Notes practical caveat: black-box objective evaluation often dominates runtime, so CMA-ES speedups may be irrelevant in many applications.  
- Points out typical CMA-ES problems have modest dimensionality (few tens), limiting the method’s practical impact except when evaluations are very cheap and d is large.  
- Recommends visualizing Et evolution (e.g., in different norms) and comparing approximated Et to that computed alongside full CMA-ES.  
- Suggests reporting actual runtimes and performance of vanilla CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.  
- Proposes additional experiments: simulation study on quadratics with varied spectra (e.g., Stich & Müller 2012) and a real-world test such as policy-gradient tuning in real-time robotics.
