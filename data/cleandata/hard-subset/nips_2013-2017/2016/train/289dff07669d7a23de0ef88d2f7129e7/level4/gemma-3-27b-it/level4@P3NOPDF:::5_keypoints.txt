- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to approximate covariance updates, reducing complexity.  
- Reduces update complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2), with μ typically O(log d).  
- Key approximation: omits estimating a random orthogonal matrix E_t in each iteration.  
- Provides an asymptotic argument (under convergence assumptions) that E_t → I, implying equivalence to full CMA-ES in the limit.  
- Empirical results show convincing speed-ups and comparable optimization performance on standard benchmark functions.  
- Compares favorably against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES implementations that use covariance update delay.  
- Notes practical limitation: objective-function evaluation time often dominates, and typical CMA-ES problems have modest dimensionality, so benefits are most relevant when evaluations are very fast and dimensions are high.  
- Requests stronger empirical/theoretical grounding despite good results, acknowledging difficulty of rigorous guarantees for CMA-ES.  
- Experiment suggestion 1: track evolution of E_t by running full CMA-ES and Cholesky-CMA-ES in parallel and compare E_t norms over trajectories.  
- Experiment suggestion 2: include baseline results for standard CMA-ES without delay strategies for clear comparison.  
- Experiment suggestion 3: perform spectral analysis on quadratic functions with varied spectra to study solution quality and E_t error versus conditioning.  
- Experiment suggestion 4: add a real-world, speed-critical application (e.g., policy-gradient real-time robotics) to demonstrate practical impact.  
- Overall assessment: well-written, significant contribution that meaningfully accelerates CMA-ES and merits the suggested additional experiments.
