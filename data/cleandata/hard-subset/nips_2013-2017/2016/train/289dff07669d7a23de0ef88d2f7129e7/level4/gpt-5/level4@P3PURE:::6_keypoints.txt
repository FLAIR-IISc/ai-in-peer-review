- Naming conflict: The name "Cholesky-CMA-ES" is already used by Suttorp et al. (2009) and the paper fails to acknowledge or resolve this.
- Algorithmic novelty: The method is an incremental modification of Krause et al. (2015); the main change appears to be calling rankOneUpdate in a loop over all individuals rather than once.
- Need for clarity: The paper must explicitly state that the primary algorithmic difference is the loop over rankOneUpdate or demonstrate additional contributions beyond this change.
- Empirical concern (Cigar): The plateau difference on the Cigar function between CMA-ES-Ref and the proposed method needs explanation (might be median plotting) and must be addressed.
- Missing baselines: The experiments omit empirical comparisons to Suttorp et al.’s Cholesky-CMA-ES and Krause et al.’s variant, notably excluding Suttorp from Figure 3, which risks overstating the contribution.
- Misleading optimality claim: Claiming “Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity” is misleading because on Sphere/separable functions full covariance isn’t needed and the claim doesn’t hold.
- Efficiency claim qualified: Even for full covariance, the proposed method is only roughly twice as efficient as Suttorp’s Cholesky-CMA-ES; fair comparisons and reconciliation of numerical-stability claims are required.
- Scalability limitation: The approach does not change the quadratic complexity of full-covariance CMA-ES, so it doesn’t alter the large-scale optimization landscape.
- Practical value: Within the design choice of maintaining a full covariance, the modification may be a practical way to reduce runtime and memory, but this is limited scope.
- Contribution assessment: Overall the change is too incremental (essentially adding a for-loop of rankOneUpdate) to justify publication without stronger justification or additional results.
- Recommendations: Resolve the naming issue, include Suttorp/Krause variants in experiments (update figures or provide supplements), and clearly articulate the primary algorithmic modification or further contributions.
- Minor factual correction: Eigendecomposition is typically performed periodically in implementations, not at every update step.
