This paper explores the use of Cholesky decomposition in place of eigen-decomposition to reduce the computational time and storage requirements of CMA-ES. The paper is generally well-written, and the proposed method appears sound. The experimental results, particularly the reported speedup rates, are encouraging and suggest this could become a standard implementation of CMA-ES. However, the originality of this work is questionable, as it appears to be a relatively straightforward extension of “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies,” with highly similar methodologies. I would appreciate clarification on this point. Furthermore, several key elements are surprisingly absent. First, the paper should demonstrate the equivalence of the proposed algorithm to formula (2); while space is limited and this has been established in prior work, it remains a crucial verification for this manuscript. Second, the claim of O(μd²) computational complexity is identical to that of CMA-ES-Ref. A detailed discussion, perhaps employing Θ-notation, is needed to justify the observed speed improvements. Third, the claim that path length error diminishes over time contradicts Hansen’s assertion; given the non-deterministic nature of the theoretical analysis, experiments specifically designed to validate this claim – rather than simply comparing final performance – are required. Finally, several minor issues require attention: 1) Please carefully review the notation throughout the paper. Specifically, *w<sub>k</sub>* in Algorithm 2 should be *α<sub>k</sub>*, and *n* in Table 1 should be *d*. 2) Line 185 contains a typographical error: ‘F’ should be ‘f’. 

In its current state, I do not believe this paper meets the standards for acceptance at NIPS.
