1. Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with triangular Cholesky factorization to reduce inverse-computation cost.  
2. Provides theoretical justification that the modification does not degrade CMA-ES performance.  
3. Achieves improved asymptotic time complexity O(µd^2) for covariance updates and optimal memory complexity.  
4. Requires the same number of objective evaluations as standard CMA-ES but yields significantly lower wall-clock time, with speed-up increasing with dimensionality.  
5. Experimental results show substantial optimization speed improvements over state-of-the-art CMA-ES with delayed decomposition.  
6. Paper is well-organized and clearly written, making methods and results easy to follow.  
7. Claim of numerically more stable computation lacks a detailed theoretical/numerical analysis.  
8. Suggests a promising extension: combine Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-dimensional approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) for very high-dimensional problems.  
9. Strengths: novel modification, solid theoretical support, and strong empirical speedups.  
10. Weaknesses: insufficient depth on numerical stability analysis and limited exploration of low-dimensional integration.  
11. Originality: presents a novel application of triangular Cholesky factorization within CMA-ES.  
12. Significance: improved complexity and practical speedups make the method a valuable contribution to optimization.
