- Proposes replacing the covariance matrix square root in CMA-ES with a triangular Cholesky factor (Cholesky-CMA-ES).
- Provides theoretical justification that this change does not significantly alter behavior under the assumption of converging covariance.
- Updates the triangular Cholesky factor using both rank-1 and rank-µ updates, extending Krause & Igel (2015).
- Contribution may be viewed as incremental since it builds on prior work, but the extension is natural.
- Delivers a thorough analysis and experimental evaluation supporting improved performance of Cholesky-CMA-ES.
- Suggests strengthening discussion of covariance convergence (Ct) and its relation to Lemma 1, referencing Beyer (ECJ, 2014).
- Minor errors to fix: σ should be σt in equation (1) and the footnote; remove the extra "by" in line 94.
- Strengths: rigorous analysis, clear theoretical motivation, and convincing experimental results.
- Weaknesses: incremental novelty, insufficient elaboration on convergence properties, and minor typographical issues.
