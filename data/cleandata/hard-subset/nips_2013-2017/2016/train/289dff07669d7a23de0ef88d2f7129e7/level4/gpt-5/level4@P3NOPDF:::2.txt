Summary
The paper proposes replacing the eigen-decomposition in CMA-ES with Cholesky-based updates to reduce time and memory costs. The manuscript is clearly written, the method is sensible, and the reported speedups are promising. If validated and positioned appropriately relative to prior work, this approach could influence practical CMA-ES implementations.

Originality and relation to prior work
My main concern is limited novelty. The method appears to be a straightforward extension of “A More Efficient Rank-one Covariance Matrix Update for Evolution Strategies.” From the current presentation, the algorithms seem essentially identical. Please clarify, in a self-contained way, what is genuinely new:
- Algorithmic differences beyond notational or implementation details.
- New theoretical results not present in the prior work.
- New empirical insights (e.g., regimes, dimensions, or update schemes where your approach behaves differently).

Missing or insufficient elements
1) Equivalence to formula (2). Even if proven previously, the paper should include either a concise proof sketch, a direct equivalence argument, or an appendix/extended material that makes the result accessible within this work. Without this, the presentation feels incomplete.

2) Complexity analysis. You claim O(mu d^2) time, which matches the CMA-ES reference. To justify speedups, please provide a precise analysis:
- Use Theta notation and count dominant operations to expose constants.
- Explain where savings come from (e.g., avoiding periodic eigen-decompositions, improved cache locality, BLAS2/BLAS3 usage, fewer matrix-vector operations).
- Detail amortized per-iteration costs, including any deferred updates.
- Quantify storage complexity improvements and their practical effect on runtime.

3) Evolution-path error claim. You state that the path error is small and decreases over time, which contradicts prior claims (e.g., Hansen). Since your theoretical analysis is non-deterministic, empirical validation is essential:
- Track the evolution-path error over iterations across multiple benchmarks and dimensions, not just final performance.
- Report statistics (means, variances, confidence intervals) over multiple runs.
- Include ablations isolating the impact of step-size adaptation and learning rates.

Experimental evaluation
- Provide thorough timing methodology: hardware, compiler settings, BLAS usage, threading, precision, and how random seeds are handled.
- Break down per-iteration time into major components (sampling, recombination, Cholesky updates, rank-one/rank-mu updates, step-size adaptation).
- Evaluate across dimensions and population sizes mu; report memory consumption and stability (e.g., numerical issues, breakdowns).
- Compare against well-optimized CMA-ES reference implementations and any recent Cholesky-based variants, controlling for implementation quality.

Clarity and presentation
- The paper is generally clear, but some essential content is missing (as above). Including a self-contained theorem/lemma set for the Cholesky updates would improve rigor.
- Add a concise pseudocode block for the full algorithm, highlighting differences from the reference CMA-ES.

Minor comments
- Notation: in Algorithm 2, wk should be alpha_k.
- In Table 1, n should be d.
- Line 185: F should be f.
- Please proofread for notational consistency and define symbols at first use.

Strengths
- Clear writing and motivation.
- Practical and plausible method with compelling speedups.
- Potential to become a widely used engineering improvement if the contribution over prior work is clearly established.

Weaknesses
- Apparent overlap with prior work; limited originality as currently presented.
- Missing equivalence argument and detailed complexity justification.
- Claims about path error are not adequately validated by experiments beyond final performance.

Recommendation
At present, the paper is not ready for NIPS. I recommend rejection in its current form. A revised version that (1) clearly delineates novelty over prior work, (2) provides a self-contained equivalence argument, (3) offers a precise complexity and constant-factor analysis, and (4) empirically validates the path-error claim with per-iteration diagnostics would be much stronger. If these issues are addressed, the work could have meaningful practical impact on CMA-ES implementations.
