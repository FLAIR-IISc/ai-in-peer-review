- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) directly, avoiding explicit covariance recomputation.
- Implements rank-one/rank-µ updates on A in O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) memory, materializing A^{-1} in O(d^2) when needed.
- Replaces CSA’s C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}; Lemma 1 argues rotation mismatch vanishes as covariance shape stabilizes.
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while achieving large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20x at d=64).
- Builds on prior Cholesky/factor-update work (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) and complements delayed-decomposition and structured CMA variants.
- Strength: strong practical impact—substantial runtime reductions without more evaluations.
- Strength: clean, simple algorithmic idea with asymptotically optimal storage for full covariance.
- Strength: theoretical rationale (Lemma 1) linking CSA approximation to vanishing rotation error.
- Strength: experiments include random rotations and relevant baselines, preserving rotational invariance in practice.
- Strength: memory-efficient by storing only triangular factor (optimal O(d^2)).
- Weakness: numerical stability claim is asserted but lacks theoretical or empirical justification for triangular-factor updates under finite precision.
- Weakness: no finite-time bound or quantified measure of the CSA approximation error from replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}.
- Weakness: need clarification whether the substitution alters theoretical invariance properties and more empirical checks on rotation invariance.
- Weakness: performance drops once matrices exceed cache (around d≈256); no detailed analysis of memory layout, BLAS usage, or cache-mitigation strategies.
- Weakness: comparisons omit limited-memory/low-rank CMA variants (e.g., LM-CMA), which are important for very high-dimensional settings.
- Weakness: reproducibility details missing—software release status, exact hyperparameters (µ, λ, weights), stopping criteria, hardware/BLAS specifics.
- Quality: algorithmic derivation is sound and experiments are thorough on classical rotated benchmarks, but finite-time analysis and broader test cases would improve rigor.
- Clarity: paper is generally well written but should better explain numerical stability, cost of Algorithm 2, when A^{-1} is materialized, and expected CSA rotation error decay.
- Originality: meaningful and original extension of triangular-factor updates to standard CMA-ES with a justified CSA modification.
- Significance: practically significant—offers a drop-in full-covariance alternative with similar evaluation efficiency but much lower runtime.
- Suggested extensions: formal/empirical stability analysis of Algorithm 2 and error growth over many updates.
- Suggested extensions: quantify CSA approximation error empirically and consider lightweight corrections.
- Suggested extensions: explore hybrids with low-rank/limited-memory models and use Cholesky updates for the low-rank part.
- Suggested extensions: investigate blocked/batched updates and memory layouts to improve cache utilization for d ≥ 256.
- Acceptance arguments: strong practical speedups, solid motivation for CSA change, clean general technique with optimal asymptotics.
- Rejection arguments: limited finite-time guarantees on CSA error and stability, and missing comparisons to leading limited-memory methods and large-scale scalability.
