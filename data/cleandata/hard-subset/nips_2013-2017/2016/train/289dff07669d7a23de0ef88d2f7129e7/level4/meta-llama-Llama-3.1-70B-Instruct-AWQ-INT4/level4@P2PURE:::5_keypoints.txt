- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES: an update scheme reducing covariance adaptation complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).
- Core idea: maintain and update a lower-triangular Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance matrix and its square root.
- Main algorithmic change: omission of estimating a random orthogonal matrix Et at each step.
- Theoretical claim: under restrictive convergence assumptions, Et asymptotically converges to identity, making the schemes equivalent.
- Empirical validation: shows general speed-up via runtime and convergence plots on standard test functions.
- Comparative experiments: benchmarks against two state-of-the-art CMA-ES variants that delay full covariance updates.
- Contribution significance: substantially speeds up CMA-ES without degrading optimization performance on synthetic tests.
- Practical caveat: in many real applications the black-box evaluation dominates runtime, so CMA-ES overhead is often negligible.
- Applicability concern: typical CMA-ES problems have modest dimensionality (tens of variables), so the targeted high-dimension, low-cost-evaluation scenario may be uncommon.
- Need for more empirical evidence due to theoretical difficulty of CMA-ES analysis.
- Suggested experiment 1: visualize Et evolution on selected trajectories and compare the approximation against full CMA-ES.
- Suggested experiment 2: report actual runtimes and performance of standard CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.
- Suggested experiment 3: perform a quadratic-function study varying spectral distributions (e.g., Stich & Müller 2012) to assess solution quality and Et error.
- Suggested experiment 4: include a real-world, speed-critical test (e.g., policy gradients in real-time robotics) with moderate parameter dimensionality.
