- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor to approximate CMA-ES covariance, reducing complexity from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2).
- Maintains and updates Cholesky factor instead of full covariance matrix or its square root.
- Positions method relative to prior CMA-ES work and runtime-reduction approaches (delayed updates, restricted models).
- Provides clear, concise algorithm description including Cholesky update rule and modified cumulative step-size adaptation.
- Theoretical justification rests on an assumption that the covariance sequence converges to a specific shape.
- Lemma shows convergence of rotation matrix Et under that assumption, but the assumption may not hold in practice.
- Empirical results are used to validate the approximation where theory is restrictive.
- Experimental evaluation is thorough, comparing against two state-of-the-art delayed-update CMA-ES variants.
- Results demonstrate significant speed-up while preserving similar optimization performance.
- Strengths: clear presentation, solid technical approach, thorough experiments, and demonstrated speed-up.
- Weaknesses: reliance on a restrictive theoretical assumption and limited testing on real-world problems.
- Paper rates high on quality, clarity, and originality, but somewhat lower on significance due to niche applicability.
- Arguments for acceptance: novel, technically sound method with strong empirical support and clear writing.
- Arguments against acceptance: restrictive theoretical assumptions, synthetic-only evaluation, and limited practical significance.
- Suggested experiment: vary the spectrum of quadratic forms to study solution quality and Et error as a function of spectral distribution.
- Suggested experiment: include a real-world test (e.g., policy gradients in real-time robotics) to complement simulations.
- Suggested analysis: plot evolution of Et along selected trajectories (in different norms) to show actual fluctuations.
- Suggested comparison: include runtimes and performance of standard CMA-ES without delay strategies for completeness.
