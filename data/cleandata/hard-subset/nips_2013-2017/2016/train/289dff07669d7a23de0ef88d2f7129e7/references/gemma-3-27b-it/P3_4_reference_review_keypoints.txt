- Overall assessment: Valuable, technically sound improvement to CMA-ES; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Problem addressed: High computational cost of covariance matrix square root in CMA-ES (O(d^2)).  
- Main contribution: Replace matrix square root with Cholesky factorization and provide theoretical justification for the substitution.  
- Theoretical support: Lemma 1 and error analysis argue convergence and acceptable approximation error from omitting the correction factor.  
- Complexity and memory: Reduces runtime complexity to O(µ d^2) and lowers memory footprint; enables faster inverse computation.  
- Empirical validation: Extensive benchmark experiments (Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) show runtime speedups without harming function-evaluation performance.  
- Practical benefits: Better scalability to higher dimensions and reduced storage requirements.  
- Writing and presentation: Paper is well-written, organized, and clear.  
- Weakness — comparisons: Limited comparison to other recent high-dimensional CMA-ES variants (e.g., low-rank methods).  
- Weakness — error discussion: Needs more practical analysis of the magnitude and effect of the omitted correction-term error on convergence speed.  
- Weakness — cache effects: Cache size and memory-access pattern impacts (notably at d=256) require deeper analysis.  
- Weakness — rotation invariance: Rotation invariance results are in the supplement and not fully integrated or discussed for practical implications.  
- For acceptance: Significant runtime reduction, strong theoretical justification, and comprehensive experiments addressing a practical bottleneck.  
- Against acceptance: Insufficient comparisons to broader state-of-the-art and limited practical error analysis.  
- Suggested improvements: Expand experimental comparisons to recent high-dim variants and include sensitivity/error analyses.  
- Suggested improvements: Clarify cache/memory effects and integrate rotation invariance findings into the main text.
