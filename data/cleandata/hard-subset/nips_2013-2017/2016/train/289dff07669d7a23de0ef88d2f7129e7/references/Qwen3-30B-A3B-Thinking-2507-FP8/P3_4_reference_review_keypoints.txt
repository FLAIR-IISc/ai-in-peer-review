- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace expensive matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce complexity.
- Claims optimal asymptotic runtime/storage O(µd²) while maintaining comparable optimization performance.
- Theoretical justification via Lemma 1: approximation error (A⁻¹ₜ for C⁻¹/²ₜ) vanishes as algorithm converges.
- Empirical results: significant speedups (up to 20× at d=64) with no increase in function evaluations across benchmarks d=4..256.
- Strength: addresses a well-known computational bottleneck in CMA-ES and situates work within prior literature.
- Strength: Lemma 1 is a novel, well-motivated theoretical insight into the approximation’s behavior.
- Strength: Thorough empirical evaluation on multiple benchmark functions and dimensions.
- Strength: Clear, reproducible implementation details and practical considerations (uses LAPACK, notes cache effects).
- Additional benefit: Cholesky yields eigenvalues and enables efficient matrix inversion as by-products.
- Weakness: Theoretical justification is incomplete—Ct need not converge in randomized CMA-ES and no error bounds are provided.
- Weakness: No quantified error bounds for step-size adaptation (equation 5), so theoretical claim of unchanged evaluations is empirical only.
- Weakness: Optimality claim is overstated—no lower-bound proof and O(µd²) is only optimal if µ = O(d), conflicting with typical µ = O(log d).
- Weakness: Empirical evaluation limited to standard benchmarks; lacks real-world applications (e.g., deep learning hyperparameter tuning).
- Weakness: Does not compare against recent large-scale CMA-ES variants like LM-CMA (Loshchilov, 2015).
- Weakness: Memory-hierarchy effects (L2 cache overflow at d=256) may reduce speedup at very large dimensions and are insufficiently explored.
- Related work: Correctly cites Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015); novelty is triangular Cholesky applied to standard CMA-ES with theoretical reasoning.
- Related work gap: Needs stronger contrast with LM-CMA and other modern approaches to complexity reduction.
- Significance: Meaningful practical contribution with large speedups, but unclear translation to real-world ML workloads.
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions to clarify theoretical limitations, qualify optimality claims, and expand empirical comparisons.
- Pro-accept points: solves known bottleneck, provides theoretical insight, shows strong empirical speedups, and offers reproducible implementation.
- Con-accept points: lacks error bounds, overstated complexity claims, limited empirical scope, and lacks comparison to recent variants.
- Overall: Solid technical contribution worth acceptance pending minor revisions to strengthen theory and broaden empirical validation.
