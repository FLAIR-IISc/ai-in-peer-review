## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a modification to the standard Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) termed "Cholesky-CMA-ES." The core idea is to replace the computationally expensive matrix square root computation with a Cholesky factorization update, aiming to reduce both runtime and memory complexity, especially in high-dimensional optimization problems. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the approach, arguing that it doesn't deteriorate performance due to the convergence properties of the algorithm, and demonstrate empirical improvements in runtime compared to standard and alternative CMA-ES implementations.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a significant bottleneck:** The computational cost of covariance matrix adaptation is a well-known limitation of CMA-ES, particularly as dimensionality increases. This work directly tackles this issue.
* **Theoretical justification:** The authors provide a thoughtful analysis of the impact of replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor, and offer a convincing argument for why the introduced error should be minimal during convergence. The inclusion of Lemma 1 is a strong point.
* **Empirical validation:**  The experimental results, while perhaps not showing a *change* in the number of function evaluations, clearly demonstrate a substantial speedup in wall-clock time, particularly in higher dimensions. The comparison against other CMA-ES variants is well-structured.
* **Clear writing & organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The motivation is clear, and the technical details are presented in a reasonably accessible manner.
* **Practical relevance:** The proposed method has the potential to make CMA-ES more applicable to large-scale optimization problems, such as those encountered in machine learning (e.g., hyperparameter tuning, reinforcement learning).

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited novelty in the core idea:**  Updating the Cholesky factor instead of the covariance matrix *itself* has been explored before (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015). The main contribution here is extending this to the standard CMA-ES and providing a theoretical justification, *and* demonstrating the performance gains with comprehensive empirical evaluation. While valuable, the core algorithmic innovation isn't entirely novel.
* **Approximation & potential impact:** While the authors provide a theoretical argument for why the approximation introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor is acceptable, the practical consequences of this approximation are not fully explored.  A more in-depth analysis of the error introduced and its effect on convergence rate in specific scenarios would be beneficial.  The reliance on Lemma 1 feels a little hand-wavy; the assumption of Ct converging isn't always guaranteed in practice.
* **Experimental setup:** The experimental setup, while comprehensive, lacks detail regarding the specific hardware used. This makes it harder to fully appreciate the magnitude of the performance gains.  More detail on the benchmarks and parameter tuning choices would also be helpful.
* **Discussion of limitations:** The authors acknowledge the quadratic scaling with dimensionality, and the potential issues with very high dimensions exceeding L2 cache. However, a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the Cholesky-CMA-ES – especially situations where it might *not* outperform the standard CMA-ES – would strengthen the paper.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound and claims are generally well-supported by the provided analysis and experimental results. It's a complete piece of work, although further investigation of the approximation’s impact would strengthen it.
* **Clarity:**  The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
* **Originality:** While building on existing work, the paper presents a valuable extension and provides a novel theoretical justification and empirical demonstration of its effectiveness. It’s a solid contribution, even if it’s not a revolutionary breakthrough.
* **Significance:** The potential to speed up CMA-ES, a widely used and effective optimization algorithm, is significant. Practitioners and researchers working with high-dimensional problems are likely to benefit from this work.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a practical bottleneck in a widely-used algorithm.
* Provides a theoretical justification for the proposed modification.
* Demonstrates clear empirical speedups.
* Well-written and clearly presented.
* Potentially significant impact on the field.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The core idea isn't entirely novel.
* The impact of the approximation is not fully explored.
* Limited discussion of the algorithm’s limitations.




**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper is a **strong accept**.  While the core idea isn’t groundbreakingly new, the theoretical justification and the demonstrated performance improvements are significant and valuable. The authors have identified a practical problem, proposed a sensible solution, and provided compelling evidence of its effectiveness. The weaknesses are relatively minor and could be addressed in a revision.  I recommend accepting this paper for NIPS.
