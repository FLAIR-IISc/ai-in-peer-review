- Overall: Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that notably speeds up covariance updates in high dimensions while retaining comparable function-evaluation performance; recommends acceptance.  
- Problem & approach: Tackles O(d^2) covariance update cost by updating the Cholesky factor directly and bounding the approximation error in step-size adaptation via Lemma 1.  
- Positioning: Correctly cites and differentiates from prior work (e.g., Suttorp et al. 2009) and related low-rank/derivative-free methods.  
- Strength — Runtime: Demonstrates a significant runtime reduction, especially as dimensionality increases (Figure 2).  
- Strength — Theory: Provides a theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing the approximation error is bounded and diminishes with convergence.  
- Strength — Complexity & storage: Achieves optimal O(µd^2) update complexity and reduces storage requirements.  
- Strength — Presentation: Well-written, organized, and aided by clear algorithms (Alg. 1 and 2).  
- Strength — Experiments: Comprehensive empirical comparison against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES across benchmarks.  
- Weakness — Lemma assumptions: Insufficient discussion and empirical evidence for the lemma’s assumptions, especially convergence of C̄_t in practice.  
- Weakness — Rotation error: Lacks detailed empirical analysis correlating the neglected correction factor (Et) with performance differences.  
- Weakness — Cache effects: Limited discussion of observed cache-size impact at d=256 and scalability mitigation strategies (e.g., block-wise updates).  
- Weakness — Hyperparameters: No analysis of sensitivity to CMA-ES hyperparameters or whether defaults remain optimal for Cholesky-CMA-ES.  
- Originality: Idea of Cholesky updates is not wholly new, but the theoretical justification and integration into standard CMA-ES with complexity analysis are novel and significant.  
- Significance: Meaningful practical impact by improving CMA-ES scalability, enabling larger/complex optimization applications.  
- Pro (accept): Runtime gains, solid theoretical backing, optimal asymptotic complexity/storage, clear presentation, thorough experiments.  
- Con (revise): Need deeper treatment of theoretical assumptions, empirical rotation-error analysis, and scalability/hyperparameter concerns.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions — expand Lemma 1 discussion, analyze rotation matrix Et in-depth, and include a hyperparameter sensitivity experiment.
