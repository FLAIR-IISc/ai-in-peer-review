### Summary of the paper

This paper addresses a well-known computational bottleneck in the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a powerful derivative-free optimization algorithm. The standard CMA-ES has a time complexity of at least ω(d²) per iteration, dominated by the decomposition of the d x d covariance matrix C, which is required for sampling and for the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA). The authors propose a new variant, the "Cholesky-CMA-ES", which reduces this complexity to an asymptotically optimal O(µd²) while maintaining the full covariance matrix, making it suitable for non-separable problems.

The core idea is to maintain and directly update a triangular Cholesky factor A (where C = AAᵀ) instead of the full covariance matrix C. While efficient rank-one updates for Cholesky factors are known, the main challenge has been the CSA path `p_σ`, which requires normalization by the matrix inverse square root C⁻¹/². This is not equivalent to normalizing by the inverse Cholesky factor A⁻¹. The key contribution of this paper is to propose an approximation where C⁻¹/² is replaced by A⁻¹ in the CSA update. The authors provide a theoretical justification (Lemma 1) for this approximation, arguing that as the *shape* of the covariance matrix converges, the error introduced by this substitution vanishes. They show empirically on several benchmark functions that this approximation does not degrade performance in terms of function evaluations, while providing a substantial speed-up in wall-clock time, especially for higher dimensions.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Strengths:**

1.  **Addresses a Significant and Practical Problem:** The quadratic complexity of CMA-ES is a major barrier to its application in higher-dimensional settings, particularly when function evaluations are cheap (e.g., in some reinforcement learning policy search or hyperparameter tuning tasks). This paper offers a well-justified and effective solution to this long-standing problem.

2.  **Novel and Sound Technical Contribution:** The critical insight is the justified approximation of the CSA update. Previous attempts to use Cholesky updates either worked only for restricted CMA-ES variants or used non-triangular factors which lack the uniqueness and efficiency properties of this approach. The theoretical argument in Lemma 1, which links the convergence of the covariance matrix shape to the vanishing error of the approximation, is elegant and provides a solid foundation for the proposed method.

3.  **Excellent Empirical Validation:** The experiments are well-designed and convincing. The paper compares the Cholesky-CMA-ES against the standard implementation (CMA-ES-Ref) and a delayed-update variant (CMA-ES/d). The results clearly show that the proposed method achieves the same convergence in terms of function evaluations while being significantly faster in terms of wall-clock time (e.g., 20x faster for d=64). The use of rotated benchmark functions correctly tests the algorithm's invariance properties.

4.  **Asymptotically Optimal Complexity:** The resulting O(µd²) time and O(d²) space complexity for a full-covariance method is asymptotically optimal. This represents a fundamental improvement over the standard O(d³) implementation.

**Weaknesses:**

1.  **Minor Technical Inaccuracy:** In the "Properties of the update rule" section, the paper claims that "the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the factorized matrix". This is incorrect. This property holds for a diagonal matrix, but not for a general symmetric positive-definite matrix. While eigenvalues can be computed from the Cholesky factor, it is not a "free" byproduct in this direct manner. This minor error should be corrected as it slightly overstates one of the secondary benefits.

2.  **Limited Discussion on the Approximation's Behavior:** The justification for the approximation relies on the convergence of the covariance matrix shape. It would be beneficial to include a brief discussion on how the approximation behaves during the transient, early phases of optimization when C_t might be changing rapidly. While the exponential weighting in `p_σ` mitigates the impact of past errors, the performance could still be affected when the search direction changes abruptly.

### Detailed Comments

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality. The proposed algorithm is well-motivated, and the claims of improved performance are strongly supported by both theoretical reasoning and thorough experimental results. It is a complete and well-executed piece of work.

**Clarity:** The paper is very clearly written and well-organized. The background on CMA-ES is concise but sufficient, and the motivation for the new approach is well-explained. The key problem with the CSA update and the proposed solution are presented logically. Algorithm 1 provides a clear summary of the final method. The figures effectively communicate the main experimental findings.

**Originality:** The work is highly original. While it builds upon prior work on Cholesky updates for ES, it solves the critical problem of integrating this efficient update with the standard CMA-ES step-size adaptation mechanism. The theoretical justification for the approximation is novel and is the key enabler for the entire method. This is a significant advance over prior art.

**Significance:** The results are highly significant. CMA-ES is a workhorse algorithm for black-box optimization in machine learning and beyond. By drastically reducing its computational overhead without sacrificing its performance on difficult non-separable problems, this work makes the algorithm practical for a much wider range of problem dimensions. This is likely to become the new standard implementation of CMA-ES for moderate-to-high dimensional problems where a full covariance matrix is feasible. Researchers and practitioners are very likely to adopt and build upon this contribution.

### Recommendation

**Accept.**

This is an excellent paper that presents a significant and practical improvement to a widely used optimization algorithm. The technical contribution is novel, the theoretical justification is sound, and the empirical results are compelling. It addresses a clear need in the community and is expected to have a high impact.
