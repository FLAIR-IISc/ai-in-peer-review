## Review of CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity

This paper proposes a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) leveraging a Cholesky decomposition to improve computational efficiency, specifically addressing the runtime and storage complexity associated with covariance matrix updates. The core idea is to update the Cholesky factor directly instead of repeatedly computing the matrix square root, offering potential benefits for high-dimensional optimization problems.

The paper begins with a concise overview of CMA-ES and its applications, contextualizing the need for improved efficiency. It clearly articulates the computational bottleneck of the original algorithm and outlines existing approaches to mitigate it, such as delayed updates or restricting model complexity. The authors then introduce the Cholesky-CMA-ES, providing a theoretical justification for its performance and detailing its algorithmic implementation.  The presentation of the theoretical arguments relating to the error introduced by approximating the coordinate system rotation is a strong point, supported by Lemma 1.  Empirical validation follows, comparing the proposed method against standard CMA-ES variants across a range of benchmark functions and dimensionality.

**Strengths:**

* **Clear Problem Statement:** The paper identifies a significant performance bottleneck in CMA-ES and clearly motivates the need for a more efficient covariance update strategy.
* **Sound Theoretical Foundation:**  The authors provide a theoretical analysis supporting the validity of their Cholesky-based approach.  The justification for the approximation and the associated Lemma 1 are well-explained.
* **Algorithmic Clarity:** The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm is presented with sufficient detail, including a pseudocode representation (Algorithm 1) and a description of the rank-one update procedure (Algorithm 2).
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The paper includes a thorough empirical evaluation, comparing the proposed method against several baselines across a variety of benchmark functions and dimensionality levels.  The inclusion of runtime analysis, in addition to the standard function evaluation count, is valuable.
* **Asymptotic Optimality:** The claimed optimal asymptotic complexity is a compelling benefit, particularly for large-scale problems.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Numerical Stability:** While the paper mentions improved numerical stability as a benefit, it lacks a detailed discussion of potential issues related to Cholesky decomposition, particularly regarding conditioning and potential breakdowns.
* **Cache Effects in Experiments:** The results note the impact of L2 cache size at d=256.  A more in-depth analysis of the scalability with respect to memory access patterns would be beneficial.
* **Comparison with Recent Work:** The paper references Suttorp et al. [2009] but could benefit from a more thorough comparison to other recent advancements in CMA-ES optimization, potentially including approaches utilizing low-rank approximations or other factorization techniques.
* **Empirical Results Presentation:** Figures are somewhat small, making detailed visual analysis challenging. The presentation of results could be improved for clarity (e.g. error bars).
* **Lack of Discussion on Parameter Sensitivity:**  The paper states that all parameters were set to default values. A brief analysis of the sensitivity of the proposed method to parameter choices could strengthen the findings.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a well-recognized computational bottleneck in a widely-used algorithm (CMA-ES).
* The proposed solution is theoretically sound and backed by empirical evidence demonstrating significant speedups, especially in higher dimensions.
* The approach offers an asymptotically optimal solution for covariance update complexity.
* The paper is clearly written and presents its ideas in a logical and well-organized manner.

**Arguments for Rejection:**

* While the experimental results are encouraging, the lack of detailed discussion regarding numerical stability and the potential impact of cache effects could raise concerns about the robustness and scalability of the method.
* The comparison to other recent CMA-ES optimizations is limited, and it's unclear how the proposed approach fares against state-of-the-art alternatives beyond the baselines tested.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization.  The Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a compelling improvement in computational efficiency for CMA-ES, particularly in high-dimensional settings. While some aspects of numerical stability and a more extensive comparison to recent work could be strengthened, the theoretical justification, clear presentation, and compelling experimental results make this a worthy contribution to NIPS.

**Recommendation:**  **Accept** with minor revisions to address the weaknesses noted above, particularly regarding numerical stability and a more comprehensive literature review.
