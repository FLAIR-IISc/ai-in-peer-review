## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, addressing a key computational bottleneck – the covariance matrix update. The proposed “Cholesky-CMA-ES” offers a significant speedup, particularly in higher dimensions, while maintaining comparable performance in terms of function evaluations. The theoretical justification, connecting the approximation to convergence properties, is a strong point. I believe this work represents a solid contribution to the field and warrants acceptance, though some minor clarifications and additional experimental analysis would strengthen it further.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The paper tackles the computational expense of updating the covariance matrix in CMA-ES, which scales as O(d<sup>2</sup>). Existing approaches either reduce update frequency (leading to slower convergence) or restrict covariance matrix complexity (potentially hindering performance on ill-conditioned problems). The authors propose leveraging the Cholesky factorization directly, updating it instead of the covariance matrix itself.  Crucially, they address a key concern with previous similar attempts – the potential for performance degradation due to an inaccurate approximation in the step-size adaptation. They provide a theoretical argument, leveraging Lemma 1, that the error introduced by this approximation is bounded and diminishes with convergence.

The authors correctly position their work within the existing literature. They cite relevant papers on CMA-ES variations (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel), low-rank approximations (Poland & Zell, Loshchilov), and the broader context of derivative-free optimization.  The comparison to Suttorp et al. (2009) is particularly important, as their method is a direct predecessor, and the authors clearly differentiate their approach.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Significant Speedup:**  The core contribution – the demonstrated runtime reduction, especially as dimensionality increases – is a substantial practical improvement. Figure 2 clearly illustrates this benefit.
* **Theoretical Justification:**  The inclusion of Lemma 1 and the accompanying discussion provides a solid theoretical foundation for the proposed approximation. This differentiates the work from purely empirical improvements. The argument regarding the convergence of the rotation matrix is insightful.
* **Optimal Complexity:**  Achieving O(µd<sup>2</sup>) complexity for the covariance update, especially with the associated storage reduction, is a significant achievement.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The motivation is clearly stated, and the technical details are presented in a logical manner. Algorithm 1 and 2 are helpful in understanding the implementation.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The comparison against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES on a range of benchmark functions is thorough and provides compelling evidence of the algorithm’s effectiveness.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Discussion of Lemma 1's Assumptions:** While the authors state the lemma’s conditions, a more detailed discussion of how these assumptions hold in practice (specifically, the convergence of C̄<sub>t</sub>) would be beneficial. Is there empirical evidence to suggest this convergence occurs in typical CMA-ES runs?
* **Empirical Evaluation of Rotation Error:** Although theoretically justified, the error introduced by neglecting the correction factor in equation (5) could be empirically investigated.  While the supplementary material mentions monitoring Et, more detailed analysis correlating this rotation change with performance differences would be valuable.
* **Cache Effects:** The authors acknowledge the impact of cache size at d=256. A more detailed discussion of how this limitation affects scalability and potential mitigation strategies (e.g., block-wise updates) would be helpful.
* **Hyperparameter Sensitivity:** The paper doesn't discuss the sensitivity of the algorithm to hyperparameter settings.  Are the default CMA-ES parameters still optimal for Cholesky-CMA-ES, or do they require tuning?



**4. Originality & Significance:**

The idea of using the Cholesky factorization directly for covariance adaptation is not entirely new (Suttorp et al., 2009). However, the key originality lies in the *theoretical justification* for the approximation and the extension to the standard CMA-ES, combined with the rigorous analysis of complexity and storage.  

The significance of this work is considerable. CMA-ES is a widely used algorithm, and reducing its runtime, particularly for high-dimensional problems, is a valuable contribution.  The improved scalability will enable the application of CMA-ES to larger and more complex optimization problems, such as those encountered in deep learning and reinforcement learning.

**5. Pro/Con Acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Significant runtime improvement with minimal performance degradation.
* Strong theoretical justification for the approximation.
* Optimal asymptotic complexity and storage requirements.
* Clear presentation and thorough experimental evaluation.

**Con:**

* Limited discussion of the assumptions underlying the theoretical justification.
* Lack of empirical analysis of the introduced rotation error.
* Potential scalability limitations due to cache effects.




**Recommendation:**

I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.  Specifically, I suggest:

1.  **Expand the discussion of Lemma 1:** Provide more detail on the conditions for its validity and evidence of convergence in typical CMA-ES runs.
2.  **Analyze Rotation Error:** Present a more in-depth analysis of the rotation matrix (Et) changes and correlate them with performance variations.
3. **Address Hyperparameter Sensitivity**: Include a small experiment to evaluate the sensitivity of the algorithm to typical CMA-ES hyperparameters.



Overall, this is a well-executed and impactful piece of work that addresses a practical limitation of CMA-ES and provides a significant improvement in its scalability. The inclusion of theoretical analysis elevates it above a purely empirical contribution, making it a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
