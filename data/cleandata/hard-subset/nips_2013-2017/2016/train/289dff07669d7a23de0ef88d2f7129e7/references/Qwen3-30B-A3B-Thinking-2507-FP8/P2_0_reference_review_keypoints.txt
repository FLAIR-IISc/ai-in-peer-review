- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing the costly matrix square root with a Cholesky factor to reduce O(d^3) computation.
- Provides a theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing the approximation error diminishes as the algorithm converges.
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations across benchmarks while substantially reducing runtime (e.g., 20x faster at d=64).
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive and reproducible: multiple test functions, dimensions d=4..256, clear pseudocode and figures.
- Paper is technically sound, well-written, and presents the theoretical and practical contributions clearly.
- Novelty: first rigorous analysis showing Cholesky-based updates do not degrade standard CMA-ES performance, distinguishing it from prior attempts.
- Significance: offers a practical drop-in method to speed up CMA-ES, enabling larger problem sizes while retaining full covariance modeling.
- Weakness: the "asymptotically optimal time complexity" claim is imprecise; actual complexity is O(μ d^2) ≈ O(d^2 log d).
- Weakness: limited discussion of numerical stability of Cholesky updates and how potential issues are mitigated.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to some recent CMA-ES variants (e.g., LM-CMA) beyond the delay strategy and Suttorp approach.
- Recommendation: strong accept — important, well-validated contribution with minor addressable concerns.
