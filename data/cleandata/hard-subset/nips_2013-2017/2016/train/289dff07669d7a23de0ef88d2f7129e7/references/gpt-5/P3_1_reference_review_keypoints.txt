- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A via O(d^2) rank-one updates to avoid explicit eigendecompositions.  
- CSA approximation: Replace C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} for the cumulative step-size adaptation, argued acceptable via RQ/polar decomposition and Lemma 1.  
- Complexity claim: Algorithm achieves O(µ d^2) time per iteration and O(d^2) memory with large wall-clock speedups up to d=256.  
- Novelty: Extends prior Cholesky-update work to standard µ > 1 CMA-ES and gives a theoretically motivated CSA approximation.  
- Strength — algorithmic: Simple, practical O(µ d^2) triangular updates integrating rank-1 and rank-µ terms with straightforward sampling/solves.  
- Strength — engineering: Reduces decomposition overhead while keeping full-covariance modeling and slightly smaller O(d^2) memory constant.  
- Strength — theory: RQ/polar decomposition argument and Lemma 1 plausibly explain why A^{-1} can substitute for C^{-1/2} as shape stabilizes.  
- Strength — empirical: Function-evaluation parity with frequent-decomposition CMA-ES and large wall-clock improvements (e.g., ~20× at d=64) on rotated benchmarks.  
- Strength — relevance: High practical impact for users needing faster full-covariance CMA-ES on moderate-to-large dimensions with cheap objectives.  
- Major flaw — spectral claim: Incorrect statement that diag(A) are square roots of eigenvalues; diag(A) are Cholesky pivots and do not give the spectrum.  
- CSA limitation: Lemma 1 is asymptotic; no quantitative bounds on bias/variance of using A^{-1} vs C^{-1/2}, especially during transients or noisy/nonstationary settings.  
- Suggested diagnostics: Measure ∥pσ(A^{-1})∥ vs ∥pσ(C^{-1/2})∥ over time or occasional ground-truth normalization; ablate occasional orthogonal-factor refresh (polar decomposition).  
- Invariance concern: Using A^{-1} introduces a rotation and further departs from affine invariance of CSA; more explicit randomized-rotation experiments with confidence intervals are needed.  
- Numerical stability: Repeated rank-one updates may accumulate round-off—need discussion of β conditions, downdates, reconditioning or rebuilds, and avoidance of forming A^{-1}.  
- Baselines/scope: Need clearer details on Suttorp-based baseline and comparisons to modern low-rank/limited-memory variants (LM-CMA/VD-CMA) to position trade-offs.  
- Complexity nuance: “Optimal” complexity claims should be softened—the method reduces constants but many implementations amortize eigendecompositions already.  
- Quality: Technically sound and well-motivated overall, but CSA theory is qualitative and the spectral error is substantive and must be fixed.  
- Clarity issues: Fix eigenvalue statements, provide more implementation details for Algorithm 2 (cholupdate/downdate, scaling), and correct malformed references.  
- Originality: Novel and useful combination extending Cholesky-update ideas to µ > 1 CMA-ES with complexity analysis.  
- Overall verdict: Strong practical contribution deserving acceptance after correcting the spectral error, adding quantitative CSA diagnostics or occasional orthogonal refresh, and addressing numerical-stability and baseline-comparison concerns.
