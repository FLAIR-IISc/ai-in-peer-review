## NIPS 2016 Paper Review: CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity

**Summary:**

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which leverages the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix for updates instead of directly manipulating the matrix or its square root. The key contribution is a theoretical justification for this approach, demonstrating it doesn't deteriorate performance, and achieving optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity, particularly beneficial for high-dimensional, ill-conditioned problems. The authors demonstrate through experiments that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves comparable performance to the standard CMA-ES in terms of function evaluations but with significant runtime improvements.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper correctly situates itself within the existing literature on CMA-ES acceleration. It acknowledges prior work on delayed updates [Hansen & Ostermeier, 2001], reduced complexity adaptations [Poland & Zell, 2001; Ros & Hansen, 2008], and Cholesky factor-based updates [Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015]. Crucially, it distinguishes itself by providing a theoretical foundation for applying the Cholesky factorization to the *standard* CMA-ES, a challenge previous attempts struggled with.  The comparison to Suttorp et al. [2009] and Krause & Igel [2015] is relevant, highlighting the improvements offered by this new approach.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Justification:** The most significant strength is the theoretical argument presented for the validity of using the Cholesky factor update, particularly regarding the potential error introduced by neglecting the coordinate rotation during step-size adaptation. Lemma 1 provides a convincing argument for the convergence of this approximation.
* **Computational Efficiency:**  The reported O(µd²) runtime complexity is a substantial improvement over the standard CMA-ES's O(d²) complexity for covariance updates. The empirical results clearly demonstrate this speedup, especially in higher dimensions.
* **Optimal Storage Complexity:** Maintaining only the Cholesky factor reduces memory requirements, a critical advantage for large-scale problems.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and organized.  The explanation of the algorithm and its theoretical underpinnings are relatively clear, even for those not intimately familiar with CMA-ES.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of the Cholesky-CMA-ES on a variety of benchmark functions, including those specifically designed to challenge CMA-ES (e.g., Diffpowers). The scaling of performance with dimensionality is well-illustrated.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Empirical Evaluation:** While the experiments are convincing, the comparison focuses mainly on runtime and function evaluation count.  A deeper analysis of the *quality* of the solutions found (e.g., precision, convergence speed near the optimum) would be valuable.
* **Dependence on LAPACK:** The reliance on LAPACK for efficiency is noted but could be a limitation for deployment in environments where LAPACK is not readily available.
* **Discussion of Error in Step-Size Adaptation:** While Lemma 1 provides theoretical support, a more detailed discussion of the practical impact of the neglected coordinate rotation in the step-size adaptation (equation 5) would be helpful. How does this error manifest in practice, and how does it affect performance on different problem types?
* **Limited Comparison to State-of-the-Art Alternatives:** While CMA-ES-Ref and Suttorp-CMA-ES are included, a comparison to more recent, highly optimized CMA-ES implementations (if available) would strengthen the analysis.
* **Lack of Detailed Analysis of Cache Effects:** The mention of L2 cache limitations at d=256 is important but could be expanded upon. A more detailed analysis of the impact of cache size and memory bandwidth on performance would be beneficial.



**Quality:** The paper is technically sound and provides a well-supported argument for its central claim.  The theoretical analysis adds significant value, and the experimental results are convincing. It's a complete piece of work within the scope of the proposed modification.

**Clarity:**  The paper is well-written and generally easy to understand, although a deeper background in CMA-ES is helpful. The notation is mostly clear, and the algorithm is described concisely.

**Originality:**  The approach of applying the Cholesky factorization to the *standard* CMA-ES, coupled with the theoretical justification, is novel. While previous work explored similar ideas, this paper provides a significant contribution in terms of theoretical rigor and performance gains.

**Significance:**  The potential for significant runtime improvements, particularly for high-dimensional problems, makes this work highly significant.  It has the potential to broaden the applicability of CMA-ES to a wider range of problems where computational cost is a major constraint. It's likely to be adopted and built upon by researchers and practitioners working in derivative-free optimization.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical justification for the proposed method.
* Significant runtime improvements demonstrated empirically.
* Addresses a key bottleneck in CMA-ES – the covariance matrix update.
* Well-written and clearly presented.
* Addresses an important and practically relevant problem.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited depth of empirical analysis beyond runtime and function evaluation count.
* Dependence on LAPACK could be a deployment hurdle.
* Further investigation of the error introduced by neglecting coordinate rotation during step-size adaptation could be beneficial.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The theoretical justification, coupled with the empirical results demonstrating significant runtime improvements, makes it a worthwhile addition to the NIPS program.  The weaknesses are relatively minor and do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work.

**Recommendation:** **Strong Accept**
