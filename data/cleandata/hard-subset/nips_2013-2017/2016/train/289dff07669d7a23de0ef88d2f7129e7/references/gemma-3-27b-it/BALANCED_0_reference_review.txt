This paper presents a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, dubbed Cholesky-CMA-ES, aimed at reducing runtime and memory complexity by using the Cholesky factorization instead of the matrix square root. The core idea is to update the Cholesky factor directly, leveraging existing fast update techniques, and to theoretically justify why this approximation doesn’t significantly degrade performance. The authors provide a theoretical analysis and empirical results demonstrating speedups, particularly in higher dimensions.

The paper is well-motivated, addressing a known bottleneck in CMA-ES – the computational cost of covariance matrix manipulation. The introduction clearly articulates the problem and positions the contribution within the existing literature on accelerating CMA-ES.  The connection to previous work, such as Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015), is appropriately acknowledged and contrasted. The theoretical justification, based on the convergence of rotation matrices, is a key strength, setting this work apart from earlier attempts.  The empirical evaluation is thorough, using standard benchmark functions and varying dimensionality to demonstrate the benefits of the proposed method. The inclusion of runtime comparisons, differentiating between function evaluations and wall-clock time, is crucial for highlighting the practical advantages.

**Strengths:**

* **Technical Soundness:** The proposed method appears technically sound, and the justification based on Lemma 1 is well-reasoned. The connection to previous work on Cholesky updates is clearly established.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The mathematical notation is clear, and the algorithm is presented in a concise manner. The authors refer to Hansen (2015) appropriately for details of the base algorithm, avoiding unnecessary repetition.
* **Originality:** While building on existing techniques, the combination of using the Cholesky factorization *with* the theoretical justification is novel. The extension of Krause & Igel’s work to the full CMA-ES is a meaningful contribution.
* **Significance:** The speedup achieved, especially in higher dimensions, is significant. Reducing the computational bottleneck of covariance matrix updates could broaden the applicability of CMA-ES to larger-scale problems, particularly in areas like reinforcement learning and hyperparameter optimization.  The focus on asymptotic complexity is important.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Empirical Scope:** While the benchmark suite is standard, it primarily focuses on continuous optimization problems.  It would be beneficial to demonstrate the performance of Cholesky-CMA-ES on more challenging, real-world problems or benchmark suites that include discontinuous or noisy objectives, as mentioned in the introduction.
* **Lemma 1's Assumptions:** The assumption of convergence of *Ct* in Lemma 1 is not always guaranteed.  A more robust analysis acknowledging the possibility of non-convergence and its impact on the approximation error would strengthen the theoretical argument.  The connection between convergence of *Ct* and the error introduced by the Cholesky approximation could be more explicitly stated.
* **Cache Effects:** The discussion of L2 cache limitations at d=256 feels a bit abrupt. A more detailed analysis of the performance impact of cache size, and potentially strategies for mitigating it, would be valuable.
* **Comparison Details:** The description of the CMA-ES-Ref and Suttorp-CMA-ES implementations lacks some detail. Specifically, the parameter settings (beyond the default values mentioned) used for these baselines should be clearly stated to ensure reproducibility.
* **Algorithm 2 Details:** Algorithm 2 lacks a bit of detail on how `v` is computed, making it a bit hard to follow.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant practical limitation of CMA-ES.
* The theoretical justification provides confidence in the method's performance.
* The empirical results demonstrate a clear speedup, particularly in higher dimensions.
* The paper is well-written and clearly explains the proposed method.

**Arguments against Acceptance:**

* The empirical evaluation could be broadened to include more challenging problems.
* The theoretical analysis relies on an assumption that may not always hold.
* Some details regarding implementation and experimental setup are missing.




**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a promising improvement over the standard CMA-ES, with potential for significant performance gains in high-dimensional problems. While there are some minor weaknesses, they do not detract significantly from the overall quality of the work. I believe the paper is worthy of acceptance, contingent on the authors addressing the points raised regarding the empirical scope, theoretical assumptions, and implementation details.



**Recommendation:** Accept (with minor revisions)
