- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update Cholesky factor directly to avoid repeated matrix square root and improve efficiency.
- Theoretical justification (including Lemma 1) argues the change does not harm convergence, focusing on step-size behavior.
- Empirical results show significant runtime speedup, especially in higher dimensions, with little increase in function evaluations.
- Achieves O(µd^2) covariance update complexity, an asymptotic improvement over standard CMA-ES.
- Paper is well-written, clear, and connects to prior Cholesky-update work.
- Experiments include thorough comparisons against multiple baselines (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES).
- Experiments are limited to standard benchmarks; more realistic/challenging domains (e.g., deep learning HPO, RL) are missing.
- Discussion of error from approximating the inverse covariance is brief and lacks quantitative analysis or mitigation strategies.
- Cache effects cause performance degradation at very high dimensions (e.g., d=256); block-wise or other mitigations are not explored.
- No ablation study to isolate contributions of different algorithmic components, especially the step-size approximation.
- Technically sound and rigorous; experimental results support claims.
- Idea is not entirely novel but extends prior work with solid theoretical backing and broader evaluation.
- Potentially significant practical impact by enabling CMA-ES in larger-scale problems.
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions to broaden experiments and analyze approximation error more thoroughly.
