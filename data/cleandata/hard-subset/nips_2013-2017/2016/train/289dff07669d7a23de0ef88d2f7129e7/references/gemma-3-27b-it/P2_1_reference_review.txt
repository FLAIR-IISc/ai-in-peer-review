## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a modification to the standard Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) called Cholesky-CMA-ES. The core idea is to utilize the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix throughout the adaptation process, rather than directly manipulating the covariance matrix itself or its square root. The authors argue this approach reduces computational complexity to O(µd<sup>2</sup>) – where µ is typically much smaller than *d* – for the covariance update step, while maintaining comparable performance to the original CMA-ES in terms of function evaluations. They provide a theoretical justification based on the convergence of the Cholesky factor and empirical results demonstrating significant speedups in runtime, especially for higher-dimensional problems.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a clear and compelling argument for why using the Cholesky factorization should not degrade performance. The lemma relating to the convergence of coordinate system rotations is a key insight.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The claimed reduction in computational complexity is a significant contribution.  The authors correctly identify the covariance matrix update as a bottleneck in the original CMA-ES, and their approach directly addresses this.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results consistently demonstrate that the Cholesky-CMA-ES is faster than the original CMA-ES and other variants, particularly as dimensionality increases. The comparisons to CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d and Suttorp-CMA-ES are well-chosen.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The explanation of the Cholesky decomposition and its application to CMA-ES is accessible.
* **Practical Relevance:** The speedups offered by this approach are valuable for practical applications of CMA-ES, especially in high-dimensional spaces, like reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Novelty in Core Idea:** While the *combination* of applying a Cholesky update to the *standard* CMA-ES, along with the theoretical justification, is novel, the idea of using Cholesky updates within CMA-ES variations isn’t entirely new (referenced in the introduction with Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel). The paper effectively extends these prior works to the full CMA-ES framework.
* **Dependence on µ:** The O(µd<sup>2</sup>) complexity is an improvement, but it's important to acknowledge that the constant factor µ still impacts performance. For extremely large µ, the benefit might diminish. This point could be explicitly addressed.
* **Discussion of Error Introduced:** While the authors discuss the error introduced by not fully correcting for coordinate rotations, the analysis of the impact of this error on performance could be more detailed. How does this error affect convergence speed in practice, and for what types of problems is it most pronounced?
* **Cache Effects:** The authors acknowledge the impact of cache size on performance at d=256, but a more detailed investigation of memory access patterns and their influence on runtime would be beneficial.




**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality (9/10):** The paper is technically sound and provides a well-supported argument for the benefits of the Cholesky-CMA-ES.  The theoretical analysis and empirical results are convincing.  Minor deductions are made for the slight lack of deep error analysis and the dependence on µ.
* **Clarity (8/10):**  The paper is generally clear, but some sections (e.g., the derivation of the Cholesky update) could benefit from more detailed explanations.  A clearer connection between the equations and the intuition behind them would be helpful.
* **Originality (7/10):** The core idea builds upon existing work but represents a significant and valuable extension, particularly in the theoretical justification and application to the full CMA-ES framework. The originality lies in the effective combination and analysis rather than a radically new concept.
* **Significance (8/10):** The potential for significantly faster optimization, especially in high-dimensional problems, makes this work important.  It’s likely to be adopted by researchers and practitioners using CMA-ES.




**Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

* Substantial runtime improvements over standard CMA-ES.
* Strong theoretical justification for the proposed method.
* Thorough experimental validation on a diverse set of benchmark functions.
* Addresses a recognized bottleneck in CMA-ES.

**Against Acceptance:**

*  The core idea builds upon previous work, limiting the degree of novelty.
*  Further analysis of the introduced error and its impact on convergence is needed.




**Overall Recommendation:**

**Accept.**  This paper presents a well-motivated and thoroughly validated improvement to the CMA-ES algorithm.  The speedups offered by the Cholesky-CMA-ES are significant and the theoretical analysis provides a strong foundation for its use. While not a revolutionary breakthrough, it represents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization and is likely to be widely adopted.  Minor suggestions for improvement were provided above; addressing these could further strengthen the paper, but are not essential for acceptance.
