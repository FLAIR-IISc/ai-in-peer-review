- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to achieve per-iteration O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) memory.
- Avoids recomputing C^{1/2}; replaces CSA's C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} and justifies ignoring the induced rotation via a continuity lemma.
- Experiments on standard noiseless benchmarks with random rotations show essentially identical function evaluations and large wall-clock speedups (up to ~20x at d=64).
- Outperforms a non-triangular Cholesky approach (Suttorp et al., 2009) and compares to a delayed-decomposition baseline.
- Builds on prior factorization and reduced-covariance CMA-ES work; novelty is extending triangular updates to multi-parent CMA-ES and providing a CSA approximation justification.
- Factorized rank-one updates are standard and appear correctly adapted; CSA approximation is the riskiest technical element.
- Lemma 1 offers intuitive continuity-based support but no quantitative error bounds relating CSA error to c1, cµ, or C’s rate of change.
- No analysis of early iterations when C changes rapidly; theoretical justification remains qualitative.
- Empirical study covers dims up to 256 with 100 trials, but excluding non-converged Rosenbrock runs biases results; success rates/time-to-target should be reported.
- Baselines could be stronger (e.g., active CMA-ES, more aggressive delay strategies); noise robustness not evaluated.
- Reproducibility: pseudocode and implementation platform (Shark, LAPACK) provided, but hardware, BLAS threading, and full parameter settings are incompletely specified.
- Paper is generally well written and organized; explanations (including why CSA needs C^{-1/2}) and algorithms are clear.
- Some references are malformed and minor editorial issues remain.
- Clarify whether triangular updates support only positive rank updates and whether active CMA-ES (negative weights) is supported or out of scope.
- Practical significance: meaningful speed and memory benefits for full-covariance CMA-ES users, though asymptotic d-dependence stays quadratic so not a large-scale solution.
- Pros: simple drop-in modification, strong runtime gains without loss of evaluation efficiency, reasonable theoretical intuition, efficient implementation with useful byproducts (eigenvalues, A^{-1}).
- Cons: qualitative CSA justification, unquantified early-iteration behavior, biased Rosenbrock reporting, missing noise and active-CMA-ES evaluations, some claims only big-O, reference issues.
- Suggestions: report success rates/penalized failures, add noise/constrained/multimodal tests, quantify rotation discrepancy decay (especially early iterations), clarify active-CMA-ES support, and provide hardware/BLAS details and reproducible code/scripts.
- Recommendation: solid, practically valuable contribution with plausible theory; leans toward acceptance pending stronger theoretical bounds and broader experimental validation.
