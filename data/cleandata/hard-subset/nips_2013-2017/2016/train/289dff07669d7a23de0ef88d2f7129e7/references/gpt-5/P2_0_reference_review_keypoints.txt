- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates lower-triangular A with C = A A^T to avoid repeated eigendecompositions.
- Decomposes rank-1 and rank-µ covariance updates into sequences of rank-1 updates on A in O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) storage.
- Replaces CSA’s C^{-1/2} normalization with A^{-1}, omitting a changing orthogonal factor E from the polar/QR relation.
- Lemma argues E_t stabilizes as normalized covariance shape converges, so ignoring E has diminishing effect due to exponential weighting.
- Empirically matches evaluation counts of a strong CMA-ES reference while giving large wall-clock speedups (up to ~20× at d=64) on rotated benchmarks to d=256.
- Outperforms a delayed-decomposition CMA-ES baseline and a non-triangular Cholesky method from prior work.
- Builds on standard CMA-ES, limited-memory variants, and prior direct-factorization update attempts; novelty is extending triangular Cholesky updates to rank-µ CMA-ES and justifying A^{-1} in CSA.
- Clear practical motivation: removes cubic eigendecomposition bottleneck for high-dimensional full-covariance adaptation.
- Sound algorithmic design: direct triangular updates, efficient triangular inverses, and clean integration into CMA-ES.
- Theoretical rationale for the CSA approximation is plausible and aligns with empirical results.
- Strong empirical evidence, implementation details (LAPACK, Shark) and pseudocode improve reproducibility.
- Incorrect factual claim: diagonal of triangular Cholesky factor are not square roots of eigenvalues; that statement must be removed or corrected.
- CSA theoretical justification is heuristic: it assumes convergence of normalized covariance and lacks finite-time or worst-case bounds for using A^{-1} instead of C^{-1/2}.
- Experimental methodology concerns: no success rates or budget-capped metrics (COCO/BBOB-style), exclusion of non-convergent Rosenbrock trials biases comparisons, and no statistical significance tests reported.
- Baseline tuning unclear: clarify whether reference CMA-ES used recommended delayed eigendecomposition settings and report effects on evaluation efficiency.
- Limited experimental scope: no tests on noisy/objective-disturbed or constrained problems where CSA behavior is critical.
- Numerical stability not addressed: possible round-off accumulation from many rank-1 Cholesky updates and no discussion of periodic refactorization or drift correction.
- Reference formatting contains glitches and parameter/experimental settings need to be spelled out for reproducibility.
- Assessment: technically sound and practically significant with meaningful novelty, but marred by one factual error, heuristic theory, and limited experiments.
- Pros for acceptance: addresses a real bottleneck, shows large practical speedups without hurting evaluation counts, provides clear algorithmic contribution and public implementation.
- Cons for acceptance: incorrect Cholesky/eigenvalue claim, lack of finite-time CSA guarantees, experimental rigor and scope need improvement, and formatting issues.
- Suggested fixes: correct/remove eigenvalue claim; provide conservative bounds on CSA path-length error; add success-rate and budget-capped evaluations (COCO/BBOB) and noisy tests; clarify/tune baselines; discuss numerical stability and occasional full refactorization; fix references and release code.
- Recommendation: lean toward acceptance conditional on addressing the factual error, strengthening analysis/experiments, and fixing presentation issues.
