- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance to reduce storage and runtime complexity.  
- Claims the approach maintains CMA-ES performance while achieving asymptotically optimal complexity for full covariance adaptation.  
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that rotation matrices converge and the Cholesky update does not degrade convergence.  
- Empirical results show significant runtime speedups, especially in high dimensions, without increasing function evaluations.  
- Builds on prior CMA-ES and Cholesky-update work, distinguishing itself by arguing no performance loss from the approximation.  
- Connects to information geometry foundations of CMA-ES to support theoretical claims.  
- Strength: strong theoretical justification addressing a key concern with prior Cholesky-based methods.  
- Strength: substantial computational efficiency and claimed O(µ d^2) complexity improvement.  
- Strength: correctly identifies asymptotic optimality limits for full covariance adaptation.  
- Strength: clear, well-structured presentation with helpful algorithms included.  
- Strength: comprehensive experiments across benchmark functions and dimensions.  
- Weakness: empirical focus on runtime; identical function-evaluation counts leave convergence behavior under-explored.  
- Weakness: limited comparison to Suttorp-CMA-ES and other Cholesky-based alternatives.  
- Weakness: inadequate discussion of potential loss of affine invariance and its implications.  
- Weakness: brief treatment of cache/memory effects (e.g., L2 cache impact at d=256) and scaling with memory constraints.  
- Weakness: lack of a direct code release link for reproducibility despite Shark library mention.  
- For acceptance: addresses major CMA-ES bottleneck, offers sound theory, and shows practical speedups without accuracy loss.  
- Against acceptance: lacks deeper convergence analysis, limited alternative-method comparisons, and insufficient discussion of invariance issues.  
- Overall: valuable, practical, and theoretically sound contribution worthy of acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Recommended revisions: analyze convergence behavior, expand Suttorp-CMA-ES comparisons, discuss affine-invariance implications, and provide source code.
