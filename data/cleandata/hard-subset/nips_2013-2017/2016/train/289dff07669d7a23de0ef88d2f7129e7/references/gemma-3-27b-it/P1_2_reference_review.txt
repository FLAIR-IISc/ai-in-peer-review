## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) termed “Cholesky-CMA-ES”. The core idea is to perform updates directly on the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix, rather than on the matrix itself, to reduce computational complexity. The authors argue that this approach maintains the algorithm's performance while achieving optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity, particularly crucial for high-dimensional, non-separable problems. They provide theoretical justification for their approach, focusing on the impact of coordinate rotations introduced by using the Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root. Empirical results demonstrate significant speedups in runtime compared to standard CMA-ES implementations, particularly as dimensionality increases, without sacrificing the number of function evaluations.

**Relation to Previous Work:**

The paper builds upon the foundational work of Hansen and Ostermeier [2001] on CMA-ES. It acknowledges prior attempts to accelerate CMA-ES through delayed updates [Hansen & Ostermeier, 2001], reduced matrix complexity [Poland & Zell, 2001; Ros & Hansen, 2008], and updates directly on the Cholesky factor [Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015]. However, it distinguishes itself by providing a theoretical argument that the proposed Cholesky update *doesn't* deteriorate performance, unlike previous methods that might compromise accuracy. The work also connects to research in information geometry [Akimoto et al., 2012] which provides theoretical foundations for the original CMA-ES update rules.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a compelling argument for why the Cholesky-based update doesn't negatively impact the algorithm's convergence, addressing a key concern with similar approaches. Lemma 1, regarding the convergence of rotation matrices, is a significant contribution.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The demonstrated speedups in runtime, particularly at higher dimensions, are substantial and directly address a major bottleneck of CMA-ES – the covariance matrix update.  The claimed O(µd<sup>2</sup>) complexity is a significant improvement.
* **Optimal Complexity:** The authors correctly identify that the achieved complexity is asymptotically optimal for full covariance matrix adaptation, recognizing the limitations of lower-complexity alternatives when dealing with ill-conditioned problems.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is well-structured and generally clearly written, with a concise introduction to CMA-ES and the motivations for the proposed method. The inclusion of Algorithm 1 and 2 is helpful.
* **Comprehensive Experiments:** The authors tested their method on a range of benchmark functions and varying dimensionality, providing a good basis for comparison with existing CMA-ES variants.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Empirical Focus:** While the experiments demonstrate a clear speedup, the performance in terms of function evaluations is *identical* to the reference CMA-ES.  This raises the question of whether the benefit is purely computational, or if there are scenarios where the approximation *could* lead to slower convergence (even with the same function evaluation count).  A more in-depth analysis of convergence behavior, beyond just the final function value, would be valuable.
* **Limited Comparison:** The comparison to Suttorp-CMA-ES seems somewhat limited.  The authors mention it in the experimental setup, but more detailed results comparing their performance are necessary to understand the trade-offs between different Cholesky update strategies.
* **Neglect of Invariance:** The paper acknowledges that the original CSA update (σ<sub>t+1</sub>) is conjectured to not be invariant under affine transformations, and the approximation introduces further potential deviations. While the theoretical justification focuses on the norm, a discussion of the potential implications of breaking this invariance would be helpful.
* **Cache Effects:** The authors briefly mention the impact of L2 cache size on performance at d=256. A more detailed analysis of how the algorithm scales with problem size and memory constraints would strengthen the work.
* **Lack of code release**: The paper states the algorithm was added to the Shark library, but a direct link or repository for reproduction would significantly increase the review process and allow for independent verification of results.



**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Addresses a significant performance bottleneck in a widely used algorithm.
* Provides a sound theoretical justification for the proposed modification.
* Demonstrates substantial speedups in practice without compromising accuracy.
* The work is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical results focus solely on runtime, with no investigation of convergence differences.
* The comparison to alternative Cholesky-based methods is limited.
* Potential implications of deviations from affine invariance are not thoroughly discussed.

**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a practical and theoretically sound improvement to a widely used algorithm, addressing a critical performance bottleneck. While some aspects of the empirical evaluation and theoretical discussion could be strengthened, the benefits of the approach are clear and significant.  I believe the paper makes a strong case for acceptance, with minor revisions to address the weaknesses outlined above.

**Recommendation:** **Accept with minor revisions.** Specifically, I recommend the authors:

1.  Provide a more detailed analysis of convergence behavior, beyond just final function value, to assess the impact of the approximation.
2.  Expand the comparison to Suttorp-CMA-ES, presenting more detailed results and discussing the trade-offs.
3.  Discuss the potential implications of breaking the affine invariance property of the original CMA-ES.
4.  Provide a link to the source code used in the experiments to allow for replication.
