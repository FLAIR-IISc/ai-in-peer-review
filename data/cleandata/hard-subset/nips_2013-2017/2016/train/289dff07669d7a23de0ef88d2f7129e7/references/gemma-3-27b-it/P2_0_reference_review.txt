## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) dubbed "Cholesky-CMA-ES". The core idea is to utilize the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix directly during updates, bypassing the computationally expensive matrix square root calculation. The authors argue that this approach maintains the theoretical guarantees of the original CMA-ES while significantly improving runtime and reducing memory footprint, particularly in high-dimensional spaces. They provide theoretical justification for their method and empirical results demonstrating its efficiency compared to standard CMA-ES implementations and other optimization techniques.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a significant bottleneck:** The paper tackles a well-known computational bottleneck in CMA-ES – the repeated calculation of the matrix square root, which becomes dominant in high dimensions.
* **Strong Theoretical Justification:** The authors provide a solid theoretical argument for why replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor doesn't deteriorate performance. The analysis regarding the convergence of the global step-size adaptation, even with the introduced approximation, is well-reasoned and insightful.  Lemma 1 is a key contribution supporting this claim.
* **Asymptotic Optimality:** The proposed method achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity for full covariance matrix adaptation, making it particularly valuable for large-scale problems.
* **Empirical Validation:**  The experiments convincingly demonstrate the runtime benefits of the Cholesky-CMA-ES, especially as dimensionality increases. The consistent performance in terms of function evaluations indicates the approximation doesn't negatively impact search efficiency.
* **Clear Writing & Organization:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized.  The background section on CMA-ES and Cholesky decomposition is helpful for readers unfamiliar with the details.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Comparison:** While the comparison to CMA-ES-Ref and CMA-ES/d is valuable, the comparison to Suttorp-CMA-ES feels somewhat brief. More detailed analysis on the performance differences in various scenarios could strengthen the claims.
* **Cache Effects:** The authors acknowledge the impact of cache size on performance for d=256. Further discussion on the practical limitations of this approach as dimensionality continues to grow (and the memory becomes a greater constraint) would be beneficial.  Exploring strategies to mitigate these limitations could be a direction for future work.
* **Complexity of Implementation:** Although the theoretical speedup is clear, the implementation of rankOneUpdate as described in Algorithm 2 might be non-trivial and require careful optimization for optimal performance in practice. This isn't a weakness of the *idea* but a practical consideration.
* **Error Analysis Detail:** While the authors provide justification for the approximation, a more detailed error analysis—perhaps quantifying the expected error introduced by replacing the matrix square root—could further bolster the theoretical argument.



**Review Criteria Assessment:**

* **Quality (9/10):**  The paper is technically sound and the claims are well-supported. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the empirical results are compelling. The work feels complete in its current form, addressing a specific and important problem within the CMA-ES framework. The authors are careful in presenting their approximation and providing justification for its validity.
* **Clarity (8/10):** The paper is mostly clearly written. The mathematical notation is standard, and the explanations are generally understandable. However, some parts of the theoretical derivation could benefit from further elaboration.
* **Originality (8/10):** While building on previous work utilizing Cholesky decomposition within CMA-ES variants (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel), the authors' key contribution is the *theoretical justification* for using the Cholesky factor in the *standard* CMA-ES without compromising performance, along with demonstrating optimal asymptotic complexity.  This is a significant and novel contribution.
* **Significance (9/10):** This work has the potential to significantly impact the application of CMA-ES to high-dimensional problems. The reduction in runtime is particularly important in domains like reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning, where numerous function evaluations are often required. The paper addresses a critical performance limitation and offers a practical solution.

**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Solves a well-defined and important problem.
* Strong theoretical justification.
* Demonstrable performance improvements.
* Clearly written and well-organized.
* Addresses a limitation of a widely used algorithm.

**Con:**

* The practical complexity of implementing the Cholesky update efficiently could be a barrier to adoption.
* Further investigation into the limitations regarding cache size and memory constraints in extremely high dimensions would be beneficial.



**Overall Recommendation:**

**Strong Accept.**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a compelling solution to a significant bottleneck in the standard CMA-ES, with strong theoretical backing and empirical validation.  While there are some minor areas for potential improvement, the benefits clearly outweigh the drawbacks. I believe this work is of high quality and will be of interest to researchers and practitioners working with CMA-ES and related algorithms.
