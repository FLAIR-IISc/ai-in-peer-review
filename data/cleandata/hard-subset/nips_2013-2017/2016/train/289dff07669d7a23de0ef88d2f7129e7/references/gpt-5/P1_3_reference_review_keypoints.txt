- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to sample x = σ A y + m, avoiding explicit C1/2 computation.
- Replaces CSA’s C−1/2 with A−1 and argues the approximation error is an orthogonal rotation that vanishes as covariance shape converges.
- Provides O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) storage per iteration with rank-1 triangular updates, yielding substantial wall-clock speedups in experiments.
- Builds on delayed-decomposition CMA-ES, restricted/limited-memory variants, and prior direct-decomposition updates; novelty is extension to rank-µ + CSA.
- Strength: clear computational contribution and significant speedups (up to 20× at d=64) while preserving evaluation counts.
- Strength: modification is constrained—sampling and covariance learning remain faithful to CMA-ES; only CSA is approximated.
- Strength: theoretical insight via lemma showing convergence of rotation factors and diminishing CSA norm error if normalized covariance shape converges.
- Strength: provides practical implementation details (Algorithm 2) and uses BLAS/LAPACK primitives.
- Strength: broad empirical evaluation on rotated and ill-conditioned functions, multiple dimensions, 100 trials each.
- Factual error: claim that diagonal entries of triangular Cholesky are square roots of eigenvalues is incorrect and must be corrected.
- Theoretical weakness: CSA approximation justification is heuristic and lacks explicit quantitative bounds on the CSA norm error during nonconverged/early phases.
- Suggestion: provide an explicit bound relating CSA error to recent rotation drift or empirically track ‖pσ‖/E{χ} distributions across algorithm stages.
- Invariance concern: replacing C−1/2 by A−1 breaks affine invariance of CSA; need quantitative measures of EtETt−1 drift and σ-update impact, not just supplemental tests.
- Experimental concerns: justify and test sensitivity to the square-root recomputation delay used for the baseline CMA-ES since it critically affects runtime.
- Reporting bias: include success rates (e.g., for Rosenbrock where failed trials were excluded) and aggregate statistics (medians, IQRs), ECDFs, and statistical tests.
- Suggest testing noisy functions to evaluate CSA robustness under approximation and comparing directly to LM-CMA on large d to contextualize trade-offs.
- Algorithmic clarity: clarify using triangular solves for A−1 v (avoid implying matrix inversion) and cite standard stable Cholesky update routines and conditioning behavior.
- Reproducibility: request public code link, exact commit, seeds, and experiment configs.
- Overall assessment: technically sound core idea, original and practically significant, but needs correction of eigenvalue claim, stronger CSA analysis, and improved experimental reporting.
- Recommendation: weak accept contingent on addressing the factual error, augmenting rotation/CSA analysis, and tightening experiments.
