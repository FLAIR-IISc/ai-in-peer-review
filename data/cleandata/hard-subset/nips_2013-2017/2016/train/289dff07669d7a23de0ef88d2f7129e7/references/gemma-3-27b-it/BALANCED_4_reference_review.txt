This paper presents a Cholesky decomposition-based variant of the CMA-ES algorithm, termed Cholesky-CMA-ES, aiming to reduce runtime and storage complexity. The core idea is to update the Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix itself, leveraging existing fast update techniques. The authors provide a theoretical justification for this approach and demonstrate its effectiveness through empirical evaluations on benchmark functions. This builds on prior work exploring Cholesky updates in CMA-ES (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015), but extends it to the standard CMA-ES framework, and importantly, attempts a theoretical grounding for the approximation introduced by avoiding a full correction factor in the step-size adaptation.

**Strengths:**

* **Addresses a practical bottleneck:** The computational cost of covariance matrix adaptation is a well-known bottleneck in CMA-ES, especially in high dimensions. This work directly tackles this issue with a theoretically motivated approach.
* **Sound theoretical justification:** The paper provides a clear argument for why the proposed approximation (omitting the coordinate rotation correction) is reasonable and doesn't fundamentally break the algorithm's convergence properties, based on the eventual convergence of the rotation matrix. Lemma 1 is a valuable contribution.
* **Empirically validated speedup:** The experimental results consistently show a significant speedup in runtime, particularly as the dimensionality increases, without sacrificing the number of function evaluations. This demonstrates the practical benefit of the proposed method.
* **Clear presentation:** The paper is well-written and logically organized. The connection to existing CMA-ES literature is clearly established. The algorithms are presented in a clear and concise manner.
* **Optimal Complexity:** The authors correctly identify the asymptotic optimality of their approach in terms of both runtime and storage complexity.
* **Implementation details:** The authors’ decision to implement the method in the Shark library and use LAPACK demonstrates a commitment to reproducibility and efficiency.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited scope of experiments:** While the results are promising, the experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.  The comparison to CMA-ES-Ref is limited to lower dimensions due to computational cost. A more detailed ablation study exploring the impact of the omitted correction factor (particularly for non-converged runs) would be beneficial.
* **Discussion of numerical stability:** The paper mentions the potential for numerical instability with the Suttorp-CMA-ES, but doesn't discuss the numerical stability of the Cholesky-CMA-ES itself. Cholesky decomposition can be sensitive to conditioning, and a discussion of this aspect, along with potential mitigation strategies, would be valuable.
* **Dependence on Lemma 1:** The theoretical justification relies heavily on Lemma 1. While the lemma itself appears sound, its assumptions (convergence of Ct) may not always hold in practice, especially in the early stages of optimization or for highly non-convex problems.  A more robust justification, or a discussion of scenarios where Lemma 1 may not apply, would strengthen the theoretical foundation.
* **Comparison to other alternatives:** While comparisons to CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d and Suttorp-CMA-ES are made, there is no comparison with other dimension reduction techniques like those proposed in Loshchilov (2014, 2015). Discussing these approaches and their trade-offs would provide more context.
* **Lack of detailed error analysis:**  The experiments focus on runtime and number of evaluations. An analysis of the solution quality obtained by the Cholesky-CMA-ES compared to the standard CMA-ES would be helpful to ensure that the approximation does not negatively impact accuracy.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper addresses a significant practical bottleneck in a widely used algorithm.
* The proposed approach is theoretically justified and empirically validated.
* The speedup achieved is substantial, especially in high dimensions.
* The paper is well-written and clearly presented.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The experimental evaluation could be more comprehensive.
* The theoretical justification relies on an assumption that may not always hold.
* The discussion of numerical stability is lacking.
* The comparison to alternative dimension reduction techniques is missing.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper with a novel and potentially impactful contribution. The Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a compelling solution to a real-world performance bottleneck in CMA-ES. While there are some weaknesses regarding the breadth of experimentation and the robustness of the theoretical justification, the strengths of the paper outweigh these concerns. I believe the paper is of high quality and makes a significant contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization.

**Recommendation:**

**Accept with Minor Revisions.**

I recommend accepting this paper with minor revisions to address the weaknesses identified above. Specifically, I suggest:

1.  **Expand the experimental evaluation:** Include results for higher dimensions, and a more thorough analysis of cases where the correction factor in step-size adaptation might be crucial.
2.  **Discuss numerical stability:** Add a section discussing the potential for numerical instability and any mitigation strategies.
3.  **Acknowledge limitations of Lemma 1:** Discuss scenarios where the assumption of Ct convergence may not hold and the potential implications.
4.  **Compare to other dimension reduction techniques.**
5.  **Provide a solution quality analysis:** Compare the solutions found by the standard and Cholesky-CMA-ES on a variety of benchmarks.
