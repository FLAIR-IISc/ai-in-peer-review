

# Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

## Summary
The paper proposes a variant of the CMA-ES algorithm, called Cholesky-CMA-ES, that replaces the matrix square root with a Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity. The authors claim their approach maintains the same performance as the standard CMA-ES while reducing runtime complexity from O(d^3) to O(µd^2) and storage complexity to O(d^2). The paper includes theoretical justification through Lemma 1 and empirical results comparing the proposed method with several CMA-ES variants.

## Strengths
1. The paper addresses a practical computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, which is relevant for large-scale optimization problems.
2. The empirical results (Figure 1-3) suggest the proposed method achieves comparable performance with significant speedups.
3. The authors provide a clear description of the algorithm and its implementation details.

## Weaknesses

### Critical Technical Errors
1. **Fundamental Linear Algebra Error**: The paper states "the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the covariance matrix" (Section 3). This is factually incorrect. For a symmetric positive definite matrix C with Cholesky decomposition C = LL^T, the diagonal elements of L are not the square roots of the eigenvalues of C. The eigenvalues of C are not directly related to the diagonal elements of L. For example, consider C = [[2, 1], [1, 2]], which has eigenvalues 3 and 1 (square roots sqrt(3) and 1), while the Cholesky factor L = [[sqrt(2), 0], [1/sqrt(2), sqrt(3/2)]] has diagonal elements sqrt(2) and sqrt(3/2), neither of which equals sqrt(3) or 1. This fundamental error undermines the paper's credibility.

2. **Incomplete Theoretical Justification**: Lemma 1 is claimed to establish that the error introduced by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} vanishes as the algorithm converges. However, the paper provides no proof for this lemma, only a vague statement. The "proof" consists of a single sentence: "This result establishes that, when Ct converges to a certain shape (but not necessary to a certain scaling), At and thus Et will also converge (up to scaling)." This is not a proper proof and fails to establish the claimed convergence properties.

### Formatting and Technical Inconsistencies
1. **Notation Errors**:
   - Equation (5) lacks proper summation index notation: "pσ,t+1 ≈ √ cσ(2− cσ)µeff t∑ k=1 (1− cσ)t−k σk ŝk" should be "pσ,t+1 ≈ √ cσ(2− cσ)µeff ∑_{k=1}^t (1− cσ)^{t−k} σk ŝk".
   - Algorithm 1 has a typo: "xi,t−mtσt" should be "xi,t - mt" (with a space).
   - Inconsistent notation: The paper uses both "Cholesky-CMA-ES" and "Cholesky-CMAES" (without hyphen).

2. **Asymptotic Complexity Claims**:
   - The paper states "the runtime complexity of the algorithm with no significant change in the number of objective function evaluations" but fails to precisely define the original CMA-ES complexity. The standard CMA-ES has O(d^3) complexity for the matrix square root calculation, not O(d^2) as implied in the text.
   - The claim of "optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity" is misleading. The storage complexity remains O(d^2) for both methods, so the claim of "optimal" storage complexity is unfounded.

3. **Reference Formatting**:
   - References have inconsistent formatting, including "et al\.,? \Q2012\E" which appears to be a LaTeX formatting error.
   - The reference to "Theoretical foundation for CMA-ES from information geometry perspective" has an incorrect citation format.

4. **Empirical Claims Without Evidence**:
   - The paper states "For d = 64 the Cholesky-CMA-ES was already 20 times faster than the CMA-ES-Ref" but provides no numerical evidence or figure reference to support this claim in the provided text.
   - The paper claims "CMA-ES-Ref and Cholesky-CMA-ES required the same amount of function evaluations to reach a given objective value" (Figure 1) but the figure is not included in the text.

### Missing Details
1. The paper fails to explain why the matrix square root is used in the original CMA-ES, which is crucial for understanding the motivation behind the proposed method.
2. The paper doesn't clarify why the approach of Suttorp et al. (2009) can't be directly applied to standard CMA-ES, despite claiming to extend their work.
3. The paper doesn't define what "optimal" means in the context of storage complexity, making the claim unsubstantiated.

## Overall Assessment
While the paper addresses a genuine computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, it is undermined by fundamental technical errors and insufficient theoretical justification. The critical linear algebra error regarding the Cholesky factor and eigenvalues is particularly concerning, as it suggests the authors lack a basic understanding of the mathematical concepts they're working with. The numerous formatting inconsistencies and technical inaccuracies further diminish the paper's credibility.

The paper's claims about asymptotic complexity and theoretical guarantees are overstated and not properly supported. The empirical results appear promising but lack sufficient detail to verify the claims. The paper does not provide a clear theoretical foundation for its approach, and the key lemma lacks a proper proof.

## Recommendation
Reject

The paper's fundamental mathematical error regarding Cholesky decomposition and eigenvalues is unacceptable for a scientific publication. While the core idea of using Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity is reasonable, the paper's execution is flawed and lacks the rigor expected for NIPS. The authors would need to completely rewrite the theoretical section, correct the mathematical errors, and provide a proper proof of Lemma 1 before the paper could be considered for acceptance.
