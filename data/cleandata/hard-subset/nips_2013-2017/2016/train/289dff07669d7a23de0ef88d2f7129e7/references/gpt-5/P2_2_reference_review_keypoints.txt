- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains a triangular Cholesky factor A and updates it instead of recomputing C’s square root.  
- Extends prior Cholesky-update ideas to standard rank-µ CMA-ES while aiming to preserve function-evaluation performance.  
- Main technical issue: CSA requires C^{-1/2}, and replacing it with A^{-1} omits an orthogonal factor Et from polar/RQ decomposition.  
- Authors justify the A^{-1} approximation via Lemma 1: after determinant normalization Et stabilizes as C converges in shape, and cumulative error vanishes due to exponential forgetting.  
- Present an O(µ d^2) per-iteration rank-one Cholesky update (Algorithm 2) with claimed optimal storage.  
- Empirical results report substantial wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) with essentially unchanged evaluation counts up to d=256 on rotated benchmarks.  
- Comparisons include a reference CMA-ES with delayed decompositions and a non-triangular Cholesky approach (Suttorp et al., 2009).  
- Cholesky rank-one update appears correct and efficient; complexity claims are plausible given the loops shown.  
- CSA approximation is justified heuristically and supported by experiments, but lacks formal error bounds or invariance guarantees.  
- Serious factual error: claim that diagonal elements of Cholesky factor equal square roots of eigenvalues is false; diag(L) are Cholesky pivots, not eigenvalues.  
- Consequently, claims like “eigenvalues for free” and enforcing bounds on principal-component variances via diag(L) are incorrect.  
- Benchmark suite and rotated variants are appropriate for testing full-covariance behavior; reporting both evaluations and wall-clock time is valuable.  
- Excluding non-converged Rosenbrock trials biases results; success rates and anytime curves should be reported.  
- Baseline fairness concern: reference baseline recomputed square root every iteration, which penalizes it; decomposition frequency discussion is needed.  
- No experiments under noise, constraints, or real-world tasks; CSA behavior can differ substantially under noise.  
- Numerical stability: triangular-only approach may help, but discussion of conditioning, accumulated round-off, and need for occasional re-factorization is missing.  
- Presentation is generally clear and well organized; CSA approximation and Lemma 1 are explained clearly.  
- Manuscript has typographical and reference formatting issues and needs precision in notation (ω/Ω/O) and QR/RQ terminology/uniqueness.  
- Originality: meaningful extension of prior Cholesky-update work to rank-µ CMA-ES with a principled CSA workaround avoiding polar decomposition.  
- Significance: practically important due to large runtime gains when linear algebra dominates; likely to be adopted by practitioners.  
- Pros: simple algorithmic change, substantial runtime gains, optimal storage, and preserved evaluation counts on tested benchmarks.  
- Cons: incorrect eigenvalue claim, heuristic CSA justification without bounds, experimental gaps (biased reporting, no noisy/real-world tests, baseline fairness), and presentation issues.  
- Suggested improvements: correct eigenvalue statements; report success rates and include non-converged runs; add noisy and real-world experiments; provide ablations comparing A^{-1} vs C^{-1/2} and measure Et; discuss numerical stability and periodic re-factorization; release code.  
- Overall recommendation: Weak accept — practically valuable and empirically strong but needs factual corrections and improved experimental/reporting rigor.
