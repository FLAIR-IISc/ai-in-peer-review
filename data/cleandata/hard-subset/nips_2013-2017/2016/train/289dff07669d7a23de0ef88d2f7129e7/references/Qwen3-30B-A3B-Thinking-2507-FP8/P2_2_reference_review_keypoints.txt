- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity while preserving performance.
- Provides theoretical justification that the error from replacing C^{-1/2}_t with A^{-1}_t vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Achieves O(µd²) runtime for covariance updates and matches CMA-ES in function evaluations, with speedups growing with dimension.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) but extends to standard CMA-ES with theoretical guarantees.
- Strong theoretical contribution (Lemma 1) showing vanishing step-size adaptation error and robust experimental design across dimensions and trials.
- Empirical results show same solution quality as reference CMA-ES with substantially faster runtimes (e.g., 20x at d=64).
- Paper is well-organized and clearly written, with consistent notation, clear algorithm description, and helpful figures and supplementary material.
- Addresses a practical bottleneck in CMA-ES, offering a drop-in, applicable speedup for high-dimensional derivative-free optimization.
- Originality is limited because Cholesky ideas existed previously; the novelty lies mainly in extending and theoretically justifying them.
- Claim of "optimal" asymptotic complexity is somewhat misleading without explicit comparisons to low-rank approaches like LM-CMA.
- Theoretical analysis assumes Ct converges, but CMA-ES lacks guaranteed convergence and the paper does not rigorously analyze convergence of Cholesky-CMA-ES.
- Numerical stability is asserted but not empirically compared to standard CMA-ES.
- The paper does not discuss integration with recent CMA-ES variants (e.g., LM-CMA) or performance on constrained/multi-objective problems.
- Overall assessment: technically sound, empirically strong, and practically valuable despite some overstated claims and limited theoretical convergence analysis.
- Recommendation: accept — significant practical improvement, clear writing, and strong experimental validation warrant publication.
