- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to cut computational cost.  
- Claims theoretical and empirical parity with standard CMA-ES while reducing runtime from O(d^3) to O(μ d^2) (μ = O(log d)).  
- Extends prior Cholesky-based work to the standard CMA-ES with a rigorous justification for the approximation.  
- Lemma 1: error from substituting C⁻¹/²_t with A⁻¹_t in step-size adaptation vanishes asymptotically.  
- Experiments show same number of function evaluations as CMA-ES and up to 20× speedup for d=64.  
- Additional benefits: lower memory use, free access to eigenvalues, and numerically stable inversion.  
- Theoretical analysis is limited to step-size adaptation; no full convergence proof for the entire algorithm.  
- Practical complexity is O(μ d^2) rather than pure O(d^2), so the improvement is slightly overstated.  
- Empirical evaluation is on benchmarks only; lacks experiments on real-world ML tasks.  
- Does not compare against other recent high-dimensional CMA-ES variants (e.g., Loshchilov’s LM-CMA).  
- Numerical stability is mentioned but not explored in depth.  
- Paper is well-written, organized, with clear pseudocode and reproducible experimental design.  
- Overall recommendation: accept — significant, practical contribution to derivative-free optimization.
