- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates lower-triangular Cholesky factor A directly, replacing C−1/2 pσ with A−1 pσ.
- Argues omitting the orthogonal rotation in CSA causes a vanishing error under mild convergence (Lemma 1).
- Provides a quadratic-time rank-µ Cholesky update routine with claimed O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) storage.
- Empirically matches reference CMA-ES in function-evaluations while significantly reducing wall-clock time, with larger gains at higher d.
- Builds on Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel and distinguishes from limited-memory/diagonal approximations by keeping full covariance adaptation.
- Strength: clear motivation—avoiding cubic decompositions is practically important.
- Strength: solid methodological contribution extending rank-one Cholesky updates to rank-µ while preserving C’s update rule.
- Strength: Lemma 1 gives intuitive theoretical support for the CSA approximation; triangular factor argument avoids non-uniqueness issues.
- Strength: asymptotically optimal time/storage for full covariance adaptation; empirical rotation-invariance probed.
- Serious technical error: claim that diag(A)^2 are eigenvalues of C is false—diag(A)^2 are Cholesky pivots, not eigenvalues.
- Theoretical guarantees are limited: Lemma 1 assumes convergence/slow variation of normalized covariance; no error bounds provided for early phases.
- Approximation weakens affine invariance; the degree of invariance loss is not quantified beyond ad-hoc rotation tests.
- Numerical stability concerns: rank-one Cholesky updates can accumulate error on ill-conditioned C; no discussion of stabilization or periodic refactorization and no comparison to standard cholupdate routines.
- Experimental concerns: baseline decomposition frequency and broader regimes (noise, extreme ill-conditioning, very high d) not fully explored; fairness of comparisons uncertain.
- Presentation issues: references/footnotes formatting problems and algorithm derivation/clarity need improvement.
- Questions for authors include correcting the eigenvalue claim, providing bounds/empirical error for replacing C−1/2 with A−1, detailing refactorization/reconditioning frequency, sensitivity to decomposition frequency in baselines, and experiments on noise/strong ill-conditioning and rotation drift metrics.
- Pros for acceptance: practical speedups without loss of eval efficiency, implementable method, useful intuition, and solid empirical evidence.
- Cons for acceptance: incorrect eigenvalue claim, limited theoretical guarantees (especially early phase), insufficient invariance and numerical-stability analysis, and incomplete experimental baselines.
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on fixing eigenvalue claim and strengthening discussions/experiments on numerical stability and invariance.
