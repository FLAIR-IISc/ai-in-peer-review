## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and theoretically sound improvement to the standard CMA-ES algorithm, focusing on optimizing the covariance matrix update process. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a significant reduction in runtime complexity, particularly in higher dimensions, without sacrificing performance in terms of function evaluations. The paper is well-written, clearly explains the motivation and methodology, and provides convincing empirical results. I believe this work represents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization and is worthy of acceptance to NIPS.

**1. Summary & Related Work:**

The core idea of the paper is to replace the computationally expensive matrix square root operation in the standard CMA-ES covariance update with a Cholesky factorization update. This has been explored previously (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause and Igel, 2015), but those approaches had limitations when applied to the full CMA-ES framework.  The authors address these limitations by providing a theoretical justification for why their Cholesky-based update doesn't deteriorate performance, specifically focusing on the impact on the step-size adaptation. They leverage Lemma 1 to demonstrate that the coordinate system rotation introduced by using the Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root becomes negligible as the algorithm converges.

The paper correctly positions itself within the existing body of work on CMA-ES acceleration. It accurately describes previous attempts to reduce the computational burden of covariance matrix updates, including delayed updates, reduced complexity models, and Cholesky factor-based approaches. Importantly, it distinguishes its contribution by providing a theoretical underpinning for the Cholesky update within the *full* CMA-ES framework, and by achieving optimal asymptotic complexity. 

**2. Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Soundness:** The theoretical justification for the Cholesky update, based on Lemma 1 and the analysis of coordinate system rotation, is a major strength. This provides confidence that the proposed method doesn’t introduce hidden biases or convergence issues.
* **Runtime Complexity:** Achieving O(µd²) complexity for the covariance update is a significant improvement over the O(d²) complexity of the standard approach, especially for large 'd' and moderate 'µ'.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results convincingly demonstrate the speedup achieved by the Cholesky-CMA-ES, particularly as dimensionality increases. The plots clearly illustrate the runtime advantage without a significant increase in function evaluations.
* **Clarity of Presentation:**  The paper is well-written and organized, making it easy to follow the methodology and understand the rationale behind the proposed approach.  The use of pseudocode (Algorithm 1 & 2) is very helpful.
* **Practical Considerations:** The discussion of memory footprint and the potential impact of cache size on performance are important and demonstrate a practical understanding of the algorithm's implementation.
* **Connection to existing libraries:** Integrating the algorithm into the Shark library is a nice touch, facilitating reproducibility and wider adoption.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are well-designed and provide strong evidence of the algorithm's effectiveness, the benchmark functions are somewhat standard.  Exploring more challenging, real-world problems (e.g., hyperparameter tuning for deep learning, complex robot control tasks) would further strengthen the evaluation.
* **Impact of Rotation Error:** Although the theoretical justification suggests the rotation error introduced by the Cholesky update is negligible, it would be beneficial to include some quantitative analysis of this error during the optimization process. The mention of analyzing this in supplementary material is good, but including a figure or table within the main paper would enhance understanding.
* **Implementation Details:** The paper mentions using LAPACK for high efficiency.  A brief discussion of any specific LAPACK routines or optimization strategies used could be useful for researchers looking to implement the algorithm themselves.
* **Comparison to other recent advancements:** A short discussion comparing to very recent advancements in CMA-ES (e.g., those focusing on variance reduction or improved step-size control) could contextualize the contribution further.



**4. Originality & Significance:**

The originality lies in the combination of a theoretically justified Cholesky factorization update *within the complete CMA-ES framework* coupled with the demonstration of optimal asymptotic complexity.  While using Cholesky factorization in CMA-ES isn't entirely novel, the provided theoretical analysis and the resulting practical speedup are significant contributions. 

The significance is substantial. CMA-ES is a widely used algorithm, and reducing its runtime complexity will have a broad impact on applications where computational resources are limited or high-dimensional optimization is required. This work could unlock new possibilities for applying CMA-ES to more complex problems.

**5. Arguments For and Against Acceptance:**

**For Acceptance:**

*  Strong theoretical foundation.
*  Significant and demonstrable runtime improvement.
*  Clear and well-written presentation.
*  Addresses a practical bottleneck in CMA-ES.
*  Potential for broad impact across various applications.

**Against Acceptance:**

*  Experiments could benefit from more challenging, real-world problems.
*  Quantitative analysis of rotation error could be strengthened.




**Final Recommendation:**

**Accept with minor revisions.** I recommend accepting this paper to NIPS.  I suggest the authors address the weaknesses identified above by:

*   Adding a figure or table demonstrating the magnitude of the rotation error during optimization.
*   Expanding the experimental section with results on at least one more complex, real-world benchmark problem.
*   Briefly discussing the relationship to recent advancements in CMA-ES beyond those already cited.
