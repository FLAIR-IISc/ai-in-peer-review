## Review of CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, termed Cholesky-CMA-ES, aiming to reduce runtime and memory footprint by utilizing a Cholesky factorization instead of the matrix square root for covariance adaptation. The core idea is to leverage fast rank-one updates for the Cholesky factor and provide a theoretical justification for why this approximation does not degrade performance.

The paper is well-structured and clearly motivated. It begins with a concise introduction to CMA-ES and its computational bottlenecks, effectively setting the stage for the proposed approach. The related work section is relevant and adequately cites previous attempts to address the computational cost of covariance adaptation. The authors clearly articulate the advantages of their approach in terms of both runtime and storage complexity.

**Quality:** The technical soundness of the proposed method appears strong. The authors provide a theoretical argument based on Lemma 1, linking the convergence of the Cholesky factor to the convergence of the covariance matrix. The claim of optimal asymptotic complexity (O(µd²)) is well-supported given the rank-one update approach to the Cholesky factor. The experimental setup is comprehensive, employing a variety of benchmark functions and dimensions. The results convincingly demonstrate a significant speed-up in runtime compared to standard CMA-ES, particularly in higher dimensions, without a noticeable increase in the number of function evaluations.  However, the discussion of potential issues with the approximation (Equation 5) and its impact on step-size adaptation could be more detailed. The statement that the error “will decrease over time” is somewhat hand-wavy and lacks further justification. A more detailed error analysis or discussion on how the convergence properties are affected by this approximation would strengthen the technical contribution.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and easy to follow. The mathematical notation is clear and consistent. Algorithm 1 and Algorithm 2 provide a clear procedural description of the Cholesky-CMA-ES. The explanations accompanying the algorithms are helpful.  However, the discussion of Lemma 1 and its connection to the error introduced by the approximation in Equation (5) could be more accessible. A more intuitive explanation of why the rotation matrices are expected to converge would be beneficial.

**Originality:** The application of Cholesky factorization with rank-one updates to the standard CMA-ES is a novel contribution. While previous work explored similar ideas (Suttorp et al., Krause and Igel), the authors explicitly address the theoretical concerns related to the global step-size adaptation and provide a justification for the approximation. The exploration of the convergence behavior of the Cholesky factor and its relation to the covariance matrix represents a significant addition to the existing literature. The inclusion of experiments exploring the impact on rotation invariance is a welcome addition.

**Significance:** The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm has the potential to significantly improve the scalability of CMA-ES to high-dimensional optimization problems. The reduction in runtime, without compromising the number of function evaluations, is a valuable contribution. This is especially relevant for applications where function evaluation is expensive, such as reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning. The authors correctly identify the applicability of the method in the context of training neural networks via direct policy search. This work could encourage wider adoption of CMA-ES in areas where its computational cost has been a limiting factor.



**Arguments for acceptance:**

*   Novel and theoretically sound approach to improving CMA-ES performance.
*   Convincing empirical results demonstrating significant runtime reduction.
*   Clear and well-structured presentation of the method and experimental setup.
*   Addresses a key bottleneck in CMA-ES – the computational cost of covariance adaptation.
*   Potential for broad impact in areas such as reinforcement learning and hyperparameter optimization.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   The error analysis of the step-size adaptation approximation could be more rigorous.
*   The explanation of Lemma 1 and its connection to error bounds could be more intuitive.
*   The effect of the approximation on convergence properties requires further investigation.




**Overall Assessment:**

This paper presents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm offers a significant improvement in runtime efficiency without sacrificing performance. While some aspects of the theoretical analysis could be strengthened, the overall quality of the work, combined with its potential impact, makes it a strong candidate for acceptance at NeurIPS.  I recommend acceptance with minor revisions to address the concerns outlined above.
