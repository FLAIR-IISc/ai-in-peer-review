- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce complexity to O(µ d²).
- Lemma 1 theoretically justifies replacing C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹ by showing the introduced error vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based approaches (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) with a key new theoretical justification.
- Empirical results: similar number of function evaluations but significant runtime speedups (up to ~20× for d=64) across benchmarks and dimensions d=4–256.
- Tests include rotation-invariance checks and show consistent optimization performance with reduced runtime.
- Strength: strong technical soundness addressing the rotation introduced by Cholesky and providing formal justification.
- Strength: comprehensive experimental validation across multiple functions and dimensions.
- Strength: high practical relevance due to substantial runtime improvements for larger d.
- Strength: clear organization and presentation, with algorithmic description and contrasts to prior work.
- Weakness: theoretical analysis is limited—assumes Ct convergence and lacks bounds on early-stage error propagation.
- Weakness: missing comparisons to more recent CMA-ES variants and latest implementations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).
- Weakness: insufficient implementation detail on numerical stability for ill-conditioned matrices.
- Weakness: scalability not evaluated for extremely high dimensions (d > 1000).
- Overall assessment: solid, well-supported contribution with main weakness being limited early-stage error analysis.
- Pros: addresses a major computational bottleneck, achieves optimal asymptotic complexity, yields consistent runtime gains without extra evaluations, theoretically justified, and easy to integrate.
- Cons: limited error analysis, lacks recent-method comparisons, sparse numerical-stability guidance, untested at very high dimensions, limited discussion of relation to other low-rank methods.
- Recommendation: accept for NIPS.
- Suggestions: expand theoretical error bounds (especially early iterations), compare to recent CMA-ES variants (e.g., Loshchilov 2015), add numerical-stability implementation notes, run experiments for d > 1000, and clarify advantages over Suttorp et al.
