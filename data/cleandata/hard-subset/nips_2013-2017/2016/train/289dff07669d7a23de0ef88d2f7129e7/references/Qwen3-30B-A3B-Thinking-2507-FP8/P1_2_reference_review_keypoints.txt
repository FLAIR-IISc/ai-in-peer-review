- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to achieve optimal asymptotic runtime and storage.
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the approximation error (using A^{-1} for C^{-1/2}) vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Demonstrates experimentally that function-evaluation counts match standard CMA-ES while wall-clock time is significantly reduced, up to 20× for d=64.
- Experiments are comprehensive: multiple benchmark functions (Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers) and dimensions d = 4..256 against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES.
- Extends prior Cholesky-based attempts to full CMA-ES with a rigorous theoretical foundation, differentiating from Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015).
- Paper is technically sound, well-supported, and experimentally thorough.
- Well-organized and clearly written; theoretical sections are dense but accessible to experts, with useful algorithmic details.
- Suggests making the connection between theoretical convergence of rotation matrices and observed runtime improvements more explicit.
- Original and significant: addresses the O(d^3) square-root bottleneck and achieves O(μd^2) runtime, enabling broader CMA-ES applicability.
- Could include a more detailed comparison with Loshchilov (2015) low-dimensional approximations.
- Strengths: strong theoretical justification, comprehensive validation, significant runtime gains, optimal storage complexity, reproducible implementation details.
- Weaknesses: limited discussion of numerical stability for Cholesky updates, experiments capped at d=256, unclear scaling to very high dimensions (d > 1000), relies on Ct convergence assumption.
- Recommendation: strong accept — meaningful contribution with solid theory, convincing experiments, and clear practical impact for ML optimization.
