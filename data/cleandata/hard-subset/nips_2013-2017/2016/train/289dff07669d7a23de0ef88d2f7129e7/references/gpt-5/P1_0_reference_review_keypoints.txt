- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates a triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to avoid repeated covariance decompositions.
- Main technical challenge: CSA needs C−1/2, authors substitute A−1 and argue via a continuity lemma that rotation errors vanish as shape stabilizes.
- Derives a rank-one Cholesky-update algorithm and extends to rank-µ updates with overall cost O(µ d^2), claimed asymptotically optimal for full covariance.
- Empirically matches original CMA-ES in function evaluations and gives large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20× at d=64) on rotated benchmarks up to d=256.
- Outperforms a delayed-decomposition reference and a non-triangular Cholesky update (Suttorp et al., 2009) in experiments.
- Technical soundness: fast update derivation is plausible and consistent with prior work; CSL approximation is the key heuristic.
- Theoretical justification (Lemma 1 + Eq.6) is qualitative, lacking quantitative finite-time error bounds or guarantees when C changes quickly.
- Empirical support is encouraging but needs stronger reporting (success rates, means/medians/IQR of evaluations, statistical tests).
- Baseline fairness requires clarification (delay frequency settings, whether triangular solves were used, parameter tuning).
- Numerical claim error: diagonal of Cholesky does not equal eigenvalue square roots; this statement must be corrected.
- Practical numerical advice: avoid forming A−1 explicitly; use triangular solves for stability and speed.
- Paper is generally well written; CSA exposition and algorithms are clear.
- Some notation typos and incomplete reference metadata; clarify delay parameter defaults and provide precise complexity statements (constants, triangular solves).
- Originality: novel application of triangular Cholesky factor to full CMA-ES and extension of rank-one updates to rank-µ in O(µ d^2).
- Significance: addresses a major runtime bottleneck, preserving full covariance modeling and enabling meaningful use in medium-to-large d settings.
- Pros: solves computational bottleneck without losing evaluation efficiency, clear algorithmic contribution, sensible theoretical argument, strong empirical speedups.
- Cons: qualitative theoretical support only, missing/unclear reporting of key metrics, factual error about eigenvalues, limited experimental scope (no noisy/constrained problems or comparisons to LM-CMA/sep-CMA).
- Suggestions: report detailed evaluation statistics and success rates, add ablations on CSA behavior early in runs, test noisy objectives, use triangular solves and discuss conditioning safeguards, correct eigenvalue claim and clarify baseline settings.
- Overall: strong, practical contribution with minor corrections and stronger empirical/theoretical reporting recommended; reviewer leans toward acceptance.
