- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing costly covariance matrix square-root with Cholesky factor updates.
- Claims optimal asymptotic time/storage complexity O(µ d²), with µ typically logarithmic in d.
- Theoretical justification argues the omitted correction factor has limited impact (supported by Lemma 1).
- Empirical results: similar function-evaluation performance to CMA-ES with substantial runtime speedups in high dimensions.
- Strong theoretical foundation with persuasive analysis of the approximation.
- Practical significance: improves scalability of CMA-ES and includes an implementation in the Shark library.
- Compelling claim of optimal complexity for covariance updates.
- Evaluation uses a well-established baseline (CMA-ES-Ref), enhancing credibility.
- Paper is generally well-written, organized, and clearly explains the algorithm.
- Novel contribution: theoretical justification and extension to standard CMA-ES despite related prior work.
- Weakness: empirical analysis limited to runtime and evaluations; lacks detailed scaling/constant-factor study.
- Weakness: limited discussion of numerical stability and potential issues for ill-conditioned problems with Cholesky.
- Weakness: complexity benefit depends on keeping µ small; impact of larger µ not fully addressed.
- Weakness: storage-optimality claim should acknowledge low-rank approaches that reduce storage at accuracy cost.
- Overall assessment: strong paper with solid theory and convincing experiments; minor areas for improvement.
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
