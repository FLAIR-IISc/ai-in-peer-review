- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce complexity to O(µd²) from O(d³), with Lemma 1 showing approximation error vanishes on convergence.

- Extends prior Cholesky-based work to standard CMA-ES and provides theoretical justification missing in earlier attempts.

- Significant practical contribution enabling scaling of CMA-ES to higher-dimensional problems relevant for RL and hyperparameter tuning.

- Strength: Strong theoretical foundation—Lemma 1 rigorously supports the Cholesky approximation.

- Strength: Clear experimental validation showing large speedups (e.g., 20× at d=64) while keeping function-evaluation counts identical.

- Strength: Practical implementation supplied (Algorithm 1 & 2) and integrated into the Shark library for reproducibility.

- Strength: Comprehensive comparisons against CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES across multiple benchmarks and dimensions.

- Strength: Additional advantages articulated—optimal storage, free eigenvalue computation for conditioning, and numerically stable inversion.

- Weakness: Limited empirical error analysis—paper lacks detailed tracking of the approximation error during optimization.

- Weakness: Limited high-dimensional evaluation—experiments stop at d=256 despite claiming scalability to much larger dimensions.

- Weakness: Insufficient discussion/experimental evidence on numerical stability for ill-conditioned problems.

- Weakness: No direct comparison with other low-complexity or low-rank approaches (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) in runtime and performance.

- Overall assessment: High-quality, well-supported contribution meeting criteria of quality, clarity, originality, and significance.

- Recommendation: Accept—contribution is significant and well-justified despite minor gaps.

- Suggested improvements: add empirical error analysis, include d=512 (or larger) results, compare with low-rank methods, and discuss numerical stability concerns.
