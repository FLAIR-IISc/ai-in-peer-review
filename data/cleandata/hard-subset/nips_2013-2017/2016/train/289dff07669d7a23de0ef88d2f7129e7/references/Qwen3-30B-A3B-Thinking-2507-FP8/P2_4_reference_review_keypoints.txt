- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES replaces matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity while preserving performance; theoretical argument shows the introduced error vanishes as the algorithm converges.

- Technical quality: Lemma 1 rigorously justifies the approximation; detailed algorithms (Algorithm 1, rankOneUpdate) provided; experiments show same function evaluations but up to 20x runtime speedup for d=64.

- Limitations acknowledged: theoretical justification depends on covariance matrix convergence (not strictly true for randomized CMA-ES) and cache effects are noted for very large dimensions (d=256).

- Clarity: paper is exceptionally clear and well-organized, with accessible background, rigorous theory, precise algorithms, and clear experimental visualizations.

- Originality: first theoretically justified approach to using Cholesky factorization in standard CMA-ES, clearly differentiated from prior Cholesky-based variants.

- Significance: reduces covariance update cost from O(d^3) to O(d^2), making CMA-ES more practical for higher-dimensional problems and providing covariance eigenvalues cheaply.

- Strengths: rigorous theoretical justification; achieves optimal asymptotic complexity O(µd^2) with no performance loss; strong empirical runtime improvements; well-written with thorough validation; yields eigenvalues for free.

- Weaknesses: reliance on convergence assumption for randomized CMA-ES; unclear performance for very large-scale problems (d > 256); missing comparisons to recent variants like LM-CMA; "optimal" complexity claim could use tighter justification.

- Overall assessment: strong, novel, and practically significant contribution to CMA-ES literature; recommended for acceptance.
