

# Peer Review: "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

## Summary and Context
This paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) that replaces the matrix square root computation with a Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity. The authors address a known bottleneck in CMA-ES where the matrix square root computation (O(d³)) dominates runtime for large dimensions. Previous approaches either delayed updates (introducing performance tradeoffs) or restricted the covariance structure (compromising performance on non-separable problems). The key contribution is a theoretically justified approximation for the step-size adaptation term that replaces C⁻¹/² with A⁻¹ (where A is the Cholesky factor), showing the introduced error vanishes as the algorithm converges.

The work builds on Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015) who explored Cholesky updates for covariance matrices, but these prior works didn't properly handle the step-size adaptation component of CMA-ES. The authors provide a rigorous theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that the error from this approximation decreases over time, making it suitable for the standard CMA-ES framework. The paper demonstrates significant empirical speedups (up to 20x at d=64) with identical function evaluation counts compared to standard CMA-ES.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Foundation**: The paper provides a clear and rigorous justification for the approximation (Lemma 1), showing that the introduced error in the step-size adaptation vanishes as the algorithm converges. This is a significant advancement over previous attempts that lacked theoretical grounding.

2. **Practical Impact**: The authors successfully address a real computational bottleneck in a widely used optimization algorithm. The implementation is straightforward to integrate into existing CMA-ES frameworks (they added it to the Shark library), and the empirical results clearly demonstrate substantial runtime improvements without compromising solution quality.

3. **Comprehensive Evaluation**: The experimental design is robust, testing on standard benchmark functions across multiple dimensions (d=4 to 256) with rotation invariance tests. The results consistently show that Cholesky-CMA-ES matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while significantly reducing wall-clock time, with speedup increasing with dimensionality.

4. **Clear Presentation**: The paper is well-organized with precise algorithm descriptions (Algorithms 1-2) and clear mathematical notation. The theoretical arguments are presented logically, and the experimental setup is well-described.

## Weaknesses

1. **Theoretical Assumptions**: While Lemma 1 provides a solid foundation, the paper relies on the convergence of Ct to a certain shape (not necessarily scaling). The authors acknowledge CMA-ES is a randomized algorithm without guaranteed convergence, and while they reference Beyer (2014) for expectation convergence in simplified algorithms, a more rigorous analysis for the full CMA-ES would strengthen the theoretical claims.

2. **Complexity Clarification**: The paper claims "asymptotically optimal" complexity, but the actual complexity is O(µd²) = O(d² log d) (since µ typically O(log d)). The paper should clarify this distinction from true linear complexity, as this is a key point for readers assessing the scalability.

3. **Limited Scope of Analysis**: The paper doesn't thoroughly address how the approach interacts with recent CMA-ES variants (e.g., for multi-objective optimization or constrained problems). While not required, a brief discussion would strengthen the broader applicability claim.

4. **Memory Footprint Detail**: While the paper states the storage complexity is optimal, it doesn't provide a concrete comparison of memory usage with other approaches (e.g., how much memory is saved compared to standard CMA-ES with delayed updates).

## Recommendation

This is a strong technical contribution that addresses a genuine bottleneck in a widely used optimization algorithm. The theoretical justification is sound, the implementation is practical, and the empirical results convincingly demonstrate significant runtime improvements without performance degradation. The paper makes a clear, incremental but valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization.

**Pro-accept arguments**:
- Solves a well-known computational bottleneck in a highly influential algorithm
- Provides a theoretically grounded solution with strong empirical validation
- Demonstrates substantial practical speedups (20x at d=64) with no loss in solution quality
- Implementation is straightforward and compatible with existing frameworks

**Con-accept arguments**:
- The complexity claim needs more precise wording (O(d² log d) vs. "optimal" O(d²))
- The theoretical justification relies on convergence assumptions that could be strengthened
- Limited discussion of interactions with recent CMA-ES variants

## Overall Assessment
The paper makes a significant contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. It successfully bridges a gap in previous work by providing a theoretically justified approach to reduce the computational complexity of CMA-ES. The practical impact is substantial, as the speedup directly enables the application of CMA-ES to larger-scale problems where the matrix square root computation was previously prohibitive. The work is well-executed, clearly presented, and makes a compelling case for the proposed method.

The minor weaknesses noted above are not sufficient to outweigh the paper's strengths. The theoretical foundation, empirical validation, and practical relevance are all compelling. I recommend acceptance at NIPS as a solid technical contribution that advances the state of the art in optimization algorithms.

The paper is well-positioned to be useful for researchers and practitioners in optimization, machine learning (particularly reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning), and evolutionary algorithms. The implementation details provided would facilitate adoption in existing frameworks. This work represents a meaningful incremental improvement that addresses a real problem in a widely used algorithm, with clear theoretical and practical justification.
