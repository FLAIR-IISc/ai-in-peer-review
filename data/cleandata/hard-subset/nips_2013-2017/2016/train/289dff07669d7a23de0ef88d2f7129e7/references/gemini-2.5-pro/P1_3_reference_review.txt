### Summary

This paper addresses the primary computational bottleneck of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES): the O(d^3) complexity of decomposing the covariance matrix in each iteration, which is required for sampling new points and for the step-size adaptation mechanism. The authors propose a new variant, Cholesky-CMA-ES, which maintains and updates a triangular Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix directly, reducing the per-iteration complexity to an optimal O(µd^2).

The main technical challenge in using a Cholesky factor directly is the step-size adaptation (CSA), which requires the matrix square root C^(-1/2) to normalize the evolution path. The authors' key contribution is to propose an approximation, replacing the required C^(-1/2) term with the inverse of their maintained triangular Cholesky factor, A^(-1). They provide a compelling theoretical justification (Lemma 1) for why this approximation is sound: as the *shape* of the covariance matrix converges, the error introduced by this approximation vanishes due to the convergence of the associated rotation matrix in the RQ-decomposition.

The paper empirically validates this new algorithm on a suite of standard benchmark functions. The results demonstrate that Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves the same sample efficiency (number of function evaluations) as the standard CMA-ES, while being significantly faster in terms of wall-clock time, with the speedup becoming more pronounced as the dimensionality `d` increases.

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Quality:** The paper is of high technical quality.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   The claims are exceptionally well-supported by both theoretical analysis and thorough empirical evaluation. The connection made between the convergence of the covariance matrix shape and the validity of the approximation is insightful.
    *   The experimental methodology is sound. The authors compare their method against appropriate and relevant baselines, including the standard implementation with delayed updates (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d) and a previous Cholesky-based approach (Suttorp-CMA-ES). Using rotated benchmark functions correctly tests for the desired invariance properties.
    *   The work is complete. It identifies a clear problem, proposes a well-motivated solution, provides theoretical backing, and validates it with strong experimental results.

*   **Weaknesses:**
    *   The theoretical argument in Lemma 1 relies on the assumption that the normalized covariance matrix C̄t converges. While this is a reasonable assumption and is supported by analysis of simpler models, the paper correctly notes that this is not strictly proven for the stochastic CMA-ES algorithm itself. However, the overwhelming success of the empirical validation makes this a very minor point.

**Clarity:** The paper is exceptionally clear and well-written.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   The motivation for the work is established very clearly in the introduction, contextualizing the problem and summarizing previous attempts to solve it.
    *   The explanation of the core technical challenge—the difference between C^(-1/2) and A^(-1) stemming from an orthogonal matrix E—is presented lucidly and is central to understanding the paper's contribution.
    *   The structure is logical, flowing from background, to the proposed method and its justification, to empirical validation. The inclusion of pseudocode (Algorithms 1 & 2) further aids reproducibility.

**Originality:** The work is highly original.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   While the idea of updating a Cholesky factor to speed up ES is not entirely new (e.g., Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause & Igel, 2015), this paper is the first to successfully apply it to the standard, modern CMA-ES without compromising its performance.
    *   The key novelty lies in identifying the precise source of the problem (the step-size update) and proposing a theoretically justified approximation. Previous works either applied to simpler ES variants or used non-triangular decompositions, which the authors argue can have convergence issues. This paper's approach is more robust and directly applicable to the most widely used version of CMA-ES.
    *   The theoretical justification via Lemma 1 is a novel and significant contribution that elevates the method from a clever hack to a principled approximation.

**Significance:** The paper's contribution is highly significant and impactful.

*   **Strengths:**
    *   CMA-ES is a cornerstone algorithm for derivative-free optimization with widespread applications in machine learning (e.g., reinforcement learning, hyperparameter tuning). Any substantial improvement to its efficiency is therefore of great importance to the community.
    *   The reported speedups are dramatic (e.g., 20x faster for d=64), making CMA-ES viable for significantly larger-scale problems than was previously practical. This directly advances the state of the art and expands the domain of problems where CMA-ES can be effectively applied.
    *   Given the superior performance and lack of any discernible downside in sample efficiency, the proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES is very likely to become the new de facto standard implementation of CMA-ES. The ideas are highly likely to be used and built upon by both researchers and practitioners.

### Arguments for and against acceptance

**Arguments for acceptance:**

1.  **Solves a Major, Long-Standing Problem:** The paper effectively solves the primary computational bottleneck of one of the most important algorithms in derivative-free optimization.
2.  **Impressive and Unambiguous Results:** The empirical results are compelling, showing a massive reduction in runtime with virtually no change in the number of function evaluations required for convergence.
3.  **Strong Theoretical Grounding:** The proposed approximation is not just a heuristic; it is supported by a solid theoretical argument that explains why it should work well, which is then confirmed empirically.
4.  **High Potential for Impact:** This work is likely to be immediately adopted by the community and will become the standard way to implement CMA-ES, enabling its application to higher-dimensional problems.
5.  **Excellent Presentation:** The paper is a model of clarity, making a sophisticated technical argument accessible and easy to follow.

**Arguments against acceptance:**

*   I can find no significant arguments against accepting this paper. The one minor limitation—that the theoretical justification rests on an assumption of convergence—is acknowledged by the authors and is overwhelmingly outweighed by the paper's strengths and the strength of the empirical results.

### Overall Recommendation

This is an excellent paper that presents a significant and practical improvement to a foundational algorithm. It is technically sound, novel, clearly written, and the results are highly impactful. I strongly recommend acceptance.
