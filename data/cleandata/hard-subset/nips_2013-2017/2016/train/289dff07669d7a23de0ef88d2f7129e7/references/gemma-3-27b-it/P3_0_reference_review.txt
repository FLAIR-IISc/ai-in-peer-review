## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Overall Assessment:** This paper presents a compelling and theoretically sound improvement to the standard CMA-ES algorithm, specifically addressing the computational bottleneck of covariance matrix updates. The Cholesky-CMA-ES variant proposed offers significant runtime improvements, particularly in higher dimensions, without sacrificing performance in terms of function evaluations. While the empirical validation is solid, the paper would benefit from a more in-depth discussion of potential limitations and a more detailed comparison with alternative approaches beyond those presented. I recommend **acceptance** with minor revisions.

**1. Summary & Relation to Prior Work:**

The CMA-ES is a powerful derivative-free optimization algorithm, but its computational cost scales poorly with dimensionality due to the frequent need for covariance matrix square root (or inverse) computations. This paper tackles this issue by proposing to directly update the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix instead of the matrix itself. This builds on earlier work by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause & Igel (2015) which explored Cholesky-based updates for specific CMA-ES variations. However, the crucial novelty here lies in providing a *theoretical justification* for why this approach doesn’t degrade performance in the standard CMA-ES setting, and demonstrating the resulting optimal asymptotic complexity.  The paper clearly positions itself within the landscape of existing approaches – infrequent updates (Hansen & Ostermeier, 2001), reduced model complexity (Poland & Zell, 2001; Ros & Hansen, 2008), and Cholesky factor updates – and convincingly argues for the superiority of its method in terms of both computational cost and theoretical guarantees.

**2. Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Soundness:** The core contribution of this paper is a rigorous theoretical justification for using the Cholesky decomposition in the standard CMA-ES, addressing a crucial gap in previous work. The Lemma 1, demonstrating the convergence of the rotation matrix, is particularly strong and provides confidence in the validity of the approach.
* **Computational Efficiency:** The demonstrated reduction in runtime complexity to O(µd²) is significant, especially for high-dimensional problems. The empirical results (Figures 1 & 2) clearly show the performance gains over existing CMA-ES variants.
* **Optimal Storage:** The method’s optimal storage complexity is a practical advantage, particularly for large-scale problems where memory constraints can be a limiting factor.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the motivation, methodology, and results. The connection to existing CMA-ES formulations is well-maintained.
* **Thorough Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments cover a diverse set of benchmark functions and varying dimensionalities, providing strong evidence for the method's effectiveness.

**3. Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Comparison:**  While the comparison to CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, and Suttorp-CMA-ES is helpful, the paper could benefit from a more detailed comparison to other state-of-the-art derivative-free optimization algorithms, such as DIRECT or Nelder-Mead (especially for lower dimensions).  This would help contextualize the benefits of Cholesky-CMA-ES more broadly.
* **Practical Considerations of Lemma 1:** While Lemma 1 provides a strong theoretical basis, the discussion of its applicability in practice could be more nuanced. The convergence of *Et* is predicated on certain conditions regarding *Ct*.  A discussion of how robust the algorithm is to deviations from these conditions would be valuable.
* **Error Analysis:**  The authors acknowledge the error introduced by replacing *st* with *ŝt* in the step-size adaptation. A more detailed analysis of the magnitude of this error and its impact on convergence, particularly in challenging landscapes, would strengthen the paper. Perhaps some experiments demonstrating the influence of this approximation on the convergence rate?
* **Cache Effects:** The authors briefly mention cache effects at d=256, but a more detailed discussion of the memory access patterns and potential optimizations to mitigate these effects would be beneficial, especially given the emphasis on computational efficiency.




**4. Arguments for and against Acceptance:**

**Pro-Acceptance:**

*  Addresses a significant bottleneck in CMA-ES, making it more practical for high-dimensional problems.
*  Provides a strong theoretical foundation for the proposed method.
*  Demonstrates substantial empirical performance gains.
*  The clarity of presentation and well-structured experimentation.

**Con-Acceptance:**

*  Comparison to a wider range of optimization algorithms would provide better context.
*  Further analysis of the approximation error in step-size adaptation is needed.
*  More in-depth discussion of practical limitations and potential optimizations.



**5. Recommendation & Suggestions for Revision:**

I recommend **acceptance** of this paper with minor revisions.  Specifically, I suggest the following:

* **Expand the related work section:**  Briefly discuss relevant algorithms outside of the CMA-ES family, particularly DIRECT and Nelder-Mead.
* **Further Error Analysis:** Provide a more detailed discussion of the error introduced by the approximation in step-size adaptation. Consider adding experiments illustrating the impact of this approximation.
* **Discuss Robustness:** Elaborate on the robustness of the algorithm to deviations from the conditions required for Lemma 1 to hold.
* **Memory Access Patterns:**  Expand the discussion of memory access patterns and potential optimizations to mitigate cache effects, especially at higher dimensions.



In conclusion, this paper presents a significant contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The Cholesky-CMA-ES variant is a valuable addition to the CMA-ES toolkit, offering substantial improvements in computational efficiency without compromising performance. Addressing the minor revisions suggested above will further strengthen the paper and solidify its impact.
