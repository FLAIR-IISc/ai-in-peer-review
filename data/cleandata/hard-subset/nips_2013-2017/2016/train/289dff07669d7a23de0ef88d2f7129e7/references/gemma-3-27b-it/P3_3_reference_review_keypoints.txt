- Overall: Valuable, practical improvement to CMA-ES; recommend acceptance with minor revisions.  
- Core idea: Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that directly updates the Cholesky factor to avoid repeated matrix square-root computations.  
- Contribution: Extends Krause & Igel (2015) from (1+1)-CMA-ES to full CMA-ES with theoretical performance guarantees.  
- Theoretical soundness: Provides strong theoretical justification (Lemma 1) addressing approximation errors in step-size adaptation.  
- Computational efficiency: Achieves O(µd²) complexity with substantial empirical speedups (e.g., ~20x at d=64).  
- Optimal storage: Reduces memory footprint by storing the Cholesky factor instead of full covariance.  
- Presentation quality: Well-written, consistent notation, clear algorithm description.  
- Experiments: Thorough benchmarking across functions including rotated tests; runtime and eval counts reported.  
- Numerical stability: Limited discussion of potential instability from repeated Cholesky updates and mitigation strategies.  
- Cache and memory effects: Insufficient analysis of cache behavior and memory-access optimizations (noted at d=256).  
- Comparisons: Lacks broader comparison with recent memory-efficient/low-rank CMA-ES variants and trade-off discussion.  
- Equation (5) clarity: Justification for Eq.(5) and connection to Lemma 1 is terse and needs expansion.  
- For acceptance: Significant speedup, strong theory, comprehensive experiments, clear writing, addresses practical bottleneck.  
- Against acceptance: Minor gaps on numerical stability, cache analysis, and comparisons to other variants.  
- Specific suggestions: Clarify Lemma 1 convergence assumption, expand Eq.(5) explanation, discuss quadratic complexity implications, report experiment hardware.  
- Future work suggestion: Explore combining Cholesky-CMA-ES with low-rank approximations for greater scalability.
