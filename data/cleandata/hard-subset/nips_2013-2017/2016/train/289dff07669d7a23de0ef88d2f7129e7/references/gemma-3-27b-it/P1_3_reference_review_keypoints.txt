- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update and store Cholesky factor instead of full covariance with theoretical justification.
- Claims optimal asymptotic storage/runtime (O(µ d^2)) and substantial runtime reductions, especially in higher dimensions.
- Maintains same number of function evaluations as standard CMA-ES while reducing wall-clock time.
- Builds on CMA-ES literature and previous Cholesky-update and reduced-complexity approaches.
- Extends Cholesky update approach to full CMA-ES and argues rotation-correction omission is acceptable.
- Properly references related work including delayed updates, reduced models, and spectral invariance.
- Provides a key theoretical result (Lemma 1) on convergence of rotation matrices differentiating it from prior work.
- Empirically demonstrates strong computational efficiency and scaling benefits (Figures 1 & 2).
- Practical implementation in Shark using LAPACK shows feasibility.
- Clear presentation with helpful algorithms (Algorithm 1 and 2).
- Comprehensive benchmarking including difficult functions (e.g., Diffpowers) and comparisons to CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES.
- Insufficient discussion of the assumptions and robustness underlying Lemma 1’s convergence result.
- Notes cache effects degrading performance at d=256 and lacks deeper analysis or mitigation strategies.
- Could compare more thoroughly to other dimensionality-reduction CMA-ES variants (e.g., low-rank adaptations).
- Rotation invariance experiments are relegated to supplementary material without a concise main-paper summary.
- Arguments for acceptance: significant runtime improvement, solid theoretical and empirical support, thorough implementation and experiments.
- Arguments against acceptance: need for more detailed theoretical assumptions and further analysis of cache/high-dimension limitations.
- Overall assessment: strong contribution; recommend accept with minor revisions to expand Lemma 1 discussion and summarize rotation-invariance results.
