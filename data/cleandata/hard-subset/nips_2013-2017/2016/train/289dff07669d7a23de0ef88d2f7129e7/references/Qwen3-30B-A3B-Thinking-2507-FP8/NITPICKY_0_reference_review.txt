

# Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

## Summary
This paper proposes a variant of the CMA-ES algorithm called Cholesky-CMA-ES, which replaces the matrix square root computation with a Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity. The authors claim to achieve O(µd²) runtime complexity for covariance updates (compared to O(d³) for standard CMA-ES) with no significant loss in optimization performance. The theoretical justification relies on the observation that the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root with a Cholesky factor diminishes as the algorithm converges. The paper includes empirical results demonstrating significant speedups for higher-dimensional problems.

## Strengths
The paper addresses a genuine computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, which has been recognized as a key limitation for large-scale applications. The proposed approach is theoretically grounded, and the experimental results show a clear speedup with no degradation in function evaluations. The paper also provides a thoughtful comparison with related work, including Suttorp et al.'s approach, and demonstrates the practical benefits of the method. The authors correctly identify the importance of the Cholesky factor for obtaining eigenvalues of the covariance matrix, which is a useful practical benefit.

## Weaknesses
Despite the promising contribution, the paper contains numerous minor technical inconsistencies that undermine its quality and should be considered for rejection:

1. **Critical Algorithmic Error**: In Algorithm 1, the line "pc,t+1 = (1− cc)pc,t + √ cc(2− cc)µeff mt+1−mtσt" is missing the division by σt. This is a fundamental error that would break the algorithm implementation.

2. **Inconsistent Notation**: The paper uses both "Ct" and "C_t" for the covariance matrix, and "mt+1−mt" versus "(mt+1 - mt)" throughout. This inconsistency makes the paper harder to follow.

3. **Typographical Errors in Key Equations**: Equation (5) should be "pσ,t+1 ≈ √ cσ(2− cσ)µeff ∑_{k=1}^t (1− cσ)^{t−k} σ_k ŝ_k" but has a typo with "σk" instead of "σ_k". Similarly, Equation (6) has inconsistent spacing and notation.

4. **Algorithm 2 Implementation Error**: In Algorithm 2, the variable "b" is initialized to 1 but never used or updated after the initialization, making it an unnecessary variable.

5. **Formatting Errors in References**: The reference list contains numerous formatting errors, such as "Akimoto et al.,? \Q2012\E" and "Hansen and Ostermeier.,? \Q2001\E", suggesting a lack of proofreading.

6. **Missing Proof for Optimality Claim**: The paper claims "the O(µd²) complexity of the update in the Cholesky-CMAES is asymptotically optimal" but provides no proof of this claim.

7. **Theoretical Justification Gaps**: The paper states "we can expect this error to be small" but provides no formal error bounds or convergence guarantees for the approximation.

8. **Inconsistent Citation Format**: Some citations include "et al." while others don't, and some references have incomplete information (e.g., missing years).

9. **Experimental Details**: The paper mentions "for d = 256 the matrix size exceeded the L2 cache" but doesn't specify the L2 cache size on the experimental machines, which is important for reproducibility.

10. **Algorithm 1 Error**: The line "At+1 ← rankOneUpdate(At+1, c1, pc,t+1)" should likely be "At+1 ← rankOneUpdate(At+1, c1, pc,t+1/σt)" since the rankOneUpdate function expects a normalized vector.

## Overall Assessment
The paper presents a technically interesting modification to CMA-ES with potential practical benefits. However, the numerous minor technical inconsistencies and omissions significantly undermine the paper's quality. As a perfectionist reviewer, I find these errors concerning because they suggest a lack of attention to detail that is expected in NIPS submissions. The missing division in Algorithm 1 is particularly troubling as it would lead to incorrect implementations.

While the contribution is potentially valuable, the paper's current state does not meet the quality standards expected at NIPS. The errors are not conceptual but rather in the presentation and technical execution, which is especially important for an algorithmic paper where precise implementation details matter.

## Recommendation
**Reject**

The paper has a potentially valuable contribution but is marred by numerous minor technical errors and inconsistencies that would make it difficult for readers to implement or verify the claims. These errors, while not affecting the core idea, indicate a lack of the meticulousness expected in a top-tier conference submission. The authors should address these issues before resubmitting.
