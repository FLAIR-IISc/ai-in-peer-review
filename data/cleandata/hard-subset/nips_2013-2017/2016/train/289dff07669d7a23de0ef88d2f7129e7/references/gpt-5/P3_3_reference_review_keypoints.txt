- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) and samples x = m + σ A y to avoid eigendecompositions.  
- Achieves O(µ d^2) time per iteration, optimal storage, and large wall-clock speedups in experiments (up to ~20× at d=64) with similar function-evaluation counts to reference CMA-ES.  
- Uses A^{-1} in place of C^{-1/2} for CSA and argues the missing orthogonal factor E varies slowly so the induced error decays via exponential averaging.  
- Provides a lemma showing orthogonal factors E_t converge under mild assumptions, giving asymptotic justification for the CSA approximation.  
- Cholesky rank-one update and rank-µ updates are standard and correctly used; complexity claims for these updates are reasonable.  
- The CSA asymptotic argument is plausible but weak: it guarantees vanishing error only if normalized covariance converges and lacks quantitative transient bounds when C changes rapidly.  
- Major factual error: claim that Cholesky diagonal entries equal square roots of eigenvalues is false in general and must be removed or corrected.  
- Modified CSA may affect rotational/affine invariance; empirical eval counts look unchanged but invariance/drift effects need clearer analysis and stress tests where C changes rapidly.  
- Baseline comparisons omit modern active CMA-ES variants (negative weights); handling downdates (β < 0) is not addressed but important for fair comparison.  
- Reproducibility issues: missing critical implementation details (frequency/strategy for computing A^{-1}, numerical stabilization, SPD safeguards) despite pseudocode and LAPACK mention.  
- Clarity issues: fix typesetting, clarify amortized complexity of reference eigendecomposition, when/how A^{-1} is computed, and explicit statement about support for negative-weight updates.  
- Novelty: updating triangular Cholesky factor for µ > 1 with CSA approximation is new relative to prior work and critique of non-triangular factorizations is insightful.  
- Significance: addresses practical bottleneck for CMA-ES, valuable when function evaluations are cheap and d is moderate-to-large, but limited by lack of active-CMA support and weak theory.  
- Pros: simple, practical idea; solid engineering with O(µ d^2) updates and optimal storage; persuasive empirical speedups without extra evaluations.  
- Cons: incorrect eigenvalue claim, CSA justification only asymptotic with no quantitative bounds, no active CMA-ES comparison, no treatment of chol-downdates, limited experimental scope (no noise/constraints; omitted failures).  
- Suggested fixes: remove/correct eigenvalue claim and propose conditioning monitors; provide theoretical or empirical bounds/diagnostics for CSA error; implement and compare active CMA-ES with stable chol-downdates.  
- Suggested experiments/reporting: include success rates, noisy/robustness tests, stress tests with rapidly changing covariance (e.g., rotated Rosenbrock/ridges), and move rotation-drift analysis to main text.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept (6/10) conditional on correcting the eigenvalue claim and acknowledging limitations; otherwise borderline.
