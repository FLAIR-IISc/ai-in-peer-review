## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a modification to the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES) algorithm, dubbed Cholesky-CMA-ES, which leverages the Cholesky factorization of the covariance matrix to achieve improved computational efficiency. The core idea is to update the Cholesky factor directly, avoiding the costly recomputation of the matrix square root in each iteration. The authors provide a theoretical justification for this approach, arguing that it doesn't deteriorate performance, and demonstrate empirically that it reduces runtime, particularly in higher dimensions, without significantly increasing the number of function evaluations.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Justification:** The paper provides a compelling theoretical argument for why replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor should not negatively impact convergence, focusing on the behavior of the step-size adaptation. Lemma 1 is a valuable result strengthening this claim.
* **Practical Improvement:** The experimental results clearly demonstrate a significant speedup in runtime, especially as dimensionality increases, confirming the practical benefits of the proposed approach.  The authors correctly identify the scaling benefits as being particularly useful for large-scale optimization problems.
* **Optimal Complexity:**  The achieved O(µd<sup>2</sup>) complexity for the covariance update is indeed an improvement over the standard CMA-ES, and the authors correctly point out its asymptotic optimality.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and clearly explains the core concepts. The connection to existing work on Cholesky updates is well established.
* **Thorough Comparison:** The inclusion of several baseline algorithms (CMA-ES-Ref, CMA-ES/d, Suttorp-CMA-ES) provides a comprehensive evaluation of the proposed approach.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Limited Scope of Experiments:** While the experiments are solid, the benchmark functions are fairly standard.  Exploring more challenging and realistic problem domains (e.g., hyperparameter optimization in deep learning, reinforcement learning tasks) would strengthen the demonstration of the method's practical value.
* **Discussion of Error Introduced:** While the authors acknowledge the error introduced by approximating the inverse covariance matrix, the discussion of its impact on performance feels somewhat brief. A more in-depth analysis of how this error manifests in practice, and potentially ways to mitigate it, would be beneficial.  The mention of the error vanishing due to exponential weighting is good, but quantifying this effect would add further rigor.
* **Cache Effects:** The paper notes the performance degradation at d=256 due to exceeding the L2 cache. While acknowledged, this suggests a limitation of the approach for extremely high-dimensional problems, and a discussion of potential mitigation strategies (e.g., block-wise updates) could be included.
* **Lack of Ablation Study:** It would be interesting to see an ablation study assessing the contribution of the different components of the algorithm – specifically, the impact of the approximation in the step-size adaptation.



**Qualitative Evaluation:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical analysis appears rigorous, and the experimental results support the claims. The work seems complete and well-considered.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clear and well-organized.  The mathematical notation is standard and the explanations are accessible.
* **Originality:** The idea of directly updating the Cholesky factor in CMA-ES is not entirely new (as acknowledged by the authors), but the theoretical justification and the comprehensive experimental evaluation constitute a significant contribution. Extending previous work to the standard CMA-ES and providing a solid theoretical backing are novel aspects.
* **Significance:** The potential speedup offered by this method is significant, especially for large-scale optimization problems. The improved efficiency could broaden the applicability of CMA-ES to domains where its computational cost was previously prohibitive.



**Arguments for/against acceptance:**

**Pro:**

* Significant runtime improvement with minimal impact on solution quality.
* Solid theoretical justification for the proposed approach.
* Well-written and clearly presented.
* Addresses a practical bottleneck in CMA-ES.

**Con:**

* Limited scope of experimental evaluation.
* Brief discussion of the impact of the approximation introduced.
* Potential limitations for extremely high-dimensional problems.



**Overall Assessment:**

I believe this paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a practical and theoretically sound improvement to the standard CMA-ES algorithm, addressing a key computational bottleneck. While the experimental evaluation could be broadened, the existing results are convincing and the theoretical justification is strong. 

**Recommendation:** **Accept with minor revisions.**  I recommend accepting this paper, with the suggestion that the authors expand the experimental evaluation to include more challenging real-world problems and provide a more detailed analysis of the error introduced by the Cholesky factorization approximation.
