- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: update and store the Cholesky factor instead of the full covariance to reduce runtime and memory while preserving performance.  
- Theoretical analysis focuses on coordinate rotations and step-size adaptation; Lemma 1 shows the Cholesky update error diminishes as the algorithm converges.  
- Empirical results show substantial runtime speedups at higher dimensions without increasing function evaluations.  
- Builds on prior CMA-ES work and prior Cholesky-update efforts, refining Krause & Igel (2015) from (1+1) to full CMA-ES.  
- Claims improved asymptotic complexity (O(µ d²) in relevant regimes) and reduced memory by storing only the triangular factor.  
- Provides a practical implementation in the Shark library for community use.  
- Strength: strong theoretical justification addressing concerns about step-size adaptation and coordinate rotations.  
- Strength: significant computational efficiency gains, especially in high dimensions.  
- Strength: reduced memory footprint via triangular Cholesky storage.  
- Strength: clear presentation of Cholesky decomposition and its relation to the covariance square root.  
- Strength: empirical validation consistently demonstrates runtime benefits without loss in optimization performance.  
- Weakness: limited discussion of numerical stability and potential issues from repeated Cholesky updates.  
- Weakness: benchmark suite is standard and may not capture ill-conditioned or real-world problems (e.g., RL, hyperparameter tuning).  
- Weakness: reliance on LAPACK raises portability and licensing considerations that aren’t fully discussed.  
- Weakness: “asymptotically optimal” time complexity claim depends on matrix multiplication algorithm; needs fuller discussion.  
- Pro-acceptance points: technically sound, theoretically justified, practically beneficial, and well-implemented with solid experiments.  
- Contra points: theoretical analysis can be hard to follow for some readers and benchmarking scope is limited.  
- Overall assessment: strong, valuable contribution to derivative-free optimization with minor issues that don’t outweigh benefits.  
- Recommendation: Strong Accept.
