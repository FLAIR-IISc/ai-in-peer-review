This paper presents a modification to the CMA-ES algorithm, termed Cholesky-CMA-ES, aiming to reduce runtime and memory complexity by updating the Cholesky factor instead of the covariance matrix directly. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the approach and empirical results demonstrating its effectiveness, particularly in higher dimensions. This builds upon previous work attempting similar optimizations (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause and Igel, 2015) but specifically addresses limitations of those approaches within the original CMA-ES framework.

**Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The core idea of utilizing the Cholesky factorization for updating the covariance is well-motivated, and the theoretical justification, particularly Lemma 1 regarding the convergence of the rotation matrix, is a valuable contribution. The analysis of complexity—achieving O(µd<sup>2</sup>)—is accurate and significant. The experimental setup is reasonably comprehensive, comparing the proposed method to established CMA-ES variants across a range of benchmark functions and dimensions. The authors acknowledge limitations (scaling with d<sup>2</sup>, L2 cache issues at high dimensions) and discuss potential future directions. However, the theoretical justification, while present, could benefit from further elaboration. The connection between the error introduced by approximating *s<sub>t</sub>* and its impact on overall convergence isn’t fully explored beyond the Lemma, leaving some question about the robustness of the approximation in practice.

**Clarity:** The paper is generally well-written and organized. The background section effectively introduces the CMA-ES algorithm and the motivation for considering Cholesky factorization. Algorithm 1 and 2 are clear and concise. However, the theoretical arguments in Section 3 are dense and might be challenging for readers not intimately familiar with the underlying mathematical concepts. The jump between equations (5) and (6), explaining the justification for the approximation, is a bit abrupt and could be smoothed out with more detailed explanation. The experimental section is clearly presented, and the figures are informative, although the axis labels could be made more prominent.

**Originality:** The work presents a novel combination of existing techniques—applying Cholesky factorization updates within the standard CMA-ES framework—while also offering a theoretical justification that differentiates it from previous attempts (Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause and Igel, 2015).  The analysis of the error introduced by approximating *s<sub>t</sub>* and the corresponding Lemma appear to be original contributions.  The paper demonstrates a clear understanding of the existing literature and positions itself effectively within it. The extension to the full CMA-ES (beyond the (1+1) case considered in Krause and Igel, 2015) is a significant improvement.

**Significance:** The results are important. A reduction in runtime complexity, especially for high-dimensional optimization problems, is a valuable contribution. The demonstrated performance gains—particularly the scaling behavior with dimensionality—suggest that Cholesky-CMA-ES could be a useful tool for practitioners working with large-scale problems in areas such as reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning. While the quadratic scaling remains a limitation, the improvement over existing methods is substantial, and the algorithm offers a practical advantage in scenarios where memory footprint and runtime are critical.  The work advances the state-of-the-art in CMA-ES by offering a more efficient implementation without sacrificing performance.



**Arguments for Acceptance:**

*   Technically sound and well-motivated approach.
*   Strong theoretical justification, including an original Lemma.
*   Demonstrated performance gains in empirical evaluations.
*   Addresses a practical limitation of CMA-ES (computational cost of covariance matrix updates).
*   Clear and concise presentation of the algorithm and experimental results.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

*   The theoretical justification, while present, could be further elaborated to address concerns regarding the robustness of the approximation.
*   The connection between the error in *s<sub>t</sub>* and overall convergence is not fully explained.
*   The quadratic scaling with dimensionality remains a limitation, although acknowledged.




Overall, this is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES algorithm offers a practical and theoretically justified improvement over existing CMA-ES implementations. While there are minor areas for improvement, the benefits of the approach outweigh the drawbacks.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
