- Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing costly matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce runtime from O(d³) to O(µd²) while aiming to preserve CMA-ES performance.
- Theoretical contribution: Lemma 1 shows the step-size adaptation error from using Aₜ⁻¹ instead of Cₜ⁻¹/² vanishes as Cₜ converges (up to scaling), providing novel justification beyond prior shortcuts.
- Empirical validation: thorough benchmarks (Sphere, Cigar, Discus, Ellipsoid, Rosenbrock, Diffpowers; d=4..256) show identical optimization performance to CMA-ES with up to 20× wall-clock speedup at d=64.
- Practical impact: addresses a real computational bottleneck, enabling CMA-ES for larger ML tasks (hyperparameter tuning, policy search) and improving memory locality despite same O(d²) storage.
- Clarity: paper is well-written with clear notation, structured algorithms, and logical flow from theory to experiments.
- Weakness — limited finite-time convergence analysis: lacks rigorous assessment of how the approximation affects convergence rate in finite (non-asymptotic) regimes.
- Weakness — lack of real-world experiments: evaluation is limited to synthetic benchmarks; no demonstrations on actual ML problems like neural network hyperparameter tuning.
- Weakness — hardware discussion inadequate: brief note on L2 cache overflow for d=256 but insufficient analysis of cache and larger-d hardware impacts.
- Weakness — ambiguous optimality claim: statement that O(µd²) is "asymptotically optimal" is asserted without formal proof or justification.
- Weakness — incomplete comparisons: does not compare against other recent complexity-reducing CMA-ES variants (e.g., Loshchilov's LM-CMA).
- Overall assessment: technically strong, novel theoretical justification, and practically effective—advances efficient derivative-free optimization.
- Recommendation: accept.
- Minor suggestion: clarify and justify the µ = O(log d) claim in complexity analysis.
- Minor suggestion: include or at least discuss expected performance on real-world ML tasks (or plan them as future work).
- Minor suggestion: expand discussion of hardware/cache effects and practical utility for larger d.
- Minor suggestion: add comparisons with more recent state-of-the-art complexity-reducing CMA-ES variants (e.g., LM-CMA).
