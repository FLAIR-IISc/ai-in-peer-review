- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates the lower-triangular Cholesky factor A of C = A A^T directly, extending Cholesky updates to standard rank-1 and rank-µ CMA-ES.  
- Main technical challenge: replacing C^{-1/2} in CSA by A^{-1} introduces an unmodeled orthogonal rotation E, which the authors ignore.  
- Theoretical argument (Lemma 1) shows rotation-induced error in the CSA norm vanishes asymptotically if the normalized covariance converges; exponential forgetting makes the error practically small.  
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function-evaluations while substantially reducing wall-clock time; asymptotic update complexity O(µ d^2) and optimal storage.  
- Situates work well relative to prior art, building on Suttorp et al. and Krause & Igel and contrasting with delayed decomposition and limited-memory/diagonal models.  
- Derivation of quadratic-time Cholesky rank-one updates is sound and consistent with prior work; complexity claim is plausible.  
- Main theoretical risk: CSA approximation lacks finite-time guarantees and relies on convergence properties not proven for the full algorithm.  
- Experiments on rotated benchmark functions up to d=256 show equal evaluation efficiency and large runtime gains (e.g., ~20x at d=64), and comparisons to delayed and non-triangular Cholesky methods.  
- Factual error: claim that diagonal elements of triangular Cholesky factor are square roots of eigenvalues is incorrect and must be corrected.  
- Evaluation completeness issues: excluding unsuccessful Rosenbrock trials biases results; should report success rates and ERT per COCO/BBOB practice.  
- Baseline fairness: reference CMA-ES baseline recomputes square root every iteration; should also test recommended delayed eigendecomposition schedules.  
- Need ablations isolating CSA approximation: compare σ dynamics with/without rotation term and quantify rotation error over time (supplementary data should be summarized).  
- Numerical stability concerns: Algorithm 2 needs discussion on conditioning, round-off accumulation, and whether periodic recomputation/reorthogonalization is required.  
- Scope limitations: experiments are synthetic; add noisy functions, constrained problems, and at least one practical ML task to strengthen impact.  
- Reproducibility: provide exact parameter settings, decomposition delay used in baselines, random seeds, and links to code/scripts.  
- Clarity: paper is mostly clear with helpful algorithms, but has typos/formatting issues, an incorrect eigenvalue statement, and unclear µ = O(log d) conditions.  
- Originality: moderately high—novel extension of triangular Cholesky updates to full rank-µ CMA-ES with a nontrivial CSA approximation argument.  
- Significance: high practical significance for full-covariance CMA-ES users due to large speedups without loss of evaluation efficiency.  
- Pros: clear algorithmic contribution, thoughtful CSA justification, strong empirical speedups, and open-source implementation.  
- Cons: incorrect spectral claim, asymptotic-only theory, biased/limited evaluation, and limited main-text analysis of σ dynamics and rotation error.  
- Recommendation: borderline accept to accept, contingent on correcting the factual error, tightening baseline comparisons/reporting, and summarizing rotation-error analyses.
