- Summary: Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factorization, claiming O(μd²) runtime and equal function-evaluation performance while extending prior work with theoretical justification.

- Strength — Quality: Solid theoretical foundation (Lemma 1 shows vanishing error) and thorough experiments showing same FEs and up to 20× runtime improvement (e.g., d=64).

- Strength — Clarity: Exceptionally well-written with clear background, pseudocode, and reproducible experimental setup.

- Strength — Originality: Advances prior Cholesky-based methods by providing theoretical justification for the standard CMA-ES rather than restricted variants.

- Strength — Significance: Practical value for optimization (RL, hyperparameter tuning) by substantially reducing runtime without degrading optimization quality.

- Weakness — Theoretical justification: Lemma 1 is asymptotic but does not fully resolve convergence guarantees of Ct in the randomized CMA-ES; Beyer (2014) reference is insufficient.

- Weakness — Numerical stability: Claim of improved numerical stability via Cholesky is not backed by empirical evidence or detailed analysis.

- Weakness — Limitations: Still quadratic in d; lacks comparison to recent low-dimensional/low-rank approximations (e.g., Loshchilov 2015) for very high-dimensional problems.

- Arguments for acceptance: Theoretically motivated efficiency gains, substantial runtime improvements with no loss in optimization quality, clear and reproducible presentation, and practical importance.

- Arguments against acceptance: Incomplete theoretical rigor for the randomized setting, unsupported numerical stability claims, and missing comparisons to large-scale approximation methods.

- Overall assessment: Strong, valuable contribution with solid theory and experiments and practical impact, though minor theoretical and empirical gaps remain.

- Final recommendation: Accept — recommend addressing numerical stability concerns and theoretical gaps in the final version.
