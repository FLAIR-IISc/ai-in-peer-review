

# Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

This paper proposes a variant of CMA-ES called Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces the matrix square root with a Cholesky factorization for covariance updates. The authors claim their approach achieves optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity while maintaining performance comparable to the original CMA-ES. The paper builds upon previous work on CMA-ES variants (Hansen and Ostermeier, 2001; Suttorp et al., 2009; Krause and Igel, 2015) and aims to address the computational bottleneck of covariance matrix updates.

## Major Technical Issues

The paper contains several critical technical errors that undermine its core claims:

1. **Incorrect Complexity Claims**: The paper claims that Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves "optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity" and "reduces the runtime complexity of the algorithm." However, the runtime complexity is O(µd²), and in standard CMA-ES, µ is typically O(d) (e.g., µ = λ/2 with λ = 4d-5d), making the complexity O(d³), which is the same as the original CMA-ES (which requires O(d³) for matrix square root computation). The paper incorrectly states "µ = O(log(d))" when in fact µ is O(d) in standard implementations.

2. **Lemma 1 Flaw**: The theoretical justification in Lemma 1 claims "ETt-1Et t→∞→ I" but provides no proof of this convergence. The authors simply state this as a fact, which is insufficient for a theoretical claim. The convergence of Et is not established, making the theoretical justification incomplete.

3. **Misrepresentation of Related Work**: The paper claims their approach "comes with a theoretical justification for why it does not deteriorate the algorithm's performance" but fails to clarify how their theoretical justification differs from Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015), who also proposed Cholesky-based updates.

## Formatting and Presentation Issues

1. The paper contains several formatting inconsistencies:
   - In the introduction: "th Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NIPS 2016), Barcelona, Spain." appears to be a template error.
   - Inconsistent citation style: sometimes "Hansen and Ostermeier, 2001," sometimes "Hansen [2015]."
   - In section 3, the paper states "µ = O(log(d))" which is factually incorrect for standard CMA-ES implementations.

2. The experimental section lacks sufficient detail to reproduce results. The paper claims "For d = 64 the Cholesky-CMA-ES was already 20 times faster than the CMA-ES-Ref" but provides no actual runtime numbers or implementation details, making it impossible to verify these claims.

3. The paper states "the storage complexity is optimal" but fails to explain why it's optimal or how it compares to other methods. The storage complexity of the Cholesky factor is O(d²), which is the same as the covariance matrix, so there's no storage advantage.

## Strengths

The paper makes a reasonable attempt to improve the computational efficiency of CMA-ES, which is a widely used algorithm. The empirical results generally support the claim that the Cholesky-CMA-ES is faster than the reference implementation. The idea of using Cholesky factorization instead of matrix square root is conceptually sound.

## Weaknesses

1. The major technical errors in complexity claims are substantial enough to invalidate the paper's core contribution. The authors claim to have achieved better complexity than the standard CMA-ES, but they've actually misstated the complexity of the standard algorithm.

2. The theoretical justification is incomplete, with a key lemma lacking proof.

3. The experimental section lacks sufficient detail to validate the claims of speedup.

4. The paper doesn't clearly differentiate its approach from previous work by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015), despite citing them.

## Recommendation

I recommend rejection. While the paper addresses a genuine computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, the technical errors in complexity claims are fundamental and undermine the paper's core contribution. The theoretical justification is incomplete, and the experimental results lack sufficient detail for verification. The paper's claims of "optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity" are incorrect given standard CMA-ES implementation parameters.

The authors have presented an interesting idea but have failed to properly analyze and communicate the complexity implications of their approach. The paper would need to be substantially revised to correct these fundamental errors before it could be considered for publication. As it stands, the paper contains multiple technical inaccuracies that prevent it from making a valid contribution to the field.
