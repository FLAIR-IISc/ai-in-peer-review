- Proposal: Replace costly matrix square root in CMA-ES with Cholesky factor updates (Cholesky-CMA-ES) to reduce runtime and memory in high dimensions.
- Theoretical claim: Authors provide analysis (Lemma 1) arguing the Cholesky approximation introduces minimal error and preserves convergence properties.
- Empirical result: Experiments show substantial wall-clock speedups, especially in higher dimensions, with similar numbers of function evaluations.
- Practical relevance: Method can make CMA-ES more applicable to large-scale problems like hyperparameter tuning and RL.
- Strength — bottleneck addressed: Tackles the known computational/ memory bottleneck of covariance adaptation in CMA-ES.
- Strength — theory: Provides a thoughtful theoretical justification for using the Cholesky factor instead of matrix square root.
- Strength — experiments: Well-structured empirical comparison against standard and alternative CMA-ES variants demonstrating speed gains.
- Strength — presentation: Paper is clearly written, logically organized, and accessible.
- Weakness — limited novelty: Updating the Cholesky factor has prior work; main novelty is extension to standard CMA-ES plus theory and experiments.
- Weakness — approximation concerns: Practical impact of the approximation and its effect on convergence rates aren’t fully explored; Lemma 1’s assumptions may be hand-wavy.
- Weakness — experimental detail: Hardware, benchmark specifics, and parameter tuning details are insufficient, making performance gains harder to contextualize.
- Weakness — limitations discussion: More thorough discussion of cases where Cholesky-CMA-ES might underperform standard CMA-ES is needed.
- Qualitative — quality: Technically sound and well-supported, though deeper analysis of approximation effects would strengthen the work.
- Qualitative — originality/significance: Valuable extension with notable practical impact, albeit not a revolutionary algorithmic advance.
- For acceptance: Addresses a practical bottleneck, offers theoretical justification, shows clear empirical speedups, and is well-presented.
- Against acceptance: Core idea not entirely novel, approximation impacts underexplored, and limitations discussion is limited.
- Overall assessment: Reviewer recommends a strong accept, viewing weaknesses as minor and addressable in revision.
