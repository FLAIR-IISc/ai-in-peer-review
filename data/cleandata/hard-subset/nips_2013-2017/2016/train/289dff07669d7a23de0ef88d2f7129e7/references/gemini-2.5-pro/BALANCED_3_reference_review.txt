### Summary of the paper

This paper addresses the computational bottleneck of the Covariance Matrix Adaptation Evolution Strategy (CMA-ES), a widely-used algorithm for derivative-free optimization. The standard CMA-ES has a complexity of ω(d^2) per iteration (typically O(d^3) due to eigenvalue decomposition) for a d-dimensional problem, which is prohibitive when d is large or the objective function is cheap to evaluate. The authors propose a new variant, Cholesky-CMA-ES, that reduces the time complexity to O(μd^2) and storage to O(d^2), which are asymptotically optimal for a full covariance matrix model.

The core idea is to maintain and update a triangular Cholesky factor `A_t` of the covariance matrix `C_t` (where `C_t = A_t A_t^T`), rather than `C_t` itself. While similar ideas have been proposed before, a key challenge has been the step-size adaptation mechanism (CSA), which requires the matrix inverse square root `C_t^(-1/2)`. The main technical contribution of this paper is to approximate `C_t^(-1/2)` with `A_t^(-1)` and provide a theoretical justification for this approximation. Through Lemma 1, the authors argue that as the shape of the covariance matrix converges, the rotational error introduced by this substitution diminishes over time due to the uniqueness of the triangular decomposition and the exponential weighting in the CSA path.

The authors conduct a thorough empirical evaluation on several standard benchmark functions, comparing their proposed Cholesky-CMA-ES with the standard CMA-ES implementation and other fast variants. The results show that Cholesky-CMA-ES requires virtually the same number of function evaluations to converge as the standard algorithm but is significantly faster in terms of wall-clock time, with the speed-up increasing dramatically with the problem dimension.

### Strengths

1.  **High Significance and Impact:** The paper tackles a well-known and critical limitation of CMA-ES, one of the most powerful and widely used black-box optimization algorithms. Its applications in machine learning, such as reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning, are often limited by this computational cost. By providing a principled and effective way to speed up the algorithm without compromising its convergence properties, this work significantly enhances the practicality and scalability of CMA-ES. The proposed method is likely to be widely adopted.

2.  **Novel and Sound Technical Contribution:** While updating a Cholesky factor is not a new idea, the key novelty here is the successful application to the modern CMA-ES by addressing the step-size adaptation (`p_σ`) update. The justification provided in Lemma 1 for approximating `C_t^(-1/2)` with `A_t^(-1)` is elegant and convincing. It correctly identifies that using a unique (triangular) decomposition is crucial to ensure that the rotational component of the transformation stabilizes, thereby making the approximation valid in the limit. This is a non-trivial insight that distinguishes this work from previous attempts.

3.  **Excellent Empirical Evaluation:** The experiments are well-designed and convincingly support the paper's claims. By comparing against the reference implementation (CMA-ES-Ref) and other fast variants on a suite of standard benchmarks, the authors clearly demonstrate the two key advantages: (1) the number of function evaluations remains unchanged, indicating that the approximation does not harm optimization performance, and (2) the wall-clock time is drastically reduced, confirming the improved time complexity. The inclusion of plots showing performance scaling with dimension effectively illustrates the method's benefits.

4.  **Clarity and Organization:** The paper is exceptionally well-written, clear, and easy to follow. The authors do an excellent job of explaining the background, motivating the problem, detailing their proposed solution, and presenting the theoretical and empirical results. The structure is logical and guides the reader effectively through the technical contributions.

### Weaknesses and Suggestions for Improvement

1.  **Scope of Theoretical Justification:** The argument in Lemma 1 relies on the assumption that the normalized covariance matrix `C̄_t` converges. While the authors rightly note that this holds in expectation for some simplified models, they also acknowledge that it is not guaranteed for a stochastic algorithm. The paper would be strengthened by a brief discussion on the potential behavior of the approximation when `C_t` does not converge to a stable shape, for example, on highly multi-modal problems where the search distribution might change drastically. While the empirical results are strong, they are on benchmark problems where convergence is generally expected.

2.  **Minor Inaccuracy in Technical Claim:** In Section 3 ("Properties of the update rule"), the paper claims that "the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the factorized matrix". This is only true if the matrix is diagonal. For a general symmetric matrix `C = AA^T`, the diagonal elements of `A` are not directly related to the eigenvalues of `C`. This claim seems incorrect and should be revised. While the Cholesky factor is still extremely useful (e.g., for estimating the condition number or for efficient inversion), obtaining the eigenvalues of `C` from `A` is not a "free" O(d) operation; it would require an SVD of `A`, which is an O(d^3) operation.

3.  **Application to Machine Learning Tasks:** The introduction motivates the work by highlighting the use of CMA-ES in reinforcement learning and hyperparameter tuning. While the benchmark function experiments are essential for validation, the paper's impact would be even more powerfully demonstrated by including at least one experiment on a relevant ML task. For example, showing the time saved on a direct policy search problem would provide a compelling, practical showcase of the algorithm's utility to the NIPS community.

4.  **Comparison with Suttorp-CMA-ES:** The paper includes "Suttorp-CMA-ES" in its experiments, which is based on a non-triangular Cholesky update. The authors argue theoretically that their triangular approach is superior because it avoids non-converging rotation sequences. However, the empirical results presented do not show a clear performance degradation (in terms of function evaluations) for the Suttorp variant on the chosen benchmarks. A more detailed discussion or an experiment designed to highlight the potential failure modes of the non-triangular update would make the argument for the superiority of the triangular approach more concrete.

### Decision

**Accept.**

This is a strong paper that makes a significant and practical contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. It solves a well-known efficiency problem in a foundational algorithm (CMA-ES) through a novel and well-justified technical approach. The claims are thoroughly supported by solid empirical evidence. The weaknesses identified are minor and can be addressed in a final version; they do not detract from the overall quality and importance of the work. This paper will be of great interest to both researchers and practitioners who use CMA-ES and other evolution strategies.
