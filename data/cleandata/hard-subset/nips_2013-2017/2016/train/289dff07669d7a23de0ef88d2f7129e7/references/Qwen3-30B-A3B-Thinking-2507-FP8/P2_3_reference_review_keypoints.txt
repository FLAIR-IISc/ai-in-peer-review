- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: replace covariance matrix square root with Cholesky factor to update covariance.
- Reduces covariance update complexity from O(d^3) to O(d^2) and achieves optimal storage complexity.
- Provides the first theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing A⁻¹ approximates C⁻¹/² with vanishing error as the algorithm converges.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: standard benchmarks, dimensions 4–256, 100 trials each.
- Empirically matches standard CMA-ES in function evaluations while achieving large runtime speedups (up to ~20x at d=64), with speedup growing with dimension.
- Authors discuss practical limitations, e.g., performance degradation when matrix size exceeds L2 cache.
- Paper is clearly written, well-organized, and reproducible (algorithms and experimental setup detailed).
- Original contribution: theoretical grounding for Cholesky updates in standard CMA-ES, distinguishing it from prior heuristic or variant-specific work.
- Notes importance of triangular Cholesky factor to maintain bijection with covariance matrix.
- Maintains ability to compute covariance eigenvalues for conditioning monitoring—useful for practitioners.
- Minor theoretical gap: assumption that normalized covariance converges (C̄t → C̄) needs more discussion for randomized/non-convex settings.
- Limited empirical comparison with other low-rank or dimensionality-reduction CMA-ES variants (e.g., Loshchilov 2015).
- Strong recommendation for acceptance due to practical significance, solid theory, and empirical validation.
- Suggested minor improvement: discuss how the convergence assumption applies in practice for non-convex problems.
