- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factor to reduce CMA-ES computational cost.
- Builds on prior Cholesky update work but addresses the previously mishandled step-size adaptation term.
- Provides a rigorous theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing the approximation error vanishes as the algorithm converges.
- Demonstrates substantial empirical speedups (up to 20× at d=64) with identical function evaluation counts to standard CMA-ES.
- Implementation is straightforward and integrated into the Shark library, facilitating adoption in existing frameworks.
- Experimental evaluation is comprehensive: benchmarks across dimensions (d=4–256) and rotation invariance tests.
- Paper is well-organized with clear algorithms, notation, and experimental descriptions.
- Theoretical claims rely on convergence assumptions for Ct that are not fully established for full randomized CMA-ES.
- Complexity claim needs clarification: actual cost is O(µ d²) = O(d² log d) rather than true linear complexity.
- Limited discussion on interaction with recent CMA-ES variants (multi-objective, constrained problems).
- Memory/storage claims lack concrete comparative numbers versus standard CMA-ES or delayed-update methods.
- Recommendation: strong accept—significant, practical contribution with sound theory and convincing empirical validation.
- Pro-accept points: solves a known bottleneck, theoretically grounded, strong empirical speedups without quality loss, easy integration.
- Con-accept points: wording of complexity claim, reliance on convergence assumptions, limited variant-specific analysis.
- Overall assessment: meaningful incremental advance in derivative-free optimization with practical impact and well-supported results.
