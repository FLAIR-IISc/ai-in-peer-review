- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES: maintain triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) and update it directly to avoid costly recomputations.
- Replaces CSA's C^{-1/2} normalization with A^{-1}, arguing the ignored rotation E introduces diminishing error as the algorithm converges.
- Provides an O(µ d^2) rank-one update applied µ+1 times per iteration and O(d^2) storage with an explicit algorithmic description.
- Empirically preserves function evaluation counts while producing large wall-clock speedups (e.g., >20× at d=64) on rotated benchmarks up to d=256.
- Builds on prior Cholesky-like and rank-one update work (Suttorp et al. 2009; Krause & Igel 2015) and contrasts with restricted covariance models and LM-CMA approaches.
- Practical contribution is high: implementable drop-in variant with significant runtime improvements for full covariance CMA-ES.
- Sensible asymptotic argument (Lemma 1) and empirical checks support the CSA approximation, but theoretical treatment is limited.
- Serious factual error: claim that diag(A)^2 are eigenvalues of C is incorrect and must be corrected.
- Theoretical support for replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} is heuristic/asymptotic; no quantitative finite-time error bounds or invariance guarantees are provided.
- Experimental methodology issues: excluding unsuccessful Rosenbrock runs biases results; success rates and ECDFs should be reported.
- Baseline CMA-ES tuning fairness is unclear — reference recomputes sqrt every iteration whereas common implementations use delayed decompositions.
- Numerical stability concerns are not analyzed: uniqueness conditions for RQ, need for occasional re-factorization, and enforcing positive diagonals are not discussed.
- Presentation issues: platform/timing breakdowns are sparse, some decomposition uniqueness claims need clarification, and references formatting needs cleanup.
- Originality is incremental but meaningful: extends known updates to multi-offspring rank-µ CMA-ES and addresses a key practical blocker (CSA normalization).
- Significance is high for practitioners needing full covariance adaptation in moderate-to-high dimensions; likely to be adopted in libraries.
- Suggestions: correct eigenvalue claim, report success rates and ECDFs, compare to tuned CMA-ES with realistic decomposition delays, provide CPU breakdowns, study finite-horizon CSA error, and discuss numerical stability/rebuild strategies.
- Pros: simple implementable algorithm, optimal asymptotic update/storage complexity, thorough experimental sweep supporting claims.
- Cons: incorrect eigenvalue claim, limited theoretical guarantees for CSA approximation, and experimental/reporting fairness issues.
- Recommendation: weak accept conditional on correcting the eigenvalue statement and improving experimental reporting.
- Reviewer confidence: moderate.
