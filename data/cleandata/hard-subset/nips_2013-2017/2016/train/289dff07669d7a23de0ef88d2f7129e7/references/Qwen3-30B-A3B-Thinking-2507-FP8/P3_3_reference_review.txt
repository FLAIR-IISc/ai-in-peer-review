

# Peer Review: "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

## Summary and Relation to Prior Work

This paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of the CMA-ES algorithm that replaces the computationally expensive matrix square root computation with a Cholesky factorization to achieve optimal asymptotic time and storage complexity. The authors build on previous work by Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015), who explored Cholesky-based updates for specialized CMA-ES variants, but extend it to the standard CMA-ES with theoretical justification for why the approximation does not degrade performance. The paper demonstrates that the proposed method achieves O(μd²) time complexity (where μ = O(log d)) instead of the standard O(d³) complexity of matrix square root computation, while maintaining equivalent function evaluation counts in empirical tests.

## Strengths

1. **Theoretical Justification**: The paper provides a compelling theoretical argument (Lemma 1) that the error introduced by using the Cholesky factor instead of the matrix square root vanishes asymptotically as the algorithm converges. This is a significant contribution over previous attempts that lacked such justification.

2. **Clear Empirical Demonstration**: The experiments convincingly show that Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves the same optimization performance (measured by function evaluations) as the standard CMA-ES while providing substantial speedups (20x for d=64). The use of multiple benchmark functions across dimensions (4-256) and careful handling of rotation invariance is particularly strong.

3. **Practical Impact**: The paper addresses a genuine computational bottleneck in CMA-ES, making it more applicable to large-scale problems where the standard implementation becomes prohibitively expensive. The authors correctly identify that this approach is valuable when maintaining a full covariance matrix is still feasible but updating it is computationally challenging.

4. **Algorithmic Clarity**: The presentation of Algorithm 1 (Cholesky-CMA-ES) and Algorithm 2 (rankOneUpdate) is clear and well-structured, making the approach reproducible.

## Weaknesses

1. **Overstated Originality**: The paper claims "optimal asymptotic storage and runtime complexity" but fails to sufficiently distinguish their approach from Suttorp et al. (2009) and Krause and Igel (2015), who also achieved O(μd²) complexity for specific CMA-ES variants. The key theoretical contribution (Lemma 1) is important, but the paper should more clearly position this as extending previous work rather than creating a fundamentally new approach.

2. **Limited Numerical Stability Analysis**: While the authors mention that the triangular structure allows for numerically stable inversion, they provide no empirical evidence of numerical stability (e.g., condition numbers of the covariance matrix, convergence behavior with ill-conditioned problems). This is particularly concerning given the sensitivity of CMA-ES to numerical issues.

3. **Insufficient Discussion of Practical Limits**: The paper notes that for d=256, the matrix size exceeded L2 cache, affecting performance. However, it doesn't discuss how this limitation scales or how the algorithm might be adapted for extremely large dimensions (beyond what the authors mention in the conclusion).

4. **Incomplete Comparison with State-of-the-Art**: The paper compares against CMA-ES-Ref (with delayed decomposition), CMA-ES/d (delayed update), and Suttorp-CMA-ES. It misses comparison with more recent CMA-ES variants like LM-CMA (Loshchilov, 2015) that also address computational complexity, which would strengthen the significance claim.

## Overall Assessment

This is a strong technical contribution that addresses a genuine computational bottleneck in a widely used optimization algorithm. The theoretical justification for the Cholesky-based approximation is particularly valuable, and the empirical results demonstrate clear practical benefits. The paper makes a meaningful contribution to the optimization literature, though it could better position itself relative to prior work on Cholesky-based CMA-ES variants.

The paper meets NIPS standards for technical quality, clarity, and significance. The theoretical foundation is sound, the experiments are well-designed, and the results have clear practical implications for scaling CMA-ES to larger problems. The main weakness is the insufficient differentiation from prior work on Cholesky-based CMA-ES variants, which slightly diminishes the perceived novelty.

## Recommendation

I recommend acceptance with minor revisions. The authors should:
1. More clearly distinguish their theoretical contribution from prior work on Cholesky-based CMA-ES
2. Add a brief discussion of numerical stability (perhaps with condition number plots)
3. Include a comparison with LM-CMA or similar recent variants

The paper makes a solid contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization and addresses a significant computational challenge in a widely used algorithm. The improvements in runtime complexity without loss of optimization quality are substantial and well-supported by the empirical results. This work will be valuable for practitioners using CMA-ES in applications where computational efficiency is critical.

**Summary of pro/con for acceptance:**
- *Pro*: Strong theoretical justification, clear empirical validation, meaningful speedup for large-scale problems, well-structured presentation
- *Con*: Overstated novelty relative to prior Cholesky-based work, limited discussion of numerical stability, incomplete comparison with recent variants

The paper advances the state of the art in practical CMA-ES implementation and would be a valuable addition to the NIPS proceedings.
