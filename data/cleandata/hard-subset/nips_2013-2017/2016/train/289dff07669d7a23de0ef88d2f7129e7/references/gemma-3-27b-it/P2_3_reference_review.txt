## Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

**Summary:**

This paper presents a modification to the standard CMA-ES algorithm, termed Cholesky-CMA-ES, which leverages the Cholesky factorization for covariance matrix updates instead of the square root. The core idea is to avoid the computationally expensive recomputation of the matrix square root at each iteration, achieving a theoretically optimal asymptotic time and storage complexity of O(µd²) where µ is typically logarithmic in d. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the approach, arguing that the introduced approximation (avoiding a correction factor in the step-size adaptation) has a limited impact on convergence due to the exponential weighting and convergence of the coordinate system. Empirical results demonstrate that the Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves comparable performance (in terms of function evaluations) to the standard CMA-ES but with significant speedups, especially in higher dimensions.

**Strengths:**

* **Theoretical Foundation:** The paper provides a clear and well-reasoned theoretical justification for the proposed Cholesky-based update. The argument regarding the negligible impact of the omitted correction factor, backed by Lemma 1, is persuasive.
* **Practical Significance:** The speedup achieved by avoiding the square root computation is significant, especially for high-dimensional problems. This makes CMA-ES more applicable to larger-scale optimization tasks.  The empirical results clearly demonstrate these speedups.
* **Optimal Complexity:**  The claim of achieving optimal asymptotic complexity for covariance updates is compelling.
* **Well-Established Baseline:**  Comparing against a strong baseline implementation (CMA-ES-Ref) enhances the credibility of the results.
* **Clear Writing:** The paper is generally well-written and organized, with a clear explanation of the proposed method and its theoretical underpinnings.
* **Implementation and Availability:** The implementation within the Shark library adds practical value.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Empirical Scope:** While the experiments are comprehensive in terms of benchmark functions and dimensionality, the comparisons are limited to runtime and function evaluations.  A more detailed analysis of the scaling behavior (e.g., constant factors in the runtime) would be beneficial.
* **Limited Discussion of Numerical Stability:** While the paper mentions the benefit of using a triangular matrix for inversion, a deeper discussion of the potential numerical stability issues associated with Cholesky decomposition (particularly in ill-conditioned scenarios) would be valuable.
* **Dependence on µ:** The achieved complexity of O(µd²) is contingent on µ being relatively small (e.g., logarithmic in d). The paper should acknowledge the potential impact of choosing a larger µ and the trade-offs involved.
* **The claim of optimal storage complexity:** While the authors claim optimal storage complexity, it’s worth mentioning that there are approaches like low-rank approximations that can offer even lower storage at the cost of potential accuracy. A brief discussion of these alternatives could strengthen the paper.



**Addressing Review Criteria:**

* **Quality:** The paper is technically sound. The theoretical analysis is rigorous, and the empirical results support the claims. The authors acknowledge the approximation involved and provide a rationale for its validity. It's a complete piece of work with a clear focus.
* **Clarity:** The paper is generally clear and well-organized. The notation is standard and the explanations are easy to follow.  The algorithm is well-described.
* **Originality:** The idea of using Cholesky factorization within CMA-ES isn't entirely new (as the authors acknowledge), but the theoretical justification and comprehensive performance evaluation represent a significant contribution.  Extending prior work to the standard CMA-ES is a novel aspect. The work is adequately referenced.
* **Significance:**  The potential impact of this work is substantial. Improving the scalability of CMA-ES, a widely-used optimization algorithm, is important for a range of applications, particularly in machine learning and reinforcement learning. The results suggest that this approach could enable the application of CMA-ES to larger and more complex problems.




**Arguments For Acceptance:**

* Strong theoretical foundation and justification.
* Significant speedup in runtime, particularly for high-dimensional problems.
* Addresses a critical bottleneck in CMA-ES (covariance matrix update).
* Well-written and clearly presented.
* Practical implementation available.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* Limited discussion of numerical stability.
*  Empirical analysis could be more in-depth (e.g., scaling behavior).
*  The theoretical benefits are contingent on keeping µ small.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that makes a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization. The Cholesky-CMA-ES offers a compelling improvement to the standard CMA-ES, addressing a key computational bottleneck while maintaining performance. The theoretical justification is solid, and the empirical results are convincing. While there are minor areas for improvement, the strengths of the paper significantly outweigh the weaknesses.

**Recommendation:** **Strong Accept.**  I believe this paper is worthy of publication at NIPS.
