- Overall: Proposes a theoretically sound Cholesky-CMA-ES that reduces runtime complexity without hurting function-evaluation performance; recommended for acceptance.  
- Core idea: Replace the expensive covariance matrix square root with a Cholesky factor update, using Lemma 1 to show induced coordinate rotation becomes negligible on convergence.  
- Positioning: Situates work among prior CMA-ES acceleration efforts, distinguishing itself by providing theory for the full CMA-ES and achieving optimal asymptotic complexity.  
- Theoretical soundness: Lemma-based analysis argues the Cholesky update does not introduce bias or convergence issues.  
- Runtime complexity: Achieves O(µ d²) covariance-update complexity versus standard O(d²), benefiting large d and moderate µ.  
- Empirical validation: Experiments demonstrate substantial runtime speedups in higher dimensions with no significant increase in function evaluations.  
- Clarity of presentation: Well-written, organized, and aided by helpful pseudocode (Algorithms 1 & 2).  
- Practical considerations: Discusses memory footprint and cache effects on performance.  
- Reproducibility: Implementation integrated into the Shark library to facilitate adoption.  
- Limited experimental scope: Benchmarks are standard; lacks evaluations on more challenging real-world tasks (e.g., deep learning hyperparameter tuning, robot control).  
- Rotation-error quantification: Theoretical claim of negligible rotation error should be supported by quantitative analysis in the main paper rather than only supplemental material.  
- Implementation detail gap: More specifics on LAPACK routines or optimization strategies used would help practitioners implement the method.  
- Comparison to recent work: Needs a brief discussion comparing to very recent CMA-ES advances (e.g., variance reduction or improved step-size control).  
- Originality: Novelty is in the theoretically justified Cholesky update within full CMA-ES and the achievement of optimal asymptotic complexity.  
- Significance: Substantial—reducing CMA-ES runtime can broaden applicability to high-dimensional and resource-limited problems.  
- Arguments for acceptance: Strong theory, clear runtime gains, good presentation, addresses practical bottleneck, and broad potential impact.  
- Arguments against acceptance: Experiments need to include harder real-world problems and a stronger quantitative analysis of rotation error.  
- Recommendation: Accept with minor revisions—add a rotation-error figure/table, include at least one complex real-world benchmark, and discuss recent related CMA-ES advancements.
