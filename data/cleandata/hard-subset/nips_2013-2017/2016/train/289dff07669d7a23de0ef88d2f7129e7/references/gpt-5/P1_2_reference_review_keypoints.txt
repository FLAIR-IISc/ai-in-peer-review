- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that updates triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T) to get O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) storage while retaining full covariance adaptation.  
- Approximates CSA by using A^{-1} instead of C^{-1/2}, arguing rotational discrepancy vanishes asymptotically via convergence of an orthogonal factor in RQ decomposition.  
- Provides a rank-one update routine for A and an algorithm with O(µ d^2) updates and empirical wall-clock speedups vs strong CMA-ES and a prior Cholesky approach.  
- Situates work well among prior speedups for CMA-ES, Cholesky-updating variants, and NES-like methods; novelty is applying triangular Cholesky update to standard CMA-ES with CSA.  
- Covariance update via rank-one Cholesky updates is technically sound and grounded in prior work.  
- Lemma 1 justifies CSA approximation under a convergence assumption, but full stochastic convergence of C is not guaranteed.  
- Incorrect claim: Cholesky diagonal elements are not the square roots of eigenvalues in general — must be corrected.  
- Replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} perturbs rotational/affine invariance, especially during fast transients; more direct analysis/ablation would strengthen claims.  
- Numerical stability concerns: repeated rank-one updates may accumulate roundoff; need discussion of conditioning, re-factorization, and whether explicit A^{-1} is formed.  
- Algorithm/implementation details need clarification: triangular solves vs explicit inversion, BLAS usage, reconditioning strategy, and parameter settings.  
- Experiments convincingly show large runtime gains without extra evaluations on rotated noiseless benchmarks up to d=256.  
- Experimental issues: exclusion of non-converged Rosenbrock runs biases results; missing tests on noisy, constrained, multimodal, and expensive-objective settings.  
- Statistical significance and failure rates should be reported to support “same evaluations” claim.  
- Suggest ablation stressing rapid rotations to probe CSA accuracy, report conditioning metrics, and include noisy/expensive-function experiments.  
- Suggest more comparisons to LM-CMA/low-rank methods at higher dimensions to delineate regimes of advantage.  
- Overall assessment: practically significant and novel extension with strong empirical support; accept conditional on correcting Cholesky/eigenvalue claim and clarifying numerical/implementation details.
