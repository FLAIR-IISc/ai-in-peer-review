- Proposal: Cholesky-CMA-ES updates a lower-triangular Cholesky factor A instead of recomputing the matrix square root, yielding O(µ d^2) per-iteration cost and optimal storage for full-covariance CMA-ES.
- CSA workaround: replaces required C^{-1/2} in CSA with A^{-1}, omitting an iteration-dependent orthogonal rotation and justifying the approximation via a lemma about convergence of normalized covariance shape.
- Empirics: matches sample efficiency of reference CMA-ES up to d=256 on rotated/ill-conditioned benchmarks and achieves large wall-clock speedups (e.g., ~20x at d=64).
- Relation to prior work: appropriately situates itself among diagonal/low-rank/restricted models and past factorization updates, extending Cholesky triangular updates to rank-µ CMA-ES and addressing CSA.
- Strength — motivation: eliminating cubic eigendecompositions is practically impactful for medium-to-large dimensions and cheap objectives.
- Strength — algorithmics: rank-1 updates of triangular Cholesky factors are O(d^2); overall O(µ d^2) per-iteration is optimal for dense adaptation.
- Strength — integration: sampling via A is straightforward and the covariance update matches the original at the C level (modulo numerics); only CSA is approximated.
- Strength — experiments and exposition: empirical support for unchanged sample efficiency and substantial runtime gains; clear explanation of why CSA error should decay as shape stabilizes.
- Weakness — theoretical limits: lemma assumes convergence of normalized covariance shape C̄t, which is not guaranteed for stochastic runs and lacks finite-time error bounds or robustness guarantees in non-stationary regimes.
- Weakness — factual error: claim that Cholesky diagonal entries are square roots of eigenvalues is incorrect unless C is diagonal; "eigenvalues for free" is invalid and must be corrected.
- Weakness — baselines/fairness: reference CMA-ES recomputes sqrt every iteration while practical implementations delay recomputation; need tuned delay schedules, amortized cost reporting, and comparison to LM-CMA.
- Weakness — numerical stability: potential round-off accumulation, downdates, and scaling issues not fully addressed; paper should discuss jitter, reconditioning, periodic refactorization, and relation to standard cholupdate routines.
- Weakness — clarity/details: derivation that scaling plus µ+1 rank-1 updates equals Eq.(2) needs sketch or reference; CSA implementation can avoid forming A^{-1} via triangular solves and should be detailed; hardware/BLAS/cache details missing for wall-clock claims.
- Quality: technically competent and empirically supported, but marred by the incorrect eigenvalue claim and limited theoretical guarantees.
- Originality: novel and practical extension of known Cholesky updates to full rank-µ CMA-ES with a new CSA approximation.
- Significance: high practical significance for CMA-ES users in ML; likely to be adopted despite modest theoretical contributions.
- Author requests: correct the eigenvalue claim, provide finite-time CSA error bounds under reasonable assumptions, detail numerical safeguards, report amortized per-iteration costs and tuned baselines (including LM-CMA), and release code/experiment scripts.
- Pros: significant runtime speedups, simple drop-in replacement, optimal per-iteration time and storage for full covariance.
- Cons: limited theoretical guarantees, incorrect eigenvalue statement, and insufficient baseline/numerical stability discussion.
- Recommendation: lean accept, contingent on correcting the eigenvalue claim and clarifying numerical and baseline details.
