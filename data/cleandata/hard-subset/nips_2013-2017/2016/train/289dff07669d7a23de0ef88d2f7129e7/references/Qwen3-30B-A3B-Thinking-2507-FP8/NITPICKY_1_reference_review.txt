

# Review of "CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity"

## Summary
The paper proposes a variant of CMA-ES called Cholesky-CMA-ES that replaces the matrix square root computation with a Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity. The authors claim their method achieves optimal asymptotic runtime complexity O(µd²) for covariance matrix updates while maintaining performance, with reduced memory requirements. The approach is theoretically justified by showing that the error from omitting the correction factor in the step-size adaptation vanishes asymptotically.

## Strengths
The paper identifies a genuine computational bottleneck in CMA-ES implementation that affects practical performance on large-dimensional problems. The algorithm description (Algorithm 1) is clear and well-structured. The experimental design is comprehensive, with multiple benchmark functions, dimensionality variations, and comparisons to relevant baselines. The paper provides valuable empirical evidence of significant speed improvements, especially for high-dimensional problems.

## Weaknesses

### Technical Inaccuracies and Omissions
1. **Complexity Claims**: The paper states "the runtime of one CMA-ES iteration is ω(d²)" but this is incorrect. The matrix square root computation requires O(d³) operations, not O(d²). This fundamental error undermines the paper's central claim about complexity improvements.

2. **Cholesky Factor Properties**: The paper claims "the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the factorized matrix" (Section 3), which is technically incorrect. The diagonal elements of the Cholesky factor relate to singular values, not eigenvalues. This is a significant theoretical error that misleads readers about the utility of the Cholesky factor.

3. **Matrix Inversion Complexity**: The paper states "a triangular matrix can be inverted in quadratic time" (Section 3), but this is false. Inverting a triangular matrix requires O(d³) operations, not O(d²). This error directly contradicts the claimed complexity improvements.

4. **Missing Proof**: Lemma 1 states a key theoretical result but provides no proof. The proof is essential for the paper's central claim about the vanishing error in step-size adaptation, yet it's omitted entirely.

5. **Incorrect Error Analysis**: The paper claims "the error caused by replacing st by ŝt is small" but provides no quantitative bounds on this error. Without error bounds, the claim that the error vanishes asymptotically is unsubstantiated.

### Methodological Issues
1. **Inconsistent Citation Format**: The references section contains inconsistent formatting with entries like "Akimoto et al\.,? \Q2012\E" and "Hansen and Ostermeier.,? \Q2001\E" which appear to be corrupted citations. This suggests poor proofreading.

2. **Experimental Limitations**: The paper reports speedup factors (e.g., "20 times faster") without providing statistical significance testing or error bars. For d=256, the paper notes "the matrix size exceeded the L2 cache" but doesn't explain how this affects the comparison with Suttorp-CMA-ES, which also uses Cholesky factorization.

3. **Implementation Details**: The paper mentions "we added our algorithm to the open-source machine learning library Shark" but doesn't provide details about how the implementation was validated or how it compares to the reference implementation beyond the reported results.

4. **Algorithm Complexity Claim**: The paper claims "the O(µd²) complexity of the update in the Cholesky-CMAES is asymptotically optimal" but doesn't clarify the baseline complexity it's comparing against. The standard CMA-ES has O(d³) complexity due to the matrix square root, but the paper fails to explicitly state this.

5. **Inadequate Comparison to Prior Work**: The paper references Suttorp et al. [2009] but doesn't sufficiently distinguish their approach from the proposed method. The paper claims "we propose to extend this update to the standard CMA-ES" but doesn't clearly explain how this differs from previous work.

## Recommendation
While the paper addresses a practical computational bottleneck in CMA-ES implementation, it contains multiple significant technical errors that undermine its central claims. The incorrect complexity claims, the misleading statements about Cholesky factor properties, and the missing proof of Lemma 1 are particularly concerning. These errors suggest a lack of rigorous mathematical understanding that is essential for a theoretical contribution.

The empirical results are promising but lack sufficient statistical rigor to support the paper's claims. The paper would benefit from correcting these technical errors and providing more thorough theoretical justification before publication.

Given the paper's fundamental technical inaccuracies, I recommend rejection. While the problem addressed is valuable, the paper fails to provide the rigorous theoretical foundation required for a NIPS publication. The errors are not minor but directly impact the paper's central claims about complexity and performance.

## Pro and Con Acceptance
**Pro Acceptance**:
- Addresses a genuine computational bottleneck in CMA-ES
- Provides clear algorithm description
- Empirical results demonstrate significant speed improvements

**Con Acceptance**:
- Contains fundamental mathematical errors (complexity claims, Cholesky properties)
- Missing proof for key theoretical claim (Lemma 1)
- Inconsistent citation format and poor proofreading
- Lacks quantitative error bounds for the approximation
- Incorrect statements about matrix inversion complexity

The technical errors in this paper are substantial enough to invalidate its central claims, making the paper unsuitable for publication at NIPS.
