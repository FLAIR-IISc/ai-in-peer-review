- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, replacing matrix square root with Cholesky factorization to reduce computational complexity.
- Achieves optimal asymptotic runtime O(µ d²) and optimal storage while maintaining full covariance adaptation.
- Provides theoretical justification (Lemma 1) that replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} does not degrade performance as error diminishes on convergence.
- Addresses the step-size adaptation challenge and argues exponential weighting reduces the impact of the approximation error.
- Thorough empirical evaluation (d = 4–256, 100 trials) showing same function evaluations but large wall-clock speedups (up to 20× at d=64).
- Authors note limitations like cache effects at d=256 and trade-offs for very large dimensions where full covariance may be infeasible.
- Builds on prior Cholesky/efficiency attempts but adds the crucial theoretical foundation missing in earlier work.
- Exceptionally clear writing, precise algorithm descriptions, effective figures (e.g., speedup vs. dimensionality), and useful supplementary material on rotation invariance.
- Original contribution: first principled theoretical explanation for why Cholesky-based approximation does not harm CMA-ES performance.
- Practical benefits beyond speed: free eigenvalue computation, numerically stable inversion, and likely easy adoption due to simplicity.
- Weakness: theoretical justification relies on an assumption about Ct converging to a certain shape and could use more discussion given CMA-ES randomness.
- Weakness: experiments limited to standard benchmark functions; more complex real-world tasks would strengthen practical claims.
- Weakness: no comparisons with other state-of-the-art large-scale optimizers (e.g., L-BFGS, low-rank methods).
- Recommendation: strong accept for NIPS 2023 — significant, well-justified contribution with clear theoretical and practical impact.
