- Paper proposes updating the Cholesky factor of the covariance matrix in CMA-ES, offering a theoretically justified alternative to full covariance updates.  
- Main novelty: theoretical justification (Lemma 1) showing rotation matrix convergence, establishing correctness in the standard CMA-ES setting.  
- Achieves optimal asymptotic runtime complexity O(µ d²) and optimal storage, reducing computational bottleneck in high dimensions.  
- Empirical results across benchmarks and dimensions show substantial runtime improvements without harming function-evaluation performance.  
- Builds on prior Cholesky-based CMA-ES work (Suttorp et al., Krause & Igel) and contrasts with infrequent-update and reduced-complexity approaches.  
- Clear, well-written presentation that connects to existing CMA-ES formulations.  
- Strength: rigorous theoretical analysis provides confidence in the method’s validity.  
- Strength: practical gains in runtime and memory make the method attractive for large-scale problems.  
- Weakness: limited empirical comparison to other derivative-free optimizers (e.g., DIRECT, Nelder–Mead) for broader context.  
- Weakness: practical applicability of Lemma 1 needs more nuanced discussion—robustness to deviations from its conditions is unclear.  
- Weakness: error introduced by replacing st with ŝt in step-size adaptation requires deeper analysis and empirical assessment.  
- Weakness: brief mention of cache effects (e.g., at d=256)—needs expanded discussion of memory access patterns and optimizations.  
- Recommendation: accept with minor revisions.  
- Suggested revisions: expand related work to include non-CMA optimizers, provide detailed error analysis/experiments for the step-size approximation, discuss robustness to Lemma 1 conditions, and elaborate on memory/cache optimization strategies.
