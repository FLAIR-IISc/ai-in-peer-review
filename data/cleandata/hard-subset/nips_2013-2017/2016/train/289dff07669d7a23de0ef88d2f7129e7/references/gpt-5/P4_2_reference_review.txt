Summary
The paper proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES, a variant of CMA-ES that maintains and updates a triangular Cholesky factor A of the covariance matrix C = A A^T, instead of maintaining C and periodically computing its square root or eigendecomposition. The key algorithmic change is to rewrite the standard rank-1 and rank-µ covariance updates as a sequence of rank-1 updates on A with overall O(µ d^2) time per iteration, and to approximate the cumulative step-size adaptation (CSA) by replacing C^{-1/2} with A^{-1}. The authors argue that this approximation introduces only a small, vanishing error as the normalized covariance “shape” stabilizes. They provide a lemma showing that the rotation component in the RQ decomposition of C^{1/2} converges under convergence of the normalized covariance, motivating the CSA approximation. Empirically, on standard noiseless benchmarks (including rotated versions), Cholesky-CMA-ES achieves essentially the same number of function evaluations as the reference CMA-ES but substantially lower wall-clock time, with growing speedups as d increases. The method is compared to a reference CMA-ES with delayed decompositions, a version with fixed delay d, and a prior non-triangular Cholesky update approach.

Relation to prior work
Prior work reduced cost by delaying decompositions [Hansen and Ostermeier], by restricting model complexity (e.g., diagonal/separable or low-rank variants) [Ros and Hansen; Loshchilov; Sun et al.], or by directly updating decompositions (Cholesky updates) for specific CMA-ES variants [Suttorp et al.; Krause and Igel]. The novelty here is extending quadratically efficient triangular Cholesky rank-1 updates to the standard multi-offspring CMA-ES with both rank-1 and rank-µ terms, and providing a theoretical justification for replacing C^{-1/2} by A^{-1} in CSA without materially degrading behavior.

Strengths
- Clear motivation: removes cubic-time decompositions that dominate runtime in moderate/large d while keeping full covariance modeling.
- Sound algorithmic engineering: O(µ d^2) update for A with rank-1 updates; triangular structure enables O(d^2) solves for A^{-1}; memory footprint is reduced compared to storing both C and its decomposition/inverse.
- Theoretical rationale: Lemma 1 and the discussion around RQ/polar factors argue that the discrepancy between using C^{-1/2} and A^{-1} in CSA vanishes as the covariance shape stabilizes, and that triangular factors avoid non-identifiability in rotations that can destabilize updates.
- Empirical support: on multiple benchmarks and their rotated variants, function-evaluation counts match the reference CMA-ES while wall-clock speedups are substantial (e.g., ~20× at d=64), consistent with the complexity analysis.
- Practical relevance: CMA-ES is widely used in ML (e.g., policy search, hyperparameter optimization), so reducing iteration cost without sacrificing sample efficiency is impactful.

Weaknesses
- A key claim is incorrect: the paper states that “the diagonal elements of a triangular Cholesky factor are the square roots of the eigenvalues of the factorized matrix.” This is false in general; diag(A)^2 are not the eigenvalues of C = A A^T (only their product equals det(C)). Thus, eigenvalues are not obtained “for free” from Cholesky; one still needs an eigendecomposition or equivalent to get eigenvalues. This should be corrected.
- The theoretical justification for CSA approximation relies on the assumption that the normalized covariance shape converges. CMA-ES convergence and shape convergence are not established in general; only heuristic or simplified expectations are cited. No finite-time error bound is provided for ||p_σ|| when using A^{-1}.
- Invariance. The original CSA is already conjectured not to be fully affine-invariant; the proposed approximation may further deviate. While rotation-invariance is probed empirically (rotated benchmarks), quantitative measures of the induced rotation E_t and their effect on step-size dynamics are only in the supplement; bringing summary statistics into the main paper would strengthen the case.
- Experimental scope. Experiments are on noiseless, unconstrained benchmarks; no results for noisy functions, constraints, or more pathological ill-conditioned functions beyond the chosen set. Also, dimensions are up to 256; extremely high d where low-rank methods dominate are not in scope (acknowledged), but an ablation on µ and its impact on runtime would help.
- Numerical stability. The stability properties of the proposed rankOneUpdate algorithm (Algorithm 2) are not discussed in depth (e.g., accumulation of roundoff over many updates, behavior when β < 0 in rank-one downdates). A reference or analysis would be helpful.
- Reproducibility details are light in the main text (hardware, BLAS threading, compiler, stopping criteria nuances), though an implementation in Shark is noted.

Comments on review criteria
- Quality: The algorithmic contribution is technically sound overall; the CSA approximation is plausible and empirically validated. However, the eigenvalue claim is incorrect and should be fixed. The theoretical part provides asymptotic arguments but lacks finite-time bounds.
- Clarity: The paper is generally well written, with clear algorithms and motivation. Some reference formatting appears garbled. Explanations around the CSA approximation and its empirical validation could be more prominent in the main text.
- Originality: Extending triangular Cholesky updates with rank-µ to standard CMA-ES and justifying the CSA approximation within this framework appears novel relative to [Suttorp et al.; Krause and Igel].
- Significance: Likely to be widely used by practitioners who need full-covariance CMA-ES. The speedups at moderate-to-high dimensions without loss in evaluations are significant.

Suggestions for improvement
- Correct the statement about eigenvalues and clarify what information is available “for free” from A (e.g., determinant, efficient solves).
- Provide either a finite-time bound or a quantitative empirical bound on ||p_σ|| discrepancy between C^{-1/2} and A^{-1} formulations, perhaps in terms of ||E_t^T E_{t-1} − I|| and its empirical decay.
- Summarize in the main paper the measured rotation changes (E_t) and their effect on step-size dynamics.
- Discuss numerical stability of Algorithm 2 (rank-one updates/downdates), referencing standard Cholesky update literature, and indicate safeguards (e.g., jitter, conditioning checks).
- Expand experiments to include a noisy benchmark and report memory usage and CPU/GPU details. Consider reporting effect of varying µ and of cache effects at larger d.
- Clarify whether A^{-1} is computed via explicit inversion or triangular solves; the latter avoids forming A^{-1} explicitly and is numerically preferable.

Pros for acceptance
- Strong practical impact: large speedups with unchanged sample efficiency for full-covariance CMA-ES.
- Clear, implementable method with open-source integration.
- Novel extension of Cholesky updates to standard CMA-ES and reasonable theoretical rationale.

Cons for acceptance
- Incorrect claim about eigenvalues from the Cholesky diagonal.
- Theoretical justification for CSA approximation is asymptotic and assumption-dependent; no finite-time guarantees.
- Experimental scope limited to noiseless, unconstrained benchmarks; limited reporting of rotation-invariance metrics in the main text.
