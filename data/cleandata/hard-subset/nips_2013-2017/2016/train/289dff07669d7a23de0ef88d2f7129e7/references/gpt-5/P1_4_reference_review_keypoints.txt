- Proposes Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains lower-triangular Cholesky factor A and samples via x = m + σAy, avoiding full matrix square root/eigendecomposition while preserving sampling invariance.
- Main challenge: CSA uses C−1/2 in pσ; replacing it with A−1 introduces a rotation Et from RQ decomposition, which authors approximate by omitting Et as tracking it is cubic-time.
- Provide a lemma showing rotations Et stabilize when normalized Ct converges, arguing the omission induces a vanishing error due to exponential forgetting in the CSA path.
- Extend rank-one and rank-µ updates to the Cholesky domain with O(µ d^2) time and O(d^2) triangular storage; A−1 can be obtained via O(d^2) triangular solves when needed.
- Experiments on standard noiseless benchmarks (including random rotations) show nearly identical function-evaluation counts to reference CMA-ES and significant wall-clock speedups that grow with dimension.
- Compared to delayed-update CMA-ES and non-triangular Cholesky update baselines; code available (Shark) improving reproducibility.
- Strength: clear, practical drop-in variant reducing per-iteration cost and storage with negligible impact on evaluations.
- Strength: solid algorithmic engineering with correct factorized rank-1 and rank-µ updates and complexity-optimal design.
- Strength: persuasive theoretical intuition (lemma) explaining why omitting rotation in CSA is benign as Ct stabilizes, focusing appropriately on ‖pσ‖.
- Strength: thorough empirical evaluation across dimensions/rotations and reporting wall-clock times.
- Weakness: incorrect claim that diagonal entries of Cholesky factor are square roots of eigenvalues of C; needs correction and alternative monitoring suggestions.
- Weakness: theoretical justification limited—Lemma 1 assumes convergence of normalized Ct which is not proven for stochastic CMA-ES; formal gap should be acknowledged and error bounds explored without full convergence.
- Weakness: approximation further deviates from affine-invariance; recommend reporting induced rotations Et, effects on pσ norms and σ trajectories, and tests on non-rotational affine transforms (scalings).
- Weakness: experimental fairness concerns—excluding non-convergent Rosenbrock runs biases results; request reporting of success rates and conditional/unconditional time-to-target and clarification of reference CMA-ES decomposition frequency.
- Weakness: limited robustness tests—only noiseless, unconstrained benchmarks; request noisy, constrained, ill-conditioned, and comparisons to limited-memory variants.
- Weakness: potential numerical stability issues from repeated rank-one Cholesky updates—ask whether periodic re-factorization or jitter is used and at what cost.
- Quality: technically sound overall but needs fixes for eigenvalue claim and more robust experimental reporting.
- Clarity: generally well written with useful pseudocode, but fix malformed references and disambiguate notation (E vs. expectation); state computational cost including A inversion.
- Originality: meaningful extension of prior Cholesky updates to rank-µ CMA-ES and a new CSA approximation with theoretical rationale.
- Significance: high practical impact—reduces cost/memory for widely used CMA-ES while preserving evaluation efficiency.
- Questions for authors: frequency of recomputing A/re-Cholesky; empirical distribution/stabilization time of Et−1T Et − I; sensitivity to µ and weights; results on noisy benchmarks and Rosenbrock success rates.
- Minor issues: fix eigenvalue claim about diag(A); clean up garbled references; clarify CMA-ES-Ref decomposition frequency.
- Arguments for acceptance: strong practical speedups, clean algorithmic contribution, open-source implementation, plausible theory, and careful empirical study.
- Arguments against acceptance: limited theoretical guarantees, incorrect eigenvalue claim, incomplete experimental scope and possibly unfair baseline configuration.
- Rating: 7 (accept) with reviewer confidence 0.75.
