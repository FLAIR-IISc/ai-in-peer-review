- Proposal: Introduces Cholesky-CMA-ES that maintains triangular Cholesky factor A (C = A A^T), samples x = m + σ A y, and replaces C^{-1/2} with A^{-1} in CSA to avoid eigendecomposition.  
- Algorithmic contribution: Derives rank-one updates for A with O(µ d^2) time (µ = O(log d)) and optimal O(d^2) storage.  
- Theoretical justification: Provides a lemma showing implicit orthogonal factor convergence and argues asymptotic correctness of using A^{-1} due to exponential memory of the evolution path.  
- Major technical error: Incorrect claim that Cholesky diagonal gives eigenvalues; diagonal gives pivots (determinant), not eigenvalues or singular values.  
- Complexity/derivation soundness: Rank-one update derivations and complexity claims are technically sound and consistent with prior work.  
- Empirics strengths: Experiments on rotated smooth benchmarks show nearly identical function-evaluation counts and substantial wall-clock speedups that increase with dimension.  
- Empirics weaknesses: Missing noisy or constrained problems, excluded Rosenbrock failures, no reported success rates or run-length distributions.  
- Suggested ablation: Should quantify deviation in ‖pσ‖ caused by A^{-1} vs C^{-1/2} (alluded to in supplement).  
- Numerical stability concern: Repeated rank-one updates may accumulate round-off error; paper should discuss occasional re-factorization, jitter, or other remedies.  
- Clarity: Well organized overall with helpful discussion of the CSA approximation, but contains typos, a formatting error in a formula, and confusing QR/RQ notational switches.  
- Reproducibility: Uses CMA-ES defaults and Shark/LAPACK, but code availability and any deviations from standard settings should be clearly stated.  
- Originality: Builds on prior Cholesky updates but novelly extends to rank-µ CMA-ES and justifies the CSA approximation without C^{-1/2}; sufficiently novel for NeurIPS.  
- Significance: High practical impact for users needing full-covariance CMA-ES in moderate-cost evaluations and large d; reported speedups are compelling and implementation is simple.  
- Pros: Addresses a real bottleneck, yields quadratic time/storage, provides clean rank-one updates, and shows strong wall-clock gains; integrated into a public library.  
- Cons: Wrong eigenvalue claim, limited theoretical guarantees (no finite-time bounds), narrow experimental scope, and minor clarity/notation issues.  
- Suggestions: Remove/correct eigenvalue claim, report success rates and noisy benchmarks, quantify transient error in ‖pσ‖, and discuss numerical-stability countermeasures.  
- Recommendation: Weak accept — useful, implementable improvement with convincing empirical evidence pending minor fixes.  
- Reviewer confidence: High on algorithmic and linear-algebra aspects; medium on empirical validation breadth.
