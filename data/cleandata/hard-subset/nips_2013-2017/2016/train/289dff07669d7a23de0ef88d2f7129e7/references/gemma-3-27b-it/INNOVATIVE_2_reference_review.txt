## Review of CMA-ES with Optimal Covariance Update and Storage Complexity

This paper tackles a critical bottleneck in the widely used CMA-ES algorithm: the computational cost of covariance matrix updates. The authors propose a Cholesky-CMA-ES variant, leveraging Cholesky factorization to achieve a theoretically optimal time and storage complexity for the update step. The core idea – replacing the matrix square root with its Cholesky factor and deriving a corresponding update rule – is a clever and potentially impactful contribution. The paper provides a theoretical justification for the method, demonstrating that the introduction of an approximate step-size adaptation doesn’t fundamentally deteriorate performance, backed by a lemma concerning convergence of coordinate system rotations. The empirical evaluation confirms the speedup, particularly in higher dimensions, while maintaining comparable function evaluation counts.

**Strengths:**

* **Novelty:** The approach of utilizing a Cholesky decomposition directly within the CMA-ES update loop, along with the theoretical justification, is genuinely novel. While previous work explored Cholesky-based updates for *variations* of CMA-ES, this paper successfully adapts it to the standard algorithm.
* **Theoretical Grounding:** The theoretical analysis, particularly the Lemma 1 regarding coordinate system convergence, provides a strong rationale for why the approximation introduced by the Cholesky update should not negatively impact performance. This is a key differentiator from simpler optimizations.
* **Empirical Validation:** The experimental results clearly demonstrate a significant runtime improvement, scaling favorably with dimensionality. The comparison against standard and alternative CMA-ES implementations solidifies the benefit of the proposed approach. The inclusion of runtime profiling for different dimensions is insightful.
* **Practical Impact:** Reducing the computational cost of the covariance update is a valuable contribution with the potential to broaden the applicability of CMA-ES to larger-scale problems, such as those encountered in deep learning or complex simulations.
* **Clear Presentation:** The paper is generally well-written and logically organized. The technical details are presented with sufficient clarity, and the connection to existing literature is well-established.

**Weaknesses:**

* **Empirical Scope:** While the experiments demonstrate a runtime improvement, the benchmark functions used are relatively standard. More challenging, high-dimensional problems reflecting real-world applications (e.g., hyperparameter tuning of large neural networks, reinforcement learning environments with high-dimensional state spaces) would further strengthen the validation. The reliance on median runtimes to highlight improvements potentially masks variability.
* **Limited Error Analysis:** While the paper provides a theoretical argument for the approximation's validity, a more detailed empirical analysis of the error introduced by replacing the matrix square root with the Cholesky factor would be beneficial. Quantifying the deviation in step-size adaptation could provide further confidence in the method’s robustness.
* **Cache Effects:** The acknowledgement of L2 cache limitations for d=256 is a good point, but the analysis doesn't fully explore the impact of memory access patterns. Further investigation into memory usage and potential optimization strategies would be valuable.
* **Step Size Adaptation Discussion:** While the theoretical justification is presented, a deeper intuition behind why the simplified step-size adaptation (removing the correction factor) works well in practice would be valuable. The explanation feels slightly hand-wavy.

**Arguments for Acceptance:**

* The paper presents a genuinely novel and well-motivated approach to a significant performance bottleneck in CMA-ES.
* The theoretical analysis is compelling and provides a strong foundation for the proposed method.
* The empirical results demonstrate a clear and substantial runtime improvement, particularly in higher dimensions.
* The potential impact of this work on the applicability of CMA-ES to larger-scale problems is significant.

**Arguments Against Acceptance:**

* The empirical evaluation could be strengthened by including more challenging, real-world benchmark problems.
* A more detailed analysis of the error introduced by the approximation would enhance confidence in the method’s robustness.



**Overall Assessment:**

This is a strong paper that addresses a long-standing challenge in CMA-ES with a clever and theoretically sound solution. While the empirical evaluation could be expanded, the presented results are convincing, and the potential impact of this work is significant. I lean heavily towards acceptance, prioritizing the novelty and potential of this approach over minor methodological imperfections. This paper represents a valuable contribution to the field of derivative-free optimization and has the potential to significantly broaden the applicability of CMA-ES.

**Recommendation:** Accept.
